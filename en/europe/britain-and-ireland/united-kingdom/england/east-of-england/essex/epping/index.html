Introduction to Epping

Epping is a charming market town at the northern tip of Epping Forest, just 17.5 miles away from the bustling city of London. Its location makes it an ideal destination for people who want to escape the noise and commotion of the big city while still being close enough to visit it. Epping is also rich in history, culture, and natural wonders, with a lot of local attractions to offer.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore everything that makes Epping a must-visit destination, including the best places to visit, food and drink scene, hiking/biking routes, local culture and customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Epping

There are plenty of places to visit in Epping, catering to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the most popular ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

§ Epping Forest District Museum - This museum showcases the rich history of the district, including the lives of local people, the forest, farming, and commerce.

§ The Queen Elizabeth Hunting Lodge - This Tudor-style lodge was built in 1543 for King Henry VIII, and it offers a unique insight into the history of hunting and forests.

§ Copped Hall - A gorgeous Georgian mansion that has been restored to its former glory, with fantastic gardens and wonderful guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Epping: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

§ Epping Forest - Claims to be the largest public open space in Greater London, with many walking and biking trails and host species and habitats.

§ Lee Valley Park - A stunning 10,000-acre park that provides a host of outdoor activities and events, including camping, golfing, bird-watching, and fishing.

§ High Beach - A small village that sits at the edge of Epping Forest, High Beach offers a tranquil setting that’s perfect for picnics, walks or a day out.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Epping

Hiking and biking enthusiasts won’t run short of trails in Epping. Here are some of the most popular routes:

§ Epping Forest Centenary Walk - A 16-mile circular walk or bike ride that goes through some of the best features of the forest, the village and countryside of Epping.

§ The Essex Way - A 81-mile (130 km) hiking route that goes through some of the most picturesque towns and villages in Essex.

§ London Loop - A 150-mile (240 km) circular walk or bike ride around London, a small section of which takes in the western edge of Epping.

Best Things to Do in Epping: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and visiting cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of other exciting activities and tours to do in Epping:

§ Take part in an archery lesson at the Queen Elizabeth Hunting Lodge. Get taught by experienced and friendly instructors against the stunning and historical backdrop.

§ Ride the Epping Ongar Railway, which offers a trip back in time with its heritage steam and diesel engines. Offers several tours, including a murder mystery train ride and vintage bus trips to the historic sites.

§ Play a game of apocalyptic-themed paintball at Campaign Paintball, a huge outdoor battlefield near the town centre. They have several playing areas and offer equipment rental and coaching, making it perfect for both new and experienced players.

Week-Long Itinerary for Epping

Day 1: Visit Epping Forest District Museum and explore the historical streets of Epping town.

Day 2: Go hiking or biking in the forest by following the Epping Forest Centenary Walk, followed by a tour of the fascinating Queen Elizabeth Hunting Lodge and its grounds.

Day 3: Visit Copped Hall and its stunning gardens and nearby High Beach for a picnic or walk around the forest.

Day 4: Spend the day fishing, bird-watching, or playing golf at Lee Valley Park.

Day 5: Take a ride on the Epping Ongar Heritage Railway, tour the countryside, and learn about local history and transportation.

Day 6: Join the apocalyptic paintball battle Day 7: Relax and enjoy the food and drink scene of Epping

Food and Drink Scene of Epping

Epping has a thriving food and drink scene that caters to all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

§ Pie and Mash - A traditional dish that can be found in many pubs and cafes, made with mashed potatoes and minced meat.

§ Eton Mess - A dessert consisting of meringue, cream, and strawberries.

§ Essex Seafood Platter - A platter of fresh seafood, including crab, lobster, oyster, and shrimp, caught straight from Essex coastlines.

Local Beverages

§ Adnams Broadside - A copper-colored, robust ale with notes of fruit and caramel. Brewed in Suffolk County since 1872.

§ Gorilla Boy Ruby Red IPA - A hazy red IPA with a dominant fruity aroma of citrus and hops.

Where to Stay in Epping: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options available for accommodations in Epping, catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the most popular ones:

§ Premier Inn Epping - A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms, great service, and breakfast.

§ White Hart Hotel - A 16th-century hotel that has been refurbished to a high standard, with friendly staff, great food and drink, and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Epping: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Epping, here are some ideas for souvenirs and local products:

§ Forest Honey - A jar of locally sourced honey, made by bees that inhabit Epping Forest and nearby countryside.

§ Epping Forest Gin - A small-batch, handcrafted gin, infused with juniper berries, coriander seeds, orris root, and other botanicals, and distilled in small copper pots.

Local Culture and Customs of Epping

§ In Epping, it’s customary to greet someone with a nod or smile when walking down the street.

§ Epping is a safe and friendly town, and visitors are often welcomed with warmth and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Epping

The best time to visit Epping is from late spring to early autumn (May to September), when the days are longer, warmer, and sunnier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Epping

Do:

§ Do go hiking or biking in Epping Forest, the heart of the town.

§ Do try the local food and beverages, especially pie and mash and forest honey.

§ Do use the local transportation system, including buses and trains, to get around.

Don’t:

§ Don’t litter or pollute the environment, including the forest.

§ Don’t be disrespectful to local customs and traditions: always greet others politely and respectfully.

§ Don’t forget to bring an extra layer for when the weather turns chilly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Epping

Epping is a safe town with a low crime rate, but visitors should still exercise caution and take common-sense precautions, such as locking doors and windows at night, not walking alone in the dark, and staying alert in crowded spaces.

Health in Epping

Epping has several medical facilities and pharmacies within easy reach, but visitors are advised to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected illnesses or injuries.

Getting to Epping: Transportation Options and Logistics

Epping is accessible by different modes of transportation, including:

§ By Train - Epping is served by the Central Line of London Underground, with trains arriving from London in around 40 minutes.

§ By Bus - Several bus routes serve Epping, coming from nearby towns and cities.

Airports Near Epping

If you’re arriving from further afield, here are some nearby airports:

§ London Stansted Airport - Located 17.5 miles away from Epping town and is only a 25-minute drive.

§ London Heathrow Airport - Located 50 miles south-west of Epping and is reachable by direct train to London and then a transfer to Epping.

Bus Stations Near Epping

§ Epping Tube Station - The starting node for several bus routes that serve Epping and many nearby areas.

§ Harlow Bus Station - A busy bus station with routes connecting to Epping and other towns in Essex.

Other Options

§ Taxi - Taxis/car rental service is available at most travel hubs, such as airports, for hire.

How to Get Around in Epping

Epping is a small town, and most attractions are within walking/biking distance or accessible by public transportation.

§ Walking - Epping is a great place for walking with beautiful surroundings, with many trails and footpaths.

§ Biking - Biking is another popular way to get around and explore the town centre, surrounding countryside and forest.

§ Bus - There are regular bus services within Epping and the district for visitors who prefer public transportation option.

Where to Go Next from Epping: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore nearby areas after visiting Epping, here are some of the best options:

§ London - Epping is conveniently connected to London by Tube, train, and bus and offers a wealth of entertainment options, including museums, galleries, parks, and attractions.

§ Harlow - A new town close to Epping that offers excellent shopping and cultural experiences, including theaters, museums, and beautiful parks.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural surroundings, rich history, and friendly locals, Epping is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and enriching getaway. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to plan your trip and explore everything that makes Epping unique and special.
