Introduction to Old Harlow

Nestled in Essex, just north of London, Old Harlow is one of the most attractive areas in the East of England. The town has a long and fascinating history, with a wealth of cultural and natural attractions that make it the perfect destination for travelers looking for something a little different.

List of Places to Visit in Old Harlow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Harlow has a rich and diverse cultural history with a plethora of museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s past. These attractions offer an insight into the people, places, and events that have helped shape the town’s development over the years.

  • The Gibberd Garden is a 7-acre garden and sculpture park, created by Sir Frederick Gibberd in 1957. It’s an essential stop for lovers of art, culture, and nature.
  • The Harlow Museum and Walled Gardens are a treasure trove for anyone interested in the history of the town. Its exhibitions on local industries, the New Town development, and agriculture make it a must-visit.
  • The St. Mary’s Church is the oldest church in Harlow, with a history that dates back to the 12th century. The stunning architecture and intricate stained glass windows are a perfect example of the town’s illustrious past.
  • The Museum of Harlow is situated in a stunning Georgian farmhouse that has been converted into a museum. The museum exhibits everything from prehistoric times through the Second World War era, making it a comprehensive history lesson for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Old Harlow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who prefer the great outdoors, Old Harlow has plenty to offer. The town is surrounded by beautiful countryside, parks, and nature reserves that are ideal for hiking or biking, or simply for taking a leisurely stroll.

  • The Epping Forest District offers a plethora of activities for nature lovers including cycling paths and walking trails.
  • Parndon Woods Nature Reserve is perfect for those who want to take a quiet walking tour among the tall trees and its tranquil river.
  • The Great and Little Parndon Park is a perfect spot for a picnic with its beautiful open space and colorful flower gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Harlow

Old Harlow is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its picturesque countryside, and weather which stays moderate throughout the year. Some of the most popular trails in the area include:

  • The Harcamlow Way, a scenic trail that starts from Harlow Town Park and goes on through Epping Forest.
  • The Essex Way, a 82-mile Long-Distance Trail starts from Epping Forest and goes all the way to Harwich in Essex, past the some of the most beautiful scenery in the county.

Best Things to Do in Old Harlow: Activities and Tours

Beyond its museums, history, walking trails, and parks, Old Harlow offers a plethora of activities, such as:

  • The Play Golf at Paringdon is an 18-hole Par 3 golf course with an academy for beginner golfers and younger visitors.
  • The Lakeside Karting is a suitable place for adrenaline rush enthusiasts, with its 380-meter circuit and 70cc race karts, which can be used by both kids and adults.
  • The FootGolf course in Harlow is an exciting fusion of soccer and golf which can be played by people of all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old Harlow

Day 1: Start with a visit to the Harlow Museum and Walled Gardens, then head to The Gibberd Garden.

Day 2: Hike the Harcamlow Way and enjoy the beautiful Epping Forest.

Day 3: Visit the St. Mary’s Church and explore the surrounding countryside.

Day 4: Experience the outdoor karting at Lakeside Karting, then relax in the Parndon Woods Nature Reserve.

Day 5: Bike the Essex Way and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 6: Play golf at Play Golf at Paringdon and cap off the day with a trip to the nearby pubs.

Day 7: Spend the day exploring the town’s other attractioons and enjoy local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Old Harlow

For foodies, Old Harlow offers a range of local dishes and beverages to savor.

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Fish and Chips from Old Harlow Fish Bar, which are the best in the town, and are a must-try for anyone visiting.
  • The Shepherd’s Pie from Gallery Cafe Harlow, which offers an outstanding take on one of England’s most beloved traditional dishes.
  • The steak and kidney pie from Victoria Cottage at Harlow Garden Centre, served with a pile of creamy carrots on top is a delight for both the stomach and senses.

Local Beverages

  • The beer at The Cock pub is famous throughout the county for its traditional ales, including the Harlow Gold, Harlow Brown, and Summertime ales.
  • The The Prince Louis offers a variety of wines from around the world to accompany its menu list offering French wines to New Zealander sauvignon blanc.

Where to Stay in Old Harlow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old Harlow offers a range of accommodation options which cater to all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to cheap hostels and guesthouses.

  • The Green Man Hotel is situated at the heart of Old Harlow, it was established in the late 14th century as the Duchy of Cornwall. The Green Man offers spectacular rooms, and a beautiful patio bar.
  • The Premier Inn Harlow Central is another option, situated on the northern fringe of the town. The hotel has spacious rooms, and its restaurant is famous for its delicious breakfasts.

What to Buy in Old Harlow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Old Harlow offers a range of souvenirs and local products for tourists to take back home.

  • The Harlow Art Trust offers exclusive artwork from a range of British and international artists, as well as art books and postcards to commemorate your visit.
  • Local farmers offer freshly grown fruits and organic vegetables at the Harlow Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Old Harlow

Old Harlow retains traditional and cultural customs that reflect its long and vibrant history. Visitoors to the town should be sure to witness some of the following traditions:

  • The annual Harlow Festival is a vibrant display of traditional music, dance, art, and cultural activities from the local area and beyond.

Best Time to Visit Old Harlow

The perfect time of year to visit Old Harlow is from May to September, which are the warmest months. April is when spring kicks off, so if you want to enjoy cool and balmy spring weather, late April can be a perfect time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Harlow

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Explore the surrounding countryside and parks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or drop cigarette butts in public places.

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or other tourists.

  • Visit the National Cycle Academy in Harlow if you’re not interested in cycling.

Safety and Health

Old Harlow is a safe town to visit, and most visitors don’t experience any health issues. However, it is always essential to take precautions to ensure uninterrupted and safe journeys.

Safety in Old Harlow

  • Avoid walking in unfamiliar areas alone, especially at night.

  • Avoid carrying large sums of cash.

  • Always comply with the instructions of local authorities and emergency services.

Health in Old Harlow

  • In case of any health emergencies, visit Princess Alexandra Hospital or contact the emergency services.

Getting to Old Harlow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Old Harlow is easily accessible by any means of transportation- via air, car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Old Harlow

  • London City Airport is the closest airport, approximately 16 miles from Old Harlow.
  • London Stansted Airport is another nearby airport, around 18 miles from Old Harlow.

Bus Stations Near Old Harlow

  • The Harlow bus station acts as the primary link between town and surrounding cities.

Other Options

  • Harlow train station offers a direct rail service to London, Cambridge and other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Old Harlow

Old Harlow is easily accessible by bus, taxi, or on foot. The Harlow Town train station is situated on the edge of the town center, and most destinations are within walking distance. Local buses also offer an inexpensive way to get around the town or visit nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Old Harlow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The historic town of Bishop Stortford, situated a short drive from Harlow
  • The market town of Epping, surrounded by the rolling hills of Epping Forest.
  • The ancient Roman settlement of St Albans.

Conclusion

Old Harlow offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, culture, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway in the countryside, an adrenaline-fueled adventure, or a cultural feast of museums and art, Old Harlow has something to offer everyone. So pack your bags today and explore the beauty of this fantastic town!
