Introduction to Harwich

Harwich, located in Essex, East of England, United Kingdom, is a charming coastal town that has been overlooked by tourists for too long. Known for its rich maritime history and architecture, Harwich offers a variety of attractions and activities for all ages. From scenic walking trails to museums and historical sites, this travel guide has everything you need to know about exploring Harwich.

List of Places to Visit in Harwich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Redoubt Fort: Built in 1808, it is a unique fortification system that was used to protect the town and its harbor during times of conflict.

  • Treadwheel Crane: Built in 1667, it’s a mechanical crane used for transferring cargo from ship to shore.

  • Harwich Maritime Museum: A museum that showcases Harwich’s rich maritime history.

  • Electric Palace Cinema: It’s one of the oldest purpose-built cinemas in Britain that still has its original projection equipment.

  • Ha’penny Pier: A picturesque Victorian pier which offers stunning views of the harbor.

Natural Attractions of Harwich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dovercourt Bay: A beautiful sandy beach that offers activities like swimming, sunbathing, and sailing.

  • Ha’penny Beach: An excellent beach for families, which provides small cafes and a play area.

  • Harwich Common: This green space is ideal for picnics, walks or jogging.

  • The Three Harwich’s: A trio of walks which offers a scenic and charming experience to see the heart of Harwich.

  • Shotley Peninsula: A perfect spot for birdwatching, walks, and cycling that offers stunning views of the Blackwater River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harwich

  • Harwich to Shotley via the backwaters: A flat route that meanders along the River Stour and offers beautiful views of the countryside.

  • Clacton-on-Sea to Harwich via Tendring and Ramsey: A scenic route that offers stunning views of the coastline.

  • Harwich to Manningtree: This is a moderately difficult route that offers sweeping views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Harwich: Activities and Tours

  • Harwich Foot Ferry: A water taxi that connects Harwich to Felixstowe, offers beautiful scenery and panoramic views on a budget.

  • Cruise on the Mayflower: A newly restored replica of the Mayflower that brings the story of the pilgrims to life.

  • Saltwater Sailing: It offers a unique opportunity to experience sailing in the idyllic waters of the River Stour.

Food and Drink Scene of Harwich

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish & Chips: It’s the quintessential British dish that Harwich does exceptionally well. Check out The Pier Fish & Chips for a delectable experience.

  • Harwich Pubs: You shouldn’t leave Harwich without sampling a pint of local ale or the traditional Sunday roast at the Red Lion.

Local Beverages

  • Harwich Brewery: Visit Harwich Brewery for their authentic and award-winning craft beers.

  • Harwich Wine and Beer Shop: Experience an extensive selection of wines and beers from all over the world at the Harwich Wine and Beer Shop.

Where to Stay in Harwich: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • The Pier Hotel: It’s the perfect place to enjoy breathtaking views of the sea and relax in luxurious surroundings.

  • The Royal Oak: Offers a perfect balance between luxury and comfort.

  • YHA: For budget-friendly accommodation, YHA offers modern, clean and comfortable options for all types of travelers.

What to Buy in Harwich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Harwich Tote Bag: While in Harwich, you will find plenty of options for purchasing a Harwich tote bag, a perfect souvenir or a functional accessory.

  • Locally Made Craft Beer: Don’t go home empty-handed, grab a few bottles of Harwich Brewery’s artisanal beer to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Harwich

  • Maritime Heritage: Harwich has a rich maritime history, which can be observed through its architecture and museums.

  • Pirate Day: Each year, Harwich, hosts a festival dedicated to pirates, with people of all ages dressed in pirate costumes and attending a parade throughout the town.

Best Time to Visit Harwich

The best time to visit Harwich is between May and September when the weather is warmer and there are more activities and tours available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harwich

Do:

  • Do explore the beautiful coastal scenery by bike or foot.

  • Do visit the Harwich Maritime Museum.

  • Do try authentic and locally sourced seafood.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the Edwardian electric-powered cinema.

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and a hat for those sunny fishing expeditions.

  • Don’t litter the nature reserves or leave things behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harwich

Overall, Harwich is a safe place to visit, but it’s always wise to keep your belongings in a safe and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Harwich

Be aware of the tick season between March and November and make sure to wear appropriate clothing and use insect repellent if going hiking or walking.

Getting to Harwich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harwich

The closest international airports are London Stansted Airport and Southend Airport. It takes approximately 1.5 hours to drive to Harwich from either airport. A direct train to Harwich International is available from London Liverpool Street station.

Bus Stations Near Harwich

The nearest bus stations to Harwich are Colchester, Ipswich, and Clacton.

Other Options

Harwich is well served by local taxi firms, and most of the tourist sites are within walking distance from each other.

How to Get Around in Harwich

The best way to get around Harwich is on foot or by cycling. Hiring a bike is easy and affordable, and it offers the opportunity to explore the beautiful countryside.

Where to Go Next from Harwich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Colchester: England’s oldest recorded town.

  • Felixstowe: A charming seaside town.

  • Aldeburgh: A beautiful and peaceful town in Suffolk.

Conclusion

Harwich is an undiscovered gem that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for all ages. Whether it’s museums and historical places, natural wonders, biking and hiking routes, or food and drink, Harwich is a town full of surprises. With this comprehensive tour guide, you are all set to explore the maritime heritage and culture of this beautiful town.
