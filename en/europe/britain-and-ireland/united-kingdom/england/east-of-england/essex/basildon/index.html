Introduction to Basildon

Basildon, located in Essex, East of England, is a hidden gem with a unique blend of nature and culture. The town is just a short distance away from London, making it an ideal destination for those wanting to explore a different side of England. Basildon boasts of a diverse range of attractions, from museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of Basildon.

List of Places to Visit in Basildon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Tyler Country Park: This park is the perfect representation of the town’s rich history and nature. The park consists of walking trails, lakes, and an old fort that was used during the Napoleonic Wars.
  • Cater Museum: The Cater Museum houses an extensive collection of historical items that showcase the life of Basildon over the years. The museum also includes a unique exhibit on the town’s history of toy-making.
  • Billericay Heritage Centre: This museum is located inside the historic library building and showcases the history of Billericay, a town near Basildon. Visitors can explore the town’s history, dating back to the Roman times.

Natural Attractions of Basildon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Eastgate Shopping Centre: If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, the Eastgate Shopping Centre has a variety of activities to offer, including crazy golf, mini bowling and an indoor adventure playground.
  • Langdon Hills Country Park: This park is perfect for nature enthusiasts and hikers, with magnificent hills offering panoramic views of the Basildon landscape. The park also includes a nature reserve where visitors can see a variety of local flora and fauna.
  • Laindon Common: For those interested in cycling and bird-watching, Laindon Common is the perfect spot. The common is an expansive area of grasslands and woods, with over 60 bird species known to reside here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basildon

Basildon offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Three Parks Trail: A two-mile-long trail that runs through three parks, this route is perfect for beginners and families.
  • Basildon Park and Ride to Pitsea: This route is approximately six miles long, providing a good challenge for experienced hikers and bikers. The trail runs through Laindon Common and Langdon Hills Country Park, providing breathtaking views.
  • Hutton Hike: This trail is approximately nine miles long and runs through beautiful countryside and various landmarks, including St. Peter’s Church and Thoby Lane Cottage.

Best Things to Do in Basildon: Activities and Tours

  • Basildon Sporting Village: For those looking for activities, the Basildon Sporting Village is a must-visit. The complex includes indoor pools, sports halls, and a fitness center.
  • Basildon Golf Course: This 18-hole golf course is perfect for golf enthusiasts of all levels. The club also offers golf lessons and a driving range for those interested in improving their skills.
  • Bingo Halls: Basildon has a few popular bingo halls, including Mecca Bingo and Gala Bingo, which offer a great atmosphere and a chance to win some money.

Week-Long Itinerary for Basildon

Day 1: Visit Wat Tyler Country Park and Cater Museum

Day 2: Explore Basildon Sporting Village and Eastgate Shopping Centre

Day 3: Hike at Laindon Common and then head to Langdon Hills Country Park for scenic views

Day 4: Visit the Billericay Heritage Centre and then head to Basildon Golf Course

Day 5: Enjoy the bingo halls and indoor games at Eastgate Shopping Centre

Day 6: Take a cycling trip along the Three Parks Trail

Day 7: Rest and relax in the hotel or guesthouse and enjoy the local food and drink scene

Food and Drink Scene of Basildon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pie and Mash: This traditional English dish is popular in Basildon and can be found in various pubs around town.
  • Fish and Chips: No trip to England is complete without trying this classic dish, and Basildon has plenty of places to indulge.
  • Sunday Roast: A staple dish in England, Sunday Roast is available at most restaurants and pubs around Basildon on Sundays.

Local Beverages

  • Essex Ale: Essex Ale is a locally brewed beer available at almost every pub around town.
  • Wine: Basildon is located close to various vineyards in Essex, and visitors can enjoy delicious locally produced wine at various pubs and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Basildon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Basildon can find a variety of accommodation options, including comfortable hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Travelodge Basildon: For those on a budget, this hotel provides basic amenities and clean rooms at a reasonable price.
  • The Bell Guesthouse: A cozy and comfortable guesthouse located in the heart of Basildon, The Bell provides guests with a homely atmosphere and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Basildon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Basildon can purchase a variety of local souvenirs and products, including:

  • Basildon Postcards and Magnets: These can be found at various tourist shops around town and make for excellent keepsakes or gifts.
  • Local Produce: Visitors can buy fresh locally grown fruits and vegetables at various markets and shops around Basildon.
  • Essex Cider and Wine: Visitors can purchase locally produced cider and wine at various shops and vineyards around Basildon.

Local Culture and Customs of Basildon

Basildon locals are known for their friendly nature and hospitality. The town has a rich culture, and visitors can learn more about it at various museums and historical sites around town. Basildon also has a thriving food and drink scene, and visitors should try to sample the local cuisine and drinks.

Best Time to Visit Basildon

The best time to visit Basildon is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and festivals in the area. However, visitors can also plan a trip during the off-season, as Basildon has attractions and events throughout the year.

  • Be loud and rude in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Basildon

Basildon is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions like any other destination. Visitors are advised to take care of their belongings and avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Basildon

Visitors can avail themselves of medical facilities at various clinics and hospitals around Basildon. Visitors should also take precautions against the sun and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Getting to Basildon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Basildon

  • London City Airport: The airport is approximately 22 miles from Basildon and can be reached via public transport or taxi.
  • London Stansted Airport: The airport is approximately 30 miles from Basildon and can be reached via public transport or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Basildon

  • Basildon Bus Station: Located in the town center, the bus station has regular services to various destinations around Essex and London.
  • Pitsea Bus Station: Located in the suburb of Pitsea, the bus station provides regular services to London and various Essex towns.

Other Options

  • Train: Basildon has a train station located in the town center, providing regular services to London and various Essex towns.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from various car rental agencies located in Basildon and nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Basildon

Visitors to Basildon can avail themselves of public transportation, including buses and trains. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Basildon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Southend-on-Sea: This popular seaside town is located just 25 miles from Basildon and boasts of an array of attractions, including its pier and amusement park.
  • Chelmsford: This historic city is just 15 miles away from Basildon and offers visitors a chance to explore a different side of Essex, including museums and historical landmarks.
  • London: The bustling metropolis of London is just 30 miles from Basildon and offers visitors a chance to explore world-renowned attractions such as the British Museum, London Eye, and Buckingham Palace.

Conclusion

Basildon is a town that offers a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. Whether you want to explore museums and historical sites, hike and bike through scenic trails, or indulge in delicious local cuisine, Basildon has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan a trip that suits your interests and budget while getting the best of Basildon.
