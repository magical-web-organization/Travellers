A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Dunstable, England

Dunstable, located in the majestic county of Bedfordshire, is a charming market town in the East of England. This town, steeped in history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway. Whether you’re interested in exploring the local attractions, enjoying outdoor activities, or just relaxing in the heart of the town, Dunstable has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Dunstable

Dunstable boasts an array of impressive attractions, ranging from culturally-important museums to natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit in the town that should not be missed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Priory Church of St Peter: This former Benedictine priory, now a parish church, was founded in the 12th century and is the oldest surviving building in Dunstable.
  2. Dunstable Downs: This designated Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty offers stunning views over the Vale of Aylesbury and is home to the famous Whipsnade Zoo.
  3. Dunstable Museum: This museum showcases the history of Dunstable and Bedfordshire from prehistoric times to the present day, and includes displays on local lace-making and hat-making industries.

Natural Attractions of Dunstable: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Chiltern Hills: This stunning rolling landscape is a walker’s paradise, offering stunning views and a range of trails to suit all abilities.
  2. Dunstable Rocks: This sandstone escarpment is popular with climbers and provides a dramatic backdrop to the town.
  3. Totternhoe Nature Reserve: This diverse nature reserve features ancient woodland, chalk grassland, and meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunstable

Dunstable is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with endless opportunities for hiking and cycling. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in and around the town include:

  1. Dunstable Downs Circular Walk: This 6-mile circular route takes you through the stunning Dunstable Downs and along the ancient Icknield Way.
  2. The Greensand Ridge Walk: This 40-mile long-distance trail takes you through the beautiful Buckinghamshire and Bedfordshire countryside, offering stunning views and unique landscapes.
  3. Whipsnade Tree Cathedral: This beautiful living cathedral, composed of trees and shrubs, provides a peaceful and scenic walking route.

Best Things to Do in Dunstable: Activities and Tours

Dunstable offers an array of activities and tours that cater to visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the top things to do in Dunstable:

  1. Whipsnade Zoo: One of the country’s largest zoos, Whipsnade Zoo is a must-visit attraction. Here you can see wild animals from around the world and even feed some of them.
  2. Airkix Indoor Skydiving: Experience the thrill of indoor skydiving in this unique indoor wind tunnel.
  3. Dunstable Leisure Centre: This modern leisure centre offers swimming pools, squash courts, and a range of fitness classes to keep you active during your stay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunstable

Dunstable can be explored in a day, but to truly appreciate everything it has to offer, a week-long stay is recommended. Here is a sample week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Priory Church of St Peter and Dunstable Museum
Day 2 Hike Dunstable Downs and visit Whipsnade Zoo
Day 3 Cycle the Greensand Ridge Walk
Day 4 Climb Dunstable Rocks and explore Totternhoe Nature Reserve
Day 5 Take an indoor skydiving session at Airkix
Day 6 Enjoy a leisurely day at Dunstable Leisure Centre
Day 7 Relax and explore the town’s charming shops and cafes

Food and Drink Scene of Dunstable

Dunstable offers a diverse food and drink scene, with an array of restaurants, cafes, and bars scattered throughout the town. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bedfordshire Clanger: A savory pastry filled with meat and vegetables.
  2. Lobscouse: A traditional stew made with beef or lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.
  3. Sticky Toffee Pudding: A classic British dessert made with dates and caramel.

Local Beverages

  1. Mead: A traditional honey-based alcoholic beverage.
  2. Bedfordshire Real Ales: A range of locally brewed beers and ales.

Where to Stay in Dunstable: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dunstable offers a range of accommodation options from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top places to stay in Dunstable:

  1. The Old Palace Lodge: This boutique hotel is housed in a converted 17th-century chapel and offers a unique and stylish stay.
  2. Cherish End Guest House: This cozy and welcoming guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a central location.
  3. Travelodge Dunstable: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Dunstable: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dunstable’s shops and markets offer a range of local products and souvenirs that make the perfect memento of your visit. Some of the top items to bring home include:

  1. Bedfordshire Lace: This locally-made lace is a unique and beautiful souvenir.
  2. Pork Pies: A regional specialty, these savory pies are perfect for a quick bite on-the-go.
  3. Black Cab Ale: This locally brewed beer is a must-try for beer lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Dunstable

Dunstable has a rich heritage and vibrant culture that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Some of the local customs to be aware of include:

  1. Morris Dancing: This centuries-old traditional dance is performed by local groups throughout the year.
  2. Dunstable Swan Jewel: This 16th-century jewel, now housed in the British Museum, was found in Dunstable and is a symbol of the town’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Dunstable

The best time to visit Dunstable is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and the town is bustling with activity. However, the shoulder seasons of spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds, making them an ideal time to visit as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunstable

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Dunstable:

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s history and heritage by visiting the local museums and cultural attractions.
  • Do enjoy the town’s natural beauty by taking a hike or bike ride through the surrounding countryside.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including regional specialties like Bedfordshire Clanger and pork pies.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Don’t disrespect the town’s traditions and customs, such as Morris Dancing and the Dunstable Swan Jewel.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities, as the weather can be unpredictable in England.

Safety and Health

Dunstable is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is important to take necessary precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety and health tips for visiting Dunstable:

Safety in Dunstable

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone in secluded areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large sums of cash or expensive items.
  • Be mindful of the weather conditions when enjoying outdoor activities and bring appropriate clothing and gear.

Health in Dunstable

  • Dunstable has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics.
  • It is advisable to bring any necessary medications with you and to ensure you have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Dunstable: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dunstable is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are the main options for getting to and around Dunstable:

Airports Near Dunstable

  • London Luton Airport: This airport is located approximately 12 miles from Dunstable and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Dunstable

  • Dunstable Busway: This bus station offers bus services throughout Bedfordshire and Hertfordshire.
  • Luton Bus Station: This station offers bus services throughout the Luton area and beyond.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train stations to Dunstable are Luton and Leagrave, both of which offer connections to London and other major UK cities.
  • Car: Dunstable is easily accessible by car, with the A5 running through the town.

How to Get Around in Dunstable

Dunstable is a compact town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you’re looking to venture further afield, the following transportation options are available:

  • Bus: Dunstable offers regular bus services that connect to the surrounding towns and villages.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available throughout Dunstable and can be hailed from designated taxi ranks or booked in advance.
  • Car Hire: Several car hire companies operate in Dunstable, making it easy to explore at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Dunstable: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dunstable is situated in a prime location for further exploration of the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • London: The vibrant and historic capital city is only an hour’s drive or train journey from Dunstable.
  • Aylesbury: This charming market town is located just a short drive away and is home to the beautiful Waddesdon Manor.
  • Cambridge: This historic university city is located approximately an hour’s drive from Dunstable and is a must-visit destination.

Conclusion

Dunstable is a hidden gem in the heart of England, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and vibrant modern lifestyle. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s museums and historical sites, get active in the great outdoors, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, Dunstable has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you some inspiration for your next visit to this wonderful town. Enjoy your stay!
