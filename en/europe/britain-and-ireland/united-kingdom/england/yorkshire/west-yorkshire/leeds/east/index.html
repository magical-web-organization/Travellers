Introduction to East

East is a vibrant and exciting part of Leeds, West Yorkshire, that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, delicious cuisine, or a mix of all three, East has got you covered. This guide will take you on a tour of the best places to visit, ways to get around, tips for staying safe and healthy, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in East

East is full of exciting attractions and hidden gems waiting to be explored. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Thackray Medical Museum: This award-winning museum takes you on a journey through the history of medicine, from Victorian quackery to modern technology.
  • Leeds industrial Museum at Armley Mills: Housed in an old mill complex, this interactive museum tells the story of Leeds’ industrial past.
  • Royal Armouries Museum: This impressive museum houses one of the world’s largest collections of arms and armour, with exhibits from ancient times to present day.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Roundhay Park: This vast park features beautiful gardens, lakes, and woodlands, as well as playgrounds, sports facilities, and even a tropical world.
  • Harewood House and Gardens: This stunning stately home was designed by Robert Adam and features beautiful gardens, a bird garden, and an adventure playground for kids.
  • Temple Newsam: This impressive Tudor-Jacobean house is set in beautiful parkland with landscaped gardens, a working farm, and even a rare breeds centre.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

If you’re looking to explore the great outdoors and get some exercise, East has plenty of options for hiking and biking. Some of the top routes to consider include:

  • The Leeds Country Way: This 63-mile circular route takes you through beautiful countryside and charming villages.
  • The Meanwood Valley Trail: This easy-to-follow trail runs for 7 miles along the Meanwood Valley, passing through parks, woods, and meadows.
  • The Trans Pennine Trail: This 215-mile trail runs from coast to coast, passing through East and providing a perfect way to explore the stunning Yorkshire countryside.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

East offers plenty of fun activities and unique tours to suit all interests and ages. Here are some of the top things to do during your visit:

  • Leeds Art Gallery: This gallery houses an impressive collection of British art, including works by Henry Moore, Barbara Hepworth, and Jacob Epstein.
  • Meanwood Valley Urban Farm: This working farm allows visitors to get up close with farm animals and learn about agriculture and conservation.
  • Escape Reality Leeds: This immersive and exciting escape room experience challenges players to solve a series of puzzles and clues in order to escape.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

If you’re planning to spend a week in East, consider the following itinerary to make sure you see all the top attractions:

Food and Drink Scene of East

East has a diverse culinary scene featuring local and international cuisine, as well as plenty of options for drinks and nightlife. Be sure to sample some of these local specialties during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire pudding: This savoury dish is a staple of Sunday roast dinners, featuring a fluffy batter with a crispy exterior.
  • Parkin: This sweet gingerbread cake is traditionally eaten on Bonfire Night (November 5th) and is made with oatmeal and molasses.
  • Fish and chips: This classic British dish features battered fish and crisp chips, often served with mushy peas and tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Leeds Pale Ale: This local beer is a favourite among craft beer drinkers, with a hoppy flavour and refreshing finish.
  • Yorkshire Tea: This iconic brand of tea is known for its strong flavour and smooth finish.
  • Rhubarb Gin: This fruity gin is made with rhubarb grown in the Yorkshire Triangle and is gaining popularity worldwide.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Clayton Hotel Leeds: This four-star hotel is conveniently located near the city centre and features comfortable rooms, a fitness centre, and a restaurant.
  • The Chapel House Serviced Apartments: These stylish apartments offer all the comforts of home, including cooking facilities, free Wi-Fi, and a flat-screen TV.
  • The High Bank Hotel: This family-run guest house offers friendly service, cosy rooms, and a hearty breakfast included in the rate.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home from your trip to East, here are some ideas:

  • Yorkshire Parkin: This traditional gingerbread cake makes a delicious souvenir and can be found at local bakeries and markets.
  • Leeds United FC Merchandise: If you or someone you know is a football fan, grab a souvenir from the Leeds United FC official store.
  • Clothing and Accessories by The Hepworth Wakefield: This modern art museum has a gift shop stocked with unique and stylish items, including clothing, jewellery, and homeware.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East is a friendly and welcoming place with a strong community spirit. Some of the local customs and traditions you might encounter include:

  • Yorkshire dialect: Locals might use terms and phrases that are unique to the area, such as “nowt” (nothing) and “ta” (thank you).
  • Yorkshire Day: Celebrated on August 1st, this day honours all things Yorkshire, with events and activities taking place throughout the county.
  • Morris dancing: This traditional English folk dance is performed by many dance groups in Yorkshire, often featuring lively music, colourful costumes, and jingling bells.

Best Time to Visit East

East is beautiful and lively all year round, but the best time to visit really depends on your interests and preferences. Spring and summer are a great time to enjoy outdoor activities and take advantage of the long daylight hours. Autumn is a great time for leaf-peeping and enjoying harvest festivals, while winter is perfect for cosying up with a warm drink and exploring the city’s indoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

When visiting East, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and follow basic etiquette. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do be friendly and polite to locals - they’re known for their warmth and hospitality.
  • Do take the time to learn about local history and culture, including the dialect, customs, and traditions.
  • Do tip wait staff in restaurants - 10% is customary.

Don’t:

  • Don’t bring up politics or religious issues in conversation - these can be sensitive topics.
  • Don’t litter or behave in an anti-social manner - respect the environment and local communities.
  • Don’t assume everyone is a football fan - not everyone is interested in the sport, despite its popularity in the region.

Safety and Health

East is a safe and welcoming place, but it’s important to take general safety precautions and be aware of any health risks. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in East

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure when out and about.
  • Use common sense and caution when travelling alone, especially at night.
  • Be respectful of local customs and avoid behaviour that could cause offence or disruption.

Health in East

  • Make sure you have adequate travel insurance that covers any activities you plan to do.
  • Stay hydrated and wear sun protection during the summer months.
  • Bring any necessary medications and be aware of local health services in case of emergency.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

East is easily accessible by a range of transportation options. Here are some of the main ways to get there:

  • By air: Leeds Bradford Airport is the closest airport to East, with regular flights to and from major UK and European destinations.
  • By train: Leeds train station is well-connected, with regular services to London, Manchester, and other major UK cities.
  • By bus: National Express and Megabus operate services to Leeds from other major UK cities.

How to Get Around in East

East is a walkable and bike-friendly area, with plenty of options for public transport. Here are some of the main ways to get around:

  • On foot: Walking is a great way to explore East’s attractions and get some exercise at the same time.
  • By bike: Leeds has a network of cycle paths and bike lanes, making it easy to get around on two wheels.
  • By bus or train: The local bus and train services are reliable and affordable, with regular routes throughout the area.
