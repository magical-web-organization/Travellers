Are you looking for a charming destination that will take you back in time? Look no further than Haworth, a picturesque village in West Yorkshire, England. Haworth is known for its rich history, stunning countryside, and literary connections, making it the perfect destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and bookworms alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey to discover everything you need to know about this stunning village. From the best places to visit and hiking trails to the food scene, where to stay, and more, we’ve got you covered.

Introduction to Haworth

Nestled in the heart of the West Yorkshire Pennines, Haworth is a village with a rich history, stunning countryside, and literary connections. Haworth is most famously known as the home of the Brontë family, who lived in the Haworth Parsonage from 1820 to 1861. The village is steeped in history, and it’s easy to see why it’s such a popular destination for visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Haworth

Haworth has a lot to offer visitors, whether you’re interested in history, literature, or simply stunning scenery. Here are some of the top places to visit in and around the village:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brontë Parsonage Museum - Visit the home of the world-famous Brontë family and learn about their fascinating lives and works.

  • Haworth Parish Church - A stunning 14th-century church that was a favourite of the Brontë family and features in many of their works.

  • Haworth Old Hall - A historic building dating back to the 16th century that has been beautifully preserved and is now a restaurant and hotel.

Natural Attractions of Haworth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Moors - Haworth is surrounded by stunning moorland, which is perfect for hiking and enjoying the beautiful scenery.

  • Brontë Waterfall - A beautiful waterfall located just outside of Haworth that can be reached by a short hike.

  • Top Withens - A ruined farmhouse that is said to have inspired the setting for Wuthering Heights.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haworth

Haworth is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with stunning moorland and countryside to explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in and around Haworth:

  • The Brontë Way - A 43-mile long-distance trail that takes you from Haworth to Gawthorpe Hall in Lancashire.

  • The Pennine Way - A long-distance trail that runs from Edale in the Peak District to Kirk Yetholm in Scotland.

  • Stanbury and Oldfield Circuit - A 7-mile circular walk that takes you through some of the most stunning countryside around Haworth.

Best Things to Do in Haworth: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the stunning countryside, there are plenty of other things to do in and around Haworth. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Vintage Bus Tours - Take a tour around Haworth on a vintage bus and learn about the history and heritage of the village.

  • Keighley and Worth Valley Railway - A heritage steam railway that runs from Keighley to Oxenhope, passing through Haworth along the way.

  • Haworth Ghost Walks - A spooky tour of the village that will take you to some of its most haunted spots.

Food and Drink Scene of Haworth

Haworth has a lot to offer when it comes to food and drink, with everything from traditional pub fare to contemporary fine dining. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire Pudding - A traditional dish made of batter and usually served with a roast dinner.

  • Parkin - A sweet gingerbread-like cake that is traditionally eaten on Bonfire Night.

  • Fish and Chips - A British classic that is best enjoyed with a side of mushy peas.

Local Beverages

  • Timothy Taylor’s Landlord - A locally brewed ale that is a favourite among residents and visitors alike.

  • Yorkshire Tea - A strong and robust black tea that is a staple in most Yorkshire homes.

  • Yorkshire Rhubarb Gin - A gin made using locally grown rhubarb, best served with tonic and a slice of lemon.

Where to Stay in Haworth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cosy guesthouse, a traditional pub with rooms, or something a bit more luxurious, Haworth has plenty of options when it comes to accommodation. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Old Silent Inn - A historic pub with rooms that is popular among walkers and hikers.

  • The Fleece Inn - A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Haworth.

  • Ashmount Country House - A luxurious guesthouse that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Haworth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from your trip to Haworth, here are some of the best local products to look out for:

  • Brontë Souvenirs - Everything from mugs to T-shirts with Brontë quotes and images.

  • Yorkshire Cheese - There are plenty of independent cheese shops around Haworth, offering a range of delicious local cheeses.

  • Worsted Wool Products - Worsted wool is a type of wool that is spun in a specific way, creating a strong and durable material that is perfect for clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Haworth

Haworth is a village steeped in history and literary connections, and its residents are proud of their heritage. Here are some of the local customs and traditions that you might encounter:

  • The Brontë Festival of Women’s Writing - An annual festival that celebrates the work of women writers and is held in September.

  • Haworth Feast - A traditional event that takes place in September and features a funfair, a parade, and plenty of food and drink.

  • The Keighley and Worth Valley Railway Beer and Music Festival - A popular event that takes place in October and features live music and plenty of local beer.

Best Time to Visit Haworth

Haworth is a beautiful destination all year round, but the best time to visit depends on what you’re looking for. If you want to enjoy the stunning countryside and go hiking or biking, then spring and summer are the best times to visit. If you’re more interested in the local events and festivals, then autumn is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haworth

If you’re planning a trip to Haworth, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Visit the Brontë Parsonage Museum - No trip to Haworth is complete without a visit to this fascinating museum.

  • Take a walk on the moors - The stunning scenery around Haworth is best enjoyed on foot.

  • Try the local food and drink - Haworth has plenty of great options when it comes to food and drink, so make sure you try some of the local specialities.

Don’t:

  • Forget your raincoat - The weather in Haworth can be unpredictable, so make sure you’re prepared for rain.

  • Leave litter in the countryside - Haworth is surrounded by stunning countryside, so please do your part to keep it clean and beautiful.

  • Ignore local customs and traditions - Haworth is a village with a rich history and strong sense of community, so be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

While Haworth is a safe destination, it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Haworth

  • Take care when hiking or biking - The moors around Haworth can be challenging, so make sure you have the appropriate equipment and are prepared for the terrain.

  • Be aware of your surroundings - Like any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Haworth

  • Drink plenty of water - It’s important to stay hydrated, especially when you’re hiking or biking.

  • Wear sunscreen - Even on cloudy days, it’s important to protect your skin from the sun.

  • Watch out for ticks - The moors around Haworth are home to ticks, so make sure you check for them after spending time outdoors.

Getting to Haworth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Haworth is a small village, but it’s well-connected by public transport and is easy to get to from major cities in the UK. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Haworth

  • Leeds Bradford Airport - The closest airport to Haworth, located approximately 17 miles away.

  • Manchester Airport - Located approximately 45 miles away from Haworth.

Bus Stations Near Haworth

  • Keighley Bus Station - Regular bus services connect Haworth to Keighley and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Train - The nearest train station to Haworth is Keighley, which is served by regular services from Leeds.

  • Car - If you’re driving, Haworth is located near several major roads, including the M62, M1, and A1(M).

How to Get Around in Haworth

Haworth is a small village, so walking is the best way to get around. If you’re planning to explore the surrounding countryside, then a bike or car might be helpful.

Where to Go Next from Haworth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Haworth is a beautiful destination that has something to offer everyone, from history and literature enthusiasts to outdoor adventurers and foodies.
