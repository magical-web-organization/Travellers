Introduction to Malham

Malham, located in the heart of the stunning Yorkshire Dales, is a charming and picturesque village that offers visitors plenty of natural beauty, history, and culture. Surrounded by rolling hills, lush green pastures, and babbling brooks, Malham is a haven for hikers, bikers, and outdoor enthusiasts. The village also boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage, from ancient monuments to traditional pubs and breweries.

List of Places to Visit in Malham

Don’t miss out on the following places to visit in Malham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Malham Cove: A natural limestone formation that offers stunning views over the village and surrounding countryside.
  • Gordale Scar: A towering gorge carved out by glacial meltwater, and popular with climbers and walkers.
  • Janet’s Foss: A beautiful waterfall and pool that is steeped in legend and folklore.
  • The Falcon Inn: A traditional pub that dates back to the 16th century and serves up local beers, ciders, and hearty pub grub.
  • St. Michael’s Church: A medieval church with a stunning stained glass window and historic graveyard.
  • Malham Tarn: A glacially-formed lake that offers great opportunities for hiking, horse riding, and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Malham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malham Cove Limestone Pavement: A series of flat limestone slabs that form a unique and interesting landscape.
  • Malham Cove Beck: A picturesque stream that flows through the village and offers great hikes and walks.
  • Malham Tarn Field Centre: A nature reserve and environmental education centre that offers educational programs and experiences.
  • Yorkshire Dales National Park: A vast expanse of countryside that is home to some of the most breathtaking scenery in England.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malham

Malham is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a wealth of trails and routes to suit all abilities. Here are some of the best hikes and bike trails in the area:

  • Malham Tarn Circular Walk: A 6-mile circular trail that takes in stunning lakeside scenery, ancient woodlands, and pastoral scenery.
  • Gordale Scar Walk: A challenging hike that takes you through the dramatic gorge and up onto the valley sides for some panoramic views.
  • Pennine Way: A long-distance hiking trail that passes through the Yorkshire Dales, en route to Scotland.
  • Mountain Biking in the Yorkshire Dales: There are plenty of trails available in the area, from family-friendly routes to gruelling hill climbs.

Best Things to Do in Malham: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Malham has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Take a guided tour of Malham Cove and learn about its interesting geology and history.
  • Visit the traditional pub The Falcon Inn and sample some local beers and ciders.
  • Take a horse riding lesson or trek through the picturesque countryside.
  • Enjoy a hike in the beautiful Yorkshire Dales National Park.
  • Experience a traditional country fair with locally sourced food and crafts.
  • Visit Malham Show, an agricultural show held annually in the village.

Food and Drink Scene of Malham

Malham offers plenty of delicious local cuisine and traditional beverages, so make sure to check out these authentic options:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire Pudding: A traditional and hearty dish made with flour, eggs, and milk served with roast beef and gravy.
  • Cumberland Sausage: A local specialty, made with coarsely ground pork, herbs and spices.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A classic dessert made with dates, butter, and syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Yorkshire Tea: A famous variety of tea that is robust and full-bodied.
  • Timothy Taylor’s Landlord Ale: A locally brewed beer that has won numerous awards for its taste and quality.
  • Aspall Cyder: A refreshing, crisp cider that is perfect for summer days.

Where to Stay in Malham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of lodging options in Malham, catering to all budgets and needs. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • The Lister Arms: A historic coaching inn with rooms that are full of character and charm.
  • Beck Hall: A boutique hotel with luxurious rooms and picturesque gardens.
  • YHA Malham: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable and affordable accommodation.
  • Street Head Farm: A cosy guesthouse that offers beautiful views and home-cooked breakfasts.

What to Buy in Malham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for authentic souvenirs and local products to bring home, check out these options:

  • Yorkshire Cheese: A variety of cheeses that are produced locally, such as Wensleydale and Blue.
  • Local Brews: Take home a bottle of Timothy Taylor’s Landlord Ale or a draught of Aspall Cyder.
  • Wools and Knits: Yorkshire has a long tradition of wool production, so consider buying a sweater or pair of socks made with local wool.

Local Culture and Customs of Malham

Malham has a rich cultural heritage, with a deep connection to the local landscape and environment. The village also prides itself on its friendly and welcoming community, so don’t be surprised if locals strike up a conversation with you in the street!

Best Time to Visit Malham

The best time to visit Malham is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry and the countryside is in full bloom. However, the area can get crowded during peak season, so consider visiting in the spring or autumn for a more peaceful experience. Winter can offer some of the most dramatic and beautiful scenery, but it can also be cold and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malham

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to bear in mind when visiting Malham:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and keep the countryside clean and tidy.
  • Take care when hiking or biking on uneven terrain or near cliffs or high drops.
  • Say hello and be friendly to locals.
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Leave gates open on farmland.
  • Disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Traveling to Malham is generally a safe and healthy experience, but it’s important to keep these tips in mind:

Safety in Malham

  • Be aware of your surroundings and take care when hiking or biking in isolated areas.
  • Stick to designated trails and avoid venturing off the beaten track.
  • Check weather conditions before heading out, as weather can change quickly in the area.
  • Take care when driving on narrow and winding roads.

Health in Malham

  • Be tick-aware when hiking or biking, as Lyme Disease is a risk in the area.
  • Always carry sufficient water and snacks, as there may be limited opportunities to buy food and drinks in remote areas.
  • Consider taking out travel insurance that covers outdoor activities.

Getting to Malham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Malham is located approximately 20 miles north-west of Skipton, in North Yorkshire. Here are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Malham

  • Leeds Bradford Airport: 30 miles away.
  • Manchester Airport: 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Malham

  • Skipton Bus Station: 20 miles away.
  • Settle Bus Station: 10 miles away.

Other Options

  • Car: A car is the most convenient way to travel around the Yorkshire Dales.
  • Train: The nearest train station is in Skipton, with connecting buses to Malham.

How to Get Around in Malham

The best way to get around Malham is on foot, horseback, or bike. Many of the local attractions and hiking trails are easily accessible from the village on foot, and rental bikes or horses are available for those who want to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from Malham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Malham is perfectly situated for exploring the rest of the Yorkshire Dales and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out:

  • Settle: A charming market town that is home to a vibrant arts community and a popular steam train.
  • Skipton: A historic market town that boasts a castle and a bustling high street.
  • The Three Peaks: A challenging hiking trail that takes you up the three highest peaks in the Yorkshire Dales.
  • The Lake District: One of the most beautiful and scenic areas of England, and a popular destination for hiking, boating, and sightseeing.

Conclusion

Malham is a beautiful and charming village that offers visitors plenty of natural beauty, history, and culture. With its picturesque countryside, historic buildings, and friendly community, Malham is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the beauty and diversity of England’s countryside.
