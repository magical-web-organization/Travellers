Introduction to Selby

Selby is a charming market town located in the southern part of North Yorkshire. Situated on the banks of the River Ouse, Selby is known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and welcoming locals. This travel guide is designed to help you make the most of your visit to Selby by providing information on the top attractions, outdoor activities, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Selby

Here are some of the top places to visit in Selby:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Selby Abbey: This 11th-century abbey is a significant historical and religious landmark and features stunning architectural details.
  • Abbey House Museum: Explore the life of a Victorian-era family with interactive exhibits, displays of period clothing and artifacts, and beautifully recreated period rooms.
  • Barlow Common Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is a hidden gem, filled with wildflowers, woodland, and a diversity of wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Selby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Selby Canal: The Selby Canal is a picturesque waterway, ideal for a tranquil canal boat ride or a scenic walk along its towpath.
  • Drax Power Station: This power station offers guided tours showcasing the latest energy innovations, plus incredible views of the nearby wind turbines in action.
  • River Ouse: The River Ouse offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and riverside walks along its serene banks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Selby

Selby offers a variety of outdoor recreation opportunities. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes:

  • Selby Canal Trail: This easy 6-mile trail follows the Selby Canal and offers plenty of scenic vistas.
  • Humber Bridge Country Park: This park offers several hiking and biking trails that take you through the stunning Yorkshire countryside.
  • Barmby to Selby cycle route: This 9-mile route takes you from the village of Barmby Moor to Selby along the River Derwent.

Best Things to Do in Selby: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do in Selby:

  • Selby Superbowl: This bowling alley offers hours of entertainment for all ages.
  • Selby Leisure Centre: The leisure centre offers a swimming pool, gym, and fitness classes.
  • Olympia Beauty Centre: Unwind and relax at this luxurious spa with a range of treatments, including facials, massages, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Selby

Day 1: Visit Selby Abbey and Abbey House Museum Day 2: Walk along the Selby Canal and explore Barlow Common Nature Reserve Day 3: Explore Drax Power Station and nearby wind turbines Day 4: Take a boat ride along the River Ouse Day 5: Cycle along the Barmby to Selby cycle route Day 6: Visit Selby Leisure Centre and Olympia Beauty Centre Day 7: Relax and take in the local culture

Food and Drink Scene of Selby

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buttered Selby Cakes: These delicious local treats are made with lard and rosewater.
  • Yorkshire Puddings: A staple dish in the region, these savory puddings are often served with roast beef.

Local Beverages

  • Black Sheep Ale: This award-winning beer is brewed locally and is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Sloe Gin: This locally produced gin is made with sloe berries and is a popular tipple for gin enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Selby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Selby offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget.

  • The Wisteria Hotel: This charming hotel is housed in a 19th-century Victorian mansion and features elegant rooms and a restaurant serving locally sourced cuisine.
  • The George Inn: This historic inn offers cozy rooms, a pub, and restaurant.
  • Premier Inn Selby: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Selby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Selby Gin: A premium gin that is distilled locally and infused with botanicals inspired by the local area.
  • Selby Cakes: These local treats are a perfect reminder of your trip to Selby.
  • Yorkshire Tea: This beloved tea brand is based in Harrogate, just a short drive from Selby.

Local Culture and Customs of Selby

The people of Selby are welcoming and friendly. The town holds regular events, including the Selby Food and Drink Festival and the Selby Traction Engine Rally, which are great opportunities to engage with the local community.

Best Time to Visit Selby

The best time to visit Selby is from May to September when the weather is warmer and there are more outdoor activities and events available to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Selby

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore the local nature reserves and outdoor recreation areas
  • Engage with the local community and attend a town event

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Be disrespectful to locals or cultural landmarks

Safety and Health

Safety in Selby

Selby is a safe town with low crime rates. It is recommended to take general safety precautions, including keeping valuables out of sight and locking doors and windows at night.

Health in Selby

There are no major health concerns in Selby. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions during outdoor activities, including wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Getting to Selby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Selby

The closest airports to Selby are Leeds Bradford Airport and Doncaster Sheffield Airport, both approximately 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Selby

The town has a bus station that is well-connected to other towns and cities in the region, including Leeds and York.

Other Options

Getting around Selby is easy and convenient by car, taxi, or bicycle. The town is also well-connected by train to other major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Selby

The town is relatively small and easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. There are also taxis and car rental options available for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Selby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • York: Just a 20-minute train journey away, York is a historic walled city with excellent museums and attractions.
  • Leeds: A vibrant city with great shops, restaurants, and nightlife, Leeds is just a short train ride away from Selby.
  • Castle Howard: This stately home and gardens are just a short drive from Selby and are a must-visit for architecture and garden enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Selby is a picturesque market town packed with history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the best the town has to offer, from its cultural landmarks and outdoor recreation opportunities to its food and drink scene and accommodations. Have a great trip!
