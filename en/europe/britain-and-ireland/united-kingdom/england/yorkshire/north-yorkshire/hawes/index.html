Introduction to Hawes

Hawes is a charming market town that is located in the heart of the Yorkshire Dales National Park. The town is known for its breathtaking countryside vistas, natural attractions, and traditional English culture. It is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a look at the best things to do, places to visit, and cultural experiences to be had in Hawes, North Yorkshire.

List of Places to Visit in Hawes

Here’s a list of the top attractions and must-visit places in Hawes:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dales Countryside Museum: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs as it helps visitors to discover the past of the Yorkshire Dales, its people, and landscapes. You will explore the interactive exhibits, historic artefacts, and informative displays presenting the town’s rural heritage and agricultural practices.
  • Gayle Mill: This unique facility showcases a perfectly working water-powered textile mill, which combines history with important local themes. Visitors can take a guided tour, see restored spinning, and weaving machinery or try a workshop in traditional craft.

Natural Attractions of Hawes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buttertubs Pass: This elevated, twisting road will take you through the Yorkshire Dales’ stunning panoramic views and natural landscapes. On the way, there are several spots to stop, including the iconic Buttertubs, a series of big limestone potholes, which locals claim were formed by the Devil tossing his butter.
  • Hardraw Force: This stunning 98ft waterfall is situated in a natural amphitheatre and believed to be the largest single drop waterfall in England. It is a popular spot among filmmakers for its scenic beauty and tranquillity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hawes

Hawes has several hiking and cycling trails that range from gentle strolls to rigorous treks. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Pennine Way: This 268-mile long footpath starts at the Peak District and ends at the Scottish Borders, crossing the Yorkshire Dales and hiking through Hawes. The landscape varies from rugged dales to remote moors and with the highest terrain in England.
  • Circular Walk from Hawes to Hardraw Force: This easy circular trail will lead you from Hawes to Hardraw Force and back, making a total of 3.5 miles. On the walk, magnificent flora and landscapes can be seen.

Best Things to Do in Hawes: Activities and Tours

Here are some exciting things to do and tour options to explore around Hawes:

  • Three Peaks Challenge: This challenge generally covers a 25-mile walk and around 5,000ft of up and downhill climbing. Three of the highest peaks in in Yorkshire, Pen-Y-Ghent, Whernside and Ingleborough, located in close proximity to Hawes are covered in this challenge. Expect panoramic vistas, incredible natural landscapes, and a wonderful sense of achievement after completing the challenge.
  • Wensleydale Creamery Visit: Cheese lovers must visit the Wensleydale Creamery to learn more about the art of cheese-making and to sample some of the best varieties. You can book a guided tour to see the making of cheese and also get to visit the cheese shop to purchase aged Wensleydale cheese and other cheeses handcrafted on site.

Food and Drink Scene of Hawes

Hawes is known for its classic English cuisine, including sweet treats, hearty meals, and locally brewed ales. Here’s what you must try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire Pudding: A savoury baked dish made from batter consisting of eggs, flour and milk, best served with roast beef and onion gravy.
  • Mince Pie: A sweet tray baked dessert featuring a mixture of dried fruit and spices and served with cream or brandy butter.

Local Beverages

  • Theakston’s Ale: A perfect pint of local beer brewed near Hawes with natural ingredients.
  • Yorkshire Tea: A refreshing tea beverage with a deep malty flavour best served with milk and a side of scones.

Where to Stay in Hawes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodations options in Hawes include a variety of budget-friendly and exclusive hotels, hostels, guest houses, and campsites. Here are some best places to consider:

  • Simonstone Hall Hotel: A luxurious countryside retreat, featuring an award-winning restaurant, a cosy bar, ample gardens and terrace spaces to relax.
  • The White Hart Country Inn: A traditional Yorkshire pub and inn offering comfortable rooms for an authentic countryside stay.

What to Buy in Hawes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hawes offers a range of locally crafted products that make great gifts and souvenirs. Here are some popular things you might want to buy:

  • Hardwicke Hall Manor Candles: These hand-poured candles come in several fragrances and can be purchased from the Dales Countryside Museum’s gift store.
  • Wensleydale Cheese: This crumbly and creamy cheese can be found in various shops and markets around Hawes.

Local Culture and Customs of Hawes

Hawes holds tight its traditions, customs, and history while embracing modern-day changes. The area is known for its agricultural heritage, and farming, making a deep impact on the town’s identity. It also has its variations in accents and dialects from various parts of the Yorkshire area.

Best Time to Visit Hawes

The best time to visit Hawes is from late March to September. During this period, the weather is mild, and the countryside is alive with greenery, flowers and the livestock enjoying the fields. The peak tourist season in Hawes is typically between July and August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hawes

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and wildlife by following the regulations listed.
  • Take appropriate precautions while enjoying outdoor activities and tours.
  • Engage with the locals to learn more about the area’s culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter the countryside, keep it clean and sustainable for the locals and the ecosystem.
  • Take shortcuts or climb any private properties, it might cause damage to the trail and local residents’ concerns.
  • Take or feed livestock except farms providing such services with permission.

Safety and Health in Hawes

Safety in Hawes

Hawes is a relatively safe town to visit, but it is essential to follow general safety guidelines. Use suitable cycling and hiking equipment and take appropriate safety precautions.

Health in Hawes

Visitors to Hawes are encouraged to consult their doctor and verify appropriate injections are taken. Visitors must also take necessary precautions for their health at every point in time, including the current public health concerns, such as Covid-19.

Getting to Hawes: Transportation Options, and Logistics

There are several transportation options available to reach Hawes, including air, bus, and taxi services.

Airports Near Hawes

Leeds Bradford International Airport is the closest airport to Hawes, located just 52 miles away, and it offers national and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Hawes

National Express runs coach services to Hawes, with a stop-over at Kirkby Stephen located 12 miles away. Local buses operate from nearby towns to Hawes regularly.

Other Options

Hawes can be accessed from the M6 Motorway, which connects to the A684 road, offering a scenic drive to Hawes via Sedbergh.

How to Get Around in Hawes

Hawes is a small town, which is easy to explore on foot and parking facilities are available. Local bus, bike, and rental car services are also available.
