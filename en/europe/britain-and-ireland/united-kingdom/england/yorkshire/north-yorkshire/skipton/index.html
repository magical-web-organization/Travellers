Introduction to Skipton

Skipton, a historic market town nestled in the heart of the Yorkshire Dales, is one of the oldest settlements in North Yorkshire, dating back to the medieval times. Known as the “Gateway to the Dales,” Skipton is famous for its well-preserved castle, beautiful countryside, and bustling market scene.

Visitors to this charming town can explore the cobbled streets, lined with independent shops, cafes, and restaurants, or take a stroll along the Leeds and Liverpool Canal, which runs through the town. Skipton is an ideal base for those looking to explore the Yorkshire Dales and beyond.

List of Places to Visit in Skipton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Skipton Castle: One of the best-preserved medieval castles in England and a popular tourist attraction. This 900-year-old castle boasts well-kept gardens, a museum, and a tea room.
  • Craven Museum and Art Gallery: This local museum showcases the history and culture of the Craven district, displaying artifacts from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • Skipton Town Hall: Built in 1862, this Neo-Gothic-style building served as the town hall until 1974. Today, it houses a museum and art gallery.

Natural Attractions of Skipton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yorkshire Dales National Park: Skipton is the perfect starting point for exploring the beautiful Yorkshire Dales National Park, with its rolling hills and lush valleys.
  • Bolton Abbey: This picturesque estate, located on the banks of the River Wharfe, is a popular spot for picnics and long walks. Visitors can explore the ruins of the 12th-century Augustinian monastery, or enjoy a leisurely stroll through the woodland paths.
  • Embsay Reservoir: Located just a few miles from Skipton, this tranquil reservoir is a popular spot for fishing, walking, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skipton

  • The Pennine Way: This famous 267-mile-long footpath runs through Skipton, making it an ideal base for those looking to embark on the trail.
  • The Dales High Way: This 90-mile-long walking route starts in Saltaire, West Yorkshire, and ends in Appleby-in-Westmorland, Cumbria, with Skipton as a prominent stop along the way.
  • The Way of the Roses: This 170-mile-long cycling route starts in Morecambe, Lancashire, and ends in Bridlington, East Yorkshire, passing through Skipton along the way.

Best Things to Do in Skipton: Activities and Tours

  • Skipton Market: Held four times a week, Skipton’s market is a lively affair and a great place to pick up local produce, souvenirs, and handicrafts.
  • Canal Boat Trips: Skipton’s canal offers leisurely boat trips through the heart of the town and beyond.
  • Skipton International Puppet Festival: This biennial event attracts puppet enthusiasts from far and wide and features a range of shows, workshops, and exhibitions.

Food and Drink Scene of Skipton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire Pudding: A classic British dish made with flour, eggs, and milk, typically served with roast beef and gravy.
  • Parkin: A traditional gingerbread cake, made with oats and black treacle, and served as a teatime treat.
  • Wensleydale Cheese: A crumbly, creamy cheese, named after the nearby Wensleydale area.

Local Beverages

  • Timothy Taylor’s Beer: A quintessential Yorkshire beer, brewed in nearby Keighley for over 150 years.
  • Wharfedale Brewery Beer: A small, independent brewery, using water from the local river, Wharfe, to create their traditional ales.

Where to Stay in Skipton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Herriots Hotel: A stylish, four-star hotel, located in the heart of Skipton.
  • The Woolly Sheep Inn: A cozy, traditional inn, offering comfortable rooms and a warm welcome.
  • Skipton YHA: A budget-friendly option, offering clean, comfortable dorm rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Skipton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Skipton Market: Pick up souvenirs and local handicrafts at Skipton’s regular market.
  • Wensleydale Cheese: Take home a taste of Yorkshire with a block of Wensleydale cheese.
  • Pendleton Woolen Mills Products: Pendleton Woolen Mills, located in nearby Skipton, sells a variety of woolen products, including blankets, rugs, and jackets.

Local Culture and Customs of Skipton

  • Skipton has a proud agricultural and industrial heritage, with many of its landmarks dating back several centuries.
  • The town is known for its community spirit and traditional events, such as the popular Skipton Sheep Day.
  • Local accents and dialects may vary in different parts of the town and surrounding areas.

Best Time to Visit Skipton

  • Skipton is a year-round destination, with plenty to see and do whatever the season.
  • Summer (June-August) is peak tourist season, with warmer weather and longer daylight hours.
  • Autumn (September-November) offers stunning colors as the leaves change, and quieter crowds.
  • Winter (December-February) is perfect for cozy, festive activities, such as a visit to the Christmas markets.
  • Spring (March-May) is ideal for outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking, as the temperature slowly starts to rise.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skipton

Do:

  • Do try the local delicacies and traditional dishes, such as Yorkshire pudding and Wensleydale cheese.
  • Do take advantage of Skipton’s location and explore the nearby Yorkshire Dales National Park.
  • Do take a stroll along the Leeds and Liverpool Canal and admire the views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t forget to check opening times of attractions, especially during public holidays and seasonal closures.
  • Don’t expect a bustling nightlife scene, as Skipton is a quieter, more rural destination.

Safety and Health

Safety in Skipton

  • Skipton is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions.
  • Take care when hiking in the hills and heed all warning signs and advice given by the park authorities.
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times and avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuable items.

Health in Skipton

  • Skipton has several pharmacies and medical facilities, although it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication with you.
  • Be aware of ticks when hiking or walking in the countryside, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Skipton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Skipton

  • Leeds Bradford International Airport (LBA): Located around 19 miles from Skipton, LBA serves domestic and international flights.
  • Manchester Airport (MAN): Located around 56 miles from Skipton, MAN is a major international airport, offering direct flights to destinations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Skipton

  • Skipton Bus Station: Located in the heart of Skipton town center, this bus station offers regular services to destinations across the region.
  • Keighley Bus Station: Located around 6 miles from Skipton, Keighley Bus Station offers bus services to destinations including Leeds, Bradford, and Halifax.

Other Options

  • Train: Skipton has a train station, with regular services to Leeds, Bradford, and other nearby destinations.
  • Car: Skipton is easily accessible by car, with good motorway connections and parking available in and around the town.

How to Get Around in Skipton

  • Walking: Skipton is a compact town and can easily be explored on foot.
  • Cycling: Cycling is a great way to explore the town and nearby countryside, and there are several cycle hire options available in and around Skipton.
  • Car: If you’re planning on exploring the wider area, hiring a car is a good option. Parking is available at various locations around Skipton.
  • Bus: There is a regular bus service connecting Skipton with nearby towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from Skipton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Haworth: This quaint village, located around 10 miles from Skipton, is famous for its association with the Bronte sisters and offers a range of literary attractions, including the Bronte Parsonage Museum.
  • Settle: This picturesque market town, located around 15 miles from Skipton, offers stunning walks in the nearby Yorkshire Dales National Park and is home to the popular Settle-Carlisle railway.
  • Leeds: This vibrant city, located around 25 miles from Skipton, offers a range of attractions, including museums, galleries, shops, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Skipton, with its rich history, stunning countryside, and friendly locals, is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of North Yorkshire. Whether you’re looking to explore the nearby Yorkshire Dales or simply relax in the town’s picturesque surroundings, Skipton has something to offer everyone. From cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to traditional food and drink and unique souvenirs, Skipton is truly a gem of the region.
