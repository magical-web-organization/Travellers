Introduction to Malton

Located in North Yorkshire, Malton is a charming market town that boasts a rich history and picturesque landscapes. Known for its food culture and relaxing atmosphere, it’s the perfect destination for travelers looking for a peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Malton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castle Howard - One of the grandest stately homes in Yorkshire, Castle Howard features impressive architecture and stunning interiors. It also offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Malton Museum - Discover the local history of Malton from prehistoric times to the present day through a variety of exhibits at this quaint museum.
  • Norton Conyers - Explore this beautiful 17th-century country house, which is said to have inspired Charlotte Bronte’s novel, “Jane Eyre”.

Natural Attractions of Malton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dalby Forest - Go on an outdoor adventure in Dalby Forest and explore its walking and cycling trails. It also has several play areas, picnic spots, and a visitor center for family-friendly fun.
  • Howardian Hills AONB - This area of outstanding natural beauty, which includes Castle Howard, offers breathtaking views and peaceful walking trails.
  • River Derwent - Take a leisurely stroll along the River Derwent and enjoy the serene views and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malton

  • Wilderness Trail - This 16km trail is perfect for hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Yorkshire Wolds Way National Trail - For avid hikers, this trail runs for 79 miles from the Humber Bridge to the seaside town of Filey.
  • Sustrans Route 165 - This scenic trail runs from Scarborough to Malton, passing through picturesque countryside, woods, and villages.

Best Things to Do in Malton: Activities and Tours

  • Malton Cookery School - Take a culinary course and learn to cook like a pro at this renowned school.
  • Malton Monthly Food Market - Taste local produce and enjoy a buzzing atmosphere at this monthly market.
  • Ryedale Vineyards - Go on a tour and tasting at this award-winning vineyard, which produces sparkling wine using traditional champagne methods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malton

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Castle Howard and its gardens
Day 2 Explore Malton Town Centre and its quaint shops and cafes
Day 3 Go on a bike ride in Dalby Forest
Day 4 Visit Norton Conyers and see the architecture and artwork
Day 5 Take a cooking class at the Malton Cookery School
Day 6 Visit Ryedale Vineyards for a tasting
Day 7 Relax by the River Derwent or explore the Howardian Hills

Food and Drink Scene of Malton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire Pudding - A classic English dish that you can’t miss. Try it with roast beef and gravy for the full experience.
  • Eton Mess - A delicious dessert made with whipped cream, meringue, and fresh berries.
  • Fish and Chips - This quintessential British dish is a must-try. Be sure to try it with mushy peas and tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Yorkshire Tea - This famous tea brand is a must-try for tea lovers.
  • Yorkshire Ale - Enjoy a pint of ale at one of Malton’s local pubs. Try the Black Sheep Ale for a classic taste of Yorkshire.

Where to Stay in Malton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Talbot Hotel - This historic hotel is located in the heart of Malton and offers luxurious rooms and fine dining.
  • The New Globe Inn - This family-run inn features cozy rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Kirkham House B&B - This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building and offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Malton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Malton Relish - This tangy relish is made from locally sourced ingredients and is a must-try for foodies.
  • Local Honey - Get a taste of Yorkshire’s natural sweetness with some honey from Malton.
  • Handmade Crafts - Browse the local markets and shops for handmade pottery, textiles, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Malton

Malton is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are encouraged to explore its rich history and vibrant food culture. It’s important to respect local customs and traditions, especially in religious places.

Best Time to Visit Malton

The best time to visit Malton is from May to September when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, the town is also beautiful in the autumn and winter months, when the leaves change colors and the Christmas markets are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malton

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Visit the local markets and fairs.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment.
  • Be disrespectful in religious places.
  • Be loud and disruptive late at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malton

Malton is a safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions like locking their doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Malton

Malton has several medical centers and pharmacies for any health needs. Visitors should also be aware of ticks in the countryside and take precautions against Lyme disease.

Getting to Malton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malton

  • Leeds Bradford Airport - 50 miles from Malton
  • Doncaster Sheffield Airport - 53 miles from Malton
  • Manchester Airport - 85 miles from Malton

Bus Stations Near Malton

  • Malton Bus Station
  • York Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train - Malton has a train station with connections to major cities like York, Newcastle, and Manchester.
  • Car - Visitors can also drive to Malton and park in one of several public car parks.

How to Get Around in Malton

Visitors can easily explore Malton on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer journeys or for those with mobility issues.

Where to Go Next from Malton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • York - Just 20 miles away, the historic city of York is a must-visit destination.
  • Whitby - This beautiful coastal town is just an hour’s drive from Malton and offers stunning views and delicious seafood.
  • North York Moors National Park - Explore the breathtaking landscapes of the North York Moors, just a short distance from Malton.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful countryside retreat or a bustling foodie destination, Malton has something for everyone. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture, it’s a must-visit destination in Yorkshire.
