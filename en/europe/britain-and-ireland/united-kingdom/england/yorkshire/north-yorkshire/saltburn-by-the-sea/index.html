Discovering Saltburn-by-the-Sea: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Located in North Yorkshire in England, Saltburn-by-the-Sea is a perfect vacation destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and breathtaking views. This coastal town has something for everyone, from charming historic sites to adrenaline-pumping outdoor activities.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the best attractions, outdoor adventures, must-try foods, and cultural customs of Saltburn-by-the-Sea. So pack your bags and get ready for the adventure of your lifetime!

List of Places to Visit in Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saltburn Cliff Lift: This historic Victorian cliff lift takes you from the town to the beach in style, providing excellent views of the coastline.
  • Saltburn Pier: Explore the pier, which is over 150 years old and has been restored to its former glory. Go fishing, enjoy the views, or try your hand at some traditional seaside arcade games.
  • Saltburn Valley Woodland Centre: A perfect place for nature lovers, the Woodland Centre offers activities such as bird watching and nature trails for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Saltburn-by-the-Sea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saltburn Beach: Take a stroll along the beach and enjoy excellent views of the coast, including the stunning Huntcliff. You can also try out paddleboarding, surfing, or even take a dip in the sea.
  • Saltburn Gill: Get closer to nature by exploring this small valley with a stream that feeds into the sea, and spotting various wildlife species.
  • Guisborough Forest and Walkway: This woodland near Saltburn is a great place for a family walk with plenty of picnic areas, cycle tracks and horse-riding tracks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Saltburn-by-the-Sea offers many scenic hiking and biking tracks that take visitors through the picturesque natural landscapes and along cliff tops, offering breathtaking views of the sea. Some popular routes include:

  • Cleveland Way: A 110-mile National Trail takes you along the stunning North Yorkshire and Cleveland heritage coast and the North York Moors.
  • Saltburn Valley Trail: A 4-mile walking or cycling trail that takes you through the beautiful valley of Saltburn-by-the-Sea, overlooking the cliffs and coastline.
  • Roseberry Topping hike: A challenging yet rewarding hike offering picturesque views of the countryside around Saltburn.

Best Things to Do in Saltburn-by-the-Sea: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing at Saltburn: Saltburn-by-the-Sea is a popular surfing destination with swell patterns that create perfect waves for surfers of all levels. You can also try out paddleboarding and kayaking.
  • Sea fishing: Saltburn Pier is a popular destination for sea fishing, where you can try your luck and catch different species of fish.
  • Saltburn Farmers’ Market: Every second Saturday of the month, visitors can explore the wide range of fresh produce, artisan foods, and handmade crafts at the market.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Day 1: Appreciate the stunning views from the Saltburn Cliff Lift, and explore the historic Saltburn Pier. Take a walk along the beach and enjoy a relaxing afternoon.

Day 2: Go hiking on the Cleveland Way, which offers an excellent combination of coastal and countryside views. In the evening, take a stroll on the pier for some traditional seaside entertainment.

Day 3: Rent a bike and explore the picturesque countryside surrounding Saltburn. Cycles can take you on routes that include Guisborough forest and walkway.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the picturesque Saltburn Valley Woodland Centre and enjoy a picnic with family.

Day 5: Try your hand at surfing or paddleboarding in the salty waters of the sea. The perfect way to get the blood pumping and enjoy the vibrant coastal scenery.

Day 6: Explore the local craftsmanship and visit the monthly Farmers’ Market and local craft market.

Day 7: Enjoy the scenic views of Saltburn whilst taking a gentle stroll on the pier eating some traditional fish and chips.

Food and Drink Scene of Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Grab a fish and chips takeaway from the town’s Fishermen’s cabins for an authentic seaside experience.
  • Yorkshire Pudding: Try traditional Yorkshire pudding stuffed with roast beef, gravy, and vegetables at one of the town’s many cafes.
  • Gingerbread: Saltburn-by-the-Sea is famous for its delicious sweet gingerbread made using a secret recipe dating back to 1880 from JB Hall, baker and founder Hall’s family bakery.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Saltburn has a wealth of great pubs serving high-quality real ale from some of the UK’s best microbreweries.
  • Yorkshire Tea: Yorkshire Tea is a staple drink in Saltburn and the rest of the county and is a perfect brew to warm you after a chilly hike.

Where to Stay in Saltburn-by-the-Sea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is an extensive range of accommodation in Saltburn for family, friends, or individuals. The high-quality, affordable options include:

  • The Spa Hotel: A luxurious and pet-friendly hotel located just a few meters away from Saltburn beach.
  • Zetland Hotel: A four-star Victorian townhouse with 57 modern rooms, offering sea views.
  • Hunters Moon Guesthouse: A family-friendly bed and breakfast offering personalized hospitality and cozy accommodation options for travellers on a budget.

What to Buy in Saltburn-by-the-Sea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saltburn Gingerbread: Pack some of this delicious local treat into your suitcase, so you can relive the taste of Saltburn back home.
  • Saltburn Tea Towels: A great souvenir to remind you of the stunning natural beauty of Saltburn.
  • Saltburn artwork: There are several museums and independent galleries dotted around Saltburn specifically dedicated to showcasing local artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Saltburn is famous for its vibrant cultural events, such as the annual Saltburn Festival of Folk Music, Dance and Song held in August. Saltburn has a strong sense of community spirit, and visitors can expect friendly local hospitality and a warm welcome.

Best Time to Visit Saltburn-by-the-Sea

The best time to visit Saltburn-by-the-Sea is between May to September, when the temperatures are warmer, making outdoor adventures and beach activities more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor adventure activities, such as surfing and paddleboarding.
  • Do take a hike on the Cleveland Way for stunning landscapes and sea views.
  • Do experience local homemade treats like gingerbread.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately when taking part in outdoor activity or hiking.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural landscape. The town relies heavily on tourism based on the natural beauty of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saltburn-by-the-Sea

It’s essential to take caution when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and surfing, and to be aware of your surroundings and remain safe at all times.

Health in Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Plenty of drinking water is advisable as Saltburn-by-the-Sea is a reasonably active place for outdoor activities, and the local supermarkets or cafes provide bottled water. Remember to pack any necessary medication in case of emergencies.

Getting to Saltburn-by-the-Sea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saltburn-by-the-Sea is a small town, and so the transport options are limited. The best way to get around is by car. Some alternative options include:

Airports Near Saltburn-by-the-Sea

  • Durham Tees Valley Airport: Approximately a 30-minute drive from Saltburn-by-the-Sea
  • Newcastle International Airport: Approximately an hour’s drive from Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Bus Stations Near Saltburn-by-the-Sea

  • Saltburn Railway Station: Providing access to central saltburn as well as providing links to Middlesbrough and surrounding towns.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis can be booked if travelling in or out of Saltburn-by-the-Sea.
  • Car Hire: Car hire can be arranged in the region as well so you can see the rest of North Yorkshire on your trip!

How to Get Around in Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Saltburn-by-the-Sea is a small enough town that travellers can walk or hire a bicycle to get around. There are plenty of places to park should you travel by cars such as the visitors car park located on Marine Parade.

Where to Go Next from Saltburn-by-the-Sea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Whitby: A picturesque town on the North Yorkshire coast with historic landmarks like Whitby Abbey.

  • James Cook Museum, Great Ayton: Discover the life and work of the famous explorer James Cook.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Saltburn-by-the-Sea is an ideal destination for those seeking a combination of outdoor adventure, culture, and scenic views. With breathtaking landscapes, delicious food, and warm hospitality, it is no surprise Saltburn-by-the-Sea is regarded as a must-visit town for tourists in the North of England.
