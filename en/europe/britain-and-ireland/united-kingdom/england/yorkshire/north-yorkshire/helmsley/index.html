Introduction to Helmsley

Helmsley is a charming town located in the Ryedale district of North Yorkshire, England. With its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and friendly locals, Helmsley has become a popular destination for tourists. Whether you are looking to explore local museums, hike through the nearby National Park, or simply relax in a cozy pub, Helmsley has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Helmsley

There are plenty of attractions and things to do in Helmsley. Here are some of the must-visit spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Helmsley Castle: A 12th-century castle that has survived numerous sieges and attacks throughout history.
  • Helmsley Walled Garden: A beautiful garden that was originally built in 1758.
  • Ryedale Folk Museum: A living museum that provides visitors with a glimpse into rural life in North Yorkshire during the 19th and early 20th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Helmsley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • North York Moors National Park: Located just a few miles from Helmsley, this expansive park offers hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities.
  • Rievaulx Abbey: A ruined abbey located in a peaceful valley just outside of Helmsley.
  • Black Hambleton: A prominent hill in the North York Moors that offers breathtaking views from its summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Helmsley

Helmsley is a great base for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are plenty of trails in the nearby North York Moors National Park, including:

  • Cleveland Way National Trail: A 109-mile trail that takes hikers through the North York Moors and along the coastline of the North Sea.
  • Sutton Bank: A hill that provides a challenging yet rewarding biking experience, with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Helmsley to Rievaulx Abbey: A gentle hike that takes visitors along a picturesque country lane and through fields and woods.

Best Things to Do in Helmsley: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Helmsley offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • Helmsley Castle Tour: A guided tour that takes visitors through the castle’s history and architecture.
  • Helmsley Brewery Tour: A tour that offers visitors a chance to see how local beer is made and to taste some of the brews.
  • Helmsley Arts Centre: A venue for live music, theater performances, and other cultural events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Helmsley

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Helmsley:

Day Activities
1 Arrival and check-in. Explore Helmsley town center and visit Helmsley Castle.
2 Take a guided tour of Helmsley Brewery and sample some of the beers.
3 Spend the day hiking in the North York Moors National Park, with a picnic lunch on the hills.
4 Visit Ryedale Folk Museum and Helmsley Walled Garden.
5 Take a day trip to Whitby, a picturesque seaside town located about an hour’s drive from Helmsley.
6 Explore the local area, visiting Rievaulx Abbey and Black Hambleton.
7 Departure day. Stop by Helmsley Arts Centre for a morning coffee before checking out.

Food and Drink Scene of Helmsley

Helmsley offers a variety of dining options for visitors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorkshire Pudding: A savory dish made from batter and traditionally served with roast beef.
  • Fish and Chips: A classic British dish that consists of deep-fried fish and potato chips.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A sweet and sticky dessert made from dates and topped with caramel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Helmsley has its own brewery, which produces a variety of traditional British ales.
  • Gin: Yorkshire is home to a variety of small-batch gin distilleries, several of which can be visited on tours.

Where to Stay in Helmsley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Helmsley offers a variety of accommodations for visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • The Black Swan: A historic hotel located in the heart of Helmsley, with individually designed rooms and a renowned restaurant.
  • The Feversham Arms Hotel and Verbena Spa: A luxurious spa hotel located in a charming building and surrounded by greenery.
  • Two Hoots Campsite: A pet-friendly camping site located a few miles outside of Helmsley, surrounded by natural beauty.

What to Buy in Helmsley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Helmsley is known for its local produce and handmade crafts. Here are some of the best things to buy as souvenirs:

  • Yorkshire cheese: High-quality cheese that is produced in small batches by local farms.
  • Shepherd’s crooks: Traditional English walking sticks that are made by hand and used by shepherds to herd their sheep.
  • Wensleydale wool: High-quality wool that is spun and made into a variety of clothing and home goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Helmsley

North Yorkshire has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors to Helmsley can experience this by exploring the town’s historical and cultural sites. The people of Helmsley are known for their warmth and hospitality, and visitors are always made to feel welcome.

Best Time to Visit Helmsley

Helmsley is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit is between April and September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the National Park offers stunning views during all four seasons, so it is possible to enjoy the area’s natural beauty throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Helmsley

Do:

  • Do explore the local area by foot or bike.
  • Do try the local beer and cuisine.
  • Do visit the historical and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for hiking and outdoor adventures.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with the locals - they are friendly and welcoming!

Safety and Health

Safety in Helmsley

Helmsley is a safe place to visit, with very low crime rates. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions and lock their doors at night. It is also important to be aware of potential hazards when hiking or exploring the natural areas, such as loose rocks or steep drops.

Health in Helmsley

Helmsley has a variety of healthcare facilities, including pharmacies and a doctor’s office. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling, and should also be aware of any potential health risks associated with outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

Getting to Helmsley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Helmsley is located about 25 miles from York and can be easily reached by car, bus, or train.

Airports Near Helmsley

  • Leeds Bradford Airport: Located about an hour and a half’s drive from Helmsley.
  • Newcastle International Airport: Located about two hours’ drive from Helmsley.

Bus Stations Near Helmsley

  • Malton Bus Station: Located about 20 minutes’ drive from Helmsley.
  • York Bus Station: Located about 40 minutes’ drive from Helmsley.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station is located in Malton, about 20 minutes’ drive from Helmsley.
  • Car: Helmsley can be reached by car from major motorways, including the A1 and the M1.

How to Get Around in Helmsley

Helmsley is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, visitors may want to rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding National Park and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Helmsley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Helmsley is an excellent base for exploring North Yorkshire and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • York: A historic city with beautiful architecture and plenty of cultural attractions.
  • Whitby: A charming seaside town with picturesque views and excellent seafood.
  • Scarborough: A family-friendly vacation spot with plenty of attractions and activities.

Conclusion

Helmsley is a unique and charming town with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you are interested in exploring the natural beauty of the North York Moors, immersing yourself in the town’s rich history and culture, or simply relaxing in a cozy pub, Helmsley is the perfect destination for a memorable vacation. Plan your trip with this guide and discover all that this beautiful town has to offer.
