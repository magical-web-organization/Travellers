A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Whitby, England

Located in North Yorkshire, England, Whitby is a charming coastal town known for its historic abbey, quaint streets, and beautiful coastline. Whether you’re looking to hike through the countryside, explore fascinating museums, or simply relax and enjoy the seaside, you’ll find plenty to do in Whitby. This comprehensive guide will provide everything you need to know for an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Whitby

Whitby has a range of attractions that appeal to all types of travelers. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Whitby has a rich history and a number of fascinating historical attractions. Some of the must-see sights in town include:

  • Whitby Abbey: This stunning ruined abbey sits high on a cliff overlooking the town and the sea. It’s a must-visit for anyone traveling to Whitby.
  • Whitby Museum: This small but informative museum is packed full of exhibits on the town’s history, from its early days through to the modern era.
  • Captain Cook Memorial Museum: This museum is dedicated to Captain James Cook, the famous explorer who began his voyages from Whitby in the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Whitby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whitby is located in a beautiful part of the country, with plenty of hills, coastline, and countryside to explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Whitby Beach: This beautiful sandy beach stretches for miles along the coast and is perfect for relaxing or swimming.
  • Whitby Harbour: This picturesque harbor is home to a variety of fishing boats and is surrounded by a range of shops and restaurants.
  • North York Moors National Park: This expansive parkland is full of hiking and biking trails, as well as small towns and villages to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whitby

Whitby and the surrounding countryside offer a range of hiking and cycling trails, from gentle walks along the coast to challenging routes through the nearby North York Moors. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Cleveland Way: This long-distance trail runs 109 miles along the coast from Helmsley to Filey and passes through Whitby.
  • The Esk Valley Walk: This 37-mile trail follows the River Esk from the coast to the Moors and provides stunning views of the area.
  • Whitby Abbey and the 199 Steps: This short walk takes you up to the iconic abbey and offers panoramic views of the town and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Whitby: Activities and Tours

Whitby offers many activities and tours to make your trip even more memorable. Here are some of the most popular things to do in the area:

  • Explore the town’s famous fish and chip shops, including The Magpie Cafe and Trenchers.
  • Take a boat trip along the coast, or even try your hand at some fishing.
  • Visit the Whitby Brewery and sample some locally brewed beers, including the famous Black Pearl stout.
  • Walk along the seafront promenade, taking in the beautiful views and scenery.
  • Join a ghost tour of the town, exploring the spookiest locations and listening to creepy stories.

Week-Long Itinerary for Whitby

If you have a week to spare, here’s an ideal itinerary for exploring Whitby and its surrounding area:

Day Activity
1 Explore the town, including the Abbey, Museum and Harbour
2 Take a boat trip along the coast and go fishing
3 Hike along the Cleveland Way or the Esk Valley
4 Visit the Captain Cook Museum and the nearby village of Staithes
5 Relax on Whitby Beach and try some local seafood
6 Join a ghost tour of the town and its most haunted locations
7 Spend the day in the North York Moors National Park, hiking or cycling

Food and Drink Scene of Whitby

Whitby has a variety of local dishes and drinks that you won’t want to miss. Here are some of the top must-tries:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: The town is famous for its fish and chips, with many restaurants and cafes offering the dish.
  • Yorkshire Pudding: A classic English dish, often served with roast beef or other meats.
  • Whitby Scampi: This local delicacy uses locally caught monkfish, coated in breadcrumbs and deep-fried.

Local Beverages

  • Black Pearl Stout: This famous local beer is brewed at the Whitby Brewery and is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.
  • Yorkshire Tea: A locally blended tea that’s perfect for a refreshing break.
  • Yorkshire Gin: A range of locally produced gin, including Masons, Whitby Gin and Harrogate Tipple.

Where to Stay in Whitby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whitby has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay in town:

  • The White Horse and Griffin: A boutique hotel in the heart of town, offering period features and modern amenities.
  • YHA Whitby: A friendly and affordable youth hostel in a stunning cliff-top location.
  • Chiltern Guest House: A family-run guesthouse offering comfortable rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Whitby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whitby is known for its local crafts and souvenirs. Here are some of the top things to buy in town:

  • Jet Jewelry: Whitby is famous for its jet, a black gemstone used for jewelry-making.
  • Whitby Abbey Model: A replica of the historic abbey, perfect for bringing home as a souvenir.
  • Fossil Hunter Kits: A fun and educational activity for children, who can go hunting for local fossils on the beach.

Local Culture and Customs of Whitby

Whitby has a rich cultural heritage and many local customs that visitors should be aware of. Some of the most notable include:

  • The town is famous for its biannual Goth festival, which takes place in April and October.
  • Folk Music is popular in the area, with the annual Whitby Folk Week taking place in August.
  • The town also has a strong maritime tradition, with many local shipbuilding and fishing industries.

Best Time to Visit Whitby

The best time to visit Whitby is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Alternatively, visit during the shoulder seasons of April to May or September to October for milder weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whitby

There are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Whitby. Here are some key do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local fish and chips - they’re some of the best in the country.
  • Visit the historic abbey and museum to learn about the town’s fascinating history.
  • Enjoy a boat trip along the coast or a walk along the beach.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for the moors and hiking trails.
  • Don’t miss out on the local beer - it’s some of the best in the country.
  • Don’t forget to take in the beautiful views and scenery.

Safety and Health

Whitby is a safe town to visit, with low levels of crime. However, as with any tourist destination, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and keep their belongings secure. In terms of health, visitors should ensure that they have up-to-date travel insurance, and should be aware of ticks, which are common in the nearby countryside and can transmit Lyme Disease.

Getting to Whitby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whitby is located on the Yorkshire coast and is easily accessible by road or rail. Here are some of the key transport options:

Airports Near Whitby

  • Durham Tees Valley Airport: Located 43 miles from Whitby, this small regional airport offers flights to UK and European destinations.
  • Leeds Bradford Airport: Located 55 miles from Whitby, this larger airport offers flights across the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Whitby

  • The town has a number of bus stops throughout, with frequent local services, as well as services to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

  • Train: The town’s railway station offers services to Middlesbrough and beyond, as well as connections to Leeds and London.
  • Car: Whitby is easily accessible by car, with a number of major roads leading to the town.

How to Get Around in Whitby

Whitby is a small town that is easily navigable on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding area, it’s worth considering renting a car or taking a local bus or train.

Where to Go Next from Whitby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore the local area further, there are a number of nearby towns and villages that are worth visiting, including:

  • Robin Hood’s Bay: A picturesque seaside village with beautiful views and stunning walks along the coast.
  • Scarborough: Another popular seaside town, with attractions such as the Sea Life Centre and the Scarborough Castle.
  • Staithes: A small fishing village with a charming harbor and cobbled streets.

Conclusion

Whitby is a charming town that offers a range of attractions for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re interested in history, hiking, or simply relaxing on the beach, Whitby is a destination that won’t disappoint.
