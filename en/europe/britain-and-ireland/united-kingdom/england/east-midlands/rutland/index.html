Introduction to Rutland

Rutland is a county located in East Midlands of England, United Kingdom. It’s a landlocked area and is one of the smallest counties in England. However, it makes up for its small size with its natural beauty, picturesque villages, historic landmarks, and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Rutland

Rutland offers visitors a plethora of places to explore. From culturally important attractions to natural wonders, there is something for everyone. Here are some must-visit destinations in Rutland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oakham Castle: This 12th-century castle is one of the most iconic landmarks in Rutland. It features a unique horseshoe-shaped Great Hall, which was constructed in the 12th century.
  • Rutland County Museum: Located in Oakham, this museum houses fascinating exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Rutland.
  • Lyddington Bede House: This 14th-century building was previously a former medieval monastic complex and is now a tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Rutland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rutland Water: This artificial lake is one of the most popular destinations for tourists in Rutland. It offers a wide range of outdoor activities, such as bird watching, fishing, and watersports.
  • Rutland Water Nature Reserve: This reserve houses numerous species of birds and animals, including the endangered Ospreys. It’s a perfect place for a peaceful walk or a picnic.
  • Normanton Church: Located on the south shore of Rutland Water, this ancient church dates back to the 14th century and is now a popular venue for wedding ceremonies and concerts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rutland

Rutland offers numerous hiking and biking trails, which are perfect for those looking to explore the county’s natural beauty. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Rutland:

  • Rutland Water Circular Route: This 23-mile circular route takes you around Rutland Water, showcasing some of the best views of the lake.
  • Rutland Round: This 65-mile trail takes you through the Rutland countryside, passing through several picturesque villages and historic sites.
  • Hereward Way: This 110-mile walking trail passes through Rutland and takes you on a journey through some of the most scenic parts of England.

Best Things to Do in Rutland: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the popular attractions and the nature reserves, there are plenty of things to do in Rutland. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Rutland to include in your itinerary:

  • Rutland Falconry and Owl Centre: Get up close and personal with some of the most impressive birds of prey in the world.
  • Artisan Cheese Fair: Held annually at Melton Mowbray, this food festival is a must-visit for all food lovers.
  • Rutland Railway Museum: Explore the history of the railway industry in Rutland and get a closer look at some of the vintage trains on display.

Food and Drink Scene of Rutland

No trip to Rutland is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Rutland:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Melton Mowbray Pork Pie: This savory pie is made with pork and a unique blend of spices, and it’s a staple of Rutland cuisine.
  • Rutland Pippin: This apple originated in Rutland and is still grown in the county today. It’s a must-try for apple lovers.
  • Stilton Cheese: This blue cheese originated in Leicestershire and is still produced in the neighboring counties, including Rutland.

Local Beverages

  • Rutland Gin: Made with botanicals sourced from Rutland, this gin is a must-try for all gin lovers.
  • Rutland Water: This bottled water comes from the large reservoir of Rutland Water, located in the county.

Rutland has numerous options for accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Rutland:
