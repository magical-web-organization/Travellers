Introduction to Rutland Water

Nestled in the heart of England, Rutland Water is an impressive man-made reservoir that stretches over 3,100 acres and is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and historical significance. Located in East Midlands, this breathtaking destination has everything from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, making it an ideal location for family vacations, romantic getaways, or solo trips.

List of Places to Visit in Rutland Water

When planning a visit to Rutland Water, there is so much to explore and experience. Here is a list of the must-see attractions, categorized by their cultural and natural significance.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Rutland County Museum - Discover the rich history and culture of the region by visiting the Rutland County Museum’s collection of art, archaeology, and local heritage displays.

  2. Oakham Castle - Established in the 12th century, Oakham Castle is an architectural gem that boasts a rich heritage. It is famously known for its horseshoe collection, which has over 200 horseshoes on display.

  3. Lyddington Bede House - This fortified manor house was built in the 14th century and was once the home of the Master of the Hospital of St. John. It is now an English Heritage site and offers a glimpse into medieval life.

Natural Attractions of Rutland Water: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Rutland Water Nature Reserve - Spanning over 1,000 acres, the nature reserve surrounding Rutland Water is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore its trails, watch wildlife, and enjoy stunning vistas.

  2. Normanton Church - This iconic church is situated on the water’s edge and is a popular spot for photographers and nature enthusiasts. Its stunning Gothic architecture makes for an impressive sight, especially at sunrise and sunset.

  3. Rutland Water Beach - With its sandy shores and crystal-clear waters, Rutland Water Beach is the perfect spot for a family picnic or a relaxing day out. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and even windsurfing in the warmer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rutland Water

Rutland Water has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the reservoir and its surroundings. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. The Rutland Water Trail - This 23-mile trail is perfect for walkers, runners, and cyclists. It circles the entire reservoir and offers stunning views of the water, woodlands, and fields.

  2. Hambleton Peninsula Trail - A shorter route that takes visitors on a circular walk around the peninsula. The trail offers panoramic views of the water and its birdlife.

  3. Normanton to Whitwell Trail - A scenic four-mile trail that takes visitors through the tranquil villages of Normanton and Whitwell. The route also passes by the impressive Normanton Church.

Best Things to Do in Rutland Water: Activities and Tours

  1. Bird-watching - With over 25,000 waterfowl visiting the reservoir each year, Rutland Water is a bird-watcher’s paradise. Visitors can take guided tours to learn more about the resident and migratory bird species.

  2. Water Sports - From sailing and windsurfing to kayaking and paddleboarding, Rutland Water offers a range of water sports for visitors to enjoy. There are also professional courses available for those who wish to learn or improve their skills.

  3. Fishing - Rutland Water is renowned for its trout fishing, making it a popular spot for anglers. Visitors can take part in guided tours or bring their own equipment to fish in the reservoir.

Food and Drink Scene of Rutland Water

When it comes to food and drink, Rutland Water has cuisine that is influenced by both British and European traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Melton Mowbray Pork Pie - A savory pie made from fresh pork and hot water crust pastry that is a staple of British cuisine.

  2. Rutland Pheasant - A local specialty made from the lean meat of wild game birds that are found in the region.

  3. Bakewell Tart - A British dessert that originated in the town of Bakewell, Derbyshire. It is made from pastry, jam, and almond filling.

Local Beverages

  1. Rutland Water Ale - A traditional ale that is brewed locally using the water from Rutland Water. It has a smooth taste and is perfect for beer enthusiasts.

  2. Belvoir Fruit Farms - A local producer of award-winning soft drinks and cordials, made using fresh fruits and ingredients from the region.
