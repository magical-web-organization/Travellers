Introduction to East Midlands

East Midlands is a region in England that encompasses six counties: Derbyshire, Leicestershire, Lincolnshire, Northamptonshire, Nottinghamshire, and Rutland. It is home to a rich history, diverse landscapes, vibrant cities, and charming towns.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or a foodie, the East Midlands region has something to offer for all types of travelers. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a deeper look at the top things to do, places to visit, hiking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more in East Midlands.

List of Places to Visit in East Midlands

There are plenty of attractions to explore in East Midlands. Here’s a list of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nottingham Castle Museum and Art Gallery

Located in the heart of Nottingham, the Nottingham Castle Museum and Art Gallery is a must-visit attraction. It offers a glimpse into the city’s history, including the legend of Robin Hood. You can also explore the art gallery that features works by world-renowned artists such as David Hockney and Lucien Freud.

Lincoln Cathedral

The Lincoln Cathedral is a stunning piece of architecture, built in the medieval period, standing tall for over 900 years. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in Lincolnshire and attracts thousands of visitors every year. The cathedral features intricate stone carvings, stained glass windows, and a vast interior that showcases the best of gothic architecture.

Derby Museum and Art Gallery

Derby Museum and Art Gallery is home to a varied collection of art, artifacts, and natural history exhibits that highlight the city’s rich history. It has an impressive collection of Derby porcelain, which is one of the most significant and best-known pieces of fine quality ceramics.

Natural Attractions of East Midlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Peak District National Park

The Peak District National Park is located in Derbyshire and offers stunning scenic views, popular hiking trails, and picturesque towns and villages. You can enjoy activities like hiking, cycling, rock climbing, and paragliding in this national park. The park has several trails that vary in difficulty, making it an ideal destination for all levels of adventure seekers.

Sherwood Forest

Located in Nottinghamshire, Sherwood Forest is a world-renowned destination and a popular place to visit for many tourists and locals alike. It is home to the legendary Robin Hood, and visitors can explore the historic trees and woodlands where Robin Hood and his Merry Men once roamed.

Rutland Water

Rutland Water is a beautiful reservoir located in Rutland that offers scenic views and a variety of water activities. You can try water sports like sailing, windsurfing, and kayaking, or simply enjoy a leisurely walk around the lake. There are also several cycling and walking trails available, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Midlands

East Midlands has some of the best hiking and biking routes in the UK. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

Dovedale Circular Walk

The Dovedale Circular Walk is a moderate hiking trail that stretches over 4 miles. It runs through picturesque countryside and offers stunning views of the valley and the River Dove. Along the way, you can also explore several caves, rock formations, and historic landmarks.

Monsal Trail

Monsal Trail is a popular cycling and walking trail in Peak District National Park. The trail is a converted railway line that runs through several tunnels and viaducts, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. It is ideal for all levels of cyclists and hikers and is a great way to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in East Midlands: Activities and Tours

East Midlands offers a range of activities and tours to suit all ages and interests. Here are some of the top things to do in the region:

Nottingham Ghost Tour

The Nottingham Ghost Tour is a spooky walking tour that takes you through the city’s haunted streets and landmarks. You can explore the historic spots with a knowledgeable guide and learn about the dark side of Nottingham.

British Motor Museum

The British Motor Museum in Warwickshire is a must-visit destination for car enthusiasts. It features a vast collection of historic, modern, and classic cars and motors, and interactive displays that showcase the evolution of British motoring.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Midlands

If you’re planning a week-long trip to East Midlands, here’s the itinerary you can follow:

Day 1: Nottingham

Explore the Nottingham Castle Museum and Art Gallery, the historic Nottingham City Centre, and the Lace Market area.

Day 2: Peak District National Park

Take a hiking or cycling tour around the spectacular Peak District National Park.

Day 3: Lincolnshire

Visit the charming town of Lincolnshire and explore the Lincoln Cathedral and the historic landmarks.

Day 4: Leicester

Discover the historic sites in Leicester, including the New Walk Museum and Art Gallery and the King Richard III Visitor Centre.

Day 5: Derbyshire

Explore the beautiful countryside of Derbyshire, including the famous Chatsworth House and the Derby Museum and Art Gallery.

Day 6: Sherwood Forest

Visit the legendary Sherwood Forest and explore the historic trees and woodlands where Robin Hood and his Merry Men once roamed.

Day 7: Rutland

Take a leisurely stroll around the Rutland Water and enjoy the scenic views and water activities.

Food and Drink Scene of East Midlands

East Midlands has a diverse food and drink scene that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Bakewell Tart

Bakewell Tart is a sweet pastry dish that originated in the town of Bakewell, Derbyshire. It is made with buttery shortcrust pastry, jam, and almond frangipane, making it a delicious and indulgent dessert.

Melton Mowbray Pork Pie

Melton Mowbray Pork Pie is a traditional dish that originated in the town of Melton Mowbray, Leicestershire. It is a savory pie that features a crispy pastry shell with succulent pork and jelly filling. It is a popular snack and can be enjoyed hot or cold.

Local Beverages

Marston’s Pedigree Ale

Marston’s Pedigree Ale is a classic beer that originated in Burton upon Trent, Staffordshire. It is a rich, malty beer with a balanced aroma, making it a popular choice among locals.

Belvoir Fruit Farms Elderflower Cordial

Belvoir Fruit Farms Elderflower Cordial is a refreshing and non-alcoholic beverage that is made with natural ingredients. It is a sweet and tangy drink and is a popular choice in the summer months.

Where to Stay in East Midlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Midlands offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Some popular options include:

Luxury Hotels

The Belfry Hotel and Resort

The Belfry Hotel and Resort is a luxurious hotel located in Warwickshire. It features a world-renowned golf course, spa, and several restaurants, making it an ideal destination for a relaxing and indulgent stay.

The Manor House

The Manor House is a luxurious boutique hotel located in Northamptonshire. It features elegant rooms, a Michelin-starred restaurant, and a spa, making it an ideal destination for a romantic getaway or a weekend retreat.

Budget Options

YHA National Forest

YHA National Forest is a budget-friendly hostel located in Derbyshire. It offers comfortable accommodation, a bar, and a kitchen, making it an ideal destination for backpackers and budget travelers.

Premier Inn Nottingham City Centre

Premier Inn Nottingham City Centre is a budget-friendly hotel located in Nottingham. It features comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a bar, making it an ideal destination for a comfortable stay in the city center.

What to Buy in East Midlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Midlands is famous for its local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the top things to buy:

Bakewell Tart

Bakewell Tart is a famous pastry dish that originated in Derbyshire. You can buy it in several bakeries and cafes in the region, making it a perfect souvenir for food lovers.

Blue Stilton Cheese

Blue Stilton Cheese is a famous cheese that originated in Leicestershire. It is a creamy, crumbly cheese with a distinct flavor and aroma, making it an ideal souvenir for cheese lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of East Midlands

East Midlands has a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the key things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • It is polite to address people with their titles, such as Mr., Mrs., or Miss.

  • Tipping between 10%-15% is customary in restaurants and cafes.

  • The UK uses the pound sterling as its currency.

Best Time to Visit East Midlands

The best time to visit East Midlands is between May and September when the weather is mild, and there are several outdoor activities available. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, visiting in the shoulder seasons (April/May and September/October) is also a good option.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Midlands

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and traditional dishes.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry appropriate clothing for outdoor activities and weather changes.
  • Use public transportation or cabs for traveling within the cities.
  • Take care of your belongings while traveling in crowded areas.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Carry large amounts of cash.
  • Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Midlands

East Midlands is relatively safe for tourists, but it is always wise to take precautions, such as

  • Being aware of your surroundings while traveling in crowded areas.
  • Not leaving valuables unattended.
  • Using official taxis when traveling at night.
  • Following traffic rules while driving or cycling.

Health in East Midlands

The healthcare system in the UK is of high quality, and visitors can avail of medical facilities in case of emergencies. It is advised to get travel insurance that covers medical emergencies during your stay.

Getting to East Midlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Midlands

The nearest airport to East Midlands is East Midlands Airport, which is located in Castle Donington, Derby. There are several domestic and international flights available from this airport.

Bus Stations Near East Midlands

East Midlands has several bus stations that offer local and national bus services. Some of the popular bus stations include Nottingham Bus Station, Leicester St Margaret’s Bus Station, and Derby Bus Station.

Other Options

Train services are available to most major cities in East Midlands, and the region is well connected by road networks.

How to Get Around in East Midlands

East Midlands has a good transportation system, and visitors can choose from several options, including trains, buses, taxis, and rental cars. It is recommended to plan your journey in advance, and you can use public transportation apps to help with your travels.

Where to Go Next from East Midlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Midlands is a great starting point for exploring other regions in UK. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Stratford-upon-Avon

Stratford-upon-Avon is a picturesque town located in Warwickshire, and it is the birthplace of William Shakespeare. It offers several attractions, including the Royal Shakespeare Company Theatre, Shakespeare’s Birthplace, and Anne Hathaway’s Cottage.

Cambridge

Cambridge is a famous university town located in the South East of England. It is home to several historic landmarks, including the Cambridge University, the River Cam, and the King’s College Chapel.

Conclusion

East Midlands is an excellent destination for travelers who are looking to explore the rich history, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine of the UK. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or a foodie, East Midlands has something to offer everyone. Follow this comprehensive travel guide, plan your trip, and get ready for a fun and memorable journey.
