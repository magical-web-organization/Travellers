Introduction to Leicester

Located in the picturesque county of Leicestershire in the heart of England, Leicester is a fascinating city with a rich history, vibrant culture, diverse population, and a plethora of tourist attractions. Whether you are a history buff, culture vulture, outdoor enthusiast or foodie, Leicester has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Leicester

Here is a comprehensive list of the top tourist attractions in Leicester:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Leicester Cathedral
  • King Richard III Visitor Centre
  • New Walk Museum and Art Gallery
  • Jewry Wall Museum
  • Abbey Pumping Station Museum
  • The Guildhall
  • Belgrave Road - the heart of Leicester’s vibrant Indian community

Natural Attractions of Leicester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bradgate Park
  • Old John Tower
  • Watermead Country Park
  • National Forest Way
  • The Great Central Walk (Leicester to Loughborough)

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leicester

Leicester and the surrounding countryside have a variety of bike and hiking routes, ranging from beginner to advanced. Some of the popular routes are:

  • The Leicestershire Round
  • Bradgate Park Circuit
  • King Richard III Trail
  • The National Forest Way
  • Leicester Skyride Route

Best Things to Do in Leicester: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy during your trip to Leicester.

  • Visit the King Power Stadium, home of the Leicester City Football Club
  • Catch a show at the Curve Theatre
  • Take a walking tour of the city with Leicester Walking Tours
  • Enjoy a local beer at one of the city’s many pubs, such as The Globe or The Criterion
  • Explore the city’s street art scene with a guided tour by Street Art Leicester

Week-Long Itinerary for Leicester

Day 1 - Explore the city centre, including Leicester Cathedral, Jewry Wall Museum, and the Guildhall Day 2 - Visit the King Richard III Visitor Centre and enjoy a walk in Watermead Country Park Day 3 - Take a day trip to Bradgate Park and Old John Tower Day 4 - Explore the New Walk Museum and Art Gallery and take a walk along the Great Central Walk Day 5 - Visit the Abbey Pumping Station Museum and walk along the River Soar Day 6 - Spend a day in the city’s vibrant Indian community on Belgrave Road Day 7 - Enjoy a leisurely day exploring the local food and drink scene

Local Culture and Customs of Leicester

Leicester’s cultural diversity is reflected in its music, art, food, and festivals. The city is home to many different communities, including Indian, African, Caribbean, and Eastern European. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting religious sites and during festivals.

Best Time to Visit Leicester

The best time to visit Leicester is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the city has events and attractions throughout the year, so there is always something to see and do.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leicester

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Explore the city’s vibrant street art scene
  • Visit the city’s cultural and historical attractions
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take a walking tour of the city

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public spaces
  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions
  • Engage in any illegal activity

Safety and Health

Safety in Leicester

Leicester is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Leicester

Leicester has good healthcare facilities, including the Leicester Royal Infirmary. Visitors are advised to take out travel insurance before visiting Leicester.

Getting to Leicester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Leicester

  • East Midlands Airport - 20 miles away
  • Birmingham Airport - 42 miles away

Bus Stations Near Leicester

  • St. Margaret’s Bus Station
  • Leicester Coach Station

Other Options

Leicester is easily accessible by train, with regular services from London, Birmingham, and Sheffield. Visitors can also travel by car or bicycle.

How to Get Around in Leicester

Leicester has good public transportation, including buses and trains. The city is also easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Leicester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nottingham - 30 miles away
  • Birmingham - 45 miles away
  • Coventry - 22 miles away
  • Peak District National Park - 40 miles away

Conclusion

Leicester is a city with a rich history, a vibrant culture, and a diverse population. Whether you are interested in history, culture, food, or outdoor activities, Leicester has something to offer. From its museums and historical attractions to its natural parks and hiking trails, Leicester is a must-visit destination.
