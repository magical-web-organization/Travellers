Introduction to Buxton

Buxton is a historic spa town located in the heart of Derbyshire in the East Midlands of England. It is renowned for its stunning countryside, natural mineral springs, and a fascinating history dating back to the Roman period. Visitors to Buxton can expect a delightful mix of heritage, outdoor adventure, and stunning landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Buxton

Are you planning a trip to Buxton but don’t know where to start? Here is our list of the must-visit sights and attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buxton Museum and Art Gallery – Explore the rich history, geology, and archaeology of Buxton and the Peak District. The museum features a range of displays from prehistoric times to present-day art and photography exhibitions.
  • The Devonshire Dome – Originally built as a stable block, the Devonshire Dome is now a beautifully restored venue that hosts a range of events and performances. Visitors can enjoy guided tours or attend one of the many cultural events held throughout the year.
  • Poole’s Cavern and Buxton Country Park – Step underground to explore the fascinating limestone caverns and stalactites that have formed over millions of years in this natural wonderland. In addition, Buxton Country Park offers fantastic walks and breathtaking views of the surrounding Peak District.

Natural Attractions of Buxton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pavilion Gardens – With 23 acres of landscaped gardens, lakes, and entertainment, Pavilion Gardens provides an ideal place to relax and unwind. Enjoy a picnic, take in the beautiful surroundings, or ride the miniature railway.
  • Solomon’s Temple – An iconic Buxton landmark, Solomon’s Temple sits atop Grin Low Hill and provides panoramic views of Buxton and the surrounding countryside.
  • Goyt Valley – A haven for hikers and nature lovers, Goyt Valley offers unrivaled scenic beauty. The valley features three reservoirs, walking trails, and fantastic views of the Derbyshire Dales.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buxton

Buxton is famous for its outdoor adventure, and we’ve got some of the best hiking and biking routes to help you explore the stunning scenery.

  • The Monsal Trail – This former railway-turned-cycleway is 8.5 miles long and takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the Peak District.
  • Limestone Way – This 46-mile trail is ideal for experienced hikers, taking you through some of the most challenging terrains in the region.
  • Derwent Valley Heritage Way – This 55-mile trail offers a fantastic mix of picturesque landscapes, river valleys, and historical landmarks, including the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Derwent Valley Mills.

Best Things to Do in Buxton: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to outdoor adventures, Buxton has something for everyone. Here are our top picks:

  • Opera House Buxton – As the newest Edwardian theatre in England, the Opera House is a stunning venue that plays host to a range of performances, from comedy to live music.
  • Buxton Spa Experience – Take a dip in one of Buxton’s hot springs, which are renowned for their healing properties.
  • Peak District Cycling – Enjoy a guided cycling tour through the stunning scenery of the Peak District and get an up-close and personal view of Buxton’s beautiful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Buxton

After a day filled with adventure, you’ll need to refuel. Buxton has a fantastic food and drink scene with plenty of delicious options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to indulge in:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bakewell Tart – A classic English dessert made with jam, almond paste, and shortcrust pastry.
  • Derbyshire Oatcake – These savory pancakes are usually served with bacon, eggs, and cheese.
  • Pudding and Toad in the Hole – A traditional British meal of sausages served with pudding, gravy and Yorkshire Pudding.

Local Beverages

  • Buxton Brewery Beers – Buxton’s award-winning brewery produces a range of craft beers that are sure to get your taste buds going.
  • Bakewell Pudding Wine – This unique wine is made from white grapes and has a slightly sweet flavor.

Where to Stay in Buxton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Buxton has plenty of accommodation options to suit varying budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Old Hall Hotel – This hotel boasts over 500 years of history and has previously hosted Mary Queen of Scots and Charles I.
  • The Palace Hotel – This Victorian-era hotel offers luxurious accommodation and a variety of leisure facilities.
  • YHA Hartington Hall – For budget travelers, this hostel is set in a beautiful 17th-century manor house and offers dormitory and private rooms.

What to Buy in Buxton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without bringing back a cultural souvenir or two. Here are some local products and souvenirs to look out for when in Buxton:

  • Blue John Jewelry – This unique mineral is found only in the Peak District and is used to make beautiful jewelry and ornaments.
  • Buxton Water – This natural mineral water is bottled straight from the source in Buxton and sold all over the country.
  • Peak District Gin – Made by the Derbyshire Distillery, this gin is made using juniper berries picked from the Peak District.

Local Culture and Customs of Buxton

The people of Buxton are warm and friendly, and the town has a rich cultural history. Make sure to respect local customs, such as being polite, friendly, and respectful, when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Buxton

Buxton is a year-round destination, but depending on your interests and preferences, your ideal time to visit might vary. Spring is an excellent time for hiking and enjoying the outdoors, while summer is ideal for festivals and outdoor events. Autumn brings beautiful colors and is perfect for photography, while winter is an ideal time for skiing and winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buxton

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Buxton, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Take a dip in the hot springs.
  • Explore the stunning countryside of the Peak District.
  • Try traditional English cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect historical monuments and sites.
  • Be rude to fellow tourists or locals.

Safety and Health

While Buxton is known for its safety and healthy environment, visitors should take standard precautions to stay safe and healthy.

Safety in Buxton

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep your valuables in safe places.
  • Only use licensed taxis and public transportation.

Health in Buxton

  • Use sunscreen to stay protected from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Make sure to stay hydrated during outdoor activities.
  • Take necessary precautions while exploring the natural beauty of Buxton.

Getting to Buxton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buxton is served by several major airports and is easily accessible by bus, train, or car.

Airports Near Buxton

  • Manchester Airport – 20.5 miles from Buxton
  • East Midlands Airport – 44 miles from Buxton
  • Birmingham Airport – 68 miles from Buxton

Bus Stations Near Buxton

  • Buxton Bus Station – Serves the town and local areas.
  • Manchester Coach Station – Serves most major UK cities.
  • Derby Bus Station – Serves East Midlands.

Other Options

  • Train – Buxton has its own train station that is well connected to major UK cities.
  • Private Car/ Taxi – Plenty of local taxi services and car rental agencies are available in Buxton.

How to Get Around in Buxton

While Buxton is small enough to explore on foot, visitors can also opt for public transportation or rent bicycles or cars for a more extensive exploration. Bus services operate within the town, and trains run to neighboring towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Buxton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Buxton, make sure to explore some of the nearby destinations and excursions.

  • Manchester – Less than an hour away by train, Manchester is a vibrant city famous for its music, art, and nightlife.
  • Chatsworth House – A beautiful stately home and gardens located in the heart of the Peak District.
  • Alton Towers Theme Park – A popular theme park located just 20 miles from Buxton.

Conclusion

Buxton is a hidden gem in the heart of England that has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan the perfect trip to Buxton. Indulge in the local history, culture, and cuisine, take in the stunning landscape, and make unforgettable memories in this beautiful town in Derbyshire.
