Introduction to New Mills

New Mills is a charming town located in High Peak, Derbyshire, in the East Midlands region of England. The town is surrounded by the River Goyt and Sett Valley, making it a perfect destination for exploring the great outdoors. Known for its industrial heritage, New Mills offers plenty of attractions to keep visitors entertained, including museums, galleries, and historic landmarks.

Whether you are looking for a weekend getaway or planning a longer vacation, New Mills has something to offer for everyone. In this travel guide, we will cover all the essential information you need to know before visiting New Mills, including the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in New Mills

Here’s a list of the must-visit places in New Mills:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Mills Heritage Centre: Housed in a former spinning mill, the centre features exhibits on the town’s history, the textile industry, and local geology.
  • New Mills Art Theatre: It’s a well-renowned community theatre that stages a wide range of productions throughout the year.
  • The Torrs Riverside Park: Spanning over 12 acres, this park contains a stunning mill pond and wooded glades with the former mill buildings on either side.
  • Torrs Hydro Scheme: A unique hydroelectric power station at the Torrs Gorge, powered by the drop of the River Goyt.

Natural Attractions of New Mills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sett Valley Trail: A 2-mile-long route that offers a scenic walk or bike ride along the former railway line.
  • Lantern Pike: A popular walking route that offers stunning views of the surrounding Peak District.
  • Millennium Walkway: A footbridge that spans the River Goyt and offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Mills

New Mills is surrounded by dramatic countryside, beautiful waterfalls, and well-marked trails, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best walking and biking routes to explore in New Mills:

  • Cown Edge Way: A picturesque trail that starts at New Mills and takes you through some of the stunning Derbyshire countryside.
  • The Gritstone Trail: A 35-mile hiking trail that links together some of the best viewpoints of the Peak District.
  • Mellor and Marple Ridge: A challenging walk that takes you through steep hills and rocky outcrops with panoramic views across the hills, valleys and towns.

Best Things to Do in New Mills: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in New Mills:

  • Visit to Sweet Memories of New Mills: This family-run sweet shop offers a wide range of traditional confectionery from the UK and around the world.
  • Take a brewery tour of Torrside Brewing: Situated just outside the town, this small brewery produces excellent craft beer and offers tours for visitors.
  • Enjoy the annual New Mills Festival: Held in September, the festival features live music, food markets, art exhibits, and theatre performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Mills

Here’s a detailed itinerary you can follow if you are planning a week-long stay in New Mills:

Day Activity
Day 1 Check into the hotel, stroll through the town, explore the New Mills Heritage Centre, and visit the Sett Valley Trail
Day 2 Visit the Torrs Hydro Scheme, enjoy the Millennium Walkway, and grab lunch at The Butterfly House Cafe
Day 3 Take a day trip to the nearby Lyme Park House and Garden, followed by dinner at The Pack Horse Inn
Day 4 Explore the Laneshaw Reservoir and Torrs Riverside Park, followed by a brewery tour at Torrside Brewery
Day 5 Visit the St. Georges Church and take a guided tour of the massive clock tower situated next to it
Day 6 Plan a leisurely hike through the Goyt Valley, soak in the beauty of the Peak District
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs, enjoy some traditional English dishes, and head back home

Food and Drink Scene of New Mills

New Mills has a flourishing food and drink scene, offering visitors a wide range of culinary delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lancashire Hotpot: A hearty lamb stew that’s perfect for a cold day.
  • Bakewell Pudding: A delicious dessert made with almond flavorings and raspberry jam.
  • Cheese and Onion pie: A savory pie with a flaky crust, filled with onions and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Bollington Brewery Ale: A traditional English beer that’s brewed locally and served in many pubs around New Mills.
  • Old Joes Cider: A popular local cider that’s known for its refreshing flavor and crisp taste.
  • Whaley Bridge Gin: One of the best gin producers in the region, Whaley Bridge Gin is produced using the finest botanicals and locally-sourced spring water.

Where to Stay in New Mills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are the best places to stay in New Mills:

  • White Hart Hotel: A traditional coaching inn situated in the heart of New Mills, offering comfortable rooms and great food.
  • Fox Hall Inn: A superb country inn located in the rolling hills of the Pennines, with excellent food, charming rooms, and lovely gardens.
  • The Butterfly House: A quirky guesthouse with comfortable rooms, a beautiful garden, and a popular cafe.

What to Buy in New Mills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs, New Mills has plenty of unique options to choose from. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Ceramics from the Marple Pottery & Tile Works: Hand-crafted ceramics and tiles produced by Marple Pottery & Tile Works are a unique memento to take home.
  • Handmade chocolates from Cotton Tree Chocolates: Handmade chocolates are an excellent gift option.
  • Local cheese from the Hartington Cheese Shop: A foodie’s paradise located near New Mills.

Local Culture and Customs of New Mills

Like most of the UK, New Mills celebrates various festivals and holidays throughout the year. One of the notable annual events is the New Mills Festival, a 2-week-long arts festival that takes place in September. Visitors can also participate in the town’s various cultural and sporting events, such as the New Mills Carnival and Bonfire Night.

Best Time to Visit New Mills

The best time to visit New Mills is during the summer months, when the weather is pleasant, and the countryside is at its best. Although peak season crowds can be a bit overwhelming, New Mills is beautiful throughout the year and offers visitors a different experience in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Mills

Here are some of the essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while travelling to New Mills:

Do:

  • Plan ahead and book your accommodation in advance to avoid last-minute hassles.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, and be courteous to the locals.
  • Always pack for all kinds of weather as the weather can be unpredictable in the UK.

Don’t:

  • Travel without appropriate insurance coverage for your health and belongings.
  • Ignore safety warnings or advice from local authorities.

Safety and Health

Here are some essential safety and health tips to keep in mind while visiting New Mills:

Safety in New Mills

  • Always wear comfortable and appropriate footwear while exploring the trails and natural landscapes.
  • Keep an eye on children while hiking or when near open water sources.
  • Stay on marked trails and avoid wandering off into the unknown.

Health in New Mills

  • Apply sunscreen and use insect repellent to avoid insect bites and sunburn.
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day, especially if you are planning to go on long hikes.
  • Carry any necessary medication with you, including any prescription medications or inhalers.

Getting to New Mills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the best transport options to reach New Mills:

Airports Near New Mills

  • Manchester Airport: The nearest international airport, located about 20 miles from New Mills.
  • East Midlands Airport: Located about 40 miles from New Mills, it’s another convenient option for international travellers.

Bus Stations Near New Mills

  • New Mills Central: Located in the town centre, it’s a well-connected bus station with regular services from nearby cities.
  • Whaley Bridge: A small town located about 3 miles from New Mills, offering bus services to many destinations across the region.

Other Options

  • Train: New Mills has two railway stations, New Mills Central and New Mills South, with regular train services from Manchester and Sheffield.
  • Car: New Mills is easily accessible by car, with major highways such as the M62 and A6 passing nearby.

How to Get Around in New Mills

The best way to get around New Mills is on foot or by bike, as the town is relatively small and easy to navigate. If you prefer public transport, there are several bus routes that connect the town with nearby attractions and destinations.

Where to Go Next from New Mills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions to visit from New Mills:

  • Buxton: A spa town located about 10 miles south-east of New Mills, known for its natural hot springs and stunning architecture.
  • Kinder Scout: A famous hill in the Peak District, popular for its scenic views and challenging hiking trails.
  • Chatsworth House: A grand English country house located about 16 miles from New Mills, known for its sprawling gardens and impressive art collection.

Conclusion

New Mills is the perfect destination for travellers looking to explore the best of Derbyshire’s natural beauty and rich industrial heritage. With its scenic walks, outstanding architecture, and charming local culture, there is something for everyone in this stunning town. Plan your next holiday to New Mills and immerse yourself in the best of what Derbyshire has to offer!
