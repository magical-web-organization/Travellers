Introduction to Belper

Belper is a charming town located in Derbyshire, in East Midlands, England. It is known for its industrial past and beautiful countryside. The town is situated on the banks of the River Derwent and is surrounded by woods and hills, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The town is also rich in cultural heritage, with several museums and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Belper

If you’re planning a trip to Belper, below are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Strutt’s North Mill: This museum tells the story of Belper’s industrial past, especially the cotton mills and inventions of the Strutt family. There’s a guided tour and a gift shop that sells souvenirs and local products.
  • St. John’s Chapel: This church dates back to the 13th century and has several interesting features, including medieval stone carvings and a Norman doorway.
  • Belper Heritage Centre: Located in the town center, this museum has exhibits on local history, industry, and culture. There’s a gift shop that sells souvenirs and local products.

Natural Attractions of Belper: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • River Gardens: This park is located on the banks of River Derwent and features beautiful gardens, a play area, and a bandstand that hosts events during the summer months.
  • Chevin Forest Park: This park is a nature lover’s paradise and has several hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the town.
  • Cromford Canal: This canal is a peaceful place for a walk or bike ride, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belper

Belper has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the countryside. Some of the top routes include:

  • Chevin Ridge Walk: This is a 3-mile circular route that takes you through woodland and offers stunning views of the town and valley.
  • Cromford Canal Walk: This is a 5-mile circular route that follows the canal and offers views of stunning countryside.
  • Belper Circular Walk: This is a 6-mile circular route that takes you through the historic and natural highlights of the town, including the River Gardens and Strutt’s North Mill.

Best Things to Do in Belper: Activities and Tours

Apart from the places to visit, Belper offers several activities and tours, including:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning Derbyshire countryside and get a bird’s eye view of Belper.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: Explore the River Derwent by canoe or kayak with one of the many hire companies in Belper.
  • Belper Ghost Walks: Take a spooky evening tour of Belper and learn about the town’s haunted history.

Food and Drink Scene of Belper

Belper has several restaurants and pubs offering a wide range of cuisines. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Derbyshire Oatcakes: A local delicacy that’s similar to crumpets, served with bacon and cheese.
  • Bakewell Pudding: A sweet pastry dessert that’s a variant of Bakewell Tart.
  • Cheese from Hartington: A cheese made in the nearby village of Hartington and sold in most local food shops.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Belper has several pubs that serve real ale brewed in the nearby countryside.
  • Whisky: White Peak Distillery, in the nearby village of Ambergate, produces high-quality whisky.

Where to Stay in Belper: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belper has several accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Below are some of the top choices:

  • The Lion Hotel: A traditional hotel with a modern twist and located in the heart of town.
  • Chip and Pin: A stylish bed and breakfast located in a renovated bank building.
  • Holly Meadow Farm: A peaceful farmhouse that offers affordable rooms and stunning views of the countryside.

What to Buy in Belper: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belper has several shops that sell souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Fresh and Local: A shop that sells fresh produce, local cheese, and handmade chocolates.
  • The Deli: A deli that sells unique food products from around the world, and also locally sourced food.
  • Kreating Rustic Charms: A shop that sells handmade jewelry made from local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Belper

Belper is known for its strong community spirit and sense of unity. The town hosts several cultural events and festivals throughout the year, including the Belper Arts Festival, held annually in May.

Best Time to Visit Belper

Belper is a great destination to visit year-round, but the best time to visit is between May-September when the weather is warm and the days are long. The town also hosts several festivals during this time, including the Belper Food Festival and Belper Summer Bonanza.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belper

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Sample the local delicacies, including Derbyshire Oatcakes and Bakewell Pudding.
  • Take a ghost walk: Explore the town’s spooky history on a guided tour.
  • Hike or bike: Enjoy the stunning countryside on foot or on wheels.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep the town clean by disposing of your waste responsibly.
  • Ignore the local culture: Respect the town’s community spirit by joining local events and festivals.
  • Be reckless: Be mindful of your safety and don’t take unnecessary risks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belper

Belper is a safe town for visitors, but it’s always advisable to take general safety precautions like locking your car and securing your possessions. Emergency services can be reached by dialing 999 (police, fire, ambulance).

Health in Belper

Belper has a few medical practices and a local hospital nearby. In the case of a medical emergency, dial 999.

Getting to Belper: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belper is well-connected to major cities in the UK, making it easy to get to. Below are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Belper

The nearest international airport is East Midlands Airport, which is about a 45-minute drive from Belper.

Bus Stations Near Belper

Belper has a bus station that serves several destinations in the region, including Derby and Nottingham.

Other Options

Belper is also accessible by train, with the nearest railway station being Ambergate, which is a 10-minute drive from the town center.

How to Get Around in Belper

Belper is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside, it’s advisable to hire a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from Belper: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belper is well-located to explore several nearby destinations, including:

  • Peak District: A national park that’s famous for its hiking trails.
  • Matlock Bath: A spa town that’s popular for its cycle hire and cable car.
  • Chatsworth House: A grand stately home that’s known for its architecture and stunning gardens.

Conclusion

Belper is a hidden gem in Derbyshire, with stunning countryside, rich culture, and a strong sense of community. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or food, there’s something for everyone. Plan your visit today and experience the magic of this charming town.
