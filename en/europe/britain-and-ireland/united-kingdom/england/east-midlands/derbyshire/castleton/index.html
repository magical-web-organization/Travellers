Introduction to Castleton

Castleton is a picturesque town located in the heart of the Peak District National Park in Derbyshire, England. It is situated at the base of a limestone hill, known as Mam Tor, and is surrounded by stunning natural beauty.

Due to its strategic location, Castleton has a rich history and is home to a number of cultural and historical attractions, as well as being an outdoor enthusiast’s dream destination. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Castleton has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Castleton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Peveril Castle: A medieval castle built by William the Conqueror’s son, overlooking the Hope Valley and the village of Castleton. It offers stunning views and an insight into castle life in the Middle Ages.

  • The Devil’s Arse Cave: The Devil’s Arse Cave, also known as Peak Cavern, is one of Castleton’s most popular attractions. A subterranean adventure that provides insight into the lives of the people who lived inside it and how it was formed.

  • St Edmund’s Church: This beautiful church dates back to the Norman era and is home to stunning stained-glass windows, including the one depicting the martyrdom of St Edmund, who is said to be responsible for bringing Christianity to England.

Natural Attractions of Castleton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mam Tor: The hill standing behind Castleton, offering amazing panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and the Hope Valley. Mam Tor is popular with hikers and climbers.

  • Winnats Pass: A beautiful, winding road that passes through a limestone gorge in the Peak District. Experience the natural beauty of the area with a drive or hike, an ideal spot for photographers.

  • Cave Dale: A beautiful dale opposite Winnats Pass, offering breathtaking views of the landscape alongside walking and hiking routes for adventurous travelers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Castleton

-Castleton Ridge Walk starts in the town centre and takes you along the Castleton Ridge, providing panoramic views of the Hope Valley. It is a moderately challenging walk with plenty of opportunities to stop and enjoy the stunning scenery.

-The Limestone Way is a long-distance footpath that runs for 46 miles from Castleton to Rocester in Staffordshire. This route is perfect for walkers who want to discover the beauty and history of the Peak District National Park.

Best Things to Do in Castleton: Activities and Tours

  • Blue John Cavern: Take a guided tour of the famous Blue John Cavern to view the world’s most beautiful Blue Fluorspar mineral and to learn about the cave’s history.

  • Rock Climbing and Abseiling: Get adventurous and join a guided rock climbing and abseiling tour in the Peak District, with Castleton as a base camp.

  • Adventure Sports: Enjoy a day of adventure sports in the nearby Ladybower Forestr. Activities include kayaking, canoeing, archery, cycling, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Castleton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bakewell Tarts: A classic Peak District dessert, originating from the nearby town of Bakewell–a tart filled with almond frangipane and jam.

  • Puddings & Sponges: A delicious local favourite dessert that is a hot custard poured over a steamed pudding or sponge.

  • Buxton Ale: A beer brewed in the nearby town of Buxton; local flavor that can be enjoyed at Castleton’s many charming pubs.

Local Beverages

  • Traditional Afternoon Tea: Enjoy traditional tea with homemade cakes, scones and slender sandwiches at any of Castleton’s cozy tea rooms.

  • Mulled Wine: Sip at mulled wine while cozying up in one of Castleton’s pubs on a cold winter evening.

Where to Stay in Castleton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Castle Inn: A historic inn just down the street from Peveril Castle, offering comfortable rooms and a vibrant atmosphere in Castleton’s main square.

  • Ye Olde Nags Head: A cozy, welcoming pub with its own guesthouse located in the heart of Castleton village.

  • The Peaks Inn: This charming guesthouse offers a range of rooms and a hearty breakfast, just a short walk away from the town centre.

What to Buy in Castleton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blue John Jewelry: Take home the beautiful Blue John mineral transformed into jewelry.

  • Peak District Products: A selection of local Peak District products, including jams, chutneys, and marmalades.

Local Culture and Customs of Castleton

  • Castleton Garland Ceremony: This unique festival is held annually on Oakapple Day, with the procession of a garland (decorated with flowers and greenery) around the entire village.

  • Hundred High Peak Lambs Festival: Occurring annually in May, this festival showcases the custom of Lakeland shepherds of “counting” sheep to determine their shearing and lambing time.

Best Time to Visit Castleton

The best time to visit Castleton is during the summer months for long daylight hours and mild temperatures (June to August). The spring and autumn months offer the same mild temperatures but with fewer crowds of tourists. As the trails and nature parks are at their prettiest during peak season, and prices are lower during the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Castleton

Do:

  • Visit Peveril Castle for a spectacular view of the village and valley.
  • Take plenty of water and snacks if you’re planning on hiking or cycling.
  • Visit Castleton’s farmers market and buy fresh local produce.

Don’t:

  • Climb on historical monuments and landmarks (like Peveril Castle).
  • Litter in the national park and help preserve the natural beauty of the area.
  • Forget to check local weather conditions before leaving for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Castleton

Castleton is generally safe for visitors. Still, it’s always a good idea to keep an eye on your belongings, especially when you’re in crowded areas or tourist attractions.

Health in Castleton

The village has a community-owned medical center. Still, most complicated medical conditions generally require a trip to the nearby city, Sheffield. Travel health insurance is a good idea in case of emergency.

Getting to Castleton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Castleton

The nearest airport to Castleton is Manchester Airport (MAN), approximately 40 miles away. It offers direct flights to and from major cities in the UK, Europe, and other international destinations. From Manchester, the journey to Castleton can be done via bus, train, or taxi service.

Bus Stations Near Castleton

Castleton is well-connected to the major cities in the region, with buses running regularly from Manchester, Sheffield, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

The village of Castleton is also well-connected by road, with easy access to major motorways. Taxis and private transfers are also available from the nearby cities or airports.

How to Get Around in Castleton

Castleton is a small village, and most attractions and accommodations are within walking distance. Travelers can park their vehicle on the street or use local bus and taxi options. If visitors want an offbeat adventure, they can rent a bicycle from one of several rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Castleton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bakewell: A charming town famous for its Bakewell tart is only a short drive away.

  • Chatsworth House: One of the largest and most magnificent houses in the UK, located about 30 minutes’ drive from Castleton.

  • Sheffield: The major city is only an hour’s drive, with excellent shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Castleton is a beautiful village located in the heart of Peak District of Derbyshire, which makes it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. This comprehensive travel guide, outlining all the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and tips, can help visitors plan their perfect trip to Castleton.
