Introduction to Eyam

Nestled in the heart of the Peak District National Park, Eyam is a picturesque village that sits on a hillside overlooking the stunning Derbyshire countryside. This charming village is known for its remarkable history, beautiful landscapes, and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Eyam

Here are some of the must-visit places in Eyam:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eyam Museum
  • Eyam Parish Church
  • Plague Cottages
  • Riley Graves

Natural Attractions of Eyam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Eyam Moor
  • Eyam Edge
  • Stanage Edge
  • Monsal Trail

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eyam

Hikers and bikers can enjoy the beautiful trails in the Peak District National Park, including:

  • Limestone Way
  • Derwent Valley Heritage Way
  • Tissington Trail

Best Things to Do in Eyam: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Eyam Museum to learn about the village’s unique history.
  • Step back in time at the Plague Cottages.
  • Take a tour of Eyam Parish Church.
  • Enjoy a hike along one of the scenic trails in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eyam

Day 1 - Explore the village and visit the Eyam Museum. Day 2 - Take a hike on Eyam Moor or Eyam Edge. Day 3 - Visit the Plague Cottages and the Riley Graves. Day 4 - Take a trip to Stanage Edge for spectacular views. Day 5 - Tour Eyam Parish Church and appreciate its architecture. Day 6 - Enjoy a bike ride along the Tissington Trail. Day 7 - Take a day trip to the nearby Chatsworth House or Haddon Hall.

Local Culture and Customs of Eyam

The village has a rich history, largely influenced by the plague outbreak that occurred in the 17th century. Locals commemorate the tragedy every year on Plague Sunday.

Best Time to Visit Eyam

The best time to visit Eyam is during spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when temperatures are mild and the scenery is breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eyam

Do:

  • Take a walk through the village cobbled streets to enjoy the picturesque views.
  • Learn about the village’s intriguing history at the Eyam Museum.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when exploring the Peak District National Park.
  • Damage or deface any of the local historical landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eyam

The village is generally a safe place to visit. It is recommended to take necessary precautions when hiking or biking along the trails of the Peak District National Park.

Health in Eyam

There are no major health concerns in the village. However, visitors must adhere to the necessary COVID-19 precautions.

How to Get Around in Eyam

The village is compact, and visitors can easily walk to most attractions. Alternatively, bicycles can be rented from nearby bike rental shops.

Conclusion
