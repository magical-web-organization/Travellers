Introduction to Crowden-in-Longdendale

Crowden-in-Longdendale is a village located in Derbyshire, in the East Midlands region of England. This charming village situated in the Longdendale valley is a perfect retreat for nature lovers, hikers, bikers, and those seeking a peaceful getaway. The natural beauty of the Pennine hills, heather moors, and picturesque reservoirs surrounding the village attracts thousands of visitors each year. Along with its beautiful countryside, Crowden-in-Longdendale also offers access to nearby cities such as Manchester, Sheffield, and Leeds, which makes it an ideal place for a day trip or weekend escape.

List of Places to Visit in Crowden-in-Longdendale

Culturally Important Attractions - Museums and Historical Places

  • Crowden War Memorial: A beautiful war memorial dedicated to the soldiers who lost their lives in World War I and II.

  • St Michael and All Angels Church: The church is an excellent example of Gothic architecture and is known for its impressive stained glass windows and Art Nouveau reredos.

  • Crowden Heritage Centre: Learn about the history and heritage of the village and its surrounding areas. The center contains historical artifacts, photographs, and information about the local fauna and flora.

Natural Attractions of Crowden-in-Longdendale - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peak District National Park: Crowden-in-Longdendale is located on the edge of the Peak District National Park, which offers a myriad of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, rock climbing, and fishing. The park is also home to various wildlife, including red deer, otters, and water voles.

  • Longdendale Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail that follows the River Etherow and passes through picturesque countryside and stunning reservoirs.

  • The Pennine Way: One of the most famous hiking trails in England. The trail passes through the village and offers hikers an opportunity to explore the rugged beauty of the Pennine Hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crowden-in-Longdendale

  • Crowden to Black Hill Hike: A challenging 9-mile hike that passes through some of the most beautiful and rugged terrain in the Peak District National Park. The hike rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding hills and reservoirs.

  • Longdendale Trail Bike Ride: A leisurely 7-mile bike ride that follows the River Etherow and offers stunning views of Crowden and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Crowden-in-Longdendale - Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports at Torside Reservoir: Canoeing, kayaking, and fishing are popular activities at Torside Reservoir.

  • Horse Riding: Several stables in the area offer guided horse riding tours through the stunning countryside.

  • Crowden Shooting Ground: A clay pigeon shooting range located on the outskirts of the village. The range offers a variety of shooting experiences for beginners and advanced shooters.

Food and Drink Scene of Crowden-in-Longdendale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bakewell Pudding: A sweet British dessert consisting of a pastry base, jam, and almond frangipane.

  • Cornish Pasty: A savory pastry filled with beef, potatoes, onions, and turnips.

  • Apple Pie: A classic British dessert consisting of a pastry crust filled with apples and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Buxton Mineral Water: A natural spring water from the nearby town of Buxton.

  • Hawkshead Brewery Beers: A local microbrewery that produces a variety of delicious and unique beers.

Where to Stay in Crowden-in-Longdendale - Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Old Hall Inn: A charming hotel with comfortable and spacious rooms, delicious food, and a cozy pub.

  • Crowden Camping and Caravan Club Site: A campsite located next to the Longdendale Trail. The site offers basic facilities and stunning views of the surrounding hills.

  • YHA Crowden: A youth hostel located in an 18th-century farmhouse. The hostel offers cozy dorms and private rooms, a self-catering kitchen, and stunning views of the hills.

What to Buy in Crowden-in-Longdendale - Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hawkshead Brewery Beer: A popular souvenir from the village. Hawkshead Brewery produces a variety of unique and delicious beers that are perfect for a gift or a treat for yourself.

  • Local Honey: The natural honey produced by the bees in the area is known for its unique taste and health benefits.

  • Handmade Crafts: The village has a thriving artisan community that produces unique and handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Crowden-in-Longdendale

The village has a rich history and a strong sense of community. The locals are welcoming and friendly, and visitors are encouraged to join in the community events. The village hosts a variety of events throughout the year, such as the annual Crowden Show, which celebrates the local farming community and their produce.

Best Time to Visit Crowden-in-Longdendale

The best time to visit Crowden-in-Longdendale is between May and September when the weather is warmer and the hills are covered in heather and wildflowers. The village can get busy during the summer months, so it’s advisable to book accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crowden-in-Longdendale

Do:

  • Enjoy the beautiful countryside and respect the wildlife.

  • Join in the community events and support the local businesses.

  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in the countryside.

  • Disturb the wildlife.

  • Ignore the safety warnings while hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Crowden-in-Longdendale

The village is a safe place to visit, but visitors should be aware of the rugged terrain and potential hazards while hiking or biking in the surrounding area.

Health in Crowden-in-Longdendale

There are no major health concerns in the village, but visitors should be aware of ticks, which are prevalent in the countryside, and the risk of Lyme disease.

Getting to Crowden-in-Longdendale - Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Crowden-in-Longdendale

The nearest airport to Crowden-in-Longdendale is Manchester Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Crowden-in-Longdendale

The village is served by several bus routes, including the 390 First Bus service, which runs from Glossop to Hathersage.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the village by train, with the nearest train station located in Glossop.

How to Get Around in Crowden-in-Longdendale

The village is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors who want to explore the surrounding area can hire a bike, car, or join a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Crowden-in-Longdendale - Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manchester: A vibrant and cosmopolitan city known for its football clubs, museums, and lively nightlife. Manchester is only 20 miles away from Crowden-in-Longdendale.

  • Sheffield: Another vibrant city, Sheffield is known for its industrial heritage, museums, and theaters. Sheffield is approximately 30 miles away from Crowden-in-Longdendale.

  • Leeds: A bustling city in West Yorkshire, Leeds is known for its museums, galleries, and lively shopping scene. Leeds is approximately 40 miles away from Crowden-in-Longdendale.

Conclusion

Crowden-in-Longdendale is a charming village that offers visitors a perfect retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its stunning countryside, outdoor activities, delicious food, and warm community, Crowden-in-Longdendale is an ideal place to spend a relaxing holiday or weekend break. Visitors will quickly fall in love with the beauty and charm of this hidden gem in the Peak District National Park.
