Introduction to Hathersage

Located in Derbyshire, in the heart of the Peak District National Park, Hathersage is a picturesque English village known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and outdoor adventures. It is a popular destination for hikers, climbers, and nature enthusiasts, with breathtaking landscapes, rolling hills, and winding rivers.

This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive insight into all the essential information you’ll need to make your Hathersage trip memorable.

List of Places to Visit in Hathersage

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hathersage Church: The historic church of St. Michael and All Angels is a beautiful example of a medieval English church, with stunning architecture and intricate carvings. It is also the final resting place of Little John, the long-time friend and companion of Robin Hood.

  2. The David Mellor Cutlery Factory and Museum: A visit to this iconic factory allows you to witness the creation of revered cutlery pieces, including patterns used by the British Royal Family. You can also take a tour of the museum here and explore the fascinating history of cutlery production in Sheffield.

  3. The National Tramway Museum: Take a trip down memory lane with a visit to this trams-only museum and discover its collection of vintage trams.

Natural Attractions of Hathersage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Stanage Edge: A famous rock-climbing spot, Stanage Edge offers spectacular panoramic views and a dramatic backdrop for a perfect photo opportunity.

  2. Hope Valley: A 10km stretch of stunning natural beauty, you can enjoy cycling, hiking or exploring charming villages along this valley trail.

  3. Bamford Edge: This is the Peak District’s hidden gem offering scenic walks and climbs, overlooking Ladybower and Derwent reservoirs and excellent high-level views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hathersage

Hathersage boasts many hiking and biking routes, which let you explore the surrounding countryside and natural wonders. Some of the popular options include:

  1. Mam Tor: A must on your hiking list, Mam Tor is the ‘Mother Hill’, an Iron Age fort atop the famous Tor.

  2. Lathkill Dale: A tranquil trail that winds along the Lathkill River and past exposed limestone cliffs.

  3. Derwent Valley: This trail takes you through rolling hills and along the famous Ladybower reservoir, one of the sources of water for Sheffield.

Best Things to Do in Hathersage: Activities and Tours

  1. Hathersage Swimming Pool: A heated outdoor pool that first opened in 1936, it’s a popular spot with locals and visitors.

  2. Hathersage Moor and Burbage Valley Walk: This relatively strenuous walk passes through beautiful countryside and moorland, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Peak District National Park Tour: Take a guided tour of the national park to explore the rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes of the Peak District.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hathersage

Day 1 - Arrive in Hathersage and explore the village.
Day 2 - Take a hike in the Stanage Edge area followed by a visit to the David Mellor Cutlery Factory and Museum.
Day 3 - Cycle along the Tissington Trail, enjoy the stunning view of the countryside, and the beautiful villages along the way.
Day 4 - Visit the National Tramway Museum and then spend the afternoon hiking at Bamford Edge.
Day 5 - Explore the picturesque Castleton village by foot, visiting the mysterious Blue John Cavern and the imposing Peveril Castle.
Day 6 - Take a guided tour of the Peak District National Park and explore the cultural heritage and natural beauty of the area.
Day 7 - Enjoy a relaxing day by the swimming pool or plan a leisurely walk along the River Derwent.

Food and Drink Scene of Hathersage

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bakewell Pudding: A local specialty, this dessert features flaky pastry, a layer of jam, and an almond-scented filling.

  2. Derbyshire Oatcakes: A savoury treat, typically served with bacon and eggs for breakfast or cheese and vegetables for lunch.

  3. Pork Scratchings: An iconic English pub snack- a crunchy and crispy delicacy made from pork skin and belly.

Local Beverages

  1. Thornbridge Brewery: A local microbrewery with a wide range of beers, including the award-winning Jaipur IPA.

  2. Hathersage Social Club: A popular bar, which serves a range of local drinks as well as spirits and cocktails.

  3. Bradfield Brewery: A traditional real ale brewery with a range of delicious cask ales.

Where to Stay in Hathersage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hathersage offers a variety of lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

The George Hotel

The George Hotel, located in the heart of Hathersage village, is a charming historic hotel with cosy rooms and great food.

YHA Castleton

The YHA Castleton is a popular hostel located in the village of Castleton, just a short drive away from Hathersage. The hostel offers affordable accommodation with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

The Plough Inn

The Plough Inn is a 16th-century coaching inn that has been converted into a cosy and comfortable guesthouse. Located just a short walk from Hathersage village, this inn offers good food and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Hathersage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Local beer from Thornbridge Brewery or Bradfield Brewery

  2. Traditional cutlery from David Mellor

  3. Photographs of Hathersage and the Peak District taken by local photographers

Local Culture and Customs of Hathersage

The Peak District is filled with a proud and rich heritage. Hathersage is home to various customs and traditions, particularly:

  1. May Day Festival: Celebrated on the first Monday of May, this age-old tradition features colourful processions and maypole dancing.

  2. Wakes Week: A yearly celebration, consisting of lively social events, carnival rides, and live music entertainment.

  3. Hathersage Carnival: An annual event, which sees the whole town come together for a day of parades, entertainments, and games.

Best Time to Visit Hathersage

Hathersage can be visited at any time of the year, but the best time to visit is between March and October when the weather is mild, and the countryside is at its most spectacular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hathersage

Do:

  1. Respect the countryside - take all litter with you and stay on footpaths.

  2. Be friendly - the locals are amicable, and they will be happy to offer advice on the best things to see and do in the area.

  3. Be prepared - bring appropriate clothing and footwear, especially when hiking or cycling.

Don’t:

  1. Discard cigarettes or any other items that may start a fire.

  2. Disturb the wildlife- take care when observing wildlife, and keep a safe distance from them.

  3. Stray into private property - always stay on footpaths and other official routes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hathersage

Hathersage is a relatively safe area. However, crime can happen anywhere, so make sure to take care of your valuables and your belongings. Do not leave your valuables in your car or in your room when you’re out. Always lock your room and car before leaving.

Health in Hathersage

The air quality in Hathersage is generally good; however, it’s always best to bring a first aid kit with you, especially when cycling or hiking. In case of medical emergencies, the closest hospital is the Northern General Hospital, Sheffield, which is 25.4 miles away from Hathersage.

Getting to Hathersage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hathersage

  1. Manchester Airport – 44 miles
  2. East Midlands Airport – 38 miles
  3. Leeds Bradford Airport – 48 miles

Bus Stations Near Hathersage

  1. Sheffield Interchange - 9 miles
  2. Chesterfield Bus Station - 12 miles
  3. Bakewell Bus Station - 6 miles

Other Options

You can also travel to Hathersage by train. The closest train station is Hathersage Train Station, which is only a 5-minute walk from the village centre.

How to Get Around Hathersage

The village of Hathersage is compact and easy to explore on foot. There are also local buses that run through the village and the surrounding areas. Cycling is also a popular mode of transportation for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Hathersage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chatsworth House

A stately home, located around 11 miles from Hathersage, offers stunning architectural heritage.

Bakewell

A picturesque town located between Hathersage and Chatsworth. Bakewell is well known for its historic church, the Bakewell Pudding, and the famous Bakewell Market.

Castleton

Castleton is a quaint village famous for its Blue John Caverns and the nearby Mam Tor. An excellent place to buy local crafts and souvenirs.

Conclusion

Hathersage is a hidden gem in Derbyshire, offering unique experiences for visitors. The village has rich heritage, stunning landscapes and a welcoming atmosphere. It is the perfect destination for nature lovers, hiking enthusiasts, history buffs and outdoor adventurers. With its warm hospitality, rich culture, and stunning natural beauty, Hathersage is undoubtedly one of England’s most beautiful destinations.
