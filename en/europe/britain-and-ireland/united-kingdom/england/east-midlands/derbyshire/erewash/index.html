Introduction to Erewash

Nestled in the heart of Derbyshire, East Midlands, Erewash is an idyllic borough renowned for its scenic beauty, rich cultural heritage, and stunning landscapes. The borough comprises several towns, including Ilkeston, Long Eaton, and Sandiacre, each with its unique charm and character.

Erewash boasts a wealth of attractions and activities, from hiking and cycling routes to museums and historical landmarks. With easy access to major cities like Nottingham and Derby, Erewash is the perfect destination for a day trip or a longer stay.

List of Places to Visit in Erewash

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover or adventure seeker, Erewash has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions to explore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Erewash Museum: Housed in a historic house in Ilkeston, the museum showcases the local history, culture and industry of the borough.

  • Morley Windmill: A Grade II listed windmill in Morley, which dates back to 1823, and offers stunning panoramic views of the Erewash Valley.

  • Elvaston Castle: An impressive Gothic country house in Derbyshire, surrounded by sprawling lawns and gardens.

Natural Attractions of Erewash: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shipley Country Park: Set in 700 acres of rolling hills, woodlands, and lakes, the park offers walking trails, cycling paths, and opportunities for fishing, boating, and bird-watching.

  • Erewash Canal: A picturesque canal that stretches for over 11 miles and passes through several towns, offering scenic views and a tranquil atmosphere.

  • Trent Lock: Where the River Trent and River Soar meet, Trent Lock offers a picturesque spot for walking, cycling, and boating, with a number of pubs and restaurants nearby.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Erewash

Erewash offers a host of scenic hiking and biking trails, offering stunning views of the countryside and the rolling hills. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Nutbrook Trail: A 10-mile trail that follows the route of an old railway line and passes through several towns, woodlands, and nature reserves.

  • Erewash Valley Trail: A 15-mile long route that runs between Trent Lock and Shipley Country Park, offering stunning views of the Erewash Valley.

  • Derby Canal Path: A 13-mile route that runs along the Derby Canal, linking several towns and villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Erewash: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing or adventurous experience, Erewash has a range of activities to suit your interests. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Visit a local brewery: Sample some of Erewash’s finest beers and ales on a brewery tour, such as the award-winning Shipstone’s Brewery in Ilkeston.

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Enjoy stunning panoramic views of the Derbyshire countryside on a thrilling hot air balloon ride.

  • Explore local markets: Visit the vibrant markets in Ilkeston and Long Eaton, and grab some fresh produce, handmade crafts, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Erewash

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Erewash, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Explore the local history and culture at Erewash Museum and Morley Windmill.

  • Day 2: Enjoy a day out at Shipley Country Park, with opportunities for hiking, cycling, and boating.

  • Day 3: Visit Elvaston Castle, with its impressive architecture and stunning gardens.

  • Day 4: Go on a brewery tour and sample some local beers and ales.

  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Derbyshire countryside.

  • Day 6: Visit the nearby town of Derby and explore its vibrant culture and heritage.

  • Day 7: Relax and unwind with a leisurely stroll along the Erewash Canal or Trent Lock.

Food and Drink Scene of Erewash

Erewash boasts a rich culinary scene, with a variety of local dishes and regional specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Derbyshire oatcakes: A savory pancake made with oats and served with bacon, eggs, or cheese.

  • Bramley apple pie: A traditional British dessert made with Bramley apples and served with custard or ice-cream.

  • Bakewell pudding: A sweet dessert made with almonds, jam, and puff pastry, originating from the town of Bakewell in Derbyshire.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: A popular type of beer that is made with traditional methods and matured in the cask.

  • Gin: Derbyshire has a number of local gin distilleries, offering unique flavors and refreshing cocktails.

Where to Stay in Erewash: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Erewash offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and interests. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel, a cozy guesthouse, or a budget hostel, you’ll find something to suit your needs. Here are some of the top places to stay in Erewash:

  • Risley Hall Hotel & Spa: A historic 11th-century country house hotel surrounded by picturesque gardens and offering luxury spa facilities.

  • Novotel Nottingham Derby: A modern hotel located near Long Eaton, offering comfortable rooms and a wide range of amenities.

  • The Derbyshire Hotel: A stylish hotel located in South Normanton, midway between Derby and Nottingham, offering contemporary rooms and an on-site spa.

What to Buy in Erewash: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Erewash is home to a range of local products and souvenirs to bring home. Here are some popular items to look out for:

  • Handmade crafts and pottery: Derbyshire has a rich tradition of pottery and craft-making, with a range of unique and quirky items to buy.

  • Real Ale: Bring home a few bottles of locally brewed real ale from one of Erewash’s many breweries.

  • Bakewell Pudding: Buy a freshly baked Bakewell pudding from one of the local bakeries, to savor the taste of Derbyshire at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Erewash

Erewash has a rich cultural heritage, with a range of customs and traditions that are still practiced today. Some of the prominent cultural events and festivals in Erewash include:

  • Erewash Festival of Light: A stunning parade of lanterns held every November, featuring illuminated floats, music, and fireworks.

  • Ilkeston Charter Fair: A medieval fair held in October, with a range of rides, games, and food stalls.

Best Time to Visit Erewash

Erewash has something to offer all year round, but the best time to visit is from late spring to early autumn, when the weather is mild and the countryside is at its most scenic. Peak season runs from June to August, while off-peak season runs from November to February.

Safety and Health

Erewash is considered a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always wise to take precautions while traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and don’t venture into unknown areas at night.

  • Keep your valuables close to you and never leave them unattended.

Getting to Erewash: Transportation Options and Logistics

Erewash is well-connected to major cities and towns in the UK, making it easily accessible by road and public transport.

Airports Near Erewash

The nearest airports to Erewash are East Midlands Airport and Birmingham Airport, both of which offer regular domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Erewash

Erewash is served by several bus stations, including Ilkeston and Long Eaton, which offer regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Erewash is well-connected by road, with easy access to major motorways such as the M1 and M42. There are also several train stations nearby, including Derby and Nottingham, which offer regular connections to major cities and towns across the UK.

How to Get Around in Erewash

Erewash is best explored on foot or by bike, with a range of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the countryside and the rolling hills. There are also several bus and train services that connect the towns and villages within Erewash and nearby cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Erewash: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Erewash is well positioned for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Derby: A vibrant and culturally rich city, located just a short distance from Erewash.

  • Nottingham: A vibrant city renowned for its history, culture, and shopping scene, located about half an hour’s drive from Erewash.

  • The Peak District: This stunning national park is just a stone’s throw from Erewash, and offers a range of activities, from hiking and biking to caving and climbing.
