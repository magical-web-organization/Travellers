Introduction to Melbourne

Melbourne is a charming market town located in the Derbyshire Dales, in the picturesque region of East Midlands in England, United Kingdom. It is a perfect base to explore the stunning Peak District National Park, which is just a stone’s throw away from the town.

Melbourne offers a mix of rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. Visitors can explore the town’s numerous museums and historical sites, indulge in outdoor activities, go on a food tour, or shop for souvenirs and local products. There’s something for everyone in Melbourne.

List of Places to Visit in Melbourne

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Melbourne that caters to all interests and preferences:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Melbourne Hall: This beautiful historic house boasts exquisite architecture and stunning gardens. It’s a must-visit for history buffs.
  • St Michael’s Church: This magnificent church is situated on the town’s main street and has a rich history dating back to the Norman Conquest of England.
  • Thomas Cook Memorial Cottage: This quaint 17th-century cottage was the birthplace of the famous travel entrepreneur, Thomas Cook. Visitors can explore the cottage and learn about Cook’s life and travels.
  • Melbourne Market Place: This vibrant market square offers a range of shops and stalls that sell everything from antiques to fresh produce.

Natural Attractions of Melbourne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Calke Abbey: This picturesque national trust estate offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and features beautiful gardens, an orangery, and a deer park.
  • Staunton Harold Reservoir: This scenic reservoir offers a variety of outdoor activities such as fishing, walking, cycling, and bird watching.
  • Melbourne Pool: This tranquil pool is perfect for a refreshing swim on a hot day or a quiet picnic by the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Melbourne

Melbourne boasts several beautiful hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Here are a few:

  • The Melbourne Pool Walk: This 1.5-mile circular route takes hikers through beautiful woodlands and past the tranquil Melbourne Pool.
  • The Cloud Trail: This scenic 17-mile cycling and walking route connects Derby to Worthington and offers stunning views of the Trent Valley.
  • Ashby Woulds Heritage Trail: This 4.5-mile trail offers scenic views of the Ashby Woulds and passes through the historic village of Ticknall.

Best Things to Do in Melbourne: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to do in Melbourne. Here are some suggestions:

  • Melbourne Food Tour: Take a food tour of Melbourne and sample some of the town’s best local cuisine.
  • Melbourne Ghost Walk: Explore the town’s spooky history on this guided ghost walk.
  • Melbourne Secret Gardens Tour: Discover the hidden gardens of Melbourne on this guided walking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Melbourne

Here is a week-long itinerary to explore the town’s history, culture and natural beauty:

Day 1 Visit Melbourne Hall and Gardens

Food and Drink Scene of Melbourne

Melbourne has a thriving food and drink scene that offers something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bakewell Pudding: This local dessert is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth. It’s a delicious combination of pastry, jam, and almonds.
  • Derbyshire Oatcakes: These savory pancakes made with oats, flour, and milk are a staple of Derbyshire cuisine and are usually served with bacon and cheese.
  • Melton Mowbray Pork Pies: These small pork pies are a delicious, portable snack and are perfect for a picnic or a day out.

Local Beverages

  • Thornbridge Brewery Beers: This award-winning brewery produces some of the best beers in the region and offers guided tours and tastings.
  • Wincle Brewery Ales: This small, family-owned brewery produces delicious craft ales using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Melbourne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Melbourne offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are a few suggestions:

  • The Coach House: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Melbourne and offers comfortable rooms and delicious breakfasts.
  • The Crewe and Harpur: This historic hotel offers cozy rooms, a restaurant, and a pub.
  • YHA National Forest: This hostel is located in a beautifully restored Georgian mansion and offers a range of rooms and dorms.

What to Buy in Melbourne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Melbourne has several shops and markets that sell unique souvenirs and local products. Here are a few ideas:

  • Melbourne Antiques Centre: This large antiques center offers a range of vintage and antique items to browse and buy.
  • The Melbourne Cheese Shop: This artisanal cheese shop sells a variety of locally produced cheeses that make perfect gifts.
  • Wayne Fleet Butchers: This traditional butcher shop offers a range of locally sourced meats, including the famous Melton Mowbray pork pies.

Local Culture and Customs of Melbourne

Melbourne is a charming, small town that takes pride in its unique heritage and traditions. Visitors should keep in mind the following local customs:

  • Punctuality is important, so it’s always best to arrive on time for appointments.
  • Locals take their tea very seriously, and it’s a good idea to take tea breaks in the afternoon.
  • The traditional greeting in Melbourne is a friendly “Hello” or “Alright”.

Best Time to Visit Melbourne

The best time to visit Melbourne is in the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild, and the town is bustling with activity. However, if you want to avoid the crowds, then spring (April-May) and autumn (September-November) are also good times to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Melbourne

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Melbourne:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.
  • Be rude or aggressive towards locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Melbourne is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to keep their belongings safe. It’s also a good idea to have travel insurance before visiting.

Safety in Melbourne

  • Keep your belongings with you at all times.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Melbourne

  • The town has several medical clinics and a hospital in the nearby city of Derby.
  • The tap water is safe to drink.

Getting to Melbourne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Melbourne is well-connected to the rest of the United Kingdom through several transportation options.

Airports Near Melbourne

  • East Midlands Airport (EMA): This airport is located 15 miles from Melbourne and offers flights to several destinations in the UK and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Melbourne

  • Derby Bus Station: This bus station is located 8 miles from Melbourne and has several connections to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Derby, which has connections to major cities in the UK.
  • Car: Melbourne is easily accessible by car and has several car rental options.

How to Get Around in Melbourne

Melbourne is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bicycle. The town also has several taxi services and a bus service that connects to other towns and villages in the region.

Where to Go Next from Melbourne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Melbourne is perfectly situated to explore the stunning Peak District National Park, which offers stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Ashbourne: This charming market town is just a short drive from Melbourne and offers quaint shops and cafes.
  • Matlock Bath: This Victorian spa town offers stunning views and a range of outdoor activities.
  • Chatsworth House: This magnificent stately home is located in the heart of the Peak District and is a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Melbourne is a charming, picturesque town that offers a range of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. From historical sites and outdoor adventures to delicious cuisine and local culture, Melbourne is a perfect destination for anyone looking to explore the beautiful region of East Midlands.
