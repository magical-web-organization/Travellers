Introduction to Cleethorpes

Located in Lincolnshire, in East Midlands, Cleethorpes is a charming coastal town on the east coast of England. The town boasts sandy beaches, scenic surroundings, and plenty of attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you’re seeking an outdoor adventure, a cultural escape, or a relaxing beach holiday, Cleethorpes is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Cleethorpes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cleethorpes Coast Light Railway: This popular attraction is a must-see for train enthusiasts and families alike. The narrow-gauge steam railway runs for almost 2 miles along the coast between Cleethorpes and Humberston.
  • Discovery Centre: Located in the heart of Cleethorpes, the Discovery Centre is an interactive museum dedicated to the town’s rich maritime history.
  • National Fishing Heritage Centre: This award-winning museum is housed in a restored 1950s fish dock and tells the story of Grimsby’s fishing industry.

Natural Attractions of Cleethorpes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cleethorpes Beach: This award-winning sandy beach is ideal for a relaxing day out. Take a stroll along the pier, build sandcastles, or enjoy some ice cream in the sunshine.
  • Humberston Fitties: This pretty holiday village is located on the edge of Cleethorpes and is a great place to explore the sand dunes and salt marshes.
  • Cleethorpes Country Park: This peaceful park covers over 150 acres of woodland, lakes, and meadows and is perfect for a picnic or a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cleethorpes

  • Linear Park to Grimsby: This scenic walk or cycle route takes you along the coast from Cleethorpes to Grimsby, passing Humberston Fitties and the National Fishing Heritage Centre.
  • Cleethorpes Country Park: The park has several walking and cycling trails to explore, ranging from easy strolls to more challenging hikes.
  • Cleethorpes to Tetney Lock: This 10-mile circular route takes you through pretty villages and countryside, along the River Humber and to a nature reserve.

Best Things to Do in Cleethorpes: Activities and Tours

  • Pleasure Island Family Theme Park: This popular attraction features over 50 rides and attractions, including thrilling rollercoasters, water rides, and children’s play areas.
  • The Magical Castle: This indoor adventure playground features a soft play area, laser tag, and mini golf, making it a great option for families on rainy days.
  • Cleethorpes Pier: Take a walk along the pier for stunning views of the coastline, or try your luck at the arcade.

Food and Drink Scene of Cleethorpes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Grimsby is known as the ‘Fish Capital of the World’, so it’s no surprise that fish and chips are a popular dish in Cleethorpes. Local favorites include Steels and Papas.
  • Lincolnshire Sausages: These famous sausages are made from locally sourced pork and have a unique tangy flavor.
  • Plum Bread: This sweet, fruit-laden bread is a Lincolnshire specialty, often served toasted with butter.

Local Beverages

  • Bateman’s Ale: This popular beer is brewed in nearby Wainfleet and is known for its distinctive flavor.
  • Sloe Gin: Made from locally grown sloes, this fruit liqueur is a must-try for those with a sweet tooth.

Where to Stay in Cleethorpes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Kingsway Hotel: This seafront hotel is located just steps away from the beach and has spacious rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • The Comat: A family-run hotel, The Comat offers cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • The beach hut: This stylish and modern bed and breakfast is located just a stone’s throw away from Cleethorpes Beach.

What to Buy in Cleethorpes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lincolnshire Poacher Cheese: This award-winning cheese is made using traditional methods from locally sourced milk and is a popular gift to bring back home.
  • Artisan Food Products: Cleethorpes has a fantastic range of artisan food producers, including jams, chutneys, and cakes, all made using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Souvenir Key Ring: Grab a souvenir keyring to remind you of your trip to Cleethorpes.

Local Culture and Customs of Cleethorpes

Cleethorpes has a rich maritime history, and its culture is heavily influenced by fishing and the sea. The town is also known for its traditional seaside attractions, such as amusement arcades, candy floss, and ice cream. Visitors should take the time to explore the town’s rich history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Cleethorpes

The best time to visit Cleethorpes is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and the sea is calm. However, the town is also lovely in the spring, with blooming flowers and the start of the cycling season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cleethorpes

Do:

  • Do visit the National Fishing Heritage Centre: This award-winning museum is a must-see for anyone visiting Cleethorpes, telling the story of Grimsby’s fishing industry.
  • Do Walk Along the Pier: Cleethorpes’s pier offers stunning views of the coastline, and it’s a great way to take in the sea air.
  • Do Try the Fish and Chips: Grimsby is known as the ‘Fish Capital of the World’, so it’s no surprise that fish and chips are a popular dish in Cleethorpes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Forget to Check the Tide Times: The sea at Cleethorpes can be dangerous at high tide, so it’s important to check the tide times before heading to the beach.
  • Don’t Be Disrespectful of Local Customs: Cleethorpes has a rich maritime history, and its culture is heavily influenced by fishing and the sea. It’s essential to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cleethorpes

Cleethorpes is a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take the usual crime-prevention measures, such as not leaving valuables unattended or visible in cars.

Health in Cleethorpes

Cleethorpes has a range of medical facilities and pharmacies for visitors who require medical assistance. Tourists should also ensure they have adequate travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Cleethorpes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cleethorpes

  • Humberside Airport: The closest airport to Cleethorpes, located just 14 miles away, has regular flights to and from several UK and European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Cleethorpes

  • Stagecoach: Stagecoach operates local bus services in and around Cleethorpes.

Other Transportation Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is Grimsby Town, which offers regular services to and from Lincoln, Sheffield, and Doncaster.
  • Car: Cleethorpes is easily accessible by car, with the A180 providing excellent road links to nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Cleethorpes

Cleethorpes is a small town, and many attractions can be explored on foot or by bike. However, visitors can also take advantage of the local bus services or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Cleethorpes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lincolnshire has a wealth of attractions and destinations to explore, including:

  • Lincoln: This historic city is located just over an hour’s drive from Cleethorpes and boasts a stunning cathedral, medieval architecture, and a vibrant food and drink scene.
  • The Lincolnshire Wolds: This rolling landscape covers over 560 square km of grassy hills, green valleys, and picturesque villages, ideal for outdoor lovers and walkers.
  • Donna Nook Nature Reserve: This coastal nature reserve is home to a colony of grey seals, and visitors can watch the seals from a safe distance during the breeding season.

Conclusion

Cleethorpes is a charming coastal town with plenty to offer visitors. From its rich maritime history to its natural attractions and exciting theme parks, there is something for everyone. So pack your bags, grab your sun cream, and head to Cleethorpes for an unforgettable holiday.
