Introduction to Boston

Located in the county of Lincolnshire in the East Midlands of England, Boston is a peaceful town that boasts a rich history and picturesque landscapes. With its stunning architecture, charming markets, and quaint shops, Boston is a hidden gem that is often overlooked but definitely worth discovering.

List of Places to Visit in Boston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Botolph’s Church: Known locally as “Boston Stump,” St. Botolph’s Church is a landmark not to be missed. Climb its 209-foot tall tower for breathtaking views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Boston Guildhall: A historical building that houses a museum dedicated to the town’s pilgrim heritage. It also hosts concerts and exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Fydell House: This beautiful Georgian mansion showcases the grand lifestyle of Boston’s wealthy 18th-century residents.

Natural Attractions of Boston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pilgrim Fathers Memorial: A stunning monument that commemorates the departure of the Pilgrim Fathers to America in 1620.
  • Witham Way Country Park: With its riverside walks and cycle routes, this park is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts looking to unwind amidst nature’s beauty.
  • Maud Foster Windmill: A photogenic, fully operational windmill that dates back to the early 19th century. Climb to the top of the mill for panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boston

  • The Viking Way: This long-distance trail covers 147 miles, starting from the Humber Bridge and ending in Rutland Water.
  • The Macmillan Way: A scenic route that covers 290 miles from Boston to Dorset, passing through stunning countryside and charming market towns.

Best Things to Do in Boston: Activities and Tours

  • Boston Market: Visit Boston’s traditional outdoor market that has been running since the 17th century. It is held every Wednesday and Saturday in the Market Place.
  • Boston Bowl: A fun-filled activity center with ten-pin bowling, laser quest, pool tables, and an arcade for all ages to enjoy.
  • Bain Valley Fisheries: For fishing enthusiasts who want to relax amidst the scenic beauty of Boston’s countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boston

Day Itinerary
1 Explore the town center, visit St. Botolph’s Church, and go shopping in the Market Place.
2 Discover the Pilgrim Fathers Memorial, enjoy a riverside walk at Witham Way Country Park, and have lunch in the park’s café.
3 Visit the Boston Guildhall Museum, take a leisurely bike ride along The Macmillan Way, and have a pub lunch in Fosdyke.
4 Discover the historic Fydell House, have a picnic in Central Park, and go shopping in the independent stores on Strait’s Quay.
5 Take a fishing trip at Bain Valley Fisheries, have an afternoon tea in a local café, and enjoy a late-evening concert at the Blackfriars.
6 Go ten-pin bowling at Boston Bowl, have a beer tasting in the local brewery, and catch a film at the West End Cinema.
7 Visit Maud Foster Windmill, go hiking along The Viking Way, and have a farewell dinner in one of the town’s award-winning restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Boston

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lincolnshire Sausage: A delicious local specialty that is typically made of coarsely ground pork and is seasoned with sage.
  • Plum Bread: A sweet bread that is traditionally made with dried fruits, cinnamon, and sugar.
  • Pea Soup: A hearty soup that is made of fresh green peas, ham, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Lincolnshire Plum Loaf: A delicious bread that is traditionally served with a glass of mead, a honey-based alcoholic drink.
  • Lafford Cheese: An award-winning, locally produced cheese that pairs perfectly with a glass of red wine.

Where to Stay in Boston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The White Hart Hotel: A charming hotel located in the town center that offers spacious rooms and delicious food.
  • Boston Lodge Guest House: A cozy townhouse that offers comfortable accommodation and personalized services.
  • The New England Hotel: A historical Victorian-era hotel that has been recently refurbished and offers a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Boston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Boston Sweets and Toffees: A local candy shop that offers handmade sweets and toffees that are perfect for a sweet souvenir.
  • Boston Breweries: A local brewery that offers a range of craft beers that make for a delicious souvenir.
  • Lincolnshire Cheese: A delicious souvenir that is available in several varieties, including the award-winning Lafford Cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Boston

Boston has a rich history and is known for its traditional events and festivities. May Day celebrations, the Christmas Market and summer music festivals are some of the events guests can enjoy.

Best Time to Visit Boston

The best time to visit Boston is between April and September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed. However, the Christmas Market in December is a must-visit for those who love the festive atmosphere, and the summer music festivals in August make it an exciting time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boston

Do:

  • Try the local food, including the famous Lincolnshire Sausage.
  • Explore the town’s historical landmarks, including the St. Botolph’s Church and Boston Guildhall.
  • Take a scenic stroll along the riverside in Witham Way Country Park.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places, including parks and walking trails.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit areas during unsociable hours.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boston

Boston is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take care while using public transport at night and avoid leaving their belongings unattended in public areas.

Health in Boston

The UK maintains high health and safety standards. Visitors are advised to carry relevant medication if necessary and keep hydrated to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Boston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Boston is easily accessible and conveniently located in Lincolnshire with regular transportation options.

Airports Near Boston

The nearest airport to Boston is East Midlands Airport (67 miles) with connections to major European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Boston

National Express operates frequent services from London Victoria Coach Station to Boston Bus Station.

Other Options

Travelers can also take the train from Nottingham or Skegness to Boston Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Boston

Boston is small enough to be explored on foot. The town also has an excellent bus network, which provides visitors with easy access to all the major attractions. Bike rentals are also available for those who want to explore the beautiful countryside.

Where to Go Next from Boston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lincoln: A beautiful and historic city with a stunning castle and cathedral.
  • Skegness: A popular seaside town with a variety of attractions and entertainment.
  • Grantham: An elegant market town with a rich history and expanding cultural scene.

Conclusion

Boston is a gem of a town that has something for everyone. Whether you are a culture enthusiast, outdoor adventurer, or foodie, Boston will not disappoint. With its mix of history and charm, Boston is a perfect destination to take in some stunning scenery and experience the thrills of small-town local culture. Plan your trip to Boston today!
