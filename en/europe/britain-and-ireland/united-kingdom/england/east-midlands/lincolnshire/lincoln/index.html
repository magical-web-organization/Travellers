Introduction to Lincoln

Lincoln, located in Lincolnshire in the East Midlands, England, is a small city with a rich history dating back to the Roman era. From its towering Gothic cathedral to its medieval castle, the city has many cultural and historical attractions to offer. Along with its stunning natural scenery, including the Lincolnshire Wolds and the River Witham, Lincoln is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. With a variety of activities and experiences to offer, Lincoln is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the beauty of England.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln

Whether you are interested in exploring historical and cultural landmarks or natural attractions, Lincoln has plenty to offer.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lincoln has an impressive collection of museums, galleries, and historical places that display its rich heritage and culture.

  • Lincoln Cathedral: A masterpiece of Gothic architecture, this towering cathedral is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city. You can climb the 338 steps of the tower to get a stunning panoramic view of the city and the countryside.
  • Lincoln Castle: This medieval fortress is home to one of only four surviving copies of the Magna Carta and a Victorian prison. The castle also offers panoramic views of the city from its battlements.
  • The Collection Museum: This award-winning museum houses an impressive collection of art and artifacts from Lincoln’s rich past. The display includes Roman artifacts, medieval manuscripts, and works by contemporary artists.
  • The Museum of Lincolnshire Life: This open-air museum showcases the social and cultural history of Lincolnshire through exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Ellis Windmill: This 18th-century windmill is one of the few working windmills in the UK. You can see how grain was milled in the past and learn about the vital role windmills played in Lincoln’s economic growth.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lincoln is surrounded by natural beauty, from rolling hills to stunning waterways. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Lincolnshire Wolds: This Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty boasts some of England’s most scenic views and picturesque towns and villages. You can enjoy hiking, cycling, or horseback riding through the countryside.
  • Whisby Nature Park: This wildlife reserve is home to various species of birds, mammals, and insects. You can walk along trails or cycle through the park to explore the beautiful ponds and trees.
  • The River Witham: This river offers a relaxing boat trip along its scenic route, or you can explore its banks by foot or cycle. There are several pubs and cafes along the river where you can take a break and enjoy the views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln

Lincolnshire offers excellent opportunities for hiking, biking, and horse riding through the scenic countryside. Here are some of the popular routes to explore:

  • The Viking Way: This 147-mile trail runs through the Lincolnshire Wolds to Rutland, offering stunning views of the coast, hills, and valleys.
  • The Water Rail Way: This 33-mile trail follows the former Lincoln to Boston rail line along the River Witham, with breathtaking views of the countryside and wildlife.
  • The Sustrans Route 1: This 24-mile trail connects Lincoln and Newark along quiet country roads with beautiful views of Lincoln Cathedral and Trent Valley.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln: Activities and Tours

Lincoln offers a wide variety of activities and tours for visitors, including:

  • Ghost Walks: Join a ghost tour to explore the spooky side of Lincoln’s history, from haunted pubs to dark alleys.
  • Steep Hill: Discover the historic cobbled street that leads from the Cathedral Quarter to the High Street. The street boasts a variety of independent shops and cafes.
  • Shopping: Visit the Bailgate area for boutique shopping and explore the High Street for high-end brands and department stores.
  • Lincoln Christmas Market: This annual market is the oldest in the UK and is held at the Lincoln Castle and Cathedral.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lincoln

  • Day 1: Visit Lincoln Cathedral and Castle, explore the Bailgate area for shopping and brunch, and enjoy a relaxing boat trip along the River Witham.
  • Day 2: Discover the mysteries of Lincoln’s haunted past with a ghost tour around the city center, followed by lunch at a traditional English pub.
  • Day 3: Explore the scenic Lincolnshire Wolds with a cycling or hiking tour through the rolling hills and picturesque villages.
  • Day 4: Visit The Collection Museum and The Museum of Lincolnshire Life to learn about the city’s rich heritage and culture.
  • Day 5: Explore the Steep Hill area for boutique shopping and stop for afternoon tea at one of the quaint cafes.
  • Day 6: Visit Ellis Windmill and Whisby Nature Park for an insight into Lincolnshire’s rural history and natural beauty.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby towns and villages like Gainsborough, Louth, and Spalding.

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln

Lincoln has a rich food and drink scene, with a variety of local dishes to try and pubs and bars to visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lincolnshire Sausages: These pork sausages are flavored with herbs and spices and are a staple of the local cuisine.
  • Stuffed Chine: This pork dish consists of cured and boiled pork with parsley in a gelatinous coating.
  • Lincolnshire Plum Loaf: This fruitcake is made with plums and spices and is a popular dessert in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Bateman’s Brewery: This family-run brewery produces a range of ales, including the popular XXXB ale, which can be found in many pubs around the city.
  • Lincolnshire Poacher Cheese: This handmade hard cheese is produced in the nearby village of Ulceby and pairs well with a pint of ale.

Where to Stay in Lincoln: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lincoln has a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference.

  • The White Hart Hotel: This historic hotel is situated at the heart of the city center and offers luxurious rooms and excellent dining options.
  • The Lincoln Hotel: This hotel overlooks the Cathedral and offers contemporary rooms with stunning views and a rooftop restaurant.
  • Travelodge Lincoln City Centre: This budget-friendly hotel offers cozy rooms and a central location.
  • YHA Lincoln: This hostel is located in the Castle Ward of Lincoln and offers affordable and comfortable dormitory-style accommodation.

What to Buy in Lincoln: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lincolnshire Poacher Cheese: This award-winning cheese is a must-try souvenir and is widely available in local shops and markets.
  • Lincoln Imp Souvenirs: The small mythical creature carved into stonework on the Cathedral is a popular souvenir, and you can find it on various products like keyrings, mugs, and t-shirts.
  • Lincolnshire Plum Bread: This sweet bread contains dried plums and is a delicious local delicacy that you can take home as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln

Lincoln has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and its people are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Be polite and courteous in public places.
  • Tipping is a common practice in restaurants and cafes.
  • Dress appropriately for visiting religious sites, such as the Cathedral.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln

The best time to visit Lincoln depends on your preference for weather and events. The summer months (June to August) are warm and sunny, and the city hosts several outdoor events like music festivals and food fairs. The winter months (December to February) are cold and frosty, but the festive season brings a magical vibe to the city with the Christmas market and decorations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln

Do:

  • Take a walking tour around the city to explore its hidden gems.
  • Chat with the friendly locals and learn about their experiences and culture.
  • Take a selfie with the Lincoln Cathedral and Castle as a backdrop.
  • Respect the heritage and history of the city.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Litter in public places or damage historic structures.
  • Disrespect locals or use offensive language.
  • Overindulge in alcohol or drugs in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln

Lincoln is a safe city, but visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone in isolated places at night.
  • Keep valuable items like cameras and phones in a secure place.
  • Follow traffic rules and use designated pedestrian crossings.

Health in Lincoln

Visitors to Lincoln should ensure they have adequate medical insurance and consult their doctor before traveling, especially if they have any pre-existing medical conditions. The city has several medical facilities and hospitals to cater to any emergency or medical needs.

Getting to Lincoln: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lincoln is well-connected to major cities and towns in the UK, and visitors can reach the city by various modes of transport.

Airports Near Lincoln

  • Humberside Airport: Located 25 miles from Lincoln, this airport offers regular flights to various domestic and international destinations.
  • East Midlands Airport: This airport is 50 miles from Lincoln and offers regular flights to various international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Lincoln

  • Lincoln Bus Station: This station offers local and national bus services, including the National Express.

Other Options

  • Car: Lincoln is accessible by car via the A1 and A46 motorways.
  • Train: Lincoln Central railway station offers regular train services to major cities like London, Nottingham, and Sheffield.

How to Get Around in Lincoln

Lincoln is a small city, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use public transport like buses and taxis to navigate around the city.

Where to Go Next from Lincoln: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lincoln is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Nottingham: This vibrant city is known for its Robin Hood connections, historic architecture, and cultural events.
  • Skegness: This seaside resort town offers sandy beaches, amusement parks, and a variety of water sports.
  • Stamford: This medieval market town is famous for its architecture, museums, and beautiful countryside.

Conclusion

Lincoln is a hidden gem in the heart of England with a rich culture, heritage, and natural beauty to explore. With its stunning landmarks, beautiful countryside, and vibrant food and drink scene, Lincoln is a must-visit destination for any traveler. Whether you are interested in exploring historical sites, hiking through the hills, or savoring local cuisine, Lincoln has something to offer for every taste and preference. So pack your bags and head to Lincoln for a memorable trip of a lifetime!
