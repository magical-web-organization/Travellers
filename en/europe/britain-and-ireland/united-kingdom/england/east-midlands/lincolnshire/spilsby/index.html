Introduction to Spilsby

Spilsby is a charming market town located in the heart of Lincolnshire. Surrounded by beautiful countryside, this historic town is filled with picturesque architecture and fascinating landmarks that will transport you back in time. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or simply want to get away from it all, Spilsby has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Spilsby has a rich history, and you can explore it through the many museums and historical places around the town. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Spilsby Theatre: The Spilsby Theatre, which dates back to 1828, is a beautifully preserved playhouse that offers a range of performances throughout the year. From classic plays to modern productions, there’s always something to see at the Spilsby Theatre.

  • Sir John Franklin Museum: Sir John Franklin was an Arctic explorer who was born in Spilsby. The museum charts his life and achievements, as well as exploring the history of the local area. With interactive exhibits and fascinating artifacts, the Sir John Franklin Museum is a must-visit for history buffs.

  • Lincolnshire Wolds Railway: The Lincolnshire Wolds Railway is a heritage railway that offers visitors the chance to ride restored steam engines through the beautiful Lincolnshire countryside. With a range of events and activities throughout the year, this is a great day out for all the family.

Natural Attractions of Spilsby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spilsby is surrounded by beautiful countryside, making it the perfect destination for anyone who loves the great outdoors. From scenic walks to thrilling bike rides, there’s something for everyone. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Lincolnshire Wolds: The Lincolnshire Wolds are an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, and it’s easy to see why. With rolling hills, beautiful woodland, and idyllic valleys, this is the perfect place for a leisurely walk or a challenging hike.

  • Cadwell Park: If you’re a thrill-seeker, Cadwell Park is the place for you. This famous racing circuit hosts a range of events throughout the year, including the British Superbike Championship.

  • Skegness Beach: Just a short drive from Spilsby, Skegness Beach is one of the UK’s best-loved seaside resorts. With miles of golden sand and a range of attractions and amenities, it’s the perfect place to spend a day by the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spilsby

Spilsby and the Lincolnshire Wolds offer some of the best walking and biking routes in the UK. Whether you’re looking for an easy stroll or a challenging trek, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are our top picks:

  • Wolds Way: The Wolds Way is a 79-mile walking route that takes in some of the most stunning scenery in the Lincolnshire Wolds. With rolling hills, picturesque villages, and beautiful countryside, it’s a must for any serious walker.

  • Viking Way: The Viking Way is a 147-mile biking route that runs from the Humber Bridge to Rutland Water. With beautiful countryside and historic sites along the way, it’s a great way to explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Spilsby: Activities and Tours

Spilsby and the surrounding area offer a range of activities and tours that are sure to keep everyone entertained. Here are some of the best:

  • Gunby Estate and Hall: The Gunby Estate and Hall is a beautiful stately home surrounded by stunning gardens and woodland. With a range of events and activities throughout the year, it’s a great day out for all the family.

  • Lincolnshire Aviation Heritage Centre: The Lincolnshire Aviation Heritage Centre is a fascinating museum that celebrates the county’s aviation history. With a range of exhibits and interactive displays, it’s the perfect place for anyone interested in planes and flying.

  • Alford Windmill: The Alford Windmill is a beautifully preserved windmill that dates back to the 19th century. With guided tours and fascinating exhibits, it’s a great way to learn about the history of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Spilsby

Spilsby is home to some fantastic food and drink, with a range of local produce and traditional dishes on offer. Here are some of our favourites:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lincolnshire Sausages: These delicious sausages are made using locally sourced pork and are a must-try for any foodie visiting Spilsby.

  • Plum Bread: This sweet fruit bread is a Lincolnshire speciality and is perfect with a cup of tea or coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Batemans Brewery: Batemans Brewery is one of the oldest family-run breweries in the UK and offers a range of traditional ales and beers.

  • Lincolnshire Plum Liquor: This fruity liqueur is made using locally sourced plums and is the perfect souvenir to take home.

  • The Red Lion Inn: The Red Lion Inn is a cosy guesthouse located just outside of Spilsby. With comfortable rooms and a warm welcome, it’s the perfect place to stay if you’re looking for a relaxing break.

What to Buy in Spilsby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spilsby is home to a range of local produce and traditional crafts, making it the perfect place to pick up a souvenir or two. Here are some of our favourites:

Local Culture and Customs of Spilsby

Spilsby has a rich history and culture, and there are a number of customs and traditions unique to the area. One of the most famous is the annual Spilsby Show, which has been running for over 100 years and is a celebration of the town’s farming heritage.

Best Time to Visit Spilsby

While Spilsby is beautiful all year round, the best time to visit is in the summer months. This is when the countryside is at its most spectacular, and there are a range of outdoor events and activities to enjoy.

Getting to Spilsby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spilsby is well-connected to the rest of the UK, with a range of transportation options available. Here’s what you need to know:

  • Airports Near Spilsby: The nearest airports to Spilsby are Humberside Airport (42 miles) and East Midlands Airport (80 miles).

  • Bus Stations Near Spilsby: The main bus stations near Spilsby are in Boston (12 miles) and Skegness (13 miles).

  • Other Options: There are also a number of train stations within easy reach of Spilsby, including Boston and Skegness.
