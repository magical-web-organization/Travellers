Introduction to Grantham

Grantham is a picturesque market town located in Lincolnshire, East Midlands, England. It is best known as the hometown of former British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher and renowned physicist Sir Isaac Newton. Visitors are drawn to Grantham for its rich history, charming architecture, beautiful green spaces, and wide range of attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Grantham.

List of Places to Visit in Grantham

Whether you’re in Grantham for a day trip or a longer vacation, these top attractions are not to be missed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Grantham Museum: Discover the town’s fascinating history at this charming museum, which features exhibits on the town’s famous inhabitants, industries, and architecture.
  2. Belton House: A stunning 17th-century country house with beautiful gardens, walking trails, and a deer park.
  3. Woolsthorpe Manor: This is the birthplace and family home of Sir Isaac Newton, where he developed many of his scientific theories and ideas.
  4. St. Wulfram’s Church: A beautiful Gothic church with stunning stained-glass windows, and the highest spire in Lincolnshire.
  5. Easton Walled Gardens: These picturesque gardens were abandoned for over 50 years before being restored and opened to the public. Now, visitors can enjoy over 12 acres of historic gardens, woodland trails, and a tearoom.

Natural Attractions of Grantham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Grantham Canal: Explore the peaceful waterways of the Grantham Canal, either by foot or by taking a guided boat tour.
  2. Ancaster Leisure: Get your adrenaline pumping with a range of outdoor activities such as high ropes, paintball, and quad biking.
  3. Hough on the Hill Windmill: Take a scenic stroll to this historic 19th-century windmill and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grantham

Grantham is surrounded by beautiful countryside, and there are plenty of walking and biking trails to explore. Here are some favorites:

  1. Viking Way: An 80-mile walking trail that passes through Grantham on its way from the Humber Bridge to the Lincolnshire coast.
  2. Grantham to Harlaxton Trail: A scenic walking trail that takes you from Grantham’s town center to the nearby village of Harlaxton, passing by beautiful countryside, historic architecture, and the Grantham Canal.
  3. The Old Wood Road: A peaceful walking trail that takes you through picturesque farmland and woods.

Best Things to Do in Grantham: Activities and Tours

  1. Grantham Ghost Walks: Take a spooky tour of the historic town center and hear chilling tales of ghosts, witches, and other supernatural entities.
  2. Grantham Beer Tour: Explore the town’s thriving brewery scene on a guided tour that takes you to some of the best pubs and microbreweries in town.
  3. Caythorpe Dash Charity Bike Ride: An annual charity bike ride that takes you through some of the most beautiful countryside in the region, with routes ranging from 20-100 miles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grantham

To make the most of your time in Grantham, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Belton House, walk in the gardens or take a boat trip on the lake. Day 2: Go for a hike or bike ride on one of the nearby trails, like the Viking Way. Day 3: Explore Grantham Museum. Day 4: Take a day trip to the seaside town of Skegness. Day 5: Visit Woolsthorpe Manor and learn more about Sir Isaac Newton and his theories. Day 6: Discover the historical sights and sounds of St. Wulfram’s Church. Day 7: Relax at Easton Walled Gardens, which has beautiful gardens and tea rooms.

Food and Drink Scene of Grantham

Grantham has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of local eateries and pubs offering delicious meals and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lincolnshire Sausage: A traditional pork sausage that’s a staple of the local cuisine.
  2. Cheese and Onion Pie: A savory pie made from buttery puff pastry, farmhouse cheese, and tangy onions.
  3. Pork Pie: A classic British dish that consists of pork, seasoned with various spices and herbs, wrapped in a pastry shell.

Local Beverages

  1. Bateman’s Ale: A family-run brewery located in nearby Wainfleet, they produce an array of cask ales, including the award-winning XB.
  2. Great Taste Award-Winning Red Wine: Made from grapes grown at Pinot Noir, which is a local vineyard near Grantham.

Where to Stay in Grantham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grantham has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some recommended options:

  1. The Angel and Royal Hotel: Located in the heart of Grantham, this historic hotel has been welcoming guests since the 12th century and offers luxurious accommodations, a restaurant and a bar.
  2. Urban Hotel Grantham: A contemporary hotel that boasts an indoor swimming pool, gym, and spa facilities.
  3. Hostel Belton: This hostel is set in the beautiful parkland setting of Belton House and is a short walk from the center of Grantham.

What to Buy in Grantham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Belvoir Castle Spirits: Award-winning spirits made from botanicals grown at Belvoir Castle, near Grantham.
  2. Grantham Gingerbread: A delicious sweet treat made from a traditional recipe that dates back over 200 years.
  3. Spire Gift Shop: A charming gift shop full of unique souvenirs and handmade items from local craftspeople.

Local Culture and Customs of Grantham

In Grantham, visitors can expect a warm welcome and a friendly community. The town has a rich history and is very proud of its heritage, so be sure to take the time to learn about local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Grantham

The best time to visit Grantham is during the summer months, from June through August, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor events and activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grantham

Do:

  1. Do take a guided tour of the town center to learn its rich history.
  2. Do try the local cuisine and drinks, such as pork pies and Bateman’s Ale.
  3. Do spend time in the town’s beautiful gardens and green spaces.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or cause damage to the town’s historic architecture.
  2. Don’t be afraid to ask for directions or recommendations from locals.
  3. Don’t forget to check opening hours and admission fees for attractions before you visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grantham

Grantham is generally a safe town, however, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Grantham

Grantham has a range of medical services available, including doctors, dentists, and pharmacies. In case of emergency, always dial 999.

Getting to Grantham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grantham

The nearest international airports are East Midlands Airport and London Stansted Airport, which are both just over an hour’s drive from Grantham.

Bus Stations Near Grantham

Grantham Bus Station is located in the town center, with regular services to nearby towns and cities such as Lincoln, Nottingham, and Peterborough.

Other Options

Grantham is also easily accessible by car or train, with direct trains from London and many other major UK cities.

How to Get Around in Grantham

Grantham is a walkable town, and most attractions and sights are located within walking distance of the town center. Taxis are also readily available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Grantham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Lincoln: Just a short bus or train ride away, Lincoln is a beautiful historic city with a stunning cathedral, castle, and vibrant nightlife.
  2. Nottingham: Famous for its links to Robin Hood, Nottingham is a bustling city with plenty of attractions, from its impressive castle to its excellent shopping and entertainment venues.
  3. Derbyshire Dales: Explore the beautiful scenery of the Derbyshire Dales, including the Peak District National Park.

Conclusion

Grantham is a charming town with a rich and fascinating history, beautiful green spaces, and a range of attractions to suit all interests. From exploring the town’s museums and historical sites to enjoying outdoor adventures and trying delicious local cuisine and drinks, there’s something for everyone in Grantham. Start planning your trip today and discover all that this charming market town has to offer.
