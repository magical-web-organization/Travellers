Introduction to Grimsby

Located in Lincolnshire, in East Midlands, in England, in the United Kingdom, Grimsby is a seaport town with a rich history and stunning scenery. The town offers a unique blend of natural and man-made attractions that appeal to a variety of visitors, including families, nature enthusiasts, and history buffs. Here is a comprehensive tour guide to help you plan your next visit to Grimsby.

List of Places to Visit in Grimsby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Grimsby has an impressive collection of museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s rich maritime and fishing heritage. Among the places to visit are the following:

  • Grimsby Fishing Heritage Centre: This award-winning museum is a must-visit in Grimsby. It offers a fascinating insight into the town’s fishing industry history with interactive exhibits, displays, and films.
  • St James’ Church: A striking medieval church that boasts of impressive stained-glass windows and a medieval font.
  • Wellow House: A restored 17th-century merchant’s house that now serves as a museum and library, showcasing Georgian and Victorian life.

Natural Attractions of Grimsby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grimsby does not disappoint when it comes to natural attractions. Visiting these sites gives you a glimpse of the town’s lovely outdoor scenery and wildlife. The following are three of the best outdoor adventures you can have in Grimsby:

  • Cleethorpes Country Park: A stunning park with over 160 acres of open space, ponds, and woodlands, perfect for a relaxing walk, birdwatching, and picnicking.
  • Weelsby Woods: A beautiful park and woodland covering over 50 acres with a children’s play area and a large pond with a viewing platform.
  • Amphibian Arc: A unique nature reserve that focuses on the conservation of amphibians and reptiles, covering a total of 16 acres. Visitors can take a tour and observe these creatures in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grimsby

Grimsby has several hiking and biking trails that offer an exhilarating experience. Here are some time-tested routes that you can take in Grimsby:

  • Wolds Way: A 79-mile trail that starts at Hessle and ends in Filey, passing through the picturesque hills of the Yorkshire Wolds.
  • Louth Navigation: This trail follows the route of a former canal that was used to transport goods between Louth and Tetney Lock via the River Louth.
  • Lincolnshire Wolds Cycle Route: This circular route starts and ends at Horncastle and covers around 69 miles of the beautiful Lincolnshire countryside.

Best Things to Do in Grimsby: Activities and Tours

Besides the scenic views and cultural attractions, Grimsby offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. A few suggestions are as follows:

  • Lincolnshire Wolds Railway: A volunteer-run heritage railway that offers visitors a unique experience of the 1950s railway history.
  • Golfing: Grimsby boasts several golf courses, such as Waltham Windmill Golf Club, Cleethorpes Golf Club, and Grimsby Golf Club, which offer visitors varying experiences depending on their skill level.
  • Fishing Tours: The town’s fishing history is legendary. Tour companies in Grimsby offer fishing tours that take visitors through the Humber estuary and the North Sea to catch cod, haddock, and mackerel.

Food and Drink Scene of Grimsby

Grimsby is known for its local food and drink culture, with seafood being the highlight of its cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grimsby Smoked Haddock: This dish, which is oven-roasted with breadcrumbs and cheese, is one of the town’s main delicacies. You can find it on the menu of most local restaurants.
  • Grimsby Traditional Fish and Chips: There are plenty of fish and chip shops in Grimsby, and trying this dish is a right of passage. You can’t go wrong with a takeaway box and eat it on the beach.
  • Lincolnshire Sausages: These sausages are made from locally sourced pork and have a distinctive herb flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Bateman’s English Ale: A local brewery in Wainfleet, near Grimsby, produces the English Ale, which is a rich and malty beer.
  • Staller’s Original Cider: A refreshing drink made from locally-sourced apples with a crisp and slightly sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Grimsby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grimsby caters to all budgets, with a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few suggestions based on budget:

  • The St. James Hotel: A luxury three-star hotel located in the heart of Grimsby.
  • The Yarborough Hotel: A 19th-century hotel with a modern feel, located close to Grimsby’s town center.
  • The County Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with basic amenities, a five-minute walk from Grimsby railway station.

What to Buy in Grimsby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Grimsby has plenty of shops and boutiques to explore. Here are some ideas:

  • Grimsby Fish Market: The fish market sells a range of seafood, ideal to take home as a gift or cook as a delicious meal.
  • St. James’ Square: A historic indoor market that sells a range of local and handmade products.
  • Freeman Street Market: A bustling indie street market selling a range of food and vintage items.

Local Culture and Customs

Grimsby has a relaxed and friendly culture, with locals welcoming visitors with open arms. You may notice some of the following customs and traditions during your visit:

  • Maypole Dancing: Maypole dancing is a traditional English custom in which a decorated pole is erected and people dance around it.
  • Fishermen’s Heritage: As a seaport town, the locals have a deep appreciation for the fishing heritage that dates back centuries.
  • Victorian Architecture: Grimsby has several old Victorian-style buildings, reflecting the town’s architectural history.

Best Time to Visit Grimsby

Grimsby is a year-round destination, with each season offering something unique to visitors. However, visiting in the summer months is advised, primarily because of the warmer weather and more extended daylight hours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grimsby

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Grimsby has some of the best seafood and traditional Lincolnshire dishes.
  • Take a Fishing Tour: Going on a fishing tour is a unique experience that allows you to explore Grimsby’s fishing heritage and enjoy the beautiful coastline.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Grimsby has a range of gardens, parks, and nature reserves that offer some fantastic outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Litter the Beaches: Grimsby beaches are beautiful, keeping them clean and litter-free is essential to preserving their natural beauty.
  • Disrespect the Fishing Heritage: Grimsby’s fishing heritage is a significant part of the town’s history, show respect to local fishermen and their customs.
  • Drive Without Valid License: Driving without a valid license is illegal and can lead to hefty fines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grimsby

Grimsby is relatively safe. However, you should take normal precautions, such as keeping an eye on your valuables and being mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Grimsby

Grimsby has a robust healthcare system, with several hospitals and medical centers catering to medical needs. It’s advisable to carry your insurance details with you.

Getting to Grimsby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Grimsby is located on the coast, just an hour’s drive from the Hull ferry port and a two-hour drive from the M25, making it accessible by road. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Grimsby

The nearest airports are Humberside Airport (HUY) and Robin Hood Doncaster Sheffield Airport (DSA). Both are about an hour’s drive from Grimsby.

Bus Stations Near Grimsby

The town has a centrally located bus station that serves regional and local destinations.

Other Options

The Lincolnshire Coast Light Railway is a heritage railway line that runs from Cleethorpes to Humberston. Catching the railway is an excellent way to see the coastline and reach several accommodation options along the line.

How to Get Around in Grimsby

Grimsby is a relatively small town, and visitors can quickly get around by foot, bike or car. The town also has a reliable bus network, with regular services to regional destinations.

Where to Go Next from Grimsby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grimsby is an excellent base to explore the wider Lincolnshire area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Lincoln: An ancient English city that boasts of impressive architecture and charming streets.
  • Humber Bridge: One of the world’s largest suspension bridges, with stunning views of the estuary.
  • Yorkshire Wolds: A beautiful and peaceful countryside area that offers a glimpse into traditional English living.

Conclusion

A visit to Grimsby is a must-do for anyone seeking to explore England’s rich maritime heritage. With its range of cultural attractions, stunning natural scenery, and modern amenities, Grimsby has something to offer everyone.
