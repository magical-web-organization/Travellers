Introduction to Skegness

Skegness is a charming seaside town located in Lincolnshire, East Midlands, in England, United Kingdom. Known as the “Jewel of the Lincolnshire Coast,” Skegness is a popular tourist destination and is famous for its beautiful beaches, stunning coastline, and vibrant seaside atmosphere.

Boasting plenty of cultural and natural attractions, there’s plenty to see and do in Skegness. This comprehensive guide will give you the lowdown on all the best places to visit, food to try, activities to do, and local customs and culture of this lovely town.

Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s history, enjoy the great outdoors, or simply relax on the beach, Skegness has something to offer to everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Skegness:

Local Culture and Customs of Skegness

The people of Skegness are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. Some customs that visitors should be aware of include tipping in restaurants and bars, being respectful of the local wildlife and environment, and not littering on the beach.

Best Time to Visit Skegness

The best time to visit Skegness is during the summer months, between June and September. This is when the weather is at its best, and there are plenty of events, festivals, and activities happening.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skegness

Do:

  • Explore the beautiful beaches of Skegness
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Take a cycling or hiking tour of Skegness

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in public places
  • Be disrespectful of the local wildlife and environment
  • Forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent

Safety and Health

Safety in Skegness

Skegness is a relatively safe place for tourists, but visitors should still take the usual precautions when traveling. This includes keeping valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Skegness

There are plenty of medical facilities in Skegness, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

How to Get Around in Skegness

Skegness is a small town, and many of the main attractions are within walking distance. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or car to get around more easily.

Where to Go Next from Skegness: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
