Introduction to Spalding

Spalding is a charming town situated in the South Holland district of Lincolnshire, East Midlands, England. It is a picturesque town with a rich history and is known for its beautiful gardens, traditional markets, and its famous tulip festival. Spalding is a perfect destination for those seeking a blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. In this travel guide, we will take you on a comprehensive tour of Spalding, with recommendations for places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, safety and health, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Spalding

Here is a list of some of the must-see places to visit in Spalding:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ayscoughfee Hall Museum and Gardens: Ayscoughfee Hall is a grade II listed building that was built in 1451 and now serves as a museum. The museum has exhibits that showcase the history of Spalding, including the town’s agricultural and horticultural heritage.

  • Chain Bridge Forge: The Chain Bridge Forge is a museum that showcases the blacksmith’s trade and tools from the 19th and early 20th centuries. Visitors can see live demonstrations of the craft by the resident blacksmith.

  • Lincolnshire Aviation Heritage Centre: This center is home to a rare, fully restored WWII bomber aircraft, the Avro Lancaster. Visitors can learn about the history of the aircraft and take a guided tour of the center.

Natural Attractions of Spalding: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spalding Water Taxi: Take a scenic ride on the Spalding Water Taxi, which offers a unique perspective of the town, including views of the River Welland, nature reserves, and local wildlife.

  • Springfields Outlet Shopping and Festival Gardens: Explore the beautiful gardens and breathe in the fresh air at Springfields Outlet Shopping and Festival Gardens. The gardens have a variety of themed areas including the Japanese Garden, Cabin Garden, and much more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spalding

Spalding has several scenic hiking and biking routes that offer panoramic views of the countryside. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Cycling route from Spalding to Ashley: This scenic cycling route covers around 18 miles and goes through the beautiful villages of Weston, Moulton, and Cowbit.

  • Walking route from Spalding to Pinchbeck: This is a pleasant, easy walk that covers around 3 miles and goes through the picturesque countryside and farmlands, offering great views of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Spalding: Activities and Tours

Spalding offers several exciting activities and tours that are suitable for all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Spalding:

  • Tulip Festival: The Spalding Tulip Festival is a yearly event that celebrates the town’s flower-growing heritage. The festival includes a variety of events such as music, family activities, and food and drink stalls.

  • Spalding Ghost Walk: Take a guided ghost walk tour of Spalding and discover the town’s supernatural history and paranormal activity.

Food and Drink Scene of Spalding

Spalding has an impressive food and drink scene, offering a variety of options for all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Fish and chips is a classic British dish that is a must-try when in Spalding. The fish is freshly caught from the local waterways, and the chips are made from locally sourced potatoes.

  • Lincolnshire Sausages: Lincolnshire is known for its delicious sausages made from locally raised pigs.

Local Beverages

  • Batemans Brewery: Batemans Brewery is a local brewery that has been producing fine ales since 1874. Visitors can take a guided tour of the brewery and sample some of the beers.

  • Fruit Wines: Lincolnshire is known for its fruit wines made from locally grown fruits such as strawberries and blackcurrants.

Where to Stay in Spalding: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spalding has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Red Lion Hotel: The Red Lion Hotel is a historic hotel located in the heart of Spalding. It offers comfortable rooms and exceptional hospitality.

  • The Chequers Inn: The Chequers Inn is a charming 17th-century inn located in Clay Lake. It offers comfortable rooms and traditional British food.

What to Buy in Spalding: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spalding has a variety of unique and locally made products that make perfect souvenirs or gifts. Here are some recommendations:

  • Tulips: Tulips are the signature flower of Spalding, and you can pick up a bouquet or two from the local florists.

  • Lincolnshire Poacher Cheese: Lincolnshire poacher cheese is a locally produced cheese that is a must-buy for cheese lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Spalding

Spalding is a multicultural town that celebrates its diversity through various events and festivals. The town is known for its tolerance and friendly residents.

Best Time to Visit Spalding

The best time to visit Spalding is during the spring and summer months when the town comes alive with festivals and events. The Spalding Tulip Festival is held in May each year and attracts visitors from all over the world.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spalding

Do:

  • Do visit the Ayscoughfee Hall Museum and Gardens.
  • Do try the fish and chips and Lincolnshire sausages.
  • Do take a guided ghost walk tour of the town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the Spalding Tulip Festival if you’re visiting in May.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spalding

Spalding is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions like they would in any other town or city.

Health in Spalding

Spalding has a wide range of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, making it easy to access medical assistance if required.

Getting to Spalding: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spalding is easily accessible by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Spalding

  • East Midlands Airport: This is the closest airport to Spalding, situated around 70 miles away.

Rail Stations Near Spalding

  • Spalding Station: Spalding station is located in the town centre and is served by regular trains.

Other Options

  • Bus: Spalding is well-connected by bus to nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Spalding

Spalding is a small town and can be easily navigated on foot. Cycling is also a popular mode of transportation and is a great way to explore the town and surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Spalding: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Spalding is ideally located, with several exciting destinations nearby. Here are some options:

  • Lincoln: This historic city is around 40 miles from Spalding and offers several attractions, including Lincoln Cathedral and Lincoln Castle.

  • Peterborough: Peterborough is a historic city located approximately 20 miles from Spalding. It has several attractions, including Peterborough Cathedral and the Nene Valley Railway.
