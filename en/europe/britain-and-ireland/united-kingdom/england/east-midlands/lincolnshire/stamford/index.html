Stamford Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Stamford is a charming town located in the heart of Lincolnshire, East Midlands, England. The town has a rich history and boasts a wide array of cultural attractions and natural wonders that make it a popular destination for travelers. In this travel guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know about Stamford, including places to visit, things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Stamford

Stamford has several attractions that will capture the attention of every traveler. Here are some of the must-see places in Stamford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stamford Museum – This museum is dedicated to showcasing Stamford’s rich history and features a wide range of exhibits that highlight the town’s heritage.
  • Browne’s Hospital – Browne’s Hospital is a medieval almshouse located in the heart of Stamford. It was founded in 1475 by William Browne, a wealthy merchant, and features a chapel and a great hall.
  • Stamford Arts Centre – This venue hosts a variety of cultural events, including concerts, exhibitions, and theater productions.

Natural Attractions of Stamford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Burghley House and Gardens – Burghley House is a magnificent 16th-century mansion set in stunning parkland, and its gardens offer breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Rutland Water – This reservoir is a great spot for outdoor activities, including biking, hiking, fishing, and birdwatching.
  • Stamford Meadows – Stamford Meadows is a tranquil parkland located on the banks of the River Welland, and it’s a popular spot for picnics and walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stamford

Stamford has several excellent hiking and biking routes that any outdoor enthusiast will appreciate. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Stamford Circular Walk – This 10-mile circular route takes in the stunning countryside surrounding Stamford and offers breathtaking views of the town.
  • Rutland Water Cycling Route – This 23-mile route follows the circumference of the reservoir and is perfect for those who want to explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Stamford: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to see and do in Stamford. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Take a walking tour of Stamford – This is an excellent way to explore the town’s historic streets and buildings. You can download a self-guided tour from the Visit Stamford website.
  • Visit the Stamford Shakespeare Company – This outdoor theater company performs plays by Shakespeare and other playwrights in the beautiful gardens of Tolethorpe Hall.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride – This is a fantastic way to see the town from the air and take in the stunning countryside surrounding Stamford.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stamford

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Stamford, here is an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in the town:

Day 1 – Arrive in Stamford and take a self-guided walking tour of the town.

Day 2 – Visit Burghley House and Gardens in the morning and then explore Stamford Meadows in the afternoon.

Day 3 – Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning and go fishing on Rutland Water in the afternoon.

Day 4 – Visit Browne’s Hospital in the morning and then explore the Stamford Arts Centre in the afternoon.

Day 5 – Take the Stamford Circular Walk in the morning and then go to the Stamford Shakespeare Company in the afternoon.

Day 6 – Visit Stamford Museum in the morning and then go for a bike ride around Rutland Water in the afternoon.

Day 7 – Leave Stamford and head to nearby destinations, such as Peterborough or Cambridge.

Food and Drink Scene of Stamford

Stamford has a thriving food and drink scene that any foodie will appreciate. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Stamford:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lincolnshire sausages – These are a local specialty and are made with sage and other herbs.
  • Bakewell tart – This classic English dessert originated in the nearby town of Bakewell and is made with almonds and raspberry jam.
  • Melton Mowbray pork pies – These savory pies are a local favorite and are made with high-quality pork and a delicious pastry crust.

Local Beverages

  • Rutland Water Ale – This is a locally brewed ale that is made with water from Rutland Water.
  • Lincolnshire Plum Bread – This is a sweet bread that is flavored with dried fruit and spices.

Where to Stay in Stamford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stamford has several excellent accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The George Hotel – This historic hotel is located in the heart of Stamford and features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • The William Cecil – This luxury hotel is located in the grounds of Burghley House and features elegant rooms, a restaurant, and a spa.
  • The Crown – This pub and guesthouse is located in the town center and offers comfortable rooms and a traditional pub atmosphere.

What to Buy in Stamford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Stamford is known for its local products, and there are many souvenirs that you can bring home to remember your trip to the town. Here are some of the best things to buy in Stamford:

  • Lincolnshire Poacher Cheese – This award-winning cheese is made in the nearby town of Ulceby and is a must-try for any foodie.
  • Stamford Pork Pies – These savory pies are a local specialty and are perfect for a picnic.
  • Stamford Ale – This locally brewed ale is available at many pubs and breweries in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Stamford

Stamford has a rich history, and its residents are proud of their cultural heritage. Here are some of the local customs and traditions that you might encounter during your visit:

  • Stamford was once a thriving wool town, and you can still see evidence of this heritage in the town’s buildings and architecture.
  • Stamford’s annual fair takes place every September and features a variety of attractions, including rides, games, and food stalls.
  • The town is known for its annual pancake race, which takes place on Shrove Tuesday and involves participants racing through the town while flipping pancakes.

Best Time to Visit Stamford

Stamford is a popular destination year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. The town can get busy during peak season, so it’s worth booking your accommodation in advance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stamford

Stamford is a safe town to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in quiet areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and never leave them unattended.

Stamford has several healthcare facilities, including doctors’ surgeries and pharmacies. If you need medical assistance during your visit, ask your hotel or hostel for recommendations.

Stamford is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the surrounding area, it’s worth considering renting a car or taking public transport.

Where to Go Next from Stamford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stamford is located in a beautiful part of the country, and there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some of the best places to go from Stamford:
