Arnold is a small suburban town located in the East Midlands region of Nottinghamshire, England. With a rich history, beautiful landscapes, and warm hospitality, Arnold is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique cultural experience. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a trip to explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat, stay, and more in Arnold.

Introduction to Arnold

Arnold is a small town located in the northern part of Nottingham. The city has a population of about 37,000 people. Arnold is known for its rich history, beautiful landscapes and warm hospitality. The town’s location close to the Peak District National Park, make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Arnold

Arnold has plenty of attractions for visitors to explore. There’s something for everyone, from museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arnold is home to several museums, galleries, and historical sites that offer unique insights into the town’s rich heritage. The most notable ones include:

  • Newstead Abbey: This historical site was home to Lord Byron and his ancestors. It boasts lush gardens and a museum that showcases the life of the poet.

  • St Mary’s Church: Located in the heart of Arnold, this church is the oldest building in the town. It boasts stunning architectural features and offers visitors a unique opportunity to learn about the town’s religious history.

Natural Attractions of Arnold: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arnold’s landscape is picturesque, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the town:

  • Gedling Country Park: This 580-acre park features a variety of landscapes, including woodland, grassland, and wetlands. It’s an excellent destination for hiking, biking, and picnicking.

  • Sherwood Forest: Located just outside Arnold, Sherwood Forest is a perfect destination for nature lovers. It offers visitors more than 1,000 ancient oak trees and many scenic trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arnold

Arnold is surrounded by many scenic trails that make it ideal for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The Robin Hood Way: This 105-mile trail is perfect for hikers. It passes through several towns and villages, including Arnold, and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • National Cycle Network Route 6: This 187-mile cycling route passes through Arnold and offers a great way to explore the town and its surroundings on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Arnold: Activities and Tours

Arnold has plenty of exciting activities to do and tours to take. Here are some of the top ones:

  • Ghost Hunting Experience: Explore the paranormal activity of Arnold with this spine-chilling ghost hunting experience.

  • Sherwood Forest Segway Adventure: This fun tour allows visitors to explore the natural beauty of Sherwood Forest on a Segway.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arnold

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your week in Arnold:

Day 1: Arrive in Arnold. Rest, settle into your accommodation.

Day 2: Visit Newstead Abbey and take a guided tour of Lord Byron’s ancestral home.

Day 3: Explore St Mary’s Church and learn about the town’s religious history.

Day 4: Hike the National Cycle Network Route 6 around Arnold.

Day 5: Join a guided ghost hunting experience and explore Arnold’s paranormal activity.

Day 6: Take a Sherwood Forest Segway Adventure.

Day 7: Depart Arnold.

Food and Drink Scene of Arnold

Arnold offers visitors a variety of dining options, from traditional British pubs to international cuisine. Here are some of our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Pies: Made with savory pastry and filled with spiced pork, these pies are a staple in British cuisine.

  • Nottinghamshire Sausage: This sausage is made from locally sourced pork and flavored with sage, giving it a unique taste.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Arnold boasts several traditional pubs that serve an excellent range of real ale.

Where to Stay in Arnold: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arnold offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Lodge at Ruddington: This stunning hotel is located just outside Arnold and offers luxurious rooms and facilities.

  • Travelodge Nottingham Arnold: This budget-friendly option offers comfortable rooms and excellent facilities.

What to Buy in Arnold: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arnold has several shops and markets that provide excellent shopping opportunities for visitors. Here’s what to buy:

  • Local Honey: Arnold honey is famous for its unique sweet taste and is a perfect souvenir to take back home.

  • Traditional Clothing: Traditional clothing such as the Robin Hood hat is a perfect souvenir to remember Arnold’s rich heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Arnold

Arnold is a welcoming town with a rich cultural heritage. The locals are friendly and always ready to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit Arnold

Arnold boasts different scenery and activities to offer based on the season. Late spring to early autumn is the ideal time to visit Arnold when the weather and nature are optimal to enjoy outdoor activities.

Getting to Arnold: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arnold is easily accessible by different transportation modes, including:

Airports Near Arnold

  • East Midlands Airport: This airport is the closest to Arnold, located about 20 miles away.

  • Robin Hood Airport: This airport is located about 30 miles away and serves international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Arnold

  • Arnold Bus Station: This bus station serves local and regional destinations.

Other Options

How to Get Around Arnold

Arnold is easy to explore on foot, but visitors can also use public transportation options such as buses and taxis to navigate the town.
