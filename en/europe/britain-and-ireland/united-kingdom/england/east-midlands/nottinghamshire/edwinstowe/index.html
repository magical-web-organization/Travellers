Introduction to Edwinstowe

Edwinstowe, located in Nottinghamshire, East Midlands, England, is a charming village known for its rich history and scenic beauty. It is situated in the heart of Sherwood Forest, where the legendary outlaw Robin Hood is said to have roamed. The village boasts of a long list of things to do and see, including culturally important attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Edwinstowe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St Mary’s Church: A Grade I listed church that dates back to the 13th century. It is known for its beautiful stained glass windows and the legendary “Green Man” carving.
  • Sherwood Forest Art and Craft Centre: An excellent place to experience the local arts and crafts scene. It features a range of unique and handmade items made by local artists and craftspeople.
  • Edwinstowe House: Once a private residence, this historic building now serves as a community hub, hosting a variety of cultural events throughout the year.
  • Sherwood Forest Visitor Centre: A must-visit attraction for nature lovers. It offers interactive exhibits about the history and ecology of Sherwood Forest.

Natural Attractions of Edwinstowe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sherwood Forest National Nature Reserve: The forest covers an area of 450 acres and is one of the best-preserved medieval forests in Europe. Visitors can explore the beautiful woodland trails, see the famous Major Oak tree, and spot wildlife such as deer and red kites.
  • Thoresby Park: A beautiful country park featuring stunning gardens, woodland walks, and a large lake. Visitors can enjoy activities such as archery, fishing, cycling, and more.
  • Rufford Abbey Country Park: A 150-acre park surrounding Rufford Abbey, the former medieval monastery. It features beautiful gardens, a lake, picnic areas, and children’s play areas.
  • Edwinstowe Cricket Ground: A scenic venue for cricket enthusiasts, set in the heart of the village. It offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edwinstowe

Sherwood Forest Trail

This 3.8-mile trail takes hikers and bikers through Sherwood Forest, passing by the Major Oak tree, the legendary hideout of Robin Hood and his band of outlaws. The trail is easy to follow and offers plenty of opportunities to spot local wildlife and take in the stunning forest scenery.

Rufford Abbey Walk

This 2.5-mile trail takes hikers and bikers through the beautiful Rufford Abbey Country Park, passing by the lake, the abbey ruins, and the impressive stonework of the Old Kitchen.

Best Things to Do in Edwinstowe: Activities and Tours

  • Sherwood Forest Segway: A guided Segway tour that takes visitors on a thrilling ride through Sherwood Forest. This is an excellent opportunity to explore the forest from a new perspective.
  • Sherwood Pines: A great place to experience the outdoors. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including cycling, hiking, and nature watching.
  • Edwinstowe Swimming Pool: An indoor swimming pool with trained lifeguards, perfect for a fun-filled day with family and friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Edwinstowe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sherwood Forest Pie: A traditional meat pie made with lamb, chicken, or game and flavored with rosemary and thyme.
  • Fried Bread: A local specialty made by frying bread in butter or lard. It is often served with eggs and bacon.
  • Cream Tea: A quintessential English teatime treat that includes scones, jam, and clotted cream.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: A locally brewed beer that has a strong, distinctive flavor.
  • Cider: A refreshing alcoholic drink made from fermented apples. The local cider is often flavored with berries and spices.

Where to Stay in Edwinstowe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Edwinstowe House: A historic building that now serves as a guesthouse and community hub.
  • Thoresby Hall: A luxurious hotel set in a stunning Victorian country house with excellent amenities such as a spa and fine dining restaurants.
  • Sherwood Forest Lodge: A cozy bed and breakfast located near Sherwood Forest.

What to Buy in Edwinstowe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Robin Hood Memorabilia: Anything from T-shirts and mugs to action figures and plush toys.
  • Handmade items: Jewelry, pottery, and sculptures made by local artists and craftspeople.
  • Local Produce: Locally produced honey, jam, and chutneys make for excellent gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Edwinstowe

The village of Edwinstowe has a rich history and culture that is deeply intertwined with the legend of Robin Hood. Visitors can learn more about local customs and traditions by attending the annual Robin Hood Festival, held every August.

Best Time to Visit Edwinstowe

The best time to visit Edwinstowe is between May and September when the weather is warmer, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed to the fullest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edwinstowe

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Enjoy the many outdoor activities available in the area.
  • Visit the local pubs and restaurants to experience the food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter in nature reserves and parks.
  • Disrespect heritage sites and historical landmarks.
  • Enter private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edwinstowe

Edwinstowe is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take the usual precautions such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Edwinstowe

Visitors should have travel insurance and ensure they have the appropriate immunizations before embarking on their trip.

Getting to Edwinstowe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edwinstowe

The nearest international airport is East Midlands Airport, located about 35 miles south of Edwinstowe.

Bus Stations Near Edwinstowe

The village is well-connected by bus, with regular services from Nottingham and Mansfield.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Edwinstowe by train, with the nearest station being Shirebrook.

How to Get Around in Edwinstowe

The village is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors can also hire a car, bike, or scooter to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Edwinstowe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nottingham: A vibrant city that combines historic landmarks with modern attractions such as shopping centers, theaters, and museums.
  • Mansfield: A former mining town with a rich history, Mansfield offers visitors a range of cultural attractions and activities, including theater, music, and art exhibitions.
  • Chatsworth House: A stunning stately home located in the Peak District, about an hour’s drive from Edwinstowe.

Conclusion

Edwinstowe is a beautiful village that offers visitors a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. From the legendary Sherwood Forest and the iconic Major Oak to the delicious local cuisine and the warm hospitality of the locals, there is something for everyone in this charming part of Nottinghamshire.
