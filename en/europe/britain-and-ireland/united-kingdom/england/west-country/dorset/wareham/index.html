Introduction to Wareham

Wareham, located in the West Country region of Dorset, United Kingdom, is a historic market town nestled between the River Frome and the Purbeck Hills. The town is known for its preserved Saxon walls, stunning natural scenery, and rich history dating back centuries.

The town is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and laid-back hospitality. Whether you’re visiting alone, with friends, or family, the town boasts a range of activities that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and travel tips for your trip to Wareham.

List of Places to Visit in Wareham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wareham Town Museum - This museum showcases the history and heritage of the town and features exhibits on the town’s connection to Lawrence of Arabia.
  • Wareham Walls and Gates - These well-preserved Saxon walls and gates were built in the 9th century and once served as a fortification to protect the town from Viking invasions.
  • St. Martin’s Church - This historic church dates back to the 11th century and features stunning architecture and beautiful stained glass windows.
  • Corfe Castle - A short drive away from Wareham, this majestic castle ruin dates back to the 11th century and offers fantastic views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Wareham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wareham Forest Trail - This picturesque forest trail is perfect for hiking and biking and offers stunning views of the lush woodland.
  • Purbeck Hills - These hills located near Wareham offer panoramic views of the surrounding area and are a great spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Poole Harbour - Located just a short drive from Wareham, this stunning natural harbor offers a range of watersports and activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wareham

  • The Purbeck Way - This 12-mile route starts in Swanage and takes you through the stunning Purbeck Hills and along the South West Coast Path.
  • Wareham Forest Trail - This trail, mentioned earlier, is an ideal route for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Hardy’s Monument Trail - A 7-mile route that takes you through stunning countryside and past Hardy’s Monument, a 72 ft. tall tower dedicated to the author, Thomas Hardy.

Best Things to Do in Wareham: Activities and Tours

  • Arne RSPB Reserve - This reserve offers fantastic opportunities to see rare birds and other wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Monkey World - Located on the outskirts of Wareham, this primate rescue center is home to over 250 primates and offers educational experiences and entertaining shows.
  • River Frome Canoeing - Rent a canoe and take a leisurely paddle down the River Frome, where you’ll experience stunning natural scenery and may even spot some otters.

Food and Drink Scene of Wareham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dorset Apple Cake - This classic British dessert made with fresh apples and local cream, is a must-try when in Wareham.
  • Dorset Blue Vinny Cheese - This uncooked blue cheese made of unpasteurized cow’s milk is an essential addition to any cheeseboard.
  • Dorset Jugged Steak - A hearty stew made with beef and vegetables, perfect for a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Purbeck Cider - Made with locally grown apples, this award-winning cider is a perfect drink to toast to your trip to Wareham.
  • Badger Ale - This popular ale is brewed locally and can be found in most pubs in the area.
  • Worth Matravers Spring Water - This naturally sparkling water is bottled at a spring in a nearby village and is a refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Wareham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Priory Hotel - Located on the River Frome, this hotel combines modern amenities with historic charm and is perfect for a luxury stay.
  • Wareham Forest Lodge Retreat - This peaceful retreat is situated in the nearby forest and offers guests a chance to relax and unwind surrounded by nature.
  • YHA Swanage Hostel - This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers who want basic accommodation with easy access to the outdoors.

What to Buy in Wareham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Purbeck Marble - This unique and colorful stone is found in a quarry near Wareham and can be fashioned into a range of souvenirs, including jewelry and sculptures.
  • Dorset Tea - This local brand of tea is made with leaves grown in the nearby Purbeck Hills and is perfect for tea lovers.
  • Wareham Pottery - This pottery, located in the town center, sells handcrafted ceramics, including bowls, plates, and vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Wareham

While Wareham is a friendly and welcoming town, it’s important to be aware of the local customs and observe them during your stay. For instance, when visiting churches, it is crucial to dress conservatively, especially for women. Additionally, when in pubs, it’s polite to offer to buy a round of drinks.

Best Time to Visit Wareham

The best time to visit Wareham is during the summer months of June, July, and August. The weather is warm and sunny, and all the outdoor attractions are open. However, if you prefer a quieter experience, you can visit during the shoulder season (March to May and September to November), when the crowds have lessened, and the prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wareham

Do:

  • Observe local customs and etiquette, especially in churches and pubs.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions, including the forests, hills, and rivers.
  • Try the local food and drink specialties.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs and etiquette.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wareham

Wareham is a safe town, but it is essential to take the usual precautions, such as locking your doors and being aware of your surroundings. The roads around Wareham can be narrow and winding, so take extra care when driving.

Health in Wareham

Medical facilities and pharmacies can be found in the town center, and emergency services can be reached by dialing 999.

Getting to Wareham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wareham

The closest airports to Wareham are Bournemouth Airport (20 miles away) and Southampton Airport (49 miles away). From there, you can take a taxi or bus to the town center.

Bus Stations Near Wareham

The main bus station in Wareham is located in the town center and offers connections to many nearby towns and cities, including Bournemouth and Poole.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Wareham is accessible from the A31 and A352 highways. If you’re traveling by train, the closest station is in Wareham itself, with regular connections to London and the South West.

How to Get Around in Wareham

Walking is the easiest way to explore the town center, but if you want to venture further afield, renting a car or bike is the best option. Taxis are available, but they are more expensive. Local buses are also available but may have limited schedules on weekends and holidays.

Where to Go Next from Wareham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Durdle Door - A stunning rock formation located approximately 15 miles west of Wareham.
  • Lulworth Cove - A scenic cove and picturesque seaside town located about 12 miles south of Wareham.
  • Bournemouth - A popular seaside resort town located about 16 miles east of Wareham.

Conclusion

Wareham is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of outdoor activities, historic landmarks, and local culture. From exploring the ancient walls to strolling through the forest trails, there’s something here for every visitor. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to have an amazing and memorable trip to Wareham.
