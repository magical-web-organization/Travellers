Introduction to Bournemouth

Located in the county of Dorset, in the West Country of England, Bournemouth is a charming seaside resort town along the English Channel. It has a mild climate, beautiful sandy beaches, and a variety of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages.

Bournemouth has a lively cultural scene, with museums, galleries, and theaters, as well as a diverse range of restaurants, pubs, and bars. Not to mention, the town boasts a plethora of natural attractions, such as parks, gardens, and nature reserves.

List of Places to Visit in Bournemouth

Here are some of the must-visit places in Bournemouth:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Russell-Cotes Art Gallery & Museum: This is a unique museum that showcases fascinating art collections and objects collected by Merton and Annie Russell-Cotes.
  • Bournemouth Aviation Museum: This museum is dedicated to aviation history, with exhibits ranging from aircraft engines to uniforms.
  • Bournemouth Natural Science Society: A museum that houses numerous natural history specimens, including fossils, minerals, and animals.

Natural Attractions of Bournemouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bournemouth Beach: This award-winning beach is one of Bournemouth’s central attractions. It has a long promenade, a pier, and plenty of beachside activities.
  • Pier Approach Gardens: Located between the pier and the beach, these botanical gardens offer a peaceful escape from busy central Bournemouth and boasts stunning views.
  • Hengistbury Head: To the east of Bournemouth, this stunning headland boasts unparalleled views of the Isle of Wight, Christchurch Harbour and the Purbeck Hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bournemouth

  • Stour Valley Way: A long distance, 60-mile trail that runs through the countryside and the towns along the River Stour.
  • Hengistbury Head Coastal Trail: This trail provides picturesque views of the coast and countryside and is approximately 5 miles in length.
  • Avon Heath Country Park: A network of several trails that winding through woodland, heathland and picturesque lakes and ponds.

Best Things to Do in Bournemouth: Activities and Tours

  • Bournemouth Balloon: Soar above the town with the Bournemouth Balloon and get magnificent views of the landscape.
  • Bournemouth Oceanarium: This aquarium has various species of marine animals and offers an insight into the underwater world.
  • Paddleboarding/Surfing: The rolling waves make the beach a hot spot for paddleboarding, surfing and other watersports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bournemouth

Here’s an example itinerary for a week in Bournemouth:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Bournemouth Natural Science Society and Russell-Cotes Art Gallery & Museum
Day 2 Explore Bournemouth Beach and Pier
Day 3 Take a hike or bike ride at Stour Valley Way
Day 4 Spend the day at Hengistbury Head
Day 5 Check out Bournemouth Aviation Museum and take a sightseeing tour of the coastline
Day 6 Relax in Pier Approach Gardens, then visit Bournemouth Oceanarium
Day 7 Enjoy a day of watersports at one of the many local beaches

Food and Drink Scene of Bournemouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dorset Blue Vinney Cheese: A cheese made from unpasteurized milk and is typically crumbly and tangy in flavor.
  • Dorset Apple Cake: A sweet cake made with apples and cinnamon and served with whipped cream.
  • Lobster: Fresh seafood, particularly lobster caught in nearby coastal waters.

Local Beverages

  • Badger Brewery: It produces a variety of beers “seasoned” with unique ingredients like chocolate, elderflowers, and ginger making them taste unique.
  • Dorset Apple Juice: Made with locally-harvested apples, this juice is perfect for accompanying meals or for a refreshing drink after outdoor activities.
  • Perry’s Cider: This company offers different cider varieties, made on a family-run orchard with only traditional methods.

Where to Stay in Bournemouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bournemouth offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • The Green House Hotel: An eco-friendly boutique hotel with a restaurant and bar, located close to the beach and town.
  • Boscombe Reef Hotel: This stylish, boutique hotel offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.
  • Bournemouth Beach Lodges: Unique and quirky beach huts offering stunning views of the coast and sea.

What to Buy in Bournemouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bournemouth Rock: A traditional sweet treat made to look like a striped rock slab, with the name Bournemouth running through the center.
  • Dorset Chutney: Delicious local chutneys made with fresh ingredients such as apples, plums, and quince.
  • Local Crafts: From pottery to textiles, there is a significant amount of local crafts that make for excellent gift items.

Local Culture and Customs of Bournemouth

Bournemouth is a peaceful, laid-back town with a lively cultural scene. Locals are welcoming, generous, and are proud of the history and cultural developments of their town. Beach culture and watersport culture is quite strong in the area.

Best Time to Visit Bournemouth

Bournemouth has a mild climate throughout the year. However, if you’re looking for sunny and dry days, the best time to visit Bournemouth is from May to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bournemouth

Do:

  • Do: respect the local coastal area and be environmentally responsible.
  • Do: walk and bike around as it is a cultural part of the town.
  • Do: review tide tables when visiting beaches.

Don’t:

  • Don’t: litter and pollute the towns and beaches.
  • Don’t: drink excessively in public areas.
  • Don’t: go swimming when there are red flags waving on the beach indicating unsafe conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bournemouth

Bournemouth is generally safe, though it is important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings in crowded areas.

Health in Bournemouth

Bournemouth has world-class healthcare facilities, including private hospitals and NHS clinics.

Getting to Bournemouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bournemouth is easy to access via plane, train, bus, or car.

Airports Near Bournemouth

  • Bournemouth Airport: Located just 10 minutes outside of the town centre of Bournemouth, this airport serves various parts of UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Bournemouth

  • Bournemouth Interchange Bus Station: A large, modern bus station located in the town center, which operates services to London, airport, and other coastal towns nearby.

Other Options

  • Train: Bournemouth is well connected with frequent trains from London as well as other towns in UK.
  • Car: Bournemouth is roughly 100 miles from London and has excellent road connectivity.

How to Get Around in Bournemouth

Bournemouth is a relatively small town, and it is easy to get around on foot or by bike. There are also reliable public transport, taxi, and car rental services available for those who prefer.

Where to Go Next from Bournemouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Poole: A neighboring town southeast of Bournemouth that has a bustling harbor and excellent beaches to explore.
  • The New Forest: A vast national park with vast landscapes, diverse wildlife, and stunning views of the forest and hills.
  • Isle of Wight: A beautiful island located just off the south coast of England, a 30-minute ferry ride from Lymington.

Conclusion

Bournemouth is a charming seaside resort town that offers a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for pristine beaches, hiking and cycling trails, or world-class museums and galleries, Bournemouth has something for everyone. With year-round mild weather and a friendly and laid-back atmosphere, it’s a perfect summer destination for a holiday. Start planning your trip to Bournemouth today!
