Introduction to Sherborne

Sherborne is a picturesque market town in the north of Dorset, United Kingdom. Known for its rich history and stunning architecture, it offers the perfect blend of old-world charm and modern amenities. The town has a population of just over 9,000 people and dates back to the year 705.

List of Places to Visit in Sherborne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sherborne Abbey

Sherborne Abbey, founded in AD 998, is a must-visit for tourists and culture buffs alike. The stunning Gothic architecture and peaceful atmosphere make it the perfect place for reflection and contemplation. Guided tours are available daily, and admission is free.

Sherborne Castle

Sherborne Castle, also known as the ‘new castle,’ was built on the ruins of a Medieval fortress and has been home to the Digby family since 1617. Visitors can explore the castle’s rich history and extensive art collection, and enjoy the beautiful gardens and lake.

The Old House

The Old House is a Grade II listed building that dates back to the early 16th century. The house is now a museum, showcasing life in Sherborne during the Tudor era. Visitors can explore the house’s collections and learn about the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Sherborne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sherborne Old Castle

Sherborne Old Castle, also known as the ‘old castle,’ is one of the town’s most popular tourist attractions. The castle ruins provide stunning views across the surrounding landscape and are the perfect place for a picnic or a romantic walk.

Sherborne Lake and Gardens

Sherborne Lake and Gardens are a haven for nature lovers and families. The beautiful gardens offer a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the town, and the lake is perfect for boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sherborne

Sherborne to Castleton

This relatively easy hike offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The route follows a well-marked trail through peaceful forests and fields, and ends at the picturesque village of Castleton.

Sherborne to Wincanton

This challenging hike takes visitors through the heart of rural Dorset. Pass through beautiful farmland and busy market towns on the way to the historic town of Wincanton.

Best Things to Do in Sherborne: Activities and Tours

Sherborne Ghost Tours

Sherborne Ghost Tours offer visitors the chance to explore the town’s eerie past. The tours take in the most haunted buildings and locations, and provide a unique insight into the town’s dark history.

Sherborne Walking Tours

Sherborne Walking Tours allow visitors to explore the town’s history and architecture at a leisurely pace. Knowledgeable guides lead you through the town’s streets and alleyways, taking in the most important sites and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Sherborne

Must-Try Dishes

Sherborne Pasty

A traditional pasty made with beef, potatoes, and onions, baked to perfection and served piping hot.

Dorset Apple Cake

A deliciously moist cake made with sweet apples, juicy raisins, and a hint of cinnamon.

Local Beverages

The Sherborne Brewery

The Sherborne Brewery is a small, independent brewery that produces a range of delicious craft beers. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample some of the company’s finest creations.

Where to Stay in Sherborne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Eastbury Hotel

The Eastbury Hotel is a beautiful 4-star hotel located in the heart of Sherborne. The hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, an award-winning restaurant, and a spa and wellness center.

The Crown Hotel

The Crown Hotel is a charming 17th-century coaching inn, located just a short walk from the town center. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a cozy bar, and a restaurant serving traditional English fare.

What to Buy in Sherborne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sherborne Gin

Produced using locally grown botanicals, Sherborne Gin is a delicious and unique spirit that makes the perfect souvenir or gift.

Sherborne Pottery

Sherborne Pottery is a local company that produces beautiful handmade ceramics. Visitors can purchase a range of unique and stylish pieces to take home as a lasting reminder of their trip to Sherborne.

Local Culture and Customs of Sherborne

Sherborne has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historic buildings, museums, and events. The town is known for its friendly residents, vibrant arts scene, and traditional festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Sherborne

The best time to visit Sherborne is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the town is bustling with activity. However, visitors can enjoy the town’s attractions and activities year-round.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sherborne

Sherborne is a safe and welcoming town, with low levels of crime and a friendly community. Visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety and security.

Health in Sherborne

There are several medical centers and hospitals in Sherborne that offer a range of healthcare services. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance and take normal precautions to prevent illness and injury.

Getting to Sherborne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sherborne

The nearest airport to Sherborne is Bristol Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away. Other airports in the area include Bournemouth Airport and Southampton Airport.

Bus Stations Near Sherborne

Sherborne Bus Station is located in the town center and offers regular services to nearby towns and cities. Other nearby bus stations include Yeovil Bus Station and Taunton Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Sherborne by train, with regular services from London, Bristol, and Exeter. There are also several major roads that connect Sherborne to other parts of the country.

How to Get Around in Sherborne

Sherborne is a small town, and visitors can easily explore its attractions and landmarks on foot. There are also buses and taxis available for those who prefer to travel by public transport.

Where to Go Next from Sherborne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lyme Regis

Lyme Regis is a charming coastal town located just over 20 miles from Sherborne. With its historic harbor, stunning beaches, and winding streets, it’s the perfect place for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Bath

Bath is a historic city located just over an hour’s drive from Sherborne. With its famous Roman Baths and stunning Georgian architecture, it’s a must-visit for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Sherborne is a hidden gem in the heart of Dorset, full of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning architecture, lush countryside, and friendly community, it is the perfect destination for visitors looking for a unique and memorable travel experience.
