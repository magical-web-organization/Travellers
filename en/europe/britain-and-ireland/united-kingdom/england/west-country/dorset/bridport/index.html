Introduction to Bridport

Bridport is a historic market town located in West Dorset, England. It is home to a population of around 13,000 people and is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, stunning countryside, and rich history. The town was once an important center for the production of rope and netting. Today, Bridport is famous for its street market, which has been held every Wednesday and Saturday since 1253.

List of Places to Visit in Bridport

Bridport is home to numerous attractions that are worth visiting. Whether you’re interested in learning about the town’s history, exploring picturesque countryside, or indulging in local food and drink, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bridport:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bridport Museum: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the town’s history, from its geological origins to the present day. Highlights include a collection of rope and netting-making machinery and displays on local industries and crafts.
  • The Town Mill: This 700-year-old watermill is now an arts center featuring galleries, a pottery, and a café. You can watch artists at work or take part in a workshop.
  • West Bay: Located just a few miles from Bridport, West Bay is a charming seaside town with a picturesque harbor, stunning beach, and rugged cliffs. It was also the location of the popular TV drama “Broadchurch.”

Natural Attractions of Bridport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Golden Cap: At 191 meters (626 feet), this is the highest point on the south coast of England. You can hike up to the summit for stunning views of the coastline. It’s a challenging ascent but very rewarding.
  • Chesil Beach: This 18-mile pebble beach is a unique geological formation and is protected as a World Heritage Site. It’s a great place for birdwatching and sea fishing, or just a scenic walk along the coast.
  • Jurassic Coast: This 95-mile stretch of coastline between Exmouth in Devon and Studland Bay in Dorset is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s one of the most beautiful and important geological sites in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridport

If you love hiking or cycling, Bridport is the perfect place for you. The town is surrounded by beautiful countryside and is home to numerous trails and routes that offer stunning views of the area. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Bridport:

  • The Jubilee Trail: This 90-mile hiking route links West Bay with Exmouth and provides stunning views of the coastal countryside of Devon, Dorset, and Somerset.
  • The West Dorset Trailway: This 10-mile route follows a disused railway line from Powerstock to Bridport and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk.
  • The South West Coast Path: This 630-mile hiking trail follows the entire coast of Devon and Cornwall and passes through Bridport.

Best Things to Do in Bridport: Activities and Tours

Bridport offers numerous activities and tours that can make your visit even more enjoyable. Here are some of the best things to do in Bridport:

  • Take a food and drink tour: Bridport is famous for its local food and drink scene. You can take a tour of the town’s best breweries, wineries, and distilleries and sample the local produce.
  • Visit the vintage market: Held every second Saturday of the month, the Bridport Vintage Market is a great place to find unique antiques and vintage items.
  • Explore the arts scene: Bridport is home to numerous galleries, art centers, and studios. You can take a tour of the best and learn about the town’s vibrant creative culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Bridport

If you’re a foodie, Bridport is the perfect place for you. The town is known for its locally-produced food and drink, and there are numerous places where you can indulge in the best that Dorset has to offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Bridport:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dorset apple cake: This traditional cake is made with local apples and is a favorite of the area.
  • West Country beef: This type of beef is raised locally and is known for its rich flavor and high quality.
  • Dorset blue cheese: This tangy cheese is made from milk produced by cows that graze on the lush pastures of Dorset.

Local Beverages

  • Palmers ale: This award-winning ale is produced locally and is a favorite of the area.
  • Furleigh Estate wines: This local winery produces high-quality wines from the best grapes grown in the Dorset countryside.
  • Black Cow vodka: This vodka is made from milk produced by cows that graze on the hills surrounding Bridport.

Where to Stay in Bridport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bridport offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel, a cozy guesthouse, or a budget-friendly hostel, you’ll find plenty of options in this vibrant town. Some of the best places to stay in Bridport include:

  • The Bull Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Bridport and offers luxurious rooms and excellent dining options.
  • The Marquis of Lorne: This cozy guesthouse is located just a few miles from Bridport and offers comfortable rooms and a great restaurant.
  • The Ropemakers: This popular pub and hostel offers affordable accommodation and a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in Bridport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bridport is a great place to find unique souvenirs and locally-produced goods to bring back home. From antiques and vintage items to locally-made cheeses and ales, there’s a wide range of products to choose from. Here are some of the best things to buy in Bridport:

  • Antique furniture and vintage items: Bridport is famous for its antiques market, which is held twice a week. You can find unique pieces of furniture, clothing, and other vintage items.
  • Local cheese and ale: Bridport is home to numerous breweries and cheesemakers, and you can find high-quality local products in many shops and markets.
  • Handmade crafts and art: Bridport has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you can find unique handmade items in many shops and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridport

Bridport is a friendly town with a strong sense of community. The locals are proud of their heritage and are always happy to share their traditions and customs with visitors. Some of the local customs and traditions in Bridport include:

  • The street market: The Bridport market is one of the oldest and largest in the country and has been held every Wednesday and Saturday since 1253.
  • The WI market: The Women’s Institute holds a monthly market where you can buy homemade cakes, jams, and other tasty treats.
  • The Dorset Knob Throwing Festival: This annual event is a fun-filled competition where participants attempt to throw a small biscuit called a Dorset Knob as far as they can.

Best Time to Visit Bridport

The best time to visit Bridport is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and the town is buzzing with activity. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, you can also visit in the quieter spring and fall months. Winter is also a great time to visit if you’re interested in the town’s arts and culture scene, as there are numerous events and festivals held during this time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bridport

Bridport is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions to stay safe. Here are some tips to help you stay safe while visiting Bridport:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Follow safety guidelines when hiking or biking on the local trails.
  • Stay away from the cliffs and steep slopes along the coast.
  • Always wear sunscreen and protective clothing when out in the sun.

Health in Bridport

Bridport is generally a healthy place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions to protect yourself from potential health risks. Here are some tips to help you stay healthy while visiting Bridport:

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Be aware of the symptoms of heat exhaustion and seek medical attention if necessary.

  • Make sure to have the appropriate vaccinations if you’re traveling from a country with a high prevalence of infectious diseases.

Getting to Bridport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bridport is located in Dorset, in the South West of England, and can be easily reached by car, train, or bus. Here are the best ways to get to Bridport:

Airports Near Bridport

The nearest international airports to Bridport are Heathrow Airport and Gatwick Airport. From there, you can take a train or a bus to Bridport.

Bus Stations Near Bridport

Bridport is well-connected by bus, with regular services to nearby towns and cities such as Weymouth and Exeter. The main bus station in Bridport is located near the town center.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by car, Bridport is located just off the A35, which provides easy access to nearby towns and cities. If you prefer to travel by train, the nearest train station is located in nearby Axminster, which offers regular services to London and other major UK cities.

How to Get Around in Bridport

Bridport is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, if you prefer to use public transport, there are regular bus services that run throughout the town and to nearby destinations. Taxis are also available if you need to travel further afield.
