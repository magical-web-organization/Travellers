Introduction to Long Ashton

Nestled in the heart of Somerset, Long Ashton is a picturesque village that provides visitors with a true taste of English country life. The village is situated just a short distance from the vibrant city of Bristol, but it feels worlds away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Long Ashton is a charming destination that boasts a rich history spanning back centuries. Here, you’ll find a host of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that promise to make your trip one to remember. From hiking and biking routes to world-class museums and historical sites, there’s something for everyone in this idyllic village.

List of Places to Visit in Long Ashton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ashton Court Mansion Ashton Court Mansion is a stunning example of 17th-century architecture that has been carefully restored to its former glory. Visitors can take a guided tour of the mansion and explore the beautiful gardens that surround it.

The Georgian House Museum The Georgian House Museum is a hidden gem that showcases the best of Georgian architecture and design. Here, you can step back in time and experience what life was like in the 18th century.

Bristol Museum and Art Gallery Located in the nearby city of Bristol, the Bristol Museum and Art Gallery is a must-see for visitors to Long Ashton. The museum boasts an extensive collection of art, artifacts, and natural history exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Long Ashton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ashton Court Estate Ashton Court Estate is a sprawling 860-acre estate that provides visitors with a stunning backdrop for a day out. Here, you can explore a range of scenic paths and trails, take in the views from the estate’s lookout point, and even try your hand at orienteering.

Dundry Hill A climb up Dundry Hill, one of the highest hills in the area, is well worth the effort. From the top, you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of Bristol and the surrounding countryside.

Blaise Castle Estate Blaise Castle Estate is a beautiful parkland that offers visitors the chance to explore a range of gardens, woodland, and open spaces. The estate is also home to a museum that celebrates the life and work of renowned inventor Isambard Kingdom Brunel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Ashton

Long Ashton is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hikes and biking routes that take visitors through some of the most stunning scenery in the area. Here are a few of the best options:

Ashton Court Estate Ashton Court Estate is home to a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. The trails take you through woodland, meadows, and even past a deer park.

Dundry Hill The climb up Dundry Hill may be tough, but the views from the top make it all worthwhile. The hill is also a great spot for mountain biking, with a range of trails to explore.

The Strawberry Line The Strawberry Line is a popular cycle route that takes you through the vales of North Somerset. The route runs from Yatton to Cheddar, passing through Long Ashton along the way.

Best Things to Do in Long Ashton: Activities and Tours

Explore the views from Noah’s Ark Zoo Farm Noah’s Ark Zoo Farm is a must-see for animal lovers, with over 100 species of animals to discover. The zoo also offers guided tours that take in the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Visit the Long Ashton Golf Club Long Ashton Golf Club is one of the most picturesque golf courses in the area, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Try your hand at fishing The area surrounding Long Ashton is home to a range of rivers and lakes that provide the perfect setting for a day of fishing. Organised tours are available for beginners, or you can simply pack a picnic and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Ashton

Day 1: Visit the Ashton Court Estate and explore the beautiful gardens and grounds. Day 2: Take a guided tour of Ashton Court Mansion and learn more about its fascinating history. Day 3: Visit the Georgian House Museum and step back in time to the 18th century. Day 4: Explore the natural wonders of Dundry Hill and take in the stunning views from the top. Day 5: Visit Noah’s Ark Zoo Farm and discover over 100 species of animals. Day 6: Try your hand at fishing in one of the many rivers or lakes surrounding Long Ashton. Day 7: Visit the Long Ashton Golf Club and enjoy a round of golf with stunning countryside views.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Ashton

Must-Try Dishes

The Bird in Hand The Bird in Hand is a traditional English pub that serves up a range of classic dishes, including fish and chips, shepherd’s pie, and sticky toffee pudding.

The Ashton The Ashton is a stylish bar and restaurant that offers a range of contemporary British dishes, with a focus on locally sourced produce.

Local Beverages

Thatchers Cider Thatchers Cider is a popular cider maker that is based in nearby Sandford. Visitors can take a tour of the cider mills and sample some of the region’s best ciders.

Butcombe Brewery Butcombe Brewery is a local brewery that produces a range of award-winning beers and ales. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample some of the beers that have made Butcombe famous.

Where to Stay in Long Ashton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Angel Inn The Angel Inn is a charming guesthouse that boasts a range of comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

The Bird in Hand The Bird in Hand not only serves delicious food but also provides comfortable lodging at an affordable price.

The Ashton The Ashton not only offers a variety of dining options, but also luxurious accommodations complete with sweeping countryside views.

What to Buy in Long Ashton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Cider Thatchers Cider is a must-try if you’re in the area, and it also makes for a fantastic souvenir to share with friends and family back home.

Local Cheese Somerset is known for its exceptional cheese production, so be sure to try some of the local cheese while you’re in Long Ashton.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Ashton

Long Ashton is a tight-knit community that takes great pride in its history and traditions. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly locals and a warm welcome.

Best Time to Visit Long Ashton

The best time to visit Long Ashton is from late spring to early autumn, when the weather is warm and the countryside is at its most beautiful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Ashton

Long Ashton is a safe and welcoming destination, with low levels of crime and a friendly local community. Visitors should still take standard precautions when travelling, such as locking car doors and being aware of surroundings after dark.

Health in Long Ashton

There are no major health concerns in Long Ashton, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any significant health risks. However, it is still recommended that visitors bring any necessary medication with them and check with their doctor before travelling.

Getting to Long Ashton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Ashton

The nearest airport to Long Ashton is Bristol Airport, which is approximately 5 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Long Ashton

Buses connect Long Ashton with the city of Bristol and other nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

Train services run from Bristol Temple Meads to surrounding towns, including Nailsea and Backwell, and taxis can be hired for short journeys.

How to Get Around in Long Ashton

Long Ashton is a small village that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Long Ashton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bristol Bristol is a vibrant city that is just a short distance from Long Ashton. Here, you can explore a range of cultural attractions, nightlife, and shopping areas.

Bath The historic city of Bath is a must-see for visitors to the area, with stunning Georgian architecture, natural hot springs, and world-famous Roman baths.

Conclusion

Long Ashton is a hidden gem that offers visitors a true taste of English country life. With a range of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, this charming village has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to relax and soak up the scenery or explore the bustling nearby city of Bristol, Long Ashton is the perfect destination.
