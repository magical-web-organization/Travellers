Introduction to Taunton

Nestled in the heart of Somerset, Taunton is a charming market town with a rich history and natural beauty. Surrounded by the Quantock Hills and the Brendon Hills, Taunton has something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions.

This guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs of Taunton. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Taunton

There are plenty of places to visit in Taunton, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Taunton is home to a number of museums and historical sites, including:

  • Somerset County Museum: The museum displays a range of exhibits covering Somerset’s history, from prehistoric times to the present day.

  • The Museum of Somerset: Located in Taunton Castle, the museum tells the story of Somerset and its people through interactive exhibits and artifacts.

  • Hestercombe Gardens: These beautiful gardens are home to a range of plants and trees, as well as a collection of historic buildings and temples.

  • The Museum of Taunton: This museum showcases the history of Taunton through exhibits on art, textiles, and local industry.

Natural Attractions of Taunton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Taunton has plenty to offer. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • The Quantock Hills: These hills offer miles of walking and cycling routes, as well as stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Blackdown Hills: Located just south of Taunton, these hills are home to a variety of wildlife and offer panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • Taunton River Tone: This beautiful river runs through the town and is a popular spot for fishing and boating.

  • Taunton Vale: This scenic valley is home to a range of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and badgers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taunton

If you love hiking or cycling, Taunton is the perfect place to explore. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Quantock Hills Circular Walk: This 25-mile route takes you through the beautiful Quantock Hills, passing through quaint villages and historic landmarks.

  • The Blackdown Hills Circular Walk: This 22-mile route takes you through the picturesque Blackdown Hills, passing through stunning woodland and farmland.

  • The Two Moors Way: This 102-mile route takes you through the heart of Devon and Somerset, passing through some of the most beautiful countryside in the region.

Best Things to Do in Taunton: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Taunton, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Some of the best include:

  • The Great Western Railway Museum: Located in the restored railway station, this museum showcases the history of the Great Western Railway.

  • Vivary Adventure Centre: This outdoor center offers a range of activities, including high ropes courses, climbing walls, and archery.

  • Taunton Racecourse: If you’re a fan of horse racing, Taunton Racecourse is a great place to visit.

  • The Brewhouse Theatre: This popular theatre hosts a range of performances and plays throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taunton

If you’re planning to spend a week in Taunton, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Museum of Somerset and explore Taunton Castle
Day 2 Spend the day hiking in the Quantock Hills

Food and Drink Scene of Taunton

Taunton has a thriving food and drink scene, with a range of local restaurants, pubs, and cafes to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Somerset Cider Chicken: This traditional dish is made with locally sourced chicken, cooked with onions, cider, cream and herbs.

  • Cheddar Cheese: Somerset is famous for its cheese, and cheddar cheese is one of the most popular local dishes.

  • Somerset Apple Cake: This traditional cake is made with locally sourced apples and topped with a crumbly, sweet topping.

Local Beverages

  • Somerset Cider: Somerset is famous for its cider, with plenty of local producers to choose from.

  • Quantock Brewery: This local brewery produces a range of traditional ales and beers, using locally sourced ingredients.

  • Luscombe Organic Drinks: This local company produces a range of organic, hand-crafted drinks, including fruit juices and sodas.

Where to Stay in Taunton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re looking for a place to stay in Taunton, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the best include:

  • The Corner House Hotel: This beautiful hotel is located in a Georgian Grade II listed building and offers luxurious accommodation and a range of facilities.

  • Yarn Market Hotel: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Taunton and offers comfortable accommodation and great service.

  • Premier Inn Taunton: This budget-friendly hotel is located on the outskirts of Taunton and offers comfortable rooms and basic facilities.

What to Buy in Taunton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Taunton, here are some great options:

  • Somerset Cider: With plenty of local producers, Somerset cider is a great gift to bring home.

  • Cheddar Cheese: Made with milk sourced from local cows, cheddar cheese is a popular local product.

  • Handcrafted Pottery: From beautiful vases to functional kitchenware, Taunton is home to a range of talented potters producing wonderful, handmade pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Taunton

Taunton has a rich history and culture, with plenty of local customs and traditions to explore. Some of the most important cultural events include:

  • Somerset County Show: This annual event showcases the best of Somerset, including local food, farming, and crafts.

  • Taunton Flower Show: This two-day event is one of the largest flower shows in the country, with plenty of displays and competitions to enjoy.

  • Taunton Literary Festival: This popular festival celebrates literature and writing, with a range of talks, workshops, and events to attend.

Best Time to Visit Taunton

The best time to visit Taunton is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of events and festivals taking place. However, the town can be visited year-round and each season brings its own unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taunton

When visiting Taunton, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites.

  • Take a hike in the Quantock or Blackdown Hills.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.

  • Ignore local safety rules when hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taunton

Taunton is generally a safe town to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Some tips to stay safe include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone or in quiet areas.

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.

  • Follow local safety rules when hiking or enjoying outdoor activities.

Health in Taunton

Taunton is a healthy town to visit, with good medical facilities and services. If you need medical attention during your stay, there are a range of surgeries and hospitals to choose from.

Getting to Taunton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taunton

The nearest airport to Taunton is Bristol Airport, which is situated around 40 miles away. Other nearby airports include Exeter Airport and Cardiff Airport.

Bus Stations Near Taunton

Taunton has a central bus station, which is served by a range of local and national bus services. Some of the main operators include First Group, Stagecoach South West, and National Express.

Other Options

Taunton is also well-connected by rail, with regular services running from London, Bristol, Exeter, and Cardiff. There are also a range of taxi services and car rental options available in the town.

How to Get Around in Taunton

Taunton is a small town, and most of the main attractions and amenities can be easily reached on foot. There are also a range of local bus services, as well as taxi services and car rental options.

Where to Go Next from Taunton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the surrounding area, Taunton is a great base for exploring the West Country and beyond. Some of the best nearby destinations include:

  • Bath: This historic city is home to a range of museums, galleries, and historic sites.

  • Exmoor National Park: This stunning national park is home to a range of wildlife, as well as miles of walking and cycling routes.

  • Glastonbury: This charming town is known for its music festival, as well as its beautiful countryside and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Whether you’re here for a weekend or a week, Taunton offers something for everyone. From its rich history and culture to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, this charming town is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the West Country and beyond.
