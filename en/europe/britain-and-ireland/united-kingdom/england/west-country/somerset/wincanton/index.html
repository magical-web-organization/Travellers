Wincanton is a small town nestled in the West Country of Somerset, England. Despite its size, it offers an array of activities and attractions for tourists. From historical sites and natural attractions to outdoor activities and delicious local cuisine, Wincanton has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best things to do, see, and experience in Wincanton.

Introduction to Wincanton

Wincanton is a charming town with a population of around 5,000. The town is situated on the River Cale and has a long and rich history dating back to the 8th century. Today, it is best known for its horse racing events and vibrant community. The town is also a great starting point for exploring the surrounding countryside.

Wincanton is rich in culture and history. Here are some of the top places you should visit while in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wincanton Museum and History Society
  • The Walled Garden at Mere
  • Shepton Mallet Prison Museum
  • Glastonbury Abbey

Natural Attractions of Wincanton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • King Alfred’s Tower

  • Stonehenge

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wincanton

Wincanton is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. The town has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Wincanton Circular Walk
  • Ten Lanes Trail
  • The Macmillan Way
  • The South West Coast Path

Best Things to Do in Wincanton: Activities and Tours

Wincanton has a range of activities to suit different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Wincanton:

  • Visit the Wincanton Racecourse
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the countryside
  • Go on a cider tasting tour
  • Explore the local farmers’ market

Week-Long Itinerary for Wincanton

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Wincanton, consider this itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Wincanton Museum and History Society in the morning and the Walled Garden at Mere in the afternoon.

Day 2: Take a hike along the Wincanton Circular Walk and enjoy a picnic lunch at the top of King Alfred’s Tower.

Day 3: Spend the day at the races and try your luck at betting on the horses.

Day 4: Visit the Glastonbury Abbey and the nearby town of Glastonbury in the morning and take a hot air balloon ride over the countryside in the afternoon.

Day 5: Explore the local farmers’ market in the morning and go on a cider tasting tour in the afternoon.

Day 6: Take a bike ride along the Ten Lanes Trail.

Day 7: Visit the Stourhead House and Gardens in the morning and Stonehenge in the afternoon.

Best Time to Visit Wincanton

The best time to visit Wincanton is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild and the countryside is in full bloom. However, if you’re a fan of horse racing, the winter months are the best time to visit as this is when the National Hunt Racing season takes place.
