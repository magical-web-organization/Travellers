Introduction to Ilminster

Located in the heart of Somerset, Ilminster offers a mix of rich history, scenic beauty, and vibrant culture. This charming town is nestled in the rolling hills of West Country, offering visitors a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.

Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Ilminster has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a virtual tour of the town, highlighting its must-see attractions, hiking and biking routes, accommodations, food and drink scene, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Ilminster

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Meeting House: This historic building dates back to the 17th century and has served as a place of worship for Quakers for over 300 years. It is open to the public and offers guided tours.
  • Heritage Centre: This museum is dedicated to the history of Ilminster and features exhibitions on the town’s development, trades, and notable inhabitants.
  • Barrington Court: This stately home and gardens were once owned by the Lyle family and are now open to the public. Visitors can explore the impressive architecture and stunning gardens.

Natural Attractions of Ilminster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blackdown Hills: This Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty offers visitors breathtaking views, rolling hills, and tranquil fields. It also provides opportunities for hiking, biking, and wildlife spotting.
  • Chard Reservoir: This tranquil and picturesque lake is surrounded by verdant fields and is an ideal spot for bird watching, picnicking, and relaxing.
  • Ham Hill Country Park: This country park offers visitors an array of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, horse riding, and wildlife spotting. It also offers scenic views of the town and Blackdown Hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ilminster

  • Blackdown Hills Circular Walk: This 8-mile circular walk takes visitors through stunning countryside and tranquil villages. It is suitable for all fitness levels.
  • Ilminster to Chard Canal Walk: This 5-mile walk follows the route of the old canal and provides a tranquil and scenic escape from the hustle and bustle of the town.
  • Ham Hill Country Park Hike: This 3.5-mile hike takes visitors to the top of Ham Hill, offering stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ilminster: Activities and Tours

  • Ilminster Arts Centre: This art center offers visitors a diverse range of exhibitions, concerts, film screenings, and workshops.
  • Ilminster Town Trail: This self-guided walking tour takes visitors through the historic streets and landmarks of Ilminster.
  • Lopenhead Open Gardens: This annual event takes place in June and features a variety of stunning gardens in and around Ilminster.

Food and Drink Scene of Ilminster

Must-Try Dishes

  • Somerset Cider: Made from local apples, this refreshing cider is a must-try for anyone visiting Ilminster.
  • Cheddar Cheese: This world-famous cheese is produced in Somerset and is a must-try for cheese lovers.
  • Exmoor lamb: Locally sourced lamb from Exmoor is succulent and delicious and is a must-try for meat lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Perry: This pear cider is a refreshing alcoholic drink made from fermented pears and is a local speciality.
  • Traditional Ale: There are several local breweries in Ilminster, producing refreshing ales that are perfect to enjoy in a local pub.
  • Apple Juice: Made from locally sourced apples, the apple juice is fresh and delicious and is available in many local cafes and bakeries.

Where to Stay in Ilminster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Ilminster Experience: This bed and breakfast is located in the heart of the town and offers guests stylish and comfortable accommodation.
  • The Beeches: This guesthouse is situated in a picturesque countryside location and offers guests tranquil and peaceful surroundings.
  • The Square & Compass: This historic inn has been providing accommodation to visitors for over 400 years and offers a unique and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Ilminster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Somerset Cider: Take home a bottle of local cider to remind you of your trip to Ilminster.
  • Cheddar Cheese: This world-famous cheese is produced in Somerset and is a delicious souvenir to bring home.
  • Local Crafts: Ilminster is famous for its local crafts, including pottery, woodwork, and textiles. You can find them in local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ilminster

  • Somerset Carnivals: These annual celebrations take place throughout Somerset from September to November and include colorful and lively parades, floats, and fireworks displays.
  • Ilminster Midsummer Experience: This annual event takes place in June and includes a range of fun activities, such as a street fair, concerts, and a fun run.

Best Time to Visit Ilminster

The best time to visit Ilminster is from April to September when the weather is warm and the days are longer. However, the town has something to offer throughout the year, so it depends on personal preference.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ilminster

Do:

  • Do explore the historic streets and landmarks of the town.
  • Do try local food and drink, including Somerset cider and Cheddar cheese.
  • Do take part in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t disturb local wildlife or farm animals.
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to local people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ilminster

Ilminster is generally a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling to a new place. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Ilminster

There are several medical and dental clinics in Ilminster that can provide routine and emergency care for visitors. It is always advisable to have travel health insurance when visiting a new place.

Getting to Ilminster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ilminster

  • Bristol Airport: This international airport is located approximately 30 miles from Ilminster and offers flights to several destinations in Europe and the UK.
  • Exeter Airport: This regional airport is located approximately 25 miles from Ilminster and offers flights to several destinations in the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Ilminster

  • Taunton Bus Station: This station provides regular services to Ilminster from Taunton and nearby towns and villages.
  • Yeovil Bus Station: This station provides regular services to Ilminster from Yeovil and nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is located in Taunton, which is approximately 13 miles from Ilminster. There are regular services to London, Bristol, and other major UK cities.
  • Car: Ilminster is easily accessible by car, with the A303 and A358 providing good road links to nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Ilminster

Ilminster is a small town and is easily explored on foot. However, there are also local buses and taxis available for visitors who prefer not to walk.
