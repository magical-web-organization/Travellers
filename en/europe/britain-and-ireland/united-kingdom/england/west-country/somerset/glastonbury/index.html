Introduction to Glastonbury

Glastonbury, located in Somerset, West Country, England, is best known for its rich history and famous music festival. But beyond the festival, Glastonbury is a charming town filled with rich cultural attractions, natural wonders, and scenic views. With its mystical and spiritual heritage, Glastonbury is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Glastonbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Glastonbury Abbey

The Glastonbury Abbey, dating back to the 7th century, is a significant landmark located in the heart of the town. This legendary abbey site is believed to be the final resting place of King Arthur and Queen Guinevere, making it one of England’s most visited and mysterious historical sites.

Chalice Well

The Chalice Well, located at the foot of Glastonbury Tor, is a beautiful garden filled with natural spring waters imbued with legends of miraculous healing properties. Visitors to this site can explore the garden and learn more about the well’s history, legends, and significance.

Natural Attractions of Glastonbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Glastonbury Tor

The Glastonbury Tor is one of the most iconic landmarks in the town, with picturesque views across the Somerset Levels. This unusual hill rising from the landscape commands an ethereal presence as a site of spiritual significance to Pagans, Christians, and many other faiths.

The Levels

The Somerset Levels, located near Glastonbury, are a network of wetlands and nature reserves that provide a beautiful and serene location for nature enthusiasts. Visitors can hike, birdwatch, or cycle on the many trails throughout the area while observing the diverse wildlife and scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Glastonbury: Activities and Tours

Specialty Tours

Specialty tours in Glastonbury cater to a wide variety of interests, from archeological sites of Anglo-Saxon occupation to local pub tours with traditional English ales.

Shopping

Visitors can explore the many specialty shops in Glastonbury for new age, mystical, and spiritual products, crystals, and unique art pieces.

Glastonbury Festival

The Glastonbury Festival is an annual music festival, held in late June, near Pilton village. This world-famous festival boasts an incredible lineup of artists, performers, and cultural experiences that draw in visitors from around the globe.

Food and Drink Scene of Glastonbury

Must-Try Dishes

Glastonbury is known for its eclectic food scene, including vegetarian and vegan options. Popular dishes include:

  • Glastonbury Brie and Apple Sandwich
  • Organic Local Cider
  • Cream Tea (tea and scones with clotted cream and jam)
  • Somerset lamb and beef dishes

Local Beverages

Cheddar and Somerset Valley ciders are some of the most popular local drinks in Glastonbury.

Local Culture and Customs of Glastonbury

Glastonbury is steeped in Pagan and Christian religious traditions, and it has become home to many spiritual practices, events, and communities. Visitors can participate in spiritual practices, join group meditation or attend events in Glastonbury.

Best Time to Visit Glastonbury

The best times to visit Glastonbury are spring and summer when the weather is mild, and the landscape is vibrant and colorful from late April to early September.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glastonbury

Glastonbury is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take necessary precautions like:

  • Being aware of crowded areas, especially during Glastonbury Festival.
