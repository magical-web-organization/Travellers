Watchet is a charming coastal town located in Somerset, a county in the South West of England. With its rich history, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant culture, Watchet is a popular destination among travelers seeking a laid-back getaway in the British countryside. In this ultimate travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best of Watchet, from its top attractions and natural wonders to its diverse food and drink scene, where to stay, and how to get there. So, let’s get started!

Introduction to Watchet

Watchet is a small town situated on the coast of the Bristol Channel. Its quaint harbor is surrounded by a charming collection of shops, museums, restaurants, and pubs, all of which are steeped in history. The town has a rich maritime heritage, which is reflected in its architecture and atmosphere, making it a unique and fascinating place to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Watchet

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventurer seeking a thrill, Watchet has plenty of things to see and do. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Watchet - Exhibiting Watchet’s rich maritime heritage, this museum is a must-visit and is situated in the heart of the town.
  • Market House Museum - Dating back to the 19th century, the museum features exhibits about the history of Watchet and surrounding areas.
  • St. Decuman’s Church - A historic church with a rich history dating back to the 8th century.
  • Daws Castle - A historic hill fort that has stood for over 2000 years! Get a magnificent view of the coastline from the top.

Natural Attractions of Watchet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Watchet Harbour - The charming harbor is the perfect spot to enjoy a peaceful stroll, boat ride, fishing, or watching the sunset.
  • East Quantoxhead Nature Reserve - This beautiful nature reserve offers stunning scenery and several footpaths for hiking and walking.
  • Watchet Beach - A great place to relax and take a dip in the sea.
  • Quantock Hills - A vast area of beautiful moorland and coast, perfect for hiking, biking, and horse riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watchet

Watchet has plenty of scenic hiking and biking routes that you can explore. Here are a few popular ones:

  • The Mineral Line Trail - A three-mile-long trail that follows the route of a former railway line connecting Washford and Watchet.
  • The Coleridge Way - A 36-mile walk that connects Nether Stowey and Lynmouth, with stunning landscapes along the way.
  • Quantock Circular Walk - This 11-mile walk takes you to some of the best views in the Quantock Hills.

Best Things to Do in Watchet: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun activities to do in Watchet? Here are some of our top picks:

  • Watchet Boat Museum - Take a ride in a beautiful old boat and feel the sea breeze in your hair, or climb aboard a paddle steamer from the early days of steam power!
  • Lido Outdoor Pool - Take a dip in one of the UK’s longest surviving outdoor pools - a heated saltwater pool that overlooks the magnificent sea.
  • Watchet Ghost Tour - Discover the spooky history of Watchet with a guided ghost tour around the town!
  • Fossil Hunting - Watchet is well known for its fossils - visit the local fossil shops or head out into the tidal zone to hunt for ammonites and other ancient treasures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watchet

If you’re planning to spend a week in Watchet, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Watchet Harbor, its quaint shops, and historic buildings.
  • Day 2: Visit the Museum of Watchet and Market House Museum to learn about the town’s history.
  • Day 3: Head out to East Quantoxhead Nature Reserve and enjoy a walk in nature.
  • Day 4: Discover the ancient Daws Castle and take in the stunning coastline views.
  • Day 5: Take a trip into the Quantock Hills and try out a hiking or biking trail.
  • Day 6: Go on a guided ghost tour of Watchet.
  • Day 7: Relax on Watchet Beach and watch the sunset over the Bristol Channel.

Food and Drink Scene of Watchet

Watchet also has a fantastic food and drink scene, with something for everyone. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Somerset Cider Cake - A sweet, moist cake made with locally grown apples and Somerset cider.
  • Cream Tea - A West Country tradition consisting of scones, clotted cream, and strawberry jam served with a pot of tea.
  • Fish and Chips - A classic British dish, enjoyed at many of Watchet’s fish and chip restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Somerset Cider - A refreshing alcoholic beverage brewed from the cider apples that the region is so famous for.
  • Exmoor Ale - A popular local beer that is brewed in the nearby town of Wiveliscombe.
  • West Country Tea - A blend of tea produced from the finest leaves grown in West Country soil.

Where to Stay in Watchet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Watchet offers a wide range of accommodation options, from charming guest houses to luxurious spa hotels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The White Horse Inn - A cozy inn located in the heart of Watchet, offering traditional Somerset hospitality.
  • Market House Bed and Breakfast - A charming B&B in a historic building that dates back to the 16th century.
  • The Royal Oak Inn - A family-run pub that offers comfortable rooms and delicious food.
  • The Washford Inn - A picturesque inn located in the nearby village of Washford, offering stylish rooms and hearty meals.

What to Buy in Watchet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique and locally-produced souvenirs to take back with you, here are a few suggestions:

  • Watchet Cider - Take home a bottle of the locally produced cider.
  • Sheepskin Products - Sheepskin rugs, slippers, and other products are produced in the West Country, and make a great gift.
  • Fossils - Buy a piece of Watchet’s history by purchasing a locally sourced fossil.

Local Culture and Customs of Watchet

Watchet is known for its rich history, maritime traditions, and strong community spirit. The town hosts several cultural events throughout the year, including the famous Watchet Music Festival and Carnival.

Best Time to Visit Watchet

The best time to visit Watchet is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also charming during the winter months, when the streets are quieter and the Christmas lights come out.

Do’s and Don’ts of Watchet

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Watchet:

Do:

  • Explore the town on foot and take in the charming sights.
  • Try local dishes, such as Somerset Cider Cake and Cream Tea.
  • Meet the friendly locals and learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets or beaches.
  • Ignore warning signs on the beaches and tidal areas.
  • Disturb wildlife and natural habitats in the nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Watchet is generally a safe place to visit, but as with any travel destination, there are a few safety and health considerations to keep in mind.

Safety in Watchet

  • Be aware of coastal erosion and cliff falls when walking on the beaches and coastal paths.
  • Always follow the safety guidelines provided by activity providers.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure, especially in tourist areas and on public transport.

Health in Watchet

  • Tap water in Watchet is safe to drink.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent, as the town is near to nature reserves and there may be insects or wildlife around.
  • Be aware of poison ivy or oak if hiking around the nature reserves.

Getting to Watchet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Watchet is easily accessible by road or rail. Here are a few options to consider:

Airports Near Watchet

Bristol Airport is the closest airport, situated around 24 miles away from Watchet.

Bus Stations Near Watchet

There are several bus stations in the area, including Taunton Bus Station and Minehead Bus Station, which are both around 30 minutes away by car.

Other Options

You can also reach Watchet by train, with the nearest train station being the Watchet railway station. Alternatively, you can take a taxi or rent a car.

How to Get Around in Watchet

Watchet is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Bikes are also a great option for exploring the nearby areas and Quantock Hills. Taxis are available for those who prefer not to walk or cycle.

Where to Go Next from Watchet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Somerset and the surrounding areas, here are a few destinations worth visiting:

  • Glastonbury - A mystical town famed for its tor, festival, and enigmatic history.
  • Exmoor National Park - A vast wild expanse of moor, coastline, and forestry.
  • Cheddar Gorge - A stunning limestone gorge and cave system filled with adventure.
  • Porlock - A small harbor town situated on the edge of Exmoor.

Conclusion

There you have it - a comprehensive guide to Watchet!
