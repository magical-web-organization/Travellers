Introduction to Bridgwater

Bridgwater is a historic market town located in Somerset, in the West Country of England. The town has a rich history and is known for its involvement in the English Civil War and the Monmouth Rebellion. Today, Bridgwater is a vibrant town with a mix of modern amenities and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Bridgwater

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bridgwater is home to several museums and historical attractions that showcase the town’s fascinating past. The Bridgwater Museum is a must-visit attraction and showcases the town’s history through its extensive collection of artefacts and exhibits. The Blake Museum is another historical attraction and is named after Admiral Robert Blake, one of Bridgwater’s most famous residents.

Other notable historical landmarks include the Bridgwater Castle ruins, the Somerset Brick and Tile Museum, and King Square.

Natural Attractions of Bridgwater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bridgwater is surrounded by beautiful countryside and offers many opportunities for outdoor adventures. The Quantock Hills, located a short drive from Bridgwater, is a designated Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty and is a popular destination for hiking, cycling, and horse riding.

Other natural attractions in Bridgwater include the Bridgwater Bay National Nature Reserve, the Willow Man sculpture at Junction 23 on the M5, and the Walled Gardens of Cannington.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridgwater

The Quantock Hills offers many hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness. One of the most popular hiking trails is the Coleridge Way, a 51-mile long-distance trail that connects Nether Stowey and Lynmouth. The trail offers stunning views of the Quantock Hills and the Exmoor National Park.

For mountain bikers, the Quantock Hills has several trails, including the Great Wood Trail and the Wills Neck Loop.

Best Things to Do in Bridgwater: Activities and Tours

Bridgwater offers many activities and tours to suit all interests. For history buffs, the Bridgwater Town Trail is a self-guided walking tour that showcases the town’s historical landmarks. For thrill-seekers, the Bridgwater Bay Kitesurfing School offers kite surfing lessons for all skill levels.

Other popular activities in Bridgwater include fishing, golfing at the Enmore Park Golf Club, and visiting the nearby Tropiquaria Wildlife Park. Guided tours are also available to explore the surrounding countryside and nearby attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bridgwater

Day 1: Visit the Bridgwater Museum and explore the town’s historic landmarks on the Bridgwater Town Trail.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Quantock Hills and hike one of the Coleridge Way trails.

Day 3: Visit the Bridgwater Bay National Nature Reserve and explore the Wills Neck Loop mountain biking trail.

Day 4: Learn to kite surf at Bridgwater Bay Kitesurfing School.

Day 5: Play a round at Enmore Park Golf Club and visit the Walled Gardens of Cannington.

Day 6: Explore nearby attractions such as Glastonbury Tor and Cheddar Gorge.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Bridgwater

Must-Try Dishes

Bridgwater is known for its traditional English cuisine, including fish and chips, bangers and mash, and steak and ale pie. The local Blackmore Farm beef is highly recommended and can be found at many of the town’s restaurants.

For a sweet treat, try a slice of Somerset apple cake or a traditional cream tea served with clotted cream and homemade jam.

Local Beverages

Bridgwater has several local breweries that produce traditional ales and ciders. The Quantock Brewery and the Cotleigh Brewery are popular among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Bridgwater: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bridgwater offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets. The Castle at Taunton is a luxury hotel housed in a 12th-century castle and offers stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

For budget-friendly options, the YHA Bridgwater and the Admiral Blake Guesthouse are highly recommended.

What to Buy in Bridgwater: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bridgwater has several local shops that sell handmade souvenirs and local products. The Bridgwater Farmers’ Market is held on the third Saturday of every month and offers locally sourced produce and crafts.

Other popular souvenirs include Somerset cider, local jam and chutney, and handmade woollen products.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridgwater

Bridgwater has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its many festivals and events. The Guy Fawkes Carnival is one of the biggest illuminated carnivals in the world and attracts thousands of visitors each year.

Another cultural event in Bridgwater is the Green Scythe Fair, a celebration of traditional farming methods and sustainability.

Best Time to Visit Bridgwater

The best time to visit Bridgwater is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also when many of the town’s festivals and events are held.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bridgwater

Do:

  • Take a guided tour of the town’s historical landmarks.
  • Enjoy traditional English cuisine, including Blackmore Farm beef.
  • Attend one of the town’s many festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Dispose of litter in public areas.
  • Ignore the rules and regulations of national parks and nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bridgwater

Bridgwater is a safe town with low crime rates, but visitors should still be vigilant with their personal belongings.

Health in Bridgwater

Bridgwater has several medical centres and hospitals, including the Bridgwater Community Hospital, to cater to any health issues.

Getting to Bridgwater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bridgwater

The nearest airports to Bridgwater are Bristol Airport and Exeter Airport, both located approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bridgwater

The Bridgwater Bus Station is located in the town centre and offers regular services to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Bridgwater is easily accessible by car, with the M5 motorway running through the town. Train services are also available from Bridgwater Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Bridgwater

Bridgwater is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. There are also several local bus services that serve the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bridgwater: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bridgwater is conveniently located near many popular destinations, including the city of Bristol and the coastal town of Weston-Super-Mare. The Glastonbury Tor and Cheddar Gorge are also nearby attractions that are worth a visit.

Conclusion

Bridgwater is a unique and charming town that offers a mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s historical landmarks or hiking in the nearby Quantock Hills, Bridgwater has something to offer for everyone.
