Langport Travel Guide

Located in Somerset, in the West Country region of England, Langport is a charming town with a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural scenery. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Langport has plenty to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Langport. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Langport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Langport and River Parrett Visitor Centre: This museum showcases the history of Langport and the surrounding area through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Muchelney Abbey: A stunning ruined abbey dating back to the 7th century. The site offers stunning views and is perfect for photography enthusiasts.
  • Aller Hill: A Bronze Age burial mound with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Hanging Chapel: A unique 15th century chapel built above an archway in the middle of the town.

Natural Attractions of Langport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The River Parrett Trail: A scenic walking route that follows the River Parrett through the Somerset Levels. It’s a great way to explore the beautiful landscapes of Langport.
  • Burrow Hill: The highest point in the area, Burrow Hill offers stunning views of Langport and the surrounding countryside. It’s a popular spot for paragliding and hang gliding.
  • Muchelney Ham: A charming village on the banks of the River Parrett, known for its beautiful old houses, narrow streets, and tranquil atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Langport

There are many great hiking and biking routes in and around Langport. Some of the most popular ones include the River Parrett Trail, the Somerset Levels, and the South West Coast Path. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or just starting out, Langport has plenty of trails to explore.

Best Things to Do in Langport: Activities and Tours

  • Langport Boat Hire: Rent a boat and explore the picturesque waterways of the River Parrett.
  • Cox’s Taylors Lakes: A great place for fishing and wildlife watching.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Soar over the stunning Somerset countryside with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Langport Treasure Trail: A fun and interactive way to explore the town and its history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Langport

Day 1: Langport and River Parrett Visitor Centre, Hanging Chapel, and Langport Treasure Trail

Day 2: Muchelney Abbey, Aller Hill, and Cox’s Taylors Lakes

Day 3: The River Parrett Trail, Burrow Hill, and Langport Boat Hire

Day 4: Hot air balloon ride over the Somerset countryside

Day 5: Leisurely day exploring the charming village of Muchelney Ham

Day 6: South West Coast Path

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the stunning natural views from your accommodation or take a day trip to nearby attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Langport

Must-Try Dishes

  • Somerset Cider: Made from locally grown apples, this is a refreshing and quintessentially English drink.
  • Cheddar Cheese: Produced in the nearby village of Cheddar, this cheese is famous around the world for its creamy texture and sharp flavour.
  • Ploughman’s Lunch: A traditional English meal consisting of bread, cheese, pickles, and cold cuts.

Local Beverages

  • Langport Brewery: A microbrewery that produces a range of craft beers and ales, all made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Burrow Hill Cider: A family-owned cider producer that makes award-winning ciders from their orchards in the Somerset countryside.

Where to Stay in Langport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hurds Hill: A luxury Georgian farmhouse that offers bed and breakfast accommodation in a tranquil setting.
  • The Old Pound Inn: A charming 16th-century pub that offers comfortable guest rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Langport Arms: A traditional coaching inn that offers elegant rooms and a cosy bar area.

What to Buy in Langport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Langport Farmers Market: Held every Friday, this market offers a range of locally produced goods and crafts.
  • Cheese from the Cheddar Gorge Cheese Company: A must-try local product that makes a great gift.
  • Traditional Somerset Cider: Take home a bottle of the local cider to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Langport

Langport has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions still observed today. Some of the most notable include the annual Langport Festival and the Wassail Ceremony, held in January to bless the local cider orchards.

Best Time to Visit Langport

The best time to visit Langport is from April to October, when the weather is mild and the countryside is in full bloom. However, Langport is a year-round destination and has plenty to offer even in the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Langport

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the Somerset countryside.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cider and cheese.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in the countryside.
  • Ignore safety signs when exploring natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the local heritage and history.

Safety and Health

Safety in Langport

Langport is a safe destination, with low levels of crime and a friendly community. However, it’s important to take sensible precautions, such as locking your accommodation and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Langport

Langport has a good standard of healthcare, with several nearby hospitals and clinics. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance before you visit, and take any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Langport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Langport

  • Bristol Airport: 36 miles
  • Exeter Airport: 46 miles
  • Southampton Airport: 96 miles

Bus Stations Near Langport

  • Langport Bus Station: Served by local buses
  • Taunton Bus Station: 13 miles away, served by national and international buses

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is Taunton, which has frequent services to London and other major UK cities.
  • Car: Langport is easily accessible by car, with good motorway links to London and the South West.

How to Get Around in Langport

Langport is a small town that is easily explored on foot or by bike. If you’re planning to explore the surrounding countryside, you may want to consider renting a car or taking a bus tour.

Where to Go Next from Langport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Glastonbury: Famous for its Tor and annual music festival, Glastonbury is a quirky town with a unique vibe.
  • Wells: Known for its stunning cathedral and historic architecture, Wells is a must-visit destination in the West Country.
  • Exmoor National Park: A stunning natural park that offers breathtaking views, hiking trails, and wildlife watching opportunities.

Conclusion

Langport is a hidden gem in the heart of the West Country, with plenty of history, culture, and natural beauty to explore. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to visit Langport and discover all that this charming town has to offer. Happy travels!
