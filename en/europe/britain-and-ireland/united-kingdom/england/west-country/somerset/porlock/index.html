A Complete Travel Guide to Porlock, Somerset, England

Porlock is a charming village located in the county of Somerset, South West England. The village dates back to the medieval era, and its picturesque and tranquil setting make it a popular destination among tourists. With miles of scenic walks, beautiful buildings, and delightful local cuisine, Porlock is the perfect place for a vacation. Here is a complete travel guide to Porlock, including places to visit, things to do, food and drink, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Porlock

Whether you are interested in history, culture, natural beauty, or outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in Porlock. Here is a list of some of the best places to visit in Porlock:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • All Saints Church – A beautiful 13th-century church that has recently been restored.
  • Porlock Museum – A fascinating museum that displays local history and artifacts.
  • Dunkery Beacon – A historic landmark that offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Porlock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Porlock Weir – A picturesque harbor with stunning views of the Bristol Channel.
  • Exmoor National Park – A vast national park with a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, and wildlife watching.
  • Porlock Bay – A scenic bay that offers breathtaking views of the coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Porlock

Porlock is the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes in Porlock:

  • South West Coast Path – A scenic hiking trail that stretches from Minehead to Poole.
  • Coleridge Way – A long-distance hiking trail that passes through Porlock and spans over three counties.
  • Porlock Hill Road – A challenging cycling route that offers stunning views of Exmoor.

Best Things to Do in Porlock: Activities and Tours

There are endless possibilities for things to do in Porlock. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Horse Riding – Take a horseback ride through the scenic trails of Exmoor.
  • Fishing – Try your hand at fishing in Porlock Bay.
  • Boat Tours – Take a boat tour along the stunning coastline of Porlock.

Week-Long Itinerary for Porlock

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Porlock:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the village of Porlock and visit Porlock Museum.
Day 2 Hike along the Coleridge Way.
Day 3 Visit Exmoor National Park and go wildlife watching.
Day 4 Enjoy a horseback ride through the scenic trails of Exmoor.
Day 5 Take a boat tour along the stunning coastline of Porlock Bay.
Day 6 Try your hand at fishing in Porlock Bay.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery of Porlock.

Food and Drink Scene of Porlock

Porlock has a diverse food and drink scene that caters to all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Porlock Bay Oysters – Fresh oysters harvested from Porlock Bay.
  • West Country Cream Tea – Scones served with clotted cream and jam.
  • Exmoor Lamb – A locally sourced lamb that is tender and delicious.

Local Beverages

  • Exmoor Ale – A local beer brewed in the village of Wiveliscombe.
  • Somerset Cider – A refreshing cider made from apples grown in Somerset.
  • West Country Gin – A gin made from locally sourced botanicals.

Where to Stay in Porlock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Porlock has a variety of accommodation options available, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Porlock:

  • The Luttrell Arms Hotel – A historic hotel located in the heart of Porlock.
  • The Castle Hotel – A luxury hotel set in a 17th-century building.
  • YHA Porlock – A budget-friendly hostel located in the village.

What to Buy in Porlock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Porlock has a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best things to buy in Porlock:

  • Porlock Bay Oysters – Fresh oysters harvested from Porlock Bay.
  • Exmoor Ales – A variety of local beers brewed in the West Country.
  • Handmade Soaps – Handmade soaps made from locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Porlock

Porlock has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some of the customs and traditions unique to Porlock:

  • The Hobby Horse – A tradition dating back to the 16th century where a man dressed as a horse parades through the village during the May Day celebrations.
  • The Porlock Apple Day – An annual event held in October to celebrate the apple harvest.

Best Time to Visit Porlock

The best time to visit Porlock is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Porlock is also beautiful during the autumn months when the leaves of Exmoor National Park turn golden and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Porlock

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Porlock:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine – Porlock has a variety of delicious local dishes that are a must-try.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions – Porlock has a rich cultural heritage that should be respected.
  • Make use of public transport – Public transport is a great way to explore Porlock and the surrounding areas.

Don’t:

  • Litter – Keep Porlock clean and tidy by disposing of litter properly.
  • Disturb the wildlife – Exmoor National Park is home to a variety of wildlife that should be admired from a distance.
  • Park in restricted areas – Be aware of parking restrictions in Porlock and stick to designated areas.

Safety and Health

Porlock is a safe destination, and there are no major health risks associated with visiting the area. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Porlock

  • Be aware of steep hills and steps – Porlock has a lot of hills and steps that can be challenging to navigate.
  • Take care when hiking – Always bring appropriate clothing and equipment when hiking in Exmoor National Park.
  • Use designated crossings when crossing the road – Be aware of traffic when crossing the road in Porlock.

Health in Porlock

  • Stay hydrated – Bring plenty of water when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Wear sunscreen – Protect your skin from the sun when spending time outdoors.
  • Take care when eating seafood – Always choose seafood from reputable sources to avoid the risk of food poisoning.

Getting to Porlock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Porlock is easily accessible by public transport and private car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Porlock

  • Bristol Airport – Bristol Airport is located approximately 60 miles from Porlock.

Bus Stations Near Porlock

  • Minehead Bus Station – Minehead Bus Station offers regular services to Porlock.
  • Taunton Bus Station – Taunton Bus Station offers regular services to Porlock.

Other Options

  • Train – The nearest train station is located in Taunton, which offers regular services to London and Bristol.
  • Car Rental – Car rental is available at Bristol Airport and Taunton.

How to Get Around in Porlock

Porlock is a small village, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, there are also cycling and bus routes available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Porlock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Porlock is an excellent base from which to explore the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Dunster Castle and Gardens – A beautiful castle and gardens located in the nearby village of Dunster.
  • Lynton and Lynmouth – A picturesque seaside town located on the North Devon coast.
  • West Somerset Railway – A historic heritage railway that runs from Minehead to Bishops Lydeard.

Conclusion

Porlock is a charming and tranquil village that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its beautiful architecture and national park to its delicious cuisine and outdoor activities, Porlock has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the magic of this beautiful village.
