Introduction to Wells

Wells is a small city in Somerset, located in the West Country region of England. It is widely known for its stunning architecture, charming streets, and the Wells Cathedral - one of the most notable landmarks in the entire country. The city has a rich history that dates back over a thousand years, with a plethora of cultural attractions and outdoor activities that make it an ideal tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Wells

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wells Cathedral

The Wells Cathedral is the primary landmark of the city and one of the most significant religious buildings in the UK. It is known for its remarkable architecture, great beauty, and rich history. The cathedral was built between 1175 and 1490, and it features some of the rarest architectural styles in the world.

The Bishop’s Palace and Gardens

The Bishop’s Palace is another landmark in the city of Wells. It is a medieval palace that was once the residence of the Bishop of Bath and Wells. The palace features charming gardens, a moat, and medieval ruins that add to the ambiance.

The Wells & Mendip Museum

The Wells & Mendip Museum was established in 1893 and is a popular attraction due to its extensive collection of historical artifacts that relate to the city’s history. It covers the geology, archaeology, and social history of Wells and the surrounding region.

Natural Attractions of Wells: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wookey Hole Caves

The Wookey Hole Caves are a stunning natural attraction situated just 2 miles from Wells. The caves are a series of limestone caverns that offer a unique and unforgettable adventure. Visitors can explore the underground caves on a guided tour, and enjoy attractions like the mirror maze, crazy golf, and a 4D cinema.

Cheddar Gorge

Cheddar Gorge is another must-visit destination near Wells. The Gorge is a natural limestone canyon with its steep sides and spectacular cliff faces that provide stunning views. Visitors can hike or cycle through the gorge, or try their hand in rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wells

Wells offers several hiking and biking routes that take visitors through some of the most scenic landscapes in the region. The West Mendip Way, which starts at the seaside town, Weston-super-Mare, enjoys views of the Bristol Channel. Visitors could make their way through the stunning Cheddar Gorge, where hikes are available around the cliffs or down in the caves at various levels.

Best Things to Do in Wells: Activities and Tours

Wells Walking Tour

One of the best ways to experience Wells is to take a walking tour. There are several guided tours available that take visitors through the city’s most charming and historical streets, famous landmarks, and places of interest, like the cathedral and Bishop’s Palace.

Hot Air Balloon Ride over Wells

For a unique experience while visiting Wells, visitors can book a hot air balloon ride available throughout the year, offering breathtaking views of the beautiful countryside, rolling hills, and local landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Wells

Must-Try Dishes

Wells has an exceptional food and drink scene, characterized by locally sourced ingredients used to create delicious dishes. A few must-try dishes include:

  • Somerset Cider Braised Pork Shoulder
  • West Country Cheese Plate
  • Steamed Exmoor Mussels with Garlic Butter
  • Pork and Apple Pie with Mash Potato
  • Traditional Roast Beef with Yorkshire Pudding and Gravy.

Local Beverages

Wells is known for its cider production, which has been a part of the economy since the 16th Century. The region produces several regional ciders, including Perry, which is made from pear juice instead of apples.

What to Buy in Wells: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wells offers several locally made products that are ideal souvenirs. The art and craft shops around the High Street pride themselves on locally produced crafts. Here are some standout items:

  • Handmade pottery and Ceramic pieces
  • Pure honey from East Lambrook
  • Local cheddar cheese
  • Ciders and Perry from local producers.

Local Culture and Customs of Wells

The local culture of Wells is deeply ingrained in religion and tradition. By attending cultural events, such as Glastonbury Music Festival and the Wells Cathedral Festival; guests can observe the local customs and how they are celebrated.

Best Time to Visit Wells

The best time to visit Wells is between May and September when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities, but tourists should avoid visiting during the peak season to avoid crowds and high costs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wells

Wells is a safe city that attracts many tourists every year. Visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take basic precautions like keeping their valuables safe, not leaving them unattended in crowded areas, and locking their accommodations’ windows and doors at night.

Health in Wells

The city of Wells has several medical facilities with excellent medical care. Visitors should purchase travel insurance in case of an unexpected medical emergency.
