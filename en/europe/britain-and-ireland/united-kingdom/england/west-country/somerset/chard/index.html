Introduction to Chard

Located in Somerset, England, Chard is a town known for its rich cultural history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. With its charming streets, beautiful gardens, and lively pubs, it’s easy to see why Chard is a popular destination among locals and tourists alike. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s historic landmarks, hiking through its beautiful countryside, or indulging in its delicious local cuisine, you’re sure to find plenty to see and do in Chard.

List of Places to Visit in Chard

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chard Museum: This small but informative museum is dedicated to the town’s rich history and is located in the center of Chard. Visitors can learn about the town’s past through exhibits that feature local artifacts, photographs, and artwork.

  • Forde Abbey and Gardens: Located just a short drive from Chard, Forde Abbey and Gardens is a beautiful historic home and garden that dates back to the 12th century. Visitors can tour the house, which is filled with art and antiques, and take a stroll through the stunning gardens.

  • Loughwood Meeting House: This historic Quaker meeting house is one of the oldest of its kind in the country and is open to visitors for tours. It’s located just a few miles outside of Chard and is well worth the trip.

Natural Attractions of Chard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blackdown Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty: Just a short distance from Chard is the Blackdown Hills, an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty that’s perfect for hiking, cycling, and enjoying the stunning scenery.

  • Chard Reservoir: This picturesque reservoir is a popular destination for fishing and birdwatching. Visitors can also enjoy a scenic walk around the water’s edge.

  • Cotleigh Brewery: For beer lovers, a visit to the Cotleigh Brewery is a must. This award-winning brewery offers tours and tastings of its delicious beers, all brewed on-site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chard

One of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of Chard is on foot or by bike. Here are a few popular routes:

  • The Blackdown Hills Circular Walk: This 11-mile circular walk takes in some of the most beautiful countryside in the region, with stunning views and plenty of wildlife to spot along the way.

  • The Chard to Ilminster Cycle Route: This mostly flat, 10-mile cycle route takes riders through some of the most picturesque villages in the area, with plenty of pubs and cafes to stop at along the way.

Best Things to Do in Chard: Activities and Tours

  • Chard Carnival: Held annually in October, the Chard Carnival is a highlights of the town’s calendar and features a procession of colorful floats and costumes.

  • Chardstock Street Fayre: This popular event takes place in August and features live music, food stalls, and craft vendors.

  • Chardstock Art Show: This annual art show is held in April and features work by local artists and craftsmen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chard

If you’re planning to spend a week in Chard, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore Chard town center, including the Chard Museum, local shops, and pubs.

  • Day 2: Visit Forde Abbey and Gardens.

  • Day 3: Hike or bike the Blackdown Hills circular walk.

  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the seaside resort of Lyme Regis.

  • Day 5: Go fishing or birdwatching at Chard Reservoir.

  • Day 6: Take a tour and tasting at Cotleigh Brewery.

  • Day 7: Attend a local event or festival, such as the Chard Carnival or Chardstock Street Fayre.

Food and Drink Scene of Chard

Must-Try Dishes

  • Somerset Cider: Made from locally grown apples, Somerset cider is a refreshing and delicious drink that’s a must-try when in Chard.

  • Cheddar Cheese: One of the most famous cheeses in the world, Cheddar cheese is made just a short distance from Chard and is a staple of the local cuisine.

  • Beef and Ale Pie: A hearty and delicious pie made with locally sourced beef and ale.

Local Beverages

  • Palmers Brewery: This local brewery has been producing award-winning beers since 1794 and is a popular destination for beer lovers.

  • Somerset Cider Brandy: Made from locally grown apples, Somerset cider brandy is a delicious and potent drink that’s perfect for sipping on a cold evening.

Where to Stay in Chard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Bell Inn: This charming inn is located in the heart of Chard and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly pub serving local ale and food.

  • Ammonite Lodge: This elegant guesthouse is just a short distance from Chard and offers spacious rooms and a delicious breakfast.

  • The Lordleaze Hotel: This modern hotel is situated in a peaceful location just outside of Chard and offers comfortable rooms and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Chard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheddar Cheese: As mentioned before, Cheddar cheese is a must-try when in Chard, so why not bring some home with you?

  • Local Cider: Take home a bottle of locally-produced Somerset cider to enjoy at home.

  • Quirky Gifts: Chard is home to many quirky independent shops, so be sure to browse for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Chard

Chard is a friendly and welcoming town, but there are a few local customs and traditions to be aware of. For example, it’s customary to buy a round of drinks for your friends in a pub, and tipping is generally expected at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Chard

If you’re planning a trip to Chard, the best time to visit is between May and September, when the weather is mild and many of the local attractions are open for the season. However, the town is also beautiful in the shoulder season, when the crowds are smaller and the countryside is ablaze with autumn colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chard

Do:

  • Do try the local cider and Cheddar cheese.

  • Do explore the beautiful countryside on foot or by bike.

  • Do attend a local festival or event.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing, even in the summer months.

  • Don’t forget to bring cash, as many local shops and pubs may not accept credit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chard

Chard is generally a safe town, but visitors should be aware of the usual safety precautions such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking car doors.

Health in Chard

Chard has a small medical center that can handle minor injuries and illnesses. For more serious conditions, visitors should seek medical attention in a nearby city.

Getting to Chard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chard

The closest airports to Chard are Exeter International Airport and Bristol Airport, both of which are about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Chard

Chard is served by a number of bus services, including National Express coaches and local services run by Stagecoach.

Other Options

If you’re traveling from London, Chard is easily accessible by train. The nearest train station is Axminster, which is just a short bus ride away.

How to Get Around in Chard

Chard is a small town, so most of the attractions are close enough to walk to. For those who prefer to drive, there is plenty of parking available, or visitors can take advantage of the local bus services.

Where to Go Next from Chard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lyme Regis: This picturesque seaside town is just a short drive from Chard and is known for its beautiful beaches and historic harbour.

  • Exeter: This ancient city is home to a beautiful cathedral, historic underground passages, and plenty of shops and restaurants.

  • Glastonbury: Just a short drive from Chard is the ancient town of Glastonbury, known for its legends of King Arthur and its spiritual significance.

Conclusion

Chard is a beautiful and welcoming town that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, and relaxation. Whether you’re interested in hiking the Blackdown Hills, sampling local cider and cheese, or exploring the town’s historic landmarks, you’re sure to find plenty to see and do in Chard.
