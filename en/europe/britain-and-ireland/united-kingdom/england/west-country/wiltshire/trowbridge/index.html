Introduction to Trowbridge

Nestled in the picturesque county of Wiltshire, Trowbridge is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its vibrant cultural scene, historic landmarks, and peaceful natural settings, Trowbridge is an ideal travel destination for anyone looking to explore the West Country.

List of Places to Visit in Trowbridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trowbridge’s rich history is reflected in the many museums and historical landmarks that the town has to offer. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Trowbridge Museum: This multi-award-winning museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that illustrate the town’s rich history, from the wool trade origins to the present day.

  • St. James’ Church: This beautiful church dates back to the 13th century and is known for its intricate architecture and stunning stained glass windows.

  • Trowbridge Arts Centre: This community-run arts and performance center features exhibits, workshops, and events showcasing local talent and culture.

  • Iford Manor and the Peto Garden: This stunning Italianate garden, located just outside of Trowbridge, features symmetrical designs and exquisite sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Trowbridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trowbridge is also home to some beautiful natural settings, parks, and trails. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions:

  • Kennet and Avon Canal: This beautiful waterway stretches 87 miles, connecting Trowbridge to Bristol and Reading. Enjoy a relaxing boat ride or take a stroll along the towpaths.

  • Trowbridge Park: This tranquil urban park boasts beautiful gardens, a children’s playground, and a skate park.

  • Southwick Country Park: This 200-acre country park is ideal for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, with its woodlands, meadows, and lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trowbridge

Trowbridge is situated in the heart of the West Country, making it an ideal place for hiking and biking enthusiasts to explore the scenic countryside and nearby villages. Here are some of the top hiking and biking trails to try:

  • The Wessex Ridgeway: This 60-mile trail passes through Trowbridge and offers stunning views of the countryside and historic landmarks.

  • The Great Stones Way: This 36-mile trail connects the ancient monuments of Avebury and Stonehenge and takes you through peaceful countryside and quaint villages.

  • The Colliers Way: This 23-mile walking and cycling path follows the former railway line between Bath and Frome, taking you through beautiful greenery and charming towns.

Best Things to Do in Trowbridge: Activities and Tours

Aside from the cultural and natural attractions, Trowbridge also offers various activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Victoria Park and Maze: This hedge maze in the heart of Victoria Park is a fun activity for all ages, providing hours of entertainment and challenges.

  • Escape Trowbridge: This escape room experience offers a thrilling challenge for groups of friends and family, with escape rooms set in different themes and settings.

  • Trowbridge Ghost Walks: Take a guided tour through Trowbridge’s haunted history and explore the tales of ghosts, mysteries, and legends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trowbridge

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to fully explore and enjoy Trowbridge and its surroundings:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Trowbridge town center, including Trowbridge Museum and St. James’ Church
Day 2 Take a boat ride or walk along the Kennet and Avon Canal, and visit Iford Manor and the Peto Garden
Day 3 Enjoy the beautiful landscapes of Southwick Country Park and back to Trowbridge for an evening performance at Trowbridge Arts Center
Day 4 Hike the Great Stones Way from Avebury to Trowbridge
Day 5 Visit nearby cities of Bath and Bristol
Day 6 Cycle the Colliers Way from Bath to Frome
Day 7 Enjoy the fun and games at Victoria Park and Maze and end the day with a spooky Trowbridge Ghost Walk

Food and Drink Scene of Trowbridge

Must-Try Dishes

Trowbridge has a fantastic food scene, with many restaurants and cafes serving up delicious local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Wiltshire ham: This cured ham is a specialty in the region and is often served with locally sourced cheeses and chutneys.

  • Beef and ale pie: This hearty pie, filled with tender beef and flavorful ale gravy, is a classic British dish.

  • Cream tea: This quintessential English afternoon tea features scones, clotted cream, jam, and a pot of tea.

Local Beverages

Trowbridge also has a vibrant drinking scene, with many craft breweries and distilleries producing unique and flavorful beverages. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Wadworth’s beer: This family-run brewery has been producing beer in Trowbridge since 1875, with their signature 6X ale being a popular choice.

  • Trowbridge gin: This small-batch, handcrafted gin is made from locally sourced botanicals and has won several awards.

Where to Stay in Trowbridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trowbridge offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the top picks:

  • The Moonraker Hotel: This 17th-century coaching inn has been beautifully restored and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • The Bath Arms: This traditional country inn, located just outside of Trowbridge, boasts beautiful rooms, a restaurant, and a pub with a garden terrace.

  • The Polebarn Hotel: This historic hotel, located in the heart of Trowbridge, has been providing comfortable and stylish rooms since 1904.

What to Buy in Trowbridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trowbridge also has some great shopping opportunities, with many independent shops and markets selling unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some top picks:

  • Trowbridge Farmers Market: This weekly market features fresh produce, baked goods, and crafts made by local vendors.

  • Tithe Barn Crafts and Gifts: This charming shop sells handmade crafts and gifts made by local artisans.

  • Paxton & Whitfield: This specialist cheesemaker sells delicious cheeses from Wiltshire and other regions in the UK.

Local Culture and Customs of Trowbridge

Trowbridge is a town with a rich history and culture, and it’s important to respect its local customs and traditions. Here are some key cultural aspects to keep in mind:

  • The wool trade: Trowbridge’s wool trade heritage is still evident in the town’s architecture, and its impact on the local economy is still felt today.

  • The Trowbridge carnival: This annual carnival, held in July, is a beloved event that brings the community together in a celebration of music, dance, and floats.

Best Time to Visit Trowbridge

Trowbridge is a town that can be enjoyed year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the best times to visit:

  • Spring (March to May): This is a great time to visit Trowbridge for flower lovers, as the countryside and parks come alive with colorful blooms.

  • Summer (June to August): This is when Trowbridge’s outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing, making it a great time to explore the town’s cultural scene.

  • Autumn (September to November): This is a quieter time to visit Trowbridge, but it’s ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts who prefer cooler weather and stunning foliage.

  • Winter (December to February): This is a great time to visit Trowbridge if you prefer fewer crowds and cozy indoor activities such as shopping, dining, or visiting museums.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trowbridge

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Explore the town on foot or by bike to fully appreciate its scenic beauty

  • Try the local beer and gin

  • Attend the Trowbridge carnival

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural settings and landmarks

  • Ignore the heritage and history of the town

  • Overbook activities and leave no time to simply enjoy the peaceful surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Trowbridge

Trowbridge is a safe and welcoming town, but as with any destination, it’s important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always keep your valuables secure

  • Take care when using public transportation

  • Be vigilant when walking or cycling on rural trails and paths

Health in Trowbridge

Trowbridge has excellent healthcare facilities, including general practitioners, hospitals, and clinics. Visitors from abroad are advised to purchase travel insurance with medical coverage before their trip.

Getting to Trowbridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trowbridge is easily accessible from the UK and Europe, with several transportation options available. Here’s how to get to Trowbridge:

  • By car: Trowbridge is located near the M4 and M5 motorways, providing easy access from London, Bristol, and other major UK cities.

  • By train: Trowbridge train station is serviced by regular trains from London, Bristol, and other major UK cities. The journey from London takes approximately two hours.

  • By bus: There are several bus services that operate to and from Trowbridge, including National Express and local bus companies.

Airports Near Trowbridge

  • Bristol Airport (BRS): Located approximately 40 miles from Trowbridge, this airport is serviced by many airlines including easyJet, Ryanair, and British Airways.

  • London Heathrow Airport (LHR): Located approximately 85 miles from Trowbridge, this airport is serviced by all major airlines and offers a range of international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Trowbridge

  • Trowbridge Bus Station: Located in the town center, this bus station offers local and regional bus services.

Other Options

  • Car rental: There are several car rental companies located in Trowbridge for those who prefer to explore the area on their own.

How to Get Around in Trowbridge

Trowbridge is a compact town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. There are also several local bus services that connect the town to nearby villages and towns. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer to get around on their own.

Where to Go Next from Trowbridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trowbridge is situated in the heart of the West Country, making it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Bath: This historic city, known for its Roman baths and Georgian architecture, is located just 12 miles from Trowbridge.

  • Salisbury: This beautiful medieval city, known for its stunning cathedral and picturesque streets, is located approximately 30 miles from Trowbridge.

  • Stonehenge: This world-famous prehistoric monument is located approximately 20 miles from Trowbridge and is an absolute must-see for visitors to England.

Conclusion

Trowbridge is a town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring the museums and historical landmarks to hiking and biking the scenic trails, there’s something for everyone in this tranquil town. We hope this tour guide has given you a good understanding of what Trowbridge has to offer and inspires you to plan your next trip to the West Country.
