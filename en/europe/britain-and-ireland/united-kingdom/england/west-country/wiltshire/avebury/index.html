Introduction to Avebury

Avebury is a small village located in Wiltshire, England. The village is known for its historic Neolithic stone circles that are considered one of the largest prehistoric stone circles in the world. The site also includes an ancient henge monument, a series of burial mounds, and other important archaeological features.

Aside from its rich cultural heritage, Avebury is also a charming countryside destination that offers visitors plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors, relax in a cozy pub, and enjoy delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Avebury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Avebury Stone Circle and Henge - The highlight of a visit to Avebury is the ancient Neolithic stone circle, which is comprised of numerous standing stones that have been arranged in a circular pattern. It is one of the most important prehistoric sites in Europe and has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Avebury Manor and Gardens - A beautiful example of a historic manor house, Avebury Manor is a must-visit attraction for history lovers. The house offers visitors a glimpse into life in a bygone era with furnished rooms displaying different periods of the manor’s history. The beautiful gardens also offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

  • Silbury Hill - This is the largest prehistoric man-made mound in Europe and dates back to around 2400 BC. Although it is not entirely clear why the mound was built, it is believed to have been an important site for the local population at the time.

Natural Attractions of Avebury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Ridgeway National Trail - The trail is around 87 miles long and stretches between Avebury and Ivinghoe Beacon in Buckinghamshire. Along the way, walkers will be treated to stunning landscapes and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • West Kennet Long Barrow - This is one of the best-preserved Neolithic tombs in Britain. The long barrow itself is surrounded by the beautiful Marlborough Downs, making it a popular spot for nature lovers and hikers.

  • Kennet and Avon Canal - This canal stretches from Bristol to Reading and passes through the beautiful countryside just outside Avebury. Visitors can take a peaceful stroll along the towpath, rent a boat or even try their hand at fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avebury

Whether you’re an avid hiker or a casual cyclist, Avebury offers numerous trails and routes to explore the surrounding countryside.

  • West Kennet Circular Walk - This is a popular 3.5-mile walk that takes visitors through the ancient Neolithic burial mound and the stunning Marlborough Downs.

  • Avebury to Barbury Castle Walk - This is a 13-mile route that takes visitors through beautiful rolling hills and ancient woodlands. Barbury Castle is an Iron Age hillfort situated on top of a high ridge, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Kennet and Avon Canal Towpath - This is a flat and easy cycle route that runs alongside the canal, providing a peaceful and scenic ride through the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Avebury: Activities and Tours

Aside from its historic attractions, there are plenty of other things to do in Avebury. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Stone Circle Tours - Take a guided tour of the ancient stone circles with a local expert and learn about the history and myths surrounding this fascinating site.

  • Avebury Ghost Tours - For a spookier experience, take the Avebury Ghost Tour and hear tales of ghosts and unexplained phenomena that have been reported in the area.

  • Avebury Pub Crawl - Sample some of the local brews and enjoy the cozy atmosphere of traditional English pubs with a self-guided pub crawl around Avebury.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avebury

Day 1: Visit the Avebury Stone Circle and Henge, Avebury Manor and Gardens, and finish the day with a relaxing walk around the village.

Day 2: Hike the West Kennet Long Barrow Circular Walk and have lunch at one of the nearby pubs.

Day 3: Visit Silbury Hill and the nearby market town of Marlborough for some shopping.

Day 4: Cycle the Kennet and Avon Canal Towpath and stop for lunch at one of the waterside cafes.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Stone Circles or, for something different, join the Avebury Ghost Tour.

Day 6: Get active with a horse-riding or fishing trip in the surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Explore the nearby villages of Lacock and Castle Combe, two delightful historic villages full of charm and character.

Food and Drink Scene of Avebury

Must-Try Dishes

Avebury offers a range of delicious local dishes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.

  • Wiltshire Ham - A local specialty, Wiltshire ham is slow-cooked honey-glazed ham, usually served with parsley sauce and mashed potatoes.

  • Cheese Platter - Try a selection of locally produced cheeses, including cheddar and Bath soft cheese, served with crackers and chutney.

  • Cider - The West Country is famous for its cider, so make sure to try a glass of local cider with your meal.

Local Beverages

In addition to cider, Avebury and the surrounding areas produce some other local beverages, including:

  • Wiltshire Beer - Sample some of the local ales produced by the Wiltshire Brewery.

  • English Wines - Try a glass of wine from one of the local vineyards, such as Kennet Valley Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Avebury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Avebury and the surrounding areas, ranging from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels.

  • Avebury Lodge Guest House - A beautiful 17th-century guesthouse located just outside of Avebury that offers comfortable rooms and a home-away-from-home atmosphere.

  • The Red Lion - A charming traditional pub with rooms, located in the heart of Avebury village.

  • The Stones Hotel - A modern and stylish hotel located just a short walk from the Avebury Stone Circle.

What to Buy in Avebury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Avebury and the surrounding areas are famous for their local crafts and produce. Here are some items to look out for:

  • Pottery - Avebury is home to several pottery studios, producing a range of beautiful, handmade ceramics.

  • Honey - The nearby village of Manton produces a range of delicious local honey, which can be purchased at local markets.

  • Books - Avebury has a rich history and there are a variety of books available that explore the area’s Neolithic heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Avebury

Avebury is a friendly and welcoming community, with a strong sense of pride in its local heritage and customs. It is important to respect the historic monuments and not to climb on them or remove any stones. Visitors should also dress appropriately for the countryside, wearing sturdy footwear and warm clothing when necessary.

Best Time to Visit Avebury

The best time to visit Avebury is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is usually mild and the village is bustling with activity. However, the spring and autumn months can also be a lovely time to visit, with fewer crowds and beautiful changing foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avebury

Do:

  • Respect the historic monuments and follow any instructions from site staff.
  • Explore the local countryside and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Climb on or remove any stones from the Neolithic monuments.
  • Litter or damage the local environment.
  • Disturb any wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avebury

Avebury is a safe and friendly village. However, visitors should always take precautions when hiking or cycling in the countryside, such as carrying appropriate clothing and staying on marked paths.

Health in Avebury

There are no major health concerns in Avebury, but visitors should ensure they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations and take appropriate precautions against ticks if hiking in the countryside.

Getting to Avebury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Avebury

The closest international airport to Avebury is London Heathrow Airport, which is around 1.5 hours away by car or public transport. Bristol Airport is also relatively close, at around 1 hour and 15 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Avebury

The nearest bus station is in the nearby town of Marlborough, which is around 6 miles away from Avebury. Regular buses run from London Victoria Coach Station to Marlborough.

Other Options

The most convenient way to reach Avebury is by car, as there are plenty of parking options around the village. However, visitors can also take a train to the nearby town of Swindon and then catch a bus or taxi to Avebury.

How to Get Around in Avebury

The best way to get around Avebury is by walking or cycling. The village is small and easily navigable by foot, and there are plenty of cycling routes in the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Avebury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bath - This historic city is around an hour away by car and is home to stunning Georgian architecture, Roman Baths, and a variety of museums and galleries.

  • Stonehenge - Another famous prehistoric site, Stonehenge is around 40 minutes away by car.

  • Oxford - The university town of Oxford is around an hour and a half away by car and offers a range of cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and theaters.

Conclusion

Avebury is a truly special destination that offers visitors a unique blend of prehistoric history, stunning natural scenery, and a friendly and welcoming community. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient monuments, hiking or cycling through the countryside, or just enjoying some traditional local food and drink, Avebury is a must-visit destination for travelers to the West Country of England.
