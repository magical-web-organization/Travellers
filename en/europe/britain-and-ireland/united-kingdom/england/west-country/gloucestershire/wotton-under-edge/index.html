A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Wotton-under-Edge, Gloucestershire

Welcome to Wotton-under-Edge, a charming market town located in Gloucestershire, in the beautiful West Country region of England. Known for its rich heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, Wotton-under-Edge is a must-visit destination for travelers of all interests. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, foodie, or adventure seeker, this guide has everything you need to plan a memorable trip to Wotton-under-Edge.

List of Places to Visit in Wotton-under-Edge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Begin your tour of Wotton-under-Edge by exploring its rich cultural heritage. Visit the following museums and historical places to learn more about the town’s fascinating past:

  • Heritage Centre: Located in a beautifully restored 17th-century building, the Heritage Centre is the perfect place to learn about Wotton-under-Edge’s history. The exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and documents that chronicle the town’s past.
  • St Mary’s Church: A beautiful medieval church with a rich history, St Mary’s is a must-visit attraction in Wotton-under-Edge. The church features stunning stained glass windows, medieval frescoes, and a beautiful oak roof.
  • The Chipping House: This quaint building dates back to the 17th century and was once used as a market hall. Today it houses a local art gallery where you can buy unique souvenirs to take home.
  • Wotton Hill: Climb to the top of Wotton Hill to enjoy stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside. The hill is also a popular spot for paragliders and hang gliders.

Natural Attractions of Wotton-under-Edge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the outdoors, Wotton-under-Edge has plenty to offer. Here are some of the town’s top natural attractions:

  • Cotswold Way National Trail: This famous hiking trail passes through Wotton-under-Edge and offers stunning views of the Cotswolds countryside. The trail is 102 miles long and takes you through some of the most scenic parts of England.
  • Westonbirt Arboretum: Located just a short drive from Wotton-under-Edge, Westonbirt Arboretum is a nature lover’s paradise. The arboretum boasts over 15,000 trees from around the world and is a popular spot for picnics, walks, and family outings.
  • Newark Park: This stunning 16th-century estate is surrounded by beautiful gardens and scenic countryside. The estate offers guided tours, children’s activities, and a tea room where you can enjoy a traditional English cream tea.
  • Woodchester Park: Another great spot for nature lovers, Woodchester Park is a beautiful wooded valley with picturesque lakes and stunning wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wotton-under-Edge

Wotton-under-Edge is famous for its hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes in the town:

  • The Cotswold Way National Trail: As mentioned earlier, this 102-mile-long trail passes through Wotton-under-Edge and offers stunning views of the countryside. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a beginner, the Cotswold Way has something for everyone.
  • Wotton Hill: This beautiful hill offers several hiking and biking trails, including the Wotton Hill Trail, which is a circular walk that takes you through some of the town’s most picturesque spots.
  • Stinchcombe Hill: A popular spot for local hikers and bikers, Stinchcombe Hill is a beautiful nature reserve with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Tyndale Monument Trail: This 2-mile-long trail takes you to the top of the Tyndale Monument, a 111-foot-tall tower that offers stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Wotton-under-Edge: Activities and Tours

Wotton-under-Edge offers several activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Wotton Electric Picture House: Enjoy a unique movie experience at the Wotton Electric Picture House, a renovated art deco cinema that shows classic films, documentaries, and the latest releases.
  • Wotton Heritage Walk: Take a guided heritage walk of the town to learn more about its fascinating history and architecture.
  • Local Food Tour: Indulge in the town’s food and drink scene by taking a local food tour. The tour includes visits to some of the town’s top restaurants, cafes, and bars, where you can try some of the best local dishes and drinks.
  • Outdoor Adventures: From paragliding and hang gliding to horse riding and fishing, Wotton-under-Edge offers a range of outdoor adventures for travelers of all interests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wotton-under-Edge

To make the most of your trip to Wotton-under-Edge, here’s an itinerary for a week-long stay:

  • Day 1: Visit the town’s top historical attractions, including the Heritage Centre, St Mary’s Church, and The Chipping House.
  • Day 2: Spend the day hiking the Cotswold Way National Trail, which passes through Wotton-under-Edge.
  • Day 3: Take a trip to Westonbirt Arboretum, a beautiful nature reserve just a short drive from the town.
  • Day 4: Explore the town’s natural beauty by visiting Woodchester Park and Wotton Hill.
  • Day 5: Indulge in the town’s food and drink scene by taking a local food tour.
  • Day 6: Enjoy outdoor adventures such as paragliding, hang gliding, or horse riding.
  • Day 7: Take a guided heritage walk of the town to learn more about its fascinating history and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Wotton-under-Edge

Must-Try Dishes

Wotton-under-Edge is known for its delicious local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Gloucestershire Old Spot Pork: This succulent pork dish is made with locally sourced meat and is a traditional favorite in the area.
  • Beef and Ale Pie: A hearty pie filled with tender beef and a rich ale gravy, this dish is perfect for a cold winter’s day.
  • Wotton Lamb: Another local favorite, Wotton Lamb is slow-roasted to perfection with a blend of herbs and garlic.

Local Beverages

In addition to its delicious food, Wotton-under-Edge is also famous for its local beverages. Here are some of the best drinks to try in the town:

  • Stroud Brewery Ales: Stroud Brewery creates some of the best craft ales in the country. Their handcrafted beers are made with organic ingredients and are a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Weston’s Scrumpy Cider: Made with locally grown apples, Weston’s Scrumpy Cider is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days.
  • Three Choirs Vineyard Wine: Located just a short drive from Wotton-under-Edge, Three Choirs Vineyard produces some of the best English wines. Their wines are made using traditional methods and are a must-try for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Wotton-under-Edge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wotton-under-Edge offers a range of accommodations for travelers of all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in the town:

  • The Swan Hotel: Another popular hotel, The Swan Hotel offers comfortable rooms, a delicious restaurant, and a stylish bar.
  • Wotton Farmhouse: For a unique and cozy stay, try Wotton Farmhouse, a traditional farmhouse that offers comfortable rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Wotton-under-Edge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wotton-under-Edge is home to several independent shops and markets where you can buy unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best places to shop in the town:
