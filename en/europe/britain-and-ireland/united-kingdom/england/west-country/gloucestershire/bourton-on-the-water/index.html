Introduction to Bourton-on-the-Water

Bourton-on-the-Water is a charming village located in the Cotswolds area of Gloucestershire, in the West Country region of England. Known as the “Venice of the Cotswolds” because of its picturesque bridges across the River Windrush, the village is a popular destination for tourists from around the world. With its quaint cottages, green meadows, and old-world charm, it’s no wonder that Bourton-on-the-Water is frequently cited as one of the prettiest villages in England.

List of Places to Visit in Bourton-on-the-Water

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cotswold Motoring Museum and Toy Collection

The Cotswold Motoring Museum and Toy Collection is a must-see attraction for anyone interested in vintage cars and toys. The museum displays a collection of 20th-century vehicles, ranging from classic British cars to exotic American cars. Visitors can also see a collection of antique toys and teddy bears, which will appeal to young visitors.

The Model Village

The Model Village is a unique attraction that features a miniature replica of Bourton-on-the-Water in 1/9th scale. The model village showcases detailed replicas of some of the village’s most famous buildings and landmarks, including the model of the model village itself.

Cotswold Perfumery

The Cotswold Perfumery is a small perfumery that has been producing luxury fragrances since 1969. Visitors can take a guided tour of the perfumery and learn about the art of perfumery. The perfumery also offers bespoke fragrances, tailored to the individual’s preferences.

Natural Attractions of Bourton-on-the-Water: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Birdland

Birdland is an attraction that animal lovers won’t want to miss. The park features over 500 birds from around the world, including penguins, flamingos, and parrots. Visitors can watch the daily penguin feeding and experience a close encounter with some of the park’s resident birds.

The Cotswold Way

The Cotswold Way is a popular hiking route that follows the Cotswold Escarpment from Chipping Campden to Bath. The trail passes through Bourton-on-the-Water and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is 102 miles long and can be completed over several days or in sections.

The Slaughters

The picturesque villages of Lower and Upper Slaughter are located a short distance from Bourton-on-the-Water. Both villages are situated on the River Eye and are known for their traditional Cotswold architecture and beautiful gardens. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the villages and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bourton-on-the-Water

The Windrush Way

The Windrush Way is a circular hike that starts and finishes in Bourton-on-the-Water. The 14-mile trail passes through scenic countryside, including the villages of Great Rissington and Broadwell. The trail is suitable for walkers of all abilities and can be completed in a day.

Bourton-on-the-Water to Stow-on-the-Wold

The Bourton-on-the-Water to Stow-on-the-Wold trail is a scenic 5-mile hike that follows the Windrush Valley. The trail passes through several pretty Cotswold villages and offers stunning views of the countryside. The trail is suitable for beginners and can be completed in a few hours.

Best Things to Do in Bourton-on-the-Water: Activities and Tours

Go on a Boat Tour

Visitors can take a relaxing boat tour along the River Windrush, which passes through the heart of Bourton-on-the-Water. The tour provides an excellent opportunity to see the village from a different perspective and learn about its history.

Visit the Farmers’ Market

The monthly farmers’ market is a popular event in Bourton-on-the-Water. The market features fresh and locally sourced produce, including fruit, vegetables, meat, bread, and cakes. Visitors can also sample some of the local food and drink.

Take a Cooking Class

Visitors can take a cooking class at The Cotswold Chef, located in Bourton-on-the-Water. The classes focus on traditional British cuisine, using fresh and locally sourced ingredients. Visitors can learn to make classic British dishes, such as fish and chips and roast beef.

Food and Drink Scene of Bourton-on-the-Water

Must-Try Dishes

Cotswold Lamb

Cotswold lamb is renowned for its quality and taste. Visitors can sample this delicious local dish at several restaurants in Bourton-on-the-Water, where it is served with seasonal vegetables.

Steak and Ale Pie

Steak and ale pie is a classic British dish that is a favorite of many locals and visitors. The pie is made with slow-cooked beef and ale and is usually served with mashed potatoes and seasonal vegetables.

Local Beverages

Cotswold Gin

Cotswold Gin is a small-batch gin that is made using locally sourced botanicals, including lavender and bay leaf. Visitors can sample this delicious gin at several bars and restaurants in Bourton-on-the-Water.

Cotswold Ale

Cotswold Ale is a traditional English ale that is brewed using locally sourced hops and malt. Visitors can sample this delicious ale at several of the village’s pubs and bars.

Bourton-on-the-Water offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from luxury hotels, traditional inns, guesthouses, and self-catering apartments.

The Dial House Hotel

The Dial House Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Bourton-on-the-Water. The hotel features stylish rooms, a restaurant serving locally sourced cuisine, and a bar offering a range of drinks and cocktails.

The Old Manse Hotel

The Old Manse Hotel is an elegant Georgian property located in the village center. The hotel offers individually designed rooms, a restaurant serving traditional British cuisine, and a bar serving a range of local beers and wines.

Budget-Friendly Options

What to Buy in Bourton-on-the-Water: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Cheese

Cotswold cheese is a delicious local cheese that is available at several of the village’s specialty food shops. The cheese has a mild, creamy flavor and is perfect for cooking or snacking.

Cotswold Tweed

Cotswold tweed is a beautiful woolen fabric that is traditionally woven in the Cotswold area. Visitors can buy tweed jackets, hats, and bags at several of the village’s clothing stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Bourton-on-the-Water

Bourton-on-the-Water is a traditional English village, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. The village is known for its annual football (soccer) match, which is played in the River Windrush on the August bank holiday Monday. Visitors are welcome to watch the match but should be prepared for a lively and boisterous atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Bourton-on-the-Water

Bourton-on-the-Water is a popular destination all year round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and summer months (April to August). The weather is mild, and the village is bursting with color and life. Visitors can enjoy the annual festivals and events, such as the Christmas lights and the summer flower show.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bourton-on-the-Water

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Take a boat tour of the River Windrush.
  • Visit the Cotswold Motoring Museum and Toy Collection.

Bourton-on-the-Water is a safe destination for visitors, but they should take the usual precautions while traveling. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, keep their valuables secure, and take care while hiking or biking.

Bourton-on-the-Water has several medical facilities, including a pharmacy and a GP surgery. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

The nearest international airports to Bourton-on-the-Water are Birmingham Airport, located 45 miles away, and Bristol Airport, located 72 miles away. Both airports have direct flights to several cities in Europe and the United States.

Bus Stations Near Bourton-on-the-Water

Bourton-on-the-Water is well-connected by bus, with regular services to Cheltenham, Gloucester, and other nearby villages and towns. The nearest bus station is located in Cheltenham, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Bourton-on-the-Water is a small village, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. Bicycles are also available for rent, which is an enjoyable way to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Bourton-on-the-Water: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cheltenham is a beautiful town located 20 miles from Bourton-on-the-Water. The town is famous for its Regency architecture, its racing festival, and its restaurants, cafes, and pubs.

Bath

Bath is a historic city located 65 miles from Bourton-on-the-Water. The city is famous for its Roman Baths, Georgian architecture, and its beautiful parks and gardens.
