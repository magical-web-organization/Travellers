Introduction to Cirencester

Nestled in the heart of the West Country, Cirencester is a charming town with a rich history dating back to Roman times. Known as the “capital of the Cotswolds,” it boasts stunning architecture, picturesque countryside, and a vibrant cultural scene.

List of Places to Visit in Cirencester

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Corinium Museum: Explore the town’s Roman heritage through interactive exhibits and authentic artifacts.

  2. Cirencester Parish Church: Admire the exquisite Gothic architecture of this historic church, which dates back to the 12th century.

  3. New Brewery Arts: Discover the best of contemporary art and craft in this converted brewery, featuring galleries, workshops, and a cafe.

  4. Cotswold Water Park: Relax by the lakeside, walk the nature trails, or indulge in watersports in this stunning nature reserve.

Natural Attractions of Cirencester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cirencester Park: Enjoy a leisurely stroll in this vast parkland, which boasts a deer park, a walled garden, and a playground for children.

  2. Siddington Nature Reserve: Spot rare birds and butterflies in this tranquil nature reserve, home to diverse habitats like woodland, meadows, and wetlands.

  3. Cotswold Way: Hike or bike along this famous trail, which spans 100 miles across stunning countryside and picturesque villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cirencester

  1. Cotswold Way: A challenging but rewarding trail running from Chipping Campden to Bath, passing through several stunning viewpoints and unique attractions.

  2. Crickley Hill Country Park: A shorter, family-friendly trail with stunning views of the Severn Vale and the Malvern Hills.

Best Things to Do in Cirencester: Activities and Tours

  1. Cotswold Balloon Safaris: Soar above the stunning Cotswold countryside in a hot air balloon, with breathtaking views of Cirencester, Gloucestershire and beyond.

  2. Cirencester Amphitheatre: Catch a play or a concert in this unique amphitheater, which offers a stunning setting with a picturesque backdrop of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cirencester

Food and Drink Scene of Cirencester

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cotswold lamb: A local specialty, known for its juicy, flavorful meat and tender texture.

  2. Gloucestershire cheese: Delicious and versatile, this cheese makes for a perfect snack or ingredient in various dishes.

  3. Bibury trout: A local delicacy, featuring freshwater trout caught locally and served in various styles, from smoked to grilled.

Local Beverages

  1. Cotswold gin: Made from local botanicals and herbs, this gin has a unique flavor profile and is used in various cocktails and drinks.

  2. West Country cider: This refreshing drink is made from locally-grown apples and comes in various flavors and strengths.

Where to Stay in Cirencester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Barnsley House: A luxury hotel with elegant accommodations, beautiful gardens, and a renowned spa and wellness center.

  2. The Old Brewhouse: A charming guesthouse with cozy rooms, a homey atmosphere, and delicious breakfast served daily.

What to Buy in Cirencester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Cotswold woolens: Warm and cozy blankets, scarves, and sweaters, made from locally-sourced wool.

  2. Handmade pottery: Unique and beautiful ceramics, crafted by local artists and available in various styles and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Cirencester

Cirencester is known for its vibrant arts scene, with many local artists, craftsmen, and theater companies contributing to the town’s cultural heritage. The town also has a strong agricultural tradition, with many local farms and markets offering fresh produce and organic products.

Best Time to Visit Cirencester

The best time to visit Cirencester is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the countryside is picturesque with blooming flowers or colorful foliage. Summer is also a great time to visit, but be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cirencester

Do:

  1. Explore the town’s charming architecture and landmarks, including the Parish Church and the Roman Amphitheater.

  2. Take a hike or bike ride along the Cotswold Way, stopping at the breathtaking viewpoints and charming villages along the way.

Don’t:

  1. Forget to try the local cheese, cider, and other specialties, which reflect the town’s unique culinary traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cirencester

Cirencester is a very safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere. However, visitors should still take precautions like locking their doors, avoiding dark alleys at night, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Cirencester

Cirencester has excellent healthcare facilities, including several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies and other health issues.

Getting to Cirencester: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Cirencester: The closest airports to Cirencester are Bristol Airport (1 hour drive) and Birmingham Airport (1.5 hours drive).

  • Bus Stations Near Cirencester: Cirencester is served by several bus companies, including Stagecoach Bus and National Express, offering connections to nearby towns and cities.

  • Other Options: Visitors can also arrive by train, with the nearest railway stations located in Kemble and Swindon, both served by Great Western Railway.

How to Get Around in Cirencester

Cirencester is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use local buses, taxis, or rental cars to get around and explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Cirencester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bath: A historic town known for its stunning Roman Baths, beautiful Georgian architecture, and unique museums and galleries.

  2. Cheltenham: A vibrant town with a rich cultural scene, including festivals, theaters, and art exhibitions.

  3. Bristol: A dynamic, creative city that boasts stunning street art, world-class museums, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Cirencester is a hidden gem in the West Country, offering a wealth of natural and cultural attractions, delicious food and drink, and a charming, welcoming atmosphere. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation or cultural exploration, Cirencester has something for everyone, making it an ideal destination for your next visit to the UK.
