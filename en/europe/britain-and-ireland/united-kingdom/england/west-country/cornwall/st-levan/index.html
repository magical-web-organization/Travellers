Introduction to St Levan

St Levan is a charming village situated on the southwest tip of Cornwall, rich in history and natural beauty. With a population of just over 200 people, this small village offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. St Levan is surrounded by rugged coastlines, rolling landscapes and steep cliffs, making it perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in St Levan

St Levan has several cultural and natural attractions, making it a popular tourist destination. Here are some of the top places to visit in St Levan.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St Levan Church
  • St Levan Well
  • Porthcurno Telegraph Museum

St Levan Church

St Levan Church has stood in the village for over 800 years, with its intricate stained-glass windows, medieval artifacts and historic graveyard — it is a must-visit for any history buff.

St Levan Well

St Levan Well is a natural spring located close to the church, with water that is believed to have healing powers. According to folklore, it was used by St Levan himself to cure the sick.

Porthcurno Telegraph Museum

A 15-minute drive from St Levan is the Porthcurno Telegraph Museum, an important site in the history of global communication. The museum showcases the evolution of modern communication, from the first telegraph cables to modern fiber-optic technology.

Natural Attractions of St Levan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Logan Rock
  • Porthgwarra Beach
  • Minack Theatre

Logan Rock

Logan Rock is a 10-tonne granite boulder that sits atop cliffs overlooking the sea. It is an impressive sight, with a legend that says it was once moved by a single man, thereby proving his strength to his beloved.

Porthgwarra Beach

Porthgwarra Beach is a secluded cove, well-liked by swimmers, snorkelers and rock pool enthusiasts. Its clear waters are perfect for swimming, and with its rocky coastline, it provides an ideal spot for exploring.

Minack Theatre

The Minack Theatre is an open-air theatre carved into the cliffs overlooking Porthcurno Bay. It has been operating for over 80 years, showcasing plays, operas and musicals with stunning sea views as the backdrop.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Levan

St Levan is located in an area famous for its miles of stunning coastline and rolling countryside, making it an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes in St Levan:

  • St Levan to Porthcurno via Logan Rock
  • Penberth to St Loy Cove
  • St Levan to Land’s End Coastal Path

The breathtaking cliffs and seascapes are what makes St Levan’s hikes and bike rides special. There are many routes to choose from to experience the natural beauty of the area. Cyclists and hikers can take a break at one of the many cafes and pubs in the towns for a bite to eat and a drink.

Best Things to Do in St Levan: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing
  • Surfing
  • Kayaking
  • Stargazing Tours

St Levan has several activities to offer, catering to visitors of all ages. Engage in a day of fishing or surfing on the beach, explore the sea caves and rocky coastline through kayaking or even go on a guided tour of the stars with a local expert.

Week-Long Itinerary for St Levan

Visitors can spend a week in St Levan and explore the nearby areas. Here’s a sample itinerary of how to spend a week in St Levan.

Day 1 - St Levan Village

Spend the day exploring St Levan Village, visiting the St Levan Church and Well.

Day 2 - Porthcurno Bay

Visit Porthcurno Bay and tour the Telegraph Museum before enjoying a show at the Minack Theatre in the evening.

Day 3 - Hiking

Experience the best hike from St Levan to Porthcurno via Logan Rock.

Day 4 - Kayaking

Spend the day exploring the sea caves and the Rocky coastline through kayaking at Porthcurno.

Day 5 - Stargazing Tour

Take a guided stargazing tour from nearby Cape Cornwall.

Day 6 - Beach Day

Enjoy a day of sun, sand and water activities at Porthgwarra Beach.

Day 7 - Land’s End

Visit Land’s End and the Longships Lighthouse for a beautiful, scenic view of the sea.

Food and Drink Scene of St Levan

In St Levan, visitors can sample traditional Cornish cuisine and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty
  • Cream Tea
  • Cornish Clotted Cream

Cornish Pasties are loved for their crumbly pastry and meat filling, making them a perfect lunchtime snack. Cream teas is a traditional Cornish afternoon tea with scones, clotted cream and jam. Cornish Clotted Cream is served with scones and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Cider
  • Cornish Ale
  • Cornish Wine

Cornish beverages are popular all across the UK, visitors can try a range of ciders, ales and wines made in the local area.

Local Culture and Customs of St Levan

The town has a rich history and culture, with a tight-knit community that welcomes visitors with open arms. The locals take pride in their traditions and are always happy to share them with tourists.

Best Time to Visit St Levan

The best time to visit St Levan is from April to September, when the weather is mild, and the countryside is in full bloom.

Safety and Health

Safety in St Levan

St Levan is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should always be cautious while walking alone on deserted paths or after dark. Ensure to check the tide before heading out to the beach.

Health in St Levan

St Levan does not have its own hospital or doctor’s surgery, but visitors can visit the nearby health center at Penzance for any medical emergencies.

Getting to St Levan: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Levan can be reached by road, rail or air.

Airports Near St Levan

Newquay Airport is 39 miles away from St Levan. Visitors can hire a car from Newquay to St Levan.

Bus Stations Near St Levan

The nearest bus station to St Levan is Penzance Bus Station, from where visitors can take a taxi to St Levan.

Other Options

Visitors can take a ferry or hire a private boat to reach St Levan, as water transport is available for transportation.

How to Get Around in St Levan

St Levan is a small village and can be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can hire a car for longer distances or take a taxi.

Conclusion

St Levan is a beautiful village that offers a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions, making it a must-visit for any traveler visiting Cornwall. With its stunning coastline, rich heritage and friendly locals, visitors will surely be enchanted by the beauty of this place.
