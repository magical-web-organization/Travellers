Introduction to Par

Par, a small village in Cornwall, is that well-kept secret that you’ll be glad you stumbled upon. Famous for its beaches, hiking trails, and unique attractions, Par has something for everyone, from adventure enthusiasts and history buffs to foodies and beach bums.

Located in West Country, England, in the United Kingdom, Par is easily accessible from major cities like London, Bristol, or Manchester. It’s surrounded by beautiful rolling hills and adorable villages that offer an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

With this comprehensive travel guide to Par, you’ll discover the best things to do, places to visit, and where to eat and stay.

List of Places to Visit in Par

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and cultural heritage, you can’t miss the museums and historical places in Par. Some of the best attractions include:

  • St. Austell Brewery Visitor Centre: Learn about the interesting history of this local brewery, one of the oldest in England, and enjoy some local craft beers.
  • Eden Project: This is a must-visit attraction in Cornwall, an immersive botanical garden with giant biodomes showcasing different ecosystems.
  • Mevagissey Museum and Aquarium: Discover the local fishing history, shipwrecks, and sea creatures in this interesting museum and aquarium.

Natural Attractions of Par: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Par is unparalleled and is waiting to be explored. Here are some of the most scenic places in the village:

  • Par Sands Beach: This stunning beach stretches for over two miles and has plenty of amenities, including a café, toilets, and parking.
  • Fowey Estuary: Take a kayak or boat tour around this stunning estuary, one of the most picturesque sights in Cornwall.
  • South West Coast Path: This famous hiking trail is ideal for experienced hikers or leisurely walkers looking for breathtaking views of the rugged coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Par

Par is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking and biking trails are no exception. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced hiker/biker, there’s a route perfect for you. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Par to Luxulyan: This route takes you through beautiful woodland, with waterfalls and the famous Treffry Viaduct of the Luxulyan Valley.
  • Boswinger to Gorran Haven: This seven-mile circular route takes you along beautiful coastal paths to the quaint village of Gorran Haven.
  • Lanhydrock Estate: If you’re a mountain biker, Lanhydrock Estate has a range of trails to suit all levels. The estate also has stunning gardens and a beautiful castle.

Best Things to Do in Par: Activities and Tours

There’s more to Par than hiking and beaches. If you’re looking for some adventure or unique experiences, check out these activities and tours:

  • Fishing Trips: Take a fishing trip on a local boat and discover the best fishing spots on Cornwall’s coast.
  • Golfing at Carlyon Bay: This 18-hole golf course is set in an idyllic location with views of St Austell Bay.
  • Par Market and Food Hall: Take a break from all the outdoor activities and head to this lively market for local products, crafts, and delicious food stalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Par

If you’re planning to spend a week in Par, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Eden Project and explore the nearby Lost Gardens of Heligan. Stay in Carlyon Bay or St Austell.
2 Take a scenic bike ride to Gorran Haven and have lunch at the village’s famous Barley Sheaf pub.
3 Go on a fishing trip in the morning and spend the afternoon exploring Fowey Estuary. Stay in Fowey.
4 Visit the picturesque Charlestown Harbour and take a guided tour of the Tall Ships used in popular movies and TV shows. Stay in Charlestown.
5 Take a leisurely hike from Par to Luxulyan, stopping at the famous Treffry Viaduct. Stay in Lostwithiel.
6 Explore the beautiful Lanhydrock Estate and gardens and try out the mountain biking trails. Stay in Bodmin.
7 Relax at Par Sands Beach and visit the Par Market and Food Hall for local products and souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Par

Par has a vibrant food and drink scene that’s worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty: A classic pastry dish with a savory filling of beef, potatoes, and onions.
  • Crab Sandwich: Freshly caught crab meat spread on soft bread with a dollop of mayonnaise.
  • Cream Tea: A delicious afternoon treat of scones, clotted cream, and jam served with tea.

Local Beverages

  • St Austell Brewery’s Ales: Try the local beers like Tribute, Proper Job, and Korev, all brewed in St Austell Brewery Visitor Centre.
  • Cornish Cyder: A refreshing drink made from Cornwall’s own apples, fermented to produce a crisp and tangy alcoholic beverage.

Where to Stay in Par: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Par has a range of options for every budget, from budget hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the best accommodation options include:

  • Carlyon Bay Hotel: This luxury hotel is set in a stunning location, overlooking a beautiful bay. It has an 18-hole golf course and spa.
  • YHA Eden Project: A budget-friendly option, this hostel is located near the Eden Project and has clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Penarth Guest House: This charming guesthouse has comfortable rooms and delicious breakfast, plus it’s close to Par Sands Beach.

What to Buy in Par: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of local products and souvenirs in Par that make excellent gifts or mementos. Here are some of the best:

  • Cornish Fudge: A sweet treat made with local cream and butter and available in various flavors.
  • Handmade Pottery: Cornwall has a long tradition of pottery, and you can find handmade pottery pieces in many shops.
  • Cornish Gin and Vodka: These locally-sourced spirits are unique and flavorful, making them perfect gifts for your friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Par

Cornwall has a rich and distinct culture that is different from the rest of England. Some of the local customs and traditions you may encounter in Par include:

  • Cornish Language: A unique language spoken by some locals, with its own literature, music, and festivals.
  • May Day Celebrations: A traditional festival celebrating the arrival of spring and new life, with dancing, music, and flower crowns.

Best Time to Visit Par

The best time to visit Par is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy. However, if you prefer fewer crowds, you can visit in the shoulder seasons, from April to May or September to October, when the weather is still pleasant, and there are fewer tourists around.

Do’s and Don’ts of Par

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Par:

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood, especially the Cornish crab.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the local flora and fauna.
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Don’t drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Par is a safe village with a low crime rate, but like any other tourist destination, you should take precautions to keep yourself safe. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Par

  • Keep an eye on your valuables and lock them in the hotel safe if possible.
  • Stick to well-lit and busy areas, especially at night.
  • Follow the signs and instructions when hiking or exploring the natural attractions.

Health in Par

  • Stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Pack some insect repellent if you’re planning to spend time outdoors.

Getting to Par: Transportation Options and Logistics

Par is easily accessible from major cities in the United Kingdom. Here are some transportation options:

  • By Car: Par is about three hours from London, and the easiest way to get here is by car. You can rent a car at any major airport or train station.
  • By Train: Par has its own train station, and you can take a train from London’s Paddington Station.
  • By Bus: There are regular buses from major cities like London, Bristol, and Manchester to Par.

Airports Near Par

If you’re flying to the United Kingdom, here are some airports near Par:

  • Newquay Cornwall Airport: This airport is about 16 miles from Par and offers flights to major UK cities like London and Manchester.
  • Exeter Airport: This airport is about 75 miles from Par and has flights to major UK and European cities.

Bus Stations Near Par

If you prefer to travel by bus, here are some major bus stations near Par:

  • Plymouth Coach Station: This bus station offers regular services to Par from Plymouth and other destinations.
  • St Austell Bus Station: This bus station is about 5 miles from Par and offers regular services from major cities like London and Manchester.

Other Options

  • Taxis and Private Transfers: You can hire a taxi or a private transfer to reach Par from major airports or train stations.
  • Bike Rentals: You can rent a bike in Par to explore the village and nearby attractions at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Par

Par is a small village, and you can easily explore it on foot. However, if you want to go to nearby attractions or beaches, you can use public transportation, rent a bike, or hire a taxi. The local bus services are reliable and connect Par to other villages and towns in Cornwall.

Where to Go Next from Par: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Cornwall, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • St Ives: One of the most picturesque towns in Cornwall, with stunning beaches, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Padstow: Famous for its food scene, with a range of restaurants offering local seafood and delicious Cornish dishes.
  • Looe: A charming fishing village with a beautiful harbor and stunning coastal walks.

Conclusion

Par is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered, with stunning beaches, natural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. This comprehensive travel guide to Par has given you a glimpse of what this village has to offer and should help you plan your trip better. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or new experiences, Par has something for everyone.
