Introduction to St Ives

Nestled on the coast of West Cornwall, St Ives is a picturesque seaside town dotted with golden beaches, turquoise waters, and rugged cliffs. The town is known for its vibrant arts scene, rich history, and stunning natural beauty, making it the perfect destination for anyone seeking a tranquil retreat.

St Ives is easily accessible by car, bus, or train from major cities such as London, Bristol, or Plymouth, or via flights to nearby airports. Once you arrive, there are numerous accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, making it an ideal vacation spot for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in St Ives

If you’re planning a trip to St Ives, here’s a list of must-visit destinations.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Barbara Hepworth Museum and Sculpture Garden: This museum is dedicated to the famous British sculptor and artist, Barbara Hepworth, who lived in St Ives for many years. It is home to some of her most iconic works, set within a beautiful garden that features sculptures amid tropical plants.

  • The St Ives Museum: Explore the town’s fascinating history and local culture at this museum that houses a vast collection of exhibits, ranging from fishing boats and nets to old photographs that paint a vivid picture of the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of St Ives: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St Ives Harbour: The picturesque harbor is the center of the town and offers spectacular views of the sea and surrounding countryside. It’s a great place to relax and watch the boats go by while enjoying an ice cream or fish and chips from one of the nearby stalls.

  • Porthmeor Beach: One of the most popular beaches in St Ives, Porthmeor is a great spot for surfing and sunbathing. The beach also offers stunning views of the town’s colorful houses and the rugged cliffs.

  • South West Coast Path: Stretching for 630 miles along the coastline, this trail offers panoramic views of the sea and surrounding countryside. A section of the path passes through St Ives and offers visitors the opportunity to explore the area’s natural beauty on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Ives

If you’re a nature lover, explore the stunning beauty of St Ives through its numerous hiking, cycling, and walking trails. Here are some popular routes to consider.

  • St Ives to Lelant Coastal Path: This trail offers expansive views of the coast and stunning panoramas from Porthkidney Sands. The route is approximately 4 miles long and takes around two hours to complete.

  • St Ives to Zennor Trail: This 6-mile long hike passes through several charming villages and offers breathtaking views of the sea and countryside. It takes approximately three hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in St Ives: Activities and Tours

Whether you want to soak up the town’s rich cultural heritage or try your hand at water activities, St Ives offers a wide range of fun-filled activities to enjoy. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Surfing Lessons: With its rolling waves and golden sands, St Ives is an ideal spot for surfing. There are many local surf schools that offer lessons for all levels and ages.

  • Tate St Ives: If you’re a fan of modern art, the Tate St Ives museum is a must-visit. The museum features a wide range of contemporary art exhibitions throughout the year.

Food and Drink Scene of St Ives

St Ives is known for its delicious seafood and authentic Cornish cuisine, which is a must-try during your visit. Here are some popular dishes and the best places to try them.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty: A classic Cornish dish made from pastry and filled with potato, onion, and beef. Try one at The Cornish Bakery, a local chain that uses traditional methods and ingredients.

  • Seafood Chowder: A rich and creamy soup made with fresh seafood and local vegetables. Enjoy a bowl at The Sloop Inn, a historic pub with a cozy atmosphere and stunning harbor views.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Cider: A refreshing cider made from locally grown apples. Try a pint of Rattler or Thatchers at The Castle Inn, a traditional pub with a large selection of ciders on tap.

Where to Stay in St Ives: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St Ives has a wide range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Carbis Bay Hotel and Estate: A luxurious hotel with stunning sea views and a private beach. The hotel features an outdoor pool, a spa, and several fine dining options.

  • The Western Hotel: A historic Victorian hotel located in the heart of the town, with cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

  • St Ives Hostel: A backpacker-friendly hostel located close to the town center. The hostel has a communal kitchen, a lounge, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in St Ives: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St Ives is known for its charming craft shops and galleries that sell handmade pottery, paintings, and jewelry. Here are some popular souvenirs to consider:

  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Locally made jewelry crafted from sea glass found on the beaches of St Ives.

  • Cornish Fudge: A delicious sweet treat made from local ingredients such as clotted cream and butter. Try some at The Fudge Box, a local candy store that sells a wide range of flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of St Ives

St Ives is a charming seaside town that values tradition and community. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Fisherman’s Festival: Held every September, this festival celebrates the town’s historic fishing industry with music, dance, and food.

  • May Day Celebrations: On May Day, locals celebrate the start of spring with a procession through the streets, featuring colorful banners and costumes.

Best Time to Visit St Ives

The best time to visit St Ives is between May and September, when the weather is mild and sunny, and most local attractions are open. However, the town can get quite crowded during peak season, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Ives

Here are some basic rules and etiquette to keep in mind during your visit to St Ives:

Do:

  • Respect the Beach: Keep the beaches and surrounding natural areas clean, and follow the warning signs and safety advice to avoid accidents.

  • Support Local Businesses: St Ives has a thriving local community, and supporting local shops, restaurants, and cafes helps to promote sustainable tourism.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Dispose of your trash properly and avoid littering in public areas.

  • Disturb the Wildlife: St Ives is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including seals and birds. Respect their natural habitat and maintain a safe distance.

Safety and Health

St Ives is a safe and healthy destination, with a low crime rate and a high standard of medical facilities. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in St Ives

  • Water Safety: Always follow the warnings and regulations when swimming or engaging in water sports, and wear a life vest if needed.

  • Traffic Safety: St Ives has narrow streets and limited parking, so be cautious when driving or cycling, and respect the local traffic regulations.

Health in St Ives

  • Sun Safety: Protect yourself from the sun’s harmful UV rays by wearing sunscreen and a hat, especially during the summer months.

  • Food and Water Safety: St Ives has excellent hygiene standards, but it’s always best to drink bottled water and be cautious with street food.

Getting to St Ives: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Ives is easily accessible by car, bus, train, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near St Ives

The nearest airports to St Ives are:

  • Newquay Airport: Approximately 40 minutes away from St Ives by car or taxi. Flights operate from major cities in the UK and Europe.

  • Bristol Airport: Approximately 2 and a half hours away from St Ives by car or train. Flights operate from major cities in the UK and worldwide.

Bus Stations Near St Ives

St Ives has several bus stations that connect it to neighboring towns and cities, including:

  • St Ives Bus Station: Located in the town center, with direct routes to Penzance, Truro, and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: If you’re coming from London or other major cities in the UK, the train is a convenient option. Trains operate from London Paddington to St Erth, and then connect to a branch line service to St Ives.

How to Get Around in St Ives

St Ives has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses, taxis, and trains. However, the town is small enough to explore on foot, and cycling is a popular option for accessing some of the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from St Ives: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St Ives is a perfect springboard to explore the surrounding areas, including:

  • Penzance: A charming Cornish town famous for its beautiful harbors, art studios, and historic architecture.

  • Land’s End: The westernmost point of England, offering stunning views and a wild, windswept landscape.

Conclusion

St Ives is a charming seaside town that offers a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of modern life. With its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant arts scene, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a tranquil retreat. We hope this guide has inspired you to plan your trip to St Ives, and that you enjoy all the town has to offer.
