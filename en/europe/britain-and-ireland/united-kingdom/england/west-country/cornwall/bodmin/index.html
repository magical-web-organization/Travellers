Introduction to Bodmin

Bodmin is a charming market town located in the heart of Cornwall, South West England, in the United Kingdom. Surrounded by lush green hills and picturesque countryside, the town is well-known for its rich history, cultural landmarks, stunning scenery, and outdoor activities. Bodmin enjoys a temperate maritime climate, with mild winters and warm summers, making it an ideal destination to visit any time of year.

List of Places to Visit in Bodmin

No trip to Bodmin is complete without visiting its top attractions. Here’s a list to get you started:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bodmin Jail: One of the most significant historical attractions in Bodmin, this former prison dates back to the 18th century and is now a museum that showcases the life and times of prisoners and staff.

  2. Bodmin Church: A beautiful 15th-century church located in the center of the town, featuring stunning stained glass windows, architecture, and historic burials.

  3. Lanhydrock: A grand National Trust property, which boasts a Victorian mansion, magnificent gardens, and miles of woodland trails and cycle routes.

Natural Attractions of Bodmin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bodmin Moor: One of the largest moors in Cornwall, featuring stunning views, hidden valleys, walking trails, and wild ponies.

  2. Cardinham Woods: A peaceful woodland area, perfect for walking, cycling, and picnicking. The woods also offer thrilling mountain biking trails.

  3. Camel Trail: A popular 18-mile cycle and walking route that runs from Padstow to Wenford Bridge, following the scenic River Camel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bodmin

Bodmin is a paradise for outdoors enthusiasts, with a plethora of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some top routes to consider:

  1. The Copper Trail: A challenging 60-mile circular walk around Bodmin Moor, which passes through stunning scenery and local historic sites.

  2. The Camel Trail: A beautiful, flat 18-mile trail that’s ideal for walking or cycling that takes you through wooded valleys and stunning coastal stretches.

  3. The Saints Way: A 30-mile hiking route connects Padstow to Fowey, passing through the beautiful countryside, ancient churches, and monuments along the way.

Best Things to Do in Bodmin: Activities and Tours

Bodmin offers a range of fun activities and interesting tours that will enhance your travel experience. Here are some of the best things to do in Bodmin:

  1. Camel Valley Vineyard: A highly acclaimed vineyard located near Bodmin, where you can take part in wine tasting along with vineyard tours.

  2. Ancient Sites Guided Tour: An informative tour of local ancient sites in Bodmin Moor by a local tour guide.

  3. Bodmin and Wenford Railway: A nostalgic steam train ride that follows a scenic route between Bodmin General and Boscarne Junction.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bodmin

If you’re planning to spend a week in Bodmin, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Bodmin Church, the iconic structure towering over the town.
  • Day 2: Hike along the Copper Trail, a 60-mile route that takes you through Bodmin Moor’s stunning scenery.
  • Day 3: Explore the National Trust’s Lanhydrock House, with an enormous Victorian mansion, gardens, and stunning woodlands.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the Tintagel Castle, the legendary home of King Arthur, or Prussia Cove, a tranquil coastal spot.
  • Day 5: Get a taste of Bodmin’s winery culture with a visit to Camel Valley Vineyard.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a day of cycling along the Camel Trail and immerse yourself in the beautiful scenery.
  • Day 7: End your trip on a historic note with a visit to Bodmin Jail Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Bodmin

Bodmin offers a range of culinary delights, from contemporary cuisine to traditional Cornish dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cornish Pasty: A pastry with a savory filling of beef, carrots, potatoes, and onions, baked to golden perfection

  2. Fish and Chips: A classic British dish, featuring fish dipped in batter and fried alongside thick-cut chips.

  3. Cream Tea – A specialty of Cornwall, which comprises freshly-baked scones, clotted cream, and strawberry jam, served with tea.

Local Beverages

  1. Cornish Ale: A range of locally brewed ales that feature rich, full-bodied flavors that showcase Cornish hops and barley.

  2. Cornish Gin: A spirit produced from locally grown flowers and herbs.

  3. Cornish Cider: A refreshing beverage created from local apple orchards, perfect for a summer drink.

Where to Stay in Bodmin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bodmin has a variety of accommodation options to suit all kinds of travelers, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some stay options to consider:

  1. The Crown Inn: A traditional Cornish pub offering comfortable rooms, great food, and a cozy atmosphere.

  2. The Lanhydrock Hotel and Golf Club: A chic, boutique hotel located in the middle of an 18-hole golf course, offering a premium hotel experience.

  3. YHA Eden Project: A beautiful hostel located near the Eden Project with a range of accommodation options.

What to Buy in Bodmin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bodmin has some unique local products and souvenirs to take home with you to remember your visit. Here are some suggestions to consider:

  1. Cornish Fudge: A mouth-watering sweet treat that’s locally made and comes in various flavors.

  2. Cornish Whiskey: A blended whiskey that features local ingredients.

  3. Cornish Clotted Cream: A thick and rich cream that’s perfect for scones and desserts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bodmin

Cornish culture is unique, with its language, music, and customs. Bodmin is no different. Here are some local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  1. Cornish language: A minority language spoken in some areas of Cornwall, which has Celtic roots.

  2. Folk music: Fiddles, accordions, and tin whistles are commonly found at local pubs, and traditional Cornish folk music is celebrated in Bodmin, especially during the festivals.

  3. May Day Celebrations: Each year, on May 1st, locals in Bodmin and other Cornish towns celebrate the arrival of spring with singing, dancing, and parades.

Best Time to Visit Bodmin

Bodmin can be visited anytime throughout the year. The busiest time is during the summer months, between June to August. However, autumn in Bodmin is quite stunning with the changing fall foliage of the trees, while spring comes alive with bloom and vibrant scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bodmin

To ensure you have a memorable, enjoyable trip, here are some tips for your visit:

Do:

  • Sample some of the famous Cornish pasties or cream teas.

  • Visit Bodmin Jail to learn about the dark history of the prison and Cornwall’s legal history.

  • Try the outdoor activities, especially hiking and mountain biking trails.

  • Take part in the local festivals and events. Peppered throughout the year, it’s a great chance to have fun, get some great food, and experience local culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural surroundings, especially in the moor and countryside.

  • Don’t park in restricted areas

  • Don’t ignore safety regulations during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Bodmin, it’s essential to be aware of safety measures and any potential health issues that may affect your trip.

Safety in Bodmin

  • Be wary of potential traffic, even on smaller roads.

  • Stick to designated paths and regulations when hiking and cycling.

  • Be aware of the coastal tide times if you’re visiting beaches.

  • Always follow safety guidelines if you’re taking part in water-based activities.

Health in Bodmin

  • Make sure to carry any medications or prescriptions with you when traveling.

  • Carry a map and compass with you, or use a GPS tracking app to stay on the right route.

  • Stay hydrated and wear protective clothing for outdoor activities.

Getting to Bodmin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bodmin is easily accessible by bus, train, or car.

Airports Near Bodmin

The nearest airport to Bodmin is Newquay Cornwall Airport, which operates flights to London and other UK and European cities.

Bus Stations Near Bodmin

National Express runs daily services to Bodmin from London, Manchester, and other cities. Visitors can also take a local bus service into Bodmin town center.

Other Options

Bodmin can be easily reached by car, with Plymouth and Exeter in the east, and Newquay in the north, being the major connecting airports.

How to Get Around in Bodmin

Getting around in Bodmin is straightforward through public transportation or renting a car. Bodmin is relatively small, with most popular attractions being within the town center. Another option is to cycle along the scenic Camel Trail

Where to Go Next from Bodmin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bodmin makes the perfect base for exploring the rest of Cornwall. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Fowey: A charismatic riverside town with narrow streets, cozy pubs, and stunning coastal views.
  2. St Ives: A charming, historic seaside town, perfect for a relaxing day out with its maze of cobbled streets and galleries.
  3. Tintagel: A small coastal village, with dramatic crags, ruins, deep caves, and historic castles.

Conclusion

Bodmin is a picturesque and vibrant town that offers a unique fusion of culture, history, and outdoor activities. Enjoyed by visitors to the area, the town represents great opportunities for exploring the natural beauties of Cornwall. Explore local landmarks and have opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, and surfing, in one of the most stunning areas of the British Isles. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re well-equipped to plan a memorable trip to this delightful Cornish town.
