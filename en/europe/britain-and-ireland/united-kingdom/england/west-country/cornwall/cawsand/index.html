Introduction to Cawsand

Cawsand is a picturesque village in Cornwall, located in the West Country of England. With its narrow streets, charming cottages and stunning coastal views, Cawsand is a popular destination for visitors seeking a quintessential British seaside experience. The village has a rich history, dating back to the 17th century when it was a hub for fishing and smuggling. Today, Cawsand is a peaceful and tranquil place, perfect for exploring the great outdoors, soaking up the local culture and enjoying delicious food and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Cawsand

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cawsand is home to several fascinating museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the village’s rich heritage.

  • Cawsand Bay Fort - A well-preserved fort that dates back to the 1860s, offering panoramic views of the Plymouth Sound.
  • Kingsand and Cawsand Heritage Museum - A small museum that showcases the history of the villages and its inhabitants, from fishing and smuggling to life during the wars.
  • St Andrew’s Church - A beautiful, Grade II listed church that dates back to the 15th century, featuring stunning stained-glass windows and a peaceful churchyard.

Natural Attractions of Cawsand: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The village of Cawsand boasts breathtaking natural beauty, with miles of rugged coastline, rolling hills and lush forests.

  • Whitsand Bay - A beautiful, secluded beach that stretches for miles and offers fantastic opportunities for swimming, surfing and sunbathing.
  • Mount Edgcumbe Country Park - A stunning park that spans 865 acres, featuring formal gardens, woodland walks, and magnificent ocean views.
  • South West Coast Path - A scenic walking route that runs along the coast, offering breathtaking views of the sea and cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cawsand

Cawsand is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Rame Head Walk - A picturesque walk that takes you along the South West Coast Path and offers stunning views of the coast and countryside.
  • Tamar Trails Centre - Offering a variety of biking and walking trails, as well as tree-top adventures and zip-lining.
  • Plymouth Cycle Trail - A flat and easy 18-mile route that runs from Cawsand to Plymouth, passing through picturesque villages and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Cawsand: Activities and Tours

Cawsand has plenty of fun and exciting activities for visitors of all ages.

  • Paddleboarding - Experience the thrill of paddleboarding in the calm waters of Cawsand Bay with Cawsand Kayak.
  • Fishing Trips - Join a local fisherman for a unique and exciting fishing trip.
  • Seashore Safaris - Explore the hidden treasures of the rocky shore with a knowledgeable guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cawsand

Day 1: Discover the history of Cawsand Bay Fort and stroll along the South West Coast Path. Day 2: Explore the botanical gardens of Mount Edgcumbe Country Park and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 3: Take a scenic bike ride along the Plymouth Cycle Trail and stop for lunch at one of the local pubs. Day 4: Walk along the Rame Head and enjoy the breathtaking views of the sea and landscape. Day 5: Join a fishing trip and try your luck at catching some fresh fish. Day 6: Go paddleboarding and enjoy the calm waters of Cawsand Bay. Day 7: Indulge in a delicious local dinner and relax in one of the cozy pubs of Cawsand.

Food and Drink Scene of Cawsand

Cawsand has a thriving food and drink scene, showcasing fresh local produce and traditional British cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips - A classic British dish, made with fresh, locally caught fish and served with crispy chips.
  • Cornish Pasty - A savory pastry filled with beef, potatoes, onions, and swede, a traditional Cornish snack.
  • Cream Tea - A quintessential English tea-time treat, consisting of tea served with scones, clotted cream and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Ale - A staple drink in the region, with plenty of local breweries producing their own unique flavors.
  • Cornish Cider - Made from locally grown apples, often served at local markets and fairs.
  • Cornish Cream Tea - A unique take on the traditional tea, featuring Cornish clotted cream and jam.

Where to Stay in Cawsand: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cawsand has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

  • The Devonport Inn - A cozy and welcoming hotel, offering comfortable rooms and great food.
  • The Edgcumbe Arms - A charming and historic pub, featuring well-decorated rooms and a warm atmosphere.
  • The Halfway House Inn - Offering family-friendly accommodation, with clean and spacious rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Cawsand: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cornish Pasties - Freshly made pasties are a popular souvenir, available from local bakeries and shops.
  • Cornish Ale/Cider - Local ales and ciders produced in the region offer a unique taste of Cornwall.
  • Cornish Ceramics - Unique and handmade pottery and ceramics from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Cawsand

Cornish culture is deep-rooted in the region, with a rich history and unique traditions.

  • Cornish Language - The Cornish language is still spoken by a small number of locals and has been recognized as a minority language in the UK.
  • Cornish Music and Dance - Traditional Cornish music and dance are celebrated at local festivals and events throughout the year.
  • May Day Celebrations - A traditional Cornish celebration, featuring music, dance, and the crowning of the May Queen.

Best Time to Visit Cawsand

The best time to visit Cawsand is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the village is buzzing with activity. However, visitors can enjoy the natural beauty and laid-back atmosphere of Cawsand year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cawsand

Do:

  • Do explore the South West Coast Path and take in the beautiful views.
  • Do try the local seafood and Cornish pasties.
  • Do participate in local festivities and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the village.
  • Don’t disturb the local wildlife or ecosystem.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm layers, as the weather can often be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cawsand

Cawsand is a safe and friendly village, but visitors should take precautions like they would in any unfamiliar place.

  • Be aware of cliffs and hazardous terrain when hiking and walking.
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean, as currents can be strong.
  • Be mindful of traffic when walking or biking on the roads.

Health in Cawsand

Visitors to Cawsand should take standard health precautions, such as keeping up with recommended vaccinations and staying hydrated. It’s also recommended to travel with any necessary medications and seek medical assistance if needed.

Getting to Cawsand: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cawsand is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus.

  • Exeter International Airport - 56.5 miles (about 1 hour 20 minutes’ drive)

Bus Stations Near Cawsand

  • Plymouth Bus Station - 7.5 miles (about 20 minutes’ drive)
  • Torpoint Ferry Terminal - 3.6 miles (about 10 minutes’ drive)

Other Options

How to Get Around in Cawsand

Cawsand is a small village that is easily walkable. Biking is also a popular option for exploring the surrounding countryside, and car rental is available for those who prefer to drive.

Conclusion

Cawsand is a charming village with plenty to offer visitors of all ages.
