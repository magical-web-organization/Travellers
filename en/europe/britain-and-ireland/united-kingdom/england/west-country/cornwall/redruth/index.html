Introduction to Redruth

Redruth is a charming town in West Cornwall, located in the United Kingdom. The town has a rich history of mining and is known for its mining heritage. Today, Redruth is a buzzing hub of cultural landmarks, natural wonders, and exciting activities for tourists. The town is also home to a range of accommodation options, and dining spots, making it an ideal destination for all kinds of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Redruth

Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Redruth has something to offer. Here are the top places to visit in Redruth:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Redruth Old Cornwall Society Museum: This museum showcases the unique and colorful history of the town.
  • East Pool Mine: This mine gives visitors an insight into the fascinating world of mining.
  • Carn Brea Castle: This historic site boasts stunning views and is a must-visit destination in Redruth.

Natural Attractions of Redruth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tehidy Country Park: Tehidy Country Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
  • Portreath to Porthtowan Coastal Walk: This coastal walk offers breathtaking views of the coast and is a great way to explore the town.
  • Gwennap Pit: This unique pit, dug out of the earth in the 18th century, is now a natural amphitheater and is perfect for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redruth

Redruth is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Redruth:

  • The Coast to Coast Trail: This trail runs from Portreath to Devoran and is perfect for both hikers and bikers.
  • The Mineral Tramways: These historic industrial trails are perfect for visitors looking to explore the mining heritage of the town.

Best Things to Do in Redruth: Activities and Tours

When it comes to activities and tours, Redruth has a lot to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Redruth:

  • Cornish Mining World Heritage Site Tours: These tours offer an in-depth look into the mining history of the town.
  • The Gwennap Pit Summer Festival: This festival is held every summer and is an excellent opportunity to explore the fascinating history of the town.
  • The Redruth Regeneration Project: This project offers visitors the opportunity to see the town’s ongoing regeneration efforts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Redruth

With so much to see and do in Redruth, planning your trip can be overwhelming. Here’s a seven-day itinerary to make the most of your time in the town:

Food and Drink Scene of Redruth

Redruth may be known for its mining heritage, but the town’s food and drink scene is also worth exploring. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty: This pastry dish is a local specialty made with meat, potatoes, and other fillings.
  • Stargazy Pie: This fish pie features pilchards poking their heads through the pastry as if they’re gazing at the stars.
  • Cream Tea: This tea-time treat consists of scones, clotted cream, jam, and tea.

Local Beverages

  • Tribute Ale: This refreshing beer is brewed locally and is a must-try for visitors.
  • Cornish Mead: This sweet honey wine is a popular local drink and is perfect for those looking for a unique beverage.

Where to Stay in Redruth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Redruth has a wide range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some top choices:

  • Penventon Park Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of the town and boasts a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and spa services.
  • The Unicorn: This traditional pub also has a guesthouse with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • The Collins Arms: This family-run guesthouse is located in the nearby village of Redruth and offers a cozy and welcoming stay.

What to Buy in Redruth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From local crafts to traditional food items, Redruth has a range of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some ideas:

  • Cornish Pasty: This delicious pastry dish makes for a perfect souvenir or snack to take home.
  • Tin and Jewelry: Redruth is known for its mining heritage, and you can find a range of tin-based jewelry souvenirs in the town.
  • Cornish Cider: This refreshing alcoholic drink is made with apples from the nearby orchards and is perfect for those looking for a unique local beverage.

Local Culture and Customs of Redruth

The local culture and customs of Redruth are deeply influenced by the town’s mining heritage. Visitors can explore this rich culture and customs by visiting local museums and attending festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Redruth

The best time to visit Redruth is in the summer months of June through August. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redruth

Do:

  • Try the local Cornish cuisine.
  • Explore the historic mining landmarks and museums.
  • Go on a hiking or biking tour of the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places and natural surroundings.
  • Touch or climb the historic landmarks and buildings.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions of the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redruth

Redruth is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take general precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and following safety guidelines during outdoor activities.

Health in Redruth

There are several medical facilities and pharmacies in and around Redruth. Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical and travel insurance when visiting the town.

Getting to Redruth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redruth

The nearest airport to Redruth is Newquay Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Redruth

Redruth has a bus station that connects the town to other major cities and towns in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Redruth by train, with the town having a railway station that connects it to other cities and towns in the country.

How to Get Around in Redruth

Redruth is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. However, visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Redruth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby towns of Falmouth and St Ives are popular tourist destinations and are worth visiting. The Eden Project, a unique botanical garden located near St Austell, is also a must-visit destination.

Conclusion

Redruth is a charming town with a rich history and culture, and a range of exciting attractions and activities for tourists. From historic landmarks and museums to scenic walks and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in Redruth. Visitors can also explore the local culture and customs, enjoy the local cuisine and beverages, and shop for souvenirs and local products. Planning a trip to Redruth is easy, with a range of accommodation options and transportation facilities available.
