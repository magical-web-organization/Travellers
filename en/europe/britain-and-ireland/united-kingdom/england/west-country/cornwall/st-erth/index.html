Introduction to St Erth

Located in the heart of Cornwall in South West England, St Erth is a quaint village with a population of just over 1,000 people. This charming village is surrounded by beautiful green hills and boasts of rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Bronze Age.

St Erth’s location makes it an ideal place to explore Cornwall’s rugged coastline, its picturesque villages, and historic towns. Its proximity to the stunning St Michael’s Mount and the popular seaside towns of St Ives, Penzance, and Newlyn also makes St Erth an excellent base for exploring the region.

List of Places to Visit in St Erth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St Erth Parish Church: This historic church is dedicated to St. Ercus and boasts of magnificent architecture and beautiful stained-glass windows. The church’s tower offers a breathtaking view of the rolling hills of Cornwall.

  • Nancegollan Round: This hill fort, located on the outskirts of the village, is one of the most well-known ancient sites in Cornwall. It is a perfect place for hiking and picnicking.

  • The Round House and Inn: The building dates back to the 14th century and is one of the oldest structures in St Erth. The inn serves excellent local food and drinks.

  • The Cornish Seal Sanctuary: The Cornish Seal Sanctuary is a great place to visit with children. It is a rescue center for seals and seabirds and has interactive exhibits, a play area, and picnic spots.

Natural Attractions of St Erth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hayle Estuary: The Hayle Estuary is a special place to explore. It is a home to rare birds like Little Egret and Spoonbill. The estuary features various walking and cycling trails.

  • Trencrom Hill: A picturesque hill is a short drive from St Erth. It’s a prominent ancient site in Cornwall that offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside and coastline.

  • Godrevy Beach: Godrevy Beach is a popular surfing spot, and its iconic lighthouse is known for inspiring the writer Virginia Woolf. The beach is ideal for a day trip or leisurely stroll along the golden sands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Erth

St Erth is a haven for hikers and bikers. Here are a few routes that you can try:

  • The Copper Trail: The Copper Trail is a well-known cycling and walking route that follows a 60-mile circuit around West Cornwall’s mining district.

  • The Bird Walk Trail: The Bird Walk Trail is a short hiking trail that starts from Hayle and features stunning estuary views and several bird watching spots.

Best Things to Do in St Erth: Activities and Tours

  • Kernow Adventure Park: Kernow Adventure Park has something for everyone. It features multiple water sports activities like wakeboarding, paddleboarding, and kayaking. They also offer adventure activities like a giant swing, zipline, and bouncy castle.

  • St Erth Jazz Festival: Every August, St Erth hosts a three-day jazz festival that attracts music lovers from all over Cornwall.

Week-Long Itinerary for St Erth

Day 1-2: Arrive in St Erth, explore the village, visit St Erth Parish Church, and enjoy the traditional Cornish cuisine at The Roundhouse and Inn.

Day 3: Head to Hayle Estuary and try the Bird Walk Trail.

Day 4: Explore the ancient Trencrom Hill and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 5: Take a surfing lesson at Godrevy Beach or just relax and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 6: Visit the Cornish Seal Sanctuary and learn about the local marine life.

Day 7: Visit nearby attractions like St Michael’s Mount or St Ives.

Food and Drink Scene of St Erth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty: A hearty pastry that has its roots in Cornwall.

  • Cream Tea: A quintessential Cornish delicacy consisting of scones, clotted cream, and jam, served with a cup of tea.

  • Cornish Clotted Cream Ice Cream: A frozen treat that is creamy, thick, and utterly delicious.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Ale: The region has a thriving brewing scene, and ales like St Austell Brewery’s Tribute Ale and Skinner’s Brewery’s Betty Stogs are favorites among locals and visitors alike.

  • Cornish Cider: The locally produced cider is made from the freshest apples grown in Cornwall and is popular throughout the country.

Where to Stay in St Erth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Old Quay House Hotel: This Victorian hotel is located on the banks of the estuary and features luxurious rooms with stunning estuary views.

  • The Star Inn: A family-run inn that offers comfortable rooms and serves traditional Cornish cuisine.

  • The Old Forge Guesthouse: An 18th-century building converted into a guesthouse, located in the heart of the village.

What to Buy in St Erth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cornish Fudge: This mouth-watering treat is a must-buy souvenir from St Erth.

  • Cornish Whiskey: A unique and pleasant flavor that distinguishes it from Scotch whiskeys.

Local Culture and Customs of St Erth

St Erth is proud of its Cornish heritage and culture, and there are several annual festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including the Feast of St. Ercus and St Erth Jazz Festival. Cornish language and literature are also actively preserved here.

Best Time to Visit St Erth

The best time to visit St Erth is between April and October, when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. However, the village is beautiful all year round, and each season has something unique to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Erth

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

  • Do sample the local food and drink.

  • Do try to learn a few Cornish words and phrases.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the streets or beaches.

  • Don’t forget to carry warm and waterproof clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in St Erth

St Erth is a safe and peaceful village with friendly locals. However, as with any place, it is advisable to take common safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in St Erth

St Erth has excellent healthcare facilities, and you can find several pharmacies and clinics nearby.

Getting to St Erth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St Erth

The nearest airport to St Erth is Newquay Airport, which is approximately 40 minutes drive away.

Bus Stations Near St Erth

St Erth has its own train station and is well-connected by local buses to surrounding towns.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are readily available in St Erth.

How to Get Around in St Erth

St Erth is a small village, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Cycling and driving are also popular modes of transportation for exploring the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from St Erth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St Ives: St Ives is a beautiful seaside town with fantastic galleries, cafés, and restaurants.

  • Penzance: This historic and vibrant town is a bustling port with a fascinating maritime history.

  • St Michael’s Mount: A medieval castle and surrounding gardens set on a tidal island in Mount’s Bay.

Conclusion

St Erth is a hidden gem that offers the perfect combination of natural beauty, historical significance, and modern amenities. With its convenient location, excellent accommodation options, and rich cultural and natural attractions, St Erth is the perfect place to explore Cornwall.
