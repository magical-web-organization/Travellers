Introduction to St Columb Major

Nestled in the heart of Cornwall in West Country, St Columb Major is a charming market town renowned for its rich history, scenic countryside, and relaxed vibes. A popular destination for tourists and locals alike, St Columb Major offers a plethora of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities for visitors of all ages and interests.

With its winding lanes, picturesque architecture, and friendly locals, St Columb Major is a quintessential Cornish town that is sure to steal your heart.

List of Places to Visit in St Columb Major

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll love exploring the cultural attractions in St Columb Major. From museums to historical landmarks, there are plenty of places to discover here, such as:

  • St Columb Major Museum: Housed in a historic Georgian building, St Columb Major Museum is a treasure trove of local history and culture. Here, you can explore the town’s past through fascinating exhibits depicting rural life, mining heritage, and more.

  • St Columb Major Church: This stunning medieval church is a must-visit for architecture enthusiasts and history lovers alike. Admire its intricate stained glass windows, ornate carvings, and peaceful atmosphere.

  • Pencarrow House and Gardens: Located just outside of St Columb Major, Pencarrow House and Gardens is a grand Georgian manor house with beautiful gardens and parkland open to visitors.

Natural Attractions of St Columb Major: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, you’ll find plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic spots to explore in and around St Columb Major. Here are a few highlights:

  • Camel Trail: This 18-mile trail follows an old railway line and offers breathtaking views of the Cornish countryside. Whether you’re walking, cycling, or horse riding, you won’t be disappointed by the stunning views and peaceful surroundings.

  • The Goss Moor: This nature reserve boasts miles of scenic trails, diverse wildlife, and stunning views. Take a leisurely stroll or a more challenging hike and experience the beauty of Cornwall.

  • Wheal Martyn: Situated just a few miles from St Columb Major, Wheal Martyn is a museum and nature reserve dedicated to showcasing Cornwall’s rich mining heritage. Here, you can explore interactive exhibits, learn about the area’s history, and enjoy the surrounding natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Columb Major

St Columb Major is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • St Columb Major Walks: This website offers a variety of self-guided walks around the town and surrounding areas, ranging from easy strolls to more challenging hikes. Discover the stunning Cornish countryside at your own pace.

  • The Camel Trail: This popular trail is perfect for cycling, walking, or horse riding. With its scenic views and easy terrain, it’s a great way to explore the beauty of North Cornwall.

  • The Saints Way: This historic trail follows the route of pilgrims who travelled between Padstow and Fowey in medieval times. Today, it’s a popular hiking route that takes you through some of Cornwall’s most beautiful countryside.

Best Things to Do in St Columb Major: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for family-friendly activities or more adventurous pursuits, St Columb Major has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • St Columb Major Agricultural Show: This annual event is a highlight of the town’s calendar, attracting visitors from across the UK. With farm animals, craft demonstrations, and live entertainment, it’s an extravaganza of country life.

  • Cornish Wine Tours: Discover the delicious wines of Cornwall on a guided tour of the region’s vineyards and wineries. Taste the local produce and learn about the history and techniques of winemaking in this unique part of the UK.

  • Bodmin Jail: Located just a short drive from St Columb Major, Bodmin Jail is a historic prison that has been converted into a museum. Explore the cells, learn about the inmates, and experience a slice of Cornish history.

Week-Long Itinerary for St Columb Major

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will give you a taste of everything St Columb Major has to offer:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s cultural attractions, including the museum, church, and local art galleries.

  • Day 2: Take a scenic hike or bike ride along the Camel Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the Cornish countryside.

  • Day 3: Discover the natural beauty of the Goss Moor and its diverse wildlife.

  • Day 4: Visit Pencarrow House and Gardens, a grand Georgian manor house with beautiful gardens and parkland open to visitors.

  • Day 5: Taste the local cuisine by visiting one of St Columb Major’s many foodie hotspots.

  • Day 6: Discover the area’s mining heritage by visiting Wheal Martyn, a museum and nature reserve dedicated to showcasing Cornwall’s rich mining history.

  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations, such as the seaside towns of Padstow or Newquay.

Food and Drink Scene of St Columb Major

St Columb Major has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of local delicacies and artisanal producers to discover. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasties: These delicious meat and vegetable pastries are a Cornish specialty and are available at most local bakeries and cafes.

  • Cream Teas: Indulge in a classic English tradition by enjoying a fresh scone served with jam and clotted cream, paired with a cup of tea.

  • Cornish Seafood: With its proximity to the sea, St Columb Major offers some of the freshest and most delicious seafood in the UK. Be sure to try the local catch of the day at one of the town’s seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Cider: Cornwall is home to some of the best cider producers in the UK, with a variety of flavors and styles to choose from.

  • Cornish Ales: If you’re a beer lover, you’ll appreciate the unique flavors and aromas of Cornish ales, made with locally grown hops and barley.

  • Cornish Gin: Cornwall is home to a growing number of artisanal gin producers, each with their own unique blend of botanicals and flavors.

Where to Stay in St Columb Major: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St Columb Major offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the most popular choices:

  • The Smugglers Inn: This historic inn offers comfortable rooms and great food in a charming setting.

  • The Falcon Hotel: With its traditional decor and friendly staff, the Falcon Hotel is a popular choice for visitors to St Columb Major.

  • YHA Treyarnon Bay: Located just a few miles from St Columb Major, this youth hostel offers affordable accommodation in a stunning coastal location.

What to Buy in St Columb Major: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to St Columb Major is complete without taking home some local treasures. Here are a few souvenirs and products to consider:

  • Cornish pottery: From traditional earthenware to contemporary designs, Cornish pottery is a popular souvenir for visitors to the region.

  • Cornish fudge: Soft, creamy, and delicious, Cornish fudge is a sweet treat that makes for a great gift.

  • Cornish wines and spirits: Bring home a bottle of locally produced wine or gin to remind you of your trip to St Columb Major.

Local Culture and Customs of St Columb Major

St Columb Major is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community and tradition. While there are no specific cultural customs to be aware of, it’s always advisable to be respectful of local etiquette and norms.

Best Time to Visit St Columb Major

St Columb Major is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm. Summer is the busiest time of year, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities available. Spring and autumn offer milder weather and fewer crowds, while winter is a quiet and peaceful time to explore the town’s cultural attractions and natural beauty.

Safety and Health

St Columb Major is generally a safe and welcoming town, with low levels of crime. However, as with any destination, it’s advisable to take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and being aware of your surroundings when out and about.
