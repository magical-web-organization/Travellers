Introduction to Charlestown

Charlestown is an historic town and port located on the south coast of Cornwall, in southwest England. This picturesque village is renowned for its Georgian harbor, which dates back to the late 18th century and was once a hub for the export of copper ore and china clay. Today, it is a popular tourist destination that offers visitors a glimpse into life in a bygone era. With its narrow streets, beautiful beaches, and plethora of attractions, Charlestown is a perfect place to explore and unwind.

List of Places to Visit in Charlestown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Charlestown is home to a number of significant cultural attractions and historical landmarks. Highlights include:

  • The Charlestown Shipwreck Centre: A fascinating museum that showcases the history of sea travel, with a particular focus on shipwrecks and salvaging.
  • Charlestown Harbour: This stunning Georgian harbour is a Grade II listed site and features a number of historic boats and vessels.
  • The Rashleigh Arms: A beautiful, historic pub that was once the home of the Rashleigh family, one of Charlestown’s most prominent families.
  • Porthpean House: This gorgeous Grade II* listed country house is a prime example of Georgian architecture and boasts stunning views of Charlestown Bay.
  • The Eden Project: A huge, world-renowned attraction that features a vast array of exotic plants and ecosystems.

Natural Attractions of Charlestown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Charlestown is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, from breathtaking coastal walks to beautiful beaches. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Charlestown Beach: A picture-perfect sandy beach that is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • The South West Coast Path: A stunning trail that runs right through Charlestown and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Portholland Beach: A secluded, shingle beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and exploring rock pools.
  • The Clay Trails: A network of cycling and walking trails that traverse the stunning countryside around Charlestown.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charlestown

Charlestown offers a range of walking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness and experience. Some of the most scenic routes include:

  • The South West Coast Path: This trail offers stunning views of the coastline and takes in many of Charlestown’s key attractions.
  • The Saints Way: A beautiful long-distance walking trail that runs from Padstow on the north coast of Cornwall to Fowey on the south coast, passing through Charlestown along the way.
  • The Great Flat Lode Trail: This circular trail takes in the beautiful Cornish countryside and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Charlestown: Activities and Tours

Charlestown offers a wealth of activities and tours that will keep you entertained for days. Here are some of the best things to do in Charlestown:

  • Take a boat tour of the harbor to see the historic ships up close.
  • Visit the Charlestown Shipwreck Centre to learn about the town’s maritime history.
  • Go for a scenic walk along the South West Coast Path.
  • Visit the historic Rashleigh Arms pub for a pint and some traditional Cornish fare.
  • Enjoy a leisurely day at Charlestown Beach.
  • Take a cooking class at the Cornwall School of Cookery.
  • Explore Porthpean House and its beautiful gardens.

Week-Long Itinerary for Charlestown

Charlestown is a perfect destination for a week-long holiday, with plenty of attractions to keep you busy. Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in Charlestown:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Charlestown and explore the town at your leisure, taking in the harbor, beach, and local shops.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of the harbor and visit the Charlestown Shipwreck Centre.
  • Day 3: Go for a scenic walk along the South West Coast Path and enjoy a picnic lunch on the beach.
  • Day 4: Visit nearby attractions such as the Eden Project or the Lost Gardens of Heligan.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a day of relaxation and pampering at one of the local spas.
  • Day 6: Take a cooking class at the Cornwall School of Cookery and learn how to make traditional Cornish dishes.
  • Day 7: Explore the local countryside on a bike or hiking tour, taking in the stunning scenery and fascinating sights.

Food and Drink Scene of Charlestown

Must-Try Dishes

Cornish cuisine is renowned for its fresh seafood, creamy dairy products, and hearty pasties. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Charlestown:

  • Cornish pasties: These hearty meat, potato, and vegetable pies are a staple of Cornish cuisine and can be found at almost every cafe and bakery in Charlestown.
  • Fish and chips: Charlestown is home to plenty of excellent fish and chip shops, serving up freshly caught seafood and crispy fries.
  • Cream tea: A British classic, cream tea consists of scones, jam, clotted cream, and a pot of tea.

Local Beverages

Cornish beer and cider are popular drinks in Charlestown, with numerous pubs and bars offering a wide variety of locally produced beverages. Some of the best places to enjoy a drink in Charlestown include:

  • The Rashleigh Arms: This historic pub serves up a great selection of local ales and ciders in a cozy, traditional setting.
  • The Pier House Hotel: A stylish and contemporary hotel that boasts a beautiful bar with views over the harbor.
  • The Longstore: This quirky bar serves up creative cocktails and a wide selection of beers and wines.

Where to Stay in Charlestown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charlestown offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay in Charlestown:

  • The Pier House Hotel: A beautiful, boutique hotel that offers stunning views over Charlestown harbor.
  • The Rashleigh Arms: This charming pub also offers comfortable guest rooms with traditional decor and modern amenities.
  • The Mevagissey Bay Hotel: Located just a short drive from Charlestown, this elegant hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites with sea views.
  • The Old Parsonage Guest House: A quaint and cozy guesthouse that offers homely, affordable accommodation in the heart of Charlestown.

What to Buy in Charlestown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cornwall is known for its locally produced crafts and food products, and Charlestown is no exception. Here are some of the best local products and souvenirs to bring home:

  • Cornish fudge: Made with dairy cream and butter, Cornish fudge is a sweet and delicious treat that is sold in many local shops.
  • Handmade ceramics: Charlestown is home to a number of talented ceramic artists, whose work can be found in various local galleries and shops.
  • Cornish ale and cider: If you’re a fan of beer and cider, be sure to pick up a bottle or two of locally produced brews to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Charlestown

Cornwall has a unique culture and heritage, with its own language, music, and traditions. Some of the cultural highlights of Charlestown include:

  • The Cornwall Folk Festival: A popular music festival that takes place in nearby Wadebridge every August.
  • The St. Piran’s Day Parade: This annual parade celebrates the patron saint of Cornwall with music, dancing, and traditional dress.
  • The Falmouth International Sea Shanty Festival: This popular festival celebrates maritime traditions with live music, food, and drink.

Best Time to Visit Charlestown

Charlestown is a great destination to visit throughout the year, with each season offering its own charm and attractions. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with plenty of sunshine and warm weather. However, spring and autumn are also excellent times to visit, with fewer crowds and cooler temperatures. Winter can be chilly, but the town has a cozy and festive atmosphere during the holiday season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charlestown

Do:

  • Do take a boat tour of the harbor and visit the historic ships.
  • Do explore local attractions such as the Charlestown Shipwreck Centre and Porthpean House.
  • Do try traditional Cornish dishes such as fish and chips and Cornish pasties.
  • Do take a walk on the South West Coast Path for stunning views of the coastline.

Don’t:

  • Don’t swim in restricted areas of the harbor or beach.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing and rain gear, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t litter or damage local wildlife and habitats.
  • Don’t forget to appreciate the natural beauty and historic charm of Charlestown without rushing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charlestown

Charlestown is a safe and welcoming town that is ideal for visitors of all ages. However, it is important to take precautions to ensure your safety, such as:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Observe any warning signs or restrictions at the beach or harbor to stay safe.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Health in Charlestown

Charlestown has a range of medical facilities, including a general practice, medical center, and pharmacy. In case of an emergency, visitors can dial 999 for assistance.

Getting to Charlestown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Charlestown is located just off the A390 road in Cornwall and is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Charlestown

  • Newquay Cornwall Airport: This airport is located just 17 miles from Charlestown and offers flights to destinations across the UK and Europe.
  • Exeter Airport: This larger airport is located approximately 70 miles from Charlestown and offers flights to a range of domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Charlestown

  • St. Austell Bus Station: This major bus station is just a short drive from Charlestown and offers connections to destinations throughout Cornwall and beyond.
  • Fowey Bus Station: This smaller bus station is located just a few miles from Charlestown and offers connections to destinations along the south coast of Cornwall.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is located in St Austell, which provides regular services to various locations in the UK.
  • Car: Charlestown is easily accessible via the A390 road.

How to Get Around in Charlestown

Charlestown is a small town that is easily navigated on foot, with most attractions within walking distance of each other. There are also plenty of options for cycling, including the Clay Trails and the Saints Way. Local buses and taxis are also available for visitors who need to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Charlestown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Charlestown is ideally situated for exploring the beautiful countryside and coastline of Cornwall. Some nearby destinations to consider include:

  • The Lost Gardens of Heligan: Located just a few miles from Charlestown, this stunning botanical garden offers a range of exotic plants and beautiful landscapes.
  • St Austell: This bustling town is just a short drive from Charlestown and offers a range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Fowey: This picturesque town on the south coast of Cornwall is just a few miles from Charlestown and boasts a range of quirky shops, cafes, and galleries.
  • The Eden Project: This world-renowned attraction is just a short drive from Charlestown and offers a vast array of exotic plants and ecosystems.

Conclusion

Charlestown is a charming and historic town that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Cornish culture and heritage. With its stunning harbor, beautiful beaches, and wealth of attractions, it is an ideal destination for anybody looking to unwind and explore the beauty and history of southwest England. Whether you’re interested in exploring the local history and culture, enjoying outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing on the beach, Charlestown has something for everyone.
