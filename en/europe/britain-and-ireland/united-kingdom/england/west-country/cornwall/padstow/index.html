Introduction to Padstow

Padstow, located in Cornwall’s West Country, is a charming seaside town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. With its picturesque harbor, sandy beaches, and rugged coastline, Padstow is a popular holiday destination among tourists.

In this comprehensive guide, we have curated a list of top attractions and activities that you must explore while in Padstow.

List of Places to Visit in Padstow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Padstow Museum: A wonderful museum that offers a glimpse into Padstow’s rich cultural heritage. The museum is situated in a beautiful old building and features exhibits on local history, fishing, and farming.
  • Prideaux Place: This historic manor house is an excellent representation of the Elizabethan era, with its beautiful architecture, art and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Padstow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Trevose Head: This stunning headland offers breathtaking views of the rugged coastline and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Camel Estuary: A nature lover’s paradise, Camel Estuary is home to an abundant array of bird and marine mammal species, making it an ideal spot for bird watching and seal spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Padstow

Padstow is the perfect destination for hikers and bikers alike. Here are some popular trails to check out:

  • The Camel Trail: This former railway line has been converted into a multi-use trail that runs between Padstow and Bodmin, offering breathtaking views of the estuary and the surrounding countryside.
  • The South West Coast Path: This walking trail covers more than 600 miles of coastline, and the Padstow section offers spectacular views of the sea and cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Padstow: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Land Rover Safari: Discover the natural beauty of Padstow on a guided Land Rover expedition, which takes visitors to some of the town’s most impressive natural landmarks.
  • A Taste of Padstow Food Tour: Sample the town’s finest dishes and local delicacies on a food tour through its narrow streets and quaint fishing port.

Week-Long Itinerary for Padstow

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel. Spend the day exploring the local markets and shops.

Day 2: Take a stroll through the town’s historic center and visit the Padstow Museum.

Day 3: Enjoy a walking tour along the South West Coast Path followed by lunch at a local pub.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Trevose Head and admire the stunning coastal views.

Day 5: Hop on a boat for a scenic tour of the Camel Estuary.

Day 6: Take a food tour and indulge in the town’s finest cuisine, followed by a relaxing day on the sandy beaches.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Padstow

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasties: A savory pastry filled with meat and vegetables. Authentic Padstow pasties come from the Chough Bakery.
  • Fresh Seafood: Sample the town’s freshly caught seafood at one of its many seaside restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Cider: Made with locally grown apples in traditional presses, Cornish cider is a great way to cool off on a hot summer day.
  • Local Beers: Try Doom Bar, Cornish Knocker, or Betty Stogs, all of which are popular local brews.

Where to Stay in Padstow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

No matter what your budget or preferences are, Padstow has a variety of accommodation options to choose from:

  • The Metropole Hotel: Located in the heart of Padstow, this hotel is known for its old-world charm and beautiful Victorian architecture.
  • B&B: Try Penjolyan, a cozy and comfortable bed and breakfast located just a short walk from the town center.
  • Hostels: YHA Eden Project and Newquay Hostel are two budget accommodation options in nearby towns.

What to Buy in Padstow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Padstow Piskie: This mythical creature is a famous legend in Cornwall. Look for Piskie-themed souvenirs as a quirky and unique keepsake.
  • Local Sea Salt: Cornish sea salt is renowned for its unique flavor and is a popular ingredient in many local dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Padstow

Padstow has a rich cultural heritage and celebrates this with several annual festivals and traditions. One of the most famous is the May Day celebrations, which includes the Obby Oss ceremony, where two costumed ‘osses dance through the town’s streets.

Best Time to Visit Padstow

The best time to visit Padstow is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are lively.

Do’s and Don’ts of Padstow

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood, it is one of the specialties of the town.
  • Do take a guided tour of the town and nearby attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen if you’re visiting during the summer months to protect against inevitable sunburns.
  • Don’t litter on the beaches. Padstow is renowned for its clean and scenic beaches, so help keep it that way by being responsible.

Safety and Health

Safety in Padstow

Padstow is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always wise to take standard safety precautions, such as securing your valuables, not leaving valuables in your car, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Padstow

Ensure that you have all the medications you need on you when you travel to Padstow. The town has a medical center and a pharmacy, but it’s always best to be prepared.

Getting to Padstow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Padstow

Newquay airport is the closest to Padstow, with flights to major UK cities like London, Manchester, and Liverpool.

Bus Stations Near Padstow

Padstow has a bus station with regular services to surrounding areas and towns, including larger cities like Plymouth and Exeter.

Other Options

Driving to Padstow is also an option, with the town located just off the A39 and close to the M5 motorway. Ferry services are available from nearby towns and cities as well.

How to Get Around in Padstow

Walking is the most convenient way to get around the town, as most of the local attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Biking is another option, with several bike rental shops available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Padstow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby places to visit include Rock, a small village across the River Camel, and the famous Lost Gardens of Heligan, located about an hour’s drive away.

Conclusion

Padstow is a beautiful and quaint seaside town that offers visitors a serene and peaceful retreat from their busy everyday lives. With its rich history, traditional cuisine, and scenic natural beauty, Padstow has something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Cornwall.
