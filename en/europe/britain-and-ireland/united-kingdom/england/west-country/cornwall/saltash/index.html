Introduction to Saltash

Located in Cornwall, in the West Country of England, Saltash is a small town that offers an authentic British experience. Known for its unique heritage and culture, stunning countryside, and rich history, the town has a lot to offer to tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Saltash

Whether you have an interest in history, nature, or just want some adventure, Saltash has something for everyone. Here are some notable attractions to add to your Saltash travel itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saltash Heritage Museum: This museum provides an insight into the town’s history and culture and showcases exhibitions about the famous Brunel railway bridge.
  • Mary Newman’s Cottage: The 15th-century cottage is said to be the home of Sir Francis Drake’s wife and offers a glimpse into the lives of the locals during the Tudor period.
  • Mount Edgcumbe House and Country Park: Home to the Earls of Mount Edgcumbe, this 17th-century stately mansion is surrounded by beautiful gardens and provides stunning views of Plymouth Sound.

Natural Attractions of Saltash: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Edgcumbe Coastal Park: Enjoy a walk in this 800-acre park that offers stunning views of the coastline, beautiful gardens, and Grade I listed buildings.
  • Tamar Trails Centre: Perfect for nature lovers, this centre offers outdoor activities like hiking, canoeing, and biking.
  • Whitsand Bay: The perfect spot for sunbathing, swimming, and surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saltash

Saltash is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Tamar Trails: Enjoy the stunning beauty of the Tamar Valley by taking a walk or cycling through the well-marked trails.
  • Rame Head Walk: This route offers some of the best views of the countryside and coastline.
  • Great Flat Lode Trail: This six-mile circular route provides a true sense of Cornwall’s mining history.

Best Things to Do in Saltash: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Saltash. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience:

  • Tamar River Cruises: Enjoy a scenic tour of the River Tamar and journey through the area’s rich maritime history.
  • Royal Albert Bridge Tour: Take a behind-the-scenes tour of the famous bridge designed by Isambard Kingdom Brunel.
  • Sailing: Join a local sailing club or book a sailing tour to experience the area’s stunning coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saltash

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most out of your trip to Saltash:

  • Day 1: Visit the Saltash Heritage Museum and explore the town centre.
  • Day 2: Explore Mount Edgcumbe House and the nearby coastal park.
  • Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking tour in Tamar Trails Centre.
  • Day 4: Take a Tamar River Cruise or explore the Royal Albert Bridge on a guided tour.
  • Day 5: Relax on Whitsand Bay or visit Plymouth’s Barbican area.
  • Day 6: Visit Mary Newman’s Cottage and explore the nearby countryside.
  • Day 7: Spend the day sailing and enjoying the stunning coastline.

Food and Drink Scene of Saltash

Saltash offers a wide range of dining options that celebrate the area’s local produce and traditional recipes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish pasty: A traditional savoury pastry filled with meat and vegetables.
  • Cream tea: Scones, clotted cream, and jam served with tea.
  • Fish and chips: Freshly caught fish served with chips.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish ales: A range of locally brewed ales and ciders.
  • Cornish gin: A range of exotic flavoured gin and tonic.

Where to Stay in Saltash: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saltash has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets. Here are some of your options:

  • The Crooked Inn: A traditional inn with charming rooms and a cosy bar.
  • China Fleet Country Club: A luxurious resort that offers facilities such as swimming pool, gym, spa, and golf course.
  • Little Fox Hotel & Restaurant: A boutique hotel with spacious rooms and a restaurant that serves delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in Saltash

Saltash has shops that sell souvenirs and local products ideal for bringing back home. Here are some of the things you might want to consider:

  • Cornish clotted cream: A creamy and delicious dessert topping.
  • Cornish seafood: Fresh seafood like mussels, lobsters, and crabs.
  • Local crafts: Handmade crafts ranging from pottery to textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Saltash

The locals are very proud of their culture and history. You will experience a sense of community in the town and the surrounding villages. Saltash has a thriving arts and music scene, with regular events and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Saltash

The best time to visit Saltash is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Alternatively, you can visit during the spring months (March-May), when the town’s countryside is covered in beautiful wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saltash

Do:

  • Respect the local culture, customs, and the environment.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the area’s natural beauty on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Litter the countryside or beaches.
  • Ignore the town’s rich history and heritage.
  • Disrespect the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Saltash is generally a safe place for tourists, with low crime rates. While the town doesn’t have a hospital, there are several clinics and pharmacies available. Do take necessary precautions like applying sunscreen, drinking enough water, and wearing comfortable clothing and shoes.

Safety in Saltash

  • Take care when walking or cycling on busy roads.
  • Be careful when exploring the coastal areas, as tides can be dangerous.

Health in Saltash

  • It’s advisable to get travel insurance and check any vaccinations beforehand.
  • Take precautions against insect bites during the summer months.

Getting to Saltash: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saltash is easily accessible by different modes of transport. Here are some transportation options and logistics to guide you:

Airports Near Saltash

  • Newquay Airport - 1 hour drive
  • Bristol Airport - 2-hour drive

Bus Stations Near Saltash

  • Plymouth Bus Station - 15-minute drive

Other Options

  • Train: Saltash has a railway station that connects to Plymouth and other major cities.
  • Driving: Saltash is easily accessible by car, with good road connections.

How to Get Around in Saltash

Saltash is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. However, for visitors who wish to explore the nearby areas, renting a bike or car is the best option.

Where to Go Next from Saltash

Saltash is an excellent base for exploring Cornwall and Devon. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to add to your travel itinerary:

  • The Eden Project - 1 hour drive
  • St. Ives - 1.5 hour drive
  • Dartmoor National Park - 1.5 hour drive

Conclusion

Saltash offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. From its natural beauty and rich history to its authentic culture and delicious cuisine, the town has something for every traveller. Use this guide to help plan your next vacation to this beautiful part of England, and get ready to create lasting memories.
