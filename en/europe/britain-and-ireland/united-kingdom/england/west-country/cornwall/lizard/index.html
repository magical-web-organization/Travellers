Introduction to Lizard

Lizard Peninsula is situated on the southern coast of Cornwall, in England, UK. It is known for its breathtaking scenery, natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. Lizard is the southernmost point of the mainland of Great Britain and has a unique location that makes it a popular destination for tourists. It attracts visitors from all over the world who come here to explore its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture.

If you are planning a trip to Lizard, this comprehensive travel guide will help you to plan your trip well by providing you with all the necessary information about this beautiful place.

List of Places to Visit in Lizard

Lizard is a small peninsula, but there are many places to visit here. Here is a list of the top attractions in Lizard:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lizard Lighthouse Heritage Center: A historical lighthouse converted into a fantastic museum that offers a panoramic view of the peninsula.
  • Goonhilly Earth Station: This is the largest satellite earth station in the world, and it offers a great opportunity to learn about space exploration and communication.
  • Trebah Garden: One of the best gardens in the UK and one of the Great Gardens of Cornwall.

Natural Attractions of Lizard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kynance Cove: A stunning destination popular with tourists due to its crystal-clear waters and dramatic cliffs.
  • Lizard Point: The southernmost point of mainland Britain is a great spot for a coastal walk.
  • Flambards Theme Park: A classic British amusement park with rides, attractions, and indoor exhibitions on British history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lizard

Lizard is a paradise for walkers and hikers as there are many beautiful coastal paths and trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking routes in Lizard:

  • Lizard Coastal Walk: A 7.5-mile walk that starts and ends at Lizard Point.
  • Saint Michael’s Way: A historic pilgrimage route that runs from Lizard to Saint Michael’s Mount.
  • Lizard Peninsula Circular Walk: A 15.5-mile hike that explores the beautiful coastline and countryside of the area.

Best Things to Do in Lizard: Activities and Tours

Here is a list of the best things to do in Lizard:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to explore the beautiful coastline of Lizard, including the famous Lizard Point.
  • Surfing: Ride some of the best waves in the UK at the beaches of Lizard.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the coastline of Lizard by kayaking or canoeing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lizard

Here’s our suggested one-week itinerary for Lizard:

  • Day 1: Visit the Lizard Lighthouse Heritage Center and enjoy the view from the top.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of Lizard’s coastline and explore the sea caves.
  • Day 3: Go hiking along the Saint Michael’s Way and visit some of the local villages.
  • Day 4: Explore Cornwall’s mining heritage with a visit to Geevor Tin Mine.
  • Day 5: Journey to Kynance Cove and explore its stunning beauty.
  • Day 6: Experience rural life at Cornish fermented bakery and taste the famous Cornish pasties.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy a day at Flambards Theme Park with family and friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Lizard

The food and drink scene in Lizard is not to be missed. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty: A baked pastry filled with beef, potato, onion, and seasoning.
  • Stargazy Pie: A traditional Cornish fish pie with sardines poking through the crust, nibbling at the sky.
  • Cornish Cream Tea: A tea served with scones, jam, and clotted cream.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Cider: A traditional cider made from Cornish apples.
  • Cornish Gin: A delicious gin made from local botanicals and herbs.

Where to Stay in Lizard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lizard offers a range of accommodation options for its visitors, from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Lizard:

  • The Polurrian Bay Hotel: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of the sea.
  • Sandsifter Hotel & Restaurant: A beautiful hotel with a restaurant serving fresh seafood.
  • Penmenner House Bed and Breakfast: A family-run guesthouse with beautiful gardens and great views.

What to Buy in Lizard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs to take home from Lizard, here are some of the best options:

  • Cornish Pasty: Take home a Cornish pasty and enjoy it on your way back home.
  • Cornish Cheese: Cornwall is famous for its cheese, so don’t forget to take some back home.
  • Cornish Jewelry: Cornwall has a rich history of mining, and its jewelry industry is world-renowned.

Local Culture and Customs of Lizard

Lizard has a unique cultural heritage that is worth exploring. The traditional way of life in Lizard is farming, fishing, and mining, and you can still see the remnants of these in the local culture. The Lizard Peninsula has a strong connection with the sea, and the local people have developed a deep love and respect for the ocean.

Best Time to Visit Lizard

The best time to visit Lizard is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you want to avoid the crowds, the shoulder season (May and September) is also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lizard

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Explore the beautiful coastline of Lizard.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or other natural areas.
  • Disturb the wildlife or natural habitats.
  • Ignore the signs and warnings for safety reasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lizard

Lizard is a safe destination for tourists, but petty theft can occur in crowded areas. To ensure that your trip is safe and secure, keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Lizard

There are no major health risks to traveling to Lizard, but it’s always a good idea to pack a first-aid kit, including any necessary medications.

Getting to Lizard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lizard

The nearest airport to Lizard is Newquay Airport, which is approximately 31 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Lizard

There are no direct bus services to Lizard, but you can get buses to nearby towns such as Helston and Truro.

Other Options

You can also drive to Lizard, take a train to the nearby stations of Truro or Penzance, and then take a bus or taxi to Lizard.

How to Get Around in Lizard

The best way to explore Lizard is on foot or by bicycle. Alternatively, you can hire a car or take public transport to reach some of the places in the area.

Where to Go Next from Lizard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations that you can visit from Lizard:

  • Saint Michael’s Mount: A beautiful tidal island with a castle and garden, located 32 miles away.
  • The Lost Gardens of Heligan: A restored garden with sculptures and a vast collection of plants, located 41 miles away.
  • Tintagel Castle: A historic castle with beautiful views of the sea, located 55 miles away.

Conclusion

Lizard is a stunning destination that has something to offer for everyone. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Lizard, Cornwall, England. Enjoy your trip to Lizard, and make the most out of your journey.
