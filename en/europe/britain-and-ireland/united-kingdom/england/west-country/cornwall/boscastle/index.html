Introduction to Boscastle

Located on the north coast of Cornwall, Boscastle is a charming fishing village, perched on a steep hill and surrounded by rugged cliffs, natural landscapes, and fascinating history. Boscastle has a unique character with its distinctive harbor, quaint cottages, and a rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Boscastle

Boscastle offers a diverse range of places to visit, including museums, historical buildings, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Boscastle’s rich heritage and culture are preserved and presented in various fascinating attractions, such as:

  • Museum of Witchcraft and Magic
  • Boscastle’s National Trust Shop, which is a former Pilchard Curing Cellar
  • The Boscastle Flood Museum, which presents the history of the village’s devastating floods in 2004, and its effects on the community

Natural Attractions of Boscastle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Boscastle’s natural landscape offers unforgettable experiences of stunning views, wildlife, and outdoor activities, such as:

  • The South West Coast Path, offering breathtaking views of the village, the harbor, and the Atlantic coastline.
  • The Valency Valley Walk, which is a beautiful hiking trail that leads to the waterfalls and woodlands.
  • St. Nectan’s Glen, a magical place that features a stunning 60ft waterfall, and believed to be a place of spiritual power.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boscastle

Boscastle is an excellent base for exploring the beautiful countryside and coastal paths of Cornwall. The village offers several options for hikes and biking routes, such as:

  • The Tintagel Castle Walk. This circular hike leads from Boscastle to Tintagel Castle, and back to Boscastle, passing through stunning scenery, including King Arthur’s Castle.
  • The Camel Trail, a scenic 18-mile biking and hiking trail that follows the Camel Estuary from Wadebridge to Padstow.

Best Things to Do in Boscastle: Activities and Tours

Boscastle offers many exciting activities and tours, such as:

  • Boscastle Fishing Trips, offering a unique experience of fishing with local fishermen, and learning about the village’s fishing history and traditions.
  • Boscastle Kayaking Tours, offering a fantastic opportunity to explore the coastline and marine wildlife by kayak.
  • Boscastle Food Tour, offering a culinary journey of the village’s best food and drink places, and learning about its food culture and history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boscastle

A week-long itinerary for Boscastle could include:

  • Day 1: Valency Valley Walk and St. Nectan’s Glen
  • Day 2: The Museum of Witchcraft and Magic and Boscastle National Trust Shop
  • Day 3: Tintagel Castle Walk
  • Day 4: Camel Trail Biking Trip, from Wadebridge to Padstow
  • Day 5: Boscastle Fishing Trip
  • Day 6: South West Coast Path Walk
  • Day 7: Boscastle Food Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Boscastle

Boscastle offers a delicious and authentic taste of Cornish cuisine, with its fresh seafood, local ingredients, and award-winning restaurants and pubs.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornish Pasty, one of the most iconic dishes of Cornwall, consisting of pastry filled with meat, vegetables, and potatoes.
  • Fish and Chips, a classic British dish, with fresh fish, crispy batter, and salt and vinegar.
  • Cornish Cream Tea, a traditional afternoon tea consisting of scones, clotted cream, and jam, served with tea or coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Cornish Cider, a delicious and refreshing drink, made with locally grown apples.
  • Cornish Ale, a high-quality beer, brewed in small batches in local microbreweries.
  • Cornish Elixir, a traditional herbal drink, made with local honey and mixed with spirits.

Where to Stay in Boscastle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boscastle offers various accommodation options, catering to different budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the most recommended places to stay in Boscastle are:

  • The Wellington Hotel, a historic coaching inn, offering comfortable rooms, excellent food, and beautiful views of the harbor.
  • Bridge House Bed and Breakfast, a cozy and welcoming guesthouse, located in the heart of Boscastle, and offering excellent service and value.
  • YHA Boscastle, a budget-friendly hostel, offering affordable and comfortable accommodation for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Boscastle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Boscastle offers a unique and delightful shopping experience, with its craft shops, gift shops, and local markets, selling various souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Cornish fudge, a sweet and creamy confection, made with local cream and butter.
  • Cornish pottery and ceramics, featuring traditional designs and local craftsmanship.
  • Cornish jewelry and accessories, made with local materials, such as tin and sea glass.

Local Culture and Customs of Boscastle

Boscastle has a fascinating cultural heritage, shaped by its history, geography, and people. Some of the most distinctive cultural customs and traditions of Boscastle are:

  • Fishing culture, celebrating the village’s fishing history and traditions, and promoting sustainable fishing practices.
  • Folklore and superstitions, reflected in the Museum of Witchcraft and Magic, and various local legends and myths.
  • Arts and crafts, featuring local artisans and craftsmen, and showcasing their skills and creativity.

Best Time to Visit Boscastle

Boscastle is a year-round destination, offering different attractions and experiences in each season. However, the best time to visit Boscastle is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the village is at its liveliest. The peak season is from July to August, when the village is busiest, and prices are highest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boscastle

Do:

  • Respect the village’s natural environment, wildlife, and cultural heritage.
  • Try local food and drink, and support local businesses.
  • Explore the village on foot or by bike, and enjoy its natural beauty and charm.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the village’s natural environment or cultural landmarks.
  • Disturb wildlife or disturb the local community.
  • Park in restricted areas or on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boscastle

Boscastle is generally a safe and friendly village, with a low crime rate and a helpful community. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards, such as:

  • Rough terrain and steep slopes, especially near the coastal cliffs.
  • Strong currents and riptides, in some of the beaches and coves.
  • Adverse weather conditions, such as heavy rain or thunderstorms.

Health in Boscastle

Boscastle offers reliable and accessible healthcare services, such as GP surgeries and pharmacies. Visitors should also follow the latest health and safety guidelines regarding the COVID-19 pandemic, such as wearing masks and maintaining social distance.

Getting to Boscastle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Boscastle is easily accessible by different modes of transport, such as:

Airports Near Boscastle

  • Newquay Cornwall Airport, located 25 miles from Boscastle, offering flights to UK and European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Boscastle

  • The Western Greyhound bus service, offering connections to nearby towns and cities, such as Tintagel, Wadebridge, and Bude.

Other Options

  • Taxi services, offering reliable and affordable transport to and from Boscastle.
  • Car rental services, available from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Boscastle

Boscastle’s compact size and pedestrian-friendly streets make it easy to explore on foot or by bike. However, visitors can also use local taxis, buses, or car rental services, to travel to nearby attractions or villages.

Where to Go Next from Boscastle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boscastle is an excellent base for exploring other enchanting destinations and attractions in Cornwall, such as:

  • Tintagel Castle, a legendary medieval castle, located two miles from Boscastle
  • The Eden Project, a world-famous botanical garden and environmental education center, located 35 miles from Boscastle
  • St. Ives, a picturesque seaside town, known for its art galleries, beaches, and stunning views, located 45 miles from Boscastle.

Conclusion

Boscastle is a hidden gem of Cornwall, offering visitors a unique and authentic experience of Cornish culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, fishing, or just relaxing and enjoying the scenery, Boscastle has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Boscastle today, and discover the magic of Cornwall’s enchanting village.
