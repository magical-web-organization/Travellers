Introduction to Jurassic Coast

Located in the West Country of England, the Jurassic Coast is a UNESCO World Heritage site that stretches more than 95 miles from Exmouth to Studland Bay. The area offers visitors a unique glimpse into the earth’s history, with rocks dating back more than 185 million years and a diverse range of both cultural and natural attractions to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Jurassic Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Etches Collection Museum of Jurassic Marine Life - This museum houses the personal collection of Steve Etches, a local fisherman who discovered a large number of fossils along the coast. Visitors can see over 2,000 fossils on display, including Ichthyosaurs, Plesiosaurs, and Dinosaurs.
  2. Lulworth Castle and Park - Originally built in the 17th century as a hunting lodge, Lulworth Castle is now a fascinating museum that explores the history of the castle and the surrounding area.
  3. Buckland Tout Saints - A historical manor house that dates back to the 12th century, Buckland Tout Saints now serves as a luxury hotel, offering visitors a glimpse into the area’s rich cultural history.

Natural Attractions of Jurassic Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Durdle Door - A natural limestone arch that has been carved by the sea, Durdle Door is one of the most beautiful and iconic features of the Jurassic Coast. The area offers stunning views and is popular with hikers and beach-goers.
  2. Golden Cap - The highest point on the Jurassic Coast, Golden Cap offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area. Hiking to the top is a must-do activity for visitors.
  3. Chesil Beach - A pebble beach that is over 18 miles long, Chesil Beach offers visitors the chance to see unique geology and stunning views of the English Channel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jurassic Coast

  1. South West Coast Path - The South West Coast Path is a National Trail that follows the coastline for over 600 miles, including the entire length of the Jurassic Coast. The route is suitable for experienced hikers and offers stunning views of the coastline and surrounding areas.
  2. Jurassic Coast Cycling Route - This route is perfect for cyclists looking to explore the Jurassic Coast at a more leisurely pace. The route takes visitors through picturesque coastal villages and past some of the area’s most iconic natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Jurassic Coast: Activities and Tours

  1. Jurassic Coast Boat Tours - Visitors can explore the coastline of the Jurassic Coast by boat, offering a unique perspective and the chance to see the area’s natural beauty from the water.
  2. Rockpooling - Visitors of all ages will enjoy exploring the rock pools along the coast, discovering the fascinating sea life that inhabits the area.
  3. Fossil Hunting - The Jurassic Coast is one of the best places in the world for fossil hunting. Visitors should be sure to check the tide times and seek advice before heading out.

Food and Drink Scene of Jurassic Coast

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Dorset Apple Cake - A traditional dessert that features sweet apples and a crumbly, buttery crust.
  2. West Country Cheddar - A creamy and delicious cheese that is famous in the region, visitors should be sure to try it during their stay.
  3. Fish and Chips - A classic British dish that should be enjoyed with stunning views of the coast.

Local Beverages

  1. Real Ale - Visitors can enjoy locally brewed real ale at one of the many pubs in the area.
  2. Dorset Cider - Made from locally grown apples, Dorset cider is a refreshing and popular drink in the region.
  3. Tregothnan Tea - The only tea grown in England, Tregothnan Tea is cultivated in Cornwall and offers a unique and delicious taste.

Where to Stay in Jurassic Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Lulworth Cove Inn - Located in a beautiful bay, this Inn offers visitors comfort and stunning sea views.
  2. Swanage Auberge - A cozy and affordable hostel located in the charming seaside town of Swanage.
  3. Mortons House Hotel - A historical hotel that dates back to the 16th century, Mortons House Hotel offers luxury accommodation and a unique cultural experience.

What to Buy in Jurassic Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Fossils - With a rich history of fossils, visitors can purchase unique and ancient souvenirs to bring back home.
  2. Ceramics - The area is famous for its ceramics, featuring unique designs inspired by the rich history and natural beauty of the region.
  3. Local Ale - Visitors can bring home locally brewed ale, featuring unique flavors and styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Jurassic Coast

Visitors to the Jurassic Coast will find a warm and welcoming culture, with locals proud of their history and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting cultural attractions and museums.

Best Time to Visit Jurassic Coast

The best time to visit the Jurassic Coast is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the sun is shining. However, visitors can also enjoy the area during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall, when the crowds are smaller and the scenery is just as beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jurassic Coast

Do:

  1. Follow basic trail etiquette and stay on designated paths.
  2. Wear sturdy and comfortable shoes for hiking or biking.
  3. Consult with tide times before setting out on any beach activity.

Don’t:

  1. Take fossils or rocks from the area.
  2. Litter or leave any waste on trails or beaches.
  3. Disturb any wildlife or sea life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jurassic Coast

Visitors should be aware of the tides when exploring the beaches and coastal areas. Visitors should also take necessary safety precautions during outdoor activities, such as hiking or biking.

Health in Jurassic Coast

Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with being near the sea, such as hypothermia and dehydration. Visitors should also take precautions against sunburn during the summer months.

Getting to Jurassic Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jurassic Coast

  1. Exeter International Airport - The closest airport to the Jurassic Coast, located approximately 25 miles from Exmouth.
  2. Bournemouth Airport - Located approximately 60 miles from the Jurassic Coast.

Bus Stations Near Jurassic Coast

  1. Weymouth Bus Station - Serves as a transport hub for the area, connecting visitors to various destinations along the coast.
  2. Bridport Bus Station - Located in the charming town of Bridport, this station offers visitors easy access to the surrounding area.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the Jurassic Coast by car or train.

How to Get Around in Jurassic Coast

Visitors can explore the area by foot, bike, or car. Public transportation options are also available, including buses and trains that connect visitors to various destinations along the coast.

Where to Go Next from Jurassic Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Exeter - A charming city that offers visitors a variety of cultural attractions, historic sites, and shops.
  2. Isle of Purbeck - An idyllic area that features stunning natural beauty and charming coastal towns.
  3. Cornwall - Located just a short distance from the Jurassic Coast, Cornwall is a picturesque region that offers visitors a variety of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

The Jurassic Coast is an area of stunning natural beauty and rich cultural history. Visitors can explore the area’s unique geology, discover ancient fossils, and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities and cultural attractions. Visitors can also enjoy local cuisine, bring home unique souvenirs, and experience the warm and welcoming culture of the region.
