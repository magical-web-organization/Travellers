A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Woodbury, Devon, UK

Woodbury is a quaint village nestled in the heart of Devon, United Kingdom. With its charming blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty, it’s an idyllic destination for tourists seeking a break from bustling cities and mundane routine. The village is home to a number of attractions, making it a must-visit place for those visiting the West Country and the UK. This comprehensive travel guide gives you everything you need to know to plan your trip to Woodbury.

Introduction to Woodbury

Woodbury is a small village located in the county of Devon, in the South-West region of the United Kingdom. It falls within the district of East Devon and is situated approximately 10 miles southeast of the city of Exeter. The village is surrounded by breathtaking greenery, picturesque views, and peaceful countryside. With a population of around 1,500, Woodbury is a tight-knit community that takes pride in its local heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Woodbury

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a casual traveler seeking a peaceful getaway, Woodbury has something to offer everyone. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • A La Ronde: This stunning 18th-century house is now a National Trust property, located within easy reach of the village. The house is famous for its shell-encrusted rooms and beautiful gardens.
  • St Swithun’s Church: Located in the center of the village, this beautiful church has been a place of worship for over 800 years and is home to a number of interesting historical artifacts.
  • Woodbury Castle: Dating back to the Iron Age, Woodbury Castle is an ancient hill fort and a popular destination for hikers and history enthusiasts alike.

Natural Attractions of Woodbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • East Devon Way: This long-distance hiking trail traverses through Woodbury and affords breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Clinton Devon Estates: This vast estate includes ancient woodland, rare wildlife, and picturesque views of the Otter Valley.
  • Clyst Valley Trail: This scenic trail passes through some of the most beautiful woodland and meadows in Devon and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Woodbury

Woodbury’s natural beauty and rich history make it the ideal place for hikers and bikers. Here are some hiking and biking routes to add to your itinerary:

  • Woodbury Castle Trail: This 2-mile circular trail offers stunning views of the village and the surrounding countryside, as well as historic points of interest.
  • East Devon Way: This 40-mile trail starts in Exmouth and ends in Lyme Regis, passing through some of the most beautiful countryside in Devon and offering spectacular views.
  • Clyst Valley Trail: This 7.5-mile trail meanders through the natural beauty of the Clyst Valley.

Best Things to Do in Woodbury: Activities and Tours

Woodbury offers a wide range of activities for tourists of all ages. Here are some must-try things to do during your visit:

  • Take a Walk Through History: Woodbury is home to numerous historical buildings and landmarks. Explore the village’s rich past by taking a walking tour through its historic streets.
  • Picnic in the Park: Spend an afternoon in the picturesque village park, enjoying a picnic with friends or family.
  • Visit a Local Farm: Get up close and personal with Devonshire’s farming community by visiting one of Woodbury’s local farms.

Week-Long Itinerary for Woodbury

If you’re planning to spend a week in Woodbury, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Disembark in Exeter and explore the city’s history and architecture.
  • Day 2: Visit the nearby town of Budleigh Salterton and enjoy its beaches and charming local shops.
  • Day 3: Spend the morning exploring Woodbury Castle and the surrounding countryside, before returning to the village to explore St Swithun’s Church and A La Ronde.
  • Day 4: Take a leisurely bike ride or hike through the Clyst Valley Trail.
  • Day 5: Explore the Clinton Devon Estates, home to over 4,000 acres of natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Day 6: Visit nearby Topsham, a quaint port town with a rich history.
  • Day 7: Spend a relaxing day in Woodbury Village Park, enjoying a picnic and the local scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Woodbury

From traditional Devonshire delicacies to modern foodie favorites, Woodbury’s food and drink scene has something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devon Cream Tea: No visit to Devon is complete without indulging in a traditional cream tea.
  • Fish and Chips: Coastal towns like Budleigh Salterton are famous for their fish and chips, so be sure to grab a bite while you’re in the area.
  • Devonshire Pasty: A savory pastry filled with meat and vegetables, the Devonshire Pasty is a local favorite and a must-try dish.

Local Beverages

  • Devonshire Cider: Made from locally grown apples, Devonshire cider is a refreshing and tangy drink perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Otter Brewery Ales: Since 1990, Otter Brewery has been producing award-winning ales in the heart of Devon. Be sure to sample their brews during your stay.

Where to Stay in Woodbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re on a tight budget or seeking luxury accommodations, Woodbury has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to consider:

  • The Maltsters Arms: This charming 16th-century inn offers cozy rooms and locally sourced food and drink.
  • Eastdon House: This historic country house boasts beautifully decorated rooms and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • The White Hart: Built in the 17th century, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and traditional British cuisine.

What to Buy in Woodbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No visit to Woodbury is complete without taking home a piece of its charm. Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider buying:

  • Devonshire Clotted Cream: This rich, creamy substance is a staple of Devonshire cuisine and makes a delicious souvenir.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Browse local jewelry stores for unique pieces made by talented craftspeople.
  • Local Artwork: Support the local art scene by purchasing paintings or pottery from a local artist.

Local Culture and Customs of Woodbury

Woodbury’s tight-knit community values hospitality, simplicity, and a love for nature. While visitors are always welcome, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Woodbury

The best time to visit Woodbury is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re seeking a peaceful retreat, consider visiting during the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Woodbury

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit:

Do:

  • Practice responsible tourism: Respect the environment and treat the local community with kindness and consideration.
  • Explore the outdoors: Take advantage of Woodbury’s natural beauty by hiking or biking through the countryside.
  • Support local businesses: Shop for souvenirs and dine at locally owned restaurants to support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help preserve the natural beauty of the village by properly disposing of all waste.
  • Disrespect local customs: Be mindful of local traditions and customs when interacting with locals.
  • Be rude to wildlife: Respect the local wildlife and keep your distance for your safety and theirs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Woodbury

Woodbury is a safe destination for visitors, and the local community is welcoming and hospitable. However, it’s always recommended to practice basic safety precautions, like securing your valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Woodbury

While the village has a small clinic, visitors are encouraged to ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage. Remember to bring any necessary medication, and be sure to take necessary precautions while outdoors to avoid any injuries.

Getting to Woodbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Woodbury is located approximately 10 miles southeast of Exeter, making it easily accessible from most cities in the UK. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Woodbury

  • Exeter International Airport: This airport is only 5 miles away from Woodbury and offers regular domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Woodbury

  • Exeter Bus Station: From here, visitors can take a local bus to Woodbury, which takes approximately 25 minutes.

Other Options

  • Train: Exeter has a major train station that connects to most major cities in the UK.
  • Car: Woodbury is easily accessible by car and has parking options for visitors.

How to Get Around in Woodbury

The village is small and walkable, making it easy to explore on foot. Alternatively, visitors can also rent a bike to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Woodbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations to visit:

  • Exmouth: This coastal town is only a short drive from Woodbury and is home to sandy beaches and watersports opportunities.
  • Dartmoor National Park: This vast national park is perfect for hiking and camping and offers breathtaking views of the countryside.
  • Sidmouth: Known for its pastel-colored buildings and charming pebble beach, this quaint town is a popular tourist destination.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or a peaceful retreat, Woodbury has something to offer everyone. With its rich history, natural beauty, and kind locals, it’s a must-visit destination for those exploring Devon and the UK. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to make the most of your visit to this hidden gem.
