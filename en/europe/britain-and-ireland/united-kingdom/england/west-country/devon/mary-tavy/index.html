Introduction to Mary Tavy

Mary Tavy is a picturesque village located in Devon, in the South West of England. It is situated within Dartmoor National Park and is surrounded by rolling hills and stunning natural beauty. The village is steeped in history and heritage, with many cultural attractions and outdoor activities to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Mary Tavy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St Mary’s Church: This stunning 13th-century church is a true masterpiece of architecture. It boasts a rich history and is home to a number of interesting artifacts and works of art.
  • Mary Tavy Old School House: This historic building used to be a school for local children but has since been converted into a museum. It houses a collection of artifacts and photographs that showcase the rich and varied history of Mary Tavy.
  • Tavistock Museum: Located just a short drive from Mary Tavy, this museum is dedicated to the history of Tavistock and the surrounding area. It features exhibits on everything from local industry to the area’s artistic heritage.

Natural Attractions of Mary Tavy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rattlebrook Peatlands Nature Reserve: This stunning nature reserve is home to a variety of rare wildlife and plant species. Visitors can explore the peatlands on foot and take in the breathtaking views of the moors.
  • Brent Tor: Located just a short drive from Mary Tavy, Brent Tor is a unique and striking landmark. The tor is home to a small church and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Lydford Gorge: This beautiful gorge is located just a short drive from Mary Tavy and is home to a stunning waterfall and a variety of trails for visitors to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mary Tavy

Mary Tavy and the surrounding area offers a wealth of hiking and biking routes. Here are just a few of the most popular:

  • The Granite Way: This 11-mile cycling and walking route passes through some of the most stunning countryside in the area. It takes visitors through the heart of Dartmoor National Park and offers breathtaking views of the moors.
  • The Tamar Valley Discovery Trail: This beautiful trail runs alongside the River Tamar and takes visitors through a variety of picturesque villages and historic sites.
  • The Mary Tavy Walk: This circular walk takes visitors through the heart of the village and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Mary Tavy: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding: There are a number of local stables that offer horse riding lessons and guided rides through the local countryside.
  • Fishing: Mary Tavy is home to a number of excellent fishing spots, including the River Tamar and various local lakes.
  • Guided Tours: There are a number of local tour operators that offer guided tours of the area, including visits to historic sites, scenic views, and local wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mary Tavy

Day 1: Explore the village of Mary Tavy, including a visit to St Mary’s Church and the Mary Tavy Old School House Museum. Day 2: Spend the day exploring the rugged beauty of Dartmoor National Park, including a hike along The Granite Way. Day 3: Visit the historic town of Tavistock and explore the Tavistock Museum. Day 4: Take a guided tour of the local countryside, including a visit to the Lydford Gorge. Day 5: Go horse riding or fishing in the local area. Day 6: Take a scenic drive out to Brent Tor and explore this unique landmark. Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of the local pubs or cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Mary Tavy

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devon Cream Tea: A classic West Country dish, Devon Cream Tea consists of scones, clotted cream, and jam. There are a number of excellent cafes and restaurants in Mary Tavy that serve this delicious dish.
  • Cornish Pasty: A popular savory dish, the Cornish Pasty is a pastry parcel filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Fish and Chips: A classic British dish, fish and chips are a must-try when visiting Mary Tavy.

Local Beverages

  • Devon Cider: Made using traditional methods, Devon Cider is a delicious and refreshing drink that is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • West Country Ale: There are a number of local breweries in the area that produce excellent West Country Ale, a rich and full-bodied beer that is perfect for a chilly evening.

Best Time to Visit Mary Tavy

The best time to visit Mary Tavy is during the summer months, from June to September. However, visitors should be prepared for crowds during this time, especially during the school holidays.
