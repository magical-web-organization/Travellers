Introduction to Clovelly

Clovelly is a picturesque village located on the North Devon coast of West Country, England. It is famous for its steep cobbled streets, stunning coastal views, and traditional fishing heritage. The village is accessed via a single steep road, which leads visitors to the harbour and the heart of the village. The village is unique as it has no vehicular traffic, and visitors must walk down the cobbled streets to fully experience this charming village.

List of Places to Visit in Clovelly

Clovelly has several attractions that are sure to interest any visitor. Here are some places to visit while in the village:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clovelly Visitor Centre and Museum: This museum houses exhibits that offer visitors an insight into the history of Clovelly and the life of its people.
  • Kingsley Museum: This museum celebrates writer Charles Kingsley, who was a former resident of the village. It features exhibits about his life and works
  • St. Peter’s Parish Church: This church dates back to the 14th century and contains stunning stained glass windows and brasses.
  • Clovelly Court Gardens: A picturesque garden that is worth visiting especially during the spring and summer months.

Natural Attractions of Clovelly: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South West Coast Path: This hiking trail offers breathtaking views of the coastline. Several sections of the trail start and end in Clovelly.
  • Hartland Peninsula: A short drive from Clovelly, this natural beauty spot features stunning cliffs, rocky coves, and hidden beaches.
  • Clovelly Beach: This is a secluded beach accessible via a steep path down the cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clovelly

Clovelly has a network of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the surrounding landscapes. The South West Coast Path is one of the most popular trails and offers panoramic views of the coastline. The Tarka Trail is a biking route that spans the North Devon countryside and is perfect for cyclists of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Clovelly: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Trips: There are several boat trips that take visitors around the Clovelly coastline. These offer a unique perspective of the village and the sea.
  • Fishing: Clovelly has a rich fishing tradition, and visitors can experience this firsthand by going on a fishing trip.
  • Clovelly Village Walks: These guided walks take visitors around the village, providing an insight into Clovelly’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clovelly

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Clovelly Visitor Centre and Museum followed by a leisurely walk around the village. End the day with dinner at The Red Lion Inn.
Day 2 Take a boat trip along the coast or visit one of the local beaches for a day of relaxation.
Day 3 Go hiking or biking along the South West Coast Path or Tarka Trail.
Day 4 Visit the Kingsley Museum, followed by lunch at one of the local cafes. End the day with drinks at The New Inn.
Day 5 Take a day trip to Hartland Peninsula and discover its hidden beaches and coves.

Food and Drink Scene of Clovelly

Clovelly has a range of options for food and drink that celebrate its fishing heritage and local produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clovelly Herring: A local delicacy featuring freshly caught herring that is smoked and served with potatoes and butter.
  • Cream Tea: A traditional English tea featuring scones, clotted cream, and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Devon Cider: A local alcoholic drink made from Devon apples.
  • Lundy Ale: A locally brewed beer served throughout the region.

Where to Stay in Clovelly: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clovelly has a range of options for accommodation, from hotels to guesthouses and self-catering apartments. Here are some top recommendations:

  • The Red Lion Inn: A traditional 13th-century inn that offers rooms with sea views and a restaurant serving local food.
  • The New Inn: A comfortable country inn located in the heart of Clovelly.
  • Trehaven Manor Hotel: A charming, family-run hotel located in the nearby town of Hartland.

What to Buy in Clovelly: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Clovelly has several shops that sell local souvenirs and products, including handmade chocolates, local crafts, and gifts. The village is also known for its herring, which can be purchased from the local shops and enjoyed back at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Clovelly

Clovelly has a rich fishing heritage that is celebrated throughout the village. The locals are very proud of their history and culture, and visitors can experience this firsthand by attending one of the local events, such as the Clovelly Lobster and Crab Feast.

Best Time to Visit Clovelly

The best time to visit Clovelly is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm with less rainfall. The village is also popular during the Christmas season when it is adorned with lights and decorations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clovelly

Do:

  • Respect the village’s no-vehicle policy and walk down the steep cobbled streets.
  • Visit the local shops and try some of the fresh local produce.
  • Attend one of the local events to fully immerse yourself in the culture.

Don’t:

  • Damage or harm the local wildlife and vegetation.
  • Litter or damage any of the village’s historic buildings or landmarks.
  • Swim in the sea without checking for safety warnings and guidance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clovelly

Clovelly is a relatively safe village with low crime rates. However, visitors should take care when walking down the steep cobbled streets, especially when the weather conditions are wet or icy.

Health in Clovelly

Clovelly has a local GP surgery located in the nearby town of Hartland. There are also several pharmacy options in nearby towns.

Getting to Clovelly: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clovelly is located in the North Devon countryside, and it is not easily accessible by public transport. However, here are some options for getting to the village:

Airports Near Clovelly

  • Exeter Airport: Located around 60 miles from Clovelly.

Bus Stations Near Clovelly

  • Barnstaple Bus Station: Located around 20 miles from Clovelly.

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can reach Clovelly via the M5 motorway and the A39 road.
  • Train: The nearest train stations are located in Barnstaple and Exeter.

How to Get Around in Clovelly

Clovelly is compact and easily walkable due to its no-vehicle policy. Visitors can walk around the village and the surrounding areas. Bikes can also become an option for exploring the nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Clovelly: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clovelly is located in a beautiful region of North Devon surrounded by stunning coastal and countryside landscapes. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting:

  • Lynton and Lynmouth: A seaside town located around 20 miles from Clovelly that features a scenic train ride between the two towns.
  • Appledore: A coastal village located around 20 miles from Clovelly and can be reached via ferry. It features charming narrow streets and colourful houses.
  • The Exmoor National Park: A beautiful national park located around 30 miles from Clovelly that offers visitors the chance to explore stunning landscapes and wildlife.

Conclusion

Clovelly is a charming village that is steeped in history and culture. Its no-vehicle policy, stunning scenery, and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for visitors who want to experience an authentic English village. Whether you want to explore the natural landscapes, sample the local food and drink, or learn about the village’s history and heritage, Clovelly has something for everyone.
