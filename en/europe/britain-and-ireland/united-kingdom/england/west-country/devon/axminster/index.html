Introduction to Axminster

Nestled in the heart of Devon, Axminster is a quaint market town known for its rolling hills, picturesque countryside, and a rich history that dates back to the Roman era. Located just a few miles inland from the scenic Jurassic Coast in southwest England, Axminster is surrounded by stunning natural beauty and is a popular destination for nature lovers and history enthusiasts alike.

With its charming stone houses and narrow streets lined with boutique shops and traditional pubs, Axminster exudes a quintessentially British feel that will make visitors feel right at home.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through Axminster’s rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, food scene, and more. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or a relaxing break, this guide has everything you need to plan your perfect trip to Axminster.

List of Places to Visit in Axminster

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Axminster Heritage Centre: Discover the fascinating history of Axminster and its people through interactive displays and exhibitions at the Axminster Heritage Centre.
  • The Thomas Whitty House Museum: Experience life in 18th-century Axminster and learn about the town’s famous carpet-making industry at the Thomas Whitty House Museum.
  • The Minster Church of St. Mary: Admire the stunning architecture and intricate stained-glass windows of the Minster Church of St. Mary, which dates back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Axminster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The East Devon Way: Hike along this scenic walking trail that spans 40 miles between Exmouth and Lyme Regis, passing through the rolling hills and picturesque countryside around Axminster.
  • The Axe Estuary: Take a leisurely stroll along the banks of the River Axe, where you can spot a variety of wildlife, including swans, kingfishers, and otters.
  • Lyme Regis Beach: Head down to the nearby coastal town of Lyme Regis and spend a day lounging on the sandy beach and soaking up the sunshine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Axminster

Whether you’re an avid hiker or a casual cyclist, Axminster and the surrounding area offer a range of outdoor activities to suit every taste and skill level. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try:

  • The Golden Cap Walk: This challenging hike takes you up to the highest point on the Jurassic Coast, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the coastline and surrounding countryside.
  • The East Devon Cycle Route: Follow this scenic cycle trail that runs along the River Otter, taking in stunning views of the countryside and passing through quaint villages along the way.
  • The Colyton to Seaton Tramway Walk: Take a leisurely stroll along the picturesque Axe Valley, where you can spot a range of wildlife and enjoy the sounds of the river and birdsong.

Best Things to Do in Axminster: Activities and Tours

  • The River Cottage Experience: Visit the famous River Cottage and discover the secrets of sustainable living and organic farming with a range of culinary courses and experiential workshops.
  • The Axe Valley Wildlife Park: Get up close and personal with exotic animals, including tigers, monkeys, and meerkats, at the Axe Valley Wildlife Park.
  • The Devon Railway Centre: Explore the history of railways at this family-friendly attraction, with model trains, interactive exhibits, and train rides around the beautiful Devon countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Axminster

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Axminster itself, including a visit to the Axminster Heritage Centre and the Thomas Whitty House Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a hike along the East Devon Way, taking in the stunning views and enjoying a picnic lunch in the countryside.
  • Day 3: Spend a day lounging on the sandy beach at nearby Lyme Regis, taking a dip in the sea and enjoying fish and chips from a local chippy.
  • Day 4: Discover the fascinating natural history of the area at the Charmouth Heritage Coast Centre, including guided fossil walks and interactive exhibits.
  • Day 5: Take a scenic cycle ride along the River Otter, stopping off at local villages and shops along the way.
  • Day 6: Visit the nearby historic town of Honiton, famous for its antique shops and lace-making heritage.
  • Day 7: Take a cookery course at River Cottage and learn to cook up a storm with fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Food and Drink Scene of Axminster

Must-Try Dishes

Axminster is known for its delicious local cuisine, with a range of traditional dishes to try. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Devonshire Cream Tea: Enjoy a quintessential English afternoon tea, including scones, clotted cream, and jam, at one of Axminster’s charming tea rooms.
  • Fresh Seafood: Sample the freshest seafood straight from the coast, including crab, lobster, and mackerel.
  • Traditional Devonshire Pasties: Try a savory pastry filled with meat and vegetables, a staple of the West Country cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Scrumpy: Sip on a glass of locally brewed Scrumpy cider, made with fresh apples from the surrounding orchards.
  • Devonshire Tea: Sample a cup of refreshing Devonshire tea, made with locally grown tea leaves and fresh milk.
  • Beer: Try a pint of locally brewed ale, such as Otter Ale, or sample a range of craft beers from Axminster’s many microbreweries.

Where to Stay in Axminster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy B&B or a luxury hotel, Axminster has a range of options to suit every taste and budget. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • The George Hotel: This historic hotel dates back to the 16th century and offers a range of luxurious rooms and suites, as well as a popular restaurant and bar.
  • The Tytherleigh Arms: This charming pub and B&B offers comfortable rooms, traditional pub grub, and a warm welcome from the friendly staff.
  • The Old Inn: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Axminster and offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices, as well as daily breakfast and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Axminster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Axminster Carpets: Take home a piece of Axminster’s iconic carpet-making heritage with a high-quality carpet or rug from one of the local factories.
  • Local Produce: Sample the delicious local produce from the nearby farms and markets, including cider, cheese, and chutney.
  • Antiques and Crafts: Browse the many antique shops and galleries in Axminster and Honiton, where you can find unique souvenirs and handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Axminster

Axminster has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to Roman times, with centuries of history and traditions passed down through the generations. Some of the local customs and traditions to look out for include:

  • Axminster Carnival: Join in the fun at the annual Axminster Carnival, which takes place in September and features a range of floats, dancers, and live entertainment.
  • Traditional Country Fairs: Visit one of the many country fairs and agricultural shows that take place throughout the year, including the Honiton Agricultural Show and the Axe Vale Festival.
  • Local Festivals: Check out the local festivals and events, such as the Colyton Goose Fayre and the Beer Rhythm and Blues Festival.

Best Time to Visit Axminster

The best time to visit Axminster is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny and the countryside is in full bloom. However, Axminster is a year-round destination, with plenty to do and see throughout the year, including festive markets and winter walks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Axminster

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and sample the delicious fresh seafood and locally produced cider and beer.
  • Do take the time to explore the stunning natural beauty of the area, including the nearby Jurassic Coast and East Devon Way.
  • Do visit the range of local museums and historical attractions, where you can learn about the rich cultural heritage of Axminster.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the local festivals and events, which showcase the vibrant culture and traditions of the area.
  • Don’t forget to pack sturdy walking shoes and weatherproof clothing, as the countryside can be muddy and unpredictable.
  • Don’t miss the chance to support local businesses and purchase unique souvenirs and gifts to take home.

Safety and Health

Safety in Axminster

Axminster is a safe and friendly town, with low levels of crime and a welcoming community. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking in isolated areas alone at night.

Health in Axminster

Axminster has a range of medical facilities and pharmacies, as well as 24-hour emergency services. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and any necessary vaccinations before visiting, particularly during the current COVID-19 pandemic.

Getting to Axminster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Axminster is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Axminster

  • Exeter International Airport: Located approximately 20 miles from Axminster, with direct flights to and from destinations across the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Axminster

  • Axminster Bus Station: Located in the center of Axminster, with regular bus services to destinations across Devon and the West Country.

Other Options

  • Train: Axminster has a train station on the main line between Exeter and London, with regular services operated by South Western Railway.
  • Car: Axminster is easily accessible by car, with good road links to the A35 and M5 motorway.

How to Get Around in Axminster

Axminster is a small town that is easily explored on foot, with most attractions within walking distance of the town center. However, there are also a range of transportation options available, including local bus services and taxi companies.

Where to Go Next from Axminster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Axminster is surrounded by a range of beautiful destinations and attractions, including:

  • Lyme Regis: This charming seaside town is located just a few miles from Axminster and is famous for its sandy beaches and fossil hunting opportunities.
  • Sidmouth: Visit the picturesque coastal town of Sidmouth, with its pretty gardens, traditional shops, and stunning sea views.
  • Exeter: Explore the historic city of Exeter, known for its ancient architecture, lively cultural scene, and range of shopping and dining options.

Conclusion

Axminster is a charming town located in the heart of Devon, with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant culture that is waiting to be explored.
