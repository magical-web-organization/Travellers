Introduction to Okehampton

Nestled in the heart of Devon, Okehampton is a quaint market town surrounded by rolling hills and stunning countryside. With a rich history dating back to Norman times, Okehampton boasts a variety of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to please visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, scenic views, or a taste of local culture, Okehampton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Okehampton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Okehampton Castle is a must-visit destination for history buffs, with its well-preserved ruins dating back to the 14th century. The castle is open year-round and offers guided tours, events, and exhibitions. Another important historical attraction in Okehampton is the Museum of Dartmoor Life, which showcases the region’s rich cultural and natural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Okehampton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Okehampton is a paradise. Dartmoor National Park and the breathtaking granite tors are located just a few miles from town. Visitors can enjoy hiking, mountain biking, horseback riding, and even wild swimming in the rivers and ponds. Other notable natural attractions in Okehampton include Meldon Reservoir and Okehampton Marshes Local Nature Reserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okehampton

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will love the trails around Okehampton. The Granite Way is a 11-mile long walking and cycling trail that stretches from Okehampton to Lydford. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and passes over the impressive Meldon Viaduct. Another great option is the Tarka Trail, which runs for almost 180 miles through North Devon’s picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in Okehampton: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Okehampton, catering to families, couples, and solo travelers. Some of the best things to do in town include visiting the Okehampton Farmers Market, taking a guided tour of the castle, exploring the Museum of Dartmoor Life, horse riding, and sailing at Roadford Lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Okehampton

Day 1: Explore Okehampton Castle and Museum of Dartmoor Life. Day 2: Hike the Granite Way and enjoy a picnic at Meldon Reservoir. Day 3: Mountain bike in Dartmoor National Park. Day 4: Take a leisurely stroll through Okehampton Marshes Local Nature Reserve. Day 5: Visit Roadford Lake and try your hand at sailing. Day 6: Discover the Tarka Trail on horseback. Day 7: Explore the charming market town of Tavistock.

Food and Drink Scene of Okehampton

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Okehampton would be complete without sampling some of the town’s local dishes. Devon cream tea, served with clotted cream and strawberry jam, is a must-try for those with a sweet tooth. Other popular dishes include Devonshire fudge, freshly baked scones, and traditional West Country cider.

Local Beverages

Devon is famous for its ciders, and you can try some of the best varieties in Okehampton. The town also offers a selection of locally brewed ales and wines, which can be enjoyed in the many cozy pubs and bars around town.

Where to Stay in Okehampton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Okehampton, ranging from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels. Some of the most popular places to stay in town include the Okehampton Castle Youth Hostel, East Hook Cottage, and the Betty Cottles Inn.

What to Buy in Okehampton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can find a variety of unique gifts and souvenirs in Okehampton. Local products such as handmade candles, pottery, and jewelry are popular choices. You can also bring home some West Country cider or Devonshire fudge for your family and friends to enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Okehampton

Okehampton has a strong history and culture that is celebrated throughout the town. Visitors can enjoy traditional Devonshire music, dance, and food at local festivals and events. The town also has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans showcasing their works.

Best Time to Visit Okehampton

Summer is the most popular time to visit Okehampton, with warm temperatures and long days perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, the town is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy fewer crowds and lower prices during the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okehampton

Do:

  • Explore the surrounding countryside and natural attractions
  • Sample local dishes and beverages
  • Visit Okehampton Castle and Museum of Dartmoor Life
  • Take a guided tour of the town
  • Join in local festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Ignore the signs and warnings in national parks
  • Overlook the small details and hidden gems of Okehampton

Safety and Health

Safety in Okehampton

Okehampton is a safe and friendly town, with one of the lowest crime rates in Devon. Visitors should still take the usual precautions and keep their belongings secure while exploring the town and surrounding areas.

Health in Okehampton

There are several medical facilities in Okehampton, including a local hospital, general practitioners, and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving in town.

Getting to Okehampton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okehampton

The closest international airports to Okehampton are located in Bristol and Exeter, both of which are around 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Okehampton

Okehampton has a bus station that connects the town to local and regional destinations. National Express also operates a daily service from the town to London.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Okehampton by car, with the town located just off the A30 and A386 highways. Rail connections are available from Exeter and Plymouth stations, with a local bus connection to Okehampton.

How to Get Around in Okehampton

Okehampton is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. For those who want to explore the surrounding areas, hiring a car or joining a guided tour is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Okehampton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those who want to explore more of Devon and the South West, the nearby towns of Tavistock, Bovey Tracey, and Totnes are excellent choices. The stunning beaches and coastal towns of North Devon and Cornwall are also within easy driving distance from Okehampton.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Okehampton is a must-visit destination in Devon. With its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, this beautiful market town is the perfect base for exploring the best that the West Country has to offer.
