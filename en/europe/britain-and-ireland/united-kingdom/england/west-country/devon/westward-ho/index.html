Introduction to Westward Ho!

Located in the breathtaking West Country of England, Westward Ho! is a charming seaside town that has captivated visitors for centuries. With its picturesque coastline, intriguing history and thrilling outdoor adventures, this destination is perfect for anyone seeking laidback days on the beach, cultural explorations, or invigorating hikes through the lush countryside. In our comprehensive Westward Ho! travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, exciting activities to try, top dining options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Westward Ho!

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Northam Burrows Visitor Centre and Country Park: This stunning park comprises of miles of golden sand dunes, rolling grassy marshland and farmland, and an array of wildlife.

  • The Cliffside Theatre Company: Make sure to catch a play or Panto at the Cliffside Theatre.

  • The Royal North Devon Golf Course: The Royal North Devon Golf Course is England’s oldest golf course, test your swing against the winds coming in from the Atlantic Ocean.

Natural Attractions of Westward Ho!: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Westward Ho! Beach: With its pristine stretch of sands and cool, clear waters, Westward Ho! Beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and surfing.

  • South West Coast Path: This stunning coastal path offers hikers and bikers unbeatable views of the rugged coastline and emerald green fields.

  • Lundy Island: This small island, located off the coast of Westward Ho!, offers a unique opportunity to explore dramatic cliffs and a wealth of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westward Ho!

Westward Ho! is home to a number of exceptional hiking and biking routes that will take you through some of the most spectacular scenery in England. Some of the top options include:

  • Tarka Trail: Follow the Tarka Trail along the disused railway line from Braunton to Meeth to take in the beautiful countryside along the River Taw.

  • Baggy Point: A great walk taking you from Croyde Bay to Baggy Point. Spectacular views of the Atlantic Ocean and coastline are the highlight of this walk.

Best Things to Do in Westward Ho!: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: With excellent surf breaks along Westward Ho! Beach, surfing is a popular activity among locals and visitors alike.

  • Fishing: Get out on the seas or cast a line on the banks of the Taw estuary for exciting fishing opportunities.

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the North Devon countryside on horseback and take in the sweeping views of the coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westward Ho!

Looking to spend a week exploring all that Westward Ho! has to offer? Here’s what your itinerary could look like:

  • Day 1-2: Spend your first days exploring the charming town center of Westward Ho! and the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 3-4: Explore the stunning beaches and coastal paths that make Westward Ho! so special.

  • Day 5-6: Try your hand at surfing, fishing, or horseback riding for some thrilling and unforgettable experiences.

  • Day 7: Spend a leisurely day savoring local dishes and drinks, and take in some of the cultural attractions that this destination is known for.

Food and Drink Scene of Westward Ho!

Must-Try Dishes

  • Westward Ho! Pie: This savory dish, featuring a mixture of potatoes, beef, and onions, is a hearty staple of the local food scene.

  • Cornish Pasty: A classic of British cuisine, Cornish pasties are large, savory meat pies that pack a flavorful punch.

  • Cream Tea: Scones with homemade clotted cream and jam — a classic Devonshire treat!

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: The Westward Ho! area is home to several award-winning breweries, producing some of the UK’s most delicious ales.

  • Devonshire Cider: A crisp, refreshing drink made from locally-sourced apples is a great accompaniment to any meal.

Where to Stay in Westward Ho!: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of excellent accommodation options in Westward Ho!, ranging from cozy bed-and-breakfasts to luxurious hotels. Some of our recommended options include:

  • The Waterside: Located on the edge of the Taw estuary, The Waterside offers stunning views and comfortable, well-appointed rooms.

  • The Royal Hotel: This historic hotel offers charming, individually-decorated rooms, a well-regarded restaurant, and a lively bar.

  • The Beach House: A modern group of aparthotel suites ideal for families and extended stay.

What to Buy in Westward Ho!: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local produce: From artisan cheese to homemade preserves, Westward Ho! is known for its delicious, locally sourced produce.

  • Crafts: Pop into some of the shops in town and pick up some locally-made ceramics, textiles, or other unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Westward Ho!

Westward Ho! retains much of its charming, traditional culture, with a strong emphasis on community events and festivals, such as the Westward Ho! ‘Shout,’ a traditional celebration involving a mass singing of ancient songs.

Best Time to Visit Westward Ho!

The summer months of June to August are an ideal time to visit Westward Ho!, with warm temperatures, sunny skies, and plenty of outdoor activities happening.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westward Ho!

Do:

  • Respect the coastline: The coast can be dangerous, so always follow safety instructions and take care when walking or swimming.

  • Try local produce: Stop off at a local farm shop or farmers’ market to sample the delicious range of local produce on offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the beaches and countryside clean by disposing of your rubbish properly.

  • Ignore safety warnings: The sea can be dangerous, so always heed any warnings or signs that indicate unsafe swimming conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westward Ho!

Westward Ho! is considered to be a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming local community. That being said, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Westward Ho!

Health facilities in Westward Ho! are of a high standard, with several clinics and pharmacies located within easy reach of the town center.

Getting to Westward Ho!: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westward Ho!

The nearest airports to Westward Ho! are Exeter Airport (EXE) and Bristol Airport (BRS), both of which offer regular domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Westward Ho!

Westward Ho! is well-served by local bus services, with frequent connections to nearby towns and cities such as Barnstaple and Bideford.

Other Options

Train services are available from Barnstaple or Tiverton Parkway to various cities, and there are two ferry options to Lundy Island from Ilfracombe and Bideford.

How to Get Around in Westward Ho!

The easiest way to get around Westward Ho! is by foot, with the town center and most popular attractions and beaches within easy walking distance. There is also a local bus service that connects Westward Ho! to nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Westward Ho!: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Barnstaple: This lively market town offers plenty of opportunities for shopping, dining, and exploring, just 11 miles away.

  • Bideford: This historic port town is located along a scenic estuary and offers a range of cultural attractions and shopping options.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or simply a chance to get away from it all, Westward Ho! is the perfect destination. With its breathtaking coastline, thriving culture and history, and wide range of activities and attractions, this charming seaside town is sure to impress even the most discerning of travelers.
