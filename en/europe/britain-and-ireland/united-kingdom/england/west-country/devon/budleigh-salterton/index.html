Introduction to Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton is a charming seaside town located in Devon, in the West Country region of England. Known for its beautiful beaches, scenic walks, and quaint local shops, it’s the perfect destination for a relaxing holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Budleigh Salterton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Budleigh Salterton has a rich cultural history that is visible in its many museums and historical sites. The Fairlynch Museum is a great place to start, as it provides a comprehensive look into the town’s past. The Budleigh Salterton Heritage Centre and the St. Peter’s Church are also worth a visit, providing visitors with an informative display of local history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Budleigh Salterton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will not be disappointed by the range of attractions Budleigh Salterton has to offer. The South West Coast Path is a popular walk, providing beautiful views of the sea and the town. Ladram Bay Beach is a great place to relax and enjoy a day at the beach, while the Otter Estuary Nature Reserve offers visitors an opportunity to spot local wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton offers visitors a range of hiking and biking routes, from easy seaside strolls to more challenging hill hikes. The East Devon Way is a popular option, offering a 38-mile long trail that takes visitors through some of the town’s most beautiful countryside. The coastline also offers several stunning walks, including the 6-mile Otter Valley and the 16-mile Exe Estuary trail.

Best Things to Do in Budleigh Salterton: Activities and Tours

For those looking for activities and tours, Budleigh Salterton offers plenty of options. The World of Country Life is a popular attraction and provides visitors with an opportunity to get a taste of rural life in the West Country. The Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival is held annually in September and showcases authors from a range of literary genres. The Budleigh Salterton Riding School provides horse riding lessons for all ages and levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Budleigh Salterton

Day 1: Arrive in Budleigh Salterton and take a leisurely stroll along the beachfront. Grab a bite to eat at one of the town’s many cafes or fish and chip shops.

Day 2: Visit the Fairlynch Museum and learn about the town’s history. Take a scenic walk along the South West Coast Path and stop off for lunch at one of the local pubs or restaurants.

Day 3: Explore the local shops and markets, picking up some souvenirs and local produce. Take a guided tour of the Otter Estuary Nature Reserve.

Day 4: Rent a bike and take a ride through the countryside, stopping off for lunch at a local farm shop. In the evening, catch a show at the Budleigh Salterton Playhouse.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Exeter, exploring the city’s many shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.

Day 6: Spend the day at Ladram Bay Beach, relaxing on the sand and enjoying the seaside views.

Day 7: Take a horse riding lesson at the Budleigh Salterton Riding School, followed by a picnic in the nearby countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Budleigh Salterton

Must-Try Dishes

Budleigh Salterton has a range of cafes, pubs, and restaurants serving up delicious local dishes. Some must-try dishes include the Devon Cream Tea, consisting of scones, jam, and clotted cream, and fish and chips, a British classic.

Local Beverages

Beer lovers will not be disappointed by Devon’s many local breweries. The nearby Otter Brewery is a great place to stop off for a drink, as is the Branscombe Vale Brewery.

Where to Stay in Budleigh Salterton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budleigh Salterton has a variety of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Those looking for a boutique experience should check out The Salty Monk, while those on a budget might like the Hostel South West.

What to Buy in Budleigh Salterton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Budleigh Salterton’s local shops and markets offer visitors a range of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some popular options include vintage posters from Fairlynch Museum, handmade pottery from the nearby market town of Ottery St. Mary, and local Devonshire fudge.

Local Culture and Customs of Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton is known for its friendly locals and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors should be aware of local customs, such as offering a friendly greeting to passers-by and removing one’s hat when entering a church.

Best Time to Visit Budleigh Salterton

The best time to visit Budleigh Salterton is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the town is bustling with tourists and locals alike.

Do’s and Don’ts of Budleigh Salterton

Do:

  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the town’s many attractions.
  • Try some traditional Devonshire dishes, such as the Devon Cream Tea.
  • Take a scenic walk along the South West Coast Path.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter on the beach or in the countryside.
  • Ignore local customs, such as removing one’s hat in a church.
  • Block the narrow, winding roads with cars or bicycles.

Safety and Health

Safety in Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and not walking alone at night.

Health in Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton has a doctor’s surgery and several pharmacies for those in need of medical attention. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for sunburn and heatstroke during the summer months, and should take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Budleigh Salterton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Budleigh Salterton is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. The nearest airport is Exeter Airport, which is located about 10 miles away. Parking is available in the town center, but visitors should be aware that the narrow, winding roads can be difficult to navigate.

Airports Near Budleigh Salterton

The nearest airport to Budleigh Salterton is Exeter Airport, which is located about 10 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton has a bus stop located in the town center, with regular buses to Exeter and other nearby towns.

Other Options

For those who prefer to travel by train, the nearest railway station is located in Exeter, about 10 miles away.

How to Get Around in Budleigh Salterton

Budleigh Salterton is a small town that is easily navigated on foot or by bicycle. For those with limited mobility, taxis and car hire are also available.

Where to Go Next from Budleigh Salterton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Budleigh Salterton is located in the heart of the West Country, providing visitors with a range of nearby destinations and excursions. Some popular options include:

  • Exeter: A vibrant city with plenty of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.
  • Dartmoor National Park: A beautiful national park with stunning countryside views.
  • Sidmouth: A popular seaside town located just a few miles away from Budleigh Salterton.

Conclusion

Budleigh Salterton is a charming seaside town that offers visitors a range of attractions, from scenic walks and beautiful beaches to local museums and historical sites. With plenty of accommodation options and nearby destinations, it’s the perfect destination for a relaxing holiday in the West Country.
