Introduction to Kingsbridge

Kingsbridge is a small town located in Devon, West Country, England. Situated between Salcombe and Dartmouth, the town is rich in both history and natural beauty. The town’s name is derived from an ancient bridge built by the Anglo-Saxons across the River Dodder, which flows through the town. Today, Kingsbridge is a popular destination for people seeking a relaxing vacation as well as outdoor enthusiasts looking for adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Kingsbridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kingsbridge has a rich history that can be explored through its many museums and historical sites. Some of the most popular among them are:

  • Museum of Rural Life: This museum has exhibits showcasing the rural life of Devon from the early 19th century to the mid-20th century.
  • Church of St. Edmund King and Martyr: A beautiful medieval church dating back to the 13th century, this church has amazing stained-glass windows and a unique painted ceiling.
  • Kingsbridge Cookworthy Museum: This museum showcases the works of William Cookworthy, who discovered the first English porcelain in nearby Plymouth.

Natural Attractions of Kingsbridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kingsbridge is surrounded by stunning natural beauty and offers a range of outdoor activities. Some must-visit natural attractions are:

  • South West Coast Path: A 630-mile long trail that passes through Kingsbridge, the South West Coast Path offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Slapton Sands: A beautiful shingle beach that extends over three miles, Slapton Sands is perfect for a relaxing day by the sea.
  • Burgh Island: A tidal island located near Bigbury-on-Sea, Burgh Island is famous for its Art Deco hotel and its association with Agatha Christie.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingsbridge

Kingsbridge has a range of hiking and biking trails that take you through the region’s beautiful countryside. Some of the best routes are:

  • Avon Estuary Walk: A 5.5-mile walk that takes you along the banks of the River Avon.
  • Kingsbridge to Salcombe Trail: An 8.7-mile trail that runs along the South West Coast Path from Kingsbridge to Salcombe.
  • Beesands and Hallsands Walk: A moderate 4.5-mile walk that takes you through the fishing villages of Beesands and Hallsands.

Best Things to Do in Kingsbridge: Activities and Tours

Kingsbridge offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Some of the best things to do in Kingsbridge are:

  • Boat trips: Take a leisurely boat trip along the River Dart or around Start Bay and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Paddleboarding: Try your hand at paddleboarding and explore the coastline from a different perspective.
  • Wine tasting tour: Visit the nearby Sharpham Vineyard and sample their award-winning wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kingsbridge

Day 1: Arrive in Kingsbridge and explore the town. Day 2: Go on a boat trip to Salcombe and explore the beautiful town. Day 3: Take a hike along the South West Coast Path to Start Point Lighthouse and back. Day 4: Spend the day relaxing on Slapton Sands. Day 5: Visit the Dartmouth Castle and explore the historic town of Dartmouth. Day 6: Explore the natural beauty of Dartmoor National Park. Day 7: Go shopping for souvenirs in Kingsbridge before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Kingsbridge

Must-Try Dishes

Kingsbridge has a range of food options to cater to different tastes. Some must-try dishes are:

  • Crab: The local crab is famous in Kingsbridge, and you can find it in many local seafood restaurants.
  • Cream Tea: The timeless classic of scones, clotted cream, and jam, the cream tea is a must-try when in Kingsbridge.
  • Devonshire Pasty: This traditional dish is a pastry filled with meat and vegetables and is a perfect snack to try when out and about exploring.

Local Beverages

Devon is known for its local beverages, and Kingsbridge is no exception. Some of the must-try local beverages are:

  • Cider: Devon is famous for its cider, and you can find it in many local pubs and bars in Kingsbridge.
  • Tinners Gin: This small-batch gin is made in South Devon and has won several international awards.
  • Sharpham Wine: Visit the Sharpham Vineyard and try their award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Kingsbridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kingsbridge has a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best options are:

  • The Old Inn (Hotel): A quintessential English inn, The Old Inn offers comfortable rooms and great food.
  • Kingsbridge Inn Backpackers (Hostel): A friendly hostel located in the heart of Kingsbridge, Kingsbridge Inn Backpackers offers affordable accommodation for those on a budget.
  • South Allington House (Guesthouse): A beautiful guesthouse located on the outskirts of Kingsbridge, South Allington House offers stunning views of the countryside.

What to Buy in Kingsbridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kingsbridge is a perfect place to shop for souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy items are:

  • Fudge: The traditional Devon fudge is a perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Pottery: There are many local potters in the region, and you can find unique pieces of pottery to bring back home.
  • Handmade Chocolates: Kingsbridge is home to some of the best chocolatiers in Devon, so don’t forget to buy some handmade chocolates to treat yourself or your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Kingsbridge

Kingsbridge has a rich heritage, and the locals are proud of their culture and customs. Some of the most important customs are:

  • May Day: May Day is celebrated in Kingsbridge with a traditional maypole dance and other festivities.
  • Torch Procession: On the last day of the Kingsbridge Fair, a torch procession is held through the town.
  • Wassailing: This ancient custom involves singing to and blessing apple trees in the hopes of encouraging a good harvest.

Best Time to Visit Kingsbridge

The best time to visit Kingsbridge is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is beautiful all year round, and you can enjoy different activities depending on the season.
