Introduction to Honiton

Honiton is a small market town located in Devon, also known as the Gateway to the Jurassic Coast. With a variety of indoor and outdoor activities, it’s a fantastic destination for tourists who seek to explore the natural beauty of the West Country of England.

This guide covers all the information you need to know about the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, the local culture, and food in Honiton.

List of Places to Visit in Honiton

Honiton has several museums, historical places, and natural attractions that are well worth a visit. Here’s a list of the top places to see while in Honiton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Allhallows Museum of Lace and Local Antiquities: The museum houses collections of Honiton lace, pottery, costumes, old photographs, and artifacts from World War I. It’s the perfect place to learn about the history of Honiton and its surroundings.
  • The Thelma Hulbert Gallery: The gallery showcases contemporary art exhibitions featuring both local and national artists. They also offer classes in art and photography.
  • St. Paul’s Church Honiton: A beautiful church built in the 19th century that has unique stained-glass windows and marble accents.

Natural Attractions of Honiton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • East Devon Way: A 40-mile trail from Exmouth to Lyme Regis, passing through Honiton. There are many walking and hiking routes to explore, which you can plan according to the time you have.
  • Honiton Hill Reserve: A nature reserve offering a mix of habitats and wildlife, including several bird species. It’s open year-round for visitors and can be accessed from many points in Honiton.
  • Blackdown Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty: A vast stretch of countryside and rolling hills, with splendid views and lots of opportunities for outdoor activities like cycling and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Honiton

Honiton and its surroundings offer various hiking and biking trails, suitable for families or experienced hikers.

  • Honiton Hill Reserve: It has several short trails grouped into different terrains and habitats, making it a popular destination for hikers.
  • East Devon Way: The eastern section of the trail passes through Honiton, offering a mix of coastal and country scenes.
  • Blackdown Hills: A 140km trail in the Blackdown Hills area, which is an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.

Best Things to Do in Honiton: Activities and Tours

Honiton has many activities to offer, which cater to different audiences, interests, and age groups. From cheese-making to artisan workshops, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular activities and tours in Honiton:

  • Deer Park Country House Hotel: A fantastic place to experience tranquil country life and an excellent destination for weddings and events in beautiful surroundings.
  • Honiton Pottery: Offers pottery classes and workshops for all skill levels.
  • The Honiton Wine Bar: A family-owned wine bar with a vast selection of premium wines and delicious tapas. They host regular live music events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Honiton

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Honiton:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Allhallows Museum of Lace and Local Antiquities, take a tour of the St. Paul’s Church Honiton, and take a stroll through Honiton’s quaint town center.
2 Enjoy a walk or bike ride through the East Devon Way, stopping at one of the many tea rooms or coffee shops in Honiton afterward.
3 Take a tour of the Honiton Pottery and take home your pottery as a souvenir. In the afternoon, attend a wine tasting and tapas at the Honiton Wine Bar.
4 Explore the Blackdown Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty with a guided tour or plan a picnic in one of the many scenic spots.
5 Visit the Thelma Hulbert Gallery and attend one of their art demonstrations. Afterward, go on a shopping spree in the boutiques and gift shops in Honiton.
6 Take a day trip to the seaside town of Sidmouth, enjoy the natural beauty at the Branscombe Beach and the Jurassic Coastline. Stop by the Donkey Sanctuary on the way back to Honiton.
7 Indulge in some relaxation at the Deer Park Country House Hotel, exploring its beautiful grounds and gardens. Afterward, return to Honiton for a final evening out at one of its pubs or restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Honiton

Honiton boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local produce, artisanal goods, and traditional dishes, which are well worth trying.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Honiton Lace or Fen Peas Pudding: A dish popular during the World War, made with local peas and bacon.
  • Devonshire Cream Tea: Enjoy a traditional Devonshire cream tea made with local clotted cream and strawberry jam, available at several tea rooms and cafes in Honiton.
  • Fresh Seafood: Honiton’s proximity to the coast ensures the availability of fresh fish and seafood, which are used in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Furze Fizz: A refreshing drink made with furze flowers and honey.
  • Sharpham Wine: A local wine, produced using sustainable and eco-friendly methods.

Where to Stay in Honiton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Honiton offers several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Honiton are:

  • Deer Park Country House Hotel: A luxurious hotel with a five-star rating and a vast estate with scenic gardens and a lake.
  • The Old Kennels: A restored farm that offers a rustic and cozy countryside experience with self-catering and bed & breakfast options.
  • The Belfry at Yarcombe: A traditional country inn offering modern amenities, including an excellent restaurant, bar, and Sunday roast.

What to Buy in Honiton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Honiton has many gift shops and boutiques that offer an array of souvenirs and local products, including Honiton lace, jewelry, and pottery. Here are some of the best shops to check out:

  • Kings Of Honiton: A shop that sells traditional and vintage gifts such as telescopes, compasses, and binoculars.
  • Thornes Farm Shop: A family-owned shop selling fresh local produce such as vegetables, fruits, and cheeses.
  • The House of Marbles: Features a handmade glassworks and marble museum. Has a gift shop for souvenirs and marble and glass products.

Local Culture and Customs of Honiton

The local culture of Honiton is deeply rooted in the history of this quaint market town. Local customs include:

  • Honiton Lace: An embroidery art unique to the area, dating back to the 16th century, which is still being practiced today. You can take classes or buy Honiton lace souvenirs from various stores in Honiton.
  • The Honiton Hot Pennies Day: A historic event that takes place on the first Tuesday in July every year, where specially marked pennies are thrown from the windows of local buildings and shops, and children collect them.
  • The Honiton Agricultural Show: An annual show featuring livestock, horse displays, food, crafts, and local produce.

Best Time to Visit Honiton

The best time to visit Honiton is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is pleasant, and most events and attractions are open. Spring and autumn are also good seasons to visit, with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Honiton

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts while in Honiton:

Do:

  • Do try Honiton lace embroidery classes or buy souvenirs made of lace.
  • Do attend the Honiton Hot Pennies Day on the first Tuesday in July if you can.
  • Do visit the Deer Park Country House Hotel for a quintessential English countryside experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind while hiking or exploring the natural attractions.
  • Don’t forget to book ahead of time when planning to attend any of Honiton’s events, as they can sell out quickly.
  • Don’t ignore the local food and drink scene, as there are several gems hidden in the town.

Safety and Health

Honiton is a safe town with low crime rates, making it an excellent destination for families and solo travelers. As with any place, visitors should exercise caution, especially while hiking or cycling in the countryside.

Safety in Honiton

  • Ensure to have your belongings on you at all times, especially in crowded areas.
  • Follow the local customs and rules of the attractions and events.

Health in Honiton

  • There is one general hospital in Honiton run by Royal Devon and Exeter NHS Trust, in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Honiton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Honiton has good public transportation options, making it easy to reach through various modes of travel.

Airports Near Honiton

The nearest airports to Honiton are the Exeter International Airport and the Bristol Airport.

Bus Stations Near Honiton

Honiton has a bus station with several buses that connect it to other towns and cities in Devon and the UK.

Other Options

Honiton is a 3-hour train ride from London, with regular trains from Waterloo Station.

How to Get Around in Honiton

Honiton is a small town, and therefore it is easy to walk to most attractions. You can also rent bicycles or hire a taxi to travel around.

Where to Go Next from Honiton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Honiton is located in a prime location to explore the many natural and cultural attractions of the Devonshire countryside. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Honiton include:

  • Sidmouth: A seaside town that’s famous for its sandstone cliffs and red cliffs.
  • Beer: An old-style fishing village that’s known for its handcrafted beer and natural beauty.
  • Exeter: A city known for its Roman and medieval architecture, including its majestic cathedral.

Conclusion

Honiton is a great destination to explore the natural beauty of the Devonshire countryside; it’s a perfect combination of culture, history, and nature suitable for all ages. This comprehensive guide should help you plan your trip to Honiton and make the most of your time in this lovely town.
