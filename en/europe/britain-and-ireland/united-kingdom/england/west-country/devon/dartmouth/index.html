Introduction to Dartmouth

Dartmouth is a small town located on the River Dart in Devon, in the West Country of England. It is best known for its historic naval college, picturesque waterfront, and winding streets filled with boutique shops and local pubs. According to the locals, Dartmouth is one of the most beautiful towns in England, and it is easy to see why! With stunning coastal views, an abundance of outdoor activities, and plenty of cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Dartmouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dartmouth is teeming with rich history and culture, and we’ve listed some of the most important museums and historical places to visit:

  • Dartmouth Castle: A well-preserved fortress overlooking the mouth of the River Dart. Explore the castle’s history and enjoy fantastic views.
  • Royal Naval College: A beautiful, ornate naval building which dates back to the 1600s and is now a training center the British Navy. The college is open to visitors and offers guided tours.
  • Dartmouth Museum: A small museum showcasing the history of Dartmouth, with exhibits ranging from prehistoric times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Dartmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dartmouth’s natural landscape makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • South West Coast Path: This 630-mile trail hugs the coastline of South West England and offers spectacular views of the dramatic cliffs and seascape of the Dartmouth area.
  • The River Dart: The river provides a variety of water activities including boating, kayaking, and paddleboarding. You can also take a ferry ride along the river, enjoy the scenic views and spot the local wildlife.
  • Greenway Estate and Gardens: This beautiful estate was the summer home of famous author Agatha Christie. It is now open for public visits and boasts several gardens and an orchard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dartmouth

Dartmouth is surrounded by beautiful countryside and nature reserves, making it a popular destination for hikers and bikers. Here are some popular trails to check out:

  • Dart Valley Trail: A moderate 16km trail taking you past some stunning views of the River Dart and through lush woodlands.
  • The Ridge and Valley Walk: A circular walk of around 4km, offering panoramic views from Dartmouth Castle and the nearby woods and farmland of North Devon.
  • Dartmoor National Park: A vast national park filled with hiking and biking trails, stunning views, and the famous Dartmoor ponies.

Best Things to Do in Dartmouth: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking, biking, and museums, here are some top things to do in Dartmouth:

  • Dartmouth Steam Railway: A steam train journey running along the coast and countryside. An excellent way to enjoy the scenic views and soak up local history.
  • Dartmouth Food Festival: This annual event takes place in October and features local food producers and chefs showcasing their best products and culinary skills.
  • Boat Trips and Fishing Charters: Take a boat trip on the River Dart or try your hand at fishing on an organized charter. There’s no better way to experience the local marine scene!

Week-Long Itinerary for Dartmouth

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long visit to Dartmouth:

Day Activity
1 Explore Dartmouth Castle and take a guided tour of the Royal Naval College
2 Take a leisurely boat trip along the River Dart and enjoy the view
3 Visit the Greenway Estate and Gardens
4 Hike the Dart Valley Trail of 16km
5 Take a steam train journey on the Dartmouth Steam Railway
6 Enjoy a food tour of Dartmouth’s local markets and food producers
7 Try your hand at fishing on a local charter and explore the marine life of the area

Food and Drink Scene of Dartmouth

Dartmouth’s food and drink scene is a blend of local seafood, traditional pub grub, local brews, and classic teas with scones. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Try this classic comfort food combo at one of the local pubs or restaurants by the waterfront.
  • Crab and Lobster: Devon is known for its delicious seafood, and fresh, locally sourced crab and lobster are must-tries in Dartmouth.
  • Cream Tea: A traditional English tea with scones, jam, and clotted cream is a must-try in the West Country.

Local Beverages

  • Otter Ale: A local beer brewed in nearby Ottery St Mary. Try it at one of the pubs in Dartmouth.
  • Sharpham Wine and Cheese: Visit Sharpham Vineyard and Dairy to sample their local wines and cheeses. The sprawling vineyard is situated on a south-facing slope overlooking the River Dart.

Where to Stay in Dartmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dartmouth offers a variety of accommodation types to suit any budget, with traditional hotels, modern apartment rentals, and cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Ship in Dock Inn: A modern inn in the heart of Dartmouth’s old town. Ideal for those looking for views of the River Dart and an authentic English pub experience.
  • Royal Castle Hotel: A historic hotel in the town center, offering stunning views of the River Dart and Dartmouth Castle.
  • Dart Marina Hotel and Spa: A contemporary luxury hotel located in Dartmouth’s harbor offering high-end amenities, spa facilities, and stunning views of the town and its surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Dartmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade crafts and bespoke jewelry to local food produce and Devonshire tea, Dartmouth has plenty of unique products and gifts for you to take back home. Here are some popular souvenirs to consider:

  • Dartmouth Pottery: Handmade pottery crafted in Dartmouth’s traditional molds and designs.
  • Dartmouth fudge: Delicious homemade fudge made in the town.
  • Dartmouth Britannia Royal Naval College merchandise: Reminds you of the town’s long-standing naval history, with an array of souvenirs available from pencils to model ships.

Local Culture and Customs of Dartmouth

Dartmouth has a strong seafaring culture and naval heritage, which is still evident in the town’s architecture, museums, and markets. The locals here are friendly and helpful, and no visit to Dartmouth would be complete without sampling the seafood and local beer culture.

Best Time to Visit Dartmouth

The best time to visit Dartmouth is in the summer months, from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring and autumn can be equally pleasant, and the attractive countryside is excellent year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dartmouth

Do:

  • Do visit the local museums and historical sites to learn about Dartmouth’s culture and history.
  • Do explore the great outdoors by hiking, biking, and water-based activities.
  • Do try the local seafood, beer, and cream tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes, as the town is hilly and has steep streets.
  • Don’t miss out on the chance to sample the local food and drink culture.
  • Don’t forget to pack warm layers, as the weather can change quickly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dartmouth

Dartmouth is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions when exploring the town and surrounding countryside. Be careful when hiking and walking as the steep streets can be slippery, and when swimming, respect the local marine life and watercrafts.

Health in Dartmouth

Dartmouth has a variety of health services available, including a local hospital, pharmacy, and several private clinics. Visitors should take their usual precautions and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Dartmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dartmouth is relatively remote, with no direct rail link. However, it is still fairly easy to get to. Here are some transportation options available for getting to Dartmouth:

Airports Near Dartmouth

The closest airports to Dartmouth are Exeter International Airport (40 miles) and Bristol International Airport (87 miles). Both airports offer several transit options into town.

Bus Stations Near Dartmouth

National Express operates routes from London Victoria Coach Station to Dartmouth, while Stagecoach operates local bus services.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can rent a car and drive to Dartmouth. The town is easily accessible by road from both the M5 motorway and the A38 Expressway.

How to Get Around in Dartmouth

The town center of Dartmouth is small and easy to navigate on foot. However, visitors may also use local taxis, buses, and ferries to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Dartmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re ready to explore beyond Dartmouth, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Exeter: The vibrant capital city of Devon is just 30 miles away and offers a wealth of shopping, dining, and cultural experiences.
  • Totnes: This charming market town is 10 miles from Dartmouth and is known for its quaint streets and quirky independent shops.
  • Plymouth: Just 27 miles away, Plymouth is a bustling port town with many attractions including the National Marine Aquarium and the Barbican waterfront.

Conclusion

Dartmouth is a charming coastal town with plenty of attractions and outdoor activities to keep visitors entertained. From its historic naval college to its beautiful countryside, there’s something to explore for everyone. Plan your trip today to experience the best of Dartmouth!
