Introduction to Lympstone

Lympstone is a charming coastal village located in Devon, West Country, England. It is situated on the eastern bank of the Exe Estuary, just a few miles south of the city of Exeter. The village offers a picturesque seaside setting, surrounded by beautiful rolling hills and stunning countryside. It is a popular destination for tourists, and there are plenty of things to see and do in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Lympstone

Whether you’re interested in exploring the history and culture of the village or enjoying the natural beauty of the area, Lympstone has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Lympstone:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Thomas à Becket Church: This historic church dates back to the 13th century and is an important landmark in the village. It features stunning architecture and an impressive collection of stained glass windows.
  • Lympstone Manor: This beautiful manor house is now a luxury hotel and restaurant. It was originally built in the 19th century and is a great example of Victorian architecture.
  • Lympstone Village Museum: This small museum is housed in a former chapel and features exhibits on the history of the village and its residents.

Natural Attractions of Lympstone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Exe Estuary: The estuary offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is home to a variety of bird species. Visitors can take a relaxing boat ride or enjoy a peaceful walk along the waterfront.
  • Woodbury Common: This beautiful nature reserve is located just a few miles from the village and offers miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning panoramic views of the countryside.
  • Lympstone Beach: This picturesque pebble beach is the perfect place to relax and enjoy the peaceful coastal setting. Visitors can take a walk along the promenade or enjoy a picnic on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lympstone

Lympstone is surrounded by beautiful countryside and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Exe Estuary Trail: This popular trail runs along the estuary and is perfect for both hiking and biking. It offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is a great way to explore the area.
  • Woodbury Common Trail: This challenging trail features steep hills and rugged terrain, but is well worth the effort for the stunning views it offers. It is a great way to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Lympstone: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring the local culture, enjoying outdoor activities, or simply relaxing and taking in the scenery, Lympstone has something to offer. Here are some of the top things to do in the area:

  • Take a boat ride on the Exe Estuary: This is a great way to see the area from a different perspective and enjoy the local wildlife.
  • Visit Lympstone Manor: This luxurious hotel and restaurant offers a fine dining experience and a chance to explore the history and culture of the village.
  • Explore Woodbury Common: This beautiful nature reserve offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and wildlife watching.

Food and Drink Scene of Lympstone

Lympstone has a thriving food and drink scene, with a range of local and international restaurants, cafes, and bars. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: This classic British dish is a popular choice in Lympstone, with many local restaurants and cafes offering their own take on the dish.
  • Seafood Platter: The village is well-known for its fresh seafood, and a seafood platter is a great way to sample some of the local delicacies.
  • Cream Tea: This traditional English treat consists of scones served with clotted cream and jam, and is a must-try when visiting Lympstone.

Local Beverages

  • Devon Cider: This refreshing alcoholic beverage is made from locally grown apples and is a popular choice in the area.
  • Otter Brewery Beer: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers, including pale ales, stouts, and IPAs.
  • Devon Cream Tea: This traditional tea is made with milk, sugar, and cream, and is a must-try when visiting Lympstone.

Where to Stay in Lympstone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lympstone offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in the area:

  • Lympstone Manor: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and a fine dining experience.
  • The Globe Inn: This charming inn offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere, as well as a variety of local beers and spirits.
  • Lympstone Bed and Breakfast: This family-run B&B offers comfortable rooms and a warm welcome, as well as a hearty breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Lympstone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lympstone is known for its local arts and crafts, as well as its fresh seafood and local produce. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Local artwork and crafts: Many local artists and artisans sell their work in galleries and shops throughout the village, including pottery, jewelry, and paintings.
  • Local seafood and produce: The village is known for its fresh seafood and local produce, including fruits, vegetables, and cheeses.
  • Devon Cream Tea: This traditional treat makes a great gift or souvenir, and is readily available in local cafes and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Lympstone

Lympstone has a rich cultural heritage, with a history dating back to Roman times. The village is known for its traditional way of life, and many residents still practice traditional crafts and hobbies such as sailing, fishing, and woodworking.

Best Time to Visit Lympstone

The best time to visit Lympstone is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny and the village is at its busiest. However, the area is also beautiful during the spring and autumn, when the countryside is ablaze with colorful foliage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lympstone

Lympstone is a safe and friendly village, with a low crime rate and a welcoming community. Visitors should take standard precautions when traveling, such as locking their doors and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Lympstone

Lympstone has a range of health facilities, including a local doctor’s office and a pharmacy. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Lympstone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lympstone is easily accessible by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Lympstone

The nearest airports to Lympstone are Exeter Airport (9 miles away) and Bristol Airport (90 miles away). Both airports offer a range of international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Lympstone

The village is served by several local bus routes, which connect to nearby towns and cities. The nearest bus station is located in Exeter.

Other Options

Lympstone is also easily accessible by car, with ample parking available throughout the village. Visitors can also take a train to the local station (Lympstone Commando), which is served by trains from Exeter and other nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Lympstone

Lympstone is a small village that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. Visitors who wish to explore the surrounding countryside can rent a car or take a local taxi.

Where to Go Next from Lympstone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lympstone is located in a beautiful part of Devon, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • Exeter: The city of Exeter is a short drive or train ride away, and offers a range of attractions, including a beautiful cathedral, historic buildings, and a bustling shopping district.
  • Topsham: This charming town is located just a few miles north of Lympstone and is known for its traditional sailing ships and beautiful waterfront.
  • Dartmoor National Park: This stunning national park is located just a short drive from Lympstone and offers miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning views of the countryside.

Conclusion

Lympstone is a charming village that offers visitors a chance to explore the beauty and history of Devon.
