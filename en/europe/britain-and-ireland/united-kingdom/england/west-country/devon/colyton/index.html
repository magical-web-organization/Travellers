The Ultimate Travel Guide to Colyton

If you’re looking for a picture-perfect destination to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, Colyton is the place to go. Nestled in the picturesque countryside of Devon, United Kingdom, Colyton boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a charming town center. In this comprehensive travel guide to Colyton, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Colyton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Andrew’s Church: This beautiful parish church dates back to the 12th century and features stunning stained-glass windows and a unique octagonal font.
  • Colyton Heritage Centre: Explore the town’s fascinating history with exhibits on everything from the area’s geology to local industries and notable figures.
  • Seaton Tramway: Take a ride on a vintage tram and enjoy stunning views of the Axe Valley and the Jurassic Coast.

Natural Attractions of Colyton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colyton Countryside Walk: This scenic walk takes you through fields and along the picturesque River Coly, passing by historic buildings and beautiful nature reserves.
  • East Devon Way: A 40-mile walking trail that takes you through stunning countryside, along the coast, and through charming villages.
  • Beer Quarry Caves: Explore this unique attraction, featuring mined caves that were used to create the famous Beer Stone used in many historic buildings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colyton

  • Blackbury Camp: A hilly walk with impressive Bronze Age forts and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Musbury Castle: A moderate walk through farmland and forest to reach the Iron Age hill fort.

Best Things to Do in Colyton: Activities and Tours

  • Axe Vale Festival: A popular annual event featuring artisan food, crafts, and live music.
  • Tram Driving Experience: Take the controls of a vintage tram on the Seaton Tramway and embark on a unique adventure.
  • Colcombe Castle: Visit a historic castle and enjoy a traditional cream tea in the castle gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Colyton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devonshire Cream Tea: Scones, clotted cream, and jam served with a pot of tea.
  • Fish and Chips: A British classic, and best enjoyed from a seaside chippy.
  • Yeovil Beer: A local beer brewed with Yeovil hops.

Local Beverages

  • Otter Brewery: Take a tour of the local brewery and enjoy a pint of Otter Ale or Otter Bitter.
  • Pebblebed Vineyards: Taste the local wine at this popular vineyard, set in beautiful surroundings.

Where to Stay in Colyton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Gerrard Arms: A historic coaching inn dating back to the 16th century, with comfortable rooms and a cozy pub.
  • Belmont House Bed and Breakfast: A charming guesthouse with traditional English furnishings and a beautiful garden.
  • Colyton Holiday Cottages: Self-catering accommodation in beautiful locations, perfect for families and those who prefer privacy.

What to Buy in Colyton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beer Stone Artifacts: Take home a piece of history with a Beer Stone artifact, available in many shops and galleries in the area.
  • Otter Brewery Beers: Bring home a few bottles of the local brew to enjoy back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Colyton

Colyton is known for its traditional English culture and customs, with many historic buildings and charming customs still observed. The town also features several community events throughout the year, such as the popular Colyton Carnival.

Best Time to Visit Colyton

The best time to visit Colyton is from May to September, when the weather is warm, and many of the outdoor attractions are at their best. The Shoulder seasons of April, and October also tend to be offering great deals, smaller crowds, and an opportunity to enjoy autumn colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colyton

Do:

  • Follow local customs and traditions, such as standing for the infamous donkey procession through town, during Colyton Carnival.
  • Try local foods and drinks, such as fish and chips or Devonshire cream tea.
  • Respect the natural environment and take care not to litter or damage the area when hiking and walking around.

Don’t:

  • Park in restricted or residential areas without permission.
  • Assume everyone speaks English to the same level of fluency. Be patient with accents, and don’t assume people will understand slang or dialects.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colyton

Colyton is a safe town, with a low crime rate, and friendly locals. As with any rural destination, it is a good idea to stay aware of your surroundings and leave valuables securely stored, making sure you lock your car and your accommodation. Don’t leave empty bags or luggage in parked cars, and be cautious when hiking and driving on the narrow roads throughout the countryside.

Health in Colyton

Colyton has a few medical practices and a pharmacy in town where you can find basic first-aid supplies. All necessary medical assistance is available in the nearest large medical centers, which are slightly outside town.

Getting to Colyton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colyton

The nearest airport to Colyton is Exeter Airport, 30 miles away. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to Colyton.

Bus Stations Near Colyton

Colyton is well-connected by bus, with regular services to Seaton, Sidmouth and Exeter.

Other Options

Train connections are possible from Axminster rail station as a London to Exeter direct service. Visitors can arrange a taxi or use the local bus service from the station to Colyton

How to Get Around in Colyton

Although the town center is quiet and walkable, a car or bike is essential for exploring the surrounding area. Several local rental companies also offer bike and auto rental options.

Where to Go Next from Colyton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Colyton is ideally located for exploring the rest of Devon, with popular destinations such as Seaton, Sidmouth, and Lyme Regis all within easy reach. A visit to Colyton can also be combined with a trip to the nearby Jurassic Coast, where visitors can explore dramatic coastal cliffs and charming seaside villages.

Conclusion

Colyton is a charming town with much to offer to visitors seeking an authentic British countryside experience, from its rich history to its stunning natural beauty. Plan your trip to Colyton using this comprehensive guide to ensure you make the most of your visit.
