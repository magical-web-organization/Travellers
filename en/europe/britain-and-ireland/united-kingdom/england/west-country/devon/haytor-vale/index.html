Haytor Vale is a beautiful village nestled in the heart of Dartmoor National Park in Devon, UK. It is an ideal destination for those who love nature, outdoor adventures, and English culture. In this guide, we will take you on a tour of Haytor Vale and provide you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip.

Introduction to Haytor Vale

Haytor Vale is a small village located in the southwest of England. It is surrounded by the picturesque Dartmoor National Park and boasts stunning views of the moors. The village is known for its rugged beauty, hiking and biking trails, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Haytor Vale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Dartmoor National Park Visitor Centre: A great place to start your trip and learn about the local flora, fauna and geology.
  • Haytor Quarries: A glimpse into the past and the history of the village’s mining industry.
  • St. Raphael’s Church: Beautiful architecture, stained glass windows, and a rich history.

Natural Attractions of Haytor Vale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Haytor Rocks: A granite tor, considered to be one of the landmarks of Dartmoor National Park.
  • The Becky Falls Trail: A scenic hike through the woodland revealing breathtaking waterfalls.
  • The River Teign Walk: A beautiful path following River Teign through the countryside, offering delightful views and natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haytor Vale

  • The Templer Way: A 18-mile walking route that meanders through the valleys and the moors.
  • The Granite Way: A cycle path with remarkable views of the moors.
  • The Haytor Hike: A high-energy hike to the top of Haytor Rocks.

Best Things to Do in Haytor Vale: Activities and Tours

  • Dartmoor Ponies: Meet the famous and friendly ponies grazing in the national park.
  • Canoe on the Teign: A unique way to see the countryside, paddle down the River Teign.
  • Dartmoor Volunteering: Engage with the local community and UNESCO heritage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haytor Vale

Day Itinerary
1 Start your trip with a visit to the Dartmoor National Park Visitor Centre in Princetown.
2 Go for a hike in the Dartmoor National Park. We recommend the Templer Way route.
3 Visit the historic village of Widecombe-in-the-Moor, which offers great insights into local life.
4 Take a cycle ride on the Granite Way followed by a picnic at Meldon Reservoir.
5 Explore the village of Haytor Vale and the local history showcased at St. Raphael’s Church and Haytor Quarries.
6 Hike to the top of Haytor Rocks and soak in the stunning views of the Dartmoor National Park.
7 Canoe on the River Teign and discover the beauty of the countryside from the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Haytor Vale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devon Cream Tea: Scones with jam, clotted cream, and a cup of tea.
  • Hogs Pudding: A sausage made from pork, fat, and oatmeal, served cooked or uncooked.
  • Creamy fish chowder: A delicious mix of local seafood and vegetables in a rich cream broth.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Drink locally brewed ale at one of the village’s pubs.
  • Devonshire Cider: Available in various flavors, it pairs well with local cheeses.
  • Dartmoor Whiskey: Award-winning whiskey distilled in nearby Bovey Tracey.

Where to Stay in Haytor Vale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Moorland Hotel: A four-star hotel with an indoor pool and outdoor hot tub, perfect for a relaxing stay.
  • YHA Dartmoor: A hostel located in the historic village of Bellever, offering affordable and comfortable stays.
  • Haytor Hotel: A family-run guesthouse with cozy rooms and scenic views of Dartmoor National Park.

What to Buy in Haytor Vale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dartmoor Honey: Pure, locally made honey with a unique taste.
  • Moorland Pottery: Handcrafted pottery and ceramics that make great gifts.
  • Artisanal Cheeses: Try the local cheese specialties like Devon Blue and Cornish Yarg.

Local Culture and Customs of Haytor Vale

Haytor Vale prides itself on its rich and unique culture. The village lays great importance on community values, making friends with the locals is all part of the experience. Take the time to learn about the community-run volunteer programs that maintain the natural beauty of Dartmoor National Park and jump in to help.

Best Time to Visit Haytor Vale

The best time to visit Haytor Vale is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is at its best, and all attractions are open to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haytor Vale

Do:

  • Take time to explore the hiking trails of the moors.
  • Learn about the cultural heritage and community values.
  • Make friends with the locals, the perfect way to experience the village.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb nature, protect Dartmoor National Park.
  • Park any parking lots, be careful of speeding on narrow roads.
  • Try to feed or disturb the moor ponies

Safety and Health

Safety in Haytor Vale

Haytor Vale is generally a safe and secure location. However, please keep in mind the following:

  • Follow the official walking and cycling routes, and stay on marked paths.
  • Take caution while driving, the moors can be tricky to navigate at night.
  • In case of emergencies, call 999.

Health in Haytor Vale

The village has no medical center, so we advise travelers to plan their trip to include a visit to the nearest town which has a hospital or medical center.

Getting to Haytor Vale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haytor Vale

  • Exeter International Airport (EXT) is the closest airport, approximately 22 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Haytor Vale

  • Dartline Coaches operates daily services to Haytor Vale from Exeter and Plymouth.

Other Options

  • By Car: Take the A380 and then follow the signs to Haytor Vale.
  • By Train: Newton Abbot Railway Station is about 12 miles away.

How to Get Around in Haytor Vale

The best way to get around Haytor Vale is by bike or on foot. You can rent bikes in nearby towns like Newton Abbott.

Where to Go Next from Haytor Vale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The picturesque town of Totnes, home to a thriving arts scene, is just a short drive away.
  • The coastal town of Torquay is located approximately 20 miles away, boasting beautiful beaches and trendy bars.

Conclusion

Haytor Vale is a beautiful and quaint village nestled in the breathtaking Dartmoor National Park in Devon, UK. It’s a destination that’s perfect for nature lovers, outdoor adventurers, and culture enthusiasts. From scenic hikes to delicious local cuisine, we hope that our comprehensive travel guide helps you plan an unforgettable trip to Haytor Vale.
