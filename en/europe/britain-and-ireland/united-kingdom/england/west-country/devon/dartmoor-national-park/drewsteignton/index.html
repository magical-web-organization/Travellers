Introduction to Drewsteignton

Nested within Dartmoor National Park, Drewsteignton is a charming village in the West Country, England. Characterized by its rolling green hills, scenic views, and rich history, Drewsteignton is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and culture buffs alike. Whether you’re looking to hike through picturesque countryside, explore cultural landmarks, or simply indulge in local cuisine, Drewsteignton is sure to satisfy. In this guide, we’ll cover the top sights, experiences, and insider tips for a memorable trip to Drewsteignton.

List of Places to Visit in Drewsteignton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Peter’s Church

    This 12th-century church is located in the heart of Drewsteignton, and is a must-visit for history buffs. The church’s historic architecture is complemented by its idyllic surroundings, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Castle Drogo

    Built by English architect Edwin Lutyens, this castle is a unique piece of architecture and historical significance. It’s located on a hilltop overlooking the valley surrounded by beautiful gardens and eventually became the last castle to be built in England.

  • The Miniature Pony Centre

    The Miniature Pony Centre is an educational, family-oriented attraction. Located near Drewsteignton, this centre is home to miniature horses, donkeys, and ponies.

Natural Attractions of Drewsteignton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fingle Bridge

    Located near Drewsteignton, Fingle Bridge is a picturesque 17th-century bridge that crosses over the River Teign. This spot is perfect for a picnic or a stroll, and visitors can also explore the surrounding countryside via hiking trails.

  • Meldon Reservoir

    Meldon Reservoir is a man-made lake located in the heart of Dartmoor National Park. Visitors can explore the lake via kayaking or fishing, and the surrounding trails are great for hiking and biking.

  • The Teign Gorge

    Considered one of the most beautiful areas in the region, the Teign Gorge boasts stunning views and picturesque scenery. Visitors can explore the area via hiking trails or simply enjoy the views from one of the many viewpoints.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Drewsteignton

  • The Teign Valley Trail

    This 12-mile trail follows the River Teign through the heart of Dartmoor National Park. Perfect for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts, the trail offers stunning views and plenty of opportunities to spot local wildlife.

  • Dartmoor Way

    The Dartmoor Way is a popular cycling trail that showcases the best of the national park. Spanning 95-miles, the trail takes cyclists through the heart of Dartmoor and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Drewsteignton: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Walks

    Visitors can explore the history and natural beauty of Drewsteignton with guided walks. These tours are led by knowledgeable locals who are happy to share their love and knowledge of the area.

  • Horseback Riding

    Located near Drewsteignton, there are stables that offer horseback riding lessons and trail rides. This is the perfect way to explore the local countryside with a unique twist.

Week-Long Itinerary for Drewsteignton

Food and Drink Scene of Drewsteignton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devonshire Cream Tea

    This dish is a classic in Devon, made up of scones served with clotted cream and jam. Several local cafes and tea-rooms serve this dish and it’s not to be missed.

  • Devonshire pasty

    Often compared to a Cornish Pasty, Devonshire Pasty also contains beef, potato, onion and a tasty pastry.

Local Beverages

  • Devon cider

    Devonshire cider is made from locally produced apples and is a refreshing and thirst-quenching drink on a hot summer day

  • Devonshire Tea

    This tea is a special blend that is often served in tea-rooms across Devon. Its aroma and taste are unique, making it a popular choice for tea-lovers.

Where to Stay in Drewsteignton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Drewe Arms

    A cosy bed and breakfast located near the heart of Drewsteignton, the Drewe arms consist of six rooms, each with its own unique charm.

  • The Old Tea House

    With stunning views of Castle Drogo, this bed and breakfast is the perfect spot to relax after a day of exploring Drewsteignton.

What to Buy in Drewsteignton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dartmoor Ale

    This beer is brewed locally and is a great choice for ale lovers looking to enjoy a pint of something special.

  • Local Crafts

    From handcrafted jewellery to pottery and woodwork, Drewsteignton offers a wide range of locally made crafts that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Drewsteignton

Drewsteignton has a strong sense of community spirit. Locals often come together for events, such as the annual village fete, and the village hall hosts regular events, including live music and theatre performances.

Best Time to Visit Drewsteignton

The best time to visit Drewsteignton is during the summer months between May-September. During this time, the weather is warm and the countryside is at its most picturesque.

Do’s and Don’ts of Drewsteignton

Do:

  • Explore the local hiking trails

    With stunning views and idyllic countryside, Drewsteignton is the perfect destination for hiking enthusiasts.

  • Indulge in local cuisine

    Devonshire cuisine is unique and delicious, with plenty of local specialties to try.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter

    Drewsteignton prides itself on its natural beauty and visitors are expected to respect this by not littering.

  • Venture off designated paths

    Dartmoor National Park is a protected area and visitors are required to stay on designated paths.

Safety and Health

Safety in Drewsteignton

Drewsteignton is generally a safe and welcoming village, although visitors should take common-sense precautions to ensure their safety and security.

Health in Drewsteignton

Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves against exposure to ticks in the countryside, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Drewsteignton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Drewsteignton

The nearest airport to Drewsteignton is Exeter International Airport, which is located approximately 23 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Drewsteignton

The closest bus station to Drewsteignton is in nearby Chagford, which is located approximately five miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Drewsteignton by car or train, with Exeter St. David’s station being the closest train station to the village.

How to Get Around in Drewsteignton

The best way to get around Drewsteignton is by car, although cycling and hiking are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from Drewsteignton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plymouth

    Located approximately 30 miles away, Plymouth is a vibrant coastal city known for its rich naval history and eclectic dining scene.
