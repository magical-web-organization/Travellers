A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Widecombe in the Moor

Widecombe in the Moor is a picturesque village located in Dartmoor National Park in Devon, England. Surrounded by rolling hills and rugged terrain, this small village offers visitors the chance to experience the natural beauty of the English countryside. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at what Widecombe in the Moor has to offer, including the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay and eat.

Introduction to Widecombe in the Moor

Widecombe in the Moor is a village located in the heart of Dartmoor National Park. It’s known for its stunning countryside views, picturesque walks, and historic landmarks. The village is home to several shops, cafes, and pubs, all of which offer visitors a taste of rural English life.

List of Places to Visit in Widecombe in the Moor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St Pancras Church: Built in the 14th century, this church is well known for its impressive stained glass windows, which depict scenes from the Bible.
  • Widecombe History Centre: This museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the history of the village and the surrounding area. It also hosts regular events and exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Widecombe Fair: This annual fair takes place every September and is a must-visit for anyone interested in local traditions and customs.

Natural Attractions of Widecombe in the Moor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Dartmoor Way: This popular hiking and biking route takes visitors on a 95-mile journey through the stunning countryside of Dartmoor.
  • Haytor Rocks: These rock formations offer breathtaking views over the surrounding landscape and are a popular spot for hikers and climbers.
  • Becky Falls: This waterfall is located just a short drive from Widecombe in the Moor and is surrounded by beautiful woodland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Widecombe in the Moor

Widecombe in the Moor is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking routes to choose from. Some popular routes include:

  • The Templer Way: This historic route is named after James Templer, who developed the local quarries. It stretches for 18 miles and takes hikers through some of the most beautiful countryside in the area.
  • Two Moors Way: This long-distance hiking trail takes visitors from Dartmoor to Exmoor and is a challenging yet rewarding experience.
  • Dartmoor Way: As previously mentioned, this 95-mile trail takes visitors on a journey through some of the most breathtaking scenery in Dartmoor National Park.

Best Things to Do in Widecombe in the Moor: Activities and Tours

  • Go horse riding: There are several riding schools in the area that offer guided tours of the local countryside.
  • Visit a local farm: Dartmoor is home to a variety of small farms, many of which welcome visitors to come and see how they operate.
  • Take a guided tour: Several companies in the area offer guided tours of Dartmoor National Park, including wildlife tours, photography tours, and historical tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Widecombe in the Moor

Food and Drink Scene of Widecombe in the Moor

Widecombe in the Moor is home to several traditional country pubs, serving classic English dishes and local ales. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devonshire cream tea: Scones served with clotted cream and strawberry jam.
  • Steak and ale pie: A hearty meat pie made with locally brewed ale.
  • Creamy seafood chowder: A warming soup made with fresh seafood caught in the surrounding waters.

Local Beverages

  • Local ales: Dartmoor has a thriving brewing scene, with several excellent breweries in the area. Try a pint of Jail Ale or Dartmoor Legend.
  • Cider: Devon is known for its delicious cider, which is made from locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Widecombe in the Moor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Widecombe in the Moor has several options for visitors looking for a place to stay. These include:

  • The Old Inn: A traditional country pub with several guest rooms.
  • The Rugglestone Inn: Another popular local pub with guest rooms.
  • Parklands Bed and Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse located just outside the village.

What to Buy in Widecombe in the Moor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Devon clotted cream: This delicious cream is made from the milk of local cows and is a must-try while in the area.
  • Dartmoor honey: Produced by local beekeepers, this honey is renowned for its unique flavor and texture.
  • Handmade crafts and artwork: Several shops in the village sell locally made crafts and artwork, including pottery, paintings, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Widecombe in the Moor

Widecombe in the Moor has a rich cultural heritage, with several traditions and customs that are still practiced today. One of the most famous of these is the Widecombe Fair, which takes place every September and celebrates the local farming community.

Best Time to Visit Widecombe in the Moor

The best time to visit Widecombe in the Moor is during the summer months, between June and September. This is when the weather is at its best, and most of the popular attractions and activities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Widecombe in the Moor

Do:

  • Take a walk or hike to explore the stunning countryside
  • Visit the local museums and historical landmarks
  • Try the local food and drink, including Devonshire cream tea and locally brewed ales

Don’t:

  • Leave your rubbish behind when hiking or cycling
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions
  • Overlook the local wildlife, including Dartmoor ponies, birds, and other species

Safety and Health

Safety in Widecombe in the Moor

Overall, Widecombe in the Moor is a safe destination, and visitors are unlikely to experience any issues. However, as with any rural area, it’s essential to take precautions when hiking or cycling, including wearing appropriate clothing and footwear and carrying plenty of water.

Health in Widecombe in the Moor

The village has several healthcare facilities, including a local hospital and several doctors’ surgeries.

Getting to Widecombe in the Moor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Widecombe in the Moor is located in the heart of Dartmoor National Park and is accessible by car or public transport.

Airports Near Widecombe in the Moor

The nearest airports are Exeter International Airport, which is around 26 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Widecombe in the Moor

There are several bus services that connect Widecombe in the Moor with other parts of Dartmoor and the surrounding area.

Other Options

Several local taxis and private hire vehicles are available for hire in the area.

How to Get Around in Widecombe in the Moor

The best way to get around Widecombe in the Moor is on foot, by bike or by car. Several bike rental companies operate in the area, and there are several car parks located in and around the village.

Where to Go Next from Widecombe in the Moor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Exeter: This historic city is home to several cultural landmarks, including the historic Exeter Cathedral and the Royal Albert Memorial Museum.
  • Plymouth: This coastal city is well known for its maritime heritage, as well as its stunning coastline and proximity to Dartmoor National Park.
  • Brixham: This picturesque fishing village is home to several quaint shops and cafes, as well as a busy harbor and stunning views over the English Channel.

Conclusion

Widecombe in the Moor is a charming and picturesque village located in the heart of Dartmoor National Park. With its stunning countryside views, historic landmarks, and outdoor activities, it’s an excellent destination for anyone looking to experience rural English life. Whether you’re a hiker, a cyclist, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this beautiful part of England.
