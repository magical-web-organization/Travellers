Introduction to Chagford

Chagford is a picturesque town located in Devon, West Country, England. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant community. With a population of fewer than 2,000 people, Chagford is the perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the charm of a small town while enjoying a variety of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Chagford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most popular cultural attractions in Chagford is the Chagford Museum. This small museum features exhibits about the town’s history, including displays about the tin mining industry that was once an important part of the local economy.

Another historical attraction worth visiting is the Holy Trinity Church. This beautiful church dates back to the 13th century and is known for its stunning stained glass windows and intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Chagford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chagford is surrounded by beautiful countryside and is an excellent destination for hikers and nature lovers. One popular hiking route in the area is the Two Moors Way, which runs through Chagford and offers stunning views of Dartmoor National Park.

For those who prefer more leisurely outdoor activities, Castle Drogo is a great place to visit. This stunning castle is surrounded by beautiful gardens that are perfect for picnics and walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chagford

In addition to the Two Moors Way, Chagford offers a variety of other hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. One popular route is the Teign Valley Walk, which runs along the River Teign and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

For mountain bikers, the Dartmoor Way is a great option. This scenic route takes you through some of the most beautiful parts of Dartmoor National Park.

Best Things to Do in Chagford: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Chagford. One popular option is to take a guided tour of the town, which will give you a better understanding of its history and culture.

Another fun activity is to visit one of the local farms, where you can learn about traditional farming methods and meet some of the animals that call Chagford home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chagford

Day 1: Take a guided tour of Chagford and explore the town’s historical attractions.

Day 2: Go hiking on the Two Moors Way and enjoy stunning views of Dartmoor National Park.

Day 3: Visit Castle Drogo and explore its beautiful gardens.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Exeter, where you can explore the city’s historic architecture and cultural attractions.

Day 5: Visit one of the local farms and learn about traditional farming methods.

Day 6: Spend the day mountain biking on the Dartmoor Way.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful views of Chagford and its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Chagford

Must-Try Dishes

Chagford is known for its excellent food scene, with a variety of local delicacies to try. One must-try dish is the Devonshire cream tea, which consists of scones served with clotted cream and jam.

Another local specialty is the Devonshire pasty, which is a savory pastry filled with meat, vegetables, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

In addition to its food, Chagford is also known for its excellent local drinks. The area is home to several breweries, and you can sample their beers at local pubs and restaurants.

Cider is another popular drink in the area, and there are several cider mills where you can learn about the production process and sample different varieties.

Where to Stay in Chagford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chagford offers a variety of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs. One popular option is the Globe Inn, a historic hotel with modern amenities and a great location in the heart of town.

For those on a budget, the Chagford Youth Hostel offers affordable accommodations in a beautiful setting.

What to Buy in Chagford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chagford is known for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, and there are many local shops where you can buy handmade items such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

If you’re looking for a foodie souvenir, try some of the local cheeses or ciders, which are sure to impress your friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Chagford

Chagford has a strong sense of community, and locals are proud of their town’s history and traditions. One of the most popular events in the area is the annual Chagford Show, which is a celebration of the town’s agricultural heritage.

Another important event is the Chagford Filmmaking Group’s Annual Festival, which showcases short films made by local filmmakers.

Best Time to Visit Chagford

The best time to visit Chagford is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the town comes alive with festivals and events.

That being said, Chagford is a beautiful destination year-round, and each season has its own unique charm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chagford

Chagford is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions while traveling. Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

Health in Chagford

There are several medical facilities in Chagford, including a GP surgery and a dental practice. In case of an emergency, call 999 or visit the local hospital in nearby Exeter.

Getting to Chagford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chagford

The closest airport to Chagford is Exeter Airport, which is located about 25 miles away. From there, you can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Chagford.

Bus Stations Near Chagford

There are several bus stations located near Chagford, including in nearby Exeter and Okehampton. Buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel around the area.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Chagford is easily accessible from the A30, which runs through Devon and Cornwall.

How to Get Around in Chagford

Chagford is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. If you prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies located in the area.
