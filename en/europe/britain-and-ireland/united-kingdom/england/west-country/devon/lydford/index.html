Introduction to Lydford

Lydford is a small village located in the West Country of Devon, in the United Kingdom. The village is surrounded by the Dartmoor National Park, making it a hub for outdoor activities. Lydford is best known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and an abundance of walking trails, cycle paths, and waterfalls. With its unspoiled landscapes, friendly locals, and charming architecture, Lydford is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat.

List of Places to Visit in Lydford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lydford Castle: A striking Norman castle built in the 11th century by King William the Conqueror, which served as a prison and a courthouse.
  • St Petroc’s Church: A beautiful historic church, built in the 13th century, with an interesting history and breathtaking architecture.
  • Lydford Gorge: A National Trust property, considered one of the deepest gorges in the south west of the UK with a 30m high waterfall.
  • Dartmoor Prison Museum: A museum that showcases the history of the infamous Dartmoor Prison, which has housed some of the UK’s most dangerous criminals.

Natural Attractions of Lydford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dartmoor National Park: A vast landscape of granite tors, picturesque villages and wooded valleys covering an area of around 368 square miles.
  • Lydford Forest: A 2,000-acre forest located on the western edge of Dartmoor, with hiking trails, mountain bike trails, and picnic areas.
  • Brentor: A small village famous for its church, which is perched on a rocky outcrop and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Tavistock Canal: A historic canal, which was once used to transport goods from Tavistock to Plymouth, has now become a popular spot for walking and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lydford

There are many hiking and biking trails around Lydford, which are perfect for exploring the wilds of Dartmoor National Park. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Granite Way: A 12.5-mile scenic route linking Okehampton and Lydford, which follows along the old railway track.
  • Lydford Gorge: A 3.5-mile round-trip hike, which takes you through dense woodland, past stunning waterfalls and along tranquil riverside.
  • Dartmoor Way: A 95-mile circular cycling route, which take you through the heart of Dartmoor National Park.
  • Burrator Reservoir: A beautiful 3.5-mile walk and cycle around the reservoir, surrounded by the natural beauty of Dartmoor.

Best Things to Do in Lydford: Activities and Tours

  • Castle Drogo: A Grade I listed castle, designed by famous architect Edwin Lutyens, offers a glimpse into early 20th-century life in Devon.
  • Lydford Cycle Hire: Experience the thrill of exploring Dartmoor with the help of Lydford Cycle Hire, who offer bicycles and helmets for hire.
  • Balanced Horse: Join the team at this horse-riding center to enjoy a leisurely ride around Dartmoor National Park.
  • Dartmoor Zoo: Home to a range of exotic and endangered animals, offering daily animal encounters and special experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Lydford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Devonshire Cream Tea: A quintessential English tea experience, served with scones, clotted cream, and jam.
  • Beef and Ale Pie: A hearty and savory pie made with locally sourced beef and a dark ale.
  • Devonshire Ice Cream: Creamy, smooth and delicious ice cream made from the milk of the region’s happy cows.

Local Beverages

  • Dartmoor Ale: A locally brewed ale, made with Dartmoor spring water, barley grown on Dartmoor National Park and English hops.
  • Scrumpy Cider: A traditional west country cider made with locally grown apples.
  • Devonshire Tea: A blend of tea leaves grown on Dartmoor with a delicate flavor that brings out the best of Devonshire Cream Tea.

Where to Stay in Lydford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Castle Inn: A charming 16th century hotel located at the heart of Lydford, offering comfortable rooms and delicious food.
  • Gages Mill Country Guest House: A beautiful guest house set amidst stunning gardens, with luxurious guest rooms and exceptional dining.
  • Bunkhouse at the Old School: A cozy and budget-friendly hostel located in a converted school building, perfect for backpackers and hikers.

What to Buy in Lydford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-carved souvenirs: Take home a memento of your visit to Lydford with local handcrafted wooden souvenirs.
  • Devonshire Apple Juice: A refreshing drink made from locally grown apples, perfect to sip on a hot summer day.
  • Dartmoor Honey: A delicious and naturally sweet honey made from the nectar of wild Dartmoor flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Lydford

Lydford has a rich cultural heritage rooted in local folklore and historical events. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to participate in local festivals and celebrations. It is customary to tip restaurant staff and leave a small gratuity for hotel staff.

Best Time to Visit Lydford

The best time to visit Lydford is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the autumn months of September and October also offer a beautiful display of natural fall colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lydford

Do:

  • Take your time to explore the natural beauty of Dartmoor National Park.
  • Try the local food and drink, such as the Devonshire cream tea and Dartmoor Ale.
  • Respect the local environment by using designated trails and disposing of litter responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the weather forecast while planning outdoor activities.
  • Feed the wild animals, as this can habituate them to humans and may lead to an unsafe situation.
  • Venture off designated trails or access private land without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lydford

Lydford is a relatively safe destination; however, visitors are encouraged to take standard precautions such as keeping an eye on personal belongings. If venturing into Dartmoor bring sufficient supplies, inform someone of your plans and keep abreast of the weather forecast to ensure safety.

Health in Lydford

The UK has a National Health Service (NHS), which provides free emergency medical treatment to anyone in need. Visitors are advised to obtain comprehensive travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Lydford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lydford

The nearest international airport to Lydford is Exeter Airport which is approximately an hour’s drive away. Alternatively, Bristol Airport is located 1.5 hours’ drive from Lydford.

Bus Stations Near Lydford

The Stagecoach 318 bus service runs daily from Tavistock to Lydford.

Other Options

Driving to Lydford is also a viable option with safe and easy-to-navigate roads, allowing visitors to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Lydford

The easiest way to get around Lydford is by car, although cycling and walking are good alternatives.

Where to Go Next from Lydford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tavistock: A charming market town located just 7 miles from Lydford, famous for its Pannier Market, Theatre and Arts Centre, and its links to Sir Francis Drake.
  • Plymouth: A vibrant city which is home to a bustling shopping district, a stunning waterfront and celebrated cultural attractions such as the National Marine Aquarium.
  • Exeter: A historic city with a wealth of culture and architecture, including the Cathedral Church of Saint Peter in Exeter, a stunning feat of Gothic architecture.

Conclusion

Lydford is the perfect destination for those seeking to experience the beauty of rural England. From natural wonders to cultural landmarks, delicious food and drink, and tons of outdoor activities, Lydford offers a wealth of exciting experiences for every traveler. With its friendly locals, rich heritage, and scenic vistas, the village may quickly become a firm favorite.
