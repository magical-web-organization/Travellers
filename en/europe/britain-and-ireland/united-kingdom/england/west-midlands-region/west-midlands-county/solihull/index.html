Introduction to Solihull

Solihull is a town located in West Midlands, England, known for its bustling centre, historic buildings, beautiful gardens, and scenic countryside. The town is situated just nine miles southeast of Birmingham and offers a plethora of attractions, activities, and experiences for visitors of all ages.

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or simply looking for a quiet escape from the city, Solihull has plenty to offer. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, top things to do, best places to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Solihull

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Solihull Heritage & Local History Centre: This museum showcases Solihull’s history and heritage, with exhibitions, archives, and artefacts.
  • Tudor Grange Park: A large park with a lake, gardens, and woodland trails.
  • St Alphege Church: This magnificent church is a blend of Gothic and Romanesque architecture and dates back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Solihull: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malvern and Brueton Park: A beautiful large park with a lake, gardens, and woodland walking trails.
  • Elmdon Nature Park: A tranquil green space with a lake, meadows, and a bird sanctuary.
  • Baddesley Clinton: A National Trust property with a moated manor house, gardens, and beautiful parkland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Solihull

  • Solihull Greenway: A scenic, 7.5-mile long walking and cycling route that passes through parks, nature reserves, and the heart of Solihull town centre.
  • Lapworth Circular Walk: A moderate 5.7-mile circular walk that takes in the Warwickshire countryside and passes through charming rural villages such as Lapworth and Packwood.
  • Shirley Circular Route: A relatively flat 7.5-mile walking and cycling route that takes in picturesque villages, nature reserves, and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Solihull: Activities and Tours

  • Solihull Arts Complex: A cultural hub which showcases live music, theatre, film, comedy and other events.
  • Solihull Ice Rink: A great family-friendly activity, suitable for all ages and abilities.
  • Solihull Moors Football Club: A local team with a passionate fanbase, playing in the National League.

Week-Long Itinerary for Solihull

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Solihull Heritage & Local History Centre, then enjoy a picnic in Malvern and Brueton Park.
Day 2 Take a hike along the Solihull Greenway, then visit Tudor Grange Park.
Day 3 Explore the charming villages of Lapworth and Packwood, then visit Baddesley Clinton.
Day 4 Visit St Alphege Church, then enjoy some ice skating at the Solihull Ice Rink.
Day 5 Take a bike ride along the Shirley Circular Route, then enjoy some live music at Solihull Arts Complex.
Day 6 Visit Elmdon Nature Park, then catch a match at Solihull Moors Football Club.
Day 7 Spend the day exploring the many shops, cafes, and restaurants of Solihull town centre.

Food and Drink Scene of Solihull

Must-Try Dishes

  • Solihull Cream Tea: A classic cream tea comprising scones, clotted cream, and locally sourced strawberry jam.
  • Birmingham Balti: A spicy curry dish originating from nearby Birmingham, typically served with naan bread.
  • Gingerbread: Solihull is known for its delicious gingerbread, which is sold in many local cafes and bakeries.

Local Beverages

  • Banks’s Brewery: A local brewery, known for its award-winning beers, located a short distance from Solihull.
  • Purity Brewing Company: Another local brewery, located in nearby Warwickshire, known for its organic beer and eco-friendly ethos.

Where to Stay in Solihull: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The St John’s Hotel: A contemporary 4-star hotel with superb facilities, located just a short walk from Solihull town centre.
  • Premier Inn Solihull Town Centre: A popular budget-friendly hotel, located in the heart of Solihull town centre.
  • Hogarths Hotel: A luxurious hotel set in stunning grounds, with exquisite features throughout and an excellent restaurant.

What to Buy in Solihull: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Solihull Gingerbread: A must-have local delicacy, available in many local cafes and bakeries.
  • Birmingham Balti Mix: A blend of spices used in the famous Birmingham Balti dish.
  • Local Beers: Choose from Banks’s or Purity Brewery to bring back a taste of Solihull to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Solihull

Solihull is a town steeped in history and culture, with many events and traditions held throughout the year. The annual Solihull Carnival takes place in June and features a parade, live music, and fairground rides. Other noteworthy events include the Shirley Food Festival and the Solihull Jazz & Blues Festival.

Best Time to Visit Solihull

The best time to visit Solihull is during the summer months, from late May to early September, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the parks and outdoor attractions are in full bloom. However, the town is a great destination year-round, with many indoor attractions, festivities, and winter markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Solihull

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical places to learn more about Solihull’s fascinating history.
  • Take advantage of the many parks, gardens, and walking trails in the area.
  • Try the local gingerbread and other delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the countryside - Solihull has some spectacular natural scenery just a short distance outside the town centre.
  • Leave without exploring the surrounding area - there are many charming villages, towns, and attractions nearby worth visiting.
  • Forget to try the local beers and other beverages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Solihull

Solihull is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially in busy areas and at night.

Health in Solihull

Solihull has several hospitals, medical centres, and clinics, providing excellent medical care for residents and visitors alike.

Getting to Solihull: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Solihull

The nearest airport to Solihull is Birmingham Airport, which is approximately six miles away and has many domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Solihull

Solihull’s bus station is located in the heart of the town centre, with regular services to nearby towns, cities, and villages.

Other Options

Solihull is easily accessible by car, with the M42 motorway passing through the town, and there are several train stations in the area, with regular services to Birmingham and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Solihull

Solihull is a relatively compact town, and many of the main attractions and amenities are situated within walking distance of each other. However, there are also several local bus services, taxis, and bike hire options available, making it easy to get around the town and surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Solihull: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Birmingham: Just a short distance away, the vibrant city of Birmingham is a must-visit destination, with attractions like the Bullring shopping centre, the once-industrial but now cultural Gas Street Basin, the iconic Selfridges building, and several other museums, galleries, and parks.
  • Warwick: A beautiful town with an impressive 11th-century castle and charming old town centre.
  • Stratford-upon-Avon: The birthplace of William Shakespeare, Stratford-upon-Avon is a picturesque town filled with Tudor architecture, gardens, and riverside walks.

Conclusion

Solihull is a fantastic destination for anyone seeking a blend of history, culture, nature, and modern amenities. With plenty of attractions, activities, and experiences on offer, visitors of all ages and interests are sure to find something to love in this West Midlands town.
