Introduction to Ellesmere

Located in Shropshire, West Midlands, Ellesmere is a picturesque market town in England that is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. This charming town is also home to a variety of recreational activities, such as hiking, biking, and water sports that make it perfect for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do in Ellesmere, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Ellesmere

If you’re planning a trip to Ellesmere, then there are plenty of places to visit that will help you experience the charming atmosphere of this town. Here are some of the top attractions in Ellesmere:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ellesmere College - this historic college is renowned for its beautiful buildings and picturesque setting. Take a stroll around the gardens and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.
  • Colemere Country Park - a stunning nature reserve that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including some rare species that are not found elsewhere in the UK.
  • The Mere - a large lake that is perfect for water sports, fishing, and relaxing picnics.

Natural Attractions of Ellesmere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hawkstone Park Follies - this unique park features a series of caves, grottos, and turrets that are perfect for exploring and enjoying the breathtaking natural scenery.
  • Whixall Moss National Nature Reserve - this protected wilderness area is a haven for wildlife and provides a fantastic opportunity to explore the beautiful natural landscapes of Ellesmere.
  • Shropshire Union Canal - a picturesque waterway that is perfect for relaxing walks and cycling trips.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellesmere

Ellesmere is the perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking routes that offer stunning scenery and challenging trails. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The Mere Walk - a picturesque 7.5-mile circular route that takes you around the beautiful Mere lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Shropshire Way - this 139-mile-long trail is one of the most popular hiking routes in the UK and offers a fantastic opportunity to explore the stunning landscapes of Ellesmere.
  • The Llangollen Canal Walk - this popular trail is perfect for hiking and cycling and takes you along the picturesque Shropshire Union Canal.

Best Things to Do in Ellesmere: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Ellesmere. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Boat trips on the Mere - Experience the peaceful beauty of the Mere in a boat trip around the lake and appreciate the scene at a completely different angle.
  • Fishing - If you’re an angler, you’ll love Ellesmere as it has several locations catering for all types of fishing including fly fishing and coarse fishing.
  • Wildlife spotting - With the diverse range of wildlife species in the region, Ellesmere is perfect for wildlife photography. You can spot dragonflies, various bird species and other creatures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ellesmere

If you’re planning on spending a week in Ellesmere, then here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive and check-in at your hotel or guesthouse. Spend the afternoon exploring the town center and visiting some of the local shops and restaurants.
Day 2: Take a boat trip at the Mere lake in the morning. Afternoon, explore Colemere Country Park and witness its impressive wildlife.
Day 3: Hike along the Shropshire Way and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
Day 4: Visit Hawkstone Park Follies and discover its hidden caves, turrets and picturesque vistas.
Day 5: Explore Whixall Moss National Nature Reserve and appreciate the breathtaking scenery of the Ellesmere region.
Day 6: Cycle along the Llangollen Canal Walk and soak up the peaceful ambiance by the waterways.
Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as Chester Castle or visit the Ironbridge Gorge UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellesmere

Ellesmere has a thriving food and drink scene, with a variety of restaurants, cafes and bars serving up delicious meals and refreshing drinks. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shropshire Hotpot - a traditional dish made with lamb, potatoes, onions, and carrots that is the perfect comfort food on a cold day.
  • Cheese and Ale pie - a savory pie made with local cheese and ale that is a beloved dish in Ellesmere.
  • Butterfly Cake - known as the unofficial cake of Ellesmere, this delicious treat is made with pink and yellow cake layers separated by jam and buttercream.

Local Beverages

  • Shropshire Gold - a locally brewed ale that is the perfect accompaniment to any meal.
  • Champagne cider - a refreshing drink that is made with local cider and sparkling wine, giving it a unique and delicious taste.

Where to Stay in Ellesmere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ellesmere has plenty of options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Moors Farm B&B - a luxurious bed and breakfast located in beautiful surroundings that offer spectacular views of the Ellesmere countryside.
  • Travelodge Ellesmere - a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms in the heart of the town center.
  • The Boathouse - A boutique hotel located right on the edge of Mere lake, offering stunning views, comfortable rooms, and excellent dining options.

What to Buy in Ellesmere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ellesmere is home to a variety of shops and boutiques that offer unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home as gifts or treats. Here are some of the top products to look out for:

  • Ellesmere Cheese - made using locally produced milk, Ellesmere cheese is a delicious and distinctive cheese that is perfect for cheese lovers.
  • Shropshire Lavender products - Shropshire is famous for its lavender fields and the local shops sell a variety of products made from the fragrant herb, including soap, candles, and essential oils.
  • Handcrafted pottery - Ellesmere is home to a variety of pottery studios and shops that sell beautiful and unique pottery items, including mugs, plates, and vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellesmere

Ellesmere is a welcoming and friendly town that is renowned for its rich cultural heritage. Here are some customs and traditions that you may come across:

  • Morris Dancing - a traditional folk dance that is popular in Ellesmere and involves dancing while wearing bells on the ankles.
  • The Ellesmere Carnival - an annual event held in July that includes a procession, fairground, and live music.
  • Ellesmere Show - an agricultural show featuring numerous competitions, including sheep shearing and cattle judging, that takes place in August.

Best Time to Visit Ellesmere

The best time to visit Ellesmere is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and the days are longer. This is also when there are many outdoor events taking place, such as the Ellesmere Carnival and the Ellesmere Show. However, autumn months are also a good time to visit as the changing colors of the trees and fields add to the charm of the place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellesmere

Do:

  • Take plenty of photos of the stunning scenery.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be polite and friendly to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas or harm the wildlife in any way.
  • Wear shoes with hard soles on the boats or within Colemere Country Park.
  • Overly expose the natural beauty spots by making too much noise or leaving traces behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellesmere

Ellesmere is a very safe town for travelers, but it’s always important to take standard safety precautions. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings and do not leave them unattended in public.
  • Be vigilant at night and avoid walking alone in unlit areas.
  • Always adhere to warning signs around natural areas and waterways.

Health in Ellesmere

There are no major health concerns for travelers to Ellesmere, but it’s always wise to take standard hygiene precautions. Tapwater in Ellesmere is drinkable and it’s always a good idea to carry a refillable water bottle with you when you’re out and about.

Getting to Ellesmere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ellesmere is well connected to the rest of the UK and can be easily reached by various modes of transportation. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Ellesmere

  • Manchester Airport - the closest international airport, located approximately 60 miles north of Ellesmere.
  • Birmingham Airport - located approximately 65 miles south of Ellesmere.

Bus Stations Near Ellesmere

  • Ellesmere Bus Station - located in the town center, with regular services to nearby towns and cities.
  • Oswestry Bus Station - located approximately 9 miles northwest of Ellesmere.

Other Options

  • Rail - the nearest train station is located in Gobowen, approximately 10 miles northwest of Ellesmere.
  • Car Rentals - Ellesmere is easily accessible by car and there are several car rental companies situated in nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Ellesmere

The town center is best explored on foot, while for longer journeys you can use public transportation services or hire a car. Bicycles can also be rented from several rental companies in the town. The Ellesmere Conservation Area Association offers a free guide to the town, including a map with walking and cycling routes.

Where to Go Next from Ellesmere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider once you’ve finished exploring Ellesmere, such as:

  • Chester - an ancient Roman city with a rich history and a variety of attractions, including Chester Cathedral and the Roman Amphitheatre.
  • Ironbridge Gorge UNESCO World Heritage Site - a historic industrial site that offers a fascinating glimpse into the birth of the Industrial Revolution.
  • Shrewsbury - a medieval town that is renowned for its atmospheric architecture and picturesque streetscapes.

Conclusion

Ellesmere is a charming town that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Whether it’s hiking in the stunning countryside, exploring historic sites, or enjoying delicious local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Ellesmere. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and charm of this historic town for yourself!
