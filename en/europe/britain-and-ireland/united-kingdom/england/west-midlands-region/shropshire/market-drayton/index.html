Market Drayton, located in Shropshire in the West Midlands region of England, is a charming market town with a rich history and stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this idyllic town. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit attractions and destinations in Market Drayton, including hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and local culture and customs.

Introduction to Market Drayton

Market Drayton is a market town in the north of Shropshire, which dates back to the medieval times. The town offers a blend of old-world charm and modern-day amenities, making it the perfect destination for those who want to explore the rich history of England while enjoying the luxuries of modern life. Market Drayton is known for its famous gingerbread and its bustling market, held every Wednesday.

List of Places to Visit in Market Drayton

Here’s a list of the top attractions that you must visit when in Market Drayton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Mary’s Church: This magnificent church is the largest in Market Drayton and dates back to the 13th century. The church has an impressive nave and chancel, as well as some beautiful stained glass windows.
  • Fordhall Organic Farm: This organic farm is a popular attraction in Market Drayton, where visitors can meet the farmer or take part in guided tours.
  • Market Drayton Museum and Resource Centre: A small museum showcasing the town’s rich history, including the famous gingerbread trade.

Natural Attractions of Market Drayton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Shropshire Union Canal: The canal is a beautiful location for a leisurely walk, a bike ride, or even a boat trip.
  • Market Drayton Country Park: A popular spot for walking, running, or relaxing and enjoying a picnic with family and friends. The park features a children’s playground and a skate park.
  • Hodnet Hall Gardens: These are stunningly beautiful gardens open to the public in the spring and summer, with a wide variety of plants, trees, and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Market Drayton

Market Drayton offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the West Midlands region. Here are some of the top picks:

  • The Shropshire Way: This walking trail is 139 miles long and offers breathtaking views and scenic landscapes throughout the county of Shropshire.
  • The Tern Valley Way: This walking trail is a 20.5-mile-long path that follows the River Tern from Market Drayton to Shrewsbury, the county town of Shropshire.
  • The Peppermill Way: This walking trail is a 35-mile-long trail that starts in the county town of Stafford, passes through Market Drayton, and ends in Nantwich.

Best Things to Do in Market Drayton: Activities and Tours

Market Drayton offers several exciting activities and tours that you must try during your visit:

  • Gingerbread Making Workshops: Learn the art of making Market Drayton’s famous gingerbread from the experts themselves.
  • Canal Boat Trips: Take a relaxing canal boat tour along the Shropshire Union Canal and appreciate the stunning scenery of this picturesque town.
  • Market Drayton Ghost Walks: Join the guided tour to discover the ghostly history and legends of Market Drayton.

Food and Drink Scene of Market Drayton

Market Drayton has a bustling food and drink scene, with a range of local and international cuisines available. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Market Drayton Gingerbread: This spicy and sweet gingerbread is the town’s most famous delicacy and is a must-try for anyone who visits.
  • Shropshire Fidget Pie: This local delicacy is a hearty pork pie filled with bacon, onions, and apples.
  • Cheese on toast with Worcestershire sauce: This is a classic British dish that’s simple and tasty.

Local Beverages

  • Hobsons Brewery: This local brewery produces some delicious handcrafted beers that are a must-try when in Market Drayton.
  • Gin from the Shropshire Distillery: This local distillery produces some of the finest gins in the country.

Where to Stay in Market Drayton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Market Drayton offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in the town:

  • The Four Alls Inn: This 17th-century inn offers comfortable rooms and a traditional pub atmosphere.
  • The Crown Hotel: This historic hotel dates back to the 16th century and offers cozy rooms in a charming setting.
  • Grove Farm B&B: This bed and breakfast located in the countryside is perfect for those looking for a quiet and peaceful stay.

What to Buy in Market Drayton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for something unique to bring back home, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Market Drayton:

  • Market Drayton Gingerbread: A pack of this famous gingerbread is the perfect souvenir to take back home and share with friends and family.
  • Organic Produce from Fordhall Organic Farm: Stock up on some delicious organic produce to take back home and savor the flavors of Market Drayton.
  • Locally Made Ceramics and Pottery: Market Drayton is home to several talented ceramic and pottery makers, whose creations make for beautiful and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Market Drayton

Market Drayton has a rich and fascinating culture that’s worth exploring. Here are some of the customs and traditions that locals take pride in:

  • The Gingerbread Trade: The town’s most famous product is its gingerbread, which has been baked here since the 1800s.
  • The Market: The town’s weekly market, held every Wednesday, has been a tradition for centuries, and is a great place to experience the hustle and bustle of local life.

Best Time to Visit Market Drayton

The best time to visit Market Drayton is during the spring and summer months, when the weather is mild, and the gardens and countryside are in full bloom.

Safety and Health

Market Drayton is a small town, and getting around on foot is easy and enjoyable. Bikes and boats can be rented for exploring the scenic countryside and canal. Taxis and buses are also available for those looking for an alternative means of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Market Drayton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
