Introduction to Oswestry

Nestled in the scenic county of Shropshire, Oswestry is a charming market town with a rich history dating back to the Bronze Age. The town has a pleasant mix of modern amenities and old world charm, making it an attractive destination for visitors from all over the world. The town is easily accessible by road and rail, making it an ideal weekend getaway destination.

List of Places to Visit in Oswestry

Whether you’re a history buff, nature-lover, or adventure enthusiast, Oswestry has something to offer everyone. Here’s a list of some of the top attractions to visit in Oswestry:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oswestry Town Museum: Situated in an 18th-century building, the Oswestry Town Museum is devoted to preserving the history and heritage of the town. The museum has an impressive collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that bring the history of Oswestry to life.
  • Whittington Castle: This 12th-century castle is located just outside Oswestry and is one of the oldest castles in the region. The castle is open to visitors and hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including medieval tournaments and falconry displays.
  • Old Oswestry Hill Fort: This ancient hill fort dates back to the Bronze Age and is a must-visit for history buffs. The fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and is surrounded by walking trails that are perfect for nature lovers.

Natural Attractions of Oswestry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Erdigg Hall: This 18th-century mansion is located just 8 miles from Oswestry and is surrounded by landscaped gardens and parkland. The estate has over 1,200 acres of parkland that are perfect for hiking, picnicking, and birdwatching.
  • Llanymynech Rocks Nature Reserve: This picturesque nature reserve is located near Oswestry and is home to a variety of rare plant and animal species. The reserve has several walking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Pontcysyllte Aqueduct: located just outside of Oswestry, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a stunning feat of engineering that offers breathtaking views over the Dee Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oswestry

Oswestry is surrounded by miles of rolling countryside that are perfect for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Offa’s Dyke Path: This 177-mile long-distance footpath runs from Chepstow in the south to Prestatyn in the north and passes through Oswestry. The path offers stunning views of the Welsh and English countryside and is ideal for hikers of all skill levels.
  • The Maelor Way: This 24-mile circular walk around the Maelor countryside is a great way to explore the area surrounding Oswestry. The walk offers beautiful scenery and a chance to see local wildlife up close.
  • Sheriffhales Loop: This 6-mile circular biking route starts and ends at the small village of Sheriffhales and takes you through some of the most picturesque countryside in the area.

Best Things to Do in Oswestry: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Oswestry, here are some top activities and tours to consider:

  • Oswestry Treasure Hunt Trail: This self-guided treasure hunt trail takes you on a journey through the historic streets of Oswestry and is a fun way to explore the town.
  • Oswestry Farmers Market: The Oswestry Farmers Market takes place on the last Friday of every month and is a great way to experience the local food and culture.
  • Oswestry Heritage Trail: This self-guided walking tour takes you on a journey through the history of Oswestry and includes stops at some of the town’s most important historical landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Oswestry

Oswestry has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the best of local produce and cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shropshire Fidget Pie: This hearty pie is made with diced pork, apples, and onions, and is a local specialty.
  • Cawl: This traditional Welsh soup is made with lamb or beef and includes a variety of vegetables.
  • Toad in the Hole: This savory dish consists of sausages baked in a Yorkshire pudding batter and is a comfort food favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Shropshire Lad: This locally brewed ale is made with locally source hops and barley and has a rich, full-bodied flavor.
  • Perry: This traditional pear cider is made with locally grown pears and is a popular choice among locals.
  • Clive’s Wines: This local winery produces award-winning wines that are made from locally grown grapes.
