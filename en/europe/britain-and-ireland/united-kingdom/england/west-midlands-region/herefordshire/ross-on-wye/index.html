Introduction to Ross-on-Wye

Nestled in scenic Herefordshire in the West Midlands region of England, Ross-on-Wye is a charming market town that offers something for everyone. Known for its picturesque setting along the River Wye and surrounded by rolling hills, Ross-on-Wye is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. If you’re planning a trip to Ross-on-Wye, this comprehensive guide has everything you need to know to make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ross-on-Wye

From fascinating historical landmarks to picturesque natural attractions, Ross-on-Wye has many sights worth exploring. Here are some of the top places to see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Market House: Located in the center of Ross-on-Wye, the Market House is a beautiful Tudor building that dates back to the 17th century. Today, it houses a museum that showcases the town’s rich history.
  • Goodrich Castle: Just a short drive from Ross-on-Wye, the imposing ruins of Goodrich Castle offer a glimpse into medieval life in England.
  • The Kymin: This hill overlooking Ross-on-Wye offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, and is home to a historic building that housed a secret society during the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Ross-on-Wye: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Symonds Yat: One of the most popular attractions in Ross-on-Wye, Symonds Yat is a stunning viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the River Wye and its surrounding landscape.
  • The Forest of Dean: Just a short drive from Ross-on-Wye, the Forest of Dean is a vast woodland area that offers numerous outdoor activities, such as cycling, hiking, and wildlife watching.
  • Wye Valley Walk: This long-distance walking trail spans 136 miles along the River Wye, offering stunning scenery and ample opportunities for wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ross-on-Wye

Ross-on-Wye is surrounded by beautiful countryside that’s ideal for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Ross-on-Wye Circular Walk: This 4-mile circular walk takes in some of the town’s most scenic spots, including the River Wye and the Market House.
  • The Wysis Way: This 55-mile trail runs from Monmouth to Bourton-on-the-Water, passing through Ross-on-Wye along the way.
  • The Forest of Dean Family Cycle Trail: This 12-mile trail is ideal for families looking for an easy and scenic bike ride through the forest.

Best Things to Do in Ross-on-Wye: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for something adrenaline-fueled or a more laid-back activity, Ross-on-Wye has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The River Wye is a popular spot for water sports, and several local companies offer canoe and kayak rentals.
  • The Ross-on-Wye Ghost Walk: Take a guided tour through the town’s dark and spooky past.
  • The Wye Valley Butterfly Zoo: This family-friendly attraction is home to a variety of exotic butterflies and insects.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ross-on-Wye

If you’re planning to spend a week in Ross-on-Wye, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore the town center, visiting the Market House and other cultural landmarks.

Day 2: Take a leisurely walk along the River Wye, stopping to explore Symonds Yat and other natural attractions.

Day 3: Enjoy a day of outdoor adventure in the Forest of Dean, with options for cycling, hiking, and wildlife watching.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of Goodrich Castle and learn about its fascinating history.

Day 5: Explore the area around Ross-on-Wye, visiting other picturesque towns such as Monmouth or Ledbury.

Day 6: Take a canoe trip down the River Wye, enjoying the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of the town’s local food and drink, such as cider and Herefordshire beef.

Food and Drink Scene of Ross-on-Wye

Ross-on-Wye and the surrounding area is known for its excellent local produce, including cider, beef, and cheese. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Herefordshire beef
  • Welsh cakes
  • Cider-battered fish and chips

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Try a variety of locally-made ciders, such as that of Westons Cider or Gwatkin Cider.
  • Real Ale: Sample the unique flavors of local breweries such as Wye Valley Brewery or Butty Bach Brewery.

Where to Stay in Ross-on-Wye: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ross-on-Wye offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, from quaint guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • The Chase Hotel: A charming Georgian hotel in the heart of Ross-on-Wye that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Crown Hotel: A cozy, family-run hotel that’s just a short walk from the town center.
  • Brookfield House: This stylish bed and breakfast is located just outside of Ross-on-Wye, offering peaceful countryside views and easy access to outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Ross-on-Wye: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home from Ross-on-Wye, consider these local products:

  • Cider: Take home a bottle (or two!) of locally-made cider as a delicious reminder of your trip.
  • Wye Valley Cheese: This artisan cheese is made locally and is a must-try for foodies.
  • Local art and crafts: Browse the town’s many boutique shops and galleries to find unique handmade souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ross-on-Wye

Ross-on-Wye is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are proud of their town’s rich history and heritage. The town has a relaxed and laid-back vibe, making it an ideal destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

Best Time to Visit Ross-on-Wye

The best time to visit Ross-on-Wye is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny and the countryside is in full bloom. However, the town has a unique charm in all seasons, so it’s worth considering a visit at any time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ross-on-Wye

To make the most of your trip to Ross-on-Wye, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities on offer, from hiking to canoeing.
  • Sample the delicious local food and drink, including cider and Herefordshire beef.
  • Explore the town’s fascinating history and cultural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the need for comfortable walking shoes - Ross-on-Wye is a great destination for exploring on foot.
  • Forget to bring appropriate outdoor gear, such as waterproof jackets and sturdy hiking boots.

Safety and Health

Ross-on-Wye is generally a safe and friendly town, but it’s worth taking some precautions to stay safe and healthy during your visit.

Safety in Ross-on-Wye

  • Take care when walking on steep or uneven paths, especially near the river or in the forest.
  • Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Ross-on-Wye

  • If you’re planning to spend a lot of time outdoors, be sure to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Ross-on-Wye has several pharmacies for purchasing over-the-counter medications.

Getting to Ross-on-Wye: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ross-on-Wye is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some options for getting there:

Airports Near Ross-on-Wye

  • Bristol Airport: 35 miles away
  • Birmingham Airport: 66 miles away

Bus Stations Near Ross-on-Wye

  • Ross-on-Wye Bus Station: Located in the town center, this station serves several local and regional bus routes.
  • Hereford Bus Station: This station is about 16 miles from Ross-on-Wye and serves several national bus routes.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is Hereford, which is about 16 miles away.
  • Car: Ross-on-Wye is conveniently located near the M50 and A40 roads, making it easy to reach by car.

How to Get Around in Ross-on-Wye

Ross-on-Wye is a small town that’s easy to get around on foot. However, if you’re planning to explore the surrounding countryside or neighboring towns, it’s worth renting a car or taking public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Ross-on-Wye: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your adventure after visiting Ross-on-Wye, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Hereford: Explore the historic city center and visit Hereford Cathedral.
  • Monmouth: This scenic market town offers charming shops and dining options.
  • Brecon Beacons National Park: Just a short drive from Ross-on-Wye, the Brecon Beacons offers stunning scenery and a wide range of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Ross-on-Wye has something to offer everyone. With its breathtaking scenery, friendly locals, and rich cultural heritage, it’s no wonder that Ross-on-Wye is becoming an increasingly popular destination for travelers. With this comprehensive travel guide at your disposal, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip.
