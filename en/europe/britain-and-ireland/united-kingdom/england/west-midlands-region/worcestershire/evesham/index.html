Introduction to Evesham

Evesham is a charming market town located in Worcestershire, in the West Midlands region of England. The town is known for its picturesque countryside, charming architecture, and rich history. Many visitors come to Evesham to explore its cultural attractions, admire its scenic views, and indulge in its delicious local cuisine. Evesham is also an excellent base for exploring the wider region, including the Cotswolds and the Malvern Hills. In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Evesham, from places to visit to where to eat and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Evesham

Here is our list of must-visit places in Evesham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Almonry Museum: This charming museum is located in a 14th-century building and provides an insight into the history and culture of Evesham.
  • Evesham Abbey: The ruins of this abbey date back to the 8th century and are a fascinating place to explore.
  • The Regal Cinema: This art deco cinema has been beautifully restored and is a great place to catch a movie or enjoy a live performance.

Natural Attractions of Evesham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • River Avon: Take a stroll along the river and enjoy the beautiful scenery and wildlife.
  • Southern Hill: This hill offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and is an excellent spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Evesham Country Park: This park is a great place to enjoy a day out with the family, with plenty of activities to keep everyone entertained.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Evesham

Evesham is surrounded by beautiful countryside, making it an excellent destination for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Blossom Trail: This 45-mile trail takes you through some of the most beautiful orchards in Worcestershire.
  • The Evesham Vale Trail: This trail takes you through the stunning countryside surrounding Evesham, passing through charming villages and past historic landmarks.
  • The Cotswold Way: This famous 102-mile trail passes through Evesham and offers breathtaking views of the Cotswolds.

Best Things to Do in Evesham: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling solo, with friends, or with family, there is plenty to do in Evesham. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Enjoy a River Cruise: Take a leisurely cruise along the River Avon and enjoy the beautiful scenery and wildlife.
  • Visit the Evesham Bell Tower: This historic tower dates back to the 16th century and is a must-visit attraction in Evesham.
  • ** Explore the Cotswolds**: Evesham is a great base for exploring the Cotswolds, with its charming villages, rolling hills, and picturesque countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Evesham

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Evesham:

  • Day 1: Explore Evesham town and its cultural attractions, including the Almonry Museum and Evesham Abbey.
  • Day 2: Take a river cruise along the River Avon and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 3: Explore the beautiful countryside surrounding Evesham on foot or bike, following one of the many trails in the area.
  • Day 4: Visit nearby historic towns, including Worcester and Stratford-upon-Avon.
  • Day 5: Spend a day in the Cotswolds, visiting charming villages and taking in the stunning scenery.
  • Day 6: Visit the Regal Cinema and enjoy a movie or live performance.
  • Day 7: Relax in one of Evesham’s many parks or gardens, such as Evesham Country Park or the Abbey Gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Evesham

Evesham is known for its delicious local cuisine, with a focus on fresh, seasonal produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Evesham Asparagus: This local delicacy is only in season for a few weeks each year in May and June, so be sure to try it while you’re in town.
  • Pershore Plum Pudding: This traditional dessert is made from locally grown plums and is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.
  • Sausage and Mash: A British classic, this dish is made with locally sourced sausages and creamy mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Worcestershire Cider: This refreshing drink is made from locally grown apples and is a great way to sample the local produce.
  • Morgans Spiced Rum: This award-winning rum is distilled locally and has a unique blend of spices and flavors.

Where to Stay in Evesham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in and around Evesham to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Wood Norton Hotel: This luxurious hotel is a former hunting lodge and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Evesham Hotel: This 16th-century hotel is located in the heart of Evesham and offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Raphael Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in a converted barn and has a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Evesham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Evesham, here are some of the best products to look out for:

  • Evesham Asparagus: Pick up a bundle of fresh asparagus to sample the local delicacy for yourself.
  • Cotswold Lavender: This locally grown lavender is used in many of the region’s beauty products and makes a lovely gift.
  • Pershore Plum Jam: Made from locally grown plums, this jam is a delicious and uniquely Evesham souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Evesham

Evesham has a rich cultural heritage and many customs and traditions that are still observed today. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and take part in events and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Evesham

Evesham is a great destination to visit at any time of the year, with each season offering its own unique attractions. The town is particularly beautiful in the spring when the cherry blossoms are in bloom and in the autumn when the leaves change color.

Do’s and Don’ts of Evesham

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Evesham:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Evesham asparagus and Pershore plum pudding.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities in the area, including hiking and biking.
  • Visit nearby towns and attractions, including Worcester and Stratford-upon-Avon.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying some of the locally grown produce.
  • Miss out on the opportunity to explore the beautiful countryside surrounding Evesham.
  • Forget to bring your camera to capture the stunning scenery and picturesque architecture.

Safety and Health

Evesham is a safe and welcoming town, with low levels of crime and friendly locals. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unlit or isolated areas at night.

Safety in Evesham

Evesham is generally safe for visitors, but it is always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night. If you’re traveling alone or in an unfamiliar area, it’s best to stick to well-lit areas and avoid isolated places.

Health in Evesham

Evesham has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, should you need medical assistance during your stay. Visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance and take any necessary precautions before traveling, such as obtaining any necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Evesham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Evesham is easily accessible by car, train, or bus, with excellent transport links to other parts of the UK. Here’s how to get to Evesham:

  • By Car: Evesham is located just off the A46 and A44, making it easy to reach by car from other parts of the UK.
  • By Train: Evesham has its own train station, with regular services to Birmingham, Oxford, and London.
  • By Bus: There are regular bus services to Evesham from nearby towns and cities, including Worcester and Cheltenham.

Airports Near Evesham

The nearest international airports to Evesham are Birmingham International Airport, Bristol Airport, and London Heathrow Airport.

Bus Stations Near Evesham

The main bus station in Evesham is located in the town center and provides services to nearby towns and cities, including Worcester and Cheltenham.

Other Options

If you prefer not to drive or take public transport, there are several private car hire companies available in the area.

How to Get Around in Evesham

Evesham is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, there are also several options for getting around, including:

  • Buses: There are several local bus services in Evesham that provide transport to nearby towns and cities.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Evesham and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • Car Hire: Several car hire companies operate in Evesham, offering a range of vehicles for hire.

Where to Go Next from Evesham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Evesham is the perfect starting point for exploring nearby towns and attractions. Here are some ideas for day trips and excursions:

  • Worcester: This historic city is just a short drive or train ride away and offers plenty of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Stratford-upon-Avon: Birthplace of William Shakespeare, this charming town is a must-visit for literature fans.
  • The Cotswolds: This beautiful region is just a short drive from Evesham, with its picturesque villages, rolling hills, and stunning countryside.

Conclusion

Evesham is a delightful market town with plenty to offer visitors of all ages and interests. With its beautiful countryside, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine, it’s no wonder that Evesham is a popular destination for travelers from all over the world.
