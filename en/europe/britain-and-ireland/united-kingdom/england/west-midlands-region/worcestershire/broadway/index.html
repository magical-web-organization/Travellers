Introduction to Broadway

Welcome to Broadway, a picturesque village nestled in the heart of Worcestershire, West Midlands in England. Known for its striking honey-colored limestone buildings and scenic views of the Cotswold Hills, Broadway offers a delightful setting for a relaxing holiday or a romantic getaway. There is so much to explore and enjoy in Broadway, from cultural and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and gastronomic delights. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Broadway and make the most of your stay in this charming village.

List of Places to Visit in Broadway

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Broadway is rich in history and culture, with numerous museums and historical landmarks. Here are some top places to visit:

  • Broadway Museum and Art Gallery: A small museum located in a picturesque Tudor house, displaying exhibits on the history of Broadway and a collection of paintings and objects of art. Admission is free.
  • Broadway Tower: A historic folly and viewpoint on the Cotswold escarpment, built by Capability Brown in 1798. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • St. Michael and All Angels Church: An ancient church dating back to the 12th century, featuring a Norman doorway, a 13th-century font, and medieval stained glass windows.
  • Snowshill Manor and Garden: A National Trust property with a treasure trove of extraordinary treasures and curiosities collected by Charles Wade.

Natural Attractions of Broadway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Broadway is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, from the rolling hills of the Cotswolds to the idyllic meadows and gardens. You can enjoy various outdoor activities here, such as:

  • Cotswold Way: A popular walking route passing through Broadway, offering scenic views of the countryside and the village.
  • Broadway Tower Country Park: A sprawling park with walking trails, wildlife spotting, and a playground for children.
  • Broadway Lavender Farm: A fragrant farm where you can stroll through fields of lavender and buy products made from the flowers.
  • Kiftsgate Court Gardens: A stunning garden featuring rare plants and a beautiful water garden, located near Broadway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Broadway

If you enjoy hiking or biking, Broadway provides an abundance of routes with varying levels of difficulty. Some must-try routes are:

  • Broadway Circular Walk: A 5-mile walking route around the village, taking you through the Cotswold countryside, village lanes, and historic sites.
  • Cotswold Round: A longer walking route that takes you through several towns and villages in the Cotswolds, including Broadway.
  • Cotswold Line Cycle Route: A 44-mile route taking you through the Cotswolds, passing through Broadway.

Best Things to Do in Broadway: Activities and Tours

Broadway offers various activities and tours to suit your interests and preferences. Check out the following options:

  • Explore the village: Take a stroll through the village, admire the beautiful architecture, and explore the local shops and cafes.
  • Go on a ghost tour: Join a guided tour of Broadway’s haunted buildings and hear spooky stories from the village’s past.
  • Wine and dine: Treat yourself to local delicacies and sample some of the finest wines of the region, with numerous fine-dining restaurants and pubs in and around Broadway.
  • Visit nearby attractions: You can visit other nearby attractions, such as Warwick Castle, Stratford-Upon-Avon, and the Cheltenham Racecourse.

Week-Long Itinerary for Broadway

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Broadway:

Day 1: Explore Broadway village Day 2: Visit the Broadway Museum and Art Gallery, walk the Cotswold Way trail Day 3: Go on a ghost tour of Broadway, have lunch at a local pub Day 4: Visit Snowshill Manor and Garden, take a stroll around Kiftsgate Court Gardens Day 5: Take a bike trip on the Cotswold Line Cycle Route, stop for a picnic in the countryside Day 6: Head to Warwick Castle, explore the castle and its grounds Day 7: Visit Stratford-upon-Avon, Shakespeare’s birthplace

Food and Drink Scene of Broadway

Broadway has many food and beverage options to cater to all types of tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cotswold lamb and beef: Known for its delicious flavor, Cotswold lamb and beef are local specialties and must-try dishes.
  • Cream tea: A traditional British afternoon treat consisting of tea, scones, clotted cream, and jam.
  • Local cheese: The Cotswolds have a rich cheese-making tradition, and you can sample various types of cheese in and around Broadway.

Local Beverages

  • Cotswold gin: A small-batch gin made with locally foraged botanicals and fresh herbs, with a distinctive flavor.
  • Local ales: The Cotswolds have many traditional English pubs that serve ales brewed in-house or by nearby breweries.
  • English wine: The region is also renowned for its wines, particularly the sparkling varieties.

Where to Stay in Broadway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Broadway has numerous options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Dormy House: A stylish hotel with beautiful interiors, spa, and excellent dining options.
  • The Lygon Arms: A historic hotel with a charming atmosphere, spa, and a beautifully manicured garden.
  • The Swan Inn: A cozy pub with comfortable rooms, delicious food, and a warm ambience.
  • Broadway Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms, friendly staff, and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Broadway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some souvenirs and local products to remember your trip to Broadway. Here are some suggestions:

  • Cotswold honey: Produced by local beekeepers, Cotswold honey is known for its delicious and unique flavor.
  • Cotswold wool: The region has a long history of wool production, and you can find a variety of woolen products, such as knitted hats, gloves, and scarves.
  • Cotswold lavender products: From lavender soaps to dried flowers, you can find various lavender products in Broadway’s gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Broadway

Broadway has a rich cultural heritage and customs that visitors should respect and appreciate. Some local customs include:

  • Respect for nature: The Cotswolds are renowned for their natural beauty, and visitors should take care not to harm the flora and fauna of the region.
  • Conservation of historic buildings: Many of Broadway’s buildings are ancient and protected by law, and visitors should take care not to damage or disturb them.
  • Politeness and manners: The British are known for their politeness and manners, and visitors should be respectful and courteous to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Broadway

Broadway can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the months of May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. The village can get crowded during peak tourist seasons, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Broadway

Do:

  • Book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak seasons.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the Cotswold countryside and natural beauty.
  • Purchase local products and support local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or damage the environment.
  • Litter the streets or the countryside.
  • Ignore the traffic rules and regulations.
  • Disturb the local residents with loud noise or reckless behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Broadway

Broadway is a relatively safe village, with low crime rates. However, visitors should always be cautious and take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Keeping an eye on their belongings.
  • Avoiding dark and deserted areas at night.
  • Using reputable taxi services or public transportation.

Health in Broadway

Visitors to Broadway should take care of their health and well-being by:

  • Carrying necessary medications and first aid kits.
  • Drinking clean and safe water.
  • Following basic hygiene practices, such as washing hands regularly and using hand sanitizers.

Getting to Broadway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Broadway

The nearest airports to Broadway are Birmingham Airport and Bristol Airport, both around an hour’s drive from the village. Arriving visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from the airport to reach Broadway.

Bus Stations Near Broadway

There are several bus stations near Broadway, including Moreton-in-Marsh and Evesham. From these stations, visitors can take local buses or taxis to reach Broadway.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Broadway by car or bike, as the village is well-connected with major roads and highways.

How to Get Around in Broadway

Broadway is a small village that can easily be explored on foot, and there are many walking trails and lanes that offer scenic views of the countryside. Visitors can also rent bikes or take local buses to explore the village and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Broadway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Broadway is located within easy reach of many other charming towns and villages in the Cotswolds. Some popular destinations include:

  • Chipping Campden: A delightful town with a historic market hall and stunning architecture.
  • Bourton-on-the-Water: A picturesque village with an idyllic river flowing through it and numerous cafes and restaurants.
  • Oxford: Known for its prestigious university and beautiful architecture, Oxford is a must-see destination for any visitor to England.
  • Bath: A UNESCO World Heritage site, Bath is renowned for its Roman baths, Georgian architecture, and excellent shopping.

Conclusion

Broadway is a charming and picturesque village that offers a perfect retreat for anyone seeking a relaxing holiday or a romantic getaway. With its rich history, natural beauty, cultural attractions, and gastronomic delights, Broadway has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your visit to Broadway and make the most of your stay in this delightful village.
