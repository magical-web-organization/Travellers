Introduction to Stourport-on-Severn

Stourport-on-Severn, located in the county of Worcestershire in the West Midlands region of England, is a quaint town that is steeped in history. This charming town boasts a rich heritage, along with spectacular landscapes and great adventures for outdoor enthusiasts.

The town was built in the 18th century as a canal town. It boasts a unique canal system with an impressive selection of locks, which makes Stourport-on-Severn a significant destination for canal boat enthusiasts.

Stourport-on-Severn has much to offer, from museums, art galleries, and historical monuments to breathtakingly beautiful natural sceneries that attract visitors from across the globe.

List of Places to Visit in Stourport-on-Severn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a fan of history and culture, Stourport-on-Severn has plenty to offer, from museums filled with fascinating exhibits to awe-inspiring historical sites recalling the town’s past.

First on the list is the Museum of British Pewter, which has exhibits showcasing the town’s rich heritage of pewter making. Its impressive collection includes old pieces that date back to the 18th century.

Another significant attraction is Hartlebury Castle, which was the former home of the Bishop of Worcester. The castle’s State Rooms are open to the public, providing a glimpse into the luxurious lifestyles of the castle’s former residents.

Finally, a visit to St Bartholomew’s Church is a must. This 14th-century church is notable for its fantastic architecture, including intricate Gothic-style details.

Natural Attractions of Stourport-on-Severn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stourport-on-Severn is renowned for its natural beauty, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking to picnicking, there are plenty of fun outdoor activities to enjoy.

Wilden Marsh Nature Reserve is a great place for nature lovers, as it is home to a diverse array of wildlife species such as otters, badgers, and birds, including kingfishers and herons.

For some fun on the water, Stourport Canal Basins is an excellent spot for fishing and boating adventures. The calm waters of the canal make it a perfect place for a relaxed afternoon boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stourport-on-Severn

If you love hiking and biking, Stourport-on-Severn won’t disappoint. The town and its surroundings offer numerous trails and paths to explore, including:

  • The Staffordshire and Worcestershire Canal Walk
  • The Worcestershire Way
  • Kinver Edge Walk

All these hiking and biking routes provide stunning views of the Worcestershire countryside and are sure to please nature enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Stourport-on-Severn

Stourport-on-Severn is famous for its food and drink scene. Local cuisine here is fantastic, offering traditional English comfort food and beverages with a local twist.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Balti: A phenomenal curry dish that originates from nearby Birmingham.
  • Pork Scratchings: A crispy and highly addictive local snack that you won’t want to miss!
  • Puddings: The town boasts various dessert options that feature different types of fruity puddings and ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Local vineyards in the Worcestershire area produce delicious, award-winning wines.
  • Breweries: Take a tour of the local breweries to try amazing beers from local producers.

Where to Stay in Stourport-on-Severn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re traveling solo or with family, Stourport-on-Severn has a wide array of accommodation options to suit your needs. Some of the luxurious hotels include The Wharf, The Granary, and The Bird in Hand. There are also affordable options like The Baldwin Residence and The Old Rectory House.

What to Buy in Stourport-on-Severn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taking a little piece of Stourport-on-Severn home with you is easy with the town’s variety of unique souvenir and gift shops. Some unique items to consider buying include:

  • Furniture: Stourport-on-Severn also has several shops selling locally crafted furniture that are both attractive and highly functional.

Local Culture and Customs of Stourport-on-Severn

Stourport-on-Severn is a relatively relaxed town, known for its friendly residents and stunning natural beauty. The town is also steeped in history, and visitors are urged to be respectful of the history and culture.

When in proximity to churches, it is advisable to dress modestly as a sign of respect. Also, don’t forget to be mindful of local customs, which include being courteous and polite and tidying up after oneself in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Stourport-on-Severn

The best time of the year to visit Stourport-on-Severn is during the warm and sunny months between May and October. The town’s outdoor activities such as hiking and canal boats are best experienced in good weather.
