Introduction to Bromsgrove

Welcome to Bromsgrove, a charming town located in Worcestershire, West Midlands, England, and known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. This travel guide will help you discover the top things to do in Bromsgrove, including the best attractions, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, accommodations, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Bromsgrove

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Avoncroft Museum of Historic Buildings: This museum features a collection of historical buildings that date back to the 14th century, including a windmill, church, and Tudor house.
  • Bromsgrove Museum: Discover the town’s fascinating history, from the Anglo-Saxon era to modern-day, through interactive exhibits, videos, and photos.
  • Hanbury Hall: This National Trust property showcases a beautiful 18th-century mansion, park, and gardens, and hosts regular events and activities.

Natural Attractions of Bromsgrove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lickey Hills Country Park: This park offers plenty of walking trails, playgrounds, and spectacular views of the area’s rolling hills and valleys.
  • Sanders Park: This large park features a lake, picnic areas, tennis courts, and a skatepark, making it ideal for a family day out.
  • Worcester and Birmingham Canal: Take a walk along this canal, which stretches for more than 30 miles and passes through scenic countryside and historic towns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bromsgrove

  • The Worcestershire Way: This 31-mile trail leads through stunning landscapes and picturesque villages, including Bromsgrove.
  • Lickey Hills Circular Walk: This 5.5-mile circular route takes you through woodlands, fields, and open spaces, and provides stunning views of the Worcestershire countryside.
  • Wyre Forest Discovery Trail: This 11-mile trail immerses you in the heart of the Wyre Forest, where you can observe wildlife, explore ancient woodlands, and admire panoramic views of the Severn Valley and beyond.

Best Things to Do in Bromsgrove: Activities and Tours

  • Go Ape: This popular tourist attraction features a challenging treetop adventure course, a zip line, and other outdoor activities.
  • Artrix: This arts venue hosts a wide range of cultural events, including concerts, plays, films, and exhibitions.
  • Visit Country Markets Bromsgrove: This farmers market offers fresh local produce, arts and crafts, and live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bromsgrove

Food and Drink Scene of Bromsgrove

Must-Try Dishes

  • Balti curry: This spicy dish originated in Birmingham, and is now a favorite across the UK.
  • Worcestershire sauce: This popular sauce was first made in Worcester in the 19th century and is now used globally in various cuisines.
  • Fish and chips: A classic British takeaway, typically consisting of battered fish and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Pubs in Bromsgrove: Enjoy a pint of locally brewed beer, at any of the historic pubs in town.
  • Tea: Take tea and cake at one of Bromsgrove’s numerous tea rooms.

Where to Stay in Bromsgrove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Premier Inn Bromsgrove Central Hotel: Affordable and centrally located hotel with comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • The Queens Head Inn: This historic inn offers cozy rooms, traditional pub food, and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Hillscourt: This country house hotel offers stunning views of the Worcestershire countryside and excellent facilities, including a restaurant and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Bromsgrove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Worcestershire sauce: Bring home a bottle of the famous Worcestershire sauce, which has been made in the area since the 19th century.
  • Artrix souvenirs: Purchase unique locally made artworks and crafts at Artrix.
  • Local produce: Visit Country Markets Bromsgrove to buy fresh local produce, including vegetables, fruits, and cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Bromsgrove

  • Greetings: In Bromsgrove, a simple ‘hello’, coupled with a smile, is a polite greeting.
  • Tipping: It is customary to tip at a rate of 10-15% at restaurants and cafes in Bromsgrove.
  • Queuing: The British are known for their love of queuing, so be prepared to stand in line and wait your turn.

Best Time to Visit Bromsgrove

The best time to visit Bromsgrove is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is sunny and warm, and the town’s outdoor attractions are at their best. Alternatively, visit during spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November) when the crowds are thinner, and nature is exquisitely beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bromsgrove

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including a pint of locally brewed beer.
  • Do respect the local customs and cultural heritage of the town.
  • Do dress appropriately for the weather condition while taking outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of military sites or buildings without permission.
  • Don’t be rowdy or disruptive when visiting pubs, clubs, or restaurants in Bromsgrove.
  • Don’t forget to check if a place is dog friendly before taking your furry friend.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bromsgrove

Bromsgrove is a safe town to visit, but as with any place, visitors should keep their valuables secure and be aware of their surroundings when out and about.

Health in Bromsgrove

Visitors to Bromsgrove will find a range of medical facilities, including GP practices, dentists, and a hospital. It is always a good idea to purchase travel insurance before visiting Bromsgrove.

Getting to Bromsgrove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bromsgrove

  • Birmingham Airport: Located approximately 22 miles from Bromsgrove and serving over 140 destinations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Bromsgrove

  • Bromsgrove Bus Station: This bus station is located in the heart of Bromsgrove and has regular services running to and from Birmingham and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Train: To get to Bromsgrove by train, catch a service from Birmingham New Street or Worcester.
  • Car: The M5 and M42 motorways both pass nearby Bromsgrove, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Bromsgrove

Walking or cycling is the best way to get around Bromsgrove town center, while using a car or bus is ideal for exploring the area further afield. Taxis are also easily accessible throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Bromsgrove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Worcester: Visit Worcester Cathedral and Discover the cultural attractions and history of this vibrant city.
  • Redditch: Explore the needle museum at Forge Mill, the ruins of Bordesley Abbey.
  • Malvern Hills: Enjoy breathtaking views from atop Worcestershire’s highest peak.
