Introduction to Worcestershire

Located in the West Midlands region of England, Worcestershire is a picturesque county that is steeped in history and dotted with stunning countryside scenery. It is also famous for its culinary heritage, including the iconic Worcestershire Sauce.

List of Places to Visit in Worcestershire

Here are some of the must-visit places in Worcestershire:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Worcester Cathedral
  • Royal Worcester Museum
  • The Commandery
  • The Tudor House Museum
  • The Elgar Birthplace Museum

Natural Attractions of Worcestershire: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Malvern Hills
  • Clent Hills
  • Wyre Forest
  • Avoncroft Museum of Historic Buildings
  • West Midlands Safari Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Worcestershire

Worcestershire has some fantastic trails, including:

  • The North Worcestershire Path

Best Things to Do in Worcestershire: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fantastic things to do in Worcestershire, consider the following:

  • Explore the Worcestershire countryside on a hot air balloon ride.
  • Head to the West Midlands Safari Park for an unforgettable animal encounter.
  • Climb to the top of the Malvern Hills for breathtaking views.
  • Visit the Worcester Racecourse for a thrilling day out.
  • Take a city tour of Worcester to learn more about the county’s rich history.

Local Culture and Customs of Worcestershire

The people of Worcestershire take pride in their unique heritage, including their famous culinary delights. They are also a friendly and welcoming bunch who are always ready to lend a hand or strike up a conversation.

Best Time to Visit Worcestershire

The best time to visit Worcestershire is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the countryside is lush and green. Another great time to visit is during the Christmas markets in November and December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Worcestershire

Follow these do’s and don’ts to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Worcestershire:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the countryside
  • Visit the local markets and shops
  • Explore the rich history of the county

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring appropriate walking shoes for the countryside
  • Litter in the countryside
  • Be too loud and rowdy in residential areas
  • Forget to pack a raincoat or umbrella

Safety and Health

Safety in Worcestershire

Worcestershire is a relatively safe place for tourists, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas, and avoid walking alone in the countryside after dark.

Health in Worcestershire

The healthcare system in Worcestershire is excellent, with a wide range of NHS and private medical facilities available to visitors. Make sure you have medical insurance before you travel.
