Introduction to Upton-upon-Severn

Upton-upon-Severn is a small town situated on the banks of the River Severn in Worcestershire, West Midlands, England. The town was founded in the 7th century, and it has a rich history and culture. Upton-upon-Severn is famous for its Georgian architecture, beautiful river-side walks, and picturesque countryside. The town attracts a variety of tourists throughout the year, especially during the summer months when various events and festivals are held.

List of Places to Visit in Upton-upon-Severn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Upton-upon-Severn has plenty of places to visit. Here are some of the must-see attractions in the town:

  • Upton Marina - A picturesque harbor along the River Severn where you can go for a boat tour or arrange a fishing trip.
  • Upton-upon-Severn Heritage Centre - A small museum that showcases the history of the town, including its famous residents and notable events.
  • The Pepperpot - A medieval tower that was once a part of a church and now serves as an observation tower. From the top, you can enjoy a panoramic view of the entire town.

Natural Attractions of Upton-upon-Severn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Upton-upon-Severn has plenty to offer. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in the town:

  • Severn Way Walk - A scenic walking trail that runs along the River Severn. The trail offers stunning views of the river, surrounding countryside, and Upton-upon-Severn town.
  • Malvern Hills - A range of hills located near Upton-upon-Severn that is popular among hikers and mountain bikers. The hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Upton Ham Nature Reserve - A wetland area located on the banks of River Severn that is home to a variety of wildlife, including birds and otters. The reserve offers walking trails and bird-watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upton-upon-Severn

If you are an adventure lover, Upton-upon-Severn has plenty of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes in the town:

  • Severn Way Walk - A 210-mile-long walking trail that runs along the River Severn. The trail starts at the source of the river in Powys, Wales, and ends at the Severn Estuary in Gloucestershire, England. Upton-upon-Severn is one of the key stops along the trail.
  • Malvern Hills - A range of hills located near Upton-upon-Severn that offers plenty of hiking and mountain biking trails. The trails range from easy to difficult and offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Upton Ham Nature Reserve - The reserve has several walking trails that offer scenic views of the wetlands and the River Severn. The trails are suitable for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Upton-upon-Severn: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun things to do in Upton-upon-Severn. Here are some of the best activities and tours in the town:

  • Upton Folk Festival - An annual folk festival that celebrates traditional folk music and dance. The festival takes place in various venues across the town and attracts visitors from all over the country.
  • Ghost Walks - A spooky walking tour that takes visitors through the town’s haunted streets and alleys. The tour is led by an experienced guide who shares the town’s ghost stories and legends.
