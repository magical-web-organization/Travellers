Introduction to Uttoxeter

Nestled in the heart of Staffordshire, Uttoxeter is a picturesque market town that offers a true taste of England. With a rich history dating back to the Roman Empire, Uttoxeter is a town that has a lot to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in exploring local history, enjoying outdoor activities, or simply relaxing and taking in the beautiful scenery, Uttoxeter has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Uttoxeter

Uttoxeter is a town that’s brimming with fascinating attractions, from world-renowned historic landmarks to hidden gems waiting to be discovered. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Uttoxeter:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Uttoxeter Museum: This fascinating museum is dedicated to the history of Uttoxeter and features exhibits that explore the town’s Roman, Saxon, and medieval past.
  • Tutbury Castle: This former royal palace dates back to the 11th century and offers visitors the chance to explore its ruins and learn about its intriguing history.
  • Alton Towers Resort: This world-famous theme park is located just a short drive from Uttoxeter and is known for its thrilling rides and family-friendly attractions.

Natural Attractions of Uttoxeter: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dove Valley Way: This scenic walking trail runs for 45 miles through the stunning countryside surrounding Uttoxeter, offering breathtaking views and the chance to spot local wildlife.
  • The Peak District National Park: One of England’s most iconic natural attractions, the Peak District is located just a short drive from Uttoxeter and is characterized by its rolling hills, rugged landscapes, and picturesque villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uttoxeter

If you’re looking for outdoor activities in Uttoxeter, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • The Staffordshire Way: This 93-mile long-distance trail runs through some of Staffordshire’s most beautiful countryside, including parts of the Peak District National Park.
  • Cannock Chase: This designated Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities, with trails ranging from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Uttoxeter: Activities and Tours

From historic landmarks to family-friendly attractions, Uttoxeter has something to offer visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Uttoxeter:

  • Explore Uttoxeter Racecourse: One of the town’s most iconic attractions, Uttoxeter Racecourse hosts regular horse racing events throughout the year, as well as other events such as music festivals.
  • Visit Uttoxeter Market: This bustling market takes place every Wednesday and Saturday and is a great place to pick up local produce, crafts, and souvenirs.
  • Take a Tour of the Local Breweries: Uttoxeter is home to several local breweries, including the award-winning Marston’s Brewery, which offer tours and tastings for visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Uttoxeter

No visit to Uttoxeter would be complete without sampling some of the town’s local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to look out for:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Staffordshire Oatcakes: Similar to a pancake, Staffordshire Oatcakes are a local delicacy made with oats and served with a variety of fillings, such as cheese, bacon, or mushrooms.
  • Bakewell Tart: This famous dessert originated in the nearby town of Bakewell and is made with a shortcrust pastry base, almond filling, and raspberry jam.
  • Potteries Pea Soup: A hearty soup made with split peas, ham, and vegetables, this dish is a local favorite in Staffordshire.

Local Beverages

  • Marston’s Beer: A local brewery that’s been producing beer since 1834, Marston’s is known for its range of real ales and craft beers.
  • Birchall’s Tea: This family-run tea company has been producing high-quality teas since 1872 and offers a range of blends to suit all tastes.
  • Sloe Gin: Made by steeping sloe berries in gin, this traditional English beverage has a sweet and tart flavor and is often served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Uttoxeter: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly options to luxurious hotels, there are plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets in Uttoxeter. Here are some of the best places to stay in Uttoxeter:

  • The Bank House: This historic guesthouse is located in the heart of Uttoxeter and offers beautifully furnished rooms, a restaurant serving locally sourced food, and a cozy bar.
  • Premier Inn Uttoxeter: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and easy access to local attractions such as Alton Towers Resort.
  • Alton Towers Hotel: Located just a short drive from Uttoxeter, this family-friendly hotel is themed around the famous theme park and offers a range of rooms and suites to suit all budgets.

What to Buy in Uttoxeter: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Uttoxeter, here are some local products to look out for:

  • Staffordshire Pottery: From traditional teapots to modern sculptures, Staffordshire is known for its high-quality pottery and ceramic products.
  • Local Honey: Beeswax from local hives is used to produce a range of delicious honey products, including infused honey and honeycomb.
  • Marmalade: Made with locally grown Seville oranges, Staffordshire marmalade is a tangy and delicious spread that’s perfect for breakfast.

Best Time to Visit Uttoxeter

Uttoxeter is a year-round destination, with something to see and do in every season. However, the best time to visit depends on your preferences:

  • Summer: The weather is warm and sunny, making it a great time for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.
  • Autumn: The leaves change color and the landscape takes on a beautiful golden hue, making it an ideal time for scenic walks and drives.
  • Winter: The town has a cozy and festive atmosphere during the winter months, with Christmas markets and other seasonal events taking place.
  • Spring: Flowers begin to bloom and the countryside comes to life, making it a great time to explore Uttoxeter’s natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Uttoxeter is a safe and welcoming town, but it’s always important to take care of your health and wellbeing during your visit. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Uttoxeter

  • Keep Valuables Safe: Like any tourist destination, Uttoxeter can attract pickpockets and other criminals, so be sure to keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be Aware of Traffic: Uttoxeter can be busy, especially during peak tourist seasons, so make sure to exercise caution when crossing roads and obey traffic signals.
  • Be Prepared for Outdoor Activities: If you’re planning to hike or bike in the local countryside, make sure to wear appropriate clothing and footwear and stay hydrated.

Health in Uttoxeter

  • Stay Hydrated: Especially during the summer months, it’s important to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Wear Sunscreen: The sun can be strong, even on cloudy days, so make sure to wear sunscreen and protect your skin.
  • Be Prepared for Allergies: If you suffer from allergies, be sure to bring any necessary medications with you and be mindful of triggers such as pollen and dust.

Getting to Uttoxeter: Transportation Options and Logistics

Uttoxeter is conveniently located and easy to reach by a variety of transportation options. Here’s what you need to know:

  • By Car: Uttoxeter is easily accessible from the M1 and M6 motorways, which connect the town to major cities such as Birmingham and Manchester.
  • By Train: Uttoxeter Train Station is located in the town center and provides regular services to cities such as Birmingham and Derby.
  • By Bus: National Express and other bus companies provide regular services to Uttoxeter from major cities such as London and Manchester.

Airports near Uttoxeter

  • Birmingham Airport: Located approximately 35 miles from Uttoxeter, Birmingham Airport is the closest major airport and offers flights to a range of domestic and international destinations.
  • Manchester Airport: Located approximately 45 miles from Uttoxeter, Manchester Airport is another convenient option with regular flights to destinations throughout the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations near Uttoxeter

  • Uttoxeter Bus Station: Located in the town center, Uttoxeter Bus Station provides connections to nearby towns and cities such as Stoke-on-Trent and Burton-upon-Trent.
  • Stafford Bus Station: Located approximately 15 miles from Uttoxeter, Stafford Bus Station provides connections to a range of destinations throughout the UK.

Other Options

  • By Taxi: Local taxi companies provide a convenient and flexible option for getting around Uttoxeter and the surrounding areas.
  • By Bicycle: Uttoxeter is a cycle-friendly town, with a range of bike rental options and scenic routes to explore.
