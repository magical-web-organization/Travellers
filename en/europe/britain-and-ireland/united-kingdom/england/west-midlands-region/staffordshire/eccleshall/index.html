Introduction to Eccleshall

Nestled in the heart of Staffordshire, Eccleshall is a quaint and charming market town that offers visitors a glimpse into England’s rich history and vibrant culture. The town is known for its medieval architecture, stunning natural scenery, and friendly locals, making it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the West Midlands region of England.

List of Places to Visit in Eccleshall

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Eccleshall has plenty to offer visitors of all interests. Here are some of the top places to visit and things to do in Eccleshall:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eccleshall Castle: One of the town’s most iconic landmarks, Eccleshall Castle was built in the 13th century and is now a Grade I listed building. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle and admire its stunning architecture and medieval features.
  • St. Mary’s Church: This historic church dates back to the 12th century and is known for its beautiful stained glass windows and unique architectural features.
  • The Ancient High House: Located in the neighboring town of Stafford, the Ancient High House is a Tudor-style building that houses a museum dedicated to the history of the town and region.

Natural Attractions of Eccleshall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sugnall Walled Garden: This 18th-century walled garden is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the town, with beautiful gardens and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Eccleshall Circular Walk: This 7-mile walking route takes visitors on a scenic journey through the town and surrounding countryside, with breathtaking views and historical landmarks along the way.
  • Cannock Chase: Just a short drive from Eccleshall, Cannock Chase is a beautiful nature reserve with miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as breathtaking views of the Staffordshire countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eccleshall

Eccleshall is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Eccleshall Circular Walk: This 7-mile walking route takes visitors on a scenic journey through the town and surrounding countryside, with breathtaking views and historical landmarks along the way.
  • Cannock Chase: Just a short drive from Eccleshall, Cannock Chase is a beautiful nature reserve with miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as breathtaking views of the Staffordshire countryside.
  • The Staffordshire Way: This long-distance walking route covers 92 miles of the Staffordshire countryside, with sections that pass through Eccleshall and nearby towns.

Best Things to Do in Eccleshall: Activities and Tours

  • Eccleshall Farmers Market: Held on the fourth Saturday of every month, this popular farmers market offers visitors the chance to sample local products and meet local farmers and producers.
  • Eccleshall Theatre Company: This amateur theatre group hosts regular performances throughout the year, with a range of shows and events to suit all ages and interests.
  • Eccleshall Wine Club: This local wine club hosts regular tastings and events, giving visitors the chance to sample some of the region’s finest wines and learn more about the local wine culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eccleshall

For visitors who want to make the most of their time in Eccleshall, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Eccleshall Castle and St. Mary’s Church
  • Day 2: Explore the local hiking and biking routes, including the Eccleshall Circular Walk and Cannock Chase
  • Day 3: Visit the Ancient High House in nearby Stafford and take a guided tour of the museum
  • Day 4: Spend the day exploring the town’s local shops and markets, including the Eccleshall Farmers Market
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Alton Towers theme park for some thrills and excitement
  • Day 6: Visit Sugnall Walled Garden for a peaceful afternoon in nature
  • Day 7: Attend a performance at the Eccleshall Theatre Company or a tasting at the Eccleshall Wine Club

Food and Drink Scene of Eccleshall

Eccleshall is known for its vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local restaurants, cafes, and pubs serving up delicious meals and refreshing drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Eccleshall:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Staffordshire oatcakes: These delicious pancakes are a local specialty and are typically served with savory fillings like bacon and cheese.
  • Staffordshire cheese: Made with milk from local Staffordshire cows, this cheese is a must-try for anyone visiting the region.
  • Eccleshall Jockey: This signature dish combines local steak with cheese and onion, making for a hearty and delicious meal.

Local Beverages

  • Lymestone Brewery: This local brewery is known for its award-winning ales and beers, which can be found in many of Eccleshall’s pubs and bars.
  • Staffordshire gin: Made with local botanicals and flavors, Staffordshire gin is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Eccleshall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eccleshall offers a range of accommodations to suit all needs and budgets, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top options for visitors:

  • The Royal Oak: This historic inn offers cozy rooms and a lively pub atmosphere, with a range of local beers and traditional pub food.
  • The Swan Hotel: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers luxurious rooms and amenities, including an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • The Coach House: This charming bed and breakfast is located just outside of Eccleshall and offers a peaceful retreat for visitors looking to escape the bustle of the town.

What to Buy in Eccleshall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to bring home a piece of Eccleshall, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to look out for:

  • Staffordshire pottery: With a long history of pottery-making, Staffordshire is known for its beautiful and unique ceramics, which can be found in many of the town’s shops and markets.
  • Local cheeses: Staffordshire cheese is a must-try for any foodie, and many local shops and markets sell a variety of cheeses made in the region.
  • Eccleshall Ale Trail map: This fun and informative map is a great souvenir for beer lovers, highlighting the town’s top pubs and breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Eccleshall

Eccleshall is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who take pride in their town’s rich history and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and etiquette, including:

  • Saying “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals
  • Dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites or cultural events
  • Being mindful of noise and disturbances, especially in residential areas

Best Time to Visit Eccleshall

Eccleshall can be enjoyed year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June - August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor events and festivals take place. The town is also particularly charming during the holiday season, with festive lights and decorations adorning the streets and buildings.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore the town’s natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities

Safety and Health

Safety in Eccleshall

Eccleshall is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Eccleshall

Eccleshall has a range of medical facilities and services, including hospitals and clinics, pharmacies, and general practitioners. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them and to purchase comprehensive travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Eccleshall: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eccleshall

The closest airport to Eccleshall is Birmingham International Airport, which is located approximately 40 miles away. Other nearby airports include Manchester Airport (72 miles away) and East Midlands Airport (45 miles away).

Bus Stations Near Eccleshall

Eccleshall is served by several local and regional bus routes, with stops in the town center and surrounding areas. Buses run regularly throughout the day, making it easy to get around the town and nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Eccleshall by train, with the nearest train stations located in nearby Stafford and Crewe. Taxis and private car hire services are also available for those who prefer more personalized transportation.

How to Get Around in Eccleshall

Eccleshall is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. For those who prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies located in the town, as well as ample parking throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Eccleshall: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eccleshall is located in the heart of the West Midlands region, making it an ideal base for exploring nearby towns and attractions. Here are some of the top destinations to visit from Eccleshall:

  • Stafford: The county town of Staffordshire, this historic town is known for its beautiful architecture, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Alton Towers: This popular theme park is just a short drive from Eccleshall and offers a range of thrilling rides and attractions for visitors of all ages.
  • Birmingham: The second-largest city in England, Birmingham is a bustling metropolis with a rich history and vibrant cultural scene, including museums, galleries, and theaters.

Conclusion

Eccleshall is a charming and picturesque town that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures and cultural attractions to delicious local cuisine and friendly locals. Whether you’re exploring the town for a weekend or a week, be sure to take advantage of all the town has to offer and immerse yourself in the unique culture and traditions of Staffordshire.
