Introduction to Hammersmith and Fulham

Hammersmith and Fulham is a vibrant borough located in West London. It is home to some of the city’s most iconic landmarks, cultural attractions, and outdoor spaces. This guide will help you explore the best of Hammersmith and Fulham, from its museums and historical sites to its hiking and biking routes. You’ll also discover where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around this bustling borough.

List of Places to Visit in Hammersmith and Fulham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Design Museum: This museum showcases works of design from different periods and disciplines, including architecture, fashion, and technology. The museum is housed in a converted 1960s building and features a range of temporary and permanent exhibitions.

  • Fulham Palace: This historical site was the residence of the Bishop of London for over 1300 years. Visitors can explore the palace’s gardens, museum, and chapel, and learn about the history of this important building.

  • Leighton House Museum: The former home and studio of the Victorian artist Frederic, Lord Leighton, this museum houses an extensive collection of his works, as well as works by other Victorian artists. The house and gardens are also notable for their Arabian Nights themed decor.

Natural Attractions of Hammersmith and Fulham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ravenscourt Park: This popular park offers green spaces, recreational facilities, and a range of events and activities year-round. Visitors can enjoy a picnic, watch a performance at the open-air theatre, or stroll through the gardens.

  • Bishop’s Park and Fulham Palace Meadows: These two green spaces, situated on either side of the Thames, offer walking paths, playgrounds, and stunning riverside views.

  • Thames Path: This 184-mile-long walking trail runs along the Thames, and offers walkers a chance to explore some of London’s most scenic locations, including Hammersmith and Fulham.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hammersmith and Fulham

  • Hammersmith Bridge to Barnes: This 2-mile walk takes visitors along the Thames, passing through some of Fulham’s most picturesque neighborhoods.

  • Wormwood Scrubs: This large open space in the north of the borough offers visitors the chance to walk, jog or cycle along the park’s trail network, or simply enjoy a picnic with friends.

Best Things to Do in Hammersmith and Fulham: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour on the Thames to see some of London’s most famous landmarks, including the Tower of London, the London Eye, and the Houses of Parliament.

  • Westfield Shopping Centre: This massive shopping center in Shepherd’s Bush is home to over 300 stores, as well as restaurants, bars, and a cinema.

  • The Apollo Theatre: This historic venue hosts a range of performances, including music, comedy, dance, and theater.

Food and Drink Scene of Hammersmith and Fulham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: This British classic is available at many pubs and restaurants around Hammersmith and Fulham.

  • Sunday Roast: Enjoy a traditional Sunday roast with all the trimmings at one of the borough’s many traditional pubs.

  • Brick Lane Curry: Head to Brick Lane for a delicious curry and experience some of London’s best Indian food.

Local Beverages

  • Fuller’s London Pride Beer: This award-winning beer is brewed in Chiswick, in the borough of Hounslow, and can be found in many pubs and bars around Hammersmith and Fulham.

  • Big Ben’s Ginger Beer: This locally brewed ginger beer is available at various bars and restaurants around the borough.

Where to Stay in Hammersmith and Fulham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton London Olympia: This hotel is located close to Kensington Olympia Station and offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant.

  • YHA London Earl’s Court Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located close to Earl’s Court Station and offers dorms and private rooms.

  • LUMA Concept Hotel London Hammersmith: This stylish hotel is located close to the Hammersmith Apollo and offers a range of amenities, including a bar and fitness center.

What to Buy in Hammersmith and Fulham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Cards: Visit one of the many independent stationery shops in Hammersmith and Fulham to find unique, handmade cards and gifts.

  • Local Artwork: The borough is home to many artists and galleries, where visitors can find unique paintings, photographs, and prints.

  • West London Honey: This local honey is produced by beekeepers in West London and is available at various farmers’ markets and food stores in the borough.

Local Culture and Customs of Hammersmith and Fulham

  • Football: Football is a popular sport in Hammersmith and Fulham, and the borough is home to two famous football clubs, Fulham and Queens Park Rangers.

  • Music and Theatre: The borough is well-known for its music and theatre scene and is home to venues such as the Hammersmith Apollo and the Bush Theatre.

Best Time to Visit Hammersmith and Fulham

The best time to visit Hammersmith and Fulham is in the spring or summer, when the weather is mild and there are many outdoor events and activities. However, the borough is a vibrant destination year-round, with plenty of indoor attractions and cultural events in the fall and winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hammersmith and Fulham

Do:

  • Do explore the borough’s many green spaces and outdoor attractions.

  • Do try some of the locally brewed beer and other beverages.

  • Do visit the design museum and other cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the chance to explore the Thames Path and other outdoor adventures in the area.

  • Don’t forget to respect local customs, such as standing on the right side of the escalator in tube stations.

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the borough.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hammersmith and Fulham

Hammersmith and Fulham is a relatively safe borough, but visitors should take regular precautions against theft and pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Hammersmith and Fulham

Visitors to Hammersmith and Fulham should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations and travel insurance before arriving. In case of an emergency, visitors can access the National Health Service (NHS) for medical care.

Getting to Hammersmith and Fulham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hammersmith and Fulham

  • Heathrow Airport: This major airport is located only a few miles from Hammersmith and Fulham, and can be reached via tube, train, or taxi.

  • Gatwick Airport: Gatwick is further away from Hammersmith and Fulham than Heathrow, but visitors can still reach the borough via train or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Hammersmith and Fulham

  • Hammersmith Bus Station: This major bus station has connections to many destinations in and around London.

  • Shepherd’s Bush Bus Station: The second-largest bus station in the borough, also provides connections to other parts of London.

Other Options

  • Public Transport: The borough is well-served by the London Underground, with stations on several lines, including the Piccadilly Line, the Hammersmith and City Line, and the District Line.

  • Taxis and Private Cars: Ride-hailing services and taxis are widely available in Hammersmith and Fulham.

How to Get Around in Hammersmith and Fulham

The best way to get around Hammersmith and Fulham is by foot, by bike or by public transport. The borough is relatively compact, and many of its main attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Hammersmith and Fulham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Richmond

This quaint town on the Thames is known for its charming countryside walks, historic attractions, and Michelin-starred restaurants.

Notting Hill

This colorful neighborhood is famous for its market, carnival, and charming streets lined with pastel-colored houses.

Conclusion

Hammersmith and Fulham is a must-visit destination in London, with its rich history, diverse cultural attractions, and beautiful outdoor spaces. Whether you want to explore the borough’s museums, walk along the Thames Path, or enjoy a pint at a local pub, Hammersmith and Fulham has something for everyone.
