Introduction to East

East London is an exciting and vibrant part of the city, offering a diverse range of attractions for visitors to explore. Known for its trendy neighborhoods, multicultural atmosphere, and flourishing arts scene, East London is a great destination for those looking to discover a different side to the city.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in East London. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, you’ll find plenty to keep you busy in this fascinating part of the city.

List of Places to Visit in East

Here are some of the most popular places to visit in East London:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of London Docklands: This fascinating museum offers a glimpse into London’s past, showcasing the city’s history as a port and industrial hub.
  • Tower of London: One of the most iconic historical sites in the city, the Tower of London has stood for over 900 years and is home to the famous Crown Jewels.
  • Victoria and Albert Museum of Childhood: This unique museum is dedicated to the history of childhood, featuring toys, games, and other artifacts from throughout history.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park: Built for the 2012 Olympic Games, this sprawling park offers a range of activities, including cycling, jogging, and swimming.
  • Canary Wharf: This bustling business district is also home to some of East London’s best restaurants, shops, and events.
  • Greenwich Park: Located in the heart of Greenwich, this beautiful park offers stunning views of the city skyline and is home to the Royal Observatory.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East London is home to many beautiful hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Lea Valley Walk: This scenic trail follows the River Lea for 18 miles, passing through urban and rural areas.
  • Lee Valley VeloPark: This world-class cycling facility offers a range of indoor and outdoor tracks for cyclists of all levels.
  • Abney Park Cemetery: Although technically a cemetery, Abney Park is a beautiful green space with winding paths and tree-lined avenues, perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

Here are a few of the best things to do in East London:

  • Brick Lane Market: This vibrant street market is famous for its vintage clothing, street food, and eclectic atmosphere.
  • Shoreditch Street Art Tours: Take a walking tour of the colorful street art in Shoreditch, guided by a local artist.
  • Bow Arts Trust: This arts organization offers workshops, exhibitions, and events featuring local artists.

Food and Drink Scene of East

East London is famous for its diverse food and drink scene, which includes everything from street food to Michelin-starred restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bangladeshi Cuisine: East London is home to a large Bangladeshi community, which has resulted in a thriving restaurant scene.
  • English Breakfast: You can’t visit London without trying a traditional English breakfast, complete with bacon, eggs, sausage, and baked beans.
  • Pie and Mash: This classic British dish is a staple of East London cuisine. Try it at one of the many traditional pie and mash shops in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: East London is home to many craft breweries, including Beavertown, Redemption Brewing, and Crate Brewery.
  • Cocktails: The area is also known for its vibrant cocktail scene, with many trendy bars offering creative drinks made with local ingredients.
  • Gin: East London has a long history of gin production, and you can still visit the historic distilleries that produce this iconic beverage.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in East London to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few of the best:

  • The Ace Hotel: This trendy hotel in Shoreditch has a 24-hour gym, rooftop bar, and on-site restaurant.
  • YHA London St Pancras: This budget hostel is located near King’s Cross station and offers private and shared rooms.
  • The Boundary: This boutique hotel in Shoreditch features stylish rooms, a rooftop bar, and a restaurant serving British and French cuisine.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home a unique souvenir from your trip to East London, here are a few ideas:

  • Vintage Clothing: The area is known for its many vintage clothing shops, where you can find everything from retro jackets to classic band t-shirts.
  • Street Art Prints: Many of the street artists in East London sell prints of their work, making it easy to take home a piece of the vibrant local art scene.
  • Local Beer and Gin: East London is home to many craft breweries and gin distilleries, making it a great place to pick up some unique drinks to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East London is known for its multicultural atmosphere, and visitors can expect to encounter a diverse range of people, languages, and customs. Some of the local customs to be aware of include:

  • Queuing: The British take queuing very seriously, so be prepared to wait your turn in line.
  • Tipping: It’s customary to leave a tip of around 10% in restaurants and bars, but it’s not expected in all situations.
  • Respect for Diversity: East London is home to a wide range of cultures and religions, and visitors should be respectful of these differences.

Best Time to Visit East

East London can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting East London:

Do:

  • Try the Street Food: East London is home to many fantastic street food vendors, offering a range of international cuisines.
  • Explore the Arts Scene: East London is famous for its thriving arts scene, so be sure to explore the many galleries, exhibitions, and events on offer.
  • Visit Local Markets: East London is home to many vibrant markets, selling everything from vintage clothing to fresh produce.

Don’t:

  • Forget to Mind the Gap: When using the London Underground, make sure to mind the gap between the train and the platform.
  • Waste Food: In Britain, wasting food is considered impolite, so try to finish everything on your plate.
  • Be Too Loud: While London is a lively and vibrant city, it’s also important to be considerate of your fellow travelers and not be too loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East London is generally considered safe for visitors, but it’s important to take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. This includes being aware of your surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended in public places.

Health in East

There are many healthcare facilities in East London, but visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance before they arrive. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you, as well as a first aid kit for minor injuries and illnesses.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

East London is well-connected to the rest of the city and can be easily reached by public transport or taxi. Here are a few options:

  • London Underground: The area is served by several Tube lines, including the Central, Jubilee, and District Lines.
  • Buses: There are many bus routes that run through East London, making it easy to explore the area by public transport.
  • Taxis: Taxis and private hires are widely available in London, but can be expensive during peak times.

Airports Near East

London is served by several airports, including:

  • Heathrow Airport: Located in West London, Heathrow is the busiest airport in the U.K. and offers a range of domestic and international flights.
  • Gatwick Airport: Located in South London, Gatwick is the second-busiest airport in the U.K. and offers a range of domestic and international flights.
  • London City Airport: Located in East London, this smaller airport offers flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near East

Several bus stations serve the East London area, including:

  • Stratford Bus Station: Located near Stratford station, this busy bus station serves several routes.
  • Whitechapel Bus Station: Located near Whitechapel station, this small bus station serves several local routes.
  • Mile End Bus Station: Located near Mile End station, this bus station serves several regional routes.

Other Options

Other transportation options in East London include:

  • Bikes: London has a bike-sharing scheme called Santander Cycles, which allows visitors to rent bikes for short journeys.
  • Boats: There are several boat services that run along the River Thames, including river buses and sightseeing tours.

How to Get Around in East

The best way to get around East London is by public transport, as the area is well-served by buses and the London Underground. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi for shorter journeys.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other parts of London, here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Central London: The heart of the city is just a short Tube journey away, offering endless attractions and activities.
  • Greenwich: Located in South East London, Greenwich is a beautiful historic town with many attractions, including the Royal Observatory and the Cutty Sark.
  • Hackney: This trendy neighborhood is home to many independent shops, restaurants, and galleries, making it a great destination for a day trip.

Conclusion

East London is a fascinating part of the city, offering a diverse range of attractions and activities for visitors to explore. With its vibrant arts scene, multicultural atmosphere, and wide range of outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in this exciting part of the city. Whether you’re visiting for a few days or a week, be sure to take the time to explore all that East London has to offer.
