Introduction to Greenwich

Greenwich is situated in the southeast of London and is a significant district that holds a variety of historical and cultural importance. It is home to Royal Museums Greenwich, known for its maritime history, and its beautiful park is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The district offers a perfect blend of natural and cultural attractions, outdoor activities, gastronomy, and stunning landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greenwich

  • Day 1: Explore Greenwich Park, visit the National Maritime Museum, and admire the view from the Royal Observatory Greenwich.
  • Day 2: Discover the historic Cutty Sark and the stunning architecture of the Queen’s House.
  • Day 3: Go on a boat ride along the Thames and visit the Old Royal Naval College and Painted Hall.
  • Day 4: Go on a walking tour of Greenwich’s hidden gems and visit the Cinema Museum.
  • Day 5: Step into history at the Churchill War Rooms and visit the iconic Tower Bridge.
  • Day 6: Visit the Science Museum, Natural History Museum, and the Victoria & Albert Museum in South Kensington.
  • Day 7: Visit the famous attraction, The O2, and enjoy a meal at one of the many cafes and restaurants with a beautiful view.

Local Culture and Customs of Greenwich

Greenwich’s rich history and diverse culture have given rise to its unique set of customs. Visitors should:

  • Respect the heritage sites and museums.
  • Follow the signs and instructions in public parks.
  • Respect the town’s sculptures, art galleries, and memorials.
  • Use public transportation and avoid taking private cars.

Best Time to Visit Greenwich

Greenwich is an all-year-round destination, but the months between March to June and September to November offer pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and reasonable hotel rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenwich

Do:

  • Follow the rules of the national history landmarks and museums.
  • Plan enough time to visit attractions and indulge in the local gastronomy.
  • Respect locals and be polite in public areas.
  • Take public transportation instead of private cars.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Smoke in public parks.
  • Vandalize public places.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenwich

Greenwich is generally safe. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe, staying aware of their surroundings, and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Greenwich

Greenwich offers excellent medical facilities. Visitors can visit Queen Elizabeth Hospital, Greenwich Dental Health Centre, and The Village Medical Centre for any medical emergencies.
