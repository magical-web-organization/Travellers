Introduction to North

Northern England, also known as the North, is a region comprising of nine counties stretching from the Scottish Border to the Midlands. It’s culturally rich and naturally beautiful, with a diverse range of attractions and experiences awaiting you. From historical landmarks, vibrant cities, stunning coastlines, and rolling hills, North England promises to inspire and enchant.

List of Places to Visit in North

  1. Manchester
  2. York
  3. Lake District
  4. Newcastle
  5. Sheffield
  6. Liverpool
  7. Durham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North England boasts of a rich heritage, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore.

  • York Minister: A stunning cathedral with a beautiful architecture and a rich history dating back to the Roman era.
  • Durham Castle and Cathedral: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with an impressive Norman architecture.
  • The Beatles Story: A museum in Liverpool dedicated to the legendary band that shaped the music industry.
  • Imperial War Museum North: A museum in Manchester dedicated to the history of modern war and conflict.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North England is known for its natural beauty, with plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

  • Lake District National Park: a stunning national park full of scenic walks, freshwater lakes, and rolling hills.
  • Kielder Water and Forest Park: The largest man-made forest in Europe, with over 200 square miles of beautiful landscapes.
  • Northumberland Coast: A 64-mile stretch of coastline with stunning beaches and historical landmarks, including the ruins of Dunstanburgh Castle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

North England offers fabulous trails and beautiful landscapes perfect for hikers and bikers.

  • Hadrian’s Wall Path: An ancient wall that stretches for 84 miles from coast to coast passing through stunning countryside and providing beautiful walks.
  • Yorkshire Dales: A stunning area of beautiful hills, valleys, and rivers offering dozens of hiking and biking routes suited for all kinds of adventurers.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

Here are some must-try activities and tours to get the most out of your North England trip.

  • Manchester United Museum and Stadium Tour: A tour of Manchester United’s Old Trafford stadium and museum, ideal for football lovers.
  • Newcastle Castle Keep: Newcastle Castle is a great place to learn about the city’s history and explore the stunning 360-degree views across Newcastle and Gateshead.
  • The Treetop Nets: An adventurous activity where visitors can walk, run, jump, and play on massive nets strung between the trees.

Week-Long Itinerary for North

Here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most out of your North England trip.

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Manchester, explore the city center, and visit Manchester United Museum and Stadium
Day 2 Head to York, explore the historic city walls, visit York Minster, and the National Railway Museum
Day 3 Drive to the Lake District, explore the stunning countryside, and enjoy a scenic walk or bike ride
Day 4 Visit Newcastle, explore the historic castle, and learn about the city’s shipbuilding heritage
Day 5 Explore the Northumberland coast, visit the ruins of Dunstanburgh Castle, and enjoy the stunning beaches
Day 6 Drive to Sheffield, explore the city’s street art and museums, and visit the Botanical Gardens
Day 7 Head back to Manchester, and spend the day shopping and exploring the city’s trendy Northern Quarter

Food and Drink Scene of North

The North of England’s local cuisine is diverse, featuring unique dishes and flavors from the region.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lancashire Hotpot: A hearty lamb stew topped with potato slices, originating from Lancashire.
  • Yorkshire Pudding: A classic side dish made from batter and typically served with roast meat and gravy.
  • Pan Haggerty: A layered dish of sliced potatoes with onions and grated cheese from North East England.

Local Beverages

  • Newcastle Brown Ale: A dark brown ale with a rich flavor and a smooth finish, brewed in Newcastle.
  • Theakston Old Peculier: A strong, dark ale brewed in North Yorkshire with a unique character.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North England offers various accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

  • The Lowry Hotel: A five-star hotel located in Manchester city center, offering a luxurious experience.
  • YHA Ambleside: A budget-friendly hostel in the Lake District offering dorm and private rooms with beautiful views.
  • Hotel du Vin York: A stylish boutique hotel in the heart of York with a stunning courtyard.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the souvenirs and local products unique to North England that you can bring back home.

  • Whitby Jet: A popular gemstone found on the North Yorkshire coast, it’s used to make jewelry and ornaments.
  • Lancashire Cheese: A creamy and tangy cheese made with cow’s milk, popular throughout the country.
  • Tweed: A woven woolen fabric material that’s synonymous with the North of England and used to make jackets, coats, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of North

The people of North England are known for their warmth and friendly demeanor. They have a strong love for music, with several notable music venues and festivals held here throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North England is between April and October when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor festivals, events, and shows that take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: North England offers an array of dishes unique to the region that you should try.
  • Visit the museums and historical sites: North England is rich in history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore.
  • Take in the stunning landscapes: North England boasts of a diverse range of landscapes, from rolling hills to stunning coastlines.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather: The weather in North England can be unpredictable, so pack warm clothes and rainwear.
  • Forget to bring your walking shoes: With its stunning landscape, North England is the perfect place for a scenic walk or hike, so ensure to pack comfortable walking shoes.
  • Miss out on pub culture: Pubs are an essential part of North England’s culture, so make sure to visit one and enjoy the live music, beer, and friendly atmosphere.

Safety and Health

Safety in North

North England is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s better to take precautions in crowded areas and late at night.

Health in North

North England has excellent medical facilities, and visitors are advised to get travel insurance and carry their medical prescriptions.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

  • Manchester Airport is the largest airport in the region and has flights to several international destinations.
  • Newcastle Airport serves the North East of England and has flights to several domestic and international destinations.
  • Leeds Bradford International Airport serves Yorkshire and has flights to several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near North

  • National Express offers bus services throughout the North of England, with stations in major cities like Manchester, Liverpool, and York.
  • Megabus is another bus service that offers services between several cities in North England.

Other Options

  • Train service is also available, with connections to major cities like Manchester, Liverpool, and Newcastle.
  • Car rental services are available, with several rental companies offering services in the region.

How to Get Around in North

North England is well connected with public transportation, including buses, trains, and taxis. Some of the best ways to get around include:

  • Train: The train is the best way to travel around North England, with several train operators providing services throughout the region.

  • Bus: The bus is another mode of transport offering convenient and affordable transport for both short and long-distance journeys.

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available and are a convenient way to travel within cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve got time to explore more of the UK, here are some nearby destinations that you can consider checking out:

  • Edinburgh, Scotland: Scotland’s capital city is a scenic train ride from North England, famous for its stunning landscapes and rich history.
  • Dublin, Ireland: A short ferry ride from the coast of Northern Ireland takes you to the charming city of Dublin, with its rich history and bustling city center.
  • Belfast, Northern Ireland: A short flight from North England takes you to Belfast, a city known for its rich history, vibrant nightlife, and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

North England is a region bursting with history, beauty, and culture. From stunning landscapes and historical landmarks to unique local cuisine and friendly people, a trip to North England is a must-do for anyone seeking to explore the UK. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to North England and make the most out of your stay.
