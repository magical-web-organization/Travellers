Introduction to South Bank

South Bank is a bustling area located in central London, along the south bank of the River Thames. This lively neighborhood is home to a variety of cultural, natural, and historical attractions, making it the ideal destination for both local and international tourists seeking to explore the city. Whether you’re interested in museums, outdoor adventures, or scenic views, South Bank has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in South Bank

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Bank boasts a rich cultural history, and there’s no better way to discover it than visiting some of the many museums and historical attractions that the area has to offer. Here are a few of the top must-visit destinations:

  • The British Museum: home to a vast collection of art and artifacts ranging from ancient Egypt to modern Europe.
  • The Tate Modern: one of the world’s most renowned modern art museums, with stunning views of the London skyline.
  • The Imperial War Museum: a comprehensive museum documenting the impact of war and conflict on Britain and the world.
  • The Shakespeare Globe: a replica of the original Elizabethan theater where Shakespeare’s plays were performed.

Natural Attractions of South Bank: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Bank offers a plethora of natural attractions that are perfect for those who love spending time outdoors. Here are some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore:

  • The London Eye: offering stunning panoramic views of the city skyline and the River Thames.
  • The South Bank Skate Park: a popular gathering place where skateboarders and BMX riders can practice their skills.
  • The Queen’s Walk: a scenic riverside promenade that offers picturesque views of the river and the city.
  • The South Bank Book Market: a charming outdoor market featuring a variety of second-hand and antique books.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Bank

For those who prefer exploring on foot or by bike, South Bank has some excellent hiking and biking routes to offer. You can explore the scenic Thames Path, which stretches along the riverbank and offers an unrivaled view of London’s iconic landmarks. Or, you could rent a bike from one of the many bike share terminals located throughout the area and cycle through the Victorian-era street fronts. South Bank also has plenty of green spaces, such as the Jubilee Gardens or the Waterloo Park that offer ample opportunities for a leisurely stroll or picnic.

Best Things to Do in South Bank: Activities and Tours

South Bank is a bustling neighborhood that offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all age groups and interests. Here are some of the top things to do when visiting South Bank:

  • Take a river cruise on the Thames and take in the sights of London from a different perspective.
  • Visit the SEA LIFE London Aquarium and explore the underwater world of marine life.
  • Wander through the Southbank Centre Complex, the largest arts center in the UK, which hosts a variety of performances and cultural events.
  • Try some of the world-renowned street foods that are sold at the food markets, which offer an eclectic mix of international flavors.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Bank

To ensure that you make the most of your visit to South Bank, a week-long itinerary is recommended. The following itinerary includes some of the most popular attractions in the area:

Food and Drink Scene of South Bank

The food and drink scene in South Bank is diverse, with a range of options for meat lovers, vegetarians, and vegans. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips, served at most of the area’s traditional pubs and restaurants.
  • Pie and mash with jellied eels, a classic East London dish that can be found at Borough Market.
  • Sticky toffee pudding, a classic British dessert served at various restaurants all around South Bank.

Local Beverages

  • Pimm’s, a British summer favourite served in many pubs and at outdoor markets and festivals.
  • Ale, an iconic English beverage that can be sampled at traditional pubs in the area.
  • English sparkling wine, gaining in popularity and produced by a number of vineyards in South East England.

Where to Stay in South Bank: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Bank has a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget. From luxury hotels and boutique guesthouses to more affordable hostels and apartment rentals, there is something for everyone. Some of the most popular options include:

  • The Savoy Hotel, a luxurious five-star hotel that has accommodated celebrities and royalty for over 130 years.
  • Premier Inn London Bridge, a budget-friendly option located just a short distance from many of the area’s attractions.
  • YHA London St Pauls, a hostel that offers affordable accommodation in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in South Bank: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To bring a bit of South Bank back home with you, consider purchasing some local souvenirs and products. A few popular items include:

  • A book from the South Bank Book Market
  • A vintage poster from the Southbank Centre
  • A bottle of English sparkling wine

Local Culture and Customs of South Bank

South Bank is a multicultural and diverse area, allowing for an atmosphere of inclusivity and openness. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and regulations by not engaging in any form of disruptive or offensive behaviours in public spaces such as excessive drinking or littering.

Best Time to Visit South Bank

The best time to visit South Bank is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is mild and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Visitors can also experience some of the area’s seasonal events and outdoor festivals, such as The Underbelly Festival at the Southbank Centre and the Classic Car Boot Sale.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Bank

Do:

  • Take a stroll along the river and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Visit as many museums and attractions as possible.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample some of the city’s famous drinking culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Engage in disruptive or disrespectful behavior.
  • Ignore local customs and rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Bank

South Bank is generally a safe area for visitors, with low rates of street crime. However, as with all tourist destinations, it is important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings. Visitors should be conscious of pickpocketing and be careful when carrying valuables. The emergency services can be contacted by dialling 999.

Health in South Bank

South Bank has several hospitals and medical centers, such as St. Thomas’ Hospital and the Evelina London Children’s Hospital, that provide a variety of medical services for visitors in need.

Getting to South Bank: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Bank is easily accessible by public transportation, and there are several options for getting to and from the area.

Airports Near South Bank

  • Heathrow Airport: located 15 miles west of South Bank, accessible by train or bus.
  • London City Airport: located 8 miles east of South Bank, accessible by train or taxi.

Bus Stations Near South Bank

  • Waterloo Bus Station: located in the heart of South Bank, providing local and national bus services.

Other Options

  • London Underground: several stations (Waterloo, Embankment, and Southwark) provide access to South Bank via the Bakerloo, Jubilee, and Northern lines.
  • Thames Clippers: a convenient and scenic way to travel by river, offering regular services to and from various points along the Thames.

How to Get Around in South Bank

South Bank is easily navigable by foot or bike, and many of the main attractions are within walking distance of each other. For those who prefer public transport, there are several bus routes and tube stations located throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from South Bank: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Bank offers easy access to several nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Westminster Abbey and the Houses of Parliament: historic landmarks located just across the river from South Bank.
  • The Tower of London: a must-visit attraction just a short distance from South Bank.
  • The South Downs: an area of natural beauty and hiking opportunities located just a few hours’ drive from London.

Conclusion

South Bank is a vibrant and dynamic neighborhood that offers something for everyone. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures and culinary delights, this central London destination is the perfect place to explore and immerse yourself in the city’s rich history and culture. With our comprehensive guide, you are sure to have an unforgettable experience in South Bank.
