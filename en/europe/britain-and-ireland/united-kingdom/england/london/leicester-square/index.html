Leicester Square, located in the heart of London’s West End, is a popular tourist destination known for its bustling atmosphere and lively entertainment scene. From theaters and museums to parks and restaurants, Leicester Square offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover all the must-visit places, exciting activities, and practical information you need to know to plan your trip to Leicester Square.

Introduction to Leicester Square

Dating back to the 17th century, Leicester Square has a rich history that has made it one of London’s most popular tourist attractions. The square is surrounded by some of the city’s best theaters, including the National Portrait Gallery, which features iconic British portraits, statues, and sculptures. Its central location provides easy access to other popular areas like Covent Garden and Piccadilly Circus.

List of Places to Visit in Leicester Square

From historical landmarks to natural attractions, Leicester Square offers an array of must-visit places. Below are some of our top picks.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best things about Leicester Square is its rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the attractions you shouldn’t miss:

  • National Portrait Gallery: Home to a vast collection of British portraits, statues, and sculptures.
  • National Gallery: Showcases Western European paintings from the 13th to the 19th centuries.
  • St. James’s Square: A historical public square that was once home to some of the city’s most prominent residents.

Natural Attractions of Leicester Square: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Leicester Square offers an array of scenic spots:

  • Leicester Square Gardens: A charming and serene green space in the middle of the bustling city.
  • Trafalgar Square: A popular spot for taking in the views and enjoying outdoor entertainment.
  • Hyde Park: One of the city’s largest parks, with a vast expanse of greenery and an array of recreational activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leicester Square

For those looking to explore the city on foot or by bike, Leicester Square offers several trails. Some popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • The Jubilee Walkway: A scenic trail that offers breathtaking views of the river Thames and the city’s iconic landmarks.
  • Regent’s Canal Towpath: A peaceful, scenic trail that winds through the city’s waterways, offering beautiful views and serene surroundings.
  • Greenwich Foot Tunnel: A pedestrian tunnel that connects the Isle of Dogs and Greenwich, offering an easy and picturesque route along the river.

Best Things to Do in Leicester Square: Activities and Tours

Leicester Square boasts an array of exciting activities and tours that cater to different interests. Some must-do activities include:

  • West End shows: Catch one of London’s world-famous musicals or plays in one of the district’s many theaters.
  • Shopping: Leicester Square has many high-end shops and boutiques where you can indulge in retail therapy.
  • Food: Savor local delicacies in one of the city’s many restaurants, cafes, and food stalls.

Food and Drink Scene of Leicester Square

One of the best things about Leicester Square is its thriving food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A traditional English dish consisting of battered fish served with chips.
  • Pie and Mash: A classic comfort food made with minced meat, gravy, and mashed potatoes.
  • Full English Breakfast: A hearty breakfast of eggs, bacon, sausage, beans, tomatoes, and toast.

Local Beverages

  • Pimm’s Cup: A refreshing summer drink made with Pimm’s No. 1, lemonade, fruits, and herbs.
  • Real Ale: A traditional British beer made from natural ingredients and served from the cask.
  • Gin and Tonic: A classic British cocktail made with gin, tonic water, and a slice of lime.

Where to Stay in Leicester Square: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Leicester Square to suit any budget. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • The Z Hotel: Located right in the heart of Leicester Square, this hotel offers affordable, high-quality accommodation.
  • The Piccadilly London West End: A stylish hotel with spacious rooms and contemporary amenities.
  • The Rathbone Hotel: A charming boutique hotel with elegant decor and a warm atmosphere.

What to Buy in Leicester Square: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Leicester Square. Some of the best include:

  • Royal Family souvenirs: Check out shops selling souvenirs themed around the British royal family, including mugs, keychains, and tea towels.
  • London-made crafts: Leicester Square is home to many independent designers and artisans selling handmade crafts and artwork.
  • Local food products: Look for specialty food stalls selling local products like artisanal cheeses, cured meats, and sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Leicester Square

Leicester Square, like the rest of London, has a rich and vibrant culture that reflects its diverse population. As a visitor, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Leicester Square

The best time to visit Leicester Square is during the summer months when the weather is mild and sunny. However, since this is the peak tourist season, expect crowds and higher prices. For a quieter experience, visit in the spring or fall when the weather is still pleasant but the crowds have thinned out.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leicester Square

As a tourist, it’s important to be aware of local customs and avoid any behavior that may be considered disrespectful. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and the occasion.
  • Tip your servers and service providers.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Speak loudly or disruptively in public areas.
  • Take photographs of people without their consent.

Safety and Health

Leicester Square is generally safe for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions to ensure your safety.

Safety in Leicester Square

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Keep valuable items like cash, phone, and passports in a safe place.
  • Use caution when using public transportation at night.

Health in Leicester Square

  • Ensure that you have adequate travel insurance before your trip.
  • Bring any necessary prescriptions with you.
  • Look for medical facilities near your accommodation in case of emergency.

Getting to Leicester Square: Transportation Options and Logistics

Leicester Square is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Leicester Square

  • Heathrow Airport: The largest and busiest airport in the UK, serving major airlines from all over the world.
  • Gatwick Airport: The second-largest airport in London, located south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Leicester Square

  • Victoria Coach Station: A major bus station serving many destinations across the UK and beyond.

Other Options

  • Tube: Leicester Square is served by the Piccadilly and Northern Lines on the London Underground network.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available throughout London and can be hailed on the street or via a taxi app.

How to Get Around in Leicester Square

Leicester Square is a walkable area and many attractions are located within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer to take public transportation, the London Underground is the most efficient and convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Leicester Square: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Leicester Square is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in London, including:

  • Covent Garden: An area known for its bustling markets and street performers.
  • Piccadilly Circus: A major tourist hub known for its neon lights and bustling atmosphere.
  • Soho: A lively neighborhood known for its nightlife and entertainment scene.

Conclusion

Leicester Square is a bustling and vibrant destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or entertainment, this iconic square in the heart of London’s West End is the perfect place to explore. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Leicester Square.
