Introduction to Southwark and Lewisham

Southwark and Lewisham are two boroughs of London that are often overlooked by tourists. However, these areas of southeast London offer a wealth of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and beautiful scenery. Southwark is home to some of London’s most iconic landmarks, such as the Tower Bridge and Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre, while Lewisham offers an escape into nature with its parks and green spaces. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do in Southwark and Lewisham, as well as where to stay, eat, and shop.

List of Places to Visit in Southwark and Lewisham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southwark and Lewisham have a rich cultural heritage, and there are many museums and historical places to explore. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Tower Bridge: This iconic suspension bridge spans the River Thames and offers stunning views of London’s skyline.
  • Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre: A reconstruction of the Globe Theatre where Shakespeare’s plays were performed in the 16th century.
  • Imperial War Museum: A museum dedicated to the history of modern warfare, with exhibits on conflicts from World War I to the present day.
  • Horniman Museum and Gardens: A unique museum with exhibits on anthropology, natural history, and musical instruments from around the world, as well as beautiful botanical gardens.

Natural Attractions of Southwark and Lewisham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for some fresh air and outdoor adventure, Southwark and Lewisham have plenty of options. Some top natural attractions include:

  • Greenwich Park: A large park with stunning views of the River Thames and the city skyline, as well as the Royal Observatory and the Prime Meridian.
  • Crystal Palace Park: A Victorian-era park with a farm, maze, dinosaur sculptures, and a concert bowl.
  • Dulwich Park: A tranquil park featuring a boating lake, a cafe, and a sports area.
  • Southwark Park: A large park with a lake, a rose garden, and a cafe.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southwark and Lewisham

Southwark and Lewisham are home to some of London’s best hiking and biking routes. Some popular routes include:

  • Thames Path: A 184-mile hiking trail that follows the River Thames through London and out into the countryside.
  • Waterlink Way: A 10-mile cycling route that runs from Greenwich to Beckenham, passing through several parks and green spaces.
  • Green Chain Walk: A network of hiking trails that covers over 50 miles and passes through a variety of parks and woodlands in southeast London.
  • Nunhead Cemetery: A historic cemetery that now serves as a nature reserve, with walking paths and a scenic viewpoint.

Best Things to Do in Southwark and Lewisham: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, Southwark and Lewisham offer plenty of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Borough Market: A bustling food market with over 100 stalls selling everything from fresh produce to international cuisine.
  • Tate Modern: A world-renowned modern art museum located in a former power station on the banks of the Thames.
  • Cutty Sark: A tea clipper ship from the 19th century that is now a museum on the River Thames.
  • Thames River Cruise: A relaxing way to see London’s sights from the water, with options for lunch, dinner, or afternoon tea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southwark and Lewisham

If you have a week to explore Southwark and Lewisham, here’s an itinerary that covers the best of both boroughs:

  • Day 1: Tower Bridge, Tower of London, and St. Katharine Docks in Southwark.
  • Day 2: Borough Market, Tate Modern, and Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre in Southwark.
  • Day 3: Imperial War Museum and Kennington Park in Southwark.
  • Day 4: Greenwich Park, Royal Observatory, and the National Maritime Museum in Lewisham.
  • Day 5: Crystal Palace Park, Dulwich Picture Gallery, and Dulwich Park in Lewisham.
  • Day 6: Horniman Museum and Gardens, Nunhead Cemetery, and Peckham Rye Park in Lewisham.
  • Day 7: Thames River Cruise from Greenwich to Westminster, with stops at the Cutty Sark and Southwark Cathedral.

Food and Drink Scene of Southwark and Lewisham

Must-Try Dishes

Southwark and Lewisham offer a diverse range of cuisine, from traditional English dishes to international flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Full English breakfast: A hearty breakfast featuring eggs, bacon, sausage, mushrooms, baked beans, and black pudding.
  • Fish and chips: A classic English dish of battered fish and french fries, often served with mushy peas and tartar sauce.
  • Pie and mash: A traditional London dish of meat pie and mashed potatoes, often topped with gravy.
  • Indian curry: London has a strong Indian influence, and there are many excellent curry houses in Southwark and Lewisham.

Local Beverages

London is known for its pubs and breweries, and Southwark and Lewisham have no shortage of options. Some must-try beverages include:

  • English ale: A rich, malty beer that is a staple of British pubs.
  • Gin and tonic: A classic British cocktail that features gin, tonic water, and a twist of lemon or lime.
  • London dry gin: A type of gin that is distilled in London and known for its dry, herbal flavor.
  • Cider: A fermented apple drink that is popular in the UK and comes in many different varieties.

Where to Stay in Southwark and Lewisham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southwark and Lewisham offer a variety of accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some top options include:

  • Hilton London Bankside: A stylish hotel located in the heart of Southwark, with modern rooms and on-site dining options.
  • Premier Inn London Lewisham: A budget-friendly hotel located near Lewisham Station, with comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • YHA London St. Paul’s: A hostel located in a historic building in Southwark, with shared and private rooms and a cafe.
  • The Clarendon Hotel: A boutique hotel located in Blackheath, with stylish rooms and views of Greenwich Park.

What to Buy in Southwark and Lewisham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back from Southwark and Lewisham, here are some local products to consider:

  • Tea: London is famous for its tea, and there are many tea shops in Southwark and Lewisham that sell a variety of blends.
  • Gin: London dry gin is a popular souvenir, and there are several distilleries in the area that offer tours and tastings.
  • Handmade crafts: There are many markets and independent shops in Southwark and Lewisham that sell handmade crafts and gifts.
  • Books: Southwark and Lewisham have a rich literary history, and there are many independent bookshops in the area that sell rare and collectible books.

Local Culture and Customs of Southwark and Lewisham

London is a cosmopolitan city with a diverse population and many different cultural traditions. Some tips to keep in mind when visiting Southwark and Lewisham include:

  • Be respectful of different religions and cultural practices.
  • Tipping is generally expected in restaurants and cafes.
  • Londoners are generally friendly and helpful, but can be reserved in public spaces.
  • The “Mind the Gap” warning on the Tube refers to the space between the train and the platform.

Best Time to Visit Southwark and Lewisham

London is a year-round destination, but some times of year are better than others for visiting Southwark and Lewisham. The best time to visit is generally from April to September, when the weather is mild and there are many outdoor events and festivals. However, London can be crowded and expensive during the summer months, so consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of spring and fall for fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southwark and Lewisham

Do:

  • Do explore the parks and green spaces in Southwark and Lewisham.
  • Do take a Thames River Cruise to see London’s sights from the water.
  • Do try some of the traditional English dishes, such as fish and chips or a full English breakfast.
  • Do visit the local markets, like Borough Market or Crystal Palace Market, for a taste of London’s food scene.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for walking and exploring.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the parks or on the streets.
  • Don’t forget to bring an umbrella or rain jacket, as London weather can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t be afraid to use public transportation, as London has an extensive network of buses and trains.
  • Don’t assume that everyone in London speaks English fluently, as many people may speak other languages as well.
  • Don’t forget to bring your sense of adventure and curiosity!

Safety and Health

Safety in Southwark and Lewisham

London is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and stay safe. Some safety tips include:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services rather than hailing a cab on the street.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or wearing expensive jewelry.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs or entering your PIN in public places.
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Southwark and Lewisham

London has a high standard of healthcare, with many hospitals and clinics throughout the city. Visitors can access healthcare through the National Health Service (NHS), but may need to pay for certain services if they are not UK residents. Visitors should also take precautions to stay healthy while traveling, such as staying hydrated, washing hands frequently, and getting adequate rest.

Getting to Southwark and Lewisham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southwark and Lewisham

London has several airports that serve international and domestic flights, including:

  • Heathrow Airport: Located 17 miles west of London, Heathrow is one of the busiest airports in the world and serves many international airlines.
  • Gatwick Airport: Located 30 miles south of London, Gatwick serves many low-cost airlines and international carriers.
  • London City Airport: Located in the heart of East London, London City serves short-haul flights to European destinations.
  • Stansted Airport: Located 40 miles northeast of London, Stansted serves many low-cost airlines and European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Southwark and Lewisham

London has several bus stations that serve domestic and international destinations, including:

  • Victoria Coach Station: Located in central London, Victoria serves many destinations throughout the UK and Europe.
  • Waterloo Bus Station: Located near the South Bank, Waterloo serves many domestic destinations in the southeast of England.
  • Stratford City Bus Station: Located in East London, Stratford serves many destinations throughout the UK.

Other Options

London is well-connected by train, with several major train stations that serve destinations throughout the UK. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as the Tube or buses, to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Southwark and Lewisham

Southwark and Lewisham are both well-connected by public transportation, including the London Underground, buses, and trains. Visitors can use an Oyster card or contactless payment to pay for fares on all modes of public transportation. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also widely available in London, and bicycles can be rented from several locations throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Southwark and Lewisham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

London is a gateway to many other destinations throughout the UK and Europe. Some nearby destinations and excursions from Southwark and Lewisham include:

  • Oxford: A historic university town with beautiful architecture and museums.
  • Cambridge: Another famous university town with picturesque streets and punting on the river.
  • Brighton: A vibrant seaside resort with a famous pier and a lively arts scene.
  • Canterbury: A medieval city with a stunning cathedral and historic streets.

Conclusion

Southwark and Lewisham may not be as well-known as some other areas of London, but they offer a diverse range of cultural and natural attractions that are well worth exploring. From the historic Tower Bridge to the tranquil parks of Lewisham, there is something for everyone in these southeast London boroughs. Plan your visit today and discover the hidden gems of Southwark and Lewisham.
