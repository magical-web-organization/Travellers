Introduction to The Farne Islands

The Farne Islands is a group of 28 small islands located off the coast of Northumberland, in North East England. This stunning area is renowned for its abundant wildlife, beautiful coastline, and historic landmarks. The Farne Islands are owned by the National Trust and are accessible to visitors from April to October each year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Farne Islands

St. Cuthbert’s Way

St. Cuthbert’s Way is a 62.5-mile walk that starts in Melrose, Scotland and ends in the Holy Island of Lindisfarne, in England. The route passes through The Farne Islands and is considered one of the most beautiful walks in the UK.

Coast and Castle Cycle Route

The Coast and Castle Cycle Route is a 172-mile journey that starts in Newcastle and finishes in Edinburgh, passing through The Farne Islands along the way.

Local Culture and Customs of The Farne Islands

The Farne Islands is steeped in rich history and heritage, from the Lindisfarne Gospels to Grace Darling’s heroic rescue mission.

Best Time to Visit The Farne Islands

The best time to visit The Farne Islands is during the summer months, from April to October when the weather is mild and most attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Farne Islands

Do:

  • Plan ahead, book tours and accommodation in advance
  • Respect the wildlife and avoid disturbing nesting birds and seals
  • Dress appropriately and bring sturdy footwear for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Introduce non-native species to the islands
  • Climb on rocks or cliffs
  • Litter or leave trash behind

Safety and Health

Safety in The Farne Islands

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially near cliffs and rocky terrain
  • Follow safety instructions from tour guides and accommodation providers
  • In case of emergency, call 999 or 112

Health in The Farne Islands

  • Be aware of tick bites when outdoors
  • Watch out for jellyfish in the water and seek medical attention if stung

How to Get Around in The Farne Islands

The Farne Islands are best explored on foot or by boat. Walking and cycling are popular options for exploring the coast and countryside, while boat tours are ideal for exploring the wildlife and scenery around the islands.
