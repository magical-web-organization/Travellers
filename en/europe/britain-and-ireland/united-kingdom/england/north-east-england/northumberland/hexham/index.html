Introduction to Hexham

Nestled in the rolling countryside of Northumberland, Hexham is a charming market town known for its rich history, stunning scenery, and friendly locals. Founded by the Romans over 1,300 years ago, Hexham has been a hub of commerce and culture for centuries, attracting visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re interested in exploring historic sites, hiking through lush forests, or savoring delicious local cuisine, Hexham has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hexham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hexham Abbey

Built in the 7th century, Hexham Abbey is one of the most important historical sites in Hexham. With its impressive Norman architecture, beautiful stained-glass windows, and striking choir stalls, the abbey is a must-visit for anyone interested in medieval history and architecture.

Hexham Old Gaol

Located in the heart of Hexham, the Hexham Old Gaol is a fascinating museum that explores the history of crime and punishment in Northumberland. With its original cellblocks, exhibits on local criminals, and interactive displays, this museum is a great way to learn about the darker side of Hexham’s past.

Moot Hall

Dating back to the 14th century, the Moot Hall is a historic building that has served as the town’s meeting place for centuries. Today, the building houses a museum that tells the story of Hexham’s rich history, from the Roman era to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Hexham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hadrian’s Wall

Built by the Romans in the 2nd century AD, Hadrian’s Wall is one of the most impressive engineering feats of the ancient world. Stretching over 73 miles from coast to coast, the wall is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-see for anyone interested in Roman history and architecture.

Allen Banks and Staward Gorge

Managed by the National Trust, Allen Banks and Staward Gorge is a beautiful woodland area that’s perfect for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife spotting. With its ancient trees, rushing waterfalls, and breathtaking views, this natural wonderland is a must-visit for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Devil’s Water

Located just outside of Hexham, Devil’s Water is a picturesque river that’s popular with anglers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. With its crystal-clear waters, stunning scenery, and abundance of wildlife, this tranquil spot is the perfect place to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hexham

Hexhamshire Common

A popular hiking and biking destination, Hexhamshire Common offers miles of scenic trails through the rolling hills of Northumberland. With its panoramic views, open heathland, and wildflowers, this area is a favorite among both locals and visitors.

Tyne Green Country Park

Located just south of Hexham, Tyne Green Country Park is a beautiful park that’s perfect for hiking, biking, and picnicking. With its riverside trails, wildlife-rich wetlands, and wooded areas, this park is a great way to experience the natural beauty of Northumberland.

Best Things to Do in Hexham: Activities and Tours

Hexham Racecourse

Located just a few miles from Hexham, Hexham Racecourse is a popular horse racing venue that hosts meets throughout the year. With its stunning views, lively atmosphere, and exciting races, this is a great way to spend a day in Hexham.

Hexham Golf Club

Nestled in the heart of the Northumberland countryside, Hexham Golf Club is a stunning course that offers a challenging round of golf along with breathtaking views. With its well-manicured fairways, challenging greens, and friendly staff, this is a must-visit for any golf enthusiast.

Food and Drink Scene of Hexham

Must-Try Dishes

Northumbrian Stottie Cake

A local favorite, the Northumbrian Stottie Cake is a hearty, savory bread that’s perfect for sandwiches or as a side with a meal.

Craster Kippers

Smoked herring that’s been traditionally made in the coastal village of Craster for over 150 years.

Local Beverages

Real Ale

Northumberland is home to some of the best real ale in the country, with local breweries such as Hadrian and Border, Allendale, and Wylam all producing top-quality beers.

Hexhamshire Gin

Crafted in small batches using local botanicals, Hexhamshire Gin is a delicious spirit that’s perfect for sipping or mixing in cocktails.

Where to Stay in Hexham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Hotels

Slaley Hall

Located just a short drive from Hexham, Slaley Hall is a stunning country hotel that offers luxurious rooms, scenic views, and a wide range of amenities, including spa treatments, golf courses, and fine dining.

Beaumont Hotel

Centrally located in the heart of Hexham, the Beaumont Hotel is a charming boutique hotel that offers comfortable rooms, friendly service, and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Hexham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hexham Abbey Souvenirs

The Hexham Abbey gift shop offers a wide range of souvenirs and gifts, including books, postcards, and other mementos.

Local Art and Crafts

Northumberland is home to a thriving arts and crafts scene, with local artists producing everything from pottery and ceramics to jewelry and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Hexham

Northumberland is a friendly and welcoming part of the country, with a strong sense of community and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re exploring the region’s rich history, sampling its delicious food and drink, or simply enjoying the beautiful scenery, you’ll find that the locals are always happy to share their love of the area with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Hexham

The best time to visit Hexham is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the countryside is in full bloom. However, the shoulder seasons of spring and autumn can also be a great time to visit, with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hexham

Do:

  • Do take a walk along Hadrian’s Wall and explore the local Roman ruins.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including smoked herring and real ale.
  • Do take the time to explore the surrounding countryside and enjoy the stunning scenery

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful of the region’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t be surprised if the locals strike up a conversation – Northumbrians are known for their friendliness!

Safety and Health

Safety in Hexham

Northumberland is generally a safe and friendly place, with low levels of crime and a welcoming community. However, as with any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take common-sense precautions such as locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Hexham

Northumberland has a good standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and medical clinics located throughout the region. However, it’s always a good idea to take out comprehensive travel insurance before your trip, just in case of any unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Hexham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hexham

The nearest airport to Hexham is Newcastle International Airport, which is located around 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Hexham

Hexham has a bus station that offers regular services to Newcastle upon Tyne and other surrounding areas.

Other Options

There are several train stations located throughout Northumberland, with direct services to London, Edinburgh, and other major cities. Taxis and private car hire services are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

How to Get Around in Hexham

The easiest way to get around Hexham is by car or taxi, as many of the town’s attractions and sites are located in rural areas outside of the town. However, there are also several local bus services that operate throughout the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Hexham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Durham

Located just a short drive from Hexham, Durham is a beautiful city with a rich history, stunning architecture, and a lively cultural scene.

Kielder Water and Forest Park

A short drive north of Hexham, Kielder Water and Forest Park is a beautiful natural area that’s perfect for hiking, biking, and fishing.

Conclusion

As you can see, Hexham is a fascinating and unique destination that’s full of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich heritage, trying out local cuisine, or simply soaking up the stunning scenery, Hexham is sure to leave you with lasting memories and a deep appreciation for this wonderful part of the world.
