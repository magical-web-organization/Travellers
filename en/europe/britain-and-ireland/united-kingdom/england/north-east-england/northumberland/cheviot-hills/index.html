Introduction to Cheviot Hills

Cheviot Hills is a range of hills situated in Northumberland, North East England. It is a border mountainous range that lies between England and Scotland and is part of the Pennine chain. The hills have been a place of natural beauty for centuries, known for its stunning peaks, winding rivers, and diverse terrain. Whether you’re an adventurer or a nature lover, Cheviot Hills offers an incredible range of activities and sights to see.

List of Places to Visit in Cheviot Hills

If you’re planning to spend your vacation in Cheviot Hills, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Flodden Battlefield: The place of England’s last medieval victory in a battle with Scotland.
  • Chain Bridge Honey Farm: The working museum for honeybees offering insights into beekeeping and the world of honey-making.
  • Alnwick Castle: A magnificent historic castle used as a location for Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter movies.

Natural Attractions of Cheviot Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Cheviot Hills: The largest of the hills, with scenic views and varying terrains to cater to hiking and biking trails.
  • Kielder Water & Forest Park: The largest artificial lake in Northern Europe with mountains and over 250,000 planted trees.
  • Black Middens Bastle House: Historic ruins of a fortified farmhouse that provides visitors a glimpse into daily life on a border farm in the 16th and 17th centuries.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cheviot Hills

Cheviot Hills has numerous walking trails to enjoy the stunning natural landscapes that it has to offer. Several cycling and mountain biking trails are also available for thrill-seekers to experience the mountains at its best. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Cheviot Summit: The highest point in the range’s long ridge offers some of the scenic natural beauty critical to the National Trail.
  • Hadrian’s Wall Path: The historical wall that runs through the Cheviot Hills and is a must walk for all to see the fascinating medieval innovation that kept communities safe.
  • The Borderloop Trail: A marked circular route that passes through the hills and along the Anglo-Scottish border, offering panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Cheviot Hills: Activities and Tours

For those looking for adventure and sight-seeing at the same time, here are some great things to do in Cheviot Hills:

  • Nature Day Tour: Over eight hours of exploring the Cheviot Hills and discovering hidden secrets of the National Park whilst walking its terrain with an experienced guide.
  • Kielder Observatory Night Tour: Discover the secrets of the universe with a night sky tour with the roaming astronomer Jamie Gallagher.
  • Horse Riding Adventure: Take a guided horse riding tour through the beautiful Cheviot Hills offering with outstanding picturesque scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Cheviot Hills

Cheviot Hills has a rich culinary culture offering a range of locally sourced produce and products straight from the farms.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stottie Cake: A traditional flatbread made using soda and flour common in the North East of England.
  • Craster Kippers: A local smoked fish delicacy from the tiny village of Craster.
  • Northumberland Sausage: A strong flavoured sausage locally seasoned with spices to suit the palate of the locals.

Local Beverages

  • Jedburgh Gin: A locally produced premium spirit made with locally sourced botanicals.
  • Cheviot Brewery: A local micro-brewery producing an extensive selection of ales and beer using locally grown hops.

Where to Stay in Cheviot Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cheviot Hills has a wide range of affordable and luxury accommodations available for visitors. Here are some of the best options:

  • YHA Alnwick: A budget hostel that offers ultimate relaxation, tranquil surrounding and memorable views of the countryside and Alnwick Castle.
  • Birchbrae Country House Bed & Breakfast: A luxury accommodation located in the heart of the Scottish Borders, featuring beautiful gardens and a serene environment.
  • The Plough Inn: A five-star boutique inn, offering modern rooms with traditional furnishings, good food, and a charming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Cheviot Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some iconic locally produced goods that will make for perfect souvenirs from your trip to Cheviot Hills:

  • Northumberland Tartan: Soft wool, perfect for a scarf or throw, woven locally in the traditional Northumberland Tartan colours.
  • Northumberland Honey: Local honey made by the Chain Bridge Honey Farm with bees from the hills surrounding the farm.
  • Craster Kippers: Locally smoked fish with a distinctive flavor that you won’t forget quickly.

Local Culture and Customs of Cheviot Hills

Cheviot Hills has a rich cultural heritage with a diverse range of traditions and customs. Some of the must-knows include their love for local produce, care for the environment and natural habitat, and preservation and respect in traditional crafts and artisans.

Best Time to Visit Cheviot Hills

The best time to visit Cheviot Hills is from April to October, where you can experience the pleasant weather and take advantage of the longer days.

  • Respect and acknowledge the local customs and traditions, adding to their once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Don’t:

  • Disturb others by making loud noises or littering in the public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cheviot Hills

Cheviot Hills are themselves considered safe places to explore. However, its remote location, steep terrains, and rugged natural surroundings mean you should always take necessary precautions when exploring these hills. Here are a few tips:

  • Take a map and compass before setting off on hikes and trails.
  • Check weather forecast conditions in advance before embarking
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear, suitable for hilly terrains and long duration outdoors.
  • Inform someone of your travel plans and tentative return time.

Health in Cheviot Hills

Cheviot Hills are remote and have limited services available, so it is important to bring appropriate supplies for first aid-related incidents. In difficult health situations, call the local emergency services and take help from a local guide.

Getting to Cheviot Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cheviot Hills can be easily reached by car or public transportation via the North East of England.

Airports Near Cheviot Hills

  • Newcastle International Airport: The nearest international airport, located approximately 75 km away from Cheviot Hills.
  • Edinburgh Airport: Approximately 120 km north of the Cheviot Hills, you can travel to the hills via the Borders railway.

Bus Stations Near Cheviot Hills

  • Newcastle Bus Station: The central bus station in Newcastle that offers connections to other major towns and cities.
  • Berwick-upon-Tweed Bus Station: This bus station is located in the town centre and is within walking distance of the train station.

Other Options

  • Car: A car is the best transportation option if you want to explore the area’s remote areas. Several car rental agencies are available for visitors.

How to Get Around in Cheviot Hills

The Cheviot Hills have a diverse range of easily navigable roadways, public transportation, and walking and biking trails, making it easy to explore the hills and appreciate their natural beauty.

Walking

Walking is the best way to experience Cheviot Hills’ natural beauty. The region has a wide network of walking trails covering around 450 miles, including the famous Pennine Way National Trail.

Cycling

Cycling is an excellent way to explore the Cheviot Hills. There are hundreds of miles of quiet, country roads, and the hills offer challenging mountain bike trails.

Where to Go Next from Cheviot Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time left after visiting the Cheviot Hills, there are several other nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting.

  • Cleveland Hills: A hilly area located in North Yorkshire, England, with the highest peak of the hills, Roseberry Topping.
  • The Lake District: A mountainous area in North West England, famous for its stunning lakes and striking natural views.
  • Edinburgh: The capital of Scotland, with a rich cultural, historical, and architectural heritage.

Conclusion

Cheviot Hills is an untouched natural haven, a must-visit destination with a rich cultural heritage and a diverse range of activities for tourists, making it a perfect destination for an immersive travel experience. Plan your next vacation today to discover the hidden treasure of Cheviot Hills.
