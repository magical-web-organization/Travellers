Introduction to Seahouses

Seahouses is a charming coastal village located on the Northumberland coast, in North East England. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant fishing culture, Seahouses is a popular destination among tourists and locals alike.

Whether you’re looking to relax on the beautiful beaches, explore the historical landmarks, or enjoy some outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Seahouses. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Seahouses.

List of Places to Visit in Seahouses

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the local history and culture, there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore in Seahouses.

1. Seahouses Lifeboat Station

The Seahouses Lifeboat Station is a museum dedicated to the history of the RNLI lifeboats and their brave crews. It showcases the dramatic rescues that have taken place off the coast of Northumberland over the years.

2. Bamburgh Castle

Bamburgh Castle is a must-see attraction when visiting Seahouses. This historic castle dates back over 1,400 years and has a rich history that’s waiting to be explored.

3. Grace Darling Museum

Located in Bamburgh, the Grace Darling Museum is dedicated to the memory of Grace Darling, who, alongside her father, risked their lives to rescue survivors from the wreck of the SS Forfarshire in 1838.

Natural Attractions of Seahouses: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, there’s an abundance of outdoor adventures and scenic views to be found in Seahouses.

1. Farne Islands

The Farne Islands are a group of small islands located just off the coast of Seahouses. They are home to a variety of wildlife, including puffins, seals, and dolphins. Visitors can take a boat tour around the islands or even land on them to get up close and personal with the wildlife.

2. Beadnell Bay

Beadnell Bay is a beautiful sandy beach located just a short drive from Seahouses. It’s the perfect place to relax and soak up the sun, or try your hand at some beach sports, like volleyball or surfing.

3. Holy Island

Holy Island, also known as Lindisfarne, is a tidal island located off the coast of Northumberland. It’s home to an ancient monastery, a castle, and some stunning scenery. Visitors can only cross the causeway at low tide, so make sure to check the tide times before you go.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seahouses

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, there are plenty of hiking and biking routes to be found in and around Seahouses.

1. St. Cuthbert’s Way

St. Cuthbert’s Way is a long-distance footpath that starts in Melrose, Scotland, and finishes at Holy Island. The route passes through some stunning scenery, including the Eildon Hills, the Cheviot Hills, and the Northumberland Coast.

2. The Coastal Path

The Coastal Path runs along the Northumberland coast and offers some breathtaking views of the sea and countryside. It’s a great route for walking or cycling, and there are plenty of stops along the way to admire the scenery or grab a bite to eat.

3. The Sandstone Way

The Sandstone Way is a 120-mile mountain bike route that runs from Berwick-upon-Tweed to Hexham. It passes through some stunning scenery, including forests, moorland, and rugged coastline.

Best Things to Do in Seahouses: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just some fun activities, there’s plenty to do in Seahouses.

1. Boat Tours

Boat tours are a great way to explore the stunning coastline and wildlife around Seahouses. There are several tour operators in the village that offer trips to the Farne Islands, Holy Island, and beyond.

2. Fishing

Seahouses has a rich fishing history, and there are plenty of opportunities to try your hand at angling. Whether you want to go sea fishing, game fishing, or fly fishing, there are lots of options available in the area.

3. Golf

For golf enthusiasts, there are several courses in and around Seahouses, including Seahouses Golf Club, Bamburgh Castle Golf Club, and Dunstanburgh Castle Golf Club.

Week-Long Itinerary for Seahouses

Day 1: Explore Seahouses village, visit the Seahouses Lifeboat Station, and enjoy some fish and chips by the harbour.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Farne Islands and spot some puffins and seals.

Day 3: Visit Bamburgh Castle and explore the historic village of Bamburgh.

Day 4: Relax on the stunning Beadnell Bay and try your hand at some beach sports.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Holy Island, explore the ancient monastery and castle, and walk the Lindisfarne Castle Walk.

Day 6: Go hiking or biking along St. Cuthbert’s Way or the Coastal Path.

Day 7: Try your hand at some sea fishing or game fishing, or hit the greens at one of the local golf courses.

Food and Drink Scene of Seahouses

Seahouses is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local delicacies to try.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fish and Chips

No visit to Seahouses is complete without trying some classic fish and chips. You’ll find plenty of great fish and chip shops in the village, all serving up fresh, locally caught fish.

2. Craster Kippers

Craster Kippers are a smoked fish delicacy that are a must-try when visiting Northumberland. The fish are caught in local waters, smoked over oak chips, and served hot or cold.

3. Lindisfarne Oysters

Lindisfarne Oysters are a local delicacy that are harvested from the tidal flats around Holy Island. They’re highly prized for their sweet, succulent flavour.

Local Beverages

1. Real Ale

Northumberland is home to a thriving craft beer scene, and there are plenty of local breweries producing delicious real ales. Some of the best include Allendale Brewery, Hadrian and Border Brewery, and Cheviot Brewery.

2. Mackem and Geordie

Mackem and Geordie are two local beers that are brewed in Sunderland and Newcastle respectively. Both are delicious, easy-drinking beers that are perfect for a summer’s day.

3. Alnwick Rum

Alnwick Rum is a spiced rum that’s made in the town of Alnwick. It’s a smooth, warming drink that’s perfect for a chilly evening.

Where to Stay in Seahouses: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Seahouses has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

1. Bamburgh Castle Inn

The Bamburgh Castle Inn is a cosy hotel located in the heart of Seahouses village. It offers comfortable rooms, delicious food, and a warm welcome.

2. The Olde School House

The Olde School House is a charming bed and breakfast located in the picturesque village of Bamburgh. It’s a great option for those looking for a peaceful, rural retreat.

3. Hostels

Seahouses also has several hostels that offer affordable accommodation for budget-conscious travellers. Some of the best include YHA Berwick, Alnwick Youth Hostel, and Hatters Hostel Newcastle.

What to Buy in Seahouses: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to take home with you, there are plenty of options in Seahouses.

1. Kippers

Craster Kippers make a great souvenir and are a delicious reminder of your time in Northumberland.

2. Northumberland Tartan

The Northumberland Tartan is a unique souvenir that’s perfect for anyone interested in the history and traditions of the region.

3. Sea Glass Jewellery

Sea Glass Jewellery is made from pieces of sea glass that are found on the local beaches. It’s a unique and beautiful souvenir that’s sure to impress.

Local Culture and Customs of Seahouses

Seahouses is a friendly and welcoming village, and locals are proud of their rich history and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture and customs, and to take the time to learn about the area’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Seahouses

The best time to visit Seahouses is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the village is a popular destination year-round, and there’s always something going on, no matter when you visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seahouses

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the stunning natural beauty of the area.

  • Do try some of the local food and drink, including fish and chips, Lindisfarne Oysters, and Northumberland Tartan.

  • Do respect the local culture and customs, and be friendly and polite to locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the local environment.

  • Don’t forget to check the tide times before visiting Holy Island.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations or advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Seahouses

Seahouses is generally a safe and welcoming village, but it’s always important to take precautions to stay safe.

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended on the beach or in your car.

  • Be aware of the tides when visiting the coast, and never underestimate the power of the sea.

  • Follow local safety advice when participating in outdoor activities, like hiking or biking.

Health in Seahouses

Seahouses has several medical facilities, including a local doctor’s surgery and a pharmacy. Visitors are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Seahouses: Transportation Options and Logistics

Seahouses is located in Northumberland, in the north-east of England. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Seahouses

The nearest airports to Seahouses are Newcastle International Airport and Edinburgh Airport, both of which are around 1.5 hours’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near Seahouses

Seahouses has a small bus station that serves local and regional routes. The nearest major bus stations are in Alnwick and Berwick-upon-Tweed.

Other Options

Seahouses is also accessible by train, with the nearest railway stations in Chathill and Alnmouth. There are also several car rental companies in the area, making it easy to explore the surrounding countryside by car.

How to Get Around in Seahouses

Seahouses is a small village, and most of the local attractions are within walking distance of each other. There are also several local bus routes and taxi services available.

Where to Go Next from Seahouses: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Seahouses is ideally located for exploring the surrounding countryside and nearby attractions.

1. Alnwick Castle

Alnwick Castle is a historic castle located just a short drive from Seahouses. It’s the setting for Hogwarts in the Harry Potter movies and has a rich history that’s waiting to be explored.

2. Northumberland National Park

Northumberland National Park is a stunning area of natural beauty that’s perfect for hiking, cycling, and wildlife spotting. It’s located just a short drive from Seahouses.

3. Newcastle-upon-Tyne

Newcastle-upon-Tyne is a vibrant city with plenty of shopping, eating, and drinking options. It’s located around 1.5 hours’ drive from Seahouses.

Conclusion

Seahouses is a beautiful coastal village that offers something for everyone. With its rich history, stunning scenery, and vibrant culture, it’s the perfect destination for a relaxing break or an action-packed adventure. Plan your trip today and discover all that Seahouses has to offer.
