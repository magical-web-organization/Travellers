Introduction to Ford and Etal

Ford and Etal is a picturesque rural enclave in Northumberland, England. Located in the north-east of England, the region boasts scenic views, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The area is dotted with charming villages and market towns, each with its unique history and traditions. Visitors to Ford and Etal will enjoy hiking, biking, exploring historical sites, and immersing themselves in the local culture. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all that Ford and Etal has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ford and Etal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heatherslaw Cornmill: This 18th-century working water mill has been fully restored and is still operational. Visitors can see how flour is milled and can try tasty treats made from the freshly milled flour.
  • Etal Castle: This 14th-century castle ruins were once a critical location during the Anglo-Scottish wars in the Middle Ages. The castle is surrounded by verdant scenic views and is a great spot for a picnic or photoshoot.
  • Lady Waterford Hall: This moderately sized museum showcases the life and work of Victorian-era artist Louisa Waterford. The historic Victorian building also serves as a community center and hosts events throughout the year.
  • Ford and Etal Estate: This estate houses several historical buildings, including Etal Manor House, the Black Bull (a 16th Century coaching tavern), and the old railway station.

Natural Attractions of Ford and Etal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Flodden Battlefield: This 16th century battlefield marks where the English king Henry VIII met the Scots. Visitors can learn about this significant battle and stand in awe of its historical significance.
  • Ford Moss National Nature Reserve: This vast peatland reserve is teeming with wildlife and serves as a protected space for species like Black Grouse, Merlin, Hen Harrier, and Golden Plover.
  • Etal Woods: This charming, dense woodland encompasses 1,500 acres of ancient trees and is home to great wildlife and bird watching.
  • River Till: This charming river flows through the valley of Ford and Etal and is perfect for a riverside walk or a nature trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ford and Etal

  • Ford and Etal Estate Walk: This 6.6 mile circular walk winds through farmland and is the perfect way to take in the natural beauty and charm of the area.
  • Flodden 1513 Walk: This 5.6-mile route takes visitors through the site of the subsequent battle and is an ideal way to learn about the history of the region.
  • Etal to Berwick Walk: This 6.8-mile trek follows an old railway line path and takes hikers through picturesque woodland and offers excellent views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ford and Etal: Activities and Tours

  • Ford and Etal Estates Railway Tour: Visitors can take a scenic train ride through the beautiful countryside. The tour takes you on a 16-mile route and stops at various places of interest such as Etal Castle, Heatherslaw Mill, and more.
  • Fishing on the River Till: The Till River is rich in Salmon, Brown Trout, and Sea Trout. Angling is allowed seasonally, and visitors can spend a relaxing day fishing on the river.
  • Heatherslaw Light Railway: This charming train trip takes visitors on a one-mile-long journey through beautiful countryside, from the Etal station to Heatherslaw station.
  • Horse Riding: Visitors can take trails of Etal Woods on horseback, admiring the beautiful landscape and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ford and Etal

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Ford Village, visit Robson’s Choice or Ford Village Shop and Tea Room.
Day 2 Visit the Heatherslaw Mill and take a cornmill tour. After that, take a walk on the Ford and Etal Estate
Day 3 Visit Lady Waterford Hall and explore the region’s arts and crafts scene. After that, enjoy lunch at Cross Inn Pub.
Day 4 Explore Etal Village and visit Etal Castle. After that, take a walk on the River Till.
Day 5 Go hiking in the Flodden Field and enjoy the beautiful views of the valley.
Day 6 Take a trip on the Heatherslaw Light Railway and visit the Black Bull Inn. After lunch, enjoy art and craft shopping in the nearby galleries.
Day 7 Go horseback riding through Etal Woods and see the wildlife and landscapes. After that, enjoy an evening in the charming village of Etal.

Food and Drink Scene of Ford and Etal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Craster Kippers: A well-known cured fish dish across the UK, made in the nearby coastal village of Craster. It’s also available in local shops and restaurants in Ford and Etal.
  • Northumberland Sausage: A traditional sausage, rosemary and garlic flavors make it different from typical British sausages.
  • Pease Pudding: it is a delicacy made from boiled split peas with onions, garlic, and pepper often served with ham or bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Real Ale is widely available in pubs throughout Northumberland. A local favorite is the ‘Newcastle Brown Ale.’
  • Northumberland Gin: This famous gin is distilled using natural ingredients in a copper still, and visitors can take a tour of the distillery in nearby Alnwick.

Where to Stay in Ford and Etal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Estate Office: A quaint cottage situated in the beautiful Ford and Etal Estate. It can accommodate four people and offers a private garden with furniture and a BBQ area.
  • B&B’s: A few local B&Bs are available within the region, including the Hemmel Bed and Breakfast; this bed and breakfast is located in nearby Cornhill-on-Tweed and offers a luxurious and comfortable stay surrounded by scenic views.
  • Camping: Camping sites are available in the region for those who want to experience the great outdoors. Riverdale Hall Hotel and Caravan sites are a few options available in the region.

What to Buy in Ford and Etal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Northumberland Tweed: Northumberland is famous for its woolen products, and tweed is a must-buy here. At Heatherslaw Mill, visitors can buy beautiful and locally made tweed hats, caps, jackets, and trousers.
  • Local Art and Crafts: Visitors can take a trip to the region’s many art galleries and shops to purchase homeware, pottery, photographs, and paintings showcasing the local area.

Local Culture and Customs of Ford and Etal

Northumberland is a proud region with a rich cultural heritage. The people of Ford and Etal are friendly and welcoming, enthusiastic to share their local traditions with visitors. The region celebrates its history and culture through several annual events like agricultural shows, folk festivals, and other cultural events. The Northumberland County Show is one such event showcasing the region’s farming skills and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Ford and Etal

The best time to visit Ford and Etal is in late spring, early autumn, or the summer months. Visitors can enjoy the warm weather, the natural beauty of the region, and the many festivals that take place in summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ford and Etal

Do:

  • Respect the local environment; littering and damaging the natural habitats are not allowed.
  • Try the local cuisines, beers, and gin, made with local ingredients.
  • Take time to enjoy the scenic views of the area while exploring.

Don’t:

  • Damage the historical attractions or landmarks.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Show disrespect or disregard for the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ford and Etal

Ford and Etal is a safe region with low crime rates, visitors should still take basic precautions such as securing their valuables, especially when hiking or biking.

Health in Ford and Etal

Visitors should carry a well-equipped first aid kit when hiking and biking. Visitors should stay hydrated, wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the weather, and ensure that they are physically fit to undertake strenuous outdoor activities.

Getting to Ford and Etal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ford and Etal

The nearest airport to Ford and Etal is Newcastle International Airport which is about 45 miles away. Another option is the Edinburgh airport located 68 miles distance and offers various domestic flights and international connections.

Bus Stations Near Ford and Etal

Berwick-upon-Tweed Bus station is the nearest bus station to Ford and Etal.

Other Options

Visitors can use trains and taxis to reach the region. The closest railway station is Chathill station which is about 22 miles away.

How to Get Around in Ford and Etal

The best and most thrilling ways to take in the natural beauty of Ford and Etal is on foot, by bicycle, horseback riding, or by car. Visitors can opt for public transport options like buses and trains for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Ford and Etal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The beautiful Holy Island of Lindisfarne, situated off the Northumberland coast and is 26 miles from Ford and Etal.
  • The magnificent Alnwick Castle, which is located in the town of Alnwick, 15 miles distance from Ford and Etal.
  • The charming seaside town of Seahouses, famous for its sandy beaches and boat trips to the Farne Islands is 25 miles distance.

Conclusion

Ford and Etal is an ideal holiday destination, offering visitors scenic views, beautiful countryside, and rich cultural heritage and scenic views. It’s the perfect place for adventure and outdoor activities, with a wide range of attractions to cater to every visitor’s needs. From stunning castles to serene rivers, from history to arts, Ford and Etal, is a must-visit for every travel enthusiast.
