Introduction to Amble

Located in Northumberland, in the North East of England, Amble is a charming coastal town famous for its beautiful beaches, seafront walks, and seafood. With a population of around 7,000 people, Amble is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the UK and beyond.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Amble, we will explore the best things to see and do in this quaint town, including where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Amble

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amble has a rich history that can be discovered in some of its cultural attractions:

  • Amble Lifeboat Station: Find out about the important work carried out by the RNLI at this museum, which showcases the history of lifeboats and the brave crews that have saved countless lives off the coast of Northumberland.
  • Coquet Island: Take a boat tour to this tiny island off the coast of Amble to discover the ruins of a medieval monastery, a lighthouse, and a wildlife sanctuary that is home to puffins, seals, and other sea creatures.
  • Warkworth Castle: Just a short drive from Amble, this impressive castle is one of the best preserved in Northumberland. Explore the history of its medieval walls and towers, and enjoy the stunning views of the countryside from the top.

Natural Attractions of Amble: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will appreciate the natural beauty that surrounds Amble:

  • Northumberland Coast Path: This scenic walking route stretches for 62 miles along the Northumberland coast, and passes through Amble, Alnmouth, and other picturesque coastal towns. Enjoy stunning views of the sea, beaches, and countryside along the way.
  • Druridge Bay Country Park: Just a short drive from Amble, this 3-mile-long beach and nature reserve is a popular spot for birdwatching, cycling, and watersports. Hike or bike the trails, and enjoy a picnic with stunning views of the coastline.
  • Braidley’s Bay: A beautiful coastal area on the outskirts of Amble, Braidley’s Bay is a popular spot for fishing, kite surfing, and simply enjoying the scenery. Admire the boats and colorful houses lining the harbor, and watch the sun set over the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amble

Amble is a great place for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around the town:

  • Coquet Estuary Trail: Follow this 7.5-mile-long trail from Amble to Warkworth, and enjoy stunning views of the River Coquet estuary and the surrounding countryside.
  • Amble Circular Walk: This 4.5-mile-long circular walk starts and ends in Amble, and takes you along the coast and through the town’s parks, woods, and gardens.
  • The Sandstone Way: For a more challenging cycling route, try the Sandstone Way, a 120-mile-long trail that starts in Hexham and ends in Berwick-upon-Tweed, passing through Amble halfway through. Enjoy the beautiful scenery of the Northumberland National Park, and visit some of the charming towns and villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Amble: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re visiting Amble solo, with family or friends, there’s plenty to do and see in the town. Here are our top picks:

  • Amble Sea Safaris: Take a boat tour to Coquet Island, enjoy a dolphin watching expedition, or go fishing with Amble Sea Safaris. Learn about the town’s maritime heritage and the local wildlife, while enjoying the scenic views of the coast.
  • Amble Treasure Hunt Trail: Explore Amble’s hidden gems with this fun treasure hunt trail, which will take you on a 1.5-mile walk around the town’s landmarks and attractions. Solve clues, learn about the town’s history, and have fun with your companions along the way.
  • Amble Puffin Festival: Held every year in May, the Amble Puffin Festival is a celebration of the town’s most famous residents: the puffins. Enjoy guided walks, talks, and boat tours to Coquet Island to see these lovely seabirds up close.

If you have a week to spare in Amble, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Amble

A trip to Amble is not complete without trying some of its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Sandwich: Amble is famous for its fresh seafood, and a crab sandwich is the perfect way to taste it. Try one at The Old Boathouse or Sea&Soil, both located near the harbor.
  • Stottie Cake: This large, round, flatbread is a staple in Northumberland’s cuisine. Try it with ham and pease pudding at the Amble Bakery.
  • Sausage Roll: A British classic, the sausage roll is an Anglo-Scottish dish that can be found in most bakeries and cafes in town.

Local Beverages

  • Muddy Fingers Beer: Brewed locally in Alnwick, this beer is made using traditional methods and local ingredients. Try it at The Wellwood or The Friend’s of the Earth Shop in Amble.
  • Dandelion and Burdock: A traditional British soft drink made from dandelion and burdock roots. Available at most cafes and shops in town.
  • Gin: The Northumberland Gin Company produces a range of premium gins using local botanicals. Try them at the Harbour Brew House or the Radcliffe’s Bar in Amble.

Where to Stay in Amble: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amble has plenty of accommodation options for every budget and taste. Here are some of the best:

  • The Wellwood: Located in the heart of Amble, The Wellwood is a traditional pub with 6 cozy guest rooms. Enjoy the warm atmosphere and the delicious food and drink served at the pub.
  • The Old Boathouse: This B&B located near the harbor offers comfortable rooms with stunning views of the sea. Try their delicious homemade breakfast, and relax on the outdoor terrace.
  • Driftwood B&B: This charming guesthouse located a few miles from Amble offers stylish rooms with modern amenities. Enjoy the scenic views of the countryside, and explore the nearby beaches and nature reserves.

What to Buy in Amble: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amble is a great place to find unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some suggestions:

  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Beautiful jewelry made from sea glass collected on the local beaches. Available at the Harbour Artisan Gallery.
  • Northumbrian Tartan: A unique fabric made using Northumberland’s colors and patterns. Available at the Amble Pin Cushion.
  • Crab Pottery: Decorative pottery inspired by the town’s fishing heritage. Available at the Harbour Artisan Gallery.

Local Culture and Customs of Amble

Amble has a strong maritime and fishing heritage, which is reflected in its food, architecture, and customs. The town is also known for its friendly and welcoming community, and its love of festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Amble

The best time to visit Amble is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town holds its famous festivals, such as the Amble Puffin Festival and the Amble Seafood Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amble

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood, especially the crab and lobster.
  • Do explore the town’s hidden gems, such as the art galleries and studios.
  • Do take a boat tour to Coquet Island to see the puffins and other wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in the countryside.
  • Don’t feed the seagulls, as it can attract unwanted attention.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothes and a raincoat, as the weather can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amble

Amble is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables in cars or unattended in public places, and being aware of their surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Amble

The town has a pharmacy and a small medical center, but visitors should bring any necessary medications and insurance documents with them. In case of an emergency, call 999.

Getting to Amble: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amble

The closest airports to Amble are Newcastle International Airport (30 miles) and Edinburgh Airport (94 miles), both of which have regular flights from major UK and international cities.

Bus Stations Near Amble

Amble has a bus station located in the town center, with regular connections to other towns and villages in Northumberland.

Other Options

Visitors can also come to Amble by train, with the nearest stations located in Alnmouth (4 miles) and Morpeth (10 miles). Taxis and car rentals are available at the stations.

How to Get Around in Amble

The best way to explore Amble is on foot or by bike, as the town center is small and easy to navigate. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Amble: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Northumberland, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Alnmouth: This picturesque coastal village is just a short drive from Amble, and offers stunning views of the sea and the countryside.
  • Bamburgh Castle: One of the most iconic castles in the UK, Bamburgh Castle is located about 30 miles north of Amble, and offers a glimpse into the history of Northumberland’s kings and queens.
  • Holy Island: A small tidal island off the coast of Northumberland, Holy Island is known for its religious and historical significance, as well as its natural beauty. Visit the Lindisfarne Priory and Castle, and enjoy the stunning views of the sea.

Conclusion

Amble is a charming town that offers plenty of things to see and do for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing beach holiday, Amble has it all. Plan your trip today, and discover the beauty of Northumberland’s coast!
