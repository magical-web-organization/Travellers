Introduction to Northumberland National Park

Northumberland National Park is located in the northernmost county of England, Northumberland. The park covers an area of 1,049 square kilometres and is home to some of the most stunning landscapes in the country. The park offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and breathtaking natural scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Northumberland National Park

If you are planning to visit Northumberland National Park, here is a list of places you should consider visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Housesteads Roman Fort

Housesteads Roman Fort is one of the most well-preserved Roman forts in Britain. It is located on Hadrian’s Wall, which was built by the Romans to protect the northern boundary of their empire.

2. The Sill: National Landscape Discovery Centre

The Sill is an award-winning tourist attraction that offers visitors an opportunity to learn about the landscape, history, and culture of the region.

3. Hexham Abbey

Hexham Abbey is a beautiful medieval church that dates back to the 7th century. It is located in the town of Hexham, which is a short distance from the national park.

Natural Attractions of Northumberland National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Sycamore Gap

Sycamore Gap is one of the most photographed spots in the country, thanks to the solitary tree that stands in the middle of the valley.

2. Kielder Water and Forest Park

Kielder Water and Forest Park is home to the largest man-made lake in Northern Europe and the largest forest in England. It offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, fishing, and watersports.

3. Cheviot Hills

The Cheviot Hills are located on the border between England and Scotland and offer visitors breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. The hills are perfect for hiking and walking, and there are several well-marked trails that visitors can follow.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northumberland National Park

Northumberland National Park is a hiker’s paradise, with over 600 miles of trails to explore. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or a beginner, there are routes that suit all fitness levels. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Pennine Way
  • Hadrian’s Wall Path
  • St Cuthbert’s Way
  • Sandstone Way
  • Reivers Cycle Route

Best Things to Do in Northumberland National Park: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for things to do in Northumberland National Park, here are some activities and tours you might enjoy:

  • Star-gazing at Kielder Observatory
  • Wildlife safari at Northumberland National Park
  • Canoeing or kayaking on Kielder Water
  • Hang-gliding over the hills
  • Horse-riding on designated trails

Week-Long Itinerary for Northumberland National Park

For those who want to plan a week-long trip to Northumberland National Park, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Hadrian’s Wall and Housesteads Roman Fort Day 2: Explore Kielder Water and Forest Park Day 3: Explore the Cheviot Hills Day 4: Visit The Sill: National Landscape Discovery Centre and Hexham Abbey Day 5: Take a wildlife safari at Northumberland National Park Day 6: Go horse-riding or cycling on designated trails Day 7: Departure from the park

Food and Drink Scene of Northumberland National Park

Northumberland National Park is known for its locally sourced produce, including lamb, beef, seafood, and cheeses. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Craster kippers: smoked herring produced in the village of Craster
  • Stottie cake: a type of bread roll that is popular in the region
  • Pan haggerty: a type of potato and cheese bake, served with sausages or bacon

Local Beverages

  • Real ale: Northumberland is known for its excellent real ales, including those produced by the Hadrian and Border Brewery and Allendale Brewery.
  • Mead: fermented honey wine that has been produced in the region for centuries.

Local Culture and Customs of Northumberland National Park

Northumberland has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can discover more about the region’s history and traditions by visiting the museums, galleries, and other cultural attractions in the area. The region is known for its traditional music and dance, and there are several festivals and events throughout the year that celebrate these traditions.

Best Time to Visit Northumberland National Park

The best time to visit Northumberland National Park is between April and October when the weather is mild, and the flora and fauna are in full bloom. The summer months are particularly popular, but visitors should book accommodation and activities well in advance to avoid disappointment.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northumberland National Park

Do:

  • Take your litter with you when hiking or picnicking.
  • Respect the local wildlife and flora.
  • Follow the designated hiking and cycling trails.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the lakes or rivers.
  • Light fires outside designated areas.
  • Disturb or damage archaeological sites and historical artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northumberland National Park

Northumberland National Park is a safe destination for visitors, but hikers and cyclists should take basic safety precautions, such as carrying a first-aid kit, bringing plenty of water, and wearing appropriate footwear and clothing. Visitors should also be aware of the risks posed by ticks, which can carry Lyme disease.

Health in Northumberland National Park

Northumberland National Park does not have any major health risks, but visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance that covers outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling.

Getting to Northumberland National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northumberland National Park is located in the north-east of England, and there are several transportation options for visitors:

Airports Near Northumberland National Park

The nearest airport to Northumberland National Park is Newcastle International Airport, which is around 35 miles away. There are regular flights from major UK cities as well as Europe.

Bus Stations Near Northumberland National Park

There are several bus stations that serve Northumberland National Park, including Newcastle Bus Station and Alnwick Bus Station. Visitors can also take buses to the park from other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to drive can follow the A1 motorway, which runs through Northumberland National Park. There are also several car rental agencies in the region.

How to Get Around in Northumberland National Park

The best way to get around Northumberland National Park is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can also hire bikes or take walking tours of the region.

Where to Go Next from Northumberland National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are planning to extend your trip after visiting Northumberland National Park, here are some nearby destinations you might want to consider:

  • Edinburgh, Scotland
  • Durham City, County Durham
  • Alnwick Castle, Northumberland

Conclusion

Northumberland National Park is a beautiful region of England, with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery. Whether you are interested in hiking, cycling, or exploring the region’s museums and historical attractions, there is something for everyone in this park. Plan your trip in advance, book your accommodation and activities, and get ready to discover one of the hidden gems of the British landscape.
