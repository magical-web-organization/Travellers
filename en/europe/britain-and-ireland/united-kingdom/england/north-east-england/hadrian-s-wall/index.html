Introduction to Hadrian’s Wall

Hadrian’s Wall, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a historic fortification in North East England. Built by the Romans, it stretches for 73 miles from the Irish Sea to the North Sea. The wall was constructed in AD 122 during the reign of Emperor Hadrian, as a defensive structure against the Barbarian invasions in the north. The wall is not just a fascinating monument for history enthusiasts, but also offers scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural insights into the ancient Roman period.

List of Places to Visit in Hadrian’s Wall

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Housesteads Roman Fort

Located in the heart of Northumberland National Park, Housesteads Roman Fort is the most preserved Roman Fort in Britain. Visitors can explore the ruined fort, discovery museum and learn about the Roman way of life.

Vindolanda

Vindolanda is a Roman fort and museum that has a well preserved ancient toilet. This historic site is in a stunning country setting with nature walks nearby.

Roman Army Museum

The Roman Army Museum is a great site to see a full-size reconstruction of a Roman cavalry and learn about the daily life of Roman soldiers. Visitors can also enjoy interactive exhibits and watch reenactments of battle scenes and drills.

Natural Attractions of Hadrian’s Wall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sycamore Gap

Sycamore Gap, located near the iconic tree, offers a stunning view of the wall and surrounding area. The nearby fells have plenty of walking routes for outdoor enthusiasts.

Cawfields Quarry

Cawfields Quarry is popular with rock climbers, as it has plenty of limestone cliffs to climb. It also has stunning views over the wall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hadrian’s Wall

Hadrian’s Wall offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors of all levels. Some popular routes include the Hadrian’s Wall Path, which runs from end to end along the wall, and the Pennine Way, which crosses the wall at several points. Visitors can also explore the Northumberland National Park, which has plenty of hiking and biking trails and stunning views of the wall.

Best Things to Do in Hadrian’s Wall: Activities and Tours

Guided Tours

A guided tour of Hadrian’s Wall is one of the best ways to discover its history and significance. Visitors can choose from various tour companies and private tour guides.

Film Locations

Fans of popular TV show, “Game of Thrones”, can visit several Hadrian’s Wall locations, which were used for filming in the series. Some of the notable locations include Housesteads Roman Fort, Aydon Castle, and Walltown Quarry.

Stargazing

Hadrian’s Wall is a great location for stargazing due to its remote location and lack of light pollution. Visitors can watch the sky at the Northumberland National Park’s Stargazing Pavilion.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hadrian’s Wall

Here is a proposed week-long itinerary for Hadrian’s Wall:

Day 1: Arrive at Newcastle International Airport. Days 2-3: Explore Newcastle upon Tyne and nearby attractions, such as the Angel of the North and BALTIC Centre for Contemporary Art. Days 4-5: Discover Hadrian’s Wall, including visiting Housesteads Roman Fort, Vindolanda, and Roman Army Museum. Day 6: Explore Northumberland National Park and enjoy outdoor adventures, such as hiking or biking. Day 7: Visit local farms and taste locally produced food and drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Hadrian’s Wall

Must-Try Dishes

Hadrian’s Wall Beef

Hadrian’s Wall beef comes from a native breed of cows that graze the Northumberland countryside. The beef is known for its marbling and flavour.

Doddington Dairy Cheese

Doddington Dairy makes a variety of award-winning cheeses, made from their own herd of cows. Some of their popular cheeses include the ‘Mellow Yellow’ and ‘Berwick Edge.’

Local Beverages

Real Ale

Hadrian’s Wall Brewery produces a range of real ales, using local ingredients and ancient brewing methods. Some of their popular ales include “Wheest,” “Ouseburn Porter,” and “To the Wall”.

Organic Cider

The Once upon a Tree produces organic ciders in the local area with the orchard located in its own grounds.

Where to Stay in Hadrian’s Wall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors have plenty of accommodation options in Hadrian’s Wall, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the popular accommodation options include:

Langley Castle

Langley Castle is a luxurious castle hotel that offers stunning views over the Northumberland countryside and comfortable private rooms.

The Sill

The Sill is a hostel that offers accommodation to visitors of all ages. Guests can stay in private or shared rooms, and enjoy the on-site restaurant and cafe.

Stonecroft Bed and Breakfast

Stonecroft Bed and Breakfast offers comfy and affordable rooms, and a great location for exploring Hadrian’s Wall.

What to Buy in Hadrian’s Wall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Hadrian’s Wall can take home plenty of souvenirs and local products, such as:

Hadrian’s Wall Souvenirs

There are plenty of souvenir shops near the wall, which sell various souvenirs, such as t-shirts, postcards, and keyrings.

Willow Products

The Hadrian’s Wall Willow Company produces unique baskets and other decorative items made from locally grown willow.

Local Culture and Customs of Hadrian’s Wall

Hadrian’s Wall is an important site in English culture, with a rich history and a lasting impact on the country. Visitors can learn about the customs and traditions of the region through its festivals, events and museums.

Best Time to Visit Hadrian’s Wall

The best time to visit Hadrian’s Wall is during the summer, from June to August when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, visitors can also enjoy the autumn colours and winter landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hadrian’s Wall

Do:

  • Do be respectful of the historic sites.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.
  • Do pack for the weather and dress in layers.
  • Do carry a map of the area with you.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t touch or remove any artefacts or material from the sites.
  • Don’t smoke or drink alcohol in the historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hadrian’s Wall

Hadrian’s Wall is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Carry a mobile phone with emergency contacts programmed.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions, especially when walking outside.
  • Follow any safety guidelines provided by tour companies or parks.

Health in Hadrian’s Wall

Visitors to Hadrian’s Wall should take care of their health by:

  • Bringing all essential medicine.
  • Drinking plenty of water, especially during outdoor activities.
  • Following COVID-19 guidelines and restrictions in place.
