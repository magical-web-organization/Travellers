Introduction to Stockton-on-Tees

Located in County Durham, in the North East of England, Stockton-on-Tees is a thriving town surrounded by beautiful countryside and rich history. The town’s industrial past can still be seen in its landmarks and attractions, while its vibrant cultural scene and outdoor activities make it a great destination for visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Stockton-on-Tees

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Preston Park Museum and Grounds - This beautiful museum is set in stunning parkland and showcases Stockton’s industrial past with exhibits on steam engines, trains, and even an old sweetshop.
  • Stockton-on-Tees Parish Church - This stunning Gothic church is a prominent landmark in Stockton and dates back to the 13th century.
  • Ropner Park - A picturesque park with a lake, gardens, and a Victorian bandstand.

Natural Attractions of Stockton-on-Tees: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tees Barrage International White Water Centre - For adventure-seekers, this exciting centre offers white water rafting, kayaking, and canoeing.
  • Wynyard Woodland Park - A beautiful park with over 1500 acres of woodlands, grasslands, and wetlands. Perfect for a relaxing walk or bike ride.
  • Roseberry Topping - A tall hill with stunning views of the countryside. It’s a popular spot for hiking, climbing, and even paragliding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stockton-on-Tees

  • The Teesdale Way - A beautiful trail that follows the River Tees for 92 miles, starting in the Pennines and ending at the coast.
  • The Thornaby Trail - A 3-mile trail that runs through the heart of Stockton and showcases the town’s industrial heritage.
  • The Billingham Beck Valley Way - A 7-mile trail that connects Billingham to the outskirts of Stockton and features woodland, wetlands, and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Stockton-on-Tees: Activities and Tours

  • Stockton International Riverside Festival - This annual festival takes place in August and has performers from all over the world.
  • The Globe Theatre - A restored Elizabethan theatre in Stockton that hosts shows, performances, and events all year round.
  • Stockton-on-Tees Market - A bustling market that takes place every Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday, selling everything from fresh produce to clothing and crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Stockton-on-Tees

Must-Try Dishes

  • Parmo - This delicious dish is a local specialty and consists of breaded and fried chicken or pork, topped with béchamel sauce and cheese.
  • Stotties - A type of bread that’s popular in the North East and perfect for sandwiches.
  • Panackelty - A hearty stew made with meat, vegetables, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Ale - Stockton has a thriving brewery scene, with local ales available at many of the town’s pubs.
  • Teesside Sauce - A spicy sauce that’s often served with Parmo. It’s rumored to be made with secret ingredients.

Where to Stay in Stockton-on-Tees: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Parkwood Hotel - A luxurious hotel that’s perfect for those who want to enjoy the countryside while still being close to the town center.
  • Metro Inns Teesside - A budget-friendly option that’s located near to many of Stockton’s attractions.
  • Kingswood Guest House - A charming guesthouse that’s popular with couples and families.

What to Buy in Stockton-on-Tees: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Teesside Sauce - A great gift for foodies, Teesside Sauce can be found at many of Stockton’s delicatessens and markets.
  • Local Crafts and Artwork - Stockton has a thriving creative community, and you can find a range of beautiful handmade crafts and artwork in the town’s galleries and shops.
  • Middlesbrough Football Club Merchandise - For football fans, a souvenir from Middlesbrough Football Club is a great choice.

Local Culture and Customs of Stockton-on-Tees

  • Friendly Locals - Stockton is known for its warm and welcoming locals, who are always happy to help visitors.
  • Industrial Heritage - The town’s past as a hub of industry is still visible in many of its landmarks and attractions.
  • Sense of Community - Stockton has a strong sense of community, with many events and festivals taking place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Stockton-on-Tees

The best time to visit Stockton-on-Tees is between May and September when the weather is mild and sunny. Many of the town’s festivals and events take place during this time, making it a great way to experience the local culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stockton-on-Tees

Do:

  • Say hello to locals - Stockton has a friendly community who are always happy to chat.
  • Try the local dishes - The Parmo and Stotties are must-try dishes for foodies.
  • Take part in local events - Stockton has a thriving events scene, with many festivals and fairs taking place throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables on display - Like any town, Stockton can have its share of crime. Keep your valuables safe.
  • Overlook the parks and outdoor areas – Stockton offers a lot of outdoor activities and nature sites to everyone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stockton-on-Tees

Stockton is a safe town, but like any place, it is wise to be vigilant and aware of your surroundings. Make sure to keep your belongings secure and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Stockton-on-Tees

The healthcare system in Stockton-on-Tees is generally good, and there are many medical facilities in the town. It’s always wise to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected health issues

Getting to Stockton-on-Tees: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stockton-on-Tees

  • Durham Tees Valley Airport - A small airport that serves many UK and European destinations.
  • Newcastle International Airport - A larger airport that offers flights to many UK and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Stockton-on-Tees

  • Stockton High Street Bus Station - Located in the heart of the town and serving many local and regional destinations.
  • Middlesbrough Bus Station - A larger station that connects Stockton to many destinations in the North East and beyond.

Other Options

  • Train - Stockton has its own train station, with regular services to destinations across the North East and beyond.
  • Car rental - Visitors can rent a car from Durham Tees Valley Airport or from one of the many rental companies in Stockton.

How to Get Around in Stockton-on-Tees

Stockton is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Public transport options include buses and trains, while taxis are readily available in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Stockton-on-Tees: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Durham - A beautiful city with a UNESCO World Heritage Site cathedral and a picturesque castle.
  • Whitby - A pretty fishing town with cobbled streets and a stunning abbey.
  • York - A historic city with a range of attractions, including the famous York Minster cathedral and the Jorvik Viking Centre.

Conclusion

Stockton-on-Tees is an exciting destination that offers a range of attractions, from outdoor adventures and cultural experiences to delicious cuisine and vibrant nightlife. With its friendly locals and stunning countryside, it’s a must-visit for travelers who want to experience the best of the North East of England.
