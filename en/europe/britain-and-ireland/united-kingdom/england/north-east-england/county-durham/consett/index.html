Introduction to Consett

Consett is a town in County Durham, North East England. It is situated on the River Derwent and surrounded by beautiful countryside. Consett is a charming town that offers visitors a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and a vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Consett

Here are the top places to visit in Consett:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Consett Steelworks Heritage Centre: This museum tells the story of the steel industry in Consett and the surrounding area. Visitors can see artifacts, displays, and videos that document the history of the steelworks and their impact on the town and its people.
  • Blackhill and Consett Park: This beautiful park features lovely gardens, a lake, and a play area for children. It is a great place to relax and take in the natural beauty of the region.

Natural Attractions of Consett: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Derwent Walk: This walking and cycling route takes visitors through the beautiful countryside around Consett. It is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the region while getting some exercise.
  • Derwent Reservoir: This large, man-made lake is a popular spot for fishing, sailing, and kayaking. It is also a great place to take a scenic walk or bike ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Consett

Consett is surrounded by beautiful countryside, and there are many hiking and biking routes in the area. Here are a few of the best routes:

  • The Derwent Walk: This 11-mile walking and cycling route takes visitors through the beautiful countryside around Consett.
  • Consett to Edmundbyers: This challenging 14-mile hike takes visitors through some of the most stunning scenery in the region.
  • Waskerley Way: This 20-mile cycle route takes visitors from Consett to the stunning Waskerley Reservoir.

Best Things to Do in Consett: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours that visitors can enjoy in Consett. Here are a few of the best:

  • Consett and the Steelworks Tour: This tour takes visitors through the town and the Consett Steelworks Heritage Centre, where they can learn about the history of the steel industry in the region.
  • Derwent Reservoir Boat Tour: This boat tour takes visitors around the stunning Derwent Reservoir, allowing them to see the natural beauty of the area from a unique perspective.
  • Consett Park: This beautiful park is a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the region. Visitors can take a stroll or have a picnic in one of the lovely gardens.

Week-Long Itinerary for Consett

For visitors who want to spend a week in Consett, here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Consett and take a walking tour of the town. Visit the Consett Steelworks Heritage Centre and Blackhill and Consett Park.
  • Day 2: Take a bike ride along the Derwent Walk.
  • Day 3: Visit the nearby town of Durham and explore its historic sites and attractions.
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour of the Derwent Reservoir.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a day of hiking in the beautiful countryside around Consett.
  • Day 6: Take a food and drink tour of Consett, sampling the best local cuisine and beverages.
  • Day 7: Depart from Consett.

Food and Drink Scene of Consett

Consett has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many excellent restaurants, cafes, and bars. Here are a few of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stottie Cake: This traditional bread from the North East of England is a must-try in Consett.
  • Pease Pudding: This savory dish made from split peas is a staple of the North East cuisine.
  • Pan Haggerty: This hearty potato and cheese casserole is a popular comfort food in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Newcastle Brown Ale: This popular beer from the nearby city of Newcastle upon Tyne is a must-try when visiting Consett.
  • Broon Ale: Another popular beer from the North East that visitors should try.
  • Tanfield Railway Ale: This beer is brewed by the Tanfield Railway, a historic railway that runs through the area.

Where to Stay in Consett: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Consett, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few of the best:

  • The Manor House Inn: This charming inn offers comfortable rooms, delicious food, and a warm welcome.
  • The Anchor Inn: This historic pub and guesthouse offers cozy rooms, a friendly atmosphere, and excellent food and drink.
  • The Deneview: This modern guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, stunning views, and a peaceful location.

What to Buy in Consett: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Consett Steelworks Memorabilia: Visitors can find a range of memorabilia from the Consett Steelworks Heritage Centre, including books, posters, and other items.
  • Local Cheese and Produce: Visitors can purchase locally produced cheese and other food items at the Consett Farmers’ Market.
  • Local Crafts and Artwork: Visitors can find a range of locally produced crafts and artworks at the various craft fairs and markets in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Consett

Consett has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this through its museums, cultural festivals, and other events. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors can enjoy the relaxed pace of life in this charming town.

Best Time to Visit Consett

The best time to visit Consett is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is in full bloom. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s natural beauty and cultural attractions during the other seasons of the year.

Safety and Health

Safety in Consett

Consett is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any serious safety issues. However, visitors should take normal precautionary measures, such as locking their doors and keeping valuables secure.

Consett has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care if necessary. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.
