Introduction to Spennymoor

Spennymoor is a quaint town located in County Durham, in North East England. It is situated a few miles away from Bishop Auckland and Durham City. The town’s rich history, stunning natural attractions, and warm locals make it a must-visit destination for tourists. In this guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, and local customs to help you plan your trip to Spennymoor.

List of Places to Visit in Spennymoor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Are you a culture enthusiast? Spennymoor has plenty of attractions that will satisfy your inner history buff. If you’re interested in local history, you can visit Spennymoor’s Heritage Centre. Here you can learn about the town’s history through interactive displays. Alternatively, you can visit the Jubilee Park, which is home to a war memorial that honors the lives of local soldiers.

Another museum worth visiting is Locomotion, which exhibits the story of railway history. The museum has unique exhibits, including the railway to Scotland, the Shildon Signal Box, and many vintage locomotives. For art lovers, Spennymoor has an art gallery called the Spennymoor Settlement, showcasing the artwork of Norman Cornish, a famous local artist from the town.

Natural Attractions of Spennymoor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spennymoor has natural beauty that attracts visitors worldwide. The town has many natural attractions, including the Tunstall Reservoir. With its tranquil atmosphere and scenic views, the reservoir is an ideal destination for a relaxing picnic. Additionally, you can visit the Hardwick Park Country Park, which includes woods, lake, and formal gardens. You can enjoy activities such as orienteering, cycling, archery, and horseback riding.

If you love wildlife, Spennymoor has got you covered with more than ten nature reserves, including the Durham Wildlife Trust. You will love the Dene valley, which is a hidden gem near Durham city that offers stunning views of the River Wear.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spennymoor

Are you an outdoor enthusiast? Spennymoor offers plenty of biking and hiking routes. The Bishop Auckland Spur Line is an excellent choice for cyclists. This six-mile route follows the path of an old railway line, from Bishop Auckland to Spennymoor. There is also the Hardwick Park Walking Trail, where you can walk and enjoy the stunning gardens, lake, and woodland. If you prefer off-road exploration, the Waskerley Way is an ideal destination since it presents history and wildlife, and is perfect for runners and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Spennymoor: Activities and Tours

When it comes to activities, Spennymoor has plenty to offer. If you’re interested in sports, check out the local football, cricket or rugby clubs. For nature lovers, the Durham Wildlife Trust offers nature walks and talks, where you can learn about Spennymoor’s wildlife.

If you’re looking to explore the town’s history, you can join the Northern Pride Walking Tour, which uncovers the LGBTQIA+ history of Spennymoor. Additionally, the North East Art Collective offers a tour to explore the art scene of Tyneside and the Northeast. Finally, music lovers will enjoy the Durham Blues, Rhythm, and Rock Festival, which takes place in Bishop Auckland each year.

Food and Drink Scene of Spennymoor

Food lovers will be delighted to know that Spennymoor has a vibrant food scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Spennymoor has a good selection of fish and chips shops, and one of the most popular is placed on The Parade. You can also try local pies, pastries, and Melton Mowbray Pork Pies.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking for a drink to complement your meals, try the local ale and cider. You can find these beverages in most pubs around the town.

What to Buy in Spennymoor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spennymoor’s local products make perfect souvenirs to bring home. You can buy Melton Mowbray Pork pies, local cheese, jams, and chutneys. Additionally, local handicrafts, such as handmade pottery and art, make excellent mementos that will remind you of your trip to Spennymoor.

Local Culture and Customs of Spennymoor

The warm and welcoming locals are what make Spennymoor unique. Respect towards others is essential in the town, and visitors are expected to follow local customs. It is customary in the region, especially in Durham and surrounding towns, for men to greet women with a nod and a “Good morning” or “Good afternoon,” regardless of age, occupation, or social standing.

Best Time to Visit Spennymoor

The best time to visit Spennymoor is during the summer months, between June and August. During this time, the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities, and many attractions are open. Additionally, summer is a popular time for events and festivals, such as the Bishop Auckland Food Festival and the Durham Miners’ Gala Day.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spennymoor

Do:

  • Respect the locals and follow local customs.
  • Try local delicacies and drinks.
  • Enjoy nature and its beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment.
  • Damage the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spennymoor

Spennymoor has a low crime rate. However, visitors should be vigilant and take precautions, such as securing their valuables and avoiding dark streets or alleys at night.

Health in Spennymoor

If you need medical attention, the nearest hospital is located in Durham City. You can also visit the Spennymoor Health Centre for minor medical treatment.

Getting to Spennymoor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spennymoor is well connected to the rest of the region. You can reach the town by:

Airports Near Spennymoor

  • Durham Tees Valley Airport

Bus Stations Near Spennymoor

  • Durham Bus Station
  • Bishop Auckland Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: You can reach Spennymoor by train from stops such as Darlington, Durham, and Newcastle.
  • Car: Spennymoor is easily accessible by car from Durham and Newcastle.

How to Get Around in Spennymoor

Spennymoor has a reliable transportation system, including buses and taxis, that can take you around the town. Additionally, you can rent a car or bike to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Spennymoor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, you can visit nearby destinations, such as Bishop Auckland, Durham City, or the beautiful seaside town of Seaham.

Conclusion

Spennymoor is a fantastic holiday destination that offers natural beauty, vibrant culture, and warm locals. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip and create unforgettable memories during your stay in the town.
