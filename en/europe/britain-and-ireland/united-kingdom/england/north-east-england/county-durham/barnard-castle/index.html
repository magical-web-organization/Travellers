Introduction to Barnard Castle

Nestled in the heart of County Durham in North East England, Barnard Castle is a picturesque market town known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery. Named after its iconic 12th-century castle, which overlooks the River Tees, this charming town offers visitors a wealth of attractions, from world-class museums and galleries to winding hiking and biking trails.

List of Places to Visit in Barnard Castle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Barnard Castle is home to some of the most culturally significant attractions in England. These museums and historical sites offer visitors a glimpse into the town’s rich history:

  • The Bowes Museum: Considered one of the most beautiful museums in the UK, The Bowes Museum is home to an outstanding collection of art, ceramics, textiles, and furniture from around the world.
  • The Witham: Originally built as a marketplace in 1844, The Witham is now a thriving arts center that hosts a range of exhibitions, performances, and workshops.
  • Barnard Castle: Dominating the town’s skyline, this iconic castle is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. Visitors can explore the castle’s ruins and learn about its fascinating history at the on-site museum.

Natural Attractions of Barnard Castle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Renowned for its stunning natural scenery, Barnard Castle is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love to hike, bike, and explore. Some of the most popular natural attractions in the area include:

  • The River Tees: Running through the center of Barnard Castle, the River Tees is a beautiful spot for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.
  • The Tees Railway Walk: Following the route of a former railway line, this scenic walk offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The North Pennines AONB: Just a short drive from Barnard Castle, this designated Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty boasts some of the most dramatic landscapes in the UK, including rolling hills, wild moorland, and ancient forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barnard Castle

Barnard Castle offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore its beautiful countryside on foot or by bike. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area include:

  • The Teesdale Way: Following the course of the River Tees, this long-distance footpath passes through some of the most stunning scenery in County Durham.
  • The Coast-to-Coast Cycle Route: Starting in Whitehaven and ending in Sunderland, this iconic cycle route passes through Barnard Castle and takes in some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the UK.
  • The Hamsterley Forest: Located a short drive from Barnard Castle, this vast forest offers visitors a range of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for wildlife spotting and outdoor adventure activities.

Best Things to Do in Barnard Castle: Activities and Tours

In addition to its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery, Barnard Castle boasts a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in the area include:

  • The Barnard Castle Farmers’ Market: Held on the first Saturday of every month, this lively market offers visitors the chance to sample some of the best locally sourced produce in the region.
  • The Barnard Castle Ghost Walks: Led by experienced guides, these atmospheric tours take visitors on a journey through the town’s dark and mysterious history.
  • The Barnard Castle Heritage Trail: This self-guided walking tour takes visitors on a journey through Barnard Castle’s rich cultural and architectural history, passing through some of the town’s most iconic landmarks and hidden treasures.

Food and Drink Scene of Barnard Castle

Barnard Castle is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for visitors to sample local cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular local dishes in the area include:

  • Stotty Cake: A type of bread made with yeast, flour, and water, Stotty Cake is a local delicacy that is often served with cheese or ham.
  • Pease Pudding: Made from split peas, water, and seasoning, Pease Pudding is a popular side dish in the North of England.
  • Pan Haggerty: A hearty potato dish made with cheese, onions, and bacon, Pan Haggerty is a favorite comfort food in the region.

Local Beverages

Barnard Castle is home to a range of local breweries and distilleries, and visitors can sample some of the best beers, ales, and spirits in the area. Some of the most popular local beverages include:

  • Durham Brewery Beer: Founded in 1994, the Durham Brewery produces a range of award-winning beers and ales using traditional brewing methods.
  • The Hills Distillery Gin: Made with locally sourced botanicals, including juniper berries and citrus peel, The Hills Distillery Gin is a popular choice for visitors who want to sample the local spirits.

Where to Stay in Barnard Castle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barnard Castle offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxurious hotels and guesthouses to budget-friendly hostels and camping sites. Some of the best places to stay in the area include:

  • The Morritt Country House Hotel & Spa: Located in a beautiful historic building, this luxury hotel offers visitors all the amenities they need to feel pampered and relaxed.
  • The Three Horseshoes Inn: This cozy pub and guesthouse is a popular choice for budget-conscious travelers who want to experience the local hospitality.
  • The Redwell Inn: Situated just outside Barnard Castle, this family-run inn offers visitors a comfortable and welcoming base from which to explore the area.

What to Buy in Barnard Castle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Barnard Castle can find a range of locally made souvenirs and products to bring home, including:

  • Local arts and crafts: The town is home to a range of artisanal shops and boutiques, where visitors can find unique handmade goods, including jewelry, ceramics, and textiles.
  • Local produce: From handmade cheeses and artisanal bread to locally brewed beer and wine, Barnard Castle is a great place to stock up on delicious local treats.
  • Local art and photography: The town’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery have inspired a thriving artistic community, and visitors can find a range of paintings and photographs that capture the essence of the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Barnard Castle

Barnard Castle has a strong cultural identity, with a rich history and a thriving arts community. Visitors to the area can experience the local culture and customs through a range of activities and events, including:

  • The Barnard Castle Farmers’ Market: This lively monthly market is a great place to meet locals and sample some of the best locally sourced produce in the region.
  • The Witham Arts Center: This thriving arts center hosts a range of exhibitions, performances, and workshops, showcasing the best of the local artistic talent.
  • The Barnard Castle Heritage Trail: This self-guided walking tour takes visitors on a journey through the town’s rich cultural and architectural history, providing insights into the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Barnard Castle

Barnard Castle is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm and attractions. However, the best time to visit the area is between May and September, when the weather is mild and sunny, and many of the outdoor attractions are at their best. Visitors should note that some of the attractions and accommodations may be closed during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barnard Castle

Barnard Castle is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. Visitors should take the usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as avoiding dark alleys at night and keeping valuables securely stored.

Health in Barnard Castle

Barnard Castle has a range of medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance and check any vaccine requirements before traveling.

Where to Go Next from Barnard Castle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors who have explored all that Barnard Castle has to offer, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to discover, including:

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing break, this beautiful town in County Durham has something for everyone.
