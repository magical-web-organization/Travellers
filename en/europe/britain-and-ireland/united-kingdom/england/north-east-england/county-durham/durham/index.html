Introduction to Durham

Situated in County Durham, in the North East of England, Durham is a historic city famous for its breathtaking scenery, outstanding architecture, and rich cultural heritage. The city offers a blend of old-world charm and modern facilities that make it an ideal destination for tourists.

From its iconic castle and cathedral, which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites, to its rich cultural attractions and thriving food and drink scene, there is no shortage of things to do and see in Durham. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Durham has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Durham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Durham Castle: One of the most iconic landmarks of the city, the castle dates back to the 11th century and is an excellent example of Norman architecture.
  • Durham Cathedral: A magnificent cathedral that dates back to the 12th century, it is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • The Oriental Museum: A museum that houses an impressive collection of artifacts from across Asia.
  • Durham University Botanic Garden: A stunning garden that boasts a diverse range of plant species.

Natural Attractions of Durham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Durham Dales: A breathtaking area of unspoiled countryside that offers everything from gentle strolls to challenging hikes and bike rides.
  • High Force: One of England’s most spectacular waterfalls, High Force is a must-visit location for nature lovers.
  • Beamish Museum: The museum is a living, breathing open-air museum that gives an immersive experience of the lives of people in the North East.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Durham

  • The Weardale Way: A 72-mile footpath that follows the River Wear from its source in the Durham Dales to the point where it meets the North Sea.
  • The Waskerley Way: A walking and cycling path that runs along the route of an old railway line.
  • The Durham Coastal Footpath: A 11-mile trail that leads from Sunderland to Seaham, offering stunning views of the coast.

Best Things to Do in Durham: Activities and Tours

  • Durham Castle and Cathedral Tour: Get an inside look at two of the city’s most iconic landmarks.
  • Durham Food and Drink Tour: Sample some of the region’s finest food and drink, including local specialties like stottie cakes and Newcastle Brown Ale.
  • Durham Ghost Tour: Discover the city’s darker side on this eerie tour that explores the ghosts and legends of Durham.

Food and Drink Scene of Durham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stottie Cake: A thick, dense bread that is a staple of the region.
  • Pan Haggerty: A potato and cheese gratin dish that is a favorite of locals.
  • Durham Lamb: A succulent lamb dish made with locally sourced lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Newcastle Brown Ale: A dark brown ale that is popular throughout the North East.
  • Durham Gin: A locally-made gin that features botanicals and flavors inspired by the region’s countryside.

Where to Stay in Durham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Radisson Blu Durham: A luxury hotel situated in the city center, with views of the River Wear and Durham Castle.
  • The Kingslodge Inn: A cozy inn that offers comfortable accommodation in the heart of the city.
  • The Garden House Inn: A family-run guesthouse that offers a warm welcome and excellent service.

What to Buy in Durham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Durham Gin and Other Local Spirits: Bring home a bottle of locally produced gin or other spirits.
  • Handmade Crafts: Durham is home to a vibrant arts and crafts scene, with many handmade items available for purchase.
  • Durham Cathedral and Castle Souvenirs: Take home a piece of the city’s iconic landmarks, with souvenirs available both on site and in many local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Durham

With a rich history spanning back over 1,000 years, the people of Durham are proud of their cultural heritage and traditions.

One of the most famous of these is the Durham Miner’s Gala, otherwise known as “The Big Meeting”, which has taken place every year since 1871. The event celebrates the city’s mining heritage and attracts visitors from far and wide.

Other notable cultural events held in Durham throughout the year include the Durham Brass Festival, the Durham Book Festival, and the Durham International Jazz Festival.

Best Time to Visit Durham

The best time to visit Durham is in the summer months, when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, spring and autumn can also be good times to visit, as the city is less crowded and prices may be lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Durham

Do:

  • Do visit Durham Cathedral and Castle, two of the city’s most iconic landmarks.
  • Do try the local cuisine, such as stottie cake and Pan Haggerty.
  • Do attend one of the city’s cultural events or festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment, as Durham is known for its beautiful scenery and conservation efforts.
  • Don’t take photographs of people without their consent, as this is considered impolite by many locals.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather, as it can be unpredictable in the North East of England.

Safety and Health

Safety in Durham

Durham is generally considered a safe city for visitors, with low levels of crime. However, visitors should always take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding unlit areas after dark.

Health in Durham

Visitors to Durham should ensure they have adequate health insurance, as medical treatment in the UK can be expensive. The city has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, and visitors should ensure they have any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Durham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Durham

  • Newcastle International Airport: Located around 31 miles from Durham city center, Newcastle Airport is the nearest airport to Durham and offers flights to destinations throughout the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Durham

  • Durham Bus Station: Located in the city center, the bus station serves a range of local and regional routes.

Other Options

  • Train: Durham has a mainline railway station that offers connections to destinations throughout the UK, including London and Edinburgh.
  • Car: Durham can be easily reached by car via the A1(M) motorway.

How to Get Around in Durham

Durham is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot. There are also a range of public transportation options available, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Durham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newcastle: The vibrant city of Newcastle is located around 15 miles from Durham and offers a range of cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks.
  • Hadrian’s Wall: An iconic UNESCO World Heritage Site that is just a short drive from Durham.
  • Northumberland National Park: A stunning area of natural beauty that offers opportunities for hiking, horse riding, and adventure sports.

Conclusion

Durham is a captivating city that offers visitors the chance to explore a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural scenery. From its historic landmarks to its outdoor adventures and rich food and drink scene, there is something for everyone in Durham, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for a unique and memorable experience.
