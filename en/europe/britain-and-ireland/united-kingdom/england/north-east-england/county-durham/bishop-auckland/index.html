Introduction to Bishop Auckland

Bishop Auckland is a historic market town located in County Durham, in the North East of England. The town is famous for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. It has a population of over 26,000 and is a popular destination for tourists from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Bishop Auckland

If you’re planning a trip to Bishop Auckland, there are many places that you must visit. Here is a list of the top attractions that you should include in your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Auckland Castle: This 900-year-old castle is the former home of the Bishop of Durham and is now a popular tourist attraction. It features extensive gardens, art galleries, and a fascinating history.
  • The Mining Art Gallery: As the name suggests, the gallery showcases the work of mining artists, depicting the history and culture of the coal mining industry in County Durham.
  • Bishop Auckland Town Hall: This Grade II listed building is the hub of the town’s cultural life. It features an array of performances, exhibitions, and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Bishop Auckland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Weardale: This picturesque valley offers breathtaking views of the Pennine Mountains and the North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. You can go hiking, cycling, or take a scenic drive through the valley.
  • Escomb Saxon Church: Located in the outskirts of Bishop Auckland, this church dates back to the 7th century and is one of the finest examples of early Christian architecture in England.
  • Hamsterley Forest: A perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, this expansive forest has miles of hiking trails, cycling paths, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bishop Auckland

Bishop Auckland is a fantastic destination for hikers and cyclists, with several trails offering stunning views of the countryside and the town. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The Weardale Way: This 77-mile trail starts from the River Wear in County Durham and takes you through the stunning landscapes of Weardale.
  • Auckland Way: This 7-mile trail takes you through the countryside landscapes of the River Wear. You can enjoy the views and even spot the historic Auckland Castle.
  • Hamsterley Forest Trails: With over 2,000 hectares of forest, this destination offers numerous walking and cycling trails of varying difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Bishop Auckland: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the historical and natural attractions, there are many other things that you can do in Bishop Auckland. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Bishop’s Loop Railway: This train ride takes you on a scenic journey through the beautiful countryside and landscapes of Bishop Auckland.
  • Auckland Castle Deer Park: This park offers the perfect opportunity to spot herds of deer grazing, and relaxing on the lush greens amidst well-maintained gardens.
  • For an adrenalin-rush activity, visitors can go karting at Sunderland karting towards Sunderland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bishop Auckland

If you’re planning to spend a week in Bishop Auckland, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Auckland Castle, and its Historical Art Gallery
Day 2 Take a relaxed walk around Kapunda Park, with its heart-warming gardens and park grounds.
Day 3 Visit St. Peter’s Chapel for Mass or quiet contemplation; and spot the town’s iconic elephant statue.
Day 4 Spend a relaxed afternoon in Bishop Auckland Town Hall; catch a performance, or enjoy eating and drinking in the town’s exciting cafe-bar and Bristol Blue Glass makers’ shop.
Day 5 Relax in The Sitting Room* in front of a log fire and enjoy a classic tea.
Day 6 Go on a hiking trail in Hamsterley Forest, or enjoy a bike ride with friends and family.
Day 7 Grab a quick bite outdoors, and finally pick-up a souvenir before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Bishop Auckland

Bishop Auckland has a diverse selection of culinary options, ranging from traditional English to Indian and Korean cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A classic British dish that you must try when in Bishop Auckland. Golden crispy chips, sprinkled with salt and pepper served with the freshest catch of fish!
  • Sunday Roast: Typically offered as a traditional lunchtime dish, Sunday roast comprises of roast potatoes, a variety of vegetables, and your choice of meat, generally roasted beef.

Local Beverages

  • The Mandlebrot Brewing Company produces artisanal craft beer brewed using classic, all-natural ingredients. Visit its taproom for some excellent cask ales to sample this local beer culture.
  • The Whitechurch Inn is a charming establishment with an excellent selection of wine and cocktails.

Where to Stay in Bishop Auckland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Bishop Auckland, catering to all interests and budgets. Some of the popular options include:

  • The Manor House Hotel: Located in the heart of Bishop Auckland, this historic hotel boasts comfortable rooms and beautiful gardens.
  • The Park Head Hotel & Restaurant: Situated in County Durham, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, a bar & restaurant serving local cuisine featuring many fresh ingredients.
  • Durham House Hotel: A charming family-run boutique hotel located in the idyllic countryside surrounding Bishop Auckland.

What to Buy in Bishop Auckland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products that you can take home as a memory from Bishop Auckland, such as:

  • Local Artisan cheeses: The Cheese Shop, has a host of home-made Northumberland Cheeses to try before buying.
  • Wine Inns: Take home a bottle of artisanal craft beer, brewed by Mandlebrot Brewing Company, using classic, all-natural ingredients. Also, pick up some artisanal wines from the town’s Whitechurch Inn.
  • Durham Cathedral Shop: Take home a keepsake from the local markets, such as craft items, ceramics, art, and other unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bishop Auckland

Bishop Auckland is a friendly, welcoming community with strong local customs and traditions. Every year, the town hosts the famous Bishop Auckland Food and Drink Festival, showcasing local producers, restaurants, and bars. There are several other festivals hosted throughout the year, showcasing the local culture, music, and arts.

Best Time to Visit Bishop Auckland

The best time to visit Bishop Auckland is from late April to early September, during the spring and summer months, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the countryside is in full bloom. However, the town also has a vibrant autumn calendar, with several festivals celebrating the autumn colors and charms of North East England.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bishop Auckland

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Speak politely to people, being courteous to the elderly, and saying “thank you” and “please” when engaging in services like restaurants and bars.
  • Be respectful of the environment when going out on hikes or visiting parks and gardens.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any public or private property.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public areas and accommodations.
  • Make fun of local customs or be insensitive to cultural differences and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bishop Auckland

Bishop Auckland is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid petty theft and be cautious when traveling at night or in less-populated areas.

Health in Bishop Auckland

Bishop Auckland has a reliable healthcare system, including several medical centers, and nearby hospitals in Durham and other surrounding areas. Visitors should ensure they follow proper hygiene measures and carry any prescribed medication.

Getting to Bishop Auckland: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to reach Bishop Auckland is by train or by road. The town has its train station, Bishop Auckland Railway Station, served by Northern and CrossCountry trains. Alternatively, visitors can arrive at Newcastle Airport, which is about 30 miles away and offers several international and domestic flights.

Airports Near Bishop Auckland

  • Newcastle airport: Approx. 30 miles away from Bishop Auckland, handling several international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Bishop Auckland

  • Bishop Auckland bus station: Situated in the heart of the town, it is serviced by several bus operators, such as National Express, Megabus, and Arriva North East.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Visitors can hail taxis from several providers in and around the town.

How to Get Around in Bishop Auckland

Visitors can explore Bishop Auckland on foot, by bicycle, or via public transport. The town has several bus services that connect to nearby areas, and there are several bike rental and hire services available.

Where to Go Next from Bishop Auckland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Apart from Bishop Auckland, there are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore, such as:

  • Durham: Just 13 miles east of Bishop Auckland, Durham has a rich medieval history and is home to Durham Cathedral.

Conclusion

Bishop Auckland is a charming town with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re planning a long vacation or a short weekend getaway, Bishop Auckland is a fantastic destination that caters to all types of travelers.
