Stanhope is a charming town situated in County Durham, in North East England. Known for its idyllic countryside, rich history and culture, and stunning natural attractions, Stanhope has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to see and do in Stanhope, and give you insider tips to make the most out of your trip.

Introduction to Stanhope

Stanhope is situated in the heart of the North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, making it an ideal place for outdoor lovers and adventure seekers. The town itself is famous for its picturesque cottages and historic buildings, including the impressive Stanhope Castle. Visitors can also explore the many shops, cafes, and restaurants in the town centre.

List of Places to Visit in Stanhope

Stanhope has a variety of places to visit, from museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are the top attractions worth checking out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stanhope Heritage Centre - This museum tells the story of the town and its surrounding area, from the Roman era to the present day.

  • Killhope Lead Mining Museum - This award-winning museum provides insight into the region’s fascinating lead-mining history.

  • St Thomas’ Church - This ancient church dates back to the 12th century and features stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Stanhope: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Weardale Railway - Take a scenic train ride and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • High Force Waterfall - This powerful waterfall is located on the River Tees and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

  • The C2C Cycle Route - This 140-mile cycling route passes through Stanhope and provides scenic views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stanhope

Stanhope has numerous hiking and biking trails to choose from, ranging from easy walks to challenging routes. Here are the top options:

  • Pennine Way - This 267-mile trail passes through Stanhope and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Waskerley Way - This 11-mile trail follows a disused railway line and is ideal for cycling or walking.

  • Durham Dales Centre Walks - These walks range from 2-6 miles and provide a great introduction to the beautiful Stanhope countryside.

Best Things to Do in Stanhope: Activities and Tours

Stanhope offers numerous activities and tours, ranging from cultural experiences to adrenaline-fueled adventures. Here are the best things to do in Stanhope:

  • Biking and Hiking Tours - Join a guided tour and explore the stunning landscapes of Stanhope.

  • Bird Watching - Stanhope is home to a variety of birds, and visitors can take guided bird watching tours to spot them.

  • Historical Tours - Take a guided tour of the town’s historic buildings and archaeological sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stanhope

To make the most out of your trip to Stanhope, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town centre, visit Stanhope Castle and St Thomas’ Church.

Day 2: Take a scenic train ride on the Weardale Railway, visit the Killhope Lead Mining Museum, and go bird watching in the surrounding area.

Day 3: Hike the Pennine Way and enjoy the stunning views of the North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.

Day 4: Visit the High Force Waterfall and take a picnic in the surrounding countryside.

Day 5: Take a cycling tour of the Waskerley Way, and go shopping for local products to bring home as souvenirs.

Day 6: Take a pottery class and learn about the region’s rich ceramics tradition.

Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of the town’s spas or enjoy a pint of local beer in one of the town’s cosy pubs.

Food and Drink Scene of Stanhope

Stanhope has a thriving food and drink scene, with a wide range of local and international cuisine on offer. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • County Durham Lamb - Tender lamb sourced from local farms and roasted to perfection.

  • Stotty Cake - A traditional bread roll that’s unique to the North East, served with a variety of fillings.

  • Pan Haggerty - A hearty dish made with layers of potatoes, onions, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Durham Brewery Beer - This award-winning local beer is a must-try.

  • Teesdale Cider - Made with locally grown apples, this refreshing cider is perfect for a hot summer day.

  • County Durham Gin - This locally distilled gin is made with botanicals native to the North East.

What to Buy in Stanhope: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Stanhope

Stanhope is a small town with a rich history and culture. The locals are known for their warm hospitality, and visitors are encouraged to take part in local events and traditions, such as the annual Stanhope Agricultural Show.

Best Time to Visit Stanhope

The best time to visit Stanhope is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s stunning natural beauty during the autumn and winter months.

Safety and Health

Stanhope is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and good healthcare infrastructure. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety and wellbeing.

Safety in Stanhope

  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas at night.

  • Keep valuables out of sight and be aware of pickpockets.

  • Follow local guidelines when hiking and exploring the surrounding countryside.

Health in Stanhope

  • Make sure you have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

  • Be aware of the local wildlife when hiking and exploring the area.

  • Pack any prescription medication you need.

Where to Go Next from Stanhope: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Stanhope is a hidden gem in County Durham that is waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality, it’s a perfect destination for those seeking a relaxing and memorable trip.
