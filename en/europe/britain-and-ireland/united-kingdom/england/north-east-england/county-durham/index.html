Introduction to County Durham

Nestled in the North East of England lies the picturesque county of Durham. Known for its stunning countryside, historic landmarks, and proud industrial heritage, County Durham is a must-visit for anyone looking for a unique and exciting travel experience. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming county.

List of Places to Visit in County Durham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The county’s rich history is reflected in the numerous museums and historical landmarks that dot the landscape. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • Durham Cathedral, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic landmarks in the country
  • Beamish, The Living Museum of the North, an open-air museum showcasing life in the North East during the 19th and early 20th centuries
  • Auckland Castle, the former residence of the Prince Bishops of Durham and home to priceless works of art

Natural Attractions of County Durham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The county boasts beautiful countryside and scenic views that will take your breath away. Here are some natural attractions and outdoor adventures to explore:

  • High Force Waterfall, one of the most spectacular waterfalls in England
  • The North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, an unspoiled landscape of heather moors and peatlands
  • Durham Heritage Coast, a stretch of coastline that offers dramatic cliffs, secluded beaches, and wildlife encounters

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Durham

County Durham is a paradise for walkers and cyclists. There are numerous trails and routes to explore, each offering unique scenery and challenges. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a casual cyclist, there’s a trail for you to enjoy.

Best Things to Do in County Durham: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, County Durham offers a range of other activities and tours. Here are a few notable ones:

  • Take a guided boat trip along the River Wear and enjoy the views of Durham’s ancient bridges and landmarks.
  • Visit the Locomotion Museum and explore the history of railway engineering and transportation in the North East.
  • Go on a ghost tour of Durham city and learn about its haunted past.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Durham

If you’re planning a week-long trip to County Durham, here’s a recommended itinerary to make the most of your visit:

Day Activity
1 Visit Durham Cathedral and explore the city
2 Hike along the Durham Heritage Coast
3 Go on a guided boat tour of Durham
4 Visit Beamish, The Living Museum of the North
5 Explore the North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty
6 Visit Auckland Castle and its art collection
7 Cycle along the Waskerley Way and enjoy its scenic beauty

Food and Drink Scene of County Durham

County Durham boasts a thriving food and drink scene, with a range of local and internationally inspired dishes and drinks to enjoy. Here are a few must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pan Haggerty, a traditional North East dish made with potato, onions, and cheese
  • Stottie Cake, a local bread often used to make sandwiches and filled with meat and salad
  • Singin’ Hinnies, sweet scones served with butter and jam

Local Beverages

  • Durham Gin, a small-batch gin made with local botanicals and herbs
  • Wylam Brewery, a craft brewery that makes a range of flavorful beers
  • Mithril Ale, a speciality ale that’s brewed in Durham.

Where to Stay in County Durham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodations in County Durham, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels and guesthouses. Here are a few recommended places to stay:

  • The Kingslodge Inn, a charming inn located in the heart of Durham city
  • The Rose & Crown, a traditional pub with comfortable rooms and a fantastic restaurant
  • Seaham Hall, a luxurious hotel and spa with stunning sea views

What to Buy in County Durham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

County Durham is home to a range of locally made products and souvenirs that make great gifts for family and friends. Here are a few to look out for:

  • Durham Blue Cheese
  • Durham Mustard
  • Northumberland Tartan

Local Culture and Customs of County Durham

County Durham is steeped in history and traditions that are upheld to this day. The people are friendly and welcoming, and visitors can expect to encounter a range of customs and celebrations throughout the year. One such tradition is the Durham Miners’ Gala, a celebration of the county’s mining heritage that takes place every July.

Best Time to Visit County Durham

The best time to visit County Durham is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and the countryside is lush and green, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and scenic walks. However, visitors will find something to enjoy throughout the year, with each season offering its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Durham

Do:

  • Take time to explore the county’s charming villages and hamlets
  • Try the local food and drink, and attend one of the many food festivals held throughout the year.
  • Be respectful of the locals and their traditions and customs

Don’t:

  • Litter in the countryside, and always practice good environmental stewardship
  • Underestimate the ruggedness of some outdoor activities like hiking, and ensure you have appropriate gear.
  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats, and always observe from a respectful distance where possible.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Durham

County Durham is a safe region, with low crime rates and welcoming locals. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring remote or unfamiliar areas, and always practice good safety habits like locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in County Durham

County Durham has a well-developed healthcare system, with a range of hospitals and clinics throughout the region. Visitors are advised to carry appropriate health insurance and take necessary precautions and vaccinations where required.

Getting to County Durham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Durham

The nearest airport to County Durham is Newcastle International Airport, which is located approximately 30 miles away. Durham Tees Valley Airport is also another option, located roughly 20 miles away, but has fewer flights available.

Bus Stations Near County Durham

Durham has several bus stations that offer reliable transportation throughout the county and beyond. You can find more information on routes and schedules on the county council’s website.

Other Options

County Durham is easily accessible by car, with ample parking available in most areas. There are also several train stations throughout the region, with frequent services to major destinations like Newcastle and London.

How to Get Around in County Durham

County Durham is a small county, and most attractions and destinations are within easy reach of one another. Visitors can choose from several transportation options, including rental cars, public buses, trains, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from County Durham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

County Durham is ideally situated for exploring some of the other regions of Northern England. Here are a few recommended destinations:

  • The Lake District National Park
  • Yorkshire Dales National Park
  • The Scottish Borders

Conclusion

County Durham is a beautiful and fascinating region that offers something for everyone. Visitors can soak up the history and culture of the area, explore its stunning countryside, and enjoy delicious food and drink. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this hidden gem in the North East of England.
