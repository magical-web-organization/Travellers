Introduction to Gateshead

Gateshead is a vibrant town located in Tyne and Wear, North East England. It is rich in culture and history, offering a plethora of attractions for visitors to explore. Gateshead is also known for its natural beauty, with stunning views of the River Tyne and surrounding countryside. In this comprehensive travel guide to Gateshead, we will provide all the information you need to plan your trip. Discover the top places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Gateshead

There are many exciting places to visit in Gateshead, ranging from museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • BALTIC Centre for Contemporary Art: This museum is a major cultural landmark in Gateshead. It features exhibitions of contemporary art, as well as events, talks, and workshops.

  • Gateshead Millennium Bridge: This iconic bridge is a true engineering marvel, connecting Gateshead to Newcastle upon Tyne. It is a popular spot for sightseeing and photography.

  • Angel of the North: This famous public sculpture stands at 66 feet tall, with wings spanning 177 feet. It is a symbol of Gateshead and can be seen from miles around.

Natural Attractions of Gateshead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chopwell Wood: This lush forest is perfect for hiking, mountain biking, and picnicking. It covers 360 hectares of land and is home to an abundance of wildlife.

  • Derwent Reservoir: This beautiful lake offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can enjoy fishing, sailing, and walking trails.

  • Saltwell Park: This Victorian park offers plenty of green space, walking paths, and even a petting zoo. It is a great place to relax and enjoy the outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gateshead

Gateshead is a great destination for those who love hiking and biking. There are many scenic routes that offer breathtaking views of the countryside. Here are some of the best:

  • Hadrian’s Wall Path: This 84-mile trail follows the iconic Hadrian’s Wall, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It offers a challenging hike with stunning views.

  • Coast to Coast Cycle Route: This 140-mile cycle route takes you from the Irish Sea to the North Sea, passing through Gateshead along the way.

  • Gateshead Millennium Bridge Loop: This 3-mile route is a great option for those looking for a shorter walk or bike ride. It takes you over the Gateshead Millennium Bridge and offers great views of the River Tyne.

Best Things to Do in Gateshead: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums and outdoor attractions, there are many other fun things to do in Gateshead. Here are some popular activities and tours:

  • Newcastle Castle: This historic castle offers guided tours that take you through its 800-year history.

  • Geocaching: This treasure-hunting activity is a great way to explore Gateshead’s natural beauty. There are over 40 geocaches hidden throughout the town.

  • Beer Tasting Tours: Gateshead is home to several local breweries, and beer tasting tours are a great way to sample some of the best brews.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gateshead

To make the most of your time in Gateshead, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the BALTIC Centre for Contemporary Art and explore the Gateshead Millennium Bridge.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking at Chopwell Wood and enjoying nature.

Day 3: Take a tour of Newcastle Castle and learn about the town’s history.

Day 4: Explore the Angel of the North and visit Saltwell Park.

Day 5: Bike the Coast to Coast Cycle Route and enjoy the surrounding countryside.

Day 6: Visit the Derwent Reservoir and enjoy the water activities.

Day 7: Take a beer tasting tour and enjoy the local brews.

Food and Drink Scene of Gateshead

Gateshead has a thriving food and drink scene, with many local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stottie Cake: This traditional bread is a Gateshead staple. It is thick, round, and often served with bacon or sausage.

  • Pease Pudding: This savory dish is made with split peas, onions, salt, and pepper. It is often served as a side dish with meat.

  • Pan Haggerty: This potato and cheese casserole is a comfort food favorite in Gateshead.

Local Beverages

  • Newcastle Brown Ale: This classic beer is brewed in Newcastle upon Tyne and is a popular choice for locals and visitors alike.

  • Cherry Kola: This caffeine-free soda is made in the North East and is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

  • Broon Ale: This is another popular beer brewed in Gateshead. It is a dark ale with a rich flavor.

Where to Stay in Gateshead: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gateshead offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some popular choices:

  • Hilton Newcastle Gateshead: This upscale hotel offers stunning views of the River Tyne and is located close to many attractions.

  • Premier Inn Newcastle (Team Valley): This affordable hotel is located near the Team Valley retail park and offers comfortable rooms at a great price.

  • YHA Newcastle Central: This hostel is located in a Grade II listed building and offers private rooms and dorms.

What to Buy in Gateshead: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Gateshead, here are some popular souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Metro Pass: The Metro is the local train system and a Metro Pass can be a great souvenir for transportation enthusiasts.

  • Local Food Products: Gateshead is known for its bread, beer, and other local specialties. Consider taking home some local food products as gifts.

  • Angel of the North Souvenirs: Many shops sell souvenirs featuring the Angel of the North sculpture, such as keychains and t-shirts.

Local Culture and Customs of Gateshead

Gateshead has a proud industrial heritage, and the town is known for its hard-working and friendly people. When visiting Gateshead, it is important to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are some tips:

  • Be Polite: Gateshead locals are generally friendly and welcoming. It is important to be polite and respectful when interacting with them.

  • Show Appreciation for the Town’s History: Gateshead has a rich industrial history, and it is important to show appreciation for this during your visit.

  • Respect the Natural Environment: Gateshead is home to beautiful natural landscapes, and it is important to respect these areas by avoiding littering and being mindful of your impact on the environment.

Best Time to Visit Gateshead

The best time to visit Gateshead is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, be prepared for crowds during peak tourist season.

Safety and Health

Gateshead is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and reliable emergency services. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Gateshead

  • Be Aware of Pickpockets: As with any destination, it is important to be aware of pickpockets and keep your valuables secure.

  • Stay in Well-Lit Areas: It is advisable to stay in well-lit areas, especially at night.

  • Avoid Dangerous Areas: Gateshead is generally safe, but it is advisable to avoid any known dangerous areas.

Health in Gateshead

  • Stay Hydrated: It is important to drink plenty of water, especially during the summer months.

  • Pack Sunscreen and/or Umbrella: The weather can be unpredictable in Gateshead, so be prepared for both sun and rain.

  • Take Any Necessary Medications: If you have any pre-existing health conditions, be sure to bring the necessary medications with you.

Getting to Gateshead: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gateshead is easily accessible via several transportation options. Here are some details on getting to Gateshead:

  • Airports Near Gateshead: Newcastle International Airport is the closest airport to Gateshead, located just 20 minutes away by car or taxi.

  • Bus Stations Near Gateshead: Gateshead Interchange is the main bus station in the town, offering connections to Newcastle, Sunderland, and other nearby towns.

  • Other Options: Gateshead is also accessible by train, with the East Coast mainline passing through the town.

How to Get Around in Gateshead

Gateshead has a reliable and affordable public transportation system, including the Metro train system and buses. Taxis are also available, as well as bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Gateshead: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gateshead is located close to several other popular destinations in the North East of England. Here are some nearby places to consider visiting:

  • Newcastle upon Tyne: This lively city is located just across the River Tyne from Gateshead and is known for its nightlife, shopping, and cultural attractions.

  • Durham: This historic city is home to Durham Cathedral, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, as well as numerous other historic landmarks and attractions.

  • Alnwick: This medieval market town is home to Alnwick Castle, which was used in the filming of the Harry Potter movies.

Conclusion

Gateshead is a town rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you want to explore museums and historical landmarks or hike through the countryside, Gateshead has something to offer every traveler.
