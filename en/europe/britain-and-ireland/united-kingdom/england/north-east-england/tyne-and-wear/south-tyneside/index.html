Introduction to South Tyneside

South Tyneside is a borough located in the North East region of England. It is situated in Tyne and Wear, United Kingdom, and is a part of Britain and Ireland. South Tyneside has an extensive coastline, featuring stunning beaches, picturesque harbours, and scenic cliffs. This region is rich in history and culture and offers a wide array of attractions and activities for its visitors.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we have compiled everything you need to know about South Tyneside. From the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and where to go next, this guide has you covered.

List of Places to Visit in South Tyneside

Here is a list of must-visit attractions in South Tyneside:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arbeia Roman Fort and Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of Roman Britain and showcases exhibits providing a glimpse into daily life in a Roman army garrison.
  • South Shields Museum & Art Gallery: This museum features interesting exhibits about South Tyneside’s history, including its maritime past, the Roman fort, and an exhibition on the local legend of the Lambton Worm.
  • Tynemouth Priory and Castle: This historic site boasts impressive ruins of a 7th-century monastery and an exciting history dating back to the 11th century.

Natural Attractions of South Tyneside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jarrow Hall: This Anglo-Saxon farm village museum provides an insight into Jarrow’s past and how people lived long ago
  • Marsden Bay: A beautiful beach with stunning cliffs, perfect for hiking and sightseeing.
  • South Marine Park: A picturesque park with an ornamental lake and a miniature steam train to ride in.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Tyneside

South Tyneside has a vast network of hiking and biking trails, perfect for exploring the natural beauty of the North East region. Some of the top hiking/biking routes you should consider trying include:

  • The Hadrian’s Wall Path is perfect for both hiking and biking, and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • The C2C Cycle Route stretches from the west coast of England to the east coast and offers breathtaking coastal views and challenging hill climbs.
  • The Tyne and Wear Heritage Way is a lengthy route that takes visitors through some of the most well-preserved parts of Newcastle-upon-Tyne, Gateshead, and South Tyneside.

Best Things to Do in South Tyneside: Activities and Tours

Here are some fun activities and tours to make the most of your visit to South Tyneside:

  • For stunning views of the coast, head to the South Shields Lighthouse and take a guided tour.
  • For an experience like no other, visit Souter Lighthouse, the first lighthouse in the world designed specifically to use electricity.
  • For some fun in the sun, try sea kayaking or paddleboarding along the South Tyneside coast.
  • For a unique evening experience, join the Ghost Walk tour in South Shields and explore the town’s spooky history.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Tyneside

Here is an itinerary to help you make the most of your week-long stay in South Tyneside:

Day Activity
1 Tour of Tynemouth Priory and Castle
2 Visit South Shields Museum & Art Gallery and Arbeia Roman Fort and Museum
3 Go sea kayaking or paddleboarding along the coast
4 Take a guided tour of the South Shields Lighthouse
5 Visit Marsden Bay and explore the historic Marsden Grotto pub
6 Join a Ghost Walk tour in South Shields
7 Spend the day hiking/biking along the Tyne and Wear Heritage Way

Food and Drink Scene of South Tyneside

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in South Tyneside:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stottie - a flat round bread that can be used as a sandwich or filled with meats and cheeses
  • Pan Haggerty - a hearty casserole made of potato, onions, and cheese
  • Pease Pudding - a savory pudding made from split peas, served with ham or bacon

Local Beverages

  • Newcastle Brown Ale - a popular beer brewed in the region since 1927
  • Broons Bitter - a local ale from the nearby Wylam Brewery

Where to Stay in South Tyneside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Tyneside has a range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some popular hotels, guesthouses and hostels that you should consider during your visit:

  • Little Haven Hotel: A luxurious four-star hotel with stunning ocean views.
  • The Britannia Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with a cozy atmosphere.
  • YHA Tynemouth: A budget-friendly hostel located in an iconic building close to Tynemouth Harbor.

What to Buy in South Tyneside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Tyneside has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some popular options:

  • Handmade sea glass jewelry
  • Local beers and ales from Wylam Brewery
  • Hand-crafted pottery by local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of South Tyneside

South Tyneside has a rich culture and a strong sense of community. Here are some customs and traditions that you may encounter during your visit:

  • The annual South Tyneside Summer Festival, which features live music, beach volleyball, funfairs and other activities.
  • The Jarrow Festival, which celebrates the town’s history and traditions through a range of events.
  • The Great North Run, an annual half marathon that starts in Newcastle and finishes in South Shields.

Best Time to Visit South Tyneside

The best time to visit South Tyneside is during the summer months, between June and August. This is when the weather is mild, and the town is bustling with events and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Tyneside

Here are some tips to help you have an enjoyable visit to South Tyneside:

Do:

  • Take part in local festivals and events
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas
  • Be loud and disruptive in quiet areas
  • Underestimate the strength of ocean currents during beach visits

Safety and Health

Safety in South Tyneside

South Tyneside is a generally safe destination for visitors, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be mindful of traffic when crossing roads and always use designated crosswalks.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas.
  • Avoid empty streets and parks after dark.

Health in South Tyneside

South Tyneside has various medical facilities, including hospitals and pharmacies, to ensure visitors’ health and safety.

Getting to South Tyneside: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Tyneside is easily accessible from most parts of the United Kingdom. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near South Tyneside

The closest airports to South Tyneside are Newcastle International Airport and Durham Tees Valley Airport. Both airports serve domestic and international flights and are located less than an hour’s drive from South Tyneside.

Bus Stations Near South Tyneside

South Shields Bus Station is the primary station serving South Tyneside. It offers regular bus services to destinations throughout the region and beyond, including Newcastle, Sunderland, and beyond.

Other Options

South Tyneside is easily accessible by car and train as well.

How to Get Around in South Tyneside

South Tyneside is a relatively compact area and easy to navigate. Visitors can use local buses and trains to get around the area, or alternatively, they can rent a car or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from South Tyneside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Tyneside is well-connected to many nearby destinations that are worth a visit. Here are some popular options:

  • Newcastle-upon-Tyne: A vibrant city with a rich history, great nightlife and shopping.
  • Durham: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with a beautiful cathedral.
  • Hadrian’s Wall: A famous Roman monument and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

South Tyneside is a beautiful and historic destination that has something for everyone. It offers stunning scenery, rich culture, and an array of attractions and activities that make it an excellent destination for a short or extended break. We hope that this guide has helped you plan your visit to this fascinating destination.
