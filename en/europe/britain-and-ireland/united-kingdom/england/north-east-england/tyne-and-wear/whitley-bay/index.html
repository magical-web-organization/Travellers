Whitley Bay is a beautiful seaside town located in Tyne and Wear, in North East England. Known for its stunning coastline, rich history, and vibrant culture, Whitley Bay is a popular destination for travelers seeking a unique and authentic British experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Whitley Bay.

Introduction to Whitley Bay

Whitley Bay is a small coastal town located on the North Sea coast of England. It has been a popular holiday destination since the early 19th century, with visitors flocking to its picturesque beaches, lively amusement parks, and scenic promenades. In recent years, Whitley Bay has undergone significant redevelopment, with new restaurants, bars, and boutique shops cropping up throughout the town, making it an exciting destination for both locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Whitley Bay

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Whitley Bay, from natural attractions to cultural landmarks. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St. Mary’s Lighthouse and Visitor Centre: Located on a small island in Whitley Bay, St. Mary’s Lighthouse is a must-see attraction for history buffs and nature lovers alike. The lighthouse was built in 1898 and is now a popular museum, showcasing the rich history of the area.

  2. Seaton Delaval Hall: This 18th-century mansion is one of the most iconic historical landmarks in Whitley Bay. The hall boasts beautiful gardens, stunning architecture, and a rich history spanning over 300 years. Be sure to take a guided tour to make the most of your visit.

Natural Attractions of Whitley Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Whitley Bay Beach: This beautiful beach is one of the most popular attractions in Whitley Bay. With its long, sandy shoreline and stunning views of the North Sea, it’s the perfect place to relax, sunbathe, and enjoy the sea breeze.

  2. Brierdene Local Nature Reserve: This stunning nature reserve is located just outside Whitley Bay and boasts walking trails, woodlands, and a variety of wildlife. It’s the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the town and immerse yourself in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whitley Bay

Whitley Bay boasts some incredible hiking and biking trails that will take you off the beaten path and into some of the area’s most stunning scenery. Here are a few of our top picks:

  1. The Northumberland Coastal Path: This popular hiking trail stretches over 100 kilometers along the Northumberland coastline, including the coast near Whitley Bay. The trail offers stunning views of the sea and takes you past some of the area’s most iconic landmarks.

  2. Hadrian’s Cycleway: This popular biking trail follows the route of Hadrian’s Wall, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located just a short distance from Whitley Bay. The trail takes you through stunning countryside, rolling hills, and small towns, making it the perfect way to explore the local area.

Best Things to Do in Whitley Bay: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Whitley Bay. Here are a few of our top picks:

  1. Surfing and Watersports: With its proximity to the North Sea, Whitley Bay is an excellent destination for watersports enthusiasts. You can rent surfboards, kayaks, and paddleboards from local outfitters and hit the waves.

  2. Amusement Parks: Whitley Bay is known for its lively amusement parks, which offer fun for the whole family. Visit the Ocean Beach Pleasure Park for classic carnival games, thrill rides, and delicious food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Whitley Bay

Whitley Bay is a small town, but there’s still plenty to see and do. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Whitley Bay

Whitley Bay boasts a fantastic food and drink scene, with plenty of local delicacies to try. Here are a few of our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips: No trip to the seaside town is complete without trying the local fish and chips. Head to one of the many chip shops in Whitley Bay for this classic British dish.

  2. Stotties: A stotty is a type of bread that originated in the North East of England. It’s a delicious and hearty staple of the local cuisine and is often filled with ham, cheese, or other toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Brown Ale: Brown ale is a type of beer that originated in Newcastle, just a short distance from Whitley Bay. Try a bottle of the local brew at one of the many pubs around town.

  2. Durham Gin: Durham Gin is a small-batch gin made with locally sourced botanicals. It’s flavorful, refreshing, and a great way to unwind after a day of exploring.

  2. The Rex Hotel: The Rex Hotel is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms, a prime location, and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Whitley Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Whitley Bay with you, there are plenty of local souvenirs and products to choose from. Here are a few of our top picks:

  1. Seaside-Inspired Art: Whitley Bay is home to many talented artists who draw inspiration from the sea. Visit one of the local galleries for a unique piece of art to hang in your home.

  2. Local Food and Drink: Stock up on local delicacies like stotties, brown ale, and Durham Gin to bring home as souvenirs or gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Whitley Bay

Whitley Bay is a friendly and welcoming town, with locals who are proud of their heritage and eager to share it with visitors. To truly immerse yourself in the local culture, try attending one of the many festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Whitley Bay Carnival or the Northern Lights Festival.

Best Time to Visit Whitley Bay

Whitley Bay is a popular destination year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re looking for warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities, visit in the summer months. For those who prefer milder temperatures and fewer crowds, fall and spring are excellent times to visit. Winter is also a great time to visit if you’re looking for a cozy seaside getaway.
