Introduction to Borrowdale

Located in the northern Lake District, Borrowdale is a picturesque valley that’s renowned for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by mountains, woodlands, and rivers, this stunning region is home to a range of outdoor activities, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re an avid hiker, a history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Borrowdale has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to do, see, and experience in Borrowdale, as well as provide practical tips and advice on everything from transport to local customs.

List of Places to Visit in Borrowdale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Honister Slate Mine - Take a tour of the last remaining slate mine in England, and learn about the 800-year-old history of this industry.
  • Lodore Falls - Visit this stunning waterfall, which has inspired countless poets and artists over the years.
  • Castle Crag - This hill fort was once a strategic point of defense, and offers some of the best views in the area.
  • St. Andrew’s Church, Rosthwaite - This historic church dates back to the 19th century, and is known for its stunning stained glass windows and peaceful setting.

Natural Attractions of Borrowdale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Derwentwater - Hire a boat, go wild swimming, or simply take a stroll along the shore of this serene lake.
  • Ashness Bridge - This iconic packhorse bridge offers stunning photo opportunities at any time of day.
  • Catbells - This popular summit offers panoramic views of the surrounding fells and lakes.
  • Allerdale Ramble - This 54-mile walking route takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borrowdale

  • Haystacks - This challenging hike takes you to the summit of one of the Lake District’s most iconic peaks.
  • Coledale Horseshoe - This circular walk takes in four fells in the area, and offers stunning views throughout.
  • Whinlatter Pass - This tough mountain bike route is not for the faint-hearted, but the views from the top are well worth the effort.
  • Grange Fell and Watendlath Tarn - This moderate hike takes you through picturesque woodlands and past a beautiful mountain tarn.

Best Things to Do in Borrowdale: Activities and Tours

  • Waterfall Tours - Take a guided tour of some of the most beautiful waterfalls in the area, including Lodore Falls and Sour Milk Gill.
  • Wild Swimming - Take a dip in one of the many natural lakes and rivers in the area, such as Derwentwater or the River Derwent.
  • Orienteering - Try your hand at map reading and navigate your way through the beautiful Borrowdale landscape.
  • Fly Fishing - Borrowdale is renowned for its excellent fishing, particularly for trout and salmon.

Food and Drink Scene of Borrowdale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland Sausage - This local delicacy is a must-try for meat lovers, and is often served with creamy mash and onion gravy.
  • Herdy Burger - This juicy burger is made from locally-sourced Herdwick lamb, and is a delicious option for lunch or dinner.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding - This sweet dessert is a Lake District classic, and is often served with custard or ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Jennings Brewery - This local brewery has been producing award-winning beers for over 180 years, and offers tours and tastings.
  • Lakeland Gin - This artisan gin is made with locally foraged botanicals and water from the fells.
  • Hawkshead Relish - This family-run business produces a range of delicious chutneys, jams, and preserves, which are perfect for a picnic or gift.

Where to Stay in Borrowdale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Scafell Hotel - This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains, as well as excellent food and drink.
  • YHA Keswick - This affordable hostel is perfect for budget travelers, and is located in the heart of Keswick town.
  • Dalegarth House - This charming guesthouse is situated in the peaceful village of Portinscale, and is within walking distance of Derwentwater.

What to Buy in Borrowdale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Herdwick Wool Products - This locally-sourced wool is renowned for its durability and distinctive grey color, and is used to make a range of products, from blankets to socks.
  • Cumberland Sausage - Take some of this delicious sausage home with you and recreate the taste of the Lake District in your own kitchen.
  • Lakeland Gin - This artisan gin is the perfect way to toast your Borrowdale adventure.

Local Culture and Customs of Borrowdale

  • Sheep farming - Agriculture, particularly sheep farming, is a hugely important part of local culture, and has been a way of life for centuries in the area.
  • Fishing - Borrowdale is renowned for its excellent fishing, and locals take great pride in their angling skills.
  • Music and poetry - The Lake District has inspired countless poets and musicians over the years, and there are regular cultural events and festivals in the area.

Best Time to Visit Borrowdale

The best time to visit Borrowdale is between May and September, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the region’s stunning natural beauty is on display all year round, and there are plenty of indoor attractions to enjoy during the cooler months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Borrowdale

Do:

  • Take your time - Borrowdale is a place to be savored, so take your time and enjoy the stunning scenery and local culture.
  • Respect the environment - The Lake District is a fragile ecosystem, so make sure you take care not to damage it during your visit.
  • Try the local food and drink - The Lake District is renowned for its delicious cuisine, so make sure you sample some of the local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment - The Lake District is a UNESCO World Heritage site, and it’s up to all of us to help preserve it for future generations.
  • Be loud or disruptive - Borrowdale is a peaceful and tranquil place, so make sure you respect the locals and don’t disturb the peace.
  • Ignore the weather - The weather in the Lake District can be unpredictable, so make sure you’re prepared and pack the right gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Borrowdale

Borrowdale is generally a safe and welcoming place, but there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure your visit is trouble-free. Stick to marked paths when hiking, and be aware of the weather conditions before you set out. There are also occasionally sheep on the roads, so be vigilant when driving.

Health in Borrowdale

Borrowdale is a relatively remote area, so it’s important to be prepared when it comes to health. Make sure you bring any medication you require with you, and check with your doctor if you have any concerns. There are pharmacies and medical services available in nearby towns and villages, but it’s always better to be safe than sorry.

Getting to Borrowdale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Borrowdale

The closest airport to Borrowdale is Manchester Airport, which is about two hours’ drive away. There are regular train and coach services between Manchester and nearby towns such as Penrith and Keswick, which is the nearest town to Borrowdale.

Bus Stations Near Borrowdale

Keswick and nearby towns such as Penrith and Cockermouth have regular bus services to Borrowdale. Bus timetables can be found online, and tickets can usually be purchased on board or in advance.

Other Options

Driving is the easiest way to get around Borrowdale and the surrounding area, as it gives you the most freedom to explore. Car rental services are available at Manchester Airport, as well as in nearby towns such as Penrith.

How to Get Around in Borrowdale

Walking and cycling are the best ways to get around in Borrowdale, as they allow you to fully appreciate the stunning scenery and local culture. There are also regular bus services between nearby towns and villages, as well as taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Borrowdale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Carlisle - This historic city is home to a range of cultural attractions and historic landmarks, including Carlisle Castle and the Tullie House Museum and Art Gallery.
  • Windermere - This popular tourist destination is known for its stunning lakeside setting, as well as its vibrant food and drink scene.
  • Hadrian’s Wall - This historic landmark is just a short drive from Borrowdale, and is well worth a visit for its rich cultural history.

Conclusion

Borrowdale is a true gem of the Lake District, with its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming local community. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, there’s something for everyone in this picturesque valley. From hiking and cycling to food and drink, there’s no shortage of things to do, see, and experience in Borrowdale. So why not pack your bags and explore this beautiful corner of England for yourself?
