Introduction to Cockermouth

Nestled in the heart of Cumbria, in North West England, lies the charming town of Cockermouth. With its rich history, stunning scenery, and welcoming locals, this town is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic English experience. From its picturesque streets lined with shops and cafes, to its breathtaking natural wonders, Cockermouth has something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, hike, eat, stay, and more in Cockermouth.

List of Places to Visit in Cockermouth

Cockermouth is bursting with attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you are interested in the town’s rich history or its natural beauty, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Cockermouth:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wordsworth House and Garden: The childhood home of poet William Wordsworth is a beautifully preserved Georgian house that brings the poet’s world to life. Visitors can explore the house and gardens and learn about the life and times of one of England’s most famous poets.
  • Jennings Brewery: Beer lovers will enjoy a visit to this historic brewery. Established in 1828, Jennings Brewery is the oldest in the Lake District and visitors can tour the brewery and learn about the history of beer-making in the region.
  • Cockermouth Castle: The ruins of this 12th-century castle are a must-visit for history buffs. Located on a hilltop overlooking the town, the castle offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Cockermouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake District National Park: Just a short drive from Cockermouth is the stunning Lake District National Park. Offering incredible natural beauty, the park is a mecca for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the country.
  • Buttermere Lake: This beautiful lake is situated in the heart of the Lake District and offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can hike around the lake or simply relax and take in the views.
  • Whinlatter Forest Park: This expansive forest park offers miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cockermouth

Cockermouth is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with miles of trails and paths to explore. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  • The Cumbria Way: This 70-mile trail takes visitors through some of Cumbria’s most stunning landscapes, including the Lake District National Park.
  • Lorton Vale Circular: This 11-mile circular hike offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding mountains and the beautiful Lorton Valley.
  • Whinlatter Forest Park Biking Trails: With over 24 miles of marked biking trails, Whinlatter Forest Park offers biking enthusiasts a chance to explore the stunning countryside on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Cockermouth: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours that Cockermouth has to offer:

  • Horse Riding: Experience the stunning Lake District landscapes on horseback with one of the many horse riding tours available in the area.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: With several rivers and lakes in the area, there are plenty of opportunities for visitors to explore the waterways by canoe or kayak.
  • Ghost Walks of Cockermouth: Take a guided ghost walk through the historic streets of Cockermouth and learn about the town’s spooky past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cockermouth

For visitors who have the luxury of spending a week in Cockermouth, here is an itinerary that will help you experience the best that the town has to offer:

1 Visit Wordsworth House and Garden, explore the historic streets of Cockermouth

Food and Drink Scene of Cockermouth

Cockermouth boasts a fantastic food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local cuisine to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland Sausage: Made with locally raised pork, Cumberland sausage is a staple of the region. Rich in flavor and texture, it’s a dish not to be missed.
  • Grasmere Gingerbread: This sweet and spicy gingerbread is a delicious treat. Made with ginger, candied peel, and a blend of spices, it’s the perfect accompaniment to a cup of tea or coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Cockermouth is home to several fantastic breweries, including Jennings Brewery. Visitors can try a variety of locally brewed ales in the town’s pubs and bars.
  • Cumbrian Whiskey: This smooth and smoky whiskey is made from the pure water and grains of Cumbria and is a must-try for any whiskey lover.

Where to Stay in Cockermouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cockermouth offers a variety of options for accommodations, from charming guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in the town:

  • The Trout Hotel: This boutique hotel offers luxurious accommodations with stunning views of the River Derwent.
  • Embleton Spa Hotel: Located just outside of Cockermouth, this hotel offers guests a chance to relax and unwind in a beautiful country setting.
  • The Old Posting House: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Cockermouth and offers comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Cockermouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Cockermouth will find no shortage of unique souvenirs and local products to take home with them. Here are some of the best items to look out for:

  • Cumberland Sausage and other local meats: Made with locally raised pork and other meats, these delicious products are a great way to taste the flavors of the region.
  • Gifts and Crafts from local shops: From handcrafted jewelry to locally produced pottery, there are plenty of unique gifts and crafts to be found in the town’s many shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Cockermouth

Cockermouth is a proud and welcoming community, with a rich history and strong sense of tradition. Visitors to the town will find warm and friendly locals who are happy to share their traditions and customs with visitors. Some of the town’s most important cultural events include:

  • Cockermouth Georgian Fair: This annual fair celebrates the town’s rich history and traditions with parades, street performers, and traditional foods and crafts.
  • Words by the Water Literary Festival: This annual festival brings together some of the world’s best-known writers and thinkers for a week of talks and discussions in the town’s historic venues.

Best Time to Visit Cockermouth

The best time to visit Cockermouth depends on what you’re looking for. The town is beautiful year-round, but the weather can be unpredictable. If you’re looking for outdoor activities, the summer months of June to August offer the warmest temperatures and longest days. If you’re interested in the area’s cultural events and festivals, spring and autumn are the best times to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cockermouth

Do:

  • Explore the town on foot: Cockermouth’s historic streets and buildings are best explored on foot. Take your time and enjoy the town’s unique charm.
  • Try the local cuisine: Cockermouth is home to some delicious local dishes and beverages. Don’t be afraid to try something new.
  • Respect local customs and traditions: Cockermouth is a proud and welcoming community with a strong sense of tradition. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Cockermouth is home to some of the UK’s most beautiful landscapes. Help keep them clean and litter-free.
  • Leave valuables in your car: Like any tourist destination, Cockermouth can be targeted by thieves. Don’t leave valuables in your car or unattended in public places.
  • Disrespect local wildlife: The Lake District is home to a variety of unique and beautiful wildlife. Respect their habitats and keep a safe distance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cockermouth

Cockermouth is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take some basic precautions to ensure their safety. These include:

  • Keeping valuables out of sight and secure
  • Sticking to well-lit public areas at night
  • Taking extra care when hiking or biking in remote areas

Health in Cockermouth

Cockermouth has excellent medical facilities, including doctors, clinics, and hospitals. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling and take basic precautions, such as using sunscreen and insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Cockermouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cockermouth is easily accessible by car, train, bus, or plane. Here are some of the best transportation options:

Airports Near Cockermouth

  • Manchester Airport is the closest major airport to Cockermouth, located approximately 115 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Cockermouth

  • There are several bus routes that serve Cockermouth, with connections to many major towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also travel to Cockermouth by train, with connections from many major UK cities.
  • Taxis and car rental services are also available in the town.

How to Get Around in Cockermouth

Cockermouth is a small town and is best explored on foot or by bike. For longer journeys, there are several options for transportation, including taxis, buses, and car rental services.

Where to Go Next from Cockermouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cockermouth is the perfect base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Keswick: This charming market town is located just a short drive from Cockermouth and offers stunning views of Derwentwater lake.
  • Scafell Pike: The highest mountain in England, Scafell Pike is a challenging but rewarding hike for experienced hikers.
  • Hadrian’s Wall: This World Heritage Site is located just a short drive from Cockermouth and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Conclusion

Cockermouth is a hidden gem in the heart of Cumbria, offering visitors stunning natural beauty, rich history, and charming local culture. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Cockermouth has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to experience the best that Cockermouth has to offer and make memories that will last a lifetime.
