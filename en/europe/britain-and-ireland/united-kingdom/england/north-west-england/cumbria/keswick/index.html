Introduction to Keswick

Nestled in the heart of Cumbria, Keswick is a charming market town that attracts visitors with its stunning Lake District National Park setting, picturesque architecture, and friendly locals. This guide will help you discover everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Keswick, England.

List of Places to Visit in Keswick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Keswick Museum offers exhibits covering the town’s history and culture, including the discovery of graphite and the poetry of William Wordsworth.
  • Castlerigg Stone Circle is a prehistoric site with 38 stones, thought to have been erected over 4000 years ago.
  • Mirehouse & Gardens is a beautiful historic house and gardens dating back to 1666, with stunning views of Bassenthwaite Lake.

Natural Attractions of Keswick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Derwentwater is a legendary lake that features tranquil walks, breathtaking vistas, and water activities, such as kayaking, sailing, and stand-up paddleboarding.
  • Catbells Lakeland Walk is a popular hiking route that offers a fantastic view of Derwentwater and the surrounding fells that is particularly stunning at sunrise.
  • Whinlatter Forest Park provides adventure seekers with mountain biking trails, tree-top nets, zip lines, and bushcraft experiences.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keswick

Keswick has an array of hiking and biking trails that suit all skill levels, including:

  • Helvellyn is a challenging hike that rewards climbers with impressive views of the Lake District’s highest peak.
  • Skiddaw offers several walking trails and summits at over 3000 feet, affording hikers excellent vistas across the towns, valleys, and lakes in the region.
  • The Borrowdale Bash is a favourite route for mountain bikers, as it takes riders to some of the most spectacular places in the Lake District.

Best Things to Do in Keswick: Activities and Tours

  • Theatre by the Lake offers a diverse range of plays, both classic and contemporary, performed in a stunning setting with stunning views.
  • Keswick Brewery Tours provides visitors with an opportunity to learn about craft beer production and try some top-quality beers
  • Alhambra Cinema is a charming town cinema that screens a mix of mainstream and independent films, and also hosts events such as live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keswick

Day 1: Arrive in Keswick, walk around the town center, and visit the Keswick Museum.

Day 2: Take a leisurely boat ride across Derwentwater, have a picnic by the lake, and hike up Catbells for sunset views.

Day 3: Go for a scenic drive around the region, taking in places like Buttermere and Glenridding, and stop for tea at a local tea room.

Day 4: Enjoy an invigorating day of mountain biking in Whinlatter Forest Park, discovering the trails overlooking Bassenthwaite Lake.

Day 5: Indulge in some local culture, exploring the Mirehouse and Gardens, before enjoying an evening at the Theatre by the Lake.

Day 6: Embark on a challenging day hike, such as Skiddaw or Helvellyn.

Day 7: Reflect on your adventures in Keswick over lunch at a local pub before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Keswick

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland sausage is a local delicacy that is a must-try for carnivorous foodies.
  • Kendal Mint Cake is a sweet treat that is a perfect energy booster during a day hike, loved by walkers and mountaineers worldwide.
  • Herdwick Lamb is a succulent dish that features one of Lake District’s most iconic breeds of sheep.

Local Beverages

  • Jennings Bitter Ale is a locally brewed beer that is the perfect complement to a hearty meal.
  • Lakeland Gin is a small-batch gin that uses locally sourced botanicals, such as Lake District juniper berries and heather, to create a unique flavour.

Where to Stay in Keswick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Keswick has accommodation options to meet every style and budget, such as:

  • The Lodore Falls Hotel & Spa is an upscale country house hotel with an award-winning restaurant, spa facilities, and stunning views.
  • YHA Keswick Hostel offers affordable backpacker-style accommodation, with shared dorms and private rooms in one of the town’s most charming historic buildings.
  • Lairbeck Hotel is a family-owned guesthouse, set in tranquil gardens with beautiful views, offering friendly service and excellent home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Keswick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lake District Art is a unique reminder of Keswick’s stunning scenery, with works from local artists featured in galleries all over town.
  • Handmade Confectionery such as fudge, toffee, chocolates, and Kendal mint cake can be found in local shops.
  • Sheepskin Products such as slippers, gloves, and hats are made from the wool of the iconic Herdwick sheep.

Local Culture and Customs of Keswick

The people of Keswick have a deep appreciation for their town’s history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to engage with locals, who are known for their friendliness and warm hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Keswick

The best time to visit Keswick is from April to October when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keswick

Do:

  • Do try classic dishes like Cumberland sausage and Herdwick lamb.
  • Do go on a boat ride on Derwentwater or take a scenic drive around the surrounding areas.
  • Do try local drinks like Jennings Bitter Ale and Lakeland Gin.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, especially in natural areas such as national parks and beauty spots.
  • Don’t park in prohibited areas or obstruct roads and driveways in the town center.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and manners, such as greetings and pleasantries, which are highly valued in the Keswick community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keswick

Keswick is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should take care when hiking and mountain biking, especially in remote areas where access to medical assistance may be limited.

Health in Keswick

Keswick has several medical facilities, including a general hospital, health center, and pharmacy. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance and carry their prescription medication, if needed.

Getting to Keswick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Keswick is situated in the North West of England in Cumbria, close to the Scottish Borders and approximately two hours from both Manchester and Newcastle.

Airports Near Keswick

  • Newcastle International Airport is the closest major airport, located 67 miles away.
  • Manchester Airport is the most popular airport for tourists, located 93 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Keswick

  • Keswick Bus Station serves regional and national routes, with frequent services to major cities.

Other Options

  • Train Station Penrith train station is 18 miles away and has direct services to London, Edinburgh, and Manchester.
  • Car Rental is a popular option for those wanting to explore the region independently.

How to Get Around in Keswick

The best way to get around Keswick is on foot, although there are bike rental options available too. Public transport and taxis are options for those who are not able to walk long distances.

Where to Go Next from Keswick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ambleside is a popular tourist destination that is located approximately 30 minutes south of Keswick, known for its art galleries, museums, and gardens.
  • Ullswater is another stunning lake that is a 40-minute drive from Keswick, offering similar activities and viewpoints as Derwentwater.

Conclusion

Keswick offers a unique holiday experience, catering to both nature lovers and culture enthusiasts. With its warm hospitality, stunning scenery and local charm, visiting Keswick is an unforgettable experience you won’t want to miss.
