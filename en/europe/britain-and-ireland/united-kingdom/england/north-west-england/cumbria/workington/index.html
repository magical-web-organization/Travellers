Introduction to Workington

Workington is a town located in Cumbria, in North West England, United Kingdom. It is situated at the mouth of the River Derwent and is surrounded by picturesque countryside, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Workington was once a major industrial hub, with a rich history dating back to the Roman era.

List of Places to Visit in Workington

Whether you’re interested in culture and history, outdoor adventures, or just want to relax and soak up the scenery, there is plenty to see and do in Workington. Here are some of the top tourist attractions to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jennings Brewery – A family-owned brewery that has been producing award-winning ales since 1828. Take a tour to learn about the brewing process and sample some of their delicious beers.
  • Wordsworth House and Garden – The childhood home of the famous poet William Wordsworth, this 18th-century Georgian house has been restored to its former glory and offers a glimpse into life in the 1700s.
  • Senhouse Roman Museum – Located on the site of an ancient Roman fort, this museum displays a fascinating collection of Roman artefacts, including a rare early Roman cavalry helmet.

Natural Attractions of Workington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake District National Park – Just a short drive from Workington, the Lake District is a popular destination for hiking, biking, fishing, and boating. This stunning national park is home to the highest mountain in England, Scafell Pike, as well as many beautiful lakes and forests.
  • St. Bees Head – A dramatic headland on the Cumbrian Coast, St. Bees is a great place to hike and enjoy the panoramic views of the Irish Sea.
  • Cumberland Wildlife Park – This 67-acre park located just outside Workington is home to over 150 different species of animals, including tigers, rhinos, and kangaroos. It’s a great place to spend the day with the family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Workington

Workington is surrounded by beautiful scenery and has many hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best trails for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Coast to Coast Cycle Route – A challenging 140-mile cycle route that takes you from Workington to the east coast of England.
  • Workington to Keswick Walk – A 20-mile hike that takes you along the Cumbrian Coast and through the beautiful Lake District National Park.
  • St. Bees Head Circuit Walk – A moderate 4.5-mile walk that offers panoramic views of the Cumbrian Coast and the Irish Sea.

Best Things to Do in Workington: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fun things to do in Workington, here are some activities and tours to consider:

  • Fishing – Workington is a popular destination for fishing, with several great spots along the River Derwent and nearby lakes.
  • Golfing at Workington Golf Club – This 18-hole golf course offers stunning views of the Cumbrian Coast and is suitable for players of all levels.
  • West Coast Motorsports – A great place to try your hand at karting, quad biking, and archery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Workington

If you’re planning to spend a week in Workington, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Workington and explore the town centre.

Day 2: Visit Jennings Brewery and the Senhouse Roman Museum.

Day 3: Take a drive to the Lake District National Park and spend the day hiking or biking.

Day 4: Visit Wordsworth House and Garden and St. Bees Head.

Day 5: Spend the day at Cumberland Wildlife Park.

Day 6: Try your hand at fishing or golfing at Workington Golf Club.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby towns like Keswick or Whitehaven.

Food and Drink Scene of Workington

If you’re a foodie, you won’t be disappointed by the local cuisine in Workington. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland Sausage – A traditional English sausage made with pork, herbs, and spices, and typically served with mashed potatoes and gravy.
  • Grasmere Gingerbread – A sweet and spicy gingerbread made from a secret recipe created in the 1850s.
  • Cumberland Rum Nicky – A traditional dessert made with dates, rum, and pastry.

Local Beverages

  • Jennings Ale – A range of award-winning beers brewed locally in Workington.
  • Cumberland Farmhouse Cider – A refreshing cider made from locally grown apples.

Local Culture and Customs of Workington

The people of Workington are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a rich cultural heritage. Some customs to be aware of when visiting include:

  • Cumberland Wrestling – This traditional form of wrestling has been practiced in Cumbria for centuries and is still popular today.
  • Cockermouth Agricultural Show – This annual event held in nearby Cockermouth celebrates local agriculture and farming traditions.

Best Time to Visit Workington

The best time to visit Workington is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm and dry, and the countryside is at its most beautiful.

Getting to Workington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Workington is easily accessible from major cities in the UK. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Workington

Bus Stations Near Workington

  • Workington Bus Station – Located in the town centre, this station offers regular services to surrounding towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Workington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of the Lake District and beyond, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

Conclusion

Workington is a hidden gem in North West England, with plenty of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities to offer. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing retreat in the countryside or an adventure-packed holiday, Workington has something for everyone.
