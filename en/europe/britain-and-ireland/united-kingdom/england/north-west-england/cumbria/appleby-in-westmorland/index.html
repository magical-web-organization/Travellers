Introduction to Appleby-in-Westmorland

Appleby-in-Westmorland, commonly known as Appleby, is a charming market town located in the Eden Valley of Cumbria, North West England. With a population of around 3,000 people, it is a peaceful and picturesque town that is steeped in history and surrounded by stunning natural scenery. Appleby dates back to the Roman times and has a rich cultural heritage that can be seen in its museums, historic buildings, and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Appleby-in-Westmorland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Appleby Castle: A beautifully restored castle that dates back to the 12th century. Visitors can take a tour and enjoy stunning views of the town from the top of the castle walls.
  • St. Lawrence’s Church: A historic church that boasts beautiful stained-glass windows and Gothic architecture. It is also home to the tomb of Lady Anne Clifford, a prominent figure in English history.
  • Appleby Heritage Centre: A small museum that showcases the history of Appleby, including its Roman, medieval, and Victorian past.
  • Appleby Grammar School: A historic school that was founded in the 16th century and has produced many notable alumni, including William Wordsworth.

Natural Attractions of Appleby-in-Westmorland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • River Eden: A scenic river that runs through the town and is perfect for a leisurely walk or picnic.
  • Pennine Way National Trail: A 268-mile-long hiking trail that passes through Appleby and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Barnard Castle: A nearby town that is home to a beautiful castle and scenic waterfalls.
  • High Cup Nick: A breathtaking natural amphitheater that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Appleby-in-Westmorland

Pennine Way National Trail

The Pennine Way National Trail is a 268-mile-long hiking trail that passes through Appleby and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is divided into 20 sections, with Appleby located on section 6. This section of the trail offers stunning views of the Eden Valley and the nearby Howgill Fells. It is a challenging but rewarding hike that is sure to impress even the most experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Appleby-in-Westmorland: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding: Appleby is home to several horse riding schools that offer lessons and guided tours of the surrounding countryside.
  • Fishing: The River Eden is known for its excellent fishing, and there are several fishing spots in and around Appleby.
  • Appleby Golf Club: A scenic golf course that offers stunning views of the Eden Valley.
  • Appleby Fair: A famous annual event that takes place in June and includes horse trading, carnival rides, and live music.

Food and Drink Scene of Appleby-in-Westmorland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland Sausage: A traditional sausage made with pork, herbs, and spices.
  • Grasmere Gingerbread: A spicy gingerbread that is a staple of the Lake District.
  • Kendal Mint Cake: A sweet, mint-flavored confection that is popular with hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Local Beverages

  • Cumbrian Ale: This locally brewed ale is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Damson Gin: A delicious gin made with locally sourced damson berries.

Where to Stay in Appleby-in-Westmorland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tufton Arms Hotel: A historic hotel that offers comfortable rooms and great food.
  • Royal Oak Appleby: A cozy pub that also offers comfortable guest rooms.
  • The Midland Hotel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Appleby-in-Westmorland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cumbrian Cheese: A delicious cheese that is produced in the nearby Lake District.
  • Local Crafts: Appleby is home to many talented artists and craftspeople, so make sure to browse the local shops for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Appleby-in-Westmorland

Appleby has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its many festivals and traditions. The annual Appleby Fair is a famous event that dates back to the Middle Ages and is known for its horse trading and carnival rides.

Best Time to Visit Appleby-in-Westmorland

The best time to visit Appleby-in-Westmorland is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are at their best.

Safety and Health

Safety in Appleby-in-Westmorland

Appleby is a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should still take basic precautions such as locking their doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Appleby-in-Westmorland

Visitors should take care to stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun while enjoying outdoor activities in the summer months.

Getting to Appleby-in-Westmorland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Appleby-in-Westmorland

The nearest airports to Appleby are Manchester Airport and Newcastle Airport, both of which are around 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Appleby-in-Westmorland

The nearest bus stations to Appleby are in Penrith and Kendal, both of which are around 30 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to the Appleby train station, which is located in the town center.

How to Get Around in Appleby-in-Westmorland

Visitors can easily get around Appleby on foot, as it is a small town with many pedestrian-friendly streets. Taxis are also available for those who need them.

Where to Go Next from Appleby-in-Westmorland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hadrian’s Wall: An ancient Roman wall that stretches across northern England and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Appleby-in-Westmorland is a charming and historic town that is perfect for visitors looking for a peaceful and authentic English experience. With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and unique cultural traditions, it is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to North West England.
