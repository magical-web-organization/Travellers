Introduction to Kendal

Located in the county of Cumbria in North West England, Kendal is a vibrant market town with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. It is known as the ‘Gateway to the Lake District National Park’ and is situated on the banks of the River Kent, which runs through the town. Kendal is a popular destination for tourists who want to experience the beauty of the Lake District, explore local history and culture, and enjoy outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Kendal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kendal has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore local history and traditions by visiting some of its museums and historical landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Kendal Castle: This impressive castle ruins date back to the late 12th century and offer panoramic views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • Abbot Hall Art Gallery: This gallery is home to an impressive collection of fine and decorative arts, including works by renowned artists such as JMW Turner, Barbara Hepworth, and Henry Moore.
  • Museum of Lakeland Life and Industry: This museum offers a glimpse into the past of Kendal and the surrounding Lake District. Visitors can explore exhibits on farming, mining, and local traditions and crafts.
  • Kendal Parish Church: This historic church dates back to the 13th century and features impressive architectural details and stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Kendal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kendal is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, and visitors can explore the great outdoors by hiking, biking, or kayaking in the nearby Lake District. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions:

  • Sizergh Castle and Garden: This impressive castle is surrounded by beautiful gardens and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Kendal Green: A large park located in the center of town, Kendal Green offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation, including walking, jogging, and picnicking.
  • Kentmere Reservoir: This beautiful reservoir is perfect for hiking and enjoying picturesque views of the Lake District.
  • Whitbarrow Scar: This beautiful limestone escarpment is a great spot for hiking, with several trails offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kendal

Hikers and bikers will love exploring the beautiful trails of the Lake District, which offer stunning views of rugged mountains, turquoise lakes, and peaceful valleys. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Helm Crag: This popular hiking trail offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding Lake District mountains and Valleys.
  • Kentmere Horseshoe: This challenging hiking trail takes visitors through the beautiful Kentmere valley and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Dales Way: This long-distance walking trail takes hikers through some of the most beautiful landscapes of the Lake District, including the Langdale Valley and the Yorkshire Dales.
  • The Cumbria Way: This long-distance hiking trail offers visitors an opportunity to explore the beauty of the Lake District, taking them through picturesque valleys, mountains, and scenic villages.

Best Things to Do in Kendal: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, visitors can experience a range of activities and tours in Kendal. Here are some of the activities that visitors shouldn’t miss:

  • Brewery Tour of Bowness Bay Brewing: Visitors can learn about the brewing process, explore the brewery, and taste a range of locally brewed beers.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The Lake District offers plenty of opportunities for water sports, and visitors can take a guided tour of the nearby River Kent or Lake Windermere.
  • Kendal Festival of Food: This annual festival is held in September and offers visitors an opportunity to sample local cuisine, including artisan cheeses, wines, and beers.
  • Kendal Farmers Market: This weekly farmers market is held in the center of town and offers visitors a chance to sample local produce, including fresh fruits and vegetables, meat, and dairy products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kendal

Visitors can easily spend a week exploring the beauty and culture of Kendal. Here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Kendal Castle and Abbot Hall Art Gallery
  • Day 2: Explore the Museum of Lakeland Life and Industry, and enjoy a picnic in Kendal Green
  • Day 3: Hike Helm Crag and enjoy stunning panoramic views of the surrounding Lake District
  • Day 4: Take a kayak tour of the River Kent or Lake Windermere
  • Day 5: Explore the beautiful gardens of Sizergh Castle, and enjoy a tour of Bowness Bay Brewing.
  • Day 6: Visit the nearby town of Windermere and take a boat tour of the lake
  • Day 7: Enjoy a day at the Kendal Festival of Food or explore the local farmers market

Food and Drink Scene of Kendal

Kendal offers a range of delicious and unique cuisine, including artisan cheeses, traditional English dishes, and locally brewed beers.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the dishes that visitors should try during their visit to Kendal:

  • Cumberland Sausage: This traditional sausage is made with pork, herbs, and spices and is a staple of English cuisine.
  • Grasmere Gingerbread: This famous local treat is a cross between a cookie and a cake, made with ginger, spices, and breadcrumbs.
  • Lancashire Hotpot: This hearty dish is made with lamb, potatoes, onions, and carrots and is a favorite of locals.
  • Kendal Mint Cake: This iconic white mint-flavored candy has been produced in Kendal since 1869 and is a popular snack for hikers.

Local Beverages

Kendal is known for its local breweries and distilleries, which produce a range of high-quality beers and spirits.

  • Bowness Bay Brewing: This local brewery produces a range of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Lakes Distillery: This local distillery produces a range of high-quality spirits, including gin, vodka, and whiskey.

Where to Stay in Kendal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kendal offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • Riverside Hotel: This elegant hotel offers stylish rooms with views of the River Kent and is located in the center of town.
  • YHA Kendal: This affordable hostel is located in a historic building in the center of town and offers visitors comfortable beds and communal amenities.
  • Kendal Hostel: This charming guesthouse offers visitors cozy rooms, communal spaces, and a central location.

What to Buy in Kendal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home a range of unique souvenirs and local products from Kendal, including artisanal cheeses, locally made crafts, and Kendal Mint cake. Here are some of the top options:

  • Lakeland Arts and Crafts: Visitors can browse a range of locally made crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Milnthorpe Market: This weekly farmers market offers visitors a chance to sample and buy fresh produce, cheeses, and meats from local vendors.
  • Kendal Mint Cake Factory: Visitors can stock up on this iconic Kendal treat at the local factory, which also offers tours and tastings.

Local Culture and Customs of Kendal

Kendal has a rich culture and local traditions that visitors can experience during their visit. Here are some of the customs and festivals to look out for:

  • Kendal Torchlight Procession: This annual procession is held in September and involves thousands of people carrying torches through the town to celebrate local history and traditions.
  • The Mintcake Mine Experience: Visitors can take a tour of the local mine that inspired the production of Kendal Mint cake and learn about the history and production of this iconic treat.
  • Westmorland County Show: This annual agricultural show is held in September and offers visitors a chance to enjoy local produce, crafts, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kendal

Kendal can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kendal

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the beautiful outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking in the Lake District.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including Kendal Mint cake and artisanal cheeses.
  • Do take part in local traditions and festivals to experience the culture of Kendal.

Don’t:

  • Don’t visit during the winter months, as the weather can be cold and wet.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment when taking part in outdoor activities.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kendal

Kendal is a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take the usual precautions when exploring the town and surrounding areas.

Health in Kendal

Kendal has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, for visitors who require medical attention. Visitors should also take precautions against tick-borne diseases, such as Lyme disease, when hiking in the Lake District.

Getting to Kendal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kendal

The nearest airport to Kendal is Manchester airport, which is approximately 90 minutes away by road. Visitors can also fly into Liverpool or Newcastle airports, which are both approximately two hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Kendal

Kendal is well connected to nearby towns and villages by a range of local bus services. The main bus station in Kendal is located in the center of town, with regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Kendal by train, with regular services from London, Manchester, and Glasgow. The train station is located in the center of town, within easy walking distance of most attractions.

How to Get Around in Kendal

The best way to get around Kendal is on foot, as most attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also travel by local bus or taxi, with regular services available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Kendal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kendal is located in the heart of the Lake District, with plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some of the top options:

  • Windermere: This beautiful town is located just a short drive from Kendal and offers stunning views of Lake Windermere.
  • Coniston: This small village is located in the heart of the Lake District and is surrounded by beautiful mountains and lakes.
  • Ambleside: Located just a short drive from Kendal, Ambleside is a popular destination for hikers, with several challenging trails in the nearby mountains.

Conclusion

Kendal is a beautiful and vibrant destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and rich culture of the Lake District. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences or local cuisine, Kendal has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit to Kendal and make the most of your trip to this stunning town.
