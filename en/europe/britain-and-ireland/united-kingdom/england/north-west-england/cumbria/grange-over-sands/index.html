Introduction to Grange-over-Sands

Nestled on the Morecambe Bay of Cumbria, Grange-over-Sands is a stunning seaside town that has been popular for centuries with holidaymakers. The charm of the town lies in its scenic views, natural beauty, serene beaches, landscapes, fresh air, and healthy living.

This town emerged as a popular tourist spot in the Victorian era when people would visit here to find a retreat from the industrial city, plus the opening of a railway station increased the number of visitors. The town has retained much of its Victorian architecture and appeal, attracting visitors who are nature lovers, history buffs, or are looking for a relaxing vacation.

The best way to explore and discover the hidden treasures of Grange-over-Sands is to plan accordingly. Therefore, we have created a comprehensive travel guide for visitors to make the most of their Grange-over-Sands experience and explore the exciting spots that are rich in natural and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Grange-over-Sands

Regardless of your interests and activities, Grange-over-Sands offers something for everyone. From historical places to natural marvels, here is the list of must-visit places in Grange-over-Sands:

Culturally Important Attractions - Museums and Historical Places

  • Cartmel Priory: Cartmel village is a walking distance from Grange-over-Sands and home to the ancient and peaceful Cartmel Priory which dates back to 1190. It is the 12th century church of St Mary and one of the most popular historical landmarks to explore in Grange-over-Sands
  • Holker Hall: This is a grand Victorian house, gardens, and deer park and an historic estate which is open to visitors. The restaurant served local, seasonal dishes.
  • Haverthwaite Steam Railway: Giving visitors an experience from the past, take a one-way train ride from picturesque Haverthwaite Station to the foot of Lake Windermere.

Natural Attractions of Grange-over-Sands - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arnside & Silverdale Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty: Discover the beauty of the coastline with the stunning natural landscapes here. Visitors can find an array of flora and fauna, bird watching spots and hiking trails while exploring this area.
  • Grizedale Forest Park: The large expanse of woodland and exhilarating forest park provides an adrenaline rush- mountain biking, ziplining and more.
  • Cumbria Coastal Way: If you’re a keen hiker, the Cumbria Coastal Way should be on your list. This route stretches from Walney Island along the coast to Arnside, offering a panoramic view of the glorious seaside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grange-over-Sands

Grange-over-Sands is the perfect town for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Grange-over-Sands:

  • Limestone Trail: This beautiful and well-marked hiking trail starts from Arnside and runs through the village of Holme and ends at Kettlewell Clough, offering breathtaking views of the countryside.
  • Witherslack Cycle Loop: This 22-mile cycle loop route takes you through the picturesque countryside and charming villages of Witherslack, Haverthwaite, and Cartmel and can be completed at a leisurely pace.
  • Bridleways of Grange and Cartmel: For those who want to explore nature while on horseback, Grange and Cartmel have several stunning routes to choose from, with choices for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Grange-over-Sands: Activities and Tours

Aside from the hiking, biking, and cultural attractions, Grange over Sands has many more exciting activities for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Tour of Local Bee Hives: The Acorn Bank Honeybee colony in Grange-over-Sands is the oldest in the region, and visitors can get up close and personal with the bees and their hive.
  • Grange Wildlife Park: A popular attraction, visitors can see rare and exotic birds and animals, including wallabies and llamas.
  • Millers Tarn Boat Hire: Visitors can rent a rowboat and row around Millers Tarn. It is serene and peaceful with incredible natural landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grange-over-Sands

To make the most of your vacation, here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Grange-over-Sands:

  • Day 1: Explore the Cartmel Priory and the nearby village of Cartmel famous for its horse-racing events.
  • Day 2: Hike up Coniston Old Man, and enjoy a tour of the Lake District National Park.
  • Day 3: Take the Haverthwaite steam train and explore the Lakes Aquarium followed by the Holker Hall and Gardens
  • Day 4: Spend a day taking in the sights and sounds of Grange-over-Sands, exploring its promenade, beaches, and gardens.
  • Day 5: Discover the beauty of the coast and Arnside Knott, where you can enjoy lunch with a view.
  • Day 6: Take the bridleways of Grange and Cartmel for a day on horseback.
  • Day 7: Spend the day shopping for souvenirs and mementos and discover the food and drink scene of this charming town.

Food and Drink Scene of Grange-over-Sands

Explore the flavors of Grange-over-Sands and indulge in unique culinary delights from around the world and traditional Cumbrian dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A famous sweet treat from the region and Grange-over-Sands in particular, Sticky Toffee Pudding is a must-have dessert if you’re in town. It consists of a sponge cake soaked in toffee sauce, served with vanilla ice cream.
  • Cumberland Sausage: Savour the taste of Cumberland Sausage which is made from pork and seasoned with aromatic spices.
  • Grange-over-Sands Seafood: With the town’s residence on Morecambe Bay, its seafood is an unbeatable local specialty. Fresh shrimps, crabs, mussels, and haddocks are the most preferred seafood dishes of the town.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Wash down the delicious Cumbrian food with a glass of locally-brewed beer. The region is famous for its variety of beers.
  • Gin: Grange-over-Sands has witnessed a boom in local gin distilleries in recent years. Visitors can taste unique locally-made gin flavors throughout their stay.

Where to Stay in Grange-over-Sands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grange-over-Sands has various accommodation options, ranging from hotels to hostels to guesthouses, catering to different budgets and needs. Some of the recommended places to stay in Grange-over-Sands are:

  • Netherwood Hotel and Spa: A luxury hotel near Lake Windermere, offering premium amenities and spa services.
  • YHA: A youth hostel chain that offers affordable accommodation, ideal for solo travelers or backpackers.
  • Bay Villa B&B: A beautiful and award-winning bed and breakfast, only a few minutes’ walk from the sea and the promenade.

What to Buy in Grange-over-Sands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grange-over-Sands is famous for its crafts and food, so bring back a keepsake as a memory of your journey. Popular items include:

  • Grange Fudge: Grange Fudge is hand-made toffee fudge made from locally sourced ingredients and comes in various flavors, perfect as a gift or to enjoy yourself
  • Cartmel Sticky Toffee Sauce: The decadent Cartmel Sticky Toffee Sauce is the perfect souvenir to take back home and introduce to your friends and family
  • Cumbrian Cheese: Cheese lovers can take home a wide variety of cheeses made from locally-sourced cow, sheep and goat milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Grange-over-Sands

Grange-over-Sands is a town rich in history and culture. Visitors should respect and follow the following cultural customs of the town:

  • Although it is an English holiday destination, it is often misinterpreted and termed Scottish. This is not appreciated by the locals as they are deeply rooted in their history.
  • Whilst exploring the town, it is customary to greet the locals and maintain a peaceful and friendly atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Grange-over-Sands

The best time to visit Grange-over-Sands is from May to September when the warm weather is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and exploring the town. July and August are the busiest months with potential crowds and higher prices, so booking your accommodation and activities well in advance is recommended. In winter, the weather may be unpredictable with fewer visitors, but it’s a great time to wander the streets and enjoy the peace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grange-over-Sands

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts that visitors should keep in mind while in Grange-over-Sands:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Greet locals with a smile and interact respectfully
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and occasion

Don’t:

  • Litter in the public places and beaches
  • Climb on stones or walls as these stones are home to wildflowers and birds
  • Disturb the natural habitats of the animals and birds in the nature reserves

Safety and Health

Grange-over-Sands is a safe town, but it’s always great to keep in mind safety tips while traveling in unfamiliar areas.

Safety in Grange-over-Sands

  • If you’re considering swimming in the sea, be careful of the changing currents, tides, and rocks along the coastline.
  • Follow normal safety precautions like locking your car and not leaving valuables in visible places.

Health in Grange-over-Sands

  • Grange-over-Sands is a green and healthy town; it is perfect for a wellness retreat as it offers the ideal better-respiratory conditions known as the “Grange-over-Sands Effect.”
  • The town has various medical facilities and health care centers in case of an emergency.

Getting to Grange-over-Sands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can travel to Grange-over-Sands through various means of transportation, either through public or private modes. The most convenient ways to reach Grange-over-Sands are:

  • By car: Visitors can reach Grange-over-Sands via M6 from the south and the A590 from the east through the Lake District.
  • By train: The nearest major rail station is Lancaster, and trains to Grange-over-Sands run regularly from there.
  • By bus: Grange-over-Sands has a bus station with regular service from Kendal, Ulverston and all the major cities of the north.
  • By plane: Visitors can fly into Manchester or Liverpool airports and then take a train, bus or rent a car from there.

Airports Near Grange-over-Sands

  • Manchester Airport: The closest airport, located 66 miles from Grange-over-Sands.
  • Liverpool Airport: Located 76 miles west of Grange-over-Sands.

Bus Stations Near Grange-over-Sands

  • Stagecoach: Grange-over-Sands Bus Station which is just a five-minutes’ walk from the town center.
  • National Express Coaches: The National Express Coach Service stops in Lancaster and Kendal and visitors can take a bus to Grange-over-Sands.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from one of the nearby airports or cities to easily travel to Grange-over-Sands.
  • Cycling: Grange-over-Sands town is cycling-friendly and has cycling routes that connect cyclists to surrounding areas, so visitors can rent a bike and cycle to the town.

How to Get Around in Grange-over-Sands

Grange-over-Sands is a compact town that can be explored on foot or bicycle. Visitors can use public transportation like buses and taxis to reach the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Grange-over-Sands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grange-over-Sands is close to many attractions and destinations, including:

  • The Lake District National Park: Just a short drive away, this beautiful national park is a must-see destination.
  • Windermere: The largest lake in the Lake District which is located to the north of Grange-over-Sands.
  • Kendal: Famed for its Kendall Mint Cake, Kendal is a historic market town with independent shops and a castle.
