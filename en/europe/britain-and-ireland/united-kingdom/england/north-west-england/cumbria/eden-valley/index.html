Eden Valley is a stunning region in North West England that is often overlooked by many visitors. However, if you are a nature lover or a history buff, you are in for a treat. The area is rich in natural beauty, with rolling hills, serene waterways, and picturesque countryside. You’ll also find a wealth of culturally important attractions, including museums, historical sites, and world-renowned landmarks. To help you make the most of your visit, we’ve put together a comprehensive guide to this hidden gem of a destination.

Introduction to Eden Valley

Located in the county of Cumbria, Eden Valley is a place of serene landscapes, charming villages, and historic sites. The River Eden, which gives the region its name, flows through the valley, and there are plenty of opportunities to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and boating.

Visitors to Eden Valley can enjoy the beauty of nature and learn about its rich history, while immersing themselves in the local culture and cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Eden Valley has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eden Valley

Eden Valley is packed with interesting attractions, both natural and man-made. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit while you’re in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carlisle Castle: This 900-year-old castle is a must-visit for history buffs. It has been the site of many important battles, and offers visitors a glimpse into the area’s tumultuous past.
  • Hadrian’s Wall: One of the most famous landmarks in the UK, Hadrian’s Wall was built nearly 2000 years ago and stretched 80 miles across the north of England. You can walk along some of the best-preserved sections of the wall and learn about the Romans who built it.
  • Tullie House Museum & Art Gallery: Located in Carlisle, this museum is dedicated to the art, history, and culture of the area. It has exhibits on everything from the Roman occupation to the local wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Eden Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pennines: The Pennines is a range of hills and mountains that runs through the centre of England. The section that runs through Eden Valley is particularly picturesque, and offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region.
  • Lake District National Park: Although technically not part of Eden Valley, the Lake District is only a short drive away and well worth the visit. Known for its stunning lakes and mountains, hiking, boating, and wildlife spotting opportunities, it is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts.
  • Ullswater: This beautiful lake is the second largest in the Lake District and is known for its stunning scenery and opportunities for boating and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eden Valley

Eden Valley is a paradise for hikers and bikers with its beautiful natural landscapes and hills providing the perfect setting for outdoor activities. Here is a list of some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Hartside to Alston: This is a challenging hike/bike ride, but well worth the effort for the breathtaking views from the top of Hartside.
  • The Penrith to Kirkby Stephen Cycle Ride: This is a 45-mile route that takes you through some of the most beautiful countryside in the area. The route is mainly on quiet country roads and offers stunning views of the Pennines.
  • The High Cup Nick Walk: This is a 10-mile hike that takes you through some of the most dramatic scenery in the region. You’ll visit High Cup Nick, a natural amphitheatre formed by a glacier, and enjoy stunning views over the Eden Valley.

Best Things to Do in Eden Valley: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Eden Valley. Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Boat tour on Ullswater: A boat tour of Ullswater is a great way to see the lake and its surroundings from a different perspective. You can take a leisurely cruise or go for a more adventurous kayaking or paddleboarding tour.
  • Carlisle Farmer’s Market: If you’re a foodie, make sure you stop by the Carlisle Farmers Market. You’ll find everything from fresh produce to artisanal cheeses and locally brewed beer.
  • Guided tours of Hadrian’s Wall: To get the most out of your visit to Hadrian’s Wall, consider taking a guided tour. You’ll learn about the history of the wall and the surrounding area from an expert local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eden Valley

If you’re planning to spend a week in Eden Valley, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Arrive in Eden Valley and settle in to your accommodation. Day 2: Visit Carlisle Castle and Tullie House Museum & Art Gallery. Day 3: Explore Hadrian’s Wall and nearby Roman sites. Day 4: Hike or bike the Hartside to Alston route. Day 5: Take a leisurely boat tour of Ullswater. Day 6: Visit the Lake District National Park for a day of hiking and exploring. Day 7: Enjoy some local cuisine and souvenirs while exploring the charming villages of Eden Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Eden Valley

Eden Valley is known for its hearty cuisine and locally brewed beer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland sausage: This delicious pork sausage is a local specialty that is a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Kendal Mint Cake: This sweet treat is a local delicacy that has been popular with hikers and climbers for over 100 years.
  • Herdwick lamb: This delicious and tender lamb is native to the Lake District and is a popular ingredient in local dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Jennings Brewery: This local brewery has been making beer since 1828 and is known for its award-winning Cumberland Ale.
  • Hawkshead Brewery: This Lake District-based brewery is known for its creative and innovative beers, as well as its commitment to sustainability.
  • English Lakes Distillery: Located in the Lake District, this distillery produces a range of award-winning whiskey, gin, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Eden Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eden Valley has a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top picks:

  • North Lakes Hotel & Spa: This is a 4-star hotel with luxurious rooms, an on-site spa, and beautiful views of the valley.
  • YHA Langdon Beck: This is a budget-friendly hostel located near the Pennines that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the area’s hiking and biking trails.
  • The Shepherds Inn: This family-run guesthouse is located near Hadrian’s Wall and offers cozy rooms and a traditional English pub.

What to Buy in Eden Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the things you might want to bring home from Eden Valley include:

  • Cumberland sausage: You can find this local delicacy at most supermarkets and grocery stores in the area and take it back home to enjoy.
  • Wool products: The Lake District has a rich history of wool production, and you can find locally made wool blankets, scarves, and other accessories.
  • Artisanal cheeses: The area is known for its cheese production, and you can find a range of delicious and unique cheeses to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Eden Valley

Eden Valley has a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. Here are some of the things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the local wildlife: Eden Valley is home to a range of rare and endangered species, so make sure to respect their habitats and avoid disturbing them.
  • Be mindful of hiking and biking trails: Eden Valley’s trails are shared by hikers, bikers, and horseback riders, so make sure to be courteous and share the space.
  • Follow the rules of historic sites and landmarks: When visiting places like Hadrian’s Wall, make sure to follow the rules and respect the historic and cultural significance of the site.

Best Time to Visit Eden Valley

The best time to visit Eden Valley is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, autumn is also a great time to visit for the stunning fall foliage, and the quieter tourist crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eden Valley

Here are some of the essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Eden Valley:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and clean up after yourself.
  • Try the local cuisine and support small businesses in the area.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the local wildlife and their habitats.
  • Ignore the rules and regulations of historic sites and landmarks.
  • Leave your trash behind on hiking and biking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eden Valley

Eden Valley is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be careful when hiking or biking on uneven terrain and steep paths.
  • Always check the weather forecast before heading out, and avoid hiking or biking in bad weather.
  • Be mindful of theft in tourist areas, and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Eden Valley

Eden Valley has good medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, it’s always a good idea to travel with travel insurance and take care of your health while on the road.

Getting to Eden Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eden Valley is located in the north of England and is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Eden Valley

The closest airports to Eden Valley are Manchester Airport and Newcastle Airport. From there, you can take a train or a bus to Carlisle or Penrith, which are both located in the heart of Eden Valley.

Bus Stations Near Eden Valley

There are several bus stations in the area, including the Penrith Bus Station, which has connections to major cities in the region.

Other Options

You can also reach Eden Valley by car or by train. The area is served by several train stations, including Carlisle and Penrith.

How to Get Around in Eden Valley

The best way to explore Eden Valley is by car, as the area is quite spread out. However, there are also several bus services that run through the area if you prefer public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Eden Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more adventures after exploring Eden Valley, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Lake District National Park: Just a short drive away, the Lake District is a haven for nature lovers and offers stunning scenery and outdoor activities.
  • Hadrian’s Wall Country: Explore the birthplace of Roman Britain and visit the many historical sites in this area.
  • Durham: This beautiful, historic city is known for its cathedral and university.

Conclusion

Eden Valley is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and history. The area is perfect for those looking for a relaxing getaway, as well as those seeking adventure and outdoor activities. Whether you’re exploring historic sites, hiking along scenic trails, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, Eden Valley is sure to leave a lasting impression.
