Introduction to Sedbergh

Located in the heart of Cumbria, Sedbergh is a quaint, historic town that’s perfect for a weekend or week-long getaway. Known for its picturesque landscapes, vibrant culture, rich history, and welcoming locals, Sedbergh is a hidden gem in the North West of England.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a foodie seeking new culinary experiences, Sedbergh has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay so you can make the most of your trip to Sedbergh.

List of Places to Visit in Sedbergh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sedbergh School - Founded in 1525, Sedbergh School is one of the oldest schools in the country and boasts a stunning 19th-century Gothic architecture. Take a guided tour to learn about the school’s fascinating history and famous alumni.
  • Farfield Mill Arts and Heritage Centre - Located in a restored Victorian woollen mill, Farfield Mill is home to a gallery, art studios, a café, and a museum showcasing the town’s textile heritage.
  • St. Andrew’s Church - Situated in the centre of town, this historic church dates back to the 12th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside from its tower.

Natural Attractions of Sedbergh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Howgill Fells - A series of rolling hills that offer breathtaking panoramic views of the town and the surrounding countryside. Take a hike or bike ride through this stunning landscape for an adventure you won’t forget.
  • River Rawthey - A picturesque river that runs through the town, offering opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and peaceful strolls along the riverbank.
  • Cautley Spout - The highest waterfall in England, located a short drive from Sedbergh. Follow the footpath to the base of the falls and take in the beauty of this natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sedbergh

Sedbergh is home to a range of hiking and biking trails that showcase the beauty of the town and its surroundings. Here are a few of our favourites:

  • The Dales Way - A stunning 85-mile trail that passes through Sedbergh on its way to Bowness-on-Windermere. Take a day trip or a multi-day hike along the trail for some of the best views of the Yorkshire Dales National Park.
  • The Howgills - A series of hills that offer plenty of challenging hikes and bike rides. Take the route from Sedbergh over Winder and up to the Calf for stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • Coniston Old Man - A challenging hike that rewards you with stunning views of Coniston Water and the surrounding fells. Start from the town of Coniston, a short drive from Sedbergh.

Best Things to Do in Sedbergh: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a fan of the great outdoors or prefer to spend your time exploring the town’s cultural heritage, Sedbergh offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy:

  • Sedbergh Tours - Take a guided tour of the town to learn about its fascinating history, including the stories of local heroes and the town’s important role in the textile industry.
  • Wildlife Watching Tours - Join one of the many tours operating in the area to spot some of the region’s most elusive wildlife, including red deer, wild boar, and buzzards.
  • Food and Drink Tours - Sample some of the best local produce on a food and drink tour of the town and surrounding countryside. Enjoy a tasting at a local brewery or distillery or indulge in a cheese platter at one of the town’s many delis.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sedbergh

Day 1: Explore the town’s museums and historical sites, including Sedbergh School, Farfield Mill, and St. Andrew’s Church.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the town before heading out on a hike or bike ride through the stunning Howgill Fells.

Day 3: Indulge in a food and drink tour, visiting local breweries, distilleries, and delis for a taste of the region’s best produce.

Day 4: Get back to nature with a wildlife watching tour or a kayak trip down the River Rawthey.

Day 5: Visit Cautley Spout, England’s highest waterfall, for a scenic hike and picnic lunch.

Day 6: Head out of town to explore nearby attractions, including Coniston Old Man or the charming town of Kendal.

Day 7: Relax and unwind with a spa day at one of the town’s luxury hotels or guesthouses.

Food and Drink Scene of Sedbergh

Sedbergh is home to a range of fantastic restaurants, cafes, and pubs, many of which specialise in using locally sourced ingredients. Here are a few must-try dishes during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland Sausage - A hearty, flavourful sausage made with locally sourced Cumberland pork.
  • Lancashire Hotpot - Another local favourite, Lancashire Hotpot is a hearty lamb and vegetable stew topped with sliced potatoes.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding - A delicious dessert made with dates and served with a rich toffee sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Hawkshead Brewery - A local brewery that produces a range of award-winning beers using traditional brewing methods.
  • Lakeland Distillery - A small-batch distillery that produces a range of artisanal spirits, including gin and whisky.

Where to Stay in Sedbergh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Sedbergh, from luxury hotels to quaint bed and breakfasts. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Black Bull Inn - A charming 17th-century inn located in the heart of town, offering comfortable rooms and a lively bar serving local ales and ciders.
  • The Bull Hotel - A luxury hotel featuring comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving locally sourced, seasonal produce.
  • The Dalesman Country Inn - Located just outside of town, this cosy inn offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Sedbergh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The town’s rich textile heritage means that there are plenty of textiles and wool products available for purchase, including handmade blankets, scarves, and jumpers. There are also a range of shops selling locally made jams, chutneys, and other foodie treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Sedbergh

Sedbergh is a close-knit community, and locals are proud of their town’s heritage and traditions. One of the most significant events in the town’s calendar is the annual Sedbergh Sheepfest, a celebration of the town’s wool industry that takes place every September.

Best Time to Visit Sedbergh

Sedbergh is a great destination to visit year-round, but the best time to visit depends on what you’re looking for. Summer is peak tourist season, with warm weather and long days perfect for exploring the region’s outdoor attractions. Spring and autumn offer cooler, milder temperatures and quieter crowds, making them ideal for hiking and other outdoor activities. Winter is a magical time in Sedbergh, with the town’s festive decorations and cosy pubs offering a warm welcome to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sedbergh

Do:

  • Take a hike or bike ride through the Howgill Fells for stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Indulge in a local food and drink tour to sample some of the region’s best produce.
  • Take a guided tour of the town to learn about its fascinating history.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring appropriate clothing and footwear for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Miss the chance to sample some of the region’s best local produce, including cheese and beer.
  • Ignore the town’s fascinating history and cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sedbergh

Sedbergh is a safe town for visitors to explore, but it’s always important to take precautions when venturing into the great outdoors. Make sure to bring appropriate clothing and footwear for hiking and biking, and be sure to follow any safety advice given by local tour operators.

Health in Sedbergh

Sedbergh has a range of medical facilities, including a general practitioner’s surgery, a dental practice, and a pharmacy. If you require emergency medical attention, dial 999 or go to the nearest Accident and Emergency Department.

Getting to Sedbergh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sedbergh

The closest airports to Sedbergh are Manchester Airport and Leeds Bradford Airport, both of which are approximately 2 hours’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near Sedbergh

The town is well-connected by bus, with regular services to nearby towns and cities, including Kendal and Lancaster.

Other Options

Travelling by car is the most convenient way to get to Sedbergh, with good road connections from the M6 motorway.

How to Get Around in Sedbergh

The town is small enough to explore on foot, but if you’re planning to venture further afield, it’s best to rent a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Sedbergh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake District National Park - Just a short drive from Sedbergh, the Lake District National Park is home to some of the UK’s most stunning scenery, including beautiful lakes, soaring peaks, and quaint villages.
  • Yorkshire Dales National Park - Another nearby national park that’s perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring.
  • Kendal - A nearby town with a rich history and cultural heritage, including a castle and several museums.

Conclusion

Sedbergh is a hidden gem in the North West of England, with plenty to offer visitors seeking an authentic taste of rural England. Whether you’re a fan of the great outdoors, a history buff, or a foodie looking to sample some of the region’s best produce, Sedbergh is a destination that’s sure to impress. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to make the most of your trip to this charming town.
