Alston Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Alston

Alston is a picturesque town located in Cumbria, in North West England. It is nestled in the Pennine Hills, overlooking the South Tyne Valley. The town is known for its quaint cobbled streets, stunning Pennine vistas, and rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, food and drink scene, local culture, safety, transportation options, and nearby destinations to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Alston

Alston has several places to visit. Here’s a list of things you can do in Alston.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alston Moor Historical Society Museum: A museum that showcases Alston’s fascinating history, including its lead-mining heritage.
  • St. Augustine’s Church: A beautiful church with stunning architecture and a rich cultural history.

Natural Attractions of Alston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pennine Way National Trail: This 268-mile trail passes through Alston and offers some of the best scenic views of the Pennines.
  • South Tynedale Railway: Take a ride on this scenic railway that runs from Alston to Kirkhaugh.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alston

Alston has some of the best hiking and biking routes in the United Kingdom. The Pennine Way National Trail is a must-visit for hikers, while mountain bikers can explore the trails around the North Pennines AONB.

Best Things to Do in Alston: Activities and Tours

  • Take a tour of the Alston Moor Historical Society Museum to learn about Alston’s cultural heritage.
  • Take a ride on the South Tynedale Railway for scenic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Explore the Pennine Way National Trail for a challenging hiking adventure.
  • Go mountain biking in the North Pennines AONB for an adrenaline-pumping experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alston

If you’re planning to stay in Alston for a week, here’s a sample itinerary that covers the best places to visit and things to do:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Alston and check into your hotel. Take a stroll around the town and explore its many attractions.
  • Day 2: Take a tour of the Alston Moor Historical Society Museum. In the afternoon, take a ride on the South Tynedale Railway.
  • Day 3 and 4: Explore the Pennine Way National Trail. Plan a different route for each day to cover as much ground as possible.
  • Day 5: Go mountain biking in the North Pennines AONB.
  • Day 6: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine and culture. Visit a local restaurant and sample some of Alston’s traditional dishes.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby lakes and explore the local area.

Food and Drink Scene of Alston

Alston has some great local cuisine and a thriving food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland sausage: A local specialty made from pork and seasoning.
  • Sticky toffee pudding: A classic British dessert made from dates and toffee sauce.
  • Alston lamb: This delicious lamb is reared on the surrounding hills and has a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Alston Brewery: This local brewery produces some great beers and ales.
  • Sillfield Farm: Visit the farm and try their locally produced cider.

Where to Stay in Alston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alston has many accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Alston:

  • Alston House Hotel: A luxurious hotel with stunning views and great amenities.
  • Lowbyer Manor Country House: A beautiful guesthouse with a relaxed atmosphere and great food.
  • Alston Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel with clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Alston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alston has many great souvenir shops that sell local products like pottery, jewelry, and textiles. You can also buy souvenirs from the Alston Moor Historical Society Museum gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Alston

Alston has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its local cuisine, arts, and crafts. The town is also home to several festivals and events throughout the year, which celebrate its history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Alston

The best time to visit Alston is during the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is mild, and there are several outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alston

Do:

  • Do explore the Pennine Way National Trail.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do visit the Alston Moor Historical Society Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t disturb the local wildlife.
  • Don’t ignore safety warnings while hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alston

Alston is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take standard precautions to stay safe. Make sure to keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings, particularly when hiking or biking.

Health in Alston

Alston has a local health center that provides primary healthcare services. If you have a medical emergency, call 999.

Getting to Alston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alston is well-connected to other major cities in the United Kingdom. Here are some transportation options to reach Alston:

Airports Near Alston

  • Newcastle International Airport (approximately 42 miles from Alston)
  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport (approximately 107 miles from Alston)

Bus Stations Near Alston

  • Alston Bus Station: Local bus services operate from here, connecting Alston to nearby towns and cities.
  • Hexham Bus Station: This bus station is 21 miles away from Alston and offers national and intercity bus services.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station is Haltwhistle station, which is approximately 15 miles from Alston.
  • Car: Alston has several car rental services available, and the town is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Alston

Alston is best explored on foot, but you can also rent a bike or car to get around. It’s a small town, so you can easily walk to most places.

Where to Go Next from Alston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alston is close to several other beautiful destinations, including:

  • Hadrian’s Wall: Approximately 25 miles from Alston, this ancient Roman wall is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Lake District National Park: Approximately 40 miles from Alston, this park is home to some of the most beautiful scenery in the United Kingdom.
  • Durham Cathedral: Durham Cathedral is a stunning example of Norman architecture and is approximately 35 miles from Alston.

Conclusion

Alston is a beautiful town with stunning views, excellent cuisine, and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. This comprehensive travel guide covers all the essential information you need to plan a trip to Alston, including where to visit, what to do, where to stay, and how to get around. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, Alston has something for everyone.
