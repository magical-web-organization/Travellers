Introduction to Eskdale Green

Nestled amidst beautiful and rugged countryside in Cumbria, North West England, Eskdale Green is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It is a picturesque, tranquil village that has maintained its traditional charm despite being popular among tourists.

Eskdale Green offers visitors a wide range of activities and opportunities to explore the local culture, history, nature, and cuisine. This article is a comprehensive guide that will help you plan your visit to Eskdale Green.

List of Places to Visit in Eskdale Green

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in the region’s history, Eskdale Green presents several museums and historical places to explore:

  • The Eskdale Mill: This is a restored 16th-century watermill that offers a glimpse of the region’s industrial past.
  • St. Catherine’s Church: A notable structure with Anglo-Saxon origins, the church is the focal point of the village.
  • Ravenglass & Eskdale Railway: Built-in 1875, this steam locomotive is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the region.
  • Hardknott Roman Fort: Located a short distance from Eskdale Green, this historic fortification dates back to over 2,000 years.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eskdale Green is the perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best natural attractions in and around the village are:

  • Scafell Pike: It is England’s highest mountain and offers breathtaking views from the summit. It is accessible via several hiking trails in the area, including the Eskdale Trail.
  • Eskdale Valley: A scenic valley with breathtaking views, streams, and waterfalls. It is ideal for hiking, picnicking, and camping.
  • Wast Water: A stunning lake located only 15 minutes from Eskdale Green. It is ideal for water sports such as kayaking, sailing, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eskdale Green

Eskdale Green is an ideal destination for hiking and biking, with numerous well-maintained trails.

  • The Hardknott Pass: A challenging route that rewards experienced hikers with stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • The Eskdale Trail: A popular trail that is 10 miles long and runs through the valley, alongside the river Esk.
  • Three Peaks Challenge: A three-day hike that takes visitors through the region’s most beautiful landscapes, including the Scafell Pike peak, and Wast Water.

Best Things to Do in Eskdale Green: Activities and Tours

There are tons of exciting things to do while visiting Eskdale Green:

  • Mountain Biking: Visitors can rent bikes and embark on a cycling adventure through the scenic countryside.
  • Fishing: Local streams are home to salmon and trout, making fishing a popular activity in the area.
  • Camping: The village offers several camping sites perfect for those looking to disconnect from city life and immerse themselves fully in nature.
  • Wildlife Watching: Visitors can spot wildlife such as the indigenous red squirrel, otters, badgers, and deer in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eskdale Green

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Eskdale Green:

Day 1 - Explore the village and visit St. Catherine’s Church and the Eskdale Mill.

Day 2 - Hike the Hardknott Pass and enjoy the panoramic views.

Day 3 - Visit the Hardknott Roman Fortification and take a ride on the Ravenglass and Eskdale Railway.

Day 4 - Kayak or sail on Wast Water.

Day 5 - Enjoy a cycling tour through the valleys and streams.

Day 6 - Take a guided hiking tour to Scafell Pike.

Day 7 - Relax, picnic, and soak up the scenic views in Eskdale Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Eskdale Green

Eskdale Green’s food and drink scene is rich in local flavors and rustic charm. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cumberland Sausages: A recipe that dates back to Roman times, the Cumberland sausage is a local delicacy made from the finest pork.
  • Cumberland Rum Nicky: A rich and sticky dessert consisting of raisins, rum, and ginger surrounded by a crusty pastry.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A decadent and sweet pudding made from dates, caramel sauce and served with ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Lakeland Ale: A local craft beer made from the finest malt and hops.
  • Whinberry Jam: A locally-produced jam that is made from the fruit of the bilberry bush. It can be enjoyed with bread, cheese or swept into a porridge bowl.

Where to Stay in Eskdale Green

Eskdale Green offers a variety of accommodation options for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best ones:

  • The Woolpack Inn: A four-star inn with peaceful and elegant accommodation and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.
  • Irton Hall: A beautiful 19th-century mansion with scenic views, luxury accommodation, and spa facilities.
  • The Boot Inn: A charming bed and breakfast serving homemade meals in a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Eskdale Green: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eskdale Green’s shops sell unique, locally produced items that make perfect souvenirs:

  • Wool Products: Locally-produced wool makes for warm and cozy souvenirs such as sweaters, scarves, and hats.
  • Handicrafts: Local artisans sell handcrafted items such as pottery, woodcarvings, and hand-woven baskets.
  • Cumbrian Delicacies: Locally-produced cheeses, jams, and fudges make perfect edible souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Eskdale Green

Eskdale Green has preserved its unique culture and customs, which visitors can witness in everyday life while exploring the village and its surroundings.

  • Eskdale Show: An annual agricultural show held in August that showcases the best of local farming and crafts.
  • The Cumbrian Dialect: The local dialect is a unique blend of Middle English and Old Norse, which can be heard in everyday conversations.

Best Time to Visit Eskdale Green

The best time to visit Eskdale Green is from May to September as the weather is mild and pleasant, and there are numerous outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eskdale Green

Do:

  • Respect the environment and follow the countryside code.
  • Try local cuisine and customs.
  • Take part in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb local wildlife.
  • Ignore the rural roads’ speed limits and narrowness.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eskdale Green

Eskdale Green is a safe village with little to no crime. However, visitors should take precautions against the terrain and weather during outdoor activities, and watch out for local wildlife.

Health in Eskdale Green

There are no major health risks associated with visiting Eskdale Green. Visitors should make sure they are up-to-date with their regular vaccinations.

How to Get Around in Eskdale Green

The best way to explore Eskdale Green is on foot or by bike. There are several rental bike services in the village that allow visitors to explore the local countryside independently.

Where to Go Next from Eskdale Green: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eskdale Green is a gateway to several nearby destinations of interest:

  • Lake District: A stunning national park with majestic landscapes, crystal-clear lakes, and popular hiking trails.
  • Hadrian’s Wall: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that was built to guard the Roman Empire’s northern frontier in AD 122.
  • Windermere: The England’s largest lake with a variety of water sports activities such as boating, kayaking and sailing available to visitors.
