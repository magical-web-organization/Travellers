Introduction to Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port is a charming town situated in Cheshire, North West England. The town is known for its industrial heritage and natural beauty. It is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and families looking for a weekend getaway. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover all the best things to see and do in Ellesmere Port, including the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, culture and customs, food and drink scene, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port has plenty of must-see attractions, from museums and historical places to natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of the best places to visit in Ellesmere Port:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Waterways Museum: This museum is dedicated to Britain’s inland waterways, with interactive displays and historic boats to explore.
  • The Cheshire Military Museum: Located in The Castle on Chester Road, the museum displays exhibits on Cheshire’s military history, including collections of weapons, uniforms, and equipment.
  • St. Mary’s Church: A beautiful church built in the 19th century and worth a visit just for its architecture.

Natural Attractions of Ellesmere Port: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rivacre Valley Country Park: A beautiful park with scenic walking trails and a lake to enjoy.
  • Whitby Park: A picturesque park with plenty of space to have a picnic with the family and enjoy the scenery.
  • Stanney Woods: Located between Ellesmere and Little Sutton, Stanney Woods has lots of trails perfect for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port offers some great hiking and biking routes with beautiful scenery along the way. Here are some of the best:

  • The Wirral Way: This 12-mile picturesque trail starts in West Kirby and ends in Hooton and is perfect for a family day out or a leisurely walk.
  • The Shropshire Union Canal Towpath: This 4.5-mile walk follows the canal from Ellesmere Port to Chester, letting you see the town from a different perspective.

Best Things to Do in Ellesmere Port: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour: Enjoy the waterways and historical features of Ellesmere Port on a boat tour offered by the National Waterways Museum.
  • Visit Chester Zoo: Only a short drive away, Chester Zoo is home to over 21,000 animals from over 500 different species. It’s a perfect day out for animal lovers of all ages.
  • Go Shopping: Visit the Coliseum Shopping Centre with a range of big-name stores and independent shops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ellesmere Port

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Ellesmere Port, here is an itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: National Waterways Museum

Spend the first day exploring the National Waterways Museum. This museum provides an insight into the history of British canals, with interactive exhibits and canal boat tours.

Day 2: Stanney Woods

Spend the day hiking or biking the trails through Stanney Woods. Located between Ellesmere and Little Sutton, it’s a beautiful and secluded woodland for outdoor enthusiasts.

Day 3: Chester Zoo

Take a half-day trip to Chester Zoo and spend the afternoon admiring the animals.

Day 4: Whitby Park

Spend the morning at Whitby Park, enjoying a picnic and playing games with the family in the beautiful surroundings.

Day 5: The Cheshire Military Museum and St. Mary’s Church

Spend the day exploring historical sites in Ellesmere Port. Visit the Cheshire Military Museum and St. Mary’s Church, both of which have significant historical importance.

Day 6: Wirral Way

Spend a day hiking on the picturesque Wirral Way, enjoying the stunning scenery and fresh air.

Day 7: Shopping

Spend the last day shopping in the Coliseum Shopping Centre, which has an array of shops including big-name brands and independent retailers.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port has a variety of food options for visitors to choose from, with many British favorites on the menu. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips: A traditional British fish dish, the best ones are served at the many restaurants and chippies around Ellesmere Port.
  • Sticky toffee pudding: A classic British dessert, often served with vanilla ice cream or custard.

Local Beverages

  • Thatchers Gold: A delicious apple cider brewed locally and available in most pubs and bars in Ellesmere Port.
  • Robinsons Brewery Beers: Ales brewed in the local area, including Trooper, named after Iron Maiden’s frontman who was born in the area.

Where to Stay in Ellesmere Port: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Ellesmere Port. Here are some recommendations:

  • Holiday Inn Ellesmere Port: A budget-friendly hotel located just a few miles from the city center and offering modern amenities.
  • Travelodge Cheshire Oaks: Located around a mile from Ellesmere Port and near to the Cheshire Oaks Designer Outlet, this hotel offers an affordable stay with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Ellesmere Port: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a local product to take home with you from Ellesmere Port? Here are some options:

  • Cheshire Cheese: Produced in the county for centuries, it is a must-buy for cheese lovers.
  • St. Mary’s Church Candle: You can purchase a premium St. Mary’s Church candle from the church souvenir shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port is a friendly, welcoming town, with a rich history and strong sense of tradition. The locals are known for being friendly and helpful, with many annual cultural events throughout the calendar year.

Best Time to Visit Ellesmere Port

The best time to visit Ellesmere Port is between May and September, where the weather is usually mild and pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellesmere Port

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drink scene. There are plenty of traditional dishes to try, and the locally crafted beers and ciders are worth sampling.
  • Do visit the National Waterways Museum. It’s a must-see attraction and provides a fascinating insight into the history of British canals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public areas. Help keep Ellesmere Port clean and tidy by disposing of litter responsibly.
  • Don’t be disrespectful towards local traditions and culture. Respect local customs and landmarks; many locals hold these in high esteem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port is generally a safe place to visit, with no significant safety concerns. However, it’s always important to take standard precautions, like locking your car, protecting valuable personal belongings, and keeping an eye out for pickpockets and opportunistic thieves.

Health in Ellesmere Port

Ellesmere Port has adequate health facilities and emergency services. For medical attention, the walk-in service at Ellesmere Port Hospital offers immediate health care for minor injuries or illnesses.

Getting to Ellesmere Port: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ellesmere Port is easy to get to via various transport options. Here are some of the options:

  • By car: Ellesmere Port is easily accessible by car via the M53 motorway, which connects to Liverpool and Chester.
  • By train: Ellesmere Port train station is located on the Wirral Line, which is operated by Merseyrail.
  • By bus: Regular bus services operate from Ellesmere Port Bus station to Chester and other nearby towns of Liverpool.

Airports Near Ellesmere Port

  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport: The nearest international airport, located just 25 miles from Ellesmere Port.
  • Manchester Airport: Located 35 miles from Ellesmere Port, Manchester Airport is well-connected to several parts of the country and the world.

How to Get Around in Ellesmere Port

The best way to get around Ellesmere Port and the surrounding areas is by car or public transportation, such as buses and trains. Taxis are also available for hire around the town center.

Where to Go Next from Ellesmere Port: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ellesmere Port is well-located for exploring the surrounding areas of Cheshire and beyond. Here are some recommended nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Chester: Only a short drive or train ride away, Chester is well-known for its Roman history, beautiful architecture, and shopping center.
  • Liverpool: A cultural hub with museums, galleries, and lots of shopping centers to explore. It is only a 30-minute drive from Ellesmere Port.

Conclusion

Ellesmere Port provides a great getaway with its scenic beauty, rich history, and an array of things to do and see, making it an ideal destination for tourists. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Ellesmere Port and ensure that you have a great time exploring all that the town has to offer.
