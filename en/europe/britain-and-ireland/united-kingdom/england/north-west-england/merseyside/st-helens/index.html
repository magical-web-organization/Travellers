Introduction to St Helens

Located in Merseyside, in the North West of England, St Helens is a vibrant town that boasts a rich cultural heritage and a variety of natural attractions. The town is famous for its history of glassmaking and coal mining, and its picturesque landscapes have inspired artists and writers for centuries. Visitors can enjoy plenty of outdoor adventures, explore museums and art galleries, and sample the local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in St Helens

Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places in St Helens:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • World of Glass: This award-winning museum chronicles the history of glassmaking in the town and includes a live glassblowing demonstration.
  • St Helens Town Hall: Built in the 1800s, the town hall’s magnificent architecture is one of its major attractions.
  • The St Helens Canal: The canal provides an opportunity for a scenic walk or bike ride, with views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of St Helens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sankey Valley Park: This park offers a range of outdoor activities such as cycling, walking, and bird watching.
  • Shirdley Hill: At 110 meters high, this hill offers stunning panoramic views of St Helens and the surrounding area.
  • Taylor Park: This beautiful park has a lake, a mini-golf course, and a play area, making it perfect for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Helens

St Helens is blessed with beautiful countryside that’s perfect for hiking, cycling and horse riding. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • St Helen’s Loop: The loop takes you through Billinge Hill, the canal, and Carr Mill dam and is approximately 9.5 miles long.
  • Rainford Circular: This trail is approximately 5 miles long and takes you through the scenic landscape of Rainford and Carr Mill Dam.
  • Sankey Canal: This flat path follows the canal and is perfect for walking, cycling, and fishing.

Best Things to Do in St Helens: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in St Helens:

  • Go Ape Liverpool: This outdoor adventure park offers a range of activities, including tree-top adventures, zip lines, and obstacle courses.
  • Haydock Park Racecourse: Enjoy a day at the races at this prestigious racecourse.
  • St Helens Theatre Royal: Watch a play or a musical at this historic theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of St Helens

St Helens has an eclectic mix of restaurants, cafes, and traditional pubs serving local cuisine. Here are some local dishes to try and local beverages to savor:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scouse: A traditional meat and vegetable stew, often served with crusty bread.
  • Wigan Kebab: A local delicacy consisting of a meat pie served between two halves of a buttered muffin.
  • Black Peas: A savory dish of slow-cooked peas served with vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Cains Bitter: A locally brewed ale with a smooth and malty taste.
  • Lancashire Tea: This distinctive blend has a rich and warming flavor that’s perfect on a cold day.
  • St Helens Cider: This fruity and refreshing cider is made using locally sourced fruit.

Where to Stay in St Helens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St Helens has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste, from plush hotels to affordable hostels and cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • Mercure St Helens: This charming hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers comfortable beds, modern amenities, and excellent service.
  • Travelodge St Helens: This budget-friendly hotel is conveniently located and offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • The Shankley Hotel: This luxurious option is located in nearby Liverpool and offers stylish rooms, an onsite spa, and a rooftop bar with stunning views of the city.

What to Buy in St Helens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St Helens offers a range of unique and locally made products that make great souvenirs. Here are some top picks:

  • St Helens Glass: Handcrafted glassware is a local specialty and makes a beautiful souvenir or gift.
  • St Helens Rugby Merchandise: Support the local team by buying some rugby merchandise, such as a team shirt or scarf.
  • Books About St Helens: Learn more about this fascinating town by picking up a local history book or novel set in St Helens.

Local Culture and Customs of St Helens

St Helens boasts a proud working-class heritage and a friendly and welcoming population. When visiting St Helens, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit St Helens

The best time to visit St Helens is in the summer months of June through September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Helens

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting St Helens:

Do:

  • Do try some of the local cuisine, such as Scouse, Wigan Kebab, or Black Peas.
  • Do explore the local countryside by hiking, biking, or horse riding.
  • Do visit the World of Glass museum to learn about the town’s glassmaking heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs or cultural landmarks.
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

St Helens is a relatively safe town, and visitors should have no problems as long as they take normal precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in St Helens

  • Avoid walking alone after dark, especially in unlit areas.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and secure when in public places.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in St Helens

  • Be sure to pack any necessary medications and bring a copy of your prescription with you.
  • Make sure you have adequate medical insurance for your trip.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when outdoors and drink plenty of water.

Getting to St Helens: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Helens is well-connected to other parts of the UK by road and public transport. Here are some options for getting to and around the town:

Airports Near St Helens

  • Manchester Airport: Approximately 30 miles from St Helens.
  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport: Approximately 11 miles from St Helens.

Bus Stations Near St Helens

  • St Helens Bus Station: Located in the town center and served by multiple bus companies.
  • Liverpool One Bus Station: Connects to St Helens via the 10A bus.

Other Options

  • Train: St Helens is served by two train stations, St Helens Central and St Helens Junction, both of which connect to major cities such as Manchester and Liverpool.
  • Car hire: Multiple car hire companies operate in the town.

How to Get Around in St Helens

St Helens is a relatively small town that’s easy to navigate by foot, bike, or bus. The town center offers plenty of shopping and dining options, and most of the major tourist attractions are within walking distance of each other. Buses are the most convenient way to get around, with multiple routes serving the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from St Helens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St Helens is located in the heart of Merseyside and offers easy access to several other popular destinations. Here are some recommended places to visit:

  • Liverpool: Home of the Beatles, Liverpool is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage and plenty of attractions.
  • Manchester: A thriving metropolis, Manchester is famous for its music, football, and shopping.
  • Southport: This seaside town is located just a short drive from St Helens and offers beautiful beaches, an amusement park, and a historic pier.

Conclusion

St Helens is a hidden gem that offers a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. From hiking and biking in the scenic countryside to exploring museums and galleries, this town has something for everyone. With a friendly and welcoming population, top-notch accommodation options, and excellent transportation links, St Helens is a must-visit destination in the North West of England.
