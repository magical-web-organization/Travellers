Introduction to Southport

Southport is a seaside town in Merseyside, located in the North West region of England in the United Kingdom. Known for its sand dunes, beaches, and fishing industry, Southport has been a popular tourist destination since the Victorian era. The town is also a nature lover’s paradise, with miles of sandy beaches and a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Whether you’re interested in learning about the town’s rich history or just want to soak up the sun, Southport has something for everyone. In this guide, we will explore the top places to visit, best things to do, and where to stay in Southport. We’ll also cover local customs, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Southport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southport has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places worth visiting. The top attractions in this category include:

  • The Atkinson: This cultural hub is home to multiple art galleries, a museum, and a library. The Atkinson showcases local artwork and is a great place to learn about the town’s past.
  • Southport Pier: The oldest iron pier in the country, Southport Pier offers stunning views of the seafront. Visitors can also explore a small museum dedicated to the pier’s history.
  • Victorian Quarter: This charming area of town features Victorian-style architecture and is home to several boutiques, cafes, and restaurants.
  • Wayfarers Arcade: This beautifully restored Edwardian arcade features a range of independent shops and boutiques.

Natural Attractions of Southport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southport is known for its stunning natural beauty, and there are several outdoor attractions worth exploring. The top natural attractions in this category include:

  • Ainsdale Beach: This expansive beach is perfect for long walks and features sand dunes and a nature reserve.
  • Botanic Gardens: These award-winning gardens feature Victorian-style landscaping and are home to a variety of rare flora and fauna.
  • Mere Sands Wood Nature Reserve: This 42-hectare site features woodlands, meadows, and wetlands, making it a popular spot for birdwatching.
  • Martin Mere Wetland Centre: This nature reserve is home to a variety of bird species, including swans, geese, and ducks. Visitors can also take a boat ride and learn about wetland conservation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southport

Southport offers several hiking and biking routes for those who love the outdoors. The top routes in this category include:

  • The Trans Pennine Trail: This 215-mile trail runs from Southport to Hornsea and is suitable for both hiking and biking.
  • The Cheshire Lines Path: This flat, 5-mile trail runs from Southport to Aintree and is perfect for families and beginner cyclists.
  • The Sefton Coastal Path: This 21-mile route follows the coastline from Crosby to Southport and offers stunning views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Southport: Activities and Tours

From amusement parks to boat tours, Southport offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. The top things to do in this category include:

  • Pleasureland: This family-friendly amusement park features a range of rides and attractions. Don’t miss the classic Victorian carousel or the thrilling log flume ride.
  • Southport Theatre: This historic venue hosts concerts, plays, and comedy shows throughout the year.
  • Southport Ghost Walks: Explore the town’s haunted history on a spooky ghost walk. Tours are led by knowledgeable guides and are suitable for all ages.
  • Southport Pier Tram: Take a ride on the Southport Pier Tram and enjoy stunning views of the seafront.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southport

To make the most out of your visit to Southport, we have put together a week-long itinerary. This itinerary includes a mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty spots, and fun activities for the whole family. Here’s a breakdown of each day:

  • Day 1: The Atkinson and Wayfarers Arcade
  • Day 2: Ainsdale Beach and Botanic Gardens
  • Day 3: Victorian Quarter and Southport Pier
  • Day 4: Martin Mere Wetland Centre and Pleasureland
  • Day 5: Mere Sands Wood Nature Reserve and Southport Ghost Walks
  • Day 6: Sefton Coastal Path and Southport Theatre
  • Day 7: Free day to explore and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Southport

Must-Try Dishes

Southport is known for its seafood and traditional British fare. The top dishes to try in this category include:

  • Fish and chips: No trip to the seaside is complete without trying fish and chips. Some of the best places to try this classic dish include The Swan, The Sandgrounder, and The Fisherman’s Rest.
  • Eccles cakes: These sweet pastries filled with currants and spices are a local delicacy. Try them at the award-winning Lovingly Artisan bakery.
  • Southport Potted Shrimps: This regional dish features shrimp in a spiced butter sauce. You can find it at several local restaurants, including The Phoenix.

Local Beverages

Southport has a thriving craft beer scene, and there are several breweries and pubs worth visiting. The top beverages to try in this category include:

  • Southport Brewery: This craft brewery produces a range of beers, including IPAs and stouts. Try the popular “Formby IPA” or the “Baywatch Blonde.”
  • The Guest House: This cozy pub features a rotating selection of cask ales and bottled beers.
  • The George: This historic pub offers live music and a range of international beers and ciders.

Where to Stay in Southport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southport offers a range of accommodation options, from affordable hostels to luxury hotels. The top places to stay in this category include:

  • The Bold Hotel: This historic hotel features elegant rooms and is located in the heart of town.
  • The Vincent Hotel: This chic, modern hotel overlooks the seafront and features a rooftop bar.
  • The Falstaff: This family-run guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • YHA Southport: This budget-friendly hostel is located near Ainsdale Beach and offers dormitory and private rooms.

What to Buy in Southport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southport offers a range of unique souvenirs and locally-made products. The top items to buy in this category include:

  • Southport rock: This traditional seaside sweet is made from sugar and flavouring and is often sold on sticks.
  • Bliss Hotel candles: These handmade candles use locally sourced ingredients and come in a range of scents, including “Seashore,” “Woodland,” and “Lavender Fields.”
  • Southport honey: This locally-produced honey is a delicious souvenir that can be found at several shops and markets around town.

Local Culture and Customs of Southport

Southport has a proud maritime history, and the local culture reflects this. Visitors can expect to find a relaxed, friendly atmosphere and a love for all things seaside-related. Fishing and water sports are popular hobbies, and the town hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, including the Southport Air Show and the Southport Food and Drink Festival.

Best Time to Visit Southport

The best time to visit Southport is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices. For a quieter visit, consider coming in the shoulder seasons of spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southport

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and traditional British fare.
  • Do explore the town’s history and culture.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural beauty spots.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in the town.
  • Don’t ignore safety warnings around the coastline.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southport

Southport is a safe town to visit, but visitors should still take precautions. The top safety tips in this category include:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.
  • Follow safety warnings around the coastline and stay away from dangerous areas.

Health in Southport

Southport has multiple medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications with them, and consider purchasing travel insurance in case of emergency.

Getting to Southport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southport is easily accessible by several forms of transportation. The top options in this category include:

Airports Near Southport

  • Manchester Airport: This major airport is located approximately 45 miles from Southport, and offers both domestic and international flights.
  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport: This smaller airport is located approximately 20 miles from Southport, and offers domestic and European flights.

Bus Stations Near Southport

  • Southport Bus Station: This is the town’s main bus station, serving both local and regional routes.
  • Liverpool ONE Bus Station: This station offers direct connections to Southport from the city centre.

Other Options

  • Train: Southport has a train station with direct connections to Liverpool and Manchester.
  • Car: Southport is easily accessible by car, with several major roads connecting the town to other cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Southport

Southport is a walkable town, and most attractions are within easy walking distance of each other. For longer distances, visitors can use the local bus service or hire a taxi. Cycling is also a popular option, and there are several rental shops around town.

Where to Go Next from Southport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southport is located near several other popular destinations in the North West region of England. The top places to visit in this category include:

  • Liverpool: This vibrant city is located approximately 17 miles from Southport and offers a range of cultural attractions and historic sites.
  • Manchester: This cosmopolitan city is located approximately 45 miles from Southport and features a range of tourist attractions, shopping, and nightlife.
  • The Lake District: This stunning national park is located approximately 75 miles from Southport and offers some of the most picturesque landscapes in the country.

Conclusion

Southport is a stunning seaside town with a rich history, diverse range of natural beauty spots, and exciting activities and tours. From its award-winning beaches to its cultural hubs, there’s something for everyone in Southport. Whether you’re looking for a family holiday, romantic getaway, or solo adventure, Southport has it all.
