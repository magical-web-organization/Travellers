Rossendale, Lancashire, is a charming destination in the North West of England. With stunning natural scenery, rich history, and a thriving food and drink scene, Rossendale offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve compiled everything you need to know to plan a trip to Rossendale.

Introduction to Rossendale

Nestled among the rolling hills of Lancashire, Rossendale is a picturesque valley town that encompasses an area of 43 square miles. The town boasts a rich industrial history that is still evidenced by its many museums and landmarks. Today, Rossendale is known for its scenic vistas, outstanding outdoor activities like hiking and biking, and a thriving arts scene.

List of Places to Visit in Rossendale

There are plenty of must-see attractions in Rossendale, and we’ve put together a comprehensive list of the top destinations you won’t want to miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll love exploring Rossendale’s numerous museums and cultural landmarks. Be sure to visit the Helmshore Mills Textile Museum, which showcases Rossendale’s pivotal role in the Industrial Revolution. Another must-see cultural attraction is the Whitaker Museum and Art Gallery, which features an impressive collection of art, artifacts, and natural history exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Rossendale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rossendale offers some of the most beautiful scenery in England. Check out the panoramic views of Rossendale Valley from the stunning Hameldon Hill. Or, take a leisurely stroll at Rossendale’s parks and countryside, like Cowm Reservoir and Stubbylee Park. For outdoor enthusiasts, Rossendale offers an array of activities including hiking, biking, and rock climbing at iconic spots like Lee Quarry and Moorland Gateway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rossendale

Rossendale is a paradise for walkers and bikers, with many routes catering to all abilities. The Rossendale Way is a 41-mile circular route that showcases the region’s stunning landscapes, natural wonders, and historic sites. There are many other hidden trails to explore, including the Mary Towneley Loop and Irwell Sculpture Trail.

Best Things to Do in Rossendale: Activities and Tours

From exciting adventure sports to relaxing spa experiences, Rossendale offers plenty of fun activities to fill your days. Horseriding at Rossendale’s horse stables, caving at Stump Cross Caverns, and skiing at Ski Rossendale are just a few examples. Unique tours of Rossendale include Dunnockshaw Farm’s sheep dog displays and the Heritage Tram Tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rossendale

For those wanting to maximise their stay, we have put together a week-long itinerary of Rossendale. Highlights include a day at Rawtenstall Market, the Rossendale Heritage Trail, visiting the sculpture park in Waterfoot, and learning about local customs at Bacup Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Rossendale

Rossendale’s dining scene is ever expanding, with an exciting array of eateries to keep your taste buds tingling. Be sure to try unique dishes like Blackburn pudding, tripe, and black peas. Additionally, Rossendale boasts a burgeoning craft beer scene with some of England’s best brews available.

Must-Try Dishes

Some must-try dishes in Rossendale include Lancashire hotpot, Butter Pie, rag pudding, and Eccles cakes.

Local Beverages

Finish off your meal with a refreshing pint of Bowland Brewery’s Hen Harrier beer or a coffee at The Clog and Billycock pub.

Where to Stay in Rossendale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rossendale offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers, including hotels, hostels, B&Bs, and guesthouses. Some top recommendations for a comfortable stay include the Wolfenden Farm Cottages for a countryside escape, the Oaks Hotel for luxury lodging, and The Whitaker B & B for a cozy home-away-from-home experience.

What to Buy in Rossendale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take some of Rossendale’s charm home with you by picking up local souvenirs. Unique gifts include handmade Lancashire cheese at Haslingden Halo, vintage finds at Rossendale Antiques, and preserved meads at Cooper King Distillery.

Local Culture and Customs of Rossendale

Rossendale has deep cultural roots that are evident in its native tongue, the Rossendale dialect, and its unique dancing traditions. Be sure to experience the locals’ passion for the Bacup Coconut Dancers and learn the history of this colorful tradition at the Bacup Natural History Society.

Best Time to Visit Rossendale

Rossendale has something to offer visitors every season, but the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is mild and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rossendale

Before visiting Rossendale, it’s important to understand the customs and etiquette of the area. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and wildlife.
  • Visit the local farmer’s market and support local businesses.
  • Learn about the town’s history through visiting museums and landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Trespass or litter in the Peak District.
  • Wear impractical shoes when hiking or walking.
  • Talk loudly or obnoxiously in public.

Safety and Health

Rossendale is generally a safe and healthy place to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions.

Safety in Rossendale

Be careful when hiking or biking, and take note of any warning signs. If you’re worried about traveling alone, it may be best to join a guided tour or stay in populated areas.

Health in Rossendale

There are no specific health risks in Rossendale, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and a first-aid kit on hand.

Getting to Rossendale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rossendale is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Rossendale

The nearest airports are Manchester International Airport, Liverpool John Lennon Airport, and Leeds Bradford Airport.

Bus Stations Near Rossendale

Rawtenstall Bus Station and the Burnley Bus Station are both options to take for a bus service in and around Rossendale.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available for travelers looking for a more convenient option.

How to Get Around in Rossendale

Once in Rossendale, travelers can utilize local bus services, taxis or car rentals to get around. Many visitors opt for exploring the area on foot or renting bikes from the popular CMB Cycles in the town centre.

Where to Go Next from Rossendale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Explore the surrounding towns and attractions like the popular Blackpool Beachfront, Manchester’s history with the football craze or traveling further afield to Liverpool for a music-inspired city tour.

Conclusion

Rossendale truly offers something for everyone, whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture lover. From enjoying beautiful scenic views to experiencing the lively food and drink scene, Rossendale is undoubtedly an unforgettable trip. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to plan your next visit to Rossendale, Lancashire.
