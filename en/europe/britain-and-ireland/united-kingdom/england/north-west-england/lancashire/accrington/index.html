Introduction to Accrington

Accrington is a small town located in Lancashire, North West England. This thriving community offers visitors a wealth of cultural and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a relaxing getaway in the beautiful English countryside.

Whether you’re interested in heritage, outdoor activities, or simply exploring a new place, Accrington has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, hike, and dine in Accrington, as well as providing useful travel tips to make your stay more enjoyable.

List of Places to Visit in Accrington

Accrington offers plenty of exciting attractions for visitors to explore, from historically significant sites to picturesque natural landscapes. Here are some of the must-see destinations for your next trip to Accrington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haworth Art Gallery - showcases British and European art from the 17th century to the present day.

  • Accrington Market Hall - one of the oldest markets in Lancashire, dating back to the 1860s.

  • Accrington Pals Memorial - commemorates the sacrifice made by the 11th Battalion East Lancashire Regiment during World War I.

Natural Attractions of Accrington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Coppice - a beautiful woodland park that is perfect for a picnic or nature walk.

  • Hyndburn Wind Farm - a striking collection of wind turbines that provides renewable energy to the local area while also offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Roughlee Waterfall - a picturesque waterfall located within the rugged terrain of the Pendle Hill area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Accrington

Accrington is a paradise for outdoors enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking and cycling adventures. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Great Harwood Circular Walk - a 6-mile route that passes through farmland, woodland, and countryside.

  • Tolkien Trail - a 6-mile hiking trail that takes visitors on a tour of the area’s literary landmarks, including the childhood home of author J.R.R. Tolkien.

Best Things to Do in Accrington: Activities and Tours

Accrington offers visitors a diverse range of activities and tours, all designed to showcase the best of this beautiful town. Some of the top things to do in Accrington include:

  • Accrington Stanley Football Club - watch a match at the Crown Ground and experience the electric atmosphere of this popular local team.

  • Oscar’s Restaurant - try the delicious locally sourced cuisine at this award-winning restaurant, which offers an ever-changing menu of seasonal dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Accrington

Looking for a comprehensive itinerary for a week-long stay in Accrington? Here’s a sample plan:

Day Activity
1 Explore the historic Haworth Art Gallery and visit the Accrington Market Hall for local produce and handmade products
2 Hike the Tolkien Trail, visiting local landmarks and enjoying the stunning scenery

5 Take in the natural beauty and breathtaking views from Hyndburn Wind Farm
6 Visit the Roughlee Waterfall and enjoy a picnic lunch in the scenic outdoor area

Food and Drink Scene of Accrington

No trip to Accrington would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here’s what you need to know:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lancashire Hotpot - a hearty stew made with lamb or beef, potatoes, and vegetables.

  • Chorley Cakes - sweet, pastry-like cakes that are filled with currants and dusted with sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Boddingtons Beer - a light, refreshing ale that originated in Manchester and is now brewed in Lancashire.

  • Lancashire Tea - a strong, flavorful tea that is a local favorite.

Where to Stay in Accrington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accrington offers plenty of lodging options for visitors, whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse. Some of the top accommodations in the area include:

  • Mercure Blackburn Dunkenhalgh Hotel and Spa - a luxurious hotel that blends 700 years of history with modern comforts and amenities.

  • Sparth House Hotel - a stunning 18th century manor house that offers a peaceful and relaxing getaway.

What to Buy in Accrington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring back from your trip to Accrington? Here are some of the best local products to consider:

  • Lancashire Cheese - a tangy, distinctively flavored cheese that is made using traditional methods.

  • Lancashire Rose - a traditional handmade soap that is crafted using locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Accrington

Accrington is known for its strong sense of community and rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions, which include:

  • Supporting the Accrington Stanley Football Club - this popular local team is an important part of the community and has a dedicated fan base.

  • Celebrating the annual Accrington Food and Drink Festival - a lively and colorful event that showcases the best in local cuisine and beverages.

Best Time to Visit Accrington

Accrington is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm. However, the best time to visit Accrington is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor activities and attractions are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Accrington

When visiting Accrington, here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine - Accrington has some of the best cuisine in Lancashire, so be sure to sample the local delicacies.

  • Do take a hike or bike ride through the stunning countryside - Accrington’s natural beauty is best experienced on a leisurely hike or bike ride.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage areas of natural beauty - Accrington is home to many fragile ecosystems, so it’s important to respect the environment and take care not to damage it.

  • Don’t make disrespectful comments about local customs or traditions - Accrington is a close-knit community that takes pride in its unique customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to embrace these cultural practices with an open mind and heart.

Safety and Health

Here’s what you need to know about staying safe and healthy during your trip to Accrington:

Safety in Accrington

Accrington is a relatively safe and peaceful town. However, visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety, such as staying aware of their surroundings at all times and not leaving valuable items unattended.

Health in Accrington

Accrington has a standard healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located throughout the town and nearby areas. Visitors are advised to take out travel insurance before their trip, in case of emergency medical treatment.

Getting to Accrington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Accrington is conveniently located near several major transportation hubs, making it easily accessible by plane, train, or bus. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Accrington

  • Manchester Airport - approximately 25 miles from Accrington; offers flights to destinations throughout the UK and Europe.

  • Blackpool Airport - approximately 27 miles from Accrington; offers flights to destinations throughout the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Accrington

  • Accrington Bus Station - located in the heart of Accrington, this station offers local and regional bus services.

  • Blackburn Bus Station - approximately 4 miles from Accrington; offers regional and national bus services.

Other Options

  • Train Travel - Accrington has its own train station, which provides direct services to major UK destinations including Manchester and Leeds.

How to Get Around in Accrington

Accrington is a small town and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. For longer journeys or trips to neighboring areas, visitors can make use of the local bus or train services.

Where to Go Next from Accrington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Accrington is surrounded by several charming towns and exciting cities, making it an ideal base for exploring the larger Lancashire area. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Blackpool - a bustling seaside resort town known for its amusement parks, arcades, and lively nightlife.

  • Pendle Hill - a rugged, mountainous region that is perfect for hiking and exploring.

Conclusion

Accrington is a charming town with a rich cultural heritage and plenty of exciting attractions for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in beautiful surroundings, Accrington has something to offer everyone. So start planning your trip today and discover why this small town is such a treasured destination in Lancashire, UK!
