Introduction to Clitheroe

Nestled in the heart of the picturesque Ribble Valley in Lancashire, Clitheroe is a charming market town that offers breathtaking natural landscapes, rich history, and a vibrant food and drink scene. With its cobbled streets, historic buildings, and friendly locals, Clitheroe is a perfect destination for a peaceful retreat or a thrilling adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Clitheroe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Clitheroe Castle Museum - One of the town’s most iconic landmarks, Clitheroe Castle is a 12th-century castle that now houses a fascinating museum showcasing the town’s rich history.

Whalley Abbey - A stunning 14th-century abbey located just a few miles from Clitheroe’s town center.

Natural Attractions of Clitheroe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pendle Hill - This legendary hill is located just a few miles outside Clitheroe, and is a popular hiking spot thanks to its breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Gisburn Forest - This beautiful forest is perfect for biking, hiking and other outdoor activities.

The Ribble Valley - One of the most beautiful areas in the United Kingdom and a designated Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. The valley is home to picturesque villages, stunning landscapes, and an abundance of wildlife, including brown hares, otters, and red deer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clitheroe

Pendle Hill Walking Route - Circular walk with a distance of 7.5 miles and takes around 4 hours. It offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

The Ribble Way - A beautiful long-distance footpath stretching for over 70 miles. The route passes through Clitheroe and offers some of the most stunning views of the Ribble Valley.

Best Things to Do in Clitheroe: Activities and Tours

Food Tour - Get a taste of Clitheroe’s diverse culinary scene with a food tour that takes you to some of the town’s best restaurants and markets.

Cycling Tour - Explore the beautiful Lancashire countryside with a guided cycling tour that takes you along some of the county’s most scenic routes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clitheroe

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Clitheroe Castle Museum.

Day 2: Head to Pendle Hill and tackle the famous walking route to get breathtaking views of the valley.

Day 3: Visit the Whalley Abbey and enjoy a scenic walk around the grounds.

Day 4: Go on a cycling tour through the picturesque Lancashire countryside.

Day 5: Take a food tour that showcases some of the town’s best culinary offerings.

Day 6: Visit Gisburn Forest and enjoy an outdoor adventure.

Day 7: Relax with a pint in one of Clitheroe’s cosy pubs or enjoy a walk along the Ribble Way.

Food and Drink Scene of Clitheroe

Must-Try Dishes

Lancashire Hotpot - A traditional British dish made with lamb, potatoes, and onions, the Lancashire Hotpot is a hearty, comforting meal that is perfect for a chilly evening.

Clitheroe Sausage - Made using locally-sourced meat and a secret spice blend, the Clitheroe Sausage is a must-try for any meat lover.

Local Beverages

Thwaites Beer - A traditional Lancashire beer brewed in Blackburn.

Blackburn Gin - A premium gin made from locally-sourced botanicals.

Where to Stay in Clitheroe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shireburn Arms - A historic inn located in the heart of the town center, the Shireburn Arms offers stylish rooms, a lovely garden, and a popular restaurant.

Fifty One and a Half Boutique B&B - A luxurious bed and breakfast with beautifully designed rooms and stunning views of the Ribble Valley.

The Spread Eagle - A charming guesthouse located just outside the town center, the Spread Eagle offers cozy rooms, a welcoming atmosphere, and great food.

What to Buy in Clitheroe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lancashire Cheese - Made using traditional methods and using locally-sourced milk, Lancashire Cheese is a must-buy for any cheese lover.

Handmade Crafts - Clitheroe is known for its talented artisans, and you’ll find plenty of handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles, to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Clitheroe

Clitheroe is known for its friendly locals, rich history and vibrant community. Visitors can expect a warm welcome and a lively atmosphere no matter where they go in the town.

Best Time to Visit Clitheroe

The best time to visit Clitheroe is during the spring and summer months, when the flowers are in bloom and the countryside is at its most vibrant. However, the town is beautiful all year round and there are plenty of indoor attractions and cozy pubs to keep you occupied during colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clitheroe

Do:

  • Do explore the town center and the beautiful surrounding countryside.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks, including the famous Clitheroe Sausage and Thwaites Beer.
  • Do be friendly and open to meeting new people.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack for all weather conditions, particularly if you plan to hike or explore the countryside.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on what to see and do in town.
  • Don’t litter and respect the natural beauty of the town’s surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clitheroe

Clitheroe is a very safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution when walking alone at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Clitheroe

Clitheroe has a good range of medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics.

Getting to Clitheroe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clitheroe

Manchester Airport - A large international airport located just over an hour’s drive from Clitheroe.

Blackpool Airport - A smaller airport located less than an hour’s drive from Clitheroe.

Bus Stations Near Clitheroe

Clitheroe Bus Station - Local and regional bus service to and from Clitheroe.

Other Options

Train- Clitheroe Train Station provides regular services to the nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Clitheroe

The town center is easily walkable, making foot the easiest and most convenient way to explore the town center. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Clitheroe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blackpool - Known for its famous beachfront promenade and classic amusement park, Blackpool is less than an hour’s drive from Clitheroe.

Preston - A vibrant, historic city located just 30 minutes away by car or train.

Conclusion

Clitheroe is a hidden gem of Lancashire that offers natural beauty, cultural richness and hospitality that visitors will always remember. This charming market town is still a bit of an off-the-beaten-path destination, making it a perfect escape from the busy tourist destinations. With its rich history, fascinating museums, unspoiled countryside, delicious food and drink, Clitheroe has something for everyone. Come enjoy all it has to offer by planing your next getaway to Clitheroe!
