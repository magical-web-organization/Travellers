Introduction to Preston

Preston is a vibrant city located in the North West of England. It is the administrative center of Lancashire and has a rich cultural heritage. The city boasts a range of exciting attractions, from museums and historical sites such as the Harris Museum and Art Gallery, to outdoor adventures like hiking and biking in the nearby countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Preston

Here are some of the top attractions in Preston:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harris Museum and Art Gallery
  • Ribble Steam Railway
  • British Commercial Vehicle Museum
  • Samlesbury Hall

Natural Attractions of Preston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brockholes Nature Reserve
  • Beacon Fell Country Park
  • Grizedale Valley

Hiking/Biking Routes of Preston

Preston has no shortage of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • The Guild Wheel - a 21-mile circular route around Preston
  • Brockholes Nature Reserve
  • The Ribble Way

Best Things to Do in Preston: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours that should definitely be on your list when you visit Preston:

  • Take a guided tour of the Harris Museum and Art Gallery
  • Board a steam train at Ribble Steam Railway
  • Explore Lancashire’s countryside on horseback with the Blackpool Riding Centre

Week-Long Itinerary for Preston

If you’re planning to spend a week in Preston, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Harris Museum and Art Gallery

Food and Drink Scene of Preston

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Preston is complete without trying some of its local cuisine. Be sure to sample:

  • Lancashire Hotpot
  • Eccles Cake
  • Chorley cakes

Local Beverages

Preston is home to a range of local breweries and distilleries, including:

  • Moorbrook Brewery
  • Bowland Brewery
  • Red Bank Cider

Where to Stay in Preston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Preston to suit all budgets:

  • The Park Hotel - a luxury 4-star hotel in the city centre
  • Ibis Preston North - a budget-friendly option just outside the city centre
  • St. Mary’s Lodge - a comfortable guesthouse in a quiet residential area

What to Buy in Preston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Preston, you might want to purchase some of the following souvenirs:

  • Local cheese from Dewlay’s Cheese
  • Handcrafted ceramics and pottery from Leach Pottery
  • Lancashire tea from Farrington’s

Local Culture and Customs of Preston

Preston has a rich cultural heritage, including a strong sporting tradition and a thriving performing arts scene. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are always welcome.

Best Time to Visit Preston

The best time to visit Preston is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Preston

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore Lancashire’s countryside
  • Visit the Harris Museum and Art Gallery

Don’t:

  • Leave any valuables in your car
  • Enter prohibited areas
  • Litter in public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Preston

Preston is generally a safe city to visit, especially in tourist areas. However, like any other city, visitors should take care with their belongings and avoid walking alone at night in unknown areas.

Health in Preston

There are several hospitals and clinics in Preston, including the Royal Preston Hospital and the Lancashire Teaching Hospital.

Getting to Preston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Preston

The closest airport to Preston is Manchester Airport, which is approximately 40 miles away. Other airports that are easily accessible include Liverpool John Lennon Airport and Leeds Bradford Airport.

Bus Stations Near Preston

Preston Bus Station is located in the city centre and is the main transportation hub for the city. National Express and Megabus buses run services to and from Preston.

Other Options

Preston is also easily accessible by train, with direct connections from London, Manchester, and Liverpool.

How to Get Around in Preston

Preston is a compact city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, there are also excellent bus and taxi services for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Preston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations to visit from Preston:

  • Manchester - a vibrant city with shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Lake District - a scenic national park with breathtaking views and outdoor activities.
  • Blackpool - a popular seaside resort with theme parks, beaches, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking to explore Lancashire’s countryside, delve into Preston’s cultural heritage, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this vibrant city. We hope this comprehensive guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Preston.
