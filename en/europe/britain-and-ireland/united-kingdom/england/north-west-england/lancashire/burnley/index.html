Introduction to Burnley

Located in Lancashire in North West England, Burnley is a historic town with a rich industrial past. From mills to museums, Burnley has a lot to offer culture seekers and nature enthusiasts alike. Whether you want to explore the town’s heritage or climb its highest peak, Burnley has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all the best things to do and see in Burnley, including natural attractions, culturally important landmarks, hiking and biking routes, local food and drink, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Burnley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Queen Street Mill Textile Museum

This former cotton mill is now a museum and an essential stop for textile enthusiasts and history buffs. The Queen Street Mill Textile Museum showcases a vast collection of machinery and equipment used in the cotton industry. The museum also has stunning displays of actual cotton looms, including a functioning weaving floor that produces cloth for order. The museum houses a unique turbine-driven mill engine, which was installed in the early 1900s and still powers the machines to this day.

Towneley Hall

One of the most important historic sites in Burnley, Towneley Hall, is a beautiful mansion located in 200 acres of spectacular parkland. Visitors can explore the extensive art collection, which features works by some of the world’s most famous artists, including Joshua Reynolds, as well as local painters. The museum also houses fascinating exhibits of the area’s natural history, with displays dedicated to birds, mammals, and insects.

Gawthorpe Hall

Gawthorpe Hall is a stunning Elizabethan country house that was built in the early 1600s. The house contains original furniture, textiles, and artwork, and visitors can experience what life was like in the upper-class society of the 17th and 18th centuries. Gawthorpe Hall has stunning gardens that are open for visitors to explore, with picturesque ponds and flower beds, and a sculpture garden.

Natural Attractions of Burnley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pendle Hill

Pendle Hill is a famous landmark in Burnley, and for many, it is one of the area’s most iconic sights. Standing at 557 metres high, Pendle Hill dominates the skyline, and the climb to the top is a favorite pastime for many. The hill has been featured in literature and folklore and is considered to be one of the most haunted places in the country.

Weavers’ Triangle

The Weavers’ Triangle is a collection of historic buildings that served as a hub for Burnley’s textile industry during the Industrial Revolution. The area is now a designated conservation area that’s open to visitors who want to explore the past and learn more about the town’s history. The Weavers’ Triangle has a superb canal-side location with plenty of outdoor spaces to explore.

Towneley Park

Towneley Park is Burnley’s largest park and is located in the grounds of Towneley Hall. With acres of lush greenery, mature trees, and rolling hills, Towneley Park is the perfect spot for a family picnic or a peaceful stroll. The park has plenty of sporting facilities, including a golf course, football pitches, and a skate park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burnley

Walks

Towneley Park

Towneley Park has a wide range of walking trails, from gentle strolls to more challenging hikes. The park has waymarked routes that take visitors around the park’s main features, including the gardens, woodlands, and river.

Thursden Valley

Thursden Valley is a tranquil wilderness area in Burnley’s countryside. The Valley has a network of clearly marked paths that visitors can use to explore the area’s unique flora and fauna. The walk is suitable for all ages and abilities.

Bike Routes

Mary Townley Loop

The Mary Townley Loop is a 45-mile long mountain bike trail that takes visitors through some of the most picturesque scenery in Burnley and the surrounding area. The trail starts and ends at Towneley Park, but cyclists can join the route at any point. The Mary Townley Loop is a more challenging route, and visitors must be confident in their abilities before embarking on this trail.

Best Things to Do in Burnley: Activities and Tours

Burnley Canal Festival

The Burnley Canal Festival is an annual event that celebrates the town’s industrial heritage. The festival has live entertainment, including dancing and music performances, and visitors can enjoy boat trips along the canal.

Pendle Hill Walk

The Pendle Hill Walk is a challenging route that takes visitors to the top of Pendle Hill. The walk offers stunning views of the countryside surrounding Burnley and is a favorite for many visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Burnley

Must-Try Dishes

Lancashire Hotpot

Lancashire Hotpot is a hearty beef stew that’s been a local staple for over 200 years. The dish features mutton, potatoes, and onions, all slow-cooked to perfection.

Eccles Cake

Eccles Cake is a local pastry made with buttery flaky pastry and filled with sweet currants. The cake is named after the village of Eccles, located just outside of Burnley.

Local Beverages

Blackburn’s Brewery

Blackburn’s Brewery is a local brewery that produces a range of craft ales, including American IPA and Hefeweizen. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and enjoy a tasting session afterward.

Burnley FC Ale

Burnley FC Ale is a local beer that’s brewed exclusively for fans of Burnley Football Club. The beer is a classic pale ale that’s characterized by its hoppy flavor and distinctive Burnley branding.

Where to Stay in Burnley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Western Oaks Hotel & Leisure Club

Located just outside of Burnley’s town center, the Best Western Oaks Hotel & Leisure Club is a comfortable, affordable option for visitors. The hotel has an indoor swimming pool, a spa, and an on-site restaurant.

Travelodge Burnley

The Travelodge Burnley is a budget-friendly option that’s located in the town center. The hotel has comfortable rooms and all the amenities visitors need to enjoy a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Burnley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pendle Hill Gin

Made with local botanicals, including heather and rowan berries, Pendle Hill Gin is a unique souvenir to bring back from Burnley. The gin has a distinctive flavor that’s characteristic of the area’s natural landscape.

Burnley Football Club Merchandise

For football fans, Burnley FC merchandise is a must-buy souvenir. Visitors can buy everything from replica shirts to scarves and hats, all featuring the club’s iconic claret and blue branding.

Local Culture and Customs of Burnley

Burnley has a rich industrial past. Still, it’s also a town that values its traditions and customs. Visitors should be aware of the town’s strong football culture, with Burnley FC being a source of pride for many locals. Visitors should also be aware of the town’s industrial heritage, with former mills and factories found around every corner.

Best Time to Visit Burnley

The best time to visit Burnley is during the summer months when the weather is warm, and the parks and outdoor spaces are in full bloom. The town also has several annual events, including the Burnley Canal Festival and the Burnley Agricultural Show, which take place during the summer months.

  • Don’t forget to try Lancashire Hotpot
  • Don’t miss a chance to explore Burnley’s industrial heritage.
  • Don’t skip the Burnley Canal Festival.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burnley

Burnley is a relatively safe town, and visitors should be able to explore without facing any significant risks. However, as with any urban area, visitors should be careful when traveling at night and should keep an eye on their belongings at all times.

Health in Burnley

Burnley has plenty of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, so visitors should have no trouble finding medical assistance if needed.

Getting to Burnley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burnley

The nearest airports to Burnley are Manchester Airport, Liverpool John Lennon Airport, and Leeds Bradford Airport. All three airports offer direct flights from many destinations around the world, with regular connections to Burnley via public transport.

Bus Stations Near Burnley

Burnley has two bus stations, The Burnley Bus Station in the town center and Burnley Bus Station Rawtenstall located outside of the town center.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Burnley by train, with direct services from major cities in the UK, including London, Manchester, and Liverpool.

How to Get Around in Burnley

Burnley has an efficient public transport network, including buses, trains, and taxis, making it easy to explore the town and the surrounding area. Visitors can also rent a bicycle from one of the many bike rental companies located around the town.

Where to Go Next from Burnley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hebden Bridge

Hebden Bridge is a picturesque town located just a short drive from Burnley. The town has a vibrant arts and music scene, with plenty of galleries, bars, and music venues to explore.

The Forest of Bowland

The Forest of Bowland is a vast natural park that’s located just outside of Burnley. The park is home to rolling hills, lush forests and is the perfect place for hiking, cycling, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Burnley is a historic town with a rich industrial past. From mills to museums, Burnley has a lot to offer culture seekers and nature enthusiasts alike.
