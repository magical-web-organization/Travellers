Introduction to Cleveleys

Cleveleys is a quaint seaside town tucked away on the northwestern coast of England, in the county of Lancashire. Renowned for its tranquil atmosphere, stunning scenery, and sandy beaches, Cleveleys is the perfect getaway destination for those seeking a peaceful and relaxing vacation. While it may not have the hustle and bustle of larger cities, Cleveleys boasts a fascinating cultural heritage, diverse wildlife, and a range of fun activities to suit all ages and interests. From exploring historical sites to sampling local delicacies, here is a guide to everything you need to know about Cleveleys, Lancashire.

List of Places to Visit in Cleveleys

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff or just looking to learn more about the local culture, then be sure to stop by some of Cleveleys’ most popular museums and historical sites.

  1. Thornton-Cleveleys Heritage Trail: This trail will take you on a journey through the town’s rich heritage, showcasing various landmarks including the Wyre Light Lighthouse and the Memorial Gardens.
  2. Rossall Point Tower: This tower is home to a museum dedicated to the rescue efforts of Captain William Cherry of HMS Mary Rose after a shipwreck in 1897.
  3. Fleetwood Museum: A short drive from Cleveleys, this local museum gives a detailed history of Fleetwood town and its surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Cleveleys: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cleveleys is home to a host of natural wonders, including serene beaches, pleasant parks, and captivating wildlife. Be sure to explore the following attractions for an unforgettable outdoor experience:

  1. The Promenade: A beautiful walkway overlooking the sea, perfect for picnics or watching the sunset.
  2. Marsh Mill: This 18th-century windmill has been restored and now stands as a popular tourist attraction, offering captivating views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. Wyre Estuary Country Park: ideal for bird watching, walking, and admiring nature, this park incorporates fields, woodland, and marshland across 150 acres.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cleveleys

Walking and cycling enthusiasts will feel right at home in Cleveleys, with countless scenic routes to choose from. From tranquil canal paths to stunning coastal routes, here is our list of the top hiking/biking trails you should explore:

  1. Cinder Path: Nature reserve route, where you can see things like wildflowers and rabbits located on the old railway track from Fleetwood to Blackpool.
  2. Thorp’s Cloud Nature Reserve: Experience the best of Cleveley’s countryside in this haven that goes to the roof of the hill.
  3. Wyre Way: A 45-mile trail that follows the River Wyre from its source to the sea and passes through the countryside near Cleveleys.

Best Things to Do in Cleveleys: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for adventure or a relaxing day out, there is something for everyone in Cleveleys. From amusement parks to marketplaces to seaside activities, here are our top recommendations:

  1. Blackpool Pleasure Beach: The ultimate amusement park experience featuring thrilling rides, roller coasters, games, and much more.
  2. Farmer Parr’s Animal World: This farm-park attraction offers lots of animal activities including hugging, feeding, and bottle-feeding for the little ones.
  3. Cleveleys Beach: Sunbathing, swimming, paddling, and sandcastle-building are all popular things visitors to Cleveleys’ beach love to do.

Food and Drink Scene of Cleveleys

While in Cleveleys, visitors can indulge in some of the best local dishes and beverages found in Lancashire. Be sure to try these local specialties:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lancashire Hotpot: A savory dish made with lamb, potatoes, and a variety of vegetables baked until tender, warm and comforting.
  2. Potted Shrimps: A sumptuous shrimp dish typically served on toast, often cooked with cream, nutmeg, and butter.
  3. Blackpool Rock: A sweet, hard stick candy in traditional seaside flavors such as mint, vanilla, and raspberry.

Local Beverages

  1. Thwaites Brewery: One of Lancashire’s most celebrated breweries, offering up a selection of beers and ales such as Lancaster Bomber and Wainwright.
  2. Higham Hall Wines: An award-winning wine merchant specializing in unique varietals and blends produced in the area’s sandy soil.

Where to Stay in Cleveleys: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation in Cleveleys, visitors can take their pick from a wide range of options catering to a spectrum of budgets. Here are our top recommendations:

  1. The Shard Riverside Inn: A cosy, family-friendly hotel situated near the beach and offering river views.
  2. Singleton Lodge Country House Hotel: Nestled in the countryside, this four-star hotel is a tranquil haven amidst nature, perfect for couples.
  3. The Port of Call: A quirky, budget-friendly, and stylish guesthouse located in the lively heart of Cleveleys.

What to Buy in Cleveleys: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to take home a unique piece of Cleveleys, there are several shopping options worth checking out. From local handicrafts to Lancashire souvenirs, these are the ideal places to find your perfect souvenir:

  1. Barefoot Artisan Shop: A shop that sells artisan products made in the north-west of England, ranging from home decor to treats.
  2. Farmer Parrs: Where local produce is the order of the day including handmade jams and chutneys, fudge and locally brewed beers.
  3. Market Days: where local traders sell antiques, foods, and other goods on Wednesdays and Saturdays.

Local Culture and Customs of Cleveleys

Cleveleys is a town renowned for its welcoming atmosphere and hospitality towards visitors. Residents are proud of their town’s traditions and enjoy sharing these customs with all who visit. When exploring Cleveleys, visitors should keep in mind the following customs and etiquettes:

  • Respect the local wildlife and nature.
  • Greeting with a smile and offering pleasantries is typical.
  • Casual clothing is suitable for most occasions.
  • Respecting the noise curfew at night is expected.

Best Time to Visit Cleveleys

Cleveleys is a fantastic destination to visit throughout the year. The peak season runs from May to September when the weather is perfect for beach activities, but the town is popular with locals all year round. The winter months are mild and can be beneficial, considering there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cleveleys

When visiting Cleveleys, here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • respect the local animals and care for the environment.
  • try the local cuisine and experience the local culture.
  • engage in conversations with locals and make new friends.

Don’t:

  • litter or harm the natural environment or wildlife.
  • make noise at night or be too loud.
  • overlook the local customs and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cleveley’s

Cleveleys is generally a safe town for visitors, but sensible precautions should always be taken to ensure your wellbeing. The following tips will help you stay safe:

  • Be careful when swimming in the sea, especially areas without lifeguards.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid rough areas during the night.
  • Wear a mask in public places to protect yourself and others.

Health in Cleveleys

The town has several facilities to take care of your health care needs. You can access a nearby health center with walk-in facilities and arrange a consultation.

Getting to Cleveleys: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cleveleys is well-connected to other towns and cities in the region, offering several transport options for visitors.

Airports Near Cleveleys

  • Manchester Airport: the largest airport reachable by car or train in about 1 hour and 20 minutes.
  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport: only 1hr away, taxis and trains are common transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Cleveleys

Visitors can get to Cleveleys by bus from nearby cities such as Blackpool or Fleetwood. Local bus services stop at Birchway Avenue and Beach Road stops.

Other Options

Visitors can use taxis, cycle hire, or even a tram service that runs between Blackpool and Fleetwood along the coast.

How to Get Around in Cleveleys

Walking is the best option for visiting attractions within the town or the coastline. However, biking is also a popular option. Visitors can rent bicycles from public hire points or use the nearby tram services that go from Fleetwood to Blackpool.

Where to Go Next from Cleveleys: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those wishing to explore the surrounding area, Cleveleys serves as an ideal base to discover nearby destinations such as:

  • Blackpool, a seaside town famous for its tower, the Pleasure Beach, and Blackpool Zoo.
  • The Yorkshire Dales, a stunning national park with breathtaking scenery, perfect for hiking, biking, or driving.
  • Lancaster, a historic city with a well-preserved castle, museums, and a vibrant arts scene.

Conclusion

With its rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty, Cleveleys provides an exceptional vacation destination that will delight all. From scenic hikes to sampling unique flavors, there’s something for everyone in this charming town on Lancashire’s coast. Let this guide help you explore the region and experience the joy of Cleveleys to the fullest!
