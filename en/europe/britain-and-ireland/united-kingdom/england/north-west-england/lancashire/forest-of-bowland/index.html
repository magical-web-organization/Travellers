Introduction to Forest of Bowland

Nestled in the rolling hills of Lancashire, North West England, the Forest of Bowland is one of the UK’s most beautiful and unspoiled natural landscapes. This Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty (AONB) boasts of stunning vistas, picturesque villages, charming market towns, and vast open spaces that attract visitors from all over the world. The Forest of Bowland covers an area of 312 square miles, and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including rare bird species and the famous Red Deer.

List of Places to Visit in Forest of Bowland

There are many places to visit in the Forest of Bowland, and it can be tough to decide which ones to prioritize. Here is a list of recommended attractions to help you plan your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lancaster Castle: This historic castle, built in the 12th century, now operates as a museum of crime and punishment. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the castle’s dark past.
  • Lancaster Maritime Museum: This museum celebrates the rich maritime heritage of Lancaster and tells the story of the historic port.
  • The Tolkein Trail: Visit the locations that inspired the famous author J.R.R. Tolkein, who frequented this region during his childhood holidays.

Natural Attractions of Forest of Bowland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beacon Fell Country Park: Covering an area of 271 acres, Beacon Fell Country Park has numerous walking trails, picnic spots, and children’s play areas.
  • Clitheroe Castle and Museum: Built in the 12th century, the castle offers spectacular views of the countryside, as well as exhibitions on the geology, archaeology, and history of the region.
  • Trough of Bowland: A winding, scenic road that offers fantastic views of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forest of Bowland

The Forest of Bowland is a popular destination for walkers and cyclists, with a range of routes available to suit all ages and abilities. Here are some recommended hiking/biking trails:

  • The Nick of Pendle: A scenic hike that offers views of the Ribble Valley, Pendle Hill, the Lake District, and Morecambe Bay.
  • The Bowland Wild Boar Park Trail: A family-friendly nature trail that includes a variety of animals and educational activities.
  • Bowland Forest High Level Route: A challenging and scenic bike ride that offers stunning views of the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Forest of Bowland: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities to do in Forest of Bowland, from exploring the great outdoors to sampling the local food and drink. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit one of the many farmers’ markets in the region and sample local produce, including artisan cheeses, handmade chocolates, and artisanal bread.
  • Go on a guided nature walk to learn about the wildlife and flora of the region.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride and see the Forest of Bowland from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Forest of Bowland

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for those planning a trip to Forest of Bowland:

  • Day 1: Arrive in the region and explore Lancaster city. Visit Lancaster Castle and Maritime Museum.
  • Day 2: Drive to Trough of Bowland and take a scenic drive along the winding road. Then visit the Clitheroe Castle and Museum.
  • Day 3: Hike up to Pendle Hill for wonderful views.
  • Day 4: Take a nature walk to explore the wildlife of Forest of Bowland.
  • Day 5: Visit the Beacon Fell Country Park for a day of outdoor activities and picnics.
  • Day 6: Visit one of the many farmers’ markets in the area and taste the local food.
  • Day 7: Take a hot air balloon ride, go cycling, or wander around some of the local villages.

Food and Drink Scene of Forest of Bowland

The Forest of Bowland is known for its delicious local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lancashire Hotpot: A slow-cooked lamb and potato casserole, this dish is a local favorite.
  • Goosnargh Chicken: A free-range, locally sourced chicken that has a unique flavor due to its special diet.
  • Clitheroe Food Festival: This annual festival celebrates the region’s food and drink, and is a great opportunity to sample local delicacies.

Local Beverages

  • Bowland Beer: This award-winning local brewery produces a range of delicious, handcrafted beers.
  • Ribble Valley Gin: This small-batch gin is made using locally sourced botanicals and is a must-try for gin lovers.

Where to Stay in Forest of Bowland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Forest of Bowland offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • The Three Fishes: This 18th century inn offers comfortable, country-style rooms and a traditional pub atmosphere.
  • The Spread Eagle Inn: This 16th century coaching inn has been lovingly restored and offers comfortable, contemporary rooms.
  • YHA Clitheroe: This popular hostel offers affordable accommodation in the heart of Clitheroe.

What to Buy in Forest of Bowland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some souvenirs or local products, here are some recommendations:

  • Forest of Bowland Guidebooks: A range of guidebooks and maps are available to help explore the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Forest of Bowland

The people of Forest of Bowland are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should try to follow some basic customs to show respect for local culture:

  • Be polite and friendly with locals, as they are known for their hospitality and kindness.
  • Try to follow the countryside code when exploring the outdoors.
  • Respect the local wildlife and keep a safe distance from them.

Best Time to Visit Forest of Bowland

The Forest of Bowland is beautiful all year round, but each season offers a different experience. The best time to visit depends on your preferences:

  • Spring (March to May): Wildflowers are in bloom, and the countryside is lush and green.
  • Summer (June to August): The weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is at its most vibrant.
  • Autumn (September to November): The trees turn into golden and orange hues, creating stunning scenery.
  • Winter (December to February): The countryside is covered in snow, creating a magical winter wonderland.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forest of Bowland

The Forest of Bowland is generally a safe area for visitors. However, it’s always important to take precautions when exploring the outdoors:

  • Always carry a map and compass and familiarize yourself with the area before venturing out.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the weather.
  • Be aware of any hazards, such as steep cliffs or fast-running water.

Health in Forest of Bowland

The Forest of Bowland is a relatively low-risk area for disease and illness. However, visitors should follow general health and safety precautions:

  • Always wash your hands before eating or handling food.
  • Seek medical advice if you experience any symptoms of illness or infection.
  • Avoid close contact with wild animals or their droppings.
