Introduction to Kirkham

Kirkham is a beautiful town located in the district of Fylde, Lancashire, UK. Known for its historic landmarks, breath-taking landscapes, and warm locals, Kirkham is a must-visit destination for every traveler.

Kirkham is situated close to major cities like Manchester, Liverpool, and Blackpool, making it easily accessible by road, rail, and air. Whether you are traveling solo, with family or friends, Kirkham offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kirkham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kirkham is steeped in history, and one of the best ways to learn more about its heritage is by visiting museums and historical places. The most popular ones include:

  • St. Michael’s Church: This historic church has been a significant landmark in Kirkham since the 13th century. Its stunning architecture, beautiful stained glass windows, and peaceful ambiance attract tourists and locals alike.

  • Kirkham Grammar School: Founded in 1549, Kirkham Grammar School is one of the UK’s oldest and most prestigious schools. The school’s impressive modern and historical architecture make it worth a visit.

  • The Old Corn Mill: This 18th-century mill is now a museum that showcases the rich agricultural history of Lancashire. The museum features interactive exhibits, informative displays, and a beautiful garden.

Natural Attractions of Kirkham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kirkham is located close to an array of scenic locations that offer ample opportunities for adventure and relaxation. Here are some of the most famous natural attractions worth visiting:

  • Dowbridge Nature Reserve: A tranquil haven for nature lovers. Set in a picturesque setting, the reserve boasts scenic hiking trails, diverse flora and fauna, and bird-watching opportunities.

  • Moss Side Farm: A family-friendly attraction offering unique experiences, such as feeding and petting farm animals, pony rides, and tractor tours.

  • Brockholes Nature Reserve: A stunning reserve with abundant natural beauty and vast wildlife. Visit Brockholes for scenic walks, picnics, and bird-watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirkham

Exploring Kirkham’s beautiful surroundings is best done on foot or bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails for an enjoyable experience:

  • Fylde Ramble: A 60-mile trail that runs through Kirkham. It covers diverse terrains, including woodlands, hills, and valleys, and offers spectacular views of the countryside.

  • The Guild Wheel: A 21-mile route that circles around Preston. The trail passes through Kirkham and features scenic riverbanks, parks, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Kirkham: Activities and Tours

Kirkham offers a variety of exciting activities and tours, catering to different interests, ages, and budgets.

  • Blackpool Pleasure Beach: A short drive from Kirkham, Blackpool Pleasure Beach features a range of thrilling rides and attractions, including one of the tallest and fastest roller coasters in the world.

  • Ribble Steam Railway: A must-visit for train enthusiasts. Enjoy a ride on a vintage steam train, explore the railway museum, and learn about the rich railway heritage of Lancashire.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kirkham

Day 1: Visit St. Michael’s Church and Kirkham Grammar School.

Day 2: Hike the Fylde Ramble trail or bike the Guild Wheel.

Day 3: Explore Dowbridge Nature Reserve and Moss Side Farm.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Blackpool Pleasure Beach.

Day 5: Visit The Old Corn Mill and Ribble Steam Railway.

Day 6: Relax and take a leisurely stroll around town.

Day 7: Visit Brockholes Nature Reserve and indulge in some bird-watching.

Food and Drink Scene of Kirkham

Must-Try Dishes

Sampling local cuisine is a vital part of any travel experience. Kirkham is home to several fantastic eateries, serving up traditional and contemporary dishes that will leave your taste buds wanting more.

  • Butter Pie: A delicious savory pie made with potatoes, onions, butter, and pastry.

  • Lancashire Hotpot: A hearty lamb stew topped with a layer of sliced potatoes.

  • Eccles Cake: A sweet pastry filled with raisins, candied peel, and mixed spices.

Local Beverages

Kirkham and the surrounding areas produce a range of delicious, locally sourced beverages. Be sure to try:

  • Lancaster Bomber Ale: A flavorsome ale made by Lancaster Brewery, showcasing the best of Lancashire.

  • Lancashire Tea: A refreshing black tea, perfect with a slice of Eccles Cake.

Where to Stay in Kirkham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kirkham offers an array of accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences. Some popular places to stay in Kirkham include:

  • The Birley Arms Hotel: A cozy hotel situated in the heart of Kirkham. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a bar and lounge, and a restaurant serving traditional British cuisine.

  • The Mill House Bed and Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast located a short distance from Kirkham. The B&B offers spacious rooms and a continental breakfast served in a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Kirkham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kirkham has a range of unique shops and boutiques selling locally produced goods, perfect for memorable souvenirs. These include:

  • Kirkham Pottery: Handcrafted pots, plates, and cups made by skilled artisans.

  • Chocolatiers of Kirkham: Delicious handmade chocolates and truffles made with local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Kirkham

Kirkham is a friendly and welcoming town, where locals cherish their cultural heritage and traditions. Respectful behavior and greetings go a long way in making your visit pleasurable.

Best Time to Visit Kirkham

The best time to visit Kirkham is from April to September when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the countryside is in full bloom. Be prepared for occasional showers and pack appropriately.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kirkham

Do:

  • Learn about the rich history of Kirkham by visiting its museums and historical landmarks.

  • Take advantage of the beautiful natural surroundings by exploring hiking and biking trails.

  • Sample local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kirkham

Kirkham is a safe and friendly town, but like any other destination, basic safety precautions should be taken.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid isolated areas, especially at night.

Health in Kirkham

Kirkham has several medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics that provide quality healthcare services. Check with your medical provider for any necessary vaccinations or health advice before visiting Kirkham.

Getting to Kirkham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kirkham

The nearest airports to Kirkham are Manchester Airport and Liverpool Airport, which are both approximately 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Kirkham

Kirkham is well-connected by buses that run between major cities like Manchester, Liverpool, and Blackpool.

Other Options

Kirkham can also be reached by train, with the nearest railway station located in Kirkham and Wesham.

How to Get Around in Kirkham

Exploring Kirkham is best done on foot or bike, but there are also taxis and buses available for transport.

Where to Go Next from Kirkham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations and excursions from Kirkham include:

  • The Lake District: A stunning national park that offers picturesque walks, breathtaking scenery, and a variety of outdoor activities.

  • Liverpool: A vibrant city with a rich history, stunning architecture, and excellent cultural attractions.

  • Blackpool: A seaside resort famous for its lively nightlife, amusement park, and sandy beaches.

Conclusion

From its historic landmarks to its stunning natural beauty and welcoming locals, Kirkham is a hidden gem that every traveler should add to their itinerary. Be sure to follow our comprehensive travel guide, plan your trip well in advance, and enjoy your time exploring this fascinating town.
