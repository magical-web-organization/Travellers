Introduction to Morecambe

Morecambe, located in Lancashire in North West England, is a beautiful seaside town that offers visitors a delightful mix of cultural and outdoor experiences. Its sandy beaches, stunning landscapes, and historical landmarks make Morecambe a popular destination among tourists from all around the world.

In this guide, we will help you plan your trip to Morecambe by providing insights into the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Morecambe

Morecambe has many attractions to offer that cater to a wide range of interests. Here are the top places to visit in Morecambe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ashton Memorial: A historical landmark that offers stunning views of Morecambe Bay.

  • Lancaster Maritime Museum: This museum showcases the fascinating history of Lancaster’s port and maritime heritage.

  • Morecambe Winter Gardens: A vintage entertainment venue that hosts concerts, plays, and comedy shows throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Morecambe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Morecambe Bay: Explore the vast sandy beaches, go for a stroll on the promenade, or take a scenic boat ride across the bay.

  • Happy Mount Park: A charming recreational park with beautiful gardens, playgrounds, and mini-golf courses, that is perfect for a family day out.

  • Heysham Nature Reserve: With over 130 different bird species, Heysham Nature Reserve is a must-visit for bird-watchers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morecambe

Morecambe boasts a spectacular landscape and countryside, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes in Morecambe:

  • Morecambe Promenade: The promenade runs for miles along the coast and provides breathtaking views of Morecambe Bay.

  • Warton Crag: A challenging hiking trail that takes you to the top of Warton Crag, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Morecambe Bay Cycleway: A scenic cycling route that passes through coastal towns and villages around Morecambe Bay.

Best Things to Do in Morecambe: Activities and Tours

Morecambe has much to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Morecambe:

  • Morecambe Bay Walks: Take a guided walk across Morecambe Bay and explore the beauty of this vast sandy beach.

  • Segway Tours: Experience the town and its stunning surroundings on a Segway tour.

  • Lancaster Canal Boat Trips: Take a canal boat tour and enjoy the stunning landscapes of Lancashire.

Week-Long Itinerary for Morecambe

To experience the best of Morecambe, we suggest the following itinerary:

Day 1-2:

Explore the cultural diversity of Morecambe by visiting Ashton Memorial, Lancaster Maritime Museum, and Morecambe Winter Gardens.

Day 3-4:

Enjoy the natural beauty of Morecambe by taking a stroll on the beach, visiting Happy Mount Park, or exploring Heysham Nature Reserve.

Day 5-6:

Experience the thrilling outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, or a scenic canal boat tour.

Day 7:

Relax and unwind on the final day of your trip with a leisurely walk along the promenade and a local meal of your choice.

Food and Drink Scene of Morecambe

Morecambe is famous for its delicious seafood and offers a variety of cuisines to satisfy your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Morecambe Bay Shrimps: Freshly caught shrimps served with butter and bread.

  • Lancashire Hotpot: A dish of meat and vegetables layered under sliced potatoes.

  • Battered Sausage: A local delicacy that makes the perfect late-night snack.

Local Beverages

  • Lancaster Brewery Ale: A locally brewed ale made with traditional ingredients.

  • Hawkshead Elderflower Cider: A refreshing cider made with locally grown elderflowers.

Where to Stay in Morecambe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Morecambe that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Morecambe:

  • Midland Hotel: A beautiful historic hotel located in the heart of Morecambe with stunning sea views.

  • YHA Morecambe: A budget-friendly hostel located just a few minutes’ walk from the beach.

  • The Crown Hotel: A cozy, family-run guesthouse located in the town center.

What to Buy in Morecambe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Morecambe offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and memories of your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy in Morecambe:

  • Morecambe Rock: A classic British sweet made with different flavored hard candy.

  • Local Art: Morecambe is home to many talented artists, and you can find their artwork at local galleries and art stores.

  • Lancashire Cheese: A delicious cheese produced locally that makes the perfect snack or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Morecambe

Morecambe is a friendly and welcoming town with a rich cultural heritage. It is customary to greet people politely and with a friendly smile. Morecambe has a robust arts and cultural scene that is worth exploring.

Best Time to Visit Morecambe

Morecambe is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm, and various outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morecambe

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood.

  • Do explore the beautiful countryside and coastline.

  • Do respect the environment and local culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.

  • Don’t ignore warning signs around the coastline.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morecambe

Morecambe is generally a safe town. However, it is recommended to keep an eye on your belongings, as pick-pocketing can occur in tourist areas.

Health in Morecambe

Morecambe has a good healthcare system, and the Royal Lancaster Infirmary provides medical care 24/7. Visitors are encouraged to have travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Morecambe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Morecambe is well-connected with various transportation options. Here are some ways to get to Morecambe:

Airports Near Morecambe

  • Manchester Airport: Approximately 1.5 hours’ drive from Morecambe.

  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport: Approximately 1.4 hours’ drive from Morecambe.

Bus Stations Near Morecambe

  • Morecambe Bus Station: Located in the town center and served by various regional and national bus services.

Other Options

  • Train: Lancaster railway station is located only a few miles away from Morecambe and offers regular services to Manchester and the rest of the UK.

  • Car: Morecambe is easily accessible by car, with good motorway connections to the rest of the UK.

How to Get Around in Morecambe

Morecambe is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, there are various public transportation options for those who prefer not to walk. Local buses operated by Stagecoach and other tour operators run regularly throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Morecambe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Morecambe is close to other exciting destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Lancaster: A beautiful city with a rich history of castles and museums.

  • Lake District: A stunning national park that offers outdoor adventures such as hiking, biking, and water sports.

Conclusion

Morecambe has something to offer for everyone, from culture vultures to outdoor enthusiasts. The town has a rich heritage, a vibrant arts and cultural scene, and spectacular landscapes. Plan your next trip to Morecambe, and you won’t be disappointed.
