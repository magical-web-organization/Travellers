A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Oldham, Greater Manchester

Located in Greater Manchester, Oldham is a vibrant town that offers an intriguing mix of historical landmarks and modern attractions. This guide will give you an in-depth look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Oldham, along with practical information and insider tips to make your trip a memorable one.

Introduction to Oldham

Oldham is a historic market town that sits on the edge of the Pennines, a range of hills and mountains that run through Northern England. The town has a rich industrial heritage, and its cotton mills were once an important part of the region’s economic landscape. Oldham has since transformed into a modern town, with an array of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions that draw visitors from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Oldham

Oldham is full of exciting attractions that offer something for every kind of traveler. Here are some of the top places to visit during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oldham Coliseum Theatre: The Coliseum Theatre is one of the most well-known theaters in the region, offering a diverse array of plays, musicals, and other live performances throughout the year.

  • Gallery Oldham: This museum offers a variety of exhibits that showcase Oldham’s rich cultural history, including art, textiles, and local artifacts.

  • Saddleworth Museum and Art Gallery: Situated on the edge of the Peak District National Park, this museum offers an extensive collection of artwork and artifacts from the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Oldham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dovestone Reservoir: Dovestone Reservoir is a scenic body of water that sits at the foot of the Pennines, providing a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and birdwatching.

  • Tandle Hill Country Park: This sprawling park offers a range of activities for visitors, including walking paths, picnic areas, and playgrounds.

  • Saddleworth Moor: This vast moorland is home to a variety of wildlife and is a popular destination for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oldham

Whether you prefer hiking or cycling, Oldham has plenty of trails to explore. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Tame Valley Way: This 12-mile trail takes you along the River Tame, providing stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Pennine Bridleway: This famous trail takes you through the heart of the Pennines, offering breathtaking views and challenging terrain for experienced hikers and bikers.

  • Daisy Nook Country Park: This picturesque park features several walking trails that wind through wooded areas and by the River Medlock, providing a peaceful escape from the bustling town center.

Best Things to Do in Oldham: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with a group, Oldham has plenty of things to see and do. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Oldham Market Hall: This bustling market offers a wide array of local goods, including fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and traditional crafts.

  • Oldham Leisure Centre: If you’re looking for some indoor fun, head to this modern leisure center, which features a swimming pool, gym, and sports courts.

  • The Old Hamlet Ghost Walk: This spooky tour takes you through the streets of Oldham, introducing you to the town’s most haunted landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oldham

To make the most of your trip to Oldham, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that highlights the best attractions and activities in the area:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Gallery Oldham to learn about the town’s history and culture.
Day 2 Explore Dovestone Reservoir and take a hike along one of the many trails in the area.
Day 3 Check out the Oldham Market Hall and sample some of the local cuisine.
Day 4 Take a tour of the Oldham Coliseum Theatre and catch a live performance.
Day 5 Visit Tandle Hill Country Park and enjoy a picnic in the scenic surroundings.
Day 6 Take a spooky ghost walk around the town and learn about its haunted past.
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs at one of Oldham’s independent retailers, then relax with a pint at a local pub.

Food and Drink Scene of Oldham

Oldham has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some of our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Eccles Cakes: This sweet treat is made with a flaky pastry shell and filled with currants, sugar, and spices.

  • Lancashire Hotpot: This hearty stew features tender lamb, potatoes, and onions, slow-cooked to perfection.

  • Bury Black Pudding: A traditional English sausage made with pig’s blood, barley, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Timothy Taylor’s Landlord Ale: This award-winning beer is brewed locally and is a favorite among beer enthusiasts.

  • Oldham Lager: This light and refreshing beer is brewed in Oldham and is perfect for a hot summer day.

  • Lancashire Tea: This classic tea blend is made with leaves from Lancashire’s tea farms and has a rich, full-bodied flavor.

Where to Stay in Oldham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oldham offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Rookery Nook Guest House: This charming guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a warm welcome from the owners.

  • The White Hart Inn: This historic hotel is situated in the heart of Oldham and offers comfortable rooms and a great location.

  • Travelodge Manchester Oldham: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, perfect for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Oldham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from your trip, Oldham has plenty of options. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Oldham F.C. Football Scarf: Support the local football team and keep warm with an Oldham Athletic F.C. scarf.

  • Oldham-made Cheese: Sample some of Oldham’s finest cheeses, made using traditional methods and locally sourced ingredients.

  • Oldham Mills Textiles: Take home a piece of Oldham’s industrial heritage with a piece of locally-made textile.

Local Culture and Customs of Oldham

Oldham has a rich cultural history, and locals take pride in their heritage. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and temples and should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Oldham

The best time to visit Oldham is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring and autumn can also be lovely times to visit, with milder temperatures and beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oldham

Do:

  • Take a stroll through Oldham’s picturesque parks and gardens.

  • Sample some of the local cuisine and beer.

  • Try your hand at hiking or biking on one of the town’s many trails.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

  • Disrespect the natural surroundings by littering or damaging local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oldham

Oldham is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid unlit and secluded areas at night and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Oldham

Medical facilities and pharmacies are readily available throughout Oldham, and visitors can access healthcare services easily.

Getting to Oldham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oldham is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some of the options available:

Airports Near Oldham

  • Manchester Airport: Manchester Airport is the closest international airport to Oldham, located approximately 20 minutes away by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Oldham

  • Oldham Bus Station: This central bus station serves many local and regional bus lines.

Other Options

  • Train: Oldham has several train stations that provide easy access to nearby towns and cities.

  • Car Rental: Car rental options are available throughout Oldham, making it simple to explore the wider area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Oldham

Oldham is a compact town that is easily explored on foot or by bike. Local buses and trains provide additional options for getting around, while car rental is also available for visitors who want more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Oldham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more places to explore after your time in Oldham, here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Manchester: Just a short train ride away, the vibrant city of Manchester offers world-class museums, galleries, and cultural attractions.

  • Peak District National Park: This stunning national park is just a short drive away and offers a wealth of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery.

Conclusion

Oldham is a charming town that offers a mix of historical landmarks, natural wonders, and modern attractions. Whether you’re interested in hiking, shopping, or simply soaking up the local culture, you’ll find plenty to explore in this delightful corner of Greater Manchester.
