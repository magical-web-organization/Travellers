Introduction to Piccadilly-East Centre

Located in Manchester, in the North West of England, in the United Kingdom, the bustling commercial district of Piccadilly-East Centre is a vibrant hub of culture, history, and entertainment. Boasting stunning architecture, a wide array of dining options and easy transport links to the greater Manchester area, Piccadilly-East Centre is a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Piccadilly-East Centre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manchester Art Gallery: showcases the best of British art, including works by local artists, as well as Europe’s finest works.
  • Manchester Museum: experience the history and culture of Manchester with fascinating exhibits on topics ranging from natural history to anthropology.
  • Manchester Athenaeum: admire the elegance of this historic building and join a tour to learn about its history as a hub of cultural activity in Manchester.
  • Piccadilly Gardens: a lively meeting spot with a park, fountains, and seasonal markets.

Natural Attractions of Piccadilly-East Centre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Heaton Park: the perfect spot for a peaceful stroll, picnic, or cycling trip. It’s home to a boating lake, a petting zoo, beautiful gardens, and many sports-playing fields.
  • Fletcher Moss Park & Botanical Gardens: explore this beautifully maintained park, featuring rose gardens, water features, play areas, and numerous walking paths for bird watching.
  • Epping Forest: an enchanting woodland north of Picadilly-East Centre, with opportunities for hiking and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Rochdale Canal Towpath: connecting Manchester-Salford Quays to Rochdale, this picturesque route is perfect for walkers, joggers, and cyclists.
  • Manchester Ship Canal Trail: a 36-mile route from Salford Quays to Liverpool, traversing through historic aristocratic homes and scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Piccadilly-East Centre: Activities and Tours

  • Manchester City Stadium Tour: Experience the history and importance of Manchester United and Manchester City football clubs and football culture with this entertaining tour.
  • Manchester Craft and Design Centre: Attend activities, shows, and lectures, and meet the artists in the creative hub of Manchester.

Food and Drink Scene of Piccadilly-East Centre

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bury Black Pudding: a type of sausage made from pork blood, pork fat, and oatmeal; a fantastic breakfast or appetizer.
  • Manchester Tart: a sweet pastry filled with jam and custard, egg custard tarts are a treat.
  • Fish and Chips: fried filets with a side of thickly cut chips; enjoy the classic English meal at hip restaurant The Bay.

Local Beverages

  • Robinsons Brewery beers: This famous Manchester brewing company has been producing award-winning beers for over 150 years.
  • Vimto: a fruity drink that is very popular in the UK; originally it was created by a Manchester business.

Where to Stay in Piccadilly-East Centre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Principal Manchester: offering 271 rooms, The Principal Manchester is a Victorian palazzo that has been beautifully restored to combine the art nouveau and modern styles.
  • Holiday Inn Express: with 192 rooms and an enviable central Manchester location, the recently refurbished Holiday Inn Express is a convenient base for exploring the area.

What to Buy in Piccadilly-East Centre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Eccles Cakes: round cakes filled with currents; an enjoyed sweet treat.
  • Manchester Tart Recipe Book: Bring home the recipe to make your own tart!

Local Culture and Customs of Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Drinking Etiquette: In general, it’s best to buy rounds of drinks, rather than everyone buying their own, especially if you’re out at the pub.
  • Tipping: 10-15% is an acceptable tipping standard when you receive good service.
  • Queuing: In the UK, queuing is a way of life. Always join a line and wait patiently.

Best Time to Visit Piccadilly-East Centre

The peak season is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is at its warmest, and most tourists visit. Winter (December to February) can be chilly, but it’s still an excellent time to visit Manchester for its markets, winter events and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Piccadilly-East Centre

Do:

  • Enjoy the unique food and drink scene in Manchester.
  • Take advantage of the city’s excellent cultural offerings.

Don’t:

  • Wear football shirts of rival teams during game days.
  • Litter or smoke in public spaces.
  • Be loud or obnoxious during quiet hours.

Safety and Health

Safety in Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Be aware of pickpockets and muggers. Keep valuables secured and be conscious of your surroundings in public areas.
  • Be sure to purchase travel insurance before your trip.

Health in Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Pack essential medication before you travel to allow medical access in case of an emergency.

Getting to Piccadilly-East Centre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Manchester Airport: Located only 9 miles (14.5km) from Piccadilly-East Centre, it is a quick train ride away.

Bus Stations Near Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Manchester Coach Station: servicing national and local coach services, the station is centrally located near the Chinatown area.
  • Manchester Airport Bus Station: regular coach services connect Manchester Airport and Piccadilly-East Centre.

Other Options

  • Metrolink Tram system: An excellent way to travel around Manchester and Piccadilly-East Centre. It’s a fast, cheap, and efficient method of transportation.
  • Taxis: Many taxi establishments are scattered across the city, and taxis are an affordable way to get around.

How to Get Around in Piccadilly-East Centre

  • Walk: Walking is the easiest way to explore the city on foot and to appreciate the architecture and atmosphere.
  • Bike: Pick up a bike from different bike-sharing schemes around Manchester.
  • Bus or tram: Manchester has an impressive public transportation system that makes it easy for travelers to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from Piccadilly-East Centre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Lake District: located just 1.5 hours away by car from Manchester, visit the majestic views, boating, and hiking trails.
  • York: a medieval city, only 2 hours by car or train from Manchester; immerse yourself in its historical landmarks and claim to the best tea in England.

Conclusion

With so much to offer in terms of culture, food, and attractions, Piccadilly-East Centre is a fantastic destination for a vacation or mini-break in Manchester. With this guide, you now have an extensive list of activities, transportation options, and important tips to ensure you get the most out of your trip. Pack your bags and get ready for an exciting adventure in Manchester’s Piccadilly-East Centre.
