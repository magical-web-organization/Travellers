Introduction to The Quays

The Quays is a world-renowned cultural and sporting destination in Greater Manchester, England. Located in the heart of Salford, this waterfront area is home to some of the best art and cultural venues in the country, including The Lowry, Imperial War Museum North, and The Manchester United Museum and Tour Centre.

Whether you’re looking to explore the area’s rich history, enjoy outdoor adventures or simply enjoy the local food and drink scene, The Quays has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit attractions, best restaurants and bars, top-rated hotels and travel tips to make your trip exciting and memorable.

Local Culture and Customs of The Quays

The Quays is a culturally diverse area where visitors can experience an eclectic mix of traditions and customs. Some of the most notable cultural celebrations include Chinese New Year, the Manchester Jazz Festival, and the Manchester Food and Drink Festival.

Best Time to Visit The Quays

The best time to visit The Quays is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. During this time, you can enjoy outdoor activities such as cycling, walking, and watersports.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Quays

Do:

  • Visit The Lowry Theatre for a show or exhibition
  • Take a tour of Old Trafford Stadium
  • Spend a day exploring the Imperial War Museum North
  • Enjoy the local food and drink scene

Don’t:

  • Wear a rival football team’s shirt near Old Trafford
  • Litter in public spaces
  • Disturb the local wildlife when on a nature trail
