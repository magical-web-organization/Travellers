Introduction to Bury

Located in Greater Manchester, Bury is surrounded by scenic countryside landscapes and historic landmarks. With its industrial heritage, bustling market towns, and cultural offerings, Bury has plenty to offer visitors looking for an authentic English experience. Whether you’re looking to explore the historical sites, indulge in delicious cuisine, or simply relax in the great outdoors, Bury is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Bury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bury has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can immerse themselves in the town’s fascinating history by exploring its many museums and historical sites.

  • Bury Art Museum: This museum features a vast collection of contemporary and traditional artworks, including pieces by local and international artists.

  • Bury Parish Church: This Grade I listed building showcases stunning medieval architecture and design, providing a glimpse into the town’s rich religious history.

  • The Fusilier Museum: Dedicated to the Royal Regiment of Fusiliers, this museum displays an extensive collection of uniforms, weaponry, and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Bury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bury is known for its scenic countryside, idyllic parks, and natural beauty, offering a range of outdoor activities and exciting adventures.

  • Burrs Country Park: This park covers more than 161 acres and features a scenic river, woodlands, and meadows, as well as outdoor activities like fishing, hiking, and cycling.

  • Clarence Park: Located in the heart of Bury, Clarence Park offers a peaceful retreat from the bustling town centre, with gardens, a playground, and a water fountain.

  • Holcombe Hill: Hiking enthusiasts are sure to enjoy the stunning scenic views of the countryside from the summit of Holcombe Hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bury

Bury has plenty of scenic hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore.

  • Irwell Sculpture Trail: This trail follows the path of the River Irwell through Bury, showcasing impressive artworks and stunning scenery along the way.

  • Mary Towneley Loop: A challenging 47-mile trail for experienced hikers and mountain bikers, the Mary Towneley Loop covers some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in the area.

  • Peel Tower: Hikers can make their way up Peel Tower to enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Bury: Activities and Tours

In addition to its museums, parks, and natural landscapes, Bury is known for offering unique experiences and exciting activities.

  • Bury Market: Known as one of the best markets in England, Bury Market offers everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts, antiques, and souvenirs.

  • East Lancashire Railway: Ride on a vintage steam train through the scenic countryside of Greater Manchester.

  • The Rock: Shop, dine, and relax at The Rock, a bustling hub of entertainment in Bury.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bury

Here is an example itinerary for a week-long trip to Bury:

1 Explore Bury Market

Food and Drink Scene of Bury

Must-Try Dishes

Bury is known for its delicious local cuisine, with must-try dishes including:

  • Bury Black Pudding: This famous blood sausage is a staple of the local diet, typically served with breakfast or as a snack.

  • Rag Pudding: A local favourite, this steamed savoury pudding is filled with meat, vegetables, and gravy.

  • Butter Pie: A simple yet delicious pie made with shortcrust pastry, mashed potatoes, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Bury Ale: A traditional English ale brewed in local breweries.

  • Manchester Gin: A small batch gin produced in nearby Manchester, featuring a range of botanicals.

Where to Stay in Bury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Village Hotel Bury: Offering comfortable rooms and spa facilities.

  • Premier Inn Bury: A budget-friendly option, with comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving full English breakfasts.

  • The Red Lion: A cosy and traditional English pub with comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Bury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bury Market Souvenirs: Pick up a range of souvenirs including local crafts, trinkets, and handmade goods.

  • Bury Black Pudding: Bring home some of the famous Bury Black Pudding and try it for yourself.

  • Lancashire Cheese: This tangy cheese is a popular local product, perfect for bringing home as a gift or a treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Bury

Bury is known for its friendly locals, unique dialect, and interesting customs. Visitors should be sure to embrace the local culture and customs during their trip, including:

  • Greeting people with a friendly “Ay up!” (a local greeting)

  • Trying local cuisine, including Bury Black Pudding and Butter Pie

  • Visiting the traditional pubs for a drink and to meet the locals

Best Time to Visit Bury

The best time to visit Bury is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bury

Do:

  • Bring your walking shoes to explore the scenic countryside

  • Try the local cuisine, including Bury Black Pudding and Butter Pie

  • Visit the local pubs to meet friendly locals and soak up the atmosphere

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring a raincoat or umbrella – the weather can be unpredictable

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions

  • Leave without trying a ride on the vintage East Lancashire Railway

Safety and Health

Safety in Bury

Overall, Bury is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should still take necessary precautions, such as keeping valuables close and avoiding poorly lit or empty streets at night.

Health in Bury

Bury has several medical facilities to cater to visitors’ needs, including doctors’ offices and pharmacies. Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medication and familiarize themselves with the locations of the nearest medical services.

Getting to Bury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bury

The closest airport to Bury is Manchester Airport (MAN), located approximately 16 km south of the town centre.

Bus Stations Near Bury

Bury Interchange is the primary bus station in the town, providing easy access to Greater Manchester and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bury by train or car, with regular train services operating to and from Manchester and other cities in the UK.

How to Get Around in Bury

Getting around Bury is easy, with a range of public transportation options available, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also explore the town on foot or by renting a bike.

Where to Go Next from Bury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manchester: Just a short train ride away, Manchester is a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage, fascinating museums, and lively nightlife.

  • Rochdale: A picturesque town with historic sites and relaxing parks, Rochdale is located only 8 km away from Bury.

Conclusion

Bury is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to immerse themselves in the rich history and culture of Greater Manchester. This comprehensive guide has provided an overview of the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Bury, making it easy to plan an itinerary and make the most out of your trip.
