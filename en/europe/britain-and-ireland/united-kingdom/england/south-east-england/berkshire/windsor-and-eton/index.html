Windsor and Eton, located in the county of Berkshire, in South East England, is a charming and historic small town that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors. The town is famous for its royal residences, beautiful architecture, cultural heritage, and scenic views. Whether you’re interested in history, architecture, outdoor activities, cuisine, or shopping, you’ll find something to love in Windsor and Eton. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town.

Introduction to Windsor and Eton

Windsor and Eton are two separate towns, but they are so close together that they are often considered one destination. Windsor is the larger of the two towns, and it is home to the famous Windsor Castle, one of the primary official residences of the British monarch. Eton, on the other hand, is a small town that is known for its prestigious public school. The River Thames flows through both towns, and you can enjoy a scenic stroll along its banks.

Windsor and Eton are located around 40 kilometers from central London, making them perfect for a day trip from the capital. The towns are also well-connected to other popular tourist destinations in the region, such as Oxford, Bath, and Stonehenge.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor and Eton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Windsor Castle is the most famous cultural attraction in the town. Windsor Castle is the oldest and largest inhabited castle in the world, and it has been the family home of British kings and queens for over 1,000 years. The castle is open to visitors, and you can explore the historic state apartments, chapel, and Queen Mary’s Dolls’ House.

St George’s Chapel, located within the grounds of the castle, is another must-visit cultural attraction. The chapel was built in the 15th century, and it has been the setting for many royal weddings, including Prince Harry and Meghan Markle’s wedding in 2018.

The Windsor Guildhall is also worth visiting. The Guildhall is a historic building that was once the seat of the borough council. The Guildhall has been used for official functions since the 17th century, and it has hosted many royal weddings, including that of Prince Charles and Camilla Parker Bowles.

Natural Attractions of Windsor and Eton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Windsor Great Park is a beautiful open space that covers over 2,000 hectares. The park is home to ancient woodlands, formal gardens, and stunning vistas that are perfect for a picnic or a leisurely walk. The Long Walk, a three-mile parkland avenue that leads from Windsor Castle to the Copper Horse statue, is a popular route for walkers and cyclists.

Boating on the River Thames is a fun way to explore the scenic surroundings of Windsor and Eton. Boat rentals are available, and you can also take a relaxing boat tour that will take you along the river and give you fascinating insights into the history of this area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor and Eton

In addition to the Long Walk in Windsor Great Park, there are several other hiking and biking routes that are worth exploring. The Windsor and Eton Riverside Trail is a six-mile route that follows the River Thames, passing through meadows, pastures, and historic sites. The route starts at Eton Bridge and ends at Bray Lock.

Best Things to Do in Windsor and Eton: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours that you can enjoy in Windsor and Eton. Some of the best options include:

  • Windsor Castle Audio Tour: Explore Windsor Castle at your own pace with an audio tour that will guide you through the castle’s magnificent State Apartments, St. George’s Chapel, and more.

  • Thames River Cruise: Take a relaxing cruise down the Thames and enjoy the scenic views while learning about the history of Windsor and Eton.

  • Changing the Guard Ceremony: Watch the Changing the Guard ceremony that takes place at Windsor Castle. This traditional ceremony features the soldiers of the British Army’s Household Division for a colorful spectacle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Windsor and Eton

Day 1: Visit Windsor Castle and St George’s Chapel.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of Eton College and explore the charming streets of Eton.

Day 3: Explore Windsor Great Park and try boating on the River Thames.

Day 4: Visit Legoland Windsor Resort, a popular amusement park that is perfect for families with children.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Oxford and explore the historic university city.

Day 6: Enjoy the food and drink scene in Windsor and Eton.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby London and explore some of the city’s world-famous attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor and Eton

Windsor and Eton have a vibrant food and drink scene that is sure to satisfy any palate. When visiting, you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Windsor and Eton lamb: This is a local specialty, and you’ll find it on the menu at many restaurants in the town.

  • Traditional British pub food: You’ll find plenty of pubs serving up classic British dishes like fish and chips, bangers and mash, and shepherd’s pie.

Local Beverages

  • Real ale: This is a traditional English ale that is brewed using only natural ingredients and a special fermentation process. Visit one of the town’s pubs for a pint of real ale.

  • Windsor and Eton gin: This is a locally distilled gin that is gaining popularity around the world. Try it in a cocktail or with tonic water.

Where to Stay in Windsor and Eton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many different types of accommodation available in Windsor and Eton. Some of the best options for visitors include:

  • The Castle Hotel Windsor: This is one of the most luxurious hotels in the town, located just 300 meters away from Windsor Castle.

  • The Christopher Hotel: This is a charming 18th-century coaching inn that offers comfortable and spacious rooms.

  • The Royal Adelaide Hotel: This is a cozy and elegant hotel that is located in a historic building in the heart of Windsor.

What to Buy in Windsor and Eton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Windsor and Eton, you should consider buying some of the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Royal-themed souvenirs: There are plenty of shops selling souvenirs featuring the British Royal Family.

  • Windsor and Eton gin: This is a great souvenir that you can bring home and enjoy with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor and Eton

Windsor and Eton have a rich and fascinating history that is worth exploring. The town has been associated with the British Royal Family for centuries, and its royal connections are still evident in the town’s architecture, traditions, and cultural events. Visitors should also be aware of some local customs, such as the tradition of punting on the River Thames, which involves propelling a flat-bottomed boat with a long pole.

Best Time to Visit Windsor and Eton

The best time to visit Windsor and Eton is during the spring and summer months (April to September), when the weather is mild and pleasant. This is also when most of the outdoor events and activities take place in the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windsor and Eton

Do:

  • Do visit Windsor Castle, St George’s Chapel, and Eton College.
  • Do take a boat tour or hire a boat on the River Thames.
  • Do explore Windsor Great Park.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites like St George’s Chapel.
  • Don’t touch the guards or get too close to them during the Changing the Guard ceremony.
  • Don’t litter or damage the historic sites and town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windsor and Eton

Windsor and Eton are relatively safe towns, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of pickpockets and keep an eye on their belongings while in crowded areas.

Health in Windsor and Eton

Visitors to Windsor and Eton do not require any specific vaccinations or medical precautions. The area has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can seek medical assistance at the local hospital or clinics if needed.

Getting to Windsor and Eton: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to Windsor and Eton, including:

Airports Near Windsor and Eton

Heathrow Airport is the nearest airport, located just 15 kilometers away from Windsor and Eton.

Bus Stations Near Windsor and Eton

The town center has a bus station that connects to other nearby towns and London.

Other Options

Visitors to London can take a train from Waterloo Station to Windsor and Eton Riverside train station, which takes around an hour.

How to Get Around in Windsor and Eton

Windsor and Eton are compact towns, and most of the attractions and sights are within walking distance. Visitors can also hire a bike, take a taxi or use public transport to get around the towns.

Where to Go Next from Windsor and Eton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring, including:

  • Oxford: A historic university city that is less than an hour’s drive from Windsor and Eton.

  • Stonehenge: A world-famous prehistoric monument that is just over an hour’s drive from Windsor and Eton.

  • Bath: A beautiful Georgian city that is renowned for its hot springs and architecture.

Conclusion

Windsor and Eton offer visitors a magical blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that makes it a perfect destination for any type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring the historic royal residences, going on outdoor adventures, or indulging in some of the local cuisine and culture, Windsor and Eton will not disappoint you. By following the tips and recommendations provided in this comprehensive tour guide, you can make the most of your trip and create unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.
