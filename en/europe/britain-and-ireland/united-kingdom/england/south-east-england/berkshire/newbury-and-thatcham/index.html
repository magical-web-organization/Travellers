Introduction to Newbury and Thatcham

Located in the ceremonial county of Berkshire in South East England, Newbury and Thatcham offer a charming blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Newbury is a bustling market town that boasts a rich heritage and vibrant contemporary scene, while Thatcham is a peaceful countryside village that is home to stunning landscapes and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Newbury and Thatcham

Whether you are interested in exploring cultural and historical landmarks or immersing in nature and the outdoors, Newbury and Thatcham have something to offer everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Newbury and Thatcham are steeped in history, and there are numerous museums and historical sites to explore. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Newbury Museum - Offers a fascinating insight into the town’s rich history and heritage.
  • West Berkshire Museum - Displays a collection of artefacts and exhibits related to Berkshire’s natural and cultural history.
  • Shaw House - A grand Tudor mansion that showcases 400 years of history and architecture.
  • Donnington Castle - A ruined medieval castle that offers stunning panoramic views from the top.
  • The Watermill Theatre - A quaint theatre with a picturesque riverside location that offers high-quality productions.

Natural Attractions of Newbury and Thatcham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you want to escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and reconnect with nature, Newbury and Thatcham have an abundance of natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Nature Discovery Centre - A wildlife conservation centre that offers educational activities for all ages.
  • Greenham Common - A vast heathland that is home to rare plant species and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Bucklebury Farm Park - A family-friendly farm that offers opportunities to interact with animals and enjoy outdoor activities.
  • Kennet and Avon Canal - A picturesque waterway that winds through peaceful countryside, dotted with charming villages.
  • Snelsmore Common Country Park - A nature reserve that offers hiking, biking, and horse riding trails, as well as birdwatching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newbury and Thatcham

Newbury and Thatcham offer a plethora of hiking and biking routes that cater to all experience levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Ridgeway National Trail - A long-distance hiking trail that offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Lambourn Valley Way - A scenic walking route that follows the course of the River Lambourn.
  • North Wessex Downs Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty - A vast area of protected natural beauty that offers a variety of hiking and biking trails.
  • The Kennet and Avon Cycle Route - A 85-mile cycling route that follows the Kennet and Avon Canal towpath.

Best Things to Do in Newbury and Thatcham: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there are numerous activities and tours to enjoy in Newbury and Thatcham. Here are some of the best:

  • Horse racing at Newbury Racecourse - Catch a thrilling horse racing event at one of the country’s premier racecourses.
  • Vineyard tours and wine tasting - Take a tour of one of the many local vineyards and enjoy tasting some of the finest English wines.
  • Hot air balloon rides - Soar over the stunning landscapes of Newbury and Thatcham on a hot air balloon ride.
  • Thames River Cruise - Take a leisurely cruise along the River Thames and enjoy the scenic views of the countryside.
  • Segway tours - Explore the countryside on a fun and unique segway tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newbury and Thatcham

If you are planning a week-long trip to Newbury and Thatcham, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Newbury, visit Newbury Museum and Shaw House.
  • Day 2: Spend a day at Bucklebury Farm Park and enjoy outdoor activities.
  • Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the countryside.
  • Day 4: Visit Thatcham village and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Day 5: Go hiking or biking in Snelsmore Common Country Park or North Wessex Downs Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.
  • Day 6: Visit the Watermill Theatre and enjoy a high-quality production.
  • Day 7: Take a Thames River Cruise and enjoy the scenic views of the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Newbury and Thatcham

Newbury and Thatcham offer a diverse range of food and drink options that cater to all tastes and budgets.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Berkshire Pork Pie - A traditional local delicacy made with Berkshire pork and seasoned with herbs and spices.
  • Lamb Stew - A hearty and comforting dish made with locally sourced lamb and vegetables.
  • Ploughman’s Lunch - A classic English dish that consists of cheese, bread, and pickles.
  • Cream Tea - A quintessential English treat that consists of scones, clotted cream, and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Newbury and Thatcham have a thriving microbrewery scene, and there are numerous local beers to try.
  • English Wine - The local vineyards produce high-quality English wines that are worth trying.
  • Cider - Cider is a popular local beverage that is often made from locally sourced apples.

Where to Stay in Newbury and Thatcham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you are looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, Newbury and Thatcham have an array of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  • Donnington Grove Hotel - A 4-star hotel that offers luxurious accommodation and stunning views of the golf course.
  • The Vineyard - A 5-star hotel that offers elegant rooms and a Michelin-starred restaurant.
  • YHA Newbury - A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable accommodation.
  • The Carnarvon Arms - A charming guesthouse that offers cosy rooms and a pub serving quality food and drink.

What to Buy in Newbury and Thatcham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home some souvenirs or local products from Newbury and Thatcham, here are some suggestions:

  • English wine - Visit a local vineyard and buy a bottle of high-quality English wine.
  • Local cheeses - Berkshire is known for its delicious cheeses, so be sure to try some and buy some to bring home.
  • Ardley Wood Wicked chutneys and relishes - These locally produced chutneys and relishes are made with high-quality ingredients and are delicious.
  • Antique items from local markets - Newbury has a famous antique market where you can find unique and rare items.

Local Culture and Customs of Newbury and Thatcham

Newbury and Thatcham have a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in their traditions and customs. Here are some interesting facts about the local culture:

  • Newbury is famous for its horse racing, and the town hosts several racing events throughout the year.
  • Thatcham is home to many old buildings and thatched cottages that give the village a charming and traditional character.
  • The local food scene heavily relies on locally sourced ingredients and traditional recipes.
  • The area is surrounded by vast landscapes of natural beauty, and outdoor activities are highly popular among locals.
  • The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and the area has a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Newbury and Thatcham

The best time to visit Newbury and Thatcham depends on your interests and preferences. The area experiences mild temperatures and rainfall throughout the year, and there are various events and festivals to enjoy. Here are some suggestions:

  • Spring (March to May) - This is a great time to visit for nature lovers, as the wildflowers and blossoms are in full bloom.
  • Summer (June to August) - The weather is warm and pleasant, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities and festivals.
  • Autumn (September to November) - The countryside is painted with stunning autumn colours, and there are various harvest events to enjoy.
  • Winter (December to February) - The area is quiet and peaceful during the winter months, and there are various Christmas events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newbury and Thatcham

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Newbury and Thatcham:

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and enjoy the stunning landscapes.
  • Visit the local museums and historical sites to learn about the area’s rich history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Newbury and Thatcham are generally safe places to visit, but it is important to take precautions.

Safety in Newbury and Thatcham

  • Take care when hiking or biking in the countryside, as trails may be uneven or steep.
  • Be aware of your surroundings in public places and avoid dark or isolated areas at night.
  • Beware of scams or pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Newbury and Thatcham

  • The area has good medical facilities, but it is recommended to carry travel insurance in case of medical emergencies.
  • If you have allergies, inform restaurants of your dietary requirements.
  • Tap water is safe for consumption.

Newbury and Thatcham are best explored on foot, bike, or car. The area has excellent public transportation options, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Newbury and Thatcham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion
