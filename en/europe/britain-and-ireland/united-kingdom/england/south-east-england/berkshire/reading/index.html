Introduction to Reading

Located in South East England, Reading is one of the most vibrant towns in the region of Berkshire. Known for its beautiful surroundings, rich culture, and historical significance, Reading has become a popular tourist destination over the years. This comprehensive travel guide provides you with information on the best places to visit, activities to do, food to eat, and cultural experiences to have while exploring this mesmerizing town.

List of Places to Visit in Reading

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Reading has a rich history and is home to several museums and historical sites that offer visitors a deep insight into the town’s past. These attractions include:

  • Reading Museum: This museum, located in the town’s center, showcases local history, art, and archeological findings.

  • Museum of English Rural Life: This museum displays the history of rural life in England and is located within the University of Reading.

  • Reading Abbey Ruins: Once a royal monastery, the Ruins of Reading Abbey are now a historical site that tourists can visit to witness the town’s ruined glory

Natural Attractions of Reading: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Reading is surrounded by natural beauty, and tourists can indulge in several outdoor activities while taking in the picturesque views of the town. The natural attractions worth exploring include:

  • Thames Path: This 184-mile path follows the River Thames and passes through the heart of Reading. The path boasts of breathtaking views and is perfect for hikers and bikers.

  • Hosehill Lake Nature Reserve: This reserve offers fantastic views of the lake, and visitors can indulge in birdwatching, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Beale Park: One of the top family-friendly attractions in Reading, Beale Park is a wildlife park that boasts of a miniature train ride, a water play area, and a petting zoo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reading

Reading boasts of several picturesque trails for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The popular hiking/biking routes in Reading include:

  • Kennet and Avon Canal: This 87-mile long trail from Bristol to Reading is perfect for walkers and bikers. The route takes you through beautiful countryside and historic landmarks.

  • The Ridgeway Trail: Spanning across 87 miles, this route is Britain’s oldest road. The route offers stunning landscapes and historical monuments along the way.

  • The Thames Path: This 184-mile trail passes through Reading and offers great views of the River Thames while walking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Reading: Activities and Tours

Reading has several activities and tours that provide visitors with a unique perspective of the town life. Some must-do activities include:

  • Punting on the River Thames: An excellent way to explore the Thames is by punting. Punting is an activity that tourists can indulge in while taking in views of the town from the river.

  • Reading Arts Centre: Visitors can watch a play or catch a live performance at the Reading Arts Centre. This center also provides workshops for artists and performers.

  • Escape rooms: Reading has several escape rooms that offer engaging team building activities for groups or individuals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Reading

Here is an itinerary that can help you plan a week-long trip to Reading:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Reading Museum and The Ure Museum of Greek Archaeology
Day 2 Explore the Thames Path and take a Punt boat tour
Day 3 Visit Reading Abbey ruins and take a historical tour of the town
Day 4 Go cycling on The Ridgeway Trail
Day 5 Visit Beale Park for a family day out
Day 6 Take a trip to the Museum of English Rural Life
Day 7 End the trip with a live performance at the Reading Arts Centre

Food and Drink Scene of Reading

Reading has a diverse food and drink scene, with options ranging from traditional English dishes to international cuisines. Some must-try food and beverages in Reading include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reading Pudding: A traditional English dish made from suet, flour, sugar, and currants.

  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A dessert made with dates, moist sponge cake, and covered in hot toffee sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Palmers Ale: A beer brewed locally with a unique flavor and texture.

  • Maiden’s Kiss: A cocktail invented in Reading, made from gin, red wine, and ginger ale.

Where to Stay in Reading: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Reading has several options for tourists looking for accommodation for their stay. These options include:

  • Malmaison Reading: A luxury hotel located next to Reading Station.

  • Ibis Reading Centre: A budget hotel option located in the heart of the town.

  • The Forbury Roseate: A grand hotel situated in the town center that boasts of an award-winning restaurant.

What to Buy in Reading: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Reading has several stores for tourists to buy souvenirs and gifts for their loved ones. Some popular local products include:

  • Reading Rock: A candy made locally with a hard exterior and chewy interior.

  • The Oracle Shopping Mall: A shopping mall in Reading that offers a wide range of shops and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Reading

Reading has a relaxed and welcoming culture, and it’s common courtesy for tourists to be polite and respect the locals. The English language is the official language in Reading, and British etiquette and manners are common practice.

Best Time to Visit Reading

The best time to visit Reading is during the summer months between June to August when the temperatures range between 20-25°C. The warm weather is perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the town’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reading

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their way of life.

  • Learn about the town’s cultural past.

  • Explore Reading’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.

  • Get drunk in public spaces.

  • Disrespect the religious or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reading

Reading is a safe town, and tourists can explore freely, but they should take necessary precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Take special care while using public transport and avoid carrying too much cash or valuables on you.

Health in Reading

Reading has a high-quality healthcare system and several hospitals and clinics. It’s always wise to invest in travel insurance before visiting the town, so you have access to medical services in case of an emergency.

Getting to Reading: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Reading

  • Heathrow Airport: The closest airport to Reading, located about 45-minutes’ drive away.

  • Gatwick Airport: 1 hour and 20-minutes’ drive from Reading.

Bus Stations Near Reading

  • Reading Station: The main bus station in Reading, located in the town center.

Other Options

  • Train: Reading has a train station that offers frequent train services to several towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Reading

Reading has a well-connected public transport system, which includes buses, trains, and bikes. The town is also small enough to explore on foot, making it a great way to experience the local vibe while walking from one attraction to another.

Where to Go Next from Reading: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Reading is located within easy reach of several towns and cities that are worth exploring. Some of the popular nearby destinations include:

  • Oxford: Located 30-minutes’ drive from Reading, Oxford has several colleges and historic buildings that are worth looking to explore.

  • Bracknell: 25-minutes’ drive from Reading, Bracknell is a small town known for its leisure facilities and green spaces.

  • Basingstoke: Located about 45-minutes’ drive from Reading, Basingstoke has several attractions, including the Milestones Museum and Basing House ruins.

Conclusion

Reading is a beautiful town located in the heart of Berkshire, England, with a rich culture and history. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, culture lover, or a history buff, reading has something to offer everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to explore Reading and make your trip memorable.
