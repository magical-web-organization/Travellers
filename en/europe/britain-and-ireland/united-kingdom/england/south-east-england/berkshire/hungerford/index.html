Introduction to Hungerford

Hungerford is a charming market town located in the rural county of Berkshire, South East England. Known for its winding streets, historic buildings, and quaint shops and eateries, Hungerford offers a delightful and peaceful escape from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Hungerford

When you visit Hungerford, be sure to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Hungerford Museum: This small museum provides an insight into the local history of the town. It houses a collection of agricultural tools, local crafts, and photographs of the town dating back to the early 19th century.
  • The Hungerford Arcade: This antique shopping arcade is a must-visit destination for lovers of antique and vintage goods. You’ll find everything from vintage clothing to antique furniture in this one-of-a-kind shopping experience.
  • St Lawrence Church: This 14th-century church is worth a visit for its beautiful architecture, ornate stained glass windows, and peaceful atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Hungerford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kennet & Avon Canal: Walking or cycling along this picturesque canal is a peaceful way to take in the beautiful countryside surroundings of Hungerford. You can also go fishing or take a boat trip along the canal.
  • Freeman’s Marsh: A wetland nature reserve with a network of trails, Freeman’s Marsh is a perfect place for birdwatching, picnics, and nature walks.
  • The Greenham Common Control Tower: A short drive from Hungerford, the Greenham Common Control Tower is a historic site that offers fantastic views over the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hungerford

Hungerford offers several excellent hiking and biking routes, including:

  • The Kennet and Avon Canal towpath: A peaceful and scenic route perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk.
  • The North Wessex Downs Way: A long-distance walking trail that takes you through the beautiful hills and downland of Berkshire and Wiltshire.
  • The Hungerford Hares: A popular local running group that organizes weekly runs for members. Join them and explore the beautiful countryside surroundings of Hungerford.

Best Things to Do in Hungerford: Activities and Tours

Here are a few activities and tours you can enjoy in Hungerford:

  • Visit the Farmers’ Market: Held every Wednesday in the Market Place, the Hungerford Farmer’s Market is an excellent opportunity to sample and buy local produce.
  • Hungerford Town Trail: Take a self-guided tour around Hungerford’s winding streets with the help of the Town Trail leaflet, available at the Tourist Information Centre.
  • The Crofton Beam Engines: A short drive from Hungerford, the Crofton Beam Engines offer a fascinating glimpse into the Victorian era of steam power.

Food and Drink Scene of Hungerford

Hungerford offers a diverse food and drink scene, featuring traditional English cuisine alongside a range of international options. Here are a few highlights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hungerford Hams: Locally produced hams that have been enjoyed in the town for centuries.
  • Lamb Koftas: Delicious spiced meatballs made with lamb mince.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A classic British dessert made with moist sponge cake and toffee sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Hungerford Spring Water: Bottled locally, this water is renowned for its purity and refreshing taste.
  • Hungerford Ale: Brewed locally, this ale is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.
  • English Breakfast Tea: No trip to England is complete without sampling this classic tea blend.

Where to Stay in Hungerford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hungerford offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The Bear Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Hungerford, the Bear Hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.
  • The Three Swans Hotel: This Grade II listed hotel offers elegant rooms and a warm, friendly atmosphere.
  • The Crown and Garter: This rustic farmhouse-style inn is located in the nearby village of Inkpen and offers cozy rooms and excellent pub food.

What to Buy in Hungerford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Hungerford, be sure to pick up some of these local products to take home as souvenirs:

  • Antique and Vintage Goods: Hungerford is famous for its antique and vintage shops where you can find unique and unusual items.
  • Hungerford Hams: Take a taste of the town home with locally produced hams.
  • Local Artwork: Visit local galleries and studios to buy beautiful artwork made by artists in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Hungerford

  • The Hocktide Festival: Held annually on the second Tuesday after Easter, this festival has been celebrated in Hungerford for over 600 years and involves parades, maypole dancing, and other festivities.
  • The John O’ Gaunt Inn: This historic pub is located in the center of Hungerford and is famous for its connections to the 14th century knight, John O’ Gaunt.

Best Time to Visit Hungerford

The best time to visit Hungerford is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and dry, and the town comes to life with a variety of local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hungerford

Do:

  • Enjoy the Countryside: Take advantage of the town’s beautiful surroundings and go for a walk, hike, or bike ride.
  • Try Local Produce: Hungerford’s food and drink scene is diverse and unique, with plenty of local specialties to try.
  • Learn About the Local History: Hungerford has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to visit.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the town tidy and dispose of your rubbish responsibly.
  • Be Rude: The locals are friendly and welcoming, so be polite and respectful in return.
  • Ignore Safety Guidelines: Stay safe by following all safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hungerford

Hungerford is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions to stay safe. Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings when out and about.

Health in Hungerford

Hungerford is a healthy town, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to keep you active and fit. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of tick bites in rural areas and take precautions to avoid them.

Getting to Hungerford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hungerford is easily reachable by several modes of transportation:

  • Train: Hungerford has its railway station, serviced by Great Western Railway trains from London Paddington and Reading.
  • Bus: Bus services from Newbury and Marlborough, as well as National Express coaches, stop in Hungerford.
  • Car: Hungerford is located just off the M4 motorway, making it easily accessible by car.

Airports Near Hungerford

  • Heathrow Airport: Just 50 miles from Hungerford, Heathrow is the closest airport with international connections.

Bus Stations Near Hungerford

  • Newbury Bus Station: Located just 10 miles from Hungerford, Newbury Bus Station offers regular services to Hungerford.

Other Options

  • Cycling: Hungerford is a cycling-friendly town, and many visitors choose to explore the surrounding countryside on two wheels.
  • Walking: Hungerford is a small town, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other.

How to Get Around in Hungerford

Hungerford is a small town, and many attractions and shopping areas are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use bicycles, hire cars, or use taxis to get around.

Where to Go Next from Hungerford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re ready to explore beyond Hungerford, the following nearby destinations and excursions are worth a visit:

  • Newbury: Just a short drive from Hungerford, Newbury offers a blend of historic and modern attractions, including the Highclere Castle and The Living Rainforest, a unique botanical garden.
  • Marlborough: This quaint market town is just a 20-minute drive from Hungerford and offers a range of attractions, including the Marlborough College and the Savernake Forest.

Conclusion

Hungerford may be small, but it offers a wealth of attractions, activities, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a lover of history, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, you’re sure to find something to suit your tastes in this charming town.
