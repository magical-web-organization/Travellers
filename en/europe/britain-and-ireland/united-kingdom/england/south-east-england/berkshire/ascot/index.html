Introduction to Ascot

Ascot, a small town in South East England, is renowned for its natural beauty and cultural landmarks. It’s located in Berkshire, a county known for its stunning countryside scenery, and is home to the world-famous Ascot Racecourse. The town has a rich history and a charming village-like atmosphere that captivates visitors.

This guide takes you on a comprehensive tour of Ascot, exploring its must-visit attractions, local culture and customs, and where to stay and eat. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history lover, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Ascot has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ascot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ascot has a wealth of cultural landmarks and museums that recount its history. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Ascot Racecourse: One of the world’s most famous horse-racing tracks, it has hosted racing events since 1711. It’s a must-visit attraction for racing enthusiasts and sports aficionados.
  • St George’s Chapel: A historic chapel located within Windsor Castle. It features magnificent architecture and is the last resting place of several British monarchs.
  • Frogmore House: A royal retreat since Queen Charlotte’s time, with a fascinating history and gardens. Most tours include a stop at St George’s Chapel and Windsor Castle too.
  • Royal Ascot Golf Course: A beautiful 18-hole golf course located adjacent to the Ascot Racecourse, with stunning views of Windsor Castle.

Natural Attractions of Ascot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ascending the heights of Ascot and looking out at the countryside will take your breath away. Be sure to visit these top spots for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Swinley Forest: A 2,600-acre forest featuring winding trails, perfect for hiking and biking. It’s a great place to spot wildlife and is home to rare species such as the British goshawk.
  • Clickem Inn: An idyllic wooded valley with a charming pub overlooking a trout stream. The perfect spot for a relaxing picnic or pint with friends and family.
  • Virginia Water Lake: The largest man-made lake in the Royal Park, lined with several wooden sculptures and ruins, beautiful rhododendron bushes and a stunning sight of the grand statue Leptis Magna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ascot

Ascot is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several trails winding through its undulating landscape. Here are the top hiking and biking routes:

  • The Jubilee Long Walk: A partially-paved walking trail that winds through the Royal Parks. It’s a great way to see the grand architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • Queen Anne’s Ride: A cycling route that begins at the Windsor Castle and Ascot Racecourse and passes through the Great Park of Windsor.
  • Beggar’s Bush: A 4 mile circular route challenging walk known as Beggar’s Bush which features varied views of Ascot and Windsor Great Park, including a 200-year-old oak tree.

Best Things to Do in Ascot: Activities and Tours

Ascot offers a range of activities and tours for visitors, whether you’re interested in history, sports, or nature. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Horse Racing at Ascot Racecourse: A must-attend event in Ascot, where you can experience the thrill of horse racing and enjoy the luxurious ambience.
  • Windsor Castle Tour: A visit to the stunning Windsor Castle and St George’s Chapel is an essential part of any trip to Ascot.
  • Biking in Swinley Forest: Enjoy the scenic beauty of Swinley Forest, biking through its winding trails and spotting wildlife.
  • Polo Matches at Guards Polo Club: Guard Polo Club, located in Windsor Great Park, offers an authentic polo experience and hosts events throughout the summer season.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ascot

If you’re planning to spend a week in Ascot, here’s a recommended itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Ascot Racecourse and explore the town center.
  • Day 2: Take a tour of Windsor Castle and St George’s Chapel.
  • Day 3: Hike or bike through Swinley Forest, and stop at the charming Clickem Inn for lunch.
  • Day 4: Explore Virginia Water Lake and the surrounding gardens.
  • Day 5: Attend a polo match at Guards Polo Club.
  • Day 6: Visit Frogmore House and ascend to the heights of Ascot for stunning views of the countryside.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day in the town center, sampling the local cuisine and buying souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Ascot

Ascot offers a range of culinary delights, from traditional pubs to high-end restaurants. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast Beef and Yorkshire Pudding: A classic British dish, served with roast potatoes, vegetables, and gravy.
  • Fish and Chips: Another British staple, consisting of battered fish and chips, served with tartar sauce and mushy peas.
  • Afternoon Tea: A quintessential British tradition, consisting of tea, finger sandwiches, scones, and cakes.

Local Beverages

  • Windsor Knot: A premium ale brewed by Windsor and Eton Brewery in Windsor, with hints of honey and malt.
  • Martini Royale: A popular cocktail served at the Ascot Racecourse, made with gin and Italian vermouth.

Where to Stay in Ascot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ascot has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best options:

  • Royal Berkshire Hotel: A luxurious five-star hotel located just a stone’s throw from Ascot Racecourse, boasting elegant decor, and exceptional cuisines.
  • Coworth Park: A beautiful five-star country house hotel surrounded by 240 acres of parkland, perfect for a relaxing getaway. It provides spa facilities, fitness activities and great food options.
  • Lyndricks House: A charming guesthouse featuring cozy rooms and a home-like atmosphere, located in a quiet residential area of Ascot.
  • Travelodge: A budget option for travelers, providing comfortable rooms, located just outside the town center.

What to Buy in Ascot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ascot offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Horse racing memorabilia: An essential item to bring back from Ascot, horse racing memorabilia varies from a small pin to large statues.
  • Windsor Knot beer: A perfect gift for craft beer enthusiasts, Windsor Knot Ale is brewed locally and is made in small batches.
  • English Tea: English tea is a staple product of Britain and what better way to bring it home straight from the land of tea and enjoy that comforting flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Ascot

Like many English towns, Ascot has its own unique culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Tipping in restaurants is customary – typically 10-15% of the total bill.
  • When driving in the countryside, be mindful of narrow roads and watch out for livestock and wildlife.
  • Dressing up for events, specifically for visiting the racecourse, is common cultural practice in Ascot.

Best Time to Visit Ascot

The best time to visit Ascot is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and numerous outdoor festivals and events are hosted. The field views and greenery are at their best in the beautiful countryside.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ascot

Do:

  • Take a guided tour: Ascot has a rich history, and a guided tour will provide an in-depth understanding of the locality’s culture.
  • Attend a horse racing event: Ascot is well-known for its horse racing events, and attending one is a must-try experience.
  • Try the local cuisine: The town has a vast range of food offerings, and one should explore the range of local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Drink excessively: Alcohol consumption is part of social culture but during race events, guests should be mindful of when and what they are drinking.
  • Smoke in areas near the racecourse: Smoking is prohibited in certain areas of the racecourse; visitors must smoke in designated areas.
  • Insult or degrade local culture: Ascot has its unique heritage and culture, visitors must respect and admire them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ascot

Ascot is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates. However, as with any destination, visitors should take certain precautions to ensure their safety.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings: Be cautious of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas.
  • Be mindful when driving in the countryside: There are many narrow roads with limited visibility, and caution should be exercised.
  • Dress for the weather: During summers do wear sunscreen and hats.

Health in Ascot

Ascot has excellent medical facilities, and visitors shouldn’t have any difficulty finding quality healthcare if required. Be sure to carry appropriate medications, if required, to stay healthy.

Getting to Ascot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ascot is conveniently located near several transport hubs, making it easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Ascot

  • Heathrow Airport: The nearest airport, located approximately 16 miles from Ascot. It’s one of the world’s busiest airports, with direct connections to several international destinations.
  • Gatwick Airport: Located approximately 45 miles from Ascot, it’s another busy airport with direct connections to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Ascot

  • Ascot Bus Station: Located in the town center and has frequent buses to neighboring towns and cities. Includes regional bus service routes.
  • Windsor Bus Station: Located in nearby Windsor, it has connections to several local and national destinations.

Other Options

  • Rail: Ascot Railway Station provides regular connections to London and surrounding towns and cities.
  • Taxi: Taxis and private cars are readily available for hire, and it’s a convenient way to travel short distances.

How to Get Around in Ascot

Ascot is a compact town, and walking is the most convenient way to explore the town center. For trips to nearby attractions, visitors can use local buses, taxis, rental cars, or bikes. It’s advisable to carry a map or use GPS to navigate the roads better.

Where to Go Next from Ascot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ascot is located in the heart of the South East England countryside, with several popular destinations just a short distance away. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Windsor: Home to the stunning Windsor Castle, a must-visit destination for history lovers.
  • London: The capital city of England, with iconic landmarks such as the Big Ben, London Eye, and Buckingham Palace.
  • Stonehenge: An ancient monument located in Salisbury, famous for its mysterious history and visitors from around the world.
  • Oxford: renowned for being home to one of the oldest universities in the world with historic colleges.

Conclusion

Ascot is a beautiful destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in history, sports, nature, or simply a relaxing getaway, Ascot has something to offer. From horse racing to hiking, biking and golf, visitors will find something for everyone. So, come and explore this captivating town in South East England!
