Introduction to Wallingford

Located in Oxfordshire, in the South East region of England, Wallingford is a charming market town brimming with culture and history. From museums, galleries, and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Wallingford has something for everyone. Whether you are a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, Wallingford is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or longer. In this travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Wallingford.

List of Places to Visit in Wallingford

Wallingford is replete with attractions of all sorts, from historical sites to cultural gems and natural retreats. Here’s our list of must-visit places in Wallingford.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wallingford Museum: The museum offers an in-depth look into Wallingford’s rich history, from the Iron Age to the present day.

  • Cholsey and Wallingford Railway: Take a trip back in time and ride a steam train from Cholsey Station, through the idyllic countryside of South Oxfordshire, and onto Wallingford Station.

  • Wallingford Castle: Explore the remains of the 11th-century castle, including the earthworks, ramparts, and the tower.

  • Wallingford Bridge: Admire the c.1770 stone bridge over the River Thames, which is still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Wallingford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wallingford Castle Gardens: The gardens offer stunning views of the castle ruins, and the River Thames.

  • Benson Lock and Weir: Watch boats come and go, and relax for a while by the River Thames.

  • Warburg Nature Reserve: Hike through the largest area of wildflower meadows in the Chilterns, and enjoy incredible views of the Oxfordshire countryside.

  • Wittenham Clumps: This nature reserve offers breathtaking views of the Thames valley, with many walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wallingford

Wallingford offers a variety of scenic trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Thames Path National Trail: This trail follows the River Thames for 184 miles from its source in the Cotswolds to the Thames Barrier.

  • The Chilterns Cycleway: This route covers 170 miles of beautiful English countryside, taking in the scenic Chiltern Hills and charming, traditional villages.

  • The Ridgeway National Trail: This trail offers 87 miles of stunning landscape, starting from Ivinghoe Beacon and ending at Avebury Stone Circle.

Best Things to Do in Wallingford: Activities and Tours

  • Chiltern Valley Winery and Brewery Tour: Taste locally-produced fine wines and real ales, and discover the secrets of traditional brewing methods with a guided tour of the winery and brewery.

  • Wallingford BunkFest: A popular event held annually in Wallingford’s Kinecroft Park, this folk festival showcases live music, dance, and traditional Morris dancing.

  • Wallingford Castle and Garden Walk: Take a guided tour through the castle and gardens, and learn about Wallingford’s history and heritage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wallingford

Day 1: Arrive in Wallingford, and check in at your hotel.

Day 2: Visit the Wallingford Museum, and walk around Wallingford Castle and Gardens.

Day 3: Explore the Cholsey and Wallingford Railway, and take a trip on a steam train.

Day 4: Hike through the Warburg Nature Reserve, and admire the Oxfordshire countryside.

Day 5: Take the Chiltern Valley Winery and Brewery Tour, and taste locally-produced fine wines and real ales.

Day 6: Go cycling along the Ridgeway National Trail, and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Day 7: Participate in the Wallingford BunkFest, and experience live music, dancing, and traditional Morris dancing.

Food and Drink Scene of Wallingford

Wallingford is famous for its delicious local dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oxfordshire Lamb: A flavorful and juicy dish made with locally-sourced lamb, served with seasonal vegetables.

  • Crusty Bread: Made with traditional bread-making methods, Wallingford’s crusty bread is a staple of the local cuisine.

  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A scrumptious dessert made with dates and served with toffee sauce and vanilla ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Wallingford Brewery ales: Try locally-brewed beers and ales, including the multi-award-winning Beechwood Bitter.

  • Spirits from a local distillery: Sip on unique spirits made by a local distillery, such as gin distilled with locally-sourced botanicals.

Where to Stay in Wallingford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wallingford offers accommodation options for all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to cozy B&Bs. Here are some top-rated places to stay in Wallingford:

  • The George Hotel: A historic coaching inn dating back to the 16th century, offering comfortable and stylish rooms.

  • The Town Arms: A charming pub with a collection of seven cozy guest rooms, serving delicious local dishes and beverages.

  • Little Gables Bed and Breakfast: A lovely B&B with beautifully-decorated rooms, and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Wallingford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs or locally-made products to take back home? Here are some options:

  • Trinket box from Wallingford Pottery: Hand-crafted by a local potter, these trinket boxes are a beautiful and unique souvenir.

  • Local honey from the Oxfordshire Honey Company: Try delicious, high-quality honey made by a local beekeeper.

  • Artwork from local galleries: Browse local galleries for a piece of artwork that reminds you of your trip to Wallingford.

Local Culture and Customs of Wallingford

Wallingford has a rich culture and heritage, influenced by the Roman, Anglo-Saxon and medieval periods. The town is famous for its festivals, traditional music, and Morris dancing. The locals are welcoming, proud of their heritage, and happy to share stories and traditions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Wallingford

The best time to visit Wallingford is from May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Keep in mind that many attractions and events close during the winter months and that Wallingford can get crowded during peak season. If you prefer a quieter experience, visit during the shoulder season in April and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wallingford

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages: Don’t miss out on the opportunity to try delicious locally-made dishes and drinks.

  • Respect local traditions and customs: Wallingford is proud of its rich culture and heritage, and respectful visitors are always welcome.

  • Explore the countryside: Wallingford is surrounded by the stunning Oxfordshire countryside, which is perfect for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas: Be respectful of the environment and dispose of your waste properly.

  • Be noisy and disruptive: Wallingford is a peaceful and quiet town, so avoid loud behavior that disturbs the peace.

  • Take photographs without permission: Be respectful of people’s privacy, and ask for permission before taking photographs of private property or people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wallingford

Wallingford is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings, locking your doors and windows at night, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Wallingford

Wallingford has several medical centers, pharmacies, and hospitals, providing excellent medical care to locals and visitors. If you plan to stay in Wallingford for an extended period, consider purchasing travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and accidents.

Getting to Wallingford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wallingford is accessible by car, train, or bus. Here are the most convenient transportation options:

Airports Near Wallingford

  • Heathrow Airport (LHR): 30 miles away from Wallingford.

  • Gatwick Airport (LGW): 55 miles away from Wallingford.

Bus Stations Near Wallingford

  • Wallingford Bus Station: Located on High Street, providing services to Oxford, Reading, and Didcot.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is Cholsey Station, which is 2 miles away from Wallingford. Cholsey Station has services to London and Reading.

  • Car: Wallingford is easily accessible by car, and there are several parking areas in town.

How to Get Around in Wallingford

Wallingford is a small town, and most of the attractions and landmarks are within walking distance. However, if you prefer, you can also rent a bike or take a taxi to visit sites that are farther away.

Where to Go Next from Wallingford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wallingford is located in the heart of Oxfordshire, making it an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Oxford: Explore the famous university town, known for its historic buildings, museums, and beautiful gardens.

  • Henley-on-Thames: Visit this charming riverside town, famous for its elegant Regatta and the River and Rowing Museum.

  • Reading: Experience the vibrant urban life of Reading, with its excellent shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Wallingford is a hidden gem in the heart of Oxfordshire, offering a variety of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions. From exploring the town’s rich history and heritage to hiking or biking through the stunning countryside, Wallingford has something for everyone. Sample delicious local dishes and beverages, stay in cozy accommodations, and enjoy the friendly and welcoming atmosphere of Wallingford. We hope that this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Wallingford, and that you will have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful town.
