Introduction to Wantage

Located in Oxfordshire, Wantage is a historic market town renowned for its outdoor adventures, scenic views, cultural attractions, and local customs. This vibrant and spirited town is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Wantage

If you’re looking for things to do in Wantage, here’s a list of places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vale and Downland Museum: The museum boasts several galleries that exhibit the history and culture of Wantage, from the Roman era to the present day.
  • Betjeman Statue and Memorial Garden: This stunning memorial garden and statue of famous poet Sir John Betjeman commemorate his love for Wantage.
  • King Alfred’s Statue: This iconic monument in Wantage’s Market Place honors King Alfred the Great, the town’s historical patron.

Natural Attractions of Wantage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ridgeway: As an ancient trackway that passes through Wantage, the Ridgeway offers tremendous views and is ideal for hiking and biking.
  • White Horse Hill: Home to the famous chalk-cut white horse and the Bronze Age Uffington Castle, this 280-foot-high landmark offers breathtaking views of the countryside.
  • Letcombe Brook Project: This conservation project aims to protect the brook’s environment and encourage visitors to enjoy the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wantage

Wantage has several hiking and biking routes, including:

  • The Ridgeway National Trail: This ancient trail connects Wantage to Avebury and stretches 87 miles.
  • The Letcombe Valley: This route is ideal for those seeking a peaceful and gentle walk.
  • The White Horse Trail: The trail passes through the famous White Horse Hill and offers stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Wantage: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours in Wantage:

  • Wantage Walking Tours: Experience Wantage’s history and culture by taking one of the town’s guided walking tours.
  • Horse-Riding: Enjoy beautiful landscapes and explore Wantage’s countryside on horseback.
  • Boswell’s Coach House: This hidden gem is home to a range of crafts and antiques and hosts a variety of events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wantage

Here’s a week-long itinerary for your Wantage adventure:

Day 1: Explore Wantage’s museums and historical landmarks.

Day 2: Hike the Ridgeway National Trail.

Day 3: Visit White Horse Hill and Uffington Castle.

Day 4: Horse-ride in the Letcombe Valley.

Day 5: Visit Letcombe Brook Project and explore the town’s local shops and markets.

Day 6: Try local cuisine and drinks at Wantage’s pubs and restaurants.

Day 7: Take a walking tour of Wantage and learn about its culture and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Wantage

Wantage has a range of delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef and Ale Pie: This traditional English dish is a must-try when in Wantage.
  • Plum Pudding: A rich, fruity dessert that’s a holiday classic in Wantage.

Local Beverages

  • Wantage Brewery: This award-winning microbrewery specializes in creating unique beers.
  • Kingswell Gin: This locally made gin is distilled in a 300-year-old barn in nearby Faringdon.

Where to Stay in Wantage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wantage has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets:

  • The Bear Hotel: A historic coaching inn that has been operating since the 16th century.
  • The Star Inn: A cozy, independently-owned inn located in the heart of Wantage.
  • Airbnb: There are several Airbnb listings in Wantage that offer unique and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Wantage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Wantage:

  • Betjeman Ale: Brewed in honor of Sir John Betjeman, this beer is available at Wantage Brewery.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Wantage’s local potters create beautiful and unique pottery pieces.
  • Local Honey: Made from the nectar of wildflowers in Wantage’s countryside, this honey is a favorite among locals and visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Wantage

Wantage has several customs and traditions unique to the town, such as the annual Betjeman Poetry Competition and the Wantage Summer Festival. The town is steeped in history and has a deep appreciation for its cultural and local heritage.

Best Time to Visit Wantage

Wantage can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wantage

Here are some do’s and don’ts for your visit to Wantage:

Do:

  • Learn about Wantage’s history and local culture by taking a walking tour.
  • Try Wantage’s local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect Wantage’s historic and cultural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage Wantage’s natural environments.
  • Disrupt local customs and traditions.
  • Overlook the town’s rich history and culture.

Safety and Health

Wantage is a safe and healthy town, but it’s always crucial to be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Wantage

  • Always carry a map and phone in case of an emergency.
  • Avoid wandering alone at night in remote areas.

Health in Wantage

  • Wantage has several medical centers available, including the Wantage Health Centre and Grove Surgery.
  • Bring a first-aid kit before you go on outdoor adventures.

Getting to Wantage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options and logistics:

  • Airports Near Wantage: Heathrow (60 miles), Bristol (65 miles), and Birmingham (80 miles) are the nearest airports.
  • Bus Stations Near Wantage: The Wantage Town Centre Bus Stop offers bus services to and from surrounding towns and cities.
  • Other Options: Taking a train to Didcot Parkway or Oxford and then taking a local bus is another option.

How to Get Around in Wantage

Wantage is a compact town, and you can easily get around by foot. Cycling and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Wantage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Wantage:

  • Oxford: A historic and vibrant university town famous for its stunning architecture and prestigious university.
  • Stonehenge: An ancient and mysterious circle of standing stones, located about 30 miles from Wantage.
  • Bath: A UNESCO World Heritage site that offers stunning architecture and beautiful countryside walks.

Conclusion

Wantage is a unique and beautiful market town that has something for everyone. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, Wantage is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
