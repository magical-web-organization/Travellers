A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Bicester, UK

Are you planning to visit the quaint town of Bicester in South East England? If yes, then you have made a superb choice! In this ultimate travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the top places to visit, the best food to try, and cultural insights that make Bicester stand out. So, let’s dive right in!

List of Places to Visit in Bicester

Bicester may be small, but it is packed with a plethora of exciting attractions that cater to various interests. Here are the must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bicester Heritage - This motor museum is a hub for vintage cars, RAF vehicles, motorbikes, and planes from the past century. It is a must-visit place for automobile enthusiasts.

  2. The Garth Park - This Georgian mansion is renowned for its picturesque architecture and has been a residence of several noble bloodlines, including the Duke of Marlborough. It has now been converted into a museum where visitors can explore the grandeur of the British Aristocracy.

  3. St. Edburg’s Church - This historic Anglican church dates back to the 12th century and features awe-inspiring architecture and an episcopal palace.

Natural Attractions of Bicester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bicester Wetlands - This natural reserve is home to several species, including otters, birds, and fish. You can take a walk or bike ride around the area, enjoy the serene ambiance, and spot wildlife.

  2. Piddington Woods - If you’re a nature lover, then you’ll appreciate this hidden gem located just outside Bicester. It is an excellent place for hiking and picnicking in the fresh air.

  3. Bicester Town Center - Although it’s not a natural attraction, the plethora of shopping options and bustling energy of the picturesque town center more than make up for it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bicester

Bicester is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It features several hiking and biking routes that cater to all skill levels. Some of the popular walking and cycling paths include:

  1. Oxford Canal Towpath - From Thrupp to Aynho, cyclists can explore the beautiful canal route while enjoying the serenity and stunning views that surround them.

  2. Bicester Heritage Walks - Bicester Heritage offers fascinating walking routes that explore its rich history and heritage. Visitors can learn about the WWI and WWII efforts while admiring the collection of vintage cars.

Best Things to Do in Bicester: Activities and Tours

Bicester’s diverse variety of attractions provides an extensive range of activities and tours for all interests:

  1. Shopping - Being Europe’s largest luxury shopping outlet, Bicester Village is a must-stop for anyone seeking the best fashion and luxury deals.

  2. The Bicester Hotel, Golf, and Spa - This exceptional resort is an excellent place for golfers and spa enthusiasts.

  3. Bicester Gliding Club - The club offers gliding packages for visitors to admire Bicester’s beauty from the skies with a unique perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bicester

Wondering how you can fit all these exciting attractions into your Bicester vacation? Here’s a week-long itinerary to take the stress off your planning phase:

Food and Drink Scene of Bicester

Bicester is renowned for its rich history, picturesque scenery and vibrant culinary scene. The town boasts a variety of local dishes and beverages for visitors to indulge in during their stay.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Oxfordshire Sausages - A signature dish made of pork sausage filled with herbs and locally sourced meat.

  2. Banbury Cakes - This spiced cake is a local delicacy that has a rich, cocoa flavor and a fruity aroma.

  3. Bangers and Mash - A staple of English cuisine, this dish features sausages, mashed potatoes, vegetables, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  1. Chiltern Brewery Ale - This locally made beer features a refreshing taste with a subtle sweetness and a malty aroma.

  2. Bicester Red Wine - A signature red wine produced in Bicester that has a rich, full-bodied flavor.

Where to Stay in Bicester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bicester has an impressive range of accommodations to cater to all types of travelers, including budget-friendly and lavish options. Some of the popular hotels in the area include:

  1. Bicester Hotel, Golf and Spa - A luxurious option that has incredible spa services and a serene ambiance.

  2. Travelodge Bicester Cherwell Valley - A budget-friendly hotel located in a prime area next to major highways.

What to Buy in Bicester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers who wish to bring home memorabilia of their trip to Bicester can choose from a variety of souvenirs and locally made products. Some of the popular options include:

  1. Banbury Cakes - These locally made cakes make the perfect gift for loved ones.

  2. Craft Items - Bicester is renowned for its unique craft items made of leather, ceramics, and textiles.

  3. Antiques - Bicester has several antique shops where visitors can buy vintage items.

Local Culture and Customs of Bicester

Bicester has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architectural landmarks, culinary scene, and festivals. The town hosts several events throughout the year, including the Bicester Festival, which showcases local entertainers, artists, and live music.

Best Time to Visit Bicester

The best time to visit Bicester is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. During these months, the town is less crowded, making it the ideal destination for travelers seeking solitude.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bicester

Do:

  1. Do try out local cuisine and explore popular cultural landmarks.

  2. Do book your accommodation in advance to avoid last-minute hassles.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage natural attractions.

  2. Don’t ignore pedestrian crossings while driving, as pedestrians have the right of way.

Safety and Health

Bicester is a relatively safe town with little risk of crime or violence. However, visitors should consider taking standard precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Bicester

  1. Always be aware of your surroundings and avoid dark alleys or poorly lit areas.

  2. Keep a watchful eye on personal belongings such as wallets, mobile phones, and laptops.

Health in Bicester

Bicester has a well-established healthcare system, and visitors can seek medical attention at nearby clinics or hospitals.

Getting to Bicester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bicester is well-connected with surrounding towns and cities, making it easily accessible by different modes of transport.

Airports Near Bicester

The nearest airport to Bicester is London Luton, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bicester

Bicester Bus Station is a hub for several local and regional buses, making it an affordable option for visitors to travel around the town.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Bicester using trains from London, Birmingham, and Oxford.

How to Get Around in Bicester

Bicester is a relatively small town, and most attractions are in proximity to each other, making it feasible to explore on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles, take buses or taxis to get around the town easily.

Where to Go Next from Bicester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several other top destinations near Bicester that visitors can explore during their stay:

  1. Oxford - Visit the prestigious University of Oxford and explore the city’s rich cultural heritage.

  2. The Cotswolds - Enjoy panoramic landscapes and picturesque villages located in the heart of England.

Conclusion

Bicester may be a small town, but it packs a punch when it comes to stunning attractions, a vibrant culinary scene, and a rich cultural heritage. With this ultimate guide, you’re ready to take on the tranquil and historical town of Bicester, South East England. Happy exploring!
