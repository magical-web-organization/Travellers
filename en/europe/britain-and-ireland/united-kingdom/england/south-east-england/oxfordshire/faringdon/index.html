Introduction to Faringdon

Located in Oxfordshire, in South East England, Faringdon is a charming market town with a rich history and picturesque countryside. The town is steeped in tradition, with several historical landmarks to explore and magnificent views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can also enjoy outdoor activities, indulge in local cuisine, and discover unique souvenirs to bring back home. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ve put together a list of the best places to visit, things to do, and insider tips to help you plan a memorable trip to Faringdon, England.

List of Places to Visit in Faringdon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Faringdon Folly Tower - A historic tower built in the 1930s that offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Faringdon Museum - A museum that showcases the town’s history, including natural history, archaeology, geology, and historical manuscripts.
  • St. Mary’s Church - An 11th-century church with a beautiful interior and historic graveyard.
  • Faringdon House - A private mansion with extensive gardens and a historic collection of artwork.

Natural Attractions of Faringdon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Ridgeway National Trail - One of England’s oldest and most scenic trails stretching from Overton Hill to Ivinghoe Beacon for 87 miles.
  • Buscot Park - A National Trust property offering exquisite gardens, Art exhibitions, and gorgeous architecture.
  • Badbury Hill and Woods - Ancient woodlands famous for bluebells, wildlife, and scenic picnic spots.
  • Coleshill Estate - A National Trust property famous for its wildlife including bats and owls and a Roman Villa.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Faringdon

Faringdon offers several excellent hiking and biking routes through the countryside, including:

  • The White Horse Trail - A circular trail that passes by the famous Uffington White Horse and other local landmarks.
  • The Thames Path- a National Trail stretching for approximately 184 miles mostly adjacent to the River Thames from its source at Thames Head near Kemble until it merges with the Thames at Teddington Lock.
  • The Cotswold Way - Runs along the Cotswold Edge escarpment of the Cotswolds hills in England. More than twenty-five prime historic monuments and neolithic burial mounds are visible from the local from the preserved landscape.

Best Things to Do in Faringdon: Activities and Tours

  • Go on a hot air balloon ride - Take in the breathtaking views from high above Faringdon and the Thames Valley.
  • Visit Farmer Gow’s Activity Farm where animals and kids can inspire an interest in nature Hop aboard some great tractor rides at Farmers Gow where you can interact with varied farm animals from pigs to ducks and more!
  • Tour a local winery - Discover the best English wines and take a guided tour of a local vineyard.
  • Shop at Folly Antiques - A unique shopping experience where you can find antique treasures and one-of-a-kind items.

Week-Long Itinerary for Faringdon

Day 1: Explore the town and visit St. Mary’s Church.

Day 2: Visit Faringdon Folly Tower and Museum.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the town and surrounding countryside.

Day 4: Visit the nearby Buscot Park and the Coleshill Estate.

Day 5: Explore the nearby Ridgeway National Trail and go on a guided tour of a local winery.

Day 6: Hike the nearby Cotswold Way and visit Folly Antiques.

Day 7: Go on a family adventure at Farmer’s Gow and participate in their great seasonal events.

Food and Drink Scene of Faringdon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sunday Roast - A classic English meal comprising of roasted meat served with vegetables, gravy, and potatoes.
  • Black Pudding - A type of sausage made from pig’s blood and oatmeal.
  • Cheese and Onion Pie - A savory pastry dish filled with cheese and onion.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Faringdon is home to several local breweries, such as the West Berkshire Brewery and White Horse brewery.
  • Cider - A popular local alcoholic beverage made from fermented apple juice.

Where to Stay in Faringdon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Old Crown Coaching Inn - A traditional coaching inn with rooms and acclaimed pub on the high street, dating back to 1540.
  • The Trout Inn - A 17th-century Inn with River views and luxurious rooms.
  • The Bell Inn - A charming guesthouse in the town centre overlooking the Market Square.

What to Buy in Faringdon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antique Treasures at Folly Antiques - Get amazing antique finds like jewellery, paintings, furniture, and other treasures at Folly antiques.
  • Local Produce - Take home fresh local produce such as artisan cheese, cider, honey, and baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Faringdon

Faringdon is a friendly town with a passion for history and local traditions. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs, including dressing modestly while visiting churches and public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Faringdon

The best time to visit Faringdon is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed. Spring and autumn (April to May and September to October) offer cooler temperatures, beautiful colors of spring blossoms and autumn leaves but can be prone to showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Faringdon

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Visit historical landmarks and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural surroundings.
  • Ignore local traffic rules.
  • Take photographs inside churches without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Faringdon

Faringdon is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take appropriate safety precautions and keep an eye on their belongings in busy tourist areas.

Health in Faringdon

The town has a medical center offering general health services along with a pharmacy. In an emergency situation, dial 999 for immediate medical assistance.

Getting to Faringdon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Faringdon

The nearest airports to Faringdon are Heathrow Airport (LHR), Bristol Airport(BRS) and Birmingham Airport (BHX). Each airport is approximately a 90-minute drive or public transport journey.

Bus Stations Near Faringdon

Faringdon is connected to regional and national bus services with the nearest stations being in the center in the Market Square departures.

Other Options

Self drive is a popular option to explore the area with Faringdon accessible from the M4 and M5 motorways. Taxis and bike rentals are readily available for visitors who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in Faringdon

Faringdon is a small town, so visitors can explore most places on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available as alternative options.

Where to Go Next from Faringdon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oxford - An hour bus ride from Faringdon, Oxford is a cultural and academic hub with several historic landmarks and world-class museums.

  • Avebury - A famous stone circle and large henge that is older than Stonehenge and is a UNESCO world heritage site.

  • Cotswolds - A region of picturesque villages and rolling hills famous for its beautiful limestone buildings, market towns, and unique boutique purchases.

Conclusion

Faringdon is a picturesque market town with a rich history and magnificent countryside. Visitors can enjoy exploring the town’s many cultural and natural attractions, participate in outdoor activities, and indulge in local cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to plan an unforgettable trip to Faringdon, England.
