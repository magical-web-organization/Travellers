Abingdon-on-Thames is a historic town nestled in the heart of Oxfordshire, in South East England. Known for its picturesque landscape and rich cultural history, Abingdon-on-Thames is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the English countryside. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through everything you need to know about Abingdon-on-Thames, from interesting places to visit and outdoor adventures to local cuisine and cultural customs.

Introduction to Abingdon-on-Thames

Abingdon-on-Thames traces its roots to the Iron Age era, with extensive Roman and Anglo-Saxon influences. Its medieval architecture, market squares, and winding streets are a testament to its rich cultural heritage. Besides its historical significance, the town boasts of beautiful parks, waterways, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Abingdon-on-Thames

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Abingdon-on-Thames has a rich history that is manifested in its many historical landmarks and museums:

  • Abingdon County Hall Museum: Learn about the town’s 1000 years of history through interactive exhibits that include art, costumes, and archaeological finds.
  • St. Nicolas’ Church: Built in the 13th century, St. Nicolas’ Church is a fine example of the town’s medieval architecture.
  • The Abbey of Abingdon: Founded in 675 AD, the Abbey of Abingdon is definitely worth a visit. Though mostly ruins, the remains of the abbey are now part of an English Heritage site, bursting with history and cultural significance.

Natural Attractions of Abingdon-on-Thames: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Abingdon-on-Thames has a stunning natural landscape perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some places tourists should visit:

  • Thames Path National Trail: The Thames Path National Trail offers some of the most scenic walks in England. The path stretches for 184 miles, from the Cotswolds to the Thames Barrier.
  • Abbey Meadows: Abbey Meadows is a public park with a man-made beach and picnic area, perfect for sunset watching.
  • Rye Farm Meadow: Rye Farm Meadow is a nature reserve that offers excellent bird watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abingdon-on-Thames

Abingdon-on-Thames offers a range of outdoor activities for visitors, including hikes and bike rides. Here are some popular routes for each:

Hiking Routes

  • Thames Path National Trail: Covering 14 miles of the Thames Path National Trail is a sight to behold, as it provides you with magnificent riverside views and the opportunity to explore the nearby charming towns and villages in the area.
  • Abbey to Thrupp Canal Walk: This walk takes you through stunning wildlife and scenic river views, starting at Abingdon and ending in Thrupp.

Biking Routes

  • The Great Western Railway Cycleway: A 20-mile trip will take you through beautiful scenery, crossing the River Thames and passing by many landmarks like the Didcot Railway Centre.
  • The Vale of White Horse Cycle Route: A 27-mile trail through Abingdon and the picturesque villages in the Vale of White Horse area.

Best Things to Do in Abingdon-on-Thames: Activities and Tours

Abingdon-on-Thames offers many unique and engaging activities and tours that visitors can take part in. Here are some of them:

  • River Thames Cruises: Take a relaxing boat ride on the River Thames and enjoy the beautiful views of the countryside.
  • The Devil’s Quoits stone circle: Visit the mysterious Devil’s Quoits stone circle, which is just a short drive away from Abingdon-on-Thames.
  • Mississippi Paddle Steamer Cruise: Enjoy a vintage experience on the Mississippi Paddle Steamer as you paddle down the Thames River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Abingdon-on-Thames

Abingdon-on-Thames has plenty for visitors to enjoy and explore, making it a perfect spot for a week-long holiday. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time in town:

Day Activity
1 Explore Abingdon County Hall Museum and the Abbey of Abingdon
2 Take a riverside walk along the Thames Path then take a River Thames Cruise
3 Enjoy the Vale of White Horse Cycle Route bike ride to some of the nearby breathtaking villages
4 Take a guided tour to Thrupp Canal Walk and enjoy the stunning views on the river

Food and Drink Scene of Abingdon-on-Thames

A trip to Abingdon-on-Thames would not be complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here’s what you need to try and drink while you’re in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Abingdon Bun: A delicious locally made currant bun.
  • Oxfordshire Lamb: A delectable twist on the classic Sunday roast lamb.
  • Abingdon Abbey Ale: A classic taste brewed in the heart of the town.

Local Beverages

  • Morland Old Speckled Hen: A classic local beer, originating from Abingdon-On-Thames since 1979.
  • Fragola Prosecco: A local sparkling wine that can be paired with the local cuisine to get the best taste.

Where to Stay in Abingdon-on-Thames: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Abingdon-on-Thames has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, here are some of the outstanding places to stay:

  • The Brewery Tap- an idyllic 14th-century inn and brewery, home to the Abingdon Bridge Brewery.
  • The Upper Reaches Hotel- a waterfront haven boasting beautiful rooms and excellent restaurant.
  • The Crown and Thistle- an award-winning luxury hotel with ensuite room amenities, including a sink carved from stone and sleekly designed bathrooms.

What to Buy in Abingdon-on-Thames: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Abingdon-on-Thames has a rich cultural heritage reflected in a variety of locally crafted souvenirs and products. Some of the top items to bring back home include:

  • Abingdon Calendar: A perfect way to remember town’s beauty and worth capturing every moment in a year.
  • Abingdon tea towels: Perfectly handcrafted souvenirs that express the beauty of the town in styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Abingdon-on-Thames

The town is proud of its cultural heritage, which it maintains and honors throughout the year. The biggest cultural event in the town is the Abingdon Michaelmas Fair, held every year to mark the Feast of St. Michael, where you can enjoy a wide mix of leisure activities, challenges, and foods. The locals dress traditionally on occasion, mainly wearing antique clothing on festive days.

Best Time to Visit Abingdon-on-Thames

Abingdon-on-Thames is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in every season. The best times to visit are the spring and summer months; April to September, when the weather is warm, and there is less chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abingdon-on-Thames

Do:

  • Do visit the Abingdon County Hall Museum.
  • Do attend the Abingdon Michaelmas Fair.
  • Do try the famous Abingdon Bun.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Abingdon-on-Thames

Compared to other UK towns of the same size, Abingdon-on-Thames has low crime rates. However, it’s always best to take precautions when visiting any city or town.

Health in Abingdon-on-Thames

Medical facilities are modern and readily available in Abingdon-on-Thames. For a non-emergency issue, consider visiting local pharmacy- the chemist.

Getting to Abingdon-on-Thames: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Abingdon-on-Thames, Oxfordshire, is relatively easy, with an extensive transportation network connecting the town to other areas of the UK, including:

Airports Near Abingdon-on-Thames

  • Heathrow Airport: 50 minutes’ drive
  • Gatwick Airport: 1 hour 25 minutes’ drive
  • Birmingham Airport: 1 hour 10 minutes’ drive

Bus Stations Near Abingdon-on-Thames

  • Abingdon Bus Station.

Other Options

  • Train: Oxford and Didcot Parkway railway stations are both within 10 minutes’ drive from Abingdon-on-Thames, providing easy access to London or further afield.
  • Car: Visitors can also travel to Abingdon-on-Thames by car via the M4, A34, and M40 motorways. Parking is available in public carparks throughout the town.

How to Get Around in Abingdon-on-Thames

Abingdon-on-Thames is a compact town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, there are also other alternatives to get around the town, including:

  • Bus: The town is served by several bus services connecting the town to nearby locations.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car from the several affiliated car rental providers at the airports or train stations.
