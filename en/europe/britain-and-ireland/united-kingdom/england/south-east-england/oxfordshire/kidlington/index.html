Introduction to Kidlington

Located in the heart of Oxfordshire, Kidlington is a charming town that perfectly blends history, culture, and nature. Often referred to as the largest village in England, Kidlington has a population of approximately 15,000 people and offers a wide range of attractions and activities that cater to all types of travelers.

From museums and historical sites to scenic vistas and outdoor adventures, Kidlington has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do and see in Kidlington, where to eat and drink, where to stay, how to get around, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Kidlington

Make sure you visit the following tourist destinations in Kidlington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture enthusiasts, Kidlington boasts an impressive array of museums and historical sites that are well worth a visit:

  • St. Mary’s Church: A beautiful and well-preserved medieval church located in the heart of Kidlington’s historic center.
  • Kidlington and District Historical Society: A museum and research center that documents the history of Kidlington and the surrounding area.
  • Langford Locks: A series of locks along the Oxford Canal that provide a glimpse into the area’s industrial past.

Natural Attractions of Kidlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kidlington and its surroundings offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and nature-based tourism:

  • River Cherwell: This picturesque river flows through Kidlington and is a popular spot for fishing, picnicking, and boating.
  • Kirtlington Park: A magnificent park that features 2 lakes, a formal garden, and over 3,000 species of trees and shrubs.
  • Bladon Quarry: An abandoned quarry that has been repurposed as a nature reserve and offers a tranquil hiking experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kidlington

Kidlington and its surrounding area offer plenty of options for hiking and biking enthusiasts:

  • Kirtlington Circular Walk: A scenic 7-mile circular walk that takes you through the villages of Kirtlington and Tackley.
  • Port Meadow: A vast open meadow that offers ample space for cycling, jogging, walking, and picnicking.
  • Oxford Canal Trail: A peaceful and scenic route that follows the historic Oxford Canal and passes through some of Oxfordshire’s prettiest countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kidlington: Activities and Tours

Kidlington has a wide range of activities and tours to choose from, including:

  • Oxford University Tour: Explore the oldest university in the English-speaking world on a guided tour of Oxford’s historic colleges and landmarks.
  • Oxford Castle and Prison Tour: Learn about Oxford’s dark and fascinating past on a guided tour of the city’s castle and prison.
  • Bicester Village Shopping Tour: Indulge in some luxury retail therapy on a shopping tour of Bicester Village, a designer outlet located just a short drive from Kidlington.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kidlington

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kidlington, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Kick-off your trip with a walking tour of Kidlington’s historic center and visit to St. Mary’s Church.
2 Explore Oxford on a guided tour and visit iconic landmarks like the Oxford University Museum of Natural History.
3 Take a leisurely hike or bike ride along the Oxford Canal or Port Meadow.
4 Spend the day visiting nearby attractions like Blenheim Palace or Bicester Village.
5 Discover the local food scene on a culinary tour of Kidlington.
6 Get adventurous with a boating excursion on the River Cherwell or try your hand at fly fishing.
7 Visit Kirtlington Park and enjoy a picnic or leisurely stroll through this stunning park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kidlington

Kidlington is home to a wide variety of eateries, from quaint cafes to upscale restaurants. Be sure to try these must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oxfordshire lamb: A tender and flavorful meat that is often served with a side of roasted vegetables and potatoes.
  • Eton mess: A delightful dessert made with fresh fruit, whipped cream, and crumbled meringue.
  • Black pudding: A traditional English sausage that is made with pork blood, fat, and oatmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: A popular local beer that is brewed in many of the region’s micro-breweries.
  • English Breakfast Tea: A classic British tea that is brewed with a blend of black teas from India and Sri Lanka.
  • Gin & Tonic: A refreshing cocktail that is often made with local artisanal gin.

Where to Stay in Kidlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kidlington offers a variety of lodging options to suit every taste and budget:

  • The Highwayman Hotel: A cozy inn that features comfortable rooms, a popular on-site restaurant, and a friendly staff.
  • Jurys Inn Oxford: A modern and chic hotel that offers stylish rooms, an on-site fitness center, and plenty of amenities.
  • Green and Blue House: A charming guesthouse that offers warm and welcoming accommodations in a quiet and secluded setting.

What to Buy in Kidlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to take home from Kidlington? Check out these local products:

  • Oxfordshire honey: A delicious and fragrant honey that is produced by local beekeepers in the surrounding countryside.
  • Oxford University merchandise: A range of apparel and souvenirs featuring the iconic crest of Oxford University.
  • Oxfordshire cheese: A delicious and savory cheese that is produced by local cheesemakers using milk from local cows.

Local Culture and Customs of Kidlington

Kidlington is a friendly and welcoming community that values tradition, hospitality, and a strong sense of community. As a visitor, you’ll want to respect local customs and traditions and be mindful of local etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Kidlington

The best time to visit Kidlington is during the summer months when temperatures are warm and the days are long. However, Kidlington is a year-round destination, and each season offers its own unique attractions and charms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kidlington

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore Kidlington’s historic center and surrounding countryside.
  • Do try the local cuisine and sample some of the region’s famous ales and wines.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions and show kindness and generosity to local residents.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage local natural areas and landmarks.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places, especially during quiet hours.
  • Don’t bring or carry illegal drugs or substances.

Safety and Health

Kidlington is a safe and friendly destination, but visitors should be aware of potential hazards and take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Kidlington

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or unfamiliar neighborhoods.
  • Keep valuables like passports, cash, and electronics in a secure location.
  • Be aware of traffic laws and road safety, especially if you plan on renting a bike or driving a car.

Health in Kidlington

  • Make sure you have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to Kidlington.
  • Practice good hygiene, including regular hand washing and sanitizing.
  • If you need emergency care or medical attention, go to the nearest hospital or clinic.

Getting to Kidlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kidlington is easily accessible by several modes of transportation:

Airports Near Kidlington

  • Heathrow Airport: Located approximately an hour’s drive from Kidlington.
  • Birmingham Airport: Located approximately an hour and a half’s drive from Kidlington.
  • Luton Airport: Located approximately an hour’s drive from Kidlington.

Bus Stations Near Kidlington

  • Oxford Bus Station: Serves as the primary bus station for Oxfordshire and is located approximately 6 miles south of Kidlington.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A convenient and affordable option for travelers who want to explore Kidlington and the surrounding area independently.
  • Taxi services: Available throughout Kidlington and surrounding towns and villages.

How to Get Around in Kidlington

Kidlington is a small and walkable town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Public transportation options include local buses and taxis, and car rentals are available as well.

Where to Go Next from Kidlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kidlington is located in the heart of Oxfordshire and is surrounded by countless other picturesque towns, villages, and attractions. Some of the most popular nearby destinations include:

  • Oxford: Just 6 miles south of Kidlington, Oxford is one of England’s most historic and culturally-rich cities.
  • Blenheim Palace: A magnificent stately home that is located just over 10 miles north of Kidlington.
  • Cotswold Hills: A range of rolling hills, idyllic villages, and historic landmarks that are located just a short drive from Kidlington.

Conclusion

Kidlington is a charming and vibrant town that offers visitors a wealth of attractions, activities, and amenities. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or outdoor adventure, Kidlington has something for everyone.
