Introduction to Thame

Nestled in the heart of Oxfordshire, Thame is a charming town that dates back to the medieval era. Its picturesque streets are lined with well-preserved historic buildings, shops, pubs, and restaurants, making it a favorite destination for history buffs, foodies, and nature enthusiasts alike. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to do in Thame, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Thame

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Thame Museum - Discover the history and culture of Thame through its exhibits, including artifacts from the town’s Tudor and Victorian eras.
  2. St. Mary’s Church - A beautiful 13th-century church that still holds regular services and has an impressive tower open to the public.
  3. Thame Town Hall - A stunning example of Georgian architecture that has been used as a venue for weddings, concerts, and other events.

Natural Attractions of Thame: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Phoenix Trail - A 7-mile trail that follows the old railway line between Thame and Princes Risborough, offering scenic views of the countryside.
  2. Cuttle Brook Local Nature Reserve - A serene nature reserve with walking paths, a pond, and plenty of birdwatching opportunities.
  3. Thame Park - A beautiful park with a manor house and gardens, dating back to the 17th century.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thame

Thame is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with several scenic hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the most popular:

  1. Phoenix Trail - As mentioned earlier, this 7-mile trail offers a leisurely walk or cycle through beautiful scenery.
  2. Ridgeway National Trail - A 140-mile trail that runs through the Chiltern Hills, just north of Thame. It’s a challenging hike but offers some of the most stunning views in the region.
  3. Chinnor Hill Nature Reserve - This is a great option for families, with a short 2-mile walk through woodland and wildflower meadows.

Best Things to Do in Thame: Activities and Tours

  1. Thame Food Festival - A popular event, held every September, showcasing the best food and drink from Oxfordshire and beyond.
  2. Black Park Country Park - Just a short drive from Thame, this country park offers activities such as Go Ape treetop adventures and boat rental on the lake.
  3. Waddeston Manor - A stunning French-style chateau with beautiful gardens, open to the public for tours and events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thame

Here’s a suggested itinerary for spending a week in Thame:

Day Activity
1 Explore Thame Museum and St. Mary’s Church
2 Visit nearby Black Park Country Park
3 Take a leisurely walk or cycle along the Phoenix Trail
4 Discover the town’s food scene with a culinary tour
5 Visit the Chinnor Hill Nature Reserve
6 Tour Waddeston Manor and its gardens
7 Relax and enjoy the town’s quaint pubs and shops

Food and Drink Scene of Thame

Must-Try Dishes

  1. English breakfast - A hearty and filling meal featuring bacon, eggs, beans, grilled tomatoes, and more.
  2. Fish and chips - A classic British dish, consisting of deep-fried battered fish and chips, served with mushy peas and tartar sauce.
  3. Bread and butter pudding - A delicious dessert made from leftover bread, raisins, custard, and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Real Ale - Thame is home to several pubs serving locally brewed ale, such as XT Brewery.
  2. Gin - Oxfordshire is known for its artisanal gin production, and Thame is no exception. Try a locally made gin and tonic at one of the town’s trendy bars.

Where to Stay in Thame: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Swan Hotel - A historic inn dating back to the 15th century, offering comfortable rooms and traditional pub fare.
  2. The Oxford Belfry - A modern hotel with spa facilities, located just a short drive from Thame.
  3. The Thatch - A cozy guesthouse with a charming thatched roof, situated right in the town center.

What to Buy in Thame: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. locally grown produce - Visit the Thame Farmers’ Market and bring home fresh fruits and vegetables, locally made jams and preserves, and more.
  2. Artisanal gin - As mentioned earlier, Oxfordshire produces some of the finest gin in the country. Bring home a bottle of locally made gin as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Thame

Thame is known for its vibrant community spirit and friendly population. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s charming streets, mingle with locals at the pubs and shops, and immerse themselves in the town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Thame

The best time to visit Thame is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is charming year-round, with plenty of indoor activities and cozy pubs and restaurants for colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thame

Do:

  • Do visit the local pubs and restaurants to experience the town’s food and drink scene.
  • Do take the time to explore Thame’s history and culture, including its museums and historic buildings.
  • Do get outdoors and explore the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect the town’s history and culture by being mindful of your actions and keeping the streets clean.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals, who are known for their friendliness and welcoming attitude.
  • Don’t forget to bring your camera, as there are plenty of photo opportunities in and around Thame.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thame

Thame is a safe town, with a low crime rate and a friendly community. However, visitors should still take precautions such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Thame

Thame has a well-equipped medical center, with several local doctors and clinics available in case of emergency. Visitors should remember to wear appropriate clothing and sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Thame: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thame

The closest major airport to Thame is London Heathrow Airport, located approximately 35 miles away. There are also several smaller airports in the region, including Oxford Airport, which is just 14 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Thame

Thame has several bus stops and stations, including the Thame Market Place and Thame Greyhound Public House.

Other Options

Thame can also be accessed by train, with regular services running to nearby stations such as Haddenham and Thame Parkway and Princes Risborough.

How to Get Around in Thame

Thame is a compact town, and most attractions can be reached on foot. Visitors can also cycle or drive if preferred.

Where to Go Next from Thame: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Oxford - Just a short drive away, Oxford is a historic university town with plenty of museums, galleries, and attractions to explore.
  2. Henley-on-Thames - This picturesque riverside town is just a 30-minute drive from Thame and offers boating, shopping, and dining opportunities.
  3. The Chilterns - Explore the stunning hills and countryside of the Chiltern Hills, located just north of Thame.

Conclusion

Thame may be a small town, but it’s big on charm and history. Whether you’re interested in exploring the local culture and customs, indulging in the town’s food and drink scene, or spending time in nature, Thame has something for everyone. Come and experience the beauty and hospitality of this charming town in the heart of Oxfordshire.
