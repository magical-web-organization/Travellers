Introduction to Sussex

Nestled in the South East of England, Sussex is a county that’s perfect for those seeking a mix of adventure and relaxation. Rich in history and culture, the region offers a plethora of attractions for tourists. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a culture vulture, or a foodie, you’ll find something to suit your taste in Sussex.

List of Places to Visit in Sussex

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arundel Castle - A stunning castle tracing back to the 11th century that has been the home to Duke of Norfolk and his family for over 800 years.
  2. Fishbourne Roman Palace - A former royal palace of the Roman Empire which was discovered during the construction in 1960’s; showcases ancient mosaics, gardens and the Roman way of living.
  3. Brighton Museum & Art Gallery - This museum displays collections of fine art, design, and history, ranging from European ceramics to contemporary art.
  4. Royal Pavilion - Built as a seaside palace for King George IV, the Royal Pavilion is a unique mix of Indian, Chinese, and European influences.

Natural Attractions of Sussex: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Seven Sisters Country Park - One of England’s most iconic and loved natural attractions with long cliffs that look out onto the English Channel. A perfect spot for picnics, walks, and beach activities.
  2. Beachy Head - A chalk cliff that stands tall on an alluring land of Eastbourne before descending gradually into a pristine beach.
  3. South Downs National Park - Stretching for 1,600 km, the park is a paradise for hikers, cyclists, and those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  4. Devil’s Dyke - This stunning dry valley, popular for picnics and walks, is known for its breathtaking views of the South Downs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sussex

Sussex offers excellent hiking and biking routes. Some of the most popular ones are:

  1. South Downs Way - This trail of 100 miles goes along the ridge of the South Downs with beautiful views and picturesque villages along the way.
  2. The Sussex Border Path - Covers the Sussex’s East and West border; the trail passes through ancient trackways, woodlands, and historic towns.
  3. Cuckoo Trail and Forest Way - A multi-use path that follows a former railway track, the trails include woods, fields, and a nature reserve.

Best Things to Do in Sussex: Activities and Tours

  1. Beach Activities - Indulge in activities like surfing at Pevensey Bay, kayaking, swimming, and paddleboarding at the beautiful coastlines of Sussex.
  2. Wine Tasting Tours - Located just an hour away from London, Sussex has blossomed in growing grapes and creating an award-winning wine scene.
  3. Sports Events - Sussex is referred to as the “Home of Cricket” and hosts annual international matches and domestic league matches.
  4. Ghost Tours - Discover the haunted spots and mysteries of Sussex with the Ghost Walk of the Lanes in Brighton or book a Ghost Bus Tour for the entire family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sussex

Day 1 - Visit Arundel Castle, WWT Arundel Wetland Centre, and Arundel Cathedral.

Day 2 - Head to Bexhill-on-Sea and visit its iconic De La Warr Pavilion for some contemporary art, then end the day with sunset views at Beachy Head.

Day 3 - Travel to Eastbourne for some cycling or hike in the South Downs National Park. Then, relax in the Victorian Grand hotel spa.

Day 4 - Take a trip to the historic town of Hastings and stroll along its famous Old Town. Afterward, head towards the George Street Quarter for some shopping.

Day 5 - Visit Brighton and its Royal Pavilion while enjoying the pebble beach, then head to the Brighton Pier for some fun rides.

Day 6 - Have a day of wine tasting at the Ridgeview Wine Estate in Ditchling; afterward, visit the market town of Lewes.

Day 7 - End your trip in Brighton by taking the Ghost Bus Tour around the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Sussex

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sussex Pudding - A steamed traditional pudding made of suet and apples.
  2. Sussex Smokie - A fish dish that is made from smoked haddock, potatoes and served with horseradish sauce and cream.
  3. Chobham Trencher - A traditional bread roll that is filled with sausages and eggs.
  4. Devil’s Dyke Ice Cream - Delicious ice cream made in a variety of flavors by the local family-run business.

Local Beverages

  1. Sussex Gin - Sussex has some excellent gin distillers, such as Blackdown Distillery.
  2. Laines Brewery Beers - A brewery founded locally in Brighton, making some of the best beer in Sussex.
  3. Sussex Wine - With South Downs becoming a wine hub, it’s a must to try some of the red and white wines made in Sussex.

Where to Stay in Sussex: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Hickstead - A luxury hotel featuring a golf course and spa, and located in the village of Hickstead.
  2. YHA South Downs - A Youth Hostel located within the South Downs National Park.
  3. The Grand Brighton - A 5-star hotel located on Brighton’s promenade.
  4. The George in Rye - A 4-star hotel located in Rye with excellent dining and a free-standing bath in every room.
  5. The Lamb at Angmering - A 16th-century coaching inn in the West Sussex Village.

What to Buy in Sussex: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sussex Trug Baskets - A trug is a traditional basket made with willow and hazel; it’s the perfect souvenir to carry all your trinkets from Sussex.
  2. Sussex Smokie - Buy the smoked haddock fishes and recreate the dish back home.
  3. Sussex Bonfire Societies’ memorabilia - Sussex has a tradition of raising money by hosting bonfire night celebrations, and it’s easy to find souvenirs of the event.
  4. Sussex Wine - Purchase a bottle or two of your favorite wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Sussex

Sussex tradition is full of customs from many years ago, the Sussex Bonfire Night is one such custom and is celebrated in the whole county with homemade flying lanterns and fireworks.

Best Time to Visit Sussex

The best time to visit Sussex is between March and November. It’s a beautiful time of year when the sun shines, and there is a beautiful array of greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sussex

Do:

  1. Explore the natural surroundings of the county; the South Downs National Park is full of stunning landscapes.
  2. Visit the many historical landmarks.
  3. Try the local wines and produce.
  4. Do sing along at the bonfire nights.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t rely solely on public transport; bicycle and car rentals are good options to explore the whole county.
  2. Don’t litter. Sussex’s full of natural landscapes, keep them clean.
  3. Don’t forget to learn the traditions and customs, respect Sussex heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sussex

Sussex is a relatively safe place, but visitors should always take the usual precautions of avoiding dark or deserted areas and always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Sussex

Visitors are always advised to bring or purchase travel insurance while visiting a new place.

Getting to Sussex: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sussex

  1. Gatwick Airport - located 39.9km away from Sussex.
  2. London City Airport - located 114km from Sussex.
  3. Heathrow Airport - located 67.5km from Sussex.

Bus Stations Near Sussex

  1. Brighton Coach Station - Located in the center of Brighton.
  2. Eastbourne Bus Station - Located in Eastbourne’s heart.

Other Options

Trains from London Victoria, London Bridge and St. Pancras take visitors to Brighton and Eastbourne in less than an hour.

How to Get Around in Sussex

In terms of public transportation, buses and trains are available to each of Sussex’s most popular, with car rentals and taxi options are also available.

Where to Go Next from Sussex: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. London, UK - Just over an hour by train to the capital city of the UK, offering some of Europe’s best sights and attractions.
  2. Newhaven, UK - Explore the Newhaven Fort that dates back to 1863
  3. Dorking, UK - Visit the Surrey Hills Area of Outstanding Beauty

Conclusion

Sussex has a vast offering of activities, natural beauty, and historic landmarks. It’s a destination that is sure to suit a diverse range of personalities with its stunning coastlines, unique heritage, and a wine scene that’s blossoming.
