Great Missenden, located in Buckinghamshire, England, is a charming village that has something to offer visitors of all ages. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something here for you. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, local culture, and travel tips for Great Missenden.

Introduction to Great Missenden

Great Missenden is a beautiful village located in the picturesque Chiltern Hills of Buckinghamshire. It is a popular destination for tourists because of its rich history, scenic beauty, and vibrant culture. Great Missenden is famous for being the birthplace of Roald Dahl, the renowned children’s author, and has several attractions dedicated to his life and work.

List of Places to Visit in Great Missenden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roald Dahl Museum and Story Centre: This charming museum is dedicated to the life and work of Roald Dahl. You can explore his writing hut, see his original manuscripts, and participate in interactive exhibits.

  • The Red Lion Pub: This historic pub dates back to the 16th century and is characterful and welcoming.

  • Old Amersham: Old Amersham is just a few miles from Great Missenden and is a historic market town with plenty of heritage and natural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Great Missenden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Chilterns Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty: Great Missenden is situated in the heart of the Chilterns, which is an area of natural beauty. Visitors can explore the many walking trails which lead through the area.

  • Roald Dahl’s Garden: This beautiful garden was the inspiration for many of Dahl’s books, and visitors can see the Gypsy Caravan where Danny, the Champion of the World was set.

  • The Misbourne River: This charming river runs through the village and is perfect for picnics and afternoon strolls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Missenden

  • The Chiltern Way: This iconic long-distance hiking trail leads through Great Missenden and takes hikers through the beautiful Chilterns.

  • The South Bucks Way: This scenic trail is perfect for those who want to explore the surrounding countryside on foot or on a bike.

Best Things to Do in Great Missenden: Activities and Tours

  • Chocolate Making Workshop: Visitors can join a chocolate-making workshop where they will learn to make delicious chocolates and truffles.

  • Bekonscot Model Village and Railway: This stunning model village offers a unique insight into life in England, from the 1930s onwards.

  • Steam Train Ride: Visitors can take a ride on the famous steam train through the Chiltern Hills and experience the area’s beautiful scenery from a unique vantage point.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Missenden

Day 1: Explore the village and visit Roald Dahl Museum and Story Centre.

Day 2: Discover the natural beauty of the Chilterns and take a hike on the Chiltern Way.

Day 3: Visit Old Amersham and enjoy the town’s historic charm.

Day 4: Take a chocolate-making workshop and enjoy a delicious lunch in one of the village’s cafes.

Day 5: Take a ride on the steam train and explore the local countryside.

Day 6: Spend the day relaxing by the Misbourne River and enjoying a picnic.

Day 7: Visit Bekonscot Model Village and Railway and enjoy a traditional English afternoon tea.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Missenden

Must-Try Dishes

  • Great Missenden Pork Pie: This traditional British dish is a must-try for any foodie visiting the village.

  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: This sweet and delicious dessert is a favourite among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • Chiltern Hills Brewery Ale: This award-winning ale is made in the village and is popular among locals.

  • English Breakfast Tea: No trip to England is complete without trying a traditional English breakfast tea.

Where to Stay in Great Missenden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Nags Head: This charming inn is located in the heart of the village and offers comfortable rooms and delicious food.

  • The Crown Inn: This historic inn is located just a few miles from Great Missenden in Old Amersham and offers visitors a traditional English pub experience.

  • The Pottery Shed: This unique accommodation is located in a converted pottery shed and offers visitors a truly unique experience.

What to Buy in Great Missenden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Roald Dahl Books and Merchandise: Visitors can purchase Roald Dahl merchandise and books at the Roald Dahl Museum and Story Centre.

  • Local Produce: Visitors can buy locally made wines, cheese, and other produce at the village’s farmer’s market.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Missenden

Great Missenden is a village that is proud of its many cultural traditions. Visitors can learn about the area’s rich history at the Roald Dahl Museum and Story Centre and explore the local culture by sampling the area’s delicious food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Great Missenden

The best time to visit Great Missenden is between April and September when the weather is warm and the hiking trails are open. However, the village has plenty to offer visitors year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Missenden

Do:

  • Do explore the village’s many hiking trails and scenic views.

  • Do try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the local wildlife.

  • Don’t miss out on the traditional English pub experience at one of the village’s historic inns.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Missenden

Great Missenden is a safe village with little crime. Visitors should exercise common sense and caution, particularly when hiking or walking alone in the countryside.

Health in Great Missenden

The village has a good healthcare system, including a local doctor’s surgery and a nearby hospital.

Getting to Great Missenden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Missenden

The closest airports to Great Missenden are Heathrow and Luton airports, both of which are a forty-five-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Great Missenden

The nearest bus stations are located in Aylesbury and High Wycombe, both of which are located a short bus ride from the village.

How to Get Around in Great Missenden

The village is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors who want to explore the surrounding area can hire a car or take public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Great Missenden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oxford: This historic university city is located just a few miles from Great Missenden and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history and culture.

  • Windsor: The historic town of Windsor is located just over an hour away from Great Missenden and offers visitors the chance to explore one of England’s most iconic landmarks, Windsor Castle.

Conclusion

Great Missenden is a charming village that has something to offer visitors of all ages. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or food, there’s plenty to explore here. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide has given you all the information you need to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of England.
