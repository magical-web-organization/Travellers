Introduction to Buckinghamshire

Located in South East England, Buckinghamshire is a picturesque and vibrant county that is steeped in history and culture. This area is known for its rolling hills, charming towns, and beautiful countryside. Whether you are looking for exciting outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or world-renowned landmarks, Buckinghamshire has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Buckinghamshire

To make the most of your visit to Buckinghamshire, make sure to visit these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Windsor Castle
  • Cliveden House
  • Waddesdon Manor
  • The Marlowe Theatre
  • The Roald Dahl Museum and Story Centre
  • Bletchley Park

Natural Attractions of Buckinghamshire: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chiltern Hills
  • Wendover Woods
  • National Trust properties
  • The Grand Union Canal
  • Salcey Forest

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buckinghamshire

Buckinghamshire offers marvellous opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts with its rolling hills, vast countryside, and natural routes. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Chiltern Way
  • Ridgeway National Trail
  • Leighton Buzzard Railway Walk
  • Pennine Way National Trail
  • Thames Path

Best Things to Do in Buckinghamshire: Activities and Tours

For those who love to indulge in exciting activities, Buckinghamshire has a range of options for everyone. Here are some of our suggestions:

  • Go on a hot air balloon ride
  • Attend a vineyard tour and tasting
  • Adventure sports such as zip-wire or climbing walls
  • Try kayaking or rafting
  • Attend a cookery class

Week-Long Itinerary for Buckinghamshire

To experience the best of Buckinghamshire, here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at a hotel of your choice in Buckinghamshire
Day 2 Explore the city sights of Buckinghamshire such as Windsor Castle and Cliveden House
Day 3 Visit the natural attractions such as Chiltern Hills, Wendover Woods, and Salcey Forest
Day 4 Explore the charming towns of Buckinghamshire including Marlowe and Beaconsfield
Day 5 Take a cooking class or visit a vineyard for wine tasting
Day 6 Attend adventure sports or go on a hot air balloon ride
Day 7 Relax and enjoy a leisurely day before departing for home

Food and Drink Scene of Buckinghamshire

Buckinghamshire is a food lover’s paradise, with a range of cuisines to try, from traditional English to international. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crispy belly pork with cider sauce, apple puree, and celeriac mash
  • Venison casserole with red wine, bacon, and mushrooms
  • Eccles cake, a local pastry filled with currants
  • Heston Blumenthal’s triple-cooked chips
  • Quenelles of pike with lobster and crayfish sauce

Local Beverages

  • Ale
  • Cider
  • English wines
  • Gin

What to Buy in Buckinghamshire: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Buckinghamshire has plenty of options to choose from. Some popular choices include:

  • Cheese from the Buckinghamshire region
  • A bottle of locally produced cider or English wine
  • A traditional Eccles cake
  • A selection of ale from the many local breweries

Local Culture and Customs of Buckinghamshire

In Buckinghamshire, people are known to be warm and welcoming. When visiting, ensure that you respect local customs and practices, such as standing on the right-hand side of an escalator, walking on the left-hand side of the pavement.

Best Time to Visit Buckinghamshire

The best time to visit Buckinghamshire is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is milder and the crowds are thinner, making it easier to explore the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buckinghamshire

Do:

  • Be respectful towards local customs and practices
  • Visit local attractions such as Windsor Castle and Waddesdon Manor
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore the natural landscapes and hiking trails

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disrespect historical artifacts or landmarks
  • Overlook the charming towns and villages
  • Engage in unruly behaviour

Safety and Health

Safety in Buckinghamshire

Buckinghamshire is a relatively safe area to visit, but like any other destination, it’s better to be safe than sorry. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be vigilant in crowded areas
  • Keep your valuable belongings safe
  • Follow local traffic regulations
  • Watch your step while hiking and biking

Health in Buckinghamshire

Buckinghamshire has a well-maintained healthcare system, and visitors can access medical services in case of any emergencies. However, it is still advisable to carry medical insurance.
