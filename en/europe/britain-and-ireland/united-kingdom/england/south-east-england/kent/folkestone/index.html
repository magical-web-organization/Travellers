Introduction to Folkestone

Folkestone is a charming coastal town located in Kent, South East England. It is a popular destination for tourists, thanks to its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. The town is known for its sweeping beaches, charming harbour, and serene countryside landscapes. Folkestone also boasts an impressive art scene, with a variety of galleries, museums, and exhibitions showcasing local talent. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or art enthusiast, there is something in Folkestone for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Folkestone

Folkestone is full of fascinating places to visit. Here’s a list of the top must-see attractions during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Folkestone Museum: Located in the town centre, the museum houses exhibits showcasing Folkestone’s history, from the early Roman period to the present day. The museum is free to enter, and well worth a visit.

  • The Battle of Britain Memorial: Dedicated to the bravery of the pilots who fought in the Battle of Britain, this memorial site hosts events, exhibitions, and tours. The memorial is located on the clifftops, providing visitors with stunning views of the coastline.

  • The Leas Promenade: This 1.5-mile promenade is a must-visit spot in Folkestone. The area features well-manicured gardens, a bandstand, and sea views.

Natural Attractions of Folkestone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The White Cliffs of Dover: Just a short drive away from Folkestone is the iconic White Cliffs of Dover. These stunning chalk cliffs rest above the English Channel, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can take a walk along the cliff edge or enjoy a picnic with family and friends.

  • The Harbour Arm: This beautiful arm was built for Folkestone Harbour and features numerous restaurants, a Champagne and Oyster Bar, and a sound and light show display during the summer months.

  • The Lower Leas Coastal Park: This 50-acre park boasts several play areas, a children’s water park, and an adventure golf course. Visitors can stroll through the park and take in the natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Folkestone

Folkestone offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes you should try:

  • The North Downs Way: This route offers stunning views of the English countryside. It is a challenging hike and best suited for experienced hikers.

  • The Elham Valley Way: This 21-mile route runs through picturesque countryside villages, farms, and orchards. It is a great trail for both hiking and biking.

  • The White Cliffs Country Trail: This route features breathtaking coastal views and cliff-top walkways. The route is 6.8 miles and is a moderate level hike.

Best Things to Do in Folkestone: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours in Folkestone to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Watersports: Folkestone’s beaches are perfect for water activities such as kayaking, paddleboarding, and surfing.

  • The Creative Quarter: This area features art galleries, artisan shops, and a variety of creative studios. Visitors can stroll through this vibrant quarter and indulge in a bit of shopping while appreciating local art.

  • The Folkestone Art Triennial: Held every three years, this art festival showcases artists from around the world. Visitors can take part in guided tours, talks, and events during the festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Folkestone

To fully explore and appreciate the beauty of Folkestone, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Folkestone Museum, explore the town centre and enjoy an evening walk along the Leas Promenade.

Day 2: Take a tour of the Battle of Britain Memorial Site and the RAF Manston History Museum.

Day 3: Explore Folkestone’s coastline via a boat trip. Enjoy some fresh seafood at the Harbour Arm.

Day 4: Explore the Lower Leas Coastal Park and, in the evening, watch the sunset over the English Channel.

Day 5: Visit the iconic White Cliffs of Dover for a refreshing hike and picnic.

Day 6: Take a bicycle ride along the Elham Valley Way, a picturesque village nestled in the rolling countryside.

Day 7: Stroll around Folkestone’s Creative Quarter, and indulging in local artisan products and sculptures.

Food and Drink Scene of Folkestone

Folkestone’s food and drink scene is a must-try for visitors. Here are some of the best dishes and local beverages to enjoy:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A favourite among locals and visitors alike is this traditional English dish. Try it at the Bay Fish and Chips restaurant, known for its sustainable fish.

  • Romney Marsh Lamb: This speciality dish is prepared with locally raised Romney Marsh lamb, slow-cooked to create a unique flavour. Try it at the Best Western Clifton Hotel.

  • Kentish Ale Pie: This hearty savoury pie is made with locally brewed ale. Try it at the Harbour Arm’s Smokehouse restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Chapel Down Wine: This award-winning wine is grown and produced in Kent. Visitors can tour the vineyard and taste the wines.

  • Kentish Cider: Produced from Kent apples, this refreshing drink is a must-try for visitors. Visitors can tour the orchards and sample the ciders.

## Where to Stay in Folkestone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Folkestone offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Best Western Clifton Hotel: A historic hotel located near the Leas Promenade, the Clifton offers comfortable and elegant rooms with sea views.

  • YHA Folkestone: This affordable hostel is located in the heart of Folkestone and offers budget-priced accommodation for backpackers.

  • The Relish: This boutique guest house offers stylish rooms and suites, a full English breakfast, and is close to the town centre.

What to Buy in Folkestone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home unique souvenirs or locally made products from Folkestone’s shops. Here are some of the best items to buy:

  • Handmade Ceramics from Georgie & Flo: These handmade ceramic dishes and vases are created by local artisans.

  • Bottled Local Beers and Ciders: Visit the Nelson Brewery to sample local beers and ciders.

  • Local Jams and Chutneys: Kent is known for its fruit orchards, and visitors can find a variety of jams and chutneys in Folkestone.

Local Culture and Customs of Folkestone

The town of Folkestone has a rich history and culture. One of their significant customs is the Folkestone Festival, held annually in July. The festival celebrates the town’s diversity, with music, art, food, and other cultural activities taking place. Other customs include visiting the local farmers’ markets on weekends and indulging in local artisanal products.

Best Time to Visit Folkestone

The best time to visit Folkestone is during the summer months, from June to August. During these months, the town is bustling with activity, and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. However, visitors can still experience the town’s beauty during the off-season as well, with a more relaxed atmosphere and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Folkestone

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including seafood, lamb, and ale pie.
  • Explore the town’s cultural scene, including its museums, galleries, and festivals.
  • Take in the natural beauty of the town, including the White Cliffs of Dover and the Lower Leas Coastal Park.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the countryside areas.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Folkestone

Folkestone is a relatively safe town for visitors. However, common safety precautions should be taken while exploring the town. Visitors should keep their belongings safe while in public areas and avoid walking alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Folkestone

Folkestone has excellent medical facilities, including the Folkestone Community Hospital and GP clinics. Visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance and pack any necessary medication for their trip.

Getting to Folkestone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Folkestone is easily accessible from London and other major cities. Here are the best transportation options:

Airports Near Folkestone

The nearest airports to Folkestone are London Gatwick Airport and London City Airport.

Bus Stations Near Folkestone

National Express runs regular buses to Folkestone from Victoria Coach Station in London.

Other Options

Visitors can take a train from London to Folkestone. The journey takes about an hour and forty minutes.

How to Get Around in Folkestone

Folkestone is a compact town, and most attractions are easily accessible by foot. Visitors can also take local buses and taxi services to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Folkestone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby towns and villages after their Folkestone trip. Here are some of the best destinations:

  • Canterbury: Famous for its historic cathedral, Canterbury provides visitors with a delightful blend of history and modernity.

  • Dover: A port town located only a few miles from Folkestone, known for its iconic white cliffs.

  • Hythe: This charming coastal town features historical buildings, antique shops, and markets.

Conclusion

Folkestone is a hidden gem in South East England, full of natural beauty, history, culture, and lovely people. Visitors can explore the town’s beautiful coastline, museums, galleries, and delicious cuisine at any time of the year. With its wide-ranging attractions, Folkestone is a perfect destination for travellers looking for a getaway from the hustle and bustle of daily life.
