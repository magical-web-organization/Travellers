Introduction to Hythe

Located on the coast in Kent, South East England, Hythe is a charming town with beautifully preserved medieval architecture, scenic landscapes, and a rich history. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor adventurer, foodie, or culture enthusiast, Hythe has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, eat, drink, stay, and experience in Hythe, as well as provide useful information for your trip planning.

List of Places to Visit in Hythe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hythe is known for its rich history and well-preserved medieval architecture. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Hythe:

  • St Leonard’s Church: Dating back to the 13th century, St Leonard’s Church is one of the oldest and most well-known landmarks in Hythe. Visitors can admire its impressive Norman architecture and stunning stained-glass windows.
  • Hythe Museum: Located in the High Street, Hythe Museum documents the town’s history and features a collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the town’s maritime, military, and social heritage.
  • The Martello Towers: Built during the Napoleonic Wars in the early 1800s, the Martello Towers were part of the defense system along the Kent coast against the French invasion. Visitors can explore their history and appreciate the stunning sea views from the top.

Natural Attractions of Hythe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hythe boasts beautiful landscapes, scenic coastal views, and tranquil nature. Explore the great outdoors with some of these recommendations:

  • Hythe Beach: A perfect spot to spend an afternoon relaxing, walking or enjoying a picnic. Beautiful views of the English Channel along with fresh sea breeze can be cherished from the shore.
  • Royal Military Canal: Boasting 28 miles of scenic walkway and surrounded by beautiful countryside, the Royal Military Canal is perfect for a peaceful stroll, cyclists or boating along the waterways
  • The Elham Valley Way: 26-mile walking route from Hythe to Canterbury through scenic valleys and along peaceful meadows. A trail specified by British Olympian Chris Brasher as “Britain’s most beautiful long-distance walk”

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hythe

Hiking Routes

  • The Saxon Shore Way
  • The Elham Valley Way
  • The North Downs Way

Biking Routes

  • Hythe to Dymchurch
  • Hythe to Ashford
  • Hythe to Canterbury

Best Things to Do in Hythe: Activities and Tours

Hythe offers a range of activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Here are some recommendations for things to do in Hythe:

  • Hythe Venetian Fete: A popular event in Hythe, held every two years in August, where the historic canal is transformed by decorated boats, water displays, and fireworks.
  • Boat Tour: Take a relaxing boat tour along the River Rother and the Royal Military Canal to admire the breathtaking natural beauty of Hythe.
  • Horse riding at Palmarsh Equestrian Centre: Visitors can experience horse riding accompanied by some of the placid and pleasant horses of Palmarsh

Week-Long Itinerary for Hythe

Day 1: Arrival and visit the St Leonard’s Church and Museum.

Day 2: Go for a ride or stroll around Royal Military Canal followed by a visit to the Martello Towers.

Day 3: Visit the Hythe Venetian Fete

Day 4: Spend the day exploring the coastal regions and enjoy the scenic view at Dymchurch Beach followed by lunch at the nearby seaside town of Folkestone.

Day 5: Walk the Elham Valley Way and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Day 6: Rent a bike and hit the trails, visit Palmarsh Equestrian Centre and try horse riding.

Day 7: Spend your last day enjoying the peaceful shoreline of Hythe beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Hythe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentish Pudding: A traditional savory dish made with beef and vegetables.
  • Kentish Ale: Kent produces some of the finest ales in the UK. Try some of the local brews at the many pubs in Hythe.

Local Beverages

  • English Breakfast Tea: England is known for its tea culture, so be sure to try the English Breakfast tea.
  • Kentish Apple Juice: Kent produces the best apples, and the apple juice is a must-try drink.

Where to Stay in Hythe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Hythe:

  • The Hythe Imperial Hotel: A recently refurbished luxury hotel dating back to the 1880s with incredible views of the English Channel.
  • The Castle Hotel: A historic hotel located close to the High Street, offering a comfortable and cozy stay.
  • Camping: For the nature lovers, Hythe has several camping options located in the woods or by the beach.

What to Buy in Hythe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kentish Cheese: Take some of the finest cheese back home as a tasty souvenir from the local stores.
  • Seaside-themed trinkets: Make your visit memorable with some of the seaside-themed trinkets and accessories that commemorate your trip to Hythe.

Local Culture and Customs of Hythe

  • English Afternoon Tea: Hythe is known for its traditional English afternoon tea tradition, so be sure to experience this cultural activity.
  • Politeness: The people of Hythe are known for their polite disposition, so make sure to always be respectful to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Hythe

The best time to visit Hythe is during the summer months (June to August). The weather is pleasant, and visitors can take advantage of the beautiful outdoor landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hythe

Do:

  • Do try the local cheese and apple juice.
  • Do explore the outdoors and take long walks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces.
  • Don’t forget to carry warm clothing as the weather may be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hythe

Hythe is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always important to take common sense precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep valuables safe.

Health in Hythe

Medical facilities are readily available in Hythe. The town has several medical clinics and dental practices, as well as a pharmacy located in the High Street.

Getting to Hythe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hythe

  • London Gatwick Airport: 69.8 miles (1.5 hours by car)
  • London City Airport: 72.3 miles (1.5 hours by car)

Bus Stations Near Hythe

  • Folkestone Bus Station: 4.7 miles (10 minutes by car)
  • Ashford International Bus Station: 14.5 miles (20 minutes by car)

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest railway station is in Sandling, about 6 miles from Hythe.
  • Car: Hythe is well-connected with major roads.

How to Get Around in Hythe

The best way to explore Hythe is by walking or cycling. However, taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Hythe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hythe serves as an excellent base for exploring the nearby areas with some popular destinations nearby:

  • Canterbury: Explore the historic city of Canterbury and visit the famous Canterbury Cathedral. 15.1 miles (25 minutes by car).
  • Dover: Visit the mighty Dover Castle, and explore the charming seaside town. 16 miles (25 minutes by car).
  • Rye: Stroll through the cobbled streets of the picturesque town of Rye, known for its historic architecture and antique shops. 25 miles (40 minutes by car).

Conclusion

Hythe is a charming town with a rich history, scenic landscapes, and excellent cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Hythe has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide helped you plan your trip to Hythe and make the most out of your visit.
