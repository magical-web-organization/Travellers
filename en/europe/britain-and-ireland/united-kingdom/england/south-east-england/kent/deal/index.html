A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Deal, Kent, South East England

Deal, located in Kent, South East England, is a charming town surrounded by panoramic vistas of the English coastline, stunning views of the English Channel, and a rich maritime history. It is a perfect destination for those who love history, outdoor activities, and scenic beauty. Whether you are planning a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, our comprehensive travel guide offers insights into the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local insights.

Introduction to Deal

Deal is a small coastal town in Kent, England. It has a long history of being a significant port and naval base, dating back to the 17th century. The town enjoys a prime location, nestled between the English Channel and the countryside of Kent, making it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Deal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Deal Castle: A historic castle built by Henry VIII, featuring a range of exhibitions, guided tours, and interactive displays.
  • The Timeball Tower Museum: A unique museum dedicated to the calibration of ships’ chronometers with the use of a timeball.
  • St George’s Church: A medieval church dating back to the 14th century that features stunning architecture and a picturesque graveyard.
  • Walmer Castle and Gardens: A former residence of the Lord Warden of the Cinque Ports, showcasing a range of period rooms, beautiful gardens, and panoramic views of the coast.

Natural Attractions of Deal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The White Cliffs of Dover: An iconic natural attraction of Britain that offers breathtaking views of the English Channel and the Kent countryside.
  • Betteshanger Country Park: A vast park with cycling trails, fishing lakes, camping facilities, and a range of outdoor activities.
  • Kingsdown Beach: A charming pebble beach with clear waters, offering excellent swimming and water sports opportunities.
  • Sandwich and Pegwell Bay National Nature Reserve: A nature reserve with a unique blend of coastal habitats and wildlife, perfect for birdwatching and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deal

The surrounding countryside of Kent and the coastlines offer a range of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Saxon Shore Way: A 163-mile long-distance path that stretches from Gravesend to Hastings, passing through Deal.
  • Dover to Deal cycleway: A flat, scenic route that takes you through quaint villages and picturesque countryside.

Best Things to Do in Deal: Activities and Tours

  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour from Deal pier to explore the stunning coastline of Kent and the English Channel.
  • Walk the Pier: Take a stroll along Deal pier to enjoy breathtaking views of the English Channel.
  • Fish and Chips Supper: Enjoy a traditional British meal of fish and chips from one of the many local fish and chip shops.
  • Visit the Market: Explore the local market in Deal, offering a range of fresh produce and local crafts.
  • Take a Brewery Tour: Take a guided tour of the Deal Brewery, famous for its premium ales.

Food and Drink Scene of Deal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A traditional British dish of battered fish and thick-cut chips.
  • Dover Sole: A locally caught and cooked fish that is highly regarded.
  • Shepherd’s Pie: A classic British dish made with minced lamb and vegetables, topped with mashed potato.

Local Beverages

  • Ale: Deal is known for its premium ales, with the Deal Brewery being the most famous.
  • Cider: Kent is known for its cider production, and Deal features several cider houses offering locally brewed cider.

Where to Stay in Deal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Royal Hotel Deal: A historic hotel boasting beautiful sea views and modern amenities.
  • Driftwood Beach House: A charming seaside guesthouse with stylish and cozy rooms.
  • The Clarendon Hotel: A family-run hotel with traditional charm and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Deal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Deal Beach Glass: Handcrafted jewelry and ornaments made from glass fragments found on the beach.
  • Kentish Cider: Locally brewed ciders with unique flavors.
  • Jams and Chutneys: Locally produced jams and chutneys made with Kent’s freshest produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Deal

The people of Deal are friendly and welcoming, with a deep appreciation for maritime history, fresh produce, and traditional British food.

Best Time to Visit Deal

The best time to visit Deal is from April to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deal

Do:

  • Try the local fish and chips.
  • Take a boat tour of the coast.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Visit the Deal Castle and other historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in public spaces.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Engage in loud or disruptive behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Deal

Deal is a relatively safe town, but visitors should stay aware of their surroundings and take precautions against pickpocketing and petty crime.

Health in Deal

The local medical facilities in Deal are comprehensive, with a hospital and several medical clinics throughout the town. Visitors are encouraged to bring any necessary medical supplies and medication.

Getting to Deal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Deal

  • London Gatwick Airport (LGW)
  • London Heathrow Airport (LHR)
  • London City Airport (LCY)

Bus Stations Near Deal

  • The nearest bus station to Deal is located in Dover, Kent.

Other Options

  • Trains: Deal has a train station, connected to London and other nearby towns.
  • Taxis: Taxis and private cars are available for hire in Deal.

How to Get Around in Deal

The best way to explore Deal is on foot or by bicycle, with several bike rental shops available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Deal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dover: Visit the famous White Cliffs of Dover and explore Dover Castle.
  • Canterbury: Visit the historic Canterbury Cathedral and explore the medieval streets of Canterbury.
  • Folkestone: A seaside town with beautiful beaches, an art-inspired shopping district, and a historic seafront.

Conclusion

Deal is a charming town with a rich maritime history, stunning natural attractions, and a warm and welcoming community. Whether you are visiting for a day or a week, there is plenty to see and do in Deal, making it a perfect destination for those who love history, outdoor activities, and scenic beauty.
