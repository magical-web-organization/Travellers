Introduction to Sittingbourne

Located in the southeastern county of Kent in England, Sittingbourne is a charming town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural significance. Once a prominent hub for the paper industry, this historic town has maintained its unique character and charm. Sittingbourne attracts adventure seekers, history buffs, and culture enthusiasts alike, who can explore its museums, cycling trails, natural attractions, food, and local culture. Discover this hidden gem with our ultimate travel guide to Sittingbourne.

List of Places to Visit in Sittingbourne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sittingbourne Heritage Museum: This museum is a must-visit for those interested in local history and heritage.
  • Milton Regis Court Hall Museum: This museum is great for exploring Milton Regis, a historic area of Sittingbourne.
  • Kent Police Museum: If you’re a fan of law enforcement history, this museum is the place to be.
  • Bredgar and Wormshill Light Railway: A permanent exhibition of the Kent countryside from the past.

Natural Attractions of Sittingbourne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oare Marshes Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, and is great for bird watching.
  • Swale National Nature Reserve: This stunning reserve is perfect for long walks and scenic views, with amazing wetlands to view.
  • St Mary’s Bay: In the summer months, enjoy a blue flag beach with the calm atmosphere a small community offers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sittingbourne

  • Sittingbourne to Canterbury: This 27-mile trail takes you through the scenic Kent countryside, starting in Sittingbourne and ending in Canterbury.
  • Sittingbourne to Faversham: This 8-mile trail takes you to Faversham, a beautiful historic town.

Best Things to Do in Sittingbourne: Activities and Tours

  • Tonge Barn: This is a beautiful restored historic building that is perfect for weddings and events. They also offer tours.
  • Live It Escape Rooms: If you’re looking for a fun and interactive experience, try out one of Live It Escape Rooms’ themed rooms.
  • Swale Boats Hire: Enjoy a family day out hiring rowing boats and exploring the islands close to the Swale.

Food and Drink Scene of Sittingbourne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentish Ale: Sample a pint of the local ale, brewed in small batches in the area.
  • Oysters: Freshly caught oysters are a local specialty, and you can find them at many seafood restaurants in the area.
  • Kentish Pudding: A regional take on the classic British pudding, this dish is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • English Tea: Sittingbourne offers a variety of teahouses where you can enjoy a traditional British-style afternoon tea.
  • Local Wines: The climate and terrain of Kent produce a variety of fruits including English wine.

Where to Stay in Sittingbourne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Beaumont: Sittingbourne’s only 4-star hotel offers modern and luxurious rooms and amenities
  • The Coniston Hotel: This historic hotel dates back to the 18th century and offers traditional English-style rooms and pub meals.
  • The Quills: This is a budget-friendly option with comfortable and clean rooms and easy access to the town centre.

What to Buy in Sittingbourne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oysters and Seafood: You can find fresh seafood at local markets and take them home to enjoy.
  • Kentish Ale: Take some home with you or pick up a souvenir glass from one of the local breweries.
  • Local arts and crafts: There are several local markets where you can buy the unique crafts made by the locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne has a rich cultural heritage, with many museums and historical sites to explore. The people of Sittingbourne are generally friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Sittingbourne

The best time to visit Sittingbourne is during the summer months when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sittingbourne

Do:

  • Explore the local museums and historical sites.
  • Take a bike or hike trip.
  • Try out the local seafood and dishes.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or walk on the newly harvested fields.
  • Littering is strictly prohibited, follow the environment rules.
  • Don’t feed any wild animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take the usual safety precautions when traveling, such as securing your valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Sittingbourne

There are several medical facilities in Sittingbourne and nearby, so you can access medical care if needed.

Getting to Sittingbourne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sittingbourne

  • London Southend Airport: 23 miles from Sittingbourne
  • London City Airport: 45 miles from Sittingbourne
  • London Gatwick Airport: 52 miles from Sittingbourne

Bus Stations Near Sittingbourne

  • Sittingbourne Bus Station
  • Swale railway station

Other Options

  • Train services from Sittingbourne to nearby towns and London are available from Swale railway station. It is the best option to travel around.
  • Car hire services are available which is the best way to tour Sittingbourne and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne is a compact town with everything in walking distance. Taxis and a good bus service is available for the comfort of tourists.

Where to Go Next from Sittingbourne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Canterbury - the historic city is just a hop away from Sittingbourne with its stunning architecture and ancient cathedral.
  • Isle of Sheppey - The island city offers a peaceful environment with tranquil sea views and great for a solitary vacation.

Conclusion

Sittingbourne is a perfect place to explore the culture and heritage of Kent. With its many museums, natural attractions, scenic bike routes, tasty local food, and friendly people, it’s no wonder that Sittingbourne is a hidden gem of the Southeast England. So pack your bags and get ready for adventure in this charming town.
