Introduction to Whitstable

Whitstable is a charming seaside town nestled on the north coast of Kent, a county located in the southeast of England. Known for its oysters, fresh seafood, and salt marshes, Whitstable has long been the destination of choice for foodies, history buffs, and nature lovers alike. The town’s picturesque landscapes, welcoming atmosphere, and vibrant culture make it a must-see destination for anyone visiting the region.

List of Places to Visit in Whitstable

From culturally important attractions to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there is never a dull moment in Whitstable. Here’s a list of the best places to visit in town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Whitstable Museum and Art Gallery: Located in the heart of town, this museum showcases the rich history and heritage of Whitstable, from its origins as a Roman settlement to its growth as a popular seaside resort in the 19th century.

  • The Whitstable Castle and Gardens: Formerly known as Tankerton Towers, this spectacular castle and garden complex boasts stunning architecture, beautiful landscaped gardens, and breath-taking views of the sea.

  • The Fisherman’s Chapel and Centre: This historic church, located in the old fish market area of town, provides a fascinating glimpse into the lives of the local fishermen whose work has been the lifeblood of Whitstable for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Whitstable: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Whitstable Harbour: The picturesque harbour is a great place to catch a glimpse of local fishermen unloading their daily catch of oysters. Take a stroll along the quay and breathe in the fresh salty air.

  • Tankerton Slopes and Whitstable Beach: Get your walking shoes on and take a stroll along the waterfront. The Tankerton Slopes offer stunning scenic views and a great spot for a picnic or an afternoon sunbathing.

  • The Oyster Beds at Whitstable Harbour: Whitstable’s most famous export is its oysters and the harbour is the perfect place to explore the beds where they are grown. Arrange a guided tour with one of the local oyster farmers to learn more about this fascinating trade.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whitstable

Whitstable is surrounded by beautiful countryside, perfect for hikes and bike rides. Here are a few of our favorite routes:

  • The Saxon Shore Way: This stunning coastal walk covers 160 miles from Gravesend in Kent to Hastings in East Sussex. The section that passes through Whitstable offers fabulous views of the sea and rugged coastline.

  • The Crab and Winkle Way: This 7-mile cycling and walking trail runs from Whitstable Harbour to Canterbury. The route follows the old railway path and is a great way to explore the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Whitstable: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or culture, Whitstable has something to offer everyone. Here are a few top things to do in Whitstable.

  • Kayaking and Paddle Boarding: Book a guided kayaking or paddleboarding tour along the coastline and watch the sunset from a unique perspective.

  • Fishing: Hire a local fishing boat and spend a day on the water. Catch your own dinner and learn about the local fishing industry from the experts.

  • Whitstable Yacht Club: For the more adventurous, the Whitstable Yacht Club offers sailing lessons and membership options for those looking to take their love of the sea to the next level.

Food and Drink Scene of Whitstable

Whitstable is famous for its seafood, and there’s no shortage of delicious options for foodies. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Whitstable Oysters: The town’s most famous dish, try them raw with lemon juice, bread and butter for the ultimate culinary experience.

  • Fish and Chips: You can’t go wrong with a classic fish and chips dish, enjoy it hot and fresh from one of the many local establishments.

  • Crab and Lobster: Indulge in a delicious crab or lobster dish, often served with creamy garlic sauce and new potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Kentish Cider: Made from locally grown apples, be sure to try the region’s famous cider.

  • Whitstable Bay Pale Ale: A locally brewed amber ale that is crisp and refreshing, perfect for a sunny day at the beach.

  • English Wine: Kent is one of the country’s largest wine-producing areas, so sample some locally-produced wine while you’re in town.

Where to Stay in Whitstable: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury or budget accommodation, Whitstable has something to suit everyone. Here are a few options to consider.

  • The Marine Hotel: A boutique hotel with beautiful sea views and luxurious amenities.

  • The Whitstable Hostel: An affordable option for budget travelers with basic amenities and a friendly atmosphere.

  • The Oyster Guesthouse: A charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of town, offering spacious rooms and a traditional English breakfast.

What to Buy in Whitstable: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a special souvenir to take home from Whitstable? Here are a few local products to consider.

  • Whitstable Oysters: Pick up a bag of fresh oysters from the harbour to enjoy at home.

  • Kentish Wine: Bring home a bottle of locally-produced wine or cider to share with friends and family.

  • Handmade Pottery: Whitstable has a strong tradition in pottery, so pick up a unique piece of ceramics from a local artisan.

Local Culture and Customs of Whitstable

Whitstable has a unique and fascinating culture that is rooted in its maritime heritage. The town is renowned for its community spirit, warm hospitality, and love of music and arts. Visitors are welcomed with open arms and encouraged to participate in the many local festivals and events that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Whitstable

Whitstable is a town that can be enjoyed year-round, but peak tourist season tends to run from June to August when the weather is at its best. Spring and autumn also offer mild temperatures and fewer crowds, making them perfect for exploring the local countryside and visiting the town’s cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whitstable

To make the most of your visit to Whitstable, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind.

Do:

  • Do try the oysters, fish and chips, and other locally-sourced seafood.

  • Do bring sturdy walking shoes for hikes and bike rides.

  • Do participate in the many local festivals and events that take place throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beach or in public places.

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat for protection from the sun.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for recommendations from locals for the best places to eat, drink, and explore.

Safety and Health

Whitstable is generally a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take the usual precautions for personal safety and be mindful of their belongings while exploring the area. In terms of health, visitors should be aware of the risks associated with consuming raw oysters and other seafood and should consult with their doctor if they have any concerns.

Safety in Whitstable

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

  • Make sure to lock up your valuables when leaving your room or rental property.

  • In the event of an emergency, dial 999 for police, ambulance, or fire services.

Health in Whitstable

  • Avoid consuming raw or undercooked shellfish if you have a weakened immune system or liver disease.

  • Always wash your hands thoroughly before eating or handling food.

  • Pack any necessary medications and bring a copy of your prescription, just in case.

Getting to Whitstable: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whitstable is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. Here are a few options for getting to and from the town.

  • By Car: Whitstable is located just off the M2 motorway, which connects London and Kent.

  • By Train: The town has a train station, with regular services to London, Canterbury, and other destinations in the region.

  • By Bus: There are several bus services that run to and from Whitstable, connecting the town to other nearby towns and cities.

Airports Near Whitstable

  • London City Airport: Located approximately 85 miles from Whitstable.

  • Gatwick Airport: Located approximately 70 miles from Whitstable.

  • Heathrow Airport: Located approximately 95 miles from Whitstable.

Bus Stations Near Whitstable

  • Whitstable Bus Station: Located in the town centre, with regular services to surrounding areas.

  • Canterbury Bus Station: Located approximately 5 miles from Whitstable, with regular services to London, Dover, and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Regular ferry services run from Dover to Calais, connecting the UK with mainland Europe.

  • Cycle Hire: Bicycle hire services are available in Whitstable, perfect for exploring the surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Whitstable

Whitstable is a relatively small town, and most of the attractions and amenities are within easy walking distance of one another. For those who prefer to cycle, bike rentals are available in town, and many of the major roads have dedicated cycle paths. In terms of public transport, local bus services provide easy access to neighboring towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Whitstable: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whitstable is the perfect base for exploring the surrounding area, with many beautiful destinations just a short drive or train ride away. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Canterbury: This historic city, located just five miles from Whitstable, is home to a stunning cathedral, quaint streets, and beautiful architecture.

  • Dover: Located 23 miles from Whitstable, the town of Dover is home to a world-famous white cliff coastline and the historic Dover Castle.

  • The Isle of Sheppey: Located just across the water from Whitstable, the Isle of Sheppey is home to beautiful beaches, nature reserves, and quaint coastal villages.

Conclusion

Whitstable is a hidden gem in Kent, England, and is not to be missed. With its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, the town offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to indulge in fresh seafood, explore beautiful coastal landscapes, or immerse yourself in local culture, you won’t be disappointed. Start planning your trip today and experience the magic of Whitstable for yourself.
