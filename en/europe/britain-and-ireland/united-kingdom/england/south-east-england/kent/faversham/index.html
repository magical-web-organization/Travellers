Introduction to Faversham

Nestled in the picturesque countryside of Kent, Faversham is a charming market town that captivates visitors with its quaint historic streets, cultural attractions, and delightful local cuisine. An easy train ride from London, Faversham is a perfect base for exploring the nearby areas of Canterbury, Whitstable, and Dover. Its rich history can be traced back to Roman times, making it an important heritage destination.

List of Places to Visit in Faversham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Faversham is home to a range of museums and heritage sites that offer a window into its intriguing past. Among the most notable ones are:

  • Faversham Steam Railway Museum: a delightful journey that showcases the town’s industrial heritage with working steam engines and train rides.
  • Maison Dieu: a medieval hospital-turned-heritage center that houses exhibitions on local history, including the town’s maritime connections.
  • Fleur de Lis Museum and Gallery: a fascinating museum that displays an extensive collection of artifacts from Faversham and its surroundings.
  • Chart Gunpowder Mills: one of the finest preserved 18th-century industrial museums that reveal the technology of gunpowder production and its impact on society.

Natural Attractions of Faversham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Faversham is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, from rolling hills to serene water bodies. Here are some of the unmissable natural attractions to explore:

  • Oare Marshes Nature Reserve: a birdwatcher’s paradise with over 200 species of birds to spot in a unique coastal wetland habitat.
  • Brogdale Collections: a fruit-growing region that preserves over 4,000 varieties of apple, pear, and cherry trees.
  • North Kent Marshes: a vast area of marshy wetlands that’s popular for scenic walks and cycling trails.
  • Abbey Physic Garden: a serene oasis in the town center that grows medicinal plants, herbs, and vegetables organically.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Faversham

Faversham boasts scenic routes that are ideal for hikers and bikers of all levels. Some notable ones are:

  • Saxon Shore Way: an exhilarating coastal trail that spans over 160 miles from Gravesend to Hastings, with charming villages and stunning views to discover along the way.
  • Elham Valley Way: a 22-mile trail that takes you through picturesque countryside, woodlands, and quaint villages.
  • Faversham Creek Trail: a 2.5-mile circular walk that showcases the town’s maritime heritage and passes by beautiful orchards, waterways, and historic buildings.

Best Things to Do in Faversham: Activities and Tours

Faversham offers plenty of experiences to keep visitors engaged and entertained. You can try your hand at:

  • Brewing beer at Shepherd Neame Brewery: Britain’s oldest brewery dating back to 1698; it offers tours and tastings of its famous ales.
  • Taking a boat trip down Faversham Creek: an idyllic 40-minute ride aboard a Thames sailing barge that offers a unique perspective of the town and its surroundings.
  • Shopping at Macknade Fine Foods: a gourmet paradise with over 170 years of trading history featuring fresh produce, deli items, and artisanal products.
  • Exploring the Swale Estuary on a paddleboard or kayak: an invigorating way to appreciate the serene beauty of this unique landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Faversham

Faversham is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of gastronomic delights to savor. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gypsy Tart: a traditional Kentish dessert made with evaporated milk, muscovado sugar, and pastry.
  • Oysters: Faversham is renowned for its succulent native oysters that are harvested from the surrounding waters.
  • Hop-pickers’ fish and chips: a hearty meal featuring beer-battered fish and locally-grown potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Shepherd Neame ales: the UK’s oldest brewery produces a range of award-winning ales, including Spitfire, which commemorates the Battle of Britain.
  • Faversham Creek Spiced Rum: the first single-batch spiced rum produced in Kent.
  • Benchley’s Kentish Cider: a refreshing cider made from locally-sourced apples.

Where to Stay in Faversham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Faversham offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay are:

The Sun Inn

A historic 14th-century inn that offers comfortable rooms, a charming restaurant, and a cozy bar with a log fire.

Judd’s Folly Hotel

A family-run hotel that boasts stunning countryside views, modern amenities, and a tranquil location.

Faversham Creek Hotel

A boutique hotel that overlooks the picturesque creek, featuring stylish rooms, a chic bar, and a garden terrace.

What to Buy in Faversham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Faversham is known for its unique local products and handicrafts that make for great souvenirs. Here are some of the things to buy:

  • Local honey: Faversham is renowned for its high-quality honey made from bees that feed on the local flora.
  • Lavender products: Lavender grows abundantly in the town, and its oil is used in various beauty and wellness products.
  • Antique collectibles: Faversham’s antique shops and markets offer a treasure trove of unique items, including vintage maps, silverware, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Faversham

Faversham has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its traditions, festivals, and customs. Some notable ones are:

  • Faversham Hop Festival: held every September, it celebrates the town’s long-standing links with hop farming with live music, dance, and street entertainment.
  • Oyster festival: a popular seafood festival that takes place in July, with a grand procession, live music, and a fireworks display.
  • Faversham Market: a weekly market that’s been held since the 12th century, offering fresh produce, crafts, and antiques.

Best Time to Visit Faversham

The best time to visit Faversham is from April to September, when the weather is mild, and the countryside is in full bloom. The town’s seasonal events also take place during this period, offering visitors a great opportunity to experience its unique culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Faversham

Faversham is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. It’s advised to:

  • Avoid unlit and deserted areas at night.
  • Secure your valuables and lock your accommodation when leaving.
  • Follow the local traffic rules and be vigilant on the roads.

Health in Faversham

Visitors to Faversham are advised to have standard vaccinations before their trip and follow the necessary health and safety measures. It’s also important to drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Faversham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Faversham is well-connected via various transportation modes, making it easy to reach and explore. Some of the options are:

Airports Near Faversham

The nearest international airports are London Gatwick and London Heathrow, both of which are about 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Faversham

There are regular bus services that link the town with neighboring areas, including Canterbury, Whitstable, and Sittingbourne.

Other Options

  • Train: Faversham has a railway station that connects it to London (St Pancras International) and other major cities in the UK.
  • Car: Faversham is easily accessible via the M2 motorway, and there are ample car rental options available.

How to Get Around in Faversham

Faversham is a compact town with most of its attractions and landmarks within walking distance. Still, visitors can use bicycles, taxis, or rental cars to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Faversham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Faversham serves as an excellent base to explore the surrounding areas of Kent, which are home to several famous attractions and landmarks. Some of the notable ones are:

  • Canterbury Cathedral: UNESCO-listed cathedral that’s counted among the most significant religious sites in England.
  • Whitstable Beach: a seaside town famous for its oysters, art galleries, and charming cottages.
  • Leeds Castle: a stunning castle with beautiful gardens, a maze, and a museum.
  • Dover Castle: a medieval castle that boasts a fascinating history and breathtaking views of the English Channel.

Conclusion

Faversham is a hidden gem of Kent, a compact town packed with cultural and natural attractions, tempting food and drink scene, and a unique local culture. Plan a trip to Faversham and discover its charming streets, stunning countryside, and remarkable history.
