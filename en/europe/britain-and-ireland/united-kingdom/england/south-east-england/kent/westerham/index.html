Westerham is a charming town located in the Kent county of South East England. This town is steeped in history, surrounded by beautiful countryside, and home to various outdoor adventures to keep visitors entertained. Whether you’re a history buff, an avid hiker, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Westerham has something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best attractions, activities, and points of interest that Westerham has to offer.

Introduction to Westerham

Westerham is situated on the edge of the North Downs, offering stunning views of the nearby countryside. This town is a popular spot for tourists, with plenty of historical attractions, artisanal shops, and excellent dining options. Here are some of the top places to visit in Westerham.

List of Places to Visit in Westerham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Churchill’s Chartwell House

Chartwell House, the former home of Sir Winston Churchill, is now a popular visitor attraction. Tour the house and gardens to learn about Churchill’s life and legacy. You can visit the studio, the bedroom, and the library where Churchill wrote and worked.

Quebec House

Quebec House is a National Trust property, once the home of General James Wolfe, the man who beat the French at the Battle of Quebec. Visitors can learn more about the building’s history from the knowledgeable staff and enjoy stunning views of the nearby countryside from the terrace.

Natural Attractions of Westerham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Emmett’s Garden

Emmett’s Garden is a stunning hillside garden with views of the Weald of Kent. The garden is home to various plants from all over the world, including exotic trees and shrubs. Visitors can take guided tours or explore the garden at their own pace.

Westerham Brewery

Westerham Brewery has been producing ales since 2004, using traditional methods that create distinct flavors. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample different beers afterward. There’s also an on-site shop where you can buy some bottles to take home as souvenirs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westerham

Westerham is surrounded by lovely countryside that lends itself well to outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in Westerham include:

The North Downs Way

The North Downs Way is a long-distance hiking trail that runs through Westerham. This trail offers incredible views of the surrounding countryside and is a great way to explore the area on foot.

Toy’s Hill

Toy’s Hill, located just outside Westerham, is a popular spot for hiking and nature walks. This area is scattered with trails and paths that offer incredible views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Westerham: Activities and Tours

In addition to the hiking and biking opportunities mentioned above, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Westerham. Here are some of the best:

Riverhill Himalayan Gardens

Riverhill Himalayan Gardens is an award-winning garden that’s been around since the 1840s. Visitors can explore these gardens and enjoy its seasonal flower displays, tea rooms, and more.

Helicopter Tours of the Kent Countryside

The Kent countryside is stunning from the ground, but it’s even more breathtaking when viewed from the air. Enjoy stunning views of Westerham and the surrounding Kent countryside with a helicopter tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westerham

Day 1: Arrive in Westerham, check into your accommodation and visit some of the town’s historical attractions like Churchill’s Chartwell House and Quebec House.

Day 2: Enjoy a morning hike or bike ride along the North Downs Way and explore the nearby Emmett’s Garden afterward. Finish off the day with a visit to Westerham Brewery for a tour and some beer tasting.

Day 3: Embark on a helicopter tour of the Kent countryside before taking a stroll through Riverhill Himalayan Gardens.

Day 4: Explore the quaint shops and artisanal food stores of Westerham, sampling some of the local cuisine as you go.

Day 5: Head out of Westerham to explore some of the surrounding area, like nearby Knole Park or Penshurst Place.

Day 6: Visit Leeds Castle, located just 40 minutes away from Westerham, with its beautiful moat and gardens.

Day 7: Take a leisurely stroll along one of the many nature trails around Westerham, such as Toys Hill or the Four Elms Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Westerham

Westerham’s food and drink scene is varied and exciting, with plenty of artisanal producers and independent food shops to explore. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Traditional Afternoon Tea In Chartwell House

Afternoon tea at Chartwell House is a must-do activity. Visitors can enjoy sandwiches, cakes, and tea surrounded by the stunning countryside.

Shepherd Neame Ale

Shepherd Neame Ale is a signature drink of the Westerham Brewery and a staple on most local menus.

Local Beverages

Squerryes Sparkling Wine

Squerryes is located a few miles from Westerham and produces delicious sparkling wine.

Biddenden Vineyards Cider

Biddenden Vineyards offers visitors the chance to sample their delicious apple cider and perry while enjoying the idyllic countryside.

Where to Stay in Westerham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westerham offers plenty of lodging options for visitors, ranging from upscale hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

The Grasshopper Inn

The Grasshopper Inn is a beautiful 18th-century inn that offers stylish rooms, a bar, and an acclaimed restaurant.

The Kings Arms Hotel

The Kings Arms Hotel is a charming Georgian mansion that’s now a boutique hotel. The hotel is in the heart of town and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Westerham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Westerham is home to many independent shops that sell everything from artisanal food items to handmade crafts. Here are some of the best places to shop:

The Cheese Room

The Cheese Room is a local shop that sells delicious artisanal cheese.

The Westerham Cyclery

The Westerham Cyclery is a popular independent bike shop that sells all sorts of cycling accessories and products.

Local Culture and Customs of Westerham

Westerham is a multicultural town that values its heritage and history. Visitors are encouraged to explore the various historical attractions and take part in cultural events to learn more about the area’s diverse traditions.

Best Time to Visit Westerham

The best time to visit Westerham is during the summer months when the weather is pleasant and the countryside is in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westerham

Do:

  • Visit the historical attractions like Churchill’s Chartwell House and Quebec House.
  • Take a hike or bike ride through the North Downs Way.
  • Enjoy local cuisine like Shepherd Neame Ale and Squerryes Sparkling Wine.

Don’t:

  • Forget to check the weather forecast and pack accordingly.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Leave without trying some of the delicious local cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westerham

Westerham is overall a safe town. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as not traveling alone late at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Westerham

Westerham has excellent medical facilities, including several doctors and a local health center.

Getting to Westerham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westerham

Gatwick is the closest and most convenient airport for visitors traveling to Westerham.

Bus Stations Near Westerham

The nearest bus station to Westerham is located in Edenbridge, just a few miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to nearby towns like Sevenoaks or Oxted and take a short bus ride or taxi to Westerham.

How to Get Around in Westerham

Westerham is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Alternatively, visitors can rent bikes or hire a taxi to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Westerham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby attractions and destinations to explore from Westerham, including:

Hever Castle

Hever Castle is a historic castle located just a few miles away from Westerham.

Penshurst Place

Penshurst Place is another historic building located a few miles away from Westerham, with stunning gardens and picturesque scenery.

Conclusion

Westerham is a charming town that’s perfect for visitors who enjoy history, outdoor activities, and artisanal cuisine. With plenty of hiking and biking routes, historical attractions, and local delicacies to try, there’s never a shortage of things to do in Westerham. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week, this town is sure to leave a lasting impression on you.
