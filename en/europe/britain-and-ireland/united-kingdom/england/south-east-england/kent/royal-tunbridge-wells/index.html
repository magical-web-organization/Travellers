Introduction to Royal Tunbridge Wells

Located in the heart of the picturesque county of Kent in South East England, Royal Tunbridge Wells is a charming spa town that boasts stunning historic architecture, lush greenery, and a vibrant cultural scene. The town is known for its natural springs and beautiful parks, making it a popular destination for those seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views. It is also home to a wide array of museums, historic sites, and cultural attractions that will delight history buffs and art enthusiasts alike. Whether you are looking to unwind, explore, or experience the local culture, Royal Tunbridge Wells has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Royal Tunbridge Wells

Royal Tunbridge Wells is a town full of fascinating places to visit. Here are some of the must-see attractions that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Tunbridge Wells Museum and Art Gallery: This museum is situated in an elegant Victorian villa and showcases the town’s rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore exhibitions on local geology, archaeology, and social history, as well as a collection of fine art and decorative objects.

  • The Pantiles: This historic colonnaded walkway dates back to the 17th century and is lined with boutique shops, galleries, cafes, and restaurants. The area is also home to the Chalybeate Spring, which was believed to have medicinal properties.

  • Groombridge Place: This magnificent country estate and gardens have been featured in several productions, including the Jane Austen adaptation, “Pride and Prejudice.” Visitors can explore the formal gardens, the Enchanted Forest, and the 17th-century house.

Natural Attractions of Royal Tunbridge Wells: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dunorlan Park: This Victorian park covers 78 acres of stunning landscaped gardens, lakes, and woodland. It is a popular spot for picnics, nature walks, and outdoor activities.

  • Tunbridge Wells Common: This expansive open space covers over 250 acres and is home to a range of flora and fauna. Visitors can go bird-watching, hiking, or join a guided nature walk.

  • The High Rocks: These striking sandstone cliffs are situated in a beautiful nature reserve and offer breathtaking views of the surrounding area. Visitors can explore the network of footpaths and enjoy the stunning geological formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Royal Tunbridge Wells

Royal Tunbridge Wells is surrounded by some of the most beautiful countryside in England, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Tunbridge Wells Circular Walk: This 27-mile circular walk takes visitors through the town’s surrounding countryside, passing through enchanting woodland, quaint villages, and beautiful countryside landscapes.

  • The Forest Way: This 10-mile trail follows the route of a disused railway line and passes through the heart of the High Weald, an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.

  • Bedgebury Forest: This beautiful forest covers over 2,000 acres and offers a range of mountain bike trails for all abilities. There are also plenty of hiking trails, as well as an adventure play area and Go Ape treetop adventure.

Best Things to Do in Royal Tunbridge Wells: Activities and Tours

Royal Tunbridge Wells is a bustling town that offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Spa Day: Royal Tunbridge Wells is known for its natural springs and there are several spas in the town where visitors can indulge in a range of treatments and therapies.

  • Walking Tour: Join a walking tour of the town and discover the fascinating history and heritage of Royal Tunbridge Wells.

  • Gin Distillery Tour: Visit the local gin distillery and enjoy a tour of the facilities, as well as a tasting session.

Food and Drink Scene of Royal Tunbridge Wells

Royal Tunbridge Wells has a thriving food and drink scene, with a range of restaurants, cafes, and bars to suit all tastes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kentish Pudding Pie: This traditional English dish is made with suet pastry and filled with beef, bacon, onions, and mushrooms. It is a hearty and delicious meal that is perfect for a cold winter’s day.

  • Oysters: The town’s proximity to the coast means that there are plenty of seafood restaurants in the area. Oysters are a local delicacy and can be enjoyed freshly shucked or cooked in a variety of ways.

  • Scotch Eggs: This classic British snack is a hard-boiled egg wrapped in sausage meat and coated in breadcrumbs. It is a tasty and filling snack that is perfect for a picnic or a quick bite on the go.

Local Beverages

  • Kentish Ales: Kent is known for its brewing heritage and there are several local breweries in the area that produce a range of delicious beers and ales.

  • Biddenden Cider: This award-winning cider is made using a blend of Kentish apples and is a refreshing and fruity drink that is perfect for a sunny afternoon.

  • Chapel Down Wine: This vineyard is located just a short drive from Royal Tunbridge Wells and produces a range of award-winning sparkling wines and still wines.

  • The Tunbridge Wells Hotel: This stylish boutique hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers spacious rooms and excellent service.

  • Local Wines and Beers: There are several local vineyards and breweries in the area that produce a range of delicious wines and beers. These make great gifts for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Royal Tunbridge Wells

Royal Tunbridge Wells is a friendly and welcoming town with a rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the customs and traditions that you may encounter during your visit:

  • Afternoon Tea: Afternoon tea is a British tradition that involves a selection of sandwiches, cakes, and scones served with tea or coffee. Many cafes and restaurants in Royal Tunbridge Wells offer afternoon tea as a special treat for visitors.

  • Morris Dancing: Morris Dancing is a traditional English folk dance that involves a group of dancers dressed in white with bells on their ankles. The dancers perform intricate moves while accompanied by musicians playing traditional folk instruments.

  • May Day Celebrations: Every year, on the first of May, the town celebrates May Day with a range of traditional festivities, including Morris Dancing, live music, and a maypole dance.

Best Time to Visit Royal Tunbridge Wells

The best time to visit Royal Tunbridge Wells depends on your personal preferences and interests. The town is beautiful all year round, with each season bringing its own unique charm and activities.

  • Spring: Spring is a great time to visit Royal Tunbridge Wells, as the parks and gardens are in full bloom and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

  • Summer: Summer is the peak tourist season in Royal Tunbridge Wells, with warm weather and long daylight hours. During this time, the town is bustling with tourists and there are plenty of festivals and events to attend.

  • Autumn: Autumn in Royal Tunbridge Wells is a beautiful time of year, with the leaves changing color and a range of seasonal activities, such as apple-picking and harvest festivals.

  • Winter: Winter is a quiet time in Royal Tunbridge Wells, perfect for those looking to escape the crowds and enjoy a peaceful break. The town is beautifully decorated for the festive season, with plenty of Christmas markets and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Royal Tunbridge Wells

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your visit to Royal Tunbridge Wells:

Do:

  • Take a Walk in the Park: The parks and gardens in Royal Tunbridge Wells are some of the most beautiful in England. Take a walk, have a picnic, or just sit and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Try the Local Gin: Royal Tunbridge Wells is home to a local gin distillery that produces a range of delicious gins. Make sure to give it a try!

  • Attend a Festival or Event: The town hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, from music festivals to food fairs. Check the local events calendar to see what’s on during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Forget to Bring Your Walking Shoes: Royal Tunbridge Wells is a town that is best explored on foot, so make sure to bring comfortable shoes for walking.

  • Miss the Local Cultural Sites: The town has a rich cultural heritage, from museums to historic buildings. Be sure to explore the local sites to get a true taste of the town’s history and culture.

  • Overpack: Royal Tunbridge Wells is a small town, so there’s no need to bring too much luggage. Pack light and enjoy the freedom of exploring the town without cumbersome bags.

Safety and Health

Royal Tunbridge Wells is a safe and healthy town to visit. However, here are some tips to ensure that you stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Royal Tunbridge Wells

  • Stay Alert: Like any tourist destination, there may be pickpockets and other criminals looking to take advantage of visitors. Stay alert and keep an eye on your belongings.

  • Watch Out for Traffic: The roads in Royal Tunbridge Wells can be busy, especially during peak tourist season. Always be aware of your surroundings, especially when crossing the road.

Health in Royal Tunbridge Wells

  • Stay Hydrated: Royal Tunbridge Wells is a popular destination for outdoor activities, so make sure to stay hydrated and bring a water bottle with you.

  • Watch for Allergies: The town is home to a range of flowering plants and trees, so if you suffer from allergies, be sure to bring any necessary medication with you.

  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: The UK may not be known for its sunny weather, but it’s still important to protect yourself from the sun’s rays. Bring sunscreen and wear a hat on hot days.

Where to Go Next from Royal Tunbridge Wells: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Royal Tunbridge Wells is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting:

  • Hever Castle: This beautiful historic castle is located just a short drive from Royal Tunbridge Wells and was once the childhood home of Anne Boleyn.

  • Ashdown Forest: This beautiful nature reserve inspired A.A. Milne’s Winnie-the-Pooh books and is a great spot for hiking and picnics.

  • Brighton: This vibrant seaside city is located just an hour’s drive from Royal Tunbridge Wells and boasts a range of attractions, including a stunning pier, quirky boutiques, and a bustling nightlife.

Royal Tunbridge Wells is a town that offers something for everyone, from the historic architecture and cultural attractions to the stunning natural parks and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to unwind or explore, Royal Tunbridge Wells is a town that will leave you feeling refreshed and inspired.
