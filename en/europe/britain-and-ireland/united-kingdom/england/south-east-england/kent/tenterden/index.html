Introduction to Tenterden

Tenterden is a charming market town located in Kent, South East England. It boasts a fascinating history, beautiful landscapes, and a range of activities for visitors to enjoy. Tenterden is known for its timber-framed cottages, cobbled streets, and historic churches, creating a charming and peaceful atmosphere for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Tenterden

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Tenterden:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in learning about the town’s history and culture, Tenterden has a range of fascinating museums and historic landmarks to visit. Some of the most popular include:

  • Tenterden Museum: This museum showcases the history of Tenterden, with exhibits on its agricultural past, local businesses, and famous residents.
  • St. Mildred’s Church: A beautiful 12th-century church, St. Mildred’s is a stunning example of Norman architecture.
  • Smallhythe Place: This National Trust site was once the home of Ellen Terry, one of the greatest actresses of the Victorian era. Today, it is a museum that showcases her life and the history of the theatre.

Natural Attractions of Tenterden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tenterden is also home to some beautiful natural attractions. Here are a few must-visit outdoor spots:

  • Bedgebury National Pinetum and Forest: This vast park is home to a variety of trees and plants from around the world. Visitors can enjoy walks, trails, and picnic areas.
  • Tenterden Vineyard: Wine lovers will adore this vineyard, which produces award-winning wines that can be sampled while enjoying the scenic views.
  • High Weald Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty: This vast area of protected land is home to miles of stunning landscapes, over 30 nature reserves, and a range of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tenterden

Tenterden is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some popular routes include:

  • The High Weald Landscape Trail: This 95-mile long-distance walking trail runs through Tenterden and showcases the stunning landscape of the surrounding area.
  • The Sustrans Route 18: This popular cycle route passes through Tenterden and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Tenterden Circular Walk: A shorter walk of around 5 miles, this circular route offers beautiful views of Tenterden and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Tenterden: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Tenterden will never be bored, with a range of activities and tours available. Here are some must-do activities:

  • Tenterden Railway: Take a ride on this stunning steam train and enjoy beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Chapel Down Winery: Experience the delicious local wines and take a tour of this winery in Tenterden.
  • White Water Rafting: For thrill-seekers, this nearby activity center offers a range of water sports, including white water rafting, kayaking, and paddleboarding.

Food and Drink Scene of Tenterden

Tenterden is known for its local produce, with a range of delicious food and drink options on offer. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Romney Marsh lamb: This succulent lamb is sourced from the nearby Romney Marshes and is known for its melt-in-your-mouth tenderness.
  • Gypsy Tart: This traditional dessert is made with evaporated milk, muscovado sugar, and pastry, creating a deliciously sweet and sticky treat.
  • Kentish Ale Pie: This savory pie is filled with local ale, beef, and vegetables, creating a hearty and delicious meal.

Local Beverages

  • Chapel Down Wine: A local winery, Chapel Down produces award-winning wines that are known for their crisp and fresh flavors.
  • Kentish Ale: Visitors to Tenterden can enjoy a range of locally brewed ales that are full of flavor and character.
  • Cobnut Coffee: Made from roasted, ground cobnuts, this local coffee is a unique and delicious flavor experience.

Where to Stay in Tenterden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tenterden offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are a few popular choices:

  • Little Silver Country Hotel: This luxurious hotel is set in a stunning Grade II listed building and offers beautiful rooms and top-notch service.
  • The William Caxton: This historic pub and hotel offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere, perfect for those on a budget.
  • The London Beach Hotel & Spa: This contemporary hotel is located just outside Tenterden and offers a range of facilities, including a spa and a golf course.

What to Buy in Tenterden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tenterden offers a range of unique gifts and souvenirs to take home. Here are a few popular choices:

  • Local produce: Tenterden is known for its delicious local produce, including Romney Marsh Lamb, cobnuts, and Kentish Ale.
  • Artisanal crafts: Visitors can purchase a range of handmade crafts, including pottery, textiles, and jewelry, made by local artisans.
  • Tenterden souvenirs: From keychains to postcards, there are plenty of Tenterden souvenirs available to take home as a memento of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Tenterden

Tenterden is a town steeped in history and culture, with a unique set of customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Tenterden is known for its historic churches, and visitors are encouraged to dress modestly when attending services.
  • Tipping is not always expected in Tenterden, but it is appreciated in restaurants and cafes.
  • Tenterden is a friendly and peaceful town, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful and courteous to locals.

Best Time to Visit Tenterden

The best time to visit Tenterden is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can enjoy Tenterden’s charm year-round, with autumn and spring offering beautiful landscapes and unique seasonal events, such as the Tenterden Folk Festival in October.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Tenterden can enjoy a safe and healthy trip by keeping a few things in mind:

Safety in Tenterden

  • Tenterden is a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should still take standard precautions, such as locking doors at night and being aware of their surroundings.
  • Visitors should be cautious when driving on narrow country roads and give way to pedestrians and vehicles.
  • Visitors should be aware of local wildlife and follow standard safety guidelines when hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Health in Tenterden

  • Tenterden has excellent healthcare facilities, including a local hospital and several GP practices.
  • Visitors should bring any essential medications with them and be aware of any allergies or medical conditions that may require attention.
  • Visitors should drink plenty of water and use sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Tenterden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tenterden is located in Kent, South East England, and is easily accessible by several transportation options.

Airports Near Tenterden

The nearest airports to Tenterden are London Gatwick and London City Airport, both of which offer flights to domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Tenterden

Tenterden has a local bus station that provides service to nearby towns and cities, including Ashford and Maidstone.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Tenterden by train, with frequent service from London, Maidstone, and Canterbury. Alternatively, visitors can drive to Tenterden, with plenty of parking available in the town center.

How to Get Around in Tenterden

Tenterden is a compact and walkable town, with many of the key attractions located within easy walking distance from each other. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike to explore the surrounding countryside or take a taxi for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Tenterden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
