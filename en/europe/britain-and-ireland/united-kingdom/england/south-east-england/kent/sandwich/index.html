Sandwich, located in Kent in South East England, is a picturesque town with a rich history that dates back to the Roman period. With its medieval architecture, stunning natural landscape, and vibrant culture, Sandwich is a delightful destination for travelers seeking a unique experience.

Below is a comprehensive travel guide to Sandwich that covers everything from the best places to visit to local customs and safety tips.

Introduction to Sandwich

With its cobbled streets, charming old buildings, and fascinating history, Sandwich is a town that has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in ancient history, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, this town has it all.

Founded in the 7th century, Sandwich became an important port and market town during the medieval period. Today, its historic streets are lined with a mix of Tudor, Georgian, and Victorian buildings that make it a pleasure to explore on foot.

List of Places to Visit in Sandwich

Sandwich has an abundance of attractions that cater to all interests. Here are some of the must-visit places in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sandwich Guildhall Museum: This 16th-century building houses a collection of historic artifacts, including ancient coins, armor, and weapons.
  • The Secret Gardens of Sandwich: These stunning gardens contain a variety of exotic plants, a beautiful pond, and Victorian glasshouses.
  • The White Mill: Built in 1760, this windmill is one of the best-preserved examples of its kind in England.

Natural Attractions of Sandwich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandwich Bay Bird Observatory: This unique conservation area is home to numerous species of birds, including migratory birds that stop here during their journeys.
  • Sandwich Bay: A vast stretch of sand dunes and beaches that offers spectacular views of the English Channel.
  • Sandwich and Pegwell Bay National Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is home to a variety of habitats, including mudflats, saltmarshes, and sand dunes, as well as numerous bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandwich

If you’re a nature lover, you’ll delight in the many outdoor adventures that Sandwich has to offer. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Viking Coastal Trail: This 32-mile trail takes you along the dramatic coastline of Kent, passing through Sandwich and other beautiful towns and villages.
  • The Saxon Shore Way: This 163-mile trail follows the coastline of Kent and East Sussex, passing through Sandwich and other historic towns and villages.
  • The Stour Valley Walk: This 100-mile walk takes you through the beautiful countryside of Kent and passes through several charming towns and villages, including Sandwich.

Best Things to Do in Sandwich: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with family and friends, there are many things to do in Sandwich that will keep you entertained. Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • The Sandwich Food Tour: This guided tour takes you through the town’s best restaurants and cafes, where you can sample local specialties and learn about the town’s culinary traditions.
  • The Sandwich Ghost Tour: This spooky tour takes you through the town’s historic streets and buildings, where you’ll learn about local legends and spooky stories.
  • The Sandwich River Tour: This scenic tour takes you along the River Stour, where you can see the town’s historic buildings from a unique perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Sandwich

Sandwich has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects its history and culture. From savory pasties to sweet treats, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sandwich Pasty: A savory pastry with meat, vegetables, and gravy, typically served with mashed potatoes and peas.
  • Kentish Ale Pie: This hearty pie is made with locally brewed beer and serves as a comfort food for many residents.
  • Eton Mess: This classic English dessert is made with strawberries, whipped cream, and crumbled meringue.

Local Beverages

  • Shepherd Neame Beer: This family-run brewery has been brewing beer for over 300 years and is famous for its award-winning ales.
  • Biddenden Cider: A popular local cider made with apples grown in the town’s orchards.

Where to Stay in Sandwich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sandwich has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. These include hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • The Salutation: A luxury hotel that offers a unique blend of comfort and style.
  • The George and Dragon: A cozy guesthouse that’s perfect for budget travelers.
  • The Fleur de Lis: An affordable hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Sandwich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Sandwich, there are many local products and souvenirs that you can bring back home to remind you of your trip. Here are some popular options:

  • Kentish Ale: You can find a variety of locally brewed ales and beers in Sandwich’s shops and supermarkets.
  • Handmade Crafts: Sandwich has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and you can find many unique handmade items in the town’s shops and markets.
  • Local Jam: Kent is famous for its fruit, and you can find a variety of jams and preserves made with locally grown fruits in Sandwich.

Local Culture and Customs of Sandwich

Sandwich has a rich history and culture that’s reflected in its architecture, food, and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect Historic Buildings: Sandwich’s historic buildings are an important part of the town’s heritage, so it’s important to treat them with care and respect.
  • Try Local Specialties: Sandwich is famous for its food, so be sure to try the local specialties and support the local businesses.
  • Be Polite and Courteous: England is known for its politeness, and Sandwich is no exception. Remember to say “please” and “thank you” and be polite and courteous to everyone you meet.

Best Time to Visit Sandwich

The best time to visit Sandwich is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and all the attractions are open. Spring and autumn are also good times to visit if you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy the lovely weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandwich

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Sandwich:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink specialties
  • Visit the town’s historic buildings and landmarks
  • Explore the town on foot or by bike

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s historic buildings or natural sites
  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Disrespect residents or other visitors

Safety and Health

Sandwich is generally a safe and healthy destination for travelers. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Sandwich

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night
  • Keep your valuables secured and avoid carrying large amounts of cash
  • Follow any local safety regulations, including signage and instructions

Health in Sandwich

  • Bring any necessary prescription medications with you, and be sure to check if any vaccines or precautions are required before you travel
  • Carry sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun, especially during the summer months

Getting to Sandwich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sandwich is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Sandwich

  • Gatwick Airport: The nearest international airport to Sandwich, located 70 miles away.
  • Heathrow Airport: A larger international airport located 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Sandwich

  • Sandwich Bus Station: Located in the town center, this bus station provides connections to other towns and cities in Kent.

Other Options

  • Train: Sandwich has its own train station, which provides connections to London and other major cities in England.
  • Car: If you prefer to drive, the town is easily accessible from the M2 and A2 motorways.

How to Get Around in Sandwich

Sandwich is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to explore at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Sandwich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the area, there are many nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit, including:

  • Canterbury: A historic town known for its magnificent cathedral and ancient walls.
  • Dover: A major port town that’s home to the iconic White Cliffs of Dover.
  • Whitstable: A charming coastal town that’s famous for its oysters and seafood.

Conclusion

Sandwich is a town that offers something for everyone, with its historic buildings, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant culture. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to this charming town, and be sure to explore all that it has to offer.
