Introduction to Isle of Sheppey

The Isle of Sheppey, located in Kent, South East England, is a beautiful place that has a diverse range of attractions. Visitors can explore the island’s cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and much more! In this travel guide, we will explore the top things to do on the Isle of Sheppey to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Isle of Sheppey

Here is a list of the top places to visit while exploring the beautiful island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Blue Town Heritage Centre – This museum showcases the island’s rich history, culture, and art.
  • Sheerness Heritage Centre – This museum exhibits the island’s naval and social history.
  • Queenborough Guildhall Museum – This historic building houses a collection of artefacts that highlights the town’s siege in the 16th century.

Natural Attractions of Isle of Sheppey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elmley National Nature Reserve – This 3,300-acre nature reserve is the perfect place for birdwatching, stargazing, and relaxing in nature.
  • Minster Abbey - This tranquil and peaceful location offers beautiful gardens and ruins of a 7th-century monastery.
  • Eastchurch Aviation Museum - This museum highlights the island’s early aviation history and war-time operations.
  • Leysdown Beach - This charming little beach is the perfect place to watch the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isle of Sheppey

The Isle of Sheppey offers several hiking/biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes worth exploring:

  • The Sheppey Round – A 27-mile circular path with striking coastal views.
  • Sheerness Way Walk – A 4-mile walkway that follows the River Medway and offers scenic views of the ocean.
  • Saxon Shore Way – A 163-mile route stretching from Hastings to Gravesend, passing through the Isle of Sheppey.

Best Things to Do in Isle of Sheppey: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for an adrenaline-packed adventure or a leisurely stroll, the Isle of Sheppey has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things you can do:

  • Try kitesurfing – With two beaches offering some of the best kite surfing conditions in the UK, kitesurfing is a must-try activity for thrill-seekers.
  • Take a wildlife tour – Explore the island’s beautiful nature reserve and observe the diverse birdlife.
  • Visit the local farmers’ market – Try some delicious local produce at the farmers’ market, which takes place every Friday.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isle of Sheppey

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for the Isle of Sheppey:

Day 1: Arrive and check into your hotel. Explore Blue Town Heritage Centre.

Day 2: Take a leisurely walk along Sheerness Way. Visit Sheerness Heritage Centre.

Day 3: Rent a bike and explore the island.

Day 4: Take a guided historic tour of the island’s natural discovery sites.

Day 5: Explore Elmley National Nature Reserve.

Day 6: Take part in an outdoor adventure activity, such as kitesurfing, paragliding, or birdwatching.

Day 7: Relax on the beaches of Leysdown. Shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Isle of Sheppey

Here are some of the food and drinks to try while on the Isle of Sheppey:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters – The Isle of Sheppey is famous for its fresh oysters, which are a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Pie and Mash – A traditional British dish that can be found at local eateries.
  • Kentish apple tart - A delicious local dessert to try.

Local Beverages

  • Shepherd Neame Beers – These beers are brewed at the oldest brewery in Great Britain located near Sheerness.
  • Local Cider – The Isle of Sheppey has some of the best cider produced in England.

Where to Stay in Isle of Sheppey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Isle of Sheppey offers a variety of accommodation options including budget-friendly hostels, cozy guesthouses, and luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Ferry House Inn – This cozy, comfortable guesthouse offers picturesque views of the ocean and delicious food.
  • Queenborough Harbour Trust B&B – This budget-friendly bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms and amazing sea views.
  • The Ship Inn – Located close to the Sheerness Beach, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and great hospitality.

What to Buy in Isle of Sheppey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy a range of local souvenirs, including ceramics, homemade jams, local mustard, Kentish apple juice, and more! The farmers’ markets are a great place to find these products, so don’t forget to stop by.

Local Culture and Customs of Isle of Sheppey

The Isle of Sheppey has a rich maritime history and culture that is reflected in its architecture, food, and local festivals. The town’s history and culture are closely linked to the sea and the local fishing and trading industries.

Best Time to Visit Isle of Sheppey

The best time to visit the Isle of Sheppey is during the spring or summer months when the weather is warm and pleasant. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities and local events during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isle of Sheppey

Getting to Isle of Sheppey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to the Isle of Sheppey using several modes of transportation:

Airports Near Isle of Sheppey

  • London Heathrow Airport - 70 miles away from the island.
  • London Gatwick Airport - 56 miles away from the island.

Bus Stations Near Isle of Sheppey

  • National Express - Direct bus services from London Victoria Coach station to Sheerness-on-Sea on the island.

Other Options

  • Car - The Isle of Sheppey can be accessed via the A249 and M2 motorway.

How to Get Around in Isle of Sheppey

Visitors can explore the island using several transportation options, including:

  • Car - Visitors can hire rental cars or use their private vehicles to explore the island.
  • Bus - The island offers several local bus services.
  • Bike - To explore the nature reserves and scenic views with a sense of adventure.

Where to Go Next from Isle of Sheppey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby destinations from the Isle of Sheppey, including:

  • Canterbury - This beautiful city is steeped in history and famous for its cathedrals, museums, and unique culture.
  • The White Cliffs of Dover - These iconic cliffs offer stunning views of the English Channel and are perfect for hiking.
  • Rochester - This historic town is known for its magnificent castle, beautiful gardens, and charming high street.

Conclusion

The Isle of Sheppey is a beautiful island with a diverse range of attractions and activities to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether it’s exploring the historic landmarks, enjoying the great outdoors, or trying the local food and drinks, visitors will have an unforgettable experience on this beautiful island.
