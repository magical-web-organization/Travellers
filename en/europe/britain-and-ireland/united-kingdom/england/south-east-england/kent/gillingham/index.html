Introduction to Gillingham

Gillingham is a charming town located in the heart of Kent, South East England. It is known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Gillingham boasts of numerous attractions for visitors to explore, including museums, historical sites, natural parks, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the top things to do in Gillingham.

List of Places to Visit in Gillingham

Here is our list of the top places to visit in Gillingham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Engineers Museum: This museum showcases the history and achievements of the Royal Engineers.
  • Guildhall Museum: It is a museum that contains artefacts, exhibits, and displays that depict the history of Medway Towns.
  • The Historic Dockyard Chatham: This dockyard is an essential historical site in the UK and is home to many historic naval vessels.

Natural Attractions of Gillingham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Riverside Country Park: An expansive and serene park with walking trails and picnic areas.
  • Capstone Farm Country Park: A beautiful park with nature trails with stunning views and wildlife.
  • Gillingham Marina: A marina located at the northern tip of the River Medway, offering scenic views and peaceful walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gillingham

Gillingham and Kent offer great routes for cycling and walking, such as the Saxon Shore Way and the North Downs Way. Alternatively, visitors can enjoy the Medway Valley Walk, a 28 km walk that runs through picturesque villages and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Gillingham: Activities and Tours

  • Chatham Historic Dockyard Tour: Discover the Chatham Historic Dockyard, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and learn about the UK’s naval history.
  • Ghost tours: Explore Gillingham in an entirely different way with a theatre group walking tour, which is great for kids and adults.
  • Gillingham Golf Course: Visitors can play a round of golf in the picturesque surroundings of Gillingham.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gillingham

Day 1: Explore the historic Dockyard and immerse yourself in the UK’s naval history. Day 2: Visit the Royal Engineers Museum and learn about their contributions. Day 3: Take a leisurely walk around Riverside Country Park and take in the serene surroundings. Day 4: Follow the Medway Valley Walk and visit the picturesque villages. Day 5: Go on a Ghost tour and experience the town’s darker side. Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Gillingham Golf Course. Day 7: Finish the week by exploring the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Gillingham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pudding Pie: A savoury dish made with meat and suet pastry.
  • Kentish Pasty: A traditional pastry filled with lamb, root vegetables, and Kent cheese.
  • Gillingham Platter: A meat-based dish that often includes pork, ham, and chicken with a side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kentish Cider: A refreshing apple cider produced in Kent.
  • Shepherd Neame Beer: A traditional British ale, brewed in the region since 1698.
  • Biddenden Vineyards: vineyard produces award-winning English wine and cider.

Local Culture and Customs of Gillingham

Gillingham has a unique culture and customs, with a rich maritime history. Visitors should expect a friendly atmosphere and a warm welcome from locals.

Best Time to Visit Gillingham

The best time to visit Gillingham is during the summer months, between May and September. However, visitors should note that this is also the peak tourist season, and prices and crowds might be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gillingham

Do:

  • Do take a walking tour of Gillingham to discover the town’s hidden gems.
  • Do try the local food and drinks, which highlight the region’s specialties.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public areas.
  • Don’t drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gillingham

Overall, Gillingham is a safe place to visit and explore. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking their belongings, to prevent theft.

Health in Gillingham

Visitors should ensure they have travel insurance in place and take the necessary precautions, such as using a mosquito net, to avoid health issues.

Getting to Gillingham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gillingham is accessible by both road and rail.

Airports Near Gillingham

London Stansted Airport and London Gatwick are the closest airports to Gillingham, both roughly 1 hour 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Gillingham

The town is well-served by bus routes, with regular services from neighbouring towns and cities.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can hire a car to explore the surrounding areas of Kent.

How to Get Around in Gillingham

Gillingham is small enough to explore on foot. Visitors can also take a taxi or hire a car to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Gillingham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Canterbury: A beautiful historic town with a stunning cathedral.
  • Maidstone: A scenic town with a historic castle and beautiful gardens.
  • Rochester: A charming town with a castle, cathedral and literary connections (Charles Dickens).

Conclusion

Gillingham is a beautiful and fascinating town with rich culture, history and outdoor adventures. Visitors can enjoy its natural amenities or learn about its naval history. We hope this comprehensive guide has been helpful in planning your visit to Gillingham.
