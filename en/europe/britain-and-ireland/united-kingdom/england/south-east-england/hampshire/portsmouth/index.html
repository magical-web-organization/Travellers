Introduction to Portsmouth

Located in Hampshire, South East England, Portsmouth is a historic city with a rich maritime heritage. It is home to the British Navy and known for its world-class museums, natural attractions, and bustling food and drink scene. With so much to see and explore, Portsmouth is a must-visit destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Portsmouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Mary Rose Museum: Witness the story of King Henry VIII’s flagship that sank in 1545.
  2. Portsmouth Historic Dockyard: Discover the history of the British Navy with various attractions including HMS Victory and the National Museum of the Royal Navy.
  3. Charles Dickens’ Birthplace Museum: Explore the birthplace and childhood home of the famous author Charles Dickens.
  4. D-Day Story Museum: Learn about the events leading up to D-Day and the Normandy landings that changed the course of World War II.

Natural Attractions of Portsmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Southsea Beach: Enjoy a day by the sea at this popular beach with a pier and mini-golf course.
  2. Portsmouth Harbour: Take a stroll around Portsmouth Harbour and enjoy the picturesque views of boats and yachts.
  3. Southsea Castle: Explore this 16th-century castle and enjoy the stunning views of the Solent.
  4. Canoe Lake: Rent a boat or take a relaxing walk around Canoe Lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portsmouth

  1. Portsmouth Coastal Path: Enjoy a scenic walk along the coast for 10 miles which offers great views of the Isle of Wight and the coast of Hampshire.
  2. Hayling Billy Trail: Perfect for a family bike ride or a leisurely walk, this trail is situated in the south of Hayling Island, offering beautiful views of the seaside.

Best Things to Do in Portsmouth: Activities and Tours

  1. Spinnaker Tower: Climb 170 meters above Portsmouth to the top of the iconic Spinnaker Tower and witness the stunning views.
  2. Ghost Tour of Portsmouth: Experience the spooky side of Portsmouth with a guided ghost tour.
  3. Gunwharf Quays: Indulge in retail therapy at Gunwharf Quays, home to a variety of designer shops, cafes, and restaurants.
  4. Portsmouth Cathedral: Visit the magnificent Portsmouth Cathedral, built-in 1180, and admire the stunning architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Portsmouth

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips: Enjoy the British classic at many of the local pubs and restaurants.

  2. Pie and Mash: A hearty meal popular in East London, this dish can also be found in Portsmouth.

  3. Pubs and Bars: Portsmouth has a number of traditional pubs and bars where you can taste locally crafted beer and cider.

Local Beverages

  1. Portsmouth Distillery: Visit the distillery and taste the gin produced locally.

  2. Portsmouth Brewery: Taste independent beer brewed on-site at the Portsmouth brewery.

Where to Stay in Portsmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Holiday Inn Portsmouth: Located within walking distance of Gunwharf Quays and Portsmouth Harbour, this modern hotel has spacious rooms and a restaurant on-site.

  2. YHA Portsmouth: A budget-friendly option for travelers. This hostel offers private rooms, dormitories, and a communal kitchen.

  3. The G Boutique Hotel: A luxurious boutique hotel located in the heart of Southsea, perfect for travelers who prefer a more upscale accommodation option.

What to Buy in Portsmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Nautical Souvenirs: Portsmouth is home to a large Naval base, and you can find various souvenirs featuring navy ships, anchors, and other nautical themes at local gift shops.

  2. Portsmouth Gin: Made by local distilleries, Portsmouth Gin is a great local product to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Portsmouth

Portsmouth has a rich maritime heritage and a welcoming atmosphere. The city is proud of its history and has a thriving arts and culture scene. Visitors are advised to follow standard British etiquette and safety precautions.

Best Time to Visit Portsmouth

The best time to visit Portsmouth is from late spring to early autumn when the weather is warm, and the city has a lively atmosphere. However, the city has plenty of indoor attractions that can be enjoyed throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portsmouth

Do:

  • Use ‘please’ and ‘thank you’ when interacting with locals.
  • Try the local beer and cider.
  • Be respectful of the city’s rich maritime history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets.
  • Touch or disturb the wildlife in natural parks.
  • Smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portsmouth

Portsmouth is a relatively safe city. However, visitors are advised to take the standard precautions when travelling such as being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas, keeping valuables safe, and not walking around alone at night in isolated areas.

Health in Portsmouth

The city has several medical centers and hospitals and visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance before traveling to Portsmouth.

Getting to Portsmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portsmouth

  1. Southampton Airport: Located about 15 miles away from Portsmouth, Southampton airport is the closest airport to Portsmouth.

  2. London Gatwick Airport: Located about 70 miles away from Portsmouth and accessible by taking a train or bus.

Bus Stations Near Portsmouth

  1. Portsmouth Bus Station: Located in the city center, this station is accessible by national and local bus services.

  2. Portsmouth Harbour Bus Station: Situated near Portsmouth Harbour and accessible by bus services from other major cities in the UK.

Other Options

Portsmouth is easily accessible by car and train, with excellent transport links from London and other major cities in the UK.

How to Get Around in Portsmouth

Portsmouth is a walkable city, and most attractions are within walking distance from each other. Visitors can also use public transportation services such as buses and trains, or rent bicycles or cars to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Portsmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Southampton: Home to the Titanic Museum and excellent shopping opportunities.

  2. Isle of Wight: An island off the south coast of England known for its stunning landscapes and adventures.

  3. Brighton: A popular seaside destination always bustling with fun activities.

Conclusion

From historic museums and natural attractions to delicious food and a welcoming atmosphere, Portsmouth is a must-visit destination for travelers. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Portsmouth with all the information you need to make your journey unforgettable.
