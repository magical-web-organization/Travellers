Introduction to Eastleigh

Eastleigh is a town located in the heart of Hampshire, just a short distance from Southampton and Winchester. The town is steeped in history, with roots dating back to the Saxon period. Nowadays, it is a lively and dynamic place, home to a plethora of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Eastleigh

Whether you are interested in exploring historical sites, experiencing the great outdoors, or indulging in the local food and drink scene, Eastleigh has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Eastleigh.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eastleigh Museum: Discover the history and heritage of Eastleigh through interactive exhibitions and artifacts.
  • The Point: A modern performing arts and culture venue, with a diverse range of events and workshops.
  • Netley Abbey: A picturesque ruin of an ancient abbey dating back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Eastleigh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Itchen Valley Country Park: A stunning collection of meadows, woodlands, and lakes, with walking trails and picnic areas.
  • Stoke Park Woods: A popular destination for bikers and hikers, with vast woodlands, streams, and wildlife.
  • Hiltingbury Lakes: A peaceful retreat with abundant wildlife, perfect for bird watching, fishing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastleigh

  • The Monarch’s Way: Follow the footsteps of King Charles II as he fled from the Battle of Worcester in 1651.
  • South Downs Way: A popular hiking trail that spans across the South of England, with breathtaking views of the countryside.
  • Meon Valley Trail: A scenic cycling and walking trail that follows the former Meon Valley railway line.

Best Things to Do in Eastleigh: Activities and Tours

  • TeamSport Go Karting: A thrilling indoor go-karting experience for adrenaline junkies.
  • Bursledon Brickworks: A unique museum that showcases the history of brickmaking in Hampshire.
  • The Steamship Shieldhall: An elegant steamship that offers leisurely cruises along the Solent and Southampton Water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastleigh

Day 1: Explore the town center, visit the Eastleigh Museum, and enjoy a meal at one of the local restaurants.

Day 2: Embrace the great outdoors and hike the South Downs Way, or head to Itchen Valley Country Park for a day of picnicking and relaxation.

Day 3: Discover the region’s rich aviation history and visit the Solent Sky Museum, or take a tour of the legendary Spitfire plane at the City of Winchester Trust.

Day 4: Indulge in the local food and drink scene and try some of the local dishes, from freshly caught fish and seafood to delectable Hampshire cheese.

Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the Meon Valley Trail or explore the stunning countryside at Stoke Park Woods.

Day 6: Take a leisurely cruise down the Solent and explore the stunning coastline.

Day 7: Experience the dynamic arts scene at The Point and catch a show or workshop.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastleigh

Eastleigh is known for its diverse and delicious culinary scene, with a range of restaurants and pubs that cater to every taste. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Eastleigh.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A quintessential British dish that is a must-try when visiting Eastleigh.
  • Hampshire Cheese: A deliciously creamy cheese that is made in the region and is perfect for pairing with wine or ale.
  • Southampton Pie: A savory pie filled with steak, mushrooms, onions, and ale, a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Ringwood Brewery Ales: Award-winning ales that are brewed locally, with a range of flavors to choose from, including Old Thumper and 49er.
  • Hampshire Sparkling Wine: A crisp and refreshing wine that is made in the region and is perfect for celebrating special occasions.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastleigh

The people of Eastleigh are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a rich cultural heritage that can be traced back to the Saxon period. The town is known for its strong community spirit, with a range of events and festivals that celebrate traditional customs and local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Eastleigh

The best time to visit Eastleigh is during the warmer months of May to September, when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, the town has something to offer all year round, with a range of indoor and outdoor activities and events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastleigh

Eastleigh is a safe and welcoming town, with low levels of crime and a strong community spirit. However, travelers are advised to take appropriate precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Eastleigh

The town has excellent medical facilities, including a range of hospitals and clinics. Travelers are advised to take out appropriate travel insurance and make sure they are up to date with their vaccinations.

Getting to Eastleigh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastleigh is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including air, train, and bus.

Airports Near Eastleigh

  • Southampton International Airport: Located just a short 10-minute drive from Eastleigh, with regular flights to a range of national and international destinations.

Train Stations Near Eastleigh

  • Eastleigh Train Station: Located in the heart of the town, with regular connections to London and other major cities in the UK.

Other Options

  • National Express Bus Services: Regular bus services run from London and other major cities to Eastleigh.
  • Car Hire: A range of car hire companies are located in and around the town, making it easy to explore the surrounding countryside at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Eastleigh

Eastleigh is a relatively small town and can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Alternatively, there are a range of bus services that connect the town to other major destinations in the region.

Where to Go Next from Eastleigh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eastleigh is located in the heart of Hampshire, with easy access to a range of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of our top recommendations.
