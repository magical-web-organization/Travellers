A Comprehensive Guide to Aldershot: Culturally Rich and Lush Green Surroundings

Aldershot is a wonderful town situated in Hampshire, South East England. It has outstanding natural beauty and is rich in history and heritage. The town offers a wide range of activities for outdoor enthusiasts, with its excellent hiking and biking routes, stunning scenic views, and lush green surroundings. This guide provides an overview of what Aldershot has to offer and how to best plan a trip for an enjoyable and memorable experience.

Introduction to Aldershot

Aldershot is a camp town founded during the Victorian era and developed into a flourishing community. The town is home to the British Army and is one of the largest military communities in the UK. Aldershot also hosts the Aldershot Garrison Sports Centre for athletes to train and practice for their events. The town has a rich cultural history, evident in its numerous museums, historical sites, stunning landscapes, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Aldershot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Army Medical Services Museum: It is the only museum in the world that portrays the history of Army medicine. Visitors can learn about the evolution of medicine, the role played by Army medics, and their valuable contribution to society. The museum exhibits the army’s medical equipment, medals, and uniforms, along with an interactive gallery and a short film projection. It is an ideal spot for both adults and children.

  2. Aldershot Military Cemetery: The cemetery is located near Aldershot Garrison’s entrance. It serves as a tribute to the soldiers who gave their lives in the First and Second World Wars. It is one of the most significant Commonwealth War Graves Cemeteries, with over one hundred graves of soldiers and their families.

Natural Attractions of Aldershot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hogs Back Brewery: If you are a beer enthusiast, you must visit this family-run brewery nestled in the lush green countryside of Tongham, Hampshire. The brewery has been crafting beer since the 1990s and is famous for its tantalizing local ales and hops. You can take a guided tour or simply head to the bar to taste some brewed beer.

  2. Hampshire and Surrey Canal: This beautiful waterway is a perfect spot for an early morning walk. It runs from the Basingstoke Canal in the west to the Wey Navigation in the east. The canal was once a busy transport route for goods, now it is an ideal spot for birdwatching, fishing, walking, and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aldershot

Aldershot offers some of the most fantastic hiking and biking trails in the UK. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced hiker/cyclist, you’ll find several trails with varying degrees of difficulty. Some popular routes include

  1. North Downs Way: The trail stretches from Farnham in Surrey to Canterbury in Kent, crossing stunning countryside and a few quaint villages. The route is suitable for all levels of walkers and cyclists, and you can expect to cover 153 miles of the rolling countryside.

  2. The Three Castles Path: The trail covers the area between Windsor and Winchester castles, passing through several beautiful spots such as Odiham Castle, Greywell Tunnel, the Basingstoke Canal, and many more.

Best Things to Do in Aldershot: Activities and Tours

Aldershot is full of fun activities, whether you are traveling with family or looking for some solo adventures. Here are some of the top activities and tours in Aldershot.

  1. Skydiving: The Ultimate Adrenaline Rush is available at the UK’s largest indoor skydiving center. Visitors can experience the thrill of free-fall without jumping from an aeroplane.

  2. Go-Karting: Unleash your inner racer at the TeamSport Indoor Go-Karting Track, located in Farnborough. Get behind the wheel and speed around their 500-metre circuit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aldershot

Day 1: Explore the military history of Aldershot, visiting the Army Medical Services Museum and Aldershot Military Cemetery.

Day 2: A leisurely stroll along the stunning Hampshire and Surrey Canal accompanied by an evening visit to the Hogs Back Brewery for some local ales.

Day 3: An adrenaline-fueled day out at Skydiving, followed by a relaxed evening at the Aldershot Lido.

Day 4: A day exploring the nearby village of Farnham, including stops at the Birdworld, Farnham Castle and Museum, and the Waverley Abbey.

Day 5: Head to the Alice Holt Forest for hiking, cycling, and a picnic amid scenic surroundings.

Day 6: Visit the buzzing Farnborough town situated a few miles away from Aldershot, home to the famous Farnborough Airshow, and plenty of restaurants and local shops to explore.

Day 7: Relax at the Aldershot Manor Park, featuring beautiful gardens, a playground for children, and an opportunity to have a picnic via the many benches spotted around the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Aldershot

The food and drink scene in Aldershot is diverse and vibrant, offering everything from traditional English dishes to exotic cuisines. Here are some popular dishes and local beverages you must try while in Aldershot.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish & Chips: A traditional British dish that is often served with mushy peas and tartar sauce, best enjoyed with a side of beer.

  2. Cherry Bakewell: A classic English pudding with sweet pastry, jam, and an almond-scented sponge cake, served with a dollop of cream or ice cream.

Local Beverages

  1. Gin: Hampshire is known for its locally brewed gins such as Twisted Nose & Bombay Sapphire, popular drinks to have at any local bar or pub.

  2. Local Ales: The Hogs Back Brewery offers many locally brewed, delicious ales that you must try.

Where to Stay in Aldershot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aldershot offers good accommodation options for visitors of any budget with several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The Aviator Hotel is a popular option situated near Farnborough, offering exquisite amenities and comfort, and a prime location.

What to Buy in Aldershot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While visiting Aldershot, you must take back some souvenirs and locally made products to cherish your memories of the place. The North Camp Market is a great spot to buy traditional and authentic artifacts, antique collections, and curiosities to decorate your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Aldershot

The locals of Aldershot are welcoming and friendly, and the town has been home to the military community. They respect and honor tradition and heritage, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful towards cultural traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Aldershot

Aldershot has a temperate maritime climate, with mild temperature fluctuations throughout the year. The ideal time to visit is from late May to early September when the weather is sunny and dry. However, if you want to experience a bit of cold and snow, visit during the winter months of December and January.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aldershot

Do:

  • Respect cultural traditions and customs while visiting any historical or cultural sight.
  • Take some time to explore the stunning natural beauty of Aldershot by going for hikes and bikes.
  • Visit the local market to purchase authentic souvenirs and curiosities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the serene natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the cultural or religious sentiments of others.
  • Travel alone in the dark in any unknown neighborhood.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aldershot

Aldershot is a relatively safe town to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions such as locking car doors, avoiding wandering alone in the dark and staying alert in crowded areas.

Health in Aldershot

Aldershot has good medical facilities, and visitors are advised to keep contact details handy in emergencies. Be careful to stay hydrated while participating in physical activities such as hiking or biking.

Getting to Aldershot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aldershot is well-connected with several transport options available for visitors. Travelers can access the town via several airports near Aldershot, such as Heathrow, Gatwick, and Southampton. Bus stations near Aldershot include the Aldershot Bus Station and Farnborough Interchange Station. Additionally, other travel alternatives such as hiring or renting cars or bikes can provide easy access to explore the town.

How to Get Around in Aldershot

Aldershot is a small town, and it is easy to travel around. Walking is the best way to enjoy the scenic beauty, and there is also an excellent bus service available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Aldershot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aldershot serves as an ideal base to explore the Hampshire region with several popular destinations within reach such as:

  1. Farnborough: A buzzing town situated a few miles from Aldershot, known for its famous aviation hub and a lively food and drink scene.

  2. Farnham: A charming market town with an eclectic mix of independent shops, restaurants and home to the Farnham Castle and Birdworld.

Conclusion

Aldershot offers plenty of cultural and natural experiences to visitors looking for an excellent escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life. The town’s rich cultural history combined with stunning natural beauty makes it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture lovers. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you plan a memorable and delightful visit to Aldershot.
