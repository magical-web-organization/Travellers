Introduction to New Forest

Located in Hampshire, South East England, the New Forest is a popular destination known for its beautiful scenery, outdoor adventures, and rich history. Established as a royal hunting ground by William the Conqueror over 900 years ago, the New Forest has since become a designated National Park, attracting visitors from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through a list of places to visit in New Forest, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Forest

Day 1: Arrival and check into your accommodation. Spend the day exploring Lyndhurst, the ‘capital’ of the New Forest.

Day 2: Head to the coast and visit Hurst Castle, before taking a ferry to the Isle of Wight.

Day 3: Go on a guided hike or bike tour of the New Forest, taking in the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Visit Exbury Gardens and Furzey Gardens for a relaxing day amidst beautiful surroundings.

Day 5: Explore the cultural attractions of the New Forest, including Beaulieu Abbey and Palace House.

Day 6: Enjoy a day of outdoor activities, such as horse riding or watersports.

Day 7: Departure

Local Culture and Customs of New Forest

The New Forest is a diverse area with a number of cultural customs. Visitors should respect these customs and rules, such as:

  • Keeping to the designated paths to avoid damaging protected wildlife
  • Being respectful of the free-roaming animals
  • Taking care not to litter or damage the natural environment

Best Time to Visit New Forest

The New Forest can be visited year-round, but the best times are during the spring and summer months, when the weather is warm and the flora is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Forest

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in the New Forest
  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife
  • Take care when driving on the narrow roads

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or damage the natural environment
  • Approach or feed the free-roaming animals
  • Park outside designated parking areas

Safety and Health

Safety in New Forest

The New Forest is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take care when hiking or biking in the area, especially in remote areas.

Health in New Forest

Healthcare in the New Forest consists of a number of hospitals, clinics, and general practitioner surgeries.
