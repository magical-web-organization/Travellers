Introduction to Burley

Burley is a charming village located in the heart of the New Forest National Park in Hampshire, South East England. The village is surrounded by ancient woodlands, open heaths, and picturesque countryside, making it the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Burley is also steeped in history and cultural attractions, offering visitors a glimpse into the area’s rich past.

List of Places to Visit in Burley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The village of Burley has a rich history that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. Some of the notable attractions include:

  • Burley Manor Museum: This museum showcases the history of the local manor, which dates back to the 13th century. It includes exhibits on the area’s agriculture, wildlife, and folklore.
  • The Wagon House: This historical site is located in the heart of the village and is one of the oldest buildings in Burley. It has been beautifully restored and currently contains exhibits on the area’s history and culture.
  • St. John the Baptist Church: This beautiful church dates back to the 12th century and features stunning architecture and stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Burley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burley is known for its breathtaking natural scenery and outdoor adventures. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Burley Park: This picturesque park is surrounded by ancient woodlands and features scenic walking and biking trails, picnic areas, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Avon Valley Path: This scenic trail follows the River Avon and offers stunning views of the countryside and local wildlife.
  • New Forest National Park: This expansive park is home to a variety of rare species and habitats including ancient woodlands, heaths, and grasslands. Visitors can explore the park on foot, by bike, or by horseback.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burley

Burley offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and opportunities to explore the area’s natural beauty. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The Burley Circular Walk: This 5.5-mile circular trail takes visitors through ancient woodlands, open heathlands, and picturesque countryside.
  • The Castleman Trailway: This picturesque trail follows the old Castleman railway line and offers stunning views of the area’s ancient woodlands, countryside, and wildlife.
  • Avon Valley Path: This scenic trail follows the River Avon and offers stunning views of the countryside and local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Burley: Activities and Tours

Burley offers a variety of activities and tours that are perfect for all ages. Some of the best things to do in Burley include:

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can take a guided horseback ride through the ancient woodlands and countryside.
  • Bird Watching: Burley is home to a variety of rare and exotic bird species, which can be spotted on guided bird watching tours.
  • Segway Tours: Visitors can explore the area’s natural beauty on a guided Segway tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burley

Day 1: Visit Burley Manor Museum and St. John the Baptist Church followed by a scenic walk through Burley Park.

Day 2: Explore the Castleman Trailway by bike or on foot. Stop for a picnic lunch and take in the scenic views.

Day 3: Take a guided horseback ride through the ancient woodlands and countryside.

Day 4: Visit The Wagon House and learn about the area’s history and culture.

Day 5: Follow the Avon Valley Path and enjoy stunning views of the river and countryside.

Day 6: Take a guided bird watching tour to spot rare and exotic bird species in the area.

Day 7: Explore the New Forest National Park on foot or by bike. Stop for a picnic lunch and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Burley

Burley is known for its delicious local cuisine and beverages. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hampshire rare breed steak: Sourced locally and served in many of Burley’s pubs and restaurants.
  • New Forest venison: A locally sourced dish that is popular among visitors and locals.
  • Cream tea: A classic British treat that consists of tea served with scones, jam, and clotted cream.

Local Beverages

  • Ringwood Brewery Beer: A locally brewed beer that is available in many of Burley’s pubs.
  • New Forest Cider: A refreshing cider that is made from locally sourced apples.

Where to Stay in Burley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burley offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors ranging from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Burley Manor: A luxurious hotel that is set in a stunning manor house and surrounded by landscaped gardens and ancient woodlands.
  • The White Buck: A cozy and welcoming pub that also offers comfortable guest rooms.
  • The Burley Inn: A historic pub that features cozy rooms and a bustling restaurant.

What to Buy in Burley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burley offers a variety of unique and locally made souvenirs and products that are perfect for bringing home. Some of the best items to buy include:

  • New Forest preserves: Delicious preserves made from locally sourced fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Burley

Burley is steeped in history and has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its many museums, historical sites, and traditions. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • The Burley Fair: A traditional fair that is held annually and features traditional carnival games, rides, and local crafts.
  • Pannage: A traditional practice in which pigs are allowed to forage for food in the New Forest’s woodlands.

Best Time to Visit Burley

The best time to visit Burley is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny. The New Forest National Park is also at its most beautiful during this time of the year.
