Introduction to Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst is a charming historic town in Hampshire, South East England. Located in the heart of the New Forest National Park, it is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes and boasts a rich cultural heritage. The town is perfect for those who love outdoor activities, history, and English countryside charm. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through some of the best things to see and do in Lyndhurst.

List of Places to Visit in Lyndhurst

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lyndhurst has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites that preserve and showcase this heritage. Some of the must-visit sites include:

  • New Forest Museum and Visitor Centre: located in the heart of the town, this museum captures the history, ecology, and culture of the New Forest through interactive exhibits, films, and artefacts.
  • Bolton’s Bench: this hilltop viewpoint offers stunning panoramic views of the town and the surrounding countryside. The site also has a historical significance, as it was used as a meeting place for the Verderer’s Court, which was responsible for managing the forest.
  • St. Michael and All Angels Church: this historic church dates back to the 13th century and is a great example of Gothic architecture. Inside, you can see beautiful stained glass windows and intricate wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Lyndhurst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Besides its historical significance, Lyndhurst is also known for its stunning natural landscapes and abundant wildlife.

  • New Forest National Park: covering more than 200 square miles, this national park is a nature lover’s paradise. You can take long walks, go scenic drives, and spot a wide variety of animals, including ponies, deer, and birds.
  • Bolderwood Deer Sanctuary: located a few miles outside the town, this sanctuary is home to wild deer herds and offers an opportunity to feed them and observe their behaviour.
  • Rufus Stone: according to legend, this ancient stone marks the spot where King William II was killed by an arrow while hunting in the New Forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst and the New Forest offer several hiking and biking trails for people of all fitness levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Lyndhurst-Emery Down-Castle Malwood Loop: this easy 6-mile loop takes you through the woods, past a historic war memorial, and through the pretty village of Emery Down.
  • Burley Railway Walk: a flat and easy 3-mile walk along an old railway line between the village of Burley and Holmsley in the New Forest. Perfect for families and beginners.
  • Brockenhurst Circular Walk: a moderate 5-mile loop through heathlands and forests, starting and ending in the charming village of Brockenhurst.

Best Things to Do in Lyndhurst: Activities and Tours

Lyndhurst offers a wide range of fun activities and tours to people of all ages. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Horse riding: explore the beauty of the New Forest on horseback with one of the many riding schools in the area.
  • Canoeing and kayaking: the Beaulieu River offers calm waters, stunning scenery, and a chance to spot wildlife from a different perspective.
  • Lyndhurst Guided Walk: join a guided walk around the town and learn about its fascinating history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst has a variety of options when it comes to food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New Forest Venison: sustainably hunted deer meat, served in various forms such as sausages, burgers, and steaks.
  • Hampshire smoked trout: the local smoked trout is a delicacy and can be enjoyed in salads, sandwiches, or on its own.
  • Cream tea: a classic English afternoon treat, consisting of scones, clotted cream, and jam, served with tea.

Local Beverages

  • Ringwood Brewery beer: a family-run brewery that produces a range of English-style ales, including the popular Old Thumper.
  • New Forest cider: made from locally grown apples, the New Forest cider is a refreshing drink that can be enjoyed on its own or with food.
  • Marlings Vineyard wine: located just outside the town, this small vineyard produces high-quality English wines that are gaining popularity.

Where to Stay in Lyndhurst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lyndhurst offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  • The Crown Manor House Hotel: a historic hotel in the town centre that offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Forest Lodge Hotel: a family-run hotel set in a tranquil location, surrounded by nature. The hotel has a restaurant and a bar, and it is loved by nature lovers and hikers.
  • Lyndhurst House B&B: a cosy and affordable B&B in the town centre, with friendly hosts and clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Lyndhurst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Lyndhurst has a lot to offer. Here are some local products to bring back home:

  • New Forest honey: produced by local beekeepers, the New Forest honey is a sweet and natural treat.
  • Local crafts: Lyndhurst has many independent shops that sell local crafts, such as pottery, glassware, and jewellery.
  • New Forest products: you can find a range of products made from locally sourced ingredients, such as jams, chutneys, and sauces.

Local Culture and Customs of Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst has a unique culture and customs that reflect its history and natural heritage. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the natural environment: the New Forest is a special place that requires attention and respect from visitors. Make sure to follow the guidelines and rules set by the national park authority, and avoid disturbing wildlife or damaging plants and trees.
  • Appreciate the history and culture: Lyndhurst and the New Forest have a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. Take the time to visit museums, historical sites, and local events, and learn about the town’s past and present.
  • Embrace the local lifestyle: Lyndhurst is a small town with a friendly and welcoming community. Take the time to chat with locals, enjoy the food and drink scene, and experience the unique rhythm of life in the New Forest.

Best Time to Visit Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst is a great destination to visit year-round, as each season brings its unique charm. However, some of the best times to visit include:

  • Spring (March to May): as the flowers and trees start to bloom, the New Forest becomes a vivid and colourful place. This is also a good time to spot newborn animals and birds.
  • Summer (June to August): the warm and sunny weather makes it an ideal time for hiking, biking, and outdoor activities. This is also the peak tourist season, so expect more crowds and higher prices.
  • Autumn (September to November): this is the time when the leaves change their colour, creating a stunning canopy of reds, oranges, and yellows. It is also a good time to spot deer during their mating season.
  • Winter (December to February): although the weather can be cold and wet, winter in the New Forest has a magical atmosphere, with frost-covered landscapes, cosy pubs, and festive events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lyndhurst

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and follow the guidelines set by the national park authority.
  • Take the time to chat with locals and learn about their lives and culture.
  • Enjoy the variety of outdoor activities and tours that are available in the area.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or damage the plants and trees in the New Forest.
  • Ignore the traffic rules and park in unauthorised areas.
  • Litter or leave behind any waste while hiking or camping.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst is a safe town, with low levels of crime and a friendly community. However, visitors should be aware of some safety measures, such as:

  • Taking care when driving on the narrow roads in the New Forest, especially during peak tourist season.
  • Wearing appropriate clothing and footwear when hiking or biking, and carrying plenty of water and snacks.
  • Being aware of the potential risks of wildlife encounters, such as ticks and adders.

Health in Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst has a good healthcare system, with several medical and dental clinics in the town. Visitors are advised to:

  • Have proper travel health insurance that covers accidents, illnesses, and emergency medical treatment.
  • Take precautions against tick bites, which can carry Lyme disease and other infections.
  • Carry any necessary medication or prescriptions, and check if they are legal in the UK.

Getting to Lyndhurst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lyndhurst is easily accessible by car, bus, or train, and there are several options to travel around the town and the New Forest.

Airports Near Lyndhurst

The nearest international airports to Lyndhurst are:

  • Southampton Airport (18 miles)
  • Bournemouth Airport (20 miles)
  • Gatwick Airport (76 miles)

Bus Stations Near Lyndhurst

The main bus station in Lyndhurst is located in the town centre, and it offers regular services to nearby towns and villages, as well as to Southampton and Bournemouth.

Other Options

Lyndhurst is also accessible by train, with the nearest station located in Ashurst (5 miles away). There are also several taxi and shuttle services available from the airports and train stations.

How to Get Around in Lyndhurst

Lyndhurst and the New Forest can be explored on foot, by bike, or by car. The town centre is relatively small and can be easily walked, while the national park offers several scenic drives and cycling routes. However, visitors are advised to take care when driving on the narrow and winding roads, especially during peak tourist season.

Where to Go Next from Lyndhurst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lyndhurst is a great base to explore the beauty and diversity of South East England. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Lymington: a picturesque coastal town with a lively marina and a range of shops and restaurants.
  • Isle of Wight: a ferry ride away from Southampton or Lymington, the Isle of Wight offers stunning beaches, scenic walks, and historical sites.
  • Winchester: the ancient capital of England, Winchester has a rich history and culture, with a stunning cathedral, museums, and galleries.

Conclusion

Lyndhurst is a hidden gem in South East England, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, you’ll find something to love in this charming town. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Lyndhurst and discover all its hidden treasures.
