Introduction to Petersfield

Located in the heart of Hampshire, Petersfield is a picturesque market town that boasts a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural beauty, and a plethora of interesting attractions. Surrounded by rolling hills and dense woodlands, the town is an ideal destination for those who seek tranquility, history, and adventure.

In this guide, we will take you on a tour of Petersfield and help you discover the best things to do, see, eat, and drink in the town. From museums and historical sites to scenic views and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in Petersfield. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Petersfield

Here are some of the top places to visit in Petersfield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Petersfield Museum: A fascinating museum that tells the story of Petersfield and its people, from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • Hinton Ampner House and Gardens: A magnificent National Trust property that boasts stunning gardens, a historic house, and a collection of fine art and furniture.
  • The Flora Twort Gallery: A charming art gallery that showcases the work of local artists and sculptors.

Natural Attractions of Petersfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South Downs National Park: A vast national park that offers endless opportunities for hiking, cycling, and horse riding, as well as stunning vistas of the natural scenery.
  • Butser Hill: The highest point in the South Downs, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Queen Elizabeth Country Park: A 2000-acre park with hiking trails, cycling routes, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petersfield

Petersfield is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous trails and routes for hiking, biking, and horse riding. Here are some of the best ones to try out:

  • The South Downs Way: A 100-mile trail that runs from Winchester to Eastbourne, passing through Petersfield on the way.
  • The Hangers Way: A 21-mile trail that takes in some of the most beautiful countryside in Hampshire, including the South Downs National Park.

Best Things to Do in Petersfield: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Petersfield. Here are some of the best:

  • Petersfield Open Air Pool: A seasonal outdoor swimming pool that is perfect for a refreshing dip on a hot summer day.
  • The Goodwood Estate: A historic estate that offers a range of activities and events throughout the year, from horse racing to vintage car shows.
  • Petersfield Ghost Tour: An eerie tour of Petersfield’s most haunted locations, led by an expert guide.

Where to Stay in Petersfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Petersfield has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best:

  • The Angel Hotel: A historic hotel in the heart of Petersfield, with luxurious rooms and fine dining.
  • The Half Moon Inn: A cosy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a traditional pub.
  • The Old Drum: A charming B&B with beautifully decorated rooms and a warm welcome.

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Petersfield include:

  • Local cheeses and meats
  • Arts and crafts from the Flora Twort Gallery
  • Sussex fruit wines and Hampshire beers

Local Culture and Customs of Petersfield

Petersfield has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong tradition of arts, crafts, and literary pursuits. Some of the local customs and traditions to look out for include:

  • The annual Petersfield Literary Festival
  • The Petersfield Arts and Crafts Society
  • The Petersfield Shakespeare Festival

Best Time to Visit Petersfield

Petersfield can be visited all year round, but the best time to come is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is in full bloom. The town is also busy during the Petersfield Musical Festival in September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Petersfield

Do:

  • Explore the South Downs National Park
  • Visit the Flora Twort Gallery
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Be disrespectful to the town’s cultural heritage
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Petersfield

Petersfield is generally a safe town, but visitors should take usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Petersfield

Visitors to Petersfield should have no issues with their health as there are many medical centers in Petersfield. People need to take care of themselves accordingly and take the required medications if necessary.

Getting to Petersfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Petersfield is well-connected to the rest of the UK by road and rail.

Airports Near Petersfield

The nearest airport to Petersfield is Southampton Airport, which is about 25 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Petersfield

Petersfield has a bus station located in the town center, with services to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can hire a car or take a taxi from nearby cities and towns, such as Portsmouth or Chichester.

How to Get Around in Petersfield

Petersfield is a small town, and most of its attractions can be reached on foot. Alternatively, visitors can hire a bike or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Petersfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Petersfield is located within easy reach of many other interesting towns and cities in the South of England. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Winchester: A historic cathedral town that is home to one of England’s oldest cathedrals and a range of interesting museums and galleries.
  • Chichester: A picturesque market town that boasts a rich history, stunning architecture, and a vibrant arts scene.
  • Portsmouth: A bustling port city that offers plenty of attractions, including the historic dockyard, museums, and galleries.
