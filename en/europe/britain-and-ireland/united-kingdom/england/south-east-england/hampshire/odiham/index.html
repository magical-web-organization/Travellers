Introduction to Odiham

Located in Hampshire, South East England, Odiham is a charming village town that offers a perfect blend of rural and urban lifestyles. The village is steeped in history, dating back to the 13th century, when it was established as a market town by King John. Today, Odiham is popular among tourists for its stunning natural beauty, antique shops, museums, and historic sites. In this guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about visiting Odiham, including the best places to visit, things to do and see, and tips for traveling to the area.

List of Places to Visit in Odiham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Odiham is home to several museums and historical places that shed light on the rich cultural and historical heritage of the village. A must-visit on any traveler’s itinerary is the Odiham Castle, a historic ruin that dates back to the late 12th century, sitting on a green expanse of land. Additionally, the Odiham Cottage Hospital Museum provides insights into the evolution of healthcare services, featuring antique medical equipment and tools. The church of All Saints serves as an example of the village’s heritage and evolution, hosting a variety of events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Odiham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts are sure to find Odiham to be a paradise of natural wonders. Visitors can walk along the towpath of Basingstoke Canal and enjoy scenic views of waterways and wildlife. The Odiham Common nature reserve is a vast expanse of woodland and heathland, which is home to numerous bird species and colorful wildflowers. At the same time, The Four Lanes End bird-watching hide on the common is perfect for birdwatchers looking for sightings of birds of prey, such as buzzards, kestrels, and peregrine falcons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Odiham

The Nately Scures walking trail is a moderate-level hiking and biking route that travels through the beautiful countryside of Odiham, passing through the meadows and woods and offers picturesque views of the village and the surrounding area. Additionally, the Basingstoke Canal towpath provides a fantastic opportunity to explore the local area on foot or by bike, with several stop-offs points that offer a range of cafes, pubs, and restaurants.

Best Things to Do in Odiham: Activities and Tours

A hot air balloon ride over the surrounding countryside is a must-do for anyone visiting Odiham. There is nothing quite like the stunning views and complete quiet of floating high in the sky, taking in the scenery from a unique perspective. An afternoon spent shopping in the village’s antique and boutique shops is also highly recommended. Visitors can explore the vintage finds at the High Street’s Antiques Emporium or browse the quirky boutique shops on The Bury.

Week-Long Itinerary for Odiham

For those who have the luxury of time, a week in Odiham can be rewarding. You can set aside a day to see the prominent Odiham Castle ruins, which dates back to the late 12th century, three other days for the museums, bird watching, and hiking or biking. A balloon experience can be arranged for the fourth day to give you a fantastic view of the village from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Odiham

Must-Try Dishes

One of Odiham’s most beloved dishes is the hearty steak and ale pie prepared in local pubs, using the freshest locally sourced ingredients. The Scottish highland beef cottage pie made with local Havard’s Farm beef, onions, and mushrooms served with greens and hand-cut chips is worth a try.

Local Beverages

Beer lovers can head to Tilly Shaw’s Microbrewery to enjoy the finest range of locally brewed beers produced using traditional recipes and techniques. The vineyards in the surrounding village yield excellent wines and offer tours and tastings at the Black Chalk Wine Estate.

Where to Stay in Odiham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation that cater to everyone’s budget in Odiham. The elegant Georgian hotel, Bel and the Dragon is a stylish hostelry that offers comfortable rooms, fine dining, and a rustic atmosphere. The contemporary styled Red Lion Hotel is conveniently located in the village center on the high street and has comfortable lodgings. For budget travelers, the rustic charm of the quaint Angel Inn offers affordable accommodation with essential amenities.

What to Buy in Odiham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Odiham is an excellent place to buy antiques, unique vintage finds, and rare collectibles. The Antiques Emporium on the High Street is the perfect place to browse through a vast selection of artifacts, including vintage clothing, jewelry, and antique furniture. At Coffee 1, you can purchase locally roasted coffee in reusable containers, and at the Odiham Cottage Hospital Museum, you can buy a range of gifts and souvenirs, from postcards to prints and maps.

Local Culture and Customs of Odiham

The village town’s strong sense of community is an essential aspect of its culture. Odiham hosts numerous festivals throughout the year, celebrating the village’s heritage, culture, and local produce. At the annual Odiham Food Festival, foodies can enjoy the freshest local ingredients prepared in novel and traditional ways.

Best Time to Visit Odiham

The village town enjoys a temperate climate, with mild winters and warm summers, making it a perfect destination throughout the year. The summer months(June to August) offer long days and warm weather, making it the popular tourist season. Autumn and spring will provide better hiking weather as the weather cools down.

Do’s and Don’ts of Odiham

Do:

  • Visit Odiham Castle and the Odiham Common Nature Reserve during your visit.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon tour over the town’s picturesque countryside.
  • Participate in the community events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural reserve
  • Forget to support local businesses such as cafes, bars, and souvenir shops.
  • Disrespect any monuments or landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Odiham

Odiham is generally a safe place for tourists. Visitors should take care of their belongings in crowded areas and avoid leaving valuables visible in parked cars. The local emergency number is 999.

Health in Odiham

Odiham benefits from a well-functioning healthcare sector and visitors should be sure to have their medical insurance in effect. The Odiham Cottage Hospital is a General Practitioner’s surgery that can offer necessary medical assistance.

Getting to Odiham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Odiham

The nearest airports to Odiham are Heathrow Airport and Southampton Airport, which are both approximately 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Odiham

Odiham is well-connected with the national bus route through a nearby fleet of bus stations in Alton, Farnborough, and Basingstoke.

Driving a Car

Visitors driving to Odiham can use the M3 and A287 for the nearest approach.

How to Get Around in Odiham

Walking is the most recommended way of getting around in the village because of its size. Bikes are available to hire through Odiham’s local bike shop, and it’s a recommended means of exploring the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Odiham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The village town is in close proximity to other famous towns in Hampshire, including Hartley Wintney, Fleet, and Hook. Visitors can also journey to the popular town of Basingstoke, which lies less than 7 miles away, and Andover, which is less than 30 miles away.

Conclusion

Odiham is a picturesque village town that is full of natural beauty, culture, and history. Visitors are sure to enjoy their trip to Odiham and its surrounding countryside with its array of indoor, outdoor, and cultural experiences that await them. This comprehensive guide has hopefully made trip planning to Odiham an easier and more enjoyable experience, offering detailed information to help you make the most of your visit.
