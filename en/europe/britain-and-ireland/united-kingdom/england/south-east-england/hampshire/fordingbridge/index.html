Complete Travel Guide to Fordingbridge, UK

Nestled in the heart of the New Forest, Fordingbridge is a small town in Hampshire, South East England. Known for its winding streets, charming local pubs and scenic landscapes, this hidden gem offers the perfect mix of natural beauty and cultural attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on an in-depth tour of Fordingbridge, exploring its rich history, scenic spots, hiking and biking routes, local cuisine and more.

Introduction to Fordingbridge

Located on the banks of River Avon, Fordingbridge is a picturesque town that dates back to the 13th century. The town’s name comes from the ancient Fording Brugge, which was once a crossing point over the river. Today, the town is a popular destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, offering a range of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Fordingbridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fordingbridge Museum: Located in the town’s former workhouse, the Fordingbridge Museum offers a fascinating insight into the town’s rich history. From tales of local smugglers to exhibitions on the Second World War, there is something for everyone here.
  • St Mary’s Church: Built in the 12th century, St Mary’s Church is one of the town’s oldest and most iconic buildings. With its beautiful stained glass windows and peaceful atmosphere, it’s a must-visit for anyone interested in history and architecture.
  • Breamore House: Just a short drive from Fordingbridge, Breamore House is a magnificent Elizabethan manor house that dates back to the 16th century. With its grand architecture and stunning gardens, it’s a great day out for the whole family.

Natural Attractions of Fordingbridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The New Forest: Covering over 200 square miles, the New Forest is a haven for wildlife, nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With its ancient woodlands, tranquil rivers and heathland, it offers a range of activities and experiences for visitors of all ages, from walking and cycling to horse riding and wildlife watching.
  • Rockbourne Roman Villa: Just a short drive from Fordingbridge, Rockbourne Roman Villa is one of the best-preserved Roman villas in the country. With its intricate mosaic floors and fascinating artefacts, it’s a must-visit for anyone interested in Roman history.
  • River Avon: Running through the heart of Fordingbridge, the River Avon is a tranquil and picturesque waterway that offers some of the best views in the town. From peaceful walks along its banks to river cruises and fishing trips, there’s something for everyone to enjoy here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fordingbridge

  • Avon Valley Path: A 34-mile trail that follows the course of the River Avon, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Castle Hill: A challenging hike that takes you to the top of Castle Hill, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the town and surrounding landscape.
  • Cycling in the New Forest: With its quiet country lanes and scenic landscapes, the New Forest is a great place to explore on two wheels. Whether you’re a seasoned cyclist or a beginner, there are plenty of routes to choose from.

Best Things to Do in Fordingbridge: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing on the River Avon: A great way to explore the town from a different perspective, canoeing on the River Avon offers a unique and unforgettable experience.
  • Horse Riding in the New Forest: Whether you’re an experienced rider or a beginner, there are plenty of opportunities to go horse riding in the New Forest. With its wild ponies and beautiful landscapes, it’s a great way to explore the area.
  • Beer Tasting at Ringwood Brewery: Located just a short drive from Fordingbridge, Ringwood Brewery offers a range of award-winning beers and ales that are sure to delight beer lovers of all tastes.

Local Culture and Customs of Fordingbridge

Fordingbridge has a rich history and cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the town. From the traditional Morris dancers that perform at local festivals to the local food and drink scene that showcases the best of Hampshire’s produce, there is always something to discover and enjoy here.

Best Time to Visit Fordingbridge

The best time to visit Fordingbridge is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. However, the town is beautiful all year round, with its autumnal colours and cosy winter atmosphere offering a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors.

Getting to Fordingbridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fordingbridge

The nearest airport to Fordingbridge is Southampton Airport, which is located approximately 20 miles away. Other nearby airports include Bournemouth Airport and London Heathrow Airport.

Bus Stations Near Fordingbridge

The nearest bus station to Fordingbridge is located in Ringwood, which is approximately 5 miles away. From there, regular bus services are available to Fordingbridge.

Other Options

Fordingbridge is easily accessible by car, with major routes including the A338 and the A31 running nearby. Alternatively, trains run regularly from London Waterloo to nearby stations such as Salisbury and Southampton, where visitors can then catch a connecting bus or taxi to Fordingbridge.

How to Get Around in Fordingbridge

Fordingbridge is a small town best explored on foot or by bike. There are also regular bus services available to nearby towns and attractions, and taxis are readily available for those who prefer to travel by car.
