Introduction to Hampshire

Hampshire, located on the southern coast of England, is a county filled with stunning natural landscapes, historic towns, and vibrant cities. It is known for its rolling hills, picturesque countryside, and quaint villages. Hampshire is also home to some of the most iconic attractions in England such as Winchester Cathedral, Stonehenge, and the New Forest National Park.

Best Things to Do in Hampshire: Activities and Tours

Local Culture and Customs of Hampshire

Hampshire has a rich heritage and unique local culture that visitors should experience. Some cultural traditions include:

  • Morris Dancing
  • Punting in Winchester
  • The Romsey and Mayoralty Show
  • Knighthood ceremonies in Winchester

Best Time to Visit Hampshire

The best time to visit Hampshire is during the summer months from June through August when the weather is warm and pleasant. If you want to avoid the crowds, visiting in the shoulder seasons of May and September can also offer a great experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hampshire

Do:

  • Try local foods and cider
  • Visit the historic towns and cities
  • Explore the natural beauty of the New Forest and South Downs
  • Attend local events and festivals
  • Follow the countryside code when hiking or biking

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions
  • Litter in the countryside
  • Park illegally
  • Overfeed the New Forest ponies

Safety and Health

Safety in Hampshire

Hampshire is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be cautious when traveling alone at night or in secluded areas, especially in the city centers.

Health in Hampshire

Medical facilities are easily accessible throughout Hampshire, and visitors should have no problem finding care if needed. Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as well as comfortable walking shoes for exploring the region.
