Introduction to Ringwood

Nestled in the western border of the New Forest National Park, Ringwood is a historic market town in Hampshire, UK. It dates back to the Anglo-Saxon era and was once a hub of the local agricultural industry. Today, it is a charming town that attracts travelers with its picturesque countryside, vibrant food scene, and outdoor activities.

In this comprehensive guide to Ringwood, we’ll explore the best things to do, see, and eat in this quaint town located in Hampshire, UK.

List of Places to Visit in Ringwood

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historical sites, or cultural attractions, Ringwood has plenty to offer. Below is a list of the top places to visit in Ringwood:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ringwood Museum: Housed in the old school building, this museum showcases the history of Ringwood from the early days to the present.
  • The Parish Church of St. Peter and St. Paul: A beautiful 13th-century church located in the heart of the town, which played a significant role in the history of Ringwood.
  • The Furlong Shopping Centre: If you’re interested in shopping, this is the best place to be.

Natural Attractions of Ringwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • New Forest National Park: Home to an array of wildlife and the perfect destination for nature lovers who enjoy hiking, cycling, or horse riding.
  • Moors Valley Country Park: A popular leisure park which includes both a play and a travel trail, and offers an abundance of outdoor activities including cycling, ziplining, and treetop adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ringwood

Ringwood boasts of idyllic walking and biking routes that take you through dense forests and breathtaking views. You can:

  • Explore the Avon Valley Path which starts in Salisbury and ends in Christchurch. The route takes you through idyllic countryside, picturesque villages, and panoramic views.
  • Walk through the Ringwood Forest, where you may encounter ancient oaks and conifers that date back centuries. The walks you through undulating terrain that is sure to excite you.

Best Things to Do in Ringwood: Activities and Tours

From picnicking in the park to high-flying adventure sports, Ringwood has plenty of things to keep you entertained. Below are some of the best things to do in Ringwood:

  • Visit the New Forest Water Park and indulge in activities like wakeboarding, paddleboarding, and kayaking.
  • Enjoy a peaceful picnic in the Moors Valley Country Park, a delightful spot with beautiful countryside views and activities for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ringwood

Day 1: Indulge in the scenic beauty of the New Forest National Park.

Day 2: Visit the Ringwood Museum to get an insight into the history of Ringwood.

Day 3: Explore Moors Valley Country Park, a popular outdoor destination filled with fun activities.

Day 4: Take a walk through the Ringwood Forest and experience the tranquil beauty of this ancient woodland.

Day 5: Go shopping for souvenirs at the Furlong Shopping Center.

Day 6: Keep your adrenalin-fueled as you indulge in the high-flying sports activities at the New Forest Water Park.

Day 7: End your trip on a high by indulging in the local food scene of Ringwood, by visiting a few of their locally-owned and run restaurants and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Ringwood

Ringwood has a vibrant food scene with plenty of restaurants, cafes, and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ringwood Ale-Pie: This savory pie is made with Ringwood Brewery’s beer and filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables, it has a rich and delicious flavor.
  • New Forest Wild Venison: The venison is sourced from the New Forest National Park, and can be found as a specialty in some of the local restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Ringwood Brewery Ale: Founded in 1978, the Ringwood Brewery produces ales that are renowned throughout the UK.

Where to Stay in Ringwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury or budget accommodation, Ringwood has something to suit every traveler’s needs. Below are some of the best places to stay in Ringwood:

  • The Original White Hart: A chic and inviting 15th-century inn hotel, offering spacious and comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  • The Lamb Inn: Situated in the heart of the town center, this Inn is the perfect place for travelers looking for traditional accommodation with a modern twist.

  • Little Forest Lodge: Located on the outskirts of Ringwood in the New Forest, this is an ideal place for those seeking a peaceful and tranquil atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ringwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

A trip to Ringwood isn’t complete without taking back souvenirs and local products for friends and family back home. You can find some of the best souvenirs and products at:

  • The Ringwood Antique and Decorative Arts Fair: Offering visitors a chance to buy some of the best antiques, decorative arts, and collectibles on the market.

  • The Furlong Shopping Center: A shopper’s paradise, with various specialty stores selling everything from clothing accessories to toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Ringwood

The town’s culture and customs are shaped by its historical past. One thing that stands out about Ringwood is its extraordinary festivals and community events. For instance, in May, residents come together, dressed in period costumes, to act out the Town’s historical events.

Best Time to Visit Ringwood

The best time to visit Ringwood is from May to September, when the weather is cozy and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ringwood

Do:

  • Respect the pristine countryside and wildlife of the New Forest National Park.
  • Visit the Ringwood Museum and explore the rich history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment or throw harmful substances in the waterways.
  • Disturb the Wildlife as this can do significant harm to the region’s animal kingdom.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ringwood

Ringwood is a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it’s wise to take precautions while exploring the area.

Health in Ringwood

Ringwood has several health centers, including private and National Health Service (NHS) providers.

Getting to Ringwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ringwood

The nearest international airports are London Heathrow (LHR) and Bournemouth Airport. Ringwood is approximately 95 miles from LHR airport and 8 miles from Bournemouth Airport, which takes around a 10-minute cab ride.

Bus Stations Near Ringwood

Ringwood bus station is located in the town center and has links to nearby towns and the surrounding attractions.

Other Options

You can hire a car, take a taxi or book a private transfer from either of the airports or your point of entry to the town.

How to Get Around in Ringwood

Ringwood is a small town and is easily navigable by walking, biking, or hopping on buses.

Where to Go Next from Ringwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The New Forest National Park is undoubtedly the major attraction, but Ringwood has some scenic and quaint towns and villages nearby that are worth exploring:

  • Lyndhurst: A picturesque town with charming shops and cafes.
  • Bournemouth: A bustling coastal city with a vibrant nightlife and an endless stretch of sandy beaches.

Conclusion

Ringwood is a hidden gem in the Hampshire region, with a rich history and culture, beautiful natural scenery, and a welcoming local community. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week-long trip, Ringwood promises to deliver unforgettable memories and experiences to last a lifetime.
