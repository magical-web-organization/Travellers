Introduction to Fleet

Nestled in the heart of Hampshire, Fleet offers a welcoming and charming experience in South East England. The town is easily accessible by train or car and offers a diverse range of attractions, from historic museums to natural outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Fleet

Whether you prefer culturally rich attractions or outdoor recreation, Fleet has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit places in Fleet:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fleet Pond Nature Reserve: With over 52 acres of heathland and woodland, Fleet Pond is the largest freshwater lake in Hampshire and is home to diverse wildlife. You can enjoy the scenic views on a walk or cycle around the lake.

  • The Gurkha Museum: This museum exhibits the history and culture of the Gurkhas – renowned soldiers from Nepal who have served in the British Army for over 200 years.

  • Fleet & Church Crookham War Memorial: This historic monument honors the locals who lost their lives during the two World Wars.

Natural Attractions of Fleet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hartland Moor National Nature Reserve: Hartland Moor is a lowland heath that’s an important habitat for several rare birds and insects. Visitors can stroll through the heath and appreciate the scenery.

  • Basingstoke Canal: This waterway is a great spot for a peaceful walk or bike ride with scenic views of the south Hampshire countryside.

  • Calthorpe Park: This local park has open spaces, a play area, and a pond for a fun family day out.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fleet

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure in Fleet, there are many hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the popular routes are:

  • The Fleet to Crookham Linear Park Walk: This 3-mile walk takes you through the peaceful green heart of Fleet and Church Crookham. The route goes through the Basingstoke Canal Centre, Potters Moors, and Calthorpe Park.

  • The Fleet Pond Trail: This trail takes you on a scenic walk around the largest freshwater lake in Hampshire, Fleet Pond Nature Reserve. The route is around 4 miles and offers stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.

  • Hartland Way: This walking and cycling trail is 14 miles long and connects Fleet to Basingstoke, with picturesque views of the Hampshire countryside.

Best Things to Do in Fleet: Activities and Tours

  • River Thames Cruise: Take a 3-hour cruise down the River Thames and explore the scenic countryside of south-east England. The cruise offers an afternoon tea, dinner, and champagne-drinking packages.

  • Airkix Indoor Skydiving: If you’re looking for adventure, try indoor skydiving at Airkix. You can experience the thrill of skydiving at any time of year.

  • Cycling Tours: Hire a bike and take a guided cycling tour to explore the beautiful countryside and charming villages around Fleet.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fleet

Day 1: Arrival and check-in, River Thames Cruise in the evening. Day 2: Visit the Gurkha Museum, followed by lunch at a local restaurant. Day 3: Hiking or Biking at Hartland Moor National Nature Reserve, followed by a picnic lunch. Day 4: Visit to Fleet Pond Nature Reserve, afternoon tea at the nearby restaurant. Day 5: Cycling Tour around the Scenic Countryside, followed by traditional English fish and chips. Day 6: Basingstoke Canal walk, evening visit to the Fleet & Church Crookham War Memorial. Day 7: Daytrip to Basingstoke, exploring the nearby attractions and local shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Fleet

There are plenty of dining options to explore in Fleet, from traditional restaurants to contemporary cafes, bars, and pubs. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A traditional British dish made with battered fried fish and chips, often served with mushy peas.

  • Beef Wellington: A classic dish made with beef fillet steak coated with mushroom and liver pâté and wrapped in puff pastry.

  • Eton Mess: A delicious dessert consisting of strawberries, meringue, and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Gurkha Beer: A locally produced Nepalese beer brewed with special barley and hops.

  • Hampshire Sparkling Wine: Known for its dry and crisp taste, Hampshire sparkling wine is perfect to enjoy with local dishes.

Where to Stay in Fleet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fleet offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some places to stay:

  • Lismoyne Hotel: A 4-star hotel with beautifully decorated rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a bar.

  • Days Inn Fleet: A budget-friendly hotel with modern rooms, an on-site restaurant, and free parking.

  • Premier Inn Fleet: A family-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and free parking.

What to Buy in Fleet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gurkha Memorabilia: Pick up souvenirs from the Gurkha Museum like books, t-shirts, and figurines, which make great gifts for friends and family.

  • Hampshire Jam and Chutney: Hampshire is known for its jams, jellies, and chutneys made from local produce. They make great gifts to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Fleet

Fleet has a rich history and culture, and locals are proud of their heritage. Respect for others is paramount in Fleet, and visitors should be sensitive to local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Fleet

Fleet can be visited year-round, but summer is the best time to enjoy outdoor recreation and sightseeing. The weather in summer is warm and sunny, with temperatures ranging between 20 - 25°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fleet

Do:

  • Be respectful of others’ privacy and personal space.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Clean up after yourself and dispose of litter responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Make noise in public places or residential areas.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Litter the natural areas or wildlife habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fleet

Fleet is a safe town, but visitors are advised to take precautions and be aware of their surroundings. Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Fleet

Fleet has access to several medical practices and pharmacies. In case of an emergency, dial 999.

Getting to Fleet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fleet is easily accessible by train, bus, and car. Here are some options to explore:

Airports Near Fleet

  • Heathrow Airport: 28 miles
  • Gatwick Airport: 38 miles

Bus Stations Near Fleet

  • Fleet Bus Station
  • Aldershot Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: South Western Railway provides efficient train services from London Waterloo to Fleet, with frequent connections throughout the day.
  • Car: Fleet is easily accessible by car via the M3 motorway.

How to Get Around in Fleet

Fleet is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Cycling is also a popular and convenient option for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Fleet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Farnborough: A popular town known for its aviation and aerospace industry.

Conclusion

A visit to Fleet promises a charming and welcoming experience that’s easy to access from London and other nearby towns. Whether you enjoy outdoor recreation, historic monuments, or traditional British cuisine, Fleet has something for everyone to enjoy. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore and experience the town like a local.
