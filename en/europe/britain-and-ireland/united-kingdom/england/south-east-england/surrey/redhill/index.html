Introduction to Redhill

Redhill, located in the county of Surrey in South East England, is a charming town popular for its beautiful natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant town center. Redhill’s natural beauty is often overshadowed by its reputation as a transport hub, being home to one of the busiest train stations in the UK, making it an excellent base for exploring nearby attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Redhill

Redhill has several significant attractions that every traveler should include in their itinerary. Below is a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Reigate Hill and Gatton Park: A National Trust protected area with beautiful gardens and landscapes.
  • Bletchingley: A medieval market town with historic buildings and a 15th-century church.
  • Bourne Hall Museum: This museum contains artwork, photographs, and exhibits that showcase the history of the local area.
  • Priory Farm: An award-winning farm that offers food and drink products with tea rooms and a play area for children.

Natural Attractions of Redhill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mercer’s Country Park: A place for leisure activities, from fishing and cycling to picnicking.
  • Earlswood Lakes: A combination of three former reservoirs surrounded by woodlands which offers water activities.
  • Ravenswood Nature Reserve: Protected woodlands for exploration, including an array of flora and fauna with walking trails for hikers.
  • Coulsdon Common: An area of open woodland and chalk downland, perfect for spotting birds like nightjars and wrynecks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redhill

Redhill is an excellent base for hiking and biking due to its natural beauty and picturesque trails. Here are some popular routes that visitors should consider:

  • North Downs Way: Runs from Farnham to Dover, encompassing parts of Surrey, and is one of the most popular paths.
  • Pilgrims’ Way: From Winchester to Canterbury, this footpath is an iconic route and gets its name from the pilgrimage to the shrine of Thomas Becket at Canterbury Cathedral.
  • Greensand Way: This scenic route stretches for 108 miles from Surrey to Kent, passing through the Surrey Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.

Best Things to Do in Redhill: Activities and Tours

Redhill offers a range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy during their stay, from adventure sports to exploring the town’s history. Here are some top things to do in Redhill:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride - Mercer’s Park is the perfect location to enjoy breath-taking views from a hot air balloon around Redhill.
  • Visit the everyman cinema - This independent cinema is known for its unique interior and offers the newest movies.
  • Live escape game - A fun-filled activity for groups, visitors are locked in a room and must solve the puzzles to escape in a limited time!
  • Discover the town’s history - Join a guide to explore the evolution of Redhill through the Victorian era and beyond.

Week-Long Itinerary for Redhill

Here is a week-long itinerary for visitors who desire a well-rounded and comprehensive experience of Redhill:

Day 1: Arrival Day

Spend your first day exploring Redhill and settling in, with a visit to the historic Gatton Park and Priory Farm for a bite to eat.

Day 2: Heritage Trail

Embark on an early morning Heritage Trail which will take you through the town’s history. Stop by everyman cinema for a good movie, followed by a visit to the Bourne Hall Museum for some history, ending with a picnic at Reigate Hill.

Day 3: Outdoor Adventures

Take a transfer to the Earlswood Lakes and spend a day fishing, enjoying a picnic before heading to Ravenswood Nature Reserve for a brisk nature walk.

Day 4: Action-packed day

Start your day with a hot air balloon ride followed by an adventurous escape room with dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 5: Retail therapy

Take a free shuttle service and explore the town on a shopping spree visit the Bletchingley village and Coulsdon Common for some fresh air and stunning landscapes.

Day 6: Hiking and Biking

Explore the Greensand Way and North Down Way, visiting some of the most beautiful sights in the area.

Day 7: Departure Day

Enjoy a free day relaxing before your departure, containing a trip to the Gatwick Aviation Museum and saying goodbye to Redhill in style.

Food and Drink Scene of Redhill

Redhill boasts an impressive food and drink scene, with several restaurants serving a variety of cuisines. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak and Ale Pie: A popular British dish served at many restaurants in the area.
  • Fish and Chips: Another classic British dish that visitors must try.
  • Sunday Roast: A traditional British meal served on Sundays, featuring roasted meats and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Enjoy craft beers in a traditional English pub.
  • Gin and Tonics: Discover delicious gins at every bar and relish in the mouth-watering flavors.

Where to Stay in Redhill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Redhill offers visitors a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some popular options are:

  • Bridge House Hotel: A beautiful and refined Georgian hotel set in the historic market town of Reigate, with sumptuous surroundings and excellent customer service.
  • Nutfield Priory Hotel and Spa: A Victorian mansion nestled in 12 acres of picturesque grounds, which offers stunning views and a luxurious spa.

What to Buy in Redhill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home some local souvenirs that remind them of their Redhill trip. Below are some to consider:

  • English Tea: Buy tea at Priory Farm store, which stocks the largest collection of English teas and beautiful teapots.
  • Surrey Honey: Buy honey from a local beekeeper’s store - taste local honey and bring them home for your friends and family.
  • Local artwork: Visit the local galleries and bring home some unique and exclusive artwork to remind you of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Redhill

Redhill has a rich local culture and customs. Visitors are expected to respect them, including

  • Saying please and thank you.
  • Queueing in an orderly fashion.
  • Treating everyone with respect and courtesy.

Best Time to Visit Redhill

Redhill can be visited throughout the year, with the best time being from March to October, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redhill

Redhill is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should exercise caution, as with any other town they visit. These tips will ensure your safety while exploring Redhill:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid leaving them in plain sight.
  • Be aware of traffic when crossing the road.

Health in Redhill

Redhill has several medical centers and hospitals to provide health care to its visitors.

Getting to Redhill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Redhill is well-connected and easily accessible through various transportation modes.

Airports Near Redhill

  • London Gatwick Airport is the nearest airport, 10 km away from Redhill.

Bus Stations Near Redhill

  • Redhill Bus Station is the go-to stop for anyone visiting Redhill via public transportation.

Other Options

  • Driving or renting a car is a convenient option for visitors who enjoy being independent on their travels.

How to Get Around in Redhill

Getting around is effortless, with several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and trains. A vast number of walkers and cyclists enjoy the scenery: With adequate planning, it is possible to go from Gatwick to Redhill in approximately 30 minutes using the public transport system.
