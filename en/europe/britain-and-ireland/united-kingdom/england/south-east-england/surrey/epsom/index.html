A Comprehensive Epsom Travel Guide: What to See, Do, Eat and More

Epsom is a peaceful, leafy town located in Surrey, South East England. With its abundance of parks and green spaces, Epsom is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts. In this guide, we’ll take a look at some of the town’s most important cultural and natural attractions, as well as providing tips on where to eat and stay. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Epsom

Epsom is best known for its famous Derby, (horse race) in June each year. However, there’s much more to the town than just horse races. With links to the ancient Romans and Anglo-Saxons, Epsom has a rich history that can be explored through its museums and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Epsom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Epsom Downs Racecourse: Visit the racecourse, the site of the famous Derby horse race which takes place on the first Saturday in June.
  • Chessington World of Adventures: An amusement park and zoo located just outside of Epsom. It’s especially great for families with children.
  • Epsom Playhouse: This theatre and arts centre hosts a variety of shows, performances and events throughout the year.
  • Bourne Hall Museum: This small museum is dedicated to the history of Epsom and Ewell, and is located in a beautiful park.

Natural Attractions of Epsom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Epsom Common: This is the largest open space in Epsom with over 176 hectares of land complete with walking and cycling paths.
  • Nonsuch Park: Located just a short distance from Epsom, this park is one of the largest in Surrey, and is full of wildflowers and wildlife.
  • Hobbledown: An adventure park for kids with farm animals, indoor play areas and more.
  • Epsom Downs: Get with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside from the top of Epsom Downs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Epsom

Epsom has plenty of walking and cycling paths throughout its many parks and nature reserves. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Epsom Downs, Ashtead Park and Prince’s Covert Circuit: This 9.5 KM loop will take you through some of Epsom’s most beautiful landscapes, including Epsom Downs and Ashtead Park.
  • Nonsuch Park to Chessington: This 5.4 KM hike will take you through Nonsuch Park and into the nearby Chessington World of Adventures amusement park.
  • Epsom Common Circular Walk: This 6.5 KM walk will take you through a mix of forests, meadows and scrubland in Epsom Common.
  • Epsom to Sutton: This 6.7 KM cycling route will take you through some of Epsom’s suburban areas before reaching the nearby town of Sutton.

Best Things to Do in Epsom: Activities and Tours

  • Go horse racing: If you’re in town during the month of June, make sure to check out the Epsom Derby, one of the most famous horse races in the world. You can also enjoy a variety of events throughout the year at the Epsom Downs Racecourse.
  • Visit the Horton Chapel: A beautiful 14th-century chapel with stunning stained glass windows.
  • Take a walking tour of the town: Learn more about the history of Epsom on a guided walking tour of the town.
  • Epsom Escape Room: Put your skills to the test in this thrilling escape room set up in an old chapel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Epsom

If you’re planning to spend a week in Epsom, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town center and take in some of the local shops, cafes, and restaurants.

Day 2-3: Visit Epsom’s cultural attractions, including the Bourne Hall Museum and the Horton Chapel.

Day 4-5: Explore the beautiful parks and outdoor spaces around Epsom, including Nonsuch Park and Epsom Downs.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby attractions like Hampton Court Palace or Kingston-Upon-Thames.

Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of Epsom’s local spas or wellness centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Epsom

Must-Try Local Dishes

  • Surrey Pudding: This savory dish is made with suet, breadcrumbs, and onions, and is traditionally served with gravy.
  • Epsom Lamb: Local lamb sourced from the surrounding countryside, either roasted or grilled and served with a mint sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Hogs Back Brewery: Try some of the delicious real ale and craft beers from Hogs Back, a local brewery just down the road from Epsom.
  • Surrey Hills Brewery: Another local brewery sourcing high quality ingredients to brew fantastic beer.

Where to Stay in Epsom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Premier Inn Epsom Central: Located right in the heart of town, this hotel is a great option for families or groups.
  • Travelodge Epsom Central: Another centrally located hotel with all the amenities you need for a comfortable and convenient stay.
  • The Lodge at Kingswood: Stay in this boutique hotel in the nearby Kingswood Village, located just a short drive or train ride away from Epsom.

What to Buy in Epsom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: Surrey, and Epsom in particular, is known for its delicious honey made from bees on the surrounding heathlands.
  • Epsom Salts: These salts were first discovered in natural springs in Epsom, hence their name. They are great for relaxation and health.

Local Culture and Customs of Epsom

  • Horse Racing: Make sure you don’t miss the races at Epsom Downs!
  • Epsom Downs Gallops: To experience the town’s equestrian heritage first hand, watch horses being exercised at dawn at the Epsom Downs Gallops.

Best Time to Visit Epsom

The best time to visit Epsom is in the summer, when the weather is warm and the outdoor attractions are in full bloom. If you’re visiting for the Epsom Derby horse race, the first weekend in June is the ideal time to plan your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Epsom

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor spaces in Epsom and get in touch with nature.
  • Do attend the famous Epsom Derby horse race.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to try the local food & drink, from the local breweries to the Surrey pudding and Epsom lamb.
  • Don’t overlook nearby attractions like Hampton Court Palace, Kingston-Upon-Thames or London.

Safety and Health

Safety in Epsom

Epsom is generally a safe place to visit, with low levels of crime. However, as with any location, it is important to take precautions such as locking your car and bike or keeping your belongings safe. Also, be cautious while walking in isolated areas.

Health in Epsom

Epsom has a variety of healthcare facilities, including clinics, hospitals and pharmacies. In case of an emergency, dial 999 for assistance, or head to the nearest hospital

Getting to Epsom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Epsom

Epsom is located roughly equidistant between London’s two major airports, Heathrow and Gatwick. From Heathrow, take the National Express coach or train to Epsom. From Gatwick, take the train to East Croydon, then change to a train to Epsom.

Bus Stations Near Epsom

There are several bus stations in Epsom, including Epsom Hospital, Epsom Clock Tower, and Epsom station.

Other Options

Epsom is well-connected by train, with regular services to London Victoria and Waterloo stations. Visitors can also take advantage of local taxi or car hire services.

How to Get Around in Epsom

Epsom is small enough to walk, but if you prefer to use public transport, there are plenty of bus or taxi services available. Or if you’re driving, there are plenty of parking spaces throughout the town, including pay and display car parks in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Epsom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Epsom’s central location makes it the perfect starting point for exploring the surrounding areas. Plan a day trip to nearby attractions like Hampton Court Palace, Kingston-Upon-Thames or the vibrant city of London with all its tourist attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in the great outdoors, local history, or simply looking for a relaxing break, Epsom has plenty to offer. With its beautiful parks, historical sites, and array of cultural attractions, there’s something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of Epsom.
