Introduction to Thames Ditton

Located in the borough of Elmbridge in Surrey, Thames Ditton is a charming village that sits on the banks of River Thames. The village boasts a rich history, as evident in its ancient buildings and structures that date back to the medieval period. Today, Thames Ditton is a popular tourist destination because of its rich culture, excellent food scene, and breathtaking scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Thames Ditton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thames Ditton is home to several unique museums and historical sites that you should not miss, including:

  1. Hampton Court Palace: This grand palace was built by Cardinal Thomas Wolsey in 1515 and was later home to King Henry VIII. It is now a major tourist attraction where visitors can explore the state rooms, beautifully landscaped gardens, and the Great Hall.

  2. The Dittons Library: This is a branch of Surrey Libraries that was built in 1889 and is a Grade II listed building. It houses a vast collection of books and also serves as a study center.

  3. All Saints Church: This historic church dates back to the 13th century and is renowned for its 16th-century clock in the tower, which still strikes the hour.

Natural Attractions of Thames Ditton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For lovers of the great outdoors, here are some great outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore in Thames Ditton:

  1. Thames Ditton Island: Take a lovely boat ride on the River Thames, and explore the enchanting Thames Ditton Island. It has stunning views, and you can also go bird watching and have a picnic.

  2. Gigg’s Hill Green: This large open space is ideal for hikes, picnics, or games of cricket. It features a playground, tennis courts, and a pavilion for events and performances.

  3. Molesey Heath: This heath covers about 140 acres of open land and woods, and its network of paths is perfect for hiking and mountain biking. Alternatively, take a leisurely nature walk or a picnic with the family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thames Ditton

Thames Ditton offers ample opportunities for hiking and mountain biking, with trails that crisscross its riverside and woodland areas. The most popular are:

  1. The Thames Path: This scenic trail follows the course of the River Thames and runs through Thames Ditton. It offers stunning views of the river and is an excellent spot for bird-watching.

  2. The Dittons Circular Walk: This is a circular 4.5 mile route that starts and ends at Thames Ditton station. It takes you on a tour of both Thames Ditton and Teddington Locks passing by beautiful fields, woods, and open spaces.

Best Things to Do in Thames Ditton: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to partake in Thames Ditton, and tours to help you explore these activities, including:

  1. The Big Tree Climbing Company: For thrill-seekers, this company offers tree climbing and zip lining activities that take place in ancient woodlands near Thames Ditton.

  2. Thames River Boats: Take a ride down the River Thames and explore the picturesque scenery, with guided tours of historic sites and landmarks.

  3. Surrey Wine School: Learn how to distinguish and appreciate different wine blends, as well as explore some of the local wine cellars in Thames Ditton.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thames Ditton

A comprehensive itinerary to explore and appreciate all that Thames Ditton has to offer would include:

Day 1: Visit Hampton Court Palace and explore the Grand Hall, State Rooms and landscaped gardens.

Day 2: Travel down the River Thames, visit the Dittons Island, have a picnic and watch the boats go by.

Day 3: Hike the Thames Path, bird-watch and take scenic pictures.

Day 4: Enjoy a day of wine tasting and winery tours of local vineyards.

Day 5: Visit the historic All Saints Church and check out the 16th-century striking clock.

Day 6: Take a stroll through Gigg’s Hill Green and enjoy the open space or challenge friends to a game of cricket.

Day 7: Take the Dittons Circular Walk and enjoy the countryside views of Teddington Locks.

Food and Drink Scene of Thames Ditton

Thames Ditton boasts a thriving food scene offering foodies a chance to taste some of the village’s best dishes and local produce.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Surrey Farmhouse Breakfast: This delicious breakfast dish is perfect for enjoying a hearty breakfast. It features the traditionally received full English breakfast elements, including eggs, bacon, sausages, black pudding, and tomatoes.

  2. English Afternoon Tea: Take part in the English tradition of afternoon tea, with delicious sandwiches, scones with freshly whipped cream and strawberry jam, and a range of teas to choose from.

Local Beverages

  1. Thames Ditton Gin: Enjoy Thames Ditton’s famous gin distilled by Goring & Aberfoyle, which combines rich flavors of spice, earthy tones, and citrus.

  2. Palmers English Sparkling Wine: Indulge in Thames Ditton’s own sparkling wine, with notes of apples, peaches, and a slightly nutty finish.

Where to Stay in Thames Ditton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thames Ditton offers a range of options when it comes to accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to affordable guesthouses and hostels.

  1. The Foley Hotel: Located in Claygate, The Foley is a beautiful hotel offering high-end experiences at affordable prices.

  2. Holiday Inn Kingston South: This hotel is located on Portsmouth Road, making it easily accessible for tourists. It is equipped with an outdoor pool, a fitness center, and meeting rooms.

  3. The Swan Hotel: This four-star hotel is situated in the heart of Thames Ditton, with 11 guest rooms, each uniquely decorated with Tudor beams and other historic details.

What to Buy in Thames Ditton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thames Ditton is known for its unique shopping experience, offering a variety of local products and souvenirs for tourists to take home, including:

  1. Handmade crafts: Thames Ditton-made crafts, such as jewelry and pottery, are available at craft markets and specialty shops.

  2. Local produce: Locally sourced fruits, vegetables, and baked goods are available from farmers markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Thames Ditton

Thames Ditton’s culture is steeped in history dating back to the medieval period. The village celebrates various cultural events throughout the year, including the Thames Ditton Regatta, which is a major tourist draw in the summer.

Best Time to Visit Thames Ditton

Thames Ditton is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is warmer, and outdoor activities are more accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thames Ditton

Do:

  1. Explore the River Thames’s scenic beauty and outdoor activities, including hiking and bird watching.

  2. Immerse yourself in Thames Ditton’s food scene, indulging in locally sourced produce and traditional English tea.

  3. Attend local festivals and cultural events to learn more about the cultural heritage of Thames Ditton.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the local environment.

  2. Ignore posted safety precautions on hiking and biking trails.

  3. Disturb the wildlife inhabitants of Thames Ditton by getting too close to them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thames Ditton

Thames Ditton is a relatively safe place for tourists, but it’s best to take some precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas and being careful when hiking and biking to avoid accidents.

Health in Thames Ditton

Thames Ditton provides medical services through the Riverbank Medical Centre, located at Windmill Lane in the village. The village also boasts several pharmacies and medical service providers.

Getting to Thames Ditton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thames Ditton is easily accessible by public transport, with several bus and train lines serving the village.

Airports near Thames Ditton

The nearest airport to Thames Ditton is London Heathrow Airport, located about 16 miles away.

Bus stations near Thames Ditton

There are several bus stops in Thames Ditton, with buses running from Kingston and other nearby towns and cities.

Other options

Visitors may also arrive in Thames Ditton by taxi, car rental, or bicycle.

How to Get Around in Thames Ditton

The best way to get around Thames Ditton is on foot, but transportation options include bikes, buses, and taxis, with bike rental available for tourists on site.

Where to Go Next from Thames Ditton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thames Ditton is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding locales, with nearby destinations such as:

  1. Richmond Park: This park is only a short distance away and offers stunning views of the wild deer that roam freely in the parkland.

  2. Kingston: This town is just a 10-minute drive away and offers a wide range of shopping and dining options, as well as a lovely river walk.

  3. Sandown Park Racecourse: Sandown is the premier racecourse for horse racing, located only 20 minutes away by car, It offers the thrill of racing and betting on horses

Conclusion

Thames Ditton is a hidden gem of Surrey, England, offering a blend of rich history, natural beauty, gastronomical delights, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a food enthusiast, or a history buff, this village has something different to offer. Plan your visit to Thames Ditton today, and you won’t be disappointed!
