Introduction to Reigate

Nestled in the heart of Surrey, Reigate is a charming town that boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty. If you’re looking for a destination that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and delicious food, look no further than Reigate.

List of Places to Visit in Reigate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Reigate Priory Museum - Learn about the fascinating history of Reigate Priory and the monks who once called it home.
  • Reigate Castle - Explore the ruins of this 11th-century castle and admire panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • RHS Garden Wisley - A must-visit for garden lovers with more than 97 acres of gardens and a vast plant collection.

Natural Attractions of Reigate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Reigate Hill and Gatton Park - A popular spot for hiking and picnicking, Reigate Hill and Gatton Park offer spectacular views of the surrounding areas.
  • Betchworth Quarry Nature Reserve – A nature reserve with a combination of open grassland, wide woodland paths, and quiet avenues.
  • Mercers Country Park – Offers picturesque views of the lake, perfect for walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reigate

Numerous hiking and biking routes can be found in Reigate, with the popular ones being:

  • North Downs Way - One of the most famous hiking trails in England, the North Downs Way stretches for over 150 miles, with portions passing through Reigate.
  • Reigate Hill and Gatton Park Walk - A 5-mile circular walk that rewards hikers with stunning views of the Surrey countryside.

Best Things to Do in Reigate: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Rooms Reigate - A chance to put your problem-solving skills to the test with a variety of themed escape rooms.
  • Reigate Caves Tunnel Tours – Take a guided tour around the caves and learn about their mysterious past.
  • Reigate Priory Park – A vast park with a lake, perfect for a picnic day and feeding ducks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Reigate

Day 1: Visit the town center, grab a bite to eat at a local restaurant, and explore Reigate Castle.

Day 2: Take a hike on Reigate Hill and Gatton Park and have a picnic lunch at a scenic spot.

Day 3: Discover the captivating history of Reigate Priory Museum and Reigate Priory Park.

Day 4: Explore the town’s natural wonders with a visit to Betchworth Quarry Nature Reserve.

Day 5: Enjoy a scenic bike ride along the North Downs Way and stop at local cafes for a meal.

Day 6: Indulge in a food and drink tour of Reigate’s local pubs and restaurants.

Day 7: Visit RHS Garden Wisley and admire the stunning gardens before departing from Reigate.

Food and Drink Scene of Reigate

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sausage Roll - A British classic, this flaky pastry filled with spiced sausage is a must-try.
  • Hedgehog Cake - A delicious delight made with chocolate, cream, and chocolate cake crumbs.
  • Sunday Roast – A traditional English dish, consisting of meat and roasted vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale - Try a pint of ale from one of Reigate’s local pubs, such as The Roe Deer or The Prince of Wales.
  • Gin and Tonic - Reigate is renowned for its local gin, and a refreshing G&T is the perfect way to enjoy a summer evening.

Where to Stay in Reigate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Reigate Manor Hotel - A historic hotel with modern amenities located just outside of the town center.
  • The Cranleigh Hotel - A luxurious boutique hotel located in the heart of Reigate.
  • YHA Holmbury St. Mary - A budget-friendly hostel nestled in the Surrey Hills.

What to Buy in Reigate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Reigate Honey - Sample some of the local honey from the beekeepers in Reigate.
  • Handmade Pottery - Browse the unique pottery pieces made by local artists at the Priory Farm Shop.
  • Reigate Stone - Take home a piece of Reigate history with a geologically unique stone quarried from the local area.

Local Culture and Customs of Reigate

Reigate is a friendly, welcoming town that values community spirit and encourages visitors to take part in local events and traditions. Respect for nature and the environment is important to the people of Reigate, with many initiatives and green spaces to protect the town’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Reigate

Reigate can be enjoyed year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild, and the flowers are in bloom. The months of July and August offer the most extended daylight hours, providing ample opportunity to take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reigate

Do:

  • Do take a hike in the beautiful Surrey Hills.

  • Do sample the locally made gin.

  • Do try a traditional English Sunday Roast.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave Reigate without visiting at least one of its historic landmarks.

  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate hiking gear, depending on the trail you choose.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on the best places to eat and drink.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reigate

Reigate is a safe town, with a low crime rate, but it is always important to take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Reigate

Healthcare services are readily available in Reigate, and emergency services can be accessed by calling 999. It is always essential to carry personal insurance with medical coverage before traveling to England.

Getting to Reigate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Reigate

The nearest airports to Reigate are London Gatwick Airport, which is located just 15 minutes away by train or taxi, and London Heathrow Airport, which is approximately 35 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Reigate

Reigate has a well-connected bus station, with regular buses running to several nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

For those driving to Reigate, the town is easily accessible from the M25 and M23 motorways, with plenty of parking available in the town center, and bike rental stations available for those who prefer to cycle.

How to Get Around in Reigate

Reigate is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Local buses and taxis are also readily available for those who prefer to travel by public transport.

Where to Go Next from Reigate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Box Hill - A popular spot for picnicking, hiking, and wildlife spotting, Box Hill is located just a short drive from Reigate.
  • Guildford – A historic town with a vibrant arts and culture scene, Guildford is located a short train ride from Reigate.
  • London - The UK’s capital city is only 40 minutes away by train, providing endless opportunities to explore world-famous attractions such as Big Ben, the London Eye, and Buckingham Palace.

Conclusion

Reigate is a hidden gem that offers plenty of activities, culture, and charm for visitors. From its natural beauty to its historic landmarks and delicious local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this delightful town.
