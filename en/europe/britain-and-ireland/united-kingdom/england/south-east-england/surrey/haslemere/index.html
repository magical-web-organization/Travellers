Introduction to Haslemere

Nestled in the heart of Surrey, Haslemere is a charming market town that offers visitors a plethora of cultural and natural attractions. With its picturesque countryside landscapes, historical monuments, and lively food and drink scene, Haslemere is a must-visit destination in South East England.

List of Places to Visit in Haslemere

When visiting Haslemere, make sure to check out the following attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haslemere Educational Museum - founded in 1888, the museum is a treasure trove of natural history exhibits, interactive galleries, and archaeological finds.

  • St Bartholomew’s Church - a Grade I listed building, the church boasts a rich historical connection with the town.

  • Halsemere Hall - a popular venue for concerts, theatre performances, and stand-up comedy shows.

Natural Attractions of Haslemere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Devil’s Punch Bowl - one of the most striking viewpoints in southern England, the Punch Bowl is a large natural amphitheatre.

  • Black Down - the highest point in the South Downs National Park, Black Down offers stunning vistas of the surrounding countryside.

  • Halsemere Commons - a stark contrast to the town’s hustle and bustle, the commons is a peaceful haven for hikers, wildlife lovers, and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haslemere

Haslemere offers countless hiking and biking trails that lead to scenic vistas, rolling hills, and tranquil countryside. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Serpent Trail - a 64-mile long footpath that passes through Haslemere’s stunning scenery.

  • Hindhead Commons and the Devil’s Punch Bowl - a picturesque loop trail that takes hikers through some of the South Downs’ most stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Haslemere: Activities and Tours

It’s not just nature and history that Haslemere has to offer. The town is brimming with activities and attractions that cater to every taste and interest. Some of our top picks include:

  • Haslemere Beer Festival - a yearly event that showcases the best local brews and live music.

  • Halsemere Fringe Festival - a six-day celebration of arts, culture, and community spirit.

  • Oakwood Specialist Cars - for motor enthusiasts, visit Oakwood Specialist Cars to see a flawless collection of premium vehicles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haslemere

Day 1: Arrive in Haslemere and explore the town’s downtown area, cafes, and boutique shops

Day 2: Take a hiking trip to Devil’s Punch Bowl and Black Down.

Day 3: Visit Halsemere Educational Museum and St. Bartholomew’s Church.

Day 4: Join a guided tour of the town’s historical monuments.

Day 5: Indulge in local cuisine and wine at one of the town’s top restaurants.

Day 6: Take a trip to nearby towns such as Farnham and Godalming.

Day 7: Go on a leisurely walk in Halsemere Commons and take in the wildlife and nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Haslemere

Haslemere offers a wide range of restaurants, cafes, and bars that cater to all tastes and budgets. Some recommended dishes and local beverages to try out:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huntington pork belly with pomegranate - a signature dish at the town’s popular gastropub, The White Horse.

  • Braised beef cheek with rosti potato - a hearty meal at The Wheatsheaf, featuring locally sourced beef.

Local Beverages

  • Greyfriars Vineyard - a premium winery specialising in award-winning sparkling wines that take guests on a tour of their vineyards.

  • Tilled Surrey - a family-run artisan brewery that offers craft beers made from local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Haslemere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haslemere offers a wide range of accommodation options to fit every budget and preference. Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodation or a budget-friendly hostel, there’s something for everyone.

  • Lythe Hill Hotel and Spa - a luxury hotel that features a spa, restaurant, and conference facilities.

  • The Georgian House Hotel and Spa - a boutique hotel nestled in the town centre with its own spa.

  • Halsemere Hostel - a budget-friendly option for travellers who want to save on accommodation costs.

What to Buy in Haslemere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Haslemere has plenty to offer.

  • Haslemere Museum Shop - features a vast range of gifts, including locally made crafts and souvenirs.

  • Halsemere Farmers Market- held weekly every Sunday morning, features fresh local produce and artisan crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Haslemere

Haslemere boasts an eclectic mix of ancient customs and contemporary culture that makes it a unique destination to visit. Visitors should take note of the following customs and traditions:

  • Bonfire Night - a yearly festival celebrated on the 5th of November, which dates back to the 17th century.

  • Haslemere Charter Fair - a yearly street fair that takes place every October and features funfair rides, food stalls, and street vendors.

Best Time to Visit Haslemere

The best time to visit Haslemere is during the summertime when the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is lush and green. However, the town is open and accessible throughout the year, and each season has its unique charm and appeal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haslemere

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the local countryside on foot or by bike.

  • Try the locally made craft beers and wines.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious or historical sites.

  • Get too rowdy in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haslemere

Haslemere is generally a safe destination for travellers, with a low incidence of crime and violence. Visitors should still take basic precautions such as locking their valuables in a safe and avoiding deserted or dimly lit areas.

Health in Haslemere

Haslemere has several primary healthcare centres and hospitals should you need medical attention during your visit.

Getting to Haslemere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haslemere

The closest airport to Haslemere is London Gatwick Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive away. London Heathrow Airport is another option, approximately an hour and thirty minutes by car away.

Bus Stations Near Haslemere

Haslemere is served by a network of local bus routes and a long-distance coach service. The town’s central bus station is located near Haslemere railway station.

Other Options

  • By Train: Haslemere railway station is situated on the Portsmouth Direct Line, connecting London Waterloo to Portsmouth and Brighton.

  • By Car: Haslemere is situated on the A3 highway that links London to Portsmouth.

How to Get Around in Haslemere

Haslemere is a walkable town that can be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bikes or take taxis to get around.

Where to Go Next from Haslemere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Haslemere is an excellent base for exploring nearby towns and villages such as:

  • Chichester - a historic cathedral city on the south coast of England.

  • Farnham - an ancient market town with a rich cultural heritage.

  • Godalming - an idyllic market town with a charming riverside location.

Conclusion

Haslemere is a charming haven for visitors who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in a tranquil countryside setting. With its cultural and natural attractions, lively food and drink scene, and famous customs and traditions, Haslemere has something to offer everyone. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural enrichment, this town should be at the top of your travel list.
