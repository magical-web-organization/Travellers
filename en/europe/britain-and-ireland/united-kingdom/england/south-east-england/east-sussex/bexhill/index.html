Introduction to Bexhill

Located in East Sussex, South East England, Bexhill is a charming seaside town that attracts visitors with its beautiful landscapes, lively cultural scene, and rich history. With its beautiful beaches, picturesque parks, and quaint streets, Bexhill has something to offer for everyone. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation, Bexhill is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Bexhill

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bexhill Museum - Learn about the local history and culture of Bexhill, East Sussex, and explore the museum’s extensive collection of artefacts.
  2. De La Warr Pavilion - Enjoy contemporary art exhibitions, live music performances, and cultural events at this iconic modernist building.
  3. St. Mary’s Church - Visit the beautiful medieval church and explore its rich history.
  4. Merriments Gardens - Explore the tranquil gardens, enjoy a cup of tea at the café, and buy plants and gardening tools at the shop.

Natural Attractions of Bexhill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bexhill Beach - Relax on the sandy beach, take a dip in the sea, and enjoy the lovely views of the English Channel.
  2. Egerton Park - Enjoy a picnic, play sports, and take a leisurely stroll around the beautiful park.
  3. Highwoods - Located on the outskirts of Bexhill, this woodland park is perfect for a relaxing walk, birdwatching, and nature photography.
  4. Cooden Beach - Located a few miles east of Bexhill, this pebble beach is perfect for a peaceful walk and stunning coastline views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bexhill

  1. Buckholt Wood - A peaceful woodland walk that takes you through Buckholt Wood, one of the oldest woodlands in East Sussex.
  2. Combe Valley Way - A scenic cycling and walking route that runs through the Combe Valley, past lakes, ancient woodland, and historic sites.
  3. Bexhill Promenade Cycle Route - Take a leisurely bike ride along the Bexhill seafront and enjoy the stunning views of the English Channel.

Best Things to Do in Bexhill: Activities and Tours

  1. Bexhill 100 Motoring Club - Enjoy vintage car shows, classic car rallies, and other motoring events throughout the year.
  2. Bexhill Heritage Trail - Discover the local history of Bexhill by taking a self-guided walking tour of the town.
  3. Bexhill Festival of the Sea - Celebrate the town’s maritime heritage with live music, food stalls, and fun activities for all ages.
  4. Bexhill Art Trail - Explore the vibrant art scene of Bexhill with this annual event that showcases the works of local artists.

Food and Drink Scene of Bexhill

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips - Try this classic British dish at one of the many seaside restaurants and cafes in Bexhill.
  2. Sussex Smokie - A delicious fish dish that consists of smoked haddock, cheese, and cream.
  3. Shepherds Pie - A hearty meat pie made with minced lamb or beef, potatoes, and vegetables.
  4. Bakewell Tart - A sweet pastry filled with jam, almond paste, and frangipane.

Local Beverages

  1. Sussex Ale - A traditional English beer that is brewed locally in East Sussex.
  2. Sussex Cider - Enjoy this refreshing apple cider that is produced in the nearby orchards.
  3. English Breakfast Tea - A classic black tea blend that is a perfect morning beverage.
  4. Freshly squeezed fruit juices - Try the locally grown apples, berries, and cherries in Bexhill.

Where to Stay in Bexhill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Cooden Beach Hotel - A luxurious hotel with stunning sea views and a private beach.
  2. The Driftwood Bexhill - A tranquil guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  3. Bexhill B&B - A welcoming bed and breakfast that is centrally located in the town.
  4. Combe Haven Holiday Park - A family-friendly holiday park that offers caravans and lodges for rent.

What to Buy in Bexhill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bexhill-made pottery and ceramics - Shop for unique pottery and ceramics that are made by local artisans and craftsmen.
  2. Local honey and preserves - Enjoy the taste of East Sussex’s fresh produce by buying locally made honey and preserves.
  3. Beach-themed souvenirs - Take home a piece of Bexhill’s seaside charm by purchasing souvenirs such as seashells, driftwood, and postcards.
  4. Antique and vintage items - Bexhill is known for its antique and vintage shops, which offer a wide selection of unique items such as furniture, clothing, and jewellery.

Local Culture and Customs of Bexhill

Bexhill has a rich cultural heritage, and it is famous for its arts and music scene. The town hosts various live music events and festivals throughout the year, including the Bexhill Festival of the Sea, Bexhill Art Trail, and Bexhill 100 Motoring Club. Moreover, Bexhill locals are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors can expect a warm reception when they visit the town.

Best Time to Visit Bexhill

The best time to visit Bexhill is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with tourists and events. Nevertheless, Bexhill is a beautiful destination all year-round, and visitors can enjoy its attractions and activities regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bexhill

Do:

  1. Take a leisurely stroll along Bexhill Beach and enjoy the stunning views of the English Channel.
  2. Explore Bexhill’s cultural attractions such as its museums, art galleries, and historic buildings.
  3. Visit the local markets and shops and buy souvenirs and locally made products.
  4. Try the local cuisine, including Sussex Smokie, Shepherds Pie, and Bakewell Tart.

Don’t:

  1. Leave your litter on the beaches or in the parks.
  2. Disturb the wildlife and natural habitats during your nature walks and bike rides.
  3. Ignore the local customs and traditions, such as tipping and queuing.
  4. Leave your valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bexhill

Bexhill is generally a safe destination, and visitors should not encounter any major safety issues during their stay. Nevertheless, it is advisable to take the usual safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Bexhill

Bexhill has adequate healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, and visitors can access medical care if needed. However, visitors are advised to travel with proper medical insurance to cover any unforeseen health issues.

Getting to Bexhill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bexhill

  1. Gatwick Airport - Located approximately an hour’s drive away.
  2. Heathrow Airport - Located approximately two hours’ drive away.

Bus and Train Stations Near Bexhill

  1. Bexhill train station - Located in the town centre, with regular trains to London, Brighton, and other nearby destinations.
  2. Cooden Beach train station - Located on the outskirts of Bexhill, with regular trains to Brighton and other nearby destinations.
  3. Bexhill bus station - Located in the town centre, with regular buses to Hastings, Eastbourne, and other nearby towns.

Other Transportation Options

  1. Taxis - Taxis are widely available in Bexhill, and visitors can book them through local taxi companies or online taxi booking apps.
  2. Car rentals - Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in the town centre, with many car rental companies available.

How to Get Around in Bexhill

Bexhill is a small town, and visitors can easily explore its attractions by walking or cycling. Moreover, there are local buses and taxis available for those who prefer public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Bexhill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Hastings - A historic seaside town that is famous for its castle, Old Town, and fish market.
  2. Eastbourne - A traditional Victorian resort town that offers beautiful beaches, parks, and gardens.
  3. Rye - A picturesque medieval town that is known for its cobbled streets, antique shops, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Bexhill is a delightful seaside town that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are interested in history, arts, or nature, Bexhill has something to offer for everyone. So, pack your bags, and get ready to discover the charming town of Bexhill, East Sussex.
