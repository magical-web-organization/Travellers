Introduction to Hailsham

Located in East Sussex, Hailsham is a charming market town that offers visitors the perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. The town is surrounded by picturesque countryside and is home to several cultural attractions, making it the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the best of East Sussex.

List of Places to Visit in Hailsham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Michelham Priory: A picturesque historic house and garden that dates back to the 13th century. Visitors can explore the house and gardens while learning about the history of the priory and the people who called it home.

  • Redoubt Fortress: A circular fortress dating back to the 19th century, the Redoubt Fortress now serves as a museum offering insight into the town’s military past.

  • The Observatory Science Centre: Home to several telescopes, this observatory helps visitors explore the wonders of the solar system and beyond.

Natural Attractions of Hailsham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Cuckoo Trail: A 14-mile pathway that follows a former railway line, the Cuckoo Trail is perfect for walkers and cyclists looking to explore the natural beauty of Hailsham.

  • Hempstead Meadows Nature Reserve: A unique combination of woodland, meadow, and wetland, the nature reserve is home to a variety of wildlife and is perfect for a peaceful walk.

  • Arlington Bluebell Walk: A picturesque woodland walk with stunning bluebells in the spring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hailsham

  • The Cuckoo Trail
  • South Downs Way
  • Willingdon Hill

Best Things to Do in Hailsham: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visit the nearby vineyards and sample some of the best British wines on a wine tasting tour that takes you through the rolling hills of East Sussex.

  • Clay Pigeon Shooting: Try your hand at clay pigeon shooting, a popular activity in the countryside surrounding Hailsham.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the stunning views of Hailsham from above with a hot air balloon ride over the town and countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Hailsham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A British classic, fish and chips can be found at many restaurants and cafes in Hailsham.

  • Sussex Cream Tea: A quintessentially British treat, Sussex cream tea consists of scones, clotted cream, and jam, served with a pot of tea.

  • Shepherd’s Pie: Another classic British dish, shepherd’s pie is a delicious and hearty meal that is perfect for a cold day.

Local Beverages

  • Harvey’s Brewery: This local brewery has been producing beer in East Sussex since 1790 and offers tours and tastings for visitors.

  • Ridgeview Wine Estate: A vineyard located just outside Hailsham that produces award-winning English sparkling wine.

Where to Stay in Hailsham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Corn Exchange: A historic building that has been converted into a boutique hotel, The Corn Exchange offers stylish and comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of Hailsham.

  • The Boship Lions Farm Hotel: A peaceful retreat on the outskirts of Hailsham, The Boship Lions Farm Hotel is set in 17 acres of beautiful countryside.

  • The Horse and Groom Inn: This traditional inn offers comfortable rooms and delicious food in a charming setting.

What to Buy in Hailsham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sussex Trugs: Handmade in Hailsham using willow, Sussex trugs are baskets that were traditionally used for carrying vegetables.

  • Arlington Bluebell Soap: Made with natural ingredients, this soap is inspired by the bluebells that bloom in the nearby Arlington Bluebell Walk.

  • Harvey’s Brewery Beer: Take home a bottle of beer from the local brewery and enjoy a taste of East Sussex at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hailsham

  • Gardening: East Sussex is known for its beautiful gardens, and Hailsham is no exception. Many residents take great pride in their gardens and even open them up to visitors during the summer months.

  • Walking: With its stunning countryside and many walking paths, walking is a popular pastime in Hailsham.

Best Time to Visit Hailsham

The best time to visit Hailsham is during the spring or summer, when the countryside is in full bloom and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hailsham

Do:

  • Do take a walk along the Cuckoo Trail and explore the beautiful countryside that surrounds Hailsham.
  • Do visit Michelham Priory and Gardens, a historic house and garden that dates back to the 13th century.
  • Do try some British classics like fish and chips, shepherd’s pie, and Sussex cream tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget your walking shoes - there are plenty of walking paths and trails to explore in Hailsham.
  • Don’t miss a visit to the Redoubt Fortress, a circular fortress dating back to the 19th century.
  • Don’t leave without trying some of the local beer or wine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hailsham

Hailsham is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take basic safety precautions such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking doors and windows at night.

Health in Hailsham

There are several medical facilities in Hailsham, including a hospital and several GP practices.

Getting to Hailsham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hailsham

The closest airport to Hailsham is London Gatwick Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Hailsham

Hailsham has a regular bus service that connects the town with nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Taxis and trains are also available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Hailsham

The town is small enough to explore on foot, but there are also plenty of cycling paths and taxis available for visitors who want to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Hailsham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eastbourne

Just a short drive from Hailsham, Eastbourne is a charming coastal town known for its beautiful beaches and historic pier.

Seven Sisters Country Park

Located on the South Downs, Seven Sisters Country Park offers stunning views of the coast and plenty of walking and cycling paths to explore.

Brighton

A vibrant and lively city, Brighton is known for its colorful beach huts, boutique shops, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

With its beautiful countryside, rich history, and charming culture, Hailsham is the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the best of East Sussex. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming market town has to offer.
