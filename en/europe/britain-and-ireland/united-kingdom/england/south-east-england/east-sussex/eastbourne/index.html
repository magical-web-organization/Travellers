Introduction to Eastbourne

Nestled in East Sussex, South East England, Eastbourne is a charming seaside town that offers visitors a glimpse of bygone times. It boasts of a rich history, stunning coastal scenery, and diverse cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in museums, hiking, or water sports, this quaint destination has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eastbourne

When it comes to places to visit, Eastbourne has something for people with all kinds of interests. Here are some of the top destinations that should definitely make it onto your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eastbourne Heritage Centre - Learn about the town’s history, from the prehistoric times to present day at this fascinating museum.
  • Redoubt Fortress and Military Museum - Discover Eastbourne’s military past, from Napoleonic times to World War II, at this impressive fortification.
  • Towner Art Gallery - Check out the latest exhibits by contemporary artists and craftspeople at this popular art gallery.
  • Birling Gap and the Seven Sisters - Take a walk along the scenic chalk cliffs and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Beachy Head - It’s the highest chalk sea cliff in Britain and the perfect place to go for a stroll on a sunny day.

Natural Attractions of Eastbourne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sovereign Harbour - It’s the largest marina complex in the UK, with a host of shops, restaurants, and cafes to explore.
  • Holywell Beach - It’s a secret beach nestled between the Eastbourne cliffs, and a perfect spot for swimming or picnicking.
  • Pevensey Bay - This quaint seaside village is just a few miles from Eastbourne and is famous for its superb seafood restaurants and stunning beach.
  • South Downs National Park - Go hiking or cycling in the beautiful chalk hills or enjoy a leisurely picnic in the sunshine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastbourne

For outdoor enthusiasts, Eastbourne has a lot of scenic hiking and biking routes. Some of the most noteworthy ones are:

  • The South Downs Way - This 100-mile trail offers panoramic views of the rolling hills, picturesque villages and historical landmarks.
  • Eastbourne Promenade - A captivating route, perfect for a relaxing seaside walk and admiring the historic pier.
  • Beachy Head - This circular route takes you around the stunning cliffs and the rolling hills of the South Downs, and it also provides a splendid view of the Eastbourne pier.

Best Things to Do in Eastbourne: Activities and Tours

Exciting activities and tours await you in Eastbourne. Here are some of the best:

  • Eastbourne Pier - It’s a landmark attraction of the town, offering shops, restaurants, activities, and rides for kids and adults.
  • Eastbourne Bandstand - Concerts and performances at the scenic seafront bandstand are a must-experience for live music enthusiasts.
  • Watersports - The beaches of Eastbourne offer an array of water sports, including windsurfing, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding.
  • Eastbourne Walking Festival - Embark on a walking tour of Eastbourne to explore the town’s rich history and natural beauty.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - Take a tour of the vineyards and sample local wines at the nearby Carr Taylor Vineyard.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastbourne

To make the most of your visit, here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene of Eastbourne

No trip is complete without sampling the local cuisine of your destination. Here are some of the must-try local dishes and beverages of Eastbourne:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips - This quintessential British dish is a must-try, and Eastbourne has some of the best.
  • Sussex Smokie - Mackerel and haddock cooked in cream and cheese sauce, served with bread or potatoes.
  • Rabbit Stew - A hearty and filling dish made with rabbit meat, vegetables and seasonings.
  • Bangers and Mash - This classic British dish features sausages and mashed potatoes served with onion gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Sussex Wine - The vineyards of Eastbourne produce some excellent wines, especially the sparkling varieties.
  • Tea - Known for its love of tea, Eastbourne has many tea rooms and cafes where you can indulge in a cup of soothing tea alongside a slice of cake or scone.

Where to Stay in Eastbourne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Eastbourne has a plethora of choices, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Some of the best options include:

  • The Grand Hotel - This iconic Victorian hotel is a landmark destination, with opulent rooms and idyllic sea views.
  • Citrus Hotel Eastbourne - Located in the town center, it’s a stylish and contemporary hotel known for its exceptional comfort and service.
  • The Cavendish Hotel - A four-star hotel known for offering excellent value, delightful ambiance and superb service.
  • The Balmoral Guesthouse – A charming family-owned guesthouse with well-furnished rooms, friendly staff, and a tranquil environment that’s perfect for a peaceful getaway.

What to Buy in Eastbourne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some souvenirs to take back home, Eastbourne offers a variety of local products and items you can purchase. These include:

  • Seaside Rock aka sweets - These candy sticks are a hit with kids and adults alike, with a variety of flavors to choose from.
  • Sussex Wine – As mentioned earlier, the vineyards around Eastbourne produce some excellent wines that you should definitely bring back home.
  • Local Artisan Crafts - Eastbourne has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans creating beautiful handcrafted items such as pottery and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastbourne

Eastbourne is known for its rich cultural heritage, evident in its town layout, architecture, and iconic landmarks. The town’s residents take pride in their customs and traditions, which include:

  • Pride of Eastbourne Festival - An annual festival that celebrates the town’s diversity, with a parade, music performances, and other festivities.
  • South Downs Folk Festival – This event showcases the best of Sussex with live music, storytelling, dance, workshops and much more each year.

Best Time to Visit Eastbourne

The best time to visit Eastbourne is between May and September when the weather is mild and sunny, and the days are long. This is the peak tourist season, and the beaches and other popular attractions get crowded. If you prefer to avoid the crowds, visit between October and April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastbourne

Do:

  • Do enjoy the scenic beauty of the coast with a seaside walk or a sunset stroll on the pier.
  • Do visit the Towner Art Gallery and discover the latest contemporary art exhibits.
  • Do try out the local dishes and beverages, including wine and tea.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs, including the environment.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places, especially on the beach, which is detrimental to the environment.
  • Don’t be reckless in outdoor activities like hiking and biking as it can lead to accidents.
  • Don’t park your car in restricted zones or block the traffic.

Safety and Health

Eastbourne is a safe destination to travel to. However, like every other city, it’s important to stay vigilant and take care of your health and safety.

Safety in Eastbourne

  • Take care of your belongings when out and about, especially on crowded beaches or at popular tourist spots.
  • Always lock your hotel room and avoid carrying too much cash while traveling.
  • Take note of the safety guidelines of the outdoor activities you plan to partake in, such as hiking or biking.

Health in Eastbourne

  • The town has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, in case of any health concerns.
  • Always carry your essential medicines, sunscreen, insect repellent, and first aid kit while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Eastbourne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastbourne is well-connected to other parts of the UK, making it easy for visitors to reach the town. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Eastbourne

  • London Gatwick Airport – 37 miles away from Eastbourne
  • London Heathrow Airport – 88 miles away from Eastbourne

Bus Stations Near Eastbourne

  • Eastbourne Bus Station – Located in the town center, it connects with various other places within East Sussex and other parts of the UK.

Other Options

  • Trains – Eastbourne Railway Station connects the town with Brighton and London.
  • Taxis – Taxis can be found in various locations throughout town.

How to Get Around in Eastbourne

Eastbourne is a small town and can be explored on foot. However, if you’re looking for alternative modes of transport, here are some options:

  • Buses – Stagecoach buses operate throughout the town and nearby areas.
  • Taxis – Taxis can be found at various locations throughout town.
  • Cycling – The town has several bike rental options, and you can also explore the nearby South Downs National Park on a cycling tour.

Where to Go Next from Eastbourne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eastbourne makes for an excellent base to explore the nearby towns, villages, and natural parks. Here are some nearby destinations that you can visit:

  • Brighton - A popular seaside destination known for its vibrant nightlife, entertainment and shopping.
  • Hastings - A historic seaside town, famous for the Battle of Hastings and a charming old town.
  • Lewes - A charming town with a rich history and cultural heritage, famous for its music and literary festivals.
  • South Downs National Park - A scenic and picturesque park, perfect for hiking and exploring nature.

Conclusion

Eastbourne is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered, with a fascinating mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its numerous attractions, outdoor activities, local cuisine and customs, and excellent accommodations and transport options, it’s a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer trip. So plan your trip today and embark on an unforgettable journey to Eastbourne.
