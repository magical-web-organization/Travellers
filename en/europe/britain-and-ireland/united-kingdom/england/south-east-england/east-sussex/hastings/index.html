Introduction to Hastings

Located in East Sussex, in South East England, Hastings is a charming coastal town filled with unique cultural experiences, history, natural beauty and delicious food. With a rich cultural heritage dating back to the Norman Conquest and a buzzing arts scene, Hastings is a popular destination for travelers seeking a mix of relaxation, adventure and exploration.

List of Places to Visit in Hastings

Hastings offers a variety of places of interest for visitors to explore. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hastings Castle: This historic castle ruins dates back to the Norman Conquest and offers stunning views over the town.

  2. Fishermen’s Museum: Dedicated to the town’s fishing history, this museum provides a fascinating insight into the local industry culture.

  3. Hastings Contemporary: This art gallery features contemporary works from British and international artists, showcasing a range of art forms from painting to sculpture and multimedia.

  4. Jerwood Gallery: Set in a modern building overlooking the seafront, Jerwood Gallery houses a collection of modern British art.

Natural Attractions of Hastings: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hastings Country Park: This expansive nature reserve offers stunning views over the sea and cliffs, with trails perfect for hiking and biking.

  2. Old Town Hastings: this charming quarter of the town features a range of independent shops, cafes, restaurants and pubs, as well as a pebble beach and seafront promenade.

  3. Hastings Pier: This historic pier was recently underwent renovation, and now offers visitors a range of activities including mini-golf, restaurants, and live music events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hastings

Hastings has an array of hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the town and coast. Here are some popular routes:

  • East Hill and Hastings Country Park: A circular route across Hastings Country Park with sea views and panoramic views from the top of East Hill.

  • The 1066 Country Walk: A 31-mile path leading from Pevensey Castle to Rye, passing by Hastings and Battle and offering breathtaking scenery of rolling hills and seaside villages.

Best Things to Do in Hastings: Activities and Tours

  1. Bexhill-on-Sea: This charming town is just a short drive from Hastings and offers a range of activities including an art deco cinema and the De La Warr Pavilion, an internationally renowned arts venue.

  2. Adventure Golf Hastings: This complex features two mini-golf courses, a treetop adventure course and a boating pond.

  3. Hastings Museum and Art Gallery: This museum showcases local history, art, antique furniture, and natural history exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hastings

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary for visitors to Hastings who want to experience the best the town has to offer:

Day Activity
1 Visit Hastings Castle and explore the Old Town
2 Take the 1066 Country Walk and enjoy the scenic views
3 Visit the Fishermen’s Museum and the Hastings Contemporary gallery
4 Go for a hike in the Hastings Country Park and grab a bite at a pub in Old Town
5 Visit the Jerwood Gallery and take a stroll along Hastings Pier
6 Spend the day in Bexhill-on-Sea, visiting the De La Warr Pavilion and enjoying the local cuisine
7 wrap up your trip with a relaxing day at the beach, sipping local beverages and enjoying the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Hastings

Hastings is home to a range of restaurants, cafes, and pubs serving delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips: No trip to the seaside town of Hastings is complete without trying this classic dish, served with mushy peas and tartar sauce.

  2. Hastings Pudding: A dessert made with breadcrumbs, golden syrup, and spices, served with a scoop of ice cream.

  3. Stargazy Pie: A traditional Cornish pie dish typically made with pilchards, onion, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  1. Harvey’s Ale: A local Sussex brew with a strong, hoppy flavor.

  2. Sovereign Bitter: A refreshing, full-bodied English ale made with a blend of East Kent Goldings and Fuggles hops.

  3. Sussex Wine: A range of locally produced wines are available in the area, including sparkling and rose varieties.

Where to Stay in Hastings: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Hastings caters to a range of travelers’ tastes. Here are some options in the area:

  • The White Rock Hotel: a stylish, boutique hotel located in the heart of Hastings Old Town, featuring sea views and modern amenities.

  • The Old Rectory: a unique, historic guesthouse with individually decorated rooms, located in the town’s quiet, residential area.

  • Hastings Youth Hostel: a budget-friendly accommodation option located a short walk from the town’s main attractions, with communal cooking facilities and social lounges.

What to Buy in Hastings: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From quirky antiques to food and beverages, Hastings has something unique for every traveler. Here are some souvenir ideas:

  • Local arts and crafts: Hastings boasts a vibrant arts and crafts scene, with unique ceramics, jewelry, and clothing available for purchase.

  • Antiques: The town is filled with unique antique dealers selling everything from vintage furniture to old books and records.

  • Local food and drink: Sample some of the town’s famous wines, locally brewed beer, or artisanal food products like jams and chutneys.

Local Culture and Customs of Hastings

Like much of the UK, Hastings has a rich cultural heritage with some unique customs and traditions. Visitors should be sure to respect the local culture and engage with it in a respectful way.

Hastings has a diverse, multicultural population, with many celebrations throughout the year including the Hastings Bonfire Night, the Fat Tuesday festival, and the Hastings Pride parade.

Best Time to Visit Hastings

The best time to visit Hastings is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the town comes alive with cultural festivals, music events, and food fairs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hastings

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Hastings:

Do:

  • Explore the Old Town and sample the local cuisine.
  • Visit the town’s seaside attractions and scenic hiking routes.
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the local population.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter on the beach or in public places.
  • Be afraid to engage with locals and ask for directions or advice.
  • Visit sensitive cultural sites without proper respect and knowledge.

Safety and Health

Hastings is generally a safe and healthy town to visit, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Hastings

  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas like the seafront or Old Town.
  • Take precautions against the sun when spending time outdoors.
  • Follow the advice of local authorities in the case of an emergency or natural disaster.

Health in Hastings

  • Make sure to have travel insurance before arriving in the UK.
  • Bring any necessary medications and a copy of your medical history.
  • Stay hydrated and seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Hastings: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hastings is well-connected to the rest of the UK via several transportation options. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Hastings

  • London Gatwick Airport: 1 hour 20 minutes by train or car.
  • London Heathrow Airport: 2 hours by train or car.

Bus Stations Near Hastings

  • Hastings bus station is located in the town center, with regular services to London and other major cities in the south of England.
  • Other local bus stations include Bexhill and Rye.

Other Options

  • Train: Hastings has a train station with direct links to London and other major UK cities.

How to Get Around in Hastings

Hastings is a compact town with most attractions within walking distance. However, there are several transportation options available:

  • Bus: Stagecoach buses cover the town and surrounding areas, with regular services to nearby destinations like Bexhill, Eastbourne, and Rye.

  • Train: The local rail network connects Hastings with other major towns and cities in the UK.

  • Bicycle: There are several bike rental options available in Hastings, with easy access to nearby cycle routes and trails.

Where to Go Next from Hastings: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hastings is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Some nearby destinations worth visiting include:

  • Rye: A charming medieval town with cobbled streets, antique shops, and historic pubs, just a short drive from Hastings.

  • Eastbourne: Located on the south coast of England, Eastbourne offers stunning beaches, scenic hiking routes, and cultural attractions like the Towner Art Gallery and the Eastbourne tennis tournament.

Conclusion

Hastings is a beautiful coastal town filled with a rich cultural history, natural beauty, and delicious local cuisine. Visitors can enjoy hiking and biking routes, explore cultural attractions and museums, and sample some of the local wines and artisanal food products. With a range of accommodation options and easy access to nearby destinations, Hastings is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic UK experience.
