Introduction to Robertsbridge

Robertsbridge is a picturesque town located in the county of East Sussex, in the south-east of England. The town is surrounded by beautiful countryside and is home to a wealth of historical and cultural attractions. It is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and relax in a charming rural setting.

List of Places to Visit in Robertsbridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Robertsbridge has a rich history, and there are many fascinating museums and historical sites to explore. A must-visit is the Robertsbridge Abbey, founded in the 12th century by Cistercian monks. Today, the ruins of the abbey are still visible, and visitors can learn about its history at the nearby Robertsbridge Abbey Visitor Centre.

For a glimpse into local history, visit the Robertsbridge Museum, located in the town’s former fire station. The museum showcases artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Robertsbridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Robertsbridge is surrounded by stunning landscapes, from rolling hills and fields to tranquil rivers. There are plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, fishing, and kayaking.

One of the town’s most popular natural attractions is Bedgebury National Pinetum and Forest, just a short drive away. This vast forest is home to a wide variety of trees and wildlife, making it an ideal place to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Robertsbridge

Robertsbridge is a great destination for hikers and cyclists, with plenty of scenic routes to explore. The High Weald Landscape Trail is a 90-mile trail that passes through the town and offers stunning views of the local countryside. The Route 18 cycle path also runs through Robertsbridge, providing a safe and scenic route for bicyclists.

Best Things to Do in Robertsbridge: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor activities, Robertsbridge offers a variety of other things to see and do. Take a ride on the Kent & East Sussex Railway, a heritage steam train that runs through the picturesque countryside. Or, head to the Bodiam Boating Station and rent a canoe or rowboat to explore the River Rother.

For a unique experience, visit the PowderMills Pottery & Gift Shop, where you can watch local artists at work and purchase handmade pottery and other crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Robertsbridge

Day 1: Explore the town’s historical sites, including Robertsbridge Abbey, the Robertsbridge Museum, and the Parish Church of St Mary the Virgin.

Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride along the High Weald Landscape Trail or Route 18 cycle path.

Day 3: Visit Bedgebury National Pinetum and Forest for a day of outdoor adventure.

Day 4: Take a ride on the Kent & East Sussex Railway or rent a boat at the Bodiam Boating Station.

Day 5: Explore the local arts scene at the PowderMills Pottery & Gift Shop.

Day 6: Relax and take in the scenery with a leisurely picnic in the countryside.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs in the town’s many antique and gift shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Robertsbridge

Must-Try Dishes

Robertsbridge is known for its fresh and locally sourced produce, and there are many delicious dishes to try. A local specialty is the Romney Marsh lamb, a breed of sheep that is raised in the nearby marshes and has a unique flavor.

For a sweet treat, try the Sussex Pond Pudding, a traditional dessert made with lemon, sugar, butter, and a suet crust.

Local Beverages

The town is home to a number of excellent pubs and breweries, serving up a variety of local beers and ciders. The Robertsbridge Club is a popular spot for a pint, while the 360 Degree Brewing Company is known for its craft beers and brewery tours.

Where to Stay in Robertsbridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great accommodation options in Robertsbridge, from cozy B&Bs to luxury hotels. The George Inn is a historic pub with rooms, offering a comfortable and unique stay. The Chequers Inn is another popular choice, with stylish rooms and an on-site restaurant.

For a more budget-friendly option, the King John’s Lodge is a well-reviewed hostel located in the heart of town.

What to Buy in Robertsbridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Robertsbridge is home to many boutique and antique shops, offering a wide range of souvenirs and local products. The town is known for its antiques, and the many shops lining the High Street are a great place to browse for unique treasures.

For a taste of local flavor, visit the Robertsbridge Farmers’ Market, held on the second Saturday of every month. Here, you can purchase fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts from local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Robertsbridge

Robertsbridge is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, but will find locals happy to engage in conversation and share their experiences.

Best Time to Visit Robertsbridge

The best time to visit Robertsbridge is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is beautiful year-round, and each season offers its own unique charms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Robertsbridge

Do:

  • Explore the local countryside on foot or by bike.
  • Visit the many historical and cultural attractions.
  • Shop for antiques and local crafts in the town’s many shops.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Leave litter or damage the local environment.
  • Be disruptive or disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Robertsbridge

Robertsbridge is generally a very safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take standard precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in Robertsbridge

The town has a local GP surgery and pharmacy, as well as several shops and restaurants that cater to special dietary needs. However, visitors with specific health concerns should consult with their own doctor before traveling.

Getting to Robertsbridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Robertsbridge

The nearest airports are London Gatwick and London Heathrow. From there, visitors can take trains or buses to the nearby town of Hastings or Rye, and take a short taxi or bus ride to Robertsbridge.

Bus Stations Near Robertsbridge

Robertsbridge has a bus station located in the town center, with regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car to explore the surrounding areas, or taking a taxi or ride-share service for shorter journeys.

How to Get Around in Robertsbridge

Robertsbridge is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of the town center. Alternatively, visitors can rent bikes from local operators or take the bus to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Robertsbridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Robertsbridge is located in the middle of some of England’s most beautiful countryside, with many nearby destinations to explore. The historic towns of Hastings and Rye are just a short drive away, as are the rolling hills of the South Downs National Park.

Conclusion

Robertsbridge is a gem of the East Sussex countryside, with a rich history and plenty of outdoor activities and cultural attractions to explore. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-packed holiday, this charming little town has something for everyone.
