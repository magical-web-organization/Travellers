Introduction to Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin are two neighboring towns located on the southeastern coast of the Isle of Wight, England. Known for their sandy beaches, amusement parks, and stunning countryside, these towns are a popular destination among British holidaymakers and international visitors alike. With an abundance of natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage, Sandown and Shanklin offer a wide range of attractions and activities for every visitor’s interest.

List of Places to Visit in Sandown and Shanklin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sandown and Shanklin boast an impressive array of museums and historical places that depict various aspects of the towns’ cultural heritage.

Dinosaur Isle

Located on Sandown’s seafront, Dinosaur Isle is a fascinating museum dedicated to the island’s unique history of dinosaur fossils. The museum displays multimedia exhibits, life-sized reconstructions, and interactive activities that provide visitors with an immersive experience of the Isle of Wight’s Jurassic past.

Shanklin Chine

Shanklin Chine is a picturesque gorge that has been carved out by a stream over thousands of years. The chine features a series of waterfalls, lush vegetation, and a fascinating museum that takes visitors on a journey through the history of Shanklin’s geology and flora.

Brading Roman Villa

Brading Roman Villa is a beautifully preserved Roman villa that was discovered in the 19th century. The villa features stunning mosaics, intricate frescoes, and a wealth of artifacts that tell the story of Roman life on the Isle of Wight.

Natural Attractions of Sandown and Shanklin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sandown and Shanklin are blessed with breathtaking natural scenery and offer a wide range of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy.

Isle of Wight Coastal Path

The Isle of Wight coastal path is a 70-mile long trail that runs from the seaside town of Cowes to the southernmost tip of the island. The trail passes through Sandown and Shanklin and offers stunning views of the island’s scenic coastline.

Sandown Bay

Sandown Bay is a long stretch of golden sand that offers excellent swimming and sunbathing opportunities. The bay is also home to a variety of water sports activities, including surfing, kayaking, and windsurfing.

Shanklin Beach

Shanklin Beach is a beautiful sandy beach that is flanked by towering cliffs. The beach offers plenty of space for sunbathing and relaxing, as well as rock pools and a pier for visitors to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin offer numerous hiking and biking trails that provide visitors with an intimate view of the island’s natural beauty.

The Wight Trail

The Wight Trail is a 60-mile circular route that takes visitors through some of the Isle of Wight’s most stunning scenery. The route passes through Sandown and Shanklin and offers breathtaking views of the coastline, countryside, and famous landmarks such as the Needles and Osborne House.

The Red Squirrel Trail

The Red Squirrel Trail is a 32-mile long cycling route that takes visitors through the heart of the Isle of Wight. The route passes through Sandown and Shanklin and offers a glimpse of the island’s quaint villages, historic landmarks, and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Sandown and Shanklin: Activities and Tours

Sandown and Shanklin offer a wide range of activities and tours that cater to every visitor’s preferences.

Sandham Gardens

Sandham Gardens is a popular amusement park located on Sandown’s seafront. The park features a variety of rides, arcades, and attractions that are sure to entertain visitors of all ages.

Shanklin Theatre

Shanklin Theatre is a beautifully restored Victorian theatre that hosts a variety of performances throughout the year. From drama to comedy, musicals to concerts, Shanklin Theatre offers something for every visitor’s taste.

Isle of Wight Zoo

The Isle of Wight Zoo is a conservation and education center that is home to a variety of rare and exotic species. Visitors can get up close to the animals and learn about the zoo’s conservation efforts and programs.

Food and Drink Scene of Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin offer a range of local and international cuisines and beverages that are sure to tantalize the taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

Crab and Lobster

The Isle of Wight is known for its fresh seafood, and Sandown and Shanklin are no exception. Visitors should try the locally caught crab and lobster, which is often served in sandwiches or salads.

Garlic Bread

Garlic bread is a local specialty that has been popularized by the island’s Italian community. Visitors will find numerous restaurants serving different variations of the dish, including cheesy garlic bread, garlic ciabatta, and garlic pizza.

Local Beverages

Isle of Wight Gin

The Isle of Wight is home to several gin producers, and visitors should sample some of the locally distilled gins, such as Mermaid Gin or Wight Mermaids Gin.

Fuggle-Dee-Dum Beer

Fuggle-Dee-Dum Beer is a locally brewed ale that is popular among beer enthusiasts. The beer is made using hops grown on the island and is named after a popular folk song.

Where to Stay in Sandown and Shanklin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sandown and Shanklin offer a range of accommodation options to suit every visitor’s budget and preferences.

The Hambledon Hotel

The Hambledon Hotel is a charming Edwardian hotel located in Shanklin. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant serving locally sourced produce, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

The Grange

The Grange is a Victorian hotel located on Sandown’s seafront. The hotel features well-appointed rooms, an outdoor swimming pool, and a range of activities for children.

The Wighthill Hotel

The Wighthill Hotel is a family-run hotel situated in Sandown. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant serving home-cooked meals, and a garden with sea views.

What to Buy in Sandown and Shanklin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors will find numerous souvenir shops and local markets in Sandown and Shanklin that sell a range of products unique to the Isle of Wight.

Isle of Wight Glass

Isle of Wight Glass is a famous glass-making company that produces stunning handcrafted glassware. Visitors can purchase one-of-a-kind glass ornaments, vases, and sculptures to bring back home.

Lavender Products

The Isle of Wight is known for its lavender fields, and visitors can purchase a range of products made from locally grown lavender, including essential oils, soaps, and candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin have a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the year with various festivals and events.

Isle of Wight Festival

The Isle of Wight Festival is a three-day music festival that takes place in nearby Newport. The festival features international headliners, local talent, and a wide range of food and drink stalls.

Isle of Wight Walking Festival

The Isle of Wight Walking Festival is a two-week event that takes visitors on guided walks through some of the island’s most stunning scenery. The festival offers walks for all abilities and interests, including historical walks, nature walks, and even ghost walks.

Best Time to Visit Sandown and Shanklin

The best time to visit Sandown and Shanklin is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are bustling with activity. The shoulder months of April to May and September to October also offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandown and Shanklin

Do:

  • Do take a walk along the Isle of Wight Coastal Path.
  • Do try the local seafood and garlic bread.
  • Do visit the museums and historical places.
  • Do enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.
  • Don’t swim in restricted areas.
  • Don’t forget to check the tide times before visiting the beach.
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin are generally safe towns, but visitors should take certain precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in public places.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Follow the safety instructions for water sports and activities.

Health in Sandown and Shanklin

The Isle of Wight is served by a modern hospital and several medical clinics. Visitors should take the following health precautions:

  • Bring any necessary prescription medications with you.
  • Apply sunscreen regularly when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Consult a doctor in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Sandown and Shanklin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sandown and Shanklin

The nearest airport to Sandown and Shanklin is Southampton Airport, which is about 26 miles away. Other airports within driving distance include Gatwick Airport and Heathrow Airport in London.

Bus Stations Near Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin are well-served by public transportation, with regular bus services connecting the towns to other parts of the island. The main bus station is located in Newport, about 10 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Sandown and Shanklin by ferry from Portsmouth or Southampton. The ferry ride takes about 45 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the departure point.

How to Get Around in Sandown and Shanklin

Sandown and Shanklin are easily accessible on foot, and visitors can explore the towns’ attractions and beaches by walking. Alternatively, visitors can rent bicycles or use local buses and taxis to get around the island.

Where to Go Next from Sandown and Shanklin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby destinations and excursions from Sandown and Shanklin, including:

Osborne House

Osborne House is a stunning palatial residence that was once the summer home of Queen Victoria. The house features opulent interiors, extensive gardens, and a museum that showcases Victoria’s life on the Isle of Wight.

Carisbrooke Castle

Carisbrooke Castle is a medieval fortification that dates back to the 12th century. The castle features a range of exhibitions, including those that depict its history as a place of imprisonment for Charles I and as a military outpost during World War II.

Conclusion

Sandown and Shanklin are two picturesque towns situated on the southeastern coast of the Isle of Wight. With their stunning beaches, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage, they are a popular destination for visitors looking for a relaxing vacation by the sea. Whether you want to explore the museums and historical sites, indulge in local food and drink, or spend your days hiking, biking, and enjoying the great outdoors, Sandown and Shanklin offer something for every visitor’s interest.
