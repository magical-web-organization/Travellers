Introduction to Horsham

Nestled in the heart of West Sussex, Horsham is a charming market town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural landmarks. With a history dating back to the Middle Ages, this town is a must-visit for history buffs and nature enthusiasts alike.

From beautiful gardens to ancient castles, Horsham has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you plan your perfect trip to this stunning town. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Horsham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Horsham Museum and Art Gallery

The Horsham Museum and Art Gallery is a must-visit for history lovers. Housed in a beautiful Victorian building, the museum showcases the town’s rich history through various exhibits, including a Victorian kitchen, a medieval gallery, and an art gallery featuring works by local artists.

St Mary’s Church

Located in the town centre, St Mary’s Church is a beautiful example of English Gothic architecture. The church dates back to the 13th century and boasts a stunning interior with intricate stained glass windows and detailed carvings.

Leonardslee Lakes and Gardens

Leonardslee Lakes and Gardens is a true gem of Horsham. This stunning estate features a range of scenic landscapes, including lakes, ancient woodlands, and a rock garden. Visitors can also spot rare wildlife species, such as wallabies and parakeets.

Natural Attractions of Horsham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Warnham Nature Reserve

Warnham Nature Reserve is a 92-acre haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can spot a variety of bird species, as well as dragonflies, butterflies, and other insects. The reserve also boasts four beautiful lakes and scenic walking trails.

Southwater Country Park

Southwater Country Park is a popular destination for families and nature lovers alike. The park features a range of outdoor activities, including fishing, sailing, and canoeing. The park also has a play area and a miniature railway for children.

Chesworth Farm

Chesworth Farm is a working farm that also serves as a nature reserve. Visitors can explore the farmland and spot some of the rarest wildlife species in the area, including orchids and water voles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Horsham

Horsham is home to a range of scenic walking and cycling routes. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Downs Link Trail: A 37-mile trail that connects Guildford to the south coast
  • The Riverside Walk: A 3-mile route that follows the River Arun
  • The Horsham Park Trail: A 2-mile circular route that takes you through Horsham Park

Best Things to Do in Horsham: Activities and Tours

Horsham Markets

The Horsham Markets are a must-visit for foodies and bargain hunters alike. The town has a farmer’s market every Saturday, where you can buy fresh produce and artisanal products from local vendors. The town also hosts a monthly French-style market that sells everything from cheese to clothing.

The Capitol Horsham

The Capitol Horsham is a theatre and cinema that showcases a range of productions throughout the year, including movies, plays, and concerts.

Guided Tours

Horsham has a range of guided tours that take you through the town’s most iconic landmarks, including the Horsham Museum, the St Mary’s Church, and the Leonardslee Lakes and Gardens.

Week-Long Itinerary for Horsham

Day 1: Horsham Museum and Art Gallery, St Mary’s Church

Day 2: Leonardslee Lakes and Gardens, Warnham Nature Reserve

Day 3: Chesworth Farm, The Riverside Walk

Day 4: Horsham Markets, The Capitol Horsham

Day 5: Downs Link Trail, Southwater Country Park

Day 6: Guided Tour of Horsham, The Horsham Park Trail

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a day in the town centre, exploring the local shops and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Horsham

Must-Try Dishes

Sussex Smokie

A Sussex Smokie is a traditional dish made of smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions. It’s a must-try for seafood lovers.

Horsham Pudding

Horsham Pudding is a dessert that features apples, sultanas, and rum. It’s a sweet treat that’s perfect for those with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

Harvey’s Brewery

Harvey’s Brewery is a local brewery that produces a range of award-winning beers and ales. Visitors can tour the brewery and taste some of their delicious beverages.

Ridgeview Wine Estate

Ridgeview Wine Estate is a family-run winery that produces some of the best English sparkling wines. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyard and taste some of the estate’s high-quality wines.

What to Buy in Horsham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Horsham Gingerbread

Horsham Gingerbread is a local delicacy that’s been around for over 200 years. It’s a sweet and spicy treat that’s perfect for those with a sweet tooth.

Local Honey

Horsham is home to a range of local honey producers. Be sure to pick up some delicious honey as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Horsham

Horsham is a charming town that values its heritage and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s customs, such as observing quiet hours in residential areas and keeping the town clean.

Best Time to Visit Horsham

The best time to visit Horsham is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Horsham

Do:

  • Do visit the Horsham Markets to taste some of the local produce.
  • Do take a guided tour of the town to learn more about its history and culture.
  • Do visit the Leonardslee Lakes and Gardens for some stunning natural views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the town or the natural attractions.
  • Don’t be loud and disruptive in residential areas.
  • Don’t ignore the town’s customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Horsham

Horsham is a safe town, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take the necessary precautions to secure their personal belongings.

Health in Horsham

Horsham has a range of medical facilities, including pharmacies and hospitals.

How to Get Around in Horsham

Horsham is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a bike or take a cab for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Horsham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brighton

Brighton is a popular seaside town located just 20 miles from Horsham. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and culture, as well as its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Arundel

Located just 9 miles from Horsham, Arundel is a medieval town that’s known for its stunning castle and picturesque streets.

Conclusion

Horsham is a beautiful town that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its stunning natural attractions and rich cultural landmarks, this town has something for everyone. Plan your trip today, and discover the best of West Sussex!
