Introduction to Worthing

Worthing is a welcoming seaside town located in West Sussex, South East England. With its beautiful beaches, historic attractions, and lively culture, Worthing is the perfect destination for anyone looking to explore the South coast of England.

Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or just relaxing on the beach, Worthing has something to offer to everyone. From its natural beauty to its rich history and culture, this charming town is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories.

List of Places to Visit in Worthing

Explore Worthing’s top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Worthing Museum and Art Gallery: Discover the history of Worthing and its surrounding area through a collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts.

  • St. Mary’s Church: Dating back to the 12th century, this historic church is an impressive example of Gothic architecture.

  • High Salvington Windmill: A well-preserved 18th-century windmill that offers a glimpse into Worthing’s agricultural history.

Natural Attractions of Worthing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Worthing Beaches: Enjoy the sun, sand, and surf at one of Worthing’s beautiful beaches. The most popular ones include Goring Beach, Lancing Beach, and Worthing Beach.

  • South Downs National Park: Take a hike or bike ride through this stunning national park, which is located just north of Worthing and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Cissbury Ring: This Iron Age hillfort is one of the largest in Sussex and provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Worthing

There are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore in and around Worthing. Some of the best routes include:

  • South Downs Way: This 100-mile trail runs through the South Downs National Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Worthing Circular Walk: A 12-mile circular walk that takes you through some of Worthing’s most beautiful scenery, including the seafront, countryside, and historic landmarks.

  • Coastal Cycle Route: A 12-mile route that takes you along the coastline and offers stunning views of the English Channel.

Best Things to Do in Worthing: Activities and Tours

  • Pavilion Theatre: Catch a show or performance at this historic theatre, which features a wide variety of productions throughout the year.

  • Worthing Pier: Take a stroll on Worthing’s iconic pier, which offers beautiful views of the sea and is home to several restaurants and bars.

  • Splashpoint Leisure Centre: Enjoy a day of swimming and waterslides at this fun-filled leisure centre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Worthing

Day 1: Explore Worthing’s historic landmarks, including St. Mary’s Church and High Salvington Windmill.

Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride through the South Downs National Park.

Day 3: Relax on one of Worthing’s beautiful beaches, such as Goring Beach or Lancing Beach.

Day 4: Enjoy a day of shopping and dining in Worthing’s town centre.

Day 5: Take a tour of Worthing Museum and Art Gallery.

Day 6: Explore the countryside with a visit to Cissbury Ring.

Day 7: Attend a show or performance at the Pavilion Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of Worthing

Worthing is home to a variety of restaurants and cafes, offering everything from traditional English fare to international cuisine. Don’t miss these must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: No trip to the seaside town is complete without trying this classic dish.

  • Roast Beef and Yorkshire Pudding: A traditional British dish that is sure to satisfy.

  • Sussex Smokies: A local specialty consisting of smoked haddock served with mashed potato and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Harvey’s Brewery Beer: A local brew that has been produced in Sussex since 1790.

  • Sussex Wine: With a climate similar to that of Champagne, the region is known for its high-quality sparkling wines.

Where to Stay in Worthing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Worthing is home to a variety of accommodation types, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some options to consider:

  • The Burlington Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Worthing town centre.

  • Seaview Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located just steps away from the beach.

  • YHA Brighton: A budget-friendly hostel located just a short train ride away from Worthing.

What to Buy in Worthing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a unique souvenir or gift from Worthing, here are some ideas:

  • Worthing Rock: A traditional sweet made from sugar and flavouring that is only found in the town.

  • Sussex Trug: A handmade basket made from willow that is commonly used for gardening.

  • Local Art: Visit one of Worthing’s art galleries or studios to find a unique piece of local art to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Worthing

Worthing is a friendly and welcoming town, but here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Tipping: Tipping is not generally expected in Worthing, but it is appreciated for exceptional service.

  • Queuing: Brits take queuing very seriously, so be sure to respect the line.

  • Etiquette: It’s polite to say “please” and “thank you” when ordering food or asking for assistance, and to avoid speaking loudly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Worthing

The best time to visit Worthing is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, Worthing is a year-round destination, with plenty to see and do during every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Worthing

Do:

  • Do try local foods and drinks, including Sussex wine and Harvey’s Brewery Beer.

  • Do explore the stunning natural scenery around the town, including the South Downs National Park.

  • Do take a stroll on the iconic Worthing Pier.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t forget to take care when swimming or enjoying water sports in the sea.

  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Worthing

Worthing is generally a safe town, with low crime rates; however, be sure to take these safety precautions:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

  • Stay safe when swimming in the sea by paying attention to warning signs and swimming within designated areas.

  • Drive carefully on the roads, paying attention to speed limits and road signs.

Health in Worthing

Worthing has a good standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and medical clinics in the town.

If you need medical assistance during your stay, contact the National Health Service (NHS) on 111 for non-emergency medical advice.

Getting to Worthing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Worthing is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Worthing

The nearest airports to Worthing are Gatwick Airport (LGW) and Southampton Airport (SOU), which are both located approximately 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Worthing

Worthing has several bus stations that provide regular services to nearby towns and cities, including Brighton and Chichester.

Other Options

You can also travel to Worthing by train, with direct services available from London Victoria Station.

How to Get Around in Worthing

Worthing is a compact town that can easily be explored on foot. There are also several bus routes that operate in and around the town.

Where to Go Next from Worthing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to explore more of Sussex? Here are some nearby towns and cities to visit:

  • Brighton: A lively and popular seaside city with a bustling nightlife and cultural attractions.

  • Chichester: A historic town with a stunning cathedral and Roman archaeological sites.

  • Arundel: A picturesque town with a beautiful castle and a charming high street.

Conclusion

Worthing is a charming and welcoming seaside town with something to offer to every visitor. With its natural beauty, rich history, and lively culture, this popular holiday destination is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind on the beach or explore the stunning countryside, Worthing is the perfect destination for your next vacation.
