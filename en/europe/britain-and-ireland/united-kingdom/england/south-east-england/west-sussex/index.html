Introduction to West Sussex

Nestled in the southeastern part of England, West Sussex is a delightful county that covers an area of 1,988 square kilometers. It lies between Hampshire to the west and East Sussex to the east. The picturesque countryside is a perfect blend of natural and cultural beauty. With stunning beaches, rolling hills, and enchanting woodlands, West Sussex is a hub for outdoor adventures.

Whether exploring the historical architecture, countryside trails, or city centers, West Sussex has something to offer to everyone.

List of Places to Visit in West Sussex

Explore the county to the fullest with these must-visit places.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arundel Castle and Gardens: This medieval castle is set on top of a hill, and it offers stunning views of the town of Arundel. The castle and gardens have been home to the Dukes of Norfolk for over 800 years.
  2. Chichester Cathedral: This impressive place of worship has been a symbol of Christianity in West Sussex for over 900 years. Adorned with unique features and artwork, it is an architectural masterpiece.
  3. Fishbourne Roman Palace: Built in the first century, the Fishbourne Roman Palace is an impressive Roman villa with striking mosaics and operational under-floor heating systems.

Natural Attractions of West Sussex: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. South Downs National Park: The South Downs National Park covers over 1,600 square kilometers of stunning scenery. From rolling landscapes and prehistoric monuments to diverse wildlife, the park is ideal for trekking, cycling, or exploring.
  2. West Wittering Beach: This stunning sandy beach has a blue flag rating and is perfect for swimming and sunbathing. A perfect spot for relaxation and family fun.
  3. Amberley Museum: This open-air museum is set in a beautiful South Downs setting and houses a collection of industrial relics, including locomotives, steam-powered machinery, and vintage buses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Sussex

The county of West Sussex offers visitors plenty of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. South Downs Way: A 100-mile footpath runs through the rolling hills of South Downs National Park, providing stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. Sussex Border Path: This is a 118 km walk and cycling path that follows the border of Sussex County. A picturesque meander through the countryside, suitable for all skill levels.
  3. Chanctonbury Ring Circular Walk: This circular hike passes through the Chanctonbury Ring on the South Downs and is known for its historical significance and panoramic views.

Best Things to Do in West Sussex: Activities and Tours

  1. Goodwood Festival of Speed: This annual event gives motor enthusiasts a chance to witness some of the world’s most iconic vehicles, including vintage cars, F1 racing cars, and motorbikes.
  2. Chichester Festival Theatre: This impressive theatre offers visitors a chance to experience a blend of drama, comedy, and music.
  3. South Downs Gliding Club: Experience flight with a bird’s-eye view of the stunning West Sussex countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Sussex

Day 1: Visit Arundel Castle and Gardens and take a stroll through the town Day 2: Explore Chichester Cathedral and stroll through historic Chichester Day 3: Hike along the South Downs Way, starting from Eastbourne and finishing in Amberley. Day 4: Visit Fishbourne Roman Palace and spend a relaxing afternoon on West Wittering Beach. Day 5: Tour South Downs National Park and enjoy a glider ride at South Downs Gliding Club Day 6: Visit Amberley Museum and explore the open-air industrial revolution exhibits. Day 7: Attend the Goodwood Festival of Speed or catch a show at Chichester Festival Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of West Sussex

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sussex Charmer Cheese: A creamy and nutty cheese made with local milk and aged for one year.
  2. Sussex Pond Pudding: An apple-filled steamed pudding served with warm custard or cream.
  3. Sussex Trug: A selection of locally grown vegetables and mushrooms, served with grilled meats or fish.

Local Beverages

  1. Sussex Ale: A locally brewed beer, well known for its full-bodied taste and floral aroma.
  2. Nyetimber: Award-winning sparkling wine produced with locally grown grapes.
  3. Sussex Cider: A traditional English alcoholic drink made from harvested apples.

Where to Stay in West Sussex: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Ockenden Manor Hotel and Spa: A historic retreat with stunning views of the South Downs.
  2. The Brookfield Hotel: A family-friendly hotel set in a quiet location overlooking the Felpham Beach.
  3. Hawley House: A charming Guesthouse perfect for a quiet getaway located in the heart of Chichester.

What to Buy in West Sussex: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sussex Sweater: A locally made sweater made of wool from West Sussex raised sheep.
  2. Sussex Bonfire Society Mug: A souvenir mug that pays homage to the local tradition of bonfires.
  3. Sussex Honey: A unique honey made from the different types of flowers found in Sussex.

Local Culture and Customs of West Sussex

West Sussex is steeped in history and tradition. The people are friendly and welcoming, and the county boasts a rich cultural heritage. Some of the iconic customs that are still practiced include the Bonfire Night commemorating the defeat of Guy Fawkes, followed by fireworks and a festive parade.

Best Time to Visit West Sussex

The best time to visit West Sussex is between June and September when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the county offers something for visitors all year round, from autumnal walks to cosy pub lunches in winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Sussex

Do:

  1. Explore the countryside trails and take in the scenic views.
  2. Visit the museums and historical places to learn about the county’s rich heritage.
  3. Try the locally brewed ales and wines.

Don’t:

  1. Ignore the customs and traditions celebrated in West Sussex, including Bonfire Night.
  2. Litter or damage the natural scenery.
  3. Overspeed and endanger wildlife on the roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Sussex

West Sussex is a safe and peaceful county, but visitors should be vigilant in crowded areas and keep valuables secured. It is also advisable to be aware of environmental risks around natural attractions such as beaches or hiking trails.

Health in West Sussex

West Sussex has several hospitals and well-equipped medical centres to cater to the health needs of both locals and visitors. It is also advisable to carry insect repellents, sunscreen and appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.

Getting to West Sussex: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Sussex

  1. Gatwick Airport: Located approximately 40km from Brighton.
  2. Southampton Airport: Located approximately 50km from Chichester.

Bus Stations Near West Sussex

  1. Chichester Bus Station
  2. Crawley Bus Station
  3. Worthing Bus Station

Other Options

There are several train stations situated within West Sussex that connect visitors to other major cities in the UK. Cycling, hiking, and driving are other transportation modes that allow visitors to explore the county.

How to Get Around in West Sussex

West Sussex is easily accessible by car or public transport. The county is also bike and walk friendly, and there are several bike rental stations throughout the region.

Where to Go Next from West Sussex: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Brighton: A vibrant seaside town with beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife, located approximately 30km from Chichester.
  2. Portsmouth: A naval city with beautiful waterfronts and naval history, located approximately 30km from Chichester.
  3. London: The capital of England, located approximately 100 km from Chichester and 80km from Crawley.

Conclusion

From rolling hills to stunning beaches, from rich cultural history to vibrant city life, West Sussex is a place that offers something for everyone. It is a place to explore and discover at a leisurely pace; a place where visitors can rejuvenate their minds and bodies with an array of outdoor activities. Pack your bags and embark on an adventure to explore this beautiful region.
