Introduction to Midhurst

Nestled in the heart of the South Downs National Park, Midhurst is a charming market town in West Sussex, England. Known for its rich history, stunning natural surroundings, and lively cultural scene, Midhurst has something for everyone.

Get ready to explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay in Midhurst in this comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Midhurst

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, you’ll find plenty to see and do in Midhurst. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cowdray Ruins: Explore the remains of Cowdray House, a 16th-century Tudor mansion that was once one of the grandest houses in England.
  • Midhurst Museum: Discover the town’s fascinating history at this local museum, with exhibits on everything from Roman times to the present day.
  • St. Ann’s Hill: Hike up to the top of St. Ann’s Hill for panoramic views of the town, and visit its historic chapel, which dates back to the 12th century.

Natural Attractions of Midhurst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South Downs National Park: Take a hike, bike ride, or horseback ride in this breathtaking national park, with rolling hills, lush valleys, and stunning chalk cliffs.
  • Petworth Park: Enjoy a picnic or leisurely stroll in this expansive park, home to a deer park, lake, and 17th-century mansion.
  • Cowdray Park Golf Course: Tee off at this top-rated golf course, with 18 holes set in the beautiful Cowdray Estate.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Midhurst

Midhurst is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails offering scenic views of the South Downs and surrounding countryside. Some of the top routes include:

  • The South Downs Way: This epic long-distance trail covers 100 miles from Winchester to Eastbourne, passing through Midhurst along the way.
  • Cowdray Estate Trail: Explore the Cowdray Estate on this circular hiking route, which takes in the ruins of Cowdray House and stunning countryside views.
  • Easy Access Trail: This wheelchair-friendly trail is perfect for families with young children or those with limited mobility, with gentle gradients and stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Midhurst: Activities and Tours

For those looking for fun and unique experiences, Midhurst has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Cowdray Park Polo Club: Catch a match or take a lesson at this world-renowned polo club, which has hosted royalty and celebrities alike.
  • Midhurst Tennis Club: Play a game of tennis or take a lesson at this friendly local club, with both indoor and outdoor courts available.
  • Cowdray Farm Shop and Café: Sample local produce and enjoy a delicious meal at this farm shop and café, located on the Cowdray Estate.

Week-Long Itinerary for Midhurst

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Midhurst:

  • Day 1: Explore Midhurst town center, with its quaint shops, pubs, and restaurants. Visit the Midhurst Museum for a dose of local history.
  • Day 2: Hike or bike the South Downs Way, with stunning views of the national park and surrounding countryside.
  • Day 3: Visit Cowdray Park Polo Club and catch a match or take a lesson.
  • Day 4: Take a scenic drive to nearby Chichester, with its stunning cathedral, Roman walls, and lively arts scene.
  • Day 5: Spend a relaxing day at Petworth House and Park, with its stunning mansion, lake, and deer park.
  • Day 6: Play a game of tennis at Midhurst Tennis Club or take a lesson.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely morning at Cowdray Farm Shop and Café, sampling local produce and enjoying the peaceful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Midhurst

Midhurst has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local restaurants, pubs, and cafes offering delicious food and drink. Here are some of our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sussex Pond Pudding: This traditional dessert features a suet pastry crust filled with a whole lemon, butter, and sugar, creating a sweet and tangy pudding.
  • Eggs Benedict: Enjoy this classic brunch dish at one of Midhurst’s cozy cafes, with poached eggs, ham, and hollandaise sauce on an English muffin.
  • Sussex Smokie: This local specialty features smoked haddock, cream, and mashed potatoes baked in the oven, creating a comforting and hearty meal.

Local Beverages

  • Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter: This traditional cask ale is brewed in nearby Lewes and is a favorite among locals.
  • Blackdown Gin: Produced on the edge of the South Downs, this award-winning gin features local botanicals and has a distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Midhurst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

No matter your budget or style, Midhurst has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Spread Eagle Hotel and Spa: This historic hotel dates back to the 15th century and features luxurious rooms, a spa, and award-winning restaurant.
  • Cowdray Estate Holiday Cottages: Stay in one of these charming converted farm buildings on the Cowdray Estate, with stunning countryside views and easy access to local attractions.
  • The Angel Inn: This cozy inn features comfortable rooms, a casual pub, and a popular restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in Midhurst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Midhurst with you by picking up some local souvenirs and products. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Cowdray Estate Wines: Produced on the Cowdray Estate, these award-winning wines feature local grapes and offer a taste of the Sussex countryside.
  • Midhurst Pottery: Pick up a unique and handmade piece of pottery at this local studio, with everything from mugs and bowls to vases and sculptures.
  • Cowdray Estate Farm Shop: Buy local produce, including meat, cheese, and fresh vegetables, at this farm shop located on the Cowdray Estate.

Local Culture and Customs of Midhurst

Midhurst has a rich and varied history, with a strong sense of community and cultural pride. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • The Midhurst Society: Founded in 1965, this local society aims to promote and preserve the history and heritage of Midhurst and its surrounding area.
  • Madhurst: This annual festival celebrates all things Midhurst, with live music, art exhibitions, food and drink stalls, and more.
  • Bonfire Night: This traditional celebration takes place on November 5th and includes a bonfire, fireworks, and a torch-lit parade through the town.

Best Time to Visit Midhurst

Midhurst is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm. The summer months (June to August) are the busiest and warmest, with plenty of outdoor activities and events taking place. The shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer milder weather, fewer crowds, and stunning fall foliage. Winter (December to February) can be chilly but is also a cozy time to explore Midhurst’s historic pubs and restaurants.

Do’s and Don’ts of Midhurst

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially Sussex Pond Pudding and Sussex Smokie.
  • Do take advantage of Midhurst’s outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and polo.
  • Do explore the town’s historic and cultural attractions, including Cowdray Ruins and Midhurst Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the stunning natural surroundings of Midhurst, including the South Downs National Park and Petworth House and Park.
  • Don’t expect a roaring nightlife scene, as Midhurst is a smaller town with a more laid-back vibe.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes, as much of Midhurst’s charm lies in its historic streets and scenic countryside.

Safety and Health

Safety in Midhurst

Midhurst is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. As with any travel destination, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car doors and keeping an eye on your belongings in public places.

Health in Midhurst

Midhurst has a range of medical facilities, including doctor’s offices, clinics, and a hospital. For emergencies, dial 999 or go to the nearest Accident and Emergency (A&E) department.

Getting to Midhurst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Midhurst is located in West Sussex, about 70 miles south of London. Here are some of the best ways to get to Midhurst:

Airports Near Midhurst

  • Gatwick Airport: This international airport is located 35 miles north of Midhurst and offers flights to destinations throughout Europe and beyond.
  • Heathrow Airport: This major international airport is located 58 miles north of Midhurst and offers flights to destinations throughout the world.

Bus Stations Near Midhurst

  • Midhurst Bus Station: This local bus station offers regular service to nearby towns and cities, including Guildford and Chichester.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore Midhurst and the surrounding countryside, with several car rental agencies located at nearby airports and train stations.
  • Train: The nearest train station is in Haslemere, about 8 miles east of Midhurst, with regular service to London and other destinations throughout the region.

How to Get Around in Midhurst

Midhurst is a small town, with most attractions and amenities within easy walking distance of each other. However, for those who prefer to drive or cycle, there are several car and bike rental options available in the town. The local bus station also offers affordable service to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Midhurst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Midhurst is conveniently located near many other popular destinations and attractions in West Sussex and beyond. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Chichester: Visit this nearby cathedral city, with its stunning cathedral, Roman walls, and lively arts scene.
  • Goodwood Estate: Explore this historic estate, with its motor racing circuit, horse racing events, and beautiful parkland.
  • Arundel Castle: Take a day trip to this stunning castle and gardens, located on the banks of the River Arun.

Conclusion

From its rich history to its stunning natural surroundings, Midhurst is a charming and vibrant town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in culture, outdoor adventures, or just a relaxing getaway, Midhurst is the perfect place to explore.
