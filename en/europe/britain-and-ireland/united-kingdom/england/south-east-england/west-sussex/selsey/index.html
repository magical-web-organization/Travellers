Introduction to Selsey

Selsey is a charming seaside town located in West Sussex, South East England. It sits on a narrow peninsular, surrounded by beautiful coastline and countryside. Selsey is known for its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re interested in exploring the cultural landmarks or outdoor activities, this town has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, where to eat, and stay, the local culture, do’s and don’ts, safety, and health, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Selsey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Selsey Lifeboat Station

Selsey has a long and proud maritime heritage, and Selsey Lifeboat Station is its flagship museum. It traces the history of the Royal National Lifeboat Institution (RNLI) and showcases the exceptional bravery of its volunteer lifeboat crew members. The museum is family-friendly, and you can learn about the town’s unique coastguard history through interactive exhibits.

Selsey Bill Nature Reserve

Selsey Bill Nature Reserve is an SSSI (Site of Special Scientific Interest) and is home to rare plant species, animals, and migrating birds. There are plenty of walking trails around the reserve, and you’ll have a chance to explore tidal lagoons and unique rock formations. Don’t forget to visit the lighthouse that dates back to the 17th century. The reserve is an ideal spot for bird-watching, especially during migration seasons.

St. Peter’s Church

St. Peter’s Church is one of the oldest buildings in Selsey, dating back to the 12th century. It has a fascinating history of destruction and reconstruction, including being destroyed by a French invasion in the 14th century. You can admire the church’s Norman architecture, including a 12th-century font and an impressive 15th-century tower.

Natural Attractions of Selsey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Beach

East Beach is a long pebble bay that offers scenic views of the sea and the nearby Isle of Wight. It’s an ideal spot for picnics, sunbathing, swimming, and beachcombing. There’s also a newly refurbished promenade that stretches the length of the beach and is perfect for a leisurely walk.

Pagham Harbour Nature Reserve

Pagham Harbour is a stunning natural reserve that offers incredible scenery of tidal mud flats, salt marshes, and a range of bird species. You can explore the reserve through various walking trails and bird hides. The visitor center also offers a range of family-friendly activities throughout the year.

Medmerry Nature Reserve

Medmerry Nature Reserve is one of the UK’s most significant coastal ‘realignment’ projects. It’s a vast area of salt marsh, mudflats, and lagoons that provides essential habitats for various plant and animal species. Medmerry offers an extensive network of paths, and boardwalks to explore the reserve and is an ideal spot for birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Selsey

Selsey and its surrounding countryside are perfect for adventurous souls who want to explore on foot or by bike. Here are some of the most popular routes.

Selsey Circular Walk

Selsey Circular Walk is a 10.5-mile route that takes you along the coast and countryside. The route starts at East Beach in Selsey, and it takes you through Pagham Harbour Nature Reserve, where you can enjoy tranquil stretches of tidal marsh. The walk is quite long, but it’s perfect for those who love to stretch their legs and enjoy the scenery.

Chichester Harbour Trail

Chichester Harbour Trail is a 13.5-mile route that takes you along coastal paths and through picturesque villages. The route starts at Fishbourne and takes you through Bosham and Emsworth before finishing in Langstone. The trail offers stunning views of the harbor, and there are plenty of opportunities to stop for refreshments along the way.

Best Things to Do in Selsey: Activities and Tours

Selsey Fishing Trips

Selsey has a long history of fishing, and today you can still go on fishing trips to catch various species of fish and crabs. Selsey Fishing Trips offers fishing excursions, which depart from Selsey’s East Beach. The trips last around four hours, and all the equipment and guidance are provided.

Selsey 4x4 Driving Experience

If you’re looking for a thrilling way to explore the countryside around Selsey, then a 4x4 driving experience is just the ticket. Selsey 4x4 Driving Experience offers various off-road experiences through local farms and trails. The experiences range from one hour to a full day, and you’ll have a chance to test your driving skills on challenging terrain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Selsey

If you’re planning to spend a week in Selsey, here’s a suggested itinerary to ensure you make the most of your stay.

Food and Drink Scene of Selsey

Must-Try Dishes

Selsey is famous for its seafood, and here are some of the must-try dishes:

Selsey Crab

Selsey crab is renowned for its flavor and is widely regarded as one of the best crabs in the country. You can try it in various restaurants across town, but the best way to enjoy it is as fresh as possible.

Selsey Cockles

Selsey cockles are another local delicacy that you shouldn’t miss. They’re available all year round, but the best time to enjoy them is during the summer months.

Local Beverages

Sussex Ale

Sussex ale is a locally brewed beer that’s available in most pubs across town. It’s a refreshing beer that pairs well with seafood dishes.

Sussex Sparkling Wine

Sussex produces some of the best sparkling wines in the country. You can try various brands at local restaurants, or you can visit a local vineyard to taste them all.

Where to Stay in Selsey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Premier Inn Selsey

Premier Inn Selsey is located just a few minutes walk from the town center and the beach. The hotel offers comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.

The Seal Hotel

The Seal Hotel is a family-owned hotel that offers rooms with sea views and access to their on-site restaurant. The hotel is just a few steps away from the beach and is ideal for those who want to enjoy the town’s coastal location.

What to Buy in Selsey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Selsey Crab Pottery

Selsey Crab Pottery is a unique souvenir that you can take home with you. The pottery is designed to look like a crab and is available in various shapes and sizes.

Selsey Cockles

Selsey cockles are also available to buy fresh to take home with you or to be sent to your address.

Local Culture and Customs of Selsey

Selsey is a town that’s proud of its maritime heritage and is known for its friendly hospitality. The town has a relaxed atmosphere and is the perfect spot for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Selsey Music Festival is also a highly anticipated annual event that takes place during the summer months.

Best Time to Visit Selsey

The best time to visit Selsey is during the spring and summer months when the weather is warm, and the town is in full swing. However, keep in mind that it can get busy during the peak season, and accommodation prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Selsey

Do:

  • Try the local seafood
  • Explore the natural reserves
  • Take a fishing trip

Don’t:

  • Leave litter on the beach
  • Ignore the tide times
  • Swim in the sea outside of designated areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Selsey

Selsey is generally a safe and friendly town. However, like any other town, it’s essential to be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Selsey

The town has a few pharmacies and medical centers. Still, for serious medical issues, it’s best to visit the hospital in Chichester, which is approximately a 30-minute drive from Selsey.

Getting to Selsey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Selsey

The nearest airport to Selsey is Gatwick Airport, which is approximately 60 miles away. From there, you can hire a car or take a train to Chichester and then take a bus or taxi to Selsey.

Bus Stations Near Selsey

Selsey is served by Stagecoach Buses, which offers regular services to various destinations in the area. Bus 51 or 51A connects Selsey to Chichester city center.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can hire a car and drive to Selsey. The town is located just off the A27 and is easily accessible by road.

How to Get Around in Selsey

Selsey town center and most of the attractions can be reached on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available, and the town has a bus service that connects it to the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Selsey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chichester

Chichester is a beautiful cathedral city that’s just a short drive from Selsey. The city offers plenty of attractions, including the Chichester Cathedral and the Pallant House Gallery.

Bognor Regis

Bognor Regis is a popular seaside town that’s famous for its amusement park and beach. It’s just a short drive from Selsey and is perfect for a day trip.

Conclusion

Selsey is a hidden gem on the South Coast of England. With its natural reserves, maritime history, and friendly locals, the town is an ideal spot for a relaxing break. This guide has provided an overview of the best places to visit, food and drink scene, activities, and much more. Whether you’re traveling solo, as a couple or with family, Selsey has something to offer everyone. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your trip today!
