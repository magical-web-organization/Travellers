Introduction to Bosham

Located on the west coast of West Sussex, Bosham is a picturesque village on the edge of Chichester Harbour, one of the largest natural harbours in England. With a history dating back to the Roman era, Bosham has a rich cultural heritage that attracts visitors from all over the world. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views, outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, and delicious cuisine in this quaint village.

List of Places to Visit in Bosham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Church of the Holy Trinity
  2. The Old Mill
  3. Bosham Quay

The Church of the Holy Trinity

The Church of the Holy Trinity is a historic church that dates back to the 11th century. The church has a unique location on the edge of the harbour, which provides stunning views of the water. The church is also known for its historic architecture, including a Saxon tower and a Norman doorway. Visitors can attend services or take a guided tour to learn about the history of the church.

The Old Mill

The Old Mill is a 17th-century windmill that has been converted into a museum. The museum showcases the history of milling in the area and the role of mills in the village’s economy. Visitors can learn about the milling process and see the original machinery that was used to grind flour and corn.

Bosham Quay

Bosham Quay is a beautiful spot on the harbour that is popular with visitors and locals alike. The quay is lined with sailboats and fishing boats, and visitors can watch the tide come in and out. There are also several shops and restaurants nearby where visitors can eat and shop.

Natural Attractions of Bosham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Chichester Harbour
  2. Bosham Walk
  3. South Downs National Park

Chichester Harbour

Chichester Harbour is the largest natural harbour in England and is an important nature reserve. Visitors can take part in a range of water-based activities, including sailing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. There are also several walking trails around the harbour that provide stunning views of the landscape.

Bosham Walk

Bosham Walk is a circular walking route that takes visitors through the village and along the harbour. The walk is approximately 2 miles long and takes visitors past historic landmarks, picturesque viewpoints, and quaint cottages. This is the perfect way to explore the village and enjoy the scenic views of the harbour.

South Downs National Park

The South Downs National Park is located a short distance from Bosham and provides visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can take part in activities such as hiking, cycling, and horse riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bosham

  1. Bosham Walk
  2. Chichester Harbour Walk

Bosham Walk

This circular walking route takes visitors through the village and along the harbour. The walk provides stunning views of the harbour and passes many historic landmarks.

Chichester Harbour Walk

This walking route takes visitors around Chichester Harbour and is approximately 8 miles long. The walk takes visitors past several nature reserves, sandy beaches, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Bosham: Activities and Tours

  1. Sailing
  2. Paddleboarding
  3. Kayaking
  4. Walking tours

Sailing

Visitors can take part in sailing lessons or rent a sailboat to explore the harbour. The harbour is perfect for sailing, with sheltered waters and beautiful scenery.

Paddleboarding

Paddleboarding is a popular activity in Bosham, with several companies offering lessons and rentals. Visitors can explore the harbour on a paddleboard and enjoy the stunning views of the landscape.

Kayaking

Kayaking is another popular activity in Bosham, with several companies offering rentals and tours. Visitors can explore the harbour and surrounding waterways at their own pace.

Walking tours

Several companies offer walking tours of the village, which provide visitors with an insight into the history and culture of Bosham. Walking tours may include visits to historic landmarks, museums, and local businesses.

Food and Drink Scene of Bosham

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bosham Rye Bread
  2. Chichester Harbour Crab
  3. Sussex Lamb

Bosham Rye Bread

Bosham Rye Bread is a local delicacy that is made with flour from the Old Mill. The flour is stoneground, which gives the bread a distinctive texture and flavor.

Chichester Harbour Crab

Chichester Harbour Crab is a local specialty that is served in many of the village’s restaurants. The crab is fresh and is prepared in a variety of ways, including in sandwiches, salads, and pasta dishes.

Sussex Lamb

Sussex Lamb is a popular dish in the region and is often served with roasted potatoes and seasonal vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Sussex Wine
  2. Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter

Sussex Wine

Sussex Wine is gaining popularity in the region and is produced in several local vineyards. The wine is made using traditional methods and has won several awards.

Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter

Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter is a local beer that is brewed in Lewes, a town nearby. This award-winning beer is made with locally sourced hops and water from the South Downs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bosham

Bosham has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can learn about the local customs and traditions by attending festivals and events throughout the year. One of the most popular events is the Bosham Festival, which takes place in the summer and includes concerts, art exhibitions, and workshops.

Best Time to Visit Bosham

The best time to visit Bosham is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is warm, and there are numerous events and festivals taking place throughout the season. However, the village is beautiful year-round and has its own charm during the cooler months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bosham

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take part in local events and festivals
  • Explore the village on foot or by bike
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect historic landmarks and buildings
  • Ignore safety warnings when engaging in water-based activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Bosham

Bosham is generally a very safe place to visit. Visitors should be aware of the tides when engaging in water-based activities, as they can be strong at times. Visitors should also be mindful of parking restrictions in the village, as parking can be limited during peak season.

Health in Bosham

The village has several medical facilities, including a doctors’ surgery and a pharmacy. Visitors should also be aware of any allergies or medical conditions before engaging in outdoor activities.
