Introduction to Arundel

Located in the heart of West Sussex, Arundel is a beautiful market town known for its rich historical heritage, outstanding natural beauty, and vibrant cultural scene. Home to one of the most stunning castles in Britain, Arundel offers travelers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Surrounded by rolling hills, picturesque countryside, and the River Arun, the town is a perfect destination for those seeking relaxation, exploration, and inspiration.

List of Places to Visit in Arundel

There are plenty of things to do and see in Arundel. Here are some of the top tourist attractions and landmarks in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arundel Castle: One of the most iconic landmarks in West Sussex, Arundel Castle is a medieval castle and stately home that boasts a rich history and stunning architecture. Built in the 11th century, the castle features magnificent interiors, impressive collections of art and antiques, and beautifully landscaped gardens.

  • Arundel Museum: Located in the town center, Arundel Museum is a small but fascinating museum that tells the story of Arundel’s history through a series of interactive exhibits and displays. From Roman times to the present day, the museum offers a glimpse into the town’s past, including its Anglo-Saxon origins, medieval history, and Victorian era.

  • St Nicholas’ Church: A beautiful Grade I listed building located on top of the town’s hill, St Nicholas’ Church is a historic place of worship that dates back to the 14th century. The church is renowned for its stunning architecture, including its intricate stained-glass windows, medieval fonts, and original carvings.

Natural Attractions of Arundel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arundel Wetland Centre: An award-winning nature reserve that is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including rare birds, frogs, and dragons. The Wetland Centre offers a range of activities, including guided walks, birdwatching, and nature trails.

  • Arundel Park: A vast 1,000-acre deer park situated next to Arundel Castle, Arundel Park is a beautiful green space that offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside. The park is home to a herd of wild fallow deer, as well as numerous walking trails and cycle routes.

  • River Arun Walk: A relaxing stroll along the banks of the River Arun is a must-do activity in Arundel. The river flows through the heart of the town and provides a tranquil setting for nature lovers and walkers. There are several other walking trails around Arundel, including the Monarch’s Way and the South Downs Way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arundel

West Sussex is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, and Arundel offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • South Downs Way: One of the most popular long-distance trails in England, the South Downs Way runs for 100 miles from Winchester to Eastbourne, passing through the stunning countryside of West Sussex. The trail offers breathtaking views of the South Downs National Park, as well as a range of wildlife, flora, and fauna.

  • Arundel Circular Walk: A picturesque 5-mile trail that circles around the town of Arundel, offering beautiful views of the River Arun, Arundel Castle, and the surrounding hills. The walk takes about 2 hours to complete and is suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

  • Monarch’s Way: A 615-mile trail that follows the escape route of King Charles II after his defeat at the Battle of Worcester in 1651. The section of the trail that passes through West Sussex offers stunning views of the countryside and a range of historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Arundel: Activities and Tours

Aside from sightseeing and outdoor activities, Arundel offers plenty of other things to do and see. Here are some of the top activities and tours in the town:

  • Arundel Brewery Tour: Take a guided tour of the award-winning Arundel Brewery and learn about the history of brewing in West Sussex. The tour includes a tasting of the brewery’s best beers.

  • Arundel Ghost Tour: A spooky tour of the town’s haunted places, including Arundel Castle, St Nicholas’ Church, and Priory Park.

  • Arundel Farmers’ Market: Held on the third Saturday of every month, the market features a range of local fresh produce, including fruits, vegetables, meats, and cheeses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arundel

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Arundel, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Explore Arundel Castle and its beautiful gardens.
  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of Arundel Museum and discover the history of the town.
  • Day 3: Cycle the South Downs Way and take in the stunning scenery.
  • Day 4: Visit the Arundel Wetland Centre and spot rare birds.
  • Day 5: Walk along the River Arun and explore the town’s historic buildings.
  • Day 6: Go on a brewery tour and sample some of the best beers in West Sussex.
  • Day 7: Spend a day at the beach in nearby Brighton or Littlehampton.

Food and Drink Scene of Arundel

Arundel has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants, cafes, and bars serving a range of excellent cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish & Chips: A classic British dish that can be found in almost every restaurant in Arundel. The fish is typically deep-fried and served with chips (French fries) and tartar sauce.

  • Sussex Smokie: A traditional dish made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions. The dish is usually served with crusty bread and is a great option for a hearty lunch or dinner.

  • Pork Pies: A delicious savory pastry that is popular in West Sussex. The pies are filled with seasoned pork and are perfect for a quick snack or lunch.

Local Beverages

  • Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter: A popular beer brewed in nearby Lewes, Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter is an English pale ale that is known for its rich malty flavor and hoppy aroma.

  • Blackdown Sussex Dry Gin: A premium gin distilled in West Sussex, Blackdown Sussex Dry Gin is made with 11 botanicals, including juniper, coriander, and local silver birch sap.

  • Bolney Estate Wine: A range of award-winning wines produced in nearby Bolney. The estate offers wine tours and tastings, as well as a vineyard shop.

Where to Stay in Arundel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arundel offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Norfolk Arms Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Arundel, the Norfolk Arms Hotel offers comfortable rooms, a bar, and a restaurant with a courtyard garden.

  • The White Swan: A traditional coaching inn that dates back to the 18th century, The White Swan offers cozy guestrooms, a pub, and a restaurant serving classic British dishes.

  • Arundel Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly option located in a historic building near Arundel Castle. The hostel offers dormitory and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a cozy lounge.

What to Buy in Arundel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arundel is known for its unique gift shops, artisan markets, and independent boutiques. Here are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs and local products:

  • Arundel Farmers’ Market: Held on the third Saturday of every month, the market offers a range of local fresh produce, including fruits, vegetables, meats, and cheeses.

  • Arundel Antiques Market: A treasure trove of antiques and collectibles, the market features over 50 stalls selling everything from vintage furniture to rare books and records.

  • The Town Crier: A souvenir shop that offers a range of Arundel-themed gifts, including postcards, magnets, and locally-made crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Arundel

Arundel is a friendly and welcoming town that prides itself on its rich heritage and vibrant cultural scene. The locals are known for their love of traditional English pubs, live music, and outdoor activities. If you’re visiting Arundel, make sure to respect the local customs and try to immerse yourself in the town’s culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Arundel

Arundel can be enjoyed at any time of the year, but the best time to visit is in the summer months (June - August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town comes alive with outdoor events and festivals. If you prefer a quieter and more peaceful time, then autumn (September - November) and spring (March - May) are also great options, with lower accommodation rates and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arundel

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take part in outdoor activities and explore the stunning countryside.
  • Visit the local markets and boutique shops.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places.
  • Ignore the town’s historical heritage and landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arundel

Arundel is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables hidden and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Arundel

Arundel has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of Lyme disease when walking or hiking in the countryside and take precautions, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellents.

Getting to Arundel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arundel is easily accessible by public transport and is located just 90 minutes away from London. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Arundel

  • Gatwick Airport: Located just 25 miles from Arundel, Gatwick Airport offers a range of international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Arundel

  • Arundel Bus Station: The bus station is located in the town center and offers regular services to nearby towns and cities, including Brighton, Chichester, and Worthing.

Other Options

  • Train: Arundel has a train station that is served by the Southern Railway, with regular services connecting the town to London, Brighton, and other cities.

  • Car: Arundel is located just off the A27 road, which connects the town to the wider road network of West Sussex and beyond.

How to Get Around in Arundel

Arundel is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel further afield, there are several transportation options available, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Arundel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arundel is a great base for exploring the wider Sussex region, which offers a wealth of attractions and destinations. Here are some of the top nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Chichester: A charming cathedral city famous for its ancient history, art galleries, and cultural events.

  • Brighton: A vibrant seaside city known for its lively nightlife, colorful architecture, and iconic pier.

  • Goodwood: A country estate that hosts a range of events throughout the year, including horse racing, motorsports, and music festivals.

Conclusion

Arundel is a hidden gem in the heart of West Sussex, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s historic landmarks, the beautiful countryside, or simply soaking up the local atmosphere, Arundel has something for everyone. So why not take a trip to Arundel and discover the best of West Sussex today?
