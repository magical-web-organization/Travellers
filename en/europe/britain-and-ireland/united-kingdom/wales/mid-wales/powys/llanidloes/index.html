Introduction to Llanidloes

Llanidloes is a charming market town nestled in the heart of Powys, Mid Wales. Surrounded by rolling hills and scenic countryside, the town boasts a rich history dating back to the 13th century. Llanidloes is an ideal destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in the beauty of rural Wales.

List of Places to Visit in Llanidloes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Llanidloes Museum: This museum houses a diverse collection of artefacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the town’s rich heritage.
  • St. Idloes Church: One of the oldest buildings in Llanidloes, St. Idloes Church dates back to the 13th century and features stunning stained glass windows and intricate wood carvings.
  • Llyn Clywedog Reservoir: This stunning man-made lake is a popular spot for fishing and water sports, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Llanidloes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hafren Forest: With its network of trails and stunning waterfalls, Hafren Forest is a must-visit destination for hikers and nature lovers.
  • The Glyndwr’s Way: This 135-mile long-distance walking trail runs through the heart of Mid Wales and passes through Llanidloes. It’s a great way to explore the area’s stunning scenery.
  • Plynlimon: Standing at 752m, Plynlimon is the highest point in Mid Wales. The mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Llanidloes

There are many hiking and biking trails in and around Llanidloes, all showcasing the stunning natural beauty of the area. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Hafren Forest Trail: This 5-mile circular route takes visitors through the heart of Hafren Forest, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Llyn Clywedog Trail: This 10-mile circular route takes visitors around the stunning Llyn Clywedog Reservoir and offers plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting.
  • The Glyndwr’s Way Trail: This 135-mile long-distance walking trail takes visitors through the heart of Mid Wales and passes through Llanidloes.

Best Things to Do in Llanidloes: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Llanidloes Market: Held every Saturday, the Llanidloes Market is the perfect place to pick up unique souvenirs and locally-made crafts.
  • Go Fishing: Llyn Clywedog Reservoir is one of the best fishing spots in Wales, offering plenty of opportunities to catch trout, salmon, and other freshwater fish.
  • Explore the Hafren Forest: With its network of trails and stunning waterfalls, Hafren Forest is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Llanidloes

Day 1: Arrival and Llanidloes Market

  • Arrive in Llanidloes and settle into your accommodation.
  • Explore the town and visit the Llanidloes Market, held every Saturday.

Day 2: Hiking in the Hafren Forest

  • Spend the day hiking in the stunning Hafren Forest, taking in the cascading waterfalls and wildlife along the way.

Day 3: Visit St. Idloes Church and Llanidloes Museum

  • Explore the fascinating history of Llanidloes by visiting St. Idloes Church and the Llanidloes Museum.

Day 4: Scenic Drive to Plynlimon

  • Take a scenic drive to Plynlimon, the highest peak in Mid Wales, and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Day 5: Fishing in Llyn Clywedog

  • Spend the day fishing in the picturesque Llyn Clywedog Reservoir.

Day 6: Explore the Glyndwr’s Way

  • Hit the trails and explore the historic Glyndwr’s Way, which passes through Llanidloes on its way through Mid Wales.

Day 7: Departure

  • Depart Llanidloes and make your way to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Llanidloes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A traditional Welsh dish made of cheese and toasted bread.
  • Laverbread: A Welsh delicacy made from seaweed.
  • Bara Brith: A fruity tea loaf often served with butter.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Ale: Brewed in the heart of Mid Wales, Welsh Ale is a must-try for any beer lover.
  • Penderyn Whisky: Produced in the nearby town of Penderyn, this award-winning Welsh whisky is famed for its smooth taste.

Where to Stay in Llanidloes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Crown Inn: A cosy, traditional pub with rooms.
  • The Unicorn: A historic coaching inn with rooms overlooking the town square.
  • The Old Mill: A charming bed and breakfast located on the outskirts of town.

What to Buy in Llanidloes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh Wool: Llanidloes is a hub of traditional Welsh wool production, and the town is home to several wool shops.
  • Local Crafts: The Llanidloes Market is a great place to find locally-made crafts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Llanidloes

  • The Welsh language is still spoken in some parts of Llanidloes.
  • Llanidloes is renowned for its strong sense of community and hosts many annual events and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Llanidloes

The best time to visit Llanidloes is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Llanidloes

Do:

  • Learn a few words of Welsh before you go.
  • Treat the countryside with respect and follow the Countryside Code.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture.
  • Leave litter behind in the countryside.

Safety and Health

Safety in Llanidloes

Llanidloes is generally a very safe place for visitors, with a low crime rate and friendly, welcoming locals.

Health in Llanidloes

There are several medical facilities in Llanidloes, including a GP surgery and a pharmacy.

Getting to Llanidloes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Llanidloes

  • Birmingham Airport (BHX): 89 miles from Llanidloes.
  • Manchester Airport (MAN): 103 miles from Llanidloes.

Bus Stations Near Llanidloes

  • Llanidloes Bus Station: Located on Great Oak Street, in the centre of town.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: A car can be hired from several companies at Birmingham or Manchester airport.

How to Get Around in Llanidloes

Llanidloes is a small town, and most places are easily accessible on foot. However, there are taxi services and bus services available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Llanidloes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aberystwyth: A charming seaside town located just 30 miles west of Llanidloes.
  • Brecon Beacons National Park: Located just 50 miles south of Llanidloes, this stunning national park is a must-visit for hikers and nature lovers.

Conclusion

Llanidloes offers visitors the perfect combination of stunning natural scenery, rich history, and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking to hike in the mountains, fish in the lakes, or immerse yourself in Welsh culture, Llanidloes has something for everyone. Visit this charming market town and discover all that this beautiful part of Wales has to offer!
