Introduction to Builth Wells

Nestled in the heart of Wales, Builth Wells is a picturesque town that is steeped in history and natural beauty. Often referred to as the “Welsh Lake District,” Builth Wells is surrounded by rolling hills and lush green valleys, making it a popular destination among outdoor enthusiasts. The town itself is small but charming, with plenty of quaint cafes, restaurants, and pubs to explore. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, there’s something for everyone in Builth Wells.

List of Places to Visit in Builth Wells

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wyeside Arts Centre: Located in the heart of Builth Wells, the Wyeside Arts Centre is a cultural hub that offers a variety of events, exhibitions, and screenings throughout the year.
  • The National Cycle Collection: If you’re a fan of cycling, be sure to check out the National Cycle Collection in Builth Wells. This museum showcases a wide variety of vintage and modern bicycles, including rare and unique models.
  • Builth Castle: Built in the early 12th century, Builth Castle is a historic landmark that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Although much of the castle is now in ruins, it’s still worth a visit for its historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Builth Wells: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Elan Valley: Just a short drive from Builth Wells, the Elan Valley is a nature reserve that offers breathtaking views of the landscape. There are plenty of walking and cycling trails to explore, and you can even stop off at one of the many small towns and villages along the way.
  • Gigrin Farm Red Kite Feeding Centre: If you’re a bird lover, then you won’t want to miss the Gigrin Farm Red Kite Feeding Centre. Here, you can watch these magnificent birds of prey as they swoop down to feed on the provided meat.
  • Brecon Beacons National Park: Just a short drive from Builth Wells lies Brecon Beacons National Park, a vast expanse of wild and rugged countryside that is perfect for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re into hiking, mountain biking, or rock climbing, there’s plenty to do in this stunning wilderness area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Builth Wells

  • The Builth Wells Circular Walk: This 4.5-mile circular walk takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding Builth Wells, offering stunning views of the area’s natural beauty.
  • The Wye Valley Walk: This long-distance trail stretches for 136 miles, running from the Welsh mountains to the sea. The section of the trail that runs through Builth Wells is particularly scenic and offers plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting.
  • The Epynt Way: This is a circular route of 55 miles, which takes you along the remote mountain ranges and valleys of the Epynt, which lie between Builth Wells and Brecon.

Best Things to Do in Builth Wells: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing on the River Wye: The River Wye is just a short distance from Builth Wells, and it’s the perfect place to try your hand at canoeing. Several local companies offer guided tours and equipment rental.
  • Golfing at Builth Wells Golf Club: Located on the outskirts of town, Builth Wells Golf Club is a picturesque 18-hole course that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Visiting the Royal Welsh Showground: Throughout the year, the Royal Welsh Showground hosts a wide variety of events, from agricultural shows to music festivals. Check their calendar ahead of time so you don’t miss out.

Week-Long Itinerary for Builth Wells

Day 1: Arrive in Builth Wells, check into your accommodation and enjoy a leisurely stroll around the town

Day 2: Explore the historical Builth Castle and Wyeside Arts Centre. In the afternoon, take a walk to the River Wye and go for a paddle.

Day 3: Take on the Elan Valley’s walking trails for a full day adventure.

Day 4: Visit the Gigrin Farm Red Kite Feeding Centre and learn about these magnificent birds. In the afternoon, take a drive through the Brecon Beacons National Park and take in the stunning views.

Day 5: Step back into history at the National Cycle Museum and cycle through the famous lanes of Wales.

Day 6: Spend the day at the Royal Welsh Showground if an event is occurring at the time of your stay. If no event is ongoing, head to the heart of the town and enjoy the local shops and cafes.

Day 7: Relax and unwind. Spend the day at one of the area’s many spas or take a scenic drive through the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Builth Wells

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A classic Welsh dish made with melted cheese, beer, and a variety of spices and herbs, served on a slice of toasted bread.
  • Lamb Cawl: A hearty Welsh soup made with lamb, potatoes, carrots, and leeks. Perfect for a cold winter’s day.
  • Bara Brith: A traditional Welsh fruitcake made with dried fruit, spices, and tea.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Builth Wells is home to several microbreweries, producing delicious real ale that is popular among locals and visitors alike.
  • Welsh Whisky: From the Penderyn Distillery, this award-winning whisky is made using traditional Welsh ingredients and methods.

Where to Stay in Builth Wells: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Caer Beris Manor Country House Hotel: Located just outside of Builth Wells, this charming hotel is set in beautiful wooded grounds and features an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • The Greyhound Hotel: This family-run hotel is located in the heart of Builth Wells and features comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • The Gro Hotel: This cozy hotel is located just a short walk from the town center and features stylish rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Builth Wells: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh wool blankets: Made from locally sourced wool and woven using traditional methods, these cozy blankets are the perfect souvenir to take home.
  • Handmade pottery: Builth Wells is home to several talented potters, and you’ll find a variety of beautiful handmade pottery available for purchase.
  • Local honey: Wales is known for its delicious honey, made from a variety of local wildflowers. Be sure to pick up a jar before you leave.

Local Culture and Customs of Builth Wells

Builth Wells has a rich cultural heritage, and local customs and traditions are still an important part of everyday life. The Welsh language, for example, is widely spoken in Builth Wells, and visitors are likely to hear it spoken as they wander around town. Another important tradition is Eisteddfodau, which are annual cultural festivals that celebrate Welsh music, language, and literature.

Best Time to Visit Builth Wells

The best time to visit Builth Wells depends on the activities you have planned. If you’re interested in outdoor activities, such as hiking and cycling, then the spring and summer months are the best time to visit. However, if you’re more interested in cultural events, then the fall and winter months are a great time to visit, as this is when many of the local festivals and events take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Builth Wells

Do:

  • Try to learn a few Welsh words and phrases. Locals will appreciate the effort, and it shows respect for the local culture.
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities, especially if you’re planning on going hiking or cycling in the mountains.
  • Be sure to sample some of the local Welsh cuisine, including traditional dishes like Welsh rarebit and lamb cawl.

Don’t:

  • Don’t assume that everyone in Builth Wells speaks English fluently. While English is widely spoken, it’s also important to respect the Welsh language and culture.
  • Don’t litter or harm the local flora and fauna.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing for the Welsh weather, which can be unpredictable at times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Builth Wells

Builth Wells is a relatively safe and friendly town, and crime rates are low. However, it’s still important to take basic safety precautions like locking your accommodation and keeping an eye on your valuables while out and about.

Health in Builth Wells

As with any travel destination, it’s important to take steps to protect your health while visiting Builth Wells. Be sure to bring any medications you may need, and stay up to date on routine vaccinations. It’s also a good idea to bring along a small first aid kit, especially if you plan on doing any outdoor activities.

Getting to Builth Wells: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Builth Wells

The closest airports to Builth Wells are Cardiff Airport and Birmingham Airport, both of which are around two hours’ drive away. From the airport, you can rent a car or take public transportation to Builth Wells.

Bus Stations Near Builth Wells

The main bus station in Builth Wells is located on Castle Street, and there are regular bus services to and from major cities like Cardiff and Swansea.

Other Options

If you’re travelling from within the UK, you may want to consider taking the train to Builth Road station, which is just a few miles from Builth Wells. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to the town center.

How to Get Around in Builth Wells

Builth Wells is a small town, and it’s easy to navigate on foot. However, if you’re planning on exploring the surrounding countryside, you may want to consider renting a car or bicycle. There are also several local taxi companies that can take you where you need to go.

Where to Go Next from Builth Wells: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Brecon Beacons: This stunning national park is just a short drive from Builth Wells, making it an ideal destination for a day trip.
  • Hay-on-Wye: Known as the “town of books,” Hay-on-Wye is a charming village that is home to dozens of independent bookstores and antique shops.
  • The Welsh Coast: If you’re in the mood for some seaside relaxation, head to the Welsh coast and explore towns like Aberystwyth and Cardigan.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Builth Wells has something to offer everyone. From the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside to the vibrant local culture, this charming town is sure to captivate visitors of all ages. So pack your bags and discover all that Builth Wells has to offer!
