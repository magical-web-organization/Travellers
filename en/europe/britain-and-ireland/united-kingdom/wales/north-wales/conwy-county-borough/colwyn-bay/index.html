Introduction to Colwyn Bay

Located in the county borough of Conwy in North Wales, Colwyn Bay is a charming coastal town known for its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and scenic hiking routes. This lively town has plenty of activities and attractions on offer for tourists of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Colwyn Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Colwyn Bay has a rich cultural heritage with several museums and historical places worth exploring, including:

  • Welsh Mountain Zoo: Learn about and interact with over 140 species of animals, birds, and even reptiles.
  • Betws-y-Coed: Known as the gateway to Snowdonia, this picturesque village is famous for its beautiful waterfalls, bridges, and pubs.
  • Mostyn Art Gallery: The gallery features a collection of contemporary art from various artists and has an excellent gift shop.
  • St. Trillo’s Chapel: A small medieval chapel situated on the coast, known for its unique architecture and rich history.

Natural Attractions of Colwyn Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From the stunning coastlines to the scenic countryside, Colwyn Bay has plenty of natural attractions that will leave you in awe. Some worth mentioning are:

  • Eryri (Snowdonia National Park): Snowdonia offers diverse activities and landscapes including beautiful peaks, waterfalls, and lakes.
  • Great Orme: A scenic hill, home to a herd of wild Kashmiri goats, offers natural walkways, trails, and provides a stunning panoramic view of Colwyn Bay.
  • Colwyn Bay Nature Reserve: A nature and wildlife oasis situated just north of Colwyn Bay where visitors can explore the coastline’s flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colwyn Bay

Colwyn Bay has some of the most stunning hiking and biking routes in the UK. Here are some popular ones:

  • Conwy Mountain: This trail is a paradise for hikers who love challenging walks whilst enjoying sensational views of the sea.
  • The North Wales Path: A 60-mile path that stretches through Northern Wales, offering stunning views of the coast and Snowdonia National Park.
  • Great Orme Circular Walk: The walk starts at Llandudno pier, and it offers visitors a chance to explore the little town and soak up the scenic views of the Welsh coast.

Best Things to Do in Colwyn Bay: Activities and Tours

Colwyn Bay has activities and tours to cater to every visitor’s needs. Here are some ideas for your next visit:

  • Water Sports: With the town being near the sea, visitors can partake in various water sports such as surfing, paddleboarding, and kayaking.
  • Zip World Fforest: A few miles north of Colwyn Bay, Zip World offers an exhilarating zip-line experience with spectacular views of the Colwyn Bay countryside.
  • Seaside Visit: Colwyn Bay has a stunning beach that is perfect for sunbathing, strolling and surfing. It is located within a short walking distance from the town centre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colwyn Bay

Day 1 - Explore Conwy Castle and town.

Day 2 - Go hiking in the Snowdonia National Park.

Day 3 - Visit the Welsh Mountain Zoo and Betws-y-Coed.

Day 4 - Discover the Mostyn Art Gallery and take a walk around Great Orme.

Day 5 - Head to Llandudno and enjoy the scenic views from the Cable Car.

Day 6 - Hike at Conwy Mountain and enjoy a seaside sunset at Colwyn Bay.

Day 7 - Visit St Trillo’s Chapel and explore the Colwyn Bay Nature Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Colwyn Bay

Must-Try Dishes

Colwyn Bay’s culinary scene is diverse, with fantastic restaurants and pubs that offer delicious local and international dishes. Here are some local must-try dishes:

  • Welsh Rarebit: A perfect blend of melted cheese, black pepper and Worcestershire sauce, and various spices, served over hot bread toast.
  • Roast Dinner: A traditional British Sunday roast with meat, vegetables, and gravy.
  • Bara Brith: A traditional Welsh tea bread made with raisins and mixed fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Penderyn Whisky: The only single malt Welsh whisky that’s handcrafted and distilled in the Brecon Beacons National Park.
  • Welsh Beers: There are a variety of local beers brewed in North Wales such as Welsh 2 badgers and Purple Moose, both made by microbreweries in Porthmadog.

Where to Stay in Colwyn Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Colwyn Bay has plenty of options for travellers looking for accommodation. Here are some of the options available:

  • The Ashmount Hotel: A charmingly decorated guesthouse situated on its hilltop overlooking Colwyn Bay.
  • The Toad Hall Hotel: A boutique hotel that offers comfortable stays in its elegantly decorated rooms.
  • Llandudno Youth Hostel: An affordable option for backpackers, this hostel is just a thirty-minute bus ride from Colwyn Bay.

What to Buy in Colwyn Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some ideas on what to buy on your next visit to Colwyn Bay:

  • Welsh Blankets and Rugs: The Welsh are known for their quality woolen textiles.
  • Cheese: North Wales has some of the best cheeses in the world; it is a must-try souvenir to bring back home.
  • Slate Products: Wales is known for its slate production, and you can find quality slate products in Colwyn Bay.

Local Culture and Customs of Colwyn Bay

Colwyn Bay has a rich history with Welsh traditions, and it is known for its warm welcome and hospitality towards visitors. Welsh customs include cultural events, such as the National Eisteddfod, which celebrates Welsh music, poetry and literature.

Best Time to Visit Colwyn Bay

The best time to visit Colwyn Bay is between June and August when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and watersports. Spring is also an excellent season to visit as it offers mild weather, perfect for exploring the countryside.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colwyn Bay

Do:

  • Respect the Welsh culture and tradition.
  • Learn a bit of Welsh.
  • Try Welsh cuisine and products.

Don’t:

  • Assume you know everything about Wales and its people.
  • Speak loudly in public places or disturb locals.
  • Litter.

Safety and Health

Colwyn Bay is generally a safe and healthy destination. Nonetheless, it is always wise to be vigilant and take precautions during your travels.

Safety in Colwyn Bay

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings while in public places.
  • Stay away from secluded places at night.
  • Wear the appropriate attire for the activities you participate in.

Health in Colwyn Bay

  • Always bring sunscreen and insect repellent, especially if you’re going hiking.
  • Tap water in Colwyn Bay is safe to drink, so carrying a refillable water bottle is encouraged.

Getting to Colwyn Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colwyn Bay

The nearest major airport to Colwyn Bay is Manchester Airport (MAN). It is about a 1-hour 30-minute drive from Colwyn Bay.

Bus Stations Near Colwyn Bay

National Express operates a bus service running from Manchester Airport and Liverpool John Lennon Airport to Colwyn Bay.

Other Options

Train: Colwyn Bay has its train station offering regular services via Virgin Trains to major cities such as Manchester, London, and Edinburgh.

How to Get Around in Colwyn Bay

Colwyn Bay is a small town that travellers can walk or bike around comfortably. For long-distance travels, it is best to opt for buses or trains that service the area.

Where to Go Next from Colwyn Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Llandudno: A beautiful Victorian seaside resort town situated a few miles west of Colwyn Bay.

  • Conwy: A historic walled town that’s home to the famous Conwy Castle and other historical landmarks.

  • Portmeirion: A beautiful Italian-style village boasting colourful buildings and scenic views.

Conclusion

Colwyn Bay is a great destination for those who are seeking a blend of adventure, history and nature in one trip. With its scenic views, spectacular beaches, and friendly locals, Colwyn Bay has something for everyone and is a destination you don’t want to miss.
