Introduction to Betws-y-Coed

Betws-y-Coed is a village located in the Conwy county borough of North Wales, United Kingdom. It lies in the Snowdonia National Park, at the confluence of the River Conwy and its tributary, the Llugwy. This picturesque village is a popular tourist destination, known for its stunning natural beauty and offering a wide range of outdoor activities. Here is a comprehensive guide to help you make the most out of your visit to Betws-y-Coed.

List of Places to Visit in Betws-y-Coed

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Michael’s Old Church: An ancient church built in the 14th century, which has now been restored, and is open for visits throughout the year.
  • Conwy Valley Railway Museum: This museum boasts a collection of model and toy trains, as well as an outdoor miniature railway ride.
  • Swallow Falls: A scenic waterfall that offers a nice walk round the rock pools.

Natural Attractions of Betws-y-Coed: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gwydir Forest Park: Located only three miles away from the village, it is the largest forest in Wales and offers a network of walking and cycling trails.
  • Mount Snowdon: Hike to the summit of the highest mountain in Wales and take in the stunning panoramic views.
  • Fairy Glen: A serene rock formation with its crystal-clear water and wooded hills, a beautiful location for a leisurely picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Betws-y-Coed

  • Swallow Falls: A gentle 1.5-mile walk through the beautiful Gwydir Forest.
  • Llyn Elsi Trail: A challenging 3.2-mile walk that leads around Llyn Elsi Lake.
  • Betws-y-Coed and Llyn Geirionydd Trail: A moderate 6-mile circular route that offers a beautiful view of the Llyn Geirionydd Lake.

Best Things to Do in Betws-y-Coed: Activities and Tours

  • Ty Hyll: Visit the quirky, eco-friendly unconventional house located in the woods of Snowdonia.
  • Tree Top Adventure Course: Challenge your limits and take on the Tree Top Adventure Course.
  • Canyoning: Get wet as you abseil through waterfalls, jump into rock pools and slide down natural flumes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Betws-y-Coed

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Betws-y-Coed, get acclimated to the area and take an evening stroll around Swallow Falls
Day 2 Hike up Mt Snowdon or take the famous Snowdon Mountain Railway to the top
Day 3 Enjoy a cycle or hike along the tracks of Gwydir Forest
Day 4 Try an adventurous outdoor activity like canyoning.
Day 5 Take a day trip to the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Conwy and visit the Castle and a town surrounded by walls.
Day 6 Take a walk and enjoy a picnic lunch in the Fairy Glen area
Day 7 Depart Betws-y-Coed with great memories of an adventurous week.

Food and Drink Scene of Betws-y-Coed

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A savory dish of melted cheese on toast with various toppings like Worcestershire sauce, mustard.
  • Cawl: A traditional hearty soup made with meat and seasonal vegetables.
  • Laverbread: A dish made from minced seaweed & oats, traditionally served mixed with oatmeal and bacon for breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Ale: Betws-y-Coed has its own microbrewery Seren brewery based at the Hafod Brewing Company and it produces some delicious local ale.
  • Penderyn Whiskey: Wales’ only whiskey distillery is located 25 miles away and offers a tour of the production process and a chance to try out their finest whiskey.

Where to Stay in Betws-y-Coed: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Craig-y-Dderwen Riverside Hotel: A beautiful hotel in the heart of the village, an ideal spot to start exploring the local area.
  • Betws-y-Coed Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • The Eagles Hotel: A cosy hotel that offers modern amenities and a traditional Welsh atmosphere.

What to Buy in Betws-y-Coed: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh Slate: The local slate from the Dinorwig Quarry is perfect material for unique gift items like coasters or memorabilia.
  • Penderyn Whiskey: Take home a deluxe bottle of the local whiskey to remind you of Wales.

Local Culture and Customs of Betws-y-Coed

  • Language: Welsh is the language spoken by many locals, and visitors in Betws-y-Coed will certainly come across road signs and buildings marked in Welsh. Although most locals do speak English, it’s a nice touch to learn and use a few local phrases.
  • Festivals: Betws-y-Coed hosts a wide range of festivals throughout the year, such as the Outdoor Arts Festival and the Snowdonia Marathon.

Best Time to Visit Betws-y-Coed

The best time to experience Betws-y-Coed is between the months of April to September, which typically have the warmest weather. The autumn months also provide the added beauty of the autumn colors on the trees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Betws-y-Coed

Do:

  • Pack for all weather conditions when visiting the area.
  • Be respectful of locals and local customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Leave any trash or litter on the trails.
  • Converse loudly in quiet areas and disturb the serene natural surroundings.
  • Ignore safety warnings when participating in any outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Betws-y-Coed

The village is generally a safe area, but tourists should still keep an eye on their belongings while visiting crowded areas.

Health in Betws-y-Coed

It is always advisable to pack your own first aid kit and to inform yourself about any health risks and vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Betws-y-Coed: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Betws-y-Coed

The closest airport is Liverpool John Lennon Airport, which is located 77 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Betws-y-Coed

The village has its own bus stop service run by the Arriva Bus Service Company, traveling six times a day to Bangor and Llandudno and other nearby villages.

Other Options

The most convenient way to explore the local landmarks around Betws-y-Coed is by renting a car, noting that public transport is not always reliable or frequent in this area.

How to Get Around in Betws-y-Coed

Travelers can explore the majority of Betws-y-Coed on foot. Many walking trails start at or close to the center of the town, and other attractions are only a short walk away. Taxis and car rentals are also available for hire locally.

Where to Go Next from Betws-y-Coed: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Caernarfon Castle: Another world heritage site situated just 20 miles north of Betws-y-Coed.
  • Portmeirion: The charming Italian village style resort town located 20 miles from the village.
  • Llanberis Lake Railway: Situated 18 miles away is a scenic railway that connects to Snowdon National Park.

Conclusion

Betws-y-Coed is a beautiful village in North Wales, providing visitors with a range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions to explore. With plenty of options available from adventurous outdoor activities to soothing scenic views, Betws-y-Coed is a destination suitable for all types of travelers. With our comprehensive guide, you can now plan your trip to the village with complete ease and have a memorable experience.
