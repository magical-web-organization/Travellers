Located in the heart of North Wales, Harlech is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Nestled at the foot of Snowdonia National Park, Harlech offers scenic views of the Welsh coast. This travel guide will take you on a journey through some of the best things to do in Harlech, including popular attractions, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. Whether you are a history buff or a nature lover, Harlech has something to offer for everyone.

Introduction to Harlech

Harlech is a medieval town that has played a significant role in Welsh history. The town is home to the iconic Harlech Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 13th century. The castle stands on top of a rocky hill and offers a panoramic view of the town and the surrounding countryside. Harlech is also known for its sandy beach, which is ideal for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing.

List of Places to Visit in Harlech

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harlech Castle: This historic castle stands as a symbol of the town’s Welsh heritage. Visitors can explore the castle’s six towers and discover its fascinating history.
  • The Royal St. David’s Golf Club: Founded in 1894, this golf club is the third oldest in Wales and offers stunning views of Snowdonia National Park.
  • St. Tanwg’s Church: This ancient church dates back to the 6th century and is known for its unique architecture and stunning stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Harlech: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harlech Beach: This beautiful sandy beach is a popular destination for swimmers and surfers alike. The beach stretches for miles along the coastline and offers stunning views of the Irish Sea.
  • Snowdonia National Park: This is the largest National Park in Wales and has some of the highest peaks in the UK. Visitors can explore the park’s scenic trails or take a ride on the Snowdon Mountain Railway to the summit of Snowdon.
  • Llyn Cwm Bychan: This picturesque lake is located in the heart of Snowdonia National Park and offers breathtaking views of the Welsh countryside. Visitors can hike along the lake’s scenic trails or go fishing in its crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harlech

Harlech is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and offers a variety of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Harlech include:

  • The Harlech Castle Circular Walk: This 3-mile trail takes visitors on a scenic tour of the town’s historic landmarks, including Harlech Castle, St. Tanwg’s Church, and the Royal St. David’s Golf Club.
  • The Rhinogydd Traverse: This challenging 16-mile trail takes visitors on a journey through Snowdonia National Park’s rugged terrain and offers breathtaking views of the Welsh coastline.
  • Coed y Brenin: This famous biking trail is located near Harlech and offers some of the best mountain biking experiences in the UK. The trail features a variety of challenges, including technical rocky sections and fast singletrack descents.

Best Things to Do in Harlech: Activities and Tours

Harlech has a variety of activities and tours that offer visitors a unique insight into the town’s history and culture. Here are some of the best things to do in Harlech:

  • Visit Harlech Castle: This iconic castle is a must-visit attraction when in Harlech. Visitors can explore the castle’s six towers and learn about its fascinating history.
  • Take a Guided Tour of Snowdonia National Park: A guided tour of Snowdonia National Park is a great way to discover the park’s hidden gems and learn about its rich history and culture.
  • Go Surfing at Harlech Beach: Harlech Beach is a popular destination for surfers and offers some of the best waves in the UK.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harlech

Day 1-2: Visit Harlech Castle and explore the town’s historic landmarks.

Day 3-4: Take a guided tour of Snowdonia National Park and discover its hidden gems.

Day 5-6: Enjoy outdoor adventures such as hiking in the Rhinogydd Mountains or biking along Coed y Brenin trail.

Day 7: Relax and unwind on Harlech Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Harlech

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A traditional dish made with a rich cheese sauce served over toasted bread.
  • Cawl: A hearty Welsh soup made with lamb and vegetables.
  • Bara Brith: A traditional Welsh fruitcake that is perfect for afternoon tea.

Local Beverages

  • Bragdy Lleu Brewery: This local brewery produces a variety of craft beers that perfectly complement the town’s cuisine.
  • Penderyn Distillery: This award-winning distillery produces some of the best Welsh whiskies in the UK.

Where to Stay in Harlech: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harlech has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Some of the popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Harlech include:

  • The Royal Goat Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Harlech and offers stunning views of Snowdonia National Park.
  • Harlech House: This family-run bed and breakfast is located close to Harlech Castle and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Y Branwen Hotel: This charming hotel is located near Harlech Beach and offers a relaxing atmosphere and exceptional service.

What to Buy in Harlech: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh pottery: Harlech is known for its unique pottery, which is handcrafted by local artisans.
  • Welsh tapestry: A traditional Welsh tapestry is a great souvenir to bring home from your trip to Harlech.
  • Penderyn whisky: A bottle of Penderyn whisky makes for a perfect gift for any whisky lover.

Local Culture and Customs of Harlech

The Welsh people are known for their warmth, hospitality, and love for their culture and history. Visitors to Harlech are advised to respect local customs and traditions and embrace the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Harlech

The best time to visit Harlech is between May and September when the weather is warm and the town is buzzing with tourists. The town also hosts a variety of festivals and events during this time, including the Harlech Food Festival and the Harlech Arts and Crafts Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harlech

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and follow the leave-no-trace principles.
  • Learn some basic Welsh phrases and try to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • litter and damage the natural environment.
  • Show disrespect to the Welsh language and culture.
  • Enter private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harlech

Harlech is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take usual precautions like keeping valuable belongings safe and avoiding walking alone at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Harlech

Harlech has a good health infrastructure with a few medical centers and hospitals. Visitors are advised to have a travel insurance to cover any medical expenses during their stay.

Getting to Harlech: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harlech

The nearest international airport to Harlech is Manchester airport, which is about 2 hours by car or train. There are also airports in Liverpool and Birmingham that offer regular flights to major cities in the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Harlech

Harlech is well connected by bus to major towns and cities in Wales. The local bus service also connects Harlech to nearby villages and towns in the region.

Other Options

The easiest way to get to Harlech is by car, and there are several car rental companies at major airports in the UK. Visitors can also take a train from London Euston station to Porthmadog and then take a bus to Harlech.

How to Get Around in Harlech

Harlech is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by using a bicycle. There are also local taxis and buses that operate in and around the town.

Where to Go Next from Harlech: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harlech is well connected by bus and train to major destinations in Wales and the UK. Some of the popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Portmeirion: This picturesque Italian-style village is located near Harlech and is known for its colorful architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • Llandudno: This charming seaside town is located on the north coast of Wales and offers stunning views of the Irish Sea.
  • Conwy Castle: This medieval castle is located in the coastal town of Conwy and is known for its unique architecture and rich history.

Conclusion

Harlech is a treasure trove of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you want to explore medieval castles, hike in Snowdonia National Park, or simply relax on the beach, Harlech has something for everyone. Visit Harlech one day and discover what makes this Welsh town so special!
