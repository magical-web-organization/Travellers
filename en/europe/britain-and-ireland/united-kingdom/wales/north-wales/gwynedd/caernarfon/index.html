Introduction to Caernarfon

Nestled along the shores of the Menai Strait in North Wales, Caernarfon is a charming town steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. From its iconic castle, stunning coastline, and rugged mountains to its vibrant local culture and delicious local cuisine, Caernarfon truly has it all.

Whether you’re an adventure seeker, history buff, foodie, or nature lover, Caernarfon will surely captivate your heart. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, and where to eat, stay, and shop in Caernarfon. So, let’s dive in and discover the magic of this Welsh gem!

List of Places to Visit in Caernarfon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Caernarfon Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most impressive medieval fortresses in the UK, Caernarfon Castle is a must-visit attraction in the town. Built in the 13th century, the castle was the birthplace of Prince Charles in 1948 and is steeped in fascinating history.

  • Segontium Roman Fort: Located just outside of the town, Segontium Roman Fort offers a glimpse into the lives of Roman soldiers who once lived here. Visitors can explore the ruins of the fort and see its impressive defensive walls, barracks, and granaries.

  • National Slate Museum: Housed in the historic workshops of the Dinorwig slate quarry, the National Slate Museum is a fantastic attraction for those interested in the industrial heritage of Wales. Visitors can see live demonstrations of slate splitting and learn about the quarrying methods used in the past.

  • St. Mary’s Church: Dating back to the 14th century, St. Mary’s Church is a beautiful example of Gothic architecture in Wales. Visitors can admire the intricate carvings and stained glass windows within.

Natural Attractions of Caernarfon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Snowdonia National Park: Located just a short drive from Caernarfon, Snowdonia National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers a variety of activities, including hiking trails, mountain biking, fishing, and kayaking.

  • Anglesey Coastal Path: For stunning coastal views, head to the Anglesey Coastal Path, which stretches for 125 miles along the island’s shoreline. Visitors can hike or bike along the path and see many beautiful beaches and coves.

  • Llyn Padarn: This stunning lake near Llanberis is a popular spot for watersports and fishing. The lake is surrounded by beautiful mountains and offers some of the best views in Snowdonia.

  • Welsh Highland Railway: Take a scenic train ride through Snowdonia aboard the Welsh Highland Railway. The journey will take you through some of the most breathtaking landscapes in Wales.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caernarfon

If you’re a fan of outdoor adventures, Caernarfon is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the area:

  • Snowdon Horseshoe: A challenging hike that takes you along the ridges of Snowdon, the highest peak in Wales.

  • Lon Eifion: A scenic bike trail that follows an old railway line between Caernarfon and Bryncir.

  • The Nantlle Ridge: A challenging hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and coastline.

Best Things to Do in Caernarfon: Activities and Tours

  • Sea kayaking: Explore the beautiful coastline of Caernarfon on a guided sea kayaking tour.

  • Zip World: Located just a short drive from Caernarfon, Zip World offers a variety of adrenaline-pumping activities, including zip lining and an underground trampoline park.

  • Caernarfon Food Tour: Sample the best local cuisine on a guided food tour of Caernarfon.

  • Boat trip to Ynys Enlli: Take a boat trip to the beautiful island of Ynys Enlli, located off the coast of the Llŷn Peninsula.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caernarfon

Day 1: Arrive in Caernarfon and explore the town’s rich history, including a visit to Caernarfon Castle and Segontium Roman Fort.

Day 2: Venture out of town to Snowdonia National Park for a day of hiking or biking on some of the park’s many trails.

Day 3: Take a guided kayaking tour along the coast of Caernarfon and enjoy stunning views.

Day 4: Head to Llyn Padarn for a day of fishing and relaxing by the lake.

Day 5: Take a train ride through Snowdonia aboard the Welsh Highland Railway.

Day 6: Visit the museums and galleries in town, including the National Slate Museum and Oriel Pendeitsh.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Anglesey and explore its charming villages and stunning coastline.

Food and Drink Scene of Caernarfon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A classic Welsh dish made with toasted bread and melted cheese.

  • Cawl: A traditional Welsh stew made with lamb or beef, potatoes, and vegetables.

  • Bara Brith: A delicious Welsh fruit cake made with tea-soaked dried fruit.

  • Laverbread: A traditional Welsh breakfast dish made with seaweed.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh beer and cider: Try some of the locally brewed beer and cider from the many breweries in the area.

  • Welsh whisky: The Penderyn Distillery, located just outside of Caernarfon, is one of Wales’ most famous whisky makers.

Where to Stay in Caernarfon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Royal Victoria Hotel: This historic hotel is located right in the heart of Caernarfon and offers stunning views of the castle.

  • Totters Independent Hostel: This charming hostel is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

  • Ty Dre Town House: This stylish guesthouse offers luxury accommodations in the heart of Caernarfon.

What to Buy in Caernarfon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh blankets and textiles: Take home a traditional Welsh blanket or other textile as a souvenir.

  • Slate products: Welsh slate has been used for centuries to create decorative objects and homeware items.

Local Culture and Customs of Caernarfon

The people of Caernarfon take great pride in their Welsh culture and language. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, and it’s always appreciated to attempt to speak a few words of Welsh.

Best Time to Visit Caernarfon

The best time to visit Caernarfon is from May to September when the weather is milder and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caernarfon

Do:

  • Try some of the local cuisine, including traditional Welsh dishes.

  • Respect the local customs and language.

  • Take in the stunning natural beauty of Caernarfon.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Be too loud or disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caernarfon

Caernarfon is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take normal safety precautions.

Health in Caernarfon

There are several healthcare facilities in Caernarfon, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies.

Getting to Caernarfon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caernarfon

  • Manchester Airport: About 97 miles away from Caernarfon.

  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport: About 90 miles away from Caernarfon.

Bus Stations Near Caernarfon

  • Caernarfon Bus Station: Located in the town center, this bus station offers regular services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Train: Caernarfon is not serviced by trains but visitors can travel to the nearby cities of Bangor or Llandudno by train.

How to Get Around in Caernarfon

The best way to explore Caernarfon is on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a guided tour for a more in-depth experience.

Where to Go Next from Caernarfon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Anglesey: A beautiful island with stunning beaches and charming villages.

  • Conwy: A historic town with a stunning castle and beautiful coastline.

  • Llanberis: A small village at the foot of Snowdon, with plenty of outdoor activities on offer.

Conclusion

Caernarfon is an enchanting town that offers something for every type of traveler. From its rich history and culture to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, Caernarfon is sure to leave a lasting impression on every visitor. So, pack your bags, start planning your trip, and get ready to discover the magic of Caernarfon!
