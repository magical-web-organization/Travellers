Introduction to Beddgelert

Located in Gwynedd, North Wales, Beddgelert is a picturesque village famous for its stunning scenery and outdoor activities. Nestled in the Welsh mountains, Beddgelert offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Beddgelert

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Slate Museum

The National Slate Museum offers visitors a glimpse into the history and industry of mining Slate in Wales. Located on the site of a former quarry, the museum offers intriguing insights into the process of turning natural slate into roof tiles and other products that were once exported all over the world.

Sygun Copper Mine

Discover the secrets of an 18th-century copper mine with a visit to the Sygun Copper Mine. The underground tours take visitors on a journey through time to see how miners worked in the past. The mine has a rich and fascinating mining history that museum guides share with visitors.

Natural Attractions of Beddgelert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Snowdonia National Park

Snowdonia National Park is a must-visit destination in Wales. It is the largest National Park in Wales and the third largest in the UK. The park offers stunning scenery, including Wales’s highest peak – Mount Snowdon. The park has something for everyone - from hiking and biking to stunning lakes and rivers for kayaking or fishing.

Aberglaslyn Pass

Aberglaslyn Pass is a scenic and stunning nature reserve that boasts a deep river gorge that cuts through the mountains. Visitors can take a scenic walk along the riverbank with mountains towering on either side.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beddgelert

There are plenty of varying routes to explore in Beddgelert with distances ranging from easy to challenging. The most famous route is the Miners Track which leads to Mount Snowdon. There are also family-friendly routes such as Gelert’s Grave Walk which only covers a small distance and provides views of local historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Beddgelert: Activities and Tours

King Arthur’s Labyrinth

Discover the mythical land of King Arthur’s Labyrinth - an award-winning underground adventure attraction. The attraction offers an experience through the mists of time, telling the stories of Welsh mythology.

Beddgelert Bikes

Beddgelert Bikes offers bike hire, guided mountain bike rides, and cycle coaching. Visitors can explore the mountains of Snowdonia and the picturesque scenery by bike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beddgelert

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Beddgelert and settle into your accommodation. Explore the small village and local shops.
2 Visit Snowdonia National Park and undertake the popular Miners Track, which leads up to Mount Snowdon.
3 Take a walk through the Aberglaslyn Pass nature reserve.
4 Take a guided mountain bike tour with Beddgelert Bikes.
5 Relax and take in the scenery. Visit Sygun Copper Mine and follow up with a visit to the National Slate Museum.
6 Head to King Arthur’s Labyrinth for an underground adventure.
7 Departure day - check out of your accommodation and make your way home.

Food and Drink Scene of Beddgelert

Must-Try Dishes

Welsh Rarebit

A famous dish of Beddgelert is Wales’s answer to cheese on toast, known as Welsh Rarebit. This famous Welsh dish features a mix of cheese, mustard and ale, served hot on toast.

Local Beverages

Welsh Ale

Wales is famous for its dark and sometimes bitter ale. Beddgelert offers many traditional Welsh pubs to sample a pint of local ale.

Where to Stay in Beddgelert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ty Gorsaf

Ty Gorsaf offers visitors stunning views of Snowdonia National Park. It is a family-friendly accommodation that includes a bar and garden area.

Caban Cyf

Caban Cyf is a budget-friendly hostel offering visitors to Beddgelert affordable accommodation with comfortable beds and traditional Welsh breakfasts.

What to Buy in Beddgelert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Slate Products

As a part of the National Slate Museum, Beddgelert offers handmade slate products, such as clock faces, coasters, placemats and custom engraved memorabilia.

Local Culture and Customs of Beddgelert

Beddgelert is steeped in Welsh history and culture. The Welsh language is spoken by a large number of people in the area and the festivals of Eisteddfod and Santes Dwynwen Day, which celebrates the Welsh patron saint of love, are celebrated in the village.

Best Time to Visit Beddgelert

The best time to visit Beddgelert is between May and September. The summer months offer visitors sunny weather and longer daylight. It is important also to note that this is the busiest time to visit the area and that advance bookings are essential.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beddgelert

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by sticking to designated paths whilst exploring the country to help preserve it for future visitors.
  • Learn a few Welsh phrases before visiting Beddgelert for an authentic experience and to show respect to the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind while exploring the countryside.
  • Avoid climbing fences, walls, or gateways, as they are usually private land.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beddgelert

When exploring the countryside of Beddgelert, ensure that you are wearing appropriate attire and footwear suitable for the conditions. Also, be aware that the terrain can sometimes be rough, especially during wet seasons, so it is crucial to take care.

Health in Beddgelert

Due to the natural environment of Beddgelert, visitors should be aware of any allergies to plants or animals which can include pollen allergies and species such as sheep and cattle.

Getting to Beddgelert: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beddgelert

The nearest airport to Beddgelert is Manchester International Airport, approximately a 3-hour drive by car.

Bus Stations Near Beddgelert

The closest bus station to Beddgelert is located in the town of Caernarfon and has a regular bus service that stops in Beddgelert city centre.

Other Options

Beddgelert is a prime location for train travel. The nearest train station is Porthmadog, and the Cambrian Coast Trains connect regularly to Birmingham and Manchester.

How to Get Around in Beddgelert

The village of Beddgelert is small enough to explore by foot. Moreover, travelling to the surrounding areas can be done by bus, taxi or bike hire.

Where to Go Next from Beddgelert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Caernarfon Castle

Located approximately 20 km from Beddgelert, Caernarfon Castle is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most stunning castles in the UK. The castle attracts visitors from all over the world with views over Snowdonia and the Menai Strait.

Conwy Castle

Conwy Castle is another UNESCO World Heritage site located approximately 35 km from Beddgelert. The fortress is notably well preserved and is one of Wales’ most impressive medieval castles.

Conclusion

Beddgelert, North Wales is a small village known for its rich history, stunning natural scenery and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide offers visitors everything they need to know when planning their next adventure in Beddgelert. From where to stay, what to eat, and things to do, Beddgelert offers an experience that will create memories that last a lifetime.
