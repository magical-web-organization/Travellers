Introduction to Bardsey Island

Bardsey Island, also known as Ynys Enlli, is a small island located off the west coast of North Wales, UK. Known for its rich natural beauty and fascinating history, Bardsey Island has been an important pilgrimage site for centuries, with its religious significance earning it the nickname, the “Island of 20,000 Saints.”

Today, Bardsey Island remains a popular destination, attracting nature lovers, historians, and adventurers alike. Whether you’re interested in exploring the island’s rugged landscapes, relaxing on its pristine beaches, or soaking up its unique cultural heritage, there’s no shortage of experiences to be had on Bardsey Island.

List of Places to Visit in Bardsey Island

If you are planning a trip to Bardsey Island, make sure to add these places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bardsey Island Heritage Centre: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the island’s history, from prehistoric times to modern-day.
  • St. Mary’s Church: Considered to be the spiritual center of Bardsey Island, this medieval church dates back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Bardsey Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mynydd Enlli: This peak rises to 167 meters high and provides stunning panoramic views of the island and surrounding coastline.
  • Porth Solfach Beach: With its crystal-clear waters and dramatic cliffs, this secluded beach is a perfect spot to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bardsey Island

Bardsey Island offers several hiking and biking trails to suit all levels of fitness and skill. Some popular trails include:

  • The Bardsey Island Circular Walk: This is a seven-mile hiking trail that takes you on a scenic loop around the island, passing by historic ruins and stunning views.
  • The South End Circular Walk: This shorter loop (2.5 miles) takes you through the island’s southern tip, offering breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Bardsey Island: Activities and Tours

  • Whale and Dolphin Watching: Take a guided boat tour to spot the abundant marine life in the area, such as porpoises, seals, and dolphins.
  • Wildlife watching: In addition to marine life, Bardsey Island is also home to various bird species, such as Manx Shearwaters and Puffins.

Food and Drink Scene of Bardsey Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: With its abundant marine life, Bardsey Island is the perfect place to experience a range of fresh seafood delicacies, such as lobster and crab.
  • Welsh Cakes: These sweet treats are a staple in Welsh culture and are often served with a cup of tea.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Beer: Wales is known for its beer and Bardsey Island has a few excellent microbreweries.
  • Welsh Whiskey: With its unique blend of malt and barley, Welsh whiskey is a must-try for whiskey lovers.

Where to Stay in Bardsey Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ynys Enlli Farm: A working farm that offers self-catering accommodation.
  • Hen Ysgol: An old schoolhouse converted into a cozy guesthouse.

What to Buy in Bardsey Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts and art: Bardsey Island is home to a vibrant arts community, and you can find unique handmade crafts and artwork in local shops.
  • Local produce: From freshly caught seafood to homemade preserves and jams, you can find a range of locally made products to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Bardsey Island

  • Welsh language: Welsh is the primary language spoken on Bardsey Island.
  • Religious significance: Bardsey Island has a deep spiritual and religious significance, and visitors are asked to respect this cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Bardsey Island

The best time to visit Bardsey Island is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is typically warmer and drier. It is also the peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodation and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bardsey Island

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural and cultural heritage.
  • Take all your trash with you when leaving the island.

    Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • Build fires or camp outside designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bardsey Island

Bardsey Island is generally a safe and tranquil place to visit, but visitors should be aware of the island’s rugged terrain and potential hazards, such as sudden changes in weather conditions and steep cliffs.

Health in Bardsey Island

Bardsey Island has limited medical facilities, so visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Bardsey Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bardsey Island

  • Liverpool John Lennon Airport is the closest airport, located approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Bardsey Island

  • The nearest bus station is in Pwllheli, approximately 10 km away from Bardsey Island.

Other Options

  • Boat: Visitors can take a boat from Porth Meudwy, a small harbor near Aberdaron, to Bardsey Island.

How to Get Around in Bardsey Island

Bardsey Island is small enough to explore on foot or bike, and there are no cars or roads on the island.

Where to Go Next from Bardsey Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aberdaron

A small village located on the Llŷn Peninsula, Aberdaron is famous for its iconic church dedicated to St. Hywyn and its picturesque beaches.

Snowdonia National Park

Located less than an hour’s drive away from Bardsey Island, Snowdonia National Park offers some of the most breathtaking landscapes and hiking trails in the UK.

Conclusion

Bardsey Island is a unique and enchanting destination that offers a wealth of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. Whether you’re looking for a tranquil getaway or an exciting outdoor adventure, a visit to Bardsey Island is an unforgettable experience.
