Introduction to Beaumaris

Beaumaris is a quaint town located on the island of Anglesey in North Wales, United Kingdom. Famous for its beautiful historical architecture, picturesque harbor, and stunning views of the Snowdonia mountain range, Beaumaris is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Wales.

List of Places to Visit in Beaumaris

There is no shortage of great attractions and activities to enjoy in Beaumaris. Whether you are interested in exploring the town’s rich history or taking in its scenic natural beauty, there is something for everyone. Here are just a few of the must-see places to visit in Beaumaris.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beaumaris Castle: A 13th-century castle built by King Edward I that is considered one of the finest examples of medieval military architecture in Europe.
  • Beaumaris Gaol: This former prison now serves as a museum offering a glimpse into the harsh realities of prison life in the 19th century.
  • Plas Newydd: A stunning 18th-century country house, home to the Marquess of Anglesey.

Natural Attractions of Beaumaris: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Menai Strait: A narrow stretch of water between Anglesey and mainland Wales that offers stunning views and a range of outdoor activities, including kayak tours, powerboat trips and wildlife watching.
  • Puffin Island: A small uninhabited island off the coast of Anglesey, that is home to a diverse range of seabirds and wildlife.
  • Snowdonia National Park: A short drive from Beaumaris, this mountainous region offers breathtaking views, hiking, and biking opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaumaris

Beaumaris offers several fantastic routes for hikers and bikers that take in the town’s stunning natural scenery. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Beaumaris to Llanddona Beach: A scenic hike that takes you along the coast to one of the area’s most beautiful beaches.
  • Llangoed Circular Walk: A moderate hike that takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding the town.
  • Menai Bridge to Llanfairpwllgwyngyll: A picturesque bike ride that takes you over one of the world’s most famous suspension bridges and through some of the area’s prettiest villages.

Best Things to Do in Beaumaris: Activities and Tours

  • Sea fishing: Whether you are an experienced angler or a complete novice, Beaumaris offers fantastic sea fishing opportunities.
  • Walking tours: Explore the town’s rich history and architecture with a guided walking tour.
  • Kayaking: Take a guided kayak tour along the Menai Strait and discover some of the area’s hidden coves and secret beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beaumaris

Day 1: Explore the town’s rich history with a visit to Beaumaris Castle and the Beaumaris Gaol museum.

Day 2: Take a kayaking tour along the Menai Strait.

Day 3: Visit Plas Newydd, a stunning 18th-century country house set in beautiful gardens.

Day 4: Visit Snowdonia National Park and hike one of the many stunning trails.

Day 5: Take a bike ride over the Menai Suspension Bridge and explore some of the charming villages of mainland Wales.

Day 6: Go sea fishing in the Menai Strait.

Day 7: Relax and stroll around the picturesque harbor town of Beaumaris.

Food and Drink Scene of Beaumaris

Beaumaris offers a diverse range of dining options, from traditional Welsh cuisine to international dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cawl: A traditional Welsh stew made with lamb, potatoes, and leeks.
  • Welsh rarebit: A delicious grilled cheese on toast dish with a savory twist.
  • Bara Brith: A sweet tea loaf made with dried fruit and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh beer: Try one of the many delicious locally brewed ales or lagers.
  • Welsh whisky: A smooth and warming alternative to traditional Scotch whisky.
  • Welsh cider: Made with locally grown apples, Welsh cider is a refreshing drink perfect for sipping on a warm summer evening.

Where to Stay in Beaumaris: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beaumaris offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses and bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • Bishopsgate House Hotel: A charming, family-run hotel with luxurious rooms and stunning views of the harbor.
  • The Bulkeley Hotel: A historic hotel with a prime location in the heart of Beaumaris.
  • The Liverpool Arms Hotel: A traditional inn with comfortable rooms and a great atmosphere.

What to Buy in Beaumaris: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From traditional Welsh souvenirs to locally made crafts and foods, Beaumaris offers plenty of shopping opportunities. Here are some of the top options:

  • Welsh blankets and textiles: Purchase one of the many beautiful blankets and textiles made with locally sourced wool.
  • Local cheese and chutney: Sample some of the fantastic cheeses and chutneys produced in the surrounding countryside.
  • Handmade crafts: Support local artists and craftsmen by purchasing one of their handmade creations.

Local Culture and Customs of Beaumaris

Welsh culture is rich and diverse, and Beaumaris is no exception. From traditional Welsh music to local festivals and customs, there is plenty to discover. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Welsh is a distinct language spoken by many people in Wales, including in Beaumaris.
  • The Eisteddfod is an annual Welsh festival of literature, music, and performance that draws visitors from all over the world.
  • Rugby is the national sport of Wales and is a significant part of Welsh culture.

Best Time to Visit Beaumaris

Beaumaris is a year-round destination, with each season offering something unique. The summer months offer warm weather and a range of outdoor activities, while the winter months are quiet and peaceful, perfect for a relaxing getaway.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beaumaris

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Beaumaris:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the fantastic outdoor activities on offer.
  • Try some of the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors.
  • Leave litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when taking part in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beaumaris

Beaumaris is a safe destination for tourists, but it is always important to take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings after dark.

Health in Beaumaris

There are several small pharmacies in Beaumaris, as well as a general practitioner’s surgery. There is also a hospital located in nearby Bangor in case of emergency.

Getting to Beaumaris: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beaumaris is easily accessible by both car and public transport. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Beaumaris

The nearest major airport is Manchester Airport, which is approximately 120 miles away. There is also a small regional airport located in Anglesey.

Bus Stations Near Beaumaris

The nearest bus station is located in Bangor, which is approximately 4 miles away. There are regular local bus services that connect Beaumaris with Bangor and other nearby towns.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies located in both Bangor and Holyhead, which offer a convenient way to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Beaumaris

Beaumaris is a small town, and most attractions are within easy walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer not to walk, there are also local bus services available.

Where to Go Next from Beaumaris: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beaumaris is located in an area of Wales known for its natural beauty and rich history. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Conwy: A beautiful medieval town located just across the Menai Strait.
  • Llandudno: A charming Victorian seaside town with a range of attractions and activities.
  • Holyhead: A busy ferry port with regular connections to Dublin and other destinations in Ireland.

Conclusion

Beaumaris is a beautiful town with a rich cultural and historical heritage. Whether you are interested in exploring its rich history or enjoying the stunning natural beauty of the surrounding area, there is plenty to see and do. Plan your perfect trip to Beaumaris today!
