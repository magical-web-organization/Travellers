Introduction to Red Wharf Bay

Nestled on the east coast of Anglesey, Red Wharf Bay is a scenic beach that spans over 4 miles. It is a stunning natural attraction that attracts visitors from near and far. With plenty of outdoor activities, scenic views, cultural attractions, and mouth-watering delicacies, Red Wharf Bay is a perfect destination for travelers of all interests.

List of Places to Visit in Red Wharf Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beaumaris Castle - This is a 13th-century medieval castle that offers visitors an insight into the history of the region. It is a must-visit for every history buff.
  • Anglesey Model Village & Gardens - A beautifully designed miniature village that takes visitors on a journey back in time. It also has gardens that are perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • Oriel Ynys Mon - It is an art museum that exhibits the works of local artists. The gallery mostly showcases the beauty of Anglesey and Wales through various forms of art.

Natural Attractions of Red Wharf Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red Wharf Bay Beach - A fantastic beach that stretches over 4 miles and offers a stunning view of the beautiful coast.
  • Parys Mountain - Located in the nearby town of Amlwch, Parys Mountain is a historic site where visitors can take a guided tour and explore the old copper mines.
  • Copper Kingdom - A museum dedicated to the history of copper mining in the area, where visitors can learn about the industry that brought prosperity and wealth to the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Red Wharf Bay

Coastal Path

The coastal path around Anglesey offers breathtaking views of the sea. It is a 125-mile-long course that passes through several sites, including Red Wharf Bay. The route is suitable for both hikers and bikers, and it is recommended to take the course in parts, as it is tiring to tackle all at once.

The Heritage Trail

The Heritage Trail is a 10-mile course that connects several historical sites, including St Cybi’s Church, Lighthouse, Holyhead mountain, and Breakwater Country Park. It is an excellent route for history enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Red Wharf Bay: Activities and Tours

Boat Trips

Take a boat trip around the bay and explore the picturesque view of the coast. There are plenty of tour operators available that offer boat trips and fishing tours.

Watersports

Red Wharf Bay is an excellent destination for watersports, including windsurfing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. Several local tour operators organize training sessions for beginners as well as advanced learners.

Week-Long Itinerary for Red Wharf Bay

Day 1 Visit Beaumaris Castle in the morning. Lunch at Dylan’s Restaurant in Menai Bridge. Afternoon visit to Oriel Ynys Mon.
Day 2 Hike the Coastal Path and take the scenic view. Afternoon, go to the nearby town of Amlwch and visit Parys Mountain.
Day 3 Spend the morning relaxing on Red Wharf Bay Beach. In the afternoon, take a boat tour around the bay.
Day 4 Visit the Copper Kingdom Museum to learn about the history of copper mining in the area. Lunch at the Red Boat Cafe.
Day 5 Rent a bike and explore the Heritage Trail. Afternoon, visit Lligwy Beach for a relaxing time.
Day 6 Take a guided fishing tour and experience the thrill of catching fish. Lunch at The Boathouse in Red Wharf Bay.
Day 7 Visit the Anglesey Model Village and Gardens. Afternoon, explore the nearby city of Bangor.

Best Time to Visit Red Wharf Bay

The best time to visit Red Wharf Bay is during the summer months, between May and August, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. However, visitors can also expect a higher number of crowds during this time. The shoulder seasons of April-May and September-October are also a good time to visit with fewer crowds and milder weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Red Wharf Bay

Red Wharf Bay is a relatively safe destination, but visitors must follow basic safety measures when engaging in outdoor activities. Always check for signs and warnings about unpredictable tides before going near the sea. Wear appropriate clothing and footwear while hiking or biking.

Health in Red Wharf Bay

Visitors must carry their necessary medication and travel insurance. It is also worth keeping in mind that the local medical and emergency services may differ from the traveler’s home country.
