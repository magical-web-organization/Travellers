Introduction to Mold

Mold is a picturesque market town located in the Clwydian Range in North Wales. The town is steeped in history and boasts stunning landscapes, offering visitors a wide range of activities and experiences to enjoy during their visit. Mold is a hub for walking and cycling enthusiasts with miles of trails leading into the nearby hills, forests, and valleys.

In this ultimate travel guide to Mold, we will explore the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and where to eat. We’ll also provide you with useful information on the town’s history, culture, and customs, as well as important travel tips to make your visit a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Mold

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Clwyd Theatr Cymru - Located in the heart of Mold, Clwyd Theatr Cymru is a leading producing theater that hosts a wide variety of performances, including plays, musicals, and dance shows.
  2. The Bailey Hill Fort - This well-preserved Norman Motte and Bailey fort is located at the edge of the town center and dates back to the 11th century.
  3. Gwrych Castle - This Grade I listed castle is located a few miles from Mold and is a stunning example of a Gothic revival castle. It has been used as a location for several TV shows and films.

Natural Attractions of Mold: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Loggerheads Country Park - A beautiful country park that offers visitors a range of activities, including hiking, cycling, and wildlife watching.
  2. Moel Famau - Located in the heart of the Clwydian Range, Moel Famau is the highest peak in the area and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Clwydian Way - This 122-mile circular path passes through the Clwydian Range Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, offering visitors a great opportunity to explore the local landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mold

  1. Offa’s Dyke Path - This 177-mile path runs from the North Welsh coast to the South Welsh coast, passing through the hills and valleys that surround Mold.
  2. The Mold Walks - A series of waymarked walks that take the visitor through some of the town’s picturesque areas, including the town center and the hills surrounding the town.
  3. Coed Nercwys - A beautiful woodland that offers visitors miles of well-marked trails for hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Mold: Activities and Tours

  1. Go Ape - A tree-top adventure that offers visitors a wide range of activities, including zip-lining, high ropes, and a free-fall experience.
  2. Celtic Quest Coasteering - A unique outdoor adventure that combines rock climbing, scrambling, and cliff jumping in a stunning coastal location in North Wales.
  3. Mold Food and Drink Festival - A popular annual festival that celebrates local food and drink producers, offering visitors the chance to sample some of the best produce from the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mold

Food and Drink Scene of Mold

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Welsh Lamb - This is a specialty of North Wales, and you will find it on most local menus.
  2. Bara Brith - This traditional Welsh fruit bread is a must-try and is perfect with a cup of tea.
  3. Welsh Rarebit - A traditional dish made with toasted bread and a savory cheese sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Cwrw Gorslas - A range of locally brewed ales that are popular in the region.
  2. Penderyn Whiskey - This award-winning Welsh whiskey is distilled in a nearby distillery and is a must-try for whiskey fans.
  3. Welsh Wine - The Welsh wine scene is growing, and you can find a range of excellent local wines at Mold’s restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Mold: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Beaufort Park Hotel - A 3-star hotel located in the heart of Mold that offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.
  2. The Druid Inn - This traditional pub and hotel is located a few miles from Mold and offers visitors a range of rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  3. Y Beudy - This beautifully restored barn is now a luxury guesthouse located in the countryside just outside Mold.

What to Buy in Mold: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Welsh Blankets - These woven blankets are a popular souvenir and can be found in several stores around Mold.
  2. Welsh Cakes - These sweet treats are a must-try and are available in several bakeries and cafes in Mold.
  3. Slate Products - Slate is a popular material in Wales, and you can find a range of slate products, including coasters, cheeseboards, and placemats, in several stores around Mold.

Local Culture and Customs of Mold

Mold is a deeply historical town, and visitors can explore the town’s rich past by visiting several historical monuments around the town. Welsh is spoken in many parts of Mold, and visitors may hear this ancient Celtic language spoken in some areas.

Best Time to Visit Mold

The best time to visit Mold is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is mild, and the countryside is at its most beautiful. However, the town is beautiful all year round, and each season has its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mold

Do:

  1. Learn a few words in Welsh.
  2. Visit the local markets and try the local produce.
  3. Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in the countryside.
  2. Ignore local advice on walking and hiking.
  3. Speak loudly in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mold

Mold is generally a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any significant safety issues. However, visitors should take care when hiking or cycling in the surrounding hills and valleys and should always follow local advice on walking routes and safety precautions.

Health in Mold

The health facilities in Mold are generally good, and visitors can access medical help when needed. Visitors from the EU can use their EHIC card to access free or reduced-cost healthcare.

Getting to Mold: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mold is well connected with several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Mold

  1. Liverpool John Lennon Airport - Approximately 30 miles from Mold.
  2. Manchester Airport - Approximately 54 miles from Mold.

Bus Stations Near Mold

  1. Mold Bus Station - Mold’s main bus station is located in the town center and offers regular services to nearby towns and cities.
  2. Wrexham Bus Station - Wrexham’s bus station is located a few miles from Mold and offers connections to major cities across the UK.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Mold by train, and the nearest train station is located in Flint, approximately six miles from Mold.

How to Get Around in Mold

Mold is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot. However, for those who want to explore the surrounding countryside and hills, a car is recommended. There are several car rental companies located in Mold that offer competitive rates.

Where to Go Next from Mold: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Snowdonia National Park - Located approximately 40 miles from Mold, Snowdonia National Park is a stunning natural area that offers visitors a range of incredible landscapes and outdoor activities.
  2. Llangollen - This quaint Welsh town is located approximately 20 miles from Mold and is popular with visitors due to its beautiful scenery and range of activities, including canoeing, kayaking, and cultural events.
  3. Chester - This medieval city is located approximately 15 miles from Mold and offers visitors a range of attractions, including the city walls, a beautiful cathedral, and impressive shopping areas.

Conclusion

Mold is a small but fascinating town that offers visitors a wealth of opportunities to explore the town’s rich history and beautiful countryside. Whether you come for the outdoor activities, local cuisine, or to experience the local culture and customs, Mold is sure to leave a lasting impression. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your visit and explore this stunning part of North Wales.
