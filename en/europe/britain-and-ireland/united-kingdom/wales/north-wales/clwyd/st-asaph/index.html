St Asaph is a charming city located in the heart of the Vale of Clwyd, North Wales. It’s known for its rich cultural heritage, outdoor activities, scenic beauty, and historic landmarks. If you’re planning a trip to St Asaph, keep reading to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and tips for a memorable journey.

Introduction to St Asaph

St Asaph, also known as Llanelwy in Welsh, is a small city with a population of approximately 3,500 people. It’s situated 30 miles from Liverpool and 70 miles from Manchester, making it a popular destination for day trips from these cities.

Originally founded in the 5th century as a monastic community, St Asaph played an important role in Wales’ religious and cultural history. Today, the city boasts a vibrant arts scene, a thriving food and drink scene, and lots of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in St Asaph

St Asaph offers a variety of attractions suitable for all interests and ages. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St Asaph Cathedral – This beautiful cathedral is one of the oldest in Wales, dating back to the 13th century. It houses many stunning works of art, including an internationally acclaimed organ and a splendid East Window.
  • The Bishops Palace – This historic palace once served as the residence of the Bishop of St Asaph. Today, it is a museum that features a collection of artifacts that showcase the city’s religious and cultural heritage.
  • St Asaph Museum – This museum provides an insight into the history and culture of the city, featuring exhibitions on everything from agriculture to traditional Welsh costume.

Natural Attractions of St Asaph: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Offa’s Dyke Path – This long-distance trail follows the 8th-century earthwork that once marked the boundary between England and Wales. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque countryside and scenic views while walking or cycling along the path.
  • Loggerheads Country Park – Located only 10 miles from St Asaph, this park is home to miles of hiking and mountain biking trails. Visitors can take in stunning views of the River Alyn and explore the sandstone cliffs and woodland.
  • The Clwydian Range – This range of hills is a protected Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty and offers breathtaking views of the Welsh countryside. Hiking trails are available for visitors who want to explore the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Asaph

St Asaph is surrounded by beautiful countryside and is a paradise for walkers and cyclists. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Vale of Clwyd Walk – This is a 17-mile walking trail that showcases the best of the Vale of Clwyd. The trail passes through several picturesque villages and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.
  • The Flintshire Leisure Tour – This is a 20-mile cycling route that takes visitors through beautiful countryside and past several fascinating historical sites, including the ruins of Flint Castle.
  • The Clwydian Way – This is a 122-mile long-distance walk that takes visitors through some of the most scenic areas of North Wales. The route includes steep climbs, rugged terrain, and stunning views of the Welsh countryside.

Best Things to Do in St Asaph: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or just looking to relax and unwind, St Asaph has plenty of activities to keep you entertained:

  • Go Ape Treetop Adventure – This adventure park features over 30 exciting obstacles, including zip lines, Tarzan swings, and rope ladders.
  • Glasfryn Parc Activity Centre – This centre offers a variety of activities, including go-karting, archery, fishing, and ten-pin bowling.
  • Denbighshire Leisure Centre – This leisure centre offers a swimming pool, gym, and sports facilities for visitors who want to stay active.

Week-Long Itinerary for St Asaph

If you’re visiting St Asaph for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore St Asaph Cathedral and Bishops Palace.
  • Day 2: Enjoy a day of hiking or mountain biking at Loggerheads Country Park.
  • Day 3: Visit St Asaph Museum and take a leisurely walk along the banks of the River Clwyd.
  • Day 4: Embark on the Vale of Clwyd Walk.
  • Day 5: Spend the day at the beach in Rhyl or Prestatyn.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as Conwy Castle or Snowdonia National Park.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a day of relaxation, perhaps indulging in a spa treatment or a round of golf at one of the many nearby golf courses.

Food and Drink Scene of St Asaph

St Asaph boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the best of Welsh cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit – This is a traditional Welsh dish that consists of a slice of toasted bread with melted cheese, mustard, and ale.
  • Cawl – This is a hearty Welsh soup made with lamb or beef served with vegetables and potatoes.
  • Bara Brith – This is a traditional Welsh fruit cake that is served with a dollop of clotted cream.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Ale – Wales is famous for its beer, and there are many local breweries in St Asaph that offer a variety of ales to visitors.
  • Welsh Whisky – This is a type of whisky that is made in Wales and is known for its unique taste.

Where to Stay in St Asaph: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St Asaph offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • St Asaph Travelodge – This budget-friendly hotel is located in the city centre and offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Tan-Yr-Onnen Guest House – This guest house is a converted farmhouse that offers stunning views of the Welsh countryside. Rooms are spacious and furnished with antique furniture.
  • Plas Efenechtyd Cottage B&B – This bed and breakfast is located in a peaceful village just outside of St Asaph and offers delightful rooms with period features.

What to Buy in St Asaph: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St Asaph is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy during your visit:

  • Traditional Welsh Clothing – You can purchase traditional Welsh clothing, including tweed jackets and kilts, at many local shops.
  • Welsh Slate Products – Wales is famous for its slate mines, and you can find a variety of slate products, including coasters, cheese boards, and ornaments, at many shops.
  • Welsh Language Books – Welsh is the native language of Wales, and you can find a variety of books on Welsh language and culture at local bookstores.

Local Culture and Customs of St Asaph

St Asaph has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Respect the Welsh Language – Welsh is an important part of Welsh culture, and you’ll hear it spoken in many places throughout St Asaph. Try to learn a few Welsh phrases to show your respect for the language and culture.
  • Be Polite and Friendly – The people of St Asaph are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness. Be sure to greet people with a smile and strike up a conversation with the locals.
  • Celebrate Welsh Festivals and Holidays – St Asaph celebrates a variety of festivals and holidays, including St David’s Day, the Welsh National Eisteddfod, and the Christmas Markets. Join in the celebrations to experience Welsh culture at its best.

Best Time to Visit St Asaph

The best time to visit St Asaph is between April and September when the weather is mild and sunny. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, festivals, and events without worrying about the rain.

However, if you’re a fan of winter sports, then the winter months are the best time to visit. The snow-capped mountains and winter sports resorts offer a unique experience that you won’t find during the spring and summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Asaph

To make the most of your visit to St Asaph, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Take a Welsh language course.
  • Explore the countryside and natural attractions of St Asaph.
  • Learn about the city’s rich cultural and religious history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t assume everyone speaks English. Try to learn a few Welsh phrases to show respect for the Welsh language and culture.
  • Don’t miss the chance to sample traditional Welsh food and drink.

Safety and Health

St Asaph is a safe and friendly city, but it’s always important to take precautions when travelling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in St Asaph

  • Keep your valuables secure and be aware of pickpockets in tourist areas.
  • Always wear sunblock when spending time outdoors.
  • Be careful when hiking or mountain biking and carry a map and compass with you.

Health in St Asaph

  • If you’re visiting from overseas, make sure you have appropriate travel insurance before you arrive in St Asaph.
  • Check with your doctor if you need any vaccinations before your trip.
  • If you have any medical conditions, bring a copy of your medical records with you.

Getting to St Asaph: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Asaph is well-connected to other parts of the UK and Europe. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near St Asaph

  • Manchester Airport – This is the closest international airport to St Asaph, located approximately 70 miles away.
  • Liverpool Airport – This airport is located approximately 30 miles from St Asaph.

Bus Stations Near St Asaph

  • St Asaph Bus Station – This bus station is located in the city centre and offers services to destinations around the region.

Other Options

  • Train – The nearest train station to St Asaph is Rhyl, which is located approximately 5 miles away.
  • Car – St Asaph is located just off the A55 Expressway, which provides easy access to major cities such as Liverpool and Manchester.

How to Get Around in St Asaph

St Asaph is a small city, and it’s easy to get around on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside, hiring a car or bike is recommended.

Where to Go Next from St Asaph: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St Asaph is located in the heart of North Wales, which offers a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Conwy Castle – This medieval castle is located approximately 20 miles from St Asaph and is one of the best-preserved castles in Wales.
  • Llangollen – This charming town is located approximately 25 miles from St Asaph and is home to the Llangollen Canal and the famous Llangollen International Musical Eisteddfod.
  • Snowdonia National Park – This park is located approximately 50 miles from St Asaph and is home to Snowdon, the highest mountain in Wales, and a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a charming city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor adventures, then St Asaph is the perfect destination for you. From exploring historic landmarks and hiking the hills to sampling traditional Welsh cuisine and learning about Welsh customs, there’s something for everyone in this beautiful corner of North Wales.
