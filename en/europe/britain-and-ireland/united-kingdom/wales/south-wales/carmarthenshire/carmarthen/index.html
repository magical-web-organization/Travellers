Introduction to Carmarthen

Located in Carmarthenshire, in South Wales, Carmarthen is one of the oldest towns in Wales, with a rich and fascinating history. It is also a vibrant and modern town with plenty of amenities and attractions for visitors.

Carmarthen is a great destination for those who want to experience Welsh culture, explore historic sites, enjoy outdoor activities, and try local food and drink. In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive overview of the best things to do and see in Carmarthen, as well as insider tips on where to stay, what to eat and buy, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Carmarthen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carmarthen has a rich and complex history that spans back to the Roman era. Some of the top museums and historical places to visit include:

  • Carmarthen Castle: Built in the 12th century, the ruins of this castle are a must-see for history buffs.
  • Carmarthenshire County Museum: Located in the heart of Carmarthen, this museum offers a fascinating insight into the county’s past.
  • National Botanic Garden of Wales: A place of natural beauty with lakes, gardens and glasshouses.
  • Kidwelly Castle: Explore the magnificent fortress with a history dating back to the Norman invasion, Kidwelly Castle is well worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Carmarthen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carmarthen is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best natural attractions to check out:

  • Afon Tywi: The River Tywi is the longest river in Wales, offering beautiful views and opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and walking.
  • Brechfa Forest: This vast forest is home to a variety of wildlife, including red kites and peregrine falcons, making it a great place for birdwatching and hiking.
  • Pembrey Country Park: This 202 hectare park offers beautiful sandy beaches, a dry ski slope, horse riding, cycling, a crazy golf course and extensive woodland and parkland to explore.
  • Laugharne Castle: Visitors can walk around the castle, explore the grounds, and enjoy the spectacular views over the estuary.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carmarthen

Carmarthen has a range of hiking and biking routes suitable for all levels of ability. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Millennium Coastal Path: This scenic route offers 22 miles of traffic-free cycling along the Welsh coast.
  • Brecon Beacons: One of the most famous places for hiking in Wales with magnificent views from the top of Pen y Fan, and Waun Fach and Black Mountain.
  • The Carmarthen Fans Walk: The highest point in the Black Mountain Range. Hikers find themselves at 886 meters, located on the southern end of the Fan Brycheiniog.
  • Pembrokeshire Coast Path: This 186-mile route runs along the beautiful Pembrokeshire coastline and offers stunning views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Carmarthen: Activities and Tours

Carmarthen has something to offer for everyone, from museum buffs to adrenaline junkies:

  • Try coasteering along the Pembrokeshire coastline with Pembrokeshire Adventure Centre.
  • Take part in a guided tour of Dolaucothi Gold Mines dating back to Roman Times.
  • Visit Folly Farm Wales, for an unforgettable family day out home to over 750 animals from 4 differents Continents, it also has its own Adventure Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carmarthen

Day 1: Visit Carmarthen Castle and stroll through the town centre, finishing with a traditional Welsh afternoon tea at Karm’en Kafé.

Day 2: Explore the Brecon Beacons National Park, starting by visiting the National Botanic Garden in Carmarthen.

Day 3: Take to the coast with a trip to Saundersfoot Harbour, where you can join the Pembrokeshire Coastal bus to Tenby.

Day 4: Rent a bike and cycle the Millennium Coastal Path in the morning, then explore the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park in the afternoon, visiting Pembroke Castle.

Day 5: Drive to the Dolaucothi Gold Mines in Llandeilo, have the guided tour and drive back to Carmarthen for a traditional Welsh dinner at Y Polyn.

Day 6: Spend the day with family and visit Folly Farm Wales for some great animal interactions.

Day 7: Take a guided walk on the Carmarthen Fans Walk with a qualified mountain pilot and finish up by having dinner at the Ferryman Delicatessen for a-Head-of-Seasons set menu.

Food and Drink Scene of Carmarthen

Carmarthen is a great place to sample traditional Welsh cuisine, as well as international dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cawl: A traditional Welsh stew made with lamb, leeks, carrots, and potatoes.
  • Welsh Rarebit: A hot cheese sauce on toasted bread, sometimes with added mustard or Worcestershire Sauce.
  • Cockles & Laverbread: A Welsh ‘delicacy’ of shellfish and a herbaceous seaweed paste.

Local Beverages

  • Penderyn Welsh Whiskey: Distilled in the Brecon Beacons National Park, this is a must-try for whiskey lovers.
  • Welsh Ales: Try Brains, the local ale brewery, situated in the centre of the town
  • Welsh Rum : Try Valt do Lobo, created by a Welsh Master Distiller in his quest for a Rum suitable for sipping.

Where to Stay in Carmarthen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carmarthen offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Ivy Bush Royal Hotel: A historic hotel set in the heart of Carmarthen, offers period features and a restaurant that serves traditional Welsh cuisine.
  • Rose and Crown Hotel: A family-friendly hotel situated in the coastal town of Saundersfoot.
  • YHA Llandovery Hostel: Budget-friendly hostel located in a cast iron building overlooking the surrounding mountain ranges.

What to Buy in Carmarthen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or unique gifts to bring home, here are some top picks:

  • Traditional Welsh blankets and textiles.
  • Trinkets and souvenirs from local museums and attractions.
  • Welsh-made food and drink products such as beer, whiskey, and ciders.

Local Culture and Customs of Carmarthen

Welsh culture is an important part of Carmarthen, and it is worth taking some time to learn about the unique customs and traditions. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • It’s always nice to try and learn a few Welsh phrases, such as “diolch” (thank you) and “croeso” (welcome).
  • Rugby is a popular sport in Wales, so don’t be surprised to see locals wearing Rugby shirts.
  • Music is also important in Carmarthen’s culture, and you may enjoy a visit to one of the many local music venues.

Best Time to Visit Carmarthen

Carmarthen is an all year round destination, however the summer months May - August offer the best chance for warm days and dry weather. August can see overnight stays increase due to the popular Welsh National Eisteddfod. Check events calendar for further information.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carmarthen

Do:

  • Take part in traditional Welsh activities and events, such as the Eisteddfod and attending Rugby games.
  • Try local food and drink.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region through hiking and exploring.

Don’t:

  • Stay confined to the town, try to explore the surrounding countryside and coastal areas to fully experience the beauty of Carmarthen.
  • Incorrectly pronounce Welsh place names to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carmarthen

While Carmarthen is typically a safe town for visitors, it is always important to take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe, such as avoiding dark alleys and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Carmarthen

Carmarthen has excellent medical facilities, including several hospitals and medical clinics. Additionally, there are several pharmacies and specialists who can provide medical support if needed.

Getting to Carmarthen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carmarthen is easy to reach by road, rail or air. Here are some of the most popular transportation options:

Airports Near Carmarthen

The closest airports to Carmarthen are Cardiff Airport and Bristol Airport, with scheduled flights available from most countries.

Bus Stations Near Carmarthen

There are several local bus operators in Carmarthen, such as First Cymru and National Express, which offer regular services to destinations throughout Wales and beyond.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Carmarthen is easily accessible by car, with good motorway links to nearby cities such as Swansea and Cardiff.

How to Get Around in Carmarthen

Carmarthen is a small town and easy to get around on foot, but if you need to travel further afield, there are several transportation options available. These include:

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Carmarthen and can be hailed from the street or booked in advance.
  • Buses: Local bus companies such as First Cymru and Swansea Bay offer regular services around the town and to nearby destinations.
  • Bicycle Hire: Hire bikes from CycleXperiences and explore Carmarthenshire by pedal power.

Where to Go Next from Carmarthen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Carmarthen is a great base to explore the surrounding area, and there are several nearby destinations worth visiting, including:

  • Swansea: A bustling coastal city just 30 miles from Carmarthen.
  • Brecon Beacons National Park: A beautiful park located just over an hour’s drive from Carmarthen, with plenty of opportunities for hiking and outdoor exploration.
  • Pembroke: Around 50 minutes drive from Carmarthen, with its own castle and beautiful scenery to take in.

Conclusion

Carmarthen is packed with history, culture, natural beauty and plenty of things to do when visiting. From exploring the historic castles and museums to discovering the great outdoors, getting to know the local culture and the food and drink scene, Carmarthenshire has something for everyone. Follow this travel guide to make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Wales.
