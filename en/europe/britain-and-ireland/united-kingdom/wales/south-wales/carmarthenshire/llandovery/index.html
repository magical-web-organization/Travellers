Introduction to Llandovery

Llandovery is a quaint and peaceful town located in the heart of Carmarthenshire, South Wales. Situated at the base of the Black Mountain, it offers stunning views of the Welsh countryside. The town is well-known for its rich history, making it a popular destination for visitors interested in culture, heritage, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Llandovery

Here are some must-visit attractions in and around Llandovery:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Llandovery Castle: Built in the 12th century, the ruins of Llandovery castle are an important landmark in the town. It offers a glimpse into the region’s past and provides an opportunity for visitors to learn about the castle’s rich history.

  • Myddfai Community Hall & Visitor Centre: This community hall provides a fascinating look into the medical history of the area. The community has been known for centuries for producing herbal remedies through several generations of the Physicians of Myddfai.

  • Y Gaer: Y Gaer, located on the eastern edge of the Brecon Beacons National Park, is a Roman fort dating back to around AD 75. Visitors can explore the remains of the fortifications and learn about what life was like for those living there during the Roman era.

Natural Attractions of Llandovery: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brecon Beacons National Park: Llandovery is located in the heart of the Brecon Beacons National Park, a protected area of outstanding natural beauty. The park is home to a variety of walking and hiking trails, as well as numerous outdoor activities such as horse riding, cycling, and fishing.

  • Llyn Brianne: Llyn Brianne is a stunning reservoir located north of Llandovery in the Cambrian Mountains. It offers a peaceful and remote setting, perfect for a picnic, stroll, or bike ride amid breathtaking scenery.

  • Dolaucothi Gold Mines: Dolaucothi is a fascinating ancient goldmine situated in the Cothi valley. Visitors can explore the underground tunnels, learn about ancient mining techniques, and try their hand at panning for gold!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Llandovery

Llandovery boasts breathtaking scenery and a range of walking and biking routes that cater to all abilities. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Brecon Beacons National Park: This park offers several walking trails, including the famous Pen-y-Fan, the highest peak in the area, and Waterfall Country - an area with a series of cascading waterfalls.

  • Llyn y Fan Fach: This is a moderate hiking trail that offers views of Llyn y Fan Fach, one of the most iconic mountain lakes in the Brecon Beacons.

  • Cambrian Mountains: Located in the north of Llandovery, the Cambrian Mountains offer some of the most challenging hiking and biking trails in the area, including the remote and rugged Elan Valley Trail.

Best Things to Do in Llandovery: Activities and Tours

  • Llyn Brianne Reserve: Visitors can enjoy a range of activities such as fishing, cycling, and hiking. The area is also home to a variety of wildlife, including buzzards, bats, and otters.

  • Dinefwr Estate: This is a historic attraction located in Llandeilo, just a few miles from Llandovery. The estate features an impressive 18th-century mansion, a medieval castle, and a beautiful wildlife park.

  • Llandovery Millenium Heritage Trail: This self-guided tour offers an opportunity for visitors to explore some of the local landmarks and learn about the fascinating history of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Llandovery

The food and drink scene in Llandovery is an important part of the town’s character. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A delicious blend of bread, cheese, and ale, Welsh Rarebit is a traditional dish that’s sure to warm you up on a cold day.

  • Laverbread: A famous Welsh dish made from seaweed, laverbread is often served as part of a hearty breakfast.

  • Bara Brith: A traditional Welsh teacake made from dried fruit, spice, and tea, Bara Brith is a perfect snack for a busy day of exploring.

Local Beverages

  • Penderyn Whisky: A single-malt Welsh whisky that’s become increasingly popular in recent years, Penderyn whisky is a must-try for any whisky enthusiast.

  • Brains Beer: A locally brewed beer that’s been produced in Wales for over 100 years, Brains Beer has become a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Llandovery: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Llandovery has an array of accommodation, including:

  • The Castle Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of the town, offering a range of rooms and suites.

  • The Drovers Bed and Breakfast: A cozy and welcoming bed and breakfast located just outside of Llandovery.

  • The Kings Head Inn: A traditional Welsh inn situated in the village of Llangadog, just a few miles from Llandovery.

What to Buy in Llandovery: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh woolen products: From blankets to scarves, Welsh woollen products are a popular souvenir for visitors to the area.

  • Ceramics: Llandovery is home to a number of independent ceramicists, creating unique and charming pieces that make great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Llandovery

Welsh is the principal language of the region, and many locals are bilingual. Visitors can learn basic phrases, such as “croeso” (welcome) and “diolch” (thank you) to make them feel more at home. The town’s rich history and culture are evident in its everyday life, with many local traditions still observed, including the Eisteddfod and the Mari Lwyd tradition.

Best Time to Visit Llandovery

Llandovery is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is warmer, and the scenery is at its best. However, the shoulder seasons of March to April and October to November are also a good time to visit, as crowds are fewer, and prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Llandovery

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Spend time exploring the stunning countryside

  • Try some of the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Rush your visit - take time to appreciate the town and the surrounding area

  • Leave without learning about the Welsh language and culture

  • Ignore the natural beauty of the area

Safety and Health

Safety in Llandovery

Llandovery is a relatively safe area, but visitors are advised to take the usual precautions, such as locking their doors and valuables when leaving their accommodation.

Health in Llandovery

Llandovery offers several medical facilities where visitors can seek medical attention if needed, including doctors’ surgeries, pharmacies, and hospitals. It’s also advisable to take out travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Llandovery: Transportation Options and Logistics

Llandovery is accessible via several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Llandovery

The nearest airports to Llandovery are Cardiff Airport and Bristol Airport, both of which offer domestic and international flights. From the airport, visitors can take a train or bus to Llandovery.

Bus Stations Near Llandovery

Llandovery is well connected to several towns and cities in Wales and beyond, with regular bus services operated by National Express and local providers.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Llandovery by car or bicycle, using one of the many scenic routes that connect Llandovery to nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Llandovery

The best way to get around Llandovery and the surrounding area is by car or bicycle, as the town is located in a rural and mountainous area, and public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Llandovery: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Llandovery is located in an enchanting area of South Wales, providing visitors with ample opportunity to explore the surrounding region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • The Brecon Beacons National Park: Located to the south of Llandovery, the Brecon Beacons is a fabulous area of natural beauty that’s perfect for outdoor activities like hiking and cycling.

  • The Gower Peninsula: A spectacular coastline with stunning beaches, the Gower Peninsula is a designated area of outstanding natural beauty and a must-visit destination for visitors to South Wales.

Conclusion

Llandovery is a hidden gem in South Wales, providing visitors with a unique combination of natural beauty, history, and culture. With a range of exciting activities and attractions, visitors are sure to fall in love with this charming town and the surrounding area.
