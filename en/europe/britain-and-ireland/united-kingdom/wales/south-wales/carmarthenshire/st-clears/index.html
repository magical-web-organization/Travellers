St Clears is a small town located in South Wales, in the county of Carmarthenshire. This picturesque town with its narrow streets and charming buildings is known for its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit places, exciting activities, and helpful tips to plan your trip to St Clears.

Introduction to St Clears

St Clears is a beautiful town that has retained much of its historic charm. It was originally an important market town and is still an ideal base for exploring the picturesque countryside of Carmarthenshire. The town has plenty of interesting landmarks and attractions, and is an excellent destination for those seeking an authentic Welsh experience.

List of Places to Visit in St Clears

St Clears offers a plethora of places to visit, with something for everyone. Here are some of the noteworthy places to visit in St Clears.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • St Clears Castle: The ruins of this Norman-era castle offer a glimpse into the town’s fascinating history.

  • St Clears Priory Church: This charming church is a must-visit, featuring original Norman architecture and a beautiful interior.

  • St Clears Town Walls: A unique and well-preserved defensive structure from the medieval era; perfect for history buffs!

  • St Clears Indoor Market: Discover local products and delights at this bustling indoor market.

Natural Attractions of St Clears: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Dylan Thomas Boathouse: Located in Laugharne, just north of St Clears, the famous Welsh poet’s home offers stunning views of the estuary and the Brandy Brook.

  • Llyn Llech Owain Country Park: This incredible countryside park features a tranquil lake and vast woodland trails, perfect for a peaceful stroll or bike ride.

  • Pembrokeshire Coast National Park: A stunning natural wonderland with impressive coastal views, great for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Clears

If you’re looking for adventure, St Clears has plenty to offer for hikers and bikers alike. Here are some of the best routes to hit when in town:

  • The Landsker Borderlands Trail: A 12-mile hiking trail starting in St Clears that takes you on a historical journey through Pembrokeshire

  • Brechfa Forest: A sprawling woodland area with a variety of well-marked trails perfect for both hikers and bikers.

  • Llys-y-Fran Country Park: This picturesque park has a 100-acre reservoir, well-marked trails, and a cycling path that takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the area.

Best Things to Do in St Clears: Activities and Tours

St Clears has plenty of activities to do, not just for adventure enthusiasts but for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in the town:

  • Go to Laugharne Castle: This castle in Laugharne offers stunning views and a great opportunity to explore Welsh history.

  • Visit the Picton Castle and Gardens: A beautiful 13th-century castle with picturesque gardens – perfect for a peaceful afternoon.

  • Take a Brewery Tour: St Clears has some excellent breweries, and taking a tour is a great way to learn about Welsh beer and get a taste of local flavors.

Week-Long Itinerary for St Clears

Here’s how you can make the most of your week-long trip to St Clears:

Food and Drink Scene of St Clears

St Clears has an excellent culinary scene that showcases Welsh cuisine and local delicacies.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Laverbread: This traditional Welsh dish, made from seaweed, can be found in many local restaurants.

  • Welsh Cakes: A sweet and buttery traditional Welsh bake that’s perfect for a snack or breakfast.

  • Bara Brith: A moist cake made with dried fruits and spices, often served with a creamy topping.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Ale: Wales has a rich tradition of brewing excellent beers and ales with local flavors.

  • Penderyn Whiskey: This award-winning Welsh whiskey is a must-try.

  • Brains Beer: A well-known beer from Wales that is widely available in St Clears and the surrounding areas.

Where to Stay in St Clears: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St Clears has an excellent range of accommodations, from luxurious hotels to charming guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Three Rivers Hotel: This family-run hotel offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside and has a beautiful outdoor pool.

  • The Plume of Feathers: A charming and historic inn that offers comfortable rooms and traditional Welsh hospitality.

  • Fronwen: A beautiful, traditional farmhouse that’s been converted into a cozy guesthouse.

What to Buy in St Clears: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to St Clears:

  • Welsh blankets: A traditional Welsh craft, woolen blankets are a popular souvenir from St Clears.

  • Welsh Lovespoons: These hand-carved wooden spoons are a symbol of love and make excellent gifts.

  • Ty Gwyn Cider: This local cider is made from locally grown apples and is widely available in St Clears.

Local Culture and Customs of St Clears

Welsh culture is unique and fascinating, and St Clears is no exception.

  • Welsh Language: While English is widely spoken in Wales, Welsh remains an essential part of the country’s cultural heritage.

  • Music and Literature: Wales has a rich tradition in music and literature, with many famous Welsh poets, writers, and musicians.

  • Eisteddfod: This is a traditional Welsh festival of literature, music, and performance, held annually in different parts of Wales.

Best Time to Visit St Clears

St Clears is an all-year-round destination, but for the best experience, it’s best to visit in the spring or summer when the weather is mild, and the countryside is in full bloom.

Getting to St Clears: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Clears is located 17 miles west of Carmarthen and is easily accessible by various modes of transport.

  • Airports Near St Clears: The nearest airport is Cardiff International Airport, located about 70 miles east of the town.

  • Bus Stations Near St Clears: Carmarthen Bus Station is the nearest bus station, located approximately 17 miles east of the town.

  • Other Options: You can also take a train to Carmarthen and then catch a bus to St Clears or rent a car and drive to the town.

How to Get Around in St Clears

St Clears is small enough to explore on foot, and there are also buses and taxis available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from St Clears: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ceredigion: This charming Welsh county offers beautiful coastal towns, stunning natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

St Clears offers something for everyone, whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or culture seeker. Plan your trip, make the most of your stay, and fall in love with the charm of this stunning Welsh town.
