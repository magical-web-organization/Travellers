Introduction to St Davids

Located in Pembrokeshire, in South Wales, St Davids is a picturesque city that is known for its stunning scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture. From awe-inspiring natural attractions to fascinating museums and historical sites, and from exciting outdoor adventures to guided tours, St Davids has something for everyone.

Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in the local culture, explore the great outdoors, or simply relax and unwind, St Davids has it all. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in St Davids, as well as provide some useful tips and insights for traveling to this beautiful part of Wales.

List of Places to Visit in St Davids

To help you make the most of your trip to St Davids, we’ve put together a list of our top picks for places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St Davids Cathedral: A stunning example of medieval architecture, St Davids Cathedral is a must-visit for anyone interested in history or architecture. Built in the 12th century, this magnificent cathedral is home to a wealth of treasures, including the shrine of St David, the patron saint of Wales.

  2. St Davids Bishop’s Palace: Once home to the powerful medieval bishops of St Davids, this magnificent palace is now in ruins, but it still offers a fascinating insight into Wales’ rich history. Visitors can explore the remains of the palace, which include the great hall, the chapel, and the bishop’s private quarters.

  3. Oriel y Parc Gallery and Visitor Centre: This award-winning gallery and visitor centre showcases the work of local artists and the natural beauty of the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park. Visitors can enjoy a range of temporary exhibitions, as well as the permanent collections of works by Graham Sutherland and other modern artists.

Natural Attractions of St Davids: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Whitesands Bay: Arguably the most beautiful beach in Wales, Whitesands Bay is a popular destination for surfers, swimmers, and sunbathers alike. With its crystal-clear waters and golden sands, this stunning bay is the perfect place to spend a lazy afternoon.

  2. St Davids Head: A rugged headland that juts out into the Atlantic Ocean, St Davids Head is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers. From the top of the headland, visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the coastline and the surrounding countryside, as well as spot a variety of seabirds and other wildlife.

  3. Ramsey Island: Located just off the coast of St Davids, Ramsey Island is a nature reserve that is home to a variety of rare and endangered species, including grey seals, choughs, and peregrine falcons. Visitors can take a boat tour of the island, or explore the walking trails that wind their way through its rugged terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Davids

St Davids is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a wide variety of hiking and biking routes to choose from.

Hiking Routes

  1. The Pembrokeshire Coast Path: One of the most spectacular long-distance walking trails in the UK, the Pembrokeshire Coast Path runs for 186 miles along the stunning coastline of Pembrokeshire. From St Davids, visitors can access the path and enjoy breathtaking views of the sea and the rugged cliffs.

  2. St Davids Head Walk: This circular walking route takes visitors around the rugged headland of St Davids Head, offering spectacular views of the coastline and the surrounding countryside.

Biking Routes

  1. Lôn Teifi: This popular cycling route follows the path of the River Teifi from Cardigan to Lampeter, passing through some of the most scenic countryside in Wales.

  2. The Preseli Hills: With their rolling hills and rugged terrain, the Preseli Hills offer a challenging and rewarding cycling experience. There are a variety of routes to choose from, ranging from easy circular routes to more challenging long-distance trails.

Best Things to Do in St Davids: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in St Davids, whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing day out.

Activities

  1. Surfing at Whitesands Bay: With its consistent waves and sandy beach, Whitesands Bay is the perfect destination for surfers of all levels. Equipment hire and surf lessons are available locally, making it easy to get started.

  2. Kayaking at Ramsey Island: Take a guided kayak tour of Ramsey Island and explore its rugged coastline and hidden coves. Along the way, you may spot seals, dolphins, and a variety of seabirds.

Tours

  1. Historic St Davids Tour: Take a guided walking tour of St Davids and discover the city’s rich history and culture. Visit the cathedral, the bishop’s palace, and other historic sites, and learn about the city’s role in Welsh history.

  2. Boat Tour to Ramsey Island: Take a boat tour of Ramsey Island and explore its rugged terrain and unspoiled beauty. Along the way, you may spot grey seals, choughs, and other wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for St Davids

Day 1: Arrive in St Davids and explore the city’s historic centre, including the cathedral and the bishop’s palace.

Day 2: Take a guided walking tour of St Davids and learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of Ramsey Island and explore its rugged terrain and unspoiled beauty.

Day 4: Spend the day hiking along the Pembrokeshire Coast Path, with lunch at Whitesands Bay.

Day 5: Take a kayaking tour of Ramsey Island and explore its hidden coves and rugged coastline.

Day 6: Visit Oriel y Parc Gallery and Visitor Centre, and enjoy a leisurely afternoon exploring the exhibitions and the surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Explore the Preseli Hills by bike, taking in the stunning scenery and rugged terrain.

Food and Drink Scene of St Davids

St Davids is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for those looking to sample local cuisine and experience the best of Welsh hospitality.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Welsh Rarebit: A classic Welsh dish, Welsh Rarebit is a rich, cheesy sauce that is typically served over toast.

  2. Laverbread: Made from seaweed, Laverbread is a popular Welsh delicacy that is often served as a side dish with breakfast.

Local Beverages

  1. Pembrokeshire Ale: Brewed at the Bluestone Brewing Company in nearby Cilgwyn, Pembrokeshire Ale is a traditional Welsh beer that is known for its rich, full-bodied flavour.

  2. Welsh Cider: Made from locally grown apples, Welsh Cider is a refreshing and sweet beverage that is perfect for a summer afternoon.

Where to Stay in St Davids: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in St Davids, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel, a budget-friendly hostel, or a charming guesthouse.

Hotels

  1. The Twr Y Felin Hotel: A luxury hotel that is housed in a restored windmill, the Twr Y Felin offers stunning views of St Davids Bay and the surrounding countryside.

  2. The City Inn: Located in the heart of St Davids, the City Inn is a comfortable and affordable option for those looking to stay in the city centre.

Hostels

  1. St Davids Bunk Barns: A budget-friendly option for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers, St Davids Bunk Barns offers comfortable and clean accommodation in a picturesque setting.

  2. YHA St Davids: Another great option for budget travelers, the YHA St Davids offers clean and comfortable dorm rooms, as well as private rooms and self-catering facilities.

Guesthouses

  1. Warpool Court Hotel: A charming guesthouse that is set in beautiful gardens, the Warpool Court Hotel offers comfortable and welcoming accommodation in a tranquil setting.

  2. Cromwell’s Inn: A traditional Welsh pub that also offers comfortable guest accommodation, Cromwell’s Inn is a popular choice for those looking to experience the best of Welsh hospitality.

What to Buy in St Davids: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from in St Davids, whether you’re looking for a traditional Welsh gift or something more unique.

Souvenirs

  1. Welsh Blankets: Made from locally sourced wool, Welsh blankets are a classic souvenir that is both practical and beautiful.

  2. Welsh Love Spoons: A traditional Welsh gift, Welsh Love Spoons are hand-carved from wood and are often given as a symbol of love and affection.

Local Products

  1. Pembrokeshire Cheese: Made from locally sourced milk, Pembrokeshire Cheese is a delicious and unique product that is perfect for cheese lovers.

  2. Welsh Whiskey: Produced at the Penderyn Distillery in the Brecon Beacons, Welsh Whiskey is a smooth and complex spirit that is a must-try for whiskey enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of St Davids

Welsh culture is steeped in tradition and history, and St Davids is no exception. Visitors to St Davids can immerse themselves in the local culture by visiting museums and historic sites, attending local festivals and events, and trying traditional Welsh cuisine.

Best Time to Visit St Davids

St Davids is a great destination to visit year-round, with each season offering its own unique beauty and charm. The summer months (June to August) are the busiest time of year, with plenty of outdoor events and activities on offer, while the winter months (December to February) are quieter but still offer plenty of cosy pubs and restaurants to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Davids

Do:

  1. Do take time to explore the natural beauty of the area, including the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park and St Davids Head.
  2. Do try traditional Welsh food and drink, such as Welsh Rarebit and Pembrokeshire Ale.
  3. Do respect the local culture and customs, including the language (Welsh) and the traditions of St Davids Cathedral and other historic sites.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural environment, and be sure to follow the countryside code when hiking or cycling.
  2. Don’t mispronounce or misrepresent Welsh names or words, and be sure to use the correct pronunciation and spelling of local place names and landmarks.
  3. Don’t ignore the importance of St Davids Cathedral and other historic sites to the local community, and be sure to show respect and reverence when visiting these places.

Safety and Health

St Davids is a safe and welcoming destination, with a low crime rate and clean and well-maintained facilities. However, visitors should still take basic precautions and follow common-sense safety measures, such as locking doors and windows, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of their surroundings when out and about.

Safety in St Davids

  1. Be aware of the tide times when visiting the local beaches, and be sure to follow any safety advice or warnings that are posted.

  2. When walking or hiking, be sure to wear appropriate footwear and clothing, and carry a map and compass if venturing into less-visited areas.

Health in St Davids

  1. St Davids has a well-equipped medical centre with experienced doctors and nurses.
  2. Visitors should be aware of the potential hazards of swimming and water sports, and should take appropriate precautions to avoid injury or illness.

Getting to St Davids: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Davids is located in West Wales, and is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options.

Airports Near St Davids

  1. Cardiff Airport: Approximately 2 hours and 30 minutes drive from St Davids.

  2. Bristol Airport: Approximately 2 hours and 45 minutes drive from St Davids.

Bus Stations Near St Davids

  1. Haverfordwest Bus Station: Approximately 20 minutes drive from St Davids.

  2. Fishguard Bus Station: Approximately 30 minutes drive from St Davids.

Other Options

  1. Train: The nearest train station is at Haverfordwest, approximately 20 minutes drive from St Davids.

  2. Car: St Davids is easily accessible by car, with good road links to the rest of Wales and the UK.

How to Get Around in St Davids

St Davids is a small and compact city that is easy to navigate on foot. For those who prefer to travel by car, there are plenty of car hire options available locally.

Where to Go Next from St Davids: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that are within easy reach of St Davids, including:

  1. Cardigan: A historic market town located on the banks of the River Teifi, Cardigan is just 30 minutes drive from St Davids and offers a range of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.

  2. Tenby: A charming seaside town that is famous for its colourful houses and golden sandy beaches, Tenby is just 45 minutes drive from St Davids and is a popular destination for families and couples.

Conclusion

St Davids is a beautiful and welcoming destination that offers something for everyone, whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing break. With its stunning scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture, St Davids is an ideal destination for anyone looking to explore the best of Wales. So why not start planning your trip today and discover all that this wonderful city has to offer!
