Introduction to Newgale

Newgale is a small coastal village located in Pembrokeshire, South Wales, renowned for its stunning sandy beach that stretches for two miles along the picturesque coastline. The village is a popular holiday destination for tourists looking to enjoy the natural beauty this part of Wales has to offer, with excellent walking and cycling routes, water sports, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will share with you the best things to do and see in Newgale, including the top attractions, activities, local cuisine, accommodation options, and transport logistics.

List of Places to Visit in Newgale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St David’s Cathedral

Located just a short distance from Newgale, St David’s Cathedral is a magnificent medieval building and one of Wales’ most important religious sites. The Cathedral dates back to the 12th century and features stunning gothic architecture that is sure to leave visitors in awe. St David’s Cathedral holds regular services and is open to visitors all year round, with guided tours available.

Pembroke Castle

Another must-visit cultural attraction is Pembroke Castle, which is just a 30-minute drive from Newgale. This magnificent castle boasts over 900 years of history and has been the birthplace of famous figures such as Henry VII. Visitors can explore the castle’s vast interior and immerse themselves in the rich history of Wales.

Natural Attractions of Newgale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Pembrokeshire Coast Path

The Pembrokeshire Coast Path is a world-famous walking route that stretches more than 186 miles around the Welsh coast, passing through some of the country’s most picturesque landscapes. Newgale is situated along the route, and hikers can enjoy the stunning views of St Brides Bay and the surrounding coastline. The walk will also lead visitors to hidden coves and pristine beaches, offering an unforgettable experience of the Welsh countryside.

Newgale Beach

The lovely sandy beach of Newgale is a popular destination for tourists looking to soak up the sun or take part in water-based activities such as surfing and kiteboarding. The beach is known for its long coastline and spectacular views of the Welsh coast, making it the perfect spot for relaxing and enjoying the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newgale

The Coast Path Walk

The Coast Path Walk is the most popular hiking trail in the area, offering breathtaking views of the Welsh coastline and the rugged cliffs that run along the coastline. The trail is challenging, but not overly so, and is ideal for those who enjoy hiking in natural surroundings. It takes approximately two hours to complete the walk, and it is recommended to walk during the summer months.

The Celtic Trail Bike Ride

For those who prefer to explore the region’s natural beauty atop a bicycle, the Celtic Trail Bike Ride is an excellent option. The trail is well-maintained and runs along the Welsh coast, providing bikers with stunning views of the sea as well as the surrounding countryside. It is a challenging but rewarding route that is well-suited to intermediate cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Newgale: Activities and Tours

Surfing

Newgale Beach is a fantastic location for surfing, with a long and wide beach making it perfect for beginners and experienced surfers alike. The coast’s waves are consistent and not too powerful, meaning that surfers can enjoy a great session without putting themselves in danger.

Coasteering

Coasteering is an excellent way to see the Welsh coastline and explore the cliffs and rocks that are characteristic of the area. This activity involves moving along the coastline, swimming, jumping, and climbing along the rocky cliffs. It is suitable for anyone who is comfortable in the water and is physically fit.

Food and Drink Scene of Newgale

Must-Try Dishes

Wales is famous for its cuisine, and visitors to Newgale should be sure to try some of the local dishes, such as Welsh rarebit, cawl, laverbread, and Bara Brith - a delicious fruitcake made with tea.

Local Beverages

Wales is home to a growing craft beer scene, and visitors to Newgale can sample some of the local beers from breweries such as Tinworks and Bluestone Brewing Company. Welsh cider is also another great option, with producers like Gwynt Y Ddraig producing high-quality ciders with locally-sourced apples.

Where to Stay in Newgale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

YHA Newgale

Located just minutes away from the beach, YHA Newgale is a budget-friendly option for visitors looking to save money on their accommodation. The hostel offers private or shared rooms, a self-catering kitchen, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Warpool Court Hotel

For those looking for a more luxurious stay, the Warpool Court Hotel is an excellent option. This four-star hotel features comfortable rooms, stunning gardens, and a restaurant serving locally-sourced cuisine.

What to Buy in Newgale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Welsh Blankets

Visitors to Newgale can purchase traditional Welsh blankets, made from local wool, and take home a piece of Welsh culture to remember their trip.

Welsh Slate

Another popular souvenir is Welsh slate, which is renowned for its quality and is a durable material that is perfect for creating beautiful, long-lasting products.

Local Culture and Customs of Newgale

Welsh culture is rich and varied, with a strong tradition of music, literature, and art. Visitors to Newgale can immerse themselves in local customs by attending traditional Welsh music sessions, exploring the history and art of the region, and visiting local galleries and museums.

Best Time to Visit Newgale

The best time to visit Newgale is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is typically warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy long days of outdoor activities and take advantage of the beautiful beaches and natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newgale

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and keep the area clean and tidy.
  • Take care when swimming and surfing in the sea, as currents can be strong.
  • Try and learn a few words of Welsh, as this is a culturally important language in the region.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in cars.
  • Ignore warning signs about dangerous areas along the coastline.
  • Ignore the local customs and laws when out in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newgale

Newgale is a relatively safe area, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with water-based activities and take appropriate safety precautions.

Health in Newgale

Visitors to Newgale should be up to date with their routine vaccinations and consider vaccinations against certain endemic diseases, such as hepatitis A and B. Visitors should also take appropriate measures to protect themselves against the sun and avoid dehydration during the warm summer months.

Getting to Newgale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newgale

The nearest airport to Newgale is Cardiff International Airport, which is approximately 98 miles away. Alternatively, visitors can also fly into Bristol Airport, which is around 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Newgale

There is a regular bus service that runs from Haverfordwest to Newgale. Visitors can also take the Pembrokeshire Coastline bus, which travels along the Welsh coastline and stops at all the major towns and attractions along the way.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Newgale by car or train. The train station closest to Newgale is Haverfordwest, which offers regular services from major UK cities such as London and Manchester.

How to Get Around in Newgale

The best way to get around Newgale is on foot or by bicycle, as the area is relatively small and easy to navigate. Visitors can also use local buses or taxis to get around if needed.

Where to Go Next from Newgale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St David’s

Located just 30 minutes from Newgale, St David’s is a historic and charming town that is home to the stunning St David’s Cathedral. Visitors can explore the town’s shops, restaurants, and galleries, or take a boat trip to nearby Ramsey Island.

Tenby

Tenby is a picturesque seaside town located around an hour’s drive from Newgale. The town’s medieval walls and cobbled streets offer visitors a glimpse into the area’s rich history, while its golden beaches and crystal-clear waters provide the perfect setting for a relaxing vacation in the sun.

Conclusion

Newgale is a remarkable destination for those looking to immerse themselves in Welsh culture and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
