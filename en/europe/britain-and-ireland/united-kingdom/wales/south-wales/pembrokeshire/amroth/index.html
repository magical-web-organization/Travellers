Introduction to Amroth

Located in Pembrokeshire, South Wales, Amroth is a charming seaside village that offers visitors a taste of history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Its picturesque beachfront and stunning views make it a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic Welsh experience.

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or an action-packed holiday, Amroth has something to offer for every kind of traveler. In this travel guide, we have compiled the best things to do and see in Amroth, including the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, local cuisine, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Amroth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amroth Castle: Built in the 19th century, Amroth Castle is a Grade II listed building that has been beautifully restored to its former glory. It now serves as a luxury holiday resort, but visitors are welcome to explore the grounds and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The National Trust Colby Woodland Garden: A beautiful natural woodland garden that spans over 150 acres, offering visitors a peaceful refuge and a chance to connect with nature. With over 800 species of flowering plants, the garden is a haven for nature lovers and a great spot for picnics.

  • Pendine Sands: A long, flat beach that was once used as a popular location for speed trials in the early 20th century. Now a popular location for family days out, the beach offers a range of activities including kite surfing, paddle-boarding, and kayaking.

Natural Attractions of Amroth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coastal Path Walk: The Pembrokeshire Coast Path stretches for 186 miles and offers breathtaking views of the coastline, wildlife, and historical sites. There are several beautiful stretches of the trail that run through Amroth and nearby villages, making it a perfect spot for hikers and walkers.

  • Amroth Beach: With its wide sandy beach and shallow waters, Amroth Beach is a popular spot for families and surfers. There are plenty of activities available, including rock pooling, surfing, fishing, and swimming.

  • Wiseman’s Bridge Beach: A quiet stretch of sand that is ideal for peace and quiet. Nature lovers will enjoy exploring the nearby caves and rock pools.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amroth

With its stunning coastal location, Amroth is a fantastic destination for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. The Pembrokeshire Coast Path offers miles of breathtaking scenery, while country lanes and back roads present an opportunity to explore the rolling Welsh countryside.

  • The Amroth Walk: A gentle circular walk that takes in the village of Amroth, the National Trust’s Colby Woodland Garden, and Wiseman’s Bridge Beach.

  • The Cresselly Arms Walk: A more challenging walk that takes in the lovely Cresselly Arms pub in Cresswell Quay. Walk along the estuary before reaching the pub, where you can stop for a pint and a bite to eat before continuing on your way.

  • The Newgale Walk: A longer, more challenging route that takes in the stunning Newgale Beach, the picturesque tiny village of Solva, and the historic city of St Davids.

Best Things to Do in Amroth: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: With its long, wide beach and consistent waves, Amroth is a great spot for surfing. There are plenty of surf schools in the area that offer lessons for all levels.

  • Boat Trips: Take to the water and explore the coastline from a different perspective. There are a variety of boat trips available, including dolphin and seal watching tours, fishing trips, and kayaking adventures.

  • Visit the Local Village: Take a stroll through the charming village of Amroth and explore its quaint shops, cafes, and pubs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amroth

  • Day 1: Visit the bustling town of Tenby, with its colourful houses, bustling harbour, and historic walls.

  • Day 2: Explore the Pembrokeshire Coast Path and take in the stunning views of the coastline.

  • Day 3: Visit the Cresselly Arms pub and enjoy the surrounding countryside on the Cresselly Arms Walk.

  • Day 4: Take a boat trip and go dolphin or seal watching.

  • Day 5: Visit the picturesque village of Solva and explore its winding streets and quaint harbour.

  • Day 6: Take a surf lesson or rent a board and hit the waves.

  • Day 7: Relax on the stunning Amroth Beach or explore the local area on the Newgale Walk.

Food and Drink Scene of Amroth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A delicious grilled cheese sandwich, topped with a savoury sauce made with ale, mustard, and Worcestershire sauce.

  • Cawl: A hearty stew made with meat and vegetables, and traditionally served with Welsh bread and cheese.

  • Bara Brith: A traditional Welsh fruit cake that is usually served with a generous slathering of Welsh butter.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Ale: Try a locally brewed ale, such as Brains or Felinfoel, at one of the many pubs in the area.

  • Welsh Whiskey: Wales has a long history of whiskey production, and there are several distilleries in the area, including Penderyn and Dà Mhìle.

Where to Stay in Amroth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Amroth Bay Holidays Park: A popular holiday park that offers a range of accommodation options, including lodges, luxury caravans, and glamping pods.

  • The Amroth Castle: A beautifully restored castle that now serves as a luxury holiday resort, with apartments and cottages available for rent.

  • The Old Post Office Bed and Breakfast: A charming, family-run B&B that offers comfortable, stylishly decorated rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Amroth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Welsh Wool: Wales has a long history of wool production, and there are several locally owned woolen mills in the area, offering high-quality woolen products.

  • Welsh Slate: Another material that has been mined and used in Wales for centuries, Welsh slate is known for its durability and is used for everything from roof tiles to kitchen countertops.

  • Welsh Love Spoons: A traditional Welsh carving that was once given by suitors to their intended brides as a symbol of their love and affection.

Local Culture and Customs of Amroth

Welsh culture is rich and vibrant, with a long history of music, literature, and art. Traditional Welsh customs include singing, dancing, and feasting, and there are several festivals and events held throughout the year to celebrate Welsh heritage.

Best Time to Visit Amroth

The best time to visit Amroth is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are busy with families and tourists. However, the area is also beautiful during the spring and autumn, when the countryside is ablaze with colour and the coastal paths are quieter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amroth

Do:

  • Explore the coastline: The Pembrokeshire Coast Path offers some of the most breathtaking views in Wales.

  • Try the local cuisine: Sample some of the delicious traditional Welsh dishes, such as cawl and Welsh rarebit.

  • Respect nature: The local flora and fauna are unique and fragile, so be sure to leave them undisturbed.

Don’t:

  • Leave rubbish: Keep the beaches and countryside clean and tidy by taking your rubbish with you.

  • Disturb wildlife: The area is home to a variety of rare and endangered species, including birds and sea mammals, so do not disturb them.

  • Forget to book ahead: Accommodation in the area can be limited, particularly during peak season, so be sure to book ahead.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amroth

Amroth is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low levels of crime and a friendly, welcoming community. However, as with any location, it is important to take sensible precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding quiet, isolated areas at night.

Health in Amroth

Healthcare services in the area are generally of a high standard, with several hospitals and clinics available in nearby towns and cities. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before visiting Amroth.

Getting to Amroth: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Cardiff Airport: 97 miles, 2 hours

  • Bristol Airport: 130 miles, 2.5 hours

Bus Stations Near Amroth

  • Tenby Bus Station: 5.6 miles, 20 minutes

  • Haverfordwest Bus Station: 15.7 miles, 30 minutes

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Tenby, with regular connections to major cities throughout the UK.

  • Car: Amroth is easily accessible by car, with major roads connecting it to nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Amroth

The best way to explore Amroth and the surrounding area is by car, as public transport can be limited. There are also several taxi services available, and bicycle hire is available from the local shops.

Where to Go Next from Amroth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tenby: A charming seaside town with colourful houses, a bustling harbour, and historic walls.

  • St Davids: A beautiful city with a rich history, stunning cathedral, and plenty of shops and cafes.

  • Pembrokeshire National Park: A stunning area of natural beauty that is home to a range of wildlife and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Amroth is a beautiful, charming seaside village that offers visitors a taste of Welsh history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning coastal location, charming local businesses, and broad range of activities, it is a perfect destination for visitors of all ages and interests.
