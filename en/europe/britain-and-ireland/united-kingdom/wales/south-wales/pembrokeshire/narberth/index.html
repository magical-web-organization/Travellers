Introduction to Narberth

Located in the heart of Pembrokeshire, Narberth is a charming town rich in culture and history. Visitors can explore the town’s independent shops, galleries, and cafes, as well as the various museums and historical sites that showcase the area’s past.

Narberth is also surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, including the Preseli Hills and the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring ancient ruins, or simply enjoying breathtaking views, Narberth has something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Narberth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Narberth Museum: Discover the town’s rich history and culture through a variety of exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Oakwood Theme Park: Located just outside Narberth, this family-friendly park offers a range of rides and attractions for all ages.
  • Llys y Fran Country Park: Enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside, as well as a range of outdoor activities including hiking, fishing, and boating.
  • Pentre Ifan: Explore one of the most impressive prehistoric sites in Wales, including the famous megalithic dolmen.

Natural Attractions of Narberth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Preseli Hills: Hike, bike, or horse ride through beautiful landscapes and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Pembrokeshire Coast National Park: Perfect for nature lovers, this park offers miles of rugged coastline, sandy beaches, and lively wildlife.
  • Skomer Island: Take a boat trip to this stunning island, home to a variety of bird species and colorful wildflowers.
  • Carew Castle and Tidal Mill: Explore this imposing medieval castle and learn about the area’s fascinating history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narberth

Narberth is a great place for those interested in hiking or biking. The following are some of the best routes to explore around town:

  • The Landsker Borderlands Trail: This 21 mile trail passes through stunning landscapes and connects the towns of Narberth and Amroth.
  • The Pembrokeshire Coast Path: Stretching over 180 miles along the Welsh coastline, this path offers breathtaking views and plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting.
  • The Preseli Hills: Hike along ancient moorland, meander through wooded valleys, and take in panoramic views from the top of the hills.

Best Things to Do in Narberth: Activities and Tours

  • Food and craft market: Held every Friday, this bustling market offers delicious local produce as well as handmade gifts and souvenirs.
  • Narberth Book Fair: Held annually in September, this event celebrates local authors and showcases a wide range of books from the area.
  • Art and Craft Trail: Explore galleries and workshops throughout the town and discover local arts and crafts.
  • Celtic Quest Coasteering: Get your adrenaline pumping by climbing, jumping, swimming, and exploring the Welsh coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Narberth

Day 1: Explore Narberth town center and visit the Narberth Museum. Day 2: Hike the Landsker Borderlands Trail or explore the Preseli Hills. Day 3: Visit the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park and take a boat trip to Skomer Island. Day 4: Experience local history by visiting Carew Castle and Tidal Mill. Day 5: Enjoy a day of activities such as coasterring, paddleboarding, or kayaking. Day 6: Discover local arts and crafts on the Art and Craft Trail or attend the Narberth Book Fair (depending on the season). Day 7: Relax and unwind with a leisurely walk around the town and a meal at one of the many local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Narberth

Narberth is known for its farm-to-table cuisine and its many independent cafes and restaurants. Don’t miss out on trying these local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Welsh Rarebit: A traditional dish made of melted cheese, bread, and spices.
  • Laverbread: A delicacy made from seaweed, often served with bacon for breakfast.
  • Bara Brith: A fruit bread that’s perfect with a cup of tea or coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Cwrw: Welsh for “beer”, there are several locally brewed beers to try in Narberth, including Bluestone Brewing Company and Caffle Brewery.
  • Penderyn Whisky: A unique Welsh whisky distilled in the Brecon Beacons National Park.

Where to Stay in Narberth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several great options for accommodations in Narberth, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels. These are some of the best options:

  • The Grove of Narberth: A luxury hotel located in a beautiful mansion, surrounded by stunning gardens.
  • Folly Farm Camping: A family-friendly camping site located just outside of Narberth and with lots of activities for kids.
  • Lawrenny Arms: A traditional pub and hotel offering comfortable accommodations and great food.

What to Buy in Narberth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Narberth without buying these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Welsh wool blankets: Keep warm with a cozy wool blanket made from local sheep.
  • Leather goods: Narberth is home to several leather workshops that produce high-quality bags, gloves, and belts.
  • Local honey: Welsh honey is delicious and can be found at many of the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Narberth

The Welsh have a rich cultural heritage, and Narberth is the perfect place to experience it. Be sure to try some of the local dishes, such as Welsh Rarebit and Laverbread, and learn a few words of Welsh (the official language of Wales). The people of Narberth are friendly and welcoming, and you’re sure to feel at home in this charming town.

Best Time to Visit Narberth

The best time to visit Narberth is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with visitors and events. However, Narberth is a great destination year-round, with plenty to see and do even in the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narberth

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s independent shops and galleries.
  • Do try some local dishes and beverages.
  • Do take a walk through the Preseli Hills for stunning views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be afraid to try your hand at some outdoor activities like coasteering.
  • Don’t forget to pack for the Welsh weather, as it can change quickly.
  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and language.

Safety and Health

Safety in Narberth

Narberth is a safe and welcoming town. However, visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings when out exploring the surrounding countryside.

Health in Narberth

Medical services are available locally, with several pharmacies and a hospital located nearby in Haverfordwest. Visitors should also be aware of ticks when hiking in the countryside.

Getting to Narberth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Narberth

The nearest airport to Narberth is Cardiff Airport, located approximately 90 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Narberth

There are a number of bus services that operate in and around Narberth, with connections to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

For those driving to Narberth, the town is easily accessible via the M4 and A40.

How to Get Around in Narberth

Narberth is a small town that is easily explored on foot. However, for those looking to venture further afield, there are several transportation options available, including bike hire and taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Narberth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Narberth is a great base from which to explore the beautiful countryside and coastline of Pembrokeshire. Some nearby destinations worth visiting include:

  • St. Davids: The UK’s smallest city is just a short drive away and home to a stunning cathedral.
  • Tenby: A quaint seaside town with a range of attractions and activities for all ages.
  • Pembroke Castle: A medieval castle located just a short drive from Narberth.

Conclusion

Narberth is a charming town with plenty of things to see and do. From its rich cultural history to its stunning natural landscapes, Narberth is a great destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Wales. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring the countryside, or simply relaxing and enjoying some great food and drink, Narberth has something to offer.
