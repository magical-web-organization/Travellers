Introduction to Skomer

Skomer is a beautiful island located off the coast of Pembrokeshire in South Wales, United Kingdom. The island is known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich wildlife, historical sites, and old traditions. If you are planning to visit Skomer, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most out of your experience.

List of Places to Visit in Skomer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most interesting places to visit on Skomer is St. Mary’s Chapel, which was built in the 13th century and is one of the oldest buildings on the island. The chapel has an interesting history, and visitors can learn about it as they explore the ruins. The Skomer Island Visitor Centre is also worth visiting, as it has a range of exhibits that give an insight into the island’s long history.

Natural Attractions of Skomer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Visitors to Skomer can look forward to enjoying the island’s stunning natural landscapes and rich wildlife. The Skomer Island Nature Reserve is home to a wide variety of animals, including puffins, seals, dolphins, and porpoises. Visitors can take guided tours to view these animals and learn about their habitats and behaviors.

For those looking for a bit of adventure, Skomer Island has several activities to offer. Visitors can explore the island’s coastline through sea kayaking tours, take part in snorkeling or scuba diving expeditions, or go on hiking or biking routes spread out across the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skomer

Skomer Island offers a variety of trails for hikers and bikers to enjoy. One of the most popular routes is the Skomer Circular Walk, which takes visitors through the island’s cliffs, sandy beaches, stunning viewpoints, and the Skomer lighthouse. Nature lovers can also take the Wildlife Walk, which offers a chance to spot the island’s native animal and bird species.

Best Things to Do in Skomer: Activities and Tours

One of the best things to do in Skomer is to take a guided tour of the Skomer Island Nature Reserve. This gives visitors an opportunity to observe the island’s rich wildlife and learn from knowledgeable guides. Visitors can also take part in a variety of activities such as sea kayaking, snorkeling, or scuba diving for an adventurous experience. Additionally, visitors can take leisurely strolls on the island’s scenic trails or relax on the beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skomer

Day 1: Arrive in Skomer, check-in to your accommodation, and explore the island’s landscapes and wildlife through leisurely strolls. Day 2-3: Take a guided tour of the Skomer Island Nature Reserve and learn from knowledgeable guides. Day 4: Enjoy adventurous activities like sea kayaking, snorkeling, or scuba diving. Day 5-6: Explore hiking/biking routes like the Skomer Circular Walk and Wildlife Walk . Day 7: Relax on the beach and enjoy the island’s local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Skomer

Must-Try Dishes

Skomer Island is known for producing some of the finest seafood in Wales. Visitors should try the island’s famous lobster and crab, or sample traditional Welsh dishes like cawl or Welsh rarebit.

Local Beverages

The most famous local beverage is Welsh beer, which is widely available and brewed locally. Additionally, visitors should try the island’s traditional mead, which is made from honey and is known for its unique taste and aroma.

Where to Stay in Skomer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can stay on Skomer Island at one of the accommodations available. The island offers a variety of options including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to suit different budget levels.

What to Buy in Skomer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors should not leave Skomer without purchasing souvenirs or taking home souvenirs. Visitors can buy products like locally produced jam, honey, beer, or other handicrafts made by local artisans. Most souvenirs are available at the Skomer Island Visitor Centre.

Local Culture and Customs of Skomer

Skomer Island has its own unique local culture and customs, which visitors can learn about during their visit. The island’s most famous tradition is the springtime puffin festival. Visitors can also learn about the island’s history and cultural traditions at the Skomer Island Visitor Centre.

Best Time to Visit Skomer

The best time to visit Skomer Island is between May and August when the weather is at its best and visitors have a chance to observe the island’s rich wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skomer

Do:

  • Respect the natural habitats of animals and birds
  • Follow the island’s visitor rules and regulations
  • Carry packed food and drinking water during outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Disturb the peace and tranquility of the island’s habitat
  • Leave waste on the island’s trails or beaches
  • Cause damage to the historical sites and ruins

Safety and Health

Safety in Skomer

Skomer Island is a safe place to visit as long as visitors follow the island’s rules and guidelines. Visitors should be careful while engaging in adventurous activities and take the necessary precautions.

Health in Skomer

Visitors to Skomer Island should be aware of any allergies or health conditions they have and carry any medications or required first aid supplies with them.

Getting to Skomer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Skomer

The nearest major airport to Skomer Island is Cardiff International Airport, which is about 2 hours by car.

Bus Stations Near Skomer

Haverfordwest Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Skomer, where visitors can catch a local bus to Martins Haven which is the closest point of departure to Skomer.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Skomer Island via a ferry service available from Martins Haven.

How to Get Around in Skomer

Visitors to Skomer Island can explore the island by foot or bike. There are bike rentals available near the visitor centre if you didn’t bring any bikes along.

Where to Go Next from Skomer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Skomer Island can explore other beautiful destinations in Wales including St. Davids, Newgale Beach, and the Pembrokeshire Coastal Path, which are all located nearby.

Conclusion

Skomer Island is a beautiful destination that offers a unique experience to visitors. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan and enjoy their trip to the fullest. Skomer Island has something for everyone, whether you are an adventure seeker, a nature lover or looking to relax and rejuvenate amidst scenic surroundings. All you have to do is pack your bags and head to Skomer Island for an unforgettable experience.
