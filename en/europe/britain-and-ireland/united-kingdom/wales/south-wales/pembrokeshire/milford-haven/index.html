Introduction to Milford Haven

Located in the heart of Pembrokeshire, South Wales, Milford Haven is a mesmerizing town that boasts an incredible natural harbor and a rich maritime history. With its stunning surroundings and excellent amenities, Milford Haven is one of the top destinations for travelers who love nature, history, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Milford Haven

Milford Haven has so much to offer to its visitors, from historical landmarks to awe-inspiring natural wonders. Here’s our list of must-visit places in Milford Haven:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Milford Haven Museum: Discover the town’s rich heritage and history through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • St. David’s Church: This ancient church, built in the 14th century, houses stunning stained glass windows and historic artefacts.
  • Picton Castle & Gardens: This magnificent castle, dating back to the 13th century, is a perfect example of medieval architecture and is surrounded by lush gardens.

Natural Attractions of Milford Haven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pembrokeshire Coast National Park: Explore the stunning landscapes of this national park, which boasts cliffs, beaches, and thriving wildlife.
  • Skomer Island: Take a boat tour to this beautiful island, home to thousands of puffins and other seabirds.
  • Marloes Sands: Spend a day relaxing on this stunning beach, which is a popular spot for surfers, kayakers, and adventurers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milford Haven

Milford Haven offers a diverse range of hiking and biking trails that meander through breathtaking landscapes and showcase the town’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Pembrokeshire Coast Path: This famous trail hugs the coastline of the national park, offering stunning views of the sea and the cliffs.
  • The Brunel Trail: This easy trail follows the path of the former railway line, which was built by the famous engineer Isambard Kingdom Brunel.
  • Abermawr Woodland Walk: This tranquil trail leads visitors through dense woodland and beautiful streams.

Best Things to Do in Milford Haven: Activities and Tours

From boat trips to wildlife encounters, Milford Haven has it all! Here are some of the best things to do and tours to take while in town:

  • Boat Trips: Take a boat trip around the harbor to observe seals, dolphins, and other marine life.
  • Coasteering: This adrenaline-pumping activity involves jumping and swimming along the rocky coastline.
  • Milford Haven Heritage Tour: A guided tour that takes visitors through the town’s fascinating maritime history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milford Haven

Day 1: Milford Haven Museum, St. David’s Church, and Marloes Sands.

Day 2: Brunel Trail bike ride, lunch at The Scoop Ice Cream Parlour, and Pembrokeshire Coast Path hike.

Day 3: Skomer Island boat tour, picnic lunch, and coasteering adventure.

Day 4: Picton Castle & Gardens visit, lunch at Teapot Cafe, and Aber Mawr Woodland Walk.

Day 5: Relax at Milford Haven Marina, have lunch at Martha’s Vineyard, and visit Little Haven Beach.

Day 6: Milford Haven Heritage Tour, Bosherton Lakes walk, and dinner at The Harbourmaster.

Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Milford Haven

Milford Haven has a vibrant food and drink scene that features fresh seafood, local produce, and traditional Welsh dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A classic British dish that Milford Haven does exceptionally well.
  • Laverbread: A Welsh delicacy made from seaweed, often served with breakfast.
  • Welsh Cakes: A traditional and delicious sweet treat that is perfect for a snack or dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Welsh Ale: A pint of the local brew at The Harbourmaster is a must-try for any beer lover.
  • Welsh Tea: Milford Haven is home to several tea shops and cafes that serve delicious Welsh blends.

Where to Stay in Milford Haven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milford Haven offers travelers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • St. Brides Bay Hotel: This charming hotel overlooks the sea and offers luxurious accommodation and excellent dining options.
  • YHA Broad Haven: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers who want to save money without sacrificing comfort.
  • Priory Bay Hotel: This historic guesthouse is set in stunning gardens and offers elegant rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Milford Haven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milford Haven is a great place to shop for souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best items to buy in town:

  • Welsh Pottery: Handmade pottery is a classic souvenir that will remind you of your trip to Wales.
  • Knitwear: Milford Haven is home to several shops that sell beautiful handmade knitwear, including scarves, hats, and sweaters.
  • Local Cheese: Wales is known for its fantastic cheeses, so be sure to take some home with you!

Local Culture and Customs of Milford Haven

Welsh culture is vibrant and unique, and Milford Haven is no exception. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind while in town:

  • Language: Welsh is spoken in some areas of the town, so don’t be surprised if you hear some Welsh phrases during your visit.
  • St. David’s Day: March 1st is St. David’s Day in Wales, and it is celebrated with parades, concerts, and other festivities.
  • Eisteddfod: This annual festival celebrates Welsh culture and features music, poetry, and other art forms.

Best Time to Visit Milford Haven

The best time to visit Milford Haven is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with tourists and locals alike. However, if you prefer a quieter and more peaceful holiday, consider visiting during the shoulder season, from May to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milford Haven

Do:

  • Do go for a hike or bike ride along the beautiful coastline.
  • Do try some local Welsh specialties, like laverbread and Welsh cakes.
  • Do take a boat trip into Milford Haven’s natural harbor.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the beaches or natural areas.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t forget to check for tide times when exploring the coastline.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milford Haven

Milford Haven is a safe destination for travelers, but like any other destination, it’s important to take some basic precautions:

  • Always lock your doors and windows in your accommodation.
  • Avoid dark and remote areas at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Milford Haven

Travelers to Milford Haven should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage, as medical treatment can be expensive in the UK. Additionally, be sure to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and bring any necessary medications with you on your trip.

Getting to Milford Haven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Milford Haven is easily accessible by plane, train, and bus. Here are your transportation options for getting to town:

  • Airports Near Milford Haven: Cardiff Airport (CWL) is the nearest airport to Milford Haven, located approximately 100 miles away.
  • Bus Stations Near Milford Haven: The nearest bus station to Milford Haven is at Haverfordwest, which is approximately 7 miles away.
  • Other Options: Train and car rental are also available and can be arranged at Cardiff Airport or Haverfordwest.

How to Get Around in Milford Haven

Milford Haven is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. Additionally, visitors can take local buses and taxis to reach farther destinations.

Where to Go Next from Milford Haven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Milford Haven is the perfect base from which to explore the surrounding area of Pembrokeshire. Here are some of the nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • St. Davids: A charming cathedral city and the smallest city in the UK.
  • Tenby: A picturesque seaside town with a historic castle and stunning beaches.
  • Pembroke Castle: A magnificent castle that dates back to the 11th century.

Conclusion

Milford Haven is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience Wales’ natural beauty, rich culture, and fascinating history. With so many things to see and do, you’ll find it hard to leave this stunning town. Plan your trip today and start exploring Milford Haven!
