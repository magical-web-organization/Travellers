Introduction to Hoy

Hoy is a small island in the Orkney Islands, Scotland. Known for its stunning landscapes, rich wildlife, and fascinating history, Hoy is a popular destination for those seeking adventure, relaxation, and cultural experiences. The island is accessible by boat or plane, with several transportation options available from mainland Scotland.

List of Places to Visit in Hoy

Hoy offers a wide range of attractions for visitors of all interests. From cultural landmarks to natural wonders, here are the top places to explore in Hoy:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hoy Heritage Centre - an award-winning museum showcasing the island’s history and culture, including its Norse and Neolithic heritage.
  • Hackness Martello Tower and Battery - a historic military fort dating back to the 19th century.
  • Ward Hill - the highest point on Hoy, with breathtaking views of the surrounding islands and mainland Scotland.

Natural Attractions of Hoy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Old Man of Hoy - an impressive sea stack rising 137 meters above the Atlantic Ocean, a popular destination for climbers and hikers.
  • The Cuilags - a range of hills in the center of the island, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • RSPB Hoy Nature Reserve - a nature reserve covering over 3,000 acres of land ideal for bird watching and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hoy

Hiking and biking are some of the most popular activities in Hoy. Here are some of the best trails and routes to explore:

  • South Walls and East Scapa Flow Trail - a 12-mile circular route that takes you through some of Hoy’s most scenic landscapes.
  • Ward Hill Trail - a challenging route leading to the summit of Ward Hill, with panoramic views of the island.
  • Rackwick Bay and Old Man of Hoy Trail - a 6.5 mile route that takes you through the stunning coastal scenery of Rackwick Bay, culminating in views of the iconic Old Man of Hoy.

Best Things to Do in Hoy: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours available in Hoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing over the Scapa Flow
  • Wildlife watching tours in Hoy
  • Horse Riding through the hills and fields of Hoy

Where to Stay in Hoy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Hoy, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • The Wee House - a cozy guesthouse with stunning views of the Old Man of Hoy and surrounding landscapes.
  • The Hoy Hotel - a historic hotel dating back to the 18th century, with modern amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • The Bothy - a traditional Orkney stone-built house suitable for couples or solo travelers.

Local Culture and Customs of Hoy

Hoy has a rich cultural heritage, including its Norse and Neolithic history. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the island’s customs and traditions, including its unique dialect and music culture.

Best Time to Visit Hoy

The best time to visit Hoy is between May and September when the weather is mild, and the island’s landscapes are in full bloom. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather, including gusty winds and rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hoy

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural and cultural heritage.
  • Take the time to explore the island’s diverse landscapes.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the island’s flora and fauna.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Underestimate the unpredictable weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hoy

Hoy is generally a safe destination for travelers, although visitors should take precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. It is essential to be aware of the weather conditions and dress appropriately.

Health in Hoy

There are no major health risks associated with travel to Hoy. However, visitors should take appropriate measures to avoid tick bites when hiking in wooded areas.

Getting to Hoy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hoy is accessible by boat or plane from mainland Scotland. Here are some transportation options available:

Airports Near Hoy

  • Kirkwall Airport - the main airport serving the Orkney Islands.

Bus Stations Near Hoy

  • Stromness Ferry Terminal - ferry services connecting the Orkney Islands to mainland Scotland.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - rental cars are available in Kirkwall, enabling visitors to explore the island at their leisure.
  • Taxi Services - taxi services are available in Kirkwall and Stromness, offering transportation around Hoy and the surrounding islands.

How to Get Around in Hoy

The best way to explore Hoy is by foot, bike, or car. There are several rental car services available in Kirkwall, and bike rentals are available in some locations. Visitors are encouraged to explore the island’s diverse landscapes at their leisure, taking the time to appreciate the stunning scenery and local culture.

Where to Go Next from Hoy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hoy is located near several other popular destinations in Scotland, including the Orkney Islands, in the Scottish mainland. Visitors can explore the mainland’s cities and towns, with several transportation options available.

Conclusion

Hoy is a hidden gem in the Orkney Islands, offering visitors a unique combination of cultural heritage, natural landscape, and outdoor adventure.
