South Ronaldsay: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Located in the Orkney Islands of Scotland, South Ronaldsay offers a unique experience to visitors who want to explore its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant local culture. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, top activities to try, local culture and customs, and more in South Ronaldsay.

Introduction to South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay is a small island located in the Orkney Islands, off the coast of Scotland. The island is known for its rugged coastline, rolling hills, and rich history, making it a popular destination for tourists looking for an authentic Scottish experience. The island is also home to a vibrant local community, which still practices many of the age-old traditions that have been passed down from generation to generation.

List of Places to Visit in South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay has many must-see attractions that showcase the island’s rich history, stunning natural scenery, and unique local culture. Here are some of the top places to visit in South Ronaldsay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Island of Hunda: Located just off the coast of South Ronaldsay, the Island of Hunda is home to a Neolithic settlement that dates back over 5,000 years. Visitors can explore the ancient ruins and learn about the island’s fascinating history.
  • Orkney Fossil and Heritage Centre: This museum showcases the rich geology and history of the island, including a collection of fossils, rocks, and minerals from around the world.
  • St. Margaret’s Hope: This quaint village is the main town on the island and home to a number of historical buildings and landmarks, including the ancient remains of the St. Margaret’s Church.

Natural Attractions of South Ronaldsay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scapa Flow: This natural harbor is known for its stunning scenery, crystal-clear waters, and rich history. Visitors can explore the area by foot, bike, or boat, and take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hoxa Head: This dramatic headland offers stunning views of the surrounding coastline and is the perfect spot for a scenic hike or picnic.
  • South Ronaldsay Beaches: The island’s rugged coastline is home to several beautiful beaches, including Sand O’ Wright and Water Sound Beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking routes that showcase the island’s stunning natural scenery. Here are some of the top routes to try:

  • The South Ronaldsay Circular Walk: This 10-mile circular walk takes you through the heart of the island, past some of the most scenic areas including St. Margaret’s Hope and the Hoxa Head.
  • The Bowman’s Howe Trail: This 4.5-mile route takes you through the island’s beautiful countryside, passing by the ancient burial site of Bowman’s Howe.

Best Things to Do in South Ronaldsay: Activities and Tours

South Ronaldsay offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some top things to do on the island:

  • Orkney Wine Company: This local winery offers tours and tastings, allowing visitors to sample the unique flavors of Orkney’s wine.
  • The Tomb of the Eagles: This ancient burial site is a must-visit for history buffs, providing a fascinating insight into the lives of the island’s earliest inhabitants.
  • Cycling Tours of South Ronaldsay: Visitors can explore the island on two wheels, with a number of cycling tours available that showcase the best of the island’s natural and cultural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay is home to a range of local delicacies and drinks, which reflect the island’s unique culture and tradition.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orkney Cheese: The island is famous for its cheese, which is made from the milk of locally sourced cows and sheep.
  • Orkney Ale Battered Fish and Chips: This dish features fresh fish, battered in locally made ale and served with crispy chips.
  • Orkney Fudge: This sweet treat is made with locally sourced cream and butter, and comes in a variety of flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Orkney Wine: This unique wine is made from locally grown berries, and has a sweet and fruity flavor.
  • Orkney Beer: The island is home to several microbreweries, which produce a range of beers using traditional brewing methods.

Where to Stay in South Ronaldsay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Ronaldsay has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay on the island:

  • The Orkney Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of St. Margaret’s Hope and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding coastline.
  • The Skerries Bistro and Rooms: This boutique hotel is located on the coast and offers stylish rooms and delicious food.
  • The Stableyard Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in South Ronaldsay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Ronaldsay offers a range of unique and handmade products that make perfect souvenirs or gifts. Here are some top products to bring home:

  • Orkney Knitwear: The island is known for its hand-knitted sweaters, hats, and scarfs, which are warm and cozy and perfect for the colder weather.
  • Orkney Crafts: The island is home to several artists and craftspeople, who produce a range of handmade pottery, glassware, and jewelry.
  • Orkney Cheese: Take home a taste of the island with locally made cheese, which comes in a range of flavors and varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay has a rich culture and tradition, which is reflected in its food, music, and art. Here are some of the key cultural experiences to try on the island:

  • Orkney Folk Music: The island has a vibrant folk music scene, with regular concerts and events showcasing the best of local talent.
  • The Ba Game: This traditional game is played on New Year’s Day, and involves two teams battling it out to carry a wooden ball to their respective ends of the town.
  • The Orkney Storytelling Festival: This annual festival celebrates the art of storytelling, with a range of workshops and performances from local and international artists.

Best Time to Visit South Ronaldsay

The best time to visit South Ronaldsay is between May and September, when the weather is mild and the daylight hours are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Ronaldsay

  • Explore the island’s rich history and ancient monuments.
  • Try the local food and drink, including Orkney Cheese and Ale.
  • Respect the island’s natural beauty and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the island’s natural environments.
  • Interfere with historic landmarks or ruins.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay is a safe and welcoming destination, but it’s important to take precautions when exploring the island’s rugged terrain. Here are some top safety tips:

  • Wear sturdy footwear when hiking or walking on the island’s uneven ground.
  • Take care around cliffs and beaches, and avoid walking near the edge.
  • Check the weather before embarking on any outdoor activities, as the island’s weather can change rapidly.

Health in South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay is a relatively remote island, and visitors should be prepared for limited healthcare facilities on the island. Here are some helpful health tips:

  • Bring any necessary medications with you, as pharmacies may be hard to find.
  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as the island’s summer months can be warm and humid.
  • Consider purchasing travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and repatriation.

Getting to South Ronaldsay: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Ronaldsay is accessible by boat or plane, with regular services operating to and from the island.

Airports Near South Ronaldsay

Kirkwall Airport is the main airport located near South Ronaldsay, with direct flights operating to and from major UK cities.

Bus Stations Near South Ronaldsay

The island is served by several local bus services, which operate to and from the main town of St. Margaret’s Hope.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach South Ronaldsay by ferry, with regular services operating to and from the mainland and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in South Ronaldsay

South Ronaldsay is a small island, and visitors can easily explore the island on foot, by bike, or by car. Several local taxi services are also available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from South Ronaldsay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Ronaldsay is located in the heart of the Orkney Islands, and visitors can easily explore the surrounding areas by boat or bus. Here are some top destinations and excursions to try:

  • The Standing Stones of Stenness: This ancient ring of standing stones is located on the nearby island of Mainland and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • The Italian Chapel: This beautiful chapel was built by Italian prisoners of war during WWII and is located on the island of Lamb Holm.
  • The Orkney Distillery: This local distillery produces a range of whiskies and gins, which visitors can sample and enjoy.

Conclusion

South Ronaldsay is a hidden gem in the Orkney Islands, offering visitors a unique and authentic Scottish experience. From its rich history and stunning natural scenery to its vibrant local culture and delicious food, there’s something for everyone in South Ronaldsay.
