Introduction to Rousay

Nestled in the heart of the Orkney Islands, Scotland’s northernmost archipelago, lies Rousay - a hidden gem known for its stunning scenery, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. With a population of fewer than 250 residents, this small island is perfect for a tranquil getaway, away from the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Rousay

No matter where you go on Rousay, you’ll be awestruck by its breathtaking beauty. Here are the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Midhowe Cairn and Chambered Cairn: A fascinating archaeological site dating back to 3000 BC, with a perfectly preserved burial chamber worth discovering.
  • Trumland House: Fascinating architecture from the late 19th century that’s also situated in a picturesque garden and overlooking stunning hillside views.

Natural Attractions of Rousay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rousay South Hill walk: One of the island’s most popular hikes, leading through the rugged terrain. It’s not an easy route, but the breathtaking panoramic views from the top make all the hard work worth it.
  • Egilsay: Explore the tiny island of Rousay, with its deserted beaches and tranquil, otherworldly atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rousay

  • Sourin to Midhowe Broch: The walk leads through some of the island’s loveliest farmland and criss-crosses ancient water mills and millponds, perfect for keen walkers.
  • The Loop walk: This hike offers a “best of” Rousay experience, with incredible sea views, bird-watching opportunities, and historically significant sites to explore.

Best Things to Do in Rousay: Activities and Tours

  • Wild Orkney Walk: A guided tour led by experts in the area, through some of the island’s most secluded landscapes.
  • Kitesurfing: Look no further than the blues of stunning Rousay’s sea to find some of the best kitesurfing spots in Scotland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rousay

If you have a week to spend on this beautiful island, we suggest the following itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Midhowe Cairn and Chambered Cairn, then a visit to Skaill Bay, explore the fishing villages of Rousay
Day 2 A visit to the rural landscape, walk, and picnic at Faraclett Head
Day 3 Visit to the bird sanctuaries and cliff-side walks, including the Loop Walk
Day 4 Visit to Egilsay
Day 5 Walk alongside the coast and enjoy the amazing sea views of Rousay
Day 6 Wild Orkney Walk
Day 7 Heritage sites - Taversoe Tuick and nearby spots

Food and Drink Scene of Rousay

Exploring the local delicacies is a must during your time on Rousay. Here are some dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orkney cheddar and oatcakes: A scrumptious combination of creamy cheddar and crispy oatcakes, typically served as a snack or starter.

Local Beverages

  • Highland Park Whisky: Scotland’s most northernmost whisky distillery, Highland Park, has been producing exceptionally fine malts for over 200 years. Taste it for yourself here!
  • Island Ale: A classic pint in the local bar.

Where to Stay in Rousay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Discovering Rousay is a unique experience, made even better when choosing to unwind in unique and beautiful accommodation. Here are our top choices:

  • Taversoe Hotel: A family-run hotel in the heart of the village with classic-style rooms and a top quality restaurant serving local produce.
  • The Rousay Hostel: Ideal for backpackers and budget travelers looking for a comfortable and affordable stay.

What to Buy in Rousay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jewelry: Orkney’s unique rocks and materials inspire the craftsmanship of its local jewelers. Get your hands on a unique piece here.
  • Rousay Ale: As mentioned, Island Ale is a classic in the local bar, for the beer lovers, this is a must-have.

Local Culture and Customs of Rousay

Rousay’s local customs and culture are steeped in the Orkney traditions and remote island life. Be ready to engage in some serious hospitality and friendliness, as well as learning about the local institutions and language.

Best Time to Visit Rousay

The peak travel season is undoubtedly in the summer. The abundant flora, fauna, and wildlife make it an excellent time to explore the outdoors and take in the scenery. However, it is worth noting that even the shoulder and off-seasons have mild weather and an aura of tranquility.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rousay

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.
  • Explore the outdoors and wild beauty of Rousay.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect the environment.
  • Ignore the locals, they are a friendly lot!

Safety and Health

Safety in Rousay

Rousay is one of the safest places in the world, with low crime rates and friendly locals. As with any remote island, it’s advisable to prepare well for outdoor activities: wear appropriate gear, bring plenty of water and snacks, and inform others of your itinerary.

Health in Rousay

The Rousay medical center provides an excellent level of care to both the community and travelers. It is also good to check with your GP what vaccinations you may need before traveling.

Getting to Rousay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rousay

The nearest commercial airport to Rousay is Kirkwall, located in the mainland of Orkney.

Bus Stations Near Rousay

Buses from Kirkwall take travelers stop in the village of Rousay, so it is worth planning your route accordingly.

Other Options

Visitors can choose to take ferries (Ro-Ro vessels) to Orkney, then use the local public transport ferry services. Taxis and bike rentals are also available for hire once you have arrived in Rousay.

How to Get Around in Rousay

Most places on Rousay are accessible by bike or foot. One can reach to the remote areas through by car and taxi services. You can navigate the area’s roads with a detailed map and careful attention.

Where to Go Next from Rousay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Finish Island

Finstown is a picturesque village located on the Mainland of Orkney, West of Rousay. This charming place to visit for those who enjoy quaint scenery and historica landparks.

Westray

Just next to Rousay lies Westray, a small, peaceful island with outstanding scenery, including birds of prey, secret gardens, and a 5,000-year-old burial chamber.

Conclusion

Rousay Island is a perfect place for travelers looking for a peaceful and unforgettable experience. Its unique character and idyllic landscapes make it stand out from other tourist destinations in the world, and the local culture and hospitality are second to none. Heed the travel tips in this guide, and you’re sure to get the most out of your Rousay adventure!
