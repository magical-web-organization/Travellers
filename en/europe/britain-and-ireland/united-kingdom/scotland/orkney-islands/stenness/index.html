Stenness Travel Guide: Explore the Best of Orkney Islands, Scotland

Are you looking for a quiet escape to a stunning destination in Scotland? Look no further than Stenness, a charming village situated in the heart of the Orkney Islands. Known for its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant culture, Stenness has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, you won’t be disappointed by this incredible destination.

Introduction to Stenness

Located in the northern part of Scotland, Stenness is a picturesque village nestled between a series of beautiful lochs. With its charming stone houses and stunning natural beauty, Stenness offers visitors a unique and unforgettable travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Stenness

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stenness is steeped in history, and there are plenty of cultural attractions to explore in the village and its surrounding areas. Some of the top places to visit include:

  • The Standing Stones of Stenness
  • Maeshowe Chambered Cairn
  • Skara Brae Prehistoric Village
  • The Ring of Brodgar

Natural Attractions of Stenness: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Stenness has plenty of natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Loch of Stenness
  • Harray Loch
  • Yesnaby Cliffs
  • Hoy Hills

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stenness

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Stenness is on foot or by bike. Here are some of the top hiking routes in the area:

  • The Hoy Hills Walk
  • The Ring of Brodgar and Skara Brae Walk
  • Loch of Stenness Circuit
  • Harray Loch Circuit

Best Things to Do in Stenness: Activities and Tours

Looking for some adventure or unique experiences while in Stenness? Check out these top activities and tours:

  • Orkney Island Hopping Tour
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding on Stenness Loch
  • Horseback Riding around Stenness
  • Fishing Tour on Harray Loch

Week-Long Itinerary for Stenness

If you’re planning to visit Stenness for a week, here is your perfect itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Orkney and explore the village center. Day 2: Visit Skara Brae Prehistoric Village. Day 3: Hike the Hoy Hills. Day 4: Take a day trip to the Isle of Hoy. Day 5: Visit the Standing Stones of Stenness and Maeshowe Chambered Cairn. Day 6: Go kayaking or paddleboarding on Stenness Loch. Day 7: Take a fishing tour on Harray Loch.

Where to Stay in Stenness: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Stenness: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir from your trip to Stenness? Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Orkney Cheese

  • Orkney Knitwear

Local Culture and Customs of Stenness

Stenness has a unique and vibrant culture that is worth learning about. Some interesting customs and traditions include:

  • Orkney Folk Festival
  • The Ba’ Game
  • West Mainland Agricultural Show

Best Time to Visit Stenness

The best time to visit Stenness is between May and September when the weather is mild and the days are long. If you’re interested in attending one of the village’s annual festivals or events, check the calendar beforehand to plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stenness

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by staying on designated paths and trails while hiking or biking.
  • Support local businesses by purchasing local products and souvenirs.
  • Be respectful of the locals’ privacy and property.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in natural areas.
  • Ignore signs and barriers that indicate off-limits areas.
  • Disrespect local customs or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stenness

Stenness is generally a safe destination for visitors; however, it’s important to take standard precautions such as locking doors and windows at night and avoiding desolate areas at night.

Health in Stenness

Stenness has excellent medical facilities and is home to various health professionals. Make sure to carry enough medication and prescriptions if you have any pre-existing health conditions.

Getting to Stenness: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stenness

The closest airport to Stenness is Kirkwall Airport, located approximately 11 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Stenness

There are regular bus services to Stenness from Kirkwall, Stromness, and other towns in the Orkney Islands.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the Orkney Islands at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Stenness

Walking or biking are the best ways to get around Stenness. However, if you need to travel longer distances or explore other parts of the Orkney Islands, renting a car is the best option.

Where to Go Next from Stenness: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore the Orkney Islands further, here are some top destinations and excursions:

  • The Isle of Hoy
  • The Yesnaby Cliffs
  • The Italian Chapel
  • The Tomb of the Eagles

Conclusion

Stenness is one of Scotland’s hidden gems, offering visitors a unique and unforgettable travel experience. From its rich history to beautiful natural landscapes and vibrant culture, this charming village has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to plan your next adventure in Stenness and discover the best this Scottish destination has to offer.
