Introduction to Stronsay

Located in the Orkney Islands of Scotland, Stronsay is a quaint and picturesque island that is perfect for those looking for a peaceful and serene vacation away from the hustle and bustle of the city. With its diverse range of attractions and activities, Stronsay is one of the must-visit destinations in the Scottish region.

List of Places to Visit in Stronsay

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Stronsay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Catherine’s Chapel: A historic chapel dating back to the 16th century that boasts of stunning architecture and a serene atmosphere.
  • Whitehall Village: This ancient village has a castle that dates back to the medieval period and offers visitors a glimpse into the island’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Fish Mart Memorial: This commemorative monument is a testament to the history of Stronsay’s fishing industry and its importance to the island’s economy.

Natural Attractions of Stronsay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Vat of Kirbuster: A geological formation formed by a natural sinkhole that offers breathtaking views of the island.
  • The Els Ness: An ideal spot for bird watchers and nature lovers, this scenic location features stunning cliffs and a variety of wildlife.
  • The Skua Trail: A walking trail that takes visitors through stunning coastal landscapes, diverse flora and fauna, and a serene atmosphere perfect for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stronsay

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a beginner, Stronsay offers diverse hiking routes that are perfect for any level of fitness. Here are some of the top hiking and biking trails in Stronsay:

  • The Howar Trail: A walking trail that takes visitors to the ancient Howar Village, one of Scotland’s oldest houses.
  • Keila Road Trail: A bike route that offers stunning views of the coast and the chance to see a variety of wildlife.
  • The Millhouse Trail: A walking trail that takes visitors to the historic Millhouse that dates back to the 18th century.

Best Things to Do in Stronsay: Activities and Tours

Stronsay offers a variety of activities for visitors of all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Stronsay:

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the island’s stunning coastline by kayak and get up close with the diverse marine life of the Orkney Islands.
  • Fishing: Stronsay is known for its fishing industry, and visitors can enjoy fishing tours to experience the island’s fishing heritage.
  • Bird Watching: With a diverse range of avian life, Stronsay is an ideal location for bird watchers.

Food and Drink Scene of Stronsay

Stronsay boasts of a rich culinary history with locally sourced ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes in Stronsay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orkney Fudge: A delicious dessert made from Orkney butter and cream that is sure to satisfy any sweet tooth.
  • Smoked Haddock Chowder: A hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and local herbs that is a popular dish among islanders.

Local Beverages

  • Dark Island Ale: A locally brewed beer that is popular among both locals and visitors.
  • Orkney Gin: A handcrafted gin that is infused with local botanicals and herbs, giving it a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Stronsay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stronsay offers a variety of accommodations for visitors to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • Stronsay Hotel: A cozy hotel that offers stunning views of the island and is located near many of the top attractions in Stronsay.
  • Smithy Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that provides a comfortable stay for visitors and is located in the heart of the island.
  • The Pier Guesthouse: A family-owned guesthouse that offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere and is located near the coast.

Local Culture and Customs of Stronsay

Stronsay has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Some of the notable customs and traditions of Stronsay include the annual Stronsay Foy, a festival that celebrates the island’s fishing industry, and the Stronsay Flag Day, a celebration of the island’s culture and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Stronsay

The best time to visit Stronsay is between May and September when the weather is mild and the island is bustling with visitors enjoying the diverse range of activities and attractions available on the island.
