Introduction to Westray

Westray is a small island in Orkney, the archipelago of islands located in the north of Scotland, United Kingdom. Inhabited by only 600 people, Westray is a beautiful and scenic destination that offers a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of the big city.

Known as the ‘Queen o’ the Isles’, Westray is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. From its beautiful beaches to its stunning viewpoints, Westray has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Westray

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Noltland Castle

Noltland Castle is a 16th-century tower house that offers a unique insight into the medieval times. The castle was built by Gilbert Balfour, the notorious Master of Mary Queen of Scots’ household, and today, it is one of the best-preserved castles in Scotland.

2. Westray Heritage Centre

Westray Heritage Centre is a museum that showcases the life, culture, and history of the island. The museum has a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that offer a glimpse into the lives of the people of Westray.

3. St. Mary’s Church

St. Mary’s Church is a 12th-century church that offers a peaceful spot to reflect and admire the historical architecture of the island. The church is still in use and is open to visitors during the day.

Natural Attractions of Westray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Noup Head

Noup Head is a dramatic cliff on the west coast of Westray. It provides a stunning viewpoint overlooking the Atlantic Ocean and is a popular spot for birdwatching. It is home to a large number of seabirds, including puffins, razorbills, and guillemots.

2. Rackwick Bay

Rackwick Bay is a beautiful white sandy beach on the east coast of Westray. It is a peaceful spot to relax and enjoy the scenic views.

3. Castle o’Burrian

Castle o’Burrian is a natural sea stack on the west coast of Westray. It is a popular spot for walkers and offers a stunning view of the coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westray

Westray is an island that is perfect for walking and cycling. There are plenty of designated paths and routes that offer stunning views of the landscape. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Westray Coastal Walk
  • Papa Westray Hiking Trail
  • Westray to North Ronaldsay Cycle Route

Week-Long Itinerary for Westray

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Westray:

Day 1: Arrive in Westray, explore the local area, and visit Westray Heritage Centre.

Day 2: Take a hiking or cycling tour of the island, checking out Noltland Castle, Castle o’Burrian, and Noup Head.

Day 3: Go birdwatching at Noup Head and take a boat trip to see the other islands of Orkney.

Day 4: Relax on Rackwick Bay and explore the nearby beaches.

Day 5: Take a fishing trip and sample some of the local seafood.

Day 6: Go horse riding or surfing on the beach.

Day 7: Explore the local area and take in some of the local culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Westray

Westray has a rich culture and heritage that is influenced by its Viking and medieval past. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their island’s unique heritage.

Best Time to Visit Westray

The best time to visit Westray is between May and September, when the weather is warm and the days are long. The island offers plenty of outdoor activities and events during this time, and the scenery is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westray

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Be polite and courteous to the locals.
  • Follow the countryside code when exploring the walking and cycling trails.

Don’t:

  • Damage or remove any of the historical or natural sites.
  • Leave any litter or waste behind.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westray

Westray is a relatively safe destination with low levels of crime. However, travelers should take basic precautions, such as locking doors and windows at night and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Westray

Medical facilities on the island are limited, so travelers should bring any necessary medication with them. It is also important to take precautions against Lyme Disease when walking or cycling in the countryside.

Getting to Westray: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westray

The closest airports to Westray are Kirkwall Airport and Sumburgh Airport.

Bus Stations Near Westray

There is a regular bus service that runs between Kirkwall and Pierowall, the main town on Westray.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Westray by ferry from Kirkwall or by private boat.

How to Get Around in Westray

The best way to get around Westray is by walking or cycling. There are plenty of designated paths and routes that offer stunning views of the landscape, and some of the attractions are only accessible by foot or bike.
