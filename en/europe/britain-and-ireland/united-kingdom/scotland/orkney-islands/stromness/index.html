Introduction to Stromness

Stromness, a small town in the Orkney Islands, Scotland, is a place of great beauty and history. With its winding streets, old-fashioned buildings, and picturesque harbor, Stromness offers a glimpse into Scotland’s past. The town is home to a rich and fascinating history that includes everything from Viking invaders to whalers and merchants. Today, Stromness is a popular tourist destination, drawing visitors from around the world who come to explore its ancient sites, stunning landscapes and vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Stromness

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stromness Museum: This museum offers visitors a chance to explore the history of Stromness, dating back to its Viking past. The museum features exhibits on Orkney’s geology, marine life, and whaling heritage.

  • Orkney Museum: This museum showcases a wide variety of exhibits on Orkney’s ancient history, including artifacts from the Vikings, Picts, and Bronze Age.

  • Maeshowe: This is a chambered tomb that is older than Stonehenge and the Great Pyramids of Egypt. Visitors can take a guided tour down into the tomb that features carvings and Viking runes.

Natural Attractions of Stromness: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scapa Flow: This is a natural harbor with turquoise blue waters surrounded by beautiful green hills. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the area that has significant historical importance in World War II.

  • The Old Man of Hoy: This 450-foot sea stack features a stunning natural formation that is popular with rock climbers. Visitors can take a boat tour around the stack or take a guided walk along the cliffs for spectacular panoramic views.

  • Hoy Hills: The rugged hills of Hoy provide visitors with some of the most stunning views of Orkney Islands. Visitors can take a hike on one of the many trails or hire a bike and explore the island on their own.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stromness

  • The Hoy Trail: This hiking trail takes visitors on a journey through the rugged hills of Hoy, offering stunning views of the surrounding islands. The trail is a must-do for hikers and nature lovers.

  • Stromness to Skara Brae: This hiking and biking trail offers visitors a chance to explore the ancient ruins of Skara Brae and the stunning coastline between the two towns.

Best Things to Do in Stromness: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Orkney is renowned for its wildlife, including the world’s largest population of killer whales. Visitors can take a whale watching tour to see these magnificent creatures up close.

  • Folk Festival: The Orkney Folk Festival is a celebration of traditional Scottish music and culture. The festival takes place in May every year and features live music, dancing, and other cultural events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stromness

Day 1 - Arrive in Stromness, explore the town’s history and culture at Stromness Museum.
Day 2 - Travel to Kirkwall to visit the stunning St. Magnus Cathedral.
Day 3 - Take a boat tour to explore the beautiful Scapa Flow.
Day 4 - Take a hike on the Hoy Trail.
Day 5 - Rent a bike and take a ride to Orkney’s archeological treasures, Skara Brae and the Ring of Brodgar.
Day 6 - Visit Maeshowe Chambered Tomb and the Old Man of Hoy sea stack.
Day 7 - Attend the Orkney Folk Festival and enjoy traditional Scottish music.

Food and Drink Scene of Stromness

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orkney Cheddar: This cheese is made from the milk of local cows on the islands. It’s perfect for cheese lovers and is often used in dishes like Orkney cheese pie or cheese scones.

  • Orkney Fudge: This creamy, buttery fudge is made from local cream and is a perfect sweet treat.

Local Beverages

  • Orkney Brewery: This brewery produces a wide variety of delicious local ales that can be enjoyed at the brewery or pubs throughout the islands.

  • Highland Park Distillery: This whisky distillery produces award-winning single malt scotch that can be tasted at the distillery or in local bars.

Where to Stay in Stromness: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Stromness Hotel: This historic hotel offers visitors beautiful views of Stromness Harbor and is located in the heart of the town.

  • Ferry Inn Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located on the waterfront and offers visitors a chance to stay in a traditional Scottish home.

  • St. Ola Hotel: This hotel is located in Kirkwall, just a short drive from Stromness, and offers visitors stunning views of the town’s beautiful harbor.

What to Buy in Stromness: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Orkney Hand-Crafted Jewelry: Visitors can find beautiful, one-of-a-kind pieces of jewelry made by local artisans.

  • Orkney Knitwear: Visitors can find beautiful, handcrafted sweaters, hats, and scarves made from local wool.

Local Culture and Customs of Stromness

Stromness is a small town where locals are friendly and hospitality runs in their veins. Visitors will find that Scots are proud of their heritage and love sharing it with visitors. However, Scots are also quite traditional, so visitors should always be respectful of local customs, etiquette, and habits.

Best Time to Visit Stromness

The best time to visit Stromness is in the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild and the days are long, with up to 18 hours of daylight. The town is at its busiest during these months, but visitors can still find plenty of space and tranquility.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stromness

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette.

  • Dress for the weather, Stromness can be quite chilly even in summer.

  • Take cash; while most shops and restaurants accept credit card, some places operate on cash.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather, it can be unpredictable all year round.

  • Be reckless when hiking, tiredness can be a contributing factor in accidents.

  • Be late, punctuality is taken very seriously in Scotland.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stromness

Stromness is a relatively safe town. Visitors should beware of treacherous sea conditions and unstable cliffs when hiking or doing any outdoor activities.

Health in Stromness

Visitors should bring any medication they require with them. While there are pharmacies in and around Stromness, it is best to have any medication in hand, especially for those on specific medications that are not readily available over the counter.

Getting to Stromness: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stromness

Kirkwall airport is the main point of entry for the island group. Visitors can take a one-hour flight from Edinburgh or Glasgow.

Bus Stations Near Stromness

Visitors can take bus services from other parts of Orkney to visit Stromness, or they can take a bus from the Kirkwall.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from the Scottish mainland to Stromness. Northlink Ferries operate the ferries from Scrabster to Stromness or Aberdeen to Kirkwall and allow visitors to bring their vehicles across.

How to Get Around in Stromness

Walking is the most popular and convenient way for visitors to explore the town. However, visitors can also rent bikes or hire a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Stromness: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Ring of Brodgar: Visitors can explore the ancient stones and history of Orkney with a visit here, an archeological must-see.

  • The Italian Chapel: A former World War II Prisoner of War camp chapel, beautifully converted into a shrine.

Conclusion

Stromness is the perfect destination for those looking to explore the rich and fascinating history of Scotland. With its stunning natural beauty and vibrant culture, this small town has something to offer everyone who visits. From ancient ruins to traditional festivals, Stromness is a place of wonder and enchantment that is not to be missed. Plan your perfect trip now!
