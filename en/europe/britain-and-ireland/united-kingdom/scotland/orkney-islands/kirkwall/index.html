Introduction to Kirkwall

Kirkwall, the largest town of Orkney Islands, Scotland, offers a perfect blend of history and natural wonders. This charming town is known for its well-preserved Renaissance and Georgian architecture and for being the main hub of Orkney. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Kirkwall has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kirkwall

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Magnus Cathedral: This stunning cathedral dates back to the 12th century and is a major landmark of Kirkwall. The cathedral boasts magnificent stained glass windows and a beautiful architecture style.
  • Orkney Museum: This museum exhibits the rich cultural heritage of Orkney, and its collection includes everything from prehistoric artifacts to contemporary art.
  • Orkney Wireless Museum: This museum showcases the history of wireless communication, a technology that had a crucial role in the defense of the British Islands during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Kirkwall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Ring of Brodgar: This Neolithic henge monument is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most impressive standing stones in Scotland.
  • Scapa Flow: This vast body of water is known for its beautiful beaches, stunning marine life, and World War I and II shipwrecks.
  • Brough of Birsay: This small tidal island offers a beautiful sunset view and is a birdwatcher’s paradise, as many seabirds nest here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirkwall

  • The Heart of Neolithic Orkney Trail: This 10.5-mile trail takes you through some of Orkney’s most historic sites, including the famous Ring of Brodgar and Maeshowe.
  • The Scapa Walk: This 5.5-mile walk offers a stunning view of Scapa Flow, taking you through lush green areas and coastal paths.
  • The North Isles Cycle Route: This 35-mile route takes you through the countryside and coast of the North Isles, offering breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Kirkwall: Activities and Tours

  • The Orkney Brewery Tour: Take a tour of Orkney Brewery and sample some of the best beers in the world.
  • The Skara Brae Tour: Discover the rich history of this prehistoric village, which offers a fascinating insight into the lives and customs of neolithic peoples.
  • The Orkney Fossil and Heritage Centre Tour: This tour showcases the ancient fossils and geology of Orkney, including the famous fish fossils from the Devonian period.

Food and Drink Scene of Kirkwall

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orkney Crab: This fresh and tasty local seafood is a must-try dish in Kirkwall.
  • Neeps and Tatties: This classic Scottish dish, consisting of mashed turnips and potatoes, is a staple in Kirkwall’s traditional cuisine.
  • Orcadian Bere Bannocks: These flatbread-like bannocks are made with a unique type of barley grown only in Orkney and have a delicious nutty flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Orkney Beer: Orkney Brewery, located in Kirkwall, produces a wide variety of beers, including their famous Red MacGregor and Skull Splitter.
  • Orcadian Whisky: Highland Park and Scapa Distillery, both located in Orkney, produce some of the finest whiskies in the world.

Where to Stay in Kirkwall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Ayre Hotel: This modern hotel is located in a prime location with spectacular views of the harbor and offers luxury amenities and delicious dining options.
  • The Albert Hotel: This cozy and welcoming hotel is located in the heart of Kirkwall’s town center and provides a warm and friendly atmosphere.
  • The Orkney Hotel: This elegant hotel offers comfortable rooms and luxurious amenities, including a spa and wellness center.

What to Buy in Kirkwall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Orkney Fudge: This traditional Scottish sweet is made with locally produced cream and butter and comes in many flavors, including vanilla, chocolate, and whisky.
  • Orkney Crafts: Orkney is home to many talented artists and craftsmen who produce unique and beautiful handcrafted items, including pottery, jewelry, and knitwear.
  • Orcadian Beef: Orkney produces some of the finest quality beef in the world, and you can buy beef products like steaks and burgers from many local butchers.

Local Culture and Customs of Kirkwall

Kirkwall’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in the region’s Viking history. The annual Ba Game, held on Christmas and New Year’s Day, is a traditional Viking ball game that dates back hundreds of years. The Vikings also had a significant influence on the local dialect, which still features many Norse words and phrases.

Best Time to Visit Kirkwall

The best time to visit Kirkwall is from May to September, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, it is also the busiest time, so be prepared for larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kirkwall

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and beer.
  • Take a wildlife tour to see seals, otters, and other marine life.
  • Take a guided tour of the historic sites led by a local historian.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the sea without a guide, as the currents can be dangerous.
  • Take any artifacts from the historic sites or beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kirkwall

Kirkwall is a very safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Kirkwall

Kirkwall has a few medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. It is advisable to bring any necessary medications with you, as not all medicines may be available here.

Getting to Kirkwall: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kirkwall

Kirkwall Airport is located just 3 miles from the town and is the main airport of Orkney Islands. There are daily flights connecting Kirkwall to Aberdeen, Edinburgh, Glasgow, and Inverness.

Bus Stations Near Kirkwall

Stagecoach and Orkney Bus are the two primary bus companies that operate in Orkney and provide local and intercity services.

Other Options

Ferries are available from Aberdeen, Scrabster, and Gills Bay to Stromness, which is just 15 miles from Kirkwall.

How to Get Around in Kirkwall

Kirkwall is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Taxis, buses, and rental cars are also available for transportation within the town.

Where to Go Next from Kirkwall: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Historic Village of Stromness: This charming coastal village is just 15 miles from Kirkwall and is known for its historic architecture and cultural significance.
  • The Island of Hoy: This beautiful island, accessible by ferry, offers stunning views and outdoor adventures, including hiking and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Kirkwall is a hidden gem that offers a perfect mix of history, natural beauty, and culture. Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or gourmet experiences, Kirkwall has something to offer. Visit this stunning town, and you will have memories that will stay with you forever.
