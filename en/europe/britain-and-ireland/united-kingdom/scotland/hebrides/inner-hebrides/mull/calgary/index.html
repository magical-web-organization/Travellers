Introduction to Calgary

Located in Mull, within Inner Hebrides, in Hebrides, Scotland, Calgary is a picturesque village surrounded by stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage. It is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a peaceful retreat. From hiking and biking routes, to museums and historical places, to local food and drink scenes, there is something for everyone in Calgary.

List of Places to Visit in Calgary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calgary has a rich cultural heritage, and there are numerous museums and historical places worth visiting. Some of the must-see cultural attractions include:

  • The Island of Mull Historical and Archaeological Society Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Mull and the surrounding islands, and houses an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  • Duart Castle: This 13th-century castle is one of the most impressive historical attractions on the island. It is located only a short drive from Calgary and offers stunning views of the Sound of Mull.
  • Iona Abbey: This ancient abbey is considered to be one of the most important religious sites in Scotland. It is located on the nearby island of Iona and can be reached by ferry from Mull.

Natural Attractions of Calgary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calgary is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and offers a vast range of outdoor activities. Here are some of the best natural attractions worth exploring:

  • Calgary Bay: This beautiful sandy beach is located just a short walk from the village and offers excellent swimming and sunbathing opportunities.
  • Ben More: This mountain is the highest on Mull, and offers spectacular views from the summit. Hiking to the top is a popular activity among visitors to the island.
  • The Isle of Staffa: This uninhabited island is famous for its unique rock formations and is home to Fingal’s Cave, a beautiful sea cave that inspired composer Felix Mendelssohn’s famous overture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calgary

Calgary offers numerous hiking and biking trails that showcase the island’s stunning natural beauty. Some of the best routes for hikers and bikers include:

  • The Calgary Art in Nature Trail: This trail is a great option for those looking for an easy hike or bike ride through beautiful woodlands and meadows.
  • Ben More Trail: This challenging trail is perfect for more experienced hikers and showcases some of the most stunning views on the island.
  • The Mull Overland Route: This multi-day route showcases some of the most beautiful landscapes on the island, and is a popular choice for adventurous hikers.

Best Things to Do in Calgary: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other exciting activities and tours to explore in Calgary. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing Tours: These tours offer a unique perspective on the island’s stunning landscapes and wildlife.
  • Whale Watching Tours: Mull is one of the best places in Scotland for whale watching, with sightings of minke whales, basking sharks, and dolphins.
  • Cultural Tours: Get a deeper insight into the island’s colorful history and heritage through guided tours of its many archaeological sites and museums.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calgary

Here is a suggested itinerary for those planning a week-long trip to Calgary:

Day 1 – Arrival and Explore the Village

  • Afternoon: Check into your accommodation and explore the village of Calgary at your own pace.

Day 2 – Hike to Calgary Bay and Visit Calgary Art in Nature Trail

  • Morning: Hike to Calgary Bay, one of the most beautiful beaches in the area.
  • Afternoon: Take a leisurely hike or bike ride on the Calgary Art in Nature Trail, which is a scenic nature trail located close to the village.

Day 3 – Visit Iona Abbey and Duart Castle

  • Morning: Take a ferry to the nearby island of Iona to visit the famous Iona Abbey
  • Afternoon: Head to Duart Castle, one of the most impressive historical attractions on the island, and learn about its fascinating history.

Day 4 – Wildlife and Whale Watching Tour

  • Morning and afternoon: Join a guided wildlife and whale watching tour to experience the best of Mull’s stunning local wildlife.

Day 5 – Explore Isle of Staffa and Fingal’s Cave

  • Morning and afternoon: Take a boat trip to the uninhabited Isle of Staffa and explore its unique rock formations and stunning sea cave, Fingal’s Cave.

Day 6 – Visit Mull Overland Route

  • Morning and afternoon: Take a hike along the Mull Overland Route, a multi-day trail showcasing some of the island’s most stunning landscapes.

Day 7 – Departure

  • Morning: Check out of your accommodation and depart from Calgary.

Food and Drink Scene of Calgary

Calgary is known for its excellent local cuisine and traditional Scottish dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages on the island:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: This traditional Scottish dish is made with sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, onions, oatmeal, and spices, and is a must-try for visitors to the island.
  • Cullen Skink: This hearty soup is a delicious blend of smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream.
  • Fish and Chips: This classic British dish is served with locally caught fish and crispy fries.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey: Scottish whiskey is world-renowned, and there are a variety of local distilleries on the island that offer tours and tastings.
  • Real Ale: There are many locally brewed ales and beers to try on the island, many of which are served in traditional Scottish pubs and bars.

Where to Stay in Calgary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodation options in and around Calgary to suit all budgets, with a range of hotels, hostels, guesthouses, and self-catering cottages. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Barn-House B&B: This charming bed and breakfast is located just minutes from Calgary Bay and offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Aros Ard B&B: This quaint B&B is situated in a stunning converted farmhouse and offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.
  • The Isle of Mull Hotel & Spa: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the nearby Sound of Mull and is a great option for those looking for a higher-end stay.

What to Buy in Calgary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many local products and souvenirs to bring back from Calgary, including:

  • Whiskey and local beer: Mull is known for its excellent whiskey and beer, which makes for an ideal souvenir or gift.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Many local artisans sell unique handmade jewelry that makes for a great keepsake.
  • Local Crafts: Mull is home to many talented craftspeople who make a range of items including pottery, woodwork, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Calgary

Calgary is renowned for its lively music scene, with many local pubs and bars hosting live music nights throughout the year. The island also has a rich cultural heritage, with a variety of festivals and events taking place annually, including the Mull Music Festival and the Mull Rally.

Best Time to Visit Calgary

The best time to visit Calgary is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warmest and the daylight hours are longer. Spring and autumn also offer a pleasant climate with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calgary

Do:

  • Pack for rain: Mull is known for its unpredictable weather, so it’s always best to bring waterproof clothing and sturdy shoes.
  • Drive with caution: The roads on the island can be narrow and winding, so it’s important to drive with caution.
  • Respect local customs: When visiting historic sites or landmarks, it’s important to respect local customs and follow any rules or guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Mull is known for its pristine natural beauty, so it’s important to dispose of waste properly and not litter.
  • Disturb wildlife: The island is home to many species of wildlife, including rare birds and marine life, so it’s important not to disturb them.
  • Ignore local customs: When visiting historic sites or landmarks, it’s important not to ignore local customs or signage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calgary

Mull is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, visitors should always take standard safety precautions.

Health in Calgary

Visitors to Calgary should be aware of the following health and medical information:

  • Medical facilities: There is a small hospital in Tobermory, the largest town on the island. In an emergency, dial 999 for an ambulance.
  • Insect bites and stings: Mosquitoes and other biting insects can be a nuisance during the summer months, so it’s important to use insect repellent.
  • Sunburn and heatstroke: Visitors should take care to protect themselves from the sun and stay hydrated during hot weather.

Getting to Calgary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calgary

The nearest major airport to Calgary is Glasgow International Airport, which has regular flights to and from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Calgary

The nearest bus station to Calgary is located in the nearby town of Tobermory, from where regular buses run to other destinations on the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or bike to explore the island, or take a ferry from the mainland to Mull.

How to Get Around in Calgary

The best way to get around Calgary and the surrounding areas is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Calgary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many destinations and excursions worth exploring in the surrounding areas, including:

  • Oban: This bustling fishing town is located on the mainland and is famous for its seafood and whisky.
  • The Ardnamurchan Peninsula: This remote peninsula is home to some of the most stunning landscapes in Scotland, including sandy beaches and dramatic cliffs.
  • The Cairngorms National Park: This vast national park is located in the heart of the Scottish Highlands and offers hiking, biking, and skiing opportunities.

Conclusion

Calgary is a stunning destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs to foodies and adventurers. With its picturesque setting, rich cultural heritage, and excellent local cuisine, it’s no surprise that Calgary is becoming an increasingly popular destination for visitors to Scotland. Plan your trip now and experience the best of this beautiful village on the island of Mull.
