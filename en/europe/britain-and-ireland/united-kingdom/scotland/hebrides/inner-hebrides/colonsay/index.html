Introduction to Colonsay

Located in the Inner Hebrides, Colonsay is a stunning island in Scotland that boasts scenic landscapes, rich history, and a unique culture. With a population of just over 130 people, Colonsay is a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to explore Colonsay and make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Colonsay

Here are some of the must-visit places in Colonsay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Colonsay House: A stunning Georgian mansion that dates back to the early 18th century. The house has beautiful gardens and is open to the public for tours.
  • Kiloran Bay: A picturesque beach that is perfect for a lazy day by the sea.
  • Old Roinn Castle: A ruined castle that dates back to the 15th century and offers stunning views of the island.

Natural Attractions of Colonsay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Oronsay Priory: A ruined Augustinian priory located on a tidal island that offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Colonsay’s beaches: Colonsay has some of the most beautiful beaches in Scotland, including Kiloran Bay, Kilpatrick Bay, and Scalasaig Bay.
  • Colonsay Wildlife Centre: A sanctuary that cares for injured wildlife, including seals, otters, and birds of prey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colonsay

Colonsay is a hiker’s paradise, and there are plenty of trails to explore on foot or bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Colonsay:

  • The Colonsay Way: A 10-mile trail that runs from Scalasaig to Kiloran Bay and offers stunning views of the island.
  • Oronsay Island Walk: A walk to Oronsay Island at low tide, which offers beautiful views of the Atlantic Ocean and the island’s rugged coastline.

Best Things to Do in Colonsay: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring the island’s natural beauty or want to immerse yourself in its culture, there are plenty of things to do in Colonsay. Here are some of the best:

  • Visit the Colonsay Brewery: Colonsay has its own brewery that produces a range of ales using traditional Scottish recipes.
  • Take a boat tour: Explore the island’s coastline and surrounding waters with a guided boat tour.
  • Visit the Colonsay Book Festival: An annual event that takes place in September and features talks and readings from notable authors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colonsay

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Colonsay:

Food and Drink Scene of Colonsay

Colonsay has a rich culinary history, and there are plenty of delicious local dishes and drinks to try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colonsay Lamb: Colonsay is known for its delicious lamb, which is grass-fed and locally sourced.
  • Seafood: Colonsay is surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean, so you can expect fresh seafood dishes, such as scallops and lobster.
  • Sourdough Bread: Colonsay has its own bakery that produces delicious sourdough bread using locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Colonsay Ales: The Colonsay Brewery produces a range of ales using traditional Scottish recipes.
  • Colonsay Gin: Produced by Wild Thyme Spirits, Colonsay Gin is made using local botanicals and has won several awards.

Where to Stay in Colonsay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Colonsay has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget. Here are some options:

  • The Colonsay Hotel: A historic hotel located in Scalasaig that offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving locally sourced food.
  • Kiloran House Bed and Breakfast: A charming guesthouse that is just a short walk from Kiloran Bay.
  • Colonsay Estate Cottages: Self-catering cottages located on the Colonsay Estate, which offer stunning views of the island.

What to Buy in Colonsay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some things to consider:

  • Colonsay Wool: Colonsay has its own flock of sheep, and you can buy locally produced wool and knitted goods.
  • Colonsay Gin: Take home a bottle of Colonsay Gin as a reminder of your trip.
  • Artwork: Local artists produce a range of artwork that is inspired by the island’s landscapes and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Colonsay

Colonsay has a unique culture that is influenced by its history and isolation. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Community Spirit: Colonsay has a strong sense of community, and locals are known for their friendliness and hospitality.
  • Traditional Ceilidh: Colonsay hosts a traditional Ceilidh every year where locals and visitors can gather for music and dancing.
  • Fishing and Farming: Colonsay has a rich history of fishing and farming, and these industries continue to play an important role in the island’s economy.

Best Time to Visit Colonsay

The best time to visit Colonsay is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, be prepared for the occasional rain shower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colonsay

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Colonsay:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the natural environment and clean up after yourself.
  • Explore the island’s natural beauty and history.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or damage any natural habitats.
  • Ignore local customs or traditions.
  • Leave any litter or waste on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colonsay

Colonsay is a safe place to visit, but be sure to take precautions when hiking or exploring the island’s rugged terrain. Always follow any safety guidelines provided by local tour operators or guides.

Health in Colonsay

Colonsay has a small medical facility that can provide basic medical services, but for serious health concerns, you may need to travel to the mainland.

Getting to Colonsay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Colonsay can be reached by ferry from Oban or Kennacraig, and there is also a small airport on the island that offers flights to and from Oban.

Bus Stations Near Colonsay

Other Options

You can also hire a car and drive to the ferry ports, or arrange a private transfer service.

How to Get Around in Colonsay

Colonsay is a small island, and the best way to get around is on foot, bike, or car. If you’re not comfortable driving, you can also hire a local taxi service.

Where to Go Next from Colonsay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Colonsay is surrounded by other beautiful islands, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are some options:

  • Iona: A small island known for its historic abbey and stunning scenery.
  • Mull: A large island with beautiful beaches and a vibrant arts scene.
  • Jura: A rugged island known for its whisky distillery and wildlife.

Conclusion

Colonsay is a hidden gem in Scotland that offers a rare glimpse into island life and a chance to escape from the hustle and bustle of modern society. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, Colonsay has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time on this picturesque island.
