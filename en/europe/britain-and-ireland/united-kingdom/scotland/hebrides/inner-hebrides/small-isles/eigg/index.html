Introduction to Eigg

If you’re searching for an off-the-beaten-path destination to explore in Scotland, look no further than Eigg. This stunning island is part of the Small Isles in the Inner Hebrides and boasts breathtaking coastal landscapes, fascinating cultural attractions, and ample opportunities for outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at what Eigg has to offer, from must-see sights and hiking trails to local cuisine and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Eigg

Whether you prefer exploring museums and historical sites or hiking through wild forests, there’s something for everyone in Eigg. Be sure to add these destinations to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Eigg History Society Museum: Learn about Eigg’s rich history through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • Kildonnan Museum: Discover the island’s ancient past and historical artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Eigg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sgurr of Eigg: The highest point on the island to trek, you will be rewarded with panoramic views that span across the sea onto the neighbouring isles.
  • Laig Bay: Stroll across the powdery white sands and appreciate the turquoise waters and sea stacks, this bay is also famous as a romantic setting in the movie “Breaking the Waves”.
  • Cliffs of Cleadale: Snap some Instagram-worthy photos at this stunning natural landmark.
  • Singing Sands: The beach is named after the unusual sound which is produced by the squeaky noise made when you walk on it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eigg

Hiking and biking are two of the best ways to enjoy Eigg’s breathtaking scenery and fresh air. Here are a few recommended routes:

  • The Eigg Circular Walk: A moderate hike, starting from Galmisdale pier, explores the north-west coasts up to Cleadale then over the hills to Singing Sands, and eventually diverges down to the pier of Laig Bay.
  • Kildonnan Bay Loop: A shorter 5 km walk beginning from the pier, passing through Kildonnan Bay and its castle ruins before returning to the port.

Best Things to Do in Eigg: Activities and Tours

While hiking and biking are popular activities in Eigg, there’s no shortage of other experiences to enjoy as well. Here are a few ideas:

  • Boat tours: Hire a local fishing boat or touring boat to discover the surrounding pristine waters of the Small Isles and Hebrides.
  • Wildlife watching: Spot seals, dolphins, eagles, and other native wildlife species in their natural habitats.
  • Cultural events: Attend a local festival like the Eigg Island community’s Music Festival which showcases renowned artists and performers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eigg

If you’re planning on spending a week on Eigg, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activities
1 Explore the history and culture of Eigg at the Eigg History Society Museum and Kildonnan Museum.
2 Take a day trip to Ardnamurchan Lighthouse, the westernmost tip of the British mainland.
3 Hike to the Sgurr of Eigg for breathtaking views of the island and its surroundings.
4 Join a boat tour to discover the Small Isles and watch dolphins and otters in their natural habitats.
5 Enjoy a relaxing day at Laig Bay and admire the stunning coastal scenery.
6 Participate in the Eigg Island community’s Music Festival during the summer months.
7 Experience local cuisine and beverages and purchase souvenirs to remember your trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Eigg

Eigg’s cuisine scene features fresh seafood and locally sourced ingredients in traditional Scottish dishes. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish made with minced lamb, oatmeal, and spices.
  • Cullen Skink: A soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream.
  • Black Pudding: A classic Scottish pudding made with pig’s blood and oatmeal served with breakfast items.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland’s most famous alcoholic beverage and a quintessential part of Scottish culture.
  • Beer: Isle of Eigg offers a craft brewery that serves locally produced beers, ales and ciders.

Where to Stay in Eigg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eigg has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget.

  • Bayview Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast provides breathtaking views of the island.
  • Pier Cottage: A stone cottage located in the heart of Eigg’s pier village.
  • Glebe Barn: Featuring small apartments, furnished suites, and kitchens, this accommodation is ideal for extended stays of a week or more.

What to Buy in Eigg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eigg has a selection of souvenirs and local products to take back home. These include:

  • Scottish Tartan Paint: A humorous souvenir which is perfect for your Scottish-themed home or desk.
  • Kildonnan Castaway: An official product made by the Eigg Castaway Restaurant in the shape of a bottle. Crabs may be preserved in brine and whiskey to be consumed later or gifted to others.
  • Handmade crafts: Eigg’s community members create handmade pottery, artwork, jewelry, and other crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Eigg

Eigg is a small island, so it’s important to respect the culture and customs of its inhabitants. Here are some local customs to be aware of:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites and residences.
  • Greet people you meet on the island, it is polite and friendly, but not too personal.
  • Be cautious of stray livestock and wildlife. Eigg’s natural environment may house a range of wild animals.
  • Avoid making too much noise or disturbing people during quiet hours.

Best Time to Visit Eigg

The island of Eigg is a year-round destination, but there are seasonal differences to consider when planning your visit. Summertime is popular for outdoor adventures and festivals, whereas winter is quieter but can still be picturesque. Mid-May to Mid-October is the recommended duration of travel.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eigg

Do:

  • Be respectful to locals and island culture in general.
  • Dress properly according to climate and visiting places on the island.
  • Try new things and explore the island.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the land and its surroundings especially in the natural environment is sensitive.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural surroundings and historical monuments and community buildings.
  • Rescue or approach marine wildlife as they are fragile and under special protection rules.
  • Flout COVID-19 restrictions by disregarding social distance rules or any other guidelines of the public safety measures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eigg

Eigg follows Scotland’s safety regulations to preserve public safety and health. Try to follow the safety instructions on any adventure activities or tours you join. For solo explorers or hiking lovers, it is suggested not to do hiking during bad weather, low visibility. Make sure to have proper protective gear and necessary equipment while exploring hills and cliffs.

Health in Eigg

The Eigg Health Centre is the island’s primary medical facility. There are also trained paramedics on the island. In case of an emergency, contact these sources of health administration.

Getting to Eigg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eigg

The nearest airport to Eigg is Glasgow Airport, which connects the island’s visitors to the mainland and the rest of Scotland.

Bus Stations Near Eigg

Hire a bus from Glasgow, then take a ferry ride that links Mallaig and Eigg to board in Lochboisdale, Barra to Eigg.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private helicopters or small planes to land on the island’s private airstrip.

How to Get Around in Eigg

Eigg is tiny and easy to explore. Visitors can explore the island’s best destinations easily on foot. Alternatively, visitors can hire bicycles or avail taxi cabs on the island.

Where to Go Next from Eigg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eigg is surrounded by a wide range of natural and cultural destinations, some of which include:

  • Isle of Skye: A Scottish island known for its rugged landscapes, fishing villages, and rich history.
  • Mallaig: Fishing town on the mainland which acts as the starting point for boat tours and ferries to Eigg.
  • Glenfinnan: This village is famous for its stunning scenery and historical Jacobite railway station.

Conclusion

Eigg is an island that provides the perfect blend of historical, cultural, and natural attractions ideal for solo travelers, couples, and families looking for an unforgettable vacation destination. By using this travel guide, you can plan your stay in Eigg and explore the best of its natural and historical landmarks, unique culture, and authentic cuisine.
