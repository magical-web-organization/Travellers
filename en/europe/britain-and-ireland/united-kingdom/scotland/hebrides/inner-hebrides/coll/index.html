Introduction to Coll

Coll is a stunning island located in the Inner Hebrides of Scotland, known for its long sandy beaches, clear turquoise waters, and diverse wildlife. With a population of around just 200 people, it’s the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy a quiet, peaceful vacation. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or simply lounging on the beach, Coll has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Coll

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Breacachadh Castle: A 14th-century castle ruins located on the southeastern coast of the island. It’s one of the most mysterious and picturesque sites on the island, surrounded by rugged cliffs and wild seas.

Natural Attractions of Coll: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fidden Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches on the island with crystal-clear turquoise waters and fine white sand. It’s perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and exploring the rock formations.
  • Bardowie Castle: A 16th-century castle located on the west coast of the island. It’s the perfect site for hikers, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coll

  • The RSPB Reserve: A nature reserve located on the northwestern side of the island, featuring stunning wildlife and an abundance of bird species.
  • Torr an Aba: A hill located on the eastern side of the island, offering a panoramic view of the island and surrounding waters.
  • Isle of Coll Circuit: An ideal hike for those looking to explore the island’s stunning landscapes, including breathtaking coastal views, crystal-clear streams, and rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in Coll: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and canoeing: A perfect way to explore the island’s natural beauty with the help of a friendly guide.
  • Whale watching: A unique experience to catch a glimpse of whales, dolphins, and other sea creatures around the island.
  • Shark diving: A thrilling opportunity to get up close and personal with one of the world’s most feared predators.

Food and Drink Scene of Coll

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coll lamb: A unique breed of lamb that’s raised on the island and is known for its distinctive taste and tender meat.
  • Fresh seafood: Oysters, scallops, and crab are all popular delicacies on the island.

Local Beverages

  • Coll gin: A locally produced gin that’s made using wildflowers and herbs from the island.
  • Whisky: Scotland’s ‘water of life’ is produced in the nearby islands and is the perfect beverage to enjoy alongside the warmth of the island’s cozy pubs.

Where to Stay in Coll: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Coll Hotel: Located close to the island’s harbor, this historic hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the sea.
  • Tigh na Mara Guesthouse: A warm and welcoming guesthouse situated on the edge of the RSPB Reserve.
  • Coll Bunkhouse: A budget-friendly option for travelers looking to explore the island without breaking the bank.

What to Buy in Coll: Souvenirs and Local Products

  • Handcrafted pottery: Local craftsmen create beautiful pieces using traditional techniques.
  • Jewelry: Unique pieces created using locally sourced materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Coll

Coll is an island rich in traditions and customs, with a strong focus on the importance of community. The island’s inhabitants are proud of their heritage, and they are always welcoming and friendly to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Coll

The best time to visit Coll is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the attractions and activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coll

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Support local businesses by eating, drinking, and shopping locally.
  • Take time to learn about Coll’s rich history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Disturb the wildlife in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coll

Coll is a safe place to visit, but take precautions when traveling alone or at night. It’s also important to be mindful of natural dangers, such as strong currents in the sea or dangerous cliffs.

Health in Coll

Coll has a limited medical center and pharmacy, so it’s important to bring any essential medications with you.

Getting to Coll: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coll

The closest airports to Coll are Glasgow, Inverness and Edinburgh. From here, you can take a regional flight to the nearby island of Tiree, followed by a ferry to Coll.

Bus Stations Near Coll

There are no direct bus routes to Coll from the mainland. However, you can take a bus to Oban or Glasgow and then take a ferry to Coll.

Other Options

Driving to Coll is not recommended, as there are no road bridges to the island. The only other option is to take a ferry from the nearby islands of Tiree or Mull.

How to Get Around in Coll

The best way to get around Coll is by bike, as the roads are quiet and there are several bike rental options available on the island. Alternatively, you can also explore the island on foot, or by car if you bring one on the ferry.

Where to Go Next from Coll: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coll is located in close proximity to several other stunning islands in the Inner Hebrides, such as Tiree, Mull and Iona. Each of these islands offers its own unique attractions and experiences, from breathtaking beaches to ancient religious landmarks.

Conclusion

Coll is a stunning and peaceful destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in hiking, beach lounging, or immersing yourself in the local culture, Coll is the perfect place to escape city life and enjoy a slower pace of vacation.
