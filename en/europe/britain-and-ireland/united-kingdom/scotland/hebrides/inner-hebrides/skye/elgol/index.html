Introduction to Elgol

Elgol is a small coastal village located on the Isle of Skye, which is part of the Inner Hebrides in Scotland. It boasts some of the most picturesque natural landscapes in the world, and offers visitors a unique blend of outdoor adventures and cultural attractions. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff or simply seeking a peaceful getaway, Elgol has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Elgol

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dunvegan Castle: Dating back to the 13th century, Dunvegan Castle is one of the oldest continuously inhabited castles in Scotland and serves as the ancestral home of the Clan MacLeod.
  • Talisker Distillery: Located in Carbost, about 30 minutes from Elgol, Talisker Distillery is one of the oldest distilleries in Scotland. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the history of whisky production and sample some of the finest single malt whiskies in the world.
  • Armadale Castle & Gardens: Situated on the southern end of Skye, Armadale Castle & Gardens is a 20,000-acre estate that includes a 19th-century mansion, museum, gardens, and nature trails.

Natural Attractions of Elgol: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loch Coruisk: Located at the base of the Cuillin Mountains, Loch Coruisk is a freshwater loch that is accessible only by boat or hiking. Visitors can take a boat tour to enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding scenery or enjoy a hike through the picturesque mountain trails.
  • The Quiraing: A natural landslip that spans over 6km, The Quiraing boasts otherworldly geological formations, including towering rock pinnacles and twisting paths. Visitors can take a hike to explore this stunning landscape.
  • Neist Point Lighthouse: Located on the most westerly tip of Skye, Neist Point Lighthouse offers panoramic views of the nearby cliffs and coastline. Visitors can take in the scenery on a leisurely walk or indulge in some birdwatching while enjoying a picnic in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elgol

Elgol is home to some of Scotland’s most remarkable hiking and biking routes, many of which lead to exceptional scenic views of the surrounding mountains and coastline. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Cuillin Ridge Traverse: A challenging and technical trail, Cuillin Ridge Traverse offers stunning views of the Skye’s varied mountain landscapes. The trail is over 12 km in length and requires hikers to traverse large boulders and rocky terrain.
  • The Fairy Pools: A beginner-friendly hike, The Fairy Pools traverse through natural waterfalls, rock formations and pools, before reaching a stunning view of the Cuillin Mountains.
  • Sligachan to Old Inn Walk: A gentle, atmospheric walk taking visitors through the majestic vistas of rolling hills and historic landmarks of the area.

Best Things to Do in Elgol: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of Loch Coruisk: Visitors can take a boat tour of Loch Coruisk, which departs from Elgol. Through this trip, visitors can explore the serene waters, stunning landscapes and rugged mountain peaks of the surrounding.
  • Wildlife Watching at Kylerhea: Located on the southern coast of Skye, Kylerhea is a prime spot for observing Scotland’s wildlife, like otters, seals, dolphins and even whales. Visitors can take a wildlife tour or go for a walk through the surrounding nature reserves.
  • Shellfish Gathering: Visitors get the opportunity to learn about shellfish gathering in Elgol, a unique activity that focuses on mussel, razor clam and king scallop, in the peaceful waters of Loch Eishort.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elgol

Day 1: Arrival in Elgol, exploring the village, and a leisurely beachside stroll. Day 2: Boat Tour of Loch Coruisk, hiking through the surrounding landscape of Cuillin Mountains. Day 3: Visiting The Quiraing and Neist Point Lighthouse and relaxing at the nearby silver-sand beach. Day 4: A tour of Talisker Distillery or visit to the nearby Carbost village, the home of the famous Summer Isles Food cooperative. Day 5: Wildlife watching at Kylerhea or exploring the outstanding nature reserves and hiking trails in the area. Day 6: Enjoy a cozy day at the village or check out the Armadale Castle and Gardens. Day 7: Depart from Elgol and return home.

Food and Drink Scene of Elgol

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish, haggis is made of sheep’s internal organs mixed with oatmeal and spices. Many local restaurants serve this specialty dish in various forms.
  • Cullen Skink: A thick soup made of smoked haddock, potatoes and onions, Cullen Skink is a traditional seafood dish that is found throughout Scottish eateries.
  • Cranachan: A dessert made of whipped cream, raspberries, whiskey and oatmeal, Cranachan is a traditional Scottish dessert that is a must-try for those with a sweet-tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland is known worldwide for the quality of its single malt whisky, and Elgol offers ample opportunities to sample some of the finest whiskies produced in the country.
  • Ale: Scotland has a booming brewing scene, and many local establishments offer a variety of craft ales that feature flavors unique to the region.

Where to Stay in Elgol: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Elgol Beach House: A cozy beachside guesthouse, Elgol Beach house offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, modern amenities and an eco-friendly atmosphere.
  • Skye Basecamp: A hostelling experience for outdoor enthusiasts, the Skye Basecamp offers budget-friendly options, with comfortable bunk-bed accommodations, and outdoor kitchen facilities.
  • Cuillin Hills Hotel: An elegant and upscale hotel located in Portree, offering luxurious accommodations, swimming pool, gourmet restaurant, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Elgol: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tartan Goods: Scotland is famous for tartans and offers many local stores selling made-to-order tartans, from individual kilts to full clan tartan regalia.
  • Scottish Whisky: As the spirits of Scotland, whisky is the perfect souvenir to take home to commemorate your trip to Elgol.
  • Handcrafted Glass and Ceramics: The Isle of Skye is an area that is highly valued for its arts and crafts, visitors can find locally made glass and ceramics reflecting the beauty of the surrounding landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Elgol

Elgol has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the Gaelic language and the island’s ancient Norse history. Visitors will find that the people of Elgol are friendly and welcoming, and many still participate in traditional forms of music, poetry, and folklore. Visitors should always respect local customs and traditions and open themselves up to learning more about the rich cultural heritage of this area.

Best Time to Visit Elgol

The best time to visit Elgol is during the summer months (May to September), when the temperature is mild, and the days are long, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring. In winter, Elgol experiences harsh winds, heavy rain, and snow, making it less conducive for touring and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elgol

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Explore the outdoors, take advantage of the natural beauty of the area.
  • Keep the environment clean and tidy.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in public areas or trails.
  • Disturb local wildlife or damage ecosystems.
  • Ignore safety precautions while undertaking outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elgol

Elgol is a safe area, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should always be vigilant, take necessary personal precautions, and protect their belongings from opportunistic thieves.

Health in Elgol

The National Health Service provides healthcare service in Scotland, and Skye has a local hospital. Visitors should be insured and carry their EHIC cards if traveling from EU countries or apply for travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Elgol: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elgol

The nearest airport to Elgol is Inverness Airport, which is 150 km away. Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Aberdeen airports are also options for visitors, from where one can connect to Elgol via road transport.

Bus Stations Near Elgol

Public transportation is scarce and limited to buses operating between Portree and Broadford. However, renting a car is recommended if driving a car is permitted on a visitor’s license.

Other Options

Ferry services operate from Mallaig and Armadale, which connect the Isle of Skye to the mainland. Visitors can opt to drive from Glasgow, Edinburgh, or Inverness to reach the island and Elgol.

How to Get Around in Elgol

The area is best explored on foot, and visitors can also rent bikes from local establishments. Car rentals are another popular option for travelers to explore the Isle of Skye and Elgol’s surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Elgol: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portree: The largest town in Skye, Portree is a bustling town with a vibrant food and beverage scene, fascinating history and abundant natural beauty.
  • Kyle of Lochalsh: A picturesque village, Kyle of Lochalsh is home to several attractions like Eilean Donan Castle, Plockton village, and the Isle of Skye’s Skye Bridge.
  • Glenelg: A popular location due to the availability of historical landmarks like the famous Iron Age Brochs and one of the highest cliffs in the British Isles.

Conclusion

Elgol is a must-visit destination for those seeking peace, natural beauty, history, and culture. This comprehensive guide provides all the necessary information to make your trip to this stunning part of Scotland memorable and enjoyable.
