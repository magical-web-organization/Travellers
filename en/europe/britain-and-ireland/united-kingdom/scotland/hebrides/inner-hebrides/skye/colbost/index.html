Introduction to Colbost

Nestled in the heart of Skye, Colbost is a picturesque village that offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. This quiet destination is known for its breathtaking scenery, rich history, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re looking to embark on an outdoor adventure, indulge in local cuisine, or simply relax and soak up the stunning landscapes, Colbost is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Colbost

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The village of Colbost is steeped in history and culture. Some of the top cultural attractions to explore are:

  • Dunvegan Castle: One of the most iconic castles in Scotland, Dunvegan Castle is steeped in history and legend. This impressive fortress has been the ancestral home of the Clan MacLeod for over 800 years.
  • Museum of Island Life: Situated just a short drive from Colbost, the Museum of Island Life provides a fascinating glimpse into life on the Hebrides over a century ago.
  • Neist Point: A dramatic cliff-top vista on the westernmost tip of Skye, Neist Point Lighthouse is an impressive sight to behold. Explore the surrounding hiking trails and breathtaking views before reaching the lighthouse.

Natural Attractions of Colbost: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Colbost’s natural beauty is truly breathtaking and boasts a wealth of opportunities for adventure and exploration. Here are some top natural attractions:

  • The Quiraing: Located nearby, The Quiraing is a must-visit for any nature lover. The magnificent natural wonder is one of the most stunning landscapes on Skye.
  • Talisker Distillery: Take a tour of the iconic Talisker Distillery and enjoy the beautiful scenery on route.
  • Loch Dunvegan: Enjoy a boat ride in the serene waters of Loch Dunvegan and experience its natural beauty, complete with the stunning backdrop of Dunvegan Castle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colbost

Colbost and its surrounding area offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Scotland. Here are some of the best routes for you to explore:

  • Old Man of Storr: Located just 25 minutes from Colbost, the Old Man of Storr offers one of the most challenging hiking routes in Skye. This path winds its way up the steep cliffs and provides stunning panoramic views at the summit.
  • The Fairy Glen: Located in the north of Skye, the Fairy Glen offers a charming and mystical setting for a hike. With rolling hills, miniature waterfalls, and twisted trees, it is a truly magical place.
  • Cycling Route: For those who prefer biking, there is a stunning cycle route that meanders through lakes and forests and past stunning Highland landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Colbost: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor activities or cultural attractions, Colbost has something to suit everyone’s tastes. Here are some top things to do in the area:

  • Sea Kayaking: Enjoy a thrilling kayaking excursion in the calm waters of Loch Dunvegan with a local guide.
  • Wildlife Watching: Take a wildlife watching boat tour to spot whales, dolphins, seals, and seabirds around Skye and the surrounding islands.
  • Sightseeing Tours: Explore the beautiful countryside with a local guide, who will show you the hidden gems of the area from ancient ruins to mountain vistas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colbost

Here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time in Colbost:

Day 1: Arrival in Colbost and Relaxation

After arriving in Colbost, take a stroll around the village to soak up the peaceful atmosphere. Head to the local pub to sample some of the region’s finest ales and relax before the next day’s adventures.

Day 2: History and Culture in Colbost

Explore the area’s rich history and culture with visits to Dunvegan Castle, the Museum of Island Life, and Neist Point.

Day 3: Natural Wonders in Skye

Embark on a hiking adventure to the Old Man of Storr and see the stunning landscape of The Quiraing. End the day with a visit to the Talisker Distillery.

Day 4: Outdoor Activities

Enjoy a sea kayaking excursion in Loch Dunvegan or take a wildlife watching tour to spot the local animals in their natural habitat.

Day 5: Scenic Views

Take a scenic drive around Skye, stopping at picturesque villages and towns, such as Portree and Dunvegan.

Day 6: Local Life

Experience the everyday life of locals with a sightseeing tour through the countryside.

Day 7: Departure from Colbost

Wrap up your trip with a hearty breakfast and perhaps a visit to the local shops to pick up some souvenirs before departing Colbost.

Food and Drink Scene of Colbost

The traditional cuisine in Colbost is rich in fresh seafood, game, and local produce. Here are some must-try dishes on a visit to the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen skink: A classic Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream.
  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs with onions, spices, and oats.
  • Cranachan: A dessert made of whipped cream, honey, whisky, and raspberries.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Talisker Distillery in nearby Carbost is a must-visit for any whisky lover. Enjoy a tasting tour of some of Scotland’s finest whisky.
  • Local Beer: The island is home to several microbreweries, such as Isle of Skye Brewing Co. and Cuillin Brewery, providing an array of delicious local beers.

Where to Stay in Colbost: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For those looking to stay nearby, there are several accommodation options in Colbost and the surrounding area. Here are some top choices:

  • The Three Chimneys: A luxury guesthouse with a Michelin-starred restaurant and stunning views of Loch Dunvegan.
  • The Pier House: A cozy guesthouse overlooking the harbor in nearby Portree.
  • Portree Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Portree.

What to Buy in Colbost: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several local products to take home as souvenirs, such as:

  • Tartan: Skye has its own tartan, and there are several shops where visitors can buy tartan souvenirs, such as kilts, scarves, and blankets.
  • Whisky: Visitors can pick up a bottle of Talisker Distillery’s famous whisky to take home.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local markets offer handmade crafts from local artisans, including pottery, knitting, and woven baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Colbost

The locals in Colbost are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Some of the local customs include:

  • Ceilidhs: Traditional Scottish dances where locals gather to enjoy music, dance, and refreshments.
  • Gaelic Language: The local language of Skye is Gaelic, and visitors may hear it spoken throughout the area.
  • Hiking and Biking: Outdoor activities are an integral part of local life, with many locals spending their weekends on hiking and biking trails.

Best Time to Visit Colbost

The best time to visit Colbost is from May to September when the weather is warmer, and the days are longer. The area is busiest during July and August, making it the perfect time for those seeking a livelier atmosphere. However, the area is still beautiful in its off-season from October to April, albeit with colder weather and shorter days.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colbost

Colbost is a relatively safe area, but some precautions are still advisable, such as:

  • Wear appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Inform someone of your hiking or biking routes.
  • Take care around cliffs and waterways.

Health in Colbost

Visitors need not take specific health precautions before traveling to Colbost. Still, it is advisable to carry out basic hygiene measures such as washing your hands frequently to avoid the spread of illnesses.

Getting to Colbost: Transportation Options and Logistics

Colbost is located in relatively remote region of Scotland, but there are several transportation options for visitors:

Airports Near Colbost

The closest airport to Colbost is Inverness Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car and drive to Colbost, or take a bus to Portree (where they can then take a taxi to Colbost).

Bus Stations Near Colbost

Visitors can take a bus from Glasgow to Portree and then take a taxi to Colbost. Alternatively, they can take a bus from Inverness to Portree and then take a taxi to Colbost.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to travel by car can follow the scenic route to Colbost via the Isle of Skye bridge.

How to Get Around in Colbost

Visitors can rent a car from any of the major car rental companies located across the UK. Alternatively, visitors can opt for guided tours or take public transportation, such as buses, to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Colbost: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can visit, including:

  • The Cairngorms: A stunning national park located in the Scottish Highlands, known for its gorgeous hiking trails, wildlife, and stunning views.
  • Isle of Lewis: A remote island located northwest of Skye, known for its ancient history and beautiful landscapes.
  • West Highlands: Home to some of Scotland’s most iconic scenery, including Glencoe, Ben Nevis, and Loch Lomond.

Conclusion

Colbost is a hidden gem in Scotland. Its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality make it an ideal destination for visitors looking to explore Scotland’s natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to embark on outdoor adventures, indulge in local cuisine, or simply relax and soak up the stunning views, Colbost is a truly special destination.
