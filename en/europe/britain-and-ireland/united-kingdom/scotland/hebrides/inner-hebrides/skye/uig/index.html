Introduction to Uig

Located in Skye, in the Inner Hebrides of Scotland, Uig boasts stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural history, and a charming food and drink scene. The area is dotted with rugged coastlines, cascading waterfalls, and picturesque mountains. It’s a place where travelers come to explore the rugged Scottish wilderness and immerse themselves in the cultural heritage of the region. This comprehensive Uig travel guide will help you plan your trip to this remote corner of Scotland.

List of Places to Visit in Uig

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Skye Museum of Island Life: A living museum that gives visitors a glimpse into the traditional way of life on Skye, including crofting, fishing, and weaving.
  • Dunvegan Castle and Gardens: The ancestral home of the clan MacLeod, this 13th-century castle is rich in history and offers guided tours of its impressive interior.
  • Fairy Glen: A mystical and enchanting glen, believed by locals to be home to fairies and magical creatures.

Natural Attractions of Uig: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quiraing: A spectacular landscape of rock formations and winding paths that offers panoramic views of the sea and mountains.
  • The Old Man of Storr: A towering rock formation that offers a challenging hike and stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Uig Bay: A picturesque seaside village that offers boating and fishing opportunities, as well as breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uig

  • The Skye Trail: A challenging and rewarding long-distance hiking route that takes you through some of the most picturesque parts of Skye, including the Cuillin Mountains and the Trotternish Ridge.
  • Trotternish Ridge: A scenic ridge that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and a challenging hike for experienced hikers.
  • Uig Woodland Walk: A shorter, family-friendly hiking trail that meanders through a beautiful woodland and offers stunning views of the bay and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Uig: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Watching: Take a boat tour to spot seals, dolphins, and whales in the surrounding waters.
  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the coastline of Uig and nearby islands from the water.
  • Horse Riding: Take a guided horseback ride through the stunning countryside of Skye.

Food and Drink Scene of Uig

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made of sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with onions, spices, and oats, then boiled in the sheep’s stomach.
  • Cullen Skink: A creamy Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, milk, potatoes, and onions.
  • Cranachan: A Scottish dessert made with whipped cream, whisky, honey, and toasted oats, topped with fresh raspberries.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland is famous for its whisky, and Skye offers a variety of whisky distilleries to tour and taste.
  • Real Ale: The local pubs in Uig often serve locally brewed ale, including the popular Isle of Skye Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Uig: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Uig Hotel: A charming hotel overlooking the bay with comfortable rooms and a locally sourced restaurant.
  • Uig Lodge: A historic lodge offering cozy rooms and a traditional Scottish breakfast.
  • Misty Glen Fingal Centre: A budget-friendly hostel with shared dorm rooms and kitchen facilities.

What to Buy in Uig: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Harris Tweed: A locally produced, handwoven woolen fabric that is used to make clothing and home goods.
  • Whisky: The whisky distilleries of Skye offer a variety of unique and flavorful whiskies to bring home.
  • Local Artwork: Many artists call Skye home, and their beautiful works can be found in local galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Uig

  • Language: Scottish Gaelic is still spoken in many parts of Skye, and visitors can listen to traditional music sung in Gaelic.
  • Highland Games: These annual sporting events are held throughout Scotland and offer a glimpse into traditional Scottish culture, including bagpipes, kilts, and heavy athletic competitions.
  • Folklore: Scots have a rich oral tradition filled with stories of fairies, elves, and other mythical creatures. Visitors can hear these tales at storytelling events around Skye.

Best Time to Visit Uig

The best time to visit Uig is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, visitors should be prepared for rain and pack warm clothing, as the weather in Scotland can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uig

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Skye by hiking, biking, or kayaking.
  • Immerse yourself in the local culture and try traditional Scottish dishes and whisky.
  • Take part in local festivities and events.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage or litter in the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Ignore warning signs or venture too close to the edge of cliffs or other dangerous areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uig

Uig is generally a safe area, but visitors should exercise caution when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. Always wear appropriate footwear and clothing and stay alert to changes in weather or ground conditions.

Health in Uig

Visitors should be prepared for changes in climate and weather and should pack appropriate clothing and medicines to address any health concerns. Emergency medical care is available in nearby hospitals.

Getting to Uig: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uig

The nearest airports to Uig are located in Inverness, Glasgow, and Edinburgh. Visitors can then rent a car or take public transportation to Uig.

Bus Stations Near Uig

The main bus station near Uig is located in Portree, which is about 15 miles south of Uig. Local buses also travel to other parts of Skye.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Uig by taking the ferry from the mainland port of Mallaig. The ferry dock is located in the center of Uig village.

How to Get Around in Uig

The best way to get around Uig and the surrounding area is by car or hire a taxi. Rentals are available at the nearby airports and in the nearby town of Portree.

Where to Go Next from Uig: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Isle of Harris: A scenic island located just south of Skye, known for its stunning beaches and traditional Scottish culture.
  • Eilean Donan Castle: A famous and well-preserved Scottish castle located just over an hour’s drive from Uig.
  • Loch Ness: A large, deep freshwater loch famous for the legend of the Loch Ness Monster.

Conclusion

Uig offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience of Scotland’s rich heritage and natural beauty. From hiking in the rugged mountains to enjoying traditional Scottish food and drink, there is something for everyone in this remote corner of Skye. Whether you are an outdoor adventurer or a culture lover, Uig is a destination that should not be overlooked.
