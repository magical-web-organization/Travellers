Raasay is a small island located in the Inner Hebrides of Scotland that can be easily overlooked. However, those who make the trip will be rewarded with stunning landscapes, rich history and culture, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide to Raasay, we will explore the top attractions, hiking routes, activities, and more. You will also discover valuable information about the local culture, customs, safety, transportation, and where to stay and eat.

Introduction to Raasay

Raasay is a scenic and unspoiled island in the Inner Hebrides that offers visitors the perfect opportunity to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and connect with nature. The island features a rugged coastline, rolling hills, and tranquil lochs, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, photographers, and hikers. The island is also rich in history and culture, with Gaelic heritage that is still evident today.

List of Places to Visit in Raasay

Before exploring the top places to visit in Raasay, it is important to note that the island is small and can easily be explored in a day or two. However, for those who wish to take their time, there is plenty to discover. Here are the top places to visit in Raasay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Raasay House: This grand mansion was once the summer home of the Macleod clan and now serves as an activity center and restaurant. Guests can enjoy guided tours of the house and grounds, or participate in activities such as archery, canoeing, and mountain biking.
  • Calum’s Road: This historic road was built by a single man, Calum MacLeod, over the course of a decade. The road provides stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape and is a testament to the resilience and determination of the people of Raasay.
  • Raasay Heritage Centre: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the island’s history and culture, with exhibits on the geology, archaeology, and industry of Raasay. Visitors can also learn about the Gaelic language and the island’s literary heritage.

Natural Attractions of Raasay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dun Caan: This mountain is the highest point on the island and offers sweeping views of the surrounding landscape. The hike to the top of Dun Caan is challenging, but well worth the effort.
  • Inverarish Forest: This forest is located in the heart of Raasay and offers visitors a chance to explore the island’s native flora and fauna. Visitors can hike along the forest trails, go bird watching, or simply enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Loch na Mna: This tranquil loch is surrounded by breathtaking scenery and offers visitors the opportunity to fish, swim, or simply relax and enjoy the view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Raasay

For those who love hiking and biking, Raasay offers a variety of trails that cater to all skill levels. From easy walks along the coast to challenging treks up mountains, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the top hiking and biking trails in Raasay:

  • Dun Caan Circular Trail: This 7-mile trail takes hikers to the summit of Dun Caan and provides stunning views of the island.
  • Raasay House Trails: Raasay House offers a variety of trails that wind through the forest and along the coast, including a 2.5-mile circular route that starts and ends at the house.
  • Inverarish Forest Trails: This forest features a variety of trails that range in length and difficulty, including the Iron Mine Trail, which takes hikers through the ruins of an old iron mine.

Best Things to Do in Raasay: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Raasay. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Kayaking: Raasay’s coast is perfect for kayaking and provides visitors with a chance to explore sea caves, spot wildlife, and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Fishing: Loch na Mna and other bodies of water on the island offer excellent fishing opportunities for those who enjoy angling.
  • Boat tours: Visitors can take a boat tour around the coast of Raasay, where they can see seals, dolphins, and whales, as well as the island’s beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Raasay

Raasay is home to some fantastic restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer visitors a taste of the local cuisine and drink. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: This traditional Scottish soup is made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: This Scottish delicacy is a savory pudding made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with oats, suet, and spices.
  • Black Pudding: This sausage-like dish is made from pork blood, oatmeal, and spices and is a popular breakfast food.

Local Beverages

  • Isle of Raasay Distillery: This distillery produces award-winning whiskey and gin that is made using locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Gaelic Ale: This beer is brewed using traditional Scottish techniques and features a rich, malty flavor.
  • Scotch Whiskey: No trip to Scotland would be complete without trying some of the country’s famous whiskey, and Raasay is no exception.

Where to Stay in Raasay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Raasay is a small island, but there are still a good variety of accommodation options available. Visitors can choose from hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, all of which offer comfortable rooms and a warm welcome. Here are some of the top places to stay in Raasay:

  • Raasay House: This historic mansion has been converted into a hotel and activity center and offers guests a unique and luxurious experience.
  • Gleann an Ronnaich Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside and a friendly and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Raasay Gallery & Guesthouse: This guesthouse features comfortable rooms and a delightful restaurant that serves locally-sourced food.

What to Buy in Raasay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those who want to take a little piece of Raasay home with them, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some of the best things to buy in Raasay:

  • Whiskey and Gin: The Isle of Raasay Distillery produces whiskey and gin that make a perfect gift for anyone who loves a good tipple.
  • Crafts and Artwork: Raasay is home to a thriving arts scene, and visitors can find a variety of crafts and artwork available for purchase.
  • Local Products: From smoked salmon to handmade soaps, there are plenty of locally-made products that showcase the best of Raasay.

Local Culture and Customs of Raasay

Raasay has a unique and rich culture that is still evident today. Visitors can expect to hear the Gaelic language spoken, see traditional music and dance performances, and learn about the island’s history and traditions. Visitors should also be aware of the local customs and etiquette, such as dressing appropriately and respecting local traditions and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Raasay

The best time to visit Raasay is between May and September when the weather is mild, and the island is alive with activity and events. However, visitors should be aware that the weather in Scotland can be unpredictable, so it is always best to bring warm clothing and rain gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Raasay

Raasay is a safe destination for visitors, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Visitors should always follow safety warnings and advice, particularly when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Raasay

Visitors should be aware that medical services on the island are limited, so it is always wise to bring any necessary medication or medical supplies with you. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of tick bites, which can lead to Lyme disease, and take precautions to avoid them.

Getting to Raasay: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to reach Raasay is by ferry from the nearby town of Sconser on the Isle of Skye. The ferry runs regularly and takes around 20 minutes. Visitors can also reach Raasay by taking a ferry from the town of Kyle of Lochalsh. There is no airport on Raasay, but visitors can fly into nearby airports on the Isle of Skye or in Inverness.

Airports Near Raasay

  • Inverness Airport: This airport is located 70 miles from Raasay and offers connecting flights to major cities in the UK and Europe.
  • Isle of Skye Airport: This airport is located on the nearby Isle of Skye and offers flights to Glasgow and other cities in Scotland.

Bus Stations Near Raasay

There are no bus stations on Raasay, but visitors can take a bus from nearby towns such as Portree on the Isle of Skye or Kyle of Lochalsh.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Raasay by car, either by driving from the nearby towns or by renting a car and taking it on the ferry. It is also possible to cycle to Raasay, either from Skye or from the mainland via the Skye Bridge.

How to Get Around in Raasay

Raasay is a small island that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a car from the nearby towns or use one of the local taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Raasay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the beauty and culture of Raasay, visitors can continue their adventure by exploring nearby destinations such as the Isle of Skye, the scenic village of Plockton, or the bustling city of Inverness.

Conclusion

Raasay is a hidden gem of Scotland that offers visitors a chance to connect with nature, explore the rich history and culture, and enjoy a peaceful and relaxing holiday. Whether you come for the hiking, the whiskey, or the stunning scenery, Raasay is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories.
