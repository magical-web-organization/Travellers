Introduction to Stornoway

Stornoway is a bustling harbor town located in Lewis, in Outer Hebrides, in Hebrides, in Scotland, in United Kingdom, in Britain and Ireland, in Europe. With its rich history and stunning landscapes, Stornoway has become a popular tourist destination for people who want to experience the very best of what Scotland has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Stornoway

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stornoway is home to numerous cultural and historical landmarks that will take visitors back in time. Below are some of the must-visit places in the town:

  • Lews Castle Museum and Archive: This historical castle overlooking Stornoway Harbor was built in the mid-19th century. It currently holds archives and exhibitions of Stornoway’s rich history and culture. The museum displays an extensive collection of artifacts on the island’s history, language, and traditions.

  • Stornoway Town Hall: This 19th-century neo-Gothic courthouse was converted into a municipal building in the 1970s. The hall is famous for its beautiful murals and the large stained-glass window that honors three Scottish legends.

  • An Lanntair Arts Centre: This arts centre is renowned for its public art exhibitions, music concerts, and theatre productions. An Lanntair has regular art exhibitions and is located right in the center of town.

Natural Attractions of Stornoway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the cultural landmarks, Stornoway offers natural attractions that are just as breathtaking. The Isle of Lewis boasts of unspoiled beaches, rugged coastline, mountainous terrains, and wildlife sightings. The following are some of the places to visit:

  • Callanish Standing Stones: These impressive ancient standing stones are predating Stonehenge. Visitors can take sightseeing tours or appreciate the stones on foot.

  • The Blackhouse: The Blackhouse is a museum that reflects the island’s traditional way of life, offering insight into the living conditions before modernization.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Stornoway

There are many hiking/biking trails in Stornoway perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. The terrain varies, offering something for everyone, from a leisurely stroll to a challenging hike that rewards with unparalleled views of the island. Some of the best routes include:

  • The West Side Coastal Walk: This 16-mile walk is a great way to explore the Isle of Lewis’s beautiful coastline and its magnificent beaches.

  • North Lewis: This mountainous area is known for its challenging hikes and scenic views. Visitors can try out Tiumpan Head or Clisham.

Best Things to Do in Stornoway: Activities and Tours

Stornoway has a range of activities that will cater to everyone’s interests, making it a perfect destination for families and solo travelers.

Some of the best things to do in Stornoway include:

  • Taking a boat trip to the uninhabited island of Shiant Islands. Visitors can look out for rare bird species and explore the island’s great natural beauty.

  • Biking around the Isle of Lewis. Cycling is a great way to explore the island’s natural beauty at a leisurely pace.

  • Sampling the local whiskies on offer. Stornoway has several distilleries that offer tours, allowing visitors to discover the secrets of traditional whisky making in Scotland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stornoway

  • Day 1: Arrive in Stornoway and visit Lews Castle Museum and Archive, followed by a walk along the harbor.

  • Day 2: Take a day trip to the Callanish Standing Stones, followed by a visit to The Blackhouse museum.

  • Day 3: Visit Stornoway Town Hall and An Lanntair Arts Centre. Take a walk around the town to admire the architecture and traditional Scottish buildings.

  • Day 4: Go hiking or biking around North Lewis.

  • Day 5: Take a boat trip to the Shiant Islands. Enjoy a picnic and take in the natural beauty of the uninhabited island.

  • Day 6: Sample the local whiskies on offer at a nearby distillery.

  • Day 7: Relax and admire the scenery at one of the secluded beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Stornoway

Must-Try Dishes

Stornoway is famous for its seafood delicacies and offers a wide range of dishes to choose from. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Fish and chips: This iconic British dish can be found in various outlets around Stornoway.

  • Smoked salmon: Stornoway is famous for its smoked salmon, handcrafted by locals in traditional smokehouses.

  • Stornoway black pudding: A tasty dish made of pork, oatmeal, and spices.

Local Beverages

Stornoway has a long history of producing some of the best whiskies in Scotland. Local whiskies are made using traditional methods, many of which are unique to the island. Local ales are also available and are a great way to unwind and enjoy a drink after a busy day.

Where to Stay in Stornoway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stornoway has several accommodation options for visitors, with something to suit all budgets. From luxury hotels to budget hostels, Stornoway has a range of places to stay.

Here are some of the best accommodation options in Stornoway:

  • The Royal Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of Stornoway and has stunning views of the harbor.

  • The Crown Inn: The Crown Inn is a traditional Scottish inn located on the main street in Stornoway. Rooms are spacious and elegantly furnished.

  • Stornoway Hostel: Stornoway hostel is a budget-friendly option located in the town center.

What to Buy in Stornoway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Stornoway offers several souvenirs and local products that are unique to the island. Visitors can buy souvenirs like hand-knitted sweaters, tweed jackets, scarves, and local whiskey.

Local Culture and Customs of Stornoway

Stornoway has a rich cultural heritage that has been passed down since ancient times. Residents celebrate numerous cultural events and festivals, including the HebCelt Fest, which is held annually in July.

The town is known for its Gaelic language and traditional music, and visitors can attend ceilidhs, where they can learn traditional dances and enjoy songs played by local musicians.

Best Time to Visit Stornoway

The best time to visit Stornoway is between May and September when the weather is mild and days are longer. Visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty of the Isle of Lewis, experience local festivals, and take part in outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stornoway

Do:

  • Be respectful of the island’s culture and customs.

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the island and explore its history and culture.

  • Try the local delicacies and sample the local whiskies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave rubbish behind when exploring the island.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

  • Disrespect the island’s wildlife or disturb the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stornoway

Stornoway is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take care of their personal belongings and avoid walking in secluded areas at night.

Health in Stornoway

Stornoway has a local hospital and several medical facilities, ensuring visitors receive medical care when required. It is advisable to have health insurance while traveling in Stornoway.

Getting to Stornoway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stornoway

The town has a small airport, Stornoway Airport, offering frequent flights to and from major cities in the UK, including Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Inverness.

Bus Stations Near Stornoway

Citylink coaches offer services to Stornoway from other Scottish cities, including Inverness and Glasgow.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Stornoway via ferry from the mainland. The ferry service to Stornoway departs from Ullapool and takes approximately 2.5 hours.

How to Get Around in Stornoway

Taxis, buses, and rental cars are available in Stornoway. However, visitors can also get around the town on foot and explore the town at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Stornoway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stornoway is surrounded by other stunning destinations in the Isle of Lewis that visitors can explore, including:

  • The Butt of Lewis: The northernmost point in Lewis boasts of a striking lighthouse and offers stunning views of the rugged coastline.

  • The Harris Tweed Company: The Harris Tweed Company is located in Tarbert and produces some of the finest Scottish tweed for jackets, coats and hats.

  • The Blackhouse Village: Visitors can see how people lived in Stornoway before modernization and see the traditional houses made from stones and thatched roofs.

Conclusion

Stornoway is a charming harbor town that is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. With several accommodation and transportation options, Stornoway is a perfect destination for all types of travelers. Whether visitors want to explore the cultural landmarks, go on wildlife tours, or relax on the beaches, Stornoway’s diverse offerings are sure to satisfy even the most discerning of travelers.
