Introduction to Great Bernera

Great Bernera, located in the Outer Hebrides of Scotland, is a hidden gem that offers breathtaking scenery and rich culture. Spanning over 21 square miles, this small island boasts a population of only 260 people and is a haven for those who seek relaxation, adventure, and stunning landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Great Bernera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bosta Beach Iron Age House - This reconstructed Iron Age House offers a glimpse into the past of the island’s inhabitants.

  • Breacleit Cairn - Visit this ancient burial site and explore the rich history of the island’s inhabitants.

  • Bernera Museum - Discover the island’s maritime heritage, crofting way of life, and the famous Bernera Tapestry at this fascinating museum.

Natural Attractions of Great Bernera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loch Bharabhat - Take a dip in this picturesque freshwater loch, surrounded by stunning moorland and hills.

  • Dun Carloway Broch - This well-preserved Iron Age fortification is located at the top of a hill with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Callanish Standing Stones - These ancient stones are a must-visit for anyone interested in history and are said to be over 5000 years old.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Bernera

  • The Bernera Way - This 22-mile hike is a challenging but rewarding path that offers stunning views of the island’s rugged coastline and rolling hills.

  • The Pentland Road Cycling Route - For those who prefer cycling, this scenic route winds along the west coast of the island and offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Great Bernera: Activities and Tours

  • Bernera Boat Trips - Explore the coastline and nearby islands with a guided boat tour and spot seals, dolphins, and whales along the way.

  • Fishing in the Atlantic - Take advantage of the island’s thriving fishing scene with a day of fishing in the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Bernera Island Tours - Join a guided tour of the island and immerse yourself in the culture, history, and natural beauty of Great Bernera.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Bernera

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stornoway Black Pudding - This traditional Scottish dish is a must-try for sausage lovers.

  • Cranachan - A delicious dessert made from whipped cream, raspberries, toasted oats, and honey.

  • Haggis and Neeps - A classic Scottish dish made from sheep organs and turnips.

Local Beverages

  • Isle of Harris Gin - A locally produced gin that is infused with the island’s maritime botanicals and has won various awards.

  • Hebridean Brewery Beers - A range of award-winning beers brewed on the nearby Isle of Lewis.

Where to Stay in Great Bernera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tigh-na-Mara - A guesthouse located in a stunningly restored Victorian house with spectacular views of the Atlantic Ocean.

  • The West Side Bunkhouse - Affordable hostel accommodation that offers a communal kitchen and dining area for self-catering.

  • The Old School House - A charming self-catering cottage that is steeped in history and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Great Bernera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Bernera Book - A book detailing the history, culture, and landscapes of the island, written by a local author.

  • The Bernera Tapestry - A beautiful tapestry that depicts the history and culture of the island.

  • Isle of Lewis Chess Set - A unique and intricate chess set made from carved stone and wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Bernera

The island of Great Bernera has a rich culture that is steeped in history and tradition. One of the most notable customs is the annual wool shearing festival, where locals come together to shear the wool from the island’s sheep, followed by a traditional ceilidh.

Best Time to Visit Great Bernera

The best time to visit Great Bernera is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the days are long.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Bernera

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Don’t ignore safety warnings or advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Bernera

The island is relatively safe for visitors, but it is important to take precautions when exploring the rugged coastline and hills. Always check the weather conditions and be aware of any potential hazards, such as rocky terrain and strong currents.

Health in Great Bernera

There are no major health risks associated with visiting Great Bernera, but it is always advisable to bring any necessary medication with you and to ensure that you have adequate travel insurance.

Getting to Great Bernera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Bernera

The nearest airport is Stornoway Airport, located on the nearby Isle of Lewis, which offers daily flights from Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Inverness.

Bus Stations Near Great Bernera

There are regular bus services that operate between Stornoway and Bernera, with stops at various points along the way.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Great Bernera by ferry from the mainland or by private boat.

How to Get Around in Great Bernera

The best way to explore the island is on foot or by bike, as the roads are narrow and winding. Visitors can also hire a car or book a taxi to explore the island’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Great Bernera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Isle of Lewis - Visit the largest island in the Outer Hebrides and explore its rich history and culture.

  • Isle of Harris - Take a day trip to the stunning Isle of Harris and explore its pristine beaches and rugged landscapes.

Conclusion

Great Bernera offers an unparalleled experience to visitors, with stunning natural landscapes, rich culture, and exciting outdoor activities.
