Introduction to Lewis

Lewis is an island located in Outer Hebrides, in Hebrides, Scotland, a part of the United Kingdom. It is an enchanting place, embellished with rugged coastlines, rolling hills, and pristine beaches. The island has a rich history of Celtic, Norse, and Scottish influences. There are plenty of things to do and places to visit in Lewis, which makes it a perfect destination for nature and history enthusiasts. This travel guide will help you explore the best of Lewis, so let’s get started.

List of Places to Visit in Lewis

Lewis has a rich cultural heritage and several natural attractions. Here are some of the best places that you must visit in Lewis.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Callanish Stones - A must-visit site in Lewis, the Callanish Stones are a collection of standing stones that were erected around 5,000 years ago. It is believed that it was used as a ritualistic or astronomical monument.
  • Lews Castle Museum and Art Gallery - Located in Stornoway, Lews Castle is a historical building that houses a museum and art gallery. It showcases the history and culture of Lewis through various exhibits and artworks.
  • Blackhouse Village - Located in Carloway, the Blackhouse Village is a museum that showcases the lifestyle and architecture of the past. It is a great place to understand the culture and heritage of Lewis.

Natural Attractions of Lewis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Luskentyre Beach - Luskentyre is one of the most beautiful beaches in Scotland, with crystal clear waters and white sand.
  • Butt of Lewis Lighthouse - The Butt of Lewis Lighthouse is a historic lighthouse that offers stunning views of the rugged coastline.
  • Mountain Biking in Carloway - Carloway is a great place to go for mountain biking, with trails that take you through the scenic countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewis

Lewis offers several hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best trails that you can explore:

  • Lionel to Ness Walk - This 32 km trail takes you through the scenic countryside of Lewis and offers stunning views of the Loch Side.
  • The Hebridean Way - This 247 km trail takes you through the Outer Hebrides, including Lewis. It offers a great opportunity to explore the natural beauty and the different islands.

Best Things to Do in Lewis: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching - Lewis offers an excellent opportunity to go whale watching, with several companies offering tours throughout the year.
  • Boat Tours to St Kilda - St Kilda is a World Heritage Site, located 64 km west of Lewis. Several boat tours are organized to visit this island, which is home to several unique species of animals and birds.
  • Fishing Trips - Fishing is a popular activity in Lewis, with companies offering fishing trips that take you through some of the best fishing spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lewis

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Lewis:

Day 1 Arrive in Stornoway, visit Lews Castle Museum and Art Gallery

Food and Drink Scene of Lewis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stornoway Black Pudding - Stornoway black pudding is a type of sausage made from beef, barley, and spices. It is a traditional dish in Lewis and is a must-try for food enthusiasts.
  • Haggis - Haggis is a savory pudding made from sheep’s liver, heart, and lungs, mixed with onions, oatmeal, and spices.
  • Scottish Salmon - The waters around Lewis are home to some of the best salmon in the world. Scottish Salmon is a must-try dish while you’re in Lewis.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky - Whisky is a popular drink in Scotland and Lewis is no exception. Several distilleries are located in Lewis, including the Abhainn Dearg Distillery.
  • Real Ale - Lewis has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries producing a variety of real ales and beers.

Where to Stay in Lewis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lewis offers several accommodation options, catering to different budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Lewis:

  • Lews Castle - A luxurious option, the Lews Castle is a historic building that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Hebridean Guest House - A budget-friendly option, the Hebridean Guest House is located in Stornoway and offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices.
  • Argyll Hotel - The Argyll Hotel is located in Uig, and offers comfortable rooms with great views of the bay.

What to Buy in Lewis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Harris Tweed - Harris Tweed is a traditional Scottish fabric made from wool and is handwoven in Lewis. It is a must-buy for anyone interested in fashion or textiles.
  • Stornoway Black Pudding - If you enjoyed the taste of Stornoway Black Pudding, you can bring some home with you.
  • Jewelry - Lewis has a rich tradition of jewelry-making, and you can buy unique pieces of jewelry made from locally-sourced materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewis

Lewis has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Celtic, Norse, and Scottish cultures. The locals have a strong attachment to their traditions and culture. The language spoken in Lewis is Scottish Gaelic, but most locals also speak English.

Best Time to Visit Lewis

The best time to visit Lewis is from April to October, when the weather is mild and pleasant. July and August are the busiest months, so it is advisable to book accommodation well in advance during this time. The winter season, from November to March, can be harsh, with strong winds and frequent storms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewis

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Lewis.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Ignore warnings or signs related to safety.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewis

Lewis is a safe place to visit, with little crime. However, it is important to follow general safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Lewis

Lewis has a good healthcare system, with hospitals and clinics located in Stornoway and other major towns. It is advisable to take travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Lewis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewis

  • Stornoway Airport - Located in Stornoway, Stornoway Airport is the main airport serving Lewis.

Bus Stations Near Lewis

  • BBC Bus Station - Located in Stornoway, the BBC Bus Station is the main bus station that serves Lewis.

Other Options

  • Ferry - Ferries operate daily to and from Lewis, connecting the island to other parts of Scotland.

How to Get Around in Lewis

The best way to get around Lewis is by car. Several car rental companies operate in Lewis, and it is advisable to book in advance during the peak season. Buses and taxis are also available, but they can be less frequent and more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Lewis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Isle of Harris - The Isle of Harris is located next to Lewis and offers some of the best beaches and natural attractions in Scotland.
  • Isle of Skye - The Isle of Skye is another popular destination in Scotland, known for its rugged landscapes and breathtaking beauty.

Conclusion

Lewis is a beautiful island, with a rich history and culture. There are several places to visit and things to do, catering to different interests and budgets. A visit to Lewis is a must for anyone looking to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Scotland.
