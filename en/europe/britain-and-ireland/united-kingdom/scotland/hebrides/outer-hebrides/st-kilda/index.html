Introduction to St Kilda

St Kilda is a remote archipelago situated on the western edge of the Outer Hebrides, Scotland. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its exceptional natural beauty, intriguing history and rare wildlife, St Kilda is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a unique and unforgettable travel experience in the UK. The islands are formed of four islets, Hirta, Dun, Soay and Boreray, surrounded by stormy seas and populated by a small and friendly community of people who continue to preserve the traditions and customs of the Gaelic culture.

List of Places to Visit in St Kilda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St Kilda Museum

The St Kilda Museum is a fascinating museum housing an extensive collection of artefacts, including photographs, letters, clothes, and household items. It provides a glimpse into the lives and culture of the people of St Kilda, their struggle for survival, their traditions and the harsh environment they lived in.

Village Bay

Village Bay is the main settlement on Hirta, the largest island. Its ruined houses, cleits (stone storage huts), and church remains tell the story of the island’s former population and the challenges they had to overcome. The bay offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, sea cliffs, and the island of Dun.

Natural Attractions of St Kilda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St Kilda Sea Adventures

St Kilda Sea Adventures offer a range of boat tours to explore the islands’ beauty from the water. From wildlife cruises that let you see the playful seals and puffins, to sea kayaking and snorkelling excursions, to fishing trips and overnight wild camping, there’s something for everyone.

The Kilda Cruises

The Kilda Cruises offer a fantastic way to experience the landscapes and wildlife of St Kilda from a different perspective. By the water, you’ll be able to witness the fascinating geology of the islands and see amazing seabird colonies, including gannets, fulmars and kittiwakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Kilda

St Kilda Walking Tours

St Kilda Walking Tours is a popular walking tour operator that offers guided tours around Hirta, the main island of St Kilda. The tours showcase the island’s natural beauty and cultural heritage while providing an exciting adventure for visitors. There are several types of tours, including a seven-hour exploration of three of the four islets, a five-hour cliff walk, and shorter walks and talks that provide insight into the Gaelic culture.

Best Things to Do in St Kilda: Activities and Tours

St Kilda Adventure Centre

St Kilda Adventure Centre is the go-to place for adrenaline junkies who want to experience all the thrills and excitement that the islands have to offer. Those who are up for hiking, rock climbing, abseiling, coasteering, and kayaking tours will find St Kilda Adventure Centre is the ideal destination. Whether a novice adventurer or an experienced mountaineer, there’s something for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of St Kilda

Must-Try Dishes

The Outer Hebrides is home to some of the tastiest seafood in the UK, and St Kilda is no exception. Langoustines, lobster, crab, and fresh fish are commonly served in all restaurants or cafes. Try the famous St Kilda lamb, which is tender, juicy and simply delicious.

Local Beverages

St Kilda Brewery creates some of the tastiest beers in Scotland, using only the finest ingredients, including locally grown hops, and water from the nearby well.

Where to Stay in St Kilda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Puffin Hostel

The Puffin Hostel is located in the heart of Village Bay. It offers comfortable rooms and dormitories, a fully equipped kitchen and lounge for guests. The Puffin Hostel is the perfect affordable option for backpackers and solo travellers who want to stay longer in St Kilda.

Glebe Cottage

Glebe Cottage is a charming B&B located in the village of Leverburgh on the Isle of Harris. Choose from their two spacious and tastefully decorated rooms, both with ensuite bathrooms, comfortable beds and breathtaking views. Guests will enjoy a friendly welcome, hearty Scottish breakfast and easy access to explore the surrounding islands.

What to Buy in St Kilda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for a unique souvenir, you must have an authentic St Kilda wool blanket, made from the fleeces of local sheep, woven using traditional methods. The St Kilda Tweed Company is another excellent option for sustainable and eco-friendly textiles, including fabric and clothing items inspired by the island’s landscapes and colours.

Local Culture and Customs of St Kilda

St Kilda has a rich cultural heritage, and the Gaelic language is still spoken by the small population that calls the islands home. The strong connection to the land and traditions is noticeable, and visitors are welcome to explore and learn about the island’s history and customs. It is essential to respect local customs, especially as visitors and understand the need to preserve the islands’ fragile ecosystem.

Best Time to Visit St Kilda

Plan your trip between May and September when the weather is mild and warm for exploring the outdoors, and many of the seasonal amenities are available for visitors. During these months, the wildlife is also more active, and the cliffs are teeming with nesting seabirds.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Kilda

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Research and plan ahead. Many amenities and activities are available on a seasonal basis only.
  • Take time to experience the island’s culture, customs and language.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or remove any wildlife, flora or fauna.
  • Litter or pollute in the nature reserves and especially in the marine environment.
  • Overstep the boundaries of maritime law or act recklessly onboard boats or vessels.

Safety and Health

Safety in St Kilda

While the islands may appear serene and peaceful, there are still natural hazards to consider. Always dress appropriately and prepare for unpredictable weather conditions, particularly at sea. It is recommended to wear sturdy hiking boots and bring appropriate safety gear when participating in any outdoor activity.

Health in St Kilda

There is no health centre or hospital on St Kilda itself. The nearest medical facilities are located on the Isle of Harris and the mainland. It is essential to bring any necessary medication with you, particularly considering remote locations and travel times.

Getting to St Kilda: Transportation Options and Logistics

St Kilda is reached by boat only. There are only two regularly scheduled passenger vessels– the Kilda Cruises and the Sea Tours St Kilda. Both depart from the Isle of Harris on the west coast of Scotland. Visitors should plan carefully as schedules are subject to weather and sea conditions.

Airports Near St Kilda

The closest international airports are in Inverness and Glasgow.

Bus Stations Near St Kilda

There are no bus stations near St Kilda.

Other Options

Charter boats and private yachts offer a unique and exclusive travel option to St Kilda. Cairn Charters specialise in bespoke and tailor-made packages, depending on visitors’ needs and preferences.

How to Get Around in St Kilda

On St Kilda, there are no roads or vehicles, making walking, cycling and boats the most common methods of getting around on the islands.

Where to Go Next from St Kilda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Outer Hebrides are home to a wealth of beautiful destinations and attractions, including the Isle of Skye, the Isle of Barra, and the Callanish Stones. On the mainland, consider exploring the Scottish Highlands and its national parks, or the charming cities of Glasgow and Edinburgh.

Conclusion

With its rugged beauty, rich history, and fascinating wildlife, St Kilda is truly a bucket-list travel destination. Follow our ultimate guide to plan your dream trip to this remote paradise and embrace the island’s natural wonders, culture and tradition.
