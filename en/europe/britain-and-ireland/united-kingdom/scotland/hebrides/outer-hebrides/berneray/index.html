Introduction to Berneray

Berneray is a small island located in the Outer Hebrides, Scotland. The island is part of the larger collection of islands known as the Hebrides and is famous for its stunning white beaches, turquoise waters, and diverse wildlife. With a population of just over 130 people, Berneray offers a peaceful, unique, and unforgettable experience for travelers seeking a getaway from the hustle and bustle of busy cities. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Berneray.

List of Places to Visit in Berneray

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Berneray Historical Society Museum: The museum showcases the rich history of the island, from the Neolithic Age to present day.
  • St. Michael’s Church: The church boasts a rich maritime history and is lined with well-preserved 19th-century memorials.
  • Borve Castle: A historic ruin offering a glimpse into medieval life on the island.

Natural Attractions of Berneray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Berneray Beach: A stunning, white sand beach perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying the beautiful views.
  • Harris Hills: A hiker’s paradise offering spectacular views of the island and the surrounding sea.
  • Berneray Wildlife Reserve: Home to a variety of migratory and nesting birds, including the rare corncrake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berneray

  • The Ring of Benassay: A 6-mile circular track that boasts stunning views of the surrounding hills, lochs, and beaches.
  • The Berneray to Rodel Cycle Route: A 14-mile route that takes in the beautiful scenery of the east coast of Harris.

Best Things to Do in Berneray: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour: Discover the rich marine life of the Hebrides with a boat tour from Berneray.
  • Kayaking: Explore the crystal clear waters of the island with a kayaking tour.
  • Visit the Machair: This marshland is home to a diverse range of wildlife and plant species.

Food and Drink Scene of Berneray

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scottish Salmon: A succulent fish famous for its taste and texture, usually served grilled or smoked.
  • Haggis: A savory meat pudding made from sheep’s offal, oatmeal, and spices, served with neeps and tatties (turnip and potatoes).
  • Cullen Skink: A hearty Scottish soup made of smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland’s national drink is a must-try, with over 120 distilleries offering the iconic tipple.
  • Craft Beer: Berneray boasts a range of local breweries offering a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Berneray: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Berneray Hostel: A comfortable, budget-friendly stay for travelers wanting to enjoy the beautiful views of the island.
  • The West Beach Inn: A charming guesthouse located on the West Beach, perfect for those seeking a peaceful stay.
  • Village Hall Campsite: Offers a basic campsite experience, but with stunning views and easy access to all the island’s attractions.

What to Buy in Berneray: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Harris Tweed: A hand-woven Scottish fabric famous for its quality and stunning colors.
  • Stornoway Black Pudding: A delicious Scottish delicacy, made from pork and oatmeal.
  • Island Jewelry: Beautifully crafted jewelry made from local stones and shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Berneray

Berneray boasts a rich cultural heritage with strong links to the surrounding islands. One of the most significant customs on the island is the annual Berneray Sheep Dog Trials, held every summer, showcasing the skills of the sheepdogs and the strong bonds that exist between local farmers and their dogs.

Best Time to Visit Berneray

The best time to visit Berneray is during the summer months (June-August) when the long daylight hours offer travelers more time to explore the beautiful island and take part in outdoor activities. The weather is generally mild, and the surrounding sea is warm enough for swimming, kayaking, and other water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berneray

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment by following the “leave no trace” principle.
  • Explore the island’s natural beauty and take part in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the nesting birds and other wildlife.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Ignore the weather warnings, especially when taking part in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berneray

Berneray is generally a safe island to visit. However, travelers need to be aware of the sudden weather changes and high tides when exploring the beaches and the hills. Visitors should also take care when driving on the single-track roads.

Health in Berneray

The island has a small medical center, and visitors are advised to bring their own medication. Travelers should also be aware of the local wildlife, particularly ticks, which can spread Lyme disease.

Getting to Berneray: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Berneray

The nearest airport to Berneray is Stornoway Airport on the Isle of Lewis, which offers flights from Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Inverness.

Bus Stations Near Berneray

Berneray is connected to the mainland by ferry and bus services. The island is served by the W17/W18 bus services, which connect with the ferry terminal at Leverburgh.

Other Options

Travelers can also drive to Berneray by taking the A85 and the A87, then taking the ferry from Uig on the Isle of Skye to Berneray.

How to Get Around in Berneray

The best way to get around the island is by car or bicycle. Visitors can rent a car or bike from the nearby town of Lochmaddy, or walk along the well-marked paths and roads to explore the island on foot.

Where to Go Next from Berneray: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Isle of Skye: A popular tourist destination famous for its stunning landscapes and ancient history.
  • St. Kilda: A remote archipelago located to the west of Berneray, offering unique wildlife and views.

Conclusion

Berneray is a unique and unforgettable destination that offers travelers stunning natural beauty, rich history, and a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of busy cities. With this comprehensive guide, you now have all the information you need to plan your perfect Berneray itinerary and make the most of your visit to this beautiful Scottish island.
