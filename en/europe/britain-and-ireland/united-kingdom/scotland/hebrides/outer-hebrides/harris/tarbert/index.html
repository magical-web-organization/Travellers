Introduction to Tarbert

Tarbert, located in the Outer Hebrides of Scotland, is a charming village that is rich in natural beauty and historical significance. This picturesque village is situated on the Isle of Harris and is a popular destination for travelers seeking to explore the rugged landscapes and cultural attractions of the Scottish isles.

In this comprehensive guide, we will help you plan your trip to Tarbert and discover everything this village has to offer, including its natural attractions, cultural heritage, accommodation options, cuisine, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Tarbert

If you’re planning a trip to Tarbert, you will be spoilt for choice when it comes to places to visit. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, this village has something for everyone. Here is a list of top places to visit in Tarbert:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Harris Tweed Company Store: Famous for its high-quality, handcrafted tweed, the Harris Tweed Company Store offers a unique shopping experience that showcases the local brand of Harris Tweed.

  • Isle of Harris Distillery: If you love whiskey, then don’t miss out on a tour of the Isle of Harris Distillery, which offers a hands-on experience of the whiskey-making process, complete with tastings and a gift shop.

  • St. Clement’s Church: This historic church was built in the 16th century and boasts a stunning interior that features intricate carved ceilings and a beautifully restored altar.

Natural Attractions of Tarbert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Luskentyre Beach: Located on the west coast of the Isle of Harris, Luskentyre Beach is a must-visit destination for its stunning white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking views.

  • Clisham: At 799 meters, Clisham is the highest peak in the Outer Hebrides and offers challenging hiking trails and panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • The Bays of Harris: This unspoiled area of coastline is home to some of the most breathtaking scenery in the Outer Hebrides, with miles of white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and stunning natural landscapes that are ideal for hiking and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarbert

Tarbert is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts looking for exciting hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes to explore in and around Tarbert:

  • Tarbert to the Bays: This route will take you on a scenic journey through the stunning coastline of the Outer Hebrides, with panoramic views of the sea and mountains.

  • Clisham Hike: If you’re up for a challenge, then don’t miss out on the Clisham Hike, which will take you to the summit of Clisham, the highest peak in the Outer Hebrides.

  • Scalpay Circular: This route is perfect for cyclists looking for a day trip with scenic views of the local coastline. The route offers a mix of challenging hills and easy flats that are perfect for all levels of cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Tarbert: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and cycling, there are plenty of other exciting activities to do in Tarbert, including:

  • Boat trips to the nearby islands are a must-do, offering unforgettable experiences of exploring the rugged coastlines of the Isle of Harris and Lewis.

  • Sea kayaking is a popular activity in Tarbert, offering a great way to explore the stunning coastline and spot local wildlife.

  • Horseback riding is available for those looking for something special. Gallop through the countryside and enjoy the breathtaking views of the local landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarbert

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Tarbert, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your accommodation

  • Day 2: Visit St. Clement’s Church and take a tour of the Isle of Harris Distillery.

  • Day 3: Hike Clisham and take in the stunning views of the Outer Hebrides.

  • Day 4: Explore the town of Tarbert, enjoying local cuisine at the restaurants and cafes.

  • Day 5: Take a boat trip to the Isle of Lewis and visit the famous Callanish Standing Stones.

  • Day 6: Enjoy the beaches and natural landscapes along the Bays of Harris.

  • Day 7: Departure from Tarbert.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarbert

Tarbert’s cuisine scene is diverse and reflects the mix of cultures that have passed through this village. There are plenty of local dishes to taste and experiences to enjoy when it comes to eating and drinking in Tarbert. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A hearty Scottish soup made from smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions, with a creamy texture.

  • Stornoway Black Pudding: A classic Scottish dish made from pork, oatmeal, and spices, served with a traditional breakfast.

  • Smoked Salmon: Made from local salmon, caught fresh in the nearby waters of Tarbert.

Local Beverages

  • Isle of Harris Gin: This local gin is crafted by the Isle of Harris Distillery and includes nine botanicals, including locally grown sugar kelp.

  • Local Whiskey: Harris is a land of whiskey, and you can find rare and exquisite local scotches among the local distilleries.

  • Beer: Taste the local brews from the local breweries and enjoy the unique flavor of the beer.

Where to Stay in Tarbert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarbert has a range of accommodation options suitable for all travelers. From cozy bed and breakfasts to luxury hotels and hostels, visitors can choose from a variety of accommodation options, including:

  • Hotel Hebrides: A charming, family-run hotel with modern facilities overlooking the harbor.

  • Gorsebank: A traditional Scottish guesthouse with a warm atmosphere, offering comfortable rooms and great hospitality.

  • Backpackers Stop: A cozy hostel for budget travelers, located in the heart of Tarbert village.

What to Buy in Tarbert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Tarbert, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products available for purchase, including:

  • Harris Tweed: A quintessentially Scottish brand, with hand-woven woolen cloth that is renowned for its quality and craftsmanship.

  • Local Whiskey: Isle of Harris Distillery produces high-quality whiskey for whiskey aficionados to take back home.

  • Stornoway Black Pudding: A must-try local dish that will make a great souvenir for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarbert

Tarbert is steeped in local traditions and customs, with a rich heritage that has been preserved for centuries. Islanders take great pride in their local heritage and traditional ways of life, including:

  • The Language: Gaelic is still spoken by around a third of the Outer Hebrides population, and you can hear this historic language spoken in Tarbert.

  • The Music: Traditional Scottish music and folk dancing are still very much alive in the village.

Best Time to Visit Tarbert

The best time to visit Tarbert is between April and September when the weather is warm and dry. Summers in the Outer Hebrides can be mild, with temperatures ranging from the mid-50s to low 60s Fahrenheit. The island also has long daylight hours, allowing for plenty of time to explore the local attractions.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Tarbert, it is important to keep safety in mind. Here are some safety and health tips to stay safe during your trip:

Safety in Tarbert

  • Always follow the rules around wildlife and to prevent erosion on the hiking trails.

  • Be careful on the local beaches, as the currents can be strong in some areas.

  • Pay attention to the weather forecast, especially if you plan to explore the coastal areas.

Health in Tarbert

  • Make sure you have adequate travel insurance before traveling to Tarbert.

  • Pack necessary first aid items and medications.

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated during outdoor activities.

Getting to Tarbert: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Tarbert is easy, with a range of transportation options that include:

Airports Near Tarbert

  • Stornoway Airport: Located on the nearby Isle of Lewis, this airport has direct flights to cities such as Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Inverness.

Bus Stations Near Tarbert

  • Clyde Property: This bus station provides regular services to Tarbert from nearby towns such as Stornoway and Glasgow.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rentals are available in nearby towns like Stornoway and Glasgow.

  • Ferry: Roughly 20 minutes from Tarbert town is Uig port where regular ferries are available to Lochmaddy on the Isle of North Uist.

How to Get Around in Tarbert

Getting around Tarbert is easy with a range of transportation options that include:

  • Hiking: This is the best way to explore the natural beauty of the area.

  • Car rental: Rental cars are available from nearby towns and cities, making it easy to explore further afield.

  • Buses: Local bus services operate around Tarbert regularly.

Where to Go Next from Tarbert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning a trip to Tarbert, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some ideas:

  • Isle of Lewis: This nearby island is home to the famous Callanish Standing Stones and is renowned for its rugged coastline and stunning landscapes.

  • Isle of Skye: This island has long been a popular destination for travelers seeking stunning scenery and exhilarating outdoor adventures.

  • Inverness: Admire the Caledonian Canal in Inverness, visit the famous Glenfiddich Distillery and soak up the rich history of the historic Highland capital.

Conclusion

Tarbert is a wonderful holiday destination that has so much to offer travelers. Boasting a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and some of the best food and drink in Scotland, it’s no wonder that this charming village is often cited as one of the top destinations in the Outer Hebrides.

Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring local history, or simply soaking up the village atmosphere, Tarbert is the perfect place to unwind and relax on your next holiday.
