Dunoon, located in Argyll and Bute, in Scottish Highlands, is a charming seaside town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and outdoor adventure. Its stunning landscapes, pristine beaches, and historical attractions make it an ideal destination for tourists seeking an authentic Scottish experience.

Here’s everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Dunoon.

Introduction to Dunoon

Dunoon is a beautiful seaside town located on the west coast of Scotland. It’s a popular holiday destination in the Scottish Highlands, offering breathtaking views of the Firth of Clyde and the Cowal Peninsula. The town has a rich history, and you can see evidence of this in the many castles, museums, and other historical sites throughout the area.

The town is also famous for its festivals and events, including the Cowal Highland Gathering, which is one of the largest Highland games in the world.

List of Places to Visit in Dunoon

When it comes to exploring Dunoon, there is no shortage of things to do and see. Here are some of the top attractions that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castle House Museum: This museum showcases the town’s rich history through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs. It’s a must-visit for history lovers.

  • Dunoon Burgh Hall: This cultural hub hosts community events, exhibitions, and workshops throughout the year. The building itself is also worth admiring, as it’s a stunning example of Victorian architecture.

  • Toward Castle: This 15th-century castle is located on the Cowal Peninsula and overlooks the Firth of Clyde. It’s a great spot for a picnic or to enjoy the stunning views.

Natural Attractions of Dunoon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Puck’s Glen: This magical woodland walk takes you through a gorge filled with waterfalls, trees, and streams. It’s an enchanting experience that will leave you in awe of the natural beauty of the area.

  • Loch Eck: This freshwater loch is surrounded by forests and hills, providing a picturesque spot for fishing, boating, and hiking.

  • Benmore Botanic Garden: This garden is home to a wide variety of exotic plants and trees from around the world. It’s a great place for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunoon

Dunoon is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with ample trails for hiking and biking. The stunning scenery will make your adventure all the more enjoyable. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • The Cowal Way: This 57-mile trail takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Scotland and offers breathtaking views of the sea and mountains.

  • Glenbranter: This tranquil forest offers several walking and biking trails that cater to different skill levels.

  • Argyll Forest Park: This park has over 300 miles of trails that wind through forests, hills, and lochs. It’s a great place to spot wildlife and birdwatch.

Best Things to Do in Dunoon: Activities and Tours

Dunoon offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Visit a whiskey distillery: Dunoon is located in the heart of Scotland’s whiskey region, and there are several distilleries in the area that offer tours and tastings.

  • Take a boat tour: Explore the stunning coastline and islands around Dunoon by taking a boat tour. You may even spot some dolphins, seals, and whales along the way.

  • Play golf: The area around Dunoon has several golf courses that offer stunning views and challenging holes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunoon

If you have a week to explore Dunoon and the surrounding area, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1-2: Check out the local museums and historical sites, such as Castle House Museum, Dunoon Burgh Hall, and Toward Castle.

Day 3-4: Hike or bike through the local forests and hills, such as Puck’s Glen, Glenbranter, and Argyll Forest Park.

Day 5-6: Take a boat tour to explore the islands and coastline around Dunoon, or visit a whiskey distillery for a tasting.

Day 7: Relax on one of the stunning beaches in the area, such as Kilbride Bay, Ardentinny Beach, or Lunderston Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Dunoon

Dunoon has a vibrant food and drink scene that celebrates local produce and traditional Scottish cuisine. Here are some dishes and beverages that you should try during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: This traditional Scottish dish is made of sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with suet and spices, then stuffed into a sheep’s stomach and boiled. It’s typically served with neeps (turnips) and tatties (potatoes).

  • Cullen skink: This creamy soup is made with smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream. It’s a hearty and delicious dish that’s perfect on a chilly day.

  • Cranachan: This dessert is made with whipped cream, honey, raspberries, and toasted oats. It’s a sweet and tangy treat that’s perfect after a meal.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey: Dunoon is located in the heart of Scotland’s whiskey region, and there are several distilleries in the area that produce some of the finest whiskey in the world.

  • Ale: Scotland is also famous for its ale, and Dunoon has several breweries that produce some fantastic brews, such as Fyne Ales and Loch Fyne Ales.

Where to Stay in Dunoon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dunoon has several accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • The Argyll Hotel: This grand, Victorian hotel offers stunning views of the sea and mountains and is located in the heart of Dunoon.

  • The Park Hotel: This modern hotel is located on the waterfront and offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.

  • The Old Schoolhouse Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in a former schoolhouse and offers comfortable and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Dunoon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Dunoon has several options to choose from. Here are some ideas:

  • Whiskey: Scotland is famous for its whiskey, and there are several distilleries in the area that produce some of the finest whiskey in the world.

  • Tartan: Tartan is a traditional Scottish fabric that’s used to make kilts, scarves, and other clothing items. You can find several shops in Dunoon that sell tartan products.

  • Shortbread: Shortbread is a traditional Scottish biscuit that’s made with butter, sugar, and flour. It’s a delicious treat that’s perfect for a snack or as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Dunoon

Scotland has a rich culture and history, and Dunoon is no exception. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting the area:

  • Kilts: Kilts are a traditional Scottish garment that is typically worn at weddings, funerals, and other formal occasions.

  • Bagpipes: Bagpipes are a traditional Scottish instrument that’s often played at weddings, funerals, and other events.

  • Ceilidh: A ceilidh is a traditional Scottish gathering that involves music, dancing, and storytelling.

Best Time to Visit Dunoon

The best time to visit Dunoon is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is milder, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. However, if you prefer a quieter atmosphere, you may want to visit during the offseason (September-May).

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunoon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Dunoon:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as wearing appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

  • Be mindful of the wildlife and environment when hiking or biking through the local forests and hills.

  • Try traditional Scottish dishes and beverages, such as haggis, whiskey, and ale.

Don’t:

  • Touch or feed wild animals, such as deer or seals.

  • Ignore warnings and safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Dunoon is a safe and healthy destination for tourists, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Dunoon

Getting to Dunoon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dunoon is easily accessible by several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Dunoon

  • Glasgow International Airport (GLA) is located approximately 25 miles away from Dunoon and offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Dunoon

  • The Clyde Coast bus service connects Dunoon to Glasgow and other towns and cities in the area.

Other Options

  • You can also drive to Dunoon from Glasgow, which takes approximately 1.5 hours.

  • If you prefer to take the ferry, you can take the Western Ferries service from Gourock to Dunoon.

How to Get Around in Dunoon

Dunoon is a small town that is easily navigable on foot, but there are also several transportation options available, such as:

  • Taxis: There are several taxi companies in Dunoon that offer reliable and affordable transportation around the town and surrounding area.

  • Buses: There are several local bus services that connect Dunoon to other towns and cities in the area.

  • Car rentals: If you prefer to explore the area on your own, you can rent a car from one of the several rental companies in Dunoon.

Where to Go Next from Dunoon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to explore more of Scotland after your visit to Dunoon, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Conclusion

Dunoon offers a unique and authentic Scottish experience, with its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, or simply a relaxing holiday, you’ll find something to love in Dunoon. So why not start planning your trip today?
