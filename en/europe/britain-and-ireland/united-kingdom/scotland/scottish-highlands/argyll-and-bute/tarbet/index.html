Introduction to Tarbet

Tarbet is a beautiful village situated in Argyll and Bute, in Scottish Highlands, Scotland. The picturesque village is located at the northeastern shore of Loch Lomond, between Crianlarich and Arrochar. It is a popular tourist destination because of its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities, making it an ideal place for nature lovers, adventurers, and peaceful seekers. In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan your visit to Tarbet.

List of Places to Visit in Tarbet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arrochar Heritage Trail: Discover the rich history of Arrochar village by walking through its historical sites, such as St Molaise Church, the old schoolhouse, and the war memorial.

  2. Ben Lomond Distillery: Learn about the finest malt whiskies produced in Scotland and sample some of the world-class Scottish whisky.

  3. Inveraray Castle: Explore the iconic castle of Inveraray, the seat of Clan Campbell, with its stunning architecture, paintings, and furniture.

Natural Attractions of Tarbet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Loch Lomond: Take a cruise ride or hike along the banks of the largest freshwater lake in Great Britain, renowned for its rural majesty and tranquillity.

  2. The Arrochar Alps: Experience the spectacular views of the mountain range that surrounds the Arrochar village. Enjoy the challenging hike or trek amid the stunning Scottish landscapes.

  3. The Cobbler: Visit the iconic mountain, also known as “Ben Arthur”, and its paths that provide a challenging walking route combined with breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarbet

Tarbet is a walker’s paradise with excellent horse-riding, hiking and biking trails that follow the scenic routes through the beautiful Highland landscape. A few popular routes are:

  1. Puck’s Glen: This 1.5 miles (2.4 km) route lies in the fairy glen of Benmore Gardens, and the surging streams, waterfalls, and colorful ferns create a stunning sight.

  2. Ben Vorlich: It is a challenging 5.4 miles (8.7 km) route located in the southern part of Arrochar Alps, famous among mountain climbers.

  3. Three Lochs Forest Drive: Drive on the forest trail that winds through Lochan Reòidhte, Loch Shira and Lochs a’Bhraoin, and witness the beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Tarbet: Activities and Tours

  1. Loch Lomond Seaplanes: Take an unforgettable seaplane tour of Loch Lomond, and enjoy the breathtaking views that can be only seen from above.

  2. Rubha nan Gall Lighthouse: Visit the oldest lighthouse at the entrance of Loch Long, and enjoy the dramatic views of the sea and the mountains.

  3. Argyll Sea Kayaking Trail: Discover the beauty of Loch Lomond and the Scottish coast by kayaking through the waters of Argyll, with the help of dedicated instructors.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarbet

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Scottish Salmon: Taste the locally sourced, fresh salmon that is smoked using traditional methods, creating a mouth-watering flavour.

  2. Haggis: Savour the classic Scottish dish of haggis made of lamb, sheep heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with spices and served with turnips and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  1. Scottish Whisky: Enjoy a sip of the finest Scottish whisky, an age-old tradition that uses locally grown barley and the purest water from Scottish streams.

  2. Real Ale: Taste the locally brewed ales, made using the finest Scottish ingredients, which are renowned for their unique taste and balance.

Where to Stay in Tarbet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Tarbet Hotel: A beautiful Victorian hotel with scenic views, comfortable rooms, delicious food, and an indoor pool and spa.

  2. Arrochar Hotel: A waterfront hotel with excellent facilities, offering mouth-watering food, comfortable rooms, and friendly staff.

  3. Rowantree Bank Guesthouse: A beautiful guesthouse offering cosy rooms with excellent views of Loch Long, a brilliant bar, and a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Tarbet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Scottish Tartan: Visit the local stores and buy the beautiful Scottish tartan designs, which are unique to each clan, and make a great souvenir.

  2. Handcrafted Jewellery: Shop for the locally made jewellery, designed using Scottish gemstones, which are a perfect gift to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarbet

The Scottish culture is unique and vibrant, and it displays through the Kilts, bagpipes, and the traditional ceilidh dance. The Highland Games is an age-old tradition, where people gather to compete in games like the tug of war and caber toss. Respect the local traditions and customs during your visit to Scotland.

Best Time to Visit Tarbet

The best time to visit Tarbet is from April to September when the weather is favorable, and the outdoor recreational activities are in full swing. Also, the summer months attract a lot of tourists, so plan your visit accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarbet

Do:

  1. Respect the Scottish culture and traditions.

  2. Wear appropriate clothing and footwear.

  3. Enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities.

Don’t:

  1. Litter and damage the natural beauty.

  2. Ignore the local traditions and customs.

  3. Disturb the wildlife and aquatic life in Loch Lomond.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarbet

Tarbet is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s essential to stay alert while walking or driving on the windy roads. Take care when hiking and follow the safety guidelines.

Health in Tarbet

Healthcare services are available at the local clinic and hospitals in the nearby towns. Order for prescriptions may take some days to arrive.

Getting to Tarbet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarbet

The closest airport to Tarbet is the Glasgow airport, located 28 miles (45 km) away. From Glasgow, you can take a bus or taxi to reach Tarbet.

Bus Stations Near Tarbet

There are regular buses from Glasgow to Tarbet, operated by West Coast Motors and Citylink.

Other Options

You can also rent a car from Glasgow and drive along the scenic route to Tarbet, which is approximately a 1.5-hour drive.

How to Get Around in Tarbet

Walking is the best way to explore Tarbet and its natural surroundings. Bicycles are available at some hotels for rental. Dogs must be leashed in public areas.

Where to Go Next from Tarbet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Loch Fyne: Visit the beautiful sea loch that’s famous for its oysters, walking trails, and salmon fishing.

  2. Glen Coe: Explore the stunning Scottish Highlands and their beautiful landscapes, perfect for hiking, climbing, and winter sports.

Conclusion

Tarbet is an ideal holiday spot packed with outdoor recreational activities, breathtaking views, natural beauty and tranquillity. Plan your visit today to one of the most beautiful destinations in Scotland. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the necessary information you need for a memorable trip to Tarbet. Enjoy your Scotland adventure!
