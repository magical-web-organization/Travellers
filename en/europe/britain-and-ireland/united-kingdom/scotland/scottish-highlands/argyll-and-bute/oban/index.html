Introduction to Oban

Oban is a picturesque town located in Argyll and Bute region of the Scottish Highlands, known for its stunning landscapes, outdoor activities, and historic attractions. The town is situated on a small bay and offers a mix of quaint shops, pubs, and restaurants.

Oban makes an excellent base for exploring the western coast of Scotland, the Inner Hebrides, and the Crinan Canal. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Oban.

List of Places to Visit in Oban

There’s so much to see and do in Oban! Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • McCaig’s Tower: A prominent landmark in Oban, McCaig’s Tower offers stunning views of the town and bay. The tower was built in the late 19th century by a wealthy banker as a monument to his family.
  • Dunollie Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of the MacDougall clan, who ruled the region in the Middle Ages. The museum has a fascinating collection of artifacts and exhibits, including historical clothing and weapons.
  • Oban Distillery: Whiskey enthusiasts will love this attraction. Oban Distillery offers tours where visitors can see the process of whiskey-making and sample some of the products.

Natural Attractions of Oban: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ganavan Sands: A beautiful beach located just outside of Oban, Ganavan Sands is popular with locals and visitors alike. Here, you can enjoy a picnic, swim, or simply take in the views of the islands of Lismore and Mull.
  • Isle of Kerrera: This small island is a short ferry ride from Oban and offers excellent hiking opportunities. There’s a circular walking path that takes you around the island, where you’ll see stunning views of the coastline, hills, and sea.
  • Kilmartin Glen: A historic valley located about 45 minutes drive from Oban, Kilmartin Glen has over 800 ancient monuments, including standing stones, burial cairns, and rock art. Archaeology enthusiasts will love exploring this area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oban

Oban and the surrounding area offer some of the best hiking and biking trails in Scotland. Here are some recommendations:

  • Oban to Fort William Cycle Route: This 30-mile route takes you through some of the most breathtaking scenery in Scotland, including Glen Coe, Ben Nevis, and the slopes of Loch Linnhe.
  • Connel Hill: This steep climb offers stunning panoramic views of Oban and the surrounding landscape. The route is approximately 5 miles long, and it’s suitable for experienced hikers.
  • Inverawe Forest: This forest has a network of trails suitable for hikers and bikers of all levels. You’ll see stunning scenery, including waterfalls, lochs, and ancient woodland.

Best Things to Do in Oban: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the natural beauty and cultural sites, there are plenty of activities to enjoy in Oban:

  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour to explore the islands of the Inner Hebrides, including Mull, Iona, and Staffa. You’ll see rugged coastlines, stunning wildlife, and historic ruins.
  • Sea kayaking: Kayaking is a great way to explore the coastline around Oban. You’ll see hidden coves, sea cliffs, and wildlife such as seals and otters.
  • Golfing: There are several golf courses around Oban, including the Glencruitten Golf Club, which offers stunning views of the bay and surrounding hills.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oban

If you have a week to explore Oban and the surrounding area, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Oban town, visit McCaig’s Tower and Oban Distillery.
  • Day 3: Hike Connel Hill in the morning and take a boat tour in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Tobermory, the colorful capital of the Isle of Mull.
  • Day 5: Visit the historic Kilmartin Glen, a 45-minute drive from Oban.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Iona and Staffa, two of the most beautiful islands of the Inner Hebrides.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at Ganavan Sands beach and the Arduaine Gardens.

Food and Drink Scene in Oban

Oban has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of pubs, bars, and restaurants serving traditional Scottish cuisine and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: This traditional Scottish dish is a must-try when visiting Oban.
  • Cullen Skink: This creamy soup made with smoked haddock is a popular dish in Scotland.
  • Scotch Eggs: These boiled eggs wrapped in sausage meat and coated in breadcrumbs are a popular snack.

Local Beverages

  • Oban Whisky: This single malt whisky is produced at the Oban Distillery and is known for its distinct, rich flavors.
  • Isle of Mull Brewery: This small brewery produces a range of craft beers, including the popular Dark Island Ale.
  • Orkney Brewery: This brewery produces a range of award-winning ales, including the popular Skullsplitter Ale.

Where to Stay in Oban: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oban offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some suggestions:

  • The Perle Oban: A luxury boutique hotel located in the center of Oban, offering stunning views of the bay and a range of modern amenities.
  • Oban Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the center of Oban, offering comfortable dormitory beds and private rooms.
  • Alderlea House: A traditional guesthouse located in a quiet residential area of Oban, offering a range of comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Oban: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oban offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products to take home:

  • Oban Whisky: Don’t forget to take a bottle of Oban whisky home with you.
  • Handcrafted Jewellery: Oban has several shops selling unique, handcrafted jewelry made from local materials, including Scottish pearls.
  • Scottish Wool Products: You’ll find a range of wool products, including tartan blankets, scarves, and hats, in the local shops in Oban.

Local Culture and Customs of Oban

The people of Oban are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and culture. Oban has a rich history and a strong cultural identity, which is celebrated through various festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Oban

The best time to visit Oban is from May to September when the weather is milder and most of the outdoor attractions are open. July and August are the busiest months, so it’s best to book your accommodation and tours in advance to avoid disappointment.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oban

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Explore the stunning natural beauty of the area
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Ignore the signs and warnings at the natural attractions
  • Leave litter in the countryside
  • Be loud or disruptive during religious or cultural events

Safety and Health

Safety in Oban

Oban is a safe town for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Oban

Oban has several medical facilities, including a hospital and pharmacies, in case of an emergency. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of tick bites and take precautions when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Oban: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oban is accessible by several modes of transportation:

Airports Near Oban

The closest major airport to Oban is Glasgow International Airport (GLA), which is about 90 miles away. From the airport, you can take a train or bus to Oban.

Bus Stations Near Oban

Oban has a bus station located in the center of town, which offers connections to Glasgow, Edinburgh, and other major cities in Scotland.

Other Options

You can also reach Oban by car or train. The train journey from Glasgow to Oban takes approximately 3 hours, and the route offers stunning views of the countryside.

How to Get Around in Oban

Oban is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. You can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas. Local buses are also available, and they offer connections to other parts of Argyll and Bute.

Where to Go Next from Oban: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oban makes an excellent base for exploring the surrounding regions of the Scottish Highlands, including:

  • Isle of Mull: Take a ferry from Oban to Mull and explore the island’s stunning landscapes, historic sites, and wildlife.
  • Fort William: A town located about 40 miles from Oban, Fort William offers excellent hiking opportunities, including Ben Nevis, the highest mountain in the UK.
  • Kintyre Peninsula: Take a scenic drive down the Kintyre Peninsula and explore the historic town of Campbeltown and the stunning beaches of Machrihanish.

Conclusion

Oban is a beautiful town that offers a perfect mix of scenic views, historic attractions, and outdoor activities. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Oban, and we look forward to welcoming you to this stunning part of the Scottish Highlands.
