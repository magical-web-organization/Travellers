Introduction to Lochgilphead

Lochgilphead, located in Argyll and Bute, in the Scottish Highlands, is a picturesque town with a rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. Visitors to Lochgilphead can enjoy the combination of outdoors and cultural experiences in one destination.

List of Places to Visit in Lochgilphead

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kilmartin Museum - take a journey through 5,000 years of Scotland’s history, and learn about the ancient monuments and artefacts found in the Kilmartin Glen.
  • Crinan Canal - Built in 1801, this canal is considered to be one of the most beautiful waterways in Britain, connecting Loch Fyne to the Atlantic Ocean.

Natural Attractions of Lochgilphead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lochgilphead Golf Club - The stunning views of the west coast of Scotland and the Sound of Jura, make this golf course an attractive choice for golf enthusiasts.
  • Argyll Forest Park - Offering a range of activities including walking, cycling, fishing, and horse riding, this park is spread over 16,000 hectares and home to a variety of wildlife such as red squirrels, eagles, and wildcats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lochgilphead

  • The Cowal Way - A 57-mile long trail that will take you through the wilderness of the Cowal Peninsula, following ancient drovers’ roads and forestry tracks.
  • The Kintyre Way - Stretching over 87 miles from Tarbert to Machrihanish, this trail will take you through some of the most stunning coastal scenery in Scotland.

Best Things to Do in Lochgilphead: Activities and Tours

  • Castle Lachlan Tours - Experience the castle, gardens and grounds, along with local stories and legends by an experienced guide.
  • Seafari Adventures - Take a guided tour of the Grand Tour of the Corryvreckan Whirlpool & Wildlife Tour and experience the heart of the whirlpool.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lochgilphead

Food and Drink Scene of Lochgilphead

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis - Scotland’s national dish made from sheep’s offal, oatmeal, and spices.
  • Cullen skink - A creamy soup made from smoked haddock, onions, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Scotch whisky - Scotland’s national drink, made from malted barley, water, and yeast.
  • Scottish craft beer - try the local brew from Fyne Ales and Loch Fyne Brewery.

Where to Stay in Lochgilphead: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Galley of Lorne Inn - a traditional inn with a restaurant and bar, located on the banks of Loch Craignish.
  • The Grey Gull Hotel - a seaside hotel in the village of Ardrishaig, located near the Crinan Canal.

What to Buy in Lochgilphead: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kiln-fired pottery from Lochgilphead Pottery
  • Scottish shortbread from Campbeltown Creamery

Local Culture and Customs of Lochgilphead

  • Scottish people are friendly and enjoy a good sense of humour.
  • Tipping is not common practice in Scotland but appreciated if you feel the service has been exceptional.

Best Time to Visit Lochgilphead

Summer is the best time to visit Lochgilphead as it is the warmest and driest season, with temperatures ranging between 15°C to 21°C. The months of July and August tend to be the busiest, with many tourists flocking to the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lochgilphead

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture
  • Carry warm clothing and rain gear
  • Take only memories, leave only footprints

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb any wildlife or plant life
  • Litter or damage any historic sites or artefacts
  • Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Lochgilphead

Overall, Lochgilphead is a safe destination with low crime rates. As with any travel destination, it is always important to exercise common sense and caution.

Health in Lochgilphead

No special vaccinations are required to travel to Lochgilphead. However, it is always advisable to check with your healthcare provider for any recommended vaccinations or travel-related health concerns.

Getting to Lochgilphead: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lochgilphead

Glasgow International Airport and Glasgow Prestwick Airport are the nearest international airports to Lochgilphead, both located around 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Lochgilphead

Citylink Bus operates a bus service from Glasgow to Lochgilphead, with several daily departures. Argyll & Bute Council also offers local bus services, with routes around Lochgilphead and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

Car hire is available at Glasgow airports and in Lochgilphead for those wishing to drive themselves.

How to Get Around in Lochgilphead

The easiest way to get around Lochgilphead is by car. However, local bus services and taxis are also available for those without access to a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Lochgilphead: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oban - a picturesque harbour town, located around 40 miles north of Lochgilphead, famous for its seafood.
  • Isle of Mull - accessible from Oban by ferry, this island is known for its unspoiled landscapes, wildlife, and rich history.

Conclusion

Lochgilphead offers a fantastic blend of historical, cultural and outdoor experiences. From exploring castles, museums, and ancient monuments, to hiking, fishing, and wildlife tours, this Scottish destination is the perfect escape from city life. Take in the stunning views and treat yourself to some delicious local food and drink during your visit to Lochgilphead.
