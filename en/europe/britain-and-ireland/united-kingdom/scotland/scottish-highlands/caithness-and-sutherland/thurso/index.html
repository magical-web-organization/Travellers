Thurso is a picturesque town in the Scottish Highlands, located in Caithness and Sutherland, Scotland, United Kingdom. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know to experience the best of Thurso. From the must-see attractions and the best hikes to the top places to eat and drink, we’ve got you covered.

Introduction to Thurso

Thurso is a small town with a big personality. Located on the north coast of Scotland, it’s the northernmost town on the mainland. It’s also one of the most beautiful towns in the Scottish Highlands, surrounded by stunning scenery, including beautiful beaches, rugged cliffs, and rolling hills.

Thurso is a town steeped in history, too. It was founded around 1000 AD and has been an important center of trade and commerce ever since. It was an important stop on the trade route between Scandinavia and Scotland and has a rich maritime history. Today, Thurso is a thriving town with a lively cultural scene and plenty of things to see and do.

List of Places to Visit in Thurso

Thurso is a treasure trove of things to see and do. Here are some of the top attractions in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Caithness Horizons Museum: This museum tells the story of Caithness and its people, from the Bronze Age to the present day.
  • Old St Peter’s Church: This historic church dates back to the 12th century and is a stunning example of early Christian architecture.
  • Dunnet Head: This is the most northerly point of mainland Britain, and the view from the lighthouse is breathtaking.

Natural Attractions of Thurso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Castle of Mey: This stunning castle is located in the nearby town of Mey and was the Queen Mother’s summer residence. It’s now open to the public.
  • Thurso Beach: This beautiful beach is perfect for a stroll or a dip in the sea.
  • Dunnet Bay: This beautiful beach has stunning views over the Pentland Firth to the Orkney Islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thurso

Thurso is a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Orkney Cycle Route: This is a stunning 130-mile circular route that takes in some of the best scenery on the Orkney Islands.
  • John O’ Groats Trail: This is a 147-mile trail that takes in some of the most stunning scenery in the Scottish Highlands.
  • Ben Loyal: This is a challenging hike that takes you up to the summit of one of Scotland’s most iconic mountains.

Best Things to Do in Thurso: Activities and Tours

Thurso has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Surfing: Thurso is known for its great waves, and there are plenty of places to rent equipment and take lessons.
  • Whisky Tasting: Scotland is known for its whisky, and Thurso has a variety of distilleries to visit and sample.
  • Wildlife Watching: The Scottish Highlands are home to a variety of wildlife, including red deer, golden eagles, and puffins.

Food and Drink Scene of Thurso

Thurso has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of great places to eat and drink. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: This is a creamy Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: This is a delicious Scottish dish made with sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, combined with oatmeal, spices, and onions.
  • Cranachan: This is a traditional Scottish dessert made with oats, cream, raspberries, and whisky.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland is known for its whisky, and there are plenty of distilleries in Thurso that offer tours and tastings.
  • Real Ale: Thurso has a thriving microbrewery scene, and there are plenty of great pubs to sample the local brews.

Getting to Thurso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thurso is located in the north of Scotland, and there are several options for getting there:

Airports Near Thurso

The nearest airport to Thurso is Inverness Airport, which is around 110 miles away. From there, you can take a bus or rent a car to reach Thurso.

Bus Stations Near Thurso

Thurso has a bus station in the town center, with regular services to and from other parts of Scotland.

Other Options

You can also drive to Thurso, with the town being located on the A9, one of Scotland’s major roads.

How to Get Around in Thurso

Thurso is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you’re planning to explore the surrounding area, it’s a good idea to rent a car or take a guided tour.
