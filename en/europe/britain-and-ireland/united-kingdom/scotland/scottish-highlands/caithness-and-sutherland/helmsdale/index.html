Introduction to Helmsdale

Nestled in the Caithness and Sutherland region of the Scottish Highlands, Helmsdale is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse of Scotland’s rich history and natural beauty. It’s a popular destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy a peaceful getaway.

With its stunning landscapes, interesting museums, tasty local cuisine, and friendly locals, Helmsdale is a destination that offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Helmsdale

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Helmsdale has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained. Here are some of the top places to visit in Helmsdale:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll love exploring the museums and historic sites in Helmsdale:

  • Timespan Museum and Arts Centre: This museum tells the story of the local area, from its geological history to the arrival of the Vikings and the Clearances. There are also art exhibitions and workshops.

  • Helmsdale Harbour: This picturesque harbor was once a bustling fishing port. Today, it’s a popular spot for fishing and has excellent seafood restaurants nearby.

  • Emigrants Statue: A poignant statue dedicated to the local people who emigrated from Helmsdale during the Highland Clearances.

  • Dunrobin Castle: Located about 30 minutes from Helmsdale, this stunning castle has beautiful gardens and an impressive art collection. It’s one of the most popular tourist attractions in the area.

Natural Attractions of Helmsdale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, Helmsdale won’t disappoint. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Helmsdale River: This river is a popular spot for salmon fishing. You can also take a peaceful walk along the riverbanks.

  • Strath of Kildonan: This beautiful valley is known for its stunning views and is popular with hikers and birdwatchers.

  • Brora Beach: A beautiful sandy beach that’s ideal for a long walk or picnic.

  • Ben Uarie: Located near Helmsdale, this challenging hike offers stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Helmsdale

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love exploring the hiking and biking trails in Helmsdale. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Strath of Kildonan Trail: This 7.5-mile trail runs along the Strath of Kildonan and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valley.

  • The Badbea Clearance Village Trail: This 3-mile trail takes you through the historic village of Badbea, where the local people lived during the Clearances. You’ll see some poignant memorials to those who lost their lives during this time.

  • The Hill o’ Many Stanes Trail: This trail takes you to a mysterious collection of standing stones that date back to the Bronze Age. It offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Helmsdale: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Helmsdale, here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Fishing Trips: Helmsdale is a popular destination for salmon and trout fishing. You can book a fishing trip with a local guide and try your luck on the Helmsdale River.

  • Wildlife Tours: The countryside around Helmsdale is home to a rich variety of wildlife, including red deer, eagles, and otters. You can book a wildlife tour and see these animals in their natural habitat.

  • Food and Drink Tours: Helmsdale is known for its delicious local cuisine, including freshly caught fish and local game. You can book a food and drink tour and sample some of the best food and drink the town has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Helmsdale

If you’re planning a one-week trip to Helmsdale, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Helmsdale. Take a walk along the riverbank and explore the town.

  • Day 2: Visit the Timespan Museum and Arts Centre. Have lunch at one of the local seafood restaurants.

  • Day 3: Take a hiking or biking tour of the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 4: Visit Dunrobin Castle.

  • Day 5: Book a fishing trip on the Helmsdale River.

  • Day 6: Take a wildlife tour and see the local wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Day 7: Enjoy some of the local food and drink on a food and drink tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Helmsdale

Helmsdale is known for its delicious local cuisine, including freshly caught fish and local game. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A thick soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and cream.

  • Venison: A local game that’s delicious when marinaded and served with seasonal vegetables.

  • Smoked Salmon: A local specialty that’s a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Highland Whisky: Helmsdale is located near some of Scotland’s most famous whisky distilleries, including Glenmorangie and Dalmore.

  • Belhaven Ale: A local beer that’s brewed in nearby Dunbar.

Where to Stay in Helmsdale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodation or luxury lodging, Helmsdale has plenty of options:

  • Bannockburn Inn: This charming inn offers comfortable rooms and delicious food.

  • Dunroamin Hotel: Located near the beach, this hotel has stunning sea views and friendly staff.

  • Helmsdale Hostel: A budget-friendly option that’s perfect for backpackers.

What to Buy in Helmsdale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Helmsdale has a rich tradition of craft making, and there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some top picks:

  • Tartan: Helmsdale has its own tartan, which makes a great souvenir.

  • Local Art: There are several galleries and artists in the area who produce beautiful local art.

  • Knitwear: Helmsdale has a tradition of knitting, and you can buy locally made knitwear, including sweaters and scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Helmsdale

Helmsdale has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when you visit:

  • Scottish Gaelic is still spoken in some parts of Helmsdale, although English is the predominant language.

  • The locals are friendly and welcoming but may be reserved at first.

  • The local cuisine is an important part of the culture, and it’s worth trying some of the local dishes.

Best Time to Visit Helmsdale

Helmsdale is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests:

  • Spring (March to May): The countryside comes alive with blossoming flowers and new wildlife.

  • Summer (June to August): The weather is warm, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

  • Autumn (September to November): The fall colors are stunning, and it’s a great time for hiking and biking.

  • Winter (December to February): The town is quieter, but it’s a great time to enjoy the local winter sports and cozy up by a fire with a good book.

Do’s and Don’ts of Helmsdale

When visiting Helmsdale, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take a hike or bike ride in the beautiful countryside.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in the countryside.

  • Ignore the local wildlife. Keep a respectful distance.

  • Cause any damage to historical sites or monuments.

Safety and Health

Here’s some advice to help you stay safe and healthy during your visit to Helmsdale:

Safety in Helmsdale

  • The town is generally safe, but it’s a good idea to take basic precautions, such as locking your doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

  • If you’re hiking in the countryside, be sure to wear appropriate clothing and footwear and bring a map and compass.

Health in Helmsdale

  • The town has a small medical center, but for serious medical issues, you may need to travel to a larger center.

  • The tap water is safe to drink, and there are no major health concerns to be aware of.

Getting to Helmsdale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Helmsdale is easy, thanks to its location on the A9 highway. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Helmsdale

  • Inverness Airport: The closest airport, located about 70 miles south of Helmsdale.

  • Wick Airport: About 20 miles north of Helmsdale, with flights to and from Edinburgh and Glasgow.

Bus Stations Near Helmsdale

  • The X99 bus runs between Inverness and Thurso, with stops in Helmsdale.

  • The 25 bus runs between Helmsdale and Inverness.

Other Options

You can also drive to Helmsdale, or take a train to nearby Helmsdale station.

How to Get Around in Helmsdale

The town is small and it’s easy to walk around. You can also hire a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Helmsdale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re keen to explore more of the Scottish Highlands, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • John o’ Groats: The most northerly point of mainland Britain.

  • Cairngorms National Park: A beautiful national park that’s perfect for outdoor activities.

  • Orkney Islands: A group of beautiful islands that are home to ancient and historic sites.

Conclusion

Helmsdale is a beautiful town that’s perfect for those looking to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its stunning landscapes, interesting museums, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Helmsdale offers a unique and authentic Scottish experience. Use this travel guide to plan your next vacation and explore everything that Helmsdale has to offer.
