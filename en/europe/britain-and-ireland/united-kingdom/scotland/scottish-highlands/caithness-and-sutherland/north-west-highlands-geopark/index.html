Introduction to North West Highlands Geopark

North West Highlands Geopark is a 1,500 km² area in the Scottish Highlands that features some of the most breathtaking scenery in Scotland. The park is home to soaring mountains, rare wildlife, and geological marvels that date back over three billion years. If you’re looking for an escape from the hectic city life, this Geopark is one of the best places to visit in Scotland.

List of Places to Visit in North West Highlands Geopark

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Museum of Scottish Lighthouses

The museum boasts the largest collection of lighthouse-related objects in the world, charting the story of manned lighthouses demonstrating the challenges of protecting mariners from Scotland’s rugged coast. Audio tours and interactive exhibits make this a great place to learn about the development of lifesaving technology, including foghorns and light types used on Scotland’s coast and lighthouses.

Caithness Horizons Museum

The Caithness Horizons Museum exhibits that bring to life the diverse culture and history of Caithness with interactive and engaging displays. The museum also features collections showcasing the early history of the region to industrial developments in the 19th and 20th centuries.

Natural Attractions of North West Highlands Geopark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sandwood Bay

Located on the western coast of Scotland, Sandwood Bay is a remote and untouched bay that’s worth every minute of the trek on the Sandwood Bay Trail. This beach highlights one of Scotland’s most captivating scenarios, a beautiful white sandy beach surrounded by terra firma. Since this beach is a great place to view the sunset, many tourists camp near this area to observe nature’s beauty.

Smoo Cave

Smoo Cave is a unique place where the walls of the cave itself tells a story of how it was formed. This magical creation of nature, in the form of a vast limestone cave, consisting of three large chambers connected by passages, is situated in Durness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North West Highlands Geopark

Old Man of Stoer

One of the best walks in North West Highlands Geopark, the Old Man of Stoer is a unique rock formation that juts out of the sea. The three-mile walk takes you along the rugged coastline, where you’ll see stunning views of the North Sea and a variety of wildlife.

Cape Wrath Trail

For the more adventurous traveler, the Cape Wrath Trail is a challenging hiking trail that spans over 200 miles through the Scottish Highlands. This demanding route follows a remote and isolated path and offers views of the incredible coastlines, mountains, and rivers of the Geopark.

Best Things to Do in North West Highlands Geopark: Activities and Tours

Lochinver Larder Walking Tour

Get a taste of the local flavors with a walking gastronomic tour which includes stops at the Lochinver Larder and the Lochinver Smokery, where you can sample traditional Scottish delicacies such as smoked salmon and Arbroath smokies.

Boat Ride in Loch Maree

Take a boat ride around the stunning Loch Maree, a freshwater lake popular among anglers and hikers. The boat ride provides opportunities for fishing and hiking around the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of North West Highlands Geopark

Must-Try Dishes

Cullen Skink

A hearty soup made from potatoes, smoked haddock, and onions, Cullen Skink is a popular local dish in the north of Scotland. It originated in the town of Cullen and is often served with warm bread or croutons.

Local Beverages

Highland Whiskey

Whiskey is undoubtedly one of Scotland’s most famous exports and the North West Highlands Geopark is home to numerous distilleries, including Glenmorangie and Balblair. You can take a tour, sample some of the region’s most delightful aged single malt whiskey and learn about the distilling process.

Where to Stay in North West Highlands Geopark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Torridon

The Torridon is a stunning luxury hotel located in the heart of North West Highlands Geopark. The hotel offers a variety of luxury suites, gourmet dining, outdoor activities, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Inver Lodge

The Inver Lodge is a cozy 4-star hotel overlooking Lochinver Bay. It offers luxurious rooms, excellent cuisine, and easy access to nearby attractions such as the Assynt Mountains.

What to Buy in North West Highlands Geopark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The region is known for its world-renowned tweeds and hand-knit woolens producing products crafted from the finest local cashmere and wool that radiates Scotland’s wild and rugged style.

Local Culture and Customs of North West Highlands Geopark

Scotland is a multicultural country, and the North West Highlands Geopark has a unique culture with its own dialect and customs. Scots are friendly and hospitable to visitors, with visitors being welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit North West Highlands Geopark

The best time to visit the North West Highlands Geopark is during the summer months of June, July, and August, with the longest daylight hours of the year making it possible to explore more. The region’s warmest temperatures are typically in July.

Do’s and Don’ts of North West Highlands Geopark

Do:

  • Take care and respect wildlife when out on walks
  • Bring durable and waterproof shoes, as it’s often wet and muddy
  • Be prepared for changeable weather conditions, as they can occur in a short timespan

Don’t:

  • Don’t cause disturbance or damage to the environment or landscape
  • Don’t litter and take your rubbish with you
  • Don’t have fires except in designated campsites and NEVER in the wild

Safety and Health

Safety in North West Highlands Geopark

The North West Highlands Geopark is an entirely safe place to visit. However, visitors must beware of the ever-changing weather conditions and be prepared for it.

Health in North West Highlands Geopark

Visitors to the North West Highlands Geopark should take extra care while traveling to avoid accidents such as falling rocks, slippery cliffs, and at times fierce weather conditions.

Getting to North West Highlands Geopark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North West Highlands Geopark

The closest airport is Inverness Airport, but Edinburgh and Glasgow airports are both about a half-day’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near North West Highlands Geopark

Citylink provides direct coach service daily to Ullapool and Thurso, with connecting services to other parts of the region.

Other Options

Some other options for getting around include car rentals and taxis options available which operates all over the region.

How to Get Around in North West Highlands Geopark

Most of the North West Highlands Geopark is rural, so walking and cycling are popular. Car rentals, local bus services, and taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from North West Highlands Geopark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Inverness

Inverness is located just over an hour’s drive from the North West Highlands Geopark and is home to a variety of attractions, including the Inverness Castle, where visitors can take a guided tour and see some of Scotland’s history.

Stornoway

Stornoway is a town on the Isle of Lewis, just three hours’ drive from the North West Highlands Geopark. Visitors can explore the town’s history, take hikes through the hills, and enjoy local cuisine.

Conclusion

North West Highlands Geopark is undoubtedly one of Scotland’s most stunning areas, offering a variety of cultural and natural attractions that are well worth exploring. With our comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip and enjoy the region’s unique beauty, local cuisines and discover new horizons for the nature lover and travelers alike.
