Introduction to Wick

Located in the Caithness and Sutherland region of the Scottish Highlands, Wick is a picturesque town brimming with history, culture, and natural beauty. Situated on the east coast of Scotland, Wick boasts stunning landscapes, scenic views, and an enviable coastline. This guide will take you through the best places to visit, activities to do, where to stay, and what to eat and drink in Wick.

List of Places to Visit in Wick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wick Heritage Museum: Celebrates the town’s vibrant history, cultural heritage, and ancient Pictish roots.

  • The Castle of Mey: This stunning estate was once owned by the Queen Mother, and it’s now a popular tourist attraction, with extensive gardens, tours of the castle, and a cafe.

  • St. Fergus Chapel: This medieval chapel dates back to the 13th century and is located close to the Wick Heritage Museum. It’s only a short walk uphill and is free to visit.

Natural Attractions of Wick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Duncansby Head: Located just a few miles from Wick, Duncansby Head is famous for its stunning sea stacks, cliff-top walks, and outstanding views of the Pentland Firth and Orkney islands.

  • Castle Sinclair Girnigoe: This atmospheric castle ruin is located on the coast of Caithness, a fifteen-minute drive from Wick. It’s free to visit and offers panoramic views of the North Sea.

  • The Whaligoe Steps: This amazing feat of engineering is a set of 320 steps that descend down to a small harbor. It’s perfect for exploring the coastline and is an excellent spot for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wick

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or just looking for a leisurely stroll while enjoying the fresh air, Wick has some of the best hiking trails in Scotland. Check out these exciting routes:

  • The John O’Groats Trail: While technically not in Wick, this coastal trail is only a few miles away and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It runs from Inverness to John O’ Groats and is perfect for hiking and cycling.

  • The South Head Trail: This 4-mile trail takes you on a steep climb to the top of South Head, offering stunning views of Wick Bay and the surrounding coastline.

Best Things to Do in Wick: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Boat Trip: Experience the stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding wildlife, including dolphins, seals, and a variety of seabirds.

  • Visit the Wick Bowling Club: This social club is perfect for a relaxing afternoon, offering a variety of drinks, snacks, and bowling games.

  • Go Fishing: If you’re feeling adventurous and love fishing, Wick’s Ullapool Loch and Loch Watten are perfect for catching brown trout.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wick

Day 1: Explore the Wick Heritage Museum and St. Fergus Chapel

Day 2: Visit Castle Sinclair Girnigoe and the Whaligoe Steps

Day 3: Take a boat trip to explore the surrounding coastline

Day 4: Hike the South Head Trail and enjoy the stunning views

Day 5: Visit the Castle of Mey, with its extensive gardens and tours of the castle

Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing afternoon at the Wick Bowling Club

Day 7: Experience fishing at Ullapool Loch or Loch Watten

Food and Drink Scene of Wick

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, all encased in a sheep’s stomach.

  • Cullen Skink: A creamy and hearty Scottish soup made with smoked haddock.

  • Venison: A staple in Scottish cuisine, venison is a lean, healthy meat that’s often served as a Sunday roast.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey: No trip to Scotland is complete without sampling the country’s national drink. Try some of the local whiskies, including Old Pulteney and Wolfburn.

  • Scottish Ale: Wick is home to the world-renowned Sinclair Brewery. Try their flagship ale, 70 Schilling.

Where to Stay in Wick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Norseman Hotel: A comfortable and well-appointed hotel overlooking Wick’s harbor.

  • The Mackay’s Hotel: Centrally located, this hotel offers a range of room sizes and on-site dining options.

  • The Old Tea Shop Guesthouse: A cozy and welcoming guesthouse, perfect for those looking for a more homey experience.

What to Buy in Wick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Caithness Glassware: Glassware made using traditional techniques and often handcrafted.

  • Scottish Tartans: From blankets to scarves to kilts, the town offers a variety of tartan merchandise to take home.

  • Smoked Fish: Wick has a large fishing industry, so it’s no surprise that they are experts in preparing and smoking fish, like salmon and haddock.

Local Culture and Customs of Wick

The people of Wick are friendly and welcoming, and the town’s culture is deeply rooted in Scottish tradition and history. Visitors should respect local customs and be aware of their surroundings, especially if exploring the countryside and smaller villages.

Best Time to Visit Wick

June to September is the best time to visit Wick, with the weather being mild and dry. The days are long, and there’s a general festive atmosphere as the town comes alive with visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wick

Do:

  • Do visit the Wick Heritage Museum and St. Fergus Chapel.

  • Do take a boat trip around the surrounding coastline.

  • Do enjoy locally produced food, such as smoked fish and venison.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

  • Don’t forget to try some of the local whiskey and ale.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wick

Wick is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates. Visitors should be cautious when exploring the countryside, as there may be uneven terrain and unstable cliffs along the coastline.

Health in Wick

While there are no specific health risks associated with Wick, visitors should wear appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities and be aware of the potential sun exposure during the summer months.

Getting to Wick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wick

The nearest airport to Wick is Wick John O’Groats Airport, which is located 3 miles north of Wick town center.

Bus Stations Near Wick

The main bus station in Wick is located in the town center, with regular services to and from Inverness and other towns and cities in Scotland.

Other Options

Traveling by train is also an option, with the nearest station located in Thurso, 17 miles west of Wick.

How to Get Around in Wick

Wick is a small town, so most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those looking to explore the surrounding countryside and villages.

Where to Go Next from Wick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • John O’Groats: A small village located just a few miles from Wick, where you can visit the northernmost point in mainland Scotland.

  • Dornoch: A historic small town with a magnificent cathedral and stunning beachfront.

  • Orkney Islands: A chain of 70 islands, located a short ferry ride from mainland Scotland. Explore prehistoric villages, stunning natural scenery, and fascinating Viking history.

Conclusion

Wick is a hidden treasure in the Scottish Highlands, offering a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re exploring the beautiful coastline, sampling local cuisine or relaxing in one of the town’s cozy guesthouses, Wick is a must-visit destination for all those looking to explore the best of Scotland.
