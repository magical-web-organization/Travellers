Introduction to Scottish Highlands

Situated in Scotland, also known as the “Land of the Brave,” Scottish Highlands is a vast and rugged region known for its stunning natural landscapes, friendly locals, rich history, and diverse culture. From towering mountains and deep lochs to charming villages and bustling cities, Scottish Highlands offers something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Scottish Highlands

Whether you’re a nature lover or history buff, Scottish Highlands is brimming with attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-see places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Culloden Battlefield: Visit the site of the famous 1746 battle between the Jacobite and British troops.
  • Loch Ness: Explore the legendary loch and learn about the mysterious creature, Nessie, at the Loch Ness Centre and Exhibition.
  • Urquhart Castle: Admire the ruins of this medieval fortress overlooking Loch Ness.
  • Inverness Castle: Take in the views of Inverness from this historic castle, which now serves as a courthouse.

Natural Attractions of Scottish Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isle of Skye: Discover the breathtaking scenery of Scotland’s largest island, including the Old Man of Storr and the Fairy Pools.
  • Glencoe: Hike through the stunning valley, which has been featured in several movies, including Harry Potter.
  • Cairngorms National Park: Explore the UK’s largest national park, home to diverse wildlife and landscapes.
  • Ben Nevis: Scale the UK’s highest mountain for panoramic views of Scottish Highlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scottish Highlands

For outdoor enthusiasts, Scottish Highlands is a paradise. Here are some of our top hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • West Highland Way: This 96-mile trek takes you through some of Scottish Highlands’ most stunning landscapes.
  • Great Glen Way: Follow the path of the majestic Caledonian Canal, from Fort William to Inverness.
  • Speyside Way: Discover the famous whisky region on this 65-mile trail through the countryside and forests.
  • Cairngorms Circuit: Hike through the Cairngorms, passing lakes, forests, and towering peaks.

Best Things to Do in Scottish Highlands: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Scottish Highlands. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in the region:

  • Whisky Tours: Visit some of Scotland’s most famous distilleries and sample the country’s signature drink.
  • Wildlife Safari: Spot red deer, eagles, otters, and other animals on a guided tour.
  • Jacobite Steam Train: Board the train featured in the Harry Potter films, and journey through stunning Scottish scenery.
  • Stargazing: With minimal light pollution, Scottish Highlands offers prime stargazing opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scottish Highlands

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your week in Scottish Highlands:

Food and Drink Scene of Scottish Highlands

Scottish cuisine is a unique blend of traditional and modern flavors, influenced by the region’s geography and history. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish made from sheep’s organs, onions, and oats.
  • Cullen skink: Smoked haddock soup, traditionally served with buttered bread.
  • Cranachan: A dessert made with whipped cream, raspberries, toasted oats, and drizzled with honey and whisky.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland’s most famous drink, produced in several distilleries in Scottish Highlands.
  • Cairngorm Brewery: Sample local craft beer, brewed using Scottish water and malts.

Where to Stay in Scottish Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy cottages to luxurious hotels, Scottish Highlands offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Kylesku Hotel: Enjoy stunning views of Loch Glencoul, and sample delicious seafood in this luxury hotel.
  • Bunchrew House Hotel: Stay in a historic 17th-century mansion overlooking Beauly Firth and indulge in fine dining.
  • Caledonian Backpackers: Budget-friendly hostel in central Inverness, perfect for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Scottish Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Scottish Highlands with you when you leave. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Tartan: Choose from a range of traditional Scottish patterns, including the iconic tartan.
  • Whisky: Bring home a bottle of your favorite whisky, or choose a rare and unique blend.
  • Harris Tweed: Hand-woven wool, used to make a range of clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Scottish Highlands

Scottish Highlands has a rich culture and history, and it’s important to understand and respect local customs and traditions. Here are some of the most notable:

  • Bagpiping: The national instrument of Scotland, and commonly played at events and celebrations.
  • Gaelic: An ancient Celtic language still spoken by some locals.
  • Highland Games: Traditional events featuring strength and skill contests, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Scottish Highlands

The best time to visit Scottish Highlands is from May to September, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, the region has breathtaking scenery year-round, and each season brings its charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scottish Highlands

To make the most of your trip to Scottish Highlands, here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try Scottish cuisine, including haggis, Cullen skink, and Highland beef.
  • Tip 10-15% in restaurants if the service is good.
  • Explore the great outdoors, and take on hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Refer to Scotland as part of England.
  • Underestimate the weather, come prepared for all types of conditions.
  • Take photographs of locals without permission.

Safety and Health

For a safe and healthy trip to Scottish Highlands, here are some important tips to follow:

Safety in Scottish Highlands

  • Be aware of weather conditions, especially when hiking or driving in remote areas.
  • Take precautions against ticks and midges, which can be present in the region.
  • Follow the Scottish Outdoor Access Code, which grants public access to most land, but with certain conditions.

Health in Scottish Highlands

  • Ensure that all your routine vaccinations are up to date.
  • If you plan on hiking or camping, bring appropriate water filtration or purification methods to avoid drinking contaminated water.

Getting to Scottish Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Scottish Highlands, depending on your departure point. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Scottish Highlands

  • Inverness Airport: Serves the capital city of the region.
  • Aberdeen International Airport: Located a few hours’ drive from Scottish Highlands.

Bus Stations Near Scottish Highlands

  • Inverness Bus Station: Provides connections to several destinations in the region.
  • Fort William Bus Station: Offers routes to nearby hotspots like Glencoe and Ben Nevis.

Other Options

  • Train: ScotRail offers services to Scottish Highlands, including the famous Jacobite Steam Train.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the region, as it offers flexibility and independence.

How to Get Around in Scottish Highlands

If you plan on traveling extensively within Scottish Highlands, a car rental is the best option. However, there are also other transportation options, including:

  • Bus: Several bus companies, including Citylink and Stagecoach, provide services within Scottish Highlands.
  • Train: The Jacobite Steam Train is an iconic must-try experience, while ScotRail provides other local services.
  • Bike: Rent a bike or bring your own to explore Scottish Highlands’ scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Scottish Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Scottish Highlands is surrounded by other exciting destinations, making it the perfect starting point for further adventures. Here are some nearby places to explore:

  • Edinburgh: Scotland’s capital city, known for its stunning architecture and rich history.
  • Glasgow: Scotland’s largest city, offering a vibrant nightlife and thriving arts scene.
  • Isle of Arran: A picturesque island, known as Scotland in miniature, with lush forests and stunning coastline.

Conclusion

Scottish Highlands is a must-see destination for adventurers, nature enthusiasts, and history buffs of all ages. Whether you’re exploring the rugged terrain, admiring the stunning natural vistas, or discovering the region’s rich culture and history, Scottish Highlands offers an unforgettable experience.
