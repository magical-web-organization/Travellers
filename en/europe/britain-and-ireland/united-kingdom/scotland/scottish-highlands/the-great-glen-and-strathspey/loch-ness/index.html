Introduction to Loch Ness

Loch Ness is a picturesque freshwater loch in the Scottish Highlands that stretches for 37 kilometers. The lake is known for its deep and murky waters, and most famously, for Nessie, the Loch Ness Monster. The lake has become one of the most visited tourist spots in Scotland, offering something for everyone, from its natural beauty to its rich history.

In this comprehensive travel guide, you will find everything you need to know about visiting Loch Ness, including places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Loch Ness

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Urquhart Castle

Urquhart Castle is one of the most iconic castles in Scotland, located on the banks of Loch Ness. It was built in the 13th century and was an important stronghold during the Wars of Scottish Independence. The castle offers stunning views of the lake and is surrounded by breathtaking Scottish countryside.

Loch Ness Centre and Exhibition

If you’re interested in learning more about the history and myths surrounding the Loch Ness Monster, then the Loch Ness Centre and Exhibition is a must-visit attraction. The exhibition uses multimedia displays to bring the story of Nessie to life, and also offers information about the geology and ecology of the area.

Caledonian Canal

The Caledonian Canal is a 60-mile-long canal that runs through the Great Glen, connecting Inverness to Fort William. The canal was built in the early 19th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding Scottish countryside.

Natural Attractions of Loch Ness: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Great Glen Way

The Great Glen Way is a long-distance hiking trail that runs for 117 kilometers from Fort William to Inverness. The trail takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Scotland, including Loch Ness and the Caledonian Canal. The trail is suitable for hikers of all abilities and can be completed in six-seven days.

Fort Augustus

Fort Augustus is a picturesque village on the southern banks of Loch Ness. The village offers stunning views of the lake and is a popular spot for fishing and boating. There are also several hiking trails in the area, including the 79-mile-long South Loch Ness Trail.

Falls of Foyers

The Falls of Foyers is a spectacular waterfall located on the eastern shore of Loch Ness. The waterfall is surrounded by beautiful Scottish woodland and offers stunning views of the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Loch Ness

Great Glen Way

The Great Glen Way is a popular long-distance hiking trail that runs through the Great Glen, from Fort William to Inverness. The trail covers 117 kilometers and takes around six-seven days to complete. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding Scottish countryside, including Loch Ness.

South Loch Ness Trail

The South Loch Ness Trail is a 79-mile-long hiking trail that takes you from Fort Augustus to Inverness. The trail is suitable for hikers of all abilities and offers stunning views of Loch Ness and the surrounding Scottish countryside.

Loch Ness 360

Loch Ness 360 is a new long-distance cycling route that circles around the entire perimeter of Loch Ness. The route is 80 miles long and takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Scotland. The route can be completed over a few days, or you can take your time and enjoy the scenery over a week or more.

Best Things to Do in Loch Ness: Activities and Tours

Loch Ness Cruise

A cruise on Loch Ness is a must-do activity when visiting the lake. There are several companies that offer sightseeing tours of the lake, with some also offering evening cruises that allow you to see the sunset over the Scottish Highlands.

Monster Hunting

No trip to Loch Ness is complete without a bit of monster hunting. There are several companies that offer boat tours and sonar scanning to try and spot Nessie. Some tours even claim to have a high success rate, so it’s worth keeping an eye out on the lake.

Fishing

Fishing is a popular activity on Loch Ness, with the lake being home to a variety of fish, including trout and salmon. You can hire a boat and fishing equipment from several places around the lake, or you can join a guided fishing tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Loch Ness

Must-Try Dishes

Haggis

Haggis is a traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s heart, liver and lungs, mixed with oats and spices and cooked inside a sheep’s stomach. It might not sound that appetizing, but it’s definitely worth trying when in Scotland.

Cullen Skink

Cullen Skink is a creamy soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions. It’s a hearty and delicious dish that’s perfect for a cold day, and it’s one of Scotland’s most famous soups.

Local Beverages

Whisky

Scotland is world-famous for its whisky, and there are several distilleries located near Loch Ness that offer tours and tastings. You definitely shouldn’t leave Scotland without trying at least a dram of whisky.

Scottish Ale

Scotland has a long tradition of brewing beer, and there are several excellent Scottish ales that you can try when in Loch Ness. Most local pubs offer a range of ales from local breweries, so it’s worth visiting a few pubs to find your favorite.

Where to Stay in Loch Ness: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Loch Ness Lodge Hotel

The Loch Ness Lodge Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in Drumnadrochit, with stunning views of Loch Ness. The hotel offers elegant rooms, a restaurant serving locally sourced food, and a spa where you can relax and unwind after a day of exploring the Scottish Highlands.

Loch Ness Hostel

The Loch Ness Hostel is a popular choice for budget travelers, located in the picturesque village of Fort Augustus. The hostel offers comfortable dormitory-style rooms, a communal kitchen, and a cozy lounge area.

Craigdarroch Inn

The Craigdarroch Inn is a charming guesthouse located in Foyers, overlooking Loch Ness. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant serving Scottish cuisine, and a bar with a wide selection of local whiskies.

Local Culture and Customs of Loch Ness

Scotland has a rich history and culture, and the locals take great pride in their heritage. It’s worth taking the time to learn a little bit about Scottish customs and traditions before your trip, and to show respect for the country’s history and culture when you’re there.

Best Time to Visit Loch Ness

The best time to visit Loch Ness is in the spring or summer, when the weather is usually mild and dry. However, Loch Ness is beautiful year-round, and each season offers something unique. Autumn is particularly beautiful, with the changing colors of the surrounding Scottish countryside making for stunning views.

Safety and Health

Safety in Loch Ness

Loch Ness is generally a very safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions when you’re in a new environment. It’s worth familiarizing yourself with the local emergency services and carrying a first-aid kit with you when exploring the Scottish Highlands.

Health in Loch Ness

Scotland has a good healthcare system, and tourists are entitled to free emergency treatment if required. It’s always important to carry your travel insurance details with you, and to take any necessary medication with you when traveling.

Getting to Loch Ness: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Loch Ness

The nearest airport to Loch Ness is Inverness Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers away. The airport offers regular flights to several UK and European destinations, making it a convenient option for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Loch Ness

The main bus station in Inverness is the Inverness Bus Station, which offers connections to several destinations in the Scottish Highlands, including Fort William and Aberdeen. There are also several local buses that travel along the shores of Loch Ness.

Other Options

If you’re arriving from the UK, you can also travel to Loch Ness by car or train. The nearest train station to Loch Ness is in Inverness, and there are regular trains from both Glasgow and Edinburgh.

How to Get Around in Loch Ness

The easiest way to get around Loch Ness is by car, as this gives you the freedom to explore the Scottish Highlands at your own pace. However, there are also several local buses that travel along the shores of Loch Ness, as well as several taxi companies that operate in the area.

Where to Go Next from Loch Ness: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Isle of Skye

The Isle of Skye is a breathtakingly beautiful island located off the west coast of Scotland. The island is home to stunning landscapes, historical landmarks, and beautiful beaches, and is a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Cairngorms National Park

The Cairngorms National Park is the largest national park in the UK, covering over 4,500 square kilometers of the Scottish Highlands. The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including red deer, ospreys, and golden eagles, and offers some of the best hiking and skiing opportunities in Scotland.
