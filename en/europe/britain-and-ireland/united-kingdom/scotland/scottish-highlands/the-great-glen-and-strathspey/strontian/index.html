Introduction to Strontian

Located in the Scottish Highlands, Strontian is a charming village enveloped by the rugged highland landscapes, stunning lochs, and deep valleys. Its history dates back to the Bronze Age, and it’s named after the mineral Strontianite, which was first discovered here in the 18th century. Strontian is also the gateway for exploring Ardnamurchan Peninsula, the most westerly point in mainland Britain.

List of Places to Visit in Strontian

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Strontian has a rich cultural heritage, which you can explore through the following attractions:

1. Sunart Oakwoods

Sunart Oakwoods is a network of ancient oak forests that has been a habitat for various flora and fauna since the last Ice Age. It has three nature reserves, namely, Ariundle, Garbh Eilean, and Rahoy Hills, which offer various walking trails and picnic areas.

2. Highland Titles Nature Reserve

This 4,000-acre nature reserve is a privately owned territory that aims to conserve the Scottish wildlife. It offers guided tours, walks, and activities such as pond dipping and bird watching.

3. Glenborrodale Castle

Glenborrodale Castle is a stunning Victorian Baronial mansion nestled on the foothills of Ardnamurchan Peninsula. It was built in 1902, and now it’s a beautiful retreat offering luxurious accommodation, spa treatments, and fine dining.

Natural Attractions of Strontian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Strontian boasts a plethora of natural attractions for all types of travelers:

1. Ardnamurchan Lighthouse

Ardnamurchan Lighthouse is a must-see attraction, which offers breathtaking views of the sea, Skye, and the Small Isles. It’s open from April to October, and you can climb up to the top to enjoy the panoramic vistas.

2. Loch Sunart

Loch Sunart is one of the most picturesque lochs in Scotland, surrounded by the steep hillsides, ancient forests, and rocky shores. You can take a scenic cruise, go fishing, or rent a kayak to explore its tranquil waters.

3. Sanna Bay

Sanna Bay is an unspoilt white-sand beach famous for its crystal-clear waters, dunes, and rock pools. It’s a perfect spot for picnics, sunbathing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strontian

Strontian has a variety of hiking and biking trails catering to all levels of fitness:

1. Fascadale Woods

Fascadale Woods is a network of oak woods stretching across the shores of Loch Sunart. It has various walking trails, picnic areas, and viewpoints offering panoramic views of the loch.

2. Ariundle Oakwood

Ariundle Oakwood is an ancient oak forest with an extensive network of walking and biking trails covering the woodland and riverside areas. It’s also home to various wildlife, including red squirrels, pine martens, and badgers.

3. Beinn Resipol

Beinn Resipol is a picturesque hill offering panoramic views of the Loch and the surrounding valleys. It’s a popular spot for hiking, hill-walking, and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Strontian: Activities and Tours

Strontian offers a variety of activities and tours, catering to all interests:

1. Wildlife Tours

Wildlife tours are popular in Strontian due to its abundant fauna, such as peregrine falcon, otters, white-tailed sea eagles, and seals. You can take guided boat or walking tours, or rent a bike and explore the back roads.

2. Boat Tours in Sunart

Boat tours in Sunart are a fantastic way to explore the Loch’s uninhabited islands, ancient castle ruins, and marine life. You can also go fishing or dive in secluded spots.

3. Ardnamurchan Point Lighthouse

Ardnamurchan Point Lighthouse is a popular attraction offering guided tours and educational programs. You can climb up to the top to have a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strontian

To make the most of Strontian, it’s recommended to follow this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the village and its surroundings.
Day 2 Hike or bike in Fascadale Woods.
Day 3 Take a wildlife tour or go fishing in Loch Sunart.
Day 4 Visit the Highland Titles Nature Reserve.
Day 5 Take a boat tour around the Sunart Islands.
Day 6 North Coast 500 road trip including a visit to Glenborrodale Castle.
Day 7 Visit Ardnamurchan Lighthouse and Sanna Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Strontian

Strontian has a vibrant food and drink scene, with various restaurants, cafes, and bars offering local and international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Cullen Skink

Cullen Skink is a classic Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions, served with bread or crackers.

2. Arbroath Smokies

Arbroath Smokies is a delicacy made by smoking haddock over hardwood chips. It’s usually served as a starter or snack.

3. Haggis

Haggis is a traditional Scottish dish made with sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, minced with onions, oatmeal, and spices, served with neeps and tatties.

Local Beverages

1. Whisky

Scotland is famous for its whisky, and Strontian has some of the best distilleries offering guided tours and whisky tasting sessions.

2. Real Ale

Strontian has numerous pubs and bars serving real ale, made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Strontian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Strontian has various options for accommodation, catering to all budgets and requirements:

1. Sunart Camping

Sunart Camping is a popular campsite in Strontian, offering various camping and glamping options, including yurts, bell tents, caravans, and pitch spaces.

2. Kilcamb Lodge Hotel

Kilcamb Lodge Hotel is a luxurious hotel in a stunning Victorian mansion, offering fine dining, spa treatments, and panoramic views of Loch Sunart.

3. Strontian Hotel

The Strontian Hotel is a family-run hotel offering comfortable accommodation, traditional Scottish cuisine, and friendly service.

What to Buy in Strontian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Strontian is famous for its locally produced crafts and products, including:

1. Whisky

Strontian has several distilleries producing whisky, which you can purchase as a souvenir or gift.

2. Aran Knitwear

Aran knitwear is a traditional Scottish clothing known for its intricate patterns and high-quality wool. You can purchase it from various local shops and boutiques.

3. Jewellery

Strontian has several jewellery makers producing unique and beautiful pieces inspired by the Scottish landscapes and traditions.

Local Culture and Customs of Strontian

Strontian has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s proud of its traditions and customs. Some of the notable customs include the traditional Highland Games, music festivals, and ceilidhs.

Best Time to Visit Strontian

The best time to visit Strontian is from April to October, as the weather is mild, and most of the attractions and tours are open during this period. However, if you’re a wildlife enthusiast, you may consider visiting in winter when you can see otters, eagles, and other species with greater ease.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strontian

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Follow the designated trails and respect the wildlife.
  • Take the time to appreciate the natural beauty of Strontian.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Damage or disturb the wildlife or their habitat.
  • Overexert yourself during outdoor activities.
  • Ignore safety guidelines and precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Strontian

The tourism infrastructure of Strontian is generally safe and well-regulated. However, it’s essential to follow some safety guidelines, especially during outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. These include:

  • Wear proper clothing and footwear.
  • Carry a map, compass, and first aid kit.
  • Check the weather forecast before heading out.
  • Inform someone of your itinerary and expected return time.

Health in Strontian

Strontian has a good medical facility, with a local GP and a hospital with 24-hour emergency services. It’s also essential to take some health precautions:

  • Drink only safe and clean water.
  • Apply sunscreen and use insect repellent.
  • Carry any necessary medication or prescriptions.
  • Be mindful of any allergies or health conditions.

Getting to Strontian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Strontian

The nearest airports to Strontian are:

  • Glasgow International Airport (130 miles)
  • Edinburgh Airport (147 miles)

Both airports have taxi and car rental services, and you can also take a shuttle bus to Fort William and then catch a local bus or taxi to Strontian.

Bus Stations Near Strontian

Strontian is serviced by several bus routes:

  • Scottish Citylink: Glasgow - Fort William - Ullapool
  • Scottish Citylink: Edinburgh - Fort William
  • West Coast Motors: Oban - Strontian - Kilchoan

Other Options

You can also reach Strontian by train from Glasgow to Fort William and then catch a bus or taxi to Strontian.

How to Get Around in Strontian

The best way to get around in Strontian is by car, as public transport options are limited, and most attractions and trails are spread out. You can rent a car from several car rental companies in Glasgow and Edinburgh airports.

Where to Go Next from Strontian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Strontian is a fantastic gateway to exploring the Scottish Highlands and nearby areas. Some of the popular excursions include:

  • Ardnamurchan Peninsula: Visit the picturesque Ardnamurchan Point Lighthouse, ancient castle ruins, and stunning beaches.

  • Isle of Mull: Take a ferry from Kilchoan to the Isle of Mull, known for its rare wildlife, ancient castles, and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Strontian is a hidden gem in the Scottish Highlands, offering a perfect blend of culture, heritage, and natural beauty. Its tranquil landscapes, fascinating history, and vibrant culture make it an ideal destination for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-filled holiday. So, pack your bags and explore this beautiful village nestled amongst the rugged Scottish Highlands.
