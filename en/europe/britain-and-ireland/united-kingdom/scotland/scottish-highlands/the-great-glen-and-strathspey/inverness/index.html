Introduction to Inverness

Inverness is a charming town located in the Scottish Highlands, known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals. The town lies at the mouth of the River Ness, making it a popular spot for fishing and cruising. It’s the perfect base for exploring the Scottish Highlands, as it’s close to many other scenic destinations, including the famous Loch Ness. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or just soaking up the local culture, Inverness has it all.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inverness

Day 1: Arrive in Inverness and explore the town centre, including Inverness Castle and the River Ness.

Day 2: Visit the Culloden Battlefield and Visitor Centre, followed by a hike on the Great Glen Way.

Day 3: Take a boat tour on Loch Ness and explore Urquhart Castle.

Day 4: Rent a bike and explore the South Loch Ness Trail.

Day 5: Visit the Inverness Museum and Art Gallery and Abertarff House.

Day 6: Go on a whisky tour and explore the local distilleries.

Day 7: Spend the day exploring the stunning Scottish Highlands, including the Cairngorms National Park.

Best Time to Visit Inverness

The best time to visit Inverness is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and sunny, and the days are long. However, the town is beautiful all year round, and each season offers unique experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inverness

Do:

  • Learn some basic Scottish phrases
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Attend a ceilidh
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take a boat tour on Loch Ness

Don’t:

  • Wear a kilt without proper Scottish attire (sporran, jacket, etc.)
  • Be disrespectful to bagpipers
  • Forget to tip in restaurants and bars
  • Ignore the stunning natural beauty of the area
  • Forget to take warm clothing and waterproofs

Safety and Health

Safety in Inverness

Inverness is generally a safe town, but common sense precautions should be taken to prevent theft and ensure personal safety. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of pickpockets and bag snatchers in crowded areas
  • Keep valuables out of sight and hidden away
  • Watch out for traffic and use designated crosswalks
  • Follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities

Health in Inverness

Inverness has good healthcare facilities, including hospitals and doctors’ offices. However, it’s always advisable to take out travel insurance before visiting any new destination. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of tick bites, particularly when hiking in wooded areas.

How to Get Around in Inverness

Inverness is a small town, so it’s easy to explore on foot. Bikes are also available for rent, and there’s a good local bus network. Taxis and private hire cars are also available, but they can be relatively expensive.
