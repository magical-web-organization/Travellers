Introduction to Grantown-on-Spey

Grantown-on-Spey is a charming town located in the Scottish Highlands, in The Great Glen and Strathspey. The town is home to stunning landscapes, rich history, and offers plenty of outdoor activities, making it a popular destination for travelers who are seeking a break from city life.

This comprehensive guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and local culture and customs in Grantown-on-Spey. Whether you’re planning a week-long holiday or a short weekend getaway, this guide will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Grantown-on-Spey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, there are plenty of places to visit in Grantown-on-Spey that will take you back in time. Here are some of the most notable culturally important attractions:

  • Grantown Museum: This museum showcases the history of the town and the surrounding area, including the Speyside whisky industry and the Highland Clearances.

  • Cromdale Church: This historic church dates back to the 18th century and is worth a visit for its beautiful architecture and peaceful atmosphere.

  • Ballindalloch Castle: Located just a short drive from Grantown-on-Spey, Ballindalloch Castle is a historic site with an impressive collection of art, antiques, and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Grantown-on-Spey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grantown-on-Spey is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, providing an abundance of outdoor activities and breathtaking views. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Cairngorms National Park: As the largest national park in the UK, Cairngorms National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Home to mountains, lochs, forests, and wildlife, there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy, such as hiking, biking, and skiing.

  • The Speyside Way: This 65-mile-long trail takes you through the stunning Scottish countryside, starting in Aviemore and ending in Buckie.

  • Lochindorb: This picturesque loch is a popular spot for fishing and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grantown-on-Spey

Grantown-on-Spey offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Scotland. Here are some of the most popular trails to explore:

  • The Anagach Woods: This woodland walk is an easy hike and perfect for families with children. With over 500 acres of woodland to explore, you can spot plenty of wildlife while enjoying the beautiful scenery.

  • Rothiemurchus Forest: Located just a short drive from Grantown-on-Spey, Rothiemurchus Forest offers a range of hiking and biking trails, including the popular Cairngorm Mountain Trail.

Best Things to Do in Grantown-on-Spey: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Grantown-on-Spey also offers a range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Whisky Tasting Tour: As Speyside is known for its whisky, take a tour to learn more about the process and sample some of the best whiskies in Scotland.

  • Golfing: Grantown-on-Spey Golf Club is a popular destination for golf enthusiasts, offering an 18-hole course with stunning views of the Cairngorm Mountains.

  • Fishing: The River Spey is famous for its salmon fishing, and there are many opportunities for fishing in and around Grantown-on-Spey.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grantown-on-Spey

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for those planning a trip to Grantown-on-Spey:

  • Day 1: Explore Grantown-on-Spey and visit the Grantown Museum.

  • Day 2: Drive to Ballindalloch Castle and take a tour.

  • Day 3: Hike in The Anagach Woods and enjoy the views.

  • Day 4: Take a tour of a local whisky distillery.

  • Day 5: Explore Cairngorms National Park and hike in Rothiemurchus Forest.

  • Day 6: Go fishing in the River Spey.

  • Day 7: Relax and unwind, taking in the beautiful Scottish scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Grantown-on-Spey

Must-Try Dishes

Scotland is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, and Grantown-on-Spey is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes to sample:

  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s offal and oats, haggis is a must-try for those who want to immerse themselves in the local cuisine.

  • Cullen Skink: A creamy soup made from smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions, Cullen Skink is a favorite among locals.

  • Cranachan: A dessert made from whipped cream, raspberries, and toasted oats, Cranachan is a sweet treat to end any meal.

Local Beverages

Apart from whisky, Scotland is also known for its beer and ale. Here are some local beverages to sample in Grantown-on-Spey:

  • Cairngorm Brewery: Located in Aviemore, Cairngorm Brewery offers a range of craft beers and ales to sample.

  • Orkney Brewery: Orkney Brewery is located just a short drive from Grantown-on-Spey and offers a range of award-winning beers and ales.

Where to Stay in Grantown-on-Spey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grantown-on-Spey offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Garth Hotel: Located in the heart of Grantown-on-Spey, The Garth Hotel is a historic hotel with a cozy and comfortable atmosphere.

  • Craiglynne Hotel: The Craiglynne Hotel is located on the outskirts of Grantown-on-Spey and offers stunning views of the Cairngorm Mountains.

  • The Spey: This family-run guesthouse is located in the center of Grantown-on-Spey and offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Grantown-on-Spey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, here are some of the best places to shop in Grantown-on-Spey:

  • Whisky Gift Shop: Located in the center of Grantown-on-Spey, the Whisky Gift Shop offers a range of whiskies and souvenirs.

  • Tartan Weaving Mill: The Tartan Weaving Mill is located in Aberfeldy and offers a range of Scottish clothing and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Grantown-on-Spey

Scotland has a rich history and unique culture, and Grantown-on-Spey is no exception. Here are some local customs and traditions worth experiencing:

  • Highland Games: Highland Games are a traditional Scottish event that showcases a range of athletic and cultural events, such as caber tossing, tug-of-war, and pipe band performances.

  • Ceilidh: A Ceilidh is a traditional Scottish social event that includes music, dancing, and storytelling.

Best Time to Visit Grantown-on-Spey

The best time to visit Grantown-on-Spey is between May and September when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are in full bloom. However, if you’re interested in winter sports, the winter months are perfect for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grantown-on-Spey

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drink.

  • Do take a hiking or biking tour of the surrounding countryside.

  • Do visit Cairngorms National Park.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural landscapes.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothes, even in the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grantown-on-Spey

Grantown-on-Spey is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Health in Grantown-on-Spey

Health facilities are located in Aviemore and Elgin, both a short drive from Grantown-on-Spey. Make sure to bring any necessary medications and travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Grantown-on-Spey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grantown-on-Spey

The closest airports to Grantown-on-Spey are Inverness Airport and Aberdeen Airport, both located approximately 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Grantown-on-Spey

Grantown-on-Spey has a bus station located in the center of town, with connections to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Driving is the easiest way to get to Grantown-on-Spey, and there are several car rental companies available at Inverness and Aberdeen airports.

How to Get Around in Grantown-on-Spey

Grantown-on-Spey is a small town, and it’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you’re planning on exploring the surrounding areas, a car rental is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Grantown-on-Spey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, such as:

  • Loch Ness: Located just a short drive from Grantown-on-Spey, Loch Ness is a popular destination for its mythical monster and stunning scenery.

  • Inverness: The city of Inverness is located approximately 45 miles away and offers plenty of cultural attractions and historic sites to explore.

Conclusion

Grantown-on-Spey is a hidden gem in the heart of the Scottish Highlands, offering stunning scenery, rich history, and plenty of outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or enjoying the local food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
