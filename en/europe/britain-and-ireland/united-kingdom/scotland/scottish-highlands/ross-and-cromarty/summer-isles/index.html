Introduction to Summer Isles

Located off the coast of Ross and Cromarty in the Scottish Highlands, the Summer Isles is a captivating archipelago of 23 stunning islands, with breath-taking beaches, unspoiled landscapes, and abundant wildlife. These mesmerizing isles offer the perfect getaway for nature lovers, hikers, photographers, anglers, and anyone seeking tranquillity and serenity.

List of Places to Visit in Summer Isles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Summer Isles may be a small archipelago, but it offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, including local museums and historical landmarks. The Museum of Scottish Lighthouses is a must-visit. The museum is located in Fraserburgh, which is just an hour’s drive from the Summer Isles. It houses a significant collection of lighthouse models, maritime artifacts, and other historical exhibits. Other culturally important places to visit include the Assynt and Ullapool museums, Lochinver Piping School, and Dunrobin Castle.

Natural Attractions of Summer Isles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Summer Isles are renowned for their natural beauty, and there is no shortage of outdoor adventures to embark on. Take a hike on one of the many scenic trails, go fishing or kayaking on the pristine waters, or visit the many beaches and coastal viewpoints. The Achmelvich Beach and Laide beach offer stunning views of turquoise waters and golden sands. The nearby Inverpolly is a National Nature Reserve, home to even more breathtaking natural wonders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Summer Isles

Explore the archipelago with a hike or bike ride, and experience the rich natural environment with every step. The Stac Pollaidh, Assynt’s signature mountain offers stunning views of the archipelago, and the Culag Woods trail offers an easy, scenic stroll. The Coigach Peninsula also offers several trails, including the Achiltibuie Circular, Badentarbat Bay Walk, and the Drumbeg - Lochinver walk.

Best Things to Do in Summer Isles: Activities and Tours

For those seeking adventure, there is a host of activities and tours to choose from. Pony trekking, wildlife cruises, canoeing, whale watching, and sea kayaking are just a few things to do. Take a boat trip around the archipelago, cruise around the dramatic coastlines, or try your hand at a range of thrilling water sports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Summer Isles

Day 1: Explore Ullapool, which houses several unique museums and delicious seafood restaurants Day 2: Take a leisurely hike through Inverpolly, known for its stunning scenery and breathtaking views Day 3: Take a boat trip to the Summer Isles, and spend some time exploring the stunning archipelago Day 4: Go fishing in one of the clear, island-enclosed waters Day 5: Explore the outer coast of the Summer Isles Day 6: Take a hot tub session or go for a relaxing spa treatment at a local spa Day 7: Go kayaking and see wildlife up close

Food and Drink Scene of Summer Isles

Must-Try Dishes

For the foodies, the Summer Isles is paradise. The Islands offer a rich culinary culture, showcasing fresh seafood such as salmon, langoustine, scallops, and crab. The area is also known for its local specialties such as smoked salmon, smoked haddock, and oatcakes. Sample these Scottish delights at the local eateries such as the Pie café, Shorehouse restaurant, and Am Fuaran Bar.

Local Beverages

The Summer Isles produce some of the best local beverages in Scotland. Whiskey and gin are readily available and produced locally. Whiskey tasting and gin workshops are available in some local distilleries. Visit the Summer Isles Bar for a beer or wine tasting and meet the friendly locals.

Where to Stay in Summer Isles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Housing options in Summer Isles range from luxurious hotels to cost-efficient hostels.

The Summer Isles Hotel is one of the best options for those wanting a little more luxury. The hotel has stunning accommodation, incredible views of the sea, and award-winning cuisine. There are several options for budget travelers such as the hostels and the campsites, where travelers can experience the serene beauty of the Scottish Highland.

What to Buy in Summer Isles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take the Scottish memories home with you with the charming mementos available to purchase in the Summer Isles; from locally produced pottery and crafts, to handmade jewelry and woolen goods. Visit the cafes and gift shops in the Ullapool and Stornoway towns for the ultimate souvenir shopping experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Summer Isles

The local culture in the Summer Isles is warm and friendly, with a strong affinity for the outdoors and traditional Scottish values. The area is known for its music, from rock and pop to folk and traditional. The Gaelic language, food, and clothing are careful reminders of the rich cultural heritage of Scotland.

Best Time to Visit Summer Isles

The Summer Isles experience a wide range of weather patterns and temperatures throughout the year, but the best time to visit is between June and September when the skies are sunny, the water is warm, and outdoor activities flourish.

Safety and Health

Safety in Summer Isles

Safety is a top priority in the Summer Isles. While exploring the natural wonders, visitors should take precautions such as wearing appropriate clothing and footwear while hiking, kayaking or rock climbing, and following the prescribed safety guidelines.

Health in Summer Isles

Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date with their regular vaccines before visiting the Summer Isles. Standard precautions, such as washing hands regularly, must be precautions to avoid the spread of any potential infections.

Getting to Summer Isles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Summer Isles

The nearest airport to Summer Isles is the Inverness airport, which is approximately 90 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Summer Isles

The Summer Isles are well-connected by bus, with major towns nearby offering regular bus services.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to the Summer Isles via car rental, bikes from the nearby towns, or take a boat or ferry to explore the archipelago.

How to Get Around in Summer Isles

Visitors can explore the Summer Isles by renting a car, bike, or using public transportation. Walking the coastline trails requires a modest amount of physical fitness, and several local tours and boat trips are available for visitors to travel.

Where to Go Next from Summer Isles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can also explore the nearby Ullapool with its quaint fishing villages, charming shops and museums, and other gorgeous Scottish cities of the Highlands including Thurso, Elgin, Kyle of Lochalsh and Fort William.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Summer Isles is a captivating, picturesque, and unspoiled archipelago with a dazzling natural beauty and cultural charm. The breathtakingly beautiful islands offer endless adventures, from kayaking to hiking and more, with plenty of opportunities for relaxation and reflection. The Summer Isles are the perfect destination to connect with nature, make unforgettable memories, enjoy rich Scottish culture and heritage, and experience the best of what the Scottish highlands have to offer.
