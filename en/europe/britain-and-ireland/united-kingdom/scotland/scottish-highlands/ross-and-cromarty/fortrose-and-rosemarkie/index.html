Fortrose and Rosemarkie: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Nestled in the picturesque countryside of Ross and Cromarty in the Scottish Highlands lies the charming villages of Fortrose and Rosemarkie. These adjacent seaside towns are brimming with culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. If you’re planning a trip to Scotland, make sure to add Fortrose and Rosemarkie to your itinerary to experience the best of what the region has to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, and more in Fortrose and Rosemarkie.

Introduction to Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Fortrose and Rosemarkie are located on the Black Isle, a peninsula in the Scottish Highlands. The villages are connected by a long stretch of beach, and their histories date back to the medieval era. Fortrose boasts beautiful Georgian architecture, including its iconic cathedral ruins, while Rosemarkie is known for its stunning coastline and scenery. Both villages offer a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of urban life, making them the perfect place to relax and unwind.

List of Places to Visit in Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture vultures, Fortrose and Rosemarkie have an array of museums and historical places to explore. One must-visit attraction is Fortrose Cathedral, which was built in the 1200s and showcases stunning medieval architecture. The Groam House Museum in Rosemarkie is another fascinating attraction, featuring Pictish stones and replicas of ancient artwork. In Fortrose, the Cromarty Courthouse Museum is a must-visit spot for history buffs interested in Scottish law, while the Hugh Miller Museum in nearby Cromarty is dedicated to the life and times of the renowned geologist and folklorist Hugh Miller.

Natural Attractions of Fortrose and Rosemarkie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for fun outdoor activities and breathtaking views, Fortrose and Rosemarkie have you covered. Take a stroll along Rosemarkie Beach and soak in the stunning coastal scenery. Chanonry Point, located between Fortrose and Rosemarkie, is a popular spot for dolphin watching. You can also explore the Fairy Glen, a beautiful gorge with cascading waterfalls, or head to the Black Isle Wildlife & Country Park to admire the local wildlife. For an adrenaline rush, try your hand at water sports like kayaking or paddleboarding at Avoch Watersports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fortrose and Rosemarkie

For avid hikers and cyclists, Fortrose and Rosemarkie offer some excellent trails to explore. The Black Isle Trail is a popular 24-mile route that takes you through the scenic countryside, while the Forts & Firth cycle route is a shorter 12-mile option. The Rosemarkie to Chanonry Point walk is another must-try route, which rewards you with views of dolphins in the Moray Firth as you make your way along the coast.

Best Things to Do in Fortrose and Rosemarkie: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more activities to add to your list, consider taking a guided tour of Fortrose and Rosemarkie. A variety of tours are available, including walking tours of the villages and boat tours to spot dolphins at Chanonry Point. Or, for a unique experience, try your hand at falconry at the nearby Black Isle Falconry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fortrose and Rosemarkie

For those planning a week-long trip, we’ve put together an itinerary that captures the best of what Fortrose and Rosemarkie have to offer:

  • Day 1: Explore Fortrose, visit the cathedral ruins and the Cromarty Courthouse Museum
  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of Rosemarkie and the Groam House Museum
  • Day 3: Dolphin watching at Chanonry Point and a visit to the Black Isle Wildlife & Country Park
  • Day 4: Hiking or cycling on the Black Isle Trail
  • Day 5: Water sports at Avoch Watersports
  • Day 6: Falconry at Black Isle Falconry
  • Day 7: Relax on Rosemarkie Beach and admire the coastal views

Food and Drink Scene of Fortrose and Rosemarkie

No trip to Scotland would be complete without sampling the local food and drink, and Fortrose and Rosemarkie have plenty of options to try. From hearty Scottish staples to fresh seafood, here are our top recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s organs and spices, typically served with potatoes and neeps (turnips)
  • Cullen Skink: A creamy soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions
  • Black Isle beef: Locally sourced, grass-fed beef that’s perfect for a hearty steak or burger
  • Shortbread: A sweet biscuit made with butter, sugar, and flour, perfect for a snack or dessert

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland is known for its world-renowned Scotch whisky, and Fortrose and Rosemarkie have a variety of pubs and bars where you can taste the local varieties
  • Beer: The Black Isle Brewery in nearby Munlochy produces a range of organic beers that are popular throughout the Scottish Highlands
  • Gin: The Scottish gin industry has boomed in recent years, and Black Isle Gin is a local favourite, boasting botanicals grown on the Black Isle itself

Where to Stay in Fortrose and Rosemarkie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fortrose and Rosemarkie offer a range of accommodation options, from boutique hotels to quaint guesthouses. For a luxurious stay, the Ness Walk Hotel in Inverness is a short drive from Fortrose and offers stylish rooms and a pristine riverside location. The Station Hotel in Avoch is a more affordable pet-friendly option, while the Fortrose and Rosemarkie Golf Club House offers stunning views of the Moray Firth and is a great choice for golf enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Fortrose and Rosemarkie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking for unique souvenirs and gifts, Fortrose and Rosemarkie offer an array of options. Black Isle Crafts in Rosemarkie sells locally made pottery, jewelry, and textiles, while the Rosemarkie Beach Cafe & Gift Shop sells homemade fudge and other treats. If you’re looking for local produce to bring home, make sure to visit the Cromarty Brewing Company, which sells a variety of locally brewed beers and ciders.

Local Culture and Customs of Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Scotland has a rich cultural history that’s still celebrated to this day, and Fortrose and Rosemarkie are no exception. One local tradition worth experiencing is the Highlands Games, which take place throughout the summer and feature events like the caber toss and tug-of-war. Another cultural touchstone is ceilidhs, lively Scottish dances that take place in local community halls and pubs.

Best Time to Visit Fortrose and Rosemarkie

The best time to visit Fortrose and Rosemarkie is between May and September, when temperatures are comfortable and rainfall is minimal. However, these are also the busiest months, so expect crowds and higher prices. For those looking for a quieter experience, consider visiting in the off-season, when you can enjoy the same sights and activities without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, from haggis to whisky
  • Take a guided tour of the villages to learn about their rich history and culture
  • Visit Chanonry Point for dolphin watching

Don’t:

  • disturb the local wildlife while exploring the countryside
  • litter on the beaches or trails
  • underestimate the weather, pack appropriate gear for rain and colder temperatures

Safety and Health

Safety in Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Fortrose and Rosemarkie are generally safe places to visit, but be aware of potentially dangerous wildlife in the countryside, such as adders and ticks. Always follow designated trails and be cautious when near cliffs or rocky shorelines.

Health in Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Visitors should have travel insurance that includes medical coverage. It’s also recommended to bring any necessary prescription medications with you on the trip. In case of a medical emergency, the nearest hospital is Raigmore Hospital in Inverness.

Getting to Fortrose and Rosemarkie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fortrose and Rosemarkie are easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Fortrose and Rosemarkie

The closest airport to Fortrose and Rosemarkie is Inverness Airport (INV), which is approximately 20 miles away. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi to Fortrose and Rosemarkie.

Bus Stations Near Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Inverness Bus Station is the closest bus station to Fortrose and Rosemarkie, with regular service from throughout Scotland. Local buses also run between the villages and nearby towns.

Other Options

For a unique experience, consider taking the Caledonian Sleeper, a train that runs between London and Inverness. From there, you can transfer to a local bus or rent a car to reach Fortrose and Rosemarkie.

How to Get Around in Fortrose and Rosemarkie

Fortrose and Rosemarkie are small villages that are easily walkable, but for those interested in exploring the surrounding countryside, renting a car is recommended. Buses and taxis are also available for local transportation.

Where to Go Next from Fortrose and Rosemarkie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fortrose and Rosemarkie are conveniently located near some of the Scottish Highlands’ most popular destinations. Consider adding these nearby spots to your itinerary:

  • Inverness: The capital of the Highlands, known for its rich history and vibrant cultural scene
  • Loch Ness: The iconic body of water famous for its mythical creature
  • Isle of Skye: A stunning island with breathtaking landscapes and charming villages

Conclusion

Fortrose and Rosemarkie are hidden gems in the Scottish Highlands, offering stunning scenery, rich history, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical places, hiking and cycling through the countryside, or indulging in local food and drink, Fortrose and Rosemarkie have something for everybody. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty of these charming seaside villages.
