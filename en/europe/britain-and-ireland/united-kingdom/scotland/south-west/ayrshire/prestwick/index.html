Introduction to Prestwick

Nestled on the coast of Ayrshire in South West Scotland, Prestwick is a charming and historic town that boasts an impressive array of cultural and natural attractions. Home to the oldest golf course in Scotland and a thriving gastronomic scene, Prestwick is the perfect destination for travelers seeking to immerse themselves in Scottish culture.

List of Places to Visit in Prestwick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Prestwick is steeped in history, with numerous museums and historical sites to explore. The Prestwick Heritage Trail is a great starting point, taking visitors on a journey through the town’s past, from the 12th century to the present day. Other notable highlights include the Prestwick Picture House, which dates back to 1921 and is still in operation today, and the Cragie Church, a beautiful example of Gothic Revival architecture.

Natural Attractions of Prestwick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to get out and explore the great outdoors, Prestwick won’t disappoint. There are plenty of walking and cycling trails to be found throughout the town and surrounding countryside, with stunning views of the sea and coast. The Prestwick Bay Nature Reserve is a particular highlight, offering a chance to glimpse a variety of bird species and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prestwick

Some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area include the Ayrshire Coastal Path, which stretches for 100 miles along the coast of Ayrshire, and the Prestwick to Troon Coastal path, which offers beautiful views of the seaside and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Prestwick: Activities and Tours

Looking for something a bit more exciting? Prestwick has plenty to offer in the way of activities and tours. The town is well-known for its golf courses, including the legendary Old Prestwick Golf Course, which dates back to 1851. Other popular activities include horse riding, sea kayaking, and even surfing on the nearby beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prestwick

For those planning a longer trip to Prestwick, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Prestwick Heritage Trail to learn about the town’s history, and explore the Prestwick Picture House.
  • Day 2: Take a walk or bike ride along the Ayrshire Coastal Path, and stop for lunch at one of the town’s many excellent eateries.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a round of golf at one of the town’s famous golf courses, or try your hand at horse riding on the beach.
  • Day 4: Visit the beautiful Culzean Castle and Country Park, just a short drive from Prestwick.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Glasgow, exploring its vibrant art scene and cultural attractions.
  • Day 6: Spend the day sea kayaking or surfing on the local beaches, or taking a guided tour of the area’s natural wonders.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of the town’s excellent cafes and restaurants, and stock up on souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Prestwick

Must-Try Dishes

Prestwick is home to a variety of excellent restaurants and eateries, serving up delicious Scottish cuisine using the freshest local ingredients. Must-try dishes include haggis, neeps and tatties, fish and chips, and of course, the classic full Scottish breakfast.

Local Beverages

No trip to Scotland is complete without a sip of the local whisky. Prestwick also boasts a variety of excellent craft breweries and gin distilleries, so be sure to sample some of the local spirits while you’re here.

Where to Stay in Prestwick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of comfortable and affordable accommodation options in Prestwick, including budget-friendly hostels, quaint guesthouses, and luxurious hotels. Some recommended options include the Parkstone Hotel, the Fernbank Guest House, and the Prestwick Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Prestwick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping for souvenirs in Prestwick, consider picking up some locally-made crafts, including jewelry, pottery, and textiles. You can also find a variety of delicious local treats, including artisanal cheeses, jams, and chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Prestwick

Prestwick has a rich and complex cultural history, with influences from various Celtic, Nordic, and Anglo-Saxon traditions. Visitors should take care to respect local customs and traditions, including the use of polite language and proper manners.

Best Time to Visit Prestwick

The best time to visit Prestwick is between April and September, when the weather is mild and the town is at its liveliest. Be sure to book your accommodations and activities well in advance, as this is the peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prestwick

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and other Scottish delicacies.
  • Do explore the area’s natural attractions, including the beautiful beaches and wildlife reserves.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended or engage in other unsafe behaviors.
  • Don’t underestimate the strength of the local tides and weather conditions.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities and adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prestwick

Prestwick is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere. Visitors should take standard precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Prestwick

Travelers to Prestwick should take care to stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun, especially during the summer months. It’s also a good idea to bring along any necessary medications or medical supplies, as some items may not be readily available locally.

Getting to Prestwick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prestwick

The nearest airport to Prestwick is the Glasgow Prestwick Airport, which offers frequent connections to major UK and European cities.

Bus Stations Near Prestwick

Prestwick is well-connected to the rest of Scotland via bus, with several major bus stations in the town and surrounding area. Buses run to and from cities such as Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Ayr.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Prestwick by train, with regular connections to Glasgow and other nearby cities. Car rental is also available, for those looking to explore the surrounding countryside at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Prestwick

Prestwick is a relatively small town, with many attractions within walking or cycling distance. Taxis and buses are also readily available, providing easy access to the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Prestwick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to continue their Scottish adventure, nearby destinations such as Edinburgh, Glasgow, and the Isle of Arran are easily accessible via train or bus.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich cultural history, immersing yourself in its natural wonders, or just sitting back and enjoying a delicious meal or drink, Prestwick has plenty to offer. Plan your trip well, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful Scottish town.
