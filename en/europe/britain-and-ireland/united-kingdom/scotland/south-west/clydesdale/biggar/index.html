Introduction to Biggar

Located in the South West region of Scotland, Biggar is a charming town in Clydesdale. It is nestled within beautiful landscapes, including the breathtaking Scottish hills and green valleys, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

While Biggar is often overlooked by tourists, it features an impressive selection of attractions and hidden gems, including historical sites, museums, local shops, and delicious cuisine.

This ultimate guide highlights the top things to do, places to visit, and food to eat in Biggar, along with hiking and biking routes, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Biggar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Biggar Museum - This museum is located in a historic building in the town centre and showcases the history of the area, including a vast collection of artefacts and displays about life in Biggar.

John Hastie Museum - This museum is dedicated to John Hastie, a local business owner and philanthropist who changed the town’s fortunes in the early 18th century. The museum showcases the various businesses that he owned, as well as replicas of the tools that he used.

Carnwath Antique Centre - This is located on the outskirts of Biggar and is home to an impressive range of antiques, including furniture, jewelry, and collectables.

Natural Attractions of Biggar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carmichael Estate - This is an excellent spot for outdoor enthusiasts. It features 200 acres of woodlands, walking, fishing, and scenic beauty.

Tinto Hill - This is a famous local landmark and popular among hikers. It is one of the highest hills in Scotland and provides fantastic panoramic views.

Biggar Gasworks - This is a restored Victorian gasworks and is now a museum or attraction. It provides a fascinating insight into this industry of the past and is worth a visit, especially for families and children.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biggar

Biggar is a paradise for hikers and bikers. It offers over 40 miles of trails and paths throughout the hills, valleys and countryside, that includes:

The Biggar Trail - A popular trail that covers the town’s perimeter, offering spectacular views of the valleys and countryside. It is ideal for both hiking and biking.

The Tinto Hill Trail - This trail leads to the summit of Tinto Hill and features steep climbs and challenging terrain. It is suitable for experienced hikers and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Biggar: Activities and Tours

The Victorian Trail - This trail is a must-do for anyone interested in history and culture. It features significant landmarks and historical sites, including the Biggar Gasworks and John Hastie Museum.

The Biggar Little Festival - This is an annual arts and cultural festival that takes place in October. It features a wide range of events throughout the town, including art exhibitions, music concerts, and theatre performances.

Gardening Scotland - This is the largest gardening event in Scotland and takes place annually at the Royal Highland Centre in June. Visitors can enjoy gardens, exhibits, and talks from the country’s top gardening experts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biggar

Day 1: Explore the town, including Biggar Museum, John Hastie Museum, and local shops.

Day 2: Head to Carmichael Estate, where you can enjoy hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Day 3: Take a scenic trip to Tinto Hill and spend the day hiking or biking.

Day 4: Visit the Biggar and Upper Clydesdale Museum and learn about the town’s local heritage.

Day 5: Attend the Biggar Little Festival and enjoy the cultural and artistic events.

Day 6: Visit the Carnwath Antique Centre to explore its collections of antiques and collectables.

Day 7: Relax at one of Biggar’s most comfortable accommodations or enjoy a meal at its popular local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Biggar

Biggar has many fantastic places to eat and drink, ranging from traditional Scottish fare to international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

Haggis - A Scottish classic dish made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, served with neeps and tatties.

Fish & Chips - Popular in the UK, especially in Scotland, fish and chips consist of battered fish and chips served with malt vinegar.

Steak Pie - A traditional Scottish dish, steak pie consists of beef, vegetables, and gravy in a puff pastry crust.

Local Beverages

Whisky - Scotland is known for its whisky, and visitors to Biggar can sample some local Scottish whiskies.

Craft Beers - Locally brewed craft beers are popular in Biggar and can be found in many bars and pubs.

Where to Stay in Biggar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Biggar has many excellent accommodation options for travelers, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels.

The Elphinstone Hotel - This is a historic hotel situated in the heart of the town and provides comfortable rooms with an elegant ambiance.

The Boar’s Head Hotel - This hotel dates back to the 17th century and features traditional Scottish décor with modern amenities.

The Kirkstyle Inn - This is a charming guesthouse located in the outskirts of Biggar and offers spacious rooms and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Biggar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handcrafted pottery - Biggar has a rich history of pottery making, and there are many local shops that offer handcrafted pottery.

Scottish shortbread - A must-try Scottish sweet is Scottish shortbread, a delicious buttery biscuit that is perfect with tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Biggar

Biggar has a rich history and culture, and locals take great pride in their heritage. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s customs and traditions, including their love for arts and culture.

Best Time to Visit Biggar

The best time to visit Biggar is from May to September, where the weather is favorable for outdoor activities and events. The town is also busier during this time due to school holidays and peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biggar

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the town.

  • Explore the town’s outdoor activities and natural beauty.

  • Visit local museums and cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any local landmarks.

  • Cause a disturbance in residential areas.

  • Take pictures without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biggar

Biggar is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take reasonable precautions when traveling, such as locking their doors and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Biggar

Biggar has a local hospital with several doctors and healthcare professionals. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage if they require any medical treatment.

Getting to Biggar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biggar

The nearest international airports to Biggar are Glasgow Airport and Edinburgh Airport. Both airports offer public transit options to Biggar.

Bus Stations Near Biggar

The closest bus stations to Biggar are in nearby towns, including Lanark and Peebles.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car if they prefer to drive to Biggar. There are several car rental agencies located in Edinburgh and Glasgow airports.

How to Get Around in Biggar

While Biggar is small enough to explore by foot, visitors can also rent bicycles or hire a taxi to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Biggar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Biggar can explore other towns in South West Scotland, including Moffat and Langholm. They can also explore the Scottish countryside or head to nearby National Parks, such as the Cairngorms.

Conclusion

Biggar is a hidden gem in Scotland that offers visitors a unique blend of nature, history, and culture. Whether travelers are interested in outdoor adventures, cultural events, or historical sites, Biggar has something to offer every kind of tourist. With picturesque surroundings and a friendly community, visitors can enjoy an unforgettable trip to Biggar and experience the best of Scotland.
