Introduction to Carluke

Located in Clydesdale, in the South West of Scotland, Carluke is a charming town that’s often overlooked by tourists. Its rural setting offers the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see in Carluke, including natural attractions, cultural and historical sites, hiking and biking routes, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Carluke

To make the most of your visit to Carluke, we’ve compiled a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Lanark World Heritage Site: An 18th-century cotton mill village that’s now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Discover the village’s rich history and learn about the Industrial Revolution.
  • Falls of Clyde: A beautiful nature reserve home to four stunning waterfalls. Admire the landscape and hike through the surrounding woodland.
  • Scottish Equestrian Centre: Perfect for horse lovers, this center offers horse riding lessons and guided treks through the countryside.

Natural Attractions of Carluke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tinto Hill: Enjoy breathtaking panoramic views at the top of this 707-meter hill. The hike is steep but rewarding.
  • The Clyde Valley: A picturesque valley with lush countryside and charming towns. Go for a scenic drive and admire the beauty of rural Scotland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carluke

Carluke’s rural setting offers fantastic opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Falls Of Clyde Walk: This 5-mile circular walk offers stunning views and takes you past three of the Falls of Clyde.
  • Tinto Hill Route: A challenging but rewarding hike up the Tinto Hill, offering fantastic panoramic views at the top.
  • Clyde Walkway: A long-distance walking path that follows the River Clyde. Walk the whole route or choose a section that interests you.

Best Things to Do in Carluke: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more adventurous things to do in Carluke, check out these activities and tours:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Head to the River Clyde for a kayaking or canoeing adventure.
  • Fishing: The River Clyde is a popular spot for fishing. Get a day ticket and enjoy the tranquility of the river.
  • Off-road Driving: For a more unique adventure, try off-road driving in Carluke.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carluke

To make the most out of your visit to Carluke, we’ve compiled a week-long itinerary:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Carluke

Carluke has a small but vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis, Neeps, and Tatties: A traditional Scottish dish made with sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, served with swede and potatoes.
  • Cullen Skink: A hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Arbroath Smokies: Smoked haddock that’s a Scottish delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland’s national drink. Try a local single malt whisky.
  • Belhaven Beer: A local Scottish beer that’s popular in Carluke.
  • Irn Bru: Scotland’s other national drink, a sweet carbonated soft drink, and beloved by locals.

Where to Stay in Carluke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carluke has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some suggestions:

  • Best Western Cartland Bridge Hotel: Located in the center of Carluke, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, a bar, and a restaurant.
  • Chain Bridge Camping and Wigwams: Situated near the Clyde Valley, this campsite offers camping pitches as well as wooden wigwams.
  • Hillhouse Bed and Breakfast: A cozy and charming guesthouse that’s surrounded by beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Carluke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs or local products to take home? Here are some suggestions:

  • Haggis: Buy a traditional haggis to take home and cook.
  • Scottish Whiskey: Bring back a bottle of Scotland’s famous whiskey.
  • Tartan: Choose a piece of tartan and have it made into a scarf, kilt, or other Scottish-inspired clothing item.

Local Culture and Customs of Carluke

Scotland has a rich culture and Carluke is no exception. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Bagpipes: A typical musical instrument of Scotland.
  • Kilts: Scottish people are proud of their national dress, the kilt.
  • Ceilidh: A traditional Scottish dance that’s sure to be found at any local festival.

Best Time to Visit Carluke

The best time to visit Carluke is from June to August when the weather is mild and the countryside is at its most beautiful. However, it’s also a popular time with tourists so expect crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carluke

To respect local customs and make the most out of your visit, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try local food and drink: Scotland is famous for its food and drink. Don’t miss the chance to try haggis, neeps and tatties, whiskey, and much more.
  • Explore the countryside: Carluke is famous for its beautiful countryside. Go for a hike, enjoy a scenic drive or visit a local farm.
  • Be polite and respectful: Scots are welcoming and friendly people. Make sure you are respectful and polite during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack waterproof clothing: Carluke is known for its rain. Pack a waterproof jacket and shoes to enjoy the countryside no matter the weather.
  • Expect the food scene to be spicy: Scottish food isn’t known for being spicy. Don’t expect fiery dishes like in other countries.
  • Be obnoxious: Scottish people can be friendly but don’t let that be an invitation to be obnoxious. Be respectful and courteous during your visit.

Safety and Health

Scotland is generally a safe country to explore. However, here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Carluke

  • Watch your valuables: Scotland is a safe country, but pickpocketing can happen in crowded cities.
  • Be aware of the surroundings: Make sure you’re aware of your surroundings when hiking or walking in the countryside.
  • Take care of the weather: The weather can be unpredictable in Scotland. Check the forecast before going out and pack accordingly.

Health in Carluke

  • Be aware of ticks: Tick bites can cause Lyme disease. Protect yourself by wearing long pants and socks when in forests or high grasslands.
  • Take Care of COVID-19: During your visit, follow local guidelines regarding mask-wearing and social distancing recommended by public health authorities.

Getting to Carluke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carluke is in the South West of Scotland, making it easy to get to by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Carluke

  • Edinburgh Airport: The nearest airport to Carluke, situated about 40 miles away.
  • Glasgow International Airport: Situated about 30 miles away from Carluke.

Bus Stations Near Carluke

  • Carluke Bus Station: Offers local and regional bus services.

Other Options

  • Car: Scotland’s roads are well-maintained, making driving easy in Carluke.
  • Train: Carluke has a train station with regular connections to other parts of Scotland.

How to Get Around in Carluke

The easiest way to get around Carluke is by car, but there are also local buses and trains available.

Where to Go Next from Carluke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to extend your trip and explore more of Scotland? Here are some nearby destinations and exciting excursions:

  • Glasgow: The largest city in Scotland, located 30 miles away from Carluke. Discover museums, galleries, and a thriving music scene.
  • Edinburgh: The capital city of Scotland, located 40 miles away. Home to iconic landmarks such as Edinburgh Castle and the Royal Mile.
  • Braemar: A picturesque village in the Cairngorm Mountains with beautiful hiking routes and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Carluke might not be as famous as other Scottish destinations, but it’s a charming town with a rich history, beautiful countryside, and friendly locals. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Carluke has it all.
