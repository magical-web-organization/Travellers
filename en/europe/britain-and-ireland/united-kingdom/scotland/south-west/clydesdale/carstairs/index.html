Carstairs Travel Guide - Discover the Charm of South West Scotland

Carstairs, located in the South West region of Scotland in the Clydesdale area, is a small town with a rich cultural heritage. This hidden gem offers a wide range of attractions, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders and scenic hiking trails. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore all the essential things you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Carstairs.

Introduction to Carstairs

Carstairs is a small town in Scotland with a population of around 2,000 people. The town is known for its picturesque countryside, vibrant culture and history, and numerous outdoor recreational activities. The town is considered as old as the 13th century and played a significant role in Scotland’s history due to its strategic location on the road between Edinburgh and Glasgow.

List of Places to Visit in Carstairs

If you are planning a trip to Carstairs, here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carstairs Junction Railway Station Museum: This museum showcases the history of the town’s railway station and its role in Scotland’s railway history.

  • Clydesdale Heritage Trail: This trail follows the River Clyde and Merrick Water through beautiful landscapes and cultural landmarks.

  • Lanark Museum: Located just a few miles from Carstairs, the museum presents the history, art, and culture of Lanarkshire.

Natural Attractions of Carstairs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crawfordjohn Sculpture Trail: This trail takes you on a journey through a Scottish upland landscape to enjoy the beauty of numerous artistic creations.

  • Falls of Clyde: This scenic beauty is a wildlife reserve with amazing waterfalls, forest trails, and a wildlife center. It’s perfect for nature lovers.

  • Tinto Hills: These hills offer scenic hiking and biking trails with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. It’s a perfect place to spend a day out in the fresh air.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carstairs

Hiking and biking are integral parts of Carstairs’ tourist activities. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails to experience the beauty of this town:

  • Crawfordjohn Sculpture Trail: This trail is perfect for both biking and hiking and is an excellent opportunity to explore Scottish upland landscapes.

  • Tinto Hill: This trail offers an excellent workout and a series of stunning views across Lanarkshire. This hike is ideal for experienced hikers.

  • Clyde Walkway: This is a 40-mile walking and cycling trail, which is excellent for those who are looking for a longer and more challenging route.

Best Things to Do in Carstairs: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours to choose from when exploring Carstairs:

  • Horseback riding: The countryside surrounding Carstairs is perfect for horseback riding, and there are several stables in the area to choose from.

  • Whisky tasting: Scotland is famous for its whisky, and there are plenty of distilleries near Carstairs that offer tasting tours.

  • Fishing: The River Clyde is well-known for its fishing, so head out to the river or nearby lochs to enjoy a day of angling.

Food and Drink Scene of Carstairs

Scotland is famous for its traditional food and drink, and Carstairs is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A delicious Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.

  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made with sheep’s offal, suet, and oats.

  • Neeps and Tatties: A classic Scottish side dish consisting of mashed turnips and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland is famous for producing some of the world’s best whisky, and there are plenty of distilleries to choose from.

  • Beer: There are several microbreweries in the area that produce excellent craft beers.

  • Tea: Scotland is famous for its tea culture, so make sure to try a cup of Scottish tea during your visit.

Where to Stay in Carstairs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carstairs has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

  • Hopetoun Arms Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Carstairs and offers excellent services and amenities.

  • Tinto Hotel: This hotel is located near Tinto Hill and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Carstairs Junction Inn: This inn is located near the railway station and offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Carstairs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are a few suggestions:

  • Scottish Tartan: The traditional Scottish tartan is a must-buy souvenir during your visit to Carstairs.

  • Scottish Whisky: Scotland is famous for its whisky, and Carstairs has several distilleries to choose from.

  • Haggis: You can purchase haggis, a traditional Scottish dish, in most grocery stores and markets during your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Carstairs

Scotland has a rich culture and heritage, and Carstairs is no exception. Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Wear appropriate clothing: Scotland is known for its temperamental weather, so make sure to bring warm clothing and rain gear.

  • Respect local traditions: Scotland has a strong sense of community, and locals take their traditions seriously. So, be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

  • Learn some Gaelic phrases: Gaelic is the traditional language of Scotland, and knowing a few phrases will help you connect with locals and appreciate the culture better.

Best Time to Visit Carstairs

The best time to visit Carstairs is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with tourists. However, if you prefer a quieter visit, the shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) can be good options.

Getting to Carstairs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carstairs is located in South West Scotland, so here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip:

Airports Near Carstairs

  • Glasgow International Airport: The airport is about 50 miles from Carstairs and is a 1-hour drive or a 2-hour train/bus journey.

  • Edinburgh Airport: The airport is about 50 miles from Carstairs and is a 1-hour drive or a 2-hour train/bus journey.

Bus Stations Near Carstairs

  • Carstairs Railway Station: The station is located in the heart of the town and connects to neighboring towns and cities.

  • Lanark Bus Station: The station is a few miles from Carstairs and connects to Glasgow and Edinburgh.

Other Options

  • Taxi: There are several taxi companies operating in the area, offering reliable and affordable services.

  • Car Rental: Car rental options are available if you prefer the flexibility and freedom of a self-drive trip.

How to Get Around in Carstairs

Carstairs is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can use public transportation, taxis or car rentals.
