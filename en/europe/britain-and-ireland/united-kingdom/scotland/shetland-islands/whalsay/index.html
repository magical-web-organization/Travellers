Introduction to Whalsay

Whalsay is a small island in the Shetland Islands, located off the northeast coast of Scotland. With a population of just over 1,000 people, Whalsay may be a tiny island, but it is filled with rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture. If you are looking for a unique and memorable travel destination, Whalsay is the perfect place to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Whalsay

Whether you are interested in learning about the island’s history, going on outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing by the sea, Whalsay has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Whalsay Heritage Centre: The Whalsay Heritage Centre is a must-visit for anyone interested in the island’s rich history. Housed in a building that was once a school, the centre offers a fascinating look at life on the island throughout the years.

  • Herring Bus Shelter: The Herring Bus Shelter is an unusual historical attraction that was once used as a shelter for workers in the island’s thriving herring industry. Today, it stands as an interesting reminder of Whalsay’s past.

  • St. Olaf’s Church: St. Olaf’s is a beautiful historic church on the island that dates back to the 18th century. It is still in use today and is open to visitors.

Natural Attractions of Whalsay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Virda Field Nature Reserve: The Virda Field Nature Reserve is a stunning area filled with wildlife and beautiful scenery. Visitors can take a leisurely walk through the reserve and enjoy the peace and quiet of the island’s natural beauty.

  • Island of Eggerness: The Island of Eggerness is a picturesque island that is only accessible at low tide. Visitors can take a short boat trip to the island and explore the stunning landscapes and wildlife that call it home.

  • Gruney Island: Gruney Island is a tiny island just off the coast of Whalsay. Visitors can take a boat trip to the island and explore the rugged landscapes and stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whalsay

Whalsay is the perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes on the island:

  • Whalsay Circular Trail: The Whalsay Circular Trail is a 6.5-mile route that takes visitors through the island’s stunning landscapes and past some of its most iconic landmarks.

  • East Loop Trail: The East Loop Trail is a challenging 8-mile route that takes visitors through some of the island’s most rugged terrain. The route rewards hikers with beautiful views of the surrounding area.

  • West Loop Trail: The West Loop Trail is a moderate 5.5-mile route that takes visitors through the island’s beautiful countryside and past some of its most historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Whalsay: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for something fun to do on Whalsay, there are plenty of activities and tours available for visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • Fishing Tours: Whalsay is famous for its fishing industry, and visitors can take a fishing tour to learn more about the island’s history and culture.

  • Bird Watching Tours: The island is a popular destination for bird watchers, with plenty of opportunities to spot rare and exotic species in their natural habitat.

  • Boat Trips: Whether you want to explore the islands around Whalsay or take a scenic tour of the coastline, there are plenty of boat trips available for visitors to enjoy.

Food and Drink Scene of Whalsay

When it comes to food and drink, Whalsay has a lot to offer visitors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shetland Reestit Mutton: A traditional dish made with salt-cured mutton, potatoes, and turnips.

  • Cullen Skink: A creamy soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.

  • Shetland Bannocks: A type of bread made from oatmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Shetland Reel Gin: A locally produced gin made with locally sourced botanicals.

  • ORKNEY Brewery Beer: While not produced on Whalsay itself, ORKNEY Brewery is located nearby and produces a range of delicious beers.

  • The Symbister Suite: A comfortable hotel located in the heart of Whalsay.

  • Shoreside Hoose: A cozy guesthouse with stunning views of the sea.

  • The Skerry: A budget-friendly hostel located close to the island’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Whalsay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a souvenir from your trip to Whalsay, there are plenty of options available. Here are some of the best local products to buy on the island:

  • Woollen Products: Shetland wool is famous around the world for its quality and softness, and visitors can buy a range of woollen products on Whalsay.

  • Local Artwork: Whalsay is home to a number of talented artists, and visitors can buy a wide range of artwork and crafts on the island.

  • Seafood: Whalsay is famous for its seafood, and visitors can buy a range of fresh and locally caught fish and shellfish to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Whalsay

Whalsay has a rich and vibrant culture that is strongly tied to the history and traditions of the island. The people of Whalsay are proud of their unique heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn more about the island’s customs and traditions during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Whalsay

The best time to visit Whalsay is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and the island is at its most vibrant. However, if you are looking for a quieter and more peaceful experience, the winter months can also be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whalsay

When visiting Whalsay, it is important to be respectful of the island’s traditions and customs. Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay:

  • Disturb the Wildlife: Visitors should not disturb any wildlife they may encounter on the island, and should keep their distance at all times.

  • Ignore Local Customs: Visitors should be respectful of the island’s traditions and customs, and should follow any local rules or regulations that are in place.

Safety and Health

Whalsay is a safe and healthy destination for visitors. Here are some tips to ensure your stay is enjoyable and hassle-free:

Safety in Whalsay

  • Be Aware of the Weather: The weather on Whalsay can change rapidly, so visitors should be prepared for all conditions and dress accordingly.

  • Watch for Cliff Edges: The island’s rugged coastline is beautiful, but visitors should take care near cliff edges to avoid accidents.

  • Be Careful on the Roads: Whalsay’s roads can be narrow and winding, so visitors should take care when driving or cycling.

Health in Whalsay

  • Bring Any Necessary Medications: Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, as access to healthcare on the island may be limited.

  • Watch for Ticks: Visitors planning to spend time in wooded areas should be aware of the risk of ticks and take precautions to avoid them.

Getting to Whalsay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whalsay is accessible by a combination of road and sea transport. Here are some of the best options for getting to the island:

  • Ferry: The ferry is the most popular mode of transport to Whalsay, with regular services from nearby Mainland Shetland.

  • Bus: There are limited bus services on Whalsay, but visitors can take a bus from the ferry terminal to the island’s main attractions.

  • Car Hire: Visitors can hire a car on Mainland Shetland and take it with them on the ferry to Whalsay.

How to Get Around in Whalsay

The best way to get around Whalsay is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car or bike on Mainland Shetland and take it with them on the ferry to the island, or they can hire a bike from one of the local rental shops on the island.

Where to Go Next from Whalsay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are looking for other destinations to visit after spending time in Whalsay, there are plenty of options nearby. Some of the best destinations for a day trip or overnight stay include:

  • Mainland Shetland: The largest island in the Shetland group, Mainland Shetland offers stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy.

  • Lerwick: The administrative capital of the Shetland Islands, Lerwick is a bustling town with plenty of shops, restaurants, and historical attractions.

  • Fair Isle: A remote island located between Mainland Shetland and the Orkney Islands, Fair Isle is famous for its unique wildlife, stunning scenery, and traditional way of life.

Conclusion

Whalsay is a unique and unforgettable travel destination, with plenty of activities, attractions, and natural beauty to enjoy.
