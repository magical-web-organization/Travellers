Foula Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide for this Scottish Island

Are you looking for a unique travel destination that offers scenic views, outdoor adventures, and rich history and culture? Look no further than Foula, a remote island located in the Shetland Islands of Scotland, United Kingdom. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about Foula, from the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay, what to buy, and even local customs and culture.

Introduction to Foula

Foula is a small, rocky island located in the North Atlantic Ocean, about 20 miles west of the Shetland mainland. The island is only 3 miles long and 2 miles wide, with a population of around 30 people. Foula is known for its breathtaking scenery, diverse wildlife, and unique heritage and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Foula

There are many places to explore on this island paradise, each with their own unique attractions. Here are some suggestions for must-visit locations in Foula.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Foula Heritage Centre: This museum is located in the former schoolhouse and features collections of historical artifacts, photographs, and manuscripts that tell the story of the island’s fascinating history and culture.
  • St. Magnus Church: This beautiful sandstone church dates back to the early 19th century and is still in use today. Inside, visitors can see the original wooden pews and pulpit, as well as stained glass windows depicting scenes from the island’s history.

Natural Attractions of Foula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Da Sneug: This is the highest point on the island and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. It is a must-visit spot for anyone with a love of the great outdoors.
  • Da Water: This spectacular waterfall is located on the west side of the island and is accessible via a scenic hiking trail. The view of the waterfall cascading into the ocean below is truly breathtaking.
  • Foula Voe: This wide inlet divides the island in half and is home to a variety of seabirds, seals, and other wildlife. Visitors can explore the area by boat or kayak and witness the natural beauty of the island up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Foula

Hiking and biking are great ways to explore the rugged terrain of Foula. There are several well-marked trails and routes on the island, each offering its own unique challenges and rewards. Here are a few of the most popular hiking and biking routes:

  • North to South: This route takes you from Leirsund in the north, down to Grutdale in the south. It covers around 6 miles and takes you through some of the most scenic areas of the island.
  • Round the Island: For those looking to take on a bigger challenge, this circular route takes you all the way around the perimeter of the island, covering around 12 miles in total. It offers stunning views of the coastline and a chance to see some of the island’s wildlife up close.

Best Things to Do in Foula: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do on this island paradise, whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or a bit of both. Here are some suggestions for the best things to do in Foula:

  • Boat Tours: Take a wildlife or sightseeing tour around the island and see seals, seabirds, and other wildlife up close.
  • Fishing: The waters around Foula are home to a variety of fish, including salmon and trout. Fishing tours and equipment rental are available for those who want to try their luck.
  • Birdwatching: Foula is home to a diverse range of bird species, including puffins, guillemots, and kittiwakes. Visitors can take guided birdwatching tours to see these beautiful creatures in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Foula

No travel experience is complete without trying the local food and drink scene, and Foula doesn’t disappoint. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: This British classic gets a unique twist on the island, with freshly caught fish and hand-cut potatoes cooked to perfection.
  • Foula Lamb: Foula’s lamb is renowned for its quality and flavor, thanks to the island’s wild and rugged terrain.

Local Beverages

  • Ale: Foula has its own microbrewery, and visitors can sample some of their unique and delicious brews at local pubs and restaurants.
  • Whisky: Scotland is famous for its whisky, and a visit to Foula isn’t complete without trying a dram or two of the best local brands.

Where to Stay in Foula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite its remote location, Foula offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. From cozy guesthouses to self-catering cottages, there is something to suit every budget and taste.

  • Foula Hotel: This family-run hotel offers comfortable accommodation and a warm welcome to all visitors.
  • Self-Catering Cottages: There are several self-catering cottages available for rent on the island, offering visitors a chance to experience the tranquility and beauty of Foula at their own pace.

What to Buy in Foula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Foula is complete without taking home a few souvenirs and local products. Here are some suggestions for what to buy:

  • Foula Sheep Wool: Foula’s rugged terrain makes it the perfect place for sheep farming, and the island’s wool is renowned for its quality and durability.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: The island has a thriving community of potters, who produce a range of unique and beautiful pieces inspired by the natural beauty of the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Foula

Foula has a rich and fascinating history and culture, shaped by its unique geography and isolation. Visitors can learn more about the island’s customs and traditions by attending local events and festivals, such as the Foula Foy, a biennial celebration of the island’s heritage and culture.

Best Time to Visit Foula

The best time to visit Foula is during the summer months, from May to August, when the weather is mild and the days are long. This is also the best time to see the island’s wildlife, as many species come to the island to breed during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Foula

Here are some important things to keep in mind when visiting Foula:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife of the island.
  • Take care when hiking or biking on the island’s rugged terrain.
  • Learn about the island’s history and culture by visiting local museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island or disturb its delicate ecosystem.
  • Take rocks or other natural materials from the island as souvenirs.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Foula is generally a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should take certain precautions to ensure their wellbeing while on the island.

Safety in Foula

  • Take care when hiking, especially near the cliffs and edges of the island.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions and dress appropriately for the outdoors.
  • Respect the privacy and property of local residents.

Health in Foula

  • Bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you, as there is no medical facility on the island.
  • Be aware of any allergies or health conditions that may be affected by the island’s environment.

Getting to Foula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Foula may be remote, but it is still accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options for getting to this island paradise:

Airports Near Foula

  • Sumburgh Airport in Shetland, Scotland is the closest airport to Foula. From there, visitors can take a ferry or small plane to the island.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also reach the island via small boat or private charter.

How to Get Around in Foula

Due to the small size of the island, the best way to get around in Foula is on foot, by bike, or by boat. Visitors can rent bikes and kayaks on the island, or arrange guided tours with local operators.

Where to Go Next from Foula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Foula is just one of many beautiful destinations to explore in Scotland and the United Kingdom. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors may want to consider:

  • Shetland Islands: The Shetland Islands are a group of islands located northeast of Scotland, each with its own unique attractions and experiences.
  • Orkney Islands: The Orkney Islands are another group of islands located off the north coast of Scotland, famous for their ancient archaeological sites and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Foula may be remote, but it is well worth the effort to get there. With its stunning scenery, unique culture, and outdoor adventures, this Scottish island paradise is the perfect destination for anyone looking to explore a truly special part of the world.
