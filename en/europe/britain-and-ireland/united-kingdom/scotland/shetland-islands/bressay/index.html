Introduction to Bressay

Located in the Shetland Islands, Bressay is a small island that is only a short ferry ride from the town of Lerwick. Despite its small size, Bressay has a lot to offer visitors. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures and scenic views or want to learn more about the local culture and history, Bressay has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, and local customs and traditions to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Bressay.

List of Places to Visit in Bressay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bressay Heritage Centre: Discover the history and culture of Bressay at this museum, which features exhibits on the island’s Viking past, fishing industry, and more.
  • Garderhouse: This historic building dates back to the 17th century and was once used as a lookout tower for pirates. Today, it is open to the public and offers stunning views of Bressay and the surrounding islands.
  • St Mary’s Church: Built in 1855, this church is one of the most important historical landmarks on the island. It features beautiful stained glass windows and a peaceful interior that is worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Bressay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Noss National Nature Reserve: Take a short ferry ride to the neighboring island of Noss, which boasts some of the most stunning landscapes in all of Scotland. Here, you can hike through the heathland, observe seabirds, and enjoy breathtaking views of the coast.
  • Maryfield Park: This park offers sweeping views of Bressay’s stunning coastline and is the perfect place to go for a walk or picnic on a sunny day.
  • Bard Head: This scenic location at Bressay’s northern tip is home to a lighthouse and stunning views of the sea and surrounding islands. Don’t miss the beautiful rock formations and crystal clear water at the Bard Sea Stack, which can be reached by foot at low tide.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bressay

  • The Coastal Walk: This 6-mile trail takes you through some of Bressay’s most beautiful landscapes and features breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding islands.
  • The Burn of Setter: This 3-mile trail takes you through some of Bressay’s most scenic valleys and offers opportunities to spot local wildlife, such as otters, seals, and various bird species.
  • The Ward of Bressay: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of the island’s highest peak and rewards you with panoramic views of the surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Bressay: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking: Take a guided tour of the coast and explore Bressay’s rugged coastline by sea kayak. This is a great way to get up close and personal with the island’s wildlife and stunning natural landscapes.
  • Fishing: Bressay is known for its rich fishing industry, and visitors can try their luck with a guided fishing tour or by visiting one of the island’s many fishing spots.
  • Guided Tours: Bressay is home to many local tour operators that offer guided tours of the island’s most popular attractions. These tours provide visitors with an in-depth look at the island’s history, culture, and natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bressay

Day 1 Explore Lerwick, the largest town in the Shetland Islands and take a ferry to Bressay
Day 2 Visit the Bressay Heritage Centre and Garderhouse
Day 3 Hike the Coastal Walk and enjoy lunch at Maryfield Park
Day 4 Take a sea kayaking tour along Bressay’s coast
Day 5 Spend the day fishing at one of Bressay’s popular fishing spots
Day 6 Visit the St Mary’s Church and hike to the summit of the Ward of Bressay
Day 7 Take a guided tour of the island and visit the Bard Head lighthouse

Food and Drink Scene of Bressay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shetland Reestit Mutton Soup: This warming soup is made with mutton that has been cured with salt and hung to dry, giving it a distinct and delicious flavor.
  • Shetland Oatcakes: These crisp, savory biscuits are made with oats and are the perfect accompaniment to a hearty bowl of soup or a plate of cheese and smoked salmon.
  • Cullen Skink: This creamy soup is made with smoked haddock and potatoes and is a popular dish throughout the Shetland Islands.

Local Beverages

  • Shetland Ale: This locally brewed ale is made with traditional ingredients and is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Shetland Reel Gin: This award-winning gin is distilled on the neighboring island of Unst and features local botanicals such as bladderwrack and angelica.

Where to Stay in Bressay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Maryfield House Hotel: This hotel offers stunning views of Bressay’s coastline and is the perfect place to relax after a day of exploring the island.
  • Bressay Lighthouse: For a unique and unforgettable experience, book a room at the Bressay Lighthouse, which offers panoramic views of the sea and surrounding islands.

What to Buy in Bressay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shetland Woolens: Bressay is known for its high-quality wool, and visitors can buy a range of products made from local sheep, including sweaters, hats, and scarves.
  • Shetland Jewelry: Bressay is home to many talented jewelers who create beautiful pieces using traditional techniques and local materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Bressay

Bressay has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply intertwined with its landscape and history. Visitors should take the time to learn about the island’s Viking past, fishing industry, and unique dialect of Scots language, which is still spoken by many locals today.

Best Time to Visit Bressay

The best time to visit Bressay is during the summer months of June to August, when temperatures are mild and daylight hours are long. However, visitors should be prepared for unpredictable weather and should bring warm clothing and rain gear year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bressay

Do:

  • Take the time to explore Bressay’s stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage.
  • Try the local food and drink, including Shetland Reestit Mutton Soup and Shetland Ale.
  • Respect the island’s wildlife and natural environment by following the Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the local wildlife or leave rubbish behind while on hiking or biking trails.
  • Ignore safety warnings or venture into dangerous areas, such as near cliffs or steep drops.
  • Consume alcohol in public places or engage in disruptive behavior that may disturb the local community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bressay

Bressay is a safe destination for visitors, but it is important to be aware of potential risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking. Visitors should always follow safety guidelines provided by guides and local authorities and should avoid dangerous areas, such as near cliffs or steep drops.

Health in Bressay

Visitors should take basic precautions to protect their health while in Bressay, such as washing their hands regularly and avoiding drinking untreated water. Medical facilities are available on the neighboring island of Lerwick in the event of an emergency.

Getting to Bressay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bressay is easily accessible via ferry from the town of Lerwick, which is located on the neighboring island of Mainland. Ferries run regularly throughout the day, and visitors can also bring their cars on the ferry for an additional fee. Alternatively, visitors can take a short flight from Aberdeen to Sumburgh Airport on the southern end of Shetland, followed by a bus or taxi to Lerwick.

Airports Near Bressay

  • Sumburgh Airport – Located on the southern end of Shetland, approximately 25 miles from Bressay.

Bus Stations Near Bressay

  • Lerwick Bus Station – Located in the center of Lerwick, approximately 5 miles from the Bressay ferry terminal.

Other Options

  • Taxis – Several taxi companies operate in Lerwick and can provide transportation to and from the Bressay ferry terminal.

How to Get Around in Bressay

Bressay is a small island that is easily navigable on foot, by bike, or by car. However, visitors should be aware that some roads are narrow and winding, and that caution should be taken when driving.

Where to Go Next from Bressay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mainland Shetland: This neighboring island is home to many attractions, including the Shetland Museum and Archives and the Sumburgh Head Lighthouse.
  • Unst: Located to the north of Bressay, Unst is home to many natural attractions, such as the Hermaness National Nature Reserve and the Keen of Hamar Nature Reserve.
  • Fair Isle: This small island located south of Bressay is home to a unique community of artisans and is a great place to learn more about traditional Shetland crafts such as knitting and weaving.

Conclusion

Bressay is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Shetland Islands. With its beautiful natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine, Bressay is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors. Take the time to explore this small but mighty island and learn more about the unique history and culture that make it so special.
