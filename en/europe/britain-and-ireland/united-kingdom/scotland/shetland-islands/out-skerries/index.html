Introduction to Out Skerries

Out Skerries is a charming archipelago consisting of three small islands located off the east coast of Shetland Islands in Scotland, United Kingdom. The islands are inhabited by a friendly and welcoming population of around 70 people, who are largely dependent on fishing for their livelihood. Out Skerries offers visitors a unique Scottish experience, filled with picturesque scenery, stunning coastlines, and rich history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Out Skerries

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The history of Out Skerries dates back to the 17th century, and visitors can learn about it through various museums and historical places. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • The Laird’s House - a fascinating museum that showcases the history of Out Skerries and its inhabitants.

  • The Chapel of Our Lady - a quaint chapel that still holds Sunday services and regular activities.

  • The Kilmartin House Museum - a museum that highlights the maritime history of Out Skerries and features an extensive collection of artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Out Skerries: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Out Skerries is blessed with natural beauty, with breathtaking scenery and abundant wildlife. Here are some of the top natural attractions and outdoor activities:

  • Boat Tours - explore the stunning coastline and the surrounding waters with boat tours.

  • Bird Watching - spot numerous seabirds that breed on the island such as puffins, guillemots, and razorbills.

  • Hiking - enjoy scenic walks or a more challenging hike up Ward Hill, the highest point of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Out Skerries

There are several hiking and biking routes in Out Skerries, all offering stunning panoramic views. Here are some popular ones:

  • Ward Hill Trail - a challenging hike to the highest point of the island, offering a panoramic view of the coastlines from the top.

  • Water Geo Path - a moderate hike from Bruray to Grunay, this trail offers stunning views with dramatic cliffs, rocky shores, and wildflowers.

Best Things to Do in Out Skerries: Activities and Tours

Out Skerries offers a plethora of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in Out Skerries:

  • Fishing Trips - experience fishing like a local with fishing tours that take you out to the waters surrounding Out Skerries.

  • Boat Tours - explore the island from the water with various boat tours, offering stunning views of the coastlines and abundant wildlife.

  • Cultural Walks - immerse yourself in the rich culture and history of Out Skerries by taking cultural walks that take you through the island’s landmarks and historical sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Out Skerries

Out Skerries is home to delicious seafood, and visitors can also enjoy fresh vegetables from local gardens. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

  • Lobster and Crab - caught in the pristine waters surrounding the island.
  • Traditional Fish and Chips - fresh fish caught in the surrounding waters paired with thick-cut chips.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky - enjoy a dram of Scotland’s national drink while enjoying the unspoiled beauty of Out Skerries.

Getting to Out Skerries: Transportation Options and Logistics

Out Skerries can be accessed via plane, ferry, or car. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Out Skerries

  • Sumburgh Airport - located in the Shetland Islands, this airport is the closest to Out Skerries. Visitors can take a connecting flight, or a taxi to the ferry port.

Bus Stations Near Out Skerries

  • Lerwick Bus Terminal - visitors can take a bus from Lerwick Bus Terminal to Bruray ferry port, and then take a ferry to Out Skerries.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - visitors can hire a car in the Shetland Islands and take it on the ferry to Out Skerries.

How to Get Around in Out Skerries

Out Skerries is a small island that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the island at their leisure.
