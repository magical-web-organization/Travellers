Introduction to Scalloway

Located in the stunning Shetland Islands, Scalloway is a charming and historic village that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Scottish history and culture. With its quaint streets, beautiful waterfront and stunning views of the surrounding countryside, Scalloway is the perfect place to relax and experience all that the Shetland Islands have to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Scalloway

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Scalloway Castle is a must-see attraction in the village. Built in the early 17th century, the castle was once the residence of the Earl of Shetland and is now open to visitors.

The Scalloway Museum is another popular attraction that showcases the history of the village. The museum has a collection of fascinating artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Scalloway’s rich cultural and maritime heritage.

Natural Attractions of Scalloway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Scalloway offers a range of activities and adventures. The coast around Scalloway is a great place for sea kayaking, fishing and hiking.

Scenic views of the surrounding countryside can be enjoyed with a walk around the village or a hike to the top of Scalloway Hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scalloway

Scalloway offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the Shetland Islands. One popular option is the South Scalloway Loop, a 5-mile circular route that takes in stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Scalloway: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Scalloway, from visiting historic sites to trying out some of the local activities.

For those interested in history, there are a number of guided tours of the village available. Alternatively, visitors can explore Scalloway at their own pace with a self-guided walking tour.

Outdoor enthusiasts can try their hand at sea kayaking, fishing or hiking on one of the many trails around the village.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scalloway

To make the most of your time in Scalloway, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the village of Scalloway and visit Scalloway Castle and the Scalloway Museum.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the surrounding countryside and enjoy scenic views of the countryside.

Day 3: Try your hand at sea kayaking or fishing.

Day 4: Hike to the top of Scalloway Hill for panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Day 5: Take a self-guided walking tour of Scalloway and visit some of the local shops and cafes.

Day 6: Try some of the local dishes and produce, including Shetland lamb and seafood.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery at one of the local beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Scalloway

Must-Try Dishes

Scalloway is known for its seafood, with local dishes including smoked haddock, crab and lobster. Another local specialty is Shetland lamb, which can be cooked in a variety of different ways.

Local Beverages

In addition to the local seafood and lamb, Scalloway also has a number of local beverages to try. These include the famous Shetland Reel gin and the Blackwood’s range of vodka and gin.

Where to Stay in Scalloway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options in Scalloway, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. One popular option is the Scalloway Hotel, which offers comfortable rooms and great food in the heart of the village.

For those on a budget, the Scalloway Youth Hostel is a great option. The hostel has a range of dormitory and private rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and communal areas.

What to Buy in Scalloway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Scalloway is a great place to find unique souvenirs and gifts. Local craftspeople sell a range of handmade goods, including pottery, jewelry and textiles. Other popular products include Shetland wool products and local food and drink products.

Local Culture and Customs of Scalloway

Scalloway has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong tradition of fishing and shipbuilding. Visitors can learn more about the local culture and traditions at the Scalloway Museum and by exploring the village streets and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Scalloway

The best time to visit Scalloway is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the village’s stunning scenery and cultural attractions year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scalloway

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try some of the local food and drink products.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and adventures available in the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scalloway

Scalloway is a safe and friendly village, with low levels of crime and a welcoming community. Visitors should take standard precautions to keep their belongings safe and avoid leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Scalloway

Health care in Scalloway is provided by the local health center and hospital. Visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance and should seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Scalloway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scalloway

The nearest airports to Scalloway are Sumburgh Airport and Lerwick-Tingwall Airport, which both offer regular flights to destinations throughout the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Scalloway

Scalloway is served by a number of local bus routes, which provide connections to other villages and towns throughout the Shetland Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Scalloway by ferry from Aberdeen, which operates several times a week.

How to Get Around in Scalloway

Scalloway is a small village that can be easily explored on foot. However, visitors may also wish to hire a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Scalloway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Shetland Islands offer a wealth of attractions and activities, including stunning beaches, historic sites and beautiful scenery. Popular nearby destinations include the villages of Lerwick and Sandwick, as well as the Shetland Nature Reserve.

Conclusion

Scalloway is a unique and charming village that offers visitors a chance to experience the rich culture and traditions of the Scottish countryside. With its beautiful scenery, historic landmarks and range of activities and attractions, Scalloway is the perfect destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty of the Shetland Islands.
