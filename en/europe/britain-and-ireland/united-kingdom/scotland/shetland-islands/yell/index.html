Introduction to Yell

Yell is a stunning island located in the Shetland Islands of Scotland. Being the second-largest island after mainland Shetland, it attracts tourists from all over the world with its rugged landscapes, picturesque villages, and beautiful sandy beaches. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a nature enthusiast, Yell has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Yell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Old Haa Museum: This 17th-century building is one of the island’s oldest buildings and is now a museum reflecting the island’s history.
  • Old Scatness Broch and Iron Age Village: This archaeological site is a must-visit for history buffs and offers an insight into the island’s Viking past.

Natural Attractions of Yell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gloup: A collapsed sea cave that provides a stunning view of the coastline and the North Sea.
  • Otter Watching: Observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat in the Yell Sound.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yell

Yell is a perfect destination for those who like to explore the outdoors. From rugged coastal paths to gentle hills, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. The best routes to try are:

  • Mid Yell Circular Walk: A 6-mile loop trail that offers beautiful views of the island’s rolling hills.
  • West Sandwick to Toft Pier: A 12-mile trail featuring stunning views of the coastline and the North Sea.

Best Things to Do in Yell: Activities and Tours

  • Beach Combing: Head to any of Yell’s beautiful sandy beaches for some relaxing and fascinating beachcombing.
  • Sea Kayaking: Take a guided kayak tour around the island’s beautiful coastlines, and take in the stunning scenery while you paddle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yell

Day 1: Arrival and Exploring Yell Day 2: Discover the Old Scatness Broch and Iron Age Village Day 3: Hike or bike the Mid Yell Circular Walk Day 4: Visit the stunning Gloup and go on an otter watching tour Day 5: Take a guided sea kayak tour around the island’s coastlines Day 6: Explore the island’s crofting heritage at The Old Haa Museum Day 7: Departure Day

Food and Drink Scene of Yell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Skink: A hearty soup made with fish and potatoes, a famous Scottish delicacy
  • Shetland Wool Week Cake: A traditional cake that originated from Shetland’s wool week

Local Beverages

  • Shetland Reel Gin: An award-winning gin made by the island’s first gin distillery
  • Anderson Craft Ales: The perfect ale to complement the local seafood

Where to Stay in Yell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Aywick Shop: A small convenience store on the island that offers unique accommodation options
  • Cullivoe Harbour Accommodation: A beautiful cottage located near the island’s only harbor

What to Buy in Yell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fair Isle Knitwear: A style of knitting that originated on the islands and is iconic to Shetland’s heritage
  • Yell Memorabilia: Take home a piece of Yell by purchasing any Yell-themed souvenirs

Local Culture and Customs of Yell

Yell has a unique culture that is rich in history and heritage. The island’s history has been shaped by the Vikings, and the locals are proud to showcase the layers of their history through their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Yell

The best time to visit Yell is between May and September when the weather is warmer and days are longer. During the summer months, there are several festivals and events on the island, including the Yell Show, a traditional agricultural show that takes place in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yell

Do:

  • Visit the Old Haa museum for a glimpse into the island’s rich history and heritage.
  • Take a guided tour to explore the island’s beautiful coastlines.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the countryside.
  • Approach or disturb any of the wildlife on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yell

Yell has a low crime rate and is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always best to take precautions and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded places.

Health in Yell

Yell does not have any hospitals on the island, but there is a medical center that provides primary healthcare services. Always make sure to have comprehensive travel medical insurance.

Getting to Yell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yell

The nearest airport to Yell is Sumburgh Airport, which is located on mainland Shetland. From there, you can take a ferry or a bus to Yell.

Bus Stations Near Yell

Yell has a limited bus service that operates between the ferry terminal and the island’s settlements.

Other Options

Ferry service is the most popular way to get to Yell from mainland Shetland. NorthLink Ferries offer daily services from Aberdeen to Shetland.

How to Get Around in Yell

The best way to explore Yell is by car or bike. There are car rental services available on the island, and bike rental services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Yell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lerwick

Lerwick is the largest town located on the mainland of Shetland Islands. It features a range of cultural attractions and shopping centers for tourists.

Unst Island

Unst is the northernmost island in the Shetland’s archipelago. It is known for its historical Viking sites and beautiful natural scenery.

Conclusion

Yell Island is a spectacular destination that offers visitors a chance to explore Scotland’s rich Viking history, rugged terrains, and picturesque coastline. With so many things to see and do on the island, plan your trip today to take in all the beauty and tranquility of this remarkable destination.
