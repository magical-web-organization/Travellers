Introduction to Walls

Located in the beautiful Shetland Islands of Scotland, Walls is a picturesque village with a rich history and natural beauty. It is located on the west coast of the mainland, and is surrounded by stunning landscapes, cliffs, and sandy beaches. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Walls has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Walls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Walls Heritage Centre: This museum showcases the unique history and culture of the village and the Shetland Islands as a whole. Exhibitions on display range from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • Lady Kirk: This 16th century church is located in the heart of the village. It is one of the oldest buildings in Walls and is well worth a visit for its stunning architecture and historical significance.
  • Scalloway Castle: Just a short drive from Walls, Scalloway Castle is an imposing fortress that dates back to the 16th century. It was once the residence of the Earl of Orkney and is a must-visit attraction for history enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Walls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bay of Clousta: This beautiful bay is located on the north coast of Walls. It offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean, as well as opportunities for hiking, fishing, and beachcombing.
  • Eshaness Cliffs: These stunning cliffs offer sweeping views of the Shetland coastline. They are also home to several natural wonders, including the famous “Drongs” sea stacks.
  • St. Magnus Bay: This bay is a popular spot for birdwatching and fishing. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the bay’s wildlife and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walls

  • Walls Circular Walk: This 7-mile circular walk takes visitors through the heart of the village and along the coast. It offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Eshaness Cliffs Walk: This 4-mile walk takes visitors along the edge of the Eshaness cliffs, offering breathtaking views of the coastline and the North Sea.
  • Muckle Roe Hike: This 6-mile hike takes visitors to the top of Muckle Roe, one of the highest points in the area. It offers panoramic views of the Shetland Islands and is a popular destination for birdwatchers.

Best Things to Do in Walls: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Tours: Walls is renowned for its excellent fishing opportunities. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the local waters and try their hand at catching mackerel, cod, and other fish.
  • Boat Tours: Boat tours are a popular way to explore the Shetland Islands. Visitors can take a tour of the surrounding waters to see dolphins, whales, and other sea life up close.
  • Cycling Tours: Cycling tours are a great way to explore the local scenery and enjoy the fresh air. Visitors can rent a bike or join a guided tour to explore the local countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Walls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shetland Fish and Chips: This classic dish is made with locally caught fish and is popular with both locals and visitors.
  • Reestit Mutton Soup: This hearty soup is a traditional dish in the Shetland Islands. It is made with salted mutton, potatoes, and onions.
  • Cullen Skink: This creamy soup is made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Shetland Ale: This local beer is brewed in the village of Lerwick and is a favorite among beer enthusiasts.
  • Aquavit: This traditional Scandinavian liquor is popular in the Shetland Islands. It is made with potatoes and flavored with herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Walls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Busta House Hotel: This historic hotel is located near Walls and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a cozy bar.
  • Setters Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Walls and offers comfortable accommodations and a warm welcome.
  • Hoka Lodge: This family-run hotel is located in a beautiful rural setting and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Walls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shetland Wool Products: Shetland wool is renowned for its quality and is popular with visitors. Visitors can buy sweaters, scarves, and other wool products at local markets and shops.
  • Local Seafood: Visitors can buy locally caught seafood, including fish, crab, and lobster, at local markets and shops.
  • Kishie Bags: These traditional bags are made from woven straw and are popular souvenirs in the Shetland Islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Walls

  • Music and Dance: The Shetland Islands have a rich musical tradition. Visitors can attend traditional music and dance events at local pubs and community centers.
  • Festivals: Walls hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Walls Agricultural Show, which showcases the best of local farming and food production.
  • Language: While the official language in the Shetland Islands is English, many locals also speak Shetlandic, a dialect that has its roots in Old Norse.

Best Time to Visit Walls

The best time to visit Walls is between April and September, when the weather is mild and the days are long. The summer months are particularly popular, as they offer the best opportunities for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walls

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment.

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walls

Walls is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as securing valuables and locking doors at night.

Health in Walls

There are no major health issues to be aware of in Walls. Visitors should ensure they have appropriate medical insurance and consult with a doctor before traveling if they have any health concerns.

Getting to Walls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Walls

The nearest airport to Walls is Sumburgh Airport, which is located on the southern tip of the Shetland mainland. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Walls.

Bus Stations Near Walls

There are no regular bus services to Walls, but visitors can take a bus to the nearby town of Lerwick and then take a taxi or rent a car to reach Walls.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Walls by ferry, which operates between Aberdeen and Lerwick.

How to Get Around in Walls

The best way to get around Walls is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in nearby towns such as Lerwick. Cycling is also a popular way to explore the local area.

Where to Go Next from Walls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lerwick: The capital of the Shetland Islands, Lerwick is a bustling town with a vibrant culture and history. It is located just a short drive from Walls.
  • Mousa: This uninhabited island is home to a well-preserved Iron Age broch and is a popular destination for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts.
  • Fetlar: This small island is home to beautiful beaches, stunning landscapes, and a unique cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Walls is a hidden gem in the Shetland Islands that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or food and drink, Walls has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to explore all that this beautiful village has to offer.
