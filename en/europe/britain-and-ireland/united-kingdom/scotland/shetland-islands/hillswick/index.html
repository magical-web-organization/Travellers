Introduction to Hillswick

Hillswick is a small town located on the rugged coastline of the Shetland Islands, in Scotland. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and those seeking a peaceful getaway. The town is surrounded by mesmerizing landscapes, cliffs, and hills that offer stunning views of the North Atlantic Ocean. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Hillswick.

List of Places to Visit in Hillswick

There are a plethora of places in Hillswick to explore. From cultural and historical attractions to natural wonders, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Shetland museum is a must-visit place for history enthusiasts. It showcases the local history and culture of the Shetland Islands and has some interesting exhibits. The nearby Croft House Museum offers insight into the daily lives of the locals in the past.

Natural Attractions of Hillswick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Eshaness Cliffs are one of the most popular attractions in Hillswick. The cliffs offer breathtaking views of the North Atlantic Ocean and surrounding hills. The nearby Eshaness lighthouse is also worth a visit. Keen bird watchers should head to the Hillswick Ness viewpoint, where one can spot puffins and other seabirds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hillswick

Hillswick has numerous hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of hikers. The Hillswick Geo Trail is a moderate hiking trail that takes visitors through the geological history of the area. The Eshaness Circular Route is a more challenging route that passes by the Eshaness Cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Hillswick: Activities and Tours

Sea kayaking, fishing and wildlife tours are some of the popular activities in Hillswick. Visitors can also go on a boat trip to sea the Shetland Islands from the water. The Hillswick Wildlife Sanctuary is another attraction that visitors can visit to see the local marine wildlife up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hillswick

A week in Hillswick is perfect for exploring the town and its surroundings. On the first day, visitors can explore the town and visit the local museums. The next few days can be spent hiking and biking in the nearby hills and cliffs. The visitors can also go on a wildlife tour or take a boat trip. The last few days can be spent on sea kayaking or fishing.

Food and Drink Scene of Hillswick

The food and drink scene in Hillswick is a blend of local and international flavors. Visitors must try out local specialties such as Shetland Mussels, Shetland lamb, and bere bannocks. There are also several cozy pubs in Hillswick, where visitors can try locally brewed beers and whiskeys.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shetland Mussels
  2. Shetland Lamb
  3. Bere Bannocks

Local Beverages

  1. Shetland Reel Gin
  2. Lerwick Brewery Ales
  3. Whisky and Beer

Where to Stay in Hillswick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options to choose from when it comes to accommodation in Hillswick. Muckle Flugga Guesthouse, Hillswick Lodge, and The Booth are some of the popular guest houses in Hillswick.

What to Buy in Hillswick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy souvenirs such as Shetland knitwear, local pottery, and handmade jewelry from local shops. There are also local markets where visitors can buy fresh produce and seafood to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hillswick

The local culture in Hillswick is influenced by the Norse heritage and the remote location of the area. Visitors can attend local festivals such as Up Helly Aa, a Viking festival held in January. Visitors are also advised to respect local customs and traditions, such as not walking on the wildflower meadowlands or taking photos of private property without permission.

Best Time to Visit Hillswick

The best time to visit Hillswick is from March to October, when the weather is milder and more accessible for outdoor activities. However, visitors must be prepared for the occasional rain and winds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hillswick

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Carry waterproof and warm clothing
  3. Explore the outdoors

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash in the countryside
  2. Disturb the local wildlife
  3. Walk on the wildflower meadowlands

Safety and Health

Safety in Hillswick

Hillswick is a safe town, but visitors must take precautions when exploring the countryside. Visitors must avoid hiking alone and must carry emergency equipment such as first-aid kits, maps, and navigation tools.

Health in Hillswick

Visitors must carry their medication and first-aid kit as medical facilities are limited in Hillswick. They must also carry sunscreen and insect repellent as there are a lot of midges during the summer months.

Getting to Hillswick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hillswick

The nearest airport to Hillswick is Sumburgh Airport, and it is approximately 50 miles away. Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Hillswick from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Hillswick

The town is serviced by regular buses, and the nearest bus station is in the nearby town of Brae.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Hillswick by ferry from the Scottish mainland.

How to Get Around in Hillswick

Visitors can hire a car or use public transport to explore the town and its surroundings. Buses, taxis, and car rental services are available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Hillswick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other towns in the Shetland Islands such as Lerwick, Brae, and Scalloway. They can also take a ferry to explore other islands such as Yell, Unst, and Fetlar.

Conclusion

Hillswick is a hidden gem that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, local culture, and adventure activities. Visitors must explore this off-the-beaten-path destination in Scotland and enjoy the stunning landscapes and adventurous activities.
