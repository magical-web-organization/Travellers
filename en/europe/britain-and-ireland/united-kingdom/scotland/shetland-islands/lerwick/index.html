Introduction to Lerwick

Lerwick is a vibrant and bustling town located in the Shetland Islands, Scotland, in the northernmost part of the United Kingdom and Europe. The town boasts a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a strong cultural identity that make it a must-visit destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Lerwick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lerwick has a deep-rooted history and heritage, which can be easily explored through its various museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Shetland Museum and Archives: This award-winning museum tells the story of Shetland’s history, culture, and heritage through interactive exhibits and artifacts. It is a great way to gain an understanding of the local way of life.
  • Fort Charlotte: A historic fortification built in the 17th century. The fort offers stunning views of the harbor and the town.
  • Clickimin Broch: An ancient roundhouse that dates back to the Iron Age. It is an impressive structure and one of the best-preserved brochs in Scotland.

Natural Attractions of Lerwick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lerwick is a paradise for nature lovers, and you can experience the beauty of the coastal landscape and wildlife through various outdoor activities. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Bressay Lighthouse: Located on the neighboring island of Bressay, this lighthouse offers stunning views of the coast and the North Sea. It is an excellent place for whale watching and spotting seabirds.
  • Beach at St. Ninian’s Isle: One of the most beautiful beaches in Scotland, located just a short drive from Lerwick. It boasts a long stretch of white sand and turquoise waters.
  • Hermaness National Nature Reserve: Located on the island of Unst, the northernmost island in the Shetland Islands. It is a popular destination for birdwatching, hiking, and the opportunity to see the incredible Northern Lights.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lerwick

Lerwick offers several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to experience the spectacular scenery of the Shetland Islands. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Shetland Way: This 373 km long-distance route connects the towns and villages of the Shetland Islands. It traverses some of the most stunning landscapes and historic sites of the region.
  • Kergord Forest: A beautiful forest area to explore on foot or by bike. It is home to various bird species and has a peaceful atmosphere perfect for relaxing.
  • Sumburgh Head Heritage Trail: A coastal path that offers breathtaking views of the cliffs, lighthouse, and seabirds. It is an easy trek and suitable for all ages.

Best Things to Do in Lerwick: Activities and Tours

Lerwick offers a plethora of activities and tours to suit every traveler’s interests. Some of the must-try things to do in Lerwick include:

  • Boat Tours: Experience the stunning coastal landscapes, see the abundant marine life and seabirds, and visit neighboring islands on a boat tour.
  • Music and Festivals: Lerwick is renowned for its vibrant music and festival scene. The Shetland Folk Festival, held in April, is an excellent opportunity to experience the traditional music and culture of the region.
  • Shopping: Lerwick has several unique shops and boutiques where you can buy traditional Shetland knitwear, jewelry, and other local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lerwick

Day 1: Explore the town of Lerwick, including the Shetland Museum and Archives, the Market Cross, and nearby cafes and restaurants.

Day 2: Hike the Shetland Way starting from Lerwick to Scalloway. You can visit Scalloway Castle and Museum and have a delicious seafood lunch.

Day 3: Take a boat tour to Noss to experience the spectacular cliffs, seabirds, and wildlife. You can pack a picnic lunch and enjoy it in the stunning outdoors.

Day 4: Visit the island of Bressay and hike to the lighthouse for panoramic views of the coast and North Sea. You can return to Lerwick for dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Unst, the northernmost island in the Shetland Islands, to visit Hermaness National Nature Reserve. You can also visit the island’s Viking sites and local shops.

Day 6: Visit the nearby town of Scalloway, known for its fishing heritage. You can explore the castle, visit local seafood cafes, and walk around the scenic harbor.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day in Lerwick by strolling around the town, visiting local shops, attending a music festival, or having a candlelit dinner in one of the town’s cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Lerwick

Must-Try Dishes

Lerwick has a vibrant food culture, with many local eateries offering traditional Shetland cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Cullen Skink: A hearty soup made from smoked haddock and potatoes.
  • Reestit Mutton: A traditional Shetland dish made from salted and dried mutton. It is usually served with boiled potatoes and vegetables.
  • Lerwick Gin: A locally distilled gin made using Shetland botanicals.

Local Beverages

Lerwick has several local beverages worth trying, including:

  • Shetland Ale: A locally brewed beer made using Shetland-grown barley.
  • Shetland Reel Gin: A gin distilled using a range of Shetland botanicals, including meadowsweet, sea pink, and juniper.

Where to Stay in Lerwick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lerwick has a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Lerwick Hotel: A four-star hotel located in the center of town, offering excellent sea views, cozy rooms, and excellent service.
  • The Shetland Hotel: A centrally located three-star hotel providing comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Peerie Hostel: A budget-friendly option with private rooms and dorms. It is located near the town center and offers a laundry facility and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Lerwick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lerwick has several unique shops selling locally made products. Some of the best souvenirs to buy include:

  • Shetland Knitwear: Lerwick is famous for its knitwear, and you can buy traditional woolen jumpers, cardigans, and accessories.
  • Shetland Jewellery: Local jewelers design and craft beautiful pieces that reflect the region’s natural beauty and Viking heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Lerwick

Lerwick has a unique culture and customs shaped by the region’s landscape, history, and heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming and take pride in their traditions and way of life. Visitors should respect local customs, such as being punctual to appointments and saying “hello” to everyone they meet.

Best Time to Visit Lerwick

The best time to visit Lerwick is during the summer months of June-August when the weather is mild, and the days are long. The Shetland Folk Festival takes place in April, and the Shetland Nature Festival takes place in July, making these months a great time to visit if you’re interested in music and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lerwick

Do:

  • Do explore the stunning natural landscapes and wildlife of the region.
  • Do try traditional Shetland dishes and local beverages.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Do say “hello” to everyone you meet.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, especially during the winter months.
  • Don’t be late for appointments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lerwick

Lerwick is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take precautions, such as locking doors and windows and not leaving valuables unattended. It is also essential to be careful when hiking or walking along the coast and to follow safety signs and instructions.

Health in Lerwick

Lerwick has excellent healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and clinics. Visitors to the region should take adequate precautions, such as using sunscreen and insect repellent, and drinking plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Getting to Lerwick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lerwick

Lerwick is serviced by Sumburgh Airport, which offers direct flights from Edinburgh, Glasgow, and Aberdeen.

Bus Stations Near Lerwick

Lerwick has a bus station located in the town center, providing regular connections to other towns and villages in the Shetland Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lerwick by ferry from Aberdeen or by taking the Northlink ferry from Orkney and mainland Scotland.

How to Get Around in Lerwick

The best way to get around Lerwick is by walking or cycling. The town is small and compact, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use the local bus service to travel to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Lerwick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lerwick is an excellent base for exploring other destinations in the Shetland Islands. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • The Island of Foula: A remote island accessible by ferry. It is a popular destination for birdwatching and hiking.
  • The Island of Yell: A stunning island known for its beaches, wildlife, and historic sites.
  • Jarlshof: An impressive prehistoric settlement located on the southern tip of Mainland Shetland.

Conclusion

Lerwick is a fascinating and unique destination that offers something for every traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s history and culture, hiking or biking through the stunning landscapes, or tasting the local food and beverages, Lerwick is the perfect place for your next adventure.
