Introduction to Crianlarich

Nestled in the heart of Scotland, Crianlarich is a picturesque village surrounded by stunning mountain ranges, glens, and lochs. While not as well-known as other Scottish destinations like Edinburgh or Glasgow, Crianlarich is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to escape the crowds and immerse themselves in Scotland’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Crianlarich

Crianlarich has something for everyone, whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor adventure. Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places in Crianlarich:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Crianlarich War Memorial
  • Crianlarich Old Kirk
  • St Fillan’s Priory
  • Glen Dochart Distillery

Natural Attractions of Crianlarich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ben More, the tallest mountain in the region
  • Rob Roy Way, a 94-mile long-distance walking route through the Scottish Highlands
  • The West Highland Way, a popular hiking trail that passes through Crianlarich
  • Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park
  • Inveraray Castle

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crianlarich

If you’re a hiking or biking enthusiast, Crianlarich is the perfect destination for you. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Ben More (1174m) and Stob Binnein (1165m) Circular
  • An Caisteal and Beinn a’Chroin
  • Crianlarich Hills
  • Glen Falloch by Bike

Best Things to Do in Crianlarich: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Crianlarich. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Boat trips on Loch Lomond
  • Wildlife watching tours

  • Golf at Killin Golf Club

Week-Long Itinerary for Crianlarich

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Crianlarich, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Crianlarich and explore the village. Visit the Crianlarich War Memorial and St Fillan’s Priory.

Day 2: Hike up Ben More and Stob Binnein and take in the stunning views from the summit.

Day 3: Explore Glen Coe and the surrounding area. Visit the beautiful Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park.

Day 4: Take a leisurely walk along the West Highland Way and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Day 5: Visit Inveraray Castle and the nearby Glen Dochart Distillery.

Day 6: Go wildlife watching or horse riding, or try your hand at golf at Killin Golf Club.

Day 7: Depart from Crianlarich.
