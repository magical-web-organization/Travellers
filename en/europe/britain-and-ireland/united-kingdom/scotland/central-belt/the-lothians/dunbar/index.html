Welcome to Dunbar, a charming coastal town located in The Lothians region of Central Belt in Scotland. This picturesque town is not only known for its natural beauty but also its rich history and culture. Whether you’re a history buff, hiker, foodie, or just seeking a peaceful getaway, Dunbar has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more in Dunbar.

List of Places to Visit in Dunbar

There are plenty of attractions to see in Dunbar, ranging from historical monuments to natural wonders. Here are some must-visit places in Dunbar.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John Muir’s Birthplace Museum: A museum dedicated to the life and legacy of John Muir, the Scottish-American conservationist, and writer who founded the Sierra Club in the USA.

  • Dunbar Town House Museum and Gallery: The Dunbar Town House Museum and Gallery is a community-run museum featuring a variety of local exhibits.

  • Dunbar Harbour: A scenic harbor, which has been a historic fishing village since the middle ages.

Natural Attractions of Dunbar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • John Muir Country Park: An idyllic park that spans over 1340 acres and is named after John Muir, the famous Scottish-American naturalist. The park boasts beautiful landscapes, including woodlands, salt marshes, and sand dunes.

  • Dunbar Castle: This ruined castle dates back to 1070 AD and is the perfect spot for a rugged coastal walk with stunning views.

  • Belhaven Bay: Belhaven Bay boasts a stunning, golden sandy beach and dunes, perfect for long walks and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dunbar

Dunbar offers some of the best countryside areas for hiking and biking. Here are some recommended routes to explore:

  • John Muir Way: This 134-mile hiking and biking trail runs through East Lothian and connects Dunbar to Helensburgh.

  • Lammermuir Hills: With rugged terrain and panoramic views, this hill range between Lauder and Gifford offers excellent routes for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Dunbar: Activities and Tours

Dunbar offers lots of fun activities for tourists. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Surfing: Dunbar is home to Belhaven Bay, one of the best surfing spots in the country.

  • Golfing: With 22 golf courses in East Lothian, including Dunbar Golf Club, the region is a haven for golf enthusiasts.

  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour from Dunbar Harbour and visit dramatic coastlines, sea caves, and hidden harbors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dunbar

Day 1: Explore John Muir’s Birthplace/ Dunbar Townhouse Museum and Gallery

Day 2: Visit Dunbar Castle and enjoy a coastal walk to Belhaven Bay

Day 3: Plan a day trip to North Berwick and visit the Scottish Seabird Centre

Day 4: Hike or bike in the Lammermuir Hills

Day 5: Take a boat tour from Dunbar Harbor

Day 6: Visit Tantallon Castle, Mellerstain House, and Gardens

Day 7: Spend a day at Belhaven Bay

Food and Drink Scene of Dunbar

Dunbar offers a wealth of dining options, from traditional Scottish fare to trendy cafes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A classic Scottish smoked haddock soup

  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish is made with sheep’s pluck (heart, liver, and lungs), onions, oats, and spices

  • Fish and Chips: A British classic, but with a Scottish twist – try it with haddock

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey: Scotland’s famous whiskey, with several distilleries in the region

  • Belhaven Brewery Beer: A Dunbar-based brewery that creates a wide selection of delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Dunbar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dunbar has plenty of options when it comes to accommodation. Here are some recommended options:

  • Bayswell Park Hotel & Spa: A luxury hotel with panoramic views over the seaside town of Dunbar

  • The Pine Marten by Marston’s Inn: A contemporary hotel with beautiful garden views

  • The Dunmuir Hotel: A family-run hotel located in the heart of the town

What to Buy in Dunbar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • John Muir Merchandise: Buy souvenirs in honor of John Muir, including books, t-shirts, and postcards.

  • Belhaven Brewery Beer: Take some locally brewed beers home for a unique taste of Dunbar.

Local Culture and Customs of Dunbar

The locals in Dunbar are friendly and welcoming. Scottish Gaelic is not commonly spoken in this area, but the traditional Scottish etiquette of hospitality is still evident in the area.

Best Time to Visit Dunbar

The best time to visit Dunbar is between May and September when the temperature is typically warm and sunny. The town also hosts several festivals during the summer season, including the Dunbar Civic Week in June and the Coastword Festival in May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dunbar

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks

  • Do respect the local culture

  • Do take advantage of the beautiful nature

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute the environment

  • Don’t be disrespectful towards locals or other tourists

  • Don’t visit historical sites without proper knowledge and awareness

Safety and Health

Safety in Dunbar

Dunbar is generally a very safe town, but as with any destination, it’s always a good idea to exercise caution and take basic safety precautions.

Health in Dunbar

Dunbar has a range of medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. It’s always a good idea to make sure you have travel insurance before you visit.

Getting to Dunbar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dunbar

Edinburgh Airport is the nearest airport to Dunbar (31 miles), and there are direct train connections from Edinburgh Waverley Station to Dunbar regularly.

Bus Stations Near Dunbar

The Dunbar bus station is located on Countess Road, providing easy access to the town from nearby cities.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are readily available for travelers in the area.

How to Get Around in Dunbar

Dunbar is a small town, which makes it easy to get around on foot. Bikes are also a popular way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Dunbar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dunbar is located in a prime position between Edinburgh and other popular Scottish destinations. Here are some recommended places to visit close to Dunbar:

  • Edinburgh: This is the perfect destination for history and culture buffs. Visit the Edinburgh Castle or stroll through the Royal Botanic Garden.

  • North Berwick: North Berwick is a picturesque coastal town located just five miles west of Dunbar. It’s known for its stunning beaches, lovely cafes, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Dunbar is a hidden gem of Scotland, where you’ll experience scenic landscapes, rich history, and a warm welcome from the locals. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or both, this lovely seaside town has something up its sleeves for everyone.
