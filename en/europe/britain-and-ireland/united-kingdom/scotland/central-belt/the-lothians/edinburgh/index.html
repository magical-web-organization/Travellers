Complete Travel Guide to Edinburgh: A Must-Visit Destination

Edinburgh is a lively city known for its enchanting architecture, stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and delightful food scene. If you’re planning a trip to Scotland, Edinburgh is a must-visit destination that can satisfy all your desires, from a well-curated museum visit to outdoor adventures, local food trails, and many more. This comprehensive travel guide will take you on a journey to discover Edinburgh’s rich culture, scenic views, best activities, and local food scene. Learn about the best places to visit, hike/bike routes, local customs, safety tips, and transportation options to make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Edinburgh

Edinburgh is the capital city of Scotland, located in the Lothian region, Central Belt, United Kingdom, Great Britain and Ireland, and Europe. Edinburgh has a population of over 500,000 people and is renowned for its rich history and culture. The city has an eclectic mix of old and new buildings, green spaces, and museums that attract visitors from every corner of the world.

List of Places to Visit in Edinburgh

When visiting Edinburgh, there are several places to add to your itinerary, each with a unique story and beauty. Here are some top-rated places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Edinburgh Castle: Perched on a rocky outcrop overlooking the city, this iconic fortress is a must-visit attraction. The castle has been a royal residence for over 1,000 years and is home to Scotland’s crown jewels.
  2. National Museum of Scotland: This museum holds over 20,000 artifacts, from art and design to science and technology. The museum’s top floor has a rooftop terrace that offers a stunning view of the city.
  3. Scottish Parliament: The parliament building is a modern architectural marvel and hosts debates, workshops, and events open to the public.
  4. Royal Botanic Garden: If you’re a nature lover, this garden is perfect for exploring over 70 acres of botanical wonders, mesmerizing landscapes, and vast greenhouses.

Natural Attractions of Edinburgh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Arthur’s Seat: This impressive hill towers over the city and offers breathtaking views of Edinburgh. The hike to the summit is rewarding, and the path is well-maintained.
  2. Holyrood Park: A vast area surrounding Arthur’s Seat is home to several hills, ponds, and wildlife. It’s a great place for a picnic or stroll.
  3. Portobello Beach: A quick trip out of the city center will lead you to one of Edinburgh’s best beaches, perfect for a stroll or a dip in the sea.
  4. Water of Leith Walkway: This 12-mile path follows the river that runs through Edinburgh and takes you through urban landscapes and lush green forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edinburgh

For the outdoor enthusiasts, Edinburgh has several hiking and biking routes to explore the city’s stunning views and lush green spaces. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes:

  1. Union Canal Towpath: This 13-mile route goes from Edinburgh to Falkirk and passes through the city’s best sites, including the Scottish National Gallery of Modern Art and the Royal Botanic Garden.
  2. Blackford Hill: A hike to the summit of Blackford Hill will reward you with an extensive view of Edinburgh’s skyline, from the castle to Arthur’s Seat.
  3. The Pentland Hills: A short drive out of the city takes you to some of Scotland’s best hillwalking trails, offering panoramic views of the city and beyond.

Best Things to Do in Edinburgh: Activities and Tours

Edinburgh has a wide variety of things to do, from cultural experiences to adventure sports. Here are our top picks:

  1. Royal Mile: Take a stroll down the Royal Mile to explore medieval architecture, historic landmarks, and souvenir shops.
  2. Ghost Tours: Edinburgh is famous for its haunted past, so why not take a chilling ghost tour and learn the dark stories of the city’s past.
  3. The Edinburgh Dungeon: This interactive attraction takes you on a journey through Edinburgh’s history of torture, execution, and darkness.
  4. Whisky Tours: No trip to Scotland is complete without tasting Scotland’s national drink. Several distilleries offer tours and tastings around the city.
  5. Harry Potter Tour: Edinburgh’s connection to Harry Potter inspired J.K. Rowling’s novels. Follow the trail of the wizard and visit the filming locations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edinburgh

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Edinburgh, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Explore the Royal Mile and Edinburgh Castle
Day 2 Visit the National Museum of Scotland, The Scottish Parliament, and Holyrood Palace
Day 3 Go on a ghost tour, The Edinburgh Dungeon, and The Real Mary King's Close

Food and Drink Scene of Edinburgh

Edinburgh has a vibrant food scene, from traditional Scottish dishes to modern cuisine and craft beer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Haggis: Scotland’s national dish is a savory pudding made of sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs.
  2. Cullen Skink: This thick soup made of smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions is a staple in Scottish cuisine.
  3. Fish and Chips: Edinburgh’s coastal location offers some of the freshest fish and crispiest chips available.
  4. Shortbread: Scotland’s famous buttery, crumbly, and delicious shortbread biscuits are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  1. Whisky: As mentioned above, Scotland’s national drink, has several distilleries around the city that offer tastings and tours.
  2. Ale and Beer: Scotland has a thriving craft beer scene, with many breweries and bars to sample from.
  3. Irn-Bru: Scotland’s other national drink is a bright orange soda, slightly bitter, and beloved by locals.

Where to Stay in Edinburgh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Edinburgh has many accommodation options catering to various budgets and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

  1. The Balmoral: A luxurious and iconic hotel located in the city center, offering stunning views and an excellent spa.
  2. The Glasshouse: A boutique hotel across from the Calton Hill, offering stylish and comfortable rooms.
  3. Stay Central Hotel: A budget hotel located in the middle of the city, walking distance from most attractions.
  4. High Street Hostel: A hostel in the heart of the Old Town offering affordable accommodation for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Edinburgh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Edinburgh has several shops and markets selling local products and souvenirs. Here are some suggestions:

  1. Tartan: Scotland’s iconic fabric comes in several designs and patterns and makes for an excellent souvenir.
  2. Whisky: Bring home a bottle of Scotland’s famous drink to enjoy or gift to friends and family.
  3. Shortbread: Edinburgh has several bakeries selling delicious shortbread biscuits that make for a perfect gift.
  4. Traditional Scottish Figurines: These hand-painted figurines, including kilts, bagpipes, and Highland cattle, are cute and quirky souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Edinburgh

Edinburgh’s locals are friendly, polite, and welcoming to visitors. However, like most Scottish cities, there are a few customs to keep in mind:

  1. Dress Appropriately: Depending on the time of year you’re visiting, make sure you dress adequately for Scotland’s weather, which can be wet and cold.
  2. Tipping: Tipping in Edinburgh is customary but not mandatory. Usually, 10% of the total bill is reasonable.
  3. Punctuality: British people value punctuality, so make sure you’re on time for any appointment or meeting.
  4. Using Scottish Words: Edinburgh has its own Patois, with some words slightly different compared to standard English. For example, Edinburghers say “Ta” instead of “Thank you.”

Best Time to Visit Edinburgh

The best time to visit Edinburgh is between May and October when the weather is mild, and several outdoor festivals and events take place. However, Edinburgh’s weather can be unpredictable, so make sure you pack clothes suitable for all climates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edinburgh

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Edinburgh:

Do:

  1. Try Haggis: Scotland’s national dish is a must-try when visiting Edinburgh.
  2. Explore the old town and the Royal Mile: Edinburgh’s rich history and culture are visible in the old town’s architecture and landmarks.
  3. Go on a walking tour: Edinburgh has several walking tours that will tell you stories about the city’s past, culture, and legends.
  4. Take the tram to the airport: Edinburgh airport’s tram is a quick, cheap, and eco-friendly way to reach the city center.

Don’t:

  1. Underestimate Edinburgh’s weather: Scotland is famous for its rainy and cold weather, so pack accordingly.
  2. Assume Scots are English: Scotland has its own identity, culture, and history that are unique and separate from Great Briten.
  3. Forget your rental car’s limitations: Edinburgh’s medieval architecture and narrow streets can be unfamiliar to drivers, so exercise caution.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edinburgh

Edinburgh is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, like most cities, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid unlit, isolated streets at night. It’s also essential to look both ways when crossing the street, as cars drive on the left side of the road in Scotland.

Health in Edinburgh

Edinburgh has several hospitals and medical centers that provide excellent medical care. For non-emergency situations, pharmacies will offer over-the-counter solutions and advice.

Getting to Edinburgh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Edinburgh has excellent transport links, making it easy to access the city from anywhere in the world. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Edinburgh

Edinburgh Airport is located about 8 miles from the city center and offers several international and domestic flights. There are various transport options to get to the city center, including bus, tram, taxi, and car rental.

Bus Stations Near Edinburgh

Edinburgh’s central bus station is located in the city center and offers national and international routes. There are several bus companies providing efficient and reasonably priced transport between Edinburgh and other cities and towns in Scotland and the UK.

Other Options

Trains are a popular mode of transport in Scotland, with several routes stopping at Edinburgh’s Waverley Station in the city center.

How to Get Around in Edinburgh

Exploring Edinburgh on foot is the best way to see everything the city has to offer. However, if you prefer public transportation, Edinburgh has an extensive bus and tram network that serves the city and surrounding areas. Taxis and “Uber” services are available but more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Edinburgh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are several places near Edinburgh you can explore, including:

  1. Glasgow: Scotland’s largest city is just an hour away by train and is known for its art, music, and culture.
  2. St. Andrews: A university town and golfing haven about an hour north of Edinburgh.
  3. The Highlands: Scotland’s remote and rugged landscape is an easy drive or tour away from Edinburgh, offering stunning scenery, wildlife, and castles.

Conclusion

Edinburgh is a must-visit destination in Scotland, offering a mix of history, culture, natural beauty, and gastronomic delights. This comprehensive travel guide introduces you to everything you need to know to plan your trip, from places to visit, things to do, local customs, safety tips, and transportation options. We wish you a pleasant trip and hope that you have a wonderful time exploring the richness and diversity of Edinburgh, Scotland’s enchanting capital city.
