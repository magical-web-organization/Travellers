Introduction to New Town

New Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the heart of Edinburgh, capital of Scotland. It was built in the 18th century to provide an elegant and spacious area for the city’s expanding population, who had outgrown the cramped conditions of Edinburgh Old Town. New Town is characterized by its Georgian architecture, wide streets, and elegant squares, and is home to many prestigious cultural institutions, shops, and restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in New Town

New Town is a hub of cultural and natural attractions, offering an array of experiences for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the must-visit places in New Town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Scottish National Gallery

The Scottish National Gallery is home to a collection of world-class art, featuring works by famous artists such as Botticelli, Raphael, and Van Gogh. It is free to enter and is located in the heart of the city.

The Georgian House

The Georgian House is a restored 18th-century townhouse, decorated in the style of the time, giving visitors a glimpse into the life and times of wealthy Georgian citizens.

St. Andrew Square

St. Andrew Square is a bustling public square home to many prestigious institutions such as the headquarters of the Royal Bank of Scotland. The square is surrounded by elegant Georgian buildings.

Natural Attractions of New Town: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calton Hill

Calton Hill is a famous landmark in Edinburgh, known for its picturesque views of the city and its many monuments, including the Nelson Monument, and the Dugald Stewart Monument. The hill is a popular spot for picnicking and stargazing.

Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh

The Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh is one of the oldest botanic gardens in the world. It boasts a collection of over 13,000 plant species across four different gardens and is a haven of calm and tranquillity in the city centre.

Princes Street Gardens

Princes Street Gardens is a beautiful public park in the heart of Edinburgh, with stunning views of Edinburgh Castle. It is a popular spot for picnicking and enjoying the sunshine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Town

New Town is well-situated for exploring the Scottish countryside. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to take:

Arthur’s Seat

Arthur’s Seat is an extinct volcano located in Holyrood Park, just a short walk from Edinburgh city centre. It offers stunning views of the city and is a popular hiking spot for locals and tourists alike.

Water of Leith Walkway

The Water of Leith Walkway is a 12-mile long path that follows the path of the River Leith through Edinburgh’s city centre. It is a beautiful trail that takes walkers through peaceful green spaces and past historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in New Town: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in New Town:

Whiskey Tasting

Scotland is renowned for its whisky, and there’s no better place to experience it than in Edinburgh. You can take a tour of one of the city’s many distilleries, or enjoy a guided whisky tasting in one of New Town’s many bars.

Shopping

New Town is home to many prestigious shops, from luxury boutiques to independent bookshops. Some popular shopping destinations include George Street, Multrees Walk, and Waverley Mall.

Ghost Tours

Edinburgh is known as one of the most haunted cities in the world, and there are several ghost tours that take visitors through the city’s spookiest locations.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Town

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your time in New Town:

Day 1: Explore Princes Street Gardens and the Scottish National Gallery.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Georgian House, followed by afternoon tea at the Signet Library.

Day 3: Hike up Arthur’s Seat for stunning views of the city, followed by a tour of the Edinburgh Gin Distillery.

Day 4: Visit the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh and enjoy a picnic lunch in the sunshine.

Day 5: Take a bike ride along the Water of Leith Walkway, stopping for lunch at one of the many waterside cafes.

Day 6: Explore St Andrew Square and the surrounding Georgian architecture.

Day 7: Take a ghost tour of the city, followed by a farewell dinner at one of New Town’s many excellent restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of New Town

New Town offers a range of dining options, from fine dining to street food. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Haggis

Haggis is a traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with onions, oats, and spices.

Cullen Skink

Cullen Skink is a thick and creamy soup made from smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.

Cranachan

Cranachan is a traditional Scottish dessert made from a mixture of cream, oats, raspberries, and whisky.

Local Beverages

Whisky

Whisky is Scotland’s national drink and is world-renowned for its complexity and depth of flavour. There are many distilleries in and around Edinburgh that offer tastings and tours.

Scottish Gin

Scottish gin is a rising star in the spirits world, with many small-batch producers making high-quality gin using local ingredients. Many bars and restaurants in New Town offer Scottish gin flights.

Where to Stay in New Town: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Town offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular options include the Balmoral Hotel, the Radisson Collection, and the smart city backpackers hostel.

What to Buy in New Town: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No visit to New Town would be complete without taking home a little piece of Scotland with you. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy:

Tartan Scarves

Tartan scarves are a quintessentially Scottish accessory and make great gifts for friends and family back home.

Edinburgh Gin

Edinburgh Gin is a delicious and unique souvenir that can be enjoyed long after your trip is over.

Local Culture and Customs of New Town

Scotland has a rich and fascinating culture, and New Town is no exception. Some important customs to keep in mind include:

Tipping

Tipping is not expected in Scotland, but it is appreciated for excellent service.

Kilts

The kilt is a traditional Scottish garment, and many men in Scotland still wear them for formal occasions.

Best Time to Visit New Town

The best time to visit New Town is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city is buzzing with festivals and events. However, Edinburgh is a year-round destination, and there’s always something to see and do, no matter when you visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Town

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting New Town:

Do:

  • Visit the Scottish National Gallery
  • Explore the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh
  • Enjoy some whisky or gin in one of the city’s many bars

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack warm clothing, even in the summer
  • Assume that everyone in Scotland wears a kilt
  • Expect to tip in bars and restaurants

Safety and Health

Safety in New Town

New Town is a relatively safe place to visit, but like any city, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables close. Avoid walking alone in poorly-lit areas at night, and be cautious when using ATMs.

Health in New Town

Scotland has a comprehensive healthcare system, and visitors to New Town can access medical care in case of emergency. However, it’s important to take care of your health while travelling by staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and taking necessary medications.

Getting to New Town: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Town

Edinburgh International Airport is the main airport serving Edinburgh and is located just 20 minutes from New Town by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near New Town

The main bus station in Edinburgh is located in the city centre, just a few minutes’ walk from New Town.

Other Options

Edinburgh is well-connected by train, with frequent connections to and from cities across Scotland and England.

How to Get Around in New Town

New Town is easy to navigate on foot, but for those who prefer to use public transport, there are many buses and trams that connect the area to other parts of Edinburgh.

Where to Go Next from New Town: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Town is well-situated for exploring the Scottish countryside. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Edinburgh Old Town

Edinburgh Old Town is just a short walk away from New Town and is home to some of the city’s most historic landmarks, including Edinburgh Castle and the Royal Mile.

The Scottish Borders

The Scottish Borders region is located just south of Edinburgh and is a beautiful area of rolling hills, charming towns, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene, New Town is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Scotland. Whether you’re interested in history, art, outdoor adventures, or simply soaking up the atmosphere, New Town has something to offer everyone.
