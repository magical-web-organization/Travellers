Introduction to East

East, located in Edinburgh, is a vibrant and charming area that offers visitors a unique blend of culture and natural beauty. It is the perfect destination for those who want to explore Scotland’s history and immerse themselves in the local culture. With its stunning landscapes, rich heritage, and artistic treasures, East has something to offer every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in East

There are a number of must-see places in East that you won’t want to miss. Here are some of the top cultural and natural attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palace of Holyroodhouse – The official residence of the British monarchy in Scotland, this palace offers visitors an insight into the country’s royal history.
  • The Royal Mile – A historic street that connects Edinburgh Castle to the Palace of Holyroodhouse, featuring a number of historic buildings and attractions.
  • The Scottish Parliament – A modern building that houses the Scottish parliament, designed by renowned architect Enric Miralles.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arthur’s Seat – A dormant volcano that offers stunning panoramic views of the city and surrounding countryside.
  • Calton Hill – A hill overlooking Edinburgh with an observatory, memorials, and scenic views.
  • Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh – A beautiful garden with diverse plant collections and a Victorian Palm House.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East has a number of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the coastline and surrounding hills. Here are a few popular routes to try:

  • The Water of Leith Walkway - A tranquil path that follows the course of the Water of Leith through the heart of the city.
  • Edinburgh to Queensferry – A stunning coastal route that takes you through the villages of Cramond and Dalmeny.
  • Holyrood Park – A hill walk with several routes including around Arthur’s Seat, Salisbury Crags and the Radical Road.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

From historic walking tours to whisky tastings, there is no shortage of things to do in East. Here are some popular activities and tours to consider:

  • Edinburgh Ghost Tour – Explore the city’s spooky side with this evening walking tour that takes you to some of Edinburgh’s most haunted spots.
  • Whisky Tasting Tour – Learn about Scotland’s national drink and try some of the finest whiskies on a tour that takes you through a number of renowned distilleries.
  • Water of Leith Canoe Trail - A unique way to explore Edinburgh by water where you can enjoy seeing otters, herons, kingfishers, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

If you have a week to spend in East, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1 – Arrive in Edinburgh and spend the day exploring the city.

Day 2 – Explore the Royal Mile and visit the Palace of Holyroodhouse.

Day 3 – Take a hike up Arthur’s Seat for panoramic views of the city and browse the shops in the trendy area of Stockbridge.

Day 4 – Take a day trip to St. Andrews and visit the historic university town.

Day 5 – Visit the Edinburgh Botanic Garden and enjoy a picnic on the grounds.

Day 6 – Take a whisky tasting tour and experience some of Scotland’s finest whisky.

Day 7 – Visit the Scottish Parliament and end the day with a delicious meal at one of Edinburgh’s top restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of East

East has a rich culinary scene with an emphasis on fresh, local ingredients. From traditional Scottish fare to international cuisine, there is no shortage of great food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis – A traditional Scottish dish made from sheep’s liver, heart, and lungs, served with neeps and tatties (turnips and potatoes).
  • Cullen Skink – A thick soup made from smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream.
  • Cranachan – A dessert made from raspberries, whipped cream, honey, and oatmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky – Scotland’s national drink, made from malted barley and aged in oak casks.
  • Edinburgh Gin – A premium Scottish gin made with locally sourced botanicals.
  • Beer – Scotland has a thriving craft beer scene, with a number of excellent breweries in the Edinburgh area.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious hotels to affordable hostels, East has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some top picks:

  • The Balmoral Hotel – A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, with stunning views of Edinburgh Castle.
  • Hostelling Scotland Edinburgh – A budget-friendly hostel located in the Leith area of Edinburgh.
  • Harris Guesthouse – A charming guesthouse located in the Portobello area of Edinburgh, offering cozy rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or gifts to bring home, East has a number of great options. Here are some local products to consider:

  • Harris Tweed – A traditional Scottish cloth made from wool, with unique patterns and textures.
  • Whisky – Scotland’s national drink, with many distilleries offering unique blends and limited editions.
  • Scottish Jewelry – Beautifully crafted jewelry made from precious stones and metals, often featuring traditional Celtic designs.

Local Culture and Customs of East

Scotland has a rich cultural heritage, with many customs and traditions that are still celebrated today. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting East:

  • Bagpipes – The sound of bagpipes is a quintessential part of Scottish culture and can often be heard at events and celebrations.
  • Tartan – A unique plaid pattern that is associated with Scotland and often worn at special occasions.
  • Ceilidhs – A traditional Scottish gathering with live music, dancing, and socializing.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is from April to September, when the weather is mild and there are many outdoor festivals and events taking place. However, even in the colder months, Edinburgh has a charm and unique atmosphere that is worth experiencing.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

As with any travel destination, there are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting East:

Do:

  • Do try haggis, whisky, and other traditional Scottish foods and drinks.
  • Do explore the city on foot, as many of the best attractions are within walking distance.
  • Do take part in a ceilidh, as it’s a great way to experience Scottish culture and make new friends.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing, as Edinburgh can be chilly even in the summer.
  • Don’t disrespect local traditions, such as wearing tartan without proper connection or meaning behind it.
  • Don’t forget to tip service industry professionals like servers, taxi drivers, and hairdressers.

Safety and Health

Edinburgh is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take common sense precautions such as avoiding dark alleys and not leaving valuables unattended. Here are some health and safety tips:

Safety in East

  • Be mindful when crossing roads, as many are narrow and busy.
  • Be aware of your personal belongings when in crowded areas such as Royal Mile during the festival season.
  • Follow road signs and signals, as the city’s traffic can be erratic.

Health in East

  • Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance before leaving home.
  • Familiarise yourself with emergency medical aid at 111, Accident and Emergencies sites in NHS Lothian, for quick response to any medical crisis.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Edinburgh from other parts of the UK:

Airports Near East

  • Edinburgh Airport is the main international airport in the Edinburgh area, with flights from many European cities and beyond.

Bus Stations Near East

  • Edinburgh Bus Station is located in the city centre, with regular services to destinations across Scotland and the UK.

Other Options

  • Train – Edinburgh has two main train stations, Waverley and Haymarket, offering frequent services to destinations across Scotland and the UK.
  • Car – East is easily accessible by car, with several major roads connecting the area to the rest of Scotland and the UK.

How to Get Around in East

East is a relatively small area and can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, the city also has an excellent public transportation system, with buses and trains connecting the area to other parts of Edinburgh and Scotland.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East is the perfect starting point for exploring the rest of Scotland. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Scottish Borders – A scenic region located south of Edinburgh, known for its history and natural beauty.
  • St. Andrews – A historic university town located on the east coast of Scotland, known for its golf courses and charming architecture.

Conclusion

East, Edinburgh, Scotland is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning landscapes, rich heritage, and vibrant cultural scene, East has something to offer every traveler. From hiking and biking to whisky tours and ghost walks, there are plenty of activities and experiences to suit all tastes. Plan your trip today and discover all that East has to offer!
