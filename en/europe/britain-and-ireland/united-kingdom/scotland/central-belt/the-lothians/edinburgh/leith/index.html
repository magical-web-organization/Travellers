Introduction to Leith

Leith is a small port town located in Edinburgh in The Lothians region of Scotland. Located on the southern shore of the Firth of Forth, Leith has a long history as a hub of maritime trade and industry. The town is home to a thriving cultural scene, a variety of restaurants and bars, and some of Scotland’s most impressive historic sites.

List of Places to Visit in Leith

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Leith is home to several museums and historic sites that highlight the town’s unique heritage. Here are some must-see cultural attractions:

  • The Royal Yacht Britannia: The former yacht of Queen Elizabeth II, now docked in Leith for tours and events.
  • The Scottish Storytelling Centre: A museum dedicated to the art and craft of Scottish storytelling.
  • The Leith Theatre: A historic venue that hosted legendary acts like AC/DC and Thin Lizzy in the 70s and 80s.
  • The Trinity House Museum: A maritime museum showcasing the history of shipbuilding and seafaring in Leith.

Natural Attractions of Leith: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Leith is also home to several natural attractions that showcase the beauty of the Scottish coastline. Here are some of the best places to take in the scenery:

  • Leith Links: A large park with green spaces for walking or running, with excellent views of the nearby harbor.
  • Newhaven Harbour: A small fishing village within Leith, this charming harbour is perfect for walking paths and great views of Firth of Forth.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leith

Leith has several great hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the local countryside. Here are some top picks for outdoor adventures in and around Leith:

  • Water of Leith Walkway: A beautiful path that winds its way through the heart of the town and through some of the area’s most stunning natural scenery.
  • John Muir Way: This 134-mile trail winds its way from Helensburgh to Dunbar and runs through several towns, including Leith.
  • Edinburgh City Centre: Visitors can rent a bicycle at famous bike shops in Edinburgh and explore Leith’s neighbouring towns like Portobello.

Best Things to Do in Leith: Activities and Tours

Leith is a busy town with plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in and around Leith:

  • Take a Food Tour: Edinburgh is known for excellent cuisine, and there are several food tours available that showcase some of the town’s most delicious dishes.
  • Visit the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh: Located just a few miles from Leith, this famous garden has an impressive variety of plant life from around the world.
  • Explore the Town Centre: Leith is home to several architecturally stunning buildings, including the historic John Knox House and Leith Parish Church.

Week-Long Itinerary for Leith

Leith is an excellent base from which to explore Edinburgh and the surrounding area. Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to the town:

  • Day 1: Explore the local area, including the Royal Yacht Britannia and the Scottish Storytelling Centre.
  • Day 2: Take a food tour of Edinburgh.
  • Day 3: Visit the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh.
  • Day 4: Hike the John Muir Way.
  • Day 5: Visit nearby towns like Portobello and Musselburgh.

  • Day 7: Take a day trip to St. Andrews.

Food and Drink Scene of Leith

Leith is famous for its hearty Scottish cuisine, and visitors will find plenty of local delicacies to sample during their visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A thick, creamy soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish of sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, cooked with onions, oats, and spices, and typically served with neeps and tatties, turnips, and potatoes.
  • Scottish salmon or trout: The nearby coastline is famous for its fresh fish, particularly salmon and trout, served in a variety of styles.

Local Beverages

Leith is home to several local breweries and distilleries that produce some of Scotland’s most famous beverages. Here are some must-try drinks:

  • Scotch Whiskey: Scotland is known for its single malt scotch, and there are several distilleries in the Leith area that offer tours and tastings.
  • Cider: Scotland has a thriving cider scene, and visitors can sample a variety of local ciders at restaurants and bars throughout Leith.
  • Craft Beer: Leith is home to several excellent craft breweries, including the famous Pilot Brewery.

Local Culture and Customs of Leith

Scotland has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors to Leith may encounter several unique customs and traditions during their stay. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Language: The official language of Scotland is English, but many Scots also speak Gaelic, particularly in the Highlands. Leith residents typically speak with a distinctive Scottish accent and use several unique colloquialisms.
  • Food and Drink: Scottish cuisine is famous for its hearty, comforting dishes, and visitors should plan to try at least a few local delicacies while in Leith. Drinking is also a popular pastime, and visitors may encounter pubs and bars that are crowded with locals enjoying a few pints.
  • Highland Games: Scotland is famous for its Highland Games, which are held throughout the country during the summer months. Visitors to Leith may have the opportunity to attend a local Games event and watch as athletes compete in traditional sporting events like caber tossing and hammer throwing.
