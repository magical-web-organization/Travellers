Introduction to Penicuik

Penicuik is a charming town located in The Lothians, in Central Belt, in Scotland, in the United Kingdom. It is a vibrant and bustling town with a unique history and culture that attracts thousands of travelers every year. The town is situated southeast of Edinburgh, and its proximity to the city makes it an ideal destination for those who want to experience both the urban and rural life of Scotland.

List of Places to Visit in Penicuik

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Penicuik House

One of the most important historical sites in Penicuik is Penicuik House, which was built in the 18th century. The house is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens. Visitors can take a guided tour of the house and learn about its history and the people who lived there. They can also explore the gardens and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

The John Buchan Story

The John Buchan Story is a museum dedicated to the life and works of John Buchan, a renowned Scottish author, and politician. The museum has a collection of his works, including the famous novel “The Thirty-Nine Steps”. Visitors can learn about his life, his works, and his legacy as a writer and public figure.

Natural Attractions of Penicuik: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pentland Hills Regional Park

The Pentland Hills Regional Park is a vast expanse of beautiful natural scenery that offers a range of outdoor activities. Visitors can hike, bike, or horseback ride through the hills and enjoy the beautiful views. There are also many trails for walking, running, and nature watching.

Roslin Glen Country Park

Another attractive natural attraction in Penicuik is the Roslin Glen Country Park. The park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the Roslin Glen Gorge. Visitors can explore the park and enjoy the tranquil atmosphere and beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Penicuik

The John Muir Way

The John Muir Way is a long-distance walking and cycling trail that starts in Helensburgh on the west coast of Scotland and ends in Dunbar on the east coast. The trail passes through Penicuik, providing picturesque views of the Pentland Hills and beautiful countryside. It is a popular destination for both hikers and cyclists.

Penicuik to West Linton

The Penicuik to West Linton route is a beautiful scenic drive that provides stunning views of the Scottish Borders. The route is ideal for experienced cyclists and hikers who want to challenge themselves. The trail offers beautiful views and a range of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy.

Best Things to Do in Penicuik: Activities and Tours

Penicuik House Tour

The Penicuik House tour is a must-do activity for those who are interested in history and architecture. Visitors can explore the stunning Georgian mansion and the beautiful gardens. The tour provides an insight into the life and times of the people who lived there.

Rosslyn Chapel Tour

The Rosslyn Chapel tour is a popular activity for those who are interested in the mysteries and legends of Scotland. The chapel is said to be the site of many myths and legends, and its intricate carvings and architecture attract visitors from all over the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Penicuik

Must-Try Dishes

Cullen Skink

Cullen Skink is a traditional Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream. It is a hearty and filling dish that is perfect on a cold day.

Haggis

Haggis is a traditional Scottish dish made with sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with oatmeal and spices, and stuffed into a sheep’s stomach. It is usually served with neeps and tatties, which are turnips and potatoes.

Local Beverages

Whisky

Scotland is famous for its whisky, and Penicuik has some of the best distilleries in the country. Visitors can take a tour of the distilleries and learn about the process of making whisky, as well as get a taste of the local brew.

Craft Beer

Penicuik also has a vibrant craft beer scene, with many local breweries that offer a range of unique and tasty beers. Visitors can sample the local brews and learn about the brewing process.

What to Buy in Penicuik: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tartan

Tartan is a traditional Scottish fabric that is used to make kilts, skirts, scarves, and other clothing accessories. Visitors can buy tartan products in Penicuik and take home a piece of Scottish culture.

Whisky

Whisky is Scotland’s most famous product, and visitors can buy a bottle of local whisky as a souvenir. There are many local distilleries in Penicuik that offer a range of unique and delicious whiskies.
