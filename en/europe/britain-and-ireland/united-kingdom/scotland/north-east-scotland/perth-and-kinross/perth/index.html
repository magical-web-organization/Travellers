Introduction to Perth

Perth, located in Perth and Kinross in North East Scotland, is a charming city full of history and natural beauty. It has been a hub of trade and commerce since medieval times and is now a vibrant city that attracts travelers from all over the world. The city has a range of cultural and natural attractions, making it a popular destination for families, couples, and solo travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more in Perth, Scotland.

List of Places to Visit in Perth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perth Museum and Art Gallery

Located in the heart of the city, the Perth Museum and Art Gallery is a must-see cultural attraction. It features a range of collections, including archaeology, natural history, and art. You can marvel at the permanent Scottish paintings collection, learn about the local history of Perth, and delve into the well-known Silver Gallery.

Scone Palace

Scone Palace is a historical gem and a great place to learn about the rich history of Scotland. It is the former home of the kings of Scotland and one of the most important sites in the country’s history. The palace was where the ancient kings of Scotland were crowned, including Macbeth and Robert the Bruce. You can explore the beautiful gardens, marvel at the architecture of the palace, and learn about the history of Scotland.

Black Watch Museum

The Black Watch Museum is dedicated to the history of Scotland’s first Highland regiment, which was formed in 1725. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, including uniforms, medals, and weapons. The museum provides an in-depth look at Scottish military history and offers interactive exhibits that give an insight into the life of the soldiers.

Natural Attractions of Perth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kinnoull Hill Woodland Park

Kinnoull Hill Woodland Park is located on the outskirts of Perth and is a great place to explore the outdoors. The park offers stunning views of the city and the river Tay. You can take a stroll through the woodlands, walk up the hill to the tower viewpoint, and see the city from a whole new perspective.

The Hermitage

The Hermitage is a National Trust for Scotland site located in Dunkeld, a small village near Perth. It offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and birdwatching. The highlight of the Hermitage is the beautiful waterfall and the picturesque paths that give you a glimpse of Scotland’s natural beauty.

Tentsmuir Forest

Tentsmuir Forest is a nature reserve located in Fife, just a short drive from Perth. It offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and cycling. The highlight of the forest is the stunning coastline, which is perfect for a leisurely walk or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perth

Perth and its surroundings have many hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside. One of the most popular routes is the Cateran Trail, a 64-mile circular route that takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Perthshire. Another popular route is the Perth to Dundee cycling path, which offers stunning views of the Golf of St. Andrews and the surrounding coastline.

Best Things to Do in Perth: Activities and Tours

Perth Racecourse

Perth Racecourse is a popular attraction and hosts a range of events throughout the year, including meetings, corporate events, and weddings. The racecourse is located in Scone Palace parklands, and it is the only racecourse in Scotland that has a turnstile-free environment.

Scottish Wildlife Trust Loch of the Lowes

Loch of the Lowes is a nature reserve located near Dunkeld, where you can see some of Scotland’s most iconic wildlife, including ospreys, red squirrels, and beavers. The reserve has many footpaths and hides for visitors to observe the wildlife.

Perth Theatre

Perth Theatre is an iconic venue that has been around for over 100 years. It offers a range of events, including drama, comedy, and music. This theatre is a must-visit for anyone interested in the arts.

Food and Drink Scene of Perth

Must-Try Dishes

Cullen Skink

Cullen Skink is a traditional Scottish soup, made with smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream. This dish is a must-try for anyone visiting Scotland, and it can be found in many restaurants and cafes in Perth.

Haggis

Haggis is a Scottish delicacy and a must-try dish in Perth. It is made from sheep’s offal and oats and is usually served with neeps and tatties. Haggis can be found in many restaurants and cafes in Perth.

Local Beverages

Whiskey

Scotland is famous for its whiskey, and Perth has many distilleries where you can try the local tipples. Some popular brands are Dewar’s, Edradour, and Glenturret.

Beer

Perth has a range of pubs and breweries where you can sample local Scottish beer. Some popular breweries are Inveralmond Brewery, Perth Brewery, and the Strathbraan Brewery.

Where to Stay in Perth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perth has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. There are many hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city center and surrounding areas, offering comfortable and affordable accommodation for all kinds of travelers.

What to Buy in Perth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perth has many shops and markets selling a range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Tartan clothing and accessories
  • Scottish whiskey and gin
  • Handmade crafts and pottery
  • Traditional Scottish sweets and chocolates

Local Culture and Customs of Perth

Perth has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about local customs and traditions. Scots are known for their love of bagpipes, kilts, and haggis, and Perth is no exception. Visitors are also encouraged to learn about the Gaelic language, which is still spoken in some parts of Scotland.

Best Time to Visit Perth

The best time to visit Perth is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and there are many outdoor events and festivals. Spring and autumn are also good times to visit, but be prepared for cooler temperatures and the possibility of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perth

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including haggis and Cullen Skink
  • Visit the museums and galleries to learn about the history of Scotland
  • Explore the outdoors and take in the natural beauty of Perth

Don’t:

  • Overlook the local customs and traditions of Perth
  • Miss out on the chance to see iconic Scottish wildlife, including red squirrels and ospreys
  • Forget your waterproof jacket, as Scotland is known for its changeable weather

Safety and Health

Safety in Perth

Perth is generally a safe city to visit, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding quiet and poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Perth

Visitors to Perth should ensure they have adequate health insurance in case of illness or injury. The city has a range of medical facilities and hospitals, but treatment can be expensive for visitors who are not covered by insurance. Mosquitoes are not a significant problem in Scotland, but visitors should take precautions against tick bites when walking in the countryside.

Getting to Perth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Perth

The nearest major airport to Perth is Edinburgh Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away. Glasgow Airport is also within easy reach, and it is about an hour and a half’s drive from Perth.

Bus Stations Near Perth

Perth has a bus station located in the city center that serves local and regional destinations. There are also several coach companies that provide bus services to other parts of Scotland and the UK.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can drive to Perth, or take a train to Perth train station, which is located in the city center and is serviced by the main UK rail network.

How to Get Around in Perth

Perth is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. It also has a range of public transport options, including buses and taxis, which are affordable and efficient.

Where to Go Next from Perth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perth is perfectly located for exploring other parts of Scotland. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • St. Andrews, a picturesque town known for its famous golf courses
  • Loch Ness, one of the most famous lochs in Scotland and home of the Loch Ness Monster
  • The Highlands, a stunning region full of rugged landscapes, friendly locals, and rich cultural heritage

Conclusion

Perth is a charming city that has something to offer everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or the great outdoors, Perth has it all. From the beautiful Kinnoull Hill Woodland Park to the iconic Scone Palace, you’ll never be short of things to see and do. So pack your bags, grab your waterproof jacket, and get ready to explore one of Scotland’s most beautiful and fascinating cities.
