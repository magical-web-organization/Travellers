Introduction to Blairgowrie & Rattray

Blairgowrie & Rattray is a town located in Perth and Kinross, North East Scotland, United Kingdom. The town is situated on the banks of River Ericht and is surrounded by beautiful countryside and hills. Blairgowrie is known as the ‘Gateway to the Glens’ and is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and outdoor adventurers. The town is also famous for its seasonal berries, particularly raspberries, which are exported all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Blairgowrie & Rattray

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Blairgowrie Museum

Blairgowrie Museum is a small museum located in the town’s High Street. The museum showcases the town’s history and has exhibits on the local crafts of berry growing and basket making, as well as information on the famous Blairgowrie Advertiser newspaper.

The Ericht Heritage Centre

The Ericht Heritage Centre is located in Rattray and is a popular destination for history enthusiasts. The centre has exhibits on the local history of the area, including the Jacobite Rebellion and the life of the famous Scottish writer, Sir Walter Scott.

Natural Attractions of Blairgowrie & Rattray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Cateran Trail

The Cateran Trail is a 64-mile long circular route that starts and finishes in Blairgowrie. The trail takes in the Angus Glens and the Perthshire countryside and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The trail can be broken down into shorter sections for those who do not want to complete the full route.

The River Ericht

The River Ericht is a beautiful river that runs through Blairgowrie. The river is popular with anglers and walkers and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The river can be easily accessed from the town centre.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blairgowrie & Rattray

Blairgowrie & Rattray has a range of hiking and biking routes to suit all abilities. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Cateran Trail
  • The Dunkeld Hermitage Walk
  • The Cat’s Whiskers Trail
  • The Rattray Headland Trail

Best Things to Do in Blairgowrie & Rattray: Activities and Tours

Blairgowrie Berries

Blairgowrie Berries is one of Scotland’s largest soft fruit farms. Visitors can pick their own raspberries and strawberries and learn about the history of berry growing in the area. The farm also has a shop selling a wide range of berry products.

Glenshee Ski Centre

Glenshee Ski Centre is located just a short drive from Blairgowrie. The ski centre has 22 lifts and 36 runs and is the largest ski resort in Scotland. Visitors can take ski lessons and hire all the necessary equipment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blairgowrie & Rattray

Day 1: Explore Blairgowrie town centre and visit Blairgowrie Museum. Day 2: Walk the Rattray Headland Trail and visit The Ericht Heritage Centre. Day 3: Cycle the Cat’s Whiskers Trail. Day 4: Take a day trip to Glenshee Ski Centre. Day 5: Hike a section of The Cateran Trail. Day 6: Visit Blairgowrie Berries and pick your own berries. Day 7: Relax and explore some of the local pubs and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Blairgowrie & Rattray

Must-Try Dishes

Blairgowrie & Rattray is famous for its seasonal berries, particularly raspberries. Visitors should try a traditional Scottish dessert of raspberries and cream. Other must-try dishes include haggis, neeps and tatties and Cullen skink.

Local Beverages

Scotland is famous for its whisky and visitors to Blairgowrie & Rattray should try a dram of the local single malt. There are also a range of local craft breweries and ales to sample.

Local Culture and Customs of Blairgowrie & Rattray

Blairgowrie & Rattray is a friendly town with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be aware of local customs such as tipping in restaurants and pubs. The locals are proud of their heritage and history and are happy to share their stories with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Blairgowrie & Rattray

The best time to visit Blairgowrie & Rattray is during the summer months when the weather is mild and the countryside is in full bloom. Visitors can also enjoy the local festivals and events that take place during this time.

Getting to Blairgowrie & Rattray: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blairgowrie & Rattray

The nearest airport to Blairgowrie & Rattray is Dundee Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away. Edinburgh Airport and Glasgow Airport are also within easy reach.

Bus Stations Near Blairgowrie & Rattray

Blairgowrie Bus Station is located in the town centre and has regular services to Perth and Dundee. There are also bus services to other parts of Scotland from Dundee and Perth.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Blairgowrie & Rattray by train. The nearest train station is located in Perth, which has regular services to other parts of Scotland and England.

How to Get Around in Blairgowrie & Rattray
