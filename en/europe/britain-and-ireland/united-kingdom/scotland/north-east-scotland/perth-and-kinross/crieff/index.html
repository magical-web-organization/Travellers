Introduction to Crieff

Crieff, located in Perth and Kinross, North East Scotland, is a quaint town that boasts of rich history, stunning scenery, and exciting outdoor adventures. This charming town is perfect for a relaxing getaway, where you can enjoy cultural attractions, outdoor activities, gastronomic delights, and locally-made products.

List of Places to Visit in Crieff

Whether you visit Crieff for a day or a week, there are plenty of must-see attractions that you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Discover Crieff’s fascinating history and traditions by visiting its cultural attractions. Here are some of the top museums and historical places in Crieff:

  • Crieff Visitor Centre: This modern interactive centre introduces visitors to Crieff and the surrounding area, including a 3D map of the local landscape.
  • Drummond Castle Gardens: Known as one of the best gardens in Scotland, Drummond Castle Gardens is a stunning formal garden that features fountains, sculptures and scenic views for the visitor.
  • Deil’s Cauldron: A unique natural feature of the town but for experienced hikers only.
  • Local History Museum: The Local History Museum provides insights into how life was like in Crieff.
  • Drummond Castle: Built in the late 15th century, this castle boasts of remarkable architecture and history.

Natural Attractions of Crieff: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, Crieff offers plenty of exciting outdoor activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Crieff:

  • The Laggan Circuit: a brilliant scenic walk around the area. The route should take around 3 hours, and it combines woods and sites of natural beauty.
  • Moncrieffe Hill: A great place to see the beauty of the countryside and perfect for hiking and enjoying nature.
  • Glen Lednock Circuit: This trail is known for its serene waterfalls and scenic views of the countryside.
  • Village Green: A local park, perfect for a picnic and includes an aviary.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crieff

Crieff is quite a small town, but it is surrounded by scenic landscapes. The best way to explore Crieff’s natural beauty is by hiking or biking. Here are some of the top trails:

  • The Lady Mary’s Walk: This leisurely walk takes you along the River Earn and is ideal for those looking for a short and relaxing stroll.
  • Crieff to Comrie Railway Path: This bike and walking trail takes you through the countryside surrounding Crieff for spectacular views.
  • The Knock Walk: A challenging hike with stunning scenery.
  • Famous Grouse Experience to Crieff Hydro Circuit: This bike trail takes you past some of Scotland’s most beautiful scenery and includes some climbing I the tour.

Best Things to Do in Crieff: Activities and Tours

Crieff offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to all kinds of interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Crieff:

  • Glenturret Distillery Tour: This tour takes visitors through the whiskey-making process and includes whiskey tasting, which is perfect for alcohol enthusiasts.
  • Highlander Foodie Tour: A guided tour where you can sample local delicacies and flavors.
  • Horse Riding: Crieff horse riding excursions are available offering a fabulous opportunity to explore the area on horseback.
  • Golfing: The town has a great golf course situated on the town’s outskirts on the banks of the River Earn.
  • Wildlife Tours: Take a nature drive or tour with a wildlife enthusiast to explore the area’s flora & fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Crieff

If you plan to spend a week in Crieff, you will find yourself with plenty of time to explore the town’s best attractions. Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Crieff:

Day 1: Visit Drummond Castle and its gardens and the Dewar’s Aberfeldy Distillery.

Day 2: Explore the Local History Museum, and check out Crieff’s independent stores for some local shopping.

Day 3: Take the Glenturret Distillery tour, the oldest whisky distillery in Scotland, and taste some whisky.

Day 4: Hike up to the Knock and enjoy the 360-degree views or take a wildlife watching tour.

Day 5: Visit nearby town Comrie, famous for its cascades, with a leisurely walk over the village green.

Day 6: Spend time at leisure in the Macrosty Gardens.

Day 7: Tour by bike or car around some the least travelled roads in Perthshire and Clydesdales.

Food and Drink Scene of Crieff

Crieff is a food and drink lover’s paradise. The town features numerous restaurants, cafes and bars that serve up delectable dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the best dishes you should try in Crieff include:

  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made from sheep offal, oats and spices.
  • Fish and Chips: A classic British dish consisting of deep-fried fish and chips.
  • Stovies: A comforting dish made from leftover cooked meat, onions, and potatoes.
  • Whiskey: A must-try drink in Crieff, where you can sample different whiskeys from local distilleries.

Local Beverages

Crieff is famous for its production of whiskey and wine. Some of the top beverages you should try in Crieff include:

  • Crieff Hydro Spring Water: This water is obtained from natural springs and is sold all over Scotland.
  • Whiskey: The town boasts of many whiskey distilleries, including Glenturret, Scotland’s oldest distillery.
  • Local Ales: Beer enthusiasts should try local ales like Inveralmond, Strathbraan and Trossachs Brewery.

Where to Stay in Crieff: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crieff has plenty of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious guesthouses and hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Crieff:

  • The Crieff Hydro Hotel: This grand Victorian hotel is perfect for those who desire a luxurious stay for their holidays in Crieff.
  • Galvelmore House: This small B&B is ideal for those who want to enjoy a more authentic and personalised experience.
  • Comely Bank Guest House: This beautifully restored Victorian villa is perfect for those wanting a more elegant yet relaxed base for their Crieff trip.

What to Buy in Crieff: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Crieff has plenty of local stores selling locally-made and sourced products. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Whiskey: Crieff is home to many whiskey distilleries, which makes it easy to find local whiskeys to bring back home.
  • Crieff Pottery: Handcrafted pottery and ceramics bearing distinctive Celtic designs are widely sold in Crieff.
  • Local Knitwear: The town’s wool mill and knitwear shop produce high-quality wool garments.
  • Scottish Shortbread: Scottish shortbread is a must-try treat when in Crieff.

Local Culture and Customs of Crieff

Crieff is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are expected to be respectful of its culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: Crieff does not have a specific dress code, but it’s advisable to dress casually but smartly.
  • Greeting People: In Crieff, shaking hands is the typical way to greet people.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Crieff, but it is customary to give a 10-15% tip at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Crieff

The best time to visit Crieff is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and pleasant and allows for outdoor activities. Also, In December, Crieff has a festive atmosphere, thanks to its Christmas market and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crieff

Here are some of the things to do and not do when in Crieff:

Do:

  • Do try and visit the town’s local festivals or events throughout the year.
  • Do try some of the local’s more unusual dishes like the black pudding.
  • Do learn some Scottish slang and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Scotland has strict environmental laws, and it’s advisable to put rubbish in bins or take it back with you.
  • Don’t drink and drive or indulge in other illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Crieff is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take standard precautions to keep themselves safe during their stay.

Safety in Crieff

  • It is advisable to keep your belongings in a safe place and not leave them unattended.
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • It is essential to have your essential medication handy as pharmacy hours can be limited.

Health in Crieff

  • Scotland has a good healthcare system.
  • Travel insurance is strongly recommended to get health coverage in case of emergencies.

Getting to Crieff: Transportation Options and Logistics

Crieff is accessible easily by car or public transportation. Here are some travel options to get to Crieff:

Airports Near Crieff

  • Edinburgh International Airport: The closest international airport that serves Crieff. It is 46 miles away from Crieff.

Bus Stations Near Crieff

  • Crieff Bus Station: The town has a local bus station that connects to other parts of Scotland.

Other Options

  • Train: Trains from Edinburgh, Glasgow and Perth regularly stop in Gleneagles, which is approximately 15 minutes from Crieff by taxi.
  • Car Hire: There are several car hire options in the towns to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Crieff

Crieff is a small town that is easy to explore on foot, but it has local buses that operates on twelve different routes covering the town area. Taxis are reasonably priced for travelling in and around the town.

Where to Go Next from Crieff: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Crieff is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations in Scotland. Here are some of the top destinations to explore:

  • Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park: A scenic park that features lochs, mountains and stunning landscapes.
  • Edinburgh: The Scottish capital is just an hour’s drive away from Crieff.
  • Perth: A historic city that is known for its beautiful architecture and interesting attractions such as Scone Palace and Perth Racecourse.

Conclusion

Crieff is perfect for those looking for a relaxing yet adventure-packed holiday. Whether you’re interested in natural beauty, cultural attractions, or delicious food and drink, Crieff has something to offer. This comprehensive guide gives you all the information you need to explore the town and nearby destinations. Plan your trip to Crieff now and experience the best of Scotland!
