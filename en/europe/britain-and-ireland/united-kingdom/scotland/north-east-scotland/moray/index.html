Introduction to Moray

Moray, a region located in the northeast of Scotland, is known for its charming rural landscape and rich history. Its diverse landscape includes rolling hills, serene beaches, and stunning coastlines, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Moray is also home to some of Scotland’s best preserved historical sites, including castles, museums, and monuments. In this travel guide, we will explore the top things to do, see, and experience in Moray.

List of Places to Visit in Moray

Here are some places that you shouldn’t miss when visiting Moray:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Elgin Cathedral: A magnificent ruin of a medieval cathedral and one of the most important historic monuments in Scotland.
  • Spynie Palace: Formerly the residence of the bishop of Moray, now a well-preserved castle ruin with beautiful gardens.
  • The Moray Firth: A beautiful coastline with stunning views and an ideal spot for dolphin watching.

Natural Attractions of Moray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cairngorms National Park: A stunning national park with amazing outdoor activities, like snowboarding, skiing, cycling, and hiking.
  • Lossiemouth East Beach: A gorgeous beach with white sands and clear waters.
  • Ben Aigen: A beautiful hilltop that offers panoramic views and is great for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moray

Moray offers a beautiful landscape and a variety of trails for hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes:

  • The Speyside Way: A 65-mile trail that follows the River Spey, ideal for hiking and cycling.
  • Burghead to Hopeman Coastal Walk: A 5-mile walk that offers beautiful views of the coast and fishing villages.

Best Things to Do in Moray: Activities and Tours

From exploring history to enjoying the great outdoors, Moray has plenty of activities to offer visitors:

  • Dolphin Watching Tour: Get close to these gentle giants of the sea with a tour that takes you on a boat ride to see dolphins.
  • Whisky Tasting Tour: Visit the Balvennie Distillery to learn how whisky is made and enjoy a tasting.
  • Cultural Tours: Learn about Moray’s rich history and heritage with a guided cultural tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moray

Here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in Moray:

Day 1 Arrival at Elgin
Arrive in Elgin and explore the town center. Visit the Elgin Museum and Elgin Cathedral. Enjoy some local food and drinks in the evening.  
Day 2 Outdoor Adventure in Cairngorms National Park
A full day of adventure in Cairngorms National Park. Try snowboarding, skiing, hiking, and cycling in the beautiful national park.  
Day 3 Exploring the Coastline
Enjoy a scenic drive along the coast and visit some lovely coastal villages like Burghead, Hopeman, and Lossiemouth. Take a walk along the beach and enjoy a local seafood lunch.  
Day 4 Castle and Distillery Tour
Visit the Spynie Palace and the Benromach Distillery to learn about the history of the region and enjoy some whisky.  
Day 5 Dolphin Watching Tour
Spend a morning on dolphin watching tour and enjoy the beauty of Moray Firth. Afternoon can be spent exploring some of the nearby fishing villages.  
Day 6 Loch Ness Day Trip
Take a day trip to Loch Ness to enjoy the beautiful scenery and learn about the famous Nessie the Loch Ness Monster.  
Day 7 Departure Day
It’s time to say goodbye to Moray and head back home, but not before soaking up a little more of Scotland’s rich history and culture.  

Food and Drink Scene of Moray

Moray is known for its traditional Scottish meals and local produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: A savory pudding made with sheep’s offal, oats, and spices.
  • Cranachan: A dessert that consists of fresh raspberries, cream, whisky, honey, and oatmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Moray is known for its fine malt whiskies, with many distilleries located in the region.
  • Craft Beer: Moray has several craft breweries that produce unique, locally brewed beers, like Windswept Brewing Co. and Spey Valley Brewery.

Moray has plenty of locally made products that make great souvenirs to bring back home. Some popular choices include:

  • Shortbread and oatcakes

Local Culture and Customs of Moray

The people of Moray are known for their friendly nature and love for their rich history and heritage. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. Scottish Gaelic is still spoken in some areas, and it’s polite to use some basic Gaelic greetings.

Best Time to Visit Moray

Moray can be enjoyed year-round with a variety of activities for all seasons. However, the best time to visit Moray is from May to September when the weather is mild, sunny and warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moray

Do:

  • Explore the beautiful countryside
  • Try the local whisky and beer
  • Respect the local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Ignore the safety guidelines when taking outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Moray

Scotland is a safe country, although usual precautions need to be taken. Visitors should be careful while taking outdoor activities, as weather conditions can change quickly and without warning. It’s important to dress appropriately and carry essential gear.

Health in Moray

There are no major health concerns for travelers to Moray. Visitors should always carry enough medication with them. It’s important to carry sunscreen and insect repellent with you, especially during summer.
