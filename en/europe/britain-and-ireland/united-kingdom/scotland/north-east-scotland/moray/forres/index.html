Introduction to Forres

Forres is a charming town located in the Moray region of North East Scotland. Surrounded by natural beauty and steeped in history and culture, Forres has something to offer to everyone. From historical landmarks and museums to outdoor adventures and scenic views, this town is a must-visit destination in Scotland.

List of Places to Visit in Forres

Here are some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss when visiting Forres:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Falconer Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and heritage of the town and the surrounding area. There are displays showcasing different aspects of Moray’s rich cultural and social history.

  • Sueno’s Stone: This 9th-century Pictish standing stone is over 20 feet tall and covered in intricate carvings that tell the story of early Scottish life. It is one of the largest and best-preserved Pictish stones in Scotland.

  • Dallas Dhu Distillery: This historic distillery was built in 1898 and is a great place to learn about the history of whisky production in Scotland. Guided tours are available, which includes a tasting of the award-winning whiskies produced here.

Natural Attractions of Forres: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cluny Hill: Cluny Hill is a beautiful park located in the center of Forres. It offers spectacular views of the surrounding area and has a network of trails that are perfect for hiking or biking.

  • Benromach Distillery & Visitor Centre: Benromach Distillery is located a short distance from Forres. It offers guided tours and tasting sessions, showcasing the history and production processes of Scotch whisky.

  • Findhorn Beach: The stunning beach is a must-visit place in Forres. Enjoy the beautiful sand, stroll along the shore or make sandcastles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forres

Forres has a variety of trails that are perfect for both hiking and biking. Here are some popular options:

  • The Dava Way: This 38-mile route is perfect for anyone looking for a long-distance hike or bike ride. It goes through some of the most beautiful and remote areas of the Scottish Highlands.

  • The Moray Way: This trail is a circular route that goes through the heart of Moray. It passes through Forres and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, mountains, and valleys.

  • The Cluny Hill Trail: This trail is a favorite of both locals and visitors. The 1.4-mile loop offers stunning views of Forres and the surrounding area from the top of Cluny Hill.

Best Things to Do in Forres: Activities and Tours

To make the most of your time in Forres, try out some of the following activities and tours:

  • Visit Culbin Forest: Take a bike ride through Culbin Forest, and see the beautiful trees and wildlife.

  • Take a guided tour of the Findhorn Foundation Ecovillage, which is located in the town of Findhorn, just a short walk from Forres. It is a spiritual community that is committed to creating a sustainable way of life.

  • Visit the Karenza Glass Studio: Located in Forres, Karenza Glass Studio offers fascinating classes and workshops in the art of glassblowing while experimenting with glass faceting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Forres

Here’s an itinerary tailored for a week-long stay in Forres:

Day 1: Falconer Museum, Mosset Tavern, Nelson’s Tower, and Findhorn Bay.

Day 2: A day trip to the stunning village of Findhorn. Try beach strolling, have lunch and explore the village.

Day 3: Tour Benromach Distillery, visit Forres Golf Club, and take a walk at the beautiful Roseisle Beach.

Day 4: Go walking at Cluny Hill, stop by Sueno’s Stone, and visit Brodie Castle.

Day 5: Culbin Forest bike riding, visit Dallas Dhu Distillery, and have dinner at the Red Lion Inn again.

Day 6: Take a walk around the Trails of Cluny’s Old Pictish Fort and see the Cluny Hill Tower before heading back to the hotel or to the closest bar.

Day 7: Take a tour of the Findhorn Foundation Ecovillage to learn about the community’s sustainable way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of Forres

Forres has a vibrant food and drink scene that is sure to satisfy any taste. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A traditional fish soup that is made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.

  • Haggis: This Scottish classic is a must-try dish. It is made from sheep’s organs, spice, and oatmeal.

  • Aberdeen Angus Beef: Aberdeen Angus is a popular breed of beef in Scotland, and you can get a taste of it at many restaurants and cafes in Forres.

Local Beverages

  • Scotch Whisky: Scotland is famous for its whisky, and Forres is no exception. There are many distilleries in the area that offer tastings and tours, including Benromach Distillery, Dallas Dhu Distillery, and Glen Grant Distillery.

  • Cairngorm Brewery: Located in Aviemore, Cairngorm Brewery produces a variety of ales and lagers, which are sold in bars and restaurants throughout Forres, Scotland.

Where to Stay in Forres: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Forres offers a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Ramnee Hotel: A luxury hotel located in a historic mansion in central Forres, Scotland.

  • Carlton Hotel: A modern hotel located on the outskirts of Forres, Scotland.

  • Ness Bank Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Forres, Scotland.

What to Buy in Forres: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some popular souvenirs and locally made products to bring home:

  • Tartan: The traditional Scottish fabric can be found in many shops throughout Forres.

  • Shortbread: This classic Scottish biscuit is a must-try while in Forres. You will find it on bakeries all over the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Forres

Forres has a rich cultural history that has developed over the years. Here are some things to keep in mind about the local customs and culture:

  • Scots Gaelic is still spoken in some areas of Scotland, including Moray.

  • Kilts, bagpipes, and the Loch Ness Monster are some of Scotland’s most well-known cultural symbols.

  • Scots are known for their hospitality, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with locals while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Forres

The best time to visit Forres is between May and September when the weather is warmer and less rainy. However, even in the colder months, there are plenty of outdoor activities and scenic views to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forres

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Forres:

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages.

  • Explore the outdoors and appreciate the natural beauty of the area.

  • Engage with locals and learn about the area’s rich cultural history.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside someone’s house.

  • Refer to a kilt as a skirt.

  • Make fun of the traditional Scottish cuisine.

Safety and Health

Visitors should be aware of the following safety and health concerns while in Forres:

Safety in Forres

Forres is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate.

Health in Forres

Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to avoid accidents and injuries. They must have proper medical insurance to cover them in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Forres: Transportation Options and Logistics

Forres is located approximately 30 miles east of Inverness. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around Forres:

Airports Near Forres

The Inverness airport (INV) is about 20 miles from Forres.

Bus Stations Near Forres

There are good bus connections from Inverness to Forres or else from Forres bus station.

Other Options

Renting a car is a good option for those who want to explore the area on their own terms.

How to Get Around Forres

Forres is a small town, and most of the popular attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors can rent a car or a bike to explore the surrounding areas. There is a local bus service going through Forres.

Where to Go Next from Forres: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting after exploring Forres:

  • Visit the historic city of Inverness, located just 30 miles west of Forres.

  • Explore the stunning Cairngorms National Park, which is less than an hour’s drive away.

-Head to Moray Firth for dolphin watching and scenic boat trips.

Conclusion

Forres is an ideal destination for those who enjoy nature, history, and culture. With its many museums and historical sites, outdoor activities, and stunning scenery, Forres is a must-see destination in Scotland. Its welcoming locals, delicious cuisine, and cozy accommodations make it a perfect place to explore.
