Introduction to Forfar

Nestled in the county of Angus, in North East Scotland, Forfar is a charming town that offers a glimpse into Scotland’s rich history and culture. From the picturesque countryside landscapes to the bustling town center, Forfar has something to offer for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Forfar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Meffan Museum and Art Gallery: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Forfar and showcases a wide range of art exhibits and artifacts from different periods.
  • Glamis Castle: A must-visit for history buffs and castle fans alike, Glamis Castle is one of Scotland’s most iconic sites and a favorite of the British Royal Family.
  • Angus Folk Museum: Located in a beautifully kept traditional courtyard area, the Angus Folk Museum offers insights into the daily lives of the people who once lived in Angus through the ages.

Natural Attractions of Forfar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loch of Forfar: Perfect for a picnic or a leisurely walk, the Loch of Forfar is a beautiful body of water that is home to many species of birds and wildlife.
  • Reekie Linn: Located in a nearby valley, Reekie Linn is a stunning waterfall that is surrounded by a lush forest, offering picturesque views and opportunities for hiking and exploring.
  • Forfarshire Beaches: For those looking for a bit of seaside relaxation, the beaches in and near Forfar offer a range of activities such as surfing, swimming, boating, or just lounging on the sand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forfar

  • The Forfar Loch Country Park Trails: The Forfar Loch Country Park trails offer an easy, picturesque walk around the Loch. With a range of paths to choose from and plenty of opportunities for wildlife-spotting, this is a great option for families with children or beginner hikers.
  • Mount Keen: For the more adventurous, Mount Keen offers a challenging hike with stunning views from the top. With a distance of around 18 kilometers and an ascent of over 900 meters, this hike requires some preparation but is worth it for experienced hikers.
  • Glen Isla: Located in the nearby Cairngorms National Park, Glen Isla offers a range of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels, surrounded by breathtaking mountain scenery and colorful forests.

Best Things to Do in Forfar: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a local whiskey Distillery: Scotland is famous for whiskey, and there are several distilleries located near Forfar that offer tours, tastings, and a chance to learn about the whiskey-making process.
  • Go fishing in the Angus Glens: With a range of rivers and lochs, Angus Glens offers some of the best salmon and trout fishing in Scotland, and guided fishing tours are available for all skill levels.
  • Explore the Angus Coastal Route: With stunning views of the North Sea, the Angus Coastal Route stretches along the coastline and offers a range of activities such as hiking, cycling, and scenic drives.

Food and Drink Scene of Forfar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arbroath Smokies: A traditional Scottish dish, Arbroath Smokies are smoked haddock that are locally sourced and prepared in a traditional wood-fired oven.
  • Cullen Skink: Another traditional Scottish dish, Cullen Skink is a thick soup made from smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Scottish oatcakes: Often served alongside cheese or smoked salmon, Scottish oatcakes are a staple of Scottish cuisine, made from oats and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey: As previously mentioned, Scotland is famous for its whiskey, and Forfar has several distilleries in the surrounding area that offer tastings and tours.
  • Beer: Several craft breweries have recently opened in Angus, offering a range of locally brewed beers.
  • Gin: Gin distilleries have also recently opened in Angus, offering a range of artisanal gins made with local botanicals and flavors.

Where to Stay in Forfar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Strathmore Arms: This traditional Scottish inn is located in the town center and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Lochview Bed and Breakfast: Located near Forfar Loch, this cozy bed and breakfast offers a peaceful retreat and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Lodge at Drumwhill: A luxurious option, the Lodge at Drumwhill is a 19th-century mansion that has been converted into a stylish guest house offering luxury suites and a private chef.

What to Buy in Forfar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Smoked Salmon: A specialty of the region, locally smoked salmon is a perfect souvenir to bring home and enjoy.
  • Woolens and Tartans: Scotland is known for its woolens and tartans, and Forfar has several shops that sell locally made scarves, hats, and kilts.
  • Scottish Whiskey: For those looking to bring home a taste of Scotland, locally produced whiskey is a great option, with several distilleries offering tours and tastings.

Local Culture and Customs of Forfar

  • Scots Gaelic: Although English is widely spoken, Forfar is part of an area where Scottish Gaelic is still spoken, along with its ancient culture and heritage.
  • Tartan: Each Scottish clan has its own unique tartan design and colors, representing their history and ancestors.
  • Kilts: Kilts are a traditional garment worn by Scottish men for formal occasions and special events.

Best Time to Visit Forfar

The best time to visit Forfar depends on your personal preferences and the activities you plan on doing. For those looking to enjoy the outdoors, spring and summer offer warmer temperatures and longer daylight hours. Autumn is ideal for those interested in autumn foliage and seasonal activities such as apple picking and harvest festivals. For those interested in winter sports and snow activities, winter is best, with snowboarding and skiing areas within easy reach.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forfar

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and whiskey.
  • Enjoy the beautiful scenery and explore the outdoors.
  • Respect the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Drive on the left side of the road unless you are used to it.
  • Disturb the wildlife or leave litter on hiking trails or outdoor areas.
  • Make fun of the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forfar

Forfar is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables. The surrounding countryside can be unpredictable, so hikers and outdoor enthusiasts should plan ahead and take precautions such as bringing proper gear, maps, and water supplies.

Health in Forfar

The town has a hospital and medical clinics, but visitors are advised to bring their prescriptions and necessary medications.

Getting to Forfar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Forfar

  • Dundee Airport: Located about 16 km away from Forfar, Dundee Airport is the nearest airport to Forfar and offers connections to several UK cities.
  • Edinburgh Airport: Located about 85 km away from Forfar, Edinburgh Airport is Scotland’s busiest airport and offers regular connections to other UK and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Forfar

  • Forfar Bus Station is located in the town center and offers connections to several other Scottish towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Trains: The nearest train station to Forfar is Dundee Train Station, offering connections to other parts of Scotland and England.
  • Car rentals: Car rentals are available from both Dundee Airport and Edinburgh Airport.

How to Get Around Forfar

Forfar is easily walkable and bikeable, with several bike rental shops available in the town center. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Forfar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St Andrews: Known for its university and golf courses, St Andrews is just a short drive away.
  • Aberdeen: One of Scotland’s largest cities, Aberdeen offers a range of cultural attractions and is located about an hour’s drive away.
  • Cairngorms National Park: Located nearby, Cairngorms National Park offers stunning natural landscapes, hiking trails, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

From its rich history and culture to its stunning countryside landscapes, Forfar offers a unique and unforgettable experience for travelers of all types. With its range of activities, attractions, and natural wonders, Forfar is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Scotland.
