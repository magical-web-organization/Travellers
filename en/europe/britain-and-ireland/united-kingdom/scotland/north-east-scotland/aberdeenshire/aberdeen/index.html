Introduction to Aberdeen

Aberdeen is a coastal city located in Aberdeenshire, in the North East of Scotland. With a population of over 200,000 people, it is Scotland’s third most populous city. Known as the “Granite City” due to its beautiful buildings made from locally sourced granite, Aberdeen is a city that is steeped in rich history, culture, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Aberdeen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aberdeen is home to many historically and culturally significant sites. Such as:

  • Aberdeen Maritime Museum: A museum dedicated to the city’s rich maritime history, with exhibits on shipbuilding, fishing, and shipping.
  • The Tolbooth Museum: Located in the historic Tolbooth building, it provides visitors with an insight into the criminal justice system in Aberdeen during the 17th and 18th centuries.
  • Provost Skene’s House: A 16th-century townhouse that has been restored to its former glory and is now a museum dedicated to illustrating the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Aberdeen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aberdeen is known for its stunning natural attractions. Some of them are:

  • Dunnottar Castle: A ruined fortress that dates back to the medieval period and sits on a clifftop overlooking the North Sea.
  • Crathes Castle: A 16th-century castle set in sprawling estate gardens and surrounded by scenic woodland.
  • The Cairngorms National Park: The largest national park in the British Isles known for its stunning landscapes, snow-capped mountains, and rich wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aberdeen

Aberdeen has numerous walking and biking trails. Some of the best and most popular trails include:

  • The Deeside Way: A 66 km long walking trail that follows the route of the old Royal Deeside Railway.
  • Bennachie: A range of hills just outside of the city that provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Formartine and Buchan Way: A 64 km long walking and cycling trail that runs from Dyce to Peterhead.

Best Things to Do in Aberdeen: Activities and Tours

Aberdeen has an abundance of activities and tours for both tourists and locals to enjoy. Some popular ones are:

  • Aberdeen Beach: A sandy beach with plenty of attractions, including an amusement park, a golf course, a leisure center, and many restaurants.
  • Dolphin watching: The Moray Firth is home to a resident population of bottlenose dolphins, and visitors can take a guided tour to see them in the wild.
  • Glen Garioch Distillery: A historic distillery that produces traditional Scottish whisky. Visitors can take a tour to see how it is made and sample different whiskies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aberdeen

Day 1: Visit the Maritime Museum and walk along the beach. Day 2: Take a tour of Dunnottar Castle and the surrounding area. Day 3: Explore the Cairngorms National Park and go on a guided hike. Day 4: Visit the historic Tolbooth Museum and Provost Skene’s House. Day 5: Go dolphin watching in the Moray Firth. Day 6: Take a trip to Glen Garioch and enjoy whisky tasting. Day 7: Visit Crathes Castle and its surrounding estate gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Aberdeen

Must-Try Dishes

Aberdeen is known for its fresh seafood, and there are several dishes that visitors must try, such as:

  • Cullen skink: A thick and hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made with sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with onions and spices, and served with neeps and tatties (turnips and potatoes).
  • Arbroath Smokie: A smoked haddock dish typically served for breakfast.

Local Beverages

Scotch whisky is, of course, the drink of choice in Aberdeen. However, the city also has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries, such as BrewDog and Six Degrees North, producing world-class beers.

Where to Stay in Aberdeen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aberdeen offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to stay in Aberdeen are:

  • The Marcliffe Hotel and Spa: A luxurious five-star hotel located in Aberdeen’s affluent West End, offering a range of spa treatments.
  • The Spires Serviced Apartments: Modern and spacious serviced apartments located in the heart of the city.
  • The Globe Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in Aberdeen’s bustling Merchant Quarter, offering hearty breakfasts and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Aberdeen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, some things you should consider buying include:

  • Cashmere: Scotland is famous for its high-quality cashmere, and several shops in Aberdeen stock it.
  • Scottish whisky: As mentioned earlier, Scotland is famous for its whisky, and several distilleries operate in Aberdeenshire.
  • Local seafood: Aberdeen’s location on the coast means fresh seafood is abundant and makes a great gift for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Aberdeen

The people of Aberdeen are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Visitors should take care to respect the local customs and traditions, including:

  • Language: Scots and Doric, a regional dialect of Scots, are recognized regional languages in Scotland and are still spoken by some Aberdonians.
  • Religion: Scotland is a predominantly Christian country, but visitors should be respectful of all faiths and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Aberdeen

The best time to visit Aberdeen is between May and September, when the weather is at its best, and many outdoor activities and festivals take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aberdeen

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially fresh seafood dishes.
  • Visit the Aberdeen Maritime Museum to learn about the city’s maritime history.
  • Take a guided hike in the Cairngorms National Park.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the Scottish weather: pack and dress appropriately for rain and colder temperatures.
  • Assume everyone speaks English: learn a few basic words in Scots or Doric as a sign of respect.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aberdeen

Aberdeen is generally a safe city, but visitors should take standard precautions to safeguard their belongings and safety, such as being vigilant around crowded areas, avoiding walking alone at night, and being aware in the city center.

Health in Aberdeen

The UK has a universal healthcare system, the National Health Service (NHS). Visitors from European countries can avail of services with their EHIC cards while those from non-EU countries will need travel insurance.

Getting to Aberdeen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aberdeen

Aberdeen International Airport is the major airport serving the city, located 7 km northwest of the city center, and is well-connected to intra-UK, and international locations.

Bus Stations Near Aberdeen

Aberdeen Bus Station is located in the city center, providing frequent services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Aberdeen is also accessible by train, with several direct trains connecting to the rest of Scotland and the UK.

How to Get Around in Aberdeen

Aberdeen has an extensive public transport network, including buses and trains, and is well-walkable. Taxis and Ubers are also available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Aberdeen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore after visiting Aberdeen, such as:

  • Edinburgh: The Scottish capital, is a must-visit, located 128 miles south of Aberdeen, and can be reached via train, bus, or car.
  • Inverness: A historic city located north of Aberdeen, serving up magnificent architecture and plenty of old-world charm.
  • Orkney Islands: Located north of the mainland, the Orkney Islands offer a unique experience characterized by a rich Viking history, unique wildlife, and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Aberdeen has something to offer everyone, whether it’s history, culture, natural beauty, food, or adventure. With this ultimate guide, visitors will be able to make the most out of their trip to Aberdeen and explore all the amazing things the city has to offer.
