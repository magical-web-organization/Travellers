Introduction to Banchory

Banchory is a charming town located in the Aberdeenshire region of Scotland, known for its picturesque countryside, outdoor adventures, and rich history. Situated along the River Dee, Banchory offers stunning views of the Scottish landscape and a wide range of activities for visitors to enjoy.

Whether you’re interested in exploring historical sites, hiking, biking, or simply taking in the local culture, Banchory has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, places to stay, and local customs to help you plan your trip to Banchory.

List of Places to Visit in Banchory

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Banchory Museum: The Banchory Museum offers a glimpse into the town’s past with exhibits on local history, geology, and industry.
  • Crathes Castle: This 16th-century castle is a beautiful example of Scottish baronial architecture. Visitors can tour the castle and its gardens, which include over 200 varieties of roses.
  • Drum Castle: Drum Castle is another stunning historic castle near Banchory. The castle features a medieval tower, Jacobean mansion, and beautifully manicured gardens.
  • Tolquhon Castle: Tolquhon Castle is a well-preserved 16th-century tower house located about 10 miles from Banchory. Visitors can explore the castle’s extensive grounds and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Banchory: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scolty Hill: Scolty Hill is a popular hiking destination near Banchory. The hill offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is an excellent spot for a picnic or to watch the sunset.
  • Cairngorms National Park: The Cairngorms National Park is the largest national park in the UK and is located just over an hour’s drive from Banchory. Visitors can hike, bike, or go wildlife spotting in this stunning wilderness area.
  • Deeside Way: The Deeside Way is a beautiful 66-mile pathway that runs from Aberdeen to Ballater, passing through Banchory along the way. The path follows the River Dee and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Falls of Feugh: The Falls of Feugh are a beautiful waterfall located just a few miles south of Banchory. Visitors can watch salmon jumping the falls in the autumn months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banchory

  • Scolty Hill Trail: The Scolty Hill Trail is a 3-mile loop that takes hikers to the top of Scolty Hill, offering stunning views of Banchory and the surrounding countryside.
  • Banchory to Ballater Trail: This 40-mile trail follows the Deeside Way from Banchory to Ballater, passing through some of the most beautiful scenery in the region.
  • Bennachie Hill: Bennachie Hill is a popular hiking destination near Banchory, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The hill has several well-marked trails ranging in difficulty from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Banchory: Activities and Tours

  • Mountain biking: There are several excellent mountain biking trails near Banchory, including the Durris Forest and the Pitfichie Forest.
  • Fishing: The River Dee is a popular spot for salmon fishing, and several local outfitters offer guided tours.
  • Distillery Tours: Banchory is located near several Scotch whisky distilleries, including Glenfiddich and Glenlivet, offering visitors a chance to sample some of Scotland’s finest spirits.
  • Horse Riding: Several stables near Banchory offer horse riding tours through the local countryside, including the beautiful Glen Tanar estate.

Food and Drink Scene of Banchory

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen skink: a Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish, made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, served with neeps and tatties (turnips and mashed potatoes).
  • Cullen Bay seafood: Banchory is located close to the coast and offers some of the freshest seafood in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Scotch whisky: Scotland is known for its excellent whisky, and several distilleries are located near Banchory.
  • Local craft beer: several breweries in the Aberdeenshire region offer delicious craft beers.

Where to Stay in Banchory: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Banchory Lodge: A luxury hotel located on the banks of the River Dee.
  • Feughside Inn: A cozy guesthouse located near the Falls of Feugh.
  • Douneside House: A stunning country house hotel located near Tarland, about 12 miles from Banchory.

What to Buy in Banchory: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Scottish tartan: Banchory is located near several Scottish shops offering a range of tartan products.
  • Local honey: Aberdeenshire is known for its delicious honey, and several local shops offer jars for purchase.
  • Scotch whisky: Purchase a bottle of Scotland’s finest whisky from a nearby distillery as a souvenir of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Banchory

  • Banchory is located in Scotland and is steeped in Scottish culture and traditions.
  • The Scottish people are known for their hospitality, and visitors to Banchory can expect a warm welcome from locals.
  • The Scottish language, Gaelic, is spoken by a small percentage of the population in Scotland.

Best Time to Visit Banchory

The best time to visit Banchory is between April and October when the weather is mild and dry. However, the town is beautiful year-round, and each season offers something unique to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banchory

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including haggis and Cullen skink.
  • Take in the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside.
  • Visit local distilleries and sample some of Scotland’s best whiskies.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing, as the Scottish weather can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t miss out on a chance to hike Bennachie Hill.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banchory

Banchory is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any safety issues. However, as with any destination, visitors should exercise basic caution and take care of their belongings.

Health in Banchory

There are several medical clinics and hospitals located in and around Banchory. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling to Scotland.

Getting to Banchory: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Banchory

  • Aberdeen International Airport (ABZ): located about 20 miles from Banchory.

Bus Stations Near Banchory

  • Aberdeen Bus Station: buses run regularly between Aberdeen and Banchory.
  • Stagecoach Bluebird: runs several bus routes through Banchory and the surrounding area.

Other Options

  • Car rental: several car rental companies operate in Aberdeen, offering visitors a chance to explore the surrounding region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Banchory

Banchory is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and buses are also available for visitors who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Banchory: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aberdeen: Scotland’s third-largest city is located just a short drive from Banchory and offers a range of attractions, including museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Cairngorms National Park: Located just over an hour’s drive from Banchory, the Cairngorms National Park offers some of the most stunning scenery in Scotland, as well as opportunities for hiking, biking, and wildlife spotting.

Conclusion

Banchory is a beautiful town located in the heart of Aberdeenshire, offering a range of attractions, outdoor adventures, and local culture. Visitors to Banchory can explore historical sites, hike in the surrounding countryside, and sample some of Scotland’s finest whiskies. Whatever your interests, Banchory is a destination not to be missed.
