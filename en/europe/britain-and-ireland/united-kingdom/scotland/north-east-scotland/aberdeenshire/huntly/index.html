Introduction to Huntly

Nestled in the heart of Aberdeenshire, Huntly is a charming town that boasts a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery. Home to numerous historical landmarks, long hiking trails, and picturesque countryside, it is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic Scottish experience. In this travel guide, we will highlight the best places to visit in Huntly, explore the local food and drink scene, provide tips on where to stay and what to buy, and offer practical advice on safety and transportation.

List of Places to Visit in Huntly

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit cultural sites in Huntly is the Huntly Castle, a 12th century ruin that has been restored to its former glory. Here you can explore the castle’s rich history and architecture, and enjoy unparalleled views of the surrounding countryside. Another cultural gem is the Leith Hall and Gardens, a stunning Edwardian country house that is reminiscent of Scotland’s aristocratic past. The venue also has lovely gardens that are perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Natural Attractions of Huntly: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perched on the River Deveron, Huntly is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty. Duff House Country Gallery is a magnificent mansion that exhibits works of art and sculptures by popular artists. Another gem is the Bin Forest trails, with miles of forest paths ideal for hiking and mountain biking. If you are an angler, explore River Deveron where anglers find ripe opportunities to trout and salmon fish on their trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntly

Huntly’s hiking and biking routes are a nature lover’s delight! For an adventurous day, walk the Deveron Way Path Trail, a 15-mile trail that takes you through scenic countryside and small towns. The Bin Forest trails are also ideal for mountain bikers, while the Huntly Nordic Ski Centre offers cross-country skiing opportunities in winter and spring.

Best Things to Do in Huntly: Activities and Tours

Are you looking for an adventurous day out? Huntly has you covered! Visitors can enjoy a plethora of activities, including archery, horseback riding, and clay pigeon shooting. You can explore the whisky-making process and discover the secrets of how they are made on guided tours of local distilleries. For families with children, the Graemeshall Loch is packed with flora and fauna and you can spot ducks, rabbits, badgers, and foxes here.

Food and Drink Scene of Huntly

Must-Try Dishes

Scotland is known for its rich culinary heritage, and Huntly has plenty of unique dishes to offer. For a delicious traditional Scottish breakfast, head to the Huntly Cafe. Visitors can also try local Scottish produce like haggis, Cullen skink, and black pudding. For a sweet treat, don’t miss the famous Scottish shortbread and traditional oatcakes.

Local Beverages

Whisky is one of Scotland’s most celebrated exports, and Huntly is no exception. You can try a variety of whiskies at local distilleries or enjoy a refreshing beer at the local pub. Aberdeenshire is also known for its cider, so don’t miss the opportunity to sample a few glasses of locally-made cider.

Where to Stay in Huntly: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation in Huntly ranges from romantic Bed and Breakfasts to affordable guesthouses. If you are looking for luxury, the historic Fife Arms Hotel is the perfect place to stay. Budget-friendly options include Lickleyhead Castle and Knockendarroch House Hotel, located near the town center. Cottage and self-catering rental options abound for families that want to enjoy a home away from home.

What to Buy in Huntly: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huntly is famous for its locally-made sauces, jams, and cheese. You can also find beautiful knitwear, pottery, and handmade jewelry in the town’s many gift shops. For a unique souvenir, consider buying a locally-produced whisky or cider, or a piece of local art to hang on your walls at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntly

Scotland has a rich cultural history, and Huntly is an exemplary representation of that heritage. Visitors can explore the town’s museums and historical landmarks while enjoying traditional Scottish food and drink. Scots have a rich storytelling and musical heritage, so it is not uncommon to stumble upon a musical gathering in a pub or local bar.

Best Time to Visit Huntly

The best time to visit Huntly depends on what you’re looking for. If you’re looking for a summer break, the months of June through August provide long daylight hours and beautiful outdoor weather. If you prefer snow and crisp winter walks, the winter months can offer a different kind of visit. Note that the weather is fairly unpredictable in Scotland, so be prepared for rain even in the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huntly

Do:

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for hiking or other outdoor activities.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local dishes and whiskey.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces or natural areas.
  • Speak too loudly or act disrespectfully in public spaces.
  • Underestimate the weather’s unpredictability.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huntly

Huntly is a safe town, with low violent crime rates. However, visitors should still take basic precautions, like keeping valuable items out of sight and locking up their doors.

Health in Huntly

Huntly has excellent health care facilities and there are local hospitals that can handle minor and major medical emergencies. That said, travelers are advised to ensure they have the appropriate health insurance before their visit.

Getting to Huntly: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huntly

The nearest airport to Huntly is Aberdeen International Airport, which has rail link access to Huntly. Visitors can also take a local taxi or bus to reach Huntly.

Bus Stations Near Huntly

Visitors arriving by bus can use Citylink, First Group, or Stagecoach buses to reach the Huntly bus station.

Other Options

Train services are available for visitors coming from other parts of the UK. Visitors arriving by car can use the M90 and M9 motorways, which connect to the Highland Highway and lead to Huntly.

How to Get Around in Huntly

Huntly is a relatively small town, and it is easy to explore on foot. Visitors can also use local buses or hire taxis to get around. Alternatively, rent a car to explore the countryside at your pace.

Where to Go Next from Huntly: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Huntly is well-connected to many other towns and cities in Aberdeenshire, so there are many options for day trips and excursions. You can choose to explore the nearby village of Rhynie for a taste of rural life, or head to Aberdeen for a day trip filled with cultural attractions and modern amenities. Further afield, visitors can take a trip to the Highlands or Edinburgh to explore Scottish history, heritage, and culture.

Conclusion

Huntly is a hidden gem in Aberdeenshire, offering a perfect blend of nature, history, and culture. With so much to explore, from scenic trails to outdoor adventure activities, Huntly is a must-visit destination for all travelers. With this comprehensive guide, you have all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to this charming Scottish town.
