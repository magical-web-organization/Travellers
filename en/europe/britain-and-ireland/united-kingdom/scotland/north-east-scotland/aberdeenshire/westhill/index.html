Westhill is a charming town situated in Aberdeenshire, North East Scotland, just a short distance away from the vibrant city of Aberdeen. With its beautiful natural surroundings, rich cultural heritage, and plethora of outdoor activities, Westhill has something to offer for every type of traveler. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a longer stay, our comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your time in this delightful town.

Introduction to Westhill

Nestled in the heart of Aberdeenshire, Westhill is a small but welcoming town known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities. The town is surrounded by rolling hills and picturesque forests, making it a perfect destination for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts. Westhill also boasts a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s fascinating past. Whether you’re looking for adventure or simply want to relax and unwind, Westhill is the perfect destination for your next holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Westhill

Westhill has a rich variety of attractions that cater to all interests, including a vast array of stunning museums, historical landmarks, and outdoor activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • the Gordon Highlanders Museum - one of the best regimental museums in Scotland, it offers an extensive collection of artifacts, including uniforms, weapons, and memorabilia, showcasing the history of the Gordon Highlanders regiment.
  • Aberdeen Maritime Museum - a must-visit site that tells the story of Aberdeen’s maritime heritage.
  • Provost Skene’s House - a 17th-century townhouse that is among the few remaining examples of the city’s architecture from that period.
  • St. Machar’s Cathedral - a beautiful church built in the 12th century with a fascinating history and impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Westhill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Countesswells Forest - a magnificent woodland on the edge of the town where visitors can enjoy miles of hiking and biking trails.
  • Duthie Park Winter Gardens - a stunning glasshouse that houses a range of exotic plants, including cacti, orchids, and tropical flowers.
  • Crathes Castle and Gardens - a grand castle with turrets and towers surrounded by beautiful gardens.
  • Balmoral Castle - the royal family’s Scottish residence, located about an hour’s drive from Westhill, offering breathtaking views of the Scottish highlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westhill

Westhill and its surroundings offer a myriad of hiking and biking routes, ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes and forest trails. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Deeside Way Walk/Cycle Path - a 41-mile path that follows the old Deeside railway from Aberdeen to Banchory.
  • Tyrebagger Hill - a prominent hill on the outskirts of Westhill boasting stunning views of the surrounding countryside, including Bennachie and the Cairngorm Mountains.
  • Scolty Hill - a challenging hike up a hilltop near Banchory, offering incredible views of the River Dee and surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Westhill: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities and tours in Westhill that cater to all interests, ranging from nature lovers to history buffs. Below are just a few things to do in this charming town:

  • Go on a Whisky Trail Tour - visit local distilleries in the area and taste the traditional Scottish drink.
  • Take a guided tour of the city - led by local guides, you’ll get to experience Westhill through the eyes of a local, discovering secret spots, hidden treasures, and insider tips.
  • Take a coastal walk - From Westhill, one can embark on a coastal walk and visit a multitude of stunning landmarks, including Castle Fraser, the Royal Lochnagar distillery, and many more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westhill

Day 1 - Arrival, explore Westhill, and visit Provost Skene’s House Day 2 - Hike the Tyrebagger Hill and explore the Westhill Golf Club Day 3 - Discover the Gordon Highlanders Museum and Duthie Park Winter Gardens Day 4 - Take a day trip to Balmoral Castle and Gardens Day 5 - Visit the Aberdeen Maritime Museum and explore Aberdeen city center Day 6 - Experience the Crathes Castle and Gardens, followed by a distillery tour Day 7 - Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Westhill

Scotland is famous for its food and drink scene, and Westhill is no exception. With an array of traditional pubs, cafes, and restaurants specializing in local cuisine, visitors are spoilt for choice.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haggis - Scotland’s national dish made of sheep’s offal and oatmeal, traditionally served with turnips and potatoes.
  • Cullen Skink - a hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Arbroath Smokie - a smoked haddock, which is perfect for lovers of seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky - Scotland’s traditional drink, which is available throughout Westhill in abundance.
  • Cider - The area surrounding Westhill is famous for its cider-making traditions, and visitors can try ciders such as the Thistly Cross Cider.

Where to Stay in Westhill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is no shortage of accommodation options in Westhill to suit all budgets and requirements. Here are some of the popular options in the area:

  • Courtyard by Marriott Aberdeen Westhill - a stylish hotel featuring modern facilities and comfortable rooms.
  • Fairways Inn of Westhill - a budget-friendly option offering clean and cozy rooms.
  • The Douneside House - a luxury guesthouse located in the heart of Aberdeenshire, offering excellent Scottish cuisine and comfortable rooms with stunning views.

What to Buy in Westhill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Westhill is known for its unique products and locally-made gifts that make for perfect souvenirs. Here are some suggestions:

  • Scottish Tartan Scarfs- Available in abundance throughout Westhill and the surrounding area, these make great souvenirs to bring back home.
  • The Dundee Cake - Originating from Dundee, this Scottish fruitcake is a must-try delicacy that visitors can bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Westhill

Westhill is a vibrant town with a strong local culture and heritage. Visitors can explore this through the town’s traditional festivals and events throughout the year, including the Aberdeen International Youth Festival, which takes place every July and offers a showcase of traditional Scottish music, dance, and art.

Best Time to Visit Westhill

The best time to visit Westhill is during the summer months (June-August), where the weather is pleasant and most outdoor activities are accessible. Winter months are cold and offer fewer opportunities for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westhill

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the traditional Scottish dishes and drinks.
  • Enjoy the outdoor activities that Westhill has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural surroundings or public places.
  • Ignore the safety guidelines while embarking on outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westhill

Westhill is a safe place for visitors, but it’s essential to keep in mind the following:

  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions when embarking on outdoor activities.
  • Take safety precautions seriously when hiking/biking in the area.

Health in Westhill

It’s essential to take precautions to ensure a healthy and safe trip to Westhill.

  • Make sure to pack appropriate clothing for the weather conditions.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities.

Getting to Westhill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Westhill by air, rail, or road.

Airports Near Westhill

  • Aberdeen International Airport, which is located 12 miles east of Westhill.

Bus Stations Near Westhill

  • Westhill is well-connected with hourly buses from Aberdeen’s central bus station.

Other Options

  • The town is easily accessible by car or taxi from Aberdeen City.

How to Get Around in Westhill

The easiest way to get around Westhill is by car, although there are also biking and walking routes for those seeking adventure on foot.

Where to Go Next from Westhill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aberdeen City - A vibrant and bustling city with a long history and culture.
  • Cairngorms National Park - One of Scotland’s most beautiful natural parks, with mountains, forests, and scenic views.
  • Stonehaven - A picturesque fishing village located along the coast.

Conclusion

Westhill is a hidden gem in Aberdeenshire, Scotland, offering a combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or just want to experience the Scottish way of life, Westhill has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit to this charming town.
