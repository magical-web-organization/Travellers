Comprehensive Tour Guide to Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire is a picturesque region located in the North East of Scotland, known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. The region is steeped in history and boasts an array of historical landmarks, museums, and cultural attractions. The countryside is also home to breathtaking scenery, rugged coastline, and some of the best hiking and biking routes in Scotland. This guide will take you through the best places to visit and things to do in Aberdeenshire, so you can make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire is a diverse region with plenty to see and do. There are many places to visit, from historical landmarks to scenic routes and museums.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aberdeenshire is known for its rich culture and history, and there are many museums and historical places to explore. Some highlights include:

  • Dunnottar Castle: One of the most iconic landmarks in Scotland, Dunnottar Castle is perched on a cliff overlooking the North Sea. This 15th-century fortress has a rich history and is said to have inspired Disney’s Brave.
  • Aberdonian: A museum dedicated to the city of Aberdeen and its rich maritime history. The museum contains a vast collection of artefacts, including ship models, paintings, and photographs.
  • Gordon Highlanders Museum: This museum pays tribute to the famous Scottish regiment, the Gordon Highlanders. The museum includes displays of uniforms, medals, and weapons, as well as a recreation of a typical WWI trench.

Natural Attractions of Aberdeenshire: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aberdeenshire is home to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in Scotland, showcasing rugged coastline, rolling hills, forests and lakes. It’s a perfect location for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers with numerous parks and reserves to explore.

  • Cairngorms National Park: Known for its stunning mountain scenery and abundance of wildlife, Cairngorms national park is a must-visit destination. The park offers a range of outdoor activities, including skiing, hiking, and cycling.
  • Loch Muick: This idyllic lake is located in the heart of Cairngorms National Park. It offers a scenic hiking trail that takes you through beautiful woodlands and majestic mountain peaks.
  • Stonehaven: This pretty coastal town south of Aberdeen features a picturesque harbour and scenic beach, perfect for long walks or a refreshing dip in the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and offers numerous hiking and biking routes through picturesque landscapes. Some of the best include:

  • Deeside Way: This 41-mile trail follows the River Dee from Aberdeen to Ballater and takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the region.
  • Bennachie: This iconic hill in Aberdeenshire is popular with walkers, offering spectacular views of the surrounding countryside. There are several well-marked trails for all abilities, so it is suitable for most ages and levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Aberdeenshire: Activities and Tours

Aberdeenshire has something for everyone, from adventure sports and cultural tours to wildlife watching and whiskey tasting. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Whiskey Tasting: Aberdeenshire is home to several excellent whiskey distilleries, offering guided tours and tastings.
  • Balmoral Castle: The Scottish residence of the Royal family is open to the public during the summer months. The castle grounds and gardens offer a unique insight into the Royal family’s history and the Scottish countryside.
  • Fishing: With its abundance of rivers and lochs, Aberdeenshire is an angler’s paradise. There are many opportunities for fly-fishing and salmon fishing throughout the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aberdeenshire

Here is a sample itinerary for a week exploring Aberdeenshire:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city of Aberdeen and visit the maritimes museum.
Day 2 Visit Balmoral Castle and take a tour of the grounds.
Day 3 Walk or bike the Deeside Way.

Day 5 Take a guided whiskey tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire has a rich culinary culture, with many seafood dishes and locally distilled whiskies and beers on offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish, made with sheep’s heart, liver and lungs, oatmeal, and spices.
  • Fish and Chips: A British staple and, fresh fish is a must-try while near the Scottish coastline.

Local Beverages

Aberdeenshire is known for its excellent whiskey distilleries and locally produced beers. Some of the best include:

  • Glenfiddich: One of the most famous whiskey distilleries in Scotland, Glenfiddich has been producing whiskey for over 100 years.
  • BrewDog: A craft beer brewery located in Aberdeenshire, brewing a range of unique beers.

Where to Stay in Aberdeenshire: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aberdeenshire offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guest houses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Marcliffe Hotel and Spa: A luxury hotel located on the outskirts of Aberdeen. The hotel features a spa, fitness centre, and award-winning restaurant.
  • BrewDog Doghouse: A unique hotel located adjacent to the BrewDog brewery. Each room comes equipped with a beer tap, providing guests with a selection of beers.

What to Buy in Aberdeenshire: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aberdeenshire has several locally produced goods worth taking home, including:

  • Whiskey: Aberdeenshire is home to several whiskey distilleries, making it an ideal location to buy whiskey souvenirs.
  • Tartan: A traditional Scottish material, available in multiple patterns.
  • Shortbread: A traditional Scottish biscuit made with butter, flour, and sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire has a unique and rich culture, featuring traditional Scottish customs and festivals. The following are some of the customs and events to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Burns Night: A celebration of the life and work of Scottish poet Robert Burns, commemorated with a traditional dinner and a recitation of his poetry.
  • Highland Games: A celebration of Scottish culture and heritage, featuring athletic competitions, pipe bands, and dancing.
  • Ceilidh: A traditional Scottish social gathering featuring music and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Aberdeenshire

The best time to visit Aberdeenshire is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild and the days are long.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aberdeenshire

When visiting Aberdeenshire, keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect local traditions: Aberdeenshire has a unique cultural heritage that should be respected.
  • Take in the scenery: Aberdeenshire is home to some of Scotland’s finest landscapes, so make sure to explore the countryside.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Aberdeenshire is known for its pristine landscape, so make sure to dispose of your rubbish properly.
  • Get too close to wildlife: Aberdeenshire has an abundance of wildlife, including deer and birds, so keep a safe distance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire is a generally safe location, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding dark, secluded areas at night.

Health in Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors are entitled to free emergency treatment in the UK. It’s advisable to take necessary precautions to avoid the spread of Covid-19, including wearing masks and practicing social distancing.

Getting to Aberdeenshire: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aberdeenshire is easily accessible through various transportation modes, including:

Airports Near Aberdeenshire

  • Aberdeen International Airport: This airport has daily flights from London and other cities in the UK, and some international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Aberdeenshire

  • Bus Services: Aberdeen and Aberdeenshire are well-served by reliable bus services, providing a convenient public transportation option.
  • Trains: Aberdeen is connected to other Scottish cities by train, making it an accessible destination.

Other Options

  • Driving: If you prefer to drive, Aberdeenshire is well-connected to the UK road network, with excellent motorways and scenic routes.

How to Get Around in Aberdeenshire

Aberdeenshire has a reliable public transportation system, including buses and trains. However, to fully explore the region, renting a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Aberdeenshire: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aberdeenshire offers an excellent base to explore other destinations in Scotland. Some popular options include:

  • Isle of Skye: Known for its rugged coastline, stunning natural beauty and unique landscape.
  • Edinburgh: The vibrant capital of Scotland known for its historical landmarks, culture and festivals.
  • The Scottish Highlands: A vast, mountainous region featuring some of Scotland’s most scenic landscapes and historic towns.

Conclusion

Aberdeenshire is a remarkable destination in Scotland, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. There’s something for everyone in this diverse region. Start planning your trip now and take in the sights, sounds, and flavours of Aberdeenshire.
