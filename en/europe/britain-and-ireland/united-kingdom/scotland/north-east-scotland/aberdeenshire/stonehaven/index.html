Introduction to Stonehaven

Located in Aberdeenshire in North East Scotland, Stonehaven is a charming town that boasts stunning historic landmarks, breathtaking natural attractions, and an array of outdoor activities. The quaint fishing village is a popular destination among locals and tourists alike, offering many sights and activities throughout the year.

In this guide, we will explore the best things to do and places to go when visiting Stonehaven. Whether you’re traveling with family or friends and looking for an adventure, Stonehaven has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Stonehaven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Stonehaven has a rich history that is reflected in the many historic landmarks, museums and galleries scattered throughout the city. Some of the most popular cultural attractions among tourists include:

  • Dunnottar Castle: The stunning 15th-century cliff-top castle is the most iconic landmark in Stonehaven. Make sure to visit the castle to learn about its history and enjoy the breathtaking views over the ocean.
  • Stonehaven Tolbooth Museum: The museum features exhibitions about the town’s history and showcases artifacts from the past.
  • St. Machar’s Cathedral: located in Old Aberdeen, St. Machar’s Cathedral is one of the most historic and important religious buildings in Aberdeen.

Natural Attractions of Stonehaven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

It’s impossible to visit Stonehaven without exploring the many natural attractions in the area. Here are some must-visit locations for nature enthusiasts and outdoor adventurers:

  • Stonehaven Bay: The beautiful and expansive beach is the perfect place to relax and take in the Scottish sunshine, with plenty of space for swimming and exploring.
  • Doulie Tower: Situated on top of Black Hill, the tower offers stunning panoramic views of the North Sea and the town of Stonehaven.
  • Fowlsheugh Nature Reserve: One of the largest seabird colonies in Europe, the reserve offers bird watchers a chance to see puffins, guillemots, petrels, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stonehaven

With its picturesque coastline and stunning landscapes, Stonehaven is a great location for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes and trails to explore Stonehaven:

  • Stonehaven to Dunnottar Castle: A scenic trail that begins at the picturesque Stonehaven harbor and leads to the stunning cliff-top Dunnottar Castle.
  • Figgat Bay Circuit: An easy trail suitable for hikers and walkers that offers stunning views of the Scottish coastline.
  • Stonehaven Coastal Path: A moderate difficulty trail that stretches for 4 miles along the coast and offers stunning views of the surrounding cliffs, tiny coves, and beautiful beaches.

Best Things to Do in Stonehaven: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours when visiting Stonehaven. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Horse Riding: Tour the beautiful landscapes of Stonehaven on horseback with one of the many providers in the area.
  • Sea Kayaking: Take a guided sea kayaking tour and explore the picturesque coastline and stunning rock formations of the area.
  • Whisky Distillery Tour: Scotland is famous for its high-quality whisky, and Stonehaven is no exception. Take a tour of one of the local distilleries and learn about the production process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stonehaven

Here’s a suggested itinerary for those staying in Stonehaven for a week:

Food and Drink Scene of Stonehaven

Must-Try Dishes

Stonehaven is home to a variety of traditional Scottish dishes that are unique to the area. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Cullen Skink: A thick and flavorful soup made with smoked haddock and potatoes.
  • Arbroath Smokies: A local specialty of smoked haddock that is cooked over hardwood embers.
  • Haggis: A savory pudding made with sheep organs and spices, traditionally served with neeps and tatties (turnips and potatoes).

Local Beverages

Scotland is also famous for its high-quality whiskies, and Stonehaven has a strong whisky-making tradition. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Glen Garioch: One of the oldest distilleries in Scotland, Glen Garioch produces a range of whiskies that embody the flavors of the region.
  • Stroud Whisky: A local distillery located just outside of the town offering a range of distinctive whiskies that are well worth a tasting.

Where to Stay in Stonehaven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Stonehaven has a range of options to choose from. Here are some of the top accommodations in the area:

  • The Ship Inn: A cozy and modern hotel located just steps from Stonehaven Harbor.
  • Arduthie House: A beautifully restored Georgian-style townhouse that offers a comfortable and elegant stay.
  • The Mill Inn: A charming traditional inn that offers well-appointed rooms and great pub food.

What to Buy in Stonehaven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to bring home? Here are some of the locally made products to consider:

  • Stonehaven Sea Glass: Found on the beaches in and around the town, these tiny pieces of glass have been polished and smoothed by the waves, creating beautiful and unique pieces of jewellery.
  • Traditional Scottish Kilts: Visit one of the many kiltmakers in the area and take home a piece of traditional Scottish clothing.
  • Arbroath Smokies: Take home a taste of Stonehaven with a bag of locally smoked haddock.

Local Culture and Customs of Stonehaven

Like most Scottish towns, Stonehaven has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through various traditions and customs such as Burns Night. The town also has a strong fishing tradition that is still celebrated through its annual harbor festival.

Best Time to Visit Stonehaven

Summer is the most popular time to visit Stonehaven, with long days and warm temperatures making it the perfect time for outdoor activities. Spring and Autumn can also be great times to visit with fewer crowds and mild temperatures, while winter can be beautiful but chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stonehaven

Do:

  • Do pack comfortable hiking shoes and outdoor clothing.
  • Do try the local Scottish cuisine.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter- abide the zero waste philosophy the area has adopted.
  • Don’t forget to bring a jacket, because the weather can be unpredictable in Scotland.
  • Don’t miss out on the local shops, craft fairs and flea markets for unforgettable souvenirs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stonehaven

Stonehaven is a safe and friendly town, but like any other tourist destination, visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and belongings. Take usual precautions such as locking accommodations, avoiding wandering alone at night, and being aware of crowds.

Health in Stonehaven

Stonehaven has medical facilities available in case of need. However, it is advisable to have travel insurance before your trip to cover any health emergencies that may arise.

Getting to Stonehaven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stonehaven is located about 15 miles south of Aberdeen, and visitors can travel to the town by the following:

  • Train: Frequent train connections from Edinburgh, Glasgow, and Aberdeen.
  • Car: Stonehaven is easily accessible by car from throughout Scotland.
  • Bus: Frequent bus services run from Aberdeen to Stonehaven, with the journey taking about 30 to 40 minutes.

Airports Near Stonehaven

The nearest airport is Aberdeen International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Stonehaven

Stonehaven has a bus station located in the center of the town.

Other Options

Taxis and bike rentals are some options if the transportation services listed above don’t meet your needs.

How to Get Around in Stonehaven

Stonehaven is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Bikes can also be rented.

Where to Go Next from Stonehaven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located on the northeast coast, Stonehaven is accessible to many other destinations in Scotland. Visitors can visit a nearby city or explore the surrounding area including:

  • Aberdeen: Scotland’s third-largest city with a rich history, stunning architecture, and scenic surroundings.
  • Dundee: A coastal city renowned as the birthplace of many world-class experts and scientists.
  • Castle Trail: Take a tour around the various castles of Scotland and explore the rich history of the region.

Conclusion

Visiting Stonehaven promises a great vacation. With its beauty, rich history, and outdoor activities, Stonehaven is a location that should be on anyone’s bucket list when touring Scotland. This guide has covered a variety of things to do, places to stay, food to try, and essential information required for a visit. So, pack your bags, travel to Stonehaven and make memories!
