Introduction to Eyemouth

Located in Scottish Borders, Eyemouth is a charming fishing village that offers visitors a unique mix of natural beauty, rich history, and exciting outdoor activities. This picturesque town sits on the coast of the North Sea and is known for its stunning beaches, rugged cliffs, and historic buildings. Visitors can explore local museums, hike or bike along scenic trails, indulge in delicious local cuisine, and stay in a variety of accommodations ranging from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

List of Places to Visit in Eyemouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eyemouth Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and culture of the town and its fishing industry. Visitors can explore interactive exhibits, learn about Eyemouth’s past, and marvel at vintage fishing equipment.
  • Gunsgreen House: Built in the 18th century, this beautiful mansion provides a glimpse into the town’s history. Visitors can take a guided tour of the house and learn about its former owners, smugglers, and the town’s maritime history.
  • St. Abb’s Head National Nature Reserve: This stunning nature reserve is home to a variety of wildlife and offers breathtaking views of the North Sea. Visitors can hike along scenic trails to explore the fascinating geology and wildlife of this unique region.

Natural Attractions of Eyemouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coldingham Beach: This beautiful sandy beach offers a perfect spot for swimming, relaxing, and sunbathing in the summer.
  • Eyemouth Harbour: Visitors can soak in the atmosphere of this bustling harbor and watch the fishing boats come and go.
  • St. Abbs Head: This dramatic cliff-top walk offers stunning views of the surrounding coastline. Visitors can hike along the trails and spot wildlife such as seabirds and seals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eyemouth

  • St. Abbs Head Coastal Path: This hike is a challenging 5.6-mile trek that rewards hikers with stunning views of the coastline and abundant wildlife.
  • Eyemouth to Coldingham Bay: This scenic bike ride takes visitors along the coast and through charming villages.

Best Things to Do in Eyemouth: Activities and Tours

  • Eyemouth Rib Boat Tours: Visitors can explore the North Sea and the Berwickshire coast aboard a high-speed tour boat.
  • Eyemouth Fishing Trips: Fishing enthusiasts can join a local skipper for a fishing trip to catch mackerel, cod, and other fish.
  • Eyemouth Golf Club: This traditional golf course offers stunning sea views and challenging terrain for golfers of all levels.
  • Ayton Castle: This impressive 19th-century castle is worth a visit to explore the grand architecture and grounds.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eyemouth

Day 1: Arrive in Eyemouth and explore the charming fishing village and harbor. Day 2: Hike along the St. Abbs Head Coastal Path and explore the stunning nature reserve. Day 3: Take a Rib boat tour to explore the Berwickshire coast. Day 4: Visit the local museums to learn about Eyemouth’s rich history and culture. Day 5: Relax on Coldingham Beach and swim in the North Sea. Day 6: Join a fishing trip and try your hand at catching your own dinner. Day 7: Play a round of golf at Eyemouth Golf Club before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Eyemouth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: This local delicacy features fresh crab meat mixed with breadcrumbs and spices and is served with a tangy tartar sauce.
  • Cullen Skink: This creamy soup is a classic Scottish dish made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Fish and Chips: Visitors can enjoy this classic British dish made with fresh fish caught in the North Sea.

Local Beverages

  • Belhaven Brewery: This local brewery produces a variety of award-winning beers, including Scottish Ale and St. Andrew’s Ale.

Where to Stay in Eyemouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Ship Hotel: This charming hotel features comfortable rooms, a cozy pub, and stunning sea views.
  • The Contented Sole: This cozy, family-run guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a homely atmosphere.
  • Eyemouth Leisure Park: This holiday park offers a variety of accommodations, from cozy cabins to luxurious lodges, for visitors looking for a longer stay.

What to Buy in Eyemouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Eyemouth Herring: This local delicacy is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Eyemouth Pottery: Visitors can pick up unique handcrafted pottery pieces to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Eyemouth

  • Eyemouth is a fishing village, and many locals work in the fishing industry.
  • Visitors should respect the local traditions and be mindful of the fishing boats and equipment.

Best Time to Visit Eyemouth

The best time to visit Eyemouth is during the summer months, when the weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy the beaches and outdoor activities. However, the town’s stunning scenery is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy a cozy, relaxing winter trip with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eyemouth

Do:

  • Do try the delicious local seafood and beer.
  • Do take a guided tour of the town to learn about its history and culture.
  • Do visit the local museums and attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disturb the local wildlife and respect their natural habitats.
  • Don’t litter and help keep the beaches and trails clean.
  • Don’t forget to pack warm layers, even in the summer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eyemouth

Visitors should pay attention to the signs and warnings on the beaches and cliffs, as the coastal terrain can be dangerous. Swimming and boating should only be done with caution and at designated areas.

Health in Eyemouth

Visitors should be aware of the risks of sun exposure and should use sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Eyemouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eyemouth

  • Edinburgh Airport: Located about 70 miles from Eyemouth, Edinburgh Airport is the closest major airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Eyemouth

  • Berwick-upon-Tweed Bus Station: This bus station is located about 8 miles from Eyemouth and offers regular bus services to the town.

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Eyemouth from nearby cities such as Edinburgh or Newcastle-upon-Tyne.

How to Get Around in Eyemouth

  • Walking: Most attractions and accommodations are within walking distance in the town center.
  • Biking: Visitors can rent bicycles to explore the surrounding areas.
  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car to explore the nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Eyemouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Edinburgh: Visitors can take a day trip to the Scottish capital to explore its rich history and culture.
  • Berwick-upon-Tweed: This charming town located about 8 miles from Eyemouth offers a variety of history, shopping, and dining options.

Conclusion

Eyemouth is a charming town that offers visitors a unique mix of natural beauty, rich history, and exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore local museums, or indulge in delicious local cuisine, Eyemouth has something for everyone. So, plan your trip to Eyemouth and discover the beauty of this stunning Scottish Borders village.
