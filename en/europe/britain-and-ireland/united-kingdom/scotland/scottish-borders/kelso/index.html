Introduction to Kelso

Located in the Scottish Borders region of Scotland, Kelso is a small market town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. Originally a settlement for monks in the 12th century, it has since grown to become a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. Surrounded by rolling hills and picturesque countryside, Kelso offers visitors an abundance of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Kelso

Kelso is home to a variety of attractions and activities that are sure to keep visitors busy and entertained. Below are some of the must-visit places in Kelso:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kelso Abbey: Visit the ruins of the 12th century Kelso Abbey, a site of great historical significance.
  • Floors Castle: One of Scotland’s largest inhabited castles, Floors Castle is open to the public and offers stunning views of the River Tweed.
  • Kelso Museum: Learn about Kelso’s rich history and culture at the local museum, which houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Kelso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Borders Abbeys Way: This long-distance trail takes you through the Scottish Borders, passing by Kelso and other historic abbeys.
  • Smailholm Tower: Explore this 15th century tower and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Harestanes Countryside Visitor Centre: A perfect place to experience the beauty of Scottish countryside, the centre offers a range of outdoor activities from horse riding to hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kelso

Kelso is surrounded by beautiful countryside that is perfect for hiking and biking. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Borders Abbeys Way: This 109 km trail takes you through the heart of the Scottish Borders, passing by historic abbeys and beautiful countryside
  • The Kelso Circular Walk: A 7 km walk around Kelso, offering stunning views of the town and its surroundings
  • The Southern Upland Way: A challenging long-distance route that runs from coast-to-coast across Scotland’s Southern Uplands.

Best Things to Do in Kelso: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Kelso offers a range of activities and tours to explore the town and its surroundings. Some of the best things to do in Kelso include:

  • Fishing on the River Tweed: Kelso is known for its excellent salmon and trout fishing, so why not give it a try?
  • Kelso Races: Enjoy a day at the races at Britain’s Friendliest Racecourse.
  • Kelso Farmers’ Market: Held every fourth Saturday of the month, the market is a great place to taste local produce and meet farmers and producers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kelso

Day 1: Arrive in Kelso and visit Kelso Abbey and the local museum.

Day 2: Take a hike on The Kelso Circular Walk followed by lunch at The Cobbles Inn.

Day 3: Visit Floors Castle and explore the gardens before enjoying afternoon tea at the castle’s café.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the Scottish Borders with a stop at Smailholm Tower and Harestanes Countryside Visitor Centre.

Day 5: Go fishing on the River Tweed followed by dinner at Ednam House.

Day 6: Visit the Kelso Races and enjoy an evening of entertainment and horseracing.

Day 7: Explore the town’s shops and finish your stay with a visit to the Kelso Farmers’ Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Kelso

Kelso has a rich culinary scene, with a variety of local eateries offering delicious food and drink. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A traditional Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: A Scottish delicacy made from sheep organs, oatmeal, and spices.
  • Selkirk Bannock: A fruit bread popular in the Scottish Borders.

Local Beverages

  • Scottish Whisky: Scotland is famous for its whisky, which is made from malted barley and aged in oak barrels.
  • Real Ale: A popular drink in the UK, real ale is a type of beer that is brewed with traditional methods and doesn’t use artificial carbonation.
  • Scottish Gin: Made with local botanicals, Scottish gin is a popular choice for gin-lovers.

Where to Stay in Kelso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kelso offers a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Ednam House: A luxury hotel located on the banks of River Tweed.
  • The Cross Keys Hotel: A boutique hotel in the heart of Kelso.
  • The Black Swan: A cozy guesthouse offering comfortable rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Kelso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kelso is home to a range of local shops and artisan producers offering unique souvenirs and gifts. Some of the best products to bring home include:

  • Scottish Tweed: A locally-produced woolen fabric, perfect for warm clothing and stylish accessories.
  • Border Tartan: A traditional patterned fabric used for kilts and other Scottish clothing.
  • Border Fine Arts: Collectible figurines featuring local wildlife and countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Kelso

Like many places in Scotland, Kelso has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through music, dance, and other local traditions. Visitors to Kelso can experience this culture first-hand by attending local festivals and events, which include:

  • Kelso Folk Festival: Held in September, the festival celebrates traditional Scottish and Celtic music.
  • Kelso Civic Week: A week-long event held in July, which includes a range of activities from parades to highland dancing.
  • Kelso Ram Sales: A major annual event for sheep farmers across Scotland, the sales take place in September and attracts visitors from far and wide.

Best Time to Visit Kelso

The best time to visit Kelso is between May to September when the weather is warm and dry. However, the town is also beautiful in the winter, with festive markets and Christmas lights making it a magical place to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kelso

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Explore the countryside and visit historic sites
  • Attend local festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car
  • Disturb wildlife
  • Ignore local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Kelso

Kelso is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take normal precautions such as locking their car and keeping valuables out of sight. In case of emergency, dial 999 for assistance.

Health in Kelso

Kelso has a range of medical facilities, including a pharmacy and doctors’ surgery. Visitors to the area should also be aware of ticks, which can cause Lyme disease. It is recommended to wear long sleeves and pants when outdoors and to check for ticks after walking in wooded areas.

Getting to Kelso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kelso is conveniently located in the Scottish Borders, with good transport links to surrounding areas. Some of the best options to get to Kelso include:

Airports Near Kelso

The nearest airport to Kelso is Newcastle International Airport, which is approximately 76 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kelso

Kelso has a bus station in the town center, with regular services to Edinburgh, Galashiels, and other local towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Kelso by train, with services running to Edinburgh and other local towns.

How to Get Around in Kelso

Kelso is a small town, so it’s easy to get around on foot. However, visitors can also hire a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kelso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kelso is surrounded by beautiful countryside and nearby towns and cities that are worth a visit. Some of the best places to go next include:

  • Edinburgh: Scotland’s capital city is just an hour’s drive away from Kelso and offers a range of cultural and historic attractions.
  • Melrose: A historic town famous for its abbey and local rugby club.
  • Berwick-upon-Tweed: A historic walled town located on the England-Scotland border.

Conclusion

Kelso is a hidden gem in the Scottish Borders, offering visitors an abundance of natural beauty and cultural attractions. From hiking and biking to exploring historic sites and enjoying delicious food and drink, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Start planning your trip today and discover all that Kelso has to offer!
