Introduction to Selkirk

Nestled in the picturesque Scottish Borders, Selkirk is a charming town situated in the south-east corner of Scotland. It is surrounded by rolling hills and beautiful forests, which make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. In this comprehensive travel guide to Selkirk, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, stay and more.

List of Places to Visit in Selkirk

Surrounded by natural beauty and rich in history, Selkirk has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Selkirk:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sir Walter Scott’s Courtroom: This museum is located in the heart of Selkirk and showcases the history of Selkirk, including a collection of artifacts related to Sir Walter Scott.
  • Halliwell’s House Museum: This museum is housed in a 16th-century townhouse and showcases the local history and culture of Selkirk.
  • Bowhill House and Country Estate: This country estate is located just outside of Selkirk and is home to a stunning house, beautiful gardens and a variety of walks.

Natural Attractions of Selkirk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Philiphaugh Salmon Centre and Visitor Centre: This visitor centre is located on the banks of the River Ettrick. You can get information on the local area, and go salmon spotting in the Philiphaugh Salmon Viewing Centre.
  • Yarrow Valley: The beautiful Yarrow Valley is located near Selkirk and is perfect for a scenic drive or a peaceful walk.
  • Ettrick Valley: This valley is located to the west of Selkirk and is known for its scenic walks and stunning countryside views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Selkirk

From easy walks to challenging hikes, Selkirk offers a variety of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes to explore Selkirk’s natural beauty:

  • The Three Brethren Loop: This is a circular walk around the Three Brethren hills, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Selkirk to Innerleithen Cycle Route: This is a popular cycling route that starts in Selkirk and goes through the beautiful countryside to Innerleithen.
  • Ettrick Valley Tough Circuit: This is a challenging hike through the Ettrick Valley, which offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Selkirk: Activities and Tours

Selkirk and its surrounding areas offer a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Selkirk:

  • Fishing: The River Tweed and its tributaries are famous for their salmon and trout fishing. You can book a fishing trip with a local provider.
  • Horse Riding: There are many horse riding schools and stables in the Selkirk area, which offer guided tours and lessons.
  • Golf: Selkirk Golf Club is a popular golf course that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.

Food and Drink Scene of Selkirk

Selkirk has a variety of restaurants, cafes and bars, which offer delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Selkirk:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cullen Skink: A traditional Scottish soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, and onions.
  • Haggis: A traditional Scottish dish made with sheep’s organs, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Whisky: Scotland is known for its whisky, and Selkirk has some of the best distilleries in the country.
  • Real Ale: Selkirk has a thriving local brewery scene, with a variety of real ale options to try.

Where to Stay in Selkirk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Selkirk has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Selkirk:

  • The Glen Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a bar, and a restaurant. It is located in the heart of Selkirk and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • **The Woodside: **This guesthouse is located in the picturesque Yarrow Valley and offers cosy rooms and beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Ettrick Valley Yurts: If you are looking for a unique accommodation experience, this eco-friendly glamping site is perfect for you.

What to Buy in Selkirk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Selkirk has a thriving local market scene, with a variety of products to choose from. Here are some of the local products to bring home from Selkirk:

  • Tweed: Selkirk is famous for its tweed products, including clothing, bags, and upholstery.
  • Local Whisky: If you are a fan of whisky, you can buy the local Selkirk distillery’s whisky to bring home.
  • Honey: The Selkirk area is known for its locally produced honey, which you can buy from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Selkirk

The local culture of Selkirk is deeply rooted in its history and heritage. The people of Selkirk are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature. If you are visiting Selkirk, it is important to respect the local customs and culture.

Best Time to Visit Selkirk

The best time to visit Selkirk is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Selkirk is a year-round destination, and each season offers a unique experience.

Getting to Selkirk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Selkirk is easily accessible by road, rail, and air. Here are some of the transportation options to get to Selkirk:

Airports Near Selkirk

  • Edinburgh Airport (45 miles)
  • Glasgow Airport (75 miles)

Bus Stations Near Selkirk

  • Selkirk Bus Station (Located in the town centre)

Other Options

  • The Borders Railway: The Borders Railway runs from Edinburgh to Tweedbank, which is 10 miles from Selkirk.
  • Car: Selkirk is easily accessible by road via the A7.

How to Get Around in Selkirk

To explore Selkirk and its surrounding areas, here are some transportation options:

  • Car Hire: Car hire is available in Selkirk and is the most convenient way to get around.
  • Public Transport: Local buses are available to explore the town and its nearby areas.
  • Walking: Selkirk is a small town, and walking is a great way to explore the local area.

Where to Go Next from Selkirk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Selkirk is located in the heart of the Scottish Borders and is surrounded by beautiful towns and villages. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Jedburgh: A historic town located 13 miles from Selkirk, known for its 12th-century abbey.
  • Melrose: A picturesque town located 9 miles from Selkirk, known for its stunning abbey and beautiful countryside views.
  • Edinburgh: The capital city, located 40 miles from Selkirk, is a must-visit destination for its history, culture, and food scene.
