Introduction to Galashiels

Located in the Scottish Borders of Scotland, Galashiels is a town steeped in history and natural beauty. Galashiels offers tourists a combination of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local cuisine. In this comprehensive travel guide to Galashiels, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to this enchanting town.

List of Places to Visit in Galashiels

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Galashiels is a town with a rich history, and the best way to explore it is by visiting some of its many museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • Abbotsford House: this stately home was once the residence of the famous writer Sir Walter Scott. Today, it has been turned into a museum and visitors can explore its vast collection of artefacts and memorabilia.
  • Old Gala House: this 16th-century building has been turned into a museum and art gallery, showcasing exhibitions from local artists as well as artefacts from the town’s history.
  • Halliwells House Museum: this museum takes visitors on a journey through the history of the Scottish Borders and the town of Selkirk. The museum is located in a 17th-century building and contains a vast collection of artefacts from the region’s past.

Natural Attractions of Galashiels: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Galashiels is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the Scottish Borders. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Glentress Forest: this forest is popular among hikers and mountain bikers and is home to a range of trails suitable for all levels of experience.
  • Eildon Hills: offering stunning views over the town and the surrounding countryside, hiking up the Eildon Hills is a must-try activity.
  • Tweed Valley Forest Park: this area is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride, with plenty of trails to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Galashiels

Galashiels has numerous hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  • The Southern Upland Way: this long-distance trail stretches from coast to coast across Scotland, and the Galashiels section runs through stunning scenery.
  • The Borders Abbey Way: this 68-mile trail takes hikers past several medieval abbeys and through some of the Borders’ most beautiful landscapes.
  • The Tweed Valley Railway Path: this flat, easy trail follows the Tweed River and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Galashiels: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun activities to do in Galashiels:

  • Visit the Borders Distillery: take a tour of this distillery and learn all about the production of whisky, the country’s national drink.
  • Take a walk around Old Galashiels: this historic area of the town is home to the Old Town Jail, market square, and Abbey Place.
  • Explore the Great Tapestry of Scotland: located in nearby Tweedbank, this exhibition showcases Scotland’s history and culture through a series of beautifully crafted tapestries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Galashiels

Day 1: Visit Abbotsford House, Sir Walter Scott’s former residence. Day 2: Hike up the Eildon Hills for stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside. Day 3: Explore Old Galashiels and visit the Old Town Jail, market square, and Abbey Place. Day 4: Take a tour of the Borders Distillery and learn all about the production of whisky. Day 5: Visit the Great Tapestry of Scotland in nearby Tweedbank. Day 6: Take a leisurely bike ride along the Tweed Valley Railway Path. Day 7: Spend the day exploring the natural beauty of Glentress Forest.

Food and Drink Scene of Galashiels

Must-Try Dishes

When in Galashiels, be sure to try these local dishes:

  • Cullen skink: a hearty soup made with smoked haddock, potatoes, onions, and cream.
  • Haggis: Scotland’s national dish, made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with oats and spices.
  • Venison: a type of meat that is popular in the area due to the abundance of deer in the Borders.

Local Beverages

In addition to whisky, here are some other local beverages to try:

  • Real ale: the Borders region is home to several breweries, producing delicious real ales.
  • Scottish gin: the rise in popularity of gin has led to several distilleries creating their own Scottish gin, made with locally sourced botanicals.

Where to Stay in Galashiels: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Galashiels has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets:

  • Macdonald Cardrona Hotel, Golf & Spa: a luxury hotel with stunning views over the golf course and surrounding countryside.
  • Kingsknowes Hotel: a cosy hotel with individually decorated rooms, offering a warm welcome to all guests.
  • Watson Lodge Guest House: a family-run guest house with comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Galashiels: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a unique souvenir, consider buying:

  • Tweed products: Galashiels is known for its tweed, and there are plenty of shops selling bespoke tweed products.
  • Border tartan: the Borders region has its own distinctive tartan, which makes for a great souvenir.
  • Local food and drink: bring home a bottle of Scottish gin or some locally produced real ale.

Local Culture and Customs of Galashiels

The Borders region has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take great pride in their traditions. Visitors should be mindful of the following customs:

  • Bagpipe music: the bagpipes are a traditional Scottish instrument and are often played at events and celebrations.
  • Hogmanay: the Scots celebrate New Year’s Eve in a grand fashion, with fireworks, music, and plenty of food and drink.
  • Tartan and kilts: traditional Scottish dress is still popular in the Borders, and visitors may see locals wearing kilts or tartan trousers at events.

Best Time to Visit Galashiels

The best time to visit Galashiels is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is at its warmest and driest, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, visitors should be prepared for rain at any time of year, as Scotland is known for its changeable weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Galashiels

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Galashiels:

Do:

  • Try traditional Scottish dishes and local beverages.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the town’s many outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack warm clothing and rain gear.
  • Ignore local customs or behave disrespectfully.
  • Leave litter on the streets or in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Galashiels

Galashiels is a safe town to visit, but visitors should still take basic precautions:

  • Keep valuables out of sight and locked away in your accommodation.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Respect wildlife and nature, and keep a safe distance from any dangerous animals.

Health in Galashiels

Visitors to Galashiels should ensure they have adequate health insurance, as medical care can be expensive in the United Kingdom. Additionally, visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Drink bottled water or use a filter if unsure of tap water quality.
  • Stay up to date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Galashiels: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Galashiels

The nearest airport to Galashiels is Edinburgh Airport, which is approximately 38 miles away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or public transportation into the town.

Bus Stations Near Galashiels

Galashiels has a central bus station with connections to other towns in the Borders region, as well as Edinburgh and other cities in Scotland.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Galashiels by train, with the nearest train station located in nearby Tweedbank. Additionally, visitors can rent a car and drive to Galashiels using the A7 and A72 roads.

How to Get Around in Galashiels

Visitors can explore Galashiels on foot or by bike, with several bike rental shops available in the town. Alternatively, visitors can use public transportation, with local buses connecting the town to other destinations in the Borders region.

Where to Go Next from Galashiels: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Galashiels is located in the heart of the Scottish Borders, making it a great base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Melrose: located just a few miles to the east of Galashiels, this historic town is home to the ruins of Melrose Abbey and is known for its cultural events and festivals.
  • Jedburgh: a picturesque town with a rich history, including the medieval Jedburgh Abbey and Mary Queen of Scots’ House.
  • Kielder Water & Forest Park: located just across the border in England, this area is perfect for hiking, biking, and wildlife spotting.

Conclusion

Galashiels offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. From exploring the town’s many museums and historical sites to hiking and biking through the stunning Scottish Borders, there is something for everyone in this enchanting town. Plan your trip today and experience the best of Galashiels.
