Introduction to Castletown

Centrally located in the Isle of Man, Castletown is a small town with a rich history and fascinating culture. It is the former capital of the Isle of Man and home to many architectural gems, picturesque streets, and stunning landscapes. The town has a great selection of museums, castles, and historic buildings that showcase the region’s unique heritage, making it a sought after destination. This guide will help you plan your Castletown itinerary by highlighting the best places to visit, stay, and eat.

List of Places to Visit in Castletown

No trip to Castletown is complete without visiting the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castle Rushen - Sitting majestically in the center of Castletown, this impressive fortress dates back to the 13th century and was once home to kings and lords. The Castle Rushen Museum is a treasure trove of Manx history and displays an extensive collection of 17th-century artifacts and exhibits.
  • Nautical Museum - The fully restored Old Courthouse on Castletown’s quayside houses an extensive collection of exhibits relating to the region’s maritime history.
  • Old House of Keys - Located across from Castle Rushen, the House of Keys is the former home of the Isle of Man’s parliament. Visitors can take a guided tour of the building and learn about the country’s parliamentary history.
  • Hango Hill - A short walk from the town center, Hango Hill is a small park that offers panoramic views of Castletown Bay. It was also once the site of a Viking settlement, so the park features a Viking ship replica.

Natural Attractions of Castletown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Langness Peninsula - This rugged and wild peninsula juts out into the Irish Sea, providing visitors with incredible panoramic views of the coastline. You can explore this area on foot or bike, and there’s a lighthouse and bird observatory to visit as well.
  • Scarlett Point - This scenic and secluded spot is an excellent place to catch a sunset and take in the beauty of the bay. There’s also a lighthouse and some beautiful coastal walks nearby.
  • Castletown Beach - The sandy beach offers a perfect spot for a seaside stroll, paddling, or swimming. It’s a great place to visit with your family or to escape the hustle and bustle of the town center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Castletown

While visiting Castletown, you can also get out and explore the region’s great outdoors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails to explore:

  • The Raad ny Foillan (The Way of the Gull) - A circular 100-mile walk around the Isle of Man that can be completed in sections. This trail runs along the coast of Castletown and provides stunning views of the Irish Sea.
  • The Millennium Way - The trail covers 28 miles from Ramsey to Castletown and offers breathtaking views of the Northern coast. It is a perfect choice for less experienced walkers who do not want to pursue more challenging terrain.
  • The Southern 100 Trail - This popular motorcycle and cycling route spans 37.73 miles and follows the path of the Southern 100 Races. The trail is suitable for experienced cyclists and features many scenic opportunities for those wanting to capture the local scenery.

Best Things to Do in Castletown: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling to Castletown alone or with family/friends, you’ll find plenty of activities to keep yourself engaged and entertained. Below is our list of the top things to do in Castletown:

  • Horse Racing - If visiting between May and September, you can cheer on some of the region’s thoroughbred horses at the Isle of Man’s horse racing track.
  • Golfing - The Isle of Man features several unique golf courses, and Castletown has two, The Castletown Golf Links, and King Edward Bay Golf Club, both located with stunning coastal views, and provide a challenging experience for avid golfers.
  • Walking tours - Guided walking tours are the perfect way to discover Castletown’s hidden treasures and learn more about the region’s fascinating past.
  • The Southern Show - Held annually in August, the Southern Agricultural Society hosts a great family fun event with animal shows, craft stalls, and interactive attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Castletown

As for food and drink, Castletown satisfies all the cravings of its visitors, with a fantastic selection of local, and international cuisines. You can try the following dishes, beverages, and dining establishments to get a good taste of the local flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Manx Kippers - A traditional breakfast dish made from smoked herrings
  • Queenies - Scallops served with garlic and lemon butter
  • Manx Broth - Thick broth made from leeks, potatoes, and beef.
  • Lobster - Castletown is famous for the delicious and fresh seafood, including freshly caught lobster.

Local Beverages

  • Okell’s Beer - A locally brewed beer that has been in production since 1850.
  • Manx Whiskey - A small-batch whiskey made from local ingredients
  • Traditional Barley Beer - Made from hops and malt and had been a vital part of the Castletown drinks scene since the medieval era.

Where to Eat and Drink in Castletown

  • The Swan - A friendly pub and bistro that offers excellent food and a relaxing ambiance.
  • Amigos Mexican Kitchen - A must-visit for delicious Mexican food in Castletown.
  • Filbeys Bistro - A restaurant known for its great Sunday Roast, Pizza, and Pasta.

Where to Stay in Castletown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Castletown features many accommodation options to suit all kinds of travelers. You can choose from the following hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • The George Hotel - A historic hotel located in the heart of Castletown Town Center
  • The Arches - A cozy guesthouse within walking distance of the beach and bay.
  • The Falcons Nest - A countryside retreat with panoramic views and an onsite brewery.

What to Buy in Castletown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring back souvenirs from Castletown, here are some unique, and locally made items that you can consider:

  • Manx Tartan Products - These include scarves, kilts, and other accessories and wearables featuring the Isle of Man’s distinct tartan.
  • Manx Ale and Spirits - Gift sets of the region’s best beers, spirits, and whiskies.
  • Kippers and Shellfish - If you are a food lover, you can take home Manx kippers and freshly caught shellfish from the local markets and harbor.

Local Culture and Customs of Castletown

The Manx culture has a rich and fascinating history, and Castletown is its cultural hub. The region recognizes Manx Gaelic, a language that originated in the Isle of Man in the 4th century AD, to be an official language of the country. So if you’re looking to immerse yourself in the local Manx culture, learning a few phrases in Manx Gaelic would be an excellent place to begin.

Best Time to Visit Castletown

Although Castletown is a year-round destination, the best time to visit is between June and September when the weather is mild and pleasant, and there is an abundance of festivals and events planned for tourists. If you enjoy seasonal activities, then visit during the horse racing season, which runs from May to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Castletown

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts when visiting Castletown.

Do:

  • Bring your layers - Castletown can be chilly, so pack warm clothes for the evenings and early mornings.
  • Use an Explorer Pass - This pass gives you access to all of the local attractions and can save you significant amounts.
  • Respect the culture - Learn the local customs, experience traditional food and drinks, and participate in cultural events to understand and respect the Manx culture fully.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Keep the town clean and beautiful; dispose of your rubbish properly.
  • Ignore traffic rules - Follow traffic rules when walking or driving around Castletown.
  • Disturb local wildlife - The beaches around Castletown are home to some rare species of wildlife. Keep a safe distance and respect their surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Castletown

Castletown is a safe place, though like any other town, visitors should be vigilant in crowded areas and keep valuables in a safe place. Road travel is generally safe, with great public transport options available.

Health in Castletown

Castletown is bustling with general practitioners, dentists and other medical professionals who are committed to providing the best care to all patients. Also, the town is home to a well-equipped hospital with round the clock emergency services that you can go to in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Castletown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Castletown

The Isle of Man Airport is located just outside Castletown and is served by several airlines. Upon arrival, you can catch a taxi, or try catching a public transport or hire a car from the airport to navigate around the town.

Bus Stations Near Castletown

Castletown Bus Station is situated in the middle of town and is regarded as the hub for other towns on the Isle of Man.

Other Options

You can also reach Castletown by ferry from Liverpool, Dublin, and Belfast, with a travel time of 2-3 hours depending on where you are coming from.

How to Get Around in Castletown

Walking, cycling, and public transportation are the best ways to get around Castletown. The town is small and easy to navigate, so walking or cycling is the best way to discover it intimately. There is also a reliable bus service that operates within the town and across the Isle of Man, or hiring a car or taking a taxi commissioned by the municipal government.

Where to Go Next from Castletown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Castletown, you can discover other exciting and culturally rich places on the Isle of Man. Here are some nearby destinations that you can explore:

  • Douglas - The island’s capital city, featuring a great shopping scene, bars, restaurants, and cafes.
  • Peel - A beautiful fishing town that offers an excellent escape from the hustle and bustle of Castletown.
  • Ramsey - A scenic town, offering sailing, kayaking, and windsurfing activities, set against the jaw-dropping backdrop of the Northern coast.

Conclusion

Castletown’s fascinating culture and history, charming streets, and stunning landscapes make it one of the best destinations to visit on the Isle of Man. Use this guide to plan your next trip to Castletown and explore the best things to do, places to stay, and where to eat and drink.
