Introduction to Peel

Peel, situated in Isle of Man in the British Isles, is a small town that attracts visitors with its rich history, scenic views, and cultural heritage. A popular holiday destination for sailing enthusiasts, Peel boasts sandy beaches and quaint fishing villages.

Whether you are here for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Peel will offer you a myriad of things to do and see. From exploring the stunning coastline to visiting museums and galleries, here is a comprehensive guide to Peel that will help you plan your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Peel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Peel Castle: Built in the 11th century, Peel Castle is a magnificent fortress perched atop St. Patrick’s Isle. Visit the castle to learn about its rich history and explore its well-preserved ruins.

  • House of Manannan: The House of Manannan is a fascinating museum that celebrates the island’s heritage and folklore. Explore the galleries and exhibitions and discover the stories of the Celts and Vikings who once ruled here.

  • Leece Museum: The Leece Museum is a small, but charming, museum that houses a collection of artifacts dating back to the island’s early history. Head here to learn more about Peel’s maritime past and seafaring traditions.

Natural Attractions of Peel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peel Hill: Hike up Peel Hill for panoramic views of the town, the coastline, and the Irish Sea. The trail is well-marked and takes you through wooded areas and grassy hills.

  • The Glens: The Glens is a network of valleys and waterfalls located just outside of Peel. Go hiking, birdwatching, or mountain biking along one of the many trails and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.

  • Fenella Beach: Fenella Beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing. Located in Peel Bay, this sandy beach offers clear waters and picturesque views of the castle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peel

Peel Hill is a popular hiking and mountain biking destination that offers amazing views of the town, coastline, and countryside. You can choose from several routes, ranging from gentle strolls to more challenging hikes.

If you prefer exploring off-road, check out the trails in The Glens. The area has a network of paths and tracks that are perfect for mountain biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Peel: Activities and Tours

  • Sailing: Sailing is one of the most popular activities in Peel. You can rent a boat or join a sailing tour to explore the coastline and the nearby islands.

  • Fishing: If you like fishing, Peel is a great place to cast your line. There are plenty of fishing spots here, both in the sea and in the nearby rivers and streams.

  • Walking tours: Take a guided walking tour of Peel to learn more about the town’s history, architecture, and culture. You can explore the streets and alleys of the old town, visit historic landmarks, and hear local stories and legends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peel

If you have a week in Peel, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Peel and settle into your accommodation. Take a leisurely stroll along the beach and enjoy the sunset over the Irish Sea.

  • Day 2: Visit Peel Castle and explore its ruins. Afterwards, visit the House of Manannan to learn more about the island’s history and folklore.

  • Day 3: Hike up Peel Hill for stunning views of the town and the coastline. In the afternoon, take a boat tour of the bay and the nearby islands.

  • Day 4: Explore The Glens and go hiking or mountain biking along the trails. In the evening, catch a live music performance at a local pub.

  • Day 5: Visit the Leece Museum to learn more about Peel’s maritime past. Afterwards, go fishing in one of the nearby rivers or streams.

  • Day 6: Take a guided walking tour of Peel to learn more about the town’s architecture and culture. In the afternoon, relax on Fenella Beach and go for a swim.

  • Day 7: Check out of your accommodation and depart Peel.

Food and Drink Scene of Peel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Manx Kipper: A popular local dish made from smoked herring, Manx Kipper is a breakfast favorite that is often served with boiled eggs and toast.

  • Queenie Scallops: Peel is renowned for its fresh seafood, and Queenie Scallops, a locally sourced delicacy, is a must-try dish. Served with a dash of lemon juice, these scallops are cooked to perfection and simply melt in your mouth.

  • Bonnag: A traditional Manx bread made from flour, sugar, milk, and butter, bonnag is a sweet and wholesome treat that is often served with butter or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Okell’s Brewery: Founded in 1850, Okell’s is the oldest brewery on the island and produces a range of handcrafted beers and ales. Take a tour of the brewery and sample some of their finest brews.

  • Manx Whiskey: The Isle of Man has its own whiskey distillery, and Manx whiskey is gaining popularity worldwide. Visit the distillery and sip on some of the island’s finest whiskey.

Where to Stay in Peel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Peel Campsite: If you enjoy camping, Peel Campsite is a great option. Located in a peaceful, rural setting, the campsite offers basic facilities and is just a short walk from the town center.

  • The Arches: The Arches is a cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Peel. The rooms are well-appointed and comfortable, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

  • The George Hotel: The George Hotel is a historic hotel located in the old town of Peel. The rooms are spacious and elegantly furnished, and the hotel has a bar and restaurant on site.

What to Buy in Peel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Manx Tartan: Manx Tartan is a traditional plaid that is unique to the Isle of Man. You can buy Manx Tartan scarves, kilts, and other clothing items.

  • Manx Knitwear: The Isle of Man has a long tradition of producing high-quality knitwear, and you can buy jumpers, sweaters, and other woolen items that are made locally.

  • Manx Whiskey: Bring home a bottle of Manx whiskey as a souvenir or a gift for your friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Peel

The people of Peel are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a strong sense of community and tradition. The locals are proud of their maritime heritage, and fishing and sailing are an integral part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Peel

The best time to visit Peel is between May and September, when the weather is mild and the days are long. The town is busiest during the summer months, so if you prefer a quieter atmosphere, visit in May or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peel

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Be friendly and courteous to the locals.

  • Take care of the environment and leave no trace.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Behave in a disrespectful or offensive manner.

  • Ignore local laws and rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peel

Peel is a safe town with low levels of crime. However, as with any destination, be vigilant and keep your belongings safe, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Peel

The Isle of Man has a well-developed healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and medical centers located on the island. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance before you travel to Peel.

Getting to Peel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peel

The nearest airport to Peel is Isle of Man Airport, which is located in Ronaldsway, about 16 miles south of Peel. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to Peel, or rent a car.

Bus Stations Near Peel

Peel has a bus station located in the town center, and there are several bus routes that connect Peel to other parts of the island.

Other Options

You can also travel to Peel by ferry from Liverpool or Heysham. The ferry terminal is located in Douglas, the capital town of the Isle of Man, which is about 8 miles east of Peel.

How to Get Around in Peel

The best way to get around Peel is on foot or by bike. The town is small and easy to navigate, and there are several cycling routes that take you through the countryside and along the coast.

Where to Go Next from Peel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Douglas: The capital town of the Isle of Man, Douglas is a lively and bustling town with a plethora of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

  • Castletown: Located just a few miles south of Peel, Castletown is a historic town that is known for its well-preserved castle and government buildings.

  • Ramsey: Located on the northern coast of the island, Ramsey is a picturesque fishing town that is famous for its beaches and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Peel is a charming and historic town that offers a diverse range of things to see and do. With its stunning coastline, scenic trails, and rich cultural heritage, it is no wonder that Peel attracts visitors from all over the world. Whether you are here for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Peel has something for everyone.
