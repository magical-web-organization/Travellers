Introduction to Westerbork

Westerbork is a village located in the province of Drenthe, in the northern region of the Netherlands. It is known for its historical significance during World War II and its beautiful natural surroundings.

The name Westerbork is derived from “Wester” meaning “western” and “bork” meaning “settlement in the woods”. This beautifully quaint village has a lot to offer, from outdoor activities, cultural and historical landmarks, delicious local cuisine, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Westerbork

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kamp Westerbork Memorial Center - This museum tells the story of the transit camp which was used to deport Dutch Jews during World War II. It also includes a touching memorial field and a cemetery.

  2. Westerbork Synagogue - A beautiful building constructed in 1856 used by the Jewish population of Westerbork. It was later used by the Nazis during the war, but today it stands as a cultural landmark.

  3. Radio Museum - This museum showcases the fascinating history of radio and television in the Netherlands.

Natural Attractions of Westerbork: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dwingelderveld National Park - This park offers great hiking and biking trails through heathlands, forests, and bogs. It is also home to a large herd of Scottish Highland cattle and other wildlife.

  2. Westerbork Observatory - Located just outside of the village, this observatory is a great place to stargaze and learn about astronomy.

  3. Hijkerveld Local Nature Reserve - This reserve is known for its birdwatching opportunities and guided tours where you can explore the flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westerbork

  1. Drenthepad - This long-distance hiking trail covers over 300 kilometers and passes through a variety of landscapes, including heathlands, forests, and small villages.

  2. Smalbroekpad - This 13-kilometer hiking trail starts in Westerbork and takes you through beautiful natural scenery.

  3. ATB Route - This 20-kilometer mountain biking trail takes you through heathlands, forests, and sand dunes.

Best Things to Do in Westerbork: Activities and Tours

  1. Guided Tour of Kamp Westerbork - A knowledgeable guide will take you through the camp and share the stories of the people who were once imprisoned there.

  2. Bike Rental - Rent a bike and explore the picturesque surroundings of Westerbork at your own leisure.

  3. Hot Air Balloon Ride - Take in the breathtaking views of the Netherlands from above with a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Westerbork

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stamppot - A traditional Dutch dish made with mashed potatoes and vegetables, sometimes served with smoked sausage and gravy.

  2. Poffertjes - A popular Dutch dessert made with small, fluffy pancakes topped with powdered sugar and butter.

  3. Boswandeling - A creamy, tropical cocktail made with coconut and pineapple.

Local Beverages

  1. Dutch beer - Try the local brews, such as La Trappe Quadrupel or Grolsch.

  2. Jenever - A traditional Dutch spirit that is similar to gin.

Where to Stay in Westerbork: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel-Restaurant Ruyghe Venne - A cozy hotel with a great restaurant and a lovely terrace.

  2. Herberg De Witte Boerderij - This picturesque guesthouse has a beautiful garden and offers traditional Dutch breakfast.

  3. B&B Hoeve de Vredenhof - A charming bed and breakfast located in the countryside.

What to Buy in Westerbork: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Drenthe Honey - Made from local bees, this honey is a delicious and natural souvenir.

  2. Drentse Turf - A traditional local pastry made with rye flour, brown sugar, and spices.

  3. Bosbergtoren Souvenir - A model of the Westerbork Observatory’s tower.

Local Culture and Customs of Westerbork

The people of Westerbork are friendly and welcoming. They are proud of their historical and cultural heritage and enjoy sharing it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Westerbork

The best time to visit Westerbork is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and the countryside is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westerbork

Do:

  • Explore the natural surroundings of Westerbork
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Learn about the village’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Disrespect cultural landmarks or memorial sites
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings
  • Overlook the village’s rich history

Safety and Health

Safety in Westerbork

Westerbork is a safe village to visit, but it is always important to take precautions, such as locking your accommodations and being aware of your surroundings when exploring the natural surroundings.

Health in Westerbork

The Netherlands is known for its high standard of healthcare, so visitors can rest assured that they will receive quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Westerbork: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Westerbork

  • Groningen Airport Eelde - located approximately 35 kilometers from Westerbork

  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport - located approximately 160 kilometers from Westerbork

Bus Stations Near Westerbork

  • Hooghalen - located only 2 kilometers from Westerbork

  • Beilen - located approximately 8 kilometers from Westerbork

Other Options

Travel by train is also a great option with easy connections from Groningen or Amsterdam to nearby cities like Assen and Beilen.

How to Get Around in Westerbork

The best way to get around Westerbork is on foot, by bike, or by car. Bike rentals are available in the village, making it easy to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Westerbork: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Assen - A vibrant city known for its museums and cultural events.

  2. Emmen - A family-friendly city with a great zoo and amusement park.

  3. Groningen - A bustling city with a rich cultural and historical heritage.

Conclusion

Westerbork is a beautiful village with a rich history and stunning natural surroundings. From the Kamp Westerbork Memorial Center to the Dwingelderveld National Park, there is something for everyone. Whether you want to explore Dutch history or enjoy the great outdoors, Westerbork is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
