Introduction to Meppel

Meppel is a charming city located in the Drenthe province in the Northern Netherlands. It’s a hidden gem known for its picturesque canals, quaint old town, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Meppel will find a host of intriguing sights and activities, from exploring historic museums to cycling along scenic routes.

List of Places to Visit in Meppel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Drukkerij Museum Meppel

If you are a print enthusiast, don’t miss the Drukkerij Museum Meppel, which showcases the history of printing and the development of the printing press.

De Oude Kerk

For history buffs and culture seekers, De Oude Kerk is a must-visit. This impressive church dates back to the 14th century and houses a beautifully-tiled chancel and a 16th-century organ.

Meppeler Museum

Meppeler Museum offers visitors a peek into the city’s rich history, featuring exhibits such as the life of Dutch farmers, the local carnival tradition, and historic artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Meppel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Reestdal

The Reestdal natural reserve is a magnificent park offering an outstanding landscape of blooming fields, tranquil forests, and picturesque streams. Perfect for nature enthusiasts and hikers.

Woldlakebos

The Woldlakebos area is the perfect place if you’re looking for a leisurely nature walk or a cycling trip. The forested area is home to a fascinating array of wildlife and vegetation that will fascinate visitors of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meppel

Avid hikers and bikers will find an abundance of natural trails to explore in and around Meppel. The Itterbeckerweg route is a 44km-long trail around Meppel that offers breathtaking views of the city’s scenic beauty and is perfect for leisurely cycling.

Best Things to Do in Meppel: Activities and Tours

Cruise Along the Meppelerdiep

One of the best ways to experience Meppel is from the water. Take an idyllic boat cruise through the city’s beautiful canal system and marvel at the picturesque scenery.

Attend a Local Festival

Meppel is famous for its local festivals and events, such as the Bloemencorso and the Meppel City Run. These are perfect opportunities to celebrate with the local community and have a great time.

Week-Long Itinerary for Meppel

Day 1 Visit the historic De Oude Kerk
Day 2 Take a cruise along the canal system and explore the quaint downtown area.
Day 3 Visit Woldlakebos and enjoy the tranquil nature surrounding Meppel.
Day 4 Discover the Reestdal nature reserve for hiking and kayaking.
Day 5 Embrace the local culture by attending a festival or event.
Day 6 Explore Meppel’s hidden gems, such as the Museum Meppel.
Day 7 Take a leisurely cycle through the Itterbeckerweg route and take in the stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Meppel

Must-Try Dishes

Slavink

Slavink is a popular Dutch dish of minced pork rolled in bacon and then baked. Try this delicious dish at any of Meppel’s local cafes or restaurants.

Erwtensoep

Erwtensoep, or Dutch pea soup, is a hearty winter meal served in towns throughout the Netherlands. It’s a must-try for those visiting Meppel in the colder months.

Local Beverages

Jenever

Jenever is a traditional Dutch spirit similar to gin, but with a unique taste of juniper. It’s a popular drink in Meppel and can be found at any local bar.

Heineken

No trip to the Netherlands is complete without trying the world-famous Heineken beer. Sample this popular drink at any of Meppel’s bars or pubs.

Where to Stay in Meppel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Meppel offers a wide range of accommodations that cater to all budgets and tastes. If you’re looking for premium lodgings, the Best Western Hotel in the city center is an excellent choice. If, on the other hand, you’re looking for a more affordable option, consider staying at the nearby Vakantieappartementen on the outskirts of the city.

What to Buy in Meppel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Drenthe Pottery

Meppel is known for its beautiful pottery, and it’s a must-buy souvenir for any visitor. Drenthe pottery is known for its traditional designs, vivid colors, and intricate patterns.

Dutch Cheese

No trip to the Netherlands is complete without trying some of the country’s famous cheese. Visit any of the local cheese shops in Meppel to sample this delicious local product.

Local Culture and Customs of Meppel

Dutch culture is world-renowned, and Meppel is no exception. The city prides itself on its vibrant local customs, art, music, and literature. Visitors to Meppel should expect a warm and welcoming reception from the friendly locals.

Best Time to Visit Meppel

The best time to visit Meppel is during the spring and summer months, between April and September. This is when the weather is mild, and the city’s outdoor attractions are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meppel

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Spend time exploring the city’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any public property.
  • Disrespect religious or political beliefs.
  • Use drugs or engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Meppel

Meppel is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take the usual precautions and keep valuable belongings safe.

Health in Meppel

The Netherlands has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors to Meppel can expect high-quality medical treatment if needed.

Getting to Meppel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Meppel

The closest airports to Meppel are Schiphol Amsterdam Airport, Groningen Airport Eelde and Bremen Airport.

Bus Stations Near Meppel

Meppel has excellent bus connections with Amsterdam, the Hague, and other cities in the Netherlands. The bus station is conveniently located near the city center, making it easy to travel around.

Other Options

Trains from Amsterdam, Utrecht, and Groningen also offer frequent service to Meppel.

How to Get Around in Meppel

Meppel is a compact city, with most attractions within easy walking or cycling distance of each other. There are also bus and taxi services available if required.

Where to Go Next from Meppel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Giethoorn

Giethoorn, known as the “Venice of the North,” is a charming nearby village famous for its narrow canals and traditional houses. It’s only a short distance from Meppel and makes for a perfect day trip.

Dwingelderveld National Park

Dwingelderveld National Park offers visitors a magnificent natural reserve of heathlands, forests, and marshlands. Great for hiking, cycling, and horseback riding.

Conclusion

Meppel is a delightful destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. From its rich cultural heritage and picturesque canals to its stunning natural beauty and local customs, Meppel is a city that truly has something for everyone.
