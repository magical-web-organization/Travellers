If you are planning a visit to Northern Netherlands, make sure to add Hoogeveen to your itinerary. Located in the province of Drenthe, Hoogeveen is a charming city with a rich history, breathtaking nature, and a vibrant cultural scene. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through our top recommendations for places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation logistics, and nearby excursions.

Introduction to Hoogeveen

Hoogeveen is a small city with a population of around 55,000 people. Its name can be traced back to the medieval era, and over the years, it has evolved into a thriving hub for trade and industry. Nowadays, Hoogeveen is known for its friendly locals, picturesque neighborhoods, and vast green spaces. The city center of Hoogeveen is home to a variety of shops, bars, and restaurants, making it a great place to explore on foot and sample the local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Hoogeveen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Museum Hoogeveen: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the history of the city, with exhibits ranging from prehistoric times to World War II. One of the museum’s highlights is the reconstructed interior of the Peat Colony, showing how people used to live and work in the area.
  • De Tamboer: This theater and music venue in the heart of Hoogeveen has a rich cultural heritage. The Tamboer regularly hosts concerts, plays, and comedy shows, making it an excellent spot to catch some live performances.
  • Hoogeveen Mausoleum: This impressive burial monument was erected in 1822 and is considered one of the oldest buildings in Hoogeveen. The Mausoleum is located in the Old Cemetery, which is also worth exploring for its historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Hoogeveen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dwingelderveld National Park: If you are a nature lover, a visit to Dwingelderveld National Park is a must. This vast protected area consists of moorlands, grasslands, heathlands, and forests. It is home to various animal species such as deer, foxes, and numerous birds. You can opt to take a guided hike or bike through the park, or explore it on your own.
  • The VAM Mountain: This artificial hill used to be a landfill site, but it has been transformed into a recreational area for outdoor enthusiasts. The VAM Mountain is the highest point of Drenthe, and on a clear day, you can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape. You can choose to hike or bike up the hill, or take the chairlift and enjoy the scenery.
  • Hoogeveen Canal: For a more laid-back outdoor experience in Hoogeveen, take a stroll along the Hoogeveen Canal. The canal stretches several kilometers through the city and is lined with beautiful parks, bridges, and typical Dutch houses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hoogeveen

  • The Dwingeloo Heath Walk: This trail takes you through the scenic Dwingelderveld National Park and is suitable for hikers of all levels. Along the way, you will pass by various natural wonders, including the Bronze Age grave on the Dwingeloo Heath, the radio telescope at Dwingeloo Observatory, and the Kraloërheide Moorland.
  • The VAM Mountain Bike Trail: This trail around the VAM Mountain is 4 kilometers long and ideal for mountain bikers. The trail features a combination of technical singletrack, wide forest paths, and a challenging uphill section to the top of the mountain. The ride is rewarded with spectacular views over Drenthe.
  • The Hoogeveen Canal Route: This bike ride follows the Hoogeveen Canal through the city and its surroundings. The route is approximately 15 kilometers long and offers a mix of countryside, parks, and urban scenery. Along the route, you will find several terraces and cafes where you can stop for refreshments.

Best Things to Do in Hoogeveen: Activities and Tours

  • Windmill De Zwaluw: This iconic windmill in the center of Hoogeveen is a must-visit attraction. It houses a bakery where you can witness the traditional Dutch method of baking bread. You can also book a guided tour to learn more about the history and functioning of the windmill.
  • Hoogeveen Beer Walk: If you are a beer lover, you cannot miss out on the Hoogeveen Beer Walk. This self-guided tour takes you to the best bars and breweries in the city, where you can sample some of the finest local beers and snacks.
  • The Outdoor Escape Room: This unique outdoor activity takes you on a treasure hunt through the city center of Hoogeveen. You will need to solve puzzles, clues and complete challenges to find the hidden treasure. It’s a great way to explore the city and have fun with your friends or family.

Food and Drink Scene of Hoogeveen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pea soup: A classic Dutch dish, this hearty soup made with split peas, smoked sausage, and vegetables is best enjoyed on a cold winter day.
  • Stroopwafels: These thin waffles with caramel syrup filling are a must-try dessert in Hoogeveen. You can find them in many bakeries and supermarkets.
  • Bitterballen: These deep-fried snacks filled with ragout are a popular appetizer in the Netherlands. They are usually served with mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Bokbier: This dark beer is traditionally brewed in the fall and has a sweet and malty flavor. It’s a great pairing with savory dishes.
  • Jenever: This Dutch gin is made from malted grain and flavored with juniper berries. It’s usually served as a digestif.
  • Dutch coffee: This coffee is spiked with brandy or advocaat (a Dutch liqueur made from eggs, sugar, and brandy) and topped with whipped cream. It’s the perfect warming drink for the winter months.

Where to Stay in Hoogeveen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Hoogeveen: This 4-star hotel is located in the center of Hoogeveen and offers luxurious rooms with all amenities. It has a restaurant, a bar, and a fitness center.
  • Stay19: This cozy bed and breakfast is situated on the outskirts of Hoogeveen and offers comfortable rooms with a homely atmosphere. The hosts are welcoming and serve a delicious breakfast.
  • Stayokay Hostel: This budget accommodation option in Hoogeveen offers clean and comfortable dormitories and private rooms. It has a large common area, a bar, and bicycle rental.

What to Buy in Hoogeveen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dutch cheese: Gouda, Edam, and Maasdam are just some of the delicious Dutch cheeses you can find in Hoogeveen. They make great souvenirs to bring home or as gifts for your loved ones.
  • Dutch ceramics: The Netherlands is famous for its stunning ceramics, and you can find a variety of handmade pottery in Hoogeveen. From mugs to plates and vases, there is something for everyone.
  • Stroopwafels: As mentioned earlier, stroopwafels are a Dutch delicacy that makes for a tasty souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hoogeveen

The people of Hoogeveen are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness. The city has a rich cultural heritage, with various festivals and events taking place throughout the year. One such event is “Hoogeveen Pakt Uit,” which translates to “Hoogeveen goes all out.” This event takes place every summer and celebrates the city’s culture and traditions, with street performances, live music, and food stands.

It’s important to note that cycling is a popular mode of transportation in the Netherlands, so be mindful of cyclists when walking on bike paths or crossing the street. Tipping is not mandatory in the Netherlands, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small amount of change as a gesture of appreciation is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Hoogeveen

The best time to visit Hoogeveen is from late spring to early autumn (May to September), when the weather is warm and pleasant. The city comes alive during the summer months, with many outdoor festivals and events taking place. However, if you want to experience a true Dutch winter, December to February is also a great time to visit, when the canals and parks are covered in snow and there’s a cozy atmosphere all around.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hoogeveen

Do:

  • Rent a bike and explore the city and its surroundings on two wheels.
  • Try the local cuisine, including pea soup, stroopwafels, and bitterballen.
  • Visit Dwingelderveld National Park and take a guided tour to learn about the flora and fauna of the area.

Don’t:

  • Walk on bike paths or cross the street without checking for cyclists.
  • Smoke in public areas. Smoking is prohibited in many outdoor spaces in the Netherlands.
  • Forget to carry cash. While many businesses accept debit and credit cards, some smaller establishments may prefer cash payments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hoogeveen

Hoogeveen is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, as with any destination, visitors should take general safety precautions, such as avoiding dark and isolated areas at night and not leaving valuables unattended in public spaces.

Health in Hoogeveen

The Netherlands has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors to Hoogeveen can access medical services at the local hospital or pharmacy. It’s advisable to purchase travel insurance before your trip to cover any medical expenses or emergencies.

Getting to Hoogeveen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hoogeveen is easily accessible by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Hoogeveen

The nearest international airports to Hoogeveen are Schiphol Airport in Amsterdam (approximately 2 hours by train) and Eelde Airport in Groningen (approximately 45 minutes by car).

Bus Stations Near Hoogeveen

Hoogeveen has a central bus station served by regional and national bus operators like Arriva and Qbuzz.

Other Options

Hoogeveen can also be reached by car via the A28 and N37 highways or by train (NS) from major cities like Amsterdam, Groningen, and Rotterdam.

How to Get Around in Hoogeveen

Hoogeveen is a compact city that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. The city has an extensive network of bike paths, making it an excellent place to cycle. Public transportation options include buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Hoogeveen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Groningen: This vibrant university city is located approximately one hour’s drive from Hoogeveen. It’s known for its beautiful architecture, charming canals, and lively cultural scene.
  • Zwolle: This historic city is located approximately 30 minutes by train from Hoogeveen and features many well-preserved medieval buildings, art galleries, and museums.
  • Assen: The capital city of Drenthe is located approximately 30 minutes by car from Hoogeveen. Its main attraction is the Drents Museum, which houses an extensive collection of archeological artifacts and modern art.

Conclusion

Hoogeveen is a hidden gem in Northern Netherlands that offers a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. From exploring the city’s museums and historical buildings to hiking and biking through its vast green spaces, there’s something for every type of traveler. Plan your visit to Hoogeveen today and discover all that this charming city has to offer.
