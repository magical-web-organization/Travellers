Introduction to Vlieland

Vlieland is a small island in the West Frisian Islands, located in the northern part of the Netherlands. It has become a popular destination for vacationers due to its unspoiled natural beauty, pristine beaches, walking and cycling tracks, and stunning landscapes. The island is a haven for people who want to relax and unwind in the laid-back atmosphere, away from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you are a nature lover, a family with kids, a solo traveler, or a couple looking for a romantic getaway, Vlieland has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Vlieland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Vlieland has a rich cultural heritage and history, which is reflected in its numerous museums and historical landmarks. Some of the must-visit museums in Vlieland include the Tromp’s Huys Museum, which is housed in an 18th-century captain’s house and showcases the island’s maritime history, the Bunkermuseum, which features exhibitions and artifacts from the Second World War, and the Reddingsmuseum, which is dedicated to the history of the island’s lifeboats, among others.

Natural Attractions of Vlieland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vlieland is a nature lover’s paradise, and the island is known for its unspoiled landscapes, pristine beaches, and diverse wildlife. Visitors can explore Vlieland’s stunning natural attractions, including the expansive Vliehors, a vast sandbank with a length of 12 km, the idyllic West-Vlieland Beach, which is a perfect spot to relax and enjoy the sun, and the Dunes of Vlieland, which offers breathtaking views of the island. For birdwatchers, the island is a paradise as well, with a variety of birds such as swans, ducks, and owls that can be found all over the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vlieland

Vlieland is a perfect destination for anyone who loves hiking or cycling. With an extensive network of hiking and biking tracks, visitors can explore the island’s stunning landscapes and nature reserves at their own pace. Some of the popular tracks include the North Sea Trail, a 6 km walking trail that offers breathtaking views of the coastline and dunes, and the Oost-Vlieland Walking trail, a scenic route that passes through the island’s picturesque villages and beaches.

Best Things to Do in Vlieland: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and cycling, Vlieland has plenty of other activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some of the best things to do on the island include horseback riding on the beach, visiting the iconic lighthouse of Vlieland, taking a seal watching tour, and exploring the island’s charming villages. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of water sports, including surfing, kiteboarding, and sailing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vlieland

Day 1 - Explore the village of Oost-Vlieland: Visit the lighthouse, Tromp’s Huys Museum, and enjoy a walk along the sea.

Day 2 - Take a tour to the nature reserve of the Vliehors and enjoy a seal watching tour.

Day 3 - Explore the Dunes of Vlieland and take the North Sea Trail to the West-Vlieland Beach.

Day 4 - Enjoy a day of cycling through the island’s picturesque countryside and visit the historical landmarks and museums.

Day 5 - Take a horseback riding tour on the beach and enjoy a sunset picnic.

Day 6 - Take a bird watching tour and explore the island’s wildlife.

Day 7 - Relax and unwind in the island’s natural surroundings, enjoying the scenic views of its unspoiled landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Vlieland

Must-Try Dishes

Vlieland is famous for its seafood cuisine, and visitors should not miss the chance to try some of the island’s delicious seafood dishes. The smoked fish platter is a popular delicacy made with freshly caught fish; other must-try dishes include herring, Zeekraal – a salty seashore plant – and the Rummikub coquille, a dish made with scallops.

Local Beverages

Vlieland is home to several local breweries that produce unique, high-quality craft beers. Be sure to try the local beers at the Tromp’s Huys Brewery, which is housed in a historic building and features a cozy tasting room.

Where to Stay in Vlieland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vlieland has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style. Visitors can choose from luxurious hotels, charming guesthouses, and cozy hostels. Some of the popular hotels on the island include the Badhotel Bruin and the Hotel Zeezicht Vlieland.

What to Buy in Vlieland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Vlieland has a unique selection of souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts for family and friends. Visitors can shop for handmade pottery, jewelry made from local materials, and traditional Dutch wooden clogs.

Local Culture and Customs of Vlieland

The people of Vlieland are proud of their cultural heritage, and visitors can learn about the island’s rich history and traditions by visiting its museums, landmarks, and festivals. One of the most famous festivals on the island is the Oerol Festival, which takes place every June and features music, theater, and art performances.

Best Time to Visit Vlieland

The best time to visit Vlieland is during the summer months between June and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island is bustling with visitors. However, visitors can enjoy the island’s beauty and tranquillity year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vlieland

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment, wildlife, and local customs.
  • Try the island’s delicious seafood cuisine and local craft beers.
  • Explore the island’s natural attractions and enjoy outdoor activities.
  • Visit the island’s museums and historical landmarks.
  • Travel with cash; the island has only a few ATMs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the island’s natural environment.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Get too close to seals or birds; they are protected by law.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vlieland

Vlieland is a safe destination with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should take necessary precautions and keep their valuables secure while exploring the island.

Health in Vlieland

There are no major health risks associated with traveling to Vlieland. Visitors should be aware of any allergies they might have and carry necessary medications with them.

Getting to Vlieland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Vlieland is accessible by ferry from Harlingen, which is located about 2 hours from Amsterdam by train. Visitors can take a train from Amsterdam Central to Harlingen, from where they can take a ferry to Vlieland. There are also bus services available from Leeuwarden to Harlingen.

Airports Near Vlieland

The nearest airports to Vlieland are the Schiphol Airport in Amsterdam and the Groningen Airport Eelde, both of which are approximately 2 to 2.5 hours away from Harlingen by train.

Bus Stations Near Vlieland

Visitors can take a bus from Leeuwarden to Harlingen, from where they can take a ferry to Vlieland.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car and drive to Harlingen, from where they can take a ferry to Vlieland.

How to Get Around in Vlieland

The best way to explore Vlieland is on foot, by bike, or on horseback. Visitors can rent bikes or horses from various rental shops on the island. There are also buses available that run between the island’s villages.

Where to Go Next from Vlieland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other islands in the West Frisian Islands, including Texel, Terschelling, and Ameland. Visitors can also travel to nearby cities like Harlingen, Leeuwarden, and Groningen.

Conclusion

Vlieland is a beautiful island that offers a unique travel experience to visitors. From its stunning natural attractions to its rich cultural heritage, there is a lot to explore and discover on this island. So whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation or an outdoor adventure, Vlieland is the perfect destination for you.
