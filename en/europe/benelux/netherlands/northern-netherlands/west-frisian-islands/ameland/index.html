Introduction to Ameland

Ameland is a picturesque and serene island in the West Frisian Islands, located in the northern part of the Netherlands. Spanning over 27 square miles, this small island has something to offer for everyone, ranging from cultural attractions to natural wonders. Ameland is popular among tourists for its serene and peaceful atmosphere, stunning landscapes, and outdoor activities.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide an in-depth guide to the island of Ameland, including where to go, what to do, where to eat, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Ameland

Whether you are a nature enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Ameland has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Ameland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ameland houses several museums and historical attractions, showcasing its rich history and culture. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Ameland include:

  • Cultural Museum Sorgdrager: This museum showcases the history and culture of Ameland and houses a vast collection of artifacts, ranging from seafaring tools to paintings and sculptures.

  • Fisherman’s Cottage: This historical attraction is a typical fisherman’s cottage that showcases the traditional lifestyle of Ameland’s inhabitants in the past.

  • Nobels Nostalgisch Museum: This private museum is a treasure trove of nostalgic items, including vintage cars, bikes, and toys, and is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and antiques.

Natural Attractions of Ameland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ameland’s rugged coastline, sand dunes, and forests offer several opportunities for outdoor activities and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions and outdoor activities to explore in Ameland:

  • Wadden Sea World Heritage Site: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this beautiful landscape consists of tidal flats and wetlands that house several species of birds, seals, and other marine life.

  • Ameland Beaches: Ameland’s long sandy beaches are perfect for a day of relaxation, swimming, and sunbathing.

  • Bunker Museum: Located in the dunes of Ameland, this museum showcases the remains of World War II bunkers and is a unique and historical experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ameland

Ameland offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the island’s stunning landscape and allow visitors to explore its natural beauty up close. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes to try in Ameland:

  • Ameland Lighthouse Route: This 10-mile-long biking/hiking trail starts from the lighthouse in Hollum and takes visitors through the island’s dunes, forests, and beach.

  • Oerd Route: This scenic trail passes through the Oerd nature reserve and offers stunning views of the Wadden Sea.

  • Kwelderpad Route: This biking/hiking route passes through the Wadden Sea and showcases Ameland’s unique flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Ameland: Activities and Tours

Ameland offers several activities and tours that provide unique and memorable experiences for visitors. Here are some of the top things to do in Ameland:

  • Horse Riding: Ameland’s sandy beaches and scenic landscapes are perfect for a horseback ride.

  • Seal Watching Tour: Hop on a boat excursion to the Wadden Sea and catch a glimpse of seals basking in the sun.

  • Sunset Sailing Tour: Experience the beauty of Ameland’s coast and watch the sun go down on a sailing tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Ameland

Ameland’s food and drink scene offer a unique and delicious taste of the island’s culture and cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Ameland:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ameland Shrimps: Sweet and succulent, these local shrimps are a delicacy and are reputed to be the best in the Netherlands.

  • Ameland Lamb: Raised on the salty pastures of the island, the lamb here has a distinct and flavor-rich taste.

  • Koolraapstamppot: A traditional Dutch dish made from mashed turnips and potatoes with smoked sausage and bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Ameland Beer: This local beer is brewed with seawater from the Wadden Sea and has a distinct crisp, salty taste.

  • Willem Barentsz Gin: Named after the famous Dutch explorer, this gin is created with dune-filtered island water and botanicals and is a must-try for any gin lover.

Where to Stay in Ameland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ameland offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the top suggestions for where to stay in Ameland:

  • Hotel Ameland: A luxury hotel located in the center of Nes, offering stunning sea views and excellent amenities.

  • Stayokay Ameland: A budget-friendly hostel located in Hollum, with a cozy atmosphere and comfortable rooms.

  • De Berkenhof: A charming guesthouse located in Buren, offering comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Ameland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ameland is known for its unique and local products, ranging from delicious food items to handmade crafts. Here are some things to buy in Ameland:

  • Ameland Shrimps: Bring home some of the famous and delicious local shrimps as a tasty souvenir.

  • Blue-And-White Pottery: Ameland produces beautiful blue-and-white pottery that is handcrafted and unique.

  • Nautical Gifts: Ameland is a seafaring island, and visitors can find several nautical-themed gifts and souvenirs, such as compasses, anchors, and ship models.

Local Culture and Customs of Ameland

Ameland’s local culture and customs are deeply rooted in its seafaring history and long-standing traditions. Here are some things to know about Ameland’s local culture and customs:

  • Seafaring Culture: Ameland has a rich seafaring culture, and many locals still make their living from fishing and shipping today.

  • Language: West Frisian, a minority language, is still spoken by some of the island’s inhabitants, alongside Dutch and English.

  • Festivals and Celebrations: Ameland celebrates several festivals throughout the year, such as the Corpus Christi processions, Paal 13 beach festival, and Sinterklaas festivities.

Best Time to Visit Ameland

The best time to visit Ameland is between May and September, when the weather is mild and sunny. July and August are peak tourist months, and visitors can expect more crowded beaches and tourist attractions during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ameland

Here are some do’s and don’ts to remember when visiting Ameland:

Do:

  • Do explore the island’s natural beauty on foot or bike.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the island’s beaches or natural areas.

  • Don’t enter private properties or restricted areas without permission.

  • Don’t forget to check the weather forecast before planning outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ameland

Ameland is generally considered a very safe destination. However, visitors should be aware of certain safety precautions, such as:

  • Be careful when swimming in the sea and follow the lifeguard’s instructions.

  • Wear appropriate clothing and shoes when hiking or cycling.

  • Keep an eye out for bicycles, as they are the most popular mode of transportation on the island.

Health in Ameland

Ameland has a modern and efficient healthcare system and visitors can access medical assistance if needed. However, visitors should also take the following health precautions:

  • Protect against sunburn and use sunscreen with a high SPF.

  • Be aware of the risks of tick bites, especially when hiking or cycling in natural areas.

  • Stay hydrated and take adequate precautions against food poisoning.

Getting to Ameland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ameland is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Ameland

The nearest airport to Ameland is Groningen Airport Eelde, located about 68 miles from the island. Visitors can also arrive at Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, which is about 96 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ameland

Visitors can take a bus to Holwerd and then take a ferry to Ameland from there. Bus services are available from several major cities in the Netherlands.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive by car or train to the nearest city of Leeuwarden, and then take a bus or taxi to Holwerd.

How to Get Around in Ameland

The best way to explore Ameland is by foot or bike. Visitors can rent bikes from several rental shops on the island and explore its many bike paths and trails. Taxis and buses are also available on the island for visitors who prefer not to cycle or walk.

Where to Go Next from Ameland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby destinations and go on excursions outside of Ameland, such as:

  • Leeuwarden: A vibrant and historical city located about 20 miles from Ameland, offering excellent shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

  • Texel: The largest of the West Frisian Islands, located about 40 miles from Ameland, is another popular destination known for its sandy beaches and beautiful natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Ameland is a small island that packs a punch, offering visitors a diverse range of experiences, ranging from cultural attractions to natural wonders. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide has provided useful information and inspiration for visitors planning a trip to Ameland. With stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and plenty of things to do and see, Ameland is an excellent destination for any traveler.
