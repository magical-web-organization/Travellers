Introduction to Franeker

Franeker is a picturesque town nestled in Friesland, the northernmost province of the Netherlands. It is renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural sceneries. With its well-preserved 17th-century architecture, quaint streets, and charming canals, Franeker offers a delightful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Franeker

There is no shortage of things to see and do in Franeker. Whether you want to learn about its fascinating history, immerse yourself in its vibrant culture, or explore its breathtaking natural landscapes, Franeker has something to offer everyone. Here’s our rundown of the top places to visit in Franeker:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Franeker City Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Franeker, with exhibits ranging from ancient artifacts to modern art installations.

  • Eisinga Planetarium: Built in the 18th century, this is the oldest working planetarium in the world. It features a complex system of gears and cogs that replicate the movements of the planets and stars.

  • Martena Museum: This museum is housed in a former city hall and features exhibits on the history and culture of Franeker, including paintings, archaeological artifacts, and more.

  • City Walls and Gates: The historic city walls and gates of Franeker are well-preserved and offer a glimpse into the town’s medieval past. You can take a leisurely stroll along the walls or visit the impressive two-story city gates.

Natural Attractions of Franeker: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Franeker Courtyard Garden: This beautifully landscaped garden is located in the heart of Franeker and features fountains, sculptures, and an abundance of colorful flowers.

  • Dykes and Polders: Friesland is well-known for its extensive network of dykes and polders, which are used to control flooding. You can take a scenic bike ride or hike along these dykes and enjoy panoramic views of the flat landscape.

  • De Alde Feanen National Park: Located just a short drive from Franeker, this national park is a must-visit for nature lovers. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare birds, otters, and beavers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franeker

Franeker and its surrounding areas boast some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in the Netherlands. Here are a few of the best:

  • Middelsee Route: This 33 km cycling route takes you through the picturesque countryside of northern Friesland, passing through historic towns, quaint villages, and stunning natural landscapes.

  • Fryslân Route: This 274 km cycling route takes you through the heart of Friesland, along scenic polders, through quaint villages, and past historic landmarks.

  • Elfstedentocht Route: This 200 km skating route takes you through eleven historic towns in Friesland, including Franeker. While it is primarily used for ice skating during the winter months, it is also a popular route for cycling and hiking during the summer.

Best Things to Do in Franeker: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a leisurely activity or an adrenaline-filled adventure, Franeker has plenty to offer. Here are a few of the best things to do in Franeker:

  • Canal Tour: Take a relaxing boat tour along the canals of Franeker and enjoy a unique perspective of the town’s historic architecture and charming streets.

  • Skydive Over Friesland: For thrill-seekers, skydiving over the beautiful Friesland countryside is an unforgettable experience, with panoramic views of the flat landscape and the Wadden Sea.

  • Golfing: Franeker boasts two challenging golf courses that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Franeker

No trip to Franeker is complete without indulging in its delicious cuisine and local beverages. Here are a few of the must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frieslander: A traditional Friesland stew made with beef, turnips, carrots, onions, and local Friesland beer.

  • Stimpstamp: Mashed potatoes, vegetables, and smoked sausage all mixed together into one hearty dish.

  • Oranjekoek: A delicious Friesland cake made with almond paste and filled with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Berenburg: This is a traditional Dutch liquor made with herbs and spices like anise, juniper, and licorice. It’s a popular drink in Friesland and often served as an aperitif or digestive.

  • Frisian beer: Friesland has a rich brewing tradition, with many local breweries producing a wide variety of craft beers.

  • Boerenjongens and boerenmeisjes: These are brandy-soaked raisins and apricots, respectively. They are a popular after-dinner treat in Friesland.

Where to Stay in Franeker: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses and budget-friendly hostels, Franeker has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Franeker include:

  • Hotel Grandcafe de Doelen: This centrally located hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Hotel Restaurant de Stadsherberg: Housed in a historic building, this hotel features spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a beer garden.

  • Stayokay Hostel Franeker: This budget-friendly hostel is a great option for backpackers and offers shared dormitories and private rooms.

What to Buy in Franeker: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Franeker is well-known for its locally-produced crafts, souvenirs, and food products, all of which make great gifts or souvenirs to bring home. Some of the top items to look out for include:

  • Pottery and ceramics: Franeker has a long tradition of pottery and ceramics, with many local artisans producing beautiful, one-of-a-kind pieces.

  • Cheese and dairy products: Friesland is known for its delicious cheeses and dairy products, including Gouda, Edam, and Frisian clove cheese.

  • Liquor and beer: Berenburg and local craft beers make great souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Franeker

Franeker has a strong cultural identity, with many local traditions, customs, and celebrations. One of the most notable is the annual Frisian horse riding event, known as the PC Hooftprijs, which takes place every August and is considered one of the most prestigious equestrian events in the Netherlands.

Best Time to Visit Franeker

The best time to visit Franeker is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny, and many outdoor events and festivals take place. However, the spring and autumn months also offer mild weather and fewer crowds, making them great times to visit as well.

Getting to Franeker: Transportation Options and Logistics

Franeker is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. Some of the best ways to get to Franeker include:

  • Airports Near Franeker: Amsterdam Airport Schiphol is the nearest international airport, located about 116 km away from Franeker.

  • Bus Stations Near Franeker: Franeker has a bus station that serves several local and regional routes, with connections to nearby towns and cities.

  • Other Options: Taxis and car rentals are also available in Franeker.

How to Get Around in Franeker

Franeker is a small town, and most tourist attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer to travel by public transportation, buses and taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Franeker: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Friesland and the Netherlands, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few of the best:

  • Leeuwarden: The capital city of Friesland is just a short drive or train ride away and offers plenty of historic landmarks, contemporary art galleries, and bustling markets.

  • Harlingen: This charming port town is just a short drive or bike ride away from Franeker and boasts stunning architecture, quaint streets, and charming canals.

  • Wadden Sea: The Wadden Sea is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located just a short drive from Franeker. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and is a popular destination for hiking, cycling, and birdwatching.
