Introduction to Stavoren

Stavoren, located in Friesland, in Northern Netherlands, is a picturesque town that’s rich in history and culture. It’s one of the oldest towns in the Netherlands, dating back to the 12th century.

Stavoren has always been a center of trade and commerce, and this is reflected in its architecture, which has a mix of traditional and modern buildings. It’s also known for its stunning natural beauty, with beautiful lakes and scenic countryside surrounding the town.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at Stavoren’s attractions, including its museums and historical places, scenic views, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, activities, tours, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more. Let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Stavoren

Stavoren has plenty of attractions that cater to both history and nature enthusiasts, here are some of the best places to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Het Vikingen Informatiecentrum: The Viking Information Centre focuses on the Viking past of Stavoren. You can learn about the town’s Viking history and see artifacts from past Viking settlements in the area.
  • Stavoren Lighthouse: The 19th-century lighthouse is open to visitors and offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside. You can climb to the top of the lighthouse to get a panoramic view of the area.
  • Stavoren Museum: Located in the town center, the Stavoren Museum showcases the town’s rich history, including its past as a thriving port town.

Natural Attractions of Stavoren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • IJsselmeer: Stavoren is located on the banks of the IJsselmeer, which is the largest lake in the Netherlands. The lake is perfect for water sports enthusiasts, including sailing, kite surfing, and windsurfing.
  • Frisian Lakes: The town is surrounded by the Frisian Lakes, which offer plenty of opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating. You can choose to take a scenic boat tour and enjoy the stunning landscapes.
  • Hoge Berg: Hoge Berg is a hill that offers a magnificent view of the surrounding countryside. You can enjoy a walk or hike up the hill and discover the breathtaking views of Stavoren.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stavoren

Stavoren’s beautiful countryside is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The area has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer views and landscapes. Some of the best trails include:

  • Frisian Woodland Route: This 20-kilometer trail takes you through the beautiful Frisian woodland and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Zuiderzee Route: This 440-kilometer route takes you through coastal towns, lakes, and countryside regions in the Netherlands. You can book a bike tour or hike through this picturesque region.

Best Things to Do in Stavoren: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat tour and explore the town and the surrounding countryside from the water. You can book a tour that stops at some of the most iconic attractions in the area.
  • Watersport Activities: The IJsselmeer is ideal for watersports, especially windsurfing and kite-surfing. You can book a windsurfing lesson or kite-surfing lesson.
  • Local Markets: Go to one of Stavoren’s local markets and discover fresh produce and traditional crafts from the area. TheStavoren open-air market is held every Thursday.
  • Visit Local Wineries: Friesland is known for its wine production. Visit a local winery in Stavoren and sample some of the best wines from this region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stavoren

Here’s a week-long itinerary for those who want to experience the town’s best attractions:

  • Day 1: Explore the town center, visit the Stavoren Lighthouse, and have a traditional Dutch dinner at one of the town’s restaurants.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour and explore the IJsselmeer and the Frisian Lakes.
  • Day 3: Visit the Viking Information Centre and learn about Stavoren’s Viking history. Then hike up to Hoge Berg for a scenic view of the town and the countryside.
  • Day 4: Visit the Stavoren Museum and learn about the town’s rich history. Then go to the local wineries in the area and sample some of the best wines from this region.
  • Day 5: Take a bike tour and discover the Frisian Woodland Route.
  • Day 6: Head to the local markets and discover fresh produce and traditional crafts from the area. Then, take a windsurfing or kite-surfing lesson.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic by the IJsselmeer or the Frisian Lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of Stavoren

Stavoren’s food and drink scene has a mix of traditional Dutch cuisine and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stevige Vissoep: This hearty seafood soup is a local specialty and can be found in most restaurants in Stavoren.
  • Fries Suikerbrood: This delicious sweet bread is a must-try for those with a sweet tooth. You can have it for breakfast or as a dessert.
  • Friese Nagelkaas: This cheese has a spicy flavor, thanks to the use of cumin seeds. It’s perfect for those looking for a unique cheese experience.

Local Beverages

  • Beerenburg: Beerenburg is a traditional Dutch gin made with a mix of herbs and spices, including juniper berries, aniseed, and ginger. It’s a local specialty and can be found in most bars and restaurants in Stavoren.
  • Friesian Wines: Friesland is known for its wine production, and you should try some of the local wines while in Stavoren.

Where to Stay in Stavoren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stavoren has a good selection of accommodations that cater to a range of budgets. Here are some top options:

  • Hotel De Vrouwe van Stavoren: This hotel is located in a historic building and has a mix of traditional and modern rooms. It’s within walking distance of most attractions in Stavoren.
  • Het Groene Dakje: This cozy guesthouse is perfect for those on a budget. It’s located in the countryside, surrounded by nature.
  • Stayokay Hostel Stavoren: This hostel offers dorms and private rooms and is ideal for those looking for a budget-friendly option. It’s located near the train station and the town center.

What to Buy in Stavoren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Stavoren has a range of souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts and souvenirs. Here are some options:

  • Cheese: Buy some Friese Nagelkaas or other Dutch cheeses from a local cheese shop.
  • Traditional Crafts: The local markets in Stavoren offer a wide range of traditional crafts, including pottery, textiles, and woodwork.
  • Friesian Wines: Take home some Friesian wines from a local winery for a taste of Stavoren.

Local Culture and Customs of Stavoren

Stavoren has a strong cultural heritage, and its traditions are still celebrated today. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Friesian Language: Friesian is a distinct language spoken in Friesland, and many locals speak it alongside Dutch.
  • St. Odulphus Procession: This annual procession takes place on the second Sunday of June and celebrates the local patron saint, St. Odulphus.
  • Bolsward-Hindeloopen Skating Tour: This annual ice-skating event takes place in January and covers a distance of 42 kilometers, starting in Bolsward and ending in Hindeloopen.

Best Time to Visit Stavoren

The best time to visit Stavoren is in the summer, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also when most attractions are open, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stavoren

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Stavoren:

Do:

  • Try local wines and Beerenburg.
  • Visit the local markets and buy some traditional crafts and souvenirs.
  • Explore the IJsselmeer and the Frisian Lakes on a boat tour.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural landscapes.
  • Ignore safety regulations while engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stavoren

Stavoren is a safe destination, with low crime rates and good public safety standards. However, it’s essential to keep in mind the following:

  • Look out for bike lanes and pedestrian paths while exploring the town.
  • Wear a life jacket while engaging in water sports activities.

Health in Stavoren

Stavoren has good health standards, and the town has a good selection of medical clinics and hospitals. However, it’s essential to keep in mind the following:

  • Drink bottled water or tap water that has been boiled.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn while engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Stavoren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stavoren

The nearest airport to Stavoren is Schiphol Airport in Amsterdam, which is around a two-hour drive from Stavoren. From there, you can take a train or rent a car to get to Stavoren.

Bus Stations Near Stavoren

Stavoren has a bus station that connects the town to other cities in the Netherlands. You can take a bus from Amsterdam, Utrecht, or Rotterdam to get to Stavoren.

Other Options

You can also take a train from Amsterdam, Rotterdam, or Utrecht to Stavoren. The train station is located near the town center, within walking distance of most attractions.

How to Get Around in Stavoren

Stavoren is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. You can also rent a bike or take a boat tour to explore the town and the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Stavoren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stavoren is located near several other towns and cities in the Netherlands, making it an ideal base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Leeuwarden: Leeuwarden is the capital city of Friesland and has a rich history and culture. It’s located around 45 minutes from Stavoren by car.
  • Hindeloopen: Hindeloopen is a small town located on the banks of the IJsselmeer, known for its colorful houses and picturesque scenery. It’s located around 20 minutes from Stavoren by car.
  • Amsterdam: Amsterdam is the capital city of the Netherlands and is known for its museums, canals, and vibrant nightlife. It’s located around two hours from Stavoren by car.

Conclusion

Stavoren is a charming town with a rich history and culture, surrounded by stunning natural landscapes. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or delicious food and drink, there’s something for everyone in Stavoren.
