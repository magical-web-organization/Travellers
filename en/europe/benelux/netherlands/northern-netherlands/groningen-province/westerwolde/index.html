Introduction to Westerwolde

Westerwolde is a picturesque region in the province of Groningen, Northern Netherlands, near the German border. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The countryside is adorned with rolling hills, meandering rivers, lush forests, and quaint villages. The region has been inhabited since the prehistoric times and has a rich history that can be explored through its numerous museums, historical buildings, and landmarks. Westerwolde is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience, away from the hustle and bustle of the big cities.

List of Places to Visit in Westerwolde

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Klooster Ter Apel: The former monastery turned museum is a cultural landmark of Westerwolde. It houses an impressive collection of medieval art, artifacts, and manuscripts. The exhibitions showcase the region’s rich history and the influence of Christianity on its heritage.
  • Bourtange Fortress: It is a star-shaped fortress built in the 16th century to defend the Dutch-German border. The fort has been restored to its former glory and has become a popular tourist destination. Visit the barracks, gunpowder magazine, and the Museum of the Dutch Revolt.
  • Sellingen Windmill: The historic windmill was built in 1856 and is still operational. Learn about the traditional technique of grinding grain and wheat into flour and enjoy the scenic surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Westerwolde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Weenderveld Nature Reserve: It is a wetland area that offers a unique landscape with diverse flora and fauna. Explore the walking and biking trails, bird watching stations, and the floating island.
  • Musselkanaal and the Mussel-Aa River: The picturesque village on the banks of the river is a haven for fishing enthusiasts, hikers, and cyclists. Follow the scenic route along the river and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Wedderbergen Recreational Area: Located on the outskirts of Wedde village, it is a popular destination for outdoor activities for all ages. Take a dip in the natural swimming pool, explore the adventure park, or play a game of mini-golf.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westerwolde

  • Westerwoldepad: It is a 80-km long walking route that takes you through the stunning countryside of Westerwolde. Follow the trail markings and pass through the idyllic villages, ancient forests, and the riverside.
  • Kiek over Diek: It is a 90-km long cycling route that follows the coastal dyke of the Wadden Sea. Pass through the charming towns, historic harbors, and enjoy the scenic views of the sea and the salt marshes.
  • Routes Around Sellingen: There are several hiking and biking routes around Sellingen that offer beautiful views of the countryside and forests. Follow the routes shaped like a leaf, mushroom, or owl, and discover hidden gems along the way.

Best Things to Do in Westerwolde: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing on Westerwoldse Aa: The river Aa is a calm and tranquil waterway that is perfect for canoeing. Rent a kayak or canoe, and paddle your way through the meadows, forests, and the small villages along the river.
  • Horse Riding in Ter Borg: The horse riding school in Ter Borg offers horse riding lessons, tours, and trails through the countryside. Explore the Westerwolde on horseback and enjoy the unspoiled nature.
  • Camping in Westerwolde: The region has several camping sites that offer a unique experience of camping in the woods, by the river or the lakes. Pitch your tent, and enjoy the peace and quiet of the outdoors.

Food and Drink Scene of Westerwolde

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot: It is a traditional Dutch dish made of mashed potatoes, vegetables, and smoked sausage. The Westerwolde version is made with kale or endive.
  • Eierbal: It is a deep-fried boiled egg covered in ragout and breadcrumbs, served hot as a snack or as a side dish.
  • Runstraat Special Beer: It is a craft beer brewed locally in the region, using only natural ingredients. The beer has a unique taste and is a must-try for beer lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: It is a traditional Dutch gin made from malted grain, flavored with juniper berries, and distilled into a smooth liquor. It is usually served with a slice of orange or lemon.
  • Thee van Oordt: It is a specialty tea made from natural ingredients, such as herbs and fruits. The tea is known for its therapeutic benefits and is a popular drink in Westerwolde.

Where to Stay in Westerwolde: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel De Waalehof: The family-owned hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, a bar, and a terrace. It is located in a quiet village and is surrounded by nature.
  • Hostel & Lodge de Lutje Koele: It is a hostel and campsite that offers unique accommodation options, such as teepees, safari tents, and hammocks. It is located near the forest and is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Bed & Breakfast Westerwolde: The B&B is located in a historic farmhouse that has been renovated into an elegant and charming retreat. It offers spacious rooms, a garden, and a breakfast buffet made with fresh local ingredients.

What to Buy in Westerwolde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Westphalian Ham: It is a cured ham that is famous for its unique taste and flavor. It is made from pure-bred German pigs and is typically aged for several months to enhance the taste.
  • Blaarkop Cheese: It is a artisanal cheese made from the milk of Blaarkop cows that are raised in the region. The cheese has a rich flavor and aroma and is a perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Traditional Pottery: The region is known for its traditional pottery that is handcrafted using the age-old techniques. The pottery comes in various forms and designs and makes for a beautiful home decor item or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Westerwolde

The people of Westerwolde take pride in their heritage, and their traditions have been passed down from generation to generation. The region has a rich cultural history that can be seen in its museums, landmarks, and even in daily life. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the region has a relaxed and laid-back atmosphere. Respect the local customs, and try to immerse yourself in the culture to get the most out of your trip.

Best Time to Visit Westerwolde

Westerwolde can be visited throughout the year, and each season offers a unique experience. The summer months from June to August are the most popular time to visit, as the weather is warm, and the days are longer. The spring and autumn months are also recommended for outdoor activities and sightseeing, as the crowds are thinner, and the rates are lower. The winter can be cold and snowy, but it is a magical time to visit and experience the festive season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westerwolde

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings while hiking and biking in the forest.
  • Follow the rules and regulations of the camping sites.

  • Leave the trails while hiking or biking.
  • Ignore the safety rules while canoeing or horseback riding.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westerwolde

Westerwolde is a safe destination, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. However, it is recommended to take basic precautions while traveling, such as keeping your valuables safe, and being mindful of your surroundings. Follow the safety rules while engaging in outdoor activities, such as canoeing, horseback riding, and hiking.

Health in Westerwolde

Westerwolde has adequate medical facilities and emergency services. The tap water is safe to drink, and the food standards are high. However, it is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and to consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Westerwolde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Westerwolde is located in Northern Netherlands, near the German border. The region is easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Westerwolde

  • Eelde Airport: It is the nearest international airport and is located 35 km away from Westerwolde. The airport offers flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Westerwolde

  • Groningen Bus Station: It is the nearest bus station to Westerwolde, approximately 45 km away. Bus services are available to and from Groningen, Amsterdam, and other major cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient and comfortable way to explore the region at your own pace. There are several car rental companies at Eelde Airport and Groningen.
  • Biking or Walking: Westerwolde is an ideal destination for biking or walking enthusiasts. There are several scenic routes and trails throughout the region that cater to all levels of fitness.

How to Get Around in Westerwolde

Westerwolde is a relatively small region that can be explored easily by car, bike, or on foot. There are several bike rental shops in the region that offer high-quality bikes and helmets for rent. The region is also well-connected by a network of buses that operate between the villages and towns.

Where to Go Next from Westerwolde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Groningen: The vibrant university town of Groningen is just 45 km away from Westerwolde. It is known for its lively nightlife, cultural attractions, and historic buildings.
  • Drenthe: The neighboring province of Drenthe is known for its megalithic tombs, scenic landscapes, and picturesque villages. Explore the Drents-Friese Wold National Park or visit the Assen TT Circuit.
  • Bremen: The German city of Bremen is just a few hours’ drive from Westerwolde. It is known for its historic old town, shopping districts, and culinary delights.

Conclusion

Westerwolde is a hidden gem in Northern Netherlands that is waiting to be explored. The region offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Plan your trip to Westerwolde, and experience the peace, tranquility, and charm of this idyllic destination.
