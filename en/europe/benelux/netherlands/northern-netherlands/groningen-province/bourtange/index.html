Introduction to Bourtange

Located in the province of Groningen in Northern Netherlands, Bourtange is a small fortified village known for its rich history, beautiful countryside, and charming architecture.

Originally built in 1593 during the Eighty Years’ War, the village was strategically located on the German-Dutch border to protect the Netherlands from German invaders. Today, it remains one of the best-preserved military fortifications in Europe, attracting visitors from all around the world who come to explore its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and vibrant local scene.

In this Bourtange travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs and traditions you need to know before you go.

List of Places to Visit in Bourtange

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bourtange Museum: This charming museum houses a magnificent collection of artifacts and objects that tell the story of the village’s history, from its early origins as a military fortification to its modern-day cultural significance.
  • The Synagogue of Bourtange: This historic synagogue is one of the oldest and most important Jewish buildings in the Netherlands. Built in 1842, it was designed in the Gothic Revival style and can be visited during guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Bourtange: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Westerwolde Forest: Covering over 300 square kilometers of land, this vast forest is a great destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. It offers an array of activities such as hiking, camping, picnicking, and birdwatching.
  • Bourtanger Moor: Located just minutes from the village, Bourtanger Moor is a beautiful and peaceful nature reserve offering scenic walking trails, diverse wildlife, and breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bourtange

  • The Vestingloop: This popular hiking route takes you on a scenic tour of the village and its fortifications, offering fantastic views of the surrounding landscape. The trail is easy to follow and can be completed in just a few hours.
  • The Bourtange Cycling Tour: If you prefer to explore the countryside on two wheels, this cycling tour is perfect for you. It takes you on a 25-kilometer journey through the region’s picturesque towns and villages, offering plenty of opportunities to stop and admire the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Bourtange: Activities and Tours

  • Fortification Tour: Led by a knowledgeable guide, this fascinating tour takes you through the village’s historic fortifications, showcasing the important role that Bourtange played in the Netherlands’ military past.
  • Bourtange Village Tour: This leisurely walking tour takes you through the heart of the village, introducing you to its charming streets, buildings, and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Bourtange

Must-Try Dishes

  • Groninger Mustard Soup: A classic Dutch dish, this hearty soup is made from potatoes, carrots, leeks, and Groninger mustard, giving it a unique spicy flavor.
  • Stewed Beef with Red Cabbage: Another local favorite, this dish is a delicious marriage of slow-cooked beef and sweet, tangy red cabbage.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin-like spirit, jenever is a perfect drink to finish off a long day of sightseeing and exploring.
  • Wijnruit Bourtange Liqueur: This aromatic liqueur is made from the wijnruit plant, which is grown in the surrounding area. It has a distinctive floral flavor and is perfect for sipping on a cold winter evening.

Where to Stay in Bourtange: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Vesting Hotel Bourtange: This charming hotel is located right in the heart of the village, offering easy access to all of its attractions and amenities.
  • Hotel Doornbosch: Set in a picturesque countryside location, this cozy hotel is perfect for those looking for a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

What to Buy in Bourtange: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dutch Cheese: Holland is famous for its delicious cheese, and Bourtange has plenty of specialty shops where you can buy unique varieties to take home.
  • Wooden Shoes: Another classic Dutch souvenir, the wooden shoe is a timeless symbol of the Netherlands’ rural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Bourtange

  • The Dutch are famously reserved, so it’s important to be polite and respectful at all times. Handshaking is the most common form of greeting, and it’s customary to say “goedemorgen” (good morning), “goedemiddag” (good afternoon), or “goedenavond” (good evening) when entering a shop or restaurant.
  • When visiting someone’s home, it’s customary to bring a small gift, such as flowers or candy.

Best Time to Visit Bourtange

The best time to visit Bourtange is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the village is bustling with tourists and locals enjoying the long days. That said, the region is beautiful all year round, so visitors can enjoy Bourtange’s charms in any season!

Do’s and Don’ts of Bourtange

Do:

  • Speak softly and avoid shouting or making loud noises in public spaces.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photographs of people.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public spaces, unless you’re in a designated smoking area.
  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home, as it’s considered impolite.
  • Be late for appointments or meetings, as punctuality is highly valued in Dutch culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bourtange

Bourtange is a very safe place to visit, with low crime rates and a relaxed, peaceful atmosphere. Visitors should, however, be vigilant when exploring the countryside, as it’s easy to get lost or disoriented in the remote areas.

Health in Bourtange

Netherlands has a highly developed health care system, and visitors can feel confident that they will receive the best possible care in the event of an emergency. Visitors from EU countries can use their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) to access free or reduced-rate medical treatment.

Getting to Bourtange: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bourtange

  • Groningen Airport Eelde (GRQ): Situated just over an hour away by car, this small airport provides direct flights to major European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Bourtange

  • Bourtange Bus Station: Located just outside the village, this station offers regular bus services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bourtange by rental car or bike, with plenty of rental outlets available in the nearby cities of Groningen and Assen.

How to Get Around in Bourtange

Bourtange is a small village, and most of its attractions are located within walking distance of each other. That said, visitors who want to explore the surrounding countryside may prefer to rent a bike or car.

Where to Go Next from Bourtange: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Groningen: One of the Netherlands’ most vibrant and cosmopolitan cities, Groningen is a great destination for history, culture, and nightlife.
  • Delfzijl: A picturesque seaside town on the banks of the Ems Delta, Delfzijl is a great destination for beach lovers, seafood aficionados, and birdwatchers alike.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or simply looking for a charming place to escape the hustle and bustle of modern life, Bourtange has something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, gorgeous countryside, and welcoming local population, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone traveling in Northern Netherlands.
