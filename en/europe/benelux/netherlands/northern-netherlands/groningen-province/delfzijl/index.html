Introduction to Delfzijl

Located in the northern province of Groningen in the Netherlands, Delfzijl is a waterfront town known for its rich history and beautiful scenery. This charming town attracts tourists from all over the world with its beautiful beaches, natural reserves, and cultural landmarks.

Delfzijl is also a gateway to the famous Wadden Sea, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with a unique ecosystem and a stunning landscape that includes sand dunes, mudflats, and offshore islands.

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover or just looking for a fun and relaxing getaway, Delfzijl is a perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Delfzijl

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Delfzijl has a wealth of historic sites and museums to explore. Here are some of the top cultural attractions you should add to your itinerary:

  • Muzeeaquarium Delfzijl – This museum showcases the maritime history and wildlife of the region, with an interesting exhibit on the ecological importance of the Wadden Sea.

  • Borg Verhildersum – This well-preserved castle located just 30 minutes from Delfzijl offers a glimpse into the aristocratic life of the past and also hosts cultural events throughout the year.

  • Farm Museum Stainkant – This open-air museum takes you back in time to the agricultural way of life of the 19th century in Groningen.

Natural Attractions of Delfzijl: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The beautiful natural surroundings of Delfzijl offer unlimited opportunities for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover or just looking for some fresh air, here are the top natural attractions you can’t miss:

  • Nationaal Park Lauwersmeer – This nature reserve is an ideal spot for birdwatching, hiking, and cycling. It is home to deer, sea eagles, and many rare species of birds.

  • Eemshotel – A unique hotel located in the middle of the Eems estuary that offers stunning views of the sea and the Wadden Islands

  • Dijksterhuisbos – This forest reserve is ideal for hiking and features beautiful lakes, ancient trees, and abundant wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delfzijl

Delfzijl is a perfect destination for those who love to hike or bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Wadloopcentrum Pieterburen – This center located just 30 minutes from Delfzijl offers guided tours through the mudflats of the Wadden Sea.

  • The Molenroute – A biking tour that takes you through several historic windmills that are still in operation

  • Hondshalstermeer – A scenic bike trail that takes you around a beautiful lake, surrounded by lush greenery

Best Things to Do in Delfzijl: Activities and Tours

Delfzijl offers many fun activities and tours that cater to different interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Delfzijl:

  • Wadlopen – Exploring the mudflats of the Wadden Sea on foot is a must-do when in Delfzijl.

  • Go-karting at Kartbaan Pottendijk – This karting track offers thrilling experiences for kids and adults alike.

  • Boat Tours – See beautiful landscapes from the water on a guided boat tour. The different tour options include harbor tours, seal watching tours, and fishing trips.

Food and Drink Scene of Delfzijl

Must-Try Dishes

The Dutch cuisine has a lot of distinctive and delicious dishes to try, and Delfzijl has many options to satisfy your cravings. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Stamppot – This traditional Dutch dish is made of mashed potatoes with vegetables, such as kale or endive, and served with smoked sausage or bacon.

  • Poffertjes – This fluffy, tiny pancake is a Dutch favorite. It is served with powdered sugar and butter.

  • Hollandse Nieuwe – A traditional Dutch dish of raw herring, served with onions and pickles.

Local Beverages

In addition to its authentic cuisine, Delfzijl also offers unique beverages:

  • Jenever – Also known as Dutch gin, this liquor stands out with its botanicals and rich flavor.

  • Heineken Beer – A world-renowned beer that originated in Amsterdam.

Where to Stay in Delfzijl: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delfzijl offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget, from luxury hotels to quaint guesthouses. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Eemshotel – A unique hotel located in the middle of the Eems estuary that offers stunning views of the sea.

  • Hotel Restaurant Boven Groningen – A centrally located hotel with a restaurant that serves authentic Dutch cuisine.

  • Havenhotel – A small hotel with stunning harbor views and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Delfzijl: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Delfzijl has an impressive selection of souvenirs and unique local products that make for the perfect memento of your trip. Here is a list of the best things to buy in Delfzijl:

  • Pottery from Atelier & Galerie Kempro – This art gallery offers beautiful handmade pottery, ceramics, and tableware.

  • Dutch Cheeses – Holland is famous for its unique cheeses, such as Gouda and Edam.

  • Souvenir from the Muzeeaquarium Delfzijl Gift Shop – Pick up a unique souvenir or a piece of marine-themed art from their gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Delfzijl

Delfzijl has a rich cultural heritage that you can experience in many ways. Here are a few cultural customs to be aware of:

  • Cycling – Dutch people love to cycle, and Delfzijl is no exception. Make sure to rent a bicycle during your stay to enjoy the beautiful landscapes.

  • Language – Although English is widely spoken, Dutch is the official language of the Netherlands. Learning a few basic phrases can come in handy.

Best Time to Visit Delfzijl

Delfzijl can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. If you’re a nature lover, the best time to visit is in the spring or summer when the flowers are in full bloom, and the weather is pleasant.

If you’re not fond of the crowds, consider visiting in the winter months, when the town is less busy, and you can explore the museums and cultural attractions more leisurely.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delfzijl

  • Try the local cuisine and unique beverages of the region.
  • Follow the traffic rules while cycling in the town.

  • Speak loudly or disrespectful in public places.

Delfzijl is a safe town with low crime rates, and you can feel secure to travel to this beautiful destination.

Health in Delfzijl

The Netherlands has a high-quality health care system, and in case of emergency, you can consult the nearest medical center.

Getting to Delfzijl: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Delfzijl

The closest airport to Delfzijl is Groningen Airport Eelde, located approximately 45 minutes by car from Delfzijl.

Another option is Amsterdam Airport Schiphol, located around 2.5 hours by train or car to Delfzijl.

Bus Stations Near Delfzijl

Delfzijl has a well-connected bus station that offers transport throughout the town and to nearby destinations.

Other Options

You can also reach Delfzijl by train or car. The town is easily accessible, and you can rent a car at the airport or the train station.

How to Get Around in Delfzijl

The best way to get around in Delfzijl is by bike. The town has an excellent network of cycling paths that take you through beautiful landscapes and scenic routes.

Another option is the local bus network, which connects Delfzijl with the neighboring towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from Delfzijl: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Groningen – The vibrant university town is a perfect destination for exploring Dutch architecture, museums, and city life. It’s located just a 30-minute drive from Delfzijl.

  • Wadden Islands – The Wadden Islands are an archipelago located off the coast of the Netherlands. Accessible by ferry from Delfzijl, they offer stunning beaches, unique wildlife, and well-preserved historic sites.

Conclusion

Delfzijl is a beautiful and unique destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just want to relax on the beach, Delfzijl is a must-visit destination in the Netherlands.
