Introduction to Groningen

Located in the northern part of the Netherlands, Groningen is a charming and vibrant city that offers visitors a mix of cultural heritage, scenic views, and outdoor activities. The city has a rich history, and its impressive architecture and well-preserved medieval buildings are a testament to its past. There’s something for everyone in Groningen, and this comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of what the city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Groningen

Whether you’re interested in exploring Groningen’s rich cultural heritage or enjoy outdoor activities, there are plenty of places to visit in the city. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Groningen is home to many museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the city’s past. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include the Groninger Museum, the Noordelijk Scheepvaartmuseum, and the Martinikerk Church.

  • The Groninger Museum is a modern art museum with a unique collection of contemporary and modern art. The museum’s striking building designed by Italian architect Alessandro Mendini is an attraction in itself.
  • The Noordelijk Scheepvaartmuseum is a maritime museum that showcases the history of shipping and boat-building in the region. It’s housed in a 16th-century warehouse in the heart of the city.
  • The Martinikerk Church is an iconic landmark in Groningen and one of the best examples of Romanesque architecture in the Netherlands. It also houses an impressive organ and a carillon with 62 bells.

Natural Attractions of Groningen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Groningen’s countryside is beautiful, and there are many opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and cycling. The city is also home to several parks and gardens that are perfect for a leisurely stroll. Some of the popular natural attractions in Groningen are:

  • The Wadden Sea is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a unique ecosystem with an ever-changing landscape. Visitors can take a guided tour or hike along the shore to explore the area’s diversity of flora and fauna.
  • The Stadspark is a large urban park that’s ideal for a picnic or a leisurely walk. It’s also home to a petting zoo and a playground for children.
  • The Hoornsemeer is a beautiful lake just outside the city that’s popular for swimming, fishing, and sailing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Groningen

Groningen is a flat and bike-friendly city, and there are many cycling routes that showcase the city’s natural beauty. Here are a few of the most popular hiking/biking routes to explore:

  • The Reitdiep Route is a picturesque cycling route that takes you along the scenic Reitdiep River, which is dotted with quaint villages and picturesque windmills.
  • The Zuidlaardermeer Route is a challenging cycling route that takes you through forests and fields to the Zuidlaardermeer lake, where you can enjoy swimming and water sports.
  • The Lauwersmeer National Park Route is a scenic cycling route that takes you through the Lauwersmeer National park, a unique wetland area teeming with wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Groningen: Activities and Tours

Groningen has many activities and tours that cater to different interests. From canal tours to beer tastings, here are our top recommendations:

  • Canal Tour is a popular way to see the city’s picturesque canals and historic buildings. There are several operators that offer guided tours, including a hop-on, hop-off option.
  • Groningen Brewery Tour is a must-do for beer lovers. The city is home to several breweries, and you can sample local beers and learn about the brewing process.
  • Groninger City Guide Tour is a walking tour that takes you through the city’s main attractions and provides insight into Groningen’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Groningen

Groningen is a city that can be explored in a few days, but there’s enough to keep visitors occupied for a week. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city center, visit the Groninger Museum, and take a canal tour.
Day 2 Visit the Noordelijke Scheepvaartmuseum and the Martinikerk Church.
Day 3 Cycle along the Reitdiep Route and explore the quaint villages and windmills along the way.
Day 4 Take a day trip to the Wadden Sea, explore the mudflats, and take a guided tour.
Day 5 Visit the Lauwersmeer National Park and hike along the scenic trails.
Day 6 Experience the local food scene and try traditional dishes like stamppot and eel.
Day 7 Visit the Hoornsemeer lake, go for a swim, and enjoy a relaxing day in nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Groningen

Groningen has a vibrant food and drink scene that’s worth exploring. The city’s cuisine is heavily influenced by its location near the sea, and there are many local dishes that are must-tries. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Groninger Mustard Soup is a hearty soup made with Groninger mustard, smoked bacon, and cream.
  • Stamppot is a traditional Dutch dish that’s popular in Groningen. It’s made with potatoes, kale, onions, and sausage.
  • Eel is a local delicacy that’s often smoked or fried and served with bread.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever is a traditional Dutch gin that’s popular in Groningen. You can try it at one of the city’s many bars and cafes.
  • Groninger Beer is a locally brewed beer that’s available at many bars and pubs in the city.

Where to Stay in Groningen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Groningen has a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. From luxury hotels to budget hostels, here are our top recommendations:

  • Prinsenhof is a luxury hotel located in a historic building in the city center. It offers elegant rooms, a restaurant, and a spa.
  • Simplon Jongerenhotel is a budget hostel popular with backpackers. It’s located near the city center and offers affordable accommodation and a friendly atmosphere.
  • The Student Hotel Groningen is a trendy hotel that caters to students and young travelers. It offers modern rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Groningen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Groningen has many unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or mementos of your trip. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

  • Groninger Mustard is a delicious local specialty that makes a great souvenir or gift.
  • Groninger Gingerbread is a traditional Dutch treat that’s often decorated with intricate designs.
  • Dutch Cheese is a must-try when in the Netherlands. You can sample and purchase local cheeses at the city’s many markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Groningen

Groningen has a unique culture and customs that are worth learning about. The city is known for its friendly and welcoming locals and its laid-back atmosphere. Here are a few of the local customs to keep in mind:

  • Cycling is the preferred mode of transportation in Groningen, and there are many bike paths and lanes throughout the city.
  • The Groningen dialect is a unique variation of Dutch that’s spoken in the city. Locals often use words and phrases that are unfamiliar to non-locals.
  • Tipping is not expected in the Netherlands, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small amount is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Groningen

Groningen can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and there are many outdoor activities and events. The winter months (December-February) can be cold and rainy, but the city’s Christmas markets and ice skating rinks are worth experiencing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Groningen

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Groningen:

Do:

  • Rent a bike and explore the city like a local.
  • Try the local cuisine. Groningen has plenty of unique and delicious dishes to sample.
  • Respect the environment. Groningen is a green city, and visitors are encouraged to use public transport or bikes instead of cars.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas. It’s against the law in the Netherlands and can result in a fine.
  • Ignore bike traffic. Bikes have the right of way in Groningen and can come from any direction.
  • Be loud and rowdy. Groningen is a laid-back city, and locals value peace and quiet.

Safety and Health

Groningen is a safe city for travelers, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major safety issues. However, it’s always important to take basic precautions like staying aware of your surroundings, keeping your valuables safe, and avoiding dark and isolated areas at night. The health system in the Netherlands is excellent, and there are many hospitals and clinics in Groningen that offer high-quality medical care.

Safety in Groningen

  • Take precautions against bike theft. Bike theft is a common problem in the Netherlands, and visitors should use a sturdy lock when leaving their bikes unattended.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in dark and isolated areas.

Health in Groningen

  • Get travel insurance. It’s important to have adequate travel insurance when visiting Groningen or any other destination.
  • Take precautions against tick bites. Lyme disease is a risk in rural areas, and visitors should wear long sleeves and pants and use insect repellent when hiking or cycling in the countryside.

Getting to Groningen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Groningen is well-connected to other Dutch cities and towns, and there are several transportation options to choose from. Here’s a quick rundown of how to get to Groningen:

  • By plane: Groningen doesn’t have an international airport, but there are several airports nearby. The closest is Groningen Airport Eelde, which offers flights to several European destinations.
  • By train: There are several trains that run from Amsterdam to Groningen, and the journey takes about two and a half hours. Trains are comfortable, affordable, and convenient.
  • By bus: There are several intercity and regional buses that connect Groningen to other Dutch cities and towns. Bus travel is affordable but can be slower than trains.

Airports Near Groningen

  • Groningen Airport Eelde is the closest airport to Groningen. It’s located about 10 kilometers south of the city and offers flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Groningen

  • Groningen Bus Station is the main bus station in the city. It’s located in the city center and offers both regional and intercity bus services.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Groningen can be reached by ferry from several Scandinavian ports, including Oslo and Copenhagen. Ferry travel is comfortable but can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Groningen

Groningen is a small and bike-friendly city, and cycling is the preferred mode of transportation for locals and visitors alike. There are also several other transportation options available, including bus, taxi, and car rental. Here’s a quick overview of how to get around in Groningen:

  • Bike: Renting a bike in Groningen is easy and affordable, and there are bike paths and lanes throughout the city.
  • Public transport: Groningen has an extensive bus network that covers the city and its surroundings. Tickets can be purchased on board or at the bus station.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Groningen and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • Car rental: Car rental is available at Groningen Airport Eelde and several locations in the city center. However, parking in the city can be difficult and expensive.

Where to Go Next from Groningen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Groningen is a great base for exploring other Dutch cities and towns and the surrounding countryside. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Leeuwarden is the capital of the neighboring Friesland province and is known for its historic buildings, canals, and cultural attractions.
  • Delfzijl is a seaside town located about 25 kilometers northeast of Groningen. It’s known for its impressive sea wall and beautiful harbor.
  • The Wadden Islands are a chain of islands located off the coast of the Netherlands and are a popular destination for outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and bird watching.

Conclusion

Groningen is a city that has a lot to offer visitors. From its rich cultural heritage to its beautiful countryside and vibrant food and drink scene, there’s something for every type of traveler. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in Groningen.
