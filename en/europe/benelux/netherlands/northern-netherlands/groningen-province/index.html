Introduction to Groningen (Province)

Located in Northern Netherlands, Groningen is a province that boasts pristine natural beauty, rich history, and innovative modern architecture. The province is the northernmost and easternmost of all twelve provinces and is bordered by Drenthe in the south and the Wadden Sea in the north. Groningen city, the largest city in the province, is known for its energetic nightlife and cultural attractions. The province is renowned for its unique blend of historic architecture and modern amenities. Plan your next vacation to this amazing destination today and experience the best Groningen has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Groningen (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Groninger Museum: The Groninger Museum is a beautiful museum that boasts contemporary art and design. The museum houses fascinating art collections of paintings, photography, and fashion.
  • Groningen City Hall: The Groningen City Hall is the oldest building in the city and is famous for its medieval architecture and rich history.
  • The Martinitoren Tower: The Martinitoren Tower is the tallest tower in Groningen City, standing at over 100 meters. Climb to the top to experience the stunning panoramic views of the city.
  • Groningen Synagogue: The Groningen Synagogue is the largest synagogue in the Netherlands outside of Amsterdam and was built in 1906. Visitors can learn about the rich history of the Jewish community in Groningen.
  • Bourtange Fortress: Bourtange Fortress is a beautifully preserved star-shaped fortress in the Oldambt region of Groningen. Constructed in the year 1593, it is one of the most significant historical landmarks in the province.

Natural Attractions of Groningen (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wadden Sea: Wadden Sea is a stunning UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest tidal wetlands in the world. It is home to over 10,000 species of plants and animals.
  • Lauwersmeer National Park: Lauwersmeer National Park is a gorgeous area that boasts vast coastal plains, tidal flats, and freshwater marshes. It is an ideal destination for hiking, camping, and biking.
  • Westerwolde: Westerwolde is located on the southeastern edge of the province and is a beautiful stretch of countryside that offers calm and relaxing views.
  • Hondsrug: Hondsrug is an area that stretches from Groningen to the province of Drenthe and is home to rolling hills and valleys. The area is perfect for hiking.
  • Schiermonnikoog: Schiermonnikoog is a picturesque island located about 16 km north of the mainland. It is a beautiful destination for visitors looking to experience unspoiled natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Groningen (Province)

  • Pieterpad: Pieterpad is a famous hiking trail in the Netherlands that runs from Pieterburen to Sint-Pietersberg in Maastricht. The trail spans a distance of 498 km, with a large part of it covering Groningen province.
  • Drenthe-Friese Wold National Park Trail: The Drenthe-Friese Wold National Park Trail is a stunning 17 km hiking trail that covers Groningen and the provinces of Drenthe and Friesland.
  • Groningen Biking Trail: The Groningen Biking Trail is a fantastic 45 km biking trail that takes you through the stunning provincial countryside.
  • Westerwolde Trail: The Westerwolde Trail is a beautiful hiking trail that passes through waterways and rolling hills. It stretches across Groningen and the Dutch-German border.

Best Things to Do in Groningen (Province): Activities and Tours

  • Canal Cruise – A visit to Groningen is not complete without a canal cruise. The cruise offers the best views of the stunning city from the water.
  • Stadspark – Stadspark is the biggest park in the city of Groningen and offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Cycling in Groningen – Groningen is known for its biking culture, and visitors can explore the city by bike through the excellent bike-sharing system in place.
  • Festivals and Events – Groningen plays host to several festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Noorderzon Performing Arts Festival, which takes place in August and offers visitors a unique cultural experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Groningen (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Groninger Mustard Soup: Groninger Mustard Soup is a creamy, tangy soup made from local mustard from the province.
  • Stamppot: Stamppot is a traditional Dutch dish that comprises mashed potatoes, vegetables, and sometimes meat. It is a popular dish in the province.
  • Poffertjes: These Dutch miniature pancakes are a popular dessert in the Netherlands, and visitors can try them at various cafes and restaurants in the province.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: Jenever is Dutch gin that is brewed in the province of Groningen. Visitors can take a guided tour of the Filliers Distillery and learn about the production process.
  • Groninger Bier: Groninger Bier is a local beer brewed in Groninger breweries. It is known for its strong and flavorful taste.

Where to Stay in Groningen (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Prinsenhof: Hotel Prinsenhof is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Groningen city. It is a perfect place to stay for a luxurious vacation.
  • NH Hotel De Ville Groningen: NH Hotel De Ville Groningen is a 17th-century building that offers visitors a blend of historic and modern accommodation.
  • The Student Hotel Groningen: The Student Hotel Groningen is a trendy hotel that caters to students and young adults. The hotel offers a range of affordable accommodation options.

What to Buy in Groningen (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Groninger Mustard: Groninger Mustard is a local delicacy that visitors can take home as a souvenir.
  • Jenever: Jenever is a popular Dutch beverage that visitors can take home as a souvenir.
  • Candlesticks: Groningen is famous for its colorful and decorative candlesticks that make a perfect souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Groningen (Province)

Groningen culture is characterized by a unique blend of historic and modern elements. The province is known for its thriving art scene, lively nightlife, and strong biking culture. The locals are hospitable and friendly, and visitors can learn about the province’s local customs and traditions by mingling with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Groningen (Province)

The best time to visit Groningen is during the spring and summer months, between April and August. During this period, the weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and events such as festivals, biking tours, and canal cruises.

Do’s and Don’ts of Groningen (Province)

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Try new foods and drinks in the province.
  • Rent a bike and explore the city.
  • Take a canal cruise to experience the best views of the province.

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause damage to the natural surroundings.
  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Engage in activities that may harm the environment or wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Groningen (Province)

Groningen is a safe province with low crime rates. Visitors should still take care of their valuables and be cautious when visiting unfamiliar areas, especially at night.

Health in Groningen (Province)

The province has excellent healthcare facilities and medical services. Visitors should ensure they have travel health insurance and carry any necessary medications with them during their visit.

Other Options

  • Train: Groningen Central Station is a major train station that connects to other cities and towns in the Netherlands.
  • Car: Visitors can also reach Groningen province by car, and the province has an extensive highway network.
  • Bike: Groningen has a thriving bike-sharing system, and visitors can rent bikes at various bike rental stations across the city.

How to Get Around in Groningen (Province)

Groningen has excellent public transportation, and visitors can easily get around using buses, trains, and trams. The province’s biking culture also means that renting a bike is an excellent way to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Groningen (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amsterdam: Amsterdam is just a two-hour train ride from Groningen Central Station and is an excellent destination for visitors looking to explore the vibrant Dutch capital.
  • Maastricht: Maastricht is a charming city with a rich history and culture. It is located in the southernmost part of the Netherlands and offers visitors a unique experience.
  • Rotterdam: Rotterdam is a bustling city known for its modern architecture and vibrant nightlife. It is also a port city and a significant transportation hub for the Netherlands.
  • Hamburg: Hamburg is a beautiful city in northern Germany and is an excellent destination for visitors looking for a unique cultural experience.
