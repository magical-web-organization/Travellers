A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Terneuzen, Netherlands

Terneuzen is a small town in Zeeland, located in Southern Netherlands. It is known as the gateway to Belgium, and offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of the region. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best of what Terneuzen has to offer, including where to stay, what to eat, and things to do.

Introduction to Terneuzen

Terneuzen is situated on the Westerschelde estuary, which separates the Netherlands and Belgium. It has a long history, dating back to the Roman era. Over the centuries, the town has been an important trading hub and was heavily fortified during World War II. Today, Terneuzen is a thriving community with a population of around 55,000.

Terneuzen has a temperate maritime climate, with mild winters and cool summers. The best time to visit Terneuzen is from April to October when you can enjoy the warm weather and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Terneuzen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Terneuzen is a town with a rich historical heritage, and there are several places you can visit to learn more about its past:

  • Het Portaal van Vlaanderen - This is a multimedia exhibition that takes you through the importance of the port to the region’s economy, culture, and history.
  • Museum Schooltijd - Step into a Dutch classroom from the past and experience how children used to study in the olden times.
  • De Vliegende Hollander - This museum is located at the old lighthouse on the Westerschelde. The museum gives visitors an insight into the history of the lighthouse and the river traffic.

Natural Attractions of Terneuzen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Terneuzen is an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike, with plenty of opportunities to enjoy scenic walks, bike rides, or birdwatching. Here are some of the best natural attractions of Terneuzen:

  • Saeftinghe Nature Reserve - This is the largest salt marsh in Europe, with an incredibly diverse range of flora and fauna. It’s a unique habitat and is home to rare birds, including ospreys and peregrine falcons.
  • De Braakman - This lake and nature reserve is known for its hiking and biking trails. It’s surrounded by lush forests and offers stunning views of the water.
  • The Westerschelde - This estuary is the busiest shipping lane in Europe and is where the Scheldt River meets the North Sea. It’s an ideal spot for birdwatching and offers some of the best sunsets in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Terneuzen

For those who enjoy outdoor activities, Terneuzen has plenty of beautiful hiking and biking routes to offer. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Terneuzen - De Braakman - Hoofdplaat - This is a 28-kilometer bike route through the De Braakman nature reserve and along the Westerschelde estuary. It offers stunning views of the countryside and water.
  • Saeftinghe Nature Reserve Hike - This is a 2-hour hike through the largest salt marsh in Europe. It’s a great opportunity to spot rare birds and learn more about this unique habitat.
  • The Panoramic Tour - This is a 52-kilometer bike route that takes you along the Westerschelde estuary, through historic towns, and beautiful countryside.

Best Things to Do in Terneuzen: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Terneuzen to keep you entertained. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Boat Tour of the Westerschelde - Take a boat tour of this busy shipping lane and see some of the biggest ships in the world up close.
  • Visit the Terneuzen Market - Held every Saturday in the center of town, this market is a great place to buy fresh produce, local treats, and souvenirs.
  • Tandem Skydiving - For those who are seeking adventure, why not try tandem skydiving? It’s an unforgettable experience that offers incredible views of Terneuzen and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Terneuzen

If you have a week to spend in Terneuzen, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore Terneuzen. Visit Het Portaal van Vlaanderen and Museum Schooltijd
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Westerschelde and visit the De Vliegende Hollander Museum
  • Day 3: Hike in the Saeftinghe Nature Reserve and visit the De Braakman Lake
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and cycle the Panoramic Tour
  • Day 5: Attend the Terneuzen Market and try local delicacies
  • Day 6: Try tandem skydiving
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beaches of Zeeland

Food and Drink Scene of Terneuzen

Terneuzen is known for its fresh seafood and hearty Dutch cuisine. Here are some dishes and drinks that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mosselen - Freshly caught mussels served with fries and mayonnaise.
  • Stamppot - A traditional Dutch dish made with mashed potatoes, vegetables, and sausages.
  • Pannenkoeken - Dutch pancakes, served with sweet or savory toppings like bacon, cheese or Nutella.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever - A Dutch gin, available in various flavors such as juniper, lemon, and cranberry.
  • Belgian Beer - Terneuzen is located close to the Belgian border, and there are plenty of Belgian beer cafes in town.
  • Dutch Coffee - A coffee that’s infused with a Dutch specialty liqueur, such as advocaat or coffee liqueur.

Where to Stay in Terneuzen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Terneuzen has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, depending on your budget and preferences.

  • Fletcher Hotel-Restaurant De Schelde - This is a 4-star hotel overlooking the Westerschelde estuary. Rooms are modern and spacious, and there’s an outdoor terrace where you can enjoy a drink in the sunshine.
  • Hostel de Zes - This is a budget-friendly option, situated in the center of town. The hostel offers comfortable rooms and a shared kitchen.
  • B&B ‘t Huys van Enaeme - This is a charming bed and breakfast housed in a historic building. Rooms are decorated in a traditional style, and breakfast is included in the price.

What to Buy in Terneuzen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Terneuzen may be small, but there are plenty of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for loved ones or mementos of your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • Zeeland Regional Products - Look out for traditional Zeeland products, such as honey, cheese, sausages, and preserves.
  • Maritime Gifts - With its importance as a port town, Terneuzen has plenty of souvenirs related to the maritime industry, such as ship models, compasses, and maps.
  • Handmade Crafts - Terneuzen has a craft and design center where you can buy handmade crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Terneuzen

Terneuzen has a unique local culture and customs that are worth exploring.

  • Zeeland Festivals - Terneuzen hosts several festivals throughout the year, celebrating local traditions, music, and food. The most notable are the Zydeco Zity Festival and the Mosselfeesten.
  • Flemish Influence - Due to its proximity to Belgium, Terneuzen has a strong Flemish influence on its culture and language. You’ll see this in the local cuisine, architecture, and dialects.

Best Time to Visit Terneuzen

The best time to visit Terneuzen is from April to October when the days are warm and sunny. The town is also busy during the summer months, with plenty of outdoor activities and events taking place. However, if you’re looking for a quieter experience, visiting Terneuzen in the shoulder season of April, May, or October is a good option.

Do’s and Don’ts of Terneuzen

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Terneuzen:

Do:

  • Try local specialties such as mussels, Dutch pancakes, and jenever.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, particularly during festivals and celebrations.
  • Bring comfortable shoes and outdoor clothing if you plan on hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places. Smoking is prohibited in most indoor spaces in the Netherlands, including bars and restaurants.
  • Cycle under the influence of alcohol. This is illegal and can result in a fine.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings. Keep Terneuzen clean and beautiful for future visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Terneuzen

Terneuzen is generally a safe town to visit, with low levels of crime. However, it’s always wise to take precautions to avoid pickpocketing or any other petty crimes.

Health in Terneuzen

The Netherlands has a high standard of healthcare, and Terneuzen has several doctors and pharmacies for emergency medical help. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance when abroad.

Getting to Terneuzen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Terneuzen is well-connected to the rest of the Netherlands and Belgium, making it easy to reach by various means of transport.

Airports Near Terneuzen

  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport - The largest international airport in the Netherlands. From there, you can take a train to Terneuzen.
  • Brussels Airport - The largest airport in Belgium, is also an option. From the airport, you can take a train or bus to Terneuzen.

Bus Stations Near Terneuzen

  • Terneuzen - The central bus station in Terneuzen connects to various cities in the Netherlands and Belgium.
  • Goes - A nearby town with a larger bus station, connecting to more extensive networks across the Netherlands.

Other Options

  • Train - The nearest train station is Goes, which connects to larger train networks across the Netherlands. From Goes, you can take a bus or a taxi to Terneuzen.
  • Car - Terneuzen is easily accessible by car, and there are plenty of parking options in the town center.

How to Get Around in Terneuzen

The best way to explore Terneuzen is on foot or by bike. The town is small and compact and has a well-developed network of bike paths. Bikes are available for rent at various locations in Terneuzen, including train stations and tourist information centers.

Where to Go Next from Terneuzen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Terneuzen’s location makes it easy to explore other nearby towns and attractions:

  • Ghent - This charming Belgian town is known for its medieval architecture, canals, and museums. It’s only 45 kilometers from Terneuzen and can easily be reached by car or by train.
  • Bruges - One of the most picturesque cities in the world, Bruges is about an hour’s drive from Terneuzen.
  • Delta Works - The largest flood protection system in the world, the Delta Works, is located nearby in Zeeland and can be visited from Terneuzen.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or adventure, Terneuzen has something to offer. With its friendly locals, delicious food, and charming ambiance, it’s a destination you won’t soon forget. We hope this guide has given you some inspiration for your next visit to Terneuzen. Happy travels!
