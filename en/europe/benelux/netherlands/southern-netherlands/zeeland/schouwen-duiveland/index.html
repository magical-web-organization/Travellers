Introduction to Schouwen-Duiveland

Nestled in the southern region of the Netherlands, Schouwen-Duiveland is a picturesque island that boasts of natural beauty, rich history, and a lively culture. The region is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world, thanks to its scenic landscapes, charming towns, and cultural richness.

Schouwen-Duiveland measures 488 square kilometers and is home to several interesting sights and attractions that are sure to leave tourists with unforgettable memories. In this travel guide, we provide a comprehensive overview of the top places to visit, things to do, and places to stay in Schouwen-Duiveland, Netherlands.

List of Places to Visit in Schouwen-Duiveland

There are several interesting places to explore in Schouwen-Duiveland, each with its own charm and appeal. Below is a list of the top sights and must-visit destinations in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stadhuismuseum - This museum is housed in a historic town hall in the town of Zierikzee and showcases the history and culture of Schouwen-Duiveland.
  • Watersnoodmuseum - This museum is dedicated to showcasing the impact of the 1953 North Sea Flood, which devastated several parts of the Netherlands, including Schouwen-Duiveland.
  • Plompe Toren - This 14th-century tower is one of the last remaining structures of a medieval monastery that was destroyed in a flood. It offers panoramic views of the region.

Natural Attractions of Schouwen-Duiveland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dunes of Schouwen - These sand dunes are one of the largest and most diverse natural areas in the Netherlands, offering a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and bird-watching.
  • Oosterschelde National Park - Home to several rare marine species, this park offers a unique opportunity to witness the rich marine life of this region through activities like snorkeling, diving, and seal watching.
  • Delta Works - This collection of dams, dykes, and other structures protects Schouwen-Duiveland from floods and offers an amazing engineering marvel to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Schouwen-Duiveland

Schouwen-Duiveland is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of this region. Some of the must-try routes include:

  • Oosterscheldepad - A 135-kilometer trail that covers the coastline and offers stunning views of the sea.
  • Deltapad - A 165-kilometer route that follows the Delta Works, showcasing the magnificent engineering structures that protect Schouwen-Duiveland.
  • Meeuwenduinen Route - A 10-kilometer trail that takes you through the scenic dune landscape of Schouwen-Duiveland.

Best Things to Do in Schouwen-Duiveland: Activities and Tours

Schouwen-Duiveland offers several activities and tours that are sure to delight tourists of all ages and interests. Some of the top things to do in the region include:

  • Visit the Beaches - Schouwen-Duiveland is home to several beaches that offer plenty of opportunities for swimming, sunbathing, and other water-based activities.
  • Go Diving - The clean and clear waters of Oosterschelde National Park offer a unique opportunity for diving enthusiasts to explore the marine life of this region.
  • Take a Tour of the Towns - The charming towns of Schouwen-Duiveland, such as Zierikzee and Renesse, offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Schouwen-Duiveland

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary that covers some of the top attractions and activities in Schouwen-Duiveland:

  • Day 1: Visit the Stadhuismuseum in Zierikzee and explore the charming town.
  • Day 2: Take a bike tour of the Oosterschelde National Park.
  • Day 3: Visit the Dunes of Schouwen for a day of hiking and bird-watching.
  • Day 4: Go diving or snorkeling in the marine-rich waters of Oosterschelde National Park.
  • Day 5: Visit Renesse, a popular beach town in Schouwen-Duiveland.
  • Day 6: Explore the engineering marvel of the Delta Works.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views at one of the beautiful beaches in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Schouwen-Duiveland

Schouwen-Duiveland offers a range of delicious food and drinks that are sure to leave your taste buds tingling. Here’s what you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mussel soup - This is a specialty of the region and features fresh mussels in a creamy soup.
  • Oosterschelde lobster - This delicate seafood is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Stoofpot - A hearty beef stew that is slow-cooked with onions, carrots, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Zeeuwse Bolus - This sweet pastry is best enjoyed with a cup of coffee.
  • Schouwen-Duiveland beer - The region offers several breweries that produce unique and flavorful beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Schouwen-Duiveland

Schouwen-Duiveland has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditional festivals, crafts, and cuisine. One of the most popular festivals is the Oesterdam Brass Festival, which features performances by brass bands from all over the world. The region also has a thriving art scene, with several galleries and exhibitions that showcase the works of local artists.
