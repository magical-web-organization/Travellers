Introduction to Vlissingen

Located in the Zeeland province in the Southern Netherlands, Vlissingen is a charming coastal town that boasts of a rich history and beautiful outdoor adventures. From museums and historical landmarks to picturesque beaches and scenic hiking trails, there’s plenty to explore in Vlissingen, making it an ideal vacation spot for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Vlissingen

Let’s dive into the top places to visit in Vlissingen, Netherlands.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zeeland Maritime Museum: Housed in a historic arsenal building, the Zeeland Maritime Museum showcases the maritime heritage of the region. Visitors can explore the collections of ships, boats, and nautical memorabilia.

  • Het Arsenaal: This museum is a combination of maritime and pirate-themed attractions, making it a hit with kids. The museum includes exhibits, a virtual reality experience, and even a live sea lion show.

  • Middelburg Abbey: Just a short drive from Vlissingen, the Middelburg Abbey is a 12th-century monastery-turned-church. Visitors can admire the Gothic architecture and even climb the church tower for a panoramic view of the city.

Natural Attractions of Vlissingen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beachfront Boulevard: Take a stroll down the spacious boulevard that runs along the waterfront and soak up the sun on the sandy beaches.

  • Westduinpark: This natural park is a great place for bird watching, hiking, and cycling. With abundant wildlife and rolling dunes, Westduinpark is a beautiful natural escape.

  • Zeeuws Maritiem MuZEEum: Experience the marine life of the region by visiting the Zeeuws Maritiem MuZEEum museum. Learn about the marine animals and their habitats, and even interact with some of the creatures through touch pools.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vlissingen

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Vlissingen offers plenty of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Hiker’s Trail: This 14-kilometer trail starts from Vlissingen town and winds its way through dunes, forest areas, and small villages.

  • Cycling Route 443: The 49-kilometer cycling route passes through picturesque landscapes, small towns, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Vlissingen: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Vlissingen:

  • Take a sunset cruise: Experience the stunning sunset views of Vlissingen from the water with a sunset cruise.

  • Visit the weekly market: Vlissingen hosts a weekly market that offers fresh produce, local goods, and handmade items.

  • Attend a festival: From summer music festivals to the annual kite festival, Vlissingen hosts several events throughout the year that showcase the local culture and traditions.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Vlissingen, here’s a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Vlissingen

The foodie scene in Vlissingen centers around fresh seafood and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kibbeling: Fried cod bites served with tartar sauce.

  • Stroopwafels: Syrup-filled waffle cookies that are a Netherlands classic.

  • Mosselen: Steamed mussels served with French fries and mayonnaise.

Local Beverages

  • Brouwcafé De Piek: A local brewery that serves refreshing craft beers and tasty bar snacks.

  • Wijnbar Seventy-Seven: A cozy wine bar that offers an extensive selection of local and international wines.

Where to Stay in Vlissingen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, Vlissingen offers accommodation options for every traveler. Here are some popular options:

  • Amadore Hotel Restaurant Arion: Located right on the beachfront boulevard, this upscale hotel offers stunning sea views and a spa.

  • Stayokay Vlissingen: A budget-friendly hostel that’s perfect for backpackers and groups, Stayokay Vlissingen offers a communal kitchen, patio, and a bar.

  • Bed & Breakfast de Kleyne Wereld: A charming guesthouse that’s housed in a historic building and offers cozy rooms and a breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Vlissingen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing back home:

  • Cheese: The Netherlands is famous for its cheese selection, and Vlissingen offers a range of local cheese shops with delicious options.

  • Delftware: Blue and white ceramic pottery that’s a classic Dutch souvenir.

  • Seashell Jewelry: Coastal towns in the Netherlands are known for their seashell jewelry, and Vlissingen offers several shops that sell unique designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Vlissingen

When in Vlissingen, it’s important to respect and understand the local customs and traditions. Here are some tips:

  • Greeting etiquette: It’s customary to shake hands when greeting someone for the first time.

  • Tipping: Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory. It’s common to round up the bill to the nearest Euro.

Best Time to Visit Vlissingen

The best time to visit Vlissingen is from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the tourist season is in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s natural beauty throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vlissingen

Do:

  • Explore the natural parks: Vlissingen offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the town’s natural beauty.

  • Try the local seafood: Vlissingen is famous for its seafood, and local restaurants offer delicious dishes.

Don’t:

  • Ignore traffic laws: Biking is a popular mode of transportation in Vlissingen, but it’s important to follow traffic laws and be aware of other cyclists.

  • Litter: Vlissingen is a clean and tidy town, and littering is considered disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vlissingen

Vlissingen is a safe town, and visitors can explore it without worry. However, it’s important to be aware of pickpockets in tourist areas and keep valuables secure.

Health in Vlissingen

Vlissingen has a public healthcare system that offers emergency services and routine care for tourists. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers overseas medical expenses.

Getting to Vlissingen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vlissingen

The nearest international airport to Vlissingen is Amsterdam’s Schiphol Airport, located about two and a half hours away by train.

Bus Stations Near Vlissingen

The town has a central bus station, which connects to neighboring towns and regions.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Vlissingen by train or car. The town is well-connected to the Netherlands’ national railway network, and several highways connect it to other regions.

How to Get Around in Vlissingen

The best way to get around Vlissingen is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bicycles at several rental shops in the city center. However, the town also has a public bus system that runs regularly and connects to neighboring towns.

Where to Go Next from Vlissingen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring:

  • Delta Works: One of the biggest engineering feats in the Netherlands, Delta Works is a series of structures that protect the country from floodwaters.

  • The Hague: The seat of the Dutch government and home to several international organizations, The Hague is a vibrant city that offers a mix of history and modernism.

Conclusion

From museums and natural parks to beaches and local cuisine, Vlissingen offers an ideal mix of culture and adventure for travelers. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect trip to Vlissingen and enjoy all the town has to offer.
