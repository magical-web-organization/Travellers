Introduction to Hulst

Located in Zeeland in Southern Netherlands, Hulst is a charming small town that boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a lively cultural scene. Situated near the Belgian border, Hulst is an ideal destination for those who want to explore a lesser-known gem of Netherlands. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing retreat or an action-packed adventure, Hulst has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hulst

Before you start planning your trip to Hulst, take a look at the most popular places to visit in this quaint town. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hulst is known for its well-preserved historic center, which is filled with beautiful churches, fortifications, and monuments. If you’re interested in history and culture, you’ll love visiting these attractions:

  • Hulst City Hall: Built in the 16th century, this stunning Renaissance building is now used for special events and ceremonies.

  • The Old Town Walls: This ancient wall encircles the historic center of Hulst and is a must-see for its stunning architecture and panoramic views of the town.

  • St. Willibrordus Basilica: This picturesque church is a popular site for wedding ceremonies and other religious events.

  • Museum Hulst: This museum showcases the history and culture of Hulst, including its military past and famous carnival traditions.

Natural Attractions of Hulst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hulst is situated in a stunning natural setting, surrounded by forests, marshes, and wetlands. If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, don’t miss these attractions:

  • De Vogel: This nature reserve is home to hundreds of bird species, making it a paradise for nature lovers and birdwatchers.

  • Hulster Moor: A beautiful hiking trail that winds through the marshes and natural areas of Hulst, this is an ideal location for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

  • Crossover: This adventure park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including rope bridges, zip lines, and climbing walls. Perfect for adrenaline junkies!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hulst

Hulst is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with numerous trails that cater to different skill levels. Whether you prefer mountain biking or leisurely walks, here are some of the best routes in town:

  • Fort St. Jan: This trail takes you through the heart of Hulst’s historic fortifications, offering stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside.

  • Hulst-Putte Cycling Route: This route is perfect for those who want to explore the stunning landscapes of Zeeland on two wheels. You’ll ride past picturesque farms, forests, and quaint villages.

  • Smokkelroute: This route follows the path of smugglers who used to transport goods between Belgium and the Netherlands. It is a fascinating journey through the history and culture of the region.

Best Things to Do in Hulst: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural beauty and historic charm, Hulst also boasts a lively cultural scene with numerous activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Hulst:

  • Carnival: Hulst is famous for its carnival celebrations, which take place every year in February and attract thousands of visitors from all over the world. Expect colorful parades, live music, and lots of food and drinks.

  • Wine Tasting: Zeeland is famous for its delicious wines, and Hulst is home to several vineyards where you can taste the local varietals.

  • Guided Walking Tours: If you want to discover Hulst’s hidden gems and learn more about its history and culture, join a guided walking tour led by a knowledgeable local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hulst

Are you planning a longer visit to Hulst? Here’s a week-long itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in town:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Hulst’s historic center and visit the Old Town Walls and St. Willibrordus Basilica.
Day 2 Take a guided tour of Museum Hulst and learn about the town’s rich history and culture. In the afternoon, go hiking in Hulster Moor.
Day 3 Bike the Hulst-Putte Cycling Route and explore the scenic landscapes of Zeeland.
Day 4 Spend the day at De Vogel nature reserve and discover its rich birdlife and natural beauty.
Day 5 Visit Crossover adventure park and try your hand at zip-lining, rope bridges, and other outdoor activities.
Day 6 Take a wine-tasting tour and sample the delicious local wines of Zeeland.
Day 7 Attend the carnival celebrations and enjoy the festive atmosphere and colorful parades.

Food and Drink Scene of Hulst

Hulst has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties that you won’t find anywhere else. Here are our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bolus: This sweet pastry is a traditional Zeeland delicacy made from cinnamon and sugar. It is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

  • Stamppot: This hearty Dutch dish is made from mashed potatoes, vegetables, and smoked sausage. Perfect for a cold winter’s day.

  • Oosterschelde lobster: This local delicacy is a must-try for seafood lovers. It’s best enjoyed with a glass of chilled white wine.

Local Beverages

  • Zeeland Beer: Zeeland is home to several microbreweries that produce delicious artisanal beers. Don’t miss the chance to taste them!

  • “Kapelse Wijn”: This delicious white wine is made from local grapes and is considered one of the best in Zeeland.

Where to Stay in Hulst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hulst offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and needs. Here are some of the best places to stay in town:

  • Hotel de Linde: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Hulst and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  • B&B Onder de Molen: This cozy guesthouse is situated in a historic windmill and offers a unique and unforgettable experience.

  • Stayokay Hulst: This modern hostel offers affordable accommodation for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Hulst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for authentic souvenirs and local products to bring home from Hulst, here are some of the best options:

  • Hulstse Babbelaars: These delicious sweets are a local specialty and make an excellent gift for anyone with a sweet tooth.

  • Miniature Windmills: Hulst is surrounded by beautiful windmills, and you can find miniature versions of them in souvenir shops.

  • Zeeland Cheese: This delicious cheese is made from local milk and is a popular gift for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Hulst

Hulst is a friendly and welcoming town with a rich cultural heritage. Some of the local customs and traditions that you may encounter during your visit include:

  • Carnival Celebrations: Hulst is famous for its colorful and lively carnival celebrations, which take place every year in February.

  • Windmills: Hulst is surrounded by beautiful windmills that have been an integral part of the town’s culture and history for centuries.

Best Time to Visit Hulst

The best time to visit Hulst depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a quick overview of the pros and cons of visiting Hulst in different seasons:

  • Spring (March-May): This is a great time to visit Hulst for outdoor activities and hiking. The weather is mild and pleasant, and the landscapes are beautiful.

  • Summer (June-August): This is peak tourist season in Hulst, with lots of visitors flocking to the town to enjoy its festivals and outdoor activities. Expect crowds and higher prices.

  • Fall (September-November): This is a quieter season in Hulst, with mild weather and beautiful autumn landscapes. It’s a great time to enjoy the town’s culture and history.

  • Winter (December-February): This is the best time to visit Hulst if you want to experience the town’s famous carnival celebrations. Expect cold weather and lots of festive cheer!

Do’s and Don’ts of Hulst

As a visitor to Hulst, here are some tips to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local food and drink specialties.

  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment.

  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.

  • Engage in illegal or harmful activities.

Safety and Health

Hulst is a safe and welcoming town, but like any other destination, there are some safety and health concerns to keep in mind.

Safety in Hulst

  • Watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas.

  • Observe traffic rules when biking or walking in town.

  • Be aware of the weather conditions and dress accordingly.

Health in Hulst

  • Make sure you have adequate travel insurance.

  • Protect yourself from ticks when hiking or spending time in nature reserves.

  • Follow basic hygiene practices to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Hulst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hulst is easily accessible from other parts of the Netherlands and Europe. Here are some of the best ways to get there:

  • By Train: The nearest train station to Hulst is in Sint-Niklaas, Belgium, which is situated about 20 kilometers away from town.

  • By Car: Hulst is easily reachable by car, with good road connections to other parts of the Netherlands and Belgium.

Airports Near Hulst

  • Brussels Airport (BRU): This major international airport is about 100 kilometers away from Hulst and offers direct flights to numerous destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Hulst

  • Hulst Bus Station: Situated in the heart of town, Hulst Bus Station offers connections to other major towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Ferry: You can take a ferry from Vlissingen to Breskens and then travel to Hulst by bus or car.

How to Get Around in Hulst

Hulst is a compact town that is easily walkable, with many of its attractions located within a short distance of one another. However, if you prefer to use public transportation, the town also has an efficient bus network that covers all major areas.

Where to Go Next from Hulst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have a few extra days to spare after exploring Hulst, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth considering:

  • Antwerp: This vibrant Belgian city is located about 35 kilometers away from Hulst and is famous for its beautiful architecture and vibrant nightlife.

  • Domburg: This pretty seaside town is located about 40 kilometers away from Hulst and is a popular destination for beach lovers.

  • Middelburg: This historic town is located about 50 kilometers away from Hulst and is known for its beautiful canals, museums, and cafes.

Conclusion

Hulst is a hidden gem of Southern Netherlands that is waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and lively cultural scene, it offers something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Hulst. Bon Voyage!
