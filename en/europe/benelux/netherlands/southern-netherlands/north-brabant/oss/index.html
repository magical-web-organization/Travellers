Introduction to Oss

Oss, located in the North Brabant province of Southern Netherlands, is a charming city that boasts a rich cultural history. With its location on the banks of the Meuse River, Oss offers both natural and architectural wonders that attract tourists from all over the world. This comprehensive tour guide will give you a comprehensive overview of the must-visit places, hiking/biking routes, activities, food and drink scene, and local culture and customs of Oss.

List of Places to Visit in Oss

When planning a trip to Oss, there are several tourists attractions and hidden gems to check out. Here’s a list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum Jan Cunen: A historic museum that houses an impressive collection of modern art and showcases Oss’ cultural heritage.
  2. The Saint John’s Cathedral: A Neo-Gothic church built in the late 19th century that boasts gorgeous stained glass windows and intricate carvings.
  3. De Maashorst: An 8,500-hectare nature reserve with ancient forests, sand dunes, and heathlands.

Natural Attractions of Oss: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. De Herpenduin: A scenic bike trail that takes you through heathlands and forests.
  2. Macharense Waarden: A vast wetland area surrounded by hills and forest that offers a serene atmosphere.
  3. The Ossenwaard: A picturesque landscape made up of beautiful grasslands and poplar trees. It’s an excellent spot for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oss

Oss is known for its scenic biking and hiking trails that take you through the city’s stunning landscapes and up-close with nature. Here are the top hiking/biking routes to check out:

  1. Maashorstpad: A 24 km hike that takes you through heathlands and forests.
  2. Molenroute: A 40 km bike trail that offers views of the Maas River and Oss’ countryside.
  3. De Ossenwaard Walk: A short, 3 km walk that showcases Oss’ striking scenery.

Best Things to Do in Oss: Activities and Tours

Oss offers plenty of things to do for all types of travelers. Here’s a list of the best things to do in Oss:

  1. Go on a boat tour of the Meuse River.
  2. Shop at the Oss market for souvenirs and local products.
  3. Attend a concert or event at the Groene Engel cultural center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oss

If you’re planning to spend a week in Oss, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 - Visit the Museum Jan Cunen Day 2 - Explore De Maashorst Day 3 - Take a boat tour of the Meuse River Day 4 - Visit Saint John’s Cathedral Day 5 - Go on a bike tour of the Ossenwaard Day 6 - Attend a concert or event at Groene Engel Day 7 - Shop at the Oss market for souvenirs and local products

Food and Drink Scene of Oss

Oss’ food and drink scene is rich in local, seasonal, and sustainable produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ossche Bol: A local puff pastry filled with a sweet bakery cream.
  2. The Dutch ‘Brokaworst’: A sausage made with ground beef, pork, and spices
  3. Bossche Balletjes: A chocolate confection filled with a sweet inner core.

Local Beverages

  1. Dutch Beer: Try out local beers from the Maasland Brewery or VandeWitte Brewing company.
  2. Jenever: A Dutch liqueur that’s usually served as an aperitif.
  3. Koffie Verkeerd: A popular Dutch coffee; translated to mean “Coffee incorrectly.”

Where to Stay in Oss: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Oss. Here are the best hotels, hostels and guesthouses to choose from:

  1. Fletcher Hotel-Restaurant Oss: A four-star hotel with sophisticated rooms, and an on-site restaurant serving local food.
  2. Motel Koolen: A budget-friendly motel with simple rooms and free parking.
  3. Bed & Breakfast Van Otterdijk: A charming guesthouse surrounded by beautiful gardens and offering excellent breakfast.

What to Buy in Oss: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oss is known for its diverse range of handicrafts and handmade products. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. Pottery: Oss is known for its beautiful and delicate pottery.
  2. Stroopwafels: These are thin waffles filled with caramel and are a tasty Dutch treat.
  3. Cheese: Local cheese consists of Gouda and Edam types.

Local Culture and Customs of Oss

The people of Oss are known to be hospitable and friendly. It’s common to see celebrations of traditional Dutch events such as Carnival and King’s Day.

Best Time to Visit Oss

The best time to visit Oss is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oss

Do:

  1. Respect local traditions and customs.
  2. Be polite and courteous to the locals.
  3. Try out the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect public monuments and cultural heritage sites.
  2. Litter in public places.
  3. Make noise after 11 pm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oss

Oss is relatively safe with a low crime rate, though it is always advisable to be cautious and watchful in crowded areas and tourist sites.

Health in Oss

There are several hospitals and medical centers in Oss that offer excellent medical services. The emergency number for medical assistance is 112.

Getting to Oss: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oss

The nearest airport to Oss is Eindhoven Airport, which is about 40 km away. There are taxis, buses and private transfer services available from the airport to Oss.

Bus Stations Near Oss

The Oss train station connects to several cities in the Netherlands, including Amsterdam, Rotterdam, and Utrecht. There are buses available from the train station to the city center.

Other Options

There are car rental services available at the Eindhoven airport and the train station that you can use to explore Oss and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Oss

Walking and cycling are the best ways to get around Oss. There are also buses and taxis available for public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Oss: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oss is conveniently located near several other cities and attractions in the Netherlands. Here are some great places to visit nearby:

  1. Eindhoven: A vibrant city known for modern architecture and technology.
  2. ’s-Hertogenbosch: A picturesque city 35 km away known for its medieval buildings and winding alleyways.
  3. Uden: A green city located 10 km away from Oss that boasts a charming downtown area.

Conclusion

Oss, Netherlands, is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the Netherlands’ cultural history and natural beauty. From its diverse range of food and drink, outdoor activities like biking and hiking, and cultural attractions, Oss has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Oss with ease.
