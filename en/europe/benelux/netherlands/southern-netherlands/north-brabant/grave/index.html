Introduction to Grave

Grave is a picturesque town located in the northeastern part of the North Brabant province in the Netherlands. The town is known for its rich history and vibrant culture, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore the beauty of the Netherlands beyond its popular cities.

Despite its small size, Grave offers a wide range of attractions, including historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, delicious food, and exciting cultural experiences. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and the local culture and customs to help you make the most of your visit to Grave.

List of Places to Visit in Grave

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historical City Center - Take a stroll through the charming streets of Grave’s historical city center and admire the beautiful architecture of the buildings that date back to the 13th century.
  • City Museum Grave - Learn about the town’s history and culture at the City Museum, situated in a 17th-century mansion in the city center.
  • Kazemattenmuseum Grave - Explore the underground tunnels and chambers that were once used as a defense system during the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Grave: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Maas River - Take a walk along the picturesque Maas River and enjoy stunning views of the water and the surrounding landscape.
  • Kraaijenbergse Plassen - This beautiful lake is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Landgoed Tongelaar - This stunning park and nature reserve is home to a variety of animals and plants. It’s perfect for a peaceful walk or bike ride in the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grave

  • Maas- en Peelliniepad - This long-distance biking and hiking trail takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding Grave, with several historic landmarks and natural attractions to explore along the way.
  • Grave-Bossche Bollenroute - This scenic biking trail takes you through the beautiful Brabant countryside, where you can stop at local bakeries to try the famous Bossche Bol, a local pastry that’s a must-try.

Best Things to Do in Grave: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of the Maas River - Get a unique perspective of the town and its surrounding countryside by taking a boat tour along the Maas River.
  • Beer Tasting Tour - Visit the local brewery and sample some of the best beer in the region while learning about the brewing process.
  • City Walking Tour - Take a guided walking tour of Grave’s historical city center and learn about the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Grave

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asperges - Also known as white asparagus, Grave is famous for this seasonal delicacy that is typically served with ham, boiled eggs, and melted butter.
  • Peperkoek - This local gingerbread is a traditional treat that’s often enjoyed with a cup of coffee or tea.
  • Bossche Bol - As mentioned earlier, this sweet pastry is a local specialty that’s a must-try for anyone visiting Grave.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Grave is known for its delicious beer, which is brewed using traditional methods and high-quality ingredients.
  • Coffee - The Netherlands is famous for its coffee culture, and Grave is no exception. You’ll find plenty of cozy cafes throughout the town where you can enjoy a cup of freshly brewed coffee.

Where to Stay in Grave: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Graaf van Den Bosch - This charming hotel is located in the heart of Grave’s city center and offers cozy rooms with modern amenities.
  • Sint Jozefklooster Guesthouse - This former monastery has been converted into a guesthouse and offers comfortable rooms with a unique ambiance.
  • Hostel Katwijk - This budget-friendly hostel is ideal for backpackers and budget travelers looking for affordable accommodation in Grave.

What to Buy in Grave: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese - The Netherlands is famous for its cheese, and Grave is no exception. Be sure to try some of the local varieties, such as Gouda or Edam.
  • Peperkoek - This local gingerbread makes a great souvenir to bring home to family and friends.
  • Delftware - This iconic blue and white pottery is a popular souvenir from the Netherlands.

Local Culture and Customs of Grave

The people of Grave are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms. It’s important to observe the local customs and etiquette, such as greeting people with a friendly hello or “dag,” and being respectful of the town’s rich history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Grave

The best time to visit Grave is during the spring and summer when the weather is mild, and the town comes alive with festivals and cultural events. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, visiting during the winter months can be just as charming and peaceful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grave

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including asperges and Bossche Bol pastry.
  • Do take a boat ride along the Maas River to see the town from a new perspective.
  • Do visit the local breweries to try some of the best beer in the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s historical buildings and landmarks.
  • Don’t forget to greet people with a friendly “hello” or “dag.”
  • Don’t miss out on trying some of the local specialties.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grave

Grave is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always important to take precautions, such as keeping your belongings close and not leaving valuables out in the open.

Health in Grave

The Netherlands has a well-developed healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care at local hospitals and clinics if needed. Be sure to bring any necessary medication or medical supplies with you, and have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Grave: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grave

The nearest international airports to Grave are Eindhoven Airport and Amsterdam Schiphol Airport. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to reach Grave.

Bus Stations Near Grave

Grave has good bus connections to neighboring towns and cities. The nearest bus station is the Grave Bus Station, which is located in the town center.

Other Options

Grave is also accessible by car, with good roads and highways connecting the town to other parts of the Netherlands.

How to Get Around in Grave

Grave is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Several bike rental shops are available in the town center, and most attractions are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Grave: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nijmegen - This nearby city is known for its vibrant student culture, historic landmarks, and lively nightlife.
  • ‘s-Hertogenbosch - This charming city is famous for its beautiful canals, historic buildings, and delicious local cuisine.
  • Biesbosch National Park - This stunning natural park is home to a wide variety of waterfowl and other animals, and offers scenic boat tours and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Grave is a hidden gem in the Netherlands, offering a unique combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food lover, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your visit to Grave and discover all the amazing things this town has to offer.
