Introduction to Breda

Breda is a charming city in Southern Netherlands and is part of the North Brabant province. The city boasts of rich history, beautiful architecture, delightful landscapes, and a vibrant culture. From its numerous museums and galleries to its bustling marketplaces, Breda has something for everyone. This travel guide aims to provide you with a comprehensive overview of the best things to do, see, eat, and experience in Breda.

List of Places to Visit in Breda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Breda’s Castle: Also known as the “Kasteel van Breda,” this stunning fortress dates back to the 13th century. It once housed the Dutch royal family, and now serves as a military academy.
  • Stedelijk Museum Breda: This museum showcases contemporary art and exhibitions and explores the city’s history and heritage.
  • Grote Kerk: This stunning gothic church has been a historical landmark in Breda since the 15th century. It features beautiful stained glass windows and an impressive organ.
  • Breda’s Begijnhof: These charming houses date back to the 16th century and were once home to the city’s Beguines, who were Catholic laywomen. They are now designated as a UNESCO world heritage site.

Natural Attractions of Breda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Singel: This canal encircles the old town of Breda and features beautiful walking paths and stunning views of the city.
  • Valkenberg Park: This lush urban park is perfect for picnics, walks, and bike rides. It features a variety of gardens, a playground, and a petting zoo.
  • Mastbos: This 500-year-old forest is situated on the outskirts of Breda and is perfect for hiking, cycling, or horse riding. It also has several picnic areas and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Breda

  • Markdal Nature Reserve: This beautiful nature reserve is located between Breda and the Belgian border. It features stunning walking and cycling paths, beautiful meadows, and streams.
  • Wilhelmina State: This stunning state forest has beautiful hiking and cycling paths, and features a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Galderse Meren: A series of small lakes with cycling and walking paths surrounding them. Perfect for a day outdoors with a picnic.

Best Things to Do in Breda: Activities and Tours

  • City Canal Cruise: Relax and experience the beauty of Breda by taking a boat tour through its canals.
  • Escape Room Breda: Test your wits and team skills by solving puzzles in this escape room experience.
  • Breda City Tour: Join an experienced guide to discover the city’s rich history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Breda

Day 1: Visit Stedelijk Museum Breda and walk around the historic Grote Kerk and Begijnhof.

Day 2: Explore Breda Castle and its hidden gardens. Take a relaxing canal cruise through the city’s waterways.

Day 3: Rent a bicycle and explore the scenic views of Mastbos.

Day 4: Learn about the Dutch Royal family and military at the KMA.

Day 5: Take a trip to the nearby Van Gogh Village and learn about the famous artist’s life.

Day 6: Experience the city’s nightlife and bars in the bustling city center.

Day 7: End the week by visiting Valkenberg Park and walking around Singel for a perfect end to your trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Breda

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stoofvlees: A hearty beef stew made with rich beer gravy. Served with fries or bread.
  • Kroketten: Deep-fried croquettes filled with beef ragout or cheese.
  • Havermoutpap: A traditional Dutch breakfast of porridge made from oats, milk, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A Dutch spirit similar to gin. Best served ice-cold.
  • Trappist Beer: A type of Belgian beer brewed by Trappist monks.
  • Bredase Bier: Locally brewed beer with a rich history.

Where to Stay in Breda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Novotel Breda: A modern hotel located in Breda’s city center.
  • Bliss Hotel: A boutique hotel situated in the historic city center of Breda.
  • Stadshotel De Klok: A warm and welcoming hotel located in the heart of Breda.

What to Buy in Breda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Delftware: Traditional blue and white pottery that originated in nearby Delft.
  • Breda Beer: Take home some locally brewed beer as a souvenir and a taste of Breda.
  • Speculaas: A traditional Dutch spiced cookie that is delicious and perfect as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Breda

  • Breda’s people are very friendly and welcoming.
  • The Dutch are punctual, and you should always arrive on time for appointments and group events.
  • Smoking is not allowed in indoor public places, including restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit Breda

The best time to visit Breda is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warmer, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, Breda is an all-year-round destination, with something to offer throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Breda

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take advantage of the city’s walking and cycling routes.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in indoor public spaces.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas.
  • Forget to validate your public transport ticket.

Safety and Health

Safety in Breda

Breda is a safe city. However, you should take the necessary precautions such as locking your doors and keeping valuables safe. In case of an emergency, dial 112.

Health in Breda

Overall, Breda has a good healthcare system. Pharmacies are widely available, and hospitals are modern and equipped with the latest medical technology.

Getting to Breda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Breda

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: The closest major airport to Breda. Located 100 km North West of the city.
  • Eindhoven Airport: Located 53 km Southeast of Breda.
  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport: Located 81 km South of Breda.

Bus Stations Near Breda

  • Breda Bus Station: Situated in the city center and has regular national and international services.

Other Options

  • Train: Breda train station is well connected to major cities across the country.

How to Get Around in Breda

Breda is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Public transport options include buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Breda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Antwerp: A bustling Belgian city famous for its diamonds, chocolate, and medieval architecture.
  • Rotterdam: A futuristic city with world-renowned architecture, museums, and a vibrant nightlife.
  • Dordrecht: A peaceful canal-side town featuring beautiful architecture and centuries-old history.

Conclusion

Breda is a beautiful city with something for everyone. From its stunning historic landmarks to its natural beauty, vibrant culture, and amazing food scene, Breda is a destination not to be missed. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to make the most out of your visit to this charming Dutch city.
