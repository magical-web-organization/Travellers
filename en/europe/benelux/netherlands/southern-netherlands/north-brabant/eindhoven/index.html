Introduction to Eindhoven

Eindhoven is a dynamic, modern city situated in the southern part of the Netherlands, in the province of North Brabant. It’s known for its innovations in technology and high-tech industries as well as its lively cultural scene. Visitors to this Dutch city will find a fascinating mix of old and new, with plenty of museums, galleries, parks, and modern architecture to explore. In this comprehensive Eindhoven travel guide, we’ll showcase the best things to see and do in the city, including top attractions, food and drink scene, accommodations, and practical tips.

List of Places to Visit in Eindhoven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Van Abbemuseum

The Van Abbemuseum is a world-renowned museum of modern and contemporary art, situated in the heart of Eindhoven. The museum’s extensive collection includes works by Picasso, Chagall, Kandinsky, and other renowned artists, as well as contemporary pieces from up-and-coming artists. The Van Abbemuseum also hosts temporary exhibitions, talks, and workshops throughout the year.

DAF Museum

The DAF Museum is an homage to the Dutch car manufacturer, DAF, which made innovative and affordable cars in the mid-20th century. The museum showcases a collection of unique cars, trucks, and motorcycles, as well as interactive exhibitions that show how the DAF engine and technology worked.

Eindhoven Museum

The Eindhoven Museum is a great place to learn about the history and culture of the city. The open-air museum has a reconstruction of an Iron Age settlement, a medieval castle, a 1920s farmhouse, and more. Visitors can learn about traditional Dutch crafts and even try their hand at weaving or forging iron themselves.

Philips Museum

The Philips Museum celebrates the history of the city’s most famous industry, electronics giant Philips. The museum’s exhibits take visitors on a journey through the company’s groundbreaking technological inventions, from the first light bulbs to the latest cutting-edge innovations.

Natural Attractions of Eindhoven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Genneper Parks

Genneper Parks is a vast nature reserve that’s perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park features hiking and biking trails, lakes, streams, and forests. Visitors can also enjoy birdwatching and fishing, as well as explore a working watermill, a butterfly garden, and a petting zoo.

Stadswandelpark

Located in the center of Eindhoven, Stadswandelpark is a beautiful green space with walking paths, fountains, and sculptures. The park also has an open-air theater that hosts concerts and performances during the summer months.

Strabrechtse Heath

Strabrechtse Heath is a stunning heathland that’s home to a variety of wildlife, including rare birds and insects. Visitors can explore the park on foot or by bike and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eindhoven

Eindhoven is a fantastic destination for outdoor activities, with many scenic hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the top routes include:

  • The Groote Heide Route, which takes visitors through forests, heathland, and agricultural landscapes.

  • The Collse Watermill Route, which passes several picturesque watermills and through idyllic countryside.

  • The Philips van Lenneppark Route, which winds through a beautiful park with ponds, sculptures, and a historic windmill.

Best Things to Do in Eindhoven: Activities and Tours

Light Tour

Eindhoven is widely known as the “city of light,” thanks to its connection to the electronic firm Philips and its innovative lightbulbs. There are many ways to experience the city’s light installations, but the best way is on a guided Light Tour, which takes visitors on a journey through the most impressive light displays.

Brewery Tour

The local beer scene is booming in Eindhoven, and visitors can discover it through a brewery tour. The city is home to several craft beer breweries that offer tours, tastings, and workshops.

Street Art Tour

Eindhoven also has a thriving street art scene, with many colorful murals and graffiti pieces located throughout the city. Take a guided walking tour to discover some of the best street art, hear stories about the artists, and learn about the local art scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eindhoven

Day 1: Explore the city center, including the Philips Museum and Stadhuisplein.

Day 2: Visit the Van Abbemuseum and take a light tour of the city in the evening.

Day 3: Spend the day at Genneper Parks, enjoying nature and outdoor activities.

Day 4: Discover the DAF Museum and explore Strabrechtse Heath.

Day 5: Take a street art tour and visit Eindhoven’s modern architecture highlights.

Day 6: Discover the history of Eindhoven at the Eindhoven Museum and then head to one of the city’s craft beer breweries.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the city’s parks and gardens, such as Stadswandelpark or Philips van Lenneppark.

Food and Drink Scene of Eindhoven

Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine in Eindhoven is a mix of traditional Dutch dishes and international flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Stamppot: A Dutch classic made of mashed potatoes and vegetables, usually served with sausage or meatballs.

  • Bitterballen: Deep-fried meatballs filled with a ragout of meat and spices, usually served with mustard.

  • Poffertjes: Miniature, fluffy pancakes dusted with powdered sugar and usually served with butter and syrup.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, the Netherlands is famous for beer. In Eindhoven, try the local craft beers from breweries like Van Moll, DDW, and Stadsbrouwerij Eindhoven. Be sure to also try Dutch gin, or jenever, which is usually served as a shot and often flavored with juniper berries.

Where to Stay in Eindhoven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eindhoven offers a wide range of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • The Student Hotel: A hip, modern hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Inntel Hotels Art Eindhoven: A fashionable hotel with a unique, artistic design.

  • Blue Collar Hotel: A trendy, affordable hostel near the city center.

What to Buy in Eindhoven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Eindhoven can bring home a variety of unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Dutch cheese and stroopwafels (a type of waffle sandwich filled with caramel)

  • Dutch design items, such as lighting, furniture, and fashion accessories

  • Dutch gin, or jenever

  • A Philips lightbulb or other high-tech gadget

Local Culture and Customs of Eindhoven

The people of Eindhoven are friendly and welcoming, but like all Dutch people, they value their privacy and personal space. Directness and honesty are highly appreciated, so don’t be afraid to express your opinions. When greeting someone, a handshake is the norm, but close friends may offer a kiss on the cheek. It’s also important to respect the local customs and etiquette when visiting the city’s museums, galleries, and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Eindhoven

The best time to visit Eindhoven is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are many outdoor events and festivals. However, the city is also worth visiting during the winter months, when the Christmas markets, ice-skating rinks, and cozy cafés create a magical atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eindhoven

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages, such as stroopwafels, cheese, and Dutch gin.
  • Take a light tour of the city at night for a unique experience.
  • Take time to explore the city’s many museums and galleries.
  • Rent a bike and explore the parks and nature reserves around Eindhoven.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone speaks English; try to learn a few basic Dutch phrases.
  • Interruptions or loud behavior are frowned upon.
  • Smoke or litter in public places; the city prides itself on being clean and environmentally friendly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eindhoven

Eindhoven is a safe and secure city, but it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight, especially in crowded areas. It’s also crucial to follow traffic rules, especially when cycling, as there are many cyclists on the road in Eindhoven.

Health in Eindhoven

The health care system in the Netherlands is high-quality, and visitors can access medical assistance if needed. It’s essential to have comprehensive travel insurance, including coverage for medical expenses.

Getting to Eindhoven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eindhoven

Eindhoven Airport is the main airport serving the city and connects to many European destinations and beyond. Other nearby airports include Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, Rotterdam The Hague Airport, and Brussels Airport.

Bus Stations Near Eindhoven

Eindhoven is well-connected by a network of buses that connect it to other parts of the Netherlands and Europe. The main bus station in Eindhoven is located near the city center and provides connections to many destinations in the region.

Other Options

Other transportation options to get to Eindhoven include train and car. The city has good rail connections to other parts of the Netherlands and Europe, and driving is also an option, as the city is well-connected by highways.

How to Get Around in Eindhoven

Eindhoven is a very bike-friendly city, and cycling is one of the best ways to get around. There are many bike rental shops in the city center that offer affordable rates. Public transportation is also good, with buses and trains providing easy access to most parts of the city and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Eindhoven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eindhoven is a great starting point for exploring the Netherlands and neighboring countries. Some top destinations near Eindhoven include:

  • Amsterdam: The capital city of the Netherlands is just 1.5 hours away by train from Eindhoven.

  • Rotterdam: The port city of Rotterdam is known for its innovative architecture and vibrant cultural scene and is also easily accessible by train.

  • Antwerp: Located just across the border in Belgium, Antwerp is a charming, multicultural city known for its art, fashion, and design.

Conclusion

Eindhoven is a vibrant and dynamic city with much to offer visitors, from its innovative industries to its cultural attractions and natural beauty. With this comprehensive Eindhoven travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan an exciting and unforgettable trip to this Dutch gem. So pack your bags, rent a bike, and get ready to explore the best of Southern Netherlands.
