Introduction to Tilburg

If you’re looking for an authentic slice of the Netherlands, look no further than Tilburg, a vibrant city in North Brabant that’s full of personality, history, and culture. Tilburg’s roots date back to the 8th century, and the city’s history is rich with innovation, industry, and artistry.

With its central location in the Netherlands, Tilburg makes a great base for exploring the region, but it also has plenty to offer visitors in its own right. Whether you’re interested in Dutch art, natural beauty, or local cuisine, Tilburg has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Tilburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum De Pont: This modern art museum was founded in 1992 and is housed in a former wool factory. It’s home to a large collection of contemporary art from the Netherlands and beyond.

  • TextielMuseum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of textiles, which have been an important industry in the Tilburg area for centuries. Visitors can see demonstrations of textile production and explore interactive exhibits.

  • Willem II Stadium: Sports fans won’t want to miss a tour of this historic soccer stadium, which is home to the Willem II soccer team.

  • De Oliemeulen: This unique zoo is home to a wide variety of animals, including exotic birds, reptiles, and mammals.

Natural Attractions of Tilburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loonse en Drunense Duinen National Park: This national park is the largest sand drift area in Western Europe. Visitors can hike, bike, or horseback ride on the park’s many trails and enjoy beautiful views of the dunes.

  • Safaripark Beekse Bergen: This safari park is home to over 1500 animals, including big cats, giraffes, elephants, and rhinoceroses. Visitors can take a safari boat or bus tour to see the animals up close.

  • Efteling: This theme park features a fairy-tale setting, thrilling rides, and live shows, making it a great destination for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tilburg

  • The Kraaijenbergse Plassen: This scenic route takes visitors through natural wetlands and offers stunning views of the Kraaijenbergse Plassen, a series of lakes.

  • The Brabantse Biesbosch: This biking route takes visitors through the scenic Biesbosch National Park, which is home to hundreds of species of plants and animals.

Best Things to Do in Tilburg: Activities and Tours

  • Piet Plezier: This horse-drawn wagon tour takes visitors through the scenic countryside around Tilburg, with stops at local landmarks and attractions.

  • City Walks Tilburg: This guided walking tour takes visitors through the historic city center of Tilburg, with stops at landmarks and historic sites.

  • Tilburgse Bier Tour: This walking tour takes visitors to some of Tilburg’s best breweries and bars, with tastings of local beers and snacks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tilburg

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city center of Tilburg, including the Museum De Pont and the TextielMuseum.
Day 2 Visit the Loonse en Drunense Duinen National Park for a day of hiking and nature watching.
Day 3 Take a tour of the Willem II Stadium and explore Tilburg’s soccer culture.
Day 4 Visit the Efteling theme park for a day of thrilling rides and fairy-tale adventures.
Day 5 Take a safari tour of the Safaripark Beekse Bergen.
Day 6 Enjoy a bike tour of the Brabantse Biesbosch.

Food and Drink Scene of Tilburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Worstenbroodjes: These Dutch pastries are filled with seasoned sausage and can be found at many local bakeries.

  • Stoofvlees: This hearty Dutch stew is made with beef, beer, and spices and is a local favorite.

  • Pannenkoeken: These thin Dutch pancakes can be topped with a variety of sweet or savory ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Tilburgs Winterbier: This dark, rich beer is brewed locally and is a popular seasonal drink.

  • Tilburgse Schrobbelèr: This herbal liqueur is a popular local digestif.

Where to Stay in Tilburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Van der Valk Hotel Tilburg: This modern hotel is located a short drive from the city center and features comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  • Mercure Hotel Tilburg Centrum: This centrally located hotel offers comfortable rooms and a fitness center.

  • Boutique Hotel Villa Tilia: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic villa and features spacious rooms and a garden.

What to Buy in Tilburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Textiles: Handmade textiles are a specialty of the Tilburg area and can be found at local markets and shops.

  • Craft Beer: Tilburg has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors can find local beers at many bars and breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Tilburg

Tilburgers are known for their friendly, welcoming nature and their love of art and culture. The city has a long history of industry and innovation, and this is reflected in the local art and design scene.

Best Time to Visit Tilburg

The best time to visit Tilburg is from May to September, when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are at their best. The city is also known for its many festivals and events, such as the Tilburg Kermis, which takes place in July and is the largest fair in the Benelux region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tilburg

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, especially the craft beer and traditional dishes.

  • Explore the city’s art and culture scene.

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their permission.

  • Drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tilburg

Tilburg is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding deserted streets at night and keeping valuable items secure.

Health in Tilburg

Tilburg has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can access medical care at local hospitals and clinics. Make sure to have comprehensive travel insurance before you arrive.

Getting to Tilburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tilburg

The closest major airport to Tilburg is Amsterdam’s Schiphol Airport, which is about 1.5 hours away by train or car.

Bus Stations Near Tilburg

Tilburg has a central bus station with connections throughout the Netherlands and Europe.

Other Options

Tilburg is also easily accessible by car or train.

How to Get Around in Tilburg

Tilburg is a small city and is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Buses and taxis are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Tilburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Breda: This historic city is located just 30 minutes from Tilburg by train and features a lively nightlife scene.

  • Den Bosch: This beautiful medieval city is known for its charming architecture and is about 45 minutes from Tilburg by train.

  • Eindhoven: This innovative city is home to a thriving technology industry and is about 45 minutes from Tilburg by train.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in art, nature, or local culture, Tilburg has something to offer. With its friendly residents, historic landmarks, and beautiful landscapes, this vibrant city is a must-see destination for any traveler to the Netherlands.
