Introduction to Sittard

Sittard is a charming town located in South Limburg, in the southern part of the Netherlands. It offers a perfect blend of rich history and natural beauty. The town has a population of approximately 37,000 and is one of the oldest cities in the country, dating back to the Roman era. Sittard is known for its lively cultural events, stunning architecture, mouthwatering cuisine, and beautiful scenery. Plan a visit to Sittard and explore the best that this town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Sittard

Sittard is a small town, but it offers plenty of attractions to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the must-visit places in Sittard:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Het Domein: Het Domein is a cultural center that showcases contemporary art, local history, and archeology. This center hosts regular exhibitions and events, including concerts and lecture series.
  • St. Peter’s Church: St. Peter’s Church is a stunning 15th-century Gothic church that showcases beautiful architecture and artwork.
  • Museum Het Land van Sittard: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the history of Sittard and its surrounding region. It showcases artifacts, artwork, and multimedia exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Sittard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Munsterkerk Gardens: Munsterkerk Gardens provides a peaceful oasis in the heart of Sittard town and is the perfect place to unwind. Visitors can enjoy a picnic or relax and enjoy the flowers and fountains.
  • Maurits Park: Maurits Park is a beautiful park that is perfect for a picnic or leisurely walk. It features a pond and a playground for children.
  • De Geleenbeekdal: De Geleenbeekdal is a stunning nature reserve that offers scenic views and is a great location for hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sittard

Sittard is surrounded by breathtaking nature, and there are several hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of the most popular routes:

  • Pieterpad: The Pieterpad is a 498-km-long hiking route that runs from Pieterburen to Maastricht, passing through Sittard. This trail is suitable for experienced hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Green Route: This 8.5-km-long biking and hiking trail runs between Sittard and Geleen. It takes visitors through beautiful forests, scenic landscapes, and quaint villages.
  • Streekpad: This hiking route runs for 288 km and enters Sittard. It leads visitors around the picturesque South Limburg’s hillsides, and quaint villages.

Best Things to Do in Sittard: Activities and Tours

Sittard offers plenty of activities to keep visitors busy, and here are some of the best things to do in this town:

  • Shop at the Market Place: The town center houses a vibrant market every Friday that offers fresh produce, local crafts, cheese, and other goodies. It is perfect for tourists to discover the lifestyle of the locals.
  • Explore the City on Foot: Sittard’s beauty can be explored on foot, and it is a great way to discover hidden gems of this town. Visitors can stroll along its historic streets and discover its beautiful architecture.
  • Take a Canal Cruise: Canal cruise is a perfect way to explore the town from a different angle, providing unique photo opportunities. Gliding through the canals provides a great way to view the town’s scenery and its history.

Food and Drink Scene of Sittard

Sittard offers local cuisine and classic Dutch dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vlaai: Limburg province is famous for its Vlaai, a traditional pie. The pastry crust is usually filled with fruit, yeast, cream, or rice pudding.
  • Limburgse Mosterdsoep: Mustard soup is a popular dish in Limburg, which consists of creamy and tangy mustard broth with vegetables.
  • Friet speciaal: French fries (belgian fries), topped with mayonnaise, curry ketchup, and finely diced onions.

Local Beverages

  • Gulpener Beer: Gulpener beer is a locally brewed beer and is one of the best-known beers in the Netherlands.
  • Limonade: Limonade is a popular drink among children, and it is a non-alcoholic and fruity drink.

Where to Stay in Sittard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Sittard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sittard is known for its locally made souvenirs and local produce. Visitors can buy the following items as souvenirs or gifts:

  • Limburgse Vlaai: Limburgse Vlaai is a traditional pastry that makes a perfect souvenir for loved ones back home.
  • Stroopwafels: These are caramel-filled waffles, which are a favorite Dutch treat and make an excellent souvenir.
  • Beer from Gulpener Brewery: The locally brewed beer makes for an excellent gift for beer lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Sittard

Sittard is a welcoming town and visitors should be respectful of its cultural norms. Here are some of the local customs and etiquettes to keep in mind:

  • Greeting Etiquettes: It is customary to greet people with a handshake and direct eye contact.
  • Dress Code: Sittard doesn’t have a strict dress code. However, visitors are expected to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and museums.
  • Tipping Norms: Tipping isn’t mandatory in Sittard, but it is customary to leave a small tip for good service.

Best Time to Visit Sittard

The best time to visit Sittard is during the summer months (June to August) when the town experiences mild weather and plenty of sunshine. Visitors can make the most of events such as the cultural festival and the open-air markets that take place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sittard

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Sittard:

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Sittard has a unique food and drink scene, and visitors should try the local delicacies.
  • Explore on Foot: Visitors should explore Sittard town on foot and take in the beautiful architecture and hidden gems.
  • Be Respectful: Visitors should be respectful of Sittard’s local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Smoke in Public Places: Smoking in public places is prohibited in the Netherlands.
  • Don’t Forget to Carry Cash: While most places accept card payments, it is advisable to carry cash, especially when shopping at local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sittard

Sittard is one of the safest towns in the Netherlands. However, visitors should take care of their belongings and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Sittard

Sittard has excellent health facilities and services. Visitors should carry travel health insurance with them and avoid drinking tap water unless it is boiled or treated.
