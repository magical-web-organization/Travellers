Introduction to Heerlen

Heerlen, located in South Limburg, is one of the hidden gems of the Netherlands. This city boasts a unique mix of rich cultural heritage, idyllic natural vistas, and a vibrant modern lifestyle. Whether it’s your first time visiting Heerlen or you are a returning visitor, you are guaranteed to find something new and exciting to discover.

List of Places to Visit in Heerlen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Thermenmuseum: Experience the ancient Roman Baths in this museum built around the original baths ruins in Coriovallum Heerlen.
  • Slot Schaesberg: A partially reconstructed castle offers a glimpse into the lives of medieval noblemen!
  • Mijnmuseum: A museum built around a retired coalmine, showcasing the lives of miners and the history of coal mining in Limburg.

Natural Attractions of Heerlen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brunssummerheide: The vast natural landscape of heathlands, woodlands, and meadows, a great place for hiking, picnics, and beautiful sunsets.
  • Caumerbeek: If you are looking for a refreshing and tranquil retreat, the Caumerbeek is a stream with a walking trail that runs along its banks in the center of Heerlen.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Heerlen

  • Route langs Kastelen (Route along Castles): The route covers five castles and a variety of natural landscapes around Heerlen, making it a unique experience.
  • Mijnmonumentenroute (Coal Mines Monuments Route): The route takes visitors back the early 20th century, with a scenic landscape of coalmines monuments in Limburg.

Best Things to Do in Heerlen: Activities and Tours

  • Join a local guide and book a walking tour of the city to explore the streets and attractions of Heerlen.
  • Take a bike tour to explore the scenic beauty of South Limburg.
  • For adventure-seekers, there are plenty of thrilling activities, like bungee jumping, paintballing, go-karting, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Heerlen

Day 1: Arrive in Heerlen and check into your accommodations.

Day 2: Visit Slot Schaesberg Castle and explore the grounds.

Day 3: Visit Mijnmuseum and experience the history of coal mining in Limburg.

Day 4: Take a bike tour of the scenic beauty of South Limburg.

Day 5: Visit the Thermenmuseum and learn about the ancient Roman Baths.

Day 6: Hike the Brunssummerheide, a vast natural landscape of heathlands, woodlands, and meadows.

Day 7: Take a walking tour of the city to explore the streets and attractions of Heerlen.

Food and Drink Scene of Heerlen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rijstevlaai: Rice cake, a traditional sweet pie made with cinnamon and sugar.
  • Zuurvlees: Sour meats, slowly stewed beef in vinegar and brown sugar, served with a slice of bread.
  • Limburgse vlaai: A pastry filled with fruits or custard and covered with crumbled dough on top.

Local Beverages

  • Limburgse wijn: Limburg’s wine is fruity, crisp, and popular all over the country, especially the Pinot Gris and Riesling varieties.
  • Brand beer: A light lager beer made in Limburg brewery since 1340, it’s one of the oldest beer brands in the Netherlands.

Where to Stay in Heerlen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Kasteel Terworm, an elegant hotel located in a medieval castle, complete with a spa and a Michelin-starred restaurant.
  • Stayokay Hostel Heerlen, a budget-friendly hostel located near the city’s train station.

Local Culture and Customs of Heerlen

Locals in Heerlen are known for their warm and friendly attitude. The traditional Limburg dialect is still spoken by many, and the locals love to share their traditional crafts, cuisine, and cultural heritage with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Heerlen

Spring and Summer are the best times to visit Heerlen, between March and September. The weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy, like markets, festivals, and carnivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Heerlen

Do:

  • Respect the locals, their customs, and their property.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Enjoy the city’s cultural heritage, museums, and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Be disrespectful towards the locals or their beliefs.
  • Ignore the safety tips and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Heerlen

Heerlen is generally a safe and friendly city, but like any other city, it’s essential to take some precautions to avoid any risks. Be aware of pickpockets and scammers in crowded places and tourist attractions. Stay on the designated hiking and biking trails, and avoid venturing out in unfamiliar or poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Heerlen

The health care system is excellent in the Netherlands, and there are plenty of hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies in Heerlen. The tap water is safe to drink, and the city is relatively clean and hygienic.

Getting to Heerlen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Heerlen

The nearest international airport to Heerlen is Maastricht-Aachen Airport, located 22.4 km away from the city center. The best way to travel from the airport to Heerlen is by bus or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Heerlen

Heerlen has two bus stations, the Central Bus Station and the Station Heerlen Woonboulevard. Both stations are well-connected to all the major cities and towns of South Limburg and the Netherlands.

Other Options

If you are traveling from Europe, you can also take a train to Heerlen’s train station, which is well-connected to all the major cities and towns of the Netherlands and beyond.

How to Get Around in Heerlen

Heerlen is a small and walkable city, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. You can also rent a bike or a scooter or use public transportation to get around, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Heerlen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Maastricht: A vibrant university city with a mix of beautiful architecture, great shopping, and mouth-watering cuisine, located only 20 km from Heerlen.
  • Valkenburg aan de Geul: A charming tourist town with mystical underground caves, perched idyllically atop the rugged hills of South Limburg, less than 10 km from Heerlen.
  • National Park De Maasduinen: A beautiful nature reserve with rolling heathlands, sand dunes, and forests along the Meuse River, less than an hour’s drive from Heerlen.

Conclusion

Heerlen, South Limburg’s cultural gem, offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and modernity, making it a great travel destination. From stunning natural vistas to rich cultural heritage, outdoor activities to indulgent cuisine, Heerlen has something for everyone. Explore the city’s museums and historical attractions, hike the scenic Brunssummerheide, visit one of the local castles, and more. Experience the city’s welcoming and friendly atmosphere, and make memories that will last a lifetime.
