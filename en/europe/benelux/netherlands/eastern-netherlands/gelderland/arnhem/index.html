Introduction to Arnhem

Arnhem, located in the eastern Netherlands in the province of Gelderland, is a vibrant city that is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its iconic bridge and World War II associated battles, Arnhem is a city that is constantly evolving with a range of attractions, museums, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in exploring the culture, cycling through picturesque landscapes, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, Arnhem caters to every traveller’s needs.

List of Places to Visit in Arnhem

Arnhem boasts a diverse range of attractions that showcase the city’s history, culture, and nature. Here are some must-visit places in Arnhem:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Airborne Museum ‘Hartenstein’: This museum documents the heroic story of the Battle of Arnhem, where Allied troops were transported behind enemy lines in an attempt to secure a bridgehead. The museum also showcases the life of civilians under German occupation.
  • Netherlands Open Air Museum: This interactive museum takes you back in time, allowing you to experience how life was in the Netherlands in the past. Highlights include a historic tramline and traditional Dutch houses.
  • Historic Cellars: The historic cellars, which run under the city centre, bear testimony to the city’s rich history. These cellars were used as shelters during World War II and have now been turned into art galleries and chic boutiques.
  • Eusebius Church: This Gothic-style church has a rich history dating back to 1452. It has also served as a watchtower during World War II and is now a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Arnhem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Park Sonsbeek: This 67-hectare park is ideal for nature lovers. The park has picturesque landscapes, waterfalls, and walking trails. Visitors can also enjoy a picnic or a relaxing afternoon in one of the park’s many cafes.
  • Posbank: The Posbank is a striking natural area where visitors can enjoy miles of cycling and hiking trails. The area is characterized by heaths, dunes, meadows, and pine forests and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Burgers’ Zoo: For animal lovers, Burgers’ Zoo is a must-visit. It has a wide range of animal species, including elephants, lions, and zebras, and provides visitors with an experience of the animal kingdom.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arnhem

Arnhem’s natural beauty and landscapes make it an ideal destination for biking and hiking. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Arnhem:

  • Arnhem - Oosterbeek Route: This 15 km route offers beautiful views of nature and the river Rhine. It passes through historic sites such as the Airborne Museum and the Oosterbeek War Cemetery.
  • Veluwezoom National Park: This national park offers a variety of hiking and biking routes, ranging from beginner to advanced levels. The park also has a visitors’ centre that provides information about the park’s flora and fauna.
  • Kröller-Müller Museum Route: This 30 km route takes you from the centre of Arnhem to the Kröller-Müller Museum. The museum has an impressive collection of art, including works by Vincent Van Gogh, and is set in the stunning Hoge Veluwe National Park.

Best Things to Do in Arnhem: Activities and Tours

Apart from the popular attractions, Arnhem has a range of activities and tours that cater to every type of visitor:

  • GelreDome Stadium: The GelreDome Stadium, home to Vitesse football club, is a popular destination for sports enthusiasts. Visitors can take a tour of the stadium and learn about the club’s history.
  • Boat Tour of the Rhine: A boat tour of the Rhine is a great way to explore the city and its surroundings from a different perspective. You can take a leisurely river cruise, or a more adventurous high-speed boat tour.
  • Fashion Quarter: Arnhem’s fashion district is a must-visit for fashion lovers. It offers a range of boutique shops, vintage stores, and fashion museums.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arnhem

To make the most of your visit to Arnhem, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city centre and visit historic sites such as the Eusebius Church, the Grote Markt, and the historic cellars.
Day 2 Take a bike tour through Veluwezoom National Park and visit the Kröller-Müller Museum.
Day 3 Visit Burgers’ Zoo and the Netherlands Open Air Museum.
Day 4 Go on a shopping spree in Arnhem’s fashion district and enjoy a picnic in Park Sonsbeek.
Day 5 Take a boat tour of the Rhine, or a high-speed boat tour.
Day 6 Go on a hike in the Posbank area and visit the Airborne Museum.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine in one of Arnhem’s many cafes and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Arnhem

Arnhem has a thriving food and drink scene, with a range of local specialities and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Stamppot: This is a traditional Dutch dish made of mashed potatoes, vegetables, and meat.
  • Kroket: A snack made of meat, ragout, and bread crumbs, usually served with mustard.
  • Schnitzel: A thin, breaded and fried meat cutlet, often served with fries and salad.

Local Beverages:

  • Grolsch beer: A popular beer in the Netherlands, Grolsch has been brewed since 1615 and is a must-try when in Arnhem.
  • Wijn van Nederland: This wine made in the Netherlands is gaining popularity and is a must-try for wine enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Arnhem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arnhem has a range of accommodation options that cater to every budget and preference. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Modez: This design hotel is located in Arnhem’s fashion district and has 20 stylish rooms inspired by fashion designers.
  • Stayokay Hostel: This budget-friendly option is located in a national monument building and offers both dormitory and private rooms.
  • De Elegast: This guesthouse is located in a renovated monastery and offers a unique accommodation experience.

What to Buy in Arnhem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arnhem is known for its unique fashion creations, but there are also other local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some top options:

  • Arnhemse Meisjes: A traditional Dutch confectionary, Arnhemse Meisjes are crispy, thin cookies made with butter and sugar.
  • Delftware: This blue-and-white tin-glazed pottery has been made in the Netherlands since the 16th century.
  • Local Art and Crafts: Arnhem has a range of art and craft markets where you can find locally made pottery, jewellery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Arnhem

Arnhem’s culture is young and innovative, with a focus on sustainability, fashion, and design. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Biking culture: Arnhem is a bike-friendly city, and cycling is a preferred mode of transportation.
  • Fashion ethos: Arnhem is known for its avant-garde fashion industry, and fashion plays a significant role in the city’s cultural heritage.
  • People: The people of Arnhem are friendly and welcoming towards visitors, and English is widely spoken.

Best Time to Visit Arnhem

Arnhem has a temperate climate, with mild temperatures and moderate rainfall throughout the year. The best time to visit is during the summer months from June to August, where temperatures range from 18°C to 22°C, and the parks and outdoor spaces are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arnhem

Do:

  • Do cycle around the city and explore the picturesque landscapes.
  • Do try the local cuisine, such as stamppot and kroket.
  • Do visit the historic cellars and learn about the city’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry a raincoat or umbrella during the rainy season.
  • Don’t litter in the parks or the streets.
  • Don’t miss visiting the Airborne Museum, showcasing the heroic story of the Battle of Arnhem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arnhem

Arnhem is a safe city, and crime rates are relatively low. However, like any other city, it pays to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions such as locking your car and securing your valuables.

Health in Arnhem

Arnhem has good healthcare facilities, and emergency services are readily available. Visitors from outside the EU are advised to obtain health insurance before travel.

Getting to Arnhem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arnhem is easily accessible by road, rail, and air, and has a range of transportation options to choose from.

Airports Near Arnhem

The nearest airport to Arnhem is the Amsterdam Airport Schiphol, which is around an hour and a half away by train. Other options include Weeze Airport in Germany, and Eindhoven Airport.

Bus Stations Near Arnhem

Arnhem has a range of bus stations, with services to and from major cities in the Netherlands and neighbouring countries. The central bus station is located next to the train station.

Other Options

Arnhem’s train station is well-connected, with direct services to major cities in the Netherlands and neighbouring countries. Additionally, taxis and car rentals are readily available.

How to Get Around in Arnhem

There are several options for getting around in Arnhem, including:

  • Bike: Arnhem is a bike-friendly city, and cycling is a popular means of transportation.
  • Bus: Arnhem has a network of buses that connects the city centre to other areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available, and pricing is regulated by the government.
  • Car Rental: There are several car rental options available for visitors who prefer to drive themselves.

Where to Go Next from Arnhem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arnhem is conveniently located and makes an excellent base for exploring other parts of the Netherlands. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Utrecht: Located in the heart of the Netherlands, Utrecht is a historic city with a beautiful canal district and a vibrant cultural scene.
  • Hoge Veluwe National Park: This stunning national park is home to diverse flora and fauna, offering visitors the opportunity to experience forests, heaths, and sand drifts.
  • Amsterdam: The capital of the Netherlands is a short train ride from Arnhem, offering visitors a range of cultural, historical, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Arnhem is a destination that has something to offer for everyone. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes, Arnhem is a must-visit city when exploring the Netherlands. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a culture lover, Arnhem will not disappoint.
