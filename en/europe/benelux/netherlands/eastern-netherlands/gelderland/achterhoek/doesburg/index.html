Introduction to Doesburg

Located in Achterhoek, Gelderland, Eastern Netherlands, Doesburg is a charming town known for its historic sites, natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Doesburg has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and local customs and culture of Doesburg.

List of Places to Visit in Doesburg

Doesburg offers a range of attractions for travelers to explore. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, you’ll find it all in this lovely town. Here’s a list of some must-visit places in Doesburg.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Doesburg’s Mustard Museum - learn about the history of mustard production in Doesburg and enjoy a tasting session

  2. Lalique Museum - explore the world of French jewelry designer René Lalique at this museum located in a beautiful 19th-century villa

  3. Doesburg Fortress - a well-preserved 17th-century fortress that was once used to defend the town

  4. Martinikerk - one of the oldest churches in the Netherlands, dating back to the 15th century

Natural Attractions of Doesburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hoge Linde Estate - a beautiful estate with a forest, gardens, and a castle that dates back to the 17th century

  2. Veluwezoom National Park - located just outside of Doesburg, this park is perfect for hiking, biking, and wildlife watching

  3. IJssel River - enjoy a boat tour on the IJssel River and take in the scenic views of the surrounding countryside

Hiking/Biking Routes of Doesburg

Doesburg is surrounded by beautiful hiking and biking trails, perfect for nature lovers. Here are some of the best routes to explore.

  1. Ooijpolder Route - a 35-kilometer bike route that takes you through scenic countryside and along the banks of the Waal River

  2. Veluwezoom National Park Route - a 30-kilometer bike route that takes you through forests, heathlands, and sand dunes

  3. Doesburg City Walk - a 5-kilometer walking tour that takes you through Doesburg’s historic city center

Best Things to Do in Doesburg: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Doesburg. Here are some of the best.

  1. Doesburg Beer Tour - explore the local beer scene and taste some of the town’s best brews

  2. Candlelight Tour of Doesburg Fortress - take a nighttime tour of the fortress, lit by candles

  3. Cheese Tasting at Packhuys - sample some of the best cheeses from the region at this historic cheese shop

Week-Long Itinerary for Doesburg

If you’re planning on spending a week in Doesburg, here’s a suggested itinerary.

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Doesburg and check into your accommodation. Take a city walk and explore the historic city center
Day 2 Visit the Lalique Museum and enjoy lunch at one of the local restaurants
Day 3 Take a bike tour of Veluwezoom National Park
Day 4 Explore Doesburg’s Mustard Museum and visit Martinikerk
Day 5 Take a boat tour on the IJssel River
Day 6 Visit the Hoge Linde Estate and enjoy a picnic in the gardens
Day 7 Take a day trip to nearby Zutphen or Arnhem

Food and Drink Scene of Doesburg

Doesburg is known for its delicious local cuisine, particularly its cheeses, sausages, and mustard. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Doesburgse mosterdsoep - a creamy mustard soup, perfect for a chilly day

  2. Doesburger pannenkoek - a savory pancake with bacon, cheese, and mustard

  3. Achterhoekse krentenwegge - a sweet raisin bread that’s perfect for breakfast or with coffee

Local Beverages

  1. Doesburgse bier - a local beer brewed by the Doesburgsche Stoombleekerij

  2. Achterhoekse wijn - a regional wine from vineyards in the nearby area

  3. Doesburgse mosterdbrandewijn - a mustard liqueur that’s both sweet and spicy

  1. Grand Boutique Hotel - a luxury hotel housed in a 17th-century building

  2. Hotel Doesburg - a cozy hotel in the historic city center

  3. Stadshotel Doesburg - a budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms

What to Buy in Doesburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some suggestions.

  1. Doesburgse mosterd - pick up some of the town’s famous mustard to take home

  2. Doesburgse mosterdsaus - a creamy mustard sauce that’s perfect for dipping or as a condiment

  3. Achterhoekse wijn - bring home a bottle of regional wine to enjoy later

Local Culture and Customs of Doesburg

Doesburg has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions still maintained today. Here are some local customs and cultural practices to keep in mind.

  1. The Doesburgse Kwèkertjes - a local choir that wears traditional costumes and performs at various events throughout the year

  2. Sinterklaas - a Dutch holiday celebrated in December, with parades, feasts, and gift-giving

  3. Carnival - celebrated in February or March, with festivities and colorful parades

Best Time to Visit Doesburg

The best time to visit Doesburg is during the spring and summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also charming during the winter months, with holiday markets and cozy cafes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Doesburg

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Doesburg.

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine, particularly the cheese, sausages, and mustard

  2. Visit the local museums and historical sites to learn about the town’s history

  3. Explore the outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty of the area

Don’t:

  1. Assume everyone speaks English - it’s always polite to learn a few basic phrases in Dutch

  2. Forget to try the local beer and wine

  3. Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Doesburg is generally a safe and healthy place to visit. However, it’s always important to take basic precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Doesburg

  1. Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas

  2. Only use licensed taxi services

  3. Follow local traffic rules and be cautious when biking or walking near busy roads

Health in Doesburg

  1. The tap water in Doesburg is safe to drink

  2. If you have any medical issues or concerns, seek medical attention from a local doctor or hospital

  3. Make sure to bring any necessary prescription medications with you

Getting to Doesburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Doesburg is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider.

Airports Near Doesburg

  1. Amsterdam Schiphol Airport is the nearest international airport, located approximately 90 kilometers away

  2. Eindhoven Airport is another nearby option, located approximately 97 kilometers away

Bus Stations Near Doesburg

  1. Doesburg Bus Station is located in the city center and offers connections to various nearby towns and cities

  2. Arnhem Bus Station is another option, located approximately 20 kilometers away

Other Options

  1. Train - Doesburg has a train station with connections to various nearby towns and cities

  2. Car - Doesburg is easily accessible by car, with good road connections to nearby cities and towns

How to Get Around in Doesburg

Doesburg is a small town, and it’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. There are also buses and taxis available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Doesburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore further afield, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering.

  1. Zutphen - a charming medieval town located approximately 15 kilometers from Doesburg

  2. Arnhem - a larger city with many historical sites and cultural attractions, located approximately 20 kilometers away

  3. Veluwe National Park - a stunning nature reserve located just outside of Arnhem, with beautiful forests, heathlands, and sand dunes

Conclusion

Doesburg is a hidden gem in the Eastern Netherlands, with a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Doesburg has something to offer. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Doesburg and explore all the town has to offer.
