Introduction to Berkelland

Nestled in the Achterhoek region of Gelderland in Eastern Netherlands, Berkelland is a picturesque destination that offers a unique combination of culture, nature, and delicious food. It’s a perfect destination for those who want to escape the crowds and immerse themselves in the tranquil atmosphere of the Dutch countryside. With its charming windmills, lush green forests, blooming fields, and winding rivers, Berkelland is sure to impress any nature lover.

List of Places to Visit in Berkelland

Here are some of the best places to visit in Berkelland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum De Scheper - This fascinating museum showcases the history of Eibergen and the surrounding area, including exhibits on agriculture, crafts, and everyday life.
  • Farm Museum Erve Brooks - Situated in the beautiful landscape of Gelselaar village, this museum is a great place to learn about traditional Dutch farm life.
  • Water Mill De Olliemölle - Built in the 16th century, this working water mill is a popular tourist attraction and a great place to see traditional Dutch milling in action.

Natural Attractions of Berkelland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loohuisbos Forest - This serene forest offers excellent hiking and biking trails, and it’s also popular among birdwatchers.
  • Zwillbrocker Venn - Located just across the border in Germany, this wetland reserve is home to a large population of flamingos.
  • De Lebbenbrugge - Set in a picturesque 18th-century farmhouse, De Lebbenbrugge provides visitors with an authentic glimpse into the traditional way of life in the Dutch countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berkelland

Berkelland is a paradise for nature lovers, with numerous hiking and biking routes that traverse the stunning landscapes of the region. Some of the most popular trails include the Achterhoekse Wandelvierdaagse, the Kastelenroute, and the Berkelland route.

Best Things to Do in Berkelland: Activities and Tours

  • Berkelland Ballooning - Experience the serene beauty of the countryside from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Canoeing on the Berkel River - Take a relaxing paddle down the Berkel River, and enjoy the picturesque scenery along the way.
  • Vintage Car Tour - Explore the charming towns and villages of Berkelland in style with a vintage car tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Berkelland

Day 1: Explore Eibergen, visit the Museum De Scheper, and take a bike ride through the Loohuisbos Forest. Day 2: Visit the charming village of Gelselaar, explore the Farm Museum Erve Brooks, and take a stroll through De Lebbenbrugge. Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful landscape of Berkelland. Day 4: Experience the stunning natural beauty of Zwillbrocker Venn and the surrounding areas. Day 5: Go canoeing on the Berkel River. Day 6: Embark on a vintage car tour of the region, taking in the picturesque countryside and charming villages. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products in local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Berkelland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pannenkoeken - These delicious Dutch pancakes can be enjoyed sweet or savory and are a popular snack or meal.
  • Krentenwegge - A sweet bread with raisins, often served at breakfast.
  • Achterhoekse Slagersworst - A smoked and cured beef sausage, popular throughout the Gelderland region.

Local Beverages

  • Berkellands Bier - This local beer is brewed using traditional techniques and high-quality ingredients.
  • Rhubarb Wine - A refreshing and fruity wine made from locally grown rhubarb.

Where to Stay in Berkelland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Restaurant De Hoofdige Boer - This family-run hotel offers comfortable rooms and an excellent restaurant serving traditional Dutch cuisine.
  • Bed & Breakfast Erve Bruggert - Set in a picturesque 18th-century farmhouse, this charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Camping Den Blanken - A peaceful campsite in the heart of Berkelland, offering both tent and caravan pitches.

What to Buy in Berkelland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Achterhoekse Slagersworst - This delicious local sausage makes the perfect gift for foodies.
  • Berkenkoning - A locally produced liquor made from birch trees.
  • Bollenkruid - A traditional mix of herbs and spices used to make stews and soups.

Local Culture and Customs of Berkelland

Berkelland is deeply rooted in tradition and folklore, with a strong sense of community and collective identity. One of the most notable customs is the annual Berkellandse Paasvuur, a large Easter bonfire that’s held throughout the region. Other customs include the Sinterklaas celebration, the Zwarte Cross festival, and various local fairs and markets.

Best Time to Visit Berkelland

The best time to visit Berkelland depends on your preferences. For nature lovers, the spring and summer months offer the best weather and the chance to see local flora and fauna in full bloom. For those interested in local festivals and cultural events, winter and fall can be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berkelland

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks, they are known for their high quality and rich flavors.
  • Do take your time to explore the charming villages and towns of Berkelland.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothes, this is a rural area and the evenings can get chilly.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations or advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berkelland

Berkelland is considered a very safe destination, with low crime and low levels of risk in general. However, it is always important to take the necessary precautions and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Berkelland

The health system in the Netherlands is of a high standard. Berkelland has several hospitals and clinics that provide excellent medical care to both locals and visitors.

Getting to Berkelland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Berkelland

The nearest international airport to Berkelland is Schiphol Airport in Amsterdam, which is approximately a 2-hour drive away. From there, you can either rent a car or take public transportation to Berkelland.

Bus Stations Near Berkelland

Berkelland is well-connected to nearby cities and towns via public transportation. The nearest bus station is in Eibergen, which is served by regional bus lines.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a taxi are also good transportation options to move around the region.

How to Get Around in Berkelland

The best way to explore Berkelland is by bike or on foot. The region has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that provide easy access to the natural beauty of the area.

Where to Go Next from Berkelland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Arnhem - The capital of Gelderland, known for its rich cultural heritage, historic sites, and designer fashion.
  • Deventer - A charming town situated on the banks of the IJssel River, famous for its medieval architecture and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Zutphen - A medieval town with beautiful historic buildings, museums, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Berkelland is a hidden gem in Eastern Netherlands, offering visitors a unique combination of culture, nature, and delicious cuisine. With its picturesque villages, stunning natural landscapes, and rich cultural heritage, Berkelland is perfect for anyone seeking a peaceful and authentic Dutch experience.
