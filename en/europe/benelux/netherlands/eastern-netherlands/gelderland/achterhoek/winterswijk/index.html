Introduction to Winterswijk

Welcome to Winterswijk, a charming town located in the Eastern Netherlands region of Achterhoek. This idyllic countryside town is famous for its diverse natural landscapes, historical sites, cultural attractions, and excellent hiking and biking trails. Winterswijk is a perfect destination for families, couples, and solo travelers who want to explore the lesser-known parts of the Netherlands. In this guide, we will take you on a journey through Winterswijk and all the best it has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Winterswijk

Here are the best places to visit in Winterswijk:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Villa Mondriaan Museum - This museum showcases the works of Dutch artist Piet Mondriaan, who lived in Winterswijk during his early years.
  2. Nationaal Onderduiksmuseum - This museum presents the stories of Dutch people who went into hiding during World War II to escape Nazi persecution.
  3. Boogie Woogie Cultural Centre - This centre hosts various cultural events like music concerts, dance performances, exhibitions, and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Winterswijk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Korenburgerveen Nature Reserve - This nature reserve boasts of unique flora and fauna species and is perfect for a relaxing or adventurous (hiking or biking) day out.
  2. Bredevoort Book Town - A picturesque village with historic buildings and multiple bookstores to explore.
  3. Ratumse Beek Nature Reserve - A small nature reserve, perfect for birdwatchers and those seeking a tranquil walk alongside a beautiful stream.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winterswijk

Winterswijk offers a plethora of well-marked hiking and biking trails that are perfect for adventurers or nature enthusiasts. Here are the top three routes:

  1. Bocholt - Unna Cycle Route - A 185 km long cycle route, connecting the Netherlands and Germany.
  2. Ahaus-Alstätten-Winterswijk - A picturesque cycling route snaking through forests, villages, and farmlands of the region.
  3. Barchemse Berg and Lochemse Berg - A 20km hiking trail through rolling hills and forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Winterswijk: Activities and Tours

  1. Rent a Bike and Explore the Region - Rent a bike and explore the stunning scenery of Winterswijk at your own pace.
  2. Attend a Live Concert or Event - Check out Boogie Woogie Cultural Centre’s events schedule and attend a live concert or exhibition.
  3. Go on a Wine Tasting Tour - Take a wine tasting tour to experience the unique flavors of the Achterhoek and learn more about the region’s wine heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Winterswijk

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Poffertjes - Mini Dutch pancakes served with butter and powdered sugar.
  2. Stroopwafel - Two thin layers of waffles with a sticky syrup filling commonly served at the fairground and along the streets.
  3. Oliebollen - Deep-fried balls of batter dusted with powdered sugar and traditionally eaten during winter and new year festivities.

Local Beverages

  1. Winterswijks Geweten - A fruity, blonde beer produced in Winterswijk’s local brewery.
  2. Achterhoeks Bessenjenever - A gin made from local berries that are infused with juniper berries and sugar.

Where to Stay in Winterswijk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Stad Munster - A luxurious four-star hotel located in the town center.
  2. De Ruisende Aar - A family-run hotel famous for its tranquil location and garden views.
  3. Erve Kots - A rustic, farmhouse-style bed and breakfast on the outskirts of Winterswijk.

What to Buy in Winterswijk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Achterhoekse Wijnen - Local wines and liqueurs made from grapes cultivated in the region.
  2. Bredevoortse kasseien - Small cobblestones used to pave streets and alleys in the Bredevoort village.
  3. Winterswijkse krentenwegge - A local pastry made with raisins and flour.

Local Culture and Customs of Winterswijk

Winterswijk is known for its traditional Dutch customs and festivities. Here are a few traditions and customs:

  1. Easter Fires - A tradition of lighting large bonfires to welcome the arrival of spring, usually on Easter Sunday.
  2. St. Nicholas celebrations - A festival that occurs annually on December 5th, where people exchange gifts, visit each other’s homes, and enjoy traditional sweets like pepernoten and kruidnoten.
  3. Carnaval - A festival held in February that involves parades in the streets and colorful costumes.

Best Time to Visit Winterswijk

The best time to visit Winterswijk is either during spring and summer, from March to September, as this period offers pleasant weather, outdoor activities, and local festivities. Winter months can be cold, with occasional snowfall and may limit outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winterswijk

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Explore the countryside and make the most of the hiking and biking trails.
  3. Try the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in nature reserves or public places.
  2. Smoke in public places.
  3. Assume everyone speaks English; try to learn and use some Dutch phrases.

Safety and Health

Safety in Winterswijk

Winterswijk is considered safe for tourists. However, as with any tourist destination, it is advisable to be cautious of pickpockets in busy areas and always keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Winterswijk

Winterswijk has good medical facilities and clinics. Pharmacies are commonly available and are usually marked with a green cross symbol.

Getting to Winterswijk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Winterswijk

The nearest airports to Winterswijk are:

  1. Flughafen Weeze, Germany (40 km)
  2. Münster Osnabrück International Airport, Germany (80 km)
  3. Airport Eindhoven, the Netherlands (102 km)

Bus Stations Near Winterswijk

Winterswijk Bus Station is located in the town center and is well connected to various destinations across the Netherlands and Germany. Additionally, there are several local bus services available that connect Winterswijk to the surrounding towns and villages.

Other Options

Train services are available to Winterswijk, with the nearest station being Winterswijk West Station.

How to Get Around in Winterswijk

The best way to explore Winterswijk is on foot, bike, or car. Buses and trains connect Winterswijk with neighboring towns and cities, and taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Winterswijk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some popular nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Deventer - A beautiful nearby Hanseatic town famous for its lively streets, historic architecture, and rich history.
  2. Arnhem - A vibrant city with plenty of cultural and historical attractions, including the famous Burgers Zoo.
  3. Zutphen - A lovely historic town with an impressive museum and picturesque streets and canals.

Conclusion

We hope this guide has provided you with all the information necessary to plan a memorable trip to Winterswijk. This countryside town has something for everyone, offering a chance to experience the best of Dutch culture, indulge in outdoor activities, taste local cuisines, and relax in scenic surroundings.
