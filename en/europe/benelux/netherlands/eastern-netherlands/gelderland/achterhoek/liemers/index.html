Introduction to Liemers

Liemers is a culturally rich land in Eastern Netherlands, situated in the region of Achterhoek, Gelderland. It is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, historical buildings, and traditional customs. The region is composed of seven municipalities: Duiven, Westervoort, Zevenaar, Rijnwaarden, Doesburg, Montferland, and Lingewaard. Liemers has a diverse mix of small towns and cities, each with its unique character and charm.

List of Places to Visit in Liemers

Here are some of the must-visit places in Liemers:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Huis Bergh - a stunning castle in the city of ‘s-Heerenberg that houses an impressive collection of art and armory.
  • The Netherlands Open Air Museum - an authentic open-air museum in Arnhem that showcases the history and culture of the Netherlands through old houses, farms, and windmills.
  • St. Martin’s Church - a historic church in the town of Doesburg, noted for its Gothic architecture and beautiful stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Liemers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Veluwezoom National Park - a paradise for nature lovers, located just outside of Liemers, with a vast landscape of forests, moors, and hills.
  • Posbank - a scenic hill that provides a stunning panoramic view of the entire Liemers area.
  • Park Sonsbeek - a lovely city park in Arnhem that offers various activities like hiking, biking, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liemers

Liemers is home to several exciting hiking and biking trails, such as:

  • River walk on the Rhine - a beautiful path that follows the Rhine River through several Liemers towns, including Doesburg, Westervoort, and Zevenaar.
  • Cycle route through Montferland - a scenic trail that explores the forested hills of Montferland.
  • Walking route in Park Lingezegen - a peaceful route in Elst’s nature park that is perfect for experiencing Liemers’ greenery.

Best Things to Do in Liemers: Activities and Tours

  • Kröller-Müller Museum bicycle tour - a fun and informative way to explore the stunning Kröller-Müller Museum, which boasts the second-largest collection of Van Gogh’s paintings in the world.
  • Sailing trips on the Rhine river - a relaxing, yet adventurous way to enjoy Liemers’ riverside views.
  • Wine-tasting tour at Wijnhoeve De Veluwe - a unique experience to savour the delicious wine produced by Wijnhoeve De Veluwe, one of the few Dutch wine estates.

Food and Drink Scene of Liemers

Liemers has a rich culinary scene that comprises of traditional Dutch cuisine and several international options.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot - a hearty Dutch dish made from mashed potatoes, kale, and sausage.
  • Poffertjes - delicious mini pancakes that are served hot and covered with powdered sugar.
  • Bitterballen - crispy fried meatballs that are often enjoyed as a savoury snack with a cold beer.

Local Beverages

  • Grolsch beer - a famous beer brewed in the village of Groenlo.
  • Jenever - a Dutch gin that is commonly served as an aperitif.
  • Apple juice - the Liemers region is known for its high-quality apple juice that is refreshing and healthy.

Where to Stay in Liemers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Liemers offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and needs. Here are some suggestions:

  • Landgoed Montferland Hotel - a luxurious hotel situated amidst beautiful forests and hills of Montferland.
  • Stayokay Arnhem - a comfortable hostel located in the heart of Arnhem’s city center.
  • Hotel Gieling Duiven - an affordable, yet stylish hotel located in the town of Duiven.

What to Buy in Liemers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Liemers is known for its beautiful handcrafted items and local products. Here are some things to consider buying as a souvenir:

  • Bronze statues by Oswald Wenckebach - the famous Dutch sculptor, Oswald Wenckebach, was born in Doesburg, and his bronze statues are available for purchase in several Liemers souvenir shops.
  • Apple juice and cider - Liemers is renowned for its apple orchards, and buying apple juice or cider from one of the local producers is an excellent way to bring a little bit of Liemers home with you.
  • Handmade wooden clogs - wooden clogs are an iconic Dutch symbol, and several local shops in Liemers sell handmade wooden clogs.

Local Culture and Customs of Liemers

Liemers is known for its open-mindedness and welcoming nature. Dutch customs such as shaking hands, making eye contact, being punctual, and keeping an appropriate distance during a conversation are considered important here. The region is proud of its musical heritage, with many famous Dutch musicians hailing from the area.

Best Time to Visit Liemers

The best time to visit Liemers is between May and September when the weather is pleasant, and several outdoor activities and events will keep the tourists engaged.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liemers

Do:

  • Try cycling or walking through nature reserves - Liemers has several beautiful nature reserves that should not be missed.
  • Visit the local markets - experiencing bustling local markets in different towns and cities of Liemers is a great way to explore the local culture.
  • Try traditional Dutch food - sampling some of the delicious and hearty traditional Dutch foods should be on every tourist’s to-do list.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather - even in the summer, the weather can be unpredictable, and tourists should come prepared with suitable clothing and footwear.
  • Forget to carry cash - not all shops and restaurants may accept credit cards, and carrying cash may save the tourists from inconvenience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liemers

Liemers is a safe and peaceful region, but tourists should take normal precautions to avoid petty crimes such as pickpocketing. It is also essential to familiarize oneself with the local traffic rules before cycling on the roads.

Health in Liemers

Liemers has excellent health facilities, with several hospitals and clinics located nearby. Tourists requiring medical attention can get immediate help through the country’s efficient emergency system.

Getting to Liemers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liemers

Liemers is primarily served by the Weeze Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car. Several other airports, including the Amsterdam Airport Schiphol and the Rotterdam The Hague Airport, are also located within a few hours’ drive from Liemers.

Bus Stations Near Liemers

The regional bus services are well-connected, with several buses running throughout Liemers and to nearby regions, including Arnhem and Nijmegen.

Other Options

Tourists can also take trains from different Dutch cities to reach Liemers. The nearest train stations are located in Arnhem, Zevenaar, and Doetinchem.

How to Get Around in Liemers

Cycling is the most popular way to explore Liemers, with several bike rental facilities located in nearby towns and cities. Public transport, including buses, and trains, is also an excellent way to get around the region. Taxis are also readily available and can be a convenient option for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Liemers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Liemers is an excellent central location with many nearby cities and towns that are worth exploring. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Nijmegen - a lively student city with a charming old town and several parks.
  • Arnhem - a city situated on the banks of Rhine that is famous for its fashion industry and WWII history.
  • Deventer - a historic town with well-maintained buildings, diverse festivals, and markets.

Conclusion

Liemers is a charming region that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, great food and drink options and several activities to do. Travellers are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this lovely corner of the Netherlands.
