Introduction to Veluwezoom National Park

Welcome to Veluwezoom National Park, one of the most beautiful natural reserves in the Netherlands. Nestled in Gelderland, in Eastern Netherlands, Veluwezoom National Park is a fabulous getaway destination, featuring a stunning blend of dense woodlands, heaths, sand drifts, and beautiful wildlife. Here’s your comprehensive guide to experiencing the beauty of Veluwezoom National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Veluwezoom National Park

Whether you’re planning a hiking or biking trip, bird-watching, or wildlife safari, Veluwezoom National Park has something to offer everyone. Here are the must-visit places in the park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Veluwsche Stoomtrein Maatschappij: This historical steam train service takes you on a trip back in time with its charming restored locomotives and vintage coaches.
  • Castle Rosendael: Castle Rosendael is a glorious example of a Dutch 18th-century country house and gardens. It’s an excellent place to learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage.
  • The Kröller-Müller Museum: This world-famous museum houses one of the largest collections of 19th and 20th-century art and sculptures.
  • Het Nationaal Park De Hoge Veluwe: This 5,500-hectare park features a diverse landscape of woodland, heathland, and sand drifts. You can explore its rich wildlife on a guided tour.

Natural Attractions of Veluwezoom National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Posbank: Located the highest point in the park, Posbank provides visitors with spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Veluwezoom Forest: This vast forest features several walking and cycling trails that wind through stunning natural scenery. It’s famous for its ancient oak groves and sand drifts.
  • The Rhederlaag Lagoon: This crystal-clear lagoon spans over 300 hectares and is a haven for bird-watchers, fishing, and water sports enthusiasts.
  • The Loenermark Heathlands: The area is known for its purple heathlands which bloom in the late summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Veluwezoom National Park

The Park boasts several walking and cycling trails suitable for nature lovers, photography and hiking enthusiasts. Some of the recommended routes include:

  • The Veluwezoom Hiking Trail: This trail elevates hikers to the slopes of the Posbank, where they can enjoy magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Rhederlaag Cycle Path: This scenic cycling path takes tourists around the Rhederlaag Lagoon. It is 30 km long and is ideal for both beginners and professional cyclists.
  • The Old Beekhuizen Hike: The route is the perfect blend of beauty and wonder with places such as ‘De Bloedsteen’, ‘Koningsberg’, ‘De Plaggenhut’ and ‘The Princess Tree’.

Best Things to Do in Veluwezoom National Park: Activities and Tours

Veluwezoom National Park offers endless possibilities for adventure, relaxation, and exploration. Here are some of the things activities you can do in the park:

  • Wildlife Safari: Join a guided safari tour and see the remarkable wildlife of Veluwezoom National Park.
  • Horse Riding: You can explore the park’s landscape on horseback while discovering the scenic beauty of the forest.
  • Star Gazing: The park boasts one of the darkest skies in the Netherlands, making it an ideal spot for stargazing.

Food and Drink Scene of Veluwezoom National Park

The park features an impressive range of culinary diversity, drawing inspiration from international cuisines and local traditions. Here’s what you must try while visiting:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot: A traditional Dutch dish of mashed potatoes and vegetables with sausage or bacon.
  • Dutch Herring: A traditional herring fish dish served with onions and pickles.
  • Kroket: A fried meat and flour-filled snack that is famous all over the Netherlands.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The Netherlands’ most famous beer, Heineken, is widely available throughout Veluwezoom National Park.
  • Jenever: A Dutch alcohol made from juniper berries and other grains.

The park offers an array of accommodation options to travelers, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses. Some of the suggested accommodations include:

  • Hotel de Bilderberg: A luxurious and traditional-style hotel featuring top-of-the-line facilities and upscale amenities.
  • Stayokay Hostel Arnhem: A budget-friendly hostel located near the Veluwezoom Forest that offers affordable rooms for travelers.
  • Chalets op de Veluwe: A cozy countryside holiday home perfect for those who want to relax in the natural setting of the park.

What to Buy in Veluwezoom National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Park boasts plenty of authentic and handmade products that make wonderful souvenirs after your trip. Here are some of the products that you should consider purchasing:

  • Wooden Cutlery: Hand-carved wooden cutlery is sold at various shops in the park and makes for a perfect gift.
  • Dutch Cheese: You’ll find a vast assortment of cheeses, ranging from traditional Dutch Gouda to unique local flavors.
  • Local Honey: Unique blends of natural honey sold in the park’s shops make for a perfect souvenir to bring back home to your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Veluwezoom National Park

The park’s culture and customs date back to the 13th century when the forests were primarily used by hunters and farmers. Today, the park is a peaceful sanctuary for wildlife, and visitors can experience the culture and customs firsthand.

Best Time to Visit Veluwezoom National Park

The park is accessible all year round, each season bringing a unique and exquisite experience.

Summer (June-August) is perfect for outdoor activities, and the wildlife is usually more active. Autumn (September-November) is perfect for enjoying the warm colors of the leaves and hiking trails. Winter (December-February) offers a peaceful and quiet experience in nature. Spring (March-May) is perfect for bird-watching and nature photography.

Do’s and Don’ts of Veluwezoom National Park

Ensure that you respect the park and follow these do’s and dont’s guidelines while enjoying your stay here:

Do:

  • Keep the park clean and well-maintained
  • Keep an eye on your surroundings, especially while exploring the park’s wilderness.
  • Observe the wildlife from a safe distance.

Don’t:

  • Litter the park
  • Take any plants or animals out of the park
  • Feed the animals

Safety and Health

While the park is generally safe, it’s important to take certain precautions to ensure your safety and well-being.

Safety in Veluwezoom National Park

  • Stay on designated paths while walking or hiking.
  • Keep an eye out for signs of wildlife and be aware of your surroundings.
  • In case of an emergency, dial 112

Health in Veluwezoom National Park

It’s important to maintain proper hygiene while exploring the park. Carry a hygiene kit with you and practice social distancing during your stay.
