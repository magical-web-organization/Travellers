Introduction to Harderwijk

Located in the heart of Veluwe, Eastern Netherlands, is the charming city of Harderwijk. This historical city dates back to the 13th century, and it is famous for its beautiful old buildings, picturesque harbor, and stunning natural surroundings.

Harderwijk is a city that is rich in history and culture, with numerous places to visit, activities to do, and delicious food to try. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical landmarks or taking an outdoor adventure in its natural surroundings, Harderwijk has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Harderwijk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Harderwijk Dolfinarium

Harderwijk Dolfinarium is the largest marine mammal park in Europe, and it is home to over 8,000 animals, including dolphins, seals, and sea lions. This park is a great place to learn about these fascinating animals and their natural habitats.

Stadsmuseum Harderwijk

Stadsmuseum Harderwijk is a museum that displays the history and culture of the city. This museum features exhibits that cover topics such as the city’s fishing industry, its architecture, and its social history.

Vischpoort

Vischpoort is a historical landmark and one of the city’s oldest buildings. This beautiful gate was built in the 14th century, and it served as the main entrance to the city.

Natural Attractions of Harderwijk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Veluwe Forest

The Veluwe Forest is a beautiful natural area that surrounds the city of Harderwijk. This forest has many hiking and biking trails, and it is home to a variety of wildlife, such as deer, foxes, and wild boars.

Wolderwijd Lake

Wolderwijd Lake is a stunning lake that is perfect for swimming, fishing, and other water sports. This lake has many beautiful beaches, and it offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harderwijk

Veluwe Route

The Veluwe Route is a 35-kilometer long hiking and biking trail that runs through the Veluwe Forest. This trail offers stunning views of the forest and its wildlife.

Old Hanseatic Towns Route

The Old Hanseatic Towns Route is a 155-kilometer long biking route that runs through several historical towns, including Harderwijk. This trail offers a unique look at the city’s history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Harderwijk: Activities and Tours

Dolphin Watching Tour

The Dolphin Watching Tour is a unique experience that allows visitors to see dolphins and other marine life up close. This tour takes place on a boat ride around the Dolfinarium park, and it is a great way to learn about these fascinating animals.

Walking Tour of the Old City

The Walking Tour of the Old City is a guided tour that takes visitors through the historical parts of Harderwijk. This tour includes visits to the city’s ancient castle, the Vischpoort, and other important landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Harderwijk

Must-Try Dishes

Haring

Haring is a traditional Dutch dish that consists of raw herrings that are served with onions and pickles. This dish is a must-try in Harderwijk, where it is sold at numerous food stalls and restaurants.

Stroopwafel

Stroopwafel is a delicious Dutch dessert that consists of two thin waffle cookies with a layer of caramel syrup in between. This dish can be found at bakeries and cafes throughout the city.

Local Beverages

Jenever

Jenever is a traditional Dutch gin that is made from juniper berries and other botanicals. This drink can be found at many bars and restaurants in the city.

Beer

Beer is a popular beverage in Harderwijk, and there are numerous local breweries that produce delicious beers. Some of the most popular local beers include Veluwe Bier and Harderwijker Bier.

Where to Stay in Harderwijk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harderwijk has many great options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include: Hotel Marktzicht, Hotel Harderwijk, and Bed & Breakfast Villa Oldenhoff.

What to Buy in Harderwijk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Harderwijk is famous for its delicious food products, such as its herrings and stroopwafels. Other great souvenirs include traditional Dutch ceramics and handmade wooden clogs.

Local Culture and Customs of Harderwijk

Harderwijk has a rich cultural heritage, and it is known for its beautiful architecture, festivals, and traditions. One of the city’s most popular festivals is the Summer Festival, which takes place every July and features live music, food, and entertainment.

Best Time to Visit Harderwijk

The best time to visit Harderwijk is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city comes alive with festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harderwijk

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Explore the city’s historical landmarks and museums
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities in the Veluwe Forest

Don’t:

  • Smoke in non-designated areas
  • Litter or damage the city’s natural environment
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Harderwijk

Harderwijk is a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to protect their valuables and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Harderwijk

Harderwijk has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that provide high-quality medical care.

Getting to Harderwijk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harderwijk

The nearest airports to Harderwijk are Amsterdam Airport Schiphol and Eindhoven Airport. Visitors can take a train or bus from the airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Harderwijk

Harderwijk has a centrally located bus station that offers connections to other cities in the Netherlands.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Harderwijk by train, car, or bicycle.

How to Get Around in Harderwijk

Harderwijk is a small city that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. However, there is also a local bus service that visitors can use to get around.

Where to Go Next from Harderwijk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harderwijk is a great base for exploring the surrounding region of Veluwe, which is known for its beautiful natural landscapes and charming towns. Some nearby destinations include Apeldoorn, Arnhem, and Nijkerk.

Conclusion

Harderwijk is a charming city that has something for everyone. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical landmarks, taking an outdoor adventure in its natural surroundings, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, Harderwijk is a destination that should not be missed.
