Introduction to Hengelo

Located in the province of Overijssel, Hengelo is a charming city in the eastern region of the Netherlands. This city is known for its rich industrial heritage, which is still visible in its unique architecture and museums. Hengelo is also a vibrant cultural hub with plenty of outdoor activities, picturesque parks, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Hengelo

Hengelo offers a wide range of attractions, from exceptional museums to beautiful natural sites. Here are some of the best places to visit in Hengelo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • TwentseWelle: Showcasing the history and culture of the Twente region, this museum features interactive exhibitions, workshops, and events.

  • Natura Docet Wonderryck Twente: Discover the wonders of nature at this museum, which focuses on the flora and fauna of the Twente region.

  • Water Museum: This unique museum explores the history and significance of water in Hengelo and the surrounding areas.

  • Historical Centre of Hengelo: Stroll through the city’s historic district to admire the stunning architecture, including traditional Dutch brick and Jugendstil buildings.

Natural Attractions of Hengelo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hof Espelo: This lovely country estate features beautiful gardens and walking paths through the woodland areas.

  • De Waarbeek: A popular amusement park with a wide range of rides and attractions, including bumper cars, a roller coaster, and a petting zoo.

  • Twickel Castle: Dating back to the 16th century, this stunning castle sits on a beautiful estate and offers guided tours of the gardens and interior.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hengelo

Explore the beauty of Hengelo with these hiking and biking routes:

  • Hiking Route: Hof Espelo: This 4km walking trail takes you through Hof Espelo’s stunning gardens and forest areas.

  • Biking Route: Fietsroute Hengelose Es: Enjoy the beautiful Dutch countryside with this 35km biking route that takes you through the Hengelose Es nature reserve.

Best Things to Do in Hengelo: Activities and Tours

Experience the best of Hengelo with these activities and tours:

  • Canoeing along the Dinkel River: Rent a canoe and paddle through Hengelo’s picturesque surroundings, including the stunning Dinkel River.

  • Twente Ballooning: For an unforgettable experience, take a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque Twente region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hengelo

Day 1: Explore the historical center of Hengelo and visit the Water Museum.

Day 2: Discover the stunning TwentseWelle Museum and its interactive exhibits.

Day 3: Enjoy the natural beauty of Hof Espelo and its walking trails.

Day 4: Visit De Waarbeek amusement park for a fun-filled day with the family.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the beautiful Twickel Castle and estate.

Day 6: Rent a canoe and paddle along the scenic Dinkel River.

Day 7: End your trip with a hot air balloon ride over the stunning Twente region.

Food and Drink Scene of Hengelo

Hengelo offers a diverse culinary scene, with something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bitterballen: Deep-fried meatballs with a crunchy crust and a soft, savory filling.

  • Poffertjes: Tiny, fluffy pancakes traditionally topped with butter and powdered sugar.

  • Stamppot: A hearty Dutch dish made from mashed potatoes, vegetables, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Grolsch Beer: A widely popular Dutch beer with a distinctive taste and a bright, hoppy aroma.

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch liquor distilled from juniper berries and other herbs.

Where to Stay in Hengelo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hengelo offers a wide range of accommodations, from modern hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some options:

  • Van der Valk Hotel Hengelo: A modern and comfortable hotel with an on-site restaurant, bar, and wellness center.

  • Stayokay Hostel Hengelo: A budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers, located in a picturesque rural setting.

  • Bed & Breakfast Oekepoek: A charming and cozy guesthouse with a beautiful garden and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Hengelo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift? Hengelo has plenty of options for you, including:

  • Dutch cheese: From Gouda to Edam, Dutch cheese is a must-try when visiting the Netherlands.

  • Stroopwafels: A delicious Dutch treat made from two thin waffles with syrup in the middle.

  • Delft Blue pottery: An iconic Dutch product featuring intricate blue-and-white designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hengelo

Hengelo has a friendly and welcoming culture, and visitors can expect to be treated with warmth and respect. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Etiquette: When greeting someone, a quick handshake will suffice. It’s important to respect personal space and avoid physical contact.

  • Language: Dutch is the official language of Hengelo, but most people also speak English.

Best Time to Visit Hengelo

The best time to visit Hengelo is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the city is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy its indoor attractions during the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hengelo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Hengelo:

Do:

  • Respect local customs: It’s important to respect Hengelo’s local customs and traditions.

  • Be polite: Hengelo residents are friendly and welcoming, and it’s important to be respectful and polite.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Hengelo is a clean and beautiful city, and visitors should be mindful of keeping it that way by avoiding littering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hengelo

Hengelo is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Hengelo

Hengelo has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can expect to receive top-quality medical care if needed. It’s important to have travel health insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Hengelo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hengelo is easily reachable by various transportation options:

Airports Near Hengelo

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: The largest airport in the Netherlands is approximately 2 hours away from Hengelo.

  • Eindhoven Airport: Eindhoven Airport is approximately 1.5 hours away from Hengelo.

Bus Stations Near Hengelo

  • Hengelo Central Station: Located in the city center, it serves both local and regional bus lines.

Other Options

  • Train: Hengelo has a well-connected train station, with regular connections to major cities like Amsterdam, Utrecht, and Rotterdam.

  • Car: Hengelo is easily accessible by car, with major highways connecting the city to other parts of the country.

How to Get Around in Hengelo

Hengelo is a small and walkable city, but visitors can also use public transportation, bikes, or rental cars to get around. The city also has a bike-sharing system called OV-Fiets.

Where to Go Next from Hengelo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hengelo’s central location makes it a great base to explore other nearby destinations, such as:

  • Deventer: A charming Hanseatic city known for its historic architecture, winding streets, and vibrant cultural scene.

  • Zwolle: A lively city with a beautiful medieval center, lovely parks, and a variety of cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Hengelo offers a unique blend of history, culture, nature, and fun attractions. From exploring the city’s industrial heritage to enjoying its natural beauty and trying its delicious food, there’s something for everyone in this lovely Dutch city. Plan your trip today and discover all that Hengelo has to offer!
