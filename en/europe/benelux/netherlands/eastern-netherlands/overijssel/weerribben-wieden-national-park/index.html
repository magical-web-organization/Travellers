Introduction to Weerribben-Wieden National Park

Weerribben-Wieden National Park is located in Overijssel province in Eastern Netherlands. It covers an area of over 10,000 hectares and is the largest lowland marsh area in Europe. The park was established in 1992 to preserve the natural resources and biodiversity of the area. It is an important habitat for many rare species of plants and animals, including the otter, black tern, and great reed warbler.

There is also the Kraggenburg Museum, which houses various exhibits on the history and culture of the area.
