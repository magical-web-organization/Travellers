Introduction to Oldenzaal

Nestled in the heart of Overijssel in the Eastern Netherlands, Oldenzaal is a town filled with rich culture, history, natural beauty and stunning architecture that will leave you awestruck. A hidden gem, Oldenzaal is often overlooked by travelers but is a must-visit destination for those seeking a more authentic and unique Dutch experience.

List of Places to Visit in Oldenzaal

There are numerous places to visit in Oldenzaal. Here are some of the must-visit attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oldenzaal boasts some of the finest museums and historical sites in the Eastern Netherlands. If you want to explore Oldenzaal’s rich history and culture, you should visit:

  • The Oldenzaal Museum, which showcases the town’s history and culture through fascinating displays and interactive exhibits.

  • The St. Plechelmus Basilica, which was built in the 11th century and is the largest and oldest religious building in the town.

  • The Palthe Huis Museum, which is housed in an 18th-century mansion and offers a glimpse into the lifestyle of the 18th-century Dutch aristocracy.

Natural Attractions of Oldenzaal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you want to explore Oldenzaal’s stunning natural scenery and enjoy some outdoor adventures, you should visit:

  • The Het Hulsbeek recreational park, which features a large lake for swimming and water sports, hiking and biking trails, and picnic areas.

  • The Kalheupink Gardens, which is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the town and boasts beautiful flower gardens, winding pathways, and stunning views.

  • The Tankenberg, which is the highest point in Overijssel and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oldenzaal

Oldenzaal is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers with its numerous trails and beautiful surroundings. Some of the best trails include:

  • The Twente Estate Trail, which spans over 387 km and offers a variety of landscapes, from forested areas to meadows and fields.

  • The Salland Estate Trail, which is a 115-km trail that takes you through stunning natural landscapes and historic landmarks.

  • The Oldenzaal City Trail, which is a 5-km trail that takes you through the town’s historical sites and charming neighborhoods.

Best Things to Do in Oldenzaal: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun activities and tours that you can enjoy in Oldenzaal, including:

  • A city tour of Oldenzaal to explore the town’s rich history, culture, and architecture.

  • A visit to the local brewery to sample Oldenzaal’s finest beers.

  • A shopping trip to Oldenzaal’s bustling markets, where you can buy locally produced goods and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oldenzaal

If you’re planning to spend a week in Oldenzaal, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town of Oldenzaal and its historical sites, including the St. Plechelmus Basilica and the Oldenzaal Museum.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Twente Estate Trail or Salland Estate Trail and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

Day 3: Visit the Het Hulsbeek recreational park and enjoy swimming, water sports, and a picnic.

Day 4: Explore the Palthe Huis Museum and learn about the lifestyle of the 18th-century Dutch aristocracy.

Day 5: Take a city tour of Oldenzaal and learn about the town’s rich history and culture.

Day 6: Visit the Kalheupink Gardens and spend a peaceful day surrounded by beautiful flowers.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Enschede or Deventer.

Food and Drink Scene of Oldenzaal

Oldenzaal boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, and there are plenty of local dishes and beverages that you should try when you visit. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oale Groond Biefstuk, which is a regional specialty that consists of a steak served with a savory sauce and potatoes.

  • Erwtensoep, which is a thick and hearty pea soup that is often served with smoked sausage.

Local Beverages

  • Grolsch, which is a locally brewed beer that is famous throughout the Netherlands.

  • Jenever, which is a Dutch gin that is flavored with juniper berries and other spices.

Where to Stay in Oldenzaal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oldenzaal offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Restaurant Het Landhuis, which is a luxurious hotel located in a beautiful estate setting.

  • Hotel de Kroon, which is a charming hotel located in the heart of Oldenzaal’s historic district.

  • B&B Sagenland, which is a cozy guesthouse located just outside of Oldenzaal and offers a peaceful rural setting.

What to Buy in Oldenzaal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Oldenzaal, be sure to bring home some souvenirs and locally produced goods to remember your trip. Here are some of the best products to buy:

  • Locally brewed beer and Dutch gin.

  • Eierkoeken, which are Dutch egg cakes that are a popular local treat.

  • Dutch cheese, which is known throughout the world for its delicious taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Oldenzaal

Oldenzaal has a rich cultural heritage, and the town is proud of its traditions and customs. Here are some insights into the local culture and customs:

  • Oldenzaal celebrates Carnival, which is a festive time when locals dress up in colorful costumes and take part in parades and street parties.

  • The town is famous for its brass band music, which is a popular cultural tradition in the Eastern Netherlands.

Best Time to Visit Oldenzaal

The best time to visit Oldenzaal is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Safety in Oldenzaal

Bus Stations Near Oldenzaal

  • Oldenzaal bus station, which is located in the town center and offers connections to nearby destinations.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Oldenzaal

Oldenzaal is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. There are also local bus services that connect the town to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Oldenzaal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the region surrounding Oldenzaal, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit:

  • Enschede, which is a vibrant university town known for its contemporary art and design scene.

  • Deventer, which is a charming medieval town located on the banks of the river IJssel.

Conclusion

Oldenzaal is a hidden gem in the Eastern Netherlands that offers a unique and authentic Dutch experience. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant food and drink scene, Oldenzaal is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the heart of the Netherlands.
