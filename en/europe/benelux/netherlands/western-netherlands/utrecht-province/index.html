Introduction to Utrecht (province)

Located in the western region of the Netherlands, Utrecht province is known for its picturesque landscapes, vibrant culture, and rich history. The province is home to some of the country’s oldest cities, including Utrecht - the province’s capital - Amersfoort, and Zeist. Utrecht province is a popular travel destination for those wishing to experience the Dutch culture beyond the flashlights of the tourist-ridden Amsterdam.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, local food and drink specialties, and useful travel tips to help you make the most of your trip in Utrecht province.

List of Places to Visit in Utrecht (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Rietveld Schröder House A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Rietveld Schröder House is an iconic building located in Utrecht designed by Dutch architect Gerard Rietveld. The house combines architecture and art, and visitors can explore its unique design and learn about Rietveld’s work.

  • Dom Tower Utrecht’s tallest and most prominent landmark, the Dom Tower, dates back to the 14th century. Visitors can climb the tower’s 465 steps for a breathtaking view of the city.

  • Museum Catharijneconvent One of the best places to experience the religious art and culture of the Netherlands, Museum Catharijneconvent displays Dutch religious artifacts from the Middle Ages up to the present day.

  • Castellum Hoge Woerd Located in the city of Utrecht, Castellum Hoge Woerd is an archaeological site that dates back to the days of the Roman Empire. The site features ancient ruins, interactive exhibits, and cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Utrecht (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Utrechtse Heuvelrug National Park With over 10,000 hectares of forest, sand dunes, and heaths, Utrechtse Heuvelrug National Park is a haven for nature enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the scenic hiking and biking trails, or go boating and fishing on the waterways.

  • De Utrechtse Venen De Utrechtse Venen is a beautiful wetland area that covers a vast area of Utrecht province. Visitors can cycle or hike along the many scenic trails that wind through the wetlands, or rent a boat to enjoy the waterways.

  • Kromme Rijn Kromme Rijn is a scenic river that flows through central Netherlands and is perfect for boating, canoeing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Utrecht (province)

  • Utrechtse Heuvelrug National Park Hike This 12 km hiking trail takes you through the breathtaking scenery of Utrechtse Heuvelrug National Park. With stunning views of the forest, sand dunes, and heaths, this hike is perfect for nature lovers.

  • Bunnik Bike Tour This 20 km easy bike tour takes you through the quaint town of Bunnik. Along the way, you’ll enjoy panoramic views of the Kromme Rijn river and explore the many historical sites in the area.

Best Things to Do in Utrecht (province): Activities and Tours

  • Canal Tour Take a breathtaking boat tour through Utrecht’s charming canals and experience the city from a different perspective. You’ll also learn about the city’s rich history and take in the stunning scenery.

  • Sail and Cycle Experience the beauty of Utrecht from both land and water with the Sail and Cycle tour. You’ll explore the city on a guided bike tour and then hop aboard a boat for a relaxing river cruise.

  • Utrecht Street Art Tour Take a guided tour and discover the fascinating world of Utrecht’s street art scene. You’ll learn about the artists, their creative process, and the meaning behind their art.

Food and Drink Scene of Utrecht (province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oliebollen A Dutch speciality, Oliebollen are delicious deep-fried dough balls that are similar to donuts. They’re typically eaten during the festive season and can be enjoyed plain or with powdered sugar.

  • Stroopwafel Stroopwafels are a Dutch sweet treat made of two thin layers of waffle-like cookie filled with caramel syrup.

  • Andijviestamppot A traditional Dutch dish, Andijviestamppot consists of mashed potatoes and endive, served with gravy and a meatball on top.

  • Karnemelkse Pap Karnemelkse Pap is a typical Dutch breakfast dish made of buttermilk and porridge. It’s served with a sprinkle of sugar and cinnamon on top.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever Jenever is a Dutch gin-like drink that’s been popular in the country for over 500 years. It’s typically served cold and in small glasses, and can be enjoyed straight or mixed with tonic water.

  • Grolsch Grolsch is a popular Dutch beer that has been produced since 1615. It has a distinct taste and comes in a variety of strengths to suit different tastes.

  • Koffie Verkeerd Koffie Verkeerd, also known as a “wrong coffee”, is a popular Dutch coffee drink. It’s made with steamed milk and espresso and is similar to a latte.

Where to Stay in Utrecht (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Hotel Karel V Located in the heart of Utrecht’s city center, the Grand Hotel Karel V is a luxury hotel set in a 14th-century monastery. The rooms are spacious and comfortable, and the hotel offers a range of facilities including a sauna and indoor pool.

  • Stayokay Soest Located in the scenic countryside of Utrecht province, Stayokay Soest is a hostel that offers comfortable rooms and a warm and friendly atmosphere. The hostel is set in a 13th-century castle that has been beautifully restored.

  • Bed and Breakfast De Oude Rijn Situated in the charming town of Harmelen, Bed and Breakfast De Oude Rijn is a cozy guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere. The guesthouse is located in a beautiful historical building and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Utrecht (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hollandse Kaas One of the most popular souvenirs to bring home from the Netherlands, Hollandse Kaas is the country’s famous cheese. There are many different types to choose from, including Gouda and Edam.

  • Delft Blue Pottery Delft Blue Pottery is a quintessential Dutch souvenir that’s been produced since the 16th century. It’s characterized by its blue and white design and is available in many different forms, including plates, vases, and figurines.

  • Stroopwafels Stroopwafels are a traditional Dutch treats that make a perfect gift to bring home. You can find them in many different sizes and packaging, making them easy to pack in your bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Utrecht (province)

  • Bicycling Culture The Netherlands is famously bicycle-friendly, and Utrecht province is no exception. You’ll often see locals cycling to work, school, or just for fun. Renting a bike is a great way to explore the province’s scenic countryside and charming towns.

  • Coffee Culture Utrecht province is known for its cozy cafés and specialty coffee shops, which are popular hangout spots for locals and visitors alike. Try some of the local Dutch coffee specialties, such as Koffie Verkeerd or Dutch coffee with a liquor.

  • Tolerance The Netherlands is known for its progressive attitudes towards diversity and inclusion, and Utrecht province is no exception. Visitors can expect a welcoming and diverse community, and travelers are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Utrecht (province)

The best time to visit Utrecht province is in the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November), when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. The summer months (June to August) can be hot and busy, with higher prices, while the winter months (December to February) can be cold and rainy, with fewer tourist attractions and activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Utrecht (province)

Do:

  • Explore the Countryside Utrecht province is home to some of the most picturesque landscapes in the Netherlands. Be sure to explore the scenic countryside and enjoy the fresh air and beautiful views.

  • Try Local Food and Beverages Dutch cuisine is known for its unique flavors and specialties, so be sure to try some of the local dishes and drinks during your visit.

  • Respect Local Customs The Dutch are known for their progressive attitudes towards diversity and inclusion. Be respectful of local customs and traditions to ensure an enjoyable and welcoming visit.

Don’t:

  • Be Loud and Rowdy Dutch culture is generally quiet and reserved. Avoid being loud and rowdy, especially in public places.

  • Wear Shoes Inside It’s considered impolite to wear shoes inside Dutch homes and some public buildings. Be sure to remove your shoes when entering these spaces.

  • Smoke in Public Smoking is prohibited in many public places in the Netherlands, including restaurants, bars, and train stations. Be sure to follow local smoking regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Utrecht (province)

Utrecht province is generally a safe destination for travelers, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as avoiding dark and desolate areas at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Utrecht (province)

The Netherlands has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect to receive quality medical attention if needed. Travelers should ensure that they have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling and consider purchasing travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Utrecht (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to Utrecht province is by air or train. The Amsterdam Airport Schiphol (AMS) is located just 40 km from Utrecht and connects to many major cities around the world. Alternatively, travelers can take a train to Utrecht Central Station from other Dutch cities or neighboring countries.

The easiest way to get around in Utrecht province is by bike or train. Alternatively, travelers can take a train to navigate between different cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Utrecht (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Hague Located just an hour’s drive from Utrecht province, The Hague is the Netherlands’ political capital and is known for its historic landmarks, beaches, and museums.

  • Rotterdam A vibrant city filled with art and architecture, Rotterdam is a great destination for those interested in modern design, living, and entertainment. It’s located just one hour from Utrecht by train.

  • Maastricht Located in the southern part of the Netherlands, Maastricht is a charming city known for its historic landmarks and cultural attractions. Travelers can take a train from Utrecht to Maastricht in about two hours.

From its charming towns and scenic countryside to its delicious food and drink specialties, Utrecht is a destination worth exploring.
