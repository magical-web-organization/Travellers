Introduction to Almere

Located in Flevoland, Western Netherlands, Almere is a modern and vibrant city that was established in 1976. With its beautiful scenery, excellent facilities, and rich cultural scene, it is becoming increasingly popular among tourists looking to explore the Netherlands. Almere is a perfect destination for families, solo travelers, and adventure enthusiasts alike.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a look at the best things to do in Almere, places to visit, natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation options, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Almere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Almere has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Museum De Paviljoens: This museum is dedicated to contemporary art and culture and has both permanent and temporary exhibitions.
  • Nationaal Bomenmuseum: This arboretum features more than 3300 different trees and shrubs from around the world.
  • Oostvaardersplassen: This nature reserve is home to a wide variety of bird species and a must-visit spot for bird-watchers.
  • CineMec: This popular cinema complex hosts movie premieres, film festivals, and special events.

Natural Attractions of Almere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Almere has incredible natural beauty and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best ways to experience it:

  • Almeerderstrand: This beach is a perfect spot to relax, sunbathe, or go for a swim.
  • Lepelaarplassen: This bird sanctuary has several walking and cycling routes and is perfect for nature lovers.
  • The New Wilderness: This 30,000-hectare area is a great place to explore the flora and fauna of the Oostvaardersplassen nature reserve.
  • FlevOnice: This is the largest artificial ice rink in Europe. It is perfect for ice skating enthusiasts and families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Almere

Almere has many incredible hiking and biking routes, making it a great destination for outdoor adventure seekers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Oostvaardersplassen Route: This 35km route will take you through the beautiful Oostvaardersplassen nature reserve.
  • Beatrix Route: This 42km route takes you through the beautiful countryside and forests of Flevoland.
  • Almere Round Route: This 90km route takes you through the city of Almere, its surroundings and Lelystad.

Best Things to Do in Almere: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for fun things to do in Almere, then check out these activities and tours:

  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat tour around the canals and lakes of Almere and admire the stunning scenery.
  • Art and Culture Tour: Discover the vibrant art and culture of Almere on this walking tour.
  • Shopping Spots Tour: Almere has some of the best shopping spots in the Netherlands.
  • Outdoor Activities Tour: Experience the thrill of outdoor activities such as biking, hiking, and kayaking in Almere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Almere

If you have a week to spend in Almere, then here is an itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1-2: Explore the Museums and Historical Sites

Spend the first two days exploring the museums and historical sites of the city. Visit Museum De Paviljoens, Nationaal Bomenmuseum, Oostvaardersplassen, and CineMec.

Day 3-4: Experience the Natural Attractions of Almere

Spend the next two days experiencing the natural beauty of the city. Visit Almeerderstrand, Lepelaarplassen, The New Wilderness, and FlevOnice.

Day 5-6: Hiking/Biking Tours

Take a break from the museums and tours, and spend the next two days exploring the hiking and biking routes of Almere. Try the Oostvaardersplassen Route, Beatrix Route, and Almere Round Route.

Day 7: Shop and Enjoy the Food and Drink

Spend your last day at some of the best shopping spots in the Netherlands before heading back home. You can also try some local dishes and beverages, such as the Poffertjes, Stroopwafel, and Bitterballen.

Food and Drink Scene of Almere

Almere has a vibrant and diverse food and drink scene that offers a unique experience to its visitors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poffertjes: These are traditional Dutch mini pancakes served with butter and powdered sugar.
  • Stroopwafel: A sweet waffle made from two thin layers of baked dough with caramel syrup filling in the middle.
  • Bitterballen: A deep-fried snack that features a crispy crust filled with a creamy beef and gravy mixture.
  • Hollandse Nieuwe: Fresh herring served with chopped onions and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Almeerse Wijn: This is a locally produced wine that is becoming increasingly popular and is made from a blend of fruits and berries.
  • Hertog Jan: This is a popular Dutch beer with a rich flavor and distinctive aroma.
  • Jenever: This is a traditional Dutch gin that is made from malt and juniper berries and is often served with tonic water.

Where to Stay in Almere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Almere has a variety of accommodation options for travelers. Here are some of the best:

  • Apollo Hotel Almere City Centre: This hotel is located in the city center and offers excellent facilities.
  • Bastion Hotel Almere: This hotel offers affordable accommodation and is located near the train station.
  • Bed & Breakfast Almere: This is a cozy and welcoming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and breakfast.

What to Buy in Almere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy in Almere:

  • Delftware: This is a famous blue and white pottery that originates from the Netherlands.
  • Cheese: The Netherlands is famous for its cheeses, so be sure to try some Gouda and Edam cheese.
  • Tulips: The Netherlands is also famous for its tulips, so you can take home some tulip bulbs or potted flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Almere

The people of Almere are friendly and welcoming to tourists. When in Almere, it’s important to:

  • Respect Local Traditions: The Netherlands has a rich culture and unique customs, so it’s best to be respectful.
  • Be Polite: When interacting with the locals, be polite and courteous.
  • Take Off Your Shoes: When visiting someone’s home, it’s customary to take off your shoes before entering.
  • Use Hand Gestures Sparingly: In the Netherlands, excessive hand gestures are considered rude.

Best Time to Visit Almere

The best time to visit Almere is in the summer months of June, July, and August. This is when the weather is warmest, and the city is alive with events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Almere

Do:

  • Try Local Food and Beverages: Almere has lots of delicious food and beverages to try, so don’t be afraid to try something new.
  • Be Respectful: The people of Almere are friendly and welcoming, so be respectful of their traditions and customs.
  • Explore the Natural Beauty: Almere has plenty of natural attractions, so make sure to explore the beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Littering is considered disrespectful and is not tolerated.
  • Smoke Weed in Public: Though marijuana is legal in the Netherlands, smoking weed in public is not.
  • Be Loud and Disruptive: The people of Almere are polite and courteous, so it’s best not to be loud and disruptive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Almere

Almere is a safe destination for tourists. However, you should always take precautions, such as:

  • Watch Your Belongings: Pickpocketing can occur in busy tourist areas, so keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: If you are out late at night, it’s best to walk with a group of people or take a taxi.

Health in Almere

Almere has good health facilities and hospitals, so you don’t have to worry about getting sick. However, it’s always best to take precautions, such as:

  • Washing Your Hands: Make sure to wash your hands regularly, especially during flu season.
  • Using Sunscreen: The sun in Almere can be strong during the summer, so be sure to use sunscreen.

Getting to Almere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Almere

The closest airport to Almere is Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, which is about 40km away.

Bus Stations Near Almere

Almere has a central train and bus station called Almere Centrum Station, which is located in the city center.

Other Options

You can also get to Almere by train or car. The city is located near the A6 motorway and is well connected to other cities in the Netherlands.

How to Get Around in Almere

Almere has an excellent public transportation system that includes buses and trains. You can also rent a bike or car to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Almere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore after your visit to Almere:

  • Amsterdam: This is a must-visit city in the Netherlands and is only 30km away from Almere.
  • Utrecht: This historic city is only 40km away from Almere and has beautiful architecture and a vibrant culture.
  • Rotterdam: This modern and dynamic city is located about 80km from Almere and has plenty of attractions to offer.

Conclusion

Almere is an exciting and vibrant city that is quickly becoming a favorite destination for tourists. With its beautiful scenery, rich culture, and excellent facilities, it is no wonder that visitors keep coming back.
