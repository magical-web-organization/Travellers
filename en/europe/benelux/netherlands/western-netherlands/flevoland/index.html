Introduction to Flevoland

Flevoland is a newly created province in the western part of the Netherlands, situated in the Benelux region of Europe. The province is surrounded by water and spans 1,417 square kilometers of both land and water, making it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Guests visiting Flevoland can expect a unique mix of nature, culture, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Flevoland

If you’re planning a visit to Flevoland, try to add these places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Batavia Stad Amsterdam Fashion Outlet
  • Batavialand
  • Museum Schokland
  • The New Land museum

Natural Attractions of Flevoland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oostvaardersplassen National Park
  • Lepelaarplassen nature reserve
  • Marker Wadden Island
  • The Windmill in Zeewolde

Hiking/Biking Routes of Flevoland

Take a walk or bike around these beautiful areas of Flevoland:

  • Geert Luchtmanspad hiking and biking trail
  • Veluwe to the IJsselmeer route
  • Flevoland Coastal Route

Best Things to Do in Flevoland: Activities and Tours

  • Take a wildlife safari in the Oostvaardersplassen National Park
  • Visit the Batavia shipyard and witness the restoration of a 17th-century replica of a Dutch East India Company vessel.
  • Visit Batavia Stad Amsterdam Fashion Outlet for some excellent shopping and entertainment.
  • Explore the Marker Wadden Islands
  • Head over to Walibi Holland amusement park for a fun-filled day.

Week-Long Itinerary for Flevoland

Day 1-2: Batavialand and Batavia Stad Amsterdam Fashion Outlet Day 3-4: Natural Attractions: Oostvaardersplassen, Lepelaarplassen, and Marker Wadden. Day 5-6: Biking and Hiking: Flevoland Coastal Route and Geert Luchtmanspad. Day 7: Walibi Holland Amusement Park

Food and Drink Scene of Flevoland

Flevoland has a unique food culture that offers visitors a taste of Dutch cuisine with a twist of local flavor.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot (a Dutch classic dish) which is mashed potato mixed with vegetables like cabbage or carrots.
  • Poffertjes (miniature pancakes),
  • Dutch herring (this seasonal delight is only available in summer)

Local Beverages

  • Bokbeer, a Bavarian beer brewed with roasted malt is the locals’ favorite.
  • Jenever, this traditional Dutch gin is distilled from juniper berries.

Where to Stay in Flevoland

Flevoland offers visitors a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to affordable guesthouses.

What to Buy in Flevoland

  • Poffertjes Pan: bring home the traditional Dutch waffle pan
  • Batavia Rum: distilled in Flevoland
  • Dutch Cheese: Sample and buy locally created cheese

Local Culture and Customs of Flevoland

Flevoland has a rich culture with many customs rooted in the province’s natural environment and historical background. One of the famous cultural events is the annual color festival- Holi Fusion - in Almere beach.

Best Time to Visit Flevoland

The ideal months for visiting Flevoland are between April and September, when the weather is warm and pleasant. During this time, visitors can also experience and participate in several exciting outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Flevoland

Do:

  • take along good hiking shoes for your adventures.
  • try learning a few Dutch phrases to communicate with the locals.
  • carry your sunscreen and hat when heading out in summer.

Don’t:

  • litter the environment.
  • obstruct the bicycle lane.

Safety and Health

Safety in Flevoland

Flevoland is a safe province with very low crime rates, but visitors should take necessary precautions.

Health in Flevoland

Flevoland has a well-developed healthcare system, and European visitors can obtain medical care through their European Health Insurance Card.

How to Get Around in Flevoland

Public bus transport, cycling, and walking are the best ways to travel around Flevoland.

Where to Go Next from Flevoland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations include Texel, Hoorn, and Amsterdam City. Excursions can include a visit to Volendam, Zaandam, or Edam.

Conclusion

Flevoland offers an exciting blend of nature, culture, and adventure that visitors of all ages can enjoy. Experience the history, cuisine, and charming culture of this beautiful province on your next trip to the Netherlands.
