Introduction to Castricum

Castricum is a picturesque town located on the North Holland Peninsula of the Netherlands. It is a small town with a population of around 35,000 people. The town boasts of beautiful natural surroundings, with dunes, forests, and beaches. Castricum is known for its serene ambiance and is a perfect destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of big cities.

List of Places to Visit in Castricum

Castricum has plenty of places to visit, and it can be tough to decide which ones to include in your itinerary. Here are some of the best spots to check out during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, you’ll definitely want to check out these places:

  • Castricum Museum: Learn about the ancient and modern history of Castricum through extensive collections, exhibitions, and artifacts.
  • Huis van Hilde: It is an archaeological museum that showcases the unique history of North Holland Peninsula with various exhibitions, workshops, and activities.

Natural Attractions of Castricum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Castricum has plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Castricum dunes reserve: This beautiful natural reserve offers breathtaking views and several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Castricum Beach: Head out to the beach for a leisurely day of swimming, sunbathing, playing volleyball, or other beach activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Castricum

Castricum is famous for its stunning nature, so hiking and biking routes are plentiful here. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Duinen van Castricum: This hike and bike route go through the beautiful nature reserve of dunes and forests and offers lovely scenic views.
  • Het Kooijperpad Trail: It is a bike trail that goes through the beautiful countryside and past waterways and windmills.

Best Things to Do in Castricum: Activities and Tours

Castricum offers a variety of fun activities for visitors. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Horse Riding: Several horse riding centers are available in Castricum, where you can have a unique experience of riding a horse through dunes and the countryside.
  • Canoeing: Rent a canoe and paddle your way through the beautiful waterways, or join a guided canoe tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Castricum

If you’re planning a full week to spend in Castricum, here’s a proposed itinerary:

1 Visit Castricum Museum and immerse in local history.

Food and Drink Scene of Castricum

Castricum offers a variety of delicious local dishes and local beverages that you must try! Here are some of the best:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Broodje Kwekkeboom: A local specialty dish featuring meatballs and red cabbage, served in a bun.
  • Kaaskroket: Fried cheese croquette, a Dutch delicacy served in cafes across Castricum.

Local Beverages

  • Jonge Jenever: A locally brewed gin that has been produced in the area since the 16th century.
  • Bittergarnituur: A locally brewed beer with a savory taste, traditionally served with a plate of snacks.

Where to Stay in Castricum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Castricum offers several excellent accommodation options such as hotels, hostels, and guesthouses for travelers to choose from. Here are some of the top recommendations:

  • Strandhotel Zoomers: A charming hotel located near the beach, offering magnificent views of the North Sea.
  • Badhotel Egmond aan Zee: A grand hotel on the outskirts of Castricum known for its luxury accommodation and excellent dining facilities.

What to Buy in Castricum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Castricum is known for its unique and locally made products, perfect as souvenirs. Here are some of the best local items to bring back home:

  • Dutch Cheese: Take home some authentic Dutch cheese, a staple of the local cuisine. There are plenty of cheese stores throughout Castricum that offer a variety of cheese flavors.
  • Wooden Shoes: Wooden shoes, or clogs, are an iconic Dutch souvenir and can be purchased in many local shops in Castricum.

Local Culture and Customs of Castricum

Castricum is a welcoming and friendly town, and the locals are helpful towards visitors. The town has preserved its traditions and customs, and you’ll find unique celebrations and festivals throughout the year. Respect the locals and their customs while you’re here, and you’ll experience a seamless stay.

Best Time to Visit Castricum

Castricum can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to visit is from April to September. During this time, the weather is ideal for outdoor activities, and several local events and festivals take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Castricum

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Castricum:

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Book your accommodation and activities in advance, especially during peak season.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Loudly disrupt the serene ambiance of the town.
  • Litter public areas and nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Castricum is generally a safe town, but it is always recommended to take adequate safety precautions during your stay:

Safety in Castricum

  • Take care of your belongings while in public areas.
  • Keep a close eye on your children.

Health in Castricum

  • Research beforehand the location of local pharmacies, hospitals, clinics, and emergency contacts.

Getting to Castricum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Castricum is well-connected to major cities in the Netherlands through several modes of transportation. Here are some ways to get here:

Airports Near Castricum

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: It is the largest airport in the Netherlands and is located around 30 minutes from Castricum.

Bus Stations Near Castricum

  • Castricum station: It is a major railway station that connects Castricum to other parts of the country.

Other Options

  • Biking or Hiking: If you’re coming from nearby towns, consider biking or hiking your way to Castricum for an unforgettable and eco-friendly experience.

How to Get Around in Castricum

Castricum is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. However, buses, taxis, and bikes are always available for those who prefer to travel more quickly or explore more areas.

Where to Go Next from Castricum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Castricum is located near several other charming towns and beautiful natural reserves that are worth visiting. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Alkmaar: A charming town known for its beautiful architecture, local markets, and the Dutch cheese museum.
  • Zaanse Schans: A beautiful open-air museum featuring traditional windmills, crafts, shops, cafes, and houses.
