Introduction to Den Helder

Den Helder is a small city located in the province of North Holland, in the western part of the Netherlands. It is best known for its naval base and picturesque views of the sea. The city is home to a vibrant cultural scene, filled with museums and historical landmarks, as well as natural attractions, including scenic beaches and hiking and biking trails. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best attractions and activities in Den Helder, as well as where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Den Helder

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marinemuseum: This is a fascinating museum that offers a glimpse into the history of the Royal Netherlands Navy and its important role in the country’s history. The museum is divided into several sections, including a submarine exhibit and a section on marine life.
  • Fort Kijkduin: This historic fort was built in the 19th century and played a vital role in the defense of the region. It now houses a museum that tells the story of the fort and the surrounding area.
  • Nationaal Reddingmuseum Dorus Rijkers: This museum showcases the history of lifesaving at sea, including the heroic efforts of the Dorus Rijkers lifeboat crew.
  • Galerie Windkracht 13: This modern art museum features contemporary works from local and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Den Helder: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kijkduin Beach: This is the perfect place to soak up the sun and enjoy the beautiful weather. The beach stretches for miles and is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Dunes of Texel National Park: This stunning natural park is home to a variety of wildlife and offers excellent hiking and biking trails. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the coastline and the dunes while exploring the park.
  • Sailors’ Monument: This historic monument is located in the center of Den Helder and offers panoramic views of the city and the sea. It is a great spot to sit and enjoy the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Den Helder

  • Strandslag Duinoord: This bike trail takes you through the dunes to the beautiful beaches of Julianadorp.
  • Helderse Duinen: This hiking trail takes you through the dunes of Texel National Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Oud Herlaer route: This bike trail takes you through the countryside surrounding Den Helder and offers scenic views of the area’s vineyards and windmills.

Best Things to Do in Den Helder: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of the naval base and enjoy views of the ships and submarines in the harbor.
  • Lighthouse Climb: The Huisduinen lighthouse offers panoramic views of the sea and the surrounding countryside. Visitors can climb to the top for a breathtaking view.
  • Segway Tours: This is a fun and unique way to explore Den Helder’s natural beauty. Visitors can take a segway tour of the dunes and beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Den Helder

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hollandse Nieuwe: This is a popular Dutch dish made with fresh herring and onions.
  • Poffertjes: These delicious mini pancakes are a popular Dutch street food.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: This is a traditional Dutch gin that is popular in Den Helder and other parts of the Netherlands.
  • Texels Bier: This is a locally brewed beer that is available in many bars and restaurants in Den Helder.

Where to Stay in Den Helder: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Wienerhof: This is a charming hotel located in the heart of Den Helder. It offers comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.
  • Hotel Cape Horn: This modern hotel is located near the harbor and offers beautiful sea views.
  • Julianadorp aan Zee Resort: This resort is located just outside of Den Helder and offers spacious apartments and access to a beautiful beach.

What to Buy in Den Helder: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dutch Cheese: The Netherlands is famous for its cheese, and visitors can sample a variety of local cheeses at shops throughout Den Helder.
  • Handmade Clogs: These traditional wooden shoes are a popular Dutch souvenir.
  • Delft Blue Pottery: This beautiful blue and white pottery is a Dutch specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Den Helder

The people of Den Helder are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should make an effort to respect their customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or cultural events and always ask for permission before taking someone’s photo.

Best Time to Visit Den Helder

The best time to visit Den Helder is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. The city can be crowded during the summer months, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Den Helder

Do:

  • Do try local specialties, such as herring and Dutch cheese.
  • Do respect the customs and traditions of the locals.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Den Helder.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t take photos without asking for permission.
  • Don’t forget to be respectful and courteous to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Den Helder

Den Helder is a safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Den Helder

The healthcare system in the Netherlands is excellent, with hospitals and clinics located throughout Den Helder and the surrounding area. Visitors should make sure they have health insurance before traveling to the Netherlands.

Getting to Den Helder: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Den Helder

The nearest airport to Den Helder is Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, which is located approximately 80 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to Den Helder.

Bus Stations Near Den Helder

Den Helder has a bus station located near the city center. From the bus station, visitors can take a bus to other parts of the Netherlands.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Den Helder from Amsterdam or other parts of the Netherlands.

How to Get Around in Den Helder

Den Helder is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take a bus or taxi if they prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Den Helder: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Texel: This beautiful island is located just off the coast of Den Helder and is known for its stunning natural beauty and picturesque villages.
  • Alkmaar: This historic city is located just south of Den Helder and is known for its cheese market and beautiful old town.
  • Zaanse Schans: This outdoor museum showcases the traditional Dutch way of life, complete with wooden windmills, clogs, and cheese.

Conclusion

Den Helder is a charming city that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you are interested in history, nature, or food and drink, Den Helder has something to offer. With its friendly locals, beautiful scenery, and rich cultural heritage, Den Helder is the perfect destination for your next vacation.
