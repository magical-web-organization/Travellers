Introduction to Monnickendam

Nestled in the picturesque district of Zaanstreek-Waterland in North Holland, Monnickendam is a charming town with an abundance of historical, cultural, and natural attractions. Its location just 15 kilometers north of Amsterdam makes it a perfect destination for a day trip or a week-long getaway.

As you stroll along its winding alleyways and admire its well-preserved 17th-century architecture, you’ll discover Monnickendam’s rich history and friendly local culture. Whether you’re interested in museums and historical landmarks, outdoor adventures and scenic views, or the food and drink scene, you won’t be disappointed by what Monnickendam has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Monnickendam

Here are some of the must-visit places in Monnickendam:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Monnickendam Museum: Housed in an old cheese warehouse, this museum showcases the town’s maritime history, including shipbuilding, fishing, and shipping trades.
  • Old Church of Monnickendam: Dating back to the 15th century, this church is a significant landmark in Monnickendam and has beautiful stained glass windows and remarkable monuments.
  • Grote Kerk: Another historic church, located in the heart of the town, known for its organ concerts held during summer.

Natural Attractions of Monnickendam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hempolder: A scenic nature reserve, perfect for cycling, hiking and bird watching.
  • Zuiderwoude: A small village surrounded by meadows and pastures, ideal for nature lovers seeking some peace and quiet.
  • Gouwzee: A lake offering a viewpoint to enjoy breathtaking views of the town, especially during sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monnickendam

For outdoor enthusiasts, Monnickendam offers some great hiking and biking options:

  • Waterland Route: This 35-kilometer biking route takes you through the scenic countryside villages, past historic windmills, and waterways.
  • Boven-Merwede Route: A 55-kilometer cycling route that takes you along a riverbank landscape of Holland with beautiful scenery all around.

Best Things to Do in Monnickendam: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours that you can enjoy in Monnickendam, some of which are:

  • Boating Tours: Witness Monnickendam from a whole new perspective by taking a boat tour of the town.
  • Wine tasting: Sample the locally produced wines while relaxing on a cozy terrace overlooking the harbor.
  • Cheese Tasting: Taste some of Holland’s most famous cheeses in local shops or markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Monnickendam

In Monnickendam, you’ll find restaurants, cafes, and bars serving delicious Dutch cuisine and seafood. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot: A traditional Dutch dish made of mashed potatoes mixed with vegetables and meat.
  • Kibbeling: A popular Dutch street food that consists of fish, battered and deep-fried.
  • Hollandse Nieuwe: A dish made of raw herring that is specially prepared and enjoyed during the summer months.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin distilled from malt wine and flavored with juniper berries.
  • Heineken beer: A world-renowned beer that originated in Amsterdam and now is a popular drink throughout the Netherlands.

Where to Stay in Monnickendam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a budget-friendly option, Monnickendam has plenty of accommodation options:

  • Suitehotel Posthoorn: A luxurious hotel located in a renovated 17th-century building.
  • Waterlandplein Airbnb: A cozy apartment perfect for budget travelers, close to shops and cafes.
  • De Vergulde Vis: A charming guesthouse offering comfortable rooms and a breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Monnickendam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Monnickendam is known for its quaint shops selling souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some items you should consider bringing home:

  • Delftware: Hand-painted porcelain with blue designs, usually depicting windmills, tulips, or other Dutch motifs.
  • Cheese: Monnickendam is famous for its Gouda and Edam cheeses, which make perfect gifts for friends and family.
  • Stroopwafel: A traditional Dutch waffle cookie filled with caramel.

Local Culture and Customs of Monnickendam

As a town steeped in history and traditions, Monnickendam has a unique culture that is worth experiencing. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Language: Dutch is the official language, but most locals speak English fluently.
  • Biking: Biking is a common means of transportation, so be aware of cyclists when walking on the streets.
  • Tipping: Tipping isn’t mandatory but is appreciated, especially in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Monnickendam

The best time to visit Monnickendam is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor events take place during this time. Spring and autumn are also good times to visit when the crowds are thinner, and the weather is still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monnickendam

Here are some things to keep in mind to make the most of your visit:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take a boat tour of the canals to learn more about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets or in nature reserves.
  • Make too much noise after 10 p.m.
  • Enter private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monnickendam

Monnickendam is a safe town with low crime rates, but it’s always wise to take precautions:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places.
  • Avoid walking in isolated areas alone at night.
  • Follow traffic rules, especially when cycling on the streets.

Health in Monnickendam

The health care system in the Netherlands is of high quality. In case of a medical emergency, dial 112, which is the emergency number. Pharmacies are available for non-emergency medical issues.

Getting to Monnickendam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Monnickendam is easily accessible by various modes of transportation:

Airports Near Monnickendam

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: Located about 40 kilometers south of Monnickendam, it’s the busiest airport in the Netherlands.

Bus Stations Near Monnickendam

  • Monnickendam Bus Station: Located near the town center, it serves various local and regional bus routes.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are available at Amsterdam Airport Schiphol and other areas.
  • Train: Amsterdam Central Station is about a 20-minute train ride away from Monnickendam.

How to Get Around in Monnickendam

The best way to explore Monnickendam is on foot or by bike. The town center is small and walkable, and bike rentals are widely available. Public transportation, including buses, is also an option for exploring the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Monnickendam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Monnickendam is located in an area with many other interesting destinations, including:

  • Amsterdam: The capital city of the Netherlands, known for its museums, canals, and nightlife.
  • Edam: A charming town famous for its cheese and 17th-century architecture.
  • Marken: A picturesque village known for its traditional wooden houses and scenic harbor.

Conclusion

Monnickendam may be a small town, but it’s packed with history, culture, and natural beauty. With its proximity to Amsterdam, it’s the perfect destination for a day trip or a week-long excursion. From its museums and landmarks to its scenic nature trails and delicious cuisine, Monnickendam has something for everyone. Plan your trip to this charming town today and experience the best of the Netherlands!
