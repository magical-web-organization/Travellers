Beemster Travel Guide

Beemster, located in the Zaanstreek-Waterland region of North Holland in the Netherlands, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a picturesque place that offers a blend of natural and cultural attractions. Its lush green polders, historic windmills, and well-preserved Dutch architecture make it a must-visit destination for travelers. Here is your comprehensive Beemster travel guide that covers all the aspects of this beautiful destination.

Introduction to Beemster

Beemster is a town located in the municipality of Purmerend, approximately 20 kilometers north-east of Amsterdam. It is famous for its agricultural landscape, which has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its unique land reclamation techniques. Beemster has a population of around 9,500 people and is a popular destination among travelers for its preserved Dutch heritage and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Beemster

Beemster has a plethora of places to visit that caters to the taste of every traveler. Here are some of the must-visit places in Beemster:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beemster Cheese Museum: Learn about the history of Beemster Cheese and how it is made.
  • Rijper Museum: Discover the history of the beautiful town of De Rijp, which was once the center of herring fishing in Northern Holland.
  • Grote Kerk: A historic church with an impressive organ and unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Beemster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • De Beemster Polder: Explore the lush green landscape of Beemster by biking or hiking on the winding trails.
  • Schermerhorn Windmill Museum: A museum that showcases the history and working of windmills that were used to keep the polders dry.
  • Beemster Arboretum: A botanical garden with a collection of over 2,000 species and varieties of trees, shrubs, and perennials.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beemster

Beemster has a variety of hiking and biking routes that are perfect for exploration of the beautiful countryside. Here are some of the popular routes you must take:

  • Beemster Ringvaart: A 35-kilometer long exploratory route that takes you through the polders and windmills.
  • De Beemsterpad: A 17.5-kilometer long hiking trail that showcases the history and beauty of the Beemster landscape.
  • Zuiderzeepad: A 500-kilometer long hiking trail that takes you through the picturesque villages and towns of the Netherlands.

Best Things to Do in Beemster: Activities and Tours

Beemster offers a range of activities and tours that cater to the interests of all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Beemster:

  • Cheese Tasting: Taste the famous Beemster Cheese and learn about its history and production.
  • Canal Tour: Take a boat tour through the canals of Beemster and enjoy the stunning views of the landscape and windmills.
  • Bike Tour: Explore the countryside of Beemster on a bike tour and discover the charming villages and farms.

Here is a week-long itinerary for Beemster that includes the best places to visit and things to do:

Food and Drink Scene of Beemster

Beemster has a rich culinary culture that reflects its agricultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Beemster:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beemster Cheese: Rich and creamy Beemster Cheese that is known for its unique taste.
  • Poffertjes: Miniature Dutch pancakes that are a popular street food in the Netherlands.
  • Stamppot: A traditional Dutch dish consisting of mashed potatoes and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Beemster Beer: A local beer that is brewed using Beemster’s spring water.
  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin that comes in various flavors and aromas.
  • Dutch Coffee: A smooth and creamy coffee that is typically served with a cookie or speculaas.

Where to Stay in Beemster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beemster offers various options for accommodation that range from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hotel De Beurs: A modern hotel with free wifi and a restaurant that serves Dutch cuisine.
  • De Rijper Eilanden: A stylish guesthouse located in an old farm that is surrounded by a garden.
  • Stayokay Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms.

What to Buy in Beemster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Beemster has plenty of options when it comes to buying souvenirs and local products to take back home. Here are some of the popular choices:

  • Beemster Cheese: A perfect souvenir that is unique to Beemster.
  • Dutch Wooden Shoes: Traditional Dutch clogs that make for an interesting souvenir.
  • Dutch Tea Towels: Colorful tea towels that are decorated with Dutch motifs and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Beemster

Beemster has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its agricultural history. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and there are many festivals and events that celebrate the Dutch culture. Beemster also has specific customs and traditions, which include:

  • Bicycling: Bicycling is a way of life in Beemster, and it is the preferred mode of transportation for both locals and visitors.
  • Dutch Greetings: The Dutch are known for their straightforwardness, and they generally greet with a handshake.
  • Coffee and Cookies: Coffee and cookies are an essential part of the Dutch culture, and it is customary to have them during meetings and gatherings.

Best Time to Visit Beemster

The best time to visit Beemster is during the spring and summer (April to September) when the weather is pleasant, and the flowers bloom. However, the charm of Beemster is not limited to any specific season, and it is a beautiful destination to visit all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beemster

Do:

  • Try the Beemster Cheese and other local dishes.
  • Explore the countryside with a bike tour or a hike.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Litter or damage historical monuments and buildings.
  • Ignore the bike lanes and pedestrian crossings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beemster

Beemster is a peaceful and safe destination with low crime rates. However, travelers must still take the necessary safety precautions, such as:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings and valuables in public places.
  • Observe traffic rules, especially when cycling or walking.
  • Use certified taxi services.

Health in Beemster

Travelers to Beemster do not require any specific vaccinations or health precautions. However, it is advisable to carry a medical insurance policy that covers emergency healthcare.

Getting to Beemster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beemster is easily accessible by various means of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Beemster

  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport: Located approximately 31 kilometers away from Beemster, Amsterdam Schiphol Airport is the nearest and largest airport in the Netherlands.

Bus Stations Near Beemster

  • Purmerend Bus Station: Located in the city center of Purmerend, the bus station offers multiple bus connections to various destinations, including Beemster.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Beemster is Purmerend Overwhere, which is approximately four kilometers away from Beemster.
  • Car: Beemster is easily accessible by car via the A7 motorway. The town also has ample parking spots for cars.

How to Get Around in Beemster

Beemster is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There is also a bus service that connects the town with nearby locations. Taxis are also available, but they can be quite expensive.

Where to Go Next from Beemster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beemster is an excellent base to explore the neighboring towns and attractions. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • De Rijp: A charming town known for its historic windmills and red-brick houses.
  • Alkmaar: A beautiful city with a well-preserved Old Town and famous for its cheese market.
  • Waterland: A region known for its picturesque villages, canals, and nature reserves.

Conclusion

Beemster is a unique destination that offers a beautiful blend of natural and cultural attractions. The town’s well-preserved heritage and picturesque countryside make it a must-visit destination for travelers.
