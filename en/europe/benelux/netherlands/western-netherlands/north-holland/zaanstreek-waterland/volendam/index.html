Introduction to Volendam

Volendam, a picturesque village located in North Holland, is a popular destination for visitors to the Netherlands. Known for its traditional Dutch wooden houses, winding streets, and charming harbor, Volendam retains its quaint, Old World charm. Its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and thriving fishing industry make it an ideal vacation spot for travelers around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Volendam

Whether you are seeking indoor cultural experiences or outdoor adventures, Volendam has something for every type of traveler. Here are the top attractions to visit during your trip to this Dutch village:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Volendam Museum – Learn about the daily life and culture of the Volendam residents through exhibits on traditional costumes, fishing industry, and music.
  2. Edam Museum – Explore the history of cheese production in Edam and learn about the town’s significance in Dutch trade and commerce.
  3. Saint Vincentius Church – Marvel at the ornate, Gothic architecture of this 17th-century church, which is adorned with stained-glass windows, religious paintings, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Volendam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dijkje – Take a leisurely walk along the Dijkje, a dike that borders the harbor and provides stunning views of the boats and the coastline.
  2. The Markermeer – Rent a boat or yacht and sail to the Markermeer, a large lake that offers ample opportunities for fishing, swimming, and watersports.
  3. De Boeltjes – Take a guided tour of De Boeltjes, a renowned herring smokehouse where you can sample freshly smoked herring served with onions, pickles, and bread.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volendam

Volendam is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering several scenic routes for exploring the area’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

Some of the most popular trails include:

  1. Walk along the Dijkje – Take a slow stroll along the harbor and enjoy the beautiful views of the sea and fishing boats.
  2. Sailing and Biking Route - Take a leisurely bike ride on the dike and catch the IJsselmeer ferry to discover the fishing village of Marken.
  3. Markermeer Circular Hike – Explore the beautiful natural scenery of the Markermeer lake and its wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Volendam: Activities and Tours

From boat tours to traditional villages, Volendam has a range of activities and tours to offer visitors.

  1. Visit the Cheese Market in Edam – Sample some of the world-renowned Edam cheese and learn about the history of cheese production in the Netherlands.
  2. Take a boat tour to Marken – Enjoy a scenic boat ride and explore the charming fishing village of Marken.
  3. Explore Volendam’s traditional wooden houses – Take a stroll through the village’s winding streets and admire the traditional Dutch architecture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Volendam

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Volendam:

Day 1 - Volendam Village Walk: Explore the village center, visit the Volendam Museum and Saint Vincentius Church, and dine on fresh herring at De Boeltjes.

Day 2 - Waterland Adventure: Rent a bike and ride along the dike to Marken, take a boat tour, and have lunch at De Taanderij.

Day 3 - Edam Excursion: Visit Edam Museum, walk through the charming, historic town center, and sample the local cheese.

Day 4 - Scenic Boat and Bike Ride: Take a boat tour to Marken, rent a bike and ride through Waterland, and have lunch at Het Wapen van Middelie.

Day 5 - Marken Villages Tour: Visit the fish auction, take a walk on Marken’s northern peninsula, and dine on traditional Dutch cuisine.

Day 6 - Amsterdam Exploration: Visit the many museums and landmarks of the capital city of the Netherlands.

Day 7 - Relaxation Day: Enjoy a leisurely day at Volendam’s beaches and on the Markermeer lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Volendam

Volendam is known for its fresh seafood and traditional Dutch dishes, such as smoked herring and stamppot (mashed potatoes). Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Freshly smoked herring – Served with onions, pickles, and bread, this is a classic Volendam dish.
  2. Stamppot – This Dutch dish consists of mashed potatoes, kale, and sausage, and can be found in most restaurants in Volendam.
  3. Eel – This fish is a staple of the Dutch cuisine, and is usually served smoked or stewed.

Local Beverages

  1. Jenever – This traditional Dutch spirit is similar to gin, and is often served with a steaming hot cup of coffee.
  2. Bokbier – A strong and dark beer, Bokbier is widely available in the fall and is perfect to pair with a hearty meal.

Where to Stay in Volendam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Volendam has several accommodations to meet the needs of every budget and preference.

Some of the best options include:

  1. Hotel Spaander – A historic hotel that has hosted many famous Dutch artists, such as Piet Mondrian and Jan Sluijters.
  2. Van der Valk Volendam – A modern hotel that offers a gym, sauna, and hot tub, and is conveniently located near the village center.
  3. Le Chapeau Rouge – This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and friendly service, and is located near the harbor.

What to Buy in Volendam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Volendam is known for its traditional Dutch wooden shoes, clothing, and fishing souvenirs. Be sure to bring home some of the local products as a souvenir:

  1. Wooden shoes – These traditional Dutch shoes, also known as clogs, make a great decorative piece or can be worn as comfortable footwear.
  2. Traditional Dutch clothing – Volendam is the perfect place to purchase traditional Dutch clothing, such as the famous Volendam costumes.
  3. Fishing souvenirs – Bring home some smoked fish or fishing gear that is synonymous with the heritage of Volendam.

Local Culture and Customs of Volendam

Volendam has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its architecture, costumes, and fishing traditions. Locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, with many proud of their cultural heritage and happy to share it with others.

Best Time to Visit Volendam

The best time to visit Volendam is during the summer from June to August when the weather is warm and the village is bustling with tourists and events. However, spring and fall can also be great times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volendam

Here are some practical tips to keep in mind while visiting Volendam:

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine, including smoked herring and traditional Dutch dishes.
  2. Take a boat tour for scenic views of the village.
  3. Bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the winding streets.

Don’t:

  1. Wear high heels or uncomfortable shoes due to the cobblestoned streets.
  2. Touch the boats, as they can be private property.
  3. Take photos of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Volendam can expect a safe and clean environment. It is recommended to purchase travel insurance and check the latest safety advice and health information before your trip.

Safety in Volendam

Volendam is a relatively safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, it is advised to take normal precautions to protect yourself from petty theft or pickpockets.

Health in Volendam

Volendam has a modern healthcare system, and emergency services are available. Remember to bring any necessary medications or prescriptions with you on your trip.

Getting to Volendam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Volendam is located in the Zaanstreek-Waterland region of the Netherlands, making it easily accessible through various means of transportation.

Airports Near Volendam

The two nearest airports to Volendam are Amsterdam Airport Schiphol, which is 29 kilometers away, and Eindhoven Airport, which is 148 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Volendam

Volendam is easily accessible by bus from Amsterdam. The bus ride takes about 30 minutes from Amsterdam’s central station and costs around €6.50.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available for visitors who prefer to travel independently and comfortably.

How to Get Around in Volendam

Volendam is a small village, and the best way to get around is on foot or by bicycle. Bikes can be rented from various rental shops in the village, ranging from €10 to €20 per day.

Where to Go Next from Volendam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Volendam is located near several other popular Dutch destinations, making it an ideal hub for excursions. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  1. Amsterdam – Explore the vibrant capital city of the Netherlands, home to museums, galleries, restaurants, and nightlife.
  2. Zaandam – Visit the world-famous windmills and learn about the region’s history of agriculture and industrialization.
  3. Enkhuizen – Discover this charming, historic town that is home to the Zuiderzee Museum, which showcases the region’s maritime history.

Conclusion

Volendam is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unforgettable adventures. With its scenic views, delicious cuisine, and welcoming locals, a trip to this Dutch village is sure to leave a lasting impression. Plan your trip now and experience the beauty of Volendam for yourself!
