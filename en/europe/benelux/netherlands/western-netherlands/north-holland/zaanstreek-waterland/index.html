Introduction to Zaanstreek-Waterland

Zaanstreek-Waterland is a charming region in the North Holland province of the Netherlands. It encompasses the areas of Zaanstad, Wormerland, Purmerend, Beemster, and Landsmeer, and is easily accessible from Amsterdam. The region is renowned for its picturesque landscapes, lush green meadows and fields, picturesque waterways, and picturesque towns.

Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, rich cultural heritage, or just want to relax and enjoy the natural beauty, Zaanstreek-Waterland has something for everyone. In this guide, we will explore the top things to see and do in this region, along with some tips to make your trip smooth and enjoyable.

List of Places to Visit in Zaanstreek-Waterland

Here are some of the top must-visit attractions in Zaanstreek-Waterland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zaanse Schans: Known for its historic windmills, Zaanse Schans is a living museum that transports you back to the Dutch Golden Age.
  • Museumstoomtram: An interactive and fascinating museum dedicated to steam trams and the industrial heritage of the region.
  • Museum Het Schip: An architectural museum that showcases unique Amsterdam School architecture and design.

Natural Attractions of Zaanstreek-Waterland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Twiske: A vast nature reserve dotted with picturesque lakes, winding waterways, and winding forest trails.
  • Het Wormer- en Jisperveld: A vast expanse of wetland, home to various species of birds, and an ideal place for nature walks and peaceful boat rides.
  • Zaanse Schans: Apart from the windmills, the area offers scenic views of the Zaan River, beautiful canals, and quaint houses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zaanstreek-Waterland

There are many scenic cycling and hiking routes throughout Zaanstreek-Waterland. Here are a few that are worth exploring:

  • Cycle route ‘‘Waterland en Wormerland’‘: A scenic route that takes you through picturesque Dutch villages, beautiful waterways, and vast expanses of nature reserves.
  • Hemlandpad: A stunning hiking trail that covers the Beemster polder and provides spectacular views all along the way.
  • De Streekpad: A 150km-long trail that takes you through the region’s most beautiful natural areas, including wetlands, old village lanes, and lovely waterfronts.

Best Things to Do in Zaanstreek-Waterland: Activities and Tours

Whether you want to immerse yourself in the region’s rich cultural heritage, relax and enjoy nature, or try some Dutch delicacies, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained in Zaanstreek-Waterland. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a boat tour: An excellent way to explore the region’s waterways and enjoy scenic views of the countryside.
  • Visit a cheese farm: The Beemster region is renowned for its cheese, and many farms offer tours and tastings. Try out the local Gouda cheese for an authentic Dutch experience.
  • Explore the historic windmills: Windmills have been a crucial part of Dutch culture, and the Zaanse Schans offers the perfect opportunity to discover the iconic structures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zaanstreek-Waterland

If you have a week to spare, here’s a suggested itinerary for exploring Zaanstreek-Waterland:

  • Day 1: Explore the Zaanse Schans and check out the historic windmills, museums, and picturesque canals.
  • Day 2: Visit the Beemster and explore the Beemster polder, cycling or hiking.
  • Day 3: Take a boat tour of the region’s waterways, relax, and enjoy the scenic beauty.
  • Day 4: Head to the Het Wormer- en Jisperveld nature reserve and enjoy bird watching and nature walks.
  • Day 5: Learn about the region’s industrial heritage at the Museumstoomtram.
  • Day 6: Join a cheese farm tour and enjoy some delicious Gouda cheese.
  • Day 7: Spend the day exploring Purmerend, seeing the landmarks and sites of historic significance.

Food and Drink Scene of Zaanstreek-Waterland

The region boasts an excellent food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties you must try!

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poffertjes: A Dutch mini-pancake, often served with powdered sugar and butter.
  • Hollandse Nieuwe: Fresh herring served with onions and pickles.
  • Bitterballen: A deep-fried snack with beef or veal ragout.

Local Beverages

  • Dutch gin (Jenever): The drink has been a favorite of the Dutch for over 400 years and is widely available in the Zaanstreek region.
  • Beer: The Netherlands has a rich beer culture, and the region boasts several breweries and iconic bars.

Make sure to pair your meal with a refreshing ‘speciaalbier’ (special beer) or a glass of Jenever to add to the experience.

Where to Stay in Zaanstreek-Waterland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a range of accommodation options throughout Zaanstreek-Waterland. Some of the recommended guesthouses, hotels, and hostels include:

  • Hotel-Restaurant De Beurs: A chic hotel in the heart of Purmerend.
  • B&B Bij de Molen: A cozy guest house that’s just a stone’s throw away from the Zaanse Schans.
  • B&B Het Dorpsplein: A modern and comfortable guesthouse located in the Beemster region.

What to Buy in Zaanstreek-Waterland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a slice of Zaanstreek-Waterland, here are some local products to consider:

  • Cheese: Beemster cheese is a must-try, and several farms offer a wide variety to suit all tastes.
  • Clogs: A quintessential Dutch souvenir, clogs make for a unique and authentic keepsake.
  • Delftware: Beautiful hand-painted ceramics, originating from the city of Delft, make for a great gift or addition to your own home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Zaanstreek-Waterland

The region prides itself on its distinctive Dutch culture and customs, which have been passed down through generations. One of the unique customs is the ‘klompdansen’ (clog dance). Traditionally, wooden shoes were worn out of necessity to protect workers’ feet in the fields, but over time, it developed into a fun dance, with intricate footwork that’s a delight to watch.

Best Time to Visit Zaanstreek-Waterland

Zaanstreek-Waterland is beautiful all year round, but the best time to visit is between April and October when the weather is pleasant, and the natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zaanstreek-Waterland

Do:

  • Do learn some basic Dutch, as it will go a long way in making your interactions with locals easier.
  • Do try out the local Dutch cheese and cuisine.
  • Do rent a bike or take a walking tour and explore the scenic countryside.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring an umbrella or a raincoat, as the weather in the Netherlands can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t bike on the highways.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to Dutch culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zaanstreek-Waterland

Zaanstreek-Waterland is a safe place to travel, but it’s still essential to take necessary precautions to stay safe. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Zaanstreek-Waterland

The Netherlands has an excellent health system, and Zaanstreek-Waterland has several hospitals and clinics. However, it’s still recommended to have good travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise.

Getting to Zaanstreek-Waterland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zaanstreek-Waterland

The closest airport to Zaanstreek-Waterland is Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, which is easily accessible via train, taxi, or bus.

Bus Stations Near Zaanstreek-Waterland

Several bus stations connect the region to Amsterdam and other nearby cities, making it an easy and practical choice.

Other Options

If you’re looking to explore the region at your own pace, renting a car or bike is a great way to do so. Bike rental services are easily available throughout the region, making it easy to enjoy scenic routes with ease.

How to Get Around in Zaanstreek-Waterland

Navigating the region is relatively easy thanks to the extensive network of roads and waterways. Buses and trains are punctual and efficient, and most major towns and attractions can be reached via public transport. Biking is also a popular option, and rental services are readily available all over Zaanstreek-Waterland.

Where to Go Next from Zaanstreek-Waterland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zaanstreek-Waterland is ideally located, and there are several nearby places worth exploring, including:

  • Amsterdam: The capital city of the Netherlands is only a short distance away and makes for an excellent day trip.
  • Alkmaar: The charming city of Alkmaar is renowned for its historic architecture and traditional cheese market.
  • Volendam: A picturesque fishing village that’s known for traditional Dutch costumes and a vibrant harbor.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and thriving food and drink scene, Zaanstreek-Waterland perfectly encapsulates the best of the Netherlands. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or looking for a relaxing getaway, this region is sure to impress.
