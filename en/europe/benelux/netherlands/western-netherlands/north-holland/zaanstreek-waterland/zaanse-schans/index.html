Introduction to Zaanse Schans

Zaanse Schans is a charming village in the Zaanstreek-Waterland region of North Holland, Western Netherlands. It was established in the 18th and 19th centuries, and it features a collection of windmills, historic buildings, and museums. Zaanse Schans is considered a living open-air museum that allows visitors to step back in time and experience traditional Dutch life and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Zaanse Schans

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Zaanse Schans Museum: This museum is housed in a historic building, and it offers a glimpse into the history, culture, and industry of the area.
  2. Zaans Museum: This museum chronicles the industrial heritage of the area, including the production of chocolate, biscuits, and paints.
  3. Windmills: Zaanse Schans has numerous windmills, which used to operate sawmills, oil mills and paint mills, among others. You can visit and tour some of these windmills.
  4. Zaanse Schans Cheese Farm: This farm produces traditional Dutch cheese and offers visitors the chance to see the cheese-making process.

Natural Attractions of Zaanse Schans: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Zaanse Schans River Tour: Experience the waterways between the windmills while taking a river cruise.
  2. De Vijf Broers: This park is an ideal place for a picnic while enjoying the scenic view of the windmills.
  3. De Jisper: Rent a bike and cycle along the waterways between the windmills.
  4. Jagersveld: Take a walk or cycle through this peaceful park, which is located just outside of Zaanse Schans.
  5. De Wijde Wormer: Take a boat tour of this beautiful lake area, which is just a short drive outside of Zaanse Schans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zaanse Schans

  1. Zaanse Schans Windmill Route: This route takes visitors on a 13-kilometer bike ride around the windmills, with stops at De Vijf Broers and the Zaanse Schans Cheese Farm.
  2. Zaandijker Loop: This 11-kilometer bike route takes visitors along the Zaanse Schans river and through the picturesque Dutch countryside.
  3. Krommenie-Assendelft Route: This 48-kilometer bike route takes visitors through several historic Dutch villages, including Krommenie and Assendelft.

Best Things to Do in Zaanse Schans: Activities and Tours

  1. Chocolate Making Workshop at De Zaanse Chocolade: visitors can participate in chocolate-making workshops and create their own custom chocolate creations.
  2. Boat Tour of Zaanse Schans: Enjoy a relaxing boat tour through the waterways between the windmills
  3. Explore the Countryside: Explore the historic Dutch countryside by bicycle or on foot
  4. Traditional Dutch Dinner: Experience traditional Dutch cuisine at one of Zaanse Schans’ many cafes and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Zaanse Schans

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Raw Herring: A traditional Dutch dish served with onions and pickles.
  2. Stamppot: A popular Dutch dish made with mashed potatoes, kale, and sausage.
  3. Kroket: A fried, breaded mashed potato ball typically filled with beef ragout.
  4. Poffertjes: Miniature pancakes served with butter and powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  1. Genever: A traditional Dutch liquor.
  2. Heineken: A popular Dutch beer, which has its headquarters in Amsterdam.
  3. Lemonade: A popular drink in the Netherlands, which comes in different flavors.

What to Buy in Zaanse Schans: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Cheese: Zaanse Schans is known for its cheese production, so bring home some delicious Dutch cheese.
  2. Clogs: Wooden clogs are a symbol of traditional Dutch culture and can be purchased in various shops throughout Zaanse Schans.
  3. Delftware: Beautiful blue and white pottery is a specialty of the Netherlands and can be found in many shops in Zaanse Schans.

Local Culture and Customs of Zaanse Schans

The Dutch are known for their liberal culture and relaxed attitude towards life. Visitors to Zaanse Schans should be aware of a few customs, including:

  • Biking: Biking is a very popular means of transportation in the Netherlands, and visitors are encouraged to rent bicycles and explore Zaanse Schans by bike.
  • Punctuality: The Dutch value punctuality and it is considered impolite to be late to appointments or meetings.
  • Tipping: In restaurants, it is customary to tip 10% of the total bill.
