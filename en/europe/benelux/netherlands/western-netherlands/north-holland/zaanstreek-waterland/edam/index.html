Introduction to Edam

Edam is a charming city located in the Zaanstreek-Waterland region of North Holland, Western Netherlands. Famous for its iconic cheese, the city boasts a rich history, beautiful architecture, and pristine natural surroundings. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Edam, offering a list of must-visit attractions, popular activities, food and drink scene, local culture, and travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Edam

Edam has numerous attractions that cater to all types of travelers. Here is a list of must-visit places in Edam.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Edams Museum: One of the best ways to get a glimpse into Edam’s rich history and culture is by visiting the Edams Museum. It showcases the city’s history and beautiful architecture and offers a range of exhibits, including artifacts, paintings, and photographs.
  • Damplein: This historic square is home to numerous historical buildings, including the town hall and church. It’s a great place to stroll around and admire the architecture.
  • Grote Kerk: This beautiful church was built in the 16th century and boasts a stunning organ and stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Edam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dijkgraaf van Edam: A bike or walking tour along the Dijkgraaf van Edam offers beautiful scenic views of the countryside.
  • Beemster Polder: A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Beemster Polder is a reclaimed lowland area that offers an incredible experience of the Dutch farmland.
  • Ijsselmeer Lake: The largest lake in the Netherlands, the Ijsselmeer Lake is perfect for water sports and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edam

Edam is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are the best routes:

  • Edam to Volendam: This scenic route takes you through beautiful countryside and charming villages, including the famous fishing village of Volendam.
  • Edam to Monnickendam: This route takes you through beautiful nature reserves, including the Het Twiske recreational area.
  • Edam to Purmerend: This route is perfect for avid cyclists and takes you through the Beemster Polder, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Best Things to Do in Edam: Activities and Tours

Edam offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Edam:

  • Cheese Market: A must-visit place in Edam, the famous cheese market is held every Wednesday morning during the summer months. Here you can witness the traditional cheese-making process and learn about Edam’s famous cheese.
  • Edam Canal Tour: A canal tour is an excellent way to explore Edam’s canals and admire the beautiful architecture along the way.
  • Bike Tour: Edam’s countryside is perfect for cycling, and a bike tour is a great way to explore the beautiful surroundings while getting some exercise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edam

Here’s a week-long itinerary for your trip to Edam:

Day 1 Arrival in Edam, check-in at hotel, explore the city center
Day 2 Visit Edams Museum, Grote Kerk, and Damplein
Day 3 Edam Canal Tour, bike tour of the surrounding countryside
Day 4 Visit Beemster Polder, Het Twiske recreational area
Day 5 Cheese market, visit Volendam
Day 6 Bike tour of the Beemster Polder
Day 7 Departure from Edam

Food and Drink Scene of Edam

Edam is known for its cheese, and there’s no shortage of cheese-related delicacies to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Edam Cheese: You can’t come to Edam without trying the cheese. The cheese is available in various ages and tastes, and you can buy some to bring home.
  • Appeltaart: A traditional Dutch apple pie, perfect with a cup of coffee or tea.
  • Poffertjes: Miniature fluffy pancakes topped with powdered sugar and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch liquor, similar to gin, made from juniper berries, malt wine, and other botanicals.
  • Heineken Beer: The famous Dutch beer can be found in bars and restaurants around Edam, as well as at the Heineken Experience in Amsterdam.
  • Koffie Verkeerd: The Dutch take on a latte, with more milk than coffee, is an excellent way to start your day.

Where to Stay in Edam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Edam has a range of accommodations to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel De Fortuna: A cozy hotel located in the heart of Edam’s historic center, just a few steps from the famous cheese market.
  • Waterland Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the countryside.
  • Stayokay Hostel: This budget-friendly option is ideal for young travelers and offers comfortable beds in shared rooms.

What to Buy in Edam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

In addition to cheese, there are several other souvenirs and local products to bring home from Edam. Here are a few ideas:

  • Delftware: This traditional blue-and-white pottery is a quintessential Dutch souvenir and can be found in various shops around Edam.
  • Stroopwafels: These thin Dutch waffles filled with caramel syrup are a must-try snack and make excellent gifts.
  • Cheese knives: A useful and practical souvenir, cheese knives are available in various sizes and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Edam

Edam has a rich history and a vibrant local culture. Here are some interesting facts and customs to know:

  • Edam Cheese: The city has been producing cheese since the 14th century, and the cheese market tradition dates back to 1594.
  • Cycling Culture: The Netherlands is known for its cycling culture, and Edam is no exception. Cycling is a popular way to get around in Edam and the surrounding countryside.
  • Tulips: The Netherlands is famous for its tulips, and Edam is no exception. During spring, you can see countless tulip fields in the surrounding countryside.

Best Time to Visit Edam

The best time to visit Edam is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are various outdoor events and festivals. The cheese market also takes place during these months. However, if you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy the beautiful fall colors, September and October are also excellent months to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edam

Do:

  • Do try the cheese.
  • Do rent a bike and explore the countryside.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places.
  • Don’t be too loud or disrespectful in public places.
  • Don’t forget to bring your umbrella, as the weather in the Netherlands can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edam

Edam is a safe city, and crime rates are low. However, as with any city, it’s important to take necessary precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Edam

The Netherlands has an excellent healthcare system, and there are numerous hospitals and medical facilities in the area. If you’re traveling from a non-EU country, make sure to have proper travel insurance.

Getting to Edam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Edam is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Edam

  • Amsterdam Schipol Airport: This is the closest airport to Edam and is about a 30-minute drive.

Bus Stations Near Edam

  • Edam Bus Station: This is located in the historic center of Edam and serves various destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train stations are in Amsterdam and Purmerend, both of which are well-connected to Edam by bus.

How to Get Around in Edam

Edam is a small city, and the best way to get around is on foot or by bike. You can also rent a car, but keep in mind that parking can be a challenge in the historic center.

Where to Go Next from Edam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Edam is located in a beautiful region of the Netherlands, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring. Here are some of the best options:

  • Amsterdam: Just a 30-minute drive, Amsterdam is a world-famous city known for its beautiful canals, museums, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Volendam: This charming fishing village is located just a few kilometers from Edam and is famous for its traditional costumes and unique wooden houses.
  • Zaanse Schans: This open-air museum located in the Zaan region is home to several historic windmills and traditional Dutch houses.

Conclusion

Edam is a charming city with a rich history, beautiful architecture, and stunning natural surroundings. It’s a perfect destination for travelers looking for a blend of culture, nature, and adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip to Edam, Netherlands.
