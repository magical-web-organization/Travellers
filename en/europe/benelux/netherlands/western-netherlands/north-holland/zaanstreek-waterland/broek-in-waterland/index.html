Introduction to Broek in Waterland

Broek in Waterland is a picturesque village in the Zaanstreek-Waterland region of North Holland, Western Netherlands. Its charming wooden houses, canals, and lush greenery make it a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic Dutch experience.

This travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information to plan a trip to Broek in Waterland. From must-visit attractions to local customs, safety tips, and transportation options, we have got you covered. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Broek in Waterland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Het Schooltje Museum - This school museum is dedicated to the educational system of the early 20th century. It offers visitors a glimpse into the past and provides a unique learning experience.

  • Waterlandsmuseum de Speeltoren - Housed in the iconic Speeltoren tower, this museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region. It has an extensive collection of historical artifacts, photographs, and artwork.

  • Grote Kerk - This impressive church dates back to the 18th century and is a marvel of architecture. Visitors can admire the intricate details of the church’s interior and learn about its fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Broek in Waterland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Waterland Nature Reserve - Located just outside of Broek in Waterland, this nature reserve is perfect for nature lovers. It offers hiking and biking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Boat Tour on the Broeker Vaart - Take a leisurely boat tour along the canals of Broek in Waterland for a unique perspective of the village’s scenic views.

  • Bike Rental - Rent a bike and explore the village’s stunning landscapes. Cycling is the perfect way to take in the fresh air and soak up the Dutch countryside’s beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Broek in Waterland

  • Broeker Route - This 10 km circular bike route is a great way to explore the village and its surrounding areas. It takes visitors through scenic countryside landscapes, picturesque villages, and past historic landmarks.

  • Waterland Route - This 20 km bike route takes visitors on a scenic tour of the region’s most beautiful landscapes, including winding rivers, green pastures, and quaint villages.

  • Molenroute - Explore the area’s historic windmills on this 23 km cycling route. Along the way, visitors will pass by several picturesque villages and scenic countryside vistas.

Best Things to Do in Broek in Waterland: Activities and Tours

  • Cheese and Clog Making Tour - Learn about Dutch cheese-making and wooden clog manufacturing on this unique tour. Visitors will have the opportunity to try on traditional wooden clogs and sample delicious Dutch cheeses.

  • Food and Wine Tasting Tour - Sample the village’s delicious local food and drink on this food and wine tasting tour. Visitors can explore traditional Dutch cuisine and try local wines and craft beers.

  • Walking Tour of the Village - Join a local guide on a walking tour of the village’s historic landmarks, churches, and picturesque neighborhoods. Visitors will gain insight into the town’s fascinating history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Broek in Waterland

  • Day 1: Begin your trip with a walking tour of the village. Visit historic sites such as the Grote Kerk and Het Schooltje Museum.

  • Day 2: Rent a bike and explore the Waterland Route.

  • Day 3: Take a leisurely boat tour on the Broeker Vaart.

  • Day 4: Join a cheese and clog making tour for a unique cultural experience.

  • Day 5: Visit the Waterlandsmuseum de Speeltoren to learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Day 6: Take the Molenroute bike tour to explore the local windmills and surrounding countryside.

  • Day 7: End your trip with a food and wine tasting tour, sampling the village’s fantastic local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Broek in Waterland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dutch Pannenkoeken - This famous Dutch pancake is a must-try dish while in Broek in Waterland. It is similar to a crepe and is usually served with powdered sugar, syrup, or fresh fruit.

  • Gezelligheid - A traditional Dutch snack made of cheese, sausage, and bread.

  • Hollandse Nieuwe Haring - A popular Dutch street food dish made of fresh raw herring served with onions and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever - A traditional Dutch liquor made from juniper berries and grain. It is often served alongside a bitterballen (a traditional Dutch snack).

  • Hertog Jan Beer - A local beer brewed in the region with distinct flavors. It is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

  • Koffie Verkeerd - A traditional Dutch coffee made with milk and served with toast or a croissant.

  • Broek in Waterland Apartment - A spacious apartment with a fully equipped kitchen, free Wi-Fi, and living room.

  • Het Witte Huis - A historic guesthouse with a charming garden, located in the center of the village.

What to Buy in Broek in Waterland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Delft Blue Pottery - The village is famous for its hand-painted Delft Blue pottery. Visitors can purchase unique pieces at local shops and markets.

  • Stroopwafels - A delicious Dutch treat, these syrup-filled waffle cookies make the perfect souvenir.

  • Tulip Bulbs - Broek in Waterland is in the heart of the Dutch tulip-growing region. Visitors can purchase tulip bulbs at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Broek in Waterland

  • Cheese Making - Cheese-making is a popular tradition in the region, with several local cheese farms producing delicious varieties.

  • Cycling - Cycling is a way of life in the Netherlands, and Broek in Waterland is no exception. Visitors can rent bikes and explore the village’s beautiful countryside landscapes.

  • Windmills - Windmills are an iconic symbol of Dutch culture, and visitors to Broek in Waterland can explore several historic windmills in the area.

Best Time to Visit Broek in Waterland

The best time to visit Broek in Waterland is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild, and the countryside is in full bloom. The village is also charming during the winter months when visitors can enjoy festive holiday markets and snow-covered landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Broek in Waterland

  • Do try the local food and drink, especially the famous Dutch pancakes and fresh herring.

  • Do visit the historic windmills of the region for a unique cultural experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Broek in Waterland

Broek in Waterland is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take common sense precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and watching out for pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Broek in Waterland

Healthcare services are easily accessible in the Netherlands, and visitors should have no trouble finding medical help if needed. Visitors should also ensure they have adequate travel insurance coverage.

Getting to Broek in Waterland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Broek in Waterland

  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport - The nearest major airport, located approximately 30 km from Broek in Waterland. It is easily accessible by taxi or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Broek in Waterland

  • Marken Express - This bus service travels between Amsterdam and Broek in Waterland, making several stops along the way.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Amsterdam to nearby Purmerend, followed by a bus or taxi to Broek in Waterland.

How to Get Around in Broek in Waterland

Renting a bike is the best way to get around and explore the village’s picturesque landscapes and surrounding countryside. Visitors can also take a leisurely boat tour along the canals or join a guided walking tour of the village’s historic landmarks.

Where to Go Next from Broek in Waterland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
