Introduction to Amstelveen

Amstelveen is a charming town located in the province of North Holland in Western Netherlands. It is a peaceful haven only a few kilometers away from the bustling city of Amsterdam. A typical Dutch town with an interesting history, Amstelveen has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Amstelveen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amstelveen Museum: This museum offers an extensive range of collections and exhibitions about the history and culture of Amstelveen.
  • Jan van der Togt Museum: A contemporary art museum situated in Amstelveen featuring both national and international art exhibits.
  • Het Schouw Theatre: A former church turned theatre, Het Schouw is a hub for cultural events and performances.

Natural Attractions of Amstelveen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amsterdamse Bos: A stunning city park that offers a range of outdoor activities including hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • De Poel: Located in the heart of Amstelveen, De Poel is a popular spot for boating and fishing.
  • Cobra Museum Sculpture Garden: A beautiful outdoor garden adorned with sculptures designed by renowned artists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amstelveen

  • Amsterdamse Bos Route: A 3 km trail through the Amsterdamse Bos featuring beautiful scenery and wildlife.
  • De Poel Route: A 5 km trail around the De Poel lake, this route showcases the picturesque beauty of Amstelveen.
  • Bovenkerkerpolder Route: A 10 km trail across the Bovenkerkerpolder, one gets to see the Dutch countryside of Amstelveen.

Best Things to Do in Amstelveen: Activities and Tours

  • Segway Tour: Experience the beauty of Amstelveen on a Segway tour through the lovely green environment.
  • Gondola Tour: Embark on a romantic ride through the picturesque canals of Amstelveen in a traditional Venetian gondola.
  • Bike Tour: Explore the city’s natural and cultural treasures with a knowledgeable guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amstelveen

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit the Amstelveen Museum, learn about the town’s history, and relax at the Amsterdamse Bos
Day 2 Participate in a bike tour and explore the beautiful sceneries of the Bovenkerkerpolder
Day 3 Visit the Jan van der Togt Museum, a contemporary art museum, and enjoy an evening show at the Het Schouw Theatre
Day 4 Enjoy a gondola or Segway tour and explore the charming canals of Amstelveen
Day 5 Visit the Cobra Museum Sculpture Garden and shop for souvenirs at the local markets
Day 6 Relax and pamper yourself at one of the local spas, or wine and dine at one of the local restaurants
Day 7 Take a day trip to the nearby town of Haarlem or Zaandam.

Food and Drink Scene of Amstelveen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot: A dish made of potatoes mashed with vegetables and meat.
  • Bitterballen: Deep-fried meatballs with a crispy exterior and a soft, flavorful inside.
  • Matjes Herring: A sweet and sour dish made with raw herring and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A Dutch gin distilled from malted barley, rye, and corn.
  • Heineken: A world-famous Dutch beer that has been brewed since 1873.

Where to Stay in Amstelveen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Hotel Amstelveen: A luxurious four-star hotel located in a serene environment.
  • Hotel Cafe Restaurant Abina: A cozy hotel featuring a restaurant with Dutch and international cuisine.
  • Amsterdam Forest Hotel: A modern hotel located between Amsterdam and Amstelveen.

What to Buy in Amstelveen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Dutch souvenirs: including wooden clogs, cheese, and tulip bulbs.
  • Artisan crafts: including hand-painted pottery, handcrafted jewellery, and local textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Amstelveen

A few cultural norms and customs to keep in mind:

  • Biking Culture: Biking is a main method of transportation in Amstelveen, so it’s essential to bike-aware.
  • Punctuality: Dutch people are very punctual, so avoid being late.
  • Politeness: Saying “thank you” and “please” are highly regarded in Dutch culture.

Best Time to Visit Amstelveen

The best time to visit Amstelveen is between April and October when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, the city is lively and buzzing with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amstelveen

Do:

  • Do tour the city by bike and explore the rich culture and scenic beauty of Amstelveen.
  • Do try traditional Dutch cuisine and drinks, such as ‘stroopwafels’ and ‘jenever’.
  • Do practice proper cycling etiquette by following road rules and using hand signals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry an umbrella or raincoat as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places in Amstelveen.
  • Don’t enter bike lanes reserved for bikes and always look out for them while crossing roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amstelveen

Amstelveen is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should keep an eye out for pickpocketing in crowded areas. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for immediate assistance.

Health in Amstelveen

Amstelveen has excellent healthcare facilities and hospitals that provide quality medical care.

Getting to Amstelveen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amstelveen

Schiphol Airport, located just 15 minutes away, is the main airport serving Amstelveen. Other nearby airports include Rotterdam The Hague Airport and Eindhoven Airport.

Bus Stations Near Amstelveen

Amstelveen bus station is situated near the town center and offers bus services to Amsterdam and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Alternative modes of transport include taxis, bike rentals, and car rental services.

How to Get Around in Amstelveen

The best way to get around Amstelveen is by bike. Bikes can be rented from hotels or bike rental shops. Public buses and taxis are also available for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Amstelveen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amsterdam: Explore the vibrant city of Amsterdam, known for its canals, museums, nightlife, and coffee shops.
  • Haarlem: Visit the medieval city of Haarlem, famous for its cobblestone streets, Gothic architecture and Grote Markt.
  • Zaandam: Visit Zaandam, known for its impressive windmills, charming buildings, and river cruises.

Conclusion

Amstelveen is a hidden gem that has something for everyone. From cultural attractions to natural beauty, from outdoor adventures to indoor activities, this lovely Dutch town has it all. Whether you’re interested in history or art, hiking or biking, shopping or eating, Amstelveen is a great destination to explore.
