Introduction to Oost

Located in the city of Amsterdam, Oost is a popular neighborhood that offers a blend of modern amenities and historic charm. Oost, which means “East” in English, is a diverse part of the city where you can find everything from trendy bars and restaurants to outdoor parks and markets.

List of Places to Visit in Oost

No matter what your interests are, there is something for everyone in Oost. Here are some of the must-see attractions in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tropenmuseum: A museum dedicated to world cultures, featuring exhibits of art, music, and photographs from around the globe.
  • Jewish Cemetery: This Jewish cemetery dates back to the 17th century and is a poignant reminder of the tragic history of the Jewish community in Amsterdam.
  • Het Scheepvaartmuseum (The National Maritime Museum): This museum showcases the history and art of navigation and shipping, with displays of old ships and shipbuilding tools.

Natural Attractions of Oost: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oosterpark: This picturesque park features a large lake surrounded by lush greenery, and is a great spot to relax and enjoy the outdoors.
  • Flevopark: Another scenic park in Oost, Flevopark features a beautiful lake, picnic areas, and biking and hiking trails.
  • Science Park Amsterdam: This academic and research park offers beautiful views of the city and has several cafes and restaurants where you can enjoy a meal or coffee.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oost

Oost is a great place to explore on foot or by bike, and there are several routes you can take to experience the area’s natural beauty:

  • Amsterdamse Bruggenroute: This route takes you on a tour of the many bridges in Amsterdam, including several in Oost.
  • Flevopark Route: A scenic route that takes you through Flevopark and along the waterfront.
  • Oosterpark Route: This route takes you through Oosterpark and past several historic landmarks in the area.

Best Things to Do in Oost: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Oost, no matter what your interests are. Here are some of the best activities and tours to take:

  • Food and Drink Tour: Sample some of the best food and drink in Oost on a guided tour of the area’s markets and cafes.
  • Street Art Tour: Oost is home to some of the best street art in Amsterdam, and a guided tour can take you to see some of the most stunning works.
  • Canal Tour: See the city from a different perspective on a canal tour that takes you through Oost and other neighborhoods in Amsterdam.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oost

If you’re planning to spend a week in Oost, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Oost

Oost is known for its diverse food and drink scene, with plenty of options for both locals and tourists.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stroopwafel: A traditional Dutch sweet made of two thin waffles stuck together with a layer of sweet syrup.
  • Poffertjes: Tiny, pillowy pancakes served with a variety of toppings, such as butter and powdered sugar.
  • Herring: A traditional Dutch herring dish that is typically served with onions and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin-style drink that can be flavored with a variety of herbs and spices.
  • Heineken: A popular Dutch beer that is now sold all over the world.
  • Coffee: Dutch coffee is known for its bold, rich flavor, and there are plenty of quaint cafes in Oost where you can enjoy a cup.

Where to Stay in Oost: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Oost, whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly hostel or a luxury hotel:

  • Generator Amsterdam: A stylish hostel with private rooms, dorms, and a bar.
  • Hotel Arena: A chic hotel with on-site dining and a bar, located in a former orphanage.
  • QO Amsterdam: A modern, eco-friendly hotel with luxurious amenities and breathtaking views.

What to Buy in Oost: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, there are plenty of options in Oost:

  • Cheese: Dutch cheeses, such as Gouda and Edam, are world-renowned and make great souvenirs.
  • Flowers: The Netherlands is known for its flower markets, and you can find beautiful bouquets and arrangements at the Dappermarkt in Oost.
  • Wine: Dutch wines may not be as well-known as those from France or Italy, but they are gaining popularity and can be found at local wine shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Oost

Oost, like the rest of Amsterdam, is known for its tolerance and liberal attitude. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as not littering and being mindful of noise levels in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Oost

The best time to visit Oost is during the spring and summer months, when the weather is mild and the parks and outdoor spaces are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oost

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Oost:

Do:

  • Do try traditional Dutch dishes, such as stroopwafel and herring.
  • Do explore the city on foot or by bike.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the area’s museums and parks.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Oost can rest assured that the area is generally safe and welcoming. However, as with any urban center, it is important to be mindful of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid theft or other crime.

Safety in Oost

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure.
  • Stick to well-lit, busy areas, particularly at night.
  • Take care when crossing roads and cycling on busy streets.

Health in Oost

  • Tap water in the Netherlands is safe to drink.
  • There are several pharmacies and medical clinics in Oost in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Oost: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oost is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options, including:

  • Train: Amsterdam Centraal Station is served by a number of train lines, and from there, visitors can take public transportation or walk to Oost.
  • Bus or tram: There are several bus and tram lines that run through Oost, making it easy to get around the area.
  • Biking or walking: Oost is a pedestrian and bike-friendly area, and many visitors choose to explore the area on foot or by bike.

Airports Near Oost

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: The main international airport serving Amsterdam is located just 20-30 minutes away from Oost by car or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Oost

  • Amsterdam Centraal Station: The main bus station in Amsterdam is located next to the train station.

Other Options

  • Uber or taxi: Taxis and ride-sharing services are widely available throughout Amsterdam.
  • Car rental: Although not necessary within the city, those who wish to explore the surrounding areas on their own can rent a car at the airport or in the city center.

How to Get Around in Oost

Oost is a pedestrian and bike-friendly area, with plenty of bike rental shops throughout the area. Public transportation, including buses and trams, is also available and easy to use.

Where to Go Next from Oost: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond Oost, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider, including:

  • Keukenhof Gardens: A beautiful botanical garden located just outside of Amsterdam, famous for its tulip fields.
  • Zaanse Schans: A historic village just outside of Amsterdam that features windmills, crafts, and traditional Dutch life.
  • Utrecht: A charming city located just 30 minutes from Amsterdam by train, featuring picturesque canals, historic architecture, and lively cafes and pubs.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in art, history, or outdoor adventures, the neighborhood of Oost in Amsterdam has plenty to offer visitors. From scenic parks and outdoor markets to museums and historical landmarks, Oost is a diverse and vibrant part of Amsterdam that is well worth a visit.
