Introduction to Zuid

Zuid is a vibrant neighborhood located south of downtown Amsterdam. It is known for its excellent museums, upscale restaurants, and charming canals. Zuid boasts an elegant mix of 19th-century architecture and modern developments, giving it a unique and sophisticated character. Whether you’re interested in arts and culture or outdoor activities, Zuid has something to offer. In this guide, we will help you explore the best attractions, activities, and accommodations in Zuid and make the most of your trip to Amsterdam.

List of Places to Visit in Zuid

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rijksmuseum: The Rijksmuseum is one of the most popular museums in Amsterdam and is home to an extensive collection of Dutch art and history. The museum features works by renowned artists such as Rembrandt and Vermeer, as well as fascinating exhibits on the country’s history and culture.
  • Van Gogh Museum: The Van Gogh Museum is one of the world’s leading art museums, with a collection of over 200 paintings and 500 drawings by the great Dutch artist Vincent van Gogh. Visitors can also learn about the artist’s life and works through interactive exhibits and multimedia displays.
  • Stedelijk Museum: The Stedelijk Museum is dedicated to contemporary art and design. It features works by artists such as Van Gogh, Mondrian, and Warhol. The museum is housed in a striking modernist building that is itself a work of art.

Natural Attractions of Zuid: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vondelpark: Vondelpark is Amsterdam’s largest park and a great place for a relaxing stroll, bike ride, or picnic. The park features several lakes, playgrounds, and cafes, as well as outdoor concerts and festivals during the summer months.
  • Amsterdam Forest: Amsterdam Forest is a large park and recreation area located on the outskirts of the city. It features miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as a variety of activities such as horseback riding, canoeing, and birdwatching.
  • Amstel River: The Amstel River is a beautiful waterway that runs through Amsterdam, offering stunning views of the city’s architecture and historic landmarks. Visitors can take a leisurely boat tour or rent a bike and cycle along the river’s scenic paths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zuid

  • Vondelpark Loop: This four-mile loop circles Vondelpark and takes visitors through picturesque gardens, ponds, and wooded areas.
  • Zuidas Green Walk: This two-mile walk takes visitors through the modern business district of Zuidas, with stops at several parks and gardens.
  • Amsterdam Forest Bike Trail: This ten-mile trail takes cyclists through the vast Amsterdam Forest, with stunning views of the park’s lakes, forests, and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Zuid: Activities and Tours

  • Canal Cruise: Experience Amsterdam from the water with a scenic canal cruise. Many tours depart from Zuid and offer informative commentary on the city’s history and culture.
  • Food Tour: Zuid is known for its outstanding restaurants and cafes. Take a food tour to sample local specialties such as herring, poffertjes, and stroopwafels.
  • Bike Tour: Amsterdam is a bike-friendly city, and taking a bike tour is a great way to explore Zuid and its surroundings. Many tours take visitors through lesser-known neighborhoods and offer insights into local life and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zuid

Day 1: Visit the Rijksmuseum and explore the surrounding area, including the Van Gogh Museum and the Stedelijk Museum.

Day 2: Take a bike tour through Vondelpark and explore the surrounding neighborhood of Oud-Zuid.

Day 3: Visit the Anne Frank House and take a canal cruise through the historic center of Amsterdam.

Day 4: Explore the modern business district of Zuidas and finish with a shopping trip at the nearby Gelderlandplein Mall.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Haarlem, known for its beautiful historic architecture and quaint shops and cafes.

Day 6: Hike or bike through Amsterdam Forest, stopping for a picnic lunch and a visit to the Goat Farm.

Day 7: Spend the day wandering the charming streets of Zuid, exploring local cafes and markets, and relaxing in one of the area’s many parks and gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Zuid

Must-Try Dishes

  • Herring: Herring is a traditional Dutch delicacy that is simply prepared and served with onions and pickles. You can find this dish at many local fish stands and markets.
  • Poffertjes: Poffertjes are small, fluffy pancakes dusted with powdered sugar and served with butter and syrup. They are a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Stroopwafels: Stroopwafels are thin waffles with a layer of caramel syrup in the middle. They are a perfect sweet treat to enjoy with a cup of coffee or tea.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: Jenever is a Dutch gin infused with flavors such as juniper berries, fruits, and herbs. It is often sipped neat or used as a base in cocktails.
  • Heineken: Heineken is the most famous Dutch beer and is brewed in Amsterdam. Visitors can tour the Heineken brewery and learn about the history and production of this iconic beer.
  • Bols: Bols is a Dutch distillery that produces a variety of liqueurs, including the classic Dutch drink Advocaat, made with eggs and brandy. Bols offers tours and tastings at its Amsterdam distillery.

Where to Stay in Zuid: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Conservatorium Hotel: This luxurious five-star hotel is located in a beautifully restored historic building and features elegant rooms and suites, a spa and wellness center, and a Michelin-starred restaurant.

  • Hotel Okura Amsterdam: This iconic hotel offers stunning views over Amsterdam and features a variety of amenities, including multiple restaurants, a spa and fitness center, and an indoor pool.

  • Hostel Van Gogh: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the heart of Zuid and offers comfortable rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lively bar.

What to Buy in Zuid: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Delftware: Delftware is a type of blue and white pottery that is synonymous with Dutch culture. Visitors can find beautiful pieces of Delftware at many local shops and markets.
  • Cheese: Dutch cheese, such as Gouda and Edam, is world-renowned for its quality and flavor. Visitors can sample and purchase a variety of cheeses at local cheese shops and markets.
  • Tulips: Holland is famous for its tulips, and Zuid is no exception. Visitors can purchase beautiful tulip bulbs and arrangements at local florists and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Zuid

Zuid’s culture is heavily influenced by Dutch history and tradition. The area is known for its excellent museums and art galleries, as well as its fine dining and cafes. The Dutch are famously tolerant and open-minded, and visitors will find Zuid to be a welcoming and accepting place. Visitors should be aware of the local custom of biking and should take care when crossing bike lanes and walking in the city.

Best Time to Visit Zuid

The best time to visit Zuid is during the spring and summer months, when the weather is mild and the city is in bloom. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so visitors should expect larger crowds and higher prices. Fall is also a great time to visit, with colorful foliage and moderate temperatures, while winter can be chilly and wet but offers festive Christmas markets and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zuid

Do:

  • Do rent a bike: Biking is the best way to see Amsterdam, and Zuid is no exception. Rent a bike and explore the charming streets and canals of the area.
  • Do try local cuisine: Zuid is known for its excellent food scene, with many fine dining and casual restaurants serving up local specialties.
  • Do visit the museums: Zuid is home to some of the best museums in Amsterdam, including the Rijksmuseum and the Van Gogh Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: The Dutch take cleanliness seriously, and visitors should respect this by disposing of their garbage properly.
  • Don’t stop suddenly in bike lanes: Bikes are a primary mode of transportation in Amsterdam, and visitors should be aware that they are sharing the road with cyclists. Don’t stop suddenly in bike lanes or cross without looking both ways.
  • Don’t forget to bring an umbrella: The Netherlands is known for its rainy weather, so visitors should be prepared with an umbrella or raincoat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zuid

Zuid is a safe neighborhood, but visitors should take standard precautions such as locking their valuables and being aware of their surroundings at all times. Bikes can be targeted by thieves, so visitors should lock them in a secured area when not in use. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for the police, fire department, or ambulance.

Health in Zuid

The Netherlands has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can easily access medical facilities and pharmacies in case of illness or injury. Visitors should also be aware of the issue of air pollution, which can be a problem in Amsterdam during certain times of the year. Those with respiratory issues should take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Zuid: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zuid

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: Amsterdam Airport Schiphol is the main airport serving Amsterdam and is located approximately 15 minutes by train or taxi from Zuid.

Bus Stations Near Zuid

  • Amsterdam Zuid Bus Station: Amsterdam Zuid Bus Station is located in the heart of Zuid and offers connections to many destinations throughout Amsterdam and beyond.

Other Options

  • Metro: Zuid is easily accessible via the Amsterdam Metro system, which offers connections throughout the city and beyond.
  • Tram: Trams are a popular mode of transportation in Amsterdam, and visitors to Zuid can take any number of tram lines to reach their destination.

How to Get Around in Zuid

Biking and walking are the best ways to get around Zuid, with many bike rental shops and well-marked bike lanes throughout the area. Public transportation, including the Metro and the tram, is also available. Taxis and rideshare services are also available for those who prefer a more direct mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Zuid: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Haarlem: Haarlem is a charming town located just 30 minutes from Amsterdam and is known for its historic architecture, museums, and quaint shops and cafes.
  • Keukenhof: Keukenhof is a famous tulip garden located just south of Amsterdam and is open for visitors during the spring months.
  • Utrecht: Utrecht is a vibrant university town located just 25 minutes from Amsterdam by train and is known for its beautiful canals, historic buildings, and lively culture.

Conclusion

Zuid is a must-visit neighborhood for anyone traveling to Amsterdam. With its charming canals, excellent museums, and fine dining and cafes, Zuid is a perfect blend of tradition and modernity. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Zuid offers something for everyone. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your trip and exploring the best of Zuid and Amsterdam.
