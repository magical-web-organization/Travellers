Aalsmeer is a charming town located in the Amstelland region of North Holland, in Western Netherlands. Known for its beautiful flower fields, picturesque canals, and bustling food and drink scene, Aalsmeer is a favorite destination for both locals and tourists alike. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and the must-visit places in Aalsmeer, along with some local customs and safety tips to ensure you have a seamless trip.

Introduction to Aalsmeer

Aalsmeer is a small town located on the outskirts of Amsterdam, renowned for its bustling flower market and beautiful waterways. Although this town may seem small, it’s rich in history and culture. The town is also home to one of the largest commercial flower auctions in the world, making it a popular destination for flower lovers.

Dotted with beautiful green spaces and scenic routes, Aalsmeer is a perfect destination for nature lovers seeking relaxation and adventure. Aalsmeer will captivate your memories with its natural beauty, rich history and with a taste of the local Dutch lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Aalsmeer

Whether you’re interested in exploring the local culture and history, indulging in the beautiful countryside or enjoying the vibrant nightlife, Aalsmeer has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aalsmeer offers a rich history and cultural experience that can be seen in various museums and historical buildings throughout the town. Here are a few must-visit spots:

  • Aalsmeer Flower Auction: If you’re interested in flowers, don’t miss the Aalsmeer Flower Auction, one of the world’s largest commercial flower auctions. The auction begins early in the morning and offers visitors a chance to witness the hustle and bustle of the flower trade.
  • Historic Centre: Take a wander around the town’s historical center to explore stunning Dutch architecture. Many notable buildings in this area are the old town hall, the Spoorlijn Monument, and the Oude Veiling flower auction building.
  • Historical Garden De Oude Pastorie: Located near the town center, the Historical Garden De Oude Pastorie is a recreated garden of a classic Dutch parsonage.
  • Museums: For history buffs, visit the Aalsmeer Museum, which shows the history of Aalsmeer or the Classic Porsche Museum, which is home to a remarkable collection of Porsche cars.

Natural Attractions of Aalsmeer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lovers of the great outdoors will love Aalsmeer’s well-kept parks, gardens, trees, and the beautiful waterways. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions to explore:

  • Westeinderplassen: This sprawling lake stretched across miles, making it a popular destination for water sports such as boating and fishing.
  • Parks: Aalsmeer is home to several beautiful parks, including The Hornmeer and the Beatrix Park.
  • Biking and Hiking Trails: For adventure enthusiasts, Aalsmeer has dozens of cycling and hiking trails. Maps are available from the local tourist office, and the trails offer varying degrees of difficulty.
  • Flower Fields: Aalsmeer is known for its beautiful flower fields. Head out to the countryside to see some of the best ones.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aalsmeer

Aalsmeer boasts a plethora of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for nature enthusiasts. Here are a few of the best routes:

  • Aalsmeer Green Route: This is a beautiful bike tour around the outskirts of Aalsmeer. The route is about 30 kilometers long, and you’ll get to see some of the most magnificent parts of the town.
  • Polders Walk: This is a 16-kilometer hike where you’ll explore Aalsmeer’s countryside with its beautiful flora and fauna.
  • Sunflower Hike: The Sunflower Hike is a short walking trail that takes visitors through a sunflower field during the peak blooming season.

Best Things to Do in Aalsmeer: Activities and Tours

Beyond the museums and nature reserves, Aalsmeer offers plenty of unique activities for visitors. Here are some of the must-do activities to add to your itinerary:

  • Boat Ride: Take a leisurely boat ride on the town’s waterways, and explore Aalsmeer’s beauty from a different angle.
  • Visit Rijksmuseum Amsterdam: Just a short train ride away is the Rijksmuseum Amsterdam, where you can see some of the world’s famous art pieces.
  • Albert Cuyp Market: Visit the famous Albert Cuyp Street Market in Amsterdam to get a taste of Dutch food and culture.
  • Dutch Cheese Tasting: Try some of the famous Dutch cheese at a cheese market during your visit to Aalsmeer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aalsmeer

If you’re planning on spending a week in Aalsmeer, here’s a comprehensive itinerary to enjoy the best of the town:

  • Day 1: Arrive and explore the town’s UNESCO sites.
  • Day 2: Visit the Aalsmeer Flower Auction and explore the Westeinder Plassen for water activities.
  • Day 3: Head out to Amsterdam and check out some of the city’s top attractions, such as the Van Gogh Museum and Anne Frank House.
  • Day 4: Go hiking or biking on Aalsmeer’s beautiful trails or explore the local museums.
  • Day 5: Visit the Albert Cuyp Market and try the local Dutch cuisine.
  • Day 6: Explore other nearby villages such as Kudelstaart, Uithoorn, and Amstelveen.
  • Day 7: Take a day to relax and enjoy a leisurely boat ride on the waterways or take part in a Dutch cheese tasting event.

Food and Drink Scene of Aalsmeer

Food enthusiasts will love Aalsmeer’s excellent selection of restaurants, cafes, and traditional Dutch bars. Here’s what you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poffertjes: Poffertjes are small Dutch pancakes that you’ll find in most eateries in Aalsmeer.
  • Stroopwafels: Stroopwafels, a popular Dutch treat, are waffles filled with caramel that you can’t miss.
  • Bitterballen: Bitterballen is a classic Dutch snack made of meat, herbs, and a crispy coating that you must try.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: Try Jenever, a liquor that’s a cross between gin and malt whiskey but with a sweeter taste.
  • Beer: Dutch beers, such as Heineken and Amstel, are popular not only in the Netherlands but all over the world.

Where to Stay in Aalsmeer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aalsmeer has plenty of accommodation options to cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Blue Mansion Hotel: A 4-star hotel located in Aalsmeer’s town center is a perfect location for travelers. The hotel offers free bike rental and a relaxing onsite terrace and restaurant.
  • Hotel Chariot: Located on the outskirts of Aalsmeer, the Hotel Chariot combines the luxury of a hotel with the charm of a traditional Dutch inn.
  • Bed and Breakfast Aalsmeer: A cozy bed and breakfast located a few miles from the town center, offering affordable and comfortable accommodation.

What to Buy in Aalsmeer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aalsmeer is famous for its flower markets, so don’t miss the opportunity to bring some of its beauty back home. Here are some other souvenirs to consider:

  • Delftware: The blue-and-white pottery, known as Delftware, is a famous Dutch souvenir.
  • Cheese: Dutch cheese, such as Gouda and Edam, are popular souvenirs to bring back home.
  • Stroopwafels: These are a famous Dutch sweet treat that you will love to add to your souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Aalsmeer

Dutch culture might be different from what you’re used to, so familiarize yourself with the local customs and culture before visiting. Here are a few things to know:

  • Punctuality: The Dutch are known for their punctuality, so ensure to arrive on time for tours or appointments.
  • Bike Culture: Cycling is a way of life in the Netherlands, so be mindful of bike lanes and always double-check before crossing them.
  • Public Transport Etiquette: The Dutch are silent commuters. So, be mindful of this etiquette when using public transport.

Best Time to Visit Aalsmeer

The best time to visit Aalsmeer is during spring and summer, as this is when the flower fields are in full bloom. However, the town is beautiful all year round, and each season has its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aalsmeer

Here’s a quick list of do’s and don’ts to make your visit to Aalsmeer as smooth as possible:

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: The Dutch are famous for their cuisine, so try out the local delicacies in Aalsmeer.
  • Carry a Valid ID: Ensure you carry your identification documents while traveling around Aalsmeer.
  • Be Respectful: Be mindful of the local customs and culture when interacting with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Forget to be Punctual: The Dutch appreciate punctuality, so don’t be late for your appointments or tours.
  • Ignore Bike Lanes: Be mindful of the bike lanes and always double-check before crossing them.
  • Be Too Loud: The Dutch prefer a quieter environment so keep noise levels to a minimum.

Safety and Health

Aalsmeer is generally safe for tourists, and the town has low crime rates. However, it’s best to stay vigilant and be mindful of your surroundings. Here are a few safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Aalsmeer

  • Keep an Eye on Your Belongings: Always keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded places.
  • Stay on Designated Paths: Stick to the designated routes when hiking or biking in Aalsmeer’s countryside.
  • Be Mindful of Weather Conditions: Aalsmeer is at risk of flooding due to its proximity to waterways, be mindful of weather conditions before taking a boat ride.

Health in Aalsmeer

  • Stay Hydrated: In warmer months, it’s essential to stay hydrated, so drink plenty of water.
  • Apply Sunscreen: If you’re spending time outdoors, apply sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun’s harsh rays.
  • Wash Your Hands Regularly: Ensure you wash your hands regularly and carry a hand sanitizer to maintain hygiene standards.

Getting to Aalsmeer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aalsmeer is well-connected to major cities through various modes of transportation. Here are your options:

Airports Near Aalsmeer

  • Schiphol Airport: Schiphol Airport is the nearest airport to Aalsmeer, located just twelve kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Aalsmeer

  • Aalsmeer Bus Station: Aalsmeer is well connected by local buses. The Aalsmeer bus station is situated in the town center and connects Aalsmeer with Amsterdam, Uithoorn and other neighboring towns.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is Schiphol Station, which is well connected to other major cities such as Amsterdam and Rotterdam.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car gives you the freedom to explore Aalsmeer at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Aalsmeer

Aalsmeer is a small town that you can easily explore on foot or by bike. Local buses and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Aalsmeer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aalsmeer is a popular tourist destination, and visitors often use it as a base to explore neighboring towns and cities. Here are some of the best destinations near Aalsmeer:

  • Amsterdam: Amsterdam is a 30-minute train ride from Aalsmeer and is home to countless tourist attractions such as the Anne Frank House.
  • Utrecht: Utrecht is a short train ride away and is home to many historical buildings and beautiful canals.
  • Haarlem: Haarlem is a quaint Dutch town located just 20 minutes away and combines rich history with modern-day culture.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Aalsmeer offers its visitors a perfect mix of history, culture, nature, and adventure. Explore the town’s historical sites, the beautiful countryside, and the charming waterways or indulge in Dutch cuisine. Whether you’re visiting for a day or a week, Aalsmeer will give you lasting memories.
