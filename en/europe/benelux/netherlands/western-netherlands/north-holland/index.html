Introduction to North Holland

North Holland is a province in Western Netherlands, known for its vibrant cities, picturesque countryside, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking to explore historic landmarks, enjoy outdoor activities, or sample local cuisine, North Holland offers a wealth of options for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in North Holland

From buzzing metropolises to charming villages, North Holland has something for everyone. Here are some of the top destinations to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam: The largest art museum in the Netherlands features masterpieces by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other Dutch masters.
  • Anne Frank House in Amsterdam: Learn about the life of Anne Frank and her family during the Nazi occupation of Amsterdam.
  • Zaanse Schans in Zaandam: A living museum showcasing traditional Dutch architecture and crafts.
  • De Hoge Veluwe National Park in Otterlo: Home to the Kröller-Müller Museum, featuring works by Van Gogh, Picasso, and Mondrian.

Natural Attractions of North Holland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keukenhof Gardens in Lisse: One of the world’s largest flower gardens, with over 7 million tulips, daffodils, and hyacinths.
  • Texel Island: A paradise for nature lovers, with beaches, dunes, and a wildlife sanctuary.
  • Hoge Berg in Den Burg: A scenic hill offering stunning views of Texel and the Wadden Sea.
  • Zandvoort Beach: A popular seaside resort town with miles of sandy beaches and water sports activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Holland

North Holland is a great destination for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and paths throughout the province. Some of the best routes include:

  • Amsterdamse Bos: A large park with over 50 km of hiking and cycling trails.
  • Veluwe National Park: Over 100 km of hiking paths through scenic dunes and forests.
  • Wadden Sea: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with guided walks and tours led by local experts.
  • Kennemerduinen: A nature reserve with rugged dunes and heathland, ideal for hiking and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in North Holland: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing sightseeing tour or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, you’ll find plenty of options in North Holland. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Canal tour in Amsterdam: Explore the city’s iconic canals and historic bridges by boat.
  • Cheese market in Alkmaar: See the traditional Dutch cheese market in action and sample some of the best cheese in the country.
  • Tulip fields tour in Lisse: Visit the world-famous Keukenhof Gardens and enjoy a scenic drive through the tulip fields.
  • Windmill tour in Zaanse Schans: Learn about Dutch windmills and see them in action at this living museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Holland

If you’re planning to spend a week in North Holland, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Amsterdam

  • Visit the Rijksmuseum and Van Gogh Museum.
  • Explore the narrow streets and canals of the historic Jordaan district.
  • Take a boat tour of the canals.

Day 2: Zaandam and Zaanse Schans

  • Visit the Zaanse Schans living museum.
  • See traditional Dutch windmills in action.
  • Explore the historic town of Zaandam.

Day 3: Alkmaar and the North Sea

  • Visit the Alkmaar cheese market.
  • Enjoy a walk on the beach in Egmond aan Zee.
  • Have dinner at a seafood restaurant in Bergen aan Zee.

Day 4: Texel Island

  • Take the ferry to Texel Island.
  • Explore the beaches and nature reserves.
  • Visit the Ecomare seal sanctuary.

Day 5: Haarlem and Keukenhof Gardens

  • Explore the historic center of Haarlem.
  • Visit the Frans Hals Museum and the Teylers Museum.
  • Visit the Keukenhof Gardens and enjoy the tulip fields in full bloom.

Day 6: Utrecht and Gouda

  • Visit the historic city of Utrecht and its iconic Dom Tower.
  • Enjoy a lunch of traditional Dutch pea soup and stroopwafels.
  • Visit the cheese city of Gouda and its famous cheese market.

Day 7: De Hoge Veluwe National Park

  • Explore the Kröller-Müller Museum.
  • Enjoy a bike ride through the park’s picturesque landscapes.
  • Have a picnic lunch amidst the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of North Holland

North Holland has a rich culinary heritage, featuring locally sourced ingredients and creative dishes that reflect the region’s cultural diversity. Here are some must-try foods and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haring (herring): A traditional Dutch dish served raw and marinated in vinegar, onions, and spices.
  • Stamppot: A hearty dish of mashed potatoes, vegetables, and sausage.
  • Poffertjes: Miniature pancakes served with butter and powdered sugar.
  • Bitterballen: Deep-fried meatballs served with mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Dutch beer: Try local brews such as Heineken, Grolsch, and Amstel.
  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin made from malt wine and flavored with juniper berries.
  • Advocaat: A creamy liqueur made with eggs, sugar, and brandy, often served with whipped cream.

Where to Stay in North Holland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Holland offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and cozy guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Waldorf Astoria Amsterdam: A luxurious hotel in the heart of Amsterdam.
  • Stayokay Hostel Amsterdam: A budget-friendly hostel with modern facilities and a prime location.
  • Strandhotel Golfzang: A beachfront hotel in Egmond aan Zee.
  • De Zon Hotel & Restaurant: A charming hotel and restaurant in Oosterend on Texel Island.

What to Buy in North Holland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products, North Holland has plenty of options to choose from. Some popular items include:

  • Delftware: Traditional Dutch ceramics featuring blue and white designs.
  • Stroopwafels: Thin waffle cookies filled with caramel syrup.
  • Cheese: Try local favorites such as Gouda, Edam, and Leerdammer.
  • Wooden clogs: A symbol of Dutch culture and craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of North Holland

North Holland has a rich cultural heritage, with traditions and customs that date back centuries. Visitors may encounter some of the following customs:

  • Bicycling: Cycling is a popular mode of transportation in North Holland, and visitors will often see locals cycling to work or running errands.
  • Tolerance: The Netherlands is known for its liberal attitudes towards drugs, sex, and other social issues, and visitors may find this reflected in the culture of North Holland.
  • Dutch language and greetings: While many locals speak English, visitors are encouraged to learn a few basic Dutch phrases and customs, such as greeting others with a handshake and three kisses on the cheek.

Best Time to Visit North Holland

The best time to visit North Holland depends on your interests and preferences. Spring is the most popular time to visit, thanks to the blooming of the tulip fields and the Keukenhof Gardens. Summer is also a great time to visit, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. Fall and winter are less crowded and offer the opportunity to experience the region’s cozy cafes, museums, and winter festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Holland

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting North Holland:

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and beverages, such as stroopwafels and jenever.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as greeting others with three kisses on the cheek.
  • Do use public transportation or rent a bike to explore the region and limit your impact on the environment.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb wildlife in nature reserves.
  • Don’t get too close to windmills or other historic landmarks, as they may be fragile or unstable.
  • Don’t assume that all locals speak English fluently or share your cultural background.

Safety and Health

North Holland is a safe and healthy destination for travelers, with a well-developed healthcare system and low crime rates. Some important safety and health tips to keep in mind include:

Safety in North Holland

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone in isolated areas at night.
  • Use caution when crossing busy streets or cycling in heavy traffic.
  • Keep your belongings secure and be vigilant for pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in North Holland

  • Make sure your vaccines are up-to-date and consider purchasing travel insurance.
  • Drink plenty of water and use sunscreen and insect repellent in summer months.
  • Use caution when trying unfamiliar foods or beverages to avoid stomach upset.

Getting to North Holland: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Holland is easily accessible via multiple modes of transportation, including:

  • Airports: Amsterdam Airport Schiphol is the largest airport in the Netherlands, and connects North Holland to destinations around the world.
  • Trains: The Netherlands has a well-developed rail network, with frequent trains connecting North Holland to major cities such as Utrecht, The Hague, and Rotterdam.
  • Buses: Bus service is available throughout North Holland, with connections to smaller towns and villages.
  • Car rental: While not strictly necessary, car rental is an option for those who prefer to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in North Holland

Getting around North Holland is easy and convenient, thanks to the region’s well-developed public transportation network. Some options include:

  • Trains: The Dutch rail system, NS, offers both intercity and regional trains that connect North Holland to other parts of the country.
  • Buses: Local and regional bus service is available throughout North Holland.
  • Metro and tram: Amsterdam boasts an extensive metro and tram system that connects the city center to outlying neighborhoods and suburbs.
  • Rental bikes: Bikes are widely available for rent throughout North Holland and are a great way to explore the countryside and smaller towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from North Holland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip or explore other destinations in the Netherlands, here are some ideas:

  • Rotterdam: The Netherlands’ second-largest city is known for its modern architecture and bustling port.
  • Utrecht: A charming city with a picturesque canal district, historic churches, and a lively student scene.
  • The Hague: The country’s political capital and home to many government buildings, museums, and art galleries.
  • Friesland: A rural province in the northern Netherlands known for its scenic landscapes, charming towns, and traditional culture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a culture vulture, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, North Holland has something for you. From the vibrant streets of Amsterdam to the picturesque countryside of Texel Island, this region offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and modern amenities. So pack your bags, book your tickets, and get ready for an unforgettable journey to North Holland.
