Introduction to Kennemerland

Located in North Holland, Kennemerland is a scenic region in the western Netherlands known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. With its diverse landscapes of sand dunes, beaches, and forests, Kennemerland is a popular tourist destination that offers a wide range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions. From exploring historical sites and museums to hiking and biking through picturesque trails, there is something for everyone in Kennemerland.

List of Places to Visit in Kennemerland

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a culture vulture, Kennemerland has plenty of exciting places to explore. Here are some of the top attractions and must-visit destinations in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Teylers Museum: Founded in 1778, this is the oldest museum in the Netherlands and known for its vast collection of fossils, minerals, and scientific instruments.
  • The Frans Hals Museum: Located in Haarlem, this museum houses a significant collection of works by the Dutch Golden Age painter Frans Hals.
  • Castle Brederode: A 13th-century castle located in Santpoort, it was destroyed in the 17th century and has been left as ruins ever since.

Natural Attractions of Kennemerland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zuid-Kennemerland National Park: A sprawling nature reserve with sand dunes, forests, and beaches, perfect for hiking and cycling.
  • Nationaal Park Kennemerland: Another nature reserve near Haarlem that is rich in biodiversity and offers a wide range of recreational activities.
  • Kennemerduinen: A nature reserve in Zandvoort that boasts stunning trails, panoramic views and houses a number of rare and endangered plant species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kennemerland

Kennemerland is known for its diverse landscapes that offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Hike through the dunes of Zuid-Kennemerland National Park, which offers some of the most scenic views of the region and an abundance of wildlife to observe.
  • Cycle through the historic city of Haarlem and its surroundings, where you will find extensive bike paths through the countryside and along the coast.
  • The Kennemerland Cycle Route: A 46-kilometer cycling route that takes you through the most beautiful parts of Kennemerland, including the sand dunes, forested areas, and charming villages.

Best Things to Do in Kennemerland: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and cycling, Kennemerland is a hub for a variety of indoor and outdoor activities. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a surfing lesson in Zandvoort, a trendy resort town known for its fantastic beach scene and great waves.
  • Join a guided walking tour of Haarlem and learn more about the city’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Visit the local farms and markets to taste the fresh produce that Kennemerland has to offer, including artisanal cheeses and freshly baked bread.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kennemerland

To fully experience everything Kennemerland has to offer, here’s a comprehensive seven-day itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Haarlem’s historic center and visit the Frans Hals Museum.
2 Hike through Zuid-Kennemerland National Park and visit Castle Brederode.
3 Visit the Teylers Museum and explore the charming town of Heemstede.
4 Cycle through the coastal towns of IJmuiden and Zandvoort.
5 Experience the local food scene with a cheese tasting and visit to the farm.
6 Visit the Kennemerduinen nature reserve and take a surfing lesson in Zandvoort.
7 Relax on the beach in Bloemendaal and enjoy the sunset with some drinks and local bites at a beach bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Kennemerland

Kennemerland boasts a diverse culinary scene, with traditional Dutch dishes, international cuisine, and fresh local produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot: A classic Dutch dish made from mashed potatoes, kale, and smoked sausage.
  • Kibbeling: Fried cod bites, often served with remoulade sauce.
  • Poffertjes: Mini Dutch pancakes that are filled with raisins and butter, perfect for a sweet treat.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin that is often served as an aperitif.
  • Grolsch beer: A popular beer brand in the Netherlands that is brewed in the region.
  • Dutch hot chocolate: Made with high-quality cocoa powder and served with whipped cream on top, perfect for warming up on a chilly day.

Where to Stay in Kennemerland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer a luxurious hotel, a cozy guesthouse or a budget-friendly hostel, Kennemerland has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Carlton Square Hotel: A luxurious hotel in Haarlem’s city center with spacious rooms and an excellent restaurant.
  • Stayokay hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in Zandvoort that offers comfortable dormitories and private rooms.
  • Bed & Breakfast Noord-Holland: A charming guesthouse in a traditional Dutch house, located in the countryside near Haarlem.

What to Buy in Kennemerland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From artisanal cheeses and freshly baked bread to handcrafted ceramics and locally made jewelry, Kennemerland offers a vast array of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • De Kaasshop in Haarlem: A cheese shop that offers a wide range of artisanal cheeses, including the famous Gouda, Edam, and Leiden cheese.
  • Simon Levelt in Haarlem: A specialty coffee and tea shop that sells high-quality blends from all over the world.
  • De Haarlemse Bakkerij in Haarlem: A bakery that sells freshly baked bread, pastries, and Dutch delicacies.

Local Culture and Customs of Kennemerland

The people of Kennemerland are known for their friendly and laid-back attitude, and they value their cultural heritage and traditions. Here are some customs and practices you might encounter:

  • When greeting someone, it’s common to shake hands and introduce yourself.
  • Bicycles are a popular mode of transportation, and cyclists have the right of way on most roads and bike paths.
  • Dutch directness is often perceived as rude or confrontational by visitors, but it’s just their way of being straightforward and honest.

Best Time to Visit Kennemerland

The best time to visit Kennemerland is during the spring and summer months (April-September), when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the natural beauty of the region is at its peak. However, the region has a lot to offer throughout the year, and many visitors enjoy the fall and winter seasons for their unique charm and winter sports opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kennemerland

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kennemerland:

Do:

  • Rent a bike and explore the region’s picturesque trails and bike paths.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample the fresh produce that Kennemerland has to offer.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, including their direct communication style.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind at the beaches or nature reserves.
  • Don’t walk on the cycling paths, as they are reserved for cyclists only.
  • Don’t forget to bring your sunscreen and a hat, especially in the summer months.

Safety and Health

Kennemerland is generally a safe and healthy destination for tourists, but it’s always important to take the necessary precautions and stay informed about any potential risks. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Kennemerland

  • Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bicycle theft, can occur in the city centers and tourist areas.
  • The water quality at the beaches is monitored regularly, so be aware of any warnings or advisories about unsafe swimming conditions.

Health in Kennemerland

  • Medical facilities in the Netherlands are of high quality, but they can be expensive for tourists without insurance.
  • Tick-borne diseases are prevalent in the region, so wear long sleeves, apply insect repellent, and check yourself for ticks after any outdoor activity.

Getting to Kennemerland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kennemerland is easily accessible by various means of transport, including public transportation, car, and bicycle. Here are some of the options for getting into the region:

  • By plane: Amsterdam Schiphol Airport is the closest international airport and offers a range of flights from all over the world.
  • By train: Both Haarlem and Zandvoort have train stations that connect to Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, and other major Dutch cities.
  • By bus: There are regular bus services that connect Kennemerland to the surrounding towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Kennemerland

Bicycles are the most popular means of transportation in Kennemerland, and most attractions and destinations are within cycling distance. There are also several other options for getting around:

  • Public transportation: Buses and trains are available, but they are not as convenient as cycling or driving.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the region at your own pace and is easy to arrange at the airports or in the cities.

Where to Go Next from Kennemerland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Netherlands after your visit to Kennemerland, there are plenty of exciting destinations to consider. Here are some nearby cities and towns worth checking out:

  • Amsterdam: Known for its canals, museums, and nightlife, Amsterdam is a vibrant city that attracts millions of visitors every year.
  • Rotterdam: A modern and cutting-edge city known for its architecture and contemporary art scene.
  • Leiden: A charming university town that is rich in history and culture and boasts beautiful canals and quaint streets.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant local scene, Kennemerland is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Netherlands. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing beach vacation, you’re sure to find something to enjoy in this beautiful region.
