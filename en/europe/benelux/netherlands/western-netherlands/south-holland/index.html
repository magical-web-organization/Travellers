Introduction to South Holland

South Holland, a province in Western Netherlands, is a unique travel destination that offers a blend of both natural and cultural attractions. Comprising of cities such as The Hague, Rotterdam, and Leiden, it has something for everyone, from art lovers and history buffs to outdoor adventurers and foodies.

List of Places to Visit in South Holland

Here are some of the top places you should visit during your trip to South Holland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mauritshuis Museum
  • Peace Palace
  • Museum De Lakenhal
  • Royal Delft
  • Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen

Natural Attractions of South Holland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kinderdijk Windmills
  • Keukenhof
  • Dunes of South Holland
  • Westbroek Park
  • Scheveningen Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Holland

South Holland offers several hiking and biking routes. Boasting beautiful landscapes and fields of colorful flowers, these trails offer picturesque views. Here are some popular routes:

  • The Green Heart
  • The North Sea Trail
  • The Cycling Circuit of the Hague
  • The Utrecht Hill Ridge

Best Things to Do in South Holland: Activities and Tours

South Holland is full of activities and tours that will enrich your experience while you’re there. Here are some of the must-do activities in South Holland:

  • Visit the cheese market of Gouda
  • Attend the famous flower parade of Aalsmeer
  • Take a canal tour in Leiden
  • Explore the Dutch countryside on a bike tour
  • Experience the nightlife of Rotterdam

Week-Long Itinerary for South Holland

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in South Holland:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Mauritshuis Museum and explore the city center of The Hague
2 Take a bike tour of Leiden and see the beautiful flower fields of Keukenhof
3 Explore the windmills of Kinderdijk and the Royal Delft Pottery Factory
4 Relax on the beaches of Scheveningen and enjoy the local cuisine
5 Visit the Museum De Lakenhal and stroll around the city center of Leiden
6 Take a canal tour of Rotterdam and visit the Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen
7 Shop for souvenirs in Rotterdam and depart from the airport

Food and Drink Scene of South Holland

The cuisine of South Holland is influenced by a combination of traditional Dutch and international flavors. Here are some local dishes and beverages you might want to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stamppot - a hearty dish made of mashed potatoes and vegetables
  • Bitterballen - deep-fried meatballs that are a popular Dutch snack
  • Poffertjes - small, fluffy pancakes soaked in butter and powdered sugar
  • Ossenworst - a traditional smoked beef sausage

Local Beverages

  • Gin
  • Jenever - a traditional Dutch gin
  • Heineken beer
  • Dutch wine

What to Buy in South Holland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Holland has a rich history and culture that is reflected in the many unique souvenirs it offers. Here are some items you may want to bring back with you:

  • Delftware pottery
  • Wooden clogs
  • Cheese knives
  • Gouda cheese

Local Culture and Customs of South Holland

The Dutch are known for their welcoming and friendly culture. South Holland is no exception. The locals are always willing to help and chat about local culture and traditions. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Shake hands with everyone upon greeting them
  • Do not interrupt someone while they are speaking
  • It is customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home

Best Time to Visit South Holland

The best time to visit South Holland is during the spring (March to May), when the famous Keukenhof gardens are in bloom. The weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. Another great time to visit is in the summer (June to August) when the beaches are open and the cities come to life.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Holland

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to South Holland:

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Rent a bike to explore the countryside
  • Respect cultural customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Smoke cannabis in public
  • Be loud and disruptive during quiet hours
  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home

Safety and Health

Safety in South Holland

South Holland is known for being a safe and secure province, but as with any travel destination, it’s best to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions.

Health in South Holland

South Holland has high-quality health care facilities, and travelers can access medical care if needed. It’s important to carry your health insurance documents with you at all times.

Airports Near South Holland

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol
  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport

Bus Stations Near South Holland

  • Leiden Centraal Station
  • Rotterdam Centraal Station
  • The Hague Central Station

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the countryside

How to Get Around in South Holland

Public transportation in South Holland is reliable, and travelers can easily explore the cities and countryside. Bikes are a popular way to get around, and several rental options are available.

Where to Go Next from South Holland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Holland is located in the heart of the Netherlands and offers easy access to other regions and destinations. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Amsterdam
  • Utrecht
  • Haarlem

Conclusion

South Holland offers a unique combination of natural and cultural attractions, making it a must-visit destination when traveling to the Netherlands. With beautiful landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality, it’s a great place to explore and create unforgettable memories.
