Introduction to Feijenoord

Feijenoord is a vibrant district located in the southern part of the city of Rotterdam, in the Western Netherlands. The area is known for its diverse community, historical landmarks, scenic views, and lively atmosphere. As a popular destination for both locals and tourists, Feijenoord has plenty to offer, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Feijenoord.

List of Places to Visit in Feijenoord

Feijenoord offers a variety of attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you want to learn about the neighborhood’s history, enjoy beautiful scenery, or have fun with the family, there is something for everyone in Feijenoord.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Feijenoord has a rich history that is reflected in its many cultural landmarks. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • De Kuip

    Known as one of the most iconic football stadiums in Europe, De Kuip is home to the Feyenoord Rotterdam soccer team. It was constructed in 1937 and has a capacity of 51,117 spectators. Tours are available for visitors who want to explore the stadium.

  • Maritime Museum

    Learn about the city’s maritime history at the Maritime Museum Rotterdam. The museum features interactive exhibits and displays showcasing the maritime heritage of Rotterdam and the Netherlands.

  • SS Rotterdam

    Discover the history of ocean liners at the SS Rotterdam, a retired cruise ship that has been repurposed as a museum-hotel. Visitors can tour the ship and learn about its maritime past.

Natural Attractions of Feijenoord: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Feijenoord has breathtaking natural scenery, offering a range of outdoor recreational activities for visitors. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Zuiderpark

    One of the largest parks in the Netherlands, Zuiderpark is a perfect place for a picnic, a walk, or a bike ride. The park has a lake, playgrounds, and sports facilities, making it a great place for families and outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Hefpark

    Hefpark is a hidden gem in Feijenoord, offering stunning views of the skyline of Rotterdam. The park is located on the site of a disused bridge, which has been transformed into an observation deck.

  • Maas River

    The Maas River runs along the southern border of Feijenoord, offering a scenic waterfront for visitors. Stroll along the riverbanks, rent a boat, or go fishing for a peaceful retreat from the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Feijenoord

For outdoor enthusiasts, Feijenoord offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the natural beauty of the area. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Maashaven-Waalhaven Trail

    This 7-kilometer trail takes visitors along the waterfront of Feijenoord, showcasing the district’s industrial heritage and its evolution over the years.

  • Gravendijkwal Cycle Route

    This 12-kilometer route takes visitors along the border of Feijenoord and the city center of Rotterdam, allowing them to explore the bustling streets and historical landmarks of the area.

Best Things to Do in Feijenoord: Activities and Tours

Feijenoord has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Feyenoord Stadium Tour

    Take a tour of De Kuip stadium and learn about the history of the Feyenoord soccer team. Visitors can explore the stands, the pitch, and the team’s locker room.

  • Rotterdam Zoo

    Visit the Rotterdam Zoo and enjoy a day in nature. The zoo is home to a variety of animals, including giraffes, lions, and gorillas.

  • Splashtours Rotterdam

    Take a tour of Rotterdam by land and water with Splashtours. The tour starts with a bus ride through the city before diving into the Maas River for a unique view of the city’s skyline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Feijenoord

Feijenoord can be explored in a week-long itinerary that covers all the must-visit places in the area. Here is a suggested itinerary for visitors to follow:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Feijenoord and explore the neighborhood. Visit De Kuip stadium and learn about the history of the Feyenoord soccer team.
  • Day 2: Visit the Maritime Museum Rotterdam and learn about the city’s maritime heritage.
  • Day 3: Explore the natural beauty of Feijenoord by visiting Hefpark and the Maas River.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of the SS Rotterdam and learn about the history of ocean liners.
  • Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the Gravendijkwal Cycle Route.
  • Day 6: Visit the Rotterdam Zoo and enjoy a day in nature.
  • Day 7: Take a Splashtours Rotterdam tour for a unique view of the city’s skyline.

Food and Drink Scene of Feijenoord

Feijenoord offers plenty of options for food and drinks, showcasing the diversity of the neighborhood’s culinary scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stroopwafels

    Feijenoord is famous for its stroopwafels, a traditional Dutch treat made of two thin waffles with a caramel syrup filling.

  • Herring

    Herring is a popular fish dish in the Netherlands, served raw with onions and pickles. Visitors can try herring at one of the local fish stands in Feijenoord.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever

    Jenever, also known as Dutch gin, is a traditional Dutch liquor that is often served as an aperitif. Visitors can try jenever at one of the local bars in Feijenoord.

  • Heineken

    Heineken is a popular Dutch beer that can be found in most bars and restaurants in Feijenoord.

Where to Stay in Feijenoord: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Feijenoord offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Feijenoord:

  • SS Rotterdam

    The SS Rotterdam offers a unique hotel experience, with rooms located on a retired cruise ship. The hotel offers a variety of room types, ranging from standard rooms to luxury suites.

  • Hotel Port

    Located in the heart of Feijenoord, Hotel Port offers comfortable accommodation at affordable prices. The hotel is close to public transportation and is within walking distance of the neighborhood’s main attractions.

  • King Kong Hostel

    For budget-conscious travelers, King Kong Hostel offers a fun and social atmosphere. The hostel has a variety of dormitory and private rooms, and guests can enjoy the rooftop terrace with views of the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Feijenoord

Feijenoord has a diverse community, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Here are some tips for understanding the local culture:

  • Language

    Dutch is the official language in Feijenoord, but many locals also speak English.

  • Tipping

    Tipping is not expected in Feijenoord, but it is appreciated for excellent service.

Best Time to Visit Feijenoord

Feijenoord can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors should be prepared for crowds and higher prices during peak season.

Getting to Feijenoord: Transportation Options and Logistics

Feijenoord is easily accessible by public transportation and private car. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Feijenoord

  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport

    Amsterdam Schiphol Airport is located approximately 60 kilometers from Feijenoord and offers many international flights.

Bus Stations Near Feijenoord

  • Rotterdam Zuid Station

    Rotterdam Zuid Station is the nearest bus station to Feijenoord, located within walking distance of the neighborhood.

Other Options
