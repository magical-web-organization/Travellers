Introduction to Delfshaven

Delfshaven is a charming port on the south side of Rotterdam, known for its iconic windmills, historic architecture, and stunning waterways. This little neighbourhood is a special place where you can experience both the traditional and modern culture of Rotterdam.

List of Places to Visit in Delfshaven

From its picturesque streets to remarkable museums, Delfshaven has much to offer to every visitor. Here is the list of top places to visit in Delfshaven that will take you on an ultimate journey of exploration.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historical Museum Rotterdam: Housed in a beautiful old mansion, this museum features exhibits showcasing 600 years of Rotterdam’s history.

  • Pelgrim Distillery Museum: Learn about the distillation process of Jenever, a Dutch gin, at this historic museum.

  • Museum of National History: Explore the region’s history through the interactive exhibits on display at this contemporary museum.

Natural Attractions of Delfshaven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delfshaven Harbour: Take a walk around the harbour and enjoy the sight of boats sailing in and out of the port.

  • Windmills of Delfshaven: These iconic windmills are an integral part of the neighbourhood’s identity and provide a beautiful backdrop for your photos.

  • Het Park: A serene green space in the middle of the city where you can have a picnic, read a book, or simply enjoy the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delfshaven

Delfshaven has several hiking and biking routes that allow you to explore the city at your own pace. Some popular routes include:

  • The Pelgrim Route: This self-guided walking tour takes you on a historical journey through the streets of Delfshaven.

  • The Historical Harbour Bike Trail: Follow this bike trail and experience the breathtaking sights of the city by the harbour.

Best Things to Do in Delfshaven: Activities and Tours

With its range of activities and tours, Delfshaven offers an unforgettable experience to everyone who visits. Here are a few things that you don’t want to miss:

  • Canal Tour: Take a boat tour along the canals of Delfshaven and immerse yourself in the history and culture of the neighbourhood.

  • Kayak Tour: Explore the city’s waterways at your own pace by kayak.

  • Segway Tour: Tour the neighbourhood at a leisurely pace on a Segway and take in the sights of Delfshaven.

Food and Drink Scene of Delfshaven

Delfshaven has a diverse food scene and is known for its delicious local dishes and beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and locally brewed beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Herring: A classic Dutch delicacy, try the herring fresh from the sea with onions and pickles.

  • Stroopwafels: These caramel-filled waffle cookies are a sweet treat that you must try while in Delfshaven.

  • Croquettes: This deep-fried Dutch food is filled with meat or vegetables and is often served as a snack.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: This traditional Dutch gin is a must-try when in Delfshaven.

  • Rotterdam Water: A locally produced artisanal beer that features in several bars in Delfshaven.

Where to Stay in Delfshaven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delfshaven has a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are a few options that cater to all types of travellers:

  • Hotel Pincoffs: A boutique hotel housed in an old customs office, this is a luxurious option in Delfshaven.

  • King Kong Hostel: A trendy hostel with stylish interiors located in the heart of the city.

  • Stayokay Hostel Rotterdam: Located in the historic district of Delfshaven, this hostel features modern amenities and vibrant decor.

What to Buy in Delfshaven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Delfshaven has several shops and markets that offer unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are a few things you should consider buying:

  • Vintage Clothing: Delfshaven has a few vintage shops that sell unique and affordable clothing from various eras.

  • Rotterdam Art Prints: Take a piece of Rotterdam’s vibrant art scene home with you by purchasing prints from one of the local galleries.

  • Dutch Cheese: Stop by a local cheese shop in Delfshaven and try some of the delicious Dutch cheeses, including gouda and edam.

Local Culture and Customs of Delfshaven

The culture of Delfshaven is a blend of traditional Dutch customs and modern influences. The people of Delfshaven are friendly and welcoming, and the neighbourhood has a relaxed and laid-back vibe. Here are a few customs to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Greeting: When meeting someone, greet them with a handshake or three kisses on the cheek.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected but is appreciated to show your appreciation for good service.

  • Respect for Personal Space: Dutch people value their personal space, so it’s best not to invade it unless you’re familiar with the person.

Best Time to Visit Delfshaven

The best time to visit Delfshaven is from April to October when the weather is pleasant, and the neighbourhood comes alive with cultural events and festivals. The summer months are especially busy, so be sure to book your accommodation well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delfshaven

To make the most of your trip to Delfshaven, here are a few things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore on Foot or by Bike: Delfshaven is a pedestrian and bike-friendly neighbourhood that is best explored by foot or bike.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Dutch cuisine is delicious, so make sure to try some of the local dishes, such as herring and stroopwafels.

  • Take a Canal Cruise: Taking a canal cruise is the best way to see the city’s waterways and learn about its history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in Public Areas: Smoking is not permitted in public places in the Netherlands, so be sure to find designated smoking areas.

  • Drink and Drive: The legal blood-alcohol limit is very low, and the penalties for drinking and driving are severe in the Netherlands.

  • Ignore Bicycle Lanes: Delfshaven has several bicycle lanes that are separate from car lanes, so be sure not to ignore them while walking or driving.

Safety and Health

Delfshaven is a safe neighbourhood to visit, with low crime rates and excellent healthcare facilities. However, as with any travel destination, it’s always good to take precautions to stay safe and healthy.

Safety in Delfshaven

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables when exploring the neighbourhood.

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings, especially in busy tourist areas.

  • Stay aware of your surroundings at all times, particularly after dark.

Health in Delfshaven

  • The tap water in the Netherlands is safe to drink, so there’s no need to buy bottled water.

  • In case of a medical emergency, call 112 to reach emergency services.

  • Be sure to pack any prescription medication you may need during your trip.

Getting to Delfshaven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Delfshaven is located in Rotterdam and is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are a few options for getting to Delfshaven:

Airports Near Delfshaven

  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport (RTM): Located just 6 km away from Delfshaven, this is the nearest airport.

  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport (AMS): This international airport is located about 50 km from Delfshaven.

Bus Stations Near Delfshaven

  • Rotterdam Centraal Station: This is the main train station in Rotterdam, where many bus lines stop.

  • Beurs Metro Station: You can also take the metro to Delfshaven from this station.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Rotterdam and can be booked through a taxi app or by phone.

  • Uber: Uber is also available in Rotterdam and is a popular option for getting around the city.

How to Get Around in Delfshaven

Delfshaven is a compact neighbourhood that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, if you prefer public transportation, Delfshaven has several options, including:

  • Metro: Delfshaven has its metro station called Delfshaven Metro Station which runs the D Line Tussendijken to Rotterdam Centraal.

  • Bus: Several bus lines run through Delfshaven and connect the neighbourhood to other parts of Rotterdam.

  • Taxi: Taxis and ride-share services are also readily available in Delfshaven.

Where to Go Next from Delfshaven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Delfshaven is a fantastic neighbourhood to explore on its own, but there are also many other exciting destinations near Rotterdam. Here are a few places to consider visiting:

  • Rotterdam City Centre: The lively city centre of Rotterdam is just a few kilometres away and has many popular attractions, including the Markthal and Cube Houses.

  • The Hague: The Hague, the political capital of the Netherlands, is just a 30-minute train ride away from Rotterdam and offers stunning architecture and beaches.

  • Delft: This charming city is just a short train ride from Rotterdam and is famous for its blue pottery and historic city centre.

Conclusion

Delfshaven is a unique neighbourhood that perfectly combines the traditional and modern culture of Rotterdam. With its winding canals, historic windmills, and vibrant food scene, Delfshaven is a must-visit destination in the Netherlands.
