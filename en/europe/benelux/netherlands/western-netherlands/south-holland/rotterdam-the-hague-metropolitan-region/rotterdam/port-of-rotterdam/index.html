Introduction to Port of Rotterdam

Located in the Western Netherlands, the Port of Rotterdam is the largest and busiest port in Europe. It plays a vital role in the economy of the Netherlands, serving as a gateway to the rest of Europe and the world.

The port covers an area of about 41 square miles and handles over 14,000 oceangoing vessels and 140,000 inland vessels annually. It is home to a wide range of industries, including petrochemicals, oil refining, and agribusiness.

In addition to its commercial importance, the Port of Rotterdam offers visitors a unique travel experience with plenty of attractions and activities to explore. In this detailed guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do, places to visit, and ways to get around the Port of Rotterdam.

List of Places to Visit in Port of Rotterdam

The Port of Rotterdam has a host of attractions that cater to different interests. Whether you’re a culture vulture, nature lover, or adventure seeker, there’s something for you. Here are the top places to check out in Port of Rotterdam:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Rotterdam – This museum celebrates the history and culture of Rotterdam through an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibitions.
  • Maritime Museum – Discover the rich maritime heritage of Rotterdam with exhibits on ship models, engines, and navigation technology.
  • Euromast Tower – A famous landmark of Rotterdam, the Euromast tower offers panoramic views of the city from a height of 185 meters.
  • Cube Houses – Designed by Dutch architect Piet Blom, these unique houses are an architectural wonder and a must-visit for design enthusiasts.
  • Rotterdam Central Station – The city’s main railway station, Rotterdam Central is an architectural masterpiece that blends classic and modern design elements.

Natural Attractions of Port of Rotterdam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kralingse Bos – This beautiful park features a lake, forests, and walking trails, making it an ideal spot for a picnic or a peaceful stroll.
  • Scheveningen Beach – This stunning beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.
  • Delfshaven – This charming old port district features beautiful canals, historic buildings, and plenty of cafes and restaurants.
  • Het Park – Located next to the Euromast Tower, Het Park is a large and peaceful green space with walking trails, gardens, and playgrounds.
  • Rotterdam Zoo – A popular attraction among families, Rotterdam Zoo is home to over 200 species of animals from around the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port of Rotterdam

For outdoor enthusiasts, the Port of Rotterdam offers a range of hiking and biking trails that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the area:

  • Rotterdam Biesbosch – This national park is located just outside Rotterdam and features a network of trails and waterways that wind through scenic wetlands and forests.
  • Kinderdijk – This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to a network of historic windmills and offers a scenic and easy walking/biking route.
  • Maasvlakte 2 – An industrial area that has been transformed into a nature reserve, Maasvlakte 2 offers several biking and hiking trails that showcase the area’s unique landscapes and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Port of Rotterdam: Activities and Tours

From boat rides and bike tours to food and drink experiences, the Port of Rotterdam has plenty of activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Boat tour of the Port of Rotterdam – Take a boat tour and get a behind-the-scenes look at the bustling port.
  • Food and drink tour – Sample the local cuisine and drinks on a walking tour of the city’s best cafes, restaurants, and bars.
  • Street art walking tour – Rotterdam is home to an impressive collection of street art, and a walking tour is the best way to see it all.
  • Cycling tour of Rotterdam – Rent a bike and explore the city’s streets and hidden gems on a guided cycling tour.
  • Visit nearby attractions – Rotterdam is well connected to other Dutch cities like Amsterdam, The Hague, and Utrecht, making it easy to take day trips to explore the wider area.

Food and Drink Scene of Port of Rotterdam

Rotterdam’s food and drink scene is a vibrant mix of international cuisines and local specialties. The city is famous for its street food, seafood, and craft beer. Here’s a rundown of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rotterdam-style croquette – These deep-fried snacks are filled with a thick gravy made with beef, veal, or ragout.
  • Herring – A classic Dutch street food, herring is usually served with onions and pickles.
  • Stroopwafel – A delicious Dutch treat made with thin layers of waffle cookies filled with caramel syrup.
  • Kibbeling – Deep-fried chunks of white fish served with a tangy sauce.
  • Patatje Oorlog – A popular snack made with French fries, peanut sauce, and mayo.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever – A traditional Dutch gin, jenever is usually served chilled and mixed with tonic or soda.
  • Bokbier – A dark, strong beer that is brewed seasonally in the Netherlands.
  • Kopstootje – A shot of jenever served with a beer on the side.
  • Genever – Similar to jenever, but with a milder flavor and lower alcohol content.

Where to Stay in Port of Rotterdam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, Rotterdam has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Hotel New York – A historic hotel located in a former shipping company building, Hotel New York offers stylish rooms and stunning views of the Maas River.
  • nhow Rotterdam – A modern and stylish hotel located in the popular De Rotterdam building, nhow Rotterdam offers comfortable rooms and panoramic views of the city.
  • King Kong Hostel – A trendy and budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of the city, King Kong Hostel offers dormitory and private rooms with quirky decor.
  • The Student Hotel – A hybrid hotel/hostel that caters to students and young professionals, The Student Hotel offers comfortable rooms and plenty of communal spaces to socialize.

What to Buy in Port of Rotterdam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Rotterdam, here are some recommended items:

  • Dutch cheese – The Netherlands is famous for its cheese, and you can find a wide variety of delicious cheeses in Rotterdam.
  • Stroopwafel – This delicious Dutch treat makes for a great edible souvenir.
  • Dutch beer and gin – The Netherlands produces some of the world’s finest gin and beer, and Rotterdam has plenty of local breweries and distilleries to choose from.
  • Vintage clothes and accessories – Rotterdam has a thriving vintage scene, and you can find plenty of unique clothing and accessories in the city’s vintage shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Port of Rotterdam

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Rotterdam:

  • Dutch people are generally friendly and speak English well, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation or ask for help if you need it.
  • Tipping is not mandatory in the Netherlands, but it is appreciated. A 10% tip is considered generous.
  • The legal drinking and smoking age in the Netherlands is 18.
  • Bicycles are a popular mode of transportation in Rotterdam, so keep an eye out for cyclists when walking on the streets.

Best Time to Visit Port of Rotterdam

The best time to visit Rotterdam is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices. Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) are also good times to visit, with mild weather and fewer crowds. Winter (November-March) can be cold and rainy, but it’s also the least crowded and cheapest season to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port of Rotterdam

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including herring, stroopwafel, and Dutch cheese.
  • Do visit the museums and art galleries to learn about Rotterdam’s rich culture and history.
  • Do take a boat tour of the port to see the city’s main attraction up close.
  • Do rent a bike and explore the city’s streets and neighborhoods.
  • Do take a day trip to nearby cities like Amsterdam, The Hague, or Utrecht.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring a rain jacket or umbrella, as Rotterdam can be rainy throughout the year.
  • Don’t be surprised if people are direct and straightforward in their communication – this is a cultural norm in the Netherlands.
  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places, as this is considered impolite and can result in a fine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port of Rotterdam

Rotterdam is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions wherever you go. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep your valuables like phone, wallet, and passport secure and out of sight.
  • Use common sense when walking alone at night and stick to well-lit streets.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and look out for pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Port of Rotterdam

The Netherlands has a good healthcare system, and Rotterdam has several hospitals and clinics that cater to locals and visitors alike. Here are a few things to keep in mind for your health:

  • The tap water in the Netherlands is safe to drink.
  • If you need emergency medical assistance, call 112 for an ambulance.

Getting to Port of Rotterdam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rotterdam is well connected to other parts of the Netherlands and Europe, making it easy to get to and from the city. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Port of Rotterdam

  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport – Located about 5 miles north of Rotterdam, this small airport serves domestic and international flights.
  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport – One of the largest airports in Europe, Schiphol is located about an hour’s drive from Rotterdam and offers flights to destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Port of Rotterdam

  • Rotterdam Central Station – The city’s main railway station also serves as a bus hub, with buses running to destinations across the country.
  • Eurolines Bus Terminal Rotterdam – Located in central Rotterdam, this bus terminal offers international bus services to cities across Europe.

Other Options

  • Train – Rotterdam is well connected by train to other Dutch cities and major European destinations.
  • Ferry – Rotterdam is a major port city, and there are regular ferry services to the UK and other European destinations.

How to Get Around in Port of Rotterdam

Rotterdam is a relatively compact city, and most of the major attractions can be easily reached on foot or by bike. The city also has an extensive public transportation network, including buses, trams, and trains. Here are some ways to get around Rotterdam:

  • Public transportation – The city’s public transportation system is efficient and affordable, with options including buses, trams, and trains.
  • Bike rental – Rotterdam is a bike-friendly city, and there are many bike rental shops in the city center.
  • Walking – Many of the city’s attractions are within walking distance of each other, making walking a convenient and enjoyable way to explore Rotterdam.

Where to Go Next from Port of Rotterdam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rotterdam is well located for day trips and excursions to other Dutch cities and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Amsterdam – The Dutch capital is just an hour away by train and is famous for its museums, canals, and nightlife.
  • The Hague – The political capital of the Netherlands, The Hague is only half an hour away by train and is home to many art museums and the Peace Palace.
  • Utrecht – A charming university town with plenty of historic buildings and cultural attractions, Utrecht is an hour away by train.
  • Brussels – The Belgian capital is just two hours away by train from Rotterdam and is known for its historic architecture and famous chocolate and beer.

Conclusion

Rotterdam is a fascinating city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and industry. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Rotterdam is sure to leave a lasting impression.
