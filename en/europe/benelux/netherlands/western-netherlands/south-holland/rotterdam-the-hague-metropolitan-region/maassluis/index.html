Maassluis is a charming town located in the Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region, in South Holland, Western Netherlands. The town boasts of a rich history and culture, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Dutch experience. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay and what to eat in Maassluis.

Introduction to Maassluis

Maassluis is a small town located near the coast of South Holland, Netherlands. The town dates back to the 16th century and is known for its historic harbors, old windmills, and picturesque canals. Maassluis is also renowned for its Dutch gin production, which has been ongoing since the 17th century.

List of Places to Visit in Maassluis

Maassluis has a lot to offer to its visitors. Here is a list of the top places to visit in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The National Henk Chabot Museum: The museum features the works of artist Henk Chabot, who was born in Rotterdam in 1894. The museum houses over 60 paintings, mostly landscapes and portraits.

  2. The Museum Maassluis: The museum features the history and culture of the town from the 17th century to the present day. The museum also has an extensive collection of Dutch ceramics from the Delft region.

  3. Tower of the Church of Saint Andrew: The tower offers a panoramic view of the town and has a carillon of 49 bells that chime every 15 minutes.

Natural Attractions of Maassluis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. De Haas nature reserve: Situated on the outskirts of Maassluis, this nature reserve offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can go hiking, cycling or take a leisurely stroll in the reserve.

  2. The Windmills of Maassluis: The town is home to two windmills, one of which is open to visitors. The windmill, named ‘De Hoop’, is a unique example of Dutch engineering and architecture.

  3. The harbor and canal district: Maassluis is known for its historic harbors and canals. Visitors can take a boat tour and explore the town from a different perspective.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maassluis

Maassluis has a variety of hiking and biking trails that cater to both the novice and the experienced. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. The Midden-Delfland Route: This 36-kilometer flat biking trail passes through the scenic countryside of South Holland, including Maassluis. The route takes about 3-4 hours to complete.

  2. The Delfland Route: This is a 60-kilometer round trip biking trail that goes from Rotterdam to The Hague. The trail takes you through Maassluis, where you can enjoy scenic views of the harbor and the windmills.

  3. The Maassluis Hiking Trail: This is a 10-kilometer hiking trail that takes you through the town, offering a glimpse of its rich history and culture. The route takes you past the windmills, the church, and the old town hall.

Best Things to Do in Maassluis: Activities and Tours

Maassluis offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all age groups. Here are some of the top things to do in Maassluis:

  1. Take a gin distillery tour: Maassluis is known for its Dutch gin production. Visitors can take a tour of the distillery and learn about the history of gin in the town.

  2. Explore the town on a canal boat tour: Visitors can take a leisurely boat tour of the town and enjoy the scenic views of the harbor and the canals.

  3. Visit the local market: Maassluis has a regular Saturday market where you can find local produce, souvenirs, and other goodies.

Food and Drink Scene of Maassluis

Maassluis has a rich food and drink scene that offers a taste of the authentic Dutch cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stamppot: A traditional Dutch dish made with mashed potatoes, vegetables, and sausage.

  2. Poffertjes: Small fluffy pancakes served with butter and powdered sugar.

  3. Kibbeling: Deep-fried cod served with a side of fries.

Local Beverages

  1. Dutch gin: Also known as Jenever, Dutch gin has been produced in Maassluis since the 17th century.

  2. Dutch beer: Netherlands has a thriving craft beer scene, and Maassluis has its share of local breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Maassluis

Maassluis has a rich cultural heritage, and the town’s residents take pride in their traditions. Here are some of the local customs and greetings to keep in mind:

  1. When meeting someone for the first time, Dutch people usually shake hands, with the exception of close friends and family.

  2. Manners are important in Dutch culture, so be sure to use ‘please’ and ‘thank you’ when interacting with locals.

  3. The Dutch take punctuality seriously, so be sure to arrive on time for appointments and meetings.

Best Time to Visit Maassluis

The best time to visit Maassluis is during the spring (March-June) and autumn (September-November) months when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. Summer (July-August) is also a popular time to visit, but expect warmer temperatures and more crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maassluis

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Maassluis:

Do:

  1. Explore the town on foot or by bike to get a better sense of its character.

  2. Try the local gin and beer, as they are an integral part of the town’s culture.

  3. Respect the local customs and keep the noise levels low, especially when passing through residential areas.

Don’t:

  2. Avoid smoking in public spaces, as it is not allowed in many areas.

  3. Don’t be late for appointments or meetings, as punctuality is important to the Dutch.

Getting to Maassluis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Maassluis is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here’s a breakdown:

Airports Near Maassluis

  1. Rotterdam The Hague Airport: The airport is located 25 kilometers from Maassluis and has connections to several European destinations.

  2. Amsterdam Schiphol Airport: The airport is located 55 kilometers from Maassluis and has connections to several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Maassluis

  1. Rotterdam Centraal Station: The station has frequent connections to Maassluis, with buses running every 10-15 minutes.

  2. The Hague Centraal Station: The station has connections to Maassluis, with buses running every 30 minutes.

Other Options

  1. Train: The nearest train stations to Maassluis are Rotterdam Centraal Station and Maassluis West Station, both of which have frequent connections to Amsterdam, The Hague, and other Dutch cities.

  2. Car rental: Maassluis is easily accessible by car, with several car rental companies operating in the town.

How to Get Around in Maassluis

Maassluis is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance from each other. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Maassluis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion
