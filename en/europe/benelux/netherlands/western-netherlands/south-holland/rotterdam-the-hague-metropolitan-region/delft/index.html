Introduction to Delft

Delft is a cozy university town located in South Holland, Western Netherlands. It is famous for its iconic blue and white pottery, charming canals, and historic landmarks. Delft offers a lot of opportunities to explore the Dutch culture and enjoy the Dutch way of life. In this travel guide, you will find useful information on the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Delft

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Royal Delft Museum

The Royal Delft Museum is a great place to start your Delft journey. You can explore the history of the famous Delft Blue pottery and learn about the craftsman ship behind the production of this world-famous porcelain. The museum offers a unique opportunity to try making your own Delftware and understand what makes it so special.

Museum Prinsenhof Delft

The Museum Prinsenhof Delft is located in a beautiful historic building that used to be a monastery, a residence for William of Orange, and now a museum. It houses a collection of Dutch Golden Age Art, an impressive exhibition about William of Orange, and a series of 17th century rooms that are among the most beautiful in the Netherlands.

Old Church

Old Church is the oldest church in Delft, built around 1240 AD. It is a unique example of Dutch Gothic architecture. The church is also the final resting place of William of Orange and Johannes Vermeer, the famous Dutch painter.

New Church

New Church is the tallest church in the Netherlands and one of the most important. It is the final resting place of the Dutch Royal Family, including William of Orange and Queen Juliana. There are also a series of impressive stained glass windows and an impressive art exhibition in the church.

Natural Attractions of Delft: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Delftse Hout

Delftse Hout is a popular park in Delft, with a large lake, a marina, and plenty of greenery. It is a perfect spot for picnics, swimming, hiking and camping. You can rent bicycles, boats, and other equipment to enjoy the park to its fullest.

Botanical Garden

The Botanical Garden in Delft is a lush green oasis in the heart of the city. It is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna and is a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Midden-Delfland

Midden-Delfland is a picturesque countryside area located right next to Delft. It is a perfect spot for hiking and cycling and is packed with charming villages, windmills, and beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delft

Delft is a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. The best trails for hiking and biking are in the Midden-Delfland area, where you can enjoy the beautiful nature and rural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Delft: Activities and Tours

Canal Tour

A canal tour is a must-do activity when visiting Delft. You can take a boat tour to explore the charming canals and see the city from a different perspective.

Beer Tour

Delft is known for its beer, and taking a beer tour is an excellent way to discover the local brews. You can visit the local breweries and learn about the brewing process while tasting some delicious beer.

Bike Tour

Bike tours are an excellent way to explore Delft and its surroundings. You can rent a bike and take a self-guided tour or join a guided tour to explore the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delft

Day 1: Visit the Royal Delft Museum and explore the historic city center. Day 2: Visit the Museum Prinsenhof Delft and the Old Church and enjoy a canal tour. Day 3: Visit the Botanical Garden and go on a bike tour to explore Midden-Delfland. Day 4: Take a beer tour and enjoy some local brews with a traditional Dutch meal. Day 5: Visit New Church and explore the charming streets and markets. Day 6: Hike through Delftse Hout and enjoy a picnic by the lake. Day 7: Visit the nearby city of The Hague to discover more Dutch culture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Delft

Must-Try Dishes

Stroopwafel

Stroopwafel is a traditional Dutch sweet treat that consists of two thin waffles joined together with a layer of caramel syrup in the middle. It is a must-try when in Delft, and you can find it in any bakery or cafe.

Dutch Cheese

Dutch cheese is among the best in the world, and Delft is a great place to taste the various varieties. You can visit the local cheese shops to try some Gouda or Edam cheese, and you can even take a cheese-making workshop to learn how to make your own.

Local Beverages

Beer

Delft is known for its beer, and you can find plenty of local brews in the pubs and bars around the city. Some of the best local beers are De Koperen Kat, De Delftse Brouwers, and De Lelie.

Jenever

Jenever is a traditional Dutch gin distilled from malt wine, and Delft is one of the best places to taste it. You can visit the Jenever Museum to learn about the history of this drink and taste some of the best Jenever in the Netherlands.

Where to Stay in Delft: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delft offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Delft are:

Hotel de Koepoort

Hotel de Koepoort is a charming hotel located in the city center, within walking distance of all the major attractions. The hotel features comfortable rooms, a cozy garden, and a delicious breakfast.

Hostel Delft

Hostel Delft is an affordable option for backpackers and budget travelers. It offers dorm rooms and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lounge area.

Leeuwenbrug Hotel

Leeuwenbrug Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, overlooking the canals. It features spacious rooms, a delicious breakfast, and an excellent restaurant.

What to Buy in Delft: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Delft Blue Pottery

Delft Blue Pottery is one of the most iconic products of Delft. You can find it in any souvenir shop or visit the Royal Delft Factory to buy some authentic Delftware.

Stroopwafel

Stroopwafel is a tasty souvenir that you can bring back home from Delft. You can find it in any bakery or supermarket around the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Delft

Delft is a traditional Dutch town with a unique culture and customs. The locals are friendly, welcoming, and always ready to share their customs and traditions with visitors. Some of the most important customs and traditions of Delft include celebrating King’s Day, cycling, and playing football.

Best Time to Visit Delft

The best time to visit Delft is between April and October when the weather is warm and dry. The city comes alive during the summer months with plenty of festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delft

Do:

  • Do rent a bike to explore the city and its surroundings.
  • Do try some local beer and cheese.
  • Do visit the famous landmarks like Old Church and the Royal Delft Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the city or parks.
  • Don’t make noise in residential areas.
  • Don’t forget to respect the customs and traditions of Delft.

Safety and Health

Safety in Delft

Delft is a safe city, but like any other city, you should take precautions to avoid any incidents. It is recommended to keep your valuables safe, avoid walking alone at night, and be cautious with strangers.

Health in Delft

Delft has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. It is recommended to have travel insurance that covers emergency medical treatment.

Getting to Delft: Transportation Options and Logistics

Delft is easily accessible by public transportation, including trains, buses, and trams. The city is located between Rotterdam and The Hague, which makes it easy to combine a visit to Delft with other nearby cities.

Airports Near Delft

The closest airport to Delft is Rotterdam The Hague Airport, which is about 20km away. Amsterdam’s Schiphol Airport is also nearby, and it is easy to reach Delft by train or bus from both airports.

Bus Stations Near Delft

Delft has a central bus station located near the train station. From here, you can take buses to nearby cities, towns, and attractions.

Other Options

Delft is located between Rotterdam and The Hague, which makes it easy to reach by train or car. It is also possible to rent a bike and cycle to Delft from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Delft

Delft is a compact city, and the best way to get around is on foot or by bike. You can rent a bike from one of the many rental shops around the city and explore the charming streets, canals, and parks at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Delft: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Hague

The Hague is the third-largest city in the Netherlands and is located just 10 km from Delft. It is home to the Dutch government and many international organizations. You can visit the famous Peace Palace, Madurodam miniature park, and the beautiful beaches.

Rotterdam

Rotterdam is a vibrant and modern city with impressive architecture and stunning skyline. You can visit the famous Cube Houses, the Euromast Tower, and the Boijmans Van Beuningen Museum.

Conclusion

Delft is a hidden gem in the Netherlands that offers a lot to explore, from historic landmarks to beautiful countryside and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Delft and discover all the best places to visit, things to do, and experiences to have in this charming Dutch city.
