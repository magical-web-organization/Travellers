Introduction to Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region is located in South Holland, in Western Netherlands. It is one of the most densely populated and industrialized areas in the country. The region is also known for its modern architecture, cultural landmarks, and scenic natural attractions. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, where to stay, local culture and customs, and safety and health tips.

List of Places to Visit in Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen

Located in the heart of Rotterdam, Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen showcases an extensive collection of art and artifacts spanning several centuries. The museum is home to works by renowned masters like Rembrandt, Van Gogh, and Monet, making it a must-see attraction for art lovers.

The Hague Historical Museum

The Hague Historical Museum chronicles the city’s rich history and cultural heritage. The museum is housed in a 17th-century palace and features exhibitions on local art, culture, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kralingse Bos

Kralingse Bos is a beautiful green space in Rotterdam that features an expansive park and lake. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and picnicking while taking in the lush surroundings of the forest.

Scheveningen Beach

Scheveningen Beach is one of the most popular beaches in the Netherlands and a favored spot for surfing, swimming, and sunbathing. The beach is adorned with several beach bars and restaurants and is a lively spot throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

The Rotterdam City Walk

This 10 km walk takes visitors on a scenic route through the city, featuring cultural landmarks like the Erasmus Bridge, famous monuments like the Cube Houses, and breathtaking views of the city’s skyline.

Dunes of the Hook of Holland

The Dunes of the Hook of Holland offers a panoramic vista of the sea and sand dunes. The 12 km biking route takes visitors through scenic spots like the Hook of Holland Lighthouse, the historic Fort 1881, and the expansive dunes themselves.

Best Things to Do in Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region: Activities and Tours

Rotterdam Harbour Boat Tour

The Rotterdam Harbour is one of the largest ports in the world. A boat tour is the best way to experience the vast size and bustling activity of the port, with an insider’s view of the port’s history, operations, and future initiatives.

Explore the Rotterdam Market Hall

The Rotterdam Market Hall is an indoor market that offers visitors a local culinary experience - with over 100 food stalls offering an array of fresh-produce, meat, and fish.

Food and Drink Scene of Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bitterballen - A deep-fried, meat-based snack that is popular in Dutch bars and restaurants.

  • Stroopwafel - A wafer-like cookie made from two thin layers of baked dough and caramel syrup.

  • Hollandse Nieuwe Haring - A local Dutch dish, consisting of fresh herring served with onions and pickles

Local Beverages

  • Jenever - Dutch gin, a popular beverage distilled from malt or rye.

  • Bokbier - A dark beer made with a high alcohol content, available in autumn and winter months.

  • Weissbier - A wheat beer, often served with a slice of lemon or orange, popular in summer months.

Where to Stay in Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mainport Design Hotel

A luxurious hotel that offers panoramic views of the Rotterdam skyline, Mainport Design Hotel is situated in the heart of the city. The hotel features a wellness center, gym, and several exquisite dining options.

King Kong Hostel

A budget-friendly option, King Kong Hostel is a trendy hostel located in the heart of the city. The hostel features clean and spacious dormitories and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with spectacular views of the city.

What to Buy in Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Delftware - A type of Dutch pottery that features blue and white designs, which originated in the city of Delft.

  • Rotterdam Summer Carnival T-Shirts and Accessories - The Rotterdam Summer Carnival is one of the region’s biggest events, featuring a vibrant parade route. T-Shirts and accessories of this event are a popular souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

The people of Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region are friendly and welcoming to visitors. When greeting locals, it’s customary to shake hands and maintain eye contact. Smoking is prohibited in most public places in the Netherlands, so visitors should be aware of designated smoking areas.

Best Time to Visit Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

The best time to visit Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region is between April and October, when the weather is mild and conducive for outdoor activities. The summer months of June through August are peak tourist season, so visitors should expect larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

Do:

  • Carry a bicycle helmet when biking routes.

  • TIP service providers - unlike in the US, service in restaurants and cafes is not commonly included in the bill.

  • Adhere to designated smoking areas.

Don’t:

  • Order coffee at the end of a meal - Dutch people generally prefer coffee to be taken after dessert.

  • Touch the art and artifacts in museums and other exhibit halls.

  • Consume drugs or engage in other illicit activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

The region is generally safe for visitors. However, it is wise to watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas, particularly around tourist sites and public transportation.

Health in Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region

The Netherlands has a highly developed healthcare system, and visitors can expect quick and comprehensive medical attention if necessary. Visitors are also encouraged to invest in travel insurance before embarking on their visit.
