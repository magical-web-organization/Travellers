Introduction to Scheveningen

Scheveningen is a picturesque seaside town situated in the Rotterdam-The Hague Metropolitan Region in South Holland, Western Netherlands. Known for its long sandy beaches, world-class resorts, and awe-inspiring natural beauty, the town offers an eclectic mix of culture, history, and adventure. Whether you’re seeking a romantic getaway or a family vacation, Scheveningen has something in store for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Scheveningen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Scheveningen is home to several museums and historical sites that explore the rich history and culture of the town. The Museum Beelden aan Zee is a magnificent sculpture museum located on the dunes overlooking the sea. The museum houses an impressive collection of contemporary art sculpture and figurative art from different artists worldwide.

The Muzee Scheveningen, a small but fascinating museum, explores the town’s maritime history and eco-systems of the North Sea. The museum showcases a wide range of exhibits concerning the town’s rich heritage, including marine animals and micro-organisms.

Perfect for history lovers, the Atlantikwall Museum, located on the outskirts of Scheveningen, is a fascinating museum that depicts the history of WWII. You get to explore the bunkers, tunnels, and observation posts used during the war, and learn about the Atlantic Wall defense system that was built by Nazi Germany.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Scheveningen prides itself on its beautiful sandy beaches and scenic landscapes. The Scheveningen Pier is the most popular tourist attraction in the area. The pier offers awe-inspiring views of the North Sea coast and excellent dining and shopping facilities.

De Pier, a 300 meters boardwalk on the North Sea coastline, is a must-visit location. The area features a wide range of classic Dutch fast food, street markets, Dutch products, and souvenir shops.

If you’re looking for something more adventurous, head out to the Zipline Scheveningen located at the heart of Scheveningen. You will experience flying over the pristine beaches while appreciating the beautiful views of the North Sea coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scheveningen

Scheveningen is renowned for its plethora of cycling and hiking trails. The Westduinpark is a stunning nature reserve that offers numerous hiking and cycling routes. The park offers some of the best hilltop views of the North Sea coast and the surrounding countryside.

If you’re a bike enthusiast, you must visit the NK Beachrace Noordwijk-Scheveningen. It’s an annual cycling event that takes place every January, and attracts thousands of visitors worldwide.

Best Things to Do in Scheveningen: Activities and Tours

From exhilarating watersports to leisure ocean walks, Scheveningen offers an endless list of activities and tours for its visitors.

Wave Surf School Scheveningen is a popular spot for surfers to catch some waves. The school offers surfing lessons and packages for beginners and experienced surfers.

The Hague Highlights Tour, a walking tour of The Hague’s famous landmarks, offers an enjoyable way to see the most important and famous parts of The Hague. The tour brings you to the Binnenhof, Noordeinde Palace, and the Peace Palace, among others.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scheveningen

To make the most of your Scheveningen holiday, we recommend the following itinerary that can keep you entertained for a week.

Day 1: Explore the beaches and enjoy the great food on the pier.

Day 2: Visit the Muzee Scheveningen and Museum Beelden aan Zee.

Day 3: Go shopping and try the local street food at De Pier.

Day 4: Hike or bike Westduinpark and its beautiful hilltop views.

Day 5: Visit Madurodam Park and discover the miniature versions of various Dutch landmarks.

Day 6: Take the “The Hague Highlights Tour.”

Day 7: Enjoy an adventurous day of watersports and sign up for the Zipline Scheveningen experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Scheveningen

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Scheveningen is diverse, and you can enjoy different types of cuisine. The town’s proximity to the sea means there is a wide variety of seafood on the menus. One must-try dish is Hollandse Nieuwe, a cured Dutch Herring, which is traditionally served with chopped onions.

Another popular Scheveningen dish is Bitterballen, a tasty Dutch fried snack, made from a mixture of beef and roux.

Local Beverages

You cannot leave Scheveningen without trying Dutch beer. The town has several taverns where you can enjoy a refreshing bottle of Heineken or any other Dutch beer of your choice.

Where to Stay in Scheveningen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Scheveningen has a range of accommodations for different budgets. From luxury beach resorts to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, the town has something in store for everyone.

The Grand Hotel Amrath Kurhaus is a luxurious five-star beach resort that offers majestic sea views, an indoor swimming pool, wellness facilities, and an excellent restaurant.

For budget travelers, Stayokay Hostel Scheveningen is an excellent option. The hostel is centrally located and is a perfect starting point for exploring the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Scheveningen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Scheveningen is famous for its art studios that produce unique handmade souvenirs, like Delft Blue pottery. The town also has several local shops selling special Scheveningen jars of sea salt and postcards.

Local Culture and Customs of Scheveningen

Scheveningen’s customs and traditions revolve around fishing, beach life, and maritime heritage. Scheveningen is famous for its New Year’s Dive, an annual event that attracts thousands of people to the town each year. During the event, locals and visitors alike jump into the freezing North Sea to celebrate the start of a new year.

Best Time to Visit Scheveningen

The best time to visit Scheveningen is from May to September, when the weather is warmer and perfect for beach activities and water sports. The town is more crowded during these months with tourists and local vacationers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scheveningen

Do:

  • Explore the beaches like the locals
  • Try the Dutch cuisine
  • Bring your camera to capture the stunning views
  • Visit Scheveningen Pier a few times during your stay

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches
  • Disturb marine life
  • Ignore the weather forecast when engaging in water sports

Safety and Health

Safety in Scheveningen

Scheveningen is generally a safe town. Crime levels are low, and locals are generally friendly and helpful. A general tip is to keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Scheveningen

Scheveningen has a well-functioning healthcare system with several hospitals and clinics throughout the town. The emergency number is 112 for any emergencies and urgent medical care.

Getting to Scheveningen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scheveningen

Scheveningen is situated approximately 16 miles from Rotterdam The Hague Airport, and 36 miles from Amsterdam Schiphol Airport.

Bus Stations Near Scheveningen

Scheveningen has several bus stations that connect to other towns and cities within the Netherlands.

Other Options

The tram is an excellent transport option, and the town has several tram stations that connect Scheveningen to other cities such as The Hague and Rotterdam.

How to Get Around in Scheveningen

Scheveningen is relatively small, which makes walking the most convenient form of transport within the town. If you want to explore other areas, renting a bike is a great alternative.

Where to Go Next from Scheveningen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Hague, located just 15 minutes away, is the closest and most recommended town to visit next. It has several attractions such as the Peace Palace and the Binnenhof.

Another recommended destination to visit is Rotterdam, a beautiful port city with magnificent architecture and trendy bars.

Conclusion

Scheveningen is a unique little gem that offers a blend of culture, heritage, and adventure. It’s a destination that every traveler must visit once in their lifetime. With our comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Scheveningen, experience the town’s local cultures, and create unforgettable memories.
