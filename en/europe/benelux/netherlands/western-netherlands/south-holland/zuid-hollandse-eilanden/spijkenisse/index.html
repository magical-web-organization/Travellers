Introduction to Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse is a charming town located in the province of South Holland, in the western region of the Netherlands. The town is surrounded by nature reserves, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. The town is also known for its cultural landmarks and museums, making it an excellent destination for history buffs. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation or an action-packed adventure, Spijkenisse has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Spijkenisse

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Spijkenisse has many museums and historical landmarks to explore:

  • Old Town Hall: This historical building dates back to the 17th century and is located in the heart of Spijkenisse’s old town. The building now houses a museum that showcases the town’s history and culture.

  • Polderhuis Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of the region and its people. The museum has a large collection of photographs, artifacts, and documents that showcase the region’s history.

  • Fortress Island: This is a small island located in the Spijkenisse river and is a historical site from the Dutch Waterline Defense System. Visitors can explore the island’s fortifications and learn about the defense system that protected the Netherlands from invaders.

Natural Attractions of Spijkenisse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spijkenisse has many natural attractions to offer for those who love the outdoors:

  • De Uitkijk: This observation tower offers stunning views of the surrounding nature reserve and is a great place to spot local wildlife.

  • Natuurspeeltuin de Flierefluiter: This playground in the middle of nature offers plenty of fun activities for children. The playground includes a water play area, rope bridges, and much more.

  • Spijkenisse Water Playground: The playground features various water features and play areas, including slides, fountains, and a river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse is located in an area with fantastic scenic cycling routes. The town and its surroundings offer various trails that cater to all skill levels. One of the most popular cycling routes in the area is the ‘Oeverlandenloop.’ This route is around nine miles long and takes you through the beautiful countryside of Spijkenisse. The route passes through several nature reserves, including De Uitkijk, and is an excellent opportunity to explore the town’s beautiful nature.

Best Things to Do in Spijkenisse: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Spijkenisse. Here are some of the best activities and tours that the town has to offer:

  • Climbing Park Adventure City: This park has various climbing trails and bridges that cater to all skill levels. It is an excellent opportunity to challenge yourself with an action-packed day out.

  • Boat Tours: Take a guided boat tour through the Spijkenisse and its surrounding waterways, learn about the history and culture of the town while enjoying a relaxing day out on the water.

  • Shopping: Shop for unique souvenirs, local crafts, and local produce at the town’s weekly market. The market is held every Wednesday and Saturday, and it offers a wide array of local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse is an excellent destination for a week-long holiday packed with activities for the whole family. Here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Polderhuis Museum and Old Town Hall to learn about the town’s history and culture.

  • Day 2: Explore nature reserves around Spijkenisse, including De Uitkijk and Natuurspeeltuin de Flierefluiter.

  • Day 3: Try out climbing or zipline park Adventure City.

  • Day 4: Take a boat tour around the waterways surrounding Spijkenisse.

  • Day 5: Rent bicycles and cycle through the beautiful countryside on the Oeverlandenloop route.

  • Day 6: Enjoy shopping and dining in the town’s weekly market and local restaurants.

  • Day 7: Visit the Fortress Island, and learn about the Dutch Waterline Defense System.

Food and Drink Scene of Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse has a fantastic food and drink scene, offering traditional Dutch and local specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ossenworst: A traditional Dutch sausage, served raw with rye bread.

  • Stroopwafels: A caramel-filled Dutch cookie.

  • Hollandse Nieuwe Haring: A traditional Dutch fish dish served with onions and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The Netherlands is famous for its beer, and Spijkenisse is no exception. Try out local beers such as Gose or the Lentebock.

  • Jenever: This traditional Dutch gin can be enjoyed straight or mixed with various fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Spijkenisse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spijkenisse offers a variety of accommodations to cater to different budgets and travel styles. Here are some of the top places to stay.

  • Hotel van der Valk Ridderkerk: This four-star hotel offers modern, comfortable rooms, spa facilities, and a restaurant.

  • Michel & Friends Hotel: This three-star hotel offers affordable accommodation and is located in the town center.

  • B&B De Botterhut: This cozy guesthouse offers personalized services, and each room is uniquely decorated to make you feel at home.

What to Buy in Spijkenisse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Delftware: The famous blue and white pottery is produced in the town of Delft, which is just a short distance away. You can buy Delftware in souvenir shops and markets in Spijkenisse.

  • Cheese: The Netherlands is known for its cheese, and you can find some of the best cheese shops in Spijkenisse.

Local Culture and Customs of Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse is a friendly and welcoming town, and the locals take pride in their town’s heritage. It is customary to greet locals with a simple ‘Goedemorgen’ (good morning) or ‘Goedenavond’ (good evening). Respect local customs and traditions, and try to learn basic Dutch phrases to communicate with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Spijkenisse

The best time to visit Spijkenisse is in the summer, between June and August, when the weather is warm and sunny. The town has many outdoor activities, making it a perfect summer destination. However, it is also a great destination for a winter holiday, with its cozy cafes and picturesque snowy landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spijkenisse

Do:

  • Try out local foods and drinks.

  • Respect the locals’ customs and traditions.

  • Explore the town’s natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.

  • Smoke in public spaces.

  • Speak loudly in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates. However, it is still advisable to take basic precautions when traveling, such as keeping an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Spijkenisse

The Netherlands has a high-quality healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in Spijkenisse. It is advisable to have health insurance and have your European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) with you.

Getting to Spijkenisse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spijkenisse

The closest airport to Spijkenisse is Rotterdam The Hague Airport, which is around 13 miles away. Another option is Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, which is around 40 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse has a bus service that connects to major cities in the Netherlands. You can take a bus from Rotterdam Central Station to Spijkenisse for a 30-minute ride.

Other Options

The town is easily accessible by car, and there are plenty of parking options available. You can also take a taxi or cycle to the town.

How to Get Around in Spijkenisse

Spijkenisse is not a big town, and walking is the best way to get around. However, you can also rent a bicycle to explore the town. There is also a bus service available that connects to major destinations.

Where to Go Next from Spijkenisse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rotterdam: The city is located just ten miles away and is a great destination for exploring modern architecture and museums.

  • The Hague: Another popular destination, around 20 miles from Spijkenisse, is known for its historical landmarks, art museums, and beautiful beaches.

  • Delft: This charming town is famous for its pottery and is the birthplace of Dutch Master painter Johannes Vermeer.

Conclusion

Spijkenisse is a wonderful destination with something for everyone, whether you are interested in outdoor activities, history, or culture. The town offers fantastic natural scenery, delicious local foods, and welcoming locals. Enjoy your trip to Spijkenisse, and do not forget to explore its neighboring destinations.
