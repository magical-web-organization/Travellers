Introduction to Kinderdijk

Kinderdijk is a small village situated in the Alblasserwaard-Drechtsteden region of South Holland, Netherlands. It is known for its iconic 18th-century windmills that are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and have become an important tourist attraction in the Netherlands. In this comprehensive tour guide to Kinderdijk, we have gathered all the information you need to plan your trip, including places to visit, best hiking/biking routes, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Kinderdijk

If you are planning to visit Kinderdijk, there are several places that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kinderdijk Windmill Complex: The iconic 18th-century windmills are the main attraction of Kinderdijk and have been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. You can take a tour of the windmills and learn about their history and importance.
  • Nederwaard Museum Mill: This museum mill is in the Nederwaard section of the windmill complex and provides visitors with a glimpse into the life of a miller in the early 20th century.
  • Blokker Windmill Museum: This museum aims to educate visitors about wind energy and its history.

Natural Attractions of Kinderdijk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Biesbosch National Park: This national park is a wetland area that contains riverine tidal forests, estuary swamps, and other wetland habitats. You can go on a boat tour to explore the national park.
  • Linge River: You can go on a boat tour on the Linge River and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kinderdijk scenic bicycle route: This scenic route is ideal for cycling enthusiasts who want to explore the beautiful Dutch countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kinderdijk

Kinderdijk has several hiking and biking trails that take you through the scenic countryside. Here are a few routes that you can try:

  • The Kinderdijk scenic bicycle route: This route is 15km long and takes you through the picturesque Dutch countryside. You can rent a bike and embark on this self-guided tour.
  • Kinderdijk and the Alblasserwaard bike route: This route covers 57km and takes you through the windmills of Kinderdijk and the scenic Alblasserwaard landscape.
  • Wandelen tussen de molens (Walking between the mills) trail: This trail is 10km long and takes you through the windmills of Kinderdijk.

Best Things to Do in Kinderdijk: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the windmills, there are several things you can do in Kinderdijk:

  • Take a boat tour: You can go on a boat tour to explore the scenic Dutch countryside and the Biesbosch National Park.
  • Visit the museums: The Nederwaard Museum Mill and the Blokker Windmill Museum are ideal places to learn about the history and importance of wind energy.
  • Try local delicacies: The Dutch cuisine is famous for its cheese, stroopwafels, and bitterballen. Do not forget to try these delicacies when in Kinderdijk.

Food and Drink Scene of Kinderdijk

When in Kinderdijk, do not forget to try the local Dutch cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bitterballen: A Dutch snack made with meat ragout, covered in breadcrumbs and fried until crispy.
  • Stroopwafels: A famous Dutch dessert made with two thin waffles filled with caramel syrup.
  • Poffertjes: Small round pancakes topped with butter and powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Genever: A Dutch alcohol similar to gin and often served as a digestif.
  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch beer that has been popular in the country for centuries.
  • Dutch coffee: A coffee that is spiked with a shot of gin or Dutch liqueur.

  • Stayokay Hostel Alblasserwaard: A budget-friendly hostel that is ideal for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Kinderdijk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs to bring home, here are a few options:

  • Kinderdijk Windmill souvenirs: You can buy miniature windmills, postcards, and other souvenirs with pictures of the windmills on them.
  • Cheese: The Dutch are famous for their cheese. You can buy Gouda cheese, Edam cheese, and other varieties as a souvenir.
  • Dutch Clogs: Wooden shoes, known as clogs, are a traditional Dutch souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kinderdijk

The Dutch are known for their liberal culture and tolerance. Here are a few customs and etiquettes to keep in mind:

  • Biking culture: Biking is a popular mode of transportation in the Netherlands. Be mindful of bike paths and cycling lanes when walking on the streets.
  • Tipping culture: Tipping in the Netherlands is not mandatory, but it is customary to leave a small tip (10% of the total bill) if the service was good.
  • Alcohol consumption: The legal drinking age in the Netherlands is 18 years. It is illegal to drink alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit Kinderdijk

The best time to visit Kinderdijk is during the spring (April-May) and autumn (September-November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are less. However, if you want to witness the tulip fields in full bloom, visit in April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kinderdijk

Here are a few do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind when visiting Kinderdijk:

Do:

  • Bring comfortable shoes: You will be doing a lot of walking and biking, so make sure to wear comfortable shoes.
  • Take a guided tour of the windmills: This will help you learn about the history and importance of the windmills.
  • Try local delicacies: Dutch cuisine is delicious, so make sure to try the local dishes and desserts.

Don’t:

  • Litter: The Dutch take environmental issues seriously, so make sure to dispose of your trash in the designated containers.
  • Ride bikes on footpaths: It is illegal to ride bikes on footpaths, so be mindful of the bike lanes and paths.
  • Smoke in public places: Smoking in public places is illegal in the Netherlands.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kinderdijk

Kinderdijk is a safe place to visit. However, it is always good to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are a few tips:

  • Keep your belongings safe: Pickpocketing can be a problem in crowded places, so keep your belongings safe.
  • Observe traffic rules: The Dutch take traffic rules seriously, so be mindful of the traffic signals when crossing the streets.
  • Swimming in the river is dangerous: The currents in the river can be strong and dangerous. Do not attempt to swim in the river.

Health in Kinderdijk

Kinderdijk has good healthcare facilities, and you can find hospitals and pharmacies in nearby cities. However, it is always good to take precautions to ensure your health:

  • Stay hydrated: The Dutch weather can be unpredictable, so make sure to stay hydrated and carry a water bottle with you at all times.
  • Pack necessary medications: If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, make sure to pack your medications. You can also find pharmacies in nearby cities.

Getting to Kinderdijk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kinderdijk is well-connected to other cities in the Netherlands. Here are a few options to get to Kinderdijk:

Airports Near Kinderdijk

  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport: The nearest international airport is Amsterdam Schiphol Airport, which is 90 km from Kinderdijk.

Bus Stations Near Kinderdijk

  • Rotterdam central station: You can take a bus from Rotterdam central station to Kinderdijk.

Other Options

  • Train: You can take a train to Rotterdam Central station or Dordrecht and then take a bus to Kinderdijk. The train journey from Amsterdam to Rotterdam takes 40 minutes.
  • Car: You can rent a car and drive to Kinderdijk from nearby cities.
