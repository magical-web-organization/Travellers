Introduction to Dordrecht

Are you looking for a hidden gem to explore in the Netherlands that’s not overrun with tourists? Then Dordrecht is the perfect destination for you! Located in the Alblasserwaard-Drechtsteden region in South Holland, Dordrecht is a charming city with picturesque canals, historical landmarks, and scenic views.

This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Dordrecht

Dordrecht is a city rich in history and culture, with several notable attractions you don’t want to miss. Here’s our list of the top places to visit in Dordrecht.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dordrechts Museum: This museum has a vast collection of Dutch paintings and art from the 17th century to the present day.

  • Het Hof van Nederland: A museum that documents the birth of the Netherlands as a state in the 16th century.

  • Grote Kerk: This Gothic church in the city center dates back to the 13th century and is a remarkable example of medieval architecture.

  • Huis Van Gijn: A 19th-century townhouse that now houses a museum where you can experience how the Dutch used to live.

Natural Attractions of Dordrecht: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Biesbosch National Park: A vast nature reserve with hiking and biking trails, kayaking and canoeing, and birdwatching.

  • Merwelanden Recreation Area: A popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts with beaches, hiking trails, and swimming areas.

  • Windmills: Dordrecht has several windmills along the canals that offer scenic views and are worth the visit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dordrecht

Dordrecht is a city perfect for exploring on foot or by bike, with several routes to choose from. Here are some of our favorites.

  • Biesbosch National Park: The National Park has several hiking and biking trails that take you through marshlands and willow trees.

  • Dordtse Biesbosch: A nature reserve on the east side of Dordrecht with hiking and biking trails.

  • Eiland van Dordrecht Route: This biking route takes you through the city center and along the canals, highlighting Dordrecht’s rich history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Dordrecht: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Dordrecht, here are some activities and tours worth checking out.

  • Boat Tour of Dordrecht: Explore the city’s canals by boat and take in the beautiful historic houses and landmarks.

  • Visit a Cheese Farm: Learn about the making of Dutch cheese and taste different varieties. The nearest cheese farm is just half an hour away from Dordrecht.

  • Markets: Dordrecht has several markets, which are great to explore to see the city’s local produce and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dordrecht

If you’re planning to spend a week in Dordrecht, here’s a proposed itinerary to help you make the most of your visit.

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city center, visit Grote Kerk and Het Hof van Nederland
Day 2 Visit Dordrechts Museum and Huis Van Gijn
Day 3 Go on a boat tour of Dordrecht and take in the beautiful views
Day 4 Hike or bike in Biesbosch National Park
Day 5 Visit a cheese farm and a local market
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Rotterdam or Kinderdijk windmills
Day 7 Relax at Merwelanden Recreation Area or go on a cycling tour of Eiland van Dordrecht

Food and Drink Scene of Dordrecht

Dutch cuisine is hearty and delicious, with several local specialties worth trying in Dordrecht.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bitterballen: Small fried balls of meat ragout, often served as a snack with drinks.

  • Stamppot: A traditional Dutch dish made of mashed potatoes and vegetables, often served with sausage or meatballs.

  • Poffertjes: Small fluffy pancakes, usually served with butter and powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A Dutch gin, usually drunk as a shot or mixed in cocktails.

  • Bock Beer: A seasonal dark beer that’s popular in the fall.

  • Limoncello: A sweet and citrusy liqueur that’s often served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Dordrecht: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dordrecht has several options for accommodation, depending on your budget and preferences.

  • B&B De Dubbelde Palmboom: A bed and breakfast located in a historic house in the city center.

  • Villa Augustus: A hotel with a beautiful garden and restaurant, located near the Merwelanden Recreation Area.

  • Stayokay Dordrecht: A hostel located in a scenic area of the city, with affordable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Dordrecht: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, Dordrecht has several options.

  • Dordrechtse Biesbosch Honey: Locally produced honey from the Biesbosch National Park.

  • Dordrechtse Moppen: A traditional biscuit made with honey and cinnamon that’s popular in Dordrecht.

  • Dordtse Worst: A savory sausage that’s a Dordrecht specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Dordrecht

Dutch culture is friendly and welcoming, with several customs worth knowing before visiting.

  • It’s customary to greet people with a handshake and direct eye contact.

  • Tipping is not always expected, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

  • The Dutch are generally punctual and appreciate being on time.

Best Time to Visit Dordrecht

The best time to visit Dordrecht is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, it’s also the busiest time of the year, so expect crowds. Spring and autumn are also excellent times to visit, with milder weather and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dordrecht

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Dordrecht.

Do:

  • Rent a bike or explore on foot to take in the city’s charm.

  • Try local specialties such as bitterballen, stamppot, and poffertjes.

  • Visit markets and cheese farms to discover local produce and crafts.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places as it’s prohibited in the Netherlands.

  • Take photographs of people without their consent.

  • Be late for appointments or reservations, as punctuality is important in Dutch culture.

Safety and Health

Dordrecht is a safe city, with low crime rates and a well-developed healthcare system.

Safety in Dordrecht

  • Keep your belongings safe and secure, especially in tourist areas.

  • Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded places.

  • Use caution when cycling, as bike accidents are common in the Netherlands.

Health in Dordrecht

  • The tap water in the Netherlands is safe to drink.

  • The Netherlands has a well-developed healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in Dordrecht.

  • Medical insurance is recommended for travelers.

Getting to Dordrecht: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dordrecht is easily accessible by several means of transportation.

Airports Near Dordrecht

  • Amsterdam Airport Schiphol: The nearest international airport, located about an hour away by train or car.

  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport: A regional airport located about 20 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Dordrecht

  • Dordrecht Centraal Station: The main bus station in Dordrecht, with several connections to nearby cities and towns.

  • Dordrecht Zuid Station: A smaller bus station located in the southern part of the city.

Other Options

  • Train: Dordrecht has several train stations, with connections to other cities and towns in the Netherlands and Europe.

  • Car: Dordrecht is easily accessible by car, with several highways and parking options available.

How to Get Around in Dordrecht

Dordrecht is a compact city and easy to explore on foot or by bike. The city also has a reliable public transportation system, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Dordrecht: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dordrecht is an ideal base for exploring the nearby destinations and taking day trips.

  • Rotterdam: A modern and dynamic city known for its architecture, museums, and commercial activities, just 20 minutes away by train.

  • Kinderdijk Windmills: A world-famous UNESCO World Heritage site with over 19 windmills, located about 30 minutes away by bike or car.

  • The Hague: The political capital of the Netherlands, known for its government and international organizations, about an hour away by train.

Conclusion

Dordrecht is a hidden gem in the Netherlands, with a charming atmosphere, rich history, and scenic views. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Dordrecht. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and markets, Dordrecht has it all. Plan your trip today, and get ready to experience the beauty of Dordrecht on foot or by bike!
