Gouda, a city located in the province of South Holland in Western Netherlands, is a must-visit destination for any traveler. The city boasts a rich history, stunning architecture, and unique cultural experiences that will leave you in awe. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will list the best places to visit, food to try, and things to do in Gouda, Netherlands. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Gouda

Gouda is a small city that is often referred to as the cheese capital of the Netherlands. Besides its world-renowned cheese, the city is known for its cultural diversity, hospitality, and stunning landscapes. Gouda is best explored on foot, with its beautiful old buildings, canals, and small cobbled streets.

List of Places to Visit in Gouda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gouda Museum: The Gouda Museum houses an impressive collection of art and artifacts that date back to the Middle Ages.
  • St. John’s Church: This 15th-century church has one of the most beautiful stained-glass windows in the Netherlands.
  • De Goudse Waag: This historical building was originally built as a cheese weighing station and is now a cheese and crafts museum.

Natural Attractions of Gouda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gouda City Hall: A magnificent city hall that stands on the market square, the building has impressive Gothic architecture.
  • Gouda Cheese Market: This is the place where the cheese is traded, and a historical reenactment of the cheese trade takes place every Thursday morning from April - August.
  • Reeuwijkse Plassen: A beautiful natural park where visitors can hike, bike, and enjoy water sports such as canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gouda

Gouda is surrounded by beautiful countryside and scenic vistas. The city offers various routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts, including:

  • Gouda-De Reeuwijkse Plassen Route: This route takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding Gouda and along the Reeuwijkse Plassen. A total of 31 km.
  • De Meije Route: A 35 km route that lets you explore the picturesque landscape around the village of Meije.
  • Gouda-Lekkerkerk Route: This route takes you along the banks of the River Lek for 24 km.

Best Things to Do in Gouda: Activities and Tours

  • Cheese Tasting Tour: Take a tour of the local cheese market and taste the world-famous Gouda cheese.
  • Canal Boat Tour: Explore the city from the water and admire its beautiful old buildings and scenery.
  • Cooking Workshop: Learn how to cook traditional Dutch dishes such as Stamppot and Haring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gouda

Here is a sample itinerary for spending a week in Gouda:

  • Day 1: Gouda Walking Tour
  • Day 2: Cheese Market and Museum
  • Day 3: Biking Tour to Reeuwijkse Plassen
  • Day 4: St. John’s Church and Gouda Museum
  • Day 5: Cooking Workshop
  • Day 6: Canal Boat Tour
  • Day 7: De Meije

Food and Drink Scene of Gouda

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stroopwafel: A wafer-thin waffle cookie with caramel syrup in between its layers.
  • Goudse Kaas: The world-renowned cheese is widely available and fresh in Gouda.
  • Oliebollen: A traditional Dutch sweet treat, served with powdered sugar on top.

Local Beverages

  • Jenever: A traditional Dutch gin that has been produced in Gouda for centuries.
  • Trappist beer: The region is famous for brewing Trappist beer, which is made by monks who live in the local monastery.

Where to Stay in Gouda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gouda offers a variety of accommodations for every budget. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Central: Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers luxurious accommodations with a view of the market square.
  • The Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms with free Wi-Fi and breakfast.
  • Bed and Breakfast De Kamer Hiernaast: A charming bed and breakfast with an outdoor terrace and garden.

What to Buy in Gouda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gouda Cheese: Of course, the world-famous cheese is a must-buy.
  • Stroopwafels: These delicious Dutch treats make for a perfect gift for loved ones.
  • Dutch Ceramics: The Netherlands is known for its beautiful ceramics, and many shops offer unique pieces made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Gouda

Gouda is known for its hospitality and friendly locals. When visiting the city, it’s important to be respectful of the local customs, including:

  • Greet people with a friendly smile and a handshake.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Don’t speak loudly or play loud music in public places.

Best Time to Visit Gouda

The best time to visit Gouda is from April to August when the cheese market takes place. The weather is also pleasant during this time, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gouda

Do:

  • Try the local beer and cheese.
  • Explore the city on foot or by bike.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs or dress inappropriately.
  • Speak loudly or behave in an unruly manner in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gouda

Gouda is generally a safe city; however, as with any city, it’s important to be vigilant and aware of your surroundings. Take basic precautions such as not leaving your valuables unattended and following safety protocols when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Gouda

The Netherlands has excellent healthcare facilities, and there are several hospitals and clinics in Gouda. Visitors are advised to purchase travel health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Gouda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gouda is easy to reach by public transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Gouda

  • Rotterdam The Hague Airport: 29 km from Gouda.
  • Amsterdam Schiphol Airport: 52 km from Gouda.

Bus Stations Near Gouda

  • Gouda Station: Easily accessible from anywhere in the city.
  • Rotterdam Bus Station: 30 km from Gouda.

Other Options

  • Train: Gouda has excellent train connections to other major cities in the Netherlands.
  • Car: Gouda is easily accessible by car, and there are ample parking options available.

How to Get Around in Gouda

Gouda is a small city, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. There are also regular bus services that connect the city with surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Gouda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Hague: The political capital of the Netherlands, home to the International Court of Justice, is only 25 km from Gouda.
  • Utrecht: A vibrant university town with a rich cultural history, is located just 30 km from Gouda.
  • Kinderdijk: Visit the famous windmills in the Dutch countryside, located only 15 km from Gouda.

Conclusion

Gouda, Netherlands, is a small city that is big on history, culture, and natural beauty. Boasting world-renowned cheese, museums, and historical sites, Gouda is a must-visit destination for any traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you have all the necessary information to plan an unforgettable trip to Gouda. Happy travels!
