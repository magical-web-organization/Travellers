Introduction to Belgium

Belgium, located in the Benelux region of Europe, is a country known for its rich history, delicious food, and beautiful architecture. It is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. Belgium’s capital city, Brussels, is considered the headquarters of the European Union and home of NATO. It is also the country that gave birth to comic book characters like Tintin and Smurfs.

List of Places to Visit in Belgium

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Belgium has a rich history, and there are many museums and historical sites to explore. The following are some of the must-visit attractions in Belgium:

The Atomium

Located in Brussels, the Atomium is a unique structure that was built for the 1958 World Expo. It is 102 meters tall and built in the shape of an iron atom. Tourists can visit the inside of the Atomium for panoramic views of the city.

Grand Place

Grand Place, located in the heart of Brussels, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and considered one of the most beautiful squares in Europe. It is surrounded by guildhalls, museums, and restaurants and is a must-visit for tourists.

The Battlefields of Flanders

To learn about Belgium’s history during World War I, visit the Battlefields of Flanders. It is located in West Flanders and home to several cemeteries, monuments, and museums that commemorate those who died during the war.

Natural Attractions of Belgium: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Belgium also has several natural attractions that are worth visiting. Here are some of the top ones:

Ardennes

The Ardennes is a forested region located in southeastern Belgium. It is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and outdoor activities. The region is also home to several castles and small villages.

The Belgian Coast

The Belgian Coast has several resorts and beaches that attract tourists during the summer months. The dunes and sandy beaches are perfect for sunbathing and water sports.

Bosland

Bosland is a vast forested region located in the Flemish-Brabant and Limburg provinces. It is the perfect destination for those who love hiking and biking.

Food and Drink Scene of Belgium

Belgium is famous for its food and drink offerings. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Belgian waffles
  • Moules-frites (mussels and fries)
  • Stoemp (mashed potatoes with vegetables)
  • Carbonnade flamande (beef stew)

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beers (including Trappist beers)
  • Jenever (Belgian gin)
  • Chocolate milk (Belgian chocolate in milk)

Local Culture and Customs of Belgium

Belgium has a rich local culture and customs worth exploring. Some of the most significant ones include:

  • The Flemish and Walloon cultures
  • Comic book culture
  • The art of lace-making

Best Time to Visit Belgium

The best time to visit Belgium is between April and June or September and October. During these months, the weather is comfortable, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belgium

Do:

  • Tip 10% at restaurants
  • Try to speak a little bit of French or Dutch
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites

Don’t:

  • Forget to validate your train ticket
  • Cut in line or jump the queue
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Belgium

Belgium is a safe country to visit, but tourists should always be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas like train stations and tourist attractions.

Health in Belgium

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and tourists should not hesitate to seek medical care if needed. Tourists should make sure they have adequate health insurance before visiting Belgium.
