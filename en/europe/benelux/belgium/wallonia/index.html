Introduction to Wallonia

Nestled in the southern part of Belgium, Wallonia is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This French-speaking region is blessed with a rich history, scenic landscapes, and a vibrant culture. With its charming small towns, breathtaking countryside views, and delicious cuisine, Wallonia is a perfect destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs of Wallonia.

List of Places to Visit in Wallonia

There’s an abundance of things to see and do in Wallonia. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Wallonia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wallonia is a region steeped in history, art, and architecture. Here are some of the culturally significant attractions you shouldn’t miss:

  • The Citadel of Namur: The impressive fortress overlooks the city of Namur and offers stunning views of the Meuse Valley.
  • The Belfry of Mons: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Belfry of Mons is the tallest bell tower in Belgium.
  • The Bastogne War Museum: A tribute to the soldiers who fought in the Battle of the Bulge during World War II.
  • The Grand Curtius Museum: A museum showcasing the art and culture of Liège.

Natural Attractions of Wallonia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural wonders of Wallonia are breathtakingly beautiful. From the rolling hills of the Ardennes to the charming waterways, the region offers a range of outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • The Ardennes Forest: A stunning expanse of forests perfect for hiking, biking, and camping.
  • The Caves of Han: Explore the underground caves and take a scenic tram ride through the wildlife park.
  • The Lake of Eau d’Heure: This artificial lake is perfect for water sports, fishing, and relaxing.
  • The City of Dinant: A picturesque town on the banks of the Meuse River, with a stunning citadel and collegiate church.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wallonia

Wallonia is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Trans-Ardennes Greenway: A 118km bike trail that follows the Meuse River through the forests and valleys of the Ardennes.
  • The RAVeL network: A network of 45 bike trails that criss-crosses through the beautiful countryside of Wallonia.
  • The Hoge Kempen National Park: A hiking paradise with over 200 km of hiking trails through the forests, heaths, and hills of the park.
  • The Tombeau du Géant: A challenging but rewarding hike that leads you to the “Giant’s Tomb,” a stunning viewpoint over the Semois Valley.

Best Things to Do in Wallonia: Activities and Tours

Wallonia has plenty to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Beer Tasting Tour: Belgium is famous for its beer, and Wallonia is no exception. Tour the local breweries and sample the delicious brews.
  • Kayaking: Paddle down the River Lesse and take in the stunning views of the Ardennes.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take to the skies and enjoy panoramic views of the picturesque Wallonian countryside.
  • The Enchanted City: A unique attraction that lets you explore the beautiful streets and architecture of Thuin through a magical augmented reality experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wallonia

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring Wallonia:

Day 1-2: Liège and its surroundings – visit the Grand Curtius Museum, stroll through the Old City of Liège, and hike or bike in the nearby Ardennes.

Day 3-4: Dinant and the Upper Meuse Valley – explore the river town of Dinant, visit the Citadel, and take a scenic cruise on the Meuse River.

Day 5-6: Namur and the Lower Meuse Valley – visit the impressive Citadel, take a stroll in the city center, and enjoy the beautiful views of the Meuse Valley.

Day 7: Charleroi and surroundings – visit the industrial city of Charleroi, explore the art galleries and museums, and take a hot air balloon ride to see the city from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Wallonia

Wallonia is home to a delicious cuisine that is unique and diverse. From hearty stews to crispy waffles, the region offers a range of dishes to satisfy all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carbonnade Flamande: A hearty beef stew cooked in beer and served with fries.
  • Waterzooi: A creamy chicken or fish stew, a popular dish in Ghent.
  • Liège Waffles: Sweet, yeasty waffles dusted with powdered sugar.
  • Boulettes à la Liégeoise: Spicy meatballs served with a sweet sauce made of apples and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian Beer: Wallonia produces some of Belgium’s finest beers, with over 800 different types to choose from.
  • Pèkèt: A traditional Wallonian fruit brandy, available in a range of flavors including apple, raspberry, and pear.
  • Jenever: A traditional Belgian gin-like drink, often served with a slice of lemon.

Where to Stay in Wallonia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wallonia has accommodation options to suit all budgets and needs. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Radisson Blu Palace Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Spa, Belgium’s famous spa town.
  • Auberge de Jeunesse de Namur: A budget-friendly hostel with great views of the Meuse River.
  • La Maison Blanche aux Volets Bleus: A charming guesthouse located in the picturesque town of Dinant.

What to Buy in Wallonia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wallonia has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some ideas:

  • Chocolate: Belgium is famous for its chocolate, and Wallonia has plenty of artisanal chocolatiers to choose from.
  • Lace: The city of Binche is famous for its delicate and intricate bobbin lace.
  • Beer: Belgium produces some of the world’s finest beers, and Wallonia has a vast range of local breweries to choose from.
  • Mustard: The town of Beauraing is home to Brasserie de la Lesse, a brewery that produces a range of mustards with beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Wallonia

The people of Wallonia are warm and welcoming towards visitors. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: Do greet people politely with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek.
  • Common Courtesy: Do say please (s’il vous plaît) and thank you (merci) when interacting with locals. — Hygiene: Do dress appropriately and maintain good personal hygiene.

Best Time to Visit Wallonia

The best time to visit Wallonia is during the spring or summer months, from March to September. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the region is alive with festivals, concerts, and outdoor activities. The autumn months of October and November are also a great time to visit, with colorful foliage and fewer tourists.

Winter can be cold and snowy, but it is also an ideal time to experience Wallonia’s winter sports. The Christmas season is also a magical time, with festive markets, lights, and celebrations throughout the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wallonia

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Wallonia:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Do explore the region’s beautiful countryside and hiking trails.
  • Do remember to greet locals politely.
  • Do carry some cash, as some smaller shops and establishments may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to try the local chocolates and sweets.
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash and expensive jewelry while traveling.

Safety and Health

Wallonia is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a stable political system. However, it is always advisable to take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding unsafe areas at night.

Safety in Wallonia

  • Don’t leave your valuable items unattended in public areas.
  • Don’t travel alone to poorly lit or isolated areas at night.

Health in Wallonia

Wallonia has good healthcare facilities and services, with a range of hospitals and clinics throughout the region. It is advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Wallonia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wallonia is easily accessible through various modes of transportation. Here are some options:

  • Airports Near Wallonia: The Brussels International Airport is the closest airport to Wallonia. Other nearby airports include Charleroi Airport and Liège Airport.
  • Bus Stations Near Wallonia: Wallonia is well-connected by bus services, with the TEC bus network operating throughout the region.
  • Other Options: The region has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting Wallonia to other parts of Belgium and Europe.

How to Get Around in Wallonia

Wallonia has an efficient public transportation system, with plenty of options to get around. Here are some ways to travel around the region:

  • Buses, Trams, and Metros: Wallonia has an extensive public transportation system, with buses, trams, and metros operating throughout the region.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in larger towns and cities, but they can be expensive.
  • Car Rentals: Renting a car is a great way to travel at your own pace and explore the region in-depth.

Where to Go Next from Wallonia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wallonia is surrounded by other beautiful destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some nearby places to add to your itinerary:

  • Brussels: The capital of Belgium is just a short train ride from Wallonia and is a must-visit destination for its museums, architecture, and food scene.
  • Bruges: An enchanting medieval town famous for its canals, chocolate shops, and picturesque scenery.
  • Amsterdam: One of Europe’s most charming and vibrant cities, with stunning architecture and a rich cultural scene.

Conclusion

Wallonia is one of Belgium’s hidden gems, with a range of attractions, activities, and natural wonders. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine, Wallonia is a destination that appeals to all types of travelers. We hope this guide has inspired you to explore the region and discover the beauty and charm of Wallonia.
