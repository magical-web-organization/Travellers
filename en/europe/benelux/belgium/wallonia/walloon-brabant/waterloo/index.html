Introduction to Waterloo

Waterloo is a charming town located in the province of Walloon Brabant, in Wallonia, Belgium. It is famous for the Battle of Waterloo where Napoleon Bonaparte was defeated which changed the course of history. The town attracts thousands of tourists each year with its rich historical significance, natural beauty, and cultural diversity.

List of Places to Visit in Waterloo

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Waterloo has something to offer you. Here are some of the top attractions and places to visit in Waterloo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lion’s Mound: This historical site is the iconic place where the Battle of Waterloo was fought. It is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and military enthusiasts. You can climb to the top of the mound and enjoy the panoramic view of the battlefield.
  • Wellington Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life of Arthur Wellesley, the Duke of Wellington, who led the British forces and defeated Napoleon Bonaparte. You can explore the permanent exhibition and learn about the events leading up to the battle and the aftermath.
  • Church of Saint Joseph: This beautiful church is located in the heart of Waterloo and is known for its neo-Gothic architecture. It is a peaceful and serene place to visit and offers a glimpse into the town’s religious history.

Natural Attractions of Waterloo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sonian Forest: This vast forest is located on the outskirts of Waterloo and is perfect for hiking, biking, and picnicking. It is home to a variety of flora and fauna and offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the town.
  • Lake Genval: This picturesque lake is located in the heart of Genval and is famous for its natural beauty. You can take a stroll around the lake, go fishing, or enjoy a picnic with your loved ones.
  • Waterloo Greenway: This peaceful walking trail is perfect for taking a leisurely stroll and enjoying the fresh air. It offers beautiful views of the countryside and the historic town of Waterloo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterloo

Waterloo offers plenty of hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails you can explore:

  • The Waterloo Battlefield Trail: This 4-mile trail is perfect for history lovers and takes you through the famous battlegrounds where the Battle of Waterloo was fought.
  • The Genval Lake Trail: This 3-mile trail takes you around the beautiful Genval Lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Sonian Forest Trail: This 5-mile trail takes you through the heart of the Sonian Forest and is perfect for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Waterloo: Activities and Tours

Waterloo offers a plethora of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Waterloo:

  • Take a guided tour of the Battlefields: This guided tour takes you through the famous battlegrounds of Waterloo and offers an in-depth look into the events leading up to the battle and the aftermath.
  • Visit the Hergé Museum: This museum is dedicated to the famous Belgian comic strip artist Hergé, who created the character Tintin. You can explore the permanent and temporary exhibitions and learn about the history of comics in Belgium.
  • Go shopping in Waterloo: The town has a variety of boutiques and shops selling local souvenirs, clothes, and accessories. You can explore the shopping streets and find a unique gift for your loved ones.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterloo

Waterloo has a diverse and vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carbonnade flamande: This is a traditional Belgian beef stew prepared with beer and onion.
  • Moules-frites: This is a quintessential Belgian dish that consists of mussels cooked in white wine served with fries.
  • Waterzooi: This is a creamy soup prepared with chicken or fish and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beer: Belgium is famous for its variety of beers, including Trappist beers, fruit beers, and lambic beers. You can find a variety of local beers in Waterloo.
  • Jenever: This is a traditional Belgian gin made with juniper berries.
  • Wine: The town is surrounded by vineyards, and you can enjoy delicious local wines with your meal.

Where to Stay in Waterloo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Martin’s Grand Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in a historic building and offers elegant rooms and suites with modern amenities.
  • Ibis Brussels Waterloo: This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of Waterloo and offers comfortable rooms and a buffet breakfast.
  • B&B La Maison de Beaurain: This charming guesthouse is located in the countryside and offers cozy rooms and a traditional Belgian breakfast.

What to Buy in Waterloo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Waterloo, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products you can buy:

  • Belgian chocolates: Belgium is famous for its high-quality chocolates, and you can find a variety of chocolate shops in Waterloo selling handmade chocolates.
  • Lace products: Waterloo is home to several lace manufacturers, and you can find a variety of lace products, including clothing, tablecloths, and doilies.
  • Local beers: You can buy a variety of local beers from the breweries in Waterloo and the surrounding areas.

Local Culture and Customs of Waterloo

Waterloo has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals take pride in their traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Respect the local customs: Waterloo has a conservative culture, and it is important to respect local customs, including dress codes and social etiquette.
  • Learn basic French: Although most locals speak English, it is always appreciated if you try to speak some French.
  • Be courteous and polite: Belgians are known for their courteous and polite behavior, and it is important to maintain a respectful attitude while interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Waterloo

The best time to visit Waterloo is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. However, if you want to avoid crowds, you can visit in the shoulder seasons, from April to May or September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waterloo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Waterloo:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Waterloo is home to several excellent restaurants that offer local and international cuisine.
  • Respect the local customs: Be respectful of local traditions and customs and follow social etiquette.
  • Carry cash: Many small businesses in Waterloo do not accept credit cards, so it is advisable to carry cash with you.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Waterloo is a clean and beautiful town, and it is important to keep it that way by not littering.
  • Be loud and obnoxious: Belgians prefer a calm and peaceful atmosphere, and it is important to avoid being loud and obnoxious in public places.
  • Ignore the historical significance: Waterloo is famous for the Battle of Waterloo, and it is important to acknowledge and respect the town’s historical significance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waterloo

Waterloo is a safe town, and the crime rate is relatively low. However, you should take standard precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe, avoiding unlit areas at night, and being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Waterloo

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in Waterloo and nearby areas. However, it is always advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Waterloo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Waterloo

The nearest airport to Waterloo is Brussels Airport, which is located approximately 30 minutes away by car. You can also take a train or bus from the airport to the Waterloo train station.

Bus Stations Near Waterloo

There are several bus stations in Waterloo, and you can take a bus to nearby towns and cities. The main bus station is the Waterloo Bus Station located in the heart of the town.

Other Options

You can also reach Waterloo by train, taxi, or car rental. The town is well-connected to nearby towns and cities via a network of highways and roads.

How to Get Around in Waterloo

Waterloo is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot or by bike. You can also take a bus or taxi if you need to travel to nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Waterloo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waterloo is located in a beautiful region of Belgium, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some recommended places to visit:

  • Brussels: The capital city of Belgium is located just 20 minutes away by train from Waterloo. You can explore the historic city center, visit museums and galleries, and enjoy the local cuisine.
  • Leuven: This quaint university town is located approximately 30 minutes away by train from Waterloo. It is famous for its beautiful architecture, lively nightlife, and local breweries.
  • Antwerp: This historic port city is located approximately 1 hour away by car from Waterloo. It is famous for its stunning architecture, diamonds, and museums.

Conclusion

Waterloo is a beautiful town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you want to explore the famous battlegrounds, go hiking in the Sonian Forest, or feast on Belgian chocolates, Waterloo has something to offer you. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore this charming town in Belgium.
