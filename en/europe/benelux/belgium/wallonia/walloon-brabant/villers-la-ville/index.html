Introduction to Villers-la-Ville

Nestled in the heart of Walloon Brabant, in Wallonia, Belgium, Villers-la-Ville is a charming town renowned for its rich history, stunning architecture, and beautiful natural scenery. The town is situated in the Benelux region, making it easily accessible to visitors from across Europe.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing escape, Villers-la-Ville has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do in the town, as well as provide helpful tips for your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Villers-la-Ville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Villers-la-Ville is steeped in history, with several cultural attractions that offer unique insights into the town’s past. Here are some must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Villers Abbey: This magnificent abbey was founded in the 12th century and is one of the most popular attractions in the town. The abbey boasts stunning Gothic architecture and is surrounded by beautiful gardens.

  • Museum of Walloon Life: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the life and culture of the Walloon region. It features exhibits on local folklore, crafts, and traditions, as well as a collection of historic artifacts.

  • Château de La Hulpe: Located just outside Villers-la-Ville, this elegant castle dates back to the 19th century and is surrounded by picturesque gardens. It now houses a modern art museum with works by renowned artists such as Magritte and Rodin.

Natural Attractions of Villers-la-Ville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Villers-la-Ville won’t disappoint. From hiking trails to stunning gardens, there are plenty of natural attractions to explore in this charming town. Some of the top outdoor attractions include:

  • La Rocq: This ancient fortress is located on a hilltop overlooking Villers-la-Ville, offering panoramic views of the town and its surroundings.

  • Bérines Park: This beautiful park features winding paths, serene lakes, and a variety of flora and fauna. It’s the perfect spot for a peaceful picnic or leisurely stroll.

  • Villers Woods: For those looking for a more challenging hike, Villers Woods offers several trails through dense forest and rugged terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Villers-la-Ville

Villers-la-Ville is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several scenic routes through the town’s picturesque countryside. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • The Abbey Route: This 9km route takes you through the heart of Villers-la-Ville and its historic abbey, offering stunning views of the town and its surroundings.

  • The Cistercian Route: This 20km route takes you through the charming villages and beautiful countryside surrounding Villers-la-Ville, as well as various historic Cistercian sites.

  • The Battle of Waterloo Route: This 30km route takes you through the key battlegrounds of the historic Battle of Waterloo, which took place just a few miles from Villers-la-Ville.

Best Things to Do in Villers-la-Ville: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s historic and natural attractions, there are several other activities and tours to enjoy in Villers-la-Ville. Here are some top things to do:

  • Brewery Tour: Villers-la-Ville is home to several local breweries, and a brewery tour is a must for any beer lover.

  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through Villers Woods, taking in the stunning natural scenery as you go.

  • Vineyard Tour: The town’s vineyards provide some of the finest wines in Belgium, and a vineyard tour is the perfect way to learn more about the region’s wine-making history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Villers-la-Ville

If you’re planning to spend a week in Villers-la-Ville, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Visit the Villers Abbey and explore the town’s historic center.

  • Day 2: Take a brewery tour and sample some of the town’s famous beers.

  • Day 3: Enjoy a scenic horseback ride through Villers Woods.

  • Day 4: Take a vineyard tour and learn more about the region’s wine-making history.

  • Day 5: Explore the surrounding countryside on the Cistercian Route.

  • Day 6: Take a trip to the Château de La Hulpe and enjoy the beautiful gardens and modern art museum.

  • Day 7: Relax and soak up the town’s charming atmosphere before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Villers-la-Ville

Villers-la-Ville is a food lover’s paradise, with several delicious local dishes and refreshing beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carbonnade Flamande: This hearty beef stew is a Belgian classic, made with beer and served with bread or fries.

  • Waterzooi: This creamy fish stew is a specialty of the Flemish region and is usually served with potatoes or rice.

  • Liège Waffles: These crispy, sugar-dusted waffles are a sweet treat and a favorite of locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Villers-la-Ville is home to several local breweries, producing some of the finest beers in Belgium.

  • Wine: The town’s vineyards produce a variety of red, white, and sparkling wines, perfect for pairing with local cuisine.

  • Jenever: This traditional Belgian gin is made with juniper berries and other botanicals, and is a must-try for any spirit lover.

Where to Stay in Villers-la-Ville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Villers-la-Ville offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Some top options include:

  • Hôtel Restaurant Nivelles Sud Van Der Valk: This modern hotel is located just a few miles from Villers-la-Ville and offers spacious rooms, a gourmet restaurant, and a range of amenities.

  • Guesthouse Les Chambres d’à Côté: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Villers-la-Ville and features tastefully decorated rooms and a lovely garden terrace.

  • Hostellerie du Mont-Aimé: This elegant hotel is situated in the town’s historic center and offers luxurious rooms, a gourmet restaurant, and a cozy bar.

What to Buy in Villers-la-Ville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Villers-la-Ville, here are some top choices:

  • Abbaye de Villers Products: The abbey produces a range of products, from soaps and candles to beer and wine, all made using traditional methods and local ingredients.

  • Artisanal Crafts: Villers-la-Ville has a thriving artisanal scene, with several workshops and stores selling handmade ceramics, jewelry, and other crafts.

  • Local Foods: From artisanal cheeses and cured meats to homemade jams and preserves, there are plenty of delicious local foods to bring home from Villers-la-Ville.

Local Culture and Customs of Villers-la-Ville

Villers-la-Ville has a rich cultural heritage, with several customs and traditions that reflect its history and local identity. Some notable customs and traditions include:

  • La Ducasse: This annual festival celebrates the town’s patron saint and features a range of processions, parades, and performances.

  • Walloon Language: The local dialect of Walloon is still spoken by many residents and is an important part of the town’s cultural heritage.

  • Folklore and Traditions: Villers-la-Ville has a rich folklore and tradition, with several annual events and customs that reflect the town’s historic roots.

Best Time to Visit Villers-la-Ville

The best time to visit Villers-la-Ville depends on your preference for weather and crowds. The summer months (June-August) offer the warmest temperatures and the most festivals and events, but can also be quite busy. The shoulder seasons of spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds, making them ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Getting to Villers-la-Ville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Villers-la-Ville is located in the heart of Belgium and is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options:

  • Airports Near Villers-la-Ville: The closest airport to Villers-la-Ville is Brussels Airport, which is about a 45-minute drive away. Several other airports, such as Charleroi and Liège, are also within a reasonable distance.

  • Bus Stations Near Villers-la-Ville: The town is served by several bus lines, including the TEC network. The nearest bus station is in Nivelles.

  • Other Options: If you’re traveling by car, Villers-la-Ville is easily reachable via the E411 highway. Several train stations, such as Nivelles and Ottignies, also offer connections to other major cities in Belgium and Europe.

How to Get Around in Villers-la-Ville

Villers-la-Ville is a relatively small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. Biking is also a popular option, with several rental shops in the town. If you’re planning to explore the surrounding areas, a car is your best bet.
