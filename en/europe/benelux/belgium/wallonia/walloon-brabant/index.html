Introduction to Walloon Brabant

Located in the southern region of Wallonia, Belgium, Walloon Brabant is a charming destination that is often overlooked by tourists. This idyllic region is characterized by rolling hills, charming villages, and picturesque countryside. Walloon Brabant is known for its rich history, delicious cuisine, and variety of outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an adventure seeker, Walloon Brabant has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Walloon Brabant

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Villers Abbey: This well-preserved Cistercian abbey dates back to the 12th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can explore the ruins of the abbey and enjoy a guided tour.
  • The Battle of Waterloo: This historic battlefield was the site of the famous 1815 battle between Napoleon Bonaparte and the British Army. Visitors can explore the battlefield and learn about the events that led to this pivotal moment in European history.
  • Hergé Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Georges Prosper Remi, the creator of the famous Tintin comic book series. The museum features a variety of exhibitions and interactive displays.
  • The Wellington Museum: This museum is housed in the former headquarters of the Duke of Wellington during the Battle of Waterloo. Visitors can learn about the battle and the role that the Duke of Wellington played in shaping European history.

Natural Attractions of Walloon Brabant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sonian Forest: This ancient forest stretches across three regions of Belgium and is a great place to go hiking and cycling. Visitors can explore the forest’s vast network of trails and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Genval Lake: This picturesque lake is located in the heart of Walloon Brabant and is a popular destination for swimming, boating, and fishing. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely picnic on the lake’s shores or go for an adventurous hike around the surrounding hills.
  • The Bierbais Lake: This lake is a great spot for birdwatching and nature walks. Visitors can catch a glimpse of local wildlife like herons, ducks, and kingfishers, while enjoying the lake’s peaceful setting.
  • The Lion’s Mound: This large hill located on the battlefield of Waterloo was built to commemorate the victory of the Duke of Wellington and the Allied Army over Napoleon. Visitors can climb the hill and enjoy panoramic views of the battlefield and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walloon Brabant

Walloon Brabant is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The RAVeL L147b: This 25-kilometer route takes cyclists through the heart of Walloon Brabant, passing through charming villages and picturesque countryside.
  • The Ittre to Ronquieres Canal: This 12-kilometer route follows the path of the Canal du Centre and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Villers-la-Ville to Marbais Trail: This 7-kilometer trail takes hikers through the beautiful Forêt de Soignes and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The La Hulpe to Ohain Trail: This 7-kilometer trail starts at the beautiful Château de La Hulpe and takes hikers through the scenic Genval Lake.

Best Things to Do in Walloon Brabant: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tours: Walloon Brabant is known for its delicious cuisine, and food tours offer visitors a chance to sample the region’s best dishes and learn about its culinary traditions.
  • Beer Tasting Tours: The region is also home to several renowned breweries, and beer tasting tours offer visitors a chance to sample some of Belgium’s best beers.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take in the stunning landscape of Walloon Brabant from a new perspective with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Spa Days: The region is home to several luxurious spas that offer a range of treatments and services to help visitors relax and unwind.

Week-Long Itinerary for Walloon Brabant

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Walloon Brabant:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic city of Waterloo and visit the famous battlefield.
  • Day 2: Visit the Cistercian abbey at Villers-la-Ville and enjoy a guided tour.
  • Day 3: Take a bike tour through the picturesque countryside around Walloon Brabant.
  • Day 4: Spend the day relaxing at a local spa.
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic Genval Lake.
  • Day 6: Explore the Hergé Museum and the Wellington Museum.
  • Day 7: Visit one of the region’s many local breweries and enjoy a beer tasting tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Walloon Brabant

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carbonnade Flamande: A hearty beef stew made with beer, onions, and spices.
  • Waterzooi: A creamy chicken or fish soup that is a Walloon Brabant specialty.
  • Tarte Al Djote: A savory tart made with cheese, spinach, and local herbs.
  • Fondue Brabançonne: A delicious fondue made with Gouda cheese, garlic, and local beer.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian Beer: Walloon Brabant is home to several renowned breweries, offering visitors a chance to sample some of Belgium’s best beers.
  • Wine: The region is also known for its production of high-quality wines.

Where to Stay in Walloon Brabant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Château de Limelette: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Walloon Brabant and offers spacious rooms and a wide range of amenities.
  • Hôtel Piano 2: This charming hotel is located in the historic city of Waterloo and offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Le Manoir de la Mancha: This guesthouse is located in a beautifully restored 18th-century mansion and offers guests a chance to experience the region’s rich history and culture.

What to Buy in Walloon Brabant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Walloon Brabant

Walloon Brabant is a proud region with a rich history and unique cultural traditions. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • The Walloon Language: While French is the most common language spoken in Walloon Brabant, many locals speak Walloon, a Romance language unique to the region.
  • Carnival Celebrations: Walloon Brabant is known for its lively carnival celebrations, which take place every year in February or March.
  • Gastronomy: The region’s cuisine is a point of pride, and locals are passionate about their food and drink traditions.

Best Time to Visit Walloon Brabant

The best time to visit Walloon Brabant is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walloon Brabant

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Walloon Brabant is known for its delicious food and drink, so make sure to sample the local specialties.
  • Visit the Local Breweries: The region is home to several renowned breweries, offering visitors a chance to sample some of Belgium’s best beers.

Don’t:

  • Forget to Tip: Tipping is not mandatory in Walloon Brabant but is appreciated for good service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walloon Brabant

Walloon Brabant is a safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions such as staying aware of their surroundings and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Walloon Brabant

Belgium has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect quality medical care if needed. However, it is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Walloon Brabant: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Walloon Brabant: The nearest airports to Walloon Brabant are Brussels Airport and Brussels South Charleroi Airport.
  • Bus Stations Near Walloon Brabant: Walloon Brabant is well-connected by bus, with several bus stations located throughout the region.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also take a train to Brussels and then connect to Walloon Brabant by bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Walloon Brabant

The best way to get around Walloon Brabant is by car, as many of the region’s attractions are spread out and not well-served by public transportation. However, there are also bus and train services available to help visitors get around.

Where to Go Next from Walloon Brabant: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Walloon Brabant is a hidden gem in the heart of Belgium, offering visitors a chance to explore its rich history, delicious cuisine, and beautiful landscapes. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an adventure seeker, this beautiful region has something for everyone.
