Namur (Province) Travel Guide

Namur (province) is located in Wallonia, Belgium. It is a province that lies in the southern part of Belgium, in the region known as Benelux, and is one of the Wallonia’s five provinces. There are many reasons why Namur (Province) is worth visiting, from its rich cultural attractions and scenic landscapes to its well-preserved history. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your next adventure to Namur (Province) and make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Namur (Province)

Namur (province) is known for its picturesque villages and green valleys, as well as its medieval castles and magnificent fortresses. Located in the heart of the country, Namur (Province) is a popular destination for visitors seeking to soak in the ancient splendours of Wallonia. The official languages in Namur (Province) are French and German, and the currency used here is the Euro.

List of Places to Visit in Namur (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Namur (province) is dotted with many historical sites and museums that are worth exploring. The most popular among these are:

  • Waterloo
  • Citadel of Namur
  • Félicien Rops Museum
  • Floreffe Abbey
  • Han-sur-Lesse Caves

Natural Attractions of Namur (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its rich cultural heritage, Namur (Province) also boasts beautiful natural landscapes that are a must-visit for any adventure enthusiast. Here are some of the most spectacular natural attractions and outdoor activities in the area:

  • Meuse Valley
  • Ardennes Mountains
  • Dinant Rocks
  • Lesse Valley
  • Molignée Valley

Hiking/Biking Routes of Namur (Province)

Namur (Province) has a great network of hiking and biking trails that take you through some of the most breathtakingly beautiful landscapes in the country. The area around the Meuse River provides ample opportunity for hiking, while the Ardennes Mountains offer many mountain biking routes that cater to beginners as well as seasoned riders.

Best Things to Do in Namur (Province): Activities and Tours

Apart from its cultural and natural attractions, Namur (Province) offers visitors many unique experiences and activities. Here are some of the top things to do in the area:

  • Visit the Namur Green Market
  • Take a ride on the Namur Tea Train
  • Go on a Beer Tasting Tour
  • Try Adventure Sports like Kayaking and Paragliding

Week-Long Itinerary for Namur (Province)

If you have a week to spend in Namur (Province), here is an itinerary you can follow:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Citadel of Namur, Namur Cathedral, and the Félicien Rops Museum.
Day 2 Take a hike in the Ardennes Mountains or go on a mountain biking tour.
Day 3 Visit the picturesque towns of Dinant and Celles.
Day 4 Go on a boating tour of the Meuse River or visit the Han-sur-Lesse Caves.
Day 5 Explore the Floreffe Abbey and the Chateau de Vêves.
Day 6 Take a beer tasting tour of the local breweries or go on a kayaking adventure on the Lesse River.
Day 7 Spend a day exploring the Meuse Valley and its many scenic attractions.

  • Carbonade flamande
  • Moules-frites

  • Stoemp and sausages
  • Tarte au sucre

Local Culture and Customs of Namur (Province)

Like the rest of Belgium, Namur (Province) has a rich and diverse cultural heritage. Its local customs and festivals are a reflection of its history and traditions. If you’re visiting Namur (Province) during the festive season, here are some of the most popular local events you can attend:

  • The Namur Wine Festival
  • The Dinant Jazz Festival
  • The Belgian National Day celebrations
  • The Belgian Beer Weekend
  • The Nismes Book and Literature Festival

Best Time to Visit Namur (Province)

The best time to visit Namur (Province) is during the summer months, between June and August. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is lush and green. However, if you’re interested in winter activities like skiing or snowboarding, then December to February is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Namur (Province)

Do:

  • Try the local beer and cuisine.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Visit museums and historical sites to learn about the area’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Cut in line or disrespect others’ personal space.
  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Namur (Province)

Namur (Province) is generally a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate and a helpful police force. However, as with any destination, visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping their valuables secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Namur (Province)

Namur (Province) has a quality healthcare system, and visitors should have no trouble finding medical help if needed. It is advisable to purchase travel insurance before leaving for your trip.

Getting to Namur (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Namur (Province) is easy, thanks to its central location and good transport connections. Below are some of the most popular ways to get to Namur (Province):

Airports Near Namur (Province)

The Brussels South Charleroi Airport and the Brussels Airport are the primary airports serving Namur (Province). From either airport, visitors can take a train to Namur (Province), which takes around an hour.

Bus Stations Near Namur (Province)

The Namur Bus Station is located in the heart of the city, and offers connections to other parts of Belgium and nearby countries.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Namur (Province) by car or train. The area is well-connected by road and rail, and the Belgian rail system is efficient and affordable.

How to Get Around in Namur (Province)

The best way to get around Namur (Province) is by car or bike. If you’re relying on public transportation, buses and trains are available, but service can be infrequent in some areas.

Where to Go Next from Namur (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Namur (Province) is a hidden gem in the heart of Belgium, with a rich cultural heritage and magnificent natural scenery.
