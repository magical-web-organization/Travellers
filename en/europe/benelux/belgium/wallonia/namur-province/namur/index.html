Introduction to Namur

Namur is a small, charming city situated in the Wallonia region of Belgium. It is the capital of the Namur Province and boasts a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. The city is known for its stunning architecture, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant local life.

List of Places to Visit in Namur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Citadel of Namur

The Citadel of Namur is a fortress that sits atop a hill overlooking the city. It has played a significant role in Belgian history, serving as both a defensive structure and a garrison. Today, visitors can tour the citadel, take in the breathtaking views of the city and surrounding area, and learn about the citadel’s history in the onsite museum.

2. Félicien Rops Museum

The Félicien Rops Museum is dedicated to the life and works of Belgian artist Félicien Rops. It houses an extensive collection of his paintings, drawings, and prints from the 19th century. The museum also hosts exhibitions that explore the life and work of other artists, writers, and poets related to Rops and his time.

3. Saint-Aubin’s Cathedral

Saint-Aubin’s Cathedral is a stunning example of Gothic architecture and stands in the heart of Namur’s old town. Visitors can admire the cathedral’s intricate details and steeples, and explore its interior to see its beautiful stained-glass windows, sculptures, and other precious objects.

Natural Attractions of Namur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Meuse River

The Meuse River flows through Namur and is an excellent place for visitors to take a stroll, enjoy a picnic, or take a boat ride to admire views of the city and surrounding landscape.

2. The Evasion Forest

The Evasion Forest is a vast green space located in the heart of Namur. It is a perfect place for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts to hike, bike, or simply take a peaceful walk.

3. Jardin Félicien Rops

The Jardin Félicien Rops is a beautiful garden located in the heart of Namur. Visitors can explore its lush gardens, picturesque paths, and admire the many plant varieties on display.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Namur

Namur offers some of Belgium’s best hiking and biking trails, from challenging uphill climbs to scenic flat stretches that wind through the city and surrounding countryside. Some popular options include:

  • La Voie des Pierres et des Légendes: a 39 km trail that takes hikers and cyclists through the scenic countryside surrounding Namur, passing by historic rock formations and various local landmarks.
  • RAVeL biking trail: a 1,350 km trail that runs throughout Wallonia, including a section that passes through Namur. The trail offers scenic views of the countryside and many opportunities to stop for food, rest, and sightseeing.

Best Things to Do in Namur: Activities and Tours

1. Beer Tasting Tour

Belgium is famous for its beer, and Namur is no exception. Visitors can sign up for a beer tasting tour of some of the city’s best breweries and learn about the brewing process and history of beer in the region.

2. Boat Tours

Boat tours along the Meuse River provide visitors with stunning views of Namur’s skyline and surrounding countryside. Some boat tours offer meals and live music, making for an enjoyable and relaxing experience.

3. Guided Walking Tours

Guided walking tours provide visitors with an excellent opportunity to learn about the city’s rich history and culture. Tour guides lead groups through the city’s historic neighborhoods, pointing out notable landmarks and sharing stories and facts about Namur’s past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Namur

Day 1: Citadel and Old Town

Explore the Citadel of Namur and its onsite museum, then spend the rest of the day wandering through the charming old town, admiring the stunning architecture and local shops and cafes.

Day 2: Félicien Rops Museum and Saint-Aubin’s Cathedral

Visit the Félicien Rops Museum to admire its collection of art and learn about the life and work of the famous Belgian artist. Afterward, take a stroll to Saint-Aubin’s Cathedral to view its magnificent Gothic architecture.

Day 3: Meuse River and Evasion Forest

Take a stroll along the Meuse River, then head to the Evasion Forest for a scenic hike or bike ride.

Day 4: Beer Tasting Tour

Take a beer tasting tour of the city’s breweries, sampling some of Belgium’s best beers and learning about the history of beer in the region.

Day 5: Boat Tour

Take a boat tour along the Meuse River to admire the stunning views of Namur’s skyline and surrounding countryside.

Day 6: Guided Walking Tour

Take a guided walking tour of the city’s historic neighborhoods, learning about Namur’s rich history and culture.

Day 7: Jardin Felicien Rops and Where to Buy Souvenirs

Visit the beautiful Jardin Felicien Rops, then spend the rest of the day shopping for souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Food and Drink Scene of Namur

Must-Try Dishes

1. Boulets à la Liégeoise

Boulets à la Liégeoise is a traditional Belgian dish consisting of meatballs cooked in a sweet and savory sauce with onions and spices.

2. Paling in ‘t Groen

Paling in ‘t Groen is a dish made with eel cooked in a green sauce made of herbs and vegetables.

3. Frites

Belgium is known for its delicious French fries, which are often served with a variety of sauces and toppings.

Local Beverages

1. Belgian Beer

Belgium is famous for its beer, and Namur is no exception. Visitors can try a variety of local brews, from light pilsners to rich stouts.

2. Jenever

Jenever is a traditional Belgian liquor made with juniper berries. It is often served as an aperitif or a digestif.

Where to Stay in Namur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Namur offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

1. Les Tanneurs

Les Tanneurs is a historic hotel located in the heart of Namur’s old town. It boasts stylish rooms, a spa, and a Michelin-starred restaurant.

2. Auberge de Jeunesse Namur

Auberge de Jeunesse Namur is a budget-friendly hostel located just a few minutes from Namur’s city center. It offers dorm-style rooms and private rooms, as well as shared kitchen and lounge areas.

3. Bed & Breakfast Le Petit Atelier du Marché

Bed & Breakfast Le Petit Atelier du Marché is a charming guesthouse situated near the city center of Namur. It offers comfortable rooms, a shared lounge area, and a lovely garden where guests can relax.

What to Buy in Namur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Chocolate

Belgium is famous for its delicious chocolate, and Namur is no exception. Visitors can bring home a variety of locally-made chocolates.

2. Lace

Lace is a traditional craft in Belgium, and Namur is home to many skilled lace makers. Visitors can purchase a variety of lace items, from decorative tablecloths to delicate lingerie.

3. Beer

Belgium is famous for its beer, and Namur offers a variety of locally-made brews that visitors can bring home to enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Namur

Namur is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors are greeted with warmth and hospitality. The city has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take pride in their history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Namur

The best time to visit Namur is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city’s parks and gardens are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Namur

Do:

  • Do try some of Belgium’s famous beers and chocolates.
  • Do explore the city’s historic neighborhoods and landmarks.
  • Do try some of the local cuisine, including Boulets à la Liégeoise and Paling in ‘t Groen.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to try and speak some basic French or Dutch. Locals appreciate the effort.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes. Namur is a city meant to be explored on foot.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers at restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Namur

Namur is generally a safe city, and visitors should not encounter any major safety issues. However, visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings when exploring the city.

Health in Namur

Belgium offers a high standard of healthcare, and visitors should not encounter any significant health issues in Namur. Visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance before visiting the city.

Getting to Namur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Namur is well-connected to other major European cities and can be easily accessed by plane, train, or car.

Airports Near Namur

The nearest airport to Namur is Brussels Airport (BRU), located approximately 60 km away. Some visitors may also choose to fly into Charleroi Airport (CRL), located approximately 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near Namur

Namur is well-connected to other Belgian cities by bus, with several buses arriving and departing daily from the city’s bus station.

Other Options

Namur can also be accessed by car or train, with several major highways connecting the city to other parts of Belgium and Europe.

How to Get Around in Namur

Namur is a compact city and can easily be explored on foot. However, visitors who prefer other transportation options can also choose to take a bus or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Namur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other charming Belgian cities nearby, such as Brussels and Liege, or take a day trip to the beautiful Ardennes region.

Conclusion

Namur is a charming and picturesque city with a rich history and culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring its many landmarks and museums, hiking or biking through its scenic countryside, or enjoying its delicious cuisine and local beer, Namur has something to offer everyone.
