Introduction to Dinant

Nestled in the picturesque province of Namur in Wallonia, the French-speaking part of Belgium, is the charming town of Dinant. Situated on the banks of the Meuse river, this stunning town is known for its dramatic Citadel, gothic architecture and stunning natural beauty. Dinant also offers a diverse range of activities, making it a perfect destination for both history buffs and nature-lovers alike.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide insight into the best places to visit, activities to do, food to eat, and things to buy in Dinant. We will also share cultural tips and information about transportation, safety, and health.

List of Places to Visit in Dinant

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Citadel of Dinant

The Citadel of Dinant, situated high on a cliff, is a must-see for history buffs or anyone interested in military history. This 19th-century fortress was once a strategic location for protecting the Meuse Valley. Today, visitors can take a funicular to the top of the cliff and explore the museum, which showcases medieval weapons and armor, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

The Collegiate Church of Notre-Dame

The Collegiate Church of Notre-Dame dominates Dinant’s skyline with its striking gothic architecture. This imposing 13th-century church has played an essential role in Dinant’s history and has survived multiple wars and invasions.

Maison Leffe

Maison Leffe is a must-visit for beer lovers. This interactive museum offers visitors an insight into the brewing process and history of the world-famous Leffe beer. Visitors can also taste different Leffe varieties and learn how to pour the perfect pint of beer.

Natural Attractions of Dinant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Meuse River

The Meuse River is the centerpiece of Dinant and is an excellent place for visitors to relax and soak up the scenery. Visitors can take a boat ride, rent kayaks or paddleboats, or simply enjoy a leisurely walk along the riverbanks.

The Grotte La Merveilleuse

The Grotte La Merveilleuse is a stunning cave system with numerous chambers filled with magnificent stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors can take a guided tour of the cave system and marvel at the natural wonders.

The Abbey of Leffe

The Abbey of Leffe is situated on the outskirts of Dinant, offering visitors a beautiful and peaceful place to unwind. The abbey was founded in the 12th century and is known for its exceptional beer and cheese production.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dinant

Dinant is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. The stunning natural scenery and miles of hiking and biking trails make it a popular destination for adventure-seekers.

Some famous biking and hiking routes in Dinant are:

The Lesse River Valley

The Lesse River Valley offers visitors an excellent opportunity to hike, bike, or canoe through the breathtaking Ardennes landscape. This route takes visitors through dense forests, rocky riverbeds, and stunning waterfalls.

The RAVeL Route

The RAVeL Route is a must-visit for cycling enthusiasts and stretches from Dinant to the Belgian-Luxembourg border. This 130km trail is beautiful and challenging, running through numerous scenic towns and villages.

Best Things to Do in Dinant: Activities and Tours

Dinant Evasion

Dinant Evasion offers unique tours for visitors, which include kayaking in the Lesse River Valley, hiking in the Ardennes Mountains, and visiting the historic city center.

Dinant Adventures

Dinant Adventures offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including rock climbing, zip-lining, and archery. It’s the perfect option for anyone looking for an adrenaline rush.

Dinant Jazz Festival

Music lovers should visit Dinant during the Dinant Jazz Festival. This popular annual event attracts thousands of visitors every year and showcases some of the best jazz talent from around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dinant

A week in Dinant is enough time to explore all the town has to offer. This itinerary includes the most famous attractions of Dinant and its surroundings.

Day 1: Explore Dinant’s City Centre Day 2: Visit the Citadel of Dinant and the Collegiate Church of Notre-Dame Day 3: Visit the Grotte La Merveilleuse Day 4: Take a biking tour of Lesse River Valley Day 5: Visit the Abbey of Leffe Day 6: Go kayaking in the Lesse River Valley Day 7: Relax in Dinant’s cafes and restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Dinant

Dinant has a diverse and exciting food and drink scene, offering visitors a chance to taste unique and delicious local dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

Couque de Dinant

Couque de Dinant is a hard, sweet biscuit, made using local honey and flour. It is a perfect snack for visitors while exploring the town.

Flamiche

Flamiche is a savory tart made with leeks and eggs. This dish is exceptionally light and perfect for a snack or light meal.

Waterzooi

Waterzooi is a delicious Flemish stew made with chicken or fish. It is typically served with potatoes and is a traditional Belgian dish.

Local Beverages

Leffe Beer

Leffe beer is one of the most famous Belgian beers, and the Brewery Maison Leffe is located in Dinant. Visitors can taste different varieties and learn all about the history and brewing process at the brewery.

Peket

Peket is a traditional Belgian liquor made from juniper berries. It is enjoyed as an after-dinner drink and is quite potent.

Where to Stay in Dinant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dinant has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

Les Terasses de la Citadelle

Les Terasses de la Citadelle is a beautiful hotel located close to the Citadel and town center. It offers stunning views of the river and town and is perfect for visitors looking for accommodation with character and history.

Youth Hostel de Dinant

Youth Hostel de Dinant is a budget-friendly option for travelers with a limited budget. The hostel is located in 19th-century building and offers free Wi-Fi and a continental breakfast.

Citadelle Guesthouse

Citadelle Guesthouse is situated near the Citadel of Dinant and offers visitors comfortable rooms with contemporary furnishings. The guesthouse has a terrace and garden area perfect for relaxing.

What to Buy in Dinant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home some of the traditional and locally made products of Dinant

Couque de Dinant

Couque de Dinant biscuits are a perfect souvenir to bring back home. They are durable, sweet, and available in many different flavors and styles.

Leffe Beer

Leffe beer is a perfect gift for any beer lover and is easily available at Maison Leffe or any other local store.

Local Culture and Customs of Dinant

Dinant’s rich history and location have given it a unique cultural identity. The town is famous for its music, traditional crafts, and cuisine.

La Descente de la Lesse

La Descente de la Lesse is an annual event that takes place in August. It is a 12km kayak trip down the Lesse River and is one of the most popular events in Dinant.

Best Time to Visit Dinant

The ideal time to visit Dinant is between April and October when the weather is mild and favorable. The town is at its busiest in August when it hosts La Descente de la Lesse event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dinant

Do:

  • Try the local beer and cuisine.
  • Respect the cultural traditions and customs of Dinant.
  • Explore the town and surroundings on foot or bike.

Don’t:

  • Litter on streets or riverbanks.
  • Touch or remove parts of historical buildings or monuments.
  • Disturb the wildlife in natural settings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dinant

Dinant is a very safe town with a minimal crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise caution when traveling and avoid walking alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Dinant

Belgium has a well-developed health care system, and medical facilities are available throughout the country. However, visitors should consider purchasing travel insurance before embarking on their trip to Belgium.

Getting to Dinant: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dinant

The nearest airport is Brussels Airport, which serves domestic and international destinations. From Brussels airport, visitors can take the train to Dinant or rent a car.

Bus Stations Near Dinant

The Dinant bus station offers a wide network of bus routes throughout the town and surrounding areas.

Other Options

The best way to get to Dinant is by train. The town is well-connected to other parts of Belgium and Europe, with frequent trains to and from Brussels, Antwerp, and Luxembourg.

How to Get Around in Dinant

Dinant is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several bus routes available that run throughout the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Dinant: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Namur

Namur, the capital of Wallonia, is a short 30-minute drive from Dinant. It is home to many cultural landmarks, museums, and galleries.

Charleroi

Charleroi, also 30 minutes away from Dinant, is a vibrant industrial town known for its beautiful architecture and excellent food scene.

Conclusion

Dinant is an absolutely charming town that offers visitors a fantastic mix of natural beauty, history, and culture. From the stunning Citadel overlooking the Meuse river to the delicious local cuisine, Dinant is sure to exceed your expectations. We hope that this travel guide provides all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Dinant.
