Gembloux Travel Guide - Discovering the Hidden Gems of Namur, Belgium

Are you planning to visit Belgium and looking for a hidden gem to explore? Look no further than Gembloux! This charming town in Namur province, in the Wallonia region, features a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the must-visit places, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and everything else you need to know to make the most of your trip to Gembloux.

Introduction to Gembloux

Gembloux is a small town located in the southern part of Belgium, surrounded by green pastures and rolling hills. It has a rich history dating back to the Roman era. During the Middle Ages, Gembloux was a popular pilgrimage site, and the town earned fame for its Benedictine abbey. Nowadays, Gembloux is a peaceful town with an abundance of cultural landmarks, museums, and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Gembloux

Gembloux offers a plethora of activities for visitors. Here are some of the must-visit places during your trip to Gembloux:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gembloux Abbey: The Benedictine abbey was founded in the 10th century and played a significant role in the town’s history. Nowadays, the abbey is home to a museum showcasing religious art and artifacts.
  • Church of Saint-Sauveur: This beautiful church was built in the 11th century and is a fine example of Romanesque architecture.
  • Gembloux Town Hall: The town hall dates back to the 16th century and features a remarkable clock tower.
  • Chateau de Corroy-le-Chateau: This stunning castle dates back to the 13th century and is located just a short drive from Gembloux. It’s a great place to explore medieval architecture and history.
  • Musee Lapidair: This museum is home to a vast collection of fossils and minerals from around the world, as well as a collection of local prehistoric artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Gembloux: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc d’Epinal: This beautiful park is an oasis in the heart of Gembloux. It features a lake, playground, and plenty of walking paths.
  • Bois de la Garenne: This forest is an excellent place for hiking and biking. You’ll find several trails that wind through the forest with stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Jardins de Sambrexpo: This garden is located just outside of Gembloux and features an array of plants and flowers. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gembloux

Gembloux is surrounded by picturesque countryside, making it an ideal location for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some recommended routes for hikers and bikers:

  • Bois de la Garenne trail: This seven-kilometer trail circles through the beautiful Bois de la Garenne forest.
  • Tienne aux Pierres trail: This 13-kilometer trail takes hikers through the stunning countryside surrounding Gembloux, passing through several small villages.
  • Ravel de l’Hocaille trail: This 30-kilometer trail follows the old railway track and takes bikers through the rolling hills and picturesque Belgian countryside.

Best Things to Do in Gembloux: Activities and Tours

  • Beer Tasting Tour: Belgium is famous for its beer, and Gembloux is no exception. Take a beer tasting tour and learn about the different brewing methods and tastes.
  • Cheese Tasting Tour: Belgium is also known for its variety of cheeses. Try a cheese tasting tour and sample some of the best cheeses available.
  • Botanical Tour: Gembloux is renowned for its botanical gardens and plant science. Take a botanical tour and discover the fascinating world of plants.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Gembloux, here’s a recommended itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Gembloux

Belgium is known for its cuisine, from moules-frites to waffles. Gembloux has its unique regional cuisine that’s worth trying during your visit. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gembloux Waffles: These waffles are made with yeast instead of baking soda and are known for their airy texture.
  • La Houppe: This traditional cheese has been produced in Gembloux, since the 16th century. It has a creamy texture and a sharp, tangy flavor.
  • Sirop de Liège: This sweet spread is made from a blend of apples and pears. It’s commonly used as a condiment for cheese and meats.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian Beer: Belgium is famous for its beer, and Gembloux has a long tradition of brewing. Try a traditional Belgian beer, like Dupont, which has been brewed in Gembloux since 1844.
  • Vin de Paille: This sweet dessert wine is made by drying grapes on straw mats before pressing them.

  • Hotel Les 3 Clés: This four-star hotel is located in the heart of Gembloux and offers elegant rooms and a restaurant.
  • Gembloux Youth Hostel: This budget-friendly option is located just outside of the city center and offers dormitory-style accommodation.
  • La Ferme de l’Hoste: This charming guest house is located in a renovated farmstead, just outside of Gembloux. It offers quaint rooms and a garden.

What to Buy in Gembloux: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

A trip to Gembloux wouldn’t be complete without bringing home some local souvenirs. Here are some of the best products to buy:

  • La Houppe: This traditional cheese is a great souvenir to bring home.
  • Sirop de Liège: This local sweet spread is perfect for your pantry or as a gift.
  • Gembloux Waffles: Bring home some of these delicious waffles as a sweet treat for yourself or to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Gembloux

Belgium has a rich cultural history, and Gembloux is no exception. Here are some key aspects of the local culture and customs:

  • Languages: The official languages of Wallonia are French and German, although many people in Gembloux also speak English.
  • Religion: Gembloux has a strong Catholic tradition, with several churches and religious landmarks throughout the town.
  • Greeting: It’s customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile.

Best Time to Visit Gembloux

The best time to visit Gembloux depends on your interests. The town has a different charm throughout the seasons. The summer months are perfect for outdoor activities, while the winter months offer cozy surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gembloux

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Gembloux:

Do:

  • Try the local food: Gembloux is known for its unique cuisine, and it’s worth trying during your trip.
  • Explore the outdoors: Gembloux has beautiful parks and forests to explore. Take advantage of it and enjoy the outdoors.
  • Respect the local culture: Belgium has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s fascinating to discover it during your visit to Gembloux.

Don’t:

  • Forget your camera: Gembloux has plenty of stunning scenery, so don’t forget your camera to capture those beautiful moments.
  • Lose track of time: It’s easy to get a little too relaxed in the tranquil surroundings of Gembloux. But make sure to keep track of time, so you don’t miss out on any activities.

Safety and Health

Gembloux is a safe and healthy place to visit. However, here is some key information to consider:

Safety in Gembloux

  • Street safety: Gembloux is a small town and generally safe. However, as with any tourist destination, be aware of pickpockets and keep your valuables safe.
  • Emergency services: The emergency number in Belgium is 112.

Health in Gembloux

  • Medical services: Gembloux has several medical facilities, including hospitals and pharmacies, in case of an emergency.
  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required to visit Belgium.

Gembloux is well-connected to other parts of Belgium and Europe. Here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip:

Airports Near Gembloux

  • Brussels Airport: Brussels Airport is the largest airport in Belgium and is located 45 kilometers from Gembloux.

Bus Stations Near Gembloux

  • Gembloux Bus Station: The main bus station in Gembloux offers connections throughout Wallonia.

Other Options

  • Train: Gembloux is located on the main rail line in Belgium, and there are regular trains to Brussels and other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Gembloux

Gembloux is a small town and easy to get around on foot or bike. It’s also well serviced by buses.

Where to Go Next from Gembloux: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gembloux is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Belgium. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Brussels: Explore the capital of Belgium and the European Union.
  • Dinant: Visit this charming town on the banks of the River Meuse, known for its stunning landscape.
  • Namur: This historic city is the capital of Wallonia and is known for its rich culture and landmarks.

Conclusion

Gembloux is a hidden gem in Belgium, with plenty to offer for visitors who want to explore the natural beauty, rich history, and traditional cuisine of the region. Plan your trip to Gembloux and immerse yourself in the captivating culture of this charming town.
