Introduction to Binche

Binche is a hidden gem located in the province of Hainaut, Wallonia, Belgium. This town is famous for its carnaval celebrations, which are recognised by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity. Binche also has a rich history, culture and tradition, making it an ideal destination for curious travellers.

Despite being a small town, Binche has a lot to offer to its visitors, with its beautiful countryside, historical landmarks, traditional museums, and quirky festivals. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have curated the must-visit attractions, curated hiking and biking trails, food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more. Let’s dive in.

List of Places to Visit in Binche

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Binche Belfry - a UNESCO World Heritage site, that dates back to the 14th century. This tower served as a defence system for the town, and today serves as a museum for showcasing local artefacts from the Middle Ages.
  • The Binche Castle - a former military base that has now been transformed into an art and culture centre, complete with exhibition spaces, a theatre, a cinema, and a bar.
  • The International Museum of Carnival and Mask - a unique museum dedicated to masks and costumes, showcasing over 10,000 exhibits from all over the world. This museum is located in the historic town centre, and is a must-visit attraction.

Natural Attractions of Binche: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Binche Forest - a vast forest covering over 2,000 hectares, with hiking and biking trails, horse-riding centres, and picnic spots. This forest is home to many bird species and offers a peaceful retreat for nature lovers.
  • The Thudinie Region - a picturesque countryside area with rolling hills, quaint farms, and charming villages. This region is perfect for outdoor activities, such as hiking or cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Binche

Binche offers several hiking and biking trails for nature lovers. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • The Tour of the Thudinie - a 32-kilometre cycling route through La Thudinie region, passing by the fields, farms and picturesque villages.
  • The RAVeL 109c - a 20-kilometre bike trail that follows the former railway line between Sars-la-Bruyere and Binche, offering stunning views of the Binche countryside.
  • The Binche Forest Trail - a 7-kilometre hike through the Binche Forest, showcasing its vibrant flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Binche: Activities and Tours

  • Attend the Binche Carnival - this carnival is a UNESCO Masterpiece of Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity and is celebrated during Shrove Tuesday.
  • Visit a Brewery - Binche is home to several local breweries that offer tastings and tours, such as “La Binchoise” or “Brasserie de la Senne”.
  • Explore the Countryside - take a hike or bike ride on one of the picturesque trails in the Binche Forest or the Thudinie region.
  • Take a Walking Tour - join a local guide to discover the historical landmarks and hidden gems of Binche.

Week-Long Itinerary for Binche

Here’s a potential week-long itinerary for a Binche trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Binche and settle in your accommodation. Day 2: Begin your day with a visit to the Binche Castle, followed by a walking tour of the town centre. Day 3: Enjoy a bike ride through the Thudinie countryside, concluding your day with a visit to a local brewery. Day 4: Take a day trip to Brussels, just one hour away from Binche. Day 5: Explore the Binche Forest, taking in the stunning views and wildlife. Day 6: Visit the International Museum of Carnival and Mask, followed by a picnic in the town square. Day 7: Depart from Binche with fond memories of your trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Binche

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tarte Au Maton - a local cake made from Maton cheese.
  • Pot-Au-Feu - a traditional French Beef Stew dish typically served with vegetables and potatoes.
  • Carbonnade Flamande - a Belgian beef stew, slow-cooked with dark beer and onions.

Local Beverages

  • La Binchoise - a traditional Binche beer, brewed in the local brewery.
  • Ricard - a French anise-flavoured aperitif, often served with water.
  • Vin Jaune - a yellow wine that is a speciality of the Jura region in France.

Where to Stay in Binche: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Binche offers various accommodation options to suit your needs. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel de la Couronne - located in the charming town centre, this hotel provides cosy rooms with modern amenities.
  • La petite Baraque - a cozy guesthouse situated amidst beautiful natural surroundings, providing a pool, and garden to relax in.
  • Auberge de la Louve - a charming hostel located in the Binche Forest, providing a peaceful retreat for nature lovers.

What to Buy in Binche: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Binche Carnival Masks - a colourful souvenir that serves as a reminder of the town’s famous carnival festival.
  • Marionette Puppets - traditional puppets representing Binche’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Maton Cheese - a local soft cheese made from cow’s milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Binche

Binche is famous for its carnaval celebrations every Shrove Tuesday. The carnival is characterised by its Gilles, or men dressed in medieval costumes wearing wooden masks, and traditionally throwing oranges into the crowd. This tradition has been celebrated for centuries and has been recognised by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity.

Best Time to Visit Binche

The best time to visit Binche is during the carnival festivities, which take place every Shrove Tuesday. However, for those who would like to avoid the crowds, late spring and early autumn are optimal times to visit Binche, allowing visitors to explore the town and countryside in more peaceful surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Binche

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the scenic countryside of the Thudinie region.
  • Take part in the carnival festivities.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the scenic outdoor areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Ignore the COVID-19 safety measures put in place.

Safety and Health

Safety in Binche

Binche is a safe town. However, as with any travel destination, visitors are advised to take precautions and stay vigilant, especially when in unfamiliar areas or well-lit areas at night.

Health in Binche

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and Binche has a hospital and several medical clinics, offering quality medical care to visitors.

Getting to Binche: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Binche

The nearest airport to Binche is Brussels Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Binche

There is a regular train service from Brussels to Binche, and the trip takes approximately an hour. The Binche train station is located in the town centre, allowing easy access to local attractions and amenities.

Other Options

Driving is also an option, with plenty of car rental agencies available at Brussels Airport and in the town.

How to Get Around in Binche

Binche is a small town that is easily navigable on foot, allowing visitors to explore the streets and alleyways at their own pace. Cycling is also a popular option, with several bike rental shops available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Binche: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Binche is well-located for exploring the wider Wallonia region. Here are some popular options:

  • Mons - a historical Belgian town located just 20 kilometres from Binche.
  • Pairi Daiza - a popular zoo and botanical garden located between Mons and Ath.
  • The Waterloo Memorial - a historical site located near Brussels and commemorating the famous Battle of Waterloo.

Conclusion

Binche is a charming and authentic town that offers visitors a glimpse into the traditional customs and rich cultural history of Wallonia. From its scenic countryside to its unique festival celebrations, Binche offers something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive Binche travel guide has inspired you to visit this hidden gem and plan your own memorable trip.
