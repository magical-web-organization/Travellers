Introduction to Mons

Mons, the capital of the Hainaut province of Wallonia, is a charming city in southern Belgium. It’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site that’s famous for its magnificent Baroque architecture, local handicrafts, scrumptious food, and serene surroundings. Mons is also known for its rich cultural heritage and has an array of cultural attractions to offer. But what sets it apart from other European destinations is its laid-back atmosphere, where you can simply stroll around, take in the sights, and unwind.

List of Places to Visit in Mons

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most famous places to visit in Mons is the gothic-style St. Waltrude’s Collegiate Church. It’s a magnificent building with a 19th-century spire that’s 87 meters high. You can also visit the Belfry, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and one of the most iconic symbols of Mons. It’s a 17th-century tower that’s 87 meters high and provides fantastic panoramic views of the city.

As for museums, you can explore the Mons Memorial Museum, located inside a former hospital. It highlights the wartime experiences of Mons and its surroundings. Another notable museum to visit is the Artotheque, which not only showcases contemporary art but also organizes workshops and courses for art enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Mons: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, the Parc du Waux-Hall is worth a visit. It’s a public park known for its stunning gardens, peaceful walkways, and tranquil water features. Meanwhile, the Pairi Daiza zoo is a popular attraction for families. It’s home to over 500 species of animals, including red pandas, elephants, and orangutans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mons

Mons has several hiking and biking routes, which are ideal for those who enjoy the outdoors. The RAVeL network, a regional network of walking and cycling paths, has routes that pass through Mons. The GRP Grande Traversée du Hainaut, on the other hand, is a hiking trail that traverses the Hainaut province and passes through Mons.

Best Things to Do in Mons: Activities and Tours

One of the most popular things to do in Mons is to join a walking tour of the city. It’s an excellent way to explore the old town and learn about its history. You can also join a guided tour of the gothic-style St. Waltrude’s Collegiate Church. Another option is to attend a local event or festival, such as the Doudou festival that takes places in June, known for its historical procession of Saint George.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mons

Day 1-2: Explore the historic center of Mons, visit the gothic-style St. Waltrude’s Collegiate Church, and walk up the Belfry for a panoramic view of the city.

Day 3: Visit the Mons Memorial Museum and learn about the wartime experiences of Mons and its surroundings.

Day 4: Take a stroll around the peace-loving Parc du Waux-Hall.

Day 5: Visit the Artotheque and immerse yourself in contemporary art.

Day 6: Join a guided tour of the gothic-style St. Waltrude’s Collegiate Church.

Day 7: Take a day trip to Pairi Daiza Zoo.

Food and Drink Scene of Mons

Must-Try Dishes

Mons has a vibrant food scene, and one must-try dish is the “tarte al djote,” a local pie made with spinach and cheese. Another delicacy is the “ris de veau,” sweetbreads of veal that are traditionally served with mushrooms.

Local Beverages

Belgium is famous for its beer, and Mons is no exception. You can taste the local brews at the Brasserie de la Senne Belge, a popular microbrewery in town. The local wine is also known for its quality, with the vineyards around Mons producing fantastic bottles.

Where to Stay in Mons: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you are looking for luxury, Mons has no shortage of high-end hotels. The Hotel Dream is a top-rated hotel in the city, offering a luxurious stay that’s perfect for couples. If you’re on a budget, the Auberge de Jeunesse de Mons is a well-equipped hostel that’s clean and comfortable.

What to Buy in Mons: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mons is known for its local handicrafts, such as lacework and pottery. You can buy these traditional souvenirs in one of the many shops in the city. The chocolate in Mons is also famous, and the artisan chocolate shops are a must-visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Mons

The people of Mons are friendly and approachable, and they love their city. It’s customary to greet people when you meet them, whether you know them or not. The locals enjoy a good beer and love to socialize, and you’ll often find that locals will be more than happy to chat with strangers.

Best Time to Visit Mons

The best time to visit Mons is during the spring and summer months when the weather is warm, and the parks and gardens are in bloom. The months of June and July are particularly exciting as that’s when the famous Doudou festival takes place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mons

Do:

  • Try the local delicacies, such as “tarte al djote” and “ris de veau.”
  • Explore the historic center of Mons and visit the top cultural attractions.
  • Join a guided tour to learn about the city’s culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying the local beers and wine.
  • Avoid speaking loudly in public spaces and at night, as this can disturb the peace.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mons

Mons is generally a safe city, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s always wise to take the usual precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping an eye on your valuables.

Health in Mons

Belgium has a high standard of healthcare, and Mons has several hospitals and medical centers. European citizens with a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) can avail of free or discounted medical treatment.

Getting to Mons: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mons

The closest airports to Mons are Charleroi Brussels South Airport, Brussels Airport, and Lille Airport. All three airports offer excellent connections to Mons by public transport.

Bus Stations Near Mons

Mons has a reliable and well-connected bus network that connects the city to other towns and cities in the region. You can use the TEC bus service to travel around Mons and the wider region.

Other Options

Mons is also accessible by train, with the city’s train station providing regular services to other cities in Belgium and neighboring countries.

How to Get Around in Mons

The historic center of Mons is compact and easily walkable, so walking is the best option for exploring the city center. If you want to explore further afield, the city has an efficient public transport system that includes buses and trams.

Where to Go Next from Mons: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mons is an excellent base for exploring other cities and towns in the Hainaut province, such as Tournai and Charleroi. Additionally, Brussels and Lille are just a short train ride away, making Mons an ideal location for a multi-destination trip.

Conclusion

Mons in Belgium is a charming, laid-back city that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and relaxation. From its famous landmarks to its local handicrafts, there’s something for everyone in Mons. So come and discover this fascinating destination for yourself!
