Introduction to Spiennes

Spiennes is a small village located in the province of Hainaut, in Wallonia, Belgium. As one of the most historically rich settlements in the region, Spiennes has a lot to offer to its visitors. From its ancient archaeological finds to its lush greenery and scenic views, Spiennes is a great place to explore whether you are a history buff, adventure-seeker, or a foodie.

In this travel guide, we have put together a comprehensive list of all the top things to see and do in Spiennes, where to eat and stay, and practical information to help you plan your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Spiennes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

SILEX’S Interpretive Center

Located in the heart of Spiennes, SILEX’s Interpretive Center is a must-visit for anyone interested in archaeology. The center showcases the history of the world-renowned Spiennes Neolithic flint mines which are situated just outside of the village. The center features a 3D multimedia presentation, a replica of a Neolithic workshop, and a series of artifacts and interpretive panels.

Saint Martin Church

Saint Martin Church is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Spiennes. Built in the Gothic period, the church features a stunning stained-glass window, a misericord, and a baptismal font. Its interior is adorned with several statues of saints and a beautiful wooden pulpit.

Natural Attractions of Spiennes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Neolithic Flint Mines

Located just a short walk from the center of Spiennes, the Neolithic Flint Mines are a must-visit for anyone interested in archaeology and history. The mines were included in the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2000 for their unique testimony to the prehistoric mining of flint. Visitors can take a guided tour of the mines, which includes exploring the underground galleries and learning about the mining techniques used by the Neolithic ancestors.

RAVeL Trail

The RAVeL Trail is a popular hiking and cycling path that runs through Spiennes. The trail offers scenic views of the countryside and the opportunity to explore the surrounding area at your own pace. There are several picnic areas along the way, making it the perfect place to pack a picnic lunch and enjoy the outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spiennes

Spiennes has numerous trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The RAVeL Trail, which runs through the village, is one of the most popular routes in the area. Another popular trail is the “Balade de la Silex,” a 6 km circular trail that takes you through the archaeological sites and the surrounding countryside. The trail is well-marked and suitable for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Spiennes: Activities and Tours

Neolithic Flint Mines Tour

A tour of Spiennes’ Neolithic Flint Mines is a must-do activity for anyone visiting the village. The guided tours take you deep into the underground galleries and provide insight into Neolithic mining techniques. Be prepared to get a little dirty, as the mines can be quite muddy at times.

Local Food Tasting Tour

Spiennes is famous for its local cuisine, and a food tasting tour is a great way to experience the village’s culinary delights. The tour takes you to some of the best restaurants and food markets in the area, and you get to sample local dishes such as “carbonade flamande,” a beef stew made with beer and onions, and “tarte al djote,” a savory tart made with chard and cheese.

Food and Drink Scene of Spiennes

Must-Try Dishes

Carbonade Flamande

Carbonade flamande is a traditional Flemish beef stew that is cooked in beer with onions and served with a side of fries. It is a hearty and comforting dish that is perfect for colder days.

Tarte al Djote

Tarte al djote is a savory tart that is made with chard and cheese. It is usually served as an appetizer or a light lunch and pairs well with a glass of local beer.

Local Beverages

Belgian Beer

Belgium is famous for its beer, and Spiennes is home to several breweries that produce some of the best Belgian beers. From the sweet and fruity kriek to the strong and complex Trappist beers, there is something for every taste.

Gin de Binche

Gin de Binche is a local gin that is made with a variety of herbs and botanicals. It has a smooth and slightly sweet taste and pairs well with tonic water and a slice of lime.

Where to Stay in Spiennes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spiennes has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

Hôtel Dream

Located in the heart of Spiennes, Hôtel Dream is a boutique hotel that offers comfortable and luxurious accommodation. The hotel features a restaurant, a garden, and a terrace, providing guests with a relaxing and peaceful atmosphere.

Hostel Mons

Hostel Mons is a budget-friendly option for those who want to experience Spiennes without breaking the bank. The hostel offers dormitory rooms and private rooms, and is located within walking distance of the town center.

What to Buy in Spiennes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Flint Jewelry

Flint jewelry is a unique and beautiful souvenir to bring home from Spiennes. Several shops in the village sell handmade jewelry made from flint, showcasing the rich mining heritage of the area.

Lace

Belgium is famous for its lace, and Spiennes is no exception. Several shops in the village sell handmade lace products, ranging from tablecloths and napkins to intricately woven lace curtains.

Local Culture and Customs of Spiennes

Spiennes is a small village with a rich cultural heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to explore the village and its surrounding areas at their own pace. The village is known for its artistic and musical events, and there are several festivals throughout the year that showcase the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Spiennes

The best time to visit Spiennes is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The village can get quite crowded during the peak tourist season, so it’s best to book your accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spiennes

Do:

  • Explore the Neolithic Flint Mines
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take a cycling or hiking tour of the RAVeL Trail

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage archaeological sites
  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Spiennes

Spiennes is a safe village with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and keep an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Spiennes

Spiennes has several hospitals and medical clinics in the surrounding area, in case of any medical emergencies. Visitors should ensure to have adequate travel insurance for the duration of their stay.

Getting to Spiennes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spiennes

The closest airport to Spiennes is Brussels Airport, which is located approximately 80 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a train or a taxi to Spiennes.

Bus Stations Near Spiennes

Spiennes has a local bus service that connects the village to nearby towns and cities, including Mons. The bus station is located in the town center, making it easily accessible to visitors.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Spiennes by car or by train. The village is well-connected to major highways and train lines, making it easy to get to from anywhere in Belgium or the surrounding countries.

How to Get Around in Spiennes

Spiennes is a small village that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bikes from several shops in the village, and there are several walking trails that showcase the local scenery and architecture.

Where to Go Next from Spiennes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mons

Mons is a nearby city with a rich history and architecture. Visitors can explore the Grand Place, visit the Belfry, and take a tour of the former coal mine of Le Grand-Hornu.

Waterloo

Waterloo is located approximately 70 km from Spiennes and is known for its historic battlefield, where Napoleon Bonaparte was defeated in 1815. Visitors can explore the battlefield and visit the Waterloo museum for insight into the historic battle.

Conclusion

Spiennes is a small village that packs a big punch when it comes to culture, history, and scenery. Whether you’re interested in archaeology, gastronomy, or outdoor adventures, Spiennes has something to offer for everyone. We hope this travel guide helps you plan your perfect trip to Spiennes and introduces you to the best attractions and experiences that the village has to offer.
