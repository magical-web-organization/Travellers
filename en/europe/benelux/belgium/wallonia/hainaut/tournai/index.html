Introduction to Tournai

Tournai, a hidden gem in the Wallonia region of Belgium, is a city that is often overlooked by tourists. This charming city, founded in the 2nd century BC, is the oldest city in Belgium and has a rich historical and cultural heritage. Located along the River Scheldt, Tournai is known for its stunning architecture, picturesque streets, and a thriving cultural scene.

In this Tournai travel guide, we will take you on a journey through this beautiful city and explore its cultural and natural highlights. We will also guide you through the best things to do in Tournai, places to stay, and local customs to make your trip to Tournai an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Tournai

When visiting Tournai, there are many places to explore. From historical sites to natural landmarks, Tournai has something for everyone. Here is a list of some of the popular places to visit in Tournai:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tournai Cathedral: This iconic cathedral is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most striking landmarks in Tournai. The construction of the cathedral began in the 12th century and took over 200 years to complete. It features a unique blend of Romanesque and Gothic architecture and is home to several valuable pieces of art, including the Tapestry Museum.

  • Museum of Fine Arts: This museum houses an impressive collection of art, including works by Flemish masters like Rubens and Van Dyck.

  • Museum of Natural History and Vivarium: This museum showcases the natural history of the region, including a collection of over 120,000 insects and a vivarium with reptiles and amphibians.

  • Belfry of Tournai: The Belfry of Tournai dates back to the 12th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It offers stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Tournai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc communal: This beautiful park is a great place to relax and enjoy the scenery. It features a playground, picnic areas, and a pond.

  • Escapades Vertes: For those seeking adventure, Escapades Vertes offers hiking, horse riding, and mountain biking tours through the picturesque countryside around Tournai.

  • Canal du Centre: A stroll along the Canal du Centre provides a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. The canal is also a popular spot for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tournai

Tournai has a variety of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several marked routes that take you through the beautiful countryside around Tournai, including:

  • The Promenade Verte: This 24km bike trail takes you through the heart of Tournai and its surroundings.

  • The Way of the Brunehaut: This 161km route takes you from Tournai to Beauvois-en-Cambresis, passing through picturesque villages along the way.

  • The Canal du Centre: A 10km trail along the canal offers beautiful views of the waterway and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Tournai: Activities and Tours

In addition to the historical and natural attractions of Tournai, there are many activities and tours to enjoy during your visit. Some of the best things to do in Tournai include:

  • Take a boat tour of the River Scheldt: Enjoy the beautiful scenery of the river and learn about the history of Tournai from a different perspective.

  • Visit the weekly farmers’ market: The market, held every Sunday, offers a variety of fresh produce, local cheeses, and handmade crafts.

  • Attend a concert at the Jacques Franck Cultural Center: This center hosts concerts, theatre performances, and art exhibits throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tournai

Here’s a suggested itinerary for spending a week in Tournai:

Day 1: Visit Tournai Cathedral and the Tapestry Museum Day 2: Explore the Museum of Fine Arts and the Belfry of Tournai Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Canal du Centre Day 4: Visit the Museum of Natural History and Vivarium
Day 5: Take a boat tour of the River Scheldt Day 6: Shop at the weekly farmers’ market and enjoy a picnic in Parc communal Day 7: Attend a concert at the Jacques Franck Cultural Center

Food and Drink Scene of Tournai

No trip to Tournai is complete without sampling its local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tarte al djote: This cheesy pie, made with green vegetables and potatoes, is a traditional dish in the Hainaut region.

  • Carbonnade flamande: This hearty dish consists of beef cooked in beer and onions, served with fries.

  • Pain à la Grecque: This sweet bread, flavored with aniseed and cinnamon, is a local specialty that is often enjoyed for breakfast or as a dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Bière de Tournai: This beer, brewed locally, is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

  • Pecket: A traditional liquor made from juniper berries and locally produced in Tournai.

Where to Stay in Tournai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tournai offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some recommended options include:

  • Hotel d’Alcantara: This boutique hotel is located in a historic building in the heart of Tournai. It features stylish rooms and a cozy lounge bar.

  • Peace & Love Hostel: This hostel is located near the train station and offers budget-friendly dorm rooms and private rooms.

  • Les studios de la grand-place: These apartments are located in the Grand Place of Tournai and feature modern amenities and panoramic views of the city.

What to Buy in Tournai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Tournai, be sure to bring back some souvenirs and local products. Some popular items include:

  • Lace products: Tournai is known for its high-quality lace, which makes a great gift for friends and family.

  • Beer: Tournai is home to several breweries, and the local beer is a great way to savor the flavor of the region.

  • Chocolate: Belgium is renowned for its chocolate, and Tournai is no exception. Be sure to pick up some locally made chocolates to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tournai

Tournai has a rich cultural heritage that includes a variety of festivals and events throughout the year. Some of the most popular festivals include the Carnival of Tournai, held in February, and the Folklore Festival, held in August. It’s worth checking the calendar to see which festivals will be taking place during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Tournai

The best time to visit Tournai is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild and the city comes alive with festivals and events. However, Tournai can be visited any time of the year, and each season has its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tournai

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beer
  • Visit the historical landmarks and cultural institutions
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Explore the surrounding countryside

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect religious landmarks or customs
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Tournai

Tournai is generally a safe city, but it’s always best to take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Tournai

Belgium has a high standard of healthcare, and Tournai has several hospitals and medical facilities in the city. It is always recommended to have travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies.

Getting to Tournai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tournai

The nearest airports to Tournai are Brussels Airport and Charleroi Airport. From both airports, you can take a train to Tournai.

Bus Stations Near Tournai

Tournai has a central bus station that provides regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

You can also reach Tournai by train. The city has a train station that connects to several destinations across Belgium and Europe.

How to Get Around in Tournai

Tournai is a compact city that can easily be explored on foot. Alternatively, you can rent a bike or take a bus to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Tournai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tournai is a great starting point to explore other nearby destinations in Wallonia, Belgium. Some popular day trips include:

  • Visit the historic city of Mons, located about 35km from Tournai.
  • Explore the picturesque countryside and wineries of the Entre-Sambre-et-Meuse region.
  • Visit the beautiful city of Namur, located about 70km from Tournai.

Conclusion

Tournai may be small, but it packs a punch when it comes to history, culture, and natural beauty. From the iconic Tournai Cathedral to the picturesque Canal du Centre, there is so much to explore in this beautiful city. We hope this Tournai travel guide has inspired you to visit this charming Belgian city and make unforgettable memories.
