Introduction to Ath

In the southwestern province of Hainaut lies Ath, a charming medieval town with a rich history and cultural significance. The city is well-known for its vibrant festivals, historical sites, and beautiful natural scenery. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best things to do, see, and eat in Ath.

List of Places to Visit in Ath

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ath is renowned for its medieval architecture and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to several museums that offer a glimpse into the history and tradition of the area.

  • The House of Giants: This 18th-century building houses a collection of puppets and masks from local carnivals and festivals.
  • The Tour Burbant: This tower was used as a defense fortification and offers stunning views of the city.
  • The Espace Gallo-Romain: This museum showcases the Roman influence on the region and houses artifacts from the Roman era.
  • The Museum of Archeology: This museum displays artifacts from the prehistoric and Gallo-Roman period.

Natural Attractions of Ath: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ath is renowned for its beautiful natural scenery, lush green forests, and stunning waterways. The city is surrounded by several parks and preserves that offer hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities.

  • The Parc de la Dodaine: This 10-hectare park is home to several walking trails, lakes, and recreational facilities.
  • The Pays-des-Collines Nature Park: This park offers an array of outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, and horseback riding.
  • The Mont-de-l’Enclus: This beautiful hill offers stunning panoramic views of the city and is a popular spot for paragliding and other aerial sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ath

Ath is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city and surrounding areas offer a wide array of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some popular trails include:

  • The RAVeL 4: This trail runs along a converted railway line and passes through several charming towns and villages.
  • The Sentier de l’Etrange: This hiking trail passes through scenic forests and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • The Tour du Pays des Collines: This 148 km trail is a favorite among experienced hikers and requires several days to complete.

Best Things to Do in Ath: Activities and Tours

In Ath, there is something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the rich history of the region, enjoying the great outdoors, or tasting the local cuisine, there are plenty of options for you.

  • Explore the Carnaval de Ath: This festival, held every year in February, is one of the most vibrant and colorful carnivals in Belgium.
  • Tour the Brasserie des Légendes: This brewery tour offers a glimpse into the brewing process, followed by a tasting of the brewery’s most famous beers.
  • Take a boat tour of the Ath waterway: This relaxing tour offers stunning views of the city and its surroundings from the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ath

If you’re planning to spend a week in Ath, here is a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic city center of Ath
  • Day 2: Visit the House of Giants and the Espace Gallo-Romain museums
  • Day 3: Hike or cycle through the Pays-des-Collines Nature Park
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour of the Ath waterway and visit the Tour Burbant
  • Day 5: Explore the Mont-de-l’ Enclus
  • Day 6: Visit the Museum of Archeology and enjoy some local cuisine
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the Parc de la Dodaine

Food and Drink Scene of Ath

Ath is renowned for its delicious local cuisine and traditional Belgian beers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Waterzooi: This traditional Flemish stew can be made with chicken or fish and is served with vegetables and cream.
  • Carbonade Flamande: This hearty beef stew is made with beer and onions and is typically served with frites.
  • Escavèche: This fish dish is prepared by marinating fried fish in vinegar and oil and is typically served cold.

Local Beverages

  • Gueuze: A beer made by blending young and old lambic beers with natural fermentation
  • Saison: A light, fruity beer with a dry finish
  • Jenever: A traditional Belgian gin made from malt wine, juniper, and other botanicals.

Where to Stay in Ath: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ath has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Europe: This charming hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • B&B La Ferme de l’Apéro: This beautiful guesthouse is surrounded by stunning countryside and offers a peaceful and tranquil experience.
  • Auberge de la Ferme: This charming inn is located in the Pays-des-Collines Nature Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Ath: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ath has a long history of traditional crafts and artisanal products. Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Ath ceramics: These beautiful, handmade ceramics are one of the city’s most famous products and make for great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Sweet treats: Belgium is famous for its chocolates, waffles, and other sweet treats, which you can find in local bakeries and confectioneries.
  • Beer: Be sure to bring home some of the region’s famous lambic beers or seasonal brews.

Local Culture and Customs of Ath

Belgium has a unique mix of French and Flemish cultures, which is reflected in the local traditions and customs. In Ath, some of the most important cultural events include the Carnaval de Ath and the Ducasse d’Ath, which take place every year and celebrate the city’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Ath

The best time to visit Ath depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and events, the summer months from June to August are the best time to visit. If you prefer a quieter experience with lower prices, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of spring and fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ath

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beers.
  • Embrace the local customs and traditions.
  • Plan ahead for major events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to tip at restaurants and cafes.
  • Damage the natural surroundings or local landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ath

Ath is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, it is always advisable to take standard precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Ath

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and emergency medical care is available 24/7. However, it is recommended to have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical expenses.

Getting to Ath: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ath

The closest international airport to Ath is Brussels Airport, located around 75 km away from the city. Several airlines operate regular flights to Brussels from major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Ath

Ath is well-connected to other cities in Belgium via the bus network, which runs regular services to major cities like Brussels and Bruges. The nearest bus station is located in the city center, close to the train station.

Other Options

Ath is also easily accessible by train and car. The nearest railway station is located in the city center, and several major highways connect Ath to other cities in Belgium and nearby countries.

How to Get Around in Ath

Ath is a relatively small city and can be explored on foot or by bike. The city also has an efficient bus network that runs regular services to nearby towns and attractions. Taxis and car rentals are also available, although they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Ath: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for nearby destinations to explore, here are some options:

  • Tournai: This historic town is located just 20 km southeast of Ath and is renowned for its Gothic cathedral and picturesque cobblestone streets.
  • Mons: This charming town is located around 40 km west of Ath and is home to several historical sites and museums.
  • Pairi Daiza: This beautiful zoo and botanical garden is located around 30 km northwest of Ath and is home to over 5,000 animals and 500 species of plants.

Conclusion

Ath is a beautiful and charming destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in the rich history and cultural heritage of the region, or you’re looking for outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine, Ath is the perfect destination for you. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this hidden gem of Belgium.
