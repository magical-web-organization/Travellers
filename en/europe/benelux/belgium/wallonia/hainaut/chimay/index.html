Introduction to Chimay

Chimay is a small town located in the province of Hainaut, in Wallonia, Belgium. Known for its world-famous Trappist beer and cheese, Chimay is a popular tourist destination for those who want to experience authentic Belgian culture. The charming town boasts of historical landmarks, natural attractions, and exciting outdoor adventures that make it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Chimay

At Chimay, visitors can take their pick from a wide variety of attractions, ranging from historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, and cultural activities. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chimay Castle: Built-in the 18th century, Chimay Castle is a magnificent example of classical architecture. Visitors can tour the castle’s beautiful gardens and stately interior, filled with ornate furnishings and period decor.
  • Musée de la Vie Rurale: This fascinating museum showcases the rural lifestyle of the Chimay region, including exhibits on farming, traditional crafts, and local folklore.

Natural Attractions of Chimay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scourmont Abbey: Home to the famous Trappist monks, Scourmont Abbey is a stunning example of Gothic architecture. Visitors can tour the abbey, attend mass, and sample their world-renowned Trappist beer and cheese.
  • Lac de Virelles: This picturesque lake is surrounded by lush forests, making it a popular spot for hiking, boating, and picnicking.
  • Valley of Eau Blanche: This scenic valley is home to beautiful waterfalls and hiking trails, making it a perfect destination for any nature lover.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chimay

Chimay is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails winding through forests and nature reserves. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Ravel Route One: This scenic route runs along an old railway line and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • GR12 Trail: This long-distance trail runs through Chimay and is perfect for avid hikers who want to explore the region in-depth.

Best Things to Do in Chimay: Activities and Tours

  • Chimay Beer Tour: Visitors can tour the famous Trappist brewery and learn about the brewing process of Chimay’s world-renowned beer.
  • Candle Making Workshop: This unique workshop allows visitors to create their own scented candles using traditional techniques.
  • Fishing: The region is home to a number of excellent fishing spots, with opportunities to catch a variety of fish species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chimay

If you’re planning to spend a week in Chimay, here’s a sample itinerary that will allow you to experience the best of the region:

Day Activity
1 Visit Chimay Castle and Gardens
2 Tour the Trappist brewery and sample the beer and cheese
3 Take a hike in the Valley of Eau Blanche
4 Visit Scourmont Abbey and attend mass
5 Take a cycling tour along Ravel Route One
6 Explore the rural lifestyle of the Chimay region at Musée de la Vie Rurale
7 Relax and unwind by Lac de Virelles

Food and Drink Scene of Chimay

No trip to Chimay would be complete without exploring the local food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chimay Tart: A delicious pastry made with local Trappist beer and cheese.
  • Carbonnade: A hearty beef stew made with Chimay beer and served with fries.

Local Beverages

  • Trappist Beer: Chimay is famous for its Trappist beer, which is brewed by the monks at Scourmont Abbey.
  • Chimay Cheese: This cheese is also made by the monks of Scourmont Abbey, and pairs perfectly with Chimay beer.

Where to Stay in Chimay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chimay offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget and preference. Here are some top options:

  • Hostellerie Le Prieuré Saint-Géry: This charming guest house is located in the heart of Chimay and offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Hotel Auberge La Grande Cure: This upscale hotel is situated in a picturesque location and features elegant rooms and a gourmet restaurant.
  • Le Petit Chapitre: This affordable bed and breakfast is perfect for travelers on a budget and offers comfortable rooms and a hearty breakfast.

What to Buy in Chimay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring home a piece of Chimay with you. Here are some top souvenirs and local products to look out for:

  • Trappist Beer and Cheese: Bring home some of Chimay’s famous beer and cheese to enjoy at home.
  • Crafts: The region is known for its traditional crafts, including pottery and ceramics.
  • Chimay Tart: This delicious pastry makes for a great gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Chimay

Chimay has a rich cultural heritage and is proud of its traditions and customs. Visitors should take care to respect local customs, including dress codes when visiting religious sites, and speaking softly in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Chimay

The best time to visit Chimay is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chimay

Here are some key do’s and don’ts for visitors to Chimay:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local beer and cheese.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Leave trash or disturb wildlife when enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Chimay can rest assured that the region is safe and secure. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. As with any travel destination, travelers should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance and consult their doctor before embarking on their trip.

Safety in Chimay

Chimay is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take care to protect their personal belongings and avoid wandering in secluded areas late at night.

Health in Chimay

Belgium has a high standard of health care, and visitors can rest assured that medical services are readily available in Chimay. Travelers should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance and carry any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Chimay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chimay is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options. Here are some ways to get there:

  • By Air: The nearest airport to Chimay is Brussels Airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive away.
  • By Train: The nearest train station to Chimay is located in Couvin, approximately 20 kilometers away.
  • By Bus: There are several bus lines that connect Chimay to other cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Chimay

Chimay is a small town that is easily navigable on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or use local transportation to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Chimay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chimay is located in the heart of Wallonia, making it a great base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Dinant: This charming town is known for its beautiful riverfront and impressive citadel.
  • Bruges: This historic city is famous for its picturesque canals and stunning medieval architecture.
  • Ardennes: This beautiful region is known for its rolling hills, dense forests, and charming small towns.

Conclusion

Chimay is a hidden gem in Belgium, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and world-class food and drink. With so much to see and do, Chimay is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for an authentic Belgian experience.
