Introduction to Liège

Liège, the largest French-speaking city in Belgium, is located in the province of Liège and is the historical capital of Wallonia. The charming city is situated on the banks of the Meuse River, and it has a rich cultural heritage and fascinating history. The city has many beautiful monuments, museums, historical buildings and parks, as well as a lively cultural scene. Liège is also known for its delicious food and drink scene, featuring tempting local specialties and delightful beers.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Liège is known for its rich cultural heritage and fascinating history. Here are some of the top cultural attractions you must visit in Liège:

Grand Curtius Museum

The Grand Curtius Museum is one of the most famous museums in the city, showcasing a vast collection of rare art and artifacts from the Renaissance to the modern-day.

The Cathedral of St. Paul

The Cathedral of St. Paul, located in the heart of Liège, is a beautiful example of Gothic architecture. It is considered one of the city’s most significant cultural landmarks.

The Archéoforum

The Archéoforum is a fascinating underground museum that showcases the archaeological findings of the city. It takes visitors to the ancient phases of the city’s construction.

Natural Attractions of Liège: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Liège has a diverse range of natural attractions and stunning scenery. Here are some of the top natural attractions you must visit in Liège:

Montagne de Bueren

Montagne de Bueren is a fascinating staircase of 374 steps, climbing up to the Citadel. It offers an impressive panoramic view of the surrounding area and makes for an excellent photo opportunity.

Parc de la Boverie

Parc de la Boverie is a beautiful park located near the Meuse River, featuring lush greenery, fountains and a sculpture garden.

The Coteaux de la Citadelle

The Coteaux de la Citadelle is a hilltop park that offers stunning panoramic views of the city. It has an extensive network of walking paths and picnic spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liège

Liège has an impressive network of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes you should try:

RAVEL La Meuse

The RAVEL La Meuse is a cycling trail that follows the Meuse River from Liège to Namur. It also connects to the RAVEL Vennbahn cycle route that runs through Germany and Luxembourg.

GR 57

The GR 57 is a popular hiking trail that extends through the Ardennes region, passing through Liège. The trail offers hikers stunning scenery of the rolling hills, forests, and streams of the region.

Best Things to Do in Liège: Activities and Tours

Liège offers an array of exciting activities and tours for tourists to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Liège:

Cultural Tours

Join a cultural tour of Liège to explore the city’s rich history and discover iconic landmarks.

Food and Beer Tours

Indulge in a food tour of Liège, where you can try the local delicacies such as waffles, crepes, and Liège-style meatballs. Also, book a beer tour to taste the region’s unique beer varieties.

Food and Drink Scene of Liège

Liège is famous for its delicious local cuisine, comprising many savory and sweet delicacies:

Must-Try Dishes

Some must-try Liège dishes are:

Boulets Liegeois

Boulets Liegeois is one of the most famous Liège dishes, comprising of meatballs in a sweet and tangy sauce, served with French fries.

Gaufres de Liege

Gaufres de Liege is a local variation of the Belgian waffles, made with a mixture of yeast dough, pearl sugar, and vanilla.

Local Beverages

Liège also has its own diverse range of beers, including:

Curtius Beer

Curtius Beer is a blonde beer produced by the Curtius Brewery, known to produce aromatic hop and yeast flavor notes.

Péket

Péket is a local variety of gin and has diverse varieties such as Juniper, Lemon, Orange, and Cherry.

Local Culture and Customs of Liège

Liège is one of Belgium’s most important cultural centers with strong Francophone culture and lifestyle. The city is famous for its unique customs, including:

  • Large market sales on Sunday mornings
  • The lively Liège Beer Festival, held annually in September

Best Time to Visit Liège

The best time to visit Liège is between May and September when the weather is sunny and pleasant, perfect for outdoor adventures and scenic walks. Moreover, winters can be a bit chilly and filled with snow, but Christmas markets and events make it a perfect time to visit Liège.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liège

Do:

  • Always greet people cordially, preferably speaking French.
  • Try some local cuisine, especially Liège waffles and boulets Liegeois.
  • Save some time for a relaxing spa or wellness treatment nearby Liège.
  • Take part in the Liège Sunday morning market scene.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry cash while shopping or eating at local cafes and bars if you don’t have a European bank account or card.
  • Avoid the topic of political debate or opinions during your visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liège

Liège is a relatively safe city for tourists, but it’s still recommended to take standard precautions and avoid darker alleys and areas at night.

Health in Liège

Liège has many health care services available, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies.

Getting to Liège: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liège

  • Liège Airport - Located on the outskirts of Liège, it operates many national and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Liège

Various national bus lines link Liège with other Belgian and European cities.

Other Options

Railway and Car-sharing networks (such as Blablacar) are other excellent options to reach Liège.

How to Get Around in Liège

In Liège, the best ways to get around are using public transportation (buses and trams), bikes, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Liège: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Locally, you can head to the beautiful town of Spa, an hour away, known for its therapeutic spas and scenic landscapes. Moreover, populous cities such as Namur or Brussels can also be reached within a day.

Conclusion

Liège is an exciting and dynamic city, with many places to visit and things to do for tourists. From its fascinating historical landmarks, beautiful natural scenery, vibrant food and drink scene, to its unique culture and customs, Liège has something to offer everyone. It is a hidden gem in the heart of Europe and a must-visit destination for those looking to explore Belgium’s rich cultural and historical heritage.
