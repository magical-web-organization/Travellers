Introduction to Liège (Province)

Liège (Province) is one of the five provinces of Wallonia, located in Belgium. It is known for its rich architectural heritage, scenic landscapes, and historical significance as an important trade center in Europe. Tourists typically visit Liège (Province) for its cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Liège (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Liège (Province) is home to several culturally significant attractions, including museums and historical sites. The following are some of the must-visit museums and historical places in the province:

  • La Boverie: A fine arts museum located in Liège that showcases works of renowned artists like Renoir, Monet, and Rodin.

  • Grand Curtius: A museum dedicated to the history and art of the city, with exhibits ranging from archaeology to fine arts.

  • Cathedral of Saint Paul: An iconic landmark of Liège featuring Gothic architecture and notable artwork.

  • Fort de Loncin: A fort that played an important role in World War I and is now a museum.

  • Maison de la Metallurgie et de l’Industrie de Liège: A museum dedicated to the industrial heritage of Liège, with exhibits on metallurgy and mining.

Natural Attractions of Liège (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Liège (Province) is also known for its picturesque landscapes, hiking trails, and opportunity for outdoor activities. Some of the most popular natural attractions in the province include:

  • Ardennes: A region known for its rolling hills, forested landscapes, and scenic views. It is a great place for outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and skiing.

  • High Fens: A natural park located in the eastern part of the province featuring extensive bogs, marshes, and heather fields.

  • Domaine de Palogne: A recreational park offering several outdoor activities like kayaking, climbing, and archery.

  • Vallée de la Hoëgne: A scenic valley offering beautiful views and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liège (Province)

Liège (Province) is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some recommended trails for your next adventure:

  • GR 573: A hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful High Fens natural park.

  • La Transardennaise: A long-distance hiking trail that starts in La Roche-en-Ardenne and ends in Bouillon.

  • Ravel Ligne 38: A biking route that takes you through picturesque towns and villages with a stunning view of the Amblève valley.

  • Chemin des Mûres: A hiking trail that takes you through the natural reserve of Hautes Fagnes.

Best Things to Do in Liège (Province): Activities and Tours

Liège (Province) offers a wide range of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some of the best things to do in the province:

  • Beer Tasting Tour: A tour that takes you through the historic breweries of Liège (Province) and allows you to taste the Belgian beers.

  • Food Tour: A tour that takes you through the culinary hotspots of Liège (Province) and allows you to taste the delicious local cuisine.

  • Boat Tour: A tour that allows you to explore the scenic waterways of Liège (Province) on a boat.

  • Spa Treatments: Liège (Province) is known for its thermal waters and spa treatments, which offer a perfect relaxation experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Liège (Province)

Here’s a week-long itinerary to explore the best attractions in Liège (Province):

  • Day 1: Visit the Cathedral of Saint Paul and explore the historic city center of Liège.

  • Day 2: Take a hiking trail in the High Fens natural park.

  • Day 3: Visit the iconic fort of Loncin and learn about its history.

  • Day 4: Take a bike ride on Ravel Ligne 38 and visit the picturesque towns and villages.

  • Day 5: Explore the Grand Curtius museum and learn about the history of the city.

  • Day 6: Take a food tour and taste the delicious local cuisine.

  • Day 7: Relax at one of the thermal spas in the province.

Food and Drink Scene of Liège (Province)

Liège (Province) offers a rich and diverse food and drink scene, with tasty local cuisine and a selection of Belgian beers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boulets à la Liégeoise: Meatballs cooked in a savory sauce made of onions and beer.

  • Gauffre de Liège: A dessert waffle made with caramelized sugar and chunks of pearl sugar.

  • Salade Liégeoise: A salad made with smoked bacon, green beans, potatoes, and vinegar dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian Beer: Liège (Province) is known for its extensive selection of Belgian beers, with popular brands like La Chouffe, Leffe, and Duvel.

  • Peket: A local Liège spirit made from juniper berries and sometimes consumed as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Liège (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Liège (Province) offers a variety of accommodation options to match different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended places to stay in the province:

  • Pentahotel Liège: A stylish hotel located in the center of Liège with modern amenities.

  • Sleepwood Hotel: A design hostel located in the city center of Liège that offers cozy and eco-friendly accommodation.

  • Le Chateau de Rendeux: A charming guesthouse located in the Ardennes region that offers comfortable rooms and beautiful views.

What to Buy in Liège (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Liège (Province) offers a variety of unique and locally made products that are perfect souvenirs to bring back home. Here are some suggestions:

  • Pottery: Liège (Province) is known for its ceramic tradition and features several workshops that produce locally made pottery.

  • Chocolate: Belgium is known for its chocolate, and Liège (Province) has several chocolatiers that produce high-quality chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of Liège (Province)

Liège (Province) has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals have several customs that are worth experiencing. Some notable customs include:

  • The Fête de Wallonie: A festival that celebrates the Walloon culture, tradition, and heritage.

  • The Festival of Saint Nicholas: A traditional festival that celebrates the arrival of Saint Nicholas and includes parades, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Liège (Province)

The best time to visit Liège (Province) is from April to June and September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. However, the province is a year-round destination with several activities and events throughout the year.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liège (Province)

Liège (Province) is generally safe for tourists, but it’s always recommended to take precautions and be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas. It’s also advised to avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Liège (Province)

Liège (Province) offers a good level of healthcare services with several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies available throughout the province. It’s also advised to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergency.
