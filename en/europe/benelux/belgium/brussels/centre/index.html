Introduction to Centre

Centre is a vibrant region located in the heart of Belgium, and it’s also the country’s capital. It offers visitors a blend of modern city life and traditional European charm, making it a favorite destination for tourists from around the world. The region is home to many historical landmarks, natural beauty, exciting outdoor activities, delicious cuisine, and friendly people. Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or family, Centre will not disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Centre

There are plenty of exciting places to visit in Centre, and the following are just some of the must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Grand Place: This is a stunning historical square located in the heart of Brussels, and it’s recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It features a grand display of Baroque architecture and is surrounded by iconic buildings like the City Hall and the Breadhouse.
  • Atomium: This is a unique structure that was built for the 1958 World Expo. It represents an iron molecule and has nine spheres connected by tubes which you can explore.
  • Royal Museums of Fine Arts of Belgium: These museums house over 20,000 paintings, sculptures, and drawings collected from the 15th century to present day.
  • Manneken Pis: The most famous statue in Centre, it’s a small bronze sculpture featuring a naked little boy relieving himself into a fountain.

Natural Attractions of Centre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ardennes: This area is located in the south of Centre and is known for its stunning outdoor environment, including forests, rugged hills, and amazing waterfalls. There are tons of outdoor activities to try, such as hiking, camping, and kayaking.
  • Zwin Nature Park: Located near the coast of the North Sea, it’s a paradise for bird lovers due to its international importance for bird migration. It’s home to more than 100 species of birds, including storks, terns, and avocets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Centre

If you’re looking for some adventurous outdoor activity in Centre, there are many biking and hiking trails in the Ardennes.

The “RAVeL” network is the perfect example of exploring Centre’s countryside on foot or by bicycle. RAVel consists of over 5000km of paths constituting small routes for bikes and pedestrians to fully immerse in fresh air and nature.

Best Things to Do in Centre: Activities and Tours

There’s never a dull moment in Centre with so many fascinating tourist attractions as well as fun and exciting things to do. We’ve listed some of the best activities and tours to help you make the most of your trip.

  • Brussels City Tour: This is a perfect way to explore Brussels and learn about its rich history with a friendly local guide. You’ll visit the major landmarks, including Grand Place, Atomium, and Manneken Pis.
  • Chocolate Workshop: Belgium is famous for its chocolates, and what better way to learn how to make them than attending a chocolate workshop?
  • Beer Tasting Tour: Another popular activity is Belgium’s beer tasting tour, where you can sample some of the finest beer in the world.
  • Canal Cruise in Bruges: Enjoy the beautiful scenery of Bruges on a canal boat tour while learning about the history of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Centre

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Centre, here’s our suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Brussels and explore Grand Place and the surrounding attractions
Day 2 Visit the Royal Museums of Fine Arts of Belgium and the Atomium
Day 3 Take a day trip to Bruges and take a canal boat tour
Day 4-5 Experience the Ardennes and participate in outdoor activities, such as hiking and camping
Day 6 Take a food tour and indulge in Belgian cuisine
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and enjoy the local markets before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Centre

Belgium is known for its delectable cuisine and the following are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Belgian Fries: These fries are a popular dish served with mayonnaise or a variety of other sauces.
  • Carbonnade Flamande: This is a savory beef stew made with beer, onions, and mustard.
  • Moules Frites: A famous Belgian dish of mussels and fries cooked in a rich broth.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian Beer: There are over 1,500 varieties of beer in Belgium, and it’s considered a national treasure.
  • Strong Coffee: Belgium’s coffee culture is strong, and it’s worth trying.

Where to Stay in Centre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for lodging in Centre, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are a few centrally-located recommendations:

  • Grand Central Hotel: A beautiful boutique hotel located in the heart of Brussels, perfect for travelers looking for luxury.
  • MEININGER Hotel Bruxelles City Center: A modern and budget-friendly option located in the city center.
  • Quirky House- Bed and Breakfast: A charming, guesthouse located a few miles from Bruges and known for its hospitality.

What to Buy in Centre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belgium has a wide range of souvenirs and local products for tourists to take home. These include:

  • Chocolates and Waffles: Belgium is famous for its chocolate and waffles, so make sure to buy some for yourself and your friends.
  • Beer and Beer Glasses: Belgian beer is world-class, and you can buy a wide variety of it there, along with beautiful beer glasses to keep the memories alive.
  • Lace: Belgium is well-known for its intricate lacework that’s sure to make an excellent souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Centre

It’s essential to respect local customs and culture in Centre, such as:

  • Greeting: In Brussels, shake hands and make direct eye contact when greeting someone.
  • Dress Code: Dressing appropriately is necessary, especially when entering churches and other religious places.
  • Language: The primary language spoken in Centre is French, and knowing a few basic phrases can go a long way.

Best Time to Visit Centre

Centre is a year-round destination, and every season has something unique to offer. However, the best time to visit is from March to May or September to November when the weather is mild, and prices are relatively low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centre

It’s always helpful to know the do’s and don’ts when traveling to a new destination.

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Belgium’s cuisine is one of its highlights, and it’s worth trying different dishes.
  • Carry Cash: Many local shops and restaurants may not accept credit cards, so it’s advisable to carry cash.
  • Learn a Little French: Knowing a bit of French may be helpful when communicating with locals.

Don’t:

  • J-walking: It is illegal to cross the streets outside of designated “zebra crossings,” so always use the crosswalks.
  • Touch Someone’s Hair: Patting someone’s hair, especially children, is considered rude in Belgium.
  • Forget to tip: Tipping is not mandatory in Belgium, but it’s encouraged to leave a few coins for good service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Centre

Centre is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always essential to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to avoid theft, especially around tourist areas.

Health in Centre

The country’s health facilities are of a high standard, and tourists can seek medical treatment in private clinics and hospitals. The EU health card is accepted in Belgium, and tourists are recommended to have travel insurance.

Getting to Centre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Centre is well connected by air, rail, and bus.

Airports Near Centre

  • Brussels National Airport (BRU): It’s the main airport in the region and is located close to Brussels City Center.

Bus Stations Near Centre

  • Brussels-North Station
  • Brussels-South Station
  • Brussels-Central Station

Other Options

  • Train: Belgium has an extensive rail network operated by NMBS/SNCB, which is well-connected to other European cities.
  • Car: Renting a car can be a good option if you plan on exploring the countryside on your own.

How to Get Around in Centre

Centre is well connected by public transportation, such as buses, trams, and trains. Most tourist attractions are accessible by foot, and cycling is a popular mode of transport in Brussels and Bruges. If you’re traveling outside the city, renting a car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Centre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Centre is the perfect base to explore nearby destinations like:

  • Antwerp: It’s a historic port city famous for its architectural treasures and diamond trade, located just an hour away.
  • Ghent: Known for its unique blend of medieval and contemporary architecture, it’s only an hour away from Brussels.
  • Paris: One of Europe’s most famous cities is only two hours away by high-speed train.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Centre is a beautiful and inviting region that offers visitors a fascinating mix of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, fantastic cuisine, and exciting adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, a food lover, or a nature enthusiast, Centre has something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination in Europe.
