Introduction to Heysel

Situated in the northern part of Brussels, Heysel is a captivating district with a rich and fascinating history. Known for hosting the 1935 World Exhibition and the Expo 58, Heysel is now home to several landmarks and attractions. Whether you are a history buff, art enthusiast, nature lover, or foodie, Heysel offers something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Heysel’s top attractions, best places to stay, food and drink scene, local culture, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Heysel

Heysel is known for its dynamic and multiethnic population, with French and Dutch being the primary languages spoken. Belgians are renowned for their warm hospitality and a healthy sense of humor. It is customary to greet people with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek, and it’s always good manners to say “please” and “thank you.” When dining out, tipping between 10% and 15% of the bill is common.

Best Time to Visit Heysel

The best time to visit Heysel is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the city hosts several festivals and events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Heysel

Belgium is considered one of the safest countries to visit in Europe. However, like any other place, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. It is always better to avoid leaving your valuables unattended, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Heysel

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system and offers both public and private medical services.
