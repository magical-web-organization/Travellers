Introduction to Brussels

Brussels, the capital of Belgium, is a city that is rich in history, culture, and stunning architecture. Located in the heart of Europe, Brussels has been a hub of international business, politics, and diplomacy for centuries. The city boasts an impressive array of historical landmarks, museums, art galleries, and lovely parks, making it a popular tourist destination. From the vibrant nightlife to the world-renowned chocolate and beer, Brussels has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Brussels

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Grand Place

At the heart of Brussels lies the Grand Place, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most beautiful squares in Europe. The intricate architecture of the Baroque and Gothic buildings that surround the square are breathtaking, particularly when illuminated at night.

Atomium

A symbol of the modern city, Atomium is a remarkable building that looks like nine giant spheres joined together, representing a molecule of iron. The Atomium offers an impressive panoramic view over Brussels and is home to an exhibition of science and history of the era in which it was built.

Musée Royaux des Beaux-Arts de Belgique

The Musée Royaux des Beaux-Arts de Belgique, or the Royal Museums of Fine Arts, is a haven for art lovers, with six floors of world-class art galleries that showcase everything from classical paintings to contemporary art.

Palace of Justice

A stunning example of European architecture, the Palace of Justice is the largest courthouse in the world. The massive structure has 27 courtrooms for civil and criminal trials and offers breathtaking views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Brussels: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cinquantenaire Park

The Cinquantenaire Park is a serene and lush green space located in the heart of Brussels. The park is home to several museums, including the Royal Museum of the Armed Forces and Military History and the Museum of Art and History.

Brussels Park

A favorite amongst locals and tourists alike, the Brussels Park is a stunning landscape that offers peaceful walks and great views of the city. The park boasts several fountains and a unique collection of statues and monuments.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brussels

Sonian Forest

The Sonian Forest is a beautiful park located on the outskirts of Brussels and is an ideal spot for hiking, walking, and cycling. The forest is home to several species of trees, wildlife, and birdlife and features several marked trails.

Forêt de Soignes

Forêt de Soignes is a vast forest that stretches across eastern Belgium and is a great spot for hiking, cycling, and picnicking. The forest is home to several species of animals and an extensive network of trails.

Best Things to Do in Brussels: Activities and Tours

Brussels Beer Tour

Belgium is world-renowned for its beer, and Brussels is the perfect place to explore some of the best breweries in the country. There are several beer tours, including Brussels Beer Adventure, that offer visitors the chance to sample and learn about the different types of beer brewed in the city.

Brussels Chocolate Tour

Chocolate is another delicacy that is synonymous with Belgium, and Brussels is a perfect destination for chocolate lovers. Several chocolate tours will take you on a journey to learn about the history, production, and tasting of the exquisite Belgian chocolate.

Food and Drink Scene of Brussels

Must-Try Dishes

Moules-frites

Moules-frites, or mussels and fries, is a classic Belgian dish that you must try when in Brussels. The dish is made with fresh mussels cooked with garlic, herbs, and white wine, and served with a portion of Belgian fries.

Waffles

Belgian waffles are famous all over the world, but it’s best to try them right where they were created. The waffles in Brussels are served with whipped cream, chocolate, or fruit compote.

Local Beverages

Belgian Beer

Belgian beer is famous worldwide for its diverse range and quality. Some of the most popular beer styles include lambic, gueuze, trappist, and several different types of ales.

Belgian Chocolate

Belgian chocolate is often considered the finest in the world. Famous chocolatiers such as Neuhaus, Godiva, and Leonidas have their origins in Belgium and are available all over Brussels.

Where to Stay in Brussels: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brussels has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Visitors can choose to stay close to the Grand Place or book a room in a quieter neighborhood. Some popular options include Novotel Brussels City Centre, Hotel Saint Nicolas, and Hilton Brussels City.

What to Buy in Brussels: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belgian Chocolates and Waffles

Belgian chocolates and waffles are famous globally, and you cannot leave Brussels without bringing some home. Some of the best chocolatiers in the city include Neuhaus, Leonidas, and Godiva.

Lace

Belgium is renowned for its intricate lacework, and Brussels is the best place to buy some beautiful handcrafted lace products.

Local Culture and Customs of Brussels

The people of Brussels are a mix of French and Dutch cultures, and they are known to be friendly and welcoming. Like many European countries, there is a certain level of formality and politeness in social interactions, and it is customary to greet and thank everyone.

Best Time to Visit Brussels

Brussels can be visited at any time of the year, but the best time to visit is in the late spring/early summer (April to June) and early fall (September to October). During this time, the weather is mild, and the crowds are manageable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brussels

Do:

  • Try the beer and chocolate
  • Visit the Grand Place
  • Be polite in social interactions

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public places
  • Barge in front of queues

Safety and Health

Safety in Brussels

Brussels is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should be vigilant in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. It is advisable not to walk alone at night.

Health in Brussels

The healthcare system in Brussels is of a high standard, but medical expenses can be expensive for visitors. It is essential to have adequate insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Brussels: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brussels

Brussels Airport is the main international gateway to the city, located about 12 km northeast of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Brussels

Eurolines, Eurolines Pass, and Flixbus are some of the popular bus options that serve Brussels.

Other Options

Brussels has excellent rail connections with other major European cities, making it easily accessible by train.

How to Get Around in Brussels

Public transportation in Brussels is efficient and extensive and includes buses, trams, and metro. Tickets can be purchased at kiosks, vending machines, or on board.

Where to Go Next from Brussels: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bruges

One of the most picturesque cities in Europe, Bruges is located a short distance from Brussels. The city is famous for its canals, cobbled streets, and fine historic buildings.

Antwerp

Antwerp, a city in the Flemish region of Belgium, is known for its diamond trade and art scene. The city is home to several museums, galleries, and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Brussels is a unique destination for visitors looking for a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its excellent public transportation, eclectic food and drink scene, rich culture, and stunning architecture, Brussels is worth exploring for a week or even longer.
