Introduction to West

Welcome to West, a lively and vibrant neighborhood in the city of Brussels. Located in the Benelux region of Europe, West is known for its cultural landmarks, scenic views, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking trails, or trying the local artisanal cheese, this neighborhood has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in West. We’ve also included information on the local culture and customs, safety and health, and transportation options to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in West

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, then you’ll love exploring the cultural landmarks in West. Here are some of our top picks:

1. Atomium

The Atomium is one of the most iconic landmarks in Brussels. This unique structure was built for the 1958 World Expo, and it represents an iron crystal molecule magnified 165 billion times. Visitors can take an elevator to the top for panoramic views of the city.

2. Horta Museum

The Horta Museum is a tribute to the works of Victor Horta, a famous Belgian architect. This museum is located in a house that he designed, and it showcases his unique Art Nouveau style.

3. Van Buuren Museum and Gardens

The Van Buuren Museum and Gardens is a stunning estate that was designed by David and Alice Van Buuren. This museum is home to a collection of antiques, including paintings, sculptures, and furniture.

Natural Attractions of West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

West is also home to some of the most beautiful natural scenery in Belgium. Here are a few outdoor adventures you won’t want to miss:

1. Bois de la Cambre

Bois de la Cambre is a large park in Brussels that offers visitors a peaceful retreat from the city. This park has a lake, walking trails, and a variety of flora and fauna.

2. Forest de Soignes

The Forest de Soignes is a large forest that extends from the outskirts of Brussels to Waterloo. This forest is home to a variety of wildlife, including rabbits, foxes, and deer.

3. Avenue de Tervueren

Avenue de Tervueren is a long and scenic avenue that runs from the Royal Palace in Brussels all the way to the town of Tervueren. This avenue is lined with trees and provides beautiful views of the city and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West

West is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

1. Woluwe Park

Woluwe Park is a large park that offers visitors a variety of recreational activities, including hiking and biking trails. This park is also home to a lake and several playgrounds.

2. Sonian Forest

The Sonian Forest is a large forest that stretches from Brussels to the town of Tervueren. This forest is home to several hiking and biking trails, as well as a variety of wildlife.

3. Red Cloister Abbey

The Red Cloister Abbey is a medieval monastery that is located in the Sonian Forest. This abbey is accessible by a hiking trail that offers beautiful views of the surrounding forest.

Best Things to Do in West: Activities and Tours

West is home to a variety of activities and tours that are perfect for visitors. Here are some of our top picks:

1. Chocolate Tour

Belgium is known for its delicious chocolate, and a chocolate tour is the perfect way to explore the city and taste some of the best chocolate in the world.

2. Beer Tasting Tour

Belgium is also famous for its beer, and a beer tasting tour is a great way to explore the local breweries and sample some of the country’s best brews.

3. Walking Tour of Brussels

Explore the streets of Brussels on foot and discover some of the city’s hidden gems, including St. Gery Market and the Manneken Pis statue.

Food and Drink Scene of West

Belgium is known for its world-class cuisine, and West is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Frites

Belgium is famous for its fries, or frites. These are typically served in a paper cone with a variety of sauces.

Moules-frites

Moules-frites, or mussels and fries, is a classic Belgian dish. The mussels are typically steamed in white wine and served with a side of fries.

Stoemp

Stoemp is a traditional Flemish dish that is made with mashed potatoes and a variety of vegetables, such as carrots, onions, and cabbage.

Local Beverages

Belgian Beer

Belgium is known for its beer, and there are several local breweries in West where you can sample some of the best brews in the country.

Jenever

Jenever is a traditional Belgian spirit that is similar to gin. This beverage is typically served in a tulip-shaped glass.

Local Culture and Customs of West

Belgium has a rich cultural heritage, and there are several local customs and traditions that you should be aware of. Here are a few:

Language

The official languages of Belgium are French, Dutch, and German. While most people in West speak French, there are also many locals who speak English.

Tipping

Tipping is not mandatory in Belgium, but it is common to round up the bill or leave a small tip of 5-10%.

Dress Code

Belgium is a fairly informal country, but it is polite to dress appropriately when visiting public places, such as museums and churches.

Best Time to Visit West

The best time to visit West is between March and May or from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. However, if you’re interested in visiting during the summertime, be aware that the city can be crowded with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of West

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting West:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, such as frites, moules-frites, and artisanal cheese.
  • Visit the local museums and cultural landmarks, such as the Atomium and Horta Museum.
  • Take a chocolate and beer tasting tour to experience the local flavors.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring comfortable walking shoes. West is a walkable neighborhood, and you’ll likely be exploring on foot.
  • Leave a big tip. Tipping is not mandatory in Belgium, and a small tip of 5-10% is sufficient.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places, such as museums or churches.

Safety and Health

Visitors to West should take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and staying aware of their surroundings. Health-wise, Belgium is a safe country, but it’s always a good idea to have comprehensive travel insurance.

Safety in West

West is a safe neighborhood, but tourists should still take standard safety precautions. Avoid carrying large sums of money or displaying expensive items such as smartphones, cameras, and jewelry.

Health in West

Belgium has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can access emergency medical care if needed. However, it’s always best to have comprehensive travel insurance before you travel.

Getting to West: Transportation Options and Logistics

West is located in Brussels, the capital city of Belgium, which means it is easily accessible by plane, train, and bus.

Airports Near West

Brussels National Airport, also known as Zaventem Airport, is the main airport in Brussels. It is located approximately 14 kilometers northeast of West and is easily accessible by train or taxi.

Bus Stations Near West

West is well-connected by bus to other parts of the city. Buses stop at several nearby bus stations, including the Simonis station and the West station.

Other Options

Visitors to West can also take a taxi or rent a car to explore the neighborhood and the surrounding areas. Public transportation is also a convenient and affordable way to get around, with tram and bus services available.

How to Get Around in West

West is a walkable neighborhood, and the best way to explore the area is on foot. However, visitors can also use public transportation, such as trams and buses, to get around the neighborhood and the city.
