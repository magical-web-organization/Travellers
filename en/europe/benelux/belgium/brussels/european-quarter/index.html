Introduction to European Quarter

Brussels is one of Europe’s most beautiful and diverse cities. It is divided into different areas, each with its own unique atmosphere and characteristics. The European Quarter, located in the center of the city, is the heart of European Union and home to several important institutions such as the European Parliament and European Commission. It also boasts a wealth of cultural, historical, and natural attractions, and offers a variety of activities, food and drink scene, accommodations, and transportation options. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to the European Quarter of Brussels.

Local Culture and Customs of European Quarter

Belgium is a country rich in history and culture, and the people of Brussels are proud of their heritage. Here are a few things to keep in mind while in the European Quarter:

  • Respect the country’s language and cultural norms
  • Avoid littering on the streets
  • Be punctual
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites

Best Time to Visit European Quarter

The best time to visit the European Quarter is in the early fall or late spring when crowds are low, and temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of European Quarter

Do:

  • Try Belgian chocolate and beer
  • Visit parks in the European Quarter
  • Dress appropriately while visiting churches

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets
  • Make too much noise late at night
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in European Quarter

Brussels is a generally safe city, but like any tourist destination, visitors should take precautions to protect their safety. The European Quarter is a heavily guarded area, and visitors should not worry about their security.

Health in European Quarter

Belgium has high-quality health care services, and the European Quarter has excellent hospitals and clinics.
