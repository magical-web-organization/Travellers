Introduction to Bruges

Looking for a charming, medieval city with picturesque canals and impressive architecture? Look no further than Bruges, I the Flemish region of Belgium. This postcard-perfect city is renowned for its well-preserved buildings, medieval streets, and winding canals that crisscross the city.

Bruges is ideal for those who love history and culture, as it’s packed with well-preserved buildings and museums that offer insight into its rich past. Here’s our ultimate guide to Bruges with a list of things to do, places to stay, and essential travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Bruges

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bruges is full of history and culture, making it a perfect destination for anyone who loves to explore and learn. These are the best museums and historical places to visit:

The Groeningemuseum

The Groeningemuseum is one of Bruges’ most prestigious art museums, located beside the main square. It is home to a vast collection of Flemish Primitives, Renaissance, and Baroque paintings. The museum’s collection includes works by world-famous artists such as Jan van Eyck, Rogier van der Weyden, and Gerard David.

The Church of Our Lady

The Church of Our Lady is an iconic landmark of Bruges. Its soaring tower is visible from almost anywhere in the city. This 13th-century church is home to Michelangelo’s Madonna and Child sculpture, which is said to be his only piece of art that left Italy within his lifetime. You can climb the tower for breathtaking views of the city.

The Beguinages

Step back in time at the Beguinages. These buildings were originally used to house single women who wanted to devote themselves to God without entering a convent. Today, the beguinages are UNESCO World Heritage site and provide a glimpse into the medieval way of life.

The Historium

The Historium is a multimedia museum that takes you back to the 15th century through virtual reality and interactive exhibits. It is a great way to immerse yourself in the history of Bruges and get a feel for what the city was like in the past.

Natural Attractions of Bruges: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bruges is not all about history and culture. It’s also a city of scenic beauty and natural surroundings. These are the best natural attractions to explore:

Minnewater Lake

Step out of the city and into the peacefulness of Minnewater lake. This beautiful spot is often called the “Lake of Love” and is a great place to relax and unwind. It is also home to many species of waterfowl and swans that add to its romantic charm.

The Canals

The canals of Bruges are one of its most enchanting features. The best way to explore the city is to take a boat tour, which offers a scenic view of the city’s architecture and takes you along the most beautiful routes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bruges

The city of Bruges is perfect for exploring on foot or by bike. There are many hiking and biking trails that you can take to explore the city and its surroundings. These are some of the most popular routes:

Minnewater Park

Minnewater Park is one of the most scenic and peaceful parks in the city. It’s a perfect place for meditative walks and socializing. You can enjoy a relaxing stroll around the lake or cycle the many paths around the park.

Damme Route

The Damme Route takes you out of the city and into the quiet countryside of Flanders. The trail starts from the Bruges train station and goes through the picturesque countryside village of Damme, which is renowned for its charming old buildings.

Best Things to Do in Bruges: Activities and Tours

Take a bike tour

A bike tour is an excellent way to explore the city’s streets and get to know its hidden gems. There are various bike tour options available: guided, self-guided, and customized cycle routes. Exploring the city in the company of locals is an excellent addition to your itinerary.

Take a chocolate workshop

Belgium is renowned for its chocolate, and Bruges is no exception. Taking a chocolate workshop is a perfect activity for people of all ages. You can learn to make chocolate, indulge in some delicious Belgian chocolate, or take a chocolate walking tour through the city.

## Week-Long Itinerary for Bruges

A week in Bruges is enough time to visit all its essential landmarks and neighborhoods. A popular itinerary would include:

Day 1: Stroll around the city center

Stroll around the historic center, visit its landmarks, and sample the local food and drink.

Day 2: Visit the museums

Visit the Groeningemuseum, the Church of Our Lady, and the Beguinages to immerse yourself in Bruges’ culture and history.

Day 3: Cycle the city

Cycle around the city to discover hidden corners and spots; take a bike tour or an electric scooter tour.

Day 4: Take a boat tour

Take a charming boat tour around the city; explore all its canals and learn about its history and architecture.

Day 5: Visit the city’s parks

Visit the city’s parks: Minnewater Park and Astrid Park, and enjoy their beautiful natural surroundings.

Day 6: Take a day trip

Take a day trip to Ghent or Brussels to explore more of Flanders or to experience the buzz of two of the most popular Belgian cities.

Day 7: Shop and Eat

Spend your last day shopping for souvenirs and eating more excellent Belgian food.

Food and Drink Scene of Bruges

The Flemish cuisine, along with its specialties such as beer and chocolate, is an essential part of Bruges. Therefore, the city features a wide variety of restaurants, cafés, and pubs. These are the ‘can’t-miss’ dishes and drinks to try when in Bruges:

Must-Try Dishes

Belgian Waffles

Belgian Waffles are a must-try. These scrumptious light, and crispy sweet waffles can be served with various toppings like whipped cream, chocolate sauce, fruits, or even ice cream.

Flemish Beef Stew

Flemish Beef Stew is a hearty dish made with beef and beer, and served with frites and salad. It’s a staple of Belgian cuisine and a must-try when in Bruges.

Moules-frites

Moules-frites is a classic Belgian dish of mussels and fries cooked in white wine with onions, celery, and herbs. It’s a perfect pairing with a refreshing pint of Belgian beer.

Local Beverages

Belgian Beer

Belgium is famous for its beer, and Bruges offers a wide variety of local beers. From fruit-flavored beers to Trappist ales, you’ll be sure to find a beer that suits you in Bruges.

Hot Chocolate

Belgium is also famous for its chocolate, and its hot chocolate is the epitome of indulgence. Rich and velvety, it’s the perfect cold-weather treat.

Where to Stay in Bruges: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bruges offers a wide variety of accommodation options, with something suitable for everyone’s taste and budget.

Hotels

Hotel Heritage

Hotel Heritage is a luxurious, historic hotel in the heart of Bruges. It’s an excellent choice for those seeking a lavish stay with a classic ambiance.

Hotel de Tuilerieen

Hotel de Tuilerieen is an elegant, 15th-century hotel with high-end facilities and a perfect location on the bank of the “Den Dyver” canal.

Hostels

St. Christopher’s at the Bauhaus

St. Christopher’s at the Bauhaus is ideal for the budget traveler. It offers great value for money and a central location in Bruges.

The Bauhaus

The Bauhaus is a stylish and contemporary hostel with good access to public transport and excellent communal facilities.

Guest Houses/Bed and Breakfasts

B&B Mary’s

B&B Mary’s is an elegant guesthouse situated in the heart of Bruges. Its beautiful décor and convenient proximity to the city center make it a great place to stay.

Canalside House with Sauna

The Canalside House with Sauna is a unique bed and breakfast located on the banks of the canal. The property’s modern amenities and scenic view make it an attractive location.

What to Buy in Bruges: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belgian Chocolate

Belgium is famous for its chocolate, and Bruges offers some of the best examples of this indulgent treat. Whether you’re after an elegant gift or a personal treat, the chocolate shops of Bruges offer a broad selection of award-winning chocolates.

Belgian Beer

Belgian beer is another must-buy. Many of the famous breweries are located outside the city, but you can purchase the best of Belgian beer at local beer shops in Bruges.

Lace Products

Bruges is famous for its exquisite lace products. From lace handkerchiefs and tablecloths, to delicate lace collars and clothing items, you can find a wide selection of lace items for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Bruges

Belgium has rich traditions and cultural customs, many of which have been established for hundreds of years. Flemish cuisine, art, architecture, and language are some of the many cultural aspects one can experience when in Bruges.

Best Time to Visit Bruges

Bruges is a fantastic destination to visit year-round. However, the best time to visit the city is during the shoulder season, from April to June, and from September to November, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bruges

Do:

  • Rent a bike to explore the city.
  • Sample the Belgian waffles or chocolate, and indulge in the local beer.
  • Take a boat tour of the city’s famous canals.
  • Visit all the city’s landmarks, including the UNESCO-listed Beguinages.

Don’t:

  • Take shortcuts through the red-light district.
  • Litter, smoke in public places, or jaywalk.
  • Make fun of the city’s customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bruges

Bruges is a relatively safe city. However, pickpocketing and bag-snatching are common, so it’s important to take care of your belongings and avoid carrying valuables with you.

Health in Bruges

Belgium has excellent healthcare facilities in case of an emergency. However, it’s essential to carry travel insurance that covers medical expenses, in case of any unexpected issues.

Getting to Bruges: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bruges

The nearest airport to Bruges is Brussels Airport. From there, you can get a train, bus, or hire a taxi to reach the city.

Bus Stations Near Bruges

There is only one central bus station in Bruges, which offers bus connections to and from other cities and towns in Belgium and surrounding countries.

Other Options

Bruges is located within easy access to the main highways and train lines, making it easy to reach via car, train, or bus.

How to Get Around in Bruges

Bruges is a small city, and thus easy to navigate on foot or by bike. The city center is entirely pedestrian-friendly, and cycling makes it easy to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bruges: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ghent

Ghent, one of Belgium’s best-kept secrets, is only a short train ride away from Bruges. This lovely city is home to a plethora of museums, excellent restaurants, and fantastic nightlife.

Brussels

Belgium’s bustling capital is only an hour away from Bruges, making a great option for a day trip. It’s a city full of history, art, and culture that you won’t want to miss.

Conclusion

Bruges is a charming, well-preserved medieval city that will capture your heart. With its mix of culture, history, and natural beauty, it’s a city that has something for everyone. This guide has provided a comprehensive overview of the best things to see, do, and eat in Bruges. Start planning your trip now, and we guarantee that Bruges will steal your heart and leave you enchanted.
