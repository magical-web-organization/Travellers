Introduction to Ostend

Nestled in the heart of the Belgian coast, Ostend is a scenic and vibrant city that offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and outdoor activities. Whether you want to soak up the sun on the beach, explore museums and historical landmarks, or indulge in some of the best Belgian cuisine, Ostend has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ostend

Here are some of the must-visit places in Ostend:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mu.ZEE: This renowned museum of modern and contemporary art features an extensive collection of Belgian and international art from the late 19th century to today.
  • Fort Napoleon: A 200-year-old military fortress that offers a fascinating glimpse into the city’s rich history.
  • James Ensor House: The former home of the famous Belgian painter James Ensor, this museum showcases his life and works.
  • Church of St. Peter and St. Paul: Located in the heart of the city, this iconic church is a testament to Ostend’s rich religious heritage.

Natural Attractions of Ostend: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ostend Beach: With miles of sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters, Ostend Beach is a must-visit spot for beach lovers.
  • Maria Hendrikapark: This 19th-century park is a perfect retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city and features beautiful walking trails, ponds, and fountains.
  • Royal Galleries of Ostend: This stunning 19th-century arcade offers sweeping views of the sea and serves as a hub for shopping and dining.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ostend

Ostend is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of scenic walking and biking routes to explore. The ‘Groen Lint’ is a green circuit that runs around the city and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and coastline. The ‘Ostend Dune Route,’ on the other hand, takes you through the scenic dune landscape of the Raversyde nature reserve.

Best Things to Do in Ostend: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the city: Join an informative walking tour and explore the city’s history, architecture, and landmarks.
  • Explore the sea-life at the aquarium: Located on the Ostend beach, the North Sea Oceanarium provides a fascinating glimpse into the marine world.
  • Visit the farmers market: Browse fresh local produce and indulge in some of the best Belgian cuisine at the Ostend Farmers Market.
  • Attend a concert at the Kursaal: This iconic concert hall hosts some of the best music events and shows in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ostend

Day 1: Explore the city’s landmarks and museums such as Mu.ZEE, Fort Napoleon and the James Ensor House;

Day 2: Take a walking tour of the city and visit the Church of St. Peter and St. Paul;

Day 3: Visit Ostend Beach and stroll along the Royal Galleries;

Day 4: Explore the Raversyde nature reserve and enjoy the Ostend Dune Route;

Day 5: Attend a concert at the Kursaal and dine at a local restaurant;

Day 6: Discover the local life at the Ostend Farmers Market;

Day 7: Relax at the Maria Hendrikapark and enjoy a picnic.

Food and Drink Scene of Ostend

Ostend has a bustling food and drink scene that offers plenty of options for both locals and tourists alike. Here are some must-try cuisines and beverages in Ostend:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moules-Frites (Mussels and Fries): A classic Belgian dish made with fresh mussels and crispy fries;
  • Paling in ‘t groen (Eel in Green Sauce): A traditional Flemish cuisine made with eel and a blend of herbs and vegetables;
  • Witloof Stoemp (Endive Stew): A hearty stew made with mashed potatoes and stewed endives.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beer: With hundreds of local breweries, Belgium is famous for its diverse and delicious beer culture.
  • Jenever: A traditional Belgian gin made from juniper berries and other botanicals.

Where to Stay in Ostend: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ostend has excellent accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and holiday homes. In the city center, you can find stylish and comfortable hotels such as Hotel Bero, Hotel Europe, and Andromeda Hotel.

For budget-conscious travelers, there are several excellent hostels and guesthouses, such as Hostel “De Draecke” and Upstairs Hotel.

What to Buy in Ostend: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ostend is famous for its unique and high-quality gifts and souvenirs. Some popular items to buy in Ostend include:

  • Belgian chocolates: Belgium is home to some of the world’s best chocolates, and Ostend is no exception. Choose from a range of artisanal chocolates at Chocolaterie Vandaele.
  • Handmade ceramics: Ostend has a vibrant tradition of pottery-making, and several local artists produce unique and beautiful ceramic wares.
  • Belgian beer: With hundreds of local breweries, Belgium is famous for its diverse and delicious beer culture. Choose from a range of beers at beer-specialty stores like Hops ‘n More.

Local Culture and Customs of Ostend

Ostend residents enjoy celebrating their culture and traditions, especially during festivals such as the Ostend Ommegang festival and the Ostend at Anchor festival. Belgians are generally reserved but polite and friendly.

Best Time to Visit Ostend

The best time to visit Ostend is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm, and the city is bustling with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ostend

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions;
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages;
  • Learn a few phrases in Dutch or French to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash on the streets or beaches;
  • Be loud and disruptive in public;
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ostend

Ostend is a safe and friendly city with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take basic safety precautions, such as not leaving your valuables unattended and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Ostend

Ostend has excellent health care facilities, and visitors can access medical services at several hospitals and clinics. However, it’s essential to have adequate health insurance before traveling to Belgium.

Getting to Ostend: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ostend is well-connected to other major cities in Belgium and Europe, making it easy to get there by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Ostend

The nearest international airports to Ostend are Brussels International Airport and Antwerp International Airport, both located within a three-hour drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Ostend

Ostend has an excellent bus network that connects the city to other parts of Belgium, such as Brussels and Bruges. The main bus station in Ostend is located next to the train station.

Other Options

Another convenient way to get to Ostend is via the Eurostar train from London to Brussels, followed by a connecting train to Ostend.

How to Get Around in Ostend

Ostend is a relatively small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. The city also has an excellent bus and tram network that covers the entire city and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Ostend: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ostend is an excellent base for exploring other nearby cities and attractions in Belgium, such as:

  • Bruges: One of the most picturesque cities in Europe, Bruges is just a 30-minute train ride from Ostend and is famous for its historic architecture, canals, and chocolate shops.

  • Ghent: Located an hour’s drive from Ostend, Ghent is known for its stunning medieval architecture, museums, and galleries.

  • Flanders Fields: Just a short drive from Ostend, Flanders Fields is a tribute to the soldiers who fought and died during World War I.

Conclusion

With its stunning beaches, rich culture, and delicious cuisine, Ostend is an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Belgium’s coastal region. Use this guide to explore the city’s top landmarks, food scene, accommodation options, and nearby attractions, and make the most of your trip to Ostend.
