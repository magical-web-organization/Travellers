Introduction to Lier

Lier is a picturesque city located in Antwerp province, in the Flanders region of Belgium. The city’s historic center is a charming mix of medieval architecture, cobbled streets, and winding canals. Lier is famous for its rich cultural heritage, having been the birthplace of some of Belgium’s most notable figures in literature, music, and art. The city was once a thriving center of commerce, and you can still see glimpses of its mercantile past in the ornate guild houses and market squares that dot its landscape.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the must-see attractions, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, and delicious food scene that Lier has to offer. Whether you’re a history buff, an art lover, or a foodie, this Flemish gem has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lier

Here are some top-rated attractions and things to do in Lier:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lier Beguinage: This UNESCO heritage site is a tranquil oasis in the heart of the city. The Beguinage was founded in the 13th century and is home to a small community of nuns. It also has a beautiful garden, a church, and various religious artifacts on display.

  • Zimmer Tower: The Zimmer Tower is a medieval watchtower built in the 14th century. It houses a carillon (a set of bells) that chimes every hour and half-hour. Climb the tower for a stunning view of the city.

  • Timber-framed houses in the Begijnhof: The Begijnhof district has many charming timber-framed houses that date back to the 16th century. They are well-preserved and are a testament to the city’s rich architectural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Lier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Binnennete River: The Binnennete River meanders through the city center, and taking a stroll along its banks is a must. You will encounter beautiful 17th-century houses, the old Tannery, and a great view of the city from its bridges.

  • Nete Valley: The Nete Valley is a vast and green space, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who love exploring nature. There are numerous cycling and hiking trails in this area that offer stunning views of Lier and its surroundings.

  • Mechelse Heide: A wonderful place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city, Mechelse Heide is an unspoiled nature reserve that is great for wildlife watching, walking, and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lier

Lier is a great place for hikers and cyclists. Below are some scenic routes that you can take:

  • Mechelse Heide Hike: A 15-km trail through the Mechelse Heide nature reserve with panoramic views of the heathlands.

  • Fietsroutenetwerk Lier: Get on your bike and explore Lier’s surrounding villages and farmhouses. The route is 36 km and covers many landmarks, including the St Bernard Abbey.

  • Fort-Kessel Loop: A 7 km hike through fields, forests, and old fortresses.

Best Things to Do in Lier: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the City by Boat: A boating tour along the Nete River and canals is a relaxing way to appreciate the beautiful architecture and landscapes of Lier.

  • Visit the Local Markets: Lier has a lively market scene, where you can find everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts. The Saturday market near the Zimmer tower attracts vendors from all over Belgium.

  • Take a Food Tour: Belgian cuisine is a must-have while in the country, and Lier is no exception. Join a local food tour to sample the best Belgian delicacies, such as Flemish stew, fries, waffles, chocolates, and beer.

Food and Drink Scene of Lier

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lierse vlaaikes: These small pastries with almond and cinnamon filling are a local dessert specialty that has been around for centuries.

  • Flemish stew: A heartwarming beef or pork stew, cooked slowly in beer and served with crispy fries.

  • Belgian chocolate: No trip to Belgium is complete without indulging in a chocolate treat. Lier has many artisan chocolateries that offer handmade delights.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian Beer: From Trappist to Abbey beer, Belgium has a fantastic craft beer culture, and Lier is no different.

  • Jenever: This Dutch gin is hugely popular in Belgium and is sometimes served as an aperitif or digestive.

Where to Stay in Lier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lier has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  • B&B Lier: A charming guesthouse in the historic district, with lovely gardens and homely rooms.

  • Best Western Plus Zimmerhof: A smartly renovated hotel in a 16th-century building, with spacious rooms and a delicious breakfast.

  • Youth Hostel Lier: A budget-friendly option located near the city center and perfect for backpackers and families. It offers private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in Lier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lierse vlaaikes: These delicious pastries make for a great gift for friends and family back home.

  • Belgian Chocolates: Handmade artisanal chocolates from Lier’s chocolatiers are a sweet reminder of your trip.

  • Lierse Koffie: A blend of coffee and chicory is a well-known coffee culture of Belgium; while in Lier, you can try this unique beverage.

Local Culture and Customs of Lier

Belgium is a country of history and folk culture, and Lier is no exception. Here are a few cultural practices unique to Lier:

  • Lierse Cramignon: This is a traditional folk dance that originated in Lier and dates back to the 17th century. It is a lively dance of couples under a canopy of multicolored ribbons.

  • The Lier Beerfeesten: A fantastic annual festival featuring a variety of Belgian beers, local food, music, and carnival games. It usually takes place in June.

Best Time to Visit Lier

Lier’s climate is relatively mild, with most visitors coming between April and October. Summer is peak season, and the city can get crowded, so it’s best to avoid weekends and holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lier

Do:

  • Do say “Bonjour” when entering a shop or restaurant as a sign of courtesy.

  • Do keep your personal belongings in safe custody, as pickpocketing is common in tourist areas.

  • Do respect the city’s diversity and be mindful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to check for bike lanes before crossing the street as cycling is a popular means of transportation in Lier.

  • Don’t litter as it is considered a serious offense.

  • Don’t drink alcohol in public places as it’s against the law.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lier

Lier is generally a safe city, but like any tourist destination, visitors should exercise caution and practice common sense. Stay away from dark, unlit areas at night, avoid flashing large amounts of cash or valuables, and be alert in crowded places.

Health in Lier

Belgium has comprehensive health care facilities, and Lier has a good network of medical clinics and pharmacies. Travelers should have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, repatriation, and evacuation.

Getting to Lier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lier is easy to reach by various means of transport.

Airports Near Lier

  • Brussels International Airport: The nearest airport is Brussels International Airport, located 20 kilometers from Lier. Many major airlines offer flights that connect Brussels to other cities around the globe.

  • Antwerp International Airport: Antwerp Airport is also an option, located about 25 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Lier

  • Lier Bus Station: Lier has a bus station situated near the city center from where buses frequently head to different parts of the city and nearby regions.

Other Options

  • Train Station: Lier railway station provides frequent services connecting City to major cities in Belgium like Antwerp, Mechelen, Ghent, and Brussels.

How to Get Around in Lier

Walk or bike! Lier city center is compact and pedestrian-friendly. Cycling is also a popular means of transportation in Lier; bike rentals are readily available either at the train station or various bike shops in the city.

Where to Go Next from Lier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many possible destinations beyond Lier. Here are some recommendations:

  • Antwerp: Known as a city of diamonds, Antwerp is a bustling port city located only 14 km from Lier, which is the most famous for its stunning Cathedral, art and fashion scene, and architectural beauty.

  • Mechelen: Located 12 km north of Lier and another fascinating Flemish city to visit, with a rich cultural heritage, architectural marvels, and a lively entertainment scene.

  • Brussels: Belgium’s capital city is a 40-minute train ride from Lier, famous for its beautiful historic center, mouth-watering waffles, and chocolates, and iconic landmarks such as Atomium and the Manneken Pis statue.

Conclusion

Lier may not be as popular as other Belgian cities, but it exudes an authentic Flemish charm that is hard to resist. From picturesque architecture, peaceful surroundings, and a vibrant food scene, Lier packs a punch for any traveler. With this guide, you can now plan an unforgettable trip to this underrated Belgian jewel and take home memories that will last a lifetime.
