Introduction to Turnhout

Turnhout, a hidden gem in Belgium, is a picturesque and historical town that boasts rich culture, ancient artifacts, and natural beauty. Located in the Antwerp province of Flanders, Turnhout is a perfect blend of small-town feel and urban sophistication. It has been a commercial center since the 12th century, and this is evident in its architecture and city layout, peppered with several impressive medieval buildings. It is also home to many great museums and art galleries, so art and history enthusiasts can enjoy their tour while in Turnhout.

List of Places to Visit in Turnhout

This section will highlight some of the top places to visit in Turnhout, Belgium.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Beguinage Museum: A World Heritage Site, the Beguinage Museum dates back to the 13th century and features an extensive collection of art, furniture, and other decorative items.

  2. Castle of the Dukes of Brabant: This stunning castle dates back to the 12th century and is well-preserved, with rich history and heritage attached to it.

  3. Taxandria Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, the Taxandria Museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of Turnhout and houses many ancient artifacts and art collections.

Natural Attractions of Turnhout: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. National Park De Hoge Kempen: Known for being one of the most beautiful places in Belgium, the national park is great for hiking, biking, and enjoying nature.

  2. Town Park: This beautifully manicured park covers 17 hectares and is full of greenery and scenic views, making it an ideal spot for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Turnhout

Turnhout is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts with many trails for hiking and biking. Some of the best options include the Mont Blanc route, the Stampadammen bicycle path, and the Archipelago route. These routes are ideal for nature lovers, who can immerse themselves in the stunning sights of Belgium’s countryside.

Best Things to Do in Turnhout: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours you can enjoy in Turnhout, including:

  1. Brewery Tours: Turnhout is renowned for its excellent breweries, so beer enthusiasts can check out places like La Trappe and Abbey of Westmalle.

  2. Boat Tours: Turnhout offers a canal boat tour, which takes visitors through the beautiful waters to experience the canal’s scenic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Turnhout

Visiting Turnhout for a week allows you to fully enjoy Turnhout’s beauty and attractions. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Beguinage Museum and Castle of the Dukes of Brabant.

Day 2: Take a hike in National Park De Hoge Kempen and explore the nearby natural attractions.

Day 3: Tour the town park, enjoy a picnic and canal boat tour.

Day 4: Visit the Taxandria Museum and more historical locations.

Day 5: Visit the local breweries and engage in beer tasting.

Day 6: Rent bikes and take a leisurely ride along the Archipelago route.

Day 7: Explore the city to shop for local products and souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Turnhout

Belgium is known for its unique and delicious cuisine, and Turnhout serves up some of the best local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Flemish Stew: A hearty stew made with beef, onions, beer, and spices.

  2. Waterzooi: A creamy soup or stew made with chicken or fish and vegetables.

  3. Stoemp: A mash of potatoes with several vegetable options.

Local Beverages

  1. Belgian Beer: Belgium is known for its extensive range of beers, and Turnhout offers some of the best, so indulge in beer tasting tours while in Turnhout.

Where to Stay in Turnhout: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Turnhout offers several hotel options, including luxury and budget accommodations. If you’re looking for luxury, Hotel Corsendonk Viane is an ideal option, while budget travelers can stay at Guesthouse De Hessie or Bed & Breakfast de Joker.

What to Buy in Turnhout: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belgium is known for its chocolates, and Turnhout also boasts its special variety of chocolates. If you’re a fan of shopping, Turnhout’s Sunday market is a great place to find local products, including lace products, tin products, and handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Turnhout

The people of Turnhout are friendly and welcoming, and they love their cultural heritage. Turnhout’s museum and art galleries hold showcases of local art and artisanal products. Turnhout is known for its wooden clogs shaped like a ship, which represents the importance of the shipbuilding industry to the area.

Best Time to Visit Turnhout

The best time to visit Turnhout is during the summer when the weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities. However, you can visit Turnhout at any time of the year, as it offers something for everyone all year-round, with several festivals, markets, and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turnhout

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try local beers, chocolates, and cuisine.
  • Explore all the tourist attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the public property.
  • Don’t smoke in public areas unless designated smoking areas
  • Don’t wear inappropriate clothing while exploring local religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Turnhout

Turnhout is generally a safe town. However, it never hurts to take precautions by keeping wallets, cellphone, and other valuables close to you while in crowded areas. Follow local guidelines and regulations related to Covid-19 pandemic to keep yourself and others safe.

Health in Turnhout

Turnhout has an excellent health care system, with several renowned hospitals that provide quality health care services. Emergency and medical services are easily accessible throughout the town.

Getting to Turnhout: Transportation Options and Logistics

Turnhout is well-connected to other parts of Belgium with several transportation options.

Airports Near Turnhout

The nearest international airport is Brussels International Airport, which is located approximately 90 km away from Turnhout.

Bus Stations Near Turnhout

The Turnhout bus station is located in the town center, providing convenient access to public transport.

Other Options

Train services are also available and provide excellent connectivity within Belgium.

How to Get Around in Turnhout

Turnhout is a walkable town, but if you prefer cycling or public transport, bike rentals are available for a reasonable price, and public buses offer easy and affordable transport throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Turnhout: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Turnhout is close to several interesting destinations that offer many attractions and locations to explore. Some of these places include:

  • Antwerp
  • Brussels
  • Leuven

Conclusion

Turnhout is a hidden gem of Belgium, with its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. With so much to see and explore, plan your week-long trip with our Turnhout Travel Guide to make the most out of your visit and bring back unforgettable memories.
