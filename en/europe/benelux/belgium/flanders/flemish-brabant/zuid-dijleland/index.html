Introduction to Zuid-Dijleland

Located in Flemish Brabant, Zuid-Dijleland is a stunning area in Flanders, Belgium, known for its natural beauty and cultural landmarks. The region is perfect for an outdoor adventure trip, and it is also rich in history, arts, and culture. In this comprehensive Zuid-Dijleland travel guide, you will discover everything you need to know to plan a perfect trip to this charming part of Belgium.

List of Places to Visit in Zuid-Dijleland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum M

Museum M is one of the most popular museums in Leuven, with its collection covering the art of the 19th and 20th centuries. The museum also hosts temporary exhibits with artists from all over the world.

Castle of Horst

Castle of Horst is a well-preserved medieval castle, located close to Leuven. The castle has an impressive history, dating back to the 15th century. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle interior and explore the surrounding gardens.

Begijnhof of Leuven

Begijnhof of Leuven is an old courtyard designed to house Beguines, members of a women’s religious community that emerged in the Middle Ages. The courtyard, located in the city center of Leuven, is full of historical architecture and picturesque gardens.

Natural Attractions of Zuid-Dijleland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

“Doode Bemde” Nature Reserve

“Doode Bemde” Nature Reserve is located in the village of Neerijse and offers visitors an opportunity to explore a vast area of wetland, meadows, and grasslands. Visitors can walk around the nature trail, enjoying the peaceful scenery and watching birds and other wildlife.

Meerdaalwoud

Meerdaalwoud is an extensive forest situated south of Leuven and has over 6700 acres of trails and forest areas to explore. Visitors can hike, cycle, or jog through the beautiful scenery of the forest.

Abbey of Villers-la-Ville

Abbey of Villers-la-Ville is an ancient Cistercian abbey founded in the early 12th century. Visitors can explore the abbey’s ruins, go on guided tours, and marvel at the rose garden while enjoying the peaceful atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Zuid-Dijleland

Hiking Trail of Vlanderbergen

The Vlanderbergen hiking trail takes visitors through breathtaking panoramas of the Zuid-Dijleland region, starting and ending in the village of Overijse. The trail stretches over 10 km and promises beautiful sceneries along with glimpses of the region’s history.

Biking Route of Brabantse Kouters

The Brabantse Kouters biking route is a 50 km-long track ideal for experienced cyclists looking for a challenge. The trail is full of inspiring views of lush green countryside dotted with idyllic small towns.

Best Things to Do in Zuid-Dijleland: Activities and Tours

Ballooning over Leuven

Ballooning over Leuven takes visitors on an unforgettable journey over the city’s landmarks, providing exceptional views of the city from the sky. It is an exclusive and original way to visit Leuven from the air.

Brewery Tour of Stella Artois

Stella Artois has been brewing some of the world’s finest beers for centuries. Visitors can take a brewery tour of Stella Artois, learning more about the brewing process, the history of the brewery, and how Stella Artois became one of the world’s best-known beer brands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zuid-Dijleland

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to the Museum M in Leuven. Afterward, explore the city’s historic landmarks.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of Castle of Horst, followed by an afternoon picnic in one of the parks in Leuven.

Day 3: Explore the Begijnhof of Leuven to learn more about the history and legacy of the Beguines. In the afternoon, take a trip to the Tivoli Park theme park in Mechelen.

Day 4: Head to the “Doode Bemde” Nature Reserve to hike or cycle through the beautiful scenery and watch the local wildlife.

Day 5: Visit the Abbey of Villers-la-Ville and marvel at the gorgeous architecture and gardens. Afterward, go to a trappist brewery in the area for a beer tasting session.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over Leuven, offering magnificent views of the city from the air.

Day 7: Take the Brabantse Kouters biking route, explore the small towns and countryside, and enjoy a picnic along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Zuid-Dijleland

Must-Try Dishes

Stoofvlees (Flemish Beef Stew)

Stoofvlees is a traditional Flemish beef stew made with dark beer to give it a rich flavor. It usually served with fries or bread.

Gouden Carolus Classic

Gouden Carolus Classic is an award-winning Belgian beer with a high alcohol content that has been brewed since 1369. It has a rich and complex flavor, making it a favorite among beer connoisseurs.

Local Beverages

Jenever

Jenever, also known as Belgian gin, is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from juniper berries. It is often served as a shot and is a popular aperitif in Belgium.

Lambic

Lambic is a traditional Belgian beer made with wild fermentation, making it tart and sour. It is a unique flavor and enjoyed by beer enthusiasts worldwide.

Where to Stay in Zuid-Dijleland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

B&B De Corazon

B&B De Corazon is a cozy and charming guesthouse in Leuven. The rooms are decorated with unique and personal touches, and the proprietors offer a helpful and friendly welcome.

Martin’s Klooster

Martin’s Klooster is a luxury hotel located in the historical center of Leuven, housed in an old monastery. The hotel’s décor is chic and stylish, and the staff provides a professional and attentive service.

What to Buy in Zuid-Dijleland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belgian Chocolate

Belgian chocolate is world-renowned, and it is a perfect gift to bring home from Zuid-Dijleland. You will find many chocolate shops in and around the main cities.

Lace from Bruges

Bruges is a city famous for its beautiful lacework. The lace is often handmade by local artisans and is a unique and special souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Zuid-Dijleland

The local people of Zuid-Dijleland are friendly and welcoming, and the region celebrates many traditional festivals throughout the year. Flemish is the local language, and visitors can try to learn some local phrases to make their trip more immersive.

Best Time to Visit Zuid-Dijleland

The best time to visit Zuid-Dijleland is from April to October when the weather is warm and pleasant. The region is at its most beautiful in the spring and autumn, with colorful foliage and welcoming temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zuid-Dijleland

Do:

  • Try the local beers and chocolates while exploring the city’s landmarks and countryside
  • Get off the beaten path and explore the region’s quiet corners for hidden gems
  • Learn some Flemish to engage with the local people and immerse yourself in the local culture

Don’t:

  • Forget to carry cash, as some small businesses and restaurants may not accept cards
  • Overlook the small villages and hamlets dotted around the region
  • Leave without trying some of the local specialties, such as Stoofvlees or Gouden Carolus Classic

Safety and Health

Safety in Zuid-Dijleland

Zuid-Dijleland is a safe and friendly region, but visitors should take the usual precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings in busy areas.

Health in Zuid-Dijleland

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can expect excellent medical care if needed. It is recommended to purchase travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical expenses.

Getting to Zuid-Dijleland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zuid-Dijleland

Brussels Airport is the closest airport to Zuid-Dijleland, located approximately 30 km away, and easily reachable by public transport or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Zuid-Dijleland

Leuven Bus Station is the main bus station in the area, with many routes connecting to major Belgian and European cities.

Other Options

Driving is also a popular option in Zuid-Dijleland, and visitors can rent cars at the airport or in the city center.

How to Get Around in Zuid-Dijleland

Zuid-Dijleland is primarily a rural region, and a car is a recommended mode of transportation to explore the countryside. However, public transportation is available, and visitors can take trains, buses, or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Zuid-Dijleland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bruges

Considered one of the most beautiful cities in Europe, Bruges is a must-visit destination in Belgium. The city’s cobbled streets, beautiful architecture, and sleepy canals make it a charming weekend getaway spot.

Brussels

Brussels is the capital of Belgium and the European Union, with a wealth of art, culture, and delicious food. The city also has a range of exciting shopping opportunities and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

With its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and charming cities, Zuid-Dijleland is a fantastic destination for tourists. Visitors can explore the local culture, enjoy delicious cuisine, and take in the stunning landscapes of this beautiful region. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Zuid-Dijleland.
