Are you looking for the perfect off-the-beaten-path destination in Belgium? Look no further than Neerijse! Located in Flemish Brabant, Neerijse is a charming village that offers a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. Here’s everything you need to know for a memorable trip to this hidden gem in Belgium.

Introduction to Neerijse

Neerijse is a small village in the Flemish Brabant province of Belgium. It has a population of around 1,200 people and is known for its beautiful countryside, historic sites, and tasty local food and drinks. The village is easily accessible from nearby cities like Brussels and Leuven, making it an ideal day trip destination.

List of Places to Visit in Neerijse

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Neerijse has several cultural attractions that are worth visiting. The most famous one is the Romanesque church of Saint Michael, which dates back to the 11th century and features beautiful interior frescoes. Other highlights include:

  • Kasteel van Neerijse: A beautiful castle built in the 16th century that now houses a restaurant and banquet hall.
  • Tumulus of Opprebais: An archaeological site that dates back to the Iron Age. It features a tumulus (a burial mound) and a reconstructed grave site.
  • Sint-Jan-de-Doperkerk: A 13th-century church that was recently restored and features a stunning stained-glass window.

Natural Attractions of Neerijse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Neerijse is located in a picturesque countryside full of natural beauty. Here are some of the best outdoor activities and scenic spots to explore:

  • Forest of Meerdaal: This forest is perfect for a hike or bike ride, with plenty of trails to choose from and beautiful panoramic views.
  • Neerijse Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including rare birds and frogs. It’s a great place for birdwatching, hiking, or just enjoying the peace and quiet.
  • Dijle River: The Dijle river runs through Neerijse and offers plenty of opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and boating. There are also several picnic areas along the riverbank where you can relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neerijse

Neerijse is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of well-marked trails that will take you through some of the most beautiful parts of the village and surrounding countryside. Some popular routes include the Neerijsese Pijl (6 km) and the Meerdaal Trail (14 km).

Best Things to Do in Neerijse: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or food, there are plenty of things to do in Neerijse. Here are some ideas to get you started:

  • Take a guided tour of the Saint Michael church and the castle of Neerijse to learn about the village’s rich history.
  • Rent a bike and explore the beautiful countryside on two wheels.
  • Visit a local brewery or vineyard and sample some of the region’s delicious beer and wine.
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Flemish dishes like stoofvlees and waterzooi.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neerijse

Day 1: Arrive in Neerijse and explore the village center. Visit the Saint Michael church and the castle of Neerijse.

Day 2: Rent a bike and go for a ride through the Forest of Meerdaal.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Tumulus of Opprebais and the Sint-Jan-de-Doperkerk.

Day 4: Visit a local brewery or vineyard and sample some of the region’s delicious beer and wine.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Flemish dishes like stoofvlees and waterzooi.

Day 6: Spend the day exploring the Neerijse Nature Reserve and the Dijle river.

Day 7: Head to nearby Leuven for a day trip and explore the city’s historic center and famous breweries.

Food and Drink Scene of Neerijse

Neerijse is known for its delicious food and drinks, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stoofvlees: A delicious beef stew made with beer and served with fries.
  • Waterzooi: A creamy soup made with chicken or fish and vegetables.
  • Flemish asparagus: A seasonal delicacy that’s usually served with hollandaise sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Geuze beer: A traditional sour beer that’s made in the Brussels region.
  • Lambic beer: A beer that’s fermented with wild yeast and bacteria, giving it a unique and complex flavor.
  • Wittekerke beer: A light, refreshing beer that’s brewed in nearby Leuven.

Where to Stay in Neerijse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Neerijse is a small village, so there are only a few options for accommodation. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Castle of Neerijse: This stunning castle now serves as a hotel and restaurant, offering luxurious rooms and delicious cuisine.
  • B&B Baron’s House: A cozy bed and breakfast located in an 18th-century farmstead.
  • Hotel The Lodge Heverlee: A modern hotel located in nearby Leuven.

What to Buy in Neerijse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Neerijse beer: Pick up a bottle (or two) of the local Geuze or Lambic beer to enjoy at home.
  • Witloof: This white vegetable is a local specialty and can be found in markets and shops throughout Neerijse.
  • Belgian chocolate: No trip to Belgium would be complete without a box of delicious chocolate!

Local Culture and Customs of Neerijse

Neerijse is a friendly and welcoming village, and locals are proud of their cultural heritage. Some local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Flemish is the primary language spoken in Neerijse, although most locals also speak English.
  • Tipping is not mandatory in Belgium, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small tip (5-10%) is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Neerijse

The best time to visit Neerijse is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer is also a great time to visit, especially if you want to take advantage of the many outdoor activities and events happening in the area. Winter can be cold and rainy, but it’s also a good time to explore the village’s cultural attractions and cozy up with a warm bowl of stoofvlees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neerijse

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit the local attractions and try the local cuisine.
  • Take advantage of the beautiful countryside and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Skip out on a chance to try some of the local beer and wine.
  • Be afraid to ask locals for recommendations or directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neerijse

Neerijse is a safe and peaceful village, and violent crime is rare. However, it’s still important to take common-sense precautions, such as:

  • Keeping your valuables secure and out of sight.
  • Being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Neerijse

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics located in nearby cities like Brussels and Leuven. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and make sure you have any necessary vaccinations before your trip.

Getting to Neerijse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neerijse

The closest airport to Neerijse is Brussels Airport, located about 25 km away. There are several taxi and bus services available to take you from the airport to Neerijse.

Bus Stations Near Neerijse

There are several bus stations located in nearby cities like Brussels and Leuven that offer services to Neerijse. The Neerijse-Sint-Michiel bus stop is located in the village center and is served by several local bus lines.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Neerijse, there are several parking options available in the village center. You can also rent a bike or take a taxi to get around the area.

How to Get Around in Neerijse

Walking, biking, and driving are the most common ways to get around Neerijse. There are also several local bus routes that serve the village and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Neerijse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Neerijse is located in the heart of Belgium, making it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding region. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Brussels: The capital city of Belgium is just a short drive or train ride away and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including the famous Atomium and Royal Palace.
  • Leuven: This historic city is known for its medieval architecture, world-renowned universities, and vibrant beer culture.
  • Bruges: This charming city is known as the “Venice of the North” and is famous for its canals, charming bridges, and historic buildings.

Conclusion

Neerijse is a hidden gem in Belgium, offering a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. With this guide, you’re ready to plan the perfect trip to this picturesque village. Enjoy!
