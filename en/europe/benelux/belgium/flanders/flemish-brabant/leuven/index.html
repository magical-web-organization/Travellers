Introduction to Leuven

Leuven is a small and vibrant city in the heart of Belgium. Home to one of the oldest universities in Europe, Leuven is known for its medieval architecture, rich cultural heritage, and lively student scene. A perfect city to explore by foot or bike, Leuven has plenty of attractions, activities, and gastronomic delights for travelers of all ages and interests. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Leuven, including where to go, what to see, what to eat, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Leuven

Leuven has a diverse range of attractions, from historical landmarks to scenic parks and museums. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Leuven:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Grand Beguinage: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the best-preserved beguinages in Flanders, with a rich history dating back to the 13th century.
  2. St. Peter’s Church: A beautiful example of Brabant Gothic architecture, this church houses several important artworks, including The Last Supper by Dirk Bouts.
  3. M-Museum Leuven: The main museum of Leuven, M-Museum houses a vast collection of modern and contemporary art, as well as artefacts from Leuven’s history.
  4. Groot Begijnhof: Another beguinage that dates back to the 13th century, Groot Begijnhof is known for its charming cobblestone streets and picturesque gardens.
  5. Town Hall: A stunning example of Gothic architecture, the Town Hall of Leuven is adorned with hundreds of statues and carvings.

Natural Attractions of Leuven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Park Abbey: A peaceful and serene park, Park Abbey features a stunning 18th-century abbey, a beautiful rose garden, and several hiking trails.
  2. Kruidtuin: Belgium’s oldest botanical garden, Kruidtuin is a great place to enjoy a tranquil stroll while admiring its vast collection of plants and trees.
  3. River Dijle: Running through the heart of Leuven, River Dijle offers plenty of opportunities for kayaking, boating, and picnicking.
  4. Vaartkom: A new neighborhood in Leuven, Vaartkom is known for its modern architecture, trendy bars, and lively nightlife scene.
  5. Castle Arenberg: Set amidst lush greenery, this 16th-century castle is now home to the Faculty of Engineering of KU Leuven and is open to the public for guided tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leuven

Leuven is a great destination for hikers and bikers, with many trails that offer stunning views and natural wonders. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes of Leuven:

  1. Abbey Walk: This 12-kilometer walk takes you through the beautiful countryside of Leuven, passing by several abbeys and castles along the way.
  2. Molenbeek Walk: A 9-kilometer walk that starts and ends at the Town Hall of Leuven, Molenbeek Walk takes you through the city’s historic neighborhoods and along the river.
  3. Grote Nete Cycle Route: This 94-kilometer cycle route takes you through the beautiful Kempen region of Flanders, passing by several charming villages and natural reserves.
  4. Dyle Valley Walk: A 28-kilometer walk that takes you along the River Dijle and through the picturesque villages of Heverlee, Oud-Heverlee, and Korbeek-Dijle.
  5. Leuven City Walk: A leisurely 5-kilometer walk that takes you through the historic center of Leuven, passing by many of its iconic landmarks and attractions.

Best Things to Do in Leuven: Activities and Tours

Leuven has plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy and entertained during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Leuven:

  1. Beer Tasting Tour: Leuven is known for its beer culture, and there are many tour companies that offer beer tastings and visits to local breweries, such as Stella Artois and AB InBev.
  2. Street Art Tour: Leuven has a vibrant street art scene, with many colorful murals and installations scattered throughout the city. Take a guided tour to discover the best of Leuven’s street art.
  3. Chocolate Workshop: Belgium is known for its chocolate, and Leuven has many chocolate shops that offer delicious treats and workshops where you can learn how to make your own.
  4. Guided City Tour: Discover the history and culture of Leuven on a guided city tour, which takes you through the city’s historic neighborhoods, landmarks, and attractions.
  5. Escape Room: Looking for a fun and challenging activity? Try an escape room, where you need to solve puzzles and clues to escape from a themed room. Leuven has several escape rooms to choose from.

Food and Drink Scene of Leuven

Leuven’s food and drink scene is vibrant and diverse, with many local specialties and international cuisines to choose from. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Leuven:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stoofvlees: A traditional Flemish stew made with beef, onions, and beer, served with fries.
  2. Witloof: Belgian endives, usually served braised or grilled, and often accompanied by ham and cheese.
  3. Waterzooi: A creamy stew made with chicken or fish, vegetables, and cream, served with bread.
  4. Speculoos: A spiced biscuit, often served with coffee or tea.
  5. Belgian Waffles: Fluffy and crispy waffles, often topped with fruit, whipped cream, or chocolate.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: Leuven is home to several famous beer brands, such as Stella Artois, Hoegaarden, and Leuvense Tripel. The city also has many traditional beer bars, where you can sample different Belgian beers and enjoy a cozy atmosphere.
  2. Jenever: A Belgian gin made with juniper berries, jenever is often served as an aperitif or after-dinner drink.
  3. Coffee: Belgium is known for its strong and rich coffee, often served with a small biscuit or piece of chocolate.
  4. Wine: Although Belgium is not known for its wine, Leuven has several wine bars and shops that offer a good selection of international wines.

Where to Stay in Leuven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Leuven has many accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxurious hotels and apartments. Here are some of the best places to stay in Leuven:

  1. Martin’s Klooster: A charming hotel located in a 16th-century monastery, Martin’s Klooster offers comfortable rooms, a beautiful courtyard, and an excellent breakfast buffet.
  2. The Lodging: A trendy and modern hostel located in the heart of Leuven, The Lodging offers stylish dorms and private rooms, a spacious common area, and a bar with an excellent beer selection.
  3. House of Today: A cozy guesthouse located in a historic building, House of Today offers beautifully decorated rooms, a shared kitchen, and a sunny terrace.
  4. Industrie Hotel: A contemporary hotel located near the train station, Industrie Hotel offers spacious and comfortable rooms, a bar and restaurant, and a fitness center.
  5. Theater Hotel Leuven: A boutique hotel located in a former theater, Theater Hotel Leuven offers elegant rooms, a bar and restaurant, and a perfect location for exploring the city.

What to Buy in Leuven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Leuven has many shops and markets that offer local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Leuven:

  1. Belgian Chocolate: A true Belgian classic, chocolate is a must-buy when visiting Leuven. Visit one of the many chocolate shops in the city to discover a wide range of chocolate products and brands.
  2. Belgian Beer: Whether you prefer blonde, double, or dark beer, Leuven has many beer shops and bars where you can buy and taste different Belgian beers.
  3. Lace: Belgium is known for its delicate and intricate lace, and Leuven has several shops that offer handmade lace products, such as doilies, tablecloths, and curtains.
  4. Speculoos: A delicious spiced biscuit that is often served with coffee or tea, speculoos makes a perfect souvenir or gift from Leuven.
  5. Flemish Art: If you’re a fan of art and culture, Leuven has many galleries and shops that sell paintings, sculptures, and other artworks by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Leuven

Belgium is a diverse and multicultural country, and Leuven is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions you may encounter during your stay in Leuven:

  1. Language: Leuven is located in Flanders, which is the Dutch-speaking part of Belgium. However, many people in Leuven also speak French, German, or English, so language should not be a barrier.
  2. Food and Drink: Belgians are known for their love of good food and drink, and Leuven is no exception. Don’t be surprised to see locals enjoying a beer or a coffee in the middle of the day, or to be offered a sweet treat by your host.
  3. Fashion: Belgium has a unique fashion scene, with many local designers and brands that are known for their avant-garde and minimalist style. If you’re a fashion enthusiast, Leuven has many boutiques and shops that offer high-quality and edgy fashion items.
  4. Music and Festivals: Belgians love music and celebrations, and Leuven has many music venues and festivals throughout the year, such as the Leuven Jazz Festival and the Marktrock Festival.

Best Time to Visit Leuven

Leuven can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit really depends on your interests and preferences. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and festivals, summer (June-August) is the best time to visit, as the weather is warm and there are many outdoor events and concerts. If you prefer fewer tourists and chilly weather, visit Leuven in winter (December-February), when the city is less crowded and the Christmas markets are in full swing. Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are also great times to visit Leuven, as the weather is mild and there are many cultural events and activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leuven

As in any destination, there are some do’s and don’ts you should keep in mind when visiting Leuven:

Do:

  1. Try Belgian beer and chocolate.
  2. Respect local customs and traditions.
  3. Rent a bike or walk to explore the city.
  4. Visit the local markets and shops.
  5. Learn a few Dutch phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  1. Drink and drive.
  2. Be loud and disrespectful in public places.
  3. Forget to tip waiters and bartenders.
  4. Forget to validate your public transport ticket.
  5. Wear inappropriate clothing in churches and other religious sites.

Safety and Health

Leuven is generally a safe city, with low crime rates and excellent healthcare facilities. However, as with any destination, be aware of pickpockets and keep an eye on your belongings. In terms of health, Belgium has a high standard of healthcare, and Leuven has many hospitals and clinics that offer emergency and non-emergency medical care. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance and take necessary precautions for common travel illnesses, such as traveler’s diarrhea.

Getting to Leuven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Leuven is easily accessible by plane, train, and bus. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Leuven

  1. Brussels Airport: Located 14 kilometers from Leuven, Brussels Airport is the main international airport in Belgium and offers many flights to European and intercontinental destinations.
  2. Brussels South Charleroi Airport: Located 70 kilometers from Leuven, Brussels South Charleroi Airport is a smaller airport that serves some low-cost airlines and regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Leuven

  1. Leuven Bus Station: Located next to the train station, Leuven Bus Station offers many national and international bus routes, operated by companies such as Flixbus and Eurolines.
  2. Brussels Bus Station: Located in the central area of Brussels, Brussels Bus Station offers many bus routes to different destinations in Belgium and Europe.

Other Options

  1. Train: Leuven has a well-connected train station that offers many national and international connections. The high-speed train (Thalys and Eurostar) connects Leuven to Paris, Amsterdam, and London, among other cities.
  2. Car: If you prefer to drive, Leuven can also be easily accessed by car, with many highways and main roads connecting it to different parts of Belgium and Europe.

How to Get Around in Leuven

Leuven is a small and compact city that can be easily explored by foot or bike. The city has many bike rental shops, and there are also several public transportation options, such as buses and trains. If you prefer to take a taxi, there are several taxi companies in Leuven that offer reliable and affordable services. Make sure to download a transportation app (such as Moovit or De Lijn) to navigate through the city and find the best transportation options for your needs.

Where to Go Next from Leuven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Leuven is a great base to explore other parts of Belgium and the Netherlands. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Leuven:

  1. Brussels: The capital of Belgium, Brussels is only a 20-minute train ride from Leuven, and offers many attractions, such as the Grand Place, the Atomium, and the European Parliament.
  2. Mechelen: A charming and historic city, Mechelen is only a 15-minute train ride from Leuven, and offers many sights, such as the St. Rumbold’s Cathedral and the Palace of Margaret of Austria.
  3. Antwerp: Known for its vibrant fashion and art scenes, Antwerp is only a 45-minute train ride from Leuven, and offers many attractions, such as the Museum Aan de Stroom and the Fashion Museum.
  4. Rotterdam: A modern and multicultural city in the Netherlands, Rotterdam is only a 1.5-hour train ride from Leuven, and offers many sights, such as the Erasmus Bridge, the Markthal, and the Maritime Museum.

Conclusion

Leuven is a charming and lively city that offers plenty of attractions, activities, and gastronomic delights for travelers of all ages and interests. From its medieval landmarks to its modern street art scene, Leuven has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has given you the necessary information and inspiration to plan your trip to Leuven. Enjoy your stay!
