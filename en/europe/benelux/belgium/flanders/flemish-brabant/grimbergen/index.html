Introduction to Grimbergen

Grimbergen is a small yet culturally rich town located in Flemish Brabant, in Flanders, Belgium. This hidden gem is best known for its beautiful and historic buildings, charming streets, and delicious beer. Grimbergen is surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills, providing visitors with an endless array of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Grimbergen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town is home to some of the most significant historical landmarks and museums in the country. These include the beautiful Benedictine Abbey of Grimbergen, the Saint Servatius Church, and the Museum of the Belgian Brewers. The ancient Benedictine Abbey of Grimbergen is a must-visit attraction for history lovers. The building dates back to the 12th century and houses a beautiful baroque church, an old brewery, a peaceful garden, and a museum that showcases the rich history of the abbey and its inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Grimbergen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Travelers looking for outdoor adventures will find plenty of opportunities in and around Grimbergen. The town is surrounded by lush forests and hills, offering some breathtaking scenic views.
The Tervuren Park is a hidden gem that welcomes visitors with 200 hectares of lush greenery, serene lakes, unbelievable walking and cycling trails for nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grimbergen

Grimbergen offers an array of trails for both hiking and biking trails. Heading towards the hills, visitors can explore the nature reserve of Het Broek, just north of the town. The forest offers a range of forest paths, ensuring nature lovers’ unforgettable experience.

Best Things to Do in Grimbergen: Activities and Tours

The town offers a plethora of must-do activities like brewery tours, bike riding, hiking, and picnic in the nearby St. Annadal Park. One can experience Beer tasting in the Brasserie de Lindemans, a brewery located to the south of town. It was developed in the early 1800s for Lambic beer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grimbergen

A week-long itinerary for Grimbergen is the best way to explore all the town has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary that includes the must-visit attractions, activities, and dining experiences:

Day Itinerary
1 Visit the Benedictine Abbey of Grimbergen
2 Explore the Tervuren Park for walking/cycling trails and museums
3 Visit the Saint Servatius Church, followed by lunch at the nearby local Brasserie
4 Participate in a bike tour/class
5 Visit the charming town of Mechelen
6 Go on a brewery tour of Brasserie de Lindemans
7 Relax or explore more before heading back home

Food and Drink Scene of Grimbergen

Must-Try Dishes

Grimbergen has many delicious dishes to offer, from local specialities to the most authentic Belgian beer. Some dishes to try while in town include traditional Flemish Beef Stew served with locally baked bread or the Region’s most loved Belgian Fries.

Local Beverages

Grimbergen is famous for its beer produced in the Benedictine Abbey. One can enjoy the authentic Grimbergen beer at the town’s local restaurants, pubs, or take a brewery tour to learn more about how it’s made and the history behind it.

Where to Stay in Grimbergen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grimbergen offers various accommodation options to stay at, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels, but if you are looking for something unique, try the charming B&B ‘The Oceanside’ that offers free bikes for guests to ride around town.

What to Buy in Grimbergen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The town has several souvenir and local shops where you can find various Belgian specialties. Some of these include Belgian Chocolate at House of Belgium-MysteryChocolateBox, Belgian Waffles, Belgian beers to take back home, and delicious Belgium Cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Grimbergen

Belgium is predominantly Catholic, and it is common to observe traditional Catholic customs in the town. The locals celebrate traditional Catholic events like the carnival season, Christmas, Easter, and Saint Nicholas Day, among others.

Best Time to Visit Grimbergen

The best time to visit Grimbergen is from April to June and September to October, offering delightful weather and avoiding the summer’s peak tourism. The months of May and July offer the town’s beautiful flower parks and festivals that attract locals and tourists alike.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grimbergen

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the Genuine Grimbergen Beer
  • Take a guided tour of the Benedictine Abbey and other historical sites
  • Venture out and explore the town on foot or by bike

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in public places
  • Consume alcohol in non-designated areas
  • Disturb the local wildlife
  • Smoke in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Grimbergen

Grimbergen is a safe town with a low crime rate, and visitors can feel at ease walking around the town’s streets. As with any travel destination, it is advised to be cautious with your belongings in crowded areas, especially on public transport.

Health in Grimbergen

Visitors can receive medical assistance at the many clinics and pharmacies located around town. It is advisable to carry your medication and travel health insurance.

Getting to Grimbergen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Grimbergen has excellent connectivity from Brussels, Belgium, and other neighboring countries like the Netherlands and Germany.

Airports Near Grimbergen

The nearest airport to Grimbergen is Brussels International Airport, which is just a 20-minute drive away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Grimbergen

Grimbergen is connected by the De Lijn bus service, with various buses reaching the town center from Different Belgian Cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also ride the train that connects Grimbergen from Brussels and from the northern part of the country.

How to Get Around in Grimbergen

The townis small enough to explore on foot, but renting a bike is the best mode of transport to explore the local area. They can be rented at the Hotel Abbey, which is located beside the Benedictine Abbey. Additionally, buses and taxis are available at reasonable rates.

Where to Go Next from Grimbergen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Apart from Grimbergen, there are several other destinations to explore in the surrounding areas. Some popular options include Brussels, Mechelen, Antwerp and Ghent.

Conclusion

Grimbergen is a hidden gem in Belgium that has plenty to offer. With its historical landmarks, beautiful scenery, delicious food/beer, and charming streets, there is something for everyone in this town. Whether you want an outdoor adventure or wish to explore the town’s rich culture and history, Grimbergen is an ideal destination to discover. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip and enjoy it to the fullest.
