Introduction to Tervuren

Tervuren, a municipality in the Flemish Brabant province of Flanders region, Belgium, welcomes travelers with its beautiful countryside, historical landmarks, and cultural attractions. Located only 20 minutes from Brussels, Tervuren is a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city center. The town is popular for its verdant parks, picturesque gardens, and hiking/biking trails that offer scenic views. Whether you’re a history buff, an art aficionado, a nature lover, or a food enthusiast, Tervuren has something that will delight you. Join us on a journey through Tervuren and discover the best places to visit, things to do, and see in this charming Belgian town.

List of Places to Visit in Tervuren

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tervuren boasts of several well-preserved historical landmarks and cultural institutions that offer a glimpse into its rich past and vibrant present. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

Royal Museum for Central Africa

The Royal Museum for Central Africa (RMCA) is a fascinating museum that offers a glimpse into the history of the Congo and its people. The museum is housed in a grand neoclassical building that was constructed in 1904 and is set in a lush green park. The museum’s collections range from ethnographic objects, natural specimens, and historical artefacts to contemporary art inspired by African culture.

Park of Tervuren and Castle Van’t Hoog

The Park of Tervuren is a vast public park teeming with greenery that spreads over 200 hectares. The park is home to Castle Van’t Hoog, a beautiful 12th-century castle that stands on the site of a medieval fortress. The castle was extensively renovated in the 19th century and transformed into an elegant residence for King Leopold II. The castle now houses the Museum of Fine Arts, which showcases an impressive collection of Flemish painters.

Tervuren War Museum

The Tervuren War Museum is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts. Housed in an old mansion, the museum houses exhibits that depict various aspects of Belgian and European military history. The displays include uniforms, weapons, and equipment, and the museum provides visitors with an insight into the lives of soldiers and civilians during the Second World War.

Natural Attractions of Tervuren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tervuren is a paradise for nature lovers, thanks to its scenic parks, forests, and outdoor recreation areas. Here are some of the natural attractions you can explore in Tervuren:

Arboretum Tervuren

The Arboretum Tervuren is a beautiful botanical garden that showcases flora from around the world. The garden spans over 92 hectares and offers visitors the opportunity to walk on scenic trails while admiring the beauty of the plants. The garden is home to over 4000 tree species and shrubs, including rare and endangered species.

Forêt de Soignes

The Forêt de Soignes, or the Sonian Forest, is a vast woodland that stretches over 4400 hectares between Brussels and Tervuren. The forest is home to many species of flora and fauna and is a popular spot for hiking, jogging, and cycling. The forest trails are well-maintained and offer some excellent scenic views.

Solvay Park

Located in the heart of Tervuren, Solvay Park is a vast public park known for its beautiful rose garden and scenic pond. The park is a popular spot for picnicking, jogging, and cycling, and offers visitors a peaceful retreat in the heart of the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tervuren

Tervuren is a paradise for hikers and bikers who love to explore nature while keeping fit. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Tervuren:

  • The Arboretum Tervuren Trail: This 7.5km trail takes hikers through the beautiful Arboretum Tervuren and showcases its large collection of trees and plants.
  • The Park of Tervuren Trail: This 14km trail is perfect for advanced hikers who want to explore the vast Park of Tervuren and take in its natural beauty.
  • The Forêt de Soignes Trail: This 30km trail takes hikers through the beautiful Sonian Forest and offers some breathtaking scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Tervuren: Activities and Tours

Tervuren is a fantastic town to explore for travelers who like to keep busy. Here are some of the top things to do in Tervuren:

  • Visit the Royal Museum for Central Africa and learn about the Congo’s history and culture.
  • Explore the beautiful Park of Tervuren and visit the Museum of Fine Arts at Castle Van’t Hoog.
  • Hike or bike through the scenic trails of the Forêt de Soignes.
  • Take a guided tour of Tervuren and learn about its rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Visit the Tervuren War Museum and discover the role Belgium played in World War II.
  • Attend a cultural event or festival in Tervuren and experience its vibrant arts scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Tervuren

Tervuren has a fantastic food and drink scene, thanks to its vibrant culinary culture and Belgian cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Tervuren:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stoemp: A traditional Belgian dish made of mashed potatoes and vegetables and served with sausages or meat.
  • Carbonnade Flamande: A beef stew cooked with onions and beer and served with crispy fries.
  • Tervuren Hotpot: A hearty meat stew made with local beef, vegetables, and beer.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beer: Belgium is famous for its excellent beer, and Tervuren has plenty of local breweries that serve high-quality beer.
  • Jenever: A traditional Belgian spirit distilled from juniper berries.

Local Culture and Customs of Tervuren

Belgium has a rich cultural heritage, and Tervuren is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions you should know when visiting Tervuren:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Tipping is not mandatory, but it is customary to leave a small amount for good service.
  • Try to speak a few words of Dutch, the local language, when communicating with locals.

Best Time to Visit Tervuren

The best time to visit Tervuren is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Tervuren’s beauty can be enjoyed all year round, and the town’s museums and attractions are open throughout the year.

When visiting Tervuren, here are some things you should and shouldn’t do:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Do visit the Royal Museum for Central Africa.
  • Do explore the beautiful parks and forests.

Safety and Health

Tervuren is a safe and healthy town for tourists to visit. However, it’s always essential to take precautions and follow basic safety guidelines. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Tervuren:

Safety in Tervuren

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuables.
  • Use a money belt or purse to keep your valuables safe.
  • Only use licensed taxis or reliable transportation services.

Health in Tervuren

  • Belgian tap water is safe to drink, and there are no specific health concerns to be aware of.
  • Make sure to carry any necessary medications with you and have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Tervuren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tervuren is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options for getting to Tervuren:

Airports Near Tervuren

Brussels International Airport (BRU) is the nearest airport to Tervuren, and it’s only a 20-minute drive away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or public transport to reach Tervuren.

Bus Stations Near Tervuren

The town is well-connected by public transport, and you can take a bus or tram from Brussels to reach Tervuren. The journey takes around 30 minutes.

Other Options

You can also reach Tervuren by car or bike. The town is well-connected by roads and cycling paths and is easily accessible from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Tervuren

Tervuren is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. You can also rent a bike or take public transport to get around. The town has a reliable bus and tram service that connects to nearby cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Tervuren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Belgium, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Tervuren:

  • Brussels: The capital of Belgium is only a 20-minute drive away from Tervuren and is home to many world-famous attractions such as the Atomium, Grand Place, and Manneken Pis.
  • Leuven: A vibrant university town famous for its beautiful Gothic architecture and beer culture, located 20 minutes away by train from Tervuren.
  • Mechelen: A beautiful medieval town located 30 minutes away from Tervuren, known for its historic buildings, museums, and festivals.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Tervuren is a small town that packs a punch in terms of history, culture, and natural beauty. From the lush parks and gardens to the well-preserved historical landmarks, Tervuren offers visitors an opportunity to explore the best of Belgium’s rich heritage.
