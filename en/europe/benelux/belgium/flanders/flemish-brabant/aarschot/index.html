Introduction to Aarschot

Located in Flemish Brabant, in Flanders, Belgium, Aarschot is a small town known for its rich history and vibrant culture. Nestled at the confluence of three rivers, Aarschot is famous for its stunning countryside, medieval architecture, and outdoor adventures.

In this guide, we will explore all the top attractions, best things to do, local culture, and safety tips to help you plan a perfect trip to Aarschot.

List of Places to Visit in Aarschot

Aarschot is home to a plethora of attractions, from ancient castles and museums to scenic countryside trails and gastronomic delights. Here are some of the top places to visit in Aarschot.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Aarschot Beguinage: The UNESCO World Heritage-listed Aarschot Beguinage is a beautiful medieval complex built in the 13th century, featuring a tranquil courtyard surrounded by charming houses, a church, and a museum.
  2. Castle van Horst: A stunning 15th-century moated castle famed for its haunted history, Castle van Horst features a collection of antique furniture, paintings, and tapestries, as well as a beautiful garden and orchard.
  3. Our Lady Church: Established in the 15th century, the majestic Our Lady Church is a beautiful example of Gothic architecture, featuring stunning stained glass windows, intricate carvings, and a towering bell tower.

Natural Attractions of Aarschot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Aarschot Nature Reserve: Spread over 38 hectares of pristine natural beauty, the Aarschot Nature Reserve is a biodiverse hotspot famous for its marshes, meadows, ponds, and hiking trails.
  2. The Demer Valley: Stretching across 100 kilometers, the scenic Demer Valley is a stunning landscape of rolling hills, verdant forests, and idyllic villages, ideal for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.
  3. Aarschot Marina: Situated on the banks of the Demer River, the picturesque Aarschot Marina is a paradise for water sports enthusiasts, offering boating, fishing, kayaking, and windsurfing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aarschot

Aarschot boasts numerous hiking and biking trails that take visitors through some of the most scenic countryside in Belgium. Here are some of the best routes to explore.

  1. Diest-Aarschot Trail: The 20-kilometer Diest-Aarschot Trail is a stunning route that takes visitors through lush countryside vistas, ancient forests, and charming villages.
  2. Hagelandse Heuvelroute: The picturesque Hagelandse Heuvelroute is a 55-kilometer uphill and downhill trail that offers stunning panoramas of the Demer Valley and the Hageland hills.
  3. Abbey Trail: The historic Abbey Trail is a 12-kilometer route that reveals the town’s cultural heritage, passing by ancient abbeys, churches, and castles.

Best Things to Do in Aarschot: Activities and Tours

Apart from enjoying the local attractions, there are plenty of exciting activities to explore in Aarschot. Here are some of the best things to do in Aarschot.

  1. Beer Tasting Tour: Belgium is famous for its beer, so why not enjoy a beer tasting tour in Aarschot, featuring a visit to the town’s breweries and taprooms, and delicious beer samples.
  2. Music Festival: Aarschot hosts various music festivals throughout the year, showcasing a diverse range of music genres, from heavy metal to folk and jazz.
  3. Christmas Market: The Aarschot Christmas Market is a festive event held in the town center, offering an array of Christmas-themed stalls, delicious food, and fun activities for all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Aarschot

Belgium is famous for its chocolate, waffles, and beer, and Aarschot is no exception. Here are some of the best local dishes and drinks to try in Aarschot.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stoofvlees: A delicious Flemish beef and beer stew served with fries.
  2. Waterzooi: A creamy Flemish stew made with chicken or fish, vegetables, and herbs.
  3. Croquettes: Deep-fried crispy balls stuffed with mashed potatoes, cheese, and ham.

Local Beverages

  1. Belgian beer: Aarschot and its surrounding regions are famous for their diverse range of Belgian beers, including the famous Trappist beers, Pils, and Pale Ales.
  2. Jenever: Also known as Flemish gin, Jenever is a local spirit made of juniper berries, herbs, and spices.
  3. Belgian Chocolate: Aarschot has several artisanal chocolate shops that produce some of the best Belgian chocolates in the country.

Where to Stay in Aarschot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aarschot has several accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Aarschot.

  1. ‘S Hertogenmolens Hotel: A beautiful historic hotel located in a converted mill, set in a tranquil location near the Beguinage.
  2. Hotel Pluimpapaver: A charming family-run hotel near the town center, featuring cozy rooms and continental breakfast.
  3. B&B Daya: A peaceful B&B set in a lush garden, offering comfortable rooms, hearty breakfast, and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Aarschot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aarschot has plenty of shopping options, featuring everything from artisanal chocolates, local beer, handicrafts, and souvenirs. Here are the top places to shop in Aarschot.

  1. Chocolatier Van Puyvelde: An artisanal chocolate shop that produces some of the best Belgian chocolates in the region, featuring unique flavors and designs.
  2. ‘De Koffiepot’: A cozy café that sells a broad range of coffee beans, tea, and local delicacies.
  3. Vierhoekhoeve: A dairy farm that produces organic milk, cheese, and ice cream, featuring a farm shop and a petting zoo.

Local Culture and Customs of Aarschot

Belgium is a multicultural country, and Aarschot is no exception. Here are some of the local customs, traditions, and festivals to expect in Aarschot.

  1. Flemish Culture: Aarschot is an integral part of the Flemish region, featuring a unique dialect, cuisine, and architecture that reflect the region’s culture.
  2. Carnaval: Aarschot celebrates Carnaval, a traditional festival featuring costumes, music, and dancing, held in late February or early March.
  3. Beer Culture: Beer is an essential part of Belgian culture, and Aarschot has several breweries, taprooms, and beer festivals that celebrate the region’s beer heritage.

Best Time to Visit Aarschot

The best time to visit Aarschot is from April to October, offering warm weather, blooming flowers, and outdoor activities. However, the town is also charming during the Christmas season, featuring festive markets and decorations.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting Aarschot.

Safety in Aarschot

Aarschot is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take standard precautions to avoid any mishap, such as carrying a copy of their passports, keeping essential items in a secure place, and avoiding secluded or dimly lit areas at night.

Health in Aarschot

Aarschot has adequate medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses and any unexpected incidents.

Getting to Aarschot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aarschot is well-connected to other Belgian and European cities, offering various transportation options.

Airports Near Aarschot

The nearest international airport to Aarschot is Brussels Airport, located 40 kilometers away. Visitors can take a train, bus, or taxi to reach Aarschot town center.

Bus Stations Near Aarschot

Aarschot has a bus station located in the town center, offering bus services to other Belgian and European destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Aarschot via train, car, or bike, depending on their preference and budget.

How to Get Around in Aarschot

Aarschot is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, bike, or car. However, visitors can also use local buses or hire taxis to travel around the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Aarschot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aarschot is surrounded by several charming towns and cities that are worth a visit, including:

  • Leuven: Just 10 kilometers away, Leuven is a vibrant city famous for its stunning Gothic architecture, ancient abbeys, and prestigious university.
  • Mechelen: Located 25 kilometers away, Mechelen is a beautiful city famed for its historic landmarks, quaint market squares, and delicious food.
  • Brussels: The Belgian capital is located 40 kilometers away, featuring world-class museums, art galleries, restaurants, and cultural attractions.
