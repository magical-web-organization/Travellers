Introduction to Keerbergen

Keerbergen is a small municipality in Flemish Brabant, located in the north-central part of Belgium. While it may be a lesser-known destination, Keerbergen is a hidden gem waiting to be explored.

The village boasts a beautiful natural landscape, charming historical sites, bustling local markets, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, local culture, or history, Keerbergen is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Belgium.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do and see, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and more in Keerbergen, Belgium.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Keerbergen has its own share of historical landmarks and museums, including:

  • The Tafelrond
  • The Groot Begijnhof
  • The Begijnhofkerk
  • The M-Museum

  • De Beeltjens

Best Things to Do in Keerbergen: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider during your visit to Keerbergen:

  • Take a guided walking tour of the village
  • Visit the local markets and sample local produce
  • Participate in a cooking class and learn to make Belgian dishes
  • Go on a bike tour of the countryside
  • Take a boat ride on Keerbergen Lake

Week-Long Itinerary for Keerbergen

To make the most of your time in Keerbergen, consider the following week-long itinerary:

  • Day One: Arrive in Keerbergen, relax and explore the local area.
  • Day Two: Take a guided tour of the village, visiting the Tafelrond and Begijnhofkerk.
  • Day Three: Go on a bike tour of the countryside and visit the Provincial Groene Gordel Hiking Trail.
  • Day Four: Participate in a cooking class and learn to make Belgian dishes.
  • Day Five: Take a boat ride on Keerbergen Lake and visit the M-Museum.
  • Day Six: Visit Boortmeerbeekse Vaart and De Beeltjens for some outdoor activities.
  • Day Seven: Spend your final day in Keerbergen exploring the local markets and sampling local produce before departing.

The local culture and customs of Keerbergen are influenced by Flemish customs, traditions, and way of life. Locals in Keerbergen are friendly and welcoming to tourists, and visitors are always encouraged to try out local customs and traditions.
