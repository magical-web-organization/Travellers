Introduction to Lochristi

Nestled in the East Flanders region of Belgium lies the picturesque town of Lochristi. Despite its small size, Lochristi is renowned for its cultural significance and natural beauty. The town is dotted with tastefully restored historic buildings, museums, and verdant parks. This comprehensive tour guide will take you through everything you need to know about Lochristi, including its top places to visit, hiking and biking trails, things to do, where to stay, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, the best time to visit, how to get there and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Lochristi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum van de Vlaamse Molen en de Voeding

Museum van de Vlaamse Molen en de Voeding is a fascinating museum that showcases the milling and food culture in Flanders. The museum’s exhibitions not only deal with the history of flour mills in the area but also provide visitors with a glimpse into the town’s culinary heritage.

Begijnhof

Begijnhof, located in the heart of Lochristi, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. This beautifully preserved historic site was built in the 1800s. It consists of a collection of houses, churches, and convents, all set behind a fortified wall. While the buildings have been updated to suit modern needs, their historic character has remained unspoiled.

Castle of Beervelde

The Castle of Beervelde, a 19th-century castle located in the heart of the town, boasts magnificent architecture and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. It is known for hosting a well-known garden fair each year. The Castle of Beervelde is surrounded by lush parks which are ideal for relaxing and enjoying a picnic.

Natural Attractions of Lochristi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Puyenbroeck Provincial Domain

The Puyenbroeck Provincial Domain is a spacious outdoor recreational area and boasts extensive green spaces that are perfect for picnics and outdoor activities. The park has several lakes, gardens, and forests, and kids will enjoy the playgrounds, swimming pools, and mini-golf. Adults will appreciate the walking and running trails, bike paths, and tennis courts.

The Verbrugghen Mill

The Verbrugghen Mill, set in Beervelde park, is well-known for its picturesque setting. The mill is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and jogging. You can enjoy the crisp, clean air as you take in the stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lochristi

There are several hiking and biking trails around the Puyenbroeck Provincial Domain in Lochristi. For a serene stroll, take the ‘Natuurwandeling’ route that will take you through the picturesque landscapes of the Provincial Domain. The ‘Eendenkooiwandeling’ route takes you around the estate’s seven-acre shelter for wildfowl, while the ‘Rode and Gele Route’ will take you through the enchanting Beervelde woods.

Best Things to Do in Lochristi: Activities and Tours

Garden Fair

The annual Garden Fair at the Castle of Beervelde is a much-awaited event. Over 200 carefully chosen horticulturalists display their stunning collection of ornamental plants and garden flowers during the three-day event. The garden fair is a perfect opportunity to sample delicious food, admire beautiful nature, and shop for unique garden accessories and tools.

Visit the Monday Market

If you’re visiting Lochristi on a Monday, make sure to check out the local market. The streets swell with people shopping for fresh vegetables, fruits, and dairy products, along with a host of other items sold by local vendors.

Thermen Katara Beauty and Wellness Centre

The Thermen Katara Wellness Centre offers nine different types of sauna, aromatherapy baths, and a range of beauty treatments that will tempt even the most scrupulous culture vulture. You’ll come away feeling replete, refreshed and rejuvenated.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lochristi

Day 1 - Visit the Castle of Beervelde and enjoy the lush parks in its surrounding area.

Day 2 – Explore the local museum, Museum van de Vlaamse Molen en de Voeding.

Day 3 – Take a relaxing swim in the facilities of the Puyenbroeck Provincial Domain.

Day 4 – Take a refreshing hike through the local woods and nature reserve.

Day 5 – Shop at the local market and sample local cuisine.

Day 6 – Visit the Thermen Katara wellness centre and indulge in a spa treatment.

Day 7 – Take a bike ride along one of the many scenic trails around Lochristi.

Food and Drink Scene of Lochristi

Must-Try Dishes

Waterzooi

A local favorite, Waterzooi is a traditional stew made with fish or poultry, eggs, and cream, served with potatoes.

Kroakemandels

Kroakemandels, or ‘crack almonds,’ are a traditional Belgian sweet treat made with almonds, syrup, and sugar.

Local Beverages

Belgian Beer

Belgium is home to some of the world’s best beer, and Lochristi is no exception. The town has a rich history when it comes to brewing beer, and today it boasts several well-known breweries and pubs.

Where to Stay in Lochristi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Den Hof

Hotel Den Hof is a four-star hotel located in the heart of Lochristi. Its rooms are spacious and equipped with modern amenities.

B&B ‘t Mirakel

B&B ‘t Mirakel is located in the countryside just outside of Lochristi. The guesthouse offers a cozy, friendly, and serene ambiance for those who prefer to stay in a tranquil countryside setting.

What to Buy in Lochristi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several unique local products to buy in Lochristi, including handmade rugs, linens, and pottery. You can also find craft beers, artisan chocolate, and local jams and cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Lochristi

Lochristi is steeped in history and home to many local traditions. Every year, on the third Saturday of September, the town hosts its popular beer festival, while the local market square is home to regular events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Lochristi

The best time to visit Lochristi is in the summer months when the weather is mild and the town is adorned with flowering plants and blossoming trees. The most significant festivals take place in early autumn and early winter, which is also a great time to visit Lochristi.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lochristi

Do:

  • Try the local beer.
  • Respect local customs, particularly around religious events or places of worship.
  • Try the local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Scatter litter in the streets or parks.
  • Take photographs of people or objects without permission.
  • Be disrespectful to others at cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lochristi

Lochristi is generally safe, and most visitors experience few problems. Always be aware of your surroundings and belongings, and do not leave personal items unattended.

Health in Lochristi

Lochristi has several well-equipped hospitals and clinics, and pharmacies are readily available. Make sure to carry your health insurance card, and take any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Lochristi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lochristi

The nearest airport to Lochristi is Brussels Airport, which is approximately 60 km away.

Bus Stations Near Lochristi

The town is serviced by several bus routes, with the Lochristi-Hijfte train station being the main hub for public transport.

Other Options

Lochristi is also easily accessible by car, as it is located close to Belgium’s main highways.

How to Get Around in Lochristi

Lochristi is an ideal town to explore on foot, but if you prefer to cycle, there are several bike rental services available. Buses also service the town, and taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Lochristi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several neighboring towns and attractions to visit from Lochristi, including the historic city of Antwerp, which is known for its art and cultural heritage, and the charming city of Gent, which offers stunning canals and Gothic architecture.

Conclusion

This comprehensive guide to Lochristi has provided all the information you need to plan your visit to this charming town, from its top places to visit, where to stay, hiking trails, and local customs. With its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, Lochristi is sure to delight visitors looking for a serene escape.
