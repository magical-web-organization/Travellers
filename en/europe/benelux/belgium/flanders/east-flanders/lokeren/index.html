Introduction to Lokeren

Located in East Flanders, near the border of the Netherlands, Lokeren is a charming town that often gets overlooked by travelers. However, those who do visit are rewarded with a quaint ambiance, a rich history, and plenty of outdoor adventures to explore. Known for its annual Lokerse Feesten and its famous caramel-filled sweets, Lokeren is a hidden gem of Belgium that is definitely worth a visit.

List of Places to Visit in Lokeren

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lokeren is home to several historical buildings and museums that offer a glimpse into the town’s past. The Lokeren Museum, located in the heart of the city, showcases the evolution of Lokeren from a small town to a bustling metropolis.

Meanwhile, the Louis Paul Boon Centre is dedicated to the famous Belgian writer Louis Paul Boon, and houses various exhibits on his life and works.

Finally, the beautiful gothic-style Saint Lawrence Church is a must-visit, with its stunning stained glass windows and breathtaking architecture.

Natural Attractions of Lokeren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lokeren also boasts several natural attractions that are worth exploring. The Molsbroek nature reserve, a protected wetland area, is an ideal spot for birdwatching and hiking. Meanwhile, the Daknam Stadium offers a calm and serene atmosphere for a stroll or jog, with scenic views to be admired.

Finally, the nearby De Ster Recreation Park offers a range of outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, and cycling, and is a hit among families and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lokeren

If you’re looking for an active way to explore Lokeren, the town’s hiking and biking routes are a perfect option. The Lokeren Cycle Route takes you on a scenic tour through the town and its surrounding areas. Along the way, you’ll discover the town’s hidden gems, including parks, museums, and historical sites.

Meanwhile, the Lokerse Moors Hiking Trail offers beautiful views of the Molsbroek Nature Reserve and other scenic areas of the town, with plenty of opportunities for birdwatching and nature photography.

Best Things to Do in Lokeren: Activities and Tours

For those seeking unique experiences, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. One popular option is a boat tour of the Lokeren Canal, which provides a lovely opportunity to take in the town’s architecture and landscapes from the water.

Alternatively, you can take a guided walking tour of the town, learning about its history and culture. Don’t forget to try some of the local caramel-filled sweets during your tour!

Week-Long Itinerary for Lokeren

If you’re planning to spend a week or more in Lokeren, there’s no shortage of things to do. Our recommended itinerary includes:

  • Day 1: Visit the Lokeren Museum and Louis Paul Boon Centre
  • Day 2: Explore the town’s sights on foot, and take a boat tour of the Lokeren Canal.
  • Day 3: Hike the Lokerse Moors Hiking Trail and explore the Molsbroek Nature Reserve
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Ghent, known for its stunning architecture and medieval history
  • Day 5: Visit the Saint Lawrence Church and the De Ster Recreation Park
  • Day 6: Take a guided tour of the town, and enjoy some local cuisine.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind with a stroll or bike ride along the town’s scenic routes.

Food and Drink Scene of Lokeren

Must-Try Dishes

Lokeren is famous for its caramel-filled sweets, but the town also has a thriving food scene. One must-try dish is the Lokerse Paardenworst, a type of sausage made from horse meat that’s cooked with onions and served with mustard and bread.

Another local delicacy is the Lokerse Vlaai, a sweet tart made with local cherries and crème fraîche.

Local Beverages

Belgium is known for its beer, and Lokeren is no exception. The town’s local breweries produce a range of delicious brews that you won’t want to miss. Try the De Klok Brewery’s Lokerse Paasbier, a seasonal beer available during Easter, or the De Moersleutel Brewery’s Kenzaki Yone, a delicious Japanese-style beer.

Where to Stay in Lokeren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options to choose from in Lokeren, depending on your budget and preferences. Some popular choices include the Hotel La Barakka, a charming guesthouse located in the town center, as well as the Hotel Eremytenhof, a luxurious option with beautiful gardens and a wellness center.

For budget-friendly travelers, the Hostellerie De Neerhof offers affordable hostel-style accommodation with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Lokeren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

In addition to the town’s caramel-filled sweets, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home from Lokeren. One popular option is the town’s famous horse sausages, which can be found in local markets and shops.

Alternatively, the De Klok Brewery produces a range of delicious beers and beer-related items, perfect for beer enthusiasts. Finally, be sure to grab some local cherries or cherry jam, a staple crop of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Lokeren

Belgium is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, and Lokeren is no exception. The town’s residents often speak both Flemish and French, so be sure to brush up on a few key phrases before your trip.

Additionally, tipping is expected in restaurants and cafes, typically around 10% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Lokeren

The best time to visit Lokeren is during the summer months, from May to September. This is when the town’s outdoor activities and events are in full swing, and the weather is ideal for exploring. Be sure to check the town’s event calendar before your trip to make the most of your visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lokeren

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including horse sausage and caramel-filled sweets.
  • Explore the town’s natural areas, including the Molsbroek Nature Reserve and the De Ster Recreation Park.
  • Visit the town’s museums and historical sites to learn about Lokeren’s past.
  • Take a boat tour of the Lokeren Canal for a unique perspective on the town.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring comfortable shoes for exploring the town on foot.
  • Miss out on the town’s outdoor activities, including hiking and cycling.
  • Be afraid to ask locals for suggestions on what to see and do in Lokeren.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lokeren

Lokeren is generally a safe town for travelers, with low crime rates and good infrastructure. However, as with any destination, visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone at night, and keep an eye on their belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Lokeren

Belgium has a high standard of healthcare, with quality medical facilities located throughout the country. In case of emergency, visitors can dial 112 for assistance. It’s also recommended that travelers have comprehensive health insurance before their trip to cover any unexpected medical costs.

Getting to Lokeren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lokeren

Brussels Airport is the closest major airport to Lokeren, located approximately 70 kilometers away. Alternatively, Amsterdam Schiphol Airport is approximately 200 kilometers away, but offers more flight options for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Lokeren

Lokeren is well-connected by bus to other major destinations in Belgium. The town’s main bus station is located in the town center, and buses run regularly to and from nearby cities like Ghent, Brussels, and Antwerp.

Other Options

Additionally, trains run regularly to and from Lokeren to other major cities in the region. If you’re driving, the town is located near major highways and is easily accessible from other parts of Europe.

How to Get Around in Lokeren

Lokeren is a small town that can easily be explored on foot, but there are other transportation options available as well. Bicycles are a popular choice, either by renting them at a local bike shop or bringing your own.

Additionally, buses run regularly to and from the town center to nearby areas, including the Molsbroek Nature Reserve and the De Ster Recreation Park.

Where to Go Next from Lokeren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belgium is a small country with plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Ghent, located approximately 15 kilometers away, is a popular choice for travelers, known for its charming streets and medieval architecture.

Meanwhile, the larger cities of Brussels and Antwerp are both within easy reach, offering plenty of historic landmarks, cultural attractions, and nightlife options.

Conclusion

From hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical sites, Lokeren has plenty to offer visitors looking for a unique Belgian experience. With a combination of natural beauty and rich culture, this hidden gem is a must-visit destination.
