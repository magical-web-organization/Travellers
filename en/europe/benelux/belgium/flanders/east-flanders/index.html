Introduction to East Flanders

East Flanders is a beautiful province located in the northern region of Belgium. It is a place where rich history, contemporary culture, and beautiful landscapes combine to offer visitors an unforgettable experience. From charming cities, picturesque countryside, to world-class museums, and delicious food, East Flanders has plenty to offer.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to East Flanders, including top places to visit, must-try dishes, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in East Flanders

East Flanders is home to many beautiful places, ranging from charming cities to picturesque countryside locations. Here are some of the top places to visit in East Flanders:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saint Bavo’s Cathedral, Ghent
  • Gravensteen, Ghent
  • Ghent Altarpiece, Ghent
  • Museum of Fine Arts, Ghent
  • Ghent City Museum, Ghent
  • Castle of Laarne, Laarne

Natural Attractions of East Flanders: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bourgoyen-Ossemeersen Nature Reserve, Ghent
  • Scheldemeersen Nature Reserve, Oudenaarde
  • Koppenberg, Oudenaarde
  • Kluisbos, Kluisbergen

Best Things to Do in East Flanders: Activities and Tours

East Flanders offers visitors plenty of activities and tours to fill their itinerary. Here some of the best things to do in East Flanders:

  • Take a boat ride along the canals of Ghent
  • Admire the stunning art at the Museum of Fine Arts, Ghent
  • Visit the Ghent Christmas Market
  • Go on a cycling tour of the Flemish Ardennes
  • Go on a brewery tour and try the local beer
  • Discover the medieval city of Bruges, which is just a short drive away.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Flanders

For those that have a more extended stay, here is a one-week itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival, explore the city of Ghent, visit the Saint Bavo’s Cathedral and the Gravensteen
Day 2 Take a cycling tour of the Flemish Ardennes
Day 3 Visit Oudenaarde, and see the Scheldt River
Day 4 Explore Laarne and visit the Castle of Laarne
Day 5 Spend the day at Donkmeer, and discover the town of Berlare
Day 6 Visit Kluisbergen and go hiking in the Kluisbos
Day 7 Visit the Ghent Christmas Market before departure

Local Culture and Customs of East Flanders

The people of East Flanders are warm and welcoming and are proud of their cultural heritage. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home
  • Respect the country’s bilingual status and try to learn some French and Dutch phrases.
  • Belgians tend to be punctual, so arrive on time for all appointments.
  • Do not litter and dispose of waste products properly.

Best Time to Visit East Flanders

The best time to visit East Flanders is during the spring and summer months when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The shoulder seasons of fall and winter also offer a unique perspective to the region’s beauty. The Christmas market in Ghent, which opens in late November each year, is also a must-visit event.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Flanders

Do:

  • Be respectful of cultural customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Carry cash, as some places may not accept credit cards.
  • Learn some basic Dutch phrases.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Get too drunk in public
  • Litter and damage the environment
  • Talk loudly in public

Safety and Health

Safety in East Flanders

East Flanders is a safe place to visit, and there are no significant safety concerns for tourists. However, as with any destination, it is always essential to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always keep your valuables such as passport, money, and electronics, safe and secured.
  • Stay aware of surroundings.
  • Be aware of transportation schedules if you are travelling alone at night.

Health in East Flanders

Belgium has a good healthcare system, and most private hospitals provide excellent medical care. However, it is always essential to have travel insurance before you come.
