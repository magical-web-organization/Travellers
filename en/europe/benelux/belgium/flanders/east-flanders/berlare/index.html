Introduction to Berlare

Located in East Flanders, Belgium, Berlare is a quaint town known for its natural beauty, including wetlands and lakes, and historical landmarks. The town is perfect for a weekend getaway or as a day trip from nearby cities like Ghent or Brussels. Its relaxed atmosphere and picturesque views make it an ideal destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and those looking for a break from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Berlare

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Donkmeer Lake: The Donkmeer Lake is a scenic spot and the beating heart of Berlare. Take a stroll, rent a boat, or go on a picnic while enjoying the stunning view.
  • Berlare Castle: This castle, built in the 17th century, is surrounded by a moat and offers a beautiful view from across the lake.
  • Donk museum: This small historical museum showcases the history and culture of Berlare and its surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Berlare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scheldeland Route: This cycling and hiking route takes you through the Scheldeland area, which includes Berlare and other nearby towns. It’s perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who want to explore the countryside and take in the scenic views.
  • Berlare Broek: This 400-hectare nature reserve is home to numerous bird species and offers a peaceful retreat. Walk through the wetlands and take in the beautiful scenery.
  • De Gavers: Just a short drive from Berlare, you’ll find De Gavers, a recreational park with lakes, walking trails, and a variety of outdoor activities like kayaking, tennis, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berlare

Berlare and its surrounding areas have many hiking and biking trails for nature enthusiasts to enjoy. Here are three of the best routes:

  1. Donkmeer Route: This 8km-long trail takes you around Donkmeer Lake, offering scenic views and tranquil spots to relax.
  2. Donk Valley Route: This 17km-long trail takes you through the Donk Valley and lets you explore the surrounding countryside.
  3. Scheldeland Route: This 90km-long route will take you on a journey through the Scheldeland area, which includes Berlare and other nearby towns.

Best Things to Do in Berlare: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of Donkmeer Lake and view Berlare Castle from the water.
  • Visit the local farmer’s market and try some locally produced cheese, honey, or jam.
  • Go bird watching at Berlare Broek and see if you can spot some of the 200 species of birds that call this nature reserve home.
  • Rent a bike and explore the Scheldeland Route.

Week-Long Itinerary for Berlare

Day 1: Take a boat tour of Donkmeer Lake and visit Berlare Castle. Day 2: Visit the historical Donk museum and take a hike through Berlare Broek. Day 3: Rent a bike and explore the Scheldeland Route. Day 4: Head to De Gavers and spend the day kayaking or fishing. Day 5: Visit nearby cities like Ghent or Brussels. Day 6: Browse the local farmer’s market and enjoy a picnic by the lake. Day 7: Take a leisurely stroll around Donkmeer Lake and enjoy the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Berlare

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stoverij: A hearty beef stew made with Belgian beer.
  • Moules-frites: A classic Belgian dish consisting of mussels and fries.
  • Filet Américain: A type of steak tartare served cold and usually eaten on a sandwich.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Belgium is famous for its beer, so make sure to try some of the local brews while in Berlare.
  • Jenever: A traditional Belgian gin-like beverage that’s popular in the region.

Where to Stay in Berlare: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel De Rode Leeuw: This charming hotel offers cozy rooms and a restaurant serving local dishes.
  • B&B ‘t Vijfde Seizoen: This bed and breakfast is located just a short walk from Donkmeer Lake and offers comfortable rooms and a terrace with garden views.
  • Villa Emma: Located in the heart of Berlare, this guesthouse offers spacious rooms and a tranquil garden.

What to Buy in Berlare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese: Berlare is known for its many local cheese producers.
  • Beer: Belgium is famous for its beer, so make sure to bring some home with you.
  • Chocolate: Belgium is also famous for its chocolate, so stop by a chocolate shop and bring some delicious treats back home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Berlare

Belgium is a country where customs and traditions still play an important role. Here are a few things to keep in mind while in Berlare:

  • Belgium is a bilingual country, so people in Berlare may speak both Dutch and French.
  • Tipping in restaurants is expected, usually around 10%.
  • When greeting someone, it is customary to shake hands.

Best Time to Visit Berlare

The best time to visit Berlare is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) can also be beautiful times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder weather. Winter (December-February) is cold and can be rainy, but it’s still a charming time to experience Berlare’s festive holiday celebrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berlare

Do:

  • Do try some of the local beers and cuisine.
  • Do take a boat tour to see Berlare Castle from the water.
  • Do take a hike through the Berlare Broek nature reserve.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the countryside and natural beauty of Berlare.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather and activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berlare

Belgium is generally a safe country, but here are a few tips to stay safe while in Berlare:

  • Keep your valuables out of sight and in a safe place.
  • Use common sense and caution when out at night.
  • Always follow traffic signals and use crosswalks.

Health in Berlare

No specific health risks are associated with Berlare, but it’s always a good idea to have travel medical insurance and check with your healthcare provider about any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Berlare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Berlare

  • Brussels Airport: The most convenient option and is about an hour’s drive from Berlare.

Bus Stations Near Berlare

  • Berlare Station: The main bus station in Berlare, with connections to nearby towns and cities.
  • Dendermonde Station: Just a short bus ride away from Berlare.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore Berlare and nearby areas.
  • Train: Berlare is easily accessible by train with connections from major cities like Ghent and Brussels.

How to Get Around in Berlare

Berlare is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There is also a local bus system that runs throughout the town and connects it to nearby cities.

Where to Go Next from Berlare: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Berlare is located in East Flanders, which is known for its historic towns and beautiful countryside. Here are a few nearby destinations:

  • Ghent: This charming university town is known for its beautiful medieval architecture and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Brussels: The capital of Belgium, Brussels is famous for its chocolate, beer, and stunning architecture.
  • Bruges: This historic city is known for its picturesque canals and well-preserved medieval architecture.

Conclusion

Berlare is a hidden gem in East Flanders that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, history, and local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day trip or a week-long getaway, Berlare has something for everyone.
