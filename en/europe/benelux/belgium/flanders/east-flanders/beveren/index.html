Introduction to Beveren

Nestled in the heart of East Flanders lies the charming town of Beveren. Known for its pristine natural surroundings, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Beveren is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. From historic museums and scenic hiking trails to local delicacies and cultural experiences, Beveren has something for everyone. This guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Beveren, so you can plan your next trip to this delightful destination with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Beveren

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are an avid history buff, then you will love exploring the cultural attractions that Beveren has to offer. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical sites in Beveren:

  • Castle of Cortewalle: This 17th-century castle is a fine example of Flemish architecture and is surrounded by a lush green park. It serves as a cultural center and hosts several exhibitions and events throughout the year.

  • Fort Liefkenshoek: Built in the 16th century to protect Antwerp from invaders, this fort is an impressive feat of engineering and is well-preserved to this day. You can take a guided tour of the fort and learn about its fascinating history.

  • Mercator Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and expeditions of Gerardus Mercator, a renowned cartographer and geographer who was born in Beveren. You can learn about his life, work, and his iconic world map, the Mercator projection.

Natural Attractions of Beveren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, then you will love hiking or biking through the stunning natural landscapes that Beveren has to offer. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Beveren:

  • Polders of Kruibeke: This vast expanse of tidal marshes, creeks, and polders is a haven for birdwatchers, nature lovers and hikers. You can take a guided tour or explore the polders on your own.

  • Clingingdael Nature Reserve: A lush green oasis in the middle of the city, Clingingdael Nature Reserve is home to several species of plants, birds, and butterflies. You can take a leisurely stroll through the reserve and enjoy its tranquil ambiance.

  • Melsele Forest: This forest is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and picnicking. It has several well-marked trails that lead to scenic viewpoints and hidden waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beveren

Beveren has miles of hiking and biking trails that traverse its idyllic countryside and picturesque towns. Here are some of the best routes in Beveren:

  • Beveren Walking Tour: This walking tour takes you through the historic town center of Beveren and covers several cultural and historical attractions, such as the Castle of Cortewalle and the St. Martinus Church.

  • Cliffs of Doel: This scenic biking route takes you through the unspoiled beauty of the Polders of Kruibeke and along the banks of the River Scheldt. You can witness the breathtaking views of the Cliffs of Doel, a stunning geological formation.

  • Fort Liefkenshoek Loop: This hiking trail takes you through the verdant landscapes of the Polders of Kruibeke and along the fortifications of Fort Liefkenshoek. You can learn about the fort’s history and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Beveren: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun and exciting things to do in Beveren. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Beveren:

  • Beveren Beer Tour: Beer is an essential part of Flemish culture, and Beveren is no exception. You can take a beer tour and learn about the brewing process, sample different types of beers, and enjoy the local hospitality.

  • Boat Tour of the River Scheldt: The River Scheldt is one of the most beautiful waterways in Europe, and you can explore it on a leisurely boat tour. You can witness the stunning views of the Flemish countryside and learn about the history of the river.

  • Cultural Events: Beveren hosts several cultural events throughout the year, such as the Mercator Festival, the Beveren Music Festival, and the Fort Liefkenshoek Heritage Days. You can participate in these events and experience the local customs, cuisine, and art.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beveren

If you have a week to spare, then you can explore Beveren and its surrounding areas at your leisure. Here is a suggested itinerary for your week-long trip to Beveren:

Day 1: Explore the Castle of Cortewalle and the St. Martinus Church in the historic town center of Beveren.

Day 2: Take a hiking tour of the Polders of Kruibeke and witness the stunning views of the River Scheldt.

Day 3: Visit the Mercator Museum and learn about the life and legacy of Gerardus Mercator.

Day 4: Take a bike tour of the Cliffs of Doel and enjoy the unspoiled beauty of the Flemish countryside.

Day 5: Visit the Fort Liefkenshoek and take a guided tour of the fort’s impressive architecture.

Day 6: Take a boat tour of the River Scheldt and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Day 7: Participate in the local cultural events, such as the Mercator Festival or the Beveren Music Festival, and experience the local customs and art.

Food and Drink Scene of Beveren

Must-Try Dishes

Flemish cuisine is renowned for its hearty, flavorful dishes that are prepared using fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Beveren:

  • Stoofvlees: This traditional beef stew is made with beer and served with fries or bread.

  • Waterzooi: A creamy stew made with fish or chicken, vegetables, and herbs.

  • Belgian Waffles: These delicious waffles are topped with fresh fruit, whipped cream, or chocolate sauce.

Local Beverages

Beer is an essential part of Flemish culture, and Beveren is no exception. Here are some of the best local beverages in Beveren:

  • Tripel d’Anvers: A strong, golden ale that is brewed in Antwerp and has a fruity, spicy flavor.

  • Gouden Carolus: A dark, malty beer with a hint of chocolate and caramel.

  • Bols: A traditional local brandy that is distilled from fruits such as apples, pears, and cherries.

Where to Stay in Beveren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beveren has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best places to stay in Beveren:

  • Van der Valk Hotel Beveren: A luxurious hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a spa, and an on-site restaurant.

  • Lilaste: A charming bed and breakfast that has a warm, homely ambiance and offers a delicious breakfast.

  • De Sampan Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers basic amenities, clean rooms, and a central location.

What to Buy in Beveren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring a piece of Beveren back home with you, then here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Belgian Chocolates: Belgium is renowned for its high-quality chocolates, and you can buy a wide variety of artisanal chocolates in Beveren.

  • Flemish Tapestry: Beveren has a rich tradition of tapestry weaving, and you can buy unique and beautiful tapestries that are made by local artisans.

  • Local Beer and Brandy: You can take home a bottle of your favorite Belgian beer or brandy as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Beveren

Beveren has a rich cultural heritage that is visible in its customs, traditions, and way of life. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Flemish hospitality is warm and friendly, and you will be welcomed with open arms.

  • Tipping is not mandatory, but it is appreciated if you receive excellent service.

  • Belgians value punctuality, so make sure to arrive on time for your appointments.

Best Time to Visit Beveren

The best time to visit Beveren is during the summer months (June - August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you want to avoid the crowds, then you can visit Beveren during the shoulder seasons (April - May and September - October) when the weather is pleasant, and the tourist season is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beveren

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, such as the stoofvlees and Belgian beer.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Take care of the environment and leave no trace when hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Be loud and disruptive in public spaces.

  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beveren

Beveren is a safe town with low crime rates, but it is always advisable to take basic cautionary measures, such as not leaving your valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Beveren

Belgium has excellent healthcare services, and Beveren has several medical facilities, such as hospitals and pharmacies, that offer high-quality medical care. Make sure to take adequate health insurance before your trip to Beveren.

Getting to Beveren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beveren is well-connected to the rest of Belgium and Europe, and you can reach it by air, road, or rail.

  • Brussels Airport: Located 45 km from Beveren, this is the largest airport in Belgium and offers direct flights to several destinations worldwide.

  • Antwerp International Airport: Located 21 km from Beveren, this airport serves several European destinations and the UK.

Bus Stations Near Beveren

  • Beveren Bus Station: Located in the town center, this bus station offers regional and intercity bus services.

  • Antwerp Bus Station: Located 18 km from Beveren, this bus station offers regional and international bus services.

Other Options

  • Train: Beveren has a train station that connects it to several destinations in Belgium and Europe.

  • Car: Beveren can be easily reached by car via the E17 and E34 highways.

How to Get Around in Beveren

Beveren is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. You can also use the local bus services or hire a taxi if you prefer.
