Introduction to Dendermonde

Located in the heart of the Flemish countryside, the historic city of Dendermonde in East Flanders, Belgium, is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure. The city is famous for its annual carnival procession, which features traditional giant puppets, and its delicious local treats such as “Mastellen” and “Aalsterse Vlaaien.” Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure enthusiast, Dendermonde has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Dendermonde

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. The Beguinage

The Beguinage is a charming little neighborhood in the heart of Dendermonde that dates back to the 13th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and home to several houses and chapels that give visitors a glimpse into the lives of the Beguines, religious women who lived there.

2. Dendermonde Town Hall

The Town Hall is one of the most important landmarks in Dendermonde. It dates back to the 14th century and was once the seat of power for the local authorities. The Town Hall is famous for its Gothic style architecture and beautifully decorated façade with statues of the counts of Flanders.

3. Our Lady Ter Tuynen Abbey

The Our Lady Ter Tuynen Abbey is a beautiful 17th-century abbey located on the outskirts of Dendermonde. It is a popular destination for spiritual retreats and features a serene garden, a chapel, and a museum.

Natural Attractions of Dendermonde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Dendermonde Nature Reserve

The Dendermonde Nature Reserve is a vast area of woodland, wetlands, and grasslands that is perfect for hiking, biking, and birdwatching. Visitors can explore the beautiful landscape and observe wildlife such as herons, owls, and foxes.

2. Dender River and Grootzand

The Dender River offers beautiful views of the city and is a popular spot for fishing and boating. The Grootzand, which is a large canal that runs through the city, is great for a leisurely stroll and offers a beautiful view of the city’s skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dendermonde

Dendermonde offers a number of hiking and biking routes for travelers who love outdoor adventures. The following routes are highly recommended:

  • The Denderroute: This 45-kilometer route follows the course of the Dender River and takes you through beautiful natural landscapes and past charming villages.

  • The Kalkense Meersenroute: This route takes you through the Kalkense Meersen, a large area of wetlands that is home to some rare birds and plants.

Best Things to Do in Dendermonde: Activities and Tours

1. Visit the Carnival Museum

The Carnival Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the city’s rich cultural heritage. It features a collection of giant puppets, costumes, and other items related to the annual carnival procession.

2. Take a Boat Tour

A boat tour is a great way to explore the city’s waterways and enjoy the stunning views of the city’s skyline and the surrounding natural beauty.

3. Visit the Market

The city’s market is a great place to sample some of the local treats such as Mastellen, a type of sweet bread, and Aalsterse Vlaaien, a type of fruit tart.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dendermonde

Day 1: Visit the Beguinage and the Town Hall Day 2: Explore the Dendermonde Nature Reserve and the Dender River Day 3: Visit the Our Lady Ter Tuyen Abbey and take a boat tour Day 4: Take a biking tour of the Kalkense Meersenroute Day 5: Visit the Carnival Museum and the market Day 6: Explore the Grootzand and the city’s historic churches Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Ghent or Brussels

Food and Drink Scene of Dendermonde

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mastellen: A type of sweet bread that is usually eaten for breakfast or as a snack.

  • Aalsterse Vlaaien: A type of fruit tart that is a specialty of the region.

  • Stoverij: A hearty beef stew that is usually served with fries.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beer: Belgium is famous for its beer, and Dendermonde is no exception. Visitors can sample a variety of local brews at the city’s many bars and taverns.

  • Jenever: A type of juniper-flavored spirit that is a specialty of the Flanders region.

  • B&B Abalona: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a quiet residential area and offers cozy rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Dendermonde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lace products: Dendermonde is known for its beautiful lace products such as tablecloths and doilies.

  • Chocolate: Belgium is famous for its chocolate, and Dendermonde is no exception. Visitors can find a variety of chocolate shops in the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Dendermonde

Dendermonde has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, festivals, and food. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dendermonde

The best time to visit Dendermonde is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city’s many outdoor attractions are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dendermonde

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Do take the time to explore the city’s natural beauty and historic landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the local environment.

  • Don’t disrespect the locals or their customs.

  • Don’t visit during the annual carnival procession if you have a fear of large puppets!

Safety and Health

Safety in Dendermonde

Dendermonde is generally a safe city, but visitors are advised to take the usual precautions and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Dendermonde

Visitors to Dendermonde are advised to have a valid health insurance policy and to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding consuming raw or undercooked foods.

Getting to Dendermonde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dendermonde is easily accessible by public transportation, and visitors can choose from several modes of transport, including:

  • Train: Dendermonde has a train station that is served by several regional and national trains.

  • Bus: The city is also well-connected by bus, with several bus lines that run throughout the city and the surrounding areas.

Airports Near Dendermonde

  • Brussels Airport: Located about 50 kilometers from Dendermonde, Brussels Airport is the closest international airport.

  • Antwerp Airport: This airport is located about 40 kilometers from Dendermonde and offers flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Dendermonde

  • Dendermonde Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and offers connections to several nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Bicycle: Dendermonde is well-connected by bike paths, and cycling is a popular mode of transport in the city and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Dendermonde

Dendermonde is a relatively small city, and most of the major attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use the city’s public transportation system, which includes buses and trains, or rent a bike to explore the city and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Dendermonde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ghent: This beautiful historic city is located about 40 kilometers from Dendermonde and is famous for its picturesque canals and beautiful architecture.

  • Brussels: The capital city of Belgium is located about 40 kilometers from Dendermonde and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Dendermonde is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s historic landmarks, enjoying the local cuisine, or simply soaking up the beautiful scenery, Dendermonde has something to offer.
