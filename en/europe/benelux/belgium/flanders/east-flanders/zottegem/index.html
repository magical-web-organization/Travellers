Zottegem is a charming and peaceful Belgian town located in the heart of East Flanders. It’s a hidden gem that has a lot to offer tourists, whether you’re a history enthusiast, a nature lover, or a foodie. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about this picturesque town, from the best places to visit to where to stay and what to eat.

Introduction to Zottegem

Zottegem is a beautiful town that’s surrounded by lush green hills and sprawling farmland. It’s known for its impressive historical landmarks, unspoiled natural scenery, vibrant cultural scene, and delicious local cuisine.

While the town’s origin dates back to the early Middle Ages, today, it’s a thriving community of approximately 26,500 people. Its location also makes it an ideal base for exploring other nearby attractions such as Gent and Brussels.

List of Places to Visit in Zottegem

Zottegem is a town that’s rich in history and culture, making it a perfect place for tourists who love exploring. Here are some of the best places to visit in Zottegem:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Egmont Palace: This stunning palace is located in the nearby city of Gent. It was built in the 16th century and is known for its intricate architecture and vast collection of artwork.
  • Zottegem Castle: This impressive 18th century castle is located in the center of Zottegem. It’s home to the town’s administration and offers a peek into the rich history of the region.
  • Sint Mauritiuskerk: This beautiful church was built in the 12th century and features Gothic architecture. It’s a great place to admire the intricate details of the building and its art.
  • Museum of Deinze and the Leie Region: Located just outside of Zottegem, this museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts and art that depicts the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Zottegem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Province Park de Gavers: This beautiful natural park offers visitors a chance to relax and enjoy the great outdoors. It boasts a tranquil lake, wildlife, and walking trails.
  • Boelarebos: This lush forest is perfect for hiking and birdwatching. It’s a great place for tourists to connect with nature and see the local flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zottegem

Zottegem’s countryside is a great place for a leisurely hike or bike ride. The region offers various routes to cater to your level of fitness and mood. One of the popular ones is De Katteman route, known for its breathtaking views of the town. You can rent a bike from nearby shops or take a guided tour too if you prefer that.

Best Things to Do in Zottegem: Activities and Tours

Besides historical and natural attractions, there are other things to do and enjoy in Zottegem. Here are some of our favorite activities and tours:

  • Beer tasting and brewery tours: Belgian beers are famous worldwide, and Zottegem is no exception. You can go on a tour of the local brewery – Strubbe – to learn about the beer-making process and, of course, taste their creations.
  • Wine tasting: If you’re more of a wine lover, you can opt for a wine tasting tour at Domein de Kluizen, which produces some of Belgium’s best wine.
  • Local markets: Take your taste buds on a culinary adventure and visit the local farmer’s market to try some authentic Belgian treats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zottegem

Day 1: Arrive in Zottegem and take a guided walking tour of the town. Visit Sint Mauritiuskerk and Zottegem Castle.

Day 2: Visit Province Park de Gavers and explore its walking trails. Have a picnic or go fishing at the lake.

Day 3: Go on a bike ride through the countryside and enjoy the scenery. Stop at local cafes to try some Belgian sweets.

Day 4: Visit nearby Gent and enjoy the city’s museums, art galleries, and cafes.

Day 5: Take a wine-tasting tour in Domein de Kluizen and try some Belgian cheese to pair with it.

Day 6: Explore Boelarebos and go on a bird-watching tour.

Day 7: Relax and unwind by enjoying the town’s leisurely pace and end your day with dinner at a local brasserie.

Food and Drink Scene of Zottegem

Belgium is known for its flavorful cuisine, and Zottegem is no exception. Here are some must-try local dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Waterzooi: This creamy soup of chicken or fish is a popular dish all over Belgium. It’s made with cream and egg yolks, giving it a rich and velvety texture.
  • Stoofvlees: Belgium’s answer to beef stew – this comforting dish is made of chunks of beef that have been slowly braised in beer and spices.
  • Belgian Waffles: Waffles are one of Belgium’s famous culinary exports and are typically served with whipped cream and fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beer: Beer lovers will have a great time sampling the local microbreweries such as Strubbe and Liefmans, which have been brewing traditional Belgian beer for centuries.
  • Belgian wine: While Belgium is not traditionally a wine-producing country, the quality of its wine is nothing to sneeze at.

Where to Stay in Zottegem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zottegem has a range of accommodation options to suit everyone’s preference and budget. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • B&B Het Stille Genoegen: This highly-rated guesthouse is peaceful and beautifully decorated. The hosts are welcoming and serve a delicious breakfast.
  • Hostellerie Kluisbos: This 4-star hotel boasts stunning natural surroundings and is ideal for a luxury getaway.
  • Hotel De Boomgaard: This cozy hotel is great for budget-conscious travelers and is located right in the center of Zottegem.

What to Buy in Zottegem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Zottegem may want to take home something as a memento of their trip. Some of the best souvenirs to buy include:

  • Belgian chocolates: No trip to Belgium would be complete without indulging in high-quality Belgian chocolate.
  • Belgian lace: Belgium is famous for its intricate lacework, and Zottegem has several shops that sell handmade lace products.
  • Beer and wine: Bring home some authentic Belgian beer or wine to enjoy with your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Zottegem

Belgium has a unique culture, and Zottegem is no exception. Here are some customs and practices to keep in mind:

  • Belgians appreciate punctuality. Ensure you arrive on time for appointments and reserve or booking.
  • Tipping is standard at cafes and restaurants, so make sure to factor it into your budget.
  • Beware of the different languages spoken in Belgium. Dutch, French, and German are all prevalent in various parts of the country.

Best Time to Visit Zottegem

The best time to visit Zottegem is from April to September, during the spring and summer seasons. The weather is mild and pleasant, and the town is at its liveliest during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zottegem

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Take proper gear while exploring the countryside.
  • Reserve your accommodation in advance.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public; smoking is prohibited in public places.
  • Be too loud; Belgians tend to be a bit reserved.
  • Be impolite, it’s important to say ‘please’ and ‘thank you.’

Safety and Health

Zottegem is a safe town, and tourists can travel around without any difficulty. The town is well connected with public transport, and it’s easy to explore on foot. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Zottegem

  • Keep your belongings safe, especially when exploring crowded places.
  • Look both ways when crossing the road.
  • Stay within well-lit areas after sunset.

Health in Zottegem

  • Belgium has an excellent healthcare system and hospitals, so it’s easy to receive medical care if necessary.
  • Make sure to drink bottled or filtered water.

Getting to Zottegem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zottegem is a well-connected town and is accessible via several transportation options.

Airports Near Zottegem

  • Brussels Airport is the closest and largest airport to Zottegem, which is approximately 64km away.

Bus Stations Near Zottegem

  • Zottegem has its bus station, and you can take a bus to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • The best way to travel in Zottegem is by foot or bike.

How to Get Around in Zottegem

The easiest way to get around Zottegem is on foot. The town center is compact, and most of the attractions are within reasonable walking distance. If you want to go further, you can rent a bike or take a bus to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Zottegem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While there are plenty of things to see and do in Zottegem, there are other places nearby that are worth exploring:

  • Gent: Known as Belgium’s best kept secret, it’s a university town with impressive architecture and charming streets.
  • Brussels: Belgium’s capital is just a short train ride away and has plenty to offer tourists, from historical landmarks to excellent cuisine.

Conclusion

Zottegem is a small but charming Belgian town that offers tourists a glimpse into Belgian culture, history, and cuisine. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and excellent transport connections, it’s an ideal destination for those who appreciate the country’s understated charm. Plan your visit according to the tips and insights shared on this travel guide for a memorable stay.
