Introduction to Waasmunster

Nestled in the heart of East Flanders in Flanders, Belgium, Waasmunster is a hidden gem that offers a mix of natural beauty and cultural richness. With its picturesque landscapes, authentic Belgian cuisine, and historically significant attractions, Waasmunster is a paradise for those seeking a unique travel experience in Europe. This travel guide will take you on a journey through Waasmunster, highlighting the best places to visit, activities to do, and local customs to experience.

List of Places to Visit in Waasmunster

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Waasmunster boasts of a rich cultural and historical heritage, with several attractions to explore.

The Abbey of Roosenberg

An architectural masterpiece, the Abbey of Roosenberg dates back to the early 20th century and is a prime example of modernist architecture. The building combines simplicity, functionality, and spirituality, making it a must-visit for architecture lovers.

The Museum of the Durme Valley

Explore the natural heritage of the Durme Valley and the local culture of the region at the Museum of the Durme Valley. The museum showcases the history and biodiversity of the region, making it a great educational tour for families with kids.

Natural Attractions of Waasmunster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Waasmunster is a stunning destination, boasting of jaw-dropping landscapes and outdoor recreational activities.

The Puitvoetbos Forest

Located on the outskirts of Waasmunster, the Puitvoetbos Forest is a paradise for nature lovers. The forest offers several hiking and walking trails spread out through idyllic landscapes, making it a perfect destination for a picnic or a day trip.

The Durme Valley

The Durme Valley is a protected nature reserve that extends up to 17 km, from Hamme to Lokeren. The freshwater river offers camping, canoeing, and kayaking opportunities for visitors, while the surrounding forests and grasslands provide a habitat for numerous rare bird species, making it a bird watcher’s paradise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waasmunster

Waasmunster has no shortage of outdoor activities, and the town’s ideal location at the edge of Durme Valley means that it is perfect for cyclists and hikers.

The Durme Valley Walk

The Durme Valley Walk is one of Waasmunster’s best-loved activity routes, offering breathtaking views of the river and the surrounding nature reserve. Visitors can rent bicycles or take the trail on foot, enjoying the full beauty of the valley.

The Waasland Loop

For those looking for a more extended bike ride, the Waasland Loop takes visitors on a 45km-long journey around Waasmunster, offering the best of the region’s countryside and communes.

Best Things to Do in Waasmunster: Activities and Tours

Waasmunster is a destination that offers a lot to do, and visitors will have numerous options to choose from when looking for activities and tours.

The Waasland Wine Tour

Belgium’s Waasland region is renowned for its wine-making prowess, and Waasmunster offers wine lovers a chance to explore its vineyards. The Waasland Wine Tour takes visitors on a journey through some of the best wineries in the region, offering an opportunity to sample and learn about the local wines.

The Flanders Fields Tour

Another popular attraction in Waasmunster is the Flanders Fields Tour. The tour takes visitors through the sites of some of the most significant battles of World War I, including the battlefields of Ypres, the Tyne Cot Cemetery, and the Menin Gate Memorial.

Food and Drink Scene of Waasmunster

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Waasmunster is complete without experiencing the local cuisine, and the town offers a wide range of delicacies for visitors to explore.

Stoverij

A staple of Belgian cuisine, Stoverij is a slow-cooked meat stew made using beef chunks, onions, and a Belgian beer sauce. The dish is usually served with fries and goes well with a cold beer.

Speculaaspasta

Speculaaspasta is a flavored spread made from Speculoos biscuits. The Nutella-like spread can be enjoyed on bread, waffles, or pancakes.

Local Beverages

Waasmunster is situated near some of Belgium’s best breweries and vineyards, making it a prime destination for beer and wine lovers

Belgian Beers

Belgium is famous for its beer, and visitors to Waasmunster can enjoy some of the country’s best brews. Popular beers from the region include Duvel, Leffe, and Chimay.

Waasland Wine

Waasland wine is renowned in the region, and visitors can enjoy a range of different wines made from both red and white grapes grown across the Waasland area.

Where to Stay in Waasmunster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waasmunster offers a range of accommodation options for visitors.

‘t Hemelbedje B&B

A cozy B&B in the heart of Waasmunster, ‘t Hemelbedje is perfect for couples or families looking for comfortable, budget-friendly accommodation. The B&B is close to several city attractions, including the town center and the Abbey of Roosenberg.

Hotel La Gare

For those looking for a luxury stay, Hotel La Gare is one of Waasmunster’s best choices. The hotel is housed in an elegantly restored train station, offering visitors an authentic Belgian experience.

What to Buy in Waasmunster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Waasmunster offers visitors a range of souvenir options, with several local products to take home.

Waaslander Cheese

Waaslander cheese is a soft spreadable cheese made from buttermilk. It is a local specialty of the Waasland region, and visitors can bring back a taste of the region with them by buying some.

Speculoos

Speculoos biscuits are a Belgian delicacy that are enjoyed across the country. Visitors to Waasmunster can buy packs of these biscuits, which are flavored with cinnamon and ginger.

Local Culture and Customs of Waasmunster

Belgium is a country with rich cultural traditions, and Waasmunster is no exception.

Kappellekensmarkt

Kappellekensmarkt is an annual event in Waasmunster that takes place at the beginning of September. The event is a street fair that celebrates the town’s local traditions and culture, with food, drinks, and local products on offer.

Flemish Giants

Flemish giant rabbits are a local specialty in Waasmunster, and visitors can learn more about these gentle giants at the local rabbit club. Visitors can also buy stuffed Flemish giant rabbits as souvenirs.

Best Time to Visit Waasmunster

The best time to visit Waasmunster is between May and September, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the town’s outdoor activities can be enjoyed most. The shoulder season of April and October is also a great time to visit, when the prices are lower and the crowds are fewer.

  • Do visit the local markets during weekends
  • Do carry warm clothing while visiting the Durme Valley

Don’t:

  • Don’t speak ill of the local culture or customs
  • Don’t litter in public areas
  • Don’t forget to enjoy the local cuisine

Safety and Health

Waasmunster is a safe destination for tourists, and there are no significant health concerns for visitors to the area.

Safety in Waasmunster

Like any other destination, tourists should be wary of pickpockets and petty theft while visiting Waasmunster. It is best to keep valuables in a secure place while out on the streets.

Health in Waasmunster

Waasmunster has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics, where visitors can seek medical attention if necessary. Visitors to the countryside areas should be wary of ticks and mosquitoes and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Waasmunster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Waasmunster is easily accessible through several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Waasmunster

The nearest international airport to Waasmunster is Brussels Airport, which is about 88 km away. The airport offers train connections to the town center or can be accessed via car/taxi.

Bus Stations Near Waasmunster

Waasmunster has a well-connected bus network, with several buses regularly traveling to nearby major destinations such as Antwerp and Brussels. Visitors can find the bus schedules and routes on the local transport authority website.

Other Options

Waasmunster can also be reached via road and rail. The town has a train station that is directly connected to several other cities in Belgium, including Gent and Antwerp.

How to Get Around in Waasmunster

The best way to explore Waasmunster is by bicycle or on foot, as the town is small and easy to navigate. Visitors can rent bicycles through local tour operators or hotels.

Where to Go Next from Waasmunster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waasmunster is situated in a prime location, making it an ideal base from which to explore the neighboring towns and cities in Belgium.

  • Ghent: One of Belgium’s cultural centers, Ghent is a university town offering a vibrant nightlife, rich history, and several cultural sites to explore.
  • Brussels: Belgium’s capital city, Brussels, is renowned for its art museums, excellent architecture, and numerous shopping opportunities.
  • Bruges: A medieval town with a romantic charm, Bruges is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers visitors a glimpse into Old-World Europe.

Conclusion

Waasmunster is a destination that offers visitors an opportunity to experience Belgian culture and natural beauty in a unique way. With its historic and cultural landmarks, outdoor activities, and authentic cuisine, Waasmunster is the perfect destination for those seeking a unique European travel experience.
