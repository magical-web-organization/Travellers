Introduction to Aalst

Located in East Flanders, Aalst is a charming medieval city in Belgium that offers a mix of rich history, cultural attractions, and natural landscapes. The city boasts of beautiful architecture, stunning parks, museums and galleries, and a vibrant culinary scene that has something for everyone visiting the city.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we explore the best things to do, see, eat, and more in Aalst to help you plan an amazing trip to this gem in the heart of Belgium.

List of Places to Visit in Aalst

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aalst is rich in history and culture and has many museums and galleries that you can visit to explore its heritage. Here is a list of the must-visit places:

  • The Carnival Museum - If you happen to be in the city during the carnival season, a visit to the Carnival Museum is a must. This museum celebrates the Aalst carnival and showcases its rich culture, tradition, and costumes.
  • The Historical Museum - This museum is home to a treasure trove of artifacts and displays that showcase the history of the city, including its medieval past.
  • Sint-Martinuskerk - This is a beautiful Romanesque-style church located in the center of the city. The church displays a unique mix of Gothic and Romanesque architecture, and houses numerous historic artworks.

Natural Attractions of Aalst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aalst is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes that offer great opportunities for outdoor adventurers. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Osbroek Park - This stunning park is located on the outskirts of the city and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The park offers several hiking and cycling trails, making it a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Scheldeland - This is a beautiful area located on the outskirts of the city, offering picturesque views of rivers, canals, and lush green landscapes. The area is great for cycling, hiking, and boat tours.
  • Den Berg - This is a park that offers a beautiful scenic view of the entire city. The park has several trails that offer a great workout for hikers and joggers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aalst

Aalst is a city that is best explored on foot or bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes that you can take in the city:

  • Dendervallei Fietsroute - This is a beautiful cycling trail that takes you through picturesque views of the Dender river valley.
  • Osbroek Nature Reserve Hike - This is a scenic hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful Osbroek Nature Reserve.
  • City Walking Tour - This is a walking tour that takes you through the beautiful and historic landmarks of the city, including the Grote Markt, Sint-Martinuskerk, and the City Hall.

Best Things to Do in Aalst: Activities and Tours

Aalst offers a range of activities and tours that you can enjoy during your trip. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Aalst Carnival - If you happen to visit the city during February, don’t miss the famous Aalst carnival, which is one of the oldest and most celebrated carnivals in Belgium.
  • Beer Tour - Belgium is known for its beer, and Aalst offers a range of beer tours that allow you to explore the local breweries and taste some of the finest beers in the country.
  • City Tour - Aalst is a beautiful city with a rich history, and taking a walking or biking tour is a great way to explore all the landmarks and learn more about the city’s past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aalst

To help you plan your trip to Aalst, here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in the city:

Day Activity
Day 1 City Tour, followed by a visit to the Historical Museum
Day 2 Scheldeland cycling tour, followed by a visit to the Carnival Museum
Day 3 Osbroek Nature Reserve Hike, followed by a beer tasting at a local brewery or pub
Day 4 Visit the Sint-Martinuskerk, followed by a shopping spree at the local markets

Day 7 Relax and enjoy the city at your leisure

Food and Drink Scene of Aalst

Belgium is famous for its cuisine, and Aalst is no exception. Here is a list of foods and drinks that you must try when in the city:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Waterzooi - This is a creamy fish stew that is flavored with white wine, herbs, and cream.
  • Stoofvlees - This is a famous Flemish beef stew that is flavored with beer, herbs, and onions.
  • Geraardsbergse Mattentaart - This is a traditional cheese tart that is produced in the nearby town of Geraardsbergen. It is made with matzah pastry and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Belgian beer - Belgians have over 1,500 different types of beer, and Aalst offers a range of local breweries and pubs that serve some of the finest beers in the country.
  • Jenever - This is a traditional Belgian gin that is flavored with juniper berries and other botanicals.

Where to Stay in Aalst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aalst offers a range of accommodations that cater to various budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Ibis Aalst Centrum - This is a budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of the city.
  • The Royal Astrid - This is a 4-star hotel that offers luxurious rooms and is located in a historic building in the city center.
  • B&B Aalsters Genot - This is a cozy bed and breakfast that offers personalized service and is located near the city’s attractions.

What to Buy in Aalst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aalst offers a range of products that make perfect souvenirs or gifts for friends and family back home. Here are some of the best items to buy:

  • Chocolates and pralines
  • Handmade lace
  • Local beer and jenever

Local Culture and Customs of Aalst

Aalst is a city with a rich local culture and customs. Here are some of the local customs and etiquette practices that you should be aware of:

  • Tipping is not expected in restaurants or cafes.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Always greet people with a smile and use polite language when speaking.

Best Time to Visit Aalst

The best time to visit Aalst is during the spring and summer months, from March to September, when the weather is favorable for outdoor activities and exploring the city’s attractions. The carnival season in February is also a great time to visit the city, but be prepared for larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aalst

Do:

  • Try the local beer and cuisine
  • Take walking or biking tours to explore the city
  • Respect local customs and values

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack comfortable walking shoes
  • Bring large bags or backpacks to religious sites or crowded areas
  • Leave without trying the Geraardsbergse Mattentaart

Safety and Health

Safety in Aalst

Aalst is a safe city with low crime rates, but it’s always a good idea to stay vigilant and take precautions to avoid pickpocketing or theft. Avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night and keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Aalst

Belgium has an excellent healthcare system, and Aalst has several hospitals and medical centers that offer quality healthcare services. Make sure to bring all the necessary medications with you, and purchase travel insurance before arriving in the city.

Getting to Aalst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aalst

  • Brussels Airport - This is the closest international airport to Aalst, located approximately 55km away.
  • Antwerp International Airport - This is another airport that is located approximately 70km away from Aalst.

Bus Stations Near Aalst

Aalst has a central bus station that is well-connected to other major cities in the region. You can take a bus from Brussels or Ghent to Aalst.

Other Options

Aalst is well-connected by a network of trains and buses that connect it to other major cities in Belgium and the surrounding regions. You can also rent a car or bike to explore the city on your own.

How to Get Around in Aalst

Aalst is a compact city that can easily be explored on foot or bike. The city also has an efficient public transport system that includes buses and trains, which makes it easy to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Aalst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aalst is located in a beautiful region that offers many exciting places to visit. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore:

  • Brussels - This vibrant city is located just 30 minutes from Aalst by train and is home to several iconic landmarks, museums, and galleries.
  • Ghent - This is a medieval city located just 30 minutes from Aalst by train. The city is famous for its stunning architecture, museums, and galleries.
  • Bruges - This picturesque city is located approximately 1.5 hours from Aalst by train and is famous for its canals, cobbled streets, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Aalst is a charming city that offers a mix of rich history, culture, and natural landscapes. With this comprehensive tour guide, you are now equipped with all the information you need to plan an amazing trip to this hidden gem in the heart of Belgium.
