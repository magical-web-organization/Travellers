Introduction to Wasserbillig

Wasserbillig is a small town located in the Moselle Valley in Luxembourg’s Benelux region in Europe. Known for its picturesque beauty, Wasserbillig is nestled between the German and Luxembourgish borders, making it an excellent destination for exploring the region. The municipality of Wasserbillig, with its 9 villages, is a popular tourist destination welcoming over two million visitors yearly. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know about planning your trip, including what to see, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Wasserbillig

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wasserbillig Castle - A structure that dates back to the 14th century.
  2. Basilica of Our Lady of Comfort - A Roman Catholic church with a rich history.
  3. Trier Imperial Baths - Ruins of ancient Roman baths that are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  4. Luxembourg City History Museum - A museum that showcases Luxembourg’s cultural heritage.
  5. The Abbey of Echternach - A Benedictine monastery with its well-known dancing procession.

Natural Attractions of Wasserbillig: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Moselle river - A perfect spot to enjoy water sports, boat tours, and a walk along the river.
  2. The Devil’s Canyon - A scenic hiking trail along a rocky gorge.
  3. The Trier High Forest - A vast forest that is home to several species of flora and fauna.
  4. Luxembourg Ardennes - A beautiful forest ideal for long walks, picnics, and camping.
  5. Schiessentumpel Waterfall - A picturesque waterfall in the Mullerthal region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wasserbillig

Wasserbillig offers excellent hiking and biking routes that take you through lush forests, rugged terrain, and scenic landscapes. Some notable routes include:

  1. Mullerthal Trail - A picturesque hiking trail through the Mullerthal region.
  2. Kellerbierg Trail - A mountain biking and hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Moselle river.
  3. Vennbahn Trail - A biking route that connects three countries (Luxembourg, Belgium, and Germany).
  4. Moselle Cycle Route - A cycling trail that takes you along the Moselle river and the vineyards.
  5. Hunsruck Hill Trail - A beautiful hiking trail that takes you through the Hunsruck hillsides.

Best Things to Do in Wasserbillig: Activities and Tours

  1. Wine Tasting - Visit one of the many vineyards along the Moselle river and taste the delicious wines.
  2. River Cruise - Enjoy a sunset cruise down the Moselle river.
  3. Motorhome Tour - Tour the region in a fully-equipped motorhome for a unique experience.
  4. Segway Tours - Discover the region in a fun and eco-friendly way.
  5. E-bike Tour - Rent an electric bike and explore the scenic routes of Wasserbillig.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wasserbillig

day 1 - Arrival and check-in to your hotel. day 2 - Visit Wasserbillig Castle and the Basilica of Our Lady of Comfort. day 3 - Hike along the Mullerthal Trail and visit Schiessentumpel waterfall. day 4 - Take a river cruise down the Moselle river and taste local wines. day 5 - Rent an electric bike and explore the regions’ cultural and natural landmarks. day 6 - Discover the Trier Imperial Baths and Luxembourg City History Museum. day 7 - Departures.

Food and Drink Scene of Wasserbillig

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bouneschlupp - A traditional Luxembourg soup made with beans, potatoes, and bacon.
  2. Judd mat Gaardebounen - A dish made with smoked pork, green beans, and potatoes.
  3. Kachkéis - A cheese spread made with garlic, butter, and cream.
  4. Riesling soup - A soup made with Riesling wine.
  5. Quetschentaart - A plum tart.

Local Beverages

  1. Moselle wines - The Moselle region is known for producing some of the finest wines in the world.
  2. Ardennes gin - A gin made with forest herbs and locally-sourced juniper berries.

Local Culture and Customs of Wasserbillig

The official languages in Wasserbillig are Luxembourgish, French, and German. Luxembourgish is the national language. The locals are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their customs. The Luxembourg National Day, celebrated on 23rd June, is a significant celebration of Luxembourg’s culture. It is a day of patriotic pageantry and military parades, followed by fireworks at night.

Best Time to Visit Wasserbillig

The best time to visit Wasserbillig is during the months of May to September when the temperatures are warm and comfortable. The region experiences cooler temperatures from October to April, making it suitable for winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wasserbillig

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Carry cash as some local restaurants and shops may not accept credit cards.
  3. Keep your surroundings clean and tidy.
  4. Speak slowly and clearly to ensure you are understood.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter in public places.
  2. Do not forget to say “bonjour” or “guten tag” when entering local stores and restaurants.
  3. Do not ignore traffic rules while driving or riding a bike.
  4. Do not walk or cycle on hiking trails designated for hiking only.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wasserbillig

Wasserbillig is safe for tourists; however, it is advisable to keep your valuables secure and not leave them unattended. The region is prone to unpredictable weather conditions, so it is essential to carry the appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.

Health in Wasserbillig

EU citizens can use their European Health Card (EHIC) to receive medical care in Luxembourg. Non-EU citizens must have adequate travel insurance and ensure they have received the recommended vaccinations before traveling.
