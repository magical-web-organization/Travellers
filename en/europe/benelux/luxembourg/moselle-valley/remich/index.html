Introduction to Remich

Located in the Moselle Valley in the eastern part of Luxembourg, Remich is home to numerous vineyards, river cruises, and charming cultural landmarks. It is a small town that offers a lot to anyone looking for a mix of adventure and relaxation in a beautiful setting in the heart of Europe.

List of Places to Visit in Remich

Remich has a wide range of tourist attractions, ranging from culturally significant landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Nicholas Church: This 13th-century church is located in the town’s center and is one of Remich’s most important landmarks.

  • Musee A Possen: This museum displays one of the country’s largest collections of photographs and portraits from the 19th and early 20th centuries, along with traditional handicrafts.

  • Hesperange Castle: This beautiful historical castle is located nearby and offers guided tours through its impressive halls and gardens.

Natural Attractions of Remich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moselle River Cruises: Take a relaxing boat ride along the river and enjoy the town’s scenic views and vineyards.

  • Remich Public Gardens: This park offers stunning views over the river, perfect for a picnic or a relaxing stroll.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Wine tasting tours are a must-do in Remich, as the town is known for its excellent wine production. Many vineyards offer tours and tastings, including the Greiveldange and Ehnen vineyards.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Remich

Remich has a variety of hiking and biking options, catering to both novice and experienced adventurers. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The Moselle Route: This 112 km bike route stretches from Metz in France to Koblenz in Germany and passes through Remich. It is perfect for a multi-day trip.

  • The Remich Nature Trail: This hike takes visitors through the town’s vineyards and provides an excellent opportunity to learn about the region’s wine production.

  • The Mullerthal Trail: This long-distance hike takes hikers through Luxembourg’s beautiful Mullerthal region, which is home to waterfalls, forests, and unique rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Remich: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours: As mentioned above, wine tasting tours are a must-do in Remich.

  • River Cruises: Take a relaxing boat ride along the Moselle River and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: For a unique experience, consider taking a hot air balloon ride with beautiful views of the town and surrounding vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Remich

Day 1: Explore the town and visit St. Nicholas Church and Hesperange Castle.

Day 2: Take a river cruise and visit the Moselle vineyards, then enjoy a wine tasting tour.

Day 3: Go hiking on the Remich Nature Trail and enjoy the views of the vineyards.

Day 4: Cycle along the Moselle Route, stopping at charming towns along the way.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride for a unique perspective of the town and vineyards below.

Day 6: Visit the Musee A Possen and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Day 7: Relax in the Remich Public Gardens and enjoy the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Remich

Remich is known for its excellent wine production, but the town also has many delicious food options.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Friture: Tiny fried fish, usually served as a snack or appetizer.

  • Judd mat Gaardebounen: A traditional Luxembourgish dish of smoked pork neck with potatoes and beans.

  • Kachkeis: Melted cheese served with bread, usually served as a hearty snack.

Local Beverages

  • Riesling: Remich is known for its Riesling wines, which are dry and slightly fruity.

  • Pinot Noir: Another popular wine variety in Remich, Pinot Noir is a red wine that pairs well with meat dishes.

Where to Stay in Remich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Remich has a few hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to choose from:

  • Hotel Saint-Nicolas: This three-star hotel is located in the town center and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  • Hostellerie des Pecheurs: Located on the banks of the river, this hotel offers beautiful views and cozy rooms.

  • Guesthouse Buergener Reinhardsmuhle: This guesthouse is located in a historic building and offers a charmingly rustic stay.

What to Buy in Remich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: The town’s excellent wine production means wine is an obvious souvenir to bring home.

  • Pottery and Ceramics: The Moselle Valley region has a rich pottery tradition, with many local artists producing unique pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Remich

Luxembourg is a multilingual and multicultural country, so it is essential to be respectful and open-minded in Remich. Locals are friendly and welcoming, so taking the time to learn some basic Luxembourgish words and phrases can go a long way in making a good impression.

Best Time to Visit Remich

The best time to visit Remich is from May to October, when the weather is mild, and the vineyards are lush and green. The wine festival in September is a highlight of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Remich

Do:

  • Be respectful of local traditions and customs.

  • Learn a few basic Luxembourgish words and phrases.

  • Try the local wine and cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended in public areas.

  • Don’t take photos inside churches without permission.

  • Don’t be too loud or disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Remich

Remich is a safe town, and violent crime is uncommon. However, visitors should take usual precautions, such as keeping valuables secure.

Health in Remich

Luxembourg has an excellent healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and medical centers in the area. Visitors should have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Remich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Remich

The nearest airport is Luxembourg Airport, which is around 25 km away from Remich. From the airport, travelers can take a taxi or public transportation to the town.

Bus Stations Near Remich

The Remich bus station is located near the town’s center, and travelers can catch buses to other parts of the country from there.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Remich by train, with the nearest train station being located in Luxembourg City. From there, travelers can catch a bus or taxi to Remich.

How to Get Around in Remich

Remich is a small town that is easily walkable. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a bus to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Remich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Luxembourg City: The capital of Luxembourg is just 20 km away and offers a wide range of museums, restaurants, and cultural landmarks.

  • Mullerthal Region: Located in the eastern part of Luxembourg, this region is known for its beautiful forests and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Remich is a must-visit for anyone looking for a relaxing yet adventurous vacation in the heart of Europe. With its stunning vineyards, charming cultural landmarks, and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in this beautiful town.
