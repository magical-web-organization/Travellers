Introduction to Toulouse

Nestled in the south-west of France, Toulouse is a vibrant city that offers visitors a rich cultural heritage, scenic views, and a lively food and drink scene. Often referred to as the ‘pink city’ due to the pinkish-red brick that adorns many of its buildings, Toulouse is home to over 1.3 million inhabitants who are proud of their history and traditions. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do and see in Toulouse, as well as provide tips on local cuisine, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Toulouse

No visit to Toulouse is complete without seeing some of its iconic landmarks and attractions. The following list highlights several must-visit places in Toulouse:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Basilique Saint-Sernin: This 11th-century Romanesque church is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is widely regarded as one of the most impressive religious buildings in France.
  • Musée des Augustins: Housed in a former monastery, this museum is home to an impressive collection of art and sculpture dating back to the Middle Ages.
  • Musée Saint-Raymond: This museum features Roman artifacts and is located in a former 16th-century college.
  • Cité de l’Espace: Located on the outskirts of Toulouse, this space-themed park features interactive exhibits and a planetarium.

Natural Attractions of Toulouse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canal du Midi: This 17th-century canal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and provides a picturesque setting for cycling or walking.
  • Jardin des Plantes: This botanical garden features over 3,000 plants and is a popular location for picnics and relaxation.
  • Pont Neuf: This 16th-century bridge offers stunning views of the Garonne River and is a popular spot for a romantic stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toulouse

If you’re looking for an active way to explore Toulouse, the city offers numerous hiking and biking routes that are perfect for exploring the surrounding countryside. Several popular routes include:

  • The Canal du Midi Trail: This 240-kilometer trail runs from Toulouse to the Mediterranean Sea and takes in beautiful countryside, charming towns, and historic landmarks.
  • The Quercy Greenway: This 100-kilometer trail runs from Cahors to Montauban, taking in the banks of the Tarn and Garonne rivers, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Toulouse: Activities and Tours

Toulouse offers a range of activities and tours for visitors, from hot-air balloon rides over the city to wine tasting in nearby vineyards. Some of the best things to do and see in Toulouse include:

  • Capitol Square: This impressive square is home to the city’s town hall and opera house and provides a great starting point for exploring Toulouse.
  • Toulouse-Lautrec Museum: Located in the former family home of the French artist Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec, this museum houses a collection of his works, as well as a range of temporary exhibitions.
  • Wine Tours: Toulouse is at the center of the wine-growing region of Gascony, and there are numerous vineyards in the surrounding countryside that offer tours and tastings.
  • Hot-Air Balloon Rides: Take a bird’s-eye view of the city and surrounding countryside with a hot-air balloon ride that offers stunning views of Toulouse and the Pyrenees Mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Toulouse

Toulouse is known for its hearty and rustic cuisine, which features traditional dishes made from local ingredients. Whether you’re looking for a classic cassoulet or a sweet treat, Toulouse has something to offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cassoulet: This hearty stew is made with white beans, duck, and sausage and is a staple of Toulouse cuisine.
  • Foie Gras: A local specialty, foie gras is made from duck or goose liver and is often served with toast or brioche.
  • Toulouse Sausage: Made from pork and flavored with red wine and garlic, this sausage is often served grilled or with beans.
  • Garbure: A hearty vegetable and meat soup that is often served as a main course.

Local Beverages

  • Vin de Pays d’Oc: This local wine is produced in the surrounding region and is often paired with traditional Toulouse dishes.
  • Cocktails at Le Whisky Bar: This trendy bar serves up a range of cocktails and spirits sourced from around the world, including a huge range of whiskies.
  • Brasserie de la Tupina: This local brewery specializes in craft beer and offers tours and tastings at their brewery in nearby Albi.

Where to Stay in Toulouse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Toulouse offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Toulouse include:

  • Hôtel Le Capitole: Located in the heart of the city, this luxury hotel offers stunning views of Capitol Square and easy access to Toulouse’s top attractions.
  • Aparthotel Adagio Toulouse Parthenon: This aparthotel offers well-equipped apartments with kitchenettes, making it perfect for families or those looking for self-catering accommodation.
  • The Flying Seagull Hostel: This friendly and welcoming hostel is located in the Saint-Cyprien district, close to the Canal du Midi and offers dormitory-style accommodation.

What to Buy in Toulouse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from Toulouse, the following local products are worth considering:

  • Violet sweets: These sweet treats are made from local violets and provide a tangy and sweet flavor.
  • Cassoulet kits: Bring home the ingredients to make your own Toulouse cassoulet, including white beans and Toulouse sausage in a kit.
  • Foie gras: A popular local delicacy, foie gras is available to buy at specialty food stores in Toulouse.

Local Culture and Customs of Toulouse

Toulouse is known for its rich culture and traditions, including the following customs and practices:

  • Carnaval de Toulouse: Taking place in February and March, this colorful carnival is a highlight of the Toulouse calendar and features parades, parties, and a range of cultural activities.
  • Basque Pelota: A traditional game that involves hitting a ball against a wall with your hand, Basque Pelota is a popular pastime in Toulouse and the surrounding region.
  • Duck Hunting: Hunting ducks is a popular pastime in Toulouse, particularly in the nearby Camargue region.

Best Time to Visit Toulouse

Toulouse is a great destination to visit at any time of year, although the best time to visit depends on your preferences and what you want to do. The following tips can help you decide when to visit:

  • Summer: Summer is a great time to visit Toulouse if you enjoy warm weather and an abundance of outdoor activities.
  • Spring and Autumn: These seasons offer milder weather and are a great time to explore the city’s cultural attractions and go hiking or biking in the surrounding countryside.
  • Winter: While Toulouse can get cold in winter, the season offers a range of festive activities, including Christmas markets and ice skating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toulouse

To make the most of your stay in Toulouse, consider the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Learn some basic French phrases to help with communication.
  • Try some of the local cuisine, including cassoulet, foie gras, and other regional specialties.
  • Visit the local markets and try some of the fresh produce available.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the nearby countryside, which offers some great hiking and biking opportunities.
  • Expect everyone to speak English – French is the main language in Toulouse.
  • Forget to bring comfortable shoes – Toulouse is a walkable city, and you’ll likely spend a lot of time on foot.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toulouse

Toulouse is a relatively safe city, although visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Pickpocketing and petty crime can occur in busy tourist areas, so keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, particularly in unlit areas.
  • Stick to well-lit and busy areas, particularly if you’re not familiar with Toulouse and its layout.

Health in Toulouse

There are no specific health concerns for visitors to Toulouse, although it’s always a good idea to ensure you have adequate travel insurance and pack any necessary medications.

Getting to Toulouse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Toulouse is well-connected to other cities in France and Europe, with numerous transportation options available.

Airports Near Toulouse

  • Toulouse Blagnac Airport is located around 8km from the city center and is the main airport serving Toulouse.
  • Carcassonne Airport is located around 105km from Toulouse and offers connections to a range of destinations in Europe.

Bus Stations Near Toulouse

  • Gare Routière de Toulouse is the main bus station in Toulouse and offers connections to cities throughout France and Europe.

Other Options

  • Toulouse-Matabiau Railway Station provides rail links to other cities in France and Europe, and there is also a tram network and bus system in operation throughout Toulouse.

How to Get Around in Toulouse

Toulouse is a relatively walkable city, with many of its attractions and landmarks located in central areas. However, there are also numerous public transportation options available, including:

  • Metro and Tram: Toulouse has two metro lines and three tram lines that are operated by Tisséo.
  • Bus: Toulouse has a comprehensive bus system that is operated by Tisséo and provides connections to the surrounding suburbs.
  • Cycling: Toulouse also has a well-developed and well-connected cycling network, with numerous bike rental options available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Toulouse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond Toulouse, there are numerous nearby destinations and excursions worth considering. Some popular options include:

  • Albi: A picturesque town located around 80km from Toulouse, Albi is home to a stunning cathedral and a range of historic landmarks.
  • Carcassonne: Located around 100km from Toulouse, Carcassonne is a fortified town that offers a range of cultural and historic attractions.
  • The Pyrenees Mountains: Located just south of Toulouse, the Pyrenees Mountains offer stunning views and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and skiing.

Conclusion

Toulouse is a vibrant and historic city that offers visitors a range of cultural, scenic, and culinary attractions. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll have everything you need to plan your visit to Toulouse, including a list of must-visit sights, local delicacies to try, where to stay, and transportation options.
