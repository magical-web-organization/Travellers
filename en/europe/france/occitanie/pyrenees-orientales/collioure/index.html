Introduction to Collioure

Collioure is a beautiful fishing village nestled in Pyrénées-Orientales, in the Occitanie region of southern France. It is known for its attractive beaches, art galleries and studios, and dynamic history. The town also offers excellent accommodation and dining options. Collioure is just a few kilometers from Spain on the Mediterranean coast and can easily be accessed by car or public transportation. Tourists flock here in summer, and it’s a favorite among locals for weekend getaways.

List of Places to Visit in Collioure

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Collioure Castle

This 13th-century castle was built by the Knights Templar to serve as a fortress. Today, it serves as a museum and is a must-visit for history buffs. The castle overlooks the Mediterranean Sea and provides a picturesque view of the bay.

Notre-Dame des Anges

Notre-Dame des Anges is a Romanesque-style church built in the 17th century. It is a significant cultural site and is visible from almost every part of the town. The church hosts many cultural events and concerts throughout the year.

Museum of Modern Art

The Museum of Modern Art exhibits art from the late 19th and early 20th centuries, including works by Matisse, Derain, and Chagall. The museum is situated at the heart of the historic district of Collioure.

Natural Attractions of Collioure: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beaches

There are several beaches in Collioure where you can sunbathe, swim, or simply relax. Plage de la Balette is known for its crystal-clear waters and is perfect for snorkeling. Plage du Port d’Avall is calm and shallow, making it ideal for families with young children.

Chemin de Fauvisme

The Chemin de Fauvisme is an art trail taking visitors on a scenic walk around Collioure. The trail takes you past notable locations which were painted and frequented by famous artists including Matisse and Derain, who painted here in the Fauvist style.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Collioure

The area around Collioure also offers hiking and biking opportunities with the Pyrénées-Orientales Mountains serving as a backdrop. The Sentier du Littoral hiking path follows the coastline for over 20 km from Argeles sur Mer to Banyuls-sur-Mer. The route offers fantastic views of the Mediterranean Sea and the coast of Spain.

Best Things to Do in Collioure: Activities and Tours

Boat Tours

Several boat tours operate from the harbor of Collioure, exploring the coastline of the Mediterranean Sea. The tours last from a few hours to a full day and usually include swimming, snorkeling, and lunch on board.

Water Sports

Kayaking, Stand Up Paddling (SUP), and windsurfing are popular water sports in Collioure. Collioure is known for its gently lapping waves making it suitable for beginners.

Food and Drink Scene of Collioure

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Collioure include:

  • Anchoyade - a traditional dish made with anchovies, garlic, and olive oil
  • Bouillabaisse - this fish soup is typical of the Mediterranean region, and Collioure offers some of the best around.
  • Fruits de mer - seafood is the star of the show in Collioure. Visitors can enjoy freshly caught squid, sardines, and hake served with locally-grown vegetables.

Local Beverages

Collioure is also famous for its wine. The town is situated in the heart of the Collioure wine region, and visitors can enjoy tasting various wines made from the native Grenache and Mourvèdre grapes.

Where to Stay in Collioure: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Collioure has a range of accommodation options suitable for different travel styles and budgets.

  • Hôtel La Casa Pairal - a stylish hotel with elegant rooms and a swimming pool. Located in the heart of the town, it’s perfect for couples and those looking for a luxury stay.

  • Hotel Mediterranee - a budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms and a central location.

  • Mas Pauline - a cozy guesthouse surrounded by lush vegetation and vineyards. The guesthouse offers breakfast and an outdoor pool and is ideal for those looking for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.

What to Buy in Collioure: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Collioure is famous for its anchovies. You can buy them fresh in local markets or in jars that are perfect for taking home as gifts. The town also has many shops selling Catalan ceramics, Limoges porcelain, and beautiful handcrafted jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Collioure

Collioure has a rich history and culture. Several festivals are held throughout the year to celebrate historical events, including The Feast of St. Vincent, which is held in August to honor the patron saint of the town’s vineyards. Visitors can also witness traditional Catalan music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Collioure

The best time to visit Collioure is during the off-season from September to May when temperatures are mild, there are fewer crowds, and accommodation is more affordable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Collioure

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette, such as saying “bonjour” when entering stores and restaurants.
  • Visit the beaches early in the morning to avoid crowds of tourists.
  • Try local cuisine, including seafood, Catalan plates, and local wine or beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Swim in restricted areas, and always follow beach safety rules.
  • Be loud and disrespectful in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Collioure

Collioure is a safe destination. Tourists should always take standard safety precautions, such as keeping valuables in a safe place and not leaving them unattended in public areas.

Health in Collioure

Collioure has excellent health facilities and good sanitation. Visitors are advised to bring their prescription medications and travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Collioure: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Collioure

The nearest international airport is Barcelona-El Prat Airport in Spain, which is located 200 km from Collioure. The Perpignan-Rivesaltes Airport is located 35 km away, with several direct flights from European cities, including Paris and London.

Bus Stations Near Collioure

The Collioure bus station serves the town and nearby areas, including Perpignan and other destinations in the Pyrénées-Orientales.

Other Options

Traveling by car is a popular option in Collioure, giving visitors more flexibility in exploring the area. Visitors can also take the train to Perpignan and then take a bus or taxi to Collioure.

How to Get Around in Collioure

Walking and cycling are the most popular modes of transportation in Collioure. Taxis and buses are also available, but they are less convenient as the town is small.

Where to Go Next from Collioure: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can go on a day trip to the nearby cities of Narbonne and Carcassonne or explore some of the nearby vineyards. The Dali Museum in Figueres, Spain is also just an hour’s drive away.

Conclusion

Collioure is an incredible destination with something for everyone. It’s an ideal location for travelers to experience the beautiful beaches, culture, history, wine, and food of France. With its mild climate, historical significance, and natural beauty, Collioure is truly a one-of-a-kind destination.
