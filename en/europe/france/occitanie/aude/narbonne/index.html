Introduction to Narbonne

Narbonne is a beautiful city located in Aude, in Occitanie, France. It’s a city steeped in history, with origins dating back to the Roman era. It was an important port city back then, and today it still boasts a lively and vibrant atmosphere, making it a great destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Narbonne

If you’re planning a trip to Narbonne, you’re in for a treat. There are plenty of attractions and places to visit in and around the city. Here are some of the best places to visit in Narbonne:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Narbo Via – a museum showcasing the rich history of Narbonne and its Roman roots.
  • Palais des Archevêques – a stunning 13th-century palace with beautiful gardens and views over the Canal de la Robine.
  • The Horreum – an underground museum showcasing the remains of a Roman warehouse.

Natural Attractions of Narbonne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Halles de Narbonne – a picturesque indoor market selling everything from wine to seafood.
  • The Canal de la Robine – a scenic canal lined with trees, perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • Parc des Sports et de l’Amitié – a beautiful park with lakes and gardens, perfect for a picnic or a game of tennis.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narbonne

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, Narbonne has plenty of hiking and biking routes to choose from. Here are some of the best routes in Narbonne:

  • Canal de Jonction – a 10 km canal path perfect for biking or walking.
  • The Chemin de la Robine – a 15 km hiking route with stunning views of the countryside and the canal.

Best Things to Do in Narbonne: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Narbonne, no matter what your interests are. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Narbonne:

  • Wine Tasting – Narbonne is located in the Languedoc wine region which is famous for its delicious wine. Take a wine tasting tour and learn more about the local wine industry.
  • Boat Ride – take a boat ride along the Canal de la Robine and enjoy the stunning views from the water.
  • Beach Day – Narbonne Plage is just a short drive away from the city and is a beautiful beach for a day of sun, sand, and sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Narbonne

If you’re planning to spend a week in Narbonne, here’s a great itinerary to follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Narbonne

Narbonne is a culinary hotspot, with a wide range of restaurants, cafes and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Narbonne:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cassoulet – a hearty stew made with white beans, meat, and sausages.
  • Macarons – delicious almond cookies that make the perfect dessert or snack.
  • Rouille – a garlic and saffron mayo which is a traditional accompaniment to fish stew.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wine – Narbonne is located in the Languedoc wine region, so there are plenty of great local wines to try.
  • Blanquette de Limoux – a sparkling wine produced in the Limoux area, near Narbonne.
  • Pastis – a traditional anise-flavored aperitif that is popular in the south of France.

Where to Stay in Narbonne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Narbonne, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Narbonne:

  • Hotel de France - located in the heart of Narbonne, offering comfortable rooms and great views.
  • Novotel Narbonne - a modern hotel with a pool and terrace.
  • Les Volets Bleus - a charming guesthouse in the countryside near Narbonne, perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Narbonne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Narbonne is famous for its wine, honey, and ceramics. Here are some souvenirs and local products worth taking home:

  • Wine – take home a bottle of local Languedoc wine.
  • Honey – the honey produced in the nearby mountains has a unique flavor and is great for cooking.
  • Ceramics – Narbonne is known for its ceramics, and you can find beautiful hand-made pieces to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Narbonne

Narbonne has a rich cultural heritage that is still alive and well today. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take great pride in their cultural traditions. The Occitan language is still spoken in the region, and you’ll see the signs of the language around the city.

Best Time to Visit Narbonne

The best time to visit Narbonne is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be hot and crowded, but it’s also the best time to enjoy the beaches nearby.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narbonne

Do:

  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Explore the city’s historical attractions.
  • Take a boat ride along the Canal de la Robine.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the local culture.
  • Expect everyone to speak English.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Narbonne

Narbonne is a safe city overall, but you should still take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in quiet areas. Be careful with your belongings and keep an eye on your valuables.

Health in Narbonne

There are no specific health concerns in Narbonne but make sure to carry your health insurance card with you at all times. The emergency number is 112.

Getting to Narbonne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Narbonne is located in the south of France and is easily accessible by public transportation and car.

Airports Near Narbonne

The closest airport to Narbonne is the Beziers Airport (BZR), which is about 35 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Narbonne

Narbonne has a centrally located bus station, with regular services to other destinations in France and Europe.

Other Options

Narbonne is easily accessible by car, and there are several highways that pass by the city.

How to Get Around Narbonne

The best way to get around Narbonne is by foot or bike. The city is small and compact, so it’s easy to walk around and explore. There are also several bike rental shops in the city.

Where to Go Next from Narbonne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Narbonne is located in the south of France and is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some of the best nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Carcassonne – a stunning medieval fortress city just an hour’s drive away.
  • Montpellier – a vibrant university city with plenty of culture and nightlife.
  • Marseille – a bustling port city with a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Narbonne is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and plenty of things to see and do. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or local cuisine, Narbonne has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover all that Narbonne has to offer.
