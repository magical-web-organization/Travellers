Are you planning a trip to Béziers, France? This charming city in the Occitanie region offers an abundance of cultural attractions, scenic views, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, there’s something in Béziers for you. In this guide, we’ll share everything you need to know to plan the perfect visit.

Introduction to Béziers

Located in the department of Hérault, Béziers is a historic city that is known for its Roman and medieval heritage. With a population of around 78,000 people, it’s the second-largest city in the region after Montpellier. Béziers is situated on a hill overlooking the Orb River, which makes it an ideal spot for beautiful views (and great Instagram photos!).

List of Places to Visit in Béziers

Béziers has no shortage of fascinating places to visit. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Béziers Cathedral: This impressive cathedral dates back to the 13th century and boasts stunning Gothic architecture.
  2. The Fonserannes Locks: These locks are located on the Canal du Midi and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  3. The Musée des Beaux-Arts: This museum displays a wide-ranging collection of art, from ancient Greek vases to modern works.

Natural Attractions of Béziers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Les Neuf Écluses de Fonséranes: This impressive feat of engineering comprises nine locks on the Canal du Midi.
  2. The Canal du Midi: This 17th-century canal links the Garonne River to the Mediterranean Sea and offers beautiful views.
  3. Plateau des Poètes: This delightful park offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Béziers

If you’re a fan of outdoor adventures, Béziers won’t disappoint. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try:

  1. The Canal du Midi cycle path: This 240-kilometre route takes you along the banks of the the Canal du Midi and offers beautiful scenery.
  2. The Chemin des Oliviers: This 7-kilometre circular walk starts in the village of Cazouls-les-Béziers and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. The Sentier Cathare: This famous hiking trail stretches for 240 kilometres and takes you through the rugged terrain of the Pyrenees.

Best Things to Do in Béziers: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, Béziers offers a variety of activities for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Take a boat trip along the Canal du Midi: This is a great way to see the region from a different perspective.
  2. Visit a local winery: The Languedoc region is known for its excellent wine, and there are many wineries in and around Béziers that offer tastings.
  3. Attend a music festival: Béziers hosts several music festivals throughout the year, including the Festival de Musique de Béziers in June.

Week-Long Itinerary for Béziers

If you’re planning to spend a week in Béziers, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Béziers

No trip to Béziers would be complete without sampling the local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cassoulet: This hearty casserole is made with meat, beans, and vegetables and is a regional speciality.
  2. Tielle: This savoury pie is filled with squid, tomato, onion, and spices.
  3. Fougasse: This bread is flavoured with olives, garlic, or bacon and is perfect for snacking on the go.

Local Beverages

  1. Languedoc wine: This region is known for its excellent wine, so be sure to try a glass or two while you’re in Béziers.
  2. Pastis: This anise-flavoured liqueur is a popular aperitif in southern France.
  3. Bière de Pézenas: This local beer is brewed in the nearby town of Pézenas and is perfect for a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Béziers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Béziers has a variety of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some recommended options:

  1. Hôtel Impérator: This elegant hotel is located in the city centre and boasts stylish rooms and a restaurant.
  2. Le Petit Platane: This charming guesthouse is situated in a renovated 18th-century townhouse and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.
  3. L’Octopus: This trendy hostel is located near the train station and offers shared and private rooms.

What to Buy in Béziers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift? Here are some local products to consider:

  1. Languedoc wine: Take a bottle (or several) of the region’s excellent wine home with you.
  2. La Tielle: You can buy this delicious savoury pie to take home and share with friends and family.
  3. Fougasse: Try making your own version of this regional bread at home with a mix from a local bakery.

Local Culture and Customs of Béziers

Béziers is known for its colourful traditions and rich history. Here are some interesting customs and cultural practices to keep in mind:

  • Bullfighting is a local tradition, and Béziers hosts a popular bullfighting festival every August.
  • The city’s Roman heritage is celebrated every year with a festival called Les Grands Jeux Romains.
  • The local dialect is Occitan, which is still spoken by some people in the region.

Best Time to Visit Béziers

The best time to visit Béziers depends on your interests and what you want to experience. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Summer (June to August) is the busiest and most popular time to visit, thanks to the warm weather and festivals.
  • Spring (April to May) and Autumn (September to October) are cooler and less busy, making them great times to visit if you want to avoid the crowds.
  • Winter (November to March) is the quietest time of year and can be a great option if you don’t mind cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Béziers

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your time in Béziers:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, including the regional wine and cassoulet.
  • Take a boat trip along the Canal du Midi for stunning views of the city.
  • Learn about the city’s history and cultural heritage by visiting museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended, especially in busy tourist areas.
  • Forget to pack appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor adventures or visits to cultural attractions.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions, such as the practice of bullfighting.

Safety and Health

Béziers is a relatively safe city for tourists, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Béziers

  • Keep your belongings secure and don’t leave them unattended in public areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in busy tourist areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in deserted areas.

Health in Béziers

  • The tap water is safe to drink in Béziers.
  • Mosquitoes can be a problem during the summer months, so be sure to pack mosquito repellent.
  • If you need medical attention, head to the nearest hospital or clinic.

Getting to Béziers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Béziers is well-connected to other parts of France and Europe, making it easy to get to. Here are your transportation options:

Airports Near Béziers

The nearest airport is Béziers Cap d’Agde Airport, which is located around 15 kilometres from the city centre. Other airports in the region include Montpellier Airport and Carcassonne Airport.

Bus Stations Near Béziers

Béziers has a bus station that is served by several regional and national bus companies, including Flixbus and Ouibus.

Other Options

If you’re travelling by car, Béziers is located on the A9 motorway, which connects to several major cities in France and Spain.

How to Get Around in Béziers

Once you’re in Béziers, there are several ways to get around:

  • Walking is a great way to see the city centre and get some exercise.
  • Buses are available for longer journeys or trips to the outskirts of the city.
  • Taxis can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • Car and bike rental options are available if you want to explore the region at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Béziers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in the region, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to check out:

  • Montpellier: This lively university city is located just 80 kilometres from Béziers and offers plenty of cultural attractions and nightlife.
  • Carcassonne: This medieval walled city is located around 100 kilometres from Béziers and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Narbonne: This charming town on the Mediterranean coast is known for its Gothic cathedral and bustling market.

Conclusion

Béziers may not be as well-known as some other French cities, but it has plenty to offer visitors. With a rich history, beautiful scenery, and delicious food and drink, it’s the perfect destination for a cultural, outdoor, or culinary adventure. We hope this guide has inspired you to visit Béziers and explore all it has to offer.
