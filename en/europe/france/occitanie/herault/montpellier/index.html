Introduction to Montpellier

Montpellier is a beautiful city located in the south of France, in the region of Occitanie. The city is known for its charming historic district, beautiful beaches, and thriving student population. Montpellier is the eighth-largest city in France and boasts a rich history and culture. Whether you’re interested in hiking, art museums, or delicious local cuisine, Montpellier has something for everyone.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montpellier

Montpellier has so much to offer that you could easily spend a week exploring the city. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the historic district
Day 2 Visit the Musée Fabre
Day 3 Go wine tasting in the Pic Saint-Loup region
Day 4 Visit local beaches, such as Palavas-les-Flots or Carnon beach
Day 5 Take a day trip to the charming town of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert
Day 6 Explore Montpellier’s natural attractions, including Parc Zoologique de Montpellier
Day 7 Relax with a local food tour or visit local markets to taste delicious French cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Montpellier

Montpellier boasts a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled shellfish
  • Pélardon cheese
  • Sètiennes (stewed snippets of beef)
  • Aligot (a cheesy mashed potato side dish)

Local Beverages

  • Languedoc wine
  • Pastis (anise-flavored liqueur)

Local Culture and Customs of Montpellier

Montpellier has a unique culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • The siesta is still observed in Montpellier- many shops and businesses close for a few hours in the afternoon.
  • Montpellier is known for its student population and vibrant nightlife.

Best Time to Visit Montpellier

The best time to visit Montpellier is from May to September when the weather is warm, and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montpellier

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Montpellier:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the historic district
  • Take a walking tour of the city

Don’t:

  • Drive or park in the historic district
  • Stay out too late on weekday nights (many places close early during the week)

Safety and Health

Montpellier is a safe and healthy city to visit. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Montpellier

  • Be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas such as the Place de la Comédie or on public transportation.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas.

Health in Montpellier

  • French tap water is safe to drink, but you can also buy bottled water.
  • Make sure you have appropriate travel insurance and health coverage before your trip.

Montpellier is a vibrant and exciting city with plenty to offer visitors.
