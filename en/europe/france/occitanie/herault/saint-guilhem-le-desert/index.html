Introduction to Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Located in the Hérault department of the Occitanie region in France, Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a small medieval village that attracts visitors with its picturesque buildings, cultural and natural attractions, and excellent hiking and biking trails. The village is nestled in the Gellone Valley and has been designated as one of the “Most Beautiful Villages in France.”

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abbey of Gellone: The 9th-century Romanesque abbey was founded by William of Gellone, a Frankish count, to house the relics of Saint Guilhem. It is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and houses valuable artworks and manuscripts.
  • Hérault River Gorges: The river flows through a deep and picturesque gorge, offering stunning views of the surrounding cliffs and natural formations.
  • Museum of Arts and Popular Traditions: The museum showcases the traditional lifestyle and crafts of the rural Hérault region, including exhibits of lace-making, pottery, and wine-making.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert Cirque: This natural amphitheater is surrounded by cliffs, and waterfalls, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Grotte de Clamouse: Located just outside the village, this towering cave system boasts amazing rock formations, underground lakes and rivers, and storybook tunnels.
  • Navacelles Cirque: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Navacelles is a geological marvel located about an hour’s drive from Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert. It offers stunning views of a canyon and waterfall plunging into a natural circular basin.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

  • Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert to Saint-Jean-de-Fos: A 4.5 km (2.8 miles) hike through the Gellone Valley and along the Hérault River, passing through vineyards and olive groves, and offering stunning views of the village and the surrounding hillsides.
  • Chemin des Pèlerins: This ancient pilgrimage route from Arles to Santiago de Compostela passes through Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert and offers excellent hiking and biking options, with breathtaking panoramas and historic sites along the way.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Hérault is renowned for its wine, and a tour of the vineyards and wineries around Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a must-do activity. You can taste regional wines, such as the Grenache-based red, the Picpoul de Pinet white, and the Clairette du Languedoc sparkling, while learning about local viticulture practices and history.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The Hérault River offers excellent opportunities for water sports enthusiasts, with plenty of options for kayaking, canoeing, and tubing, as well as swimming and relaxing on the riverbanks.
  • Village Tour: Take a guided walking tour of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert, led by a local expert, to discover the hidden gems and stories of this atmospheric medieval village.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tielle Sétoise: A savory pie filled with cuttlefish, tomatoes, onions, and spices, and served as a main course or a snack. It is a regional specialty, typical of the fishing villages along the Mediterranean coast.
  • Daube de bœuf à la languedocienne: A beef stew cooked with red wine, vegetables, garlic, and herbs, and served with crusty bread or pasta. It is a hearty and flavorful dish, perfect for cold days.
  • Fougasse d’Aigues-Mortes: A sweet bread shaped like a leaf and flavored with orange blossom water and anise seeds. It is a traditional dessert of the Languedoc region, and is served on festive occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Languedoc Wines: The Hérault region is known for its diverse and high-quality wines, which include reds, whites, rosés, and sparkling wines. You can taste the wines at local wineries or at specialty wine bars in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert.
  • Pastis: A herbal liqueur made with anise, fennel, and licorice, and served with cold water and ice. It is a popular aperitif in the south of France, and is said to have tonic and digestive properties.
  • Bière artisanale: Craft beer is a growing trend in the Languedoc region, with many microbreweries producing innovative and flavorful beers, often using local ingredients such as lavender, honey, and chestnuts.

Where to Stay in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge de Val Moureze: A charming guesthouse located just outside Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert, with comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving regional cuisine.
  • Hôtel Restaurant des Gorges de l’Hérault: A cozy hotel situated in a former silk factory, overlooking the Hérault River and offering easy access to hiking and biking trails.
  • La Maison de Leonie: A stylish bed and breakfast located in a renovated 18th-century house, with spacious rooms, a garden terrace, and a gourmet breakfast.

What to Buy in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coral Jewelry: Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is known for its fine coral jewelry, which is handcrafted by local artisans and sold in many shops and markets around the village.
  • Fougasse: The sweet bread mentioned earlier in the article is often packaged and sold in souvenir shops around the village.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: The Languedoc region has a rich tradition of pottery and ceramics, and Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert has many shops and workshops where you can buy handmade plates, bowls, and vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a village steeped in history and traditions, and it is important to respect and honor the local culture when visiting. Some general tips include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and museums.
  • Greet locals with a polite “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir.”
  • Follow the posted rules and regulations when hiking or biking on the trails.
  • Respect the natural environment and do not leave any litter or waste behind.
  • Try to learn some basic French phrases, as English is not always widely spoken in the village.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

The best time to visit Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and all the attractions and activities are open. July and August can be quite crowded, however, so consider visiting in May or September for a more peaceful experience. The village is especially beautiful in the fall, when the surrounding hills and vineyards turn golden and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

  • Do try the local cuisine and wine, and explore the nearby vineyards and wineries.

  • Don’t enter private property or disturb the local flora and fauna.
  • Don’t neglect to wear comfortable shoes and clothing when hiking or biking.
  • Don’t forget to carry enough water and snacks on your outdoor adventures to stay hydrated and energized.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a safe and peaceful village, with very low crime rates and a friendly and welcoming community. However, it is always wise to take general safety precautions, such as:

  • Keep your valuables, documents, and passport in a secure and safe place.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas or poorly lit streets.
  • Be aware of possible pickpockets and scams in crowded tourist areas.
  • Follow the posted signs and warnings when hiking or biking on the trails.

Health in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert has a few small medical clinics and pharmacies, but for serious medical emergencies, you may need to go to the nearest hospital, which is in Montpellier, about 45 minutes away by car. It is advisable to have travel health insurance and carry a basic first aid kit when visiting.

Getting to Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

The nearest international airport to Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is the Montpellier-Méditerranée Airport (MPL), which is about 50 km (31 miles) away. Other local airports include Béziers Cap d’Agde Airport (BZR) and Nîmes Alès Camargue Cévennes Airport (FNI).

Bus Stations Near Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

The Languedoc region has an efficient and extensive public transportation network, and there are several bus lines that connect Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert to nearby towns and cities, such as Montpellier, Béziers, and Lodève. The bus station of Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is situated at the entrance of the village.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a taxi service to get to Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert, as the village is well-connected to the regional highways and roads. The village also has a large parking area, just outside the main entrance, where you can park your car for free and walk to the attractions.

How to Get Around in Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert

Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a small and pedestrian-friendly village, with most of the attractions and amenities located within walking distance from each other. If you prefer to ride a bike, you can rent one at one of the local rental shops or from your accommodation. There is also a free shuttle service, La Navette des Gorges de l’Hérault, which operates during the summer months and connects Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert with nearby villages and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is ideally situated for exploring the many attractions and activities of the Languedoc region, and there are many nearby destinations and excursions that you can take. Some popular options include:

  • Montpellier: The vibrant university city of Montpellier has a rich cultural heritage, many museums and galleries, and a lively nightlife scene.
  • Pézenas: This historic town is famous for its antique shops, colorful markets, and well-preserved architecture, and makes for a charming day trip.
  • St-Guilhem-le-Désert to St-Guilhem to Saint-Gervais-sur-Mare: The scenic drive from Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert to Saint-Gervais-sur-Mare takes you through the rugged and wild hills of the Languedoc, with stunning views of the countryside and many hidden gems along the way.

Conclusion

Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a magical and enchanting destination that offers something for everyone, from history buffs to nature lovers, foodies to adventure seekers. With its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural beauty, and welcoming locals, Saint-Guilhem-le-Désert is a truly unforgettable destination that will leave you with memories that will last a lifetime.
