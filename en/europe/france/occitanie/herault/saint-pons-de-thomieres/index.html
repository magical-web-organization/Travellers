Introduction to Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

Nestled in the heart of the Haut-Languedoc Natural Park, Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is a small town known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty. Located in the Hérault department of Occitanie, in Southern France, this quaint village is an ideal location for a relaxing getaway.

With its cobbled streets, beautiful architecture and scenic views, Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the world. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or the great outdoors, this charming town has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de la Cloche et de la Sonnaille - This museum is dedicated to the history of bells and chimes, and features exhibits of ancient bells from all over the world.

  • Église Saint-Pons - This stunning church was built in the 12th century and is classified as a historical monument. Its imposing façade and beautiful stained-glass windows are a must-see.

  • Ancien Palais de Justice - Built in the 17th-century, the Old Palace of Justice is now home to the Municipal Library and the Tourist Office.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Pons-de-Thomières: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gorges d’Héric - This area is popular with hikers and climbers, who come to explore the stunning waterfalls and unique rock formations.

  • Plateau du Caroux - This beautiful mountain range is perfect for hiking, cycling, and skiing during winter. With its breathtaking views and rich flora and fauna, it’s an ideal place to escape into nature.

  • Lac du Salagou - Located just a short drive from Saint-Pons-de-Thomières, this beautiful lake is surrounded by vineyards and olive groves. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, and boating, as well as picnics on the shore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

With its stunning natural scenery, Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are plenty of hiking and biking trails in the area that lead through forests, past stunning waterfalls, and up to mountain peaks. Some of the most popular routes include the GR7, which passes through Saint-Pons-de-Thomières on its way from Andorra to the Atlantic coast.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours - The area around Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is known for its wine production, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries in the area that offer wine-tasting tours.

  • Canoeing and Kayaking - The nearby Orb River is a popular destination for canoeing and kayaking, and there are plenty of rental companies that offer equipment and guided tours.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides - For a unique perspective on the area’s stunning natural scenery, consider a hot air balloon ride with one of the local companies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

Day 1: Explore the town of Saint-Pons-de-Thomières, taking in the historic sites and museums.

Day 2: Take a hike in the Gorges d’Héric, stopping to enjoy the stunning waterfalls along the way.

Day 3: Visit the nearby Lac du Salagou, where you can enjoy swimming, boating, and a picnic on the shore.

Day 4: Spend the day exploring the Plateau du Caroux, taking in the stunning views and rich flora and fauna of the area.

Day 5: Enjoy a wine-tasting tour of the local vineyards.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride for a unique perspective on the area’s stunning natural scenery.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the town’s local cuisine and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cassoulet - This hearty dish of white beans and meat is a regional specialty, and a must-try for anyone visiting the area.

  • Fougasse - A type of flatbread that is a popular snack throughout the region.

  • Roquefort Cheese - One of the most famous cheeses in France, Roquefort is an essential part of any visit to the area.

Local Beverages

  • Languedoc Wines - The area around Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is known for its wine production, particularly for reds.

  • Pastis - A popular anise-flavored liqueur that is often served as an aperitif.

  • Muscadet - A light, dry white wine that is perfect for sipping on a warm summer day.

Where to Stay in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge de Combes - This charming guesthouse is located just outside of town and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • L’Hôtel de l’Orb - This centrally located hotel is housed in a beautiful 19th-century building and features elegant rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

  • Le Camping du Caroux - For those who prefer the outdoors, this campsite offers stunning views of the mountains and easy access to hiking trails and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Roquefort Cheese - Take home a taste of the region with a piece of Roquefort cheese.

  • Languedoc Wines - Don’t forget to bring back a bottle or two of local wine, as a reminder of your time in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières.

  • Handmade Pottery - The area around Saint-Pons-de-Thomières has a rich tradition of pottery-making, and there are plenty of local studios where you can find unique pieces to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

As with many small towns in France, Saint-Pons-de-Thomières has a strong sense of local culture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect these customs, which include:

  • Greeting everyone you meet with a polite “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir”

  • Following local customs regarding meal times, such as eating a light lunch and a heavier dinner in the evening

  • Dressing modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites

Best Time to Visit Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

The best time to visit Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be quite hot and crowded, and many locals take their holidays during this time, resulting in some closures and reduced services.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is a generally safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night

  • Keep valuables, including cameras, phones, and passports, secure and out of sight

Health in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

Visitors to Saint-Pons-de-Thomières should take the usual precautions to avoid illness or injury, such as:

  • Drinking plenty of water to stay hydrated

  • Using sunscreen to protect skin from the sun’s rays

  • Wearing appropriate footwear and clothing while hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities

Getting to Saint-Pons-de-Thomières: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

The closest airports to Saint-Pons-de-Thomières are Montpellier-Méditerranée Airport (about 1.5 hours by car) and Carcassonne Airport (about 1.5 hours by car).

Bus Stations Near Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

The nearest major bus station is in nearby Béziers, which has connections to many major cities in France.

Other Options

The area around Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is best explored by car, as public transportation can be limited. Visitors can rent a car at the nearby airports or train stations.

How to Get Around in Saint-Pons-de-Thomières

The best way to get around Saint-Pons-de-Thomières is on foot, as the town is small and easily walkable. For those looking to explore the surrounding area, renting a car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Pons-de-Thomières: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Béziers - This historic town is known for its beautiful architecture and is home to the famous Béziers Feria, an annual festival celebrating bullfighting and wine.

  • Montpellier - This vibrant university town is a hub of culture and nightlife and offers visitors a taste of modern France.

  • Narbonne - This ancient Roman town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its beautiful cathedral and impressive historic sites.
