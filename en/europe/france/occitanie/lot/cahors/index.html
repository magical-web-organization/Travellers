Introduction to Cahors

Cahors is a lovely town located in the Occitanie region of southern France. This charming destination is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture.

The town is most famous for its iconic medieval bridge and beautiful vineyards. It is situated near the Lot River and surrounded by gorgeous hills, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.

In this guide, we will take a comprehensive look at the top things to do, see, and experience in Cahors, along with some practical tips and advice to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Cahors

Cahors offers a wealth of attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

With its rich history and vibrant culture, Cahors is home to several fascinating museums and historical places to explore, including:

Museum of Resistance

The Museum of Resistance is an excellent place to learn about the French Resistance during WWII. It offers an immersive and thought-provoking experience that will leave you feeling humbled and inspired.

Pont Valentré

Pont Valentré is one of the most iconic landmarks in Cahors. It is a stunning medieval bridge that dates back to the hundred years war. The bridge has seven arches and three towers along with the ramparts.

Cahors Cathedral

The Cathédrale Saint-Étienne de Cahors is a remarkable piece of French Gothic architecture from the 13th century. This magnificent cathedral boasts intricate carvings and beautiful stained glass windows that are truly breathtaking.

Natural Attractions of Cahors: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or just taking in the picturesque scenery, Cahors offers plenty of opportunities to enjoy the great outdoors. Here are some of the top natural attractions to check out:

Pech Merle Cave

Pech Merle Cave is a prehistoric cave in the Célé valley. It is known for its amazing array of stalactites, stalagmites, and cave drawings that are more than 25,000 years old.

The GR 36 Hiking Trail

If you’re into hiking, the GR 36 trail is definitely worth exploring. It offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside, as well as easy access to a variety of small villages along the way.

Lot River

The Lot River meanders through Cahors and its surroundings, providing visitors with ample opportunities for canoeing, kayaking or rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cahors

Cahors offers several great hiking and biking routes that are perfect for exploring the town’s beautiful surroundings. Some of the top routes worth checking out include:

The Lot Valley Bike Path

The Lot Valley Bike Path is a 160 km route that stretches from Cahors to Aiguillon in southwest France. This path follows the river through lovely countryside and villages and is perfect for those keen on staying fit and enjoying the views.

The Way of Saint James

The Way of Saint James (also known as ‘Santiago de Compostela’) is a 780 km hiking route that crosses France and ends in Spain. The route follows in the footsteps of medieval pilgrims and passes through some of France’s most beautiful regions, including Cahors.

Best Things to Do in Cahors: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fun and exciting things to do in Cahors, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some top activities and tours to consider adding to your itinerary:

Wine tasting in Cahors

Cahors is home to some of the best red wines in France that are made with Malbec grapes. There are plenty of vineyards open for tastings, and a tasting tour is a great way to explore the winemaking culture and experience the flavors of Cahors.

Visit the local markets

Cahors has a rich and diverse food scene. The local markets are a must-visit to get a taste of the local cuisine, from cheeses and charcuterie to fresh fruit and vegetables.

Hot Air Balloon Ride over Cahors

For a unique and unforgettable view of Cahors, book a hot air balloon ride. It is an excellent way to enjoy the beautiful countryside and get a spectacular view of the town itself.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cahors

If you have a week in Cahors, here is an itinerary you can follow to make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore Cahors town - Visit the Pont Valentré, Cathédrale Saint-Étienne, and the Museum of Resistance.
  • Day 2: Pech Merle Cave - Go on guided tours of the Prehistoric Pech Merle Cave with your family.
  • Day 3: Wine Tasting - Take part in a wine-tasting tour in Cahors
  • Day 4: Hiking the Way of St. James - Hike the famously picturesque Way of St. James for a whole day.
  • Day 5: Kayaking in the Lot River - Explore the Lot River by renting a kayak and paddle at your leisure.
  • Day 6: Hot Air Balloon Ride - Take up a hot-air balloon ride and experience breathtaking views.
  • Day 7: Day Trip to Rocamadour - Visit Rocamadour, a famous medieval pilgrimage site in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Cahors

Cahors is known for its rich culture and traditions, including the town’s famous vineyards, cuisine and regional festivals. The local people are friendly and welcoming, and you’ll likely find the town to be a warm and inviting place to visit.

Best Time to Visit Cahors

The best time to visit Cahors is from April to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are many festivals and celebrations to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cahors

  • Keep your valuables with you at all times
  • Travel in well-lit and well-populated areas
  • Follow the local laws and customs.

Health in Cahors

  • Make sure to pack appropriate clothing for varying temperatures
  • Carry any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Cahors: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cahors is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Cahors

  • Toulouse-Blagnac Airport (TLS), which is about 100km away from Cahors.

Bus Stations Near Cahors

  • Cahors Bus Station is the main intercity bus station and is located within walking distance from the town center.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available at Toulouse airport and in Cahors town for those who want to self-navigate.
  • Trains are also available, with regular routes to Paris and Languedoc.

How to Get Around Cahors

Cahors is a relatively small town, so walking is an excellent option for getting around. However, if you prefer, bike rental services, car rentals, and taxi services are also widely available.

Conclusion

Cahors is a charming destination that offers a perfect combination of natural beauty, history, culture, and gastronomy. Whether you’re interested in hiking, wine tasting, or exploring the region’s rich history, Cahors is the perfect destination for you. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful town. Bon voyage!
