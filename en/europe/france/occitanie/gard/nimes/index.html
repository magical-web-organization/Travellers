A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Nîmes, France

Nîmes is a beautiful city located in the Gard department of Occitanie, Southern France. This charming city is often referred to as the “Rome of France” due to its rich Roman history and stunning architecture.

This comprehensive travel guide provides helpful information to plan your trip to Nîmes. From the best places to visit and things to do to local customs, culture, and where to stay, this article will help you explore Nîmes in-depth.

List of Places to Visit in Nîmes

Nîmes is home to numerous places to visit and explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Nîmes:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arena of Nîmes: One of the most impressive Roman arenas in the world. Built in the 1st century AD, this arena is used as a bullfighting ring and concert venue.

  • Maison Carrée: A well-preserved temple built in the 1st century AD, dedicated to Gaius and Lucius Caesar, grandsons of Augustus.

  • Musée de la Romanité: A beautiful museum that offers an extensive collection of Roman artifacts, including mosaics, ceramics, and sculptures.

  • Jardins de la Fontaine: Beautiful gardens that include a Roman temple, a water tower, and a canal.

Natural Attractions of Nîmes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pont du Gard: A UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its impressive Roman aqueduct that spans the Gardon River.

  • Les Gorges du Gardon: A natural park that offers stunning views and hiking trails.

  • Camargue: A natural park that is a paradise for birdwatchers, horse riders, and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nîmes

Nîmes is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts, offering several popular routes that provide stunning views of the city and surrounding areas.

  • Roc de Gachonne: A popular route that passes through the Jardin des Plantes and is known for its stunning views over the city.

  • Plateau des Costières: A challenging, circular 20km route that offers beautiful views of the surrounding vineyards and olive groves.

Best Things to Do in Nîmes: Activities and Tours

Nîmes offers several exciting activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Nîmes:

  • Take a guided tour of the Arena of Nîmes: Explore this impressive Roman arena and learn about its history and uses.

  • Attend a bullfighting event: Experience the thrill of watching a bullfighting event in the Arena of Nîmes.

  • Visit a vineyard: Nîmes is known for its delicious wines, and taking a vineyard tour is a great way to learn about the production process and sample some of the local wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nîmes

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Nîmes, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Nîmes

Nîmes offers a rich culinary experience with its traditional dishes and drinks to savor. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brandade de Nîmes: A delicious fish dish made from salt cod mixed with olive oil, garlic, and mashed potatoes.

  • Pichade: A savory dish made with fried bread soaked in wine and topped with piperade (a mixture of onions, peppers, and tomatoes).

  • Tapenade: A dish made from pureed olives, capers, and anchovies, commonly served as an appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Pastis: A traditional French drink made from licorice and flavored with anise.

  • Costières-de-nîmes: A locally produced wine that comes in red, white, and rosé varieties.

  • Eau de Vie: A local spirit that is made from the distillation of fruits or wine.

Where to Stay in Nîmes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nîmes has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, whether you’re looking for luxury hotels or budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Nîmes:

  • Hotel Imperator Nîmes: A luxurious, four-star hotel in the heart of Nîmes, situated near the Arena of Nîmes.

  • Hotel de l’Amphithéâtre: A stylish, modern hotel located near the Arena of Nîmes.

  • Le Cheval Blanc: A charming, budget-friendly hotel situated in the city center.

  • Les Aubuns Country Hotel: A guesthouse located in the countryside, offering stunning views and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Nîmes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nîmes offers plenty of souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Nîmes:

  • Nîmes Denim: A unique, locally produced denim that is crafted using traditional techniques.

  • Picholine Olives: A type of olives exclusively grown in Nîmes that have a buttery taste, usually available in jars or vacuum packed.

  • Foutas: A traditional cotton cloth that is used as a beach towel, tablecloth, or wrap.

Local Culture and Customs of Nîmes

The local culture of Nîmes is colorful and vibrant. Here are some local customs to be aware of:

  • French is the official language, but English is also widely spoken.
  • Nîmes celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Feria de Nîmes, a bullfighting event that takes place in May and September.
  • Tipping is not mandatory, but it is appreciated in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Nîmes

The best time to visit Nîmes is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. The city is bustling with festivals and events during this time, making it an exciting time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nîmes

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Nîmes:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Take a vineyard tour and sample some of the locally produced wines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Avoid making loud noises, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Nîmes is generally a safe city for travelers. However, it is always advisable to take safety precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Nîmes

  • Avoid carrying valuables with you when exploring the city.
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night.
  • Be wary of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Nîmes

  • Make sure to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Be aware of the food and drink you consume to avoid food poisoning.

Getting to Nîmes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nîmes is well-connected to other major cities in France. Here are some transportation options to get to Nîmes:

Airports Near Nîmes

  • Nîmes–Alès–Camargue–Cévennes Airport (FNI): The nearest airport, located just a few kilometers outside of Nîmes.

  • Montpellier–Méditerranée Airport (MPL): Located approximately 52 km away from Nîmes.

Bus Stations Near Nîmes

  • Nîmes Bus Station: Located in the city center, offering easy access to other major cities in France.

Other Options

  • Train: Nîmes has a well-connected railway station that connects it to other major cities in France.

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car from any of the major car rental companies to explore Nîmes and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Nîmes

Nîmes is a small city, and most of the tourist attractions are located within walking distance. However, the city also has a reliable public transportation system that includes buses and trams.

Where to Go Next from Nîmes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra days to explore other nearby destinations from Nîmes, here are some great options:

  • Arles: A beautiful city located just 30km from Nîmes, known for its impressive Roman ruins and the paintings of Vincent Van Gogh.

  • Avignon: A historic city located 45 km from Nîmes, famous for its stunning papal palace and the annual Avignon Festival.

  • Pont du Gard: A UNESCO World Heritage site located just 22 km from Nîmes, known for its impressive Roman aqueduct.

Conclusion

Nîmes is a beautiful city that offers plenty of attractions, activities, and cultural experiences. Plan your itinerary accordingly and book your trip to Nîmes to explore this Roman city’s unique history and architecture. Enjoy your journey!
