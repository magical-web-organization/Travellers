Introduction to Millau

Whether you’re looking for a thrilling outdoor adventure or a peek into France’s rich cultural history, Millau has something for everyone. Located in Aveyron, a department in the Occitanie region of France, Millau is a small but vibrant city with a lot to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and foods to try in Millau.

List of Places to Visit in Millau

From museums to natural attractions, Millau has plenty of options for sightseeing. Here are some of the most popular places to visit while in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Millau Viaduct - This iconic bridge over the Tarn River is the tallest in the world, and a must-see for any visitor to Millau.
  • The Musée de Millau et des Grands Causses - This museum showcases the cultural history of the region, including traditional crafts and the impact of the local landscape on daily life.
  • The Beffroi, or bell tower, of Millau - Located in the city’s historic center, the Beffroi dates back to the 12th century and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Millau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Montpellier-le-Vieux - This natural park features impressive landscapes with unique rock formations that were shaped by erosion over millions of years.
  • Gorges du Tarn et de la Jonte - These dramatic river canyons are ideal for hiking, biking, and rock climbing.
  • The Causse du Larzac plateau - This expansive high plateau features rolling hills dotted with charming villages, ancient ruins, and scenic vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Millau

Millau’s natural surroundings are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Le Sentier des Gorges du Tarn - This famous trail follows the river through the stunning gorges of Tarn.
  • The GR71 trail - This long-distance hiking trail takes visitors across the Causse du Larzac plateau, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Vélorail de Larzac - Visitors can pedal along an abandoned railway track, exploring the region’s lush greenery and quaint villages.

Best Things to Do in Millau: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Millau offers plenty of other ways to explore the region. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Take a guided tour of the Roquefort cheese caves - Located just outside of Millau, these natural limestone caves are home to the world-renowned Roquefort cheese.
  • Go paragliding with Millau Parapente - Visitors can experience the thrill of soaring over Millau and its surrounding landscape.
  • Visit the weekly market in the city’s historic center - This bustling market offers fresh local produce, crafts, and other unique souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Millau

To make the most out of your trip to Millau, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Millau

No trip to Millau is complete without sampling some of the region’s delicious food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aligot - This traditional dish is made from mashed potatoes and melted cheese, served in soft and stretchy layers.
  • Tripous - This hearty dish uses the four stomachs of a lamb, combined with pork belly, garlic, and white wine.
  • Roquefort cheese - This world-famous blue cheese is aged in the natural limestone caves of Roquefort-sur-Soulzon, located just outside Millau.

Local Beverages

  • Marc de Raisin - This traditional brandy is made from the leftover pulp and skins of grapes from the local vineyards.
  • Bergeron, or Clairette de Die - This sparkling wine comes from the nearby Diois region, and is made from sweet Muscat grapes.

Where to Stay in Millau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Millau offers a variety of accommodations to fit every budget and traveler preference. Here are some of the best options:

  • Budget: Le Ridin - This hostel offers affordable dorms and private rooms, located just a short walk from the city center.
  • Mid-Range: Hôtel Emma Calvé - This charming hotel is set in a historic building, and offers spacious rooms with modern amenities.
  • Luxury: Château de Creissels - This stunning castle-hotel overlooks the Tarn River, and offers luxurious rooms and delicious gourmet dining.

What to Buy in Millau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

In addition to its mouth-watering food and drink, Millau offers plenty of souvenirs and local products to take home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some of the best options:

  • Leather gloves - Millau is known for its luxury leather gloves, which are made using traditional techniques that have been passed down for generations.
  • Pottery - The region is home to talented ceramic artists who create beautiful and unique pottery.
  • Local wines and spirits - Bring home a bottle of Marc de Raisin or Bergeron to impress your friends back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Millau

Millau is a city with a rich cultural history, and it’s worth taking the time to learn about the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • The Occitan language - While most people in Millau speak French, many still speak the local Occitan language, which has its own unique range of vocabulary and pronunciation.
  • Traditional festivals - Throughout the year, Millau hosts a variety of traditional festivals, such as the Fête de la Transhumance, which celebrates the seasonal migration of sheep to higher pastures in the mountains.
  • Respect for the environment - Millau’s natural surroundings are its greatest asset, and it’s important to respect the environment by using eco-friendly travel methods and leaving no trace behind.

Best Time to Visit Millau

The best time to visit Millau is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. Summer can be quite hot, and the city can get packed with tourists. Winter can be cold and rainy, but it’s still a beautiful time to see the city’s natural surroundings covered in snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Millau

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Millau:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine - Millau is known for its delicious food and drink, so don’t be shy about exploring the local culinary scene.
  • Take advantage of the natural surroundings - Whether it’s hiking, biking, or paragliding, there are plenty of ways to experience the stunning landscapes of Millau.
  • Respect the environment - Be mindful of your impact on the local environment, and follow Leave No Trace principles to preserve the area for future visitors.

Don’t:

  • Speak too loudly - French culture places a high value on manners and respect, so speaking too loudly or being overly boisterous can be seen as impolite.
  • Forget your sunscreen - The sun can be strong in Millau, even in the cooler months, so be sure to protect your skin.
  • Assume everyone speaks English - While many people in Millau speak basic English, it’s always helpful to learn a few key French phrases before your trip.

Safety and Health

Millau is generally a safe city for visitors, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions while traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Millau

  • Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas like markets or tourist attractions.
  • When driving or biking on winding mountain roads, be cautious of oncoming traffic and take your time.
  • Use caution when exploring natural areas, especially if you’re not familiar with the terrain.

Health in Millau

  • Check with your doctor before traveling to make sure you have up-to-date vaccinations and medications.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot summer months.
  • Be cautious when eating or drinking unfamiliar foods and beverages, and make sure to wash your hands regularly.

Getting to Millau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Millau is accessible by plane, train, and bus. It’s also easy to get around once you’re in the city. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Millau

  • Rodez-Aveyron Airport (RDZ) - Located approximately 60 kilometers from Millau, this airport serves regional airlines such as Air France and Ryanair.

Bus Stations Near Millau

  • Millau Bus Station - Located in the city center, this bus station serves various cities throughout France, including Paris, Montpellier, and Toulouse.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Renting a car is a great way to explore Millau and its natural surroundings at your own pace.
  • Train - The nearest train station to Millau is in the nearby city of Sévérac-le-Château.

How to Get Around in Millau

Many of Millau’s attractions are within walking distance from the city center. Additionally, there are several transportation options for getting around the city, including:

  • Taxis
  • Buses
  • Biking

Where to Go Next from Millau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region around Millau, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Albi - This charming medieval city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and is famous for its red brick cathedral and Toulouse-Lautrec museum.
  • Saint-Affrique - Located in the Sorgues Valley, this small town offers scenic views and delicious local cuisine.
  • Saint-Georges-de-Luzençon - This charming village is located just a short drive from Millau, and offers stunning views of the Tarn Gorges.

Conclusion

Millau is a hidden gem in the south of France, offering visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and cultural history of Aveyron. From the iconic Millau Viaduct to the region’s unique cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and experience the best that Millau has to offer.
