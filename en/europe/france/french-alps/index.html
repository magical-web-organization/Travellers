Introduction to French Alps

Located in the southeastern part of France, the French Alps are a mountain range that includes some of the highest peak in Europe such as Mont Blanc and the highest ski resorts in the country. The French Alps attract visitors all-year-round, as they offer a wide range of activities, from skiing in the winter to hiking and mountain biking in the summer, as well as stunning views and delicious local cuisine. In this comprehensive guide, we will help you plan your perfect trip to the French Alps with details on the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in French Alps

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de la Photographie - located in Chamonix, this museum showcases the history of photography in the Alps.
  • Ecrins National Park - a stunning park that is home to around 700 glaciers, 10,000 species of fauna and a multitude of crystal-clear lakes.
  • Fort Barraux - this impressive fortress is more than 350 years old and is situated at the foot of the Chartreuse mountain range.

Natural Attractions of French Alps: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aiguille du Midi - take a cable car to the top of Aiguille du Midi for a breathtaking view of the French Alps.
  • Annecy Lake - located in the Hautes-Savoie region, Annecy Lake is a serene place for nature lovers.
  • Mer de Glace - this glacier is one of the largest in Europe and can be reached by train from Chamonix.

Hiking/Biking Routes of French Alps

  • GR20 - this is the toughest trek in Europe, located in the Corsican mountains in the French Alps.
  • Mont Blanc Circuit - a 10-day trek ranging from France to Italy and Switzerland.
  • Route des Grandes Alpes - it is not a trek or a hike, but a scenic route that takes you through the heart of the French Alps.

Best Things to Do in French Alps: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding - French Alps offer some of the best ski resorts in Europe, such as Courchevel, Val d’Isère and Chamonix.
  • Mountain Biking - there are hundreds of mountain biking routes in the Alps, and some of the best ones are in Les Gets and Morzine.
  • Paragliding - experience the breathtaking beauty of the French Alps from a unique perspective with paragliding.

Week-Long Itinerary for French Alps

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit Annecy Lake and the charming city of Annecy
Day 2 Go skiing or snowboarding in Val Thorens or Les Menuires
Day 3 Take a day trip to Chamonix and Aiguille du Midi
Day 4 Hike the Mont Blanc Circuit
Day 5 Visit Ecrins National Park for a day of outdoor activities
Day 6 Explore the historic city of Grenoble and visit the Museum of Revolution
Day 7 Relax in one of the many natural hot springs in the area

Food and Drink Scene of French Alps

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tartiflette - this traditional dish from Savoie region is made with potatoes, onions, bacon, and Reblochon cheese.
  • Raclette - a melted cheese dish served with boiled potatoes, vegetables and cured meats.
  • Crozet Au Gratin - a gratin dish made with Crozet pasta, bacon, onions, and Beaufort cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Chartreuse - a green or yellow herbal liqueur produced by Carthusian monks since the 18th century.
  • Savoy Wine - try the white and red wines from this region that are made with unique grape varieties.

Where to Stay in French Alps: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Les Suites - Maison Bouvier - located in Chamonix, this hotel offers chic modern suites with stunning views of Mont Blanc.
  • Le Sherpa - a family-run hotel located in Val Thorens, offering cozy rooms and a warm atmosphere.
  • La Maison Jaune - a hostel with a rustic style located near the charming town of Morzine.

What to Buy in French Alps: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Savoie Cheese - bring back home some of Reblochon, Tomme and Beaufort cheeses.
  • French Alpine Honey - made from nectar gathered from alpine flowers, this delicious honey is perfect for breakfast and desserts.
  • Traditional Knives - find the famous Opinel and Laguiole knives, which are made by French artisans since the 1800s.

Local Culture and Customs of French Alps

The people of the French Alps have a rich history and culture that is displayed in their cuisine, architecture, and traditions. They are known to be avid mountaineers and skiers, with a love for nature and the outdoors. The culture of the French Alps is steeped in art and history, with museums and galleries showcasing the heritage of the region.

Best Time to Visit French Alps

The French Alps are a year-round destination, but the best time to visit will depend on your interests. For skiing and snowboarding, the winter months from December to February are the best time to go. For outdoor activities such as hiking and mountain biking, the summer months from June to September are ideal. Spring and autumn months are great times to enjoy the beauty of the French Alps without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of French Alps

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities
  • Learn a few key phrases in French
  • Try the local cuisine and wine

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Ignore safety warnings on outdoor activities
  • Be too loud or disrespectful in public places
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in French Alps

The French Alps are generally safe, but it is important to take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities. Always listen to weather forecasts and follow the instructions of professional guides. Bear in mind that some mountain passes might be closed during winter.

Health in French Alps

Medical facilities are available throughout the region, but it is advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses. There are no specific health risks associated with the French Alps, but it is important to protect yourself from the sun and stay hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to French Alps: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near French Alps

The major airports are Geneva Airport, Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport, and Chambery-Savoy Airport.

Bus Stations Near French Alps

Bus services are well covered by Flixbus and Ouibus that stop in most of the towns and villages in the French Alps.

Other Options

Trains run between Paris and Chambéry, with connections to other locations in the French Alps. Driving is also an option with rental cars available at the major airports.

How to Get Around in French Alps

There are several ways to get around the French Alps:

  • Biking - rent a bike and explore the scenic paths and routes of the region.
  • Public transportation - buses and trains run frequently and provide easy access to many of the main attractions.
  • Car rentals - rental cars are available at the major airports, and driving allows for more flexibility and freedom.
  • Walking - walking is a great way to get to know local towns, avoid traffic and enjoy the beautiful landscapes of the French Alps.

Where to Go Next from French Alps: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Avignon - visit the historical city of Avignon, located in the Provence region of France.
  • Lyon - the gastronomic capital of France, Lyon is a must-visit destination known for its food, culture, history and museums.
  • Nice - explore the beautiful seaside city of Nice with its stunning beaches and quaint Old Town.

Conclusion

The French Alps offer a unique combination of natural beauty, rich culture, and endless outdoor activities. Whether you are an adventurer looking for a thrill or a foodie seeking to indulge in local cuisine, the French Alps have something for everyone. Plan your perfect trip with this comprehensive travel guide, and experience all that this beautiful region has to offer.
