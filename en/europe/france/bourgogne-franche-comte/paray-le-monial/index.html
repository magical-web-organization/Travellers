Introduction to Paray-le-Monial

Located in the heart of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté, France, Paray-le-Monial is a small, charming town that has been attracting visitors from all over the world for centuries. Known for its religious significance, historical architecture, and picturesque countryside, Paray-le-Monial offers a unique experience that will satisfy the needs of every type of traveler.

In this travel guide, you will discover the best things to do and see in Paray-le-Monial, the best places to eat and stay, and advice on how to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Paray-le-Monial

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Basilique du Sacré-Cœur

The Basilique du Sacré-Cœur, also known as the Sacré-Cœur Basilica, is one of the most iconic landmarks in Paray-le-Monial. Built in the early 19th century, this stunning neo-Gothic church is known for its stained glass windows and ornate decor.

Moulin Liron

Moulin Liron is a 17th century windmill that is now a museum. Visitors can explore the machinery and learn about the history of the flour milling industry.

Hôtel de Ville

The Hôtel de Ville, or Town Hall, is a beautiful 18th century building that houses the town’s administrative offices. Its facade is typical of the French classical style and is a must-see for architecture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Paray-le-Monial: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Paray-le-Monial Canal

The Paray-le-Monial Canal is a scenic waterway that runs through the town. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk along the banks or rent a boat to explore the area.

Le Moulin Rouge

Le Moulin Rouge is a charming park located on the outskirts of Paray-le-Monial. It features a small lake, wooded areas, and a playground, making it the perfect spot for a picnic or a family outing.

Colline de Dun

Colline de Dun is a hill that offers stunning panoramic views of Paray-le-Monial and the surrounding countryside. It’s the perfect spot to watch the sunset or take a romantic stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paray-le-Monial

Paray-le-Monial is surrounded by beautiful countryside, making it a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

The Greenway

The Greenway is a 750 km trail that runs from Paris to Marseille, passing through the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region. The section that runs through Paray-le-Monial is relatively flat and offers stunning views of the countryside.

Canal du Centre

The Canal du Centre is a 90 km long waterway that connects the Saône and Loire rivers. Visitors can bike or walk along the towpath, which is lined with trees and offers views of the canal and surrounding countryside.

Voie Verte

Voie Verte is a 75 km trail that runs from Chalon-sur-Saône to Mâcon. It passes through Paray-le-Monial and offers a nice mix of rural landscapes and historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Paray-le-Monial: Activities and Tours

Parc des Combes

Parc des Combes is a theme park that offers a range of activities for all ages, including roller coasters, water rides, and a zoo. It’s a great place to spend a day with the family.

Wine Tasting

Bourgogne-Franche-Comté is famous for its wine, and there are plenty of wineries in the region that offer tastings. Some of the best wines to try include Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and Aligoté.

Hot Air Balloon Ride

For those looking for a unique way to see Paray-le-Monial and its surroundings, a hot air balloon ride is a must. The views are breathtaking and the experience is unforgettable.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paray-le-Monial

Day 1

Arrive in Paray-le-Monial and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town, starting with a visit to the Basilique du Sacré-Cœur.

Day 2

Take a bike ride along the Canal du Centre. Stop for lunch in one of the small towns along the way.

Day 3

Spend the day at Parc des Combes.

Day 4

Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning. Spend the afternoon at Moulin Liron.

Day 5

Go wine tasting in the nearby vineyards.

Day 6

Hike to the top of Colline de Dun for panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Day 7

Spend the day relaxing at Le Moulin Rouge park.

Food and Drink Scene of Paray-le-Monial

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Paray-le-Monial include:

Charolais Beef

Charolais Beef is a type of beef that is produced locally in the Charolais region. It’s known for its tenderness and flavour.

Bresse Chicken

Bresse Chicken is a type of chicken that is raised in the Bresse region. It’s considered one of the best types of poultry in the world and is a must-try for foodies.

Escargots de Bourgogne

Escargots de Bourgogne, or Burgundy snails, are a delicacy in the region. They are typically served with garlic butter and herbs.

Local Beverages

Bourgogne Wine

Bourgogne Wine is some of the best wine in the world. Some of the top wineries in the region include Domaine des Comtes Lafon, Domaine Romanee Conti, and Domaine Leroy.

Marc de Bourgogne

Marc de Bourgogne is a type of brandy that is made from the grape skins, stems, and seeds left over from the winemaking process.

Where to Stay in Paray-le-Monial: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel de la Basilique

The Hotel de la Basilique is located in the heart of Paray-le-Monial and offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

Le Charollais

Le Charollais is a charming bed and breakfast located just outside of Paray-le-Monial. The rooms are spacious and the breakfast is delicious.

Hotel Terminus

Hotel Terminus is a budget-friendly option that offers simple, clean rooms and a central location.

What to Buy in Paray-le-Monial: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Cheese

Bourgogne-Franche-Comté is famous for its cheese, and Paray-le-Monial offers a range of options to choose from. Some of the most popular cheeses include Comté, Morbier, and Bleu d’Auvergne.

Mustard

Dijon, a town located in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region, is known for its mustard. Visitors can find a range of mustards in Paray-le-Monial that make perfect gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Paray-le-Monial

Religion plays an important role in the culture of Paray-le-Monial. The town is home to several religious sites, including the Basilica of the Sacred Heart and the Chapel of the Apparitions. Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting these sites and show respect for the religious customs of the region.

Best Time to Visit Paray-le-Monial

The best time to visit Paray-le-Monial is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, the town is charming year-round and there is always something to see and do.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paray-le-Monial

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local wine and cheese.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or behave disrespectfully in religious sites.
  • Leave without trying the local cuisine.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment while hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paray-le-Monial

Paray-le-Monial is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should always take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding dark, isolated areas at night.

Health in Paray-le-Monial

Paray-le-Monial has good healthcare facilities, including a hospital and several pharmacies. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Paray-le-Monial: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paray-le-Monial

The nearest airport to Paray-le-Monial is Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport (LYS), which is approximately a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Paray-le-Monial

The main bus station in Paray-le-Monial is located in the town center, next to the train station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Paray-le-Monial from nearby cities such as Lyon or Dijon.

How to Get Around in Paray-le-Monial

Paray-le-Monial is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Paray-le-Monial: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mâcon

Mâcon is a beautiful town located on the banks of the Saône River, approximately an hour’s drive from Paray-le-Monial. It’s known for its wine and stunning architecture.

Dijon

Dijon is the capital of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region and is known for its mustard and well-preserved medieval architecture.

Conclusion

Paray-le-Monial is a charming town that offers a unique experience for visitors. From its religious sites and historical architecture to its scenic countryside and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in Paray-le-Monial. With this travel guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your trip and have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful part of France.
