Introduction to Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne

Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne is a charming village in the Jura Mountains of eastern France, located in the region of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté. This small town is known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maison de la Forêt: This old hunting lodge, located in the heart of the forest, has been transformed into a nature center that offers various exhibitions and activities related to the forest.
  • The Church of Sainte-Anne: This 18th-century church is a beautiful example of the local architecture. It is dedicated to Sainte-Anne, the patron saint of the village.
  • Chateau de Joux: Located a short drive from Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne, this castle is a must-visit for history buffs. It has a rich history dating back to the 11th century and has been used as a fortress, prison, and museum.

Natural Attractions of Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cascade du Hérisson: This stunning waterfall is located in the nearby Hérisson Valley. It is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Forêt de Chaux: This beautiful forest stretches over 20,000 hectares and is perfect for hiking and biking. There are also many hidden lakes and ponds to discover.
  • Lac de Chalain: This lake is a great spot for swimming, boating, and fishing. It is surrounded by beautiful forests and mountains and is perfect for a relaxing day trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne

Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne is known for its beautiful hiking and biking trails. Some popular routes include:

  • Le Sentier des 7 Cascades: This 15-km trail takes you through the Hérisson Valley, passing by seven stunning waterfalls along the way.
  • Le Tour du Lac de Chalain: This easy, 10-km trail takes you around the beautiful Lac de Chalain.
  • La Grande Traversée du Jura: This famous hiking trail spans over 400 km and takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the Jura Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne: Activities and Tours

  • Caving: The Jura Mountains are home to many geological wonders, including caves and grottos. There are many local companies that offer guided caving tours.
  • Wine and Cheese Tasting: The Jura region is famous for its delicious wines and cheeses. Some local vineyards and cheese producers offer tours and tastings.
  • Snow Sports: During the winter months, the Jura Mountains become a popular ski and snowboard destination. There are many ski resorts in the area, including Métabief and Les Rousses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne

Day 1: Visit the Maison de la Forêt and go for a hike in the Forêt de Chaux Day 2: Explore the village of Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne and visit the Church of Sainte-Anne Day 3: Take a day trip to the Chateau de Joux Day 4: Go caving in the local caves or visit some nearby vineyards and cheese producers Day 5: Spend the day swimming and relaxing at Lac de Chalain Day 6: Go skiing or snowboarding at a nearby ski resort Day 7: Do a final hike on the Sentier des 7 Cascades or the Grande Traversée du Jura

Food and Drink Scene of Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Comté Cheese: This is a hard, unpasteurized cheese that is aged for at least four months. It has a nutty and slightly sweet flavor and is perfect for snacking or cooking.
  • Poulet de Bresse: This is a type of chicken that is raised in the nearby Bresse region. It is known for its delicate flavor and tender meat.
  • Jura Wine: The Jura region produces many delicious wines, including Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and Savagnin. These wines are often aged in oak barrels and have a unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Absinthe: This strong, anise-flavored spirit was once banned in many countries due to its alleged hallucinogenic properties. However, it is now legal and is a popular local drink in the Jura region.
  • Vin Jaune: This is a unique wine that is aged for at least six years in oak barrels. It has a nutty and slightly oxidized flavor and is often drunk as an aperitif.
  • Jura Beer: There are several local breweries in the Jura Mountains that produce a variety of delicious beers. Some popular brands include Brasserie des Cimes and La Débauche.

Where to Stay in Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel des Lacs: This hotel is located on the shores of Lac de Chalain and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant with lake views.
  • Gîte de La Source Bleue: This charming guesthouse is located in a quiet area of Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne and has a beautiful garden.
  • Le Penaroyal: This hostel is located in the nearby town of Arbois and is perfect for budget travelers. It offers dorm rooms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge.

What to Buy in Nans-sous-Sainte-Anne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Comté Cheese: This delicious cheese is the perfect souvenir to bring home. It can be found at most local markets and specialty food shops.
  • Absinthe: If you’re a fan of strong alcohol, you might want to bring back a bottle of local absinthe.
  • Wood Products: The Jura Mountains are known for their woodworking traditions. You can find many beautiful and unique wood products, such as cutting boards and bowls, in local shops.
