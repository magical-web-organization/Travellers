Introduction to Nevers

Nevers is a charming town in Bourgogne-Franche-Comté, France. With a population of just over 35,000, it’s a picturesque destination for travelers seeking a peaceful getaway. The town is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know when planning a trip to Nevers.

List of Places to Visit in Nevers

Nevers has something to offer for everyone. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, here are some places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palais Ducal: Former home of the Dukes of Nevers, this castle is now a museum showcasing medieval architecture and history.
  • Maison de la Culture: This cultural center hosts exhibitions, shows and concerts year-round, and houses one of the largest libraries in the region.
  • Cathédrale Saint-Cyr-et-Sainte-Julitte: One of the oldest and most beautiful cathedrals in Burgundy-Franche-Comté, it was built in the Gothic style starting in the 11th century.

Natural Attractions of Nevers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loire River: The longest river in France has many trails, parks, and beautiful points of view along its banks for long country walks or cycling.
  • Parc Roger-Salengro: This large park in the center of town is perfect for a leisurely walk or picnic. The park also offers a lake, playgrounds, and offers a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Forêt Domaniale des Bertranges: This vast forest is only a short drive from Nevers, and is perfect for a day of hiking or bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nevers

With its rolling hills and picturesque landscapes, the region surrounding Nevers makes for the perfect hiking and biking destination. Here are some of our favorite routes to explore:

  • Bike tour along the Loire river: This 300km route starts in Nevers and follows the Loire river through the beautiful French countryside.
  • Hiking through the Sancerre vineyards: This 7km hike takes you through the vineyards of Sancerre and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Bike route through the Valley of the Yonne River: This 180km route takes you through the Valley of the Yonne River, passing through small towns and picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in Nevers: Activities and Tours

Looking for something fun to do while in Nevers? Here are some activities and tours to consider:

  • Wine Tasting in Sancerre: Sample the delicious wines of Sancerre during a guided tour of the local vineyards.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the beauty of Nevers from the air with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Boat Tour on the Loire River: Take a relaxing boat tour on the Loire river and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nevers

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Nevers, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Nevers, check into your hotel and explore the town center.
  • Day 2: Visit Palais Ducal in the morning and go for a bike ride along the Loire river in the afternoon.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to the Sancerre vineyards and enjoy a wine tasting and tour.
  • Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of the region with a hike through the Forêt Domaniale des Bertranges.
  • Day 5: Take a boat tour along the Loire river and visit some of the charming towns along the way.
  • Day 6: Visit Cathédrale Saint-Cyr-et-Sainte-Julitte in the morning and spend the afternoon in Parc Roger-Salengro.
  • Day 7: Depart Nevers and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Nevers

Nevers has a rich food and drink scene, offering traditional French cuisine and local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poulet à la diable: A spicy roasted chicken dish that is a Nevers specialty.
  • Boeuf Bourguignon: A classic French dish made with beef, red wine, and vegetables.
  • Millefeuille: Puff pastry dessert layered with cream and topped with caramelized sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Sancerre Wine: This crisp, refreshing wine is made in the Sancerre region and goes well with local dishes.
  • Cider: Made with apples from the surrounding orchards, this cider is a popular choice for locals and visitors alike.
  • Moutarde de Nevers: A traditional Dijon mustard recipe with a twist. This mustard has a unique blend of local spices that make it stand out.

Where to Stay in Nevers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nevers offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and needs. Here are some great options:

  • Hôtel de Verdun: Located in the heart of town, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and great service.
  • Le Grand Monarque: This historic hotel is located in an 18th-century building and offers elegant rooms and a beautiful courtyard.
  • La Maison du Four: This quaint guesthouse is located just outside of town and offers peaceful surroundings and affordable prices.

What to Buy in Nevers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to bring home? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Moutarde de Nevers: This delicious mustard makes for a great souvenir and is perfect for cooking with.
  • Pottery: Nevers is known for its beautiful ceramic pottery, which makes for a great gift or keepsake.
  • Wine: Bring home a bottle (or two) of Sancerre wine to enjoy with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Nevers

French culture and customs are an important part of life in Nevers. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Language: French is the official language of Nevers, but many locals also speak English.
  • Greetings: It’s customary to greet people with a handshake or “bonjour” (good day) when arriving or leaving a store, restaurant, etc.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in France, but it’s customary to leave a small amount (10%) for exceptional service.

Best Time to Visit Nevers

The best time to visit Nevers is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons (April or October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Nevers

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Nevers:

Do:

  • Do sample the local food and drink: Nevers offers some delicious traditional French cuisine and local delicacies.
  • Do try to speak some French: While many locals speak English, attempting to converse in French is always appreciated.
  • Do respect the local customs: French culture and customs are an important part of life in Nevers, so be respectful and open-minded.

Don’t:

  • Don’t expect everything to be open on Sundays: Many shops and restaurants are closed on Sundays, so plan accordingly.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately: French people generally dress well, so avoid showing up to a restaurant or museum in flip flops and shorts.
  • Don’t be loud or obnoxious: French people value politeness and respect, so be mindful of your volume and behavior in public places.

Safety and Health

Nevers is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and good healthcare facilities. However, it’s always important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Nevers

  • Be aware of your surroundings: As with any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Avoid walking alone at night: While Nevers is generally safe, it’s always a good idea to avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation: Pickpocketing can be a problem on public transportation, so keep an eye on your belongings at all times.

Health in Nevers

  • Stay hydrated: Nevers can get quite warm during the summer months, so be sure to stay hydrated and avoid dehydration.
  • Use sunscreen: Protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays by using sunscreen and wearing a hat during outdoor activities.
  • Bring any necessary medications: If you have any preexisting medical conditions, be sure to pack any necessary medications or supplies.

Getting to Nevers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nevers is easily accessible by both air and land. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Nevers

The nearest airports to Nevers are:

  • Paris-Orly Airport: Located approximately 2 hours by car from Nevers.
  • Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport: Located approximately 2 hours by car from Nevers.

Bus Stations Near Nevers

There are several bus stations near Nevers, including:

  • Nevers Gare Routière: This is the main bus station in Nevers, with connections to many other destinations in France and beyond.
  • Gare de Moulins-sur-Allier: This station is located approximately 45 minutes by car from Nevers.

Other Options

  • Train: Nevers has a train station with connections to Paris and other major cities in France.
  • Car: Nevers is easily accessible by car, with major highways running through the town.

How to Get Around Nevers

Nevers is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you’re planning to explore the surrounding region, here are some transportation options:

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is the easiest way to explore the surrounding countryside and nearby towns.
  • Public Transportation: Nevers has a local bus system that connects to the surrounding region, but schedules can be limited.
  • Bike Rental: You can explore Nevers and the surrounding region by bike, with many rental shops in town.

Where to Go Next from Nevers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nevers is a great base for exploring the surrounding region and nearby destinations. Here are some destinations to consider:

  • Moulins: This charming town is approximately 45 minutes by car from Nevers and offers museums, historic sites, and beautiful architecture.
  • Bourges: This beautiful town is approximately 1 hour by car from Nevers and offers an impressive cathedral, museums, and a historic town center.
  • Sancerre: This picturesque town in the Loire Valley offers vineyards, wine tasting, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Conclusion

Nevers is a hidden gem in the heart of France, offering a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s museums and historic sites or hiking through the surrounding countryside, there’s something for everyone in Nevers.
