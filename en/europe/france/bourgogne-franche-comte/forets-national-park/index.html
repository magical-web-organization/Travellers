Introduction to Forêts National Park

Nestled in the heart of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté, Forêts National Park is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, this national park reserve offers an unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages. The park spans over 16,000 hectares and is home to an abundance of flora and fauna species. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or just relax in nature, Forêts National Park has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Forêts National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château-Chinon Museum - Explore the region’s rich cultural heritage at this museum, which features exhibits on the town’s history, regional costume, and Gallo-Roman artifacts.
  • Bibracte Archaeological Site - Take a trip back in time at this ancient Celtic town, which was once the capital of the Aedui tribe and played a significant role in Gaul’s history.
  • Cathedrale Saint-Lazare - Visit this iconic cathedral, which dates back to the 12th century and features stunning architectural details.
  • Ternant Castle - Discover the captivating history of this 15th-century castle, which is now open to the public for tours.

Natural Attractions of Forêts National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Pannecière - Spend a relaxing day by the water at this picturesque lake, which offers opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Mount Beuvray - Hike to the top of this scenic mountain and take in panoramic views of the park and surrounding countryside.
  • The Waterfall of Gouloux - Marvel at the beauty of this stunning natural wonder, which cascades down a rocky cliff in the heart of the park.
  • The Morvan Hills - Embark on a nature walk through the lush green hills and valleys of Forêts National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forêts National Park

Forêts National Park has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the opportunity to explore the park’s diverse landscapes and natural wonders up close. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Tour du Morvan - This 200-kilometer trail is a popular route for both hikers and bikers, offering panoramic views of the park’s mountains, forests, and lakes.
  • Le Chemin des Moines - This 12-kilometer trail takes you through the heart of the park, offering stunning views of the countryside and historical landmarks along the way.
  • Mont Beuvray Circuit - This challenging 16-kilometer trail takes you to the summit of Mount Beuvray, the highest peak in the region, and offers unforgettable panoramic views of the park.

Best Things to Do in Forêts National Park: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Forêts National Park has something for everyone. Some must-do activities include:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing - Take to the water and explore the beautiful lakes and rivers of the park on a kayaking or canoeing excursion.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Soar above the park and take in breathtaking panoramic views on a scenic hot air balloon ride.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - Taste some of the region’s best wines on a guided tour of local vineyards and wineries.
  • Guided Nature Walks - Learn about the park’s flora and fauna and natural habitats on a guided nature walk led by a local expert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Forêts National Park

Forêts National Park offers enough activities and attractions to fill a week-long itinerary with adventure and relaxation. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Forêts National Park and explore the grounds of your accommodation.
  • Day 2: Take a guided nature walk to learn about the park’s flora and fauna and natural habitats.
  • Day 3: Spend the day kayaking or canoeing on the park’s picturesque lakes and rivers.
  • Day 4: Embark on a wine tasting tour and learn about the region’s rich wine-making traditions.
  • Day 5: Hike to the summit of Mount Beuvray and take in panoramic views of the park and surrounding countryside.
  • Day 6: Relax by the water at Lake Pannecière and enjoy a picnic lunch.
  • Day 7: Depart Forêts National Park and indulge in a morning of hot air balloon riding and take in unforgettable panoramic views of the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Forêts National Park

The region surrounding Forêts National Park is famous for its gastronomy, with an abundance of local culinary specialties and delicious wines. Some must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boeuf Bourguignon - This classic beef stew is a signature dish of the Burgundy region, made with tender beef, red wine, carrots, and French herbs.
  • Poulet à la Bressanne - This chicken dish is made with farm-raised Bresse chicken, which is known worldwide for its tender, succulent meat.
  • Epoisses de Bourgogne - This soft, creamy cheese is made from cow’s milk and is famous for its rich, spicy flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Burgundy Wine - The region is famous for its world-class Pinot Noir and Chardonnay wines, which are perfect for sipping during an indulgent wine tasting tour.
  • Marc de Bourgogne - This regional brandy is made from the pomace of Burgundy grapes and is a perfect after-dinner digestif.
  • Crème de Cassis - This sweet, blackcurrant liqueur is a popular ingredient in cocktails and is a staple beverage of the region.

Where to Stay in Forêts National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Forêts National Park offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hostellerie de Levernois - This luxurious hotel is located just a short drive from the park and offers spacious rooms, a Michelin-starred restaurant, and an on-site wine cellar.
  • Le Clos de la Bussière - This charming guesthouse is nestled in the heart of the park and offers cozy rooms, a peaceful garden, and a delicious breakfast buffet.
  • Auberge de la Chaloire - This rustic hostel is perfect for budget travelers and offers clean, comfortable accommodations, a communal kitchen, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Forêts National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Forêts National Park is home to an array of locally made souvenirs and products that make perfect gifts or keepsakes. Some top picks include:

  • Handcrafted Pottery - The region is famous for its colorful, handcrafted pottery, which is sold at local markets and shops.
  • Local Honey - Made by local beekeepers, the honey of the region is famous for its rich flavor and is perfect for drizzling on toast or adding to sweet and savory dishes.
  • Regional Wines - Bring a bottle or two of Burgundy wine back with you as a souvenir of your visit to Forêts National Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Forêts National Park

The region surrounding Forêts National Park is steeped in tradition and history, with a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. Some cultural highlights include:

  • Regional Festivals and Fairs - Throughout the year, the region hosts a range of festivals and fairs showcasing local traditions and customs, including the famous wine festival held in nearby Beaune.
  • Gallo-Roman Artifacts - The area is home to a wealth of ancient Roman ruins, including the Bibracte Archaeological Site, which provides a fascinating glimpse into the region’s ancient history.
  • Local Crafts - The region is known for its handcrafted pottery, enamels, and tapestries, all of which make beautiful souvenirs or gifts.

Best Time to Visit Forêts National Park

Forêts National Park is a year-round destination, with activities and events taking place throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is during the summer (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny and the park’s outdoor attractions are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forêts National Park

Do:

  • Pack comfortable walking shoes if you plan to hike or bike in the park.
  • Respect the park’s natural habitats and wildlife.
  • Take part in local festivals and cultural events.
  • Try the region’s gastronomy and local wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the park’s natural habitats.
  • Ignore safety guidelines on outdoor activities.
  • miss out on the park’s cultural highlights.
  • Overindulge in local wines and spirits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forêts National Park

For the most part, Forêts National Park is a safe destination. However, as with any outdoor adventure, there are some safety precautions you should keep in mind, such as:

  • Staying on marked trails when hiking or biking.
  • Bringing a first-aid kit and plenty of water on outdoor excursions.
  • Following the park’s fire regulations and guidelines to prevent forest fires.

Health in Forêts National Park

While there are no specific health risks associated with visiting Forêts National Park, it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling to a new destination, such as:

  • Ensuring you have the necessary vaccinations and travel insurance before your trip.
  • Staying hydrated and protecting yourself from the sun during outdoor activities.
  • Bringing any necessary medication or personal health equipment.

Getting to Forêts National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Forêts National Park

  • Dijon-Bourgogne Airport - Located approximately 1.5 hours from the park by car
  • Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport - Located approximately 2 hours from the park by car

Bus Stations Near Forêts National Park

  • Autun Bus Station - Located approximately 30 minutes from the park by car
  • Chateau-Chinon Bus Station - Located approximately 45 minutes from the park by car

Other Options

  • Train - The nearest train station is in Autun, which offers connections to major regional cities.
  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a great option for exploring the park and surrounding region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Forêts National Park

The best way to get around Forêts National Park is by car or bike, as public transportation options are limited. Renting a car or bike is easy and affordable, with many rental companies located throughout the region. Additionally, many of the park’s attractions and activities are within walking distance of each other, making it easy to explore on foot.

Where to Go Next from Forêts National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Forêts National Park is located in the heart of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté, a region steeped in history and culture. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Beaune - A picturesque town known for its medieval architecture and famous wine festival.
  • Dijon - The region’s capital, Dijon offers stunning architecture, great shopping, and a wealth of cultural attractions.
  • Châteauneuf-en-Auxois - A charming hilltop village known for its beautiful castle and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Conclusion

Forêts National Park is an idyllic destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and foodies alike. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and warm hospitality, this national park offers an unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re looking to hike the mountains, relax by the lake, or indulge in some local wine and cheese, Forêts National Park has something for everyone.
