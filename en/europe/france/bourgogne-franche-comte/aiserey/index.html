Introduction to Aiserey

Aiserey is a charming little village located in the heart of Bourgogne-Franche-Comté, France. From its medieval architecture to the friendly locals, Aiserey offers a unique and authentic French experience. Whether you want to explore the historical landmarks, enjoy the natural beauty, or indulge in the local cuisine, this guide will help you plan the perfect trip to Aiserey.

List of Places to Visit in Aiserey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chateau d’Aiserey: A 17th-century castle built for the Seigneurs of Aiserey. The castle is now privately owned and not open to the public, but it’s a beautiful sight from the outside.
  • Église Saint-Apollinaire: A Romanesque church built in the 12th century. Don’t miss the beautiful stained glass windows and impressive bell tower.

Natural Attractions of Aiserey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Roches: A beautiful natural area with stunning views of the surrounding vineyards and forests. Perfect for hiking and picnics.
  • Lac Kir: A small lake located just outside of Dijon, perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aiserey

  • Circuit des sources et des lavoirs: A 12km hiking trail that takes you to various sources and wash houses in the area.
  • La Voie Verte: A 30km bike path that runs from Dijon to Fixin. This scenic path takes you through the beautiful countryside and vineyards of Burgundy.

Best Things to Do in Aiserey: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Explore the famous vineyards of Burgundy and sample some of the region’s finest wines.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Get a unique view of the area from a hot air balloon, and enjoy the peaceful silence as you float over the vineyards and forests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aiserey

Food and Drink Scene of Aiserey

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coq au Vin: A traditional French dish made with chicken, red wine, and mushrooms. The dish is slow-cooked, allowing the flavors to blend together beautifully.
  • Boeuf Bourguignon: Another classic French dish made with beef, red wine, and vegetables. The dish is hearty, flavorful and perfect on a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Burgundy Wines: Burgundy is famous for its wine, particularly its Pinot Noir and Chardonnay.
  • Kir: A traditional French drink made with crème de cassis and white wine.

Where to Stay in Aiserey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Le Castel: A beautiful guesthouse located in an old castle. The rooms are spacious, and the grounds are stunning.
  • Les Roches: A charming guesthouse located near the natural area of Les Roches. The rooms are cozy and comfortable, and the breakfast is delicious.

What to Buy in Aiserey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Burgundy Wine: Bring home a bottle of the region’s finest wines.
  • Dijon Mustard: A classic French condiment made with mustard seeds and white wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Aiserey

The locals in Aiserey are friendly and welcoming, but it is still important to follow some basic French customs. When greeting someone, always shake hands and maintain eye contact.

Best Time to Visit Aiserey

The best time to visit Aiserey is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be very busy, and the weather can be quite hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aiserey

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public spaces.
  • Disrespect the locals or their property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aiserey

Aiserey is a very safe place for travelers. However, you should still take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Aiserey

The healthcare system in France is excellent, and there are several hospitals and clinics located in nearby cities.

Getting to Aiserey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aiserey

  • Dijon Bourgogne Airport: The closest airport to Aiserey is located just 15km away.
  • Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport: Another nearby airport located just 180km from Aiserey.

Bus Stations Near Aiserey

  • Dijon Bus Station: Located just 15km away from Aiserey.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Aiserey

The easiest way to get around Aiserey is by car, although there are also several bike paths and hiking trails in the area.

Where to Go Next from Aiserey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beaune: Visit the historic town of Beaune and explore the famous Hospices de Beaune.
  • Dijon: Explore the medieval city center of Dijon and enjoy the local cuisine.

Conclusion

Aiserey is a charming and authentic French village that offers a unique travel experience. Whether you want to explore the rich culture and history, enjoy the natural beauty, or indulge in the local cuisine, Aiserey has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today!
