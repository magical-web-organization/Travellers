Introduction to Autun

Located in the Burgundy region of France, Autun is a small town that is full of history, culture, and natural beauty. This medieval town is known for its Roman artifacts, Gothic architecture, natural parks, and scenic views. It is a charming destination that attracts both history buffs and nature lovers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the best things to do and see in Autun, as well as provide you with useful information to help plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Autun

Autun may be a small town, but there is an abundance of things to see and do. From cultural landmarks to scenic views, here is a comprehensive list of places to visit in Autun.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cathedral Saint-Lazare
  2. Temple of Janus
  3. Musée Rolin
  4. Musée Lapidaire
  5. Roman Theater

Natural Attractions of Autun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Morvan Regional Natural Park
  2. Lac des Settons
  3. Mont Beuvray
  4. Gorges de la Canche
  5. La Roche de Solutré

Best Things to Do in Autun: Activities and Tours

Autun has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Autun:

  1. Taste local wine and cheese on a vineyard tour
  2. Take a hot air balloon ride over Autun
  3. Visit a local farm to learn about cheese-making or beekeeping
  4. Take a guided walking tour of the town or surrounding areas
  5. Attend the Festival of Saint-Lazare in September

Week-Long Itinerary for Autun

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Autun:

Day 1: Visit the Cathedral Saint-Lazare and the Roman Theater Day 2: Hike Mont Beuvray in Morvan Regional Natural Park Day 3: Explore the town on a guided walking tour and visit Musée Rolin and Musée Lapidaire Day 4: Take a vineyard tour and taste local wine and cheese Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride over Autun or visit a local farm Day 6: Visit Temple of Janus and Gorges de la Canche Day 7: Relax at Lac des Settons and enjoy the scenic view

Local Culture and Customs of Autun

The people of Autun are proud of their history, culture, and gastronomy. They have a strong connection to nature and spend their leisure time exploring the region’s hills and forests. One local tradition that must be followed is trying a glass of Cassis with white wine, known as a Kir.

Best Time to Visit Autun

The best time to visit Autun is from April to October, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town can get very crowded during the peak tourist season in July and August, so consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of April to June or September to October for a quieter experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Autun

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine, especially the Burgundy wine and cheese
  2. Visit the Cathedral Saint-Lazare and Musée Rolin
  3. Explore the natural beauty of the Morvan Regional Natural Park

Don’t:

  1. Forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking tours
  2. Leave without trying the local specialty, Escargots de Bourgogne
  3. Plan to visit during the winter months when many attractions are closed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Autun

Autun is a safe place for tourists, but like any destination, it is important to take precautions. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

Health in Autun

There are several medical facilities available in Autun, including a hospital and several medical clinics. It is important to have travel insurance and make sure you have any necessary vaccinations before visiting France.

Getting to Autun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Autun

The nearest airports to Autun are Lyon-Saint Exupéry International Airport and Geneva International Airport. Both airports are about a 2.5-hour drive from Autun and offer connections to major cities in Europe and the rest of the world.

Bus Stations Near Autun

Autun is well-connected by bus to other major cities in France, including Paris, Dijon, and Lyon. The main bus station in Autun is located in the town center.

Other Options

Another popular way to explore the Burgundy region is by car. There are several car rental agencies available at both Lyon and Geneva airports.

How to Get Around in Autun

The best way to get around Autun is on foot. The town is relatively small and easy to navigate. For longer distances, it is recommended to use a rental car or public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Autun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Autun is a great base for exploring the Burgundy region of France. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Dijon: A historic city famous for its mustard and wine
  2. Beaune: A charming town known for its wine cellars and vineyards
  3. Cluny: A historic town known for its abbey and medieval architecture
  4. Châteauneuf-en-Auxois: A picturesque hilltop village with amazing views

Conclusion

Autun is a hidden gem in the Burgundy region of France that offers a mix of history, culture, and nature. Whether you are interested in history, food and wine, outdoor adventures, or scenic views, Autun has something to offer for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to this charming town and make the most of your visit.
