Introduction to Dijon

Located in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region of France, Dijon is a beautiful city steeped in both history and culture. It is known for its mustard, wine, and architecture. Dijon is also a gateway to the famous Burgundy wine region and home to some of the most amazing landmarks in Europe.

List of Places to Visit in Dijon

Dijon has numerous attractions and landmarks, from museums and historical places to natural attractions and scenic views. Here is a list of the top places you should visit while in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Musée des Beaux-Arts de Dijon
  2. Palace of the Dukes of Burgundy
  3. Musée Magnin
  4. Notre Dame de Dijon
  5. Jardin Botanique de l’Arquebuse

Natural Attractions of Dijon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Parc de la Colombière
  2. Lake Kir

  4. Dijon Climbing Gym

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dijon

Local Culture and Customs of Dijon

The locals in Dijon are welcoming and friendly towards visitors. They’re proud of their heritage and their love of good food, wine, and nature. While there are no specific customs to abide by, it’s important to be respectful of the local traditions and culture.

Best Time to Visit Dijon

The best time to visit Dijon is in the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and the landscape is colorful. However, if you plan to attend the annual Dijon International Gastronomy Fair, then February is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dijon

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine
  2. Explore the historic city center
  3. Visit local vineyards and wineries
  4. Dress appropriately when visiting places of worship
  5. Learn a little bit of French before you go

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places
  2. Disrespect local customs and culture
  3. Take photos in places where it’s clearly prohibited
  4. Be too loud in public places
  5. Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Dijon

Dijon is a safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s still important to take precautions, especially at night. Avoid carrying too much cash and be aware of your surroundings. In case of an emergency, dial 112.

Health in Dijon

Dijon has good healthcare facilities in case of emergency. Make sure to have comprehensive travel insurance before you travel. French pharmacies are well-stocked with medication, but always carry your medication with you in case of an emergency.

Getting to Dijon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dijon is accessible by air, train, bus, and car.

Airports Near Dijon

  1. Dole-Jura Airport (DLE)
  2. Lyon Airport (LYS)

Bus Stations Near Dijon

  1. Gare de Dijon ville
  2. Gare de Fontainebleau-Avon

Other Options

Dijon has a great public transportation system with buses and trams that operate throughout the city. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer private transport.

How to Get Around in Dijon

Dijon has an excellent and well-organized public transport network. Buses and trams operate regularly throughout the city, making it easy to get around. For those looking to explore the countryside, car rental is the best option.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a fan of history, culture, wine, or beautiful landscapes, Dijon has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now equipped with all the information you need to plan a fantastic trip to Dijon. Bon voyage!
