Introduction to Luberon

Luberon, located in Vaucluse, Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, France, is known for its stunning natural beauty, quaint hilltop villages, and rich cultural heritage. It is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, hikers, and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Luberon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château de Lourmarin - A beautifully restored 16th-century castle-turned-museum that gives visitors a glimpse into the life of the French aristocracy.
  • Musée des Arts et Métiers du Vin - A museum dedicated to the history and production of wine in Luberon, complete with tastings and educational exhibits.
  • Abbaye Notre-Dame de Sénanque - A Cistercian abbey that dates back to the 12th century and is still active today. It is known for its stunning lavender fields and unparalleled serenity.
  • Fort de Buoux - A well-preserved ancient fortress that once played a strategic role in protecting the region.

Natural Attractions of Luberon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colorado Provençal - A jaw-dropping natural landscape of rust-colored hills and rock formations, best explored on foot or by bike.
  • Gorges de Régalon - A serene hiking trail that follows a river through lush vegetation and scenic cliffs.
  • Mont Ventoux - A towering peak that challenges hikers and cyclists with its steep incline, but also rewards them with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Les Ocres de Roussillon - A surreal landscape of red and yellow rock formations, made all the more striking by the contrast with the surrounding greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luberon

Luberon is a hiker’s paradise, with a range of trails that cater to all levels of experience. Some popular routes include:

  • GR 97 - A rugged, 100km trail that passes through some of the most picturesque villages in Luberon.
  • Le Sentier des Ocres - A shorter, 30-minute hike that takes you through the stunning ochre cliffs of Roussillon.
  • Circuit des Fontaines - A moderate hiking trail that passes through several natural springs and offers breathtaking views of the Luberon hills.

For cyclists, the roads of Luberon offer a challenging yet rewarding route, with plenty of picturesque stops along the way.

Best Things to Do in Luberon: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours - A leisurely way to experience the famous wines of Luberon.
  • Cooking Classes - A hands-on way to learn the secrets of traditional Provençal cuisine.
  • Hot Air Balloon Tours - A breathtaking way to experience the stunning natural landscape of Luberon from above.
  • Markets - Luberon’s markets are full of local produce, crafts, and souvenirs, and are a must-visit for any tourist.

Week-Long Itinerary for Luberon

Day 1: Explore the stunning town of Lourmarin and visit the Château de Lourmarin and Musée des Arts et Métiers du Vin.

Day 2: Hike the Gorges de Régalon and take in the scenic views.

Day 3: Visit the Abbaye Notre-Dame de Sénanque and the Colorado Provençal.

Day 4: Take a cycling or driving tour through the Luberon hills, stopping at picturesque villages along the way.

Day 5: Attend a cooking class and learn the secrets of traditional Provençal cuisine.

Day 6: Visit the Fort de Buoux and Les Ocres de Roussillon.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a hot air balloon tour over the stunning landscape of Luberon.

Food and Drink Scene of Luberon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ratatouille - Luberon’s take on this classic French dish is a colorful, hearty vegetable stew.
  • Daube - A slow-cooked beef stew that is often accompanied by creamy polenta or mashed potatoes.
  • Gigot d’Agneau - A succulent leg of lamb that is usually roasted and served with fragrant Provençal herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Luberon is known for its rich, full-bodied red wines, as well as its crisp, refreshing whites.
  • Pastis - An anise-flavored apéritif that is a beloved staple of Provençal life.

Where to Stay in Luberon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Le Mas de la Tour - A charming guesthouse in the heart of Luberon with personalized service and beautiful views of the surrounding hills.
  • La Coquillade - A luxurious hotel with an on-site spa, restaurant, and vineyard, perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • Hostellerie Le Paradou - A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving classic Provençal cuisine.

What to Buy in Luberon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lavender - Luberon is known for its fragrant, world-famous lavender fields, and lavender-based products, such as soap, oil, and sachets, make great souvenirs.
  • Provence Pottery - This region is known for its vibrant, colorful ceramics, from plates and vases to unique kitchenware like olive oil jugs and garlic graters.
  • Olives and Olive Oil - The olives of Luberon are some of the best in France, and their oils are a must-try, with flavors ranging from mild to spicy.

Local Culture and Customs of Luberon

Luberon is a region with a rich cultural history and proud traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as dress codes when visiting religious sites, and keep noise levels low in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Luberon

The best time to visit Luberon is from May through October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the lavender fields are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luberon

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be mindful of noise levels in residential areas
  • Try the local wines and cuisine

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas
  • Ignore warning signs on hiking trails or cycling routes

Safety and Health

Safety in Luberon

Luberon is a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Luberon

The tap water in Luberon is safe to drink, and there are hospitals and pharmacies in the region in case of emergency.

Getting to Luberon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Luberon

  • Avignon Provence Airport
  • Marseille Provence Airport
  • Nice Côte d’Azur Airport

Bus Stations Near Luberon

  • Aix-en-Provence Bus Station
  • Avignon Bus Station
  • Marseille Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train - The TGV from Paris stops at Avignon TGV station, from which you can take a local train or bus to Luberon.

How to Get Around in Luberon

The best way to get around Luberon is by car, as public transportation is limited, and many picturesque sites are spread out across the region. Cycling or hiking is also a great option for those who prefer a more active approach.

Where to Go Next from Luberon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Avignon - A stunning medieval city with a rich history and plenty of cultural attractions.
  • Aix-en-Provence - A lively university town with beautiful architecture, bustling markets, and a charming old town.
  • Côte d’Azur - A breathtaking coastal region with glamorous resorts, beautiful beaches, and world-famous cuisine.

Conclusion

Luberon is a beautiful, vibrant region with something to offer everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, culture, or cuisine, you’ll find plenty to love in this charming corner of France.
