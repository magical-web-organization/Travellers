Avignon Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Avignon

Located in Vaucluse, in Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, in France, Avignon is a historical city with rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty. It is considered one of the most beautiful cities of France. With its well-preserved medieval architecture, artworks, museums and papal palaces, the city provides a great travel experience for history and culture enthusiasts.

Introduction to Avignon

Avignon is the capital of Vaucluse, in southeastern France. It is known as the City of Popes for its association with the Roman Catholic Church. It is a beautiful city on the banks of the Rhône River, surrounded by vineyards, lavender fields, and olive groves. Avignon is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts numerous visitors every year.

List of Places to Visit in Avignon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palais des Papes: A beautiful palace built in the 14th century, which is the largest Gothic palace in the world. It was the seat of the papacy in the 14th century, and houses numerous artworks, paintings, sculptures, and frescoes.
  • Musée du Petit Palais: It is a museum housed in a beautiful palace that houses a collection of Italian paintings from the 13th to the 16th century, including works of Botticelli and Luca Signorelli.
  • Rocher des Doms: A lookout point from where you can get a panoramic view of the city and the Rhône River. It has a beautiful garden with fountains and benches to relax.
  • Musée Calvet: It is a museum dedicated to the fine arts, paintings, sculptures, and artifacts of Avignon’s rich heritage.

Natural Attractions of Avignon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Alpilles: A mountain range with beautiful landscape, scenic views, and outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and climbing.
  • The Rhône River: A beautiful river flowing through Avignon, with numerous parks, scenic spots, and outdoor activities like kayaking, fishing, and boating.
  • Pont Saint-Bénezet: A beautiful bridge built in the 12th century, providing stunning views of the Rhône River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avignon

  • Mont Ventoux: A mountain with challenging yet rewarding hiking and biking routes.
  • Luberon: A mountain range with beautiful scenic views, hiking, and biking routes.
  • La Véloroute du Pont du Gard: A biking route along the Rhône River and the Pont du Gard.

Best Things to Do in Avignon: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Avignon is known for its wines, which are produced in the nearby vineyards. There are numerous wine tasting tours available.
  • Boat Tour: A boat tour of the Rhône River provides a unique perspective of the city’s landmarks and surroundings.
  • Museums and Palaces: Avignon has numerous museums and palaces that showcase the city’s rich history and culture. A tour of these places is a must-do activity in Avignon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avignon

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit Palais des Papes and Musée du Petit Palais

Food and Drink Scene of Avignon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pistou Soup: A traditional soup made with vegetables, beans, and pesto sauce.
  • Ratatouille: A stew made with vegetables like tomatoes, zucchini, eggplants, and bell peppers.
  • Tian: A baked dish made with layers of vegetables like onions, zucchini, tomatoes, and eggplants.
  • Fougasse: A pastry made with olive oil, herbs, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Avignon is known for its wines, including Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Gigondas, and Vacqueyras.
  • Pastis: A liquor made with anise and other herbs.

Where to Stay in Avignon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous options for accommodation in Avignon, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best options are:

  • La Mirande: A luxurious hotel housed in a palace, providing an authentic Avignon experience.
  • Hotel d’Europe: A 5-star hotel located in the heart of Avignon, with beautifully designed rooms and world-class amenities.
  • Le Cloître Saint-Louis Hotel: A hotel housed in a 16th-century building, providing modern comforts and amenities.
  • Auberge de Jeunesse: A budget hostel with clean rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Avignon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lavender Products: Avignon is known for its lavender fields, and you can buy lavender oil, soap, perfumes, and other products.
  • Wines: Avignon produces some of the best wines in France, which you can buy from local vineyards and wine shops.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: Avignon is known for its beautiful pottery and ceramic products, which make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Avignon

Avignon is a historical city with strong cultural influences. The local people are friendly and hospitable, and the city hosts numerous cultural festivals, events, and concerts throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Avignon

The best time to visit Avignon is from May to September, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the city hosts numerous festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avignon

Do:

  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Visit museums and historical places.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the city and its surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore safety guidelines, especially while hiking and biking.
  • Miss out on the local wines and culinary delights.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avignon

Avignon is a safe city, but usual precautions should be taken to prevent thefts and pickpocketing.

Health in Avignon

Avignon has excellent healthcare facilities, and tourists should have valid health insurance before arriving in the city.

Getting to Avignon: Transportation Options and Logistics

The following are the transportation options available to get to Avignon.

Airports Near Avignon

  • Marseille Provence Airport: Approximately 100 km from Avignon.
  • Avignon-Provence Airport: Approximately 8 km from Avignon.

Bus Stations Near Avignon

  • Avignon Bus Station: Located in the city center, providing numerous bus connections to nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Avignon has a well-connected train station, with trains from Paris, Lyon, and Marseille.
  • Car Rental: Various car rental companies are available at the airports and bus stations.

How to Get Around in Avignon

Avignon has a well-connected public transportation system, including buses and taxis, and visitors can also rent bikes to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Avignon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous options for nearby destinations and excursions from Avignon, including:

  • Aix-en-Provence: A beautiful city with numerous cultural attractions and historical places, located approximately 80 km from Avignon.
  • Marseille: A historical port city with numerous landmarks and attractions, located approximately 100 km from Avignon.
  • Luberon: A mountain range with beautiful scenery and outdoor activities, located approximately 50 km from Avignon.

Conclusion

Avignon is a beautiful city that provides a great travel experience for history, culture, and nature enthusiasts. From its well-preserved medieval architecture to its rich culinary delights and natural beauty, Avignon has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Avignon with our comprehensive travel guide, and enjoy your stay in this beautiful city.
