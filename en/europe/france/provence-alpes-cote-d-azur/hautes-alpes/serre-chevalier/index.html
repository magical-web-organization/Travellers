Serre-Chevalier: Your Ultimate Travel Guide

Serre-Chevalier, located in Hautes-Alpes, in Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, in France, in Europe, is a stunningly beautiful destination that attracts thousands of tourists every year. This awe-inspiring resort town sits at a height of 1,200 meters and is surrounded by lush greenery, picturesque peaks and valleys, and the meandering Guisane River. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie seeking outdoor adventures, a history buff seeking to learn about the region’s rich culture and heritage, or just looking to relax and soak in the breathtaking views, Serre-Chevalier has something for everyone. So, let’s dive into this comprehensive guide and discover all that Serre-Chevalier has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Serre-Chevalier

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, then you’ll love exploring the culturally rich attractions of Serre-Chevalier. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Le Musee d’Art Sacre - This museum showcases a stunning collection of religious art and artifacts spanning several centuries.
  • Chateau de Briancon - This magnificent fortress dates back to the 18th century and is filled with history.
  • Eglise Sainte-Catherine de Sienne - This beautiful church was built in the 18th century and is known for its striking Baroque architecture.
  • Les Grands Bains du Monetier - This historic bathhouse has been around since the Roman era and is famous for its thermal baths and stunning architecture.

Natural Attractions of Serre-Chevalier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Serre-Chevalier is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The region’s stunning natural beauty is unmatched and the options for adventure are endless. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in Serre-Chevalier:

  • Serre-Chevalier Ski Resort - This world-renowned ski resort offers some of the best snow sports and skiing opportunities in the world.
  • Le Pont de l’Alpe - This stunning bridge overlooks the Guisane River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Lac du Pontillas - This serene alpine lake is a popular spot for hiking, picnics, and swimming in the summer months.
  • Montagne de l’Aiguillette - This mountain offers breathtaking views of the Serre-Chevalier valley and is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Serre-Chevalier

Serre-Chevalier is a hiker’s and mountain biker’s paradise. The region boasts a wide range of trails, ranging from easy walking paths to challenging mountain bike courses. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Serre-Chevalier:

  • Le tour du Queyras - This is a stunning 7-day hike that takes you through the heart of the Queyras National Park. Along the way, you’ll pass through beautiful meadows, wooded valleys, and rugged peaks.
  • La Route des Balcons du Briançonnais - This hiking trail runs along the balcony of the delightful Serre-Chevalier valley and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Le tour des Cerces - This beautiful trek takes you through the Cerces Massif and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • La Voie Muletiere - This historic trail was once a major trade route and offers a glimpse into the region’s rich history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Serre-Chevalier: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Serre-Chevalier. From adrenaline-fueled activities to relaxing tours, this region has it all. Here are some of the top things to do in Serre-Chevalier:

  • Skiing and snowboarding - Serre-Chevalier is home to one of the most extensive ski areas in Europe, with over 250 km of slopes and 100 lifts.
  • Paragliding - Experience the thrill of flying over the beautiful Serre-Chevalier valley with a paragliding tour.
  • White water rafting - The Guisane River offers some of the best white water rafting opportunities in the region and is a favorite among adventure seekers.
  • Wine tasting tours - The Provence region is renowned for its delicious wines, and Serre-Chevalier is no exception. Take a tour of the local vineyards and indulge in some of the region’s best wine.

Food and Drink Scene of Serre-Chevalier

No trip to Serre-Chevalier is complete without indulging in some of the region’s delicious food and drink. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Daube Provencal - This hearty beef stew is filled with tender meat and flavorful vegetables and is a Provence classic.
  • Tarte Tropezienne - This light and fluffy cream-filled cake is a must-try dessert in the region.
  • Poulet aux Ecrevisses - This delicious dish combines juicy chicken with juicy freshwater crayfish and creamy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pastis - This anise-flavored liqueur is a popular drink in Provence and is usually served with water and ice.
  • Biere de Montagne - This crisp and refreshing beer is brewed locally in Serre-Chevalier and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Roussillon Wine - This delicious red wine is produced in the Roussillon region of France and is highly praised for its bold flavors and intense aromas.

Where to Stay in Serre-Chevalier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Serre-Chevalier boasts a wide range of lodging options, ranging from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the top lodging options in the region:

  • Le Grand Aigle Hotel & Spa - This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Serre-Chevalier and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Auberge de la Paix - This cozy guesthouse is located in the charming village of Villeneuve and is known for its warm hospitality.
  • Club Med Serre-Chevalier - This all-inclusive resort is perfect for families and offers a wide range of activities and amenities.

What to Buy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Serre-Chevalier is known for its amazing local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Lavender Products - Provence is famous for its fragrant lavender, and Serre-Chevalier is no exception. Buy some lavender soaps, sachets, or oils to take home with you.
  • Cheese - The region is famous for its delicious cheeses, especially the Banon cheese, which is cured in chestnut leaves.
  • Pottery - The charming town of Briancon is known for its beautiful handmade pottery, which makes for a perfect souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Serre-Chevalier

Serre-Chevalier is steeped in rich history and culture, and the locals take great pride in preserving their traditions. Here are some of the most important customs and traditions in the region:

  • Language - While French is the official language, many locals also speak Provencal, a traditional Occitan language.
  • Family values - Family is highly valued in Serre-Chevalier and many families often gather for traditional meals and celebrations.
  • Festivals - The region hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Fete de la Saint-Jean and the Fete des Bergers.

Best Time to Visit Serre-Chevalier

Serre-Chevalier is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm and beauty. Here’s a breakdown of the best time to visit Serre-Chevalier:

  • Winter (December to March) - This is the ideal time to visit if you’re a snow sports enthusiast. The slopes are open, and the snow conditions are perfect.
  • Spring (April to May) - This is a great time to visit if you prefer milder weather. The flowers are in bloom, and the scenery is breathtaking.
  • Summer (June to August) - This is a great time to visit if you’re looking to hike, bike, or swim in the many lakes and rivers in the region.
  • Fall (September to November) - This is a great time to visit if you’re looking for fewer crowds and beautiful fall colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Serre-Chevalier

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife - Serre-Chevalier is home to many native animals and birds, so be sure to respect their habitats and space.
  • Learn the local customs - Take the time to learn about the region’s culture and customs and show respect for them.
  • Try the local cuisine - Provence is famous for its delicious cuisine, so be sure to indulge in some of the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind - Be sure to dispose of your trash properly and leave the region better than you found it.
  • Ignore safety rules - Always follow the safety rules when engaging in outdoor activities, and ensure you’re equipped with the right gear and equipment.
  • Be impolite or disrespectful - The locals are friendly and welcoming, so always be polite and respectful when interacting with them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Serre-Chevalier

Serre-Chevalier is a relatively safe destination, but always take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always wear a helmet - Whether skiing, biking, or engaging in any other potentially dangerous activity, always wear a helmet to protect your head from injury.
  • Stay on marked trails - Always stay on marked trails when hiking or biking and avoid venturing off into unknown terrain.
  • Be aware of weather conditions - Weather conditions can quickly change in the mountains, so always be aware of the forecast and prepare accordingly.

Health in Serre-Chevalier

Serre-Chevalier has an excellent healthcare system and is well-equipped to handle any medical emergencies. However, it’s always best to take precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some health tips:

  • Stay hydrated - The mountain air can be dry, so be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Protect yourself from the sun - The sun’s rays can be strong in the mountains, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing.
  • Be mindful of altitude sickness - If you’re not used to the altitude, you may experience altitude sickness, so be sure to take it easy and give yourself time to acclimate.
