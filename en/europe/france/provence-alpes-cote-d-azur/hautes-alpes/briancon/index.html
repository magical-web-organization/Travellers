If you’re looking for a place to escape the hustle and bustle of city life in France, Briançon is the perfect destination. Located in the Hautes-Alpes region of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, in Europe, this alpine town boasts cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure opportunities. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, local cuisine, accommodations, and more in Briançon.

Introduction to Briançon

Briançon, nestled in the heart of the French Alps, is a town with rich history, culture, and natural beauty. It is also the highest town in France, situated at an altitude of 1,326 meters. With its stunning mountain views, ancient architecture, and narrow cobblestone streets, Briançon is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and culture-seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Briançon

From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Briançon. Here are the top tourist attractions to add to your must-visit list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Briançon citadel: This is the most iconic attraction in Briançon. Built in the 17th century by Vauban, a military engineer commissioned by King Louis XIV, the citadel is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the fortress and ramparts with views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Musée de la Savoie: This museum displays traditional objects from life in Savoy, dating back to the 18th century. It’s located in the former Ursuline convent of Briançon.
  • L’Église Collégiale: This is a 16th-century church with beautiful, intricate paintings and artwork.
  • Cité Vauban: This is a restored village with traditional houses situated around a central square. It’s a great spot for a relaxing stroll.

Natural Attractions of Briançon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Le Prorel cable car: Take a ride on the cable car up to the top of Le Prorel mountain for panoramic views of the town and surrounding mountains.
  • Parc National des Ecrins: This national park is a nature lover’s paradise with a network of hiking trails, waterfalls, and glaciers.
  • Grande Gargouille: This is a scenic drive that passes through picturesque mountain villages with breathtaking views.
  • Fort des Trois Têtes: This fortress on the top of a hill offers stunning views of the town and surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Briançon

Briançon is also a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and mountain biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Chemin du Roy: This is a historic trail that follows the route that King Louis XIV took to travel between Paris and Italy. The trail passes through Briançon and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Le Chemin de la Guisane: This is a pleasant walk that follows the Guisane river and offers great views of the mountains and the old town.
  • Le Grand Tour des Cerces: This is a challenging hiking trail that takes you through the Cerces mountain range and offers spectacular views of glaciers, valleys, and lakes.

Best Things to Do in Briançon: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Briançon. Here are a few:

  • Skiing: Briançon is a popular ski destination with access to some of the best ski resorts in France, including Serre Chevalier.
  • Paragliding: Take a tandem flight with a licensed instructor and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Rafting and kayaking: The Guisane river offers plenty of opportunities for water-based activities, ranging from gentle floats to rapids-level excitement.
  • Wine-tasting tours: Sample the delicious local wines from the nearby region of Côtes-du-Rhône.

Week-Long Itinerary for Briançon

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Briançon:

Day Activity
1 Explore the historic citadel and ramparts
2 Take the cable car to Le Prorel mountain and enjoy the panoramic views
3 Hike or bike on the Chemin du Roy

5 visit the Parc National des Ecrins for a nature walk
6 Experience the thrill of paragliding

Food and Drink Scene of Briançon

No trip to France is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks in Briançon:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tarte aux myrtilles: A delicious blueberry tart that is a specialty of the region.
  • Pieds paquets: This traditional dish is made with tripe and sheep feet, served with a tomato sauce.
  • Ratatouille: A delicious vegetable stew made with tomatoes, eggplant, peppers, and zucchini.

Local Beverages

  • Chambéryzette: This is a tasty local liqueur made with wine, herbs, and fruit.
  • Eau de vie: A strong brandy distilled from locally-grown fruit.

Where to Stay in Briançon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Briançon has a variety of accommodations to fit every budget and preference. Here are some recommended options:

  • Hotel de la Chaussée: A highly-rated budget-friendly hotel situated in the heart of the town, with easy access to all the major attractions.
  • Auberge de Jeunesse: A modern hostel with a warm and inviting atmosphere, located near the citadel.
  • La Ferme de Belline: A charming guesthouse with a rural atmosphere, surrounded by beautiful gardens and orchards.

What to Buy in Briançon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products to bring home from your trip to Briançon:

  • Cheese: The region is famous for its delicious cheeses, particularly fromage de Montagne.
  • Honey: The local honey is known for its unique flavor and health benefits.
  • Pottery: The region is known for its traditional pottery, which makes for a unique and authentic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Briançon

Briançon has a rich cultural history, with many traditions that have been passed down through generations. Here are some notable customs:

  • Fête des Bergers: This is an annual festival that celebrates the region’s sheep herding culture with food, music, and dancing.
  • French Alpine cheese swords: These are special swords used to cut large rounds of cheese into small wedges, a tradition that has been passed down through generations.
  • Traditional crafts: The region has a rich history of traditional crafts, including pottery, embroidery, and weaving.

Best Time to Visit Briançon

The best time to visit Briançon is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking to hit the slopes, winter is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Briançon

To make the most of your trip to Briançon, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore the historic citadel and ramparts.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including the famous Tarte aux myrtilles.
  • Do hike or bike the Chemin du Roy.
  • Do visit the Parc National des Ecrins for a nature walk.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing, even in the summer months.
  • Don’t bring your ski gear in the summer months, as the ski resorts are closed.
  • Don’t forget to try the local liqueur, Chambéryzette.

Safety and Health

Briançon is generally a safe and healthy place to visit. However, here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Briançon

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially if you’re hiking alone.
  • Follow all safety instructions for outdoor activities like paragliding or rafting.
  • Watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas, such as the citadel.

Health in Briançon

  • The high altitude can cause altitude sickness, so be sure to acclimate slowly.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Carry any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Briançon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to help you get to Briançon:

  • Airports Near Briançon: The nearest airports are in Turin, Milan, and Grenoble.
  • Bus Stations Near Briançon: There are several bus companies that serve the town, including Flixbus and Ouibus.
  • Other Options: You can also drive or take a train to Briançon.

How to Get Around in Briançon

The best way to get around Briançon is by foot. The town is relatively small, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, there are also buses and taxis available if you need them.

Where to Go Next from Briançon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue your adventure beyond Briançon, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Grenoble: This vibrant university town is known for its rich history, culture, and beautiful architecture.
  • Montgenèvre: This ski resort offers over 400 kilometers of slopes and scenic views of the Alps.
  • Val-d’Isère: Another popular ski resort, Val-d’Isère offers some of the best skiing in the world.

Conclusion

Briançon is a charming alpine town with something for everyone. From cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to delicious cuisine and comfortable accommodations, this gem in the French Alps has it all. Plan your trip today and get ready to experience the beauty of Briançon for yourself.
