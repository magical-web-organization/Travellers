Introduction to Hautes-Alpes

Hautes-Alpes is a department located in the southeastern region of France, bordering Italy to the east. It boasts breathtaking scenery, from the snow-capped mountains to the lush valleys and crystal clear lakes. It is a year-round destination, attracting outdoor enthusiasts with its skiing and snowboarding during the winter months and hiking and biking trails in the warmer seasons. The cultural attractions, including museums and historical sites, are also a draw. Plus, the local cuisine and wine are a must-try for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Hautes-Alpes

Here are some of the top places to visit in Hautes-Alpes:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • (1) Musée de la Maison du Berger: This museum offers a glimpse into the lives of the shepherds and their families who used to live in the mountain pastures of Hautes-Alpes.
  • (2) Musée Gassendi: Located in Digne-les-Bains, this museum showcases art and science in a unique way.
  • (3) Cathédrale Notre-Dame-du-Bourg: This Roman Catholic cathedral dates back to the 12th century and is known for its stunning architecture.
  • (4) Fort de Mont-Dauphin: Constructed by the famous French military engineer Vauban, this fort features intricate construction and designs.

Natural Attractions of Hautes-Alpes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • (1) Lac de Serre-Ponçon: This lake is a popular spot for swimming and water sports, as well as a great spot for picnics on its banks.
  • (2) Parc National des Écrins: This national park covers over 90,000 hectares and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna.
  • (3) Les Gorges du Verdon: Known as the “Grand Canyon of Europe,” these gorges offer breathtaking views and opportunities for kayaking.
  • (4) Col d’Izoard: This pass offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, and is a popular spot for cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hautes-Alpes

Hautes-Alpes is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. With a range of hiking and biking trails, you can enjoy the beautiful scenery at your own pace. Here are some of the best routes:

  • (1) GR5: This trail is perfect for experienced hikers, covering over 2,600 kilometers across Europe, including a section that passes through Hautes-Alpes.
  • (2) Tour of the Oisans: This trail covers the Oisans region of the French Alps and offers stunning views of snow-capped peaks and picturesque villages.
  • (3) Col du Galibier: This iconic cycling route is a must-do for any seasoned cyclist, offering incredible scenery and challenging climbs.
  • (4) Montagne de Ceüse: This mountain offers some of the best rock climbing in France, with over 500 routes for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Hautes-Alpes: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Hautes-Alpes, whether you are traveling alone or with a group:

  • (1) Skiing and Snowboarding: With over 400 kilometers of slopes, Hautes-Alpes is a winter sports paradise.
  • (2) Paragliding: Take to the skies and enjoy the incredible views of the mountains and valleys.
  • (3) Wine Tasting: Discover the local wines and learn about the wine-making process on a tour of the vineyards.
  • (4) Rafting and Kayaking: The rivers of Hautes-Alpes offer exciting opportunities for white-water rafting and kayaking.

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Hautes-Alpes:

Food and Drink Scene of Hautes-Alpes

Must-Try Dishes

Hautes-Alpes is known for its hearty cuisine, with dishes that are perfect for warming up after a day on the slopes. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • (1) Tourtons: These fried or baked turnovers are filled with a variety of ingredients including cheese, potatoes, and bacon.
  • (2) Daube Provençale: This beef stew is a staple of Provençal cuisine, richly flavored with red wine and aromatic herbs.
  • (3) Tarte aux Myrtilles: This blueberry tart is a classic dessert of the region, often served with crème fraîche.
  • (4) Pissaladière: Similar to a pizza, this dish features a thin crust topped with caramelized onions, anchovies, and olives.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Côtes du Luberon: This red wine is produced in the Luberon region of Hautes-Alpes and is known for its fruity flavor and subtle tannins.
  • (2) Bière de Briançon: This local beer is brewed in the town of Briançon, using water from high-altitude sources and locally grown hops.

Where to Stay in Hautes-Alpes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hautes-Alpes offers a range of accommodations for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • (1) Chalet-Hôtel de Serre Ratier: This charming hotel is located in the village of Chantemerle and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • (2) La Maison du Guil: This beautiful guesthouse is set in a restored 16th-century building and features a garden and terrace with panoramic views.
  • (3) Le Taha’a Island Resort & Spa: This luxurious resort is located on its own private island and offers a range of amenities, including a spa and water sports.

What to Buy in Hautes-Alpes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Hautes-Alpes:

  • (1) Cheese: The region is known for its delicious cheeses, such as Banon and Reblochon.
  • (2) Lavender Products: Lavender is a staple of Provencal culture and can be found in soaps, perfumes, and candles.
  • (3) Honey: Hautes-Alpes is home to a range of honey producers, offering unique flavors such as chestnut and heather.

Local Culture and Customs of Hautes-Alpes

Hautes-Alpes has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of French and Italian influences. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • (1) Food and Wine Culture: Food and wine are an important part of the culture in Hautes-Alpes, with many local festivals and celebrations centered around them.
  • (2) Respect for Nature: The locals have a deep respect for the natural environment and take measures to protect it.
  • (3) Hospitality: The people of Hautes-Alpes are known for their warm and friendly hospitality, welcoming visitors with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Hautes-Alpes

The best time to visit Hautes-Alpes depends on your interests. If you are into winter sports, then the best time to go is from December to April. If you are interested in hiking and outdoor activities, then the best time is from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hautes-Alpes

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and stunning scenery.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Ski or snowboard off-piste without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hautes-Alpes

Hautes-Alpes is a safe destination, but it’s always good to take precautions when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid secluded areas at night. Make sure to lock your doors at night and be wary of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Hautes-Alpes

The health care system in France is excellent, and there are hospitals and clinics throughout the department. Make sure to have comprehensive health insurance before traveling, and take any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Hautes-Alpes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hautes-Alpes

The closest airports to Hautes-Alpes are Marseille Provence Airport (MRS), Nice Côte d’Azur Airport (NCE), and Grenoble-Isère Airport (GNB).

Bus Stations Near Hautes-Alpes

There are several bus companies that operate in Hautes-Alpes, including FlixBus, Eurolines, and OUIBUS.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, the closest train station to Hautes-Alpes is in Grenoble. You can also rent a car and drive to Hautes-Alpes, which allows you to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Hautes-Alpes

The best way to get around Hautes-Alpes is by car, as it allows you to explore the region at your own pace. There are also several bus companies that operate in the department, as well as taxis and bike rentals in larger towns.

Where to Go Next from Hautes-Alpes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after your visit to Hautes-Alpes:

  • (1) Avignon: This historic city is known for its well-preserved medieval architecture and famous bridge, Pont d’Avignon.
  • (2) Aix-en-Provence: This picturesque town is known for its thermal springs and is the birthplace of the famous artist Paul Cézanne.
  • (3) Monaco: This tiny principality is home to the famous Monte Carlo Casino, as well as many luxurious yachts and cars.

Conclusion

Hautes-Alpes is a beautiful region of France, offering a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and stunning scenery. From skiing and snowboarding to hiking and biking, there is something for everyone in Hautes-Alpes. And with its delicious cuisine and warm hospitality, it is a destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler.
