Introduction to Mercantour National Park

Mercantour National Park is situated in the Alpes-Maritimes department of the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region in southeast France. The national park is bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the south, Italy to the east, and the Alpes de Haute Provence to the north. The park’s area spans over 685 square miles and houses several remarkable trails, natural wonders and cultural attractions. If you’re planning a trip to France and you love the outdoors, hiking and scenic views, Mercantour National Park should make it on your bucket list of places to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Mercantour National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-see museums in Mercantour National Park is the Museum of Art and History in Saint-Martin-Vésubie. This museum offers a rich history of the Vésubie Valley and exhibits mountains, culture, and ancient traditions local to Mercantour.

The Saint-Etienne-de-Tinée, Guillaumes, and Barcelonnette are an important part of Mercantour’s history and culture, so stop by their historical landmarks. Other historical landmarks are the Col-de-Tende, the municipality of Tende, forts of the Savoyard Route, Square Galibert and the Royal Bridge.

Natural Attractions of Mercantour National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature and outdoor lovers have a lot of things to explore in Mercantour National Park. You can take a leisurely stroll down the valley or opt for a rugged and challenging hike, as there are trails suitable for all skill levels.

The Lac d’Allos is a large lake in the center of the park and the highest natural alpine lake in Europe. It is a perfect spot for swimming, fishing, and hiking. Other places worth seeing include the Vallon de la Madone, the Col de la Bonnette, the Cayolle and the Red-rock Gorge of Daluis.

In winters, Mercantour National Park turns into a winter wonderland where you can enjoy skiing, ice climbing, and snowshoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mercantour National Park

Mercantour National Park has over 600 miles of hiking and biking trails, crossing magnificent mountain scenery and valleys. Some popular trails are the GR5 Route, which passes through the beautiful Vallée du Haut-Var and the famous “balcony trail” Chemin de la Merveille in the Gorges of the Cians.

One of the best routes for cycling is the Route des Grandes Alpes that spans over 450 miles from Lake Geneva to the French Riviera, passing through Mercantour National Park.

Best Things to Do in Mercantour National Park: Activities and Tours

There is a diverse range of activities to indulge in while in Mercantour National Park. You can go rock climbing, horse riding, paragliding, and rafting. You can even join a guided tour and learn about the park’s history and culture. Guided tours are available throughout the year, and they provide an in-depth insight into the area’s flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mercantour National Park

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Mercantour National Park:

  • Day 1: Explore the Saint-Martin-Vésubie valley and visit the Museum of Art and History.
  • Day 2: Hike the Mercantour massif and discover the high mountains.
  • Day 3: Visit the Vallon de la Madone, the Col de la Bonnette, and the Red-rock Gorge of Daluis.
  • Day 4: Learn about the park’s flora and fauna on a guided tour. Attend a show at the Open-Air Theatre of Saint-Martin-Vésubie.
  • Day 5: Go skiing and snowboarding.
  • Day 6: Visit the Forts of the Savoyard Route, Square Galibert, and the Royal Bridge.
  • Day 7: Relax by the Lac d’Allos and enjoy some swimming, fishing, or hiking.

Food and Drink Scene of Mercantour National Park

Must-Try Dishes

The region is known for its delicious dishes, and you shouldn’t leave without trying some of them. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Pissaladière: a delicious tart with caramelized onions, tomatoes, and anchovies, topped with olives.
  • Socca: a chickpea flour pancake, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
  • Ratatouille: a combination of eggplant, zucchini, and tomato stew that is perfect for vegetarians.
  • Daube Niçoise: a hearty beef stew that is slow-cooked in red wine and spices.

Local Beverages

The region also has some excellent wines that are worth trying, such as the Bellet AOC wine and the Bandol AOC wine. You should also try the local beer, La Cagole, and the Pastis, a popular anise-flavored liquor.

Where to Stay in Mercantour National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mercantour National Park has a diverse range of accommodation options, such as hotels, hostels, guesthouses, campsites and vacation rentals. There are also several options for those who prefer a more immersive experience, such as staying in a mountain refuge. Some of the popular accommodation options include:

  • Le Petit Refuge: a charming mountain retreat with a sauna and hot tub.
  • La Grande Bastide: a lovely bed and breakfast with an outdoor pool.
  • Les Balcons Du Viso: a cozy guesthouse situated in the heart of the park.

What to Buy in Mercantour National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the souvenirs and local products you can buy in Mercantour National Park include lavender, honey, olive oil, and artisanal soaps. You should also check out the artisanal woodwork, ceramics, and textiles made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Mercantour National Park

The people of Mercantour National Park are known for their friendliness, hospitality, and resilience. They are passionate about their history, culture, food, and nature, and they will gladly share their knowledge with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Mercantour National Park

The best time to visit Mercantour National Park is from May to October, as the weather is warm and dry, and the trails and the outdoor activities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mercantour National Park

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Follow safe hiking and biking protocols.
  • Carry a map and a compass.
  • Learn about the history and the culture of the park.

Don’t:

  • Litter.
  • Feed the wildlife.
  • Pick flowers or plants.
  • Smoke in protected areas.
  • Start fires.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mercantour National Park

Mercantour National Park is generally safe to visit, but you should take precautions, such as:

  • Check the weather forecast before you hike and prepare accordingly.
  • Carry enough water and food with you, especially if you plan to hike in remote areas.
  • Stay on the marked trails and consult the park authorities in case of emergency.

Health in Mercantour National Park

There are several healthcare facilities in Mercantour National Park, such as hospitals and medical clinics. You should also carry a basic first-aid kit with you and ensure that you have valid health insurance.

Getting to Mercantour National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mercantour National Park

The closest airport to Mercantour National Park is Nice Côte d’Azur Airport, which is around 60 miles away. You can also fly into Marseille Provence Airport or Toulon-Hyères Airport.

Bus Stations Near Mercantour National Park

There are daily buses from Nice to Mercantour National Park. The journey takes approximately three hours.

Other Options

You can also drive to the park if you have a car. If you don’t have a car, you can rent one or hire a taxi or a transfer service.

How to Get Around in Mercantour National Park

The best way to get around Mercantour National Park is by hiking or biking. You can also drive or take local buses to reach some of the more remote areas in the park.

Where to Go Next from Mercantour National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Mercantour National Park include:

  • Nice: a beautiful coastal city with museums, art galleries, restaurants, and beaches.
  • Antibes: a charming medieval town with a lovely old town and a bustling marina.
  • Monaco: a glamorous tiny principality famous for its casinos, yachts, and the Grand Prix.

Conclusion

Mercantour National Park is a paradise of natural beauty, serene landscapes, and rich cultural experiences. Whether you want to hike the trails, go skiing, or explore local culture and cuisine, this park is an excellent destination for all kinds of travelers. With this comprehensive guide, you can easily plan your next adventure and enjoy all that Mercantour National Park has to offer.
