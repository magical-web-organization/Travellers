Introduction to Cannes

Cannes is a beautiful coastal city located in the Alpes-Maritimes region of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, France and attracts millions of visitors every year. It is known for its stunning beaches, luxurious hotels, and high-end boutiques and restaurants. Cannes is famous throughout the world for hosting the Cannes Film Festival, which takes place every year in May, and is one of the most glamorous events on the international film calendar.

List of Places to Visit in Cannes

If you’re planning to visit Cannes, here are the top places you should explore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de la Castre: This museum is located atop of the Suquet Hill and houses an impressive collection of historical and archaeology artefacts from prehistoric times to the present day.

  • Musée de la Mer: Situated in a former prison, this museum dedicated to marine life and archaeology preserves scores of underwater treasures from the depths of the Mediterranean Sea.

  • Villa Ephrussi de Rothschild: This is a magnificent villa located on the Saint-Jean-Cap-Ferrat peninsula and features impressive gardens and architecture from the 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Cannes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Île Saint-Honorat: Just off the coast of Cannes, this small island is home to an ancient silent monastery, beautiful gardens and vineyards.

  • Croisette Promenade: One of the most iconic landmarks of the city, this pedestrian walkway overlooking the Mediterranean Sea is both picturesque and relaxing.

  • Le Suquet Hill: Take a stroll along the winding alleyways to explore the old town of Cannes and appreciate some of the most stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cannes

Cannes offers numerous hiking and biking routes each with its breathtaking natural beauty and destination worth a visit.

  • Le Chemin de la Corniche: This relatively easy hike, which takes approximately one and a half hours, is one of the best panoramic walks offering stunning views of the Mediterranean sea, the Lerins Island and the Esterel Massif.

  • Parc Naturel Départemental de la Croix des Gardes: Enjoy approximately 5 kilometres of hiking trails with stunning views including the Lérins Islands and even the Esterel Mountains.

  • Parc de la Valmasque: Located between Cannes and Antibes, more reminiscent of a fairy tale forest than a city park, offers 360 ha’s of breathtaking nature and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Cannes: Activities and Tours

Cannes offers a wealth of exciting activities both on and offshore. You can cruise the French Riviera aboard a yacht, run wild in one of their waterparks, or wander the halls of ancient museums. Here are some of the must-try things to experience.

  • Cannes Film Festival: Join this world-renowned festival that has been going on for more than 70 years!!!

  • Gala Dinner at the Palais des Festivals: It is one of the most celebrated and memorable experiences of Cannes is a dinner, gala or ball at the Palais des Festivals.

  • Shopping at Rue d’Antibes: A shopping street with a variety of high-end brands and markets is perfect for taking a break from the beach and discovering the city’s way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cannes

If you’re fortunate enough to be spending a week in the beautiful city of Cannes, you want to make sure you make the most out of your vacation. The following itinerary will ensure your week is packed with the best attractions.

Day Event/Activity
Day 1 Relax at the beach and enjoy tasty seafood at the Boulevard de la Croisette
Day 2 Take a walk through the old town and visit the Musée de la Castre
Day 3 Visit the fortified city of Antibes to see the Picasso Museum and explore its ramparts
Day 4 Explore the Lérins Islands and their historical and religious monuments
Day 5 Take a hike across the Massif de l’Estérel or the countryside
Day 6 Discover the beauty of Grasse and its perfumeries
Day 7 Take a trip to Nice and stroll through the unique markets and old town of the city

Food and Drink Scene of Cannes

The food and drink scene in Cannes is truly a reflection of its local culture and famous French cuisine. We’ve gathered the best dishes and beverages to taste which represent the true essence of Cannes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salade niçoise: A staple dish in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region, this salad typically includes tuna, anchovies, tomatoes, lettuce, and olives, topped with a vinaigrette dressing.

  • Bouillabaisse: This French fish soup, traditionally made with Mediterranean fish, served with croutons and a saffron aioli.

  • Ratatouille: A healthy Mediterranean classic dish, Ratatouille is more delicious made from summer veggies like eggplant, tomatoes, and zucchini in a savory sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Rosé Wine: This light pink wine is a staple in the region and pairs well with seafood and salads alike.

  • Pastis: An anise-flavored aperitif, Pastis is enjoyed throughout the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region and can be enjoyed on its own or mixed in with water.

Where to Stay in Cannes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cannes has a vast range of accommodations, hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, to suit all budgets and travel preferences. Here are some of the top recommendations to stay in during your trip.

  • Hôtel Martinez Cannes: A luxurious hotel with a private beach and Michelin-starred restaurants.

  • Five Seas Hotel Cannes: A boutique hotel in the heart of the city, with a rooftop pool and spa.

  • La Villa Cannes Croisette: A charming guesthouse located 5 minutes from the beach and the city center.

What to Buy in Cannes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a little piece of Cannes back home, here are some of the most popular souvenirs and local products to purchase during your stay.

  • Perfumes from Grasse: The famous French perfume town of Grasse is located about 30 minutes outside of Cannes, where you will find some of the world’s finest fragrances.

  • Provencal Soaps: Made with high-quality ingredients, these soaps are a perfect gift or souvenir to take back with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Cannes

Cannes is a city rich in history and culture. Here are some of the local customs and traditions that you might notice while traveling in Cannes.

  • Greetings: In France, it is customary to greet one another with a friendly kiss on both cheeks, especially during professional and social contexts.

  • Dress Code: Cannes is known for being a chic and fashionable city, and it is recommended that visitors dress accordingly, especially when visiting high-end restaurants and events.

Best Time to Visit Cannes

Cannes is a popular destination year-round, depending on what you’re looking for. The city has mild, Mediterranean weather most of the year, making it an excellent place to visit year-round. However, the best time to visit Cannes is during the summer months if you want to enjoy the beaches, outdoor activities, and popular events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cannes

Here are some of the essential do’s and don’ts of Cannes to help you navigate the city and make the most of your trip.

Do:

  • Do dress to impress: Dressing smartly is considered a sign of respect and is essential, especially when attending high-end events.

  • Do take some time-out on the beaches: Cannes has plenty of beaches to enjoy, make sure to spend some time taking in the sun and the beautiful Mediterranean sea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch food directly: In France, it is customary not to touch food with your bare hands, including bread.

  • Don’t order a cappuccino after lunch: In France, cappuccinos are considered a breakfast drink, and should not be ordered after lunchtime.

Safety and Health

While Cannes is a relatively safe city to visit, it is always important to take precautions and be prepared when traveling.

Safety in Cannes

  • Be aware of pickpockets: As with any major city, pickpockets are prevalent in busy areas, so always keep an eye on your belongings.

  • Avoid walking alone at night: It is always best to avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Cannes

  • Stay hydrated: Cannes can be very hot during the summer months, so it’s essential to stay hydrated by carrying water with you at all times.

  • Pack sun protection: Sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses are essential during the summer months when the sun is at its strongest.

Getting to Cannes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cannes is well-connected to the rest of France and Europe, with several transportation options available to visitors.

Airports Near Cannes

  • Nice Côte d’Azur Airport: Located approximately 30 minutes from Cannes, Nice Airport is the city’s closest international airport and serves various airlines and destinations from around the world.

Bus Stations Near Cannes

  • Gare Routière de Cannes: Located in the heart of the city, Cannes’ main bus station connects the city to other destinations throughout France.

Other Options

  • Train: Cannes’s main train station is located in the city center, offering connections to destinations throughout France and Europe.

  • Boat: Cannes has an excellent marina, offering connections to the nearby Lérins Islands and other destinations along the coast.

How to Get Around in Cannes

Cannes is a relatively small city, making it easy to get around on foot, but there are also several other convenient transportation options available.

  • Bus: Cannes has an extensive bus network, offering connections throughout the city and to nearby destinations.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available throughout Cannes, with taxi ranks located throughout the city.

  • Bicycle: Bicycles can be rented throughout Cannes, allowing visitors to explore the city and nearby areas at their tempo.

Where to Go Next from Cannes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning an extended trip to Cannes, you might want to explore some of the nearby destinations as well.

  • St. Tropez: Located approximately 1.5 hours from Cannes, this famous French resort town is home to luxury boutiques, beaches, and nightlife.

  • Monaco: A glamorous principality located approximately 45 minutes from Cannes, known for its casinos, yacht-lined harbor, and Formula One Grand Prix.

  • Antibes: Located only a short drive from Cannes this destination famous for its old town, luxurious yachts and the Picasso Museum is a great day trip.

Conclusion

Cannes is a city that offers something for everyone, with its beautiful beaches, world-renowned events, and a charming old town. In this travel guide, we have shared some of the best things to do, where to stay, and local culture to help you make the most of your visit to this stunning destination in France.
