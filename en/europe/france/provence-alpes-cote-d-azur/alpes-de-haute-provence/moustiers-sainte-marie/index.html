Introduction to Moustiers Sainte Marie

Moustiers Sainte Marie is a small, picturesque town set in a narrow valley in the Alpes-de-Haute-Provence region of southeastern France. It is renowned for its stunning natural surroundings, including the steep cliffs that surround the town, and its charming Provençal character with narrow streets lined with artisanal shops filled with pottery, lavender, and olive oil products.

The town is known for its unique location below a rocky outcropping, on top of which is a golden star – “La Star de Moustiers” – suspended on chains. Legend has it that the star was given in gratitude by a knight who was held captive and prayed to the Virgin Mary for his release.

List of Places to Visit in Moustiers Sainte Marie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Notre-Dame de Beauvoir: a 12th-century chapel situated on a hill above Moustiers. It offers a panoramic view of the town, the nearby Lac de Sainte Croix, and the Verdon Gorge.
  • Musée de la Faïence: a museum dedicated to the local pottery-making tradition, featuring exhibitions of both ancient and contemporary ceramics.
  • Chapelle des Pénitents: a 17th-century chapel adorned with colorful frescoes and a beautiful wooden ceiling. The chapel displays religious artifacts and art pieces.

Natural Attractions of Moustiers Sainte Marie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Verdon Gorge: a stunning canyon located just a few kilometers from Moustiers. It is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts offering hiking and kayaking opportunities.
  • Lac de Sainte-Croix: a pristine blue-green lake in the heart of the Verdon Natural Regional Park. It is perfect for swimming, fishing and water sports.
  • Plateau de Valensole: a vast plateau where lavender fields erupt into color in June and July.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moustiers Sainte Marie

Moustiers Sainte Marie and its surroundings provide an ideal landscape for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Here are some recommended trails:

  • The Sentier Martel: a famous hiking trail that winds its way through the Verdon Gorge, taking a full day to complete.
  • Les Portes du Verdon: a family-friendly route that starts at the Lac de Sainte Croix and takes about 2 hours to complete.
  • Around the Plateau de Valensole: a gentle bike ride through lavender fields and small Provençal villages.

Best Things to Do in Moustiers Sainte Marie: Activities and Tours

  • Market day: discover the local colorful markets on Wednesday and Saturday mornings, where you can find fresh produce, lavender products, and handmade ceramics.
  • Visit the local olive oil mills and participate in a tasting.
  • Take a sunset boat tour on the Lac de Sainte Croix and admire the spectacular views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moustiers Sainte Marie

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Moustiers Sainte Marie:

Day 1: Arrive in Moustiers Sainte Marie – Explore the town and visit Notre-Dame de Beauvoir.

Day 2: Visit the famous Verdon Gorge – Walk along the Sentier Martel.

Day 3: Take a bike ride around the Plateau de Valensole – Lavender fields galore.

Day 4: Visit the Musée de la Faïence – Learn about the tradition of local pottery-making.

Day 5: Kayak down the Verdon River – An unforgettable experience of the canyon.

Day 6: Drive around the region – Visit small villages like Aups and Cotignac and sample local wines and olives.

Day 7: Take a sunset boat tour on the Lac de Sainte Croix – A perfect way to end the trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Moustiers Sainte Marie

Must-Try Dishes

Daube Provençale: a hearty beef stew, slow-cooked in red wine and herbs.

Ratatouille: a popular vegetable stew made with eggplant, tomatoes, and zucchini.

Local Beverages

Pastis: a strong anise-flavored aperitif.

Bellet: the local wine, made with a blend of white and red grapes.

Where to Stay in Moustiers Sainte Marie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moustiers Sainte Marie offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets:

  • Hotel Le Relais: a comfortable 3-star hotel located in the heart of the town.
  • La Bastide de Moustiers: a luxury hotel with a gourmet restaurant, owned by Alain Ducasse.
  • Camping Saint Clair: a campsite located on the shores of the Lac de Sainte Croix.

What to Buy in Moustiers Sainte Marie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery and ceramics from local artisans – Moustiers Sainte Marie is famous for its faïence, a type of tin-glazed earthenware.
  • Lavender products – such as soap, sachets, and essential oil. Moustiers Sainte Marie lies in the heart of the lavender fields of Provence.
  • Olives and olive oil – the region is known for producing some of the best olive oil in France.

Local Culture and Customs of Moustiers Sainte Marie

Moustiers Sainte Marie has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the Provençal customs and traditions, particularly in its cuisine and artisanal crafts. Respect for the church and the ancient chapel, Notre-Dame de Beauvoir, is also an important part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Moustiers Sainte Marie

The best time to visit Moustiers Sainte Marie is from May to September when the weather is warm, and the lavender fields and markets are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moustiers Sainte Marie

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Take care of the environment – the region’s natural beauty is fragile.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  • Touch any of the ancient frescoes or artifacts on display in the Chapelle des Pénitents.
  • Make noise or disturb the peace and tranquility of the town and surrounding area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moustiers Sainte Marie

Moustiers Sainte Marie is generally a safe town, but like any popular tourist destination, it is important to take precautions:

  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Pay attention to trail markers when hiking or biking on the nearby trails, and avoid hiking alone.
  • Don’t try to climb to the La Star de Moustiers, as it is prohibited.

Health in Moustiers Sainte Marie

Make sure to drink only bottled or treated water to avoid any stomach problems. Sunscreen and hats are essential as the weather can be very hot during the summer months.

Getting to Moustiers Sainte Marie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moustiers Sainte Marie is accessible by different modes of transportation:

Airports Near Moustiers Sainte Marie

The nearest airports are Marseille Provence Airport and Nice-Côte d’Azur Airport, which are approximately 130 km and 140 km away, respectively.

Bus Stations Near Moustiers Sainte Marie

There is a local bus service called “Les Bus du Verdon” that connects Moustiers Sainte Marie with other towns in the region, such as Castellane, Aups, and Riez.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to explore the region, and there are several car rental companies at the airports and in Moustiers Sainte Marie. Alternatively, you can also join a guided tour that includes transportation.

How to Get Around in Moustiers Sainte Marie

The town itself is small and easily walkable. However, to explore the surrounding areas, such as the Verdon Gorge, Lac de Sainte Croix, or Plateau de Valensole, a car or a bike rental is highly recommended.

Where to Go Next from Moustiers Sainte Marie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Moustiers Sainte Marie is in the heart of the Alpes-de-Haute-Provence region, which means there are many other destinations and excursions you can take:

  • Aix-en-Provence: a lively university city known for its architecture, markets, and art museums. It is approximately 100 km away.
  • Avignon: a historic walled city with impressive landmarks like the Papal Palace and the famous bridge. It is approximately 150 km away.
  • The Luberon Regional Nature Park: a breathtaking landscape characterized by rolling hills, lavender fields, and charming villages like Gordes and Roussillon. It is approximately 70 km away.

Conclusion

Moustiers Sainte Marie is a destination that has something for everyone – from natural beauty to cultural heritage, outdoor adventures to shopping and dining. It is a jewel of the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, and a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to experience the charm and beauty of the region.
