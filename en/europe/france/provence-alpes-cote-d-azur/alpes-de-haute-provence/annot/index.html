Annot is a peaceful village located in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence, in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region in southeastern France. Known for its scenic beauty, outdoor recreational activities, cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Annot attracts travelers from around the world. To help you plan your trip, we present a comprehensive travel guide for Annot, including recommendations on the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and important cultural information.

Introduction to Annot

Annot is a charming village nestled in the mountains of Provence. It is known for its picturesque landscapes, historical architecture, and friendly locals. Visitors can stroll along the ancient narrow streets, admire the old stone houses, and soak up the tranquility of the place. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the many cultural, natural, and gastronomic treasures of Annot.

List of Places to Visit in Annot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Annot has a rich historical background, and there are plenty of cultural attractions to visit. The following museums and historical sites are must-visit places in Annot:

  • Musée des Tourneurs sur Bois et la Vallée du Haut-Verdon - a museum showcasing the woodworking and handicrafts unique to Annot
  • Église Notre-Dame-de-l’Assomption - a beautiful 16th-century church with stunning architecture and religious art
  • Tour de l’Horloge - a medieval tower with a clock and bell, which used to be part of the old defensive walls of the village

Natural Attractions of Annot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Annot is located in a stunning natural setting, surrounded by picturesque mountains, forests, and rivers. There are plenty of outdoor recreational activities for visitors to enjoy. The following natural attractions should not be missed:

  • Gorges du Verdon - A spectacular canyon with turquoise blue waters flowing through it, it offers kayaking, rafting and excellent hiking.
  • Les Grès d’Annot - A rock-climbing site with extraordinary limestone formations, surrounded by luscious green woodlands.
  • Col d’Allos – A scenic Alpine pass is open to motorist only during the summer months, which allows spectacular views over the valleys and nearby region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Annot

Annot has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that take visitors on an enchanting journey through the local landscape. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Crêtes de l’Estoublaisse - a beautiful hike with exquisite views across the region starting at the parking near Annot train station.
  • La Piste Bleue - A dream route for cycling enthusiasts. This bike route covers a dirt road around 162 km from Barcelonnette to Nice.
  • GR4 Trail - A long and legendary trail that takes hikers through the high peaks and plateaus of the Verdon and Ubaye Valleys.

Best Things to Do in Annot: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of exciting activities to do. Annot has a wide range of activities when it comes to livening up your stay. Here are some of our recommendations.

  • Go rock climbing at Grès d’Annot with a professional climbing guide to lead you.
  • Take a horseback ride in the surrounding countryside, accompanied by a local guide, who will show you the best spots for panoramic views.
  • Visit the weekly farmers market to buy fresh and local products, ranging from cheese, honey, fruits, and vegetables, to meat and seafood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Annot

To experience the best that Annot has to offer, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Annot’s historical and cultural sites. Day 2: Go on a scenic hike to Grès d’Annot and the countryside. Day 3: Take a half-day trip to Gorges du Verdon for kayaking or lazing by the river. Day 4: Take a day trip to Nice, located only a two-hour drive away, and experience the French Riviera. Day 5: Visit Col d’Allos for breathtaking panoramic views of the region. Day 6: Participate in a fun-filled outdoor activity like rock-climbing or horseback-riding. Day 7: Take a stroll through the weekly farmers’ market and indulge in the local charms.

Food and Drink Scene of Annot

Annot is a foodie’s paradise, with a great blend of local and continental cuisine. There are plenty of options for those hoping to taste Annot’s best dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Daube Provençale: Provencal-style beef stew cooked with red wine, herbs, and vegetables.
  • Niçoise salad: A fresh and vibrant summer salad consisting of lettuce, tomatoes, black olives, boiled egg, anchovies, and fresh tuna.

Local Beverages

  • Pastis: A traditional Provencal drink made with water and anise-flavored aperitif.
  • Rosé Wine: Producing excellent rosés!

Where to Stay in Annot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Annot offers a great range of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top recommended places to stay in Annot:

  • L’Escapade - A charming guesthouse with rustic and modern rooms starting from €70 per night.
  • Hôtel Lac et Forêt - a popular hotel located in the heart of the village. The rooms have balconies with stunning mountain views. Prices start from €77 per night.

What to Buy in Annot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Annot, there are some great local products and souvenirs to buy. Don’t forget to take these with you as memories of your trip.

  • Pottery: Annot is famous for pottery made from the rare clay found in the surrounding hills and mountains.
  • Cheese: Taste the unique flavors of locally produced goat and sheep’s cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Annot

Annot has a rich cultural heritage, with unique traditions and customs. Here are some interesting facts about Annot’s local culture:

  • Annot’s people are proud of their heritage, and they hold cultural events throughout the year, including village fairs, live music, and dance shows.
  • The village is well-known for its craftsmen who produce unique wooden items such as toys, utensils, and jewelry.

Best Time to Visit Annot

The best time to visit Annot is from May to October when the weather is at its best, and the natural landscape is in full bloom. However, keep in mind that July and August can be busy with a lot of tourists visiting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Annot

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Annot, follow these handy tips:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Take a stroll along the old streets and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.
  • Do try the local wines and dishes, and participate in cultural events if possible.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activities that harm the natural environment.
  • Disrespect any religious monuments, traditions, or people

Safety and Health

Annot is a safe and healthy place to visit. However, some precautions you should know include:

Safety in Annot

  • Keep an eye on your belongings when traveling and avoid flaunting your valuables around too much.
  • If going rock climbing, ensure to follow the necessary safety rules, and go through a licensed guide.

Health in Annot

  • The local water is generally safe to drink.
  • Bring sunscreen and wear hats and sunglasses during sunny days.

Getting to Annot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Annot is well-connected to other French cities via train, bus, and road. Here are some transportation options you can look into:

Airports Near Annot

  • Nice Côte d’Azur Airport (99 km)
  • Marseille Provence Airport (134 km)

Bus Stations Near Annot

  • Nice Bus Station
  • Marseille Bus Station

Other Options

  • High-speed trains like TGV from Paris connect to the nearest stations, Digne or Saint-André-les-Alpes.

How to Get Around in Annot

To explore Annot, you can either walk through the village, rent a bike or hire a car.

Where to Go Next from Annot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can take when visiting Annot:

  • Moustiers-Sainte-Marie: A charming village known for its pottery and situated about an hour away from Annot.
  • Gorges du Daluis: A stunning canyon with characteristic red rock formations situated about 30 km from Annot.

Conclusion

Annot is a beautiful, serene village with cultural, natural, and gastronomic attractions that will enchant any visitor. With this travel guide, you now have all the information needed to plan an unforgettable trip to Annot. Come and explore one of France’s hidden gems for a rich and unique experience.
