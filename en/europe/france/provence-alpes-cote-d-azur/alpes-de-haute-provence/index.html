A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Alpes-de-Haute-Provence, France

Nestled in the heart of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur in France, Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is a beautiful region with an abundance of natural and cultural attractions. This guide will take you through the best things to do and see in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to get around.

Introduction to Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

With an area of 6,925 square kilometers, Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is a landlocked region located in the southeastern part of France. It shares its borders with Italy, Hautes-Alpes, Var, and Vaucluse. The region is a perfect blend of breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and adventure activities.

List of Places to Visit in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

There’s no shortage of fabulous destinations to visit in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence. Below are just a few of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sisteron Citadel: a military fortification built in the 14th century that overlooks the town of Sisteron.
  • Musée Gassendi: an art museum that features a permanent collection of works by prominent artists, including Pablo Picasso, Fernand Léger, and Joan Miró.
  • The Prehistoric Museum of Quinson: a museum showcasing the prehistoric life and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gorges du Verdon: a canyon with turquoise blue water and stunning limestone cliffs, known as the “Grand Canyon of Europe.”
  • Luberon Regional Nature Park: a protected natural park filled with lavender fields, olive groves, and vineyards.
  • Mercantour National Park: a vast national park known for its diverse landscapes, including lakes, glaciers, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is a great destination for all levels of hikers and bikers. Some of the best routes include:

  • The GR4 hiking trail: This route takes you on a 650-kilometer journey from the Atlantic Ocean to the Mediterranean Sea, passing through the heart of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence.
  • The Bléone - Vallée de l’Asse: A stunning 70-kilometer route with well-maintained paths and breathtaking views.
  • The Luberon Bike Trail: A 236-kilometer route that takes cyclists through the rolling hills and picturesque villages of Provence.

Best Things to Do in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence: Activities and Tours

  • Visit local markets: Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is known for its vibrant weekly markets, where you can buy fresh produce, cheese, and other local products.
  • Soak up the sun: The region is blessed with an abundance of sunshine, making it perfect for outdoor activities such as swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Get a bird’s eye view of the stunning landscapes of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence by taking a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

Day 1: Start your trip in Sisteron and visit the Citadel and the Notre Dame des Pommiers Church.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the Lavender Fields of Valensole and the charming village of Moustiers-Sainte-Marie.

Day 3: Experience the natural beauty of the Gorges du Verdon with hiking, swimming, and kayaking.

Day 4: Explore the historic town of Digne-les-Bains and visit the Musée Alexandra David-Néel.

Day 5: Visit the picturesque hillside village of Forcalquier and enjoy the panoramic views from the citadel.

Day 6: Discover the beauty of Mercantour National Park and hike to the stunning Lac d’Allos.

Day 7: End your trip in the lavender capital of Sault and take a hot air balloon ride over the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

Alpes-de-Haute-Provence offers a wide variety of delicious local food and drinks. Below are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Daube: A stew made with beef, onions, and red wine.
  • Socca: A thin pancake made with chickpea flour.
  • Tapenade: A spread made with olives, capers, and anchovies.

Local Beverages

  • Rosé Wine: Alpes-de-Haute-Provence boasts some of the best rosé wines in France, including the Côteaux de Pierrevert label.
  • Pastis: A local anise-flavored drink that is popular throughout the region.

Where to Stay in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alpes-de-Haute-Provence has a variety of accommodation options from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Le Mas des Pinèdes: A charming hotel set in lush gardens near the town of Manosque.
  • Bastide de Moustiers: A luxurious hotel with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside near the village of Moustiers-Sainte-Marie.
  • Gîte de Bois Lambert: A cozy bed & breakfast located near the trails of the Mercantour National Park.

What to Buy in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lavender products: Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is famous for its lavender fields, so don’t forget to purchase some lavender soaps, sachets, and oils.
  • Olive oil and Olive-based products: The region produces some of the best olive oil in France, so be sure to sample and bring some home.
  • Faience pottery: A traditional earthenware that is hand-painted and produced locally.

Local Culture and Customs of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

The region is steeped in rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on agriculture and rural life. Some important customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Respect the region’s wildlife and natural habitats.
  • Be mindful of local customs and cultural practices.
  • Always bring appropriate attire when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

The best time to visit Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is between June to September when the weather is warm, and the sun is shining. This is also the time when you’ll find the most outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

Do:

  • Respect the rules of the hiking and biking trails.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Visit local landmarks and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural habitats.
  • Visit religious sites without appropriate attire.
  • Ignore local traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

  • Always lock your rental car doors and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be cautious when swimming in natural water sources.
  • Wear appropriate safety gear when participating in adventure activities.

Health in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, particularly during the warm summer months.
  • Be aware of the risks of sunburn and wear sunscreen.
  • Check for ticks and use insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Alpes-de-Haute-Provence: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

  • Marseille Provence Airport
  • Nice Côte d’Azur Airport

Bus Stations Near Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

  • Manosque Bus Station
  • Digne Bus Station

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a popular and convenient way to get around Alpes-de-Haute-Provence.
  • Taking a train from Paris to Aix-en-Provence and then renting a car is also an option.

How to Get Around in Alpes-de-Haute-Provence

The best way to get around Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is by renting a car or taking a bike. Public transportation is available, but can be limited in some areas.

Where to Go Next from Alpes-de-Haute-Provence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mont Ventoux: A mountain known for its cycling routes and stunning views.
  • Avignon: A historic city in the heart of Provence known for its papal palace and famous bridge.
  • Cannes: A glamorous seaside resort in the south of France known for its film festival and luxury shopping.

Conclusion

Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is a treasure trove of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary delights. Whether you’re a hiker, foodie, or history buff, this region has something to offer everyone. With beautiful landscapes, vibrant markets, and rich cultural heritage, Alpes-de-Haute-Provence is sure to delight every traveler.
