Introduction to Manosque

Located in the heart of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region, Manosque is a charming town known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. From museums and historical places to hiking and biking trails, there are plenty of things to see and do in Manosque. In this tour guide, we will take you on a journey of discovery, highlighting the best places to visit, activities to do, and local food and drinks to try.

List of Places to Visit in Manosque

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Musée Jean Giono

The Musée Jean Giono is a tribute to the famous French writer, Jean Giono, who spent most of his life in Manosque. The museum offers a comprehensive collection of his works, manuscripts, photographs, and personal items.

Église Saint-Sauveur

The Église Saint-Sauveur is a beautiful Romanesque church dating back to the 12th century. The church features stunning stained glass windows and a majestic organ that is worth admiring.

Château de l’Emperi

The Château de l’Emperi is a 13th-century castle that was later used as a barracks and a prison. Today, the castle serves as a museum that showcases the history of Manosque and the surrounding region.

Natural Attractions of Manosque: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mont d’Or

The Mont d’Or is a large hill that offers breathtaking views of the Luberon area. The hill is a great spot for hiking, picnicking, and soaking in the stunning scenery.

Parc de Drouille

The Parc de Drouille is a peaceful oasis in the heart of Manosque. The park features lush greenery, a pond, and walking trails. It’s a great place to unwind and relax.

Les Gorges du Verdon

Les Gorges du Verdon is a natural wonder that should not be missed. The Verdon River flows through the breathtaking gorge, offering opportunities for swimming, kayaking, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manosque

Chemin de la Cerise

The Chemin de la Cerise is a scenic hiking trail that winds through cherry orchards and olive groves. The trail offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside.

Les Mourres

Les Mourres is a unique rock formation that resembles a miniature version of Utah’s Bryce Canyon. The area offers great hiking and rock climbing opportunities.

Véloroute du Calavon

The Véloroute du Calavon is a popular cycling route that stretches from Apt to Cavaillon. The trail features stunning scenery, quaint villages, and plenty of places to stop for a meal or a drink.

Best Things to Do in Manosque: Activities and Tours

Guided Walking Tours

If you want to get the most out of Manosque, take a guided walking tour. Learn about the history, culture, and architecture of the town from a knowledgeable guide.

Lavender Fields Tour

Manosque is famous for its lavender fields, which bloom in the early summer. Take a tour of the fields to enjoy the fragrant blooms and learn about the cultivation of lavender.

Food and Wine Tasting Tours

Provence is known for its delicious food and wine. Take a food and wine tasting tour to sample the best that Manosque and the surrounding area have to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Manosque

Must-Try Dishes

Daube Provençale

Daube Provençale is a hearty beef stew that is slow-cooked with red wine and vegetables. It’s a classic dish that will warm you up on a cold day.

Pissaladière

Pissaladière is a tart made with onions, olives, and anchovies. It’s a great appetizer or snack that pairs well with a glass of local wine.

Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a vegetable stew made with tomatoes, eggplant, zucchini, and peppers. It’s a healthy and flavorful dish that is popular in Provence.

Local Beverages

Pastis

Pastis is the most famous alcoholic beverage in Provence. It’s an anise-flavored drink that is usually served with ice and water.

Rosé Wine

Provence is known for its delicious rosé wine. The region produces some of the best rosés in the world, which pair perfectly with local cuisine.

Lavender Liqueur

Lavender Liqueur is a sweet and fragrant drink that is made from distilled lavender flowers. It’s a great way to end a meal in Manosque.

Where to Stay in Manosque: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Les Mées

Hotel Les Mées is a charming hotel located in the heart of Manosque. The hotel features comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant that serves delicious local cuisine.

La Bonne Étape

La Bonne Étape is a luxury hotel located in the countryside outside of Manosque. The hotel features elegant rooms, a gourmet restaurant, and a spa that offers a range of treatments.

La Parenthèse

La Parenthèse is a cozy guesthouse located in a renovated farmhouse. The guesthouse features comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Manosque: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lavender Products

Manosque is famous for its lavender products, including essential oils, soaps, and perfumes. Bring home some lavender goodies as a souvenir of your trip.

Olives

Provence is known for its delicious olives, which come in a variety of flavors and sizes. Pick up a jar of olives to enjoy at home.

Pottery

Manosque is home to many talented potters who create beautiful pieces of pottery. Visit a local pottery shop to find a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Manosque

Manosque is a friendly town where locals take pride in their cultural heritage. Respect the customs and traditions of the town, including the siesta (a mid-day break) and the importance of family.

Best Time to Visit Manosque

The best time to visit Manosque is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. The lavender fields bloom in June and July, which is a particularly beautiful time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manosque

Do:

  • Do take a guided walking tour to learn about the history and culture of the town.
  • Do try local dishes such as daube Provençale and pissaladière.
  • Do visit the Lavender Fields and Les Gorges du Verdon for stunning natural beauty.
  • Do respect the customs and traditions of the town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to take a siesta in the middle of the day when many shops and attractions are closed.
  • Don’t leave without trying local wines and pastis.
  • Don’t forget to bring a hat and sunscreen if you plan to visit during the summer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manosque

Manosque is a safe town, but it’s always a good idea to be cautious when traveling. Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Manosque

Manosque has good quality medical facilities and pharmacies. If you need medical attention, go to the nearest hospital or emergency room.

Getting to Manosque: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manosque

The closest airport to Manosque is Marseille-Provence Airport, which is located about 1 hour and 20 minutes away by car. Other nearby airports include Nice Côte d’Azur Airport and Avignon-Provence Airport.

Bus Stations Near Manosque

Manosque has a bus station that serves regional destinations. Bus companies such as LER and Lignes Express Régionales offer connections to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Manosque is located on the A51 motorway, which connects the town to other parts of France. There are also trains that serve the town, including the TER Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur.

How to Get Around in Manosque

The best way to get around Manosque is on foot. The town center is compact and easy to navigate. If you want to explore the surrounding countryside, consider renting a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from Manosque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aix-en-Provence

Aix-en-Provence is a beautiful city located about 1 hour and 15 minutes away by car from Manosque. The city is known for its stunning architecture, cultural events, and food scene.

Avignon

Avignon is a historic city that is famous for its papal palace and bridge. The city is located about 1 hour and 20 minutes away by car from Manosque.

Marseille

Marseille is a vibrant coastal city that is known for its lively port, beaches, and food scene. The city is located about 1 hour and 30 minutes away by car from Manosque.

Conclusion

Manosque is a hidden gem in the heart of Provence that is full of surprises. From its rich culture and history to its stunning natural beauty, there is something for everyone in this charming town. Use this tour guide to plan your next trip to Manosque and discover all that this town has to offer.
