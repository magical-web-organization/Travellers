Introduction to Île du Levant

Île du Levant, located in the Var department of the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region of France, is a small but stunning island with a population of just over 100 people. The island is a part of the Hyères archipelago and is known for its picturesque beaches, diverse flora and fauna, and naturist lifestyle. It’s a perfect location if you’re looking for a quiet, peaceful, and relaxing vacation with a hint of adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Île du Levant

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Despite its small size, Île du Levant has a rich history, and there are a few historical places and museums that you can visit while you’re here.

Forte de l’Hélice: Built-in the 19th century, Forte de l’Hélice is a military fort located on the eastern side of the island. It’s one of the few surviving structures from the Second World War. You can take a guided tour of the fort and learn about its history and significance.

Chapelle Saint-Anne: The Chapelle Saint-Anne is a small chapel located in the center of the island. It was built in the 12th century and is one of the oldest buildings on the island. The chapel features beautiful architecture and stunning frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Île du Levant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Île du Levant is home to some of the most beautiful and breathtaking natural attractions in France. The island has a diverse landscape that ranges from hillsides with olive and pine trees to gorgeous coastline with beaches and rocky cliffs. Here are some natural attractions you must explore when visiting Île du Levant.

Plage des Grottes: Plage des Grottes is a small, isolated beach located on the eastern side of the island. The beach is perfect for swimming and sunbathing, and the crystal clear water offers excellent visibility for snorkeling.

Fort de la Vigie: Fort de la Vigie is a military fort located on a cliff overlooking the sea. It provides an excellent view of the island and is a great spot for watching the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Île du Levant

Île du Levant has numerous hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are two trails that you should explore while on the island.

Sentier du Littoral: Sentier du Littoral is a walking trail that runs along the coast of the island. The trail offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, rocky cliffs, and picturesque coves.

La Boucle des Trois Forts: La Boucle des Trois Forts is a circular hiking trail that takes you past three military forts on the island: Fort de la Vigie, Fort de l’Hélice, and Fort de Balaguier. The trail offers an excellent opportunity to explore the island’s history and enjoy its scenic wonders.

Best Things to Do in Île du Levant: Activities and Tours

Île du Levant offers a unique range of activities, from relaxing naturist beaches to more active adventures like hiking and kayaking. Here are some of the best things to do on the island.

Naturist Beaches: Île du Levant is known for its naturist beaches. There are several beaches on the island where clothing is optional, including Plage des Grottes, Plage de la Fourmigue, and Plage d’Argent.

Kayaking: Kayaking is a popular activity on the island, and you can rent a kayak and explore the beautiful coastline of Île du Levant.

Week-Long Itinerary for Île du Levant

If you have a week to spare and want to explore everything the island has to offer, here’s a sample itinerary for you.

Day 1-2: Explore the island’s history and culture by visiting Forte de l’Hélice, Chapelle Saint-Anne, and Fort de la Vigie.

Day 3-4: Spend time on the island’s naturist beaches and relax under the Mediterranean sun.

Day 5-6: Go hiking or kayaking and take in the natural beauty of the island.

Day 7: Enjoy a delicious meal at one of the island’s restaurants and buy some souvenirs to take back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Île du Levant

When it comes to food and drink, Île du Levant offers a unique array of options, including traditional French cuisine and local specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

Soupe au Pistou: Soupe au Pistou is a traditional French soup made with a variety of vegetables, pasta, and pesto. It’s a perfect comfort food and tastes even better when eaten with fresh bread.

Bouillabaisse: Bouillabaisse is a traditional Provencal fish stew made with various types of fish, shellfish, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Vin de Porquerolles: Vin de Porquerolles is a local wine that’s produced on the nearby island of Porquerolles. It’s a dry and fruity white wine that pairs perfectly with seafood.

Pastis: Pastis is a popular anise-flavored liquor that’s commonly consumed as an aperitif in Provence.

Where to Stay in Île du Levant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Île du Levant has several accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay on the island.

Hôtel Méditerranée: Hôtel Méditerranée is a 3-star hotel located on the eastern side of the island. The hotel offers comfortable rooms with beautiful views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.

Les Galeries: Les Galeries is a guesthouse located in the center of the island. The guesthouse is known for its warm hospitality and homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Île du Levant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, here are some of the best options.

Olive Oil: Île du Levant is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is produced using the island’s abundant olive groves.

Handmade Soap: Île du Levant has several boutique shops that sell handmade soaps crafted from natural ingredients like olive oil, lavender, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Île du Levant

Île du Levant is known for its naturist lifestyle, and visitors are expected to respect this culture and dress accordingly. The island has a relaxed and laid-back atmosphere, and people take their time to enjoy the simple pleasures of life.

Best Time to Visit Île du Levant

The best time to visit Île du Levant is between May and September when the weather is warm and dry. During this time, you can enjoy the island’s beaches and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Île du Levant

Do:

  • Respect the naturist culture of the island.
  • Take time to explore the island’s history and natural beauty.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and wine.
  • Buy souvenirs and local products to take back home.

Don’t:

  • Wear clothes in the naturist areas.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Ignore the island’s rich history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Île du Levant

Île du Levant is a safe destination, with a relatively low crime rate. However, visitors should be mindful of their belongings and take necessary precautions to avoid theft.

Health in Île du Levant

There are no major health risks on Île du Levant, and visitors are not required to take any specific vaccines or medications.

Getting to Île du Levant: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Île du Levant

The nearest airport to Île du Levant is Toulon-Hyères Airport, which is located on the mainland, about 20 miles away from the island. From the airport, you can take a ferry to the island.

Bus Stations Near Île du Levant

There are no bus stations on Île du Levant. However, you can take a bus to the nearby town of Hyères and then take a ferry to the island.

Other Options

You can also arrive in Île du Levant by taking a train to the nearby town of Le Lavandou and then taking a ferry to the island.

How to Get Around in Île du Levant

The best way to explore Île du Levant is on foot or by bike. You can also rent a scooter if you want to explore the island more quickly and efficiently.

Where to Go Next from Île du Levant: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that you can visit from Île du Levant, including the following:

  • Porquerolles: Porquerolles is a nearby island known for its stunning beaches and unspoiled nature.

  • Toulon: Toulon is a seaside town located on the mainland and has a rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Île du Levant is a hidden gem in the south of France that’s worth exploring. The island’s picture-perfect beaches, rich history, and unique culture make it an excellent destination for travelers looking for a relaxing and peaceful vacation.
