Introduction to Saint-Tropez

Located in the Var department of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, Saint-Tropez is a popular coastal town in southeastern France. Known for its glamorous lifestyle and vibrant summertime atmosphere, Saint-Tropez has a rich history dating back to the Roman times. The town has been a favorite destination for artists and intellectuals such as Picasso and Colette, and today attracts tourists from all over the world.

Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation on the beach or an adventurous exploration of the outdoors, Saint-Tropez has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to this stunning destination.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Tropez

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de l’Annonciade: A museum dedicated to modern art, with works by famous artists such as Matisse, Derain, and Signac.

  • Citadelle de Saint-Tropez: A fortress built in the 17th century to protect the town from pirates and invaders. Today, it houses a maritime museum with exhibits on the history of Saint-Tropez.

  • Chapelle Sainte-Anne: A beautiful chapel built in the 16th century, located on a hill overlooking the town.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Tropez: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage de Pampelonne: A stunning 5km long sandy beach with crystal-clear waters, lined with beach clubs and restaurants.

  • Saint-Tropez Beaches: Other than Pampelonne, several beaches in Saint-Tropez are worth a visit including Plage de la Bouillabaisse, Plage des Graniers, Plages des Salins, and plage de la Moutte.

  • Foret des Maures: A large forested area with several hiking trails, beautiful views, and picnic spots.

  • Sentier du Littoral: A coastal trail that offers some of the most stunning views of Saint-Tropez.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Tropez

  • L’Estérel: A natural park located in the mountains just north of Saint-Tropez, with stunning views and various hiking and biking routes.

  • Le Massif des Maures: A mountain range south of Saint-Tropez with several trails leading to picturesque villages and panoramic views.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Tropez: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour: Experience the stunning coastline of Saint-Tropez on a boat tour or rent your own boat for a personalized tour of the beaches and neighboring islands.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Provence is known for its wines, and Saint-Tropez has several vineyards that offer tours and tastings of their wines.

  • Water Sports: From paddleboarding to jet skiing, there are plenty of water sports activities available in Saint-Tropez.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Tropez

Day 1: Explore the town and visit the Citadelle de Saint-Tropez and Chapelle Sainte-Anne.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the coastal area and visit some of the beaches.

Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking tour in L’Estérel or Le Massif des Maures.

Day 4: Visit the Musée de l’Annonciade and explore the town’s art scene.

Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour of the Provence vineyards.

Day 6: Enjoy some water sports activities on the beach.

Day 7: Indulge in the local cuisine and enjoy the nightlife scene in Saint-Tropez.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Tropez

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bouillabaisse: A traditional seafood stew made with fish and shellfish.

  • Tarte Tropézienne: A pastry filled with cream and flavored with orange blossom water.

  • Salade Niçoise: A salad made with tuna, tomatoes, olives, and anchovies.

Local Beverages

  • Rosé wine: Provence is known for its high-quality rosé wines, which are a perfect accompaniment to a sunny day on the beach.

  • Pastis: An anise-flavored spirit enjoyed as an aperitif.

  • Citron Pressé: A refreshing drink made with fresh lemon juice, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Saint-Tropez: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel Byblos Saint-Tropez: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Saint-Tropez, with a spa, pool, and several dining options.

  • La Ponche: A boutique hotel located in a historic building in the old town of Saint-Tropez, with stunning views of the harbor.

  • Le Mouillage: A guesthouse located on a hill overlooking the town, with a pool and beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Saint-Tropez: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Provençal fabrics: Provence is known for its colorful fabrics, which make for great souvenirs or gifts.

  • Santons: Small figurines made of clay that represent traditional Provencal characters.

  • Olive Oil: Provence is known for its high-quality olive oil, which makes for a great gift or souvenir to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Tropez

Saint-Tropez is known for its glamorous lifestyle, but it also has a rich history and traditional culture. The town is known for its many festivals and events, such as the Les Voiles de Saint-Tropez sailing regatta, the Fête de la Saint-Pierre fisherman’s festival, and the Fête du Château firework display. The locals speak French, but many also speak English, especially in the tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Tropez

The best time to visit Saint-Tropez is in the summer months of June to September, when the weather is sunny and warm, and the town is buzzing with tourists and activities. However, this also means that it can be crowded and expensive. If you prefer a quieter and more peaceful vibe, visit in the offseason from October to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Tropez

Do:

  • Do dress up for a night out, especially in the more upscale bars and restaurants.

  • Do take a boat tour of the coastline to see the beautiful beaches and islands.

  • Do try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and a hat, as the sun can be intense during the summer.

  • Don’t expect to find budget accommodations, as Saint-Tropez is known for its luxury hotels and villas.

  • Don’t forget to respect the local culture and customs, such as dressing appropriately when entering churches or chapels.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Tropez

Saint-Tropez is generally a safe destination, but as with any tourist destination, you should take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings from pickpockets and other petty crimes. Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and in the evening.

Health in Saint-Tropez

Saint-Tropez has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and pharmacies. It is also recommended to purchase travel insurance before your trip in case of any unexpected illnesses or accidents.

Getting to Saint-Tropez: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Tropez

The closest airport to Saint-Tropez is Nice Côte d’Azur Airport, which is about 90km away. From here, you can rent a car, take a taxi, or catch a bus to Saint-Tropez.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Tropez

There are several bus stations in Saint-Tropez, with regular bus services connecting the town to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

You can also reach Saint-Tropez by train, with the closest train station being Gare de Saint-Raphaël-Valescure, which is about 40km away.

How to Get Around in Saint-Tropez

The best way to get around Saint-Tropez is on foot, as the town is relatively small and easy to navigate. Alternatively, you can rent a bicycle or scooter to explore the surrounding areas and beaches. Taxis are also available, but they can be quite expensive.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Tropez: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cannes: A coastal town about an hour’s drive from Saint-Tropez, known for its film festival and high-end shopping.

  • Aix-en-Provence: A beautiful city about 2 hours from Saint-Tropez, known for its charming old town and rich cultural heritage.

  • Nice: A vibrant city on the French Riviera, about 1.5 hours from Saint-Tropez, known for its stunning architecture, museums, and beaches.

Conclusion

Saint-Tropez is a stunning destination with a rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful scenery. Whether you are looking for a relaxing beach vacation, an adventurous outdoor exploration, or a cultural exploration of the town’s art scene and history, Saint-Tropez has something for everyone. Plan your trip using this comprehensive guide and enjoy all that this stunning destination has to offer.
