Introduction to Camargue

Located in the south of France, Camargue is a unique region that boasts a natural and cultural heritage worth exploring. It is a wetland area in Bouches-du-Rhône, in Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, where the Rhône River reaches the Mediterranean Sea. It is home to diverse landscapes, from salt marshes and lagoons to beaches and green plains. What characterizes Camargue the most is, without a doubt, its emblematic white horses, black bulls, and pink flamingos.

In this travel guide, you will discover the highlights of Camargue and plan your trip accordingly. From natural reserves and cultural landmarks to outdoor activities and local cuisine, Camargue has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Camargue

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Camargue has a rich cultural heritage and a past marked by its occupation by different civilizations. You will find ancient cities, Roman ruins, and museums dedicated to local traditions and crafts.

Arles Amphitheatre

Arles, located at the northern border of Camargue, is a city that used to be a significant commercial center during the Roman era. The Arles Amphitheatre is a preserved arena that hosted gladiatorial games. It has two levels of arches and can host up to 25,000 spectators.

Ancient City of Glanum

Glanum is an archeological site near the town of Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, at the east end of Camargue. It was a Hellenistic and Roman city founded in the 3rd century BC and destroyed during the Middle Ages. You can discover the ruins of public and private buildings and the sanctuary dedicated to the Celtic god Glanis.

Camargue Museum

The Camargue Museum, located in the town of Arles, presents the natural and cultural heritage of the region. You will learn about the salt industry, traditional crafts, and the Camargue lifestyle. It also hosts temporary art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Camargue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Camargue is renowned for its exceptional natural landscapes made of lagoons, beaches, and reserves. Nature lovers can’t miss the following natural attractions:

Parc Naturel Régional de Camargue

The Parc Naturel Régional de Camargue is a natural reserve spreading over 150,000 hectares. It is the perfect place for birdwatching, horse riding, and hiking. In the park, you can see wild bulls, horses, and flamingos living in their natural habitat. You can also enjoy the view of the Mediterranean Sea and the Rhône River.

Salins du Midi

The Salins du Midi are salt marshes in the heart of Camargue. Since the Middle Ages, salt production has been an essential activity in the region. You can take a guided tour and learn about the natural processes of salt production. You can also buy local salt and other regional products at the shop.

Plage de Piémanson

The Plage de Piémanson is a wild and sandy beach in the east of Camargue. It is a popular spot for camping and kitesurfing. You can enjoy the sea and the sun while admiring the view of the natural dunes that protect the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Camargue

Camargue is an ideal place for cycling and hiking enthusiasts. You can rent bikes or join a guided excursion, and discover the area on two wheels. You will find unpaved roads and trails that are perfect for a peaceful ride, surrounded by the sounds and colors of nature.

La ViaRhôna

La ViaRhôna is a cycle path along the Rhône River that crosses Camargue. It is 815 km long and connects Lake Geneva in Switzerland to the Mediterranean Sea, passing through France. You can enjoy the scenery of the river and the Camargue landscapes while cycling.

GR 653D

The GR 653D is a walking trail that follows the footsteps of pilgrims on their way to Santiago de Compostela in Spain. It crosses Camargue from Arles to Saint-Gilles and is 30 km long. You will discover the Roman monuments and the pilgrimage churches along the way.

Best Things to Do in Camargue: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the cultural landmarks and natural reserves, Camargue offers exciting activities and tours for all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things you can do in the region:

Horseback Riding

Horseback riding is a popular activity in Camargue, where the white horses are part of the local tradition. You can go for a guided tour to discover the natural reserves and the marshes, accompanied by a passionate guide who will explain the history and customs of the region.

Bulls and Cowboys Show

Camargue is also famous for the bullfighting events and the working cowboys who raise the bulls and horses. You can attend a show that combines acrobatics, music, and dance, and learn more about the Camargue folklore.

Boat Tour

You can take a boat tour and navigate the lagoons and canals of Camargue. You will admire the wildlife and the landscapes from a different perspective and learn more about the local ecosystems.

Food and Drink Scene of Camargue

In Camargue, you will taste the flavors of the Mediterranean cuisine combined with the local ingredients and traditions. You can’t leave the region without trying:

Must-Try Dishes

Gardiane de Taureau

The Gardiane de Taureau is a traditional stew made of bull meat, onions, and red wine. It is cooked slowly for several hours in a red earthenware dish and served with fresh pasta or rice.

Fougasse

The Fougasse is a savory bread that comes in different varieties in Camargue. It can be plain or stuffed with cheese, olives, or bacon. It is a typical snack or starter that you can find in bakeries and markets.

Local Beverages

Vin des Sables

The Vin des Sables is a wine made of white or grey grapes grown in the sand dunes of Camargue. It has a mineral flavor and pairs well with seafood. You can taste it in local cellar doors or restaurants.

Pastis

Pastis is an anise-flavored liquor that is popular in the south of France. It is usually served as an aperitif, diluted with water, and accompanied by olives or almonds. It is best enjoyed in the shade of a terrace, facing the sea.

Where to Stay in Camargue: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Camargue offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to hostel rooms. You can choose to stay in:

Hôtel L’Estelle en Camargue

The Hôtel L’Estelle en Camargue is a 5-star hotel located in the town of Saintes-Maries-de-la-Mer, near the beaches and the natural reserves. It has a spa, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool. The rooms have a private balcony or terrace with a view of the garden or the sea.

Auberge Cavalière du Pont des Bannes

The Auberge Cavalière du Pont des Bannes is a 4-star hotel located in the middle of the natural reserve of Camargue. It offers horse riding and boat tours, as well as a restaurant and a bar.

La Maison d’Isabelle

La Maison d’Isabelle is a guesthouse located in the town of Arles, near the cultural landmarks and the restaurants. It has a garden and a terrace, and the rooms are decorated with a mix of antique and contemporary furniture.

What to Buy in Camargue: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring a piece of Camargue back home, you can choose among the following souvenirs and local products:

Salt

Camargue salt is a natural product that is harvested manually in the salt marshes. It is a delicate and flavorful salt that you can use in your cooking or as a decoration on your table. You can find it in the Salins du Midi shop or in local markets.

Gardiane de Taureau

You can also buy a jar of Gardiane de Taureau as a souvenir or a gift. It is a traditional dish that can be stored for several months if kept in a cool and dry place. You can find it in local delicatessen shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Camargue

Camargue has a unique culture and customs that have been preserved for centuries. One of the most iconic traditions is the bullfighting events, which are held throughout the year and attract many visitors. In Camargue, the bullfighting is not about killing the bull, but about taming it and earning its respect. The bullfighters, called “razeteurs,” wear special clothes and use a hook called “crochet” to snatch the ribbons tied to the bull’s horns.

Another important aspect of the Camargue culture is the music and dance. The “Sianso,” a Provencal language song, is a popular tune that accompanies the local festivals and gatherings.

Best Time to Visit Camargue

The best time to visit Camargue depends on your preferences and interests. If you want to see the flamingos and the countryside in bloom, spring is the ideal season, from March to May. If you prefer the beach and the outdoor activities, summer is the busiest time, from June to August. If you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy the mild weather, autumn is a good option, from September to November. Winter is not recommended as many attractions are closed, and the weather can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Camargue

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and the natural environment
  • Try the local cuisine and buy regional products
  • Greet the locals with a “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir”
  • Learn some basic French expressions

Don’t:

  • Approach the bulls or the horses without a professional guide
  • Take pictures of the gypsies without their permission
  • Swim in the natural reserves or the canals
  • Leave trash in the nature

Safety and Health

Safety in Camargue

Camargue is a safe region to visit, but you should take some precautions to avoid accidents and thefts. You should:

  • Wear cream and a hat to protect yourself from the sun
  • Follow the safety instructions during the outdoor activities and the shows
  • Lock your valuables in a safe or bring them with you
  • Be aware of pickpockets in the crowded areas

Health in Camargue

Camargue has a good health system, and you can find pharmacies and hospitals in the major towns. You should take care of your health by:

  • Drinking bottled water or tap water that has been boiled
  • Wearing appropriate clothing and avoiding overheating
  • Protecting yourself from the mosquito bites, especially in the summer

Getting to Camargue: Transportation Options and Logistics

Camargue is accessible by different means of transportation, depending on your starting point and your preferences:

Airports Near Camargue

The closest airports to Camargue are:

  • Marseille-Provence airport, at 90 km
  • Montpellier-Méditerranée airport, at 54 km

From the airports, you can take a bus or rent a car to reach Camargue.

Bus Stations Near Camargue

The main bus stations in Camargue are:

  • Arles bus station, with connections to Marseille and Avignon
  • Saintes-Maries-de-la-Mer bus station, with connections to Montpellier and Nîmes

Other Options

You can also reach Camargue by train, with stations in:

  • Arles, served by SNCF
  • Nîmes, served by SNCF and TGV
  • Montpellier, served by SNCF and TGV

How to Get Around in Camargue

The best way to get around in Camargue is by car, as the public transportation can be limited. You can rent a car at the airports or the major towns. If you prefer a more ecological way of transportation, you can rent a bike and explore the area on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Camargue: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip and discover other places in the south of France, you can visit:

Arles

Arles is a city located at the northern border of Camargue. It is known for its Roman ruins, Gothic and Romanesque monuments, and Van Gogh’s paintings. You can walk in the old town and discover the hidden courtyards and squares.

Aigues-Mortes

Aigues-Mortes is a fortified city located at the northwest of Camargue. It has preserved its medieval walls and towers and is famous for its salt marshes and pink salt. You can take a tour of the ramparts and taste the local products.

Marseille

Marseille is a vibrant city located at 130 km from Camargue. It is the second-largest city in France and a cultural and economic hub. You can walk in the old port, discover the Cité Radieuse, designed by Le Corbusier, and taste the iconic bouillabaisse.

Conclusion

Camargue is a unique and beautiful region of France that deserves to be discovered. From the natural reserves and the beaches to the cultural landmarks and the local traditions, Camargue offers a rich and diverse travel experience. Plan your trip well, and you will make unforgettable memories.
