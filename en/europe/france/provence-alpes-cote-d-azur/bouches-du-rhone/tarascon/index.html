Introduction to Tarascon

Located in the scenic region of Bouches-du-Rhône, in the heart of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, Tarascon is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This quaint yet vibrant town is nestled between the Rhône River and the Alpilles mountain range, offering stunning natural vistas and rich cultural experiences. This ancient Roman city boasts impressive historical landmarks and charming streets lined with ancient buildings and contemporary cafes.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, foodie, or adventure enthusiast, Tarascon has something for every traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and drink, local customs, transportation options, and nearby destinations. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Tarascon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Château de Tarascon

The stunning Château de Tarascon, overlooking the Rhône River, is a highlight of any trip to Tarascon. This 15th-century fortress was built to protect the town and was later used as a prison, salt store, and military barracks. Today, visitors can explore the castle’s expansive halls, towers, and courtyards and discover its fascinating history.

Les Frères Gaussen Museum

Step back in time and discover the rich cultural heritage of Tarascon at Les Frères Gaussen Museum. This small yet impressive museum showcases traditional Provençal life, including costumes, textiles, pottery, and furniture. The museum’s well-curated exhibits provide valuable insights into the local history and customs of Tarascon.

St. Martha’s Collegiate Church

The St. Martha’s Collegiate Church is a stunningly beautiful Gothic church located on the outskirts of Tarascon and well worth a visit. The church is dedicated to St. Martha, who is said to have tamed the Tarasque, a mythological monster that once roamed the town. Visitors can admire the church’s intricate stained glass windows and ornate decor, including a beautiful altarpiece.

Natural Attractions of Tarascon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Les Alpilles

The Alpilles mountain range provides a stunning backdrop to Tarascon, offering a range of outdoor activities in a spectacular natural setting. Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore miles of scenic trails, while rock climbers can test their skills on the rugged cliffs. The Alpilles also offers a wealth of flora and fauna, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers.

Rhone River Cruise

A relaxing way to discover Tarascon is by taking a Rhône River cruise. Visitors can soak up the town’s highlights from a unique angle and enjoy picturesque views of the surrounding countryside. Many boats offer lunch or dinner cruises, which are a unique way to experience the region’s cuisine while admiring the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarascon

Tarascon boasts an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the town and its surroundings. The trails range from easy to challenging, making them ideal for all fitness levels. Some popular routes include the Alpilles Massif hike, the Réservé des Granettes hike, and the Tarascon industrial heritage bike route.

Best Things to Do in Tarascon: Activities and Tours

Tarascon Walking Tour

A guided walking tour is an ideal way to discover Tarascon’s charming streets, ancient buildings, and local highlights. Visitors can choose from several tours, including the “Tarascon Tourist Train” and the “Tarascon Guided Walking Tour,” both of which provide valuable insights into the town’s history and culture.

Provence Wine Tour

No trip to Tarascon is complete without exploring the region’s rich wine culture. The Provence Wine Tour is a must-do activity that allows visitors to explore local vineyards, sample the region’s wines, and learn about the wine-making process. This tour provides an excellent opportunity to taste some of the finest wines in the region and appreciate the local wine culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarascon

Must-Try Dishes

Bouillabaisse

This hearty fish stew is a local specialty in Tarascon and is made with a variety of fresh fish, vegetables, and herbs. Bouillabaisse is best enjoyed with crusty bread and a glass of local wine.

Ratatouille

Another classic Provençal dish, ratatouille, is a flavorful vegetable stew made with eggplant, zucchini, peppers, and tomatoes. This dish is often served as an accompaniment to grilled meats and fish.

Local Beverages

Côtes du Rhône Wine

The Côtes du Rhône wine region is renowned for its rich, fruity, and full-bodied red wines. Some of the best vineyards in the region can be found near Tarascon, making it an ideal place to taste and explore the local wine culture.

Pastis

A popular anise-flavored liqueur, Pastis, is often served as an aperitif in Tarascon. This refreshing drink is best enjoyed on a hot summer day while relaxing at a local café.

Where to Stay in Tarascon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarascon offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse, a charming boutique hotel, or a modern hostel, you’ll find plenty of options in town. Some popular options include the Hôtel Le Terminus, Hôtel de Provence, and the Auberge du Mas d’Alivon.

What to Buy in Tarascon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tarascon offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home. Some popular items include traditional crafts, pottery, textiles, and olive oil. The town’s markets and boutiques are a great place to find these one-of-a-kind gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarascon

Tarascon is a town steeped in tradition and local customs, many of which have been passed down through generations. The town’s annual “Fête de la Tarasque” is a highlight of the local calendar and celebrates the legend of St. Martha and the Tarasque. During this festival, the town’s streets are alive with parades, music, and revelry, making it an ideal time to visit.

Best Time to Visit Tarascon

The best time to visit Tarascon is during the spring or fall months when the weather is mild, and the town is less crowded. In the summer months, the town can get quite busy with tourists, and temperatures can soar. However, the summer months also offer a range of outdoor activities, including festivals and concerts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarascon

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including the fish stew Bouillabaisse and the anise-flavored drink Pastis.
  • Do explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, including the Château de Tarascon and St. Martha’s Collegiate Church.
  • Do take a hiking or biking tour of the Alpilles mountain range for stunning natural vistas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the local customs and traditions of Tarascon.
  • Don’t touch or climb on any ancient monuments or buildings.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water when hiking or biking in the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarascon

Tarascon is a relatively safe town, and visitors should not encounter any major safety issues. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions, such as staying aware of your surroundings and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Tarascon

Medical facilities in Tarascon are generally good quality, and visitors can access a range of healthcare services if needed. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Tarascon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarascon is easily accessible by a range of transportation options, including planes, trains, buses, and cars.

Airports Near Tarascon

The nearest airport to Tarascon is Marseille-Provence Airport, located approximately 60 km away. From the airport, visitors can hire a car, take a taxi, or catch a bus to Tarascon.

Bus Stations Near Tarascon

Tarascon has a bus station that provides regular services to nearby towns and cities, including Avignon and Nîmes.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Tarascon by train, with the town’s main train station offering services to major cities and towns in the region, including Marseille and Nice.

How to Get Around in Tarascon

Tarascon is a small town that is easily navigable on foot. Visitors can also hire bikes or scooters to explore the town and its surroundings at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Tarascon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tarascon is ideally situated for exploring the surrounding region, with many nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Some popular options include the Pont du Gard, the Palais des Papes Palace, and the Les Baux-de-Provence castle ruins.

Conclusion

Tarascon is a hidden gem in the heart of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur, offering a range of cultural, natural, and gastronomic experiences. This comprehensive guide has explored the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and drink, local customs, transportation options, and nearby destinations. With so much to see and do in Tarascon, it’s sure to be an unforgettable trip for every traveler.
