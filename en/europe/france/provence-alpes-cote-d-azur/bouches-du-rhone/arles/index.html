Introduction to Arles

Arles, a city located in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur region of France, is a must-visit destination for travellers who want to explore the country’s history and culture. Popular for its Roman and medieval heritage, Arles has been recognised as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is also an important place for art lovers, who can discover the works of renowned painters such as Vincent Van Gogh, who lived and worked here. This guide is aimed at helping you explore the hidden gems of this vibrant city.

List of Places to Visit in Arles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amphitheatre of Arles - A well-preserved Roman amphitheater which is still used for events today.
  • Alyscamps - The city’s ancient Roman necropolis, still home to many well-preserved sarcophagi.
  • Musée Reattu - A museum housing a collection of paintings, including works by Picasso.
  • Musée de l’Arles Antique - A museum featuring a collection of ancient Roman artifacts and models.
  • The Cryptoporticus - A subterranean structure from ancient Roman times.

Natural Attractions of Arles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Camargue - A stunning nature reserve famous for its pink flamingos, wild horses, and abundance of bird species.
  • Les Baux-de-Provence - A picturesque medieval village situated on a rocky plateau
  • The Alpilles Natural Regional Park - A small range of hills, perfect for hiking and exploring the great outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arles

  • The Chemin des Conques - A beautiful hiking trail that leads to the hilltop village of Les Baux-de-Provence.
  • The Montagnette - A limestone massif, rich in fauna and flora, perfect for hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Arles: Activities and Tours

  • Van Gogh Trail - Follow in the footsteps of Vincent Van Gogh and see the places where he lived, worked and found inspiration.
  • Horse Riding Tours - Explore the Camargue on horseback with an experienced guide.
  • Wine Tour - Experience the best of Provence’s vineyards and taste the region’s famous wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Arles

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gardianne de Taureau - A traditional stew made from bull meat and fennel.
  • Ratatouille - A stew made with aubergines, peppers and courgettes.
  • Salad Camarguaise - A salad made with tuna, tomatoes, onions, and peppers, often served with a boiled egg on top.

Local Beverages

  • Pastis - An anise-flavoured liqueur traditional to the region.
  • Côtes du Rhône wine - A popular wine originating in the Rhône valley.

Where to Stay in Arles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel Le Calendal - A charming hotel located in the town centre.
  • Hôtel du Forum - A modern hotel located near the amphitheatre.
  • Le Cloître - A guesthouse with a beautiful courtyard, located in a tranquil area.

What to Buy in Arles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Camargue Salt - A sea salt harvested from the Camargue region.
  • Lavender - Provençal lavender is famous for its aroma and is used to make soaps, oils, and perfumes.
  • Olives - Provence produces excellent olive oil and olives.

Local Culture and Customs of Arles

Arles is a town rich in traditions, and visitors are welcome to participate in many of these customs. Bullfighting is a popular pastime, and the town celebrates its heroes, the toreros, every year in September. The famous Feria takes place in Arles twice a year, in Easter and September, with parades, live music, and street parties.

Best Time to Visit Arles

The best time to visit Arles is in spring and summer when the weather is sunny and warm. The summer months of July and August are the peak tourist season, and visitors should expect crowds. If you plan to attend the Feria, you should reserve your accommodation well in advance to avoid disappointment.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arles

Do:

  • Attend a bullfight and experience local traditions.
  • Visit the weekly market to sample the fresh produce and traditional delicacies.
  • Be respectful to locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Wear offensive clothing when visiting the sites of religious or cultural significance.
  • Litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arles

Arles is considered a safe city for tourists. However, visitors should always take precautions against pickpocketing and theft, particularly on public transport and in crowded areas.

Health in Arles

There are no major health risks to travellers visiting Arles. Visitors should take normal precautions regarding sun and heat exposure.

Getting to Arles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arles

*Nîmes–Alès–Camargue–Cévennes Airport - 20 km from Arles.

Bus Stations Near Arles

  • Arles Bus Station - located in the centre of the town.

Other Options

  • Train from Marseille - A regular train service from nearby Marseille takes only around 30 minutes to reach Arles.

How to Get Around in Arles

The best way to get around Arles is on foot, as many of the town’s attractions are located near the city centre. However, for those who want to explore further afield, bikes are available for hire. Public transport, including buses and trains, is reliable and affordable.

Where to Go Next from Arles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marseille - A vibrant port city with a rich history and culture.
  • Avignon - Home to the famous Papal Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the annual Avignon Festival.
  • Saint-Tropez - A chic resort town on the French Riviera, famous for its nightlife and beaches.

Conclusion

Arles is an ideal destination for travellers looking to explore French history and culture while also enjoying a range of outdoor activities. With its abundant natural and cultural attractions, vibrant food and drink scene, and friendly locals, it is one of the gems of Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur. Whether you are travelling alone or with family and friends, Arles is sure to delight and engage - so book your trip today!
