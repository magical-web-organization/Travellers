Food and Drink Scene of Aix-en-Provence

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bouillabaisse: A classic Provencal fish stew made with local ingredients and served with crusty bread.
  • Calissons: A sweet almond-flavored confection that is a specialty of Aix-en-Provence.
  • Ratatouille: A savory vegetable dish made with fresh local produce, including eggplant, zucchini, and peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Rosé Wine: Aix-en-Provence is well-known for producing some of the best rosé wines in France.
  • Pastis: An anise-flavored liqueur that is popular in Provence.

Where to Stay in Aix-en-Provence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel de Gantès: A charming boutique hotel located in the heart of the old town.
  • Le Pigonnet: A luxury hotel set in a 19th-century mansion with beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • Citéa Aix-en-Provence: A budget-friendly option located near the city center.

What to Buy in Aix-en-Provence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Santalwood Products: Aix-en-Provence is known for its high-quality, locally made Santalwood products, including perfumes, soaps, and lotions.
  • Calissons: As mentioned above, Calissons are a specialty of Aix-en-Provence and are an excellent souvenir to bring home.
  • Lavender Products: Lavender is a staple of the Provencal landscape, and visitors can find locally made lavender products, including soaps, oils, and candles, at most local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Aix-en-Provence

Aix-en-Provence is a culturally rich city with several traditions and customs that visitors should be aware of. One of the most important is the siesta, a daily break taken in the mid-afternoon when most shops and restaurants close. Visitors should plan their activities around this time and be aware that some services may not be available.

Best Time to Visit Aix-en-Provence

The best time to visit Aix-en-Provence is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April – May) and autumn (September – October). During these times, the weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aix-en-Provence

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Do visit local markets and shops.
  • Do take a guided tour to learn about the city’s fascinating history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect cultural traditions and customs.
  • Don’t forget to take a siesta during the mid-afternoon.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aix-en-Provence

Aix-en-Provence is a safe city, but visitors should be aware of pickpockets and keep their belongings secure in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Aix-en-Provence

Visitors to Aix-en-Provence do not need any special vaccines or medications, but it is important to take precautions against tick bites when hiking. Emergency medical services are available throughout the city.

Getting to Aix-en-Provence: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Aix-en-Provence is easy, and there are several transportation options available, including:

  • Marseille Provence Airport: The closest airport to Aix-en-Provence, with several international and domestic airlines.
  • High-Speed Train: Aix-en-Provence is accessible via the TGV high-speed train, with connections to major French cities such as Paris and Lyon.
  • Bus: Several bus companies offer connections to Aix-en-Provence from major cities throughout France.

How to Get Around in Aix-en-Provence

The city center of Aix-en-Provence is easily walkable, but visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and trams, to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Aix-en-Provence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aix-en-Provence is a great starting point to explore the beautiful region of Provence. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Marseille: The vibrant port city of Marseille is only a short distance from Aix-en-Provence and is filled with cultural landmarks, beautiful beaches, and lively nightlife.
  • Avignon: Known for its stunning architecture and rich cultural history, Avignon is a must-visit destination for history and culture enthusiasts.
  • Côte d’Azur: The picturesque coastline of the French Riviera is only a few hours away and is a perfect destination for beaches, seafood, and sun.

Conclusion

Aix-en-Provence is a charming and historic city with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, art, nature, or food, there’s no shortage of things to see and do. With this comprehensive guide in hand, you’ll be well prepared to plan your next vacation to this beautiful region of France.
