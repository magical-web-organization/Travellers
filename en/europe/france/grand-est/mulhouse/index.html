Introduction to Mulhouse

Located in the Grand Est region of France, Mulhouse is a small town that has a lot to offer. Known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty, Mulhouse is the perfect destination for travelers who are looking to explore off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Mulhouse

No trip to Mulhouse would be complete without a visit to these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cité de l’Automobile: This museum is home to the largest collection of automobiles in the world, including some of the most iconic cars in history.

  • Musée de l’Impression sur Etoffes: This museum showcases the history of textile printing and is a must-visit for anyone interested in design or fashion.

  • Musée Historique de Mulhouse: This museum documents the history of Mulhouse and the surrounding region.

  • Écomusée d’Alsace: Located just outside Mulhouse, this living museum features traditional Alsatian buildings and shows what life was like in the region centuries ago.

Natural Attractions of Mulhouse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Zoologique et Botanique de Mulhouse: This park is home to over a thousand animals and a variety of plant species. It’s a great place to spend a day with family or friends.

  • The Ballon d’Alsace: This mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and is a popular spot for hiking and skiing.

  • La Petite Camargue Alsacienne: This wetland area is home to a variety of animals and plants and is a great place for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mulhouse

Mulhouse and the surrounding region offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Some of the best routes include:

  • Tour des 4 Lacs: This 31-kilometer trail takes you through forests and past four beautiful lakes.

  • Vallée de la Thur: This trail runs along the Thur River and takes you through charming villages and scenic countryside.

  • Tour du Grand Ballon: This challenging route takes you up the highest peak in the Vosges mountains and rewards you with stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Mulhouse: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Alsace is known for its delicious wines, and there are plenty of tours that will take you to local wineries for tastings.

  • Alsace Bike Tour: This tour takes you through charming villages and past vineyards and allows you to sample the region’s famous food and wine along the way.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: See Mulhouse from above during a hot air balloon ride, where you’ll get a bird’s-eye view of the town’s historic buildings and beautiful natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mulhouse

If you have a week in Mulhouse, consider this itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the town’s historic center, including the Temple Saint-Étienne, the Musée Historique de Mulhouse, and the Cité de l’Automobile.

  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Colmar, a charming town just 40 minutes from Mulhouse.

  • Day 4-5: Enjoy the great outdoors with a hike in the mountains or a visit to the Parc Zoologique et Botanique de Mulhouse.

  • Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour in the Alsace wine region.

  • Day 7: End your trip with a hot air balloon ride over Mulhouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Mulhouse

No trip to Mulhouse would be complete without sampling some of the local food and drink.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tarte Flambée: A thin crust pizza topped with onions, bacon, and cream.

  • Choucroute: A traditional Alsatian dish made with sauerkraut and various meats.

  • Baeckeoffe: A casserole dish made with a variety of meats and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Alsace Wine: From crisp and refreshing Rieslings to rich and bold Pinot Noirs, the wines of Alsace are a must-try.

  • Kugelhopf: A sweet cake made with raisins and almonds. It’s often served with coffee or tea.

Where to Stay in Mulhouse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel du Parc: Located in the heart of Mulhouse, Hotel du Parc is a great option for those who want to be close to the town’s top attractions.

  • Hotel Bristol: This boutique hotel offers stylish and comfortable accommodations just a few minutes’ walk from the train station.

  • B&B Hôtel Mulhouse Centre: This budget-friendly option offers clean and basic accommodations in a central location.

What to Buy in Mulhouse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Textiles: Mulhouse has a rich history of textile production, and you can find a variety of fabrics, linens, and other textiles to bring home as souvenirs.

  • Pottery: Alsace is known for its pottery, which often features bright colors and unique designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mulhouse

In Mulhouse and the surrounding region, it’s common to greet people with “Bonjour” (good day) and “Au Revoir” (goodbye). It’s also considered polite to say “s’il vous plaît” (please) and “merci” (thank you) when ordering food or asking for help.

Best Time to Visit Mulhouse

The best time to visit Mulhouse is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mulhouse

Do:

  • Tip your server in restaurants and cafes.

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.

  • Learn a few basic French phrases, such as “Bonjour” and “Merci”.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.

  • Bring large bags or backpacks into museums or other attractions.

  • Assume that everyone speaks English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mulhouse

Overall, Mulhouse is a safe place to visit. However, as with any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep your belongings safe.

Health in Mulhouse

No special vaccinations are required for travel to Mulhouse. However, it’s always a good idea to consult with your doctor before traveling abroad.

Getting to Mulhouse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mulhouse

  • EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg: This airport is located just 25 minutes’ drive from Mulhouse and serves many major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Mulhouse

  • Gare Central: This is the main bus station in Mulhouse, and it serves a variety of local and regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Mulhouse is well-connected to the rest of France and Europe via train.

How to Get Around in Mulhouse

Mulhouse is a walkable town, and many of its top attractions are located within walking distance of each other. However, if you need to travel further afield, there are also buses, trams, and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Mulhouse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Strasbourg: This beautiful city is just an hour away by train and is home to the famous Strasbourg Cathedral and the European Parliament.

  • Freiburg: Located just across the border in Germany, Freiburg is a charming town known for its Gothic cathedral and lively student scene.

Conclusion

Mulhouse is a hidden gem in the heart of Europe, with plenty to offer travelers who want to experience the region’s rich history, natural beauty, and delicious food and wine. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this charming town and make the most of your time there.
