Introduction to Metz

Metz is a beautiful city located in the Grand Est region of France. With a rich history and cultural heritage, Metz boasts an impressive array of historical landmarks, museums and galleries, lush gardens, and bustling markets. The city is known for its distinctive mix of Gothic and Romanesque architecture and is home to some of France’s most stunning churches and cathedrals.

Whether you’re a history buff, an art lover, or simply looking to explore the outdoors, Metz has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide covers all the essential information you need to plan your trip to this charming city.

List of Places to Visit in Metz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Metz has a fascinating history that is reflected in its many museums and historical landmarks. The city is home to the Centre Pompidou-Metz, one of France’s leading modern and contemporary art museums, housing some of the world’s most exciting contemporary art exhibitions.

Another must-visit cultural attraction in Metz is the Musée de la Cour d’Or, which showcases the city’s rich heritage, spanning from prehistoric times to the present day. The museum’s archeological collection, comprising many Roman mosaics and ancient artifacts, is particularly noteworthy.

If you’re interested in religious architecture, you’ll want to visit the spectacular Saint-Étienne Cathedral and the Basilica of Saint-Pierre-aux-Nonnains, a Romanesque church that is one of the oldest in France. The Chapelle des Templiers, dating back to the 12th century, is another historical gem that should not be missed.

Natural Attractions of Metz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In Metz, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to connect with nature. The city’s many parks and gardens provide a peaceful respite from urban life, and the picturesque River Moselle promises stunning views of the city from different perspectives.

The Parc des Arènes offers a peaceful haven for picnickers and hikers, with its beautiful gardens, sprawling lawns, and leafy shady spots. The Parc de la Seille, with its shimmering pond and walking paths, is another great spot to relax and take in the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Metz

If you’re looking for more outdoor adventures, Metz offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and cycling. The Moselle River Trail (La Voie Bleue), a popular cycling route, takes you on a scenic journey through charming villages, vineyards, and picturesque landscapes.

The Moselle Trail (Le Chemin des écluses de la Moselle), is another great option for walkers and cyclists with its fascinating locks and charming villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Metz: Activities and Tours

When you’re ready to take a break from touring the city’s many cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of other activities in Metz to keep you entertained.

Visit the bustling local markets, such as the Marché Couvert de Metz, where you can sample local cuisine and regional specialties. Take a boat tour of the Moselle River, or participate in a wine tasting tour of the nearby vineyards.

For music lovers, the Metz Opera House offers a diverse range of performances, from classical to modern concerts. And for families visiting with kids, the Parc Walygator amusement park is a must-visit destination.

Week-Long Itinerary for Metz

To make the most of your trip to Metz, we suggest the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Discover the historical centre of Metz, including the St-Etienne Cathedral and the Musee de la Cour d’Or.

Day 2: Explore the Centre Pompidou-Metz and visit the nearby Parc des Arènes.

Day 3: Take a walking or cycling tour of the city or boat tour through the Moselle River.

Day 4: Visit the medieval town of Pont-a-Mousson, 20 kilometers west of Metz.

Day 5: Discover the scenic villages along the Moselle River, including Arnaville and Pagny-sur-Moselle.

Day 6: Go to the wine region of Moselle and visit a vineyard for wine tasting.

Day 7: Spend the day at Parc Walygator amusement park before bidding farewell to Metz.

Food and Drink Scene of Metz

Must-Try Dishes

Metz’s culinary scene is a must-try for foodies and travelers who love to explore the local cuisine. Try the famous Quiche Lorraine, a savory pie filled with bacon, eggs, cream, and cheese, which is recognized as one of the best dishes in the region.

Another local delicacy is the Pâté Lorrain, a savory pastry filled with a delicious blend of pork, beef, and spices. You may also want to try the hot, spiced wine known as Vin Chaud, perfect for warming up on cold winter evenings.

For sweet treats, don’t miss the Macarons de Boulay, popular small meringue-based sweets filled with a variety of flavors, including raspberry, chocolate, and vanilla.

Local Beverages

Moselle is known for its vineyards and wines, so during your stay in Metz, you mustn’t forget to sample the local wines and spirits. These include the AOC wine from the Moselle Valley and the many beer varieties that are popular in the entire Grand Est region.

Where to Stay in Metz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Metz offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, from basic hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some top picks for hotels include the Grand Hotel de Metz, Hotel La Citadelle Metz, and the Novotel Metz Centre. For budget travelers, we recommend the Ibis Metz Centre Gare, which offers great affordability and is centrally located.

What to Buy in Metz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For souvenirs and local products to bring back home, check out the many markets and boutiques in the city. Popular items include locally-made candles, soaps, glassware, pottery, textiles, and souvenirs with local historic figures, including Robert Schuman, one of the founding fathers of the European Union.

Local Culture and Customs of Metz

Metz’s rich cultural heritage and history are reflected in its local customs and traditions. One local custom worth exploring is the Cathedral Christmas Market, which is known for its locally-made artisanal goods and delicious festival foods.

Metzlings (the local people of Metz) are known to be warm, hospitable, and friendly, and appreciate when visitors try to learn their language and understand their local customs. It’s customary to greet people with a cordial “bonjour” and “au revoir,” and be polite and respectful towards service staff and fellow travelers.

Best Time to Visit Metz

The best time to visit Metz is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are fewer. The city’s mild climate means that it can be visited at any time of year, but with pleasant sunny weather from April to October, the city comes to life with outdoor events and activities during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Metz

Do:

  • Learn a few French phrases; locals will appreciate your effort to communicate with them.
  • Try the regional cuisine, including Quiche Lorraine and Pâté Lorrain.
  • Respect local customs and cultural traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets; throw trash in designated bins.
  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Metz

Metz is generally a safe city, but like any tourist destination, pickpocketing, and petty theft can happen. We recommend keeping your valuables safe and being wary of your surroundings, particularly in crowded or touristy areas.

Health in Metz

France’s healthcare system is among the best in the world, and visitors are eligible for free medical care in case of an emergency. Travelers should also ensure that they have adequate travel insurance to cover them in case of an accident or illness.

Getting to Metz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Metz is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it easy to get to. Transport options include:

  • Air: Metz-Nancy-Lorraine Airport is just 25 km away from the city center.
  • Train: The city has two main train stations, Gare de Metz-Ville and Gare de Metz-Sablon, which are directly connected to major cities in France and Europe.
  • Bus: Regular bus services run in and around the city, providing convenient transport options for visitors who do not have their vehicle.

How to Get Around in Metz

Metz is a compact city, and it’s easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. The city also has an extensive public transport system, including buses and trams, which can take you all over the city and beyond. If you’re planning on exploring the surrounding countryside, renting a car may be a good option.

Where to Go Next from Metz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re done exploring Metz, there are plenty of nearby destinations that are worth visiting. Some popular nearby cities include Strasbourg, Nancy, and Luxembourg City, all within driving distance. For outdoor enthusiasts, the nearby Lorraine Regional Nature Park offers spectacular countryside views and is ideal for hiking and skiing activities.

Conclusion

Metz has something for everyone, from history enthusiasts and art lovers to foodies and nature lovers. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip, make the most of your time in the city, and discover all the wonders of this charming French destination.
