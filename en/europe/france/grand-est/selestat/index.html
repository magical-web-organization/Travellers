Introduction to Sélestat

Located in the heart of Alsace, Sélestat is a charming town with a rich history and cultural heritage. The town is known for its beautiful architecture, picturesque streets, and stunning surroundings. Sélestat is also famous for its Christmas markets, which attract visitors from all over the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, hiking trails, food scene, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sélestat

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Humanist Library.

Day 2: Visit Sainte-Foy Church and the Musée des Arts et Traditions Populaires.

Day 3: Hike from Sélestat to Dambach-la-Ville.

Day 4: Visit Mont Sainte-Odile, Ortenbourg Castle, and Monkey Mountain.

Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour along the Alsace Wine Route.

Day 6: Visit Maison du Pain d’Alsace and the Romanesque House.

Day 7: Explore Sélestat’s Christmas markets.

Best Time to Visit Sélestat

The best time to visit Sélestat is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town comes alive with festivals and events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sélestat

Sélestat is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Sélestat

Medical facilities in Sélestat are of a high standard, and there are plenty of pharmacies in town. Visitors should ensure that they have appropriate travel insurance before traveling.
