Introduction to Strasbourg

Strasbourg is the capital city of the Grand Est region in France. Located in the eastern part of the country, the city is famous for its picturesque old town, canals, and the stunning Cathedral of Notre-Dame. Strasbourg has been inhabited since Roman times and as a result, has a rich cultural and historical heritage.

Whether you are interested in history, art, architecture, or gastronomy, Strasbourg has something to offer for everyone. This guide will help you plan your trip to Strasbourg, with information on where to go, what to see, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Strasbourg

Strasbourg has a wealth of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and exciting activities. Here are some of the must-visit places in Strasbourg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Cathedral of Notre-Dame de Strasbourg: a masterpiece of Gothic architecture dating back to the 12th century. The cathedral is home to the famous Astronomical Clock and offers stunning views of the city from its viewpoint.
  • Palais Rohan: a former episcopal palace, now a museum, exhibiting the decorative arts, paintings, and archaeological collections from Strasbourg.
  • Alsatian Museum: located in the heart of the Petite France district, this museum showcases the history and culture of Strasbourg and the Alsace region.
  • Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art: a must-visit for art lovers, this museum displays works of art from the 1870s to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Strasbourg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc de l’Orangerie: a beautiful park with a large lake, a small zoo, and beautiful gardens. There are plenty of activities to do in the park, such as mini-golf, paddle-boating, and pony rides.
  • Petite France: a charming neighborhood with colorful, half-timbered houses and narrow streets along the Ill river.
  • Ill River Boat Tour: enjoy a relaxing boat ride down the river while admiring the views of the city from the water.
  • Montagne Verte: a forested hill on the outskirts of Strasbourg that is perfect for a hike or a bike ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strasbourg

Strasbourg is surrounded by beautiful countryside, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes of Strasbourg:

  • Grande Île: a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Grande Île is the historical center of Strasbourg and a pedestrian-only area. You can explore the city on foot or rent a bike, enjoying the picturesque views along the way.
  • Parc de la Montagne Verte: this forested hill has various hiking trails and mountain bike routes, offering fantastic views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • La Bruche Cycling Route: a 60-kilometer ride that follows the river Bruche from Molsheim to Strasbourg, passing through beautiful forests, charming villages, and vineyards.

Best Things to Do in Strasbourg: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of things to do and tours to explore in Strasbourg. Here are some suggestions:

  • Take a Food Tour: Strasbourg is known for its traditional Alsatian cuisine, with dishes like choucroute, flammekueche, and baeckeoffe. A food tour is an excellent way to discover the local culture and taste some of the best dishes.
  • Visit the Strasbourg Christmas Markets: the city is famous for its Christmas markets, attracting millions of visitors every year. The markets feature handmade crafts, local food, and plenty of festive cheer.
  • Try an Escape Room: if you are looking for a fun and challenging activity, try one of the many escape rooms in Strasbourg. You will have to solve puzzles, riddles, and clues to escape in time.
  • Take a Wine Tasting Tour: the Alsace region is renowned for its white wines, especially Riesling, Pinot Gris, and Gewürztraminer. Take a wine tour and discover the local vineyards and wineries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strasbourg

If you have one week to spend in Strasbourg, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Cathedral of Notre-Dame and climb to the top for stunning views of the city.
2 Take a boat tour of Strasbourg and explore the charming Petite France neighborhood.
3 Discover the cultural attractions of Strasbourg by visiting the Alsatian Museum, the Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art, and the Palais Rohan.
4 Rent a bike and explore the city’s green spaces, such as the Parc de l’Orangerie or Montagne Verte.
5 Take a day trip to the nearby Alsace Wine Route and enjoy wine tasting and incredible views of the vineyards and villages.

Food and Drink Scene of Strasbourg

Strasbourg has a rich culinary tradition, with a mix of French and German influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Choucroute: a hearty dish made with sauerkraut and various meats, such as sausages, pork, and ham.
  • Flammekueche: a thin-crust pizza-like dish topped with crème fraîche, onions, and bacon.
  • Baeckeoffe: a casserole made with potatoes, onions, and various meats, such as beef, pork, and lamb.
  • Tarte à l’oignon: a savory onion tart made with a buttery crust.

Local Beverages

  • Alsace Wine: Strasbourg is located in the Alsace wine region, known for its white wines such as Riesling, Gewürztraminer, and Pinot Gris.
  • Alsatian Beer: brewed locally, Alsatian beer is a must-try for beer lovers. The most famous local beer brands are Kronenbourg and Fischer.

Where to Stay in Strasbourg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Strasbourg has plenty of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some suggestions:

  • Hotel Rohan: a boutique hotel located in the heart of Strasbourg, near the Cathedral and the Petite France neighborhood.
  • Adonis Hotel Strasbourg: a budget-friendly hotel located in the city center, offering modern rooms with all amenities.
  • Hostel Strasbourg: a great budget option for backpackers and solo travelers, offering clean rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Guesthouse Les Remparts: a charming guesthouse located in a historic building in the city center, offering cozy rooms and a lovely courtyard garden.

What to Buy in Strasbourg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some suggestions:

  • Christmas decorations: Strasbourg Christmas markets offer handmade crafts and ornaments, including blown glass, wooden carvings, and candles.
  • Foie gras: a traditional French delicacy, foie gras is a popular gift item or souvenir.
  • Alsatian wine: bring back a bottle or two of the famous Alsace white wine, available at local wineries and shops.
  • Lace tablecloths: the region is known for its delicate lacework, and buying a tablecloth is a perfect souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Strasbourg

Strasbourg has a unique blend of French and German cultures. Here are some customs and traditions:

  • Bilingualism: many locals speak both French and German, and it is not uncommon to hear a mix of both languages.
  • La Petite France: the historic quarter of Strasbourg with half-timbered houses, narrow streets and canals, and a charming atmosphere. It is a favorite destination for locals and tourists alike.
  • Christmas Markets: Strasbourg is famous for its Christmas markets, which have a long tradition and attract millions of people every year.

Best Time to Visit Strasbourg

The best time to visit Strasbourg depends on your interests and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

  • Summer: from June to August, the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events, such as music festivals and cultural exhibitions.
  • Christmas season: from November to December, the city is vibrant with Christmas markets, concerts, and festive cheer. The markets are a must-visit attraction and are perfect for a winter weekend getaway.
  • Spring and fall: The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. The city’s gardens and parks bloom with flowers in the spring, while the fall season offers beautiful colors and stunning scenery in the surrounding countryside.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strasbourg

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Strasbourg:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially traditional Alsatian dishes.
  • Visit the Cathedral of Notre-Dame and climb to the top for fantastic views.
  • Take a boat tour of the city, exploring the picturesque canals and bridges.
  • Use public transportation, such as the tram, to get around the city quickly.
  • Learn a few French or German phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not dress too casually, especially when visiting the Cathedral and other cultural attractions.
  • Do not forget to validate your tram ticket before getting on the tram.
  • Do not tip too much, as it is not customary in France.
  • Do not forget to greet people with “Bonjour” or “Morgen” (French and German for “hello”)

Safety and Health

Strasbourg is a safe city to travel to. However, it’s always essential to take some precautions and be aware of the risks. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Strasbourg

  • Pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas, such as the Christmas markets and the tram.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas and streets.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Strasbourg

  • The tap water is safe to drink.
  • There are plenty of hospitals and medical facilities in the city, with excellent healthcare services.
  • Remember to bring any prescription medication you require, and obtain travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Strasbourg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Strasbourg is located in the center of Europe, making it easy to reach by various transportation modes. Here are some suggestions:

Airports Near Strasbourg

The nearest airport to Strasbourg is Strasbourg Airport (SXB) located about 10 km southwest of the city center. Another option is EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg (BSL), located about 100 km southwest of Strasbourg.

Bus Stations Near Strasbourg

Strasbourg has several bus stations connecting the city to other cities in France and Europe. The main bus station is located at Place de l’Etoile, right in the city center.

Other Options

Strasbourg is well-connected by train and has several high-speed train connections to other cities in France and Europe. The city has two main train stations: Strasbourg-Ville and Strasbourg-Roethig.

How to Get Around in Strasbourg

Strasbourg has an excellent public transportation system, including trams, buses, and bikes. Here are some suggestions on how to get around:

  • Trams: the tram network covers most of the city, offering quick and efficient transport.
  • Bikes: Strasbourg has an extensive network of bike paths, and the city’s bike-sharing scheme, Vélhop, makes it easy to rent bikes for a day or more.
  • Buses: buses are a good option if you are traveling to the suburbs or the surrounding villages.

Where to Go Next from Strasbourg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Strasbourg is an excellent starting point for day trips and excursions to nearby destinations. Here are some suggestions:

  • Colmar: a beautiful city located in the heart of the Alsace wine region, only an hour away from Strasbourg.
  • The Black Forest: a stunning region in Germany, famous for its natural beauty, picturesque villages, and outdoor activities.
  • Baden-Baden: a spa town located in southwestern Germany, known for its hot springs, thermal baths, and luxury boutiques.
  • Basel: a charming Swiss city located on the border with France and Germany, offering excellent museums, art galleries, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Strasbourg is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and breathtaking scenery. Whether you are interested in history, art, gastronomy, or outdoor activities, Strasbourg has something to offer for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Strasbourg, ensuring that you make the most of your time in this charming city.
