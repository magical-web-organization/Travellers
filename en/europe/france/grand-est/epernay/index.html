If you’re planning a trip to France, be sure to put Épernay on your list of destinations to visit. Located in the region of Grand Est, Épernay is known for its champagne, gorgeous scenery, and rich history. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything there is to see and do in Épernay, from the top attractions and natural wonders to the best places to stay and eat.

Introduction to Épernay

Épernay is a charming town in the Champagne region of France, less than a 2-hour drive from Paris. Known as the “Capital of Champagne,” Épernay is home to some of the world’s most famous champagne houses. The town is situated in the heart of the Marne Valley, surrounded by lush vineyards and rolling hills.Épernay has a rich history dating back to the Roman times, and the architectural and cultural influences from that era still exist today. The town is also surrounded by stunning natural beauty, with opportunities for hiking and biking around the vineyards and forests.Épernay is a perfect destination for wine lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Épernay

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Épernay, from exploring the famous champagne houses to hiking through the vineyards. Here are some of the top places to visit in Épernay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Le Clos Chauveau

Le Clos Chauveau is a charming estate dating back to the 18th century, located just a few minutes away from Épernay. The estate was acquired by Champagne Piper-Heidsieck and was carefully restored to its original state to showcase the rich history of the region. The tour of the estate takes visitors through the cellars and gardens, highlighting the architectural and cultural influence of the Romans.

Musée du Vin de Champagne et d’Archéologie Régionale

Situated in the heart of Épernay, the Musée du Vin de Champagne et d’Archéologie Régionale showcases the history and culture of the Champagne region. The museum features a collection of ancient artifacts and exhibits on wine-making and the role of champagne in French culture.

Château de Montmort

Located just outside of Épernay, the Château de Montmort dates back to the 16th century. The castle has been carefully restored and houses a collection of antique furniture, paintings, and sculptures. The castle also hosts various events and cultural exhibitions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Épernay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Avenue de Champagne

Avenue de Champagne is the most famous street in Épernay and features some of the most iconic champagne houses in the world. The street is lined with gorgeous mansions, while the cellars below contain millions of bottles of champagne. Walking along the avenue is one of the best ways to explore the town and experience the local culture.

Parc Naturel Régional de la Montagne de Reims

The Parc Naturel Régional de la Montagne de Reims is a stunning national park located just a short drive from Épernay. The park is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including deer, foxes, and eagles, and offers many opportunities for hiking and biking. The Montagne de Reims is also an important champagne-producing area, making it a must-visit for wine lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Épernay

The surrounding hills and vineyards of Épernay make it the perfect spot for hiking and biking. Some of the best routes to explore include:

Route du Champagne

The Route du Champagne is a 40-kilometer driving route through the Champagne region, including Épernay. The route offers breathtaking views of the vineyards and wine cellars, making it one of the best ways to experience the region.

La Voie Verte

La Voie Verte is a scenic cycling path that connects Épernay to the surrounding countryside. The path offers stunning views of the Marne Valley, as well as access to the vineyards and natural attractions of the region.

Best Things to Do in Épernay: Activities and Tours

For those looking for more structured activities and tours, Épernay has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Épernay:

Champagne House Tours

Épernay is home to some of the most famous champagne houses in the world, and taking a tour of one of these houses is a must-do activity. Some of the most popular houses to visit include Moët & Chandon, Mercier, and Perrier-Jouët. The tours offer a unique opportunity to learn about the history and production of champagne, as well as to sample some of the finest wines in the world.

Hot Air Balloon Rides

For those looking for a more adventurous activity, hot air balloon rides are a popular option in Épernay. The rides offer stunning views of the vineyards and surrounding countryside, as well as a unique perspective on the town itself.

Week-Long Itinerary for Épernay

For those looking to spend more time in Épernay, here’s an example of a week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Épernay, explore Avenue de Champagne and Maison de Champagne Gabriel Boutet.

Day 2: Take a tour of Champagne Pierre Peters winery, followed by a hike through the vineyards.

Day 3: Visit Musée du Vin de Champagne et d’Archéologie Régionale, followed by a night tour of Champagne Mercier’s cellars.

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride over Épernay and the surrounding vineyards, followed by a picnic in the Parc Naturel Régional de la Montagne de Reims.

Day 5: Explore the Château de Montmort, followed by a scenic drive along the Route du Champagne.

Day 6: Visit the local farmer’s market and participate in a champagne tasting workshop.

Day 7: Depart Épernay.

Food and Drink Scene of Épernay

No trip to Épernay is complete without sampling the local cuisine and wine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Boudin Blanc

Boudin Blanc is a traditional French sausage made with pork, milk, and spices. The sausage is a popular dish in Épernay and is often served with mashed potatoes and a white wine sauce.

Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a classic French dish made with chicken, bacon, mushrooms, and red wine. The dish is typically prepared with a local champagne, making it a must-try in Épernay.

Local Beverages

Champagne

Épernay is known for its world-renowned champagne, and no visit to the town is complete without sampling some of the local varieties. Some of the most famous champagne houses in Épernay include Moët & Chandon, Perrier-Jouët, and Laurent-Perrier.

Ratafia

Ratafia is a sweet aperitif made with local wine and brandy. The drink is often served with desserts or as an after-dinner drink. It’s a must-try for those looking to experience the local culture and cuisine.

Where to Stay in Épernay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Épernay has a range of accommodations to suit all types of travelers and budgets. Here are some of the top options:

Hôtel de la Paix

Hôtel de la Paix is a luxurious 4-star hotel located in the heart of Épernay. The hotel features a spa, wellness center, and an on-site restaurant serving local cuisine.

Le Clos Margot

Le Clos Margot is a charming bed and breakfast located just outside of Épernay. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a peaceful garden, perfect for those looking for a relaxing getaway.

What to Buy in Épernay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Épernay is known for its champagne, making it the perfect place to pick up a few bottles to bring home. Local delicacies like mustard and honey are also popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Épernay

French culture values politeness, punctuality, and respect for others. When visiting Épernay, it’s important to greet people with a friendly “Bonjour” and to always say “S’il vous plaît” and “Merci.” It’s also important to dress appropriately and to be mindful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Épernay

The best time to visit Épernay is in the fall, during the grape harvest season. The weather is mild, and the town is bustling with activity. However, Épernay is a popular destination year-round, and there’s always something to see and do.

Do’s and Don’ts of Épernay

Do:

  • Try the local champagne.
  • Explore the surrounding vineyards and countryside.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be late for appointments.
  • Wear overly revealing clothing.
  • Speak loudly in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Épernay

Épernay is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take basic precautions like keeping valuables close and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Épernay

Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical coverage for illnesses and accidents. Be sure to research the local hospitals and clinics before arriving.

Getting to Épernay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Épernay is easily accessible from Paris and other cities in the region. Here are the best transportation options:

Airports Near Épernay

The closest international airport to Épernay is Roissy-Charles de Gaulle Airport in Paris, which offers direct train and bus connections to Épernay.

Bus Stations Near Épernay

Épernay is serviced by a number of bus companies, including Ouibus and Flixbus, offering connections to other cities in the region.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a popular option for exploring the Champagne region, allowing visitors the freedom to explore at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Épernay

Épernay is a small town, and walking is the best way to explore the city center. Taxis and buses are also available for those looking to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from Épernay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Épernay is ideally located for exploring other destinations in the Champagne region, as well as nearby cities like Paris and Reims. Some popular excursions include:

Reims

Reims is another charming town located in the heart of the Champagne region. Reims is home to the famous Notre-Dame de Reims cathedral and some of the world’s most famous champagne houses, including Veuve Clicquot and Taittinger.

Paris

Paris is just a short train ride away from Épernay, making it an easy day trip. Paris is home to some of the most famous landmarks in the world, including the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre-Dame Cathedral.

Conclusion

Épernay is a charming town nestled in the heart of the Champagne region of France. From the stunning vineyards and champagne houses to the rich history and local culture, there’s something for everyone in Épernay. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next trip and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
