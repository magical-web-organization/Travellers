Introduction to Colmar

Colmar is a fairytale-like town located in Grand Est region of France. This small town has often been compared to Venice, with its winding canals, colorful half-timbered houses, and flower-lined streets. Colmar is a perfect destination for those looking to explore the culture, history, and beauty of a small French town. In this guide, we will explore all the must-visit places in Colmar, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Colmar

Colmar has a variety of tourist attractions for travelers of all ages and interests. Here are some of the must-visit places in Colmar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Unterlinden Museum: This world-renowned museum is located in a former convent and has a vast collection of artworks ranging from ancient to modern times.
  • Bartholdi Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and works of the famous French sculptor Frédéric-Auguste Bartholdi, who created the Statue of Liberty in New York City.
  • The House of Heads: This unique building is known for its ornate façade featuring 106 sculpted heads.
  • Pfister House: One of the oldest buildings in Colmar, the Pfister House is a well-preserved example of Renaissance architecture.
  • Eglise Saint-Martin: This beautiful 13th-century Gothic church is home to many historical and religious artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Colmar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Little Venice: A picturesque neighborhood along the Lauch River, known for its colorful houses and charming atmosphere.
  • Parc du Champ de Mars: This park is a perfect spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll, with its stunning views of the Colmar skyline.
  • Lac Blanc: A mountain lake situated at an altitude of 1,200 meters, where you can hike, bike, or simply enjoy the views.
  • Kaysersberg: A nearby town in the heart of the Alsatian wine region, famous for its beautiful architecture and scenic vineyards.
  • Route des Vins d’Alsace: A stunning drive through the Alsace wine region, with beautiful views of the countryside and vineyards.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colmar

Colmar is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with its many hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best options:

  • Colmar Canal Path: A scenic cycling and walking trail that runs along the Lauch River and through the heart of Colmar.
  • Munster Valley Trail: A challenging hiking trail that takes you through the Vosges Mountains and offers stunning views.
  • Wine Route Cycling Trail: A beautiful cycling trail through the vineyards of Alsace, with stops at local wineries and villages.
  • Ballons des Vosges Regional Park: A vast national park that offers plenty of hiking trails, biking routes, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Colmar: Activities and Tours

Colmar has many exciting things to do, and there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the must-try activities and tours in Colmar:

  • Wine Tasting: Visit a winegrower in Colmar or nearby towns and sample the famous Alsace wines.
  • Christmas Markets: Colmar is famous for its charming Christmas markets that run throughout the month of December.
  • Boat Tour: Sail down the canals of Colmar on a boat tour and admire the beautiful half-timbered houses.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride over the vineyards and mountains of Alsace and enjoy breathtaking views.
  • Segway Tour: Ride a Segway through Colmar and explore the sights and landmarks of the town in a fun and unique way.

Food and Drink Scene of Colmar

One of the highlights of visiting Colmar is trying the local food and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Colmar:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Flammekueche: A thin-crust pizza-like dish with onions, bacon, and crème fraîche.
  • Tarte Flambée: A traditional Alsatian dish made with thin bread dough, bacon, onions, and crème fraîche.
  • Choucroute: A hearty dish made with sauerkraut and various meats like sausages, ham, and pork.

Local Beverages

  • Alsace Wine: Colmar is located in the heart of the Alsace wine region, known for its dry white wines.
  • Vin de Noix: A sweet wine made with green walnuts and spices.
  • Kirsch: A cherry brandy that’s popular in the region.
  • Bière de Noël: A special seasonal beer that’s brewed during Christmas time.

Where to Stay in Colmar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Colmar has a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel La Maison des Têtes: A luxurious boutique hotel located in a beautifully restored 17th-century building.
  • Le Colombier Suites: A cozy guesthouse with stylish rooms and a warm ambiance.
  • Hostel Colmar: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Colmar.
  • Hôtel Quatorze: A modern and trendy hotel with chic rooms and an on-site bar.

What to Buy in Colmar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Colmar is known for its charming local products that make perfect souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Colmar:

  • Alsatian Gingerbread: A delicious traditional cookie with honey, cinnamon, and almonds.
  • Cuckoo Clocks: Handcrafted wooden clocks that are a popular souvenir in the region.
  • Wine: Bring home a bottle of the famous Alsace wine as a souvenir.
  • Riesling Jelly: A unique local product made with Riesling wine, sugar, and pectin.

Local Culture and Customs of Colmar

Colmar has a rich history and culture that’s worth exploring. Here are some of the customs and traditions that are unique to Colmar:

  • Fasenacht Festival: A pre-Lenten festival that features parades, costumes, and music.
  • Alsatian Folk Music: Colmar has a vibrant folk music scene and many local bars and venues feature live music.
  • Alsatian Language: Alsatian is a Germanic dialect that’s spoken in Colmar and the surrounding areas.

Best Time to Visit Colmar

The best time to visit Colmar is during the spring and summer months, from March to September. The weather is pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. Another great time to visit is during the Christmas market season, which runs from late November to late December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colmar

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Colmar:

Do:

  • Try the local wine and food.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions like the canals and parks.
  • Take a boat tour to see the town from a different perspective.
  • Learn a few basic French phrases before your trip.

Don’t:

  • Forget to dress appropriately for the weather, especially during the winter months.
  • Take photos of private properties without asking for permission.
  • Be loud and disruptive, especially in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Colmar is a safe and healthy town to visit, but it’s always good to take some basic precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Colmar

  • Keep your valuables secured.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Use licensed taxis or public transportation.

Health in Colmar

  • Make sure you have adequate travel health insurance.
  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated in the summer months.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid any health concerns.

Getting to Colmar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Colmar is well-connected to the rest of France and Europe. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Colmar

  • EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg: The nearest airport is located about 45 minutes from Colmar.
  • Strasbourg Airport: About an hour’s drive from Colmar.

Bus Stations Near Colmar

  • Colmar Bus Station: Served by both regional and national bus companies.

Other Options

  • Train: Colmar has a well-connected train station with regular connections to nearby towns and cities.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great option if you’re planning to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Colmar

Colmar is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot. However, there are other options as well:

  • Bike: Colmar has a well-developed cycling infrastructure with many bike rental options.
  • Public Transportation: Colmar has a convenient bus system that connects the town to its suburbs and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Colmar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Colmar is surrounded by many beautiful towns, cities, and attractions that are worth visiting. Here are some of the best nearby destinations:

  • Strasbourg: A charming city known for its rich culture and architecture, located about an hour’s drive from Colmar.
  • Eguisheim: A picturesque town known for its stunning vineyards and beautiful architecture.
  • Haut-Koenigsbourg Castle: A stunning medieval castle located in the Vosges Mountains.
  • Black Forest: A scenic mountain range located across the border in Germany, known for its beautiful nature and quaint towns.

Conclusion

Colmar is a beautiful town that’s full of history, culture, and natural beauty. This comprehensive guide has covered everything you need to know to plan your visit to Colmar, from places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and what to eat. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Colmar has something to offer. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore this charming town in Grand Est, France.
