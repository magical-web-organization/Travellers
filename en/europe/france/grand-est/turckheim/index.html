Introduction to Turckheim

Turckheim, a charming medieval town in France, is a hidden gem in the Grand-Est region. With its old houses and cobblestone streets surrounded by vineyards, this town has a unique charm that has captivated visitors for centuries. Apart from its historical significance, Turckheim also offers breathtaking views of the Vosges Mountains, and it is an ideal destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Turckheim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Eglise Sainte-Anne: This church is an iconic symbol of Turckheim and boasts an impressive baroque interior and Renaissance décor.
  • La Maison des Têtes: This 16th-century house has amusing sculptures of men’s heads adorning its façade.
  • La Tour des Voleurs: This tower was once used as a prison but it now houses the local history museum. Visitors can learn about the town’s past through its collections of artifacts and historical documents.

Natural Attractions of Turckheim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vineyards Tour: Turckheim is famous for its wine, and visitors can take a guided tour of the vineyards to learn about the production process and sample some of the local wine.
  • Parc des Cigognes: This park is home to several storks, as well as a variety of other birds and animals. Visitors can explore the park’s network of walking trails and enjoy the natural scenery.
  • The Walls of Turckheim: These medieval walls are a reminder of the town’s turbulent past. Visitors can walk along the walls and enjoy the panoramic view of the old town and the surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Turckheim

Turckheim is surrounded by hills and forests, making it an ideal destination for hikers and bikers. Here are some top routes to try out:

  • The GR5 Trail: This long-distance trail starts in Rotterdam and ends in Nice, running right through Turckheim on its way. The section around Turckheim offers stunning views of the Vosges Mountains.
  • The Vosges Mountains: Visitors can explore the Vosges Mountains along hiking or mountain biking paths. The Alsace Wine Route is also a great biking option that passes through the area, allowing visitors to discover the beautiful countryside on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Turckheim: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Turckheim is home to several vineyards, such as Cave de Turckheim, where visitors can participate in wine tasting tours and sample delicious wines.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride and see the beautiful landscape of Turckheim from above.
  • Guided Tours: Experienced guides offer group tours on foot or bike around the town and its surroundings, providing a local’s insight into Turckheim’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Turckheim

Day 1: Explore the town’s landmarks, including Eglise Sainte-Anne, La Maison des Têtes, and La Tour des Voleurs. Stop for coffee along the way at one of the local cafes.

Day 2: Take a vineyard tour at one of the local vineyards, followed by wine tasting at Cave de Turckheim.

Day 3: Head to the Parc des Cigognes to see the storks and other birds, and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 4: Embark on a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the breathtaking views of the town and surrounding area.

Day 5: Hike along the GR5 Trail or mountain bike through the Vosges Mountains.

Day 6: Take a walking tour of the town with a local guide to learn about its history and architecture.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine and wine before heading to the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Turckheim

Must-Try Dishes

Turckheim has a culinary scene celebrating the mix of French and German traditions of this part of France. These are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Tarte flambée (Alsacian pizza): Made with smoky bacon, crème fraîche, and onions baked on thin crispy bread.
  • Choucroute garnie: A hearty dish that features sauerkraut, potatoes, and a variety of sausages and meats.
  • Baeckeofe: A meat and potato stew cooked in a ceramic dish that is baked in the oven for a long time, creating an intense flavor.

Local Beverages

Turckheim is world-famous for its wine, especially Gewürztraminer, which pairs well with the local cuisine. Visitors can also sample the local beer, which is served in most restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Turckheim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Turckheim has a variety of accommodations for all budget ranges. A few popular options include:

  • Hotel des Deux Clefs: A historic hotel located in the heart of Turckheim, with a cozy ambiance and several room types.
  • Hostellerie Schwendi: A 16th-century building offering comfortable accommodation with views of the town.
  • La Grange du Couvent: An elegant bed and breakfast offering beautiful views of the vineyards that surround Turckheim.

What to Buy in Turckheim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: Turckheim is known for its Gewürztraminer wine, and there are many local vineyards where visitors can purchase bottles to take home.
  • Trinkets: There are many small local shops in Turckheim offering trinkets, such as keychains, fridge magnets, and postcards.

Local Culture and Customs of Turckheim

Turckheim has a rich history, which is visible in its architecture and traditions. The town hosts several cultural events throughout the year, such as the annual wine festival and Christmas markets.

Best Time to Visit Turckheim

The best time to visit Turckheim is between May and September when the weather is pleasant and the town celebrates several festivals. The town is also beautiful during Christmas time, with its festive decorations and warm ambiance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turckheim

Do:

  • Explore the town’s historical landmarks and the scenic views of the Vosges Mountains
  • Sample the local cuisine and wine
  • Participate in cultural events and festivals

Don’t:

  • Drive within the old town as the roads are narrow
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Turckheim

Turckheim is generally safe, but it is always best to take precautions, such as keeping valuable personal items safe and secure.

Health in Turckheim

Turckheim has excellent medical facilities that visitors can access in case of an emergency. Visitors should also have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any health care they may need.

Getting to Turckheim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Turckheim

The nearest airport to Turckheim is EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg, which receives flights from several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Turckheim

Turckheim does not have a bus station, but the neighboring towns of Colmar and Munster have bus connections to Turckheim.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Turckheim by train, with the nearest train station located in Colmar, which is 10 km away.

How to Get Around in Turckheim

Visitors can explore the town on foot or by bike. Taxis and Uber are also available for those who prefer public transport.

Where to Go Next from Turckheim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Colmar: A charming town known for its old world charm and French culture.
  • Strasbourg: The capital of the Grand Est region, Strasbourg is famous for its Gothic architecture and European Union Parliament.
  • The Vosges Regional Natural Park: A vast park perfect for nature lovers, with scenic hiking paths and wildlife spotting opportunities.

Conclusion

Turckheim is an ideal destination for those looking to explore French culture, enjoy the scenic views of the Vosges Mountains, and sample some of the local wine and cuisine. With its rich history, charming architecture, and outdoor activities, this town will leave visitors with lasting memories.
