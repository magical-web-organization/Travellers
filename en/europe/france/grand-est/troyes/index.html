Introduction to Troyes

Located in the Grand Est region of France, Troyes is a charming city with a rich history and culture. With its impressive Gothic architecture, medieval houses, and cobbled streets, Troyes is guaranteed to transport you back in time. The city is also known for its vibrant nightlife, delicious cuisine, and beautiful natural landscapes. In this comprehensive travel guide to Troyes, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this enchanting destination.

List of Places to Visit in Troyes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cathédrale St-Pierre-et-St-Paul: This magnificent cathedral dates back to the 13th century and boasts beautiful Gothic architecture. It’s one of the most popular tourist attractions in Troyes.
  • Musée d’Art Moderne: Located in an old bishop’s palace, this museum features a variety of contemporary art exhibitions.
  • Hôtel-Dieu-le-Comte: This impressive building, once a hospital, now houses Troyes’ history museum, including a collection of artifacts from the 12th to the 19th centuries.
  • Les Églises de Troyes: Troyes is home to several beautiful churches that are worth a visit, including the Église Sainte-Madeleine and the Église Saint-Pantaléon.

Natural Attractions of Troyes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Forêt d’Orient: This nearby forest boasts beautiful hiking trails and opportunities for water activities on the nearby lakes.
  • Lac d’Orient: The largest of the three lakes in the Forêt d’Orient, Lac d’Orient, is a great place for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing.
  • Champagne Vineyards: Troyes is located in the heart of the Champagne region, and a visit to the nearby vineyards is a must-do activity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Troyes

  • The Voie Verte: This old railway line has been converted into a scenic bike trail that runs for 42 kilometers through the beautiful countryside around Troyes.
  • The Greenway of the Seine Valley: Discover the most beautiful sites of the Seine Valley by following this 21-km Greenway by bike or foot.

Best Things to Do in Troyes: Activities and Tours

  • Troyes Walking Tour: Join a walking tour of the city and discover the top historical sights and hidden gems.
  • Food Tour of Troyes: Explore the city’s vibrant food scene and sample delicious local specialties.
  • Champagne Tasting Tour: Take a tour of the nearby vineyards and sample some of the world’s finest champagne.

Week-Long Itinerary for Troyes

Day 1: Explore the city centre and visit its top cultural attractions such as the Hôtel-Dieu-le-Comte and the Cathédrale St-Pierre-et-St-Paul.

Day 2: Visit the nearby Champagne vineyards and discover how the famous bubbly is made.

Day 3-4: Take a bike ride along the Voie Verte or the Greenway of the Seine Valley and explore the beautiful countryside surrounding Troyes.

Day 5-6: Spend some time relaxing at the nearby lakes in the Forêt d’Orient or go hiking in the forest.

Food and Drink Scene of Troyes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Andouillette: A traditional sausage made from pork intestines and tripe, typically served with a mustard sauce.
  • Chaource Cheese: A delicious creamy cheese from the Champagne region.
  • Boeuf Bourguignon: A hearty beef stew that originated in neighboring Burgundy.

Local Beverages

  • Champagne: Need we say more? A visit to Troyes isn’t complete without a tasting of the world’s most famous bubbly.
  • Cider: The nearby Normandy region is famous for its cider, and this French beverage pairs perfectly with any meal.

Where to Stay in Troyes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel du Cirque: This cozy hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • La Maison de Rhodes: Housed in a beautiful 16th-century building, this luxury hotel offers elegant rooms and a central location.
  • L’Embellie Chambres d’Hôtes: This charming guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a warm atmosphere at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Troyes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Champagne: Bring home a bottle or two of the local specialty to enjoy with friends back home.
  • Pottery: Troyes is known for its beautiful ceramic pottery, with a variety of unique designs and patterns available.
  • Dijon Mustard: Although not technically from Troyes, Dijon mustard is a classic French souvenir that can be found in many shops throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Troyes

In Troyes, as in the rest of France, it’s customary to greet people with a kiss on each cheek. The French also take their cuisine very seriously, so don’t be surprised if you find yourself spending several hours enjoying a meal at a local restaurant.

Best Time to Visit Troyes

The best time to visit Troyes is either in the spring or the fall, when the temperatures are moderate and the crowds are smaller. However, if you’re eager to explore the nearby lakes and forests, summer can be a great time to visit as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Troyes

Do:

  • Try the locally produced champagne and cider.
  • Explore the nearby vineyards and forests.
  • Take part in a walking tour of the city.

Don’t:

  • Forget to greet people with a kiss on each cheek.
  • Visit restaurants or shops during the afternoon break (usually from 12-2).
  • Wear shorts and flip-flops in public - the French tend to dress up more than Americans or Brits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Troyes

Troyes is a safe city for tourists, and violent crime is rare. However, be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas and take precautions to keep your belongings safe.

Health in Troyes

France has a high-quality healthcare system, and Troyes has several hospitals and medical clinics. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case of emergency.

Getting to Troyes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Troyes

  • Paris Charles de Gaulle International Airport: The largest airport in France, Charles de Gaulle is located approximately 150 kilometers from Troyes.
  • Paris Orly Airport: Located 200 kilometers from Troyes, Orly is another option for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Troyes

  • Troyes Bus Station: Located in the city center, the Troyes Bus Station connects with several cities throughout France.

Other Options

  • Train: Troyes has a train station that connects with several cities throughout France.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the nearby countryside and vineyards.

How to Get Around in Troyes

Troyes is a small city, and most attractions can be easily reached on foot. However, if you’re planning to explore the nearby countryside or vineyards, renting a car or a bike is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Troyes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Reims: A short drive from Troyes, Reims is a beautiful city that’s known for its impressive cathedral and delicious champagne.
  • Burgundy: Just a few hours’ drive from Troyes, Burgundy is another region of France that’s famous for its wine and charming medieval villages.
  • Paris: Troyes is located just a few hours from Paris, making it a great side trip for those visiting the City of Light.

Conclusion

Troyes is a hidden gem in the Grand Est region of France, with something to offer everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor activities, or delicious food and wine, Troyes is sure to charm you with its unique blend of old-world charm and modern sophistication. We hope that this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Troyes. Bon voyage!
