Introduction to Voiron

Voiron is a charming town situated in the Isère region of France, in the picturesque Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes area. Surrounded by lush greenery, mountains, and valleys, this town offers an unmatched natural landscape, peppered with historical monuments and modern attractions. If you are planning a trip to France, Voiron is a must-visit destination that offers something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Voiron

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château de Voiron: The Château de Voiron, also known as the Museum of Vourey, is a splendid example of architecture from the 16th century. It houses a museum that showcases the town’s history through exhibits, including artifacts, sculptures, and paintings.
  • Musée Mainssieux: Musée Mainssieux is a museum and library dedicated to the history of Voiron. The museum features a collection of art, photographs, manuscripts, and documents from the region.
  • La Maison des Allouettes: La Maison des Allouettes, translated as “the house of larks,” is a 17th-century mansion known for its ornate façade.

Natural Attractions of Voiron: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lac de Paladru: A popular spot for water-based adventures, Lac de Paladru is a beautiful lake surrounded by rolling hills and lush greenery. It’s an ideal place to swim, fish, boat, and relax.
  • Le Grand Ratz: Le Grand Ratz is a vast natural park that offers opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and picnicking. The park features stunning natural rock formations and waterfalls.
  • Lavender Fields: Voiron is known for its scenic lavender fields. During the peak season, visitors can witness the vibrant purple fields that cover the town’s hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Voiron

  • Voie verte: Voie verte is a scenic cycling and hiking path that runs between Voiron and La Murette. It passes through picturesque countryside and quaint French villages, making it an ideal route for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Le sentier du Patrimoine: Le sentier du Patrimoine, or the Heritage Path, is a hiking trail that connects Voiron to the neighboring villages of Vourey and Saint-Jean-de-Moirans. The trail winds its way through the lush green hills, featuring stunning panoramic views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Voiron: Activities and Tours

  • Chocolate Tour (Chartreuse Chocolatier): Voiron is known for its mouth-watering chocolates, and taking a chocolate tour with Chartreuse Chocolatier is a must-do activity for all chocolate lovers. The tour takes visitors through the chocolate-making process and lets them taste the finished product.
  • Fishing and Boating on Lake Paladru: Rent a boat or go fishing at Lake Paladru, a stunning lake in the heart of Voiron. Enjoy the fresh air and picturesque scenery while spending quality time with your loved ones.
  • Family-Friendly Fun at Argila Café Pottery: Spend an afternoon at Argila Café Pottery, where visitors can make and paint their pottery creations and enjoy snacks and refreshments afterward.

Food and Drink Scene of Voiron

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mousse de Fromages: Mousse de Fromages is a light and airy cheese mousse made with Swiss cheese, herbs, and cream. It is an excellent appetizer or addition to any meal.
  • Galette de Saint-Genix: Galette de Saint-Genix is a traditional sweet pastry made from brioche dough, raspberry coulis, and almond cream. This buttery and flaky dessert is a local favorite and a must-try for anyone visiting Voiron.
  • Truite du Vercors: Truite du Vercors is a dish of locally caught trout served with Provençal herbs, tomatoes, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Chartreuse: Chartreuse is a herbal liqueur made by Carthusian Monks in the nearby Chartreuse Mountains. It can be enjoyed as a digestif or as an ingredient in cocktails.
  • Vin de Savoie: Vin de Savoie is a popular wine produced in the French Alps. Voiron’s proximity to the region makes it an ideal place to sample the wine.

Where to Stay in Voiron: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kyriad Voiron Chartreuse: Kyriad Voiron Chartreuse is a modern hotel located in the heart of the town. It features comfortable rooms and on-site dining.
  • Château des Ayes: Château des Ayes is a romantic guesthouse set in a historic building. The rooms are luxuriously furnished with period antiques, and guests can enjoy a complimentary breakfast each morning.
  • Gîte Le Chant de L’eau: Gîte Le Chant de L’eau is a charming guesthouse located just outside of Voiron. The rooms are cozy and comfortable, and guests can enjoy a continental breakfast in the garden.

What to Buy in Voiron: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Voiron Chocolates: Voiron is known for its delicious chocolates, and visitors should not leave without sampling some of the region’s finest chocolates.
  • Chartreuse: Chartreuse is a traditional herbal liqueur made exclusively by the Carthusian Monks. Bring home a bottle as a unique souvenir from your visit to Voiron.
  • Lavender Products: Voiron is home to some of the most beautiful lavender fields, and visitors can bring home some lavender-based products such as lotions, soaps, or sachets as a refreshing reminder of their trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Voiron

Voiron is a town steeped in history and local traditions. Visitors should respect local customs and etiquette, such as greeting people with a “Bonjour” and using “vous” instead of “tu” when addressing strangers or elders. Tipping is not mandatory but always appreciated in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Voiron

The best time to visit Voiron is from May through September when the weather is mild and dry, and the region’s charm is fully visible. This period is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Voiron

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s numerous cultural attractions and historical sites.
  • Do take a bike ride or hike along one of the many scenic trails.
  • Do bring home some of the region’s delicious chocolates as a unique souvenir.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the town’s natural beauty. Be sure to explore the nearby lakes and valleys.
  • Don’t rush the experience. Take your time to enjoy the town’s charming ambiance fully.

Safety and Health

Safety in Voiron

Voiron is a safe town. Visitors should exercise normal precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Voiron

France has a world-class healthcare system. In case of an emergency, visitors should call 15 (emergency medical services), 17 (emergency police services), or 18 (emergency fire services).

Getting to Voiron: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Voiron conveniently through several travel options listed below.

Airports Near Voiron

  • Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport: Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport, located about 60 km from Voiron, is the nearest major airport. The airport serves several international carriers and provides access to many domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Voiron

  • Voiron Bus Station: Voiron Bus Station is served by several regional bus operators, including Transisère. It provides connections to major towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Voiron is well-connected by rail, and visitors can reach the town via trains from major cities such as Grenoble, Lyon, and Chambéry.
  • Car Hire: Visitors can hire a car to reach Voiron. Several car rental companies operate from airports and rail stations.

How to Get Around in Voiron

Visitors can easily explore the town on foot or by bike. Taxis are available for hire, and several bus operators provide services from the town to the nearby villages.

Where to Go Next from Voiron: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grenoble: Grenoble, located about 30 km from Voiron, is a picturesque town in the French Alps. It is known for its historical landmarks, museums, and art galleries.
  • Chambéry: Chambéry is a charming town located 50 km from Voiron. It is a town with historic quarters and features such as Castle of the Dukes of Savoy and Equestrian statue of Boigne.
  • Lyon: Lyon, situated about 90 km west of Voiron, is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its historical architecture, museums, and fantastic cuisine.

Conclusion

Voiron is a magnificent town situated in the heart of France. From natural landscapes to historical landmarks, it offers a range of activities and attractions to fit every traveler’s interests. Whether you want to relax and enjoy the town’s ambiance or you want to explore the surrounding wilderness, Voiron has something for every visitor. Plan your trip today and experience this charming town for yourself!
