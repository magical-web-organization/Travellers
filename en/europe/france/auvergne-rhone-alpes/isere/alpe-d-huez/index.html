Introduction to Alpe d’Huez

Located in the heart of the French Alps, Alpe d’Huez is a world-renowned ski resort that also offers a wealth of summer activities for travellers. Famous for its ski slopes, it attracts thousands of visitors each year to the area, which offers stunning natural beauty along with a fascinating mix of cultural and historic attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Alpe d’Huez

When you’re planning a trip to Alpe d’Huez, you’ll want to make sure you know what to do and where to go. Here are some of the best places to visit in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée d’Huez et de l’Oisans - A historical museum that provides a fascinating insight into the local culture and history. You’ll learn about the development of Alpe d’Huez as a ski resort, as well as the area’s agricultural and mining past.

  • Fort de l’Olive - A military fort built in the late 1800s, which played a key role in protecting the region during the First and Second World Wars. Explore its tunnels and gun emplacements, and enjoy fantastic views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Alpe d’Huez: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pic Blanc - The highest peak in the Alpe d’Huez ski area, it offers panoramic views of the mountains and surrounding valleys. Take the cable car to the top and enjoy skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and paragliding.

  • Lac Besson - A scenic lake nestled in the mountains, Lac Besson is a popular destination for hiking and mountain biking in the summer, and cross-country skiing and snowshoeing in the winter.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alpe d’Huez

Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a beginner looking for an outdoor adventure, Alpe d’Huez offers a range of trails to explore. From easy strolls to challenging routes, you’re sure to find something that suits your interests and fitness level.

Some popular routes include:

  • The La Fare Trail - A scenic route that takes you through the forests and meadows of the La Fare area, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • The Sarenne Trail - The longest black run in the world, it’s a challenging route that offers breathtaking views of the valley and surrounding peaks.

Best Things to Do in Alpe d’Huez: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Alpe d’Huez, you won’t be disappointed. The area is home to a wealth of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Skiing and Snowboarding - Alpe d’Huez is one of the top ski resorts in the world, with over 250 km of ski slopes.

  • Paragliding - Take in the stunning views of the mountains and valleys from a bird’s eye view.

  • Ice skating - A great activity for families, Alpe d’Huez offers several rinks for outdoor ice-skating.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alpe d’Huez

If you’re planning a trip to Alpe d’Huez, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Musée d’Huez et de l’Oisans and explore the town.

Day 2-3: Enjoy skiing and snowboarding on the slopes.

Day 4: Take a cable car to Pic Blanc and enjoy the scenic views. Return to the town and visit some of the local shops and cafes.

Day 5: Go ice-skating in the morning and take a guided tour of the Fort de l’Olive in the afternoon.

Day 6-7: Hike the Sarenne or La Fare trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Alpe d’Huez

When it comes to food and drink, Alpe d’Huez offers a range of delicious options that reflect the local culture and cuisine. From hearty mountain dishes to sweet desserts, there’s something for everyone.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fondue - A popular cheese-based dish enjoyed by locals and tourists alike, it’s a great way to warm up after a day on the slopes.

  • Tartiflette - A potato gratin dish that features Reblochon cheese, bacon, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Chartreuse - A herbal liqueur made by monks in the nearby Chartreuse Mountains.

  • Beaufort - A cow’s milk cheese that pairs well with red wine.

Where to Stay in Alpe d’Huez: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alpe d’Huez offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels and chalets. Here are some recommended options:

Best Time to Visit Alpe d’Huez

The best time to visit Alpe d’Huez depends on your interests and activities. For skiing and snowboarding, the winter months are the best, and for hiking and outdoor activities, the summer is ideal.

Safety and Health

When visiting Alpe d’Huez, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Alpe d’Huez

  • Always wear proper safety gear when engaging in outdoor activities.

  • Follow the guidelines and safety warnings issued by local authorities and activity providers.

Health in Alpe d’Huez

  • Altitude sickness can be a concern for some visitors. Be aware of the symptoms and take precautions if necessary.

  • Travel insurance is recommended to cover any unexpected medical issues.

Getting to Alpe d’Huez: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alpe d’Huez is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Alpe d’Huez

Grenoble-Isère Airport is the closest airport to Alpe d’Huez, located approximately 100 km away. Other options include Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport and Geneva International Airport, both of which are over 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Alpe d’Huez

The Transaltitude bus service operates daily between Grenoble train station and Alpe d’Huez, with several stops along the way.

Other Options

Driving is also a popular option, with car rentals available at all major airports and train stations.

How to Get Around in Alpe d’Huez

The best way to get around Alpe d’Huez is by car or public transportation. Local bus services run throughout the town, from the ski lifts to the museums and restaurants.

Where to Go Next from Alpe d’Huez: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the French Alps, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Les Deux Alpes - Another popular ski resort with a lively après-ski scene.

  • Grenoble - A vibrant university town with a rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an avid skier, a lover of the great outdoors, or simply looking for a unique cultural experience, Alpe d’Huez has something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, fascinating history, and diverse range of activities and attractions, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveller to France.
