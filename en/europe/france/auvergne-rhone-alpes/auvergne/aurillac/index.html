Are you looking for a unique travel experience in France that isn’t overrun with tourists? Then Aurillac might be the perfect destination for you. This charming town in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region of southern France is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. From its impressive museums and historical landmarks to its picturesque hiking trails and delicious local cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Aurillac.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, eat and drink, where to stay, local customs and more, so you can make the most of your trip to Aurillac.

Introduction to Aurillac

Located in the heart of the Cantal department, Aurillac is a small town with a big personality. Founded in the 3rd century, Aurillac has a rich history that can still be seen in its well-preserved medieval center. The town is famous for its production of Romanesque art, wine, cheese, and sausage. The locals, known as “Aurillacois,” are warm and welcoming, making it easy for visitors to feel at home.

List of Places to Visit in Aurillac

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most impressive museums of Aurillac is the Musée d’art et d’archéologie d’Aurillac which is home to a huge collection of Romanesque art. The museum is located in a 17th-century private mansion and exhibits collections on the archaeology, ethnography, and fine arts of the Cantal region. A trip to the Château Saint-Etienne is also highly recommended. This ancient fortification houses a museum of Aurillac’s history and houses a vast collection of documents and objects. At the Aurelie Museum, visitors can learn about the town’s famous celebrity, actress and singer, Aurelie Dupont.

Natural Attractions of Aurillac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Massif Central mountain range is visible from Aurillac and provides a dramatic backdrop to the town. There are many stunning natural wonders to explore in the surrounding area such as the Puy Mary mountain and the Gorges de la Jordanne.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aurillac

Aurillac is also a great destination for hikers and bikers. Some popular trails of Aurillac include the GR 400, the Tour des Monts du Cantal, and the GR 4 through the Cantal department.

Best Things to Do in Aurillac: Activities and Tours

Aurillac has plenty of activities to keep visitors entertained, ranging from water sports such as canoeing and kayaking on the nearby lakes, to hot air balloon rides over the picturesque region of Auvergne.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aurillac

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Musée d’art et d’archéologie d’Aurillac
  • Day 2: Take a trip to the nearby town of Salers, famous for its cheese and picturesque medieval center
  • Day 3: Explore the Château Saint-Etienne and its museum of Aurillac’s history
  • Day 4: Hike the beautiful Puy Mary mountain
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride over Auvergne
  • Day 6: Go canoeing or kayaking on one of the nearby lakes
  • Day 7: Enjoy a day of relaxation at a local spa

Food and Drink Scene of Aurillac

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine of Aurillac is hearty and delicious, featuring dishes such as truffade, a traditional dish made from potatoes and cheese, and aligot, a mixture of mashed potatoes and melted cheese. The town is also famous for its sausages and cheeses, which are a must-try for any foodie.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Aurillac should try one of the local wines such as Cotes d’Auvergne or one of the many locally brewed beers like Saint-Flour Blanche.

Where to Stay in Aurillac: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aurillac has a wide variety of accommodations to suit all budgets, ranging from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels such as Hotel Le Square, which is located in the heart of the old town.

What to Buy in Aurillac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Aurillac, pick up some locally made souvenirs such as ceramics, lacework, and handmade leather goods. The town’s famous cheeses and sausages are also excellent gifts to bring home to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Aurillac

Aurillac is a friendly and authentic town that welcomes visitors with open arms. To show respect to the locals, it’s important to say “Bonjour” when entering a store and “Au revoir” when leaving. During festivals and holidays, it’s common to see locals dressed in traditional costumes to celebrate.

Best Time to Visit Aurillac

The best time to visit Aurillac is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aurillac

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Visit the historic landmarks and museums.
  • Explore the beautiful natural landscapes.
  • Embrace the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Forget to say “Bonjour” and “Au revoir” when entering/exiting a store.
  • Arrive late to meals.
  • Get too loud in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aurillac

Aurillac is a safe destination and visitors shouldn’t be worried about their safety during their stay. However, as with any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when necessary.

Health in Aurillac

Visitors to Aurillac should ensure they have comprehensive health insurance that covers medical emergencies and accidents. Check with your doctor to see if any vaccinations are required before traveling.

Getting to Aurillac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aurillac

The closest airport to Aurillac is Aurillac-Tronquières Airport, but many visitors will find it more convenient to fly into the larger nearby cities of Clermont-Ferrand or Toulouse.

Bus Stations Near Aurillac

There are several bus stations in Aurillac that connect the town with nearby cities such as Rodez and Toulouse.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Aurillac by train. The town has its own train station, which is serviced by regional trains.

How to Get Around in Aurillac

One of the best ways to see Aurillac is on foot or by bike. The town’s compact size makes it easy to explore on foot, while the surrounding countryside is perfect for biking. Visitors can also use the local bus service to get around.

Where to Go Next from Aurillac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aurillac’s central location makes it an ideal base for exploring other parts of the region such as the scenic beauty of the Monts du Cantal, the city of Rodez, or the picturesque riverside town of Saint-Flour.

Conclusion

Aurillac is a charming and authentic French town that offers visitors a unique travel experience. From its fascinating history and culture to its natural beauty, there’s something for everyone in Aurillac. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to this beautiful corner of France.
