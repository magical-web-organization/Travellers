Introduction to Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand is situated in the heart of France and is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, stunning architecture, and beautiful natural landscapes. The city is located in Auvergne, in the region of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, and is home to over 140,000 people.

Often overshadowed by its larger and more popular counterparts, Clermont-Ferrand is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. From its famous volcanic mountain to its charming old town, the city has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at Clermont-Ferrand and its many attractions, activities, and amenities to help you plan your trip to this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Clermont-Ferrand

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history, culture, and art, then Clermont-Ferrand has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained. Here are some of the top museums and historical places in the city:

  • Cathedral of Clermont-Ferrand: This Gothic cathedral is one of the city’s most iconic landmarks and is known for its black lava stone exterior and stunning stained-glass windows. The cathedral dates back to the 13th century and is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.

  • Roger Quilliot Art Museum: This modern art museum is located in the historic Hôtel de Ville and features collections of contemporary, modern, and classical art. The museum is home to more than 15,000 pieces of art and is a great place to spend a few hours exploring.

  • L’Aventure Michelin Museum: This museum is dedicated to the Michelin tire company and showcases the history and evolution of the brand. Visitors can see vintage advertisements, old machinery, and interactive exhibits that bring the story of Michelin to life.

  • Basilique Notre Dame du Port: This Romanesque church is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and dates back to the 11th century. The church is known for its beautiful portal, which features intricate carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Clermont-Ferrand: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Clermont-Ferrand is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, from its picturesque parks and gardens to its stunning volcanic mountain. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the city:

  • Puy de Dôme: This iconic volcanic mountain is one of the city’s most famous landmarks and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area. Visitors can take a train, hike, or bike to the top, where they will be rewarded with breathtaking scenery.

  • Jardin Lecoq: This peaceful park is located in the heart of the city and is known for its beautiful flowers, fountains, and sculptures. The park is a great place to relax, have a picnic and soak up some sun.

  • Parc des Sports Marcel Michelin: This park is home to the ASM Clermont Auvergne rugby team and is a must-visit destination for sports fans. Visitors can watch a game, tour the stadium, or shop for merchandise at the team store.

  • Natural Regional Park of the Volcanoes of Auvergne: This beautiful protected area is located just outside of the city and is home to a wide variety of wildlife and natural wonders. Visitors can hike, bike, or drive through the park and explore its many trails and scenic overlooks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand and its surrounding areas offer a plethora of hiking and biking options for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top trails in the area:

  • Puy de Dôme Trail: This challenging hiking trail takes visitors to the top of the iconic Puy de Dôme mountain, where they can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • La Voie Verte: This 45-kilometer trail is an old railway line that has been converted into a scenic biking route. The trail passes through forests, fields, and small towns and is a great way to explore the countryside.

  • Le Mont-Dore Trail: This moderate hiking trail takes visitors through the beautiful Monts-Dore mountain range and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Puy de Sancy Trail: This challenging hiking trail takes visitors to the summit of the highest mountain in the Massif Central range and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Clermont-Ferrand: Activities and Tours

Clermont-Ferrand has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the top things to do in the city:

  • Hot-Air Balloon Ride: This scenic tour takes visitors high above the city and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. The tour includes a champagne toast and is a must-do activity for anyone visiting Clermont-Ferrand.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Clermont-Ferrand is located in the heart of France’s wine country, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries to explore in the area. Visitors can take a guided wine tasting tour of the local vineyards and sample some of the region’s finest wines.

  • Historic Walking Tour: This guided walking tour takes visitors through the city’s historic old town and showcases some of its most famous landmarks and monuments. Visitors can learn about the city’s rich history and culture while enjoying a leisurely stroll through its streets and alleys.

  • Volcano Experience: This interactive museum is located at the base of Puy de Dôme and offers visitors a chance to learn about the region’s volcanic history. The museum features interactive exhibits, multimedia displays, and a 4D theater that brings the story of the volcano to life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clermont-Ferrand

If you’re planning to visit Clermont-Ferrand for a week, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the city:

Day 1: Visit the Cathedral of Clermont-Ferrand and explore the historic old town.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the nearby village of Montpeyroux and explore its charming streets and vineyards.

Day 3: Visit the Roger Quilliot Art Museum and spend the afternoon exploring the Jardin Lecoq.

Day 4: Take a hot-air balloon ride over the city and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.

Day 5: Explore the Parc des Sports Marcel Michelin and catch a rugby game.

Day 6: Hike to the top of Puy de Dôme and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Day 7: Take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards and sample some of the region’s finest wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Clermont-Ferrand

No trip to France is complete without indulging in some delicious local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Clermont-Ferrand:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Potée Auvergnate: This hearty stew is made with a variety of meats, vegetables, and beans and is a staple of the local cuisine.

  • Truffade: This cheesy dish is made with sliced potatoes, garlic, and Tomme cheese and is a must-try for cheese lovers.

  • Puy lentils: These small, flavorful lentils are grown in the nearby village of Le Puy and are a staple of the local cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Auvergne wine: This region is known for its delicious red and white wines, which are made from the Gamay and Pinot Noir grape varieties.

  • Volvic water: This natural spring water is bottled in the nearby town of Volvic and is known for its purity and freshness.

Where to Stay in Clermont-Ferrand: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clermont-Ferrand has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the top places to stay in the city:

  • Best Western Plus Hôtel Gergovie: This modern hotel is located in the heart of the city and features comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.

  • The Five Elements Hostel: This eco-friendly hostel is located in the historic old town and offers affordable accommodations in a friendly and welcoming atmosphere.

  • Villa Pascaline: This charming guesthouse is located in a quiet residential neighborhood and features beautiful gardens, comfortable rooms, and excellent customer service.

What to Buy in Clermont-Ferrand: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home from Clermont-Ferrand. Here are some of the best things to buy in the city:

  • Michelin tires and merchandise: The region is home to the famous Michelin tire company, and visitors can buy a range of Michelin-related souvenirs and merchandise.

  • Puy lentils: These small, tasty lentils are a staple of the local cuisine and make a great gift or souvenir to bring home.

  • Local wine: The region is known for its delicious wines, and visitors can buy bottles of red, white, and rosé to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand is a friendly and welcoming city that is proud of its rich history and culture. Here are some local customs and traditions to be aware of during your visit:

  • Bisous greeting: In Clermont-Ferrand, it is customary to greet someone with a kiss on each cheek.

  • Public transportation etiquette: When using public transportation, it is customary to offer your seat to someone who is elderly, pregnant, or disabled.

  • Wine culture: Wine is a central part of the local culture, and it is customary to enjoy a glass or two with meals and social gatherings.

Best Time to Visit Clermont-Ferrand

The best time to visit Clermont-Ferrand is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. The city can get crowded during this time, so it’s important to book your accommodation and tours in advance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Avoid dark and deserted areas at night: Stick to well-lit and populated areas when walking around the city at night.

  • Be aware of pickpockets: Keep a close eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings when walking around the city.

  • Follow traffic laws: If you’re driving in the city, be sure to obey traffic laws and drive safely.

Health in Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand has good healthcare facilities and services, but visitors should still take precautions to stay healthy during their stay. Here are some health tips to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Stay hydrated: The city can get quite hot during the summer months, so be sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.

Getting to Clermont-Ferrand: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clermont-Ferrand is well-connected to the rest of France and Europe and can be easily reached by plane, train, or bus. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider when planning your visit:

Airports Near Clermont-Ferrand

The city has its own airport, Clermont-Ferrand Auvergne Airport, which is located just 6 kilometers from the city center. The airport is served by several airlines, including Air France, Ryanair, and EasyJet.

Bus Stations Near Clermont-Ferrand

The city’s main bus station is Gare Routière de Clermont-Ferrand and is located in the city center. The station is served by several bus companies, including FlixBus and Eurolines.

Other Options

If you’re traveling by train, the city has two train stations: Gare de Clermont-Ferrand and Gare de Clermont-La Rotonde. The stations are served by several national and regional train lines, including the TGV.

How to Get Around in Clermont-Ferrand

Clermont-Ferrand is a small city that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. The city also has an efficient public transportation system that includes buses and trams. Visitors can purchase tickets from kiosks or vending machines located at stops and stations.

Where to Go Next from Clermont-Ferrand: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clermont-Ferrand is a great base for exploring the surrounding area, which includes charming villages, stunning natural landscapes, and a variety of cultural and historic attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Montpellier: This vibrant city is located just two hours by train from Clermont-Ferrand and is known for its beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and lively nightlife.

  • Vulcania: This science and theme park is located just outside of Clermont-Ferrand and is dedicated to the study of volcanoes and geology.

  • Château de la Batisse: This beautiful castle is located just 30 minutes from Clermont-Ferrand and offers guided tours and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
