Introduction to Mont Dore

Nestled in the heart of the Auvergne region in central France, Mont Dore is a charming town famous for its stunning natural landscapes and diverse outdoor activities. Located in the Puy-de-Dôme department of the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region, Mont Dore is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a relaxing break in the midst of breathtaking mountains and picturesque valleys.

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of Mont Dore, covering the best places to visit, must-try food and drink, accommodation options, and transportation alternatives, among other things.

List of Places to Visit in Mont Dore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mont Dore offers a rich cultural heritage that reflects the region’s history and traditions. Some of the top cultural attractions to visit include:

  • La Maison du Tourisme - This museum houses a collection of exhibits that showcase the history, culture, and traditions of the Auvergne region.

  • Eglise Saint-Roch - This historical church boasts beautiful Gothic architecture and is home to some of the most stunning stained-glass windows in the region.

  • Thermes Mont Dore - This spa complex has been a favorite destination for tourists and locals alike since the 19th century. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of spa treatments, including thermal baths, massages, and body wraps.

Natural Attractions of Mont Dore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mont Dore is surrounded by a natural wonderland known for its spectacular views, pristine lakes, and lush forests. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Puy de Sancy - This magnificent volcanic peak stands at an altitude of over 6,000 feet and is the highest mountain in the Massif Central. Visitors can hike to the summit or take a cable car from Mont Dore to enjoy stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Lac de Guery - Surrounded by the picturesque forests of the Massif du Sancy, this stunning lake is a peaceful oasis where visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, and hiking.

  • Cascade Le Queureuilh - This beautiful waterfall is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery and is the perfect spot for a refreshing dip in the water on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mont Dore

Mont Dore has a variety of hiking and biking trails that cater to different skill levels. Some of the top-rated routes include:

  • GR30 - This trail leads through the heart of the Auvergne region and offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside. It’s a challenging 105-mile trek that is ideal for experienced hikers.

  • Puy de Sancy Loop - This easy 6.5-mile trail takes hikers around the stunning Puy de Sancy mountain, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Col de la Croix Morand - This moderate trail takes bikers to the highest point of the Dore Mountains and offers stunning views of the valley below.

Best Things to Do in Mont Dore: Activities and Tours

Mont Dore has a range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy, including:

  • Paragliding - Soar high above the mountains and enjoy stunning panoramic views on a heart-pumping paragliding adventure.

  • Horseback Riding - Explore the surrounding countryside on horseback and experience the natural beauty of the region.

  • Skiing - During the winter months, visitors can hit the slopes and enjoy world-class skiing in the Massif du Sancy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mont Dore

Day 1: Explore the town center, visit the tourist office and learn about the region’s history and culture.

Day 2: Visit La Maison du Tourisme and discover the local traditions and customs.

Day 3: Take a cable car to the summit of Puy de Sancy, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 4: Visit Lac de Guery and enjoy a range of outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, or hiking.

Day 5: Take a spa day at the Thermes Mont Dore and enjoy a range of spa treatments.

Day 6: Enjoy an adventurous activity like paragliding, horseback riding, or skiing.

Day 7: Visit the Eglise Saint-Roch and learn about the region’s religious heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Mont Dore

Must-Try Dishes

Mont Dore has a range of local dishes that visitors must try, such as:

  • Truffade - A dish made with potatoes, cheese, and garlic that is typically served with bacon and sausage.

  • Pounti - A savory cake made with leftover bread, pork, and vegetables.

  • Aligot - A creamy dish made with potatoes, cheese, and garlic, often served with charcuterie and salad.

Local Beverages

Mont Dore is famous for its local liqueurs and wines, such as:

  • Gentiane - A herbal liquor made from a flowering plant of the same name that grows in the region.

  • Vin de Pays d’Auvergne - A range of red, white, and rosé wines made from locally grown grape varieties.

Where to Stay in Mont Dore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mont Dore has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences, including:

  • Le Caprice des Neiges - A boutique hotel located near the town center, offering comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

  • Les Camélias - A guest house that offers stunning mountain views, delicious breakfast, and excellent service.

  • Le Monteillet - A charming hostel located close to the ski slopes and offering affordable accommodation for solo travelers and group adventures.

What to Buy in Mont Dore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mont Dore has several local products that visitors can take home as souvenirs, such as:

  • Cheese - The Auvergne region is famous for its variety of cheese, including Saint-Nectaire, Cantal, and Bleu d’Auvergne.

  • Pottery - Visitors can find a range of unique ceramics and pottery made by local craftsmen and women that reflect the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  • Gentiane Liqueur - Take home a bottle of this delicious herbal liquor made from the gentian plant that grows in abundance in the Auvergne region.

Local Culture and Customs of Mont Dore

Mont Dore has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the region’s history and traditions. Some of the cultural customs and traditions to observe include:

  • Language - French is the official language, but visitors can also hear the local dialect known as Auvergnat, which has its own unique expressions and turns of phrase.

  • Food - The local cuisine is known for its hearty, rustic dishes made with locally sourced ingredients.

  • Festivals - Mont Dore hosts several annual festivals and events, including the Festival of Saint Roch, which celebrates the town’s patron saint.

Best Time to Visit Mont Dore

The best time to visit Mont Dore is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The winter months (December to February) are also a great time to visit, especially for skiing enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mont Dore

Do:

  • Do explore the surrounding natural beauty by hiking, biking, or horseback riding.

  • Do try the local dishes and beverages and experience the region’s culinary heritage.

  • Do attend the local festivals and cultural events that showcase the region’s history and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave without taking a thermal bath at the Thermes Mont Dore.

  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing if you’re visiting during the winter months.

  • Don’t neglect to explore the charming town center, which boasts an array of shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mont Dore

Mont Dore is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid poorly lit or deserted areas at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight to prevent theft.

Health in Mont Dore

Mont Dore offers excellent medical facilities and services. However, visitors should take the following precautions to avoid health-related issues:

  • Carry a sufficient supply of any necessary medications.
  • Use sunscreen to protect against sunburn, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Mont Dore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mont Dore

The nearest airports to Mont Dore are:

  • Clermont-Ferrand Auvergne Airport - Located about 45 minutes by car from Mont Dore.

  • Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport - Located about 2.5 hours by car from Mont Dore.

Bus Stations Near Mont Dore

  • Clermont-Ferrand Bus Station - Located about 45 minutes by car from Mont Dore.

  • Issoire Bus Station - Located about 35 minutes by car from Mont Dore.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Mont Dore by train or car.

How to Get Around in Mont Dore

Mont Dore is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. Buses and taxis are also available for transportation within the town and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mont Dore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mont Dore is a great base to explore the surrounding area, and visitors can also take day trips to nearby destinations such as:

  • Vulcania - A unique theme park that explores the science and mysteries of volcanoes.

  • Clermont-Ferrand - The capital city of the Auvergne region that boasts a rich history and vibrant cultural scene.

  • Le Puy-en-Velay - A charming town famous for its religious heritage and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Mont Dore is an enchanting destination that offers visitors the chance to experience the best of the Auvergne region. From its stunning mountain ranges and natural landscapes to its rich cultural heritage and delicious cuisine, Mont Dore has something for everyone.
