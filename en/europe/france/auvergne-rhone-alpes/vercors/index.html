Introduction to Vercors

Nestled in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region of France, Vercors is a picturesque mountain range known for its breathtaking natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. With stunning vistas, endless outdoor activities, and charming villages, Vercors is an ideal destination for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts alike.

List of Places to Visit in Vercors

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Memorial of the Resistance
  • Vassieux-en-Vercors Memorial, Museum, and Cemetery
  • Prehistoric Museum of Vercors
  • Salt Mines of Grotte de Thais
  • Choranche Caves
  • Castle of Sassenage
  • Abbey of Léoncel

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vercors

Vercors boasts a range of stunning hiking and biking trails for all levels. Begin with an easy stroll in the lush forests or challenge yourself with a more rigorous mountain trek. Top trails include:

  • GR9: One of the most popular hikes in Vercors, this trail leads to the summit of Mont Aiguille.
  • Sentier du Facteur: This historic trail, once used by postmen, offers panoramic views of the Vercors valley.
  • VTT des Giboulées: A mountain biking trail that winds through stunning alpine scenery straight out of a fairy tale.

Best Things to Do in Vercors: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding over the mountains
  • Rock climbing at the Cirque d’Archiane
  • Skiing and snowboarding in the winter months
  • Canyoning in the Gorges de la Bourne
  • Horseback riding through the forests
  • Hot air balloon rides over the Vercors plateau
  • Guided tours of traditional cheese farms

Week-Long Itinerary for Vercors

Day 1: Explore the charming village of Pont-en-Royans and the nearby Gorges de la Bourne. Day 2: Trek to the summit of Mont Aiguille and marvel at the stunning alpine views. Day 3: Visit the Memorial of the Resistance and learn about Vercors’s role in the WWII resistance movement. Day 4: Take a day-trip to the nearby towns of Grenoble and Valence for some urban exploration. Day 5: Discover Vassieux-en-Vercors and the memorial, museum, and cemetery dedicated to those lost in a WWII massacre. Day 6: Enjoy a day of skiing or snowboarding in the winter months or embark on a leisurely hike in the Vercors Regional Natural Park during the warmer seasons. Day 7: Take a guided tour of a local cheese farm and sample some of the region’s famous fromage.

Local Beverages

Local Culture and Customs of Vercors

Vercors is known for its strong sense of community and rich cultural heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and there is a strong emphasis on protecting and preserving the natural environment. Visitors are encouraged to respect the region’s cultural traditions and natural resources during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Vercors

The best times to visit Vercors are during the spring and summer months (May-September) when the weather is mild and the wildflowers are in bloom. However, the winter season (December-March) also offers exciting outdoor activities such as skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vercors

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take advantage of the hiking and biking trails
  • Sample some of the local cuisine
  • Enjoy the stunning alpine views

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or disturb the natural environment
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities
  • Ignore the region’s rich cultural heritage

Safety and Health

Safety in Vercors

While Vercors is generally a safe destination, visitors are advised to take basic safety precautions such as locking car doors and avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Vercors

Visitors to Vercors are advised to check with their healthcare provider regarding recommended vaccinations and other health precautions prior to travel. It is also important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen during outdoor activities.

Getting to Vercors: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vercors

The closest airports to Vercors are Grenoble-Isere Airport and Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport.

Bus Stations Near Vercors

Buses run regularly from Grenoble to Vercors, with stops in villages such as Villard-de-Lans and Lans-en-Vercors.

Other Options

Driving a car is a popular option for getting around Vercors, as it allows for more flexibility in exploring the region.

How to Get Around in Vercors

The best way to explore Vercors is on foot, bike, or car. There are also local buses that run between villages and towns in the region.

Where to Go Next from Vercors: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grenoble: A bustling city known for its rich history and culture
  • Chamonix-Mont-Blanc: A popular ski resort town with stunning views of Mont Blanc
  • Pont-en-Royans: A charming village with a famous hanging house and picturesque river views
  • Valence: A historic city with a thriving gastronomy scene

Conclusion

Vercors is a hidden gem in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region of France, with endless natural beauty and cultural richness to discover. From breathtaking mountain vistas to charming villages and must-try local cuisine, Vercors is a perfect destination for anyone seeking an unforgettable adventure.
