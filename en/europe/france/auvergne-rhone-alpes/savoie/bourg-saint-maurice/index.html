Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Bourg-Saint-Maurice, France

Bourg-Saint-Maurice is a picturesque commune located in the Tarentaise Valley of Savoie, in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region of France. This beautiful town is surrounded by stunning mountains and offers visitors a plethora of activities and attractions to explore. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a cultural buff, Bourg-Saint-Maurice won’t disappoint. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do and places to visit in Bourg-Saint-Maurice, accommodations, food and drink, and more.

Introduction to Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Bourg-Saint-Maurice is a popular ski destination and known for its proximity to Les Arcs resort. The town has a rich history and dates back to the Roman era. It’s a great place to explore cultural landmarks and indulge in outdoor activities. In the town center, visitors can marvel at the stunning works of art such as the Saint-Martin church and Chapel of Saint Grat. Nature lovers will appreciate the town’s natural surroundings and beautiful landscapes, including its rivers and dense forests.

List of Places to Visit in Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Musée des Costumes et des Traditions de Savoie - This museum hosts exhibitions that showcase the costumes and traditions of Savoie, including the traditional dress of women and men, furniture, and artifacts.
  2. Chapelle Saint Grat - Visitors can marvel at this stunning chapel that dates back to the 17th century. The chapel’s intricate carvings and beautifully painted ceiling are a testament to the talented artisans of the past.
  3. Saint-Martin Church - Located in the town center, this church is famous for its stunning architecture and intricate artwork on the walls and ceilings. Visitors can take in the rich history of the church while marveling at the stunning stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Bourg-Saint-Maurice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Aiguille Rouge - This peak is the highest in Les Arcs, rising 3,226 meters above sea level, and offers visitors a stunning panoramic view of the surroundings.
  2. Vanoise National Park - A great place for a day trip, Vanoise National Park is a protected area famous for its diverse wildlife, glaciers, and lakes. Visitors can explore the park’s numerous hiking and biking trails.
  3. Mont Pourri - This beautiful mountain peak offers visitors a chance to experience the area’s natural beauty while hiking or rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Bourg-Saint-Maurice boasts a plethora of hiking/biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the area. Here are some of the best:

  1. Chemin du Petit Bonheur - This easy trail takes visitors through forests and meadows and provides stunning views of Mont Blanc.
  2. Col du Palet - This intermediate hiking trail takes visitors through stunning glacier valleys, past crystal-clear streams, and provides a panoramic view of the area.
  3. La Route des Chapelles - This intermediate biking trail is a great way to explore the town’s rich cultural heritage. The trail takes visitors past the area’s stunning chapels and provides an opportunity to explore rural France.

Best Things to Do in Bourg-Saint-Maurice: Activities and Tours

  1. Skiing and Snowboarding - Bourg-Saint-Maurice is a popular winter ski destination and offers visitors a range of skiing experiences. Les Arcs ski resort offers a wide variety of terrains for skiers of all levels.
  2. Rafting and Kayaking - The area’s many rivers are an ideal location for water sports enthusiasts. Visitors can experience the excitement of river rafting and kayaking while taking in the stunning scenery.
  3. Wine Tasting Tours - The area is famous for its exquisite wines. Visitors can participate in wine tasting tours and learn about the winemaking process while enjoying a variety of delicious wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Visitors can easily enjoy a week-long itinerary in Bourg-Saint-Maurice. Here is a brief itinerary:

Day 1 - Explore the town center, including the Saint-Martin Church and Chapel of Saint Grat.

Day 2 - Hike along the Chemin du Petit Bonheur trail.

Day 3 - Explore Les Arcs ski resort.

Day 4 - Take a day trip to Vanoise National Park.

Day 5 - Enjoy a wine-tasting tour.

Day 6 - Take a rafting or kayaking trip down a local river.

Day 7 - Explore the Mont Pourri mountain peak hike.

Food and Drink Scene of Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Raclette - A traditional dish made with melted cheese, boiled potatoes, and cured meats.
  2. Tartiflette - A hearty potato and cheese bake, usually served with ham or bacon.
  3. Crozets - Small square-shaped pasta made from buckwheat or wheat flour, usually served with ham or cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Vin de Savoie - A delicious white wine with a fruity flavor that pairs well with local Savoie dishes.
  2. Génépi - An herbal liqueur made from the flowers of the Artemisia plant, It is a traditional aperitif that’s usually served after dinner.

Where to Stay in Bourg-Saint-Maurice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hôtel Arolla - This cozy hotel is famous among tourists for its friendly staff, traditional Alpine décor, and delicious French breakfast.
  2. Hôtel Mercure Les Arcs 1800 - A modern hotel located in Les Arcs is perfect for those who want to enjoy the ski resort’s many activities.

What to Buy in Bourg-Saint-Maurice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Homemade Jams - Local farmers and artisans make excellent homemade jams using locally sourced berries.
  2. Cheese - Savoie is famous for its cheese, and visitors can’t leave without trying Beaufort, Abondance, or Tomme de Savoie.
  3. Honey - The area’s honey is famous for its unique flavor, and visitors can’t leave town without bringing home a jar.

Local Culture and Customs of Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Bourg-Saint-Maurice has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to explore architecture and traditions of the area. The town is known for its patron saint, Saint-Martin, and holds an annual festival in his honor.

Best Time to Visit Bourg-Saint-Maurice

The best time to visit Bourg-Saint-Maurice is during summer (June to September), when the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Do:

  1. Do try the local food and wines.
  2. Do try outdoor activities such as skiing and hiking.
  3. Do interact with the locals and learn about their rich cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t ignore the area’s safety guidelines for outdoor activities.
  2. Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothes and gear for hiking and skiing.
  3. Don’t forget your travel insurance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Bourg-Saint-Maurice is a safe town, but visitors should always take necessary precautions when indulging in outdoor activities. Visitors are advised to follow safety guidelines for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.

Health in Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Visitors should carry their prescription medication and consult their physician before traveling to Bourg-Saint-Maurice. The town has an excellent healthcare system, but visitors should always carry their health insurance.

Getting to Bourg-Saint-Maurice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bourg-Saint-Maurice

The nearest airports to Bourg-Saint-Maurice are Chambéry-Savoie Airport (69 km) and Geneva Airport (149 km).

Bus Stations Near Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Bourg-Saint-Maurice is well connected to other towns and cities by bus. The town has a bus station that provides regular services to the nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Bourg-Saint-Maurice by train. The town has a train station that provides services to other cities in the region and France.

How to Get Around in Bourg-Saint-Maurice

Visitors can get around town easily on foot. For those who want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car is a great option.

Where to Go Next from Bourg-Saint-Maurice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Annecy - This beautiful town is famous for its stunning lake and medieval architecture.
  2. Chamonix - A famous ski destination with stunning views of Mont Blanc and other Alpine peaks.
  3. Geneva - A multinational city famous for its museums, cultural landmarks, and stunning architecture.
