Introduction to Savoie

Located in the southeastern region of France, Savoie boasts a rich history, stunning landscapes, and world-renowned cuisine. The region is known for its picturesque mountain ranges, historic towns and villages, and delicious local food. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure or a relaxing escape, Savoie is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Savoie

Savoie has a wealth of attractions to explore, from historic landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some must-visit sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chateau de Chambéry, Chambéry
  • Musée des Beaux-Arts, Chambery
  • La Chocolaterie de Puyricard, Aix-les-Bains
  • Musée Opinel, Savoie

Natural Attractions of Savoie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Trois Vallées ski resort
  • Aiguille du Midi, Chamonix
  • Lac du Bourget, Aix-les-Bains
  • Mont Blanc, Rhône-Alpes

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savoie

Savoie is a hiker’s paradise, with gorgeous trails for all experience levels. Here are some popular routes:

  • Tour du Mont Blanc
  • Col du Galibier
  • Tour du Beaufortain
  • Tour de la Vanoise

Best Things to Do in Savoie: Activities and Tours

  • Wine and cheese tasting
  • Hot air balloon rides
  • Paragliding
  • River rafting

Week-Long Itinerary for Savoie

Day 1: Visit Chambery and explore the historic Chateau de Chambery

Day 2: Take a scenic drive to Mont Blanc and explore the nearby hiking trails

Day 3: Visit Albertville and tour the Olympic Museum

Day 4: Spend the day at Les Trois Vallées ski resort

Day 5: Explore the charming town of Annecy and take a boat tour of the Lake Annecy

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning landscapes of Savoie

Day 7: Visit Aix-les-Bains and relax in the thermal baths

Local Culture and Customs of Savoie

Savoie is steeped in rich culture and tradition. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  • Respect local dress codes in religious sites
  • Always greet locals with “Bonjour”
  • Try to speak some basic French

Best Time to Visit Savoie

The best time to visit Savoie is from December to March for winter sports, and from June to September for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savoie

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and drink
  • Learn some basic French
  • Respect local customs

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites
  • Leave without trying a local cheese

Safety and Health

Safety in Savoie

Savoie is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still keep the following safety tips in mind:

  • Keep valuable possessions secure
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas
  • Always follow instructions given by ski instructors

Health in Savoie

Savoie has excellent medical facilities, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions:

  • Stay hydrated and protect your skin from the sun
  • Check your health insurance policy for coverage in France
  • Bring any necessary prescription medication with you

Getting to Savoie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Savoie is easily accessible by plane, train, or bus.

Airports Near Savoie

  • Chambéry Airport
  • Geneva Airport
  • Lyon Airport

Bus Stations Near Savoie

  • Chambéry bus station
  • Grenoble Bus station
  • Annecy bus station

Other Options

  • Rent a car from Lyon or Geneva Airport
  • Take a taxi or shuttle from nearby airports

How to Get Around in Savoie

Savoie is well-connected with public transportation.

  • Bus: Savoie has an extensive bus network with regular services to various towns and villages
  • Train: The French national railway connects Savoie with the rest of the country
  • Taxi: Taxis are available at most train stations and airports

Where to Go Next from Savoie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lyon
  • Geneva
  • Val d’Isère
  • Grenoble

Conclusion

Savoie is a fantastic destination for visitors looking for culture, natural beauty, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a solo traveler or on a family trip, Savoie has something for everyone. Be sure to take the necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip, and don’t forget to try some of the local cheese before you leave.
