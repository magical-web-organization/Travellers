Introduction to Les Arcs

Nestled in the French Alps, Les Arcs is a popular ski resort that offers year-round activities for outdoor enthusiasts. Made up of four different villages, Les Arcs offers a variety of activities that cater to skiers, snowboarders, hikers, bikers, and mountain climbers. The resort offers plenty of opportunities to take in the stunning Alps scenery while enjoying exhilarating outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Les Arcs

Les Arcs is home to several cultural and natural attractions that cater to a wide range of interests and tastes. Here is a list of must-visit places in Les Arcs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maison des Glaciers: This museum features exhibitions on the local flora and fauna, as well as the history and culture of the area.
  • Chapelle Notre Dame des Vertus: This chapel dates back to the 18th century and is a must-visit for its stunning Baroque architecture.
  • Musée de la Montagne: This mountaineering museum houses exhibits on the history of mountaineering in the region.

Natural Attractions of Les Arcs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aiguille Rouge: The highest peak in Les Arcs offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and is accessible via the Terrasse and Varet cable cars.
  • Lac des Millefonts: This mountain lake offers a beautiful spot for picnicking, swimming, and fishing.
  • Parc national de la Vanoise: This national park offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, bird-watching, and wildlife observation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Les Arcs

Les Arcs offers numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience and fitness. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Chemin de l’Arc: This scenic trail takes hikers through the forested areas of Les Arcs and offers stunning views of the valley below.
  • Mont Jovet: This trail takes hikers to the top of Mont Jovet, one of the highest peaks in the area, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding region.
  • Col de l’Aiguille: This trail takes hikers through the heart of the Vanoise National Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Les Arcs: Activities and Tours

Les Arcs offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained during their stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Les Arcs:

  • Skiing and snowboarding: Les Arcs is best known as a ski resort and offers plenty of well-groomed ski slopes for visitors of all levels.
  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and enjoy stunning views of the Alps from above with a paragliding tour.
  • White-water rafting: The Isère River that runs through Les Arcs offers plenty of white-water rafting opportunities for thrill-seekers.
  • Helicopter tours: Take in the stunning views of the French Alps from the air with a helicopter tour of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Les Arcs

Les Arcs offers a variety of dining options for visitors to the resort.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beaufort cheese fondue: A traditional French dish made with Beaufort cheese, served with fresh bread and potatoes.
  • Tartiflette: A hearty dish made with potatoes, bacon, onions, and Reblochon cheese.
  • Raclette: A dish that features melted cheese served with meats, potatoes, and bread.

Local Beverages

  • Chartreuse: A herbal liqueur that has been made by monks in France since the 1700s.
  • Genepi: A liqueur made from Alpine herbs and spices.
  • Vin de Savoie: A local wine that is grown in the nearby vineyards.

Where to Stay in Les Arcs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Les Arcs offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended options:

  • Hotel Mercure Les Arcs 1800: A comfortable hotel offering stunning views of the valley below.
  • Chalet le Bornian: A cozy, family-run guesthouse with quick access to the main ski lifts.
  • Auberge de Cassiel: A charming hostel that is great for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Les Arcs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Les Arcs is home to several shops that offer unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best stores to visit:

  • Skidoc Sport: This store offers a great selection of ski gear, snowboards, and outdoor clothing.
  • Le Petit Turin: This store offers delicious chocolates and other sweet treats that are made locally.
  • La Baïta du Loup: A great place to pick up local flavors, such as Beaufort cheese, sausages, and wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Les Arcs

Les Arcs has a unique culture and customs that celebrate its mountain heritage. Visitors can enjoy local festivals and events that showcase the region’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Les Arcs

Les Arcs is a popular destination all year round. The best time to visit depends on what activities you want to enjoy.

  • Winter: December to March is the best time for skiing and snowboarding.
  • Spring: April and May are great for enjoying the wildflowers in bloom and the great weather for outdoor activities.
  • Summer: June through August is ideal for hiking and outdoor sports.
  • Fall: September and October is a great time to avoid crowds and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Les Arcs

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Les Arcs:

Do:

  • Dress in warm layers when visiting during the winter months.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities available.

Don’t:

  • Leave any waste behind when taking part in outdoor activities.
  • Ignore safety guidelines for outdoor adventures.
  • Be disrespectful to local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Les Arcs

Les Arcs is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should take appropriate precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Les Arcs

Les Arcs has a good healthcare system with well-equipped medical facilities. Visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Les Arcs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Les Arcs

  • Lyon-Saint Exupery Airport: 2.5 hours by car or shuttle.
  • Geneva International Airport: 2.5 hours by car or shuttle.

Bus Stations Near Les Arcs

  • Bourg Saint Maurice: The closest bus station to Les Arcs.

Other Options

  • Train: Eurostar trains run direct from the UK to Bourg Saint Maurice in the winter months.
  • Car rental: Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Les Arcs.

How to Get Around in Les Arcs

Les Arcs has a good bus service that links the villages and ski lifts. Visitors can also walk between the villages or take a taxi if they prefer.

Where to Go Next from Les Arcs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Les Arcs is a great base from which to explore the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Tignes: A popular ski resort that is a short drive from Les Arcs.
  • Les Deux Alpes: Another popular ski resort that is a short drive from Les Arcs.
  • Parc national de la Vanoise: A beautiful national park that is great for hiking, wildlife watching, and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Les Arcs is a great destination for anyone who loves the outdoors. With its stunning natural scenery, a variety of outdoor activities, delicious food, and unique culture and customs, Les Arcs has something to offer visitors of all ages and interests. So, pack your bags and plan your visit to this beautiful French destination.
