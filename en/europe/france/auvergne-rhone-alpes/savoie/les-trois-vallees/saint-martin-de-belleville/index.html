Introduction to Saint Martin de Belleville

Nestled in the Savoie region of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, Saint Martin de Belleville is a picture-perfect village surrounded by stunning mountain scenery. The village has a rich history and is full of traditional alpine architecture, with wooden chalets and stone buildings dating back to the 16th century.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Martin de Belleville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in local history and culture, Saint Martin de Belleville has plenty of museums and historical places to visit. The local museum, Musée de Saint Martin, is a great place to start. Here, you’ll learn about the village’s history, including its role in the French Resistance during World War II.

Another fascinating attraction is the Chapel of Notre-Dame de la Vie, which dates back to the 17th century. The chapel boasts stunning Baroque architecture and is an important pilgrimage site for local people.

Natural Attractions of Saint Martin de Belleville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The French Alps are known for their incredible natural beauty, and Saint Martin de Belleville is no exception. The village sits in the heart of the Les Trois Vallées ski area, one of the most extensive and diverse ski regions in the world. There are plenty of outdoor adventures to enjoy, from skiing and snowboarding in the winter to hiking and mountain biking in the summer.

For a unique outdoor experience, head to the Aiguille du Fruit, a peak that stands at over 3,000 meters. From here, you’ll enjoy stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Martin de Belleville

Saint Martin de Belleville is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of trails to explore for all skill levels. One of the most popular routes is the Bouquetin Trail, which starts in Saint Martin de Belleville and takes you through stunning Alpine scenery, including glaciers, rivers, and wildflower meadows.

If you prefer biking, head to the Bike Park of Les Menuires, just a short drive from Saint Martin de Belleville. Here, you’ll find an extensive network of trails for all levels, from beginner to expert.

Best Things to Do in Saint Martin de Belleville: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and skiing, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Saint Martin de Belleville. One of the most unique experiences is to take a hot air balloon ride over the Alps, which offers breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys below.

For a taste of local life, head to the weekly market, held every Wednesday in the village square. Here, you’ll find a variety of local products, including cheese, sausages, and handcrafted goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint Martin de Belleville

To help you plan your trip, here’s a week-long itinerary for Saint Martin de Belleville:

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Martin de Belleville

The French Alps are known for their world-class cuisine, and Saint Martin de Belleville is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Raclette: A melted cheese dish served with boiled potatoes, ham, and pickles
  • Tartiflette: A potato gratin made with Reblochon cheese, bacon, and onions
  • Crozets: A type of pasta made with buckwheat flour, typically served with cheese or bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Genepi: A herbal liqueur made with Alpine herbs
  • Vin de Savoie: A local wine made with grapes grown in the Savoie region
  • Chartreuse: A green or yellow liqueur made by Carthusian monks and flavored with a secret blend of herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Saint Martin de Belleville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Saint Martin de Belleville, from cozy guesthouses to luxury chalets. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Alpen Ruitor: A 4-star hotel located just a short walk from the ski lifts, with an indoor pool, sauna, and steam room.
  • Chalet La Loy: A luxury chalet in the heart of the village, with stunning mountain views and a private hot tub.
  • Les Chalets de la Lune: A collection of charming guesthouses located on the slopes, with easy access to the ski area.

  • Handcrafted Goods: Saint Martin de Belleville is full of local artisans, offering everything from pottery and jewelry to textiles and woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Martin de Belleville

Like many small villages in France, Saint Martin de Belleville has a rich cultural heritage. The village has a strong sense of community, and local festivals and traditions are an important part of life here.

One of the most unique festivals is the Fête des Bergers, or Shepherds’ Festival, which takes place every August. The festival celebrates the village’s agricultural heritage and includes a procession of sheep, accompanied by traditional music, dancing, and food.

Best Time to Visit Saint Martin de Belleville

The best time to visit Saint Martin de Belleville depends on your interests. If you’re a skier or snowboarder, the winter months are the best time to visit. The ski season typically runs from the end of November to late April.

If you’re more interested in hiking and outdoor adventures, the summer months are ideal. The weather is warm, and the scenery is breathtaking.

Saint Martin de Belleville is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. It’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions, like locking doors and windows, and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Saint Martin de Belleville

Getting to Saint Martin de Belleville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint Martin de Belleville

The closest airports to Saint Martin de Belleville are:

  • Chambéry Airport: 90 km distance
  • Lyon Saint-Exupéry Airport: 180 km distance

Bus Stations Near Saint Martin de Belleville

There are several bus companies that operate services to Saint Martin de Belleville, including:

  • Altibus: Operates a regular shuttle service from Chambéry Airport and Lyon Saint-Exupéry Airport to Saint Martin de Belleville.
  • Ouibus: Operates a regular service from Paris to Saint Martin de Belleville.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Saint Martin de Belleville is easily accessible from many cities in France. The village is located just off the A43 highway, about a 2-hour drive from Lyon and a 3-hour drive from Geneva.

How to Get Around in Saint Martin de Belleville

The easiest way to get around Saint Martin de Belleville is by foot. The village is small and easy to navigate, and most attractions are within walking distance.

If you prefer, there’s also a local bus service that connects Saint Martin de Belleville with other villages and ski areas in the Les Trois Vallées region.
