Introduction to Tignes

Tignes is a beautiful alpine town located in Savoie, in Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, in France. With stunning valleys, glaciers, and charming villages, it is a popular tourist destination for both summer and winter vacations. The town offers excellent skiing, snowboarding, and hiking opportunities, along with top-notch restaurants, bars, and shops.

In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at Tignes and its picturesque surroundings, as well as provide travel tips and recommendations to help you plan your perfect vacation.

Food and Drink Scene of Tignes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fondue Savoyarde (cheese fondue)
  • Tartiflette (potato and cheese gratin)
  • Crozets (buckwheat pasta dish)
  • Raclette (melted cheese served with potatoes and cold cuts)

Local Beverages

  • Génépi (herbal liquor commonly found in the French Alps)
  • Vin Chaud (mulled wine)
  • Chartreuse Green (a famous liqueur made by Carthusian monks)

Local Culture and Customs of Tignes

Tignes has a rich local culture and customs worth exploring. The town is known for its cheese production, wildlife, and mountain traditions. In the winter, Tignes hosts many events such as ice-driving or the ski and snowboard competitions. Meanwhile, summer is a popular season for the local jazz and music festivals.

Best Time to Visit Tignes

The best time to visit Tignes depends on your interests. For winter sports enthusiasts, the ski season from December to April is the ideal time. However, if you prefer outdoor hiking, summer is the season for you (mid-June to mid-September).

Do’s and Don’ts of Tignes

Do:

  • Try all the local wine and liquor
  • Respect the mountain environment
  • Take part in local festivals
  • Enjoy your ski and snowboard experiences

Don’t:

  • Litter or serve a noisy atmosphere
  • Mock the local culture and traditions
  • Leave any waste while you are skiing or snowboarding

Safety and Health

Safety in Tignes

Tignes is a relatively safe town, but accidents can happen if you are not careful while engaging in extreme sports. Remember to stay hydrated and protected when exploring the mountainous regions.

Health in Tignes

The town has several health centers and clinics that can offer quality healthcare services to tourists and locals. Ensure to have health insurance when you travel to Tignes.

How to Get Around in Tignes

The town is easily walkable or use the free shuttle bus service provided by the town council in the winter season. There are also many taxi services and rental car options available.
