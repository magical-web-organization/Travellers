Introduction to La Clusaz

La Clusaz is a charming ski resort town located in the Haute-Savoie department of the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region in France. It is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts, but the town also has plenty to offer during the summer months. From historic sites to natural attractions, and a wide range of outdoor activities, La Clusaz has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, accommodations, food and drink, local culture, transportation, and more. Whether you are planning a short weekend trip or a longer vacation, this guide will help you make the most of your visit to La Clusaz.

List of Places to Visit in La Clusaz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Art and History: This museum houses a collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from the region’s rich history.
  • Heritage House: This museum gives visitors a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle of the area’s inhabitants.
  • Church of St. Foy: This historic church dates back to the 17th century and boasts stunning Baroque architecture.

Natural Attractions of La Clusaz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aravis Range: The stunning Aravis Range offers some of the best views of the town and its surroundings.
  • Lake Confins: This beautiful lake is perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing in the summer months.
  • Grotte des Nants: This impressive cave system is a must-visit for anyone interested in geology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Clusaz

La Clusaz offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the surrounding landscapes through hiking and biking. Some of the top trails include:

  • The Col de Balme hike: This 5-hour hike offers stunning views of the Aravis Range and is known for its wildflowers.
  • The La Clusaz-Beauregard mountain bike trail: This scenic trail takes riders through forests and meadows, offering plenty of challenges along the way.
  • The Massif des Aravis hiking trail: This 3-day hike takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes in the area, including mountain peaks, waterfalls, and alpine forests.

Best Things to Do in La Clusaz: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: La Clusaz is known for its world-class ski slopes, which cater to skiers and snowboarders of all levels.
  • Paragliding: Get a bird’s eye view of the town and surrounding mountains with a thrilling paragliding experience.
  • Ice Skating: The town has several ice skating rinks to choose from, where you can glide on the ice and take in beautiful views.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Clusaz

Day 1: Explore the town and visit the Museum of Art and History.

Day 2: Hit the slopes and spend the day skiing or snowboarding.

Day 3: Take a guided hiking tour of the Aravis Range.

Day 4: Visit Lake Confins for a relaxing day of swimming and boating.

Day 5: Try paragliding or take a hot air balloon ride for stunning aerial views.

Day 6: Visit the Grotte des Nants to explore impressive underground caverns.

Day 7: Relax and indulge in the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of La Clusaz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fondue Savoyarde: This classic dish consists of melted cheese, wine, and garlic, which is then dipped with bread.
  • Charcuterie: La Clusaz is known for its cured meats, including sausages and ham.
  • Crozets: These small, square-shaped noodles are a local specialty and can be enjoyed with a variety of toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Genepi: This herbal liqueur is made from a variety of alpine herbs and is a popular after-dinner drink.
  • Vin de Savoie: The region is known for its excellent wines, which pair well with the local cuisine.
  • Biere de Savoie: The town has several microbreweries that produce delicious craft beer.

Where to Stay in La Clusaz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Beauregard: This 4-star hotel offers stunning panoramic views of the Aravis Range and is located in a prime location near the ski slopes.
  • Hotel Alpen Roc: This cozy hotel is perfect for those looking for a more traditional, chalet-style accommodation.
  • Les Chalets de la Serraz: These charming chalets are perfect for families or large groups and come with all the amenities needed for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in La Clusaz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Savoyard cheese: La Clusaz is known for its delicious cheese, which makes a great gift for foodies.
  • Salami from Aravis: This delicious cured meat is another popular souvenir.
  • Local crafts: The town has several artisanal shops that sell handmade crafts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of La Clusaz

The town’s strong Savoyard culture is evident in its cuisine, crafts, and traditional architecture. Visitors should be aware of local customs, such as respecting quiet hours during nap time and dressing appropriately for the local places of worship.

Best Time to Visit La Clusaz

The winter months (December to March) are the best time to visit for skiing and snowboarding. During the summer months (June to August), the town offers a wide range of outdoor activities, such as hiking and mountain biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Clusaz

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be loud during nap time or after quiet hours.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Clusaz

La Clusaz is generally a safe town, but visitors should still take basic precautions, such as locking valuables in a safe at their accommodation, staying alert in crowded areas, and avoiding poorly lit areas at night.

Health in La Clusaz

There are several medical clinics and pharmacies in the town, and emergency services are available throughout the area.

Getting to La Clusaz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Clusaz

The nearest airports to La Clusaz are:

  • Lyon–Saint-Exupéry Airport: approximately 140 km away
  • Geneva Airport: approximately 65 km away

Both airports offer regular shuttle services to the town.

Bus Stations Near La Clusaz

The town has a bus station, where you can catch buses to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Those driving to La Clusaz can take advantage of the town’s ample parking facilities, and the town is also accessible by train.

How to Get Around in La Clusaz

The town is small and easily navigable on foot, but visitors can also use the local shuttle bus and taxi services.

Where to Go Next from La Clusaz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Annecy: This stunning town is known for its picturesque canals and historic landmarks.
  • Chamonix: This world-famous ski resort offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the world.
  • Mont Blanc: Visitors can take a day trip to the highest peak in the Alps for stunning panoramic views.

Conclusion

La Clusaz is a charming town that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and delicious cuisine. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit and make the most of your time in this beautiful corner of France.
