Paris is a city with many faces. Its 20 arrondissements help divide the various neighborhoods and aspects of this famous city. The 10th arrondissement is located on the right bank of the Seine River and is home to a wealth of culture and charm. Whether you’re interested in food, museums, history, or just a relaxing stroll, the 10th arrondissement has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll highlight some of the best attractions and activities in the area, as well as provide tips for making the most of your time in this vibrant neighborhood.

Introduction to 10th Arrondissement

The 10th arrondissement is known for its lively atmosphere and diverse population. It’s a district where locals and visitors alike come for the vibrant arts scene, picturesque canal Saint-Martin, lively nightlife, and modern boutique shops.

Despite being home to some trendy neighborhoods, the 10th arrondissement hasn’t lost its authentic charm and still offers original Parisian bistros and cafes with delicious food, often sourced from local markets. Art enthusiasts will love this district, which has numerous museums and galleries. Roughly eight miles contain a lot of history, so much so that the area is known as the “New Athens.”

List of Places to Visit in 10th Arrondissement

There are several fantastic attractions to discover while exploring the 10th arrondissement. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature
  • Passage Brady, Bollywood District
  • St. Vincent de Paul Church
  • Hotel du Nord (featured in Marcel Carne’s movie, “Hotel du Nord”)
  • Gare du Nord

Natural Attractions of 10th arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canal Saint-Martin
  • Jardin Villemin garden
  • Jardin des Récollets park
  • Bassin de la Villette

Hiking/Biking Routes of 10th arrondissement

For an active day outdoors, the Promenade Plantée trail is an ideal way to explore the district. It’s a former railway line that starts at Bastille and leads to the Botanical Gardens. The Canal de l’Ourcq cycling route is over 100 kilometers long, tracing its way through Paris to the town of Meaux.

Best Things to Do in 10th arrondissement: Activities and Tours

For those who fancy museum visits, the Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature is a one-of-a-kind institution featuring over 1,500 exhibits related to hunting. The Passage Brady, popularly known as the Bollywood District, is where you can spot a burst of color, culture, and countless spices. Gare du Nord is an essential transportation hub, featuring impressive Baroque architecture.

For a taste of the local food and drink, try a French wine and cheese tasting session at Le Comptoir General. Visitors can also opt for a guided walking tour of the charming canal areas or even a walking tour featuring historic buildings and street art. Other notable things to do include attending a live music performance at Point Éphémère, a cultural center with a focus on emerging artists, and tasting some local cuisine at the bustling street market on Rue de Chabrol.

Week-Long Itinerary for 10th arrondissement

Day 1: Wander from Gare du Nord and explore the local shops and street markets Day 2: Visit the Museum of Hunting and Nature followed by a stroll around Jardin Villemin Day 3: Take a bike ride on the Canal de l’Ourcq trail, stopping at picturesque Bassin de la Villette Day 4: Walk along the canal and take canal cruises Day 5: Day trip to Château de Vincennes, a historic castle on the outskirts of Paris Day 6: Visit the Passage Brady, Jewish neighborhood and Bollywood district Day 7: Explore the galleries and local boutiques of the vibrant district.

Food and Drink Scene of 10th arrondissement

When it comes to Parisian cuisine, the 10th arrondissement offers plenty of options. From traditional bistros offering quiches and French onion soup, to newer up-and-coming restaurants showcasing creative fusion dishes, you’ll find something for every preference and budget. For those seeking to taste something locally unique, here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Croissants and “pain au chocolat” from Boulangerie Thierry Marx
  • French onion soup and escargot at Au Père Louis
  • Croissants and Kouign Amman from Breizh Café
  • Falafel sandwiches at L’As du Fallafel

Local Beverages

  • Cidre (French cider)
  • French wine from the focus on organic and biodynamic wine-making of Le Verre Volé
  • Local beer at Brasserie de la Goutte d’Or

Local Culture and Customs of 10th arrondissement

Parisians are polite in nature and appreciate it when visitors attempt to speak French. They’re usually reserved and may seem at times to be less open to making small talk than visitors might expect.

Best Time to Visit 10th arrondissement

The best time to visit the 10th arrondissement is during the spring and fall months. April to June or September to November has more mild, comfortable temperatures, with fewer crowds and lower prices than the summer months when Paris can be overly busy.

Do’s and Don’ts of 10th arrondissement

Do:

  • Visit more local neighborhoods away from the tourist crowds
  • Learn basic French phrases
  • Be respectful to locals and their customs

Don’t:

  • Ignore proper etiquette while dining out. Avoid talking loudly in cafes and resist the temptation to tip excessively.
  • Assume everyone speaks English fluently. Try to speak in French as much as you can.
  • Forget to pack comfortable walking shoes and a jacket during the cooler months.

Safety and Health

Safety in 10th arrondissement

The 10th arrondissement is relatively safe, though tourists should always be vigilant about pickpockets in crowded areas, especially if carrying purses and wallets. Always keep belongings close, and never leave them unattended. It’s also essential to be cautious when crossing the roads since Parisian traffic can be chaotic.

Health in 10th arrondissement

France has one of the best public healthcare systems globally, so travelers can rest assured that they are in good hands should they fall sick during their visit. However, it’s wise to have proper travel insurance before embarking on a trip to Paris. If you need any specific medication, stock up before your trip since pharmacies may have different regulations than what you’re used to.

Getting to 10th arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Paris has several airports, with Charles de Gaulle (CDG) being the largest one. The easiest and fastest way to reach the 10th arrondissement is via the train station. The Gare du Nord connects Paris to major European cities and is a hub for numerous international rail lines.

Airports Near 10th arrondissement

  • Charles de Gaulle Airport
  • Orly Airport
  • Beauvais-Tillé Airport

Bus Stations Near 10th arrondissement

  • Gare de l’Est

Other Options

  • Métro stations
  • RER station
  • Taxis and ride-sharing (such as Uber)

How to Get Around in 10th arrondissement

The 10th arrondissement is relatively compact and easily walkable, but you can also get around via public transport, such as buses or the metro. It’s also possible to rent a bike or take a boat tour on the canal, both of which can be a fun way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from 10th arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Paris offers many amazing nearby destinations, meaning you need not leave the region for a few days or even a week. For a quick day trip, you can venture out to Château de Vincennes, a former royal residence just outside of Paris. For more extended visits, consider heading to nearby cities such as Versailles or Chartres.

Conclusion

The 10th arrondissement is a diverse neighborhood with something for everyone. From the vibrant canal area to the rich cultural history and trendy shopping districts, this district of Paris is a must-visit. When planning your trip, remember to take some time to explore the lesser-known areas of the city and immerse yourself in the local culture and cuisine for an unforgettable experience.
