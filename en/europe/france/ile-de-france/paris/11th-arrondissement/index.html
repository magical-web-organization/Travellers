Introduction to 11th Arrondissement

The 11th arrondissement of Paris is a bustling neighborhood located on the right bank of the River Seine. It is known for its culturally diverse population, vibrant nightlife, and rich history. The area is home to many iconic landmarks, including the Bastille Square, the Père Lachaise Cemetery, and many museums and galleries.

Week-Long Itinerary for 11th Arrondissement

Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip to the 11th arrondissement:

Day 1: Explore the Bastille Square and its surrounding markets and cafes. Day 2: Visit the Père Lachaise Cemetery and pay tribute to famous artists and historical figures buried here. Day 3: Take a Food Tour and discover the local cuisine and delicacies of the 11th arrondissement. Day 4: Hike or bike along Promenade Plantée or Coulée Verte René-Dumont. Day 5: Visit Musée Edith Piaf, Musée du Fumeur, and Maison des Métallos to learn about the history and culture of the area. Day 6: Attend a concert or show at the Opera Bastille Day 7: Take a canal cruise or stroll along the Canal Saint-Martin and enjoy its picturesque views.
