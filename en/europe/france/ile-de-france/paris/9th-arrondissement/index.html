Introduction to 9th arrondissement

The 9th arrondissement of Paris is a vibrant and bustling neighborhood located in the heart of the city. It is home to some of the most iconic landmarks and attractions in Paris, including the Palais Garnier and the Galeries Lafayette. The 9th arrondissement is also known for its lively nightlife and artistic scene, which includes the legendary Moulin Rouge cabaret. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, the 9th arrondissement has plenty to offer. This comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your trip to this exciting Parisian neighborhood.

List of Places to Visit in 9th arrondissement

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palais Garnier: A stunning opera house that is a masterpiece of 19th-century architecture.
  • Musée de la Vie Romantique: A museum dedicated to the Romantic era, with exhibits on the personal life and works of writer George Sand and painter Ary Scheffer.
  • Musée Gustave Moreau: A museum showcasing the works of the Symbolist painter Gustave Moreau, who lived and worked in the 9th arrondissement.
  • Musée Grévin: A wax museum that features lifelike figures of historical and contemporary celebrities.

Natural Attractions of 9th arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Monceau: A picturesque park with beautiful walking paths and romantic gardens.
  • Rue des Martyrs: A charming and historic street that is lined with artisanal shops, bakeries, and patisseries.
  • Basilique du Sacré-Cœur: A white-domed basilica that offers breathtaking panoramic views of Paris.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 9th arrondissement

  • Montmartre Hill: A scenic hike up a hill in the 9th arrondissement that offers stunning views of the city.
  • Promenade Plantée: A beautiful park that spans three miles and runs from the 12th arrondissement to the 9th arrondissement, allowing visitors to hike or bike through elevated gardens and scenic walkways.

Best Things to Do in 9th arrondissement: Activities and Tours

  • Moulin Rouge: A legendary cabaret that offers lively shows that feature cancan dancers, acrobats, and other variety acts.
  • Galeries Lafayette: One of the most famous department stores in the world, featuring high-end fashion and luxury goods.
  • Opéra national de Paris: A world-renowned opera company that performs at the Palais Garnier and the Opéra Bastille.
  • Les Folies Bergère: A performing arts theater that has hosted a variety of musicals, plays, and cabaret acts.

Food and Drink Scene of 9th arrondissement

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coq au Vin: A classic French dish made with chicken, red wine, and bacon.
  • Bœuf Bourguignon: A slow-cooked beef stew flavored with red wine, mushrooms, and herbs.
  • Escargots: A dish of cooked snails that are seasoned with garlic butter or sauce.

Local Beverages

  • French Wine: France is known for its wine, and there are plenty of varieties to try in the 9th arrondissement.
  • Kronenbourg Beer: A popular beer from France that is known for its crisp and refreshing taste.
  • Pastis: A traditional French aperitif that is flavored with anise and typically mixed with water.

Local Culture and Customs of 9th arrondissement

The 9th arrondissement is a lively and artistic neighborhood that is known for its theaters, museums, and galleries. The area has a rich history of art and literature, with artists and writers such as Gustave Moreau and George Sand having lived and worked in the neighborhood. The area is also known for its fashion, with the Galeries Lafayette being one of the world’s most famous department stores. When in France, be sure to acquaint yourself with a few customs as the French practice a different lifestyle from Americans. France is a culture with a rich gastronomic history; the food and wine in the 9th arrondissement are of excellent quality. Additionally, Parisians are known for their warm hospitality, so it’s always a good idea to learn a few French phrases to show your appreciation.

Best Time to Visit 9th arrondissement

The best time to visit the 9th arrondissement is during the spring and summer months (March-August) when the weather is mild and the flowers are in bloom. Due to the neighborhood’s central location, it is a popular spot for tourists throughout the year, but the shoulder season (September-November) is a great time to avoid the crowds and still enjoy pleasant weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of 9th arrondissement

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in French delicacies.
  • Explore the museums and theaters of the 9th arrondissement.
  • Take in the scenic views of Paris from the Sacré-Cœur or Montmartre hill.
  • Learn a few French phrases and engage with the locals in their native language.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing or footwear when visiting a church.

Safety and Health

Safety in 9th arrondissement

The 9th arrondissement is generally a safe and friendly neighborhood, but as with any major city, it’s always important to take precautions. Be careful of pickpockets in tourist hotspots and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables around with you. Be cautious when crossing streets, as traffic can be heavy in some areas. As a traveler, you should also purchase travel insurance that will cover you in case of emergency.

Health in 9th arrondissement

The healthcare system in France is generally considered to be of high quality, but it’s always important to have medical insurance when traveling. There are many pharmacies located throughout the 9th arrondissement that can provide basic medical care and over-the-counter medications. In the event of an emergency, dial 112 to reach the French emergency services.

Getting to 9th arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

The 9th arrondissement is located in the heart of Paris, making it easy to reach via public transportation. The nearest airports are the Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) and the Orly Airport (ORY), both of which offer convenient connections to Paris via train or bus. You can also reach the 9th arrondissement via train from other parts of France. The neighborhood is served by several metro and bus lines that offer easy access to other areas of Paris.

Airports Near 9th arrondissement

  • Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG): 23 km (14.29 mi) north of the 9th arrondissement.
  • Orly Airport (ORY): 19 km (11.81 mi) south of the 9th arrondissement.

Bus Stations Near 9th arrondissement

  • Gare du Nord: A major bus station that serves as a transportation hub for Paris and surrounding areas.
  • Gare Saint-Lazare: A train and bus station that serves the 9th arrondissement and its surrounding neighborhoods.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Paris, but they can be expensive, especially during peak hours.
  • Ride-Sharing Services: Companies like Uber and Lyft offer service in Paris, providing an affordable alternative to taxis.
  • Bicycles: Paris has a bike-sharing program called Velib, which allows visitors to rent bikes and explore the city on two wheels.

How to Get Around in 9th arrondissement

The 9th arrondissement is a compact neighborhood that is easy to explore on foot. Walking is the best way to take in the neighborhood’s charming streets and historic landmarks. Additionally, the neighborhood is served by several metro and bus lines, which are an affordable and convenient way to travel to other areas of Paris. Ride-sharing services like Uber and Taxis are also available at your disposal.
