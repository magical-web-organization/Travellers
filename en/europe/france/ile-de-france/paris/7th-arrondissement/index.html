Introduction to 7th Arrondissement

7th arrondissement is located on the Left Bank of the Seine River in Paris. It is known for its beautiful parks, charming streets, stunning architecture, and iconic landmarks. The district is considered to be one of the most affluent and exclusive areas of Paris, attracting visitors who are interested in exploring its cultural heritage and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in 7th Arrondissement

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Musée d’Orsay

The Musée d’Orsay is a world-famous art museum that houses French works from the mid-19th to early 20th centuries. It is known for its impressive collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces, including works by Monet, Manet, Degas, and Van Gogh.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a Parisian marvel that has become a symbol of the city. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the city from the top of the tower, take a tour of the tower, dine in a restaurant, or take a romantic stroll around the base.

Champ de Mars

The Champ de Mars is a large park located beneath the Eiffel Tower. It is a popular place to relax and enjoy a picnic while admiring the stunning views of the tower.

Les Invalides

Les Invalides is a complex of buildings that was originally built as a hospital and home for disabled veterans. It now houses several museums, including the Musée de l’Armée, which displays a vast collection of military history and arms, and the Tomb of Napoleon Bonaparte.

Natural Attractions of 7th Arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jardin des Tuileries

The Jardin des Tuileries is a beautiful park that stretches between Place de la Concorde and the Louvre Museum. It is a popular spot for visitors to unwind and enjoy the scenery, including ornate fountains, beautiful statues, and abundant greenery.

Parc du Champ de Mars

Parc du Champ de Mars is a perfect spot to relax and take in the stunning views of the Eiffel Tower. With its wide open green spaces, it is also a popular spot for picnics, games, and other outdoor activities.

Seine River Cruise

Taking a boat cruise along the Seine River is a must-do activity in Paris. Visitors can explore the city from a unique perspective, passing under beautiful bridges and past some of Paris’s most iconic landmarks, including the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre-Dame Cathedral.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 7th Arrondissement

One of the best ways to explore 7th arrondissement in Paris is on foot or by bike. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through beautiful streets or enjoy a bike ride along the Seine River, taking in stunning views of the city and some of its most iconic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in 7th Arrondissement: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours available in 7th arrondissement. Visitors can explore the arrondissement on foot, take a bike tour, hop on a Segway, or even take a cooking class to learn how to make classic French cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for 7th Arrondissement

Day 1: Visit the Eiffel Tower and enjoy the park at Champ de Mars.

Day 2: Discover the works of famous French artists at the Musée d’Orsay.

Day 3: Explore the history of the military and government of France at Les Invalides.

Day 4: Take a bike tour of 7th arrondissement, stopping at Jardin des Tuileries and other scenic spots.

Day 5: Take a boat cruise along the Seine River, exploring the city from a unique perspective.

Day 6: Spend the day relaxing in the Parc du Champ de Mars or take a cooking class to learn how to make classic French cuisine.

Day 7: Visit the nearby Palace of Versailles, an easy day trip from 7th arrondissement.

Food and Drink Scene of 7th Arrondissement

Must-Try Dishes

One of the highlights of visiting Paris is enjoying the delicious food on offer. 7th arrondissement is home to some of the city’s most popular restaurants and cafes, including:

Le Jules Verne

Located on the Eiffel Tower’s second floor, Le Jules Verne restaurant offers some of the most breathtaking views over Paris. Foodwise, it serves modern French cuisine with a focus on seafood dishes.

La Fontaine de Mars

La Fontaine de Mars is a classic French restaurant that has been around since 1908. It’s renowned for its steak tartare, escargots, and duck confit.

Café Constant

Café Constant is known for its haute cuisine offerings. The signature dish is the steak frites, a classic French dish that the restaurant takes the utmost care to perfect.

Local Beverages

Paris is known for its wine, and there are plenty of cafes, bars, and restaurants in 7th arrondissement that offer a great selection of wines from around France. The district is also home to some of the city’s best cocktail bars and pubs that offer a vast range of local and international beverages.

Where to Stay in 7th Arrondissement: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options available in 7th arrondissement, ranging from luxurious five-star hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in 7th arrondissement include:

Hôtel de l’Université

Located in the heart of Saint-Germains, the Hôtel de l’Université is a charming and friendly hotel with spacious and comfortable rooms and an excellent location.

Hotel Duquesne Eiffel

The Hotel Duquesne Eiffel is a boutique hotel that offers an exclusive stay in the heart of Paris. The hotel is tastefully decorated, and guests can enjoy stunning views of the Eiffel Tower from many of the rooms.

Eiffel Tower View Apartment

For visitors who want the ultimate view of the Eiffel Tower, the Eiffel Tower View Apartment is a luxurious home away from home. The apartment is tastefully decorated and features a private terrace with jaw-dropping views over the city

What to Buy in 7th Arrondissement: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

7th arrondissement is home to some of Paris’s most popular shopping districts, offering unique souvenirs and high-quality local products. Some of the best places to shop in 7th arrondissement include Rue Cler, Rue Saint-Dominique, and Le Bon Marché.

Visitors can purchase items such as local cheeses, wines, and chocolates, as well as Parisian fashion items, including clothing, shoes, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of 7th Arrondissement

The culture of 7th arrondissement is deeply rooted in Parisian history and heritage. It is a district that exudes elegance, sophistication, and exclusivity. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and dress appropriately.

Best Time to Visit 7th Arrondissement

The best time to visit 7th arrondissement is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, visitors can enjoy the district’s charm and beauty year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of 7th Arrondissement

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress appropriately.
  • Carry a bag to collect your trash.
  • Try local food and drink.
  • Explore the arrondissement on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Ignore social cues or norms.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Overstay your welcome in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in 7th Arrondissement

7th arrondissement is a safe place to visit. Visitors are advised to exercise caution around the Eiffel Tower, where pickpocketing can be a concern. It’s also recommended to avoid wandering around deserted areas alone at night.

Health in 7th Arrondissement

Health services are readily available in 7th arrondissement. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and repatriation.

Getting to 7th Arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near 7th Arrondissement

Paris-Charles de Gaulle Airport is the nearest airport to 7th arrondissement. It is located approximately 22 miles north-east of the district. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, train, or bus to reach the arrondissement.

Bus Stations Near 7th Arrondissement

Gare Montparnasse is the closest bus station to 7th arrondissement, located approximately 2 miles south of the district. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach their destination.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach 7th arrondissement by train, taxi or car. Once there, the best way to explore the district is on foot, by bike or using public transport.

How to Get Around in 7th Arrondissement

7th arrondissement is easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use the metro or public transport. Taxis and private car services are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from 7th Arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations that visitors can explore after visiting 7th arrondissement, including:

  • Palace of Versailles: A grand palace and gardens located just outside Paris, which was once the home of the French royal family.

  • Montmartre: A charming and picturesque artistic neighborhood in Paris that is famous for its bohemian lifestyle and beautiful views over the city.

  • Champs-Élysées: A cosmopolitan district that is home to some of the city’s most famous landmarks, including the Arc de Triomphe and the Place de la Concorde.

Conclusion

7th arrondissement is one of Paris’s most charming and exclusive districts. Visitors can explore its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and enjoy high-class dining and shopping options. With its rich history and distinct culture, 7th arrondissement is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Paris.
