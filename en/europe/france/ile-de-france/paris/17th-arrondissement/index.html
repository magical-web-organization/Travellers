Introduction to 17th Arrondissement

The 17th arrondissement of Paris is situated in the northwest of the city and is known for its bustling streets, lively atmosphere, and diverse culture. It is home to several landmarks, exquisite museums, and stunning parks that make it an ideal destination to explore. Whether you are a culture seeker or an outdoor enthusiast, the 17th arrondissement has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through everything you need to know for your next trip to this exciting neighborhood.

List of Places to Visit in 17th Arrondissement

From museums, historical sites to beautiful parks, and natural attractions, there are plenty of places to visit in the 17th arrondissement. Here are some must-see destinations you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée Cernuschi - This museum is one of the finest in Paris. Its extensive collection of Asian art and culture includes nearly 3,000 pieces from China, Japan, and Korea.
  • Musée de la Vie Romantique - The museum is dedicated to the Romantic period in France and showcases the works of many notable artists, including George Sand and Chopin.
  • Église Saint-Pierre de Montmartre - This church is one of the oldest in Paris and boasts beautiful Baroque architecture.
  • Parc Monceau - This park is perfect if you’re looking for a quiet retreat. The park is beautifully landscaped and features a pond, a bridge, and several statues that punctuate the landscape.

Natural Attractions of 17th Arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Square des Batignolles - Located at the heart of the 17th arrondissement, this park is surrounded by stunning flower gardens and is ideal for picnics or just taking a relaxing stroll.
  • Bois de Boulogne - This vast park located on the outskirts of Paris is perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, picnicking, or just relaxing in nature.
  • Parc de la Villette - This is a vast park situated in northeastern Paris and offers a range of activities like picnicking, kayaking, and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 17th Arrondissement

One of the best ways to explore the 17th arrondissement is to go on a walking or cycling tour. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  • Promenade Pereire - This lovely walkway stretches for 1.3 km and is dotted with benches and pretty pergolas to take a break in.
  • Bois de Boulogne - The park is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer beautiful views of the city and nature.

Best Things to Do in 17th Arrondissement: Activities and Tours

Apart from the usual sightseeing, there are several fun activities and tours to keep you entertained in the 17th arrondissement.

  • Cooking classes - Learn how to cook delicious French cuisine using fresh ingredients at a cooking class.
  • Wine tasting - Discover the delights of French wine at a champagne or wine tasting tour.
  • Shopping - The Marché biologique de Batignolles offers visitors a chance to taste fresh produce and buy unique souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for 17th Arrondissement

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to the 17th arrondissement:

  • Day 1: Visit the Musée Cernuschi and Parc Monceau.
  • Day 2: Explore the Bois de Boulogne.
  • Day 3: Visit the Église Saint-Pierre de Montmartre and Musée de la Vie Romantique.
  • Day 4: Take a cooking class or go wine tasting.
  • Day 5: Explore the Parc de la Villette.
  • Day 6: Visit the Charles de Gaulle Museum.
  • Day 7: Spend the day shopping at Marché biologique de Batignolles.

Food and Drink Scene of 17th Arrondissement

Paris is known for its exquisite culinary scene, and the 17th arrondissement is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Croissants - This flaky pastry is a classic French breakfast staple.
  • Coq au Vin - A classic French dish made of chicken cooked in red wine with onions, bacon, and mushrooms.
  • Boeuf Bourguignon - This slow-cooked beef stew is flavored with red wine and beef stock for a rich, hearty flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - France is world-renowned for its wine-making, and the 17th arrondissement offers visitors a chance to sample some of the best wines in the world.
  • Pastis - A traditional anise-flavored drink that is popular in the summer months.

Where to Stay in 17th Arrondissement: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The 17th arrondissement has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best:

  • Hotel Parc Monceau - This elegant hotel is located a stone’s throw from the Parc Monceau and offers beautiful views of the park.
  • Les Jardins de Villa Marceau - This charming guesthouse has a cozy garden and is located close to several top attractions in the 17th arrondissement.

What to Buy in 17th Arrondissement: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring back home, here are some options to consider:

  • Artisanal cheese - France is world-renowned for its cheese, and the 17th arrondissement has several artisanal cheese shops that offer a wide range of delicious varieties.
  • Local wine - Wine from the 17th arrondissement is a perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Handmade jewelry - Visit one of the many artisanal jewelry shops in the 17th arrondissement for one-of-a-kind pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of 17th Arrondissement

The residents of the 17th arrondissement love their food, wine, and culture. It is customary to greet people with a friendly “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir” when you enter a shop or restaurant. It’s also important to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and museums.

Best Time to Visit 17th Arrondissement

The best time to visit the 17th arrondissement is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November). During this time, the weather is mild, and the crowds are relatively smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of 17th Arrondissement

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Get off the beaten path to explore the arrondissement’s hidden gems.

Don’t:

  • Speak too loudly in public places.
  • Flash your valuables in crowded areas.
  • Ignore traffic rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in 17th Arrondissement

Paris is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take precautions when visiting the 17th arrondissement. Stay vigilant against pickpockets and follow basic safety guidelines. If you plan to explore the city at night, stick to well-lit areas.

Health in 17th Arrondissement

France has a good public healthcare system, and visitors are eligible for emergency treatment. It’s advisable to take out comprehensive travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to 17th Arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to the 17th arrondissement is easy thanks to Paris’s excellent transport network. Here are some of the best options:

Airports Near 17th Arrondissement

Paris has two main airports - Charles de Gaulle and Orly. Both offer excellent transportation links to the city via shuttle bus, train, and taxi.

Bus Stations Near 17th Arrondissement

Paris has several bus stations that offer transport to all parts of the city. The Gare Montparnasse and Gare du Nord are two of the most popular.

Other Options

The 17th arrondissement is well-connected to the rest of the city via the excellent metro network. Taxis are available throughout the city, and bike rentals are a great option for exploring at your own pace.

How to Get Around in 17th Arrondissement

The easiest way to get around the 17th arrondissement is on foot or by bicycle. The neighborhoods are well-connected by the excellent metro network, and taxis are widely available.

Where to Go Next from 17th Arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From the 17th arrondissement, there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Here are some exciting options:

  • Visit the Palace of Versailles for a day trip.
  • Explore the stunning Montemartre neighborhood.
  • Head to the nearby city of Lille for a weekend getaway.

Conclusion

The 17th arrondissement is a vibrant neighborhood that has something to offer for every type of traveler. From its stunning parks to its museums and historical landmarks, there is no shortage of activities to enjoy. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your next trip to this amazing part of Paris.
