Introduction to 18th Arrondissement

The 18th arrondissement is home to Montmartre, the highest hill in Paris, and is known for its bohemian charm, artistic roots, and stunning views of the city. This neighborhood has a rich history and is packed with cultural, artistic, and natural attractions that make it a must-see destination for visitors to Paris.

List of Places to Visit in 18th Arrondissement

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de Montmartre: This museum showcases the artistic and cultural heritage of Montmartre, with exhibitions of historic photos, paintings, and artifacts that bring the neighborhood’s rich artistic past to life.
  • Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Paris: This magnificent Romano-Byzantine basilica serves as a spiritual and cultural center for Parisians and visitors alike. Its stunning white domes are visible from all over the city and offer breathtaking views of the city from its highest point.
  • Espace Dali: This museum is dedicated to the surrealist works of Salvador Dali and is a must-see for fans of his work and those interested in contemporary art.
  • Le Bal: This art exhibition space explores the intersections between art, politics, and society, with exhibitions that challenge and provoke visitors to think deeply about our world.

Natural Attractions of 18th Arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc des Buttes-Chaumont: This park is a green oasis in the heart of Paris and is a favorite spot for locals to spend a lazy afternoon. The park offers stunning views of the city and is home to a picturesque lake, waterfalls, and a grotto.
  • Montmartre Vineyard: Located in the heart of Montmartre, this vineyard is a charming spot for a peaceful walk and photo opportunity. Visitors can purchase a bottle of the vineyard’s wine to take home as a souvenir.
  • Rue Saint-Vincent: This picturesque street is located on the northern slope of Montmartre and is lined with historic houses and beautiful gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 18th Arrondissement

  • Montmartre Walking Tour: This self-guided walking tour takes visitors on a scenic route around Montmartre, passing by the neighborhood’s most iconic landmarks and attractions.
  • Canal Saint-Martin Bike Tour: This guided bike tour takes visitors along the picturesque Canal Saint-Martin, offering stunning views of the city’s historic bridges, cafes, and shops along the way.

Best Things to Do in 18th Arrondissement: Activities and Tours

  • Moulin Rouge: This iconic cabaret is a must-see for visitors to Paris, with its world-famous dancers, elaborate costumes, and lively music.
  • Food Tour: Take a food tour of 18th Arrondissement to discover the neighborhood’s delicious cuisine, including traditional French dishes like escargot and coq au vin.
  • Street Art Tour: Discover the vibrant street art scene in 18th Arrondissement on a guided walking tour that takes visitors to some of the neighborhood’s most impressive murals and artworks.

Food and Drink Scene of 18th Arrondissement

Must-Try Dishes

  • Escargot: This classic French dish features snails in garlic butter and is a must-try for visitors to Paris.
  • Coq au Vin: This traditional French dish is made with chicken, red wine, and mushrooms and is a staple of French cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: France is famous for its wine, and 18th Arrondissement is no exception. Visitors should try a glass of local wine at a traditional Parisian café.
  • Absinthe: This anise-flavored spirit is famously associated with Parisian bohemians and can be found at select bars and restaurants in 18th Arrondissement.

Where to Stay in 18th Arrondissement: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Le Pigalle: This charming boutique hotel is located in the heart of 18th Arrondissement and offers stylish rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Vintage Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers clean and comfortable dorm and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge area.
  • Montmartre Residence: This elegant guesthouse offers spacious and luxurious rooms in a 19th-century mansion, located just a few steps from the Sacré-Cœur.

What to Buy in 18th Arrondissement: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wine: Visitors should take home a bottle of local wine from the Montmartre Vineyard, located in the heart of 18th Arrondissement.
  • Artisanal Chocolates: Paris is famous for its chocolate, and 18th Arrondissement is home to several local chocolatiers that offer handmade, artisanal chocolates and truffles.

Local Culture and Customs of 18th Arrondissement

18th Arrondissement is known for its bohemian and artistic roots, and visitors should take the time to explore the neighborhood’s many cultural attractions, including its art museums, galleries, and street art scene. Visitors should also be aware of Parisian customs and follow basic etiquette, including greeting locals with a polite “bonjour” and dressing appropriately for various situations.

Best Time to Visit 18th Arrondissement

The best time to visit 18th Arrondissement is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the tourist crowds are smaller. However, visitors can also enjoy the neighborhood’s many attractions year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of 18th Arrondissement

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the neighborhood’s many cultural and natural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine, including traditional French dishes and local wines.
  • Be respectful of Parisian customs and etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing, including shorts and tank tops, in cultural and religious sites.
  • Be loud and disrespectful in public places.
  • Overindulge in alcohol and disrupt the peace of the neighborhood.

Safety and Health

Safety in 18th Arrondissement

18th Arrondissement is a relatively safe neighborhood, although visitors should exercise common sense and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. Pickpocketing and petty theft can be an issue in tourist areas, so visitors should keep their valuables close and secure.

Health in 18th Arrondissement

France has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can access quality medical care in case of emergency. Visitors should also be aware of common health concerns, including sunburn and dehydration during the summer months.

Getting to 18th Arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access 18th Arrondissement via various transportation options, including air, bus, and train.

Airports Near 18th Arrondissement

  • Charles de Gaulle Airport: Located 22 km from 18th Arrondissement, this is the largest international airport in Paris.
  • Orly Airport: Located 25 km from 18th Arrondissement, this airport serves primarily domestic and European flights.

Bus Stations Near 18th Arrondissement

  • Gare du Nord: The largest train and bus station in Paris, this station offers connections to destinations throughout Europe.
  • Porte de Clignancourt: This bus station offers connections to suburban destinations in the Paris metropolitan area.

Other Options

  • Metro: 18th Arrondissement is served by several metro stations, including Abbesses, Anvers, and Pigalle.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Paris, although visitors should be aware of their reputation for being expensive.

How to Get Around in 18th Arrondissement

The best way to get around 18th Arrondissement is on foot or by bike, as the neighborhood’s narrow streets and steep hills can make driving difficult. Visitors can also take the metro or bus to access various attractions and destinations throughout the neighborhood.

Where to Go Next from 18th Arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Louvre Museum: This world-famous museum is located in the heart of Paris and features an impressive collection of artwork and artifacts from around the world.
  • Palace of Versailles: This opulent palace is located just outside of Paris and was the former residence of French royalty.
  • Champagne Region: Visitors can take a day trip from Paris to explore the Champagne region, home to some of the world’s best sparkling wine.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in culture, history, or nature, 18th Arrondissement is a must-see destination for any visitor to Paris. With its rich artistic heritage, stunning views, and vibrant street life, this neighborhood is sure to charm and captivate visitors from around the world.
