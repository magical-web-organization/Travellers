Introduction to 2nd Arrondissement

Located in the center of Paris, the 2nd arrondissement is filled with vibrant cultural attractions and scenic views. From must-see museums and historical landmarks to local cuisine, this arrondissement has something for everyone. Visitors can explore the charming streets while indulging in delicious patisseries, visiting world-renowned museums like the Musée du Louvre, and hiking or biking through the city’s beautiful gardens.

List of Places to Visit in 2nd arrondissement

Below is a list of the must-visit attractions in the 2nd arrondissement:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Musée du Louvre: This world-renowned museum houses thousands of artworks and historical artifacts, including the Mona Lisa.

  2. Musée des Arts Decoratifs: This museum boasts a collection of more than 150,000 objects showcasing the art of French living from the Middle Ages to the present day.

  3. Galerie Vivienne: A beautiful 19th century arcade filled with designer boutiques, bookstores, and cafes.

Natural Attractions of 2nd arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jardin des Tuileries: A stunning public garden that stretches between the Louvre and Place de la Concorde, offering scenic views of the City of Lights.

  2. Passage des Panoramas: One of the oldest and most charming passages in Paris that offers a glimpse into the city’s past architecture.

  3. The Place des Victoires: A beautiful and historic square lined with designer boutiques and gastronomic restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 2nd arrondissement

  1. Promenade Plantée: A unique scenic trail that runs on a viaduct above the city, offering beautiful views of parks, gardens, and historic landmarks.

  2. La Coulée Verte: A peaceful pedestrian and bike path running from the Bastille to the Bois de Vincennes Park.

Best Things to Do in 2nd arrondissement: Activities and Tours

  1. Paris Urban Adventures: Offering a wide range of tours from street art to food tours of Paris’ neighborhoods.

  2. Opera Garnier Tour: A visit to the beautifully ornate and historic Opera Garnier.

Week-Long Itinerary for 2nd arrondissement

Day 1: Explore the Musée du Louvre and Jardin des Tuileries.

Day 2: Visit the Musée des Arts Decoratifs and shop at the Galerie Vivienne.

Day 3: Take a promenade plantée and la Coulée Verte hike/bike ride to explore the city’s green spaces.

Day 4: Visit iconic landmarks like the Opera Garnier and discover the picturesque Place des Victoires.

Day 5: Ramble through the charming streets of the 2nd arrondissement and enjoy Paris’ cobbled passages and hidden treasures.

Day 6: Take a culinary tour of the arrondissement and taste the local fare.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and explore other nearby attractions like the Champs-Elysées.

Food and Drink Scene of 2nd arrondissement

Paris is famous for its delicious gastronomy that combines simplicity, taste, and refinement. Here are a few local specialties to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Croissants: A classic French breakfast pastry.

  2. Escargots: Cooked snails that are drizzled with garlic herb butter.

  3. Coq au Vin: A hearty chicken and red wine stew.

Local Beverages

  1. Champagne: Known as the king of French wines, the Champagne region is located just one and a half hours from Paris.

  2. French Wine: France is one of the world’s biggest wine exporters, and Paris has a wide range of wine bars catering to all budgets.

Where to Stay in 2nd arrondissement: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The 2nd arrondissement is home to a range of affordable, mid-range, and luxurious accommodation options to cater to all visitors’ desires.

Budget:

  1. Hotel 34B Astotel: Located in the heart of the 2nd arrondissement, this affordable boutique hotel provides comfortable rooms for an enjoyable stay.

Mid-Range:

  1. Le Meurice: A five-star hotel that offers elegant rooms and unparalleled service.

  2. Hotel de Nell: A sleek and stylish boutique hotel that features modern amenities and design.

Luxury:

  1. Hotel Costes: A chic and luxurious five-star hotel with a popular lounge and courtyard.

What to Buy in 2nd arrondissement: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The 2nd arrondissement’s local vendors offer a wide array of unique and authentic souvenirs that visitors can take home to remember their trip.

  1. Le Comptoir General: A vintage shop that sells locally made home goods and accessories.

  2. La Bohème: A boutique specializing in handmade French leather goods.

Local Culture and Customs of 2nd arrondissement

French culture and customs are ubiquitous in the 2nd arrondissement. Visitors should make an effort to learn a few French phrases, as locals appreciate it when visitors try to speak the language. When entering a shop, it is customary to greet the shopkeeper with a polite ‘Bonjour.’

Best Time to Visit 2nd arrondissement

The best time to visit the 2nd arrondissement is during the months of April to June or September to November when the crowds are fewer, and the weather is mild. Paris has long painted for being a rainy city, but it is also sunny, so carry an umbrella with you.

Do’s and Don’ts of 2nd arrondissement

Do:

  1. Visit the Musée du Louvre to explore world-renowned artworks.

  2. Try local dishes like croissants, escargots and coq au vin, among others.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to greet shopkeepers with a polite “Bonjour” when entering shops.

  2. Don’t take photos of locals or street performers without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in 2nd arrondissement

The 2nd arrondissement is generally safe to visit, and visitors should take the same care they would in any major city. Occasionally, there can be pickpocketing, so it is essential to be aware of your surroundings and belongings.

Health in 2nd arrondissement

France has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors should not worry about access if they need medical assistance. Tourists can access medical facilities in public and private hospitals, but it is essential to have medical insurance before traveling.

Getting to 2nd arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near 2nd arrondissement

  1. Charles De Gaulle Airport: Located 25 km north of Paris, it provides international and domestic flights.

  2. Orly Airport: Located 18 km from Paris and provides both international and domestic arrivals and departures.

Bus Stations Near 2nd arrondissement

  1. Gare du Nord: The main bus station in Paris serving national and international destinations.

  2. Gare de Bercy: A bus station that serves destinations in the southeast of the country.

Other Options

  1. Train: Paris has an extensive train network that connects to towns and cities throughout France and Europe.

  2. Metro: The metro system runs throughout Paris and is a quick and affordable way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in 2nd arrondissement

Visitors can easily explore 2nd arrondissement on foot or by bike. The arrondissement offers several bike rental stations, making biking around the city a genuinely enjoyable experience. Visitors can also use the metro for transportation.

Where to Go Next from 2nd arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to the 2nd arrondissement will enjoy a wide range of Paris destinations and excursions, including:

  1. Eiffel Tower: One of the most iconic landmarks in the world, the Eiffel Tower is located in the 7th arrondissement and can easily be reached by metro.

  2. Montmartre: A charming quarter filled with artists, street performers, and the stunningly iconic Sacré-Cœur Basilica.

Conclusion

The 2nd arrondissement is one of Paris’s most fascinating neighborhoods, offering a unique mix of cultural landmarks, local cuisine, and scenic views. With this guide and tips, visitors can make the most out of their trip to this beautiful arrondissement in Paris.
