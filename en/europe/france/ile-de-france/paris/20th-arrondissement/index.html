Introduction to 20th Arrondissement

The 20th arrondissement of Paris, located in the eastern part of the city, is known for its lively atmosphere, cultural richness, and beautiful parks. It is a vibrant district that attracts locals and tourists alike, with its unique blend of vibrant city life and peaceful green spaces. The arrondissement is known for its diverse cultural heritage, a mix of modern and older architecture, cosmopolitan neighborhoods, and an array of entertainment options.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture, and transportation to the 20th arrondissement.

List of Places to Visit in 20th Arrondissement

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Père Lachaise Cemetery: The final resting place of many famous people, including Oscar Wilde, Edith Piaf, and Jim Morrison.
  • Musée Édith Piaf: A museum dedicated to the life and music of the French singer Edith Piaf.
  • La Bellevilloise: A cultural center located in a former factory, offering exhibitions, concerts, and various events.
  • Cité Internationale Universitaire de Paris: A campus for university students from all over the world, featuring various cultural and social events.

Natural Attractions of 20th Arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc de Belleville: A beautiful park with stunning views of the city, featuring a playground, basketball court, and picnic areas.
  • Parc des Buttes-Chaumont: A large park with a lake, waterfalls, and a suspension bridge. It also offers a panoramic view of Paris.
  • Promenade Plantée: A pedestrian walkway built on an abandoned railway line, surrounded by gardens and greenery.
  • Bois de Vincennes: A huge park and woodland with a zoo, a lake, an aquarium, and numerous walking and cycling paths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 20th Arrondissement

The 20th arrondissement has a wide range of options for hikers and bikers. Some of the most popular routes are:

  • Coulée Verte René-Dumont: A 4.7 km long walkway built on top of an old railway line, offering a peaceful path through the city.
  • La Petite Ceinture: An old railway line that has been converted into a trail for hikers and bikers.
  • Parks of the 20th Arrondissement: Parc des Buttes-Chaumont, Parc de Belleville, and Bois de Vincennes are excellent places for walking, jogging, and cycling.

Best Things to Do in 20th Arrondissement: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Street Art: The 20th arrondissement is known for its colorful street art. Take a walking tour and admire the murals and graffiti.
  • Enjoy Live Music: La Maroquinerie, Le Studio de l’Ermitage, and La Bellevilloise are popular music venues featuring different genres of music.
  • Try some Wine and Cheese: Visit La Cave des Papilles, a wine store with an extensive selection of wines, and try some of the French cheeses.
  • Watch a Movie: Le Louxor is a historic cinema palace that has been completely renovated and now features both classic and contemporary films.

Food and Drink Scene of 20th Arrondissement

Must-Try Dishes

  • Croissants: A classic Parisian breakfast pastry.
  • Falafel: Head to Rue des Rosiers and sample the best falafel in the city!
  • Baguettes and Cheese: Fresh baguettes and French cheese are a match made in heaven.
  • French Onion Soup: A warm and hearty soup that will satisfy any appetite.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Try some local red, white, or rose, and experience the tradition of drinking wine in France.
  • Coffee: Order a café au lait or cafe crème, for a truly local experience.
  • Craft Beer: Do check out local breweries like Les Trois 8 and La Pirata Brewery.

Where to Stay in 20th Arrondissement: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mama Shelter Paris East: A quirky and colorful hotel with a rooftop bar and live music.
  • Hôtel Paris Gambetta: A cozy hotel with comfortable rooms, located near Père Lachaise Cemetery.
  • Les Piaules Hostel: A trendy and affordable hostel in Belleville, with modern facilities and a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in 20th Arrondissement: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Books: La Libraire de Paris offers a vast collection of French books and literature.
  • Wine: Visit La Cave des Papilles and select from a wide range of French wines to bring back home.
  • Artisanal Products: Marché aux Puces de la Porte de Montreuil offers a plethora of vintage and unique artisanal products to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of 20th Arrondissement

Parisians love to take their time and enjoy their meals with friends and family. Additionally, don’t forget to pronounce the French words correctly, and always greet people with a “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir” depending on the time of day.

Best Time to Visit 20th Arrondissement

The summer months (June to August) are ideal times to visit, with warm weather, longer days, and numerous outdoor festivals and events. However, the 20th arrondissement is breathtaking in autumn and spring as well with cooler temperature and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of 20th Arrondissement

Do:

  • Learn some French phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Visit local markets and support small businesses.
  • Feel free to take public transportation, taxis, or walk around the city.

Don’t:

  • Avoid rush hours as public transportation can be crowded.
  • Don’t be offended if people don’t immediately reciprocate a smile or greeting from a stranger.

Safety and Health

Safety in 20th Arrondissement

The 20th arrondissement is relatively safe for travelers. But like any city, stay alert and beware of pickpockets in high tourist areas.

Health in 20th Arrondissement

EU Citizens can use their EHIC card to receive state-funded medical treatment in France. For non-EU nationals, medical insurance is compulsory and recommended.

Getting to 20th Arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near 20th Arrondissement

  • Paris-Orly Airport: Around 45 minutes’ drive away from the 20th arrondissement.
  • Charles de Gaulle Airport: Approximately one hour’s drive from the 20th arrondissement.

Bus Stations Near 20th Arrondissement

  • Gare de l’Est: A major bus station with connections to other parts of France and Europe.
  • Gallieni Bus Terminal: Adjacent to the Metro terminal, and serves international buses.

Other Options

  • Metro: Multiple Metro stations like Porte des Lilas, Belleville, and Gambetta serve the 20th arrondissement.
  • Taxi: Taxis are an easy and comfortable way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in 20th Arrondissement

  • Metro: The best and economical way to explore the district.
  • Bus: The bus networks of RATP provide an extensive network.
  • Walk: This arrondissement is small enough for exploring on foot, and you can enjoy the city’s street life.

Where to Go Next from 20th Arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Canal Saint-Martin: Explore the canal and its surroundings, lined with cozy cafes and local shops.
  • Montmartre: Head to the top of Montmartre to see one of Paris’s best-known landmarks: the Sacré-Cœur Basilica.
  • Versailles: Visit the Palace of Versailles, an enchanting UNESCO World Heritage Site and learn the history of the “Sun King,” King Louis XIV.

Conclusion

The 20th arrondissement is a vibrant Parisian neighborhood that offers a blend of culture, history, art, and nature. It is a beautiful district to explore and is a hidden gem for people looking to explore Paris beyond the typical touristy places.
