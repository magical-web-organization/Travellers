Introduction to 12th Arrondissement

Not as well-known as some of the other arrondissements in Paris, the 12th boasts a rich cultural heritage and several notable landmarks. This district is located on the right bank of the Seine River and is home to both historic and modern attractions, making it an exciting destination for travelers of all interests.

List of Places to Visit in 12th Arrondissement

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Musée National d’Art Moderne

The Musée National d’Art Moderne is the largest museum of modern art in Europe, and a must-visit for art aficionados. The museum houses a vast collection of modern and contemporary art, including works by Picasso, Matisse, and many others.

Place de la Bastille

Place de la Bastille is notorious for the political prison that once stood there. The prison was stormed on July 14th, 1789, marking the beginning of the French Revolution. Today, the square is home to the Opéra Bastille, a beautiful modern opera house.

Promenade Plantée

Promenade Plantée is a green oasis in the heart of the city. This elevated park stretches for 4.7km and is adorned with trees, flowers, and beautiful views of the city.

Bois de Vincennes

The Bois de Vincennes is Paris’s largest public park, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake, go for a jog, or rent a bike and explore the many trails.

Natural Attractions of 12th Arrondissement: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Coulée Verte René-Dumont

Also known as the “Green Belt,” this beautiful park occupies the space that was once a railway line. Visitors can stroll along the lush greenery and unique architecture, bustling with urban life and numerous picturesque views.

Paris Zoological Park

Located in the Bois de Vincennes, this zoological park is home to over 1,000 animal species. The park has undergone a recent transformation to provide more green spaces and a better living environment for the animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of 12th Arrondissement

Bois de Vincennes

There are 42 km of bike trails winding through the Bois de Vincennes, making it a perfect spot for a leisurely ride through the lush greenery. For those looking for more of a challenge, the trails can be quite hilly in places.

Best Things to Do in 12th Arrondissement: Activities and Tours

Bateaux Parisiens River Cruise

One of the best ways to explore Paris is by boat, and the Bateaux Parisiens River Cruise is a quintessential Parisian experience. The cruise offers a unique perspective of the city, with panoramic views of the Eiffel Tower, Notre Dame, and many other famous attractions.

Explore local markets

There are several markets in the 12th arrondissement where visitors can find fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and unique souvenirs. The Marché d’Aligre and the Marché Aux Fleurs Reine Elizabeth II are both worth a visit.

Week-Long Itinerary for 12th Arrondissement

Day 1: Explore the Bastille neighborhood and visit the Opéra Bastille Day 2: Visit the Musée National d’Art Moderne and explore the Coulée Verte René-Dumont Day 3: Take a bike ride through the Bois de Vincennes Day 4: Visit the Paris Zoological Park Day 5: Explore the local markets and try some French delicacies Day 6: Take a River Cruise along the Seine Day 7: Relax in the Promenade Plantée and enjoy the city views

Food and Drink Scene of 12th Arrondissement

Must-Try Dishes

Croissants

The 12th arrondissement is home to some of the best bakeries in the city. Try a traditional buttery croissant from one of the many boulangeries.

Steak Frites

No trip to France is complete without trying the famous Steak Frites. Several restaurants in the 12th arrondissement serve up this classic dish.

Local Beverages

Wine

France is world-renowned for producing some of the best wines in the world. The 12th arrondissement has several wine bars where visitors can sample and learn about the different varieties.

Where to Stay in 12th Arrondissement: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hôtel du Midi Paris Bastille

This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of the Bastille neighborhood, with easy access to many of the main attractions in the 12th arrondissement.

Mama Shelter Paris East

This trendy hotel offers quirky rooms and a lively atmosphere. The terrace bar is a great spot for a drink after a long day of exploring.

What to Buy in 12th Arrondissement: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jardin21

This boutique shop sells unique home furnishings and accessories from local designers. Visitors can find something stylish and artisanal to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of 12th Arrondissement

The 12th arrondissement is known for its eclectic mix of cultures, with many immigrants from North Africa, Asia and Europe living here. Visitors can find many cultural festivals and events throughout the year, like the Bastille Day parade.

Best Time to Visit 12th Arrondissement

The best time to visit the 12th arrondissement is from April to June and September to November when the crowds are smaller, and the weather is mild. Paris is known for its beautiful Spring and Fall seasons, perfect for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of 12th Arrondissement

Do:

  • Remember to try some French delicacies.
  • Visit the local markets and shop souvenirs.
  • Take a bike ride through the Bois de Vincennes park

Don’t:

  • Forget to visit the Promenade Plantée park.
  • Be insensitive to the multicultural heritage of the district.

Safety and Health

Safety in 12th Arrondissement

The 12th arrondissement is generally safe, but visitors should take care to keep an eye on their belongings in crowded areas and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in 12th Arrondissement

France has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can access medical services through public and private clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical costs.

Getting to 12th Arrondissement: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near 12th Arrondissement

Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) and Orly Airport (ORY) both have transportation options that connect to the 12th arrondissement.

Bus Stations Near 12th Arrondissement

Several bus stations located throughout the city with direct routes to the 12th arrondissement.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi, ride-sharing services, or rent a car to get to the 12th arrondissement.

How to Get Around in 12th Arrondissement

The 12th arrondissement has an excellent public transit system with buses, trains, and metros that connect to all the other arrondissements in Paris. Walking and cycling are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from 12th Arrondissement: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Versailles: Just a short train ride from Paris, Versailles is a stunning palace with beautiful gardens and fountains.
  • Fontainebleau: This charming town is located just a short drive south of Paris and is known for its beautiful forest and medieval castle.

Conclusion

The 12th arrondissement is an exciting destination with a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature. Whether you’re interested in art, food, or outdoor activities, there’s something in the 12th arrondissement for everyone. Plan your trip and discover the hidden treasures of this exceptional district of Paris!
