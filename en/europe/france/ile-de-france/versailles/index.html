Introduction to Versailles

Located in the Île-de-France region of France, Versailles is known for its opulent palace, stunning gardens, and rich history. Originally a small village, it became a hub for French royalty in the 17th century when Louis XIV transformed a hunting lodge into the magnificent Château de Versailles.

Today, Versailles attracts millions of tourists each year who come to explore its cultural treasures, natural beauty, and charming village life. In this guide, we’ll show you everything you need to know to make the most of your visit to this beautiful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Versailles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Château de Versailles is the crown jewel of Versailles, and there’s no shortage of other historical and cultural treasures to explore in the area.

The Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most-visited attractions in France. The palace itself is an incredible feat of architecture and engineering, with grand halls, ornate ceilings, and impressive works of art adorning every corner. The Palace also offers audio-guided tours, which makes the experience more immersive (there is no need to rush through each hall if you take the audio-guided tour).

The Hall of Mirrors

The most famous room in the palace is the Hall of Mirrors. It is a grand ballroom found in the central section of the Versailles Palace. It is a perfect example of Baroque architecture and decoration with its gilded moldings and ornate mirrors.

The Trianon Palaces and Marie Antoinette’s Hamlet

To escape the formality and rigidity of court life, Louis XIV constructed two smaller palaces - the Grand Trianon and Petit Trianon - which were reserved for his personal use. Marie Antoinette, wife of Louis XVI, also enjoyed these pleasures and had her own village constructed on the palace grounds. The hamlet included a working dairy farm, gardens, and several houses.

Musée Lambinet

This museum was founded in 1932 in a neoclassical mansion dating back to the 18th century. It houses fine art and artifacts from Versailles’ history.

Eglise Notre-Dame

Eglise Notre-Dame, a church built in the 18th century, is a unique example of the architecture of the time. Inside visitors can see the chapel for the King and Queen of France. The dome and bell tower are also famous sights here.

Natural Attractions of Versailles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its rich history, Versailles offers plenty of outdoor activities and breathtaking natural sights.

The Gardens of Versailles

The Gardens of Versailles are a sprawling 800-hectare park with stunning manicured lawns, fountains, sculptures, and ponds. It is said that once you are in the garden, it is easy to get lost in the paths. The gardens are an attraction in themselves with its beauty and vastness.

The Forest of Versailles

The Forest of Versailles, which spans over 30,000 acres, is an incredible natural wonder that provides visitors with many hiking and mountain biking trails. The forest trail goes on for kilometers in the midst of greenery, ponds, and beautiful wildflowers. It’s easy to spend an entire day wandering through it all.

Arboretum Chèvreloup

This is a beautiful garden with more than 2,500 plant species. It is the largest botanical garden in France which spreads across 200 hectares of land. The arboretum attracts a lot of nature enthusiasts due to its countless variety of flora and fauna species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Versailles

Versailles offers various hiking and biking trails that lead you through the gardens, the forest, and an array of picturesque landscapes.

Hiking trails

The High Walk, the Forest Trail, and the Path of the Ingénieurs are some of the hiking paths in Versailles where visitors could choose from. The sites along these trails vary from viewing beautiful fountains to hiking along woodland.

Biking trails

The bike trails are as varied as the hiking trails and offer scenic routes. They take you through countryside landscapes and even by the palace. Renting bikes is easy as there are many bike rental shops available around the palace.

Best Things to Do in Versailles: Activities and Tours

Versailles is a destination that offers more than what’s listed on the itinerary. Visitors can opt for activities and tours they enjoy.

Hot air balloon rides

Visitors can experience the view from above with a hot air balloon ride which takes them over stunning 18th-century French architectural beauty, natural parks, and beautiful landscapes.

Evening entertainment

This performance takes place in the orangery, outside of the Palace of Versailles. It showcases the audience’s operatic and symphonic concerts. It’s an unforgettable way to enjoy an evening at the palace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Versailles

Deciding on an itinerary for a week-long stay in Versailles must include taking time to explore both history and the atmosphere. Here’s what a typical week in Versailles could look like:

Day 1: Walk through the palace and fountains

Day 2: Experience a leisurely stroll or bike ride through the beautiful lawns and gardens.

Day 3: Take a trip to the Grand Trianon, located on the outskirts of the palace.

Day 4: Explore the Versailles market, Queen’s hamlet, and the Palace stables..

Day 5: Go for a hike through the forest of Versailles or experience hot air balloon tour.

Day 6: Take a food tour that covers from traditional fare to classic French desserts and macaroons.

Day 7: Embark on an excursion to visit the other enchanting cities of France - if you have time, plan a day trip to the beautiful town of Giverny to see Monet’s gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Versailles

There is no shortage of delicious cuisine in Versailles. From classic French dishes to local delicacies, visitors can indulge in unforgettable dining experiences. Below are some of Versailles’ culinary highlights.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the sensational culinary dishes you should indulge in on your trip to Versailles are:

French Onion Soup

A classic dish that is both hearty and flavorful.

Croissants

This staple breakfast item is a must-have while in France. Enjoy it with a sweet coffee or chocolate.

Escargots

A culinary delicacy in France, these snails are often cooked in garlic butter and parsley.

Ratatouille

A vegetable stew made with tomatoes, eggplant, peppers, onions, and zucchini.

Tarte Tatin

A traditional dessert that is made up of caramelized apples on top of a crispy pastry.

Local Beverages

Versailles also offers a wide range of local drinks to help wash down all the food. Here are a few favorites:

French Wine

France is famous for its refined wine and a lot of variants are available here. The Palace of Versailles itself is even said to produce wine.

Cider

It’s the norm in France to drink cider, and the tradition has only grown over the years. Try the cider made from the apples grown in the palace’s orchards for a unique experience.

Espresso

A classic and strong coffee that is enjoyed throughout Europe, and France has their take on the espresso that is a robust cup of coffee that is enjoyed after meals.

Where to Stay in Versailles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Versailles offers various options for accommodations catering to all budgets. Here are a few of the available options:

Grand Hotel Versailles

If you are looking for luxury, you can try out the Grand Hotel Versailles. Its central location is in an elegant 18th-century building, it provides top-class amenities and a spa.

Hotel Atelieres Saint-Louis

This hotel in Montbauron provides a charming cottage feel. It is located within walking distance of the Palace of Versailles and has a lovely garden and multiple room types available.

Camping Le Versailles

The camping site is located just outside the town and provides tent and caravan accommodation. It is a cost-effective alternative and offers a casual atmosphere.

What to Buy in Versailles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visiting Versailles is an excellent opportunity to pick up local souvenirs and gifts to commemorate your trip. Here are some things to consider:

Souvenir Items

You can find souvenir items like keychains, fridge magnets, postcards, and mugs at various gift shops throughout the palace. Many of these souvneir shops sell unique gifts including items made with fabrics that replica of historic royal clothing.

Garden seeds

A perfect souvenir for green-fingered friends - pick up seedlings to grow your own stunning flower garden.

Macarons

The macaroons in Versailles are famous. Visitors can purchase some from the various local patisseries and bakeries for a sweet and delicious souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Versailles

Understanding local culture is essential while traveling to France. Here are a few things to keep in mind while exploring Versailles:

Language

While most locals speak French, tourists can usually manage to communicate in English very easily.

Dress Codes

While visiting the palace, dress your best to match the elegance of the palace that is high-end.

Tipping

It is not mandatory to tip in France, but you may round up to the nearest Euro as a tip to show your gratitude for the service.

Best Time to Visit Versailles

The best time to visit Versailles is during the spring and fall. During the summers, it is always crowded with tourists, especially in July and August. There are also loads of visitors during the Christmas holidays too - planning ahead all the time works!

Do’s and Don’ts of Versailles

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Versailles:

Do:

  • Visit the palace early in the day to avoid long queues.

  • Explore the gardens on foot or by a bicycle.

  • Dress appropriately to match the glitz and the glamour and for visa purposes too. Especially if you are going to the palace for a formal dinner or function.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos inside the palace if you find “no photo sign”.

  • Don’t litter in any public places. Maintain cleanliness and hygiene always.

  • Don’t get drunk or create a ruckus in public places. It is against the law and might result in arrest.

Safety and Health

Versailles is a safe destination for tourists with little to no crime to be concerned about. However, below are a few things to stay aware of in regards to safety and health:

Safety in Versailles

Even though without any violent crimes noted, it is advisable not to stray alone or walk in empty or dark paths in the high-traffic tourist areas - Pick-pocketing could occur. Also, it’s always better to take care of your belongings, especially while exploring the local markets.

Health in Versailles

All visitors to France should have comprehensive travel insurance or a European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) - before traveling. Healthcare in France is top-notch but insurance is a must-have as medical facilities could be quite pricey.

Getting to Versailles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Versailles offers easy access to tourists via buses, trains, and flights. Each of the modes of transportation has its perks.

Airports Near Versailles

The nearest airport to Versailles is Orly Airport which is about 20 km away from the palace.

Bus Stations Near Versailles

Buses to Versailles run from the major bus stations like Porte de Saint-Cloud and Champs de Mars near the Eiffel Tower. The service is readily available and is a comparatively cheap mode of transportation.

Other Options

The train system is the most convenient mode of transport to reach Versailles from Paris and nearby towns. Tickets are easily available at train stations allowed for easy transport in the area, and they run fairly frequently too.

How to Get Around in Versailles

Walking or cycling is the best way to explore the palace and its royal grounds - it will be a lot more fun too. Public transport is reliable and Uber services and taxi cabs are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Versailles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Exploring Versailles is stupendous, but there’s plenty more to see in the vicinity

Giverny

Giverny is located an hour away from Versailles and is a famous town for the eminent painter Claude Monet’s house and gardens. The picturesque garden was the backdrop of some of his most famous works in lily ponds, and is now a museum dedicated to his life and work.

Chartres

Chartres is a medieval city located in the Centre-Val de Loire region of northern France. It takes less than two hours to get here by train - the beautiful Gothic architecture and stained glass windows make this destination magical.

Conclusion

Versailles is a destination that offers a combination of rich cultural history, natural beauty, and exceptional French cuisine. This comprehensive guide includes everything you need to know about exploring the palace and its surrounds. So, plan well ahead of your visit and enjoy the opulence of one of the most luxurious palaces in the world.
