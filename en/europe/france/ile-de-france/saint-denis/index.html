Saint-Denis is a commune in the northern suburbs of Paris, located in the Île-de-France region of France. It is known for being the burial site of French kings and queens, as well as the location of the Stade de France, which hosted the 1998 FIFA World Cup. This town is perfect for history buffs and sports enthusiasts alike, with plenty of attractions to explore. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a look at the best things to see and do in Saint-Denis, as well as where to eat, stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Denis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Basilique Cathedral of Saint-Denis

The Basilique Cathedral of Saint-Denis is a Gothic-style church that was originally built in the 12th century. It is the final resting place of many French kings and queens, including Marie Antoinette and Louis XVI. Visitors can take a guided tour of the cathedral, which includes a visit to the crypt where the royals are buried.

Musée d’Art et d’Histoire

The Musée d’Art et d’Histoire (Museum of Art and History) is located near the Basilique Cathedral of Saint-Denis. It features a collection of artwork and artifacts from the Middle Ages to the 20th century. Visitors can view paintings, sculptures, and decorative objects from various periods of French history.

Maison d’Eduard Branly

The Maison d’Eduard Branly is a historic house museum that was once the home of the inventor and physicist, Eduard Branly. The museum is dedicated to his life and work, and visitors can see some of his original inventions, including early prototypes of the radio.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Denis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parc de la Légion d’honneur

Located on the banks of the Seine River, the Parc de la Légion d’honneur is a large park that is perfect for a day of relaxation. It offers plenty of green space for picnics and sports, as well as walking and cycling paths.

Port de Saint-Ouen

The Port de Saint-Ouen is a scenic spot along the Seine River that is perfect for a leisurely stroll. Visitors can watch the boats go by and admire the river views while enjoying a coffee or ice cream from one of the nearby cafes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Denis

The Canal Saint-Denis

The Canal Saint-Denis is a popular spot for cyclists and walkers. It runs for 6.6 kilometers and offers stunning views of the city. The path is well-marked and easy to follow, making it a great option for beginners.

Tour Pleyel

For a more challenging hike, try the Tour Pleyel. This 6-kilometer loop takes visitors through the neighborhoods of Saint-Denis and offers some of the best city views. Be sure to wear sturdy shoes, as the trail can be steep in some areas.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Denis: Activities and Tours

Stade de France Tour

For sports enthusiasts, the Stade de France Tour is a must-do activity. Visitors can explore the locker rooms, the media center, and the pitch itself. They can also learn about the stadium’s history and the events that have been held there.

Les Puces de Saint-Ouen

Les Puces de Saint-Ouen is one of the largest flea markets in the world, and it is a great place to shop for vintage clothing, furniture, and other unique items. It is open on weekends and is a great way to spend a day browsing through the various stalls.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Denis

Must-Try Dishes

Hachis Parmentier

Hachis Parmentier is a French version of shepherd’s pie and is a popular comfort food in Saint-Denis. It’s made with mashed potatoes, ground beef, and often includes a variety of vegetables.

Croissant

Croissants are a classic French pastry that are a must-try when visiting Saint-Denis. They are made with buttery, flaky pastry and can be enjoyed on their own or with jam and coffee.

Local Beverages

Wine

France is known for its wine, and Saint-Denis is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a glass of red or white wine with their meal or while relaxing in one of the town’s many cafes.

Coffee

Coffee is also a popular beverage in Saint-Denis, and visitors can enjoy a variety of blends and roasts in the many cafes found throughout the town.

Where to Stay in Saint-Denis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Novotel Paris Saint Denis Stade Basilique

The Novotel Paris Saint Denis Stade Basilique is a modern hotel located near the Stade de France. It offers comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi, as well as a restaurant and bar.

Ibis Saint-Denis Stade Ouest

The Ibis Saint-Denis Stade Ouest is a budget-friendly hotel located near the Stade de France. It offers clean and comfortable rooms, as well as free Wi-Fi and a 24-hour snack bar.

Le Grand Hôtel de Normandie

Le Grand Hôtel de Normandie is a charming guesthouse located a short walk away from the Basilique Cathedral of Saint-Denis. It offers comfortable rooms and a continental breakfast in the morning.

What to Buy in Saint-Denis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cheese

France is known for its cheese, and Saint-Denis is no exception. Visitors can sample a variety of cheeses at the town’s various markets, including the famous Brie and Camembert.

Macarons

Macarons are a classic French pastry that can be found in many bakeries in Saint-Denis. They are small, colorful biscuits that are often filled with cream or jam and are perfect for a sweet treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is a multicultural town that is home to people of many different ethnicities and religions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, especially when visiting religious sites such as the Basilique Cathedral of Saint-Denis.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Denis

The best time to visit Saint-Denis is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the busiest time of year, so be prepared for crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Denis

Do:

  • Visit the Basilique Cathedral of Saint-Denis
  • Try the local cheese and pastries
  • Take a tour of the Stade de France

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and etiquette
  • Be disrespectful when visiting religious sites
  • Leave your valuables unattended in crowded areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is a safe town, but visitors should take normal safety precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding dark, secluded areas at night.

Health in Saint-Denis

France has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care at the numerous hospitals and clinics in Saint-Denis.

Getting to Saint-Denis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Denis

The nearest airport to Saint-Denis is Charles de Gaulle Airport, which is located approximately 20 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is well-connected by bus, and visitors can use the public transport network to travel to and from the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Saint-Denis by train, with the nearest station being Gare de Saint-Denis.

How to Get Around in Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use the public transport network to travel around the town, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Denis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Paris

Paris is located just a short train ride away from Saint-Denis and is home to some of the world’s most famous attractions, including the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre Dame Cathedral.

Versailles

Versailles is located approximately 50 minutes away by train from Saint-Denis and is home to the famous Palace of Versailles, as well as vast gardens and parks.

Conclusion

Saint-Denis is a beautiful town that is steeped in history and culture. Whether you are a sports enthusiast or a history buff, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Be sure to take the time to explore the town’s many museums, markets, and parks, and try some of the delicious local cuisine. With so much to see and do, Saint-Denis is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Île-de-France region of France.
