Introduction to Levallois-Perret

Located in the Île-de-France region of France, Levallois-Perret is a charming and culturally rich town that deserves to be on every traveller’s bucket list. The town is known for its bustling streets, chic boutiques, innovative architecture, and landscaped parks. Situated only a few metres away from Paris, it is an ideal destination for those who want to explore France off the beaten path. In this ultimate guide, we will take you through the top things to do in Levallois-Perret, the best hikes and biking routes, the food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Levallois-Perret

Here is a list of some of the top attractions you should not miss during your trip to Levallois-Perret:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée du Montparnasse: This museum displays the works of local artists and showcases the history of the Montparnasse neighbourhood.
  • Musée National Jean-Jacques Henner: This museum displays the works of French artist Jean-Jacques Henner and other artists of his time.
  • Espace Champerret: A modern exhibition centre that hosts many art, culture and trade shows throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Levallois-Perret: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc de la Planchette: A charming and peaceful park, nestled amid the residential area, ideal for a relaxing walk or picnic.
  • Parc de la Vallée aux Loups: A vast park with beautiful flora and fauna featuring a pavilion that once belonged to the famous Romantic author Chateaubriand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Levallois-Perret

Levallois-Perret is also an excellent destination for hiking and cycling lovers. Here are some scenic routes that you should explore:

  • La Coulée Verte - an old railway that has been transformed into a picturesque promenade that spans almost 6km long.
  • Promenade des Impressionnistes - Take a tour of the Seine River and explore the picturesque path of artists who once frequented the area.

Best Things to Do in Levallois-Perret: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of entertaining things to do in Levallois-Perret. Here are some of our favourites:

  • Michalak Masterclass - Learn how to make the best pastries and baked goods from famous chef Christophe Michalak.
  • Moulin Rouge Show - Explore one of the world’s most famous cabarets and be entertained by its unforgettable music and dancing.
  • Segway Tour - Learn about Levallois-Perret’s rich history and culture on a guided Segway tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Levallois-Perret

Levallois-Perret has a vibrant and diverse food scene that will delight your palate. Here are some dishes and drinks you have to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Croque-Monsieur - A famous French ham and cheese sandwich topped with béchamel sauce and broiled until golden.
  • Coq au vin- Braised chicken made with red wine and vegetables.
  • Tarte Tatin - An upside-down apple pie akin to caramelized apples on a buttery base.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Levallois-Perret, being a French town, produces a wide variety of wine.
  • Cider - A refreshing fermented apple drink that is quite popular.
  • Coffee - Coffee is a staple in French culture, and you will find many cafes that serve coffee in Levallois-Perret.

Where to Stay in Levallois-Perret: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Levallois-Perret has plenty of accommodation options that fit different preferences and budgets. Some of our favourite options are:

  • Mercure Paris Levallois Perret - A 4-star hotel located in the heart of the town, with spacious and comfortable rooms.
  • Résidence Courcelle - A quaint and homely guesthouse close to the Champerret Exhibition Centre.
  • Café Hotel de l’Avenir - A reasonably priced hotel located near popular attractions such as Moulin Rouge and Arc de Triomphe.

Local Culture and Customs of Levallois-Perret

French culture and traditions are ubiquitous in Levallois-Perret. It’s always nice to learn about cultures that you are visiting. Here are some customs and traditions that you should keep in mind:

  • Always greet people when you walk into a store or a restaurant.
  • The French take their dining seriously, and meals can last up to two hours.
  • French is the official language, and locals appreciate it if you learn a few basic words in their language.

Best Time to Visit Levallois-Perret

The best time to visit Levallois-Perret is from July to September, when the weather is warm, and you can enjoy outdoor activities. The town is less crowded during this season, and airfares and accommodation prices are reasonable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Levallois-Perret

Here are some essential dos and don’ts to keep in mind while travelling to Levallois-Perret:

Do:

  • Learn a few words of French before you arrive.
  • Greet people politely when entering any shop or restaurant.
  • Try local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disrespectful towards locals.
  • Wear too much revealing clothing or swimwear in public places.
  • Forget to say “Merci” after receiving a service.

Safety and Health

It is always good to be cautious while travelling anywhere. Keep these tips in mind while travelling to Levallois-Perret:

Safety in Levallois-Perret

Levallois-Perret is a relatively safe destination, but it’s still good to be cautious. Keep the following things in mind:

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place.
  • Don’t accept offerings from strangers on the street.
  • Follow traffic rules and regulations.

Health in Levallois-Perret

Levallois-Perret is equipped with hospitals and pharmacies. Here are some emergency numbers you should keep:

  • Police: 17
  • Fire Brigade: 18
