Introduction to Meaux

Located in Île-de-France, in France, Meaux is a historic city known for its cultural landmarks, stunning natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. This charming city offers plenty of things to see and do, making it a popular destination for travelers of all ages. In this guide, we’ll be giving you a comprehensive overview of the best places to visit, things to do, and food to try in Meaux.

List of Places to Visit in Meaux

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cathedral of Saint-Etienne

One of the most famous landmarks in Meaux is the Cathedral of Saint-Etienne. This stunning cathedral features Gothic architecture and is home to many religious relics.

Museum of the Great War

For history buffs, the Museum of the Great War is a must-visit attraction. This museum showcases the events of World War I and features a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits.

Bishop’s Palace

Next to the Cathedral of Saint-Etienne is the Bishop’s Palace, which houses the Diocese Museum of Meaux. This museum has an extensive collection of religious art and manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Meaux: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Meaux-Boutigny Golf Club

Meaux-Boutigny Golf Club is nestled between forests and fields and offers a scenic and challenging 9-hole course.

Les Jardins Fruitiers de Laquenexy

Les Jardins Fruitiers de Laquenexy is a paradise for nature lovers. It is an orchard garden with more than 15,000 fruit trees. Visitors can enjoy walking through the garden and tasting various fruits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meaux

Meaux and its surrounding area boast numerous hiking and biking trails to explore the local natural beauty. Some of the top trails include:

  • Meaux Forest
  • Coulommiers Campagne
  • Pâtis Route

Best Things to Do in Meaux: Activities and Tours

Hot Air Balloon Rides

Experience the breathtaking beauty of Meaux with a hot air balloon ride. Tour companies offer balloon flights throughout the region, giving travelers a unique view of the city and its surrounding areas.

Walking Tours

Join a guided walking tour of the city and discover Meaux’s hidden gems. Walk around the historic streets and learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Meaux

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Meaux:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Cathedral of Saint-Etienne and the Bishop’s Palace
2 Explore the Museum of the Great War
3 Go hiking in Meaux Forest or Pâtis Route
4 Visit Les Jardins Fruitiers de Laquenexy
5 Go on a hot air balloon ride
6 Join a guided walking tour of the city
7 Relax and enjoy the city at your leisure

Food and Drink Scene of Meaux

Must-Try Dishes

Brie Cheese

Brie cheese is a signature dish of the Meaux region. This local cheese is creamy and delicious.

Pâté de Meaux

Pâté de Meaux is a smooth and savory paté that’s a popular appetizer throughout France.

Local Beverages

Champagne

Champagne is a sparkling wine produced in the nearby Champagne region.

Cider

Cider is a popular alcoholic drink made from fermented apple juice.

What to Buy in Meaux: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Brie Cheese

Brie cheese is a must-try dish in Meaux, and it’s an excellent souvenir to bring home too.

Champagne

Champagne is one of Meaux’s most popular drinks, and buying a bottle of it to take home is a great way to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Meaux

The local culture of Meaux is heavily influenced by religion and history. The locals are very proud of their heritage and are happy to share it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Meaux

The best time to visit Meaux is during the spring and fall when the temperatures are mild, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meaux

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the cultural landmarks
  • Explore the city on foot

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places
  • Ignore local customs and etiquette
  • Speak loudly or disruptively in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Meaux

Meaux is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions while traveling.

Health in Meaux

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in Meaux, so travelers should have no trouble finding medical help if needed.

Getting to Meaux: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Meaux

Meaux is conveniently located near several domestic and international airports, including:

  • Charles De Gaulle Airport
  • Orly Airport
  • Paris Vatry Airport

Bus Stations Near Meaux

The Meaux Bus Station is conveniently located in the city center and connects to many nearby towns.

Other Options

Travelers can also take a train from Paris, which takes around 25 minutes.

How to Get Around in Meaux

The best way to explore Meaux is on foot, but travelers can also take buses or taxis to reach specific destinations.

Where to Go Next from Meaux: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Meaux is close to several exciting destinations, including:

  • Paris
  • Disneyland Paris
  • Reims, famous for its champagne

Conclusion

Meaux is a charming city with a rich history and culture, offering plenty of things to see and do. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan a memorable trip to Meaux, filled with delicious cuisine, breathtaking natural scenery, and exciting cultural landmarks.
