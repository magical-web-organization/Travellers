Introduction to Marne-la-Vallée

Marne-la-Vallée is a new town in the eastern suburbs of Paris, located in the Seine-et-Marne department in Île-de-France, France. The town was built in the 1960s as a response to the housing shortage in Paris, and it is known for its world-famous Disneyland Paris theme park. Marne-la-Vallée also boasts a mix of natural beauty and modern architecture, making it a great destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Marne-la-Vallée

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marne-la-Vallée has quite a few culturally important attractions. Among them is the Château de Champs-sur-Marne, which is a beautifully restored 18th-century mansion with a stunning garden. The Musée de la Grande Guerre is a must-visit for history buffs, as it showcases the artifacts and stories from World War I. The Val d’Europe Shopping Center is also worth checking out, as it is designed in a modern and sleek style that is sure to impress.

Natural Attractions of Marne-la-Vallée: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for natural beauty, Marne-la-Vallée has plenty of options. The Parc des Félins is a beautiful park that is home to many big cats such as tigers, lions, and cheetahs. The Parc des Capucins is also worth a visit, as it is a beautiful park filled with picturesque bridges, fountains, and sculptures. The Île-de-Loisirs de Jablines-Annet is an outdoor recreational park that offers a variety of activities such as swimming, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marne-la-Vallée

Marne-la-Vallée has a wide range of hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views of the town’s natural beauty. For example, the Canal de l’Ourcq is a scenic 100-kilometer bike trail that runs alongside stunning waterways, green fields, and farms. The Coulée verte René-Dumont is a popular green walkway that stretches for 10 kilometers through the heart of Marne-la-Vallée.

Best Things to Do in Marne-la-Vallée: Activities and Tours

For the ultimate Marne-la-Vallée experience, try some of these exciting activities and tours. The Disneyland Paris theme park is, of course, a must-visit destination, especially if you are traveling with kids. The Seine River Cruise is a great way to see Paris from a different perspective, while the Champagne Tour takes visitors to the famous champagne region of France.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marne-la-Vallée

For a week-long trip to Marne-la-Vallée, a suggested itinerary could include:

  • Day 1: Disneyland Paris
  • Day 2: Val d’Europe Shopping Center and Château de Champs-sur-Marne
  • Day 3: Parc des Félins and Parc des Capucins
  • Day 4: Canal de l’Ourcq bike trail and Coulée verte René-Dumont walkway
  • Day 5: Musée de la Grande Guerre
  • Day 6: Seine River Cruise and Champagne Tour
  • Day 7: Île-de-Loisirs de Jablines-Annet

Food and Drink Scene of Marne-la-Vallée

No travel guide is complete without a section dedicated to food and drink. Marne-la-Vallée has an excellent food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local cuisine and famous beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Marne-la-Vallée include the classic French dish, coq au vin, which consists of chicken in a red wine sauce, and the buttery escargots de Bourgogne, which are baked snails. You should also try the ratatouille, a traditional French vegetable stew.

Local Beverages

Wine is the most famous beverage associated with France, and Marne-la-Vallée has some of the best. The champagne region of France is very close to Marne-la-Vallée, so it’s a great opportunity to try some of the best champagne in the world.

Where to Stay in Marne-la-Vallée: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marne-la-Vallée has plenty of options to suit any budget or style. The Disneyland Paris has its own hotels, where many visitors choose to stay. The Hotel ibis Marne La Vallee is a popular choice for its convenient location near Disneyland and the Val d’Europe Shopping Center. The Best Western Plus Hotel is another option, located in the heart of the town and within walking distance of many attractions. For budget-conscious travelers, hostels like La Ruche Montreuil are a great choice.

What to Buy in Marne-la-Vallée: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some souvenirs and local products to bring home, there are plenty of options in Marne-la-Vallée. One of the most famous products from Marne-la-Vallée is the Brie de Meaux cheese, which is known for its creamy texture and taste. French wine and champagne are also popular choices for souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Marne-la-Vallée

French culture is steeped in history, marked by famous artists and intellectuals, and known for its fashion, wine, and cuisine. In Marne-la-Vallée, it’s normal to say “bonjour” or “bonsoir” when entering a store or restaurant. Tipping is not required in France, but it is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Marne-la-Vallée

The best time to visit Marne-la-Vallée is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny. During these months, you can also enjoy many outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marne-la-Vallée

Do:

  • Say “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir” when entering a store or restaurant
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, such as champagne and Brie de Meaux cheese
  • Visit Disneyland Paris theme park and other attractions
  • Take a Seine River Cruise and Champagne Tour
  • Practice social etiquette and be polite

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring your passport or ID for traveling purposes
  • Be loud or disrespectful in public places
  • Assume English is widely spoken, try to learn some basic French
  • Be late for appointments or reservations

Safety and Health

Safety in Marne-la-Vallée

Marne-la-Vallée is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take some precautions. Make sure to keep your belongings safe and secure, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Marne-la-Vallée

France has a high standard of healthcare, and there are plenty of hospitals and clinics in Marne-la-Vallée. If you need medical attention, the nearest hospital is the Centre Hospitalier de Marne-la-Vallée.

Getting to Marne-la-Vallée: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marne-la-Vallée

The nearest airport to Marne-la-Vallée is Charles de Gaulle Airport, which is about 30 kilometers away. Another option is Paris-Orly Airport, which is about 42 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Marne-la-Vallée

Marne-la-Vallée has excellent bus service options. Lines A and B of RER serve Disneyland Resort Paris with Marne-la-Vallée–Chessy station.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Marne-la-Vallée is by train. Marne-la-Vallée has two train stations: Marne-la-Vallée-Chessy TGV and Marne-la-Vallée-Chessy RER.

How to Get Around in Marne-la-Vallée

Marne-la-Vallée is well-connected by its local transportation options. The RER commuter train network is the best way to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from Marne-la-Vallée: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marne-la-Vallée is located in the Île-de-France region, which boasts plenty of destinations and excursions. For example, Paris is just a short train ride away, and Versailles is also nearby with its stunning château and gardens. Champagne is a popular day trip from Marne-la-Vallée, which offers visitors the chance to sample some of the world’s best sparkling wine.

Conclusion

Marne-la-Vallée is a perfect destination for travelers of all ages and interests with its exciting attractions, natural beauty, and rich cultural and historical significance. From exploring its world-renowned theme park to discovering its architecture and natural wonders, Marne-la-Vallée is an unforgettable experience that is sure to captivate and inspire.
