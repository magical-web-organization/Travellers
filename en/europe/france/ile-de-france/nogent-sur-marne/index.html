Introduction to Nogent-sur-Marne

Nogent-sur-Marne is a picturesque town located in the eastern suburbs of Paris, France. With its charming streets, beautiful parks, and quaint buildings, it is a must-visit destination. Nogent-sur-Marne offers a perfect escape from the hectic city life with its calm and peaceful atmosphere. Whether you are an art lover, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie, this town has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Nogent-sur-Marne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Museum of Nogent-sur-Marne

The Museum of Nogent-sur-Marne is a museum that features the region’s history as well as past local industries. Here you can find various artefacts such as ceramics, sculptures, and other objects related to the town’s rich history.

The Church of Notre Dame des Rivières

The Church of Notre Dame des Rivières is a beautiful building that dates back to the 12th century. Here, visitors can admire the unique architecture with its beautiful stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Nogent-sur-Marne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bois de Vincennes

Bois de Vincennes is a large park that offers scenic views, boat hire or rental, pony rides, and has plenty of picnic areas. Visitors can enjoy the walking paths, bike trails, and nature during their visit.

The Marne River

The Marne River, which runs through the town, is a great spot for water sports such as kayaking and canoeing. It is lined with beautiful trees and offers a serene place to enjoy a walk or picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nogent-sur-Marne

There are many scenic routes and trails to explore in Nogent-sur-Marne. The Bois de Vincennes has both biking and walking trails, including the Allée Royale which features some of the most scenic and serene views.

Best Things to Do in Nogent-sur-Marne: Activities and Tours

Take a boat ride on the Marne River

Rent a boat and explore the scenic Marne River while taking in the picturesque views of the town.

Take a guided tour of the Museum of Nogent-sur-Marne

Discover the town’s rich history by taking a guided tour of the Museum of Nogent-sur-Marne with an expert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nogent-sur-Marne

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Nogent-sur-Marne and explore the nearby areas. Day 2: Take a bike ride in Bois de Vincennes and have a picnic. Day 3: Take a boat tour of Marne River. Day 4: Visit the Church of Notre Dame des Rivières. Day 5: Explore the shops and local culture of the town. Day 6: Visit the nearby town of Fontenay-sous-Bois. Day 7: Relax and enjoy a day in the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Nogent-sur-Marne

Must-Try Dishes

Brie de Meaux

Brie de Meaux is a tasty and creamy cheese that is a regional specialty and must-try when visiting the town.

Gougères

Gougères are small, savory pastries filled with cheese that are popular in the region.

Local Beverages

French Wine

The region is known for its wine, and dining in town offers the opportunity to have an authentic wine experience.

Where to Stay in Nogent-sur-Marne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Le Nogentel

Le Nogentel is a mid-range hotel located conveniently and has great amenities and services for guests.

ibis Nogent sur Marne

Ibis Nogent sur Marne is a budget hotel with excellent reviews to provide an affordable stay in town.

What to Buy in Nogent-sur-Marne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take something special home from Nogent-sur-Marne, shop for delicious French pastries or regional wines to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Nogent-sur-Marne

The town has a charming and slow-paced environment, with friendly locals who appreciate visitors who are respectful of their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Nogent-sur-Marne

The best time to visit Nogent-sur-Marne is spring time (May) or autumn (October) to avoid the crowds that come in during the summer months, as well as to witness the town’s natural beauty as the leaves change colour during the fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nogent-sur-Marne

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be mindful of the environment when visiting the beautiful parks.
  • Practice basic French words such as greetings and simple phrases.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Leave trash or litter behind.
  • Ignore the polite French customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nogent-sur-Marne

Nogent-sur-Marne is a safe place to visit. You should follow standard safety precautions and be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Nogent-sur-Marne

There are no special health precautions needed when visiting Nogent-sur-Marne. However, it is always recommended to have travel insurance when travelling abroad to ensure you are protected when facing unforeseen health issues.

Getting to Nogent-sur-Marne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nogent-sur-Marne

The Charles de Gaulle Airport and Orly airports are both great options for flying into Paris and then taking the train to Nogent-sur-Marne.

Bus Stations Near Nogent-sur-Marne

Both FlixBus and Eurolines have daily buses from Paris and other neighbouring cities to Nogent-sur-Marne.

Other Options

If trains and buses aren’t an option, you can hire a private taxi to take you to Nogent-sur-Marne.

How to Get Around in Nogent-sur-Marne

Walking or biking around the town would be the best way to get around the scenic parks and streets of Nogent-sur-Marne.

Where to Go Next from Nogent-sur-Marne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fontenay-sous-Bois

The nearby town of Fontenay-sous-Bois is a quaint, smaller version of Paris, without the hustle and bustle.

Créteil

Créteil is another nearby town that has a charming city vibe with numerous options for dining and exploring.

Conclusion

With its historical museums, scenic parks, and unique culture and customs, Nogent-sur-Marne is a wonderful destination that has something to offer all its visitors. Be sure to visit this lovely town to experience the best of what France has to offer.
