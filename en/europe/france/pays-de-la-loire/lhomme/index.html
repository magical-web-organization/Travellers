Introduction to Lhomme

Situated in the stunning region of Pays de la Loire in western France, Lhomme is a picturesque town surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills and the tranquil Sarthe River. This hidden gem offers visitors an authentic experience of French rural life, with a rich history and culture to discover.

List of Places to Visit in Lhomme

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ecomusée du Perche Sarthois

Explore the history of rural life in Sarthe through the collection of traditional tools, clothes, and objects of everyday life at Ecomusée du Perche Sarthois. The museum aims to preserve the memory of the local culture and traditions of the Perche region and offers visitors an immersive experience in the past.

Château de Lude

Built in the 15th century, the Château de Lude is a stunning fortress that showcases the splendor of Renaissance architecture. Visitors can explore the castle’s fascinating history, including its transformation from a medieval fortress to a stately château.

Natural Attractions of Lhomme: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vallee du Loir Natural Park

Located near Lhomme, the Vallee du Loir Natural Park is a haven for nature lovers and outdoor adventurers. Tourists can hike through dense forests, spot various flora and fauna, and admire the stunning landscapes of the Loir River Valley.

Sarthe River

A visit to Lhomme would not be complete without a relaxed walk along the Sarthe River. Admire the serene natural beauty and wildlife while enjoying some peace and quiet.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lhomme

Chemin des Dames

The “Ladies’ Road” offers visitors a scenic and peaceful walk that takes you along the Sarthe riverbanks. En route, visitors can stop and visit historic buildings, such as the Saint-Martin Church.

Circuit du Haut Anjou

One of the best cycling routes around Lhomme is the Circuit du Haut Anjou. The winding roads and tranquil countryside scenery make this route a perfect way to spend a relaxing day in the picturesque Pays de la Loire region.

Best Things to Do in Lhomme: Activities and Tours

Canoeing on the Sarthe River

One of the must-do activities for visitors to Lhomme is to rent a canoe from a nearby rental company and paddle your way down the tranquil Sarthe River. Enjoy the scenic beauty and explore the wildlife of Pays de la Loire at your leisure.

Hot-Air Balloon Ride

An exciting way to witness the stunning landscapes of Lhomme is to take a hot-air balloon ride. Get a bird’s-eye view of the town and its breathtaking surroundings while enjoying an unforgettable adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Lhomme

Must-Try Dishes

Rillettes

A traditional dish from Sarthe, rillettes are made from pork belly or pork loin that is slow-cooked with onions, garlic, and herbs. It’s perfect as a spread on baguette slices with a glass of wine.

Tarte Tatin

Indulge in a slice of the famous French dessert, Tarte Tatin, a caramelized apple tart that originated in the Loire Valley.

Local Beverages

Coteaux du Loir Wine

Lhomme is situated in the heart of the Coteaux du Loir wine region, known for its crisp whites and fruity reds. Be sure to sample the local wines while visiting and take home a bottle or two as a souvenir.

Where to Stay in Lhomme: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel de la Gare

The Hotel de la Gare is a cozy, family-run hotel located near the train station. It offers clean and comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

Le Moulin de Monternault

For those looking for a romantic and tranquil place to stay, Le Moulin de Monternault is an excellent choice. Set in a beautifully restored water mill, it offers comfortable rooms and a charming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lhomme: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sarthe Honey

Sarthe is known for its delicious honey. Grab a jar or two to take home and enjoy on your toast or in your tea.

Handmade Pottery

Lhomme’s artisans specialize in crafting beautiful, handmade pottery. Head to one of the local shops and pick up a unique piece to add to your collection.

Local Culture and Customs of Lhomme

The people of Lhomme are proud of their rich culture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to take part in local festivals and events throughout the year, where they can witness traditional dances, try local food and drinks, and mingle with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Lhomme

The best time to visit Lhomme is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months can get busy and hot, but it’s an excellent time to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lhomme

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the local cultural and historical attractions
  • Take part in local festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Wear loud or revealing clothing in religious or historical places
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Lhomme

Lhomme is a safe and friendly town, with hardly any crime. However, visitors should still be careful when walking alone or at night and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Lhomme

The health care system in France is excellent, and visitors can easily access doctors and hospitals in case of an emergency.

Getting to Lhomme: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lhomme

The nearest airport to Lhomme is Tours Airport, located about 70 km away.

Bus Stations Near Lhomme

The town has several bus stops that connect to various locations across the Loire Valley, including Le Mans and Tours.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car from Nantes or Tours and drive to Lhomme, which takes approximately two hours.

How to Get Around in Lhomme

The best way to explore Lhomme is on foot or by bike. There are also local bus services that connect the town to nearby attractions and neighboring towns.

Where to Go Next from Lhomme: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Le Mans

Just a short drive away is the bustling city of Le Mans, famous for its 24-hour car race and historic landmarks, such as the Cathedral of St Julien and the Palace of the Counts of Maine.

Tours

Located about an hour and a half from Lhomme, Tours is a charming city known for its stunning architecture, gourmet cuisine, and beautiful gardens.

Conclusion

Lhomme is a unique and charming destination that offers visitors a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or exploring the local cuisine, this hidden gem in Pays de la Loire is sure to leave a lasting impression.
