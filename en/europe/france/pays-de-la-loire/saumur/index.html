Introduction to Saumur

Saumur is a small town located in the beautiful region of Pays de la Loire in France. Situated along the banks of the Loire River, this charming town is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and world-renowned wine. Saumur is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, culture seekers, and foodies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Saumur

Saumur offers a variety of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some must-visit places in Saumur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château de Saumur: This impressive castle dates back to the 14th century and is famous for being one of the most beautiful castles in the Loire Valley. Visitors can enjoy a self-guided tour and explore the castle’s many rooms and courtyards.
  • Musée des Blindés: This museum is a must-visit for history and military enthusiasts. It features an impressive collection of tanks and armored vehicles from various wars and conflicts.
  • Musée de la Cavalerie: Located within the walls of the Château de Saumur, this museum focuses on the history of cavalry and horseback riding. Visitors can learn about the important role that horses played in combat and everyday life.

Natural Attractions of Saumur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loire River: Take a leisurely stroll along the banks of the Loire River and enjoy the stunning views of the town’s historic bridges and buildings. Visitors can also take a boat tour along the river and learn about the history of Saumur.
  • Le Cadre Noir: This prestigious horse-riding school is a true gem of Saumur. Visitors can attend a show and watch the riders and horses perform synchronized movements and jumps.
  • Parc de la Vallée: This beautiful park offers a variety of activities for visitors of all ages, including a playground, mini-golf course, and zip line. The park is also home to a diverse range of wildlife and flora.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saumur

Saumur is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are many hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the best trails include:

  • La Loire à Vélo: This 900-kilometer route runs along the Loire River and is a great way to explore the region’s beautiful countryside and picturesque villages.
  • Les Rives de Thouet: This 18-kilometer trail runs along the banks of the Thouet River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Parcours de La Pomme: This challenging mountain biking route takes you through the beautiful forests of the Anjou region and offers stunning panoramic views of Saumur and the Loire Valley.

Best Things to Do in Saumur: Activities and Tours

Saumur offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Saumur:

  • Wine Tasting: Saumur is known for its world-renowned wine, and visitors can enjoy a variety of wine tasting tours and experiences throughout the town and surrounding vineyards.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the stunning views of the Loire Valley from above with a hot air balloon ride. This is a truly unforgettable experience that is sure to leave you with lasting memories.
  • Horseback Riding: Saumur is the perfect destination for horse lovers, and visitors can enjoy a variety of horseback riding tours and experiences throughout the town and surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saumur

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Saumur, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Visit the Château de Saumur and explore the town’s historic center.
  • Day 2: Take a wine tasting tour of the nearby vineyards.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a hot air balloon ride over the Loire Valley.
  • Day 4: Visit the Musée des Blindés and learn about military history and vehicles.
  • Day 5: Take a horseback riding tour of the nearby countryside.
  • Day 6: Explore the Parc de la Vallée and enjoy the park’s many activities.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the town’s many cafes, restaurants, and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Saumur

Saumur is known for its world-class wine and delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fricassée de Poulet: This traditional chicken dish is made with white wine, cream, and mushrooms and is a true Saumur classic.
  • Tarte Tatin: This classic French dessert features caramelized apples and a buttery pastry crust and is a must-try during your visit.
  • Goulineau: This delicious sausage dish is made with pork, garlic, and red wine and is a staple of the Saumur region.

Local Beverages

  • Saumur-Champigny: This red wine is known for its light, fruity flavor and is made from Cabernet Franc grapes grown in the region.
  • Coteaux de Saumur: This white wine is known for its hint of grapefruit and citrus and pairs perfectly with seafood and light salads.
  • Bière de Loire: This locally brewed beer is a crisp and refreshing option for those looking to try something new.

Where to Stay in Saumur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saumur offers a variety of accommodations for visitors of all budgets. Some of the best places to stay in Saumur include:

  • Château de Verrières: This stunning chateau turned hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites overlooking the town and surrounding countryside.
  • La Sterne de Loire: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in the heart of the town and offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Camping Saumur: This camping site is a great budget-friendly option for those looking to enjoy the great outdoors during their visit.

What to Buy in Saumur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saumur is known for its many local products and souvenirs. Here are some must-buy items to bring home with you:

  • Wine: Saumur is known for its world-renowned wine, and visitors can bring home a bottle (or two) to enjoy later.
  • Hand-Painted Pottery: The town is home to many talented artists and pottery workshops where visitors can purchase unique, hand-painted pieces.
  • Saffron: The nearby village of Turquant is renowned for its saffron production, and visitors can purchase the spice from local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Saumur

Saumur is a town rich in history and culture, and there are many local customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit. The French language is spoken throughout the town, and visitors should be sure to greet locals with “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir” and say “Merci” or “Au Revoir” when leaving. When dining at a restaurant, it is customary to leave a small tip (usually around 10%).

Best Time to Visit Saumur

The best time to visit Saumur is during the spring and summer months (April to September) when the weather is warm and the town is bustling with outdoor activities and events. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s many attractions during the fall and winter months (October to March) when the crowds are smaller and the town is quieter.

Safety and Health

Saumur is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should still exercise caution and common sense. Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

France has a world-class healthcare system, and visitors to Saumur can expect quality medical care if needed. However, it is always a good idea to bring any necessary medications and travel insurance during your trip.

Getting to Saumur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saumur is easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Saumur

The nearest airport to Saumur is the Tours Val de Loire Airport (approximately 66km away). Visitors can also fly into the Paris-Charles de Gaulle Airport and take a train or hire a car to get to Saumur.

Bus Stations Near Saumur

Saumur’s main bus station is located in the town center and offers regular service to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to get to Saumur from nearby towns or airports.

How to Get Around in Saumur

Saumur is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, visitors can also rent a bike or take a bus or taxi to get around the town and surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Saumur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion
