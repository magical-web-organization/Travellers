Introduction to Les Sables d’Olonne

Located in the French region of Pays de la Loire, Les Sables d’Olonne is a picturesque seaside resort town that boasts sparkling sandy beaches, and a captivating maritime heritage. Situated on the Atlantic coast of France, the town is famous for its historical connections to sailing, shipbuilding and fishing. Les Sables d’Olonne has something to offer everyone, whether it’s walking along the promenade enjoying the sea views, exploring the town’s museums and historical sites, or taking an outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Les Sables d’Olonne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Les Sables d’Olonne has a rich culture and history which can be explored by visiting its numerous museums and historical sites. Some of the most popular places to visit include:

  • Vendee Globe Village: A must-visit destination for sailing enthusiasts, the Vendee Globe Village is a free museum that tells the story of the famous Vendee Globe Round the World race, which departs from Les Sables d’Olonne every four years.
  • Musee de l’Abbaye de Sainte-Croix: An art museum located in a former 17th-century abbey, this museum boasts an impressive collection of modern art, including works by Arman, Viallat, and Tinguely.
  • Chateau de Talmont-Saint-Hilaire: A medieval castle located just a few miles outside of Les Sables d’Olonne, the Chateau de Talmont-Saint-Hilaire offers a fantastic glimpse into the region’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of Les Sables d’Olonne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Les Sables d’Olonne is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The town offers an array of outdoor activities such as:

  • Beach Activities: Les Sables d’Olonne has miles of stunning and sandy beaches, perfect for sunbathing and swimming.
  • Boat Tour: Take a boat ride to explore the expansive harbor and admire the beautiful coast.
  • Cycling: With excellent bike paths throughout the town, Les Sables d’Olonne is a great place to explore on two wheels.
  • Puy du Fou: Located 70 km from Les Sables d’Olonne, this historical theme park offers a unique selection of outdoor shows and experiences that reveal the history of France.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Les Sables d’Olonne

Hiking and biking around Les Sables d’Olonne is the best way to enjoy the natural beauty of the area. Some of the best routes to consider include:

  • Sentier des Douaniers: This trail is a great way to explore the coast. It runs between Saint-Gilles-Croix-de-Vie and Les Sables d’Olonne, offering stunning views of the ocean.
  • Le Parcours Aventure: This is a fun tree-top adventure park where visitors can zip-line through the trees or try other outdoor activities.

Best Things to Do in Les Sables d’Olonne: Activities and Tours

Les Sables d’Olonne offers an array of exciting things to do and tour options to choose from. Whether you want to explore the town’s historical sites or indulge in its outdoor adventures, some of the best things to do are:

  • Visit the Town’s Markets: Les Sables d’Olonne is known for its fantastic outdoor markets where visitors can experience the local culture and find great bargains.
  • Boat Tour of the Harbor: Taking a boat tour is a fantastic way to see the traditional ships and yachts that occupy the harbor.
  • Wine Tasting at Domaine Saint-Nicolas: Located a few miles outside of town, Domaine Saint-Nicolas is a vineyard that offers a unique wine-tasting experience to its visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Les Sables d’Olonne

To make the most out of your trip to Les Sables d’Olonne, it’s recommended that you create a week-long itinerary that includes all the must-see places. Some of the suggested itinerary options include:

Day 1-2: Spend the first two days exploring Les Sables d’Olonne’s beautiful beaches and indulging in outdoor activities like bike-riding, swimming, and boat tours.

Day 3-4: Explore the town’s cultural attractions by visiting museums, Chateau de Talmont-Saint-Hilaire castle, and the Abbaye de Sainte-Croix art museum.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the historic theme park Puy du Fou.

Day 6-7: Wrap up your stay by taking a wine-tasting tour at Domaine Saint-Nicolas and take a bike ride around the Sentier des Douaniers trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Les Sables d’Olonne

Les Sables d’Olonne contains a vibrant local food and drink scene, offering delicious dishes and beverages unique to the region.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mouclade: A local specialty of Les Sables d’Olonne, Mouclade is made from mussels that are cooked in cream and white wine.

  • Tourtisseau des Sables d’Olonne: A traditional pastry flavored with candied fruits, this sweet treat pairs well with coffee or tea.

Local Beverages

  • Vin de Pays des Fiefs-Vendéens: Recognized for its distinctive taste, this regional wine is made from grape varieties rarely found in other parts of France.

  • Pineau des Charentes: A fortified wine made by the mixing of grape juice and cognac, Pineau des Charentes is a popular local drink known across France.

Where to Stay in Les Sables d’Olonne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Les Sables d’Olonne offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different preferences and budgets. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Hotel Atlantic: Located right on the beach, Hotel Atlantic offers stunning views of the ocean, and guests can enjoy tasty seafood at the hotel restaurant.

  • Les Hirondelles: Located in the town center, Les Hirondelles is a charming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms, a garden, and a relaxing terrace.

What to Buy in Les Sables d’Olonne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Les Sables d’Olonne with you, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are a few local products and souvenirs to consider:

  • Fleur de Sel: A type of sea salt harvested in the nearby salt marshes. It is a popular local specialty that can be found in the town’s markets and gift shops.

  • Blue and White Striped Clothing: A classic Breton style that originated in the maritime region of Brittany. It can be found in various shops around Les Sables d’Olonne.

Local Culture and Customs of Les Sables d’Olonne

Les Sables d’Olonne has a rich maritime history, which has significantly impacted the local traditions and customs. The town has an array of unique customs, including its maritime-themed festivals, such as the Vendée Globe Round the World race, which is held every four years.

Best Time to Visit Les Sables d’Olonne

The best time to visit Les Sables d’Olonne is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town’s outdoor activities and attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Les Sables d’Olonne

Do:

  • Try the local seafood. Being a fishing port, Les Sables d’Olonne boasts exceptional seafood dishes.
  • Participate in the town’s maritime-themed festivals, such as the Vendée Globe Round the World race.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the town or on the beach.
  • Don’t forget sunscreen and beachwear when visiting in the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Les Sables d’Olonne

Les Sables d’Olonne is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always advisable to keep valuables such as wallet, passport, and phones in a secure location, and avoid any scantily lit areas in the night.

Health in Les Sables d’Olonne

French health care is generally of a high standard. However, it’s recommended to have comprehensive health insurance before traveling to Les Sables d’Olonne.

How to Get Around in Les Sables d’Olonne

Les Sables d’Olonne is a relatively small town, and most of the attractions can be reached easily by walking. However, if you prefer other transportation modes, there are additional options, including:

  • Bicycles: Les Sables d’Olonne boasts excellent bike paths that make it easy and convenient to rent and use a bike to get around.
  • Taxis: Local taxis are available in Les Sables d’Olonne and can be used to get around town or to travel to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Les Sables d’Olonne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Les Sables d’Olonne is a fantastic base from which to explore other nearby destinations. Here are a few recommended day-trip options:

  • La Rochelle: Located about 150 km north of Les Sables d’Olonne, La Rochelle is a stunning coastal city that boasts picturesque streets, architecture, and history.
  • Nantes: Located about 115 km east of Les Sables d’Olonne, Nantes is a vibrant cosmopolitan city with an array of attractions, including historical landmarks, artistic venues, and excellent restaurants.

Conclusion

Les Sables d’Olonne is a fantastic destination that offers an array of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and an abundance of local food and drink scene. The town is an ideal location for anyone looking to enjoy a mix of relaxation and excitement. From its beautiful beaches to its maritime-themed festivals and attractions, Les Sables d’Olonne has something for everyone to enjoy.
