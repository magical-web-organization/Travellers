Introduction to Saint-Nazaire

Saint-Nazaire is a quaint port town on the Atlantic coast of France, in the Pays de la Loire region. It has a rich history of shipbuilding and was once a major strategic location during World War II. Today, it is becoming a popular tourist destination because of its beautiful beaches, historical sites, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Nazaire

There are many places to visit in Saint-Nazaire. Here are a few must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Escal’Atlantic - A museum dedicated to the history of transatlantic liners, located inside a former submarine base.
  2. Ecomusée - A museum showcasing the cultural heritage of Pays de la Loire.
  3. The Submarine Base - A Nazi bunker converted into an art center that holds occasional exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Nazaire: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Plage de la Courance - A sandy beach spanning over a mile long with clear water suitable for swimming and water sports.
  2. Parc Paysager - A picturesque park with exotic trees, small ponds, and playgrounds for children.
  3. The Saint-Nazaire Bridge - A stunning suspension bridge stretching over 3 km connecting Saint-Nazaire to Saint-Brevin-Les-Pins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Nazaire

Saint-Nazaire is a great place to engage in outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes:

  • The Sentier des Daims - A 5km circular path in the Parc Paysager that passes through a deer park.
  • La Loire à Vélo - A 900km route along the Loire River that passes through Saint-Nazaire.
  • Les Marais Salants - An 8 km track alongside salt marshes with views of the Loire estuary.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Nazaire: Activities and Tours

Here are some fun and exciting things to do in Saint-Nazaire:

  • Take a boat tour to explore the Loire estuary and the numerous islands it houses.
  • Visit the Airbus Plant - The largest in Europe - and learn about the production of airplanes.
  • Try out water sports like jet skiing, kayaking, and surfing on the beaches of Saint-Nazaire.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Nazaire

If you are planning a week-long stay in Saint-Nazaire, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit The Submarine Base in the morning, go to Plage de la Courance in the afternoon
2 Take a boat tour to explore the Loire Estuary
3 Head out to Ecomusée in the morning, visit Escal’Atlantic in the afternoon
4 Visit Parc Paysager in the morning, take the Sentier Des Daims by noon
5 Take La Loire à Vélo in the morning, visit the Airbus plant in the afternoon

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Nazaire

Saint-Nazaire is known for its seafood and other delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mussels prepared with cream and butter
  • Oysters served fresh with a squeeze of lemon
  • Fried sardines served with garlic butter

Local Beverages

  • Muscadet wine - a crisp white wine grown on the nearby Loire Valley vineyards
  • Guérande salted beer - a beer brewed using Guérande salt for a unique taste

Where to Stay in Saint-Nazaire: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Nazaire has several accommodation options that suit every budget and preference.

  • Hermitage Hotel - A luxurious hotel with a private beach and elegant rooms with sea views.
  • Hotel La Luna - A family-friendly hotel situated near the beaches and parks.
  • Saint-Nazaire Youth Hostel - A budget hostel suited for backpackers and students.

What to Buy in Saint-Nazaire: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Saint-Nazaire:

  • Sea Salt - salt harvested from the salt marshes near Guérande and packaged in pretty jars.
  • Biscuits - shortbread cookies made in Saint Nazaire with local butter and flour.
  • Chocolates - handmade pralines with a unique filling of sea salt caramel.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Nazaire

The locals of Saint-Nazaire are reserved but friendly and hospitable to visitors. The culture of Saint-Nazaire is influenced by its maritime and shipbuilding history, with festivals and events related to these themes held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Nazaire

The best time to visit Saint-Nazaire is from June to September when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, it’s worth noting that this is also the peak tourism season, which means higher prices and more crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Nazaire

Do:

  • Get lost in the vibrant street markets in the city center.
  • Respect the environment, and avoid littering in natural areas.
  • Order seafood dishes in restaurants as they are some of the best in the region.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes with high heels when visiting the beach.
  • Forget to try the local wines and seafood as they are a significant part of the culinary scene in Saint-Nazaire.
  • Speak loudly in public places as it’s considered impolite in most establishments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Nazaire

Saint-Nazaire is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates. However, it’s essential to take precautions, such as watching your belongings in crowded areas and not leaving valuables in your car or hotel room.

Health in Saint-Nazaire

There are several pharmacies and healthcare centers in Saint-Nazaire that offer quality services. Saint-Nazaire also has many hospitals and clinics providing medical care and emergency services.

Getting to Saint-Nazaire: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Nazaire

The nearest airports to Saint-Nazaire are:

  • Nantes Atlantique Airport, located 45 km away from the town center.
  • Rennes Bretagne Airport, located 135 km away from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Nazaire

Saint-Nazaire has a well-developed network of buses and trains, so getting around is easy and convenient. A few popular bus companies operating in Saint-Nazaire are:

  • Lila Presqu’Ile
  • Stran

Other Options

There are also taxis available in Saint-Nazaire. You can also rent a car to drive around.

How to Get Around in Saint-Nazaire

Walking is the best way to explore the town center. However, you will need to use public transport, rent a bike, or use taxis to get around to more distant destinations.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Nazaire: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Saint-Nazaire:

  • Visit the charming seaside town of La Baule, located just 20 km from Saint-Nazaire.
  • Explore the medieval castle town of Guérande and the nearby salt marshes.
  • Head to the city of Nantes, located 60 km from Saint-Nazaire, to visit the famous Castle of the Dukes of Brittany and the Jardin des Plantes.

Conclusion

Saint-Nazaire is the perfect destination for those who love history, nature, and delicious food. With its abundance of attractions and activities, it is sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits. Plan your trip to Saint-Nazaire today and experience the wonder of this charming French port town.
