Introduction to La Baule

La Baule is a small coastal town located in the Loire-Atlantique department of Pays de la Loire, France. The town is known for its stunning sandy beach, blue waters, and high-end resorts lining its shores.

List of Places to Visit in La Baule

There are plenty of attractions and landmarks to check out while in La Baule. Here is a list of the most popular destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Baule Escoublac Train Station: Explore the picturesque railway station featuring a unique Moorish architecture.
  • Musée Aeronautique de la Presqu’île Côte d’Amour: Discover an extensive collection of vintage and modern aircraft, including WWII planes and helicopters.
  • Château de Careil: Take a trip to the 19th-century castle and roam around its gardens and winery.

Natural Attractions of La Baule: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaches of La Baule: offering a seven-kilometer-long stretch of sandy beach with various water sports such as kite surfing, paddleboarding, and wind-surfing.
  • Marais Salants: A salty marsh and humid ecosystem that provides a habitat for plant and animal life as well as provides salt.
  • Brière Nature Park: An ideal excursion for birdwatchers with over 300 species of birds and canals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Baule

La Baule has many scenic walking and cycling routes to explore its beauty. If you’re into hiking, here are a few routes worth checking out:

  • Sentier du Douanier: An easy route offering stunning views of the coast and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Le Parc des Dryades: A tranquil park with lush forests, ponds, and tracks for jogging or walking.

Best Things to Do in La Baule: Activities and Tours

La Baule has an extensive range of activities, from water sports to sightseeing tours. Here is a list of some must-try activities:

  • Jet ski and Parasailing: Explore the area’s natural beauty through a high-speed Jet ski or flying high in the sky through parasailing.
  • Cycle Tours: A cycle tour of the countryside near La Baule and explore its vineyards and local specialties.
  • Vineyard visits: Visit one of the several local wineries, such as the ‘SCEA Domaine des 3 celliers,’ and taste its delicious wine varieties.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Baule

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to La Baule:

Day Activity
Day 1 Stroll along the seven kilometers of beaches.
Day 2 Visit Château de Careil and its beautiful gardens.
Day 3 Visit Parc des Dryades and go cycling.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Brière Nature Park
Day 5 Go for a sightseeing tour to the Musée Aeronautique.
Day 6 Enjoy watersports like jet skiing or parasailing.
Day 7 Explore Le Sentier du Douanier.

Food and Drink Scene of La Baule

The food scene in La Baule offers a rich variety of local specialties and cuisine from classic French dishes to international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moules Frites: a classic French dish made with fresh mussels and fries.
  • Andouille: A tangy local sausage that has been smoked over beechwood before being cooked.
  • Salicorne: A plant of the coastal region that grows and sold in jars.

Local Beverages

  • Muscadet: A dry white wine that pairs well with seafood dishes.
  • Coteaux d’Ancenis: A regional sweet wine that goes well with desserts and cheese.

Where to Stay in La Baule: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Baule has a wide range of accommodations with options ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here’s a list of the top-rated places to stay in La Baule:

  • Hermitage Barriere Hotel: A luxury 5-star hotel suitable for travelers looking for an exclusive stay.
  • Mercure La Baule Majestic: A mid-budget hotel, perfect for families and couples.
  • Hotel La Mascotte: A cozy guesthouse perfect for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in La Baule: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Salt of Guérande: A high-quality salt produced in Guérande.
  • Breton Stripes: Traditional blue striped nautical shirts made from local cloth.

Local Culture and Customs of La Baule

La Baule is a blend of modern-rural lifestyle enriched by its history and culture. The French people are polite and courteous, and it’s customary to greet people with “Bonjour”.

Best Time to Visit La Baule

The best time to visit La Baule is during the summer, from June to August. With warm temperatures and long days, visitors can make the most of the scenic beaches and lively nightlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Baule

Do:

  • Spend time exploring the seven-kilometer beach stretch of La Baule.
  • Greet locals with a ‘Bonjour.’
  • Enjoy the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Leave without tasting the local cuisine.
  • Forget to carry an umbrella and waterproof jacket during the summer.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Baule

La Baule is a relatively safe place to visit, like the rest of France. However, travelers need to be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas and keep their personal belongings safe.

Health in La Baule

La Baule has medical facilities with trained professionals that can offer first-class medical care.

Getting to La Baule: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Baule

The Nantes Atlantique Airport is the closest airport to La Baule and is located about 80 kilometers away from the town.

Bus Stations Near La Baule

There are several bus services that operate in La Baule, including the Ctrl bus services that link central Nantes to La Baule.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach La Baule by train from Paris or Nantes. The La Baule Escoublac railway station is the closest railway station and is just a few steps from the beach.

How to Get Around La Baule

La Baule is a small town that is easy to navigate, and most destinations are within walking or cycling distance.

Where to Go Next from La Baule: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

La Baule is an ideal base for exploring nearby areas and attractions such as:

  • Nantes: A city known for its art and history.
  • Saint-Nazaire: A city with rich seaport and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

La Baule is an excellent destination for those inquiring French beauty, culture, and food. Whether you are looking for a relaxing experience or an adventure, La Baule is the place to visit. Enjoy the endless stretches of sandy beaches, exciting tours, and local specialties while being surrounded by lush greenery and historic architecture.
