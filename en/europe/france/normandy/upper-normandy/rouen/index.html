Introduction to Rouen

Located in the Upper Normandy region, Rouen is one of the most vibrant cities in France, filled with culture, history, and gastronomic delights. This ancient city is situated on the Seine River and features stunning architecture and a rich tapestry of events throughout the year. Whether you are a foodie, history buff, or outdoor enthusiast, Rouen has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Rouen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rouen is a city steeped in history and boasts an array of museums and historical sites:

  • Le Gros-Horloge: The Gros-Horloge is an astronomical clock that dates back to the 14th century. It is located in the heart of the city and is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Rouen.
  • Rouen Cathedral: This 800-year-old gothic cathedral is located in the heart of Rouen and is famous for Monet’s paintings.
  • Joan of Arc Museum: The museum houses an impressive collection of art and artifacts relating to Joan of Arc, including a statue dedicated to Joan in Place du Vieux-Marché where she was burnt at the stake.
  • Historial Jeanne d’Arc: This multimedia museum is dedicated to the life and death of Joan of Arc. Visitors can experience her thrilling story through interactive exhibits and multimedia displays.

Natural Attractions of Rouen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rouen has vast natural attractions that offer breathtaking views and outdoor activities:

  • Jardin des Plantes: This beautiful botanical garden was established in the 18th century and is home to an impressive collection of exotic plant species.
  • Bois des Moutiers: The Bois des Moutiers is a stunning park filled with trees, flowers, and a beautiful house designed by British architect Edwin Lutyens.
  • Foret Domaniale de Roumare: This forest is home to a variety of wildlife and is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, and cycling on the trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rouen

Rouen has several hiking and biking trails for visitors:

  • La Boucle des Coteaux: This 10 km route takes you through the hills overlooking Rouen, offering beautiful views of the city.
  • Le Tour des Lacs: A 20 km route that takes you around the six lakes in the area, providing an immersive experience of Rouen’s nature.
  • Les Boucles de la Seine: This 41 km trail along the Seine is perfect for cycling enthusiasts. The trail offers stunning views of the Seine valley, with several stops along the way to explore Rouen’s hidden gems.

Best Things to Do in Rouen: Activities and Tours

  • Street art tour: Rouen is a haven for street art lovers, and a tour is a great way to experience the city’s vibrant art scene.
  • Cruising on the Seine: Take a relaxing cruise on the Seine and enjoy the views of the city from the river.
  • Gourmet tour: Rouen’s food scene is something special, with several tours offering visitors an opportunity to sample local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rouen

Here is a sample itinerary for the best way to spend a week in Rouen:

Day 1: Visit the Gothic Rouen Cathedral, Gros-Horloge, and Old Town. Day 2: Discover Rouen’s art museums and galleries. Day 3: Hike in the Roumare Forest or explore the Jardin des Plantes. Day 4: Visit the Historial Jeanne d’Arc and the Joan of Arc Museum. Day 5: Take a food tour, sampling regional specialties. Day 6: Take a day trip to Giverny to visit Monet’s house and garden. Day 7: Cruise on the Seine River and enjoy the city views.

Food and Drink Scene of Rouen

Must-Try Dishes

Rouen is famous for its gastronomy, and these are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Canard à la Rouennaise: A roast duck dish that is served rare, cooked in an intricate process for over 10 hours.
  • Tarte Normande: An apple tart made with sweet shortcrust pastry and Normandy apples.
  • Andouillette: A traditional pork sausage made with tripe that is popular in Rouen’s restaurants.

Local Beverages

Rouen is home to several breweries, cideries, and distilleries, making it a fantastic place to sample local beverages. These are some of the must-try drinks:

  • Cidre: Normandy’s calvados apple cider is the most famous drink in the region.
  • Pommeau: A combination of apple juice and Calvados, a smooth and sweet alcoholic drink.
  • Calvados: A famous apple brandy that the region is known for.

Where to Stay in Rouen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rouen has a variety of accommodations to fit any budget:

  • Hotel de la Cathedrale: Located in the Old Town, near the Cathedral and the Gros-Horloge clock
  • Hotel Rouen Saint Sever: Located in the Saint-Sever district, which is famous for its shopping and restaurants.
  • Gite des Carmes: A charming B&B located in the heart of Rouen’s old town.

What to Buy in Rouen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rouen has several markets and shops where you can buy local products and souvenirs:

  • Marché Saint-Marc: A farmer’s market that is open every Saturday morning, selling locally grown fruit and vegetables.
  • Aux Grands Magasins: A lifestyle store that sells unique souvenirs and gifts.
  • Maison Guillaume: An upscale store that sells local delicacies, including chocolate, cheese, and cider.

Local Culture and Customs of Rouen

French is the official language in Rouen, but many people also speak English. When visiting, it’s good to learn some basic French phrases.

Best Time to Visit Rouen

The best time to visit Rouen is from April to October, as the weather is mild and there are several annual festivals taking place throughout the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rouen

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect Rouen’s architecture, history and culture.
  • Take time to appreciate the natural beauty of the city.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.
  • Be rude to the locals.
  • Wear shorts in churches, cathedrals, or museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rouen

Rouen is generally a safe city, but be careful when walking around alone at night and in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Rouen

Rouen has several hospitals and clinics that can help in case of an emergency.

Getting to Rouen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rouen

  • Rouen Airport: Rouen airport is the closest airport, located 10 km from the city center.
  • Deauville-Normandie airport: Located 89 km from Rouen.

Bus Stations Near Rouen

  • Rouen bus station: located in the city center, has several services to various destinations across France.
  • Saint-Pierre-des-Corps: Is the main hub for rail services to Paris and other parts of France.

Other Options

Train or car are the recommended modes of transportation when traveling to Rouen.

How to Get Around in Rouen

The best way to get around Rouen is on foot, by bike or using public transportation bus or metro.

Where to Go Next from Rouen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rouen is a great hub for exploring Normandy region. Here are some nearby destinations to visit:

  • Giverny: Known for Claude Monet’s house and gardens.
  • Mont Saint-Michell: a rocky tidal island and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Deauville: a seaside town with beautiful beaches and casinos.

Conclusion

Rouen is a beautiful city with a rich history, an impressive food scene, and natural beauty. A trip to Rouen offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, making it a must-visit destination when traveling to Normandy.
