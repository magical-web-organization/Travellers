Introduction to Caen

Situated in the Lower Normandy region of France, Caen is a city steeped in history with a rich heritage. It is known for its beautiful architecture, museums, culinary delights, and thrilling outdoor adventures. If you’re planning a visit to northern France, make sure to include Caen on your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Caen

Caen boasts a variety of attractions that cover cultural significance, natural beauty, and exciting activities. Below is a list of must-visit places in Caen for anyone traveling to the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Caen is a city of significant historical importance. Many museums and historical sites pay tribute to the events that took place here. A few of these places include:

Caen Memorial Museum

The Caen Memorial Museum is one of the best-known World War II museums in France. It focuses on the history of World War II but also highlights other conflicts and the nature of peace. It is recommended to spend at least three hours exploring the museum.

Chateau de Caen

The Chateau de Caen is a fortress that was built in the time of William the Conqueror, serving as a pivotal defensive structure in the area. Today, it features a museum dedicated to the history of Normandy.

Abbaye-aux-Hommes and Abbaye-aux-Dames

Built in the 11th century by William the Conqueror, the Abbaye-aux-Hommes and Abbaye-aux-Dames hold a significant place in Caen’s history. The Abbaye-aux-Hommes now serves as a city hall and museum while the Abbaye-aux-Dames has a museum dedicated to the women of the past.

Natural Attractions of Caen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to spend some time in Caen’s lush forests and rolling hills, get your hiking boots ready. Here are some of the natural attractions you can visit:

Pegasus Bridge

The Pegasus Bridge is the famous spot where, during World War II, British soldiers crash-landed gliders, seized the bridge, and ultimately secured it for the Allies. Walking the trail gives hikers scenic views of the Ouistreham canal and beyond.

Bois de l’Eveque

Bois de l’Eveque is a natural park in the heart of the city, providing a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching, hiking, and biking or play a game of tennis on one of the many courts available.

Beaches and Sand Dunes

Caen also houses many sandy beaches and dunes, providing an exciting outdoor activity. You can explore the sand dunes and coastline by foot or horseback, or try your hand at a range of watersports including windsurfing, sailing, and kite surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caen

If you’re looking to explore the beauty of Caen, take the time to explore some of the scenic trails and routes for hikers and bikers.

The Greenway

The Greenway is a 10km trail that connects Caen and Ouistreham. It is a route of great interest for nature lovers, offering a pleasant and easy cycling or hiking experience.

Chemin du Halage

Chemin du Halage is a paved bike and walking path that runs along the Caen Canal. It’s a great way to enjoy your surroundings while biking or walking without feeling disturbed by traffic.

Best Things to Do in Caen: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for some entertaining outdoor activities and tours, Caen does not disappoint.

Segway Tour of Caen

Discover the highlights of Caen in an entirely new way—by a Segway tour. The tour operator provides a thorough briefing and orientation before heading off on the tour. It is one of the most fun ways to see the city.

Horse Riding at Les Ecuriets de La Baronnie

Les Ecuriets de La Baronnie puts emphasis on quality time spent with the horses, allowing you to experience nature in a unique way. The tour leads you through the lush forests of the city and then to the beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caen

A week in Caen can give you enough time to explore the city and its surrounding areas. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Caen Memorial Museum and the Chateau de Caen.

Day 2: Explore the Abbaye-aux-Hommes and Abbaye-aux-Dames and wander over to the nearby church, Eglise Saint-Pierre.

Day 3: Drive or bike down to the Pegasus Bridge, followed by a picnic on the Ouistreham Canal.

Day 4: Stroll down to Bois de l’Eveque and then walk to the beaches of Hermanville-sur-Mer and Lion-sur-Mer.

Day 5: Take a leisurely bike ride down the Greenway towards Ouistreham.

Day 6: Enjoy a Segway tour of the city, ending the day with dinner in Place Saint-Sauveur.

Day 7: Take a horseback ride through Les Ecuriets de La Baronnie, completing your tours of Caen in style.

Food and Drink Scene of Caen

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Caen is ever complete without indulging in its famous dishes.

Teurgoule

Teurgoule is a traditional rice pudding from Normandy, made with milk, rice, sugar, and cinnamon. It is best enjoyed when warm, with a sprinkle of cinnamon on top.

Tripes A La Mode

Tripes a la Mode is a Normandy delicacy consisting of calf stomach, which is slow-cooked in a mixture of red wine, onions, and spices. It is usually served with potatoes or boiled new peas.

Local Beverages

  • Calvados: A distilled apple brandy unique to Normandy.
  • Pommeau: A sweet alcoholic drink made from apple juice and Calvados.

Where to Stay in Caen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget accommodations or luxury hotels, Caen has plenty of options to suit your needs.

Hotel Mercure Caen Centre Port de Plaisance

Centrally located in the heart of Caen, this hotel provides a comfortable stay with well-equipped rooms and excellent hospitality.

Hotel Novotel Caen Cote De Nacre

Located in the heart of Caen, this hotel is an excellent choice for travelers on a budget. It offers comfortable rooms, an onsite restaurant, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Caen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cider and Calvados

Cider and Calvados are two of the best local products of Caen. Purchase them from a local farmer or grocery store.

Lace

Calvados Lace is a popular handmade product in Caen and can be found in various shops and bazaars. The intricate lacework is a treasure to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Caen

In Caen, it’s always polite to greet someone with “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir” depending on the time of day. It is also customary to dress conservatively when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Caen

The best time to visit Caen is from July to September when the weather is warm and inviting. However, the city is beautiful in all seasons and has numerous indoor activities to keep you entertained in case of inclement weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caen

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks. They are unique and delicious.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic French and try to converse with locals.
  • Use public transport to navigate the city.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be late; the French value punctuality.
  • Don’t speak too loud in public places like restaurants.
  • Don’t litter or smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caen

Caen is considered a relatively safe city. However, it’s always recommended to keep your valuables safe and secure. Make sure to lock your doors and windows when leaving your hotel room.

Health in Caen

Caen has a reliable healthcare system with several hospitals and healthcare clinics. In case of an emergency, dial 112.

Getting to Caen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re coming from Paris, London or any city in Europe, there are several transportation options available.

Airports Near Caen

Caen Carpiquet Airport is a small, local airport located just 10 km west of the city center. It provides daily flights to Paris and other French cities.

Bus Stations Near Caen

Caen has a central bus station located near the train station. The station provides frequent connections to other French cities, including Paris, Rouen, and Bayeux.

Other Options

Several trains run between Caen and other French cities like Paris, Cherbourg, and Le Mans.

How to Get Around in Caen

Getting around Caen is convenient and easy. Caen has an extensive public transport network with buses and trains running at regular intervals. Bikes are also available for rent in the city.

Where to Go Next from Caen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that you can visit from Caen. These include:

  • Bayeux: home of the Bayeux Tapestry and many other historical sites
  • Honfleur: beautiful port town with plenty of galleries and museums
  • Mont-Saint-Michel: a small, rocky island off the coast of Normandy, featuring a magnificent abbey

Conclusion

Caen is a city boasting a rich history, vibrant local culture, and incredible natural beauty. We hope this guide has provided you with the information you need to make your trip to Lower Normandy memorable. From the numerous historical sites and outdoor adventures to the delicious local cuisine and stunning natural parks, a visit to Caen will surely be unforgettable.
