Introduction to Granville

Granville is a small medieval town located in Lower Normandy. This charming coastal city is divided into two parts; the upper town and the lower town, separated by a steep cliff on which the old fortress stands. It is known for its rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and cultural significance. The town is surrounded by the sea, making it a perfect spot for outdoor lovers. Granville is also famous for its delicious cuisine, world-class museums and galleries, and warm hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Granville

There are several exciting and culturally significant tourist attractions in Granville, which one should not miss. Here is a list of must-visit places you can explore during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée d’Art Moderne Richard Anacréon - This museum houses contemporary art and is named after its founder, Richard Anacréon. It features works of famous artists like Picasso, Braque, Matisse, and many more. The museum is open every day except Tuesday from 10:00 am to 12:00 pm and from 2:00 pm to 6:00 pm.
  • Musée Christian Dior - The museum is dedicated to the famous French fashion designer Christian Dior. It displays Dior’s haute couture designs and other art collections related to fashion. The museum is open every day from 10:00 am to 6:00 pm.
  • Musée du Vieux Granville - It exhibits Granville’s history, from prehistoric times to the present. It also displays a collection of Norman furniture, costumes, and objects related to Norman life. The museum is open every day except Monday from 10:00 am to 12:30 pm and from 1:30 pm to 6:00 pm.
  • Château de Granville - It is a magnificent castle located on the summit of the hill in Granville. The castle has a long history dating back to the 12th century. It now houses the town hall offices and is opened to visitors on certain days.
  • Jardin Christian Dior - It is a beautiful garden dedicated to the famous French fashion designer Christian Dior. The garden has an exceptional view of the sea and hosts some of Dior’s fashion shows. The garden is open every day from 10:00 am to 6:00 pm.

Natural Attractions of Granville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Le Roc des Harmonies - It is a natural cliff that divides the upper town from the lower town. It offers a breathtaking view of the sea and is the perfect spot for taking pictures.
  • Pointe du Roc - The Pointe du Roc is a promontory that juts out into the sea, offering stunning views of the coastline and the sea. With walking trails, picnic areas, and natural beauty, the Pointe du Roc is a must-visit spot for nature lovers.
  • Plage de Donville-les-Bains - It is located about two kilometers from Granville and offers sandy beaches where you can relax and enjoy different water sports.
  • Les Falaises de Champeaux - Champeaux Cliffs is a stunningly beautiful natural landscape located on the east of Granville and offering serene views of the sea.
  • Ilot du Rocher du Talus - It is a small island in the middle of the sea surrounded by rocks. You can visit it by boat and experience the island’s natural beauty and the sea’s tranquillity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Granville

Granville has several hiking and biking routes for visitors that let them enjoy the town’s natural beauty. These routes lead you through several secret beaches, rocky cliffs, forests, and countryside and offer breathtaking scenic views.

  • **Costal Path ** - It is a 15-kilometer long path from the Pointe du Roc to the Donville-les-Bains Plage. It offers stunning views of the sea and is one of the most popular hiking routes among visitors.
  • **La Vallée du Lude ** - It is a 6.5-kilometer route that passes through the Lude Valley and offers easy accessibility for a family walk.
  • **La Vallée de la Mue ** - It is a 9.5-kilometer route that starts from the St Nicholas church and passes through several small valleys until it reaches the Mue river near the Donville-les-Bains Plage.

Best Things to Do in Granville: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours that you can explore during your trip to Granville.

  • Granville Food Tour - Take a food tour and discover the local delicacies of Granville such as oysters, seafood platters, apple cider, and much more.
  • Entertainment Shows - Granville offers several entertainment shows like street performers and music performances during the summer season.
  • Sailing and Boat Tours - Experience the sea’s tranquility and watch dolphins, seals, and other sea animals passing by during your boat tour.
  • Fishing Tours - Granville is a perfect place for fishing enthusiasts wanting to explore the nearby sea. You can book a tour and wait for the fish to bite freely.
  • Horse Riding Tours - If you want to experience the countryside of Granville, take a horse riding tour and explore the region’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Granville

Granville features a unique culinary art that blends local seafood with Normandy’s specialties, giving it a unique gastronomic identity that will leave you wanting more.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coquilles Saint-Jacques - It is a dish made of scallops served in a shell with a layer of garlic butter and breadcrumbs.
  • Moules Marinières - It is a dish made of mussels cooked in white wine and garlic and served with French fries.
  • Tarte aux Pommes - It is a delicious apple tart made with local cider, sugar, and shortcrust pastry.
  • Camembert and Livarot Cheese - Granville is renowned for its soft cheese, and Camembert and Livarot are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Cider - Normandy cider is a must-drink in Granville.
  • Calvados - Normandy’s famous apple brandy is a specialty of Granville.

Where to Stay in Granville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Granville has a wide range of accommodations to choose from. There are several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that offer affordable and luxurious accommodations.

  • Hotel des Bains - Located on the seafront, the Hotel des Bains is a luxurious hotel with elegant decor offering amenities like a heated pool, spa, and tennis court.
  • Hotel Restaurant Mercure Granville Le Grand Large - Located near the beach, this hotel has a stunning view of the sea and offers easy access to the town center.
  • Hostellerie de la Baie - This hotel offers affordable accommodation with a cozy and comfortable setting perfect for a short stay.
  • La Haute Lande - A typical Norman-style mansion characterized by a serene garden and a cozy atmosphere, offering a charming and comfortable stay for the guests.

What to Buy in Granville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery products - Granville has several pottery manufacturers producing high-quality pots and ceramic items.
  • Cider and Calvados - Normandy’s famous apple cider and Calvados are perfect gifts to bring back home.
  • Camembert and Livarot Cheese - Granville’s famous soft cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Granville

Granville has a unique culture that is an assimilation of traditions, customs, and habits of the Norman and Breton regions. Elegance, simplicity, and authenticity some of the key features of Granville’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Granville

The best time to visit Granville is between May and September during the summer season. During this season, the town experiences a moderate climate and offers several outdoor activities and entertainment shows.

Do’s and Don’ts of Granville

Do:

  • Respect the local customs, traditions, and culture.
  • Try new cuisine and local drinks.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Granville by taking part in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter on the streets and beaches.
  • Do not swim in unguarded areas.
  • Do not touch or harm the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Granville

Granville is a safe place to travel but ensure to take standard safety precautions. Keep your valuables near you, avoid walking alone at night, and pay attention to your surroundings.

Health in Granville

The town has several medical facilities and hospitals and provides health care services of high quality.

Getting to Granville: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to reach Granville.

Airports Near Granville

The nearest airport to Granville is Rennes Saint-Jacques Airport.

Bus Stations Near Granville

The town has a bus station, from where you can take local buses to access nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

You can take the train from Paris to Granville. The direct express train runs twice a day, and the standard train runs several times a day.

How to Get Around in Granville

The best way to explore Granville’s beauty is by walking. You can also hire taxis, bikes, or cars to travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from Granville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Granville is surrounded by several nearby towns and cities that are worth visiting. Here are a few popular options:

  • Avranches- A town located south-west of Granville, famous for its beautiful botanical gardens.
  • Mont Saint Michel- A small island famous for having an abbey that dates back to the 8th century.
  • Bayeux - A town known for Bayeux Tapestry, which dates back to the 11th century.

Conclusion

Granville is a beautiful town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and culture. The above guide will help you explore the town in-depth, giving you the best experience and lasting memorable memories. Have an enjoyable and safe travel!
