Your Guide to Avranches, France

Avranches is a charming town located in Lower Normandy, in the Normandy region of France. Steeped in history with stunning natural scenery, Avranches is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic experience in France. In this comprehensive guide, we have compiled all the information you need to plan your trip to Avranches, including the best things to see, do, and eat, as well as tips on where to stay and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Avranches

Located in the heart of Normandy, Avranches is home to several cultural and natural attractions that are worth a visit. Here are our top must-visit places in Avranches:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée Municipal: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the rich history of Avranches and its surrounding areas. The museum has a large collection of artifacts, including documents, paintings, and sculptures, dating back to the Gallo-Roman period.

  • Scriptorial d’Avranches: This museum is dedicated to the famous 13th-century manuscript, Mont-Saint-Michel, and houses a permanent exhibition showcasing the history and importance of this iconic manuscript.

  • Jardin des Plantes d’Avranches: This botanical garden is located on the outskirts of the town and is a perfect spot for a relaxing stroll. The garden features a variety of plant species, including rare and exotic plants from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Avranches: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mont-Saint-Michel: Mont-Saint-Michel is an iconic abbey and one of the most famous landmarks of France. Located just 40 minutes from Avranches, this UNESCO World Heritage site is a must-visit.

  • Chausey Islands: The Chausey Islands, located just off the coast of Avranches, are a beautiful group of islands that offer visitors a chance to escape from the hustle and bustle of the town and soak in nature’s beauty.

  • Sélune River: The Sélune River is a beautiful river that runs through Avranches, offering visitors a chance to enjoy outdoor activities like fishing, kayaking, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avranches

Avranches is home to several stunning hiking and biking routes that offer visitors a chance to see the town’s pristine natural beauty up close. Some of the best routes include:

  • The GR 223: This is a challenging hiking trail that runs along the coast, offering stunning views of the English Channel.

  • The Vire Valley: This biking route is a popular choice for visitors looking to explore the stunning Vire Valley and its surrounding areas.

  • Le Mont-Saint-Michel Trail: This trail takes you right up to the majestic Mont-Saint-Michel, offering stunning views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Avranches: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Tour of Mont-Saint-Michel: A tour of Mont-Saint-Michel is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that any traveler to Avranches should not miss. We recommend taking a guided tour to fully appreciate the history and significance of this iconic landmark.

  • Visit the Avranches Botanical Garden: Take a stroll through the peaceful Avranches Botanical Garden and enjoy the beautiful plant species that the garden has to offer.

  • Sample the Local Cider: The cider produced in Normandy is world-renowned for its unique flavor. Take a tour of the local cider farms and sample some of the best cider that the region has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avranches

Day 1: Arrive in Avranches and take a walking tour of the town.

Day 2: Explore the stunning Mont-Saint-Michel and take a guided tour of the abbey.

Day 3: Take a tour of the local cider farms and sample some of the best cider that the region has to offer.

Day 4: Hike along the GR 223; take in the stunning views of the English Channel.

Day 5: Visit the Scriptorial d’Avranches and learn more about the significance of the Mont-Saint-Michel manuscript.

Day 6: Take a relaxing stroll through the peaceful Avranches Botanical Garden.

Day 7: Depart Avranches.

Food and Drink Scene of Avranches

As a traditional French town, Avranches is home to a variety of delicious food and drink options that visitors should not miss. Here are our top recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galette-saucisse: A popular snack in Normandy, this dish consists of a sausage wrapped in a savory buckwheat crepe.

  • Moules-frites: This dish of mussels and chips is another local favorite and is usually served with a side of homemade mayonnaise.

  • Crêpes: Sweet or savory, crepes are a must-try when visiting France. Try the classic crepe with Nutella or cheese and ham.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Produced in Normandy, the cider in Avranches is world-renowned for its unique and delicious flavor.

  • Calvados: A type of apple brandy that is made in Normandy and is a popular after-dinner drink in Avranches.

Where to Stay in Avranches: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avranches has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Le Moulin de Jean: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Avranches and offers guests spacious and comfortable rooms.

  • Hotel Patton: Located close to the town center, Hotel Patton offers guests a comfortable and convenient stay in Avranches.

  • Le Relais Saint Michel: A 5-star hotel located just a short drive from Mont-Saint-Michel, Le Relais Saint Michel offers guests luxury accommodation in a stunning location.

What to Buy in Avranches: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cider: Bring home a bottle of the world-renowned cider from Normandy.

  • Calvados: This traditional apple brandy makes for the perfect souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Avranches

As a traditional French town, Avranches has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are a few tips to help visitors blend in:

  • Greetings: When meeting someone for the first time, it is customary to shake hands.

  • Dress Code: French people tend to dress smartly, so dress appropriately when visiting public places.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in France, as it is included in the bill.

Best Time to Visit Avranches

The best time to visit Avranches is during the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) months, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are less intense.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avranches

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the cider and calvados.

  • Take a guided tour of Mont-Saint-Michel to fully appreciate the history and significance of this iconic landmark.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes. There will be a lot of walking involved when exploring Avranches.

  • Don’t forget to bring a camera to capture the stunning natural beauty of Avranches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avranches

Avranches is a safe town to visit. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, like being aware of their surroundings and not leaving their belongings unattended in public places.

Health in Avranches

France has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can easily access medical care if needed. It is always recommended to purchase travel insurance before embarking on any trip.

Getting to Avranches: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Avranches

  • Rennes Airport: Located 66 miles from Avranches, Rennes Airport is the closest international airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Avranches

  • Avranches Bus Station: Located in the town center, Avranches Bus Station is easily accessible and offers a variety of routes to surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a popular and convenient way to explore Avranches and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Avranches

Avranches is a small town that is easily navigable on foot. However, visitors can also use public transportation, rent a bike, or drive a car to explore the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Avranches: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bayeux: Located 35 miles from Avranches, Bayeux is a charming town that is known for its historic tapestry.

  • Caen: Located 54 miles from Avranches, Caen is a beautiful town with a rich history and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Avranches is a hidden gem in Normandy that offers visitors a glimpse into French culture and history, all while surrounded by stunning natural scenery. From Mont-Saint-Michel to local cider farms, Avranches has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Avranches and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
