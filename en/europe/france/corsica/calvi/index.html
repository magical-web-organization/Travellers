Introduction to Calvi

Calvi is a picturesque coastal town located in the northwest part of Corsica, France. It is known for its citadel, beaches, and natural beauty. Calvi is a popular tourist destination for those who seek adventure, relaxation, or a combination of both.

List of Places to Visit in Calvi

There are plenty of places to explore in Calvi, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Citadel of Calvi: Built in the 13th century, this fortress offers stunning views of the sea and the mountains. History buffs will appreciate the numerous exhibits that chronicle Corsica’s turbulent past.
  • Chapelle de Notre-Dame de la Serra: Perched on a hilltop overlooking the town, this 19th-century chapel is a popular spot for tourists to take in panoramic views of Calvi.
  • Maison Bonaparte: This museum, located in Ajaccio (about 81 km from Calvi), is the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte. It showcases memorabilia related to his life and reign as emperor.

Natural Attractions of Calvi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage de Calvi: This white sandy beach stretches for four kilometers and boasts crystal clear waters. It is a great spot to relax and soak up the sun.
  • Grotte des Veaux Marins: This sea cave is located near Calvi and can only be reached by boat. Visitors can marvel at the rock formations, stalactites, and play of light.
  • Monte Cinto: This is the highest peak in Corsica, and it is a popular destination for hikers and mountaineers. It offers breathtaking views of Calvi and the surrounding region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calvi

Calvi is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of trails that offer stunning views of the region’s beauty. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  • GR 20: This path is considered one of the toughest long-distance hikes in Europe. It covers 168 km and takes about two weeks to complete. It passes through some of the most beautiful spots on the island.
  • Sentier des Douaniers: This coastal trail stretches for over 300 km and affords hikers stunning views of the Mediterranean.
  • Boucle de Bonifatu: This 5.5 km round-trip trail offers hikers a chance to see the Bonifatu forest and its famous “piscines naturelles” (natural pools).

Best Things to Do in Calvi: Activities and Tours

Calvi offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Calvi:

  • Water sports: Visitors can enjoy a variety of water sports such as kayaking, paddleboarding, and windsurfing.
  • Boat tours: Boat tours are a great way to explore the coastline and caves near Calvi.
  • Wine tasting: Corsican wines are famous for their quality and unique taste. Visitors can take a wine-tasting tour and sample some of the best wines in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calvi

For those who want to experience everything that Calvi has to offer, here is a sample week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Calvi

Corsican cuisine is rich and diverse, with influences from French and Italian cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brocciu: This is a type of cheese made from sheep or goat milk. It is used in many Corsican dishes such as omelets and ravioli.
  • Fiadone: This is a type of cheesecake made with brocciu, lemon zest, and sugar.
  • Civet de sanglier: This is a stew made with wild boar meat and red wine.

Local Beverages

  • Pietra: This is a beer made with chestnut flour. It has a unique taste and is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Myrtle: This is a liqueur made from myrtle berries. It is used as a digestive after meals.
  • Vermentino: This is a white wine made from Vermentino grapes. It is known for its floral aroma and fruity taste.

Where to Stay in Calvi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calvi offers a variety of accommodation options to fit different budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best options:

  • La Signoria: This 5-star hotel is located in a restored 18th-century mansion. It boasts a spa, a swimming pool, and a Michelin-starred restaurant.
  • Camping La Pinède: This campground is located near the beach and offers cabins and tents for rent.
  • Villa Calvi: This guesthouse is located in the citadel and offers stunning views of the sea. It has a swimming pool and a terrace.

What to Buy in Calvi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Corsica is famous for its local products such as honey, olives, and chestnuts. Here are some souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home:

  • Corsican knives: These knives are made by local craftsmen using traditional techniques. They are known for their unique design and quality.
  • Corsican wine: Visitors can buy local wines such as Ajaccio, Patrimonio, and Figari.
  • Corsican honey: This honey is known for its unique flavor and aroma. It is made by bees that collect nectar from the local flora.

Local Culture and Customs of Calvi

Calvi has a rich and distinct culture that is influenced by its history and geography. Here are some things to know:

  • Corsican language: Corsican is a Romance language that is spoken by the locals. It has Italian influences and is still taught in schools.
  • Music and dance: Corsicans have a rich musical tradition, with polyphonic singing being a standout feature. Visitors can enjoy concerts and festivals that showcase the local music and dance.
  • Fiesta di l’Assunta: This festival is held on August 15th and is a celebration of the Assumption of Mary. It is an important event in Calvi’s cultural calendar.

Best Time to Visit Calvi

The best time to visit Calvi is from May to October. The weather is pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. July and August are the busiest months, and visitors should expect higher prices and crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calvi

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Calvi:

Do:

  • Do try the local food and wine.
  • Do explore the citadel and nearby beaches.
  • Do try some outdoor activities such as hiking and water sports.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and a hat.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t expect everyone to speak English, try to learn some basic Corsican phrases.

Safety and Health

Calvi is a safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution and common sense. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Calvi

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dark and isolated areas at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure.
  • Follow beach safety rules and be aware of currents and tides.

Health in Calvi

  • Corsica is a tick-infested area, so visitors should take precautions such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.
  • Visitors should also be aware of the risk of dehydration and heatstroke during summer months.

Getting to Calvi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calvi is well-connected to the rest of Europe and France. Here are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Calvi

  • Calvi-Sainte-Catherine Airport: This airport is located 7 km from Calvi and offers direct flights to Paris and Nice.
  • Bastia-Poretta Airport: This airport is located about 1 hour and 40 minutes from Calvi and offers flights to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Calvi

  • Calvi Bus Station: This station is located in the town center and offers connections to nearby towns and cities such as Ajaccio and Bastia.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Visitors can take a ferry from mainland France or Italy to Calvi’s port.

How to Get Around in Calvi

The best way to explore Calvi is on foot. However, visitors may want to rent a car to explore the nearby towns and attractions. Public transportation is also available, with buses and trains connecting Calvi to other parts of Corsica.

Where to Go Next from Calvi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Corsica is a beautiful island with plenty of destinations to explore. Here are some nearby towns and attractions that visitors can visit:

  • Bonifacio: This town is located in the southern part of Corsica and is known for its stunning cliffs, beaches, and medieval architecture.
  • Corte: This town is located in the center of Corsica and is known for its citadel and museums.
  • Scandola Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is located near Calvi and offers stunning landscapes, caves, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Calvi is a beautiful town with a rich history and culture. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, explore cultural attractions, and indulge in the local food and wine. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Calvi with ease and confidence.
