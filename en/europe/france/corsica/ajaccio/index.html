Ajaccio Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Ajaccio is a stunning city located in the southwestern part of the French island of Corsica, which is part of France. It is known for its beautiful beaches, historical landmarks, and picturesque landscapes. In this comprehensive travel guide to Ajaccio, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your next vacation to this beautiful city.

Introduction to Ajaccio

Ajaccio is a vibrant city best known as the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte. It is the prefecture of the Corse-du-Sud department of France and is located 210 nautical miles southeast of Marseille. The city is situated on the west coast of Corsica and has a population of about 75,000 people.

Ajaccio is a popular tourist destination due to its mild climate, beautiful beaches, and rich history. The city has a rich cultural heritage and beautiful architecture that makes it a must-visit destination for travelers looking to immerse themselves in the beauty of Corsica.

List of Places to Visit in Ajaccio

Ajaccio has so many beautiful sights to see, and here are some of the top places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte: This is the house where the Emperor of France, Napoleon Bonaparte, was born on August 15, 1769. It is now a museum which showcases the life of the famous emperor.
  2. Musée Fesch: This art museum has a collection of over 16,000 works of art, including the largest collection of Italian Renaissance paintings outside of Italy.
  3. Maison Bonaparte: The ancestral home of the Bonaparte family houses a museum that displays the golden age of Corsica and the life of the Bonaparte family.
  4. Citadelle d’Ajaccio: This citadel was built in the 15th century and has a rich history. It offers a magnificent view of the city and the Gulf of Ajaccio.

Natural Attractions of Ajaccio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Les Calanques de Piana: These stunning calanques are some of the most beautiful natural wonders in Corsica. They are located in the Gulf of Porto and have been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  2. Capo di Feno Beach: This is one of the most beautiful beaches in Corsica, and it is known for its crystal clear waters, white sand, and breathtaking sunsets.
  3. Îles Sanguinaires: A group of small islands located on the west coast of Ajaccio. These islands are famous for their red rocks and beautiful natural landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ajaccio

  1. Monte Gozzi: This is a mountain that stands at 705 meters and has a hiking trail that offers an excellent view of Ajaccio and the surrounding areas.
  2. L’Archipel des Sanguinaires: This coastal trail leads to the beautiful islands of Îles Sanguinaires.
  3. Le Sentier des Crêtes: This hiking trail is one of the most scenic and challenging trails in Ajaccio. It offers panoramic views of the sea and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ajaccio: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat Tour: Take a boat tour around Ajaccio’s coastline and visit some of the hidden beaches and coves.
  2. Scuba Diving: Ajaccio has some of the best diving spots in Corsica, and visitors can experience the beauty of the Mediterranean sea.
  3. Food Tours: Go on a food tour and sample some of Corsica’s traditional cuisine and local delicacies.
  4. Water Sports: From kayaking to jetskiing, Ajaccio offers a wide range of water sports activities that will keep you entertained.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ajaccio

Day 1: Visit the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte and Maison Bonaparte, followed by a stroll down Cours Napoléon.

Day 2: Take a boat tour around the coastline and visit some of the hidden beaches and coves.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Monte Gozzi and take in the breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding areas.

Day 4: Visit the Musée Fesch and have lunch at one of the traditional Corsican restaurants in the city.

Day 5: Visit the Citadelle d’Ajaccio and take a walk down the history lane of Ajaccio.

Day 6: Go scuba diving or snorkeling, explore the underwater beauty of the Mediterranean.

Day 7: Relax at Capo di Feno Beach and watch the beautiful sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Ajaccio

The food scene in Ajaccio is fantastic, and visitors can savor some of the local delicacies, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fiadone - A traditional Corsican cheesecake made with local fresh cheese and flavored with lemon or orange.
  2. Brocciu - A cheese that is typical of Corsica used in many traditional dishes, including pizza, omelets, and pastries.
  3. Cacciucciu - This is a mouth-watering seafood stew that contains a variety of fish, shellfish, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Pietra - Corsica’s famous beer is brewed using chestnut flour, giving it a unique taste.
  2. Muscat - Ajaccio is well known for its sweet and fruity Muscat wine.
  3. Eau de vie - A strong alcoholic drink made with local herbs and fruits.

Where to Stay in Ajaccio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodations in Ajaccio that cater to different budgets, including:

  1. Hôtel Napoléon Bonaparte - For those looking for luxury, this hotel provides a spectacular view of the city and the Gulf of Ajaccio.
  2. Hôtel Fesch - A charming and comfortable hotel located in the heart of Ajaccio.
  3. Auberge de Jeunesse - A cozy and affordable hostel located near the city center.

What to Buy in Ajaccio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a memory of their trip to Ajaccio include:

  1. Corsican honey - This honey is flavorful, organic, and honey lovers take it as a delight.
  2. Corsican Charcuterie - A variety of cured meats including sausages, ham, and salami can be purchased in Ajaccio.
  3. Corsican cheese - Brocciu, and Tomme de Brebis, both unique cheeses of Corsica.

Local Culture and Customs of Ajaccio

The locals of Ajaccio value their traditions and history, and visitors are advised to respect their customs and beliefs. The Corsican language is prevalent in Ajaccio since the city has a unique dialect of French. The city’s culture is also famous for its music, folkloric traditions, and gastronomy.

Best Time to Visit Ajaccio

Ajaccio enjoys a mild Mediterranean climate throughout the year, making it a great place to visit at any time. However, the best time to visit the city is between May and October, as this is when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of festivities and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ajaccio

Do:

  1. Respect the locals and their customs.
  2. Take time to enjoy the traditional Corsican cuisine.
  3. Visit Ajaccio’s historical landmarks and museums.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the custom and tradition of local people.
  2. Leave without buying some of the local products as souvenirs.
  3. Litter around the beaches and coastal areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ajaccio

Ajaccio is a relatively safe city, and travelers can move around the city without any fear. However, it is always advisable to be cautious and take the necessary precautions.

Health in Ajaccio

There are no significant health risks when traveling to Ajaccio. Still, travelers are advised to use sunscreen to avoid sunburn and dehydration, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Ajaccio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ajaccio is well connected to the mainland and other European cities by air, sea, and land.

Airports Near Ajaccio

Ajaccio has its airport, Ajaccio - Napoléon Bonaparte Airport, which has both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Ajaccio

There are several bus stations in Ajaccio, making it easy to access nearby areas of interest.

Other Options

Ferry services are available from nearby ports in Marseille, Nice, and Toulon, making it an easy option for travelers who want to bring their car or travel to nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Ajaccio

Ajaccio is a relatively small city, so travelers can explore most of it by foot. However, for those who want to cover more ground, there are also other options such as bicycle rentals, taxis, or buses.

Where to Go Next from Ajaccio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations worth visiting from Ajaccio include:

  1. Bonifacio - A coastal city located on the southern part of Corsica with rich cultural heritage and historical landmarks.
  2. Corte - A beautiful mountain town known for its hiking trails and stunning scenery.
  3. Calvi - A beautiful coastal town located on the northern part of Corsica with a wide range of water sports activities.

Conclusion

Ajaccio is a city that offers something for everyone, from history enthusiasts to beach lovers and nature seekers. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and hospitable people, Ajaccio is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty of Corsica. We hope that this comprehensive guide has been helpful, and we wish you a happy and safe journey to Ajaccio!
