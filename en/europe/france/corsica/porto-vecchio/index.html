Introduction to Porto-Vecchio

Porto-Vecchio is a stunning town located in the southern part of Corsica, France. The town is situated on a hilltop and offers beautiful views of the Mediterranean Sea. Porto-Vecchio is known for its stunning beaches, historic landmarks, and charming old town. It is a popular destination for tourists who want to experience a mix of culture, history, and natural beauty.

## List of Places to Visit in Porto-Vecchio

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s history or experiencing its natural beauty, Porto-Vecchio has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of top places to visit in Porto-Vecchio:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Citadelle de Porto-Vecchio: A 16th-century Genoese fortress that is now a museum. It houses a collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from the town’s history.
  • Eglise Saint-Jean Baptiste: A beautiful church located in the heart of the old town. It was built in the 19th century and features elaborate frescoes and stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Porto-Vecchio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palombaggia Beach: A beautiful white sandy beach overlooking the clear turquoise water of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Bavella Mountains: A range of mountains famous for their stunning peaks, gorges, and natural pools. The Bavella Mountains are a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Porto-Vecchio

Porto-Vecchio is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are several hiking and biking routes in and around the town. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The L’Ospedale Forest Trail: A beautiful trail that takes you through a dense forest of pine trees, oak trees, and chestnut trees. The trail offers stunning views of the Gulf of Porto-Vecchio.
  • The Conca-Cavu Trail: A challenging trail that takes you through the stunning Bavella Mountains. The trail has several parts with steep inclines and descents, and is perfect for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Porto-Vecchio: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Porto-Vecchio, whether you’re interested in outdoor activities or cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to explore the stunning coastline and nearby islands.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit a local winery to taste Corsica’s famous wines.
  • Water Sports: Go kayaking, scuba diving, snorkeling, or stand-up paddleboarding along the coast.

Week-Long Itinerary for Porto-Vecchio

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Porto-Vecchio, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the old town, visit the Citadelle de Porto-Vecchio and the Eglise Saint-Jean Baptiste
Day 2 Visit the Palombaggia Beach and take a boat tour
Day 3 Hike the L’Ospedale Forest Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch
Day 4 Take a day trip to the Bavella Mountains and go rock climbing
Day 5 Go wine tasting and learn about Corsican wines
Day 6 Spend the day kayaking, scuba diving, or snorkeling
Day 7 Relax on the beach and enjoy the stunning view of the Mediterranean Sea

Food and Drink Scene of Porto-Vecchio

Porto-Vecchio is known for its delicious food and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Veal dish: This is a traditional Corsican dish made with veal, onions, and tomatoes, and served with polenta.
  • Fiadone: A sweet cheese cake made with brocciu cheese and topped with lemon zest.

Local Beverages

  • Corsican wine: Corsica is known for its delicious wine, particularly the red wine made from the Niellucciu grape.
  • Chestnut beer: This beer is made using chestnut flour and has a unique flavour.

Where to Stay in Porto-Vecchio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Porto-Vecchio has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Porto-Vecchio:

  • Hotel Casa del Mar: A luxurious hotel located on the beachfront offering stunning views and comfortable accommodation.
  • Auberge U Castellu: A charming guesthouse located in the old town, offering traditional Corsican hospitality and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Porto-Vecchio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Porto-Vecchio is known for its locally made products, including wine, cheese, and olive oil. Here are some other souvenirs to bring home:

  • Corsican knives: These are traditional knives made with local materials, including wood and bone.
  • Pottery: Porto-Vecchio has a thriving pottery industry, so you can find beautiful handmade ceramics to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Porto-Vecchio

Corsica has a unique culture that is influenced by its history and geography. Here are some local customs and etiquette to keep in mind:

  • Corsicans are proud of their culture and history, so respect their traditions and beliefs.
  • Corsicans are generally friendly and welcoming, but can be reserved with strangers.
  • It is customary to greet people with a handshake or a kiss on both cheeks.

Best Time to Visit Porto-Vecchio

Porto-Vecchio is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June to September) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, be aware that this is also the busiest time of the year, so expect crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Porto-Vecchio

Here are some things to do and avoid during your visit to Porto-Vecchio:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the surrounding area.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places, as it is considered disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Porto-Vecchio is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Porto-Vecchio

  • Keep your valuables close to you and avoid carrying large sums of money in public places.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Take precautions when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or water sports.

Health in Porto-Vecchio

  • Make sure you have adequate travel insurance before your trip.
  • Drink bottled water or bring a reusable water bottle with a filter.
  • Apply sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from sunburn and mosquito bites.

Getting to Porto-Vecchio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Porto-Vecchio is easily accessible from other major cities in France. Here are some transportation options to get to Porto-Vecchio:

Airports Near Porto-Vecchio

  • Figari-South Corsica Airport (FSC): Located 25 km from Porto-Vecchio, it is the closest airport.
  • Ajaccio-Napoléon Bonaparte Airport (AJA): Located 138 km from Porto-Vecchio, it is a larger airport with more international flights.

Bus Stations Near Porto-Vecchio

  • The main bus station in Porto-Vecchio is located in the town center and offers connections to other cities in Corsica.

Other Options

  • You can also rent a car or take a taxi from Figari-South Corsica Airport.

How to Get Around in Porto-Vecchio

Porto-Vecchio is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. You can also rent a car or a bike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Porto-Vecchio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Porto-Vecchio is a great base for exploring other destinations in Corsica. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Bonifacio: A stunning medieval town located 25 km south of Porto-Vecchio.
  • Les Calanques de Piana: A beautiful coastal area known for its red cliffs and turquoise waters, located 100 km northwest of Porto-Vecchio.
  • The Scandola Nature Reserve: A UNESCO World Heritage site featuring stunning landscapes and a variety of wildlife, located 175 km north of Porto-Vecchio.

Conclusion

Porto-Vecchio is a beautiful town located in the south of Corsica. It offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it an excellent destination for tourists. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and experience all that Porto-Vecchio has to offer.
