Cargèse is a charming village located on the western coast of Corsica, France. Known for its breathtaking scenery, beautiful beaches, and rich history, Cargèse is a must-visit destination for travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Cargèse, as well as provide tips for a safe and enjoyable journey.

Introduction to Cargèse

Cargèse is a small, peaceful town that is known for its unique blend of cultures. Originally, Cargèse was a Greek colony, and it still has strong Greek influences, as seen in its architecture and food. However, it’s also a popular spot for tourists, particularly during the summer months, which has led to a significant increase in tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Cargèse

There are many exciting places to visit in Cargèse, ranging from historic sites and museums to beautiful natural settings. Here are a few recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eglise Grecque Orthodoxe Saint Spyridon: This Greek Orthodox Church is one of the main historical sites in Cargèse and is known for its beautiful architecture.
  • Notre-Dame de la Sagesse: This church is located on a hill overlooking the town and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Musée Marc Petit: This museum is dedicated to the works of the painter Marc Petit and features paintings that depict Corsica’s beautiful landscapes.

Natural Attractions of Cargèse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage du Pero: This stunning beach is located just outside of Cargèse and is a great spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Mont Gozzi: This mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area and is a popular spot for hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cargèse

The hiking and biking routes in Cargèse are some of the best in Corsica, with trails that take you through forests, mountains, and along the coast. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Cargèse:

  • Sentier des Crêtes: This trail follows the ridge lines along the coast and provides stunning views of the Mediterranean and surrounding mountains.
  • Mare e Monti: This trail takes you through diverse landscapes, including forests, rocky cliffs, and seaside villages.

Best Things to Do in Cargèse: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are a variety of other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Cargèse:

  • Scuba Diving: The waters off the coast of Cargèse are home to an abundance of marine life, making it a great spot for scuba diving.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to explore the beautiful coastline of Cargèse and discover hidden coves and beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cargèse

If you’re planning to spend a week in Cargèse, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Cargèse and visit Eglise Grecque Orthodoxe Saint Spyridon and Notre-Dame de la Sagesse.
  • Day 2: Hike Sentier des Crêtes and enjoy the beautiful views of the coast.
  • Day 3: Spend a day at Plage du Pero, swimming and enjoying the beach.
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour of the coastline and discover hidden coves and beaches.
  • Day 5: Visit Musée Marc Petit and learn more about the history and culture of Corsica.
  • Day 6: Hike Mare e Monti and explore the diverse landscapes of Cargèse.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy your last day in Cargèse.

Food and Drink Scene of Cargèse

Corsican cuisine is rich and diverse, and Cargèse offers an abundance of options for food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fiadone: A delicious Corsican cheesecake made with fresh cheese, sugar, and lemon zest.
  • Charcuterie: Corsican cured meats, including lonzu, coppa, and figatellu.

Local Beverages

  • Pietra: A popular Corsican beer made with chestnuts.
  • Muscat: A sweet wine made from muscat grapes.

Where to Stay in Cargèse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of options for accommodations in Cargèse, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Casa di Mare: This hotel offers beautiful rooms with stunning sea views and is located just steps away from the beach.
  • Casa Mare e Monti: This guesthouse is located in a traditional Corsican building and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Auberge du Menhir: This hostel offers a friendly and relaxed atmosphere and is popular with adventurers and hikers.

What to Buy in Cargèse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Cargèse, be sure to pick up some souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some popular options:

  • Corsican Honey: Made from wildflowers and maquis, Corsican honey is delicious and unique.
  • Olive Oil: Corsican olive oil is known for its high quality and is a popular local product.
  • Calisson: A traditional Corsican sweet made from almonds and candied fruit.

Local Culture and Customs of Cargèse

Cargèse is a unique blend of cultures, with strong Greek influences mixed with traditional Corsican culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes for churches and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Cargèse

The best time to visit Cargèse is during the summer months, between June and September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cargèse

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local dishes and products.
  • Explore the beautiful natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or in natural areas.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing at religious sites.
  • Argue with locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Cargèse is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and good healthcare facilities. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions and stay aware of their surroundings.

Safety in Cargèse

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended.
  • Stay aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Cargèse

  • Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance that covers emergency medical treatment.
  • Bring sunscreen to protect against the sun’s harmful rays.

Getting to Cargèse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cargèse is located on the west coast of Corsica and is easily accessible by plane, bus, or car.

Airports Near Cargèse

  • Ajaccio – Napoléon Bonaparte Airport: This airport is the closest to Cargèse and is located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Cargèse

  • Ajaccio Bus Station: Buses from Ajaccio to Cargèse run several times a day.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option for visitors who want to explore Corsica at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Cargèse

The best way to get around in Cargèse is by car or bike. Buses are also available, but schedules may be limited outside of peak season.

Where to Go Next from Cargèse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Corsica, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Porto: This charming fishing village is located about an hour’s drive from Cargèse and is known for its stunning natural beauty.
  • Piana: This village is located near Porto and is famous for its stunning Calanques, or rocky creeks.
  • Scandola Nature Reserve: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located near Cargèse and is home to endangered species and unique flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Cargèse is a unique and beautiful destination that offers something for everyone. From hiking and biking to exploring the town’s history and culture, there are endless opportunities for discovery and adventure in this charming seaside village. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be prepared to make the most of your visit to Cargèse and experience the best that Corsica has to offer.
