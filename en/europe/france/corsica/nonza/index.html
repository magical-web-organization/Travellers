Introduction to Nonza

Nonza is a picturesque town situated on the west coast of Corsica in France. It is known for its black sandy beach, stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, and its charming architecture. This hidden gem of Corsica is perfect for a family vacation or a solo backpacking trip, where you can enjoy its beauty and tranquility. In this travel guide, we will explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, the local culture, and must-try dishes & drinks of Nonza.

List of Places to Visit in Nonza

There are many fantastic places to visit in Nonza. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nonza Tower: This historic tower offers panoramic views of the sea and the village. It was built in the 16th century and has walls that are nearly 5 feet thick, which helped protect the villagers from invaders.
  • Eglise de la Sainte Trinité: This beautiful Catholic church was built-in the 19th century and is known for its stunning stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Nonza: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage de Nonza: This black sandy beach is a must-visit. It is unique and offers stunning views of the sea. However, be aware that the waves can be mighty strong.
  • Cap Corse: This is the northernmost part of Corsica and is home to a beautiful natural reserve. You can explore the hiking trails that take you through the wilderness and up to the mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nonza

If you are a hiking or biking enthusiast, you will love Nonza. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Sentier des Douaniers: This is a challenging coastal trail that offers breathtaking views of the sea and mountains.
  • GR20: This is a renowned trail among hikers that offers a challenging and rewarding experience.

Best Things to Do in Nonza: Activities and Tours

There are a lot of fun things to do in Nonza:

  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: You can rent kayaks and paddleboards and explore the sea. This is a great way to enjoy the beautiful coastal scenery.
  • Wine Tasting: Corsica is renowned for its wine production, and Nonza is no exception. You can do wine tastings at the local vineyard and enjoy the flavors of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nonza

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Nonza:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Visit the Nonza Tower and explore the village.
Day 2 Hike the Sentier des Douaniers trail and explore the coastline.
Day 3 Visit the Plage de Nonza and enjoy a day at the beach.
Day 4 Do a wine tasting tour and explore the local vineyards.
Day 5 Visit the Cap Corse and explore the natural reserve.
Day 6 Take a day trip to the nearby island of Elba and explore the beaches and historical sites.
Day 7 Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Nonza

When it comes to food in Nonza, you will not be disappointed. Here are some must-try dishes & drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brocciu Cheese: This is a traditional Corsican cheese that is used in many dishes. You can try it in a salad or baked on bread.
  • Corsican Lamb: This dish is a staple of the region, and the slow-cooked lamb is served with chestnuts and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Corsican Wine: Corsica is known for producing fantastic wines, and you can find them at many local wineries and restaurants.
  • Pietra beer: This beer is brewed in Corsica and has a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Nonza: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nonza has many accommodation options to choose from:

  • Hotel Santa Maria: This hotel is located on the seafront and offers stunning views of the sea and mountains.
  • Nonza Beach Hostel: This is a budget-friendly hostel that offers a relaxed atmosphere and stunning views of the beach.

What to Buy in Nonza: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, here are a few local products to bring home:

  • Corsican Honey: You can find high-quality honey at the local markets and shops.
  • Corsican Charcuterie: The local charcuteries offer a variety of cured meats, including ham, sausage, and salami.

Local Culture and Customs of Nonza

Corsica has a unique culture, and it is essential to respect the local customs and etiquette. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Language: French is the official language, but Corsican is also spoken widely.
  • Greetings: It is customary to greet people with a handshake or a kiss on both cheeks.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected, but it is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Nonza

The best time to visit Nonza is during the summer months, from June to September. However, keep in mind that this is also the busiest time of the year, and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nonza

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Nonza:

Do:

  • Enjoy the scenery: Nonza is incredibly scenic, and you should take the time to enjoy it.
  • Respect the local customs: Corsicans are proud of their culture, and you should take the time to learn about it.
  • Try the local cuisine: Corsican food is delicious, and you should try as much of it as possible.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind: It is essential to keep Nonza clean and tidy.
  • Disturb the wildlife: Corsica has a diverse range of flora and fauna, and it is important not to disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore safety warnings: The sea in Nonza can be dangerous, and it is essential to follow safety warnings.

Safety and Health

Nonza is a safe place to visit, but it is essential to keep these tips in mind:

Safety in Nonza

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Corsica is a safe place but it is important to be aware of your surroundings.
  • Be careful around the sea: The currents around Nonza can be strong, and it is important to follow safety warnings.

Health in Nonza

  • Stay hydrated: Corsica can get hot during the summer months, and it is important to stay hydrated.
  • Take precautions against mosquitoes: Mosquitoes can be a nuisance in Corsica, and it is important to take the necessary precautions.

Getting to Nonza: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nonza is located on the west coast of Corsica, and here are a few transportation options:

  • By Plane: You can fly to Corsica - Calvi Sainte Catherine Airport, which is about a 40-minute drive from Nonza.
  • By Bus: You can take a bus from Ajaccio or Bastia, but keep in mind that services may be limited.
  • By Car: You can rent a car and drive to Nonza.

Airports Near Nonza

  • Calvi Sainte Catherine Airport: This is the closest airport to Nonza, and it is situated about 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Nonza

  • Ajaccio Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Ajaccio and is about a two-hour drive from Nonza.

Other Options

  • Ferry: You can take a ferry from Toulon, Nice, or Marseille to Calvi, which is about a 40-minute drive from Nonza.

How to Get Around in Nonza

The best way to get around Nonza is on foot. However, if you want to explore further out, you can rent a car or a scooter.

Where to Go Next from Nonza: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations to add to your itinerary:

  • Bonifacio: This is a beautiful town situated on the coast of Southern Corsica and is known for its stunning cliffs.
  • Ajaccio: This is the capital of Corsica and is a great place to explore its rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Nonza is a beautiful and tranquil destination that is perfect for those looking to escape the crowds and enjoy some peace and serenity. This guide will help you plan your trip and explore the best of what Nonza has to offer. Enjoy your trip, and don’t forget to take in its natural beauty and rich culture.
