Introduction to Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

Located in Landes, in the region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine in France, Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is a charming little town with plenty to offer visitors. Famous for its thermal baths and natural beauty, Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is an excellent destination for those looking to relax and rejuvenate.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arènes de Dax: An ancient Roman amphitheater that can hold up to 15,000 people. It is used for bullfights and other cultural events.
  • Le Musée de Borda: A museum showcasing the history of thermalism in Dax from the 19th century to the present day. The museum offers stunning views of the Adour river and is worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Paul-lès-Dax: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Forêt de l’Adour: A beautiful forest with walking trails and picnic spots.
  • Lac de Christus: A lovely lake that provides plenty of water sports such as kayaking, paddle boarding, and more.
  • Sources d’Houstalet: A natural mineral spring that is known for its healing properties.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

  • Les berges de l’Adour: A beautiful riverside path with stunning views of the Adour river.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the thermal baths: Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is known for its thermal baths, where visitors can relax and rejuvenate.
  • Take a bike ride around the town: Rent a bike and explore the charming streets and beautiful countryside of Saint-Paul-lès-Dax.
  • Enjoy water sports on Lac de Christus.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

Must-Try Dishes

  • Foie gras: A delicacy made from duck or goose liver.
  • Ttoro: A traditional Basque fish soup made with local fish and seafood.
  • Magret de canard: A dish of duck breast that is commonly served in France.

Local Beverages

  • Armagnac: A strong brandy that is produced in the nearby Gers region.
  • Bordeaux wine: A famous wine produced in the region of Bordeaux, which is located just a few hours away from Saint-Paul-lès-Dax.

Where to Stay in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel du Lac: A charming hotel close to Lac de Christus.
  • Hôtel le Richelieu: A 4-star hotel with a spa and wellness center.
  • Hôtel des Thermes: A hotel located near the thermal baths.

What to Buy in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is a small town with a close-knit community. The people of Saint-Paul-lès-Dax are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the relaxed pace of life in this charming town. The Basque culture is prominent in this region, so you may see traditional Basque clothing and hear the Basque language spoken in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

The best time to visit Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, if you want to take advantage of the thermal baths, it’s best to visit during the summer months when they are open.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is very safe for visitors. However, as in any town or city, there are some risks like pickpocketing or petty theft. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions when traveling and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Saint-Paul-lès-Dax

Saint-Paul-lès-Dax is known for its thermal baths, which are believed to have healing properties. However, visitors with any underlying health conditions are advised to consult a healthcare professional before taking part in any activities that may impact their health.
