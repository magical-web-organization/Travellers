Introduction to Biscarrosse

Biscarrosse is a charming town located in the Landes department of Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France. It is situated on the coast of the Atlantic Ocean and is known for its long, sandy beaches and beautiful pine forest. The town is a popular holiday destination among tourists from all over the world, thanks to its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage.

If you are planning a trip to Biscarrosse and looking for the top attractions and things to do, then this travel guide is for you. Here, we have compiled a comprehensive list of places to visit, activities to do, and local insights to help you plan a perfect trip to Biscarrosse.

List of Places to Visit in Biscarrosse

Biscarrosse is full of interesting attractions and cultural landmarks that are worth visiting during your stay. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Biscarrosse:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Traditions and History of Biscarrosse - A museum that showcases the history and traditional way of life of Biscarrosse.
  • Hydraviation Museum - A museum that focuses on the history of seaplanes and water-based aircraft.
  • Chateau de Montbron - A stunning castle that dates back to the 18th century and features beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Biscarrosse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Biscarrosse Beach - A long, sandy beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  • Lake Biscarrosse - A huge lake that is ideal for fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Landes Forest - A beautiful pine forest that is perfect for hiking, biking, and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biscarrosse

Biscarrosse is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes to take in the area:

  • La Route des Lacs - A 48 km scenic cycle route that takes you through the stunning Landes Forest and past several beautiful lakes.
  • Le Sentier du Littoral - A 15 km hiking trail that runs along the coast of Biscarrosse, offering breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • La Velodyssee - A 1,200 km cycling route that runs along the coast of the Atlantic Ocean, passing through Biscarrosse.

Best Things to Do in Biscarrosse: Activities and Tours

Biscarrosse offers plenty of fun and exciting activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Biscarrosse:

  • Try your hand at surfing - Biscarrosse Beach is a popular spot for surfers, with plenty of surf schools and rental shops to choose from.
  • Explore the local waterways - Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore Lake Biscarrosse and its many hidden coves and inlets.
  • Take a seaplane tour - Experience the thrill of flying over the stunning coast and lakes of Biscarrosse in a seaplane tour.
  • Visit the local markets - Discover the local culture and cuisine at the various markets around Biscarrosse, where you can find fresh produce, handicrafts, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biscarrosse

If you have a week to spend in Biscarrosse, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Traditions and History of Biscarrosse and explore the town’s charming streets and cafes.

Day 2: Spend a day at the beach, swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.

Day 3: Rent a bike and explore the Landes Forest, stopping for a picnic lunch along the way.

Day 4: Take a seaplane tour of Biscarrosse and its surrounding areas.

Day 5: Visit the Hydraviation Museum and spend the afternoon exploring Lake Biscarrosse by kayak or paddleboard.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Arcachon, known for its stunning beaches and oyster farms.

Day 7: Spend the day shopping at the local markets and sampling the delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Biscarrosse

Biscarrosse has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local specialties to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Magret de Canard - A popular local dish of duck breast served with a rich sauce.
  • Gâteau Basque - A traditional pastry filled with cream or fruit preserves.
  • Pintxos - A type of tapas that originated in the Basque Country, often served with seafood or cured meat.

Local Beverages

  • Armagnac - A type of brandy that is produced in the nearby Gascony region.
  • Wine - Biscarrosse is located in the Bordeaux wine region, known for its excellent reds and whites.
  • Pineau des Charentes - A sweet fortified wine that is produced in the nearby Charente department.

What to Buy in Biscarrosse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Biscarrosse

Biscarrosse has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong Basque influence. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the laid-back lifestyle of the area. When you visit Biscarrosse, be respectful of the local customs, including the siesta (a midday break) and the importance of family and community.

Best Time to Visit Biscarrosse

The best time to visit Biscarrosse is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you prefer a quieter and cooler experience, you may want to visit during the shoulder seasons of April to May or September to November.
