Introduction to Poitiers

Poitiers is a historic city and commune situated in western France. Located in the Vienne region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine, it is home to several impressive landmarks, including an ancient amphitheater and the iconic Notre-Dame la Grande church, which dates back to the 11th century. Poitiers is a charming destination that is perfect for travelers looking for a glimpse into French history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Poitiers

If you’re planning a trip to Poitiers, here are some of the top destinations and attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palais des Comtes de Poitou - a stunning palace that used to serve as the residence of the Counts of Poitou in the 10th century, it is now a museum that showcases medieval art and artifacts.
  • Musée Sainte-Croix - houses a vast collection of regional and medieval art, including sculptures, paintings, and other artifacts.
  • Futuroscope - an amusement park that offers a futuristic multimedia experience through 3D simulators, IMAX cinemas, and other digital technologies.
  • Musée Rupert de Chièvres - a museum that displays a collection of artworks, ceramics, and historical artifacts. It also houses the Diocesan Museum, which showcases various religious items.

Natural Attractions of Poitiers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc de Blossac - a popular green space that encompasses over 9 hectares and features beautiful flower gardens, fountains, and walking trails.
  • La Vallée des Singes - a primate sanctuary that is home to over 300 apes and monkeys from across the globe. Visitors can observe the animals in their natural habitats and learn more about their behaviors and characteristics.
  • Planète des Crocodiles - an indoor crocodile habitat that features large enclosures and interactive exhibits where visitors can observe the reptiles up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poitiers

Poitiers offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • La Voie Verte - a scenic cycling trail that stretches over 42 kilometers and runs along the River Clain. The path is mostly flat, making it suitable for cyclists of all levels.
  • GR® 655 - an extensive hiking trail that spans over 1,400 kilometers and connects the city of Vézelay to Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port.
  • Puy de Pampaud - a challenging hiking trail that leads to a stunning lookout point that offers panoramic views of Poitiers and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Poitiers: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for exciting and engaging activities to do in Poitiers, here are some suggestions:

  • Les Balades de Poitiers - a guided walking tour that takes visitors on a journey through the city’s historic landmarks, including the Notre-Dame la Grande church and the ancient ruins of the Roman amphitheater.
  • Escape Game Poitiers - a thrilling escape room experience that challenges participants to solve puzzles and riddles to escape from a themed room.
  • Restaurants and Cafés - Poitiers is renowned for its diverse culinary offerings, from French gastronomy to international cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include steak frites, escargots, and tarte tatin.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poitiers

If you’re planning to stay in Poitiers for a week, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1 Explore the city’s main attractions, including the Palais des Comtes de Poitou, Musée Sainte-Croix, and Notre-Dame la Grande church.
Day 2 Spend the day at the Futuroscope theme park and enjoy its 3D simulators and IMAX cinemas.
Day 3 Take a guided walking tour of the city, including its hidden gems and historical landmarks.
Day 4 Spend the day hiking or cycling along La Voie Verte or the Puy de Pampaud trail.
Day 5 Visit the Parc de Blossac or La Vallée des Singes and enjoy some time in nature.
Day 6 Have a culinary adventure and try some of the local delicacies at Poitiers’ best restaurants and cafés.
Day 7 Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as the Château de Chenonceau or Saint-Savin Abbey.

Food and Drink Scene of Poitiers

Poitiers is a foodie paradise, with plenty of restaurants and cafés offering a wide range of culinary experiences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to savor during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Broyé Poitevin - a traditional Poitevin cake made from flour, butter, sugar, and eggs.
  • Confiture de Poires Tapées - a unique type of pear jelly that is slowly cooked and dried before being boiled to create a paste.
  • Fouace - a sweet, airy bread that is typically served with honey, butter, or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Pineau des Charentes - a popular regional wine that is made by mixing cognac and grape juice to create a balanced sweet and sour flavor.
  • Chinon Rouge - a red wine that is produced in the Loire Valley and is known for its fruity and smooth taste.
  • Breuvage Vert - a green-colored local beverage made from apple and honey, with a slight minty flavor.

Where to Stay in Poitiers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Poitiers has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some top picks for hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Hotel de l’Europe - a budget-friendly hotel situated in the city center, with comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • Best Western Poitiers Centre Le Grand Hotel - a four-star hotel that offers modern and stylish rooms, exceptional service, and stunning views of the city.
  • Jacques Cœur Residence Hotel - an elegant aparthotel that offers spacious and fully-equipped apartments, exceptional amenities, and a prime location in the historic city center.

What to Buy in Poitiers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Poitiers is known for its local specialties, including food, wine, and handmade crafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Stained Glass Artwork - Poitiers is renowned for its beautiful and intricate stained glass artwork, which makes a perfect souvenir or gift.
  • Pineau des Charentes - a local wine that is perfect for wine lovers and connoisseurs.
  • Artisanal Pottery - handmade pottery and ceramics are an integral part of Poitiers’ craft scene, with many craftsmen specializing in unique and intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Poitiers

Poitiers is a city that values its rich cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and practices, including:

  • Greetings - the French custom is to greet people with a kiss on each cheek.
  • Table Manners - Poitevins value good table manners and consider it impolite to talk with your mouth full or lean your elbows on the table.
  • Respect for religion - Poitevins are generally very respectful of religious practices, and visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and show reverence when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Poitiers

The best time to visit Poitiers is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April-May) and fall (September-October), when the weather is mild, and crowds are fewer. Summer (June-August) is the peak tourist season, with warmer temperatures and higher hotel rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poitiers

When traveling to Poitiers, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some French - while English is widely spoken in Poitiers, locals appreciate it when visitors take the time to learn some basic French phrases.
  • Explore the city on foot - Poitiers’ historic city center is best explored on foot, so wear comfortable shoes and take plenty of breaks.
  • Try the local cuisine - Poitiers is renowned for its gastronomy, with plenty of delicious dishes and unique flavors to discover.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive - Poitevins value their peace and quiet, so avoid making unnecessary noise or acting in a disruptive manner.
  • Dress inappropriately - Poitiers is a conservative city, so dress modestly and avoid wearing revealing or offensive clothing.
  • Forget to tip - while not mandatory, it’s customary to leave a small tip (around 10%) in restaurants and cafés.

Safety and Health

Safety in Poitiers

Poitiers is generally a safe city, but visitors are advised to take basic precautions to avoid petty theft and other crimes. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay vigilant in crowded areas - pickpocketing is a common problem in tourist areas and crowded places.
  • Avoid walking alone at night - stick to well-lit and populated areas when walking at night or take a taxi or public transportation instead.
  • Be aware of traffic - French drivers can be aggressive, so exercise caution when walking or biking on the roads.

Health in Poitiers

Poitiers has excellent healthcare facilities, with hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies located throughout the city. Visitors are encouraged to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and to carry essential medication with them.

Getting to Poitiers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Poitiers is well-connected to other parts of France and Europe through several means of transportation. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

  • By Plane - Poitiers-Biard Airport serves domestic and international routes, including flights from London, Edinburgh, and Lyon.
  • By Train - Poitiers’ main train station is located in the city center and offers high-speed trains (TGV) that connect to Paris, Bordeaux, and other major cities in France and Europe.
  • By Bus - Poitiers has several bus stations that offer regional and national routes, including Eurolines and Flixbus.

How to Get Around in Poitiers

Poitiers is a compact and walk-able city, and most of its attractions are located within the city center. Visitors can easily explore the city on foot, by bike, or by public transportation, including trams and buses.

Where to Go Next from Poitiers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region around Poitiers, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • La Rochelle - a charming coastal town that is known for its beautiful sandy beaches, historic port, and medieval architecture.
  • Château de Chenonceau - a stunning Renaissance chateau that is situated over the River Cher, and is surrounded by beautiful gardens and parkland.
  • Futuroscope - an amusement park that offers a futuristic multimedia experience through 3D simulators, IMAX cinemas, and other digital technologies.

Conclusion

Poitiers is a city that offers visitors a glimpse into French history, culture, and gastronomy. With its stunning landmarks, natural attractions, and excellent transportation links, Poitiers is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a memorable and unforgettable experience. Whether you’re a history buff, a wine lover, or a nature enthusiast, you’re sure to find something to love in Poitiers.
