Introduction to Angoulême

Nestled in the department of Charente in the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region of France, Angoulême is a picturesque town that boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant cultural scene. Known as the “balcony of the Southwest” for its stunning views of the surrounding countryside, Angoulême is a popular destination among nature lovers, adventure seekers, and history buffs alike. With a variety of attractions, activities, and events, Angoulême is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of France.

List of Places to Visit in Angoulême

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Angouleme Cathedral: A beautiful Romanesque-style cathedral atop a hill, offering panoramic views of the city.
  • Musée de la bande dessinée: A museum dedicated to comic books and illustration with both permanent and temporary exhibits.
  • Château de la Mercerie: A stunning 19th-century castle now home to the Museum of Paper.
  • Ducale Palace: The historic residence of the counts of Angoulême, now an exhibition center.

Natural Attractions of Angoulême: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Coulée Verte: A park that runs along the ramparts of the old town, perfect for a peaceful walk amidst nature’s serenity.
  • L’Isle d’Espagnac: A small town located on the outskirts of Angoulême, with picturesque views of the Charente River.
  • Golf du Cognac: A golf course with stunning views of the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Angoulême

  • Le Bois de la Grande Garenne: A beautiful forest perfect for a scenic hike or bike ride.
  • Circuit des remparts: A circuit that travels around the ramparts of the old town of Angoulême.
  • The Greenway: A 15-kilometer designated biking route that runs along the Charente River.

Best Things to Do in Angoulême: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Old Town: A picturesque town that dates back to medieval times, offering beautiful old buildings, unique boutiques, and excellent restaurants.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: Get a bird’s eye view of the beautiful Charente countryside, and enjoy the spectacular scenery from above.
  • Visit the Vineyards: Explore the neighboring vineyards, learn about the wine-making process, and enjoy a wine tasting session.

Food and Drink Scene of Angoulême

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cognac-Marinated Foie Gras: A staple of the Charente region, this dish features deboned duck or goose liver marinated in Cognac, often served with toasted bread or chutney.
  • Mouclade: A traditional mussel dish cooked in a cream and white wine sauce, flavored with onion and curry powder.
  • Pineau des Charentes: A sweet and fruity aperitif made from mixing cognac and grape must, a must-try drink of Charente.

Local Beverages

  • Cognac: The world-famous spirit that the French region is renowned for, a strong liquor made from grapes and traditionally distilled in copper pot stills.
  • Pineau des Charentes: A sweet and fruity aperitif made from mixing cognac with grape must, exclusive to the region.
  • Charentais Apple Juice: A slightly sour apple juice, a perfect refreshing drink for those hot summer days in the region.

Where to Stay in Angoulême: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Angoulême offers a wide range of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels.

  • Hôtel Mercure Angoulême Centre: A contemporary hotel in the heart of the city, equipped with modern amenities.
  • Chambres d’Hôtes Les Jardins d’Angoulême: A charming guesthouse with a garden terrace, located near the old town and museums.
  • Hôtel Campanile Angoulême Ouest - Saint-Yrieix: A budget-friendly hotel, perfect for those looking for affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Angoulême: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cognac and Pineau des Charentes: The region’s signature drinks, a perfect gift or souvenir to take home.
  • Charentaise Slippers: Traditional French slippers, comfortable and stylish, originated from the Charente region.
  • Perfumes from the Worth Palace: The Worth Palace, which is based in nearby town of Cognac, custom-makes fragrances for visitors.
  • Charente Pottery: Handmade ceramics that reflect the history and art of the Charente region.

Local Culture and Customs of Angoulême

The people of Angoulême are proud of their rich cultural heritage, which they celebrate through various events and festivals throughout the year. Respect for traditions and the slower pace of life are highly valued in this region.

Best Time to Visit Angoulême

The best time to visit Angoulême is from June to September. Summer offers warm weather, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities, soaking up the sun, and enjoying the local culture, but the crowds might get overwhelming. During autumn and winter, the town is like a charmed painting with the added warmth of a fireplace on the chilly evenings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Angoulême

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases of French, it will help you communicate with locals more easily.
  • Respect the traditional pace of life and don’t rush people.
  • Try the local beverages and traditional French delicacies.
  • Take time to explore the hidden gems of the town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t expect shops, restaurants, and cafes are always open on the weekend.
  • Don’t litter on the streets or in public spaces.
  • Don’t over-tip in restaurants; it is not obligatory in France.
  • Don’t ignore French manners and customs. Try to greet with ‘Bonjour.’

Safety and Health

Safety in Angoulême

Angoulême is a safe town, but it is always advisable to stay alert and aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Watch out for pickpocketing in touristy areas.

Health in Angoulême

The health sector in France is excellent. There are many clinics, hospitals, and pharmacies throughout the region. It is recommended that you have health insurance to cover you for any treatment you may require.

Getting to Angoulême: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Angoulême

  • Angoulême-Cognac International Airport: The nearest airport, only a few miles from the city, offering flights to Paris, Nice, and other destinations in Europe.

Bus Stations Near Angoulême

  • SNCF Angoulême: A railway station close to the city center that offers connections to other parts of France.
  • Gare Routière: The central bus station, located next to the train station, for buses running in and out of the town.

Other Options

  • Driving: Car rental is a viable option for exploring the town and surrounding areas, with easy access to well-maintained roads.
  • Cycling: A healthy way of traveling, and a very popular mode of transport in Angoulême.

How to Get Around in Angoulême

  • On Foot: With the town’s compact size, walking is an excellent way to explore the old district.
  • By Bus: The town’s public buses offer a reliable and cost-effective way to travel around the region.
  • By Car: Renting a car is an excellent option for exploring the surrounding countryside and nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Angoulême: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cognac: Located a short trip south-west of Angoulême, this charming town is world-renowned for the eponymous drink, Cognac.
  • Bordeaux: Just a train ride away, Bordeaux offers an ideal escape, with many restaurants, museums, galleries, and the exceptional wine culture.

Conclusion

Angoulême is a destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and opportunities to relax and unwind. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with everything you need to plan your trip and make the most of this picturesque town. Visit Angoulême for a trip that you’ll cherish for years to come!
