Introduction to Pau

Pau is a magnificent city located in the Pyrénées-Atlantiques department in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France. This charming city is surrounded by picturesque mountains, unique architecture, rich culture, and incredible landmarks. Known for its scenic beauty, outdoor adventures, and mouth-watering cuisine - Pau is a must-visit destination for every traveler.

In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, eat, stay, and things to do in Pau. Experience outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local cuisine with our ultimate travel guide of Pau, France.

List of Places to Visit in Pau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pau has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its museums and historical places. Here are some of the top culturally important attractions to visit:

  • Château de Pau: The historic castle that was once the home of French royalty is a must-see attraction. The castle is now a National Museum that houses tapestries, paintings, sculptures, and a collection of art and artifacts.
  • Musée des Beaux-Arts: It’s a beautiful museum that showcases the works of famous artists such as Rembrandt, El Greco, and Goya.
  • Palais Beaumont: A perfect blend of traditional and modern architecture, Palais Beaumont is a conference center that is famous for its beautiful gardens and fountains.
  • Boulevard des Pyrénées: A stunning boulevard that offers a panoramic view of the Pyrenees.

Natural Attractions of Pau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pau is home to some of the breathtaking natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit in Pau:

  • Pyrénées National Park: An ideal destination for hiking, skiing, and snowboarding amidst the scenic mountains and valleys of France.
  • Le Belvédère: A viewpoint that provides a stunning panoramic view of Pau city and the Pyrenees mountains.
  • Parc Beaumont: A beautiful park that offers plenty of green space and walking trails for those seeking a quiet escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pau

Pau is known for its outdoor activities, and hiking and biking are some of the most popular activities for adventurous travelers. There are several hiking/biking trails in and around Pau that are perfect for exploring the surrounding natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Pau:

  • GR10: The Grand Randonnée 10 trail is one of the most famous hiking trails in Europe. Spanning over 865 km, this trail offers beautiful views of the Pyrenees mountains.
  • Circuit du Vic-Bilh: A 70-kilometer cycling and hiking trail that is perfect for those looking for a challenging and picturesque route.

Best Things to Do in Pau: Activities and Tours

Pau has plenty of exciting things to offer, from adrenaline-pumping activities to relaxing tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Pau:

  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and experience breathtaking views of the Pyrenees mountains while paragliding.
  • City Tour: Take a guided tour of the city and explore the historic landmarks and architectural wonders of Pau.
  • Wine Tour: Discover the famous vineyards surrounding Pau, taste the local wines, and learn about the rich history of winemaking in France.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pau

To make the most of your visit to Pau, here is a perfect week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Château de Pau and explore the historic city center.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to the Pyrénées National Park and enjoy the stunning natural landscape.
  • Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Palais Beaumont and enjoy a relaxing afternoon in the Parc Beaumont.
  • Day 4: Explore the Musée des Beaux-Arts and the Boulevard des Pyrénées.
  • Day 5: Go paragliding or take a cable car to Le Pic du Jer to enjoy panoramic views of the city.
  • Day 6: Take a wine tour and taste the local wines of the region.
  • Day 7: Spend your last day hiking the Circuit du Vic-Bilh or relaxing in one of the cafes around the city center.

Food and Drink Scene of Pau

Pau is known for its delicious cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Pau:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pintxos: A Basque tapas-style dish that consists of a slice of baguette topped with a variety of toppings such as cheese, ham, and seafood.
  • Garbure: A hearty soup made with a variety of vegetables, beans, and meat.
  • Pau’s cheese plate: A delicious assortment of local cheeses, often served with fig jam and honey.

Local Beverages

  • Jurançon wine: A sweet white wine produced locally near Pau that pairs well with the local cuisine.
  • Cidre: An apple cider that is a popular drink in the region.
  • Basque beer: Enjoy a cold beer made by the local brewery, La Brasserie du Pays Basque.

Where to Stay in Pau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pau has some fantastic accommodation options that cater to every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Pau:

  • Hotel Parc Beaumont: A five-star luxury hotel that offers elegant rooms, an upscale restaurant, and a beautiful spa.
  • Hotel Villa Navarre: A four-star hotel that features sophisticated rooms, lush gardens, and an outdoor pool.
  • Hostel Gastama: A budget-friendly hostel located in the historic city center that offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Pau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pau has several local products and souvenirs that you can take back home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Pau:

  • Beret: A traditional French hat that is made locally in Pau.
  • Basque pottery: Hand-painted pottery that is a typical souvenir of the region.
  • Jurançon wine: The locally produced sweet white wine makes a perfect gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Pau

Pau has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting Pau:

  • Greetings: It’s common to greet people with a kiss on both cheeks.
  • Siesta: It’s an afternoon nap or break that is an essential part of the Spanish and French culture and is observed in Pau.
  • Basque language: While French is the official language of Pau, the Basque language is spoken by some locals and is a vital part of the city’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Pau

The best time to visit Pau is during the spring season (April-May) and the autumn season (September-November). During these periods, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pau

Here are some of the things to do and avoid in Pau:

Do:

  • Enjoy the city’s fantastic food and wines.
  • Explore the historic landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • Take advantage of the beautiful outdoor activities and scenic beauty.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun while exploring the city.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately while visiting religious or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pau

Pau is a safe city, but it’s always essential to be cautious and aware of your surroundings while exploring the city. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Pau

Pau has excellent healthcare facilities, and emergency services are readily available in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Pau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pau

Pau Pyrénées Airport is the main airport that serves Pau, and it’s located about 7 km from the city center. You can also consider flying to Toulouse-Blagnac Airport or Bordeaux-Mérignac Airport, which are both about two hours away from Pau.

Bus Stations Near Pau

Pau has well-connected bus stations that offer transportation to other parts of France. The primary bus station is the Gare Routière de Pau.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Pau from other cities in France. You can use the French National Railway Service’s website, www.oui.sncf, to book your tickets.

How to Get Around in Pau

Pau is a small city, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. There are also several bus routes that operate within the city. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Pau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pau is surrounded by several beautiful destinations that are worth a visit. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Pau:

  • Biarritz: A coastal town that is famous for its beaches and surf culture.
  • Lourdes: A small town that is famous for its religious pilgrimages and beautiful churches.
  • Bayonne: A charming town that is known for its Basque culture and delicious chocolate.

Conclusion

Pau is a hidden gem of France, and it’s the perfect destination for those seeking adventure, scenic beauty, and a rich cultural experience.
