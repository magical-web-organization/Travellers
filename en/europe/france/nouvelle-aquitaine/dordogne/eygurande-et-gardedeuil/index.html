Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil Travel Guide

Located in the heart of Dordogne, in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil is a charming French village that offers beautiful natural sceneries, a rich cultural heritage, and delicious gastronomy. For travelers who want to escape from the bustling city life and immerse themselves in the tranquil beauty of a rural French village, Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil is an ideal destination. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to this enchanting village with ease.

Introduction to Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil is a small village surrounded by rolling hills, forests, and rivers. The village has a population of approximately 1,000 people, and its history dates back to the Middle Ages. The village has a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical sites to explore. The village is also known for its delicious culinary traditions, such as foie gras, truffles, and wine, which can be enjoyed at local restaurants and markets.

List of Places to Visit in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château de Montaigne: The castle where Michel de Montaigne, a famous French philosopher, wrote his essays in the 16th century.
  • Moulin de la Veyssière: A 16th-century watermill that has been restored and now houses a museum about the history of milling.
  • Église Saint-Pierre-et-Saint-Paul: The village church with a 12th-century Romanesque bell tower and a 19th-century Gothic-style nave.

Natural Attractions of Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Naturel Régional Périgord-Limousin: A natural park that covers 1,800 square kilometers and offers hiking, cycling, and horseback riding trails.
  • River Isle: A scenic river that runs through the village and offers opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and swimming.
  • Forêt de la Double: A vast forest that covers 50,000 hectares and provides a habitat for a variety of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

  • Circuit du Moulin de la Veyssière: A 9 km hiking trail that starts from the Moulin de la Veyssière and passes through the forest and countryside.
  • Circuit de la Vallée de l’Isle: A 14 km cycling trail that follows the River Isle and offers beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Circuit des Lacs de la Jemaye: A 22 km hiking trail that takes you around the lakes of La Jemaye and offers stunning natural sceneries.

Best Things to Do in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Visit the local vineyards and taste some of the best wines of the region at Domaine de la Jaubertie or Château de Tiregand.
  • Truffle Hunting: Join a truffle hunting tour and learn about the process of finding and harvesting truffles in the forests.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy a bird’s-eye view of the village and the surrounding countryside on a hot air balloon ride with Montgolfières du Périgord.

Food and Drink Scene of Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

Must-Try Dishes

  • Foie Gras: A delicacy made from the liver of a duck or goose that is typically served in a terrine with bread or wine.
  • Truffles: A rare and expensive delicacy that is harvested from the forests and used in various dishes such as omelets, pasta, or meats.
  • Périgordine Salad: A salad made with duck gizzards, smoked ham, walnuts, and lettuce, dressed with walnut oil and vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Bergerac Wine: A wine produced in the surrounding vineyards, primarily from the Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Malbec grapes.
  • Vin de Noix: A wine made from green walnuts, sugar, and red wine that is usually served as an aperitif.
  • Périgordine Beer: A locally brewed beer made with hop, barley, and wheat.

Where to Stay in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Château de Lalande: A 16th-century castle that has been converted into a luxury hotel with a swimming pool and gardens.
  • Les Voyages du Cœur: A cozy bed and breakfast in the heart of the village with comfortable rooms and friendly hosts.
  • Camping La Grande Veyière: A campsite located in the forest that offers fully equipped tents and caravans for rent.

What to Buy in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Foie Gras: A jar of foie gras from a local farm makes a great souvenir or gift for foodies.
  • Truffles: A small jar of truffle paste or salt makes a unique and delicious souvenir.
  • Wooden Toys: The village is known for its traditional wooden toys, such as spinning tops, cars, and dolls, which make great gifts for children.

Local Culture and Customs of Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

The inhabitants of Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil are proud of their rich cultural heritage and traditions. They celebrate several festivals throughout the year, such as the Fête de la Musique in June, the Fête de l’Été in July, and the Fête de Noël in December. The village also has a strong culinary culture, with several local specialties, such as foie gras, truffles, and wine, that are celebrated and enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Best Time to Visit Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

The best time to visit Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil is during the months of April to October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural sceneries are at their best. The village is also lively during the summer season, with several festivals and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

  • Participate in the cultural festivals and events.
  • Explore the natural landscapes on foot, bike, or horseback.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil is a safe village with low crime rates. However, travelers should take appropriate safety measures while participating in outdoor activities and keep their belongings safe.

Health in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

The village has a small medical center and pharmacy. Travelers should have health insurance when visiting the village and take necessary precautions against ticks and other insects during outdoor activities.

Getting to Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

The closest airport to Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil is Bergerac Dordogne Périgord Airport, which is approximately 40 km away. Other nearby airports include Bordeaux-Mérignac Airport and Limoges-Bellegarde Airport.

Bus Stations Near Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

The village has a bus station that connects it to nearby towns and cities such as Périgueux and Bordeaux.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach the village by train or car. The nearest train station is in Périgueux, which is approximately 25 km away, and the village can be accessed by car via the A89 motorway or the D6089 road.

How to Get Around in Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil

The best way to explore the village and its surroundings is on foot, bike, or car. The village has several hiking and cycling trails, and a car allows visitors to explore the surrounding countryside at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Périgueux: A historical city with Roman ruins and a beautiful cathedral, located approximately 25 km from Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil.
  • Sarlat-la-Canéda: A historical town with beautiful medieval architecture and a vibrant market, located approximately 57 km from Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil.
  • Bordeaux: A cosmopolitan city known for its wine, culture, and history, located approximately 125 km from Eygurande-et-Gardedeuil.
