Introduction to Périgueux

Périgueux is located in the heart of the Périgord region, known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty and delicious food. The town is situated in the south-west of France, in the Dordogne department of the region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine. Périgueux is an important cultural center of the area, home to numerous historic landmarks, excellent museums and art galleries.

List of Places to Visit in Périgueux

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cathédrale Saint-Front: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this stunning cathedral is a must-visit attraction in Périgueux. Built in the 12th century, it boasts a unique Romanesque architecture and is one of the main symbols of the town.
  • Musée Vesunna: This museum displays Gallo-Roman artifacts, with a special focus on the ancient city of Vesunna. The museum building itself is a work of art, designed by famous architect Jean Nouvel.
  • Périgord Museum of Art and Archaeology: This museum is dedicated to the art and history of the Périgord region, spanning from the prehistoric era to the present day. You can explore interesting exhibits on local archaeology, natural history, and fine art.
  • Tour Mataguerre: This medieval tower is located in the center of the town and provides breathtaking views of the city, including the Cathedral Saint-Front.

Natural Attractions of Périgueux: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jardin des Arènes: This public garden is located near the Gallo-Roman amphitheater of Vesunna, and features beautiful flowers, trees, and statues.
  • Les Jardins de Marqueyssac: Just a short drive from Périgueux, this popular park features beautifully landscaped gardens that are perfect for a relaxing walk. You can enjoy stunning views of the Dordogne River and the surrounding countryside from the top of the cliffs.
  • Grotte de Lascaux: The prehistoric cave paintings of Lascaux is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and one of the most important examples of prehistoric art in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Périgueux

Périgueux offers several hiking and biking routes that traverse the beautiful countryside and scenic villages of the region. Some popular routes are:

  • Le Chemin de St-Jacques: This ancient pilgrim route leads through the Périgueux region, towards the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela in Spain.
  • La Voie Verte: This converted railway line provides a pleasant biking or walking experience, with opportunities to visit charming villages and hamlets along the way.
  • Circuit de l’Eau et de la Terre: This 54km hiking trail explores the natural beauty of the Périgord Noir region, with stunning views of the Dordogne Valley and the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Périgueux: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the beauty of the Périgord landscape and towns from the air with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Canoeing on the Dordogne River: Rent a canoe and enjoy a scenic day on the river, taking in the sights of beautiful castles and villages along the way.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: The Périgord region is known for its delicious wines, and there are many tours available that visit local wineries and vineyards.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn to cook the traditional dishes of the region with expert chefs who specialize in local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Périgueux

Day Activity
Monday Visit the Cathedral Saint-Front and Tour Mataguerre, followed by lunch at a local café or brasserie. In the afternoon, explore the Musée Vesunna and the Périgord Museum of Art and Archaeology.
Tuesday Take a leisurely bike ride on the Voie Verte and visit the charming village of Trélissac, followed by a wine tasting tour in the afternoon.
Wednesday Hike the Circuit de l’Eau et de la Terre, taking in the breathtaking views of the Dordogne Valley and forests. In the evening, attend a traditional French dinner with live music.

Friday Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning, followed by a visit to the Jardin des Arènes and lunch at a local bistro. In the afternoon, attend a painting class with a local artist.
Saturday Explore the Les Jardins de Marqueyssac and enjoy a picnic lunch with local wine and food. In the evening, attend a jazz concert in a local club.
Sunday Take a day trip to the nearby town of Sarlat-la-Canéda, known for its medieval architecture and excellent cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Périgueux

Must-Try Dishes

  • Foie Gras: This traditional dish is made of the fatty liver of duck or goose and is considered a delicacy in the region.
  • Truffles: Périgord is known for its truffles, which are used in many traditional dishes, including omelettes and pastries.
  • Walnut Cake: Made with locally grown walnuts, this cake is a staple of Périgord dessert cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Bergerac Wine: The Bergerac region produces excellent wines, including the white wines made with the Sauvignon Blanc grape.
  • Vin de Noix: This sweet, nut-flavored wine is made with green walnuts and is a popular aperitif in the region.

Where to Stay in Périgueux: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Le Moulins de la Vézère: This charming guesthouse is situated in an old watermill and features comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Château les Merles: This elegant hotel is located in the countryside just outside of Périgueux and combines traditional French architecture with modern amenities.
  • Hotel Restaurant L’Ecluse: This small hotel is located next to the river and features comfortable rooms and excellent cuisine.

What to Buy in Périgueux: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Foie Gras: This local delicacy is a must-buy for foodies visiting Périgueux.
  • Truffles: Fresh or preserved truffles are a great souvenir to bring home from Périgueux.
  • Walnut Oil: Made with locally grown walnuts, this oil is delicious and healthy, and a staple of Périgord cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Périgueux

The Périgord region is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its traditional cuisine, crafts, music, and dance. Visitors to Périgueux should respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes when visiting sacred sites or attending cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Périgueux

The best time to visit Périgueux is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are many cultural events and festivals to attend. However, a visit in the fall or spring can also be rewarding, with fewer crowds and the changing colors of the landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Périgueux

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try the traditional dishes and wines of the region.
  • Visit the historical landmarks and museums.
  • Take part in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local culture and customs.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting sacred sites.
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol.

Safety and Health

Safety in Périgueux

Périgueux is a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard precautions to protect their belongings and themselves from pickpockets and other petty criminals.

Health in Périgueux

Périgueux has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where travelers can seek medical care if needed. It’s also recommended that visitors pack and carry any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Périgueux: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Périgueux

The nearest airports to Périgueux are the Bergerac-Roumanière Airport (EGC) and the Brive–Souillac Airport (BVE), which offer domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Périgueux

Périgueux has a bus station with connections to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Périgueux by train, with the Périgueux railway station connecting with major cities in France and the rest of Europe.

How to Get Around in Périgueux

Périgueux is a compact town that is easily navigated on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and taxis, to get around town and explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Périgueux: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Périgueux is a great starting point for exploring the beautiful countryside and historic sites of the Périgord region, including:

  • Sarlat-la-Canéda
  • Bergerac
  • Les Eyzies-de-Tayac-Sireuil
  • Rocamadour

Conclusion

Périgueux is a rich cultural and natural oasis located in the heart of the Périgord region of southwestern France. With its stunning historical landmarks, beautiful landscapes, and delicious cuisine, it’s a destination worth visiting for any traveler looking to experience the very best of French culture and natural beauty.
