Introduction to Dordogne

Dordogne is a gorgeous department located in the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region of southwestern France. It is a perfect destination for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts alike. Moreover, Dordogne is known for having some of the most well-preserved historical sites in France, mainly the prehistoric caves adorned with wall drawings. This guide will showcase all of the things you need to know to make the most of your time in Dordogne.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dordogne

Dordogne has some of France’s most stunning countryside routes and is a great place for bike or hiking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most preferred routes for hikers and bikers alike:

  • Sarlat - Beynac-et-Cazenac (21 km)
  • Les Eyzies - Sarlat (35 km)
  • Saint-Cyprien - Domme (29 km)
  • Eymet - Monpazier (46 km)
  • Monpazier - Cadouin (34 km)

Dordogne is packed with thrilling activities, especially outdoor adventures. During your visit, don’t forget to try these activities and tours:

  • Caving.
  • Hot air ballooning.
  • Taste Périgord Black Truffle.
  • Join on a wine tasting tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dordogne

If you are planning a week-long trip to Dordogne, here is an itinerary that you can follow to cover all of the popular attractions:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Sarlat-la-Canéda and spend the day exploring the town.
  • Day 2: Visit Lascaux Caves and then head to Saint Léon Sur Vézère for picturesque surroundings.
  • Day 3: Spend the day in the Dordogne valley, traveling along the numerous castles, and visiting the beautiful valley of Beynac-et-Cazenac.
  • Day 4: Go for an excursion to the stunning medieval village of Domme.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the lovely riverside village of La Roque-Gageac.
  • Day 6: Go on a hot air balloon ride to see Dordogne from above.
  • Day 7: Take a walk through the Jardins de Marqueyssac. Before leaving, stop by Sarlat-la-Canéda for souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Dordogne

Dordogne has a deep-rooted history and culture that resonates in every corner of the department. Here are a few customs and traditions that you are likely to encounter during your visit:

  • Dordogne is famous for its ancient stone architecture, primarily castles and chateaux.
  • Traditional craft skills are still widely practiced, especially lacemaking and woodcarving.
  • Hunting is still very popular in Dordogne.
  • Sunday is still considered a day of rest, and most shops and restaurants are shut
  • Start conversations with “bonjour” and end them with “au revoir” or “merci.”

Best Time to Visit Dordogne

The best time to visit Dordogne for ideal weather conditions is from early June to September. August is a peak tourist time, and Dordogne is understandably busy with tourists at this time. The autumn season, especially October and November, is a great time to visit because of the spectacular autumn foliage and ample local delicacies.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dordogne

Do:

  • Sample local delicacies such as foie gras and truffles.
  • Practice basic French greeting etiquette.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for hikes and long walks.
  • Carry cash, just in case.

Don’t:

  • Forget to visit the prehistoric caves adorned with wall drawings.
  • Disrespect any religious beliefs.
  • Assume everyone speaks English.
  • Travel without travel insurance, especially when going hiking and biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dordogne

Dordogne is a safe place for tourists, and the people are friendly and welcoming. However, it is crucial to remain cautious of your surroundings, especially in busy tourist areas, to avoid petty theft.

Health in Dordogne

Dordogne has a high-quality healthcare system, and it is advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.
