Introduction to Eymet

Eymet is a charming medieval town nestled in the heart of Dordogne, in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France. With its cobbled streets, narrow alleys, and half-timbered houses, Eymet exudes old-world charm and is a perfect destination for travelers looking to experience the authentic French countryside. The town offers a range of attractions suitable for families, couples, solo adventurers, and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Eymet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Château de Bridoire - This beautiful 15th-century castle is an iconic landmark of Eymet and houses a museum dedicated to the history of the region.

  2. Moulin de Porchères - A well-preserved windmill that offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside.

  3. Église Notre-Dame d’Eymet - A stunning Gothic church with beautiful stained-glass windows and intricate carvings that date back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Eymet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Canal du Midi - A picturesque waterway that winds its way through Eymet, offering stunning views and excellent opportunities for cycling and hiking.

  2. Parc Naturel Régional des Cévennes - A magnificent national park that offers an array of hiking trails, mountain biking routes, and spectacular natural landscapes.

  3. Lac de Laouzas - A serene lake that offers great water sports activities like swimming, sailing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eymet

  1. The Canal du Midi Bike Trail - Follow the scenic Canal du Midi on your bike for a relaxing and scenic ride.

  2. The GR7 Hiking Trail - A breathtaking trek that will take you through the heart of the Parc Naturel Régional des Cévennes.

  3. The Clamouse Trail - Explore the stunning caves and rock formations of this trail, as you hike through the picturesque town of Clamouse.

Best Things to Do in Eymet: Activities and Tours

  1. Go Wine-Tasting - Eymet is known for its famous wines, so take a tour of the vineyards and sample some of the region’s finest vintages.

  2. Visit a Market - The town holds a weekly market every Thursday morning, where you can buy fresh produce, handicrafts, and souvenirs.

  3. Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride - Experience the stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside from high above in a hot air balloon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eymet

Food and Drink Scene of Eymet

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cassoulet - A hearty stew made with sausage, duck confit, white beans, and vegetables

  2. Truffles - A unique and prized delicacy that is found in the region’s forests and used in a variety of dishes, such as soups, sauces, and salads

  3. Périgord Lamb - A succulent and flavorful lamb that is raised in the Périgord region

Local Beverages

  1. Bergerac Wine - A rich and fruity wine that is produced in the Bergerac region, just outside of Eymet

  2. Armagnac - A strong brandy that is distilled in the region, with a unique flavor that has a hint of vanilla and dried fruit

Where to Stay in Eymet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. La Maison Forte de Reignac - A luxurious and elegant chateau that offers comfortable and spacious rooms, a pool, and stunning views of the countryside.

  2. Le Clos Castel - A charming and romantic bed and breakfast that is housed in an 18th-century farmhouse, with lovely gardens and a pool.

  3. Les Tilleuls - A cozy and inviting guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast, served in a charming courtyard garden.

What to Buy in Eymet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Perigord Truffles - A unique and prized delicacy that is found in the region’s forests. You can buy it fresh or in the form of truffle oil, truffle honey, and truffle cheese.

  2. Foie Gras - A rich and flavorful liver pate that is a staple of French cuisine.

  3. Local Wines - Bergerac and Bordeaux wines are a must-try when visiting Eymet.

Local Culture and Customs of Eymet

Eymet is a charming and welcoming town that values its traditions and cultural heritage. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and they take great pride in their history and local customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s traditions and customs, such as dressing appropriately when visiting churches and other cultural landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Eymet

The best time to visit Eymet is from May to September when the weather is warm, and the countryside is at its most beautiful. The town also holds several festivals and events during the summer months, such as the Fête des Vins de Bergerac and the Eymet Medieval Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eymet

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and values.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Explore the town’s cultural landmarks and natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting churches or other cultural landmarks.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eymet

Eymet is a safe and peaceful town, with friendly locals and a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take normal safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Eymet

Eymet has a good healthcare system, with several clinics and hospitals located in and around the town. Visitors are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance before traveling to Eymet.

Getting to Eymet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Eymet by several means of transport.

Airports Near Eymet

  1. Bergerac Dordogne Périgord Airport - 30km from Eymet
  2. Bordeaux-Mérignac Airport - 140km from Eymet

Bus Stations Near Eymet

  1. Bordeaux - The bus service from here takes around 2 hours and 30 minutes to reach Eymet.
  2. Bergerac - Bus service from here takes around 1 hour to reach Eymet.

Other Options

  1. Train - Train service is available between Bordeaux and Bergerac. Visitors can then take a bus service to reach Eymet.

How to Get Around in Eymet

Eymet is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles from several rental services to tour around the town and explore the beautiful surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Eymet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Sarlat-la-Canéda - A stunning medieval town with ancient architecture and cultural landmarks that offer a glimpse into France’s rich history and heritage

  2. Lascaux Caves - A prehistoric cave complex with ancient paintings that showcase early human life and art.

  3. Saint-Émilion - A charming and picturesque village that is famous for its world-renowned wines and stunning countryside.

Conclusion

Eymet is a charming and picturesque town that offers a wealth of attractions and experiences for visitors. From its historic landmarks and natural wonders to its excellent food and wine scene, Eymet has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Eymet now and get ready to discover France’s best-kept secret.
