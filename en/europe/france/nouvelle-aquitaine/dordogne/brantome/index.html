Introduction to Brantôme

Nestled in the heart of Dordogne, in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France, lies the picturesque small town of Brantôme. Situated on the banks of the Dronne River, Brantôme is surrounded by lush forests and green hills, making it a haven for nature lovers. The town’s rich history and culture have been well preserved, with ancient buildings and monuments still standing tall. Brantôme’s cobbled streets, quaint cafes, and lively markets all contribute to its idyllic charm. This travel guide will offer you a comprehensive tour of everything Brantôme has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Brantôme

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or an art lover, then Brantôme has plenty of cultural landmarks to explore.

  • The Brantôme Abbey is a prominent structure and a testament to 8th-century architecture. The abbey’s grounds house over six centuries of religious history and artifacts. The abbey is open to visitors for guided tours, and you can also take part in the mass services.

  • Musée Fernand Desmoulin is a museum situated in the heart of Brantôme that showcases the works of local artist Fernand Desmoulin, who lived and worked in Brantôme. The museum has an extensive collection of sculptures, painting, and ceramics by the artist that art enthusiasts can enjoy.

  • The Château de Bourdeilles is a magnificent castle and a testament to medieval architecture. The castle houses a collection of furniture, paintings, and other artifacts that date back to the 15th century. You can take a guided tour of the castle’s interior and garden, which offer picturesque views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Brantôme: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brantôme’s natural attractions offer visitors an opportunity to indulge in various outdoor activities, including hiking, canoeing, and swimming.

  • The Grotte de Villars is a series of underground caves situated in the hills surrounding Brantôme. The caves contain well-preserved rock formations and paintings that date back to prehistoric times. You can take a guided tour of the caves and marvel at the natural beauty of the rock formations.

  • The Parc Naturel Régional Périgord Limousin is a nature reserve that is home to various species of flora and fauna. Visitors can hike, bike, or even horse ride through the park’s trails, which offer scenic views of the lush forests and hillsides.

  • The Dronne River is a popular spot for swimming, kayaking, and canoeing. The river flows through Brantôme, and visitors can rent kayaks or canoes to explore the river and its surroundings at their leisure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brantôme

Brantôme is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore.

  • The Voie Verte des Berges de L’Isle is a 27km trail that runs along the Isle River and connects several nearby towns. The trail is perfect for hiking or cycling, and visitors can take their time to explore the scenic landscapes and picturesque towns along the way.

  • The Sentier de St Jacques de Compostelle is a 2,500km long-distance hiking trail that passes through Brantôme. Visitors can take a day hike or undertake a longer adventure along this historic trail.

Best Things to Do in Brantôme: Activities and Tours

From cultural landmarks to outdoor activities, there’s no shortage of things to do in Brantôme.

  • Take a boat ride along the Dronne River and marvel at the scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests, an experience you’ll never forget.

  • Visit La Meridienne, a local antique store that sells a vast collection of antique French furnishings, paintings, and ornaments. It’s a fascinating shopping experience, and you’ll pick up something that truly reflects your love of France.

  • Enjoy a wine tasting experience at one of the many local vineyards and sample some of the finest wines of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Brantôme

Brantôme’s food scene is a proud reflection of Nouvelle-Aquitaine’s culinary culture. The town is famous for its exceptional food and fine wine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Foie Gras is a regional delicacy and one of the dishes that best highlights Brantome’s culinary culture. It’s made from duck or goose liver, and it’s served with toast or crackers.

  • Dordogne lamb is tender and succulent meat that is a staple of French cuisine. The lamb is usually roasted and served with roasted vegetables.

  • Truffle dishes are also popular in Brantôme, with dishes like Truffle Omelette and Truffle Risotto being among the most prized.

Local Beverages

  • Bergerac wine is a locally produced wine that is a must-try when visiting Brantôme. The wine is renowned for its fruity flavors and crisp taste.

  • Pineau des Charentes is a regional aperitif that is popular with locals. The drink is a blend of cognac and grape juice, giving it a sweet and fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Brantôme: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brantôme has a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Moulin de Vigonac is a luxury hotel situated in a 16th-century mill, overlooking the Dronne River. The hotel offers beautifully appointed rooms with balconies that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Les Residences du Parc is a budget-friendly option, offering comfortable guest rooms with modern amenities like Wi-Fi and air conditioning. The guesthouse is located close to the town center, allowing for easy access to local attractions and restaurants.

  • Hotel Restaurant Charbonnel is a mid-range option that offers comfortable rooms with en suite bathrooms and a restaurant that serves delicious French cuisine. The hotel is located in the heart of Brantôme, making it an ideal base to explore the town.

What to Buy in Brantôme: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home from Brantôme, including:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics are a popular purchase in Brantôme, with many local artists creating unique pieces.

  • Foie Gras is a popular local food item that can be vacuum-sealed for easy transport.

  • Walnut oil is another popular local product that is used in many local dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Brantôme

Brantôme values its history and its culture, and visitors should always respect local customs and traditions while they are in the town. French is the official language, and it’s always polite to learn a few simple phrases before visiting.

Best Time to Visit Brantôme

The best time to visit Brantôme is from April to October, when the weather is mild, and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brantôme

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take part in local festivals and celebrations.

  • Try regional specialties like foie gras and wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the town’s historic landmarks.

  • Wear beachwear or revealing clothing in public places.

  • Take photographs of locals without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brantôme

Brantôme is a safe town for tourists, but visitors should always take precautions to protect themselves and their valuables.

Health in Brantôme

Visitors should take care to drink bottled water and be sure to wash fruits and vegetables well. It’s always advisable to have travel health insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Brantôme: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brantôme is accessible via various modes of transport, including:

Airports Near Brantôme

The nearest airports to Brantôme are:

  • Bergerac Dordogne Périgord Airport (62km)

  • Bordeaux–Mérignac Airport (132km)

Bus Stations Near Brantôme

  • Brantôme Bus Station is the only bus station in the town, and it can be reached via various bus routes that connect the town to the surrounding regions.

Other Options

  • The town is also well connected via the rail network, with trains to Paris, Bordeaux, Lille, and other major cities in France.

How to Get Around in Brantôme

Walking or cycling is the best way to explore Brantôme, as it is a relatively small town. Taxis are also available for those who prefer not to get around on foot or by bike.

Conclusion
