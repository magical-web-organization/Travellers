Introduction to Monpazier

Nestled in the heart of Dordogne, Nouvelle-Aquitaine, Monpazier is a charming medieval village that exudes timeless beauty and old-world allure. The bastide town was established in 1284 by King Edward I of England and has remained largely unchanged over the centuries. With its cobblestone streets, half-timbered houses, and picturesque squares, Monpazier is a destination that will transport you back in time.

List of Places to Visit in Monpazier

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Monpazier is steeped in history and culture, which can be seen in the numerous museums and historical sites scattered throughout the village. Here are some of the must-sees:

  • Musee du Chateau: This museum gives visitors an insight into the region’s history with a particular focus on the Hundred Years War.

  • Eglise Notre Dame de l’Assomption: This 17th-century church is a magnificent example of the French Baroque period.

  • La Place des Cornieres: This impressive square is the iconic heart of Monpazier. It is surrounded by beautiful 14th-century half-timbered houses with covered arcades, housing cafes, restaurants, and artisan shops.

  • Monpazier Market: Held on Thursdays, the market is an excellent occasion to immerse yourself in the local culture, taste the regional cuisine, browse handicrafts and shop for souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Monpazier: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts, Monpazier offers a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic vistas.

  • The Gavaudun Valley: Located just a 20-minute drive from Monpazier, the Gavaudun Valley is surrounded by unspoiled nature, elegant castles, and enchanting villages. It is an ideal spot for hiking, rock climbing, and kayaking.

  • Biron Castle: The Biron Castle is located on a hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. It is one of the most notable medieval castles in France and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history.

  • The Dordogne River: The Dordogne River is only a short drive away and can be explored by boat or kayak. The river is surrounded by spectacular views of the countryside, providing a peaceful and scenic escape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monpazier

Monpazier is surrounded by a wide range of hiking and biking trails, offering unforgettable scenic experiences.

Some popular routes include:

  • The Monpazier to Monflanquin Trail: This 24 km trail will take you through scenic countryside, past traditional farms and chateaux, before ending at the stunning village of Monflanquin.

  • The Monpazier to La Roque-Gageac Trail: This 30 km trail takes you through the charming villages of the Dordogne Valley, with opportunities to stop and explore many attractions along the way.

  • The Monpazier to Biron Castle Trail: This 18 km trail leads you on a journey of stunning landscapes and picturesque villages before arriving at the breath-taking Biron Castle.

Best Things to Do in Monpazier: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of exciting activities and tours to participate in while visiting Monpazier.

  • Private Village Tours: Explore Monpazier’s most famous landmarks and off-the-beaten-track locations with a local guide.

  • Horseback Riding: Ride through the scenic countryside and soak in Dordogne’s natural beauty with a guided horseback ride.

  • Hot Air Balloon Tours: Experience Monpazier from a unique perspective with a hot air balloon tour. Admire stunning views of the region while flying high above it all.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: The Dordogne region is famous for its wine, and there are numerous vineyards and wineries in the area where you can taste some of the region’s finest vintages.

Food and Drink Scene of Monpazier

One of the highlights of any trip to Monpazier is the chance to try some of the local cuisine and wine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Confits de Canard: Slow-cooked duck legs that are flavorful and tender.

  • Truffles: Known as “black diamonds,” these delicious mushrooms add a unique flavor to any dish.

  • Foie Gras: A French delicacy made from duck or goose liver, typically served with crusty bread and chutney.

  • Cepes: A type of mushroom that is often served in rich sauces, perfect for indulging in a hearty meal.

Local Beverages

  • Bergerac Wine: The Bergerac region is just a short drive from Monpazier, and is known for its excellent vineyards and produces some of the best wine in France.

  • Armagnac: A type of brandy, made in the Gascony region surrounding Monpazier. It is often served as an after-dinner drink.

  • Monbazillac Wine: This sweet wine is made exclusively from grapes grown near Monbazillac, a small village located just a few miles from Monpazier.

Where to Stay in Monpazier: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a wide range of accommodation options available in Monpazier to fit any budget or preference.

Some of the top choices include:

  • Le Belvedere Hotel: This luxury hotel offers charming rooms with beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Le Lion D’Or: This historic hotel dates back to the 16th century and offers a unique and charming experience to its guests.

  • Auberge de l’École: This cozy guesthouse offers a traditional French experience with its charming rooms and excellent restaurant.

What to Buy in Monpazier: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Monpazier, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home with you.

Some ideas include:

  • Foie Gras: A local delicacy that is perfect for bringing a taste of Monpazier home with you.

  • Wine: The Dordogne region is famous for its wine, so bringing a bottle or two home is a great way to remember your trip.

  • Handicrafts: Monpazier is home to many artisans producing high-quality pottery, leather goods, and other handicrafts that make for unique and authentic souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Monpazier

When visiting Monpazier, it’s important to respect the local customs and culture. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting churches or other places of worship.

  • Tipping is not expected in France but is always appreciated for good service.

Best Time to Visit Monpazier

The best time to visit Monpazier is during the spring and summer months, from May to September. The weather is mild, and the outdoor attractions are at their best during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monpazier

Do:

  • Do try some of the local cuisine and wine, like Foie Gras and Bergerac Wine.

  • Do explore the Gavaudun Valley and nearby towns like Monflanquin, Sarlat, and Rocamadour.

  • Do take a guided tour to make the most of your trip.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or other places of worship.

  • Don’t forget sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monpazier

Monpazier is a safe destination, and crime rates are very low. However, visitors can still take precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Keep your valuables in a secure place, like a hotel safe.

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended in public places.

Health in Monpazier

France has a universal healthcare system, so visitors can access medical care if needed. It’s also a good idea to pack any medication you may need, as well as insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Monpazier: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monpazier

The nearest airport to Monpazier is Bergerac Dordogne Périgord Airport, which is about 40 km away.

Other airports within driving distance include:

  • Bordeaux-Mérignac Airport

  • Toulouse-Blagnac Airport

Bus Stations Near Monpazier

There is no direct bus service to Monpazier, but travelers can take a bus to the nearby town of Bergerac, which is only a short drive away.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get to Monpazier and explore the area.

How to Get Around in Monpazier

Monpazier is best explored on foot. The village is small and pedestrian-friendly, making it easy to stroll around and take in the sights.

Where to Go Next from Monpazier: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting nearby destinations from Monpazier worth exploring. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Sarlat-la-Canéda: An hour’s drive to the south, Sarlat-la-Canéda is a charming town that is known for its historic architecture, gourmet cuisine, and bustling markets.

  • Rocamadour: A two-hour drive to the east, Rocamadour is a stunning clifftop village that is one of France’s most visited sites. It is famous for its religious significance, as well as its stunning scenic views.

Conclusion

With its unique blend of natural beauty, historic architecture, and delicious cuisine, Monpazier is a destination that has something for everybody. Whether you’re hiking the scenic trails of the Dordogne Valley or savoring the local delicacies of the region, Monpazier is a town that will leave you with lasting memories.
