Welcome to Beynac-et-Cazenac, a village with a rich history located in the beautiful region of Dordogne, in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France. Beynac-et-Cazenac sits on the banks of the Dordogne river and is renowned for its stunning panoramic views, beautiful architecture, and unique culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical places, outdoor adventures, or indulging in local food and drink, this travel guide has everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Beynac-et-Cazenac.

Introduction to Beynac-et-Cazenac

Beynac-et-Cazenac is a village of about 550 residents and is named after its two castles, the Château de Beynac and the Château de Cazenac. Towering above the village, the Château de Beynac is a historical monument that has been classified as one of France’s most beautiful castles. The village also boasts an 11th-century Romanesque church, a stunning medieval bridge, and charming narrow streets lined with artisanal shops and cafés.
