Introduction to Bordeaux

Nestled in the heart of Gironde, in the region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine, Bordeaux is one of the most picturesque cities in France. Known for its magnificent architecture, lush vineyards, and rich history, Bordeaux is an ideal destination for travelers looking to immerse themselves in French culture.

Whether you’re interested in discovering the city’s world-renowned museums and historical landmarks, exploring its stunning natural scenery, or indulging in its famous wine and food scene, Bordeaux has something for everyone.

Read on to discover all the best places to visit, eat, and stay in Bordeaux, as well as insider tips on local culture, customs, safety, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Bordeaux

Bordeaux boasts a variety of tourist attractions to suit every interest, from culturally significant museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bordeaux is home to some of the most prestigious museums and historical sites in France, including:

  • The Musée d’Aquitaine
  • The Museum of Fine Arts
  • The CAPC Museum of Contemporary Art
  • The Cité du Vin wine museum
  • The Saint-André Cathedral
  • The Place de la Bourse
  • The Grosse Cloche bell tower

These landmarks provide an excellent opportunity for visitors to immerse themselves in French art, history, and culture.

Natural Attractions of Bordeaux: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for a more outdoorsy experience, Bordeaux has plenty to offer. Some of the most popular outdoor attractions in Bordeaux include:

  • The Jardin Public park
  • The Parc Bordelais
  • The Botanical Garden
  • The Garonne River
  • The Saint-Emilion vineyards

From leisurely walks to adventurous hikes and bike rides, you’re sure to find something to keep you entertained and active in Bordeaux.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bordeaux

For adventurous travelers interested in exploring Bordeaux’s stunning landscapes on foot or by bike, there are several popular routes to choose from. These include:

  • The Saint-Jacques-de-Compostelle hiking trail
  • The Roger Lapébie bike path
  • The Canal des Deux Mers bike route

With varying levels of difficulty and distance, these routes are a great way to explore the natural scenery and take in the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Bordeaux: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many tourist attractions, Bordeaux offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors looking for unique experiences. Some popular activities and tours include:

  • Wine tasting tours
  • Cooking classes
  • River cruises
  • Street art tours
  • Segway tours

These activities provide a fun and immersive opportunity to discover Bordeaux and its surrounding areas from different perspectives.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bordeaux

To help you plan your trip to Bordeaux, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Bordeaux and explore the city’s historic center

Day 2: Visit the museums and galleries of Bordeaux

Day 3: Take a wine tasting tour in Saint-Emilion

Day 4: Explore the Garonne River by boat

Day 5: Go on a bike ride along the Canal des Deux Mers

Day 6: Take a day trip to the Dune of Pilat

Day 7: Visit the local markets and stock up on souvenirs before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Bordeaux

Bordeaux is renowned for its delicious cuisine and world-class wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Entrecote Bordelaise - a juicy steak with shallot and red wine sauce
  • Canelé - a small, crispy cake with a soft, custard-like interior
  • Foie gras - a rich, buttery liver pate

Local Beverages

  • Red wine, particularly Bordeaux blends
  • White wine, such as Sauvignon Blanc and Semillon
  • Armagnac - a brandy produced in southwestern France
  • Cognac - a high-quality brandy made from white grapes

Where to Stay in Bordeaux: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bordeaux offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxury hotels to affordable hostels and cozy guesthouses, there’s something for everyone.

Some popular options include:

  • The InterContinental Bordeaux Le Grand Hotel
  • The Mama Shelter Bordeaux hotel
  • The Ibis Bordeaux Centre Gare Saint-Jean hotel
  • The Fronval guesthouse

What to Buy in Bordeaux: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home a piece of Bordeaux with you, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Some popular options include:

  • Bordeaux wine and spirits
  • Cannelés and other traditional French pastries
  • French cheeses and gourmet food products
  • Handmade crafts and accessories

Local Culture and Customs of Bordeaux

When visiting Bordeaux, it’s important to be aware of local customs and culture. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • The French are known for their formality and reserve, so it’s important to be polite and respectful at all times
  • French greetings typically involve a kiss on each cheek, so be prepared to embrace this custom when meeting new people
  • The French take their food and wine very seriously, so it’s important to savor and enjoy your meals thoroughly

Best Time to Visit Bordeaux

The best time to visit Bordeaux depends on your preferences and travel goals.

Spring and autumn are generally ideal, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer is the peak season, with warm temperatures and plenty of events and festivals to enjoy, but also more tourists.

Winter is the low season, but still enjoyable with fewer visitors and seasonal markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bordeaux

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bordeaux:

Do:

  • Try local wine and cuisine
  • Be courteous and polite to locals
  • Be mindful of local customs
  • Dress nicely and appropriately, especially when dining out

Don’t:

  • Spit out wine during tastings
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public
  • Wear excessively revealing or casual clothing
  • Assume that everyone speaks English

Safety and Health

Bordeaux is a relatively safe city, but it’s important to take standard safety precautions such as using common sense, keeping valuables secure, and being aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Bordeaux

  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas
  • Use caution when crossing the street, as traffic can be heavy

Health in Bordeaux

  • Make sure your routine vaccinations are up-to-date
  • Take precautions to avoid tick bites in wooded areas, as Lyme disease is present

Getting to Bordeaux: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bordeaux is easily accessible by multiple transportation options.

Airports Near Bordeaux

  • Bordeaux-Mérignac Airport
  • Bergerac Dordogne Périgord Airport

Bus Stations Near Bordeaux

  • Bordeaux Bus Station
  • Saint-Jean Train Station

Other Options

  • Train services (TGV, Intercités)
  • Car rental or rideshare services

How to Get Around in Bordeaux

Bordeaux boasts an efficient public transportation network, including a bus and tramway system. Additionally, bike rentals and taxis are readily available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Bordeaux: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering as an extension to your Bordeaux itinerary. Some popular options include:

  • The Arcachon Bay
  • The Dune of Pilat
  • The city of Toulouse
  • The Pyrenees mountains

Conclusion

Bordeaux is an incredible city that offers a rich cultural experience for travelers. The above guide will give you the best insight into the must-sees and top things to do to ensure you make the most of your trip. Embrace your inner adventurer and indulge in wine and food to have a grand trip to Bordeaux.
