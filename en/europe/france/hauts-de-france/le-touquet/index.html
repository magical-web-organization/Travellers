Introduction to Le Touquet

Located in Hauts-de-France, Le Touquet is a charming coastal town in France that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and modern luxury. Visitors can experience the sparkling sea, golden sand beaches, dense pine forests, and quaint shops and restaurants.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Le Touquet. Whether you’re planning a week-long vacation or just a weekend getaway, this guide will help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Le Touquet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Museum of Le Touquet: This museum houses a vast collection of art and artefacts that reflect the town’s history and culture. Visitors can learn about the town’s development as a holiday destination and its rich artistic heritage.

  2. The Notre-Dame-de-la-Mer Church: This striking church in the heart of Le Touquet features a unique mix of Gothic and Art Deco architecture. Visitors can admire the intricate stained-glass windows and the beautiful gardens that surround the church.

Natural Attractions of Le Touquet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Le Touquet Beach: The long sandy beach is one of the town’s most popular attractions. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports on the crystal-clear waters.

  2. The Aqualud Waterpark: This indoor waterpark features a range of aquatic attractions, including slides, wave pools, and lazy rivers. It’s an excellent place for families and kids of all ages to have fun and cool off on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Le Touquet

  1. The Cliffs and Dunes Nature Reserve: This nature reserve covers over 130-hectares of sand dunes, cliffs, and wooded areas. Visitors can follow the walking and cycling trails through the reserve and admire the pristine landscapes.

  2. The Forest of Le Touquet: This dense pine forest is a perfect place for hiking and cycling. It covers an area of over 8,000 acres and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Le Touquet: Activities and Tours

  1. The Sand Yachting: Visitors can try their hand at sand yachting and experience the thrill of racing across the sand dunes on a windy day. It’s an exciting and unique adventure suitable for all ages.

  2. The Le Touquet Golf Course: This internationally-renowned golf course is a must-visit for golf lovers. It offers stunning views of the sea, lush greens, and challenging holes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Le Touquet

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town centre, enjoy local cuisine at one of the local restaurants, and visit the Notre-Dame-de-la-Mer Church.
Day 2 Spend the day on the beach and take a dip in the clear blue waters.
Day 3 Visit the Le Touquet’s Museum and learn about the town’s rich culture and history.
Day 4 Hike or bike through the Cliffs and Dunes Nature Reserve.
Day 5 Take a day trip to nearby towns, such as Boulogne-sur-Mer or Abbeville.
Day 6 Try your hand at sand yachting or visit the Aqualud Waterpark.
Day 7 Play a round of golf at the Le Touquet Golf Course, then relax at a local spa or wellness centre.

Food and Drink Scene of Le Touquet

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Coquilles Saint-Jacques: This classic French dish features succulent scallops cooked in butter and white wine.

  2. Moules Frites: A delicious dish of mussels and chips, which is a staple in the French cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Local Beer: The north of France is famous for its beer. Visitors can try a range of locally brewed craft beers, including the Le Touquet Blonde and the Ch’ti.

  2. Cider: This refreshing drink is made from locally grown apples and is an excellent accompaniment to a meal or enjoyed on its own.

Where to Stay in Le Touquet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Le Touquet can choose from a range of accommodation options to suit their budget and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  1. Hotel Barriere Le Westminster: A luxurious hotel that offers a great location, elegant rooms, and excellent service.

  2. The Jules Boutique Hotel: A quirky and creative hotel that offers chic rooms and an excellent location.

What to Buy in Le Touquet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. La Creme de Caramel: This creamy caramel sauce is made from local ingredients and makes a perfect accompaniment to desserts or a gift for family and friends.

  2. Chocolate from L’Atelier du Chocolat: This artisanal chocolate shop offers a range of delicious chocolates made from high-quality ingredients, including the famous macarons.

Local Culture and Customs of Le Touquet

Le Touquet is renowned for its artistic heritage and cultural attractions. Visitors can explore the town’s history, art, and architecture through its museums, galleries, and historic buildings.

Best Time to Visit Le Touquet

The best time to visit Le Touquet is from late spring to early autumn, when the weather is warm but not too hot, and the tourist crowds are smaller.

Getting to Le Touquet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Le Touquet

The nearest airport to Le Touquet is the Lille Airport, which is approximately 100 kilometres away.

Bus Stations Near Le Touquet

There are several local and regional bus services that connect Le Touquet to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train or rent a car to get to Le Touquet.

How to Get Around in Le Touquet

Visitors can get around Le Touquet by bike, on foot, or by public transport. Bicycle rentals are available at various locations, and the town’s compact size makes it easy to explore on foot.

Where to Go Next from Le Touquet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Le Touquet can take day trips to nearby cities and towns, including:

  1. Boulogne-sur-Mer: A charming coastal town with a beautiful harbour and historic buildings.

  2. Abbeville: A historic town famous for its art and architecture.

Conclusion

Le Touquet is a perfect destination for visitors who want to combine natural beauty with cultural attractions. Visitors can explore the town’s history, enjoy delicious local cuisine, and relax on the beautiful beaches.
