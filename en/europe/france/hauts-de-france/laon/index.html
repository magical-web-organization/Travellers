Introduction to Laon

Located in the Hauts-de-France region of France, Laon is a medieval town known for its stunning Gothic architecture, picturesque hills, and historical significance. Known for its turbulent past and strategic location, Laon is a popular tourist destination for history buffs, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers.

Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, partake in outdoor activities, or indulge in a culinary journey, Laon has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and other helpful tips to plan your trip to Laon.

List of Places to Visit in Laon

Laon is a treasure trove of beautiful sights and unique experiences. From medieval fortifications to stunning natural vistas, here are the top places to visit in Laon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Laon Cathedral: This stunning masterpiece of Gothic architecture is one of the top must-see attractions in Laon. Built in the 12th century, the cathedral offers a glimpse into the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  2. Notre-Dame Abbey: Founded in the 9th century, the abbey is a striking example of Romanesque architecture in France. It is also home to a museum that showcases art and religious artifacts.
  3. Porte d’Ardon: This imposing gate was built in the 14th century and is one of the only surviving ones out of the four original gates that led into the town. It is a symbol of Laon’s past as a fortified city.

Natural Attractions of Laon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Plateau de la Justice: This beautiful plateau offers stunning panoramic views of the town and its surroundings. It is a great spot for a hike or a picnic.
  2. Laon Forest: Covering over 900 hectares of land, the forest is home to several hiking trails, fishing spots, and camping sites. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
  3. Parc des Oiseaux et des Animaux: This animal park is located within the Laon Forest and is home to a variety of birds and animals, including deer, foxes, and peacocks. It is a great place to take the kids for a fun day out.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laon

Laon and its surroundings offer a plethora of hiking and biking opportunities for both beginners and experienced enthusiasts. Here are some top trails to explore:

  1. La Trans’Oise: This 100-kilometer trail starts in Compiègne and ends in Laon, passing through some of the most stunning countryside in northern France.
  2. Laon to Coucy-le-Château: This 16-kilometer trail takes you through tranquil forests, rolling hills, and quaint villages.
  3. Le Chemin des Dames: This historic walking trail spans over 30 kilometers and takes you through some of the most significant battle sites of World War I.

Best Things to Do in Laon: Activities and Tours

Laon is brimming with activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Whether you want to learn about the town’s history, immerse yourself in French culture or explore its natural beauty, there is something for everyone. Here are the best things to do in Laon:

  1. Take a guided tour of Laon Cathedral and Notre-Dame Abbey
  2. Explore the town’s medieval ramparts and gates
  3. Sample local cuisine and wine on a food and wine tour
  4. Enjoy a hot-air balloon ride over the town
  5. Attend the Fête Médiévale de Laon, a medieval festival that takes place every year in June.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laon

If you’re planning to spend a week in Laon, here is an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Laon’s medieval town, visit the cathedral, and take a guided tour of the town’s ramparts.
Day 2 Take a scenic hike or bike ride in Laon Forest or visit the Parc des Oiseaux et des Animaux
Day 3 Visit the nearby town of Soissons, known for its Abbey of St. Jean des Vignes and Romanesque architecture.
Day 4 Take a wine-tasting tour of the local vineyards and sample some of the finest wines in the region.
Day 5 Visit some of the World War I battle sites along the Chemin des Dames trail.
Day 6 Enjoy a hot-air balloon ride over the town and the surrounding countryside.
Day 7 Attend one of the town’s annual festivals, such as the Fête Médiévale de Laon.

Food and Drink Scene of Laon

If you’re a foodie at heart, Laon won’t disappoint with its variety of local cuisines and wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Le Pot’je Vleesch: This hearty meat dish is made with several types of meat, including beef, pork, and chicken, all slowly cooked in beer and served with fried potatoes.
  2. Maroilles cheese: A pungent cheese that is made in the town of Maroilles, near Laon. It is a staple of French cuisine and is often paired with bread or wine.
  3. Ficelle Picarde: A delicious and classic French dish made with ham and mushrooms rolled in a crepe, baked with a cream sauce and grated cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Champagne: Laon is located in the Champagne region of France, known for its sparkling wine production. Head to one of the local wineries, such as the Champagne Pannier, to sample some of the finest champagne in the region.
  2. Bière de noël: A seasonal Christmas beer that is brewed in Laon and is only available during the holiday season.
  3. Liqueurs: The region around Laon is known for its production of liqueurs, such as Cointreau and Grand Marnier. Visit one of the local distilleries, such as the Distillerie de la Limonaderie, to taste some of the finest French liqueurs.

Where to Stay in Laon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Laon offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a cozy guesthouse, you’ll find it here. Some of the top options include:

  1. Hotel de la Banniere de France: Located in the heart of Laon’s historic district, this 17th-century hotel offers elegant rooms, a spa, and a restaurant that serves regional cuisine.
  2. Hotel du Golf de l’Ailette: A modern hotel located just outside of Laon, overlooking the scenic Lac de l’Ailette. It offers a range of facilities including a swimming pool, a golf course, and a Spa.
  3. Le Refuge: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Laon’s old town, offering cozy rooms, a shared kitchen, and a terrace with views of the cathedral.

What to Buy in Laon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Laon is famous for its traditional crafts and locally made products. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  1. Pottery: Laon has a rich tradition of pottery making. Visit one of the local workshops, such as Poterie de la Houssiere, to see the artisans at work and purchase some unique French ceramics.
  2. Maroilles Cheese: Laon is located near the town of Maroilles, which is famous for its production of the pungent cheese. Stock up on some cheese from one of the local shops or markets.
  3. Champagne: The town is located in the heart of the Champagne region and is known for its production of the sparkling wine. Visit one of the local wineries for a bottle of the finest bubbly in France.

Local Culture and Customs of Laon

Laon has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in its history. The town is proud of its traditions, and visitors are encouraged to participate in the many festivals and events that take place throughout the year. Some of the notable celebrations include:

  1. Fête des Remparts: Held every two years in August, this festival commemorates the town’s history and heritage with reenactments, parades, and medieval games.
  2. Fête de la Musique: A music festival that takes place every year in June, featuring live concerts, street performers, and cultural events.
  3. Foire de la Saint Martin: A popular fair that takes place in November, featuring local stalls selling crafts, food, and beverages.

Best Time to Visit Laon

The best time to visit Laon is during the spring and summer months, from May to September when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the town is also beautiful during the autumn and winter months when the crowds are fewer, and the town takes on a more relaxed and peaceful atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laon

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Laon:

Do:

  1. Try to speak French whenever possible. French is the official language of the town, and using a few basic phrases will earn you some respect from the locals.
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites, such as the cathedral and the abbey.
  3. Leave a tip of 10% in restaurants and cafes if you enjoyed your meal.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter. The town takes pride in its pristine environment and littering is strictly prohibited.
  2. Don’t be too loud or rowdy, especially during the night.
  3. Don’t forget to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a religious site.

Safety and Health

Laon is generally a safe and healthy destination for travelers. However, it’s essential to take some basic precautions to ensure a trouble-free trip.

Safety in Laon

  1. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas, such as markets and train stations.
  2. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you.
  3. Use common sense and stay alert when walking alone at night.

Health in Laon

  1. Make sure your routine vaccinations are up to date.
  2. Bring any necessary prescription medication with you.
  3. Purchase travel insurance before your trip to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Laon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Laon is well-connected to major cities in France and Europe. Here are the best transportation options to reach Laon:

Airports Near Laon

  1. Paris-Charles de Gaulle Airport: Located approximately 100 kilometers from Laon, the airport is a major international hub that offers connections to several other destinations.
  2. Brussels Airport: Located approximately 150 kilometers from Laon, the airport offers connections to several other destinations in Europe.

Bus Stations Near Laon

  1. Gare de Laon: The town’s main bus station connects it to several other destinations in the surrounding region.
  2. Gare routière de Compiègne: Located approximately 40 kilometers from Laon, the bus station offers connections to several other destinations in France and Europe.

Other Options

  1. Train: Laon has a train station that connects it to several other major cities in France, including Paris, Lille, and Reims.
  2. Car: If you’re driving, Laon is accessible via the A26 motorway and is approximately two hours from Paris.

How to Get Around in Laon

The best way to explore Laon and its surroundings is on foot. The town’s medieval center is compact and easy to navigate with several marked trails and walking paths. Cycling is also a popular option, and several bike rental companies can provide you with the necessary gear.

Where to Go Next from Laon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other parts of northern France, here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Reims: A beautiful city that is famous for its Champagne production and stunning cathedral.
  2. Compiègne: A historic town that is known for its beautiful castle and parks.
  3. Soissons: A town that is rich in history and culture and is known for its beautiful Abbey of St. Jean des Vignes.

Conclusion

Laon is a town that has something to offer to everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and exciting activities, Laon is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. We hope this guide has given you some inspiration for planning your trip to Laon. Bon voyage!
