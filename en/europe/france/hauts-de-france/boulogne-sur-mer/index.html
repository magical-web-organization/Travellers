Introduction to Boulogne-sur-Mer

Boulogne-sur-Mer is a charming port city located in Hauts-de-France, in the north of France. The city boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a thriving culinary scene. Boulogne-sur-Mer is also a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts due to its hiking and biking trails, as well as its proximity to the ocean. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or simply taking in the beauty of your surroundings, Boulogne-sur-Mer has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Boulogne-sur-Mer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nausicaa - This is the largest aquarium in Europe and one of the most popular tourist attractions in Boulogne-sur-Mer. Visitors can explore over 50 different exhibits and see marine life from across the world.
  • Chateau-Musée - This castle houses the Boulogne-sur-Mer Museum, which features exhibits on the history of the city, as well as art and artifacts from the local region.
  • Basilique Notre-Dame de l’Immaculée Conception - This stunning basilica was built in the mid-19th century and boasts an impressive marble altar and stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Boulogne-sur-Mer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cap Blanc-Nez - This stunning cliff is located about 12 km north of Boulogne-sur-Mer and offers breathtaking views of the English Channel.
  • Cap Gris-Nez - Another beautiful cliff that is slightly further north than Cap Blanc-Nez. There are hiking trails that lead up to the cliff where visitors can take in stunning views of the coastline from above.
  • Côte d’Opale - This stretch of coastline is known for its beautiful beaches and rocky cliffs. It’s a great place for a scenic drive or a seaside picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boulogne-sur-Mer

Boulogne-sur-Mer is a popular destination for hikers and bikers due to its varied trails and stunning natural beauty. Some popular routes include:

  • Sentier Littoral - This coastal trail is perfect for hikers and runs all along the coast from Boulogne-sur-Mer to Le Touquet.
  • La Vélomaritime - Known in English as the “Channel cycleway”, La Vélomaritime is a long-distance cycling route that runs all along the coast of the English Channel.
  • Forêt Domaniale de Boulogne - This forest is less than 10 km from Boulogne-sur-Mer and offers a variety of hiking and biking trails through ancient oak and beech trees.

Best Things to Do in Boulogne-sur-Mer: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours - Visitors can take a variety of boat tours in Boulogne-sur-Mer that range from leisurely sightseeing to deep-sea fishing trips.
  • Local Markets - Boulogne-sur-Mer is known for its excellent fresh seafood, and visitors can experience local cuisine by visiting one of the many markets in the city.
  • Wine Tasting Tours - Boulogne-sur-Mer is located in the heart of French wine country, and visitors can take tours of local vineyards and wineries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boulogne-sur-Mer

Day 1: Visit the Chateau-Musée and the Basilique Notre-Dame de l’Immaculée Conception
Day 2: Take a boat tour and explore the nearby coastline
Day 3: Experience local cuisine at a market or restaurant
Day 4: Explore the Forêt Domaniale de Boulogne
Day 5: Spend the day at Nausicaa
Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour
Day 7: Hike or bike along the Sentier Littoral or La Vélomaritime

Food and Drink Scene of Boulogne-sur-Mer

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips - Boulogne-sur-Mer is famous for its fresh fish, and locals have a particular love for fish and chips.
  • Coquilles Saint-Jacques - Scallops in white wine sauce are a local favorite and a must-try dish.
  • Tarte au Maroilles - This tart made with a locally produced cheese called Maroilles is a savory treat that pairs well with a glass of wine.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - The region around Boulogne-sur-Mer is known for its beer production, and there are several local breweries that offer a wide range of beers.
  • Wine - France is famous for its wine, and Boulogne-sur-Mer is no exception. Visitors can take wine tasting tours of local vineyards and wineries.
  • Cider - Normandy, which is located just west of Boulogne-sur-Mer, is famous for its cider production. Visitors can sample locally produced ciders in the area.

Where to Stay in Boulogne-sur-Mer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel de la Matelote - This hotel is located close to the port and offers views of the harbor. It also has an on-site restaurant that serves local cuisine.
  • Le Saint-Charles - This hotel is located near the beach and offers modern accommodations with a nautical theme.
  • La Maison du Parc - This guesthouse is located in the Forêt Domaniale de Boulogne and offers a secluded and peaceful place to stay.

What to Buy in Boulogne-sur-Mer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maroilles Cheese - This locally produced cheese is a must-try, and visitors can bring home a wheel of their own to enjoy.
  • Seafood - Boulogne-sur-Mer is known for its fresh seafood, and visitors can bring home fresh fish, scallops, and shellfish from one of the local markets.
  • Beer - There are several local breweries in and around Boulogne-sur-Mer that offer a wide range of beers that visitors can bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Boulogne-sur-Mer

The people of Boulogne-sur-Mer are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and there are a few customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of:

  • It’s customary to greet people with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek.
  • Tipping is not required but is appreciated.
  • Dress is generally casual, but visitors should dress appropriately if they plan on visiting a religious site.

Best Time to Visit Boulogne-sur-Mer

The best time to visit Boulogne-sur-Mer is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, so visitors should expect larger crowds and higher prices. Spring and fall are both good times to visit as well, as the weather is mild and there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boulogne-sur-Mer

Do:

  • Visit local markets to sample fresh seafood and other local treats.
  • Explore the city’s rich history by visiting museums and historical sites.
  • Take advantage of the great hiking and biking trails in the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying the local seafood.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Rely solely on public transportation, as some areas of the city are better reached by car.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boulogne-sur-Mer

Boulogne-sur-Mer is generally a safe city, but visitors should take basic precautions such as keeping an eye on their valuables and avoiding deserted areas at night. Visitors should also be aware of any pickpockets or petty theft in busy tourist areas.

Health in Boulogne-sur-Mer

Visitors to Boulogne-sur-Mer should ensure that they have updated vaccinations and travel insurance before arriving. The local medical facilities are generally of a high standard, but visitors should have a basic understanding of French or bring a translator in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Boulogne-sur-Mer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Boulogne-sur-Mer

The nearest international airport to Boulogne-sur-Mer is Charles de Gaulle Airport in Paris. From there, visitors can take a train or rent a car to reach Boulogne-sur-Mer. There is also a local airport in Le Touquet that offers flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Boulogne-sur-Mer

Visitors can reach Boulogne-sur-Mer by bus from several nearby cities including Calais, Dunkirk, and Le Touquet. The local bus station is located in the city center and offers easy access to the rest of the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Boulogne-sur-Mer by train or car. There are several train stations in the area that offer frequent service to nearby cities, and there are several car rental agencies located within the city.

How to Get Around in Boulogne-sur-Mer

Boulogne-sur-Mer is a walkable city, with many of the main attractions located within easy walking distance of each other. For longer trips, there is a local bus system as well as taxis and car rental agencies.

Where to Go Next from Boulogne-sur-Mer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Calais - This city is less than 40 km from Boulogne-sur-Mer and is home to several historic landmarks and museums.
  • Le Touquet-Paris-Plage - This coastal resort town is located about 20 km to the south of Boulogne-sur-Mer and is known for its sandy beaches and thriving nightlife.
  • Lille - This city is located about 100 km from Boulogne-sur-Mer and boasts beautiful architecture, an impressive range of museums, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Boulogne-sur-Mer is a magical destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, and foodies alike. From its stunning coastline and ancient forests to its world-class cuisine and charming city center, Boulogne-sur-Mer has something to offer everyone. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or simply a getaway from the hustle and bustle of everyday life, Boulogne-sur-Mer is the perfect place to escape to.
