Introduction to Amiens

Located in the region of Hauts-de-France, Amiens is a charming city in northern France that is steeped in history and culture. Known for its stunning Gothic architecture, picturesque canals, and vibrant cultural scene, Amiens is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to France. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, delicious food, or just a relaxing vacation, Amiens has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Amiens

Here are our top picks for the best places to visit in Amiens.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amiens Cathedral: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest Gothic cathedrals in France, Amiens Cathedral is a must-visit attraction. Inside, you can see beautiful stained glass windows, ornate sculptures, and the stunning nave.

  • Jules Verne House: The former home of the famous author Jules Verne, this museum offers a fascinating look into the life and works of the visionary writer.

  • Musée de Picardie: This museum houses a vast collection of artwork and artifacts from the region of Picardie, including ancient Roman ruins, medieval art, and modern paintings.

  • Hortillonnages: These beautiful floating gardens are a unique feature of Amiens and offer a serene escape from the bustling city.

Natural Attractions of Amiens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saint-Leu neighborhood: Located along the River Somme, Saint-Leu is a charming neighborhood filled with colorful half-timbered houses, trendy cafes, and boutique shops.

  • Les Hortillonnages Nature Reserve: This maze of canals, paths, and islands is located just outside the city and is a perfect spot for nature lovers.

  • Somme River: Take a stroll along the scenic Somme River, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the city.

  • Parc Saint-Pierre: This expansive park is home to beautiful gardens, walking trails, and even a small zoo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amiens

Amiens has plenty of walking and biking routes that meander through the city and the surrounding countryside. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • The Green Route: A 30-kilometer trail that takes you through the Hortillonnages, the countryside, and the Somme Valley.

  • The Chemin du Halage : This route follows the Canal de la Somme and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Somme Valley Cycle Route : A 120-kilometer-long route that takes you through stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Amiens: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour: Explore the canals and floating gardens of Amiens with a relaxing boat tour.

  • Attend a show at the Maison de la Culture: This cultural center hosts a wide variety of events, including concerts, dance performances, and theater productions.

  • Visit the Amiens Market: Held every Wednesday and Saturday, this bustling market is the perfect place to sample fresh produce, cheeses, and meats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amiens

Day 1: Visit Amiens Cathedral and the Jules Verne House. Day 2: Explore the Musee de Picardie, followed by a relaxing stroll through the Hortillonnages. Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Somme River, stopping to enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 4: Visit the Saint-Leu neighborhood and enjoy a meal at one of the many trendy cafes. Day 5: Take a boat tour and enjoy some of the canals and scenic views of Amiens. Day 6: Go shopping for souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Day 7: Relax at the Parc Saint-Pierre and enjoy the beautiful gardens and walking trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Amiens

Amiens has a rich culinary culture, with plenty of delicious dishes and local beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Macarons d’Amiens: These almond-based cookies are a local specialty and make the perfect souvenir to take home.

  • Ficelle Picarde: This dish consists of a crepe filled with ham, mushrooms, and cheese, and is a classic of Picardy cuisine.

  • Andouillette: A spiced sausage made from pork intestine, this is a true taste of Picardy.

Local Beverages

  • Biere de Picardie: This light and refreshing beer is brewed right in Amiens and is perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.

  • Genepi: This herbal liquor is made from the artemisia plant and is a popular digestif in Amiens.

Where to Stay in Amiens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Marotte: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of Amiens and boasts stylish rooms and a gourmet restaurant.

  • Hotel Le Prieure: A charming 3-star hotel housed in a renovated 19th-century building, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

  • Auberge de la Vallée de la Trie: This quaint guesthouse is located in the countryside outside of Amiens and offers cozy rooms and a peaceful environment.

What to Buy in Amiens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Macarons d’Amiens: These almond-based cookies are a local specialty and make the perfect souvenir to take home.

  • Chocolate: Amiens is known for its high-quality chocolate, and there are plenty of chocolate shops in the city where you can find delicious treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Amiens

Amiens has a rich cultural heritage, with a long history of arts and literature. The people of Amiens are warm and welcoming, and you’ll find that they take pride in their city’s cultural identity. The Amiens Jazz Festival and the Christmas Market are two popular events that showcase the city’s vibrant culture.

Best Time to Visit Amiens

The best time to visit Amiens is during the spring and summer months, when the weather is mild and the gardens and canals are in full bloom. The Christmas Market is also a popular time to visit, with its festive atmosphere and delicious treats.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amiens

Do:

  • Explore the city on foot or by bike to get a feel for the local culture and history.

  • Try local specialties like macarons d’Amiens and some of the delicious Picardy dishes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t speak loudly or be disrespectful in public places like museums or churches.

  • Don’t forget to take the time to relax and enjoy the slower pace of life in Amiens.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amiens

Amiens is a safe city for tourists, but like any urban area, there are some precautions you should take to stay safe. Stick to well-lit areas, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Amiens

The French healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, and there are several hospitals and medical clinics in Amiens. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Amiens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amiens

The nearest airports to Amiens are Paris-Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) and Beauvais Airport (BVA), both of which are located about two hours away by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Amiens

There are several bus companies that operate in Amiens, including Flixbus and Eurolines. The main bus station is located in the city center, near the train station.

Other Options

There are also several train stations in Amiens, making it easy to get around the city and explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Amiens

The best way to explore Amiens is on foot or by bike. The city is relatively small, making it easy to get around on foot. There are also several bike rental companies in the city, allowing you to explore the surrounding countryside at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Amiens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beauvais: This small town is home to a beautiful cathedral and a charming old town.

  • Paris: The City of Light is just a short train ride away and offers endless attractions, from the Eiffel Tower to the Louvre Museum.

  • Reims: Known for its stunning cathedral and Champagne vineyards, Reims is a must-visit destination for wine lovers.

Conclusion

From its stunning Gothic cathedral to its charming canals and vibrant cultural scene, Amiens is a true gem in northern France. Whether you’re looking to explore the rich history and culture of the city, or just relax and enjoy its beautiful surroundings, Amiens has something to offer everyone. We hope that this travel guide has been helpful in planning your visit to this beautiful city.
