Introduction to Tours

Tours is a charming city located in the Centre-Val de Loire region of France. Known for its stunning architecture, rich history, and welcoming locals, Tours is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to France. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Tours. From the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and what to eat, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Tours

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tours is a city steeped in history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore:

  • Musée des Beaux-Arts de Tours: This museum houses an impressive collection of historical and contemporary artworks, including pieces by famous French artists such as Monet and Rodin.

  • Château de Tours: A stunning castle that dates back to the 14th century, the Château de Tours now houses a museum that explores the history of the region.

  • Basilique Saint-Martin: This beautiful basilica is one of the most important religious sites in Tours, and is dedicated to Saint Martin, one of the patron saints of France.

Natural Attractions of Tours: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tours is also home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in the country:

  • Parc des Expositions: A sprawling park that is perfect for a leisurely stroll, the Parc des Expositions is home to beautiful gardens, a lake, and even a small farm.

  • Loire Valley: Located just outside of Tours, the Loire Valley is known for its stunning countryside, meandering river, and charming towns and villages.

  • Jardins des Prébendes d’Oé: One of the most beautiful parks in Tours, the Jardins des Prébendes d’Oé is home to a variety of different flowers and plants, as well as several ornamental ponds and fountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tours

Tours is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to choose from. Some of the best include:

  • The Loire à Vélo: A popular cycling route that takes you along the Loire River, passing charming towns, vineyards, and scenic countryside.

  • Chinon to Saumur Bike Trail: This beautiful bike trail takes you through the heart of the Loire Valley, passing through stunning countryside and picturesque towns and villages.

Best Things to Do in Tours: Activities and Tours

Tours is a city that is packed with things to see and do. Whether you’re interested in history, art, or just want to explore the city’s unique culture, there is something for everyone. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Tours Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of the city, and discover its most famous landmarks and hidden gems.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Explore the local vineyards and sample some of the region’s best wines on a guided wine tasting tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tours

If you’re planning to spend a week in Tours, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Explore the Old Town and visit the Basilique Saint-Martin. Day 2: Visit the Musée des Beaux-Arts de Tours and the Château de Tours. Day 3: Take a bike tour of the Loire Valley. Day 4: Enjoy a relaxing day in the Jardins des Prébendes d’Oé. Day 5: Visit the charming town of Chinon, and explore its historic castle. Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards. Day 7: Visit the Parc des Expositions and enjoy a picnic by the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Tours

Must-Try Dishes

Tours is a food lover’s paradise, with plenty of delicious local specialties to try:

  • Rillettes: A hearty pork spread that is perfect for spreading on bread or crackers.

  • Tarte Tatin: This famous French dessert is made with caramelized apples and served with a dollop of crème fraîche.

  • Géline de Touraine: A local breed of chicken that is known for its tender meat and delicious flavor.

Local Beverages

Tours is also known for its wine and other local beverages:

  • Vouvray AOC: A crisp, refreshing white wine that is made from Chenin Blanc grapes.

  • Coteaux du Layon AOC: A popular dessert wine that is made from Chenin Blanc grapes.

Where to Stay in Tours: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tours offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget:

  • Hotel Oceania L’Univers Tours: Located in the heart of the city, this charming hotel offers elegant rooms and excellent amenities.

  • Hotel Kyriad Tours Centre: A budget-friendly option that is located just a short walk from some of the city’s top attractions.

  • Le Grand Tours: This luxurious hotel is housed in an 18th-century mansion, and offers stunning rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Tours: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Tours has plenty to offer:

  • Macarons: These delicate French cookies come in a variety of different flavors, and make the perfect sweet treat to bring home.

  • Leather Goods: Tours is home to several leather goods shops, where you can find beautifully crafted bags, belts, and other accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Tours

Tours is a city that values its history and culture, and visitors should be respectful of its customs and traditions:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Always say “bonjour” when entering a shop or restaurant.
  • French is the official language of Tours, so it’s a good idea to learn a few basic phrases before you visit.

Best Time to Visit Tours

The best time to visit Tours is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tours

Do:

  • Explore the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Take a bike tour of the Loire Valley.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring comfortable walking shoes.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Leave without trying a macaron or two!

Safety and Health

Safety in Tours

Tours is a safe city for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions:

  • Keep your valuables secure.
  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in busy areas.

Health in Tours

Tours is a relatively healthy city, but it’s always a good idea to take basic health precautions:

  • Wash your hands frequently.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Be aware of any food allergies or sensitivities.

Getting to Tours: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tours

The closest airport to Tours is Tours Val de Loire Airport, which is located just a short drive from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tours

Tours is well-connected by bus, with several stations located throughout the city.

Other Options

You can also reach Tours by train, with regular services connecting the city to Paris and other major destinations in France.

How to Get Around in Tours

Tours is a relatively small city, and many of its top attractions can be easily reached on foot. However, if you prefer to use public transportation, there are several options to choose from, including buses and trams.

Where to Go Next from Tours: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tours is located in the heart of the Loire Valley, which is home to many other charming towns and villages. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Amboise: A picturesque town that is located just a short drive from Tours, Amboise is known for its stunning château and beautiful gardens.

  • Château de Chambord: One of the most famous châteaus in the region, the Château de Chambord is a must-visit for anyone interested in French history and architecture.

Conclusion

Tours is a beautiful city that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, food and wine, or outdoor adventures, Tours has it all. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan an unforgettable trip to this charming French city. Bon voyage!
