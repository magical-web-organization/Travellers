Introduction to Parc Régional du Perche

Located in the heart of France, Parc Régional du Perche is a beautiful natural park spread across two regions - Eure-et-Loir and Orne. The park covers a vast area of more than 182,000 hectares, including vast forests, rolling hills, and picturesque villages that reflect the traditional rustic French culture.

This park is perfect for travelers looking for a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors come to the park to admire its natural beauty, the diverse plant and animal species, and enjoy peaceful outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and horse-riding.

List of Places to Visit in Parc Régional du Perche

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perche Museum

The Perche Museum is a cultural attraction dedicated to the heritage of Parc Régional de Perche. The museum has different exhibits displaying the history, traditional culture, and natural habitat of the region. Visitors can learn about the flora and fauna of the park or see special exhibitions that celebrate local art and architecture.

The Chateau of Nogent-le-Rotrou

The Chateau of Nogent-le-Rotrou is a castle that dates back to the 11th century and is a must-visit attraction in Parc Régional du Perche. Visitors can explore the castle’s rich history and discover the many different architectural styles that make up the building. The castle has numerous rooms filled with treasures such as old books, paintings, and antique pieces. The chateau also provides some spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Parc Régional du Perche: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Perche Forest

The Perche Forest is the most significant natural attraction in Parc Régional de Perche. It’s a vast woodland that covers almost 11,000 hectares and has several hiking and cycling trails. Enthusiast bird watchers can spot a variety of bird species here. It’s a great place for a peaceful walk, horse riding, or a picnic amid lush greenery.

Perche Ornithological Reserve

Located on the shores of the Perche Lake, this birdwatching site is a perfect place to observe beautiful water fowl, songbirds, and raptors from the lake’s shores or hide. Visitors can walk along nature trails and birdwatching hides to appreciate the natural beauty of the Perche.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parc Régional du Perche

The park has an extensive network of biking and hiking routes that traverse through typical French landscapes of meadows and gentle hills. Choose from numerous trails that cater to all levels of fitness and interest. The Escapades hike is a short 4-6 km trail, suitable for beginners, whereas the Grande Randonnée 22 trek is a long-distance hike mostly suited for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Parc Régional du Perche: Activities and Tours

Percheron Horse Farm Tour

The Percheron Horse Farm offers an exciting tour of their stables where visitors can learn about breeding these magnificent horses from grooming, feed, and training. They demonstrate how these horses were used in the past to help farmers plow their fields or pull loaded wagons.

Craft Workshops Saint-Amant-la-Bussière

Participate in an art and crafts workshop where visitors can learn different artistic techniques such as ceramics, woodwork, and glass blowing. They provide these workshops to all age groups customized to their skill level, and you can take your creations home as a souvenir.

Week-Long Itinerary for Parc Régional du Perche

Day 1: Start your trip to Perche by exploring the charming village of Nogent-le-Rotrou. Highlights include the historic Chateau of Nogent-le-Rotrou and wandering the streets of the medieval old town.

Day 2: Visit the Percheron Horse Farm to learn about these majestic horses and the vital role they’ve played in the region’s agricultural heritage. Keep the afternoon free for a relaxing walk through the beautiful Les Rimons Gardens and admire the stunning gardens and fountains.

Day 3: Hike the Escapades trail for a gentle introduction to the park’s stunning scenic beauty.

Day 4: Take a trip to the Perche Ornithological Reserve and observe the native bird species in their natural habitat.

Day 5: Learn about the region’s traditional crafts by attending an art and crafts workshop in Saint-Amant-la-Bussière.

Day 6 and 7: Spend the last two days of your trip as a relaxing retreat at the beautiful Les Bois-Francs spa to soothe your tired muscles.

Food and Drink Scene of Parc Régional du Perche

Must-Try Dishes

Perche Boudin

The Perche Boudin is a sausage with distinct white meat and a unique coating that leaves a smoky flavor.

Tarte Tatin

This traditional French dessert is a favorite of everyone. The tarte tatin is made with caramelized apples and a crispy pastry base, served with cream or ice-cream. A perfect dessert to end your meal on a sweet note.

Local Beverages

Cider

The region is famous for its cider, and visitors must try the sparkling apple juice from the Perche region.

Calvados

Calvados is a spirit made from distilled apple juice and aged in oak barrels. It is famous around the world and has a unique flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Parc Régional du Perche: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Parc Régional du Perche has no shortage of comfortable and cozy accommodations. Visitors can select from beautiful guesthouses, elegant hotel rooms, and charming hostels.

Les Bois-Francs

Les Bois-Francs is an excellent option for an upscale and luxurious stay. The hotel has modern amenities and a beautiful indoor pool. The spa is also a popular choice among visitors for relaxation and wellness.

Les Jardins de la Cour

This beautiful guesthouse is located in the center of the village of Nogent-le-Rotrou. It has comfortable rooms and exceptional hospitality, so visitors feel right at home.

What to Buy in Parc Régional du Perche: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The region produces exceptional artisanal crafts, and visitors can take home some beautiful souvenirs.

Local markets sell handmade pottery, textiles, and local food products like cheese, meat, and cider.

Local Culture and Customs of Parc Régional du Perche

The region has a strong tradition of rural handicraft, and many workshops and markets display this rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the region’s history through art exhibits, craft workshops, and local food markets.

Best Time to Visit Parc Régional du Perche

The best time to visit Parc Régional du Perche is during late spring to summer (May to August) when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parc Régional du Perche

Do:

  • Respect the environment and natural habitat of the park.
  • Explore the local art and culture.
  • Hike the trails and visit the natural reserves and nature hides.

Don’t:

  • Be loud when visiting scenic areas or wild animals.
  • Leave the garbage behind.
  • Harm any animals or plants in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parc Régional du Perche

Visitors to the park need to be careful while hiking or cycling, as there are steep routes in the wilderness, which can be dangerous for inexperienced trekkers. Visitors should follow the park’s safety guidelines and inform someone before taking an extended trek.

Health in Parc Régional du Perche

Visitors should always remain hydrated and carry hiking or cycling gear to avoid any injuries while in the park.

Getting to Parc Régional du Perche: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parc Régional du Perche

The closest airport to Parc Régional du Perche is Paris-Orly Airport, which is located around 90 km from the park.

Bus Stations Near Parc Régional du Perche

Nogent-le-Rotrou is the main bus station in the region for those who prefer public transport. There are also different coach companies around the region that offer services to the park.

Other Options

Visitors can always hire cars or motorbikes from different companies around the region to set off exploring the area.

How to Get Around in Parc Régional du Perche

The best way to get around Parc Régional du Perche is by car or bike. You should note that public transport may be limited to certain parts of the park’s interior, so visitors should arrange transport accordingly.

Where to Go Next from Parc Régional du Perche: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby tourist attractions include the Loire Valley, Chartres, or the Normandy region. The Loire valley is popular for its grand castles and picturesque vineyards, whereas Chartres is popular for its stunning Gothic cathedral and beautiful old town.
