A Comprehensive Guide to Blois, France

Nestled in the heart of Centre-Val de Loire in France, the city of Blois is renowned for its rich history, stunning architecture, and scenic beauty. This comprehensive guide has everything you need to plan your next trip to this beautiful city and make the most out of your vacation.

Introduction to Blois

Blois is a charming city located in the heart of France, known for its captivating blend of medieval and Renaissance-era architecture, winding cobblestone streets, and picturesque buildings that are steeped in history. This enchanting city has been the site of many significant events in French history and is home to several castles, museums, and beautiful gardens that take visitors back in time.

List of Places to Visit in Blois

There are plenty of places to visit and explore in Blois. The city is brimming with cultural landmarks, natural wonders, and other attractions that are perfect for tourists. Here are some of the must-see places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château Royal de Blois: This stunning chateau is the most popular tourist attraction in Blois. It has an ornate mix of Gothic, Renaissance, and classical architecture, and was the residence of several French kings and queens.
  • Maison de la Magie: Known for its impressive collection of magic books and memorabilia, this museum is perfect if you’re fascinated by illusions and magic shows.
  • Musée d’Histoire Naturelle de Blois: This natural history museum houses a diverse collection of animal specimens and fossils, making it an ideal destination for visitors interested in nature and biology.

Natural Attractions of Blois: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Grande Sauldre: One of the most picturesque areas in and around Blois, La Grande Sauldre offers stunning natural views. The river runs alongside beautiful forests, making it a perfect location for hiking, picnicking, and biking.
  • Parc des Mées: This nature park is home to a variety of rare plant and animal species. It also has winding trails that are perfect for hiking or biking, making it the perfect place to escape to nature.
  • Château de la Douve: This beautiful chateau is located in a pristine wooded area, making it a must-see attraction for visitors who don’t want to miss out on Blois’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blois

  • Blois-Chambord Trail: This 47.5 km-long trail runs between the two cities and takes you through some of the most scenic locations in Blois. It’s perfect for hiking, cycling, or walking along with the Loire River.
  • Route des Châteaux à Vélo: This is a scenic bike route that runs from the town of Blois to the beautiful Château de Chambord. The 61 km route passes by incredible landscapes and historic architecture, making it one of the most beautiful cycling routes in France.

Best Things to Do in Blois: Activities and Tours

  • Hot-Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride and soar above the stunning landscapes of Blois. You’ll experience the incredible natural beauty of the city as well as France’s iconic architecture from above.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Blois is home to some of the best wine regions in France, and a wine tasting tour is a perfect way to enjoy them. Take a guided tour of the wine regions and sample some of the best wines Blois has to offer.
  • Countryside Charter Tour: Experience the real France by taking a countryside charter tour. You’ll explore small villages, local farms, and artisanal producers, and get a real taste of local life and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Blois

When it comes to food and drink, Blois offers an eclectic mix of traditional and modern cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Escargots de Bourgogne: These snails are a French delicacy and are usually served as an appetizer. They’re cooked with garlic butter and parsley, and are a must-try dish for adventurous eaters.
  • Coq au Vin: This traditional French dish is usually served in a red wine sauce with mushrooms and onions.
  • Cheese Selection: No French cuisine experience is complete without sampling the many varieties of cheese available in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Wines: Blois is known for its fine wines, especially red wines like Chinon and Bourgueil. They go perfectly with cheese and other dishes, making them a must-try for wine lovers.
  • Bières de Blois: This local beer is brewed using traditional techniques and has a unique flavor that can’t be found anywhere else.

Where to Stay in Blois: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Blois, but here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Hotel Mercure Blois Centre: This is a luxury hotel situated in the center of Blois. It has a swimming pool, a fitness center, and a restaurant serving traditional French cuisine.
  • Le Monarque: This elegant hotel has spacious rooms with modern amenities and is located within walking distance of several top attractions in Blois.

What to Buy in Blois: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home with you, make sure to check out these shops:

  • La Maison du Terroir: This shop sells local produce, including wine, cheese, and olive oil, that make excellent souvenirs.
  • La Chouquette: This bakery sells traditional French pastries, such as croissants and éclairs, that are perfect to eat on-the-go or take home as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Blois

When visiting Blois make sure to be aware of these local cultural customs:

  • Festivals and Events: Blois hosts several festivals every year, including the Festival de la Rose in May and the Fêtes Johanniques in June.
  • Greeting: The French often greet each other with a handshake or a kiss on each cheek, a custom known as “la bise”.

Best Time to Visit Blois

The best time to visit Blois is during the months of April to June and September to October. These are the best months to explore the outdoors and take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blois

When visiting Blois, it’s important to keep in mind these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Learn a few basic French phrases. It will make it easier for you to communicate with locals and show respect for their culture.
  • Try the local cuisine. Blois has an amazing food scene, and exploring it is one of the best ways to appreciate the culture and history of the region.
  • Be aware of your surroundings. Pickpocketing and petty theft can be common in tourist areas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t assume everyone speaks English. Although many locals do speak English, it’s important to have a few basic French phrases memorized to make communication easier.
  • Don’t forget to tip. In France, it’s customary to leave a tip at restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blois

Blois is generally a safe city, but it’s always important to keep your belongings within sight, especially in tourist areas. It’s also recommended to keep your valuables in the hotel safe when you’re out exploring.

Health in Blois

There are no major health risks in Blois, but it’s always important to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months when temperatures can get very high.

Getting to Blois: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blois

The closest major airport to Blois is the Tours Val de Loire Airport, which is approximately 60 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to the city.

Bus Stations Near Blois

The Blois bus station is located downtown and is easily accessible from all parts of the city. Buses also frequently travel between Blois and other nearby cities, including Orléans and Vierzon.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, the Blois train station is located in the city center and offers services to many major cities in France.

How to Get Around in Blois

There are several transportation options available in Blois, including buses, taxis, and rental cars. Some of the best areas to explore on foot include the city center and several nearby parks and gardens.

Where to Go Next from Blois: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning a longer trip to France, consider visiting some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Château de Chambord: Just a short drive from Blois, this stunning chateau is one of the most beautiful in the region.
  • Tours: Located about an hour from Blois, Tours is a beautiful city renowned for its art galleries, museums, and stunning cathedrals.

Conclusion

Blois is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a taste of the history, culture, and natural beauty of France. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your time in this enchanting city.
