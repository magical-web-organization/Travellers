Introduction to Carnac

Carnac is a beautiful town located in Brittany, in northwest France, known for its magnificent prehistoric stone monuments and picturesque beaches. With its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty, Carnac offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the wonders of the past and the present. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Carnac, including places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Carnac

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carnac is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase its rich cultural heritage, including:

  • Musee de Prehistoire: This museum is dedicated to prehistoric civilization and houses a vast collection of artifacts, tools, and weapons, dating back to the Neolithic period.

  • Le Cairn de Saint-Michel: This is one of the largest prehistoric burial monuments in Europe, dating back to 4200 BCE. It consists of several burial chambers and provides a glimpse into the lives and customs of the ancient people who inhabited this region.

  • Tumulus de Kercado: This is another prehistoric burial mound, dating back to 4000 BCE, that contains several chambers and corridors, decorated with carvings and engravings.

Natural Attractions of Carnac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carnac is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, from the stunning coastline to the lush forests and meadows. Some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore include:

  • La Grande Plage: This is the main beach of Carnac, with white sands, crystal-clear waters, and plenty of activities, from swimming and sunbathing to windsurfing and sailing.

  • Les Alignements de Menhirs: This is a collection of prehistoric standing stones, dating back to 4500 BCE, that stretch over 4 km and are arranged in rows and circles.

  • Maison des Marais: This is a nature reserve that offers hiking trails, birdwatching, and picnic areas, amidst a stunning landscape of marshes, lakes, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carnac

Carnac boasts a network of hiking and biking trails that take you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the region. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Les Marais Salants: This trail takes you through a beautiful salt marsh, where you can see the traditional methods of salt production and enjoy the stunning views of the bay.

  • La Ria d’Etel: This is a coastal trail that takes you along the estuary of the River Etel, with breathtaking views of the sea, the beaches, and the cliffs.

  • Les Megalithes: This route takes you through the prehistoric monuments of Carnac, including the standing stones, the burial mounds, and the dolmens.

Best Things to Do in Carnac: Activities and Tours

Carnac offers a diverse range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences, from cultural and historical tours to outdoor adventures and water sports. Some of the best things to do in Carnac include:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the Gulf of Morbihan, and discover the stunning islands, bays, and beaches that are scattered along the coast.

  • Kite Surfing: With its strong winds and wide-open spaces, Carnac is the perfect destination for kite surfing, one of the most exhilarating water sports.

  • Wine Tours: Brittany is not known for its wine production, but it has a unique range of wines and ciders that are worth tasting and exploring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carnac

If you have a week to spend in Carnac, you can explore the town and its surroundings thoroughly. Here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Les Alignements de Menhirs and Le Cairn de Saint-Michel
  • Day 2: Explore Musee de Prehistoire and Tumulus de Kercado
  • Day 3: Hike or bike Les Marais Salants
  • Day 4: Relax on La Grande Plage and explore the town center
  • Day 5: Take a boat tour of the Gulf of Morbihan
  • Day 6: Visit Maison des Marais and hike La Ria d’Etel
  • Day 7: Go kite surfing and try some local wine and cider

Food and Drink Scene of Carnac

Must-Try Dishes

Brittany is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, with an emphasis on seafood, dairy, and local produce. Some of the must-try dishes in Carnac include:

  • Creperie: This classic Breton dish comes in two types, savory and sweet, and is filled with anything from cheese and ham to chocolate and fruit.

  • Galette: This is a savory crepe made with buckwheat flour and filled with eggs, cheese, spinach, mushrooms, or any other ingredients you like.

  • Seafood: Carnac is famous for its fresh seafood, from oysters and scallops to mussels and lobster. Try them in any local restaurant, and you won’t be disappointed.

Local Beverages

Breton culture values its traditional drinks, such as cider and beer, and it has a rich history of winemaking. Some of the local beverages to try in Carnac include:

  • Cider: Brittany is famous for its cider production, made from fermented apples, and it comes in many varieties, from sweet to dry and sparkling.

  • Beer: Breton beer is another delicious drink, made from local grains and hops, and it is often served with seafood and crepes.

  • Wine: Although Brittany is not known for its wine production, it has a unique range of wines that are worth exploring, such as Muscadet, Gros Plant, and Coteaux d’Ancenis.

Where to Stay in Carnac: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carnac has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxurious hotels and resorts. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel La Licorne: This cozy hotel is located in the heart of Carnac, with comfortable rooms, a garden, and a terrace.

  • Hotel Les Alignements: This charming hotel is situated near the prehistoric standing stones, with spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Camping Le Moulin de Kermaux: This campsite is located in a beautiful natural setting, with pitches for tents, caravans, and motorhomes, as well as accommodations in mobile homes and chalets.

What to Buy in Carnac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Carnac offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts or reminders of your trip, such as:

  • Pottery: Brittany has a rich tradition of pottery making, and Carnac has many shops and studios that offer handmade ceramic dishes, vases, and other decorative items.

  • Salt: Brittany is also known for its artisanal salt production, and you can find many varieties of salt in Carnac, from fleur de sel to smoked sea salt.

  • Crepe pans: If you love to cook, you can bring back a traditional crepe pan made of cast iron, along with some recipes and ingredients to make your own crepes at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Carnac

Breton culture is distinct and proud, with its own language, music, dance, and dress. Some of the local customs, festivals, and traditions to experience in Carnac include:

  • Fest-Noz: This is a traditional Breton dance and music festival, held throughout the summer, with live bands, food, and dancing.

  • Breton language: Although most people speak French in Carnac, you can still hear and learn some basic Breton phrases, such as “degemer mat” (welcome) and “kenavo” (goodbye).

  • Breton dress: You can see some locals wearing traditional Breton dress, which consists of a striped shirt, a beret, and wooden clogs.

Best Time to Visit Carnac

The best time to visit Carnac is from May to September, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the town is buzzing with festivals, events, and activities. The peak season is in July and August, so expect crowds and higher prices. If you prefer fewer crowds and lower prices, you can visit in the off-season, from October to April, but be prepared for colder weather and fewer activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carnac

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including crepes, seafood, and cider.
  • Explore the prehistoric monuments and museums to learn about Carnac’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor adventures, such as hiking, biking, and kite surfing.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as speaking French and trying some basic Breton phrases.
  • Bring comfortable shoes and clothes, especially if you plan to hike or bike.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the prehistoric monuments, as they are fragile and protected.
  • Expect everyone to speak English, as French is the official language in Carnac and Brittany.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious or cultural sites.
  • Swim or surf in dangerous areas, and always follow the safety instructions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carnac

Carnac is a safe and welcoming town, but as with any tourist destination, it’s important to take some basic precautions, such as:

  • Keep your valuables and documents in a safe place, such as a hotel safe or a money belt.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in deserted or poorly lit areas.
  • Watch out for pickpockets and scams, such as fake beggars or salespeople.
  • Follow the safety instructions when hiking, biking, or participating in water sports.
  • Carry a map, a phone, and emergency numbers in case of any problems.

Health in Carnac

Brittany has a modern and comprehensive health system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in Carnac and nearby towns. Some useful tips to stay healthy in Carnac include:

  • Drink bottled water, as tap water may contain high levels of minerals.
  • Protect yourself from the sun, especially during the peak hours of 11 am to 3 pm.
  • Use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants in wooded and marshy areas.
  • Pack any necessary medications, and bring a health insurance card and a list of emergency contacts.
  • Follow the safety guidelines during the Covid-19 pandemic, such as wearing a mask, washing your hands, and social distancing.

Getting to Carnac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carnac is easily accessible by several modes of transport, and there are several options to consider, depending on your budget and preferences.

Airports Near Carnac

The closest airport to Carnac is Lorient South Brittany Airport, which is about 50 km away. Other options include Nantes Atlantique Airport, which is about 150 km away, and Rennes Saint-Jacques Airport, which is about 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Carnac

Carnac has a bus station that operates several local and regional lines, connecting to nearby towns and cities. There are also several national and international bus companies that operate from Nantes or Rennes.

Other Options

You can also reach Carnac by train, with the nearest train station at Auray, about 15 km away. From there, you can take a regional train or a taxi to Carnac.

How to Get Around in Carnac

Carnac is a small and compact town, and most of the attractions are within walking or biking distance. If you prefer to use public transport, there are several options, such as:

  • Bus: There are several local and regional bus lines that operate in Carnac and nearby towns.
  • Taxi: You can find several taxi companies in Carnac, and they are useful if you want to visit other towns or attractions outside the town.
  • Bike rental: There are several bike rental companies in Carnac, and it’s a great way to explore the town and the surroundings on your own.

Where to Go Next from Carnac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Carnac is located in a strategic position, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore, such as:

  • Quiberon: This is a beautiful peninsula, located about 20 km from Carnac, with stunning beaches, cliffs, and a charming town center.

  • Vannes: This is a historic town, located about 30 km from Carnac, with a medieval center, a beautiful port, and several museums and galleries.

  • Belle-Île-en-Mer: This is the largest island in Brittany, located about 20 km from Carnac, accessible by ferry, and famous for its rugged coastline, stunning beaches, and unspoiled beauty.

Conclusion

Carnac is a treasure trove of history, culture, and natural beauty, and it offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in prehistoric monuments, outdoor adventures, or delicious cuisine, you’ll find it all in this charming town. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip to Carnac with confidence and explore everything it has to offer.
