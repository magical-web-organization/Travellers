Are you planning a trip to Brittany and looking for a charming town to explore? Look no further than Dinan, a well-preserved medieval town with a rich history, stunning architecture, and breathtaking views of the Rance River. This comprehensive guide will help you discover the best of Dinan, from must-see cultural attractions to off-the-beaten-path hikes and biking routes. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or just looking for a relaxing escape, Dinan has something for everyone.

Introduction to Dinan

Located in the northwest region of France, Dinan is a picturesque town in the department of Côtes-d’Armor, just a short drive away from Saint-Malo and the famous Mont-Saint-Michel. With a population of around 11,000 inhabitants, Dinan is renowned for its well-preserved medieval architecture, charming cobbled streets, and scenic views of the Rance River. The town’s history dates back to the 11th century, and it played a crucial role in Brittany’s long and complex past.

List of Places to Visit in Dinan

Here is a list of the best places to visit in Dinan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château de Dinan: This impressive castle was built in the 14th century and offers panoramic views over the town and the Rance River.
  • Musée Yvonne-Jean Haffen: This museum showcases the work of Yvonne-Jean Haffen, a Breton painter who lived in Dinan for most of her life.
  • Basilique Saint-Sauveur: This stunning basilica dates back to the 12th century and features impressive stained-glass windows, sculptures, and frescoes.
  • Place des Merciers: This charming square is lined with half-timbered houses and is one of the most picturesque spots in Dinan.
  • Ramparts of Dinan: These ancient fortifications offer breathtaking views over the town and the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Dinan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Rance Valley: This scenic valley offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region, with stunning views over the river and the countryside.
  • Le Jardin Anglais: This beautiful garden, located near the château, features a variety of trees, flowers, and herbs, as well as a pond and a playground for kids.
  • Le Petit Train de Dinan: This tourist train takes visitors on a scenic tour of the town, passing by some of the most iconic landmarks and viewpoints.
  • Les Vieilles Charrues: This annual music festival attracts thousands of visitors to Dinan every summer, with world-renowned musicians performing on various stages around the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dinan

Dinan offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Brittany, with stunning views over the Rance River and the surrounding countryside. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • The Rance River Trail: This 34-kilometer trail follows the banks of the Rance River from Dinan to Dinard, passing through picturesque villages and scenic viewpoints.
  • La Vallée de la Rance à Vélo: This cycling trail is part of the larger EuroVelo 4 route and takes visitors on a journey through the heart of Brittany, with stunning views of the river and its estuary.
  • La Voie Verte: This 22-kilometer trail is dedicated to walkers, cyclists, and rollerbladers and follows the former railway line from Dinan to Dinard, passing through beautiful forests and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Dinan: Activities and Tours

In addition to its cultural and natural attractions, Dinan offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Dinan:

  • Take a guided tour of the town’s landmarks and hidden gems, such as the underground tunnels and the secret gardens.
  • Rent a kayak or a canoe and explore the Rance River at your own pace, with stunning views of the castle, the basilica, and the ramparts.
  • Sample some of the local delicacies at the town’s numerous food markets and specialty shops, such as the famous Breton crêpes and the artisanal cheeses.
  • Attend one of the town’s many festivals and events, such as the Fête des Remparts or the Jazz à l’étage festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Dinan

Dinan offers a diverse and delicious food and drink scene, with a wide range of local specialties and artisanal products. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galettes and crêpes: These iconic Breton specialties come in many varieties, from sweet to savory, and are perfect for a quick snack or a full meal.
  • Kouign-amann: This famous Breton pastry is made with layers of butter and sugar and is one of the most indulgent treats you’ll ever taste.
  • Far breton: This classic Breton dessert is made with eggs, milk, flour, and prunes, and is a staple of traditional Breton cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Dinan is famous for its refreshing and fruity cider, which comes in many varieties and is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Chouchen: This Breton alcoholic beverage is made with honey and water and has a sweet and smooth taste.
  • Breton beer: The region of Brittany has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local microbreweries producing high-quality beers with unique flavors and aromas.

Where to Stay in Dinan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dinan offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dinan:

  • Hotel Arvor: This 4-star hotel is located in the heart of the town and features elegantly decorated rooms, a spa, and a gourmet restaurant.
  • Hostellerie de la Renaissance: This charming guesthouse is housed in a historic building and offers comfortable and affordable rooms, as well as a cozy restaurant and bar.
  • Auberge de la Porte: This budget-friendly hostel is located just outside the town’s walls and offers basic but clean and comfortable rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and lounge.

What to Buy in Dinan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dinan is famous for its local products and artisanal crafts, including:

  • Breton biscuits: These delicious cookies come in many flavors, such as butter, chocolate, and caramel, and are a perfect gift for your friends and family.
  • Pottery and ceramics: Dinan has a thriving pottery scene, with many local artisans producing high-quality plates, bowls, and cups with unique designs and patterns.
  • Local wines and spirits: Dinan is located in the heart of the Breton wine region, and you can find many local wines and spirits in the town’s specialty shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Dinan

Dinan has a rich and diverse culture, with many traditions and customs that have been preserved over the centuries. Here are some of the most important cultural events and customs in Dinan:

  • The Fête des Remparts: This biennial festival is held in July and celebrates the town’s medieval past, with a grand parade, jousting tournaments, and other historical reenactments.
  • Breton music and dance: Brittany has a rich musical and dance tradition, and you can often hear live music and see traditional dances in Dinan’s streets and squares.
  • The Breton language: Breton is a Celtic language that is still spoken by a small number of people in Brittany, and you can often see signs and hear conversations in Breton in Dinan and other towns in the region.

Best Time to Visit Dinan

The best time to visit Dinan is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and many festivals and events are held in the town. However, you can also visit Dinan in the spring or fall, when the town is less crowded, and the prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dinan

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts of visiting Dinan:

Do:

  • Visit the town’s cultural attractions, such as the château, the basilica, and the Musée Yvonne-Jean Haffen.
  • Sample some of the local delicacies, such as the Breton crêpes, the Kouign-amann, and the cider.
  • Take a walking or biking tour of the town’s landmarks and hidden gems.

Don’t:

  • Park your car in the narrow and steep streets of the town center, as it can be difficult to find a spot and navigate the tight corners.
  • Enter private properties or monuments without permission or proper guidance.
  • Take photos of people or children without their consent.

Safety and Health

Dinan is a safe town for tourists, with low crime rates and a welcoming and friendly atmosphere. However, it’s always advisable to take some basic precautions, such as:

Safety in Dinan

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in deserted areas or at night.
  • Keep your valuables, such as phones, wallets, and passports, in a safe place, such as a hotel safe.
  • Follow the local traffic rules and regulations, as driving in Dinan’s narrow and steep streets can be challenging.

Health in Dinan

  • Make sure to carry any medication or prescription drugs with you, and consult your doctor in advance if you have any medical conditions or allergies.
  • Drink bottled water or boiled water, as tap water can be of variable quality.
  • Use sunscreen and hat during the warmer months as the sun can be harsh in the region.

Getting to Dinan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dinan is easily accessible from many parts of Brittany and France, thanks to its well-connected airports, train stations, and bus routes. Here are some of the best ways to get to Dinan:

Airports Near Dinan

  • Dinard-Pleurtuit-Saint-Malo Airport: This small airport is located just 20 minutes away from Dinan and offers daily flights to Paris, London, and other destinations in France and Europe.
  • Rennes Airport: This larger airport is located around an hour away from Dinan and offers a wider range of national and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Dinan

  • Dinan Bus Station: This bus station is located in the town center and offers connections to nearby towns and villages, as well as regional and national destinations.
  • Saint-Malo Bus Station: This larger bus station is located just 30 minutes away from Dinan and offers connections to many cities in France and Europe.

Other Options

  • Train: Dinan is served by several nearby train stations, such as Dinan, Saint-Malo, and Rennes, with connections to many national and international destinations.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the scenic roads and countryside around Dinan, and there are many car rental agencies in the town and nearby airports and train stations.

How to Get Around in Dinan

Dinan is a compact and walkable town, with many landmarks, shops, and restaurants located within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside or visit nearby towns and villages, you can use the following transportation options:

  • Bike rentals: There are many bike rental shops in Dinan, and renting a bike is a great way to explore the Rance River Trail or the Voie Verte.
  • Car rentals: There are many car rental agencies in Dinan, and renting a car is a great way to explore the wider region and visit nearby attractions and towns.
  • Public transportation: Dinan has a well-connected bus system, with regular connections to nearby towns and villages, such as Saint-Malo, Dinard, and Rennes.

Where to Go Next from Dinan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Brittany, there are many nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Dinan, such as:

  • Saint-Malo: This historic walled city is just a short drive away from Dinan and offers stunning beaches, a bustling port, and many cultural attractions.
  • Mont-Saint-Michel: This iconic island monastery is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is located less than an hour away from Dinan.
  • Cap Fréhel: This scenic cape offers breathtaking views over the sea and the rocky coast, with many hiking and biking trails to explore.

Conclusion

Dinan is one of the most charming and picturesque towns in Brittany, with a rich history, stunning architecture, and breathtaking views of the Rance River. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Dinan has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to explore the best of Dinan and make unforgettable memories on your next trip to France.
