Introduction to Vannes

Vannes is a city in Brittany, situated in the northwestern part of France. The city is known for its beautiful architecture, historic landmarks, and rich cultural heritage. The city is a great place to explore for those who love history, architecture, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Vannes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Fine Arts: The museum is located in the historic mansion of former bishop, and it houses an extensive collection of artworks, sculptures, and artefacts from the 17th and 18th centuries.
  • Château de l’Hermine: The castle is an iconic landmark in the city, and it served as a residence for the Dukes of Brittany during the 15th century. The castle features Gothic architecture, beautiful gardens, and a museum that houses a collection of items related to the history of the city.
  • The Old Town: The Old Town of Vannes is a picturesque area with cobblestone streets, half-timbered buildings, and historic landmarks. It’s an ideal area to explore if you enjoy strolling around and enjoying the architecture.

Natural Attractions of Vannes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gulf of Morbihan: The Gulf of Morbihan is a beautiful natural area, featuring scenic views, beaches, and islands. Visitors can explore the Gulf by boat and witness the beauty of the natural surroundings.
  • Parc Naturel Regional du Golfe du Morbihan: The park is a nature reserve that features a diverse range of wildlife and habitats, including wetlands, forests, and marshes. Visitors can take a guided tour of the park to learn more about the flora and fauna of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vannes

  • Vannes to Auray: The trail connects Vannes to the town of Auray and is a popular route for hikers and bikers. The trail is approximately 16 kilometers long and passes through beautiful natural areas and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Vannes: Activities and Tours

  • Vannes Food Tour: The tour takes visitors to different local restaurants and cafes to sample traditional Breton dishes and learn more about the local cuisine.
  • Boat Tour of the Gulf of Morbihan: The boat tour provides visitors with the opportunity to explore the Gulf of Morbihan and appreciate the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vannes

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Old Town of Vannes. Visit the Château de l’Hermine and the Museum of Fine Arts.
Day 2 Take a guided tour of the Parc Naturel Regional du Golfe du Morbihan.
Day 3 Rent a bike and follow the Vannes to Auray trail to explore the natural surroundings.
Day 4 Take a boat tour of the Gulf of Morbihan.
Day 5 Visit the Saint-Pierre Cathedral and the Jardin des Remparts.
Day 6 Take a Vannes Food Tour to explore the Breton cuisine.
Day 7 Relax on one of the beautiful beaches in the Gulf of Morbihan.

Food and Drink Scene of Vannes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galettes: Galettes are traditional Breton pancakes made with buckwheat flour and are stuffed with a variety of fillings, including cheese, ham, and vegetables.
  • Kouign-amann: Kouign-amann is a Breton pastry that is made with layers of butter and sugar. It’s a popular dessert in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Cider is a popular alcoholic drink in Brittany, made from fermented apples.
  • Muscadet: Muscadet is a white wine that is produced in the Loire Valley region and is a popular drink in Vannes.

Where to Stay in Vannes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Villa Valencia: This modern bed and breakfast is located in the historic center of Vannes, next to the Chateau de l’Hermine.
  • Best Western Plus Hôtel Vannes Centre-Ville: This hotel is located in the heart of the city, within walking distance to many of the top attractions in Vannes.
  • Hotel Escale Oceania Vannes Centre: This hotel is located near the harbor area in Vannes and offers views of the Gulf of Morbihan.

What to Buy in Vannes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Breton Stripes: Breton stripes are a traditional pattern of stripes that are commonly seen on clothing, bags, and accessories in the region.
  • Cider: Cider is a popular drink in Brittany and is an excellent choice to bring back home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Vannes

  • Language: The official language of Vannes is French, but many locals also speak Breton, an indigenous language of Brittany.
  • Festivals: There are many festivals and celebrations throughout the year in Vannes, including the Fête des Remparts, a medieval festival that takes place every two years.

Best Time to Visit Vannes

  • The best time to visit Vannes is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are many outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vannes

Do:

  • Do learn a few phrases in French to communicate with locals.
  • Do try the Breton cuisine, especially the galettes and kouign-amann.
  • Do participate in local festivals and celebrations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent if traveling during the summer months.
  • Don’t leave valuable items unattended in public areas.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vannes

  • Vannes is generally a safe city for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.
  • Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas, such as markets and festivals.

Health in Vannes

  • There are no major health concerns or vaccinations required for traveling to Vannes.
  • It’s always important to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Vannes: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Train: Vannes is connected to major cities in France by train.
  • Bus: There are frequent buses that run between Vannes and other cities in Brittany.
  • Car: Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Airports Near Vannes

  • Lorient South Brittany Airport: Approximately 50 kilometers from Vannes.
  • Nantes Atlantique Airport: Approximately 110 kilometers from Vannes.

Bus Stations Near Vannes

  • Gare Routière de Vannes: This is the main bus station in Vannes that connects the city to other cities in Brittany.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are ferries that connect Vannes to other cities along the coast of Brittany.

How to Get Around in Vannes

  • Walking: The city center of Vannes is small and can easily be explored on foot.
  • Biking: Bikes can be rented from various rental companies in the city.
  • Bus: There are local buses that run through the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Vannes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Carnac: A small town in Brittany known for its megalithic structures.
  • Rennes: The capital of Brittany, known for its historic architecture and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Vannes is a charming city that has something to offer for everyone. From historic landmarks and museums to natural attractions and outdoor activities, Vannes has it all. The Breton cuisine, local festivals, and friendly locals make this city an unforgettable destination. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next trip to Vannes and make the most out of your visit!
