Introduction to Roscoff

Located in the Brittany region in France, Roscoff is a charming seaside town that boasts of a rich history, breathtaking natural scenery, and a vibrant cultural scene. Known for its stunning beaches, picturesque harbors, and world-class seafood, Roscoff is a popular destination for those who want to relax and unwind, as well as those who seek adventure and excitement.

List of Places to Visit in Roscoff

Roscoff has a long list of must-visit places that offer something for everyone, whether you’re into history, culture, nature, or adventure. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Maison des Johnnies et de l’Oignon de Roscoff: This museum honors the onion growers who traveled to the UK to sell their produce. It provides an insight into the lives and traditions of these onion farmers and features exhibitions that explore the history and culture of this unique trade.

  • The Jardin Exotique et Botanique de Roscoff: This garden is a must-visit for horticulture enthusiasts, showcasing a vast collection of exotic plants and flowers from around the world.

  • The Chapelle Sainte-Barbe: Located on the cliffs overlooking the sea, this 17th-century chapel is a cultural icon that boasts of stunning frescoes and an impressive altar.

Natural Attractions of Roscoff: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sentier des Douaniers: This hiking trail along the coast offers some of the most breathtaking views of the sea, cliffs, and beaches.

  • The Ile de Batz: A short ferry ride from Roscoff, this island is a paradise for nature lovers, with its sandy beaches, picturesque villages, and stunning botanical garden.

  • The Roscoff Thalassotherapy Center: This seawater spa offers a variety of treatments that harness the healing benefits of the ocean, such as massages, hydrotherapy, and relaxation sessions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roscoff

Roscoff is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the coastline and the mountains. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Grande Randonnee 34 (GR34): This long-distance hiking trail stretches over 2000 km along the Atlantic coast, offering hikers the opportunity to experience some of France’s most impressive landscapes.

  • The Velodyssey: This cycling route starts in Roscoff and follows the Atlantic coast all the way to Spain, passing through some of the most picturesque seaside towns in France.

Best Things to Do in Roscoff: Activities and Tours

Roscoff offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Roscoff:

  • Take a boat tour of the coast: This tour offers an opportunity to discover Roscoff’s stunning coastline, hidden coves, and rocky cliffs.

  • Learn how to cook seafood: Roscoff is famous for its fresh seafood, and what better way to experience it than by learning how to prepare it yourself?

  • Visit a local market: Roscoff’s market is a must-visit for foodies, offering an array of local produce, including seafood, cheese, and vegetables.

Week-Long Itinerary for Roscoff

For those who want to experience everything Roscoff has to offer, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and explore the town

Day 2: Hike the Sentier des Douaniers

Day 3: Visit the Jardin Exotique et Botanique de Roscoff and the Chapelle Sainte-Barbe

Day 4: Take a day trip to Ile de Batz

Day 5: Relax at the Roscoff Thalassotherapy Center

Day 6: Take a cooking class and visit the market

Day 7: Depart Roscoff

Food and Drink Scene of Roscoff

Roscoff is known for its delicious seafood, locally grown vegetables, and traditional Breton cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crevettes de Roscoff: These are the local shrimp, which are small but packed with flavor.

  • Galettes: A savory pancake made of buckwheat flour and filled with a variety of fillings, such as cheese, ham, and vegetables.

  • Kig ha farz: A hearty stew made of pork, potatoes, and buckwheat dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Breton cider: Made from locally grown apples, Breton cider is a refreshing alternative to wine and beer.

  • Chouchen: A sweet wine made of honey, Chouchen is a traditional drink in Brittany.

Where to Stay in Roscoff: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Roscoff offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Brittany & Spa: A luxury hotel overlooking the harbor, offering a spa, pool, and gourmet restaurant.

  • Hotel Aux Tamaris: A charming boutique hotel located near the beach, featuring a lovely garden and comfortable rooms.

  • La Maison des Johnnies et de l’Oignon: A unique guesthouse located in a former onion warehouse, offering comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Roscoff: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Roscoff, don’t forget to bring back some souvenirs and local products that will remind you of this charming town. Some of the best options include:

  • Caramel au beurre salé: A delicious local specialty, this salted butter caramel can be found in many shops and markets in Roscoff.

  • Handwoven baskets: Made of natural fibers, these baskets are a traditional craft in Brittany.

  • Cidre de Roscoff: This locally produced cider is a refreshing drink that can be enjoyed on its own or with food.

Local Culture and Customs of Roscoff

Roscoff has a unique culture that is deeply rooted in its history and traditions. Here are some customs and traditions that you should be aware of:

  • Onions: Roscoff is famous for its onions, which were traditionally grown by local farmers and sold in the UK. Today, the onion festival is still a popular event in the town, featuring parades, music, and food.

  • Language: Breton is spoken by some locals in Roscoff, although French is the main language.

  • Dress code: While casual wear is acceptable in most places, it is recommended to dress up a bit for dinner at a restaurant.

Best Time to Visit Roscoff

The best time to visit Roscoff is from May to September, when the weather is sunny and warm, and the town is bustling with tourists and locals enjoying the summer season. However, if you prefer a quieter and more peaceful trip, you may visit during the shoulder seasons (April/May and September/October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Roscoff

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Roscoff:

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and Breton cuisine.

  • Learn a few basic French phrases to communicate with locals.

  • Enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the town and its surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Leave your valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roscoff

Roscoff is generally a safe town, but it is still important to take basic precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

  • Beware of pickpockets in public places.

  • Follow the rules and regulations of any outdoor activities you engage in.

Health in Roscoff

France has a well-developed healthcare system, and medical care is easily accessible in Roscoff. However, it is still recommended to have travel insurance that covers healthcare and emergency medical evacuation.

Getting to Roscoff: Transportation Options and Logistics

Roscoff is easy to reach from Paris, as well as other major cities in France and Europe. Here are some transportation options to get to Roscoff:

Airports Near Roscoff

The closest airports to Roscoff are in Brest and Lorient.

Bus Stations Near Roscoff

The nearest bus station to Roscoff is located in Morlaix, which is 17 km away from the town.

Other Options

  • Trains: The nearest train station to Roscoff is in Morlaix, which is well-connected to major cities in France.

  • Car: You can also rent a car and drive to Roscoff, which is located on the N12 highway.

How to Get Around in Roscoff

Roscoff is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, it is recommended to rent a bike or a car. Taxis and buses are also available for local transportation.

Where to Go Next from Roscoff: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roscoff is located in a region that offers a wealth of destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some nearby places worth visiting:

  • Brest: A vibrant city located about 75 km from Roscoff, Brest offers a rich cultural, historical, and culinary scene.

  • Concarneau: A picturesque seaside town located about 100 km from Roscoff, Concarneau is known for its stunning beaches and historic fortress.

  • Quimper: A charming town located about 100 km from Roscoff, Quimper is famous for its medieval center, Gothic cathedral, and pottery.

Conclusion

Roscoff is a must-visit destination for those who want to experience the best of Brittany, with its stunning natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you want to relax and unwind, or seek adventure and excitement, Roscoff offers a wide range of activities and attractions that cater to all interests and preferences.
