Introduction to Brocéliande

Brocéliande is a mythical forest in Brittany, France, famous for its legends of King Arthur, Merlin the magician, and the Knights of the Round Table. It covers over 7000 hectares in the commune of Paimpont and is home to rare species of wildlife and beautiful scenic views.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Brocéliande has something to offer everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will take you on a journey through the enchanted forest and reveal the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Brocéliande

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Château de Comper: This castle turned museum is dedicated to the legends of King Arthur and Merlin.

  • Musée de la Résistance Bretonne: Learn about the French Resistance during World War II in Brittany.

  • Église de Tréhorenteuc: This church is famous for its stained glass windows depicting Arthurian legends.

Natural Attractions of Brocéliande: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Val sans Retour: This valley is known for its fairy tales and features stunning rock formations.

  • Fontaine de Barenton: This fountain is said to have healing powers and is the mythical birthplace of Merlin.

  • La Porte des Secrets: This interactive experience will take you on a journey through the forest’s myths and legends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brocéliande

Brocéliande has over 42 hiking trails, ranging from beginner to advanced. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • The Paimpont Forest Loop: This 12 km loop takes you through the heart of the forest.

  • Val sans Retour Circuit: This 8 km circuit takes you through the valley of no return.

  • Merlin Trail: This 8 km trail takes you through the legend-filled forest and ends at the Fontaine de Barenton.

Best Things to Do in Brocéliande: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding: Take a guided horseback ride through the forest and experience the magic of Brocéliande.

  • Canoeing/kayaking: Adventure through the waterways of the forest and see it from a different perspective.

  • Hot air balloon rides: Take to the skies for stunning views of Brocéliande and the surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brocéliande

Day 1: Arrival and settling in at your chosen accommodation.

Day 2: Visit the Château de Comper and take a stroll around the nearby lake, Paimpont.

Day 3: Hike along the Paimpont Forest Loop trail and have a picnic lunch in the forest.

Day 4: Explore the Val sans Retour valley and see the rock formations up close.

Day 5: Take a horseback ride through the forest, lapping up the magical atmosphere.

Day 6: Go canoeing or kayaking on the lakes in the forest.

Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Brocéliande

Must-Try Dishes

  • Breton Galette: A savory buckwheat crepe filled with ham, cheese, and egg.

  • Crêpes: Sweet or savory, these thin pancakes are a staple in Brittany.

  • Kouign-amann: A buttery, flaky pastry similar to a croissant.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Made from locally grown apples, this refreshing drink pairs well with a galette or crepe.

  • Chouchen: A traditional honey wine brewed in Brittany.

Where to Stay in Brocéliande: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chateau du Pin: A stunning castle hotel set in acres of gardens, only a few kilometers away from Paimpont forest.

  • Hotel le Relais de Brocéliande: A 3-star hotel located just 1 km away from the forest entrance.

  • Les Jardins de Brocéliande: A beautiful guesthouse surrounded by the forest and gardens.

What to Buy in Brocéliande: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jewelry: Inspired by the legendary tales of Brocéliande, buy a piece of Arthurian legend in the form of jewelry.

  • Breton cider: Take home a bottle of locally produced cider as a souvenir of your trip.

  • Pottery: Shop for traditional Breton pottery that is perfect for serving your next meal.

Local Culture and Customs of Brocéliande

Bretons are proud of their culture and language. Try learning a few common phrases to show respect and appreciation. There is also a great importance placed on communal gatherings and events, so be sure to join in if the opportunity arises.

Best Time to Visit Brocéliande

The best time to visit Brocéliande is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the forest is alive with blooming wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brocéliande

Do:

  • Respect the nature of the forest and take care not to disturb wildlife.

  • Learn a little bit of French to communicate with the locals.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and terrain you will be hiking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any trash behind in the forest.

  • Ignore signs and warnings from park rangers or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brocéliande

Brocéliande is generally safe for tourists. However, it is important to stay on marked trails and not wander off into uncharted areas of the forest. Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Brocéliande

There are no specific health risks associated with Brocéliande. However, it’s always a good idea to pack a small first aid kit on hikes, take plenty of water and sunscreen, and inform someone of your planned route for safety purposes.

Getting to Brocéliande: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brocéliande

  • Rennes Airport - 50 km away

  • Nantes Airport - 162 km away

Bus Stations Near Brocéliande

  • Gare Routière de Ploërmel

  • Gare Routière de Rennes

Other Options

  • Car rental is available from airports or train stations.

  • Private transfers can be arranged in advance.

How to Get Around in Brocéliande

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Brocéliande. However, bikes are also a popular option for getting around the forest and nearby towns such as Paimpont. Buses are also available but have limited schedules and routes.

Where to Go Next from Brocéliande: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saint-Malo: A beautiful walled city famous for its beaches, seafood, and maritime history.

  • Mont Saint Michel: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, with a stunningly large tidal island monastery.

Conclusion

Brocéliande is a place of magic and legend that has captured the imaginations of visitors for centuries. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, its natural beauty and rich cultural history make it a must-see destination. Plan your trip today and be prepared to fall under its enchanting spell.
