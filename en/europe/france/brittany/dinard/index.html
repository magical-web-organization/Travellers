Introduction to Dinard

Dinard, located in Brittany, France, is a beautiful coastal town known for its stunning beaches, historic architecture, and luxurious villas. With its mild climate and beautiful scenery, Dinard is a popular destination for both French and international tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Dinard

Dinard is known for its stunning architecture, beautiful beaches, and rich history. Here are some of the must-visit places in Dinard:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Malo Citadel
  • Dinard Museum of Arts and History
  • Villa Diodati

Natural Attractions of Dinard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage de l’Ecluse
  • Pointe du Moulinet
  • Promenade Clair de Lune

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dinard

Dinard has a number of beautiful hiking and biking trails that take you through some of the most scenic areas of the town. Some popular routes include:

  • The GR34 coastal path
  • The Emerald Coast bike trail
  • Bain-de-Bretagne hiking trail

Best Things to Do in Dinard: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities to do in Dinard, such as:

  • Taking a boat tour around the coastline
  • Exploring the town’s historic architecture on a walking tour
  • Trying your hand at windsurfing or kitesurfing
  • Catching a movie at the town’s historic movie theater, Cinéma Pleurtuit

Week-Long Itinerary for Dinard

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Dinard Museum of Arts and History Day 2: Take a guided tour of St. Malo Citadel and visit the Solidor Tower and Museum of Cape Horniers Day 3: Spend the day relaxing at Plage de l’Ecluse Day 4: Hike along the GR34 coastal path and visit Pointe du Moulinet Day 5: Spend the day trying out water sports like windsurfing or kitesurfing Day 6: Take a boat tour around the coastline and explore the beaches and coves Day 7: Visit the town’s historic movie theater, Cinéma Pleurtuit, or attend a local music or cultural festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Dinard

Dinard has a rich food and drink culture, with plenty of traditional dishes to try and local beverages to sample.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galettes: savory crepes made with buckwheat flour
  • Kouign-amann: a sweet pastry made with butter and sugar
  • Moules Marinières: mussels in a garlic and white wine sauce

Local Beverages

  • Cidre: a traditional Breton apple cider
  • Chouchen: a local mead made with honey
  • Breizh Cola: a regional cola made with Breton sugar

Where to Stay in Dinard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dinard has a range of options for travelers with different budgets and preferences.

  • Luxury hotels: Hotel Barriere Le Royal and Hotel Castelbrac
  • Boutique hotels: Hotel Didier Meril and Hotel Villa Reine Hortense
  • Budget options: Hostel Le Bois Du Barde and Hotel Alizes

What to Buy in Dinard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs to bring back from Dinard include:

  • Locally-made pottery and ceramic dishes
  • Breton striped shirts and other traditional clothing
  • Caramel au Beurre Salé: a popular salted caramel candy

Local Culture and Customs of Dinard

Like much of Brittany, Dinard has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, food, and music. The town is known for its annual music and cultural festivals, including the Dinard Jazz Festival and the Dinard Film Festival.

Best Time to Visit Dinard

The best time to visit Dinard is during the summer months between June and September when the weather is warmest and the activities and festivals are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dinard

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the galettes and seafood
  • Respect the town’s architecture and historic buildings
  • Attend one of the town’s annual festivals

Don’t:

  • Leave litter on the beaches or other public places
  • Speak loudly or be disruptive in public spaces
  • Ignore local customs and etiquette

Safety and Health

Safety in Dinard

Dinard is generally a safe place for tourists, however, it is always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Stick to well-lit areas at night and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Dinard

France has a high standard of healthcare and there are several hospitals and clinics in Dinard in case of emergency.

Getting to Dinard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dinard

The closest airport to Dinard is Dinard-Pleurtuit-Saint-Malo Airport, which is just a few kilometers from the town center. Other nearby airports include Rennes Airport and Nantes Atlantique Airport.

Bus Stations Near Dinard

The town has several bus stations, including Dinard Bus Station and nearby Saint-Malo Bus Station.

Other Options

You can also reach Dinard by train, with local trains stopping at the Dinard station, or by car via the A13 and A84 highways.

How to Get Around in Dinard

Dinard is a compact town and is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There are also local buses that connect the different neighborhoods and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Dinard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations to visit from Dinard include:

  • The walled city of Saint-Malo
  • The ancient city of Dinan
  • The island monastery of Mont Saint-Michel

Conclusion

Dinard is a beautiful coastal town with a rich cultural heritage, stunning beaches, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re interested in hiking, water sports, or simply exploring local history and culture, there is something for everyone in this charming town.
