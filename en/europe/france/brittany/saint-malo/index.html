Introduction to Saint-Malo

Located in the region of Brittany, in the northwest of France, Saint-Malo is a picturesque walled city that offers visitors a unique glimpse into its rich history. Known for its scenic beauty, fascinating historical landmarks, and delicious cuisine, Saint-Malo is a perfect place for both relaxation and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Malo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chateau de Saint-Malo: The castle is an imposing fortress from the 15th century, located right in the heart of the old town. The castle now houses the town museum and exhibits a collection of weaponry, pottery and fine arts, amongst other displays.
  • Cathedrale Saint-Vincent: This stunning Gothic cathedral is located in the heart of the city and dates back to the 12th century. The cathedral is famous for its beautiful rose windows and its impressive collection of religious artifacts.
  • La Maison de Jacques Cartier: This small museum explains the life and journeys of Jacques Cartier, a famous explorer who discovered Canada. Visitors can see tools, maps and other artefacts that Jacques Cartier used on his journeys to new lands.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Malo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage de Bon Secours: A beautiful white sand and shingle beach that stretches for over two kilometers, making it one of the largest beaches in Saint-Malo. An ideal spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • National Park of the Bay of Mont Saint-Michel: This park is home to a rich and diverse array of wildlife, including birds, seals and otters. The park offer excellent opportunities for hiking or cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Malo

  • GR34: Known locally as the “customs trail” this 200km path around Brittany’s coastline. The St-Malo section passes by rocky cliffs, sandy coves and pretty harbours and offers stunning views of sea, land and sky.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Malo: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Ramparts of Saint-Malo: Take a stroll around the imposing walls of the city to see breath-taking views of the sea and the surrounding areas
  • Visit the Aquarium and touch-tank: Admire over 10,000 species of marine life and have the chance to get up close with interactive feeding times. A great activity for families with kids.
  • Take a guided Tour of the Saint-Malo: Learn about the colourful past of Saint-Malo from the medieval times to its role in world war 2.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Malo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crêpes: Saint-Malo is famous for this sweet or savoury specialty that can be found in markets or restaurants all over the city. Try them with sugar and butter or cheese and ham for a quick snack.
  • Oysters: Enjoy fresh oysters from Cancale on the north of Saint-Malo, a delicacy in the region, the local varieties are famous throughout France.
  • Kouign-Amann: A Breton specialty, this buttery flaky pastry is perfect for a sweet breakfast or a quick snack.

Local Beverages

  • Cidre: A refreshing alcoholic beverage made from local apples for a sweet and fruity cider.
  • Chouchen: A local liquor made from honey and featuring a unique and intense taste, served after dinner or enjoy it as a cocktail.

Where to Stay in Saint-Malo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Malo has a wide selection of accommodation available from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Be sure to book in advance during the peak months of July and August to avoid disappointment.

Luxury Hotels

  • Grand Hotel des Thermes: A 5-star palace hotel with sea views and a luxurious spa and wellness centre.
  • Hotel Le Nouveau Monde: A 4-star hotel with modern styling, located just outside the city walls, with views of the sea.

Mid-Range Hotels and Guesthouses

  • Chez Anne & Mat: Centrally located, this guesthouse has been recently refurbished to include all modern amenities and a lovely owner.
  • La Maison des Armateurs: A stunning 4-star hotel housed in a classic 18th-century townhouse, only 300 meters from the beach.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Le D’Ici et D’Ailleurs: A quirky and fun hostel right in the heart of the old town of l Saint-Malo.
  • Hotel Cartier: A budget-friendly hotel in the center of Saint-Malo, only walking distance from the city’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Saint-Malo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Breton Striped Shirts: Made famous by sailors, these woolen blue and white striped T-shirts or jumpers should be at the top of your shopping list.
  • Handmade Paperweights: These beautiful and delicate paperweights are made from glassblowing techniques from the 19th century and are perfect as souvenirs or as gifts.
  • Local Cider: Buy some local apple cider from one of the farmer’s markets and enjoy it on the beach while watching the sunset.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Malo

The people of Saint-Malo are proud of their cultural heritage and preservation of centuries-old traditions. They are particularly protective of the city’s ramparts and medieval buildings. In day-to-day life, it is customary to greet someone with a “Bonjour monsieur/madame” and a kiss on each cheek.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Malo

The best time to visit Saint-Malo is from late May to early October when the weather is excellent, and the days are long. The peak season is from mid-June to mid-September when hotels and restaurants can be crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Malo

Do:

  • Try to speak a little French as this is always appreciated.
  • Wear comfortable shoes, as the city has plenty of cobblestone streets and hilly terrain.
  • Book your accommodation in advance during peak season.

Don’t:

  • Don’t make noise after 10 pm as the city’s residents take their surroundings seriously.
  • Don’t forget to explore outside of Saint-Malo too, as this town serves as a perfect base to explore other nearby attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Malo

Saint-Malo is generally a safe city, and violent crimes are rare. However, tourists should be mindful of pickpockets and to keep your personal belongings zipped up and in front of you in crowded places.

Health in Saint-Malo

No specific vaccinations or medical precautions are necessary for traveling to Saint-Malo. For any medical problems, visitors can access the many healthcare establishments in the city.

Getting to Saint-Malo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Malo

The nearest airport is Dinard-Pleurtuit-Saint-Malo airport (DNR), located just four kilometers south of Saint-Malo. Another option is Rennes Bretagne Airport, which is located about an hour and a half east from Saint-Malo.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Malo

Saint-Malo is a major bus hub in northwestern France, and multiple buses link it with various cities in France and beyond. The station is located near the city center, making it a convenient option.

Other Options

Another option is taking the train, the main train station is located just outside the walls of Old Town Saint-Malo.

How to Get Around in Saint-Malo

Saint-Malo is a relatively small city that is easy to navigate on foot. However, there is a public transportation system available called the Illenoo bus service that operates in the town and connects surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Malo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cancale: A port town famous for its fresh oysters, located just twelve kilometers from Saint-Malo.
  • Dinan: A charming medieval town 30 minutes inland, the town’s historic streets, walls, and gates have been beautifully preserved.

Conclusion

Saint-Malo is a charming, beautiful and historic city, perfect for a weekend getaway or a longer holiday. It has something for everyone from the delicious food, stunning scenery, and rich culture and history of Brittany. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this wonderful city.
