Introduction to Rennes

Rennes is the capital city of Brittany, France, offering a blend of history, culture, and modernity. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, gastronomical delights, and charming locals. Rennes’s vibrant energy and heartening ambiance have made it a popular tourist destination in France.

List of Places to Visit in Rennes

If you’re planning a trip to Rennes, here are some must-visit places you can’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Fine Arts - This museum houses a vast collection of European art from the 15th to the 21st century.

  2. Parlement of Brittany - This 17th-century building is a masterpiece of French Baroque architecture. It currently houses the Court of Appeal.

  3. Saint George Palace - The palace was built between 1670 and 1700 in the classical style and is a representation of some of the most remarkable achievements in French architecture in the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Rennes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Thabor Park - An elegant English-style public park showing Mother Nature’s beauty through stunning rose gardens, lakes, and 18th-century mansions.

  2. Canal Saint-Martin - Composed of about 150 km of navigable waterways and bordered by a towpath perfect for walking or cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rennes

Rennes is also a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The city has several trails that offer stunning views of Brittany’s breathtaking beauty and European history. The most popular routes are:

  1. The GR39 - Stretching from Saint-Malo to Tregastel, this hiking trail follows the Emerald Coast and reveals some of the region’s most spectacular scenery, including a beautiful forest between Les Iffs and Montfort-sur-Meu.

  2. GR347 Rennes to Nantes - A long-distance hike that traverses Brittany and covers 165 km, taking hikers on a journey through unforgettable landscapes, such as the region’s lakes, rivers, and local vineyards.

Best Things to Do in Rennes: Activities and Tours

  1. Discover the local food culture - Food lovers can enjoy French gourmet cuisine with a unique Breton flair. Savor the best traditional dishes, including galettes, seafood, and crepes.

  2. Go on a city tour - Explore the city’s rich cultural heritage by taking a walk, bike ride, or guided tour of the town’s most iconic landmarks and historical sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rennes

Here’s a week-long itinerary for your trip to Rennes:

Day 1 Visit the Parlement of Brittany and the Museum of Fine Arts
Day 2 Take a city tour and explore the old town of Rennes
Day 3 Spend a day outdoors at Thabor Park or the Canal Saint-Martin-route
Day 4 Visit the Saint George Palace in the morning and then explore the shopping district of Rennes in the afternoon
Day 5 Go on a biking/ hiking trail. For a more scenic view of the city, ride towards the canal de Nantes a Brest.
Day 6 Experience the local food culture and savor the best traditional dishes, including galettes and seafood.
Day 7 Take a day trip to Mont Saint Michel and experience France’s most iconic landmark.

Food and Drink Scene of Rennes

Rennes is famous for its gastronomical delights, and one can savor the best traditional flavors in the following:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Galette - The most famous dish from Brittany, made of buckwheat flour and stuffed with either savory (salty) or sweet ingredients like ham, cheese, and egg.

  2. Seafood - The port city of St-Malo is famous for its seafood. Places like Avel and La Cabane à Huîtres near the covered market are great to try out some fresh oysters, crab, and scallops.

Local Beverages

  1. Cidre - An apple-based alcoholic drink available in several different varieties from sweet to dry.

  2. Chouchen - A traditional alcoholic drink produced from fermenting honey and yeast.

Where to Stay in Rennes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rennes offers a wide range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, with prices ranging from budget to luxury. Here are some of the top-rated accommodations to stay in Rennes.

  1. Balthazar Hotel & Spa Rennes - This 5-star hotel offers luxurious rooms, a swimming pool, a spa, and an excellent on-site restaurant.

  2. Arvor - Located in the historic center of Rennes, this family-run hotel offers cosy rooms with a vintage French interior and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Rennes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rennes is known for its beautiful tapestries, Breton fishermen shirts, and ceramics. The following are some items you can take back home as souvenirs.

  1. Tapestries from workshop Pinton - The finest wool tapestries in France.

  2. Hand-painted ceramic plates and cups from the Pottery of Rennes - perfect for a classy French touch in your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Rennes

Rennes has a rich cultural heritage with a unique Breton influence. The locals pride themselves on their intellectual and cultural life, the city’s heritage, history, and unique way of life. St. Malo, Dinard, and Cape Fréhel express this distinct way of life, adapting to and arranging nature.

Best Time to Visit Rennes

The best time to visit Rennes is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and accommodation rates are lower. Summers can be hot, but few tourists come to Brittany. Winter, on the other hand, sees the least number of tourists with unpleasant temperatures, but the city is still worth the visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rennes

Do:

  1. Try the local food and drinks

  2. Explore the city by foot, bike, or public transport

Don’t:

  1. Litter or smoke in public spaces

  2. Disrespect the local culture or customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Rennes

Rennes is a safe city for tourists. However, like any urban destination, it’s best to be aware of your surroundings and avoid flaunting your valuables.

Health in Rennes

Rennes boasts of excellent healthcare services. In case of any medical emergencies, call 15 (emergency medical services) or visit to the nearest hospital which will attend to your immediate medical needs.

Getting to Rennes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rennes

Rennes International Airport is the main airport in the city and is served by several major airlines, such as Air France, Ryanair, and Flybe.

Bus Stations Near Rennes

The Gare Routière de Rennes is the main bus station in the city and operates several bus routes to and from Rennes and other destinations in France and Europe.

Other Options

One can also opt to take a train to Rennes, which is better suited for long-distance travel.

How to Get Around Rennes

Rennes is a walkable, cycle-friendly city. The city also has an efficient public transport system that includes buses and metro. Travelers can use the bus and metro by buying a ticket from a machine or a convenience store.

Where to Go Next from Rennes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Saint-Malo - A beautiful port city with a fantastic view of the English Channel and old town streets inhabited with medieval fortresses and ramparts and food delicacies such as oysters.

  2. Mont Saint Michel - A UNESCO World Heritage site and an iconic landmark in France, it consists of a small village surrounding an ancient abbey, with a beautiful panorama of the bay.

Conclusion

Rennes is an excellent destination for those who love French culture and history. With its unique blend of traditional and modern culture, gastronomical delights, charming locals, and scenic beauty, Rennes is sure to leave a lasting impression on your heart. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to this fantastic city so that you can experience its unique way of life to the fullest.
