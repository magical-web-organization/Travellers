List of Places to Visit in Lorient

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Submarine Base

The Submarine Base is one of the most iconic sites in Lorient and is now being reused as a cultural venue. Massive bunkers do not only provide a glimpse into Lorient’s wartime history, but also host contemporary art exhibitions, concerts, and festivals throughout the year.

Eric Tabarly Sailing Museum

Located on the right bank of the Scorff River, this museum is dedicated to portraying the works and achievements of French sailor Eric Tabarly, who was born in Lorient. From his first multihull boat to the famous Pen Duick VI, visitors can dive into his impressive career and get a glimpse of some historic sailing pieces.

Natural Attractions of Lorient: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Groix Island

Undoubtedly, Groix Island is the top natural attraction near Lorient. Famous for its emerald waters, secluded beaches, and challenging cliff walks, the island is an excellent place to spend a day and enjoy the great outdoors.

Kerhinet Marshes

Kerhinet Marshes offer visitors the perfect opportunity to enjoy the scenery of Lorient’s natural environment. Walk through the nature reserve for a chance to spot rare and unique birdlife, and don’t forget your camera, as the photo opportunities are endless.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lorient

The Coastal Trail

Lorient’s Coastal Trail is one of the most scenic routes in the city, running along the entire coast for 45 kilometers. Follow the path to enjoy breathtaking views of the sea, sand dunes, cliffs, and countryside along your way.

Best Things to Do in Lorient: Activities and Tours

Market Day

Experience the most authentic side of French life at Lorient’s street market which takes place twice a week. Visitors can take in the sights, sounds and smells of the bustling market, picking up some unique souvenirs, and fresh produce.

Boat Tours

Take a relaxing boat tour down the Scorff River and experience Brittany from a whole new angle. The area’s rich maritime history is on display, and the tour guide will happily share local stories and fun facts about the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lorient

Day 1 Visit The Submarine Base and Eric Tabarly Sailing Museum
Day 2 Spend the day exploring the Groix Island
Day 3 Visit the Kerhinet Marshes and take a boat tour
Day 4 Shop in Lorient’s market streets and visit the La Thalassa Museum
Day 5 Spend the day relaxing on Larmor Beach followed by a coastal walk
Day 6 Explore the city center and visit the Palace of Congress
Day 7 Enjoy a picnic in the Park of Lorient before leaving town

Food and Drink Scene of Lorient

Lorient’s vibrant food and drink scene offer a true taste of the region. Whether you’re looking to try the local seafood, traditional crepes or a simple baguette, Lorient has it all.

Must-Try Dishes

Kouign-Amann

One of Brittany’s most iconic desserts, Kouign-Amann, is a tender, buttery pastry that’s caramelized in sugar. It’s a must-try when in Lorient.

Local Beverages

Cider

Brittany is famous for its locally brewed cider. Whether sweet or dry, locals take their cider seriously, and it pairs well with many of the local dishes.

Where to Stay in Lorient: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lorient offers a range of accommodations options that will suit different preferences and budgets.

Best Budget Option: Les Gens de Mer

Les Gens de Mer, located in the heart of the city, offers a comfortable and friendly stay at an affordable price. It’s a 10-minute walk to the marina, and the hotel also offers bike rentals to explore the area.

Best Mid-Range Option: Mercure Lorient Centre

The Mercure Lorient Centre is a popular four-star hotel, located in the business district of Lorient. Clean and modern rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a fitness center make this the perfect choice for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Lorient: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Lorient, don’t forget to bring home a few treasures for your loved ones or as a reminder of your trip. Not to mention, the local handmade crafts and souvenirs also support the community.

Local Handicrafts

Lorient is known for its traditional silver-work, hand-woven linen, and locally produced pottery. Local markets are the perfect place to buy unique pieces to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lorient

Norms and customs may differ from your home country, so it’s important to be respectful of local traditions. Farmers markets and festivals are an essential part of the community, and supporting local businesses and products is highly valued.

Best Time to Visit Lorient

The best time to visit Lorient is during the summer months when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, this also means that Lorient will be busier during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lorient

Do:

  • Visit the local farmers market
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Ignore the importance of local marine life
  • Be disrespectful of local culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Lorient

Lorient is a safe place to visit and enjoy, however, visitors should always take precautions to ensure their safety is maintained throughout their trip. It’s important to be aware of potential pickpockets in crowded areas and to make use of hotel safes to secure personal belongings.

Health in Lorient

France is known for its high-quality healthcare and tourists can have peace of mind knowing that medical facilities are easily accessible in Lorient.

Getting to Lorient: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lorient

The closest airport to Lorient is Lorient Bretagne Sud Airport (LRT). However, flights are limited.

Bus Stations Near Lorient

The Lorient bus station is located in the city center, making it an accessible transportation option for tourists.

Other Options

Driving is always an option, with numerous highways linking Lorient to other major French cities.

How to Get Around in Lorient

Lorient is a very walkable city, so visitors can explore on foot easily. However, rentals, such as bicycles or cars, are also readily available from local vendors for those looking for a faster or more comfortable option.

Where to Go Next from Lorient: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Carnac

Known for its rows of standing stones, Carnac is a short drive from Lorient and well worth a visit for those interested in historic monuments.

Quiberon

For a relaxing day trip, Quiberon offers visitors a tranquil seaside getaway filled with lush scenery and plenty of nature walks.

Conclusion

Lorient is a destination that encompasses rich history, picturesque settings, and mouth-watering cuisine. With a range of attractions and activities available, Lorient is equally suitable for families, couples, and solo travelers. Start planning your unforgettable trip to Lorient today!
