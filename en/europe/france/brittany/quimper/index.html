Introduction to Quimper

Located in the region of Brittany, Quimper is a charming city with a rich history and culture. The city is well-known for its beautiful Gothic cathedral, traditional Breton architecture, and vibrant art scene. Quimper is also a great base from which to explore the stunning coastline of Brittany. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about Quimper.

List of Places to Visit in Quimper

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cathédrale Saint-Corentin: This stunning Gothic cathedral is one of the most famous landmarks in Quimper. The cathedral is located in the heart of the city and is well-worth a visit.

  • Musée des Beaux-Arts de Quimper: This art museum features an impressive collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts from the 14th to the 21st century.

  • Maison de la Faïence: This museum showcases the history and art of Quimper’s famous faïence pottery. Visitors can see a variety of pottery pieces, from traditional Breton designs to contemporary pieces.

  • Château de Keriolet: A stunning castle built in the 19th century, the Château de Keriolet is now a museum. Visitors can see the furnished rooms of the castle and learn about the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Quimper: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rivière de l’Odet: Known as the most beautiful river in France, the Odet river runs through Quimper and provides scenic views and opportunities for fishing and boating.

  • L’Île-Tudy: A small island located off the coast of Brittany, L’Île-Tudy is a beautiful getaway for those looking for a relaxing day by the sea. The island is home to picturesque beaches and a quaint fishing village.

  • Bois du Poërop: Located just outside of Quimper, this forest offers hiking and biking trails through beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quimper

  • La boucle des rivières (the river loop): This six-mile loop begins at the Odet river and takes visitors through beautiful forests and countryside.

  • Le circuit des chapelles (the chapel circuit): This eight-mile circuit takes visitors through the countryside surrounding Quimper and past several historic chapels.

Best Things to Do in Quimper: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of Quimper’s old town: Visitors can explore the cobbled streets and traditional Breton architecture of Quimper’s historic old town.

  • Visit the Quimper market: Held every Saturday morning, the market is the perfect place to sample local produce and products.

  • Attend a Breton music and dance festival: Quimper is known for its vibrant music and dance culture, with many festivals held throughout the year.

Food and Drink Scene of Quimper

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kouign-Amann: This Breton pastry is made with puff pastry and dough folded in layers, then sweetened and caramelized.

  • Galette Complète: A savory buckwheat crepe filled with ham, cheese, and an egg.

  • Cidre: A popular cider made from Breton apples.

Local Beverages

  • Chouchenn: A traditional Breton mead made with honey.

  • Lambig: A fortified brandy made from Breton cider.

Where to Stay in Quimper: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel Gradlon: Located in the heart of the historic old town, this hotel is perfect for those looking to explore the city on foot.

  • Le Logis du Stang: This cozy guesthouse is located just outside of Quimper and offers peaceful gardens and countryside surroundings.

  • Auberge de Jeunesse de Quimper: This youth hostel is located in the city center and is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Quimper: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Faïence Pottery: Quimper’s famous faïence pottery is a must-buy for anyone visiting the city. Look for pieces with traditional Breton designs.

  • Cider: Many local cider producers sell bottles of their delicious beverages at markets and stores throughout Quimper.

Local Culture and Customs of Quimper

  • Breton Dance: Breton dance is a popular pastime in Quimper and visitors may have the chance to see a live performance during their visit.

  • French Language: Although English is spoken at many tourist attractions, it is always appreciated when visitors attempt to speak French.

Best Time to Visit Quimper

The best time to visit Quimper is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and the city hosts many festivals and events. However, spring and fall can also be beautiful times to visit, with fewer crowds and pleasant temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quimper

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Take a walking tour of the old town.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local language and culture.

  • Be loud and disruptive in public.

  • Visit historic sites without proper respect and decorum.

Safety and Health

Safety in Quimper

Quimper is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take normal safety precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Quimper

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to France. Tap water is considered safe to drink in Quimper.

Getting to Quimper: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Quimper

  • Quimper-Cornouaille Airport (UIP)

  • Brest Bretagne Airport (BES)

Bus Stations Near Quimper

  • Quimper Bus Station

  • Gare Routière de Quimper

Other Options

  • Train: Quimper train station is well-connected to other cities in France and Europe.

  • Rental car: Visitors may also choose to rent a car to explore Quimper and the surrounding area on their own.

How to Get Around in Quimper

Quimper is a relatively small city and is best explored on foot. However, visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and taxis, to get around.

Where to Go Next from Quimper: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pont-Aven: This charming village is known for its art scene and is located just a short drive from Quimper.

  • Concarneau: A picturesque fishing village located on the coast of Brittany.

  • Pointe du Raz: A stunning peninsula located at the westernmost point of Brittany.

Conclusion

Quimper is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Brittany. With its stunning architecture, rich culture, and beautiful natural surroundings, there’s something for everyone in Quimper. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to this beautiful city.
