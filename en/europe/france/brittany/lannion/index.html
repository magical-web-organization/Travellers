Introduction to Lannion

Lannion is a charming city located in Brittany, the northwest region of France. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cultural scene, Lannion is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the treasures of France. In this guide, we will take you through the top attractions, activities, and experiences that Lannion has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Lannion

Lannion has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its many attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in and around the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Centre of Lannion: Take a stroll through the historic center of Lannion, where you can see medieval houses, timber-framed buildings, and the Church of Brelevenez.
  • Château de Tonquédec: Explore the ruins of this medieval fortress and its beautiful surrounding park.
  • Musée des telecoms: Discover the history of French telecommunications at this interactive museum.

Natural Attractions of Lannion: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sentier de Douaniers: Go for a hike along this coastal path, which offers stunning sea views and rugged landscapes.
  • Île-Grande: Rent a bike and explore this beautiful island, which boasts gorgeous beaches, woodland trails, and quaint fishing villages.
  • Lannion Bay: Take a boat tour or go kayaking in this large bay, which is home to many species of seabirds and marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lannion

Lannion has many fantastic trails and bike routes that allow visitors to explore the stunning natural scenery of the region. Here are some of the best:

  • GR34: This long-distance coastal trail stretches over 1,200 kilometers along the Brittany coastline, offering breathtaking views of the sea and coastal landscapes.
  • VéloRoute Vallée du Léguer: Follow this cycle route along the river valley of Léguer, passing through charming Breton villages and countryside.

Best Things to Do in Lannion: Activities and Tours

Lannion offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit all interests. Here are some of the best:

  • Boat Tour to the Île de Bréhat: Take a boat tour to visit the picturesque Île de Bréhat, an idyllic island with a Mediterranean feel.
  • Breton Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Breton dishes using fresh, local ingredients at a cooking class.
  • Wine Tasting at the Cellier de la Ville Gilles: Sample some of the best wines of the region at this cellar, and learn about the local wine-making traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lannion

If you have a week to spend in Lannion, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
1 Explore the historic center of Lannion and visit the Church of Brelevenez
2 Hike the Sentier des Douaniers and explore the surrounding coastline
3 Visit the Château de Tonquédec and the Musée des telecoms
4 Tour the Île de Bréhat by boat and enjoy a seafood lunch
5 Take a cooking class and prepare traditional Breton dishes
6 Cycle the VéloRoute Vallée du Léguer and visit the Cellier de la Ville Gilles
7 Relax on the beaches of Île-Grande and watch the sunset over Lannion Bay

Food and Drink Scene of Lannion

Annion is a paradise for foodies, with a rich culinary heritage and a variety of fresh, local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crêpes: These thin, delicious pancakes come in both sweet and savory varieties, and are a staple of Breton cuisine.
  • Sausages and charcuterie: Brittany is known for its flavorful sausages and cured meats, which are often served with traditional Breton cider or beer.
  • Seafood: Lannion is located near the coast and offers a bounty of fresh seafood, including oysters, mussels, and lobster.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: This fermented apple drink is a staple of Breton cuisine and comes in sweet and dry varieties.
  • Beer: Brittany has a long tradition of brewing beer, with many small breweries producing unique and flavorful varieties.
  • Wine: The region around Lannion produces some excellent wines, including fruity reds and crisp whites.

Where to Stay in Lannion: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lannion offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best:

  • Hôtel des 4 vents: A cozy, family-run hotel located in the heart of Lannion.
  • Auberge Le Melezin: A charming guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a lovely garden.
  • Le Goëlo: A modern hotel located near the beach of Île-Grande.

What to Buy in Lannion: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lannion is known for its traditional crafts and local products, which make great souvenirs to bring home. Here are some of the best:

  • Pottery: Brittany has a long tradition of pottery-making, and Lannion has many ceramic artists producing beautiful pieces.
  • Lace: Lannion is the center of the “dentelle de Lannion” tradition, famous for its delicate lacework.
  • Salted caramels: These delicious caramels are made using Breton sea salt, and make a sweet and savory treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Lannion

Lannion has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its history and location in Brittany. Breton culture is distinct from the rest of France, with its own language, music, and customs. Visitors to Lannion can experience this unique culture through its festivals, music events, and culinary traditions.

Best Time to Visit Lannion

The best time to visit Lannion is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Lannion is a popular destination during this time, so be sure to book accommodation and tours in advance. The shoulder season, from April to May and September to October, also offers mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lannion

To make the most of your visit to Lannion, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Breton crêpes, cider, and seafood.
  • Learn basic French phrases, as Breton is still spoken in some parts of the region.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, including the Breton language and cultural festivals.

Don’t:

  • Skip the sunscreen, as the Brittany sun can be strong even on cloudy days.
  • Expect shops and restaurants to be open all day long, as many businesses close for a midday break.
  • Use English as your first language when interacting with locals, as this can be seen as disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Lannion is a safe destination for travelers, with low rates of crime and violence. However, visitors should be aware of the following safety and health considerations:

Safety in Lannion

  • Always lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Beware of pickpockets and keep your valuables close in crowded areas.

Health in Lannion

  • Make sure to have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.
  • Use insect repellent and take other precautions to avoid tick bites, which can transmit Lyme disease.
  • Drink bottled water or boil tap water before consumption, as tap water may contain harmful bacteria.

Getting to Lannion: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lannion is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some of the best options:

Airports Near Lannion

  • Lannion-Côte de Granit Airport: Located in the nearby town of Servel, this small regional airport offers flights to several destinations in France and the UK.

Bus Stations Near Lannion

  • Lannion Bus Station: Located in the city center, this bus station is served by several regional and national bus operators.

Other Options

  • Train: Trains from Paris and other major French cities arrive at nearby stations such as Guingamp, Morlaix, and Saint-Brieuc.
  • Car: Lannion is located near several major highways, making it easy to access by car.

How to Get Around in Lannion

Lannion has a good public transportation system, including buses and taxis. However, to explore the region more thoroughly, it is best to rent a car or bike. Bikes can be rented from various companies in Lannion, and car rentals are available at the airport and in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Lannion: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lannion is situated in an excellent location for day trips and excursions to nearby destinations. Here are some of the best:

  • Perros-Guirec: This picturesque seaside town is just a short drive from Lannion, and offers beautiful beaches and stunning coastal landscapes.
  • Ploumanac’h: Visit the Pink Granite Coast, known for its unique rock formations and crystal-clear waters.
  • Île de Bréhat: This idyllic island can be reached by boat from nearby ports, and offers charming villages, beautiful beaches, and stunning sea views.

Conclusion

Lannion is a vibrant and fascinating destination that offers visitors a unique taste of Breton culture and history. With its beautiful landscapes, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Lannion is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the wonders of France.
