Bédée is a charming small town located in Brittany, France. Nestled in lush greenery, Bédée is a tranquil haven that boasts a plethora of attractions and exciting activities. From museums to natural wonders, hiking routes to delicious local dishes, Bédée is the perfect destination for those looking to discover a slice of authentic France. Introduction to Bédée

Bédée is a small town located in the department of Ille-et-Vilaine in Brittany, France. The town is situated at the foothills of the Monts d’Arrée mountain range, which means that it is surrounded by raw natural beauty. Bédée boasts a rich and fascinating history, with archaeological evidence suggesting that the town has been inhabited since prehistoric times. Bédée is also famous for its religious heritage, with a cluster of chapels, old churches, and abbeys dotted throughout the town.

List of Places to Visit in Bédée

Whether you are a history enthusiast, nature lover, or adventure seeker, there is something for everyone in Bédée. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bédée:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abbaye Notre-Dame de Bon-Repos - A beautifully preserved 12th-century abbey that is steeped in history and culture. The abbey is home to a museum that houses a fascinating collection of medieval artifacts.

  • Château du Rocher-Portail - A stunning castle that dates back to the 15th century. The castle boasts impressive architecture and is surrounded by lush green gardens.

  • Chapelle Saint-Jacques - A beautiful chapel that is dedicated to Saint James. The chapel is located in the heart of Bédée and is a great place to soak up the town’s religious heritage.

Natural Attractions of Bédée: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Forêt de Brocéliande - A sprawling forest that is said to have been the inspiration for many of the tales of King Arthur and the Knights of the Round Table. The forest is home to a network of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Val sans Retour - A picturesque valley that is steeped in legend and folklore. The valley is said to be where Morgana imprisoned her lovers, and is home to some of the most stunning views in Brittany.

  • Lac de Trémelin - A freshwater lake that is a great spot for swimming, kayaking and paddleboarding. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery, making it the perfect place to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bédée

Bédée is the perfect destination for those looking to explore the great outdoors. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Bédée:

  • GR37 Hiking Trail - A 95-kilometer hiking trail that passes through Bédée. The trail takes you through beautiful countryside, rolling hills, and pretty villages.

  • La Voie Verte - A 50-kilometer biking trail that runs from Bédée to Dinan. The trail takes you through scenic countryside and past historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Bédée: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for adventure or relaxation, there are plenty of things to do in Bédée. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides - Experience the stunning natural beauty of Bédée from a whole new perspective with a hot air balloon ride.

  • Cider Tasting Tours - Brittany is famous for its cider, and there are plenty of farms in and around Bédée that offer cider tastings and tours.

  • Fishing - Bédée is home to several rivers and lakes that are teeming with fish. Whether you are a seasoned angler or a beginner, there are plenty of opportunities to cast your line and try your luck.

Food and Drink Scene of Bédée

One of the highlights of a trip to Bédée is the delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crêpes Bretonnes - Thin pancakes that are made with buckwheat flour and served savory or sweet.

  • Galette Saucisse - A typical Breton street food made with a pork sausage wrapped in a crêpe.

  • Far Breton - A traditional dessert that is similar to a clafoutis, made with prunes and custard.

Local Beverages

  • Cider - Brittany is famous for its cider, and there are plenty of farms in and around Bédée that produce and sell cider.

  • Kir Breton - A refreshing cocktail made with cider and crème de cassis.

  • Chouchen - A traditional Breton honey wine that is sweet and fruity.

Where to Stay in Bédée: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bédée offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from charming guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top places to stay in Bédée:

  • Le Clos du Tay - A beautiful guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms in a peaceful setting.

  • Manoir de Bodion - A stunning 15th-century manor that has been converted into a luxurious hotel.

  • Domaine des Doucins & Spa - A beautiful spa hotel that is set in lush greenery.

What to Buy in Bédée: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bédée offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for loved ones or keepsakes to remind you of your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • Breton Hats - Traditional Breton hats are a popular souvenir and come in a range of styles and colors.

  • Pottery and Ceramics - Brittany is also famous for its pottery and ceramics, and there are several local artisans in Bédée who produce beautiful pieces.

  • Cider - As mentioned before, Brittany is famous for its cider, so why not bring a bottle or two back home?

Local Culture and Customs of Bédée

Bédée, like many towns in Brittany, has a rich and distinct cultural heritage. Breton customs and traditions are still prevalent in the town, and visitors can expect to witness traditional music, dance, and festivals throughout the year. Breton is also spoken in Bédée, although most locals can also speak French.

Best Time to Visit Bédée

The best time to visit Bédée is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is warm and pleasant, and the town comes alive with festivals and events. September and October are also good months to visit if you want to avoid the crowds of the high season while still enjoying mild weather and beautiful autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bédée

Do:

  • Try Breton cuisine and cider.

  • Explore the great outdoors and go hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • litter or damage the environment.

  • disrespect religious sites or buildings.

  • be too loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bédée

Bédée is a safe town with very low crime rates. However, it is always important to be vigilant and take basic precautions to ensure your safety, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bédée

France has a high-quality healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in and around Bédée. Visitors from the EU can use their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) to access medical treatment. Visitors from outside the EU are advised to purchase travel insurance that includes medical coverage.

Getting to Bédée: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bédée is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. The closest airports are Rennes and Nantes, which are both a little over an hour’s drive from Bédée. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Bédée

  • Rennes Airport - About a 40-minute drive from Bédée.

  • Nantes Airport - About a 90-minute drive from Bédée.

Bus Stations Near Bédée

  • Rennes Bus Station - About a 30-minute drive from Bédée.

  • Josselin Bus Station - About a 30-minute drive from Bédée.

Other Options

  • Train - The closest train station is in Rennes, which is about a 30-minute drive from Bédée.

  • Car Rental - Car rental companies are available at the airports and train stations.

How to Get Around in Bédée

The best way to explore Bédée is on foot or by bike. The town is small and easy to navigate, and there are plenty of scenic routes to discover. If you want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Bédée: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bédée is located in the heart of Brittany, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some ideas:

  • Dinan - A charming medieval town that is famous for its historic landmarks and beautiful architecture.

  • Saint-Malo - A picturesque coastal town that is famous for its beaches and rich maritime history.

  • Mont Saint-Michel - A stunning 8th-century abbey that is located on a small island overlooking the sea.
