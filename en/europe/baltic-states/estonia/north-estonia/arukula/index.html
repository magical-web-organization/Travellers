Introduction to Aruküla

Aruküla is a small picturesque village located in the northern part of Estonia. Surrounded by forests, hills, and rivers, Aruküla offers visitors an opportunity to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy the tranquil beauty of the countryside. Despite being a small village, Aruküla has plenty to offer, including scenic views, outdoor adventures, museums, local cuisine, and more. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Aruküla.

List of Places to Visit in Aruküla

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or nature, there is something for everyone in Aruküla. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Aruküla:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aruküla Church: Built in the 14th century, the Aruküla Church is one of the oldest and most significant cultural landmarks in the village. The church is open to visitors and offers guided tours.
  • Museum of Villem Reiman: Villem Reiman was a famous Estonian writer who lived in Aruküla. The museum is dedicated to his life and works, and visitors can learn about his life and legacy.

Natural Attractions of Aruküla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aruküla River: The Aruküla River runs through the village, offering visitors a chance to enjoy kayaking, fishing, and swimming in the summer.
  • Kiva Mountain: Kiva Mountain is a popular hiking destination, offering scenic views of the village and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aruküla

Aruküla has a variety of hiking and biking routes available for nature enthusiasts. One of the most popular routes is the Kiva Mountain trail, which offers breathtaking views of the village and surrounding countryside. Another popular trail is the Aruküla River Trail, which follows the river from the village to the nearby town of Kuusalu.

Best Things to Do in Aruküla: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding: There are several horseback riding tours available in Aruküla, offering visitors a chance to explore the countryside on horseback.
  • Kayaking: The Aruküla River is a popular kayaking destination, with several tours available for visitors.
  • Villem Reiman Museum tour: The Villem Reiman Museum offers guided tours, where visitors can learn about the life and works of the famous Estonian writer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aruküla

For those who want to experience all that Aruküla has to offer, here is a week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Aruküla, check into your hotel or guesthouse.
  • Day 2: Visit the Aruküla Church and Museum of Villem Reiman.
  • Day 3: Take a hiking or biking tour of Kiva Mountain.
  • Day 4: Go kayaking on the Aruküla River.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Villem Reiman Museum.
  • Day 6: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine.
  • Day 7: Departure from Aruküla.

Food and Drink Scene of Aruküla

Apart from the scenic beauty, Aruküla also has a rich culinary heritage. The local cuisine is a blend of traditional Estonian and Russian dishes, with an emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kama: A traditional Estonian dish made from roasted barley, rye, and wheat flour.
  • Pirukas: A savory pastry filled with meat, vegetables, or cheese.
  • Vareenikud: Similar to pierogi, these are dumplings filled with potato, cheese, or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Aruküla Beer: A local specialty, Aruküla beer is brewed using traditional methods and local ingredients.
  • Vana Tallinn: A sweet liqueur made from rum and various spices.

Where to Stay in Aruküla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in and around Aruküla, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Kau Manor: A luxurious manor house hotel located just outside of Aruküla.
  • Aruküla Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the center of the village.
  • Aruküla Guesthouse: A family-run guesthouse with a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Aruküla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Aruküla can bring home a piece of the village’s rich cultural heritage by purchasing local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the popular items:

  • Handmade pottery: Aruküla is known for its traditional handmade pottery, which makes for a unique and memorable souvenir.
  • Local honey: The honey produced in the surrounding countryside is known for its rich flavor and high quality.
  • Villem Reiman books: Visitors can purchase books by the famous Estonian writer at the museum dedicated to his life and works.

Local Culture and Customs of Aruküla

Aruküla has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of Estonian and Russian traditions. Visitors can explore the traditional dress, folk music, and dance of the region at local festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Aruküla

The best time to visit Aruküla is during the summer months, from May to August, when the weather is warm and the countryside is in full bloom. However, visitors can also enjoy the picturesque beauty of the village in the winter, when the snow-covered landscape offers a different kind of charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aruküla

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take care of the environment while enjoying outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect local people or customs.
  • Take any artifacts or souvenirs from historical sites or museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aruküla

Aruküla is a safe and peaceful village, with very low crime rates. Visitors should still take normal precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unsafe areas at night.

Health in Aruküla

There are several medical facilities in and around Aruküla, including clinics and hospitals. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Aruküla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aruküla

The closest airport to Aruküla is Tallinn Airport, which is approximately 45 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or shuttle bus from the airport to Aruküla.

Bus Stations Near Aruküla

There are several bus stations in nearby towns, such as Kuusalu and Kose, that offer connections to Aruküla. The bus is the most convenient way to reach the village.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Aruküla by car or bike, taking advantage of the well-maintained road network.

How to Get Around in Aruküla

The best way to get around Aruküla is either by car or bike. A car rental or bike hire is available in the village, and visitors can explore the surrounding countryside at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Aruküla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Aruküla, including:

  • Lahemaa National Park: A scenic national park with forests, lakes, and historic manor houses, located approximately 20 km from Aruküla.
  • Tallinn: The capital city of Estonia, with a rich history and vibrant cultural scene. Tallinn is approximately 45 km from Aruküla.

Conclusion

Aruküla offers visitors an opportunity to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy the peaceful beauty of the Estonian countryside. With a rich cultural heritage, picturesque landscapes, and a variety of outdoor activities, Aruküla is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies alike.
