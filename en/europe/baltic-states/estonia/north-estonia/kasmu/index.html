Introduction to Käsmu

Käsmu is a picturesque village situated in the Lahemaa National Park on North Estonia’s coast. This area is often referred to as the Estonian Riviera, thanks to its stunning landscapes and beautiful set of sea bays. Käsmu is known as the “captain’s village,” which dates back to the early 19th century, when shipbuilding and shipping became popular in the region.

Käsmu has a rich history and culture, dating back to the Middle Ages, and it has become a popular tourist destination in recent years, offering visitors a wide range of attractions and activities. In this travel guide, we will explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, and the delicious local cuisine of Käsmu.

List of Places to Visit in Käsmu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Käsmu Maritime Museum: Learn about the rich maritime history of Käsmu through various displays and exhibitions. Experience the life and work of the village’s captains and fishermen and explore the nautical collections featuring artifacts such as ship models and navigation tools.

  • Käsmu Lighthouse: The lighthouse is one of the village’s landmarks, standing tall on the top of the hill with breathtaking coastal views. The lighthouse’s story dates back to the 17th century, when an aged shepherd woman became the keeper of the light, keeping it burning for 45 years.

  • Käsmu Church: A beautiful wooden church that has been in use since the beginning of the 20th century. Its classic wooden architecture is influenced by the shipbuilding techniques which make the shipbuilding image.

Natural Attractions of Käsmu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Viru bog trail: Take a walking tour of the Viru bog trail, a fantastic natural marvel in the village area. This is a unique place with plank walks above the bog pond, where visitors can explore the vast Estonian wild nature.

  • Altja village: Altja is a well-preserved coastal fishing village with several museums, historical sites, and exhibits. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque landscapes, seafood restaurants, and stunning views of the Altja Bay.

  • Oandu Forest: Oandu forest is known for its hiking, nature trails, and stunning views of the sea. The forest offers visitors the opportunity to explore the flora and fauna of the region with minimal environmental impact.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Käsmu

  • Käsmu Coastal Trail: The Käsmu coastal trail is a beautiful 5.5 km hiking/biking trail that takes visitors along the spectacular coastline of the Estonian Riviera. The trail offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding nature, and it is suitable for all levels of hikers and bikers.

  • Oandu-Ikla hiking trail: This 370 km hiking/biking trail connects the Oandu trail to the Ikla border crossing with Latvia. This is one of the most extensive hiking trails in Estonia, offering visitors the opportunity to explore diverse landscapes, ecosystems, and historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Käsmu: Activities and Tours

  • Boat trip: A boat trip is a fantastic way to explore the magnificent sea views of the Estonian Riviera. Visitors can get an up-close view of the village from the sea and experience the life of the Käsmu fishermen.

  • Fishing trip: Fishing is a popular hobby in Käsmu, and visitors can have a unique opportunity to catch fish like perch and cod with the help of local fishermen.

  • Kayaking: Kayaking is a unique way to explore the beautiful bays, coves, and inlets of Käsmu. A guided tour will provide visitors with an insight into the marine life and ecosystems of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Käsmu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast potatoes with dill sauce: a traditional and simple dish of oven-roasted potatoes with a creamy dill sauce, made with fresh dill, sour cream, and a little bit of sugar.

  • Smoked fish platter: Käsmu is famous for its smoked fish platters, including smoked salmon, trout, and sea-bass. These platters are often served with a side salad, potatoes, and horseradish sauce.

  • Pickled herring: Pickled herring is a staple of Estonian cuisine and is often eaten as a snack or as an appetizer. The herring is pickled with vinegar, sugar, and spices, giving it a slightly sweet and sour flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Kännu Kukk: Kännu Kukk is a type of beer that is popular in the region. This beer is brewed with natural ingredients and has a smooth, fruity flavor.

  • Vana Tallinn: Vana Tallinn is a traditional Estonian liquor that is made with rum, spices, and vanilla. It is often served after dinner as a digestive.

Where to Stay in Käsmu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Käsmu Captain’s Village Guesthouse: A beautiful and cozy guesthouse located in the heart of the village. The rooms are decorated with traditional Estonian designs and offer stunning sea views.

  • Merekivi Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with a breathtaking view of the sea. The rooms are clean and comfortable, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

  • Käsmu Nature House: A budget-friendly option for nature lovers. The guesthouse is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and offers a wide range of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Käsmu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fish products: Smoked fish platters and fish pates are some of the local products to look for when in Käsmu.

  • Handicrafts: Käsmu is home to various stores and markets selling beautiful handmade crafts, including pottery, textiles, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Käsmu

Käsmu has a rich cultural heritage, dating back to the Middle Ages, and it is deeply rooted in maritime traditions and customs. The captains of Käsmu were revered members of the community, and their lives and work are celebrated in the local museums and exhibitions.

Best Time to Visit Käsmu

The best time to visit Käsmu is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is suitable for swimming and water sports activities. However, the autumn season is also a popular time to visit, offering visitors a chance to experience the stunning fall foliage and the autumn harvest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Käsmu

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and enjoy the fresh seafood.

  • Take a boat trip and explore the sea bays of Käsmu.

  • Respect the natural environment and follow environmental guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas and follow the laws and regulations to protect the environment.

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

  • Disturb the wildlife and natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Käsmu

Käsmu is a safe and peaceful village, and there are no major safety concerns. However, visitors should exercise caution when visiting natural areas, such as forests and bogs, and follow environmental guidelines to prevent accidents.

Health in Käsmu

There are no major health risks in Käsmu, and visitors can drink tap water and eat the local food without any problems. However, it is always recommended to have travel insurance and to consult a doctor before visiting.

Getting to Käsmu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Käsmu

The nearest international airport to Käsmu is Tallinn International Airport, which is located about 90 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to the village.

Bus Stations Near Käsmu

Käsmu is well-connected by buses to Tallinn and other nearby cities and towns. Visitors can take a bus from the Tallinn Bus Station to Käsmu and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Käsmu, enjoying the scenic landscape and exploring the nearby regions at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Käsmu

Käsmu is a small village, and visitors can easily explore the village on foot. Bikes and cars are also available for rent in the village.

Where to Go Next from Käsmu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Viinistu Art Museum: Viinistu Art Museum is a unique museum situated in a former fish cannery in Viinistu village, showcasing the works of contemporary Estonian artists.

  • Lahemaa National Park: Lahemaa National Park is one of Estonia’s largest and most diverse parks, offering various outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and wildlife watching.

Conclusion

Käsmu is a beautiful and unique destination, offering visitors a chance to explore Estonia’s maritime heritage and experience the tranquil natural environment of the country’s northern coast. With its beautiful diversity, delicious cuisine, and exciting activities, Käsmu is an excellent choice for a weekend getaway or an extended holiday.
