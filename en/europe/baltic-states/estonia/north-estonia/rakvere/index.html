Introduction to Rakvere

Rakvere is a small town located in North Estonia, in the Baltic States, in Europe. It’s known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. The town’s history dates back to the 13th century, and it has been a site of many battles and occupations throughout the centuries.

Rakvere is home to many attractions, including its famous castle, beautiful parks, and museums. The town is also a great starting point for outdoor adventures, with plenty of hiking and biking trails, lakes, and forests nearby. Rakvere offers an authentic Estonian experience, with friendly locals, delicious food, and traditional customs to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Rakvere

No visit to Rakvere is complete without experiencing some of its most popular attractions. Here’s a list of the best places to visit:

1. Rakvere Castle

Built in the 13th century, the Rakvere Castle is the town’s most famous landmark. This majestic castle has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout its history, but the current structure dates back to the 16th century. Visitors can explore the castle’s towers, walls, and underground passages to learn about the castle’s rich history.

2. Tarvas Statue

The Tarvas Statue is a symbol of Rakvere and depicts a legendary animal - the aurochs or European bison. The statue is located in the town square and is a popular spot for taking photos. Visitors can learn about the folklore behind the statue and the significance of the aurochs in Estonian culture.

3. Rakvere Museum

The Rakvere Museum is located in a historic wooden building and has exhibits on the town’s history and traditions. Visitors can learn about the town’s medieval past, as well as its more recent history under Soviet occupation. The museum also has exhibits on traditional crafts and customs in Rakvere.

4. Pikk Street

Pikk Street is a charming cobblestone street lined with colorful houses and traditional shops. It’s a great place to wander, shop for souvenirs, and experience the town’s unique culture.

5. Vallimäe Hill Park

Vallimäe Hill Park is a beautiful park located in the heart of Rakvere. It offers scenic views of the town, as well as a glimpse into the town’s history. The park has several walking trails, picnic areas, and a viewing platform.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rakvere has a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical places to explore. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions:

1. Rakvere Museum

As mentioned before, the Rakvere Museum is a great place to learn about the town’s history and traditions.

2. Rakvere Old Town

Rakvere’s Old Town is a well-preserved medieval town center with several historic buildings and churches. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn more about the town’s rich history and architecture.

3. Rakvere Theatre

The Rakvere Theatre is a modern theater that hosts plays, concerts, and other cultural events throughout the year. It’s a great way to experience Estonian culture and entertainment.

Natural Attractions of Rakvere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rakvere is known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

1. Lahemaa National Park

Lahemaa National Park is located just a short drive from Rakvere and is one of Estonia’s largest national parks. It offers stunning views of the Baltic Sea, as well as hiking and biking trails, fishing opportunities, and wildlife viewing.

2. Rakvere Lakes

Rakvere Lakes are a series of lakes located near the town center. Visitors can enjoy swimming, boating, and fishing in the lakes, as well as hiking and picnicking nearby.

3. Rakvere Forests

Rakvere is surrounded by beautiful forests that offer a peaceful retreat from the town center. Visitors can hike, bike, or simply enjoy a picnic surrounded by nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rakvere

Rakvere has several hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views of the town and surrounding countryside. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Rakvere-Oandu hiking route - a 25 km trail that takes visitors through forests, bogs, and lakes
  • Rakvere-Palmse hiking route - a 9 km trail that passes through Lahemaa National Park
  • Rakvere cycling route - a 50 km route that takes visitors through the town’s historic landmarks and beautiful countryside

Food and Drink Scene of Rakvere

Rakvere has a delicious food and drink scene, with traditional Estonian dishes and local beverages to try. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vastlakukkel - a traditional Estonian sweet roll served during the Vastlapäev holiday
  • Peipsi pikeperch - a regional freshwater fish dish served with potatoes or rice
  • Verivorst - a blood sausage made with pork and barley

Local Beverages

  • Kali - a non-alcoholic beverage made with rye bread and malt
  • Vana Tallinn - a sweet and spicy liqueur made with rum, vanilla, and citrus
  • Saaremaa limonaad - a fizzy drink made with locally sourced ingredients

Local Culture and Customs of Rakvere

Rakvere has a rich cultural heritage, with several traditions and customs that are still celebrated today. Here are some of the most important ones:
