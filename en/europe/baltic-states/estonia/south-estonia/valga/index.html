Introduction to Valga

Valga is a picturesque town situated in South Estonia near the border shared with Latvia. The town is often referred to as the ‘Gateway to Estonia’ due to its central position and proximity to important cultural sites. Valga has a long history dating back to the 13th century and boasts the beautiful Valga train station, a renowned example of art nouveau architecture. Valga is a town steeped in history and natural beauty, providing tourists with an exciting blend of architecture, culture, and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Valga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Valga Museum

Valga Museum is undoubtedly the highlight of the town’s cultural scene. The museum is dedicated to the town’s rich history and showcases various exhibitions and displays depicting the past and present of Valga and Estonia.

Aakre Manor

Aakre Manor is an ancient agricultural estate that has been around since the 14th-century. Visitors to the manor can enjoy its lovely baroque-style architecture, as well as its serene park and gardens.

Valga St. John’s Church

Valga St. John’s Church is a striking architectural masterpiece that dates back to the 1870s. The church’s awe-inspiring chancel and altar will leave visitors awestruck.

Natural Attractions of Valga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Soontaga Nature Reserve

Soontaga Nature Reserve is a great place to experience the natural beauty of Valga. The reserve is home to various plant and animal species, including the rare peat moss frog.

Pedeli river

Pedeli river is an ideal place to relax and enjoy the scenery. Visitors can enjoy fishing, kayaking or swimming in its crystal-clear water.

Latvia-Estonia border marker

The Latvia-Estonia border marker is a unique and quirky attraction. The giant metal structure marks the border and is worth a visit for an excellent photo opportunity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valga

Valga is a great place for cyclists and hikers. Visitors can explore the town and its surroundings on various bike and hiking trails. Some of the most famous trails include:

  • Pedeli hiking trail
  • Taagepera-Valga hiking trail
  • Jaanikese-Pedeli hiking trail

Best Things to Do in Valga: Activities and Tours

Segway Tours

Segway tours are an exciting way to explore the attractions of Valga. Tourists can ride on an electric scooter and discover the town’s history, culture, and natural beauty.

Horseback Riding

Horseback riding is a unique and enjoyable way to explore the area surrounding Valga. Tourists can take a guided tour of the nearby forests and fields, as well as visit local farms and see how Estonians keep their horses.

Street Art Tour

Valga boasts some fantastic street art that is often overlooked by tourists. A street art tour is a great way to discover the hidden treasures in the back alleys and side streets of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Valga

Valga offers an exciting food and drink scene, providing visitors with the chance to discover traditional Estonian cuisine. There are local restaurants and cafes which offer an authentic Estonian food experience.

Must-Try Dishes

Kama

Kama is a traditional Estonian dish comprising of ground grains, often paired with buttermilk or kefir. The dish is usually had as breakfast or a dessert.

Roast Pork

Roast Pork is another traditional Estonian dish, famous for its juicy and flavorful meat. It is always served with lingonberry jam.

Black Bread

Black Bread is a staple in the Estonian diet. It is a dense, dark rye bread that has a nutty flavor and chewy texture.

Local Beverages

Kali

Kali is a traditional Estonian drink made from fermented bread and hops. It has a unique taste that is both sweet and sour, making it a popular beverage among the locals.

Vana Tallinn liqueur

Vana Tallinn liqueur is a sweet and spicy liqueur made from natural ingredients. The drink has a caramel-like taste and is perfect for sipping after dinner.

Where to Stay in Valga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valga has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

Hotel Metsis

Hotel Metsis is a charming hotel situated in the town center. It offers cozy rooms, Wi-Fi, and tasty breakfast.

Hostel Allik

Allik Hostel is a great budget option for travelers looking to stay in Valga. It offers comfortable rooms, shared kitchen spaces, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Valga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Valga provides exciting shopping opportunities for tourists looking to take home local products and souvenirs. Some of the best items to buy include:

Estonian Wool Sweaters

Estonian wool sweaters are renowned for their quality and unique designs. Tourists can find them in various shops in Valga.

Handmade Wooden Crafts

Handmade wooden crafts are widely available in the town. Tourists can take home souvenirs such as bowls, spoons, or even intricate models of famous Estonian landmarks.

Local Culture and Customs of Valga

Estonians are open, friendly, and welcoming people. The locals take great pride in their heritage, which is evident in their hospitality and customs. Visitors are expected to be polite and respectful of the country’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Valga

The best time to visit Valga is during the Spring or Summer months, from May to September. The weather is warm and pleasant, providing the perfect opportunity to explore the town’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valga

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Explore the town’s natural beauty.
  • Try authentic Estonian cuisine and local beverages.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disrespectful of the country’s customs and traditions.
  • Underestimate the importance of proper etiquette and dress in public places.
  • Waste food or litter the streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valga

Valga is a safe and secure town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still be mindful of their belongings and avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables with them.

Health in Valga

Valga has a high-quality healthcare system, ensuring that tourists receive the best medical care possible during their stay. Visitors are advised to buy comprehensive travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Valga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Valga is easily accessible by air, bus or car. The nearest airports are Riga International Airport (Latvia), and Tallinn Airport (Estonia).

Airports Near Valga

  • Riga International Airport (Latvia)
  • Tallinn Airport (Estonia)

Bus Stations Near Valga

  • Valga Bus Station
  • Riga Bus Station (Latvia)

Other Options

Private taxi and car rental services are available for tourists who want more flexibility.

How to Get Around in Valga

Valga is easy to navigate on foot, but visitors may also use local taxi companies, bikes, or hire a car to explore.

Where to Go Next from Valga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tartu

Tartu is a stunning university town located in South Estonia. It boasts a rich cultural history and architecture, museums, and parks. It is a two-hour drive from Valga.

Tallinn

Tallinn is the capital city of Estonia, located three hours’ drive from Valga. It is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage-listed Old Town, a vibrant nightlife, and a thriving food scene.

Conclusion

Valga is a beautiful town located in South Estonia with a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty and exciting food and drink scene. Visitors can explore its museums, historic sites, and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. If you are planning a visit to Estonia, do not miss out on the charming town of Valga.
