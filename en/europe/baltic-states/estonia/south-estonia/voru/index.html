Are you looking for a new travel destination that offers outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and natural beauty? Look no further than Võru! Located in South Estonia and surrounded by rolling hills and pristine forests, Võru offers a wealth of activities and sights for visitors to enjoy. This comprehensive travel guide will give you an overview of all the best things to do in Võru and help you plan the perfect trip to this charming town.

Introduction to Võru

Võru is a small town of just over 12,000 people located in South Estonia, near the border with Latvia and Russia. The town is known for its beautiful natural surroundings, including forests, lakes, and rivers, as well as its rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Võru can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to kayaking and fishing. The town also has a vibrant cultural scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and cultural events throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Võru

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Võru County Museum: this museum showcases the history and culture of Võru and the surrounding region, with exhibits on local art, customs, and traditions.
  • Võru Katariina Church: this beautiful church dates back to the 17th century and is one of the oldest buildings in Võru.
  • Seto Farm Museum: located just outside Võru, this museum features traditional Seto farmhouses and exhibits on Seto culture and traditions.
  • Piusa Caves Visitor Center: this center offers tours of the nearby Piusa Caves, which are some of the most unique and interesting geological formations in Estonia.

Natural Attractions of Võru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kubija Nature Reserve: this reserve features scenic hiking trails and beautiful views of Kubija Lake, as well as opportunities for birdwatching and fishing.
  • Tamula Beach: located on the shores of Lake Tamula, this beach offers swimming, sunbathing, and water sports in the summer months.
  • Taevaskoja Nature Reserve: this reserve is known for its unique sandstone cliffs and scenic hiking trails along the Ahja River.
  • Haanja Nature Park: located just outside Võru, this park features a variety of hiking trails, as well as opportunities for skiing and snowshoeing in the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Võru

  • Haanja Nature Park: this park offers a variety of trails, including the 24-km-long Haanja hiking trail and the 113-km-long Rõuge hiking trail.
  • Kubija Nature Reserve: this reserve features several hiking trails of varying length and difficulty, as well as opportunities for mountain biking.
  • Võhandu river valley: this scenic valley is a popular destination for hiking and biking, with numerous trails winding along the river and through the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Võru: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on the Võhandu River: this river is known for its beautiful scenery and is a popular destination for kayaking and canoeing.
  • Fishing in Kubija Lake: this lake is stocked with a variety of fish, including pike, perch, and carp, making it a great destination for anglers.
  • Visiting Võru’s summer music festival: each summer, Võru hosts a celebrated music festival featuring classical and contemporary music performances.
  • Taking a guided tour of Võru’s historic Old Town: explore the town’s charming architecture and learn about its intriguing history with a guided walking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Võru

Day 1: Arrive in Võru and explore the town’s Old Town and local shops and restaurants.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Piusa Caves Visitor Center and explore the unique underground caverns.

Day 3-4: Spend two days exploring Haanja Nature Park, hiking or skiing, depending on the season.

Day 5: Visit the Seto Farm Museum and learn about the traditional culture and customs of the Seto people.

Day 6: Explore the scenic Võhandu river valley with a hiking or biking tour.

Day 7: Relax on Tamula Beach and enjoy the beautiful views of Lake Tamula before departing Võru.

Food and Drink Scene of Võru

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sült: a traditional Estonian dish made with pork, often served cold as an appetizer.
  • Mulgipuder: a hearty dish made with potatoes, groats, and pork, typically served with sour cream.
  • Vana Tallinn: a popular Estonian liqueur made with rum, spices, and vanilla.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer from Võru Brewery: this local brewery produces a variety of high-quality beers, including the popular Võru Tume.
  • Viru Valge vodka: this premium vodka is made in Estonia and is known for its smooth taste and subtle aroma.

Where to Stay in Võru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kubija Hotel and Nature Spa: located within Kubija Nature Reserve, this hotel offers beautiful views and access to hiking trails and other outdoor activities.
  • Võru Guesthouse: this centrally located guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and easy access to Võru’s Old Town.
  • Taevaskoja Salamaa: located in the heart of Taevaskoja Nature Reserve, this guesthouse offers easy access to hiking trails and other outdoor adventures.

What to Buy in Võru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Seto handicrafts: these beautiful and unique handicrafts, including woven fabrics and pottery, make excellent gifts and souvenirs.
  • Handmade jewelry from local artisans: Võru is known for its talented jewelry makers, who produce a wide range of high-quality pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Võru

Võru has a strong sense of cultural identity, with traditions and customs that are distinct from other parts of Estonia. Visitors to Võru can experience this unique culture through local festivals and events, such as the Seto Külavoor music festival and the Võru Folk Festival.

Best Time to Visit Võru

The best time to visit Võru depends on your interests. Summer is the busiest time of year, with warm weather and the most outdoor activities and events. Fall is a great time to visit for autumn colors and mushroom picking, while winter brings opportunities for skiing and snowshoeing. Spring is a quieter time of year, but still offers beautiful scenery and mild weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Võru

Do:

  • Try traditional Estonian dishes and local beverages
  • Take a guided tour of Võru’s historic Old Town
  • Explore the town’s natural surroundings with a hiking or biking tour

Don’t:

  • Discount the importance of Võru’s local culture and customs
  • Leave Võru without trying the local craft beer or vodka
  • Miss out on the opportunity to explore the town’s scenic natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Võru

Võru is generally a safe town, but visitors should take normal precautions against theft and pickpocketing. When hiking or camping in the surrounding forests, be aware of the possibility of tick-borne illnesses and take measures to protect yourself, such as using insect repellent and wearing long pants and sleeves.

Health in Võru

Visitors to Võru should ensure that they have up-to-date vaccinations and travel insurance before arriving. The town has several medical clinics and pharmacies for minor illnesses or injuries. In case of a medical emergency, dial 112 for assistance.

Getting to Võru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Võru

The closest international airport to Võru is Tallinn Airport, located approximately 246 km away. Visitors can rent a car or take a bus or train from Tallinn to Võru.

Bus Stations Near Võru

Võru has a bus station located in the center of town, with regular service to Tallinn and other major cities in Estonia and nearby countries.

Other Options

Visitors to Võru can also rent a car or book a private transfer from Tallinn or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Võru

Võru is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. There are also local buses that run throughout the town and surrounding areas, as well as taxis available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Võru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tartu: known as Estonia’s “cultural capital,” Tartu is home to numerous museums, galleries, and cultural events.
  • Riga, Latvia: the beautiful capital of Latvia is located just a few hours’ drive from Võru.
  • Piusa Caves: explore more of Estonia’s underground world with a visit to the nearby Piusa Caves.

Conclusion

Võru is a hidden gem in South Estonia, offering natural beauty, cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or exploring the town’s unique cultural heritage, Võru has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Võru and start exploring all that this charming town has to offer!
