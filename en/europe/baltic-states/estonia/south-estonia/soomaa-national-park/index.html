Introduction to Soomaa National Park

Soomaa National Park is one of the essential tourist destinations in Estonia, located in the South of the country, and is known as the “land of bogs.” Covering a vast area of about 390 km², it offers visitors an exciting combination of bogs, rivers, and forests with an abundance of wildlife. You can also explore the traditional rural lifestyle, enjoy local cuisine, and experience unique adventures while visiting this national park.

List of Places to Visit in Soomaa National Park

Soomaa National Park has many destinations worth visiting, and here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Riisa Study Trail: This site is of great cultural and historical importance, with several old buildings and structures of the 19th century Estonian cultural history. It educates visitors on how people lived and survived in the earlier days.
  • The Kuresoo Bog Museum: This museum provides visitors with the opportunity to learn about the five different bogs found in Soomaa Park and their importance through various exhibitions, documentaries, and videos.

Natural Attractions of Soomaa National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kõpu Nature Reserve Boardwalk: This site is perfect for walking and cycling, and visitors can enjoy the scenic viewpoints in the forest and the swampy areas.
  • Canoeing in Soomaa Park: Visitors can paddle down the rivers and explore the breathtaking natural beauty of Soomaa national park. The park offers four significant rivers for canoeing, the Pärnu River, the Halliste River, the Navesti River, and the Raudna River.
  • Angling: Soomaa National Park has several lakes and rivers with an abundance of fish. A perfect destination for fishing enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soomaa National Park

Soomaa Park offers numerous hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels. Here are some popular trails:

  • The Beaver Trail: This is an excellent hiking trail that takes you through the forest and various landscapes such as bogs and swamps.
  • The Rõuge Trail: This trail is perfect for advanced hikers and mountain bikers. It offers stunning views of hills, lakes, and trees.

Best Things to Do in Soomaa National Park: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to consider when visiting Soomaa National Park:

  • Canoeing: Soomaa offers a fantastic opportunity to experience canoeing in slow-moving rivers.
  • Bird-watching: Soomaa National Park has an abundance of birds, and an experienced guide can help visitors spot a range of species.
  • Bear-watching: Visitors can experience bear-watching in Soomaa National Park, but it is important to note that it is a seasonal activity that is only available in late spring and early fall.

Food and Drink Scene of Soomaa National Park

Whenever visiting a new location, sampling the local cuisine adds to the overall experience. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks while visiting Soomaa National Park.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mulgipuder, a traditional Estonian dish made from potatoes, onions, and barley.
  • Wildberry jam, commonly found in Soomaa as it is known for its berry production.

Local Beverages

  • Vana Tallinn, a traditional Estonian bitter-sweet liqueur.
  • Kali, a low alcohol drink made from fermented bread.

Where to Stay in Soomaa National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available to tourists in Soomaa National Park, ranging from inexpensive hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommended options:

  • Raudsilla Entertainment Complex: This complex offers a range of accommodation options, including apartments, guest rooms, and cottages, and offers many activities such as canoeing, fishing, and a sauna.
  • Kõrvenõmme Holiday House: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms for an affordable price. The balcony and terrace provide stunning views of the surrounding scenery.

What to Buy in Soomaa National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Soomaa National Park, tourists can bring back unique souvenirs and products, some of which include:

  • Handcrafted items made from locally sourced wood.
  • Handwoven textiles, such as woolen socks and mittens.
  • Jams and honey made from locally grown produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Soomaa National Park

Estonia is unique in its culture and customs. Here are some customs to keep in mind while visiting Soomaa National Park:

  • Estonia’s culture places great emphasis on acceptance and respect for the environment and nature.
  • The Estonian language is prevalent, and visitors may want to learn a few phrases or words.

Best Time to Visit Soomaa National Park

Soomaa National Park is open all year round, but the most popular times to visit are from April to October. Visitors can experience a full range of activities during this time, such as canoeing, hiking, and bird-watching.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soomaa National Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment and preserve nature.
  • Use designated camping areas when spending the night.
  • Pack appropriately for various weather conditions.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind; keep the park clean.
  • Disturb wildlife.
  • Public nudity is not allowed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Soomaa National Park

Soomaa National Park is generally safe, but it is always advised to take necessary precautions and plan for a trip with a reputable tour operator.

Health in Soomaa National Park

Visitors should be well-prepared for various weather conditions and stay hydrated when hiking or canoeing.
