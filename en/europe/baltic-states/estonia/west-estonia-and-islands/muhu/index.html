Introduction to Muhu

Muhu is a small island located in the West Estonia and Islands region of Estonia, part of the Baltic states and situated in Europe. Despite its size, the island is full of unique cultural and natural attractions that make it a hidden gem for travelers. Muhu is known for its traditional architecture, including the famous Muhu farmhouse, and its rich history and folklore.

List of Places to Visit in Muhu

Muhu Island has something for everyone, from culturally important museums and historical places to natural attractions with scenic views. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Muhu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Muhu Museum: Learn about the island’s history and traditions, including the Muhu farmhouse and the island’s unique clothing and handicrafts.
  • Koguva Village: Explore the best-preserved village in Muhu, with its old Nordic-style architecture, church, and museum.
  • St. Catherine’s Church: Visit the oldest church on the island, constructed in the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Muhu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Liiva-Kihelkonna hiking trail: Follow this trail to discover the island’s natural beauty through forests, meadows, and coastline views.
  • Pädaste Manor: Enjoy the beautiful landscape, gardens, and a luxury spa in this historical manor, which also offers a range of outdoor activities like fishing and cycling.
  • Jõe village beach: Relax on the sandy beach and enjoy the crystal-clear waters of the Muhu Strait, featuring the best coastal views on the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Muhu

Exploring Muhu Island on foot or by bike is a must-do activity. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to experience Muhu’s beautiful nature:

  • Liiva-Kihelkonna hiking trail: A 30 km trail crossing forests and meadows along the coastline.
  • Muhu Island circular hike: A 75 km trail leading to the most scenic places on the island, including Pädaste Manor, St. Catherine’s Church, and Koguva Village.
  • Pädaste-Tuhu nature trail: A 2 km trail with a beautiful view of the sea and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Muhu: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other interesting activities and tours to explore on Muhu Island, including:

  • Food tours: Discover the local cuisine and visit the island’s farms and restaurants to taste traditional dishes.
  • Boat tours: Explore the Muhu Strait and discover hidden beaches, cliffs, and islands.
  • Fishing: Try your hand at fishing in the Baltic Sea or on the island’s freshwater lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of Muhu

Muhu Island is famous for its traditional cuisine, which includes locally-sourced seafood, sauerkraut, homemade bread, and various meat dishes. Along with food, the island also has various local drinks that one should not miss, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pökkel (smoked meat): A traditional Estonian dish made of pork or beef and smoked over juniper fire.
  • Rosolje (beetroot and potato salad): A cold salad made of beetroots, potatoes, carrots, pickles, herring, eggs, and sour cream.
  • Muhu bread: A dense, dark rye bread with a distinctive sour-sweet taste.

Local Beverages

  • Kali: A non-alcoholic fermented beverage made from rye bread and flavored with sugar and spices.
  • Vana Tallinn: A sweet and strong liquer containing rum, vanilla pods, cinnamon, and citrus peels.

Where to Stay in Muhu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Muhu Island has a range of accommodation options for every budget, ranging from luxury hotels to guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Pädaste Manor: A luxury spa hotel with beautiful rooms and suites, set in a restored 16th-century manor.
  • Männiku Turismi-ja Tervisekeskus: A cozy guesthouse offering self-catering houses and apartments.
  • Muhu Gospa: A comfortable spa hotel with sea views, located near the coast of Muhu.

What to Buy in Muhu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Muhu Island is known for its unique handicrafts, local cuisine, and natural products. Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Muhu matts: Handmade woolen blankets and carpets with colorful patterns.
  • Muhu Leib: Rye-based bread made in traditional ovens and flavored with caraway seeds.
  • Juniper products: Crafted from locally-sourced juniper wood, items such as bowls, spoons, and coasters make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Muhu

Muhu Island has its unique culture that is influenced by both Estonian and Scandinavian traditions. Local customs and beliefs include:

  • Muhu Farmhouse: A typical Muhu-style house, featuring a thatched roof, wooden frame, and a large chimney.
  • Muhu clothing: Traditional clothing made of wool or linen, featuring vibrant patterns and embroidery.
  • Folklore: Many stories and legends about Muhu’s history and culture are still told and celebrated on the island.

Best Time to Visit Muhu

The best time to visit Muhu Island is during the summer months, from June to August. The temperatures are pleasant, and most attractions and activities are open during this time. In the winter, the island is covered in snow, and many activities, restaurants, and accommodations are closed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muhu

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the beautiful nature and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any trash behind.
  • Disrespect the local culture or beliefs.
  • Take photos of locals without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Muhu

Muhu Island is generally a safe place to travel, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, as with any travel destination, it’s important to take necessary safety precautions, such as:

  • Keeping valuables safe and secure.
  • Using a money belt or secured bag.
  • Following local laws and customs.

Health in Muhu

There are no serious health concerns when traveling to Muhu Island. However, it’s always advisable to get travel insurance and check with your doctor for any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Muhu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Muhu Island is connected to the mainland of Estonia via ferry. There are several options to get to Muhu, including:

  • By ferry: Regular ferries run between Virtsu and Kuivastu ports on the mainland and Muhu.
  • By car: It’s possible to take your car on the ferry or rent a car on the island.
  • By bus: Several bus lines operate to/from Kuivastu port.

Airports Near Muhu

The nearest airport to Muhu Island is Tallinn Airport, located around 190 km away.

Bus Stations Near Muhu

Several bus lines operate to/from Kuivastu port, which is the closest port to Muhu Island.

Other Options

It’s also possible to arrive in Muhu by private boat or charter flight.

How to Get Around in Muhu

Muhu Island is small and easily navigable either by renting a car or a bicycle. Bike rental is available in the villages, and most hotels offer car rentals too. There are also buses that run between the main villages and attractions on the island.

Where to Go Next from Muhu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Muhu Island is a good starting point to explore other destinations in Estonia. These include:

  • Saaremaa: A neighboring island to Muhu, known for its unique nature, Kuressaare castle and the Kaali meteorite crater.
  • Tallinn: The beautiful old town of Tallinn, the capital of Estonia, is just a few hours’ drive away from Muhu.

Conclusion

Muhu Island is a unique travel destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural traditions, and delicious food.
