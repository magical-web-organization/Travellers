Introduction to Vilsandi National Park

Vilsandi National Park is located in West Estonia and Islands, in the Baltic states of Europe. The lush pine and spruce forests, pristine beaches, and biodiversity of flora and fauna make this National Park a must-visit destination for nature lovers. This protected area consists of Vilsandi Island, 160 smaller surrounding islands, and the coastal area.

List of Places to Visit in Vilsandi National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vilsandi Lighthouse: Built-in 1809, the lighthouse is the oldest surviving lighthouse in the Baltic states and offers a stunning view of the surrounding area.
  • Saaremaa Museum: Located in Kuressaare, the museum exhibits Saaremaa’s history and culture, including shipwrecks, amber, and castle artifacts.
  • Angla Windmills: Visit the five well-preserved windmills in Angla, dating back to the 18th century, for a glimpse of Estonian rural culture.

Natural Attractions of Vilsandi National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vilsandi Island: The main attraction of the park, Vilsandi Island, offers a great opportunity for wildlife sighting, such as grey seals, moose, and various bird species, including the majestic white-tailed eagle.
  • Karala Ponds: Located in the eastern part of the park, the ponds are perfect for fishing and swimming.
  • Panga Cliffs: Formed over 400 million years ago, the cliffs offer a stunning view of the Baltic Sea and are home to various plant and animal species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vilsandi National Park

  • Vilsandi Nature Trail: A 5 km path through the island’s forests and meadows dotted with seabird-watching towers.
  • Sääretirp Peninsula: A 3 km loop trail around Säärte village offers a beautiful coastal view.
  • Saaremaa Hiking Route: Covers 370 km through Saaremaa Island, allowing visitors to explore the diverse flora and fauna of the island.

Best Things to Do in Vilsandi National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Paddle through the calm sea waters around the islands and spot seals and birds.
  • Bird Watching: The park is home to over 240 bird species, including migratory birds, and bird watching tours are available.
  • Sauna: Try the traditional Estonian sauna experience while appreciating the beautiful surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vilsandi National Park

Day 1-2: Arrive at Kuressaare and explore the city; visit the Saaremaa Museum.

Day 3: Hike the Vilsandi Nature Trail and watch seabirds from the towers.

Day 4: Kayak around the islands; enjoy the marine life sightings.

Day 5: Visit Angla Windmills and Panga Cliffs.

Day 6: Relax and enjoy the sauna experience.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Vilsandi National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mulgipuder (mashed potatoes with barley): A traditional Estonian dish made with potatoes, bacon, and barley.
  • Kama: A traditional Estonian dessert made of flour, buttermilk, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Kali (fermented bread drink): A non-alcoholic drink made of bread, sugar, and yeast.
  • Vana Tallinn (rum-based liqueur): A popular Estonian liqueur with a sweet and spicy taste.

Where to Stay in Vilsandi National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Saaremaa Hotellid: A hotel chain offering various hotel options in Saaremaa, including Kuressaare, with modern amenities and sea view rooms.
  • Vilsandi Hotell: Located in the National Park, this hotel offers a peaceful and cozy stay with rustic interior décor and traditional sauna experience.

What to Buy in Vilsandi National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber jewelry: Baltic Amber is known for its beauty and quality, and Saaremaa has various shops offering handmade jewelry.
  • Saaremaa honey: The rich aroma of the honey from Saaremaa’s diverse vegetation is a must-try local product.

Local Culture and Customs of Vilsandi National Park

The local culture of Vilsandi National Park reflects the islanders’ resilience and love for nature. The Alutaguse Folk Festival, held annually, showcases various Estonian folk music and dance performances. Visitors are advised to dress appropriately and respect the locals’ culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Vilsandi National Park

The best time to visit Vilsandi National Park is from May to September when the weather is warmer and the flora and fauna are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vilsandi National Park

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and do not leave any trash behind.
  • Follow designated trails and bird-watching towers.
  • Try the traditional sauna experience.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife habitat.
  • Pick up or damage plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vilsandi National Park

The park is generally safe for visitors, but they should avoid swimming in unauthorized areas and follow relevant signs. Visitors should also be cautious of ticks during June to August and protect themselves from insect bites.

Health in Vilsandi National Park

Health care facilities are available in Kuressaare; visitors are advised to have health insurance and take necessary precautions, including carrying prescribed medicines and using insect repellents.

Getting to Vilsandi National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vilsandi National Park

  • Kuressaare Airport: Located 37 km away, with regular flights to Tallinn.

Bus Stations Near Vilsandi National Park

  • Kuressaare Bus Station: Regular bus services from Tallinn and other parts of Estonia.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Connections from Virtsu to Muhu Island and from Saaremaa’s harbors to other Estonian ports.

How to Get Around in Vilsandi National Park

The best way to explore the National Park is by walking, hiking, and biking on designated trails. Car rental services are available in Kuressaare and Tartu for visitors planning to travel around the island.

Where to Go Next from Vilsandi National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tallinn: The capital city of Estonia, known for its medieval Old Town, is a 3.5- hour drive from Kuressaare.
  • Pärnu: Located 2.5 hours away, Pärnu is a popular summer destination with sandy beaches and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Vilsandi National Park is a rare gem in Estonia that offers a perfect blend of natural and cultural attractions. From the stunning coastal views to the welcoming locals, Vilsandi National Park is a must-visit destination for any traveler interested in experiencing the best of Estonia.
