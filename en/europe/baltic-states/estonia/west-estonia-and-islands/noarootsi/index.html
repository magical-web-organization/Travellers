Introduction to Noarootsi

Noarootsi is a charming coastal town located in West Estonia and Islands, in the Baltic States, in Europe. The town is surrounded by breathtaking nature that includes dense forests, marshland, rugged coastlines, and pristine beaches. Noarootsi is a popular destination for those seeking to escape city life to enjoy the outdoors, immerse themselves in local culture and history, and indulge in traditional Estonian food and drink. In this travel guide of Noarootsi, we will take a deeper look at the amazing sites and experiences that await you.

List of Places to Visit in Noarootsi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roosta Adventure Park

Roosta Adventure Park is a must-visit destination located in Noarootsi’s beautiful forests. You can explore the park on a high ropes course, zip lines, and climbing walls. The park caters to people of all ages and offers various difficulty levels to provide a fun and safe experience.

St. Catherine’s Lutheran Church

St. Catherine’s Lutheran Church is a beautiful baroque church that dates back to the 18th century. The church is known for its impressive architecture and is considered one of the most valuable cultural sites in Estonia.

Rannarootsi Museum

The Rannarootsi Museum is a unique destination that highlights the Swedish history of Noarootsi. The museum is set up in a traditional farmhouse, which makes for an immersive experience that showcases how life was in the past.

Natural Attractions of Noarootsi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Osmussaar Island

Osmussaar Island is one of the best day trips you can take from Noarootsi. The island is known for its rugged landscapes, deserted beaches, lighthouses, and diverse wildlife, making it a hotspot for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.

Pakri Peninsula National Park

Pakri Peninsula National Park is a stunning natural destination that is perfect for hiking and exploring nature. The park features diverse landscapes that include pine forests, bogs, and rocky coastlines, allowing visitors to experience the best of Estonia’s natural beauty.

Paldiski Cliffs

The Paldiski Cliffs is a breathtaking destination, known for its scenic views and birdwatching opportunities. Visitors can hike to the top of the cliffs for an unforgettable panoramic view of the Baltic Sea and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noarootsi

Noarootsi is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, thanks to its many trails that span across beautiful natural landscapes. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • the Pakri Peninsula trail
  • the Jägala waterfall trail
  • the Põhja-Kõrvemaa Nature Reserve trail

Best Things to Do in Noarootsi: Activities and Tours

Kayaking on the Baltic Sea

Kayaking on the Baltic Sea is a great experience, and Noarootsi’s coastline offers fantastic opportunities to do so. Paddle through pristine waters and deserted beaches as you witness the stunning scenery and wildlife, such as seals and sea eagles.

Estonian Sauna Experience

The Estonian sauna is an essential part of the country’s culture, and a must-try experience while in Noarootsi. You can choose between private saunas or public ones, which include a range of facilities like cold-water pools and cafeteria.

Week-Long Itinerary for Noarootsi

Day 1: Arrival in Noarootsi – explore the town and sample the traditional Estonian cuisine.

Day 2: Visit Roosta Adventure Park and explore the beautiful forests.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Osmussaar or visit the Paldiski Cliffs.

Day 4: Go kayaking on the Baltic Sea or hiking in Pakri Peninsula National Park.

Day 5: Relax and unwind at one of the spas in Noarootsi.

Day 6: Take a cultural tour and visit St. Catherine’s Lutheran Church and the Rannarootsi Museum.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Noarootsi

Must-Try Dishes

Noarootsi has excellent traditional Estonian cuisine to sample. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Kama
  • Verivorst (blood sausage)
  • Karask

Local Beverages

Estonia produces a variety of local drinks, including beer, vodka, and cider. Some of the must-try beverages include:

  • Viru Valge Vodka
  • Lehe Pruulikoda beer
  • Siider cider

Local Culture and Customs of Noarootsi

The local culture of Noarootsi is influenced by its Swedish heritage. Visitors can witness the traditions and customs of the locals through the various cultural events that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Noarootsi

The best time to visit Noarootsi is during the summer months between June to August. These months offer the best weather conditions and provide ample opportunity to explore the natural landscapes during hiking and biking tours.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noarootsi

Noarootsi is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors should take necessary precautions when exploring the natural landscapes and adhere to safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Noarootsi

Noarootsi has several health facilities and hospitals that offer medical services to visitors. Visitors are advised to carry their health insurance and register with their embassy or consulate when visiting the country.

Getting to Noarootsi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Noarootsi can be accessed through several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Noarootsi

The Lennart Meri Tallinn Airport is the nearest international airport to Noarootsi. It is located around 70km from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Noarootsi

Regular buses connect Noarootsi to the nearby towns of Haapsalu and Tallinn.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Noarootsi by taxi or car.

How to Get Around in Noarootsi

Noarootsi is a small town, and visitors can explore it on foot or by bike. There are also several car rental options available for those who prefer to drive around.
