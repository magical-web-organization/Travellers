Introduction to Vormsi

Vormsi is a mesmerizing island located in the West Estonian archipelago, surrounded by the Baltic Sea. It is known for its rich history, stunning vistas, and abundant marine life. The island is approximately 9 kilometers wide and 13 kilometers long, making it the fourth-largest island in Estonia. The population of Vormsi is approximately 340 inhabitants, who are mostly of Swedish descent, and Estonian is the official language. Vormsi’s unspoiled beauty makes it a perfect destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Vormsi.

List of Places to Visit in Vormsi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vormsi Museum: The museum showcases the history and traditions of Vormsi island. It exhibits a vast collection of artifacts, including medieval tombstones, traditional costumes, fishing nets, and locally made handicrafts.

  • Saxby Lighthouse: The lighthouse is an iconic symbol of Vormsi. It was built-in 1875 and is the only active lighthouse on the island. Visitors can climb to the top and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding sea and countryside.

  • St. Olav’s Church: The church dates back to the 13th century and is one of the oldest buildings on the island. The church is named after King Olav II of Norway, who patronized it. The church has undergone several renovations and restorations over the centuries.

Natural Attractions of Vormsi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vormsi Nature Reserve: The nature reserve is located on the western coast of Vormsi island and covers an area of approximately 5,500 hectares. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several endemic species.

  • Ramsi Cliff: The Ramsi Cliff is the highest cliff on the island and offers spectacular views of the Baltic Sea. It is also home to several bird species, including sea eagles, cormorants, and gulls.

  • Söderby Stone: The Söderby Stone is a 10-meter high stone formation located on the eastern coast of Vormsi. It is believed to be a Viking Age monument and is considered one of the most important cultural landmarks of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vormsi

Vormsi is a perfect destination for hikers and bikers seeking stunning scenery and challenging terrain. The island has several hiking and biking routes, ranging from easy seaside strolls to rugged treks through dense forests. The most popular hiking and biking trails on the island include:

  • Ramsholmen Nature Reserve Trail: The trail traverses through the beautiful Ramsholmen Nature Reserve, which is home to several rare plant species and offers breathtaking views of the sea.

  • Söderby Cliff Trail: The trail follows the Söderby Cliff and offers stunning views of the Baltic Sea. It also passes through several historic landmarks, including the Söderby Stone.

Best Things to Do in Vormsi: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Kayaking is a popular activity on Vormsi, as it offers a unique perspective of the island’s stunning coastline and wildlife. There are several kayaking tours available on the island for adventurers of all levels.

  • Fishing: Vormsi is known for its rich marine life, making it an excellent destination for fishing enthusiasts. Visitors can hire a fishing charter and explore the waters around the island for a chance to catch perch, pike, and zander.

  • Swimming: Vormsi has several pristine beaches, offering visitors an opportunity to relax and soak up the sun. The island’s crystal clear waters are also perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Food and Drink Scene of Vormsi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Eel: Vormsi is known for its smoked eel, which is a traditional delicacy on the island.

  • Sõrve Sausage: Sõrve Sausage is a famous regional sausage that is made with a blend of pork and beef, seasoned with traditional spices, and smoked over juniper branches.

  • Kama: Kama is a popular traditional Estonian dessert that is made by mixing flour, peas, and barley. It is usually served with soured milk or yogurt.

Local Beverages

  • Vana Tallinn: Vana Tallinn is a famous Estonian liqueur that is made with a blend of rum, vanilla, and various spices.

  • Saaremaa Beer: Saaremaa beer is a popular beer brewed on the nearby island of Saaremaa. It is made with locally sourced barley and hops and has a distinct herbal and earthy flavor.

Where to Stay in Vormsi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vormsi has several accommodation options ranging from hotels and holiday homes to guesthouses and camping sites. Some of the popular accommodation options on the island include:

  • Vormsi Sääre Holiday Home: This cozy holiday home is located on the southern tip of the island and offers stunning views of the Baltic Sea. It is perfect for families and groups of friends.

  • Lõo Guesthouse: Lõo Guesthouse is located in the heart of Vormsi island and offers comfortable and affordable accommodation.

  • Pädaste Manor: Pädaste Manor is a luxurious five-star hotel located on the nearby island of Muhu. It is only a short ferry ride away from Vormsi and is perfect for couples and honeymooners seeking a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Vormsi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vormsi Wool: Vormsi wool is a traditional product made from high-quality sheep wool. Visitors can buy locally made woolen products such as blankets, socks, and hats.

  • Handicrafts: Vormsi is known for its rich handicraft traditions, which include woodcarvings, basket weaving, and pottery. Visitors can buy locally crafted handicrafts as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Vormsi

The culture of Vormsi is deeply intertwined with its long and storied history. The island was originally settled by Vikings in the 13th century, and throughout its history, it has been home to German, Swedish, and Russian communities. The island’s rich cultural heritage is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit Vormsi

The best time to visit Vormsi is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island is bustling with activity. The shoulder months (May, September) are also a great time to visit, as the crowds are smaller, and the scenery is still stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vormsi

Do:

  • Respect the island’s cultural heritage and traditions.
  • Enjoy the island’s natural beauty while leaving no trace behind.
  • Try the island’s traditional cuisine and local beverages.
  • Learn some basic Estonian phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the island’s delicate ecosystem.
  • Don’t disturb the island’s wildlife.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t forget to check the ferry schedules before leaving for the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vormsi

Vormsi is generally a safe destination for travelers. The island is well-connected to the mainland by ferry, and there are no major safety issues. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions when traveling on foot or by bike.

Health in Vormsi

Vormsi has a basic medical facility that provides emergency care. Visitors are advised to carry their necessary medications and consult their doctor before traveling to the island if they have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Vormsi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Vormsi is located in the West Estonian archipelago and is well-connected to the mainland by ferry. The island can be reached by taking a ferry from Rohuküla harbor, which is approximately 2.5 hours from Tallinn by car or bus.

Airports Near Vormsi

  • Tallinn Airport (TLL) - Approximately 100 kilometers away from Rohuküla harbor.

Bus Stations Near Vormsi

  • Tallinn Bus Station - Approximately 110 kilometers away from Rohuküla harbor.

Other Options

  • Private Car: Visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi from Tallinn to Rohuküla harbor.

How to Get Around in Vormsi

Vormsi is a small island and can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by car. Bikes and cars can be rented on the island, and there are several hiking trails and scenic routes for visitors to explore.

Where to Go Next from Vormsi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Matsalu National Park: Matsalu National Park is located on the mainland and is famous for its wetlands and diverse birdlife.

  • Muhu Island: Muhu Island is located near Vormsi and offers stunning beaches, historic landmarks, and luxurious hotels.

Conclusion

Vormsi island is a hidden gem in the West Estonian archipelago and is a perfect destination for travelers seeking natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive tour guide provides all the necessary information for visitors to explore the island and make the most of their trip to Vormsi.
