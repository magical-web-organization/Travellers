Introduction to Ruhnu

Ruhnu is a small, remote island located in the Gulf of Riga, which is a part of the Baltic Sea. This hidden gem is known for its stunning sandy beaches, traditional lifestyle, and unique mix of Swedish and Estonian cultures.

Ruhnu has a total area of 11.9 square kilometers, and its population is around 70 people. The island is accessible by ferry from Pärnu, Munalaid, or Roomassaare, but the sailing schedule varies depending on the season. Therefore, planning ahead is necessary.

Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply relaxing by the seaside, Ruhnu has something for everyone. Keep reading to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and other useful information for your trip to Ruhnu.

List of Places to Visit in Ruhnu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ruhnu Museum

Learn more about the island’s fascinating history and traditions by visiting Ruhnu Museum, which is located in a traditional wooden house. The museum features exhibits about the island’s flora and fauna, local handicrafts, and the lives of the island’s inhabitants throughout the centuries.

Ruhnu Church

Ruhnu Church, which was built in 1644, is one of the oldest wooden churches in Estonia. Its walls are adorned with exquisite paintings, and the church is still used for religious services today.

Natural Attractions of Ruhnu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rannapaargu Cliff

Rannapaargu Cliff is the highest point on the island, offering breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding landscape. It is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Sääre Tirp

Take a walk on Sääre Tirp, a long and narrow peninsula on the southern tip of the island. The peninsula is covered in pine forests and has numerous serene beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ruhnu

Ruhnu offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of experience. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Rannapaargu Trail: A 6 km trail that leads to the highest point on Ruhnu Island.
  • Sutu Path: A 3 km trail that winds through juniper forests and ends at a lakeside beach.
  • Sääre Tirp Trail: A 9 km trail that explores the southern tip of the island.

Best Things to Do in Ruhnu: Activities and Tours

Explore the Island by Bike

Rent a bicycle and explore the beautiful beaches and forests of Ruhnu at your own pace.

Take a Boat Trip

Take a boat trip around the island to see marine life, spot seals, and admire the coastline.

Attend Traditional Festivals

Ruhnu hosts several traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Midsummer Festival in June and the Harvest Festival in September.

Food and Drink Scene of Ruhnu

Must-Try Dishes

Ruhnu Bread

Ruhnu is famous for its homemade bread, which is made using locally grown rye and wheat.

Smoked Fish

Enjoy the freshly caught and smoked fish that the island is famous for, such as herring and salmon.

Local Beverages

Ruhnu Vodka

Try the island’s signature spirit, Ruhnu Vodka, which is made using a secret recipe passed down through generations.

Where to Stay in Ruhnu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ruhnu Guesthouse

The island’s main guesthouse is the Ruhnu Guesthouse, which offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

Ruhnu Camping Site

For a more affordable option, stay at the Ruhnu Camping Site, which has several cabins and spaces for tents.

What to Buy in Ruhnu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Ruhnu is known for its traditional handicrafts, including knitted items, lacework, and pottery.

Ruhnu Vodka

Bring home a bottle of Ruhnu Vodka to enjoy as a souvenir or a gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruhnu

Ruhnu has preserved its traditional way of life, which reflects its unique mix of Estonian and Swedish cultures. Visitors can experience this through local cuisine, traditional festivals, and handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit Ruhnu

The best time to visit Ruhnu is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, be prepared for occasional rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruhnu

Do:

  • Respect the island’s traditional way of life.
  • Take your trash with you and dispose of it properly.
  • Try the local cuisine and handicrafts.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or plants on the island.
  • Swim without checking for currents and tides.
  • Drive off-road or disturb private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ruhnu

Ruhnu is considered a safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions against theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Ruhnu

The island has a small medical center that provides basic healthcare services. Visitors with more serious medical conditions should seek treatment on the mainland.

Getting to Ruhnu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ruhnu

The nearest airport is Pärnu Airport, which has daily flights to Tallinn and other cities in Estonia.

Bus Stations Near Ruhnu

The closest bus station is in Roomassaare, but the ferry port is a short walk away.

Other Options

Visitors can take a ferry from Pärnu, Munalaid, or Roomassaare to Ruhnu Island.

How to Get Around in Ruhnu

Visitors can rent bikes to explore the island, or take a taxi or public bus to get around.

Where to Go Next from Ruhnu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kihnu Island

Take a day trip to nearby Kihnu Island, which has a rich culture and several museums.

Pärnu

Visit Pärnu, a charming seaside town known for its sandy beaches and historic architecture.

Conclusion

Ruhnu is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and traditional way of life. Whether you want to explore the island’s forests and beaches, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, Ruhnu has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover this charming island for yourself.
