Introduction to West Estonia and Islands

West Estonia and Islands are one of the hidden gems of Europe, located on the shores of the Baltic Sea. They offer a unique mix of cultural wonders, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, making them an ideal destination for any traveler. The region comprises three large islands – Hiiumaa, Saaremaa, and Muhu – and the western part of mainland Estonia.

List of Places to Visit in West Estonia and Islands

Below are a few must-visit places in West Estonia and Islands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kuressaare Castle: One of the best-preserved medieval castles in the Baltic region.
  • Koguva Village Museum: An open-air museum established in a 16th-century village.
  • Hiiumaa Museum: Offers insights into the island’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of West Estonia and Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Matsalu National Park: A paradise for bird watchers and nature lovers.
  • Panga Cliff: A 21-meter high cliff, known for its unique geological formations.
  • Vilsandi National Park: An archipelago of small islands and home to a wide variety of animal species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Estonia and Islands

The region offers several hiking and biking trails that provide stunning views of the natural beauty of West Estonia and Islands. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Saaremaa Coastal Trail: A 387 km long route that takes you through the coastline of Saaremaa Island.
  • Kõpu Nature Trail: A 10-km long track through the forests and bogs in Hiiumaa Island.
  • Pärnu Coastal Meadow Trail: A 16 km trail around the Pärnu Bay, offering beautiful views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in West Estonia and Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Kaali Meteorite Crater: Explore the meteorite craters that were formed more than 4000 years ago.
  • Enjoy the traditional Sauna: Relax in the traditional Finnish-style sauna that is popular in West Estonia and Islands.
  • Watch Wildlife: Go on a safari tour or a guided walk to experience the island’s wildlife, including deer, wild boar, and foxes.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Estonia and Islands

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to West Estonia and Islands:

Day 1: Arrival in Tallinn and drive to Pärnu. Day 2: Explore Pärnu and its beaches. Day 3: Drive to Saaremaa Island and visit Kuressaare Castle. Day 4: Visit the Kaali Meteorite Crater and the Sõrve Lighthouse. Day 5: Hike the Saaremaa Coastal Trail. Day 6: Explore Hiiumaa Island and visit the Kõpu Nature Reserve. Day 7: Return to Tallinn, explore the city and depart.

Food and Drink Scene of West Estonia and Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Black Bread: A dark, sturdy bread that is a staple in Estonian cuisine.
  • Smoked Fish: The island’s fishing heritage means that the sea-fresh fish is smoked to perfection.
  • Karask: A traditional Estonian bread that is sweetened with honey or syrup

Local Beverages

  • Vana Tallinn: A sweet, spiced rum-based liqueur that’s great as an after-dinner drink.
  • Kali: A drink made with fermented rye bread, it is refreshing and unique.
  • Beer: Estonia has a proud brewing tradition, and its beers are a must-try!

Where to Stay in West Estonia and Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The West Estonia and Islands region have a range of accommodations, catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few of the options:

  • Pärnu: Hotel Legend, Seedri Apartment, and Hotel Bristol are popular options.
  • Saaremaa Island: Hotel Arensburg, Saaremaa Spa Hotel, and Pädaste Manor are great choices.
  • Hiiumaa Island: Padu Hotell and Hermannuse Maja are well-respected hotels at reasonable rates.

What to Buy in West Estonia and Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-knitted woolens: Find traditional woolen sweaters, scarves, and gloves in local markets.
  • Handmade ceramics: Beautiful and unique handmade ceramics are found in local studios and craft shops.
  • Juniper products: Bring home juniper-wood items such as cutting boards, jewellery, or other souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of West Estonia and Islands

The islands have a unique mix of cultural influences, from German to Russian to Swedish. People are still adapting to the new era, and they have held onto their traditional fabric. Authentic folk dances, local customs, and dishes are still alive in the region.

Best Time to Visit West Estonia and Islands

Summer, from June to August, is the perfect time to visit the West Estonia and Islands. The weather is warm, and the sun stays out for most of the day. However, the slightly cooler temperatures of spring and autumn offer their unique charm, perfect for those who dislike crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Estonia and Islands

Do:

  • Try the traditional Estonian Sauna.
  • Respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Wander among nature to explore the islands fully.

Don’t:

  • Wear your shoes indoors.
  • Speak derogatory remarks or behave rudely.
  • Pluck flowers or disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Estonia and Islands

The West Estonia and Islands are generally safe, but common sense precautions should still be taken, such as locking doors and avoiding unfamiliar areas after dark.

Health in West Estonia and Islands

Medical care is available in all the major towns in West Estonia and Islands, with a few large medical centers in Pärnu and Saaremaa.

Getting to West Estonia and Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Estonia and Islands

The nearest airport is in Tallinn, which is serviced by several airlines, including budget airlines such as Ryanair and WizzAir.

Bus Stations Near West Estonia and Islands

Buses connect Tallinn with Pärnu and Saaremaa Island, with regular services throughout the day.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are also available for travelers who want to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in West Estonia and Islands

Bicycles, cars, and taxis are the best ways to move around the West Estonia and Island regions. There are bike rentals available in major cities and towns, car rental offices, and taxis from major companies.

Where to Go Next from West Estonia and Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tallinn

The capital city of Tallinn is a short distance away and is a must-visit destination, with fantastic restaurants, shopping, and attractions.

Riga

Riga, the capital of Latvia, is also not too far away, offering visitors more Baltic charm and culture.

Conclusion

West Estonia and Islands are an absolute paradise for travelers looking for natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventures. With a mix of quaint towns, unspoiled beaches, and ancient castles, the islands have something to offer everyone.
