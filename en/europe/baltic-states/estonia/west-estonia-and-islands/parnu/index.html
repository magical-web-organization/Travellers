Introduction to Pärnu

Pärnu is a charming coastal town located on the southwest coast of Estonia, situated just a few kilometers from Latvia. Known as the summer capital of Estonia, Pärnu is famous for its sandy beaches, beautiful parks, and spa resorts. This small town has been a popular tourist destination for centuries, attracting visitors from all over the world. With its rich cultural heritage, fascinating history, and stunning natural beauty, Pärnu offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Pärnu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pärnu boasts a rich cultural heritage, with a range of museums and historic sites that provide insight into its past. The following are some must-visit sites:

  • Pärnu Museum - Located in an 18th-century granary building, this museum showcases Pärnu’s history and culture through a range of exhibitions.

  • Red Tower - One of the oldest buildings in Pärnu, this tower dates back to the 15th century and provides panoramic views of the town.

  • St. Elizabeth’s Church - A stunning Neo-Gothic church built in the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Pärnu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its cultural heritage, Pärnu is also famous for its breathtaking natural beauty. The following are some of the outdoor adventures and scenic views that Pärnu offers:

  • Pärnu Beach - Pärnu’s main attraction is its sandy beach, which stretches for several kilometers and has been awarded the Blue Flag for cleanliness and safety.

  • Kihnu Island - Take a day trip to Kihnu Island and explore its unique culture and traditions, scenic views, and untouched nature.

  • Valgeranna Adventure Park - Perfect for adrenaline junkies, this park offers zip-lining, aerial obstacle courses, and other challenging activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pärnu

Pärnu is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Pärnu Coastal Hiking Trail - This trail takes you through beautiful forests, sandy beaches, and wetlands, providing stunning views along the way.

  • Vändra Cycling Route - A 58-kilometer route through picturesque landscapes, charming villages, and historic sites.

  • Soomaa National Park - Not far from Pärnu, this park offers several hiking trails through wetlands, forests, and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Pärnu: Activities and Tours

Pärnu offers a variety of activities and tours to make the most of your visit, such as:

  • Spa Treatments - Pärnu is famous for its spa resorts, offering a range of treatments, from aromatherapy to massage, to rejuvenate and relax.

  • Boat Tour - Take a boat tour along the Pärnu River and enjoy the scenic beauty of the town and its surroundings from a unique perspective.

  • Golfing - Pärnu has several golf courses, including Pärnu Bay Golf Links for golf aficionados.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pärnu

If you have a week to explore Pärnu, here is an ideal itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Pärnu

Pärnu offers a variety of traditional Estonian and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mulgi Kapsad - A traditional dish from southern Estonia made from sauerkraut and barley.

  • Karask - A dense bread made from rye flour.

  • Leivasupp - A dessert soup made from rye bread, cream, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Vana Tallinn - A sweet rum-based liqueur flavoured with spices and citrus.

  • Kali - A non-alcoholic drink made from black bread and yeast.

  • Estonian Beer - Estonia is known for its excellent beer, with a range of craft breweries offering unique and flavourful brews.

Where to Stay in Pärnu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pärnu has plenty of accommodations, from luxurious spa resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Hedon Spa & Hotel - A luxurious spa resort with a sauna, swimming pool, and modern rooms.

  • Villa Wesset - A boutique hotel in a historic building, offering cosy rooms and a traditional Estonian breakfast.

  • Kanali Villa - A charming guesthouse located near the beach, offering comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Pärnu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pärnu has a rich selection of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementoes.

  • Amber Jewelry - The Baltic region is known for its high-quality amber, and Pärnu has several stores offering amber jewelry.

  • Black Bread - Estonia’s staple food, rye bread, is a must-try. Local bakeries offer fresh loaves.

  • Kihnu Island Embroidery - Kihnu Island is known for its unique embroidery patterns, which make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Pärnu

Estonia has a rich cultural heritage that permeates every aspect of life in Pärnu. Respect for nature and traditional customs is highly valued. Estonians are known for their love of saunas, and saunas remain an essential part of daily life in Pärnu and throughout Estonia. Socializing with friends and family over a meal is also an important cultural value, so be sure to enjoy the local food and drinks with your loved ones.

Best Time to Visit Pärnu

The best time to visit Pärnu is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the days are long. The beach, parks, and outdoor attractions are in full swing during this time. However, if you want to avoid crowds, visit during the shoulder season, when the weather is still pleasant but not as hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pärnu

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Take off your shoes when entering a person’s home.

  • Visit the local markets and sample the traditional foods.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment.

  • Don’t be overly loud or disruptive in public areas.

  • Don’t bring up sensitive topics, such as the Soviet era, unless the conversation is initiated by a local.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pärnu

Estonia is known for being one of the safest countries in Europe, and Pärnu is no exception. However, it is always wise to take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuable items unattended, taking care when crossing the road, and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Pärnu

Pärnu has several hospitals and medical facilities, and it is recommended to purchase travel insurance before your trip. Tap water is safe to drink in Pärnu, but it may differ from your home country’s water supply. Be sure to check for any travel advisories before travelling.

Getting to Pärnu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pärnu

Pärnu does not have its own airport, but there are several nearby airports that cater to the region, including:

  • Tallinn Airport - Located in Estonia’s capital city, Tallinn Airport is the largest airport in Estonia and is approximately a two-hour drive from Pärnu.

  • Riga International Airport - Located in Latvia’s capital city, Riga International Airport is approximately a two-hour drive from Pärnu.

Bus Stations Near Pärnu

Pärnu has a central bus station serving both domestic and international routes. The Estonian bus company, Lux Express, offers a range of trips throughout Estonia and nearby regions, making it easy to get to Pärnu and other destinations.

Other Options

The best way to explore Pärnu is on foot or by bike. Taxis are also readily available and offer a convenient way to get around the town.

How to Get Around in Pärnu

Pärnu is a small town that offers easy access to most attractions, and walking is the best way to explore its beauty. Renting a bike is a great option as well, and many hotels and bike rental services offer bikes for rent at reasonable prices.

Where to Go Next from Pärnu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions you can explore:

  • Tallinn - Estonia’s beautiful capital city, known for its medieval Old Town and vibrant nightlife.

  • Saaremaa Island - An unspoiled island with a dense forest and beautiful beaches.

  • Lahemaa National Park - A beautiful park showcasing Estonia’s natural beauty.

Conclusion

Pärnu is a gem located on the southwest coast of Estonia, offering visitors a range of cultural and outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or nature lover, Pärnu is a must-visit destination. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit, and experience everything this charming coastal town has to offer.
