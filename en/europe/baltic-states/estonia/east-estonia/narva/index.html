If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Estonia, then Narva is the perfect choice. Located in the eastern part of the country, Narva boasts a rich history and stunning natural scenery that will make your visit to Estonia unforgettable. From hiking and biking to exploring its unique heritage, Narva has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top things to see and do in Narva.

Introduction to Narva

Narva is a small city situated on the banks of the Narva River, which forms the border between Estonia and Russia. Because of its strategic location, Narva has been the site of many battles throughout history, most recently in World War II. Today, Narva is a peaceful city that offers visitors a chance to explore its unique heritage and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Narva

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Narva Castle: Narva Castle is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city. Built in the 13th century, it has been beautifully restored and now houses a museum that showcases the history of the city. It’s a must-visit for history lovers.
  • Pimeaed Park: Pimeaed Park, or “Dark Garden,” is a beautiful park filled with trees, trails, and historic ruins. It’s a great place to take a stroll and get away from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Narva Museum: The Narva Museum is another must-visit attraction that showcases the history and culture of Narva. It has fascinating exhibitions on the history of warfare in the region, as well as on the natural history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Narva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Narva-Jõesuu Beach: Narva-Jõesuu Beach is a beautiful stretch of white sand that’s perfect for swimming in the summer. It’s a popular spot for sunbathing and picnicking, and offers stunning views of the Gulf of Finland.
  • Puhtitsa Convent: The Puhtitsa Convent is a beautiful complex of buildings that’s situated in the middle of a forested area. The convent has a peaceful and tranquil atmosphere, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Narva Waterfall: The Narva Waterfall is one of the largest waterfalls in Estonia, and is a must-visit attraction for nature lovers. Located on the Narva River, it offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narva

Narva has several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for those who want to explore the beautiful natural scenery of the city. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Kreenholmi Park to Narva Castle: This route takes you through Kreenholmi Park, which offers stunning views of the Narva River, and then on to Narva Castle.
  • Narva-Jõesuu to Oru Park: This route takes you along the scenic coastline from Narva-Jõesuu to Oru Park, which is a beautiful forested area.
  • Puhtitsa Convent to Kudruküla Village: This route takes you through the beautiful countryside around Narva, and offers stunning views of the Puhtitsa Convent and Kudruküla Village.

Best Things to Do in Narva: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Old Town: Narva’s Old Town is filled with beautiful historic buildings and charming cobblestone streets. Take a stroll through the Old Town and explore its many shops and restaurants.
  • Take a River Cruise: Take a relaxing boat ride down the Narva River and see the city from a different perspective. Many companies offer river cruises that include dinner and live music.
  • Visit the Narva Art Gallery: The Narva Art Gallery is a must-visit attraction for art lovers. It has several exhibitions that showcase the work of both local and international artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Narva

Day 1: Explore the Old Town and visit the Narva Art Gallery. Day 2: Visit Narva Castle and the Narva Museum. Day 3: Take a river cruise down the Narva River. Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Narva at Puhtitsa Convent and Kudruküla Village. Day 5: Hike or bike one of Narva’s scenic routes. Day 6: Relax at Narva-Jõesuu beach. Day 7: Visit the Narva Waterfall and explore Pimeaed Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Narva

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karask: Karask is a traditional Estonian bread that’s made with rye flour and served with butter or sour cream.
  • Sült: Sült is a traditional Estonian dish made with pork, gelatin, and seasonings. It’s served cold and is a popular appetizer.
  • Kohuke: Kohuke is a sweet snack that’s made with curd cheese and coated in chocolate.

Local Beverages

  • Vana Tallinn: Vana Tallinn is a traditional Estonian liqueur that’s made with rum, spices, and vanilla.
  • Saku Originaal: Saku Originaal is a popular Estonian beer that’s light and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Narva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Narva-Jõesuu Spa: This luxurious spa hotel is located in Narva-Jõesuu and offers stunning views of the Gulf of Finland.
  • Inger Hotel: The Inger Hotel is a modern hotel that’s located in the heart of Narva.
  • Guesthouse Herman: This charming guesthouse is located in Narva’s Old Town and offers comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Narva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber Jewelry: Estonia is known for its high-quality amber, and Narva is a great place to buy amber jewelry.
  • Linens and Textiles: Narva is known for its beautiful linens and textiles. Look for locally made tablecloths, towels, and bedspreads.
  • Estonian Sweets: Bring home a taste of Estonia with locally made sweets like chocolates, candies, and marzipan.

Local Culture and Customs of Narva

Narva has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. Estonians are known for their love of nature and their hospitality, so be sure to take the time to appreciate the beautiful scenery and meet the friendly locals.

Best Time to Visit Narva

The best time to visit Narva is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, be prepared for crowds during the peak summer months of July and August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narva

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore Narva’s unique heritage and stunning natural scenery.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially traditional Estonian dishes like karask and sult.
  • Do take a river cruise down the Narva River for a different perspective on the city.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites like the Puhtitsa Convent.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment. Estonia has a strong commitment to preserving its natural beauty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Narva

Narva is a safe city to visit, but like any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect yourself from petty crime.

Health in Narva

Estonia has a good healthcare system that’s available to all visitors. If you need medical help during your stay in Narva, visit one of the local hospitals or clinics.

Getting to Narva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Narva

The nearest airport to Narva is the Tallinn Airport, which is located about 200 kilometers away. From there, you can take a bus or train to Narva.

Bus Stations Near Narva

Narva has a central bus station that offers connections to several cities in Estonia, as well as to Russia and Latvia.

Other Options

If you’re coming from Tallinn, you can take a train to Narva. The journey takes about 3-4 hours and offers stunning views of the Estonian countryside.

How to Get Around in Narva

Narva is a small city that’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to venture further afield, there are several bus routes that connect the different parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Narva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tallinn: The capital city of Estonia is just a few hours away from Narva and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Pärnu: This beautiful seaside resort town is a popular destination for its sandy beaches, spas, and lively nightlife.
  • Tartu: Tartu is Estonia’s second-largest city and is known for its beautiful historic buildings and vibrant cultural scene.

Conclusion

Narva is a hidden gem in Estonia that’s just waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and charming Old Town, Narva offers visitors a chance to explore the best of Estonia. We hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your visit to Narva, and that you have a wonderful time exploring everything this unique city has to offer.
