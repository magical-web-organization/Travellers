Introduction to Jelgava

Located in the Zemgale region of Latvia, Jelgava is a charming city that showcases both natural beauty and cultural heritage. The city is filled with interesting landmarks and monuments, museums, and gardens. Jelgava is also known for its popular festivals and events, such as the International Baltic Ballet Festival and the Jelgava City Festival.

List of Places to Visit in Jelgava

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jelgava Palace: Built in the 18th century, Jelgava Palace is a major landmark that highlights the rich history and culture of the city. The palace used to be the residence of the Duke of Courland, and now serves as a museum that houses various exhibitions and art collections.

  • Jelgava Holy Trinity Church: This iconic Baroque-style church is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Jelgava. Visitors can admire the beautiful paintings and sculptures inside, and take in the church’s serene atmosphere.

  • Jelgava History and Art Museum: This museum provides a comprehensive overview of the city’s history and cultural heritage through various exhibits and installations.

Natural Attractions of Jelgava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lielupe River: Lielupe River is the perfect place for kayaking, fishing, and other water activities. It is also a great spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll along the river banks.

  • Pedestrian Bridge Across Lielupe: This bridge offers a stunning view of the river and the lush greenery surrounding it.

  • Jelgava Recreation Park: Located close to the city center, Jelgava Recreation Park has various walking and cycling paths, playgrounds, and picnic areas. It’s an ideal spot for families looking for a fun day out.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jelgava

Jelgava has numerous trails and paths for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. The most popular ones include the Pedvāle – Kursīši Hiking Trail, which offers beautiful views of Lielupe River, and the Mežotne Hiking Trail that features remarkable historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Jelgava: Activities and Tours

  • Jelgava City Festival: Celebrated annually in late May, the Jelgava City Festival is a vibrant event that showcases the best of the city’s cultural heritage. It features live music performances, street food stalls, and a grand parade.

  • International Baltic Ballet Festival: This is a must-attend event for lovers of dance and performing arts. It is held every two years in June and features world-class ballet dancers from various countries around the world.

  • Jelgava Open-Air Museum: This museum provides a glimpse into the traditional life and culture of the Latvian people, with exhibits such as reconstructed farmhouses, barns, and workshops.

Food and Drink Scene of Jelgava

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pirags: A traditional Latvian pastry that is stuffed with bacon and onions. It’s a staple food in many Latvian households.

  • Latvian Black Bread: This bread is dark, dense, and has a nutty flavor. It’s usually eaten with cheese or butter.

  • Sklandrausis: A sweet pastry made of rye dough and filled with mashed carrots, potatoes, and spices. It’s a popular dessert in Latvia.

Local Beverages

  • Riga Black Balsam: This is a traditional Latvian alcoholic drink that is made from a blend of herbs, roots, and berries.

  • Latvian beer: Latvia has a long tradition of brewing beer, and there are many excellent local breweries that produce delicious variations of this beverage.

Where to Stay in Jelgava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jelgava has various accommodation options to suit all budgets. The most popular ones include:

  • SemaraH Hotel Jelgava: A luxurious 4-star hotel that features modern rooms, spa facilities, and an on-site restaurant.

  • Hotel Jelgava: This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Jelgava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber Jewelry: Latvia is renowned for its high-quality amber, and Jelgava is the perfect place to find unique amber jewelry pieces.

  • Local honey: Jelgava has many beekeepers who produce honey that is considered to be of very high quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Jelgava

The Latvian people are known for being hospitable and friendly. It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home, and to greet others with a handshake or a hug. The traditional Latvian costume is still worn on festive occasions, and traditional folk music and dance are an integral part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Jelgava

The best time to visit Jelgava is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the city is also worth visiting during the winter months when the Christmas markets and festivities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jelgava

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Latvian phrases: Even though English is widely spoken in Jelgava, it’s always appreciated when tourists make an effort to learn some local words and phrases.

  • Respect local customs and traditions: The Latvian people take pride in their culture and heritage, and it’s important to show respect for their customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: Tipping is not mandatory in Latvia, but it’s always appreciated in restaurants and cafes.

  • Wear shoes indoors: It’s considered impolite to wear shoes inside someone’s home in Latvia.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jelgava

Jelgava is generally a safe city, but visitors should be careful when walking alone at night and avoid poorly lit areas. It’s also wise to keep personal belongings, such as wallets and phones, in a secure place.

Health in Jelgava

Jelgava has well-equipped hospitals and medical facilities that can provide quality healthcare services to both locals and tourists. Visitors are advised to have all their necessary vaccinations up-to-date before traveling to Latvia.

Getting to Jelgava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jelgava

  • Riga International Airport: Located approximately 45 km from Jelgava, this is the largest airport in Latvia and is served by various international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Jelgava

  • Jelgava Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the city and provides connections to various towns and cities across Latvia.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at Riga International Airport and drive to Jelgava.

  • Train: The Jelgava Train Station provides daily connections to Riga and other Latvian cities.

How to Get Around in Jelgava

The best way to explore Jelgava is by walking, as most of the attractions are located in the city center. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bicycle or take a bus to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Jelgava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Riga: The capital city of Latvia is only a 35-minute drive from Jelgava and offers more historical landmarks, restaurants, and cultural events to explore.

  • Kemeri National Park: A serene park that features natural sulfur springs, forests, and historical buildings. It’s located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes from Jelgava.

Conclusion

Jelgava is a hidden gem in Latvia that combines natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. It’s a must-visit destination for travelers who want to experience something unique and off-the-beaten-path. With its variety of attractions and activities, there’s something for everyone in Jelgava.
