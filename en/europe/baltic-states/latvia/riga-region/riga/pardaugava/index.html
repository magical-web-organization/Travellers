Introduction to Pārdaugava

Pārdaugava, or “across the Daugava”, is one of Riga’s most interesting and diverse districts. Located on the western bank of the Daugava River, it is home to some of the city’s most important cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures.

Although it is often overshadowed by the more famous Old Town, Pārdaugava is a vibrant and fascinating place to explore, with a rich history and unique personality all its own.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Pārdaugava, from museums and historical sites to hiking and biking routes, food and drink, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Pārdaugava

Before we dive into the details, here’s a rundown of some of the top attractions in Pārdaugava:

  • Riga Castle
  • Latvian National Museum of Art
  • Kipsala International Exhibition Centre
  • Andrejsala Creative Quarter
  • Riga Central Market
  • Vansu Bridge
  • Riga Motor Museum
  • Mezaparks

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pārdaugava is home to some of Riga’s most significant cultural sites, including museums, galleries, and historical landmarks.

Riga Castle

One of the district’s most impressive sights is Riga Castle, located on the banks of the Daugava River. Originally built in the 14th century, the castle has been home to various Latvian rulers throughout the centuries and currently houses the offices of the Latvian president.

Guided tours of the castle are available, allowing visitors to experience the rich history and architectural beauty of this iconic landmark.

Latvian National Museum of Art

If you’re interested in art and culture, be sure to visit the Latvian National Museum of Art. This impressive museum houses one of the largest collections of Latvian art in the world, including works from the Middle Ages to the present day.

The museum’s extensive collections cover everything from paintings and sculptures to decorative arts, photography, and more.

Andrejsala Creative Quarter

For a taste of Riga’s vibrant arts scene, head to Andrejsala Creative Quarter. This former industrial district has been transformed into a hub of creativity, with galleries, artists’ studios, and performance spaces nestled among historic warehouses and factories.

Take a stroll through the area to discover the latest exhibitions and performances, or simply soak up the bohemian atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Pārdaugava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond its cultural treasures, Pārdaugava is also home to some of Riga’s most impressive natural landscapes. From scenic views to hiking and biking routes, there’s plenty to explore in this beautiful district.

Vansu Bridge

One of Pārdaugava’s most iconic landmarks is the Vansu Bridge, which spans the Daugava River. From the bridge, you can enjoy stunning views of the city skyline and the river below.

Mezaparks

For an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, head to Mezaparks. This expansive park is home to a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking trails, a lake for swimming and boating, and a popular open-air amphitheater for concerts and events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pārdaugava

If you’re looking to get out and explore the natural beauty of Pārdaugava, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • The Riga Green Route: This 35-km bike trail takes you through some of Riga’s most beautiful parks and natural areas, including Pārdaugava’s Mezaparks.
  • The Andrejsala-Avantgard Route: Take a leisurely walk or bike ride through Andrejsala Creative Quarter, where you can explore the area’s many galleries, studios, and performance spaces.
  • The Riga Circle Route: This 95-km bike route takes you on a scenic tour of the city and its surrounding areas, including Pārdaugava and the banks of the Daugava River.

Best Things to Do in Pārdaugava: Activities and Tours

Looking for more ideas on what to do in Pārdaugava? Here are a few popular activities and tours to consider:

  • Central Market Tour: Take a guided tour of Riga’s iconic Central Market, located in Pārdaugava. Learn about the market’s history, taste some local specialties, and enjoy behind-the-scenes access to this vibrant hub of commerce.
  • Riga Castle Tour: Discover the history and beauty of Riga Castle on a guided tour of the castle’s most impressive rooms and architectural features.
  • Pārdaugava Food Tour: Experience the district’s diverse culinary scene on a guided walking tour of its best restaurants, cafes, and bars.

Food and Drink Scene of Pārdaugava

Latvian cuisine is a unique mix of Eastern European and Scandinavian influences, with hearty meat dishes and flavorful soups alongside fresh seafood and berry desserts.

In Pārdaugava, you’ll find plenty of restaurants, cafes, and bars serving up a wide range of local and international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular Latvian dishes to try include:

  • Pelmeni: These Russian-style dumplings are a beloved comfort food, often filled with meat, potatoes, or cheese.
  • Rupjmaize: This dense, dark bread is a staple of Latvian cuisine, often served with butter or spreads.
  • Karbonāde: This traditional pork cutlet is breaded and fried, typically served with a side of potatoes or sautéed vegetables.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Latvia is perhaps best known for its beer and herbal teas. Look for these locally made specialties:

  • Aldaris beer: Made in Riga, Aldaris is one of Latvia’s most popular beer brands, with a range of flavors and styles to choose from.
  • Dzintars herbal teas: These flavorful teas are made from a blend of local herbs and spices, and are a great way to relax and unwind after a busy day of sightseeing.

What to Buy in Pārdaugava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home from Pārdaugava? Here are a few ideas:

  • Linen products: Latvia is known for its high-quality linen products, including tablecloths, napkins, and clothing.

  • Local food products: From handmade chocolates to artisanal cheeses and jams, there are plenty of local food products to sample and bring home from Pārdaugava.

Local Culture and Customs of Pārdaugava

Latvia has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from a range of Eastern European and Scandinavian traditions. Visitors to Pārdaugava can experience this unique blend of cultures through its art, food, and music.

Best Time to Visit Pārdaugava

The best time to visit Pārdaugava is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the parks and outdoor spaces are at their most beautiful.

However, the district has plenty to offer year-round, with festivals and events happening throughout the year and winter sports activities available during the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pārdaugava

To make the most of your time in Pārdaugava, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Safety and Health

As a relatively safe and stable country, Latvia is a great destination for travelers. However, it’s still important to take basic precautions to ensure your safety and health while visiting.

Safety in Pārdaugava

While Pārdaugava is generally safe for travelers, use common sense and take precautions like:

  • Keeping your valuables and important documents in a secure location.
  • Avoiding unlit streets and alleys at night.
  • Being aware of your surroundings and trusting your instincts.

Health in Pārdaugava

Latvia has a well-developed healthcare system, with high-quality medical care available in most cities and towns. If you need medical attention while in Pārdaugava, visit the nearest hospital or clinic.

Getting to Pārdaugava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pārdaugava can be reached easily by a variety of transportation options, from flights to buses and trains.

Airports Near Pārdaugava

The closest airport to Pārdaugava is Riga International Airport, which is located just a short distance from the district and offers direct flights to many major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Pārdaugava

Pārdaugava is well-connected to other parts of Riga and Latvia by bus. The main bus station in the district is located on Kipsalas iela, near the International Exhibition Centre.

Other Options

If you’re traveling from other parts of Latvia or nearby countries, you can also reach Pārdaugava by train or car.

How to Get Around in Pārdaugava

Once you’re in Pārdaugava, there are a number of transportation options to help you explore the district.

Some of the most popular options include:

  • Biking: With its extensive network of bike trails, Pārdaugava is a great destination for cyclists.
  • Public transportation: The district is well-served by buses and trams, making it easy to get around quickly and affordably.

Where to Go Next from Pārdaugava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve explored all that Pārdaugava has to offer and are looking for more adventures in Latvia, there are plenty of nearby destinations to consider.

Here are a few top picks:

  • Cesis: This charming medieval town is known for its beautiful castle and historic architecture, as well as its vibrant cultural scene.
