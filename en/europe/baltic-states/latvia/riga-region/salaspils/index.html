Introduction to Salaspils

Located in the Riga region of Latvia, in the heart of the Baltic states in Europe, Salaspils is a charming town that is both historically and culturally significant. Despite being a small town, Salaspils has a lot to offer, including fascinating museums, historical sites, and stunning natural landscapes. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with an in-depth look at everything Salaspils has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Salaspils

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Salaspils Concentration Camp Memorial Ensemble This museum is dedicated to the history of the Salaspils Concentration Camp, which was established by Nazi Germany during World War II. The exhibition includes photographs, documents, and testimonies from survivors. Visitors can also explore the remains of the concentration camp, including the barracks, watchtowers, and the Monument to the Victims of Fascism.

  • Salaspils Old Cemetery Founded in the 16th century, the Salaspils Old Cemetery is one of Latvia’s oldest cemeteries. It is home to many important historical figures, including soldiers from the First and Second World Wars, victims of the Holocaust, and prominent Latvian politicians and intellectuals.

  • Salaspils History and Art Museum This museum focuses on the cultural history of Salaspils and its surrounding areas. The collection includes folk art exhibits, antique furniture, archaeological findings, and rare books.

Natural Attractions of Salaspils: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Latvian Ethnographic Open-Air Museum This open-air museum is located a short drive from Salaspils and provides a window into Latvia’s rural history and traditions. The museum displays various typical farmsteads, from different regions of Latvia in the late 19th and early 20th century.

  • Salaspils Botanical Garden The Salaspils Botanical Garden is a beautiful oasis with over 7,000 species of plants, including rare and endangered species. Visitors can go for a stroll among the gardens and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding environment.

  • Ceriņu Forest Trail This trail is a beautiful 7km long hiking or biking route that winds through the Salaspils forest. The trail has a designated starting point and endpoints and is marked with signs that provide information about the environment and species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salaspils

  • Ceriņu Forest Trail A beautiful 7km long hiking or biking route that winds through Salaspils forest. Enjoy the peace of nature and discover the wonders of the forest with this scenic route.

  • Salaspils Park A 15 km path for cycling, walking and running, linking the Salaspils City area to the Dreiliņi and Mārupe areas. The route runs through the local parks, lush forests, and charming countryside.

Best Things to Do in Salaspils: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Salaspils concentration camp memorial This must-see destination provides the opportunity to learn about the atrocities committed by Nazi Germany in Latvia and honour the victims of the Salaspils concentration camp.

  • Take a guided tour of the Salaspils Old Cemetery Make your way through this beautiful 16th-century cemetery with a knowledgeable guide, who can provide you with information on the prominent people who are buried there and the history of the site itself.

  • Explore the Salaspils History and Art Museum Learn about the cultural history of Salaspils and the surrounding area, including folk art exhibits, antique furniture, archaeology, and rare books.

Food and Drink Scene of Salaspils

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pīrāgi A traditional Latvian dish, pīrāgi is a pastry filled with bacon and onion.

  • Kāposti ar speķi This hearty dish consists of cabbage with bacon and potatoes. The ingredients are mixed together and then baked until crispy and delicious: a must-try for comfort-food lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Black Balsam A traditional herbal liqueur that is known for its strong flavour and medicinal properties. Black Balsam is made from a secret recipe passed down through generations of Latvian herbalists.

  • Riga Balsam Another popular herbal liqueur, famous throughout Latvia. With its complex blend of herbs and spices, It has been a favourite for over two centuries.

What to Buy in Salaspils: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber Jewelry Amber is a popular souvenir in Latvia; you can find various styles of traditional and modern items, such as necklaces, bracelets, earrings, and more.

  • Rye Bread Rye bread is an essential part of Latvian cuisine, and it’s a great souvenir to take back home. It has a unique flavour and is a cherished part of the cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Salaspils

Latvian culture has a rich and diverse history that is deeply rooted in traditional customs and beliefs. Salaspils is no exception and has its unique customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of, such as:

  • Midsummer Festival One of Latvia’s most significant events is the midsummer festival, or Jāņi, celebrating the summer solstice. This festival is one of the biggest events in Latvia, where people celebrate with traditional food, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Salaspils

The best time to visit Salaspils is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the natural landscapes of the town are in full bloom. During this time, visitors can experience many cultural events, festivals, and outdoor activities, making it an excellent time to explore everything Salaspils has to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salaspils

Do:

  • Learn a few Latvian phrases to communicate with locals and show your interest in the culture.

  • Take part in traditional festivals and events to experience the best of Latvian culture.

  • Respect local customs, traditions and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water as it is not safe for drinking.

  • Take photographs of people or cultural sites without asking for permission.

  • Litter in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salaspils

Salaspils is a safe town with low crime rates. However, travellers should still take precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables in a safe place.

Health in Salaspils

Travellers should have up-to-date vaccinations and appropriate medical insurance before travelling to Salaspils. Mosquitoes, ticks, and flies are common during the summer months, so visitors should take appropriate precautions, such as wearing insect repellent and protective clothing.

Getting to Salaspils: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salaspils

  • Riga International Airport Located 36km from Salaspils, Riga International Airport is the primary airport serving the region. There are bus and taxi services available from the airport to Salaspils.

Bus Stations Near Salaspils

  • Salaspils Bus Station Located in the town centre, Salaspils Bus Station is well-connected to several major cities in Latvia and other Baltic countries, with frequent bus services available.

  • Riga Bus Station Located in the heart of Riga, only a short distance from Salaspils, Riga Bus Station is the main transportation hub for the Baltic States, with buses travelling to all major destinations.

Other Options

  • Train Salaspils is connected to Riga and other major towns in Latvia by train. Visitors can take advantage of regular services offered to travel between towns or cities inexpensively.

  • Car rental Renting a car is a convenient way to explore Salaspils and the surrounding area, and there are car rental places available in Riga International Airport and the center of Riga.

How to Get Around in Salaspils

The best way to get around Salaspils is on foot or by bike. The roads are well-maintained, and the town is small enough to explore easily on foot. Visitors can also rent bikes in Salaspils and take advantage of its well-designed biking trails.

Where to Go Next from Salaspils: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Riga The capital of Latvia, Riga, is located less than 20 km away from Salaspils. Visitors can explore the city’s old town, stunning architecture, and excellent food scene.

  • Sigulda Located 40 km from Salaspils, Sigulda is a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful castles, hiking trails, and bobsleigh track.

Conclusion

Salaspils is a hidden gem, filled with fascinating museums, stunning natural landscapes, and unique traditions and customs. Whether visitors are interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in Salaspils. A visit to Salaspils allows travellers to dive into Latvia’s rich culture, explore its history, and connect with its natural beauty and friendly people.
