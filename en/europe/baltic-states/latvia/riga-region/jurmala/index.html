Introduction to Jūrmala

Jūrmala is a coastal city located in the Riga region of Latvia, in the Baltic states of Europe. It’s a popular vacation destination known for its sandy beaches, pine forests, and spa resorts. The city has a unique blend of modern and historic architecture, reflecting its long history as a spa town since the 19th century. Its close proximity to Riga makes it an accessible destination for travelers seeking a beach escape with a city twist.

List of Places to Visit in Jūrmala

Jūrmala offers a wide range of attractions for tourists. Here are the top places to visit in Jūrmala:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jūrmala City Museum - This museum offers visitors an in-depth look at the history and culture of Jūrmala, including its evolution from a small fishing village to a bustling spa town.
  • Jūrmala Open-Air Museum - This living museum showcases traditional Latvian rural architecture and culture. Visitors can explore restored farmhouses, mills, and other structures from the 18th and 19th centuries.
  • Dubulti Lutheran Church - One of the oldest surviving buildings in Jūrmala, this church dates back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Jūrmala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jūrmala Beach - The city’s most famous attraction, the 33 km long sandy beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.
  • Kemeri National Park - This nature reserve boasts forested dunes, bogs, and mineral springs. Visitors can hike the many trails throughout the park.
  • Dzintari Forest Park - This park offers visitors a peaceful escape from the busy beach scene by offering scenic trails perfect for walking, hiking or cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jūrmala

With hundreds of kilometers of biking and hiking trails offered throughout the region, visitors are able to enjoy a myriad of activities in the great outdoors of Jūrmala. From beginner family-friendly piste to even more challenging terrain, visitors have ample opportunities to explore the varied landscapes throughout the city.

Best Things to Do in Jūrmala: Activities and Tours

There is something for everyone in Jūrmala, whether visitors are seeking adventure, relaxation, or culture. Some of the top things to do in Jūrmala include:

  • Spa treatments - Enjoy a variety of massage and spa treatments available throughout the city.
  • Concerts - Attend concerts and other cultural events at the majestic Dzintari Concert Hall.
  • Aqua Park Livu - One of the most popular attractions in Latvia, this indoor and outdoor water park boasts endless waterslides and pools to keep visitors of all ages entertained.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jūrmala

In a week you can explore both Jūrmala and Riga comfortably.

Day Activities
1 Visit Jūrmala City Museum, explore the Dubulti Lutheran Church, and enjoy an afternoon on the beach.
2 Spend the day at Kemeri National Park hiking the trails and discovering the mineral springs.
3 Take a trip to Livu Aqua Park, one of the most popular attractions in Latvia.
4 Explore Dzintari Forest Park, perfect for relaxing strolls or even cycling.
5 Attend a concert at the Dzintari Concert Hall.
6 Visit the Jūrmala Open-Air Museum and experience traditional Latvian culture.
7 Take a day trip to nearby Riga, the capital city of Latvia. Explore the city’s rich history and culture, and visit some of the famous landmarks such as Riga Old Town, Art Nouveau district and the Art Museum Riga Bourse

Food and Drink Scene of Jūrmala

When it comes to dining, Jūrmala offers a unique mix of local and international cuisine. Visitors can indulge in everything from fresh seafood to traditional Latvian dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rupjmaize - Latvian rye bread that is dense, flavorful, and perfect with butter and cheese.
  • Rassolnik - Hearty soup made with pickled cucumbers, potatoes, and beef.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass - A refreshing, non-alcoholic drink made from fermented bread, spices and water.

Where to Stay in Jūrmala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Travelers can choose from a variety of accommodations in Jūrmala, ranging from luxurious spa resorts to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options available.

  • Baltic Beach Hotel and Spa - 5-star hotel on the beach with an onsite spa and wellness centre.
  • Vienības prospekts Apartment - Offering apartments with views of Jomas Street.
  • Roseville Apartments - A perfect quiet spot to stay in Jūrmala.

What to Buy in Jūrmala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take a piece of Jūrmala home with them by shopping for local products and souvenirs. The city is known for handcrafted items, including traditional Latvian textiles, ceramics, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Jūrmala

One unique aspect of Jūrmala is its history as a spa town since the 19th century. During this period, many wealthy and influential people would flock to Jūrmala to take the curative waters and refreshments.

Best Time to Visit Jūrmala

The best time to visit Jūrmala is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. It’s important to note that this is also peak tourism season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jūrmala

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including smoked fish and traditional Latvian dishes.
  • Visit the museums and other cultural attractions.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities available, including hiking and water sports.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Engage in any illegal or unethical activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jūrmala

Jūrmala is generally a safe city for tourists, however, as with any destination, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Standard precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding unlit, isolated areas can minimize the risk of theft or assault.

Health in Jūrmala

There is no particular health risk in Jūrmala, however, visitors should purchase travel medical insurance and make sure they have all necessary vaccinations before heading to the region. Additionally, visitors should take precautions against sunburn and dehydration during the summer months.

Getting to Jūrmala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Jūrmala by a variety of means of transportation.

Airports Near Jūrmala

  • Riga International Airport - Located approximately 25 km from Jūrmala, this airport is served by numerous airlines and offers flights to destinations across Europe.

Bus Stations Near Jūrmala

  • Jūrmala Bus Station - This bus station offers connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Train - Visitors can take the train from Riga Central Station to Jūrmala Station, which takes approximately 25 minutes.
  • Taxi / ride-hailing apps - Visitors can also take a taxi or ride-hailing apps like Bolt which are widely used in Latvia.

How to Get Around in Jūrmala

Jūrmala is fairly easy to navigate and visitors can reach many of the city’s attractions by walking or cycling. Visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and trains, as well as taxis or ride-hailing apps.

Where to Go Next from Jūrmala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Jūrmala, visitors can easily visit nearby destinations and take day trips to explore more of Latvia. Some of the best destinations to visit include:

  • Riga - Latvia’s capital city is just a short train ride away, and boasts a rich history, vibrant culture, and numerous attractions.
  • Sigulda - A picturesque town known for its breathtaking landscapes and stunning medieval castle.

Conclusion

Jūrmala is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a relaxing beach vacation coupled with cultural and outdoor experiences. With its world-renowned spa resorts, natural wonders, and rich culture, there’s something for everyone in Jūrmala. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip to Latvia’s favorite destination on the beach.
