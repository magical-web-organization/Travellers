Introduction to Kėdainiai

Located in Aukštaitija, Lithuania, Kėdainiai is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Since the 14th century, the town has been a hub of Lithuanian cultural and historical significance. Kėdainiai’s beautifully preserved Old Town boasts several grand churches and stately manors, making it a must-see destination for history buffs.

But the town is not just steeped in history and culture. Kėdainiai is also home to several natural wonders that are sure to leave you awestruck, from pristine lakes and forests to picturesque hills. Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or a glimpse into Lithuania’s rich heritage, Kėdainiai has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kėdainiai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kėdainiai is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a peek into the town’s cultural and architectural heritage.

The Church of St. George

The Church of St. George is the most prominent and spectacular architectural monument of Kėdainiai. Its construction began in 1604, and it was completed in 1650. The church’s stunning Baroque altar is one of the most impressive in Lithuania.

Kėdainiai Manor

The Kėdainiai Manor is one of the most beautiful manors in Lithuania and one of the best examples of early Baroque architecture in the country. The manor dates back to the 17th century and features a unique architectural style that is sure to leave you breathless.

The Museum of Kėdainiai Region

The Museum of Kėdainiai Region showcases the town’s rich cultural and architectural heritage. Visitors can learn about the history of the town and its people, see collections of 18th and 19th-century clothing, household objects, and textiles, and explore exhibits about the revolutionary period of 1918-1919 in Lithuania.

Natural Attractions of Kėdainiai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kėdainiai’s stunning natural scenery is a must-see for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Širvėna Regional Park

Širvėna Regional Park is one of Lithuania’s most beautiful nature reserves. The park’s dense forests, rolling hills, and pristine lakes are home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, elk, and beavers. The park is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

Biržai Regional Park

Biržai Regional Park is another nature reserve that offers breathtaking views and outdoor activities. The park’s top attractions include the impressive Biržai Castle, the Varpas Hill Fort, and the picturesque Lake Širvėna. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and swimming in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

Kėdainiai City Tour

The Kėdainiai City Tour is a scenic, easy hike that takes visitors through the town’s historic Old Town. Visitors can explore centuries-old churches, grand manors, and colorful alleyways while learning about Kėdainiai’s fascinating history.

Žeimena River Route

The Žeimena River Route is a popular biking route that offers stunning views of the Žeimena River. Cyclists can enjoy 40 km of scenic trails that take them through picturesque small towns, historic sites, and beautiful forests.

Best Things to Do in Kėdainiai: Activities and Tours

Kėdainiai offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best:

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Experience Kėdainiai’s stunning natural beauty from a unique perspective with a hot air balloon ride. Soar above the treetops and take in breathtaking views of the region’s rolling hills, pristine lakes, and dense forests.

Wine Tasting Tour

Explore Lithuania’s emerging wine scene with a wine tasting tour in Kėdainiai. Sample delicious wines from local Vineyards and wineries while learning about the region’s wine-making history and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the region’s rich culinary heritage and emerging wine industry. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Kėdainiai Black Pudding

Kėdainiai black pudding is a traditional Lithuanian dish made from pork blood, barley, and pork fat. The dish is typically served with potatoes, pickles, and mustard.

Potato Zeppelins

Potato Zeppelins are another typical Lithuanian dish. They are made from grated potatoes and stuffed with meat, cheese, mushrooms, or curd.

Local Beverages

Lithuanian Beer

Lithuania is famous for its beer, and Kėdainiai is home to several breweries that offer a wide range of beer varieties. A few local breweries to try include Gubernija, Kalnapilis, and Svyturys.

Lithuanian Wine

Lithuania’s wine scene is still developing, but several wineries in and around Kėdainiai are making a name for themselves. Local wines to try include the varieties produced by the Liepkalnis winery and the Kėdainiai grape plantation.

Where to Stay in Kėdainiai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kėdainiai offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

Hotel Apvalaus Stalo Klubas

Hotel Apvalaus Stalo Klubas is a 4-star hotel located in the town center. The hotel features modern rooms equipped with all the necessary amenities, as well as a spa and wellness center.

Guesthouse Birželio Vėjai

Guesthouse Birželio Vėjai is located on the shores of Lake Biržai and offers cozy rooms and picturesque lake views.

Hostel Krispolia

Hostel Krispolia is a budget-friendly option located in the heart of Kėdainiai’s Old Town. The hostel offers dormitory and private rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Kėdainiai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kėdainiai offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts. Here are a few to check out:

Honey Products

Kėdainiai is home to several honey farms that produce high-quality honey and other honey-based products, such as mead and beeswax candles.

Handmade Crafts

Kėdainiai has a thriving community of artisans and craft makers who produce handmade ceramics, textiles, and wooden crafts. These make for unique and beautiful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are a few to keep in mind:

Christmas Eve Customs

Christmas Eve is a significant holiday in Kėdainiai, and locals have several customs and traditions associated with it. One such tradition is to leave an empty chair at the dinner table for an unexpected guest or a wandering soul.

Lithuanian Folk Music

Lithuanian folk music is an essential part of the town’s cultural heritage. Several local groups and ensembles perform traditional music at festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Kėdainiai

The best time to visit Kėdainiai is between June and September when the weather is mild and the region’s natural beauty is at its peak. This is also the peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kėdainiai

Do:

  • Visit Kėdainiai’s Old Town and historical sites to learn about the town’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Explore Kėdainiai’s stunning natural scenery by hiking, biking, or taking a hot air balloon ride.
  • Try local dishes and beverages to get a taste of the region’s culinary traditions.

Don’t:

  • Skip Kėdainiai’s cultural attractions in favor of outdoor activities. The town’s history and architecture are a must-see.
  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothes for hiking and biking.
  • Leave without trying the local honey and other bee products.

Safety and Health

Kėdainiai is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always important to take basic safety precautions such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai is a safe town, but tourists should still take common sense safety precautions such as staying in well-lit areas at night, not carrying large sums of cash, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai has a good healthcare system, and visitors can find pharmacies and medical facilities throughout the town.

Getting to Kėdainiai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kėdainiai

The closest airport to Kėdainiai is Vilnius International Airport, which is around 100 km away. Visitors can take a bus or hire a car to reach Kėdainiai from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai has a bus station that connects the town with other Lithuanian cities and towns, including Vilnius and Kaunas.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kėdainiai by train or car. The town is located just off the A6 highway, which connects Vilnius and Kaunas.

How to Get Around in Kėdainiai

Kėdainiai is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. However, visitors who want to explore the surrounding nature reserves will need to hire a car or use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kėdainiai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kėdainiai is located in the heart of Lithuania and is an excellent starting point for exploring the country. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Vilnius

Vilnius is Lithuania’s charming capital city, located just 100 km from Kėdainiai. The city is known for its stunning Old Town, Baroque architecture, and vibrant cultural scene.

Kaunas

Kaunas is Lithuania’s second-largest city and is located just 60 km from Kėdainiai. The city boasts a beautiful Old Town, several museums and historical sites, and a thriving modern art scene.

Kėdainiai is a fascinating destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you want to explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, hike through its stunning natural scenery, or delve into Lithuania’s emerging wine industry, Kėdainiai has it all.
