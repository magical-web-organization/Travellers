Introduction to Baltic states

The Baltic states, comprising of Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania, are located in northern Europe and have a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and a diverse range of experiences to offer visitors. From gorgeous fairytale-like cities to golden beaches, lush green forests to serene lakes, the Baltic states have it all. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, food and drink scenes, accommodations, and everything else you need to know to plan your perfect trip to the Baltic states.

Food and Drink Scene of Baltic states

The Baltic states offer unique and diverse cuisines that reflect the region’s varying cultural influences over time. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cepelinai, potato dumplings stuffed with meat or curd cheese, Lithuania
  • Rupjmaize, Latvian dark rye bread, Latvia
  • Smoked fish, Estonia
  • Kulfi, a sweet cheese pie, Lithuania
  • Kama, a mix of grains and flours, Estonia/Latvia

Local Beverages

  • Kvass, a fermented rye bread drink, Lithuania/Latvia
  • Black Balsam, a herbal liqueur, Latvia
  • Vana Tallinn, a rum-based liqueur, Estonia
  • Beer, various local brands

Local Culture and Customs of Baltic states

The Baltic states have a rich cultural heritage, with many unique customs and traditions still being practiced today. For example, Sauna rituals in Estonia, the Kupolines (traditional Lithuanian wooden houses with thatched roofs), and traditional weaving techniques used to make handicrafts that reflect the region’s cultural longevity.

Best Time to Visit Baltic states

The best time to visit the Baltic states usually falls between May and September when the weather is mild and the days are long. During that time, the cities and countryside blossom with flora and fauna, allowing visitors to relish in the seasonal beauty of the region. The winter months of December to March, which offer fewer crowds and are ideal for winter sports activities, can also be a great time to visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baltic states

The Baltic states have a relatively low crime rate, and the region is mostly safe for travelers. Visitors are advised to be aware of pickpocketing and to avoid walking alone in an unfamiliar city at night. It is better to avoid political rallies or large gatherings.

Health in Baltic states

Public health standards are generally good in the region. Visitors are advised to take the standard precautions against diseases such as tetanus and tick-borne illnesses. Tap water is drinkable in Estonia and Latvia, but bottled water is recommended for consumption in Lithuania.
