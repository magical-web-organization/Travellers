Introduction to Kalamata

Kalamata is a vibrant city located in Messenia, in Peloponnese, Greece. It is the second-largest city in the region and is known for its stunning natural beauty, cultural significance, and rich culinary scene. The city is surrounded by mountains, olive groves, and the beautiful Mediterranean sea, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. Kalamata is also a hub of historical and cultural significance, with a variety of museums, monuments, and landmarks. In this travel guide, we will walk you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, and much more to help you plan your perfect trip to Kalamata.

List of Places to Visit in Kalamata

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kalamata boasts a rich history and culture that can be explored through its museums and many historical sites. Some must-visit places in this category are:

The Kalamata Castle

The Kalamata Castle is situated at the top of a hill and offers a panoramic view of Messenia and the Gulf of Messenia. The Castle was built in the 13th century and is a perfect example of military architecture in the region.

The Archaeological Museum of Messenia

The Archaeological Museum of Messenia showcases a range of artifacts and exhibits from the prehistoric and classical periods, including an impressive collection of pottery, coins, and sculptures.

The Benakeion Museum of Kalamata

This museum exhibits a range of local art and material culture from the 18th to the 20th centuries, including traditional costumes, jewelry, furniture, and paintings.

Natural Attractions of Kalamata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The city of Kalamata has a variety of natural wonders, including mountains, trails, and beaches. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore in Kalamata:

Mount Taygetus

Mount Taygetus is the highest mountain range in the Peloponnese. It offers many hiking trails for beginners and experts, and you can enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

The Voidokilia Beach

The Voidokilia Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Greece. It has crystal clear waters, fine sand, and a fascinating curved shape.

The Polylimnio Waterfalls

The Polylimnio Waterfalls are located in a lush natural park in Messinia and offer incredible views of the surrounding landscape. This is an ideal spot for a picnic or a refreshing swim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalamata

Kalamata is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The region has a variety of trails that cater to both beginners and advanced hikers. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area include:

Aristomenous Trail

This trail is a 17 km path starting from the village of Aristomenous. It passes through beautiful natural landscapes and historical sites such as the Temple of Apollo Epicurius Awakas and the Neda river. It is ideal for both hiking and biking.

Mount Taygetus

Mount Taygetus offers a variety of hiking trails for all levels of fitness. The trails range from moderate to difficult, making it ideal for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Kalamata: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the above sites, there are many other things to do in Kalamata. These include:

Olive Oil Tasting Tour

Kalamata is known for its olive oil, and there are many places in the city where you can try different varieties of olive oil and learn about traditional production techniques.

Fishing Tour

Take a fishing tour in the Kalamata Gulf and catch fish while enjoying the beauty of the sea.

Wine Tasting Tour

Visit the vineyards and wineries located in Messenia and have the opportunity to taste some of the best wines in Greece.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kalamata

Here is a week-long itinerary for Kalamata:

Day 1: Visit the Kalamata Castle and the Archaeological Museum of Messenia.

Day 2: Hike the Aristomenous Trail.

Day 3: Visit the Polylimnio Waterfalls and enjoy a picnic.

Day 4: Take a fishing tour in the Kalamata Gulf.

Day 5: Visit the Benakeion Museum of Kalamata and explore the local art and material culture.

Day 6: Go olive oil tasting and wine tasting.

Day 7: Relax at the Voidokilia Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Kalamata

Kalamata is known for its rich cuisine, which is influenced by Mediterranean flavors. Here is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Kalamata Olives

Kalamata olives are famous worldwide, and you must try them when you visit the city. They are usually served with olive oil, bread, and feta cheese.

Souvlaki

Souvlaki is a popular Greek dish made of grilled meat, vegetables, and pita bread, topped with tzatziki sauce.

Pastourma

Pastourma is a type of cured meat made of beef, which is thinly sliced and seasoned with spices.

Local Beverages

Kalamata Wine

The region is famous for its wine, and you can taste some of the best wines in Greece when you visit the local vineyards.

Ouzo

Ouzo is a traditional Greek alcoholic drink made of anise and is usually served with appetizers.

Where to Stay in Kalamata: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kalamata has a range of accommodation options for all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

Pharae Palace Hotel

The Pharae Palace Hotel is located in the city center and offers a comfortable stay with modern amenities.

Villa Teo

Villa Teo is a guesthouse located near the beach and offers a cozy and peaceful stay with beautiful sea views.

Elektra Hotel

The Elektra Hotel is located near the Kalamata Castle and offers a beautiful view of the city.

What to Buy in Kalamata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kalamata has a variety of local products that you can bring home as souvenirs, including:

Olive Oil

Kalamata is known for its olive oil, and you can purchase high-quality olive oil from the local markets.

Traditional Costumes

You can purchase traditional Greek costumes and jewelry from the local stores.

Handmade Ceramics

Kalamata has a range of artisans that make beautiful ceramic pieces, including vases, plates, and bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalamata

Greece has a rich culture, and Kalamata is no exception. Some customs and traditions that you should know before visiting Kalamata include:

Greek Hospitality

Greece is known for its hospitality, and you will find the same warmth and friendliness when you visit Kalamata.

Siesta

Many businesses in Kalamata have a siesta, which is a midday break. Therefore, it’s a good idea to plan your day accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Kalamata

The best time to visit Kalamata is from June to September when the weather is warm, and you can enjoy the beaches and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalamata

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen when you are outdoors to protect your skin from the sun.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Overstay your visa.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Photograph people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Kalamata:

Safety in Kalamata

Kalamata is generally safe, but it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as taking care of personal belongings and not wandering in poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Kalamata

The city has many hospitals and clinics that provide excellent medical care. It’s advisable to have travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical expenses.

Getting to Kalamata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the transportation options to get to Kalamata:

Airports Near Kalamata

The Kalamata International Airport is located 7 km west of the city and operates domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kalamata

Kalamata has a bus station that connects it to many destinations in Greece, including Athens and Thessaloniki.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available for transport around the city and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Kalamata

Kalamata is a small city, and you can easily explore the main attractions on foot. Buses and taxis are also available for transport.

Where to Go Next from Kalamata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Kalamata are:

Ancient Messene

Ancient Messene is a well-preserved archaeological site located 37 km north of Kalamata. It offers a glimpse into the ancient Greek civilization and culture.

Mani Peninsula

The Mani Peninsula is a beautiful region located south of Kalamata. It has many traditional villages, stunning beaches, and rugged mountain landscapes.

Conclusion

Kalamata is a beautiful city that has something for everyone. Whether you are interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, food, or wine, Kalamata has it all. We hope that this guide has helped you plan your next trip to this enchanting destination in Greece.
