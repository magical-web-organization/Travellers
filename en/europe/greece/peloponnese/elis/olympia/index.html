Introduction to Olympia

Located in Peloponnese, Greece, Olympia is a sacred sanctuary dedicated to Zeus, the father of Greek gods. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the ancient Olympic Games, an event where all Greek city-states send their athletes to compete against each other. Olympia also served as the center of Ancient Greek religion and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Olympia

There are plenty of places to visit in Olympia, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Archaeological Museum of Olympia: This museum hosts a vast collection of ancient Greek sculpture and artifacts, including the statue of Hermes by Praxiteles and the Nike of Paionius.
  2. Temple of Zeus: The temple holds the famous Statue of Zeus, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
  3. Olympic Stadium: The stadium is where the ancient Olympic games were held every four years, and it still captures the spirit of the ancient event.

Natural Attractions of Olympia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Kronos: This mountain offers stunning views of the sanctuary and the surrounding landscape. You can hike to the top and explore the cave of the Nymphs.
  2. Alfeios River: The river runs through the sanctuary of Olympia, and there are plenty of spots to enjoy a picnic, fishing, or a swim.
  3. Kaiafas Lake: The lake is a natural hot spring with a natural spa surrounding it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Olympia

Olympia is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with fantastic routes for hiking and biking. Here are some of the most popular:

  1. Olympia Archeological Site trail: This trail takes you through the ancient ruins of Olympia, offering a glimpse of Greece’s rich history and culture.
  2. Kaiafas Lake trail: This trail takes you around the lake, allowing you to enjoy the natural beauty and thermal springs.
  3. Valley of the Nymphs trail: This trail winds its way through the stunning valley of the Nymphs, immersing you into the ancient Greek mythology.

Best Things to Do in Olympia: Activities and Tours

  1. Olympic Games reenactment: You can participate in a modern-day version of the Olympic games, including running, high jump, and discus throwing.
  2. Olympia Food Tour: Olympia is known for its traditional cuisine, and a guided food tour is the perfect way to sample everything from olive oil to local wines.
  3. Art and Culture Tour: There are several guided tours available, taking you to the top cultural attractions of Olympia, including its museums and historical sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Olympia

Here is an example of a week-long itinerary for Olympia:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a local hotel. Day 2: Visit the Archaeological Museum of Olympia, the temple of Zeus, and the ancient stadium. Day 3: Hike to Mount Kronos and explore the cave of the Nymphs. Day 4: Join a food tour and taste the local cuisine of Olympia. Day 5: Explore the Alfeios River and enjoy a picnic or swim. Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Valley of the Nymphs and soak up the rich Greek mythology. Day 7: Departure from Olympia.

Food and Drink Scene of Olympia

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Moussaka: A delicious casserole made with eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.
  2. Souvlaki: Skewers of marinated meat (lamb, chicken, or pork) grilled over hot coals.
  3. Tzatziki: A tangy dip made with strained yogurt, cucumbers, garlic, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  1. Agiorgitiko Wine: A dry red wine made from the Agiorgitiko grape, grown exclusively in the Peloponnese.
  2. Tsipouro: A traditional Greek spirit made from grapes or other fruits.
  3. Frappé: A cold coffee drink made with instant coffee, water, sugar, and ice.

Where to Stay in Olympia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodations in Olympia, ranging from luxury hotels to basic guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  1. Hotel Europa: A 4-star hotel with a swimming pool, a spa, and a restaurant.
  2. Olympia Palace: A cozy guest house with comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  3. Olympion Asty Hotel: A 3-star hotel located in the heart of Olympia city.

What to Buy in Olympia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Olives and Olive Oil: Greece is known for its high-quality olives and olive oil, and you can find a variety of types in Olympia.
  2. Wine: Olympia is surrounded by vineyards, making it an excellent place to buy local wines.
  3. Handmade Crafts: You can find a range of handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles, in the local stores and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Olympia

Greek culture is famous worldwide for its strong traditions, flavorful cuisine, and hospitable people. Greeks are proud of their heritage, and they expect their visitors to respect their customs and behave respectfully.

Best Time to Visit Olympia

The best time to visit Olympia is during the spring and early summer months, between April and June, or in the fall, between September and November. The weather is pleasant, and the crowds are fewer, making it the perfect time to explore the ancient ruins and enjoy the natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Olympia

Do:

  1. Do respect Greek culture and traditions.
  2. Do dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  3. Do use basic Greek phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t overindulge in alcohol in public places.
  2. Don’t litter or damage historical sites.
  3. Don’t be late for appointments or tours.

Safety and Health

Safety in Olympia

Olympia, like most tourist destinations, experiences petty crime and theft. Travelers should be vigilant and take necessary precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding dimly lit areas at night.

Health in Olympia

Greece has a good healthcare system, and travelers can access healthcare services from public and private hospitals. However, it’s essential to have travel health insurance to cover any medical needs.

Getting to Olympia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Olympia

The closest airport to Olympia is the Araxos International Airport, about 100km away. Kalamata International Airport is the nearest international airport, which takes about 2.5 hours of travel time.

Bus Stations Near Olympia

KTEL operates regional buses across Peloponnese, and you can take a direct bus from Athens to Olympia.

Other Options

You can also take a train from Athens to Kiato and then continue to Olympia by bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Olympia

The best way to move around in Olympia is on foot as a central city and most of the attractions are within walking distance. You can also take a taxi or rent a car or a bike to explore further.

Where to Go Next from Olympia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations to explore after visiting Olympia, including:

  1. Nafplio: A beautiful coastal city located about 2 hours from Olympia, known for its Venetian fortress, cobbled streets, and stunning architecture.
  2. Ancient Corinth: An archaeological site renowned for the Temple of Apollo and the Acrocorinth fortress, located about 1.5 hours from Olympia.
  3. Mount Olympus: One of the highest peaks in Greece, located about 4 hours from Olympia.

Conclusion

Olympia is a destination that has something for every traveler. Whether you are here to explore ancient ruins, enjoy outdoor adventures, or indulge in its delectable cuisine, Olympia is a must-visit place in Greece. With this comprehensive tour guide to Olympia, you are all set to plan your next adventure.
