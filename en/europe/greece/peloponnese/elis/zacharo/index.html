Introduction to Zacharo

Located in the Elis region of Peloponnese, Greece, Zacharo is a charming coastal town that attracts visitors from all over the world. Known for its natural beauty, rich history, and delicious local cuisine, Zacharo has something to offer everyone. In this guide, we will walk you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Zacharo, as well as provide insider tips on local customs and safety.

List of Places to Visit in Zacharo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zacharo is home to many historical sites and museums. We recommend visiting the following:

  1. The Museum of Ancient Elis: This museum showcases the history and culture of the ancient city of Elis.

  2. The Archaeological Museum of Olympia: Learn about the history and origin of the Olympic Games in this informative museum.

Natural Attractions of Zacharo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zacharo has plenty of outdoor attractions for adventure lovers:

  1. Zacharo Beach: This sandy beach stretches 10km in length and is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

  2. The Neda Waterfalls: A beautiful spot for hiking and picnic. Visitors can trek through dense forests, rivers, and waterfalls for a refreshing experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zacharo

Zacharo offers plenty of hiking and biking trails for enthusiasts, including:

  1. Rigani Hill Trail: Follow this trail through the hills that run parallel to the coastline for stunning views of the landscape and the Ionian Sea.

  2. Tetrazio Forest Trail: This trail takes you through an enchanting forest with a collection of rare trees, including oak and chestnut.

Best Things to Do in Zacharo: Activities and Tours

Other exciting activities to do in Zacharo include:

  1. Wine Tasting: Try the excellent local wines of Zacharo, accompanied by flavorful Mediterranean cuisine at the region’s finest vineyards.

  2. Horse Riding: Ride along scenic trails and embrace the breathtaking views of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zacharo

To get the most out of your trip to Zacharo, follow this itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the beach and try watersports Day 2: Visit the Museum of Ancient Elis Day 3: Hike the Tetrazio Forest Trail and visit the waterfalls. Day 4: Experience horseback riding and wine tasting tour. Day 5: Visit the Archaeological Museum of Olympia. Day 6: Rest and relax at Zacharo beach. Day 7: Explore the town of Zacharo, trying authentic local dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Zacharo

The food and drink scene in Zacharo is an exciting fusion of traditions and modernism. Many restaurants, cafes, and bars offer authentic, home-cooked traditional dishes accompanied by the local wine.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tsigarelli: This dish is made using tender goat meat and cooked with bitter and wild greens.

  2. Teganosto: This dish features calf meat, slow-roasted, and served with potatoes or a salad.

Local Beverages

The local wines are a must-try in Zacharo. With vineyards located in the region, wines like Moschofilero and Agiorgitiko are excellent for complementing any meal.

Where to Stay in Zacharo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zacharo offers plenty of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best accommodation options are:

  1. Pleiades Sea and Mountain Villas: These villas feature a swimming pool and outdoor terrace, overlooking the Ionian Sea.

  2. Hotel Lagouvardos: Located near the beach, this hotel has a bar by the pool, restaurant which serves traditional dishes and offers luxurious rooms.

What to Buy in Zacharo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some local products that make for great souvenirs include:

  1. Olive Oil: Zacharo is known for its premium extra virgin olive oil. Look for it at local shops or markets.

  2. Handicrafts: You will find plenty of locally made handicrafts and decorative items in Zacharo’s markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Zacharo

Zacharo has a rich, vibrant culture that is closely associated with traditional Greek customs. As a visitor, it’s important to respect local customs, including:

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Be respectful towards elders and use formal language when addressing them.
  • Avoid provocative clothing, especially when dining out.

Best Time to Visit Zacharo

The best time to visit Zacharo is during the late spring or early autumn months, when the weather is pleasant, and prices are low. The peak season runs from July to August when prices may be high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zacharo

Do:

  • Try the local wines and cuisine.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Wear provocative clothing in public places.
  • Disrespect elders or locals.
  • Litter or damage the natural beauty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zacharo

Zacharo is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should take some safety precautions. This includes:

  • Avoid leaving valuables unattended
  • Always lock your car and hotel rooms
  • Avoid walking alone in dark areas at night

Health in Zacharo

Zacharo has excellent medical facilities, including a hospital and pharmacies. Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance before your trip, and pack any necessary prescription medicines.

Getting to Zacharo: Transportation Options and Logistics

The best way to reach Zacharo is by road.

Airports Near Zacharo

The nearest airport is Kalamata International Airport, which is around 55 km away from Zacharo.

Bus Stations Near Zacharo

Zacharo has a bus station that connects to nearby locations, such as Kyparissia, Katakolo and Olympia, among others.

Other Options

Taxis are available in Zacharo for short distances or you can rent a car.

How to Get Around in Zacharo

The best way to get around Zacharo is to rent a car. This will give you the flexibility to explore all of the attractions and natural beauty in and around the region at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Zacharo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Zacharo are:

  1. Katakolon Port: A quaint fishing village, Katakolon port is famous as the gateway to Olympia, the birthplace of the Olympic Games.

  2. Pylos: A picturesque town, Pylos is home to many beaches and lush forests, and is only 49km away from Zacharo.

Conclusion

Zacharo is a beautiful and fascinating destination with plenty to offer. Whether you’re into history, natural beauty, or simply looking for a village to relax in, Zacharo is the perfect place to visit the heart of Peloponnese in Greece, and we hope this guide has helped you plan your trip.
