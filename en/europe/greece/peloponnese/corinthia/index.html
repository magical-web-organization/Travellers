Your Comprehensive Travel Guide to Corinthia, Greece

If you’re looking to explore the rich cultural heritage and breathtaking scenery of Greece, Corinthia, located in Peloponnese, Europe, is the perfect destination for you. Situated on the narrow stretch of land that connects the Peloponnese peninsula to the mainland, the region boasts a diverse landscape of rugged mountains, lush valleys, and sparkling seas. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, accommodations, local cuisine, and more. Plan your trip with us and experience all that Corinthia has to offer.

Introduction to Corinthia

Corinthia is home to some of the most historically significant sites in Greece. The region has been inhabited since prehistoric times and has played a major role in Greek history and culture. Corinth, the ancient capital, was one of the most important cities in ancient Greece and a major hub for trade and commerce.

The region is easily accessible by car, bus, and train, making it an ideal destination for tourists looking to explore this hidden gem of Greece. The people are friendly, the food is delicious, and the scenery is breathtaking. It’s no wonder that Corinthia is fast becoming a popular destination for travelers looking for an authentic Greek experience.

List of Places to Visit in Corinthia

Corinthia is full of exciting places to visit, from historical landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the top attractions you shouldn’t miss on your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ancient Corinth – Stroll through the ancient ruins of this important city and marvel at the incredible architecture and archaeology. Highlights include the Temple of Apollo and the ancient agora.
  2. Acrocorinth – This impressive fortress is perched atop a 1,800-foot hill and boasts stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Don’t miss the medieval citadel and the Temple of Aphrodite.
  3. Archaeological Museum of Corinth – Learn about the rich history of Corinthia and see some of the region’s most important artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Corinthia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Geraneia – Hike to the summit of this spectacular mountain and enjoy breathtaking views of the Gulf of Corinth.
  2. Lake Vouliagmeni – Take a dip in the mineral-rich waters of this beautiful lake and enjoy the serene surroundings.
  3. Ancient Nemea – Venture into the lush Nemean Valley and explore the ancient ruins and temples.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corinthia

Corinthia is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a plethora of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Mount Ziria – This challenging hike takes you to the peak of Mount Ziria, the third-highest mountain in the Peloponnese.
  2. Lake Dasiou – Bask in the tranquil setting of this hidden gem, surrounded by verdant forests and stunning mountain vistas.
  3. Canal Route – Take a leisurely bike ride along the scenic Corinth Canal and soak in the magnificent views of the sea and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Corinthia: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun things to do in Corinthia, from water sports to cultural tours. Here are some of the best activities to consider:

  1. Sea Kayaking – Explore the stunning coastline of Corinthia on a guided sea kayaking tour.
  2. Olive Oil Tasting – Sample some of the finest locally produced olive oils and learn about the traditional methods used in their production.
  3. Wine Tasting – Take a tour of one of the region’s many wineries and taste some of the finest Greek wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Corinthia

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Corinthia, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day Activities
1 Visit Ancient Corinth, Acrocorinth, and the Archaeological Museum of Corinth
2 Hike to the summit of Mount Ziria and enjoy the stunning views
3 Take a guided bike tour along the scenic Corinth Canal
4 Explore the ancient ruins of Nemea and visit a local winery
5 Relax on the shores of Lake Vouliagmeni and take a dip in the mineral-rich waters
6 Go sea kayaking along the stunning coastline of Corinthia
7 Visit a local olive grove and taste some of the region’s finest olive oils

Food and Drink Scene of Corinthia

Greek cuisine is world-renowned for its fresh, healthy ingredients and delicious flavors. Corinthia is no exception, with a wide variety of local specialties to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kokoretsi – A savory grill-staple made from seasoned lamb’s offal, wrapped in lamb’s intestines.
  2. Hilopites – A flavorful egg noodle dish topped with creamy feta cheese.
  3. Kleftiko – A slow-cooked lamb confit dish traditionally wrapped in parchment and baked in a wood fire oven until succulent.

Local Beverages

  1. Tsipouro - a strong spirit distilled from the residue of pressed grapes
  2. Wine - the Corinth region has a long and proud wine-making history, and is known for distinctive reds, whites and rosés

Where to Stay in Corinthia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Corinthia, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Hotel Alkyon – Located in Loutraki, this beachfront hotel boasts stunning views of the Corinthian Gulf and excellent amenities.
  2. Acrocorinth Boutique Hotel – A luxury hotel located at the foot of Acrocorinth, with beautifully designed rooms, stunning views and local friendly hospitality.
  3. Trikoupi Guesthouse – A charming, family-owned guesthouse situated in the heart of Corinth town.

What to Buy in Corinthia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Corinthia is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, both of which provide inspiration for unique and memorable souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  1. Olive Oil – Take home a bottle of the finest locally produced olive oil as a reminder of your trip.
  2. Traditional Handicrafts – From colorful woven rugs to hand-painted pottery and embroidery, there’s no shortage of handmade treasures to choose from.
  3. Local Wines – Discover the unique flavors of Corinthian wines and take home a bottle or two as a special gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Corinthia

Greek culture is known for its rich history, warm hospitality, and love of good food and company. In Corinthia, you’ll find a welcoming community eager to share their traditions and customs with visitors. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  2. Embrace the slow pace of life in Greece and take the time to savor the little things.
  3. Be open to new experiences and don’t be afraid to try new foods or participate in cultural activities.

Best Time to Visit Corinthia

The best time to visit Corinthia depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is the peak tourist season, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities. However, if you prefer cooler weather and fewer crowds, consider visiting in the spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corinthia

To make the most of your trip to Corinthia and avoid any cultural misunderstandings, here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  1. Learn some basic Greek phrases to help you communicate with the locals.
  2. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  3. Tip generously for good service, usually about 10 to 15 percent of the total bill.

Don’t:

  1. Touch or remove any artifacts from archaeological sites.
  2. Drink tap water, as it may not be safe to consume.
  3. Argue or engage in aggressive behavior, which is frowned upon in Greek culture.

Safety and Health

Corinthia is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always wise to take some basic precautions to ensure a healthy and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Corinthia

  1. Lock your valuables in a hotel safe or keep them with you at all times.
  2. Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  3. Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or on public transportation.

Health in Corinthia

  1. Make sure your vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Greece.
  2. Wear insect repellant to protect against mosquito-borne diseases.
  3. Drink bottled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Corinthia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Corinthia is easily accessible from major cities in Greece. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Corinthia

  1. Athens International Airport - located just 75 minutes southeast of Corinthia with direct flights to many international locations.
  2. Kalamata International Airport – located 2 hours south of Corinthia.

Bus Stations Near Corinthia

  1. Kiato Bus Station - located just 10 minutes east of Corinthia
  2. Corinth Bus Station – located in the heart of the city.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or take a train from Athens or Thessaloniki. Trains run once daily and are a comfortable option for those who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in Corinthia

The best way to see Corinthia is by car, which will allow you to explore the region at your own pace. Local buses and taxis are also available, but may be limited outside the main tourist areas.

Where to Go Next from Corinthia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Corinthia is surrounded by many other exciting destinations that are definitely worth a visit, including the following:

  1. Athens – The capital city of Greece with its world-famous monuments such as the Parthenon, amazing museums and galleries, and bustling nightlife.
  2. Nafplio – Often referred to as the most picturesque city in Greece, Nafplio is packed with history, stunning views, and fantastic food.
  3. Mycenae – The ancient fortified city known for its impressive architecture, rich history, and striking views from the hill on which it sits.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality, Corinthia is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Greece. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or simply relaxing in the beautiful surroundings, Corinthia has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your dream trip to Corinthia. Safe travels!
