The Ultimate Travel Guide to Corinth, Greece

Corinth is a picturesque and historic town located in Corinthia, Peloponnese, Greece. With its panoramic sea views, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage, Corinth is an unforgettable destination. From ancient ruins to hiking trails, here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to Corinth.

Introduction to Corinth

Corinth has a fascinating history that dates back to ancient times. The city was strategically located between Athens and Sparta, making it a crucial locus of trade and conflict. Today, Corinth is a peaceful and prosperous town that offers visitors a glimpse into Greece’s past and present.

List of Places to Visit in Corinth

Corinth offers a variety of attractions for visitors to explore, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Acrocorinth: Acrocorinth is a fortress that sits atop a mountain overlooking Corinth. The fortress has been in use since ancient times and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Archaeological Museum of Corinth: The Archaeological Museum of Corinth houses artifacts from Ancient Corinth and the surrounding area, including pottery, statues, and coins.

  • The Temple of Apollo: The Temple of Apollo is a well-preserved ancient temple that was built between the 7th and 6th centuries BC.

Natural Attractions of Corinth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Vouliagmeni: Lake Vouliagmeni is a unique natural wonder that’s located just a few kilometers from Corinth. The lake is fed by hot springs and is renowned for its therapeutic properties.

  • Mount Geraneia: Mount Geraneia is a mountain range that offers excellent hiking opportunities and stunning views of the Saronic Gulf.

  • Kalamaki Beach: Kalamaki Beach is a beautiful seaside destination that’s located just south of Corinth. The beach is secluded and offers clear waters that are perfect for swimming and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corinth

Corinth is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails that offer breathtaking views and challenging terrain. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Corinth include:

  • The Olive Tree Grove of Ancient Corinth: This trail takes hikers through a scenic olive grove that was once the site of the ancient city of Corinth.

  • The Diolkos Trail: The Diolkos Trail follows the path of the ancient Diolkos, a stone road that was used to transport ships across the Isthmus of Corinth.

  • Mount Ziria Trail: The Mount Ziria Trail is a challenging hike that takes hikers to the summit of Mount Ziria, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Corinth: Activities and Tours

Corinth offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to every interest. Some of the best things to do in Corinth include:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Corinth is known for its excellent wine, and there are several wineries in the area that offer tours and tastings.

  • Sailing Tours: The Saronic Gulf is a stunning destination that’s best explored by boat. There are several sailing tours that depart from Corinth and offer visitors the chance to explore the beautiful coastline.

  • Cycling Tours: Cycling tours are a great way to explore the countryside around Corinth and offer a fun and active way to see the sights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Corinth

To make the most of your visit to Corinth, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Archaeological Museum of Corinth and explore the ruins of Ancient Corinth
2 Hike the Olive Tree Grove of Ancient Corinth or take a cycling tour of the countryside
3 Visit the Fortress of Acrocorinth and enjoy the panoramic views of the area
4 Take a sailing tour of the Saronic Gulf and visit some of the nearby islands

Food and Drink Scene of Corinth

Corinth has a rich culinary tradition that’s characterized by simple, fresh ingredients and bold flavors. Some of the must-try dishes in Corinth include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A classic Greek dish made with eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.

  • Souvlaki: Grilled skewers of meat (usually pork or chicken) served with pita bread and tzatziki.

  • Fasolada: A hearty bean soup that’s often served with crusty bread and olives.

Local Beverages

  • Corinthian Wine: Corinth is known for its excellent wine, particularly the red wines made from the Agiorgitiko grape.

  • Tsipouro: A strong distilled spirit that’s typically served as an aperitif.

  • Frappé: A foamy iced coffee drink that’s a Greek specialty.

Where to Stay in Corinth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Corinth to suit every budget and preference. Some of our top picks include:

  • Hotel Pegasus Suites: This luxurious hotel is located on a hilltop overlooking the city and offers breathtaking views and top-notch amenities.

  • Diolkos Studios: These modern and stylish studios are located in the heart of Corinth and offer excellent value for money.

  • Ferticos Hotel: This family-run hotel offers comfortable and affordable accommodation and is located just a short walk from the city center.

What to Buy in Corinth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Corinth has a variety of local products that make great souvenirs or gifts. Some popular options include:

  • Corinthian Wine: As mentioned earlier, Corinth is known for its excellent wine.

  • Olive Oil: The Peloponnese is famous for its high-quality olive oil, and there are several local producers in the area.

  • Handmade Pottery: Corinth has a long tradition of pottery making, and there are several local artisans who produce beautiful handmade pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Corinth

Greece has a rich cultural heritage, and Corinth is no exception. To fully appreciate the local culture and customs, we recommend the following tips:

  • Learn a few Greek phrases: Locals appreciate visitors who make an effort to speak their language, even if it’s just a few basic phrases.

  • Respect religious sites: Greece is a deeply religious country, and visitors should dress appropriately and behave respectfully when visiting churches and other religious sites.

  • Tipping: Tipping is generally expected in restaurants and cafes, and a 10% tip is standard.

Best Time to Visit Corinth

The best time to visit Corinth is in the spring (March - May) or fall (September - November), when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. However, visitors can enjoy the beauty of Corinth all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corinth

Do:

  • Explore the ancient ruins of Corinth
  • Visit the local wineries and try some of the excellent wine
  • Take a hike or cycle through the beautiful countryside
  • Visit the local beaches and enjoy the clear waters

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Litter or damage the natural environments by leaving trash
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Corinth

Corinth is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Corinth

Visitors to Corinth should ensure that their routine vaccinations are up to date. It’s also a good idea to bring a basic first aid kit, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Getting to Corinth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Corinth is easily accessible by air, land, and sea.

Airports Near Corinth

The closest major airport to Corinth is Athens International Airport (ATH), which is located approximately 90 kilometers from Corinth.

Bus Stations Near Corinth

There are several bus stations in the area that offer direct connections to Athens and other destinations.

Other Options

Another popular option is to rent a car and explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Corinth

The best way to get around Corinth is by car or by taxi. There are also several bus routes that serve the area, but these can be infrequent and unreliable.

Where to Go Next from Corinth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations that are within easy reach of Corinth include:

  • Athens: The Greek capital is just a short drive or bus ride away and offers visitors a wealth of historic and cultural attractions.

  • Mycenae: This ancient archaeological site is located just a short drive from Corinth and features the remains of a fortified city that dates back to the Bronze Age.

  • Epidavros: This historic town is home to an impressive ancient theater that’s still used for performances today.

Conclusion

Corinth is a must-visit destination that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With its stunning scenery, rich history, and charming local culture, Corinth is an unforgettable destination that will leave you with lasting memories.
