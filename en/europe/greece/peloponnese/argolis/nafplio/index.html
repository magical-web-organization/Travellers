Introduction to Nafplio

Located in the region of Argolis, in the Peloponnese peninsula of Greece, Nafplio is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse into its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. Once the capital of Greece and an important seaport, Nafplio boasts a stunning old town, a picturesque waterfront, a lively food scene, and a range of outdoor activities. Here is a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to Nafplio.

List of Places to Visit in Nafplio

From ancient ruins and museums to scenic beaches and hiking trails, Nafplio has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Nafplio:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palamidi Fortress: Built in the 18th century, this imposing fortress stands on a hill overlooking the town and the sea. Visitors can climb up the fortress walls to enjoy panoramic views of Nafplio and the surrounding area.

  • Bourtzi Castle: Located on a small islet in the middle of Nafplio’s harbor, this castle was originally built by the Venetians in 1473 and later used as a prison and a hotel. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and can be reached by boat.

  • Archaeological Museum of Nafplio: Housed in an elegant neoclassical building, this museum displays a collection of artifacts from the ancient sites of Mycenae, Tiryns, and Nafplio, as well as from the Byzantine and Venetian periods.

  • National Gallery of Nafplio: This art museum features works by Greek artists from the 19th and 20th centuries, including Nikiforos Lytras, Giannis Tsarouchis, and Yiannis Moralis.

Natural Attractions of Nafplio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arvanitia Beach: This small pebble beach is located in a stunning cove just a short walk from the old town. Visitors can swim in the crystal-clear water, sunbathe on the beach, and enjoy views of the towering cliffs and the sea.

  • Karathona Beach: One of the most popular beaches in the area, Karathona is a long, sandy beach with calm waters and plenty of facilities, including cafes, restaurants, and sun loungers.

  • Syntagma Square: The main square of Nafplio’s old town is a charming place to explore. Lined with neoclassical buildings, cafes, and restaurants, it is a lively gathering place for locals and visitors alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nafplio

Nafplio is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, perfect for outdoor activities and adventure. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Arvanitia-Palamidi Trail: This 5-km hiking trail starts at Arvanitia Beach and winds its way up to the Palamidi Fortress, offering stunning views of Nafplio and the Saronic Gulf. It takes about 2 hours to complete.

  • Acronauplia Trail: This 3-km hiking trail starts at the old town and leads up to the Acronauplia fortress, passing by several churches and monuments along the way. It takes about 1.5 hours to complete.

  • Nafplio-Ligourio Bike Route: This 18-km bike route starts in Nafplio and goes through the olive groves and scenic countryside to the ancient theater of Epidaurus in Ligourio. It takes about 2 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Nafplio: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the town’s historic sites and natural attractions, Nafplio offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Nafplio:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Nafplio is located in the heart of the famed wine region of Nemea, known for its Agiorgitiko grape variety. Visitors can take a wine tasting tour of the local wineries and vineyards to sample the region’s delicious wines.

  • Sea Kayaking Tours: Explore the beautiful coastline of Nafplio on a sea kayaking tour. Paddle around the hidden coves, rocky cliffs, and crystal-clear waters, and enjoy a unique perspective of the town from the sea.

  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Greek dishes, such as moussaka, tzatziki, and dolmades, in a fun and interactive cooking class. You’ll learn about the ingredients and techniques used in Greek cuisine and get to enjoy the delicious results.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nafplio

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Nafplio, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Nafplio and explore the old town, including the Palamidi Fortress, Bourtzi Castle, and the Archaeological Museum.

  • Day 2: Visit the Epidaurus theater and explore the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 3: Go sea kayaking or take a boat tour of the nearby islands of Hydra and Spetses.

  • Day 4: Take a wine tasting tour of the local wineries and vineyards.

  • Day 5: Hike the Arvanitia-Palamidi Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the town and the sea.

  • Day 6: Relax on the beach or take a day trip to the nearby town of Nafplio.

  • Day 7: Depart Nafplio and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Nafplio

Nafplio is known for its delicious food and drink, featuring fresh ingredients and traditional recipes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample in Nafplio:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: With its location on the coast, Nafplio is famous for its fresh seafood dishes, such as grilled octopus, fried calamari, and stuffed squid.

  • Souvlaki: This Greek specialty is a popular street food made with grilled meat (usually pork or chicken) and served with pita bread, tomatoes, onions, and tzatziki sauce.

  • Spanakopita: This savory pastry is made with spinach and feta cheese wrapped in filo dough and baked until crispy and golden.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Nafplio is part of the Nemea wine region and produces some of the best wines in Greece. Try the local Agiorgitiko red wine or the Moschofilero white wine.

  • Tsipouro: This traditional Greek spirit is similar to Italian grappa and is made from distilled grape pomace.

Where to Stay in Nafplio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nafplio offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and style, from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Nafplio:

  • Amphitryon Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of Nafplio’s old town and offers elegant rooms and suites with views of the sea, the town, or the fortress.

  • Pension Marianna: This charming guesthouse is located just a short walk from the old town and offers comfortable rooms with traditional decor and a cozy atmosphere.

  • Hotel Perivoli: This boutique hotel is set in a beautiful garden and features spacious rooms and suites with private balconies overlooking the valley.

What to Buy in Nafplio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Nafplio, here are some ideas:

  • Olive oil: Nafplio is surrounded by olive groves, and the local olive oil is considered some of the best in Greece.

  • Ceramics: Nafplio has a long tradition of pottery-making, and you can find a range of handmade ceramics, from plates and bowls to vases and decorative pieces.

  • Honey: The region around Nafplio produces high-quality honey from wildflowers and thyme, which makes a sweet and delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Nafplio

Nafplio has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by its long history and diverse population. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of while visiting Nafplio:

  • Respect religious sites: Greece is an Orthodox Christian country, and Nafplio has many churches and monasteries that are important religious sites. Visitors should dress modestly and avoid loud noises and photography inside these sites.

  • Siesta time: Like many Mediterranean countries, Greece has a tradition of taking a midday nap, or siesta, during the hottest part of the day. Many shops and businesses close during this time, so visitors should plan their activities accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Nafplio

The best time to visit Nafplio is during the spring (April-June) and fall (September-November), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be very hot and crowded, but it’s also the peak tourist season, with many festivals and cultural events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nafplio

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Nafplio:

Do:

  • Explore the old town: Nafplio’s old town is a maze of narrow streets, alleys, and squares, with neoclassical buildings, museums, and cafes around every corner.

  • Try the local cuisine: From fresh seafood to traditional Greek dishes, Nafplio has a range of delicious food options to try.

  • Take a boat tour: See Nafplio from the sea by taking a boat tour around the harbor or to the nearby islands of Hydra and Spetses.

Don’t:

  • Touch artifacts in museums and historical sites: Many of the artifacts and exhibits in Nafplio’s museums and historical sites are fragile and irreplaceable. Visitors should avoid touching or damaging these items.

  • Take photos inside churches and monasteries: Photography is not allowed inside many of Nafplio’s churches and monasteries, as these are considered sacred sites.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public: Nafplio is a conservative town, and visitors should dress modestly in public areas, especially in churches and other religious sites.

Safety and Health

Nafplio is a safe and healthy destination for travelers, with high-quality medical facilities and low crime rates. However, visitors should take common-sense safety precautions and be aware of the following:

Safety in Nafplio

  • Watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas, such as the old town or the bus station.

  • Be careful when driving on the narrow, winding roads in the countryside.

  • Follow local customs and traditions, such as respecting religious sites and avoiding loud noises and disruptive behavior in public.

Health in Nafplio

  • Greece has high-quality medical facilities, but visitors should have adequate health insurance and seek medical attention if necessary.

  • Tap water in Nafplio is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended.

Getting to Nafplio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nafplio is located about 165 km from Athens and can be reached by several transportation options.

Airports Near Nafplio

  • Athens International Airport: About 150 km from Nafplio, the Athens airport is the closest major airport. There are regular bus and train services from the airport to Nafplio.

  • Kalamata International Airport: About 120 km from Nafplio, Kalamata airport is a smaller airport with limited international connections. There are regular bus services from the airport to Nafplio.

Bus Stations Near Nafplio

  • KTEL Bus Station (Athens): Located in the suburb of Kifissos in Athens, the KTEL bus station offers regular services to Nafplio.

  • Nafplio Bus Station: Located in the center of the town, the Nafplio bus station offers connections to other towns and cities in the area.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Athens and Nafplio, but can be expensive for long-distance trips.

  • Rental Cars: You can rent cars at the airports or in the towns themselves.

How to Get Around in Nafplio

Nafplio is a small town, and most of its attractions can be reached on foot. However, to travel further afield, you can use the following transportation options:

  • Buses: KTEL buses operate regular services between Nafplio and other towns and cities in the area.

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available and can be hailed on the street or called from a taxi stand. However, they can be expensive for longer journeys.

  • Rental Cars: You can rent cars from several agencies in Nafplio and explore the surrounding area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Nafplio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of the Peloponnese region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Mycenae: This ancient city is home to the famous Lion Gate and Tomb of Agamemnon, and is just a short drive from Nafplio.

  • Epidaurus: This ancient theater is famous for its incredible acoustics and is located just a short drive from Nafplio.

  • Hydra and Spetses: These nearby islands offer a relaxing day trip from Nafplio and can be reached by boat.

Conclusion

Nafplio is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Greek history, culture, and natural beauty. With its charming old town, stunning beaches, and outdoor activities, there’s something for everyone in Nafplio. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
