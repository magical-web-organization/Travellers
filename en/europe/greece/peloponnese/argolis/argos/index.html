Your Complete Guide to Argos, Greece

Welcome to Argos, a hidden gem located in the region of Argolis, in Peloponnese, Greece. This ancient city is not as popular as some of the more well-known destinations in Greece, but it offers a unique blend of history, culture, natural beauty, great food, and friendly people. In this guide, we’ll explore all the best things to see and do in Argos, and help you plan an unforgettable trip to this fascinating city.

List of Places to Visit in Argos

If you’re wondering what to see in Argos, there’s plenty of choices. From ancient ruins to natural wonders, there are enough attractions to keep you busy for days. Here are some of the must-visit places in Argos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ancient Theatre of Argos: This well-preserved theater is thought to be one of the oldest in the world, dating back to the 5th century BC.
  • Larisa Fortress: A magnificent ancient fortress built in the 6th century BC that overlooks the city.
  • Archaeological Museum of Argos: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts from the ancient times, including pottery, sculptures, and tools.
  • Akropolis of Argos: The ancient acropolis of Argos is a fascinating archaeological site that offers stunning views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Argos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Oros: Head out on a hike to the top of this mountain to enjoy magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lernean Hydra: A series of freshwater springs that offer a picturesque natural setting for a hike or picnic.
  • Monastery of Panagia Varnakova: A beautiful monastery located in the mountains, which offers scenic views and a historic setting.
  • Port of Mycenae: A picturesque port town that is famous for its crystal clear waters and fresh seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Argos

Argos is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best routes for adventure junkies:

  • Mount Oros Summit Trail: A challenging 8.3-mile trail that rewards hikers with breathtaking panoramic views of the valley and the Gulf of Argolis.
  • Lerna Cave Trail: A moderate 4-mile hike that takes you to the Lerna Cave, known for its natural stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Karnezeika Trails: A network of trails covering the forests of Karnezeika, offering scenic views of the countryside.
  • Biking through the city streets: Rent a bike and explore the city’s winding streets and alleyways at your leisure.

Best Things to Do in Argos: Activities and Tours

  • Olive Oil Tasting: Argos is famous for its high-quality olive oil, and you can taste some of the best varieties by joining a local tour or visiting one of the many olive oil mills in the area.
  • Truffle Hunting: Join a local guide and their dog on a hunt for truffles, an experience that combines the thrill of the chase with the satisfaction of enjoying a delicious meal made with your finds.
  • Wine Tasting: You won’t be disappointed in the local wines, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries in the nearby Nemea region, where you can sample some of the best varieties of the Peloponnese.
  • Greek Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Greek dishes using local ingredients with expert chefs through classes or workshops that take place in the heart of Argos.

Week-Long Itinerary for Argos

If you’re planning to spend a week in Argos, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and explore the city. Visit the Ancient Theater and the Larisa Fortress.

Day 2: Visit the Akropolis of Argos. In the evening, take a cooking class and enjoy a traditional Greek dinner.

Day 3: Hike the Mount Oros Summit Trail. Return to Argos in the evening for some delicious olive oil tastings.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Mycenae and the ruins of the ancient city.

Day 5: Visit the Archaeological Museum of Argos. Take a wine tasting tour in Nemea.

Day 6: Go truffle hunting in the countryside. Enjoy a relaxing dinner in one of the local tavernas.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby beaches of Nafplio and Tolo.

Food and Drink Scene of Argos

The cuisine in Argos is a mix of traditional Greek dishes and international flavors inspired by the regional produce. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokkinisto: A classic Greek meat dish made with beef and tomato sauce.
  • Fasolakia Lathera: Steamed green beans with tomatoes, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Arni Sto Forno: Slow-cooked lamb with potatoes and herbs.
  • Galaktoboureko: A sweet pastry made with custard and phyllo dough.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: The local spirit of Argos, served as an aperitif or a digestif.
  • Retsina: A white wine that is made unique by the addition of pine resin.
  • Amalia: A red wine made from the Agiorgitiko grape, which is grown in the nearby Nemea region.

Where to Stay in Argos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Argos offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, and rustic guesthouses, there’s something for everyone. Below are some of the top options in the area:

  • Palladion Boutique Hotel: A cozy hotel located near the Akropolis of Argos that offers comfortable rooms and suites.
  • Kallirroe Hotel: A beautifully restored townhouse that offers accommodation with a charming local flavor.
  • Argos Guesthouse: A family-run guesthouse located in the heart of the city, offering a comfortable and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Argos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of things to bring back from Argos that will remind you of your trip. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products:

  • Olive Oil: Take home a bottle of the world-renowned olive oil made in the local olive groves.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Look for handmade jewelry crafted in the local style, using gold and silver.
  • Honey: Argos is famous for its high-quality honey, made from the nectar of the wildflowers growing in the surrounding mountains.
  • Handmade Rugs: There are several artisanal rug-making shops in Argos that offer beautiful and intricately woven rugs and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Argos

The people of Argos are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their local culture and heritage. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the traditions of the local people, such as dress codes when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Try to learn a few Greek phrases to show your appreciation for the local culture.
  • Tipping is not expected, but it is appreciated if the service is exceptional.

Best Time to Visit Argos

The best time to visit Argos is during the spring and summer months (April to September), when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The months of July and August can get quite busy with tourists, so if you prefer a quieter experience, consider visiting in the shoulder season (April to June or September).

Do’s and Don’ts of Argos

Do:

  • Explore the city’s narrow streets and alleys on foot.
  • Try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood, olive oil, and wine.
  • Take a day trip to the nearby beaches of Nafplio, Tolo, or Mystras.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural sites in the area.
  • Expect too much English to be spoken outside of tourist destinations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Argos

Argos is a relatively safe city for travelers, but like any urban area, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe. Here are a few tips:

  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.
  • Keep your valuables in a secure place, such as a hotel safe.

Health in Argos

Overall, Greece is a very safe and healthy country to visit, but it’s always a good idea to take some precautions. Here are some tips to stay healthy during your trip to Argos:

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid stomach problems.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Bring insect repellent to protect against mosquitos and other bugs.

Getting to Argos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Argos is well connected to the rest of Greece, whether you’re arriving by plane, train, or bus.

Airports Near Argos

The closest international airport to Argos is Athens International Airport (ATH), located approximately 80 miles away. Once you arrive in Athens, you can either rent a car and drive to Argos (a two-hour drive), take a bus, or arrange for a private transfer.

Bus Stations Near Argos

There are several bus stations in Argos, which offer connections to other cities in the Peloponnese, as well as Athens.

  • KTEL Bus Station: The largest bus station in Argos, which offers connections to Athens and several other popular destinations.

Other Options

If you prefer to take the train, you can take a train from Athens to Argos, which takes approximately 2.5 hours.

How to Get Around in Argos

Argos is a small and walkable city, and many of the top attractions are located within easy walking distance of each other. For longer distances, you can rent a car or a bike, take a taxi, or use the local buses.

Where to Go Next from Argos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Argos is located in the heart of the Peloponnese region of Greece, which is rich in history and culture. Some of the nearby destinations that you may want to explore include:

  • Nemea: Famous for its vineyards and wineries, located just 20 miles away.
  • Nafplio: A beautiful coastal city known for its charming streets and historic fortress, located just 9 miles away.
  • Mycenae: An ancient city known for its impressive citadel, only 17 miles away.
  • Mystras: A UNESCO World Heritage site boasting impressive medieval ruins, only 70 miles away.

Conclusion

Argos is a beautiful and intriguing destination that offers a unique blend of culture, history, natural beauty, and great food. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, hiking in the mountains, or simply relaxing by the beach, there’s something for everyone in this fabulous city. We hope that this guide has given you some inspiration and guidance for planning your trip to Argos.
