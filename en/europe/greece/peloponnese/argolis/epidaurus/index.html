Introduction to Epidaurus

Epidaurus is a charming ancient city located in Argolis, a region in Peloponnese, Greece. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is famous for its stunning natural landscape, rich history, and cultural heritage. Its most prominent attraction is the Epidaurus Theater, a classical Greek theater that draws thousands of visitors every year. But Epidaurus has much more to offer, from fascinating museums and ancient ruins to scenic hiking trails and breathtaking beaches.

With this comprehensive travel guide, we’re here to help you plan your visit to Epidaurus and discover its treasures. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or just looking for a peaceful retreat, there’s something for everyone in this ancient Greek gem.

List of Places to Visit in Epidaurus

When traveling to Epidaurus, these are the top places that you should put on your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Site of Epidaurus: This site includes the famous Epidaurus Theater, known for its incredible acoustics, as well as the Sanctuary of Asklepios, a sacred healing center. The sanctuary includes ancient temples, baths, and fountains, and it’s a fascinating insight into ancient Greek medicine and religious practices.
  • Epidaurus Museum: This museum is located within the archaeological site and houses a collection of ancient artifacts from the region, including sculptures, inscriptions, pottery, and coins.
  • Mycenae: This ancient city-state is located a short drive from Epidaurus and is famous for its archaeological site, which includes the Lion Gate, the Palace of Agamemnon, and other impressive structures.

Natural Attractions of Epidaurus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palaia Epidaurus Beach: This beautiful stretch of beach offers clear waters for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Nafplio: This charming town is located a short drive from Epidaurus and boasts Venetian architecture, cobbled streets, and plenty of seaside charm.
  • Arachnaio Mountain: This stunning mountain range rises above Epidaurus and offers an incredible view of the region from the top.
  • Ancient Epidavros Trail: This hiking trail takes you from the archaeological site to the top of a hill overlooking the sea, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Epidaurus

Epidaurus is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many beautiful trails that lead through breathtaking landscapes. Some popular ones include:

  • Ancient Epidavros Trail: This 11km trail takes you from the archaeological site to the top of a hill, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape along the way.
  • Mount Parnon Trail: This challenging 26km trail takes you through the mountains of the Peloponnese and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and peaks.
  • Parnitha National Park Trail: This 30km trail takes you through the heart of Parnitha National Park, home to stunning forests, wildlife, and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Epidaurus: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting the top attractions in Epidaurus, there’s plenty more to do and see in this ancient Greek gem. Here are some top activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Wine tasting tour: Visit the nearby vineyards and taste some of the best wines of the region, accompanied by local cheeses and delicacies.
  • Olive oil tasting tour: Discover the secrets of Greek olive oil and sample some of the best oils of the region on this informative tour.
  • Cooking class: Learn how to make some of the most delicious Greek dishes, from moussaka to dolmades and baklava, under the guidance of a local chef.
  • Beach day: Head to one of the many beautiful beaches near Epidaurus and spend a day soaking up the sun and swimming in the clear waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Epidaurus

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Epidaurus, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the archaeological site of Epidaurus, including the theater and sanctuary, and the Epidaurus Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a scenic hike along the Ancient Epidavros Trail and enjoy lunch at a traditional taverna.
  • Day 3: Explore the charming seaside town of Nafplio and visit the Palamidi Fortress.
  • Day 4: Discover the ancient city-state of Mycenae and its impressive archaeological site.
  • Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour of the nearby vineyards and enjoy a sunset dinner overlooking the sea.
  • Day 6: Relax on the beautiful beaches of Palaia Epidaurus and enjoy some water activities.
  • Day 7: Take a cooking class and learn how to make some of the most famous Greek dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Epidaurus

Greek cuisine is famous all over the world, and Epidaurus has plenty to offer in terms of local delicacies and traditional dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Moussaka: A traditional Greek dish made of layers of eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce, and baked until golden perfection.
  • Souvlaki: Skewered meat (usually pork, chicken, or lamb) seasoned with herbs and grilled over an open flame.
  • Gyros: Thinly sliced meat (usually pork or chicken) wrapped in a pita with tomato, onions, and tzatziki sauce.
  • Dolmades: Grape leaves stuffed with rice, herbs, and spices.

Local Beverages:

  • Ouzo: An anise-flavored aperitif served with ice and water.
  • Retsina: A traditional Greek wine flavored with pine resin.
  • Tsipouro: A strong grape-based spirit served as an appetizer or after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Epidaurus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Epidaurus has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Orfeas: Located in the heart of Epidaurus, this hotel offers comfortable rooms with sea or mountain views, a swimming pool, and easy access to the archaeological site.
  • Ktima Michelle: This charming guesthouse is located in the nearby village of Lygourio and offers cozy rooms, a swimming pool, and a traditional Greek breakfast.
  • Epavlis Hotel: This boutique hotel is located in the nearby village of Palea Epidavros and offers stunning sea views, a modern spa, and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Epidaurus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Epidaurus is complete without bringing home some authentic souvenirs and local products. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Olive oil: Greece produces some of the best olive oil in the world, and Epidaurus has plenty to offer. Look for bottles labeled “extra virgin” for the highest quality.
  • Honey: Greek honey is famous for its rich taste and beneficial properties. Look for jars of raw honey from local producers.
  • Handmade ceramics: Epidaurus has a rich history of pottery making, and you can find beautiful handmade pieces at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Epidaurus

Greek culture is rich in traditions, customs, and rituals, and Epidaurus is no exception. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending ceremonies.
  • Greeks are known for their hospitality, so don’t be surprised if you’re invited to a meal or offered a drink by a friendly local.
  • Tipping is not expected but appreciated, especially in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Epidaurus

The best time to visit Epidaurus is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April through June) and autumn (September through November). During these months, the weather is mild, the crowds are smaller, and the prices are cheaper. For those who want to experience the summer vibe, July and August are the peak season; however, prices are higher, and the crowds are larger.

Do’s and Don’ts of Epidaurus

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Epidaurus:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and sample some traditional dishes.
  • Take a hike or bike through the stunning natural landscapes of the region.
  • Visit the archaeological site of Epidaurus and learn about ancient Greek history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural or cultural heritage of the region.
  • Don’t disrespect religious customs or traditions.
  • Don’t ignore safety regulations or warnings during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

The safety and health of visitors to Epidaurus are generally excellent. Here are some guidelines to keep in mind:

Safety in Epidaurus

  • Keep an eye on your personal belongings when in crowded areas or public transport, especially in urban zones.
  • Always follow the safety signs and regulations when hiking or biking in the natural areas to avoid injury.

Health in Epidaurus

  • Epidaurus has an excellent medical infrastructure.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions and drink plenty of water when out in the sun.

Getting to Epidaurus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Epidaurus is located in the Peloponnese region of Greece, approximately two hours from Athens. Here are some transportation options for getting there:

  • By car: You can easily rent a car at Athens International Airport and drive to Epidaurus via the Attica Road and National Highway 7.
  • By bus: KTEL buses operate daily between Athens and Epidaurus.
  • By taxi: Taxis are available at Athens International Airport, and the journey takes around two hours.

Airports Near Epidaurus

The closest airport to Epidaurus is Athens International Airport (ATH).

Bus Stations Near Epidaurus

The main bus station in Epidaurus is located near the archaeological site.

Other Options

There is no train service to Epidaurus, but you can take a train to Corinth and then transfer to a bus.

How to Get Around in Epidaurus

Epidaurus is a compact city, and most attractions are located within walking distance of each other. If you’re planning to venture further out, here are your options:

  • By bus: KTEL buses operate regularly throughout Epidaurus and the surrounding areas.
  • By taxi: Taxis are widely available in Epidaurus and can be easily hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • By car: There are several car rental companies in Epidaurus, and renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding region at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Epidaurus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Epidaurus is conveniently located near several other charming destinations that are worth a visit. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Nafplio: This charming seaside town is located just a short drive from Epidaurus and boasts Venetian architecture, cobbled streets, and plenty of seaside charm.
  • Poros: This stunning island is located just a short ferry ride from the town of Galatas and offers stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Hydra: This car-free island is located just a short ferry ride from the city of Ermioni and offers stunning natural landscapes, serene beaches, and charming villages.

Conclusion

Epidaurus is a fascinating destination that combines breathtaking natural beauty with rich history and culture. From ancient ruins to stunning beaches, traditional cuisine to outdoor adventures, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this ancient Greek gem. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan your visit to Epidaurus and discover its treasures while enjoying the best that Greece has to offer.
