Introduction to Mycenae

Mycenae is a picturesque town located in the northeastern part of the Peloponnese peninsula in Greece. It is renowned for its archaeological treasures, including the ancient citadel ruins, tombs, and imposing palaces like the Palace of Nestor. Many of these ancient structures date back to the Mycenaean civilization, which thrived in the area between the 16th and 12th centuries BCE.

Visitors to Mycenae can step into ancient history by exploring the historic sites and ruins, as well as enjoying the beautiful natural scenery and mouthwatering local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Mycenae

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Archaeological Site of Mycenae

The main attraction of Mycenae is the Archaeological Site of Mycenae, which features the ancient citadel that dates back to the Bronze Age, as well as other fascinating relics of the Mycenaean civilization. Visitors can explore the ruins of the cyclopean walls, the Lion Gate, tholos tombs, and the palace.

Archaeological Museum of Mycenae

The Archaeological Museum of Mycenae exhibits an impressive collection of artifacts and relics that depict various aspects of life during the Mycenaean civilization. The museum houses over 2,000 items, including weapons, pottery, and jewelry.

Natural Attractions of Mycenae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Zara

Mount Zara is a prominent mountain range that offers visitors a breathtaking view of Mycenae’s natural beauty. The hiking trails are well marked, and nature lovers can explore the mountain’s flora and fauna while soaking in the stunning scenery.

Nemea Valley

The Nemea Valley is a scenic route that winds through picturesque vineyards and olive groves. Visitors can explore the vineyards, taste the local wine, and enjoy the breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mycenae

Mycenae offers some of the most awe-inspiring hiking and biking trails that cut through its rugged terrain. Some popular routes include the trek up Mount Zara and the scenic trails around the Nemea Valley. Visitors can rent bikes, and some hiking tours are also available.

Best Things to Do in Mycenae: Activities and Tours

Explore the Mycenaean Ruins

The Mycenaean ruins are a must-visit attraction in Mycenae. Visitors can explore the Palace of Nestor, the Lion Gate, and other stunning ruins, as well as learn about the history of the Mycenaean civilization.

Wine Tasting Tours

Visit the local wineries and indulge your palate in a wine-tasting tour. The Nemea Valley is famous for its outstanding wines, and visitors can enjoy a variety of white, red, and rose wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Mycenae

Must-Try Dishes

Sofrito

Sofrito is a famous local dish made from veal cooked in garlic and wine sauce and served with mashed potatoes.

Revithada

Revithada is a traditional chickpea soup cooked with onions, lemon, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

Nemea Wine

The Nemea Valley is known for producing some of the best wines in Greece, including Agiorgitiko, a full-bodied and fruity red wine that pairs well with local cuisine.

Greek Coffee

Greek coffee is a traditional coffee preparation method that involves brewing ground coffee in a small pot and serving it with a glass of cold water.

Where to Stay in Mycenae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mycenae has plenty of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, that cater to various budgets. Some popular options include Hotel Mycenae, Hotel Heraion, and the Pension Akrothalassia.

What to Buy in Mycenae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olive Oil

Greece is famous for its olive oil, which is made from local olive groves. Visitors can purchase authentic olive oil from local markets.

Local Pottery and Ceramics

Mycenae is famous for its pottery and ceramics, which are crafted by local artisans using traditional techniques. Visitors can purchase handmade pottery to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mycenae

Mycenae is a traditional town with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors are advised to dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious sites and museums. It is also customary to greet locals with a “Kalimera” (good morning) or “Kalispera” (good evening).

Best Time to Visit Mycenae

The best time to visit Mycenae is during the spring and fall months, from April to June and September to November, respectively. The weather is pleasant, and the crowds are thinner during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mycenae

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and museums.

  • Greet locals with a “Kalimera” (good morning) or “Kalispera” (good evening).

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch or remove any artifacts from the archaeological sites.

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural sites and parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mycenae

Mycenae is a relatively safe town, with most crimes being petty theft. Visitors are advised to take normal precautions and avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

Health in Mycenae

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water, and ensure that their vaccinations are up-to-date before visiting Greece.

Getting to Mycenae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mycenae

The closest major airport to Mycenae is Athens International Airport, which is about 160 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to travel to Mycenae.

Bus Stations Near Mycenae

Mycenae is connected to major cities in Greece by bus. Visitors can take a bus from Athens or Nafplio to reach Mycenae.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to reach Mycenae. For those arriving by sea, the port of Piraeus is the nearest port to Mycenae.

How to Get Around in Mycenae

Mycenae is a small town, and most of the sites and attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bikes or hire a taxi to explore the town and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mycenae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nafplio

Nafplio is a beautiful historic city located about 20 km away from Mycenae. Visitors can explore the beautiful architecture, including the Palamidi Fortress and the Bourtzi Fortress.

Epidaurus

Epidaurus is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for the ancient theater and other ancient structures that are steeped in history.

Conclusion

Mycenae is an enchanting town that boasts a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and mouthwatering traditional cuisine. Visitors can explore the town’s ancient attractions, hike and bike through its picturesque landscape, and taste the exquisite local wines. A trip to Mycenae promises to be a quintessential Greek experience that will leave visitors with unforgettable memories.
