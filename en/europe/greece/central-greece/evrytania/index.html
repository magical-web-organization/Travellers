Introduction to Evrytania

Located in the heart of Greece, Evrytania is a mountainous region filled with pristine natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and a welcoming community. Known for its rustic villages, stunning landscapes, and outdoor activities, this region is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and adrenaline junkies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Evrytania

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The National Resistance Museum in Koryschades showcases the struggles of the Greek resistance movement during World War II.
  2. The Byzantine Monastery of St Luke is an important landmark with beautiful frescoes and stunning views of the ravine below.
  3. The Prousos Monastery is one of the most significant monasteries in Greece.
  4. The Archaeological Museum of Kremasta contains artifacts from the ancient city of Kremasta and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Evrytania: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Explore the Agrafa mountains with a guided hike or a self-guided trek.
  2. Raft through the fast-flowing Tavropos river or go kayaking through the Potamia canyon.
  3. Experience the beauty of Lake Kremasta by kayaking, fishing, or taking a leisurely boat tour.
  4. Admire the scenic cascades of the Karpenissiotis River.
  5. Take a dip in the thermal springs of the natural reserve located in Koniakos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Evrytania

Evrytania boasts a plethora of hiking and biking opportunities, making it an excellent destination for adventure lovers. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Agrafa Mountains trails
  2. Megalo Horio trails
  3. The Kalarytes mountain route
  4. Panta Vrechei route
  5. Stournareika Canyon trek

Best Things to Do in Evrytania: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a 4x4 tour of the Agrafa mountains with a local guide.
  2. Take a horseback ride through the lush forests and rugged trails of the region.
  3. Experience skiing, snowboarding, or snowshoeing in the Velouchi Ski Resort during the winter season.
  4. Take a culinary journey in the traditional villages, tasting local dishes and drinks.
  5. Learn about the region’s traditions and customs through cultural festivals and events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Evrytania

Day Activity
1 Visit the National Resistance Museum, explore the city of Karpenisi, and try traditional Greek dishes in local taverns.
2 Hike the Gorgian bridge or take a horse ride in Megalo Horio.
3 Kayak and relax in Lake Kremasta, followed by a visit to the Prousos Monastery.
4 Take a mountain bike tour, visit the Koryschades village, and admire the St Nicholas Monastery.
5 Ski or snowshoe in Velouchi Ski Resort, based on the season.
6 Take a 4x4 tour of the Agrafa Mountains and Kamena Vourla thermal springs.
7 Go bungee jumping over the Tavropos river canyon or take a final stroll in the city of Karpenisi before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Evrytania

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Giaprakia: minced meat and rice wrapped in vine leaves.
  2. Kokoretsi: skewered and roasted lamb intestines.
  3. Fricasse of wild hare: a thick sauce dish made with tender meat cooked in red wine and cinnamon.
  4. Tsipouro: a local spirit made from grapes and served as an aperitif.
  5. Spina cheese: slightly sour-and-spicy cheese with a rich texture.

Local Beverages

  1. Cider: made from fermented apples, pears, or quince.
  2. Raki: a traditional anise-flavored spirit.
  3. Wines: try the red varieties, like Mavroudi and Vlahiko.

Where to Stay in Evrytania: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Saloon Park is a luxury hotel located near Karpenisi, surrounded by trees, and with easy access to the city’s market.
  2. Xenios Chalet is a guest house located in Ano Trikala, offering beautiful views and comfort in a natural environment.
  3. Guesthouse Selini is a traditional guest house located in Megalo Horio, offering access to the stunning landscapes and outdoor activities.
  4. Petit Resort is a luxury four-star hotel in the picturesque village of Krikello.

What to Buy in Evrytania: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Apaki: smoked meat with a distinctive flavor from the traditional curing process.
  2. Handmade pottery and ceramics from local artisans.
  3. Chestnuts and local honey.
  4. Handmade wooden decorative items.
  5. Embroidered fabrics such as tablecloths and napkins

Local Culture and Customs of Evrytania

Evrytania has a unique blend of traditions, customs, and celebrations. Here are some of the essential cultural events in the region:

  1. Kremastos Festival: Takes place on August 6th and includes folk dancing, singing, and music.
  2. Smugglers Festival: Takes place on the second weekend of August and includes theatrical performances, concerts, and a treasure hunt game.
  3. Wine Feast of the Good Grape Harvest: Takes place on the last Sunday of September and includes grape collecting, wine making demonstrations, and wine tastings.

Best Time to Visit Evrytania

The best time to visit Evrytania is from May to October, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Winter months from December to February are suitable for skiing and snow sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Evrytania

Do:

  1. Respect the environment and do not litter.
  2. Wear appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.
  3. Learn basic Greek phrases, as locals appreciate it.
  4. Try the local cuisine and attend cultural events

Don’t:

  1. Damage the natural and historical landmarks.
  2. Show disrespect to the local customs and traditions.
  3. Leave water taps open or take excessively long showers, as water is precious.

Safety and Health

Safety in Evrytania

Evrytania is generally safe; however, like everywhere else, crime exists. Be aware of pickpockets and keep an eye on your belongings. In case of an emergency, dial 112.

Health in Evrytania

Overall, Evrytania is a healthy region with no specific health hazards. However, like in any mountain environment, altitude sickness may occur in some individuals. Be sure to drink enough water and take breaks while engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Evrytania: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Evrytania

The closest airports are Athens International Airport or Thessaloniki International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Evrytania

The central bus station in Athens is KTEL Liosion, which offers daily bus services to Karpenissi. The trip takes around 4-5 hours.

Other Options

Taxis or car rentals are always good transportation options. You can also take a ferryboat to reach any of the islands near the region. Check the schedules and availability beforehand.

How to Get Around in Evrytania

The best way to travel in Evrytania is by car or motorbike, especially if you want to visit remote areas. Buses are the second-best option for traveling between cities in the region.

Where to Go Next from Evrytania: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Meteora: A UNESCO World Heritage Site consists of several rock formations topped by monasteries. This site is around 2.5 hours by car from Evrytania.
  2. Delphi: This ancient sanctuary is known for the Oracle of Apollo and the Temple of Apollo. It takes around 2.5 hours to reach Delphi by car.
  3. Athens: The Greek capital offers a myriad of iconic landmarks, including the Acropolis and the Parthenon. Athens is around 3 - 4 hours by car.

Conclusion

Evrytania is a must-visit destination in Greece, offering a range of outdoor activities, stunning natural landscapes, unique cultural experiences, and welcoming locals. Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or a mix of both, Evrytania has something for everyone. Plan your trip accordingly, and make sure to see everything this beautiful region has to offer.
