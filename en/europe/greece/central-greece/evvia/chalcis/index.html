Introduction to Chalcis

Chalcis is a city on the island of Evvia in Central Greece, situated around 80km north of Athens. The city has a long history dating back to the 8th century BC and is famous for its cultural and natural attractions. The city has recently become a popular destination for tourists because of its picturesque coastline, outdoor adventures, and charming Greek ambiance.

Food and Drink Scene of Chalcis

One of the highlights of visiting Chalcis is the traditional Greek cuisine scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Taramasalata
  • Grilled Octopus
  • Moussaka
  • Souvlaki

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek alcoholic beverage made from anise, usually served with a plate of mezedes (small plates of appetizers).
  • Greek Wine: Chalcis is home to some of the finest vineyards that produce delicious varieties of wine.
  • Greek Coffee: Rich in taste and culture, the strong and frothy Greek coffee is a must-try during your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Chalcis

Greek culture is known for its warm hospitality and traditional customs. While visiting Chalcis, it is essential to follow and respect the following customs:

  • Greeting and kissing on both cheeks is a common way of saying hello.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect Greek Orthodox religious events and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Chalcis

The best time to visit Chalcis is during the months of June to September, when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities and water sports. The city is also lively with festivals and traditional Greek cultural events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chalcis

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the mountain ranges through hiking/biking trails.
  • Respect Greek culture and customs during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside the church.
  • Interrupt locals during the Mediterranean siesta time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chalcis

Chalcis is a relatively safe tourist destination. However, it is crucial to take standard precautions such as:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Avoid wandering alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Chalcis

There are several medical facilities and doctors available in Chalcis. However, it is best to carry necessary medications such as motion sickness pills and sunscreen when visiting outdoor attractions.

Getting to Chalcis: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to reach Chalcis:

Airports Near Chalcis

The nearest international airports to Chalcis are:

  • Athens International Airport
  • Skiathos Island National Airport

Bus Stations Near Chalcis

Chalcis has a well-connected bus network with frequent buses running from Athens to Chalcis. The bus ride is around 90 minutes long.

Other Options

  • Renting a car or a bike

How to Get Around in Chalcis

Chalcis is a small and easily walkable city. Taxis and buses are also available for traveling to nearby scenic spots and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Chalcis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Evvia Island is known for its stunning and picturesque coastline, and there are several nearby destinations worth explorating after Chalcis:

  • Eritrea
  • Edipsos
  • Aedipsos-Thermae Sylla Spa
  • Styra

Conclusion

Chalcis is a beautiful tourist destination with something to offer for everyone. From outdoor adventures to traditional Greek cuisine, the city is a perfect blend of natural and cultural beauty. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most of your visit to Chalcis while ensuring a safe and enjoyable stay.
