Kymi, located in Evvia, Central Greece, is a small town that is often underrated but has a lot to offer to visitors. From its cultural attractions to natural wonders, Kymi has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ve listed down everything you need to know about Kymi, including places to visit, things to do, where to stay, how to get around, and more.

Introduction to Kymi

Kymi is a coastal town situated in the northern part of Evvia, Greece. It is surrounded by hills and mountains, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The town is renowned for its naval history and production of a traditional Greek pastry, known as ‘’Loukoumi’’. The town is small and can be explored on foot within a day.

List of Places to Visit in Kymi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kymi is rich in history and culture, and visitors can learn more about it by visiting its museums and historical places:

  • The Folklore Museum of Kymi
  • Kymi’s Naval Museum
  • The Monastery of Galataki

Natural Attractions of Kymi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kymi has several natural attractions, which are perfect for outdoor adventures and offer scenic views:

  • The Chapel of Agia Paraskevi
  • Mount Olympus
  • The Waterfalls of Dimosari

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kymi

Kymi offers excellent hiking and biking trails for visitors who love outdoor adventures, such as the:

  • Trek to the peak of Mount Olympus
  • Pedal along the coastline of Kymi
  • Discover the Waterfalls of Dimosari on foot

Best Things to Do in Kymi: Activities and Tours

In Kymi, visitors can participate in several activities and tours, including:

  • Sampling the Loukoumi at the Kymi Loukoumi Production Facility
  • Touring the town on a scooter
  • Horseback riding along the beach

Local Culture and Customs of Kymi

Kymi has a unique and rich culture, reflecting the history of the town. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect for religion and the church
  • Traditional Greek hospitality towards visitors
  • Politeness and courtesy to local people

Best Time to Visit Kymi

The best time to visit Kymi is during the spring (April to June) when the temperature is pleasant. The town is also less crowded during this time, making it easier to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kymi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the town by foot or bike

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Litter while enjoying outdoor adventures
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Kymi

Kymi is a safe town, and visitors needn’t worry about their safety. However, it is always better to take some precautions:

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Be alert while enjoying outdoor adventures

Health in Kymi

Kymi has several health facilities, and visitors can access them in case of any medical emergency. Additionally, visitors should take necessary precautions while enjoying outdoor activities, such as applying sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Getting to Kymi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kymi

The closest airport to Kymi is Athens International Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car, hire a taxi, or take a bus to Kymi.

Bus Stations Near Kymi

Kymi has a bus station that connects it to various cities in Greece, including Athens.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kymi via ferry. The town has a small port that connects it to other Greek islands, including Chios and Lesbos.

How To Get Around in Kymi

Kymi is a small town and can be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Kymi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
