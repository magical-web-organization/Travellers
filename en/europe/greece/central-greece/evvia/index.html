Introduction to Evvia

Evvia, also known as Euboea, is the second-largest Greek island after Crete, located in the Aegean Sea. It’s an island of contrasts with a rich history, beautiful beaches, rugged mountains, and traditional architecture. The island is approximately 180 kilometers long and covers an area of 3,657 square kilometers. This comprehensive guide is your ultimate handbook for exploring the stunning natural beauty and cultural heritage of Evvia.

List of Places to Visit in Evvia

Evvia is a fantastic travel destination for those seeking to explore the hidden gems of Greece. The island is rich in history and is home to several ancient archeological sites. Here are some of the top tourist attractions to visit on your trip to Evvia.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Archaeological Museum of Eretria
  • The Ancient Theatre of Eretria
  • The Temple of Apollo Daphnephoros
  • The Monastery of Saint David

Natural Attractions of Evvia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Petrified Forest
  • The Dimosaris Gorge
  • The Lihadonisia Islands
  • Karystos Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of Evvia

Evvia is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The island is home to fantastic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s landscape. Be sure to check out the following routes:

  • The Dirfis Trail
  • The Kouvara Trail
  • The Ochi Trail
  • The Andronikos Trail

Best Things to Do in Evvia: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Evvia has many other fun activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do on the island:

  • Sailing tours around the Lihadonisia Islands
  • Sea Kayaking in Karystos
  • Wine Tasting at local vineyards
  • Cooking Classes

Food and Drink Scene of Evvia

Greek cuisine is known for its fresh ingredients and simplicity. Evvia is no exception, with several traditional dishes to try, and local wines and spirits to taste.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fasolada (a traditional bean soup)
  • Grilled octopus
  • Moussaka
  • Taramasalata

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro (a grape-based pomace brandy)
  • Retsina (a white wine with a distinct pine resin flavor)
  • Metaxa (a Greek spirit made from brandy, wine, and herbs)

Local Culture and Customs of Evvia

Greek culture is diverse, with many regional customs and traditions. Some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of when visiting Evvia include:

  • Greeting people with a kiss on the cheek
  • Using “please” and “thank you” generously
  • Dressing modestly when visiting religious sites

Best Time to Visit Evvia

The best time to visit Evvia is during the spring (April to May) and fall (September to November) when the temperature is mild and the crowds are thin. Summer (June to August) is the peak season, and the island can be busy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Evvia

Do:

  • Learn some Greek phrases
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment
  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Swim in unauthorized areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Evvia

Evvia is considered a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s always essential to take precautions while traveling, such as securing your belongings, staying alert, and avoiding any risky activities.

Health in Evvia

Evvia has several medical facilities available for visitors in case of any emergency. It’s recommended to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses while traveling.

Getting to Evvia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Evvia

The nearest international airport to Evvia is Athens International Airport (ATH), located 80 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to reach Evvia.

Bus Stations Near Evvia

The KTEL bus service connects Evvia with Athens and other cities in Greece. Many buses run daily, making it easy to travel to and from the island.

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available on the island to explore at your leisure.
  • Ferries from central Greece are also available.

How to Get Around in Evvia

The best way to get around Evvia is by car or motorbike rental. Most of the tourist attractions are easily accessible by road. Buses are also available for intercity travel but may have limited schedules.
