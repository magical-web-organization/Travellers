Are you looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Greece? Look no further than Edipsos, a charming town in Evia island, Central Greece. Known for its natural hot springs, lush vegetation, and fascinating history, Edipsos is a hidden gem waiting to be explored.

Whether you want to relax in thermal baths, hike through verdant forests, or discover ancient ruins, Edipsos has something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to see and things to do in this enchanting town.

Introduction to Edipsos

Located in the northeastern part of Evia, Edipsos is a seaside town known for its therapeutic thermal springs. The locals have been soaking in these springs for centuries, believing in their healing properties. Apart from the hot springs, Edipsos is also famous for its breathtaking natural beauty, historical landmarks, and traditional cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Edipsos

Check out our list of must-see attractions and landmarks in Edipsos.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Roman Bath: One of the most famous thermal springs in Edipsos, this bath dates back to the Roman era.
  • The Cave of Sylla: A mystical cave steeped in legends and myths, named after the famous sorceress Sylla.
  • The Ancient City of Edipsos: Wander through the ruins of the ancient city, where you can still spot the remains of temples, a theater, and a market square.
  • The Folklore Museum of Edipsos: Learn about the town’s cultural traditions and history through a collection of exhibits showcasing artifacts, costumes, and household items.

Natural Attractions of Edipsos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gouvon Beach: A serene beach with crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Mount Telethrion: Hike up this mountain and be rewarded with magnificent views over the Aegean Sea and Edipsos.
  • Lichadonisia Islands: A group of small islands located near the town, offering a picturesque landscape and a perfect spot for snorkeling or diving.
  • Agiokampos Wetlands: A protected area with great biodiversity, home to many rare bird species, ideal for nature lovers and birdwatchers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edipsos

If you love hiking or cycling, Edipsos has many options for you. The following routes will allow you to explore the town and its surroundings while keeping active.

  • Edipsos Thermal Springs Trail: A 6-kilometer trail that passes through the town’s hot springs and offers stunning views of the sea.
  • The Waterfalls of Drymona: A moderate hiking trail through the lush forest of Drymona, leading to picturesque waterfalls.
  • Mount Ochi Trail: A challenging trail for experienced hikers, climbing up to the highest peak of Evia, offering breathtaking panoramic views.

Best Things to Do in Edipsos: Activities and Tours

Take part in the following activities and tours to experience the best of Edipsos.

  • Hot Springs Tour: Visit the town’s famous thermal baths, relax, and rejuvenate your body with the healing properties of the water.
  • Sea Kayaking Adventure: Explore the coastline of Edipsos and its hidden caves and coves by kayaking, led by experienced guides.
  • Food Tour of Edipsos: Sample the town’s delicious local cuisine and wine, through a guided tour of local markets, shops, and restaurants.
  • Scuba Diving: Discover the underwater world of Edipsos, through a thrilling scuba diving experience, in the crystal-clear waters of the Aegean Sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Edipsos

No trip to Edipsos is complete without experiencing the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Revithokeftedes: Delicious chickpea balls, with herbs and spices, served as meze.
  • Kleftiko: A traditional dish made of lamb or goat, slow-cooked with potatoes, onions, and herbs.
  • Lalaggia: A sweet pastry covered with sesame seeds, served with honey.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: A strong spirit distilled from grapes, often served as an aperitif or digestif.
  • Retsina: A white or rosé wine, with a distinctive pine resin flavor, popular in Greece.
  • Frappé: A frothy, iced coffee beverage, popular in Greece, and served with or without milk.

Where to Stay in Edipsos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Edipsos offers plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks.

  • Thermae Sylla Spa & Wellness Hotel: A luxurious hotel, with direct access to Edipsos’ thermal springs, offering spa and wellness services.
  • Hotel Artemis: A family-run hotel, near the town’s beach, with cozy rooms and sea views.
  • Guesthouse Kallirroi: A budget-friendly guesthouse, located in the heart of the town, with friendly staff and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Edipsos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Edipsos home with you, through these locally-made souvenirs and products.

  • Thermal Socks: Keep your feet warm during the colder months, with soft and cozy socks made of local wool.
  • Olive Oil: Edipsos produces high-quality olive oil, with a fruity taste and a strong aroma.
  • Honey-based Products: Local honey is used to make a variety of products, from soap to beauty creams, known for their nourishing and moisturizing properties.

Local Culture and Customs of Edipsos

Edipsos has a rich traditional culture, influenced by its historical background and the local community’s way of life. Respect for elders and hospitality are highly valued. On religious and local feast days, it’s common to see colorful parades and festivals held throughout the town.

Best Time to Visit Edipsos

The best time to visit Edipsos is from late May to early October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the tourist season is in full swing. During the winter, the town is quieter, but you can still enjoy the hot springs and the natural beauty of the surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edipsos

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Respect the town’s historical and natural landmarks.
  • Follow the etiquettes of the local community.

Don’t:

  • Wear swimsuits outside the beach area.
  • Litter in the town or the natural sites.
  • Interrupt others in their hot spring sessions.

Safety and Health

Edipsos is generally a safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always recommended to take basic precautions, such as locking your valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Edipsos

  • Take extra caution when swimming in the sea, as there can be strong currents in some areas.
  • Follow safety guidelines during outdoor activities, such as hiking or kayaking.
  • Stay aware of changes in the weather, especially during the winter months.

Health in Edipsos

Edipsos’ thermal springs are known for their healing properties, and many people visit the town specifically for this reason. However, it’s always recommended to consult with a doctor before soaking in the hot springs, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Edipsos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Edipsos is easily accessible, with several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Edipsos

Athinaios International Airport (ATH) is the nearest international airport to Evia. You can then take a taxi, rent a car or take a bus to Edipsos.

Bus Stations Near Edipsos

KTEL buses run regularly between Athens and Edipsos, as well as to other towns in Evia.

Other Options

You can also reach Edipsos by car, either by taking the Ferry from Arkitsa to Edipsos, or via the Chalkida bridge.

How to Get Around in Edipsos

The best way to get around Edipsos is by foot, as the town is small and easy to navigate. You can also rent a car or bicycle to explore the surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Edipsos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Evia island, check out these nearby destinations and excursions.

  • Chalkida: The capital city of Evia, famous for its old-town charm, picturesque harbor, and lively nightlife.
  • Karystos: A scenic coastal town, with imposing mountains, crystal-clear beaches, and archaeological sites.
  • The Dragon Houses of Styra: A group of mysterious stone buildings, believed to be ancient tombs or temples.

Conclusion

Edipsos is a destination that should not be missed, with its unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. From relaxing in thermal baths to hiking through scenic mountains, Edipsos has something for every type of traveler. We hope this travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to this enchanting town.
