Introduction to Athens

Athens, the capital of Greece, is one of the most historical and culturally rich cities in the world. Its legacy dates back to over 2,500 years ago, with some of the greatest architectural wonders of ancient times still standing here. The city is also renowned for its bustling food and drink scene, lively nightlife, and captivating art and music culture. Athens attracts millions of visitors every year, and once you’re here, there’s no end to the things you can see and do.

List of Places to Visit in Athens

There are numerous locations to explore and discover in Athens. Here is a list of places that you shouldn’t miss during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Acropolis and Parthenon
  • The Acropolis Museum
  • The National Archaeological Museum
  • The Ancient Agora of Athens
  • The Panathenaic Stadium (aka Kallimarmaro)
  • The Temple of Olympian Zeus
  • The Theater of Herodes Atticus

Natural Attractions of Athens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Lycabettus
  • The National Garden
  • The Athens Riviera
  • The Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center
  • The Attica Zoological Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Athens

Athens offers several hiking and biking routes, where you can explore the city’s natural surroundings and admire breathtaking views. Some of the top options are:

  • Mount Hymettus
  • The Philopappos Hill Walk
  • The Parnitha National Park Hike
  • The Athens Coastline Bike Tour

Best Things to Do in Athens: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of the city
  • Visit local markets and food stalls
  • Attend a concert or live music performance
  • Spend a day at the beach
  • Take a cooking class

Week-Long Itinerary for Athens

Here’s a suggested itinerary for those who want to experience everything the city has to offer:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Acropolis, The Acropolis Museum, and explore the Plaka District
2 Take a walking tour of downtown Athens, visit Monastiraki Square and the National Archaeological Museum
3 Visit the ancient Agora of Athens and the nearby Kerameikos Cemetery
4 Explore Mount Hymettus and the National Garden
5 Take a day trip to the Athens Riviera
6 Spend the day at the Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center
7 Visit the Parnitha National Park and the Attica Zoological Park

Food and Drink Scene of Athens

The gastronomic culture of Athens will leave you spoilt for choice. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to explore:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvlaki - Greek kebab made with meat (pork, chicken, or lamb) and vegetables served on a skewer
  • Moussaka - layers of sauteed eggplants, tomatoes, minced meat, and bechamel sauce, baked to perfection
  • Greek Salad - a fresh salad made with tomatoes, cucumbers, feta cheese, olives, and onions
  • Spanakopita - a delicious savory pastry made with spinach and feta cheese filling and crispy phyllo dough

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo - an anise-flavored aperitif served with ice and water
  • Mythos - a light and refreshing Greek beer
  • Frappé Coffee - Greek iced coffee, blended with milk and sugar

Local Culture and Customs of Athens

When visiting Athens, it is essential to be aware of local customs and etiquette. Greeks typically greet each other by shaking hands and are warm and welcoming to visitors. It is important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites like the Acropolis or temples. Tipping is not compulsory, but it is customary to round up bills or leave small change in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Athens

The best time to visit Athens is from March to May and September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are relatively low. However, keep in mind that Athens tends to be busy all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Athens

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Greek phrases like “Kalimera” (Good Morning) and “Efharisto” (Thank you)
  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals
  • Try to eat at local cafes and restaurants rather than fast food chains
  • Visit smaller neighborhoods like Exarchia and Petralona

Don’t:

  • Jaywalk or cross the street without using the designated crosswalks and zebra crossings
  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites
  • Touch ancient monuments or graffiti on historical sites
  • Feed stray animals

Safety and Health

Safety in Athens

Athens is considered a safe city as long as you practice basic safety measures like being aware of your surroundings and avoiding isolated areas at night. Stay vigilant for pickpocketing in crowded areas and keep an eye on personal belongings. It is best to store passports and travel documents in a safe place and carry a photocopy with you.

Health in Athens

Athens has a high standard of healthcare facilities, but the cost of medical care can be high. It is essential to have travel insurance before visiting Athens, which covers medical expenses. Tap water in Athens is safe to drink, but it is advisable to drink bottled water.

Getting to Athens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Athens has excellent transport links from all over the world.

Airports Near Athens

  • Athens International Airport (ATH) - located approximately 20 miles from the city center, accessible by metro, bus, and taxi

Bus Stations Near Athens

  • KTEL Kifissou - located approximately 2.5 miles from the city center, accessible by metro and taxi
  • Liosion Bus Terminal - located approximately 4 miles north of the city center, accessible by metro and taxi

Other Options

  • Train - Athens has a central train station located approximately 20 minutes from the city center, which provides connections to several locations throughout Greece

How to Get Around in Athens

The most convenient way to get around Athens is by using the public transport system. The metro network covers most of the city and is efficient and affordable. Taxis are also available throughout the city, but be sure to check if the meter is running.

Where to Go Next from Athens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several fascinating destinations near Athens worth exploring:

  • Delphi - ancient sanctuary and archaeological site located approximately 2.5 hours from Athens
  • Corinth - an ancient city located on the Isthmus of Corinth, approximately 1 hour from Athens
  • Aegina - A charming island located in the Saronic Gulf, approximately 50 minutes from Athens
  • Nafplion - A picturesque town located in the Peloponnese region, approximately 2.5 hours from Athens

Conclusion

Athens is an incredible destination that is sure to leave you awe-inspired. With its incredible historical sites, delicious food, and breathtaking landscapes, Athens has something for everyone. Whether you’re here for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation, this travel guide will help you make the most of your visit.
