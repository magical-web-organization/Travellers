Introduction to Spata

Spata is a small municipality located in Attica, within Central Greece. Known for its lush green landscapes, Spata offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town boasts a rich cultural heritage with ancient sites, museums, traditional shops, and exquisite restaurants. In this guide, we will walk you through all the exciting things you can do in Spata, including the must-see attractions, best places to eat, local culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Spata

Spata offers an array of breathtaking natural and historical attractions. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Spata has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of Spata’s must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archeological site of the ancient Greek city of Vravrona: Delve into the rich history of ancient Greece by visiting the archeological site of Vravrona. The place is home to a temple of Artemis, which was once a significant religious center during the Mycenaean period. The site also houses a museum with a vast collection of artifacts from the ancient Greek and Roman empires.

  • Monastery of Saint Ephraim: This is a stunning 10th-century monastery situated in the heart of Spata. The monastery features Byzantine architecture with impressive frescoes, intricate mosaics, and rich history. Inside the church, you will find the remains of Saint Ephraim, which brings many pilgrims to the area.

  • Nea Recteion: If you’re interested in the Byzantine Empire, make Nea Recteion your destination of choice. It is a well-preserved Byzantine monument with stunning architectural features like chapels and fortification walls.

Natural Attractions of Spata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Gerania: Mount Gerania is a natural wonder home to an array of wildlife and an abundance of hiking trails. The views from the top of the mountain are breathtaking, and you can even find small springs throughout the hiking trails.

  • Vravrona Bay: This sandy beach is probably the most popular in the area and offers crystal-clear water, crystal like water, and spectacular views of the nearby islands.

  • Spata Forest: Spata Forest is a densely wooded area located north of Spata, and provides excellent opportunities for hiking and exploring nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spata

Spata offers a wide range of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a quick, easy hike or a challenging trail to test your physical limits, Spata has trails for all levels. Some of the popular hiking/biking routes in Spata include:

  • Mount Gerania Hiking Trail: This is one of the most popular trails in Spata. It is a moderate hike with stunning views of the surrounding nature and mountain range.

  • Vravrona Trail: This trail is located in the archeological site of Vravrona and takes you through an ancient temple and sanctuary with plenty of stunning views of the area and the nearby beaches.

Best Things to Do in Spata: Activities and Tours

Spata offers exciting activities and tours that will make for a memorable vacation. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Wine Tours: Spata is home to numerous wineries that produce some of the best wines in Greece. Take a wine tour to learn about the winemaking process and sample some of the wines.

  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to make authentic Greek dishes by taking a cooking class in Spata. You will learn how to make traditional Greek dishes while enjoying the local wines.

  • Spa Day: Treat yourself to a relaxing spa day in Spata. You’ll find plenty of spas and wellness centers that offer massages, facials, and other wellness treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Spata

Greece is famous for its delicious food and drinks, and Spata is no exception. From local taverns to fine-dining restaurants, you’ll find something for every taste bud.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: This is a popular Greek dish made with eggplant, potatoes, and ground beef. It is then topped with a delicious béchamel sauce and baked to perfection.

  • Souvlaki: Souvlaki is a famous Greek dish made with skewered meat, typically lamb, along with vegetables like tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers. It is commonly served as street food in Greece.

  • Gyro: Gyro is a Greek fast food dish made with roasted meat, usually pork or chicken, tomatoes, onions, and tzatziki sauce. It is served in pita bread and is a popular meal option for tourists and locals alike.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: Ouzo is an anise-flavored Greek alcoholic beverage that is usually served with the appetizers. It is a must-try for anyone visiting Greece.

  • Metaxa: Metaxa is a Greek spirit made from grapes and flavored with herbs. It is usually served as an after-dinner drink in Greece.

What to Buy in Spata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spata is known for its artisanal crafts, olive oil, and honey products. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Spata include:

  • Olive oil: Greece is famous for its high-quality olive oil, and Spata is home to some of the best. Look for extra virgin olive oil produced locally and take home a bottle.

  • Honey: Spata is also known for its exquisite honey. Look for honey made from the local wildflowers, which is said to have a sweeter taste.

  • Greek pottery: Spata has a flourishing pottery scene, and you’ll find plenty of artisans selling traditional Greek pottery products like vases, mugs, and plates.

Local Culture and Customs of Spata

Spata has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s worth taking the time to learn about the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the things you should know about Spata’s culture and customs:

  • Respect for religion: Greece is a deeply religious country, and Spata is no exception. Be respectful of religious sites and customs.

  • Dress code: While Spata is a small town, it is an excellent idea to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Spata

The best time to visit Spata is during the spring and fall seasons. The weather is pleasant, and the crowds are lighter. Summer, while the busiest with tourists, can be very hot and crowded. Winter also gets cold and rainy, and some attractions may close during that period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spata

Do:

  • Learn basic Greek phrases: The locals appreciate it when visitors make an effort to learn the local language.

  • Be respectful: Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Littering: Greece is a cleanliness conscious country, please dispose of your waste in the waste bins.

  • Making excessive noise: Avoid being too loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spata

Spata, like any other Greek town, is generally safe for tourists. However, it’s a good idea to take the common precautions you would take while traveling in any foreign country. Avoid deserted areas at night and be mindful of your property and belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Spata

For minor health issues, you can visit one of Spata’s local clinics. However, for more serious medical conditions or emergencies, we recommend seeking medical attention at Athens International Airport’s Emergency Center.

Getting to Spata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spata is conveniently located near Athens International Airport. Here are some transportation options you can use to get to Spata:

Airports Near Spata

  • Athens International Airport: This is the nearest major airport to Spata. The airport is located approximately 10 km from the town.

Bus Stations Near Spata

  • KTEL Attikis Bus Station: This bus station is located close to Athens International Airport and provides transportation to Spata.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car at Athens International Airport and drive to Spata.

How to Get Around in Spata

Spata is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to explore beyond the town or need transportation to visit one of the nearby attractions, you can use a taxi service or a car rental.

Where to Go Next from Spata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Greece, here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can add to your itinerary:

  • Athens: Athens is Greece’s capital and an excellent destination for art, culture, history, and shopping enthusiasts.

  • Sounion: Sounion, known for its ancient temple of Poseidon and scenic views, is a popular day trip destination from Spata.

  • Mycenae: This ancient archaeological site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the famous Lion Gate and the Tomb of Agamemnon.

Conclusion

Spata is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be explored. With its rich history, natural beauty, culinary delights, and laid-back atmosphere, it is an excellent destination for any traveler seeking a peaceful escape. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Spata has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan the perfect Spata vacation.
