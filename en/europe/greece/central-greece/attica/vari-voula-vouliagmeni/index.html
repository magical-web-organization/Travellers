Introduction to Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Nestled on Greece’s central coast in the region of Attica, Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is a charming seaside town famous for its natural beauty, rich culture, and stunning beaches. The town is a popular tourist destination, offering a variety of attractions and activities for travelers of all types. Whether you want to explore the town’s cultural heritage, immerse yourself in nature, or simply soak up the sun on a picturesque beach, you’ll find plenty to do in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local cuisine to try in this beautiful coastal gem.

List of Places to Visit in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Vouliagmeni Lake

One of the most popular attractions in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni, Vouliagmeni Lake is a natural phenomenon that has been attracting visitors for centuries. The lake is situated underneath a limestone hill and is connected to the sea via underground channels. The mineral-rich water is believed to have healing properties, and many visitors come to swim or relax in the tranquil surroundings. The lake is also home to a variety of marine life, including fish and turtles.

2. Archaeological Museum of Vouliagmeni

Located in the heart of the town, the Archaeological Museum of Vouliagmeni is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum houses a collection of artifacts from the ancient city of Anavissos, which was destroyed in the 5th century AD. Visitors can see pottery, jewelry, and other objects that offer a glimpse into daily life in ancient Greece.

Natural Attractions of Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Astir Beach

Astir Beach is one of the most exclusive beaches in the area, offering crystal clear waters and stunning views of the Saronic Gulf. The beach is surrounded by pine trees and boasts luxurious amenities like sun loungers and parasols. It’s an excellent spot for swimming, sunbathing, and people-watching.

2. Vari Beach

Vari Beach is a more laid-back option than Astir Beach, with a more local vibe and fewer crowds. The beach stretches for several kilometers and offers plenty of space to spread out. The water is calm and warm, making it perfect for a swim or a paddleboard session.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are several hiking trails and biking routes in the area, offering stunning views of the coast and the surrounding hills. Some of the most popular trails include the coastal trail from Vari to Vouliagmeni and the Mount Hymettus Trail, which takes you through the beautiful pine forest that covers the mountain.

Best Things to Do in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni: Activities and Tours

1. Water Sports

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni boasts some of the best water sports facilities in Greece, with a variety of activities on offer, including paddleboarding, kayaking, and windsurfing. There are plenty of rental shops in the area where you can rent equipment and book lessons.

2. Horseback Riding

The surrounding countryside is perfect for horseback riding, offering stunning views of the coastline and hills. Several ranches in the area offer guided tours and lessons, catering to riders of all levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Day 1: Begin your trip with a visit to Vouliagmeni Lake, where you can swim and relax in the healing waters. In the afternoon, head to the Archaeological Museum of Vouliagmeni for a glimpse into the area’s rich history.

Day 2: Spend the day at Astir Beach, one of the most exclusive beaches in the area, where you can relax in style and soak up the sun.

Day 3: Take a leisurely hike along the coastal trail from Vari to Vouliagmeni, taking in the stunning views of the coastline and the sparkling blue sea.

Day 4: Rent a paddleboard or kayak and explore the coast from a new perspective. Alternatively, book a windsurfing lesson and ride the waves.

Day 5: Visit Mount Hymettus and hike through the beautiful pine forest, taking in the stunning views of the surrounding hills and the city of Athens.

Day 6: Go horseback riding in the beautiful countryside, exploring the hills and coastline from a different perspective.

Day 7: Spend your last day in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni relaxing on Vari Beach, swimming in the warm waters or just soaking up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Must-Try Dishes

The food in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is a delicious mix of traditional Greek and Mediterranean cuisine. Some standout dishes include:

  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat on a skewer, served with pita bread and tzatziki.

  • Moussaka: A classic Greek dish featuring layers of eggplant, minced meat, and potatoes, topped with a creamy sauce.

  • Octopus: Grilled or boiled octopus, served with olive oil and lemon.

Local Beverages

Greece is known for its wine, and Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is no exception. The area produces a range of excellent wines, including white, red, and rosé varieties. Other popular drinks include ouzo, a traditional Greek anise-flavored spirit, and tsipouro, a strong grape brandy.

Where to Stay in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the top hotels in the area include the Divani Apollon Palace & Thalasso, the Athenian Riviera Hotel, and the Plaza Vouliagmeni Strand Hotel. For budget-friendly options, consider the Vouliagmeni Suites or the Beachside Bungalows.

What to Buy in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, you will definitely want to bring home some local olive oil, which is considered some of the best in the world. Other popular souvenirs include handmade ceramics, leather goods, and traditional crafts like woven rugs and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Greece has a rich cultural heritage that is still very much alive in the present day. Some customs and traditions you might encounter in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni include:

  • Religious festivals: Greece is predominantly Orthodox Christian, and you may encounter religious festivals and processions during your visit.

  • Family values: Family is extremely important in Greek culture, and you’ll notice that many businesses are family-owned and operated.

  • Hospitality: Greek people are known for their warmth and hospitality, and you’re sure to be welcomed with open arms during your stay in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni.

Best Time to Visit Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

The best time to visit Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, the area is also pleasant in the shoulder seasons of May and October, when the crowds are thinner, and the weather is still mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample the wine.
  • Learn a few basic Greek phrases to show respect for the local language.

Don’t:

  • Throw away your trash in public places.
  • Touch or remove ancient artifacts.
  • Speak too loudly in public places.
  • Ignore the warning signs at the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is a very safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and a highly visible police presence. However, you should still take basic precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and not walking alone at night.

Health in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Greece has a high standard of healthcare, and Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni has several medical facilities and pharmacies in case of emergency. However, you should still take basic precautions like using sunscreen, drinking plenty of water, and avoiding excessive alcohol consumption.

Getting to Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

The closest international airport to Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is Athens International Airport, which is located about 23 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or public transportation to the town.

Bus Stations Near Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

There are several bus stations in the area, including the Vari Bus Station and the Vouliagmeni Bus Station. Buses run regularly to Athens and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or take a taxi from Athens to Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni, which is about a 30-40 minute drive away.

How to Get Around in Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is a relatively small town, and most of the attractions and amenities are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore further afield, you can rent a bike, take the bus or a taxi, or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is located in the heart of the Attica region, making it an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations like Athens, Sounion, and the island of Aegina. You can also take day trips to other coastal towns like Glyfada and Vouliagmeni.

Conclusion

Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni is a delightful coastal town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing on the beach, you’ll find plenty to do and see in this beautiful part of Greece. We hope this guide has helped you plan the perfect trip and that you have a wonderful time exploring all that Vari-Voula-Vouliagmeni has to offer.
