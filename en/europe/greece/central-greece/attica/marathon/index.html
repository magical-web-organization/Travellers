Introduction to Marathon

Marathon, a small town located in the area of Attica in Central Greece, is known worldwide for the historic Battle of Marathon that took place here in 490 BC. The town is surrounded by beautiful nature, including Mount Pentelicus to the northeast, forests to the east, and the Aegean Sea to the west. Tourists come to Marathon to enjoy the pristine beaches, hike through stunning terrain, and explore the rich archaeological and cultural heritage of the area.

List of Places to Visit in Marathon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Marathon: This museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the fascinating history of the region, with exhibits ranging from prehistoric times to Roman times.
  • Tomb of the Athenians: Situated between Marathon and Rhamnous, this monument is believed to be the resting place of Athenian soldiers who fought in the battle of Marathon.
  • Ancient Sanctuary of the Egyptian Gods: This ancient temple complex is dedicated to the gods Serapis and Isis, and was built in the Hellenistic period.
  • Pan’s Cave: A grotto that was sacred to the god Pan during ancient times, located atop a hill overlooking the sea and the bay of Marathon.

Natural Attractions of Marathon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Marathon Lake: An artificial lake created in the ’30s for irrigation and recreational purposes. The lake is surrounded by beautiful hills and is popular for fishing, swimming, and birdwatching.
  • Marathon Beach: A vast sandy beach with crystal clear water and breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea. The beach is approximately 40km long.
  • Mount Pentelicus: A range of high hills that separates Marathon from Athens, covered with rich vegetation and forests. The main peak (Parnes) is over 1,400m in height and offers stunning views of the Attica basin.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marathon

  • Marathon – Nea Makri Trail: This trail connects the seaside town of Nea Makri to Marathon via dense forests, picturesque hills, and stunning views of the sea. It stretches for approximately 10km and is marked throughout.
  • Mount Pentelicus trails: There’s a network of marked trails in the hills and mountains of Mount Pentelicus, ranging from easy to challenging. Among the most popular trails are the hike to the peak of Parnes, and the route to Pan’s cave.
  • Marathon Dam trail: A relatively flat trail that circles Marathon Dam, stretching for approximately 15km. The trail is both scenic and informative, with viewing areas and informational signs along the way.

Best Things to Do in Marathon: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the beach: Marathon beach is one of the biggest and most beautiful in Greece. You can swim, sunbathe, rent a beach umbrella and chair, or enjoy water sports like windsurfing, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Take a historical tour: For those interested in the Battle of Marathon, there are several tours available that explore the battle site, the tomb of the Athenians, and the other remarkable archaeological sites in the area.
  • Hiking and Biking: The natural scenery surrounding Marathon is stunning, with challenging hikes and mountain bike routes available to explore the area.
  • Visit the Archaeological Museum: Marvel at the collection of artifacts from ancient times and immerse yourself in the rich history of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Marathon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Kleftiko: A traditional Greek dish that consists of slow-cooked lamb with vegetables and potatoes. The meat is juicy and tender, cooked in parchment paper to seal in the flavor.
  • Htapodi:</b> A delicious grilled octopus dish, tenderized with vinegar and olive oil, usually accompanied by a side of boiled greens.
  • Frappe: A refreshing summer drink that consists of instant coffee, sugar, and water. All mixed in a shaker with ice and served in a tall glass.

Local Beverages

  • Retsina wine: A white or rose wine with a unique pine resin aroma, originating from the region of Attica.
  • Tsipouro: A strong distilled spirit similar to grappa, made from the pomace of grapes.

Where to Stay in Marathon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Marathon Beach Resort: A beachfront hotel with spacious rooms, pools, and a private beach.
  • The Marathon Inn Hotel: A charming family-run hotel with a garden and traditional atmosphere.
  • Athens International Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located near the center of Athens, about 30 km from Marathon.

What to Buy in Marathon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Marathon pottery: The traditional ceramics made in Marathon are famous for their quality and beauty. They come in various shapes and sizes.
  • Evvoiki olive oil: This extra virgin olive oil is produced in the olive groves of Marathon, known for their high-quality olives.
  • Marathon figs: The Marathon fig has a sweet flavor and succulent texture. You can find them fresh or dried in local markets or specialty shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Marathon

Greece is known for its hospitality, and Marathon is no exception. Locals are friendly, curious, and always ready to offer a helping hand. It’s important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, especially women who should cover their shoulders and knees.

Best Time to Visit Marathon

The best time to visit Marathon is from April to October, when the weather is sunny and warm. July and August are the hottest and most crowded months, so it’s best to avoid peak season to enjoy more serene surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marathon

Do:

  • Do try authentic Greek cuisine: Greek food is delicious, and there’s no better place to try it than in Greece.
  • Do visit cultural and historical sites: Marathon is known for its rich cultural heritage, and there’s plenty to explore in the area.
  • Do interact with locals: The people of Marathon are friendly, and engaging with them can offer insight into the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Greece’s natural beauty is one of the country’s main attractions, so it’s important to keep it clean and respectful.
  • Don’t forget to carry cash: Some places in Marathon might not accept credit cards, so it’s always a good idea to carry some cash as a backup.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marathon

Marathon is a safe town, and travelers shouldn’t face any significant security issues. However, it’s still advised to take basic safety precautions such as not leaving your belongings unattended, especially on the beach.

Health in Marathon

Greece has a generally well-developed healthcare system, and Marathon has a medical center offering basic medical care. It’s recommended to purchase health insurance before your visit. Sun protection is also crucial due to the strong Greek sun.

Getting to Marathon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marathon

  • Athens International Airport: Situated in Spata, Athens, it’s the most convenient airport to get to Marathon.

Bus Stations Near Marathon

  • KTEL Attica Buses: Operate frequent services between Athens and Marathon.

Other Options

  • Renting a car: Renting a car is an excellent option to explore Marathon and the surrounding areas at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Marathon

The best way to get around Marathon is by car. Taxis are also available and often used to get to and from the Athens airport.

Where to Go Next from Marathon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Athens: The vibrant city of Athens is just a 30-minute drive from Marathon. Visit the ancient Acropolis or enjoy a night out in the city center.
  • Cape Sounion: A stunning promontory that’s home to the ancient Temple of Poseidon, with breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea.
  • Rafina: A charming fishing village located about 20km from Marathon, home to a picturesque harbour with many cafes and restaurants.

Conclusion

Marathon is a beautiful and fascinating destination in Greece, offering numerous outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, we hope you’ll be able to plan a memorable trip to Marathon with ease.
