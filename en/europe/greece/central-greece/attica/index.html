Introduction to Attica

Attica is a beautiful region located in Central Greece. It is one of the most visited destinations in Greece, thanks to its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and bustling towns and cities. As the centre of ancient Athens, Attica has an incredible cultural legacy, as well as a wealth of natural attractions and opportunities for outdoor adventures.

Attica has countless attractions and sights to see. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Acropolis of Athens
  • Ancient Agora of Athens
  • National Archaeological Museum of Athens
  • Museum of Cycladic Art
  • Cape Sounion and the Temple of Poseidon
  • Temple of Olympian Zeus
  • Erechtheion.
  • Theater of Dionysus

  • First Cemetery of Athens

Best Things to Do in Attica: Activities and Tours

Attica offers an array of activities and tours to suit all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Attica:

  • Visit the Acropolis Museum
  • Discover vibrant Monastiraki Flea Market
  • Hike along the Attica coastline to Cape Sounion
  • Explore Plaka, the oldest neighbourhood in Athens
  • Visit the Church of Saint George in Oropos
  • Boat tour around Aegina island

Local Culture and Customs of Attica

Attica has a rich cultural heritage that has been shaped over centuries of Greek history and mythology. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, which include:

  • Dress appropriately for religious sites and ceremonies
  • Be respectful of local customs and cultures
  • Keep noise levels down, especially in residential areas

Best Time to Visit Attica

The best time to visit Attica is during the months of April to June and September to October, as the weather is mild and comfortable. The peak tourist season is during the summer months of July and August, when the temperature can rise to over 30°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Attica

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to help you navigate Attica:

Do:

  • Visit the Acropolis of Athens
  • Try traditional Greek cuisine
  • Learn some basic Greek phrases
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Ignore dress codes for religious sites and ceremonies
  • Drink tap water
  • Rent a motorcycle or ATV without proper licensing

Safety and Health

Attica is generally a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always exercise caution, especially in crowded tourist areas, and be aware of pickpockets. The tap water in Attica is not potable, so it is best to drink bottled water.

Getting to Attica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Attica

How to Get Around in Attica

Attica has an excellent public transportation system, including buses, metro, trolleys and taxis. Hiring a car is also a good option for those who want to explore more remote parts of the region.
