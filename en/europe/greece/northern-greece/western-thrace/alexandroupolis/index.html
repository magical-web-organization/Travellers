Introduction to Alexandroupolis

Alexandroupolis is a charming city located in Western Thrace, in Northern Greece, in Europe. The city is situated on the coast of the Aegean Sea, and its natural beauty attracts many visitors each year. Alexandroupolis has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its museums, monuments, and architecture. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, there’s something for everyone in Alexandroupolis.

List of Places to Visit in Alexandroupolis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ecclesiastical Museum of Alexandroupolis

The Ecclesiastical Museum of Alexandroupolis is located within the Holy Metropolis of Alexandroupolis. It showcases the history of the Christian Orthodox faith in Western Thrace and includes rare artifacts, such as old manuscripts, icons, and religious vestments.

Ethnological Museum of Thrace

The Ethnological Museum of Thrace is dedicated to exploring the culture and history of the Thrace region. It has a collection of traditional costumes, pottery, tools, and objects used in everyday life, offering an insight into the region’s past.

Alexandroupolis Light House

The Alexandroupolis Light House is an iconic landmark, located at the end of the city’s port. The Light House is a testament to the city’s maritime heritage and provides an excellent viewpoint to enjoy the stunning sunset over the Aegean Sea.

Natural Attractions of Alexandroupolis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Delta of Evros River National Park

The Delta of Evros River National Park is a protected area that covers approximately 200 square kilometers of wetlands, forests, and sand dunes. The park is home to hundreds of bird species, including storks, flamingos, and pelicans. Visitors can explore the park by hiking trails or canoeing in the rivers.

Makri Beach

Makri Beach is a beautiful sandy beach located at the southeastern end of Alexandroupolis. The crystal clear waters and tranquil surroundings make it a perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.

Turkish Fortress of Feres

The Turkish Fortress of Feres is a historic landmark of Alexandroupolis, located 28 km northeast of the city. The fortress dates back to the 14th century and offers panoramic views of the Evros river valley and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alexandroupolis

Dadia-Lefkimi-Soufli Forest National Park

The Dadia-Lefkimi-Soufli Forest National Park is located approximately 100 km from Alexandroupolis and is home to many species of birds of prey, including the rare black vulture. The park has several marked hiking and biking trails, suitable for all levels of fitness and experience.

Avantas Gorge

The Avantas Gorge is a scenic and secluded hiking spot, located approximately 60 km from Alexandroupolis. The gorge has a natural water pool, surrounded by pine trees, making it a perfect spot to cool off on a hot day.

Best Things to Do in Alexandroupolis: Activities and Tours

Sea Kayaking

Sea kayaking is a popular activity in Alexandroupolis, with many tour operators offering trips along the coast. Paddling in the crystal clear waters of the Aegean Sea and exploring the nearby caves and cliffs is an unforgettable experience.

Jeep Safari

Jeep safaris are a fun way to explore the countryside around Alexandroupolis. Visitors can expect to see stunning vistas, hidden waterfalls, and exciting off-road trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Alexandroupolis

Must-Try Dishes

Soutzoukakia

Soutzoukakia are spicy meatballs in tomato sauce, flavored with cumin and garlic. This traditional dish is typically served with rice or French fries.

Marathopita

Marathopita is a savory pie filled with fennel, herbs, and cheese. This dish is perfect as a snack or a light lunch.

Baklava

Baklava is a sweet pastry made of layers of filo pastry, nuts, and honey. This dessert is a classic in Greece and is a must-try when visiting Alexandroupolis.

Local Beverages

Ouzo

Ouzo is a traditional alcoholic drink of Greece, made from aniseed and grapes. The drink is usually served chilled with meze, small plates of appetizers.

Tsipouro

Tsipouro is a potent grape-based spirit, typically served as a digestif after a meal.

Where to Stay in Alexandroupolis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

Alexander Beach Hotel

The Alexander Beach Hotel is a luxurious 5-star hotel located on Makri Beach. The hotel has a private beach, an outdoor pool, and offers many activities, such as tennis and volleyball.

Grand Hotel Egnatia

The Grand Hotel Egnatia is a 4-star hotel located in the heart of Alexandroupolis. The hotel has comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop bar with panoramic views of the city.

Hostels

Alexander Hostel

The Alexander Hostel is a friendly and affordable hostel, located in the city center. The hostel has dormitory rooms, private rooms, and a communal kitchen and lounge area.

Guesthouses

Eleftheria’s Traditional Guesthouse

Eleftheria’s Traditional Guesthouse is a charming accommodation option, located in the village of Feres. The guesthouse has traditional architecture, modern amenities, and friendly hosts who offer local tips on activities and tours in the area.

What to Buy in Alexandroupolis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olive Oil

Olive oil is a staple of Greek cuisine, and the olive groves around Alexandroupolis produce some of the finest olive oils in Greece. Visitors can buy bottles of cold-pressed olive oil to take home as a souvenir or gift.

Honey

Honey is another typical product of Greece, and the region around Alexandroupolis produces delicious wildflower honey. Visitors can find jars of honey in local markets and specialty shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Alexandroupolis

Greeks are known for their hospitality and love for food, and the people of Alexandroupolis are no exception. Visitors can expect warm welcomes, invitations to share a meal or a drink, and a genuine interest in learning about other cultures.

Best Time to Visit Alexandroupolis

The best time to visit Alexandroupolis is during the spring and early summer (April-June) or autumn (September-November), when the weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists. The summertime (July-August) can be hot and crowded, but it’s an excellent season for beach lovers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alexandroupolis

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Wear comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring
  • Take part in outdoor activities, such as sea kayaking or jeep safaris

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas
  • Ignore local dress codes at religious sites
  • Be disrespectful to elders or authorities
  • Overstay your visa or enter the country illegally

Safety and Health

Safety in Alexandroupolis

Alexandroupolis is a safe city, with low crime rates. However, like in any destination, visitors should take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Alexandroupolis

Alexandroupolis has good medical facilities and hospitals, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid common health issues such as sunburn and dehydration. It’s recommended to carry sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water when exploring natural areas.

Getting to Alexandroupolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alexandroupolis

Alexandroupolis International Airport “Democritus” is the nearest airport, located approximately 6 km away from the city center. The airport serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Alexandroupolis

The bus station of Alexandroupolis is located in the city center and provides regular bus services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Alexandroupolis by train or by car. The city is connected by rail to several destinations in Greece, while it’s linked by highway with the major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Alexandroupolis

The easiest way to get around Alexandroupolis is by walking or by using public transportation. The city has a reliable and affordable bus service, which covers most of the main tourist attractions and neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Alexandroupolis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thassos Island

Thassos Island is a beautiful island located in the northern Aegean Sea, known for its sandy beaches, pine forests, and traditional villages. Visitors can reach Thassos Island by ferry from Alexandroupolis, and a day trip or a weekend getaway is highly recommended.

Kavala

Kavala is a charming city located 160 km away from Alexandroupolis. It has a rich history and several landmarks worth visiting, such as the Castle of Kavala and the Kamares aqueduct.

Conclusion

Alexandroupolis is a hidden gem in Northern Greece, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and history. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or delicious food and drink, Alexandroupolis has something to offer for everyone. Let this comprehensive guide inspire you to plan your next trip to Alexandroupolis and explore all the wonders it has to offer.
