Introduction to Western Thrace

Western Thrace is a lesser-known region located in the northern part of Greece. Sandwiched between Bulgaria and Turkey, this area is home to a diverse range of cultures and ethnicities that have shaped its rich history and traditions. From the Ottoman legacy and Byzantine influences to its Roman and ancient Greek roots, Western Thrace is a treasure trove of historical, archeological, and cultural wonders.

Beyond its rich heritage, Western Thrace is also famous for its breathtaking natural beauty. Nestled between two mountain ranges and washed by the Aegean Sea, the region boasts scenic drives, charming towns, peaceful beaches, and majestic mountain landscapes that attract nature lovers, adventurers, and those in search of authentic Greek hospitality.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Western Thrace, including:

List of Places to Visit in Western Thrace

Western Thrace is divided into three regional units: Xanthi, Rodopi, and Evros. In each of these regions, there are plenty of sights and attractions worth exploring. Here is a list of the most popular and culturally significant places you can visit in Western Thrace:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Xanthi Old Town: This picturesque town is renowned for its well-preserved Ottoman architecture, cobbled streets, and traditional mansions. Among the most notable attractions here are the Folklore Museum, the Archaeological Museum, and the Ethnological Museum. You can also visit the restored Ottoman Tobacco Market and the 19th-century Kavala aqueduct.

  • Castle of Avdera: Located in the Rodopi region, this impressive castle was built in the 12th century by the Byzantine Emperor Alexios III. Today, it houses a small museum showcasing artifacts from the ancient city of Avdera, which was once an important commercial and cultural center.

  • Didymoteicho: This bustling town has a rich history dating back to ancient times. Here you can visit the Byzantine Castle, the Ottoman Clock Tower, and the Folk Museum. You should also check out the famous tobacco auction house, where farmers come to sell their crop.

Natural Attractions of Western Thrace: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Evia Island: This large island is located just off the coast of Northern Greece and is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. You can explore its dense forests and rivers, try your hand at fishing, or relax on one of its pristine beaches.

  • Dadia Forest Reserve: This stunning natural reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including rare and endangered bird species such as the griffon vulture. Here you can go on a guided jeep safari or hiking tour to explore the lush landscape and see the wildlife up close and personal.

  • Istranca Mountains: These mountains separate Western Thrace from Turkey and offer stunning views of both countries. Hiking trails wind through the rugged terrain, and you can stop at traditional mountain villages to sample local cuisine and hospitality.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Thrace

Western Thrace is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with numerous trails and paths winding through its mountains, forests, and valleys. Some popular routes include:

  • E6 European Long Distance Path: This 4,000-kilometer path starts in Finland and ends in Turkey, passing through Western Thrace along the way. It offers a chance to explore the region’s natural and cultural wonders, including the Evros Delta National Park, the Istranca Mountains, and the Nestos River.

  • Vistonida Lake: This beautiful lake is located near the town of Xanthi and offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and biking. A popular route is to cycle around the lake, which takes about three hours and offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Dadia Forest Reserve: As mentioned previously, Dadia Forest Reserve offers guided hikes and jeep safaris through its stunning terrain. You can choose from a range of routes based on your fitness level and interests.

Best Things to Do in Western Thrace: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Western Thrace is renowned for its high-quality wine, particularly the Roditis grape variety. You can go on a guided wine-tasting tour of the region, which will take you to local vineyards, wineries, and cellars to sample the best vintages and learn about the winemaking process.

  • Therapeutic Baths: Western Thrace has several natural hot springs that are believed to have therapeutic properties. You can visit the Eftapir Thermal Springs to soak in the warm water and enjoy the healing benefits.

  • Ottoman Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Ottoman dishes like pilaf, moussaka, and kleftiko with a local chef. You’ll get to sample your creations at the end of the class and take home the recipes to recreate the dishes at home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Western Thrace

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Western Thrace. Of course, you can adjust this based on your interests and preferences:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Xanthi and explore the Old Town.

  • Day 2: Visit the Ancient City of Avdera and explore the surrounding area.

  • Day 3: Go on a hiking tour in Dadia Forest Reserve.

  • Day 4: Visit the town of Didymoteicho and its cultural attractions.

  • Day 5: Explore the beaches and hot springs in the Evros Delta National Park.

  • Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and visit local vineyards and wineries.

  • Day 7: Relax on the beaches of Evia Island or go on a guided fishing tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Western Thrace

No trip to Greece is complete without experiencing its rich culinary culture, and Western Thrace has its own distinctive flavors and tastes that reflect the region’s diverse history and traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kavourmas: This dish is made from beef, pork or lamb, which is cooked in its own fat along with spices and herbs. It’s typically served with bread, potatoes, or rice.

  • Fırın: A dish of potatoes, meat, and vegetables cooked in a clay pot.

  • Ekmek kadayıfı: A dessert made from shredded phyllo dough, sugar syrup, and walnuts or pistachios.

Local Beverages

  • Rakı: An anise-flavored alcoholic drink that is popular throughout the Balkans and the Middle East.

  • Ouzo: A Greek version of the above drink, which is typically served with mezze and seafood.

  • Roditis Wine: A grape variety that is unique to Western Thrace and produces white wines that are crisp, refreshing, and acidic.

Where to Stay in Western Thrace: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Western Thrace has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some popular choices:

  • Hotel Faros: Located in Xanthi Old Town, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the city.

  • Yannis Resort: A luxurious resort located near the Thracian Sea, offering a range of amenities, including a spa, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.

  • Xanthi Hotel: Situated in the heart of Xanthi, this hotel is the perfect base for exploring the town’s cultural attractions.

What to Buy in Western Thrace: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products you should consider buying during your visit to Western Thrace:

  • Handmade Carpets and Rugs: Western Thrace has a rich tradition of weaving, and you can browse through a range of carpets and rugs in local shops and markets.

  • Tobacco: Western Thrace is famous for its high-quality tobacco, so if you’re a smoker, you might consider buying some local tobacco products to bring home.

  • Olive Oil: Greece is famous for its olive oil, and Western Thrace is no exception. Look for local olive oil brands in shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Western Thrace

Western Thrace is home to a diverse range of ethnic groups, including Turks, Pomaks, and Roma. Each of these groups has its own traditions and customs, which have been shaped by the region’s history and geography. Some general observations about the culture and customs of Western Thrace include:

  • Hospitality: Western Thrace is known for its warm and welcoming hospitality, and visitors are often treated like family.

  • Religion: Islam is the dominant religion in Western Thrace, and you’ll see many mosques and minarets throughout the region.

  • Festivals: Western Thrace has a range of festivals throughout the year that showcase its rich cultural heritage. Some notable ones include the Pomak Wedding Festival, the Ottoman Rose Festival, and the Kavala Cultural Festival.

Best Time to Visit Western Thrace

The best time to visit Western Thrace is during the spring (April - June) or autumn (September - November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. If you’re looking for a beach holiday, the summer months (July - August) are the best, although it can get quite hot and crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western Thrace

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Western Thrace:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and mosques.

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink too much alcohol in public places.

  • Ignore local traffic laws and regulations.

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Western Thrace is a relatively safe region, and incidents of crime and violence are rare. However, as with any destination, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips:

Safety in Western Thrace

  • Be aware of pickpockets in busy tourist areas.

  • Avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuables with you.

  • Follow the advice of your tour operator or guide when exploring remote or rural areas.

Health in Western Thrace

  • Western Thrace has good healthcare facilities, but it’s still recommended to have travel insurance before your trip.

  • Mosquitoes can be a problem in rural areas, especially during the summer. Bring mosquito repellent and wear long sleeves and pants in the evening to reduce the risk of insect bites.

Getting to Western Thrace: Transportation Options and Logistics

Western Thrace is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here are some options:

Airports Near Western Thrace

The nearest airports to Western Thrace are:

  • Kavala International Airport: Serves the town of Kavala and the surrounding area.

  • Alexandroupolis International Airport: Located in the northeastern part of the region, near the town of Alexandroupolis.

Bus Stations Near Western Thrace

  • Xanthi Bus Station: Located in the center of Xanthi town, this station serves buses to Thessaloniki and other cities in Greece.

  • Alexandroupolis Bus Station: Serves buses to and from Athens and other regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in the town of Alexandroupolis, and trains run to Thessaloniki and Athens.

  • Car rental: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car at the airports or in the major towns.

How to Get Around in Western Thrace

To explore the region, you can use public transport or rent a car. Buses and taxis are available in the major towns, and there are also organized tours and excursions to popular sights and attractions. Hiring a car is a good option if you want to explore off-the-beaten-track places and enjoy the freedom of driving.

Where to Go Next from Western Thrace: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Western Thrace is well-connected to other destinations in Greece and neighboring countries. Here are some suggestions for where to go next:

  • Kavala: A picturesque town located in the northeastern part of Greece, known for its charming old town, ancient monuments, and scenic beaches.

  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece, known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Istanbul: If you have time and are feeling adventurous, you can cross the border into Turkey and visit the cosmopolitan city of Istanbul.

Conclusion

Western Thrace is a region that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or just relaxing on the beach, you’ll find plenty to do and see here. With its rich traditions, warm hospitality, and stunning natural landscape, Western Thrace is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be discovered.
