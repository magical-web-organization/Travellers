Preveza is a picturesque town in Epirus, located on the Ionian coast of Northern Greece. With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and welcoming atmosphere, Preveza is a must-visit destination for travelers exploring the region. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, top activities to enjoy, local food and drink specialties, accommodations, and more. So, let’s dive in!

Introduction to Preveza

Preveza is a charming town located in Northern Greece, with a population of roughly 20,000 residents. Its history dates back to ancient times, when it served as an important harbor city for the ancient Greeks and Romans. Today, Preveza boasts a charming Old Town with traditional buildings, narrow streets, and small plazas. The town also features several museums, parks, and beaches, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both cultural and natural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Preveza

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Preveza Archaeological Museum: This museum showcases artifacts from the region’s ancient past, including statues, pottery, and jewelry.
  • Nicopolis Museum: This museum is dedicated to the Roman city of Nicopolis, located just outside of Preveza.
  • Venetian Castle: Located in the heart of the town, the Venetian Castle is a must-visit attraction with stunning views of the city.
  • Kassopi: This ancient site is located just outside of Preveza and features ancient ruins of a city settlement.

Natural Attractions of Preveza: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Acheron River: A popular destination for rafting and kayaking enthusiasts, the Acheron River is also renowned for its picturesque scenery.
  • Monolithi Beach: This beautiful beach is located just outside of Preveza and is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Zalogo: This historical place is known for its breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding area and is a popular destination for hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Preveza

  • Acherontas Trail: This scenic trail follows the Acheron River and is perfect for nature lovers and hikers.
  • Katara Pass: This challenging route offers stunning views of the Ionian Sea and is ideal for experienced hikers and mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Preveza: Activities and Tours

  • Nicopolis Archaeological Park Tour: Explore the ancient Roman city of Nicopolis with a guided tour.
  • Acherontas River Rafting Tour: Experience the thrill of rafting down the Acherontas River with a professional guide.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Greek dishes with a local chef.

Food and Drink Scene of Preveza

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat served on a skewer, usually with vegetables and pita bread.
  • Tzatziki: A traditional Greek sauce made with yogurt, cucumber, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Pastitsio: A baked pasta dish made with ground meat and béchamel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: A strong alcoholic drink made from grape skins and seeds.
  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek liquor made with anise.

Where to Stay in Preveza: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nafs Hotel: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Preveza and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Captain’s House: This charming guesthouse is located in the Old Town and features traditional-style rooms and a beautiful courtyard.
  • Hotel Preveza City: This central hotel offers clean and spacious rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Preveza: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: Preveza is known for its high-quality olive oil, which makes for a delicious and healthy souvenir to bring back home.
  • Wine: Epirus is home to some of Greece’s best wine regions, and Preveza is no exception.
  • Traditional crafts: Preveza features several artisan shops selling handmade pottery, textiles, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Preveza

  • The people of Preveza are known for their hospitality and warm welcome to visitors.
  • Greek is the official language, but many locals speak English as well.
  • Tipping is not expected in Greece, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small amount is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Preveza

The best time to visit Preveza is during the summer months of June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the shoulder seasons of spring and fall offer milder weather and fewer crowds, allowing for a more peaceful and authentic travel experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Preveza

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the ancient ruins and historical sites.
  • Pack comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs without permission at religious sites or private properties.
  • Wear revealing clothing at religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Preveza

Preveza is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always recommended to take normal precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended, avoiding deserted areas at night, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Preveza

Preveza has a sufficient healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located in the town. It’s recommended to have travel insurance and bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Preveza: Transportation Options and Logistics

Preveza is easily accessible by air, road, and sea.

Airports Near Preveza

The closest airport to Preveza is Aktion National Airport, which is located about 5 km from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Preveza

Preveza is connected to several major cities in Greece, including Athens and Thessaloniki, via intercity bus services.

Other Options

You can also reach Preveza by ferry from nearby islands, such as Corfu and Paxi.

How to Get Around in Preveza

The best way to explore Preveza is on foot, as most attractions are located within the town center. However, taxis and public transportation options are also available.

Where to Go Next from Preveza: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Parga: A picturesque seaside town with stunning beaches and Venetian architecture.
  • Zagorochoria: An area of 46 traditional villages located in the heart of Epirus, famous for its stunning natural scenery and hiking trails.
  • Lefkada: A beautiful island with crystal-clear waters and stunning beaches.

Conclusion

Preveza is a hidden gem in Northern Greece, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, hiking scenic trails, or simply relaxing on the beach, Preveza has something to offer every traveler. Start planning your next adventure today!
