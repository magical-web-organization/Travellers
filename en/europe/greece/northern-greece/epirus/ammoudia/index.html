Introduction to Ammoudia

Ammoudia is a beautiful coastal town located in the northwestern region of Greece, known as Epirus. Ammoudia is a hidden gem in Greece, offering breathtaking scenery, crystal-clear waters, and untouched landscapes. The village is renowned for its long and wide beach of golden sand and its excellent water sports facilities. With its unique disposition on the banks of the Acheron River, Ammoudia is the ideal base for exploring the lush and mountainous Epirus region.

List of Places to Visit in Ammoudia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Folklore Museum

The Folklore Museum is dedicated to preserving the cultural heritage of the town. It displays historical objects, agricultural tools, traditional clothes, musical instruments, and household items, all of which provide insights into the local way of life.

The Ancient Necromancy Oracle of Acheron

Acheron is renowned for its oracle, as the ancient Greeks believed that the spirits of the dead could come from the underworld and answer people’s questions. The archaeological site of the Necromancy Oracle of Acheron is situated 11 km from Ammoudia and consists of a network of tunnels and underground rooms that make up a ritual sanctuary.

Natural Attractions of Ammoudia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Acheron River

The Acheron River, which flows into the Ionian Sea, features stunning landscapes of verdant vegetation and rich wildlife. Acheron is famous for its cool and crystal-clear waters, which provide numerous opportunities for swimming, paddling, and rafting. Kayaks, canoes, and pedal boats are available for rent throughout the town.

Hiking Trails

The area around Ammoudia offers a multitude of hiking trails of varying difficulty, leading through scenic gorges, stunning coastline, and verdant valleys. These routes provide a great way to experience the natural beauty of the region, discover hidden waterfalls or quaint traditional villages, and breathe in the fresh mountain air.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ammoudia

Hiking is undoubtedly the best way to explore the beautiful landscapes of Ammoudia. The most popular route is the 9 km trail from Ammoudia to the Necromancy Oracle of Acheron. The route follows the banks of the river for the most part and passes through beautiful olive groves and rocks. The hike will take about 2 – 3 hours, and you can take a refreshing dip in the Acheron River at the midpoint of the trail. If you prefer biking, you can rent a bike from one of the local rental shops in town, which is a great way to explore Ammoudia’s scenic beauty.

Best Things to Do in Ammoudia: Activities and Tours

Water Sports

Ammoudia is well known for its water sports facilities, including windsurfing, kite-surfing, and paddle-boarding. You can rent equipment from one of the local surf shops and embark on an exciting adventure on the Ionian Sea.

Boat Tours

Boat tours are an excellent way to explore the beautiful Greek coastline and geological formations surrounding Ammoudia. The tour will take you on a journey across the Ionian Sea, showcasing stunning landscapes and natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Ammoudia

Must-Try Dishes

Souvlaki Gyro and Souvlaki Sti Pita

Souvlaki is a popular Greek street food made with char-grilled skewered meat, normally pork, beef, or chicken. Souvlaki gyros and souvlaki sti pita are the most favorite street food for locals and visitors, often referred to as gyros and pita. It is usually served with fresh tzatziki, a yogurt-based sauce.

Tsipouro

Tsipouro is a traditional Greek alcoholic beverage that is typically enjoyed as an aperitif or after-dinner drink. It is often made from grapes, and the flavor and alcohol content can vary, depending on the method and region in which it is produced.

Local Beverages

Frappe

Frappe is a refreshing and frothy iced coffee beverage that is popular in Greece during the summer months. Made with black coffee, sugar, and ice cubes, it is frothed together in a shaker and served in a tall glass with a straw.

Ouzo

Ouzo is a traditional Greek drink, similar to tsipouro, often served as an aperitif or after-dinner drink. It is a clear, anise-flavored liquor that turns cloudy when mixed with water or ice.

Where to Stay in Ammoudia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ammoudia has a range of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. Whether you want to stay in a luxurious 5-star hotel or a rustic guesthouse, there are plenty of options to choose from.

Hotel Ammos Bay

Hotel Ammos Bay is a popular choice for travelers, situated next to the beautiful beach of Ammoudia. The hotel features luxurious rooms and suites, a swimming pool, a restaurant, and bars.

Guesthouse Ammoudia

The Guesthouse Ammoudia is a charming and rustic boutique hotel that is perfect for couples and budget travelers. The hotel features comfortable rooms, beautiful gardens, and a charming courtyard where you can enjoy small meals and drinks.

What to Buy in Ammoudia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olive Oil and Olives

Epirus is known for its excellent-quality olive oil, so it’s no surprise that Ammoudia also produces some of the best. You can visit local oil mills and taste the different varieties of olive oil and find the perfect bottle to bring home. Olives, especially marinated ones, are also famous local produce that makes an excellent souvenir.

Handmade Jewelry

There are many local boutiques in Ammoudia that sell handmade jewelry made by local artisans. You can find unique and beautiful pieces of jewelry that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ammoudia

Ammoudia is a small and authentic Greek village, where locals are respectful of their traditions and hospitality is extended to visitors. People in Ammoudia are regular church-goers, so tourists should keep in mind that churches and religious symbols should be treated with respect.

Best Time to Visit Ammoudia

The best time to visit Ammoudia is from May to September when the weather is warm and perfect for swimming in the Ionian Sea. The peak season is in July and August, so expect larger crowds and higher prices. The shoulder seasons of May-June and September-October are great times to visit too since the weather is excellent, and room rates are relatively lower.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ammoudia

Ammoudia is generally a safe place for travelers. However, like any other tourist destination, there are some petty theft and pickpocketing incidents to watch out for. It is advisable to keep your valuables safe and secure, especially in crowded markets and bus stations.

Health in Ammoudia

Make sure to carry insect repellent and sunscreen when you visit Ammoudia, as some areas may have mosquitoes and sunburn can be a real risk. The city has a medical center in case of emergencies.

Getting to Ammoudia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ammoudia

The nearest major airport to Ammoudia is Corfu International Airport, which is served by many international airlines. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to Ammoudia, which takes around 2 hours.

Bus Stations Near Ammoudia

There are regular bus services from Athens and Thessaloniki to the nearby towns of Preveza and Igoumenitsa. From there, you can take a taxi or hire a car to Ammoudia, which will take you around 30 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to drive can rent cars from the nearby towns of Preveza or Igoumenitsa or arrive in their cars on the numerous roads that lead to Ammoudia.

How to Get Around in Ammoudia

Ammoudia is small enough to explore on foot, but there are also other transport options like taxis, rental cars, and bikes. The town also has a local bus service that runs to nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Ammoudia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Parga

Parga is a picturesque, Venetian-style town located in the region of Epirus. The town has an impressive Venetian fortress, narrow streets with colorful houses, and beautiful beaches. Parga can be reached from Ammoudia in just an hour’s drive.

Zagorohoria

Zagorohoria is a region of 46 traditional villages built on the slopes of Mount Pindos, characterized by its stone-built houses that blend perfectly into the natural environment. The region is renowned for its beautiful stone bridges, gorges, and hiking trails. The distance from Ammoudia to Zagorohoria is approximately a three-hour drive.

Conclusion

Ammoudia is a hidden gem in Greece, offering visitors a unique and authentic Greek experience. The village combines beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, stunning scenery, and traditional Greek culture, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Greece. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to explore the best of Ammoudia, Epirus, Greece!
