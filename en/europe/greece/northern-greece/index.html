Introduction to Northern Greece

Northern Greece, also known as Macedonia and Thrace, is located in the northern part of Greece and is bordered by Albania, North Macedonia, Bulgaria and Turkey. It is a region that is steeped in history, with ancient ruins and landmarks that offer a glimpse into Greece’s glorious past. The region is also known for its natural beauty, with pristine beaches, soaring mountains, and picturesque villages scattered throughout the countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Greece

Northern Greece has a rich and diverse culture, with influences from the Balkans, the Ottoman Empire, and Ancient Greece. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to experience:

  • Kefi dancing
  • Bouzouki music
  • Traditional handcrafted costumes
  • Traditional festivals, such as Carnival in Patras and the Macedonian Festival of Floralia.

Best Time to Visit Northern Greece

The best time to visit Northern Greece depends on what you want to do and see. If you’re looking for outdoor activities and adventure, the best time to visit is in the spring and summer months (April to September). If you want to explore the cultural attractions and festivals in the region, the best time to visit is during the winter months (October to March).

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Greece

Here are some of the things to do and avoid when visiting Northern Greece:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Learn a few words of Greek
  • Explore the lesser-known villages and towns
  • Take part in the local festivals and celebrations

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Be loud and boisterous in public places
  • Engage in excessive drinking and partying
  • Leave your litter on the beaches or in the national parks
