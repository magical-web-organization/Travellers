Your Comprehensive Travel Guide to Kavala, Greece

If you’re looking for a destination that has everything – history, culture, natural beauty, and amazing food – then Kavala is definitely the place for you. This beautiful city is located in the northern part of Greece, in the region of Macedonia, and is filled with plenty of things to see and do.

Introduction to Kavala

Kavala is a historic city filled with charm and character. Its picturesque harbor is home to a number of bars and restaurants where you can indulge in delicious cuisine while enjoying the beautiful views. The city also boasts a number of interesting museums and historical sites, as well as plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, making it a perfect destination for any type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Kavala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kavala is filled with historical and cultural sites that are definitely worth a visit. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Castle of Kavala: A historic fortress that dates back to the Byzantine era and offers breathtaking views of the city and the harbor.
  • The Imaret: A beautifully restored Ottoman building that currently operates as a luxurious hotel. It is a great place to take a stroll and admire the architecture.
  • The Archeological Museum of Kavala: A fascinating museum that houses artifacts from the ancient city of Philippi, which is located nearby.
  • The Municipal Museum: A museum that showcases the history and culture of the city and the region, featuring exhibitions dedicated to ancient technology, traditional costumes, and much more.

Natural Attractions of Kavala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kavala is surrounded by natural beauty, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Nestos Delta: A beautiful wetland area that is home to many species of birds, making it a perfect destination for birdwatching.
  • Kerdylia: A stunning mountainous area that is perfect for hiking. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Falakro Ski Resort: A ski resort located in the nearby mountains. It is a great destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kavala

Kavala is a great destination for hikers and bikers who want to explore its natural beauty. Some of the best trails include:

  • The Egnatia Trail: A trail that follows the ancient Roman road of Egnatia, passing through stunning natural landscapes and historic villages.
  • The Trail of the Philippi Battlefield: A trail that takes you through the site of the ancient Battle of Philippi, where the armies of Julius Caesar and Mark Antony defeated the forces of Brutus and Cassius.
  • The Kavala City Trail: A trail that takes you through the city’s historic neighborhoods, showcasing its beautiful architecture and rich history.

Best Things to Do in Kavala: Activities and Tours

Aside from its natural and cultural attractions, Kavala has plenty of other things to offer. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Boat tours of the harbor: A great way to see the city from a different perspective is by taking a boat tour of the harbor.
  • Wine tasting tours: Kavala is known for its delicious wines, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries to visit.
  • Culinary tours: Kavala’s food scene is amazing, and taking a culinary tour is a great way to explore its cuisine and learn about local specialties.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kavala

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kavala, here’s a great itinerary to follow:

Day 1-3: Explore the city’s cultural attractions, such as the Castle of Kavala, Municipal Museum, and Archeological Museum of Kavala. Take a boat tour of the harbor and enjoy some of the city’s amazing cuisine.

Day 4-5: Go on a hiking or biking adventure to explore the natural beauty of the region. Visit Nestos Delta or Kerdylia for breathtaking views.

Day 6-7: Visit nearby destinations such as the ancient city of Philippi, Drama, and Xanthi to get a taste of the wider region.

Food and Drink Scene of Kavala

Kavala is known for its amazing cuisine and delicious wines. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bougatsa: A pastry filled with cheese or custard that is a popular breakfast dish.
  • Kavala Shrimp: This local specialty is served in various ways and is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Soutzoukakia: Meatballs made with spices, garlic, and breadcrumbs. They are usually served with tomato sauce and rice.
  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of phyllo dough, nuts, and honey.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek liquor that is usually served as an aperitif before a meal.
  • Tsipouro: A strong, grape brandy that is commonly consumed after a meal.
  • Wine: Kavala is known for its delicious wines, including the Xinomavro and Roditis varietals.

Where to Stay in Kavala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kavala offers plenty of options when it comes to accommodation. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Imaret Hotel: This beautiful Ottoman building has been tastefully restored and operates as a luxurious hotel.
  • Lucy Hotel: A contemporary hotel located in the heart of the city, offering modern amenities and beautiful views.
  • Archontiko Apartments: A set of charming apartments located in a historic building, offering plenty of character and charm.

What to Buy in Kavala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kavala has plenty of great souvenirs and local products that you can bring home. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Olive oil: Kavala is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is perfect for cooking.
  • Honey: The region is home to many beekeepers who produce delicious honey.
  • Kavala Shrimp: You can buy these locally caught shrimp in various forms, including fresh or frozen.
  • Handicrafts: The region is known for its traditional handicrafts, such as pottery, woven textiles, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Kavala

Kavala has a rich cultural heritage and many interesting customs. Residents are proud of their history, and you can see evidence of this in the many exhibitions and museums. The city also has a vibrant nightlife and is known for its music and dance traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kavala

The best time to visit Kavala is between April and October, when the weather is warm and pleasant. Summers can be hot, but the nearby sea provides some relief. Winter months can be cold and wet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kavala

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Kavala shrimp and Bougatsa.
  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions of the city and the region.
  • Take a boat tour of the harbor for beautiful views of the city.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the safety rules while hiking or biking in the mountains.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kavala

Kavala is a safe destination, but you should still take standard precautions such as keeping your valuables safe and be aware of pickpockets and theft. When hiking or biking, always stick to established trails and carry a map and plenty of water.

Health in Kavala

Kavala has good healthcare facilities, but it is always recommended to have travel insurance and check with your doctor before traveling. Remember to carry mosquito repellent as the region has mosquitoes during the summer months.

Getting to Kavala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kavala is easily accessible by air, road, and sea. Here are some of the main transportation options:

Airports Near Kavala

The nearest airport to Kavala is “Alexander the Great” International Airport in Thessaloniki, which is located about 160 km away. From there, you can take a bus, train, or taxi to Kavala.

Bus Stations Near Kavala

Kavala has a central bus station that connects it to Thessaloniki, Athens, and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

There are also ferry services that operate from nearby islands, such as Thassos and Samothrace, and you can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around.

How to Get Around in Kavala

Kavala is a fairly small city and is easily walkable. However, if you want to explore the region, you will need a car, bike, or scooter to get around.

Where to Go Next from Kavala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Greece, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions you can take. Some of the top places to visit include:

  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece is a beautiful destination filled with history and culture.
  • Philippi: An ancient city located about 20 km from Kavala that is famous for its ruins and historical importance.
  • Xanthi: A beautiful city that is famous for its Ottoman-era architecture and beautiful natural surroundings.

Conclusion

Kavala is a hidden gem that should definitely be on your list of places to visit in Greece. With its beautiful natural scenery, rich history and culture, amazing food, and friendly locals, it is a destination that will leave you with unforgettable memories.
