Introduction to Amphipolis

Amphipolis, located in Northern Greece, is a city filled with extensive history and natural beauty. Situated on the banks of the Strymon River and surrounded by lush green mountains, Amphipolis has been inhabited since prehistoric times. The city was once an important cultural and political center during the Hellenistic period and has a rich history, including the conquest by the Romans and the establishment of Christianity during the Byzantine era. Today, Amphipolis is a popular tourist destination, known for its cultural and natural attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amphipolis

Day 1: Visit the Amphipolis Archaeological Site and Museum and see the famous Lion of Amphipolis.

Day 2: Go hiking or biking on the Mount Pangaio trail and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Day 3: Explore the ancient ruins of the Acropolis of Amphipolis.

Day 4: Take a boat ride to Lake Volvi and enjoy swimming, fishing or take a picnic with you.

Day 5: Visit the Kasta Tomb and learn about its history.

Day 6: Go bird watching in the Strymon River delta and experience the unique natural ecosystem of the area.

Day 7: Take a cultural tour of the city and discover the local traditions, customs, and cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Amphipolis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bougatsa: a pastry filled with custard, cheese or minced meat.
  • Mpougatsa: a custard-filled pastry.
  • Souvlaki: grilled meat often served in a pita with vegetables and tzatziki sauce.
  • Koulouri: a circular bread ring sprinkled with sesame seeds.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: a traditional alcoholic drink made from grape pomace.
  • Ouzo: an anise-flavored drink with a high alcohol content.

Local Culture and Customs of Amphipolis

Amphipolis has a rich cultural heritage with a strong focus on ancient Greek history. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and you will find a warm atmosphere in the city. Visitors should respect local customs, including dressing modestly when visiting churches and religious sites and not drinking alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit Amphipolis

The best time to visit Amphipolis is during the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and mild. Summers (July to August) can be hot and crowded, while winters (December to February) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amphipolis

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take a cultural tour of the city.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches and religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amphipolis

Amphipolis is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Amphipolis

The tap water in Amphipolis is safe to drink, and there are medical facilities available if needed.

Getting to Amphipolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amphipolis

The nearest airport to Amphipolis is Thessaloniki International Airport (SKG), which is approximately 70 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach Amphipolis.

Bus Stations Near Amphipolis

There are regular bus services from Thessaloniki and Serres to Amphipolis. The bus station is located in the city center, making it easily accessible for visitors.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Amphipolis by car or hire a private driver to take them there.

How to Get Around in Amphipolis

Getting around Amphipolis is easy, with local buses, taxis, and rental cars available for visitors.
