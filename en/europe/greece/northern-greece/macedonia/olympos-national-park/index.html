Introduction to Olympos National Park

Nestled in Northern Greece, Olympos National Park is a stunning natural wonder that encompasses an array of landscapes and experiences for visitors. Home to soaring peaks, alpine lakes, verdant forests, and diverse wildlife, this park is renowned for its natural beauty, perfect for avid hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts.

While exploring the park, you can also learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage by visiting historic landmarks and museums or just enjoy the warm hospitality of the local communities.

List of Places to Visit in Olympos National Park

The park’s wide range of attractions makes it easy to craft an itinerary that suits your interests. From outdoor adventures to cultural activities, here is a list of top places to visit in Olympos National Park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Royal Tombs of Aigai: Located in Vergina, the museum houses the tomb and rich ancient treasures that belonged to the Macedonian king Philip II, father of Alexander the Great.

  • Church of the Holy Apostles: This 14th-century Byzantine church features breathtaking frescoes and a fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Olympos National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Olympus: The highest peak in Greece is a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts, with several routes showcasing the astonishing nature of the park.

  • Enipeas River: Visitors can raft or hike along this picturesque river, winding through the deep and verdant canyon.

  • Gorge of Orlias: The 8-kilometer long gorge is a favorite among adventurers, with breathtaking landscapes and challenging routes to conquer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Olympos National Park

With the diverse landscaped, Olympos National Park is a hiker’s paradise, offering hiking trails of various difficulties. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Mount Olympus Trail: This scenic trail takes hikers along the ancient path of the gods with many stunning views and landscapes.

  • Prionia to Litochoro Trail: Catapult into the breathtaking landscapes with rivers, forests, and snow-capped mountains on this challenging alpine trail.

  • Prionia Trail: A moderate route along a rushing river leading to spectacular views of the peaks towering above.

Best Things to Do in Olympos National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking the trails, there are several other activities and tours visitors can enjoy in Olympos National Park. Some of the top things you shouldn’t miss include:

  • Horse Riding: Take a scenic horse ride in the foothills of Mount Olympus, an extraordinary way to experience the natural beauty of the park.

  • Bird Watching: With over 200 bird species, Olympos National Park is an excellent destination for bird enthusiasts, offering rare sightings such as the Golden Eagle.

  • Greek Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Greek dishes and indulge in mouthwatering Greek cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Olympos National Park

A week-long trip to Olympos National Park can offer ample time to explore its stunning beauty and rich culture. Here’s a sample itinerary you can consider:

  • Day 1: Explore the park’s diverse flora and fauna, enjoying a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • Day 2: Discover the historic landmarks and museums of the region, such as the Royal Tombs of Aigai and the Church of the Holy Apostles.

  • Day 3 - 4: Hit the trails and conquer the Gorge of Orlias, Prionia Trail, and Mount Olympus Trail, among others.

  • Day 5: Enjoy a scenic drive through the parks mountains, and have a picnic by the Enipeas River.

  • Day 6 - 7: learn Greek cooking skills and attend cultural events showcasing local traditions and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Olympos National Park

The food scene in Olympos National Park is as diverse as its landscapes, with fresh ingredients and traditional recipes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A hearty casserole combining layered potatoes, eggplants, minced meat, and bechamel sauce, oven-baked to perfection.

  • Souvlaki: Popular street food consisting of meat skewers, grilled to perfection over charcoal, served with a variety of garnishes.

Local Beverages

  • Retsina: A white or rose wine containing the resin of pine trees, producing unexpectedly delicious flavors.

  • Ouzo: A Greek spirit typically served with a side of water or ice and often sipped as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Olympos National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation options in Olympos National Park are diverse, remember to pack your sleeping bag if you plan to wild camp in the park. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Kriopigi Beach Hotel: Located on a green hill, overlooking the sea; this luxurious hotel features rooms and suites decorated with traditional Greek motifs.

  • Pelion Homes: A stunning complex of traditional houses dappled around the mountains and forests of Pelion, offering top facilities and views.

  • Archontiko Naoumidi: This boutique guesthouse captures the essence of traditional Greek hospitality in its renovated 19th-century mansion.

What to Buy in Olympos National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olympos National Park offers an array of local products and souvenirs that make for excellent gifts and memories. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Olive oil: Sample and stock up on the region’s high-quality olive oil.

  • Honey: Often referred to as nature’s gold, you can purchase an array of authentic honey at the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Olympos National Park

The region’s culture is vibrant and rooted in ancient traditions, with food, music, and dance at the forefront. The area also boasts beech tree forests that feature ancient beehives; beekeeping is an ancient tradition here, and still, today, locals trade in honey and honeycomb.

Best Time to Visit Olympos National Park

The best time to visit Olympos National Park is during the shoulder seasons, April to May or September to October. During these months the weather is comfortable, and the crowds are light, making it an ideal time to explore the park.

Do’s and Don’ts of Olympos National Park

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to help you make the most of your experience:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace.

  • Follow the park rules and regulations, especially those related to camping and lighting fires.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife or approach them. Wild animals can pose a danger if they feel threatened or provoked.

  • Damage the flora or fauna by picking flowers or vegetation or fishing without a license.

Safety and Health

While visiting Olympos National Park, keep in mind some safety and health tips to enjoy your trip to the fullest.

Safety in Olympos National Park

  • Always hike with a buddy and a map. Preferably a local guide would be beneficial to ensure your safety.

  • Ensure you’re wearing proper clothing, including shoes and sunglasses, to protect yourself from the sun, rain, or cold weather.

Health in Olympos National Park

  • Hydration is key, so ensure to carry water or sports drinks to remain hydrated.

  • Check for ticks often as the park has a high population of these creatures.

Getting to Olympos National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Olympos National Park is easily accessible by bus and car. Visitors can fly to several nearby airports, including Thessaloniki International Airport, and rent a car or take public transportation. Here are some of the transportation options to consider.

Airports Near Olympos National Park

  • Thessaloniki International Airport: Located 102 miles by car from Olympos National Park.

  • Kavala International Airport: Located 192 miles by car from Olympos National Park.

Bus Stations Near Olympos National Park

  • Katerini Bus Station: With regular buses to Olympos National Park from Katerini.

  • Thessaloniki Bus Station: With regular intercity buses available.

Other Options

If you have more independence and prefer to drive yourself, consider renting your own vehicle in Thessaloniki.

How to Get Around in Olympos National Park

While in the park, you can navigate by foot, organized tours, car or bike. For some hiking trails, it’s more comfortable, and recommended to use a horse to assist navigating.

Where to Go Next from Olympos National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re traveling to Greece, visiting Olympos National Park should be high on your list, but don’t forget other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular destinations to consider:

  • Mount Olympus: Just four miles away from Olympos National Park, this is a popular excursion option for visitors that have fallen in love with the stunning scenery of the mountain.

  • Veria: This nearby town boasts well-preserved Ottoman architecture and several historic churches, making it a popular destination among history buffs.

Conclusion

Olympos National Park is a nature lover’s paradise that offers a diverse range of attractions. From historic landmarks to scenic hiking trails, the park has everything you need to craft a memorable adventure in Greece. So, pack your gear, grab your map, and plan a trip to Olympos National Park today!
