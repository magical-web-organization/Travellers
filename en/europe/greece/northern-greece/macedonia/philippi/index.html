Philippi is a historically and culturally rich city in Northern Greece, known for its connection to the legendary Roman Empire and for being where Saint Paul founded the first church in Europe. The city also boasts stunning natural landscapes, which offer an exciting outdoor adventure and recreational opportunities. If you’re planning a trip to Philippi, look no further than this comprehensive travel guide to help you make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Philippi

Philippi is a city located in the Macedonian region of Northern Greece, with a population of around 18,000 people. The city has a long-standing history, dating back to the 4th century BC, when it was originally founded by the Macedonian king Philip II. After the Roman Empire conquered the region, Philippi became an important center for trade and commerce and eventually became a Christian hub with the arrival of St. Paul later in the first century BC.

Today, Philippi still stands as an important historical and cultural destination for travelers looking to discover and appreciate the rich heritage of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Philippi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Philippi’s rich history is reflected in its many museums and historical sites scattered throughout the city. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • The Ancient Theater of Philippi: built in the 4th century BC, is an impressive example of ancient Greek architecture and was the site of significant political and cultural events.
  • The Philippi Archaeological Site: Here visitors can explore the remains of the ancient city, including the agora, public buildings, and ancient ruins.
  • The Kavala Archaeological Museum (48 km away): Houses some of the most exciting artifacts of the Greek, Roman, and Byzantine period.

Natural Attractions of Philippi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thrilling outdoor activities await outdoors enthusiasts visiting Philippi.

  • Lailia Forest: Just about 10 kilometers from Philippi lies one of the largest forests in Greece, Lailia Forest. With the lush trees, cool streams, and wildlife, it is an excellent spot for exploration.
  • Mount Paggaio: Crossing the rural areas of Philippi, this beautiful mountain range offers outstanding views and trails to go hiking. The mountain has four peaks over 1,800 meters high, and there are numerous hiking trails.
  • The Abandoned Maronia Mines: In the West of Philippi, adventurous visitors can explore the abandoned Maronia Mines; treasured for its underground lakes, magnificent chambers, and tunnels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Philippi

Philippi is an excellent destination for those who love to walk and hike. The Kavala - Nestos Nature park offers a great variety of routes and trails, some of which are:

  • Krizo to Toxotes hiking route: This route tracks throughout the forest and is 14 kilometers long. It starts from the Krizo Village and ends at Toxotes.
  • 1,050-meter Hike in Mt Paggaio: This three-hour hike takes visitors to the top of the mountain, with inspiring views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Philippi: Activities and Tours

Philippi has a broad range of activities and tours to make your trip unforgettable.

  • Kayaking on the River Nestos: Follow the river’s winding route, offering the chance to observe many species of rare plants and animals while enjoying the scenery.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Explore the wine country and see grapes being harvested at the traditional vineyards in the surrounding area.
  • Half-Day Photographic Tour: If you’re a keen photographer, sign up for a Photographic Tour as it will offer the opportunity to capture the spectacular beauty of Philippi.

Food and Drink Scene of Philippi

No trip to Philippi would be complete without sampling its local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A classic Greek dish made with eggplant and minced meat and topped with a creamy béchamel sauce.
  • Koulouri: a typical Greek breakfast pastry; a ring-shaped bread coated with sesame seeds.
  • Taramosalata: a fish roe salad dip.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: a traditional spirit made from grape pomace, tsipouro is distilled in Macedonia and often served with meze.
  • Red and White Wine: The region of Philippi produces some of the tastiest wines in Greece due to its favorable climate and landscape.

Where to Stay in Philippi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Philippi provides a variety of accommodation choices, and whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel, a small guesthouse, or something in between, you’ll find it here.

  • Philippi Hotel: A 4-star hotel centrally located with fully equipped rooms, a restaurant and a bar.
  • Nestos River Melistas Guesthouse: Unique countryside accommodation ideal for nature lovers.
  • Ornea Village Restored House: Fully restored traditional village house, just 15 minutes away from the city center.

What to Buy in Philippi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Philippi can take home some of the city’s cultural riches with a locally-crafted souvenir, including:

  • Greek Olives: Fresh, delicious olives, made from the olive trees scattered throughout the region
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Philippi has a tradition of making handmade pottery, produced by using traditional methods that have been passed down through generations.
  • Greek Musical Instruments: Visitors who love music should take home a local instrument, such as the bouzouki; a stringed instrument essential to Greek music.

Local Culture and Customs of Philippi

Philippians have a unique culture and customs, and some of them are:

  • Family Values: Family values play a vital role in Philippi’s society, with respect for the elders being highly prioritized.
  • Punctuality: Philippians take punctuality very seriously; arriving even five minutes late to an appointment is considered rude.
  • Communication: Similar to other Greek culture, Philippians prefer open and friendly communication and are social by nature.

Best Time to Visit Philippi

The best time to visit Philippi is in the early spring or late fall where the weather is pleasant, and the tourist crowds are lower. However, if you’re interested in summer activities like river kayaking, plan to visit between June and August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Philippi

Do:

  • Learn some basic Greek words and phrases, which will assist a long way when interacting with locals.
  • Respect the local customs and be well-mannered.
  • Enjoy local Greek food and wine and partake in traditional celebrations and festivals

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious places.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings or archaeological sites.
  • Be impolite or disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Philippi

Philippi is generally considered a safe place to visit, both day and night, with the safety of visitors being the utmost priority for the locals. Nonetheless, normal safety precautions should be observed while traveling, such as:

  • Keeping an eye on personal belongings and never leaving them unattended.
  • Sticking to well-lit and well-traveled spaces while walking alone at night.

Health in Philippi

In terms of general health concerns, visitors should follow basic hygiene practices. The water is entirely safe, and there are medical facilities in the city center and nearby town of Kavala that can provide medical assistance if required.

Getting to Philippi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Philippi is quite a remote location, but getting there is easier than you might think.

Airports Near Philippi

The nearest airport to Philippi is Kavala International Airport. It is conveniently connected to different European destinations with direct flights, particularly during the summer season.

Bus Stations Near Philippi

Philippi Bus Terminal, located near the city center, is where visitors can catch inter-city buses that connect Philippi to other destinations in Greece. The terminal is serviced by various bus companies.

Other Options

There are rental cars, taxis, and private transfers from Kavala to get to Philippi. Renting a car is a preferable option, as it provides the flexibility of independent travel.

How to Get Around in Philippi

Getting around Philippi is relatively easy, with bus services operating in central areas. If you have time and want to explore the region fully, then renting a car is the best option. There are also taxis available in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Philippi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Philippi serves as an excellent base for excursions and exploration of the surrounding region.

  • Kavala: A beautiful city, rich with a cultural heritage, is 45 kilometers away from Philippi.
  • Thassos Island: This pristine island is only 100 kilometers away from Philippi and shall offer you some of the most beautiful beaches in Greece
  • Drama: A buzzy town, just 30 kilometers from Philippi, Drama, offers a vast range of entertainment, restaurants and museums to explore.

Conclusion

Philippi is Northern Greece’s undiscovered gem, with a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. It is an extraordinary destination that never ceases to amaze us. From the intricate archaeology to the lush green mountains and wildlife that reign across the place, there are sufficient activities and experiences to create a remarkable journey. We hope that this travel guide has provided valuable insights that will better your visit to Philippi.
