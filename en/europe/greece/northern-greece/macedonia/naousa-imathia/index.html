Introduction to Naousa (Imathia)

Naousa (Imathia) is a hidden gem of Northern Greece, located in the Imathia region. This small town is rich in history, tradition, and natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for those who want to experience the authentic Greek lifestyle away from the bustling tourist crowds. From exploring its ancient sites to trekking the scenic trails or tasting the local culinary delicacies, Naousa (Imathia) is a place to discover and explore.

List of Places to Visit in Naousa (Imathia)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Naousa Folklore Museum: Explore the life and traditions of the Greek people of Imathia through a rich collection of artifacts and exhibits. The museum is housed in a traditional mansion, dating back to the 19th century.

  • The Ancient City of Mieza: Discover the ancient ruins of Mieza, the birthplace of Aristotle’s famous philosopher-theory. The site houses the Aristotle’s School greenhouse and is surrounded by natural beauty.

  • The Byzantine Museum: Housed in the Historic Center, this museum holds the most significant collection of Byzantine art in Northern Greece.

Natural Attractions of Naousa (Imathia): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Vermio: This majestic mountain range offers hiking, climbing, and skiing in the winter. The highest peak of the range is the peak of Smolikas.

  • Edessa Waterfalls: Visit the spectacular Edessa Waterfalls, where the waterfalls from a height of 70 meters. The site includes a beautiful park, a small zoo, and a traditional mill.

  • The Grapes of Naousa Vineyard Trail: Follow the trail and explore the vineyards, taste the local wines from the local wineries, and discover how they are made.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naousa (Imathia)

  • The Great Vermio Traverse: This trail takes hikers from the top of the ridge to the mountain village Seli. The trail is 20km long and takes an average of 6 hours.

  • The Wine & Dine Ride: This biking route passes through the vineyards of Naoussa and introduces visitors to the rich wine culture of the region.

Best Things to Do in Naousa (Imathia): Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Experience the great Naoussa wine culture by visiting the local wineries and tasting the varieties of wine, along with the traditional food that accompanies them.

  • Greek Cooking Class: Learn the traditional Greek cuisine, from Tzatziki to Souvlaki, and get a masterclass on how to cook these dishes in Greek style.

Food and Drink Scene of Naousa (Imathia)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kokoretsi: A traditional Greek dish made of seasoned lamb’s intestines wrapped around a skewer and grilled until crispy.

  • Soutzoukakia: Meatballs made from beef and pork seasoned with cumin, allspice, and garlic, served with tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Naoussa Wine: The most famous local beverage of Naousa. Made from the Xinomavro grape, which grows exclusively in the area.

  • Tsipouro: This is a strong alcoholic drink made from the distillation of the grape’s remains after pressing. It is served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Naousa (Imathia): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Villa Elia: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Naousa, with comfortable and pleasant rooms.

  • Aris Guesthouse: Located within walking distance to the historic center of Naousa, this guesthouse provides an authentic Greek experience.

What to Buy in Naousa (Imathia): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Naousa Keyring: A metal keyring shaped as a basket, a symbol of the town.

  • The Vermio Honey: This aromatic honey comes from the beehives of Mount Vermio, famous for its rich flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Naousa (Imathia)

The people of Naousa (Imathia) are proud of their colorful traditions and culture. The town celebrates many religious and festive occasions, and visitors are always welcome to join the festivities.

Best Time to Visit Naousa (Imathia)

The best time to visit Naousa (Imathia) is during spring and fall. The weather is mild, and the natural beauty of the town is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naousa (Imathia)

Do:

  • Try the local wine and cuisine.

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the town.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive.

  • Disrespect the people’s beliefs, culture, and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naousa (Imathia)

Naousa (Imathia) is a safe town with friendly people. However, visitors should take the necessary safety precautions, such as locking their doors and windows at night and being aware of pickpockets and scammers in tourist areas.

Health in Naousa (Imathia)

The town has a small medical facility, and visitors should ensure adequate health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Naousa (Imathia): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naousa (Imathia)

There are two airports in Thessaloniki, Macedonia International, and the Domestic Airport.

Bus Stations Near Naousa (Imathia)

The town’s main bus station is located in the center, and buses run regularly to and from Thessaloniki.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option to explore Naousa (Imathia) and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Naousa (Imathia)

Walking is the best way to explore the town’s historic center. Taxis are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Naousa (Imathia): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece, known for its rich history, culture, and nightlife.

  • Meteora: A UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its monasteries built on top of towering rock formations.

Conclusion

Naousa (Imathia) is a town that will enchant you with its natural beauty, historical richness, and authentic, welcoming hospitality.
