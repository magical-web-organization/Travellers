Nea Phokea, located in Kassandra, in Chalkidiki, in Macedonia, in Northern Greece, is a beautiful coastal city that has a lot to offer to its visitors. With its rich history, stunning beaches, and picturesque landscapes, it’s no wonder it’s becoming an increasingly popular travel destination. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best things to do, places to visit, local culture and customs of Nea Phokea, Greece.

Introduction to Nea Phokea

Nea Phokea, also known as Nea Fokea, is a small coastal town located on the eastern shore of the Kassandra peninsula, about 70 kilometers from Thessaloniki. It is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and its charming old town, which features narrow, winding streets and traditional Greek architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Nea Phokea

There are many interesting and beautiful places to visit in Nea Phokea. Here are a few that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Byzantine Tower: Located in the small port of Nea Phokea, this tower dates back to the 15th century and is considered to be one of the most important landmarks of the town.
  • The Church of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary: A beautiful church located in the heart of the old town, featuring stunning architecture and artwork.
  • The Folklore Museum: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the town’s traditional way of life and its cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Nea Phokea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pine Tree Forest: Located just outside the town, this beautiful pine tree forest is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a peaceful picnic.
  • The Beaches: Nea Phokea is home to several stunning beaches, including Siviri Beach, Kallithea Beach, and Xenia Beach, all of which are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • The Peninsula: Hiking or biking along the Kassandra peninsula is a great way to explore the beautiful scenery and enjoy the Mediterranean climate.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nea Phokea

The Kassandra Peninsula is home to many hiking and biking trails that offer spectacular views of the area’s natural beauty. One of the most popular routes is the trail that leads from Nea Phokea to Hanioti, a small town located to the south.

Best Things to Do in Nea Phokea: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Nea Phokea. Here are a few of the best:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour around the coastline of the Kassandra Peninsula and admire the stunning scenery.
  • Water Sports: There are many water sports available in Nea Phokea, including snorkeling, scuba diving, windsurfing, and more.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: The Kassandra Peninsula is home to many excellent vineyards, and you can enjoy a wine tasting tour to sample some of the local wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nea Phokea

If you’re planning to spend a week in Nea Phokea, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Nea Phokea, settle in at your hotel, and head out to explore the town’s old town
2 Visit the Byzantine Tower and the Church of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary, and take a stroll through the pine tree forest
3 Spend the day at the beach, swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying water sports
4 Take a boat tour around the coastline of the Kassandra Peninsula
5 Explore the Kassandra peninsula on foot or by bike
6 Take a wine tasting tour and sample some of the local wine
7 Depart from Nea Phokea

Food and Drink Scene of Nea Phokea

The food and drink scene of Nea Phokea is a combination of traditional Greek dishes and modern fusion cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A traditional Greek dish made with layers of minced meat, eggplant, and potatoes topped with a creamy béchamel sauce.
  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat or vegetables on a skewer, usually served with pita bread and tzatziki sauce.
  • Greek Salad: A delicious salad made with tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, feta cheese, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek alcoholic drink made from anise and herbs.
  • Rakomelo: A warm drink made from honey, cinnamon, and raki.

Where to Stay in Nea Phokea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Nea Phokea to suit different budgets and needs. Some popular options include:

  • The Hanioti Grandotel: A luxurious hotel located in nearby Hanioti.
  • Hotel Margarita Sea Side: A mid-range hotel located near the beach.
  • Guesthouse Maria: A family-run guesthouse located in the old town.

What to Buy in Nea Phokea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to buy souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are a few options:

  • Olive Oil: Greece is famous for its delicious olive oil, and you can find high-quality local olive oil in Nea Phokea.
  • Handmade Ceramics: There are many small workshops and stores in the old town selling handmade ceramics.
  • Souvenirs: You’ll find a variety of souvenirs, including t-shirts, magnets, and keychains, at local gift shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Nea Phokea

The local culture and customs of Nea Phokea are deeply rooted in traditional Greek values. The people here are known for being friendly and hospitable, and it’s common for locals to welcome visitors into their home for a meal or a drink.

Best Time to Visit Nea Phokea

The best time to visit Nea Phokea is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and the beaches are open. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, the spring and fall months can also be a good time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nea Phokea

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn a few basic Greek phrases before you arrive.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Haggle over prices at local markets.
  • Leave your belongings unattended on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nea Phokea

Nea Phokea is considered to be a safe destination for travelers. However, as with any tourist destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in Nea Phokea

There are no specific health risks associated with traveling to Nea Phokea. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel health insurance and to bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Nea Phokea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nea Phokea

The nearest airport to Nea Phokea is Thessaloniki International Airport “Macedonia”, which is located about 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Nea Phokea

There are several bus stations near Nea Phokea, including the KTEL Chalkidikis bus station in Nea Moudania, which offers daily connections to Thessaloniki and other destinations.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car at Thessaloniki International Airport and drive to Nea Phokea. Alternatively, you can take a taxi or hire a private transfer from the airport.

How to Get Around in Nea Phokea

The best way to get around in Nea Phokea is on foot, as most of the town’s attractions are within walking distance. However, if you need to travel outside of the town, you can rent a car, take a taxi, or use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Nea Phokea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that you might consider visiting after exploring Nea Phokea, including:

  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece, Thessaloniki is a fascinating blend of ancient history and modern culture.
  • Mount Olympus: Home to the ancient Greek gods, Mount Olympus is a must-visit destination for hikers and nature lovers.
  • Sithonia: The middle peninsula of Chalkidiki, Sithonia is known for its pristine beaches and unspoiled natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or simply lazing on the beach, Nea Phokea has something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, it’s the perfect destination for your next vacation. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip and enjoy everything that Nea Phokea has to offer.
