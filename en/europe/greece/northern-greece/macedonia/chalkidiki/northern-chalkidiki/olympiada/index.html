Introduction to Olympiada

Located in Northern Chalkidiki, in Macedonia, Greece, Olympiada is a small town nestled in a picturesque bay and surrounded by lush green forests. It is situated on the east coast of the Halkidiki peninsula and lies at the foot of the mountain range that separates it from the inland regions of Greece. With its long sandy beaches, scenic hiking trails, and rich cultural heritage, Olympiada is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a mix of relaxation and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Olympiada

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Olympiada has something to offer. Here are some of the most popular attractions in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ancient Stagira: The birthplace of the great philosopher Aristotle, located just a short drive away from Olympiada.
  • Aristotle Park: A museum and park dedicated to Aristotle, with exhibits on his life and work.
  • Archaeological Museum of Olympiada: Featuring artifacts from the ancient city of Stagira.

Natural Attractions of Olympiada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olympiada Beach: A long stretch of sandy coastline that offers various water sports activities like swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking.
  • Mount Holomondas: A stunning mountain range that runs behind Olympiada, with many beautiful hiking trails to explore.
  • Stratoniki Canyon: Located in the countryside of Olympiada, the Stratoniki Canyon offers stunning views, rock climbing options, and picturesque waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Olympiada

Olympiada has an abundance of hiking and biking routes suitable for different levels of experience and stamina. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Olympiada to Ancient Stagira: Follow the path from Olympiada to the ancient city of Stagira and enjoy the panoramic views of the Aegean Sea.
  • Mount Holomondas: Explore the various paths on Mount Holomondas and discover the rich flora and fauna, including ancient oak trees, wildflowers, and birds of prey.
  • Olympiada to Stratoniki: Hike through the beautiful Stratoniki Canyon and enjoy its natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Olympiada: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Olympiada:

  • Explore the local villages around Olympiada, such as Stratoniki, Pyrgadikia, and Ierissos, and discover their unique history and culture.
  • Take a boat trip to Ammouliani Island, a small island nearby, where you can enjoy the crystal-clear waters and sandy beaches.
  • Try some water sports at Olympiada beach, such as windsurfing, parasailing, and jet-skiing.

Food and Drink Scene of Olympiada

Olympiada offers a range of local and traditional dishes that are worth trying. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat on skewers, served with pita bread and tzatziki sauce.
  • Moussaka: A layered dish with ground beef, eggplant, potato, and béchamel sauce.
  • Greek Salad: A classic salad with tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions, feta cheese, and olives.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek anise-flavored alcoholic drink.
  • Retsina: A white or rosé wine that has been flavored with pine resin.
  • Tsipouro: A strong, distilled spirit made from the pomace of grapes.

Where to Stay in Olympiada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Olympiada offers various options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Olympiada Hotel: A charming, family-run hotel, located in the heart of Olympiada.
  • Ouzas Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with stunning views of the sea.
  • Enastro Apartments: Modern, self-catering apartments with a garden and BBQ facilities.

What to Buy in Olympiada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some great options:

  • Olive oil: Greece is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Olympiada is no exception. It’s the perfect gift for foodies and cooking enthusiasts.
  • Handmade crafts: There are many local artisans that sell handmade crafts, like pottery, embroidery, and jewelry.
  • Honey: Northern Greece is famous for its honey, which comes in various flavors depending on the local flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Olympiada

Greece is a country rich in culture and traditions, and Olympiada is no exception. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and monasteries.
  • Smoking is prohibited in public areas.
  • It’s considered impolite to leave food on your plate.

Best Time to Visit Olympiada

The best time to visit Olympiada is during the summer months, from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and cooler temperatures, consider visiting in spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Olympiada

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the nearby villages to discover Greece’s rich culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Throw toilet paper in the toilet. Use the bin provided.
  • Touch the ancient artifacts in the museums and archaeological sites.
  • Use flash photography in the monasteries and churches.

Safety and Health

Olympiada is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, here are some safety and health precautions to keep in mind:

Safety in Olympiada

  • Always carry your passport and other important documents with you.
  • Use the hotel safe to store your valuables.
  • Follow the local rules and regulations when driving.

Health in Olympiada

  • Make sure to get travel insurance before your trip.
  • Carry a first-aid kit with you when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Drink bottled water or use a water filtration system.

Getting to Olympiada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Olympiada is well-connected to other parts of Greece, making it easy to get to. Here are some ways to reach Olympiada:

  • By plane: The nearest airport is Thessaloniki International Airport. From there, you can rent a car or take a bus to Olympiada.
  • By bus: There are daily buses from Athens and Thessaloniki to Olympiada.
  • By car: You can rent a car from Thessaloniki or Athens and drive to Olympiada.

How to Get Around in Olympiada

Olympiada is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, to explore the surrounding areas, you may want to rent a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Olympiada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spare, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Kavala: A beautiful coastal city with a picturesque harbor and a rich cultural heritage.
  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece, with a vibrant nightlife and a rich history.
  • Mount Athos: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it’s a monastic republic located on a peninsula that’s only accessible by boat.

Conclusion

Olympiada is a hidden gem that offers a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning beaches, scenic hiking trails, and welcoming locals, it’s a destination that’s worth adding to your travel bucket list. Whether you’re looking for relaxation or adventure, you’re sure to find something that suits your tastes in Olympiada.
