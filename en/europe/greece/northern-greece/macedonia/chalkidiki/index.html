Introduction to Chalkidiki

Situated in Macedonia, northern Greece, Chalkidiki is an appealing destination for both locals and tourists alike, featuring stunning beaches, natural parks, historical landmarks and authentic Greek hospitality. Thus, whether you are an adventure seeker seeking to unwind in outdoor activities or a culture lover wanting to embark on an educational journey through world heritage sites, Chalkidiki is the ideal place for you.

List of Places to Visit in Chalkidiki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The region of Chalkidiki boasts a rich history and a great cultural heritage, which can be best explored by visiting the following places:

  • Ancient Stageira: birthplace of the Greek philosopher Aristotle, where you can find ruins of archaeological and historical significance.
  • Petralona Cave: a magnificent natural wonder, where the famous Petralona skull, 200,000 years old, was discovered.
  • Byzantine Tower of Ouranoupoli: a magnificent fortification built in the 14th century, which today operates as a Museum and preserves the area’s legacy.
  • Church of Saint Paul in Neos Marmaras: a small parish church depicting the unique architectural style of the Greek Macedonian region.

Natural Attractions of Chalkidiki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are in search of a harmony between thrill and elegance, you can choose to visit the following sites in Chalkidiki:

  • Mount Athos: a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with steep, magnificent cliffs and charming monasteries. You can book a boat tour to admire the beauty of the surroundings.
  • Kriopigi Beach: sits on the edge of a pine forest and boasts splendid views of turquoise bays. Ideal for peaceful sunbathing and swimming.
  • Porto Carras Grand Resort: a luxurious complex, which features a selection of bars, restaurants, swimming pools, a casino and outdoor activities, such as golf courses, horse riding, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chalkidiki

Chalkidiki would not be complete without the lovely trails and biking paths that it offers:

  • Aristotle’s Path: a path leading you to the top of Athos, which provides panoramic landscapes.
  • Mineral Paths: a network of paths surrounding the mineral springs in the region passing through lakes and forest scenarios.
  • Sithonia Circular Trail: a hiking route around Mount Itamos, where trekkers can enjoy stunning views of both mountains and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Chalkidiki: Activities and Tours

To indulge in the rich experiences that Chalkidiki offers, here are some activities and tours for your consideration:

  • Boat Tour to Mount Athos: a boat ride from Ouranoupoli to Mount Athos, an opportunity to marvel at stunning architecture and monasteries.
  • Sea Kayak at Vourvourou: the perfect way to explore hidden coves and beaches, offering the opportunity to swim in the crystal-clear waters of the Aegean.
  • Wine Tasting: a wine-tasting tour through vineyards in the region, which encapsulates the cultural experience of Chalkidiki.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chalkidiki

Below is a reasonable itinerary split into a 7-day program, for travelers who want to experience the best that Chalkidiki offers.

Food and Drink Scene of Chalkidiki

When it comes to the local cuisine, Chalkidiki offers fresh sea products and dishes, with traditional recipes that date back to ancient times.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvlaki: earthenware skewers of pork.
  • Tzatziki: a tangy yoghurt sauce with cucumber and garlic.
  • Greek Salad: a fresh salad with tomatoes, cucumber, and feta cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: a locally distilled strong grape spirit.
  • Ouzo: an anise-flavored liqueur served with meze.
  • Red Wines: Local red wine is average quality, but it meets the expectation of wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Chalkidiki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chalkidiki offers a broad range of accommodation options, regardless of your budget and preferences. Some of the best choices are:

  • Ikos Olivia Resort: a five-star resort with many indoor and outdoor swimming pools, giving guests full access to the Aegean Sea experience.
  • Eagles Palace: a luxurious beachfront Resort that offers high-end dining, entertainment, and relaxation.
  • Hotel Alexandros Palace: a family-friendly resort on the Kassandra peninsula, with a private sandy beach, sports facilities, and its spa center.

What to Buy in Chalkidiki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To take some local-Chalkidiki flavor back home, here are some local products and souvenirs to consider:

  • Olive Oil: Extra virgin and fresh from local trees in the region.
  • Leather Sandals: authentic, traditional handmade sandals, which are an ideal present for men and women.
  • Honey: made by bees that pollinate flowers in high-altitude fields surrounding Chalkidiki.

Local Culture and Customs of Chalkidiki

Apart from the culinary feats, Chalkidiki beholds rich cultures and folklore performances and traditions. Here are some customs to respect during your visit:

  • Dress modestly when visiting local churches and monasteries.
  • Celebrate local traditions and festivals, like the ‘Cherry Festival’ in Arnea.
  • Try the “Greek” life: People of Chalkidiki are proud of their traditions and history, so join in and learn more!

Best Time to Visit Chalkidiki

The best time to visit Chalkidiki is between May and September, when temperature averages hover around 20°C - 30°C, providing ideal weather to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, swimming, sunbathing, etc.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chalkidiki

Do:

  • Try many local foods and drinks, as it’s the cornerstone of the local culture and identity.
  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Pack your trekking boots and sunscreen for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t invade monasteries
  • Don’t forget to remove shoes while entering sacred sites.
  • Don’t litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chalkidiki

  • It’s generally safe to travel to Chalkidiki, however, as with most places, exercise caution late at night and pay attention to your drinks.
  • Always keep an eye on your belongings while you are in crowded areas and don’t leave valuables unattended.

Health in Chalkidiki

  • Although overall a safe destination, you might be at risk of catching common travel illnesses such as stomach upsets. So, pack necessary medications, and ensure to drink bottled water.

Getting to Chalkidiki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chalkidiki

Chalkidiki is served by two airports:

  • Thessaloniki Airport (SKG): Located 90 km from Chalkidiki.
  • Kavala Airport (KVA): Located 170 km from Chalkidiki.

Bus Stations Near Chalkidiki

If you wish to travel by bus, Chalkidiki is served daily through several bus routes originating from Thessaloniki, and it takes 2-3 hours.

Other Options

  • By Car: Rent-a-car options are available at both Thessaloniki and Kavala Airports.
  • By Taxi: Taxis are also available at both airports and in major towns.

How to Get Around in Chalkidiki

Rental cars, motorcycles, and bicycles are available in Chalkidiki for transport. Nonetheless, tourists could easily move between villages by bus, while there is also a ferry service to transport people between parts of the coastal region.

Where to Go Next from Chalkidiki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece, is famous for its history, food, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Mount Olympus: a well-known ski resort and also the mythical residence of Ancient Greek Gods.
  • Kavala: a seaside city with an impressive history and sensational Ottoman architecture, situated on the Aegean Sea coast.

Conclusion

Chalkidiki is genuinely a treasure trove of experiences, where visitors can discover ancient history, taste authentic Greek cuisine, soak up in breathtaking natural beauty and indulge in fantastic outdoor activities and adventure. Pack your bags and embark on a journey of a lifetime to this Northern Greek gem.
