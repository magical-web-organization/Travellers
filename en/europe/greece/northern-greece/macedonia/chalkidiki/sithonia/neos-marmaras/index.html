Neos Marmaras is located in Sithonia, in Chalkidiki, in Macedonia, in Northern Greece, in Greece, in Europe. This charming Greek town offers visitors a diverse range of attractions, including stunning beaches, historical sites, and hiking trails. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about Neos Marmaras, from the top things to do and see to the best places to eat and stay.

Introduction to Neos Marmaras

Neos Marmaras is a picturesque coastal town located on the western shores of Sithonia, the middle of Chalkidiki’s three peninsulas. It boasts crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and a stunning view of Mount Athos. The town is built on a hill overlooking the turquoise sea, giving visitors a stunning panoramic view. Neos Marmaras is a popular destination for tourists seeking sun, sand, and sea, but it also offers a wide range of other activities and attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Neos Marmaras

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Parthenonas Village: A traditional Greek village located on a hill overlooking Neos Marmaras. It has preserved its unique architectural style and offers visitors a glimpse into the traditional way of life of old Greece.
  • The Sarti Byzantine Tower: Built during the Byzantine era, this well-preserved tower highlights the town’s rich history and culture.
  • The Byzantine Castle in Toroni: Located 20 kilometers south of Neos Marmaras, this fortress is one of the most important historical landmarks in the region.

Natural Attractions of Neos Marmaras: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kelyfos Island: This island is known as the turtle island because of its shape, which resembles a turtle. Visitors can enjoy diving, swimming, and snorkeling in its various coves.
  • Porto Carras Beach: One of the most popular beaches in the region, Porto Carras Beach offers crystal-clear waters and a stunning view of the sea.
  • Mount Itamos: The tallest mountain in Sithonia. It offers several hiking trails, stunning panoramic views of the Aegean Sea, and the opportunity to explore the area’s diverse flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neos Marmaras

There are numerous hiking and biking trails in the beautiful surroundings around Neos Marmaras. Here are some of the most recommended routes:

  • Mount Itamos Trail: A moderate three-hour hike to the top of the mountain, offering breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding region.
  • Parthenonas - Neos Marmaras Trail: A short but picturesque trail that passes through Parthenonas village and ends in Neos Marmaras.
  • Toroni - Porto Koufo Trail: A scenic route that passes by some of the most beautiful beaches in the area, including Toroni Beach and the hidden Porto Koufo Bay.

Best Things to Do in Neos Marmaras: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Trips: Explore the stunning coastline and hidden bays of the region with a boat tour.
  • Water Sports: With crystal-clear waters and endless beaches, Neos Marmaras offers plenty of opportunities to enjoy various water sports activities such as snorkeling, diving, windsurfing and paddle boarding.
  • Wine Tasting: Learn about the local wine culture with a wine tasting tour. Sip on some of the region’s finest wines while enjoying stunning views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neos Marmaras

Day 1: Explore the charming village of Parthenonas and enjoy the breathtaking panoramic view from its hilltop.

Day 2: Visit the Byzantine Tower in Sarti and explore the town’s beautiful beaches, known for their crystal-clear waters and white sand.

Day 3: Discover the natural beauty of the Kelyfos Island and enjoy swimming and snorkeling in its various coves.

Day 4: Explore the hidden gems of the region with a boat tour, including Porto Koufo Bay and the surrounding beaches.

Day 5: Hike to the top of Mount Itamos and enjoy the stunning panoramic view of the Aegean Sea.

Day 6: Enjoy various water sports activities such as windsurfing, paddle boarding, and diving.

Day 7: Relax at one of the region’s beautiful beaches and sip on some of the local wine.

Food and Drink Scene of Neos Marmaras

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dakos: A traditional Greek dish made of barley rusk, fresh tomato, olive oil, and feta cheese, served with capers and olives.
  • Moussaka: A layered dish of ground beef or lamb, eggplant, and potatoes topped with a creamy béchamel sauce.
  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat (usually pork, chicken, or lamb) served in a pita with tomato, onion, and tzatziki sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: A traditional Greek spirit distilled from grape marc, served as an aperitif or a digestif.
  • Ouzo: A classic Greek spirit made from anise, with a strong licorice flavor that is usually served with small snacks.

Where to Stay in Neos Marmaras: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Neos Marmaras, catering to all tastes and budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Porto Carras Meliton Hotel: A luxurious five-star hotel situated on the sandy beach, offering panoramic views of the sea and Mount Athos.
  • Arktos Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located just 300 meters from the town’s center, offering cozy rooms and a swimming pool.
  • Hotel Petunia: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Neos Marmaras, offering comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Neos Marmaras: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: Greece is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Neos Marmaras is no exception. Look for bottles of extra-virgin olive oil made from locally grown olives.
  • Honey: Another essential product of Greece, honey is produced in abundance in the region, particularly from the nettles and thyme that grow wild in the area.
  • Local Crafts: Browse the market stalls for traditional Greek handicrafts such as pottery, embroidery, and hand-painted ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Neos Marmaras

Greece has a rich cultural heritage, and locals in Neos Marmaras take pride in their customs and traditions. Be sure to respect local customs, including dress codes and customs when visiting churches.

Best Time to Visit Neos Marmaras

The best time to visit Neos Marmaras is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, visit during the shoulder seasons - May, September, or October, when the weather is still pleasant, and the beaches are less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neos Marmaras

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress codes.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Enjoy the stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without permission.
  • Litter on the beaches or the streets.
  • Swim in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neos Marmaras

Neos Marmaras is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always best to take sensible precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Neos Marmaras

Greece offers visitors high-quality healthcare services, including public and private hospitals. Visitors from EU countries should bring their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) to access free healthcare.

Getting to Neos Marmaras: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neos Marmaras

  • Thessaloniki International Airport “Macedonia” is the nearest international airport, located 90 kilometers from Neos Marmaras.

Bus Stations Near Neos Marmaras

  • Thessaloniki Central Bus Station (KTEL) operates daily services to Neos Marmaras.

Other Options

  • Car rental is readily available at Thessaloniki International Airport and Thessaloniki Central Bus Station.

How to Get Around in Neos Marmaras

The best way to explore Neos Marmaras is on foot. However, renting a car or scooter is a great way to reach more distant destinations in the surrounding area. Taxis, buses, and boats are also available for transportation within the town and to neighboring areas.

Where to Go Next from Neos Marmaras: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount Athos: Located on the eastern peninsula of Chalkidiki, Mount Athos is home to several ancient monasteries and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Nikiti: A small traditional village located 13 kilometers from Neos Marmaras, known for its beautiful beaches and friendly locals.
  • Thessaloniki: The second-largest city in Greece, Thessaloniki has a rich history and vibrant culture, with many museums and ancient monuments to explore.

Conclusion

Neos Marmaras is a hidden gem in the heart of Greece, offering a wide range of activities and attractions. From historical sites to natural wonders, visitors will never be short of things to do. Whether you’re a solo traveler or a family looking for a relaxing beach vacation, Neos Marmaras has something for everyone.
