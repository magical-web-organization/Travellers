Introduction to Kastoria

Kastoria is a small town located in Northern Greece, close to the border with Albania and North Macedonia. Kastoria is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and vibrant local community. The town is situated on the shore of Lake Orestiada, surrounded by the mountains of the Kastoria region. This unique setting provides visitors with breathtaking scenery and opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Kastoria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kastoria has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its many museums and historical places. Some of the most important destinations for history buffs are:

  • Byzantine Museum of Kastoria: showcases the art and architecture of the Byzantine Empire.
  • Museum of the Macedonian Struggle: highlights the local history of the political struggle against the Ottoman Empire.
  • Folklore Museum of Kastoria: offers a glimpse into the traditional way of life in Kastoria and the surrounding regions.

Natural Attractions of Kastoria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kastoria is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The natural beauty of the region provides a great backdrop for hiking, trekking, and mountain biking. Some of the top natural attractions in Kastoria are:

  • Lake Orestiada: a must-see destination, where visitors can enjoy boat trips, fishing, and swimming in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Grammos Mountain: offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.
  • Vitsi Mountain: a popular destination for hiking and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kastoria

Kastoria has plenty to offer for hiking and biking enthusiasts who want to enjoy the stunning natural scenery of the region. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Perasma Ski Center Hiking Trail: a 5 km hike that takes visitors to the summit of Perasma Ski Center, located at an altitude of 1,820 meters.
  • Vitsi Mountain Hiking Trail: a 7 km hike that starts from the village of Mikrolimni and ends at the summit of Vitsi.
  • Kastoria-Grammos Mountain Biking Route: a 35 km route that starts from Kastoria and goes through the villages of Nestorio and Vryta.

Best Things to Do in Kastoria: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the cultural attractions of Kastoria, visitors can also enjoy other fun activities and tours. Some of the top options include:

  • Boat trip around Lake Orestiada: a relaxing way to enjoy the stunning natural scenery of the region.
  • Wine tasting tours: Kastoria is known for its excellent wine production, and visitors can enjoy tours of local vineyards and wineries.
  • Mountaineering tours: for adventurous visitors, there are many mountaineering tours available to explore the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kastoria

If you are planning to spend a week in Kastoria, here is a sample itinerary that allows you to explore the top destinations of the region:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel of choice. Spend the afternoon exploring the historic center of Kastoria.
  • Day 2: Visit the Byzantine Museum, the Folklore Museum, and the Macedonian Struggle Museum.
  • Day 3: Take a boat trip around Lake Orestiada and relax by the lakeside.
  • Day 4: Go on a hiking tour to Vitsi Mountain and enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding region.
  • Day 5: Visit Nestorio village and enjoy a wine tasting tour at a local vineyard.
  • Day 6: Go on a mountaineering tour of Grammos Mountain and explore the stunning natural scenery of the region.
  • Day 7: Departure day - say goodbye to Kastoria and take with you unforgettable memories.

Food and Drink Scene of Kastoria

Must-Try Dishes

Kastoria is known for its traditional cuisine and fresh local ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes in Kastoria are:

  • Kourkouto: a local dish made with pork meat and pilaf rice.
  • Mpoureki: a pie made with cheese, spinach, and local herbs.
  • Trout: the local fish, which is caught in Lake Orestiada and served roasted or fried.

Local Beverages

Kastoria is also known for its excellent wine production. The most famous local wines are:

  • Xinomavro: a red wine with a distinct aroma and full body.
  • Malagouzia: a white wine with a delicate floral aroma and fresh taste.

Where to Stay in Kastoria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kastoria has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Kastoria are:

  • Esperos Palace Luxury & Spa Hotel: a 5-star hotel overlooking Lake Orestiada, offering luxurious amenities and services.
  • Limneon Resort & Spa: a 4-star hotel located in a scenic location, offering spa services and outdoor swimming pool.
  • Guesthouse Kastoria: a budget-friendly option, located in the historic center of Kastoria.

What to Buy in Kastoria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kastoria is known for its local products, which make great souvenirs and gifts for loved ones. Some of the top local products to buy in Kastoria are:

  • Fur products: Kastoria is famous for its high-quality fur products, such as jackets, coats, and hats.
  • Honey: the surrounding region is abundant in natural honey, which is sold in local shops and markets.
  • Woven textiles: Kastoria has a long tradition in weaving, and visitors can find a variety of woven textiles, such as blankets, rugs, and pillowcases.

Local Culture and Customs of Kastoria

Kastoria has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its architecture, art, and traditions. The local community is known for its hospitality and warm welcome to visitors. Some of the local customs and traditions to look out for are:

  • Carnival celebrations: Kastoria is famous for its carnival celebrations, which take place in February each year, and include parades, music, and dancing.
  • Marriage customs: Kastoria has unique marriage customs, such as the “turning of the spindle,” which symbolizes the bride’s transition from girlhood to womanhood.
  • Easter celebrations: Kastoria celebrates Easter with unique customs, such as the “burning of Judas,” which involves creating a scarecrow and burning it in a ceremony on Easter Sunday.

Best Time to Visit Kastoria

The best time to visit Kastoria is from spring to fall, when the weather is mild and allows visitors to enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views. The peak tourist season is in summer, and visitors can expect crowded beaches and higher accommodation prices during this time. However, Kastoria is also charming in the off-season, when visitors can enjoy a more relaxed atmosphere and lower prices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kastoria

Kastoria is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take usual precautions, such as not leaving valuable items unattended and being aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Kastoria

Kastoria has a good healthcare system with hospitals and medical facilities available in the town. Visitors should have medical travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Kastoria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kastoria has good connectivity with nearby towns and cities.

  • Kastoria National Airport Aristotelis located 12 km from Kastoria and connects with Athens, seasonally.

Bus Stations Near Kastoria

  • KTEL Kastorias Bus Station: located in the town center and connects with nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rental: a good option for visitors who want to explore the region at their own pace.
  • Taxi: easily available in Kastoria and can be hired for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Kastoria

Kastoria is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town center on foot. Public transportation and taxis are also available for longer distances or to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Kastoria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kastoria is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding region, which has many other attractions and destinations. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Prespa Lakes: a beautiful natural site located approximately 25 km from Kastoria, famous for its crystal-clear lakes and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Florina: a small town located approximately 60 km from Kastoria, famous for its vibrant cultural scene and beautiful architecture.
  • Veria: a town located approximately 120 km from Kastoria and famous for its historical and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Kastoria is a hidden gem in Northern Greece, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality from the local community. Whether you are into hiking, biking, exploring museums, or trying new foods and wine, Kastoria has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you will be able to plan an unforgettable trip to Kastoria and discover all that this stunning region has to offer.
