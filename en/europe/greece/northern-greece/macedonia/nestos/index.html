Introduction to Nestos

Nestos is a hidden gem located in Northern Greece, amidst the idyllic hills and creeks of the Rhodope mountains. It is a region blessed with natural beauty, undeniable cultural heritage, and friendly people. Nestos is situated near the border of Bulgaria, offering a unique blend of Slavic and Greek cultures. In this ultimate travel guide, we offer you all the necessary information about Nestos, including what to do, where to stay, what to buy, and when to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Nestos

Nestos offers a plethora of tourist attractions. Here is a list of places that must find a place on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The ancient city of Drakolimni
  • The Byzantine castle at Nea Kermeni
  • The Ottoman Old Town in Xanthi
  • The Ecclesiastical Museum in Paranesti

Natural Attractions of Nestos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nestos Canyon and river
  • Lake Vistonida
  • Vryta Kyklos
  • The forest of Livaditis

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nestos

Nestos is the perfect destination for adventure seekers. It offers some of the most beautiful hiking and mountain biking routes in Greece. Here are some of the must-do hikes and biking routes:

  • Trail around Nestos Canyon
  • Hike to the Ancient Drakolimni
  • Biking trail to the forest of Livaditis
  • Lake Vistonida circular bike route

Best Things to Do in Nestos: Activities and Tours

Nestos offers endless possibilities for sports enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the unique activities that you can do while in Nestos:

  • River rafting on the Nestos River
  • Kayaking in the lake Vistonida area
  • Horseback riding through the mountainous terrain
  • Jeep Safari to the valley of Nestos

Food and Drink Scene of Nestos

Nestos is a gastronomic paradise, offering a mix of Greek and Slavic cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kavourma (slow-cooked pork in a clay pot)
  • Moussaka
  • Koulouri (ringed bread)
  • Bekri Meze (pork cooked with peppers and onions)

Local Beverages

  • Rakomelo (Traditional Greek alcoholic drink)
  • Ouzo
  • Tsipouro

Where to Stay in Nestos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nestos offers a wide range of accommodations, suitable for all budgets. Here are some of the top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the region:

  • Avdira Rooms, Xanthi
  • Hotel Nestos, Stavroupolis
  • Elatos Guesthouse, Livadero

What to Buy in Nestos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nestos is famous for its local handicrafts, including embroidery, woven fabrics, and pottery. You can also take back home local products such as olive oil, honey, and wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Nestos

Nestos is a region with a fascinating blend of Greek and Slavic cultures. Folk music and dance play an integral role in the local culture. The people of Nestos are known for their hospitality and friendly nature.

Best Time to Visit Nestos

The best time to visit Nestos is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the weather is pleasant, and there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nestos

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the region.
  • Carry your ID card at all times.
  • Try to learn some local words and phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter around the natural trails and canyon.
  • Avoid sleeping during the mid-day due to high temperatures during summers.
  • Do not forget to wear appropriate hiking gear while on trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nestos

Nestos is a safe region, with low crime rates. However, it is advisable to take care of your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Nestos

The health facilities in Nestos are limited. Ensure that you carry your necessary medication and travel insurance.

Getting to Nestos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nestos is well connected via road and rail networks. Here are some of the transportation options for Nestos:

Airports Near Nestos

  • Kavala International Airport (KVA)
  • Thessaloniki Airport (SKG)

Bus Stations Near Nestos

  • Xanthi Bus station
  • Drama Bus Station

Other Options

  • Taxis and Private transfers from nearby cities can also be arranged.

How to Get Around in Nestos

The best way to explore Nestos’ natural trails and mountainous terrain is by renting a car or bike. Alternatively, you can also use the local buses or take part in an organized tour.

Where to Go Next from Nestos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit after exploring Nestos:

  • Thessaloniki
  • Komotini
  • Philippi archaeological park
  • Mount Athos

Conclusion

Nestos is a hidden gem that deserves to be explored. From its natural beauty to its fascinating cultural heritage, Nestos has something to offer to every traveler. Use this comprehensive guide to plan a memorable trip to Nestos, and explore all that this hidden gem has to offer.
