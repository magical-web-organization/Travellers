Introduction to Mykonos

Located in the heart of the Cyclades, Mykonos is one of the most popular destinations in Greece. Glittering with crystal-clear waters, stunning beaches, and narrow streets adorned with white-washed houses and vibrant bougainvillea, Mykonos is a paradise for travelers looking to unwind and soak up the sun. But Mykonos isn’t just about beaches; it’s a hub for history buffs and culture lovers, featuring an array of museums, historic landmarks, and cultural festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Mykonos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Mykonos: A must-visit for history buffs, the museum showcases a vast collection of artifacts, ceramics, and vessels from prehistoric times to the Hellenistic period.
  • Folklore Museum of Mykonos: Showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Mykonos, the museum houses a collection of traditional costumes, folk art, and household items used by locals for centuries.
  • The Windmills: An iconic landmark of the island, the windmills are a popular spot for visitors and offer a fantastic view of the town and the Aegean Sea.

Natural Attractions of Mykonos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delos Island: Take a day trip to the uninhabited neighboring island of Delos, a UNESCO World Heritage site that features some of the best-preserved ancient Greek ruins.
  • Paradise Beach: One of the most popular beaches on the island, Paradise Beach is known for its golden sand, crystal-clear water, and vibrant beach bars.
  • Little Venice: A picturesque part of the town, Little Venice features colorful, Venetian-inspired houses that are precariously built over the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mykonos

  • Hiking trail to Armenistis Lighthouse: A moderately challenging hike that takes you through rugged mountains, stunning views of the sea, and ends at the 19th-century Armenistis Lighthouse.
  • Biking Trail to Houlakia Beach: A scenic bike tour that takes you through the charming villages of Mykonos and ends at the secluded Houlakia Beach.

Best Things to Do in Mykonos: Activities and Tours

  • Sailing Tour of the Island: Discover the picturesque coastline of Mykonos on a private sailing tour.
  • Kayaking Adventure: Explore the hidden coves and crystal-clear waters of Mykonos with a guided kayaking tour.
  • Scuba Diving Experience: Explore the underwater world of Mykonos with a scuba diving tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Mykonos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kopanisti: A creamy, tangy cheese spread that’s a staple of Mykonian cuisine.
  • Louza: A traditional cured pork dish, seasoned with herbs and spices and served thinly sliced.
  • Taramasalata: A popular dip made with fish roe, olive oil, and lemon juice.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek spirit made with anise and served as an aperitif.
  • Raki: A strong, clear, and potent spirit resembling grappa.

Where to Stay in Mykonos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bill & Coo Suites and Lounge: A luxury boutique hotel overlooking the sea with elegant suites and excellent service.
  • MyCocoon Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers both private rooms and shared dorms in a central location.
  • The A Hotel by Mykonos Arhontiko: A charming, family-run property with an outdoor pool and stunning views of the sea.

What to Buy in Mykonos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Greek Sandals: Handmade leather sandals in unique designs and colors.
  • Greek Jewelry: Gold and silver jewelry in intricate designs that reflect the traditional Greek style.
  • Mykonian Delicacies: Take home some local products such as olive oil, honey, and wine, which are available in most shops throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Mykonos

  • Respect for Religion: Greeks take their religion seriously, and it’s essential to dress modestly and avoid entering churches during services.
  • Be Punctual: Greeks are proud of their punctuality, so try to arrive at your scheduled time.
  • Stay Safe during Parties: Mykonos is known for its wild party scene, but it’s essential to stay safe and watch out for yourself and others while enjoying the nightlife.

Best Time to Visit Mykonos

The best time to visit Mykonos is from May to October when the weather is warm, and the island’s many beaches are in full swing. July and August are peak tourist season, and the island can get crowded, so if you prefer a more serene and peaceful vacation, plan your visit in June or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mykonos

Do:

  • Try the Local Food: Mykonos has a rich culinary tradition, and it’s essential to sample some of the local delicacies.
  • Explore the Island’s Hidden Gems: Mykonos is full of hidden coves, charming villages, and scenic routes, so make sure to explore beyond the tourist hotspots.
  • Be Considerate: Respect local customs and be mindful of others while enjoying the island’s beaches and nightlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter: The island is known for its pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters, so make sure to dispose of your trash properly.
  • Overindulge in the Nightlife: While Mykonos is known for its fantastic party scene, it’s essential to stay safe and avoid overindulging in alcohol.
  • Skip the Sunscreen: The sun in Mykonos can be intense, so don’t forget to wear sunscreen and protect yourself from sunburns and heatstroke.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mykonos

Mykonos is a relatively safe destination with a low crime rate. However, as with any tourist destination, travelers should exercise caution and be mindful of their surroundings and valuables, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Health in Mykonos

There are no significant health risks associated with traveling to Mykonos. However, it’s essential to protect yourself from the sun, stay hydrated, and avoid overindulging in alcohol.

Getting to Mykonos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mykonos

Mykonos is served by the Mykonos Island National Airport (JMK). The airport is located four kilometers southeast of the town and can be easily reached by taxi, bus, or rental car.

Bus Stations Near Mykonos

There are several bus stations throughout the town, which offer connections to most parts of the island. The main bus terminal is located in Fabrika Square, and schedules can be found online or at the station.

Other Options

Ferries and catamarans connect Mykonos to several nearby islands and mainland Greece, including Athens, Santorini, and Naxos. There are also frequent boat tours and taxi boats that operate around the island.

How to Get Around in Mykonos

The best way to get around Mykonos is by renting a car, motorcycle, or ATV. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive, especially during peak tourist season. For a more eco-friendly option, visitors can also rent bicycles or e-bikes or use the island’s bus network.

Where to Go Next from Mykonos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santorini: One of the most popular destinations in Greece, Santorini is known for its breathtaking sunsets, stunning beaches, and picturesque villages.
  • Naxos: The largest island in the Cyclades, Naxos boasts of beautiful beaches, a rich ancient history, and charming medieval villages.
  • Paros: Known for its tranquil beaches, traditional architecture, and authentic Greek culture, Paros is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a more laid-back vacation.

Conclusion

Mykonos is a destination that caters to all kinds of travelers- whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore the island’s rich history and culture. From its picturesque narrow streets to the crystal-clear waters of its beaches, Mykonos is sure to capture your heart and make for a memorable vacation.
