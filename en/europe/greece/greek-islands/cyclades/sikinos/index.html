Introduction to Sikinos

Tucked away in the Cyclades islands of Greece, Sikinos offers a slice of laid-back Greek life that’s hard to come by in other tourist-flooded destinations. The island may be small, but it is bursting with authentic experiences, quaint villages, and rugged natural scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Sikinos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sikinos is rich in history, with its roots tracing back to ancient times. Some of the must-visit historical sites and museums in Sikinos that will give you a taste of its fascinating past include:

  • Monastery of Zoodochou Pigis: A beautiful and well-preserved monastery that provides a glimpse into orthodox monastic life.
  • Castle of Sikinos: A labyrinth-like medieval castle that offers a fantastic view of the island and the surrounding sea.
  • Ancient Episkopi: An excavation spot where you’ll discover remnants of one of the oldest churches in Greece.

Natural Attractions of Sikinos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sikinos is home to several picturesque beaches, charming villages, and idyllic landscapes. Here are some of the natural attractions that are worth checking out:

  • Beaches: Agios Georgios, Dialiskari, and Alopronoia are some of the most beautiful beaches in Sikinos.
  • Village of Kastro: A quaint traditional village with cobbled streets, white-washed houses, and stunning sea views.
  • Episkopi Hill: A popular hiking spot that offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sikinos

Sikinos is a hiker’s paradise, with plenty of trails winding through the island’s rugged terrain. Some of the popular hiking trails that you shouldn’t miss include:

  • Sikinos Trail: A 9.5 km hiking trail that takes you through the most scenic villages of Sikinos.
  • Monastery of Zoodochou Pigis Trail: A 4.5 km trail that combines history and nature, offering a gorgeous view of the sea.
  • Episkopi Hill Trail: A 3.5 km trail that takes you up to Sikinos’ highest peak.

Best Things to Do in Sikinos: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and exploring the island’s cultural and natural attractions, visitors to Sikinos can engage in various activities, including:

  • Wine Tasting: Sikinos produces some of the finest wines in the Cyclades, and you can sample some of them at local wineries, including the Manalis Winery.
  • Yoga and Meditation Retreats: If you’re looking to rejuvenate and unwind, you can sign up for a yoga and meditation retreat, where you’ll combine holistic practices with stunning natural scenery.
  • Sunset Cruises: Enjoy a romantic and unforgettable sunset cruise around the island and witness the beauty of Sikinos’ sunset.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sikinos

Here’s a week-long suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Sikinos:

Day 1: Arrive in Sikinos, settle into your accommodation, and explore the nearby village of Kastro. Day 2: Hike to the Monastery of Zoodochou Pigis, explore the monastery, and enjoy a scenic picnic with a view. Day 3: Visit the Castle of Sikinos and take a stroll through the stone streets of the medieval village. Day 4: Relax on the beach at Dialiskari or Agios Georgios and sample the seafood at a local tavern. Day 5: Visit the Panagia Pantanassa Church and hike up to Episkopi Hill to watch the sunset. Day 6: Rent a motorbike or a car to explore the island’s hidden gems, including remote beaches and picturesque villages like Chora. Day 7: Indulge in a wine tasting tour and sample some of the island’s finest wines before departing from Sikinos.

Food and Drink Scene of Sikinos

Sikinos offers a delightful culinary experience, with a focus on fresh, local, and seasonal ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Revithada: A traditional Greek chickpea stew served with bread and drizzled with olive oil.
  • Sikiniotiko syglino: A smoked pork that is left to cure for over a month and is served thinly sliced.
  • Karpouzopita: A watermelon pie made of fresh watermelons mixed with nuts, sugar, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sikinos Wine: Produced exclusively in Sikinos, this dry white wine has a pleasant acidity and fruity taste.
  • Raki: A traditional Greek spirit made from distilled grapes, served as an aperitif or digestif.
  • Herbal Tea: Sikinos is renowned for its herbal teas, made from locally grown herbs such as thyme, sage, and chamomile.

Where to Stay in Sikinos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options to suit all budgets in Sikinos, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Below are some of the best places to stay on the island:

  • Windmill Bella Vista: A family-run hotel located in Chora with stunning views of the Aegean Sea.
  • To Steki: A cozy guesthouse in Kastro village with comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Agnanti Suites: A boutique hotel with modern amenities, an infinity pool, and exceptional sea views.

What to Buy in Sikinos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sikinos might be a small island, but it’s big on charm, and there are plenty of souvenir shops where you can buy local products and unique keepsakes. Some of the best things to take home with you from Sikinos include:

  • Sikinos Wine: You can buy Sikinos’s famous wine to take home or as a gift for family and friends.
  • Local honey: Sikinos is renowned for its high-quality honey, sourced from the island’s wildflowers.
  • Handmade pottery: Sikinos has a long tradition of pottery-making, and you can buy handcrafted plates, bowls, and other ceramics as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sikinos

Sikinos has a rich cultural heritage and is famous for its traditional feasts and festivals. Some of the customs and traditions that you should know before visiting Sikinos include:

  • The Feast of Agios Panteleimonas: A religious festival celebrated on July 27th with traditional music, food, and dancing.
  • The Easter Celebrations: A week-long celebration with unique rituals and customs, including the burning of an effigy and midnight mass.
  • Traditional Marriage: Sikinos is known for its traditional wedding customs, which involve the groom carrying his bride to their new home in a procession called ‘Antiparousia.’

Best Time to Visit Sikinos

The best time to visit Sikinos is between May and September when the weather is pleasant, and all tourist facilities are open. June-July and September are the best months to avoid the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sikinos

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and monasteries.
  • Explore the hiking trails and natural scenery of the island.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or natural landscapes.
  • Touch or remove any historical or cultural artifacts.
  • Disrespect the local culture or people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sikinos

Sikinos is generally a safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, always take precautions, especially when traveling solo at night.

Health in Sikinos

Visitors to Sikinos should take the necessary precautions during their stay, including drinking bottled water and using sunscreen to avoid sunburns.

Getting to Sikinos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sikinos

There are no airports on Sikinos Island. However, the nearest airports are:

  • Santorini Airport: Located on the nearby island of Santorini, you can reach Sikinos by a ferry service that takes around 2 to 3 hours.
  • Milos Airport: Located on the island of Milos, you can reach Sikinos by a ferry service that takes around 1 hour and 15 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Sikinos

There are no bus stations on Sikinos Island. However, you can take a local bus service on the island that connects villages and beaches.

Other Options

  • Ferry: You can take a ferry from Athens’ port of Piraeus to Sikinos Island.
  • Private Transfer: You can hire a private transfer service to pick you up from your hotel in Athens and drive you to the port, where you can take a ferry to Sikinos.

How to Get Around in Sikinos

The best way to explore Sikinos Island is on foot, but you can also rent bicycles, motorbikes, or cars to explore the hidden gems and natural wonders of the island.

Where to Go Next from Sikinos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sikinos is part of the Cyclades Island group, and there are several neighboring islands that you can explore, including:

  • Ios Island: Just a short ferry ride away, Ios offers stunning beaches, lively nightlife, and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • Folegandros Island: A tranquil island with picturesque villages, stunning cliffs, and clear turquoise waters.
  • Santorini Island: A world-renowned destination for its caldera views, beautiful sunsets, and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Sikinos is a hidden gem in the Cyclades islands of Greece that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and authentic Greek island life. Whether you’re a history enthusiast, a nature lover, or looking for a peaceful retreat, Sikinos has something to offer. Plan your visit to Sikinos today and immerse yourself in the beauty of this lesser-known Greek island.
