Introduction to Anafi

Anafi is a small, quiet, and pristine island located in Cyclades, in the Aegean Sea. It’s one of the least-known islands in Greece, making it perfect for those looking for a serene and secluded getaway. Anafi is a hidden gem with untouched beauty, dramatic landscapes, charming villages, and beautiful beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Anafi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Monastery of Zoodochos Pigi: This is a beautiful monastery located in the heart of Anafi. It’s a serene and spiritual place that you shouldn’t miss when visiting Anafi.
  • The Ancient City of Anafi: This ancient city was built in the 7th century BC, and it’s one of the oldest settlements in the Cyclades. You can explore the ruins and learn about the history of the island.

Natural Attractions of Anafi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kalamiotissa Beach: One of the most popular beaches on the island, Kalamiotissa Beach, is a secluded paradise surrounded by steep cliffs that offer incredible views.
  • Anafi Mountain: Anafi Mountain is the highest point on the island. Hiking up this mountain will give you breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and the sparkling blue waters of the Aegean Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anafi

Anafi has many hiking and biking trails that will take you through some of the most stunning landscapes on the island. Some popular routes include:

  • The Trail to Kalamos Beach: The trail to Kalamos Beach is a popular route that takes you through olive groves and rocky landscapes. At the end of the trail, you’ll be rewarded with the stunning views of the beach.
  • The Trail to the Monastery: This trail takes you to the Monastery of Zoodochos Pigi, where you can spend some time exploring this ancient monastery and taking in the stunning views around it.

Best Things to Do in Anafi: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Beaches: Anafi is home to many pristine beaches that are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Explore the Local Villages: Anafi has several charming villages that are worth exploring. With their narrow streets and traditional architecture, they offer a glimpse into the local way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of Anafi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fava: Anafi is famous for its delicious fava beans, which are cooked with onions and tomatoes to make a mouthwatering dish.
  • Ladenia: Ladenia is a traditional Anafi dish that resembles a pizza. It’s made with a thin crust, onions, and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Anafi Wine: The local wine of Anafi is made with grapes that grow in the island’s rich volcanic soil. The wine has a distinct taste and is a must-try during your visit to Anafi.
  • Rakomelo: Rakomelo is a drink made with raki, honey, and spices. It’s a warming drink that’s perfect for chilly nights.

Where to Stay in Anafi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anafi offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets.

  • Hotel Aegli: Hotel Aegli is a great option for those looking for a comfortable stay. It’s located in the heart of Anafi port and offers stunning views of the sea.
  • Guesthouse Anafi: This family-run guesthouse is located in Chora and offers cozy rooms with traditional Cycladic architecture.
  • Anafi Camping: Anafi Camping is a budget-friendly option for those who want to stay close to nature. It’s located near the beach and offers camping sites and simple rooms.

What to Buy in Anafi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Anafi Honey: Anafi honey is known for its quality and taste.
  • Local Jewelry: Anafi is home to many talented jewelers who make unique and beautiful pieces of jewelry.
  • Traditional Textiles: You can find many shops in Chora selling traditional textiles and embroidery that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Anafi

Anafi has a rich culture and traditions that date back to ancient times. The locals are proud of their heritage and love to share it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Anafi

The best time to visit Anafi is from late spring to early autumn (May to September). The weather is warm and pleasant, and the island is less crowded than other popular tourist destinations in Greece.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anafi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Explore the island’s beaches and natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or in nature.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Be too loud or rowdy in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anafi

Anafi is generally a safe place to visit. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions like locking your doors and valuables in your room when you’re out exploring.

Health in Anafi

Anafi has a small health center that can provide basic medical care. However, for more serious medical needs, it’s best to go to a nearby hospital on a larger island.

Getting to Anafi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Anafi is not easily accessible, but the journey is worth it.

Airports Near Anafi

The closest airports to Anafi are:

  • Santorini National Airport (JTR)
  • Mykonos International Airport (JMK)

Bus Stations Near Anafi

The closest bus stations to Anafi are:

  • Piraeus Bus Station
  • Rafina Port Bus Station

Other Options

The best way to get to Anafi is by taking a ferry or a hydrofoil from Piraeus Port or Rafina Port.

How to Get Around in Anafi

There is no public transportation on the island, but it’s easy to walk around the villages. Renting a car or a scooter is a great way to explore the island and visit the more remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Anafi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anafi is a great base for exploring other nearby islands, including:

  • Santorini
  • Mykonos
  • Naxos

Conclusion

Anafi is a hidden gem in the Aegean Sea that’s waiting to be explored. With its pristine beaches, beautiful landscapes, and rich culture, it’s the perfect destination for those looking for a relaxing and authentic Greek island experience. Plan your trip today and discover all that Anafi has to offer.
