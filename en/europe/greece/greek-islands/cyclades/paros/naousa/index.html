Introduction to Naousa

Naousa is a charming Greek town located on the island of Paros in the Cyclades archipelago, renowned for its picturesque alleys, traditional architecture, and its lively atmosphere. The town has a rich history and culture that can be seen in its museums, historical landmarks, and ancient ruins. Naousa is also known for its outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. Visitors can explore the turquoise waters, pristine nature, and picturesque landscapes that the region has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Naousa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Folklore Art Museum of Cycladic Civilization: Located in the town center, this museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history and culture of the region. It has a vast collection of artifacts dating back to the ancient times, including traditional costumes, pottery, and sculptures.

  • Venetian Castle: This magnificent castle is a testament to the town’s rich history and served as a crucial strategic point during the Venetian occupation. Visitors can climb to the top of the castle for a panoramic view of the town and the bay.

  • Old Mines of Paros: The ancient quarries are an impressive sight to behold. Visitors can explore the underground tunnels, which were used to extract marble and limestone used in the construction of ancient Greek monuments.

Natural Attractions of Naousa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kolymbithres Beach: This stunning beach is known for its unique rock formations that have been carved by the wind and the sea. Visitors can enjoy swimming in crystal-clear water, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

  • Naousa Bay: This scenic bay is a perfect spot for water sports enthusiasts, offering activities such as windsurfing, kite-surfing, and paddleboarding. Visitors can also take a boat tour to explore the hidden bays and coves of the region.

  • Mount Profitis Ilias: This scenic mountain offers spectacular views of the town and the surrounding area. Visitors can hike to the summit and enjoy a panoramic view of the Cyclades islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naousa

  • Paros Park Trails: Paros Park offers several hiking and biking trails that lead to stunning beaches, secluded coves, and ancient ruins. Visitors can choose from a variety of trails, ranging from easy to challenging, depending on their fitness level and preferences.

Best Things to Do in Naousa: Activities and Tours

  • Watersports: Visitors can enjoy a variety of water sports activities such as windsurfing, kite-surfing, and paddleboarding in Naousa Bay.

  • Boat Tours: Boat tours are a great way to explore the region’s hidden bays, coves, and beaches. Visitors can book a tour and enjoy a day out on the sea, swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

  • Wine Tours: Paros is known for its excellent wines, and visitors can book a wine tour to explore the vineyards and taste the local wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Naousa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saganaki: Fried cheese is a popular appetizer in Greece, and saganaki is a must-try dish when visiting Naousa.

  • Tzatziki: This yogurt-based dip is a favorite among Greeks and is served with grilled meat or pita bread.

  • Seafood: Naousa is known for its seafood, and visitors can enjoy the catch of the day in one of the many seaside taverns.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: This traditional Greek drink is made from anise and is often served as an aperitif. It is a must-try beverage when visiting Naousa.

  • Wine: Paros is known for its excellent wines, and visitors can taste the local wines on a wine tour.

Where to Stay in Naousa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Senia: This charming hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the Aegean Sea. It is located within walking distance of the town center and the beach.

  • Joseph Studios: These modern apartments are equipped with all the necessary amenities and are located just a few minutes’ walk from the town center.

  • Narges Hotel: This family-friendly hotel offers spacious rooms and a swimming pool. It is located just a short walk from the beach and the town center.

What to Buy in Naousa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry: Visitors can buy unique handmade jewelry made by local artisans in the town center.

  • Marble Souvenirs: Paros is known for its marble quarries, and visitors can buy souvenirs made from local marble.

Local Culture and Customs of Naousa

The locals in Naousa are known for their warm hospitality, and visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s customs and traditions. Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other sacred sites, and avoid littering in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Naousa

The best time to visit Naousa is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm. July and August are the busiest months, and visitors should book their accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naousa

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.

  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.

  • Touch or remove artifacts from archaeological sites.

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches and other sacred sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naousa

Naousa is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding secluded areas after dark.

Health in Naousa

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to avoid sunburn and dehydration during the hot summer months. It is also advisable to have travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies.

Getting to Naousa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naousa

The closest airport to Naousa is Paros National Airport (PAS), which is about 20 minutes’ drive from the town center. Visitors can also fly to Athens International Airport (ATH) and take a ferry or a flight to Paros.

Bus Stations Near Naousa

Visitors can take a bus from Athens to Paros and then take a taxi or a bus to Naousa.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from Athens or other nearby islands to Paros and then take a taxi or a bus to Naousa.

How to Get Around in Naousa

The best way to get around Naousa is on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or a scooter to explore the town and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Naousa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Antiparos: This small island is just a short ferry ride from Paros and is known for its vibrant nightlife, crystal-clear water, and stunning sunsets.

  • Santorini: This popular Greek island is just a short boat ride from Paros and is known for its whitewashed houses, blue domed churches, and stunning views.

Conclusion

Naousa, Paros offers a plethora of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor activities, delicious food and drinks, and so much more. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and culture, enjoy a variety of outdoor activities and sports, and taste the local cuisine and wines. Naousa is an excellent destination for anyone looking for a combination of relaxation, adventure, and culture.
