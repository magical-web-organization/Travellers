Introduction to Ampelas

Welcome to Ampelas, a serene fishing village nestled on the northeastern coast of Paros, one of the picturesque Cycladic islands in Greece. Known for its crystal-clear waters and unspoiled natural beauty, Ampelas is an off-the-beaten-path destination for travelers seeking an authentic Greek experience. The village is home to a small but vibrant community of fishermen, farmers, and artisans who have preserved their traditional way of life and hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Ampelas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ampelas may be a small village, but it is rich in history and culture. Start your tour at the Folklore Museum of Paros, which showcases the island’s customs, traditions, and costumes through a collection of rare artifacts, photographs, and manuscripts. Another must-visit attraction is the ancient marble quarries of Marathi, where you can see impressive remnants of Parian marble production from the 6th century BC to the 19th century AD.

Natural Attractions of Ampelas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ampelas is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the secluded coves and beaches along the coast, or take a guided sailing tour to discover hidden gems like the Blue Lagoon and the uninhabited island of Despotiko. You can also hike the trails of the nearby Kefalos Hill, where you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of the Aegean Sea and the surrounding islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ampelas

Ampelas offers several hiking and biking routes, ranging from easy strolls to challenging trails for experienced adventurers. One of the most popular routes is the Xilokeratidi Trail, a circular route that starts and ends in Ampelas and passes through the wild hills of Paros, offering breathtaking views of the island’s flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Ampelas: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and watersports, there are plenty of other things to do in Ampelas. Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional Greek dishes like tzatziki, moussaka, and dolmades using fresh ingredients sourced from local farms. Or, join a wine tasting tour and sample some of the best wines of Paros, such as the dry white Malagousia and the sweet red Vinsanto.

Food and Drink Scene of Ampelas

Ampelas is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kakavia: a hearty fish soup made with fresh catch, vegetables, and herbs
  • Revithada: a stew made with chickpeas, onions, garlic, and olive oil
  • Loukoumades: small deep-fried dough balls glazed with honey and cinnamon

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: a strong spirit made with grape pomace
  • Ouzo: an anise-flavored aperitif
  • Parian wines: dry white and sweet red varieties

Where to Stay in Ampelas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ampelas offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. If you prefer a peaceful retreat, the Antiparos View Villas offer luxury villas with private pools and panoramic sea views. For a more affordable option, the Parian Village Beach Hotel provides cozy rooms and apartments in a tranquil setting.

What to Buy in Ampelas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ampelas is famous for its traditional handicrafts, such as handmade ceramics, woven textiles, and carved wooden objects. There are also several local farmers’ markets where you can buy fresh produce, spices, and honey, as well as local cheese and olive oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Ampelas

Ampelas has a strong cultural identity shaped by its history and geography. The locals are proud of their traditional way of life and are happy to share it with visitors. Respectful behavior and conversation are appreciated, and it’s common to greet others with a friendly “yassou”.

Best Time to Visit Ampelas

The best time to visit Ampelas is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is ideal for swimming and watersports. July and August can be crowded, so if you prefer a quieter atmosphere, consider visiting in early or late season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ampelas

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Support local artisans and farmers by buying their products
  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public spaces
  • Dress inappropriately for religious sites and events
  • Pick flowers or disturb wildlife without permission
  • Ignore safety guidelines for activities like hiking and watersports

Safety and Health

Safety in Ampelas

Ampelas is a safe and peaceful destination with low crime rates. However, it’s always wise to take basic precautions like locking your accommodation and keeping your valuables in a safe place. In case of emergency, dial 112 for assistance.

Health in Ampelas

Ampelas has a small medical center that provides primary care services. It’s recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and repatriation in case of serious illness or injury.

Getting to Ampelas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ampelas

The nearest airport to Ampelas is the Paros National Airport, located about 15 km south of the village. It operates direct flights from Athens and other Greek islands, as well as some European cities during the summer season. Alternatively, you can fly to the Mykonos Airport or the Santorini Airport and take a ferry or a speedboat to Paros.

Bus Stations Near Ampelas

Paros has a good public bus network that connects Ampelas to other parts of the island. The main bus terminal is located in the capital town of Parikia, about 15 km southwest of Ampelas.

Other Options

You can also arrive in Paros by ferry or speedboat from various ports in Athens, other Greek islands, and Turkey. The main ports in Paros are Parikia and Naoussa, which are both within a short driving distance from Ampelas.

How to Get Around in Ampelas

Ampelas is a small village that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. You can also rent a car or a scooter to visit nearby attractions and beaches. Taxis and private transfers are available but are relatively more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Ampelas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ampelas is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Paros and nearby islands. Here are some suggested destinations and excursions:

  • Antiparos: a small island with a stunning cave and beautiful beaches
  • Parikia: the capital town of Paros with a charming old town and a medieval castle
  • Naoussa: a picturesque fishing village with a lively nightlife and trendy boutiques
  • Mykonos: a cosmopolitan island with luxury resorts, upscale restaurants, and vibrant nightlife

Conclusion

Ampelas is an enchanting destination that offers an authentic glimpse into the rich culture and natural beauty of the Cyclades. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or both, Ampelas has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to Ampelas and discover its hidden gems.
