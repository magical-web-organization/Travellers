Introduction to Naxos

Located in the heart of the Cyclades, Naxos is one of the most diverse islands in Greece, showcasing stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and a vibrant culture. Despite its beauty, Naxos has remained relatively undiscovered by tourists, making it the perfect destination for travelers who want to escape the crowds and explore a hidden gem. With its stunning beaches, towering mountains, and picturesque villages, Naxos is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Greece.

List of Places to Visit in Naxos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Naxos has a rich history, and visitors should not miss out on exploring the island’s culturally significant attractions. A visit to the following museums and historical places will give you a glimpse into Naxos’s past:

  • The Archaeological Museum of Naxos: The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts from the prehistoric, Archaic, Classical, Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods.

  • The Portara: This imposing marble gate is all that remains of the ancient Temple of Apollo and is considered one of the most iconic landmarks of Naxos.

  • The Venetian Museum: Showcases the history of the island during the Venetian occupation.

  • The Medieval Castle: The Castle of Naxos, also known as the kastro, is an impressive fortification built in the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Naxos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Naxos is also home to a range of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the natural wonders that Naxos has to offer:

  • Mount Zas: At 1,004 meters, Mount Zas is the highest peak in the Cyclades and offers stunning views of the island’s rugged landscape.

  • The Cave of Zas: Located on the slopes of Mount Zas, the Cave of Zas is said to be the birthplace of the god Zeus.

  • Apollo’s Temple: The Sanctuary of Apollo is an important archaeological site that provides stunning panoramic views of the island.

  • Agios Prokopios Beach: Considered one of the best beaches in Greece, Agios Prokopios Beach boasts crystal-clear waters and a long stretch of white sand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naxos

Naxos is a hiker’s paradise, with trails that weave through the island’s varied terrain, from mountain peaks to coastal cliffs. Biking is also a popular way to explore the island, with rental options available in various locations. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes on Naxos:

  • Hiking Route 1: Filoti - Mount Zas (3-4 hours, 6.8km)

  • Hiking Route 2: Melanes - Flerio (3-4 hours, 9.3km)

  • Hiking Route 3: Danakos - Eggares - Sagri (3-4 hours, 9.2km)

  • Biking Route 1: Naxos Town - Agios Prokopios Beach (3 hours, 11km)

  • Biking Route 2: Halki - Filoti - Apeiranthos (6 hours, 27km)

Best Things to Do in Naxos: Activities and Tours

While there is no shortage of things to do on Naxos, here are some of the best activities and tours to keep in mind:

  • Kitesurfing on Agios Georgios Beach: Naxos is known as one of the best kitesurfing destinations in Europe, thanks to the island’s consistent winds.

  • Sunset horseback ride: Enjoy a unique perspective of the island’s breathtaking views on horseback.

  • Wine tour: Sample the local wine at the island’s wineries, such as Moraitis Winery and Vallindras Distillery.

  • Scuba Diving: Explore the crystal-clear waters around Naxos and discover a wealth of marine life.

Week-long Itinerary for Naxos

The following itinerary will give you a glimpse into how you can spend a week on Naxos:

Day 1: Explore the Old Town of Naxos and visit the Portara at sunset.

Day 2: Hike Mount Zas and visit the Cave of Zas.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the island of Koufonisia.

Day 4: Visit the Archaeological Museum of Naxos and explore the Castle.

Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the villages of Halki and Filoti.

Day 6: Relax on Agios Prokopios Beach and go kitesurfing.

Day 7: Take a sunset horseback ride and enjoy a wine tasting at Moraitis Winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Naxos

Naxos is known for its rich culinary traditions, and visitors should not miss the opportunity to sample the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Naxos potato: This unique potato has a delicious, buttery flavor and is served in many dishes on the island.

  • Kitron Liquor: This is a traditional liqueur made from citron leaves. It is served as an aperitif or digestif.

  • Kokkinisto: Kokkinisto is a hearty stew made with beef or veal, tomato sauce, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Citron liqueur: This liqueur is made from citron leaves and has a sweet, refreshing taste.

  • Naxos wine: Naxos has several wineries that produce excellent wines, including Assyrtiko, Malagousia, and Kitrino.

Where to Stay in Naxos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Naxos has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses on the island:

  • Hotel Grotta: This family-run hotel is located in Naxos Town and has beautiful views of the sea.

  • Margo Studios: These spacious studios are situated in the village of Agios Prokopios, just steps from the beach.

  • Kavos Boutique Hotel: Located in the village of Agios Prokopios, this hotel offers luxurious rooms with private balconies and sea views.

  • Nissaki Beach Hotel: This hotel is situated on a private beach and offers guests access to a pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Naxos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Naxos is famous for its unique products and handicrafts, making it the perfect destination for souvenir shopping. Here are some of the local products to look out for:

  • Potatoes and other local produce

  • Citron products, such as jam and liqueur

  • Traditional Greek pottery and ceramics

  • Handmade jewelry and textiles

Local Culture and Customs of Naxos

Naxos has a vibrant culture, and visitors should take the time to participate in the island’s customs and traditions. Here are some of the customs and traditions unique to Naxos:

  • The Carnival of Naxos: In February or March, the island comes alive with colorful costumes and parades.

  • Saint George’s Day: On April 23, the villagers of Galanado celebrate Saint George with music, dancing, and food.

Best Time to Visit Naxos

The best time to visit Naxos is from May to October, with the peak tourist season being July and August. The weather is warm and sunny during these months, making it perfect for outdoor activities and beach fun.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naxos

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take in the stunning natural beauty of the island.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect historical monuments and sites.

  • Swim in non-designated areas.

  • Overlook the smaller villages on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naxos

Naxos is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and a welcoming local community. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as watching their belongings and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Naxos

The healthcare system in Naxos is modern and of an excellent standard. Visitors should take standard precautions such as drinking bottled water and wearing sunscreen while exploring the island.

Getting to Naxos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naxos

The closest airport to Naxos is the Naxos Island National Airport, which offers flights from Athens and other nearby islands.

Bus Stations Near Naxos

The central bus station in Naxos Town connects the island’s villages and towns.

Other Options

Ferries and high-speed catamarans operate regularly to and from Athens, Santorini, and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Naxos

The easiest and most convenient way to get around Naxos is by car or motorbike rental. Taxis and buses are also available, but they can be less frequent and reliable than a private rental.

Where to Go Next from Naxos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore beyond Naxos, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • The island of Paros: Located just a short ferry ride away from Naxos, Paros has a charming and relaxed atmosphere, with beautiful beaches and picturesque villages.

  • The Small Cyclades: A group of four islands located south of Naxos, the Small Cyclades boast pristine beaches, clear waters, and a laid-back vibe.

  • Santorini: One of the most iconic destinations in Greece, Santorini offers stunning views of the Aegean sea, unique architecture, and world-class dining and shopping.

Conclusion

Naxos is a fascinating island that offers a perfect mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventure, relaxation, or a combination of both, Naxos has something to offer everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your dream trip to this hidden gem of the Greek Islands.
