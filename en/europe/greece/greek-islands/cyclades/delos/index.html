Delos is a small rocky island in the Cyclades group of Greek islands which has a rich history and is full of ancient ruins. It is located near the center of the Aegean Sea, just a few miles away from Mykonos. Since ancient times, Delos has been considered a sacred island, and it was believed to be the birthplace of Apollo and Artemis. Today, Delos is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular tourist attractions in Greece. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your visit to Delos.

Introduction to Delos

Delos is a fascinating island with a rich history. It has been a place of worship for centuries and was once one of the most important religious centers in Greece. Today, it is home to some of the best-preserved ancient ruins in the country.

The island has a small population and no permanent residents, so you won’t find any accommodations here. Instead, tourists come here on a day-trip from nearby Mykonos, which is just a short boat ride away. The best time to visit Delos is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Delos

Delos has a lot of sites to explore, including ancient ruins, museums, and scenic viewpoints. Here’s a list of the top places to visit on the island.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Terrace of the Lions: This is the most iconic landmark of Delos. The Terrace of the Lions is a row of marble lions that once guarded the Sacred Way leading to the sanctuary of Apollo.

  2. The Temple of Apollo: This was one of the most important religious sites in ancient Greece, and it was believed to be the birthplace of Apollo and Artemis. The temple was destroyed several times over the centuries, but its ruins are still visible today.

  3. The Delos Archaeological Museum: This museum houses some of the most important artifacts found on the island, including sculptures, pottery, and mosaics.

Natural Attractions of Delos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Kynthos: This is the highest point on Delos, and it offers panoramic views of the island and the surrounding sea.

  2. The Agora of the Italians: This is a large open area with stunning views of the sea. It was once a marketplace for Italian merchants.

  3. The Sacred Lake: This is a serene pond surrounded by temples and other ruins. It was believed to be a place of purification in ancient times.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delos

Delos is a small island, but there are still plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. There are several trails that lead to scenic spots on the island, including Mount Kynthos and the Agora of the Italians. Visitors can rent bikes or take a guided tour to explore the island on foot.

Best Things to Do in Delos: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the ancient ruins and hiking trails, there are plenty of other things to do on Delos. Here are some ideas:

  1. Take a guided tour of the island: This is a great way to learn about the history and culture of Delos.

  2. Attend a cultural event: Delos hosts several festivals and cultural events throughout the year, including concerts, theater performances, and dance shows.

  3. Relax on the beach: Delos has several beautiful beaches, including the popular St. Anne’s Beach and the secluded Kryftos Beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delos

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Delos, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Mykonos, take a ferry to Delos, explore the ancient ruins and museums.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Mount Kynthos for stunning views of the island and the Aegean Sea.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the island and learn about the history and culture of Delos.

Day 4: Relax on the beach and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 5: Visit some of the lesser-known ruins on the island, including the House of Dionysus and the House of Cleopatra.

Day 6: Attend a cultural event or festival on Delos.

Day 7: Take a day-trip to nearby Mykonos and explore the town and beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Delos

While there are no restaurants on Delos, visitors can find a few cafes and snack bars near the ancient ruins. Here are some of the most popular dishes and beverages to try on the island.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Moussaka: This traditional Greek dish is made with layers of eggplant, minced lamb, and creamy béchamel sauce.

  2. Souvlaki: These are small skewers of grilled meat, typically served with pita bread and tzatziki sauce.

  3. Fava: This is a delicious dip made from yellow split peas, onions, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  1. Retsina: This is a traditional Greek wine that is flavored with resin. It has a unique taste that is both sweet and savory.

  2. Ouzo: This well-known Greek spirit is made from aniseed and is a popular drink for celebrations.

Where to Stay in Delos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Since Delos has no permanent residents, there are no accommodations on the island. Visitors typically stay in nearby Mykonos, which has a range of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to choose from. Some popular options include the Belvedere Hotel, the San Giorgio Mykonos, and the Myconian Imperial.

What to Buy in Delos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Delos is not known for its shopping scene, but visitors can find a few shops near the ancient ruins. Some popular souvenirs include handmade jewelry, pottery, and olive oil soap. Visitors should also look for local products like honey, cheese, and wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Delos

Delos has a rich cultural history and is considered a sacred island. Visitors should be respectful of the ancient ruins and follow the dress code when visiting religious sites. It’s also important to be mindful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Delos

The best time to visit Delos is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. The island can get very hot and busy during the summer months, so it’s best to avoid that time if possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delos

Do:

  • Do wear comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.
  • Do bring sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water for staying hydrated.
  • Do take a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of the island.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the ancient ruins.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Don’t swim in the Sacred Lake or other prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Delos

Delos is a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas, and avoid walking alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Delos

Delos is a relatively small island with limited medical facilities. Visitors with medical conditions should bring any necessary medications and consult their doctor before traveling to Delos.

Getting to Delos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Delos is accessible by ferry from nearby Mykonos. Visitors can take a ferry from Mykonos port to Delos, which takes about 30 minutes. There are several ferries throughout the day, and the timetable may change depending on the season.

Airports Near Delos

The nearest airport to Delos is Mykonos International Airport, which is just a short ferry ride away.

Bus Stations Near Delos

There are no bus stations on Delos.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private boat or yacht to Delos, or they can join a guided tour that includes transportation to the island.

How to Get Around in Delos

Delos is a small island, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. There are no cars or other vehicles on the island, so hiking and biking are popular ways to get around. Visitors can also rent bikes or take a guided tour to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Delos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Delos, visitors can head to nearby Mykonos to enjoy the town’s shopping, dining, and nightlife. Mykonos also has several beautiful beaches to explore, including Paradise Beach and Super Paradise Beach.

Conclusion

Delos is a unique and fascinating destination for travelers interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re exploring ancient ruins or relaxing on the beach, there’s something for everyone to enjoy on this beautiful Greek island.
