A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Kea, Greece

Kea Island sits in the heart of the Cyclades - a group of Greek Islands in the Aegean Sea - providing visitors with stunning landscapes, cultural and historical marvels, a great variety of water and outdoor activities, and culinary gems. Dubbed as a hidden gem of the Cyclades, this island is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for authentic Greek experiences away from the crowds. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore Kea in depth and showcase everything you need to know before setting off on the journey.

Introduction to Kea

Kea, also known as Tzia, covers an area of 131 square kilometers and has a population of around 2,500 people. It is an under-the-radar island, meaning that it’s less touristy and more laid-back, providing a glimpse into Greek life, culture, and traditions. Kea impresses visitors with its stony hills, emerald waters, and verdant vegetation while creating a serene and relaxing atmosphere that supplies an ideal escape from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Kea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kea, known for its ancient civilization, is an ideal destination for cultural enthusiasts. Here are the top places that showcase the island’s rich history:

  • Karthaia Archaeological Site: Karthaia is a significant site located on the southeastern coast of the island. Explore the remains of two ancient cities - Poiessa and Karthaia - while enjoying a panoramic view of the Aegean Sea and the island of Andros.
  • Kea Archaeological Museum: This museum provides visitors with an insight into the island’s history and culture, presenting impressive collections of artifacts from excavations on the island.
  • Lion of Kea: One of the most famous landmarks of the island, this preserved colossal marble lion sculpture made in the 6th century BCE, is a must-visit. It can be found at the entrance of Liontas, a notable site for Mycenaean findings.

Natural Attractions of Kea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kea’s diverse landscapes offer outdoor enthusiasts a wide range of experiences. From scenic coastal paths and verdant valleys to hidden beaches and crystal-clear waters, Kea has it all. Here are the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Koundouros Beach: Located on the West coast, this beach boasts crystal-clear waters, white sands, and an ideal bay for water sports enthusiasts.
  • Kaliskia Beach: A serene and peaceful beach located in the northeastern part of the island. It is ideal for those seeking solitude and relaxation.
  • Hiking Trails: If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, Kea provides an array of options. Hike to the ruins of the ancient city of Karthaia or walk through olive groves, vineyards, and oak woods to discover the island’s hidden coves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kea

Kea has an extensive network of hiking and biking routes, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are the top routes to explore:

  • Hiking Route Ioulida- Otzias: This route starts from the island’s capital, Ioulida, and leads to Otzias beach on the northeastern coast, taking you through a stunning landscape of oak forests, valleys, villages, and panoramic views.
  • Hiking Route Karthaia: If you want to explore the ancient city of Karthaia, this route is for you. It leads you through a serene landscape of olive groves and lets you discover ancient ruins of various city structures.
  • Mountain Biking Route Vourkari- Pisses- Episkopi- Pera Meria: Mountain biking fans will enjoy this 17km route that takes you through lush nature and lets you discover the island’s terrain.

Best Things to Do in Kea: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking, biking, and water sports, Kea offers plenty of other activities and tours. Here are the top picks:

  • Scuba Diving in Kea: Explore the island’s marine life and underwater world with a scuba diving tour. Acacia diving center and Poseidon diving academy are offering scuba diving sessions all year round.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook authentic Greek dishes with local chefs and take home the secret recipes of tzatziki, moussaka, and more.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Kea has several family-run vineyards and wineries producing local wine from unique varieties of grapes. Enjoy wine tasting tours and discover the secrets of this rare wine.
  • Sunset or Full Moon Sea Kayaking Tours: Paddle your way to unexplored coves, hidden beaches, and iconic landmarks while enjoying breathtaking sunsets or full moon sceneries.

Food and Drink Scene of Kea

Kea’s food scene has gained popularity in recent years thanks to its authentic cooking methods, fresh ingredients, and creative twists on traditional Greek cuisine. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paspala: A delicious breakfast or snack dish consisting of traditional Greek bread, cheese, tomato, oregano, and olive oil.
  • Fava: A traditional Kea dish made from split peas, caramelized onions, and olive oil, usually served as a starter.
  • Kouneli Sti Gastra: Tender rabbit pieces, slowly cooked with onions, tomato, and cinnamon in a ceramic pot.

Local Beverages

  • Kea Beer: A locally brewed beer made with the freshest water, barley, and hops.
  • Rakomelo: A warm and potent traditional Kea drink made with honey, and Rakí - a local spirit distilled from grapes.

Where to Stay in Kea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kea offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from high-end hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are the top picks:

  • Aigis Suites Kea: A luxurious five-star hotel that provides guests with unparalleled comfort, breathtaking sea views, and exceptional services.
  • Thea Apartments Kea: An outstanding, value-for-money accommodation option that offers comfortable rooms, a hot tub, and a magnificent view over Vourkari Bay.
  • Margkona Studios: A cozy and budget-friendly choice that offers a central location, comfortable rooms, and a traditional Greek ambiance.

What to Buy in Kea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kea has a lot of local products and souvenirs to take back home, including:

  • Kea Honey: Taste the purest honey you’ve ever had, produced from wildflowers of Kea.
  • Keaditis Wine: Discover the island’s unique wine and take some of this rare variety back home.
  • Kea Ceramics and Pottery: Find artistic ceramics and pottery made by local artisans and give your home interior a touch of traditional Greece.

Local Culture and Customs of Kea

Kea has a rich culture and history whose customs and traditions are still practiced today. One notable one is the “Kathara Deftera” - a traditional purification custom celebrated on the first day of Lent, where locals clean their houses thoroughly to welcome the Lent period.

Best Time to Visit Kea

The best time to visit Kea is from late spring to early autumn (May to October). During this time, the island has good weather conditions, plenty of sunshine, and moderate temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kea

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Try the local food and wine
  • Visit Kea’s cultural and historical attractions
  • Spend time exploring the island’s natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disturb the peace and serenity of the island.
  • Dress inappropriately while visiting religious and historical sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Kea

Kea is generally a safe destination, but it’s always advisable to take usual precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding dark alleys at night.

Health in Kea

There are no particular health concerns for Kea, but it’s always best to take necessary precautions and have your travel insurance before you arrive.

Getting to Kea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kea

The nearest airport is Athens International Airport (Eleftherios Venizelos), which is approximately 30 kilometers from the port of Lavrio, where FerryBoats depart four to five times a day for Kea.

Bus Stations Near Kea

If you’re traveling from Athens, you can take a bus from the Athens Liossion Station to Lavrio, where you can take a ferry to Kea.

Other Options

It’s also possible to rent a car or hire a taxi from Athens and drive to Lavrio.

How to Get Around in Kea

There are several ways to get around Kea, including:

  • By Car or Scooter: Renting a car or a scooter is the easiest way to get around the island, allowing you to explore it at your own pace.
  • On Foot: Kea is a small island, and most of the attractions and beaches are reachable on foot.
  • By Bus: Public buses connect the island’s main villages and beaches. The schedule varies from one season to another, so it’s best to check with the driver for updated information.

Where to Go Next from Kea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more secluded beaches, charming villages, and unique experiences, here are the nearby destinations you should consider visiting:

  • Kythnos: Known for its mineral springs and sandy beaches, Kythnos is an ideal destination to relax and explore authentic Greek villages.
  • Andros: The second-largest island of the Cyclades is famous for its aristocratic architecture, hiking trails, and stunning beaches.
  • Sounion: A peninsula situated southeast of Athens, famous for its iconic Temple of Poseidon, offering breathtaking sunsets and scenic views of the Aegean Sea.

Conclusion

Kea is undoubtedly one of Greece’s hidden gems, providing a unique blend of culture, history, adventure, and Greek hospitality. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip and discover everything this hidden paradise has to offer.
