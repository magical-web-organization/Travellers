Milos is a small island located in the Cyclades, one of the most breathtaking groups of islands in the Greek archipelago. Despite being one of the lesser-known islands, Milos has a lot to offer to visitors, including a diverse range of natural beauty, stunning beaches, impressive cultural heritage, unique cuisine, and a rich local tradition.

Introduction to Milos

Milos Island is a true gem, with a volcanic landscape rich in minerals, turquoise waters, and white-washed villages. The island offers its visitors a relaxed atmosphere, friendly locals, delicious food, and a wealth of historical sights and natural wonders to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Milos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Milos Island is steeped in history and boasts numerous important cultural attractions from ancient times to the modern era.

Archaeological Museum of Milos

The Archaeological Museum of Milos offers visitors a fascinating glimpse into the island’s rich history. The museum is located in the center of Plaka and houses artifacts from the prehistoric times to the classical era.

Catacombs of Milos

The Catacombs of Milos are ancient underground cemeteries located in the town of Trypiti. These catacombs date back to the first century AD and were in use until the fifth century AD.

Folklore Museum of Milos

The Folklore Museum of Milos illustrates the traditional customs, costumes, and everyday life of the Milos people. This museum is located in the beautiful village of Plaka and showcases various art exhibits of local artisans.

Natural Attractions of Milos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Milos is a paradise for nature lovers, with its unique landscape, and diverse terrain. Visitors can explore the island’s natural beauty from the land, sea, and air, with countless indoor and outdoor activities to choose from.

Sarakiniko Beach

Sarakiniko Beach is one of the most unique beaches in Milos Island and offers visitors a unique lunar-like landscape. The beach boasts smooth white rock formations, crystal clear turquoise waters, and provides visitors with an unforgettable swimming experience.

Kleftiko Beach

Kleftiko is only reachable by boat but it is one of the most stunning attractions in Milos. Its crystal-clear waters and white rock pillars are a must-visit! Visitors can go on a boat trip from Milos and visit this beautiful and isolated beach.

Plathienia Beach

Plathienia Beach is located near the traditional fishing village of Skinopi and offers stunning beach views in a tranquil setting. Visitors can take a walk from the village or hire a taxi to reach the beach and enjoy its crystal-clear water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milos

Milos has an extensive network of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the island’s diverse terrain and soak in its natural beauty. The trails offer visitors the opportunity to explore the island’s exceptional landscape, visit local villages and archaeological sites, and take in stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Milos: Activities and Tours

Milos offers an exciting range of activities and tours to suit every taste and budget. Visitors can choose from boat trips, guided walking tours, and off-road jeep safaris that will take them off the beaten track.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milos

Day 1: Explore Plaka. Day 2: Visit the beaches of Sarakiniko and Kleftiko. Day 3: Explore Milos historic sites, and spend time at Paliochori beach. Day 4: Go on a boat tour to see the island’s sea caves and beaches. Day 5: Visit the Folklore Museum and enjoy the sunset on the Venus de Milo. Day 6: Go on a guided walk of the island and visit catacombs and ancient ruins. Day 7: Take a day trip to neighboring islands such as Kimolos.

Food and Drink Scene of Milos

Milos offers its visitors a distinctive blend of traditional Greek and Middle Eastern cuisine. Visitors will find a variety of delicious dishes prepared with fresh local ingredients, including seafood, cheese, and fresh produce. Milos’s cuisine also offers a unique selection of traditional desserts and drinks that visitors should not miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Psaropita
  • Crackling Cheese (Sfougato)
  • Spicy Sausage (Ladenia)
  • Roasted Eggplants (Melitzanosalata)

Local Beverages

The island is famous for its sweet white wine that pairs perfectly with seafood. Other local drinks visitors should try include Rakomelo, an herbal liqueur that is perfect for cold nights, and soumada, an almond-flavored drink that is perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Milos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milos Island offers an extensive range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors can choose from a range of budget and luxury options that suit their preferences and budget.

What to Buy in Milos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milos Island has a wide selection of traditional handicrafts and local products that visitors can bring home as souvenirs. Some of the most popular souvenirs to take back home include handmade ceramics, embroidered linens, local cheeses & honey, and bottled wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Milos

Milos has a rich tradition that dates back thousands of years and is a significant part of local’s daily life. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture and customs by attending local festivals, traditional events, and exploring the island’s many archaeological sites.

Best Time to Visit Milos

The best time to visit Milos is from late May to mid-October. This period offers visitors better weather and warmer temperatures, as well as the chance to experience the island’s numerous festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milos

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the island’s beaches and natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind
  • Neglect to protect yourself from the sun
  • Take photos of locals without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Milos

Milos is a relatively safe destination with low crime rates. Visitors should still take precautions when traveling and be aware of their surroundings at all times, particularly when visiting remote areas.

Health in Milos

Visitors are advised to take standard precautions to avoid common illnesses such as sunstroke, dehydration, and insect bites. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before traveling to Milos.

Getting to Milos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Milos

Milos is served by Milos Island National Airport, which has daily flights to Athens and other Greek islands.

Bus Stations Near Milos

Milos has a bus station located in Adamas harbor which offers routes to the main villages of the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Milos by ferry or catamaran from Athens, Santorini, and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Milos

Milos has an efficient public transportation system, including a reliable bus network, taxis, and rental cars. Visitors can also explore the island on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Milos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Milos is well-positioned for visitors to explore other islands in the Cyclades, such as Santorini, Mylos, and Naxos.

Conclusion

Milos Island is a hidden gem in Greece that is well worth a visit. With its rich history, stunning beaches, unique natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, it is an excellent travel destination for anyone seeking a relaxed and laid-back experience.
