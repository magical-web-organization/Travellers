Introduction to Megalochori

Nestled in the heart of Santorini in the Cyclades of the Greek Islands, Megalochori is a charming village with a rich cultural history and breathtaking natural beauty. This traditional village boasts a unique mix of Greek and Venetian architecture, with white-washed houses, narrow alleys, and picturesque courtyards that make it an ideal destination for those seeking a romantic and peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Megalochori

Santorini is famous for its dramatic landscape, gorgeous beaches, and stunning sunsets, but Megalochori offers its own unique attractions for visitors. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Megalochori.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Explore Megalochori’s rich history and culture through the village’s museums and historical landmarks.

  • The Bellonio Cultural Foundation: This museum showcases a collection of traditional clothing, books, photographs, and artifacts that tell the story of Santorini’s cultural heritage.
  • The Wine Museum Koutsoyannopoulos: Learn about the history of wine production in Santorini and sample some of the excellent wines produced on the island.
  • The Megalochori Caldera Viewpoint: Get a panoramic view of the Caldera, a stunning natural geological feature that Santorini is famous for.

Natural Attractions of Megalochori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Megalochori’s scenic beauty and natural landscape are perfect for outdoor adventurers and nature lovers.

  • The Santorini Arts Factory: A contemporary arts and cultural center that hosts regular exhibitions, musical performances, and plays.
  • The Akrotiri Archaeological Site: This archaeological site dates back to the Bronze Age and provides a glimpse into the ancient civilization that once inhabited Santorini.
  • The Perissa and Kamari beaches: Megalochori is located near some of Santorini’s finest beaches, which are known for their black volcanic sand and crystal clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Megalochori

Hiking and biking are fantastic ways to discover the beautiful surroundings of Megalochori. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes.

  • The Santorini Fira-Oia Hiking Trail: This trail winds through picturesque villages, vineyards, and along the caldera, offering incredible views of the island’s unique landscape.
  • The Ancient Thira Hiking Trail: This 3.7 km trail takes you to the top of Mesa Vouno, an archeological site that offers a glimpse into the island’s rich history.
  • The Akrotiri to Red Beach Biking Route: This scenic route takes you along the coast of the island, offering stunning views of the caldera and the Aegean Sea.

Best Things to Do in Megalochori: Activities and Tours

Megalochori offers many exciting activities and tours that will delight visitors of all ages.

  • Cruise to the Caldera: This boat tour offers breathtaking views of the island’s famous caldera, a geological formation that is the result of a volcanic eruption.
  • Greek Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Greek dishes with a local chef and then enjoy your meal overlooking the Caldera.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Sample some of Santorini’s best wines with a local expert, who will guide you through the unique wine-making process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Megalochori

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for those traveling to Megalochori:

  • Day 1: Explore Megalochori’s quaint streets, visit the Bellonio Cultural Foundation, and enjoy a traditional Greek lunch at one of the village’s charming tavernas.
  • Day 2: Discover the archaeological site of Akrotiri, followed by a leisurely afternoon on Perissa beach.
  • Day 3: Take a scenic hike along the Fira-Oia hiking trail, stopping at traditional villages and sampling delicious local food along the way.
  • Day 4: Soak up the culture of the island with a wine-tasting tour and an evening at the Santorini Arts Factory.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a day on Kamari beach, followed by a seafood dinner overlooking the Caldera.
  • Day 6: Experience the thrill of riding ATV bikes along the coastline, with a stop at the beautiful Red Beach.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Megalochori’s luxurious spas, followed by a romantic sunset dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Megalochori

The food and drink scene in Megalochori is a mix of traditional Greek dishes and modern culinary delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Santorini fava: A creamy, yellow split pea hummus, typically served as a dip.
  • Pork souvlaki: Pieces of grilled pork skewered on a stick and typically served with pita bread and tzatziki.
  • Tomatokeftedes: Tomato fritters typically served with a lemon dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Assyrtiko wine: A crisp, dry white wine that is unique to Santorini and pairs well with seafood and salads.
  • Ouzo: A clear, anise-flavored liquor that is typically served as an aperitif.
  • Frappé: A refreshing, frothy, and icy Greek coffee drink.

Where to Stay in Megalochori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Megalochori has many accommodation options that cater to a range of tastes and budgets.

  • Athermi Suites: A luxurious hotel that offers magnificent Caldera views, private pools, spa treatments, and delicious food.
  • Meli Meli: A beautiful, budget-friendly boutique hotel with charming decor, comfortable beds, and friendly staff.
  • Chelidonia Villas: A collection of stunning, traditional villas with private pools, outdoor hot tubs, and breathtaking Caldera views.

What to Buy in Megalochori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Megalochori has several shops that sell local products and souvenirs to take home with you.

  • Kastania Extra Virgin Olive Oil: Take home some of Megalochori’s finest olive oil, a staple ingredient in Greek cuisine.
  • Traditional Ceramics: Visit the local ceramics workshops and pick out a unique piece to bring home, such as bowls, plates, and vases.
  • Local Wines: Bring home a bottle or two of Santorini’s famed Assyrtiko white wine, which pairs perfectly with seafood and Greek salads.

Local Culture and Customs of Megalochori

The people of Megalochori are proud of their rich cultural heritage, and visitors to the village should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some helpful tips.

  • Dress Conservatively: In respect of local customs, it’s best to wear conservative clothing (covering the shoulders and knees) when visiting churches or other places of worship.
  • Be Respectful: Megalochori is a peaceful village, and visitors should respect the locals and their way of life.
  • Learn Some Greek Phrases: While many locals speak English, making an effort to learn a few basic Greek phrases will show your respect for the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Megalochori

The best time to visit Megalochori is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny, with temperatures ranging from 20 – 30°C. July and August are peak seasons, with high temperatures, large crowds, and higher prices. Visit during the shoulder seasons (April to June and September to October) for more affordable prices, fewer crowds, and cooler weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Megalochori

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Sample the local food and wine.
  • Learn some basic Greek phrases.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Inappropriately expose yourself at the beach or in public areas.
  • Bargain at local shops or restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Megalochori

Megalochori is a safe and friendly village for visitors, with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take standard travel precautions, such as keeping track of personal belongings and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Megalochori

Megalochori has medical facilities ensuring quality treatments if need may arise. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for emergency services.

Getting to Megalochori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Megalochori is conveniently located on Santorini Island, making it easy to get to. Here are some of the transportation options to consider.

Airports Near Megalochori

The nearest airport is Santorini’s Thira National Airport which is about 15km from Megalochori. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, rent a car or take a bus to the village.

Bus Stations Near Megalochori

Visitors can also reach Megalochori by bus, with regular bus services available from Thira and other parts of the island.

Other Options

There are also ferry services that operate from the mainland of Greece to Santorini’s main port, Athinios. Visitors can then take a bus or taxi from the port to the village.

How to Get Around in Megalochori

Megalochori is a small village, and it’s easy to explore it on foot. There are also car, scooter, and ATV rentals available for visitors who want to explore the island and its nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Megalochori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Megalochori is located in the heart of Santorini, making it an ideal base for exploring the island and its nearby attractions. Here are some of the popular places to visit nearby.

  • Fira: Santorini’s largest town with a vibrant nightlife, stunning Caldera views, and fantastic restaurants.
  • Oia: A picturesque village famous for its stunning sunsets and blue-domed chapels.
  • Pyrgos: A quaint, traditional village that is famous for its castle ruins and panoramic views of the island.

Conclusion

Explore the charming village of Megalochori with our comprehensive travel guide. Discover the village’s unique mix of cultural heritage, breathtaking natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. Plan your trip now for an unforgettable experience in one of Santorini’s most beautiful destinations.
