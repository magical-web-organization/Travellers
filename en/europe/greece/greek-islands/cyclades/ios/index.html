Introduction to Ios

Ios is a small island located in the Cyclades group of the Aegean Sea, in Greece. It is known for its stunning beaches, picturesque villages, and vibrant nightlife. Ios is often called the party island of Greece, but there is much more to explore and experience than just partying. This travel guide will help you discover all the hidden gems that Ios has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ios

From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and scenic views, there is something for everyone in Ios. Here are the must-see places you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Homer’s Tomb: According to Greek mythology, Homer, the poet who wrote the Iliad and the Odyssey, was buried in Ios. You can visit his tomb and pay your respects to this literary legend.

  • Chora: Ios’ main town, Chora, is a maze of winding streets and whitewashed houses that offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea. It is also home to some of the island’s best restaurants, bars, and shops.

  • Panagia Gremiotissa Monastery: This 18th-century monastery sits on top of a hill overlooking Chora and offers breathtaking views of the island.

Natural Attractions of Ios: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manganari Beach: This secluded beach on the south coast of Ios is often named as one of the best in Greece. It has crystal-clear waters and a sandy shore that stretches for miles.

  • Psathi Beach: This quiet and peaceful beach has turquoise waters and is surrounded by cliffs covered in vegetation.

  • Koubara Cave: This cave offers an exciting adventure for those who love hiking and exploration. The cave has a natural pool where you can swim, and you can also climb down to the sea from the cave’s entrance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ios

If you love hiking or biking, Ios offers some fantastic opportunities to explore the island’s rugged landscape. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Ios Trail: This 15-kilometer trail connects some of the most beautiful villages and beaches on the island. It takes about 5 hours to complete, and you can also hire a guide to make the most of your experience.

  • Homers’ Path: This trail leads to Homer’s tomb and offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea. It is a relatively easy walk that takes about 1.5 hours.

  • Panagia Gremiotissa Monastery Trail: This trail leads from Chora to the monastery on top of the hill. It is a steep climb, but the views are worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Ios: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the natural beauty of Ios, there are also plenty of activities and tours you can do. Here are some of the best:

  • Boat Tours: Ios has many beautiful beaches and coves that are only accessible by boat. You can take a boat tour and explore the coastline, swim in secluded beaches, and enjoy the stunning scenery.

  • Scuba Diving: The crystal-clear waters around Ios are perfect for scuba diving. You can explore colourful marine life, ancient shipwrecks, and impressive rock formations.

  • Nightlife: Ios is known for its vibrant nightlife. There are many bars and clubs where you can dance the night away and enjoy live music.

Food and Drink Scene of Ios

Ios has a rich culinary culture, and its cuisine is influenced by the island’s history and geography. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ladotyri: This is a hard cheese made with olive oil and is a local specialty in Ios.

  • Revithada: This is a chickpea stew that is slow-cooked with onions, garlic, and tomatoes.

  • Stuffed Tomatoes: These are tomatoes stuffed with rice, herbs, and sometimes meat or seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: This is a traditional Greek drink made with anise and is often served with meze (small plates of food).

  • Assyrtiko: This is a white wine made from the Assyrtiko grape, which is native to Santorini but is also grown on Ios.

  • Rakomelo: This is a hot, sweet, and spicy drink made with raki (a distilled spirit) and honey.

Where to Stay in Ios: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ios offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Liostasi Hotel & Suites: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea and has a spa, a pool, and a restaurant.

  • Far Out Beach Club: This beachfront hostel offers dorm rooms and private rooms and has a pool, a nightclub, and a restaurant.

  • Armadoros Hotel: This family-run hotel is located near Mylopotas Beach and offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Ios: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Ios, here are some of the best local products to buy:

  • Handcrafted Ceramics: Ios has a long tradition of pottery, and you can find beautiful handcrafted ceramics in many shops.

  • Olive Oil: Ios produces some of the best olive oil in Greece, and you can buy it in local shops and markets.

  • Local Wines and Spirits: Ios has a growing wine industry, and you can find local wines and spirits in many shops and restaurants.

Local Culture and Customs of Ios

Ios has a rich cultural history, and its people are proud of their traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when you visit:

  • Churches and Monasteries: Ios has many churches and monasteries, and it’s important to dress appropriately when you visit them. Men should wear long pants, and women should cover their shoulders.

  • Language: Greek is the official language, but many people speak English, especially in tourist areas.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Greece, but it is customary to leave a small amount of change (around 10%) at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Ios

The best time to visit Ios is from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry, and the island is bustling with tourists. July and August are the peak tourist months, and the island can get crowded. If you prefer a quieter experience, consider visiting in May or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ios

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when you visit Ios:

Do:

  • Explore the island’s natural beauty: Ios has some of the most beautiful beaches and scenery in Greece, so make sure you take advantage of it.

  • Try the local cuisine: Ios has a rich culinary tradition, and its food is delicious. Make sure you try the local dishes and beverages.

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Greeks are proud of their cultural heritage, so make sure you respect their customs and follow local etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Drink and Drive: Greek law has strict penalties for driving under the influence of alcohol, so never drink and drive.

  • Be disrespectful to locals: Greeks are friendly and hospitable people, but they also take pride in their traditions and culture. Be respectful and courteous to locals.

  • Litter: Ios is a beautiful island, and it’s important to keep it clean. Don’t litter, and dispose of your trash properly.

Safety and Health

Ios is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always important to take precautions when you travel. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Ios

  • Watch out for traffic: Ios has narrow roads and many motorbikes, so make sure you stay alert when walking or driving.

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like in any tourist destination, there is a risk of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Keep your belongings safe and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Ios

  • Sun protection: The sun can be intense in Greece, especially during the summer months. Make sure you wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.

  • Stay hydrated: Make sure you drink plenty of water, especially if you’re spending time outside or doing physical activities.

  • Stay safe in the water: The currents and waves around Ios can sometimes be strong, so make sure you swim in designated areas and follow lifeguard instructions.

Getting to Ios: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ios is well connected to other islands and mainland Greece, and there are several transportation options available. Here’s how to get there:

  • By Air: The closest airport to Ios is Santorini National Airport, which is served by several airlines. From Santorini, you can take a ferry to Ios.

  • By Ferry: Ios is connected to several other Cyclades islands, including Santorini and Mykonos, as well as the mainland port of Piraeus. Ferries are the most common way to get to Ios.

Airports Near Ios

  • Santorini National Airport

Bus Stations Near Ios

There are no bus stations on Ios, but there are bus stops in Chora and other villages.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are available on the island, but they can be expensive.

  • Rental Cars and Scooters: You can rent a car or scooter on the island, but it’s important to have a valid driver’s license and insurance.

How to Get Around in Ios

Ios is a small island, and many of the sights and attractions are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore further afield, there are several transportation options available:

  • Buses: There are buses that run between Chora and other villages, as well as to the main beaches on the island.

  • Taxis: Taxis are available on the island, but they can be expensive.

  • Rental Cars and Scooters: You can rent a car or scooter on the island, but it’s important to have a valid driver’s license and insurance.

Where to Go Next from Ios: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Greece after your visit to Ios, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Santorini: This iconic island is known for its stunning sunsets, white-washed houses, and blue-domed churches. It’s located about 2 hours by ferry from Ios.

  • Mykonos: This popular destination is known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and cosmopolitan atmosphere. It’s located about 2 hours by ferry from Ios.

  • Cruise to Delos: Delos is a nearby island that is home to ancient Greek ruins and archaeological sites. You can take a day trip from Ios to Delos and explore this historic site.

Conclusion

Ios is a beautiful and exciting destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking to party the night away, explore ancient ruins, or simply relax on the beach, Ios has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect vacation to this enchanting Greek island.
