Introduction to Kavos

Kavos is a beautiful beach resort located on the southern coast of Corfu, in the Ionian Islands of Greece. Its stunning sandy beaches, crystalline waters, and lively nightlife make it a top destination for travelers looking for a fun and sun-filled vacation. Kavos is also home to natural wonders, ancient landmarks, and traditional customs that are worth exploring. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll share everything you need to know to make the most of your stay in Kavos.

List of Places to Visit in Kavos

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a party animal, Kavos has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit attractions that you shouldn’t miss during your stay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kavos’s Maritime Museum: A small museum showcasing the island’s seafaring heritage.
  • Achilleion Palace: A 19th-century palace built by Emperor Franz Joseph I of Austria. It features ornate architecture and stunning gardens.
  • Old Town of Corfu: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Corfu’s historic center boasts Venetian, French, and British influences, as well as narrow alleyways, charming squares, and elegant mansions.

Natural Attractions of Kavos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Asprokavos: The southernmost point of Corfu, Cape Asprokavos offers spectacular views of the Ionian Sea.
  • Lake Korission: A large lagoon and nature reserve that’s home to rare bird species and unique wildlife.
  • Korission Beach: A serene and unspoiled beach, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kavos

The scenic trails and bike routes of Kavos are a great way to explore the island’s natural beauty and enjoy some exercise. Some popular routes include:

  • The Corfu Trail: A 220-km long-distance route that crosses the island, passing through scenic villages, olive groves, and stunning landscapes.
  • Asprokavos Trail: A short but challenging hiking trail that leads to Cape Asprokavos and offers breathtaking views of the coastline.
  • Kavos to Lefkimmi Trail: A scenic bike route that follows the coast from Kavos to Lefkimmi and passes through quaint villages and peaceful beaches.

Best Things to Do in Kavos: Activities and Tours

Kavos offers plenty of activities and tours that will keep you entertained and engaged throughout your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Kavos:

  • Beach Activities: Swim, snorkel, or sunbathe on one of Kavos’s many beautiful beaches, such as Kavos Beach, Agios Petros Beach, or Arkoudilas Beach.
  • Watersports: Try exciting water sports like jet skiing, paddleboarding, parasailing, or windsurfing.
  • Boat Tours: Explore the coast and nearby islands with a boat tour that includes swimming stops, snorkeling, and lunch.
  • Party Nights: Kavos has a legendary nightlife scene with numerous bars, clubs, and beach parties that go on until the early morning hours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kavos

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kavos, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Arrival in Kavos, check-in at your hotel, and explore the town center
2 Beach day at Kavos Beach or Agios Petros Beach
3 Boat tour to nearby islands, lunch on board, and swimming stops
4 Hiking/biking tour of the Corfu Trail or Asprokavos Trail
5 Visit to Achilleion Palace and explore Corfu Old Town
6 Watersports and party night at one of Kavos’s many clubs
7 Relaxing day at Korission Beach or Lake Korission, dinner at a local taverna

Food and Drink Scene of Kavos

Kavos has a rich culinary tradition influenced by Greek, Italian, and Mediterranean cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvlaki: Grilled skewers of marinated meat (usually pork or chicken), served with pita bread, tzatziki, and salad.
  • Moussaka: A baked casserole dish made with layers of eggplant, potatoes, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.
  • Pastitsada: A spicy pasta dish made with beef or chicken, tomatoes, onions, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A strong anise-flavored spirit that’s usually served as an aperitif or with meze (small dishes).
  • Tsipouro: A distilled spirit made from pomace (grape residues) that’s similar to Italian grappa.
  • Corfiot Beer: Kavos has its own brewery, producing fresh and tasty beer that you can sample at local bars and tavernas.

Where to Stay in Kavos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kavos offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mayor Capo di Corfu: A five-star hotel that’s located right on the beach and features a spa, a swimming pool, and all-inclusive packages.
  • Lemon Grove Hotels & Villas: A three-star hotel that’s nestled in a beautiful citrus grove and offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Morfeas Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that’s close to the beach and nightlife spots, with friendly staff and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Kavos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kavos has many souvenir shops and local markets where you can find unique gifts and products to bring back home. Here are some suggestions:

  • Olive Oil: Corfu is famous for its high-quality extra-virgin olive oil, which you can buy at local shops and markets.
  • Koum Kouat: A traditional liqueur made from the bitter oranges of Corfu, that you can find at the island’s distilleries and shops.
  • Ceramics and Pottery: Many local artisans make beautiful ceramics and pottery pieces inspired by traditional Corfiot patterns and motifs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kavos

Kavos has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that’s shaped by its geography, history, and people. Some of the local customs and traditions that you might encounter during your stay include:

  • Religious Festivals: Kavos celebrates several religious festivals throughout the year, such as Easter, St. Spyridon’s Day, and the Assumption of the Virgin Mary.
  • Music and Dance: Greeks love music and dance, and Kavos has a lively music scene with traditional musicians and modern DJs playing a variety of genres.
  • Hospitality: The Greeks are known for their warmth, generosity, and hospitality, so be prepared to be welcomed with open arms wherever you go.

Best Time to Visit Kavos

The best time to visit Kavos is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and nightlife are in full swing. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and cooler weather, you can also consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of May and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kavos

Here are some tips on what to do and not to do during your stay in Kavos:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when going to the beach.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Get involved in illegal activities or drug use.
  • Be disrespectful or offensive to the locals or other tourists.

Safety and Health

Kavos is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take some precautions to ensure your well-being. Here are some tips:

Safety in Kavos

  • Watch your belongings and don’t leave valuables unattended.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Use common sense and avoid risky behaviors that could put you in danger.

Health in Kavos

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Wear insect repellent, especially during the summer months.
  • Keep your medication and first aid kit handy.

Getting to Kavos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kavos is well-connected to the rest of Corfu and Greece, with several transportation options available. Here are some ways to get to Kavos:

Airports Near Kavos

  • Corfu International Airport: The closest airport to Kavos, located about 40 km north. It’s served by many airlines, including Ryanair and EasyJet.

Bus Stations Near Kavos

  • Lefkimmi Bus Station: The bus station that serves Kavos, with connections to Corfu Town and other parts of the island.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car at Corfu International Airport or in other parts of the island, and drive to Kavos.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Corfu, but they can be expensive for long distances.

How to Get Around in Kavos

Kavos is a relatively small town, and you can easily walk or bike around the center and the beaches. However, if you want to explore other parts of the island, you’ll need a car, a scooter, or a bus. Here are some options:

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car at Kavos or other parts of the island and explore at your own pace.
  • Scooter Rental: Scooters are a popular alternative to cars, as they’re more affordable and easy to park.
  • Bus: The local bus service connects Kavos to Corfu Town and other parts of the island, with frequent and affordable routes.

Where to Go Next from Kavos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re staying for longer than a week in Kavos, or if you want to explore more of Corfu and Greece, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Palaiokastritsa: A picturesque village located on the west coast of Corfu, with stunning beaches, coves, and caves.
  • Parga: A charming coastal town on the mainland, with Ottoman and Venetian architecture and a beautiful castle.
  • Albanian Riviera: A scenic coastal region of Albania, located just across the Ionian Sea from Corfu, with pristine beaches and historic towns.

Conclusion

Kavos has something for everyone, from stunning beaches and natural wonders to ancient landmarks and lively nightlife. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has given you a good idea of what to expect and how to plan your trip. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Kavos will welcome you with open arms and memories that will last a lifetime.
