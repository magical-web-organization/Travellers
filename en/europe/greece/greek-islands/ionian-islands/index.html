Introduction to Ionian Islands

Located in the northwest coast of Greece, Ionian Islands is a cluster of seven islands blessed with picture-perfect beaches, crystal-clear waters, olive groves, and lush green hills. The islands are Kerkyra, Paxos, Lefkada, Ithaca, Zakynthos, Kefalonia, and Kythira. Each of these islands has an individual character and attractions, making them a perfect destination for any type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Ionian Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the unique things about Ionian Islands is its rich history and culture. Every island holds a treasure trove of ancient monuments, museums, and historical places worth exploring.

Some of the top culturally important attractions in Ionian Islands are:

  • The Achilleion Palace in Kerkyra: Built-in the tradition of ancient Greek and Roman architecture, the palace was once the summer residence of Queen Elizabeth of Austria.
  • Korgialenio Historic and Folklore Museum of Ithaca: It features antique objects, photographs, and manuscripts that showcase the island’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Castle of Kefalonia: The breathtaking castle displays Frankish, Venetian, and Byzantine influences in its architecture.

Natural Attractions of Ionian Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its cultural attractions, Ionian Islands boasts of outstanding natural wonders which are perfect for those who love the great outdoors.

Here are some of the top natural attractions in Ionian Islands:

  • Shipwreck Beach in Zakynthos: The beach is one of the most photographed attractions in the Ionian Islands with its white sand, turquoise waters, and rusty shipwreck sitting on the shore.
  • Melissani Cave in Kefalonia: A mystical underground sea cave with crystal clear waters, surrounded by lush greenery and stalactites.
  • Egremni Beach in Lefkada: Known for its crystal-clear waters, sandy shores, and stunning cliffs overlooking the Ionian Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ionian Islands

The Ionian Islands are also a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its rugged hills, cobbled paths, and scenic routes. Here are some of the best trails to explore on foot or by bike:

  • Mount Aenos in Kefalonia: A challenging climb that rewards with breathtaking views of the island and the neighboring islands.
  • Corfu Trail in Kerkyra: A 220 km trail that passes through olive groves, forests, and ancient coastal villages.
  • West Coast Trail in Lefkada: A stunning trail that offers unobstructed sea views and rocky terrain ideal for mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Ionian Islands: Activities and Tours

Ionian Islands offer a complete package of fun activities that cater to all ages, interests, and budgets.

Here are some of the top things to do in Ionian Islands:

  • Marine activities: From swimming, snorkeling, scuba diving, and windsurfing, Ionian Islands have a plethora of beaches, coves, and bays that offer plenty of opportunities to enjoy underwater adventures.
  • Boat tours: Exploring Ionian Islands by boat is an excellent way to discover hidden coves, tiny beaches, and sea caves scattered along the coastline.
  • Cultural tours: Ionian Islands have a rich tradition of music, dance, and gastronomy. Take a cultural tour or join a traditional festival to witness the islands’ customs and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ionian Islands

If you’re planning to stay in Ionian Islands for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Kerkyra. Explore the Venetian old town, the Achilleion Palace, and enjoy the sunset at the Old Fortress.
  • Day 3-4: Kefalonia. Visit the Melissani Cave, Myrtos Beach, Mount Aenos and the castle of Kefalonia.
  • Day 5-6: Lefkada. Hike on the west coast trail, bathe in Egremni Beach and visit Nydri Waterfalls.
  • Day 7: Zakynthos. Visit the Shipwreck Beach, the Blue Caves, and the Dionysios Solomos Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Ionian Islands

Greek cuisine is world-famous for its fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and healthy recipes. Ionian Islands offer an exciting culinary scene with dishes that combine traditional Greek recipes with local flavors and ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sofrito in Kerkyra: Beef or veal cooked in white wine with garlic, parsley, and vinegar.
  • Kreatopita in Lefkada: A rustic meat pie with spinach, cheese, and herbs.
  • Kefalonian meat pie in Kefalonia: A savory pastry filled with beef, onions, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Robola wine in Kefalonia: A high-quality white wine made from the Robola grape variety.
  • Kumquat liqueur in Corfu: A sweet and tangy liqueur made from the locally grown Kumquat fruit.

Where to Stay in Ionian Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ionian Islands offer plenty of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels, cozy guesthouses, and apartments.

Here are some of the best places to stay in Ionian Islands:

  • Domes Zeen Chania in Zakynthos: A five-star resort designed for total relaxation and offering stunning views and world-class amenities.
  • The White House Lefkada in Lefkada: A charming guesthouse with traditional features, excellent service, and an excellent central location.
  • Nafsika Hotel in Kerkyra: A budget-friendly hotel with a family-friendly atmosphere, great facilities, and easy access to the city center.

What to Buy in Ionian Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping in Ionian Islands is a delightful experience, with plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the finest local products and souvenirs to bring home:

  • Olive oil from Kefalonia and Lefkada: A high-quality olive oil with a fruity flavor and a long history on the islands.
  • Caramelized Kumquat in Corfu: A sweet treat made from the Kumquat fruit and ideal for pairing with cheese or ice cream.
  • Ladokoulouro bread in Zakynthos: A type of bread made with olive oil and coated in sesame seeds, sold in local bakeries.

Local Culture and Customs of Ionian Islands

The Ionian Islands have a rich and colorful cultural heritage that reflects its diverse history and influences. The local people are warm and hospitable, and they take pride in preserving their customs and traditions.

Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting Ionian Islands:

  • Respect religious sites and customs: Many of the Ionian Islands have religious sites and monuments that are of great importance to the locals. Visitors are requested to dress appropriately and be respectful while visiting.
  • Learn a few words in Greek: The locals appreciate visitors who make an effort to learn a few Greek phrases.
  • Observe quiet hours: Many of the hotels and neighborhoods have designated quiet hours during the afternoon and late at night. Observe them out of courtesy of your fellow travelers and residents.

Best Time to Visit Ionian Islands

The best time to visit Ionian Islands is during the spring (April-June) and fall (September-October) months when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are not as thick as during the peak summer months.

July and August are the peak months of the tourist season, and you can expect to find plenty of visitors, high prices, and crowded beaches and streets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ionian Islands

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts that will help you make the most of your trip to the Ionian Islands:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Learn some basic Greek phrases.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or parks.
  • Enter religious sites without proper attire/manners for reverence.
  • Partake in unethical animal tourism activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ionian Islands

Ionian Islands are generally safe, with low crime rates and no significant health hazards. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, like avoiding isolated locations, carrying a copy of their documents, and being wary of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Ionian Islands

Ionian Islands have modern healthcare facilities and hospitals that offer quality medical care. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance and carry a basic first aid kit for minor injuries or illnesses.

Getting to Ionian Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ionian Islands are relatively easily accessible through various major modes of transportations:

Airports Near Ionian Islands

  • Corfu International Airport (CFU) in Corfu.
  • Zakynthos International Airport (ZTH) in Zakynthos.
  • Kefalonia International Airport (EFL) in Kefalonia.

Bus Stations Near Ionian Islands

  • KTEL Kerkira in Corfu - offers intercity routes from all Corfu’s villages to the capital.
  • KTEL Lefkadas - offers direct routes from Athens, Thesaloniki and other major Greek cities.
  • KTEL Kefalonias - offers extensive services connecting the island’s ports with the main cities on the islands, with connections to Athens, other Greek cities and other islands.

Other Options

  • You can reach the island of Kythira by ferry from Neapoli in the Peloponnese.
  • Savvy travelers can also opt for ferries to move from island to island, including Leivadia-Lefkada, Nydri -Meganisi, and Kyllini Zakynthos.

How to Get Around in Ionian Islands

The best way to get around the Ionian island to island is to rent a car or scooter, as this allows the freedom to explore at your own pace and discover hidden gems and picturesque routes. Additionally, all islands offer a taxi service, bike, and bus as alternative forms of transportations that serve intercity and city areas.

Where to Go Next from Ionian Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more adventure after exploring the Ionian Islands, there are other nearby destinations worth exploring.

Here are some suggestions:

  • Athens: The Greek capital is unmissable, a city steeped in history, with ancient landmarks worth exploring.
  • Mediterranean Cruise: Explore the Mediterranean by ship, stopping at Genoa, Rome, and Barcelona while enjoying the scenery of the high seas.
  • Zagori National Park: Located in the mountains of northern Greece, Zagori National Park is a fascinating destination for adventure seekers with its white-water rafting, hiking. and stunning vistas.

Conclusion

Ionian Islands offer tourists an endless supply of attractions, scenery, and experiences, making them an ideal destination for family holidays, couples getaways, and adventure seeker groups. When planning your visit, consider the itinerary above to make the most out of your trip.
