Introduction to Skala

Skala is a charming coastal town located on the southern coast of Kefalonia, one of the Ionian Islands in Greece. With its picturesque beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking views, Skala has become a popular destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. Whether you want to explore the town’s cultural landmarks, enjoy outdoor activities, or simply soak up the sun on the beach, Skala has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Skala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roman Villa: The Roman Villa is an archaeological site that dates back to the Roman Empire. The ruins of the villa include impressive mosaics, wall paintings, and a bath complex. It’s a great place for history buffs to explore.

  • St. George’s Castle: Located atop a hill, St. George’s Castle is a 16th-century fortress that offers stunning views of the town and the sea. The castle was built by the Venetians to protect the island from attacks.

  • Katavothres: Katavothres is a geological phenomenon where seawater disappears underground and flows into the Melissani Lake. It’s also a popular spot to watch the sunset.

Natural Attractions of Skala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kaminia Beach: Kaminia Beach is a beautiful, secluded beach that is known for its crystal-clear waters and soft sand. The beach is surrounded by lush greenery and is an ideal place for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.

  • Mount Ainos National Park: Mount Ainos National Park provides a fantastic opportunity to explore the island’s natural beauty. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

  • Xi Beach: Xi Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Skala. The beach has unique red and white sand, which makes it a perfect place for taking photos. There are also various water sports activities available, such as jet skiing and surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skala

Skala offers many options for hiking and biking enthusiasts. You can take a scenic trek through Mount Ainos National Park or explore the rugged coastline along the Kefalonian Way hiking trail. Cycling is another great way to explore the island, with many rental shops available in Skala town.

Best Things to Do in Skala: Activities and Tours

There is plenty to do in Skala, whether you are seeking adventure or relaxation. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Take a Boat Tour: There are various boat tours available in Skala, taking you to nearby islands and beaches. You get the chance to explore the underwater world while snorkeling or scuba diving.

  • Visit the Local Farmer’s Market: Every Thursday, first-floor town hall square is bursting with flavor, colors, and smells as dozens of farmers from all over the island exhibit their produce.

  • Visit the Vineyards: Kefalonia is known for its wines, and Skala has some of the best vineyards. You can visit the vineyards, taste some wine, and learn about the history of wine production in Kefalonia.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skala

If you’re looking for a week-long itinerary for Skala, try this:

  • Day 1: Visit the Roman Villa, explore Skala’s town center, and walk along the beach.

  • Day 2: Rent a bike and cycle to the nearby villages and countryside.

  • Day 3: Take a boat tour and visit the nearby islands.

  • Day 4: Hike through Mount Ainos National Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

  • Day 5: Relax on Kaminia Beach and enjoy the sun.

  • Day 6: Visit the vineyards and taste some of Kefalonia’s famous wines.

  • Day 7: Explore St. George’s Castle and watch the sunset at Katavothres.

Food and Drink Scene of Skala

Must-Try Dishes

Kefalonia Island has a distinct cuisine, with seafood being the main highlight.

  • Kreatopita: A delicious traditional lamb pie with aromatic herbs cooked in every household in Kefalonia.

  • Sofrito: A stew with beef, cloves, and garlic.

  • Taramosalata: A typical Greek dish made with cod roe, lemon juice, and breadcrumbs.

Local Beverages

Kefalonia is known for its wine. Robola is the most famous wine variety on the island, but there are various other ones to explore. If you’re looking for something refreshing, try the lemonade which is a local favorite.

Where to Stay in Skala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Skala offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxurious resorts to budget guesthouses.

  • Tesoro Blu Hotel and Spa: A luxurious hotel with a private beach, outdoor pool, and spa.

  • Oskars Studios and Apartments: A budget-friendly option with studios and apartments for rent, located just minutes from the beach.

  • Villa Eora Guesthouse: A peaceful guesthouse with stunning views of the Ionian Sea, located away from the hustle and bustle of the town.

What to Buy in Skala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kefalonia is known for its high-quality olive oil, wines, and honey. You can also find handmade silver jewelry, ceramics, and traditional Greek costumes. Don’t forget to pick up some local sweets such as Mantolato, Pasteli, and Melomakarona.

Local Culture and Customs of Skala

The locals of Skala are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and traditions. Greeks are known for their hospitality, so don’t be surprised if someone offers to buy you a drink or a meal.

Best Time to Visit Skala

The best time to visit Skala is in the summer, between June and August. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skala

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

  • take along an underwater camera to capture aquatic life.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals or their culture.

  • Litter on the beaches or in the town.

  • Go out in the sun without sunscreen and a hat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Skala

Skala is a safe town, but visitors should take care not to leave their belongings unattended on the beach.

Health in Skala

Visitors should make sure to carry essential medication and have appropriate travel insurance coverage. The tap water in Skala and throughout Kefalonia is safe to drink.

Getting to Skala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Skala

Kefalonia International Airport (EFL) is the nearest airport to Skala. From the airport, you can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Skala.

Bus Stations Near Skala

KTEL Kefalonias is the local bus service that connects Skala to other parts of the island. The bus station is located in the town center.

Other Options

You can also reach Skala through ferries and hydrofoils. There are also various tour operators that offer transportation services to and from Skala.

How to Get Around in Skala

Skala is a small town, so it’s easy to explore on foot. You can also rent a bike or scooter to explore the island. Taxis and buses are also available, with many car rental agencies in Skala town.

Where to Go Next from Skala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Skala is well located for exploring the island. Some of the popular nearby destinations include:

  • Argostoli: The island’s capital, Argostoli is a vibrant town located about 35km away from Skala

  • Lixouri: Lixouri is a beautiful town located on the western coast of the island, about 30km away from Skala.

  • Fiskardo: A picturesque fishing town located in the northern part of Kefalonia Island.

Conclusion

Skala is an idyllic destination that provides stunning views, adventures, and rich culture. With its unique mix of history, nature, and activities, Skala has become a top destination for travelers seeking an unforgettable holiday in Greece.
