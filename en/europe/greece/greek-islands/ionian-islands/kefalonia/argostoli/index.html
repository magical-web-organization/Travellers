Introduction to Argostoli

Argostoli is the capital and largest city of Kefalonia, an island in the Ionian Sea of Greece. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant local culture, Argostoli is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring Greece. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast looking for adventure or a history buff interested in the island’s past, Argostoli has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Argostoli

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Argostoli: A must-visit museum that showcases the island’s rich history from ancient times to the present day.
  • Korgialenio Historical and Folklore Museum: This museum houses exhibits on traditional local crafts, as well as artifacts from the island’s past.
  • The Napoleon Bonaparte Monument: This monument commemorates the French conqueror’s short stay on the island in 1797.
  • Saint Theodore Lighthouse: The lighthouse is a popular attraction offering stunning views of the city and its surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Argostoli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Myrtos Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Greece, boasting clear blue waters and stunning white cliffs.
  • Melissani Cave: A mesmerizing underground lake with crystal clear water that can only be accessed via boat.
  • Mount Ainos National Park: A stunning nature reserve that offers hiking trails through lush forests, gorgeous waterfalls, and panoramic views.
  • Drogarati Caves: A series of caverns with unique rock formations and stalactites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Argostoli

  • The Ainos Trail: A 25km trail that leads hikers through the beautiful Mount Ainos National Park.
  • The Fanari Route: A 5km scenic route that winds along the shore of Argostoli Bay.
  • The Koutavos Lagoon Trail: A 5km trail that goes through marsh and woodland areas around Koutavos lagoon.

Best Things to Do in Argostoli: Activities and Tours

  • Enjoy a boat tour around Argostoli Bay and see adorable sea turtles.
  • Take a tour of the local vineyards and taste traditional Greek wine.
  • Visit the traditional Kefalonian villages with a jeep safari tour.
  • Try scuba diving to explore the wide variety of marine life in the surrounding waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Argostoli

Day Activity
1 Explore Argostoli and take in the magnificent views from the Saint Theodore Lighthouse.
2 Visit Myrtos Beach and spend the day relaxing.
3 Take a boat tour and go sightseeing around Argostoli Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Argostoli

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A traditional Greek dish made of layers of eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.
  • Kefalonian meat pie: A savory pie stuffed with minced meat and vegetables, baked to perfection.
  • Savoro: Fried fish with a garlic and vinegar sauce.
  • Robola wine: A local white wine made from grapes grown on the slopes of Mount Ainos.

Local Beverages

  • Frappe: A cold blended coffee made with instant coffee, sugar, and water.
  • Ouzo: A traditional anise-flavored alcoholic drink.
  • Local Honey and Citrus Liqueur: A sweet and tangy traditional drink made locally.

Where to Stay in Argostoli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Emelisse Nature Resort: A luxurious resort with stunning views of the Ionian Sea.
  • Odyssey Hotel: An affordable hotel within walking distance of the city center and the beach.
  • F Zeen Boutique Hotel: A stylish boutique hotel with private pools in some rooms.
  • Xenios Villas: A complex of autonomous, traditional villas overlooking the Fisherman Village of Katelios.

What to Buy in Argostoli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Robola Wine: Bring home a bottle of the local wine and taste the flavor of Kefalonia.
  • Olive Oil and Olives: Taste the local and delicious olive oil and olives handmade on the island.
  • Kefalonian Pasteli: A traditional Greek candy made of sesame seeds and honey.
  • Feta Cheese with Sundried Tomatoes: A typical delicacy of Greece.

Local Culture and Customs of Argostoli

Argostoli is a laid-back, friendly city with a warm and vibrant local culture. Greeks are known for the importance they place on family, so it’s not uncommon to see families and extended family members spending time together in public places. Also, Greeks love to dance, so don’t miss the opportunity to attend a traditional Greek wedding or festival where you can experience authentic Greek music and dances.

Best Time to Visit Argostoli

The best time to visit Argostoli is between May and September when the weather is pleasant with average temperatures ranging from 25 to 30 degrees Celsius and the sea is warm for swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Argostoli

Do:

  • Respect local customs and religious traditions.
  • Try local food like Moussaka and Kefalonian meat pie.
  • Dress modestly when visiting monasteries and churches.

Don’t:

  • Don’t swim in the sea after a heavy meal.
  • Don’t pick flowers or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Don’t forget to tip at restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Argostoli

Argostoli is a safe city, but it’s always wise to take normal precautions such as not leaving valuable possessions unattended.

Health in Argostoli

The island’s medical services are of high standard. Consult your doctor to find what kind of preventive measures you should take for any underlying medical conditions.

Getting to Argostoli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Argostoli

  • Kefalonia International Airport, “Anna Pollatou” (EFL): Located 8 km south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Argostoli

  • KTEL Kefalonias Station: Located in Argostoli near the central square.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Available near the city center.
  • Ferry: Ferries operate daily from the city’s harbor, connecting Kefalonia with mainland Greece and other islands.

How to Get Around in Argostoli

The city center is best for exploration on foot or bike. There are also regular bus services from the central station to other parts of the island.

Where to Go Next from Argostoli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fiskardo: A picturesque seaside village located on the northern tip of Kefalonia.
  • Lixouri: A small town located across the bay from Argostoli, reachable by a 25-minute ferry ride.
  • Zakynthos: A beautiful island located just a short ferry ride away from Kefalonia.

Conclusion

Argostoli, the capital of Kefalonia, is a perfect blend of history, nature, and local culture, making it an ideal destination for any traveler. With our comprehensive tour guide, you can make the best of your trip by exploring the best attractions, activities and tasting the local food and drinks. Enjoy your time in Argostoli!
