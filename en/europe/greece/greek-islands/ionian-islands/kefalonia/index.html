Introduction to Kefalonia

Located in the Ionian Islands of Greece, Kefalonia is a stunning destination that boasts rugged mountains, crystal clear waters, and charming villages. Kefalonia is the largest of the Ionian Islands and offers a unique blend of natural wonders, ancient remains, and cultural attractions.

This comprehensive tour guide is designed to help travelers make the most of their trip to Kefalonia. The guide includes a list of top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, a week-long itinerary, and information on local culture, food, and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Kefalonia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit places in Kefalonia is the Archaeological Museum of Argostoli, which houses a significant collection of ancient artifacts. Another essential attraction is the Mycenaean Tomb of Mazarakata, which dates back to the 13th century BC and offers a fascinating insight into the island’s history.

Visitors can also explore the ruins of the St. George Castle, built-in 1504, and the Argostoli Lagoon, where they can observe the endangered loggerhead sea turtles. The Monastery of Agios Gerasimos and the Katavothres are other cultural must-sees.

Natural Attractions of Kefalonia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kefalonia is known for its natural beauty, with several breathtaking beaches, caves, and mountains. The famous Myrtos Beach is a magnet for travelers, nested between steep cliffs on the west coast of the island.

The Mellisani Cave and the Drogarati Cave are also worth visiting to see stalactites, stalagmites, and an underground lake that shimmers in an enchanting blue light. Additionally, hiking in Ainos National Park, which is renowned for its diverse fauna and flora, is a great adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kefalonia

Kefalonia offers several opportunities for hikers and bikers, from gentle forest trails to steep mountain peaks.

The ascent to Mount Ainos, the tallest peak of Kefalonia, takes around three hours. The route leads through lush forest land and is suitable for experienced hikers. Alternatively, travelers can explore the tranquil beauty of the northern villages, such as Fiskardo, by bike.

Best Things to Do in Kefalonia: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Kefalonia offers many other activities for visitors to enjoy.

Taking a boat trip is one of the most popular activities as visitors can discover secret beaches, turquoise waters and explore sea caves. Another popular attraction is the horse-riding tour to explore the island’s beauty, or book a guided tour to the islands’ charming vineyards. Water sports aficionados can indulge in activities like snorkeling, kayaking, and jet-skiing on the island’s pristine beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kefalonia

For visitors who have a week to spare, Kefalonia offers a well-rounded experience ensconced in natural beauty, cultural gems and immersive experiences. The itinerary blends relaxation with cultural and adventure possibilities, featuring beaches, national parks, and historical landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Kefalonia

Must-Try Dishes

Kefalonia’s local cuisine is a feast for foodies, with the island’s unique flavors often influenced by its neighboring islands like Crete. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Kreatopita (meat pie)
  • Patsas (tripe soup)
  • Pastitsio (pasta with meat and béchamel sauce)
  • Sfougato (cheese pie)
  • Robola wine

Local Beverages

The island of Kefalonia is famous for its wine, with the signature Robola wine which is produced from the Robola grape, found exclusively on the island. Visitors can tour Robola wineries and sample the distinct taste.

What to Buy in Kefalonia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kefalonia is famous for its virgin olive oil, soaps, and locally produced honey, among other handicrafts. Other items to consider as souvenirs include traditional Greek textiles, designer clothes, and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Kefalonia

Travelers to Kefalonia should be aware of local customs to avoid any offense. Religion and family life play an essential role in the daily life of the locals, and visitors should dress modestly when entering churches or religious sites. Additionally, shop owners and locals of Kefalonia are considered proud and hospitable, so it’s essential to be respectful and courteous.

Best Time to Visit Kefalonia

Kefalonia’s high season runs from June to August when the weather is sunny and warm. The shoulder season of April to May or September to October is ideal for visitors who wish to avoid tourist crowds and enjoy pleasant weather. The island is less crowded, and there is still plenty to do and explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kefalonia

Do:

  • Respect Kefalonian culture and customs.
  • Taste local cuisine and wine.
  • Take a boat trip to neighboring beaches.
  • Shop from locally owned and operated shops.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in churches or religious sites.
  • Drink and drive.
  • Litter on the beaches or natural trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kefalonia

Kefalonia is a safe destination for travelers. However, travelers should take standard precautions such as avoiding dark, isolated areas or being over-friendly with strangers.

Health in Kefalonia

Although Kefalonia is considered a relatively safe destination, travelers should take standard precautions such as washing hands frequently and having up-to-date travel vaccinations where necessary.

Getting to Kefalonia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kefalonia

The easiest way to get to Kefalonia is by air, with Kefalonia International Airport (EFL) serving the island. Several international airlines fly direct to Kefalonia from various European Union countries.

Bus Stations Near Kefalonia

KTEL buses are the most common mode of transport for getting around Kefalonia. Buses are frequent and offer a comfortable and cost-effective option for traveling throughout the island. Bus stations are located in all the major towns.

Other Options

Ferries and hydrofoils connect Kefalonia to nearby islands such as Zakynthos and Corfu. Alternatively, visitors can travel to Kefalonia by road via the mainland and take the ferry from the small port of Astakos on the mainland.

How to Get Around in Kefalonia

The easiest and most convenient way of getting around Kefalonia is by car. Visitors can rent a car or motorbike from one of the many rental companies on the island. Taxis are also available but can be quite expensive compared to renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Kefalonia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kefalonia is well-connected with other islands via ferry services. Visitors can continue their adventure and head to the nearby locations such as:

  • Zakynthos
  • Lefkada
  • Corfu

Conclusion

Kefalonia is a captivating destination with a wide range of attractions, activities, and beautiful scenery. This comprehensive tour guide aims to help visitors make the most of their trip by providing information on everything from local food to where to stay and where to go. With the right preparation, visitors can explore all the wonders that Kefalonia has to offer and create unforgettable memories.
