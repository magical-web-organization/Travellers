Introduction to Zakynthos

Zakynthos, also known as Zante, is a Greek Island located in the Ionian Sea on the west coast of the Greek mainland. It boasts a lively music and cultural scene, beautiful beaches, underwater caves, and a unique ecosystem that is home to several endangered species, making it a great destination for beach lovers, adventure seekers, and nature enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Zakynthos

Here are the top attractions tourists must visit when in Zakynthos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Solomos Museum: Named after Zakynthos’ national poet Dionysios Solomos, this museum exhibits his personal effects and honors his contributions to Greece’s literary history.
  • Byzantine Museum: Located in Zakynthos Town, this museum exhibits Byzantine art and icons from the early Christian period.
  • Venetian Castle: An ancient fortress that was once used as a fortress by the Venetians in the 16th century, it is now a popular tourist attraction with stunning panoramic views.

Natural Attractions of Zakynthos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Navagio Beach: Arguably the most famous beach in Greece, Navagio is surrounded by towering white cliffs and crystal clear waters and is home to a stranded shipwreck.
  • Blue Caves: Located in the northern part of the island, these underwater caves can be explored by swimming, diving, or by a boat tour.
  • Turtle Island/Zante Island: Named for its population of loggerhead turtles, this island is a protected area that can be visited on a boat tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zakynthos

Zakynthos offers several hiking and biking trails for visitors who want to explore the island’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Vrachionas Mountain Trail: This trail takes hikers to the highest point of the island and provides stunning views of the surrounding sea.
  • Kampi to Maries Trail: This trail passes through small villages and olive groves before reaching the village of Maries with its beautiful church.
  • Argassi Foothill Trail: This trail begins in the coastal resort town of Argassi and passes through beautiful forests and streams along the way.

Best Things to Do in Zakynthos: Activities and Tours

Zakynthos offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Zakynthos:

  • Shipwreck Beach Tour: Visitors can take a boat tour to Navagio Beach and the Blue Caves, as well as other hidden beaches that are only accessible by small boats.
  • Scuba Diving: With its clear waters and rich marine life, Zakynthos is a great place to go diving and is suitable for both beginners and experienced divers.
  • Watersports: From windsurfing to jet skiing, there are plenty of watersports available for thrill-seekers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zakynthos

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Zakynthos, including some of the top attractions we’ve mentioned above:

  • Day 1: Visit Zakynthos Town and explore the Solomos Museum and Byzantine Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour to Navagio Beach and the Blue Caves.
  • Day 3: Hike the Vrachionas Mountain Trail and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Day 4: Take a scuba diving trip and discover the amazing underwater world of Zakynthos.
  • Day 5: Visit the Venetian Castle and have a traditional Greek lunch.
  • Day 6: Rent a bike and explore the village of Maries and the surrounding olive groves.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beach and try some of the local food and drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Zakynthos

Greek cuisine is famous all over the world for its delicious flavors and use of fresh ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tzatziki: A refreshing dip made with yogurt, cucumbers, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Feta: A salty, crumbly cheese that is an essential ingredient in Greek cuisine.
  • Moussaka: Layers of eggplant, minced meat, and bechamel sauce, baked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A popular anise-flavored distilled liquor that is traditionally served with seafood.
  • Robola: A white wine that is produced exclusively on the island and is known for its crisp, dry taste.

Where to Stay in Zakynthos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zakynthos offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Zakynthos:

  • Zante Plaza Hotel & Apartments: This beachfront hotel offers comfortable rooms and apartments, a spa, and a swimming pool.
  • Oasis Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Zakynthos town, a short walk from the main square and the port.
  • St. John Villas & Spa: This chic resort offers luxurious villas, a spa, and a private beach.

What to Buy in Zakynthos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zakynthos offers plenty of local products and souvenirs that you can bring back home as a memory of your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Olive oil: The island is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is available in shops and markets around the island.
  • Traditional Greek sweets: From baklava to loukoumades, there are plenty of sweet treats to try in Zakynthos.
  • Handmade ceramics and pottery: Zakynthos has a long tradition of pottery and ceramics, and there are many local shops selling unique and beautiful pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Zakynthos

Zakynthos has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its music, food, and architecture. The island is also known for its religious traditions and festivals, including Holy Week and the Feast of St. Dionysios, the island’s patron saint.

Best Time to Visit Zakynthos

The best time to visit Zakynthos is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm for swimming and snorkeling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zakynthos

Here are some tips to keep in mind while traveling to Zakynthos:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take part in local festivals and celebrations.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the natural areas.
  • Haggle in shops or markets - it is not a part of Greek culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zakynthos

Zakynthos is a safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking up valuables and keeping an eye on personal belongings on crowded beaches.

Health in Zakynthos

Visitors should take care when swimming and diving, as tides and currents can be strong. Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available.

Getting to Zakynthos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Zakynthos by air or sea.

Airports Near Zakynthos

The island has its airport, Zakynthos International Airport, with direct flights from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Zakynthos

The island has several bus stations that connect the major towns and villages on the island.

Other Options

A ferry connects Zakynthos to the ports of Killini and Kyllini on the mainland.

How to Get Around in Zakynthos

Visitors can get around the island by bus, taxi, or by renting a car or a bike. Buses run regularly between the main towns and villages on the island, while taxis are readily available in the main towns and tourist resorts.

Where to Go Next from Zakynthos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zakynthos is a great starting point for exploring the other Ionian Islands, such as Corfu and Kefalonia. Visitors can also take day trips to the nearby mainland towns of Olympia and Nafplio.

Conclusion

Zakynthos has something for everyone, from beautiful beaches to historic sites and delicious food. We hope our guide has helped you plan your trip to this amazing island, and that you will enjoy exploring it like a local!
