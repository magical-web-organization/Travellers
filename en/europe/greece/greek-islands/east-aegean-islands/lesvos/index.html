Introduction to Lesvos

Lesvos is a Greek island located in the northern Aegean Sea, off the western coast of Turkey. Known for its stunning beaches, traditional villages, and natural beauty, Lesvos is a popular destination for travelers looking to explore the quieter side of Greece. With a rich history dating back to ancient times, the island also offers plenty of cultural and historical attractions to discover.

List of Places to Visit in Lesvos

If you’re planning a trip to Lesvos, here are some of the must-visit places to include on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lesvos is home to many cultural and historical sites that are worth visiting, including:

  • The Archaeological Museum of Mytilene, which houses a fascinating collection of artifacts from ancient times, including pottery, sculptures, and coins.
  • The Castle of Mytilene, a Byzantine fortress that dates back to the 12th century and offers stunning views of the city and the sea.

Natural Attractions of Lesvos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lesvos is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of outdoor activities and breathtaking landscapes to explore, such as:

  • The Petrified Forest of Lesvos, a UNESCO World Heritage site that contains some of the largest petrified trees in the world, dating back millions of years.
  • The beach of Eftalou, with its crystal-clear waters and hot springs that are said to have healing properties.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lesvos

Lesvos is an ideal destination for avid hikers and bikers, with many scenic routes to choose from. Some of the most popular include:

  • The route from the village of Agiassos to the Monastery of Taxiarchis, which offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The trail along the coast from Petra to Anaxos, which winds through olive groves and offers sweeping views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Lesvos: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Lesvos, such as:

  • A boat tour around the island’s coastline, offering stunning views of the rugged cliffs and hidden coves.
  • A cooking class with a local chef, where you can learn how to prepare traditional Greek dishes using fresh, local ingredients.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lesvos

If you’re planning to spend a week in Lesvos, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore Mytilene, the island’s capital, and visit the Archaeological Museum
2 Hike the trail from Agiassos to the Monastery of Taxiarchis
3 Relax on the beach at Eftalou and soak in the hot springs
4 Take a boat tour around the island’s coastline
5 Visit the Petrified Forest and the nearby village of Sigri
6 Attend a cooking class with a local chef
7 Spend the day exploring the villages of Molyvos and Petra

Food and Drink Scene of Lesvos

Lesvos has a rich culinary tradition, with many delicious dishes and local specialties to sample. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sardines, grilled or fried and served with a squeeze of fresh lemon and a sprinkle of salt.
  • Revithada, a hearty chickpea soup flavored with garlic and olive oil.
  • Ladotyri, a cheese made from sheep’s milk and aged in olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo, an anise-flavored liquor that’s the national drink of Greece.
  • Rakomelo, a traditional drink made with honey, cinnamon, and raki (a type of spirit).
  • Assorted local wines, such as Moschato and Agiorgitiko.

Where to Stay in Lesvos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lesvos offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular choices include:

  • Hotel Pyrgos of Mytilene, located in the heart of the city and offering stunning views of the sea.
  • Molyvos Manor, a charming guesthouse in the village of Molyvos, with traditional architecture and modern amenities.
  • Aegeon Hotel, a budget-friendly option in the town of Skala Kallonis, offering comfortable rooms and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Lesvos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home from Lesvos, here are some ideas:

  • Olive oil, which is produced in abundance on the island and known for its high quality.
  • Local herbs and spices, such as rosemary, thyme, and oregano, which grow wild in the hills and mountains.
  • Handmade pottery and ceramics, available in many shops and markets throughout the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Lesvos

Lesvos has a rich cultural heritage, with many festivals and traditions that are still celebrated today. Some of these include:

  • The Festival of Agios Haralambos, held every February in the village of Thermi, featuring music, dancing, and traditional food.
  • The Festival of Sardines, held every July in the village of Kalloni, featuring delicious seafood dishes and live music.
  • The procession of the Holy Cross, held every August in the village of Agiassos, where a wooden cross is carried up the steep hill to the church of Panagia.

Best Time to Visit Lesvos

The best time to visit Lesvos is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be quite hot, with temperatures often reaching above 35°C, while the winter can be windy and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lesvos

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Lesvos:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as covering up when entering churches and other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, and support the island’s small businesses by shopping at local markets and stores.
  • Take the time to explore the lesser-known villages and hidden gems of the island.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas, as this may be considered disrespectful or inappropriate.
  • Litter or damage any of the island’s natural or cultural treasures.
  • Be afraid to ask for help or advice from the locals, who are known for their hospitality and friendliness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lesvos

Lesvos is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Lesvos

The standard of healthcare in Lesvos is high, with several clinics and hospitals located throughout the island. Travelers are advised to bring their own prescription medication and to purchase travel insurance before leaving home.

Getting to Lesvos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lesvos

Lesvos is served by the Mytilene International Airport, located just 8 km south of the island’s capital city. Several airlines offer direct flights from cities throughout Europe, including Athens, London, and Frankfurt.

Bus Stations Near Lesvos

There are several bus stations located throughout the island, with regular services connecting the major towns and villages. The main bus station is located in Mytilene, with services to Kalloni, Petra, and other destinations.

Other Options

Ferries and boats are also available to transport you to and from the island, with regular services connecting Lesvos to other Greek islands, as well as Turkey and other nearby countries.

How to Get Around in Lesvos

The best way to get around Lesvos is by car or motorcycle, as this allows you to explore the island at your own pace and visit more remote locations. Alternatively, there are several bus routes connecting the major towns and villages, or you could hire a taxi or take a boat tour to explore the coastline and nearby islands.

Where to Go Next from Lesvos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to continue your Greek adventure after visiting Lesvos, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth considering:

  • Chios, another beautiful Greek island located just a short ferry ride away from Lesvos.
  • Turkey, just a short boat ride across the Aegean Sea from Lesvos, where you can explore ancient ruins and indulge in Turkish cuisine.
  • Athens, the vibrant capital city of Greece, offering a fascinating mix of ancient history and modern culture.

Conclusion

Lesvos is a truly stunning island with something to offer every traveler, from the breathtaking natural landscapes to the rich history and cultural traditions. With this travel guide, you should be well-equipped to plan your trip and make the most of your time on this beautiful island. Safe travels!
