Introduction to Mytilene

Mytilene, located in the Greek island of Lesvos in East Aegean Islands, is a stunning city rich in culture and natural beauty. Known for its ancient history and breathtaking scenery, Mytilene is a must-visit destination in Greece. The city is characterized by its charming narrow streets, historical landmarks, and blue-flag beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Mytilene

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visiting the local museums and historical places in Mytilene is a great way to learn about the city’s rich culture and history. Some popular sites to see include:

  • The Archaeological Museum of Mytilene, which features a collection of ancient objects and artifacts.

  • The Castle of Mytilene, which was built in the 6th century and is considered an important part of the city’s defense system.

  • The Museum of Modern Art, which features contemporary art exhibits from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Mytilene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mytilene is also known for its natural beauty, and there are several outdoor activities to enjoy in the city. Some popular sites to see include:

  • The Petrified Forest of Lesvos, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • The Ouzo Barbayannis Museum, which is a museum dedicated to the local ouzo distillery and offers guided tours.

  • The Moria Refugee Camp, which is a testament to the resilience and strength of the human spirit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mytilene

Mytilene offers several scenic bike and hiking routes for nature enthusiasts. Some popular routes to explore include:

  • The Byzantine Castle Route, which offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea.

  • The Mount Olympus Trail, which takes hikers up to the summit of the mountain for panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • The Kastro Hill Hike, which is a short but challenging hike that takes visitors up to the ancient castle of Mytilene.

Best Things to Do in Mytilene: Activities and Tours

Mytilene offers a wide variety of activities and tours for both children and adults. Some popular things to do include:

  • The Aegean Boat Tour, which takes visitors on a boat trip around the island to secluded beaches and coves.

  • The Lesvos Gastronomy Tour, which offers visitors the chance to taste some of the island’s best local and traditional food and wine.

  • The Olive Tree Tour, which takes visitors on a tour of the island’s olive groves, one of the oldest and most important Mediterranean crops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mytilene

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Mytilene:

Day 1: Visit the Archaeological Museum of Mytilene, the Castle of Mytilene, and the Lesvos Gastronomy Tour.

Day 2: Hike the Byzantine Castle Route, and then spend the evening exploring the city’s nightlife.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the nearby island of Chios, known for its beautiful beaches and historic architecture.

Day 4: Visit the Museum of Modern Art, the Petrified Forest of Lesvos, and the Ouzo Barbayannis Museum.

Day 5: Take a bike ride along the scenic coastal route, and then spend the evening strolling along the beach promenade.

Day 6: Visit the Moria Refugee Camp, and then spend the afternoon at the hot springs near Eftalou.

Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day exploring the city’s old town, shopping for souvenirs at the local markets, and savoring local cuisine at one of Mytilene’s many restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Mytilene

Mytilene offers a diverse and delicious food and drink scene that is sure to please any palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sardines of Kalloni, a local seafood dish made with fresh sardines and served with lemon and olive oil.

  • Saganaki, a fried cheese dish that is a favorite appetizer of the locals.

  • Moussaka, a traditional Greek dish that layers eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo, an anise-flavored liquor that is the national drink of Greece.

  • Retsina, a white wine flavored with pine resin and a popular accompaniment to seafood dishes.

  • Tsipouro, a strong, clear brandy that is traditionally served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Mytilene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mytilene offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • The Archontiko Petridi, a traditional guesthouse in the heart of the old town.

  • The Heliotrope Boutique and Resort, a luxurious hotel perched on a hill overlooking the Aegean Sea.

  • The Fresh Boutique Hotel, a modern and stylish hotel located just a few minutes’ walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Mytilene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mytilene is known for its local products and handcrafted souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to bring home:

  • Olive oil, which is produced on the island and is considered some of the best in Greece.

  • Pottery, which is made by local artisans and comes in a range of colors and patterns.

  • Ouzo, which can be purchased at the local distillery or at one of the many markets in town.

Local Culture and Customs of Mytilene

Mytilene is a traditional Greek city with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and observing quiet hours during the afternoon siesta.

Best Time to Visit Mytilene

The best time to visit Mytilene is during the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer is peak tourist season, with higher temperatures and more tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mytilene

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Greek phrases to help you communicate with the locals.

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional drinks.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Overdress or wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

  • Speak loudly or cause a disturbance in public places.

  • Touch or disturb the ancient ruins or artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mytilene

Mytilene is generally a safe city, but visitors should exercise caution and take common-sense precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Mytilene

Visitors to Mytilene should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and should consult with a healthcare professional before traveling. Tap water is safe to drink, but visitors should be aware of the risk of heatstroke and sunburn during the summer months.

Getting to Mytilene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mytilene is easily accessible by air, sea, or land, and there are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Mytilene

Mytilene International Airport is the primary airport serving the island and is located just a few kilometers from the city center. There are regular flights to Athens and other major cities in Greece, as well as international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Mytilene

There are several bus stations serving Mytilene, with regular services to other towns and cities on the island, as well as Athens and Thessaloniki.

Other Options

Ferry services connect Mytilene to other islands in the Aegean Sea and to the Turkish coast. Car rental is also a popular option for visitors who want to explore the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Mytilene

Mytilene is a relatively small city, and many of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. There are also several options for getting around:

  • Buses, which connect the city center to other parts of the island.

  • Taxis, which can be easily hailed on the street or booked in advance.

  • Rental cars or bikes, which offer visitors more flexibility and independence.

Where to Go Next from Mytilene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mytilene is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding islands and mainland Greece. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Chios, a nearby island known for its historic architecture and picturesque beaches.

  • Athens, the capital city of Greece and home to several ancient landmarks and museums.

  • Thessaloniki, a vibrant coastal city famous for its food scene and nightlife.

Conclusion

Mytilene offers visitors a unique blend of ancient history, natural beauty, and modern attractions. From the museums and historical places to the outdoor adventures and local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming Greek city. Plan your visit today and discover the best of Mytilene.
