Introduction to Mesta

Mesta is a medieval town located on the Greek island of Chios in the East Aegean Islands. This charming village is set apart by its beautiful architecture, narrow streets, and traditional Greek culture. Visitors can explore Mesta’s intriguing history and take part in exciting activities such as biking, hiking, and sailing.

List of Places to Visit in Mesta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mesta’s rich history and culture can be explored through its museums and historical sites. The main attraction in Mesta is the famous Mastic Museum, where visitors can learn about the island’s tradition of producing mastic gum. Other notable sites include the Byzantine churches of Taxiarches and Agios Ioannis, which contain beautiful frescoes and religious artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Mesta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mesta is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, and visitors can explore the area through various outdoor activities. Hiking, biking, and horseback riding are popular choices, and the surrounding beaches and crystal-clear waters are perfect for swimming and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mesta

Mesta’s natural landscapes can be explored through various hiking and biking routes. Visitors can hike to the top of Mount Amani, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of the island, or bike along the scenic coastal roads. The routes also take visitors through Mesta’s charming villages, where they can take a glimpse into traditional Greek culture.

Best Things to Do in Mesta: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, visitors to Mesta can enjoy a range of activities and tours. Sailing tours of the Aegean Sea are popular, as are visits to local wineries and farms. There are also opportunities to explore the surrounding islands, such as with a day trip to the neighboring island of Oinousses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mesta

For visitors planning a week-long stay in Mesta, there are endless opportunities for adventure and relaxation. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival, explore local beaches

Day 2: Visit Mastic Museum, take a guided tour of the village

Day 3: Hiking to Mount Amani, picnic lunch

Day 4: Sailing tour of the Aegean Sea

Day 5: Bike tour of the surrounding villages, visit local wineries

Day 6: Day trip to Oinousses

Day 7: Shopping for souvenirs, farewell dinner

Food and Drink Scene of Mesta

Must-Try Dishes

Mesta’s cuisine is characterized by its use of fresh, local ingredients and traditional cooking techniques. Some popular dishes to try include kleftiko, lamb baked in parchment paper, and dolmades, stuffed grape leaves. For dessert, try karydopita, a walnut cake made with local honey and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

The island of Chios is known for its production of ouzo, a traditional Greek liquor made with anise and other herbs. Visitors should also try the local wines, such as muscat, as well as the traditional Greek coffee.

Where to Stay in Mesta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mesta offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. For a unique experience, visitors can stay in one of the village’s traditional guesthouses, which offer a glimpse into traditional Greek culture. Some popular options include the Archangel Michael’s Mansion and the To Petrino Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Mesta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Mesta can bring a piece of traditional Greek culture home with them through the various local products available for purchase. Mastic gum is a popular local product, as are handmade textiles, pottery, and jewelry. Visitors can also bring home local wines and olive oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Mesta

Mesta is a small village that values its traditional Greek way of life. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when exploring the village. The locals are friendly, and visitors will likely be invited to participate in traditional Greek festivals and activities.

Best Time to Visit Mesta

The best time to visit Mesta is during the summer months, between June and September. The weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and waters are perfect for swimming and boating. However, visitors should be aware that the island can get crowded during peak season, so it’s best to book accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mesta

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly
  • Try local cuisine and products
  • Explore the surrounding natural landscapes
  • Participate in traditional Greek festivals and activities

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Leave litter or damage the environment
  • Take photographs of locals without asking for permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Mesta

Mesta is a safe village, and visitors are unlikely to experience any significant safety issues. However, it’s still important to take standard precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of any potential hazards when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Mesta

The island of Chios has several medical facilities that can provide medical care to visitors. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as staying hydrated and using insect repellent, to avoid any potential health issues.

Getting to Mesta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mesta

The nearest airport to Mesta is the Chios Island National Airport, which has regular flights from Athens. From the airport, visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Mesta.

Bus Stations Near Mesta

Mesta is accessible by bus from various parts of Chios Island. The main bus terminal on the island is located in the capital city of Chios.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Chios by ferry from various ports in Greece and Turkey.

How to Get Around in Mesta

Mesta is a small village that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mesta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Mesta can explore. Some popular options include the neighboring island of Oinousses, the town of Pyrgi, and the beautiful beaches of Vroulidia and Elinta.

Conclusion

Mesta is a charming and traditional Greek village that offers visitors a unique experience into Greek culture and history. With its beautiful natural landscapes, delicious cuisine, and exciting activities, Mesta is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a picturesque and adventurous holiday.
