Introduction to Chios

Chios is a stunning island located in the East Aegean Sea, part of the Greek Islands group. It is the fifth largest island in Greece and boasts a rich history and culture, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. Known as the birthplace of Homer, Chios has a long and fascinating history, which is reflected in its many museums, historical sites, and cultural events.

List of Places to Visit in Chios

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chios Archaeological Museum: This museum tells the story of Chios from ancient times to the present day, featuring exhibits on the island’s history, culture, and art.
  • Nea Moni Monastery: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this monastery was founded in the 11th century and features beautiful Byzantine artwork and architecture.
  • Mastic Museum: Learn about the island’s famous mastic trees and the process of producing mastic, a unique ingredient used in many local products.
  • Castle of Chios: This imposing castle dates back to the Byzantine era and offers stunning views of the island and nearby sea.

Natural Attractions of Chios: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Volissos: This picturesque village is nestled in the hills of Chios and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Mavra Volia Beach: Featuring black pebble beaches and crystal-clear waters, this beach is a must-see for nature lovers and beachgoers alike.
  • Agio Gala Natural Arch: A natural limestone arch that offers breathtaking views of the island’s coastline.
  • Olympi Cave: An impressive cave system that is home to thousands of stalactites and stalagmites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chios

Chios is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty. Some popular routes include:

  • Mastichochoria Trek: This 8km trail takes visitors through the island’s famous mastic villages, offering a glimpse into traditional Chiot life.
  • Mesta to Pyrgi Trek: This 14 km hike takes visitors through two charming medieval villages, with stunning views of the island’s interior.
  • Anavatos Loop: This 8km circular route takes visitors to the abandoned ghost town of Anavatos, with views of the surrounding hills and coastline along the way.

Best Things to Do in Chios: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Chios is known for its wine, and there are many tours available that allow visitors to sample local varieties and learn about the island’s winemaking traditions.
  • Beach Day: With over 50 beaches to choose from, visitors should definitely plan a day to relax and soak up the sun on one of Chios’s stunning beaches.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Greek and Chiot dishes with a cooking class. Many classes also include a trip to the local market to source ingredients.
  • Boat Tour: Explore the island’s coastline and nearby islets on a boat tour, with opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, and even dolphin watching.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chios

Day 1: Arrive in Chios and spend the day exploring the island’s capital, Chios Town.

Day 2: Visit the Nea Moni Monastery and explore the nearby villages of Avgonyma and Anavatos.

Day 3: Take a wine tasting tour of the island’s wineries.

Day 4: Explore the medieval villages of Mesta and Pyrgi and hike the Mesta to Pyrgi trek.

Day 5: Spend a day at the beach, perhaps visiting Mavra Volia or Karfas Beach.

Day 6: Take a boat tour of the island’s coast and nearby islets.

Day 7: Visit the Chios Archaeological Museum and the Mastic Museum before departing the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Chios

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chiot Pita: A savory pie filled with cheese, herbs, and meat.
  • Kanellada: A sweet pastry filled with cinnamon and sugar.
  • Mastelo: A type of lamb or goat cooked in a ceramic pot until tender.

Local Beverages

  • Mastic Liqueur: Made from the resin of the mastic tree, this liqueur has a unique and refreshing taste.
  • Chios Wine: Chios produces a range of wines, including dry whites and sweet dessert wines.

Where to Stay in Chios: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chios offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular options include:

  • Chios Chandris Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on Karfas Beach.
  • Spilia Village: A charming boutique hotel located in the hills above the town of Chios.
  • Mesta Mastic: A traditional guesthouse located in the medieval village of Mesta.

What to Buy in Chios: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mastic Products: Chios is famous for its mastic trees, which produce a resin that is used in many local products, such as soaps, skincare products, and even chewing gum.
  • Lace: Chios is also known for its lace, which is handmade using traditional techniques.
  • Local Wines: Bring home a bottle of Chios’s delicious wine to enjoy back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Chios

Chios has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its festivals, traditions, and everyday life. Some customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Easter Celebrations: Easter is the most important holiday in Greece, and Chios is particularly known for its unique and elaborate Easter celebrations, which include fireworks and a mock battle between the two main churches in Chios town.
  • Respect for the Dead: The island has a strong tradition of respect for the dead, with many funerary monuments and customs that reflect this respect.
  • Hospitality: Greek hospitality is legendary, and Chios is no exception. Visitors can expect to receive a warm welcome and generous hospitality during their stay on the island.

Best Time to Visit Chios

The best time to visit Chios depends on your interests and preferences. The island is beautiful year-round, but some key considerations include:

  • Summer: Peak tourist season, with warm temperatures and crowded beaches.
  • Spring and Fall: Mild temperatures and fewer tourists, making it a great time for outdoor activities.
  • Winter: Cool temperatures, but many festivals and events during the holiday season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chios

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Chios has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine: Chios has a rich culinary tradition, and visitors should take the opportunity to try local dishes and ingredients.
  • Take part in local events and festivals: Chios has many festivals and cultural events throughout the year, and visitors can learn a lot about the island’s culture by taking part in these events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment: Chios’s natural beauty is one of its main attractions, and visitors should take care to preserve the environment and avoid littering or damaging natural sites.
  • Disrespect religious sites or customs: Many of Chios’s cultural customs and traditions have religious significance, and visitors should be respectful of these sites and customs.
  • Expect everything to be open and running on time: Chios follows the Greek tradition of “island time,” so visitors should be prepared for things to be a bit more relaxed and flexible than in other destinations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chios

Chios is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other destination, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also be cautious when swimming in the sea, as currents can be strong in some areas.

Health in Chios

Chios has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and medical centers located around the island. Visitors should be aware of any pre-existing conditions they may have and take necessary precautions, such as bringing any required medication with them.

Getting to Chios: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chios is easily accessible by air and sea. Some key transportation options include:

Airports Near Chios

  • Chios Island National Airport: Located just outside of Chios town, the island’s airport offers connections to Athens and other major Greek cities.

Bus Stations Near Chios

  • Chios KTEL Bus Station: Located in Chios town, the bus station offers connections to other towns and villages on the island, as well as to Athens and other locations in Greece.

Other Options

  • Ferries to Chios: Chios is also accessible by ferry, with connections to nearby islands and mainland Greece.

How to Get Around in Chios

Chios has a good transportation system, with buses and taxis available for getting around the island. Visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore the island at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Chios: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chios is a beautiful destination in its own right, but visitors may also want to explore nearby destinations and attractions, such as:

  • Lesvos: A nearby island known for its natural beauty and vibrant culture.
  • Ephesus: An ancient city in nearby Turkey that is one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world.
  • Izmir: A vibrant Turkish city with a rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Chios is a beautiful island with a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, hiking through the hills, or simply relaxing on the beach, Chios has something to offer every visitor. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect vacation to this amazing island and experience everything it has to offer.
