Introduction to Mastichochoria

Mastichochoria is a cluster of 24 picturesque medieval villages located in the southern part of Chios island in Greece. This region is famous for its production of mastic, a resin extracted from trees that has been used since ancient times for its medicinal and aromatic properties. The villages of Mastichochoria have maintained their traditional architecture, with Genoese-style houses and narrow streets, creating a unique and charming atmosphere for visitors to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Mastichochoria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mastichochoria Museum in Pyrgi: This museum showcases the history and production of mastic in Chios, as well as the traditional way of life in the Mastichochoria region. Visitors can learn about the process of extracting mastic through interactive exhibits and view historical artifacts and photographs.
  • The Medieval Castle of Mesta: This well-preserved medieval castle is located in the heart of Mesta village and is a magnificent example of Genoese architecture. Visitors can explore its impressive walls, towers, and narrow streets that are interconnected in a labyrinthine way.
  • The Cave of Olympi: This is a natural cave located in the village of Olympi that was used as a sanctuary in ancient times and later by Christians as a place of worship. Visitors can see the cave’s impressive stalactites and stalagmites, as well as the remains of frescoes on the walls.

Natural Attractions of Mastichochoria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Beach of Mavra Volia: This unique black pebble beach is located in the village of Emporio. Its crystal-clear waters and volcanic rocks offer a breathtaking view and an ideal spot for swimming and snorkeling.
  • The Komi Forest: This natural park is located in the village of Komi and is an excellent spot for hiking and birdwatching. It is also home to some endangered species of flora and fauna, such as the Chios Oak and the Golden Eagle.
  • The Mastihohoria Walking Trails: Mastichochoria has numerous walking trails that connect the villages and offer scenic views of the landscape. The trails are well-marked and vary in difficulty, allowing visitors to choose the one that suits them best.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mastichochoria

  • The Mastichochoria Trail: This is a 25 km hiking trail that starts from the village of Vessa and passes through several villages, offering visitors a chance to explore the traditional architecture of the region.
  • The Mastichochoria Mountain Bike Trail: This is a 37 km trail that starts from the village of Pyrgi and passes through several villages, offering visitors a chance to admire the beauty of the scenery while enjoying a challenging ride.

Best Things to Do in Mastichochoria: Activities and Tours

  • Mastic Production Tour: Visitors can learn about the traditional way of harvesting and processing mastic by taking a tour at one of the many mastic-producing factories in the region.
  • Medieval Castle Tour: Visitors can explore the well-preserved medieval castles of Mesta and Pyrgi with a knowledgeable local guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Mastichochoria

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souma: This is a traditional Chios spirit made from figs and is often served as an aperitif.
  • Mastelo: This is a slow-cooked lamb dish that is wrapped in parchment paper and cooked in a sealed clay pot for hours. The result is a tender and flavorful dish that is unique to the region.
  • Trigona Chios: These delicacies are small triangles of pastry filled with sweet mastic cream.

Local Beverages

  • Masticliqueur: This is a sweet liqueur made with mastic that is served as a digestif.
  • Chios Beer: This is a locally-produced beer that is made with barley, water, and hops, and has a distinctive taste.

Where to Stay in Mastichochoria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mastichochoria has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose to stay in the traditional guesthouses of the villages, or opt for more modern hotels located in the nearby port town of Chios.

What to Buy in Mastichochoria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mastic products: Visitors can buy mastic resin, gum, and oil, as well as a range of cosmetic products made with mastic.
  • Ceramic pottery: Mastichochoria is also known for its traditional ceramic pottery, which makes for a beautiful and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mastichochoria

The people of Mastichochoria are proud of their traditional way of life and are happy to share it with visitors. It is common to hear the sound of the church bells ringing throughout the villages and to see locals dressed in their traditional garments during festivals and celebrations, which take place throughout the year. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and dress appropriately when visiting churches and other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Mastichochoria

The best time to visit Mastichochoria is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and all the attractions and activities are available. The peak season is July and August, when the villages are busy with tourists and the prices are at their highest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mastichochoria

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and dress appropriately when visiting churches and other cultural attractions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, especially those made with mastic
  • Bring comfortable shoes for walking and hiking

Don’t:

  • Pick or damage the mastic trees, which are protected by law
  • Leave trash or litter in the villages or natural parks
  • Drive recklessly on the narrow village streets

Safety and Health

Safety in Mastichochoria

Mastichochoria is a safe destination for visitors, with a low crime rate and a friendly local community. However, visitors are advised to take common-sense precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and not leaving possessions unattended.

Health in Mastichochoria

Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies during their trip. Visitors should also bring any prescription medications they require and be aware that certain rural areas may not have easy access to medical facilities.

Getting to Mastichochoria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mastichochoria

The nearest airport to Mastichochoria is Chios International Airport, which has regular flights to Athens and other destinations in Greece.

Bus Stations Near Mastichochoria

Chios has a comprehensive public transportation system, with regular bus services connecting the villages of Mastichochoria to the nearby towns of Chios and Karfas.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or a motorbike to explore the region on their own. Taxis are also available, but visitors are advised to negotiate the fare in advance.

How to Get Around in Mastichochoria

The best way to get around Mastichochoria is by foot, as the villages are all within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also hire a local guide or rent a bike to explore the area more thoroughly.

Where to Go Next from Mastichochoria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to the nearby island of Psara, which is famous for its beautiful sandy beaches and traditional way of life. Alternatively, visitors can explore the town of Chios, which has a charming harbor, a castle, and several museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Mastichochoria is a hidden gem of the Greek Islands, offering visitors a chance to explore a unique and traditional way of life and discover the natural and cultural attractions of this beautiful region. Whether visitors choose to hike the trails, lounge on the beaches, or sample the local cuisine, there is something here for everyone to enjoy.
