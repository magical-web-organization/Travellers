Skyros is a hidden jewel nestled in the middle of the Aegean Sea, in the Sporades Islands of Greece. This scenic island is perfect for those seeking natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. In this guide, we will take you through everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Skyros.

Introduction to Skyros

Located in the Sporades Islands, Skyros is one of Greece’s many hidden gems. This island is steeped in history and filled with natural beauty. Skyros boasts miles of unspoiled beaches, olive groves, rolling hills, and a rugged coastline. The island has a rich culture and history, which is evident in its traditional architecture, museums, and festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Skyros

Skyros has a range of enigmatic and charming places to visit, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re intrigued by the history and culture of Skyros, make sure you visit the following museums and historical places:

  1. The Byzantine Castle: This castle is perched on top of a hill overlooking the main town of Skyros. It was built during the 6th century AD and served as a fortress for the islanders during invasions.

  2. The Manos Faltaits Museum: This museum is filled with traditional costumes, pottery, and artifacts from Skyros’ past. The museum houses a collection of art and paintings from the famous Skyrian artist, Manos Faltaits.

  3. The Archaeological Museum of Skyros: This museum showcases ancient artifacts and exhibits that detail the island’s history from the Bronze Age to the Byzantine Era.

Natural Attractions of Skyros: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking adventure or natural beauty, Skyros has several outdoor and natural attractions:

  1. Beaches: Skyros has some of the most secluded beaches in the Aegean, including Kalamitsa, Pefkos, and Glyfada. These beaches are known for their turquoise waters and picturesque surroundings.

  2. The Skyros Horse: This unique breed of horse is native to the island, and it is considered one of the oldest in the world. You can take a horseback ride across the island’s rugged terrain and picturesque trails.

  3. Mount Olympus of Skyros: This towering mountain reaches 792m high and provides breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea and the surrounding Sporades islands. You can even hike to its summit and be rewarded with stunning views of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skyros

Skyros offers several biking and hiking trails that allow visitors to explore the island’s many hidden corners. Some of the best routes include:

  1. Skyros Hiking Trail: This trail encompasses a network of paths that allow visitors to explore the island’s rugged terrain, along with its lush forests, chapels, churches, and ancient ruins.

  2. Skyros Biking Trail: This trail is more geared towards cycling enthusiasts, and it takes you through some of the most scenic spots on the island, including the beaches and ancient villages.

Best Things to Do in Skyros: Activities and Tours

Skyros offers a range of activities and tours suitable for everyone, including the following:

  1. Boat Tours: Skyros has several boat tours that take you around the island, showing you its many hidden coves and scenic beaches.

  2. Cooking Class: Taste the flavors of Skyros by taking a cooking class with one of the island’s local chefs. You will learn how to prepare traditional dishes such as stuffed onions and goat stew.

  3. Skyros Circus Festival: Experience a unique festival that celebrates the island’s longstanding circus traditions. You will witness breathtaking acrobatics, fire breathers, clowns, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skyros

Below you’ll find a suggested week-long itinerary for your stay in Skyros:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Skyros, explore the town’s historic center
Day 2 Visit the Byzantine Castle, the Manos Faltaits Museum, and the Archaeological Museum

Day 5 Take a boat tour around the island or experience an authentic cooking class

Food and Drink Scene of Skyros

Skyros has a reputation for having some of the best food and drinks in Greece, with a focus on fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Below, we list some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Strapatsada: This is a traditional Skyrian dish made from ripe tomatoes, peppers, onions, and eggs. It’s often served with crusty bread.

  2. Spoori: Spoori is a local specialty, a type of cheese made from the milk of the Skyros sheep. It’s often served as a snack with bread and olives.

  3. Maina: Maina is a type of pasta commonly found on the island. It’s often served with fresh tomato sauce and a sprinkle of feta cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Ouzo: A popular alcoholic drink in Greece, Skyros’ ouzo is said to be of excellent quality.

  2. Rakomelo: This is a traditional Greek drink made from raki, which is infused with honey and spices such as cinnamon and cloves. Rakomelo is often served as an aperitif.

  3. Wine: Skyros produces some excellent wines, thanks to its warm climate and fertile soil. White wines such as Athiri and Assyrtiko are particularly popular.

Where to Stay in Skyros: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Skyros has many accommodations to cater to different types of travelers. The island offers everything from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some options include:

  1. Hotel Nefeli: This hotel is located on the edge of the old town of Skyros and offers sweeping views of the Aegean Sea. It’s a 4-star luxury hotel with an outdoor pool, a restaurant, and a bar.

  2. Pegasus Studios: This is a budget-friendly option, located on a hill, and is surrounded by greenery. The rooms are well-appointed, and there’s a small kitchenette for cooking.

  3. Karavos: This is a traditional guesthouse situated in the historic center of Skyros. It offers a mix of traditional and modern rooms, and some feature a balcony with excellent views.

What to Buy in Skyros: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Skyros has a range of local products and souvenirs that you can take back home with you. Here are a few recommendations:

  1. Pottery: Skyrian pottery has a long tradition, and it’s possible to find beautiful pottery items that make for excellent souvenirs.

  2. Local herbs and spices: Due to the island’s climate, there is an abundance of herbs and spices grown on Skyros. You can get them at local markets and bring them home to infuse your cuisine with an authentic Skyrian flavor.

  3. Textiles: Skyros has a long tradition of weaving, and there are many high-quality textiles and woven products available to buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Skyros

Skyros has a rich and diverse culture, with traditional costumes, dances, and festivals. The island also has its own unique language, Skyrian, which is a dialect of Greek. Here are a few local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Skyrians are known for their friendliness and hospitality. A handshake is the traditional way to greet someone.

  • Respect the local customs when visiting temples or other religious sites. Cover your shoulders and avoid wearing revealing clothing.

Best Time to Visit Skyros

The best time to visit Skyros is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and dry. The island’s beaches and hiking trails are at their best during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skyros

Below are some do’s and don’ts to help you make the most out of your trip to Skyros:

Do:

  • Try to learn a few words of Skyrian before you arrive.

  • Respect the island’s unique culture and heritage.

  • Make an effort to try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural sites or cultural monuments.

  • Disrupt local customs or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Skyros is a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Skyros

  • Be careful when swimming in the sea, as there can be strong currents.

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended on the beach.

  • Follow local advice and signposts when hiking to ensure your safety.

Health in Skyros

  • Make sure you have travel insurance before you arrive in case of an emergency.

  • Bring mosquito repellent as mosquitoes can be prevalent in certain areas of Skyros.

  • Always drink bottled water to avoid any potential health issues.

Getting to Skyros: Transportation Options and Logistics

Skyros is accessible from Athens and other major cities in Greece.

Airports Near Skyros

The closest airport is Skiathos International Airport, which is about an hour away by ferry.

Bus Stations Near Skyros

KTEL buses run from Athens to Skyros, but the trip takes around 5 hours with a ferry ride at the end.

Other Options

If you prefer a more personalized experience, you can book a private transfer or taxi to take you directly to Skyros. This can be arranged in advance and is ideal for those traveling in groups or with children.

How to Get Around in Skyros

Skyros is a relatively small island, and you can easily explore it by bike, car, or on foot. Alternatively, you can take a taxi or a bus to get around the island.

Where to Go Next from Skyros: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time, you might consider visiting the island of Alonissos, which is a short ferry ride away from Skyros. Alonissos is known for its stunning beaches, secluded coves, and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Skyros is an enchanting island with something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature lover or a history buff, there’s plenty to see and do here. With this guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to Skyros and experience everything this hidden beauty has to offer.
