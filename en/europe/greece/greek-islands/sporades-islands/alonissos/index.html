Welcome to Alonissos, an unspoiled island in the Sporades Archipelago of Greece. Known for its crystal-clear waters, stunning beaches, rich flora and fauna, and interesting cultural heritage, Alonissos will satisfy all types of travelers: from nature lovers and adventure seekers to history and culture buffs.

Introduction to Alonissos

Alonissos is the third largest island in the Sporades chain, located in the Aegean Sea. The island is famous for its National Marine Park, the largest protected marine area in Europe, home to various sea species such as the Mediterranean monk seal and the sea turtle. The island was once a major trading hub, where merchants exchanged goods from the Middle East, the Aegean, and the Mediterranean.

List of Places to Visit in Alonissos

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Alonissos has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Alonissos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alonissos Museum: Housed in a 19th-century neoclassical building, the Alonissos Museum showcases the island’s rich history and culture, with archaeological finds, paintings, and folk art.
  • Hora: The old town of Alonissos, Hora, is built on a hilltop, with narrow alleys, traditional architecture, and breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea.
  • Monasteries: Alonissos has several monasteries that date back to the Byzantine era, such as the Monastery of Agios Ioannis Prodromos and the Monastery of Agios Dimitrios.

Natural Attractions of Alonissos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • National Marine Park of Alonissos: A must-visit for nature lovers, the National Marine Park of Alonissos is a protected area that encompasses the northwestern part of the island, including six smaller islands. Visitors can join boat trips, go snorkeling, and observe the local flora and fauna.
  • Patitiri Beach: Located in the main harbor of Alonissos, Patitiri Beach is a pebble beach with crystal-clear waters. It’s an ideal spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Kokkinokastro: An ancient citadel on the western side of the island, Kokkinokastro offers spectacular views of the Aegean Sea and the neighboring islands. It’s a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alonissos

Alonissos is a hiker’s paradise, with several hiking and biking trails that showcase the island’s natural beauty. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Tzortzi Gialos: A hiking trail that starts from the beach of Tzortzi Gialos and leads to Kokkinokastro, passing through olive groves and pine forests.
  • Marpounta: A scenic biking route that connects the villages of Marpounta, Kalamakia, and Agios Dimitrios, with views of the Aegean Sea and the National Marine Park.

Best Things to Do in Alonissos: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and swimming, Alonissos offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Alonissos:

  • Boat Trips: Join a boat trip to explore the hidden beaches and coves of Alonissos, as well as the neighboring islands of Skopelos and Skiathos.
  • Scuba Diving: Discover the underwater world of the National Marine Park of Alonissos, with scuba diving tours that cater to all levels of experience.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Greek dishes with local ingredients, such as fava beans, onion pie, and stuffed calamari, in a cooking class led by a local chef.

Food and Drink Scene of Alonissos

Alonissos has a rich culinary tradition, with dishes that reflect the island’s history and geography. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Alonissos:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Patatato: A slow-cooked lamb stew with garlic, potatoes, and red wine, traditionally cooked in a large pot on an open fire.
  • Tuna Pie: A savory pie made with fresh tuna, onions, and herbs, wrapped in phyllo pastry and baked in the oven.
  • Poultry with Plums: An autumn dish made with free-range chicken, plums, and red wine, served with rice pilaf.

Local Beverages

  • Anambrusco: A sweet red wine made from the anamneseis grape, a rare variety that grows exclusively on the island.
  • Tsipouro: A strong distilled spirit made from grape pomace, often served as an aperitif or with meze.

Where to Stay in Alonissos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alonissos offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Alonissos:

  • Ikion Eco Boutique Hotel: A luxury hotel in the village of Patitiri, with stylish rooms, a beautiful garden, and a pool with sea views.
  • Pension Votsi: A cozy guesthouse in the fishing village of Votsi, with simple, yet comfortable rooms and a terrace overlooking the Aegean Sea.
  • Camping Halkidiki: A campsite close to the beach of Chrysi Milia, with tents and bungalows for rent, as well as a restaurant and a mini-market.

What to Buy in Alonissos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alonissos has a rich handicraft tradition, with products that reflect the island’s history and culture. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from Alonissos:

  • Olive Oil: Alonissos produces high-quality olive oil, made from the island’s olive groves that date back to ancient times.
  • Sea Sponges: The island of Alonissos has a long tradition in sponge diving, and the local sea sponges are known for their quality and durability.
  • Folk Art: Alonissos has a vibrant folk art scene, with artisans that create ceramics, textiles, and wood carvings with traditional designs and motifs.

Local Culture and Customs of Alonissos

Alonissos has a rich cultural heritage, with traditions that date back to ancient times. The island’s inhabitants are known for their hospitality and warmth, as well as their respect for nature and the environment. Religious festivals and feasts are an integral part of the island’s culture, with music, dance, and food that reflect the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Alonissos

The best time to visit Alonissos is from mid-April to mid-June, and from September to mid-October, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thin. July and August are peak tourist seasons, with hot temperatures and crowded beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alonissos

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and environment of the National Marine Park of Alonissos.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some basic Greek phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural sites of the island.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Bargain in stores or restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alonissos

Alonissos is a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take some precautions to ensure a trouble-free trip:

  • Be cautious when swimming in the open sea, and follow the guidelines of the lifeguards.
  • Lock your valuables in the hotel safe, and be aware of pickpockets in crowded places.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid the risk of digestive problems.

Health in Alonissos

Alonissos has a public health center in Patitiri, which provides medical services for visitors and residents. Visitors are advised to bring their own medicine and first-aid kit, and to have travel insurance that covers emergency medical and evacuation expenses.

Getting to Alonissos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alonissos is accessible by plane and boat, with several options that suit different budgets and schedules.

Airports Near Alonissos

The nearest airport to Alonissos is Skiathos Airport, served by domestic and international flights. From Skiathos, visitors can take a ferry or a hydrofoil to Alonissos.

Bus Stations Near Alonissos

There are no bus stations in Alonissos, but visitors can take a bus from Athens or Thessaloniki to Volos, and then a ferry or a hydrofoil to Alonissos.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car in Skiathos or Volos, and then take a ferry or a hydrofoil to Alonissos. There are several car rental agencies in Alonissos that offer cars, scooters, and bicycles for rent.

How to Get Around in Alonissos

Alonissos has a good public transportation system, with buses that connect the main villages and beaches of the island. There are also taxis and rental cars, scooters, and bicycles that provide more flexibility and privacy. Walking and hiking are also great ways to explore the island’s hidden gems.

Where to Go Next from Alonissos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alonissos is located in the heart of the Sporades Archipelago, and visitors can easily reach the neighboring islands and destinations:

  • Skopelos: Known for its green landscapes, traditional architecture, and beaches, Skopelos is a popular destination for nature and culture lovers.
  • Skiathos: Famous for its cosmopolitan vibe, nightlife, and beaches, Skiathos is a hotspot for luxury tourism and summer fun.
  • Pelion: A mountainous peninsula on the mainland of Greece, Pelion offers a unique blend of sea and mountain landscapes, with traditional villages, lush forests, and clean beaches.

Conclusion

Alonissos is a hidden gem in the Aegean Sea, with a rich cultural heritage, unspoiled nature, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, Alonissos will offer you a unique and authentic travel experience. We hope that this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Alonissos, and that you will discover the island’s hidden treasures and secrets.
