Introduction to Tilos

Tilos is a small island that is part of the Dodecanese archipelago in Greece. It is known for its rugged natural beauty, serene beaches, and rich history. The island is off the beaten path, making it perfect for those who want to escape the crowds and explore a lesser-known Greek destination.

List of Places to Visit in Tilos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Tilos has plenty to offer. Start with the following attractions:

  • The Charkadio Cave: One of the most famous attractions on the island, this cave was inhabited during the Neolithic period and contains many interesting geological formations.
  • The Monastery of Agios Panteleimon: A well-preserved monastery that was built in the early 18th century. The monastery contains an interesting collection of icons and artifacts.
  • The Castle of the Knights of St. John: Built in the 15th century, this castle is a testament to the island’s history as a strategic location for defending against invasions.

Natural Attractions of Tilos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who enjoy spending time in nature, Tilos is a paradise. Some of the highlights include:

  • Eristos Beach: A stunning sandy beach surrounded by high mountains and cliffs. It is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Mikro Chorio: An abandoned village that is now in ruins, Mikro Chorio has an eerie beauty that is perfect for photography and exploration.
  • The Livadia Trail: A beautiful hiking trail that starts from the port of Livadia and leads to the interior of the island. It is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Tilos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tilos

Tilos is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many trails that take you through scenic areas of the island. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • The Messaria Trail: This trail starts in the village of Messaria and takes you through olive groves, small valleys, and historical sites.
  • The Prophet Elias Trail: This trail starts at the top of the Prophet Elias mountain and offers stunning views of the island and the sea.
  • The Livadia to Megalo Horio Trail: This trail starts in Livadia and takes you through the medieval village of Megalo Horio.

Best Things to Do in Tilos: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the island, there are many other activities and tours worth checking out. Some of the best options include:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour around the island and discover the many beaches and coves that are only accessible by boat.
  • Scuba Diving: Discover the underwater world around Tilos with a scuba diving trip. There are several dive centers on the island that offer tours for all levels of experience.
  • Olive Oil Tastings: Visit one of the island’s olive oil presses and taste the delicious local olive oil, which is famous for its quality and flavor.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tilos

If you are planning to spend a week on Tilos, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive on Tilos and explore the port town of Livadia.
  • Day 2: Spend the day relaxing on Eristos Beach and hiking the nearby trails.
  • Day 3: Visit the Charkadio Cave and the Monastery of Agios Panteleimon.
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour around the island and discover the hidden beaches and coves.
  • Day 5: Explore the abandoned village of Mikro Chorio and hike the Livadia Trail.
  • Day 6: Visit the Castle of the Knights of St. John and enjoy a sunset over the sea.
  • Day 7: Take a scuba diving trip and taste the local olive oil before departing from Tilos.

Food and Drink Scene of Tilos

Must-Try Dishes

Tilos has a rich culinary tradition that is based on fresh local ingredients and traditional recipes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Tilopsomo: A traditional bread that is made with olive oil and is perfect for dipping in olive oil and local honey.
  • Stuffed Tomatoes: A popular dish that is made with local tomatoes stuffed with rice, herbs, and ground meat.
  • Kolokassi: A traditional Greek vegetable that is similar to taro root and is cooked in a tomato sauce with onion and garlic.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Tilos is known for its delicious local wine and ouzo. There are also many cafes and bars where you can enjoy a cold beer or a refreshing cocktail.

Where to Stay in Tilos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tilos has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Dolphin Studios: A family-run business that offers comfortable studios and apartments with sea views. It is located in Livadia and is close to many restaurants and shops.
  • Digenis Studios: Located in the heart of Megalo Horio, this guesthouse offers traditional accommodation with panoramic views of the island.
  • Irene Studios: Located in the village of Eristos, this accommodation offers tranquil surroundings and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Tilos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tilos is known for its high-quality local products, including olive oil, honey, and wine. You can also find handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles that make great souvenirs to bring back home. Some of the best places to shop for local products include the village of Megalo Horio and the port town of Livadia.

Local Culture and Customs of Tilos

Tilos has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its many festivals and celebrations. The island is also known for its friendly and welcoming locals who are always happy to share their traditions and customs with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Tilos

The best time to visit Tilos is from late spring to early autumn when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August can be quite busy, so consider visiting in June or September to avoid the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tilos

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and do not leave trash behind.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Worry about finding ATM’s as there is only one on Tilos, in Livadia.
  • Damage the historical sites or artifacts.
  • Disrespect the locals or the island’s customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tilos

Tilos is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is always important to take basic safety precautions and avoid leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Tilos

Tilos has a small medical center and a pharmacy where you can get basic medical treatment. If you have any serious health concerns, it is best to seek medical attention on a larger nearby island like Rhodes.

Getting to Tilos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tilos

The nearest airports to Tilos are Rhodes and Kos. From there, you can take a ferry to the island.

Bus Stations Near Tilos

Tilos has a small bus station that operates daily routes to the main villages and beaches on the island.

Other Options

You can also get to Tilos by ferry from nearby islands like Rhodes, Kos, and Nisyros.

How to Get Around in Tilos

The best way to explore Tilos is on foot or by bike, as the island is small and easy to navigate. You can also rent a car, scooter, or a quad bike from the various rental shops in Livadia.

Where to Go Next from Tilos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of the Dodecanese islands, consider taking a ferry to nearby destinations like Rhodes, Kos, and Nisyros. You can also take day trips to the nearby islands of Halki and Alimia.

Conclusion

Tilos is a hidden gem in the Greek islands that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, rich history, and local culture. Whether you are looking for a relaxing beach holiday or an active adventure, Tilos has plenty to offer. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of this beautiful island!
