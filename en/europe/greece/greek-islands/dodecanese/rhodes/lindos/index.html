Introduction to Lindos

Lindos is a picturesque village on the eastern coast of the Greek island of Rhodes. The village is known for its vibrant history, whitewashed houses, winding streets, and iconic landmarks. Lindos is a popular tourist destination because of its beautiful beaches, historic buildings, and delightful Mediterranean climate.

Throughout the village, you will encounter plenty of shops, tavernas, and cafes ideal for avid tourists. The town also offers a free shuttle service that brings visitors from the car park to the town center as vehicles are not permitted inside the medieval walls.

Here is a comprehensive tour guide for Lindos, including all the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Lindos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Acropolis of Lindos – The Acropolis is undoubtedly the most significant attraction in Lindos and considered one of the most impressive ancient structures in Greece. The hill towers over the village and features several ruins of ancient temples from the Hellenistic, Byzantine, and Knights’ periods.

  2. The Church of the Panagia – This medieval church is probably one of the oldest structures in the village, built-in 1300. It features a unique blend of Byzantine and Gothic architecture and is noted for its elegant white bell-tower.

  3. The Tomb of Kleovoulos – This tomb is supposedly the final resting place of Kleovoulos, a wise man from the 6th century BC and a part of the Seven Sages of Greece. Its location, which is about a kilometer from the village, offers spectacular views of the ocean.

  4. The Castle of the Knights of Saint John – Known as the Castle of the Knights of Saint John, the castle was constructed on the site of a fortification dating back to the 14th century. It has a sizeable defensive wall and a rectangular tower overlooking the bay.

Natural Attractions of Lindos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Saint Paul’s Beach – Saint Paul’s is almost an enclosed bay, surrounded by two bays on either side, with crystal-clear water that is perfect for swimming and snorkeling. Lined with tavernas and sun loungers, the beach is an ideal spot to catch a boat ride to other nearby beaches, including Tsambika and Fournoi.

  2. Monte Smith Hill – Monte Smith is a popular viewing platform in Rhodes that provides stunning panoramic views over the ocean and the old town of Rhodes. It is also famous for its archaeological site that features a large statue of Apollo, the famed stadium of Rhodes, and an ancient theatre that can accommodate 8000 spectators.

  3. Vliha Bay – Vliha Bay is a crescent-shaped settlement that is highly recommended for water-based activities. Swimming, kayaking, windsurfing, and paddleboarding are some of the most popular activities here.

  4. Seven Springs (Epta Piges) – Seven Springs is situated in a dense forest that exudes a surreal feel to anyone who visits it. Visitors to resort can walk from the parking lot, which is approximately 200 meters away, to reach the springs at the end of the path. The area is perfect for nature lovers, and there’s a restaurant close by where visitors can dine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lindos

Lindos is ideal for hiking and biking. The location is surrounded by varied terrain, making it suitable for all-level riders. For those who want to explore the route themselves, there are several bike rentals within the village. Below, we have mentioned some of the best trails of Lindos.

  1. Lindos to Lardos Route: This route is 17km long, starting in Lindos and leading to the beautiful village of Lardos and its historical sites. The trip takes approximately 90 minutes to complete.

  2. Lindos to Vlicha Bay: This route is 16km long, beginning in Lindos and leading to Vliha Bay. It is an excellent biking trail that passes through picturesque villages and lush greenery.

  3. Lindos to Pefkos: This route is 14km long starting in Lindos and taking visitors on a ride to the trendy resort town of Pefkos, known for its lovely beaches and stunning natural scenes.

Best Things to Do in Lindos: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore the Medieval Town: Lindos is a charming place to wander around. Visitors can explore the town by wandering its narrow alleys, with dazzling archways, rooftop restaurants with stunning views, and countless shops.

  2. Enjoy Watersports: Lindos is a perfect spot for swimming, windsurfing, and paddle boarding. The place is surrounded by various beaches, making it an ideal weekend destination for seaside activities.

  3. Visit the Village of Lardos: It is a lovely village filled with historical sites and a thriving food and drink scene. The village is just a few miles away from Lindos, and it is worth visiting.

  4. Experience a Local Cooking Class: Greek cuisine is known for its fantastic flavors and healthy ingredients. Visitors can engage in cooking classes where they will learn how to prepare traditional Aegean dishes such as moussaka, dolmades, and tzatziki.

Food and Drink Scene of Lindos

Lindos is home to several hidden-gem restaurants that cater to every culinary preference. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages of Lindos.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lamb Kleftiko – A succulent and tender lamb dish that is cooked with onions, tomatoes, and red wine. It is served with potatoes and is a staple dish in Greek cuisine.

  2. Prawns Saganaki – This dish is made from freshly caught prawns, and it is cooked in a tomato-based sauce flavored with herbs and feta cheese.

  3. Baklava – An iconic dessert of Greek cuisine. It is a flaky pastry filled with walnuts, pistachios, and honey and drizzled with a syrup made from sugar and water.

Local Beverages

  1. Ouzo – A traditional Greek drink made from aniseed, and it gets its unique taste from the distillation of the herb.

  2. Wine – Greece is known for its excellent wine, and Lindos produces several grape varieties that are used to make some of the best local wines.

Where to Stay in Lindos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lindos offers a wide variety of accommodation options that cater to different price points and preferences. Below are some of the top options for tourists in Lindos.

  1. Lindos Blu Luxury Hotel & Suites – Lindos Blu is a hotel located on the cliffs of Vlicha Bay, a few minutes’ drive away from Lindos village. It offers spectacular views of the Mediterranean Sea and is perfect for a luxurious holiday.

  2. AquaGrand Luxury Hotel Lindos – This hotel is situated near Saint Paul’s beach, on a hill with a stunning view of the beach. It features beautiful gardens, spacious rooms and suites, and a fantastic spa.

  3. Lindos Horizon Studios – A perfect option for budget-conscious travelers. The hotel is just a few minutes’ walk from the Lindos village and is comfortable and clean.

What to Buy in Lindos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lindos offers several shopping options for visitors to take home some local products as souvenirs. Below are some of the top things to buy while in Lindos.

  1. Olive Oil Soap – A pure, natural soap that is made from olive oil and is free of harsh chemicals.

  2. Handmade Jewelry – The jewelry shops in Lindos offer a wide range of handcrafted items that make for perfect souvenirs.

  3. Herb and Tea Blends – Lindos’ climate makes it ideal for growing a wide variety of herbs and spices that are used to make delicious blends of tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Lindos

Lindos is rich in cultural heritage, and the locals here take immense pride in their traditions. Below are some of the essential information to keep in mind while in Lindos.

  1. Dress Code – When visiting religious landmarks, it is essential to maintain conservative dress codes.

  2. Tips – It is customary to tip servers in restaurants, but it is not mandatory.

  3. Language – Although Greek is the primary language spoken on the island, English is widely spoken in Lindos, making it convenient for tourists.

Best Time to Visit Lindos

The best time to visit Lindos is from April to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and tourists can enjoy various outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lindos

Do:

  1. Dress Conservatively when visiting religious landmarks
  2. Check out the sunset at Saint Paul’s Bay
  3. Try the local delicacies

Don’t:

  1. Wear swimsuits or beachwear when walking around the town
  2. Litter on the beaches
  3. Forget to carry sunblock and a hat during the summers

Safety and Health

Safety in Lindos

Lindos is a safe place to visit, but it is essential to stay vigilant, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Lindos

Visitors to Greece are advised to have the required vaccinations done before arriving in the country. Adequate travel insurance must also be taken to cover any medical emergencies that may occur.

Getting to Lindos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Lindos via several modes of transport, including private transfers, public transport, and taxis.

Airports Near Lindos

The nearest airports to Lindos are:

  1. Rhodes International Airport – 55 km from Lindos
  2. Kos Island International Airport – 190 km from Lindos

Bus Stations Near Lindos

Lindos Bus Terminal is located approximately 400 meters from the entrance of Lindos. From here, visitors can get buses to destinations such as Rhodes town, Lardos, and Pefkos.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies in Lindos, making it convenient for visitors to explore the island at their leisure. Additionally, ferry services are available from Rhodes to several Greek Islands, including Kos, Kalymnos, and Symi.

How to Get Around in Lindos

Getting around Lindos is easy, with several modes of transport available like:

  1. Walking – Lindos is accessible by foot, and visitors can access most of the town’s attractions by walking.

  2. Taxis – Taxies offer a convenient and affordable way to get around Lindos.

  3. Buses – Buses are an easy and affordable mode of transport, and Lindos is well connected to other parts of Rhodes.

Where to Go Next from Lindos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the destinations that visitors can explore after leaving Lindos:

  1. Rhodes Old Town – A UNESCO world heritage site that features medieval architecture, narrow streets, and spectacular landmarks.

  2. Symi Island – A small island near Rhodes that offers visitors an insight into traditional Greek culture.

  3. Halki Island – A tiny island, which is an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of tourism.

Conclusion

Lindos is a must-visit destination if you are traveling to Greece. The village is rich in history and culture, offers plenty of activities to do, and features some of the most beautiful beaches in the country. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the necessary information to explore the town at your leisure and make the most out of your trip.
