Introduction to Rhodes (city)

Rhodes is the largest of the Dodecanese Islands in the Aegean Sea and is located in the eastern part of the Greek Islands. Its historical significance and beautiful landscapes make it a popular destination for tourists. Rhodes city is the capital of the island and is known for its medieval old town, stunning beaches, and rich culture.

List of Places to Visit in Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city has a lot to offer for travelers of all tastes. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, here are some of the top places to visit in Rhodes city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes: The Palace of the Grand Master is a must-visit historical attraction in Rhodes city. This medieval castle was built by the Knights of Saint John in the 14th century and now houses a museum that displays artifacts from the Byzantine, Ottoman, and Knights periods.

  • Archaeological Museum of Rhodes: The Archaeological Museum of Rhodes is home to an impressive collection of ancient artifacts from throughout Rhodes’ history. It’s a great place to get a comprehensive understanding of the island’s history.

  • Acropolis of Rhodes: The Acropolis of Rhodes is an ancient citadel that dates back to the Hellenistic period. It’s a breathtaking historic site that’s well worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Rhodes (city): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lindos Beach: Lindos Beach is one of the most picturesque beaches on the island and is famous for its crystal-clear waters. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, and relax while admiring the stunning views.

  • Mt. Filerimos: Mount Filerimos is a beautiful hill that offers incredible panoramic views of the island. It’s a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

  • Butterfly Valley: Butterfly Valley is a picturesque nature reserve that’s home to thousands of colorful butterflies. It’s a great place to relax and take in the beauty of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city has a lot of natural beauty that can be explored on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Rhodes city.

  • Medieval Old Town: The medieval old town of Rhodes city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and it’s best explored on foot. It’s a great place to take a leisurely stroll and admire the historic architecture.

  • Rhodes Island National Park: The Rhodes Island National Park is a vast area that’s perfect for hiking and biking. It offers stunning views of the island and is a must-visit for nature lovers.

  • Valley of the Butterflies: The Valley of the Butterflies is a beautiful hiking trail that runs through the stunning Butterfly Valley. It’s a great place to see the natural beauty of the island up close.

Best Things to Do in Rhodes (city): Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Rhodes city, whether you prefer history, culture, or outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Rhodes city.

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to explore the beautiful coastline of the island and experience the stunning views from the water.

  • Food and Wine Tours: A food and wine tour is a great way to experience the local cuisine and culture of Rhodes city.

  • Scuba Diving: The clear waters around the island make it an excellent spot for scuba diving. Explore the underwater beauty of the island with a diving tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city has plenty to offer, and a week is just enough time to explore the best of it. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Rhodes city.

  • Day 1: Explore the medieval old town of Rhodes city
  • Day 2: Visit the Palace of the Grand Master and the Archaeological Museum of Rhodes
  • Day 3: Take a boat tour around the island
  • Day 4: Enjoy a day at the Lindos Beach
  • Day 5: Hike Mt. Filerimos and explore the Valley of Butterflies
  • Day 6: Go scuba diving or take a food and wine tour
  • Day 7: Relax and soak up the sun at one of the island’s beautiful beaches

Food and Drink Scene of Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city offers a diverse range of delicious local cuisines and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvlaki: Souvlaki is a popular Greek dish that consists of grilled meat on a skewer. It’s often served with pita bread and Tzatziki sauce.

  • Moussaka: Moussaka is a traditional Greek dish made with layers of eggplant, ground meat, and béchamel sauce.

  • Fish: Rhodes city is surrounded by water, making it an excellent place to enjoy fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: Ouzo is a popular Greek liquor that’s often enjoyed as an aperitif or digestif.

  • Wine: Rhodes city has a thriving wine industry, and the island is known for producing excellent varietals.

Where to Stay in Rhodes (city): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rhodes city has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and tastes.

  • Luxury Hotels: Some of the best luxury hotels in Rhodes city include the Sheraton Rhodes Resort and the Lindos Blu Luxury Hotel & Suites.

  • Budget Accommodation: Budget travelers can choose from a range of hostels and guesthouses, such as the Stay Hostel Apartments and the Old Town Senses Boutique Hotel.

What to Buy in Rhodes (city): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rhodes city offers a vast array of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home.

  • Olive products: Greece is known for its excellent olives, and Rhodes is no exception. Bring back some olive oil, soap, or food products.

  • Ceramic products: Rhodes has a rich history of pottery, and the island offers beautiful and unique ceramic products.

Local Culture and Customs of Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city has a rich culture and history that’s influenced by various civilizations. The locals are known for their hospitality and warmth.

Best Time to Visit Rhodes (city)

The best time to visit Rhodes city is from May to September when the weather is warm and dry. However, if you prefer fewer crowds, visiting in the shoulder season (April/May or September/October) is also a great option.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rhodes (city)

Do:

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions
  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Do take care of the environment and avoid littering

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect religious sites and customs
  • Don’t ignore the dress code in religious sites
  • Don’t be loud and disruptive in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city is a safe destination, and tourists are unlikely to face any major safety issues. However, it’s always best to take standard precautions, such as avoiding dark alleys and carrying valuables securely.

Health in Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city has excellent medical facilities and clinics. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications and to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Rhodes (city): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rhodes (city)

Rhodes International Airport (Diagoras) is the main airport on the island and is located approximately 14 km from Rhodes city.

Bus Stations Near Rhodes (city)

There are regular bus services that connect Rhodes city to other parts of the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to reach Rhodes city by ferry or by hiring a car.

How to Get Around in Rhodes (city)

Rhodes city is best explored on foot. However, visitors can also use taxis, buses, or rental cars to get around.

Where to Go Next from Rhodes (city): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rhodes city is a great base for exploring other parts of the island. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Lindos: Lindos is a picturesque town located on the eastern coast of the island. It’s famous for its Acropolis and beautiful beaches.

  • Symi: Symi is a small island near Rhodes that offers stunning scenery and historic sites.

Conclusion

Rhodes city is a beautiful destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its medieval old town to its stunning beaches, Rhodes city is a must-visit for any traveler.
