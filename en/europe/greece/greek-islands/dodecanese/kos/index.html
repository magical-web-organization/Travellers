Introduction to Kos

Kos is a stunning island located in the Dodecanese group of Greek islands, known for its sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and idyllic villages. It is also a place full of history and culture, with numerous monuments and ancient ruins. The island is a perfect destination for leisure travelers, history buffs, and adventurers.

List of Places to Visit in Kos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kos is steeped in history and culture, with many museums and historical places to visit. Some of the must-visit sights include:

  • Asklepion: Asklepion is a world-renowned archaeological site located on a hill in the center of the island. It is said to be the birthplace of modern medicine and was dedicated to Asklepios, the god of healing.
  • Castle of the Knights: The Castle of the Knights is a symbol of the island’s Venetian and medieval past. It was built in the 15th century and offers great views of the town and the sea.
  • Hippocrates Tree: The Hippocrates Tree is a historic landmark on the island and is said to be the spot where Hippocrates, the father of modern medicine, taught his students. The plane tree is believed to be more than 2,000 years old.
  • Municipal Market: The Municipal Market is a traditional marketplace that dates back to 1934. It offers a wide range of local products such as olive oil, honey, herbs, and spices.
  • Archaeological Museum: The Archaeological Museum displays a wide range of ancient artifacts from the island, including sculptures, mosaics, and ceramics.

Natural Attractions of Kos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kos is not just a place of history and culture. It is also home to many breathtaking natural attractions. Some of the top ones include:

  • Therma Beach: Therma Beach is located in the village of Agios Fokas and is known for its hot springs. The water is said to have healing properties and is a popular spot for relaxation.
  • Mount Dikeos: Mount Dikeos, the highest mountain on the island, is a beautiful spot for hiking and exploring the island’s natural landscape.
  • Plaka Forest: Plaka Forest is located in the central part of the island and is home to various flora and fauna, as well as natural springs.
  • Aspri Petra Caves: Aspri Petra Caves, located in the village of Antimacheia, are natural caves that are believed to have been used as a place of worship by ancient Greeks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kos

Kos offers some excellent hiking and biking routes that showcase the island’s beauty and natural wilderness. Some popular options include:

  • Asfendiou Trail: This hiking trail allows hikers to explore the stunning Asfendiou Valley and offers panoramic views of the island.
  • Ilias Mountain Trail: The Ilias Mountain Trail is an uphill trail that leads to the summit of Mount Dikeos. The trail offers breathtaking views of the island’s landscape.
  • Paradisi Canyon Trail: The Paradisi Canyon Trail is a relatively easy hiking trail that offers scenic views of the Kos countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kos: Activities and Tours

Kos offers a wide range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some popular options include:

  • Boat Tours: Boat tours are a popular activity on the island. They allow visitors to explore the island’s coastline and nearby islands.
  • Water Sports: Water sports such as windsurfing, kitesurfing, and parasailing are available on many of the island’s beaches.
  • Cooking Classes: Visitors can learn about local cuisine by taking cooking classes that teach them how to prepare traditional Greek dishes.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Kos is known for its wine production, and visitors can take wine tasting tours to explore the island’s vineyards and learn about the winemaking process.
  • Yoga Retreats: Kos is a popular destination for yoga retreats, offering visitors an opportunity to relax, rejuvenate, and connect with nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kos

Day 1: Explore the town of Kos and visit landmarks such as the Hippocrates Tree and the Castle of the Knights.

Day 2: Take a boat tour to nearby islands such as Kalymnos and Pserimos.

Day 3: Visit the Asklepion and the Archaeological Museum.

Day 4: Explore the countryside on a bike tour or hike.

Day 5: Relax on one of the island’s beaches, such as Tigaki or Kefalos.

Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and explore the island’s vineyards.

Day 7: Visit the traditional villages of Zia and Pyli and explore the island’s local culture and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Kos

Must-Try Dishes

Kos is known for its delicious Greek cuisine, with many traditional dishes that visitors must try. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Moussaka: Moussaka is a baked dish made with layers of eggplant, minced meat, and tomato sauce, topped with a creamy béchamel sauce.
  • Souvlaki: Souvlaki is a popular Greek dish made of grilled meat (usually pork or chicken) served with vegetables and pita bread.
  • Spanakopita: Spanakopita is a savory pastry made of spinach and feta cheese wrapped in phyllo dough.
  • Feta cheese: Feta cheese is a staple of Greek cuisine and is used in many dishes.

Local Beverages

Kos is also known for its wine production, with many local wineries producing quality wines. Some of the popular local beverages include:

  • Retsina: Retsina is a traditional Greek wine that is flavored with pine resin.
  • Ouzo: Ouzo is a strong anise-flavored liquor that is popular in Greece.

Where to Stay in Kos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kos offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Astir Odysseus Kos Resort & Spa: This luxurious resort offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea and has a spa, pool, and multiple restaurants.
  • Kos Bay Hotel: This mid-range hotel is located near the town center and offers comfortable rooms and a pool.
  • Phaethon Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is located near the beach and offers basic rooms and a pool.

What to Buy in Kos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kos offers many souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home as gifts. Some popular options include:

  • Olive oil: Greece is known for its olive oil production, and visitors can purchase high-quality olive oil from many local shops.
  • Honey: Kos is also known for its honey production, with many local beekeepers producing delicious honey.
  • Sponges: Sponges are a traditional product of the island, with many shops selling natural sponges that are harvested from the Aegean Sea.

Local Culture and Customs of Kos

Kos has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions and customs that visitors should be aware of. Some important things to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for religion and customs: Greece is a religious country, and visitors should be respectful of the Orthodox Christian customs and traditions.
  • Dress code: Visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not obligatory in Greece, but it is customary to leave a small tip (around 10%) for good service.

Best Time to Visit Kos

The best time to visit Kos is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island is buzzing with tourists and activities. July and August are the busiest months, while May and September offer a quieter and more relaxed atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kos

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and sample traditional dishes.
  • Do visit the archaeological sites and historical landmarks.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t expect everyone to speak English, especially in rural areas.
  • Don’t visit religious sites in beachwear.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kos

Kos is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Kos

Kos has a good healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics on the island. Visitors should have travel health insurance and be aware of the general health risks, such as sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Kos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kos can be conveniently reached by air and sea.

Airports Near Kos

The island has an international airport, Kos Island International Airport, with direct flights from many European cities.

Bus Stations Near Kos

Kos has a good bus network, with buses connecting the major towns and villages on the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kos by ferry, with daily services from Piraeus, Rhodes, and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Kos

Kos can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by car. Bike rentals are widely available on the island, while car rentals can be booked in advance or on arrival.

Where to Go Next from Kos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kos is a great base for exploring nearby islands and destinations, such as:

  • Kalymnos: Kalymnos is a beautiful island known for its crystal-clear waters and stunning scenery. It is a popular destination for diving and rock climbing.
  • Rhodes: Rhodes is a historic and picturesque island, known for its medieval town and ancient ruins.
  • Bodrum: Bodrum is a Turkish town across the sea from Kos. It is known for its lively nightlife and scenic views.

Conclusion

Kos is a beautiful and diverse island that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation, an adventurous outdoor experience, or a cultural and historical journey, Kos has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip to Kos with ease and make the most of your time on the island.
