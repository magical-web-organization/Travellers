Astypalaia is a stunning island in the Aegean Sea that is part of the Dodecanese island chain in Greece. Known for its picturesque landscape and crystal-clear waters, Astypalaia is a destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and rich cultural experiences. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the highlights of this wonderful Greek island destination.

Introduction to Astypalaia

Astypalaia has a rich cultural history dating back to the Neolithic Period, which is visible through the island’s architecture and archaeological sites. The island has been under different regimes throughout history including the Byzantines, the Venetians, and the Ottomans, which has played a significant role in shaping the local culture. Today, Astypalaia is a great representation of the fusion of different cultures.

List of Places to Visit in Astypalaia

Astypalaia has a myriad of places that offer year-round entertainment and excitement. Here’s a list of must-see sights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Venetian Castle: The iconic castle dates back to the 14th century and offers a spectacular view of the Aegean Sea.
  • Querini Castle: The castle was built by the Venetians in the late 15th century to protect the island from pirate attacks.
  • Archaeological Museum of Astypalaia: The museum highlights the archaeological finds from the island.
  • Astypalaia Windmills: An enchanting array of historic windmills situated atop a hill in Chora of Astypalaia.

Natural Attractions of Astypalaia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Livadia Beach: Located in the heart of Astypalaia, this picturesque beach is great spot for swimming, snorkeling, and other water activities.
  • Kaminakia Beach: A secluded and less crowded option, Kaminakia Beach has clear waters and beautiful sand.
  • Vatses Beach: Accessible by boat, Vatses Beach is part of a nature reserve and boasts crystal-clear waters.
  • Tria Nisia: A trio of uninhabited islands just off the coast of Astypalaia, perfect for a day trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Astypalaia

Exploring the island on foot or by bike is a great way to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Astypalaia. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Chora Trail: A short 1.5-kilometre route that takes you through the traditional white buildings of Chora.
  • The Koutsomytis Trail: Perfect for beginners, this 3-kilometre trail offers stunning views of the island’s landscape and coastline.
  • The Maltezi Trail: For more experienced hikers, this 6-kilometre trail takes you on an adventure through forests and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Astypalaia: Activities and Tours

Astypalaia has a wide range of activities and tours, from water sports to cultural experiences. Here are some of the best:

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the hidden coves and rugged coastline of Astypalaia by sea kayak.
  • Traditional Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Greek cuisine from a local chef.
  • Scuba Diving: Discover the underwater world of Astypalaia and discover shipwrecks and marine life.
  • Sunset Sailing: Take a romantic sunset sailing trip and admire the stunning Aegean Sea under the golden glow of the setting sun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Astypalaia

If you have a week to explore Astypalaia, here’s a recommended itinerary to make the most of your time:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the Island of Chora
  • Day 3: Visit the Historic Sites and Museums
  • Day 4: Relax on the Beach
  • Day 5: Take an Adventure Trip
  • Day 6: Indulge in Local Cuisine and Explore Local Shops
  • Day 7: Short Excursions to the Surrounding Islands

Food and Drink Scene of Astypalaia

Astypalaia is known for its traditional Greek cuisine and local delicacies. Here’s our list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marathopites: A traditional savory pie made with local herbs and greens.
  • Fava: A traditional Greek dish made of yellow split peas, olive oil, and onions.
  • Psarosoupa: A Greek fish soup made with various types of fish and vegetables.
  • Stuffed Eggplant: A vegetarian dish made of eggplants stuffed with onions, tomatoes, and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Rakomelo: A Greek liqueur made from raki and honey.
  • Wine: Astypalaia is known for its dry white wines made from local grapes.

Where to Stay in Astypalaia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Astypalaia has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Vithos Rooms: A charming guesthouse that offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea.
  • Kalderimi Traditional Houses: A cozy, traditional guesthouse with a unique atmosphere.
  • Astropalia Hotel: A luxury hotel with a modern design and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Astypalaia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Astypalaia offers a range of traditional products that make for perfect souvenirs. Here are some of the best to bring home:

  • Handcrafted Ceramics: Astypalaia is known for its ceramic products, including plates, bowls, mugs, and more.
  • Olive Oil: Greece is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Astypalaia has some of the best.
  • Honey: Locally produced honey is a must-try when visiting Astypalaia.
  • Spices: Astypalaia’s herbs and spices are a great way to add a touch of Greek cuisine to your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Astypalaia

Astypalaia’s traditional culture and customs are an essential part of the island’s identity. Here are some things to know before visiting:

  • Respect the traditional architecture of the island
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites
  • Avoid wastefulness and water conservation

Best Time to Visit Astypalaia

The best time to visit Astypalaia is between May and October when the weather is warm, and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, if you prefer a quieter atmosphere, visit the island in the shoulder seasons of April and November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Astypalaia

To make the most of your time in Astypalaia, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the island’s stunning natural beauty
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect religious sites and traditions
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Astypalaia

Astypalaia is a relatively safe destination with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to take precautions to keep yourself safe during your trip.

Health in Astypalaia

The island has one medical center and a pharmacy, which can assist with minor medical issues. Ensure you have travel insurance to cover emergencies. It is also necessary to have up-to-date vaccinations/inoculations before visiting.

Getting to Astypalaia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Astypalaia

The island has an airport operating year-round with daily flights from Athens. The flight duration is approximately 50 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Astypalaia

Astypalaia has a bus terminal that connects with major Greek towns and cities, including Athens.

Other Options

The island has several car rental companies that can provide you with flexibility and convenience to explore at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Astypalaia

Astypalaia has a well-connected network of roads with plenty of transport options:

  • Buses: Astypalaia has a regular, reliable bus service that takes you to all parts of the island.
  • Taxis: Taxis offer a convenient and stress-free way to travel to your destination in Astypalaia.
  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are an excellent option if you plan to travel more, offering greater freedom and flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Astypalaia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Astypalaia is well-connected to other Greek islands, making it a perfect base for exploring the wider region. Here are some nearby destinations:

  • Kalymnos: Known for its natural beauty and limestone rock formations, Kalymnos is a destination for rock climbers and hikers.
  • Rhodes: A historic island with ancient ruins and a vibrant nightlife.
  • Nisyros: A unique island with an active volcano and picturesque villages.

Conclusion

Astypalaia is a dream destination for those looking for natural beauty and rich cultural experiences. This comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit to this picturesque Greek island.
