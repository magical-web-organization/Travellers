Introduction to Kasos

Kasos is a small island in the southernmost region of Greece’s Dodecanese group. It is a hidden gem that seems to go unnoticed by many tourists. However, it has a rich history and beautiful landscapes that you won’t find anywhere else.

Despite its small size, Kasos has a lot to offer its visitors. Whether you’re seeking an adventure in the great outdoors or looking for a relaxing getaway, this island has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll highlight some of the top attractions to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Kasos

Kasos is an island full of beautiful landscapes, historic sites, and cultural landmarks. Here are a few places that you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Folklore Museum of Kasos: This museum features exhibits that offer insight into the island’s history, its traditional way of life, and its local customs.
  • The Church of Agia Marina: This charming church dates back to the 19th century and is one of Kasos’ most notable cultural landmarks.
  • The Archaeological Museum of Kasos: Discover artifacts and monuments from Kasos’ ancient past, including Hellenistic, Roman, and Christian times.

Natural Attractions of Kasos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Trail of Agios Andonios: This hiking trail is just over 2 miles long but offers some of the most stunning views of the island.
  • The Beach of Emborio: This is one of the island’s most picturesque beaches and is perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • The Caves of Antiperatos: Explore the unique cave formations and diverse flora and fauna in this nature preserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kasos

Hiking and biking are two of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Kasos. Here are some of the top routes to consider:

  • The Trail of Mount Prionas: This 4-mile trail takes you on a scenic journey through the stunning landscapes of Kasos.
  • Cycling Tour of the Island: Take a leisurely bike ride around the island and explore hidden corners and breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Kasos: Activities and Tours

Kasos has a lot to offer its visitors. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Day Trip to the nearby island of Karpathos: Explore the rugged and mountainous terrain of the island just north of Kasos.
  • Boat Excursion to Makronissos Beach: This remote and isolated beach is a true hidden gem that can only be accessed by boat.
  • Scuba Diving Trip: Kasos is home to some of the most beautiful dive spots in Greece. Explore the underwater world and its marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kasos

For visitors who want to stay a little longer, here is a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time on the island:

Food and Drink Scene of Kasos

No trip to Kasos is complete without sampling the local culinary delights. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Krasotyri: A cheese made from sheep’s milk and matured in wine barrels.
  • Kasian Pie: A savory pie made with Kasios cheese, butter, eggs, and fine phyllo dough.
  • Fried Kasos Goat: A succulent leg of goat, flavored with garlic, rosemary, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: A traditional Greek anise-flavored alcoholic drink, typically served with ice and water.
  • Raki: A homemade distillate made from the leftover grape pomace, similar to Italy’s grappa.
  • Karpathian Wine: A Dodecanesean wine with a distinct flavor, thanks to the volcanic soil.

Where to Stay in Kasos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodation options on the island to suit every taste and budget. Here are some of the options to consider:

  • Kassos Bay Hotel: A luxurious hotel situated just 2 km from the airport, offering stunning views of the Aegean Sea.
  • Kasos Villas: A collection of breathtaking villas with infinity pools and views of the island’s rugged landscapes.
  • Niriides Studios: A family-run guesthouse in Fry, Kasos’ capital that offers comfortable rooms and warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Kasos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kasos has a rich tradition of craft-making, so there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some items you won’t want to miss:

  • Handmade Ceramics: Kasos has a long history of ceramic production dating back to the classical period.
  • Embroidery: The traditional embroidery of Kasos has won awards and is highly prized for its quality and fine detail.
  • Krasotyri Cheese: Take a piece of Kasos home with you. You can buy this tasty cheese at the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kasos

Kasos has a unique culture and customs worth noting. Here are a few things that you should keep in mind as a visitor:

  • The island is deeply rooted in traditional customs, so take time to learn about the local celebrations, such as the panigyria (religious festival).
  • Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites such as the Church of Agia Marina.
  • The islanders speak a Kasian dialect of Greek, so some common phrases may be useful to know before you visit.

Best Time to Visit Kasos

The best time to visit Kasos is between May and September, when the weather is warm, and the seas are calm. However, visitors should be aware that the high tourist season can make the island crowded at times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kasos

To ensure a smooth trip, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind while visiting Kasos:

Do:

  • Respect Kasian customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take time to explore the island’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in public places.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Be disrespectful or overly loud in public places.

Safety and Health

Keeping yourself safe and healthy is always a top priority when traveling. Here’s what you should know:

Safety in Kasos

  • Kasos is a safe island with low crime rates.
  • Visitors should take care when swimming in the sea, as currents can be strong.
  • Roads in Kasos may be steep and winding, so take care when driving or cycling.

Health in Kasos

  • Drinking tap water in Kasos is safe, but bottled water is always a better choice.
  • Visitors should apply sunscreen to protect against the strong Aegean sun.
  • Bring any necessary medication, as local healthcare facilities may be limited.

Getting to Kasos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kasos is a remote island, but it is accessible via several transportation options. Here are some of the ways to get to Kasos:

Airports Near Kasos

  • Kasos Island Airport: Flights operate from Athens airport, Rhodes, and Karpathos.

Bus Stations Near Kasos

  • There is a bus station in Merihas, with direct connections to the ports of Neos Marmaras and Pireaus.

Other Options

  • Public ferry services operate from Piraeus to Kasos.
  • You can also access the island from Karpathos and Rhodes.

How to Get Around in Kasos

The island is small and easy to explore, with several transportation options available:

  • Rental cars and motorbikes: These can be rented on arrival and are an excellent option for visitors who want to explore the island’s remote corners.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in the towns and villages.
  • Bicycles: Visitors can rent bicycles to explore the island at a slower pace.

Where to Go Next from Kasos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Greece after your visit to Kasos, here are some nearby destinations worth considering:

  • Karpathos Island: This nearby island has a rugged landscape and unspoiled beaches.
  • Rhodes: With its rich medieval history, Rhodes is a must-visit destination for any visitor to Greece.
  • Crete: This large island has so much to offer visitors, from stunning beaches to fascinating cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Kasos is a hidden gem in Greece that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural experiences and stunning natural landscapes. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, this island has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your visit to Kasos, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
