Introduction to Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo, also known as Megisti, is a small Greek island located in the Dodecanese group of islands in the southeastern Aegean Sea. It is the easternmost island of Greece and one of the most remote and secluded destinations in the country, offering a unique experience to visitors. Kastellorizo boasts colorful neoclassical houses, cobblestone streets, and an idyllic harbor. It is a perfect destination for those who like to escape crowds and enjoy a peaceful vacation in a lovely setting. The island has a fascinating history, and its cultural heritage is evident in the architecture, museums, and traditions that are still preserved today.

List of Places to Visit in Kastellorizo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Castle of the Knights of St. John

The Castle of the Knights of St. John is the most prominent landmark in Kastellorizo and offers a panoramic view of the island. The castle was built in the 14th century by the Knights of St. John to defend the island’s harbor against pirates and invaders.

The Museum of Kastellorizo

The Museum of Kastellorizo is located in the central square, and it exhibits a collection of significant materials and artifacts, including the traditional costume of the island, old photographs, and a model of a Kastellorizian ship. There is also a section dedicated to the island’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Kastellorizo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Blue Cave

The Blue Cave is one of the most popular attractions on the island and is located on the northeast coast of Kastellorizo. It is a sea cave with crystal-clear blue water that reflects the sunlight, creating a magical effect. Visitors can access the cave by boat, and it is recommended to visit in the morning when the light is at its best.

The Lycian Tomb

The Lycian Tomb is an ancient tomb located on a hill overlooking the harbor. It is carved into the rock and dates back to the 4th century BC. The tomb is an impressive sight, and it offers a magnificent view of the island and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo offers plenty of opportunities for walking and hiking. There are many trails that lead to scenic viewpoints and secluded coves. One of the most popular routes is the walk to Mandraki Bay, a secluded beach on the island’s east coast. Another popular trail is the walk to the Palaiokastro, an ancient acropolis, located on a hill above the town.

Best Things to Do in Kastellorizo: Activities and Tours

Kastellorizo offers plenty of activities that will keep visitors entertained, including:

  • Swimming and snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of the Aegean Sea.
  • Exploring the island’s beaches, some of the most beautiful in Greece.
  • Renting a boat and taking a tour around the island, enjoying the stunning coastline and hidden coves.
  • Taking a day trip to nearby islands like Ro and Strongyli.
  • Enjoying the island’s nightlife, which is relatively quiet but has many lovely tavernas and bars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kastellorizo

Day 1: Arrival and explore the town and harbor.

Day 2: Visit the Blue Cave and take a boat tour around the island.

Day 3: Hike to the Palaiokastro for stunning views, followed by a lazy afternoon on the beach.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Ro Island, home to a rare species of seals, and enjoy the stunning nature.

Day 5: Visit the Museum of Kastellorizo to learn about the island’s history and culture, followed by an evening visit to the castle for sunset views.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby island of Strongyli, famous for its WW2 naval wrecks and underwater ruins.

Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day in Kastellorizo, exploring the town and soaking in the sun and the relaxed atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Kastellorizo

Must-Try Dishes

Kastellorizo has a unique and delicious cuisine, influenced by the island’s history and location. Some of the popular local dishes to try are:

  • Megisti pie, a savory pie with local cheese, spinach, and herbs.

  • Psarosoupa, a fish soup with fresh tomatoes, onions, and herbs.

  • Avgotaraho, a type of caviar made from the eggs of grey mullet, a delicacy unique to the island.

Local Beverages

Kastellorizo also has a strong tradition of producing local drinks. Raki, a spirit made from distilled grape must, is one of the most popular drinks on the island. There are also several excellent local wineries, producing wines from indigenous grape varieties, such as Athiri and Aspro.

What to Buy in Kastellorizo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kastellorizo offers a wide range of unique souvenir options for travelers, including:

  • Local herbs, such as thyme and rosemary, used in traditional dishes.

  • Traditional handmade ceramics and embroidery.

  • Megisti sponges, prized for their soft texture and quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo has a unique culture that reflects the island’s history and location. The locals still celebrate traditional dances and customs, such as Easter and carnivals. One of the most significant cultural events is the Fisherman’s Feast, held in August, where the locals celebrate the sea and the island’s fishing tradition.

Best Time to Visit Kastellorizo

The best time to visit Kastellorizo is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and dry. This period is also the peak season, when the island is busiest. Those who prefer a quieter experience may consider visiting in April, May, or October, when the crowds are fewer, and the weather is still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kastellorizo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious places.

  • Sample the local cuisine and wines.

  • Take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of the Aegean Sea.

Don’t:

  • Leave without exploring the island’s natural beauty and cultural heritage.

  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.

  • Cause damage to the environment by littering or disturbing wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo is a safe destination, and crime is rare on the island. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to prevent theft or other mishaps, such as not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo has a medical center, but visitors should bring their own prescription medicines and medical insurance. It is also advisable to take precautions to prevent illnesses, such as wearing sunscreen and drinking plenty of water.

Getting to Kastellorizo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kastellorizo

There is only one airport near Kastellorizo, the Kastellorizo Island Public Airport. It operates flights to Athens and other Greek islands during the peak season.

Bus Stations Near Kastellorizo

There are no bus stations on the island, but visitors can take a ferry or a boat to reach Kastellorizo from other Greek islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kastellorizo by ferry from Rhodes Island or by taking a private boat tour from Kas in Turkey.

How to Get Around in Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo is a small island, and the best way to get around is by foot or bicycle. There are also taxis available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Kastellorizo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kastellorizo is an ideal starting point for exploring other nearby islands in the Dodecanese group or for taking a day trip to Kas in Turkey. Rhodes, the largest Dodecanese island, is only a few hours ferry ride away and is worth visiting for its stunning medieval architecture, beaches, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Kastellorizo is a hidden gem in the Aegean Sea, offering visitors a unique experience with its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and relaxed atmosphere.
