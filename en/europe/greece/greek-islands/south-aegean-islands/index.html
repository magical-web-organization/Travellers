Introduction to South Aegean Islands

South Aegean Islands is a beautiful cluster of islands located in the southeastern part of Greece. This stunning archipelago, home to over 200 islands, provides a picturesque glimpse into the Greek culture and lifestyle. The islands are divided into five different groups - Cyclades, Dodecanese, Northeastern Aegean, Sporades, and Saronic Gulf Islands, each with its own distinct characteristics and charm. The crystal-clear blue waters, golden sandy beaches, whitewashed houses, and ancient ruins make the South Aegean Islands a mecca for tourists seeking to escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

  • Karpathos Island - Olympos village to Diafani
  • Santorini Island - Fira to Oia hiking trail
  • Naxos Island - Mylopotas beach to Agia Kyriaki trail
  • Skiathos Island - Koukounaries to Lalaria hiking

Food and Drink Scene of South Aegean Islands

Must-Try Dishes

The South Aegean Islands are famous for their mouth-watering dishes that you should sample while visiting, including:

  • Dakos - A traditional salad with tomatoes and feta cheese over a toasted barley rusk.
  • Moussaka - A classic Greek dish made with layers of potatoes, eggplant, and beef or lamb.
  • Souvlaki - Grilled meat skewers with vegetables and potatoes.
  • Fava - A delicious dish made from yellow split peas.
  • Fish soup - A hearty soup made with fresh fish.

Local Beverages

Here are some of the local beverages of the South Aegean Islands:

  • Raki - A strong alcoholic drink made from fermented grape skins.
  • Ouzo - A popular Greek liqueur made from aniseed.
  • Santorini’s Wines - Greek wines that are unique due to the island’s volcanic soil.
  • Greek Coffee - A strong brew served with sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of South Aegean Islands

The South Aegean Islands have a rich cultural heritage that you must respect and observe when visiting. Here are some of the cultural practices to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches, monasteries or other religious sites.
  • Avoid making a sign of ‘OK’ with your fingers, as it is considered rude.
  • Sit and eat with your hands, if offered, during traditional meals.
  • Show respect to elders and greet people warmly.

Best Time to Visit South Aegean Islands

The best time to visit the South Aegean Islands is from April to June and September to October when the temperatures are mild, and the beaches and tourist attractions are less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Aegean Islands

Do:

  • Respect the cultural customs and traditions of the locals.
  • Bring your own beach towels and sunscreen to save money.
  • Try out the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes as there are numerous cobblestone streets.
  • Be polite and respectful to the locals and other tourists.

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe nude or topless on the beaches.
  • Remove ancient artifacts or rocks from their original location.
  • Use flash photography in museums and other cultural sites.
  • Get drunk in public areas and disturb the peace.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Aegean Islands

The South Aegean Islands are generally safe, but accidents and thefts can happen. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Store your valuables in your hotel safe or carry them with you.
  • Beware of pickpockets and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Always wear a seatbelt when traveling by car or taxi.
  • Be careful while swimming as the currents can be strong.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions and natural disasters like wildfires.

Health in South Aegean Islands

It is advisable to carry a small first aid kit with you when traveling to the islands. Here are some other health tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water.
  • Wear sunscreen and avoid excessive exposure to the sun.
  • If you have any allergies or medical conditions, carry your medication.
  • Use insect repellents to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Consult your doctor before traveling and get vaccinated if necessary.

The main airports near the South Aegean Islands are:

  • Rhodes International Airport (Diagoras) (RHO)
  • Mykonos Island National Airport (JMK)
  • Santorini International Airport (JTR)
  • Heraklion International Airport (HER)
  • Kos Island International Airport (KGS)

Bus Stations Near South Aegean Islands

The islands are well connected via buses, and the main bus stations are located in:

  • Rhodes
  • Mykonos
  • Santorini
  • Crete

Other Options

Apart from air and bus travel options, you can also get to the South Aegean Islands by ferry. You can take ferries from:

  • Athens (Piraeus port)
  • Heraklion, Crete
  • Kos Island
  • Paros

How to Get Around in South Aegean Islands

The best way to explore the South Aegean Islands is on foot or via public transportation. Buses and rental cars are available, but driving can be challenging due to narrow, winding roads. Taxis and bikes are also popular options.
