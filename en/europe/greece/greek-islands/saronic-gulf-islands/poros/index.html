Poros, situated in the Saronic Gulf Island of Greece, is an idyllic holiday destination that offers a mix of historical landmarks, spectacular natural beauty, and charming local culture. With its crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, quaint village squares, and traditional architecture, Poros is the perfect escape for those looking to relax and unwind. This guide provides all the information you need to make the most of your visit, from the top attractions to visit in Poros to where to eat, stay, and shop.

Introduction to Poros

Poros is a small island located approximately 58 kilometers from Athens. The island is connected to the mainland by a narrow strip of land, making it easily accessible by ferry from Piraeus, or by car from other major cities. The island is known for its quaint, traditional atmosphere with picturesque streets and a laid-back vibe that transports visitors back in time.

List of Places to Visit in Poros

Poros has plenty of cultural, historical, and natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Archeological Museum of Poros: Exhibits artifacts from ancient Poros, including ancient pottery and an impressive mosaic floor.
  • The Monastery of Zoodochos Pighi: A historical monastery dating back to the 18th century with a beautiful courtyard, museum, and a stunning view of the town of Poros.
  • The Russian Naval Station: A cultural landmark that commemorates the presence of Russian soldiers in Poros during the Greek War of Independence.

Natural Attractions of Poros: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hionon: A popular spot for hikers, with stunning views of Poros town and the surrounding islands.
  • Love Bay: A secluded and picturesque beach located on the northern shore of Poros Island, known for its pristine turquoise waters and rocky cliffs.
  • Lemon Forest: A scenic forest that connects the towns of Poros and Galatas, providing a lovely spot for a peaceful walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Poros

For adventure enthusiasts, Poros offers a range of hiking and biking trails that lead you to the most scenic parts of the island. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Poros to Galatas Trail: A gentle walk through the Lemon Forest that offers stunning views of both Poros and Galatas towns.
  • The Monastery of Zoodochos Pighi and Lighthouse Trail: A challenging trail that takes you through the forest to the monastery and then up to the panoramic viewpoint of Poros Lighthouse.

Best Things to Do in Poros: Activities and Tours

Poros is a hub for water sports activities, including canoeing, kayaking, and swimming with the chance to spot dolphins. Some of the other things to do in Poros are:

  • Rent a boat to explore the island’s hidden coves and bays.
  • Discover the deep waters of Poros and enjoy snorkeling or diving in different depths.
  • Take a sunset cruise and witness the stunning amber hues with a glass of wine in your hand.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poros

With numerous attractions to see in Poros, a week-long itinerary is perfect to see the highlights of the island. Here’s an itinerary that we recommend:

  • Day 1: Start your day with a visit to the Archaeological Museum of Poros. After lunch, go for a refreshing dip at one of Poros’s beaches then proceed to dinner at one of the traditional tavernas.
  • Day 2: Go for a morning hike up to the Monastery of Zoodochos Pighi and take a peek into the 18th-century monastery’s museum. After that, cross over to Galatas and enjoy some afternoon coffee and mezzes.
  • Day 3: Take a boat trip to Daskalio and go snorkeling and exploring the island all day long.
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and cycle to some of the island’s unspoilt beaches like Neorion.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Russian naval base to understand the region’s history.
  • Day 6: Take a sunset cruise around the island and admire the beautiful amber colors of the sunset over the Saronic Gulf.
  • Day 7: Spend the last day exploring Poros’s streets and architecture by yourself. Savor some great Greek food and enjoy your time before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Poros

Greek cuisine is famous worldwide, and Poros Island is no different. Here are some dishes to try and beverages to sample whilst in Poros:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tzatziki: A tangy yogurt dip that adds flavor to any meal.
  • Fava: A type of split-pea puree, typically served as an appetizer.
  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat skewers with vegetables and traditionally served with tzatziki.

Local Beverages

  • Ouzo: An anise-flavored drink that’s usually served as an aperitif.
  • Retsina: A traditional Greek wine flavored with pine resin.

Where to Stay in Poros: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Poros has something for everyone when it comes to accommodation options, whether you’re looking for budget-friendly hostels or luxurious resorts. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Green Hill Suites: For luxury seekers, Green Hill Suites offers spacious, fully equipped suites with balconies that feature breathtaking sea views.
  • Golden View Beach Hotel: The Golden View Beach Hotel offers guests a great location, right by the beach, and has great options for delicious breakfast.
  • Cascade Wellness and Lifestyle Resort: Cascade Wellness and Lifestyle Resort is a fantastic choice if you’re looking to be pampered, with an on-site spa, fitness center, and private beach.

What to Buy in Poros: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take back home, Poros won’t disappoint. Some items worth considering include:

  • Olive oil: Greece is famous for excellent quality olive oil, and Poros’s Olive Tours provide visitors with everything they need to know about the making of the ‘liquid gold.’
  • Handmade Ceramics: Grab a unique souvenir that signifies Greece’s ceramics creation by collecting plates, cups, glasses, and a collection of other assorted ceramic pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Poros

Poros Island has a rich cultural heritage, and locals are proud of their history. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind while there

  • Churches and Monasteries are seen as sacred places; therefore, it’s important to dress appropriately (no tank tops or miniskirts).
  • Greeks eat their dinners late in the evening; most restaurants aren’t open before 8 pm.
  • During August, Poros takes part in the Regatta, a significant cultural event that sees teams from all over Greece race to become the champion of champions.

Best Time to Visit Poros

The best time to visit Poros is between June and September when the weather is sunny, and the days are long. The high season is in July and August, so if you prefer fewer crowds, plan your visit in June or September. Remember to pack comfortable clothes, and bring some sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun during the harsh Greek summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poros

When planning for Poros, it’s always helpful to know what to expect and what you should avoid.

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Protect yourself from the harsh sun rays.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the heat; the sun can be unforgiving, so bring the necessary sun protection.
  • Don’t litter or damage historical sites and monuments.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for religious sites.

Safety and Health

Poros is generally a safe island and harbours no significant health warnings. The Greek health system has great precision, but it’s highly recommended to arrange travel insurance before you arrive to avoid any unforeseen costs.

Safety in Poros

  • Always make sure to lock your accommodation’s windows and doors while leaving.
  • Be aware of sudden weather changes, especially when taking hiking or sea routes.
  • Follow the directions of the staff when indulging in water sports activities

Health in Poros

  • Drink bottled water to avoid any complications.
  • It’s always best to bring a first-aid kit with you, especially if you’re going to be indulging in any outdoor activities.

Getting to Poros: Transportation Options and Logistics

Poros has excellent connectivity with the rest of Greece. There are multiple options available to reach the island:

Airports Near Poros

The nearest airport to Poros is the Athens International Airport located 58 km away.

Bus Stations Near Poros

Buses run daily from Athens to Galatas, the nearest station to Poros, every hour starting at 6 am. Galatas is a short ferry ride away from Poros.

Other Options

The other options to reach Poros are by taxi or private transfers from the airport or Athens city. Ferries run every day from Piraeus port to Poros.

How to Get Around in Poros

Walking is the easiest way to get around in the town areas of the island. However, if you’re planning to explore the villages and beaches, it might be best to rent a car or bike. Taxis and the public transport system on Poros are reliable and suitable for most tourists, with frequent buses traveling back and forth to the main areas of the island.

Where to Go Next from Poros: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Poros is an excellent base to explore the other islands and destinations nearby in Greece. Some options for excursions and nearby destinations include:

  • Aegina Island: Located approximately 20 km from Poros, Aegina is a stunning, traditional island with beautiful architecture and culture to explore.
  • Hydra Island: A picturesque island with no cars and an old-world feel. Explore the quaint streets by foot or hire a donkey.
  • Athens: For a city break, visit Athens, Greece’s capital, and a treasure trove of ancient sites.

Conclusion

Poros is one of Greece’s hidden gems, offering visitors unparalleled natural beauty, a rich cultural heritage, and an authentic Greek experience. Whether you’re planning a romantic getaway or a family vacation, this guide will help you navigate the island’s top attractions, where to eat and drink, and what to expect during your visit. As you explore the island’s quaint streets, charming villages, and beautiful beaches, you’re sure to fall in love with the island’s laid-back atmosphere and breathtaking scenery.
