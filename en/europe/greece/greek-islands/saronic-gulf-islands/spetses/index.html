Introduction to Spetses

Spetses is a Greek island located in the Saronic Gulf Islands, in the Attica region of Greece. It is a charming and serene island that is perfect for a relaxing vacation away from the hustle and bustle of the city. Spetses Island has a rich history and a unique culture that attracts thousands of tourists every year.

List of Places to Visit in Spetses

Spetses is a beautiful island with many attractions to explore. For your convenience, we have divided the attractions into two categories: culturally important attractions, and natural attractions of Spetses.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of Spetses is its rich history, which is evident in the numerous museums and historical places that dot the island. Here are some of the must-visit ones:

  1. The Spetses Museum: The museum is housed in the mansion of Hatzigiannis-Mexis and showcases the island’s rich history, culture, and traditions.

  2. The House of Bouboulina: The museum is located in the old port of Spetses and gives visitors insight into the life of the Greek heroine and revolutionary, Laskarina Bouboulina.

  3. The Church of Agios Nikolaos: This is one of the oldest churches on the island and is located near the harbor. It is a popular attraction for tourists who want to discover the religious and cultural traditions of the island.

Natural Attractions of Spetses: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spetses is a perfect destination for those who love outdoor activities and nature. The island is home to pristine beaches, scenic hiking trails, and stunning landscapes. Here are some of the best natural attractions of Spetses:

  1. Beaches: The island boasts of several gorgeous beaches, each with its unique charm. Some of the popular beaches include Agia Paraskevi, Agioi Anargyroi, and Xylokeriza.

  2. Biking and Hiking Trails: You can explore the island’s scenic beauty by hiking or biking along the trails that meander through the landscape.

  3. Cave of Bekiris: This secluded cave is located near Agios Konstantinos and is a popular attraction for visitors who want to experience the island’s natural splendor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spetses

Spetses Island’s beautifully rugged landscape makes it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The island has several hiking and biking routes that cater to different levels of expertise. Here are some of the popular ones:

  1. Old Village Tour: This route covers about 7 kilometers and takes you through the charming traditional villages of the island.

  2. Lighthouse Tour: This route, which covers about 11 kilometers, takes you to the lighthouse on the island’s southern tip, where you can enjoy stunning panoramic views of the ocean.

  3. Spetses Ring Road: The Spetses Ring Road is a popular biking route that covers around 26 kilometers and takes you through some of the island’s most beautiful landscapes and attractions.

Best Things to Do in Spetses: Activities and Tours

Spetses is a perfect holiday destination for those who love outdoor activities and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to do on the island:

  1. Horse-drawn carriage ride: This is a must-do experience on the island. You can take a relaxed ride through the old town and enjoy the beautiful architecture and scenery.

  2. Boat Tour: You can explore the nearby islands and landmarks by taking a boat tour around Spetses Island.

  3. Scuba diving: The crystal clear waters surrounding Spetses Island offer the perfect opportunity for scuba diving to discover the marine world.

Food and Drink Scene of Spetses

Spetses Island has a variety of culinary treats to offer; here are some must-try dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Spetses fish soup: The soup is made from freshly caught fish and is a popular delicacy on the island.

  2. Revithia: A flavorsome stew made from chickpeas, onions, tomatoes, and herbs.

  3. Pitaroudia: Small felafel-like deep-fried balls made from chickpeas, herbs, and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Mastiha: A sweet sipper that is made from resin from the Pistacia Lentiscus plant.

  2. Retsina: The local wine that is flavored with resin from pine trees.

  3. Tsipouro: A strong alcoholic beverage that is typically served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Spetses: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spetses Island has many accommodations that cater to different tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay on Spetses:

  1. Poseidonion Grand Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on the island’s old port. It offers stunning views of the ocean and world-class amenities.

  2. Kastro Hotel: A modern hotel located in the center of the old town. It offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  3. Villa Nika: A traditional guesthouse located in the heart of the island’s old town. It offers charming rooms and personalized service.

Local Culture and Customs of Spetses

Spetses Island has a unique culture and customs that reflect its rich history and traditions. One of the most significant cultural events is the reenactment of the naval battle of Spetses that takes place every September. The islanders take to the sea clothed in traditional costumes and fire cannons and muskets to commemorate the battle that took place between the Greeks and Turks in 1822.

Best Time to Visit Spetses

The best time to visit Spetses Island is from late spring to early autumn when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August are the busiest months, and you should expect the island to be crowded. September is the best time to visit if you want to experience the island’s cultural events, such as the reenactment of the naval battle of Spetses.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spetses

Before traveling to Spetses, it is good to know a few do’s and don’ts to ensure a pleasant stay.

Do:

  1. Respect the island’s customs and traditions.

  2. Use sunscreen and a hat when going to the beach or hiking.

  3. Try the island’s famous fish soup and other local delicacies.

Don’t:

  1. Litter the island, keep it clean.

  2. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

  3. Take souvenirs such as pebbles, shells, or sea urchins from the beach.

Safety and Health

Spetses Island is a safe destination, but here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting the island.

Safety in Spetses

  1. Be careful when exploring the island’s hiking and biking trails as they can be slippery and uneven.

  2. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas such as the old town or the port.

  3. Stay clear of the sea urchins in the shallow waters when swimming.

Health in Spetses

  1. Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and a first-aid kit when visiting the island.

  2. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.

  3. Consult a doctor before traveling if you have any pre-existing health conditions.

Getting to Spetses: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Spetses island is easy, and there are several transportation options to choose from.

Airports Near Spetses

The closest airport to Spetses is Athens International Airport, which is located in Athens, around 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Spetses

There is no direct bus from Athens to Spetses. However, you can take a bus from Athens to the port city of Kosta and then take a water taxi to Spetses.

Other Options

You can also get to Spetses by car or taxi by driving to the port city of Kosta, where you will find the water taxi that will take you to the island.

How to Get Around in Spetses

Spetses Island is a small island, which means that you can explore most of the island by walking. Another option is to rent a bike or scooter to get around and explore the island at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Spetses: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
