Introduction to Hydra

Hydra is a small and chic Greek island nestled in the Saronic Gulf, known for its crystal-clear waters, gorgeous beaches, and centuries-old architecture. Its tranquil harbor, picturesque mountain backdrop, and vibrant cultural scene make it an ultimate summer destination.

What sets Hydra apart from other islands is its car-free environment, offering visitors an opportunity to explore on foot, horseback, or by boat. Few cars are allowed on the island, making it a haven for walkers, hikers, and bikers. Discover everything you need to know about the best places to visit and things to do in Hydra, along with some insider tips for a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Hydra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hydra offers a rich tapestry of history, culture, and arts, reflecting a unique blend of Ottoman, Venetian, and Greek influences.

One of the must-see attractions is the Historical Archives Museum, located in a renovated former mansion. It showcases historical documents from the Greek Revolution, paintings, jewelry, and traditional costumes.

The Museum of Hydra is another must-visit place, exhibiting an impressive collection of nautical artifacts, paintings, and old maps. It also provides an insight into the island’s maritime heritage.

Don’t forget to visit the Ecclesiastical Museum, located at the Monastery of the Assumption. The museum boasts an impressive collection of Byzantine icons, manuscripts, and 16th-century wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Hydra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hydra features pristine beaches, hiking trails, and breathtaking views of the Saronic Gulf. Make sure to check out the following outdoor activities and scenic spots:

  • Vlychos beach: a sun-kissed beach with crystal-clear waters, located on the eastern side of Hydra

  • Mandraki beach: a secluded bay, accessible only by boat or foot, boasting stunning views of the island

  • Mount Eros: a mountain that offers panoramic views of the island and surrounding sea, ideal for hiking enthusiasts

  • Hydra Horses: a horse riding center located in Kamini, offering horseback riding tours of the island’s landscapes

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hydra

Hydra Island offers a rich network of hiking and biking trails, starting from the port and leading to various parts of the island. A hike to Mount Eros is one of the most popular routes, offering breathtaking views of the island’s unique landscapes.

Another popular trail is the Hydra Trail, which covers the entire island, taking about six hours to complete. The trail passes through picturesque villages, stunning scenery, and hidden beaches. It’s best to wear comfortable shoes and carry plenty of water when undertaking these hikes.

Best Things to Do in Hydra: Activities and Tours

Hydra Island offers a diverse range of activities and tours, catering to visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the top activities to add to your bucket list:

  • Hydra SUP: Stand-up paddleboarding tours of the island’s coastlines and crystal-clear waters, perfect for adventurous travelers

  • Hydraiki Diving Center: Discover the underwater world of Hydra with the island’s premier diving center, offering diving tours and lessons for both beginners and seasoned divers alike

  • Hydra Hiking: Guided hiking tours that explore the island’s beautiful nature and historic sites

  • Hydra Horseback Riding: Gallop through the island’s hills and beaches with this unique horseback riding experience

Food and Drink Scene of Hydra

Must-Try Dishes

Hydra’s cuisine is emblematic of Greek cuisine, with a wide range of fresh ingredients and international influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes on the island:

  • Seafood: fresh fish, octopus, and squid are staples on the island

  • Revithokeftedes: a common appetizer made from chickpeas

  • Tomatokeftedes: tomato fritters, perfect as a snack or appetizer

  • Ladolemono: a typical local dressing made with lemon and olive oil

Local Beverages

Hydra offers a wide range of refreshments to quench your thirst, including local wines and beers. Here are some of the must-try local beverages:

  • Ouzo: an anise-flavored Greek spirit, perfect as an after-dinner drink

  • Tsipouro: a grape brandy, traditionally served with meze

  • Hydra Ale: a locally brewed beer made with Hydra spring water and hops

Where to Stay in Hydra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hydra has a wide range of accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to upscale hotels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Hydra Hotel: a top-rated hotel located in the heart of the port, offering luxurious rooms and stunning views

  • Four Seasons Hydra: a family-friendly hotel located on a hilltop, offering panoramic views of the island and the sea

  • Pension Erofili: a cozy and affordable guesthouse, located in the heart of Hydra town

What to Buy in Hydra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hydra is famous for a wide range of local products, including olive oil, honey, and traditional sweets. Here are some of the top products to buy on the island:

  • Hydra Sponge: a natural sponge harvested from the surrounding sea, used for exfoliation and cleaning

  • Hydra Pistachios: locally grown pistachios, delicious as a snack or gift

  • Local Wines: Greece is famous for its wines, and Hydra’s vineyards offer exceptional wines with unique flavors

  • Traditional Clothing: Hydra is home to many clothing and accessory stores that sell traditional Greek fashions

Local Culture and Customs of Hydra

Hydra has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique blend of Ottoman, Venetian, and Greek cultures. The island’s locals pride themselves on their hospitality, traditions, and customs. Respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Hydra

The best time to visit Hydra is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most of the island’s attractions and outdoor activities are open. July and August are peak tourist season, and the island’s population can double in size.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hydra

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions

  • Explore the island’s cultural and historical attractions

  • Swim in the crystal-clear waters of the Saronic Gulf

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island’s pristine beaches and nature trails

  • Swim in unauthorized areas, as it could be dangerous

  • Wear high heels or stiletto shoes, as the island’s cobbled streets can be difficult to navigate

Safety and Health

Safety in Hydra

Hydra is generally safe for travelers, with low crime rates. However, it’s always best to take basic precautions, such as locking your valuables in a hotel safe when traveling with them, especially when swimming or sunbathing.

Health in Hydra

Hydra has a modern medical center, serving the island’s visitors and residents alike in case of emergencies. Tap water is safe to drink, and all restaurants and food stalls meet the health and safety standards.

Getting to Hydra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hydra

The closest airports to Hydra are Athens International Airport and Eleftherios Venizelos Airport. From Athens, visitors can take a bus, taxi, or water taxi to reach the island.

Bus Stations Near Hydra

Buses run regularly between the Athens bus station and the port city of Piraeus, where visitors can catch a ferry to Hydra.

Other Options

There are several water taxi services running from the Athenian Riviera and the port city of Piraeus to Hydra.

How to Get Around in Hydra

One of the best ways to explore the island is by foot, as most of the island is pedestrianized and vehicle-free. Taxis and water taxis are also available, and there are several bike rental options on the island.

Where to Go Next from Hydra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore after their Hydra trip, including:

  • Poros Island: A beautiful, serene island with stunning landscapes, beaches, and sights

  • Spetses Island: Known as the “island of aromas,” with a rich history, beaches, and hiking trails

  • Nafplio: A charming town, built on the slopes of a hill, with stunning architecture and scenic vistas

Conclusion

Hydra offers visitors a unique chance to explore a car-free, chic, and culturally rich Greek island. From beaches and mountain hikes to gastronomical delights and outdoor activities, Hydra has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, prepare to plan your ultimate Hydra trip.
