Introduction to Kissamos

Kissamos is a small town on the west coast of Crete that is full of history and natural beauty. Taking a step back from the overtouristed areas of Crete, Kissamos is a great place to explore with a rental car or bike. It is situated between the stunning beaches of Falasarna and Elafonisi. With its breathtaking natural attractions, historical sites, vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, it is no surprise that this small town has a lot to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kissamos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Kissamos
  • Ancient Falasarna and Sirikari
  • Polirrinia
  • Venetian Gianus Fortification

Natural Attractions of Kissamos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Balos Lagoon
  • Elafonisi Beach
  • Falasarna Beach
  • Kedrodasos Beach
  • Topolia Gorge
  • Milia Gorge
  • Gramvousa Island

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kissamos

Kissamos is a hiker’s paradise, with some of the most unique and breathtaking hikes in Crete. The best hiking and biking trails include:

  • E4 European Trail
  • Gramvousa Peninsula Trail
  • Polyrrinia Trail
  • Deliana-Kampos-Gonia Trail

Best Things to Do in Kissamos: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Balos Lagoon by boat or by hiking
  • Sunset at Falasarna beach
  • Learn about the Cretan flora at Botanical Park and Gardens of Crete
  • Try wine tasting at Dourakis Winery
  • Take a Donkey ride through the traditional village of Milia

Week-Long Itinerary for Kissamos

Day 1 Arrive at Kissamos and settle in your accommodation

Food and Drink Scene of Kissamos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalitsounia (local cheese and herb-filled pastries)
  • Dakos (traditional Cretan salad with dried bread, tomatoes, cheese, and herbs)
  • Lamb with stamnagathi (wild greens)
  • Grilled octopus
  • Tzatziki (yogurt and cucumber dip)

Local Beverages

  • Raki (traditional Greek distilled spirit)
  • Tsikoudia (another type of Greek distilled spirit)
  • Wine (local wines from Dourakis Winery and Manousakis Winery)

Local Culture and Customs of Kissamos

Kissamos is a vibrant town with a rich culture. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs, such as cover your shoulders and knees while visiting churches
  • Respect the local wildlife and fauna
  • Tipping is not expected but always appreciated if the service was great

Best Time to Visit Kissamos

The best time to visit Kissamos is during the summer months, from June to September when the weather is hot and sunny, making it perfect for beach lovers. Keep in mind that July and August are the peak seasons and may get crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kissamos

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Be respectful to the local culture and customs
  • Visit the stunning natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or natural areas
  • Ignore the local culture and traditions
  • Swim in prohibited areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Kissamos

Kissamos is a safe town, and the local authorities ensure the safety of tourists. It is recommended to take the same safety measures you would in any other city, such as being aware of your surroundings and not leaving your valuables unattended.

Health in Kissamos

The healthcare system in Kissamos is excellent, and there are many hospitals and clinics throughout the town for medical emergencies. It is recommended to get vaccinated before visiting Greece to prevent any illnesses.

Getting to Kissamos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kissamos

The closest airport to Kissamos is in Chania, which is about 34 km away. Another option is the Heraklion airport, which is about 170 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kissamos

There are many bus routes connecting Kissamos with the nearby cities. The main bus stop is located in the town center, making it easy to commute to the nearby cities like Chania, Rethymnon, and Heraklion.

Other Options

Renting a car or a bike is one of the best ways to explore the town, with many rental companies available in Kissamos.

How to Get Around in Kissamos

The best way to get around Kissamos is on foot, especially when exploring the town center. Renting a car or scooter is another great option to explore the nearby beaches and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Kissamos is a hidden gem in Crete, offering a blend of rich culture, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant food scene. Whether you are an adventure seeker or a beach lover, Kissamos has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip with this comprehensive tour guide and get ready for an unforgettable journey!
