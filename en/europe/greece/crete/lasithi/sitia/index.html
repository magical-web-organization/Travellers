Sitia is a hidden gem located in the eastern part of Crete, Greece. This charming town offers a perfect blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure that makes it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic Greek experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve put together everything you need to know before planning a trip to Sitia.

Introduction to Sitia

Sitia is a small town located in the Lasithi region, at the eastern edge of Crete. This charming town has a population of just over 9,000 and is known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Sitia is located approximately 137 km east of Heraklion, which is Crete’s largest city. This town is also known for being the birthplace of one of Greece’s most famous poets, Vitsentzos Kornaros.

Food and Drink Scene of Sitia

Sitia has some incredible local cuisine that shouldn’t be missed. Here are some dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dakos: A popular Cretan salad
  • Boureki: A local vegetable pie
  • Kalitsounia: Sweet cheese pastries
  • Sfakianes Pites: Sweet or savory pies

Local Beverages

  • Tsikoudia: A traditional Cretan spirit
  • Raki: A distilled, grape-based brandy
  • Local wines: Try the Liatiko, Vilana, or Thrapsathiri varietals.

Local Culture and Customs of Sitia

Sitia has a rich cultural and historical heritage that’s worth exploring. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and monasteries.
  • Follow table etiquette when eating at local restaurants.
  • Be polite and respectful when interacting with locals.
  • Try to learn a few basic Greek phrases to show your appreciation for the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Sitia

The best time to visit Sitia is during the spring and autumn when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. The summer months (June-August) can get quite busy, and the temperatures can soar, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sitia

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and monasteries
  • Try local dishes and drinks
  • Be polite and respectful to the locals

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural sites
  • Leave without trying the local cuisine
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Make unnecessary noise late at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Sitia

Sitia is a safe destination where crime rates are low. However, it’s always recommended to take general safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Sitia

Crete has excellent medical facilities, and Sitia has a small hospital and a few medical clinics in the vicinity. Make sure to carry your medical insurance card with you when traveling, and take adequate precautions to avoid dehydration and heatstroke during the summer months.

The best way to get around Sitia is by renting a car or a bike. You can also use taxis, though they can be expensive. Buses operate throughout the region, but they may not be the most convenient option for travel.
