Introduction to Lasithi Plateau

Situated in the heart of Crete, Lasithi Plateau is a stunningly beautiful natural paradise known for its rugged yet serene landscapes, ancient history, vibrant culture, and unparalleled hospitality. At an altitude of 815 meters, the plateau is nestled between towering mountains, fertile valleys, and fragrant groves of olive and almond trees, making it a must-see destination for travelers who seek adventure, tranquility, and authentic experiences that enrich the mind, body, and soul.

List of Places to Visit in Lasithi Plateau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lasithi Plateau boasts a wealth of cultural and historical attractions that showcase its rich heritage dating back to the Minoan era and beyond. Some of the must-visit places in the plateau include:

  • Folklore Museum of Kritsa: This charming museum is a treasure trove of artifacts, costumes, tools, and other items that showcase the traditional way of life of the people of Crete.
  • Dikteon Cave: According to Greek mythology, this cave in the mountains was the birthplace of Zeus. The cave is open to visitors and features stunning stalactites, stalagmites, and underground lakes.
  • Moni Kera: This 13th-century Byzantine church is famous for its frescoes that depict scenes from the New Testament. It is located in the village of Kritsa and offers breathtaking views of the valley.
  • Lasithi Windmills: These iconic windmills, dating back to the 19th century, are located near the village of Psychro and offer a glimpse into the agricultural history of the plateau.

Natural Attractions of Lasithi Plateau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lasithi Plateau is an outdoor enthusiast’s dream come true, with a variety of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails that traverse its rugged terrain, as well as numerous natural wonders that showcase its unique beauty. Some of the must-see attractions in the plateau include:

  • Karfi: This ancient Minoan settlement, located at the highest point of the plateau, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and the sea. It is accessible via a hiking trail that starts from the village of Avrakontes.
  • Waterfall of Milona: This beautiful waterfall is located in a hidden gorge near the village of Neapoli. The hike to the waterfall takes about an hour and passes through lush vegetation and rocky formations.
  • Cave of Psychro: This cave, located near the village of Psychro, is believed to have been a place of religious worship in ancient times. It features stunning stalactites, stalagmites, and underground lakes and can be reached via a short hike from the village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lasithi Plateau

Lasithi Plateau is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a variety of trails that cater to all levels of skill and fitness. Some of the best routes in the plateau include:

  • E4 European Long-Distance Path: This trail passes through the plateau and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is suitable for experienced hikers and takes about 5 days to complete.
  • Route of the Windmills: This 16-km biking trail starts from the village of Havgas and passes through the iconic Lasithi windmills, offering panoramic views of the plateau.
  • Trail of the Gorge of Saint Nicholas: This hike starts from the village of Plati and passes through a scenic gorge, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Lasithi Plateau: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Lasithi Plateau that cater to all tastes and interests, such as:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: The plateau is famous for its delicious wines made from local varieties of grapes, such as the white Vilana and red Kotsifali. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the vineyards and wineries and sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Cretan dishes using fresh, locally sourced ingredients and spices. Classes are offered by various local chefs and restaurants.
  • Donkey Riding Tour: Explore the plateau in a unique way by riding a donkey and following scenic trails that pass through olive groves and small villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lasithi Plateau

Day 1: Arrive at Lasithi Plateau, check into hotel or guesthouse, explore the nearby villages of Kritsa and Tzermiado Day 2: Hike to Dikteon Cave and Moni Kera, visit Lasithi Windmills Day 3: Bike the Route of the Windmills, stop by the village of Psychro, and explore the Cave of Psychro Day 4: Visit the Folklore Museum of Kritsa and the Waterfall of Milona Day 5: Wine Tasting Tour and Cooking Class Day 6: Donkey Riding Tour Day 7: Depart from Lasithi Plateau and visit nearby destinations, such as the town of Agios Nikolaos or the island of Spinalonga.

Food and Drink Scene of Lasithi Plateau

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine of Lasithi Plateau is a reflection of its cultural and historical heritage, featuring simple yet flavorful dishes that use fresh, locally sourced ingredients and spices. Some of the must-try dishes in the plateau include:

  • Dakos: A traditional Cretan dish made with barley rusks, fresh tomatoes, olive oil, feta cheese, and oregano.
  • Sfakiani Pita: A sweet or savory pie made with traditional Cretan cheese and coated in honey or sugar.
  • Apaki: Smoked pork that is marinated in vinegar and spices and served as a snack or appetizer.

Local Beverages

Lasithi Plateau is home to some of the best wines and spirits in Greece, such as:

  • Tsikoudia: A strong alcoholic drink made from grape marc or pomace, similar to Italian grappa.
  • Vilana and Kotsifali Wines: White and red wines made from native grape varieties that thrive in the plateau’s high altitude and distinct microclimate.

Where to Stay in Lasithi Plateau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lasithi Plateau offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences, from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in the plateau include:

  • BioAgriturismo Dourakis: A family-run eco-agriturismo that offers comfortable rooms and apartments, organic meals, and stunning views of the plateau.
  • Sarikampos Traditional Village: A charming guesthouse that features traditional architecture, cozy rooms, and a lush garden.
  • Sivas Hilltop Retreat: A luxury villa that offers panoramic views of the plateau, an infinity pool, and upscale amenities.

What to Buy in Lasithi Plateau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lasithi Plateau is famous for its traditional handicrafts, such as:

  • Pottery: Handmade ceramics and pottery that showcase the unique styles and techniques of the local artisans.
  • Textiles: Intricately woven rugs, blankets, and other textiles that feature traditional motifs and patterns.
  • Cold-Pressed Olive Oil: A staple of the Cretan diet, this oil is produced by local farmers using traditional methods and is renowned for its rich flavor and health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Lasithi Plateau

The people of Lasithi Plateau are known for their warm hospitality, strong sense of community, and deep respect for their cultural heritage and natural surroundings. Visitors to the plateau are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Dressing modestly when visiting churches and monasteries.
  • Saying “Yassou” (hello) and “Efharisto” (thank you) to locals.
  • Respectfully observing the local festivals and celebrations, such as the Feast of Saint George in Kritsa.

Best Time to Visit Lasithi Plateau

The best time to visit Lasithi Plateau is from April to October, when the weather is mild, flora and fauna are in full bloom, and outdoor activities are abundant. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain and cooler temperatures during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lasithi Plateau

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the plateau through hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife or the livestock of the local farmers.
  • Touch or remove artifacts or objects from archaeological sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lasithi Plateau

Lasithi Plateau is a safe destination for travelers who take common-sense precautions, such as:

  • Avoiding unmarked hiking trails or remote areas without a guide.
  • Staying aware of the weather conditions and avoiding outdoor activities during extreme weather.
  • Keeping valuables secure and being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Lasithi Plateau

Lasithi Plateau has a well-developed healthcare system, with several clinics and pharmacies located in the nearby towns and villages. Visitors are advised to take the following health precautions:

  • Drink bottled or purified water to avoid stomach problems.
  • Use sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Check for ticks and wear insect repellent to avoid tick-borne diseases.

Getting to Lasithi Plateau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lasithi Plateau can be accessed by various modes of transportation, such as:

Airports Near Lasithi Plateau

The nearest international airport is Heraklion Airport, located about 65km southeast of the plateau. Several airlines offer direct flights to Heraklion from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Lasithi Plateau

The main bus terminal in Heraklion offers daily connections to the nearby towns and villages, such as Tzermiado and Kritsa. Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car at the airport or bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive at the plateau via private transfer or guided tour from Heraklion or other nearby destinations, such as Agios Nikolaos or Elounda.

How to Get Around in Lasithi Plateau

The best way to get around Lasithi Plateau is by car or bike, as the plateau is vast and the public transportation system is limited. Visitors can rent a car or bike at the airport or bus station, or hire a private driver or tour guide.

Where to Go Next from Lasithi Plateau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lasithi Plateau is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations in Crete, such as:

  • Agios Nikolaos: A picturesque seaside town on the northeastern coast of Crete, known for its charming harbor, vibrant nightlife, and scenic beaches.
  • Spinalonga Island: A small island off the coast of Crete that was a leper colony in the 20th century and is now a popular tourist attraction for its historic ruins and stunning views.
  • Chrissi Island: A small uninhabited island south of Crete that is famous for its crystal-clear waters and pristine beaches.

Conclusion

Lasithi Plateau is a destination that offers a unique blend of nature, culture, history, and adventure that is sure to delight and inspire travelers from all walks of life. Whether you’re seeking a quiet retreat from the hustle and bustle of modern life or an action-packed vacation that challenges your physical and mental limits, Lasithi Plateau has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of this hidden gem in the heart of Crete.
