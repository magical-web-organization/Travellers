Introduction to Lasithi

Located in the eastern part of the island of Crete, Lasithi Prefecture is an ideal destination for those looking to experience both natural and cultural wonders. Here you will find breathtaking landscapes, historical monuments, and a rich culinary scene, making Lasithi a must-visit destination for any traveler to Greece.

List of Places to Visit in Lasithi

There are plenty of places to visit in Lasithi, from culturally important attractions to breathtaking natural landscapes. Here’s a list of must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Agios Nikolaos
  • Monastery of Toplou
  • Palace of Knossos
  • Archaeological Site of Gournia

Natural Attractions of Lasithi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vai Beach
  • Richtis Gorge
  • Lasithi Plateau
  • Samaria Gorge

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lasithi

Lasithi offers numerous hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular routes are:

  • Mount Dikti
  • Kavousi Gorge
  • Kritsa Gorge
  • Xerokampos

Best Things to Do in Lasithi: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling
  • Boat tours to Spinalonga Island
  • Jeep tours through the rugged Cretan mountains
  • Olive oil and wine tastings

Week-Long Itinerary for Lasithi

Day 1: Arrive and check into your accommodations, relax at the beach and explore the local scene of Agios Nikolaos.

Day 2: Visit the archaeological sites of Gournia and the Palace of Knossos.

Day 3: Take a boat tour to Spinalonga Island, followed by relaxation on the beach in Elounda.

Day 4: Explore the scenic trails and breathtaking views of Richtis Gorge.

Day 5: Visit the Monastery of Toplou and take in the centuries-old architecture and rich cultural history of the region.

Day 6: Take a drive to Lasithi Plateau and spend some time hiking or horseback riding.

Day 7: Depart Lasithi and head to Chania, another historic city on Crete.

Food and Drink Scene of Lasithi

Lasithi boasts a rich culinary scene featuring traditional dishes and local products. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dakos: a type of rusk or paximadi topped with chopped tomatoes, feta cheese, and olive oil.
  • Kalitsounia: small cheese pies typically filled with local cheese or herbs.
  • Sfakia pie: a delicious traditional dish filled with cheese, honey, and almonds.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: a traditional distilled alcoholic beverage made from grapes.
  • Cretan wine: The wine of Crete is a favorite of many locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Lasithi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lasithi offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some recommendations:

  • Luxury: Daios Cove Luxury Resort and Villas
  • Mid-range: Mochlos Mare Apartment Hotel
  • Budget: Dimitra Apartments

What to Buy in Lasithi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Lasithi will find plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, including traditional textiles, handmade ceramics, and olive oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Lasithi

Greek culture is rich and diverse, and Lasithi has its own unique customs that travelers should be aware of. Some of these include:

  • Greeks tend to be more relaxed when it comes to timekeeping, so don’t be surprised if things don’t run precisely on schedule.
  • Tipping is not always expected in Greece
  • Respect religious customs and traditions, especially during holidays and in religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Lasithi

The best time to visit Lasithi is during the spring and fall months when the weather is cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lasithi

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect the cultural traditions and customs of the region
  • Plan ahead and book tours and activities in advance

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t disrespect religious sites or traditions
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated in the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lasithi

Overall, Lasithi is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions such as locking their doors at night, not flaunting expensive items, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lasithi

No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Lasithi, and health care facilities are adequate but may be limited in some areas. Visitors should carry sufficient health insurance and take any necessary precautions to protect against heat stroke and injury while engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Lasithi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lasithi

The nearest airport to Lasithi is Heraklion International Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive from the region.

Bus Stations Near Lasithi

There are many bus stations throughout the region, making public transportation a convenient option for getting around Lasithi.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car in Heraklion and explore the region at their own pace. Ferries are also available to take travelers to nearby islands and coastal towns.

How to Get Around in Lasithi

The most convenient way to get around Lasithi is by rental car, although public transportation options such as buses and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lasithi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations to Lasithi include:

  • Chania: a historic seaside town in the western part of Crete.
  • Santorini: a nearby island known for its picturesque views, stunning architecture, and volcanic landscapes.

Conclusion

Lasithi is a true gem of Crete, offering a rich blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With plenty to see and do, Lasithi is an unforgettable destination that will leave a lasting impression on any traveler fortunate enough to visit.
