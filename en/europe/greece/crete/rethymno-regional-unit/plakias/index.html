Introduction to Plakias

Located in Rethymno, Crete, Plakias is a charming seaside village with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking natural landscapes. The town is surrounded by towering cliffs, olive groves, and rocky hills, making it a perfect holiday destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Plakias has a small but vibrant town center, where visitors can find cafes, taverns, and shops selling goods and handicrafts made by local artisans. The town boasts a rich history dating back to the Minoan era, and there are traces of this history scattered throughout the area.

List of Places to Visit in Plakias

Plakias has something for everyone, from culturally significant museums to adrenaline-pumping outdoor activities. Here are some of the must-visit places in Plakias.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Panagia Preveli Monastery: Built-in the 16th century, this historic monastery is an important cultural site with stunning Byzantine architecture and a rich collection of books and manuscripts.
  • The Fortezza: The Fortezza is a massive fortress perched on a small hill, built-in 1573 to protect Rethymno from invaders. Today it’s open to visitors who want to explore the ruins and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Archaeological Museum of Rethymno: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the history and culture of Rethymno.

Natural Attractions of Plakias: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plakias Beach: This pristine beach boasts crystal-clear waters, soft sand, and a stunning backdrop of cliffs and hills. It’s one of the most beautiful beaches in Crete and a top destination for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.
  • Gorge of Kotsifos: Kotsifos Gorge is an awe-inspiring natural wonder that attracts hikers and nature lovers. The gorge is surrounded by towering cliffs, beautiful flora, and fauna.
  • Skinaria Beach: This small secluded beach is a hidden gem with crystal-clear waters, rocky shoreline, and stunning natural scenery. It’s perfect for a quiet day of swimming, sunbathing, or snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plakias

Plakias is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Kotsifos Gorge Trail: This trail leads hikers through the impressive Kotsifos Gorge, with scenic natural views along the way.
  • Preveli Beach Trail: This trek takes hikers on a journey through a vast expanse of olive groves with stunning views of Preveli Beach.
  • Plakias to Damnoni Trail: This biking trail offers breathtaking scenery, with rugged hills, rugged coastline, and panoramic views of the Libyan Sea.

Best Things to Do in Plakias: Activities and Tours

Plakias offers a range of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best:

  • Boat Tour of Preveli Beach: A boat tour of Preveli Beach is a must-do activity, and visitors can enjoy the stunning scenery of crystal-clear waters and the towering cliffs of the nearby Palm Tree Forest.
  • Scuba Diving: The crystal-clear waters of Plakias provide an excellent opportunity for scuba diving enthusiasts to explore the underwater seascapes of Crete.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting tour of local vineyards and sample some of the finest wines in Crete.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plakias

A week-long itinerary for Plakias could include:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Plakias and enjoy some of the local cafes, shops, and restaurants.
  • Day 2: Visit the Panagia Preveli Monastery, and then go on a boat tour of Preveli Beach.
  • Day 3: Go hiking through Kotsifos Gorge, and then visit the Fortezza fortress in Rethymno.
  • Day 4: Take a scuba diving excursion and explore the underwater seascapes of Crete.
  • Day 5: Rent a bike and go on the Plakias to Damnoni Trail.
  • Day 6: Visit the Archaeological Museum of Rethymno and then take a wine tasting tour.
  • Day 7: Relax on Skinaria beach and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Plakias

Plakias has a vibrant food and drink scene, with local dishes, wines, and beverages that visitors must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dakos: A traditional dish made with barley rusk, grated tomato and feta cheese.
  • Stifado: A hearty meat stew made with beef, onions, and spices.
  • Fresh Seafood: Plakias is known for its fresh seafood. Visitors must try grilled octopus, squid, and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A traditional Cretan alcoholic drink, often served as a digestif.
  • Local Wines: Visitors must try some of the finest wines in Crete, including Malvasia, Mandilari, and Vilana.

Where to Stay in Plakias: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Plakias has several accommodation options for visitors, depending on their budget and preferences. The following are some of the best options:

  • Lamon Hotel: A luxurious hotel located by Plakias Beach with an outdoor pool and stunning views of the sea.
  • Myrtis Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to the town center.
  • Foinikas Apartments: A family-friendly accommodation option with fully equipped apartments and stunning sea views.

What to Buy in Plakias: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy some of the finest local products and souvenirs in Plakias, including:

  • Olive Oil – Cretan olive oil is famous for its rich flavor and health benefits.
  • Handmade Jewelry – Plakias has several shops that sell unique, artisanal jewelry made from silver, gold, and semi-precious stones.
  • Locally made ceramics – Visitors can buy unique ceramics and pottery at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Plakias

Plakias is known for its warm hospitality, and visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by participating in various cultural events and festivals throughout the year. The town celebrates several religious festivals, including the Feast of Panagia in August and the Feast of St. John in June.

Best Time to Visit Plakias

The best time to visit Plakias is in the summer months, from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming and water sports. However, visitors can also enjoy the stunning natural scenery in the spring and fall months when the temperatures are milder.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plakias

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat while exploring the outdoors.
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Dispose of waste irresponsibly. Plakias has a beautiful natural landscape, and visitors must take care to keep it clean.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Be excessively loud or disruptive, especially during quiet hours.

Safety and Health

Plakias is generally a safe destination, and visitors need not worry too much about safety concerns. However, it’s essential to take basic safety precautions like locking doors and windows, especially at night.

Safety in Plakias

  • Avoid walking alone in secluded areas at night.
  • Take basic precautions when swimming or engaging in water sports.
  • Be cautious when renting vehicles or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Plakias

  • Visitors must carry their health insurance documents.
  • Make sure to stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.
  • Wear appropriate footwear and clothing when hiking or biking.

Getting to Plakias: Transportation Options and Logistics

Plakias is accessible by several modes of transportation, making it easy for visitors to get there.

Airports Near Plakias

  • Chania International Airport is 95 km away from Plakias and about a two-hour drive.
  • Heraklion International Airport is 115 km away and is about a two-and-a-half-hour drive.

Bus Stations Near Plakias

  • Rethymno Bus Station is 35 km away from Plakias.

Other Options

  • Visitors can take a taxi or hire a private car to reach Plakias.

How to Get Around in Plakias

Plakias is a small town, and visitors can explore most of the area on foot. However, visitors can rent bikes or scooters to explore remote areas that are not accessible on foot.

Where to Go Next from Plakias: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Plakias is surrounded by several beautiful destinations and excursions worth exploring, including:

  • Rethymno: A vibrant seaside town with stunning architecture, historic monuments, and an exciting nightlife.
  • Preveli Beach: A pristine beach located close to Plakias, perfect for a day trip.
  • Samaria Gorge: One of the most breathtaking natural wonders in Crete, and a popular trekking destination.

Conclusion

Plakias is a charming town with beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and stunning natural landscapes. Whether it’s swimming, hiking, or indulging in some local cuisine, visitors are spoilt for choice with things to see and do in this one-of-a-kind destination in Crete, Greece.
