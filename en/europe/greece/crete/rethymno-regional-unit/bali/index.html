Introduction to Bali

Located in the Rethymno regional unit of Crete, Bali is a charming coastal town that boasts blue waters, soft sand beaches, and picturesque landscapes. It is an ideal vacation destination for those who want to soak up the sun and enjoy the natural beauty of Greece.

This travel guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Bali.

Local Culture and Customs of Bali

Bali is a town that is proud of its culture and traditions. Visitors are welcomed with gracious hospitality and are encouraged to respect local customs, such as dress codes when entering religious sites, and tipping etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Bali

The best time to visit Bali is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and dry. September and October are also good months to visit when the temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bali

Do:

  1. Respect local traditions and customs
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  3. Try the local food and beverages

Don’t:

  1. Cause disturbance or harm to animals and wildlife
  2. Use flash photography in museums and churches
  3. Litter in public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Bali

Bali is generally a safe destination for travellers. However, visitors should take standard precautions against pickpockets and petty theft.

Health in Bali

There are no health risks specific to Bali, Greece. It is always advisable to have travel insurance and stay hydrated during the hot summer months.

How to Get Around in Bali

The best way to get around in Bali is on foot or by bicycle. There are also taxis and car rentals available for those who prefer more comfortable transportation.
