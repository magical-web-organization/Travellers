Introduction to Rethymno (regional unit)

The Rethymno regional unit is situated in the heart of Crete, Greece. It is framed by the Aegean Sea to the north and the Libyan Sea to the south. This picturesque region is known for its sandy beaches, rugged mountains, ancient architecture, and delectable cuisine.

Rethymno is a perfect blend of history, nature, and culture. It has something to offer for every type of traveler, from adventure seekers to history buffs, beach lovers to foodies. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Rethymno.

List of Places to Visit in Rethymno (regional unit)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rethymno is home to numerous museums and historic landmarks that offer visitors an insight into the region’s rich history and culture. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • The Fortezza of Rethymno is a massive Venetian fortress built in the 16th century and offers breathtaking views of the city and sea.

  • The Archaeological Museum of Rethymno showcases a treasure trove of relics from ancient settlements that gives a glimpse into the island’s history.

  • The Monastery of Arkadi is one of the most significant monasteries in Crete and played a vital role in the struggle for independence in the 19th century.

  • The Town Hall is a neo-classical building located in the heart of the old town, and its architecture is a testament to the region’s cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Rethymno (regional unit): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rethymno is blessed with a breathtaking natural landscape that offers visitors numerous outdoor activities and scenic views. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • The Samaria Gorge is a magnificent national park that offers visitors an opportunity to experience the stunning natural beauty of the region.

  • The Preveli Beach is located at the mouth of a river and surrounded by palm trees and dramatic cliffs, offering visitors a picture-perfect view.

  • The Melidoni Cave is home to impressive stalactites and stalagmites that have formed over millions of years, providing visitors with an unforgettable underground adventure.

  • The Arkadi Gorge is a stunning walking trail that offers trekkers an opportunity to experience the untouched wilderness and natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rethymno (regional unit)

Rethymno is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts who want to experience the region’s natural beauty and landscapes. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Rethymno:

  • The E4 European Walking Path runs through the Rethymno region and provides an opportunity for visitors to explore the best scenic trails.

  • The Perivolia tou Orinou Trail is a great route that takes visitors through the rolling hills and breathtaking landscapes surrounding the Orinos Peak.

  • The Rouvas Forest Trail is located in the beautiful Rouvas Gorge, and visitors can experience the region’s flora and fauna while taking a leisurely hike or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Rethymno (regional unit): Activities and Tours

Rethymno offers visitors an array of activities and tours that can enhance your experience of the region. Here are some of the best things to do in Rethymno:

  • Visit the local wineries and indulge in some wine tasting, where you will have the opportunity to sample some of the local wines and appreciate the scenic vineyards.

  • Take a boat tour of the south coast of Crete, where you can experience the beauty of the Libyan Sea, see the deserted beaches, and encounter some of the marine life.

  • Visit the local markets and bazaars, where you can find souvenirs, local crafts, and fresh produce.

  • Indulge in water sports like snorkeling, windsurfing, and scuba diving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rethymno (regional unit)

Here is a sample week-long itinerary of Rethymno:

Day 1: Visit the Fortezza of Rethymno and experience the Venetian architecture, followed by a walk around the old town.

Day 2: Take a trip to the Preveli Beach and spend the day soaking in the sun and surf.

Day 3: Hike the Arkadi Gorge and experience the natural beauty of Rethymno.

Day 4: Visit the Monastery of Arkadi, learn about the region’s history, and take a walk around the beautiful gardens.

Day 5: Take a boat tour of the south coast or indulge in some water sports.

Day 6: Visit the local wineries and enjoy some wine tasting, followed by a visit to the local bazaars.

Day 7: Explore the Melidoni Cave and see the impressive stalactites and stalagmites that have formed over millions of years.

Food and Drink Scene of Rethymno (regional unit)

The food and drink scene in Rethymno is a fusion of Mediterranean, Greek, and Cretan flavors. Visitors can try traditional dishes prepared with locally sourced ingredients and relish the intense flavors of the region.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dakos: A healthy salad of barley rusk topped with tomatoes, feta cheese, and olives.

  • Mpougatsa: An indulgent dessert of filo pastry stuffed with sweet cream and dusted with icing sugar.

  • Giouvetsi: A wholesome meal of baked lamb or beef with orzo pasta and grated cheese.

  • Kalitsounia: A savory pastry stuffed with a mixture of cheeses, herbs, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: This potent grape-based spirit is a staple drink in Rethymno and can be enjoyed as a digestive after meals.

  • Tsikoudia: A grape-based distillate that is a close cousin of the Italian grappa. It is used as an aperitif before a meal.

  • Cretan Wine: The Rethymno region is renowned for its excellent wine, and visitors can indulge in some wine-tasting tours and savor the flavors and aromas of the local wine.

Where to Stay in Rethymno (regional unit): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous options for accommodation in Rethymno, ranging from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Rethymno:

  • Rimondi Boutique Hotel: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of the old town and offers visitors an authentic experience of the region’s culture and heritage.

  • Ikones Seafront Luxury Suites: These spacious suites offer breathtaking views of the sea and are an excellent choice for visitors seeking an indulgent stay.

  • The Lemon Tree House: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of the old town and offers visitors a cozy and comfortable stay.

  • Johaness Inn: This budget-friendly hostel is a great option for backpackers and solo travelers looking for a comfortable and affordable stay.

What to Buy in Rethymno (regional unit): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rethymno is renowned for its handicrafts, food products, and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Rethymno:

  • Olive Oil: The Rethymno region is famous for its high-quality olive oil, which is considered some of the best in the world.

  • Ceramics: Visitors can find an array of handmade ceramics, including plates, vases, and bowls, that reflect the region’s cultural heritage.

  • Local Honey: The Rethymno region produces some of the finest honey in Greece, which is harvested from the wildflowers that grow in the region.

  • Leather Products: Visitors can find a wide range of leather products, including shoes, bags, and belts, that are handmade and of exceptional quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Rethymno (regional unit)

The local culture and customs of Rethymno are deeply rooted in the region’s long and rich history. Visitors should respect the traditions and customs of the locals, which include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and places of worship

  • Greet locals with a smile and exchange pleasantries

  • Take off shoes when entering homes and religious sites

  • Avoid excessive drinking or rowdiness in public places

Best Time to Visit Rethymno (regional unit)

The best time to visit Rethymno is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and visitors can enjoy the region’s scenic beauty and outdoor activities.

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region

  • Visit the historic sites and landmarks

Safety and Health

Safety in Rethymno (regional unit)

Rethymno is generally a safe place for visitors to travel, but visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety, including:

  • Keeping an eye on personal belongings and valuables

  • Avoiding going out alone at night

  • Staying within well-lit and populated areas

  • Following traffic rules and regulations

Health in Rethymno (regional unit)

Medical facilities in Rethymno are of a high standard, and visitors can find hospitals and clinics in the city and surrounding areas. Visitors should take standard health precautions, including:

  • Drinking bottled or boiled water to avoid waterborne diseases

  • Applying sunscreen and using insect repellent

  • Planning for any necessary vaccinations before arrival

Getting to Rethymno (regional unit): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rethymno (regional unit)

The nearest airport to Rethymno is the Heraklion International Airport, which is located about an hour and a half’s drive away. Visitors can catch a bus or hire a taxi to get to Rethymno from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Rethymno (regional unit)

Visitors can also take a bus from major cities in Greece to get to Rethymno. The main bus stations in Rethymno are located in the city center and offer connections to other destinations on the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car, motorbike, or bicycle to explore the region and its attractions. The roads are well maintained, and visitors can enjoy scenic drives through the mountains and beaches.

How to Get Around in Rethymno (regional unit)

Visitors can get around Rethymno by bus, taxi, or by renting a car, motorbike, or bicycle. Buses are the most affordable and convenient option for getting around the city and its surrounding areas, with frequent services to popular destinations.

Where to Go Next from Rethymno (regional unit): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are numerous destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after visiting Rethymno, including:

  • Chania: This beautiful town is located about an hour’s drive from Rethymno and is known for its Venetian harbor, cobbled streets, and charming old town.

  • Elafonisi Beach: Located on the southwestern tip of Crete, this stunning beach is renowned for its pink sand and turquoise waters.

  • Knossos Palace: Located near Heraklion, this ancient palace is considered one of the most important archaeological sites on the island.

Conclusion

Rethymno is a paradise for travelers seeking a blend of heritage, nature, and adventure. From the stunning beaches to the magnificent mountains, the region offers visitors a chance to explore the best that Crete has to offer. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their perfect trip to Rethymno, experience the local culture and customs, and create long-lasting memories.
