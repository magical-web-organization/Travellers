Introduction to Meteora

Meteora is an area of extraordinary natural beauty located in the Trikala region of Thessaly in Greece. It is famous for its towering rock formations, which rise up to 400 meters from the plain below. The name “Meteora” means “suspended in air,” which perfectly describes the surreal experience of standing at the foot of these massive cliffs and gazing up at the monasteries that seem to be hovering in the sky.

List of Places to Visit in Meteora

Meteora is home to six monasteries, each with its own unique history and architecture. Visitors can explore the monasteries and their beautiful frescoes, as well as enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The six monasteries are:

  • Great Meteoron Monastery
  • Varlaam Monastery
  • Roussanou Monastery
  • St. Nicholas Monastery
  • Holy Trinity Monastery
  • St. Stephen’s Monastery

Apart from the monasteries, there are also many other culturally important attractions and natural wonders to discover in Meteora.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Natural History Museum of Meteora is a great place to learn about the geology and flora and fauna of the area. The Municipal Art Gallery of Kalambaka features works by Greek artists from the 19th and 20th centuries. The Archaeological Collection of Kalambaka showcases artifacts from the Neolithic era up until Roman times. And the Byzantine and Christian Museum of Kastraki houses a collection of icons and religious artifacts from the Byzantine era.

Natural Attractions of Meteora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Meteora is also an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can hike or bike through the surrounding hills and valleys to get up close and personal with the unique landscapes of the region. The rock formations themselves offer opportunities for rock climbing and rappelling. And the various lookout points around the area offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meteora

There are numerous hiking and biking routes throughout the Meteora area, each offering its own unique views and challenges. Some noteworthy routes include the Path of the Monks, which connects several monasteries and other sites of interest, and the Megalo Papingo-Astraka Refuge route, which ascends to the Astraka Refuge and offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Meteora: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the various monasteries and hiking routes, there are many other activities and tours available in Meteora. Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride for a bird’s-eye view of the landscape, or go on a sunset or stargazing tour. There are also guided tours of the monasteries and other cultural attractions available, as well as horseback riding tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Meteora

For those looking to make the most of their time in Meteora, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the Great Meteoron Monastery and the surrounding hiking trails.
  • Day 2: Visit the Varlaam Monastery and go on a guided tour of Kalambaka, including its museums and historic sites.
  • Day 3: Hike the Path of the Monks, visiting several monasteries along the way.
  • Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride or go on a sunset tour.
  • Day 5: Visit the Holy Trinity Monastery and explore the scenic lookout points around the area.
  • Day 6: Go on a horseback riding tour or explore the local food and drink scene.
  • Day 7: Visit the Byzantine and Christian Museum of Kastraki and the Natural History Museum of Meteora before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Meteora

Meteora has a rich culinary tradition that is heavily influenced by the locally grown produce and the traditional cooking methods of the region. Visitors should be sure to try the following:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spetzofai: A spicy sausage and pepper stew.
  • Pita: A flaky pastry filled with cheese, spinach, or meat.
  • Moussaka: A hearty dish made with layers of eggplant, ground meat, and béchamel sauce.
  • Galaktoboureko: A sweet pastry made with phyllo dough and custard.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A traditional Greek spirit made from distilled grapes.
  • Tsipouro: Another popular Greek spirit made from distilled grape skins and stems.
  • Wine: The region produces a wide variety of high-quality wines, including reds, whites, and rosés.

Where to Stay in Meteora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Meteora has a range of accommodation options available, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Meteora: A 4-star hotel located in Kastraki, with stunning views of the surrounding monasteries.
  • Dellas Boutique Hotel: A 4-star hotel located in Kalambaka, with elegant décor and a spa.
  • Camping Vrachos Kastraki: A budget-friendly camping site located in Kastraki, with beautiful views and a cozy café.

What to Buy in Meteora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs and local products to bring home from Meteora include:

  • Icons and religious artifacts
  • Locally produced wine and spirits
  • Handmade pottery
  • Olive oil and other local products

Local Culture and Customs of Meteora

Meteora is a region with deep cultural and religious traditions. Visitors should be respectful when exploring the various monasteries and other cultural sites, and dress modestly when entering religious buildings. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering monasteries and churches.

Best Time to Visit Meteora

The best time to visit Meteora is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the landscape is lush and green. Summer can be quite hot, with temperatures reaching up to 35°C, and the winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meteora

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when entering religious buildings.
  • Remove shoes before entering monasteries and churches.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the peace and quiet of the monasteries and other cultural sites.
  • Wear revealing clothing when entering religious buildings.
  • Touch or remove any items from the monasteries or other cultural sites.
  • Drive recklessly or park in unauthorized areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Meteora

Meteora is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to avoid theft and scams.

Health in Meteora

Visitors to Meteora should ensure that they have up-to-date vaccinations and carry any necessary medications with them. The region is generally safe from infectious diseases, but precautions should still be taken to avoid mosquito bites and other common health risks.

Getting to Meteora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Meteora

The nearest airport to Meteora is the Thessaloniki International Airport, which is approximately 235 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Meteora.

Bus Stations Near Meteora

The main bus station serving Meteora is in the nearby town of Kalambaka. From there, visitors can take a taxi or walk to their accommodation.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Meteora by car or train. The closest train station is in the nearby town of Trikala, and there are several car rental companies in the area.

How to Get Around in Meteora

The best way to get around Meteora is by car or taxi, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also explore the area by hiking or biking, as many of the sites of interest are accessible via hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Meteora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many other destinations to explore in the surrounding area, including:

  • The nearby town of Trikala, with its historic Old Town and many museums and cultural sites.
  • The UNESCO World Heritage Site of Delphi, with its ancient ruins and stunning views.
  • The nearby coastal city of Volos, with its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Meteora is a truly unique and awe-inspiring destination, with something to offer every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural exploration, or just soaking up the beautiful scenery, you’re sure to find something to love in Meteora. Start planning your trip today!
