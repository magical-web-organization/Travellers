Introduction to Plastira Lake

Located in the region of Thessaly in central Greece, Plastira Lake is a stunning artificial lake named after Nikolaos Plastiras, a general and politician who served as Prime Minister of Greece in the late 1940s. It was created in the 1950s for irrigation purposes and now serves as a popular tourist destination due to its natural beauty and outdoor activities. The lake covers a surface area of 25 square kilometers and is surrounded by lush green mountains, making it a picturesque spot for anyone seeking adventure and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Plastira Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Monastiri - located in the nearby Monastiraki village, this museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the region.

  • The Castle of Neraida - perched on a rocky hilltop, this medieval castle offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

  • The Church of Agios Nikolaos - located in the town of Neochori, this beautiful Byzantine-style church features stunning frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Plastira Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseback riding - several riding centers offer horseback riding tours to explore the surrounding countryside.

  • Hiking - the area is filled with hiking trails, ranging from easy to challenging, and providing amazing views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

  • Kayaking and canoeing - visitors can rent kayaks and canoes to explore the lake at their own pace.

  • Fishing - the lake is home to several types of fish, including trout and carp, making it a popular spot for fishing enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plastira Lake

There are several hiking and biking routes around Plastira Lake, each offering stunning viewpoints and the chance to explore the surrounding natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Trail of the Centaurs - a well-marked trail that covers 27 kilometers around the lake. It takes about two days to hike and offers stunning views of the lake, forests, and villages.

  • The Forest of Pezoula - this easy trail passes through a beautiful forest and offers stunning views of the lake.

  • The Kedros Trail - a challenging trail that climbs to the top of Kedros Mountain and provides breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Plastira Lake: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the villages around the lake - explore the charming local villages and enjoy the traditional cuisine.

  • Take a boat tour of the lake - enjoy the beautiful scenery from the water and learn about the history and culture of the area.

  • Visit the nearby Meteora Monasteries - listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site, these stunning monasteries are perched on top of towering rock formations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plastira Lake

Day 1: Arrive at Plastira Lake, take a boat tour of the lake, and get settled into your accommodations.

Day 2: Explore the villages around the lake, including Neochori, Moshato, and Kryoneri.

Day 3: Hike the Trail of the Centaurs.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby Meteora Monasteries.

Day 5: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the lake at your own pace.

Day 6: Horseback riding or biking around the lake.

Day 7: Relax and soak in the natural beauty of Plastira Lake before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Plastira Lake

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tsipouro - a traditional Greek spirit made from grape skins.

  • Kokoretsi - a lamb dish wrapped around various internal organs.

  • Tyropita - a Greek cheese pie filled with feta cheese and often served as a breakfast or snack item.

Local Beverages

  • Plastiras Lake Black Ale - a locally brewed beer made using natural spring water.

  • Tsipouro - a traditional Greek spirit that is often enjoyed as an aperitif or with dessert.

Where to Stay in Plastira Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Plastiras Lake Resort - a luxurious resort that offers stunning views of the lake.

  • To Patriko Mas - a traditional guesthouse located in the village of Moshato.

  • Archontiko Tzoufi - a charming guesthouse located in the village of Kryoneri.

What to Buy in Plastira Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oregano - the region is known for producing high-quality oregano, which makes for a great souvenir to bring back home.

  • Artisanal cheese - the region is known for producing a variety of high-quality cheeses, including feta and graviera.

Local Culture and Customs of Plastira Lake

The people of Plastira Lake are known for their warm and welcoming hospitality. They celebrate a variety of traditional festivals and holidays throughout the year, including the Carnival of Kryoneri in February and the Feast of Agia Paraskevi in July. Greek Orthodox Christianity is the predominant religion in the area, and visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Plastira Lake

The best time to visit Plastira Lake is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there is less chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plastira Lake

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the wildlife in the area.

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the national park.

  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plastira Lake

Plastira Lake is a safe place for tourists to visit. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of wildfires during the dry summer months and should follow local guidelines on fire safety.

Health in Plastira Lake

There are no major health risks associated with visiting Plastira Lake. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Plastira Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plastira Lake

The nearest international airports to Plastira Lake are Athens International Airport and Thessaloniki International Airport, both of which are approximately a 3-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Plastira Lake

Several bus companies provide services to the town of Karditsa, which is the main hub for transportation to Plastira Lake. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach the lake.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Plastira Lake by train or by renting a car and driving themselves. The region is well-connected by major highways and roads.

How to Get Around in Plastira Lake

The best way to get around Plastira Lake is by renting a car or a motorbike. This provides visitors with more freedom and allows them to explore the surrounding countryside at their own pace.
