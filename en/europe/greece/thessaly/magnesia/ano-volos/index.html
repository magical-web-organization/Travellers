Located in Magnesia, Thessaly, Greece, Ano Volos is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This charming village is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, things to do, local culture, food, and more in Ano Volos.

Introduction to Ano Volos

Ano Volos is a small village located just north of the city of Volos. The village sits on the slopes of Mount Pelion, a beautiful mountain known for its lush forests, pristine beaches, and charming villages. Ano Volos is a traditional Greek village, known for its winding streets, beautiful old houses, and stunning views of the sea.

List of Places to Visit in Ano Volos

If you are planning a trip to Ano Volos, you will not be disappointed. The following are the top places to visit in Ano Volos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ano Volos Museum of Folklore and History – This museum is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Ano Volos. The museum features exhibits on local customs, traditional clothing, and more.
  2. Agios Georgios Church – This beautiful church dates back to the 17th century and is known for its stunning iconostasis and frescoes.
  3. Pelion Steam Train – Take a historical train ride through the lush forests of Mount Pelion and admire the beautiful scenery along the way.

Natural Attractions of Ano Volos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Pelion Hiking Trails – Discover the beauty of Mount Pelion on some of the best hiking trails in Greece. The trails are well-marked and pass through stunning forests, mountain streams, and hidden waterfalls.
  2. Mylopotamos Beach – This stunning beach is located just a short drive from Ano Volos. The beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and beautiful scenery.
  3. Chania Waterfall – This waterfall is located just outside of Ano Volos and is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ano Volos

Ano Volos is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ano Volos:

  • Tsagarada to Fakistra Beach – This 6km route takes hikers through beautiful forest trails and ends at Fakistra Beach.
  • Damouchari to Agios Ioannis – This scenic 5km route offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea and passes through olive groves, quaint chapels, and old stone houses.
  • Hania to Agios Nikolaos – This 8km route takes hikers through beautiful forests and valleys, and ends at the stunning Agios Nikolaos Beach.

Best Things to Do in Ano Volos: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, here are some of the best things to do in Ano Volos:

  • Visit the Ano Volos Museum of Folklore and History.
  • Take a ride on the historic Pelion Steam Train.
  • Hike or bike on the beautiful trails of Mount Pelion.
  • Swim or relax on the beautiful beaches of the Aegean Sea.
  • Drink the local tsipouro, a traditional Greek spirit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ano Volos

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Ano Volos:

Day Activity
1 Arrive, explore the old town of Ano Volos

4 Visit the beautiful Mylopotamos Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Ano Volos

Ano Volos is known for its delicious local cuisine and traditional Greek products. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gemista – Stuffed peppers and tomatoes
  • Spanakopita – Spinach pie
  • Gigantes – Giant butter beans in tomato sauce
  • Tsitsiravla – Wild greens with garlic and olive oil

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro – Traditional Greek spirit
  • Raki – Another type of traditional Greek spirit
  • Local wine – Pelion is known for producing excellent wines

Where to Stay in Ano Volos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ano Volos has plenty of accommodation options suitable for all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Ano Volos:

  • Archontiko Naoumidi – A beautiful guesthouse located in the heart of Ano Volos.
  • Hotel Pelion Resort – A modern hotel located in the nearby village of Portaria.
  • Guesthouse Platanofylla – A charming guesthouse surrounded by lush vegetation.

What to Buy in Ano Volos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ano Volos is known for its traditional Greek products and crafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Handmade pottery
  • Local honey
  • Olive oil
  • Tsipouro or raki
  • Traditional clothing and jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Ano Volos

Ano Volos has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions, customs, and festivals. Here are some of the most significant ones:

  • Carnival – The village celebrates Carnival with parades, feasts, and traditional masquerades.
  • Easter – Easter is a significant holiday in Greece, and Ano Volos celebrates it with solemn processions and festive meals.
  • August 15th – The village celebrates the Assumption of the Virgin Mary with a grand festival featuring music, dance, and delicious food.

Best Time to Visit Ano Volos

The best time to visit Ano Volos is in the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months can be busy and hot, while the winter months are mild but can be rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ano Volos

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ano Volos:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites
  • Try the delicious local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions
  • Speak loudly or behave inappropriately

Safety and Health

Safety in Ano Volos

Ano Volos is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always helpful to take precautionary measures such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Ano Volos

There are several medical facilities in the area, including hospitals and private clinics. It is essential to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Ano Volos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ano Volos is easily accessible by car, bus, and train. The following are the best ways to get to Ano Volos:

Airports Near Ano Volos

The closest international airport to Ano Volos is Thessaloniki Airport, which is around 200km away. The airport is served by several airlines, including Aegean Air, Ryanair, and Easyjet.

Bus Stations Near Ano Volos

Ano Volos is connected to nearby villages and towns by a network of buses operated by KTEL. Buses run regularly to Volos, the regional capital, as well as other destinations in Thessaly.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, the nearby city of Volos is served by several trains a day from Athens and Thessaloniki.

How to Get Around in Ano Volos

The best way to get around Ano Volos is on foot, as the village is small, and the streets are narrow and winding. Taxis are also available for longer trips or for transporting luggage.

Where to Go Next from Ano Volos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ano Volos is an excellent base for exploring the beautiful Pelion Peninsula. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Volos – The regional capital is a bustling city known for its beautiful waterfront, lively nightlife, and historic buildings.
  • Makrinitsa – This beautiful village is known for its traditional architecture, stunning views, and cobblestone streets.
  • Tsagarada – Another charming village known for its beautiful beaches and lush forests.

Conclusion

Ano Volos is a beautiful village that offers visitors an authentic Greek experience. From its rich history and culture to its stunning natural scenery, there is something for everyone in Ano Volos. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to visit this hidden gem in Magnesia, Greece.
