Introduction to Volos

Located in Magnesia, in Thessaly in Greece, Volos is a beautiful city that offers a mix of picturesque landscapes and cultural attractions. This coastal city has something to offer every traveler, from iconic museums and historical sites to scenic beaches and countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Volos

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Volos divided into culturally important attractions, natural attractions, and popular activities and tours:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Volos has a rich history and culture, and you can explore it through the numerous museums and historical sites in the city:

  • Archaeological Museum of Volos: The museum displays exhibits that date back to the Stone Age, Bronze Age, and Byzantine period.
  • Athanasakeion Archaeological Museum: This museum features exhibits such as statues, coins, and ceramics from the ancient city of Demetrias.
  • Theophilos Museum: This museum pays tribute to the famous folk painter from Lesbos, Theophilos, whose works are displayed here.
  • The Castle of Volos: Located on the hill of Palea, the castle offers a panoramic view of Volos and the Pagasetic Gulf.

Natural Attractions of Volos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Volos has a beautiful coastline, mountains, and countryside, making it perfect for outdoor activities and scenic views:

  • Pelion Mountain: Pelion is a beautiful place for hiking, mountain biking, and skiing during the winter months.
  • Argonauts Path: The path is located on the coast of the Pagasetic Gulf and is famous for its natural beauty and views of the sea.
  • Makrinitsa Village: This picturesque village on the slopes of Pelion offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Volos: Activities and Tours

Volos has a variety of activities and tours that’ll keep you entertained throughout your stay:

  • Volos City Tour - A guided tour that takes you through the city’s cultural landmarks
  • Pagasetic Gulf Cruise - A boat tour of the scenic Pagasetic Gulf
  • Horseback Riding in Pelion - A perfect way to explore the mountains and the countryside during the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volos

Volos provides many opportunities to explore the outdoors through hiking and biking:

  • Centaurs Path: A trail that starts from the port of Volos and goes through some of Pelion’s most scenic locations.
  • Milies - Kala Nera Trail: A beautiful trail with waterfalls and rivers that connects two picturesque villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Volos

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring Volos and its surrounding areas:

  • Day 1 - Explore the city’s cultural landmarks such as the Archaeological Museum of Volos and The Castle of Volos.
  • Day 2 - Visit the village of Portaria and take the funicular to Makrinitsa Village.
  • Day 3 - Go on a Pagasetic Gulf cruise and visit the nearby island of Trikeri.
  • Day 4 - Visit the traditional village of Tsagkarada and hike through the well-known Centaurs Path.
  • Day 5 - Take a horseback riding trip in Pelion.
  • Day 6 - Visit Meteora Monasteries, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Day 7 - Spend the day relaxing at one of the nearby beaches such as Horefto Beach or Agios Ioannis Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Volos

Here are some local dishes and beverages you can try while in Volos:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boureki - A pie made of zucchini, potatoes, and cheese.
  • Tzatziki - A dip made from yogurt, garlic, and cucumber.
  • Saganaki - Fried cheese served with lemon.
  • Fava - A dish made of yellow split peas topped with onions and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro - A traditional spirit made from grapes, similar to grappa.
  • Retsina - A white wine with a unique taste.
  • Ouzo - A traditional Greek anise-flavored drink.

Where to Stay in Volos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Volos offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses:

  • Domotel Xenia Volos - A luxurious beachfront hotel with stunning views of the Pagasetic Gulf.
  • Hotel Aegli - Located in the heart of Volos, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a rooftop bar.
  • Leda Suites - A budget-friendly option located in the picturesque village of Afissos.

What to Buy in Volos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Volos is known for its traditional products such as:

  • Olive oil - Made from local olives, the olive oil in Volos is of high quality and great for cooking and drizzling on salads.
  • Honey - The region is known for its honey, and you can find different varieties in the local markets.
  • Handmade jewelry - Volos has many talented artisans who create beautiful handmade jewelry using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Volos

The locals in Volos are friendly and welcoming, and it’s essential to respect their customs and traditions. The Greeks love their food, family, and dancing, and you’ll find plenty of cultural events, festivals, and celebrations throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Volos

The best time to visit Volos is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, the shoulder seasons of May and September offer pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volos

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Volos:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the city’s historical landmarks.
  • Spend time in nature, hiking, and enjoying the scenery.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Litter or damage any of the natural or historical sites.

Safety and Health

Volos is a relatively safe city, and you shouldn’t experience any major safety issues. However, take regular precautions such as locking your belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Safety in Volos

As with any city, tourists should be cautious of pickpocketing and scams targeting tourists. But generally, Volos is a safe town.

Health in Volos

Volos has many medical centers and hospitals in case you require medical attention.

Getting to Volos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Volos is easily accessible through various transportation options, including air, bus, and car:

Airports Near Volos

The nearest airport to Volos is Nea Anchialos Airport, which is around 30km away.

Bus Stations Near Volos

There are daily buses from Athens to Volos, and it takes about four hours to reach Volos by bus. The bus station in Volos is located in the city center.

Other Options

Traveling by car is also an option, and several car rental agencies operate at the airport in Volos.

How to Get Around in Volos

Volos is a small city, and you can explore it on foot. You can also use the public transportation system, which includes buses, taxis, and trains.

Where to Go Next from Volos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Volos is located in an area with many other nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Meteora Monasteries - A UNESCO World Heritage Site located a 2-hour drive from Volos.
  • Skiathos Island - A popular destination known for its beautiful beaches.
  • Trikala - A city located 48km away that has historical and archaeological sites worth visiting.

Conclusion

Volos has a rich history, beautiful scenery, and a vibrant cultural scene that attracts visitors from all over the world. With this travel guide, you can easily plan your next visit to this beautiful city and explore the best it has to offer.
