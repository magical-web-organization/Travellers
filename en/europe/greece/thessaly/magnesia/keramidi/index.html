Keramidi, located in the Thessaly region of Greece, is a beautiful and historic town that attracts tourists from all over the world. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and unique culture, Keramidi has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Keramidi, including where to go, what to do, and what to eat.

Introduction to Keramidi

Keramidi is a small town located in the region of Magnesia in central Greece. It is nestled in the foothills of Mount Pelion on the east coast of the country. The town is known for its quaint charm, narrow streets, and traditional architecture. Keramidi has a rich history dating back to the ancient Greek times, with many interesting cultural landmarks and historical sites to visit. Visitors to Keramidi enjoy exploring the natural beauty of the surrounding area, hiking the trails, and relaxing on the beaches of the Aegean Sea.

List of Places to Visit in Keramidi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Keramidi Castle - A stunning medieval castle located on the outskirts of the town. It offers panoramic views of the surrounding area and is a popular spot for photographers.

  2. The Monastery of St. George - This historic monastery is located just outside Keramidi, and is home to a museum with a collection of religious artifacts.

  3. The Old Town - Located in the heart of Keramidi, the old town is a maze of narrow streets and alleys, lined with traditional buildings and houses. It’s a great place to explore and soak up the local culture.

Natural Attractions of Keramidi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Pelion - This stunning mountain range offers some of the most spectacular views in the region. Visitors can enjoy hiking or biking along the many trails, or take a scenic drive to enjoy the views.

  2. Agios Ioannis Beach - This beautiful sandy beach is just a short drive from Keramidi. It’s a great place to relax and soak up the sun or take a refreshing swim in the crystal-clear water.

  3. The Pinakates Waterfall - This stunning natural landmark is located just outside Keramidi. It’s a popular spot for hiking and enjoying the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keramidi

Keramidi is surrounded by beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the stunning natural beauty of the region. Some popular routes include:

  1. The Centaurs Path - This picturesque trail takes hikers through lush forests and scenic valleys, and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  2. Pelion Cycling Tour - This scenic bike tour takes visitors along the coast and through the countryside, and is a great way to explore the area while getting some exercise.

Best Things to Do in Keramidi: Activities and Tours

  1. Olive Oil Tasting - Keramidi is known for its high-quality olive oil. Visitors can attend a tasting and learn about the history and production of this important local product.

  2. Traditional Greek Dancing - Visitors can learn about the local culture by attending a traditional Greek dancing event, which is held in the town square throughout the summer.

  3. Boat Tours - Keramidi is located on the coast, and visitors can take boat tours to nearby islands and beaches, or even go on a sunset cruise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keramidi

Day 1 - Explore the Old Town of Keramidi and visit the Monastery of St. George

Day 2 - Go hiking or biking on the Centaurs Path

Day 3 - Take a boat tour to a nearby island or beach

Day 4 - Visit the Pinakates Waterfall and enjoy a picnic in the surrounding area

Day 5 - Spend the day relaxing on Agios Ioannis Beach

Day 6 - Attend a traditional Greek dancing event in the town square

Day 7 - Visit Keramidi Castle and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding area

Food and Drink Scene of Keramidi

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kourabiedes - Traditional Greek almond shortbread cookies dusted with powdered sugar, often enjoyed during the holiday season.

  2. Moussaka - A classic Greek dish made with layers of baked eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.

  3. Tzatziki - A refreshing dip made with yogurt, garlic, and cucumber, often enjoyed with pita bread or as a topping for grilled meats.

Local Beverages

  1. Tsipouro - A traditional Greek spirit made from grape pomace, similar to Italian grappa.

  2. Retsina - A unique Greek wine that is infused with pine resin, giving it a distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Keramidi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Archontiko Elda – A traditional guesthouse located in the heart of Keramidi, offering authentic Greek hospitality and comfortable rooms.

  2. Villa Alevrota – A beautifully restored 19th-century villa with stunning views of the surrounding area and all the modern amenities needed for a comfortable stay.

  3. Alexandros Palace Hotel & Suites – A luxury resort with breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea, offering a wide range of amenities and activities.

What to Buy in Keramidi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Olive Oil - Keramidi produces some of the best olive oil in Greece, which makes for a delicious and healthy souvenir.

  2. Handmade Pottery - Keramidi is known for traditional pottery, incorporating local designs and ancient methods.

Local Culture and Customs of Keramidi

Keramidi is a traditional Greek town, and visitors should expect to encounter a warm and welcoming community that values family, food, and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Keramidi

The best time to visit Keramidi is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keramidi

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially olive oil and seafood dishes.

  • Learn a few Greek phrases to communicate with locals.

  • Follow local customs and traditions, including dress codes when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

  • Expect services to be open during the siesta period in the early afternoon.

  • Disrespect local culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keramidi

Keramidi is generally a safe place to travel, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Keramidi

Visitors should ensure they have all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Greece. Keramidi has a local health clinic where visitors can receive basic medical care, and the nearest hospital is in the nearby town of Volos.

Getting to Keramidi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keramidi

The nearest airport to Keramidi is the Volos International Airport (VOL), which is located about 40 minutes’ drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Keramidi

KTEL bus lines operate buses to Keramidi from Volos. Visitors can take a bus from the Volos Bus Station to Keramidi.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car at the airport or in Volos to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Keramidi

Keramidi is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Keramidi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. The nearby village of Milies - A picturesque village located in the mountains, known for its historic train station and stunning views.

  2. The city of Volos - A thriving port city with a rich history and culture, offering everything from museums and galleries to shopping and nightlife.

Conclusion

Keramidi is a beautiful and historic town that has something for everyone. Visitors can soak up the local culture, explore stunning natural landmarks, and enjoy delicious local cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are ready to plan your perfect trip to Keramidi, Greece.
