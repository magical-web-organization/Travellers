Xinovrysi is a charming village located in South Pelion, Magnesia, in Thessaly, Greece. Surrounded by lush greenery and crystal-clear waters, this hidden gem boasts a perfect combination of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Xinovrysi has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Xinovrysi.

Introduction to Xinovrysi

Xinovrysi is a traditional Greek village nestled in the heart of Mount Pelion, just a short distance from the Aegean Sea. The village is surrounded by dense forests and crystal-clear rivers, making it a haven for nature lovers. The architecture of the village reflects the traditional style of the mainland, with stone houses, narrow streets, and picturesque squares.

List of Places to Visit in Xinovrysi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Xinovrysi is steeped in history, and visitors can explore the village’s rich cultural heritage through its museums and historical sites.

  • Archontiko Milia: This is a beautifully restored old mansion that now serves as a cultural center. Visitors can admire the traditional architecture and learn about the history of the mansion and the village.
  • The Holy Temple of Saint Haralambos: A beautiful church that dates back to the 16th century, known for its intricate frescoes and stunning gold leaf iconography.
  • The Folklore Museum of Xinovrysi: Located in a traditional stone house, this museum features exhibits on local traditions, costumes, and handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Xinovrysi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Xinovrysi’s natural beauty is breathtaking, and visitors can explore the great outdoors through hiking, biking, and swimming.

  • Hiking in the Forests of Xinovrysi: There are numerous hiking trails in and around the village that lead through lush forests and stunning gorges. Trails vary in difficulty, so there’s a hike for every level of fitness.
  • Biking in Pelion: Explore the wider Pelion region on two wheels by following scenic routes from Xinovrysi to nearby villages and beaches.
  • Fakistra Beach: A stunning beach with crystal-clear turquoise waters and fine sand, surrounded by steep cliffs and lush vegetation. This beach is known for its natural beauty and is accessible via a 30-minute hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xinovrysi

Xinovrysi is home to many hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the opportunity to explore the great outdoors. Some popular routes include:

  • The Trail of Centaurs: A steep trail that leads to the village of Tsagkarada, passing through dense forests and scenic viewpoints. The trail is named after the mythological creatures that were believed to inhabit Pelion.
  • The Route of Watermills: A gentle trail that follows the course of a river through the village, passing by the old watermills that once powered the village’s thriving textile industry.
  • The Pelion Coastal Trail: This 53-kilometer trail takes hikers from the village of Kalamaki to the village of Damouchari, passing through some of the region’s most stunning beaches and scenic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Xinovrysi: Activities and Tours

Xinovrysi offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all interests and ages.

  • Cooking Classes: Learn to cook traditional Greek dishes with a local chef and experience the flavors and aromas of Greek cuisine.
  • Horse Riding: Horse riding tours are available in and around Xinovrysi, offering visitors a chance to explore the village’s stunning surroundings on horseback.
  • Olive Oil Tasting: Visit a local olive grove and learn about the production of high-quality olive oil that Pelion is famous for. Visitors have the chance to taste and purchase local olive oils.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xinovrysi

Day 1: Arrival in Xinovrysi, check-in at your preferred accommodation, and enjoy a leisurely stroll around the village.

Day 2: Explore the village’s cultural heritage by visiting the Holy Temple of Saint Haralambos and the Folklore Museum of Xinovrysi.

Day 3: Embark on a hiking or biking adventure by choosing one of the scenic trails around the village.

Day 4: Spend the day relaxing on Fakistra Beach and soaking up the sun and crystal-clear waters.

Day 5: Visit the charming villages of Tsangarada and Makrinitsa, which are located nearby and are known for their stunning architecture.

Day 6: Learn to cook traditional Greek dishes with a local chef, followed by an olive oil tasting experience.

Day 7: Depart from Xinovrysi and continue exploring the wider Pelion region.

Food and Drink Scene of Xinovrysi

Must-Try Dishes

Xinovrysi’s food scene revolves around traditional Greek cuisine, with an emphasis on fresh, local ingredients.

  • Pita Pelion: A savory pie filled with greens, fennel, onions, and dill, wrapped in homemade phyllo pastry.
  • Tsipouro: A traditional Greek spirit made from the grape marc that is left over after pressing wine grapes.
  • Kourabiedes: A traditional Greek cookie made with almonds, sprinkled with powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: A traditional Greek spirit made from the grape marc that is left over after pressing wine grapes.
  • Retsina: A white wine made from the resin of pine trees that is native to Greece and is known for its distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Xinovrysi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xinovrysi offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from charming guesthouses to luxury hotels.

  • Villa Rusula: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of the village and offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Enastron Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers spacious rooms decorated with traditional Pelion-style furnishings and a beautiful garden.
  • 12 Months Luxury Resort: A luxurious resort located just a short drive from Xinovrysi, featuring private suites and villas, a pool, and a spa.

What to Buy in Xinovrysi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Xinovrysi offers a range of souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home as a memento of their trip. Some popular options include:

  • Traditional Textiles: Xinovrysi was once a hub of textile production, and visitors can purchase traditional textiles such as scarves, rugs, and tablecloths.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local artisans produce a range of handmade crafts, including jewelry, ceramics, and woodcarvings.
  • Olive Oil: Pelion is known for its high-quality olive oil, and visitors can purchase bottles of local olive oil to take home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Xinovrysi

Xinovrysi is a closely-knit community that values hospitality, respect, and tradition. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, such as dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites and greeting locals warmly.

Best Time to Visit Xinovrysi

The best time to visit Xinovrysi is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer is the busiest time of year, and visitors should expect higher prices and larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xinovrysi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Try local specialties and dishes

Don’t:

  • Leave rubbish behind
  • Ignore warning signs when hiking or swimming
  • Bargain too aggressively when purchasing souvenirs

Safety and Health

Safety in Xinovrysi

Xinovrysi is a safe village to visit, but visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as securing valuables and locking doors.

Health in Xinovrysi

Travelers should make sure they are up to date with routine vaccinations, such as the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine and the flu vaccine. It’s also recommended to have a Hepatitis A vaccine for most travelers.

Getting to Xinovrysi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Xinovrysi by several means of transport, including:

  • Air- The nearest airport is in Volos, which is approximately 60 kilometers from Xinovrysi.
  • Bus- Buses connect Xinovrysi to nearby towns and cities, including Volos and Athens.
  • Car- Visitors can rent a car and drive from Athens or Thessaloniki to Xinovrysi.

How to Get Around in Xinovrysi

Xinovrysi is a small village that can easily be explored by walking. Visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas can hire a car, bike, or join a tour.

Where to Go Next from Xinovrysi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Xinovrysi is located in the heart of Pelion, making it an ideal base for exploring the wider region.

  • Volos: A vibrant coastal city located approximately 50 kilometers from Xinovrysi, known for its stunning architecture and lively nightlife.
  • Skiathos Island: This beautiful island is accessible by a short ferry ride from the nearby port of Volos, and is known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Meteora Monasteries: These stunning monasteries are located approximately two hours’ drive from Xinovrysi and are perched atop towering rock formations.

Conclusion

Xinovrysi is a hidden gem of Pelion, offering visitors a perfect combination of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or food, there’s something for everyone in Xinovrysi. Plan your trip now and discover what this charming village has to offer!
