Introduction to Tsagkarada

Nestled in the northeast part of Mount Pelion, Tsagkarada is a charming village in the heart of Magnesia region in Thessaly, Greece. Known for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, and traditional architecture, Tsagkarada is a popular destination for nature lovers and those seeking a tranquil escape from the city.

List of Places to Visit in Tsagkarada

Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural landmarks, natural wonders, or outdoor adventures, Tsagkarada has something for every type of traveler.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Old School of Tsagkarada: Now converted into a library and cultural center, the old school of Tsagkarada is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of the village.
  • The Church of Agia Paraskevi: Built in the 18th century, this beautiful Byzantine church features stunning frescoes and is one of the oldest landmarks in Tsagkarada.
  • The Museum of Folk Art and History: Learn about the village’s rich history and traditional way of life at this fascinating museum, which showcases a collection of artifacts and objects used in daily life.

Natural Attractions of Tsagkarada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mylopotamos Beach: Famous for its crystal-clear waters and natural beauty, Mylopotamos Beach is a must-visit for beach lovers in the area.
  • Damouchari Beach: This secluded beach is perfect for a peaceful day in the sun, with its calm waters and picturesque surroundings.
  • The Centaur’s Path: Follow in the footsteps of the legendary half-human, half-horse creatures said to have lived on Mount Pelion. This stunning hiking trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest and coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsagkarada

Tsagkarada is an excellent destination for those looking for hiking and biking opportunities. Some of the best routes include the Centaur’s Path, the Tsagkarada – Mouresi route, and the climb to the peak of Mount Pelion for those seeking a challenge.

Best Things to Do in Tsagkarada: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding: Discover the beauty of Mount Pelion’s trails and forests in a unique way by exploring on horseback.
  • Cooking classes: Learn how to cook traditional recipes from the region and take home the knowledge and skills needed to recreate them at home.
  • Wine tasting: The region is known for producing some excellent wines, so take a tour of the vineyards and enjoy some tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tsagkarada

Day 1-2: Explore the village’s cultural landmarks, including the Old School of Tsagkarada and the Church of Agia Paraskevi.

Day 3-4: Spend time exploring the surrounding natural beauty of Mount Pelion, including hiking the Centaur’s Path and taking a dip in Mylopotamos Beach.

Day 5-6: Experience some of the region’s unique activities, such as a horseback riding tour or a cooking class.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Volos or the quaint village of Vizitsa.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsagkarada

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Tsagkarada, be sure to try some of the local specialties, including:

  • Spetsofai: a spicy sausage and peppers dish that is a popular regional delicacy
  • Fava: a creamy bean dip typically served as an appetizer
  • Pebre: a sauce made with tomatoes, red peppers and spices, served as a dip or condiment

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: a strong spirit often served as an aperitif or digestif
  • Rakomelo: a warm, spiced honey raki drink that is perfect for sipping on a chilly evening

Where to Stay in Tsagkarada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse or a luxury hotel, there are plenty of options in Tsagkarada. Some popular choices include:

  • Hotel Tsagkarada: a traditional hotel located in the heart of the village, with stunning views of the Aegean Sea
  • Amanita Guesthouse: a charming guesthouse nestled in the forest, with cozy rooms and a beautiful courtyard.
  • Archontiko Nanou: a luxurious hotel housed in a beautiful 19th century mansion, which features stylish rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

What to Buy in Tsagkarada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: the region is known for its high-quality olive oil, which makes for a perfect souvenir or gift.
  • Handmade ceramics: pick up a beautiful and unique ceramic piece made by a local artist.
  • Dried herbs: the region is famous for its variety of herbs, including oregano and thyme, which make for a tasty and aromatic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsagkarada

Tsagkarada is known for its warm and welcoming locals, who are proud of their traditions and heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, which include celebrating local festivals and enjoying the simple pleasures of life.

Best Time to Visit Tsagkarada

The best time to visit Tsagkarada is during the summer months of June to September, when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities and beach visits. However, the region is also beautiful in the spring and fall when the landscape is lush with greenery and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsagkarada

Do:

  • Try the local specialties: Tsagkarada is known for its traditional cuisine, so be sure to sample some of the local dishes.
  • Take the time to explore on foot: The village and surrounding area are best explored on foot, so wear comfortable shoes and don’t be afraid to get lost.
  • Be respectful of the locals: The people of Tsagkarada are known for their warm hospitality, so be sure to return the favor and show respect for the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment: Tsagkarada is known for its pristine natural beauty, so make sure to protect it by not littering or damaging the landscape.
  • Disrespect the local customs: Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including dress codes and religious practices.
  • Expect a bustling nightlife scene: Tsagkarada is a tranquil and peaceful village, so don’t expect a lively nightlife scene. Instead, take the opportunity to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsagkarada

Tsagkarada is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Tsagkarada

Medical care is available in the village, but visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Tsagkarada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsagkarada

The closest airports to Tsagkarada are the Skiathos Island National Airport and the Volos Nea Anchialos National Airport, both of which offer flights from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Tsagkarada

Tsagkarada is easily accessible by bus, with regular services departing from Volos and Athens.

Other Options

For those looking for a more unique experience, consider taking a train or ferry to the region. The Pelion railway is a scenic and picturesque route that connects nearby towns and villages, while the ferry from Volos connects to the nearby islands of Skiathos and Skopelos.

How to Get Around in Tsagkarada

The village is small and easily navigable on foot, but visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Tsagkarada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Volos: a charming port city with an old-world charm and an energetic nightlife scene
  • Makrinitsa: also known as “the balcony of Mt. Pelion,” this picturesque village offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and traditional architecture.
  • Skiathos and Skopelos Islands: take a ferry from nearby Volos and explore these beautiful islands known for their stunning beaches and lush greenery.

Conclusion

Tsagkarada is a charming village in the heart of Mount Pelion in Greece, offering a mix of cultural landmarks, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural landmarks or enjoying outdoor adventures, Tsagkarada has something to offer. With plenty of accommodations, transportation options, and nearby destinations to explore, it’s the perfect destination for your next holiday.
