Introduction to Neochori

Neochori is a charming village located in the Northeast Pelion region of Greece. This beautiful destination is nestled in the lush greenery of Mount Pelion, offering breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea and the surrounding mountain range. Neochori is a perfect destination for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts, with its rich history and diverse landscapes. From hiking through forest trails to exploring traditional architecture, there is something for everyone in Neochori.

List of Places to Visit in Neochori

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Folklore Museum of Zagora: This museum holds a vast collection of cultural heirlooms, traditional clothing, and handicrafts from past centuries.
  • The Old Church of St. Nicholas: This church is a great example of traditional Pelion architecture and dates back to the 17th century.
  • The Archaeological Site of Sesklo: A prehistoric settlement that dates back to the 6th millennium BC and is considered one of the most important archaeological findings in Greece.

Natural Attractions of Neochori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Damouhari Beach: This lovely beach is a great spot for swimming and sunbathing and is surrounded by stunning hills and cliffs.
  • St. George’s Chapel: A beautiful chapel overlooking the Aegean Sea with lovely views of the Pelion peninsula.
  • The Milesi Path: A scenic trail that leads to a natural spring, ideal for a refreshing swim in clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neochori

Neochori is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts and offers various hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of fitness. The Pelion Mountain has a network of trails, and here are some must-try hikes in Neochori:

  • The Centaur Trail: A picturesque trail that winds through the forests, offering stunning views of the Aegean and the Pelion peninsula.
  • The Trail to Tsagarada: A popular trail that leads from Neochori to the traditional village of Tsagarada.

Best Things to Do in Neochori: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the village: Stroll through the cobblestone streets, and admire the traditional architecture, charming houses, and hidden corners.
  • Visit the local markets: Neochori has a beautiful market with fresh produce and local crafts.
  • Take a boat trip: Discover the Pelion coast and secluded bays on a boat trip from the port of Damouhari.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neochori

Day 1: Arrive in Neochori and explore the village. Day 2: A scenic hike on the Tsagarada trail. Day 3: Visit the Milesi Path and enjoy a swim in the natural spring. Day 4: A day trip to the Archaeological Site of Sesklo and the nearby village of Zagora. Day 5: Take a boat trip and explore the unique beaches of the Pelion Coast. Day 6: Bike through the hills and enjoy the stunning views of the Aegean. Day 7: Relax in Neochori, and enjoy the local cuisine and wine.

Food and Drink Scene of Neochori

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A traditional Greek dish with layers of juicy meat, eggplants, and potatoes, covered with creamy béchamel sauce.
  • Fasolada: A delicious soup made of white beans, tomatoes, and vegetables, drizzled with olive oil.
  • Pita bread: Freshly baked bread, often served with various dips like tzatziki.

Local Beverages

  • Retsina: A Greek white wine infused with pine resin.
  • Tsipouro: A strong, distilled spirit made from grape skins and seeds.
  • Frappé: A frothy iced coffee made with instant coffee, sugar, and ice.

Where to Stay in Neochori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Neochori offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Guesthouse Anna: A lovely guesthouse with cozy rooms and breathtaking views of the Aegean.
  • Pension Zouzoula: A quaint pension located in the heart of Neochori, offering traditional rooms.
  • 12 Months Luxury Resort: A luxurious resort with spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, spa, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Neochori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pelion Honey: A famous Greek honey known for its high quality and distinct flavor.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: Neochori has numerous shops selling traditional handicrafts like textiles, pottery, and wood carvings.
  • Local Wines: The region produces various wines of excellent quality, such as Tsipouro and Retsina.

Local Culture and Customs of Neochori

Neochori has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to ancient times. Pelion, in general, is famed for its unique music, dance, and folklore traditions. Visitors can experience the local customs and culture through various events and festivities that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Neochori

The best time to visit Neochori is during the spring and autumn, as the temperatures are pleasant, and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. Summertime is a bit crowded, and the temperatures can be hot, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neochori

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Take care of the natural environment.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in churches and religious sites.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neochori

Neochori is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should still take standard precautions like not leaving valuables in plain sight and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Neochori

Neochori has a small medical center that can handle minor injuries and illnesses. For serious medical issues, visitors should seek medical attention in nearby towns like Volos.

Getting to Neochori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neochori

The nearest airport is located in Athens, 336 km away from Neochori. Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car from the airport to reach Neochori.

Bus Stations Near Neochori

There are frequent bus services from Athens to Volos throughout the day. From Volos, visitors can take a bus or a taxi to reach Neochori.

Other Options

Driving to Neochori is also an option. Visitors can rent a car in Athens or Volos and follow the scenic route to reach Neochori.

How to Get Around in Neochori

Neochori is a small village, and visitors can easily explore the village center on foot. However, visitors can also rent bicycles, motorcycles, or cars to explore the surrounding areas and nearby villages.

Where to Go Next from Neochori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The City of Volos: This bustling city is 1-hour away from Neochori and is known for its beautiful architecture and rich culture.
  • The Traditional Village of Tsagarada: A lovely village located 10 km away from Neochori, known for its stunning natural scenery and traditional architecture.

Conclusion

Neochori is a hidden gem in Pelion, Greece, offering a blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. From its picturesque streets to its stunning trails and beaches, Neochori is a great destination for those seeking a unique experience. Visitors can explore the village’s rich history and enjoy its local cuisine, wines, and handicrafts. Don’t miss the chance to experience the warmth and hospitality of the Pelion locals while you are here.
