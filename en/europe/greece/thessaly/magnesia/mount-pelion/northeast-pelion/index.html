Introduction to Northeast Pelion

Northeast Pelion is a charming and scenic region located in Mount Pelion, in Magnesia, in Thessaly, in Greece, in Europe. This region’s natural beauty ranges from lush forests to stunning beaches, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and anyone looking for a peaceful and relaxing getaway.

In this comprehensive tour guide to Northeast Pelion, we will help you discover the best places to visit, the top things to do and see, where to stay, what to buy, and much more. So let’s dive into the heart of Northeast Pelion!

List of Places to Visit in Northeast Pelion

Northeast Pelion is a region of rich cultural and natural diversity. We have compiled a list of the best places to visit, including museums, historical sites, and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Folk Art and History of Pelion: This museum showcases the rich history and traditions of the region through various exhibits and artifacts.

  • The Monastery of St. Nicolas of Pau: This monastery, located on the slopes of Pelion, is renowned for its stunning architecture and beautiful frescoes.

  • The Castle of Agios Georgios Nilias: This Byzantine castle dates back to the 13th century and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Northeast Pelion: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Damouhari Beach: This secluded and stunning beach is the perfect place to soak up some sun and swim in the crystal clear waters.

  • Horto Beach: This long and wide beach offers a beautiful view of the Pagasitic Gulf and is surrounded by lush greenery.

  • Tsagarada: This picturesque village is known for its beautiful churches, traditional architecture, and stunning natural surroundings, including the stunning waterfall of Damouchari.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast Pelion

Northeast Pelion is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The region offers a wide range of hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of experience, from easy family walks to challenging mountain hikes.

  • The Centaurs’ Path: This 11 km trail takes hikers through beautiful forests, streams, and waterfalls, and offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea.

  • The Old Stone Trail: This relatively easy trail takes hikers through the charming traditional village of Tsagarada and offers excellent views of the Pagasitic Gulf.

  • The Pelion Railway Trail: This trail follows the route of the historic Pelion railway line and offers stunning views of the surrounding nature and traditional villages.

Best Things to Do in Northeast Pelion: Activities and Tours

Northeast Pelion offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Northeast Pelion:

  • Horseback riding: Experience Northeast Pelion’s natural beauty on horseback by taking a guided horseback tour.

  • Sailing and Watersports: Take advantage of the crystal clear waters of the Aegean Sea by participating in a variety of water sports, including sailing, windsurfing, and kayaking.

  • Wine Tasting: Explore the traditional vineyards and winemaking traditions of Northeast Pelion by taking a guided wine tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast Pelion

Northeast Pelion offers a rich culinary tradition rooted in local ingredients and traditional recipes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spoon Sweets: These are traditional sweet preserves made from various fruits, including quince, rose petals, and figs.

  • Burrito: A traditional dish made from chestnut flour and served with honey and walnuts.

  • Kolokythopita: A savory pie made from fresh zucchini, onions, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: A traditional Greek spirit made from grape skins and seeds, often enjoyed as an aperitif or digestif.

  • Amaretto: This traditional almond liqueur is perfect for a post-meal drink.

  • Raki: A traditional alcoholic drink made from grape pomace, and often flavored with various fruits and herbs.

Where to Stay in Northeast Pelion: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northeast Pelion offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels.

  • 12 Months Luxury Resort: This luxury resort is located in Tsagarada and offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea and traditional architecture.

  • Vivere Ad Plenitudinem Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in the traditional village of Milies and offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

  • Petradi Hotel: This award-winning hotel is situated in the charming village of Portaria and offers luxurious rooms and suites, a restaurant serving traditional cuisine and stunning surroundings.

What to Buy in Northeast Pelion: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northeast Pelion offers a variety of local products and crafts that make perfect souvenirs to bring home. Some of the best items to buy include:

  • Local Honey: Northeast Pelion’s honey is renowned for its high quality and unique flavor, perfect for a taste of the region.

  • Handmade Ceramics: The region’s abundant clay deposits have made it a hub for traditional pottery and ceramics.

  • Traditional Weavings: The region’s weaving traditions date back centuries and offer a wide range of traditional products such as rugs, tablecloths, and blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast Pelion

The local culture and customs of Northeast Pelion are rich and diverse. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting the region:

  • Hospitality: The locals of Northeast Pelion are known for their warm and welcoming nature, so be sure to engage with them.

  • Traditional Festivals and Events: The region’s traditional festivals and events are an excellent way to experience the local culture and traditions.

  • Respect for the Environment: The natural beauty of Northeast Pelion is its most precious asset, so be sure to respect the environment while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Northeast Pelion

The best time to visit Northeast Pelion is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April-June) and fall (September-October). The weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner, allowing visitors to experience the region’s natural beauty without the crowds. However, if you prefer warmer weather, summer (July-August) is also a great time to visit.

Do:

  • Respect the environment: Northeast Pelion is a natural wonder, so be sure to respect the environment and leave no trace.

  • Engage with the locals: The locals of Northeast Pelion are known for their warm and welcoming nature, so don’t be afraid to engage with them.

  • Try the traditional cuisine and beverages: Northeast Pelion’s traditional cuisine and beverages are an important part of the region’s culture, so try them while you’re there.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northeast Pelion

Northeast Pelion is generally a safe destination to visit, with a low crime rate and a welcoming local population. However, visitors should still take basic precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Northeast Pelion

Northeast Pelion doesn’t pose any significant health risks for visitors. Tap water is generally safe to drink, but it’s always advisable to drink bottled water. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of sunburn and heatstroke during the hot summer months.

Getting to Northeast Pelion: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northeast Pelion

The nearest international airport to Northeast Pelion is the Thessaloniki International Airport, located approximately 215 km away from the region. Visitors can also fly to Athens International Airport, which is approximately 315 km away.

Bus Stations Near Northeast Pelion

The region’s major towns and villages are well-connected by bus to the rest of Greece, with regular services operated by KTEL. The main bus station in the region is located in Volos.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Northeast Pelion by train, with services provided by the Greek Railways. For those driving, the region is accessible by car via the national road network.

How to Get Around in Northeast Pelion

The best way to get around Northeast Pelion is by car or by taxi. Car rental services are available in all major towns and villages, while taxis can be found throughout the region.

Where to Go Next from Northeast Pelion: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northeast Pelion is located in a region of Greece rich in natural beauty and cultural heritage. Some nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring include:

  • The Meteora Monasteries: This UNESCO world heritage site is home to stunning rock formations and ancient monasteries.

  • The city of Volos: This bustling port city is known for its vibrant street markets, museums, and restaurants.

  • The Skiathos Island: This beautiful island is renowned for its stunning beaches and crystal clear waters.

Conclusion

Northeast Pelion is a hidden gem of Greece, offering visitors a mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and great cuisine.
