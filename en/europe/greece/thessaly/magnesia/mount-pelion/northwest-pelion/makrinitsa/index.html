Introduction to Makrinitsa

Located in the northwest of Mount Pelion in Greece, Makrinitsa is a small village in Magnesia that has become a popular tourist destination due to its stunning views of Pagasitikos Gulf, exquisite mansions, and charming cobbled streets. The village has managed to retain its authenticity and traditional character, making it a perfect destination for travelers looking for a relaxing and authentic Greek experience.

List of Places to Visit in Makrinitsa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Folk Art and History of Pelion: Located in a restored mansion, the museum houses a collection of folk art and traditional objects that depict the rich cultural heritage of Pelion.
  • Theotokos Church: A picturesque church located on the highest point of the village offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Agios Ioannis Prodromos Monastery: A beautiful monastery with a stunning view, located a few kilometers away from Makrinitsa in Agios Ioannis village.

Natural Attractions of Makrinitsa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tsagarada: A small village located a few kilometers away from Makrinitsa, which is known for its natural beauty, beaches, and hiking trails.
  • Hiking trails: Makrinitsa is situated in the heart of Mount Pelion, providing access to picturesque and well-marked hiking trails with spectacular views along the way.
  • Ski center: Makrinitsa is also home to the Agriolefkes Ski Center, which offers an excellent opportunity for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports activities during the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Makrinitsa

There are many hiking and biking routes available from Makrinitsa, suitable for different fitness levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Kissos-Makrinitsa Trail: A 10 km route that follows the ancient cobbled path that connects Kissos and Makrinitsa villages.
  • Makrinitsa-Vizitsa Trail: This route connects Makrinitsa and Vizitsa villages and offers fantastic views of the Pagasitikos Gulf.
  • Makrinitsa-Tsagarada Trail: A picturesque 15 km trail that starts from Makrinitsa and ends in Tsagarada with stunning views of both villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Makrinitsa: Activities and Tours

  • Enjoy the panoramic views: Makrinitsa is famous for its fantastic views of the surrounding landscape, making it an excellent place to enjoy the sunset or sunrise.
  • Visit the local market: Every Wednesday, a local market takes place in Makrinitsa, selling traditional products such as honey, herbs, and locally produced cheese.
  • Take a cooking class: Learn traditional Greek cooking techniques and recipes through a cooking class offered by one of the local hospitality venues.

Food and Drink Scene of Makrinitsa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spetzofai: A traditional dish made with green and red peppers, tomatoes, onions, and garlic, slow-cooked in olive oil with local sausages or pork.
  • Fava: A creamy yellow split pea dip, traditionally served as an appetizer with bread or pita.
  • Pita Margarites: A specialty pastry made with local cheese, eggs, and flour.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: A distilled spirit made from the pomace of grapes, similar to Greek’s traditional ouzo, but with a higher percentage of alcohol.
  • Pelion Mountain Tea: A herbal tea infused with wild herbs and flowers typical of Mount Pelion’s landscape

Where to Stay in Makrinitsa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Makrinitsa offers a good range of accommodation options, with many hotels, hostels, and guesthouses available to cater to different preferences and budget ranges. Some examples of popular options include:

  • Archontiko Repana: A beautifully restored mansion offering luxurious rooms and suites with traditional decor and stunning views.
  • Krisaki Traditional Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with well-appointed comfortable rooms, situated at the center of the village.
  • Alkistis Hotel: A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms with great views of the Pagasitikos Gulf and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Makrinitsa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey: Pelion is known for its excellent quality honey, known for its medicinal properties and rich flavor.
  • Herbs: The region is rich in herbs, and local markets offer a wide variety of dried herbs, including mountain tea, chamomile, and oregano.
  • Handmade pottery: Visit the local shops, offering handmade pottery items of different styles and colors, used in the local culture and cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Makrinitsa

Makrinitsa has preserved its authentic appearance and traditional lifestyle, making it an excellent destination to experience local customs and culture. Customs and traditions include:

  • Traditional architecture: The village is home to beautiful restored mansions, with local houses featuring traditional Pelion architecture with slate roofs and wooden balconies.
  • Greek Orthodoxy: The village is home to numerous churches and shrines, and religion plays an important role in the local community.
  • Festival of Panagia: The festival of Panagia, the largest religious event, takes place in Makrinitsa on August 15th, attracting pilgrims from all over Greece.

Best Time to Visit Makrinitsa

The optimal period to visit Makrinitsa is during the spring and autumn months, from April to June and September to November. During this time, the weather is mild, and the visitor’s crowd is low, allowing for a more authentic experience of the village.

Do’s and Don’ts of Makrinitsa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the village.
  • Try the local food and drink offerings, especially the Pelion Mountain Tea.
  • Try to speak some Greek, which will help you better communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Damage or remove any historical or cultural items.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Makrinitsa

Makrinitsa is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates. However, be careful when hiking or skiing, as mountain activities come with a higher degree of risk.

Health in Makrinitsa

There is a medical center in Makrinitsa for minor health issues. In case of an emergency, the nearest hospital is located in Volos city.

Getting to Makrinitsa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Makrinitsa

The nearest airport is Nea Anchialos airport, located approximately 50 km away from Makrinitsa.

Bus Stations Near Makrinitsa

Intercity buses traveling from Athens and Thessaloniki to Volos city offer frequent connections. From Volos, local buses or taxis can reach Makrinitsa.

Other Options

It is possible to rent a car in Athens or Thessaloniki or hire a taxi.

How to Get Around in Makrinitsa

Makrinitsa is a small village which can be easily explored on foot, appreciated for its traditional, picturesque character and the limited road access.

Where to Go Next from Makrinitsa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pelion Mountain Villages

Other mountain villages nearby, including Tsagarada, Pinakates, Vizitsa, and Milies, offer a similar traditional character and lifestyle that is typical throughout the region of Pelion.

Pelion Beaches

There are several beautiful beaches located along the eastern and western coasts of Pelion, accessible by rented car or local buses.

Conclusion

Makrinitsa is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a relaxing, authentic, and picturesque Greek experience. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and traditional architecture, it offers something to please everyone.
