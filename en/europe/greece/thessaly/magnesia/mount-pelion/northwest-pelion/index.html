Introduction to Northwest Pelion

Located in the Thessaly region of Greece, Mount Pelion is known for its stunning natural scenery and cultural heritage. Northwest Pelion is particularly famous for its pristine beaches, lush forests, and traditional villages that attract visitors from all around the world. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, water sports, or just relaxing on a beautiful beach, Northwest Pelion has plenty to offer. In this guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions of this beautiful region and help you plan an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest Pelion

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northwest Pelion is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich heritage. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • The Folklore Museum of Zagora: This museum showcases the traditional life and folk art of the Zagora village.
  • The Milies Folklore Museum: This museum features a collection of traditional costumes, household items, and tools used in daily life in the Milies village.
  • The Old Station of Milies: This historic railway station was built in the late 19th century and is now a museum that tells the story of the Pelion railway.

Natural Attractions of Northwest Pelion: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Northwest Pelion is unparalleled, with stunning coastal landscapes, towering mountains, and dense forests. From challenging hikes to leisurely scenic drives, here are some of the top outdoor attractions:

  • Agios Ioannis Beach: This gorgeous beach features crystal-clear water and stunning views of the Aegean Sea. It’s a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.
  • Hiking Trails: Northwest Pelion is home to numerous hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the landscape. The most popular hiking routes include the Tsagarada to Mylopotamos and the Centaur’s Path.
  • Skiing: During the winter months, the Agriolefkes Ski Resort is one of the most popular attractions in Northwest Pelion. The resort offers skiing and snowboarding lessons for all skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest Pelion

Northwest Pelion is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with trails and routes that cater to all skill levels. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or just starting out, here are some of the top hiking and biking routes:

  • Tsagarada to Mylopotamos: This 6 km hike takes you through dense forests, over wooden bridges, and past crystal-clear streams. The trail ends at the beautiful Mylopotamos Beach.
  • Centaur’s Path: This 8 km trail follows the footsteps of the legendary centaurs and offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea.
  • Cycling Routes: There are several cycling routes in Northwest Pelion, ranging from easy tracks to challenging mountain trails. Some of the most popular routes include the Zagora to Chorefto, and the Agios Georgios Nilias to Vizitsa.

Best Things to Do in Northwest Pelion: Activities and Tours

Northwest Pelion offers plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Water Sports: Agios Ioannis Beach is the perfect spot for windsurfing and kitesurfing, while Plaka Beach is ideal for paddleboarding and kayaking.
  • Boat Tours: Explore the beautiful coastline of Northwest Pelion with a boat tour that takes you to secluded coves and hidden beaches.
  • Horse Riding: Take a horseback ride through the lush forests and trails of Pelion for a unique and peaceful experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northwest Pelion

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Northwest Pelion, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Volos and explore the city’s shopping and dining scene.

Day 2: Visit the Milies village and take a scenic train ride on the historic Pelion railway.

Day 3: Spend the day at Agios Ioannis Beach, where you can enjoy water sports and soak up the sun.

Day 4: Explore the traditional village of Zagora and visit the Folklore Museum.

Day 5: Take a hiking or cycling tour and experience the natural beauty of Pelion.

Day 6: Enjoy a boat tour of the stunning coastline and visit hidden beaches and coves.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the region’s luxury spas, or take a horseback ride through the lush forests.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest Pelion

Must-Try Dishes

Northwest Pelion is known for its delicious local cuisine, which features fresh seafood, herbs, and vegetables. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Spetzofai: This spicy sausage and tomato dish is a staple of the Pelion cuisine.
  • Sardeles Pastes: These small fried fish are served as an appetizer or snack and are perfect with a cold beer.
  • Hortopita: This traditional pie is made with a mixture of greens, feta cheese, and savory pastry.

Local Beverages

In addition to the delicious food, Northwest Pelion also offers a variety of locally-produced beverages that are well worth trying:

  • Tsipouro: A traditional Greek spirit made from distilled grapes or pomace.
  • Raki: Similar to Tsipouro, Raki is a strong spirit made from distilled aniseed.
  • Sideritis: A traditional herbal tea that is known for its healing properties.

Where to Stay in Northwest Pelion: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious beach resort or a rustic mountain cabin, Northwest Pelion has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the region:

  • Hotel Esklarintha: This charming hotel is located in the Milies village and features beautiful views of the mountains and forests of Pelion.
  • Archontiko Stafylopati: This traditional guesthouse is located in the Zagora village and offers comfortable rooms and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Hotel Agriolefkes: This luxurious ski resort is located near the Agriolefkes Ski Center and features modern rooms, a spa, and stunning mountain views.

What to Buy in Northwest Pelion: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Northwest Pelion, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some of the best items to take home:

  • Handmade ceramics and pottery from one of the local artisan workshops.
  • Local honey, which is known for its unique flavor and medicinal properties.
  • Traditional textiles and embroidery, which are still made by hand in many of the region’s villages.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest Pelion

The people of Northwest Pelion are known for their warm hospitality and traditional way of life. When visiting the region, it’s important to remember to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some of the top customs to keep in mind:

  • Remove your shoes when entering a home or sacred place.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches or temples.
  • Greet locals with a friendly “Yassou” or “Kalimera”.

Best Time to Visit Northwest Pelion

The best time to visit Northwest Pelion depends on what you’re looking to do and see. The summer months (June-August) are ideal for beach activities and water sports, while the winter months (December-February) are perfect for skiing and snowboarding. The spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are ideal for hiking and cycling, as the weather is mild and the scenery is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest Pelion

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Enjoy the beautiful natural scenery and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwest Pelion

Northwest Pelion is generally very safe, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s still important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when out at night. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Lock your valuables in your hotel safe.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas.
  • Use caution when driving on mountain roads, especially during inclement weather.

Health in Northwest Pelion

Travelers to Northwest Pelion are recommended to be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), tetanus, and influenza. It’s also advisable to pack a first-aid kit and any necessary medication.

Getting to Northwest Pelion: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northwest Pelion

There are several airports in the region that serve Northwest Pelion, including:

  • Nea Anchialos National Airport (VOL): This airport is located just 30 minutes from Volos and offers flights to several European destinations.
  • Skiathos Island National Airport (JSI): This airport is located on the nearby Skiathos Island and offers flights to Athens and other Greek destinations.

Bus Stations Near Northwest Pelion

There are several bus stations in the region that offer connections to various destinations in Greece, including:

  • Volos Intercity Bus Station: This station is located in the heart of Volos and offers connections to Athens, Thessaloniki, and other major cities in Greece.

Other Options

In addition to flying and taking the bus, other transportation options in Northwest Pelion include renting a car or taking a taxi. It’s important to note that many of the mountain roads in the region can be narrow and winding, so it’s important to exercise caution and drive defensively.

How to Get Around in Northwest Pelion

Getting around Northwest Pelion is fairly easy, with several transportation options available. Here are some of the best ways to get around:

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the region on your own schedule.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in most towns and villages, and can be a convenient option for short trips.
  • Public Transportation: Buses connect most of the towns and villages in Northwest Pelion, and are an affordable option for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Northwest Pelion: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northwest Pelion is surrounded by several other beautiful regions and destinations that are well worth visiting. Here are some of the top nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Meteora: This UNESCO World Heritage Site features stunning monasteries perched atop towering rock formations.
  • Athens: The capital city of Greece is just a few hours’ drive from Northwest Pelion and offers a wealth of history, culture, and nightlife.
  • Mount Olympus: The mythical home of the ancient Greek gods, Mount Olympus offers world-class hiking and picturesque landscapes.

Conclusion

Northwest Pelion is a breathtakingly beautiful region of Greece that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, water sports, or just relaxing on beautiful beaches, Northwest Pelion has plenty to offer. We hope this guide has given you some inspiration and ideas for planning your dream vacation to this stunning region.
