If you’re looking for a charming destination in Greece that’s off-the-beaten-path, Nea Anchialos might be the perfect choice. This coastal town is located in Magnesia, in Thessaly, and offers a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and picturesque scenery. In this travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and local customs and culture, to help you plan your trip to Nea Anchialos.

Introduction to Nea Anchialos

Nea Anchialos is a serene coastal town located in central Greece. It’s well-known for its stunning views of the Pagasetic Gulf and the beautiful Mount Pelion. This town is also steeped in fascinating history, which can be explored through its various museums and ancient ruins. Additionally, Nea Anchialos has a vibrant local culture and customs that can be experienced through its delicious cuisine, lively festivals, and traditional dances.

List of Places to Visit in Nea Anchialos

There are numerous places to visit in Nea Anchialos that will help you discover the town’s stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ancient City of Anchialos: Explore the fascinating ruins of the city’s ancient Acropolis and its surrounding walls.
  • The Museum of Anchialos: Learn more about the town’s history and culture at this museum, which includes important artifacts from the ancient Anchialos.
  • Monastery of Panagia Xenia: Discover this beautiful Byzantine monastery that dates back to the 17th century and boasts stunning architectural details.

Natural Attractions of Nea Anchialos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Pelion: Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore this beautiful mountain range, which offers scenic views of the surrounding area and a chance to spot endemic flora and fauna.
  • Pagasetic Gulf: Take a walk along the town’s waterfront and enjoy stunning views of the gulf, with its crystal-clear waters and picturesque harbors.
  • Nisia Wildlife Reserve: Embark on an unforgettable bird-watching adventure at this protected reserve that is home to a diverse range of bird species, including the elusive Dalmatian Pelican.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nea Anchialos

Nea Anchialos is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails and peaks to explore. Here are some of our recommended routes:

  • Mount Othrys: This challenging hike is well worth the effort, as it offers panoramic views of the Pagasetic Gulf and the surrounding mountains.
  • Pelion’s Eastern Trail: This scenic trail starts at the coastal town of Kala Nera and takes hikers through olive groves, dense forests, and picturesque villages, before reaching the peak of Mt. Pelion.
  • Katochori-Lefokastro Trail: This tranquil trail begins at the mountain village of Katochori and takes hikers through picturesque landscapes, including traditional vineyards, olive groves, and natural springs.

Best Things to Do in Nea Anchialos: Activities and Tours

Here are some exciting activities and tours that should definitely be on your to-do list when visiting Nea Anchialos:

  • Food Tour: Take a gastronomic journey and taste authentic local cuisine and traditional dishes.
  • Dance to Traditional Music: Attend a local festival or event to experience traditional live music and dance performances.
  • Horse Riding: Explore the stunning scenery of Nea Anchialos on horseback, which is an exciting and unique way to experience the outdoors.

Food and Drink Scene of Nea Anchialos

No trip to Greece is complete without sampling the delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks to enjoy in Nea Anchialos:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiganopsomo: a warm and crispy bread that’s traditionally served with fresh tomatoes and cheese.
  • Gourounopoulo me selino: slow-roasted pork with celery and herbs, served with tzatziki.
  • Loukoumades: golden fried dough balls, drizzled with honey and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Tsipouro: a strong distilled spirit often served as an aperitif.
  • Ouzo: this anise-flavored liquor is typically enjoyed as a digestive.
  • Retsina: a pine resin-flavored wine that’s perfect for savoring during leisurely meals.

Where to Stay in Nea Anchialos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a wide range of accommodation options in Nea Anchialos. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Orologopoulos Mansion: This stunning mansion hotel is set in a beautifully renovated 19th-century building and offers an unparalleled experience of Greek luxury.
  • Seaside Studios: These modern and comfortable studios are located in a peaceful, scenic area, and offer direct access to the gulf for perfect sunset views.
  • Labela Guesthouse: This charming and cozy guesthouse is situated in the heart of the town, and is ideal for those who want to experience the traditional Greek hospitality and culture.

What to Buy in Nea Anchialos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nea Anchialos is famous for its delicious cuisine, unique handmade crafts, and traditional textiles. Here are some of our top picks for souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Handmade Belts: local artisans create beautiful handcrafted belts, traditionally made from authentic leather.
  • Honey: this sweet and delicious honey is locally produced and makes an excellent gift for friends and family.
  • Traditional Embroidery: take home a piece of Nea Anchialos’ heritage, with high-quality, handmade embroidery that is unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Nea Anchialos

Nea Anchialos is steeped in fascinating local culture and customs that can be experienced through the town’s festivals, dances, and traditional music. However, it’s important to be mindful and respectful of these customs when visiting. Here are some important local customs to bear in mind:

  • Hospitality: Greeks are renowned for their hospitality and warmth. Expect to be welcomed and treated like family.
  • Dress Code: Nea Anchialos is a conservative town, so it’s important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  • Use of Language: Most locals speak English, but it’s always appreciated when visitors try to speak the local language, which is Greek.

Best Time to Visit Nea Anchialos

The best time to visit Nea Anchialos is during the spring and summer months, from March to September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, which makes it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the town’s stunning scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nea Anchialos

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the Food: Greek cuisine is one of the best in the world and Nea Anchialos is no exception. Make sure to try as many traditional dishes as you can.
  • Respect the Culture: Always be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Nea Anchialos has a stunning natural beauty that’s best experienced by foot or bike. Make sure to find time for outdoor activities in your itinerary.

Don’t:

  • Overpack: Nea Anchialos is a laid-back town, so leave your formal attire at home and pack light.
  • Overlook the Culture: Nea Anchialos is steeped in fascinating history and culture, so make sure to explore it thoroughly.
  • Forget to Tip: Tipping is customary in Greece, so remember to tip waiters, hotel staff, and taxi drivers.

Safety and Health

Nea Anchialos is a safe and welcoming town. However, it’s always important to be mindful of your surroundings and take necessary precautions. Here’s some essential information on safety and health:

Safety in Nea Anchialos

  • Pickpocketing: Keep your valuable items close to you and avoid flaunting them in public areas.
  • Swimming Safety: Take extra care when swimming or boating in the gulf.
  • Emergency Numbers: Call 112 for all emergency services.

Health in Nea Anchialos

  • Vaccinations: There are no mandatory vaccinations required for Greece. However, it’s always best to speak with your doctor before traveling.
  • Water: Tap water in Nea Anchialos is safe to drink, but bottled water is readily available for those who prefer it.
  • Medical Services: Medical services in Nea Anchialos are adequate, and there are several pharmacies and hospitals available.

Getting to Nea Anchialos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nea Anchialos is located 27 kilometers southeast of the city of Volos, making it easily accessible via various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Nea Anchialos

  • Nea Anchialos National Airport: This airport is located just 4 kilometers from Nea Anchialos and is serviced by several domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Nea Anchialos

  • Volos Intercity Bus Station: Buses depart daily from this station and make the 30-minute journey to Nea Anchialos.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Consider renting a car to explore the surrounding area at your leisure.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available and can take you directly to your destination in Nea Anchialos.

How To Get Around in Nea Anchialos

Nea Anchialos is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However for those who prefer to use other modes of transportation, here are some options:

  • Bus: Local buses are available and can take you to nearby destinations, including Volos, for a small fee.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available and can be hired for longer distances, such as to Mount Pelion.
  • Bike: Rent a bike from one of the local shops and explore the beautiful town and its surroundings at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Nea Anchialos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nea Anchialos is in close proximity to several other interesting destinations in Greece. Here are some recommended places to visit:

  • Volos: This bustling city is just 30 minutes away by bus and is home to an excellent archaeological museum, as well as beautiful beaches and a lively dining scene.
  • Metropolitan St. Ignatius Monastery: This Byzantine monastery, located near the town of Neochori, is renowned for its stunning frescoes and mosaics.
  • Skiathos Island: Take a ferry from Volos and explore the stunning beaches and landscapes of this beautiful island.

Conclusion

Nea Anchialos is a picturesque Greek town that’s perfect for those looking to explore the country’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. With numerous attractions, activities, and local cuisine to enjoy, Nea Anchialos is a destination that should be on everyone’s travel list. Plan your trip today, and experience all that this charming town has to offer!
