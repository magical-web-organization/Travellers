Introduction to Greece

Greece is one of the most historically and culturally rich countries in the world, blessed with a plethora of natural beauty, friendly locals, and delicious food. It is located in Southern Europe, at the crossroads between Asia, Africa, and Europe. Greece is home to over 2000 islands, ancient ruins, and stunning beaches, making it one of the most popular tourist destinations around the globe.

In this comprehensive guide, we will cover all the essential information for you to plan an amazing trip to Greece.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greece

Day 1: Arrival in Athens, explore the city’s ancient historical sites.

Day 2-3: Head to Naxos and experience the charming town’s local culture.

Day 4-5: Then proceed to Mykonos, soak in the nightlife and stunning beaches.

Day 6-7: Finally, visit Santorini, witness the beautiful sunset and unique geography.

Best Time to Visit Greece

The best time to visit Greece is from April to June and from September to November. The weather is mild and there are fewer crowds, making it easier to explore the attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greece

Do:

  1. Visit the ancient archaeological sites.
  2. Try the Greek cuisine.
  3. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  4. Learn some Greek phrases.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the Greek flag and symbols.
  2. Visit religious sites while wearing inappropriate clothing.
  3. Photograph locals without permission.
  4. Cause harm to marine life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greece

Greece is a safe place to visit and crime rates are relatively low. However, petty theft may occur in crowded areas, so it’s essential to keep an eye on your belongings and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Greece

Greece has an excellent healthcare system, and medical facilities are easily accessible. There are no mandatory vaccinations required for visitors to Greece, but it’s always good to consult your doctor before traveling.

How to Get Around in Greece

The best way to explore Greece is by renting a car. The country has a well-established road network and scenic drives are a must-do. The public transportation system is also a reliable option and includes buses and trains.
