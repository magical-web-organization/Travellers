Introduction to Bavarian Alps

Nestled in the majestic Alps mountain range, Bavarian Alps (aka Bavarian Alps) is a breathtaking destination in Germany, known for its picture-perfect landscapes, charming villages, and rich cultural heritage. The area is renowned for its outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, skiing, and snowboarding, making it a mecca for adventure enthusiasts all year round.

With its endless awe-inspiring scenery, Bavarian Alps attracts visitors from all over the world, making it one of the most popular regions in Europe. In this Bavarian Alps Tour Guide, we will provide an in-depth look at the major attractions and provide a comprehensive plan to help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Bavarian Alps

Bavarian Alps is home to an abundance of tourist attractions and iconic landmarks, Here is a list of must-visit places in Bavarian Alps.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Neuschwanstein Castle: A fairytale castle that inspired Cinderella’s castle in Disneyland.
  • Linderhof Palace: A favorite of King Ludwig II, the palace is a breathtaking example of Baroque and Rococo architectural styles.
  • Wieskirche: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with a rich history and unique architecture.
  • Munich Residenz: One of the largest palaces in Europe, dating back to the Middle Ages.
  • Obersalzberg Documentation Centre: A museum honoring the victims of the Nazi regime.

Natural Attractions of Bavarian Alps: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zugspitze: The highest peak in Germany, with stunning views of the surrounding Alps.
  • Eibsee: A crystal-clear lake nestled at the foot of Zugspitze, perfect for swimming and hiking.
  • Partnach Gorge: A scenic place for hiking and sightseeing, particularly during the summer months.
  • Hoher Kranzberg: A natural lookout point for panoramic views of the town of Mittenwald.
  • Herrenchiemsee Palace: A magical palace on an island in Lake Chiemsee.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bavarian Alps

One of the best ways to experience the beauty of Bavarian Alps is by going on a hike. There are countless hiking and biking trails of varied difficulty levels to choose from. Here are some of the best routes for hikers and cyclists:

  • Watzmann Hocheck: A moderately difficult hike for experienced hikers, offering stunning views of the Watzmann Summit, the second-highest peak in Germany.
  • Kurpark: A scenic trail through this picturesque park in the heart of Garmisch-Partenkirchen.
  • Krün Circuit: A gentle hiking trail perfect for families and beginners.
  • Rauschberg Circuit: A challenging trail with an altitude difference of 680 meters, offering a stunning view of Lake Chiemsee.
  • Zugspitze Runde: A stunning trail with breathtaking views for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Bavarian Alps: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural attractions, Bavarian Alps offers a range of adventurous activities and tours to indulge in. Here are some of the best things to do in Bavarian Alps:

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: The perfect destination for winter sports enthusiasts.
  • White Water Rafting: A thrilling water activity through the Isar River in the heart of Bavarian Alps.
  • Visit the Christmas Market in Munich: Romantic and magical with a traditional Bavarian atmosphere, the market offers a unique Christmas experience to remember.
  • Museum Visits: Discover history and culture at the interesting museums available in Bavarian Alps.
  • Birds of Prey Show: A fascinating show featuring various bird species including eagles, owls, and falcons in the wild

Week-Long Itinerary for Bavarian Alps

If you are planning a week-long trip to Bavarian Alps, then this itinerary will give you a taste of the region’s diverse tourist attractions.

Day 1-2: Explore Munich and its culturally rich heritage. Day 3-4: Discover King Ludwig’s splendid castles. Day 5-6: Visit beautiful lakes, go hiking, and explore the natural beauty of the region. Day 7: Relax in one of the scenic spa resorts in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Bavarian Alps

No trip can be complete without trying the local cuisine of Bavarian Alps. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Weißwurst: A traditional Bavarian sausage usually enjoyed as a breakfast meal.
  • Brezen: A type of pretzel that is popularly consumed with beer.
  • Schweinshaxe: A roasted pork shank that is popular among locals and visitors alike.
  • Apfelstrudel: A sweet pastry dish, commonly served with vanilla sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Weissbier: A type of German wheat beer, typically served in a tall, narrow glass.
  • Riesling wine: A dry white wine that pairs well with most dishes.
  • Glühwein: A popular hot spiced wine traditionally served during the Christmas season.

Where to Stay in Bavarian Alps: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bavarian Alps offers an array of options for accommodation to suit every type of traveler, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay while in Bavarian Alps:

  • Garmisch-Partenkirchen: A charming mountain resort town catering to families and winter sports enthusiasts alike.
  • Berchtesgaden: A hilltop town famous for its scenic views and rich history.
  • Mittenwald: A small, picturesque town offering a cosy atmosphere and some vibrant local culture.

What to Buy in Bavarian Alps: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bavarian Alps is famous for its locally made souvenirs and products, many of which make unique gifts for loved ones. Here are some of the best souvenirs to take back home:

  • Tracht: Traditional Bavarian clothing such as lederhosen and dirndls.
  • Beer Steins: Unique and beautifully crafted mugs that come in various sizes and designs.
  • Kocherlball Souvenir: A souvenir from the dynamic early-morning dancing event held annually in Munich.
  • German Salami: A local delicacy offering bold flavors and unique aromas.

Local Culture and Customs of Bavarian Alps

Bavarian Alps has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take pride in their traditions and customs. Here are some of the things to keep in mind when visiting the region:

  • Bavarian Alps has a conservative and traditional culture, so ensure to dress modestly in public places.
  • Beer is a big part of the region’s culture, and Munich has several written rules and regulations related to the brewing law that governs beer-making.
  • Bavaria has a distinct dialect of German, which may not be easily understandable by those who speak Standard German. Locals are typically welcoming to tourists, though.

Best Time to Visit Bavarian Alps

The best time to visit Bavarian Alps depends on the preferred activity and the season. Here’s an overview of the best times to visit:

  • Summer (June-August): Great for hiking, cycling, and other outdoor activities.
  • Autumn (September-November): A great time to enjoy the region’s scenic beauty during the mild weather.
  • Winter (December-February): Perfect for winter sports lovers, as Bavarian Alps experience high levels of snowfall during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bavarian Alps

As a tourist in Bavarian Alps, there are certain things you should and shouldn’t do. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, especially German beer.
  • Respect local customs, traditions and dress appropriately.
  • Use public transportation or opt for eco-friendly travel options.

Don’t:

  • Pack valuables when going hiking or engage in any outdoor activities.
  • Litter or disrespect the environment.
  • Touch public statues or artwork.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are crucial considerations when traveling to Bavarian Alps. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Bavarian Alps

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and cautious of pickpocketing.
  • Take proper precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, especially during winter sports season.
  • Use safety equipment, such as helmets during cycling or skiing activities.

Health in Bavarian Alps

  • Ensure to keep hydrated, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.
  • Use sunscreen to avoid sunburn.
  • Consult a medical professional for any allergies or health concerns before traveling.

Getting to Bavarian Alps: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are numerous options for getting to Bavarian Alps, depending on your location and intended method of travel. Here are some of the ways to reach the region:

Airports Near Bavarian Alps

  • Munich International airport: The nearest international airport approximately 1 hour drive from Bavarian Alps.
  • Salzburg International airport: Approximately 1.5 to 2 hours from most tourist destinations in Bavarian Alps.

Bus Stations Near Bavarian Alps

  • RegioJet: Operates bus transportation from Prague to Garmisch-Partenkirchen.
  • Flixbus: Provides buses from different cities in Germany to various tourist destinations.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: The most convenient option to get around and explore the area at your pace.
  • Train: Operated by Deutsche Bahn, the national rail operator in Germany

How to Get Around in Bavarian Alps

Bavarian Alps has an extensive public transportation system that covers most tourist destinations. Here are some of the best ways to travel around the region:

  • Buses: A reliable and affordable way to travel around the local area.
  • Trains: An efficient way to travel between the main cities and towns.
  • Rental Cars: A convenient option to explore the area at your pace.

Where to Go Next from Bavarian Alps: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have got more time after exploring Bavarian Alps region, here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions you should consider:

  • Innsbruck: The capital of the Austrian Tyrol region, Innsbruck a charming city with a strong cultural scene.
  • Zell am See: A picturesque lakeside town renowned for its water sports and winter activities.
