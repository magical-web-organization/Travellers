Petroșani is a city in Hunedoara County, Transylvania, Romania. Known for its beautiful natural surroundings and rich cultural heritage, it is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. In this ultimate travel guide, we will take you on a journey through Petroșani, exploring its best attractions, cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine.

Introduction to Petroșani

Petroșani is a small city nestled in the heart of the Jiu Valley in Transylvania. Surrounded by the Retezat and Parâng mountains, it is a paradise for nature lovers seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views. The city itself has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several museums and historical landmarks. Petroșani’s location also makes it a perfect base for exploring the nearby attractions of Transylvania, such as the cities of Cluj-Napoca and Sibiu.

List of Places to Visit in Petroșani

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Miner’s Park - A unique open-air museum that showcases the history of coal mining in the region. Visitors can explore an authentic coal mine, ride a miners’ train, and learn about the daily life of miners.

  • The Museum of Petroșani - A museum dedicated to the city’s history and culture. Here, visitors can find exhibits on local traditions, industries, and famous personalities.

  • The Gothic-Style Orthodox Church - A beautiful church built in the 19th century that features stunning stained-glass windows and intricate wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Petroșani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Retezat Mountains - One of the most beautiful mountain ranges in Romania, the Retezat Mountains offer endless opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, skiing, and rock climbing.

  • The Parâng Mountains - Another popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts, the Parâng Mountains offer excellent hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding valleys.

  • The Jiu River Canyon - A breathtaking natural wonder that has been declared a protected area. Visitors can enjoy a scenic drive or hike along the canyon and admire the impressive rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petroșani

Petroșani is surrounded by the beautiful mountains of Retezat and Parâng, making it a perfect spot for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • The Transalpina Road - A scenic route that crosses the Parâng Mountains and is considered one of the best driving routes in the world.

  • Retezat National Park - A nature reserve with over 80 glacial lakes and several hiking trails for all levels of experience.

Best Things to Do in Petroșani: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do in Petroșani:

  • Take a guided tour of the coal mine - Explore the history of coal mining in the region and learn about the daily life of miners.

  • Visit the nearby Transfăgărășan Road - A spectacular driving route that crosses the Făgăraș Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Enjoy a day of skiing - The nearby ski resorts of Parâng and Straja offer excellent slopes for skiers of all levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Petroșani

Day 1 - Explore the Miner’s Park and learn about the history of coal mining in the region.

Day 2 - Take a hiking or biking trip to the nearby Retezat National Park.

Day 3 - Visit the Museum of Petroșani and learn about the history and culture of the city.

Day 4 - Drive along the scenic Transalpina Road and enjoy the stunning views of the Parâng Mountains.

Day 5 - Explore the nearby city of Sibiu, known for its charming historic center and beautiful architecture.

Day 6 - Enjoy a day of skiing at the nearby ski resorts of Parâng or Straja.

Day 7 - Visit the Gothic-Style Orthodox Church and admire the stunning stained-glass windows and intricate wood carvings.

Food and Drink Scene of Petroșani

No trip to Petroșani is complete without trying some of the local dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale - Traditional Romanian dish made of minced meat and rice wrapped in cabbage leaves.

  • Mămăligă - A cornmeal dish similar to polenta and served with cheese or sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Țuică - A traditional Romanian brandy made from plums or other fruits.

  • Vin Brulé - A hot spiced wine that is perfect for cold winter evenings.

Where to Stay in Petroșani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Rusu - A modern hotel located in the city center with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

  • Pensiunea Eden - A guesthouse nestled in the mountains of Parâng, perfect for those seeking a quiet retreat.

  • Hostel Oncești - A budget-friendly hostel located near the Miner’s Park, perfect for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Petroșani: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Petroșani is known for its traditional crafts and locally-made products. Here are some items to look out for:

  • Handmade wooden souvenirs - Look for wooden spoons, bowls, and other items handmade by local artisans.

  • Local honey and cheese - Petroșani is known for its high-quality honey and tasty cheese, perfect for bringing home as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Petroșani

The people of Petroșani are known for their warm hospitality and love of tradition. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Greeting locals with a polite “Buna Ziua” - This is the traditional greeting used by locals, make sure to use it when meeting new people.

  • Taking off your shoes in other people’s homes - It is customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home in Romania, so make sure to do so to show respect.

Best Time to Visit Petroșani

Petroșani is a year-round destination with something to offer in every season. However, the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the hiking trails are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Petroșani

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine - Petroșani is known for its delicious traditional dishes, make sure to try them.

  • Visit the local markets - The markets in Petroșani are a great place to experience the local culture and find unique souvenirs to bring home.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs - Make sure to respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering someone’s home.

  • Leave trash on the hiking trails - Make sure to pack out all your trash and leave the trails clean for other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Petroșani

Petroșani is a safe city, but as with any destination, it is important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions. Here are a few tips for staying safe:

  • Avoid isolated areas at night - Stick to well-lit areas and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Watch out for pickpockets - Keep your belongings close and be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas.

Health in Petroșani

Petroșani has excellent medical facilities, and emergency services are available 24/7. Make sure to have adequate travel insurance before your trip, and carry any necessary medications with you.

Petroșani is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by public transportation. Buses and taxis are available for those who want to travel farther afield.

Where to Go Next from Petroșani: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Petroșani is a perfect base for exploring the nearby attractions of Transylvania. Here are a few destinations to consider:

  • Cluj-Napoca - A vibrant city known for its lively cultural scene and charming historic center.

  • Sibiu - A beautiful medieval city with stunning architecture and a rich cultural heritage.

  • Bran Castle - Known as “Dracula’s Castle,” this historic fortress is a must-visit for fans of gothic literature and history.

Conclusion

Petroșani is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re an avid hiker, a history buff, or a food lover, this city has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip now and explore the beauty of Transylvania in Petroșani.
