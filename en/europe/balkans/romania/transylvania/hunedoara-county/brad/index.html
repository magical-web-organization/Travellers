Introduction to Brad

Brad is a small town in Hunedoara County, located in Transylvania, Romania. It is situated in the heart of the Apuseni Mountains, offering visitors a unique experience of natural beauty, exciting outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. The town was founded in the 16th century and has a rich history and cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best of Brad, including top attractions, where to stay, how to get there, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Brad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brad Castle

Brad Castle is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the town. The castle was built in the 15th century and has a rich history. Visitors can explore the castle’s inner courtyard and towers, which offer panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. The castle is also home to a museum that features a collection of medieval weapons, armor, and art.

Brad Monastery

The Brad Monastery is a beautiful monastery located on the outskirts of the town. It was built in the 18th century and is known for its beautiful frescoes and iconography. Visitors can attend religious services and explore the monastery’s grounds and gardens.

The Roman Castrum of Castro

The Roman Castrum of Castro is an ancient Roman fortification located just outside Brad. It dates back to the 2nd century AD and was built to protect the Roman Empire’s northern borders. Visitors can explore the ruins of the fortification and learn about the Roman Empire’s history in the region.

Natural Attractions of Brad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Retezat National Park

Retezat National Park is a stunning natural park located just a short drive from Brad. It is home to some of the most beautiful landscapes in Romania, including the Retezat Mountains, glacial lakes, and wildlife. Visitors can hike, bike, and camp in the park, and enjoy the stunning views and fresh mountain air.

Brad Mountains

The Brad Mountains offer visitors a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, horseback riding, and skiing. The mountains are home to several peaks, including Piatra Morgan, which offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Dealul cu Dor Nature Reserve

The Dealul cu Dor Nature Reserve is a protected area located near Brad. It is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, wild boar, and birds of prey. Visitors can hike through the reserve’s forests and meadows, enjoying the peaceful scenery and observing the local flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brad

Brad has plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer visitors a chance to explore the town’s natural beauty and scenic views. Some popular trails include:

  • The Transalpina Trail: This is a popular hiking trail that runs through Brad and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • The Brad-Murenilor Trail: This is a beautiful hiking trail that leads to the Murenilor waterfall, located just outside Brad. The trail is easy to moderate and offers beautiful scenery.

  • The Brad-Revolutiei Trail: This trail leads to the site of the former Revolution of 1848, an important historical event in Romania’s history.

Best Things to Do in Brad: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Brad include:

Food and Drink Scene of Brad

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Brad include:

  • Ciorbă: A traditional Romanian soup made from meat, vegetables, and sour cream.

  • Sarmale: Stuffed cabbage rolls filled with minced meat and rice.

  • Mici: Grilled meatballs made from a mixture of beef, lamb, and pork.

Local Beverages

Some of the local beverages in Brad include:

  • Tuica: A traditional Romanian brandy made from plums.

  • Wine: Transylvania is known for its high-quality wines, and Brad has some excellent local wineries.

  • Beer: Romania has a long brewing tradition, and Brad has several microbreweries that offer a range of local beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Brad
