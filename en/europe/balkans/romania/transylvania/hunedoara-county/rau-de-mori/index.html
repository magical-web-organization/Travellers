Introduction to Râu de Mori

Nestled in the heart of the Hunedoara County in Transylvania, Râu de Mori is a small village known for its unspoiled nature, historical landmarks, and rich cultural heritage. The name Râu de Mori translates to “Stream of the Dead” in English, which refers to the legend of a battle that was fought in this area during the Middle Ages. Today, Râu de Mori is a popular destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat and a glimpse into Romanian rural life.

List of Places to Visit in Râu de Mori

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Corvin Castle: One of the largest and most impressive castles in Europe, Corvin Castle is a must-visit attraction when in Râu de Mori. The castle was built in the 14th century and is a fine example of Gothic-Renaissance architecture. Visitors can explore the castle’s towers, dungeons, and lavish interiors, as well as learn about its fascinating history.
  • Ethnography Museum: Housed in a traditional Transylvanian house, the Ethnography Museum showcases the region’s traditional way of life. Visitors can admire the intricate wood carvings, traditional costumes, and household items used by the locals in their everyday lives.
  • Dracula’s Coffin Cave: Located in the nearby village of Coroiești, Dracula’s Coffin Cave is a natural wonder that is steeped in folklore. According to legend, Vlad the Impaler, the inspiration behind Bram Stoker’s Dracula, used to hide in the cave during battles. Today, visitors can explore its underground chambers and admire the stalactite and stalagmite formations.

Natural Attractions of Râu de Mori: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Râușor Ski Resort: The Râușor Ski Resort is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts. Situated in the picturesque Retezat Mountains, the resort boasts over 20 km of ski runs, as well as cross-country skiing and sledding facilities.
  • Râu de Mori Gorge: The Râu de Mori Gorge is a scenic marvel that offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can take a hike through the gorge and admire the towering rock formations and rushing waterfalls.
  • Retezat National Park: The Retezat National Park is a haven for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Home to over 80 glacial lakes and a rich variety of flora and fauna, the park offers countless hiking trails that lead to breathtaking mountain vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Râu de Mori

  • Râu de Mori Gorge Trail: This 10 km trail takes visitors through the heart of the Râu de Mori Gorge, offering stunning views of the natural wonders along the way.
  • Retezat National Park Trail: This 15 km trail takes visitors through the heart of the Retezat National Park, offering wonderful scenic views of the park’s rich flora and fauna.
  • Transalpina Bike Trail: This 120 km trail is one of the most scenic bike routes in Europe, taking visitors through the Carpathian Mountains and offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Râu de Mori: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding Tour: Explore the beautiful Transylvanian countryside on horseback and discover the region’s hidden gems.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Sample some of Romania’s finest wines at a local vineyard and learn about the region’s wine-making traditions.
  • Craft Workshop Tour: Take a hands-on approach to learning about the local culture and participate in a workshop where you can learn traditional crafts such as woodcarving and pottery.

Food and Drink Scene of Râu de Mori

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale: A traditional Romanian dish made of minced meat and rice wrapped in cabbage leaves.
  • Mamaliga: A staple food in Romania, mamaliga is a kind of polenta made of cornmeal and served with various meats and cheeses.
  • Cozonac: A sweet bread filled with walnuts, raisins, and cocoa powder, typically served during holidays and special occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Tuică: A strong plum brandy that is a traditional Romanian drink.
  • Țuică cu miere: A variation of tuică that is made by adding honey to the mixture.
  • Vin de Corcova: A red wine produced in the nearby village of Corcova that is known for its unique flavor and aroma.

Where to Stay in Râu de Mori: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pensiunea Calul Albastru: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Râu de Mori, offering comfortable rooms and traditional Romanian hospitality.
  • Hotel Castel Dracula: A unique hotel located near Corvin Castle, that has vampire-themed rooms and decor.
  • Vila Retezat: A family-friendly guesthouse situated in the Retezat Mountains, offering stunning views and easy access to outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Râu de Mori: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Crafts: Local artisans make traditional crafts such as hand-painted pottery, wood carvings, and braided wool rugs that make perfect souvenirs.
  • Plum Brandy: Bring home a bottle of tuică or țuică cu miere to enjoy a taste of Romania.
  • Retezat Cheese: Made from the milk of sheep and cows that graze in the Retezat Mountains, Retezat cheese is a local delicacy that is well worth trying.

Local Culture and Customs of Râu de Mori

Râu de Mori is a small village with a close-knit community that takes pride in their cultural heritage. Visitors are welcomed with warmth and hospitality, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile. The locals are proud of their history and traditions, and visitors are encouraged to respect them.

Best Time to Visit Râu de Mori

The best time to visit Râu de Mori is between May and October when the weather is mild and the natural attractions are at their best. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and outdoor activities during this time, as well as attend local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Râu de Mori

Safety and Health

Safety in Râu de Mori

Râu de Mori is a safe destination for travelers. However, as with any destination, visitors are advised to exercise caution and take necessary safety precautions. Make sure to secure your valuables and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in Râu de Mori

There are no major health concerns related to visiting Râu de Mori. However, visitors are advised to drink bottled water and to be up to date on their routine vaccinations. In case of an emergency, there are medical facilities available in nearby towns.

Getting to Râu de Mori: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Râu de Mori

The nearest airports to Râu de Mori are Sibiu International Airport and Cluj-Napoca International Airport, both located approximately 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Râu de Mori

The closest bus station to Râu de Mori is in the nearby town of Petroșani, which is approximately 30 km away.

Other Options

Râu de Mori is also accessible by car or taxi, and it is located approximately 19 km away from the city of Hunedoara.

How to Get Around in Râu de Mori

The best way to get around Râu de Mori is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent a bike from local vendors or participate in a guided bike tour.

Where to Go Next from Râu de Mori: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hunedoara: Visit the city of Hunedoara and explore its historical landmarks, including Hunyadi Castle and the Michael Klein Museum.
  • Deva: Head to the city of Deva and hike up to Deva Fortress, a medieval castle that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Sarmizegetusa Regia: Visit the ancient Dacian ruins of Sarmizegetusa Regia, which date back to the 1st century BC and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
