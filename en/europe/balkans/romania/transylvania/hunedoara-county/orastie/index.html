Introduction to Orăștie

Orăștie is a charming town located in Hunedoara County, in the heart of Transylvania, Romania. It is situated on the southern hillsides of the Apuseni Mountains, overlooking the Mures River valley. With a population of approximately 20,000 people, Orăștie is a small but vibrant town with rich history and culture. The town is famous for its ancient Dacian fortress, which is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

List of Places to Visit in Orăștie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sarmizegetusa Regia

Sarmizegetusa Regia is an ancient Dacian fortress and the most significant historical attraction in Orăștie. The fortress was built in the first century BC and served as the capital of the Dacian Kingdom. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit attraction for anyone visiting Orăștie.

Museum of Dacian and Roman Civilisation

The Museum of Dacian and Roman Civilisation is another significant cultural landmark in Orăștie. The museum features displays of ancient weapons, pottery, coins, and other artifacts from the Dacian and Roman periods. It provides a fascinating insight into the history and culture of the region.

Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas

The Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas is a beautiful place of worship located in the heart of Orăștie. The church features impressive frescoes, paintings, and religious icons, and it is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in religious architecture and art.

Natural Attractions of Orăștie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Retezat National Park

Retezat National Park is one of the most beautiful and popular national parks in Romania, located just a short drive from Orăștie. The park features stunning mountain scenery, pristine lakes, and abundant wildlife, making it an excellent destination for hiking and camping enthusiasts.

Cinciş Lake

Cinciş Lake is a beautiful natural attraction located just outside Orăștie. The lake is surrounded by mountains and features crystal clear water, making it a popular destination for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Secul Natural Park

Secul Natural Park is another beautiful natural attraction near Orăștie. The park features vast forests, mountains, and scenic views, making it a great destination for hiking and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orăștie

Orăștie and its surroundings offer a wide range of hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Some of the most recommended routes include:

  • The Dacian Fortresses Circuit - a 60 km long trail that connects six ancient Dacian fortresses in the area.
  • Retezat Mountains Circuit - a challenging, multi-day hike around the stunning Retezat Mountains.
  • Cinciş Lake Circuit - a moderate, 12 km long trail that takes you around the beautiful Cinciş Lake.

Best Things to Do in Orăștie: Activities and Tours

Guided Tours of Sarmizegetusa Regia

Guided tours of Sarmizegetusa Regia are available for visitors looking to learn more about the ancient fortress and the history of the Dacian Kingdom. The tours are informative and provide a fascinating insight into the lives of the ancient Dacians.

Wine Tasting Tours

Hunedoara County is known for its excellent wines, and several wineries in the area offer wine tasting tours for visitors to enjoy. The tours provide an opportunity to sample some of the best wines in Romania, all while enjoying the beautiful scenery of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Orăștie

Day 1-2: Explore Sarmizegetusa Regia and the Museum of Dacian and Roman Civilisation.

Day 3-4: Spend two days hiking or biking through the Retezat Mountains and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas and discover the religious architecture and art in the area.

Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and sample some of the best wines in the region.

Day 7: Relax by Cinciş Lake and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Orăștie

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mici - a grilled dish made of beef and lamb, served with mustard and bread.
  • Zacusca - a vegetarian spread made of roasted vegetables, perfect as an appetizer or a side dish.
  • Papanași - a dessert made of fried dough and jam, served with sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Fetească Neagră - a red wine with a rich and fruity flavor, grown in the region.
  • Tuică - a strong alcoholic beverage made of plums, popular in Romania.

Where to Stay in Orăștie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Sarmis

Hotel Sarmis is a four-star hotel located in the heart of Orăștie. It features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a spa, making it an excellent choice for travelers looking for a relaxing stay.

Pensiunea Gio

Pensiunea Gio is a guesthouse located just outside Orăștie. It features comfortable rooms, a garden, and a terrace with beautiful views of the surrounding countryside, making it an excellent option for travelers looking for a peaceful and scenic stay.

What to Buy in Orăștie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional embroidered blouses and shirts, perfect as a gift or a memorable souvenir.
  • Local wines, such as Fetească Neagră or Tămâioasă Românească, to enjoy at home or give as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Orăștie

Orăștie has a rich cultural heritage and customs that are still practiced today. Visitors to the area can experience traditional folk music, dance, and festivals, all while enjoying the warm hospitality of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Orăștie

The best time to visit Orăștie is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is pleasant, and the outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orăștie

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Enjoy the beautiful natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites.
  • Litter in natural areas or cultural sites.
  • Disturb local wildlife or ecosystems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orăștie

Orăștie is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime. Travelers should take basic precautions to protect their belongings and stay safe while exploring the area.

Health in Orăștie

Romania has a well-established healthcare system, and medical facilities in Orăștie are accessible and of a high standard. It is recommended to have travel insurance before traveling to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Orăștie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Orăștie

The closest airport to Orăștie is the Sibiu International Airport, located approximately 80 km away. The airport is served by several airlines and provides good connections to other major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Orăștie

Orăștie has a well-connected bus station, served by several regional and national bus companies. Buses operate regularly to and from major cities in Romania, such as Bucharest and Cluj-Napoca.

Other Options

There are several car rental agencies in the area, making it easy for travelers to explore the region at their own leisure.

How to Get Around in Orăștie

Orăștie is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer to use public transport.

Where to Go Next from Orăștie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Corvin Castle

Corvin Castle, also known as Hunedoara Castle, is a magnificent Gothic fortress located in Hunedoara, approximately 30 km from Orăștie. The castle is one of the most impressive and well-preserved castles in Europe and is definitely worth a visit.

Sibiu

Sibiu is a beautiful and historic city located approximately 80 km from Orăștie. The city features a charming old town with cobbled streets, colorful houses, and several museums and art galleries.

Conclusion

Orăștie is a beautiful and historic town in Romania, offering visitors a wide range of cultural, natural, and outdoor activities. With its stunning scenery, ancient fortresses, and warm hospitality, it is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Romania.
