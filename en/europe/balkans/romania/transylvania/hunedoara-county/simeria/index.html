A Complete Travel Guide to Simeria, Romania

If you’re looking for a unique travel destination in Europe, Simeria is definitely worth a visit. Located in Hunedoara County, in Transylvania, Romania, Simeria offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine.

In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and insider tips for a memorable vacation in Simeria, Romania.

List of Places to Visit in Simeria

Simeria is a small town, but it has plenty of interesting places to visit for tourists. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Simeria Citadel - a medieval fortress built in the 13th century by the Teutonic Knights; it’s the symbol of the town and offers a great view of the surroundings
  • Roman Catholic Church of St. Margaret - a beautiful baroque church built in the 18th century; it has impressive frescoes and decorations
  • Local History Museum - a small museum that showcases the history of the town and the region; it has artifacts and exhibits from the Roman, medieval, and modern periods

Natural Attractions of Simeria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mures Gorges Natural Reserve - a beautiful natural park with stunning landscapes, limestone cliffs, and hiking trails
  • Simeria Waterfall - a picturesque waterfall located in the Mures Gorges Natural Reserve; it’s a popular spot for hiking and picnics
  • Muntii Metaliferi Mountains - a range of mountains that offer great opportunities for hiking, biking, and skiing in winter

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simeria

Simeria is surrounded by beautiful mountains and natural parks, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes around Simeria:

  • Mures Gorges Trail - a challenging hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful Mures Gorges Natural Reserve; it offers stunning views of the limestone cliffs and the river
  • Muntii Metaliferi Trail - a moderate hiking trail that takes you to the Metaliferi Mountains; it’s a great spot for bird watching and enjoying the scenery
  • Biking Trail to Cincșor Monastery - an easy biking trail that takes you to the 14th century Cincșor Monastery; it offers scenic views of the countryside

Best Things to Do in Simeria: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to do in Simeria. Here are some of the best things to do in town:

  • Visit the Local Market - a bustling market that sells fresh produce, handmade crafts, and traditional foods; it’s a great spot to people-watch and experience the local culture
  • Take a Wine Tasting Tour - Simeria is famous for its wines, especially the Fetească Neagră variety; take a tour of the local vineyards, taste the wines, and learn about the winemaking process
  • Attend a Traditional Festival - Simeria hosts several festivals throughout the year, celebrating food, wine, and local customs; join the locals and experience the traditional dances, music, and costumes

Week-Long Itinerary for Simeria

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Simeria, here’s a suggested itinerary that allows you to see the best of the town and the surrounding area:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Simeria Citadel and Roman Catholic Church of St. Margaret
Day 2 Hike in Mures Gorges and visit the Simeria Waterfall
Day 3 Take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards
Day 4 Visit the Local History Museum and explore the town’s streets and shops
Day 5 Take a day trip to the nearby city of Hunedoara and visit Hunedoara Castle
Day 6 Hike in Muntii Metaliferi Mountains and have a picnic lunch

Food and Drink Scene of Simeria

Simeria has a rich culinary tradition, with a focus on hearty and flavorful dishes made with local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Varză a la Cluj - a cabbage-based dish with smoked pork, bacon, and sausages; it’s a popular winter dish
  • Paprikaș - a stew made with chicken, vegetables, and paprika; it’s a comfort food
  • Sfințișori - a sweet pastry made with flour, sugar, and walnuts; it’s a traditional dessert

Local Beverages

  • Fetească Neagră Wine - a red wine variety that’s famous in the region; it has a rich and fruity taste
  • Țuică - a strong plum brandy that’s a staple in Romania; it’s often served as an aperitif or digestif
  • Palincă - a fruit brandy made with apples, pears, or plums; it’s a popular drink during winter holidays

Where to Stay in Simeria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Simeria has several accommodation options for travelers, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Pension La Radu - a cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a lovely garden; it’s a great choice for couples and families
  • Hotel Turist - a three-star hotel located in the center of Simeria; it has spacious rooms and great amenities
  • Hostel Lido - a budget-friendly hostel with dormitory and private rooms; it’s ideal for solo travelers and backpackers

What to Buy in Simeria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Simeria has a variety of handicrafts and local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to bring home:

  • Handmade Pottery - Simeria has a long tradition of pottery-making; you can find beautiful handcrafted ceramic plates, bowls, and vases in the local markets
  • Handwoven Carpets and Rugs - the town’s weavers create intricate and colorful carpets and mats using traditional techniques
  • Fetească Neagră Wine - don’t forget to bring home a bottle of Simeria’s famous red wine variety

Local Culture and Customs of Simeria

Simeria has a unique culture and customs that are influenced by its location in Transylvania and its history. Here are some of the interesting facts about the local culture:

  • Traditional Costumes - Simeria has a traditional costume that’s worn during festive occasions; it’s colorful and includes embroidered shirts, woolen skirts, and headscarves
  • Vampires - Simeria is located in Transylvania, the land of Dracula; the town has its own vampire legend, “Castranova the Vampire,” who terrorized the locals in the 18th century
  • Romanian Orthodox Religion - the majority of the population in Simeria is Romanian Orthodox; there are several beautiful churches and monasteries in the area

Best Time to Visit Simeria

The best time to visit Simeria is during the spring or autumn, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are fewer. The peak tourist season is in July and August, when the temperatures can be high and the town can be crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simeria

To have a smooth and enjoyable stay in Simeria, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn a few Romanian phrases and words before your trip; locals appreciate it when foreigners make an effort to speak their language
  • Respect the local customs and traditions; dress modestly when visiting churches or monasteries, and avoid loud behavior or public displays of affection
  • Try the local dishes and drinks; Romanian cuisine has a lot to offer, and Simeria has some mouth-watering specialties

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the safety rules when hiking or biking in the mountains; always carry water, snacks, and proper gear, and stay on the marked trails
  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in the natural parks; be responsible and dispose of your waste properly
  • Don’t drink and drive; Romanian laws on driving under the influence are strict, and the penalties are severe

Safety and Health

Simeria is a safe town for tourists, and the crime rate is low. However, like in any other place, there are some safety and health issues to keep in mind:

Safety in Simeria

  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded places, such as markets or festivals; keep your valuables in a safe place and be vigilant
  • Take the necessary precautions when hiking or biking in the mountains; check the weather forecast, and follow the safety guidelines
  • Be cautious when crossing the streets; the drivers in Romania can be reckless, and the traffic can be chaotic

Health in Simeria

  • Make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and evacuations
  • Drink bottled water, especially if you have a sensitive stomach; tap water is safe, but it may have a different taste or smell
  • Pack sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses for sunny days; the sun can be strong, especially in summer

Getting to Simeria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Simeria is located in central Romania, and it’s easily accessible by various means of transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Simeria

  • Sibiu International Airport - located 85 km from Simeria; it’s the closest airport with international connections
  • Cluj-Napoca International Airport - located 154 km from Simeria; it’s a larger airport with more international connections

Bus Stations Near Simeria

  • Simeria Bus Station - located in the center of town; it has daily connections to major cities in Romania, such as Cluj-Napoca or Bucharest
  • Hunedoara Bus Station - located 15 km from Simeria; it has connections to smaller towns and villages in the area

Other Options

  • Train - Simeria has a train station that connects it to major cities in Romania, such as Cluj-Napoca or Timisoara
  • Car Rental - you can rent a car and drive to Simeria; the roads are well-maintained, and the traffic is generally light

How to Get Around in Simeria

Simeria is a small town, and you can easily walk or bike around the main attractions. However, if you want to explore the surrounding area, you have several options:

  • Public Transportation - there are several bus lines that connect Simeria to nearby villages and towns; you can buy tickets at the bus station or from the driver
  • Taxi - there are several taxi companies in Simeria; make sure to negotiate the fare in advance, and ask for a receipt
  • Car Rental - you can rent a car and explore the area at your own pace; there are several car rental companies in Simeria

Where to Go Next from Simeria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Simeria is located in a picturesque region of Romania, with plenty of interesting places to visit and things to do. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Simeria:

  • Hunedoara - a medieval city located 15 km from Simeria; it’s famous for its stunning Hunedoara Castle and its historic center
  • Alba Iulia - a charming city located 79 km from Simeria; it’s famous for its impressive Alba Carolina fortress and its beautiful old town
  • Transfagarasan Road - a scenic road that crosses the Fagaras Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the landscapes; it’s located 130 km from Simeria

Conclusion

Simeria is a hidden gem in Romania, with a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Simeria has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and discover the best of Transylvania!
