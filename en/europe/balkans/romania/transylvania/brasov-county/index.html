Introduction to Brașov County

Brașov County is located in the heart of Transylvania, in Romania. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and cultural traditions, it is a destination that appeals to every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Brașov County

If you’re planning a trip to Brașov County, then you’ll definitely want to add these destinations to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bran Castle: This Gothic-style fortress is known as “Dracula’s Castle” and is one of the most popular tourist sites in Romania.

  • Black Church: Gothic-style church located in the city center of Brașov, known for its impressive architecture and historical significance.

  • Prejmer Fortified Church: UNESCO World Heritage Site, built by German colonists in the 13th century for defense purposes.

Natural Attractions of Brașov County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Piatra Craiului National Park: Beautiful mountain range with hiking trails, stunning views, and wildlife sightings.

  • Bucegi Mountains: Perfect for skiing in the winter months and hiking in the summer, with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Libearty Bear Sanctuary: The largest bear sanctuary in Romania, providing a safe haven for rescued bears.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brașov County

Brașov County is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most notable routes include:

  • Seven Ladders Canyon: A challenging hiking trail with stunning views and unique natural rock formations.

  • Postavaru Massif: Popular for rock climbing and hiking, with stunning views of the surrounding forest.

  • Transfagarasan Highway: One of the most popular driving routes in Europe, the Transfagarasan Highway offers scenic views and thrilling hairpin turns.

Best Things to Do in Brașov County: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the colorful houses of Sighișoara, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Take a cable car ride to the top of Timișul de Jos peak for a fantastic view of Brașov city.

  • Take a traditional horse-drawn carriage ride through the beautiful hills of the county.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brașov County

Day 1: Arrival in Brașov, explore the city center and Black Church

Day 2: Bran Castle, Rasnov Fortress, and nearby natural reserves

Day 3: Seven Ladders Canyon and Piatra Craiului National Park

Day 4: Visit the town of Sighișoara

Day 5: Day trip to Bucharest or Sinaia

Day 6: Skiing in Poiana Brasov or hiking in Bucegi Mountains

Day 7: Departure from Brașov

Food and Drink Scene of Brașov County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale: Stuffed cabbage rolls, served with polenta and sour cream.

  • Papanași: A traditional Romanian dessert made with cottage cheese and sour cream.

  • Mici: Grilled minced meat rolls served with mustard and fresh bread.

Local Beverages

  • Țuică: Traditional Romanian brandy made from plums or apples

  • Romanian wines: Romania has a rich history of winemaking, with several regions producing high-quality wines.

Where to Stay in Brașov County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Aro Palace: Located in the heart of Brașov, with stunning mountain views and luxurious amenities.

  • Bella Muzica: A small hotel located in the historic center of Brașov, with unique decor and a cozy atmosphere.

  • Villa Hermani: A family-run guesthouse in Bran village, with stunning views of Bran Castle and the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Brașov County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: Brașov County is known for its traditional pottery, with several local artisans creating beautiful pieces.

  • Wool blankets and socks: Keep warm during the colder months with locally-made wool products.

Local Culture and Customs of Brașov County

Brașov County is known for its rich and diverse cultural traditions, including:

  • Folk dances and music.

  • Traditional handicrafts, including pottery and weaving.

Best Time to Visit Brașov County

The best time to visit Brașov County is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brașov County

Do:

  • Try traditional Romanian dishes and drinks.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Learn basic Romanian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind when hiking or exploring natural reserves.

  • Ignore dress codes at religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brașov County

Brașov County is generally safe for tourists, with low crime rates and friendly locals.

Health in Brașov County

It is recommended to have travel health insurance when visiting Brașov County, and to be up to date on routine vaccinations.

Getting to Brașov County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brașov County

  • Bucharest Henri Coanda International Airport

  • Sibiu International Airport

Bus Stations Near Brașov County

  • Brașov Bus Station

  • Sinaia Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: Brașov is well connected to other cities in Romania by train.

  • Car: Brașov is easily accessible by car, with several highways connecting it to other major cities in Romania.

How to Get Around in Brașov County

The best way to get around Brașov County is by car, as public transportation is limited. However, local buses and taxis are available in most towns.

Where to Go Next from Brașov County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sighișoara: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, with beautiful medieval architecture.

  • Bucharest: Romania’s capital city, with a vibrant nightlife and cultural scene.

  • Sinaia: Home to the stunning Peleș Castle and beautiful natural reserves.

Conclusion

Brașov County is a destination that offers something for everyone - from historic castles to natural reserves, from traditional handicrafts to modern cuisines. Plan your perfect vacation with this comprehensive travel guide, and discover all that Brașov County has to offer.
