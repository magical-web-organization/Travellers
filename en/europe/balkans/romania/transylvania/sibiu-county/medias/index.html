Introduction to Mediaș

Mediaș, a quaint and charming town located in Sibiu County, Transylvania, is a great destination for those looking to experience the authentic Romanian culture and traditions. The town dates back to the 13th century and is surrounded by picturesque hills and forests, offering scenic views and outdoor adventures. Along with its rich history and natural beauty, Mediaș is also known for its local cuisine, handmade crafts, and warm hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Mediaș

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mediaș Citadel

One of the top attractions in Mediaș is the Mediaș Citadel. The citadel dates back to the 16th century and is a symbol of the town’s strength and fortification during the Ottoman invasions. The citadel features four towers and bastions, a museum, and an exhibition on the history of the town and the surrounding area.

The Evangelical Church

The Evangelical Church, an architectural masterpiece built in the Gothic style, was constructed in the 14th century and is located in the town’s central square. The church served as a focal point in the town’s religious and cultural life for centuries and features an interior decorated with wall paintings and sculptures.

The Museum of History and Archaeology

The Museum of History and Archaeology features an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich history of the town and the surrounding area. Visitors can witness everyday life in Mediaș in ancient times and discover the town’s cultural and economic development.

Natural Attractions of Mediaș: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dumbrava Forest

Dumbrava Forest, located just outside Mediaș, is a true natural wonder. The forest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including some rare and protected species. Hiking or biking through the forest is an excellent way to experience the area’s natural beauty.

Târnava Mare River

Târnava Mare River, which flows through Mediaș, is a great spot for fishing or taking a leisurely boat ride. The river is surrounded by scenic hills and forests, providing a peaceful atmosphere for nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mediaș

For those who love outdoor adventures, Mediaș offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the area’s natural beauty and historical landmarks.

Citadel and Cemetery Route

The Citadel and Cemetery Route is a flat, easy, and scenic biking path that takes visitors to the citadel and on to the historic cemetery, where some of Romania’s famous figures rest.

The Sparrow’s Nest Route

For those seeking more challenging hiking or biking paths through the forest, the Sparrow’s Nest Route is an ideal option. The route takes hikers or bikers to the top of the hills surrounding Mediaș, from where they can get a panoramic view of the town.

Best Things to Do in Mediaș: Activities and Tours

Horse-drawn Carriage Tour

A horse-drawn carriage tour is a unique experience for visitors. The tour takes visitors to the town’s most important landmarks and gives them a glimpse of life in a traditional Transylvanian town.

Traditional Crafts Tour

For those interested in local crafts and traditions, a traditional crafts tour is an excellent choice. Visitors can learn how to make handmade crafts such as traditional pottery and woodcarvings and experience the daily life of local artisans.

Food and Drink Scene of Mediaș

Must-Try Dishes

Mediaș is famous for its local cuisine and traditional dishes. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Sarmale

Sarmale is a popular Romanian dish that consists of minced meat, rice, and vegetables wrapped in cabbage leaves and cooked in tomato sauce. This dish is often served during Romania’s winter holiday season and is perfect for warming up during a cold day in Mediaș.

Varză a la Cluj

Varză a la Cluj is a cabbage stew made with pork, paprika, and sour cream. This dish is hearty and flavorful and is a staple of the Transylvanian cuisine.

Cozonac

Cozonac, a sweet bread filled with walnuts, raisins, and chocolate, is a traditional Romanian dessert that is enjoyed during the winter holidays.

Local Beverages

Tuică

Tuică is a strong alcoholic beverage made from plums, and it is often served as an aperitif or digestif during meals.

Wine

Mediaș is a wine-producing region, and visitors can taste high-quality red and white wines. Fetească Neagră is the most famous variety produced in the region.

Where to Stay in Mediaș: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mediaș offers various accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Hotel BinderBubi

Hotel BinderBubi, located in the heart of the old town, is a four-star hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a spa, and a gourmet restaurant. The hotel also features an outdoor terrace, where guests can enjoy beautiful views of the town and the nearby hills.

Pensiunea La Rocca

Pensiunea La Rocca is a charming guesthouse located in a renovated medieval building. The guesthouse features cozy rooms and a restaurant that serves local cuisine.

What to Buy in Mediaș: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mediaș is famous for its handmade products and traditional crafts. Here are some of the must-buy items:

Pottery

Mediaș is known for its traditional pottery, and visitors can find a variety of pottery items such as plates, bowls, and mugs that are decorated with traditional motifs and designs.

Woodcarvings

Local artisans in Mediaș create beautiful woodcarvings such as figurines, bowls, and furniture, using traditional techniques and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Mediaș

Mediaș, like most Romanian towns, has a rich cultural and religious history that is reflected in its architecture, traditions, and cuisine. One of the most important annual events in Mediaș is the Easter celebration, which involves traditional religious services, food, and customs.

Best Time to Visit Mediaș

The best time to visit Mediaș is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is mild, and nature is in full bloom. During the winter months, Mediaș can be quite cold and snowy, and some attractions might be closed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mediaș

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage cultural landmarks.
  • Don’t be loud or disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mediaș

Mediaș is a generally safe town, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions such as safeguarding their belongings and avoiding dark and unlit areas at night.

Health in Mediaș

Romania has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care in local hospitals and clinics if needed. It is advisable to have health insurance that covers medical treatment while visiting Romania.

Getting to Mediaș: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mediaș

The closest airport to Mediaș is Sibiu International Airport, which is located 54 km away. Other nearby airports are Târgu Mureș International Airport and Cluj-Napoca International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Mediaș

Mediaș has several bus stations that connect the town with other destinations in Romania. International visitors can take a bus from larger cities such as Cluj-Napoca or Bucharest, which have international airports.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore the area and reach Mediaș, or take a train to Sibiu or other nearby cities and continue by bus or taxi to Mediaș.

How to Get Around in Mediaș

Most of the town’s attractions are within walking or biking distance. Visitors can rent a bicycle from local rental shops or take a horse-drawn carriage tour of the town. Taxis and local buses are also available for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Mediaș: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sighișoara

Sighișoara, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is located just 35 km from Mediaș. The town is famous for its romantic architecture, fortified walls, and cobblestone streets, and it is one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Europe.

Biertan

Biertan, located 60 km from Mediaș, is home to one of Romania’s most impressive fortified churches. The church, built in the Gothic style, features an intricate wooden door and a defensive tower, making it an important symbol of Romanian history and culture.

Conclusion

Mediaș, with its rich history, natural beauty, and welcoming locals, offers visitors a unique and memorable experience. Whether you are interested in history, nature, food, art, or adventure, Mediaș has something to offer for all kinds of travelers.
