Introduction to Sibiu

Located in the heart of Transylvania, Sibiu is a charming medieval city that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural diversity. With its cobbled streets, gothic architecture, and delicious cuisine, it’s no wonder Sibiu has become a top tourist destination in Romania.

Founded by German settlers in the 12th century, it has an intriguing history that reflects both Romanian and European influences. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking in the nearby mountains, or simply relaxing in a traditional coffee shop, Sibiu has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Sibiu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Brukenthal National Museum: Located in the historic center of Sibiu, this museum has an impressive collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts from all over Europe.
  • The ASTRA National Museum Complex: Situated in the Dumbrava Forest, this open-air museum showcases traditional Romanian village life from the past century.
  • The Council Tower: Climb to the top of this 13th-century tower for a panoramic view of the city.
  • The Evangelical Church: This stunning Gothic church is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city.

Natural Attractions of Sibiu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Păltiniș Ski Resort: Hit the slopes in the winter months and enjoy scenic hiking trails during the summer months.
  • Cindrel Mountains: With peaks reaching over 2,000 meters, these mountains offer some of the best hiking and camping opportunities in Romania.
  • Băile Felix: Located just outside of Sibiu, this natural spa offers thermal baths and relaxation for the whole family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sibiu

  • The Transfăgărășan Road: Known as one of the most scenic drives in Europe, this winding road offers stunning views of the mountains.
  • The Olt River Gorge: Hike along the river and through the forest, taking in the sights of waterfalls and natural rock formations.
  • The Cindrel Mountains: With dozens of trails to choose from, hikers and bikers can explore the natural beauty of the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Sibiu: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided walking tour of the historic center of Sibiu.
  • Attend a performance at the Radu Stanca National Theatre.
  • Taste the local cuisine, including traditional dishes such as sarmale (stuffed cabbage rolls), mici (grilled ground meat rolls), and papanasi (cottage cheese donuts).
  • Visit the local markets and shops to find unique souvenirs and handmade crafts.
  • Attend one of the many festivals that take place in Sibiu, including the International Theatre Festival and Jazz Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sibiu

Day 1 Explore the historic center and visit the Council Tower.
Day 2 Visit the ASTRA National Museum Complex and enjoy a traditional Romanian lunch.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Băile Felix and relax in the natural hot springs.
Day 4 Hike in the Cindrel Mountains and enjoy a picnic in the meadows.
Day 5 Experience the local cuisine on a food tour of Sibiu.
Day 6 Take a guided bike tour of the surrounding countryside.
Day 7 Attend a performance at the Radu Stanca National Theatre and enjoy a nightcap in a traditional coffee shop.

Food and Drink Scene of Sibiu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale: Stuffed cabbage rolls filled with ground pork and rice.
  • Mici: Grilled ground meat rolls served with mustard and bread.
  • Papanasi: Cottage cheese donuts served with sour cream and jam.
  • Ciorbă: Traditional Romanian sour soup made with various meats and vegetables.
  • Mămăligă: A polenta-like dish made with cornmeal and served with cheese and sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Tuică: A traditional Romanian plum brandy.
  • Vinars: A Romanian grape brandy.
  • Ursus Beer: A popular Romanian beer made in Cluj Napoca.
  • Red Wine from Dealu Mare Vineyards: This region of Romania is known for producing high-quality red wines.

Where to Stay in Sibiu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Sibiu: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Pensiunea Buon Gusto: A quaint guesthouse located in the historic center.
  • Smart Hotel Sibiu: A modern hotel located near the airport.
  • JugendStube Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in a historic building.

What to Buy in Sibiu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics from Corund Village.
  • Local honey and jam from the Cindrel Mountains.
  • Traditional Romanian textiles and clothing from local markets.
  • Hand-carved wooden objects and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sibiu

  • Romanians are known for their hospitality, so don’t be surprised if strangers invite you into their homes for a meal or a drink.
  • The majority of Romanians are Orthodox Christians and observe religious holidays throughout the year.
  • Sibiu has a large Hungarian population, so it’s common to see signs in both Romanian and Hungarian.

Best Time to Visit Sibiu

The best time to visit Sibiu is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer months can be hot and crowded, while winters can be harsh and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sibiu

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including traditional Romanian dishes.
  • Explore the city on foot and take in the beautiful architecture.
  • Visit the local markets and shops to find unique souvenirs.
  • Attend one of the many festivals that take place throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for walking on the cobbled streets.
  • Don’t take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waiter or tour guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sibiu

Sibiu is a safe city for tourists, but it’s still important to take precautions such as not walking alone at night and being aware of your surroundings in crowded areas.

Health in Sibiu

Romania has a national health insurance program, but visitors are advised to have travel insurance to cover unexpected medical costs. It’s important to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

The best way to explore Sibiu is on foot, but taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Sibiu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cluj-Napoca: A vibrant university town with a rich cultural scene.
  • Sinaia: A stunning mountain resort town with traditional architecture and beautiful castles.

Conclusion

Sibiu is a beautiful and unique destination that offers something for every traveler. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, this city will leave you with memories that last a lifetime.
