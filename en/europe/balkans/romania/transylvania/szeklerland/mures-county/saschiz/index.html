Introduction to Saschiz

Located in the beautiful region of Szeklerland in Transylvania, Saschiz is a picturesque town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and rich history. The town is surrounded by the stunning Carpathian Mountains and is home to a variety of cultural landmarks, museums, and historical buildings. With its peaceful setting and charming architecture, Saschiz is a perfect destination for anyone looking for an authentic European experience.

List of Places to Visit in Saschiz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fortified Church - Built in the 14th century, this UNESCO World Heritage site is a must-visit attraction in Saschiz. The church is well-preserved and features stunning Gothic-style architecture.
  • Transylvanian Saxons Museum - This small museum provides an interesting insight into the history and culture of the Saxons in Transylvania.
  • Peasant Fortified Church Museum - Located in Fofeldea, a village near Saschiz, this museum showcases the history and architecture of peasant fortified churches in the region.

Natural Attractions of Saschiz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Magura Codlei Forest - This dense forest is home to a range of wildlife and offers spectacular hiking and biking trails. Visitors can explore the forest on their own or take a guided tour.
  • Saschiz Nature Reserve - This protected area is situated on the outskirts of Saschiz and boasts breathtaking views of the surrounding natural landscape. Visitors can hike, camp and explore the reserve’s unique flora and fauna.
  • Tatars’ Hill - Located in the center of Saschiz, this hill provides stunning views of the town and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saschiz

  • Magura Codlei Trail - This 10 km trail takes visitors through some of the most beautiful parts of the Magura Codlei Forest.
  • Saschiz Nature Reserve Trail - A 7.5 km trail that offers stunning views of the nature reserve’s diverse fauna and flora.
  • Covasna-Transylvania Route - A popular 208 km biking trail that takes visitors through Saschiz and other picturesque Transylvanian towns.

Best Things to Do in Saschiz: Activities and Tours

  • Saxon Traditions Tour - This guided tour takes visitors through the traditional Saxon villages of the region, showcasing local customs, architecture, and lifestyle.
  • Wine Tasting - Visitors can explore the surrounding vineyards and sample some of the region’s finest wines.
  • Traditional Cooking Classes - Learn how to prepare local delicacies and participate in traditional cooking classes conducted by local chefs.

Food and Drink Scene of Saschiz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale - A traditional dish made of minced meat rolled in cabbage leaves and served with sour cream.
  • Mici - Small grilled meat rolls, served with mustard, bread, and beer.
  • Gulas - A hearty meat stew, often served with bread.

Local Beverages

  • Szekelykapa - A traditional plum brandy, serves as a digestive or welcome drink.
  • Transylvanian Wine - This region is known for producing high-quality wines, featuring varieties like Feteasca Neagra, Feteasca Regala, and Pinot Noir.

Where to Stay in Saschiz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Saschiz Hotel - A cozy hotel, offering traditional accommodation with modern amenities.
  • Saschiz Hostel - A budget-friendly accommodation option, providing comfortable beds in shared or private rooms.
  • Valea Verde Retreat - A luxurious eco-retreat located near Saschiz, offers sustainable hospitality, farm-to-table dining, and nature-inspired rooms.

What to Buy in Saschiz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Crafts - Visit local markets and workshops to purchase traditional craft and textile products like pottery, embroidery, and lace.
  • Woolen Products - Saschiz is known for its wool production and visitors can find blankets, scarves, and socks made of local wool.

Local Culture and Customs of Saschiz

  • Folk Art - Saschiz is renowned for its folk art, which is rooted in the area’s rich history and culture.
  • Saxon Architecture - Saschiz is home to many well-preserved Saxon buildings that showcase unique craftsmanship and architecture.
  • Szekely Flag - The Szekely flag can be seen throughout the town, as it represents the cultural identity of the local Szekely people.

Best Time to Visit Saschiz

The best time to visit Saschiz is between May and September. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The town also hosts several festivals, including the Saschiz Plum Festival, which takes place in September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saschiz

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Shop at local markets and support local businesses.
  • Try traditional dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Litter or damage natural or historical landmarks.
  • Ignore safety precautions while engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saschiz

Saschiz is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should still use common sense and take basic safety precautions. Avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night and keep valuable belongings in a safe place.

Health in Saschiz

Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling to Saschiz. The town has a small hospital and several private clinics, but serious medical emergencies may require transport to a larger city for treatment.

Getting to Saschiz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saschiz

The nearest airport to Saschiz is the Targu Mures International Airport, which is around 50 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Saschiz

Several bus companies operate regular services to Saschiz from major Romanian cities like Brasov, Bucharest, and Cluj-Napoca.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from nearby cities like Brasov or Targu Mures.

How to Get Around in Saschiz

The best way to get around Saschiz is on foot or by bicycle. Most of the town’s attractions are within walking distance, and bicycles can be rented from several local shops.

Where to Go Next from Saschiz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Brasov - A bustling city located around 70 kilometers from Saschiz, known for its medieval architecture and historic landmarks like the Black Church.
  • Sighisoara - A beautiful medieval town, located around 35 kilometers from Saschiz, and known for its old citadel walls and beautiful architecture.
  • Biertan - A UNESCO World Heritage site located around 30 kilometers from Saschiz, and known for its fortified church and traditional Saxon architecture.

Conclusion

Saschiz is a hidden gem in the Transylvanian region, offering visitors a perfect combination of natural beauty and rich culture. With its charming architecture, beautiful landscape, and warm hospitality, Saschiz is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in Romania.
