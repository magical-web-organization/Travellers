Covasna County is a charming and picturesque destination located in Szeklerland in Transylvania, Romania. This unspoiled region is situated in the heart of the Balkans and is known for its scenic landscapes, natural springs, historic sites, and diverse culture. Whether you’re an avid adventurer, a culture enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Covasna County has something to offer for everyone.

Introduction to Covasna County

Covasna County is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The county is located in the eastern part of Transylvania and is surrounded by the Carpathian Mountains. The region is home to numerous picturesque villages, beautiful rivers, and stunning waterfalls. It is also known for its natural hot springs and spas, which make it an excellent destination for relaxation and wellness tourism.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Covasna County

Covasna County is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. You can explore the region’s breathtaking landscapes and scenic vistas by hiking or biking on the following trails:

  • Harghita Mountains Trail
  • Red Lake Trail
  • Salt Trail
  • Szekler Trail

Best Things to Do in Covasna County: Activities and Tours

Covasna County is an ideal destination for those seeking an active vacation. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Relax in a natural hot spring or spa
  • Explore the unique local culture, customs, and traditions
  • Go trekking or hiking in the Harghita Mountains
  • Take a scenic boat ride on St. Anna Lake

Week-Long Itinerary for Covasna County

If you have a week to spare, we recommend the following itinerary for Covasna County:

Day 1-2: Visit the historic Fortified Church of the Prejmer Village and explore the nearby Harghita Mountains.

Day 3-4: Explore the historic town of Sfântu Gheorghe and the fascinating History Museum. Relax in one of the many natural hot springs in the county.

Day 5-6: Discover the scenic St. Anna Lake and hike in the Red Lake area.

Day 7: Learn about the unique local culture and customs by visiting the Citadel of Târgu Secuiesc.

Local Culture and Customs of Covasna County

Covasna County is famous for its diverse culture, customs, and traditions. Here are some of the most popular customs and traditions:

  • Szekler Gate: A decorative and symbolic entrance to the homes of Szekler families.
  • Dance of the Bear: A traditional dance dating back to the 16th century.
  • Transylvanian Saxons: A distinct ethnic group with a rich cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Covasna County

The best time to visit Covasna County is in the summer months, from June to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its best. The winter months are also a popular time to visit, due to the abundance of winter sports activities and the stunning snowy landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Covasna County

When visiting Covasna County, it is essential to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn a few words of the local language.
  • Respect the local dress code.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine.
  • Be friendly and open to locals.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Touch the traditional costumes and textiles on display.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Covasna County is a safe destination for travelers. It is recommended to have travel insurance before your trip. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Covasna County

  • Avoid walking alone at night in remote areas.
  • Stay on designated hiking and biking trails.
  • Follow the local traffic laws.

Health in Covasna County

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to prevent sunburn and insect bites.
  • Carry any necessary medications with you.
