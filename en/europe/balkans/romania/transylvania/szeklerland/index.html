Introduction to Szeklerland

Szeklerland is a historic region located in the southeastern part of Transylvania, Romania. It is known for its beautiful mountain landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant traditional communities. Despite being a lesser-known destination, it offers a unique and authentic experience for travelers who seek to explore off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Szeklerland

  • Sighisoara: a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Europe. It is the birthplace of Vlad the Impaler, also known as Dracula.
  • Corund: a village famous for its traditional pottery, handmade by local craftsmen.
  • Lake Saint Anne: a stunning glacial lake surrounded by picturesque forests and mountains.
  • Red Lake: a natural marvel created by a landslide and named after the red clay that colors its water.
  • Bicaz Gorge: a stunning natural reserve with spectacular views, hiking trails, and rock climbing opportunities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Szekely National Museum: located in Sfantu Gheorghe, it showcases the history, traditions, and art of the Szekely people.
  • Fortified Churches: there are many fortified churches in Szeklerland, built by the Saxons during the Middle Ages and reflecting the region’s multicultural history. Some of them are Prejmer, Harman, and Viscri.
  • Szekelykapuk: traditional wooden gates decorated with mystical symbols, found in front of many houses in Szeklerland.

Natural Attractions of Szeklerland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harghita and Giurgeu Mountains: ideal for hiking, skiing, and rock climbing. The highest peak is Harghita Madaras, at 1801 meters.
  • Miercurea-Ciuc Botanical Garden: a green oasis in the middle of the city, with over 2000 plant species and beautiful landscapes.
  • Praid Salt Mine: a unique underground attraction with therapeutic properties for respiratory diseases.
  • Tarcu and Cheile Boghii Natural Reserves: protected areas with diverse flora and fauna, popular for their tranquility and natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Szeklerland

  • Transylvania Bike Trail: over 600 kilometers of picturesque cycling routes that connect Sighisoara, Sibiu, and Brasov.
  • Harghita Mountain Trails: diverse hiking trails with various difficulty levels, such as the Harghita Tour, the Muntii Ciucului Circuit, and the Harghita Gepard Trail.
  • Poloniny National Park: a large protected area in Slovakia, right across the border from Szeklerland, with stunning mountain landscapes and a rich biodiversity.

Best Things to Do in Szeklerland: Activities and Tours

  • Climb the Harghita Madaras Peak for a stunning view of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Take a traditional pottery workshop in Corund to learn the skills and techniques of the local craftsmen.
  • Visit the Szekely National Museum in Sfantu Gheorghe for a deep dive into the region’s history, traditions, and culture.
  • Explore the fortified churches and traditional Szekelykapuk in the villages of Prejmer and Viscri.
  • Join a guided tour of the Praid Salt Mine to learn about its history, geology, and therapeutic properties.

Food and Drink Scene of Szeklerland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Varosleves: a hearty soup made with meat, vegetables, and sour cream, typical for the Szekely cuisine.
  • Kürtőskalács: a spiral-shaped pastry, also known as chimney cake, made of sweet dough and coated in cinnamon, sugar, or nuts.
  • Szekelykaposzta: a delicious stew of sauerkraut, pork, and sour cream, usually served with polenta.
  • Chimney Mushroom: a popular dish made of mushrooms, cheese, and sour cream, wrapped in chimney cake dough and baked in an oven.

Local Beverages

  • Palinka: a strong fruit brandy made of plums, apples, or cherries, usually served as an aperitif or a digestif.
  • Bere: a type of beer that is brewed in local microbreweries, characterized by its full flavor and distinct aroma.

  • Zen Garden Resort: a peaceful retreat in the middle of nature, with comfortable rooms, spa facilities, and organic cuisine.
  • Bella Vita Hotel: a cozy hotel in the center of Targu Mures, with a charming garden and a friendly staff.
  • Dara Guesthouse: a traditional guesthouse in Corund, with authentic Szekely architecture and pottery workshops.

What to Buy in Szeklerland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Szekelykapuk: traditional wooden gates decorated with mystical symbols, handmade by local craftsmen.
  • Pottery: handmade ceramics with intricate patterns and motifs, made in the traditional Corund style.
  • Palinka: a celebrated local spirit, available in many flavors and varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Szeklerland

Szeklerland is known for its rich culture and traditions, deeply rooted in the history and identity of the Szekely people. The Szekely community has preserved its distinct language, customs, and folklore, which are reflected in the region’s architecture, cuisine, and celebrations. The Szekelys are proud of their unique identity and have a strong sense of community, solidarity, and hospitality towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Szeklerland

The best time to visit Szeklerland is during the summer months, from June to September when the weather is mild and sunny, and outdoor activities are at their best. However, the region is also beautiful in the winter, with plenty of skiing opportunities, cozy local festivals, and Christmas markets. It is essential to pack warm clothes and appropriate gear for the season, as the weather can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Szeklerland

Do:

  • Respect the local customs, traditions, and architecture.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Learn a few basic words in Hungarian or Romanian.
  • Explore the natural landscapes and outdoor activities.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or their property.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs.
  • Litter or damage natural or historical sites.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious or cultural places.
  • Expect everyone to speak English.
  • Over-tip in restaurants or bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Szeklerland

Szeklerland is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, it is essential to take the usual precautions while traveling, such as avoiding secluded areas at night, keeping valuable belongings secure and concealed, and being aware of potential scams or pickpocketing. It is also advisable to have a valid travel insurance policy that covers medical emergencies and other unexpected events.

Health in Szeklerland

Szeklerland has a modern healthcare system with well-equipped hospitals and clinics. However, medical services can be expensive, and it is advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. Travelers should also take the necessary precautions to prevent common illnesses, such as drinking bottled water, avoiding raw or undercooked food, and getting vaccinated against preventable diseases if needed.

Getting to Szeklerland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Szeklerland

  • Targu Mures International Airport (TGM): located about 70 kilometers from Sfantu Gheorghe, with direct flights to several European destinations.
  • Cluj-Napoca International Airport (CLJ): located about 170 kilometers from Sighisoara, with direct flights to several European destinations.
  • Bucharest Henri Coanda International Airport (OTP): located about 350 kilometers from Szeklerland, with international flights from major cities worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Szeklerland

The most convenient way to get around Szeklerland is by car, as public transportation can be limited and infrequent. However, there are several bus stations that connect the major towns and cities in the region, such as:

  • Sfantu Gheorghe Bus Station: located in the center of the city, with connections to Brasov, Targu Mures, and Cluj-Napoca.
  • Targu Mures Bus Station: located in the center of the city, with connections to several destinations in Romania and Hungary.
  • Sighisoara Bus Station: located near the city center, with connections to several destinations in Transylvania and Romania.

Other Options

  • Taxi: taxis are available in all major cities and towns in Szeklerland, but it is essential to use licensed taxis with a visible meter to avoid scams or overcharging.
