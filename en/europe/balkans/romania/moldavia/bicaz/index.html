Introduction to Bicaz

Bicaz is a small town situated in the Moldavia region of Romania. It is known for its stunning nature and outdoor activities, making it a popular destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts. Bicaz is also rich in history and culture, offering a range of museums and landmarks to explore.

In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with everything you need to plan your trip to Bicaz. From places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scenes, to local culture and customs, we have got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Bicaz

Bicaz is a town that has much to offer, including culturally important attractions, stunning natural landmarks, and adventurous outdoor experiences. Here is a list of places to visit in Bicaz:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bicaz Gorge - This is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Bicaz. The gorge runs for about 8 km and is flanked by towering cliffs, making it a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing.

  2. Red Lake - A beautiful alpine lake situated in the Eastern Carpathians. It is known for its stunning red color, which is caused by iron oxide deposits. The lake has a range of water activities, including fishing and boating.

  3. The Bicaz Fortress - This fortress was built by the Moldavian prince Petru Rares in the 16th century. Today, it stands as a testament to the region’s rich history and culture.

  4. The Church of the Nativity of the Virgin Mary - This beautiful and historic church was built in 1599 and is still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Bicaz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hășmaș Mountains - These are some of the most scenic mountains in Romania, offering a range of hiking and rock climbing options.

  2. The Bicaz Canyon - This natural wonder is a great spot for hiking, cycling, and rock climbing. It is also known for its stunning views and unique rock formations.

  3. The Izvorul Muntelui Lake - This lake is one of the largest reservoirs in Romania, offering a range of water activities such as fishing, boating, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bicaz

Bicaz is a hiker’s paradise, with a range of trails and routes that cater to all levels of experience. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes Bicaz has to offer:

  1. The Hiking Trail to the Bicazului Gorges - This trail runs through the gorges and offers some of the most stunning views in the area. The trail is about 10 km long and takes about 6 hours to complete.

  2. The Biking Trail around Lake Izvorul Muntelui - This trail takes you around the lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The trail is about 30 km long and takes about 3 hours to complete.

  3. The Hiking Trail to the Hășmaș Mountains - This trail takes you up the mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys. The trail is about 15 km long and takes about 8 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Bicaz: Activities and Tours

Bicaz offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Bicaz:

  1. Rock Climbing in Bicaz Gorge - Bicaz Gorge is known for its challenging rock climbing routes, making it a great spot for experienced climbers.

  2. Boat Tour of Red Lake - Take a boat tour of Red Lake to see the stunning scenery and learn about the lake’s history and geology.

  3. Fishing in Izvorul Muntelui Lake - Izvorul Muntelui Lake is a popular spot for fishing, with a range of fish species available, including carp and pike.

Food and Drink Scene of Bicaz

Bicaz has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects the town’s history and culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sarmale - A traditional Romanian dish made with cabbage leaves stuffed with a mix of minced pork and beef, rice, and vegetables.

  2. Papanași - A dessert made with cottage cheese, eggs, flour, and sugar. It is fried and typically served with sour cream and fruit preserves.

  3. Mici - A type of grilled sausage made with minced meat, garlic, and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuică - A strong alcoholic beverage made from plums. It is traditionally served as a welcome drink.

  2. Beer - Romania has a thriving beer culture, and there are several local beers to try, such as Ursus, Timișoreana, and Ciuc.

Local Culture and Customs of Bicaz

Romania has a rich history and culture, and Bicaz is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions:
