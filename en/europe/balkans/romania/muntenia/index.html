Introduction to Muntenia

Nestled in the heart of Romania, Muntenia is a picturesque region that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. This region boasts a rich history and culture, breathtaking natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. The name Muntenia is derived from the Romanian word “munte,” which means mountain. The Carpathian Mountains are one of the main features of this region, but Muntenia has so much more to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Muntenia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Muntenia is home to many culturally important attractions. If you want to explore the history of this region, don’t miss visiting the following:

  • Bran Castle - also known as Dracula’s Castle, this medieval fortress is a must-visit for anyone interested in Gothic architecture and vampire lore.
  • Peles Castle - located in the Prahova Valley, this Neo-Renaissance castle was built in the late 1800s and is now a museum.
  • Bucharest National Museum of Art - this museum holds one of the most extensive collections of Romanian art in the world.
  • The Palace of the Parliament - this massive government building is the world’s second-largest administrative building after the Pentagon.
  • Snagov Monastery - nestled on a small island in Snagov Lake, this historical monastery is surrounded by stunning natural beauty and is a pilgrimage site for many locals.

Natural Attractions of Muntenia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Muntenia is also a paradise for nature lovers. Take a break from the city and discover these beautiful natural attractions:

  • Bucegi Mountains - the Bucegi Mountains are a hiker’s paradise. The mountain range spans over 300 square kilometers and offers breathtaking views.
  • Danube Delta - this UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to an abundance of flora and fauna, making it a perfect place to observe wildlife.
  • Cozia National Park - the park is home to a pack of wolves, a variety of birds, and hiking trails suitable for any level of difficulty.
  • Cocos Island - this small island on the Danube River is famous for its beautiful sunsets and water sports activities.
  • Piatra Craiului National Park - the park’s name means “Prince’s Stone” and is also popular with hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Muntenia

For adventure enthusiasts, Muntenia offers excellent hiking and biking opportunities. The Bucegi Mountains offer many hiking trails for all levels of experience; one of the most popular is the 8-kilometer-long “Babele” route. Cyclists will enjoy the 400-kilometer-long Iron Gates Bike Trail, which runs parallel to the Danube River.

Best Things to Do in Muntenia: Activities and Tours

Muntenia is full of exciting activities and tours. To make the most of your trip, try these:

  • Food Tour of Bucharest - a food tour in Bucharest is a perfect way to discover the city’s culinary delights.
  • Wine Tasting in Dealu Mare - Dealu Mare is one of Romania’s most renowned wine regions. Take a wine tour and taste some locally made wines.
  • Explore the Danube Delta - take a boat tour and explore one of Europe’s most unique natural wonders.
  • Peles Castle Tour - explore one of the most beautiful castles in Romania and learn about its history and architecture.
  • Traditional Handicraft Tours - discover ancient Romanian traditions like egg painting, pottery making, or weaving, and create unique souvenirs and memories.

Week-Long Itinerary for Muntenia

Muntenia has so much to offer that it’s hard to see everything in just a few days. Here’s a week-long itinerary that will allow you to experience some of the region’s top attractions:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Bucharest and explore the city
2 Visit Peles Castle and Brasov City
3 Take a hiking trip to Bucegi Mountains and stay overnight in a mountain cabin
4 Discover the Danube Delta on a boat tour
5 Go on a wine tour in Dealu Mare
6 Visit Bran Castle and Rasnov Fortress
7 Attend a traditional handicraft workshop and enjoy a farewell dinner

Food and Drink Scene of Muntenia

Must-Try Dishes

Romanian cuisine is hearty and delicious, and Muntenia is no exception. Some dishes you have to try while in this region are:

  • Sarmale - cabbage rolls stuffed with minced meat and rice
  • Mici - grilled minced meat rolls
  • Varza a La Cluj - meat and rice stuffed cabbage rolls baked in a tomato sauce
  • Paprika Pork Stew - a delicious pork stew made with paprika, potatoes, and bell peppers
  • Papanasi - a sweet dessert made with cottage cheese and served with sour cream and jam

Local Beverages

Muntenia is known for its wine and brandy. Some of the local alcoholic beverages you should try are:

  • Fetească Neagră - a red wine made in Dealu Mare
  • Ursus Beer - a national beer brand that is popular in Romania
  • Tuica - a traditional Romanian brandy made from plums or other fruits

Where to Stay in Muntenia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Muntenia has plenty of accommodation options suitable for any budget. Some of the options are:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Bucharest Old Town - an elegant hotel located in the city center
  • Hotel Belvedere - a budget-friendly hotel with stunning views of the mountains
  • Podul Dambovitei Guesthouse - located near the Snagov Prahova River, and offers cozy rooms and excellent service
  • Hostel Miorita - a hostel located in the center of Bucharest, perfect for backpackers

What to Buy in Muntenia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some unique souvenirs and local products from Muntenia, such as:

  • Traditional Romanian Blouses - also known as “ie,” Romanian blouses are a symbol of femininity and an integral part of the country’s cultural heritage.
  • Fagaras Mountains Cheese - a famous cheese made from sheep’s milk, aged in caves in the Fagaras Mountains
  • Handcrafted Ceramics - discover traditional Romanian pottery at one of the many pottery workshops in the region
  • Handmade Carpets - you can find some excellent handmade carpets in the city of Pitesti.

Local Culture and Customs of Muntenia

Muntenia is full of tradition, and some customs and events are worth knowing:

  • Martisor - a traditional festival that celebrates the beginning of spring. People exchange small gifts called “martisors” - small brooches made of red and white string and a small trinket.
  • Mărțișorul - a small red and white trinket
  • The “Călușarii” - a traditional dance of the region’s shepherds and farmers, who perform to synchronize with the animals’ cycle in the springtime.

Best Time to Visit Muntenia

Muntenia offers a pleasant stay all year round, with different activities depending on the season. If you want to enjoy the sun, visit between May and August, and if you prefer mountain adventures, plan your visit between June and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muntenia

Do:

  • Try Romanian food and drink
  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions of the region
  • Follow local customs and traditions
  • Keep an open mind and an adventurous spirit

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Stay in your comfort zone; try new things
  • Leave the region without trying some traditional handmade souvenirs

Safety and Health

Safety in Muntenia

Muntenia is generally a safe destination, but be careful when traveling to remote areas or hiking alone. Always keep your valuables in a safe place and avoid showing wealth in public.

Health in Muntenia

Ensure that you have travel insurance that covers medical expenses before visiting Muntenia. It’s also advisable to get a vaccination for Hepatitis A and B and a tetanus shot if necessary.

Getting to Muntenia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Muntenia

Bucharest Henri Coanda International Airport (OTP) is the main airport serving Muntenia. Other airports are Buzau Airport, Giurgiu Airport, and Targoviste Airport.

Bus Stations Near Muntenia

Bucharest Bus Station is the most popular and serves most cities in the region. Other bus stations are Pitesti Bus Station, Ploiesti Bus Station, and Alexandria Bus Station.

Other Options

Train journeys in Muntenia might be slower, but they have magnificent views and are an excellent way to travel. Taxi and car rentals are also available, but we advise you to choose licensed ones.

How to Get Around in Muntenia

The most accessible way to explore Muntenia is by car. You can rent a car at any airport and many hotels in Bucharest. Public transportation options are also available, but they might be less comfortable than a rented car.

Where to Go Next from Muntenia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Romania, consider visiting the Danube River or Transylvania. Brasov and Sighisoara are great examples of medieval towns with preserved history and culture. If you have more time to travel further, you can explore the surrounding countries of Bulgaria, Serbia, Hungary, or Ukraine.

Conclusion

Muntenia is a beautiful region with something for everyone. From natural beauty to cultural heritage and outdoor adventures to delicious cuisine, there’s always something to discover. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will encourage you to discover Muntenia’s magic and create unforgettable memories.
