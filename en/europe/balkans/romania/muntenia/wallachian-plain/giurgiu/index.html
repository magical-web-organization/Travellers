Introduction to Giurgiu

Situated in the Wallachian Plain of Muntenia, Giurgiu is a beautiful city that offers a mix of natural beauty and rich culture. The city is located in the Balkans, in Europe, and is known for its scenic views, historical landmarks, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a cultural immersion, outdoor adventure or both, Giurgiu has something to offer to everyone.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a tour of Giurgiu, exploring its must-visit places, food and drink scene, local culture, and customs, and everything else you need to know to make your visit truly memorable.

List of Places to Visit in Giurgiu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Old Clock Museum: This museum is housed in a historic tower and offers a glimpse into the history and artistry of clockmaking from the 18th to 19th century.
  • Hagi Prodan Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life of Hagi Prodan, a national hero who fought for Romania’s independence from Ottoman rule.
  • Giurgiu Fortress: This fortress was built in the 14th century and is a symbol of the city’s rich history. It is open to visitors and offers panoramic views of the Danube River.
  • The Princely Church: This 16th-century church is dedicated to Saint Nicholas and features impressive frescoes and intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Giurgiu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Danube River: Take a stroll along the Danube river and enjoy stunning views of the river and the city.
  • Ilfov Forest: Take a hike in the Ilfov Forest, which spans across an area of 17 hectares and is located in the eastern part of Giurgiu.
  • Dunavatul de Jos Natural Park: This natural park covers an area of 6 hectares and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the park’s trails and have a picnic in the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Giurgiu

  • The Danube Bike Trail: This trail runs along the Danube river and is perfect for cyclists of all levels. Riders can enjoy scenic views of the river and the city while biking.
  • The Ilfov Forest Hiking Trail: This trail takes hikers through the Ilfov Forest and offers an opportunity to explore the park’s flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Giurgiu: Activities and Tours

  • Danube River Cruise: Take a cruise along the Danube River and enjoy the picturesque views of Giurgiu and the surrounding areas.
  • Visit the Market: Visit the local market and experience the bustling atmosphere of the city. Sample local produce and interact with the friendly locals.
  • Explore the Nightlife: Giurgiu has a vibrant nightlife scene, with numerous bars and clubs open till the wee hours.

Food and Drink Scene of Giurgiu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saramura: A traditional fish soup made with onions, carrots, and spices.
  • Mici: A grilled sausage made with minced meat and spices, often served with bread and mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Tuica: A strong fruit brandy made from plums or apples.
  • Vinars: A local brandy made from the distillation of grape must, often aged in oak barrels.

Where to Stay in Giurgiu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Best Western: A 4-star hotel located in the heart of the city, offering comfortable rooms and a beautiful view of the Danube River.
  • Hotel Danubia: A budget-friendly hotel located in the city center, offering clean and cozy rooms.
  • Guesthouse Casa Cazian: A beautiful guesthouse located in a quiet area, offering a peaceful stay surrounded by nature.

What to Buy in Giurgiu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woven Rugs: Giurgiu is known for its traditional rugs, which are made from wool and hand-woven by local artisans.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Local pottery is a popular souvenir choice, with various decorative designs and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Giurgiu

  • Religion: The majority of the population in Giurgiu is Orthodox Christian, and religious festivals are celebrated with great enthusiasm.
  • Folklore: Giurgiu has rich folklore passed down through generations, with traditional music, dance, and clothing still prevalent in the region.

Best Time to Visit Giurgiu

The best time to visit Giurgiu is from May to September when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Giurgiu

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks, including Tuica and Mici.
  • Do visit the local markets and interact with the friendly locals.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the natural attractions and outdoor areas.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and a hat for protection from the sun.
  • Don’t make loud noises after 10 PM as it is considered disrespectful in Giurgiu.

Safety and Health

Safety in Giurgiu

Giurgiu is overall a safe city. However, visitors should take general precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding dark streets at night.

Health in Giurgiu

Visitors do not need any vaccinations to travel to Giurgiu. However, it is recommended that travelers have medical insurance that covers emergency medical treatment and evacuation in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Giurgiu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Giurgiu

The closest airport to Giurgiu is Henri Coanda International Airport, which is located in Bucharest, about an hour’s drive away from Giurgiu.

Bus Stations Near Giurgiu

There are regular bus services to and from Giurgiu from most major cities in Romania.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Giurgiu via train or car.

How to Get Around in Giurgiu

The best way to get around in Giurgiu is by public transport or walking. Taxis are also readily available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Giurgiu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bucharest: The capital city of Romania is a must-visit destination located just an hour’s drive away from Giurgiu.
  • Ruse, Bulgaria: This historic city is located just across the bridge from Giurgiu and is easily accessible by car or bus.

Conclusion

Giurgiu is a beautiful city that offers a mix of natural beauty and rich culture. From hiking and biking to exploring cultural landmarks, there is something to do for everyone. With its authentic food scene, friendly locals, and beautiful natural attractions, Giurgiu is an off-the-beaten-path destination that is worth a visit.
