Introduction to Bucharest

Bucharest, also known as the “Paris of the East”, is the vibrant capital of Romania. It is located in the Wallachian Plain, in Muntenia, in the Balkans, in Europe and has a population of over 2 million people. Bucharest is a city of contrasts, where rich cultural heritage meets modern architecture, and traditional cuisine blends with international flavors. It is a city that has something for everyone, whether you are interested in history, culture, nightlife, or outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the best things to do and see in Bucharest, explore its local culture and customs, and get transportation and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Bucharest

Bucharest is a city full of attractions and landmarks to visit. Here is a list of the most important and must-see places in Bucharest:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Palace of the Parliament: The second-largest administrative building in the world after the Pentagon, this massive structure is a must-visit while in Bucharest. It was built during the Communist era and houses the National Museum of Contemporary Art and the Chamber of Deputies.
  • The National Museum of Art of Romania: This museum has an impressive collection of Romanian and European art, including works by Rembrandt, Rubens, and Monet.
  • The Village Museum: Located in a beautiful park, this open-air museum showcases traditional Romanian architecture and rural life.
  • The Romanian Athenaeum: This architectural masterpiece is home to the George Enescu Philharmonic Orchestra and hosts many cultural events throughout the year.
  • The National Museum of History of Romania: This museum traces the history of Romania from prehistoric times to the present day and is housed in a beautiful neoclassical building.

Natural Attractions of Bucharest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Herastrau Park: The largest park in Bucharest, it is located north of the city and has a lake, a village museum, and many recreational activities.
  • Carol Park: This park is home to the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, the Mausoleum of the National Liberation Heroes, and the Astronomical Institute.
  • Cismigiu Gardens: Located in the city center, this park is perfect for a relaxing walk or a picnic.
  • Tineretului Park: This park is ideal for jogging, biking, or playing sports. It also has a lake and a playground for children.
  • The Bucharest Delta: Located on the outskirts of the city, this natural reserve is a paradise for bird watchers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bucharest

Bucharest is not typically associated with hiking and biking, but there are some great routes for those who like outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails to explore in Bucharest:

  • Calea Victoriei: This is one of the main boulevards in Bucharest and is great for cycling or walking. It is home to many historical buildings and landmarks, including the Royal Palace and the National Museum of History.
  • The Delta Trail: This is a great hiking and biking trail that starts from the outskirts of Bucharest and follows the banks of the Dambovita River. It offers great views of the city skyline and the surrounding nature.
  • The Herastrau Trail: Located in the Herastrau Park, this trail is perfect for both biking and walking.
  • The Cotroceni Trail: This trail starts at the Cotroceni Palace and takes you through the beautiful Cotroceni neighborhood, where you can see many historical and architectural landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Bucharest: Activities and Tours

Bucharest offers a wide variety of activities and tours for all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best things to do in Bucharest:

  • City Tour: Take a city tour to explore the main sights and landmarks of Bucharest.
  • Nightlife Tour: Bucharest has a vibrant nightlife scene, with many clubs, bars, and pubs to choose from. Take a nightlife tour to discover the best spots and venues.
  • Food Tour: Romania has a rich culinary tradition, and Bucharest offers a wide variety of traditional and international dishes. Take a food tour to taste the best of Bucharest.
  • Wine Tour: Romania is famous for its wines, and Bucharest has many wine bars and shops that offer some of the best Romanian wines. Take a wine tour to discover the best varieties and producers.
  • Bike Tour: Discover the city’s landmarks and hidden gems on a bike tour. It is a fun and eco-friendly way to explore the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Bucharest

Romanian cuisine is flavorful, hearty, and diverse, with influences from Turkish, German, and Hungarian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Bucharest:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale: Stuffed cabbage rolls with minced meat, rice, and vegetables.
  • Mici: Grilled minced meat rolls seasoned with garlic and paprika.
  • Ciorba: A sour soup that comes in many varieties, including chicken, beef, and vegetable.
  • Mamaliga: A traditional Romanian polenta made with cornmeal and served with cheese, sour cream, and meat.
  • Papanasi: Fried doughnuts made with cheese and served with sour cream and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Tuica: A potent plum brandy that is the national drink of Romania.
  • Palinka: A fruit brandy made from plums, apricots, or other fruits.
  • Beer: Romania has a rich beer culture, with many great breweries and beer bars to choose from.
  • Wine: Romania produces some excellent wines, especially in Transylvania and Moldova.

Where to Stay in Bucharest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bucharest has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Bucharest:

  • Grand Hotel Continental: This five-star hotel is a masterpiece of neoclassical architecture and offers luxury amenities and services.
  • Hilton Garden Inn Bucharest Old Town: This four-star hotel is located in the heart of the Old Town and offers modern rooms and a rooftop terrace.
  • Podstel Bucharest: This hostel is located in the central area of Bucharest and offers cozy and affordable dorm rooms.
  • The Mansion Boutique Hotel: This guesthouse is located in a historical building and offers stylish and elegant rooms and a courtyard bar.

What to Buy in Bucharest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bucharest is a great place to find unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Bucharest:

  • Traditional crafts: Bucharest has many markets and street vendors selling traditional crafts such as pottery, textiles, and woodcarvings.
  • Romanian wines: Romania produces some excellent wines, especially in Transylvania and Moldova. Look for bottles of Feteasca Neagra or Pinot Noir.
  • Tuica and Palinka: These potent fruit brandies make great souvenirs and gifts. Look for bottles with local labels.
  • Honey: Romania has a long tradition of beekeeping, and its honey is known for its quality and flavor.
  • Salami and Cheese: Romanian salami and cheese are famous for their taste and aroma. Look for local producers and small shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Bucharest

Romanians are known for their hospitality, warm spirit, and passion for life. Here are some local customs and cultural practices in Bucharest:

  • Greetings: Romanians greet each other with a handshake or a kiss on the cheeks, depending on the level of familiarity.
  • Time: Romanians are punctual and appreciate punctuality. It is considered impolite to be late.
  • Tipping: Tipping is standard practice in Romania, and it is customary to leave a tip of 10% to 15% in restaurants and cafes.
  • Dress Code: Bucharest is a modern and cosmopolitan city, but it is still respectful to dress modestly when visiting churches and religious sites.
  • Language: The official language of Romania is Romanian, but many people speak English, especially in tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit Bucharest

The best time to visit Bucharest is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bucharest

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bucharest:

Do:

  • Do try traditional Romanian dishes and drinks.
  • Do visit some of the local markets and street vendors.
  • Do explore the city on foot or by bike.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Do experience the local nightlife.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, stick to bottled water instead.
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended or in plain sight.
  • Don’t take unlicensed taxis.
  • Don’t take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Don’t make political or religious comments unless you know the person well.

Safety and Health

Bucharest is a safe city for tourists, but like any big city, it has its share of petty crime and scams. Here are some safety and health tips for Bucharest:

Safety in Bucharest

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep a close eye on your belongings.
  • Don’t walk alone at night in deserted or poorly lit areas.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services.
  • Be cautious when using ATM’s or exchanging money.
  • Be careful when crossing the street, as traffic can be heavy and chaotic.

Health in Bucharest

  • Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance before you arrive in Bucharest.
  • Stick to bottled water and avoid ice cubes in drinks.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • Take precautions against food and water-borne diseases by washing your hands frequently and avoiding raw or undercooked foods.
  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Romania.

Getting to Bucharest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bucharest is easily accessible by air, bus, and train. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around Bucharest:

Airports Near Bucharest

  • Henri Coanda International Airport: The main airport of Bucharest.
  • Aurel Vlaicu International Airport: Located in the north of Bucharest, it serves mainly low-cost carriers.

Bus Stations Near Bucharest

  • Bucharest Central Bus Station: Located in the city center, it serves mainly domestic routes.
  • Autogara Militari: Located in the west, it serves mainly international routes.

Other Options

  • Train: Bucharest is connected by train to many major cities in Romania and Europe.
  • Car rental: You can rent a car to explore Bucharest and the surrounding areas.
  • Public transportation: Bucharest has an extensive public transportation network, including buses, trams, and metros.

How to Get Around in Bucharest

Bucharest is a big city with traffic congestion, and sometimes driving can be a challenging experience. However, there are many alternative transportation options available:

  • Public transportation: Bucharest has an integrated public transportation network, including buses, trams, trolleybuses, and metros. The tickets can be bought from the stations or kiosks, and they are valid for multiple rides.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Bucharest, and the prices are usually reasonable. However, make sure to use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services like Uber or Bolt to avoid scams.
  • Bike Sharing: Bucharest has a bike-sharing system called Velo, with many docking stations throughout the city.
  • Walking: The city center is compact and walkable, with many pedestrian areas and sidewalks.

Where to Go Next from Bucharest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bucharest is centrally located in Romania, making it a perfect starting point for exploring the country. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Transylvania: Known for its medieval towns, castles, and legends, Transylvania is a must-visit region in Romania.
  • Brasov: A beautiful city located in the heart of the Carpathian Mountains, Brasov is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs.
  • Danube Delta: The second-largest river delta in Europe, the Danube Delta is a protected natural reserve and a paradise for bird watching and outdoor activities.
  • Targoviste: A city with an important historical heritage, Targoviste was the capital of Wallachia during the 15th and 16th centuries.

Conclusion

Bucharest is a city full of contrasts, where rich cultural heritage meets modern architecture, and traditional cuisine blends with international flavors. It is a city that has something for everyone, whether you are interested in history, culture, nightlife, or outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide has helped you discover the best things to do and see in Bucharest, explore its local culture and customs, and get transportation and safety tips. Enjoy your visit to Bucharest!
