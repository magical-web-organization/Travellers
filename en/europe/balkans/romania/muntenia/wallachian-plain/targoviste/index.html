Introduction to Târgoviște

Located in the heart of the Wallachian Plain in the Muntenia region of Romania, Târgoviște is a hidden gem of the Balkans. It’s a city that’s steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure. Târgoviște is the former capital of Wallachia, and the city’s rich history is evident in the numerous cultural landmarks and historic sites throughout the city.

List of Places to Visit in Târgoviște

Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural heritage or experiencing the natural beauty of the region, Târgoviște has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Târgoviște:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of any trip to Târgoviște is the abundance of culturally significant landmarks and historical sites. For history buffs, the city is a treasure trove of artifacts and stories. Some of the must-see places to visit include:

  • Princely Court: The site of the former palace of the Wallachian princes, Princely Court was established in the 15th century and served as the administrative center of Wallachia until the capital was moved to Bucharest in the 19th century. Today, the site is home to a museum that houses collections of medieval armor, weapons, and other fascinating artifacts from the region’s ancient history.
  • The Chindia Tower: Built in the 15th century, the Chindia Tower is one of Târgoviște’s most iconic landmarks. Originally constructed as a lookout tower, the Chindia Tower was later used as a prison and eventually transformed into a museum. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for a panoramic view of the surrounding city.
  • The Museum of the Court: Located in the historic Princely Court complex, the Museum of the Court houses a vast collection of archaeological finds, artifacts, and exhibits that trace the city’s history from the ancient Dacian era to the modern day. Also, visitors can explore the recreated rooms of the former palace.

Natural Attractions of Târgoviște: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Târgoviște’s natural beauty is just as impressive as its cultural landmarks. Visitors looking to get outdoors and explore the surrounding countryside have plenty of options to choose from, including:

  • Ialomita Cave: Located just outside Târgoviște, the Ialomita Cave is one of the region’s most beautiful natural wonders. The cave system consists of a series of underground caverns, waterfalls, and crystal clear pools. Visitors can take a guided tour through the cave to marvel at the stalactites and stalagmites that adorn the caverns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Târgoviște

The natural beauty of Târgoviște also makes it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • The Ialomita Gorges Trail: This is a popular hiking route that takes you through some of the region’s most scenic areas. Along the way, you’ll encounter waterfalls, rock formations, and lush forests that are home to a variety of wildlife.
  • Biking in the Forest: For cycling enthusiasts, Târgoviște’s forested areas are perfect for a day of mountain biking. The terrain is challenging, but the views are breathtaking, and the experience is worth it.

Best Things to Do in Târgoviște: Activities and Tours

Târgoviște’s vibrant culture, rich history, and natural beauty make it an ideal destination for an adventure-filled vacation. Here are some of the best things to do while visiting Târgoviște:

  • Explore the Historic Center: Take a stroll through the historic center of the city and marvel at the well-preserved medieval architecture.
  • Visit the Local Markets: Shop for souvenirs, local crafts, and fresh produce at the bustling markets dotted throughout the city.
  • Enjoy Traditional Cuisine: Sample the best of Romanian cuisine at one of Târgoviște’s many traditional restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Târgoviște

Romanian cuisine is famous for its hearty, flavorful dishes that feature a range of fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Târgoviște is no exception, and visitors will find a wealth of options to choose from when it comes to dining. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample while in Târgoviște:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale: This dish consists of stuffed cabbage rolls filled with a mixture of minced pork, beef, and vegetables. It’s a beloved staple of Romanian cuisine, and visitors to Târgoviște won’t want to miss out on trying it.
  • Mici: A popular street food in Romania, mici is a grilled sausage made from minced beef and pork that’s flavored with garlic and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Țuica: A traditional Romanian spirit made from distilled plums, țuica is a popular drink in Târgoviște and throughout Romania.
  • Romanian Wine: Romania is home to a thriving wine industry, and visitors to Târgoviște should sample some of the region’s best varietals.

Where to Stay in Târgoviște: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Târgoviște offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses, visitors will find everything they need for a comfortable and relaxing stay. Some of the top options include:

  • Hotel King: This four-star hotel is located in the heart of Târgoviște and offers comfortable accommodations and excellent amenities.
  • Guesthouse Victoria: If you’re looking for a more affordable option, Guesthouse Victoria offers clean, comfortable rooms with friendly service.

What to Buy in Târgoviște: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Târgoviște is home to a rich tradition of craftsmanship and handmade goods. Visitors looking for unique souvenirs and locally made products will find plenty to choose from, including:

  • Traditional Romanian Clothing: Târgoviște has a thriving textile industry, and visitors can purchase top-quality, handmade garments and accessories.
  • Local Honey: The region surrounding Târgoviște is known for its honey production, and visitors can purchase jars of the sweet stuff to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Târgoviște

Romanians are known for their warmth and hospitality, and visitors to Târgoviște will no doubt feel welcome in this charming city. Some customs and cultural traditions to be aware of include:

  • Greetings: Romanians typically greet one another with a handshake and direct eye contact. It’s considered impolite to avoid eye contact or to be overly familiar with strangers.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Târgoviște, and visitors should plan to leave a gratuity of around 10 percent at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Târgoviște

The best time to visit Târgoviște is during the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summers in Târgoviște can be hot and humid, while winters are cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Târgoviște

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Târgoviște:

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Romanian.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Expect warm hospitality and friendly service from the locals.

Don’t:

  • Touch artifacts or exhibits in museums and cultural sites.
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing in public.
  • Leave valuables unattended or in unlocked vehicles.

Safety and Health

Târgoviște is a generally safe place for tourists, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Târgoviște

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.
  • Use taxis or public transportation to get around after dark.

Health in Târgoviște

Visitors to Târgoviște should ensure that their routine vaccinations are up to date and consider getting a hepatitis A vaccine before travel. It’s also important to practice good hygiene, including washing your hands frequently and avoiding eating uncooked or undercooked foods.

Getting to Târgoviște: Transportation Options and Logistics

Târgoviște is easily accessible from other major cities in Romania and throughout the region. Here are some of the best transportation options for getting to and around Târgoviște:

Airports Near Târgoviște

The nearest major airport to Târgoviște is Henri Coandă International Airport in Bucharest, which offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Târgoviște

Târgoviște is well-connected by bus to other cities in Romania and throughout the region. Visitors can take a bus from Bucharest or other major cities to Târgoviște.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train or hire a car to get to Târgoviște.

How to Get Around in Târgoviște

Târgoviște is a relatively small city, and most visitors can easily get around on foot. However, for those who prefer to use public transportation, there are buses and taxis available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Târgoviște: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Târgoviște is located in a beautiful region of Romania that offers plenty to see and do. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Brasov: This beautiful city in the Carpathian Mountains is a popular destination for its medieval architecture, vibrant nightlife, and natural beauty.
  • Bucharest: Romania’s capital city is a dynamic metropolis that offers something for everyone, from museums and cultural landmarks to trendy restaurants and bars.
  • Bran Castle: Fans of Bram Stoker’s Dracula will want to visit this famous castle, which is said to have inspired the novel.

Conclusion

Târgoviște is a city that’s full of surprises, from its rich history and vibrant culture to its stunning natural landscape. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural landmarks or venturing out to the nearby mountains and forests, Târgoviște is sure to leave a lasting impression on you. So pack your bags and prepare for an adventure of a lifetime!
