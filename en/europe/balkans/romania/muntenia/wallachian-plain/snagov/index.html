Snagov is a beautiful destination located in the Wallachian Plain, in Muntenia, Romania. It’s a hidden gem that offers everything from natural attractions to cultural landmarks. Whether you’re planning a family vacation or a romantic escapade, Snagov has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will cover all the essential information you need to know to make your trip to Snagov unforgettable.

Introduction to Snagov

Snagov is a small commune with a population of less than 5,000 people. It’s a resort town known for its beautiful lake, Snagov Lake, which is surrounded by dense forests. The lake is a popular destination for swimming, fishing, and hiking. The town is also known for being the final resting place of Vlad the Impaler, the inspiration behind the character of Dracula.

List of Places to Visit in Snagov

Snagov has a lot of attractions that will appeal to history buffs, culture lovers, and outdoor enthusiasts. Here is a list of must-visit places in Snagov:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Snagov Monastery: a 14th-century Orthodox monastery, where Vlad the Impaler is believed to be entombed.

  • Museum of Snagov Monastery: a small museum located within the monastery that features a collection of religious artifacts and manuscripts.

  • Snagov Club: a beautiful art nouveau building that used to be a casino in the early 1900s and now serves as a cultural center.

Natural Attractions of Snagov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Snagov Lake: The largest lake in the area, surrounded by a lush forest and home to various fish and bird species. It’s an excellent spot for kayaking, swimming, fishing, or just relaxing by the shore.

  • Snagov Forest: A vast forest that covers over 6,000 hectares and is home to an abundance of wildlife, including bears, wolves, deer, and foxes. You can take a hike or bike ride through the forest and enjoy the scenic views.

  • Cernica Monastery: a beautiful 17th-century monastery known for its impressive architecture and serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Snagov

Snagov’s natural beauty makes it an excellent location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • Snagov Forest Trail: a 19 km circular trail that takes you through the dense forest and offers fantastic views of the lake.

  • Cernica Monastery Trail: a 12 km trail that starts from Snagov Monastery and takes you through the forest towards Cernica Monastery.

Best Things to Do in Snagov: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and relaxing by the lake, there are plenty of other activities you can do in Snagov. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Fishing: Snagov Lake is known for its excellent fishing opportunities. You can rent a boat or a fishing rod and try your luck.

  • Horse Riding: There are several horse riding centers around Snagov where you can take a ride through the forest or along the lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Snagov

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Snagov:

Day 1: Arrive in Bucharest and take a private transfer to Snagov. Check into your hotel and relax.

Day 2: Explore Snagov Monastery and the Museum of Snagov Monastery. Stroll around Snagov Lake and enjoy the scenery.

Day 3: Take a hike through Snagov Forest and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Visit Cernica Monastery and hike the Cernica Monastery Trail.

Day 5: Go fishing in Snagov Lake or take a horseback ride through the forest.

Day 6: Spend the day at the Snagov Club and enjoy the various cultural activities on offer.

Day 7: Relax by the lake, take a swim, or rent a boat and explore the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Snagov

Snagov’s food scene is a blend of traditional Romanian cuisine and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarmale: Cabbage rolls stuffed with a mixture of rice, pork, and spices.

  • Mici: Grilled minced meat rolls served with mustard and bread.

Local Beverages

  • Tuica: A strong plum brandy that is popular throughout Romania.

  • Wine: Romania is known for its excellent wines, and Snagov is no exception. Try a glass of Feteasca Regala, a white wine that is popular in the area.

Where to Stay in Snagov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Snagov offers a variety of accommodations options that cater to different budgets and tastes. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Snagov Club: This historic building has been converted into a hotel, featuring comfortable rooms and great views of the lake.

  • Vila 23 Snagov: A cozy guesthouse that offers a homey atmosphere and excellent hospitality.

What to Buy in Snagov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, here are some suggestions:

  • Woven Textiles: Snagov is famous for its beautiful textiles, including rugs, tablecloths, and traditional costumes.

  • Handmade Ceramics: You can find a range of pottery and ceramic products that are handcrafted by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Snagov

Romania has a rich culture, and Snagov is no exception. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches or monasteries.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially during religious holidays.

Best Time to Visit Snagov

Snagov is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm, and the lake is perfect for swimming and water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Snagov

Do:

  • Respect the environment and take your trash with you.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Swim in unauthorized areas of the lake.

  • Disturb the wildlife and follow the proper safety precautions when hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Snagov

Snagov is a safe destination, but you should take the appropriate safety precautions.

  • Use common sense and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings.

Health in Snagov

Ensure you have a comprehensive health insurance plan that covers emergencies in Romania. Avoid drinking tap water and only consume bottled water or boiled water.

Getting to Snagov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Snagov

The closest airport to Snagov is Henri Coanda International Airport, located in Bucharest.

Bus Stations Near Snagov

There is no direct bus to Snagov from Bucharest. However, you can take a bus from Bucharest to Snagov’s nearby towns, and then take a taxi or hire a private transfer to Snagov.

Other Options

You can also rent a car and drive to Snagov, or arrange for a private transfer from Bucharest to Snagov.

How to Get Around in Snagov

Snagov is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and private transport options are also available.

Where to Go Next from Snagov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Romania, here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Bucharest: The capital of Romania, known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Brasov: A beautiful medieval town located in Transylvania, known for its impressive fortress and ski resorts.

Conclusion

Snagov is a hidden gem that has something to offer everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to culture lovers. With our comprehensive guide, you’ll have all the necessary information to plan the perfect trip to Snagov. Enjoy your trip!
