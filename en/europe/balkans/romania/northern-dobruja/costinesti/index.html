Introduction to Costinești

Costinești is a charming, small-scale seaside town nestled in the Northern Dobruja region of Romania, located in the Balkans of Europe. This hidden gem is unique due to its untouched beauty and the fact that it has been untouched by mass tourism. Compared to the other Romanian coastal towns, Costinești has retained much of its romantic waterfront charm. The town is well known for its long sandy coastline, with a beach that stretches for around 3 kilometers. Costinești is not only a destination for beach lovers, but it is also home to some stunning landmarks such as museums, historical landmarks and some of the most spectacular natural scenery in Romania.

List of Places to Visit in Costinești

Costinești has an array of places to visit that promises to satisfy the wanderlust of even the most demanding traveller.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Costinești Train Station

The impressive Costinești Train Station is a landmark feature, originally built in the early 1900s. This place gives a unique perspective on Romania’s railway system history and its strong tradition of rail travel.

Shipwreck Museum

Costinești’s Shipwreck Museum is fascinating for history buffs. It showcases the history, disasters and perilous journeys that took place along the waters of the Black Sea.

Natural Attractions of Costinești: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Costinești Beach

The long, golden Costinești Beach is an ideal spot to relax, soak up the sun and enjoy a refreshing dip in the Black Sea. Stretching for 3 kilometers, it is perfect for a stroll at sunrise.

Costinești Nature Reserve

Costinești Nature Reserve is a protected area located along the Black Sea Coast. Offering a beautiful perspective on nature at its finest, this reserve boasts many native flora and fauna species and is a must-visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Costinești

Costinești has many beautiful hiking and biking trails, perfect for those looking to explore the stunning outdoors.

The Black Sea Trail

The Black Sea Trail is an excellent route that will take you along the stunning coastline of the Black Sea, with breathtaking panoramic views and plenty of spots to rest and enjoy the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Costinești: Activities and Tours

Costinești has many activities and tours for tourists, leaving everyone spoilt for choice.

Sailing

A sailing trip around the Black Sea is highly recommended, providing a unique perspective on the quaint seaside town and its surrounds.

Scuba Diving

Costinești has some of the clearest, most pristine waters in Europe, making it the perfect destination for scuba diving and snorkelling enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Costinești

A week-long itinerary for Costinești would include a combination of leisure and sightseeing, such as:

  • A visit to the Shipwreck Museum
  • A day of hiking along the Black Sea Trail
  • Exploring the Costinești Nature Reserve.
  • Sailing on the Black Sea and taking in the picturesque landscape it offers
  • Visiting the Costinești Train Station and learning about the history of railways in Romania.

Food and Drink Scene of Costinești

One of the highlights of any trip to Romania is experiencing its unique cuisine and local drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

Mâncare de Mazăre

Mâncare de Mazăre is a hearty bowl of stew, made with peas, potatoes, and beef seasoned with expressive local herbs.

Ciorbă de Burtă

Ciorbă de Burtă is a traditional Romanian soup that is a must-try for every visitor. It is a hearty soup made with tripe, vegetables, and sour cream and is particularly popular among locals.

Local Beverages

Tuică

Tuică is a spirit made from plums and is a popular local drink, often called “the Romanian whisky.” It is usually consumed before or after the main course.

Where to Stay in Costinești: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Costinești offers a range of accommodation options that will suit every type of traveller’s budget.

Hotel Costinești

Considered the premier destination for those looking for a luxurious stay, Hotel Costinești offers all the modern amenities that ensure a comfortable and pleasurable stay with stunning views of the Black Sea.

Hostel Casa Ardeleana

Hostel Casa Ardeleana is perfect for budget travellers and backpackers. It is situated in the heart of the town and provides clean and comfortable rooms and also features an attached restaurant.

What to Buy in Costinești: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many unique and traditional Romanian souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Costinești.

Local Culture and Customs of Costinești

Costinești has a rich and fascinating cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to learn about Romanian culture through a variety of means, including museums, festivals and local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Costinești

The best time to visit Costinești is during the summer months from June to September. During this time, visitors can expect warm weather perfect for sunbathing and taking a swim.

Do’s and Don’ts of Costinești

Travellers should be mindful of local customs and beliefs while visiting. Here are some things to consider:

Do:

  • Dress politely when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the locals by learning a few basic Romanian phrases when conversing with them.
  • Carry cash as many local shops and guesthouses are cash-only.

Don’t:

  • Don’t visit religious sites or churches in swimwear or beach attire.
  • Don’t talk loudly in public spaces as this is considered impolite.
  • Don’t trespass on private property while exploring the natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Costinești is a safe destination for travellers, but visitors should take passive safety measures to ensure their safety and good health during their stay.

Safety in Costinești

Travellers should take care of their valuables and avoid walking alone in dark, isolated areas at night time. It is also important to take care whilst swimming in the sea and read safety notices near beaches, as there are strong undercurrents.

Health in Costinești

Visitors should carry adequate travel insurance and be up-to-date with their routine vaccinations. Visitors are advised to avoid drinking water from unprotected sources and only drink boiled or bottled water while there.

Getting to Costinești: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Costinești is in Constanta, roughly 50km from the town centre. Visitors can take a shuttle or hire a car at the airport to get to Costinești.

Airports Near Costinești

The nearest airport to Costinești is in Constanta.

Bus Stations Near Costinești

Costinești has a regional bus service that operates from Costinești Bus Terminal to most major towns in Romania.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi from Victoria Square.

How to Get Around in Costinești

Once in Costinești, visitors can easily explore the town on foot or rent bicycles.

Where to Go Next from Costinești: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Costinești is in close proximity to many notable Romanian tourist destinations, making it an excellent base for nearby day trips.

Mangalia

Mangalia is a historical city located in the Southern part of Romania, offering a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture and a glimpse into Romanian history, along with beaches and seaside restlessness.

Constanta

Constanta is the closest significant city to Costinești and a popular tourist destination. It is home to many historic landmarks such as the iconic Casino building and the Roman ruins at the archaeological park.

Conclusion

Costinești is an unmissable hidden gem in Northern Dobruja filled with spectacular scenic views, compelling history and traditions, and a buzzing culture scene. Whether you are into outdoor activities or prefer a more relaxed experience, Costinești offers something for everyone. Book your trip for a memorable vacation today!
