Introduction to Belgrade

Belgrade, the capital city of Serbia, is situated at the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers in the Balkans. This historic city has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore its museums and historical buildings, enjoy the outdoor adventures and scenic views, and relish its vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Belgrade

Visitors to Belgrade will never run out of things to do. From interesting museums to ancient historical landmarks, natural parks, and local experiences, the city has something for everyone.

  • Nikola Tesla Museum
  • Belgrade Fortress
  • Kalemegdan Park
  • St. Sava Temple
  • The Royal Palace

Local Culture and Customs of Belgrade

Belgrade has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Serbs are traditional people with a significant respect for their history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Belgrade

The best time to visit Belgrade is from April to October when the weather is sunny and pleasant enough for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belgrade

Do:

  • Learn a few Serbian phrases
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious landmarks
  • Respect the locals

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without asking for permission
  • Tilt your head up when drinking rakija

Safety and Health

Safety in Belgrade

Belgrade is generally a safe city, but visitors should be cautious of pickpocketing and scamming incidents around tourist attractions and crowded areas.

Health in Belgrade

Healthcare in Belgrade is adequate, and there are many public and private medical centers offering various services.

Getting to Belgrade: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belgrade is accessible from various corners of the world through its international airport and reliable public transport system.

How to Get Around in Belgrade

Getting around Belgrade is easy using public transportation systems such as buses and trains. Visitors can also hire a taxi, rent a car or use a bike-sharing system.
