Introduction to Prijepolje

Prijepolje is a small town located in the Podrinje region of Serbia, in the Balkans, in Europe. Although not a well-known tourist destination, it offers a beautiful setting for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and skiing. In addition to its natural beauty, the town boasts a range of cultural and historical attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to explore Serbia beyond its main cities.

List of Places to Visit in Prijepolje

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Prijepolje has a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical sites that are worthy of a visit. One of the most well-known museums is the Museum of Prijepolje, which houses a collection of archaeological, ethnographic, and artistic artifacts from the local area. Other noteworthy attractions include:

  • The Church of St. George, a medieval church located in the town center.

  • The Mileševa Monastery, a 13th-century monastery situated about 25 kilometers from Prijepolje. The monastery is renowned for its frescoes, including the famous “White Angel” painting.

  • The Turkish bathhouse, known as the “hammam”, which dates back to the Ottoman period.

Natural Attractions of Prijepolje: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Prijepolje is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. One of the best places to explore is the Tara National Park, which features pristine forests, deep canyons, and pristine lakes. Visitors can hike the many trails, go rafting on the Tara River, or simply take in the stunning views.

Other natural attractions in Prijepolje include:

  • The Uvac Special Nature Reserve, which is home to endangered species such as the Griffon vulture. Visitors can take a boat tour of the Uvac Canyon and see the birds in their natural habitat.

  • The Gradina Cave, which is one of the largest caves in Serbia. The cave features stunning stalagmites and stalactites, and has been inhabited by humans since prehistoric times.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prijepolje

Prijepolje is an excellent destination for hikers and bikers, with a range of trails to suit all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Hajla mountain trek, which takes you up to the summit of Hajla mountain and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • The Pobijenik mountain trek, which is a more challenging trail that takes you through the forest and up to the peak of Pobijenik mountain.

  • The Tara River Canyon bike trail, which follows the path of the river and takes you through some of the most scenic parts of Tara National Park.

Best Things to Do in Prijepolje: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Prijepolje. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Going rafting on the Tara River, which is one of the best spots for whitewater rafting in Europe.

  • Taking a boat tour of the Uvac Canyon to see the Griffon vultures up close.

  • Visiting local farms to try traditional dairy products such as cheese and yogurt.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prijepolje

For those who want to explore the best of Prijepolje, here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Prijepolje, including the Museum of Prijepolje and the Church of St. George.

  • Day 2: Go hiking in the Tara National Park.

  • Day 3: Visit the Mileševa Monastery and the Turkish bathhouse.

  • Day 4: Take part in a rafting excursion on the Tara River.

  • Day 5: Go on a bike tour of the local area.

  • Day 6: Visit the Uvac Special Nature Reserve and take a boat tour of the canyon.

  • Day 7: Relax and sample local food and drinks, such as cheese and yogurt.

Food and Drink Scene of Prijepolje

Must-Try Dishes

Prijepolje has a rich culinary tradition, with many dishes that are unique to the local area. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Kajmak, a type of cream made from boiled milk that is often served with bread.

  • Kačamak, a dish made from cornmeal and cheese that is typically eaten for breakfast.

  • Čorba od đuge, a hearty soup made from beef and vegetables.

Local Beverages

In addition to the food, Prijepolje is known for its excellent wine and brandy. Some of the best local specialties include:

  • Knjaževača, a type of brandy made from plums.

  • Žuti cvet, a white wine that is produced locally.

Where to Stay in Prijepolje: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prijepolje has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Hotel Bistra, a four-star hotel located in the heart of the town.

  • The Guesthouse Jankovic, a family-run guesthouse just outside of town that offers comfortable rooms and traditional cuisine.

  • The Hostel Smiljka, a budget-friendly hostel that is popular with backpackers.

What to Buy in Prijepolje: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring back from Prijepolje include:

  • Traditional dairy products such as cheese and yogurt.

  • Local wines and brandies.

  • Handmade crafts such as pottery and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Prijepolje

Prijepolje has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions that are unique to the local area. Visitors should be sure to respect these customs, which include:

  • Dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Removing shoes before entering someone’s home.

  • Greeting people with a handshake or kiss on the cheek.

Best Time to Visit Prijepolje

The best time to visit Prijepolje is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the hiking trails are at their best. However, winter can also be a great time to visit, especially for skiing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prijepolje

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Take part in outdoor activities such as hiking and rafting.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.

  • Visit in winter without proper gear if planning on outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prijepolje

Prijepolje is a generally safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions and be aware of their surroundings. Some tips to stay safe include:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas at night.

  • Be cautious when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or rafting.

Health in Prijepolje

Prijepolje has a modern healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located in the town. However, visitors should still take precautions to protect their health, such as:

  • Ensuring that all vaccinations are up to date.

  • Drinking bottled or purified water.

  • Protecting against ticks and other insect bites when hiking in wooded areas.

Getting to Prijepolje: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prijepolje

The nearest airport to Prijepolje is the Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport, which is located approximately 250 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or rent a car to get to Prijepolje.

Bus Stations Near Prijepolje

There are several bus stations in Prijepolje that offer connections to other parts of Serbia and beyond. Some of the most popular routes include Belgrade, Novi Sad, and Sarajevo.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Prijepolje by train, although the journey can be slow and is not always very comfortable. Alternatively, it is possible to rent a car and drive to Prijepolje from other parts of Serbia or neighboring countries.

How to Get Around in Prijepolje

The best way to get around Prijepolje is by car, as public transport can be limited. Visitors can rent a car from one of the rental agencies in town, or from the airport in Belgrade.

Where to Go Next from Prijepolje: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Prijepolje is located in a beautiful part of Serbia, with several other destinations nearby that are worth exploring. Some of the best places to visit include:

  • Zlatibor, a popular mountain resort that is known for its ski slopes and natural beauty.

  • Užice, a town located about 50 kilometers from Prijepolje that boasts several historical landmarks, including the Ottoman-style Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge.

  • Čačak, a city located about 80 kilometers from Prijepolje that has a rich cultural heritage, including several museums and art galleries.

Conclusion

Prijepolje is a hidden gem in Serbia, offering a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in hiking, rafting, or simply exploring local museums and historical sites, this town has something for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you have all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Prijepolje.
