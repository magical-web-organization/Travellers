Introduction to Užice

Užice, located in Podrinje region of Serbia, is a charming city with a rich history and cultural heritage. The city is surrounded by gorgeous landscapes, including the Dinaric Alps and the Zapadna Morava River. With its well-preserved ancient architecture, excellent museums, and natural beauty, Užice is a must-visit destination.

This travel guide aims to provide you with a comprehensive tour of the city. It includes a list of the top places to visit, activities to do, where to stay, and what to eat. Let’s explore!

List of Places to Visit in Užice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kadinjača Memorial Complex: This memorial complex is dedicated to the Battle of Kadinjača, which was fought in November 1941 during World War II. The complex includes a memorial park, the tomb of soldiers, and the museum.
  • National Museum Užice: This museum is dedicated to the history of Užice and its surroundings. It has a vast collection of archaeological and ethnographic artifacts, as well as art exhibits.
  • Old Town: The Old Town of Užice contains several historical buildings, including the Tower of Užice, the Ducal Court, and the Stari Grad Museum.
  • Museum of Karakaj: This small museum displays the traditional way of life of a typical Serbian family in the 19th century. You can also try out traditional crafts such as weaving and pottery-making.

Natural Attractions of Užice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zlatibor: A mountainous area that offers beautiful views, hiking trails, and skiing in the winter.
  • Uvac Special Nature Reserve: This reserve is known for its meandering river and unique landscapes. Visitors can go on a boat tour to see the impressive cliffs and caves.
  • Tara National Park: A national park located in the Dinaric Alps that offers adventure sports such as rafting, kayaking, and canyon hiking. It also has some of the best viewpoints in Serbia.
  • Stopica Cave: An impressive underground cave with large halls and narrow passages. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the cave’s history and geological features.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Užice

Užice is an excellent destination for those who love outdoor activities. The city has some fantastic hiking and biking routes that lead through beautiful landscapes. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Zlatibor Circular Route: A circular route that takes you through forests, pastures, and charming villages. The trail is around 50km long and is suitable for intermediate bikers and hikers.
  • Tara National Park Trails: Tara National Park has over 100km of hiking trails that lead through stunning landscapes, including canyons, waterfalls, and forests. Some of the best routes include the Banjska Stena Trail and the Crveni Potok Trail.
  • Kapaonik Mountain: Located around 30km from Užice, Kapaonik Mountain is an excellent destination for winter sports, hiking, and biking. The mountain has several trails of different difficulty levels, including the Pancicev Vrh Trail, which is the longest trail (around 25km long) and offers fantastic views of the surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Užice: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of the Old Town: Join a guided tour of Užice’s Old Town and learn about the city’s history and culture.
  • Go on a food tour: Taste the local cuisine and drinks on a food tour and discover the traditional dishes of Užice.
  • Visit the local markets: Explore the colorful markets of Užice, where you can find traditional crafts and local products.
  • Attend a festival: Užice hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Autumn Salon festival and the Ethno Market Festival.
  • Relax in one of the city’s spas: Užice is famous for its thermal spas, where you can rejuvenate and relax amid stunning natural landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Užice

If you have a week to spare, here’s an itinerary that will cover the best attractions of Užice:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the Old Town and visit the National Museum Užice and the Kadinjača Memorial Complex.
  • Day 3-4: Visit the natural attractions of Užice, including Tara National Park and Uvac Special Nature Reserve.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a food tour and try some local dishes and drinks.
  • Day 6: Take a guided tour of Kapaonik Mountain and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate in one of the city’s thermal spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Užice

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kajmak: A traditional Serbian dairy product that tastes like a combination of cheese, cream, and butter. It’s often served with bread or meat.
  • Cevapi: Grilled minced meat served with onions, kajmak, and lepinja (a type of bread).
  • Pljeskavica: A type of Serbian hamburger, made with beef or pork.
  • Urnebes: A popular salad made with shredded cheese, peppers, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija: A traditional Serbian brandy made from fruit (plum, apricot, pear, etc.).
  • Jelen Pivo: A popular Serbian beer with a distinct taste.
  • Vranac: A red wine produced in the Balkans, known for its rich taste and aroma.

Where to Stay in Užice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Užice has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Zlatibor: A four-star hotel located in the heart of Užice, offering comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Hotel Oxa: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Hostel Podrum: A cozy hostel located in the Užice Old Town.
  • Vila Dijana: A guesthouse located in a quiet area near the city center. It offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Užice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: Užice is famous for its traditional pottery, and you’ll find shops selling unique handmade pieces.
  • Local honey: Serbia is known for its high-quality honey, and Užice has some of the best.
  • Rakija: Bring home a bottle of traditional Serbian brandy as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Užice

Užice has a rich cultural heritage and a unique way of life. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Serbians are known for their hospitality, so expect to be welcomed warmly.
  • Smoking is allowed in some restaurants and cafes, so keep that in mind if you’re a non-smoker.
  • The Serbian Orthodox Church is an important part of the culture, and many people observe religious customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Užice

The best time to visit Užice is from May to September, when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re a winter sports enthusiast, then December to February is the ideal time for skiing and snowboarding on the nearby mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Užice

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Visit the natural attractions of Užice.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Ignore the local customs and practices.
  • Be rude to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Užice

Užice is generally a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid any incidents. Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas, and avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.

Health in Užice

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in Užice, and most health services are of high quality. It’s recommended that visitors have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Užice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Užice

The nearest airport to Užice is the Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport, around 150km away. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to Užice.

Bus Stations Near Užice

Užice has a well-connected bus station, with regular services to other major cities in Serbia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Train travel is also a viable option, with a direct train service from Belgrade to Užice.

How to Get Around in Užice

Užice is a small city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, if you prefer public transportation, there are several buses and taxis that you can use.

Where to Go Next from Užice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Užice is an ideal base to explore other parts of Serbia. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Belgrade: Serbia’s capital city, known for its vibrant nightlife and cultural heritage.
  • Novi Sad: A charming city located on the Danube River, famous for its fortress and music festivals.
  • Mokra Gora: A village located in the Dinaric Alps that offers stunning views, train rides, and a historical museum.

Conclusion

Užice is a hidden gem of Serbia, offering a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Explore the Old Town, try the local cuisine, and visit the stunning natural attractions of the city. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your Užice adventure.
