Introduction to Novi Sad

Located in the region of Vojvodina, Serbia, Novi Sad is a charming city with a mix of cultures and beautiful landscapes. Known as the “Athens of Serbia,” Novi Sad is often overshadowed by its capital, Belgrade, but it is definitely worth a visit.

List of Places to Visit in Novi Sad

Novi Sad is a city with an impressive history, stunning architecture, and natural attractions. Here are some must-visit places in Novi Sad:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Petrovaradin Fortress: Explore the famous fortress with breathtaking views of the Danube River and the city.
  • Museum of Vojvodina: Discover the history, art, and culture of the Vojvodina region.
  • Rajkova Pecina: Excavated Roman site with frescoes and mosaics.
  • Synagogue: a beautiful historic building dating back to 1909 with stunning architectural details

Natural Attractions of Novi Sad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fruska Gora National Park: Enjoy nature and go hiking, biking, or bird watching.
  • Danube Park: This park located in the center of Novi Sad, is perfect for a leisurely stroll and to escape the city’s hustle and bustle.
  • Sremski Karlovci vineyards: Sample delicious wine, while admiring the vineyards’ beautiful scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novi Sad

Novi Sad is an ideal place for those who enjoy being surrounded by nature while engaging in outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Some of the popular routes of Novi Sad are:

  • Fruska Gora Mountain trek
  • Danube riverbank biking

Best Things to Do in Novi Sad: Activities and Tours

  • Join a guided tour of Petrovaradin Fortress
  • Attend Exit Festival in July - great music and arts festival
  • Visit the Strand: a summertime hot spot with beaches, restaurants, and a swimming pool

Week-Long Itinerary for Novi Sad

Day 1: Arrival in Novi Sad, explore the city center Day 2: Excursion to Sremski Karlovci, visit vineyards and wine tasting Day 3: Discover the Petrovaradin Fortress Day 4: Hike in Fruska Gora National Park Day 5: Explore the Museum of Vojvodina
Day 6: Relax on the beach at the Strand Day 7: Departure from Novi Sad

Food and Drink Scene of Novi Sad

Serbian cuisine is a mix of Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, and Balkan influences, and Novi Sad is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cevapi: Grilled minced meat sausages on somun bread with onions, kajmak, and ajvar.
  • Pljeskavica: A Serbian-style burger, made with minced meat, onions, and spices.
  • Gibanica: A traditional Serbian pie with layers of phyllo pastry and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Rakia: Fruit brandy is an essential Serbian drink. Plum, pear, and grape rakias are very famous in Novi Sad.
  • Wine: Novi Sad is well-known throughout the world for its fantastic wines. Some must-try wines are Bermet and Traminac.

Where to Stay in Novi Sad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodations in Novi Sad, ranging from luxury hotels to affordable hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Vojvodina: A luxurious hotel in the city center, popular amongst couples and families.
  • Voyager Bed & Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse with friendly staff in the city center.
  • Chillton Hostel: A friendly hostel for budget travelers with a great location.

What to Buy in Novi Sad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bermet: A wine-based aperitif made with local herbs and spices. It’s a must-try and perfect gift for someone back home.
  • Delicacy: Try some traditional Serbian sauces like kajmak or ajvar, or various types of sausages or specialized sweet delicatessen.
  • Traditional Clothing: Folk costumes and specific embroidered patterns on skirts, shirts, and dresses that they can wear on special occasions in rural areas.

Local Culture and Customs of Novi Sad

Serbia is a country with a rich cultural background that has influenced the people of Novi Sad. Some of the customs to keep in mind while visiting Novi Sad are:

  • Greetings: Serbians tend to greet each other with three kisses on the cheek.
  • Dress Code: Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Hospitality: Serbian people are known for their kindness and warmth, expect to be invited and welcomed into homes of local people

Best Time to Visit Novi Sad

The best time to visit Novi Sad is from May to September when the weather is warm, and the days are longer. This period is also the most of the Croatian tourist destinations with plenty of visitors. For people who want to avoid such a crowd, it’s best to visit Novi Sad during April and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novi Sad

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks
  • Explore the city by foot to truly appreciate its beauty and atmosphere
  • Visit museums and learn about the city’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and culture
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended
  • Don’t ignore the locals when they offer their hospitality

Safety and Health

Safety in Novi Sad

Novi Sad is a generally safe city for travelers, but like any other city, pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas. Ensure you keep an eye on your belongings and avoid unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Novi Sad

Serbia has a well-functioning healthcare system, and medical facilities and doctors are available in Novi Sad. Ensure you have comprehensive travel insurance before you travel in case of emergency.

Getting to Novi Sad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Novi Sad

The Nikola Tesla International Airport in Belgrade is Serbia’s largest airport, located approximately 80 kilometers from Novi Sad. From there, you can take a pre-booked taxi or bus to reach Novi Sad city center.

Bus Stations Near Novi Sad

The central bus station is located in the city center of Novi Sad, and daily bus connections are available to all cities and towns in Serbia and some international destinations.

Other Options

Rent a car from Belgrade if you want to explore more of Serbia. The drive from Belgrade to Novi Sad takes around 1.5 hours.

How to Get Around in Novi Sad

Public buses are the most convenient way of traveling in Novi Sad. Tickets can be purchased from kiosks or onboard the bus. Taxis are also available for quick transport around the city.

Where to Go Next from Novi Sad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Belgrade: The capital of Serbia is only an hour’s drive from Novi Sad.
  • Sremski Karlovci: A picturesque town located a few kilometers from Novi Sad is known for its vineyards and historic landmarks.
  • Fruska Gora National Park: A nature retreat with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and bird watching.

Conclusion

Novi Sad is a hidden gem that’s just waiting to be explored. From stunning natural beauty to a fascinating history and culture, the city has it all. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, or world-class cuisine, Novi Sad has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover what makes this city truly special.
