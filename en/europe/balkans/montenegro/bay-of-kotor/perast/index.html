Perast is a picturesque village located in the heart of the Bay of Kotor, nestled between stunning mountains and crystal-clear waters. This small town boasts a rich culture, history, and architecture that has earned it a special place on the map of Montenegro. With this comprehensive travel guide to Perast, we will help you plan your perfect trip, highlighting the best things to do, places to visit, and sights to see.

Introduction to Perast

Perast is a charming coastal town located on the Adriatic Sea, in the heart of the Bay of Kotor. It is known for its picturesque waterfront, beautiful churches, and stunning mountain views. The town is small, with a population of only 350 people, which contributes to its intimate and peaceful atmosphere. As you wander through the town’s narrow streets, you will feel the authentic Mediterranean vibes of this hidden gem of Montenegro.

List of Places to Visit in Perast

Perast has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historical landmarks, architecture, and museums. The following are some must-visit places while in Perast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Our Lady of the Rocks - a unique artificial island with a stunning 16th-century church and a museum.
  • St. Nikola’s Church - this Baroque-style church has stunning views of the Bay and is home to several important paintings and frescoes.
  • Bujovic Palace - a stunning mansion dating back to the 17th century that today houses the town’s museum.

Natural Attractions of Perast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vrmac Hill - if you are a hiking enthusiast, then Vrmac Hill is an ultimate destination for you. Known for its stunning vistas, this hill offers mesmerizing views of the Bay of Kotor.
  • St. Elijah Hill - this hilltop offers an unforgettable sunset view of the Bay of Kotor.
  • Island of St. George - the island is a natural reserve perfect for nature lovers and offers an excellent opportunity for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perast

For outdoor enthusiasts visiting the city, Perast has numerous hiking and biking routes for its visitors. The following are a few of the popular routes:

  • Vrmac Hill Hiking Trail - a 10km circular trail that starts from the old town of Kotor and takes you to the top of Vrmac Hill and back.
  • St. Elijah Hill Hiking Trail - a 4 km hike offering breathtaking views of the Bay of Kotor and the surrounding mountains.
  • Island of Our Lady of the Rocks Hike - a 1 km hike, including a ferry ride to the island, where you can explore the church and museum.

Best Things to Do in Perast: Activities and Tours

Perast offers unique experiences for its visitors, from exploring its beautiful landmarks to discovering its local cuisine. The following are some of the must-do activities and tours:

  • Boat Tours of the Bay of Kotor - this popular tour allows you to discover the bay and its stunning vistas, swim in its clear waters, and observe the dolphins born in Bigova Bay.
  • Traditional Cooking Classes - discover the secrets of Montenegro’s traditional cuisine by learning from the locals themselves.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling - this activity will take you to the hidden treasures of the underwater world of the Bay of Kotor.

Food and Drink Scene of Perast

Perast offers an incredible culinary experience, with a mix of Mediterranean and Balkan cuisine. The following are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Octopus - a local specialty, grilled to perfection with parsley, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Cevapi - a Balkan classic of grilled minced meat served on pita bread with onions and cheese.
  • Montenegrin lamb - slow-cooked lamb, served with vegetables and potato puree.

Local Beverages

  • Vranac - an indigenous red wine of Montenegro, with a full-bodied and fruity flavor.
  • Rakija - a strong spirit made from distilled fruits, including grapes, plums, or apricots.
  • Kafa - a traditional Turkish coffee served with sugar and water.

Where to Stay in Perast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perast provides a variety of accommodation options suitable for all budgets, from luxury seafront hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are a few of the popular options:

  • Hotel Conte - a luxurious 4-star hotel with an unbeatable location and breathtaking views of the sea.
  • Palace Jelena Rooms - a charming, traditional guesthouse housed in a 400-years-old building.
  • Apartments Vjera - situated in the heart of the old town, this cozy apartment is perfect for those on a budget.

What to Buy in Perast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Perast

Perast is a town proud of its rich culture, and visitors have the opportunity to witness its traditional celebrations throughout the year. Local customs also include:

  • Residents dress in navarro (traditional dress) for formal events.
  • The Carnival known as “Fasinada” takes place every July 22nd, where local boats are filled with stones to create a permanent base to expand the coast next year.

Best Time to Visit Perast

The best time to visit Perast is from May to September, when the weather is at its best, and the town hosts numerous festivals and cultural events. July is an especially buzzing month with the Fasnada Carnival taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perast

Do:

  • Do dress modestly when visiting churches.
  • Do try the local cuisine and visit the local market.
  • Do take a boat tour to discover the Bay of Kotor.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perast

Perast is one of the safest towns in Montenegro, and the crime rate is very low. However, tourists should always be cautious, with common-sense precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Perast

Montenegro has a good standard of healthcare, but it is always advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting the country. Visitors should also be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles and tetanus before traveling to Montenegro.

How to Get Around in Perast

Perast is a small town that is easily accessible by foot. However, for those who want to explore further, renting a bike or car is an excellent way to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Perast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perast is an excellent base for exploring the neighboring towns and national parks. Some of the popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Kotor - a picturesque fortified town located only 8 kilometers from Perast.
  • National Park Durmitor - a national park renowned for its stunning landscapes and hiking trails.
  • Budva - a lively coastal town famous for its nightlife and beaches.

Perast is a hidden gem of Montenegro that offers a unique cultural and natural experience for its visitors.
