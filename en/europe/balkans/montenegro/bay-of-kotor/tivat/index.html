Introduction to Tivat

Tivat is a charming coastal town located in the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro, a hidden gem of the Balkans. It is a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful and relaxing getaway. The town offers a unique blend of historic appeal, natural beauty, and exciting activities that can keep travelers of all ages and interests entertained. Tivat is also easily accessible, making it an excellent base for exploring Montenegro’s other famous attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Tivat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Naval Heritage Collection - A museum with an impressive collection of boats, planes, and other exhibits related to Montenegro’s naval history.
  • Buca Summer Residence - A beautifully preserved 18th-century mansion-turned-museum, housing a collection of historic artifacts and art pieces.
  • Church of St. Roko - A stunning church known for its intricate frescoes and impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tivat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Flower Island - A small island located in the Bay of Kotor, famous for its exotic flowers and breathtaking views.
  • Vrmac Ridge - A popular destination for hiking and trekking, offering spectacular views of Tivat and the Adriatic Sea.
  • Kotor Bay - The majestic bay is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and its serene beauty can be enjoyed through sailing or kayaking tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tivat

  • Tivat is surrounded by scenic hills and mountains, and there are many excellent hiking and biking routes for travelers to explore. One of the most popular is the Vrmac Ridge trail, which offers a challenging yet rewarding trek with panoramic views of the bay and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Tivat: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours - Take a boat tour of the coastline, exploring hidden coves, remote beaches, and historic towns.
  • Water Sports - From jet-skiing to parasailing, Tivat offers a range of thrilling water sports to keep adrenaline junkies entertained.
  • Wine Tasting - Sample some of the best wines of the Balkans by organizing a wine tasting tour to one of the local vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Tivat

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burek - A savory pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables, topped with a sprinkle of sesame seeds.
  • Cevapi - Grilled minced meat sausages typically served with pita bread, onion, and sour cream.
  • Kačamak - A hearty cornmeal porridge topped with cheese or sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija - A potent brandy, usually made with plums or grapes, commonly served as an aperitif or as a digestive after meals.
  • Vranac - A robust red wine made from local grapes, considered as Montenegro’s most emblematic wine.
  • Niksicko Pivo - A popular Montenegrin beer, perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Tivat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tivat offers a wide variety of accommodation options for all budgets, including charming guesthouses, cozy hostels, and luxurious hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Regent Porto Montenegro - A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Porto Montenegro, offering stunning views of the marina and the surrounding mountains.
  • Montenegrin Hostel Pomodora - A friendly and affordable hostel located in the center of town, offering comfortable rooms and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Villa Royal - A stylish guesthouse with gorgeously decorated rooms and breathtaking sea views.

What to Buy in Tivat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil - Montenegro is known for producing high-quality olive oil, which makes for an excellent gift or souvenir.
  • Handmade Crafts - From traditional woolen socks to hand-carved wooden figurines, Tivat has plenty of unique handicrafts to take home with you.
  • Local Wine - Montenegro produces some of the finest wines in the Balkans, and many wineries offer tasting tours, as well as the opportunity to purchase your favorite bottles to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tivat

Montenegrin culture is heavily influenced by its rich history and geographical location, with a blend of Eastern and Western European influences. The locals are warm and welcoming, and hospitality is highly valued. Traditionally, family and community hold significant importance, and many customs, such as music, dance, and religious celebrations, are still prevalent today.

Best Time to Visit Tivat

The best time to visit Tivat is from May to September, when the weather is sunny and warm, and the sea is perfect for swimming and water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tivat

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Attend the local festivals and celebrations.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect historic or religious sites.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public places.
  • Discuss politics or controversial topics with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tivat

Tivat is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. Nonetheless, it’s always best to exercise caution when traveling and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Tivat

Montenegro has a well-developed healthcare system, and Tivat has several modern medical facilities. Nonetheless, it’s always best to carry travel insurance and essential medications.

Getting to Tivat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tivat

The Tivat Airport, also known as “Golubovci Airport,” is a small yet efficient airport located just 5 kilometers away from the town center. It offers direct flights to various European cities, including Moscow, Belgrade, Vienna, and Istanbul.

Bus Stations Near Tivat

Tivat has a central bus station with regular bus routes to major Montenegrin cities, including Budva, Kotor, and Podgorica. Buses are generally clean, comfortable, and affordable.

Other Options

Travelers can also consider driving to Tivat or taking a ferry from nearby Italian cities such as Ancona or Bari.

How to Get Around in Tivat

Tivat is a small and walkable town, but renting a car or scooter can provide greater flexibility and the opportunity to explore the scenic countryside. Taxis are also a convenient and affordable option.

Where to Go Next from Tivat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kotor - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its medieval old town and stunning bay views.
  • Budva - A lively coastal town with a beautiful beach and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Lovcen National Park - A stunning nature reserve located just an hour’s drive away from Tivat, with breathtaking views of the coast and the mountains.

Conclusion

Tivat is a Montenegrin gem that offers everything you need for a perfect holiday, from stunning natural beauty to exciting activities and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore historic sites, or experience adventure, Tivat has it all. So, pack your bags, and head to Tivat for the ultimate vacation experience.
