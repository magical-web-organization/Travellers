Explore Plav: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Nestled in the heart of the North Montenegrin Mountains, Plav is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Plav is the perfect destination for adventure seekers and culture buffs alike. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover all that Plav has to offer, from its must-see attractions to its finest restaurants and local customs.

Introduction to Plav

Plav is a stunningly beautiful small town surrounded by the Prokletije Mountains. Located in the north of Montenegro, near the borders with Kosovo and Albania, it is a gateway to the stunning Bjelasica Mountain range, the Prokletije Mountains themselves, and the beautiful Plav Lake. The historic town center is characterized by the Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian architecture and showcases the blend of cultures that has influenced the region.

List of Places to Visit in Plav

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The history and culture of Plav come to life through its many museums and historic sites. Here are some must-see attractions:

  • Plav Castle: a medieval fortress located on a hill towering above the town.
  • Plav Ethnographic Museum: displays artifacts of traditional Montenegrin life, including costumes, folk music, and local crafts.
  • Plavski muzej: showcases the history and culture of the local Kelmendi tribe.
  • Grlja Waterfall: a stunning natural wonder located in the National Park of Prokletije Mountains.

Natural Attractions of Plav: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Plav is famous for its stunning natural scenery and outdoor adventures that visitors can enjoy. Here are some must-see natural attractions:

  • Plav Lake: the largest lake in Montenegro, set amid the beautiful mountain landscape, offers an excellent spot for water sports and fishing enthusiasts.
  • Prokletije National Park: contains one of the most spectacular and untouched mountain ranges in Europe characterized by craggy peaks, wildlife, and stunning glaciers.
  • Visitors Center Plav: exhibits the region’s flora and fauna, including rare bird species and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plav

Plav has always been famous for its hiking and biking trails, offering spectacular views and challenging terrain. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Hajla Mountain, a famous attraction, is the third-highest mountain in Montenegro. In summer, it transforms into a natural oasis with diverse plant species and animals like bear and wolves.
  • The Komovi Mountains, a beautiful hiking area, is best known for its scenic views, refreshing natural springs, and beautiful landscapes.
  • Visitors can also hire a bicycle and explore the mountainous terrain on one of the town’s marked trails.

Best Things to Do in Plav: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Plav offers a range of activities and tours, catering to all visitors’ tastes. Some of the best things to try include:

  • Rafting on the Lim River and kayaking on Plav Lake: adventurers can experience the full power of the local waters by cruising through the stunning landscapes for a unique and unforgettable trip.
  • Horseback riding: Enjoy the area’s scenic beauty on horseback while taking in the breathtaking mountain and valley views.
  • Fishing: Plav Lake is one of the most beautiful and bountiful fishing spots in Montenegro.

Food and Drink Scene of Plav

Plav’s cuisine is easily one of Montenegro’s most diverse and flavorful. The town offers plenty of delicious local dishes and beverages, reflecting its blend of cultures. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cicvara, a cornmeal-based dish with a creamy texture, is a traditional Montenegrin breakfast and is often served with milk
  • Montenegrin lamb, roasted with potatoes and vegetables
  • Kacamak- is made of cornmeal and cheese and goes well with cooked meat or beef soup.
  • Burek- a pastry filled with cheese, meat, or potato and often popular as a breakfast dish

Local Beverages

  • Rakija is the most popular alcoholic beverage in Montenegro and often find in various flavors such as grape, quince, and plum brandy.
  • Wine- Montenegro offers a range of locally produced wines, featuring a dry and fruity flavor.
  • Locally produced beer is another exciting option with Zolta Piva and Trebjesa Piva being the most famous and easily available in restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Plav: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Plav offers several accommodation options, such as hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors can choose from a wide range of price points and amenities.

Hotels

  • Hotel Kula Damjanova
  • Hotel Džogazi, located on the shore of Plav Lake, offering stunning views and modern amenities
  • Hotel Boračko Jezero, a peaceful escape nested in the beautiful natural scenery of the Plav landscapes

Hostels

  • Hostel Kula, located in the town center and with easy access to local attractions
  • Hostel Popovic, a spacious and comfortable hostel located in a quiet area of the town

Guesthouses

  • Guesthouse Muzlijada, often referred to as one of Plav’s hidden gems, offering stunning views and hospitality
  • Guesthouse Plavski Gusar, located near the oldest restaurant in the town, offering comfortable rooms and delicious meals.

What to Buy in Plav: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Plav’s vibrant culture comes alive through its souvenirs and local products. Visitors can take a piece of Plav back home as a memento. Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Locally made cheese: Montenegro offers some of the best cheese in the region, such as Njeguci cheese, cheese from Pljevlja, and cheese from Berane.
  • Handmade crafts: Visitors can buy Knitting products, embroidery, and traditional Montenegrin costumes
  • Honey: Montenegro production is famous for its unique taste, quality, and health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Plav

Plav’s culture has been shaped by numerous ethnic groups over the centuries, providing a unique and colorful blend of traditions and customs. Visitors should be aware of the following local culture and customs:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, such as the use of hand-gesturing instead of shaking hands
  • Learn a few local phrases as locals appreciate it when foreigners make an effort to learn their language.

Best Time to Visit Plav

Plav can be visited all year round as each season offers different experiences. However, the best time to visit would be from June to September, when the weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy hikes and outdoor activities amid the stunning landscape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plav

Do:

  • Respect locals and their customs
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Explore the town on foot or by bike to experience the beauty of its natural surroundings.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and equipment for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites and locations
  • Engage in activities that harm the environment, like littering.
  • Take pictures of strangers without their consent.

Safety and Health

Plav, like most European towns, is generally safe for tourists. Visitors are urged to take regular safety precautions such as any other town’s big or small by keeping a close eye on their belongings and not engaging in reckless behavior.

Safety in Plav

  • Visitors should remain vigilant and avoid walking alone at night, particularly in remote and secluded areas.
  • Avoid exchanging money on the street or with strangers. Only use reputable exchange points
  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas like markets or tourist sites.

Health in Plav

  • Visitors should inquire about necessary vaccinations before the trip
  • Plav’s tap water is safe to drink, but visitors might prefer to purchase bottled water instead.

Getting to Plav: Transportation Options and Logistics

Plav is well-connected with various transportation options to reach it easily.

Airports Near Plav

The nearest airports are located in Podgorica and Tirana. Podgorica Airport is the most convenient as it’s served by several international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Plav

Buses from Podgorica and other major Montenegrin cities run regularly.

Other Options

  • Visitors can rent a car and drive to Plav as it offers the flexibility to explore the region at their own pace.
  • Taxi services are available from the airport and major cities.

How to Get Around in Plav

Plav is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use taxi and car rental services to reach more remote locations nearby.

Where to Go Next from Plav: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors looking to explore more of Montenegro or other parts of the Balkans from Plav will have numerous nearby destinations and excursions.

  • Zabljak, located at the heart of the Durmitor Mountains, is a popular year-round destination for hiking, skiing, and outdoor activities
  • Berane, located just 20kms from Plav, is another picturesque town famous for its traditional architecture and incredible natural scenery.
  • Tirana, the capital of Albania, is easily accessible from Plav and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and food.

Conclusion

Plav is a stunning and magical destination that will leave travelers with unforgettable memories. Its stunning natural scenery and unique blend of culture and heritage make it one of Europe’s best-kept secrets. This comprehensive travel guide provides all the information visitors need to explore Plav and experience its beauty and warmth to their fullest. With this guide in hand, guests can be sure of having a memorable and enjoyable trip.
