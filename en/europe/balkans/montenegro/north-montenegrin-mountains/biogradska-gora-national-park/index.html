Introduction to Biogradska Gora National Park

Biogradska Gora National Park is a protected area in Montenegro’s northwestern region of Bjelasica. It was founded in 1952 to safeguard the vast forestland and the natural resources that the park offers. Biogradska Gora National Park is one of the largest national parks in Montenegro, covering an area of 54 square kilometres. It is also one of the few remaining primeval forests in Europe, featuring a diverse range of flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Biogradska Gora National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Biogradska Lake: The park’s number one attraction, a magnificent glacial lake surrounded by dense forest. It’s also possible to hike around the lake.
  • The Biogradska Gora Visitor centre: The visitor center offers exhibitions on the biodiversity of the park, fauna, and flora. Moreover, the attraction offers guided tours that explain the park’s history
  • Ethnographic Museum: The museum located in a traditional Montenegrin house. It features events, customs, and traditions of the area’s past and present inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Biogradska Gora National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hiking: The park’s over 30 kilometer hiking trails, all of which are well-marked and maintained. The trails offer a chance to explore the park’s natural beauty, including its lakes, rivers, and moors.
  • Biking: The biking trails inside the park offer stunning views and are an excellent way to explore the park.
  • Canopy walk in Biogradska Gora - The walkway consists of five hanging glass-walled platforms that allow visitors to get an eagle’s eye view of the National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biogradska Gora National Park

The park has many hiking and biking trails, ranging from leisurely strolls to more challenging treks. Some of the most popular hikes and bike routes include:

  • Biogradska Lake trail - 3 km trail around the lake
  • Crna Glava- Djurdjevik pass trail
  • Zekova Glava trek - the second-highest spot in Bjelasica

Best Things to Do in Biogradska Gora National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Jeep Safari tour - Take a jeep safari across the national park to experience the park’s biodiversity.
  • Fishing: Visitors can enjoy fishing in the Biogradska Lake and nearby rivers.
  • Camping: Camping is allowed in designated areas, making it an excellent opportunity for those looking for a more authentic and natural way of enjoying their stay.
  • Horse riding - Explore the national park’s trails on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biogradska Gora National Park

Day 1: Explore the National park’s museum and visitor center. Day 2: Take the Crna Glava- Djurdjevik pass trail. Day 3: Relax and fish at Biogradska lake. Day 4: Hike around Zekova Glava. Day 5: Take a jeep safari across the national park. Day 6: Explore the villages near Biogradska Gora National Park. Day 7: Enjoy your last day by horse riding.

Food and Drink Scene of Biogradska Gora National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cicvara: This dish made from corn flour, cream, and cheese
  • Kacamak: Similar to Cicvara. Made of corn flour and potatoes
  • Kačamak with kajmak or milk cream and cheese
  • Roasted lamb or pork - a traditional meal in Montenegro’s northern region

Local Beverages

  • Rakija: A traditional Balkan brandy made from fruits
  • Wine: Montenegro’s climate and soil are perfect for wine production, so don’t forget to try some of its local wine varieties.

Where to Stay in Biogradska Gora National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Biogradska Gora Lodge, located inside the park, offers visitors an opportunity of experiencing the park’s natural beauty.
  • Durmitor National Park Hotel: Located near the park, this hotel provides its guests with comfortable rooms and amenities.

What to Buy in Biogradska Gora National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey: Montenegro is known for its honey, so take home some jars as gifts for your friends and family.
  • Cheese: Biogradska Gora and nearby regions are known for their cheese production. Mlaisnica cheese is a specific type unique to the national park.

Local Culture and Customs of Biogradska Gora National Park

The traditional customs and lifestyle of the North Montenegrin Mountains’ inhabitants still exist in Biogradska Gora. Inhabitants of the area are known for their hospitality and kindness. Visitors ought to respect the people’s traditional ways of life.

Best Time to Visit Biogradska Gora National Park

The best time to visit the park depends on what activities you plan to engage in inside the park. The months of May-June and September- October when temperatures are mild and crowds fewer, might be perfect for more relaxed activities like hiking. However, if you’re looking to go fishing, the best time to visit is between June- August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biogradska Gora National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the park’s inhabitants.
  • Respect the park’s biodiversity and do not leave waste.
  • Take water and food with you when exploring the park.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that could alter the park’s ecosystems.
  • Deviate from marked hiking and biking trails.
  • Leave litter in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biogradska Gora National Park

Biogradska Gora National Park is a safe place to visit, but proceed with caution if exploring parts of the park beyond structured routes. Visitors should also be aware that some wild animals might roam the park, such as bears and wolves.

Health in Biogradska Gora National Park

Visitors should ensure they drink plenty of water since the park experiences high temperatures. Also, campers should take precautions against insect and animal bites. It is also important to carry necessary medication, such as antihistamines and painkillers, if necessary.

Getting to Biogradska Gora National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biogradska Gora National Park

The closest airports are Podgorica Airport and Tivat Airport, both approximately 100km away.

Bus Stations Near Biogradska Gora National Park

Visitors may catch a bus in the city of Kolasin, headed towards Plav. Get off at the Lake Biogradska stop, and the park is 3 km away.

Other Options

There are car rentals in the area; hence driving to the park and other Montenegrin sites is an option.

How to Get Around in Biogradska Gora National Park

The park’s trails are well marked and maintained, providing visitors with various options for vehicles, hiking and biking trails, and horse riding. Mountain bikes can be rented on-site.

Where to Go Next from Biogradska Gora National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Skadar lake, which is located south of the park, offers breathtaking views and water-based attractions.
  • Durmitor, another national park located west of the park, is accessible by car and features numerous attractions and scenic views.

Conclusion

Biogradska Gora National Park is an exciting destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and cultural heritage lovers. From the vast primeval forests to the scenic views, to the adrenaline-filled activities, Biogradska Gora National Park offers something for everyone.
