Introduction to Bar

Located in the Montenegrin South Coast, Bar is the perfect place for those seeking an authentic Mediterranean experience with a twist of Balkan culture. The town offers a fascinating blend of ancient ruins, stunning beaches, and scenic landscapes that make it one of the most sought-after destinations in Montenegro.

This tour guide will take you on an exciting journey through the marvels of Bar and help you plan your trip accordingly. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Bar.

List of Places to Visit in Bar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bar’s incredible history dates back to the ancient times. The town has a rich cultural heritage, with many historical sites and museums that preserve this legacy. Here are some of the must-visit places in Bar:

  • Bar Castle: A picturesque fortress built in the 10th century during the Byzantine Empire. The castle affords stunning views of the town and the sea.

  • Stari Bar: A medieval town that was destroyed during the First World War. The town has been restored and is now a living museum that offers a glimpse into Montenegro’s past.

  • Archaeological Museum: This museum showcases the artifacts and history of the town and the surrounding area. A must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Bar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bar’s natural beauty is stunning, with pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rugged mountains. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Bar:

  • Lake Skadar: The largest lake in the Balkan Peninsula, Lake Skadar is located just a short drive from Bar. It offers visitors a chance to explore its tranquil waters, see an array of birds, and witness the rugged beauty of the mountains.

  • Sutomore Beach: A popular beach in Bar, Sutomore beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and stunning sunsets. It’s perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and paddleboarding.

  • Rumija Mountain: This stunning mountain range provides excellent hiking trails with panoramic views of the town and the sea. It’s an ideal destination for adventurers and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bar

Bar offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Montenegro. If you’re into exploring the great outdoors, then you’ll love the following routes:

  • The Rumija Mountain Trail: A moderate trail that offers panoramic views of the sea and the town. It’s perfect for both hikers and mountain bikers.

  • The Veleka Beach Trail: An easy trail that takes you through olive groves, serene beaches, and breathtaking views of the Adriatic Sea. An excellent trail for beginners and families.

Best Things to Do in Bar: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do in Bar:

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the coastline of Bar in a sea kayak and discover hidden coves, caves, and pristine beaches.

  • Wine Tasting: Montenegro is known for its wine, and Bar is no exception. Visit one of the local wineries, and discover the unique flavors of the region.

  • City Tour: Take a guided tour of the town, and learn about its rich history and culture. The tour includes visits to top attractions, such as the Castle of Bar and Stari Bar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bar

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to Bar:

Day 1: Arrival in Bar, check-in to your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town. Day 2: Visit the Castle of Bar and take a guided tour. In the afternoon, head to Sutomore beach for some relaxation. Day 3: Take a guided tour of Stari Bar and explore its ancient ruins. In the afternoon, visit the Archaeological Museum. Day 4: Hike the Rumija Mountain Trail and enjoy the breathtaking views. Day 5: Go sea kayaking and explore the coastline of Bar. In the afternoon, visit one of the local wineries for a wine tasting. Day 6: Take a day trip to Lake Skadar and explore its tranquil waters and lush vegetation. Day 7: Depart Bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Bar

Montenegro is famous for its Mediterranean cuisine, and Bar is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Bar:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cevapi: Grilled meat sausages served with pita bread and onions. It’s a popular street food in the Balkan region.

  • Grilled Seafood: Bar is known for its fresh seafood, including octopus, squid, and fish. It’s often grilled and served with lemon and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from fruit, such as plums, grapes, or raspberries.

  • Montenegrin Wine: Montenegro has many wineries that produce excellent wines, including Vranac, Plantaže, or Château Vodice.

Where to Stay in Bar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bar offers plenty of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bar:

  • Hotel Princess: Located in the heart of Bar, this four-star hotel offers comfortable rooms with stunning views of the sea.

  • Apartments Kalamper: These modern and spacious apartments are perfect for families or groups of friends. They are located just a few minutes walking distance from the beach.

  • Hostel Old Town Bar: If you’re on a budget, this affordable hostel offers dorm-style accommodation and is conveniently located in the historic center of Stari Bar.

What to Buy in Bar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bar has many local products that make excellent souvenirs or gifts. Here are some of the top products to bring home:

  • Olive Oil: Montenegro is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Bar has several producers that offer a variety of flavors.

  • Wine: Montenegro’s wines are unique, and you can find many local wineries that sell excellent quality wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Bar

Montenegro’s culture is an ancient mix of Byzantine, Venetian, and Ottoman influences, along with the traditional practices of the Balkan region. Here are some of the local cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Montenegrins appreciate greetings and formal pleasantries. It’s customary to greet someone before discussing business.

Best Time to Visit Bar

The best time to visit Bar is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Make sure to try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the historical sites and museums.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beach.
  • Don’t take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Don’t wear inappropriate clothing when visiting a religious site.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bar

Bar is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to stay safe in Bar:

  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs.
  • Be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Bar

Montenegro has excellent health care facilities, with hospitals and clinics located throughout the country. It’s recommended to have adequate travel insurance before visiting Bar in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Bar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bar

The nearest international airport to Bar is Podgorica Airport, located approximately 40 km from the town. Tivat Airport is another airport nearby and is approximately 60 km from Bar.

Bus Stations Near Bar

Bar has a modern bus station that serves destinations throughout Montenegro and neighboring countries. Buses run regularly to and from Podgorica, Budva, and Kotor.

Other Options

If you prefer to rent a car, there are several car rental companies in Bar that offer competitive rates. Taxis and private transfers are also available, and many hotels can arrange transportation for their guests.

How to Get Around in Bar

Bar is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot. In addition to walking, there are buses and taxis available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Bar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bar is located in the Montenegrin South Coast, which is dotted with charming towns and attractions. Here are some of the top destinations to visit near Bar:

  • Budva: A picturesque town with a medieval old town, sandy beaches, and a lively nightlife.
  • Kotor: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning medieval architecture, surrounded by towering mountains and calm bay waters.
  • Ulcinj: A lively town known for its beaches and historic castle, located only a short drive away from Bar.

Conclusion

Bar is a hidden gem in Montenegro that offers visitors a chance to discover ancient ruins, stunning natural landscapes, and unique Mediterranean cuisine. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, Bar has something for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Bar, Montenegro.
