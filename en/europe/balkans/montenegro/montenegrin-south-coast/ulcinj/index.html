Introduction to Ulcinj

Ulcinj is a charming coastal town located on the southern coast of Montenegro, in the Balkans region of Europe. Set against the stunning backdrop of the Adriatic Sea, Ulcinj boasts a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking scenery, and a warm, laid-back atmosphere.

The town is famous for its picturesque old town, sandy beaches, beautiful sunsets, and delicious seafood. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply seeking a relaxing break by the sea, Ulcinj offers something for everyone.

In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Ulcinj’s best attractions, outdoor adventures, food and drink scene, and local customs. We’ll also provide practical tips on getting around, staying safe, and making the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ulcinj

Discover the top places and things to do in Ulcinj with our comprehensive list of attractions and activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ulcinj has a rich history spanning more than 2,000 years, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore in the town:

  • Old Town of Ulcinj - A must-visit attraction, the old town of Ulcinj is a charming maze of narrow streets, beautiful squares, and historic buildings, including the 17th-century Balsic Tower and the 14th-century Ulcinj Castle.

  • Ethnographic Museum - Housed in a beautifully restored 18th-century house in the heart of the old town, this museum showcases the traditional way of life in Ulcinj.

  • Museum of Archaeology - This museum displays artifacts from Ulcinj’s ancient past, including Roman and Illyrian pottery, coins, and jewelry.

Natural Attractions of Ulcinj: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ulcinj is a paradise for nature lovers, with its stunning coastline, lush forests, and rolling hills. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the town:

  • Valdanos Bay - Located just outside Ulcinj, this secluded bay is surrounded by olive groves and blessed with crystal-clear waters, making it a perfect spot for swimming and sunbathing.

  • Ada Bojana Island - This idyllic island is situated at the mouth of the Bojana River, offering a pristine beach and a unique atmosphere. It’s also a popular spot for windsurfing and kitesurfing.

  • Lake Skadar National Park - A short drive from Ulcinj, this beautiful national park boasts stunning scenery and rich wildlife, including more than 260 bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ulcinj

Ulcinj and its surrounding areas offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include:

  • Bunave Hills - This scenic route takes you through forests and hills, offering stunning views of the Montenegrin coast.

  • Ulcinj Salinas - This flat and easy route is perfect for beginners, offering breathtaking views of Lake Skadar and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Ulcinj: Activities and Tours

When it comes to activities and tours, Ulcinj has plenty to offer from water sports to cultural experiences to off-the-beaten-path excursions:

  • Boat Tour to the Ada Bojana Island - Take a boat tour to this beautiful island and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Montenegrin coast.

  • Traditional Fishing Experience - Join the local fishermen and learn how to catch fish using traditional methods.

  • Birdwatching Tour - Explore the rich birdlife of Lake Skadar National Park with a guided birdwatching tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ulcinj

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Ulcinj:

  • Day 1: Explore the old town of Ulcinj and visit the Ethnographic Museum.
  • Day 2: Hike the Bunave Hills and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Day 3: Take a boat tour to the Ada Bojana Island and go windsurfing or kitesurfing.
  • Day 4: Visit the Museum of Archaeology and take a sunset stroll along the Valdanos Bay.
  • Day 5: Join a traditional fishing experience and learn about the local way of life.
  • Day 6: Take a birdwatching tour of Lake Skadar National Park and enjoy the breathtaking scenery.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beach and try some delicious seafood in one of the town’s many restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Ulcinj

Montenegro’s gastronomy is quite unique thanks to influences from neighboring Balkan countries and the Mediterranean. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ulcinj:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cevapcici - Grilled sausages served with bread and garnished with diced onions.
  • Burek - A pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Stuffed Bell Peppers - Bell peppers stuffed with rice, meat, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija - A strong brandy made from fermented fruit, usually plums or grapes.
  • Wine - Montenegro produces a variety of wines, including Vranac, a red wine made from the Vranac grape.
  • Beer - Nikšićko is the most popular beer in Montenegro.

Where to Stay in Ulcinj: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ulcinj offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste:

  • Hotel Palata Venezia - A historic hotel located in the heart of the old town, featuring beautiful rooms and stunning views of the sea.

  • Hotel Mediteran Ulcinj - A modern hotel located on the Valdanos Bay, offering comfortable rooms and a beachfront restaurant.

  • Hostel Pirate - A cozy hostel located in the old town, providing affordable accommodation and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ulcinj: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, here are some ideas:

  • Olive Oil - Montenegro produces some of the best olive oil in the world, and Ulcinj is home to many olive groves.

  • Salt - Ulcinj’s salt pans are famous for producing high-quality salt, which is an essential ingredient in many Montenegrin dishes.

  • Lacework - Ulcinj is known for its intricate lacework, which is handmade by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Ulcinj

Montenegrins are known for their hospitality and warmth. It’s essential when visiting Ulcinj, to respect the locals’ customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when outside beach or nightclub areas.

Best Time to Visit Ulcinj

The best time to visit Ulcinj is between June and September when the town is bursting with life, and the weather is sunny with occasional rains. During winter, fewer people visit, further reducing accommodations and activity options.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ulcinj

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Ulcinj:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when exploring the old town or visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink, such as cevapcici and rakija.

Don’t:

  • Litter and disrespect the environment.
  • Visit isolated beaches without proper safety measures.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Ulcinj is a relatively safe place with a low crime rate. However, there are some safety and health concerns to keep in mind:

Safety in Ulcinj

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended on the beach or in public places.
  • Take care when swimming in the sea, especially during strong currents.

Health in Ulcinj

  • Be sure to use sunscreen, as the sun can be strong.
  • Keep hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Watch out for ticks, which may carry diseases such as Lyme disease.

Getting to Ulcinj: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ulcinj is easily accessible from the surrounding areas by bus, taxi, or car:

Airports Near Ulcinj

  • Podgorica International Airport - approx. 1.5 hours’ drive from Ulcinj.

  • Tivat Airport - approx. 2 hours’ drive from Ulcinj.

Bus Stations Near Ulcinj

  • Ulcinj Bus Station - located in the town center, with regular connections to the rest of Montenegro.

  • Bar Bus Station - located approx. 25 minutes’ drive from Ulcinj, with connections to other Balkan countries.

Other Options

  • By car - Ulcinj is easily accessible from the major cities in Montenegro and the surrounding Balkan countries.

How to Get Around in Ulcinj

Ulcinj is a relatively small town and can be easily explored on foot. Nevertheless, to explore the surrounding areas and natural reserves, we recommend renting a car. There are also plenty of taxis and buses available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Ulcinj: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations worth visiting from Ulcinj include:

  • Budva - A bustling coastal town with medieval old town and great nightlife, just an hour’s drive from Ulcinj.

  • Kotor - A UNESCO-listed coastal town with stunning scenery and rich cultural heritage, located approx. 2.5 hours’ drive from Ulcinj.

Conclusion

Ulcinj is a hidden gem situated on the scenic southern coast of Montenegro. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality, this charming town has something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, a relaxing beach holiday, or a taste of local culture, Ulcinj is the perfect destination.
