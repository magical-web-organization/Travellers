Introduction to Montenegrin South Coast

Montenegrin South Coast is a beautiful destination in Montenegro and is located on the Balkans Peninsula in Europe. The South Coast region features stunning natural beauty, cultural history, and pristine beaches. The South Coast is a popular destination and attracts tourists from all over the world.

In this travel guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit in Montenegrin South Coast, along with recommendations for food, accommodation, and things to do. We will also provide valuable tips for travelers to make the most of their time in this stunning destination.

List of Places to Visit in Montenegrin South Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kotor Old Town: A UNESCO World Heritage site that offers visitors an insight into Montenegrin history and architecture. It is surrounded by a medieval wall, and the cobbled streets are lined with historic buildings, restaurants, and cafes.
  • Budva Old Town: Another UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to the 15th century, located on the coast. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views and explore the narrow streets, galleries and boutiques.
  • Cetinje: The royal capital of Montenegro, which is known for its historic landmarks, architecture, and museums, including King Nikola’s Palace, Cetinje Monastery, and the National Museum of Montenegro.
  • Perast: A small baroque town located near the Kotor Bay that is famous for its historic architecture and churches.

Natural Attractions of Montenegrin South Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lovćen National Park: A mountainous park that has a diverse range of flora and fauna species and is a popular destination for hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing.
  • Skadar Lake: The largest lake in the Balkans, located on the border of Montenegro and Albania. The lake is home to more than 260 bird species and offers stunning views, as well as boat rides and fishing tours.
  • Sveti Stefan: A small, picturesque island located on the coast near Budva that has become a luxury resort over time.
  • Ada Bojana: A river island that is known for its sandy beaches and rich vegetation, making it a popular destination for swimming, windsurfing, and kite-surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montenegrin South Coast

Montenegrin South Coast is an excellent destination for those who love outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • Vrmac Peninsula: A popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Bay of Kotor and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Krivosije: A challenging hiking route located in the northern part of Montenegro that attracts experienced hikers.
  • Rumija Mountain: A popular biking route that features uphill and off-road portions through stunning scenery, offering great views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Montenegrin South Coast: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Montenegrin South Coast, and some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can explore the stunning coastline of Montenegrin South Coast by boat, taking in the breathtaking views and visiting some of the smaller islands, such as Sveti Marko Island.
  • Wine Tours: Montenegrin South Coast is home to some of the best wineries in Montenegro. Visitors can take a wine tour and sample some of the region’s finest wines.
  • Kayaking: An excellent way to explore the coastline and beaches of Montenegrin South Coast, providing a unique perspective on the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montenegrin South Coast

Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip to Montenegrin South Coast:

Day 1: Kotor

Explore the ancient city walls and the atmospheric Old Town of Kotor. Have lunch by the sea and walk up the hill to see the breathtaking views of the bay.

Day 2: Lovćen National Park

Hike through the national park to explore the diverse flora and fauna. Enjoy the stunning views of the bay from the peak of Lovćen mountain.

Day 3: Budva and Perast

Visit Budva Old Town, followed by a trip to Perast, a charming town located on the coast, to visit its historic landmarks.

Day 4: Skadar Lake

Enjoy a boat ride on Skadar Lake, take in the beautiful scenery and learn about the local wildlife and fish species.

Day 5: Sveti Stefan

Visit the island and enjoy a day at the beach, trying out some of the water sports, such as windsurfing and kayaking.

Day 6: Ada Bojana

Spend a day enjoying the sandy beaches and lush vegetation of the Ada Bojana river island.

Day 7: Cetinje

Explore the royal capital of Montenegro, visiting King Nikola’s Palace, Cetinje Monastery, and the National Museum of Montenegro.

Food and Drink Scene of Montenegrin South Coast

Montenegrin cuisine is influenced by various cultures, including Mediterranean, Turkish, and Hungarian. Some of the must-try dishes in Montenegrin South Coast include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cevapi: Grilled meat sausages served in a warm flatbread, usually served with onion and sour cream.
  • Burek: A savory pastry filled with cheese, meat, or spinach, best served warm and fresh.
  • Riblja Čorba: A fish soup made with different types of fish and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Vranac: A dark, full-bodied red wine that is fermented on its skins.
  • Rakija: A strong fruit brandy made from various fruit, such as plum, pear, and grape.

Where to Stay in Montenegrin South Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montenegrin South Coast has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top-rated options include:

  • Hotel Splendid: A luxurious hotel located on the beach in Becici that offers stunning sea views, indoor and outdoor pools, and a spa.
  • Old Town Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Kotor’s Old Town that offers clean and comfortable dorms and private rooms.
  • Villa Stevanovic: A family-friendly guesthouse located in the small village of Petrovac that offers a peaceful and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Montenegrin South Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: Montenegrin South Coast is famous for its high-quality olive oil, which makes for an excellent souvenir and gift.
  • Traditional Clothing: Visitors can purchase traditional Montenegrin clothing, such as folk costumes and woolen caps.
  • Wine: Montenegrin South Coast produces some of the finest wines in the region, and a bottle of Vranac will make for an excellent souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Montenegrin South Coast

Montenegrin South Coast has a rich cultural heritage, which is influenced by different cultures. Some of the customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of include:

  • Hospitality: Montenegrin locals are known for their hospitality and welcome visitors with open arms.
  • Traditional Festivals: Montenegrins celebrate various traditional festivals, such as the Boka Night, which takes place in Kotor in August.
  • Religion: Orthodox Christianity is the dominant religion of the region, and visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Montenegrin South Coast

The best time to visit Montenegrin South Coast is during the summer months, between June and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, the shoulder season, from April to May and from September to November, can be a great time to visit, as the crowds are smaller, and the weather is still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montenegrin South Coast

Do:

  • Respect the Local Customs: Learn about the cultural customs and traditions and follow them when visiting religious sites and attending festivals.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Montenegrin South Coast has a range of delicious local dishes that visitors should try.
  • Take the Time to Relax: Montenegrin South Coast is a great destination to unwind and relax. Take the time to slow down and enjoy the beaches and stunning scenery.

Don’t:

  • Overdo the Sun: Be sure to apply sunscreen and wear sun protection when spending time on the beaches, as the sun can be strong.
  • Disrespect the Environment: Montenegrin South Coast has stunning natural beauty, and visitors should take care not to litter or disturb the ecosystem.
  • Use Offensive Language or Gestures: Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and avoid using offensive language or gestures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montenegrin South Coast

Montenegrin South Coast is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still take precautions, such as keeping belongings close, avoiding unlit areas at night, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Montenegrin South Coast

Montenegro has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical facilities in the main cities. Visitors should have adequate health insurance and be aware of the risks associated with the sun, food, and water.
