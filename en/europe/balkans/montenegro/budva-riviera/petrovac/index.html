Introduction to Petrovac

Located in the Budva Riviera region of Montenegro, Petrovac is a charming seaside town that boasts crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and beautiful scenery.

Historically, Petrovac was known as a small fishing village, but over time, it has evolved into a popular tourist destination for visitors from all over the world.

Whether you are looking for cultural attractions, natural wonders, adventure activities, or simply soaking up the sun on the beach, Petrovac is an excellent choice for your next vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Petrovac

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Petrovac is home to various historical and cultural attractions that are worth exploring.

One of the most popular is the Petrovac Fortress, which dates back to the 16th century and offers stunning views of the town and the coastline.

In addition to the fortress, the Olive Museum is a unique attraction that highlights the cultural significance of olive oil to the Montenegrin people.

Natural Attractions of Petrovac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Petrovac is not only blessed with beautiful beaches, but also with stunning natural landscapes.

A popular excursion from Petrovac is a visit to the Skadar Lake National Park, a vast area that offers a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking, and birdwatching.

For those who prefer a more relaxing outing, a visit to the nearby Island of Sveta Nedjelja is a must-do. The island has a tranquil atmosphere and is accessible by boat from Petrovac.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petrovac

For outdoor enthusiasts, there are many hiking and biking trails in the surrounding mountains. Mt. Lovcen National Park, is easily accessible by car and offers several hiking trails for all levels of fitness.

The Coastal Path is one of the most scenic trails in Montenegro. The trail takes you through the rocky coastline between the villages of Sveti Stefan and Buljarica, and allows you to witness the stunning sea views.

Best Things to Do in Petrovac: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, there are several must-do activities in Petrovac.

One of the popular activities is a boat tour to the nearby coves, islands, and beaches. You can take a scenic cruise from Petrovac harbor to explore the surrounding coastline, stopping in small coves for swimming and snorkeling.

Another activity worth experiencing is the Petrovac Aquarium. The aquarium hosts numerous fish species that are unique to the Adriatic sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Petrovac

Day 1: Explore Petrovac’s historical sites, visit the Petrovac Fortress, and Olive Museum.

Day 2: Take a boat excursion to nearby coves, islands, and beaches, including the Island of Sveta Nedjelja.

Day 3: Trek through the nearby mountains, such as Lovcen National Park.

Day 4: Relax on Petrovac’s beaches or take a coastal path walk to Sveti Stefan.

Day 5: Visit the Skadar Lake National Park and indulge in outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, or birdwatching.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as Budva Old Town or Kotor city.

Day 7: Enjoy local food or try some of the local wines on offer, or visit nearby cafés and bars to experience the local nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Petrovac

Must-Try Dishes

Montenegrin cuisine is heavily influenced by the Mediterranean flavors and seafood. The must-try dish while in Petrovac is the black risotto, a seafood-infused rice dish that is cooked with squid ink.

Other must-try dishes include the grilled fish and the cheese pie, which is a traditional pastry dish filled with cheese and spinach.

Local Beverages

Vranac, a red wine that is indigenous to the Balkans, is the most popular wine variety in Montenegro.

Other local spirits, such as the famous Rakija, a fruit brandy, and the Crnogorsko Pivo, a lager beer, are worth trying.

Where to Stay in Petrovac: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Petrovac, which cater to different budgets and preferences.

Some of the popular choices are the Hotel Danica, Hotel Palas, and the Montenegro Hostel.

What to Buy in Petrovac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping, visitors to Petrovac can find traditional local crafts, homemade olive oils, and other souvenir items at the many market stalls that line the streets.

Local Culture and Customs of Petrovac

The Montenegrin people are known for their hospitality and friendly nature. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs, such as the relaxed pace of life, and to take part in local festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Petrovac

The best time to visit Petrovac is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and the sea is calm. During this period, visitors can enjoy various festivals and events, including a seafood festival and a carnival parade.

Do’s and Don’ts of Petrovac

Do:

  • Try local dishes such as black risotto
  • Take a boat excursion around the coves, islands, and beaches
  • Take part in local festivals and celebrations

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites
  • Overdrink and engage in bad behavior in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Petrovac

Petrovac is generally safe, and visitors can move around without fear. However, normal precautions such as keeping valuables in a safe and avoiding dark and deserted areas at night should be followed.

Health in Petrovac

Medical facilities are available in Petrovac, and visitors are advised to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses before traveling.

Getting to Petrovac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Petrovac

The nearest airport to Petrovac is Tivat International Airport, which is about 35 kilometers away.

Other airports nearby are Podgorica International Airport and Dubrovnik International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Petrovac

Petrovac has a bus station that is well connected to major destinations in Montenegro, such as Bar, Budva, and Kotor.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Petrovac by taxi or car rental, with local car rentals available throughout the town.

How to Get Around in Petrovac

Because Petrovac is a walkable city, most of the major attractions are within walking distance. However, taxis, buses, and car rentals are available for those who prefer them.

Where to Go Next from Petrovac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations that are worth visiting include the Budva Old Town, Kotor City, Herceg Novi, and Skadar Lake National Park.

Conclusion

Petrovac is a stunning seaside town with natural attractions, cultural artifacts, delicious food, and beautiful beaches. It’s an ideal destination for visitors looking for a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. Plan your trip to Petrovac, and you’ll have an unforgettable experience in Montenegro.
