Introduction to Western Balkans

Western Balkans is a region located in southeastern Europe that comprises the territories of Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Kosovo, Montenegro, North Macedonia, and Serbia. The region boasts an impressive array of natural landscapes, ranging from rugged mountain ranges and deep river valleys to pristine beaches and turquoise lakes. Western Balkans is renowned for its rich historical and cultural heritage, with numerous ancient cities, monuments, and museums that are testimony to the region’s rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Western Balkans

Western Balkans offers a wealth of attractions that cater to a wide range of interests. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Western Balkans won’t disappoint you. The region is dotted with numerous cultural landmarks that provide a glimpse into the past and the rich heritage of the region. Here are some of the best historical places and museums to visit in Western Balkans:

  • National Museum of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Sarajevo
  • Museum of Yugoslavia, Belgrade
  • Bunkers of Albania, Tirana
  • Old Bridge Area of the Old City of Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina
  • Topkapi Palace, Peja, Kosovo

Natural Attractions of Western Balkans: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Western Balkans is blessed with a stunning natural landscape, encompassing majestic mountains, dense forests, sparkling lakes, and dazzling beaches. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Western Balkans:

  • Plitvice Lakes National Park, Croatia
  • Durmitor National Park, Montenegro
  • Valbona Valley National Park, Albania
  • Biogradska Gora National Park, Montenegro
  • Skadar Lake National Park, Montenegro

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Balkans

Western Balkans is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with some of the best hiking and biking trails in Europe. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Via Dinarica trail in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, and Croatia
  • Peaks of the Balkans trail in Albania, Kosovo, and Montenegro
  • Komovi Mountain Bike Trail, Montenegro
  • Velebit Mountain Trail, Croatia

Best Things to Do in Western Balkans: Activities and Tours

Western Balkans offers a wide range of activities to suit all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Visit the Old Bazaar in Skopje, North Macedonia
  • Explore the medieval fortified city of Kotor, Montenegro
  • Go on a wine tasting tour in Croatia or Serbia
  • Take a boat ride through the breathtaking Lake Ohrid, North Macedonia/Albania
  • Experience Albania’s pristine beaches in the Albanian Riviera

Week-Long Itinerary for Western Balkans

If you’re planning to spend a week in Western Balkans, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Start your trip in Dubrovnik, Croatia Day 2-3: Explore the stunning Plitvice Lakes National Park, Croatia Day 4: Visit the ancient city of Kotor, Montenegro Day 5: Experience the natural beauty of Valbona Valley National Park, Albania Day 6: Discover the vibrant city of Belgrade, Serbia Day 7: Conclude your trip by exploring Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Food and Drink Scene of Western Balkans

The Western Balkans region has a diverse culinary scene, with a wide range of traditional dishes and beverages that reflect the region’s cultural traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ćevapi (grilled minced meat) in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Serbia, and Montenegro
  • Roštilj (grilled meat) in Croatia
  • Kacamak (cornmeal-based dish) in Montenegro
  • Byrek (savory pastry) in Albania
  • Ajvar (roasted red pepper relish) in Serbia

Local Beverages

  • Rakija (fruit brandy) in all countries of Western Balkans
  • Red wine from Herzegovina and Montenegro
  • Beer, especially Nikšićko beer from Montenegro

Local Culture and Customs of Western Balkans

Western Balkans is rich in cultural traditions and customs that have evolved over centuries, reflecting the region’s diverse history and heritage. Here are some key cultural aspects to note:

  • Hospitality and generosity are highly valued in all countries in Western Balkans
  • Religion plays a significant role in the daily life of people in Western Balkans, with Orthodox Christianity, Catholicism, and Islam being the dominant religions
  • Traditional folk music and dances are an integral part of the region’s cultural heritage

Best Time to Visit Western Balkans

The best time to visit Western Balkans is during the shoulder season, from April to June and September to November, when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are fewer. However, if you’re looking for a beach holiday, July and August are the best months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western Balkans

When traveling to Western Balkans, here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few local phrases in the native language of the country you’re visiting
  • Tip 10-15% in restaurants

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Criticize a person’s religion or ethnic group
  • Leave your belongings unattended in public places

Safety and Health

Western Balkans is a safe region to travel within, but like any other destination, it’s essential to take basic safety precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Western Balkans

  • Be cautious while traveling alone at night
  • Stay in well-lit areas
  • Be aware of pickpocketing in crowded public spaces

Health in Western Balkans

  • Make sure to have all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Western Balkans
  • Drink only bottled water
  • Be wary of street food and unhygienic food practices
