Introduction to Targovishte

Nestled in Southern Dobruja, in Bulgaria, in the Balkans, in Europe, Targovishte is a hidden gem for travelers looking to explore off-beaten tracks. While it may not be as well-known as some of the bigger cities in Bulgaria, Targovishte offers plenty of natural and cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, delicious food, and welcoming locals.

Discover the beauty of Targovishte with this comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Targovishte

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historic Places

Targovishte is rich in history and culture, with many museums and historic landmarks to explore.

  • Regional Museum of History Targovishte: Learn about the history of the region, from ancient times to present day.
  • Holy Trinity Monastery: Explore this 19th-century monastery which houses beautiful frescoes, and is home to many religious relics.
  • Historical Museum of Ravno: Discover the rich history of this small town, which was an important center of mining and metallurgy in the past.

Natural Attractions of Targovishte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty to do in Targovishte, from hiking and biking to exploring caves and waterfalls.

  • Chudnite Skali Nature Reserve: Explore the unique rock formations and beautiful landscapes of this stunning nature reserve.
  • Madara Rider: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic relief is carved into a huge rock cliff and depicts a horseman accompanied by a dog, a lion, and an eagle, carrying in his hand a spear upside down. The perceived mystical elements of its composition and the panoramic view from it, makes it an iconic site.
  • Waterfall Ovchaga: This beautiful waterfall makes for a refreshing stop while exploring the surrounding forests and trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Targovishte

Targovishte is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with trails and routes for hiking and biking for all skill levels.

  • Vrachanski Balkan Nature Park: With dozens of trails ranging from leisurely strolls to challenging hikes, this park makes for a great day trip.
  • Kaliakra Cape: Hike along the beautiful coast of the Black Sea and explore the stunning ruins of an ancient fortress.
  • Green Escape Bike Park: For cycling fans, this park offers a variety of well-maintained trails through the forests and hills of Targovishte.

Best Things to Do in Targovishte: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, there’s plenty to see and do in Targovishte.

  • Taste Local Wines: Book a wine tour and enjoy delicious local wines produced in the region.
  • Explore Targovishte Old Town: Wander through the charming narrow streets of the Old Town, where you’ll find plenty of places to eat, drink, and shop.
  • Take a Day Trip to Varna: Explore the seaside city of Varna, just a short drive from Targovishte, for a day of exploring museums, eating good food, and relaxing on the beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Targovishte

Day 1: Arrival and settling in at the hotel or guesthouse. Day 2: Visit the Regional Museum of Targovishte and explore the Old Town. Day 3: Take a day trip to Varna. Day 4: Hike around Chudnite Skali Nature Reserve. Day 5: Visit the Madara Rider and explore the nearby villages. Day 6: Take a wine tour and sample local wines. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Targovishte

Must-Try Dishes

Targovishte is known for its hearty, flavorful cuisine that relies heavily on locally sourced ingredients. Don’t miss these traditional dishes on your trip to Targovishte.

  • Bulgur: A side dish made from wheat groats boiled in water or stock, Bulgar is served with many main dishes in Targovishte.
  • Tarator: A refreshing cold soup of cucumber and yoghurt, with dill and walnuts, Tarator is a staple dish in the region.
  • Kebapche: A grilled minced meat sausage that is often served with a side of fries or salad.

Local Beverages

Targovishte’s wine-making traditions go back centuries, and today the region is a well-regarded wine destination, with many local wineries. There are also plenty of options for locally-brewed beer and rakia, the traditional fruit brandy.

Where to Stay in Targovishte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Targovishte offers plenty of options for accommodation in a variety of price ranges.

  • Hotel Idol: This 3-star hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and an outdoor pool.
  • Guest House Kashtata: This charming guesthouse in the Old Town offers cozy rooms, a restaurant, and a garden.
  • Hostel Little Prague: For budget travelers, this hostel offers dorm rooms and private rooms for a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Targovishte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: Targovishte is known for its unique wine grape varieties, like Red Misket and Dimyat. Bring home a bottle or two as a souvenir.
  • Handmade Souvenirs: Look for locally-made handicrafts and ceramics in the Old Town.
  • Cheese: Targovishte is known for its variety of soft and hard cheeses, made from cow, sheep, and goat milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Targovishte

In general, Bulgarians are welcoming and hospitable people. Respect for elders and religion are also important in Bulgarian culture, and visitors are often invited to join in local festivities and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Targovishte

The best time to visit Targovishte is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny, and the nature is at its finest. Be aware that this is also peak tourist season, so expect crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Targovishte

Do:

  • Greet people with a smile and a handshake.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting monasteries and churches.
  • Try local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals.
  • Ignore dress codes at religious sites.
  • Drink tap water, only bottled water is recommended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Targovishte

Targovishte is generally a safe place for travelers. However, as with any destination, exercise caution and keep your belongings safe. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Targovishte

No vaccinations are required for travel to Bulgaria, but it’s always a good idea to check with your doctor before traveling. The tap water in Targovishte is not recommended for drinking, so stick to bottled water.

Getting to Targovishte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Targovishte

The nearest airports are Varna (60 km) and Bucharest (249 km). From there, you can rent a car or take a bus to Targovishte.

Bus Stations Near Targovishte

Targovishte Bus Station is located in the center of the city, with frequent connections to Sofia, Varna, and other major cities in Bulgaria.

Other Options

You can also rent a car to explore Targovishte and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Targovishte

Targovishte is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you plan to explore the surrounding natural parks and trails, renting a car would be the best option.

Where to Go Next from Targovishte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Varna: Bulgaria’s third-largest city offers beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and a vibrant nightlife.
  • Veliko Tarnovo: A picturesque town full of history, Veliko Tarnovo is home to the magnificent Tsarevets Castle and many other notable landmarks.
  • Ruse: A charming city on the banks of the Danube, Ruse has a rich history and is known for its beautiful architecture.

Conclusion

Targovishte may not be as well-known as other Bulgarian destinations, but it offers plenty of natural and cultural attractions, friendly locals, and delicious food and wine. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to explore all that Targovishte has to offer.
