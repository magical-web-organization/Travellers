Introduction to Samokov

Samokov is a small town located in the western part of Bulgaria, in the beautiful region of Shopluk. It is surrounded by the majestic Rila mountains and is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. The town is home to many historical sites, museums, and galleries, making it a popular destination for both tourists and locals.

Despite being a small town, Samokov has much to offer to travelers who are looking for an authentic Bulgarian experience. From exploring museums and galleries to trying local cuisine and embarking on outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Samokov.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and more in Samokov.

List of Places to Visit in Samokov

There are many places to visit in Samokov, both historical and natural. Here’s our list of the top attractions in Samokov.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Samokov Historical Museum

The Samokov Historical Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. The museum has a rich collection of exhibits that tell the story of Samokov’s past, from the ancient Thracian settlements to the Ottoman rule and the Bulgarian National Revival. The museum is located in the town center and is open from Tuesday to Saturday.

Samokov Art Gallery

The Samokov Art Gallery is an excellent place to admire local art and get a taste of Bulgaria’s rich cultural heritage. The gallery features works by local artists, as well as renowned Bulgarian painters, sculptors, and graphic designers. The permanent collection includes over 2,500 works of art, and there are also temporary exhibits throughout the year.

The Church of St. Nicholas

The Church of St. Nicholas is a beautiful example of Bulgarian National Revival architecture. The church was built in the 19th century and is dedicated to St. Nicholas, the patron saint of Samokov. The church’s interior is adorned with frescoes by some of the most famous Bulgarian artists of the time, including Zahari Zograf and Dosyu Koyuv.

Natural Attractions of Samokov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rila National Park

Rila National Park is the largest and most famous national park in Bulgaria, located just 15 kilometers from Samokov. The park is home to some of the highest peaks in the Balkan Mountains, including Musala, the highest peak on the Balkan Peninsula. Rila National Park is also home to some of the most beautiful lakes, waterfalls, and valleys in Bulgaria.

Borovets Ski Resort

Borovets Ski Resort is the largest and most popular ski resort in Bulgaria, located just 10 kilometers from Samokov. The resort offers world-class skiing and snowboarding facilities, as well as other winter sports such as snowshoeing, ice skating, and sledding. Borovets is also a popular destination for hiking and mountain biking in the summer.

Iskar Reservoir

Iskar Reservoir is a beautiful artificial lake only 12 kilometers from Samokov. The lake is a popular destination for swimming, boating, and fishing, and there are many picnic areas and hiking trails around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Samokov

Samokov is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers. Here are our top hiking and biking routes in the area.

Musala Peak

Musala Peak is the highest peak in the Balkan Mountains, located in Rila National Park. The hike to the summit is challenging but rewarding, with spectacular views of the Rila Mountains and the surrounding valleys.

Malyovitsa Peak

Malyovitsa Peak is another popular hiking destination in Rila National Park. The hike to the summit is less challenging than Musala Peak but still requires some level of fitness. The views from the summit are breathtaking, with views of the valley and the surrounding peaks.

Best Things to Do in Samokov: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking and exploring Samokov’s cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of other exciting activities to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Samokov.

Horse Riding Tours

Horse riding tours are a popular activity in Samokov, with numerous stables and riding centers in the area. The tours offer a unique chance to explore the beautiful countryside and mountains of Samokov while enjoying the company of friendly and well-trained horses.

Snowshoeing

Snowshoeing is a fun and easy way to explore the winter wonderland of Samokov. The guided tours can take you through the Rila Mountains, Borovets Ski Resort and many other snow-covered trails.

Week-Long Itinerary for Samokov

Samokov offers a diverse range of activities for visitors, ranging from outdoor adventures to cultural pursuits. Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Samokov that combines the best of both:

Day 1: Arrive in Samokov and explore the town center, including the Samokov Historical Museum, the Samokov Art Gallery, and the Church of St. Nicholas.

Day 2: Hike to Malyovitsa Peak in Rila National Park.

Day 3: Take a horse riding tour in the morning, followed by an afternoon of relaxation at Iskar Reservoir.

Day 4: Explore Borovets Ski Resort and try out skiing or snowboarding.

Day 5: Visit the Rila Monastery, one of the most famous and beautiful Orthodox monasteries in Bulgaria.

Day 6: Take a guided snowshoeing tour through the snowy forests and valleys of Rila National Park.

Day 7: Leave Samokov, but not without first trying some of the delicious local cuisine and buying some souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Samokov

No trip to Samokov is complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks in Samokov.

Must-Try Dishes

Bansko Kapama

Bansko Kapama is a traditional Bulgarian dish made with slow-cooked meat, vegetables, and spices. It is a hearty and filling dish that is perfect for cold winter days.

Banitsa

Banitsa is a savory pastry made with phyllo dough, feta cheese, and eggs. It is a popular breakfast food and can be found in bakeries and cafes throughout Samokov.

Local Beverages

Rakia

Rakia is a traditional Bulgarian brandy made from distilled fruit. It is a strong alcoholic beverage that is often served as an aperitif or digestif.

Mavrud Wine

Mavrud Wine is a unique Bulgarian wine made from the Mavrud grape variety. The wine has a rich, full-bodied flavor and is perfect for pairing with local cuisine.

Where to Stay in Samokov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Samokov has a good range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of our top picks for where to stay in Samokov.

Hotel Rila

Hotel Rila is a modern hotel located in Borovets Ski Resort, just 10 kilometers from Samokov. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a spa, and world-class skiing facilities.

Guesthouse Petra

Guesthouse Petra is a charming guesthouse located in the heart of Samokov. The guesthouse has comfortable rooms, a garden, and a terrace where you can enjoy stunning views of the town and the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Samokov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Samokov is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, woodcarvings, and embroidery. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Samokov.

Pottery

Samokov’s pottery is famous throughout Bulgaria and is known for its intricate designs and colorful patterns. You can find pottery in most gift shops and local markets in Samokov.

Embroidery

Embroidery is another traditional handicraft that is popular in Samokov. The designs are often inspired by nature, and the colors are bright and vibrant.

Local Culture and Customs of Samokov

Bulgarian culture is rich and diverse, and Samokov is no exception. Here are some customs and traditions that you may encounter in Samokov.

Kukeri

Kukeri is a traditional Bulgarian ritual that takes place during the winter months. Men dress up in elaborate costumes and masks and perform dances to ward off evil spirits.

Baba Marta

Baba Marta is a Bulgarian holiday that takes place on March 1st. It is believed to mark the beginning of spring, and people traditionally exchange red and white bracelets as a sign of good luck and health.

Best Time to Visit Samokov

The best time to visit Samokov depends on what you’re interested in. The winter months are best for skiing and other winter activities, while the spring and summer months are ideal for hiking, horse riding, and exploring the natural beauty of the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Samokov

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.
  • Practice responsible tourism and respect the natural environment.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the local people or culture.
  • Damage or litter the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety rules and regulations in ski resorts and other outdoor areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Samokov

Samokov is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety while traveling, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Samokov

Bulgaria has a good healthcare system, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance. If you need medical attention while in Samokov, the best place to go is the Samokov Hospital.

Getting to Samokov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Samokov

The closest airport to Samokov is Sofia Airport, which is located about 60 kilometers away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to Samokov.

Bus Stations Near Samokov

Samokov is well-connected to the Bulgarian bus network, with several daily connections to major cities such as Sofia, Plovdiv, and Blagoevgrad.

Other Options

You can also get to Samokov by train, although the train station is located about 10 kilometers outside of town.

How to Get Around in Samokov

Samokov is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas or go to Borovets Ski Resort, it’s best to rent a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Samokov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Samokov’s location makes it an excellent base for exploring the surrounding areas of Bulgaria. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Samokov.

Sofia

Sofia is the capital city of Bulgaria, located about 60 kilometers from Samokov. The city has a rich history and culture, with many museums, galleries, and historical sites to explore.

Plovdiv

Plovdiv is one of the oldest cities in Europe, located about 150 kilometers from Samokov. The city is famous for its Roman ruins, Ottoman-era architecture, and vibrant cultural scene.

Conclusion

From exploring museums and galleries to hiking in the Rila Mountains and trying local cuisine, Samokov has much to offer to visitors. Whether you’re interested in culture, history, or outdoor adventures, Samokov is a must-visit destination in Bulgaria.
