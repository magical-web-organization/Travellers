Introduction to Plovdiv

Plovdiv, the second-largest city in Bulgaria, is located in the Upper Thracian Plain, in the heart of the Balkans. This ancient Bulgarian city is over 8,000 years old and is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Europe. The city was originally called Philipopolis after King Philip II of Macedonia, who conquered the city in the 4th century BC.

Today, Plovdiv is a vibrant and modern city that’s packed with history, culture, and amazing experiences. The city has a relaxed atmosphere, with friendly locals, picturesque scenery and great weather throughout the year. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie or culture vulture, Plovdiv has something for everyone.

In this guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, and attractions to see in Plovdiv.

List of Places to Visit in Plovdiv

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Plovdiv has a rich history and is home to many museums and historical landmarks that are worth visiting. Here are some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Ancient Theater of Philippopolis: One of the most impressive landmarks in Plovdiv, the theater was built in the 2nd century AD and is still in use today. It can accommodate over 7,000 people.
  • Roman Stadium: Located in the city center, the Roman stadium (aka “Stadium of Trimontium”) was built in the 2nd century AD and was used for chariot and foot races.
  • Old Town: A charming, historic area of the city featuring narrow cobblestone streets, colorful houses, and traditional architecture.
  • Regional Ethnographic Museum: This museum showcases the cultural and historical heritage of the Plovdiv region, including traditional costumes, crafts, and folk art.
  • Tsar Simeon’s Garden: A popular, scenic park located in the city center, Tsar Simeon’s Garden is the perfect place to take a relaxing stroll and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Dzhumaya Mosque: A 15th-century mosque located in the center of Plovdiv that serves as a reminder of Bulgaria’s Ottoman past.

Natural Attractions of Plovdiv: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Plovdiv has plenty of options for you. With stunning views, challenging hikes, and peaceful nature trails, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in Plovdiv:

  • The Rhodope Mountains: A beautiful mountain range with miles of hiking trails and breathtaking views.
  • Bachkovo Monastery: Located just outside of Plovdiv, Bachkovo Monastery is surrounded by beautiful mountain scenery and is home to some of Bulgaria’s most beautiful frescoes.
  • Hisarya Hot Springs: These natural hot springs have been used for rejuvenation and relaxation for over 2,000 years.
  • Devetashka Cave: One of the largest and most beautiful caves in Bulgaria, Devetashka Cave is home to many unique rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plovdiv

Plovdiv is an excellent destination for hikers and bikers, with many beautiful trails and spectacular views. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Smolyan Lakes - A challenging hike that takes you through some of Bulgaria’s most beautiful countryside.
  • Chernatitsa Ecopath - A gentle hike that takes you through stunning mountain views and relaxing nature trails.
  • The Rhodope Mountains - With several different routes to choose from, the Rhodope Mountains offer some of the most scenic and challenging hikes in Bulgaria.

Best Things to Do in Plovdiv: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Plovdiv, whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Take a free walking tour of the city center - A great way to discover the city’s history and hidden gems.
  • Visit the Roman Amphitheatre - A must-see attraction that dates back to the second century AD.
  • Enjoy a traditional Bulgarian meal - Plovdiv is known for its delicious and authentic cuisine.
  • Check out the Kapana Arts District - This creative neighborhood is full of galleries, boutiques, and workshops.
  • Take a bike tour of the city - A fun and unique way to explore Plovdiv’s historic sites and landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plovdiv

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Plovdiv, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore Old Town and visit some of the city’s museums, including the Ethnographic Museum and the Historical Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Bachkovo Monastery and the surrounding mountains.
  • Day 3: Visit the Roman Amphitheatre and Ancient Stadium in the morning, then spend the afternoon relaxing in Tsar Simeon’s Garden.
  • Day 4: Take a guided bike tour of the city, stopping at some of the city’s hidden gems and local hotspots. In the evening, enjoy a traditional Bulgarian meal in the city center.
  • Day 5: Hike through the stunning Rhodope Mountains and enjoy the breathtaking views.
  • Day 6: Explore Plovdiv’s arts district and check out some of the local galleries and workshops.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to the natural hot springs at Hisarya for a relaxing soak.

Food and Drink Scene of Plovdiv

Plovdiv is a food lover’s paradise, with a wide variety of traditional Bulgarian cuisine and international dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebapche: A traditional Bulgarian dish made from grilled minced meat served with fresh bread and salad.
  • Banitsa: A pastry filled with cheese and eggs and often served for breakfast.
  • Shopska Salad: A refreshing salad made with fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and white cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Rakia: A traditional Bulgarian fruit brandy that’s typically served as an aperitif or a digestive.
  • Wine: Bulgaria has a long history of winemaking, and the local wines are excellent and affordable.

Where to Stay in Plovdiv: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Plovdiv has a wide variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Plovdiv:

  • Hotel Evmolpia: A charming, boutique hotel located in the heart of Old Town.
  • Hostel Mostel: A popular, budget-friendly hostel with a great location and excellent amenities.
  • Guest House Old Plovdiv: A historic guesthouse with spacious rooms and a beautiful courtyard.

What to Buy in Plovdiv: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Plovdiv has plenty of excellent options. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • Kapana Creative District: This creative neighborhood is home to many art galleries, craft shops, and boutiques.
  • Plovdiv Central Market: A bustling market filled with fresh fruits, vegetables, and local products.
  • Kukerica: A shop that specializes in handmade traditional Bulgarian perfumes and cosmetics.

Local Culture and Customs of Plovdiv

Bulgarian culture is rich and unique, with many traditions that are upheld to this day. Here are some of the most important customs and traditions in Plovdiv:

  • Kukeri Festival: A traditional Bulgarian festival that takes place in Plovdiv every year, featuring colorful costumes, dancing, and music.
  • Martenitsi: Small red and white amulets that are worn on March 1st to celebrate the coming of spring.
  • Orthodox Christianity: The predominant religion in Plovdiv and Bulgaria, with many beautiful churches and religious festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Plovdiv

The best time to visit Plovdiv is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, the spring and fall are also excellent times to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plovdiv

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks - Bulgarian cuisine is delicious and unique, and you’ll find plenty of mouth-watering dishes to sample in Plovdiv.
  • Visit the museums and historical landmarks - Plovdiv has a rich history, and there are many interesting museums and historical sites to explore.
  • Take a stroll through Tsar Simeon’s Garden - It’s a beautiful park with stunning views of the city.
  • Follow local customs and traditions - Bulgarians are proud of their culture and traditions, and they appreciate visitors who respect their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or mosques - This applies to both men and women.
  • Eat with your hands - Meals are typically eaten with utensils in Bulgaria.
  • Be disrespectful to locals - Bulgarians are warm and friendly people, but they expect visitors to be respectful and courteous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plovdiv

Plovdiv is a generally safe city, and violent crime is relatively rare. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded places.

Health in Plovdiv

Bulgaria has a good healthcare system, and Plovdiv has many excellent hospitals and clinics. However, it’s still a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Plovdiv: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plovdiv

Plovdiv International Airport is the closest airport to the city, with regular domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Plovdiv

Plovdiv has a central bus station that connects to many domestic and international destinations.

Other Options

You can also get to Plovdiv by train or car, with several national and international routes available.

How to Get Around in Plovdiv

Plovdiv is a very walkable city, with many of the top attractions located within walking distance of each other. However, you can also take a taxi, bus or tram to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Plovdiv: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Bulgaria, there are many nearby destinations and excursions that are worth checking out. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Sofia: The Bulgarian capital, located just two hours from Plovdiv by bus or train.
  • Rila Monastery: One of Bulgaria’s most popular attractions thanks to its stunning architecture and mountainous surroundings.
  • Belogradchik Rocks: Otherworldly rock formations that look like they were lifted straight out of a fantasy novel.

Conclusion

Plovdiv is a city with a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and a vibrant culture. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or delicious food, Plovdiv has something for everyone. This guide has introduced you to some of the city’s top attractions, places to stay, and things to do, so you can plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful Bulgarian city.
