Introduction to Strandzha

Located in the southeastern part of Bulgaria, Strandzha is a hidden gem in the Balkans that has recently started to gain popularity among tourists thanks to its unique combination of cultural heritage and unspoiled nature. The region is named after the Strandzha Mountains, which cover most of its territory and are home to some of the oldest forests in Europe.

Apart from stunning natural landscapes, Strandzha boasts a rich history that dates back to ancient times, as evidenced by the numerous archaeological sites and cultural landmarks that can be found throughout the area. Visitors to Strandzha have a lot to explore and enjoy, including hiking and biking trails, picturesque villages, traditional cuisine, and local customs that have remained largely unchanged for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Strandzha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Thracian Tomb of Kazanlak: This UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the best-preserved examples of Thracian tomb architecture, dating back to the 4th century BC.

  • Museum of Strandzha Nature Park: This museum offers an in-depth look at the flora and fauna of the region, as well as the cultural heritage of the local communities.

  • Beglik Tash: An open-air sanctuary that served as a ritual site for the Thracians between the 14th and the 4th centuries BC.

Natural Attractions of Strandzha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Strandzha Nature Park: Covering a total area of over 1,161 km², this nature park is the largest protected area in Bulgaria and is home to a vast array of plant and animal species, as well as numerous hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy.

  • Silistar Beach: This pristine beach is located on the Black Sea coast and offers breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding forests.

  • Veleka River: The Veleka River runs through the heart of Strandzha and is famous for its crystal clear water and abundant wildlife, including rare species of fish and birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strandzha

  • The Strandzha Mountains: With a total length of over 100 km², the Strandzha Mountains offer some of the best hiking and biking trails in Bulgaria, with routes ranging from the easy to the challenging.

  • Veleka River Trail: This 17-km trail follows the Veleka River from the village of Sinemorets to the mouth of the river at the Black Sea, offering stunning views of the river and the surrounding mountains.

  • The Beglik Tash Trail: This 7-km trail leads visitors to the ancient ritual site of Beglik Tash, passing through dense forests and breathtaking mountain views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Strandzha: Activities and Tours

  • Village Tour: Take a tour of the local villages and get a taste of traditional Bulgarian life, including workshops on pottery, weaving, and other crafts.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Discover the unique flavors of Bulgarian wine by visiting some of the local vineyards and wineries in the region.

  • Photography Tour: Explore the fascinating landscapes of Strandzha with the help of a professional photographer, who will guide you to the best spots for capturing stunning images.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strandzha

Day 1: Visit the Thracian Tomb of Kazanlak and the Museum of Strandzha Nature Park.

Day 2: Go on a hiking trip to the Strandzha Mountains.

Day 3: Explore the villages of Brashlyan and Kosti.

Day 4: Visit the Beglik Tash site and go on a picnic in the forest.

Day 5: Take a boat trip along the Veleka River.

Day 6: Discover the Black Sea coast and visit Silistar Beach.

Day 7: Join a photography tour of the region and capture the stunning landscapes of Strandzha.

Food and Drink Scene of Strandzha

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chomlek: A traditional Bulgarian dish made with meat and potatoes, cooked in a clay pot and served with yogurt.

  • Kachamak: A local specialty made of cornmeal, served with cheese and butter.

  • Banitsa: A Bulgarian pastry made of filo dough filled with cheese, spinach, or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Rakia: A strong alcoholic beverage made from fermented fruit, usually grapes or plums.

  • Menta: A sweet mint-flavored alcoholic drink.

  • Boza: A non-alcoholic drink made from fermented wheat or corn.

Where to Stay in Strandzha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Strandzha: A recently renovated hotel located in Malko Tarnovo, the largest town in the region.

  • Sinemorets Beach Resort: A beachfront resort offering luxurious accommodation with stunning sea views.

  • Guesthouse Strandzha: A traditional Bulgarian guesthouse located in the village of Brashlyan, offering a peaceful and authentic atmosphere.

What to Buy in Strandzha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics: Strandzha is famous for its traditional pottery, which is still made by hand using techniques passed down from generation to generation.

  • Honey: The region is famous for its high-quality honey, made from the nectar of wild flowers and plants found in the local forests.

  • Traditional cloth and fabrics: Visitors can buy traditional Bulgarian textiles, including carpets, rugs, and other fabrics, in the local villages.

Local Culture and Customs of Strandzha

Strandzha is known for its rich folklore and cultural heritage, which is still very much alive today. Visitors can experience traditional Bulgarian customs and rituals, such as the Kukeri dance, which is performed at festivals and special occasions. The local communities are also famous for their hospitality and warm welcome to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Strandzha

The best time to visit Strandzha is from April to October, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the forests and hills are at their most colorful. In the summer months, visitors can enjoy the beaches and the Black Sea coast, while spring and autumn offer perfect conditions for hiking and exploring the local villages.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strandzha

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the national parks and protected areas.

  • Disrespect the local communities and their customs.

  • Turn a blind eye to littering and pollution.

Safety and Health

Safety in Strandzha

Strandzha is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should exercise caution when hiking or exploring the forests and mountains, and be aware of the potential risks associated with wild animals or sudden weather changes.

Health in Strandzha

Visitors to Strandzha should ensure that they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations and seek advice from a travel health professional before their trip. Tap water in the area is generally safe to drink, although visitors may prefer to drink bottled water.

Getting to Strandzha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Strandzha

The closest airports to Strandzha are Burgas Airport and Varna Airport, both of which are served by regular flights from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Strandzha

The main bus station in Strandzha is located in Malko Tarnovo, with connections to major towns and cities across Bulgaria.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Strandzha by train or car, although driving in the area may be challenging due to the narrow, winding roads.

How to Get Around in Strandzha

The best way to explore Strandzha is on foot or by bike, with numerous hiking and biking trails throughout the region. Local buses and taxis can also be used to reach some of the more remote areas of the region.

Where to Go Next from Strandzha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sozopol: A charming coastal town on the Black Sea, famous for its historical architecture and lively atmosphere.

  • Plovdiv: The second-largest city in Bulgaria, known for its ancient Roman ruins and vibrant cultural scene.

  • Istanbul: The cultural capital of Turkey and a melting pot of history, architecture, and cuisine, located just across the border from Strandzha.

Conclusion

Strandzha is truly a hidden gem in Eastern Europe, offering visitors a unique combination of cultural heritage and natural beauty that is hard to find elsewhere. From stunning views of the mountains and forests to traditional Bulgarian cuisine and customs, there is something for every type of traveler in this fascinating region. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a culture buff, or simply in search of the perfect beach, Strandzha is sure to leave a lasting impression on your heart and soul.
