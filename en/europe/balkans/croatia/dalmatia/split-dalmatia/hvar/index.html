A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Hvar, Croatia

Hvar is a beautiful town located in Split-Dalmatia, in Dalmatia, Croatia. It is situated on an island in the Adriatic Sea and is known for its gorgeous beaches, stunning landscapes, and rich history. If you are planning a trip to Hvar, here is everything you need to know before you go.

List of Places to Visit in Hvar

There are many wonderful places to visit in Hvar. From museums to natural attractions, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hvar has a rich history, and there are many museums and historical places you can visit to learn more about it. Here are some must-visit places:

  • The Hvar Museum
  • The Franciscan Monastery and Museum
  • The Benedictine Monastery
  • The St. Stephen’s Cathedral
  • The Fortica Fortress

Natural Attractions of Hvar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hvar is surrounded by stunning landscapes and natural attractions. If you are an outdoor enthusiast, you will love exploring these places:

  • Pakleni Islands
  • Stari Grad Plain
  • Brusje Village
  • Sveta Nedjelja Village
  • Dubovica Beach
  • Sveta Nedjelja Cliffs

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hvar

Hvar has many hiking and biking routes that offer spectacular views of the island. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Hvar Island Trail
  • Hvar Hill Challenge
  • Stari Grad Plain Path
  • Dol-Carpe Diem Wine Tour

Best Things to Do in Hvar: Activities and Tours

In addition to museums and natural attractions, Hvar has plenty of fun activities and tours to offer. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • Wine Tasting Tour
  • Kayaking Tour
  • Island Boat Tours
  • Rock Climbing Adventure
  • Sunset Cruise

Week-Long Itinerary for Hvar

If you are planning to stay in Hvar for a week, here is an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Hvar Museum
Day 2 Take a Wine Tasting Tour
Day 3 Hike the Hvar Island Trail
Day 4 Explore Stari Grad Plain
Day 5 Kayak around Pakleni Islands
Day 6 Relax on Dubovica Beach
Day 7 Take an Island Boat Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Hvar

Hvar is known for its delicious food and excellent wines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Black Risotto
  • Grilled Seafood
  • Octopus Salad
  • Pašticada (stewed beef)

Local Beverages

  • Plavac Mali wine
  • Rakija (fruit brandy)

Where to Stay in Hvar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hvar has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some popular choices:

  • Amfora Hvar Grand Beach Resort
  • Riva Hvar Yacht Harbour Hotel
  • Pharos Hvar Bayhill Hotel
  • Villa Nora Hvar
  • Hostel Villa Skansi

What to Buy in Hvar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some great options:

  • Lavender Products
  • Olive Oil
  • Wine
  • Salt
  • Hvar Lace

Local Culture and Customs of Hvar

Hvar has a unique culture and customs worth learning more about. Here are some facts to keep in mind:

  • Hvar is known as the “Sunniest Island in Croatia.”
  • The island has a tradition of lace-making that dates back to the Renaissance.
  • Famous figures such as Pablo Picasso and Prince Edward VIII have visited Hvar.

Best Time to Visit Hvar

The best time to visit Hvar is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is warm, and all attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hvar

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Explore the outdoor attractions of Hvar
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Visit the museums and historical places

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring the outdoors
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting churches or religious sites
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Hvar

Hvar is a very safe destination for tourists. However, here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid leaving valuables in plain sight
  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas
  • Follow all safety instructions when engaging in outdoor activities

Health in Hvar

Hvar has good medical facilities and hospitals, but it is still recommended that you purchase travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Hvar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Hvar can be easy or a bit tricky depending on your preferences and location. Here are some transportation options to choose from:

  • Ferries: This is the most popular way to get to Hvar.
  • Catamarans: If you want a faster way to get to Hvar, you can take a catamaran.
  • Private Boats: You can also hire a private boat to take you to Hvar.

Airports Near Hvar

  • Split Airport
  • Dubrovnik Airport

Bus Stations Near Hvar

  • Split Bus Station
  • Dubrovnik Bus Station

Other Options

  • Car rental
  • Private Transfer

How to Get Around in Hvar

Hvar is a small island, but it has many transportation options to choose from:

  • Taxi Boats: This is the most popular way to get around the island.
  • Scooters and Bikes: You can also rent a scooter or bike.
  • Public Transportation: There are buses that run between Hvar Town and other destinations on the island.

Where to Go Next from Hvar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore other parts of Dalmatia after visiting Hvar, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Dubrovnik
  • Split
  • Mljet National Park
  • Krka National Park

Conclusion

Hvar is a beautiful destination that offers a mix of natural beauty and rich cultural history. Whether you want to soak up the sun on the beaches, explore museums and historical sites, or engage in outdoor activities, there is something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are ready to plan a great trip to Hvar, Croatia.
