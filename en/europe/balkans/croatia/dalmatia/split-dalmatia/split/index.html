Introduction to Split

Nestled on the Adriatic coast of Croatia, Split is a historic city that offers visitors a blend of ancient history, beautiful beaches, and exciting activities. Split is the second-largest city in Croatia, with a population of around 200,000 people. Split’s Old Town, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is home to the 4th century Diocletian Palace, Split’s most popular tourist attraction and the historic city center. The city’s many cafes, bars, and restaurants, add to the charm and excitement of the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Split

Split is a Catholic country, and it is important to respect the religious and cultural traditions of Croatians. Croatians are friendly and hospitable to tourists, and visitors are expected to behave respectfully towards the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Split

The best time to visit Split is in the summer months between June and September when the weather is warm and sunny. The months of May and October are also good times to visit as the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Split

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the nearby islands and beaches
  • Learn some words in Croatian
  • Explore the city on foot

Don’t:

  • Touch historical monuments and relics
  • Dress inappropriately
  • Drive without a valid driving license or insurance

Safety and Health

Safety in Split

Split is a generally safe city for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take the usual precautions and keep a close eye on their valuables in crowded areas.

Health in Split

Tourists do not require any specific vaccinations to enter Croatia. The country has a good healthcare system, and visitors can find medical help at clinics and hospitals across the city.
