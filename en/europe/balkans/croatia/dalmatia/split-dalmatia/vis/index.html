Introduction to Vis

The island of Vis, located in the Split-Dalmatia region of Croatia, is a hidden gem in the Balkans. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and delicious cuisine, this island is the perfect destination for those seeking a relaxing getaway.

Vis is also home to two major towns, Vis Town and Komiza, both of which offer plenty of activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, or just some time to relax on the beach, Vis has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Vis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Vis has a long and storied history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Neolithic era. Visitors interested in learning more about the island’s history should be sure to visit the following museums and historical places:

  • Vis Heritage Museum: Housed in a former 16th-century Venetian fortress, this museum offers a comprehensive look at the island’s history through artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits.
  • Greek Cemetery: Located just outside of Vis Town, this cemetery is the final resting place for Greek soldiers who died on the island during World War II.
  • Fort George: This hilltop fortress offers stunning views of the island and the Adriatic Sea, as well as a look back at the island’s military history.

Natural Attractions of Vis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vis is known for its stunning natural landscape, and visitors looking to get in touch with nature won’t be disappointed. Some of the island’s top natural attractions include:

  • Stiniva Beach: This secluded cove has been named one of the best beaches in Europe, thanks to its clear, calm waters and dramatic cliffs.
  • Blue Cave: Located on the nearby island of Bisevo, this natural wonder is a must-see. Visitors can take a boat tour to see the cave’s bright blue waters.
  • Mount Hum: This mountain offers scenic hiking trails and stunning views of the island and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vis

Vis is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several hiking and biking routes across the island. Some popular options include:

  • Milna Beach to Srebrna Bay: This easy hike takes visitors past two of Vis’s most beautiful beaches.
  • Vis to Komiza: This more challenging hike takes visitors along the coast, with stunning views of the sea and surrounding islands.
  • Vis Island Bike Tour: This guided tour takes visitors across the island, with stops at some of the top natural and cultural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Vis: Activities and Tours

In addition to the island’s natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some top options include:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Vis is home to several wineries, and visitors can take a guided tour to sample some of the island’s best wines.
  • Sea Kayaking Tour: This tour takes visitors along the island’s coast, with stops at some of the most beautiful beaches and coves.
  • Cave Tour: In addition to the Blue Cave, Vis is home to several other caves that are worth exploring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vis

Vis is the perfect destination for a week-long getaway, and visitors can create an itinerary that includes a mix of relaxation, cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Vis Town and spend the day exploring the town’s historic sites and sampling some of the local cuisine.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour to see the Blue Cave and other nearby natural attractions.
  • Day 3: Spend the day relaxing on one of Vis’s beautiful beaches, such as Stiniva Beach or Srebrna Bay.
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and explore some of the island’s hiking and biking trails.
  • Day 5: Visit the Vis Heritage Museum and other cultural attractions.
  • Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and sample some of the island’s best wines.
  • Day 7: Spend the day sea kayaking around the island’s coast and visiting some of the more secluded beaches and coves.

Food and Drink Scene of Vis

No visit to Vis would be complete without sampling some of the island’s delicious cuisine and local wines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peka: This traditional Croatian dish features meat and vegetables slow-cooked under a metal dome.
  • Octopus Salad: Made with fresh octopus, tomatoes, onions, and olive oil, this salad is a refreshing summer dish.
  • Black Risotto: Made with squid ink and rice, this dish is both delicious and visually striking.

Local Beverages

  • Vugava: This local white wine is made from the vugava grape and is a perfect pairing for seafood dishes.
  • Plavac Mali: This red wine is made from the plavac mali grape and is the perfect accompaniment to meat dishes.
  • Rakija: This brandy made from distilled fruit is a popular local digestif.

Where to Stay in Vis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vis offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some top options include:

  • Hotel San Giorgio: This luxury hotel in Vis Town offers stunning sea views and elegant accommodations.
  • Hostel Central Vis: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the heart of Vis Town and offers both dormitory-style and private rooms.
  • Villa Kamenica: This guesthouse in Komiza offers comfortable accommodations and a peaceful garden setting.

What to Buy in Vis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Vis is known for its local products, including olive oil, wine, and lavender. Visitors looking for souvenirs to bring home should consider the following:

  • Olive oil: Vis is home to several olive oil producers, and visitors can take home some of the island’s high-quality olive oil.
  • Wine: Vis’s local wines are a must-try, and visitors can bring home a bottle of their favorite variety.
  • Lavender: Vis’s lavender fields are a sight to behold, and visitors can purchase lavender oil, soap, and other products to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Vis

Vis has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Greek, Roman, Venetian, and Austro-Hungarian cultures. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and not littering on the island’s beaches.

Best Time to Visit Vis

The best time to visit Vis is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island’s beaches and outdoor attractions are open. July and August are the busiest months, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vis

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do take the time to explore the island’s natural and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the island’s beaches or natural areas.
  • Don’t visit religious sites inappropriately dressed.
  • Don’t visit the island’s military installations, as they are off-limits to the public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vis

Vis is a safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and a well-maintained infrastructure. Visitors should take usual precautions to safeguard their valuables and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Vis

Vis has a well-equipped medical center, and visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before arriving. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you, as pharmacies on the island may have limited supplies.

Getting to Vis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vis

The nearest airport to Vis is Split Airport, which is located on the mainland and is served by several airlines from destinations across Europe.

Bus Stations Near Vis

Vis is well-connected to several major bus stations in Croatia, including Dubrovnik, Zagreb, and Split.

Other Options

Vis is also easily accessible by ferry, with regular connections from Split and Hvar.

How to Get Around in Vis

Vis is best explored on foot or by bike, and visitors can rent bikes on the island. Taxis are also available, but visitors should negotiate fares in advance.

Where to Go Next from Vis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vis is located near several other popular destinations in Croatia, including Hvar, Split, and Dubrovnik. Visitors can take ferries or buses to these destinations, or arrange for private transportation.

Conclusion

Vis is a stunning destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore cultural attractions, or embark on outdoor adventures, Vis has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit today and discover all that this hidden gem in the Balkans has to offer.
