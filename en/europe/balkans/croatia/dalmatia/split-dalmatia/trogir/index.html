Introduction to Trogir

Trogir is a small medieval town located in the Split-Dalmatia region of Croatia. Set on a tiny island, Trogir is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most beautiful towns in Croatia, with an interesting history and culture. This coastal town is situated just 18 kilometers away from Split, the second largest city in Croatia, and is a perfect place for a day trip or as a base for exploring the region further.

List of Places to Visit in Trogir

There are plenty of places to visit in Trogir, from cultural and historical attractions to natural beauty spots, and outdoor activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trogir is a town full of historical and cultural attractions. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • The Cathedral of St. Lawrence: A stunning Romanesque-Gothic cathedral located in the heart of Trogir’s old town. The cathedral was built from the 13th to the 15th century and houses a beautiful collection of artworks.
  • The Kamerlengo Fortress: A medieval fortress built at the end of the 15th century to protect Trogir from the Ottoman Empire.
  • The Chapel of St. John the Baptist: A beautiful 14th-century chapel located on the south-western side of the town.
  • The City Museum: A museum located in the old town, which provides a comprehensive guide to the history of Trogir from Roman times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Trogir: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trogir also has some stunning outdoor attractions that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Pantan Nature Park: An excellent wetland area located just outside of Trogir, perfect for bird watching, exploring the forests and getting away from the bustle of the town.
  • The Island of Ciovo: Located just across the bridge from Trogir, Ciovo is perfect for swimming, sunbathing and exploring the local fauna and flora.
  • Marjan Hill: This hill offers stunning panoramic views of Trogir and the surrounding countryside. Walking or biking around the eco-park is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trogir

Trogir offers some fantastic hiking and biking routes, from scenic coastal trails to challenging mountain paths. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Trogir circular bike trail: A 20 km trail around the town, covering some of the beautiful countryside and olive groves.
  • Panonic Hills: A more challenging hike through the hills that surround Trogir, the best time to explore this area is in the early morning or late afternoon in summer.

Best Things to Do in Trogir: Activities and Tours

Trogir offers various activities and tours that will make your stay more enjoyable. Some of the things to do include:

  • Sailing around the Adriatic: Many tour operators offer catamaran and sailing boats for hire, perfect for exploring the beautiful coastline of the area.
  • Food and wine tours: Croatian cuisine is famous for its Mediterranean flavors, and a food and wine tour around Trogir is the perfect way to experience it.
  • Scuba diving: The crystal clear waters around Trogir are perfect for diving, and there are several diving schools and certified dive centers available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trogir

Trogir’s small size makes it an ideal destination for a week-long itinerary. Here is a suggested seven-day itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the old town and visit the St. Lawrence Cathedral and Kamerlengo Fortress.
  • Day 2: Visit the nearby Island of Ciovo.
  • Day 3: Visit the Pantan Nature Park.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of the Krka National Park.
  • Day 5: Take a sailing trip around the Adriatic.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a food and wine tour around Trogir.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beaches of the island of Ciovo or hike around Marjan Hill.

Food and Drink Scene of Trogir

Croatian cuisine is a mixture of Mediterranean and continental influences, making it rich in flavors. Trogir has a fantastic food and drink scene, with traditional local dishes and a selection of local wines.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the dishes that are must-try in Trogir include:

  • Pasticada: A traditional beef stew cooked in a rich red wine sauce and served with gnocchi or pasta.
  • Octopus salad: Fresh octopus mixed with potatoes, onions, and parsley.
  • Black risotto: Rice cooked with squid ink and seafood, also known as squid ink risotto.

Local Beverages

Croatia’s wine scene is rapidly developing, and the local wines of Trogir are worth trying. Some of the best wines are:

  • Plavac Mali: A full-bodied red wine that goes great with meat dishes.
  • Pošip: A crisp white wine that pairs well with seafood.

Where to Stay in Trogir: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trogir has a fantastic selection of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Trogir Palace: A luxury hotel that offers spectacular views of the old town and fantastic amenities.
  • Villa Maslina: A small family-run guesthouse with a beautiful garden and comfortable rooms.
  • Hostel Trogir: A budget-friendly hostel located just outside the old town, perfect for backpackers.

What to Buy in Trogir: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trogir has an excellent range of local products and souvenirs to buy. Here are some of the best souvenirs to take home:

  • Olive oil: The local olive oil is award-winning and makes for a great souvenir.
  • Lavender products: The region around Trogir is famous for its lavender, and you can buy lavender-scented soaps, oils, and candles.
  • Hand-made pottery: Trogir has a thriving ceramics industry, and the hand-made pottery is worth buying.

Local Culture and Customs of Trogir

Croatia has a rich culture and customs, deeply influenced by its Mediterranean and continental neighbors. In Trogir, some of the local customs include:

  • Traditional music: Trogir has a rich music scene, and you can experience traditional Croatian music in one of the many local bars and cafes.
  • Festivals: Trogir has many festivals throughout the year, including the Trogir Summer Festival and the Donkey Race in August.
  • Family values: Croatia is very family-oriented, and it’s common to see entire families eating out together or enjoying leisure activities on weekends.

Best Time to Visit Trogir

The best time to visit Trogir is during the shoulder season between April and May or September and October when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trogir

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Visit the natural parks and beaches around Trogir.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious buildings.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trogir

Trogir is a safe destination, but it’s always advisable to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips:

  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Always wear a helmet when biking.

Health in Trogir

Croatia has a good healthcare system, and Trogir has several clinics and hospitals. Here are some healthcare tips:

  • Use sunscreen during the summer months.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Be aware of ticks when hiking in wooded areas.

Getting to Trogir: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trogir is easy to reach by land, sea, and air.

Airports Near Trogir

Split Airport is just 5 kilometers away from Trogir and has many international and domestic flights connecting Trogir to other destinations in Europe and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Trogir

Trogir has a bus station in the center of the town, with regular buses connecting it to other destinations in Croatia.

Other Options

Trogir is also accessible by ferry, and many tour operators offer cruises around the Adriatic.

How to Get Around in Trogir

Trogir is a small town, and the best way to get around is on foot. Biking is also a popular option, and many rental shops offer bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Trogir: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trogir is an ideal base for exploring the many beautiful destinations in Croatia. Here are some of the nearby destinations to explore:

  • Split: The second-largest city in Croatia and the capital of the Dalmatia region.
  • Hvar: A beautiful island in the Adriatic, famous for its lavender fields and Mediterranean cuisine.
  • Dubrovnik: One of the most beautiful cities in Croatia, with a rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Trogir is a hidden gem in Croatia, a charming medieval town with an interesting history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you can explore Trogir’s best attractions, cuisine, and culture, and experience the best of what Croatia has to offer.
