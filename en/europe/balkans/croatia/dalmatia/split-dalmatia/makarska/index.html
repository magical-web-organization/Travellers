Introduction to Makarska

Located on the Adriatic coast of Croatia, Makarska is a picturesque coastal town known for its stunning natural beauty and vibrant culture. With a history dating back to ancient times, the town is home to a range of historical and cultural attractions, as well as a variety of outdoor activities for nature enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking to soak up the sun on the beach or explore the town’s rich history, Makarska has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Makarska

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Makarska Museum and Gallery - Housed in a 16th-century Venetian mansion, the museum showcases a collection of artifacts and artworks that chronicle the town’s history.
  • St. Mark’s Cathedral - Built in the 18th century, this Baroque-style cathedral is renowned for its stunning architecture and ornate interior.
  • Franciscan Monastery - This 16th-century monastery features a museum with exhibits on the history of the monastery and the town.

Natural Attractions of Makarska: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Biokovo Nature Park - This pristine park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, as well as several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and coastline.
  • Makarska Beach - With crystal-clear waters and pebble beaches, Makarska Beach is a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, or trying out water sports.
  • Mount Biokovo - This towering mountain offers visitors the chance to hike to the summit and take in panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Makarska

Makarska is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the best routes include:

  • Biokovo Skywalk - This suspended walkway offers a unique perspective of the stunning Biokovo mountain range.
  • St. Jure Summit Trail - This challenging trail leads to the highest peak of the Biokovo mountain range and offers breathtaking views of the coastline.
  • Makarska Riviera Bike Tour - This leisurely bike tour takes visitors through some of the region’s most picturesque villages and scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Makarska: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours - Explore the islands and secluded coves of the Adriatic Sea on a boat tour, which often includes activities like snorkeling and swimming.
  • Wine Tasting Tours - Croatia is renowned for its wine, and Makarska offers several wine tasting tours that allow visitors to sample some of the region’s best varietals.
  • Nightlife - Makarska has a vibrant nightlife scene, with several bars and clubs offering live music and DJ sets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Makarska

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s historic landmarks, including the Makarska Museum and Gallery and St. Mark’s Cathedral.
  • Day 2: Visit Biokovo Nature Park and hike along one of its scenic trails.
  • Day 3: Take a boat tour to explore the nearby islands and secluded coves.
  • Day 4: Sample some of the region’s renowned wine on a tasting tour.
  • Day 5: Relax on one of the town’s stunning beaches and try out some water sports.
  • Day 6: Take a leisurely bike tour through the picturesque villages of the Makarska Riviera.
  • Day 7: Enjoy the town’s vibrant nightlife scene and soak up the local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Makarska

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peka - A traditional Croatian dish made with meat, potatoes, and vegetables cooked under a bell-shaped lid.
  • Seafood Risotto - A rich and flavorful risotto made with fresh seafood and local ingredients.
  • Octopus Salad - A light and refreshing salad made with tender octopus, fresh herbs, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Croatian Wine - Makarska is located in the heart of Croatia’s wine-growing region, and visitors can sample some of the country’s best varietals.
  • Rakija - A traditional Croatian brandy made from distilled fruit.

Where to Stay in Makarska: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Makarska has something for every traveler. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hotel Osejava - A modern hotel located near the beach with stunning sea views and a range of amenities.
  • Hostel Makarska - A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of the town with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Villa Riva - A charming guesthouse located near the town center with spacious rooms and a quiet garden.

What to Buy in Makarska: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Croatian Products - Makarska offers a range of locally-made products, including olive oil, lavender, and honey.
  • Croatian Pottery - Handmade pottery is a popular souvenir in Croatia, and visitors can find a range of styles and designs in Makarska.
  • Beachwear and Local Fashion - With its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife, Makarska is a great place to pick up some new beachwear or local fashion items.

Local Culture and Customs of Makarska

Croatia has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors to Makarska can immerse themselves in the local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind during your visit include:

  • Respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Tipping is not expected, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small tip is appreciated.
  • Croatia is known for its relaxed and friendly culture, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals and immerse yourself in the local way of life.

Best Time to Visit Makarska

The best time to visit Makarska is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August are the busiest months, with more crowds and higher prices, so consider visiting in May, June, or September for a more peaceful experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Makarska

Do:

  • Explore the town’s rich history and cultural attractions.
  • Try some of the local seafood and wine.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities and stunning natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Haggle with shopkeepers or vendors, as it is not a common practice in Croatia.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or disrupt the peaceful way of life in the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Makarska

Makarska is a safe and welcoming town with a low crime rate. As with any travel destination, it is a good idea to remain aware of your surroundings and take common-sense precautions to stay safe.

Health in Makarska

Croatia has a good healthcare system, and visitors to Makarska can expect to find quality medical care if needed. It is recommended to travel with comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses and repatriation.

Getting to Makarska: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Makarska

The nearest international airport is Split Airport, located approximately 90 kilometers north of Makarska. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Makarska.

Bus Stations Near Makarska

Makarska has a central bus station with regular connections to cities and towns across Croatia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Makarska by car, ferry, or private transfer.

How to Get Around in Makarska

Makarska is a small town, and visitors can easily explore on foot. Buses and taxis are also available for longer distances or for getting to and from the airport.

Where to Go Next from Makarska: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dubrovnik - Known for its stunning Old Town, Dubrovnik is a popular destination located approximately 2.5 hours south of Makarska.
  • Split - The second-largest city in Croatia, Split is home to an abundance of historical landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • Hvar - Located on a picturesque island, Hvar is known for its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture, Makarska is a must-visit destination for travelers to Croatia. From exploring the town’s historic landmarks to soaking up the sun on the beach, there is something for everyone in this picturesque coastal town. Plan your visit today and discover all that Makarska has to offer.
