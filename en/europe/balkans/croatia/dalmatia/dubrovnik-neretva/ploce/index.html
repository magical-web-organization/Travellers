Ploče is a small town located in the Dubrovnik-Neretva County of Dalmatia, Croatia. It is situated on the Adriatic coast, making it an ideal destination for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get to Ploče to help you plan your next trip.

Introduction to Ploče

Ploče is a town with a rich history that dates back to ancient Roman times. It is located on the Neretva River delta and has a population of around 6,000 residents. The town boasts a beautiful natural setting, with stunning views of the sea and mountains. Ploče is also well-known for its bustling port, which is an important hub for local commerce.

List of Places to Visit in Ploče

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Franciscan Monastery: This 17th-century monastery is located on a hilltop and offers stunning views of the sea. It is home to a museum with a collection of valuable artworks, including paintings and sculptures.
  • Narona Archaeological Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of the area dating back to Roman times. It has extensive exhibits of Roman artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and coins.
  • Ploče City Museum: This museum highlights the history and culture of the town. It has permanent exhibits showcasing local art, crafts, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Ploče: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baćina Lakes: This is a collection of seven freshwater lakes located just a few kilometers north of Ploče. It is a perfect spot for swimming, fishing, and water sports.
  • Vidova Gora Mountain: This mountain is located in the Biokovo Nature Park and offers breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding countryside. It is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ploče

Ploče boasts a diverse range of hiking and biking routes that are suitable for all levels of experience. Some popular routes include:

  • Vidova Gora Mountain Trail: This hiking trail is a strenuous but rewarding climb to the summit of Vidova Gora. It offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Bacina Lakes Biking Trail: This is a scenic and relatively easy cycling route that takes you around the Bacina Lakes. It offers fantastic views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ploče: Activities and Tours

  • Beach Hop: Ploče is home to several beautiful beaches, each with its unique charm. Beach hopping is a perfect way to explore the coastline, soak up the sun, and experience the local culture.
  • Boat Tours: Ploče is a hub for local water transport, making it easy to explore the neighboring islands and towns. Boat tours are a fantastic way to enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
  • Wine Tasting: The surrounding area is known for its top-quality wines, so why not sample some local varieties with a wine tasting tour?

Week-Long Itinerary for Ploče

Day 1: Arrive in Ploče and check into your accommodation. Explore the town and enjoy a local meal. Day 2-4: Spend the mornings sunbathing on one of the nearby beaches. In the afternoons, explore the Bacina Lakes or take a bike ride along the coastline. Day 5: Take a boat tour to one of the neighboring islands, such as Korčula or Hvar. Day 6: Visit the Narona Archaeological Museum and the Franciscan Monastery. Day 7: Enjoy some wine tasting and relax on the beach before departing Ploče.

Food and Drink Scene of Ploče

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brudet: This is a traditional fish stew made with a variety of locally caught fish.
  • Peka: This dish is made by cooking meat and vegetables under a bell-shaped lid on an open fire. It is often served with potatoes and local herbs.
  • Pašticada: This is a classic Croatian meat dish made with beef, spices, and red wine.

Local Beverages

  • Croatian Wine: The surrounding area is known for its top-quality wines, with red and white varieties that pair well with local cuisine.
  • Rakija: This is a traditional Croatian brandy made from fruit, often served as a digestive or an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Ploče: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ploče offers a range of accommodation options, from small guesthouses to larger hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Mlini: This hotel is located in the center of Ploče and offers comfortable rooms and proximity to local attractions.
  • Apartment Tona: This is a family-run guesthouse with spacious and modern rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Ploče: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: The surrounding area is known for producing some of the best olive oil in the world. Pick up a bottle or two to take home.
  • Local Craft: Ploče has a rich tradition of handmade crafts, including ceramics, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Ploče

The people of Ploče are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors are often greeted with open arms. The town boasts a rich cultural heritage, with a vibrant arts and music scene.

Best Time to Visit Ploče

The best time to visit Ploče is in the summer when the weather is warm, and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, the town can get crowded during this time, so consider visiting in the shoulder seasons (May or September) for a quieter experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ploče

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially fresh seafood dishes.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beaches or in the town.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ploče

Ploče is a safe town, and crime rates are relatively low. However, it is always advisable to take precautions such as locking your doors and keeping your valuables safe.

Health in Ploče

Tap water in Ploče is safe to drink, and there is a hospital and several pharmacies in the town.

Getting to Ploče: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ploče

The nearest airport to Ploče is Dubrovnik Airport, located around 100 kilometers to the south. Several international airlines operate flights to Dubrovnik, making it a convenient transfer point for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Ploče

Ploče has a well-connected bus station with regular services to neighboring towns and cities, including Dubrovnik and Split.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Ploče is easily accessible by car. There are several rental car companies available at Dubrovnik Airport.

How to Get Around in Ploče

The town is small enough to explore on foot, but to venture further afield, consider renting a car or motorbike. There are also local buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Ploče: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dubrovnik: Known as “The Pearl of the Adriatic,” Dubrovnik is an iconic Croatian destination famous for its picturesque Old Town and stunning sea views.
  • Split: This vibrant city offers a mix of ancient history and modern attractions. Visit the Diocletian’s Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and explore the bustling markets and shops.

Conclusion

Ploče is a hidden gem in Croatia, offering a perfect mix of nature, culture, and history. With a rich culinary tradition, stunning natural scenery, and welcoming locals, it is an ideal destination for anyone looking for an authentic Croatian experience. Plan your next trip to Ploče with this comprehensive guide to ensure an unforgettable vacation.
