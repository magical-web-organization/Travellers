Introduction to Mljet

Dubbed the “greenest island” in the Adriatic, Mljet is a hidden gem in Croatia’s Dalmatia region. This picturesque island boasts pristine beaches, turquoise waters, lush forests, and breathtaking views. Its tranquil and unspoiled ambiance makes it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Mljet is located in southern Croatia, in the Dubrovnik-Neretva region, and is part of the Balkans. The island covers an area of approximately 100 square kilometers and has a population of around 1,100.

List of Places to Visit in Mljet

Mljet has numerous cultural and natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Mljet:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Odysseus Cave - A legendary cave mentioned in Homer’s Odyssey, this natural wonder can be explored by boat or swimming.
  • Mljet National Park - A protected area that covers around a third of the island, Mljet National Park boasts two saltwater lakes, a 12th-century Benedictine monastery, and hiking trails.
  • St. Mary’s Island - Located in Mljet National Park, this tiny island features a 17th-century church and stunning views.
  • Roman Palace - In the village of Polače, you’ll find the ruins of a Roman palace dating back to the 1st century AD.

Natural Attractions of Mljet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saplunara Beach - A sandy and secluded beach on the eastern side of the island.
  • Blace Beach - A picturesque pebble beach with shallow turquoise waters and stunning views of the mainland.
  • Cycling and Hiking Trails - Mljet has over 43 kilometers of well-marked and maintained trails that offer views of the island’s diverse landscapes, including hilltops, forests, and scenic coastlines.
  • Island of St. Ana - Located near Saplunara, St. Ana is a stunning islet famous for its untouched natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Mljet: Activities and Tours

Mljet offers a range of activities and tours that cater to diverse interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Mljet:

  • Kayak and Paddleboarding Tours - Explore hidden coves and beaches with a guided kayak or paddleboarding tour.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling - Discover the island’s diverse marine life with a scuba diving or snorkeling trip.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - Visit one of Mljet’s wineries and sample some of the local wines.
  • Sunset Sailing Tour - Enjoy a romantic sunset cruise along the island’s coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Mljet

Mljet’s cuisine is heavily influenced by its proximity to the sea and its natural bounty. Local dishes feature fresh seafood, grilled meats, and vegetables. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Octopus and Potato Salad - A refreshing and flavorful salad made with boiled octopus and potatoes seasoned with olive oil and vinegar.
  • Peka - A traditional Croatian dish made with lamb or veal, potatoes, and vegetables, slow-cooked under a bell-shaped lid, and served with homemade bread.
  • Grilled Fish - A simple yet delicious dish made with fresh fish drizzled with olive oil, lemon, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Plavac Mali Wine - A full-bodied red wine made from indigenous grape varietals grown on the island.
  • Rakija - A potent and aromatic brandy made from various fruits, including grapes, plums, and figs.
  • Herb Liqueurs - Mljet is known for its locally made herb liqueurs, such as sage and rosemary, which have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Mljet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mljet offers a range of accommodation options to suit various budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mljet:

  • Hotel Odisej - Located in Pomena, Hotel Odisej is a comfortable and affordable hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding sea and forest.
  • Villa Vinka - A family-run guesthouse in Saplunara that offers cozy rooms and an on-site restaurant serving local dishes.
  • Eco Art House - A unique and eco-friendly hostel located in the village of Babine Kuće, offering tranquil surroundings and creative arts workshops.

What to Buy in Mljet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mljet has a rich artisanal tradition, and you can find a variety of handmade products and souvenirs. Here are some unique items to bring home:

  • Olive Oil - Mljet is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is produced using traditional methods and is perfect for drizzling on salads or dipping bread.
  • Local Honey - Mljet’s honey, harvested from the island’s fragrant wildflowers, is renowned for its unique flavor and medicinal properties.
  • Ceramics - Mljet has a long tradition of pottery-making, and you can buy locally made ceramics, such as bowls, mugs, and vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Mljet

Mljet’s culture is heavily influenced by its natural surroundings and its history. The island has a rich folk tradition, and you can experience local music and dance performances during festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Mljet

The best time to visit Mljet is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, you can visit in the shoulder seasons of May or October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mljet

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the island’s natural and cultural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • litter or degrade the island’s natural habitats.
  • disregard safety precautions while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • behave inappropriately in religious or historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mljet

Mljet is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise caution when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking, cycling, or swimming, and follow proper safety guidelines.

Health in Mljet

Mljet has health centers and pharmacies that provide basic medical care. However, visitors should have travel insurance that covers emergency medical treatments. It’s also essential to carry insect repellent, as there is a risk of tick-borne illnesses in the island’s forests and fields.

Getting to Mljet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mljet is accessible by various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Mljet

The nearest international airport is Dubrovnik Airport, which has frequent flights to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Mljet

The closest bus station is in the town of Ston, on the Pelješac Peninsula, from where you can take a ferry to Mljet.

Other Options

You can also reach Mljet via private boat or yacht charter, which offers a unique and luxurious way to explore the island and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Mljet

Mljet is best explored on foot, bike, or boat. You can rent bicycles or boats in most villages, and there are several taxis and buses that operate on the island. However, the best way to explore Mljet is by hiking its well-marked trails, which offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Mljet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mljet’s location makes it an ideal base to explore nearby destinations. Here are some of the best places to visit near Mljet:

  • Korčula - This medieval town, located on the island of Korčula, is famous for its historic landmarks, such as the St. Mark’s Cathedral and the Marco Polo House.
  • Dubrovnik - Known as the “Pearl of the Adriatic,” Dubrovnik is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its historic city walls, stunning architecture, and cultural festivals.
  • National Park Krka - Located on the mainland, this protected area boasts stunning waterfalls, lakes, and trails.

Conclusion

Mljet is a beautiful and tranquil island in Croatia that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. From exploring ancient ruins and natural wonders to enjoying local cuisine and wine, there is something for everyone in Mljet. So pack your bags and start exploring this hidden gem.
