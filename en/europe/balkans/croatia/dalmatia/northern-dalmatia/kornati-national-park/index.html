Introduction to Kornati National Park

Kornati National Park is a stunning archipelago of 89 beautiful islands in Northern Dalmatia, Croatia. The park covers an area of 220 square kilometers and was declared a protected national parks on 7th July 1980. It is a part of the larger Kornati Cluster – a group of over 140 small islands and islets.

The park covers an area of nearly 220 square kilometers and it is dominated mainly by dolomitic limestone and covered with abandoned fields of olive groves, fig trees and vineyards. Kornati National Park is the ultimate destination for nature lovers, outdoor adventurers, and culture enthusiasts, with its unique geography, crystal-clear waters, and rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Kornati National Park

Kornati National Park has a lot to offer visitors with its stunning natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. In this section, we will highlight some of the top places to visit while exploring this national park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kornati National Park is rich in history and culture, with a variety of museums and historical sites to explore.

  • Tureta Fortress - Built-in the 6th century, this fortress still stands today, providing visitors a glimpse into the area’s significant historical events.
  • Church of St. Mary - An ancient Catholic Church made of stone and is one of the most prominent landmarks of this beautiful park.
  • Museum of Kornati – Located on the island of Kornat, this museum provides insights into the park’s history, underwater life, and ecosystem.

Natural Attractions of Kornati National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kornati National Park is known for its natural beauty, and there are plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy in the park.

  • Snorkeling and Scuba diving – Kornati National Park is an amazing place for snorkeling and scuba diving, offering people a unique chance to explore the park’s underwater life.
  • Boating – One of the best ways to explore Kornati National Park is by boat. Rent a boat or take a private tour to explore the different islands’ breathtaking views.
  • Swimming and sunbathing – Visitors can enjoy the crystal clear waters and relax on the beaches of Kornati National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kornati National Park

Exploring Kornati National Park’s natural beauty on foot or bike is a must-do activity for adventurous travelers. Visitors can choose from several well-marked trails, including:

  • Cycling Route - A 15 km trail that passes through some of the best sights, such as the island of Kornat, offering spectacular views.
  • Hiking Route - Visitors can hike the 7km trail, starting from the Telascica Bay and reach the peak of the highest point in Kornati Island, Metlina, at 237 meters.

Best Things to Do in Kornati National Park: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of exciting things to do and tours to take while visiting Kornati National Park. You can enjoy the park’s nature by choosing from the following activities:

  • Boat tours – Visits can explore the park’s numerous islands and hidden coves by signing up for boat tours such as from Telascica Bay.
  • Kayaking - It’s an excellent way to have an adventure and explore the park’s islands, nudging your kayaks to hidden beautiful beaches.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - Learn about the local culture and wine-making traditions by enjoying wine tasting sessions in the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kornati National Park

Here is a sample one-week itinerary that covers the top things to do and places to visit in Kornati National Park:

Day 1 Arrive in Kornati National Park, get settled

Food and Drink Scene of Kornati National Park

Kornati National Park is famous for its fresh seafood, traditional Dalmatian dishes, and local wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brodet - Brodet is a popular local fish stew, made with a variety of fresh fish and vegetables. It is perfect for a hearty lunch or dinner.
  • Grega’s beans - It is a classic Dalmatian dish made with boiled beans, tomato paste, and garlic. It is served with olive oil and fresh parsley.
  • Crni rizot - Black risotto is a local delicacy made with squid and squid ink. It has a unique dark color, and you’ll love its rich flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Babić red wine - Babić is a fruity red wine that pairs perfectly with seafood dishes.
  • Rakija - Rakija is a delicious Croatian brandy made with fruits such as cherries, figs, or grapes, and it is often served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Kornati National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kornati National Park is a great place to stay because it offers different types of accommodations that suit every budget and need. Here are some options:

  • Apartments - There are numerous apartments in the park’s surrounding area, and visitors can find rentals that fit their needs easily.
  • Guesthouses - For a more intimate experience of Kornati National Park, visitors can stay in a cosy guesthouse.
  • Campsites - For the more adventurous campers, there are a few campsites that offer the chance to sleep under the stars and wake up to the sound of wildlife.

What to Buy in Kornati National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kornati National Park offers a variety of unique and traditional items that visitors can take home as souvenirs. Here are some ideas:

  • Olive oil - Kornati National Park is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is made from indigenous olive varieties.
  • Sea salt - Sea salt production has a long tradition in the region, and visitors can take home a bag of high-quality sea salt as a souvenir.
  • Traditional lacework - Kornati National Park is home to a long lace-making tradition, and visitors can purchase beautifully crafted lace products.

Local Culture and Customs of Kornati National Park

Learning about local customs can offer an enriching experience for visitors. Here are some of the customs and traditions you might encounter in Kornati National Park:

  • Religious Festivals - Visitors can experience the local culture and traditions by participating in the park’s various religious festivals and processions.
  • Fishing - Fishing is an essential aspect of the local culture and is a way of life for many communities in the park.
  • Traditional Music and Dances - Visitors can experience the energetic and vibrant local music and dance performances.

Best Time to Visit Kornati National Park

The best time to visit Kornati National Park is from May to September. This period offers warm and sunny weather, perfect for water activities and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kornati National Park

Here are some of the recommended do’s and don’ts of visiting Kornati National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the park’s rules and preserve its natural beauty.
  • Pack sunscreen, a hat, and comfortable shoes to ensure your comfort during outdoor activities.
  • Try local seafood dishes and wines.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beach or in the park areas
  • Remove any plants or animals from the park
  • Swim alone and avoid going to dangerous or unmarked places inside the park.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips that visitors should be aware of while exploring Kornati National Park:

Safety in Kornati National Park

  • Always follow park signs and adhere to park regulations.
  • Check weather updates regularly before going on outdoor activities.
  • If you’re renting a boat, make sure to read and follow local boat regulations such as having proper safety equipment onboard.

Health in Kornati National Park

  • Bring enough drinking water, especially if you’re planning to hike or bike in the park.
  • Apply insect repellent and wear appropriate clothing to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Kornati National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kornati National Park is accessible by various modes of transportation. Visitors can take the following options:

Airports Near Kornati National Park

Zadar airport is the nearest airport to Kornati National Park, located 35km away. Split airport is also close by and located 60km away.

Bus Stations Near Kornati National Park

Zadar Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Kornati National Park. Visitors can take a taxi to the park’s entrance from the bus station or rent a car to drive to the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Kornati National Park by taking a ferry or catamaran from Zadar, a popular tourist destination in Croatia.

How to Get Around in Kornati National Park

Visitors can explore the park on foot/bike or take a boat tour to visit different islands in the park.

Where to Go Next from Kornati National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kornati National Park is located near many beautiful destinations that visitors can explore, such as:

  • Telascica Nature Park – It Is Located South of Kornati Park, characterized by coves and cliffs that rise above the sea.
  • Krka National Park – A short drive from Kornati Park, famous for its stunning waterfalls and Skradinski buk.
  • Zadar – A beautiful coastal city filled with stunning architecture, beaches and exciting attractions.

Conclusion

Kornati National Park is a beautiful and unique destination in Croatia that offers visitors unforgettable experiences in the park’s natural beauty. From cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to local cuisine and customs, there are so many things to see and do in this magical place. Plan your visit today to experience everything it has to offer.
