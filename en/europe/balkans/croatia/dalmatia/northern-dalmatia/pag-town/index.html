Introduction to Pag (Town)

Pag is a small town located on the Island of Pag in Northern Dalmatia, Croatia. The town is situated between the stunning Adriatic Sea and the rugged Velebit Mountain, boasting breathtaking views, a rich cultural heritage, and an authentic gastronomic experience. Pag town is known for its white lace, unique Pag cheese, and exquisite lamb.

Due to its secluded location, Pag town serves as a perfect destination for solitude-seekers and nature lovers who want to escape the bustling cities and immerse themselves in serenity, stunning landscapes, and history. Read on to find out all the places you must visit, activities to do, and food to try when in Pag, Croatia.

List of Places to Visit in Pag (Town)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pag Lace Gallery: The Gallery showcases the unique Pag lace, an intricate type of lace made by hand and passed down through generations of Pag women. The Gallery presents lace-making workshops where visitors can learn the techniques used to make the intricate patterns.

  • Duke’s Palace: Located in the center of the town, the Duke’s Palace is a classic medieval building with a fascinating history dating back to the early 14th century. The palace has a museum that showcases the cultural and historical traditions of Pag town.

  • The Salt Museum: The Salt Museum in the town of Pag is the first of its kind in the Adriatic region. The Museum displays the history and production of Pag Salt, which has been extracted from the Adriatic Sea for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Pag (Town): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zrce Beach: If you are looking for a place to relax and soak up the sun, Zrce Beach is the perfect place. The Beach is famous for its crystal clear blue water, white sand, and a vibrant nightlife.

  • Pag Triangle Route: This scenic drive offers a breathtaking view of rocky hills, crystal clear blue sea, and the neighboring islands. The route is popular among hikers and bikers.

  • Sveti Vid Mountain: The highest peak on the island of Pag offers mesmerizing views of the Adriatic Sea. This peak is perfect for hiking and watching the sunrise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pag (Town)

  • Pag Triangle Route: It is an excellent route for both hiking and biking enthusiasts. The 140-kilometer route offers a breathtaking view of the island and neighboring islands.

  • St. Roko Path: A path that leads to the highest peak, Sveti Vid. It is a challenging route but is worth the effort once at the summit.

  • Lun Olive Grove Trail: It is a 10-kilometer trail that takes you through the Lun Olive Grove where you can view over 1,500 olive trees that are over 1,000 years old.

Best Things to Do in Pag (Town): Activities and Tours

  • Cheese and Wine Tasting: Pag Town is known for its authentic Pag cheese and excellent wine. Take a guided tour and taste some of the best wine and cheese from the region.

  • Boat Tour: Explore the Adriatic Sea and the neighboring islands with a boat tour. Enjoy swimming in the crystal clear blue water, snorkeling, and admiring the stunning views.

  • Bungee Jumping: If you are an adrenaline junkie, Pag town has a spot for bungee jumping. Take a plunge from the bridge and get an unforgettable experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pag (Town)

Day 1: Arrive in Pag and relax by the beach Day 2: Explore the Duke’s Palace and the Pag Lace Gallery Day 3: Hike up Sveti Vid Mountain and witness the stunning views Day 4: Rent a bike and cycle through the Lun Olive Grove Trail Day 5: Participate in cheese and wine tasting Day 6: Take a boat tour around neighboring islands Day 7: Bungee jump from a bridge and end the trip on an adventurous note.

Food and Drink Scene of Pag (Town)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb: The lamb in Pag town is a must-try dish that is cooked in a traditional way. It is served with roasted potatoes and a side of cold white wine.

  • Pag Cheese: The authentic Pag cheese is the most popular cheese in the area and is made from the milk of sheep that graze on the island’s unique vegetation.

  • Fritule: It is a traditional pastry that is deep-fried and served with jam. It is a perfect dessert or snack.

Local Beverages

  • Žutica: A traditional Croatian beer that has a sweet taste with a slight bitterness.

  • Babić: A red wine that is common in the region and has a strong flavor.

  • Lozovača: It is an herb brandy that is popular in the area and is served as a digestive after meal.

Where to Stay in Pag (Town): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Pagus: A modern and luxurious hotel that offers a range of amenities including a pool, spa, and gym.

  • Pansion Vila Marija: A cozy guesthouse that is located near the center of the town.

  • Hostel Pag: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable accommodation for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Pag (Town): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pag Lace: The intricate lace that is made in Pag is a beautiful and unique souvenir to bring home.

  • Pag Cheese: Buy the authentic cheese that is made from the milk of the sheep that graze on the island’s unique vegetation.

  • Salt from Pag: The Pag salt has been extracted from the Adriatic Sea for centuries and has an authentic taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Pag (Town)

  • Pag Lace: Pag Lace is a unique handicraft that is passed down through generations of Pag women. The lace has a distinctive design and is used to make various types of clothing and home decor.

  • Local Festivals: Pag town hosts various cultural and religious festivals throughout the year, including the Pag Summer Carnival, which happens in July, and the Pag Lace Festival, which happens in June.

Best Time to Visit Pag (Town)

The best time to visit Pag town is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and warm. The peak season is from July to August, and the town is bustling with tourists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pag (Town)

Pag town is generally safe, but visitors should take precautions and avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Pag (Town)

Pag town has several medical facilities, including pharmacies, clinics, and hospitals. Visitors should have travel insurance and be aware of any potential health hazards, such as sunburn and heatstroke.

Getting to Pag (Town): Transportation Options and Logistics

Pag town is accessible by road and sea.

Airports Near Pag (Town)

  • Zadar Airport: The closest airport to Pag town is Zadar airport, which is 54 kilometers away from the town.

  • Split Airport: The Split airport is around 239 kilometers away from Pag town.

Bus Stations Near Pag (Town)

  • Zadar Bus Station: Bus services run frequently from Zadar to Pag town.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are regular ferries that run from the port of Prizna to the island of Pag.

How to Get Around in Pag (Town)

The best way to get around Pag town is by renting a bike or car. Taxis and public transportation are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Pag (Town): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zadar: A city on the mainland that is only an hour’s drive from Pag. Zadar is known for its rich history, excellent restaurants, and stunning seaside views.

  • Kornati National Park: This national park is famous for its unique biodiversity and stunning landscapes. Take a boat tour from Pag town to explore the park.

  • Krka National Park: This park is known for its beautiful waterfalls, rivers, and hiking trails.

Pag town is a hidden gem that offers a unique experience for visitors. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and authentic gastronomy, Pag town is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and foodies.
