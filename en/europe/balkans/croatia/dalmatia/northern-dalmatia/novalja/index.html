Introduction to Novalja

Novalja is a small town located on the island of Pag, in Northern Dalmatia, Croatia. It’s a place best known for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and outdoor activities, making it a popular summer destination among tourists. The town itself is surrounded by serene nature, with crystal-clear sea on one side and rugged mountains on the other, providing visitors with a unique experience of both land and sea. With its vibrant atmosphere and rich cultural heritage, Novalja is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Croatian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Novalja

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Novalja City Museum: Housed in a charming 15th-century house, this museum showcases the cultural and historical heritage of Novalja and its surroundings.
  • The Pag Lace Gallery: A unique museum dedicated to the world-renowned Pag lace, a traditional handmade lace famous for its intricate designs.
  • The Roman Aqueduct: Visit the remains of this ancient aqueduct, built in the 1st century to supply water to the town of Novalja and witness one of the greatest engineering feats of the past.

Natural Attractions of Novalja: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zrce Beach: This famous beach is known for its crystal-clear waters, stunning scenery, and vibrant nightlife. It’s a popular spot for beach sports like beach volleyball and wakeboarding.
  • Lun Olive Grove: A unique place where visitors can explore ancient olive groves that date back to the Roman Empire.
  • Planjka Beach: A secluded beach surrounded by stunning cliffs and crystal-clear waters, perfect for relaxation and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novalja

  • Stogaj Hill: A challenging hike that rewards you with stunning views of Novalja Bay and the islands of Rab and Cres.
  • Girenica Hill: A moderate hike that takes you through Novalja’s rolling hills, olive groves, and scenic vineyards.
  • Pag Island Bike Trail: A 115-kilometer trail that crosses the island of Pag, offering visitors various landscapes and unforgettable views of the Adriatic Sea.

Best Things to Do in Novalja: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Explore the vineyards and olive groves of Novalja, learn about the region’s winemaking traditions and taste some of the best wines produced on the island.
  • Rent a Boat Tour: Rent a boat and explore the hidden beaches and coves of Novalja’s coastline at your own pace.
  • Diving Tour: Discover the underwater world of Novalja, with its unique marine fauna and flora, and explore the sunken shipwrecks and WWII artifacts found in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Novalja

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pag Cheese: This is the most famous cheese produced on the island of Pag. It has a distinct aroma and flavor due to the unique conditions under which it is produced.
  • Lamb from Pag Island: Pag island’s lamb is known for its tender meat and delicate flavor, thanks to the aromatic herbs that the sheep graze on.
  • Octopus Salad: A light and refreshing dish made with tender octopus, tomatoes, onions, and parsley.

Local Beverages

  • Pag Island Wine: Produced from the local grape varieties, Pag wines are known for their rich aromas and flavors, making them a great accompaniment to local dishes.
  • Pag Island Brandy: Made from the local herbs, Pag Island brandy is a strong and aromatic drink best consumed as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Novalja: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Luna: A modern and stylish hotel located in the heart of Novalja, close to all the main attractions of the town.
  • Hostel 1: Affordable and comfortable hostel located within walking distance of the town center and the beaches.
  • Apartment Seaside: Fully equipped apartments located near the beaches of Zrce and the town center, providing visitors with a comfortable and secluded stay.

What to Buy in Novalja: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pag Lace: A unique and valuable souvenir, handmade by the local women of Pag using a traditional technique passed down generations.
  • Pag Salt: Known for its pure, exquisite taste, this sea salt is harvested from the centuries-old saltworks on the island of Pag.
  • Pag Cheese and Wines: Take home locally produced Pag Island cheese and wines to enjoy the flavors of Northern Dalmatia long after you’ve left Novalja.

Local Culture and Customs of Novalja

  • Family Ties: Family ties are very important in Novalja, and family-owned businesses make up the majority of the town’s dining and accommodation options.
  • Religious Celebrations: Novalja has a rich religious heritage, with many celebrations and festivals, including the famous Feast of St. Rok, taking place throughout the year.
  • Pag Lace Making: Pag Lace making is a traditional skill passed down through generations of women on the island of Pag and is a UNESCO-protected tradition.

Best Time to Visit Novalja

The best time to visit Novalja is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches and outdoor activities are in full swing. July and August are the peak tourism months, with many festivals and events taking place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novalja

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the island’s natural beauty and historical heritage.
  • Try the local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches.
  • Disrespect the locals or their traditions.
  • Damage the fragile ecosystem of the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Novalja

Novalja is a safe town, with a low crime rate, and it’s safe to walk around day or night. However, keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places like beaches or festivals.

Health in Novalja

Novalja has several medical facilities, including a hospital and a pharmacy. If you need medical assistance, ask your hotel or accommodation provider for guidance.

Getting to Novalja: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Novalja

The nearest airports to Novalja are the Zadar and Rijeka airports, both around 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Novalja

Novalja has a bus station located in the town center, with daily connections to nearby cities like Zadar, Split, and Zagreb.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Novalja by ferry or by car via the Pag Bridge, which connects the island of Pag to the mainland.

How to Get Around in Novalja

The best way to get around Novalja is on foot or by bicycle, as most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Novalja: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zadar: A beautiful town known for its Venetian and Roman ruins, located 80 kilometers from Novalja.
  • Krka National Park: A stunning national park known for its waterfalls and nature trails, located around 150 kilometers from Novalja.
  • Plitvice Lakes National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its stunning lakes and waterfalls, located around 200 kilometers from Novalja.

Conclusion

Novalja is a charming town that offers something for everyone, from stunning beaches to rich cultural heritage, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Croatian experience. Plan your trip wisely, and follow our guide to make sure you don’t miss out on anything this beautiful town has to offer.
