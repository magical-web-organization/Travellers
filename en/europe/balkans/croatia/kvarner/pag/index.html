Introduction to Pag

Located in the northern part of Dalmatia, part of Croatia’s Kvarner region, lies the mesmerizing island of Pag. Characterized by its rugged and rocky terrain, crystal-clear waters, and stunning landscapes, Pag is a perfect destination for travelers who are looking for a less crowded, off-beat destination. With long stretches of sandy beaches, great hiking and biking trails, and a wealth of local culture and traditions, Pag offers endless opportunities to create unforgettable memories.

List of Places to Visit in Pag

Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or with family, Pag has something for everyone. Here’s a list of must-visit places while you’re on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Salt: a must-visit for history buffs and those interested in the culture and traditions of the region. The museum showcases the history of salt mining, an integral part of Pag’s economy for centuries.
  • Pag Lace Gallery: displaying some of the finest examples of Croatian lace, the Pag Lace Gallery is a great spot to learn about the island’s tradition of lace making.
  • The Franciscan Monastery of St. Mary: a well-preserved 15th-century monastery that houses a rich collection of artworks and religious artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Pag: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zrće Beach: known as the party capital of Croatia, Zrće Beach is perfect for travelers looking for non-stop fun and entertainment. With a lively atmosphere, crystal blue waters and hosts of adrenaline-fueled water sports activities, Zrće Beach is a must-visit for everyone.
  • Vidova Gora: offering spectacular views of the entire island, Vidova Gora is the highest peak in all of Croatia. A perfect spot for nature lovers and hikers, Vidova Gora is a treat for photographers too.
  • Pag Triangle: located near the village of Zavratnica, the Pag Triangle is a natural phenomenon that has puzzled scientists for centuries. Comprising of three rocks standing in a triangular formation, the Pag Triangle is a fascinating site to witness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pag

Pag Island is a paradise for outdoor adventurers, with a plethora of trails, paths, and roads that offer thrilling experiences for hikers and bikers alike. Here are some routes to explore:

  • Pag Karst Trail: stretching across 85 km, the Pag Karst Trail is perfect for hikers. The trail takes you through some of the most scenic areas of the island and features stunning views of the coastline.
  • Cycling Route no 15: cycling enthusiasts will love this route that covers most of the island and takes you through some of the most picturesque regions, including Blato and Bašaca.

Best Things to Do in Pag: Activities and Tours

  • Wine tasting: Pag Island is famous for its wines, particularly its white wine variety. Plan a visit to Lun and Stara Novalja wineries, and you’ll be treated to some of the finest wines Croatia has to offer.
  • Cheese tasting: Pag is famous for producing one of the most expensive cheeses in the world - the Paski Sir or Pag cheese. A visit to the Gligora Dairy is a must if you’re a cheese lover.
  • Cultural tours: exploring the island’s rich history and traditions is a great way to experience the local culture. Join a cultural tour and visit some of the island’s iconic landmarks and cultural sites with an experienced guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pag

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Salt, explore the old town of Pag, and dine at a local restaurant. Day 2: Take a boat trip to the neighboring island of Rab. Day 3: Go wine tasting at Lun and Stara Novalja wineries, followed by a visit to the Gligora Dairy. Day 4: Hike along the Pag Karst Trail and visit the stunning Vidova Gora peak. Day 5: Explore the Zrće Beach and indulge in some water sports activities. Day 6: Take a cultural tour of the island and discover Pag’s rich history and traditions. Day 7: Relax at one of the island’s sandy beaches, followed by a dinner at a local seafood restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Pag

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paski Sir: the island’s most famous product, the Pag cheese is a must-try for cheese lovers.
  • Kuhani Paski Škarpin: a Pag specialty, Kuhani Paski Škarpin comprises of John Dory fish, potatoes, and onions cooked in white wine.
  • Janjetina pod Peke: a traditional Croatian dish, Janjetina pod Peke is made from lamb roasted under a bell.

Local Beverages

  • White wine: Pag Island is home to some of Croatia’s finest white wines.
  • Rakija: a popular Croatian brandy that includes various fruit varieties.

Where to Stay in Pag: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pag offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference.

  • Hotel Plaza: a luxury hotel situated near the old town of Pag that boasts stunning sea views.
  • Camp Straško: a family-friendly campsite with excellent facilities and location.
  • Apartments Gospoja: a budget-friendly apartment option situated near Zrće Beach.

What to Buy in Pag: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Paski Sir: a perfect gift for cheese lovers.
  • Pag Lace: an excellent souvenir if you’re interested in Croatian handicrafts.
  • Olive Oil: Pag Island’s olive groves are known for producing some of the finest olive oils in Croatia.

Local Culture and Customs of Pag

Pag Island has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their traditions.

  • Music and dancing: Pag Island has a rich musical tradition, with Klapa, a type of Dalmatian folk singing, being popular with the locals.
  • Lace making: Pag lace has been declared an important cultural heritage of Croatia by UNESCO. Take a tour of one of the lace-making workshops and learn about the traditional art form.
  • Salt mining: Salt has been an important part of Pag’s economy for centuries. Take a tour of the Museum of Salt and find out how the salt mining industry has shaped the island’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Pag

The best time to visit Pag Island is during the summer (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, if you’re interested in outdoor activities like hiking and biking, the best time to visit is during Spring (April and May) and Autumn (September and October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Pag

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the island’s famous cheese and wine.
  • Explore the island’s rugged and picturesque landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Swim in restricted areas or during bad weather conditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pag

Pag is considered to be a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Pag

Croatia has a well-developed healthcare system, and medical facilities on the island are up to par with international standards. It’s important to have adequate travel insurance before visiting the island.

Getting to Pag: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pag

Zadar Airport is the closest airport to Pag, located approximately 70 km away. From there, you can either rent a car or take a bus to the island.

Bus Stations Near Pag

Buses run regularly from Zadar to Pag, with the journey taking approximately 2 hours.

Other Options

If you plan to drive to Pag from the mainland, you can take the Pag Bridge or the Prizna-Zigljen Ferry.

How to Get Around in Pag

The best way to explore the island is by car or bike. You can also use public transport, such as local buses or taxis, to get around.

Where to Go Next from Pag: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zadar: a popular tourist destination known for its historical sites, museums, and stunning coastline.
  • Krka National Park: located approximately 100 km away from Pag, Krka National Park features stunning waterfalls and hiking trails.
  • Plitvice Lakes National Park: a UNESCO World Heritage Site located approximately 150 km away from Pag, Plitvice Lakes National Park is famous for its lush landscapes and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Pag Island is a hidden gem in Croatia, offering visitors a perfect blend of stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisines. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or a mix of the two, Pag Island has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set to plan your trip to Pag and create unforgettable memories that you’ll cherish forever.
