Introduction to Malinska

Nestled in the heart of the island of Krk in the Kvarner region of Croatia lays Malinska – an idyllic coastal town known for its gorgeous sandy beaches, lush greenery, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. The town’s unique blend of modern life with rich culture, delicious cuisine, and rustic scenery has made it a popular travel destination in recent years, attracting visitors from all over the world. In this guide, we’ll explore Malinska’s top attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Malinska

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Franciscan Monastery - A medieval architectural marvel that houses precious artworks, a museum, and a chapel, offering breathtaking views of the sea.
  • The Church of St. Mary Magdalene - A 16th-century Neo-Gothic church. Also, visit the lovely square (Trg sv. Franje) right next to it.
  • Porat Village - A quaint and picturesque village steeped in history with unique architecture and narrow streets.

Natural Attractions of Malinska: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rajska Plaža - One of the most popular beaches on the island with crystal clear water and golden sand surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Malinska Beach - A beautiful beach consisting of both sand and gravel, excellent for swimming and sunbathing, offering fantastic views of the sunset and the surrounding hills.
  • St. Nicholas - A beautiful little island sanctuary located in the bay of Malinska, available for hire with boat and perfect for an evening picnic while watching the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malinska

Malinska is surrounded by natural beauty and is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some fantastic hiking and biking routes that you should explore:

  • Malinska - Porat - Njivice - A 20 km cycle route along the coast that passes through scenic landscapes and fascinating villages. The route is moderately challenging but rewarding, with plenty of cafes and restaurants.
  • Haludovo Beach - Rajska Plaža - A 6 km hiking trail perfect for exploring Krk’s wild nature.
  • Veli Vrh - Cross the island - A demanding 16 km hike that offers a fascinating way to explore the scenic landscape of Malinska.

Best Things to Do in Malinska: Activities and Tours

  • Windsurfing and Kitesurfing - Malinska’s windsurfing and kitesurfing spots are among the best in the region, with the ideal wind conditions and ample opportunities for beginners and experienced surfers alike. Kiteboarding school is available in the area.
  • Jet Skiing - Adrenaline-seekers can opt for jet-ski rentals and experience the thrill of speeding along Malinska’s coast.
  • Boat Tours - Take a boat tour to explore Malinska’s coast, learn more about the region’s history and culture, and enjoy breathtaking landscapes from the sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malinska

Day 1: Spend the day relaxing at Rajska Plaža and enjoying water activities like paddleboarding or scuba diving.

Day 2: Explore Malinska’s history by visiting the Franciscan Monastery and the Church of St. Mary Magdalene.

Day 3: Take a hike on the island’s trails, which offers breathtaking scenery and a chance to connect with nature.

Day 4: Go for a bike ride along the coast, and afterward, explore Porat Village.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the neighboring islands of Susak or Cres to experience the difference in lifestyle and culture.

Day 6: Explore Malinska’s nightlife, which features many bars, pubs, and nightclubs.

Day 7: Head out on a boat tour to nearby attractions and see the area’s natural beauty from the sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Malinska

A visit to Malinska is not complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb in Peka - A local delicacy where lamb is combined with potatoes and vegetables and baked under a dome-shaped lid for several hours.

  • Fresh Seafood - With Malinska’s coast, this is a no brainer. Don’t miss out on the delicacies such as black squid ink pasta, grilled octopus, and traditional fish dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija - The local grappa made from local fruits such as plum, apricot, and cherry.

  • Wine - A prime location for wine production, Malinska offers many local varieties, such as Vrbnička Žlahtina and Malvazija Istarska.

Where to Stay in Malinska: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malinska offers a variety of accommodations to match all types of travelers, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses, motels, and hostels to luxurious hotels, private villas, and Airbnb homes.

  • Boutique Hotel Marina - A luxurious beachfront hotel in the center of Malinska, which offers sea views, gyms, massages, and wellness centers.

  • Bed and Breakfast Villa Dagmar - A peaceful, secluded retreat, which has Air-conditioned rooms, a private pool in the garden, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Malinska: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lavender - Croatia’s most known souvenir that you can buy in Malinska. You can find everything from sachets to essential oils, soaps, candles, and more.

  • Olive Oil - Krk island produces some of the best organic olive oil that you can buy straight from local farmers.

Local Culture and Customs of Malinska

Malinska culture is a mix of Croatian and Italian, with a relaxed lifestyle and friendly locals. Croatia and Italy have influenced Malinska with a cuisine enriched with olive oil, herbs, seafood, and excellent wine. The locals are famously friendly, so don’t be afraid to ask for directions in case of getting lost.

Best Time to Visit Malinska

The best time to visit Malinska is from May to September when the weather is sunny and warm, and swim-friendly temperatures. July and August can be crowded, so try to avoid these months if you want a quieter experience. However, the off-peak season offers cheaper prices and emptier beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malinska

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially seafood dishes.

  • Plan outdoor activities during daylight hours.

  • Learn to say a few basic Croatian phrases, such as “Dobar Dan” (Good day) and “Hvala” (Thank you).

Don’t:

  • Expect locals to speak fluent English. Although many locals speak English, it’s better to learn the basic Croatian phrases.

  • Forget to wear sunscreen, as sunburns are common near the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malinska

Malinska is a safe and friendly town, but be aware of pickpockets and bag-snatchers in crowded areas and beach clubs. Respect the natural environment when hiking or cycling in national parks.

Health in Malinska

Malinska has many medical offices and pharmacies, but you should have traveler’s insurance since medical treatment can be costly. Don’t drink tap water; always go for the bottled one.

Getting to Malinska: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malinska

The nearest airport to Malinska is Rijeka International Airport, which offers flights to and from several international destinations. Private transfers or rental cars are available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Malinska

Malinska has a bus station located in the town center, which offers regular bus services to other destinations on the island. Flixbus also operates to Malinska from various international cities.

Other Options

  • Ferries - There are daily ferry services linking Rijeka to Krk.

  • Taxi - Taxi services are available throughout the town and offer safe and reliable transfers.

How to Get Around in Malinska

Malinska is small enough to explore on foot, but if you plan on visiting the outer regions or island hopping, you can opt for taxis, rental cars, or motorbikes.

Where to Go Next from Malinska: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Krk Town - Explore the island’s administrative hub, which is rich in history, culture, and beautiful beaches.

  • Baska - Discover the national park’s breathtaking mountainous landscape and scenic beaches, only 30 kilometers away from Malinska.

  • Vrbnik - a tiny, picturesque village located on a hill above the sea and the sprawling vineyards.

Conclusion

Malinska is a charming coastal town, perfect for a relaxing seaside retreat. It provides a unique blend of natural beauty, historical attractions, delicious cuisine, and vibrant nightlife. With this comprehensive guide, you will have all the insider knowledge to explore the best that Malinska has to offer.
