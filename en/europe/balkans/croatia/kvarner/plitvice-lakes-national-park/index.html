Introduction to Plitvice Lakes National Park

Plitvice Lakes National Park is a natural wonderland situated in Kvarner, Croatia. It is home to a series of 16 interconnected lakes, their cascading waterfalls, and breathtaking limestone canyons. The park spans over 295 square kilometers and, as a United Nations Educational, Scientific, and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) World Heritage site, attracts over a million visitors every year.

The park is open all year round, and each season provides a unique experience for visitors. In the winter, the park is blanketed with snow and transformed into a winter wonderland. In the spring and summer, the park comes alive with blooming flowers and a vibrant green landscape. And in the fall, the changing colors of the leaves provide stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Plitvice Lakes National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in the history and culture of Plitvice Lakes National Park, there are a few museums and historical sites to visit:

  • Plitvice Lakes Museum: This museum, located near Entrance 2, provides insight into the history and development of the park, including the flora and fauna of the area.

  • The Homeland War Museum: This museum, located in the village of Turanj, just a short drive from the park, provides a unique insight into the war that occurred in Croatia from 1990 to 1995.

  • Old Town Drežnik: This historic town, located just outside the park, dates back to the 11th century and boasts a medieval fortress, a round tower, and the Church of St. George.

Natural Attractions of Plitvice Lakes National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking adventure or wanting to experience the natural beauty of the park, there are many scenic views and outdoor activities to explore:

  • Veliki Slap: This waterfall, which stands at an impressive height of 78 meters, is one of the tallest in Croatia and can be seen from multiple vantage points within the park.

  • Kozjak Lake: The largest lake within the park, Kozjak offers many opportunities for boating, swimming, and hiking. Visitors can hike the path that circles the lake or take a boat ride to explore its tranquil waters.

  • Plitvice Lakes Walking Trails: There are eight different walking trails to choose from, each offering its own unique experience of the park’s natural beauty. The trails range from easy to moderate in difficulty and vary in length from 3.5 kilometers to 18 kilometers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plitvice Lakes National Park

For the more adventurous visitors, Plitvice Lakes National Park offers many hiking and biking routes:

  • Hiking Trail A: This trail is the shortest and takes visitors through the upper lakes and along some of the park’s most iconic waterfalls.

  • Hiking Trail B: This trail is slightly longer and takes visitors further into the park, providing views of the lower lakes.

  • Hiking Trail C: This trail is the longest and takes visitors through the entire park. Visitors can expect to spend around six hours hiking this trail.

  • Biking Trails: For those who prefer biking, there are several bike trails to explore, including a 6-kilometer trail through the forest and a 17-kilometer trail that takes visitors through the park’s most beautiful landscape.

Best Things to Do in Plitvice Lakes National Park: Activities and Tours

For those who would like a more guided experience, there are many tours and activities available:

  • Guided Walking Tours: Guided walking tours through the park are available in several languages and include expert insights into the park’s history, flora, and fauna.

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of the park’s tranquil lakes, providing stunning and unique vantage points of the park’s natural beauty.

  • Zip Line Tours: For the adrenaline-seekers out there, a zip line tour is available, providing a thrilling perspective of the park’s landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plitvice Lakes National Park

If you have a week to spare, here is a suggested itinerary for exploring Plitvice Lakes National Park:

  • Day 1-2: Visit the museums and historical sites in and around the park.

  • Day 3-4: Explore the upper and lower lakes on hiking trails A and B.

  • Day 5: Take a boat tour of Kozjak Lake and explore the surrounding area on foot.

  • Day 6: Take a biking tour through the park.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the serene beauty of the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Plitvice Lakes National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Croatian cuisine is famous for its fresh seafood, grilled meats, and diverse range of cuisines. Some of the must-try dishes of Plitvice Lakes National Park include:

  • Pašticada: A traditional Croatian dish that is slow-cooked beef stew served over homemade pasta.

  • Ćevapi: Grilled minced meat served with onions and bread.

  • Strudel with Cottage Cheese: The dessert is a perfect end to any meal, made with thin phyllo dough filled with sweetened cottage cheese.

Local Beverages

Croatia is famous for its production of wine and spirits. Some of the local beverages to try include:

  • Rakija: A type of fruit brandy made from plums, grapes, or apricots.

  • Malvazija: A white wine produced in the Istrian peninsula that is crisp and refreshing.

  • Plavac Mali: A red wine produced in the Dalmatia region that is full-bodied and flavorful.

Where to Stay in Plitvice Lakes National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in and around Plitvice Lakes National Park, suited to any taste and budget. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Jezero: Located within the park, this hotel offers lakeside views and modern amenities.

  • Ethno Houses Plitvica Selo: This is a unique accommodation option that combines natural and traditional elements to create a cozy and authentic experience.

  • Villa Mukinja: This hotel is located just a few minutes’ walk from the park entrance and offers comfortable, modern rooms and excellent cuisine.

What to Buy in Plitvice Lakes National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those wanting to bring a piece of Plitvice Lakes National Park home, here are some souvenirs to consider:

  • Honey: Plitvice Lakes National Park has a rich history of beekeeping, and its honey is known for its unique flavour and high quality.

  • Lavender Products: Lavender is grown in the park and can be found in a variety of products, including essential oils, soaps, and lotions.

  • Homemade Jams: Berries grow wild in the park, and homemade jams made from raspberries, strawberries, and blueberries can be found at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Plitvice Lakes National Park

The local culture of Plitvice Lakes National Park is a mixture of traditional Croatian elements and the unique culture of the area. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering homes or churches.

Best Time to Visit Plitvice Lakes National Park

The best time to visit Plitvice Lakes National Park is during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall. The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. However, each season provides unique experiences in the park, so it is recommended to visit at any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plitvice Lakes National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Stay on designated paths and trails.

  • Bring comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for hiking.

  • Check weather conditions before starting your hike or bike ride.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife.

  • Litter in the park.

  • Smoke or light fires in non-designated areas.

  • Bring pets into the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plitvice Lakes National Park

While Plitvice Lakes National Park is relatively safe, visitors should be aware of potential risks. Visitors should watch their footing on wet or slippery paths or rocks and should be aware of falling rocks or other natural hazards. Visitors should also be aware of the threat of forest fires and follow all park rules and regulations to prevent them.

Health in Plitvice Lakes National Park

Visitors should be aware of the potential effects of the park’s high altitude and should take necessary precautions, such as drinking plenty of water and wearing sunscreen and a hat. Those with existing medical conditions should consult a physician prior to visiting the park to ensure that it is safe for them to do so.

Getting to Plitvice Lakes National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plitvice Lakes National Park

The two closest airports to Plitvice Lakes National Park are Zagreb International Airport, located about two hours’ drive away, and Zadar Airport, located about an hour and a half away.

Bus Stations Near Plitvice Lakes National Park

There are several bus stations near Plitvice Lakes National Park that serve destinations both near and far. The most popular bus station is in the nearby town of Korenica, where visitors can take a bus to Split, Dubrovnik, and Zagreb, among other destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Plitvice Lakes National Park by car or by joining a guided tour.

How to Get Around in Plitvice Lakes National Park

Visitors can either explore Plitvice Lakes National Park on foot, by bike, or by boat. Many guided tours are also available for those who prefer a guided experience.

Where to Go Next from Plitvice Lakes National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those who want to explore more of Croatia, there are many destinations nearby that are worth visiting:

  • Zagreb: Croatia’s capital city, located about two hours’ drive away, provides a unique blend of tradition and modernity.

  • Zadar: A coastal town located about an hour and a half away, known for its stunning sunsets and historic landmarks.

  • Split: Another popular coastal town, located about three hours’ drive away, known for its ancient Roman architecture and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Plitvice Lakes National Park is a truly magical destination that should be on every traveler’s itinerary. Its natural beauty and diverse range of attractions make it a must-visit destination in Croatia. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, culture, or history, Plitvice Lakes National Park has something for everyone.
