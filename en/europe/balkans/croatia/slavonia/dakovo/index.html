Đakovo is a charming town located in Slavonia, a region in Croatia known for its scenic beauty and rich history. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Đakovo has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Đakovo.

Introduction to Đakovo

Đakovo is known for its beautiful countryside, rich cultural heritage, and sports events. The town is situated on the right bank of the Drava River, surrounded by green fields and forests. The town was founded in the 9th century and has a rich history that can be seen in its architecture and museums.

List of Places to Visit in Đakovo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Đakovo Cathedral: Visiting Đakovo Cathedral is a must-do when in town. The cathedral is one of the largest and most beautiful in Croatia, with its Romanesque and Gothic architecture. It is also the seat of the Archdiocese of Đakovo-Osijek, making it an important religious and cultural center.
  • Gothic City Hall: Đakovo’s Gothic City Hall is a stunning example of Gothic architecture. The building has a rich history and was first built in the 18th century. It is now a popular tourist attraction and houses a museum on the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Đakovo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kopacki Rit Nature Park: Kopacki Rit is one of Europe’s largest wetlands and is home to many rare and endangered species of birds and animals. Visitors can take a boat ride through the wetlands or take a walk along the many nature trails.
  • Biljsko plato plateau: Biljsko plato is a beautiful plateau situated on the Drava River. It is a perfect spot for hiking, fishing, and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Đakovo

Đakovo has many beautiful hiking and biking routes that will take you through picturesque villages, lavender fields, and vineyards. The Kocje bike trail is a 50 km long trail that offers stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Đakovo: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding: Đakovo is famous for its purebred Lipizzaner horses. Visitors can take a guided tour of the state stud farm and watch the beautiful horses in action.
  • Wine tasting: Slavonia is known for its wine production, and Đakovo has many vineyards where visitors can taste the local wines.
  • Sports events: Đakovo hosts many sports events throughout the year, including the Đakovo International Half Marathon and the International Equestrian Tournament.

Week-Long Itinerary for Đakovo

Day 1: Visit the Đakovo Cathedral and the Gothic City Hall. Day 2: Explore the Kopacki Rit Nature Park. Day 3: Take a bike tour of the surrounding vineyards. Day 4: Visit the State Stud Farm and see the Lipizzaner horses. Day 5: Relax at the Biljsko plato plateau. Day 6: Taste the local wines at one of the many vineyards. Day 7: Attend a sports event in town.

Food and Drink Scene of Đakovo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Slavonian Fisherman’s Stew: A hearty soup made with freshwater fish and vegetables.
  • Kulen: A spicy dried sausage that is a local specialty.
  • Baranja Squares: A pastry filled with cheese and bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Slavonian Wine: Slavonia is known for its wine production, and Đakovo has many vineyards where visitors can taste the local wines.
  • Rakija: A strong alcoholic drink made from fruit, usually plum or grape.

Where to Stay in Đakovo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Đakovo has many accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Mursa: A 4-star hotel located near the town center, with comfortable rooms, spa services, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Guesthouse Albatros: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a great location.
  • Hotel Patria: A 3-star hotel with a modern design, located in a quiet part of town.

What to Buy in Đakovo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lipizzaner horse figurines: The Lipizzaner horses are a local specialty, and visitors can buy figurines of these beautiful horses.
  • Hand-painted ceramics: Đakovo has a long tradition of pottery making, and visitors can buy hand-painted ceramics as souvenirs.
  • Slavonian wine: Slavonia is known for its wine production, and Đakovo has many vineyards where visitors can buy the local wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Đakovo

Đakovo has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. One of the most famous traditions in Đakovo is the Đakovački vezovi festival, which celebrates the town’s cultural heritage. The festival takes place every year in June and features music, dance, and traditional costumes.

Best Time to Visit Đakovo

The best time to visit Đakovo is in the summer, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also when many of the town’s festivals and events take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Đakovo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Visit the cultural and historical attractions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be excessively loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Đakovo

Đakovo is a safe town, but visitors should take basic safety precautions like locking their cars and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Đakovo

Đakovo has excellent healthcare facilities and pharmacies, but visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical coverage.

Getting to Đakovo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Đakovo

The nearest airports to Đakovo are Osijek Airport (28 km) and Zagreb Airport (200 km).

Bus Stations Near Đakovo

Đakovo has a bus station with connections to many cities in Croatia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Đakovo or take a train.

How to Get Around in Đakovo

The best way to get around Đakovo is by car. Taxis and rental cars are available in town.

Where to Go Next from Đakovo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Osijek: A charming university town located on the banks of the Drava River, with a rich cultural heritage and many attractions.
  • Baranja: A scenic region known for its vineyards, forests, and nature parks.
  • Vukovar: A historic town known for its multicultural heritage and 18th-century Baroque architecture.

Conclusion

Đakovo is a hidden gem in Croatia, with a rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious food and wine. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a relaxing vacation, Đakovo has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to Đakovo and that you have a wonderful time exploring this beautiful town.
