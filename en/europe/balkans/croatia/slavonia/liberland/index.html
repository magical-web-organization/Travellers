Introduction to Liberland

Liberland is a microstate located in Eastern Europe, nestled between Croatia and Serbia. It is the world’s newest country, declared in 2015 by its founder, Vít Jedlička. The country comprises of 7 square kilometers of land in the disputed Danube Valley. Despite its small size, it is a unique destination that offers stunning natural scenery, culturally significant landmarks, and a rich history. In this travel guide, we will delve into the best places to visit, how to get around, and cultural dos and don’ts.

List of Places to Visit in Liberland

Whether you’re into outdoor adventures or prefer to soak up the culture, Liberland has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit attractions in the country:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Liberland Museum: This museum showcases the country’s history, culture, and art. The museum is situated in the capital city of Liberland, and the exhibits include a wide range of artifacts that showcase the country’s history.

  • Kőszegi Palace: This historical palace is located in the heart of the country. Built-in the 19th century, the palace has since been converted into a museum. It houses a collection of unique artworks, including paintings, sculptures, and tapestries dating back to the Renaissance era.

  • Sankhara Temple: This is the first temple of the country and is located in the capital city of Liberland. The temple offers a serene environment to meditate and worship.

Natural Attractions of Liberland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Danube River: The Danube is a major river that runs along the western border of the country. This river provides ample opportunities for water sports and activities such as boating, kayaking, and canoeing. Additionally, the riverbank also offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Liberland Forests: Liberland is home to vast forests of oak, pine, and beech, beautiful natural parks that offer unmatched hiking trails and picnic spots with excellent forest views.

  • Liberty Island: This Island is an excellent spot for bird watching and exploring the country’s unique ecosystem. The island is home to rare bird species such as the Cormorant, Gray Heron, and Goosander.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liberland

Liberland offers many opportunities for both hiking and biking excursions. Here are some of the most scenic routes to explore:

  • Liberland Trail: This hiking trail is a must-do for all visitors, as it offers an excellent opportunity to explore the country’s terrain. The trail covers almost 10 kilometers through the forests, offering stunning views of the Danube River.

  • Bike Trails: Liberland has world-class mountain biking routes that explore different terrains, from the silky-smooth streets of Vinogradišće and Podunavlje plains to the rugged and rocky hills and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Liberland: Activities and Tours

Liberland provides visitors with endless outdoor and recreational activities, from kayaking and exploring the river, hiking, mountain biking, and sightseeing tours. Some of the top-fashioned activities and tours in the country include:

  • Danube Fun Rafting: This is a unique activity where you can enjoy rafting in some of the most scenic parts of the Danube River, from the Trieste Pier to Novi Sad.

  • Danube Delta Cruise: This tour allows visitors to cruise along the Danube and see some of the most beautiful sceneries in the country. It encompasses a hike through the forest and a visit to the famous Sankhara Temple.

Week-Long Itinerary for Liberland

A weeklong exploration of Liberland is perfect for discovering the country’s diverse cultures and scenic views. Here is a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival in Liberland – check into your hotel and head out for a guided walking tour of the city.

Day 2: Explore the famous Sankhara Temple and the Kőszegi Palace.

Day 3: Join a Danube Delta Cruise and explore the natural wonders of Liberland.

Day 4: Enjoy a day hike in one of Liberland’s vast forests.

Day 5: Visit the unique Liberty Island and bird watch.

Day 6: Do a bike tour exploring the country’s hills and mountains.

Day 7: Departure day - spend the morning exploring before departing on onwards travel.

Food and Drink Scene of Liberland

The food scene in Liberland is heavily influenced by the neighboring countries of Serbia and Croatia. The local cuisine predominantly consists of grilled meat, seafood, and stews. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pljeskavica: This is a grilled beef or pork patty seasoned with a variety of herbs and served with onions and ajvar sauce.

  • Čevapi: This is a grilled meat dish consisting of beef, pork, or lamb sausages served on a bed of onions and ajvar sauce.

  • Burek: This is a pastry filled with meat, spinach, or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija: This is a traditional fruit brandy served as a digestive. The locals consider it one of the best in the region.

  • Wine: Liberland produces some of the best wines in the region, mainly Chardonnay, Merlot, and Cabernet Sauvignon.

Where to Stay in Liberland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Liberland is a small country with limited accommodation options. However, there are plenty of budget-friendly guesthouses, hostels, and hotels available. Some of the best options include:

  • Royal Guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and amenities with access to some of the best outdoor activities of Liberland.

  • Liber Hotel is a budget-friendly option located in the heart of the capital. The hotel offers excellent services and facilities and is within walking distance of many attractions.

What to Buy in Liberland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Liberland, some of the locally produced and culturally relevant items to bring home include:

  • Liberland T-shirts: These are some of the most unique items that you can find, made in the local country.

  • Local wines: Liberland produces some of the best wines, making it an ideal gift to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Liberland

Liberland is a crossroads of different cultures, and as such, visitors need to be aware of the country’s customs and traditions. Some of the customs include being respectful in churches and temples, avoid littering, and drinking responsibly in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Liberland

The best time to visit Liberland is between May and October when temperatures are moderate and the weather is mostly dry. The months of July and August are the most popular and offer the best opportunities for outdoor activities. Be mindful that from December to March, the country becomes too cold and challenging to engage in many outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liberland

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

  • Be friendly and respectful to everyone you meet.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment.

  • Drink excessively in public areas.

  • Enter a church or temple without covering your shoulders or wearing shorts, skirts or dresses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liberland

Liberland is a safe country for tourists and locals alike. The crime rates are low, and visitors need not worry about their security while exploring the country.

Health in Liberland

Liberland has a good healthcare system, but visitors should still ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any healthcare emergencies they may encounter.

Getting to Liberland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Liberland is not accessible by commercial flights. However, the country can be accessed via other means of transportation such as:

Airports Near Liberland

  • Osijek Airport, which is 97 kilometers away from the capital

  • Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport, which is 154 kilometers away.

  • Zagreb Airport, which is 199 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Liberland

  • Novi Sad Bus Station - 33 kilometers away.

  • Apatin Bus Station - 39 kilometers away.

  • Osijek Bus Station - 111 kilometers away.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Rental cars are available from major cities, and visitors can explore the country at their leisure.

  • Private transfers: Private transfers to Liberland are also available from major cities such as Osijek and Belgrade.

How to Get Around in Liberland

Getting around Liberland is easy and convenient. Visitors can choose to walk if they want to explore the capital or engage in outdoor activities, or hire a bike. Private transportation and taxis are available as well, but expenses can add up quickly.

Where to Go Next from Liberland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Liberland can be combined with other cities in Croatia or Serbia. Some of the most popular destinations to visit next include:

  • Serbia’s capital city of Belgrade, which is 151 kilometers away.

  • Croatia’s Osijek city, which is 105 kilometers away.

  • Croatia’s capital city of Zagreb, which is 199 kilometers away.

Conclusion

Liberland is a unique destination that offers a blend of rich history and stunning natural sceneries. From exploring the forests and Danube River to visiting historic landmarks, visitors will not be disappointed with what the country has to offer. This travel guide has covered some of the best places to visit in Liberland, activities, and tours to try, and cultural customs to be aware of. With its friendly locals, breathtaking views, and unique culture, Liberland is undoubtedly a destination worth visiting.
