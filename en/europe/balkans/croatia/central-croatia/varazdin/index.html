Introduction to Varaždin

Varaždin is a beautiful city situated in Central Croatia, known for being the cultural, historical, and economic center of the Varaždin County. The city was once the seat of the local government, and its wealth and grandeur are still evident today in the well-preserved Baroque-style buildings and monuments. The city’s idyllic location, close to the Drava River and surrounded by hills and forests, makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Varaždin

Visitors to Varaždin will find plenty to see and experience, ranging from cultural and historical landmarks to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Varaždin Castle - A magnificent castle built in the Baroque style, surrounded by a beautiful park. It houses a museum dedicated to local history, art, and culture, including a collection of old weapons, musical instruments, and portraits.
  • Stari Grad Fortress - A medieval fortress built in the 14th century, located on top of a hill overlooking the city. Visitors can explore the fortress’ underground chambers, climb its towers, and enjoy breathtaking views of the city and surrounding areas.
  • Franciscan Monastery and Church of St. John the Baptist - A beautiful complex of Baroque buildings, including a church with richly decorated interiors, frescoes, and altarpieces. The monastery houses a library with rare books and manuscripts.
  • Town Hall and Municipal Museum - A grand building that used to be the center of local government. The museum showcases the city’s history and cultural heritage, with collections of art, crafts, and archaeology.
  • Cathedral of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary - A Renaissance-style cathedral with a striking bell tower, housing valuable works of art and relics.

Natural Attractions of Varaždin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Koprivnica-Križevci County - A stunning area of green hills, valleys, and forests, rich in flora and fauna. Visitors can explore many hiking and cycling trails, go fishing, or enjoy a picnic in the countryside.
  • Drava River - A picturesque river that flows through the city, offering opportunities for fishing, boating, and swimming. The riverbanks are also ideal for walking, jogging, or cycling.
  • Lake Varazdin - A man-made lake in the south of the city, surrounded by lush vegetation and offering a variety of water sports and activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varaždin

Varaždin is a paradise for walkers, hikers, and cyclists, with numerous trails and paths to discover. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Varaždin - Koprivnica - A long-distance hiking and cycling trail that goes through beautiful natural areas, historic sites, and traditional villages. It is around 57 kilometers long and takes around 7-8 hours to complete.
  • Varaždin Castle Park - A lovely park with many walking paths, flower gardens, and benches. Visitors can stroll around the park’s lake, admire the Baroque statues and fountains, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.
  • Medjimurje County - A nearby county with many hiking trails, ranging from easy to challenging, through vineyards, meadows, and forests. Visitors can explore the local wine cellars, try local specialties, and learn about the region’s history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Varaždin: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, there are many things to do and enjoy in Varaždin, depending on your interests and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

  • Crafts Workshops - Varaždin is known for its rich tradition of handcrafts, including lace-making, embroidery, pottery, and woodcarving. Visitors can attend workshops and classes, learn about the techniques and cultural significance of these crafts, and create their own souvenirs.
  • Music Festivals - Varaždin hosts several music festivals throughout the year, showcasing various genres and styles, from classical to modern. The most famous one is the Varaždin Baroque Evenings, held in September, which attracts many prominent musicians and ensembles from around the world.
  • Wine Tasting and Tours - Varaždin and the surrounding areas are renowned for their excellent wines, especially white ones. Visitors can explore the local vineyards, taste the different varieties, and learn about the process of winemaking. Many wine cellars also offer guided tours and gourmet meals.

Food and Drink Scene of Varaždin

Croatian cuisine is diverse and flavorful, influenced by various Mediterranean, Slavic, and Austrian culinary traditions. Varaždin is no exception, with plenty of local specialties and delicacies to try. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Varaždin Kissing Cake - A sweet pastry filled with custard cream, often served as a dessert or snack.
  • Čobanac - A spicy stew made with various meats, vegetables, and paprika, enjoyed with bread or potatoes.
  • Peka - A traditional dish cooked in a clay pot, consisting of meat, potatoes, and greens, seasoned with herbs and olive oil.
  • Strudel with Savoy Cabbage - A savory pastry filled with minced meat and cabbage, baked until golden brown.
  • Fish Paprikash - A fish stew made with onions, red pepper, and tomatoes, seasoned with paprika and served with pasta or rice.

Local Beverages

  • Riesling - A dry, fruity white wine from the Varaždin region, with a floral aroma and a crisp taste. It pairs well with fish, pasta, and salads.
  • Šljivovica - A strong brandy made from plums, popular throughout Croatia and the Balkans. It is often served as an aperitif or digestif.
  • Karlovacko Beer - A lager-style beer made by the Karlovac Brewery, with a smooth taste and a refreshing finish. It is perfect for hot summer days.

What to Buy in Varaždin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Varaždin home with you, there are several items to choose from, which reflect the city’s history, culture, and art. Here are some ideas:

  • Lace Products - Varaždin has a long tradition of lace-making, and visitors can buy various lace items, such as tablecloths, doilies, and handkerchiefs. The lace museum and workshops are excellent places to learn more about the craft and its significance.
  • Pottery and Ceramics - Another traditional craft of Varaždin is pottery, with many local artisans producing beautiful and unique pieces, using traditional techniques and designs.
  • Wine - As mentioned earlier, Varaždin and the surrounding areas are famous for their excellent wines, and many wineries offer wine tasting and purchase options.
  • Art and Craft Souvenirs - Varaždin has a vibrant art and craft scene, with many galleries, studios, and shops selling handmade jewelry, paintings, sculptures, and other artworks. These items make great gifts for art lovers and collectors.

Local Culture and Customs of Varaždin

Varaždin has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its architecture, music, crafts, and cuisine. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and they value tradition and family-oriented values. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Greetings - When meeting someone, it’s customary to shake hands and say “dobar dan” (good day) or “dobro jutro” (good morning). Kisses on the cheek are common among close friends and family members.
  • Dress Code - Varaždin is a relatively conservative city, and visitors are advised to dress modestly, especially when visiting churches, monasteries, and other religious sites. It’s also recommended to wear comfortable shoes, as the city’s streets and sidewalks can be uneven.
  • Food and Drinks - When invited to someone’s home for a meal or a drink, it’s polite to bring a small gift, such as flowers, chocolates, or wine. It’s also customary to toast before starting to eat or drink, saying “živjeli” (cheers).
  • Folklore and Festivals - Varaždin has a rich tradition of folklore and festivals, with many local customs and rituals dating back centuries. Visitors can experience these traditions during the many festivals and cultural events held throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Varaždin

The best time to visit Varaždin is from late spring to early autumn, between May and September, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the nature is in full bloom. The peak season is July and August, when the city hosts many music festivals, exhibitions, and other events. The winter months, from December to February, can be cold and damp, but also festive, with many Christmas markets and fairs held in the city center.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varaždin

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Varaždin:

Do:

  • Try local specialties and drinks, such as Varaždin Kissing Cake, Čobanac, Riesling, and Šljivovica.
  • Respect the local customs and cultural traditions, such as greeting people politely, dressing modestly, and observing religious sites’ rules.
  • Explore the city’s architecture, history, and art, by visiting its museums, galleries, and landmarks.
  • Participate in outdoor activities, such as hiking, cycling, fishing, and swimming.
  • Take some time to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Varaždin County.

Don’t:

  • Make noise or disturb other people, especially in residential areas or at night.
  • Litter or pollute the environment, and respect the local flora and fauna.
  • Drink and drive or use narcotics, which are strictly forbidden and carry severe legal penalties.
  • Enter someone’s home or private property without their permission and respect their belongings.
  • Insult or discriminate against anyone based on their ethnicity, religion, gender, or sexuality.

Safety and Health

Varaždin is generally a safe and healthy city, with relatively low crime and accident rates, and good medical facilities. However, visitors should take some precautions and keep some basic rules in mind:

Safety in Varaždin

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded places, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Use official taxis or public transport to move around the city, and avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas or at night.
  • Follow traffic rules and signals, and wear a helmet when cycling.
  • Be cautious when swimming or boating in the Drava River or Lake Varazdin, and check the weather forecasts and water conditions beforehand.
  • In case of an emergency, dial 112 or seek help from the nearest police station or hospital.

Health in Varaždin

  • Bring any necessary medication or medical supplies with you, as they may not be available or easily accessible in Varaždin.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water, especially if you have a sensitive stomach or are prone to diarrhea.
  • Wear sunscreen and appropriate clothing to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays and avoid heatstroke.
  • Take basic hygiene precautions, such as washing your hands regularly, and avoiding contact with sick people or animals.
  • In case of a medical emergency, call 112 or go to the nearest hospital or clinic. Make sure to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Varaždin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Varaždin is easily accessible from other parts of Croatia and Europe, with several transportation options available. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Varaždin

The nearest international airport to Varaždin is Zagreb International Airport (ZAG), located 90 kilometers south of the city. From there, visitors can take a bus, taxi, or a rented car to reach Varaždin.

Bus Stations Near Varaždin

Varaždin has a central bus station, located near the city center, where buses from various Croatian and European cities arrive and depart. Visitors can check the schedules and buy tickets online or at the station’s ticket office.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Varaždin, as the city is located near several major highways and roads, connecting it to the rest of Croatia and Europe. Rental cars are available at the airports and in the city center, but it’s recommended to hire a local driver or take a taxi if you are not familiar with the local traffic rules and roads.

How to Get Around in Varaždin

Varaždin is a relatively small and compact city, with a well-developed public transport system, consisting of buses and taxis. Visitors can also explore the city on foot, as most of the attractions, restaurants, and accommodations are within walking distance. Bikes are also popular, especially for longer trips to the nearby countryside and hills.

Where to Go Next from Varaždin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Varaždin is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Croatia and neighboring countries, as several interesting destinations are within short driving or train distance. Here are some suggestions:

  • Zagreb - The capital city of Croatia, located 80 kilometers south of Varaždin, with many cultural, historical, and culinary attractions.
  • Plitvice Lakes National Park - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its stunning lakes and waterfalls, located around 120 kilometers southwest of Varaždin.
  • Ljubljana - The charming capital city of Slovenia, located around 95 kilometers northwest of Varaždin, with a beautiful old town, lively cafes, and museums.
  • Vienna - The elegant capital city of Austria, located around 130 kilometers north of Varaždin, with imperial palaces, museums, and theaters.

Conclusion

Varaždin is a hidden gem of Central Croatia, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, nature, and adventure. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s Baroque-style landmarks, hiking or cycling in the nearby hills, or tasting the local wines and delicacies, Varaždin has something to offer for everyone. So pack your bags, and come and discover the beauty and charm of this beautiful city yourself!
