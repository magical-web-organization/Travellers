Introduction to Cahul

Cahul is a picturesque city located in southern Moldova, near the country’s border with Romania and Ukraine. This small town is surrounded by vast vineyards, green hills, and flowing rivers, which gives visitors a chance to immerse themselves in nature while getting a taste of authentic Moldovan culture. In this Cahul travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and many more.

List of Places to Visit in Cahul

When it comes to sightseeing in Cahul, there are some must-visit places that showcase the city’s rich history and vibrant culture. Here are some of the top attractions in Cahul:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cahul City Museum - This museum displays various artifacts, documents, and images that outline the history of Cahul from ancient times to present-day Moldova.
  • Saint Nicholas Church - This historic church is a fine example of Moldovan Orthodox culture and a beautiful work of architecture.
  • Cahul Fortress - The ruins of a medieval fortress with a rich history are located at the top of a hill overlooking the city and the Nistru River.

Natural Attractions of Cahul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Padurea Domneasca Natural Reserve - The reserve is perfect for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and hikers, with its endless hiking and walking trails, fresh air, and stunning views.
  • Ribnita Canyon - Take a trip to Ribnita Canyon and enjoy the picturesque views of the valley formed by the Prut River, which carves its way through the Dniester Plateau.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cahul

Cahul provides various options for hiking and biking routes, offering a chance to experience the city’s spectacular natural beauty. Here are some of the favourites:

  • Cahul to Rosu - Vii - The hiking route stretches from Cahul to the vineyards of Rosu-Vii, providing panoramic views of the rolling hills and vineyard landscapes.
  • Padurea Domneasca National Park - This park has a range of hiking and biking trails that will take you to the natural wonders of the forest.

Best Things to Do in Cahul: Activities and Tours

Cahul offers plenty of activities and tours for travellers of all kinds. Here are the best things to do in Cahul:

  • Wine-tasting at Milestii Mici underground Winery - Milestii Mici winery is situated just 20 km from Cahul and has the largest wine collection in the world according to the Guinness World Records.
  • Beekeeping Tours - Take a tour to learn about the honey production process and the ancient art of beekeeping in Cahul
  • Relax at a thermal spa - Cahul is home to some of the best thermal spas in Moldova, where you can experience the healing powers of mineral-rich water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cahul

If you are planning a week-long itinerary in Cahul, here is a suggestion for how to spend your time:

Day Itinerary

Food and Drink Scene of Cahul

Moldovan cuisine is hearty and flavorful, with a mix of influences from the neighbouring countries of Romania, Russia, and Ukraine. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages in Cahul.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mamaliga cu Branza si Smantana - a traditional cornmeal-based dish served with sour cream and cheese
  • Drob de miel - Traditional Moldovan Easter dish made from lamb’s liver wrapped in vine leaves
  • Sarmale cu varza acra - stuffed cabbage rolls filled with minced meat and rice

Local Beverages

  • Divin - Cahul is one of the best regions in Moldova to sample this local brandy. It’s a type of cognac made from high-quality grapes and aged in oak barrels.
  • Moldovan wine - Wine production is a significant part of Moldovan culture, with Cahul being a prime location for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Cahul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cahul offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from cozy guesthouses to mid-range hotels. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Hotel Olbia - The luxury hotel is situated in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms, spa treatments, and stunning views of the city.
  • Pensiunea Casa Domneasca - A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the city center, providing clean and comfortable rooms and a home-like feel.

What to Buy in Cahul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you’re in Cahul, you should consider taking some local gifts to bring back home as souvenirs. Here are some suggestions:

  • Hand-made pottery - Local artisans create beautiful pieces of pottery using traditional techniques.
  • Traditional clothing - Moldovan folk costumes are unique and colourful, and buying a piece of traditional clothing can be a memorable souvenir.
  • Wine and Divin - Moldovan wine is well-known for its exceptional taste and heritage, and Cahul boasts premium quality wine brands alongside non-commercial small batch producers.

Local Culture and Customs of Cahul

Moldova has a unique culture, which is a blend of Romanian, Slavic, and Balkan influences. Here are some customs and norms you should be aware of when visiting Cahul:

  • Hospitality - The locals are friendly and hospitable, and you will often be invited into homes for a meal or a drink during your Cahul sightseeing.
  • Respectful Attire - Dress conservatively while visiting churches or other places of worship.
  • Festival celebrations - Check schedules online as the city regularly organizes festivals that attract tens of thousands of people each year.

Best Time to Visit Cahul

The best time to visit Cahul is from May to September when the weather is warm, sunny and which makes it ideal for sightseeing, hiking, or cycling. July and August are the busiest months, so plan accommodations, transportation, and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cahul

Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit to Cahul:

Do:

  • Try the Local Food and Drinks - Moldovan cuisine is a unique blend of flavours and styles, so take advantage and try the different dishes and wines.
  • Learn Some Basic Phrases - Learning a few simple words in Romanian can go a long way and help you communicate better with locals.
  • Take Public Transportation - Public transportation is available in Cahul and can be a cost-effective way to get around.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing to religious sites - Cover your shoulders and legs when visiting religious places in Cahul.
  • Flash Cash and Valuables - While Cahul is a relatively safe town, keep your valuables out of sight and wallets in bags.
  • Be Afraid to Explore - Cahul is a friendly and hospitable city. Explore and make new friends.

Safety and Health

Cahul is generally a safe town to visit, but like any destination, it’s wise to take precautions. Here are a few safety tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Cahul

  • Avoid Going out Alone at Night - Stick to well-lit and heavily trafficked areas if you’re out late at night.
  • Keep Valuables Safe - Make use of your hotel’s safe or a nearby safety deposit box to protect your valuables.
  • Travel Informed - Keep yourself updated with the latest travel advice or news alerts.

Health in Cahul

  • Vaccinations - Check with your doctor before traveling to ensure you have all the necessary vaccinations.
  • Stay Hydrated - Consume plenty of water and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Medical Care - There are adequate medical facilities in Cahul, but make sure to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Cahul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Cahul is convenient and straightforward. Here are the transportation options.

Airports Near Cahul

  • Chisinau International Airport - Located 165 km north of Cahul, this airport receives numerous flights from European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Cahul

  • Cahul Bus Station - Direct buses operate hourly to both Chisinau and Chișinău.

Other Options

  • Taxis - There are taxi services from the airport or major cities such as Chisinau.

How to Get Around in Cahul

Cahul is small and compact, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike. However, for further exploration, there are other transportation options:

  • Buses - Cahul has a well-organized bus system that operates throughout the city and outlying areas, including the surrounding villages.
  • Taxis - Taxis are also available in Cahul and can be more convenient for longer trips or reaching places not covered by the bus routes.

Where to Go Next from Cahul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cahul serves as a perfect base for exploring other nearby towns and attractions in Moldova, Romania, and Ukraine. Here are some places to consider for your day trips or excursions:

  • Iasi, Romania - Only 2 hours away and home to the Palace of Culture and several other landmarks

  • Odessa, Ukraine - Located on the Black Sea coast, the city boasts stunning architecture and natural beauty, about 3.5 hours away from Cahul.

Conclusion

Cahul is full of surprises and culture, offering a chance to soak up the best of Moldova. From the scenic countryside and outdoor adventures to the delicious food and wine, there is something for everyone in this charming city. Follow this guide to ensure you experience the very best of Cahul. Enjoy an unforgettable journey.
