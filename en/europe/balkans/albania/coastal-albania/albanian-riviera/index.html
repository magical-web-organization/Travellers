Albanian Riviera is a picturesque coastline that stretches about 195 kilometers along the Ionian Sea. Located on the western side of Albania, the region is flanked by the Ceraunian Mountains and boasts of breathtaking beaches and crystal-clear waters that make it a haven for beach lovers.

If you’re planning to visit Albanian Riviera soon, this comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to make your trip a memorable one.

Introduction to Albanian Riviera

Albanian Riviera offers an array of activities to suit different tastes, whether you’re after adrenaline-pumping activities, a relaxed beach holiday, or an opportunity to explore Albania’s rich history and culture. The region is made up of several charming towns, such as Saranda, Himara, and Vlore, each unique in its own way and offering different attractions that you will surely love.

List of Places to Visit in Albanian Riviera

The Albanian Riviera boasts of a wide variety of attractions, making it an exciting destination for travelers. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Butrint National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this archeological park boasts of ancient ruins dating back to Greek, Roman, and Byzantine periods and surrounded by lush vegetation and a beautiful lake.
  • Gjirokastra Castle: This 12th-century fortress sits atop a hill in the UNESCO-recognized city of Gjirokastra and boasts of impressive military architecture and breathtaking views of the town below.
  • Apollonia Archeological park: A Greek-Roman city from the 7th Century BC, Apollonia is a historical site located in Fier region, in the middle of olive groves.

Natural Attractions of Albanian Riviera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Llogara National Park: A pristine wonderland ideal for hiking, trekking, and wildlife viewing, the park has such an unparalleled beauty that you will want to spend hours taking in the views.
  • Blue Eye of Albania: a natural marvel, this crystal-clear water spring located in the middle of a forest is named after its mesmerizing blue colors that make it look like an underwater eye.
  • Lekuresi Castle: worth the drive from Saranda, the castle has a panoramic vista over the town and the Ionian Sea, a perfect place to catch a beautiful sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Albanian Riviera

For the more adventurous travelers, Albanian Riviera offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region:

  • Albania Adventure tour: a tour for experienced riders seeking adventure, taking place in the Sharra Mountains.
  • Lungomare: a breathtaking coastal cycling route from Saranda to Ksamil and back.

Best Things to Do in Albanian Riviera: Activities and Tours

The Albanian Riviera offers an array of activities that tourists can enjoy, from relaxing on the beach to exploring the region’s rich culture and history. Here are some suggestions:

  • Sailing Tour in Karaburun Peninsula: a full-day trip to hidden caves and secluded beaches along the Karaburun Peninsula.
  • Snorkeling Tour: explore the underwater world, and maybe spot dolphins and sunfish, along the Albanian coast.
  • Olive Oil Tour: learn about the country’s olive oil tradition and culture by visiting a local olive grove and traditional oil processing site.

Week-Long Itinerary for Albanian Riviera

Here’s a sample itinerary for spending a week in Albanian Riviera:

  1. Day 1-3: Visit Saranda and its nearby attractions such as Butrint National Park and Blue Eye.
  2. Day 4-5: Explore the seaside town of Himara and its nearby beaches, as well as Llogara National Park.
  3. Day 6-7: Wander the charming town of Vlore and its nearby attractions like Apollonia Archaeological Park and the Sazan Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Albanian Riviera

Albanian cuisine features rich flavors and influences from the Mediterranean, the Balkans, and the Ottoman Empire. Seafood dishes are a specialty of the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tave Kosi: a traditional lamb and yogurt casserole that is a staple in Albanian cuisine.
  • Fergese: a baked dish made with peppers, tomatoes, and local cheese.
  • Stuffed peppers with grated cheese and olive oil, served with bread)

Local Beverages

  • Rakia: the region’s homemade brandy distilled from grapes, plums, or mulberries.
  • Albanian Wines: with unique grape varieties that have been cultivated for centuries.

Where to Stay in Albanian Riviera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Albanian Riviera offers different types of lodging to suit different preferences and budget, from luxurious resorts to charming guesthouses. Here are some suggestions:

  • Firefly Boutique Hotel: located near the beach, this hotel boasts of stylish rooms, an outdoor swimming pool, and breathtaking sea views.
  • Smeralda Beach Resort: A family-friendly resort set amid olive groves with private beach access and has excellent facilities, like two swimming pools, a spa, and a classic Albanian restaurant.
  • Guest House Villa Eco Resort: if you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodation and a chance to interact with friendly locals, this guesthouse located in a quaint and peaceful area is the perfect choice for you.

What to Buy in Albanian Riviera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Albanian Riviera has a rich cultural heritage and unique local products. Here are some souvenirs and products you must bring home:

  • Hand-Embroidered Items: traditional carpets, pillowcases, and table runners with beautifully crafted embroidery.
  • Olive Oil: Sample the region’s finest extra virgin olive oils made from local olive groves.
  • Handmade Jewelry: handmade silver jewelry crafted by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Albanian Riviera

Travelers to Albanian Riviera can experience a unique blend of Albanian traditions with local influences. The region has its music, dance, and art, and hospitality is highly valued. One thing to keep in mind is to respect the locals and their traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Albanian Riviera

The best time to visit Albanian Riviera is between June and September, where you can enjoy long hours of sunshine and warm temperatures. July and August are the peak months and can be crowded, so consider visiting in June or September to avoid crowds and still enjoy good weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Albanian Riviera

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending traditional events.
  • Explore the local culture and traditions by engaging with locals.
  • Learn a few basic Albanian phrases as locals appreciate tourists who make an effort to speak their language.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.
  • Litter the beaches or natural parks.
  • Drink tap water- there are several available water brands that are cheaply priced.

Safety and Health

Safety in Albanian Riviera

Albanian Riviera is generally a safe destination for travelers, but like any other place, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid leaving your belongings unattended on beaches and carry essentials such as your passport and cash in a secured bag or pouch.

Health in Albanian Riviera

Make sure you talk to your doctor and get all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Albania. Also, carry insect repellent, sunscreen, and any medications you might need. Drinking water is safe from local brands, but always stay hydrated.

Getting to Albanian Riviera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Several transportation options are available for getting to Albanian Riviera, such as air, bus, train, and taxi.

Airports Near Albanian Riviera

The nearest international airports to Albanian Riviera are the Mother Teresa International Airport in the capital city, Tirana, and the Ioannis Kapodistrias International Airport in nearby Corfu, Greece, which has direct flights to several European cities.

Bus Stations Near Albanian Riviera

Several bus companies operate in the region, making it a convenient and affordable way to get around the Albanian Riviera. For example, Furgon Alb operates buses from Tirana to Saranda, while buses from Greece that go through Albania stop in Himara, Vlorë, and Saranda.

Other Options

Another option is to hire a car and drive to Albanian Riviera, giving you the flexibility to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Albanian Riviera

Several transportation options are available for getting around in Albanian Riviera, such as buses, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Albanian Riviera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to continue their adventure after exploring Albanian Riviera, we recommend some nearby destinations:

  • Berat: UNESCO-recognized town located in the central part of Albania.
  • Tirana: Albania’s dynamic capital city known for its colorful architecture, nightlife, and lively street markets.
  • Theth National Park: an unspoiled alpine wilderness located in northern Albania that boasts of breathtaking natural scenery and wildlife.

Conclusion

Albanian Riviera is a hidden gem in Europe that offers an array of activities, from soaking up the sun on its beautiful beaches to exploring its cultural heritage and immersing yourself in local traditions. With this comprehensive guide, you now have all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Albanian Riviera. Bon voyage!
