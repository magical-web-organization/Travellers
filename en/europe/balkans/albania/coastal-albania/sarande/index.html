Introduction to Sarandë

Sarandë is a picturesque coastal city located in southern Albania. It is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by majestic mountains, Sarandë is a perfect destination for those who love the beauty of nature and want to explore the area’s history and culture. Whether you’re planning a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a solo adventure, Sarandë has something to offer everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top things to see and do in Sarandë, so you can plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Sarandë

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Castle of Lëkurësi - This beautiful castle is located on a hill overlooking the city. It’s an ancient Venetian castle built in the 16th century and was an essential defensive structure to protect the city from invading forces. The castle provides marvelous views of the city and the sea.

2. Archaeological Museum of Sarandë - Located in the city center, this museum displays exhibits from the ancient civilizations that have passed through the region. The museum also houses an impressive collection of local art, ceramics, and traditional costumes.

3. Synagogue Complex - The Synagogue Complex is one of the oldest and most beautiful synagogues in Albania. Though partially destroyed by the Nazi invasion in WWII, this incredibly impressive monument dates back to the 5th century BCE.

Natural Attractions of Sarandë: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Blue Eye - This natural spring is located just outside of Sarandë in the village of Muzinë. The water is beautifully clear and blue in a natural terracing that is perfect for any avid swimmer looking to relax and soak up some sun.

2. Kakome Bay - This picturesque beach is located in the southern part of Sarandë. The beach is secluded and has crystal clear waters, making it ideal for snorkeling and swimming.

3. Butrint National Park - Situated on the south of the city, this park has a stunning setting where the natural beauty merges with history. The site is rich in archaeology and ecological beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarandë

Sarandë is surrounded by mountains providing plenty of hike and bike opportunities. The trails vary from beginner to advanced difficulty.

1. Mount Cika Hike - This trail is an ideal introduction to the area’s stunning views, as it provides one of the most exceptional panoramas in the region. With an altitude of 2000m above sea level, hikers are blown away with the scenery.

2. Borsh Waterfall Hike - Borsh Waterfall is only an hour hike from the city center, and it’s an excellent spot in both summer and winter. This beautiful waterfall flows into a pool, providing a stunning natural freshwater swimming pool.

3. Glaroni Beach Mountain Biking - This ride is the perfect combination of scenic adventure and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Sarandë: Activities and Tours

1. Ksamil Islands Boat Tour - This tour takes you to the four magnificent islands that comprise the Ksamil archipelago. The turquoise waters and crystal clear waters of Albania’s southern coast make it an unforgettable trip.

2. Scuba Diving - Scuba divers have the chance to explore Sarandë’s stunning marine life, including sea turtles and fish. There are plenty of diving centers where one can take a full certification program or just a couple one-time dives.

3. Olive Oil Making and Tasting Tour - Olive oil has been a staple in Mediterranean cuisine for centuries. This tour takes you to an olive grove, where you will learn about the olive oil-making process and have the chance to taste some of Albania’s best olive oil.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sarandë

Day 1: Explore the city center; visit the Archaeological Museum and Castle of Lëkurësi.

Day 2: Visit Butrint National Park and enjoy the natural beauty merge with history.

Day 3: Take a beach day to relax and enjoy the sun on one of the stunning beaches of Sarandë.

Day 4: Take a boat tour to the Ksamil islands to discover the beauty of Southern Albania’s coast.

Day 5: Explore the local culture and try Albanian cuisine through a delicious olive oil making and tasting tour.

Day 6: Hike through the beautiful mountain range around Sarandë and enjoy stunning views of the city and coast.

Day 7: Take the day to relax and explore the different hidden gems Sarandë has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Sarandë

Sarandë has a rich culinary history, and there is something for every taste bud. Here are some of the best dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

Tave Kosi - This traditional dish is found in Albania and is made up of layers of flavorful cooked lamb and rice, onion, garlic, and meat broth. It is then covered in a mix of yogurt mixed with flour and baked in an oven.

Fërgesë - This is a vegetarian dish made with pepper and cottage cheese.

Baklava - This sweet dessert is made using pistachios and is syrupy sweet and delicious.

Local Beverages

Raki - Raki is the national alcoholic beverage of Albanians. It is served before meals as a means of opening the appetite.

Skanderbeu Beer - This is a local brew that has won many awards from international competitions in recent years.

Albanian Wine - The country has a rich wine culture, and the region produces world-class wines.

Mare Nostrum Hotel - A modern seafront hotel providing an ancient Mediterranean vibe.

Hairy Lemon Hostel - A hostel located near the beach in Borsh, only 20 km from Sarandë.

Guesthouses

B&B Dionis - A guesthouse located in a quiet area of the city, providing an authentic experience of Albanian hospitality.

What to Buy in Sarandë: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenirs

There are many unique souvenirs to buy in Sarandë, including handcrafted items from local artisans. Some of the most popular souvenirs include Mediterannean olive oil, traditional local clothes, and items made of copper, such as coffee pots and traditional copper jug pitchers.

Local Products

Olive Oil - Albanian olive oil is of high quality and is made from first-press olives.

Raki -This traditional Albanian drink is made from grapes and has a unique flavor.

Wine - Albanian winemakers take great pride in their production and create some of the world’s most excellent wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarandë

Albanians are a hospitable people, and guests are treated with the utmost respect. It is customary to serve coffee or tea when visitors arrive. Albanians also believe in the concept of “besa” which means standing up for one’s promises and keeping one’s word.

Best Time to Visit Sarandë

The best time to visit Sarandë is between May and September when the weather is warm and perfect for lounging on the beach. July and August are the peak seasons, and it tends to get busy with many tourists. However, it’s still possible to get refreshing sea breezes along the coast of Albania’s Ionian Sea for relaxation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sarandë

Do:

  • Dress modestly, as Sarandë is a predominantly Muslim country.

  • Try the local cuisine, it’s delicious!

  • Bring euros, as it is the preferred currency.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Wear shoes inside a mosque or place of worship.

  • Discuss politics or religion, as they are sensitive topics.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarandë

Sarandë is a pretty safe city. However, it’s essential to take standard precautions concerning safety practices like being cautious with new friends’ trust and walking around alone at night.

Health in Sarandë

It’s essential to keep hydrated and take caution. Use sunscreen, seek shade, and wear a hat when the sun is intense. Take care when consuming local foods, and use bottled water when brushing your teeth or drinking, just be cautious as to what goes in your body. In case of illness, it’s best to consult a doctor.

Getting to Sarandë: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sarandë

Sarandë doesn’t have an airport. The nearest airports are:

  • Corfu International Airport, Greece - 37 km.

  • Ioannina National Airport - 150 km, especially if one wants a more personal mode of transportation.

Bus Stations Near Sarandë

  • Sarandë Bus Station is located within the urban part of the city next to the city’s port.

  • Furgon Bus Station is about 20 km from Sarandë.

Other Options

  • Taxi

  • Car Rental

  • Private Transfer

How to Get Around in Sarandë

The city center of Sarandë is relatively small and easy to walk around. However, there are many options to get around, such as:

  • Local buses and taxis

  • Rental cars, bikes, and scooters

  • Private transfers

Where to Go Next from Sarandë: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Many attractive destinations are close to Sarandë, including:

  • The Ancient City of Butrint

  • The stunning Blloku i Palma coves, which are just a 20-minute drive from Sarandë

  • The charming hill town of Gjirokastra is within a short driving distance of a day trip.
