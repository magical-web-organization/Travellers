Introduction to Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Nestled on the coast of Albania, Divjaka-Karavasta National Park is a natural gem located in the Balkans. Known for its sprawling sand dunes, pristine pine forests, and rich wetlands, the national park is a haven for fauna and flora alike. In this comprehensive guide, we will reveal the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history enthusiast, you are in for a treat. Divjaka-Karavasta National Park is home to many museums and historical sites, including the famous Apollonia Archaeological Park. This ancient city, located in the heart of the park, features a museum and several ruins, such as the Odeon, Bouleuterion, and the stunning Temple of Artemis.

Natural Attractions of Divjaka-Karavasta National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The park is replete with stunning natural beauty and has several outdoor activities for adventure seekers. One can visit the Salt Pans to witness the centuries-old salt extraction process, hike the Shtojzovalle Trail, a challenging 8.5 km of ridge with incredible views, or get up close with pelicans and other birds at the Karavasta Lagoon, the largest lagoon in Albania.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Divjaka-Karavasta National Park has a plethora of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers various trails ranging from easy to challenging, such as the 28 km long Rrogozhina trail, which can be done on a bike. Make sure to equip yourself with necessary safety gear such as helmets, gloves, and knee-pads.

Best Things to Do in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park: Activities and Tours

Divjaka-Karavasta National Park offers several activities and tours for visitors. Consider paddle boating in the Karavasta Lagoon, horseback riding, bird watching, camping, fishing, or experience a traditional Albanian village life with activities like wine tasting, and olive oil harvesting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Divjaka-Karavasta National Park offers a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit. This itinerary includes visiting historical sites, hiking trails, exploring wetlands, and the salt pans, followed by a day trip to the nearby beautiful city of Berat.

Food and Drink Scene of Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Food is an essential part of Albanian culture, and Divjaka-Karavasta National Park offers several delicious options for visitors.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes include Tave Elbasani (lamb, peppers, and tomatoes slow-cooked in a casserole), Byrek (savory phyllo dough pastries filled with meat or cheese), and Fergese (grilled peppers and tomatoes mixed with cottage cheese).

Local Beverages

To quench your thirst, we recommend the locally brewed beer “Korca” and the Albanian wine variety “Kallmet.”

Where to Stay in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses offer different options for visitors in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park. Some of the popular options include Hotel Veliera, Elesio Hotel, and Camping LaGuna Beach.

What to Buy in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Divjaka-Karavasta National Park offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, such as traditional Albanian clothes, handcrafted pottery, and wooden carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Being a part of Albanian culture, visitors are advised to respect the cultural norms and values of the locals. Albania is a predominantly Muslim country, and visitors are recommended to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

The best time to visit Divjaka-Karavasta National Park is between April and September. During these months, the temperature is mild, and it is the most suitable time to experience nature fully.

Do’s and Don’ts of Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Do:

  • Respect the cultural norms and values of the locals.
  • Stay on designated trails to avoid damaging the fragile ecosystem.
  • Carry a refillable water bottle to reduce plastic waste.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park.
  • Disturb the wildlife or their habitat.
  • Smoke or use fire in the park, as there is a risk of starting wildfires.

Safety and Health

Safety in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

The park is relatively safe for visitors. However, it is recommended to take the necessary precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas and carrying a basic medical kit.

Health in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Make sure to protect yourself against the sun and stay hydrated while exploring the park. Consider packing sunscreen, a hat, and insect repellent to stay safe.

Getting to Divjaka-Karavasta National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Divjaka-Karavasta National Park is well connected to nearby cities via buses, taxis, and private transfer services.

Airports Near Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

Tirana International Airport is the closest airport to the national park, located around 54 km away.

Bus Stations Near Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

The closest bus station to the national park is in the nearby village of Divjake.

Other Options

Private transfer services are also available for visitors who prefer a more comfortable and stress-free journey.

How to Get Around in Divjaka-Karavasta National Park

The best way to explore Divjaka-Karavasta National Park is by walking, biking, or horseback riding. Visitors can also hire taxis or private vehicles.

Where to Go Next from Divjaka-Karavasta National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular attractions near Divjaka-Karavasta National Park include the beautiful town of Berat, the Skenderbeg Castle, and the buzzing capital city of Tirana.

Conclusion

Divjaka-Karavasta National Park is a must-visit destination in Albania that offers a unique experience to visitors. It has stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and boundless opportunities for outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan the perfect trip to Divjaka-Karavasta National Park for an unforgettable experience.
