Introduction to Prokletije

Nestled in the northeastern part of Albania, Prokletije is a mountain range that is a must-visit for adventure and nature enthusiasts. Known as the “Balkan Alps,” Prokletije’s rugged peaks and breathtaking scenery will leave visitors in awe. With a rich cultural heritage and tradition of Albania, Prokletije offers visitors a unique opportunity to immerse themselves in local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Prokletije

There are numerous places to visit in Prokletije that cater to a diverse range of interests. Here are some top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ethnographic Museum in Vusanje is a great place to learn about the culture of the surrounding area and get a better understanding of traditional life in Prokletije.
  • Rozafa Castle is a well-preserved castle that dates back to the 4th century. It provides a historic and scenic tour.
  • The Redzepagic Tower is a testament to the region’s past with a display of arms and ornaments.

Natural Attractions of Prokletije: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valbona Valley National Park is an important attraction of the region where tourists can participate in outdoor activities like hiking and trekking. The trip to the park provides amazing views and nature photography opportunities.
  • The Rugova Canyon offers rock climber and hiker a perfect occasion to get involved with the beautiful setting and enjoy thrilling adventures.
  • Thethi National Park is another outdoor enthusiast heaven. The adventure is worth the scenery, majestic waterfalls, streams, and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prokletije

Prokletije offers visitors numerous hiking and biking trails. Some popular routes are:

  • The Peaks of Balkan Trail is a long-distance hike, covering six countries and some of the most beautiful mountain ranges in the world. In Albania, the trail covers Prokletije and links Prizren, Valbona Valley, Theth, and Vermoshtica.
  • Hiking to the summit of Maja Jezercë is another popular option. Standing tall at 2694m, it is the highest peak in Albania.
  • The Komani Lake to Valbona trail rewards hikers with breathtaking views of the valley and a thrilling boat ride.

Best Things to Do in Prokletije: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, there are other activities that tourists can participate in Prokletije, such as:

  • Water sports in Koman Lake offers the possibility to engage some of the fun parts swimming outdoors. This form of tourism is recently getting well-known in Albania and is a must-try.
  • Cultural tours provide visitors with an insight into the local cuisine, customs, and traditions. They can savor a taste of traditional food and drinks of the region.
  • Skiing/snowboarding enthusiasts can visit the mountains during winter and enjoy some of the most incredible ski slopes in the Balkans.

Food and Drink Scene of Prokletije

Albania’s cuisine is a mix of Ottoman and Mediterranean influences, and Prokletije’s culinary scene offers some unmissable dining experiences.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Flija is a traditional dish composed of multiple layers of crepes, cheese and yoghurt which is baked over an open flame and is a must-try for cheese lovers.
  • Qebapa is a perfect food for meat lovers. They are made of grilled ground meatballs and are often served with pita bread, onions, and spices.
  • Baklava is a sweet pastry dish glazed with honey syrup and is characterized by its light flaky layers with a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Local Beverages

  • Raki is an alcoholic drink made from grapes and is a popular drink in the region. It is often served as an aperitif.
  • (Turkish-style) coffee is an essential part of Albanian culture and often compliments sweet dishes like Baklava.

Where to Stay in Prokletije: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are different options available to stay in Prokletije ranges from staying at budget-friendly hostels/guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Some recommended options are:

  • Hotel Berisha is a budget-friendly hotel situated in a strategic location and offers stunning views of the valley.
  • Villa Gjecaj is another budget-friendly option that offers a comfortable stay and is conveniently located near the popular hiking trails.
  • Hotel Fusha is a luxurious option that offers modern amenities like an indoor pool and spa facilities.

What to Buy in Prokletije: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rugova’s Canyon Honey is popular among tourists for its unique taste and is an excellent gift for honey lovers.
  • hand-woven rugs, pillows, and blankets make a perfect souvenir for those who appreciate handicrafts and cultural heritage.
  • Llokume, a type of Albanian Turkish delight with various flavors like rose, lemon, and orange, is also an excellent souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Prokletije

With its rich cultural heritage, visitors to Prokletije will have the opportunity to learn about and be part of the local customs and traditions. The Albanian people take pride in their hospitality and traditions. They are welcoming and warm-hearted, making them one reason to visit the region.

Best Time to Visit Prokletije

The best time to visit Prokletije is during the summer (June-August); however, the fall (September-November) also provides stunning views of the mountains’ changing colors. This period sees a lower influx of tourists, which makes it perfect for those visitors who want a more intimate experience with the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prokletije

Do:

  • Do try local food and drinks.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Do dress appropriately for the mountains’ climate and terrain.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the trails and parks.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t camp outside the designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prokletije

The Prokletije Mountains are safe to explore, but it is advisable to keep a few things in mind while hiking.

  • Stick to designated hiking trails.
  • Always carry a map and a compass.
  • Always carry enough water and food.

Health in Prokletije

Prokletije Mountains are far from health care facilities, and there is a risk of catching water-borne diseases. Here are a few health-related tips:

  • Always carry water purification tablets.
  • Get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and Typhoid.
  • Wear insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Prokletije: Transportation Options and Logistics

The best way to reach Prokletije is by air, which will land you to Podgorica, the capital of Montenegro. From there, you can take a bus/taxi to reach Prokletije.

Airports Near Prokletije

  • Podgorica Airport - Montenegro’s capital is the closest airport and is approximately 115 km from Prokletije.

Bus Stations Near Prokletije

  • Montenegro Bus Station - There are several bus companies that connect Podgorica to Prokletije, and the journey takes around 4 hours.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available in Podgorica, but it is recommended to be well equipped with the mountainous and rugged road trip’s driving expertise.

How to Get Around in Prokletije

Bus services are available on the main road that runs through Prokletije’s northern side, connecting Montenegro to Albania. Other transport options are taxis, minibusses, and hiking on foot on the designated trails.

Where to Go Next from Prokletije: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Prokletije is in the northeastern region of Albania and is close to some of the other favorite destinations in the Balkans; they are:

  • Shkodra city, located 30km south-east from Prokletije, is known as the cultural and historical capital of Northern Albania.
  • Kruja is another popular tourist destination located 75km south-east from Prokletije and is a well-preserved Ottoman-style citadel.
  • The Adriatic coastline 120km away has some of the most beautiful beaches, crystal clear waters, and unique gastronomic offerings in Albania.

Conclusion

Prokletije is a paradise for travelers interested in outdoor activities, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. The region’s rich culture, stunning views, and warm hospitality make it a must-visit destination in the Balkans. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or looking for a relaxing holiday, Prokletije has something for everyone. Plan your trip, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
