    • Located in southeastern Albania, Fir of Hotova National Park is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Spanning over 34,000 hectares, the park is primarily known for its ancient fir trees, some of which are over 500 years old, and its diverse wildlife. Fir of Hotova National Park is home to brown bears, wolves, wild cats, lynx, deer, eagles, falcons, and numerous other species that create a perfect ecosystem that is perfect for myriad nature-based activities.

    Introduction to Fir of Hotova National Park

    Fir of Hotova National Park is one of the largest and oldest parks in Albania, established in 1966. It is situated in the Albanian Alps, with Kokaj Mountain looming above it. The park’s name comes from the dense fir forests that cover its mountainous terrain, and it is considered one of the most important plant communities in the Balkans.

    List of Places to Visit in Fir of Hotova National Park

    Fir of Hotova National Park is an intriguing spot to explore, with numerous natural and cultural attractions. Here are a few places to visit in the park:

    Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

    1. Permet Ethnographic Museum: Located just outside the park in Permet, this museum is rich in Albanian history and culture. It features traditional attire, tools used in daily life, and crafts made by local artisans.

    Natural Attractions of Fir of Hotova National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

    1. Shebenik-Jabllanice National Park: Located in the nearby region, this park has several places to discover and enjoy the natural wonders of the region. Here, visitors can hike to see the beautiful views.

    Hiking/Biking Routes of Fir of Hotova National Park

    Fir of Hotova National Park offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails for visitors looking to explore the park’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular trails include:

    1. The Kokaj Trail: This is one of the most scenic and longest trails in the park, commencing from the Kokaj village. It leads visitors through the park’s dense forests and offers beautiful views along the way.
    2. The Gjeshtenje Trail: This trail starts in the village of Gjeshtenje and takes you through various terrains. It is considered an excellent route for mountain bikers.

    Best Things to Do in Fir of Hotova National Park: Activities and Tours

    1. Wildlife Watching: Fir of Hotova National Park is home to a diverse range of wildlife. At times, visitors may spot brown bears, wolves, wild cats, lynx, deer, eagles, falcons, and other wildlife while hiking or exploring the park.
    2. Camping: Visitors can enjoy camping in the park, and there are several campsites to choose from.

    Week-Long Itinerary for Fir of Hotova National Park

    Day 1: Arrive in Fir of Hotova National Park and spend the day exploring Permet Day 2: Hike or cycle along the Kokaj Trail. Day 3: Go wildlife watching in the park or head to Shebenik-Jabllanice National Park. Day 4: Spend the day at Vjosa River. Day 5: Enjoy a day of cultural discovery in the nearby towns of Gjirokastra or Berat. Day 6: Explore the park’s dense forests and crystal-clear waters. Day 7: Depart Fir of Hotova National Park

    Food and Drink Scene of Fir of Hotova National Park

    Albanian cuisine is rich in flavors, thanks to the country’s long history. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

    Must-Try Dishes

    1. Tave Kosi: This is a national dish made up of braised lamb, rice, and yogurt.
    2. Flija: A layered pastry that is a must-try dish.

    Local Beverages

    1. Raki: A strong fruit brandy, known as the national drink of Albania.
    2. Wine: Albania’s wine industry is growing rapidly, with local vineyards producing some excellent wine varieties.

    Local Culture and Customs of Fir of Hotova National Park

    The locals of Fir of Hotova National Park have long traditions and customs, such as coffee ceremonies, preparing homemade meals using locally sourced ingredients, and storytelling sessions.

    Best Time to Visit Fir of Hotova National Park

    The best time to visit Fir of Hotova National Park is from June to September. The weather is pleasant and perfect for enjoying outdoor activities. If you like cooler weather and skiing, visit during the winter months from December to March.

    Safety and Health

    Safety in Fir of Hotova National Park

    Visitors should take precautions while visiting Fir of Hotova National Park, such as avoiding going off-trail while exploring, staying alert while hiking, and following the park’s rules and regulations.

    Health in Fir of Hotova National Park

    The park’s location in the mountains means visitors should carry a first aid kit with them. Tick bites are common in the region, so insect repellents and preventative measures are recommended.

    Getting to Fir of Hotova National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

    Airports Near Fir of Hotova National Park

    Tirana International Airport is the closest airport to Fir of Hotova National Park, with regular flights to several destinations across Europe.

    Bus Stations Near Fir of Hotova National Park

    The nearby city of Permet has several bus stations, offering transport to and from Fir of Hotova National Park.

    Other Options

    Visitors can rent a car from Tirana International Airport or nearby cities like Berat or Gjirokastra.

    How to Get Around in Fir of Hotova National Park

    Walking is the best way to get around Fir of Hotova National Park, allowing visitors to explore the park’s beauty on foot. Biking is also an excellent way to get around, with various biking trails available.

    Where to Go Next from Fir of Hotova National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

    2. Gjirokastra: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site retaining the architecture and the soul of a hundred years ago.
