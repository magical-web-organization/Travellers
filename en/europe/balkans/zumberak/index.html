Introduction to Žumberak

Nestled in the Balkans region of Europe, Žumberak is a hidden gem that offers visitors a true taste of traditional Croatian rural life. With its picturesque villages, rolling hills, and unspoiled natural beauty, Žumberak has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more, to help you make the most of your trip to Žumberak.

List of Places to Visit in Žumberak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Castle - A medieval castle with a rich and fascinating history.
  • Veliki Tabor - A stunning fortress that dates back to the 12th century.
  • Ethnographic Museum - Explore the traditional way of life in Žumberak through exhibits showcasing furniture, clothing, and household items of the past.

Natural Attractions of Žumberak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Žumberak-Samoborsko gorje Nature Park - Hike or cycle through this protected area and take in magnificent views of the countryside.
  • Japetić Mountain - Climb to the top of the mountain for breathtaking views.
  • Mrežnica River - Go rafting or kayaking on this clean and clear river that offers pristine water and stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Žumberak

Žumberak is perfectly suited for outdoor enthusiasts and adventurers. With numerous hiking and biking trails available throughout the region, visitors can get lost in nature and truly escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes in Žumberak include:

  • Slapnica Route - This trail is perfect for beginners and offers stunning views of the rolling hills and lush forests of Žumberak.
  • Grebengrad Route - For those seeking a more challenging hike/bike ride, the Grebengrad route is for you. This trail will take you to the top of Grebengrad Mountain, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the region.
  • Plešivica Wine Route - This route offers a unique opportunity to explore the vineyards and wineries of the region in addition to enjoying the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Žumberak: Activities and Tours

  • Wine tasting - Žumberak is known for its high-quality wines, so be sure to visit some of the local vineyards and wineries to sample the region’s best.
  • Swimming and Relaxing - The region is scattered with pristine lakes and rivers perfect for a refreshing swim or picnic.
  • Cultural Festivals - The local people take great pride in their traditions, and visitors are welcome to participate in the many festivals throughout the year that celebrate local cuisine, music, and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Žumberak

Day 1: Arrive in Žumberak, rest, and explore the local area.

Day 2: Explore the castles and museums of the region, including the Ethnographic Museum and Veliki Tabor.

Day 3: Hike/bike through the stunning scenery of Žumberak-Samoborsko gorje Nature Park.

Day 4: Discover the beautiful Mrežnica River and enjoy some water activities such as kayaking or rafting.

Day 5: Take a day trip to neighboring cities such as Zagreb or Samobor.

Day 6: Visit some of the local vineyards and wineries and enjoy some wine tasting.

Day 7: Relax by the lake and take in the natural beauty of Žumberak before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Žumberak

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cvarkuše - Fried dough with bacon and cheese.
  • Žumberačka salenjaka - A savory pie filled with cheese and eggs.
  • Roasted pig on a spit - Commonly served at local festivals and events, this dish is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Žumberačka rakija - A strong, homemade plum brandy that is typically served as an aperitif.
  • Wine - Žumberak is known for its high-quality wines, including Graševina and Plavac Mali.

Where to Stay in Žumberak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Frankopan - Located in the heart of Žumberak, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving up local cuisine.
  • Eko Selo Žumberak - Stay in wooden huts and enjoy a unique and eco-friendly experience.
  • Rooms Špehar - Offering simple yet cozy accommodations, this guesthouse is perfect for those on a budget.

What to Buy in Žumberak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Lace - Žumberak is known for its intricate lacework, and visitors can find a variety of handmade items to take home.
  • Local Wine - Bring home a bottle of Žumberak’s famous wine to enjoy after your trip.
  • Wood Carvings - The locals are skilled at woodcarving, and visitors can find unique, handmade souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Žumberak

The people of Žumberak are friendly and welcoming, and they take great pride in their traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local culture and customs, which include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Always greet people with a friendly “Dobar dan” or “Bok”.
  • When invited to someone’s home, bring a small gift such as flowers or wine.

Best Time to Visit Žumberak

The best time to visit Žumberak is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural beauty of the region is at its best. This is also the peak tourist season, so expect crowds and book your accommodations well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Žumberak

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and stunning natural scenery.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in the natural parks and lakes.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Žumberak

Žumberak is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables hidden.

Health in Žumberak

Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance, including coverage for medical emergencies, before embarking on their trip.

Getting to Žumberak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Žumberak

  • Zagreb Airport - Located about 45 kilometers from Žumberak, Zagreb airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Žumberak

  • Karlovac Bus Station - Located about 30 kilometers from Žumberak, Karlovac Bus Station offers daily buses to major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is an excellent way to explore Žumberak and the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Žumberak

The best way to get around Žumberak is by car. Renting a car is the most convenient option, and will allow you to explore the region at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Žumberak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plitvice Lakes National Park - Venture out from Žumberak to the strikingly beautiful Plitvice Lakes National Park.
  • Zagreb - Explore the vibrant capital city of Croatia, and take in its rich culture and history.
  • Samobor - Visit this charming medieval town, try some local specialties, and admire the beautiful architecture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or culture, Žumberak has something to offer. From hiking and biking in nature, to sampling traditional cuisine and wine, to exploring medieval castles and museums, there is something for everyone in Žumberak.
