Introduction to Veles

Veles is a city in Povardarie, North Macedonia, located in the Balkans region of Europe. It is known for its rich history and cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and warm and welcoming locals. Whether you’re a culture vulture, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, Veles has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Veles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Veles has a rich history that can be traced back to the Neolithic era. As a result, the city is home to several important historical and cultural attractions. Some of them are:

  • Veles Museum: It is a museum situated in the centre of the city, exhibiting artefacts from the Bronze Age to modern times.

  • St. Panteleimon Monastery: A picturesque Orthodox Church and Monastery, located on the outskirts of Veles.

  • Isar Fortress: The ruins of a fortress dating back to the 5th century BC, situated on the banks of the Vardar River.

Natural Attractions of Veles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Besides the historical and cultural attractions, Veles is also blessed with natural beauty that must be visited. Some of the places that offer panoramic views and adventure are:

  • Marko’s Towers: It is a group of rock formations located on the outskirts of the city. It’s a popular spot for rock-climbing and offers panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

  • Vodno Mountain: A great place to hike, bike, or take a leisurely stroll, as it offers some of the best views of the city.

  • Vardar River: A calm and serene waterbody, suitable for fishing, kayaking, and other water-sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Veles

Veles is an excellent location for hiking enthusiasts and bikers, with many beautiful trails to choose from. Some of the most popular routes are:

  • Gradsko Lake: A picturesque lake on the outskirts of Veles, with a 5 km hiking trail that circles around it.

  • Vardar River Trail: A 10 km biking and hiking trail that runs along the Vardar River.

  • Marko’s Towers Hiking Trail: A 9 km hike that starts from Marko’s Towers and takes you through the scenic hills and forests around Veles.

Best Things to Do in Veles: Activities and Tours

Other than hiking, biking, and exploring the historical and cultural attractions, Veles has several other activities and tours to offer, such as:

  • Wine Tasting: Visit the local vineyards and taste some of the finest wines in the Balkans.

  • Traditional Macedonian Cooking Class: Learn how to cook some of the most delicious Macedonian dishes from the locals themselves.

  • City Tours: Explore the city and learn about its history, culture, and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Veles

Must-Try Dishes

Macedonian cuisine is a combination of Balkan, Mediterranean, and Middle Eastern influences, resulting in a unique culinary experience that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Some traditional Macedonian dishes that you must try in Veles are:

  • Tavche Gravche: A delicious bean stew that is a staple in Macedonian cuisine.

  • Ajvar: A roasted red pepper and eggplant spread, used as a condiment in many dishes.

  • Pastrmajlija: A flatbread with a special topping, made of salted and dried meat.

Local Beverages

Along with delicious food, Veles also offers several local beverages that cannot be missed. Some popular ones are:

  • Rakija: A strong alcoholic beverage made from fermented fruit, popular throughout the Balkans.

  • Vino: Macedonian wine is known for its unique taste and aroma. There are several vineyards near Veles where you can go wine-tasting.

  • Coffee: Macedonian coffee is strong and delicious. Grab a cup from one of the many street vendors in Veles.

Where to Stay in Veles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Veles has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Veles are:

  • Hotel Uni Palace: A luxurious hotel in the city centre, with all modern amenities and excellent service.

  • Beva Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in a quiet neighbourhood near the city centre.

  • Hostel Vila: A hostel with a great location, near all the major attractions of the city.

What to Buy in Veles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Veles is known for its handmade crafts and local products. Some unique things to buy in Veles are:

  • Handmade Carpets and Rugs: Handwoven carpets and rugs are a specialty of the region.

  • Macedonian Folk Costumes: Traditional costumes, worn during festivals and celebrations, are popular souvenirs.

  • Handmade Pottery: The local pottery is made using traditional techniques and is both beautiful and functional.

Local Culture and Customs of Veles

Macedonia is a blend of different cultures, with influences from the Ottoman Empire, the Byzantine Empire, and Christianity. Locals are friendly and hospitable, and love to share their culture and traditions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Veles

The best time to visit Veles is during the spring and fall months (April-June and September-November). The weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer, making it an ideal time to explore the city and the surrounding areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Veles

Do:

  • Learn a few Macedonian phrases to communicate with the locals.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Bargain at the local markets and shops.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Veles

Veles is a safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Veles

The healthcare system in North Macedonia is good, with many hospitals and medical centres located in and around Veles. It’s advisable to have medical insurance before travelling.

Getting to Veles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Veles

The nearest international airport is Skopje International Airport, located 60 km from Veles. There are several international flights that operate from Skopje to major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Veles

Veles has a central bus station, with daily buses to all major cities in North Macedonia and neighbouring countries.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a good option, as it gives you the freedom to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Veles

Veles is a relatively small city, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation within the city.

Where to Go Next from Veles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Veles is located in the heart of the Povardarie region, with several nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting. Some of them are:

  • Prilep: A small city known for its tobacco production, historical architecture, and beautiful scenery.

  • Bitola: A historic city known for its Ottoman architecture, museums, and natural beauty.

  • Tikvesh Lake: A picturesque lake surrounded by hills and vineyards, offering excellent opportunities for boating and fishing.

Conclusion

Veles is a city that offers a blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for travellers of all types. It’s a perfect place to relax, unwind, and experience the warm hospitality of the Macedonian people. With our comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in Veles, North Macedonia.
