Introduction to Ohrid

Nestled on the shores of the eponymous lake, Ohrid is often regarded as the jewel of North Macedonia. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a bustling town center that blends tradition and modernity, it is a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Ohrid

No visit to Ohrid is complete without experiencing its myriad of iconic attractions and sights. Here are some of the best places to visit in Ohrid, divided into two categories.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ancient Theatre of Ohrid
  • Holy Mary Perybleptos
  • The National Museum of Ohrid
  • Samoil’s Fortress

Natural Attractions of Ohrid: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Ohrid
  • Plaoshnik
  • St. Naum Monastery
  • Galicica National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ohrid

Ohrid is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with rugged trails and scenic paths that take you through some of the region’s most breathtaking landscapes. Some of the best routes to try include:

  • Ohrid-Kaneo St. Stefan - 3 km
  • Ohrid - Skopje - 165 km
  • Ohrid - Prespa - 35 km

Best Things to Do in Ohrid: Activities and Tours

From exploring the city’s rich cultural heritage to indulging in its adventurous spirit, the range of things to do in Ohrid is diverse and exciting. Some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Ohrid are:

  • Visit the Ancient Theatre of Ohrid
  • Take a boat tour of Lake Ohrid
  • Hike the Galicica National Park
  • Go kayaking in the lake

Week-Long Itinerary for Ohrid

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Ohrid, here’s how you could spend your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Old Town of Ohrid and visit St. Sophia Church
2 Take a day trip to St. Naum Monastery and enjoy the lake activities
3 Hike to Samuel’s Fortress and enjoy the stunning views
4 Visit Galicica National Park and go on a guided hike
5 Go scuba diving or paragliding in the lake
6 Go on a guided kayaking tour around the lake
7 Visit the National Museum of Ohrid and Ancient Theater of Ohrid

Food and Drink Scene of Ohrid

The food and drink scene in Ohrid is a blend of traditional dishes and contemporary flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tavche Gravche (a bean stew dish)
  • Pastrmajlija (pastry with pork or meat toppings)
  • Ajvar (a roasted red pepper dip)
  • Ohrid trout

Local Beverages

  • Rakija (a fruit brandy)
  • Macedonian wine
  • Turkish coffee
  • Boza (a fermented drink made from maize or millet)

Local Culture and Customs of Ohrid

Ohrid is a city with a rich history and cultural heritage. Some of the local customs and traditions you should be aware of before visiting include:

  • Greetings always involve a handshake and eye contact
  • Smoking is quite common in public places
  • Macedonians are quite religious, and it is best to dress conservatively when visiting religious buildings

Best Time to Visit Ohrid

The best time to visit Ohrid is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, the shoulder seasons of May/June or September/October offer pleasant weather and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ohrid

Before you visit Ohrid, keep in mind these Do’s and Don’ts to ensure a pleasant experience:

Do:

  • Visit the Icon Gallery
  • Try the local cheeses and wines
  • Take a dip in Lake Ohrid
  • Explore the city’s rich history

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water
  • Smoke in public buildings
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious buildings

Safety and Health

As a tourist, it is important to be aware of the safety and health precautions that come with visiting a new city.

Safety in Ohrid

Overall, Ohrid is a safe destination for travelers. However, you should still exercise caution and keep your valuables in a secure place.

Health in Ohrid

Be sure to drink bottled water to avoid any chance of stomach problems, and ensure you have travel insurance that covers medical treatment.

How to Get Around in Ohrid

Ohrid is a compact city that can easily be covered on foot. However, you can also use public transportation or rent a bike or a car to explore the city and its surroundings.

Conclusion

Ohrid is a captivating and soulful destination that blends its rich cultural heritage with natural beauty. From the iconic landmarks to the adventurous spirit of the town, Ohrid truly has something for everyone.
