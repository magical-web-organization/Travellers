Introduction to Bihać

Nestled in the heart of Bosanska Krajina, Bihać is a picturesque town located in the Una-Sana Canton of Bosnia and Herzegovina. Surrounded by lush green hills and tranquil waters of the Una River, Bihać is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. This hidden gem in the Balkans is less known to tourists, making it an ideal place for a peaceful and authentic travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Bihać

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Fethija Mosque: This 16th-century Ottoman mosque located in the old town of Bihać is the centerpiece of the city’s Islamic heritage.

  2. Una National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the park is home to the stunning Una River waterfalls and rich biodiversity of flora and fauna.

  3. Pliva Waterfall: Located just outside Bihać, this 22-meter high waterfall is a must-visit natural attraction.

  4. Ostrožac Castle: This 16th-century castle stands on the banks of the Una River and offers a glimpse into the region’s medieval history.

Natural Attractions of Bihać: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Una River: With crystal-clear waters and lush greenery, the Una River is a haven for water sports enthusiasts.

  2. Strbacki Buk Waterfall: Located in the heart of Una National Park, this waterfall is a stunning natural wonder with crystal-clear turquoise water.

  3. Una Regatta: A popular summer event in Bihać that attracts water sports enthusiasts from all over the world.

  4. Plitvice Lakes National Park: Only 2 hours drive from Bihać, this national park is famous for its enchanting lakes and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bihać

  1. Una National Park Hiking Trail: A well-marked trail through the park’s lush forests and rocky terrains offering breathtaking views of the Una River.

  2. Una River Bike Trail: A scenic bike route through the picturesque landscapes along the river’s banks.

  3. Vrbas Canyon Hiking Trail: A 10-kilometer trail through the scenic Vrbas Canyon that offers adrenaline-filled adventure amidst nature.

Best Things to Do in Bihać: Activities and Tours

  1. Rafting: The Una River offers class II-IV rapids for adventure seekers.

  2. Kayaking: Enjoy the thrill of paddling through the rapids of the Una River amidst stunning natural surroundings.

  3. Canyoning: Bihać offers some of the best canyoning experiences in the Balkans.

  4. Traditional Bosnian Cooking Class: Learn the art of preparing Bosnian cuisine with a cooking class.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bihać

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the old town of Bihać and visit the Fethija Mosque.
Day 2 Visit the Pliva Waterfall, Ostrožac Castle, and Una National Park.
Day 3 Rafting or kayaking on the Una River.
Day 4 Hiking through the Una National Park.
Day 5 Canyoning in the nearby canyons.
Day 6 Biking along the Una River.
Day 7 Traditional Bosnian cooking class and market visit.

Food and Drink Scene of Bihać

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ćevapi: Grilled minced meat served with flatbread, onions, and sour cream.

  2. Burek: A savory pastry filled with meat or cheese.

  3. Sudžukice: Small sausage bites made with beef and spices.

  4. Pita: A pastry filled with either cheese, potato, or spinach.

Local Beverages

  1. Rakija: A traditional alcoholic drink made from distilled fruit.

  2. Bosnian Coffee: A strong, aromatic coffee served with a side of Turkish delight.

Where to Stay in Bihać: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Opal Exclusive: A luxury hotel located in the city center with river views and spa facilities.

  2. Guesthouse Una: A cozy guesthouse located on the banks of the Una River.

  3. Hostel Majdas: A budget-friendly hostel in the old town of Bihać.

What to Buy in Bihać: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Una Salt: A high-quality salt extracted from underground sources near the Una River.

  2. Handicrafts: Traditional Bosnian handicrafts such as copper kitchenware, woodwork, and pottery are great souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Bihać

Bosnian culture is a mix of Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, and Slavic influences. Hospitality is integral to Bosnian culture, and guests are often invited to share a meal with their host. Islamic culture also plays a significant role in Bihać, with mosques and Islamic education playing a vital role in the local community.

Best Time to Visit Bihać

The best time to visit Bihać is from May to September when the weather is warm and pleasant. The summer months are also ideal for outdoor activities and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bihać

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.

  2. Be friendly with the locals.

  3. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water, stick to bottled water.

  2. Smoke in public areas.

  3. Flash expensive jewelry or cameras in the street.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bihać

Bihać is a safe place for tourists, but it’s always advisable to be cautious in unfamiliar surroundings. It is recommended not to travel alone at night and to avoid leaving valuables in the car.

Health in Bihać

It is advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical coverage when visiting Bihać. Medical facilities in Bihać are limited, and tourists may need to travel to nearby towns for emergency medical treatment.

Getting to Bihać: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bihać

  1. Zagreb International Airport: The closest major airport located approximately 145km from Bihać.

  2. Sarajevo International Airport: Located approximately 250km from Bihać.

Bus Stations Near Bihać

  1. Bihać Bus Station: Located in the city center, offering regular bus services to other Bosnian towns.

  2. Zagreb Bus Station: Located across the border in Croatia, offering regular bus services to Bihać.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers can be arranged from nearby airports and towns to Bihać.

How to Get Around in Bihać

Walking is a great way to explore the old town of Bihać. Taxis and rental cars are also readily available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Bihać: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Plitvice Lakes National Park: A stunning national park located 2 hours drive from Bihać.

  2. Zadar: A beautiful coastal city located approximately 3 hours drive from Bihać.

  3. Sarajevo: The capital city of Bosnia and Herzegovina, located approximately 4 hours drive from Bihać.

Conclusion

Bihać is a hidden gem in the Balkans, offering an authentic travel experience amidst stunning natural surroundings and rich cultural heritage. With its outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine, Bihać is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for a unique travel experience.
