If you’re looking for a destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Mostar should be on the top of your travel list. This city in the heart of Herzegovina, in Bosnia and Herzegovina, in Balkans, in Europe is famous for its iconic bridge, historic landmarks, and scenic landscapes. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Mostar, covering everything from the must-visit attractions to the best food and drink options and local customs and traditions.

Introduction to Mostar

Mostar is a city in southern Bosnia and Herzegovina, located on the Neretva River. It is known for its Ottoman-era architecture, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. The city was heavily damaged during the War of Bosnia in the early 1990s, but has been rebuilt and restored, and is now a popular tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Mostar

When it comes to the top attractions in Mostar, the list is endless.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stari Most Bridge: This iconic bridge, originally built in the 16th century, was destroyed during the war and rebuilt in 2004. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of peace and unity.
  • Old Town: The historic Old Town of Mostar is a testament to its rich heritage. Walk through the cobbled streets and alleyways, and admire the mix of Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian architecture.
  • Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque: This 17th-century mosque is located in the Old Town and offers stunning views of the city from its minaret.
  • Muslibegović House: This Ottoman-style mansion, built in the 18th century, is now a museum that showcases the life and culture of the Ottoman Empire.

Natural Attractions of Mostar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kravice Waterfalls: Located about 40km from Mostar, these stunning waterfalls are a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Blagaj Tekke: This 15th-century Sufi monastery is located on the Buna River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Vrelo Bune: This natural spring feeds the Buna River and is a popular spot for boat tours and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mostar

Mostar is surrounded by mountains and offers several hiking and biking routes. The most popular ones include:

  • Mt. Velez: This is the highest mountain in the Mostar region and offers stunning panoramic views from its summit.
  • Bijele Vode: This scenic trail takes you through the rocky landscapes and lush forests of the Dinaric Alps.
  • Prenj Mountain: This is one of the most challenging hikes in Bosnia and Herzegovina, but the views from the top are worth it.

Best Things to Do in Mostar: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Mostar, whether you’re into history, culture, or outdoor adventures. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Walking Tour of Mostar: A guided tour of the city’s top attractions, including the Old Town, Stari Most Bridge, and Muslibegović House.
  • Rafting on the Neretva River: This thrilling activity takes you on a ride through the rapids and canyons of the Neretva River.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Enthusiasts can enjoy a visit to one of Herzegovina’s wineries and sample their finest wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Mostar

Mostar’s food scene is a reflection of its multicultural heritage, with a mix of Ottoman, Balkan, and Mediterranean flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ćevapi: Grilled meat sausages, usually served with onions, ajvar, and pita bread.
  • Burek: A filo pasty filled with cheese, meat, or spinach.
  • Begova Čorba: A hearty soup with beef and vegetables, usually served with homemade bread.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija: A popular Balkan fruit brandy, usually made from plums, cherries, or grapes.
  • Wine: Herzegovina is known for its excellent wines, including Blatina, Žilavka, and Vranac.
  • Turkish coffee: A strong, sweet, and aromatic coffee, often served with Turkish delight.

Where to Stay in Mostar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Mostar, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Hotel Mepas: A five-star hotel located in the city center, offering stylish rooms and suites.
  • Hostel Majdas: A popular budget-friendly hostel located in the Old Town, offering dorm rooms and private rooms.
  • Villa Anri: A family-run guesthouse located on the outskirts of the city, offering comfortable rooms, a garden, and a pool.

What to Buy in Mostar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mostar is a great place to buy souvenirs and local products. Some of the best options include:

  • Handicrafts: Mostar is known for its traditional handicrafts, including woodcarvings, copperware, and pottery.
  • Turkish Delight: A sweet, chewy candy usually made from sugar, cornstarch, and nuts.
  • Local wines and spirits: Bring home a bottle of Herzegovinian wine or rakija as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mostar

Mostar has a rich culture and traditions, influenced by the Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian empires. Here are some of the local customs and traditions:

  • Greeting: In Mostar, it is customary to shake hands and exchange pleasantries when meeting someone.
  • Dress code: Mostar is a modest city, and it is advisable to dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious landmarks.
  • Religion: The majority of the population in Mostar is Muslim, and visitors should show respect for Islamic customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Mostar

The best time to visit Mostar is in spring (April to May) and autumn (September to October), when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and crowded, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mostar

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mostar:

Do:

  • Learn a few Bosnian phrases, and use them when interacting with locals.
  • Try local cuisine and wines.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage natural or historical landmarks.
  • Do not haggle too aggressively in markets or souks.
  • Do not wander into unsafe areas or streets at night.

Safety and Health

Mostar is a safe city for tourists, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and not leaving their belongings unattended.

Safety in Mostar

  • Be aware of pickpockets and petty theft in crowded areas.
  • Do not leave your valuables unattended in public places.
  • Avoid political discussions or getting involved in public protests.

Health in Mostar

  • It is advisable to drink bottled water or boiled water in Mostar.
  • Make sure to get travel insurance before your trip.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues during your stay.

Getting to Mostar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mostar is accessible by air, road, and rail. The nearest airports are located in Sarajevo and Dubrovnik, and there are daily bus and train services linking Mostar to other major cities in Bosnia and Herzegovina and Croatia.

Airports Near Mostar

  • Sarajevo International Airport: Located about 125km from Mostar, with regular flight connections to major European cities.
  • Dubrovnik International Airport: Located about 150km from Mostar, with regular flight connections to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Mostar

  • Mostar Bus Station: Located in the city center, with daily bus services to other major cities in Bosnia and Herzegovina and Croatia.
  • Sarajevo Bus Station: Located in the city center, with regular bus services to Mostar, Dubrovnik, and other cities in Bosnia and Herzegovina and Croatia.

Other Options

  • Train: There are daily train services linking Mostar to other major cities in Bosnia and Herzegovina and Croatia.
  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore Mostar and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Mostar

Mostar is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and buses are also available, but visitors should negotiate the price in advance.

Where to Go Next from Mostar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mostar is a great base for exploring other parts of Bosnia and Herzegovina and Croatia. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Sarajevo: The capital of Bosnia and Herzegovina, located about 130km from Mostar, offers a blend of Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian culture and history.
  • Dubrovnik: A stunning coastal city in Croatia, located about 150km from Mostar, known for its medieval walls and beautiful beaches.
  • Medjugorje: A small town located about 30km from Mostar, known for its Marian apparitions and pilgrimage sites.

Conclusion

Mostar is a gem of a city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, you’ll find plenty to explore and experience in this charming town.
