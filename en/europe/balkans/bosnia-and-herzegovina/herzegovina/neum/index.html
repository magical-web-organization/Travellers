Introduction to Neum

Neum is a charming town located in Herzegovina, Bosnia and Herzegovina. As the only coastal town of Bosnia and Herzegovina, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. With its picturesque beaches, crystal-clear waters, heritage sites, and rich gastronomy, Neum has something to offer for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Neum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Franciscan Monastery
  • Turkish House
  • The Old Town

Neum has a rich history, and its cultural heritage is well preserved in its museums and historical sites. The Franciscan Monastery dates back to the 14th century and is an important pilgrimage site. The Turkish House is a fine example of the Ottoman architecture that has been preserved to this day. The Old Town is a labyrinth of narrow streets, traditional houses, and charming cafes.

Natural Attractions of Neum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Neum Beach
  • Hutovo Blato Nature Park
  • Lake Boracko

If you love outdoor adventures, you will love Neum. The town’s central attraction is its stunning pebble beach that stretches over several kilometers. Hutovo Blato Nature Park, located near Neum, is a bird-watchers’ paradise and offers plenty of activities such as kayaking, fishing, and hiking. Another popular natural attraction in Neum is Lake Boracko, known for its serene beauty and stunning mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neum

  • The Neum-Klek Coastal Trail
  • The Vjetrenica Cave Trail
  • Dubravska Greda Trail

Hiking and biking in Neum are popular outdoor activities, and several trails cater to enthusiasts of all levels. The most popular hiking trail here is the Neum-Klek Coastal Trail that provides stunning views of the Adriatic Sea. The Vjetrenica Cave Trail is another popular hiking trail that takes you through the lush green forest, rocky terrains, and ends with a stunning view of the Vjetrenica Cave. Dubravska Greda Trail is an excellent option for mountain bikers, providing challenging uphill climb and a thrilling downhill ride.

Best Things to Do in Neum: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours
  • Wine Tasting and Vineyard Tours
  • Tour of Neum Old Town

Boat tours offer an excellent way to explore the stunning coastline and the pristine waters of the Adriatic Sea. Wine tasting tours provide an opportunity to discover the unique flavors of the local wines and learn about the process of winemaking. A tour of the Neum Old Town will take you through its charming streets and give you a glimpse of its rich cultural and historical heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Neum

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ćevapi
  • Burek
  • Sarma

Neum has a rich gastronomy, influenced by both the Mediterranean and the Ottoman cuisine. Ćevapi, a dish with grilled meat served with pita bread, is a local specialty that you must try. Burek, a crispy pie filled with meat or cheese, is another popular dish. Sarma, a dish made of stuffed rolls of grape, cabbage, or chard leaves, is a must-try in the winters.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija
  • Wine

Rakija, a traditional fruit brandy, is the most popular local beverage in Neum. It is usually served as an aperitif or a digestive after meals. Wine, with its unique flavors, is another popular local beverage that is worth trying.

What to Buy in Neum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil
  • Wines
  • Local Handicrafts

Olive oil and wines are popular local products that make for excellent souvenirs. You can also buy locally made handicrafts, such as traditional carpets, pottery, and wooden utensils, to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Neum

The locals in Neum are warm and friendly, and hospitality is an essential aspect of their culture. Islam and Christianity are the two primary religions, and mosques and churches are scattered throughout the town. It is essential to dress modestly while visiting religious sites or attending a religious function.

Best Time to Visit Neum

The best time to visit Neum is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and the sea is perfect for swimming. For those who prefer a quieter atmosphere, the winter months are an excellent time to visit, with smaller crowds and agreeable temperatures for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neum

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach
  • Take photographs of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Neum

Neum is a safe and peaceful town, and incidents of crime are rare. However, it is advisable to take care of your valuables and avoid exploring isolated areas after dark.

Health in Neum

As with any travel destination, it is essential to take the necessary precautions to stay healthy while staying in Neum. Drink only bottled water and avoid consuming raw or undercooked food. Consult a doctor regarding any vaccinations and carry a first-aid kit.

Getting to Neum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neum

The nearest airport to Neum is Dubrovnik Airport in Croatia, which is 88km away. Several shuttle services operate between the airport and Neum.

Bus Stations Near Neum

Several bus services operate between major towns and cities in Bosnia and Herzegovina and Neum. Some of the popular options include the Neobas and Centrotrans bus services.

Other Options

Taxis are another convenient option to travel in and around Neum. Alternatively, you can rent a car and explore the town and its surroundings at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Neum

Neum is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Alternatively, you can hire a bicycle or scooter to explore the town and its surroundings. Taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Neum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dubrovnik, Croatia
  • Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina
  • Korcula, Croatia

Neum is an excellent base to explore the surrounding towns and cities. Dubrovnik, Croatia, known for its stunning Old Town, is just an hour’s drive away. Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina, famous for its Ottoman Bridge, is a two-hour drive from Neum. Korcula, Croatia, known for its untouched natural beauty and wine-making industry, is just a ferry ride away.

Conclusion

Neum is a hidden gem in the Balkans, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. Plan your perfect trip to Neum with this comprehensive guide and explore all that this charming town has to offer!
