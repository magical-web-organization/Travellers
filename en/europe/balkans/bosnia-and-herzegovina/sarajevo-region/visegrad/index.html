Višegrad, located in the Sarajevo Region of Bosnia and Herzegovina, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This small town in the Balkans offers a unique blend of rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re an adventurer seeking outdoor activities or a history enthusiast interested in UNESCO sites, Višegrad has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay and eat, and local customs.

Introduction to Višegrad

Višegrad is a small town nestled in the Drina River valley of the Bosnian Mountains. It’s known for its cultural heritage, and one of its landmarks, Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s a perfect destination for those seeking to experience Bosnia and Herzegovina’s authentic lifestyle as it’s still untouched by mass tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Višegrad

Višegrad has cultural, historical, and natural attractions that are a must-visit for all travelers. Here are some of the top places to see in Višegrad.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge: This historic bridge crossing the Drina River is a masterpiece of Ottoman architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built-in 1577, the bridge is not only an important landmark but also a symbol of Višegrad.

  • Andricgrad: This small town, built-in 2011, is a cultural project named after the Nobel Prize-winning author, Ivo Andric. Andricgrad consists of a library, cinema, theatre, museums, and galleries.

  • Tekija: This medieval monastery is located in the city center and dates back to the 16th century. It is one of the most important historical and cultural sites in Višegrad.

Natural Attractions of Višegrad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Drvengrad: It’s a small ethno-village, also known as Kustendorf, located in the nearby Mokra Gora region. The village was built by the eminent Serbian filmmaker, Emir Kusturica. It’s a perfect spot for nature lovers and those seeking peace and tranquility.

  • Kokin Brod: This village is located near the border with Serbia. It’s a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering several hiking and biking trails.

  • Višegrad Spa: This natural thermal spa offers a relaxing and rejuvenating experience. It’s located in a picturesque valley surrounded by mountains, making it a perfect spot to unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Višegrad

Višegrad is a paradise for hikers and cyclists, offering a plethora of trails that cater to beginners and experts alike. Here are some of the most popular hiking/biking routes in Višegrad.

  • Via Dinarica: It’s a long-distance hiking/biking route that runs through several Balkan countries, including Bosnia and Herzegovina. The Bosnian section of the route starts from Višegrad and passes through the Durmitor National Park.

  • Kokin Brod-Rača: This is a moderate hiking route that starts from the Kokin Brod village and ends at the Rača village. The trail offers stunning views of the Drina River valley and the surrounding mountains.

  • Sopotnica Waterfall: Although not a hiking route, this waterfall is a must-visit for nature lovers. It’s located near the Sopotnica village, and the path leading up to the waterfall offers breathtaking views of the mountainous countryside.

Best Things to Do in Višegrad: Activities and Tours

Apart from the scenic beauty, Višegrad offers several activities and tours that make your visit more memorable. Here are some of the best things to do in Višegrad.

  • Rafting on the Drina River: It’s a popular activity among tourists and locals alike. The Drina River offers several rapids that cater to beginners and experts.

  • Visit to Andricgrad: A guided tour of Andricgrad offers an excellent opportunity to learn more about the region’s culture and history.

  • Traditional Bosnian Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Bosnian dishes like cevapi, burek, and baklava from the locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Višegrad

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long trip to Višegrad.

Days Activities
1-2 Visit Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge, Tekija monastery and stroll around the city center.

Food and Drink Scene of Višegrad

Višegrad is known for its traditional Bosnian cuisine, hearty portions, and delicious flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cevapi: Grilled minced meat served with onion, ajvar, and pita bread.

  • Burek: A pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

  • Karahodžiča: A traditional Bosnian stew made of lamb, vegetables, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Rakija: A traditional alcoholic drink made from fermented fruit, typically plums.

  • Bosanska kafa: A thick, strong coffee served in small cups.

Where to Stay in Višegrad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Višegrad offers several accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of our top picks.

  • Hotel Andric: A four-star hotel located in the heart of the town, offering comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.

  • Guesthouse Bajka: A cozy guesthouse located near the Drina River. It offers clean rooms, a serene ambiance, and delicious breakfast.

  • Hostel Sokolina: A budget-friendly hostel located near the Mehmed Paša Sokolović Bridge. It offers dormitory-style rooms, a shared kitchen, and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Višegrad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Višegrad is famous for its traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some of the local products to bring back home.

  • Filigree Jewelry: Handmade jewelry made from silver or gold wire.

  • Sevdalinka CD: Sevdalinka is a traditional Bosnian music genre. The CD makes for a perfect souvenir and a memorable way to experience the local culture.

  • Handmade Carpets: Višegrad is known for its high-quality handmade carpets, which make for a perfect souvenir and a beautiful addition to your home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Višegrad

Višegrad has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals take pride in preserving their traditions. Here are some local customs to keep in mind while visiting Višegrad.

  • Dress Modestly: Bosnia and Herzegovina is a conservative country, and it’s crucial to respect the local customs. Avoid wearing revealing clothes, especially when visiting religious sites and mosques.

  • Greet with a Smile: The locals in Višegrad are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness. It’s customary to greet people with a smile and a friendly “Dobar dan” (Good day).

  • Remove Shoes Before Entering Homes: It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s house or a mosque, so make sure to pay attention to this custom.

Best Time to Visit Višegrad

The best time to visit Višegrad is from May to September when the weather is warm and pleasant. July and August can get crowded with tourists, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Višegrad

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Višegrad.

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Bosnia and Herzegovina is a conservative country, and it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try Traditional Bosnian Cuisine: Višegrad is known for its delicious food, and it’s a must-try while visiting.

  • Learn About the Local Culture: Višegrad has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s worth taking the time to learn more about it.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Religious Sites: Bosnia and Herzegovina has several religious sites, and it’s crucial to respect them.

  • Be Loud and Boisterous: The locals in Višegrad are friendly and hospitable, but it’s important not to be loud and boisterous in public places.

  • Take Pictures Without Permission: It’s essential to ask for permission before taking pictures of people, especially women, as it’s considered disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Višegrad is a safe destination to visit, with a relatively low crime rate. However, it’s essential to take the necessary precautions, such as keeping your valuables in a safe place and being aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Višegrad

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: It’s best to avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.

  • Be Wary of Scams: There may be some scams targeted at unsuspecting tourists, so be vigilant and careful when dealing with strangers.

Health in Višegrad

  • Stay Hydrated: The weather can get hot and dry, so make sure to drink enough water and fluids to stay hydrated.

  • Use Sunscreen: The sun is intense, and it’s essential to use sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburns.

Getting to Višegrad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Višegrad is relatively accessible, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Višegrad

  • Sarajevo International Airport: It’s the nearest airport to Višegrad, located around 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Višegrad

  • Višegrad Bus Station: It’s located in the city center and operates regional and domestic routes.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Višegrad, and the rates are reasonable.

  • Rent a Car: If you’re looking for more flexibility in terms of transportation, you can rent a car from Sarajevo International Airport.

How to Get Around in Višegrad

Višegrad is a small town, and most of the attractions are accessible on foot. However, if you’re looking for more flexibility, you can use the following transportation options.

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Višegrad, and the rates are reasonable.

  • Bicycle: You can rent a bicycle from several rental shops in the town.

Where to Go Next from Višegrad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Višegrad is a perfect base to explore the surrounding areas of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

  • Sarajevo: The capital city of Bosnia and Herzegovina is located around 120 km away from Višegrad.

  • Mokra Gora: A small village located across the border in Serbia, offering a scenic train ride through the mountains.

  • Tara National Park: Located in Serbia, it offers a stunning natural reserve and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Višegrad is a hidden gem in Bosnia and Herzegovina, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and nature. With its breathtaking scenery, warm hospitality, and rich cultural heritage, this small town in the Balkans should definitely be on your travel bucket list.
