Introduction to Nordic Countries

The Nordic countries consist of Norway, Sweden, Finland, Denmark, and Iceland. This region is famous for its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and unique customs. Nordic countries are a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors every year.

List of Places to Visit in Nordic Countries

There are many places to visit in Nordic countries, ranging from bustling cities to serene countryside. Some of the must-visit places in Nordic countries are:

  • Copenhagen, Denmark
  • Helsinki, Finland
  • Oslo, Norway
  • Reykjavik, Iceland
  • Stockholm, Sweden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nordic countries are rich in history and culture, and there are many museums and historical places to explore. Some of the best ones are:

  • The Viking Ship Museum in Oslo, Norway
  • The National Museum of Finland in Helsinki, Finland
  • The National Museum of Iceland in Reykjavik, Iceland
  • The Royal Palace in Stockholm, Sweden
  • The National Museum of Denmark in Copenhagen, Denmark

Natural Attractions of Nordic Countries: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nordic countries are known for their natural beauty, and there is no shortage of outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the best natural attractions are:

  • The Northern Lights in Iceland
  • The Fjords in Norway
  • The Archipelago Sea in Finland
  • The Lapland wilderness in Sweden
  • The Faroe Islands in Denmark

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nordic Countries

The Nordic countries offer some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the world. Some of the popular routes are:

  • The Kungsleden Trail in Sweden
  • The Rallarvegen Route in Norway
  • The Archipelago Trail in Finland
  • The Laugavegur Trail in Iceland
  • The Søndervig Route in Denmark

Best Things to Do in Nordic Countries: Activities and Tours

Nordic countries offer a wide array of activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Dog sledding in Lapland, Finland
  • Whale watching in Iceland
  • Visiting the Christmas markets in Stockholm, Sweden
  • Exploring the fjords in Norway
  • Biking in Copenhagen, Denmark

Week-Long Itinerary for Nordic Countries

If you are visiting Nordic countries for a week, the best itinerary is:

  • Day 1: Explore Copenhagen, Denmark
  • Day 2: Visit Oslo, Norway
  • Day 3: See the Northern Lights in Reykjavik, Iceland
  • Day 4: Go hiking in Lapland, Sweden
  • Day 5: Visit Helsinki, Finland
  • Day 6: Explore Stockholm, Sweden
  • Day 7: Relax in the Blue Lagoon in Iceland

  • Hotel d’Angleterre in Copenhagen, Denmark
  • Hotel Continental in Oslo, Norway
  • Hotel Kämp in Helsinki, Finland
  • Radisson Blu Royal Viking Hotel in Stockholm, Sweden
  • Hotel Rangá in Iceland

What to Buy in Nordic Countries: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nordic countries are famous for their local products, from handmade crafts to delicious food items. Some of the best things to buy are:

  • Handmade woolen sweaters from Iceland
  • Finnish Sauna products from Finland
  • Danish ceramics from Denmark
  • Norwegian chocolates from Norway
  • Swedish glassware from Sweden

Local Culture and Customs of Nordic Countries

Nordic countries have unique customs and traditions that are worth experiencing. Some of the notable customs and traditions are:

  • Fika in Sweden
  • Hygge in Denmark
  • Sauna in Finland
  • Janteloven in Norway
  • Whaling in Iceland

Best Time to Visit Nordic Countries

The best time to visit Nordic countries is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and the days are long. The winter months (December-February) are the best time to see the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nordic Countries

Do:

  • Do take off your shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Do dress modestly when visiting churches
  • Do try local cuisine
  • Do respect personal space

Don’t:

  • Don’t be overly chatty with strangers
  • Don’t be late for appointments
  • Don’t cut in line
  • Don’t drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Nordic Countries

Nordic countries are generally safe, with low crime rates. However, it is important to be aware of pickpocketing in tourist areas.

Health in Nordic Countries

Nordic countries have a high standard of healthcare. It is recommended to have travel insurance, as medical expenses can be expensive.

Getting to Nordic Countries: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Nordic countries is easy, with many transportation options available. Some of the best options are:

  • Flying into international airports in Oslo, Helsinki, Stockholm, or Copenhagen
  • Taking a ferry from nearby countries such as Germany or Estonia
  • Traveling by train or bus within the Nordic countries

Airports Near Nordic Countries

  • Oslo Airport, Gardermoen, Norway
  • Helsinki Airport, Finland
  • Stockholm Arlanda Airport, Sweden
  • Copenhagen Airport, Denmark
  • Keflavik International Airport, Iceland

Bus Stations Near Nordic Countries

  • Oslo Bus Terminal, Norway
  • Kamppi Center Bus Station, Helsinki, Finland
  • Stockholm Hudiksvallsgatan Bus Terminal, Sweden
  • Copenhagen Central Bus Station, Denmark
  • Hlemmur Bus Station, Reykjavik, Iceland

Other Options

  • Ferry service between Helsinki, Finland and Tallinn, Estonia
  • Train service between Copenhagen, Denmark and Malmö, Sweden
