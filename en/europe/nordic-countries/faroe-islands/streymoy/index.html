Streymoy, the largest of the 18 stunning islands of the Faroe Islands, is a must-visit destination for those looking to discover the raw beauty and unparalleled serenity of the Nordic countries. This island is known for its dramatic landscapes, scenic villages, and vibrant culture that have been shaped by centuries of Viking heritage. We have curated a comprehensive travel guide to ensure that your exploration of Streymoy is filled with adventure, relaxation, and unforgettable experiences.

Introduction to Streymoy

Streymoy covers an area of 347 sq. km and is home to a population of around 24,000 Faroese. The capital city, Tórshavn, is located on the east coast of Streymoy and serves as the cultural and administrative center of the Faroe Islands. The island is connected to the neighboring island of Vágar through a sub-sea tunnel and is easily accessible by road, ferry, and air.

List of Places to Visit in Streymoy

Streymoy has a range of attractions that cater to different interests. From museums and historical sites to breathtaking natural wonders and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in history and culture, Streymoy has a lot to offer. The following are some of the must-visit cultural landmarks on the island:

  • Historical fortresses: Streymoy has two fortresses that date back to the 16th century - Skansin, located in Tórshavn, and Skálavík, located on the western coast of the island.
  • National Museum of the Faroe Islands: Located in Tórshavn, this museum houses a collection of artifacts, art pieces, and historical documents that trace the Faroese culture back to the Viking Age.
  • Nólsoyar Páll statue: This bronze statue pays homage to Poul Nolsøe, a national hero of the Faroe Islands who fought for the rights of the Faroese people in the Danish Parliament.

Natural Attractions of Streymoy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Streymoy’s breathtaking natural scenery is a testament to its remoteness and Nordic beauty. Some of the must-visit natural attractions on the island include:

  • Saksun: This picturesque village is situated on the west coast of Streymoy and is known for its turf-roofed houses, lagoon, and breathtaking beach.
  • Tjørnuvík: This is the northernmost village of Streymoy and is known for its black sand beach and beautiful hiking trails.
  • Glyvrar: This charming village is located on the southeastern coast of Streymoy and is known for its scenic views of the Atlantic Ocean, harbor, and traditional Faroese houses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Streymoy

Streymoy’s rugged terrain is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the island’s natural wonders on foot or by bike. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes on the island:

  • Villingardalsfjall: This challenging 6km hiking trail starts at the outskirts of Tórshavn and takes you to the summit of one of the highest mountains on the island. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains, valleys, and villages along the way.
  • Senderød: This 5km hiking trail starts at the village of Hvalvík and takes you through stunning valleys and hillsides that overlook the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Saksun to Tjørnuvík: This 6.5km hiking trail takes you through some of the most scenic landscapes of Streymoy, including the picturesque village of Saksun and the stunning black sand beach of Tjørnuvík.

Best Things to Do in Streymoy: Activities and Tours

Streymoy offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups. Some of the best things to do on the island include:

  • Boat tours: Explore the stunning fjords and coastline of Streymoy on a scenic boat tour.
  • Fishing tours: Experience traditional Faroese fishing techniques and catch your own dinner on a guided fishing tour.
  • Whale watching: Witness the magic of these magnificent creatures from the comfort of a boat.
  • Horseback riding: Experience the breathtaking landscapes of Streymoy on horseback.
  • Birdwatching: The Faroe Islands are home to a wide range of bird species, including puffins, gannets, and kittiwakes.
  • Visit the fish market: The Tórshavn fish market is a vibrant hub of activity, where you can taste some of the freshest seafood in the Nordic region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Streymoy

If you are planning a week-long trip to Streymoy, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time on the island:

Day 1: Arrive at Vágar Airport and take the sub-sea tunnel to Streymoy. Check into your hotel in Tórshavn and explore the city's cultural landmarks, including the National Museum and Skansin Fortress.
Day 2: Explore the charming village of Kirkjubøur, visit the historical ruins, and hike to the scenic Koltur Island.
Day 3: Experience traditional Faroese life in the picturesque village of Saksun and hike to the beautiful black sand beach of Tjørnuvík.
Day 4: Take a boat tour of Streymoy's stunning coastline and fjords.
Day 5: Go hiking in the beautiful surrounding mountains of Villingardalsfjall.
Day 6: Visit the charming fishing village of Glyvrar and go birdwatching.
Day 7: Spend your last day in Tórshavn, exploring the city's innovative food scene and shopping for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Streymoy

Streymoy has a unique culinary scene that is influenced by traditional Faroese ingredients and cooking techniques, as well as modern Nordic cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grind og spik: This is a traditional Faroese dish made with whale meat, blubber, and potatoes.
  • Ræstur fiskur: This fermented fish dish is a staple in Faroese cuisine and is typically served with potatoes and turnips.
  • Lamb: Faroese lamb is considered to be one of the best in the world, owing to the island’s rich pastures and clean environment.

Local Beverages

  • Black Death: This is a strong Faroese schnapps made from potatoes and flavored with herbs.
  • Beer: Faroe Islands has some of the finest microbreweries in the world that are renowned for producing top-notch artisanal beer.

Where to Stay in Streymoy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Streymoy has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay on the island include:

  • Hotel Føroyar: This hotel is situated on a hilltop, overlooking Tórshavn, and offers panoramic views of the island. It is known for its modern Scandinavian design and impeccable service.
  • Guesthouse Marknagil: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of the Tórshavn and offers cozy rooms and easy access to the city’s landmarks.
  • Gjáargarður Guesthouse: This guesthouse is situated in the scenic village of Gjógv and offers stunning views of the North Atlantic Ocean. It is also known for its restaurant that serves traditional Faroese dishes.

What to Buy in Streymoy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Streymoy offers a range of unique local products and souvenirs that make for great gifts and mementos of your trip. Some of the best things to bring home from Streymoy include:

  • Hand-knitted woolen products: Faroese wool is renowned for its quality and warmth, and you can find a range of hand-knitted products on the island, including hats, scarves, and sweaters.
  • Artisanal handicrafts: Faroese artisans produce a range of handicrafts, including wooden boats, jewelry, and pottery.
  • Local food products: Bring home some of the local seafood, meats, and dairy products that are exclusive to the Faroe Islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Streymoy

Faroese people are known for their warm hospitality and unique culture that is shaped by a mix of old and new traditions. Here are some tips to help you navigate Streymoy’s local culture:

  • Be respectful: Faroese people are very respectful of their culture and history, so it’s important to be mindful of local customs and practices.
  • Say “takk fyrir” or “thank you” when given something: This is a polite way to show gratitude and respect.
  • Participate in local events and festivals: The Faroe Islands have a range of cultural and social events throughout the year, and participating in them is a great way to get to know the locals and experience the island’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Streymoy

The best time to visit Streymoy is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. This is also the peak tourist season, so it’s important to book accommodation and activities in advance. Visiting Streymoy during the shoulder season (May, September, and October) can also be a good option for those looking to avoid crowds and enjoy lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Streymoy

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife: The Faroe Islands have a fragile ecosystem and are home to a wide range of wildlife, so it’s important to be respectful and avoid littering or disturbing the wildlife.
  • Book tours and activities in advance: Streymoy’s tourism industry is growing, so it’s important to book popular tours and activities in advance to avoid disappointment.
  • Try the local cuisine: The Faroe Islands have a unique culinary scene that is worth exploring in depth.

  • Disrupt local events and festivals: The Faroe Islands have a range of cultural and social events throughout the year, so it’s important to be respectful and avoid disrupting them.

  • Ignore local customs and practices: The Faroe Islands have a strong cultural identity and set of customs, so it’s important to be aware of local practices and customs.

Getting to Streymoy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Streymoy is easily accessible by road, ferry, and air, and is well-connected to other destinations in the Faroe Islands and beyond.

Airports Near Streymoy

The nearest airport to Streymoy is Vágar Airport, which is located on the neighboring island of Vágar. The airport has regular flights to and from Copenhagen, Denmark, and other European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Streymoy

Streymoy has an extensive bus network that connects the island’s towns and villages. Tórshavn Bus Terminal is the main bus station on the island and offers regular services to other destinations in the Faroe Islands.

Other Options

Ferries are also available to and from Streymoy. The island is connected to the neighboring islands of Eysturoy, Vágar, and Sandoy by ferry.

How to Get Around in Streymoy

Streymoy has a range of transportation options available, which include renting a car, taking a bus, or biking. Taxis are also available but can be expensive. Exploring the island on foot is also a popular option for those looking to take in the breathtaking scenery at a leisurely pace.

The Faroe Islands offer a range of nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring. The following are some of the best destinations to visit after Streymoy:
