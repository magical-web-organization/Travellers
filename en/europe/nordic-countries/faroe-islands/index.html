Comprehensive Travel Guide to Faroe Islands

Faroe Islands is a group of 18 stunning islands located in the North Atlantic, halfway between Iceland and Norway. It is a part of the Nordic countries, in Europe, and is known for its natural beauty, rugged landscapes, and unique culture. In this comprehensive guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your visit to Faroe Islands.

Introduction to Faroe Islands

Faroe Islands is a self-governing territory within the Kingdom of Denmark, with a population of about 50,000 people. The islands have their own language, Faroese, in addition to Danish, and are known for their independent spirit and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Faroe Islands

Faroe Islands is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, with many stunning attractions to explore. Here are some of the top places to visit in Faroe Islands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National Museum of Faroe Islands: Learn about Faroese culture, history, and traditions at this fascinating museum located in the capital city of Tórshavn.
  2. Kirkjubøur: Explore the ruins of an ancient church and visit the home of the Bishop of the Faroe Islands, one of the oldest inhabited wooden houses in Europe.
  3. Ólavsøka Festival: Every July, the capital city celebrates the National Day of Faroe Islands with concerts, parades, and traditional Faroese sports.

Natural Attractions of Faroe Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sørvágsvatn: This stunning lake is situated on the island of Vágar and is known for its optical illusion, appearing to be hundreds of feet above the ocean.
  2. Fossá Waterfall: One of the highest waterfalls in Faroe Islands, located in the northern part of the island of Streymoy.
  3. Bøsdalafossur Waterfall: A breathtaking waterfall located on the cliffs of Vagar Island.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Faroe Islands

Faroe Islands is a hiker’s paradise, with many stunning trails and breathtaking views. Some of the top routes to explore include:

  1. Slættaratindur: The highest peak in Faroe Islands, it offers breathtaking views of the archipelago from the summit.
  2. Múlafossur Waterfall Trail: A scenic trail leading to the picturesque waterfall on the island of Vágar.
  3. Lake Sørvágsvatn Trail: A challenging trail that leads to the stunning lake with an optical illusion mentioned earlier.

Best Things to Do in Faroe Islands: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do in Faroe Islands, from hiking and fishing to exploring the local culture and traditions. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  1. Fishing Tours: Experience the rich fishing culture of Faroe Islands with guided fishing tours.
  2. Puffin Tours: Get up close and personal with the adorable puffins that populate the islands during the summer months.
  3. Whale Watching Tours: Watch these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat on a whale watching tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Faroe Islands

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Faroe Islands:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Tórshavn and explore the capital city, visit the National Museum of Faroe Islands.
2 Explore the island of Streymoy, including the Fossá Waterfall and the village of Saksun.
3 Hike to the summit of Slættaratindur, the highest peak in Faroe Islands.
4 Visit the island of Vágar, including the picturesque Bøsdalafossur Waterfall and the village of Gásadalur.
5 Take a guided fishing tour or explore the historic village of Kirkjubøur.
6 Go on a puffin or whale watching tour, or visit the stunning Sørvágsvatn lake.
7 Relax in Tórshavn and sample some of the delicious local cuisine before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Faroe Islands

Faroe Islands is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, with an emphasis on locally sourced ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Grind og spik: A traditional Faroese dish consisting of whale meat.
  2. Ræst kjøt: Fermented sheep meat that has been hung and dried.
  3. Fiskur við spónn: Codfish served with potatoes and a creamy sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Faroese Beer: Try some of the locally brewed beers, including Okkara, Føroya Bjór, and Sct. Jacobs.
  2. Faroese Schnapps: Known as Black Death, this potent spirit is made from potatoes and caraway seeds.

Where to Stay in Faroe Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Faroe Islands, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  1. Hotel Føroyar: A luxury hotel located on a hill overlooking Tórshavn.
  2. Gjáargarður Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located in the village of Gjógv.
  3. Hostel Kerjalon: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Tórshavn.

What to Buy in Faroe Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Faroe Islands, be sure to pick up some unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the top options:

  1. Knitwear: Faroese wool is famous for its quality, and you can find beautiful knitwear in many shops on the islands.
  2. Books: Faroese literature is rich and varied, and there are many bookstores selling works in Faroese and other languages.
  3. Local Handicrafts: You will find many shops selling traditional Faroese handicrafts, including pottery, woodcarvings, and woven textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Faroe Islands

Faroe Islands has a rich and unique culture, with many traditions and customs that are still practiced today. Here are some of the key aspects of Faroese culture:

  1. Music: Faroese music is an essential part of the culture, with many popular musicians and festivals celebrating the local music scene.
  2. Sports: Faroe Islands has a strong sports culture, with traditional sports such as rowing and football being very popular.
  3. Faroese Language: Faroese is the official language of the islands, and it is taught in schools alongside Danish.

Best Time to Visit Faroe Islands

The best time to visit Faroe Islands is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long and bright. However, it is also possible to visit Faroe Islands year-round, with each season offering its own unique charm and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Faroe Islands

In Faroe Islands, visitors should be aware of local customs and traditions. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately, especially when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages, including the traditional Faroese dishes and locally brewed beers.

Don’t:

  1. Disturb wildlife or damage the environment.
  2. Ignore local customs and traditions.
  3. Expect shops and restaurants to be open late at night.

Safety and Health

Faroe Islands is a safe and healthy destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of the following:

Safety in Faroe Islands

  1. Be cautious when hiking or exploring the rugged terrain, as it can be dangerous in some areas.
  2. Take precautions when driving, as the roads can be narrow and winding.

Health in Faroe Islands

  1. There are no major health risks in Faroe Islands, but visitors should pack any necessary medication and consult their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Faroe Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Faroe Islands is accessible via air and sea, with several airlines and ferry companies offering service to the islands. Here are some of the logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Faroe Islands

  1. Vágar Airport: Located on the island of Vágar, the primary airport serving Faroe Islands.
  2. Tórshavn Airport: A small airport located just outside the capital city of Tórshavn.

Bus Stations Near Faroe Islands

  1. Bus Terminal in Tórshavn: The main bus station in Faroe Islands, offering connections throughout the islands.

Other Options

  1. Ferry: Several ferry companies offer service to Faroe Islands from Denmark, Norway, and Iceland.

How to Get Around in Faroe Islands

The best way to explore Faroe Islands is by car, as it offers the flexibility to explore and take in the stunning landscapes at your own pace. There are also several bus companies offering service throughout the islands.

Where to Go Next from Faroe Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Faroe Islands is located in the heart of the North Atlantic, making it an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are some of the top options:

  1. Iceland: Discover the rich natural beauty of Iceland, located just a short flight away from Faroe Islands.
  2. Denmark: Explore the vibrant cities and rich culture of Denmark, located just a ferry or plane ride away.

Conclusion

Faroe Islands is a unique and beautiful destination, offering stunning natural landscapes, rich culture, and unique traditions. With this comprehensive guide, you now have all the information you need to plan your visit and make the most of your time in Faroe Islands.
