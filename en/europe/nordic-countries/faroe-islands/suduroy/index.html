Suðuroy Travel Guide - Explore the Best of Faroe Islands!

If you’re looking to escape from the city hustle and bustle and explore a quiet paradise, Suðuroy in Faroe Islands might just be the perfect destination for you! Known for its rugged landscapes, scenic views, and rich cultural heritage, Suðuroy offers everything from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and mouth-watering local cuisine. Here’s your ultimate guide to Suðuroy, complete with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip!

Introduction to Suðuroy

Suðuroy is the southernmost island in the Faroe Islands and is known for its unique natural beauty. With a population of only 4,500 people and an area of around 163 square kilometers, it’s a perfect destination for those looking to escape the crowd and immerse themselves in the region’s tranquil atmosphere. The island is accessible by ferry from the capital city, Tórshavn.

List of Places to Visit in Suðuroy

Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Suðuroy:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Suduroyar Fornminnissavn - This museum offers a glimpse into Suðuroy’s rich cultural heritage, with exhibits showcasing the island’s history, traditions, and folk art.

  2. Ørðavík - A small village known for its well-preserved traditional houses and scenic views.

  3. Hvannhagi - A historical site that offers a beautiful view of the island’s coastline.

Natural Attractions of Suðuroy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Beinisvørð - A stunning cliff that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding sea.

  2. Vágseiði - A popular spot for hiking, with scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  3. Suduroyarnakkur - A historic lighthouse with fabulous panoramic views of the sea, hills as well as the whole island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suðuroy

Suðuroy is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails that offer stunning views of the island’s rugged terrain. Some of the best hiking trails in Suðuroy include:

  1. Hiking trail to Hvannhagi - A moderate level hike that offers panoramic views of the island’s coast.

  2. Hiking trail to Kunoyarnakkur - A steep hike that offers panoramic views of the northern part of the island.

  3. Biking trail to Vágseiði - A scenic route that will take you through Suðuroy’s beautiful countryside.

Best Things to Do in Suðuroy: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Suðuroy:

  1. Boat tour to Beinisvørð - Take a boat tour to Beinisvørð and experience the stunning views of the towering cliffs from a different perspective.

  2. Fishing - Grab a fishing rod and enjoy some of the best fishing spots in the Faroe Islands.

  3. Bird watching - Suðuroy is home to several bird species, including puffins and razorbills, making it a perfect spot for birdwatching.

Food and Drink Scene of Suðuroy

No visit to Suðuroy is complete without trying the island’s delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Suðuroy:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Skerpikjøt - It is a traditional Faroese dried mutton delicacy and a must-try dish.

  2. Ræstur fiskur- Fermented fish of the Faroe islands is a strong seafood flavor that may not be for everyone.

  3. Grind og spik - Meatballs made of pilot whale meat and blubber. It’s not suitable for vegetarians.

Local Beverages

  1. Brennivín - a strong Icelandic liquor that is popular in the Faroe Islands too.

  2. Faroese beer - Gull and Mikkeller is a famous local beer brewed in the Faroe Islands.

Where to Stay in Suðuroy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Suðuroy:

  1. Hotel Tvøroyri - A modern hotel located in the heart of Tvøroyri with all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

  2. Suðuroy Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

  3. Pensionat - A bed and breakfast located in a converted 19th-century farmhouse, offering a taste of traditional Faroese hospitality.

What to Buy in Suðuroy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Suðuroy:

  1. Jumpers - Traditional Faroese woolen jumpers that are perfect for keeping warm on chilly days!

  2. Knitted hats and scarves - Hand-knitted hats and scarves made of the finest wool are also popular souvenirs in Suðuroy.

Local Culture and Customs of Suðuroy

The Faroese people are known for their unique cultural heritage, which is reflected in the island’s traditions, food, and events. Some of the customs to keep in mind while visiting Suðuroy include:

  1. Respect the environment - Faroese people have a strong connection with nature, and they respect it. Follow the trails and keep the place clean.

  2. Respect private property - Barsnæs is one of Suðuroy’s most scenic places, but it is also private land. Keep a good distance while admiring the view.

Best Time to Visit Suðuroy

The best time to visit Suðuroy is during the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is mild, and tourist attractions are easily accessible. The island can be a bit damp sometimes, so bring waterproof clothing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suðuroy

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Suðuroy:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions - Learn about the local customs and traditions and respect them.

  2. Take your trash with you - Faroese islands take their environmental conservation seriously, and it’s best to take your trash with you.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t intrude into private property - Private property is to be respected, and you should avoid intruding into it.

  2. Don’t touch the wildlife - The birds and animals in Suðuroy are to be admired from a distance, and it’s best not to touch or disturb them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suðuroy

Suðuroy is generally safe for tourists, but it’s best to keep these pointers in mind:

  1. Be cautious while hiking - The rugged terrain of Suðuroy can be challenging, so it’s best to be cautious while hiking.

  2. Stay on the marked trails - It’s easy to get lost on the island’s unmarked trails, so it’s best to stick to the marked ones.

Health in Suðuroy

Suðuroy has a good healthcare system, and visitors can avail of medical facilities if required. It’s best to carry your medication, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions.

Getting to Suðuroy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Suðuroy is accessible by ferry from Tórshavn, the capital city of the Faroe Islands. The ferries run regularly, and the trip takes around 2 hours.

Airports Near Suðuroy

The nearest airport to Suðuroy is Vágar Airport, which is located on the island of Vágar, around 50 kilometers from Tórshavn.

Bus Stations Near Suðuroy

Suðuroy island doesn’t have any bus stations; car rentals are available instead.

How to Get Around in Suðuroy

The best way to get around Suðuroy is by renting a car. Taxis are also available, but they are more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Suðuroy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions to explore around Suðuroy include:

  1. Visit Mykines - A beautiful island with pristine nature and stunning sea cliffs.

  2. Visit Tórshavn - Explore the capital city of the Faroe Islands and experience its unique culture and traditions.

Conclusion

Suðuroy is one of the most beautiful and unique destinations in the Faroe Islands, with its rugged terrain, panoramic views, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a quiet escape from the city, Suðuroy has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next vacation to this stunning island and create unforgettable memories!
