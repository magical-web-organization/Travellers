Introduction to Eidsvoll

Located in the scenic Oslo region of East Norway, Eidsvoll is a charming town that attracts visitors from all over the world. This town has a rich history, with many important cultural landmarks that are worth exploring. Additionally, Eidsvoll boasts an abundance of natural attractions, from hiking trails to picturesque views. Whether you’re a history buff or nature lover, Eidsvoll has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eidsvoll

Here are some must-visit places in Eidsvoll:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eidsvollbygningen: This historical building has served as a venue for many important events throughout Norway’s history, including the signing of Norway’s constitution in 1814. Today, it serves as a museum where visitors can learn about Norway’s past.
  • Eidsvollsbygningen: This historic home once belonged to Johan Caspar Herman Wedel Jarlsberg, a prominent figure in Norway’s history. Today, it is open to visitors as a museum where you can take a glimpse into the life of a Norwegian aristocrat.
  • Eidsvoll Church: This beautiful church dates back to 1785 and is known for its stunning stained glass windows and baroque architecture. It’s worth a visit for its calming ambience.
  • Sagstuen Farm: The Sagstuen Farm is a restored 18th-century farm that is open to visitors as a museum. Here, you can get a glimpse into what farming was like in Norway in centuries past.
  • Dal Gård: A historic farmhouse dating back to the early 19th century, Dal Gård is now a museum where visitors can explore life in the Norwegian countryside in centuries past.

Natural Attractions of Eidsvoll: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mjøsa: The largest lake in Norway, Mjøsa boasts stunning views and is a great place for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Eidsvollsmarka: This forested area offers a variety of hiking trails, and in the winter you can go skiing or snowshoeing there.
  • Vardefjell: This hill offers panoramic views of Eidsvoll and is a great place for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eidsvoll

Eidsvollsmarka is home to many hiking and biking trails. Here are some popular routes:

  • Ormlia: This is a popular 6.5 km hiking trail that offers beautiful views of the forested area.
  • Vardefjellet: This is a moderately challenging 5.5 km trail that takes you to the top of Vardefjell where you can enjoy stunning views of Eidsvoll.

Best Things to Do in Eidsvoll: Activities and Tours

  • Take a tour of Eidsvollsbygningen: This historic building is a must-visit for anyone interested in Norwegian history.
  • Explore Mjøsa: Spend a day boating, fishing, or swimming in Norway’s largest lake.
  • Go hiking in Eidsvollsmarka: Take a scenic hike through the forested area of Eidsvollsmarka, and enjoy breathtaking scenic views.
  • Visit the Sagstuen Farm: This restored 18th-century farm is now a museum that offers a glimpse into what farming was like in Norway in centuries past.

Food and Drink Scene of Eidsvoll

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål: This lamb and cabbage dish is a Norwegian favorite and is known for its hearty flavor.
  • Koldtbord: This buffet-style meal is a common sight at Norwegian celebrations, featuring a variety of cold dishes like smoked salmon and cured meats.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Scandinavian spirit, aquavit is typically served as a digestif after a meal.
  • Aass Brewery: Founded in 1834, Aass Brewery is Norway’s oldest brewery and offers a range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Eidsvoll: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Eidsvoll Gjestgiveri: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Eidsvoll and offers comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Sundvolden Hotel: This hotel is situated on the banks of Lake Tyrifjorden and boasts stunning scenic views.
  • Comfort Hotel RunWay: This modern hotel is located near the Oslo airport and is a great choice for those with early morning flights.

What to Buy in Eidsvoll: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Norwegian sweaters: These warm and cozy sweaters are perfect for chilly Norwegian nights.
  • Handmade pottery: Eidsvoll is home to many talented potters, and their pieces make great souvenirs.
  • Woodcarvings: Norwegian woodcarvings are known for their intricate designs and make great decorations for your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Eidsvoll

Norwegians place a strong emphasis on family and community, and are known for their egalitarian values. Visiting someone’s home is often a more intimate affair than it may be in some other cultures, and punctuality is taken seriously.

Best Time to Visit Eidsvoll

The best time to visit Eidsvoll is during the summer months, from May to September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eidsvoll

Do:

  • Take the time to explore Eidsvoll’s history.
  • Venture out into nature and experience the beautiful scenery.
  • Bring a sweater, as the weather can be chilly even in the summer.

Don’t:

  • Assume that Norwegians are reserved or unfriendly- they are often quite warm and welcoming once they get to know you.
  • Forget to be respectful of your hosts if you are invited into someone’s home.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eidsvoll

Eidsvoll is generally a very safe place to travel to, but visitors should still exercise caution, particularly when traveling alone at night.

Health in Eidsvoll

Norway has a very high standard of healthcare, so visitors should have no trouble finding quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Eidsvoll: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eidsvoll

The closest major airport to Eidsvoll is Oslo Airport (OSL), which is located approximately 20 kilometers south of Eidsvoll.

Bus Stations Near Eidsvoll

Eidsvoll is served by several local bus companies, including Ruter and Timekspressen.

Other Options

Eidsvoll is also accessible by train from Oslo.

How to Get Around in Eidsvoll

The best way to get around Eidsvoll is by car, but there are also local buses that run throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Eidsvoll: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oslo: Norway’s capital city is located less than an hour’s drive from Eidsvoll and is definitely worth a visit.
  • Hamar: This small town is located on the shores of Lake Mjøsa and boasts several interesting museums and cultural landmarks.
  • Lillehammer: This picturesque town is located approximately one hour’s drive from Eidsvoll and is surrounded by beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Eidsvoll may be a small town, but it has so much to offer visitors. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and welcoming culture, it’s no wonder why Eidsvoll is a popular destination among tourists to Norway. Hopefully, this guide has given you some ideas for what to do and see while you’re there!
