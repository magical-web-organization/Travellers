Introduction to West

West is located in the Oslo region of East Norway, in the heart of the Nordic countries in Europe. Known for its stunning natural beauty, fascinating cultural attractions and exciting outdoor adventures, West is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Norway.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take a closer look at the top things to do, see and eat in West, as well as provide helpful tips and advice on where to stay and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in West

Whether you’re an art lover or a thrill-seeker, West has something for everyone. Here’s a list of some of the top attractions in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Viking Ship Museum: Home to some of the world’s best-preserved Viking ships, this museum is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Kon-Tiki Museum: Explore the fascinating story of Thor Heyerdahl’s journey across the Pacific on a balsa wood raft.
  • Holmenkollen Ski Museum and Tower: Learn about the history of skiing in Norway and take a thrilling ride on the ski jump simulator.
  • Oslo City Museum: Discover the history of Oslo and its people through a variety of engaging exhibits.

Natural Attractions of West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nordmarka Forest: Hike or bike through this vast forest just outside of Oslo and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Oslo Fjord: Take a boat tour of this beautiful fjord and see the city from a different perspective.
  • Sognsvann Lake: Enjoy a relaxing picnic or swim in this picturesque lake surrounded by lush green forest.
  • Bygdøy Peninsula: Explore the beautiful beaches and historic landmarks on this scenic peninsula just outside of Oslo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West

West is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Kolsåstoppen: This challenging hike offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Vestmarka: This popular hiking and biking area offers a variety of trails for all skill levels.
  • Hvervenbukta to Skullerud: This scenic biking route takes you through some of the most beautiful areas of West.

Best Things to Do in West: Activities and Tours

  • Oslo Opera House Tour: Take a guided tour of this iconic building and learn about its fascinating architecture and history.
  • Oslo Pass: Get access to over 30 museums and attractions with this convenient and money-saving pass.
  • Fjord Cruise: Take a relaxing boat tour of Oslo Fjord and see the city from a different perspective.
  • Skiing: Hit the slopes at some of Norway’s best ski resorts, including Tryvann and Holmenkollen.

Week-Long Itinerary for West

If you have a week to spend in West, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore the Viking Ship Museum and Kon-Tiki Museum in Oslo Day 2: Take a boat tour of Oslo Fjord and visit the Akershus Fortress Day 3: Head to Nordmarka Forest for a day of hiking or biking Day 4: Visit the Oslo Opera House and take a tour of the building Day 5: Spend the day skiing at Tryvann or Holmenkollen Day 6: Take the Oslo Pass and visit a variety of museums and attractions Day 7: Relax on the beaches of Bygdøy Peninsula

Food and Drink Scene of West

No trip to West would be complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål: Norway’s national dish of lamb and cabbage
  • Gravlaks: Cured salmon served with mustard sauce
  • Kjøttkaker: Meatballs made with a blend of beef and pork

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: Norway’s signature spirit made with herbs and spices
  • Karsk: A traditional hot drink made with coffee and moonshine
  • Norwegian beer: Try local favorites like Ringnes, Mack and Aass

Where to Stay in West: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Continental Oslo: A luxury hotel in the heart of the city, with stunning views and top-notch amenities.
  • Anker Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel with clean and comfortable rooms, located close to public transportation.
  • Frogner House Apartments: Spacious and modern apartments with fully equipped kitchens, perfect for longer stays.

What to Buy in West: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to bring home a piece of West? Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to look for:

  • Handcrafted Norwegian sweaters and wool products
  • Local cheeses, such as brunost and gudbrandsdalsost
  • Hand-painted Norwegian wooden clogs

Local Culture and Customs of West

Norwegian culture is rich in tradition and history. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting West:

  • Norwegians are known for their love of nature and outdoor activities, so be sure to take advantage of the many opportunities to explore the great outdoors.
  • Tipping is not expected in Norway, as service charges are typically included in the bill.
  • Norwegians tend to be reserved and may not always engage in small talk, but they are friendly and helpful if you need assistance.

Best Time to Visit West

The best time to visit West depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities, the summer months of June through August offer long daylight hours and mild temperatures. If you’re interested in winter sports like skiing, the months of December through March offer the best conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of West

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting West:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many opportunities for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking and skiing.
  • Try the local cuisine, including Norway’s famous seafood and traditional dishes like fårikål and kjøttkaker.
  • Be prepared for changeable weather conditions, and pack accordingly.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English, as some locals may not be fluent.
  • Expect to find extensive nightlife options in West, as the area is more focused on outdoor activities and cultural attractions.
  • Forget to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home, as this is a common Norwegian custom.

Safety and Health

West is a safe and healthy destination, with a high standard of medical care and low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic precautions:

Safety in West

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Always wear a helmet when biking, and take care when hiking or skiing in remote areas.
  • Be prepared for changeable weather conditions, and be aware of the risk of hypothermia.

Health in West

  • Norway has a high standard of medical care, and emergency services are available throughout the country.
  • No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Norway, but it’s always a good idea to ensure your routine vaccinations are up to date.
  • Tap water is safe to drink throughout Norway.

Getting to West: Transportation Options and Logistics

West is easily accessible from Oslo and the surrounding areas. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near West

  • Oslo Airport Gardermoen: The largest airport in Norway, with many international flights.
  • Sandefjord Airport Torp: A smaller airport with several low-cost airlines.

Bus Stations Near West

  • Oslo Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Oslo, with connections to destinations throughout Norway.

Other Options

  • Train: The Norwegian State Railways offers frequent train service between Oslo and other destinations in Norway.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the surrounding area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in West

West is easy to get around, with a comprehensive public transportation system and many options for biking and walking. Here are some ways to get around:

  • Oslo Metro: The metro system in Oslo is clean, efficient and easy to use.
  • Bus: Bus service in West is frequent and reliable, with many routes connecting to Oslo and other nearby destinations.
  • Bike: West is a bike-friendly area, with many dedicated bike lanes and trails throughout the region.

Where to Go Next from West: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore the surrounding areas, there are many exciting destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few options:

  • Bergen: Known as the “Gateway to the Fjords,” Bergen is a charming city with plenty of history and culture to explore.
  • Drammen: A picturesque town with beautiful parks and gardens, just an hour’s drive from Oslo.
  • Lillehammer: Host of the 1994 Winter Olympics, Lillehammer is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Conclusion

West is a remarkable destination that offers a wealth of cultural attractions, stunning natural beauty and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in history, food, or outdoor activities, West has something to offer everyone.
