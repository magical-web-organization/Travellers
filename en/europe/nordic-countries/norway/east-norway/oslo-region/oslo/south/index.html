This comprehensive travel guide to South, Oslo has everything you need to plan your perfect trip, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South, Oslo, offers a diverse range of culinary experiences, from local delicacies to international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fårikål - lamb stew with cabbage and whole black pepper
  2. Raspeballer - potato dumplings served with bacon and lingonberry jam
  3. Gravlaks - cured salmon
  4. Rakfisk - fermented fish
  5. Kanelboller - cinnamon rolls

Local Beverages

  1. Aquavit - distilled spirit flavored with caraway, dill, and other herbs
  2. Aass beer - traditional Norwegian beer
  3. Glogg - mulled wine

Local Culture and Customs of South

The people of South, Oslo, value their cultural heritage and traditions. Some of the customs and traditions include:

  • Respect for nature and the environment
  • Family-focused lifestyle
  • Christmas celebrations
  • Skiing and other winter sports

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the days are long. Winter months from November to March are also suitable for winter sports activities like skiing, snowboarding, and ice skating.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

Oslo Airport, Gardermoen, is the main airport in South, Oslo. There are regular flights from most major international destinations.

Bus Stations Near South

The Central Bus Station in Oslo is the nearest bus station to South. There are buses that run from major cities in Norway to Oslo.

Other Options

Travellers can also opt for trains that run from Oslo to South, or they can drive and park in one of the designated parking areas.

How to Get Around in South
