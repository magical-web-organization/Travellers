Introduction to Hamar

Nestled in Hedmark, East Norway, Hamar is a picturesque destination that boasts scenic views and a rich cultural heritage. The city is famous for its ancient buildings, museums, and outdoor activities. This travel guide will help you explore the best places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

When in Hamar, there’s no shortage of exciting places to visit. Here is a list of the top attractions that should be on your must-see list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hedmarksmuseet
  • Kirsten Flagstad Museum
  • Vikingskipet Olympic Arena
  • Domkirkeodden
  • Hegefjellshotellet

Natural Attractions of Hamar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Best Things to Do in Hamar: Activities and Tours

Hamar caters to a wide range of interests, and here are some of the best activities and tours that you can experience.

  • Visit the Kirsten Flagstad Museum
  • Explore the city’s rich history at the Hedmarksmuseet
  • Try your hand at skiing or snowboarding at Kvitfjell Ski Resort
  • Check out the Hamar Waterpark
  • Experience the Vikingskipet Olympic Arena
  • Go on a cycling tour with Hamar Cykleklubb
  • Take a scenic walk around Lake Mjøsa
  • Listen to live music at Hegefjellshotellet

Food and Drink Scene of Hamar

Hamar’s food and drink scene is diverse and exciting. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål - boiled lamb and cabbage stew
  • Tjukkmjølk - thick buttermilk porridge
  • Rømmegrøt - sour cream porridge with sugar and cinnamon
  • Pølse med lompe - a hot dog wrapped in a potato pancake

Local Beverages

  • Brennevin - traditional Norwegian liquor
  • Mjød - a sweet, honey-based drink
  • Nøgne Ø - a famous local beer
  • Aquavit - a popular Norwegian spirit

Local Culture and Customs of Hamar

Hamar, like most Norwegian cities, has a rich and distinctive culture, and here are some customs you may encounter:

  • Saying “takk for maten” after a meal is customary
  • Respecting personal space is important in public
  • Drinking alcohol in public is strictly prohibited
  • Punctuality is highly valued.

Best Time to Visit Hamar

The best time to visit Hamar is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in the countryside.

Do's and Don'ts of Hamar

Here are some things you should and shouldn’t do when in Hamar:

Do

  • Try traditional Norwegian dishes
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try skiing, biking, and other outdoor activities
  • Enjoy the scenic views

Don't

  • Drink alcohol in public places
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Be excessively loud or disruptive in public
  • Be late for appointments or dates

Safety and Health

Safety in Hamar

Hamar is a relatively safe place, but it’s always good to exercise caution when going out at night and when in crowded areas.

Health in Hamar

Norway has a very high standard of healthcare, and there are a few medical centers and hospitals in Hamar.
