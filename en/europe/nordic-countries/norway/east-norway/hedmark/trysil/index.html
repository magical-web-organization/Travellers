Your Ultimate Guide to Trysil, Norway

Nestled in the heart of Hedmark, East Norway, Trysil is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its ski slopes in winter, Trysil offers visitors an array of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary delights year-round. From biking and hiking trails to museums and historical landmarks, there is something for everyone in Trysil.

List of Places to Visit in Trysil

Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or a cultural connoisseur, Trysil has it all. Here’s a list of must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Trysil Bygdetun (Trysil Village Museum) – Explore the history and culture of Trysil through interactive displays and traditional buildings.

  2. Eidskog Museum – A scenic museum featuring local history, Viking artifacts, and exhibitions on rural life in Norway.

  3. Engerdal Museum and Cultural Centre – Learn about the Sami culture, traditional farming, and forestry techniques at this museum.

Natural Attractions of Trysil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Trysilfjellet – Ski, snowboard, or take snowmobile rides on the slopes of Norway’s largest ski resort.

  2. Trysil Hestesenter – Experience the beauty of Trysil on horseback.

  3. Lake Femunden – Take a boat ride or hike to enjoy the stunning views and wildlife spotting opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trysil

Trysil boasts over 1,000 kilometers of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Trysilfjellet Rundt – A popular 12 km cycling route around the mountain area that offers stunning views.

  2. Fjellrunden Trysil – A moderately challenging 11.5 km hiking trail that takes you through the mountain valley for breathtaking views.

  3. Femundsskjæringa – A scenic route that runs along Lake Femunden and ends up at the border with Sweden.

Best Things to Do in Trysil: Activities and Tours

  1. Canoe trip on the Glomma River – Experience the natural beauty of Trysil on a guided canoe trip.

  2. Go fishing – Trysil has excellent fishing opportunities in Lake Trysil and River Trysil.

  3. Trysil Bike Arena – Rent a bike and explore the forest trails and terrain parks designed for tearing up the mountain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trysil

Looking for the perfect week-long itinerary for Trysil? Here’s a sample that includes all the must-do activities:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Trysil, check into your accommodation, and explore the village.
Day 2 Hike Trysilfjellet or bike the Fjellrunden Trysil trail. Stop for lunch at a traditional Norwegian restaurant.
Day 3 Experience Lake Femunden with a boat tour or hike around the surrounding area of the lake.
Day 4 Take a guided canoe trip on the Glomma River and enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.
Day 5 Visit Trysil Village Museum, Eidskog Museum, or Engerdal Museum and Cultural Centre.
Day 6 Spend the day skiing or snowboarding at Trysilfjellet.
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and local products in Trysil, then depart.

Food and Drink Scene of Trysil

When it comes to food and drinks, Trysil has plenty of options for visitors to indulge in the local cuisine:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rakfisk – traditional Norwegian fish ferment dish served with flatbread and sour cream.

  2. Sheep’s head or fårikål – lamb stew with cabbage, a popular winter dish.

  3. Lutefisk – dried and salted cod fish soaked in lye, then boiled or baked and served with potatoes and bacon.

Local Beverages

  1. Akevitt – a Norwegian distilled spirit, similar to schnapps, typically drunk with fish.

  2. Beer – a range of local beers are available, including Ølakademiet, Macksøl, and Nøgne Ø.

Where to Stay in Trysil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trysil has a wide range of accommodation options, from hotels to guesthouses, and cabins. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Radisson Blu Mountain Resort & Residences – A luxurious hotel with spa facilities, restaurants, and a perfect location for winter sport enthusiasts.

  2. Trysil Hyttegrend – A popular option for families, these charming cabins are located near the ski slopes.

  3. Trysil Hostel – A budget-friendly option with shared amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Trysil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home a piece of Trysil, here are some recommendations for souvenirs:

  1. Traditional Sami jewelry – made with materials such as reindeer antlers, silver, and pewter.

  2. Knives – Trysil is known for knife making, and you can find unique designs for all purposes throughout the village.

  3. Local foods – take home some Rakfisk, fårikål or traditional berry jam from the Trysil region.

Local Culture and Customs of Trysil

Trysil has a rich cultural heritage, including the Sami people and their traditional customs. Visitors should be mindful of local customs and dress appropriately, especially when visiting museums, churches, or other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Trysil

The best time to visit Trysil depends on your preference and interests. If you’re a winter sports enthusiast, ski season starts from late November to April. In the summer, Trysil is perfect for outdoor adventure activities, as the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trysil

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions

  2. Dress appropriately for the weather and activities you’re participating in

  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave trash behind

  2. Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities

  3. Be disrespectful to locals or other travelers.

Safety and Health

Whether you’re hiking in the mountains or skiing, it is always essential to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

Safety in Trysil

  1. Always follow the safety guidelines and instructions provided by activity operators.

  2. Check the weather conditions and be prepared for any changes.

  3. Carry a first aid kit and a mobile phone.

Health in Trysil

  1. Norway has a public health system, but visitors should purchase travel insurance before traveling.

  2. Make sure you have any necessary prescriptions with you.

  3. Check and follow the Covid-19 guidelines and restrictions for traveling to Norway.

Getting to Trysil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travelling to Trysil is easy, with a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Trysil

The closest airport to Trysil is Oslo Gardermoen Airport, which is about 170 km away. From the airport, you can take a shuttle bus or a private taxi to Trysil.

Bus Stations Near Trysil

Several bus companies offer services to Trysil from Oslo, Lillehammer, and other major towns/cities in Norway.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, it takes approximately two to three hours to reach Trysil from Oslo or Lillehammer.

How to Get Around in Trysil

Trysil has a good transportation system, including buses and taxis. Hiring a car is also another option for those who want more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Trysil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trysil is surrounded by other charming mountain towns and villages that are worth a visit, including:

  1. Lillehammer – Home to the 1994 Winter Olympics, Lillehammer is a beautiful town with an array of cultural attractions, including the Maihaugen Museum and Lillehammer Art Museum.

  2. Røros – A well-preserved mining town and a Unesco World Heritage site with wooden houses and copper mines. This town is perfect to explore Norwegian folk music and artisanal crafts.

Conclusion

Trysil is truly a year-round destination with endless opportunities for outdoor activities, cultural exploration, and culinary indulgence. Plan your perfect trip to Trysil with this comprehensive guide, and get ready to enjoy the natural beauty, local culture, and friendly people that Norway is known for.
