Introduction to Stokke

Stokke is a small town located in the Vestfold region of Eastern Norway. It has a population of approximately 12,000 people and is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, rich cultural history, and friendly locals. Stokke is a popular destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in Norway’s natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply relaxing in a peaceful setting, Stokke has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Stokke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stokke is home to several museums and historical sites that provide insight into the town’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Vestfossen Kunstlaboratorium: This contemporary art museum is located in a former paper mill and showcases exhibitions from leading artists from around the world.
  • Stokke Bygdetun: This open-air museum features traditional Norwegian buildings from the 17th and 18th centuries and provides visitors with a glimpse into the country’s rural history
  • Midgard Historical Centre: The Midgard Historical Centre explores the Viking Age and the influence of the Vikings on Norwegian culture.

Natural Attractions of Stokke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main draws of Stokke is its stunning natural beauty. There are plenty of outdoor adventures to be had, including:

  • The Coastal Path: This 22 km hiking trail takes visitors along the coastline of Stokke and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding fjords and landscapes.
  • The Tønsberg-Notodden Canal: This canal is one of the most unique attractions in Stokke and is perfect for a scenic boat tour or kayaking trip.
  • Bøkeskogen Park: This beautiful park features walking trails, picnic areas, and a stunning arboretum. It’s a great place to relax and take in the natural beauty of Stokke.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stokke

For outdoor enthusiasts, Stokke has some of the best hiking and biking trails in Norway. Some of the top routes include:

  • Tønsberg - Hof - Vasser - Stokke: This 42 km cycling route takes riders past lush farmland, beautiful forests, and charming villages.
  • Langestrand Tjølling - Sandefjord: This scenic hiking trail takes visitors through the stunning countryside of Stokke and Sandefjord.
  • Stokke - Ulvik - Melsomvik: This 14 km hiking trail takes visitors through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Stokke and offers stunning views of the surrounding fjords.

Best Things to Do in Stokke: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or with friends, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to keep you busy in Stokke. Some of the must-try experiences include:

  • Kayaking: Take a guided kayak tour of the breathtaking Tønsbergfjord and explore the rugged coastline of Stokke.
  • Horseback riding: Go for a scenic horseback ride through the picturesque countryside of Stokke.
  • Hunting and fishing: Stokke is known for its world-class hunting and fishing opportunities, and there are plenty of guides and outfitters who can help you get started.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stokke

If you’re planning to spend a week in Stokke, there are plenty of things to see and do. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the cultural attractions of Stokke, including Vestfossen Kunstlaboratorium and Stokke Bygdetun.
  • Day 3: Take a scenic boat tour of the Tønsberg-Notodden Canal and enjoy the stunning scenery along the way.
  • Day 4-5: Hike or bike along the Coastal Path and take in the breathtaking views of the fjords and landscapes.
  • Day 6-7: Go hunting or fishing in the area, or simply relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Stokke.

Food and Drink Scene of Stokke

Must-Try Dishes

Norwegian cuisine is known for its fresh ingredients and simple yet delicious flavors. Some of the top dishes to try in Stokke include:

  • Gravlaks: This traditional Norwegian dish consists of raw salmon that has been cured in salt, sugar, and dill.
  • Fiskesuppe: This hearty fish soup is made with a variety of local fish and seafood and is a staple of Norwegian cuisine.
  • Krumkake: This delicate, wafer-thin cookie is a traditional Christmas dessert in Norway.

Local Beverages

Norway is also known for its craft beer and spirits. Some of the top beverages to try in Stokke include:

  • E.C. Dahls: This historic brewery in Trondheim is known for its delicious craft beers and offers tours and tastings.
  • Aquavit: This traditional Scandinavian spirit is made from potatoes and herbs and has a distinct anise flavor.
  • Mjød: This sweet, honey-based wine is one of the oldest alcoholic beverages in the world and has been enjoyed in Norway for centuries.

Where to Stay in Stokke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stokke has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Some of the top options include:

  • Walaker Hotell: This charming hotel is located in a historic building and features comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Norwegian cuisine.
  • Stokke Apartment: This spacious and modern apartment is perfect for families or groups and is located close to the town center.
  • Hallevik Camping: This family-friendly campsite is located on the shores of the Tønsbergfjord and offers a range of cabins and campsites.

What to Buy in Stokke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Stokke, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Some of the top options include:

  • Handmade Norwegian sweaters: Stokke is known for its traditional knitwear, and there are several shops in town where you can purchase handmade sweaters and accessories.
  • Local fish and seafood: The town’s location along the fjords means that it has access to some of the freshest and most delicious seafood in the world.
  • Handicrafts: Stokke has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and there are several shops where you can purchase handmade pottery, glassware, and other items.

Local Culture and Customs of Stokke

Norwegian culture is known for its emphasis on simplicity, honesty, and environmentalism. Visitors should be respectful of these values and try to learn about the country’s cultural heritage. Some other customs and norms to be aware of include:

  • Tipping: Tipping is not required in Norway, as service charges are usually included in the bill.
  • Dress code: Norway has a casual dress code, so visitors should feel comfortable wearing comfortable, practical clothing.
  • Personal space: Norwegians value their personal space, so visitors should be mindful of this when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Stokke

The best time to visit Stokke depends on your preferences and interests. For those looking to enjoy outdoor adventures and activities, the summer months of June through August are the best choice. For those interested in cultural attractions and museums, the winter months of November through February offer fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stokke

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and be mindful of your impact on the landscape.
  • Try local cuisine, including fresh seafood and traditional dishes.
  • Learn about the country’s cultural heritage and customs.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English, although most Norwegians do speak English fluently, learning a few Norwegian phrases can go a long way.
  • Be loud and obnoxious in public places.
  • Underdress for the weather - it can be chilly even during the summer months, so pack appropriately.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stokke

Stokke is a safe and welcoming town for visitors. However, visitors should take basic precautions to stay safe, such as staying aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Stokke

Norway has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect excellent medical care if needed. It is recommended to purchase travel insurance before your trip, as medical costs in Norway can be expensive.

Getting to Stokke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stokke

The closest airport to Stokke is Sandefjord Airport, Torp. It’s located approximately 20 km from Stokke and offers flights to several European destinations, including London and Amsterdam.

Bus Stations Near Stokke

There are several bus stations located near Stokke, including:

  • Tønsberg bus station
  • Sandefjord bus station
  • Larvik bus station

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Stokke by train or car, with several train stations and major highways located within a short drive of the town.

How to Get Around in Stokke

Stokke is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors who wish to explore the surrounding countryside can rent a bike or car or take public transportation, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Stokke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stokke is located in the heart of Vestfold, which means that visitors have access to several nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the top options include:

  • Larvik: This lively town is located approximately 15 km from Stokke and offers a range of cultural attractions and outdoor activities.
  • Sandefjord: This vibrant coastal town is located a short drive from Stokke and is known for its seafood, historic architecture, and beautiful scenery.
