Introduction to Aremark

Nestled in the heart of Østfold, Aremark is a hidden gem that is often missed by travelers heading to bigger cities like Oslo and Bergen. This small town is known for its serene lakes, winding hiking trails, and rich cultural heritage. With plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy and numerous historical sites and museums to explore, Aremark is the perfect destination for those looking to get off the beaten track and experience the authentic beauty of Norway’s countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Aremark

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aremark Historical Society Museum

The Aremark Historical Society Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. Located in the center of town, this small museum offers a glimpse into the past of Aremark and its surrounding areas. Exhibits include clothing and household items from the 18th and 19th centuries, as well as old photographs and documents.

Marker Church

The Marker Church, built in 1880, is one of the most beautiful churches in Northeaster Norway. The church features beautiful stained glass windows and intricate wood carvings, and is also home to the Marker Church Museum. The museum showcases artifacts from the church’s history, including old religious texts and altar decorations.

Natural Attractions of Aremark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Øymarksjøen

Lake Øymarksjøen is the biggest and most popular tourist attraction of Aremark. The lake is known for its crystal-clear waters, which are perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating. There are several picnic areas located around the lake, as well as hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Høysandfjellet

Høysandfjellet is the highest mountain in Aremark, standing 551 meters above sea level. There are several hiking trails that lead to the summit, offering panoramic views of the surrounding forests and lakes. At the top of the mountain, there is a viewing tower where visitors can take in the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aremark

Aremark is a hiker’s paradise, with trails that lead through dense forests, along glistening lakeshores, and up majestic mountains. One of the most popular hiking trails in Aremark is the Lake Øymarksjøen trail, which leads hikers around the beautiful lake and through the surrounding forests. Other popular hiking trails include the Høysandfjellet trail and the Skjerketjønn trail.

For biking enthusiasts, Aremark has several scenic routes that take riders through some of the most beautiful parts of the countryside. The Bjørkebekk route is a popular biking trail that passes through rolling hills and picturesque farmland.

Best Things to Do in Aremark: Activities and Tours

Kayaking on Lake Øymarksjøen

The crystal-clear waters of Lake Øymarksjøen are perfect for kayaking, and there are several rental shops located in town where visitors can rent kayaks for the day. Paddling around the lake is a relaxing and peaceful way to take in the surrounding scenery.

Marker Canal Boat Tour

Take a boat tour down Marker Canal, which was once used to transport timber from the forests to the nearby sawmill. The tour offers a unique way to learn about the history of Aremark, as well as the surrounding area.

Food and Drink Scene of Aremark

Must-Try Dishes

Rakfisk

Rakfisk is a traditional dish that is popular in Aremark. It is made from fermented fish, which is served with boiled potatoes and sour cream. The dish has a strong flavor and is an acquired taste, but it is definitely worth trying.

Rømmegrøt

Rømmegrøt is a creamy porridge made from sour cream, milk, and flour. It is usually served with sugar and cinnamon on top, and is a classic Norwegian comfort food.

Local Beverages

Aremark Øl

Aremark Øl is a locally brewed beer that is made from pure spring water and locally sourced barley. The beer has a crisp, refreshing taste and is the perfect drink to enjoy on a warm summer day.

Trollheimen Cider

Trollheimen Cider is a delicious apple cider that is made from locally sourced apples. The cider is crisp and refreshing, and pairs perfectly with a plate of Norwegian cheese and crackers.

Where to Stay in Aremark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aremark Camping

Aremark Camping is a beautiful campground located on the shores of Lake Øymarksjøen. The campsite offers tent and RV sites, as well as cabins and a hostel. The amenities include a playground, a picnic area, and a dock for boating and fishing.

Berg Gård

Berg Gård is a traditional farmstead that has been converted into charming guesthouses. The guesthouses are beautifully decorated and offer all the modern amenities, while still retaining their rustic charm.

What to Buy in Aremark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aremark is known for its locally made products and crafts. Some of the best souvenirs to bring home include handmade wool sweaters, artisanal cheeses, and locally made jams and preserves.

Local Culture and Customs of Aremark

Aremark is a small town, and the locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. The town has a rich cultural heritage, and traditional Norwegian customs are still practiced here.

Best Time to Visit Aremark

The best time to visit Aremark is during the summer months (June-September), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, Aremark is beautiful year-round, and each season offers its own unique charm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aremark

Aremark is a small and safe town, and there are generally no major safety concerns for visitors. However, visitors should take caution when hiking or participating in outdoor activities, and always follow the safety guidelines.

Health in Aremark

There are no major health concerns in Aremark. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate medical insurance before visiting, and should always carry any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Aremark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aremark

The nearest airports to Aremark are Gardermoen Airport (OSL) in Oslo and Moss Airport (RYG) in Rygge.

Bus Stations Near Aremark

There are several bus stations located near Aremark, including Fredrikstad, Sarpsborg, and Oslo.

Other Options

There are also several car rental companies located in Oslo, which provide convenient transportation options for visitors.

How to Get Around in Aremark

The best way to get around in Aremark is by car. Rentals are available at most major airports and in larger towns, and driving in Norway is safe and easy.

Where to Go Next from Aremark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that are worth a visit from Aremark, including the cities of Oslo and Fredrikstad. Visitors can also head to the nearby Halden Canal and explore the surrounding countryside.
