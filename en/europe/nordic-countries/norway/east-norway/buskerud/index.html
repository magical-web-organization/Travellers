Comprehensive Tour Guide for Buskerud

Buskerud is an idyllic county located in East Norway, in the Nordic countries of Europe. It is surrounded by stunning mountains, lush green forests, and picturesque lakes, making it a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, things to do, cultural attractions, where to stay, when to visit, safety, and more.

Introduction to Buskerud

Buskerud is the perfect destination for those who want to enjoy the beauty of Norway’s stunning landscapes. The county is known for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant cultural scene, and rich history. Buskerud is home to several museums, art galleries, and historical sites that highlight the region’s rich culture and heritage. Furthermore, Buskerud is known for its adventure activities, such as skiing, hiking, and fishing.

List of Places to Visit in Buskerud

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kongsberg Silver Mines: The Kongsberg Silver Mines are a UNESCO World Heritage site and Norway’s largest historic mining area. Visitors can learn about the history of silver mining in Norway and explore the mines.

  • Hallingdal Museum: The Hallingdal Museum is a regional museum and outdoor museum that highlights the history and culture of Hallingdal Valley.

  • Blaafarveværket: Blaafarveværket is an old mining corporation that is now converted into a gallery of art and crafts.

Natural Attractions of Buskerud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hardangervidda National Park: Hardangervidda National Park is the largest national park in Norway and a popular summer hiking destination with a vibrant ecosystem.

  • Ringeriksbadet: Ringeriksbadet is a water park that offers a range of aquatic activities, such as water slides, diving boards, and saunas.

  • Hemsedal Ski Resort: Hemsedal Ski Resort is the perfect destination for those who want to enjoy winter sports, like skiing and snowboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buskerud

Buskerud is known for its stunning scenery and a wide range of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the great outdoors:

  • Rallarvegen: Rallarvegen is a popular bike trail that runs between Haugastøl and Flåm.

  • Krokkleiva: Krokkleiva is a scenic hiking trail that takes visitors to the top of a mountain and offers stunning views of the valley below.

  • Hallingskarvet National Park: Hallingskarvet National Park is a popular hiking destination that offers trails that range from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Buskerud: Activities and Tours

Buskerud has a range of activities and tours that cater to different types of travelers:

  • Husky sledging: Visitors can experience a dog sled tour to explore the beautiful landscapes of Buskerud.

  • River rafting: River rafting is a popular activity in Buskerud, and visitors can enjoy rafting down the Numedalslågen river.

  • Traditional farm visits: Visitors can experience the traditional farm life of Buskerud and enjoy the local foods and hospitality.

Week-Long Itinerary for Buskerud

Day 1: Explore Kongsberg Silver Mines and learn about the history of silver mining in Norway.

Day 2: Hike in Hardangervidda National Park and immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Norway.

Day 3: Visit Blaafarveværket and experience the art and craft culture of Buskerud.

Day 4: Take a dog sled tour and explore the stunning landscapes of Buskerud.

Day 5: Enjoy river rafting on the Numedalslågen river.

Day 6: Experience traditional farm life and local foods by visiting a local farm in Buskerud.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the serene views of Buskerud from your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Buskerud

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål: Fårikål is a traditional lamb stew that is popular in Buskerud.

  • Pinnekjøtt: Pinnekjøtt is a traditional Norwegian Christmas dish made from lamb ribs.

  • Kransekake: Kransekake is a traditional Norwegian cake that is made during festive occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Akevitt: Akevitt is Norway’s national spirit and a popular alcoholic beverage.

  • Roberg Cider: Roberg Cider is a locally produced cider made from apples grown in Buskerud.

Where to Stay in Buskerud: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buskerud offers several options for accommodation that cater to different budgets and preferences:

  • Gaustablikk Høyfjellshotell: Gaustablikk Høyfjellshotell is a luxury hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Vatnahalsen Høyfjellshotell: Vatnahalsen Høyfjellshotell is a traditional Norwegian mountain hotel that offers easy access to the hiking trails of Hardangervidda National Park.

  • Hønefoss Motel: Hønefoss Motel is an affordable option for travelers who want to explore Buskerud without breaking the bank.

What to Buy in Buskerud: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sweaters and woolen products: Buskerud is known for its high-quality wool and knitted products, such as sweaters, scarves, and gloves.

  • Handmade crafts: Buskerud has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and visitors can find unique handmade products, like pottery, glass, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Buskerud

The local culture of Buskerud is heavily influenced by its natural surroundings, history, and traditions. The people of Buskerud are known for their hospitality, and visitors can expect to be welcomed with open arms. However, it is important to respect the local customs and traditions when visiting Buskerud.

Best Time to Visit Buskerud

The best time to visit Buskerud is between June and August, which is the peak tourist season in Norway. During this time, visitors can enjoy the stunning natural scenery, warm weather, and a range of outdoor activities. However, Buskerud is also a great destination during the winter months, when visitors can enjoy winter sports and snow-based activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buskerud

Do:

  • Do try traditional Norwegian food.

  • Do explore the scenic biking and hiking trails.

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural areas.

  • Don’t ignore the safety rules while enjoying adventure sports.

  • Don’t forget to purchase travel insurance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buskerud

Buskerud is a very safe place to visit, but visitors should still exercise caution. It is important to follow the safety rules while enjoying adventure sports, like skiing, hiking, and river rafting. Additionally, travelers should be aware of the potential hazards in the natural areas, like slippery rocks and fast-moving rivers.

Health in Buskerud

Norway has a high standard of health care, and Buskerud is no exception. Visitors can find several medical facilities and hospitals throughout the county. However, it is important to purchase travel insurance before traveling to Buskerud.

Getting to Buskerud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buskerud

The nearest airports to Buskerud are Sandefjord Airport, Oslo Lufthavn, and Torp Sandefjord Airport. Visitors can reach Buskerud from these airports via train or bus.

Bus Stations Near Buskerud

Buskerud is well connected to other parts of Norway via buses. Some of the major bus stations in Buskerud are Hønefoss bus station, Hallingdal bus station, and Drammen bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Buskerud via train. The major train stations in Buskerud are Hønefoss railway station, Kongsberg railway station, and Drammen railway station.

How to Get Around in Buskerud

Buskerud has an extensive public transportation network, and visitors can get around easily via buses, trains, and taxis. For those who want to explore the scenic beauty of Buskerud, renting a car is an ideal option.

Where to Go Next from Buskerud: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oslo: Oslo is Norway’s capital city, located just a few hours away from Buskerud. It is known for its stunning fjords, museums, and cultural attractions.

  • Telemark: Telemark is a scenic region located south of Buskerud and is known for its stunning hiking trails, skiing resorts, and picturesque landscapes.

  • Bergen: Bergen is a historic city located on Norway’s western coast and is known for its stunning fjords, UNESCO-listed Bryggen Wharf, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Buskerud is a beautiful region of Norway that offers a range of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in hiking, skiing, or cultural attractions, Buskerud has something to offer every type of traveler.
