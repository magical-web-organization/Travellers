Introduction to Hemsedal

Nestled in the heart of Buskerud, in East Norway lies the stunning mountain resort town of Hemsedal. Boasting an average of 220 days of snow each year, Hemsedal is a renowned winter sports destination. However, this town is a year-round gem, with activities for everybody in every season. With pristine natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Hemsedal is a must-visit destination in Norway.

List of Places to Visit in Hemsedal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hemsedal Bygdatun: A charming museum showcasing the local history of Hemsedal.
  • Grindaflethytta: A historic farmstead, now a popular stop on the Hemsedal Heritage Trail.

Natural Attractions of Hemsedal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hemsedal Ski Resort: A world-famous ski resort, boasting over 50 trails for skiers and snowboarders of all levels.
  • Hemsedal Skisenter: Ideal for families, this ski resort has a magic carpet and gentle slopes for beginners.
  • Hemsedal Fjellandsby: For a magical cross country skiing experience and stunning views of Hemsedal.
  • Rjukandefossen Waterfall: A picturesque waterfall that is ideal for a leisurely hike and picnic.
  • Haugastøl Bergsjo: A gem for the nature lover, a serene lake surrounded by mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hemsedal

Hemsedal is an absolute heaven for hikers and bikers, with endless options for trails. A must-do hike is to the peak of Storhøvda, which will give you a sweeping view of Hemsedal from over 1500 meters above sea level. Bikers should try the popular route to Gol, or opt for the challenge of the Hemsedal Mountain Bike Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Hemsedal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rømmegrøt: A traditional dish, made with sour cream and flour, usually served with cured meats and flatbread.
  • Fårikål: A beloved Norwegian dish of lamb stewed with cabbage and served with potatoes.
  • Kjøttkaker: Meatballs made from minced beef or pork, served with gravy, lingonberry sauce, and boiled potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A popular Norwegian spirit usually served as an accompaniment to meals or for celebrations.
  • Beer: Hemsedal Brewery produces an impressive range of high-quality beers.

Where to Stay in Hemsedal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you want a luxury ski-in/ski-out chalet, a boutique hotel or a cozy vacation home, Hemsedal has something for everybody.

  • Skarsnuten Hotel: A luxurious ski-in/ski-out hotel and spa.

  • Hemsetunet: A charming guesthouse that sits along the Hemsila River.

  • Hemsedal Café and Hostel: Budget-friendly accommodation in the center of town with a lively atmosphere.

Local Culture and Customs of Hemsedal

Hemsedal offers a unique and fascinating glimpse into Norway’s rural past. Traditional crafts, ancient farming techniques, reindeer herding, and mountain goat farming are still a way of life here.

Best Time to Visit Hemsedal

Hemsedal is a snowy playground from December until April and is lovely in the summertime when the mountain wildflowers are in full bloom and the days are long and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hemsedal

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the area and take nothing but photographs.
  • Try traditional Norwegian food.
  • Be open to experiencing the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Drive off-road, even in the winter.
  • Ignore cultural customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hemsedal

Most of Hemsedal’s activities are quite safe, but visitors need to be cautious of the mountain environment. Be aware of the Risks of snow avalanches and be sure to inquire about the proper safety equipment for any off-piste skiing or snowmobiling.

Health in Hemsedal

Norway is perhaps one of the healthiest countries in the world, with great healthcare facilities and strict food safety measures. Tap water is safe to drink, and you’ll find pharmacies in the bigger towns in the area.

Getting to Hemsedal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hemsedal

  • Oslo Airport – 195km
  • Sandefjord Airport – 279km

Bus Stations Near Hemsedal

  • Gol Station – 20km
  • Hønefoss Station – 100km

Other Options

Visitors can reach Hemsedal via car. A trip from Oslo takes around 3 hours and is a straightforward drive.

How to Get Around in Hemsedal

A car is the most convenient and practical way to get around Hemsedal, especially if you want to explore the surrounding areas and attractions on your own. However, Hemsedal also has a reliable shuttle bus service.
