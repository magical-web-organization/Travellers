Introduction to Rauland

Rauland is a picturesque mountain village nestled in Telemark, a region in East Norway. Known for its stunning scenery and outdoor adventures, Rauland offers a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. The village is surrounded by mountains, lakes, and forests, providing opportunities for activities like hiking, biking, fishing, skiing, and snowboarding.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Rauland, we will explore everything you need to know to plan your trip to this beautiful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Rauland

Rauland has plenty of places to visit and explore. Here is a list of some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rauland Folk Museum: Learn about the local history and culture through displays of traditional costumes, tools, and buildings.

  • Rauland Historical Hotel: Established in 1890, this hotel is a historic landmark with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and lakes.

  • West-Telemark Museum Eidsborg: Explore the region’s history through exhibits on agriculture, architecture, and handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Rauland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hardangervidda National Park: This park is the largest national park in Norway and has beautiful hiking trails, waterfalls, glaciers, and other natural attractions.

  • Gygrestolen Mountain: Hike to the summit of this mountain for breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Raulandsfjell Mountain: This mountain is a popular ski resort during winter and has fantastic hiking and biking trails in summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rauland

Rauland has plenty of hiking and biking trails, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Rauland-Åmot Trail: This 12 km trail takes you through scenic terrain and ends at the beautiful Rauland Lake.

  • Tuddal Hiking Trail: This 6 km trail leads you through Tuddal valley and provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Raulandsfjell Panorama Trail: This trail takes you to the summit of Raulandsfjell Mountain and provides panoramic views of the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Rauland: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Rauland is a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts, with several ski resorts in the area.

  • Fishing: The region has several lakes and rivers that are perfect for fishing.

  • Snowshoeing: Explore the winter landscape of Rauland on snowshoes.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the beautiful lakes and rivers of Rauland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rauland

Day 1 - Explore Rauland Folk Museum and Raulandsfjell Mountain.

Day 2 - Go fishing in one of the many lakes in the region.

Day 3 - Hike the Rauland-Åmot Trail and end at Rauland Lake.

Day 4 - Take a day trip to Hardangervidda National Park and explore its many natural attractions.

Day 5 - Enjoy a day of skiing or snowboarding in one of Rauland’s ski resorts.

Day 6 - Go snowshoeing or take a guided snowmobile tour.

Day 7 - Relax and unwind at a local spa or enjoy the local food scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Rauland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål: A traditional Norwegian dish made with mutton, cabbage, and potatoes.

  • Smalahove: Another traditional Norwegian dish made with sheep’s head, usually served with mashed rutabaga and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Norwegian spirit made from potatoes and flavored with herbs and spices.

  • Beer: Rauland has several local breweries that produce a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Rauland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rauland has plenty of options for accommodation, whether you are looking for luxury hotels or budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Rauland Høgfjellshotell: A luxury hotel with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and lakes.

  • Rauland Mountain Lodge: A cozy mountain lodge with comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

  • Rauland Vandrerhjem: A budget-friendly hostel with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Rauland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Rauland is known for its traditional handicrafts, including woven textiles and wood carvings.

  • Local Produce: You can buy locally produced cheese, jam, and other food items in the village.

Local Culture and Customs of Rauland

The people of Rauland are proud of their local culture and traditions. One of the most visible aspects of their culture is the traditional costumes, which are still worn for special occasions and festivals. The region is also known for its traditional music, dance, and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Rauland

The best time to visit Rauland depends on your interests. If you are interested in outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing, then June to August is the best time to visit. If you are interested in skiing or snowboarding, then December to April is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rauland

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take care of the environment and leave no trace.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines for outdoor activities.

  • Disrespect local traditions or customs.

  • Hike or bike off designated trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rauland

Rauland is generally a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it is essential to follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and skiing. Always wear appropriate gear, follow marked trails, and carry a map and compass.

Health in Rauland

Norway has excellent healthcare facilities, but they can be expensive for visitors. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance that covers emergency medical expenses before visiting Rauland.

Getting to Rauland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rauland

The nearest airports to Rauland are Oslo Gardermoen Airport, Torp Sandefjord Airport, and Bergen Airport.

Bus Stations Near Rauland

There are several bus stations near Rauland, including Veggli, Rjukan, and Kongsberg.

Other Options

You can also drive to Rauland or take a train to Kongsberg or Rjukan and then take a bus to Rauland.

How to Get Around in Rauland

The best way to get around Rauland is by car, although you can also use public transport like buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Rauland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gaustatoppen: This peak is one of the most famous in Norway, and the views from the summit are truly breathtaking.

  • Hardangerfjord: This fjord is known for its scenic beauty and is a popular destination for cruises.

Conclusion

Rauland is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts and anyone looking to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life. With plenty of natural attractions, activities, and cultural experiences, Rauland offers something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to this beautiful destination.
