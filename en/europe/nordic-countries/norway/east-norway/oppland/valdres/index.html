Introduction to Valdres

Valdres is a scenic region located in Oppland, East Norway. It comprises six municipalities: Vestre Slidre, Øystre Slidre, Vang, Nord-Aurdal, Sør-Aurdal, and Etnedal. Surrounded by majestic mountain ranges, forests, and crystal-clear lakes, Valdres is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The region is also rich in history and culture, with numerous museums and historical sites to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Valdres

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Valdres Folk Museum: This open-air museum in Fagernes showcases the local traditions of Valdres.
  • Nøsen Culture Centre: A historic farmhouse in Bagn that offers a glimpse into the region’s rural life.
  • Lomen Stave Church: A beautifully preserved wooden church dating back to the 12th century.
  • Vang Stone Church: A Gothic-style church with frescoes and intricate woodcarvings from the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Valdres: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jotunheimen National Park: Home to Norway’s highest peaks, glaciers, and diverse wildlife.
  • Besseggen Ridge: One of Norway’s most popular hiking trails, known for its breathtaking views of Gjende lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Vassfaret Bear Park: A sanctuary for bears and other rescued animals in Etnedal.
  • Valdresflye: A high mountain plateau with stunning views of the surrounding valleys and peaks.
  • Randsfjorden Lake: A popular destination for fishing, kayaking, and cruising.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valdres

Valdres offers countless opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts of all levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Peer Gynt Trail: A scenic trail that follows the footsteps of the famous Norwegian fairy tale character, Peer Gynt.
  • Bitihorn: A challenging hike that rewards with stunning views of Jotunheimen and Valdresflye.
  • Vangsmjøsa Rundt: A pleasant bike ride around the Vangsmjøsa lake that also offers glimpses into local history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Valdres: Activities and Tours

  • “Fjellpuls” Adventure Park: A family-friendly park with zip lines, obstacle courses, and other outdoor activities.
  • Valdres Traditional Food Walk: A guided tour that takes participants through the local food scene, with tastings of traditional dishes and drinks.
  • River Rafting on the Begna River: An adrenaline-pumping experience that is popular among adventure seekers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valdres

Day 1: Arrive in Fagernes and visit the Valdres Folk Museum.

Day 2: Hike to the summit of Bitihorn.

Day 3: Explore the Jotunheimen National Park, including the Besseggen Ridge trail.

Day 4: Visit the Lomen Stave Church and Nøsen Culture Centre.

Day 5: Take a bike ride around Vangsmjøsa lake and explore the local history and culture.

Day 6: Experience “Fjellpuls” Adventure Park for a day of outdoor fun.

Day 7: Go river rafting on the Begna River before departing from Valdres.

Food and Drink Scene in Valdres

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rømmegrøt: A traditional Norwegian porridge made with sour cream and flour.
  • Fårikål: A lamb and cabbage stew that is Norway’s national dish.
  • Pinnekjøtt: A salt-cured lamb dish that is commonly eaten during the Christmas season.

Local Beverages

  • Akevitt: A traditional Scandinavian distilled spirit made with potatoes and seasoned with herbs and spices.
  • Beer from Valdres: Local breweries such as Valdres Gardsbryggeri and Duggfrisk Bryggeri offer a range of artisanal beers.

Where to Stay in Valdres: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valdres offers a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Fefor Høifjellshotell: A historic hotel with stunning views of the Jotunheimen National Park.
  • Thon Hotel Fagernes: A modern hotel in the heart of Fagernes town.
  • Smedsmo Camping: A family-friendly camping site with cabins and RV spots.

What to Buy in Valdres: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Knives from Valdres: Handmade knives crafted by local artisans in the traditional way.
  • Valdres Sweaters: Warm woolen sweaters in traditional Norwegian patterns.
  • Valdresbunad: A traditional Norwegian costume that dates back to the 19th century.

Local Culture and Customs of Valdres

Valdres has a rich culture and history, with a particular focus on agriculture and handicrafts. The region is famous for its traditional folk costumes, music, and dance, which are still actively practiced today.

Best Time to Visit Valdres

The best time to visit Valdres is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, winter is also a popular time to visit, especially for winter sports enthusiasts, since Valdres is one of Norway’s premier ski destinations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valdres

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Stay on marked trails while hiking.
  • Try traditional food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety warnings while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valdres

Valdres is a very safe destination for travelers. However, it is recommended to take standard precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Valdres

No specific vaccines are required for traveling to Valdres. The region has good infrastructure and healthcare facilities.

Getting to Valdres: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Valdres

The nearest airports to Valdres are Oslo Gardermoen Airport and Fagernes Airport.

Bus Stations Near Valdres

Valdresekspressen runs daily bus services from Oslo Bus Terminal to Fagernes and other municipalities in Valdres.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a popular option to get around Valdres and explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Valdres

Valdres is well-connected with a network of roads, making it easy to get around by car or public transportation. Biking and hiking are also popular ways to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Valdres: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lillehammer: A picturesque town known for hosting the 1994 Winter Olympics, located less than 2 hours from Valdres.
  • Geirangerfjord: One of Norway’s most scenic fjords, located about 4 hours from Valdres.
  • Bergen: A charming coastal town that is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located about 5 hours from Valdres.

Conclusion

Valdres offers a unique experience for travelers looking to explore Norway’s natural beauty, rich culture, and history. With its stunning mountains, crystal-clear lakes, and scenic hiking trails, Valdres is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From traditional food and drink to local handicrafts, Valdres has something for everyone.
