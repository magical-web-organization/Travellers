Introduction to Rosendal

Rosendal is a small village in Hordaland, Western Norway, which is known for its historical and cultural significance, natural attractions, and scenic beauty. Rosendal is committed to preserving its rich history, and visitors can still admire the stone buildings of the 17th century barony.

List of Places to Visit in Rosendal

Rosendal boasts a variety of attractions, both natural and man-made, that are worth visiting. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Baroniet Rosendal

Baroniet Rosendal is a magnificent 17th-century estate converted into a museum. It’s a great place to learn about the history of the Norwegian nobility and how they lived during that time. The museum includes several buildings with guided tours available, as well as gardens, a park, and a cafe.

Malmangernuten Mountain

Malmangernuten is one of the most popular mountains in the region. The peak offers a fantastic view of Rosendal and Hardangerfjorden, making it an unforgettable spot to view the sunset.

Folgefonna National Park

Rosendal is the gateway to Folgefonna National Park, a beautiful nature reserve established in 2005. The park is renowned for its stunning glaciers, stunning snowscapes, and crystal-clear glacial lakes.

Stone Park

The stone park is yet another unique attraction located in Rosendal. It’s a park filled with sculptures made from stones naturally shaped. The park is a popular spot to view the unique art sculptures during summertime.

Natural Attractions of Rosendal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sundal Valley

Hiking in Sundal valley is one of the most invigorating things to do in Rosendal. You will be rewarded with stunning views of towering cliffs, gushing waterfalls, and idyllic rivers.

Bondhusvatnet Lake

Bondhusvatnet Lake is a glacial lake that’s another popular natural attraction located only a short drive from Rosendal. The route to this lake is recognized as one of the most beautiful trails in the area.

Hardangerfjorden

Experience the beauty of the fjords as you explore this 180 km-long fjord by boat or tour. The Hardanger Region can proudly say that it is the only fjord destination in the world that has been listed on The National Geographic Traveler 2021 Best of the World list.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rosendal

Hiking in Rosendal is a famous outdoor activity, however, cycling also coming in momentum. The village boasts some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Norway. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Rosendal:

Malmangernuten Hiking Trail

This hiking trail will lead you to the summit and offer breathtaking views of Rosendal and Hardangerfjorden. The trail is steep in some parts, but worth the effort.

The Tysnesbolgen Coastal Route

The Tysnesbolgen Coastal Route is a well-marked cycling route that overlooks the Hardanger fjords. The route begins at Rosendal and continues through charming coastal towns like Tørring, Hodnanes, and Varaldsøy.

Best Things to Do in Rosendal: Activities and Tours

When visiting Rosendal, there is so much to do. Here is some of the best things to do while in Rosendal.

Outdoor Adventures

Enjoy fishing, kayaking, biking, and hiking with fantastic views all around. Some tours may even take into consideration the experience of complete beginners, like kayaking on the fjords.

Food and Drink Scene of Rosendal

The village of Rosendal will delight you with its local flavors. Here are some of the most popular, and must-try, dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Raspeballer

A traditional Norwegian dish consisting of grated potatoes, flour, and salt. Raspeballer is often served with sausage and bacon, but at times it can also be served with meatballs.

Rømmegrøt (sour cream porridge)

A dish made with sour cream, flour, and butter, served with cured meat or sugar.

Local Beverages

Cider and Beer

The Rosendal fjordsides are the perfect growing environment for a variety of fruits. Therefore, local breweries and makers work with the fruit brought from local farms in making regional ciders, a must-try.

Where to Stay in Rosendal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rosendal offers plenty of accommodation options, ranging from boutique hotels and guesthouses to traditional farmhouses, which will provide you with an authentic Norwegian experience. Here are some of the best places to stay in Rosendal:

Rosendal Tourist Hotel

Built in 1645, this historic hotel has modern facilities and consists of 20 rooms with fantastic views of the fjords.

Ryggahus Gjestgiveri

Ryggahus Gjestgiveri is a cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Rosendal. It has 10 beautifully furnished rooms and serves a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Rosendal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rosendal offers some unique souvenirs, local products, and gifts that are one of a kind. Here are some of the things you can purchase:

Handmade souvenirs

Buy locally made souvenirs that are handcrafted and locally produced. You can find things like handmade clothes, wood carvings, jewelry, and more at the shops and markets in the village.

Local honey and cider

Buy some of the locally produced honey and cider from farms and orchards near Rosendal. The flavors are unique to the region and great as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Rosendal

The people of Rosendal are proud of their heritage, history, and traditional customs. It is encouraged to take the time to learn and explore the local customs and history of the area.

Best Time to Visit Rosendal

The best time to visit Rosendal is during the summertime, as this is when the area enjoys more temperate weather and has long days. However, the winter months can provide fantastic winter sport activities like skiing, snowmobiling, and husky sledding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rosendal

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and rules
  • Wear hiking boots when exploring the countryside
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Take time to explore the fjords and nature around Rosendal

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Wear inappropriate clothing while hiking
  • Ignore the extreme weather
  • Disrespect the local culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Rosendal

Rosendal is generally safe, and the locals are helpful and welcoming. As with any outdoor activity, it is essential to take precautions, such as carrying waterproof clothing, wearing proper footwear, carrying insect repellent and sunscreen.

Health in Rosendal

Medical facilities are limited in Rosendal; however, rescue services are well in place. As a visitor, it is recommended to have travel insurance.

Getting to Rosendal: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are numerous transportation options for getting to Rosendal, including:

Airports Near Rosendal

The nearest airport is Bergen Airport Flesland, which is located an hour’s drive from the village. From here, you can take the Flybussen airport shuttle bus or rent a car.

Bus Stations Near Rosendal

Buses run daily between Bergen and Rosendal. There are also timetabled coaches that run during the busy summer months.

Other Options

Visitors traveling by car are well served by the E39 highway from Bergen. Visitors coming from Oslo or western regions may consider taking a ferry from Stavanger or Bergen.

How to Get Around in Rosendal

Walking is the best way to get around and explore the village. For those looking to travel further, renting a car or bike is another great option. Some tours offer a bus service that will show you the scenic routes that will take you to famous destinations within Rosendal.

Where to Go Next from Rosendal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rosendal is located near some fascinating locations that are worth exploring. Here are some of those destinations:

  • Bergen: The historic Hanseatic city of Bergen is just over an hour’s drive from Rosendal and is an excellent place to explore the rich history of the region.
  • Hardangerfjord: The Hardanger region is famous for its fjord scenery, and visitors should consider exploring this area in more depth.
  • Trolltunga: Trolltunga is an awe-inspiring rock formation that is about a three and a half-hour drive from Rosendal.

Conclusion

Rosendal is a beautiful destination that has something to offer for everyone, whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just relaxation. Use this guide to plan your visit, and you will be sure to have an unforgettable time in this gorgeous village in Norway.
