Introduction to Austevoll

Austevoll is a charming municipality located in the county of Hordaland, in Western Norway. With its idyllic landscape, cultural landmarks, and bustling fishing industry, Austevoll is a perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Norweigan experience.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Austevoll, featuring top attractions, activities, accommodations, local culture, transportation, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Austevoll

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Austevoll is proud of its rich maritime heritage, with several museums and historical sites dedicated to the fishing industry. Some of the most popular attractions in this category include:

  • Austevoll Maritime Museum – a museum showcasing the history and evolution of Austevoll’s fishing tradition.
  • Bekkjarvik Gjestgiveri – a 17th-century inn that offers a glimpse into the country’s olden days.
  • Glesvaer Rorbu – a seaside village where visitors can enjoy a relaxing day on the beach and explore the fishing culture of Norway.

Natural Attractions of Austevoll: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Austevoll is a dream destination for lovers of the outdoors, with its breathtaking scenery, diverse wildlife, and thrilling activities. The top natural attractions in Austevoll include:

  • Austevoll Islands – an archipelago of small islands with picturesque landscapes, perfect for day trips and sea kayaking.
  • Andersnatten – a popular hiking spot that offers stunning panoramic views of Austevoll.
  • Langenuen – a fjord surrounded by mountains and forested hills, where visitors can enjoy sailing, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Austevoll

Austevoll offers some of the most picturesque hiking and biking trails in Norway. The best trails in Austevoll include:

  • Stolma – a popular hiking and cycling trail that takes you through pine forests, mountain slopes, and spectacular views of the sea.
  • Storekalsøy – a scenic path that winds through the pristine natural beauty of Austevoll’s hinterland, with glimpses of the sea, forest, and wildlife.
  • Hovden – a challenging trail that rewards hikers with jaw-dropping views of the hillside and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Austevoll: Activities and Tours

Austevoll offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Some of the best things to do in Austevoll include:

  • Fishing – Austevoll is renowned for its thriving fishing industry, and visitors can experience it for themselves by participating in a fishing tour or chartering a boat.
  • Sea kayaking – the shallow waters off Austevoll’s archipelago make it an ideal place for kayaking, with plenty of hidden coves, unspoiled beaches, and stunning wildlife.
  • Island hopping – with over 130 small islands surrounding Austevoll, visitors can explore the charming fishing communities and see the unique beauty of each island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Austevoll

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Austevoll, the following itinerary will make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Austevoll islands, including Kolbeinsvik and Storekalsøy island
  • Day 3-4: Hike or cycle Stolma trail, and enjoy guided fishing tours.
  • Day 5-6: Discover Austevoll Museum, Bekkjarvik Gjestgiveri, and cultural activities.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beaches of Glesvaer Rorbu or Heng, and enjoy seafood feast in a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Austevoll

Must-Try Dishes

Austevoll is famous for its seafood, most notably its herring. There are many delicious dishes to try when visiting Austevoll, including:

  • Sursild – pickled herring served with sour cream and onions.
  • Fårikål – mutton stew with cabbage, often served as a traditional Norwegian dish.
  • Skreimølje – cod stew with liver and roe, flavorful and rich in nutrients.

Local Beverages

Austevoll also has a vibrant drinking culture, with a range of locally sourced beers and spirits. Some of the most popular beverages in Austevoll include:

  • Austevoll aquavit – a potent spirit infused with herbs, spices, and the essence of the sea.
  • Austevoll beer – handcrafted beers brewed with the finest local ingredients, including hops and malted barley.
  • Austevoll cider – made from the freshest apples, this crisp drink is a perfect accompaniment to any meal.

Where to Stay in Austevoll: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Austevoll has a range of affordable and comfortable accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular places to stay in Austevoll include:

  • Bekkjarvik Gjestgiveri – a historic inn with modernized amenities and rooms overlooking the stunning harbor.
  • Solstrand Hotel & Bad – a luxurious hotel offering unparalleled views of the fjords and deliciously fresh food.
  • Glesvaer Rorbu – cabin-style accommodation located in a charming fishing village with boats rental and beaches nearby.

What to Buy in Austevoll: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Austevoll also has an array of locally produced goods and souvenirs that visitors can bring home as a reminder of their trip. These include:

  • Handmade knitwear – woolen hats, mittens, and scarves of excellent quality made by local artisans.
  • Austevoll herring – savory canned fish that can be brought home, and tastes great with Norwegian bread or crackers.
  • Traditional handicrafts – unique pieces of art, including wooden trolls, carved figurines, and painted ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Austevoll

Austevoll has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience it by participating in local customs and traditions. Some of the most noteworthy customs in Austevoll include:

  • Midsummer celebrations – locals gather to acknowledge the summer solstice with bonfires, concerts, and traditional food.
  • Herring market – a vibrant festival held every August to celebrate the herring, with vendors selling freshly caught fish, local artisanal products, and traditional performances.
  • Holiday traditions – the Yuletide celebrations are a festive time of year, with Christmas markets, carol singing, and traditional dishes.

Best Time to Visit Austevoll

The best time to visit Austevoll is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is mild, and the days are long, perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the archipelago. Additionally, the herring market in August is a must-see event for lovers of food, music, and culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Austevoll

Do:

  • Respect the environment – Austevoll is known for its pristine natural beauty, and visitors should take care not to litter or damage the environment.
  • Learn some Norwegian – although many locals speak English, learning some basic Norweigan phrases can make your time in Austevoll more enjoyable.
  • Experience the local culture – Austevoll has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take the time to learn about and participate in local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety warnings – while Austevoll is a relatively safe destination, visitors should pay attention to any warning signs or weather alerts.
  • Fish without a license – visitors can purchase a fishing license from authorized vendors to fish in Austevoll’s waters legally. Fishing without a permit can incur fines or other penalties.
  • Overstep cultural boundaries – visitors should respect local customs and traditions, and avoid behavior that may offend locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Austevoll

Austevoll is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise normal precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended or walking alone in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Austevoll

Austevoll has a high standard of healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical assistance if needed. It is recommended that visitors carry appropriate travel insurance and any necessary medications.

Getting to Austevoll: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Austevoll by several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Austevoll

The nearest airport to Austevoll is Bergen Flesland Airport, located approximately 30 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Austevoll

The bus station closest to Austevoll is located in Bergen city, with direct bus services to Bekkjarvik and Kolbeinsvik.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Austevoll by car or ferry, with routes available from Bergen or Stavanger. There are also some chartered boats available for visitors who want to explore the archipelago.

How to Get Around in Austevoll

There are several transportation options for getting around Austevoll, including:

  • Car rental – visitors can rent a car from the airport or other major cities to explore Austevoll and its surrounding areas.
  • Public transportation – Austevoll has several bus routes linking the major towns, but they may not provide comprehensive access to the lesser-known areas.
  • Taxi – taxis can be found in all major towns and can provide transportation to desired destinations. It is recommended to arrange fees beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Austevoll: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Austevoll is perfectly located for travelers who want to explore more of Norway’s stunning natural beauty and bustling cities. Some of the must-visit destinations from Austevoll include:

  • Bergen – Norway’s second-largest city, with historic sites, cultural attractions, and world-class seafood restaurants.
  • Hardangerfjord – considered one of the most beautiful fjords in Norway, with glaciers, waterfalls, and charming villages.
  • Stavanger – a picturesque port city offering scenic views, museums, and an excellent nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Austevoll is a hidden gem of Western Norway, with its authentic culture, stunning natural landscapes, and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural activities, or just a relaxing getaway, Austevoll has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to this unique destination and experience the best of Norwegian culture and lifestyle.
