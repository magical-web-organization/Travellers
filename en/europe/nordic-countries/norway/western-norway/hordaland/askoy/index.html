Introduction to Askøy

Askøy is a stunningly beautiful and rugged island located off the coast of the city of Bergen. With over 25,000 residents, it has everything you would need to explore the scenic landscapes of Western Norway. You’ll find a variety of outdoor adventure activities, cultural experiences, historic landmarks and so much more. This guide will give you a comprehensive overview of what to expect when visiting Askøy.

List of Places to Visit in Askøy

Askøy offers a wide range of activities and things to see during your stay. From cultural landmarks to natural scenic routes, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the key places to visit while in Askøy.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haugland Hovedgård: A grand manor house dating back to the 1700s and is now home to a museum displaying artefacts from the house and its history.
  • Herdløypa: A multimedia exhibit showcases the history of the Herdle family and their life on the island dating back to the Viking Era.
  • Agatunet: A historical open-air site with well-preserved farmsteads, sheds and houses from several centuries.

Natural Attractions of Askøy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kollevågen: Located on the west coast of the island, this beach is a great place to relax and enjoy the views of the North Sea.
  • Knut Herdals veg: A scenic road that takes you across the Askøy Bridge and provides beautiful views of the fjords.
  • Øvre Eidfjordvatnet: An excellent spot for fishing and water sports.
  • Strusshamn: A picturesque coastal fishing village with views of the fjord.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Askøy

Askøy has a range of scenic hiking and biking routes to explore the area. Here are some of the top routes to explore while on the island.

  • Tveitevannet rundt: A 10 km route around the lake Tveitevannet with partly steep uphill parts, offering stunning views of Askøy
  • Sjursnesstien: A charming path along the sea cliffs in the west part of the island
  • Lerøy rundt: A 9 km biking trail that runs across the island and takes you through beautiful forestry
  • St. Olavsleden: A 642 km spiritual hiking trail from Oslo to Trondheim and passes through Askøy

Best Things to Do in Askøy: Activities and Tours

Askøy has a variety of activities and tours that cater to the adventurous traveler. Here are some great things to do while on the island.

  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and paddle along the coast of Askøy.
  • Fishing: Join a fishing trip and catch your dinner.
  • Sailing: Take a day trip on a sailboat to explore the fjords surrounding Askøy.
  • Helicopter tour: Get a bird’s eye view of the beautiful Askøy landscapes from a helicopter.

Week-Long Itinerary for Askøy

If you’re looking to visit Askøy for a week, it’s recommended that you spend your days exploring the island’s scenic landscapes, historic landmarks, and outdoor sports activities. Here’s a recommended week-long itinerary for your stay:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Agatunet
Day 2 Hiking and biking
Day 3 Visit Haugland Hovedgård
Day 4 Kayaking along the coast
Day 5 Visit Strusshamn harbor
Day 6 Take a boat trip in the fjords
Day 7 Relax on Kollevågen

Food and Drink Scene of Askøy

The food and drink scene in Askøy is influenced by the fresh local ingredients and traditional Norwegian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål: A traditional Norwegian stew made from lamb, cabbage, and potatoes.
  • Fiskekaker: Fish cakes that are very popular in Norway.
  • Kjøttkaker: Meatballs typically served with peas, carrots, potatoes and lingonberry sauce

Local Beverages

  • Aass Pilsner: A local beer brewery based in Drammen.
  • Gildestad Eplemost: A refreshing local apple cider.

Where to Stay in Askøy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Askøy offers a range of accommodation options for travelers looking for a place to stay. Here are some of the top choices on the island.

  • Thon Hotel Bergen Airport: A convenient hotel located near the Bergen Flesland Airport.
  • Knapphullet Fjordhotell: A hotel situated in a peaceful location with stunning views of the fjords.
  • Haraldsplass Hotel: A centrally located hotel in Bergen City near the city center.

What to Buy in Askøy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Askøy offers plenty of souvenir options for travelers to bring back home. Here are some souvenirs that you can pick up while on the island.

  • Local arts and crafts: Visit Agatunet or Herdløypa to purchase traditional Norwegian crafts.
  • Furs: Norwegian furs are of high quality and are well-known in Europe.
  • Norwegian Salmon: The salmon produced in Norway is of the highest quality and is a great souvenir for seafood lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Askøy

Askøy has a rich history and culture dating back to the Viking era. Norwegian culture is a mix of modern and traditional values. Locals are respectful and punctual. It is encouraged to shake hands with both men and women when meeting them for the first time.

Best Time to Visit Askøy

The best time to visit Askøy is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy. However, if you prefer cooler temperatures and the chance for winter sports, then visiting in December or January is recommended.

Do’s and Don’ts of Askøy

It’s always important to be respectful of the local customs and traditions while traveling. Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow when visiting Askøy.

Do:

  • Be on time for appointments and meetings.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s house or apartment.

Don’t:

  • Be overly loud or disruptive.
  • Leave the table before everyone is finished eating.
  • Be critical of the Norwegian culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Askøy is considered a safe destination for travelers, but it’s essential to practice common sense and take precautions. Here is a brief overview of safety and health while on the island.

Safety in Askøy

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Always wear a life jacket when on a boat.
  • Check the local weather before heading out on outdoor activities.

Health in Askøy

Norway has a high standard of medical care, and travelers should have no problem finding quality medical facilities in Bergen City. It’s important to have travel insurance and to take necessary precautions against COVID-19.

Getting to Askøy: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Askøy:

Airports Near Askøy

  • Bergen Flesland Airport: The nearest airport is located in Bergen City, approximately 35 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Askøy

  • Bergen Busstasjon: Located in Bergen City, where you can take a bus to your destination on the island.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Another option is to rent a car and drive to Askøy using the bridges.

How to Get Around in Askøy

There are several ways to get around Askøy:

  • Car: Renting a car is the most convenient way to explore the island.
  • Bus: There is a local bus service that runs through the island.
  • Bike: Renting a bike is a great option to explore the scenic routes around Askøy.
  • Walking: Some of the attractions on the island are quite close, so walking is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Askøy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Norway has plenty of destinations worth visiting. Here are some nearby destinations you may want to consider visiting after your stay in Askøy.

  • Bergen City: A charming city with historic landmarks, museums, and great food
  • Hardanger Fjord: Norway’s second-longest fjord, known for its stunning scenery, and outdoor activities
  • Flåm: A picturesque village located along a fjord, accessible by boat or car

Conclusion

Askøy is truly one of the hidden gems of Norway. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and diverse activities, it is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to explore Western Norway. We hope this guide has been helpful to you in planning your next trip to Askøy. Enjoy your visit!
