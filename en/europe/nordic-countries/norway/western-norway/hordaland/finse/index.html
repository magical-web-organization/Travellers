Introduction to Finse

Finse is a picturesque mountain village located in Hordaland, Western Norway. Situated at 1,222 meters above sea level, it is known as the highest railway station in Northern Europe, and it also offers stunning views of the surrounding mountainous landscape. This town has a population of approximately 20 residents and is the perfect getaway spot for nature lovers, hikers, and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Finse

Whether you are visiting Finse for a day or a couple of weeks, there’s plenty to see and do here. Here is a list of the top attractions in Finse:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Finse 1222 Hotel: Located near the train station, this hotel dates back to the early 20th century and features unique architecture and design.
  • Hardangervidda National Park Center: Get an insight into the history and nature of the Hardangervidda National Park from the multi-media exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Finse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hardangerjøkulen Glacier: This massive ice cap is the sixth largest glacier in Norway and can be accessed by hiking or skiing from Finse.
  • Vikinghaugane: This viewpoint is perfect for admiring the scenic views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Finse

Finse is known for its excellent hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of this region. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The Hardangervidda Plateau trail: Enjoy hiking amidst the wild, open landscape while admiring the stunning views of glaciers and valleys.
  • Rallarvegen Trail: A popular biking route, the Rallarvegen Trail offers a network of beautiful paths that wind through mountains, valleys, and fjords.

Best Things to Do in Finse: Activities and Tours

  • Dog Sledding: Experience the thrill of dog sledding through the snowy terrain of Finse.
  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and cruise through the beautiful Hardangerfjord, where you might spot harbor seals and porpoises.

Week-Long Itinerary for Finse

Here is a week-long itinerary to make the most of your time in Finse:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Finse and check-in to your accommodation. Spend the evening exploring the village.
2 Hike to Hardangervidda National Park to admire the scenery.
3 Join a dog-sled tour or go cross-country skiing near the town.
4 Explore the fjords on a kayaking tour.
5 Take a train ride to Flam and enjoy the beautiful views of the mountainous landscape.
6 Go ice fishing on Lake Finsevatnet, and try your luck catching whitefish and trout.
7 Visit the nearby town of Geilo and explore its cultural and historical attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Finse

One of the best ways to experience the local culture of Finse is by trying out the traditional dishes and local drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pinnekjøtt: This is a traditional Norwegian dish of lamb rib, which is salted, dried, and steamed.
  • Fårikål: This is another popular Norwegian dish consisting of boiled lamb and cabbage.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: This is an alcoholic beverage popular throughout the Nordic countries and is made from potatoes and spices.
  • Karsk: This is a local drink made by mixing coffee and moonshine.

Where to Stay in Finse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Finse offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels:

  • Finse 1222 Hotel: This is the ultimate luxury hotel in Finse and offers world-class amenities to guests.
  • Finsehytta Hostel: This is a budget-friendly option that offers basic accommodation and affordable rates.
  • Bergensbanen Hostel and Hotel: This property presents basic and comfortable accommodations for a reasonable cost.

What to Buy in Finse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs to take back home to remember your trip to Finse:

  • Lefse: This is a traditional Norwegian flatbread that is often filled with butter and sugar.
  • Handmade woolen items: Finse is known for its locally made hats, scarves, and gloves made of wool.

Local Culture and Customs of Finse

The local culture of Finse is heavily influenced by its proximity to nature and the traditional lifestyles of the people who live here. Visitors should be respectful of these customs and be mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Finse

The best time to visit Finse is between June and September when the weather is warm with long days and comfortable temperatures. It is important to note that in winter, the village and surrounding areas can be significantly colder and covered in snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Finse

When visiting Finse, it’s important to keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Be respectful of nature, and avoid leaving any waste behind.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and carry necessary gear for the environment.
  • Be mindful of cultural customs.

Don’t:

  • Feed wild animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Finse

Finse is generally a safe place for visitors, but here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Carry necessary gear and equipment for the expeditions.
  • Ensure you follow all safety regulations and guidelines while on-site.
  • Keep a careful eye on the wild animals to prevent any injury or risk of harm.

Health in Finse

Tap water in Finse is clean and safe to drink. However, visitors are advised to carry bottled water.

Getting to Finse: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to get to Finse, including:

Airports Near Finse

The nearest airport to Finse is Oslo Gardermoen Airport, which is a few hours drive from the village.

Bus Stations Near Finse

Visitors can take the bus from cities near Finse, such as Bergen or Oslo, to reach the village.

Other Options

Finse can also be reached by rail, as it is located on the Bergen Railway Line, which is a scenic route offering beautiful views of Norway.

How to Get Around in Finse

The village of Finse is small, and many of the attractions are within walking distance. However, there are also bicycles available on rent and guided tours available to travelers.

Where to Go Next from Finse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to visit after Finse:

  • Geilo: A charming town for skiing and snowmobiling, and snow camping.
  • Voss: A popular destination for river sports and kayaking.
  • Flam: A scenic village famous for its train journey through Norway’s mountainous landscape.

Conclusion

Finse is a hidden gem in Norway’s rugged landscape and is the perfect destination for nature lovers, hikers, and adventure enthusiasts. With its picturesque views, outdoor activities, and delicious cuisine, Finse is sure to leave you with a memorable travel experience.
