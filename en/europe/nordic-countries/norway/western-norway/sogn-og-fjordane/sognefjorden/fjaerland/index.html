Introduction to Fjærland

Located in Sognefjorden, in Sogn og Fjordane, Western Norway, Fjærland is a serene village surrounded by stunning fjords, glaciers, and mountains. It’s a nature lover’s paradise with endless opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, kayaking, and wildlife spotting. Fjærland is also a cultural hub with world-renowned museums and historical sites that showcase the rich heritage of the region. From natural wonders to cultural gems, Fjærland has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Fjærland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Norwegian Glacier Museum: Discover the geological and glaciological history of Norway and explore the Jostedalsbreen National Park. The museum has interactive exhibits, a panoramic film, and outdoor trails that provide a fascinating insight into the effects of climate change on the glaciers.
  • Bøyabrui: Learn about the local folklore and traditions of the area at this historic site that features an 18th-century watermill, a sawmill, and a blacksmith’s workshop.
  • Fjærland Book Town: Visit one of the world’s most unique bookstores, housed in historic buildings that are home to second-hand bookshops and local crafts stores.

Natural Attractions of Fjærland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jostedal Glacier: This 487-square-kilometer glacier is the largest in continental Europe and an UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s a must-visit spot for any nature enthusiast and offers fantastic hiking and glacier walking opportunities.
  • Supphellebreen Glacier: This glacier is known for its striking blue ice formations and can be reached via a scenic 45-minute hike from the Fjærland Fjordstue Hotel.
  • Fjærlandsfjord: Explore the crystal-clear waters of this majestic fjord via a kayak rental from the Fjærland Fjordstue Hotel.
  • Mundal Valley: Take a leisurely stroll or a challenging hike in one of the most beautiful valleys in Norway. The valley boasts stunning waterfalls, lush forests, and scenic panoramic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fjærland

  • Nærøyfjord and Aurlandsfjord: This three-day hiking trail is one of the most popular in Norway and offers breathtaking views of the fjords, mountains, and valleys.
  • Flatbrehytta Trail: This 12-kilometer round-trip hike is suitable for all levels and offers stunning views of the Jostedal Glacier and the surrounding landscape.
  • Jostedalsbreen National Park: With 60 hiking trails ranging from easy walks to challenging treks, this park offers endless opportunities for exploration and adventure.

Best Things to Do in Fjærland: Activities and Tours

  • Glacier Walking: Experience walking on the ice with guided tours led by professional glacier guides.
  • Fishing: Cast a line in Fjærland’s pristine waters and try your luck at catching salmon, trout, and char.
  • Whale Watching: Take a boat trip to the nearby coastal towns of Sognefjorden to spot dolphins, orcas, and porpoises.
  • Nordic Skiing: Enjoy skiing on groomed trails in the winter wonderland of Fjærland.

Food and Drink Scene of Fjærland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fjellkanten Suppe: A hearty soup made with smoked salmon, leeks, potatoes, and cream.
  • Fjærland Brown Cheese: A sweet and slightly nutty cheese made from goat’s milk.
  • Kjøttkaker: Norwegian meatballs made with ground beef or pork, served with potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Scandinavian spirit made from caraway or dill and served chilled.
  • Cider: Fjærland is known for its delicious apple and pear ciders, made with locally grown fruits.

Where to Stay in Fjærland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fjærland Fjordstue Hotel: A charming hotel that offers spectacular views of the fjord, glacier, and mountains. It has an on-site restaurant that serves local cuisine and offers kayak and bike rentals.
  • Bøyum Camping: This family-run campsite is situated in a peaceful location and offers a range of accommodation options, including cabins and tents.
  • The Green Travelers Hostel: A cozy and eco-friendly hostel that provides budget-friendly accommodation for travelers.

What to Buy in Fjærland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Knitwear: Purchase locally made woolen hats, scarves, and gloves as a cozy souvenir of your trip to the Norwegian fjords.
  • Books: Fjærland Book Town is a treasure trove of second-hand and rare books that make fantastic gifts.
  • Fjærland Brown Cheese: Bring home a taste of Norway with this delectable local cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Fjærland

  • The people of Fjærland are proud of their cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and norms.
  • Norwegians value personal space and privacy. It’s polite to maintain a respectful distance from people and avoid touching them unless necessary.

Best Time to Visit Fjærland

The best time to visit Fjærland is from June to August when the weather is mild, and the days are long. The midnight sun is a unique phenomenon that occurs during the summer months, allowing visitors to enjoy outdoor activities for almost 24 hours a day. The winter months from December to February are ideal for Nordic skiing and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fjærland

Do:

  • Do take your time and enjoy the natural beauty of Fjærland.
  • Do sample the local cuisine and purchase unique souvenirs.
  • Do respect the cultural heritage of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to respect people’s personal space and privacy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fjærland

  • Fjærland is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take precautions when venturing into isolated areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Fjærland

  • Visitors to Fjærland are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance as medical expenses can be high.
  • Tap water in Fjærland is considered safe to drink.

Getting to Fjærland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fjærland

The nearest airports to Fjærland are Bergen Airport (BGO) and Sogndal Airport (SOG). From there, you can take a bus, taxi or rental car to reach Fjærland.

Bus Stations Near Fjærland

The Fjordekspressen bus service runs daily between Bergen and Førde, with a stop in Fjærland. The bus leaves from the Bergen Bus Station and takes approximately 4 hours to reach Fjærland.

Other Options

Driving is an excellent option to enjoy the scenic beauty of Fjærland. Car rental options are available at major airports and cities in Norway.

How to Get Around in Fjærland

Walking and biking are the best ways to explore the village of Fjærland. Rental bikes are available at the Fjærland Fjordstue Hotel. Taxis and buses are also available for longer trips and excursions.

Where to Go Next from Fjærland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Balestrand: A picturesque village situated on the banks of the Sognefjord known for its Art Nouveau architecture and stunning views.
  • Briksdal Glacier: A popular destination and a must-visit spot for any nature lover. The glacier is easily accessible and is located in the Jostedalsbreen National Park.

Conclusion

Fjærland is a hidden gem in Western Norway, offering a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions. From glaciers to hiking trails and museums to local cuisine, Fjærland has something to offer for everyone. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your visit to Fjærland and discover the best this charming village has to offer.
