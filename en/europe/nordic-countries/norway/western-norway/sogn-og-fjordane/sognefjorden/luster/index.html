Introduction to Luster

Nestled in Sognefjorden, in the heart of Sogn og Fjordane, in Western Norway, lies Luster - a hidden gem of stunning natural beauty and cultural significance. The region boasts awe-inspiring landscapes, including the UNESCO-listed Nærøyfjord and Jostedalsbreen National Park, Europe’s largest glacier. Luster is home to friendly locals, rich history and traditions, and a plethora of outdoor activities and attractions that will leave you in awe. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local cultural practices - everything you need to know for an unforgettable Luster experience.

List of Places to Visit in Luster

If you’re planning a trip to Luster, you won’t be short of places to visit. Here are some of the top must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Urnes Stave Church: Built-in 1130 and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Urnes Stave Church is the oldest and most exquisite stave church in Norway.
  • The Feigumfossen Waterfall: A stunning natural wonder that cascades down a rocky face in layers of white foam, providing a spectacular sight for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Luster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nærøyfjord: A UNESCO-listed marvel of nature, Nærøyfjord is a narrow, winding fjord that stretches for 17km through steep mountains and crystal-clear waters.
  • Jostedalsbreen National Park: Covering 1,310 square km, the Jostedalsbreen National Park offers many thrilling adventures, including glacier hiking, scenic walks, horseback riding, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luster

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Luster has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that crisscross through the majestic mountains and fjords.

Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes include the Fuglestegi trail, Marifjøra-Flydalsjuvet coastal route, and the Molden mountain-top hike, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of Lustrafjorden.

Best Things to Do in Luster: Activities and Tours

Luster offers plenty of options to keep you occupied during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Fjord cruises: The fjord cruises offer a unique way to explore the region and its stunning natural attractions.
  • Kayaking: Explore the fjords and local waterways in a kayak.
  • Horseback Riding: Embark on a horseback riding adventure, enjoy scenic trails, and explore the picturesque countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Luster

A week is not enough to explore all that Luster has to offer. Still, if you’re pressed for time, we have created a perfect itinerary for a week-long visit:

Day 1: Arrival in Luster, explore the region’s natural attractions

Day 2: Jostedalsbreen Glacier hike or Nigardsbreen glacier tours

Day 3: Fjord cruise on Sognefjorden or Nærøyfjorden

Day 4: Lustrafjorden kayaking or fishing trip

Day 5: Visit the Urnes Stave Church and local museums

Day 6: Horseback riding in Lusterfjorden or the Marifjøra-Flydalsjuvet coastal route hike

Day 7: Depart from Luster

Food and Drink Scene of Luster

Luster is known for its simple but tasty dishes: seafood and meat dishes cooked with natural herbs and spices. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rømmegrøt: A traditional Norwegian porridge made with sour cream and butter.
  • Fårikål: A lamb and cabbage stew, considered Norway’s national dish.
  • Krumkaker: A cone-shaped waffle served with whipped cream and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Fløjtteikar: A traditional beer made of malted barley.
  • Karsk: A coffee and moonshine mixed drink that is Norway’s signature tipple.

Where to Stay in Luster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luster offers plenty of accommodation options that suit every traveler’s budget and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  • Nigardsbreen Camping: Affordable camping facilities that offer excellent views and proximity to Norway’s picturesque glaciers
  • Fjaerland Fjordstove Hotel: A comfortable and picturesque lakeside hotel that is perfect for nature lovers.
  • The Walaker Hotel: A historic hotel that features distinctive laced curtains, antique decor, and stunning mountain views.

What to Buy in Luster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home, Luster has unique and locally made items, including:

  • Norwegian woolen sweaters and winter wear
  • Hand-blown glassware and ceramics
  • Handcrafted silverware and jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Luster

Luster is a region with a rich culture and history, and to fully experience it, travelers need to adapt to some of its customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Luster

Luster has a mild maritime climate that makes it accessible all year round. Summer is the peak tourist season when the weather is warm, and the attractions are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luster

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Take care of the environment

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Be loud or disruptive to the locals’ peace
  • Ignore safety instructions and regulations in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Luster

Luster is generally a safe region, but travelers must observe safety guidelines and regulations when engaging in outdoor activities or touring.

Health in Luster

Luster has an adequate healthcare system, but travelers must have valid health insurance to access quality healthcare services in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Luster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Luster

The nearest airport to Luster is the Sogndal airport, which is about 30 minutes’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near Luster

Travelers can also use bus services from the nearest terminal, Sogndal bus station, which is 40 minutes’ drive from Luster.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are also available for a more personalized and private transfer experience.

How to Get Around in Luster

Travelers can use various modes of transport to get around in Luster, including public buses, taxis, bicycles, car rentals, and on foot.

Where to Go Next from Luster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Luster, travelers can visit nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Flam: A picturesque village that features the Flam Railway, one of Norway’s most scenic train rides.
  • Bergen: A charming port city with a historic harbor district and the famous Bryggen Wharf.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals, Luster is a destination that will undoubtedly captivate travelers. From hiking and biking to exciting outdoor activities and picturesque scenery, Luster is a must-visit for any traveler planning a trip to Norway. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can now plan the perfect itinerary for your visit to Luster, ensuring you have an unforgettable experience.
