Introduction to Molde

Nestled in Møre og Romsdal, in Western Norway, Molde is a picturesque town that boasts a rich cultural heritage, scenic views, and plenty of outdoor activities. Known as the “City of Roses,” Molde is dotted with well-manicured gardens that bloom with beautiful flowers in the summer. Visitors to this charming town can expect a wide range of attractions, including museums, hiking and biking trails, and a lively food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Molde

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Molde:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Romsdal Museum, which showcases regional culture and history
  • Molde International Jazz Festival, which takes place every summer
  • Molde Cathedral, a beautiful stone church that dates back to the 1950s

Natural Attractions of Molde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Varden viewpoint, which offers stunning views of the mountains and fjords
  • Trollstigen, a winding road that leads to some of the most breathtaking scenery in Norway
  • Moldejazz’s boat trip on Romsdalsfjord which sets sail during the festival season.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Molde

Molde is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the country. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Romsdalseggen, a challenging hike that rewards with views of beautiful mountain ranges, valleys, and fjords
  • Molde Panorama Trail, an easy hike that offers stunning views of Molde and the surrounding countryside
  • Molde-Fannefjord Trail, a scenic bike ride that takes you through picturesque coastal landscapes

Best Things to Do in Molde: Activities and Tours

  • Molde International Jazz Festival for music aficionados
  • Kayaking in the Romsdalsfjord for adventure enthusiasts
  • Attend the Jul in Molde, Molde’s Christmas fair when visiting during mid-December.
  • Visit The Romsdal Museum, with its beautiful nature reserves, from May to September.
  • Try some local and traditional dishes at Aker Brygge or The Corner.

Week-Long Itinerary for Molde

Here’s how to spend a week in Molde:

Day Activity
1 Explore Molde and get acquainted with the city’s main attractions
2 Take a boat tour on Romsdalsfjord and visit some of the small islands in the vicinity.
3 Take a road trip and explore Trollstigen, one of the most scenic roads in Norway
4 Go on the Romsdalseggen hike to experience some stunning views of mountains and fjords
5 Visit the Romsdal Museum and the Molde Cathedral
6 Go kayaking in the Romsdalsfjord and explore the fjords
7 Relax and unwind at the Molde International Jazz Festival and enjoy some of the local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Molde

Molde’s food and drink scene is worth exploring, and visitors can try traditional Norwegian dishes such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Traditional fish soup (Romsdals Fish Soup)
  • Meatballs (Kjøttkaker)
  • Brown cheese (Brunost)

Local Beverages

  • Aass Brewery Beer
  • Mack Brewery Beer
  • Cider from LofotCider

Where to Stay in Molde: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Molde offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Molde include:

  • Molde Fjordstuer, a luxurious hotel that overlooks the fjords
  • Comfort Hotel Nobel, a modern hotel located in the heart of the city
  • Molde Hostel, a budget-friendly option that offers great value for money

What to Buy in Molde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Molde can take home a range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Traditional handmade knives (Lefseknivar)
  • Knitwear made of wool
  • Romsdal bunad (a traditional Norwegian costume)

Local Culture and Customs of Molde

Molde has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Norwegians are known for being punctual, so visitors should make sure to arrive on time for meetings and appointments. Additionally, it’s customary to remove shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Molde

The best time to visit Molde is during the summer months. The weather is usually mild, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking, in the daylight. The Molde International Jazz Festival also takes place in downtown Molde, making it an ideal time to visit the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Molde

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Molde:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be punctual
  • Explore the great outdoors

Don’t:

  • Carry large amounts of cash
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Be disruptive in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Molde

Molde is a safe city for travelers, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major safety concerns. However, as with any city, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Molde

Healthcare in Norway is of a high standard, and visitors can access medical care at a hospital or clinic if needed. Visitors should bring a European Health Insurance Card or insurance policy to access health services.

Getting to Molde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Molde is accessible by plane, train, bus, and car. Some of the transportation options available include:

Airports Near Molde

  • Molde Airport, which is located a few kilometers outside of Molde

Bus Stations Near Molde

  • Molde Bus Terminal, which offers services to several destinations in Norway

Other Options

  • Train stations in Åndalsnes and Dombås, which are located a short distance from Molde and offer fast and efficient services to Oslo and other major cities in Norway.

How to Get Around in Molde

Molde is a walkable city, and visitors can explore the city center on foot. However, for longer distances, visitors can use public transport, such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Molde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Molde’s location makes it an ideal starting point for exploring the nearby attractions, which include:

  • The Geirangerfjord, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its breathtaking scenery
  • The Atlantic Road, a winding road that offers stunning views of the ocean and islands
  • Trollveggen, one of the tallest vertical rock faces in Europe

Conclusion

Molde is a hidden gem in Norway, offering a range of attractions for visitors to explore. From scenic views to cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures, Molde has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to plan your trip and discover all the beauty that Molde has to offer!
