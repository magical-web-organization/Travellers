Introduction to Aure

Located in the Møre og Romsdal region of Norway, Aure is a small and charming town with a population of only 3,500 people. This picturesque town offers visitors a chance to experience its rich history, scenic countryside, and unique culture. With plenty of activities and attractions to choose from, Aure is the perfect destination for those who enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, or simply immersing themselves in the local way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Aure

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aure Museum

One of the must-visit places in Aure is the Aure Museum, which provides visitors with a glimpse into the town’s rich history. The museum showcases the traditional way of life in Aure through exhibits of traditional farming and fishing techniques, artifacts from ancient times, and typical life in the town.

Kvernes Stave Church

Another place of cultural significance in Aure is the Kvernes Stave Church. This beautiful and well-preserved church was built in the late 12th century and is located in a picturesque countryside location. The church is still used for services, and visitors can explore its interior and exterior at no charge.

Natural Attractions of Aure: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jøssundfjorden

For those who love to take in natural beauty, Jøssundfjorden is the place to go. This stunning fjord, which is surrounded by steep mountains, offers visitors the chance to go fishing or to take a scenic boat ride. Jøssundfjorden is also a popular spot for kayaking.

Hestfjellet Mountain

Hestfjellet Mountain is another popular destination for visitors to Aure. The mountain offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, and for those who love outdoor activities, there are several hiking trails that lead to the summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aure

For those who enjoy hiking or biking, Aure has no shortage of options. Hiking routes to the top of Hestfjellet Mountain are challenging but offer rewarding views. The Jøssundfjorden area also has several hiking and biking paths, and for the truly adventurous, the coastal area of Aure has trails that offer stunning views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Aure: Activities and Tours

Deep-Sea Fishing

Deep-sea fishing is one of the most popular activities in Aure, and visitors can book a tour for a chance to catch fish such as cod, mackerel, and salmon. Several companies offer guided tours, and all necessary equipment is provided.

Tour of Local Farms

For those who want to experience local agriculture, there are several guided farm tours in Aure. Visitors can learn how locals breed livestock, plant crops, and produce milk and cheese products. Some of the tour packages include tasting the fresh and locally sourced produce.

Food and Drink Scene of Aure

Must-Try Dishes

Fårikål

Fårikål is one of Aure’s most famous dishes, and it is a flavorful stew made with lamb, boiled cabbage, potatoes, and onions. Fårikål is usually served during the fall and is a staple dish in Norway.

Rømmegrøt

Rømmegrøt is a traditional Norwegian sour cream porridge that is typically served with salted meat or fish. The dish is creamy and savory, and it is commonly eaten for breakfast or lunch.

Local Beverages

Akevitt

Akevitt is a traditional Norwegian spirit made from potatoes or grains and flavored with a variety of herbs and spices. It is usually consumed as an after-dinner drink, and it is commonly referred to as “water of life”.

Norwegian Beer

Norwegian beer has become increasingly popular in recent years, and with several local breweries in the area, it’s easy to get your hands on a locally produced beer.

Where to Stay in Aure: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Aure have several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Hotel Aure has a great location in the town center, while Jøssundfjorden Camping is an excellent option for those who prefer a quieter stay near the fjord.

What to Buy in Aure: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Aure is known for its high-quality handicrafts, including woolen slippers, sweaters, and blankets. These items make for excellent souvenirs and gifts.

Seafood

Norway is famous for its fresh seafood, and Aure is no exception. Visitors can take home smoked salmon, codfish, or herring, which make great gifts for lovers of Nordic cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Aure

In Aure, as in the rest of Norway, punctuality is highly valued. If someone invites you to dinner at 6 pm, it’s essential to arrive on time. Additionally, unlike in other countries, it’s common in Norway to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Aure

The best time to visit Aure is from May to August when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, the fall months can be an excellent time to visit for those who want to take part in local festivals that include traditional Norwegian cuisine and customs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aure

Do:

  • Do be respectful of other people’s time and culture.
  • Do take off your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Do respect the environment, and always clean up after yourself when hiking or fishing.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in Aure; always dispose of your trash properly.
  • Don’t be late if someone invites you to dine with them.
  • Don’t be too loud or disruptive when staying in shared accommodations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aure

Norway is known for being one of the safest countries in the world, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still practice basic safety precautions such as using a safe in their hotel room, not carrying large amounts of cash, and always being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Aure

Norway has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors who need medical attention can visit the local hospital in Kristiansund, located just a short drive from Aure. Visitors should also pack warm clothing and rain gear for the cold and often wet Norwegian weather.

Getting to Aure: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aure

The nearest airport to Aure is Kristiansund Airport, which is located about 30 minutes away by car. Several regional and international airlines fly to Kristiansund, including Wizz Air, Norwegian, and SAS.

Bus Stations Near Aure

Kristiansund Bus Terminal is the nearest bus station to Aure and is well-connected to several major cities in Norway, including Oslo and Bergen.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to travel by car can rent one from the Kristiansund Airport, or train travel to Trondheim and renting one there. Alternatively, a ferry runs from Trondheim to Kristiansund, providing scenic views of Norway’s coast and fjords.

How to Get Around in Aure

For visitors interested in getting around town, the local bus system provides convenient and affordable transportation. The bus services are operated by AtB and provide connections to Kristiansund, Trondheim, and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Aure: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Aure, visitors can explore other parts of the Møre og Romsdal region, including the charming town of Kristiansund, which is known for its colorful buildings and excellent seafood. Visitors can also take a boat tour of the famous Geirangerfjord or explore the beautiful beaches of Petroduralma.

Conclusion

From its scenic fjords and mountains to its rich cultural history and delicious cuisine, Aure has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide has provided visitors with a guide to the town’s best attractions, outdoor activities, and accommodations, as well as local customs and tips for a safe and convenient stay. With so much to see and do, Aure is truly a destination worth visiting when traveling to Norway.
