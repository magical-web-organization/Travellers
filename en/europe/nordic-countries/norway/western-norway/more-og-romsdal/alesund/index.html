Introduction to Ålesund

Ålesund is a coastal town located in Møre og Romsdal, Western Norway. The town is known for its unique Art Nouveau architecture, picturesque islands, and scenic views of the fjords. It is rich in culture and history and offers an abundance of outdoor activities and adventures.

In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, hikes, activities and tours, food and drink scene, where to stay, and cultural landmarks of Ålesund. So let’s begin!

List of Places to Visit in Ålesund

Discover the best attractions around Ålesund that must not be missed in any case.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jugendstilsenteret: Learn about the history and art of Art Nouveau in Ålesund at this museum.
  • Aksla Viewpoint: Visit this viewpoint for spectacular views of Ålesund and the surrounding fjords.
  • Atlanterhavsparken Aquarium: Explore the underwater world of Norway and learn about the marine life in the fjords.
  • Museum of Fisheries: Learn about the history of fisheries in Norway and its impact on the economy and culture.

Natural Attractions of Ålesund: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Aksla: Take a hike up this mountain for breathtaking panoramic views of the town and the fjords.
  • Borgundfjord: Take a scenic drive along this picturesque fjord for stunning views of the countryside.
  • Runde Island: Visit this island for bird-watching and seal-watching, and see the remarkable landscape of the coast.
  • Tueneset and Hessafjord: Take a relaxing walk in this scenic coastal area for stunning views of the fjords.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ålesund

Ålesund is a paradise for hikers and bikers with some of the most scenic routes and attractions.

  • Mt. Sukkertoppen Trail: A short, easy hike to the summit of Mt. Sukkertoppen with beautiful views of the fjords.
  • Løvenskioldvatnet Lake Trail: A scenic biking route from Ålesund to Løvenskioldvatnet Lake with beautiful countryside views.
  • Sykkeltur til Skodjevatnet: An adventurous route starting from Ålesund to Skodjevatnet Lake with scenic mountains and fjords.

Best Things to Do in Ålesund: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the fjords with a chance to see seals and puffins.
  • Kayaking: Explore the coastal area of Ålesund by kayak.
  • Fishing: Try fishing for salmon or cod in the fjords.
  • Skiing: Hit the slopes at the nearby ski resort of Strandafjellet.

Food and Drink Scene of Ålesund

Discover the local dishes and beverages that you must try in Ålesund.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rakfisk: A traditional Norwegian dish of fermented trout.
  • Fårikål: A hearty stew made with lamb, cabbage, and potatoes.
  • Brunost: A unique Norwegian brown cheese made from goat’s milk.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A popular Norwegian spirit made with herbs and spices.
  • Øl: Try local beers from Ålesund’s popular breweries like Bryggeri 13.

Where to Stay in Ålesund: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of options for accommodation in Ålesund, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Hotel Brosundet: An upscale hotel located in a renovated 1916 building with views of the sea.
  • Comfort Hotel Nobel: A modern hotel with bright rooms and stunning views of the fjords.
  • Ålesund Vandrerhjem: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of the city, ideal for backpackers.

What to Buy in Ålesund: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Ålesund home with you in the form of souvenirs and local products.

  • Art Nouveau-inspired souvenirs: Purchase souvenirs inspired by the town’s Art Nouveau architecture.
  • Local seafood and cheeses: Bring back some of the local delicacies such as smoked salmon and brown cheese.
  • Knives and handmade crafts: Shop for handmade Norwegian knives and other traditional crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Ålesund

Get familiar with the local culture and customs of Ålesund and enjoy it like a local.

  • Reserved nature: Norwegians respect and appreciate nature, and visitors should follow suit by not littering or disturbing wildlife.
  • Respect for privacy: Norwegians are private individuals and value their personal space, and visitors should respect their privacy and personal boundaries.

Best Time to Visit Ålesund

The best time to visit Ålesund is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm, and the days are long. However, the town’s charming winter scenes should not be missed either.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ålesund

Do:

  • Respect nature
  • Respect privacy
  • Dress warmly in winter
  • Try local delicacies

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Disturb wildlife
  • Act inappropriately in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Ålesund

Ålesund is an extremely safe town, with a low crime rate.

Health in Ålesund

The healthcare system in Ålesund is excellent, and visitors can rest assured in case of any health emergencies.

Getting to Ålesund: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Ålesund is easy and convenient with many transportation options available.

Airports Near Ålesund

  • Ålesund Airport: Located just 17 km from the town and serviced by major airlines like SAS and Norwegian.

Bus Stations Near Ålesund

  • Ålesund Bus Terminal: Located right in the heart of the town, and serviced by local bus companies like NOR-WAY Bussekspress and Vy.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is located in Åndalsnes, about 2 hours away by bus or car.

How to Get Around in Ålesund

Getting around Ålesund is easy thanks to its excellent public transportation system.

  • Buses: A network of buses operates regularly within Ålesund and the surrounding area.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Ålesund, but they can be expensive.
  • Biking: Rent a bike and explore the town and nearby countryside at your own leisure.

Where to Go Next from Ålesund: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Extend your stay in Norway with these exciting destinations that are a short distance from Ålesund.

  • Bergen: A picturesque coastal town known for its colorful wooden houses and scenic views of the fjords, accessible via a 5-hour drive or 6-hour scenic train ride.
  • Geirangerfjord: A famous fjord with stunning waterfalls and scenic views, accessible via a ferry or a day trip from Ålesund.
  • Trollstigen: A scenic mountain road with breathtaking views and hairpin turns, accessible via a 2-hour drive from Ålesund.

Conclusion

Ålesund is a stunning destination in Norway that is sure to captivate visitors with its unique Art Nouveau architecture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. From exploring museums and historical landmarks to hiking and kayaking, there are plenty of things to see and do in Ålesund. So plan your trip now and embark on an exciting adventure in this beautiful corner of the world.
