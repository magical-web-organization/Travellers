Introduction to Valldal

Valldal is a peaceful village nestled in the heart of Møre og Romsdal, Western Norway. It is a well-known and popular destination for people who love outdoor activities. The village is situated at the end of the picturesque valley Valldalen, surrounded by breathtaking mountains and the magnificent fjord. Valldal offers its visitors a unique combination of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide for Valldal, we will explore its must-visit places, entertainment activities, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture, safety and health, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Valldal

Valldal offers a wide range of places for visitors to explore, each with its own unique charm and history. Here are some of the must-visit places in Valldal:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Valldal Activity Park: The activity park has an adventure park, a climbing wall, zip lines, and a children’s playground. It also offers guided tours to explore the Valldal valley.
  2. Norwegian Fjord Centre: The Norwegian Fjord Centre is a museum that showcases the history and culture of the fjords. Visitors can learn about the area’s unique flora and fauna, geological formations, and the life of the local people who have lived in the fjord region for centuries.
  3. Borgund Church: The Borgund Church is a stave church dating back to the 12th century. It is one of the most well-preserved stave churches in Norway, with intricate carvings and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of Valldal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Trollstigen: Trollstigen is a popular tourist attraction in Norway, known for its stunning views and winding roads. Visitors can hike or take a car up the mountain to see the beautiful vistas.
  2. Geirangerfjord: The Geirangerfjord is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its pristine waters and steep mountains. Visitors can take a cruise to explore the fjord and witness its beauty up close.
  3. Valldal Naturopplevingar: Valldal Naturopplevingar is an outdoor adventure company that offers hiking, kayaking, and rafting tours. Visitors can explore the natural beauty of Valldal while getting an adrenaline rush.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valldal

Valldal offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for people of all skill levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Valldal Rundt: This hiking trail takes visitors around Valldal village, showcasing its stunning scenery, including the river, mountains, and fjord.
  2. Romsdalseggen Ridge: The Romsdalseggen Ridge trail offers some of the most incredible views of Norway’s fjords and mountains. Although challenging, it is a must-do for those who can make it.
  3. Tafjordfjella: Tafjordfjella is a vast mountain range that is perfect for hiking, camping, and skiing. The Tafjordfjella National Park offers several well-marked trails of varying difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Valldal: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities that visitors can enjoy in Valldal:

  1. Fishing: Valldal is an excellent destination for fishing enthusiasts. The fjord has a wealth of fish species, including salmon, trout, and cod. Visitors can participate in organized fishing trips or fish independently.
  2. Fruit Picking: Valldal is famous for its fruit farms, and visitors can participate in fruit-picking activities. The farms offer a plethora of fruits, including strawberries, raspberries, cherries, and apples.
  3. Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is an excellent way to explore the natural beauty of Valldal. Visitors can take a guided tour of the area on horseback or ride independently.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valldal

For visitors who plan to stay for a week, here is a sample itinerary for Valldal:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Norwegian Fjord Centre and go hiking around Valldal village.
Day 2 Take a hiking tour of Trollstigen, exploring its winding roads and panoramic views.
Day 3 Explore the Geirangerfjord by boat.
Day 4 Participate in an outdoor adventure tour with Valldal Naturopplevingar, such as kayaking or rafting.
Day 5 Take a guided horseback tour through the area.
Day 6 Go fishing in the fjord or visit a fruit farm to pick fresh fruit.
Day 7 Relax and explore Valldal at your own pace. Visit Borgund Church or take a leisurely bike ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Valldal

Valldal has several restaurants and cafes that offer traditional Norwegian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Norwegian Salmon: Norwegian salmon is famous worldwide, and Valldal is no exception. Visitors can try freshly caught salmon cooked in traditional Norwegian style.
  2. Reindeer: Reindeer is a popular dish in Norway, and visitors can try this unique meat in Valldal.
  3. Brynjekake: Brynjekake is a traditional cake made with cream, sugar, and almonds. It is a must-try dessert in Valldal.

Local Beverages

  1. Akevitt: Akevitt is a traditional Norwegian spirit made with potatoes and caraway seeds. It is commonly served as a pre-dinner drink and pairs well with fish dishes.
  2. Cider: Valldal is famous for its apple orchards, and visitors can try locally brewed cider.
  3. Karsk: Karsk is a potent alcoholic beverage made with coffee and moonshine. It is a popular drink during the winter months and is said to keep you warm.

Where to Stay in Valldal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valldal has several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Valldal:

  1. Valldal Fjordhotell: The Valldal Fjordhotell is a luxury hotel located by the fjord. It offers a restaurant, bar, spa, and several outdoor activities, including kayaking, hiking, and fishing.
  2. Valldal Camping: Valldal Camping is a budget-friendly option that offers several types of accommodation, such as cabins, tents, and caravans.
  3. Valldal Aktivitetspark: The Valldal Aktivitetspark offers several types of accommodation, including cabins and dorms. It is an excellent choice for visitors who want to participate in outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Valldal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Valldal is famous for its fruit farms, and visitors can buy locally made products, such as jams, juices, and ciders. Visitors can also buy traditional Norwegian souvenirs, such as Viking-inspired jewelry, Sami handicrafts, or traditional knitwear.

Local Culture and Customs of Valldal

Valldal has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique mix of Norwegian, Sami, and Viking history. The local people are proud of their traditions and customs, and visitors can experience this by participating in cultural events and visiting historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Valldal

Valldal is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm. However, the best time to visit Valldal is from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valldal

Do:

  1. Respect the local people and their customs.
  2. Dress appropriately for the outdoor activities you plan to participate in.
  3. Try the traditional Norwegian cuisine and locally brewed beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  2. Approach wild animals, as they can be dangerous.
  3. Ignore the weather warnings and safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valldal

Valldal is a safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities. Always follow the safety guidelines and weather warnings provided by local authorities.

Health in Valldal

Valldal has a modern healthcare system, with several hospitals and health centers. Visitors from the European Union can use their European Health Insurance Cards (EHICs) to access healthcare services in Valldal.

Getting to Valldal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Valldal is easily accessible by several means of transportation:

Airports Near Valldal

The nearest airport to Valldal is Ålesund Airport, Vigra (AES), which is about 60 km away from Valldal. There are regular flights to Ålesund from Oslo and Bergen.

Bus Stations Near Valldal

Visitors can take buses from Ålesund to Valldal. The journey takes about 1 hour and 30 minutes. There are also several buses from Oslo and Bergen to Ålesund.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Valldal, as the village is connected to the E136 road. Rental cars are available at Ålesund Airport.

How to Get Around in Valldal

The best way to get around Valldal is by car or bike. Visitors can also walk to explore the village. For outdoor activities, participating in organized tours is the safest option.

Where to Go Next from Valldal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore from Valldal:

  1. Ålesund: Ålesund is a picturesque coastal city known for its Art Nouveau architecture. It is only an hour’s drive away from Valldal.
  2. Andalsnes: Andalsnes is a small town located at the end of the Romsdalsfjorden. It is a popular destination for hiking and offers the starting point of the Romsdalseggen Ridge hike.
  3. Sunndalsøra: Sunndalsøra is a town located on the Sunndalsfjord, known for its salmon fishing and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Valldal is a gem in Western Norway, offering its visitors a blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural heritage. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will make your trip planning a bit easier and help you make the most out of your visit to Valldal. Whatever you do, don’t forget to take in Valldal’s stunning views and unique atmosphere while you’re there.
