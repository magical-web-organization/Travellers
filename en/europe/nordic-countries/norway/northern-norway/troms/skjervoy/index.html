A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Skjervøy, Norway

Nestled in the breathtaking region of Troms, in Northern Norway, lies the charming coastal town of Skjervøy. With its stunning vistas, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor adventures, Skjervøy has become a popular destination among travelers from all across the globe. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore all the things Skjervøy has to offer, from its natural beauty to its cultural highlights, making it the perfect location for your next adventure.

Introduction to Skjervøy

Welcome to Skjervøy, located in the Northern region of Norway. Also known as the “Gateway to the Arctic,” Skjervøy is a small coastal town with towering mountains and beautiful views of the sea. The town’s history dates back to 1855 when it was established as a market town. Today, Skjervøy has become one of the top tourist destinations in Northern Norway, attracting visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Skjervøy

If you’re planning a visit to Skjervøy, make sure to add the following places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Skjervøy boasts a rich cultural heritage, which can be seen in its numerous museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Skjervøy Church: Built-in 1935, this striking building stands out thanks to its unique shape and intricate decoration. Visitors can admire the beautiful stained glass windows and impressive altar made from Norwegian marble.
  • Skjervøy Coastal Museum: This open-air museum showcases the coastal way of life in Northern Norway. Featuring stunning displays of fishing boats, equipment, and traditional fishing cabins, the museum gives visitors a glimpse into the area’s vibrant history.
  • Sommarøy Island: Just a short ferry ride from Skjervøy, Sommarøy Island is a picturesque fishing village famous for its pristine beaches and clear blue waters.

Natural Attractions of Skjervøy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Skjervøy is renowned for its natural beauty, making it ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Whale Safari: Taking a whale safari is one of the best ways to experience the beauty of Skjervøy’s marine life. Watch as majestic humpback, orca, and minke whales swim in their natural habitat.
  • Hiking: Skjervøy is surrounded by towering mountains, showcasing some of the most spectacular hikes in Norway. Kågen island, Arnøya Island and Jægervatnet is the most popular.
  • Northern Lights: Skjervøy is located in the Aurora belt and attracts visitors from all over the world who come to experience the Northern Lights in all their glory.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skjervøy

One of the most popular activities in Skjervøy is hiking, making it the perfect location for adventurers. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes:

  • Mount Storhaugen: This hike provides a challenge, taking visitors to the highest peak on Arnøya Island. On reaching the peak, visitors will enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kågtind Mountain: This path will take you to the top of Kågen island’s highest mountain at 884 meters providing a stunning view.
  • Biking: Explore Skjervøy’s idyllic landscape on two wheels along the islands of Arnøya Island or Kågen Island, enjoying views of the water and the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Skjervøy: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring museums and historical sites, Skjervøy hosts a number of unique activities and tours, including:

  • Midnight sun kayaking: Experience the unique beauty of Skjervøy in the middle of the night. Paddle your kayak into the calm waters while you enjoy the midnight sun, in spring and summer season.
  • Sami Culture Tour: Discover the vibrant culture of the Sami people, Northern Norway’s historical ethnic minority. Learn about their traditional way of life and how they have adapted over the years.
  • Dog Sledding: Glide across the snowy terrain as you’re pulled on a sled by a team of huskies exploring the winter wonderland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skjervøy

To help you make the most of your stay in Skjervøy, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Skjervøy, walk through the city center and visit Skjervøy Church.
  • Day 2: Take a whale safari and spend the afternoon exploring Skjervøy’s museums and historical landmarks.
  • Day 3: Visit Sommarøy Island and its white sand beaches, go hiking or fishing.
  • Day 4: Go dog sledding or snowmobiling in the winter wonderland with Lyngen alps as a backdrop.
  • Day 5: Go hiking, take a bike tour or spot the Northern Lights.
  • Day 6: Take a cultural tour and learn about the Sami people or book a kayaking tour.
  • Day 7: Explore Skjervøy’s surroundings, visiting Kågen or Arnøya Island for more scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Skjervøy

When it comes to food and drink, Skjervøy has some unique local dishes and delicacies that warrant trying.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stockfish: This traditional dish is a Norwegian specialty that involves drying out common fish like cod, which is then preserved and rehydrated to make a healthy meal rich in proteins and vitamins.
  • Arctic char: The Arctic Char is a fresh fish, with white-pink meat that provides a delicious culinary experience.
  • Kveite: Halibut from local deep waters, often pan-fried or baked, served in a variety of ways reflecting the local culture.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: Norway’s national liquor is also highly present in Skjervøy, its unique flavour comes from the production process, which often includes herbs and spices like caraway and dill.
  • Beer: Skjervøy has its own micro-brewery, Reinøy Brygghus, producing its unique beer.

Where to Stay in Skjervøy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Regardless of your budget or preferences, Skjervøy has a range of accommodation options to fit your needs. These include:

  • Monstervik Arctic Lodge: Built-in traditional style but with comfort, conveniently located with great views over the Skjervøy Island.
  • Amfi Skjervøy Kystferie: This beautiful property sits right on the seafront, offering breathtaking views of the water and the surrounding mountains.
  • Skjervøy Hostel: For those looking to explore Skjervøy on a budget, this hostel provides a perfect compromise between affordability and comfort.

What to Buy in Skjervøy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re on the hunt for some authentic local souvenirs, try these shops:

  • Hemsen Handel: Located in the heart of Skjervøy, this store offers a range of handmade gifts and clothing made by local artists and crafters.
  • Kajakk & Fritid: This store has a range of outdoor gear and equipment to tackle Skjervøy’s rugged terrain.
  • Skjervøy Gardsysteri: A cheese farm where you can purchase different varieties of cheeses and other dairy products made by locals in the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Skjervøy

Skjervøy boasts a unique and fascinating local culture shaped by its isolation and Arctic climate. Some of the customs and traditions worth exploring include:

  • Sami culture: The indigenous Sami community in Northern Norway has a rich culture and history worth exploring. Learn about their traditional music, myths, and customs at the Sami museum.
  • Midnight sun: From early May to mid-August, Skjervøy has 24 hours of daylight; this plays a significant role in the local culture and the locals’ work routine.

Best Time to Visit Skjervøy

The best time to visit Skjervøy depends on what you’re drawn to. In the summer, the town comes alive with activities and festivals, while winter provides visitors with stunning views of the Northern Lights. It’s best to plan your travels sometime in the months from May to September to experience optimal weather conditions and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skjervøy

Before visiting Skjervøy, here are some essential things you should know:

Do:

  • Bring sturdy shoes and warm clothes.
  • Respect the locals, culture, and nature; remember that you are a guest in their community.
  • Protect the environment; pick up your litter, and leave the surroundings as found.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the Arctic weather conditions; it changes hourly so be prepared for all situations.
  • Don’t venture off hiking trails without a guide.
  • Don’t forget the basics like sunscreen and mosquito repellent in summer.

Safety and Health

When visiting Skjervøy, being adequately prepared and informed is essential for your safety and wellbeing. Here are some tips:

Safety in Skjervøy

  • Always have a reliable GPS and map, informing somebody of your planned route beforehand, especially if you are hiking.
  • Be aware of the dangers while kayaking in the polar region.
  • Pay close attention to weather warnings and prepare accordingly.

Health in Skjervøy

  • Always drink bottled water.
  • Don’t overlook applying mosquito repellent while hiking or outdoors.
  • Have your insurance up to date before traveling.

Getting to Skjervøy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Skjervøy is a bit remote, but there are several options to travel to the town.

Airports Near Skjervøy

  • Tromsø Airport, Langnes (TOS) located 80 km from Skjervøy is the nearest airport with connections to Oslo.

Bus Stations Near Skjervøy

  • Skjervøy Bus Terminal, located in the heart of town, has buses running to Tromsø, Alta, and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Ferry boat: a coastal express-dock in Skjervøy with routes between Kirkenes and Bergen.
  • Hurtigruten, a Norwegian cruise line, also stops in Skjervøy.

How to Get Around in Skjervøy

While in Skjervøy, the choice of transport will depend on your chosen activities, here are the options:

  • Public transport, local buses in town or hiring a car can be found in Tromsø or connect at Skjervøy Bus Terminal.
  • Bicycle rental is available from the Amfi Skjervøy Shopping Center or Kajakk & Fritid.
  • Boat rental or kayaks are also available.

Where to Go Next from Skjervøy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more adventure, Skjervøy is an excellent base from which to explore the surrounding areas, including:

  • Nordkapp: Due to its location, Skjervøy is a perfect staging ground when planning a trip to the stunning Nordkapp.
  • Tromsø: Catch a bus from Skjervøy Bus Terminal to Tromsø, also known as the “Paris of the North,” and explore the city’s museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • The Lyngen alps: A very popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts, the Lyngen alps are also home to stunning scenery, with snowcapped peaks and clear Arctic skies.

Conclusion

Skjervøy has so much to offer, from its natural beauty and outdoor activities to its rich cultural heritage and culinary delights. We hope this comprehensive travel guide to Skjervøy has given you valuable insights on the town and has been helpful in planning your journey. Don’t hesitate to explore this unique Norwegian gem and make it a destination of your next adventure.
