Introduction to Troms

Troms is a region located in Northern Norway, known for its stunning outdoor landscapes, cultural landmarks, and vibrant Northern Lights. The region also offers an array of activities such as skiing, hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Troms is a popular tourist destination, especially during the winter months, but it also attracts visitors all year round. This guide will provide a detailed look at Troms, including its must-visit attractions, local culture, and practical tips for a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Troms

To fully experience Troms, there are several must-visit attractions that travelers should not miss. Here are some of the most prominent cultural and natural landmarks to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you want to explore Troms’ fascinating history and culture, you should visit these places in Troms:

  • Tromsø Cathedral
  • Tromsø Museum
  • Polaria

Tromsø Cathedral is a historic landmark in Tromsø city center and is designed in Gothic Revival-style architecture. The Museum provides a chance for visitors to learn about the history and culture of Troms and its people. Polaria is an Arctic aquarium and museum that provides an interactive experience of the Northern Lights, an ice cave, and many polar animals.

Natural Attractions of Troms: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Troms also offers many breathtaking natural landmarks and outdoor activities, including:

  • Northern Lights
  • Fjords
  • Målselv Mountain Village
  • Lyngen Alps
  • Ersfjord Beach

The Northern Lights is one of Norway’s natural wonders, and Troms is an excellent place to witness this magical phenomenon. The region also has several fjords to explore, such as the Lyngenfjord. Målselv Mountain Village, located in the Målselv Valley, is an ideal place to take a break from the city’s hustle and bustle and enjoy the scenic mountains. The Lyngen Alps offer a wide range of outdoor activities, including skiing and hiking, while Ersfjord Beach is a peaceful spot to enjoy a relaxing stroll or a swim in the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Troms

Troms is an excellent destination for those who love hiking and biking, and there are several routes to explore. Some popular hiking trails include:

  • Tromsdalstinden
  • Blåmannsisen Glacier
  • Kvaløya Island
  • Senja Island

Tromsdalstinden is Tromsø’s highest mountain, and the hike takes about seven hours to complete. Blåmannsisen Glacier, located in Lyngenfjord, is perfect for a challenging hike and offers stunning views. If you prefer a shorter hike, you can explore Kvaløya Island or Senja Island, known for their beautiful landscapes and easier trails.

Best Things to Do in Troms: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Troms offers many other activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Troms:

  • Dog sledding
  • Reindeer sledding
  • Whale watching
  • Midnight Sun Kayaking
  • Skiing

Dog sledding is a must-try winter activity where you can experience the rush of dogs pulling you through the winter landscapes. Reindeer sledding is another popular winter activity that gives you the chance to learn more about the traditional Sámi culture. Troms is also a great place to spot whales, including humpback whales and orcas, in their natural habitat. During the summer, you can embark on a Midnight Sun Kayaking tour or ski in the Lyngen Alps in the winter.

Week-Long Itinerary for Troms

If you have a week to spend in Troms, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Tromsø city and visit Tromsø Cathedral and Polaria museum.
Day 2 Take a northern lights tour and enjoy the magical aurora lights.
Day 3 Experience dog sledding or reindeer sledding in the winter wonderland.
Day 4 Visit the Målselv Mountain Village and enjoy the scenic views.
Day 5 Embark on a whale watching tour from Tromsø.
Day 6 Go skiing or enjoy outdoor adventures in the Lyngen Alps.
Day 7 Relax at Ersfjord Beach or explore Kvaløya Island or Senja Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Troms

Troms offers a distinct Norwegian cuisine influenced by local and regional ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • King Crab
  • Reindeer meat
  • Fresh fish from local markets

King Crab is one of Troms’s famous seafood dishes and can be found in many restaurants. Reindeer meat is also a popular dish in Troms and is usually served with potatoes and lingonberry jam, a traditional Norwegian side dish. Local markets in Tromsø city offer fresh fish, including salmon, cod, and herring, which are delicious when prepared in traditional Norwegian ways.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit
  • Local beer

Aquavit is a traditional Norwegian spirit made with herbs and spices like caraway, anise, and fennel. Local beer is also popular in Troms and can be found in many pubs and bars. Craft breweries serve local and international beers.

Where to Stay in Troms: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Troms offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some options to consider:

  • Radisson Blu Hotel Tromsø
  • Comfort Hotel Xpress Tromsø
  • Tromsø Lodge & Camping
  • Mack Hotel

Radisson Blu Hotel Tromsø is a luxury hotel located in the city center and offers excellent amenities. If you’re looking for a budget-friendly option, Comfort Hotel Xpress Tromsø is an excellent choice. Tromsø Lodge & Camping is a great option for camping, and the Mack Hotel is perfect for beer lovers, as it overlooks the Mack Brewery.

What to Buy in Troms: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Troms offers many unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Handknit wool sweaters and mittens
  • Sami silver jewelry
  • Reindeer skin products

Handknit wool sweaters and mittens are perfect for cold weather, and Sami silver jewelry is a must-buy to showcase traditional Norwegian design. Reindeer skin products such as rugs and wallets can also be found in many local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Troms

The Troms region has a rich history and culture, and travelers should respect its customs. Some customs to consider while in Troms include:

  • Politeness and respect for elders
  • Proper hiking and outdoor etiquette
  • Sustainability

Norwegians value politeness and respecting elders, so it’s important to be mindful of this while interacting with locals. Hiking and outdoor etiquette are also essential, and travelers should follow proper trail rules and avoid littering. Travelers can be sustainable by carrying out their trash, taking eco-friendly transportation options, and learning more about Troms’s wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Troms

Troms is an all-year-round destination. However, the best time to visit depends on what experience you are looking for. The winter months (December-February) are great for those seeking winter activities such as the Northern lights, dog sledding, and skiing. The summer months (June-August) are perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and kayaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Troms

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling in Troms:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities
  • Try local cuisine and products

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Wander off the marked trails
  • Disrespect locals’ customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Travelers should consider their safety and health while in Troms. Here are some tips:

Safety in Troms

  • Check the weather before embarking on outdoor activities
  • Wear proper gear for outdoor activities
  • Follow the rules of the road while driving

Health in Troms

  • Norway has excellent healthcare facilities. bring copies of your medical history and travel insurance.
  • Use insect repellents as there are many mosquitos in Troms in some seasons
  • Use appropriate sunscreen to protect your skin from UV radiation.

Getting to Troms: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to reach Troms:

Airports Near Troms

  • Tromsø Airport
  • Harstad/Narvik Airport

Tromsø is the largest city in Troms County, and Tromsø airport is the best option for travelers flying domestically and internationally. Harstad/Narvik Airport is another airport but is located further south and may require additional travel time.

Bus Stations Near Troms

  • Tromsø Bus Station
  • Harstad Bus Station

Bus stations in Tromsø and Harstad serve several routes across Troms and neighboring regions, making them a convenient option for traveling.

Other Options

While flying and bus are the primary options, travelers can also reach Troms via train or ferry, which both offer a scenic journey along the coastlines of Norway.

How to Get Around in Troms

Troms offers several transportation options to explore the region, including:

  • Car rental
  • Bus
  • Bicycle
  • Boat tours

Travelers who want to explore Troms’s natural attractions in-depth might prefer renting a car or joining a boat tour, while bus and bicycle options might be best for those planning to stay in Tromsø and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Troms: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Troms is a great base to explore other nearby destinations, such as:

  • Lofoten Islands
  • Svalbard Archipelago
  • North Cape

In addition to these destinations, travelers can also explore other coastal towns and cities, including Harstad and Bodø, both offering scenic landscapes and authentic Norwegian culture.

Conclusion

Troms offers an array of cultural and natural attractions in a pristine Arctic environment. From hiking to skiing and experiencing the Northern Lights, Troms has something for everyone. This guide will help you plan your trip to Troms, including must-visit attractions and practical tips to make the most of your holiday in Northern Norway.
