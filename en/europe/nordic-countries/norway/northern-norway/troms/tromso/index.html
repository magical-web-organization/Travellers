Introduction to Tromsø

Located in the Arctic Circle in Northern Norway, Tromsø is a vibrant and modern city that offers a unique mix of urban attractions, breathtaking natural wonders, and rich cultural heritage. Known as the “Gateway to the Arctic,” Tromsø is a popular destination for travelers who want to explore the Arctic wilderness, witness the spectacular Northern Lights, enjoy outdoor adventures, and learn about the local Sami culture.

List of Places to Visit in Tromsø

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tromsø is home to several museums and historical sites that provide insight into the city’s fascinating past and present. The must-visit cultural attractions in Tromsø include:

  • The Tromsø Museum: This world-renowned museum is dedicated to the history, culture, and natural environment of Northern Norway and the Arctic. The museum’s exhibits showcase the Sami culture, Arctic ecosystems, and the region’s history and industry.

  • The Perspektivet Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Tromsø and the surrounding areas through photographs, paintings, and artifacts.

  • Tromsø Cathedral: Built-in 1861, this beautiful cathedral is the northernmost Protest church in the world. The cathedral’s stunning stained-glass windows depict scenes from the Bible, and its interior is adorned with intricate woodcarvings.

Natural Attractions of Tromsø: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tromsø is surrounded by pristine wilderness and stunning natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The top natural attractions in Tromsø include:

  • The Arctic Cathedral: This striking modernist cathedral is one of Tromsø’s most iconic landmarks. The cathedral’s unique triangular shape and stunning glass mosaic façade make it a must-visit attraction.

  • Fjellheisen Cable Car: This cable car offers breathtaking views of Tromsø and the surrounding mountains and fjords. The cable car takes visitors to the top of Mount Storsteinen, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the city.

  • Polaria: This arctic aquarium and science center is dedicated to the Arctic’s wildlife and climate. Visitors can see live seals, touch real snow, and learn about the region’s geography, technology, and ecology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tromsø

Tromsø offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the city’s natural beauty and rugged terrain. The most popular hiking and biking routes in Tromsø include:

  • The Tromsø Island Walk: This easy and picturesque walk takes visitors around the city’s stunning coastline, past beaches, parks, and historic sites.

  • The Cable Car Hike: This challenging hike takes visitors to the top of Mount Storsteinen via the Fjellheisen Cable Car. The trail offers stunning views of Tromsø and the surrounding mountains.

  • The Arctic Alpine Garden: This botanical garden on the island of Tromsøya offers visitors a chance to see the Arctic’s rare and unique flora. Visitors can walk through the garden’s pathways and learn about the region’s plants and natural habitats.

Best Things to Do in Tromsø: Activities and Tours

Tromsø offers a wide range of activities and tours designed to introduce visitors to the city’s rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. The best things to do and see in Tromsø include:

  • Aurora Hunting: Tromsø is one of the best places in the world to see the Northern Lights. Visitors can take guided tours that take them to the best viewing spots and offer tips for photographing the aurora borealis.

  • Dog Sledding: This popular activity allows visitors to experience the Arctic wilderness and travel like the locals. Visitors can take guided dog sled tours through the mountains and forests surrounding Tromsø.

  • Midnight Sun Kayaking: During the summer months, Tromsø experiences nearly 24 hours of daylight. Visitors can take guided kayaking tours that take them around the city’s coastline and fjords to experience the midnight sun.

If you have a week to spend in Tromsø, here’s a suggested itinerary that will allow you to see the city’s best attractions and experience its unique culture and outdoor adventures:

Food and Drink Scene of Tromsø

Tromsø has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the Arctic’s unique culinary traditions and local ingredients. The must-try dishes and beverages in Tromsø include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fårikål: This traditional Norwegian stew is made with lamb, cabbage, and potatoes and is a staple of the Norwegian diet.

  • Røkt Laks: Tromsø is renowned for its smoked salmon, which is commonly served as an appetizer or main dish in local restaurants.

  • Reindeer Meat: Reindeer meat is a specialty in Tromsø and is often served as a steak or in a stew.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: This traditional Norwegian spirit is flavored with herbs and spices and is often served as an aperitif.

  • Karsk: This Northern Norwegian drink is made by mixing coffee and moonshine and is a popular beverage among locals.

  • Beer: Tromsø has several local breweries that produce high-quality craft beers, including Mack Brewery, the oldest brewery in Norway.

Where to Stay in Tromsø: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tromsø offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. The best places to stay in Tromsø include:

  • Clarion Hotel The Edge: This modern and luxurious hotel offers stunning views of Tromsø and the surrounding mountains and fjords.

  • Tromsø Lodge & Camping: This budget-friendly accommodation option is located in a picturesque setting on the island of Kvaløya and offers cabins, camping sites, and hostel rooms.

  • Smarthotel Tromsø: This centrally located hotel offers affordable rooms with modern amenities and is within walking distance of Tromsø’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Tromsø: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tromsø offers several unique and authentic products that make great souvenirs or gifts for friends and family. The best products to buy in Tromsø include:

  • Sami Handicrafts: The Sami people are the indigenous people of Northern Norway and are famous for their traditional handicrafts, including woolen clothing, jewelry, and bags.

  • Arctic Seafood: Tromsø is renowned for its fresh and flavorful seafood, including smoked salmon, king crab, and shrimp.

  • Traditional Norwegian Sweaters: Norwegian sweaters are known for their intricate designs and high quality and make a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Tromsø

The people of Tromsø are proud of their unique cultural heritage and traditions, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect these customs. Some of the local culture and customs of Tromsø include:

  • The Sami Culture: The Sami people are the indigenous people of Northern Norway and have their own language, customs, and traditions. Visitors can learn about the Sami culture through museums and guided tours.

  • Midnight Sun: During the summer months, Tromsø experiences nearly 24 hours of daylight due to its location north of the Arctic Circle.

  • Northern Lights: Tromsø is one of the best places in the world to see the Aurora Borealis or Northern Lights, a natural phenomenon that occurs in the winter months.

Best Time to Visit Tromsø

Tromsø is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. The best time to visit Tromsø for:

  • Outdoor Adventures and Midnight Sun: Late May to July.

  • Northern Lights and Winter Sports: Late November to early April.

  • Cultural Festivals: June to August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tromsø

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, including the Sami culture.

  • Be prepared for the weather, as Tromsø experiences extreme temperatures and weather conditions.

  • Take guided tours and activities to learn about the local history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or the natural environment.

  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.

  • Assume that everyone speaks English, as Norwegian is the official language in Tromsø.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tromsø

Tromsø is generally a safe and welcoming destination for travelers, but visitors should be aware of the potential risks and take appropriate precautions, such as:

  • Dressing appropriately for the weather.

  • Avoiding areas that are known for crime or unsafe road conditions.

  • Taking guided tours when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Tromsø

Tromsø has a modern and well-equipped healthcare system that provides high-quality medical care for residents and visitors. Visitors should:

  • Have appropriate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and repatriation.

  • Seek medical attention if they experience any health problems or injuries.

  • Be aware of the potential risks associated with extreme temperatures and weather conditions.

Getting to Tromsø: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tromsø can be reached by air, train, bus, and car. The most popular ways to get to Tromsø are:

Airports Near Tromsø

  • Tromsø Airport Langnes: This is the main airport in the region, with domestic and international flights to major cities in Norway and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Tromsø

  • Tromsø Bus Station: This is the main bus terminal in Tromsø, with connections to other cities in Northern Norway and Sweden.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Narvik, which is about 4 hours away by car or bus.

  • Car: Tromsø is connected to other parts of Norway by several major highways, including the E6 and E8.

How to Get Around in Tromsø

Tromsø is a small and walkable city that can easily be explored on foot. However, visitors can also use public transportation or taxis to get around the city. The most popular transportation options in Tromsø include:

  • City Buses: Tromsø has a reliable public bus system that operates throughout the city and its suburbs.

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Tromsø and can be hired on the street or by phone.

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent cars from several rental companies located in the city center or at Tromsø Airport.

Where to Go Next from Tromsø: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tromsø is a great base for exploring the Arctic region and visiting nearby destinations. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Tromsø include:

  • Svalbard: This stunning archipelago is located between Norway and the North Pole and is known for its polar bears, glaciers, and stunning natural beauty.

  • Senja: This scenic island is located south of Tromsø and is famous for its rugged coastline, fjords, and mountains.

  • Narvik: This historic town is located south of Tromsø and is known for its WWII museum, railway, and ski resort.

Conclusion

Tromsø is a fascinating and unique destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you want to explore the Arctic wilderness, witness the Northern Lights, learn about the Sami culture, or enjoy outdoor adventures, Tromsø has it all. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Tromsø and discover all that this amazing destination has to offer. Happy travels!
