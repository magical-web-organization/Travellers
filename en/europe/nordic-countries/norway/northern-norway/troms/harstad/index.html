Introduction to Harstad

Harstad is a picturesque municipality located in the Troms region of Northern Norway. The town is small but charming, filled with friendly locals who are always happy to show visitors around. Harstad is surrounded by beautiful fjords and scenic mountains that make it an ideal location for outdoor enthusiasts. The town’s rich history and cultural attractions are also a draw for many visitors.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the must-see attractions in Harstad, the best places to stay, the food and drink scene, and much more. So, let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Harstad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trondenes Historical Center

The Trondenes Historical Center is a museum and exhibition center that documents the region’s history. The center is located in an old whaling station that dates back to the 19th century. Visitors can explore the indoor exhibitions as well as the outdoor open-air museum, which includes a reconstructed Viking longhouse.

Adolf Gun

The Adolf Gun is a German coastal artillery battery that was built during World War II. The gun was intended to protect the town from Allied forces, but it was never used. Today, it is open to visitors who can tour the site and learn about its history.

Grottebadet

Grottebadet, located on the island of Grytøya, is a cave system that was used as a public bathhouse in the 1950s. The cave is now a popular tourist attraction where visitors can see the rock formations and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Harstad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Keipen

Keipen is a mountain that stands at 884 meters above sea level. It is one of the tallest mountains in the region and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. Visitors can hike to the top of Keipen for a breathtaking view of Harstad and the nearby fjords.

Tjeldsundet Bridge

The Tjeldsundet Bridge is a suspension bridge that connects Harstad to the town of Tjeldsund. The bridge offers stunning views of the fjords and mountains and is a popular spot for photography.

Gressholmen Beach

Gressholmen Beach is a beautiful white sand beach that is located on the island of Andøya, just a short drive from Harstad. The beach is surrounded by mountains and has crystal-clear water that is perfect for swimming. It’s a great place to spend a relaxing day on the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harstad

Harstad has an abundance of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Keipen: This mountain hike is challenging but offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Grytøya Island Trail: This hiking trail takes visitors through the dense forest and along the coast of Grytøya Island.
  • Harstadmarka: The Harstadmarka trail system is perfect for mountain biking. The area has a range of different trails that offer varying levels of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Harstad: Activities and Tours

Northern Lights Safari

One of the most popular activities in Harstad is the Northern Lights Safari. Visitors can take a guided tour in search of the elusive Aurora Borealis. The tour guides are knowledgeable and know the best spots to see the lights.

Sea Eagle Safari

The Sea Eagle Safari is a unique experience that takes visitors out to sea to see the region’s largest bird of prey: the sea eagle. The tour offers stunning views of the coastline and plenty of opportunities to see the eagles in action.

Fishing Tour

Fishing is a popular activity in Harstad, and visitors can take a guided tour to try their luck at catching cod, halibut, and other fish. The guides are knowledgeable and will take visitors to the best fishing spots in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Harstad

Must-Try Dishes

Fårikål

Fårikål is a traditional Norwegian dish made with lamb and cabbage. It’s a hearty, flavorful dish that is perfect for the colder months.

Rømmegrøt

Rømmegrøt is a Norwegian porridge that is typically served with butter and brown sugar. It’s a comforting, creamy dish that is perfect for breakfast or as a dessert.

Rakfisk

Rakfisk is a fermented fish dish that is a delicacy in Norway. It’s made by fermenting trout or Arctic char in brine and is often served with bread, potatoes, and sour cream.

Local Beverages

Aquavit

Aquavit is a traditional Scandinavian spirit that is made with potatoes or grain and flavored with herbs and spices. It’s typically served as a shot and is often consumed as part of a traditional Norwegian meal.

Beer

Norway has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors to Harstad will find a range of local beers on tap at the town’s many pubs and bars.

Where to Stay in Harstad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harstad has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Some popular options include:

  • Clarion Collection Hotel Arcticus: This hotel is located in the heart of Harstad and offers modern, comfortable rooms.
  • Thon Hotel Harstad: This hotel is located near the town center and offers spacious, well-appointed rooms and a free breakfast buffet.
  • Gullhaugen Pensjonat: This guesthouse is located just outside of Harstad and offers cozy, traditional rooms.

What to Buy in Harstad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lefse

Lefse is a traditional Norwegian flatbread that is made with potatoes and flour. It’s often served with butter and sugar and is a popular souvenir for visitors to Harstad.

Wool Products

Norway is known for its high-quality wool products, and visitors to Harstad can find a range of handmade wool hats, sweaters, and mittens in local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Harstad

Norwegian culture is known for its emphasis on simplicity, honesty, and hard work. Visitors to Harstad can expect to find friendly locals who are eager to share their love of the outdoors and their rich cultural history.

Best Time to Visit Harstad

The best time to visit Harstad is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to take part in. Winter is also a popular time to visit for those who want to see the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harstad

Do:

  • Take a guided tour of the Trondenes Historical Center to learn about Harstad’s rich history.
  • Try the local seafood, which is some of the freshest in Norway.
  • Get outdoors and explore the region’s hiking trails and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone speaks English. It’s always good to learn a few basic Norwegian phrases before your trip.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Leave trash or litter behind when exploring the region’s natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harstad

Harstad is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing, and take appropriate precautions.

Health in Harstad

Norway has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors to Harstad can access medical care if needed. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance before embarking on any trip.

Getting to Harstad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harstad

Harstad/Narvik Airport, located 44 kilometers from Harstad, is the closest airport to the town. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or taxi to reach Harstad.

Bus Stations Near Harstad

Harstad has a bus station located in the town center, with connections to other towns in the region.

Other Options

Visitors to Harstad can also take the Hurtigruten, a coastal express ferry that runs between Bergen and Kirkenes, and stops in Harstad.

How to Get Around in Harstad

Harstad is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take the local bus to reach nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Harstad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lofoten Islands

The Lofoten Islands are a stunning archipelago located off the coast of Northern Norway. The islands are known for their dramatic landscapes and scenic fishing villages.

Tromsø

Tromsø is the largest town in Northern Norway and is known for its lively cultural scene and stunning views of the Northern Lights.

Conclusion

Harstad is a hidden gem in Norway, with stunning natural beauty and a rich cultural history. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture buff, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your trip to Harstad and discover all that this beautiful region has to offer.
