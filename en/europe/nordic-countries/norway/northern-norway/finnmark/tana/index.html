Introduction to Tana

Tana, located in the Finnmark region of Northern Norway, is home to the indigenous Sámi people. It is a popular destination for travelers who are looking for a nature-filled holiday experience with plenty of cultural and historical attractions to discover. Despite its remote location, Tana provides a unique chance to see pristine wilderness, enjoy local traditions and a rustic way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Tana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are looking for a deep dive into the culture and history of Tana, you can visit various museums and historical places in the town, such as:

  • Tana Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Sámi people. It has exhibits on traditional clothing, reindeer husbandry, and the Sámi way of life.

Natural Attractions of Tana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the museums and historical places, Tana offers some fantastic natural attractions you don’t want to miss. Here are the top ones:

  • Tana River: The Tana River is one of Norway’s biggest waterways, and it is known for its excellent salmon fishing. If you are up for a bit of an adventure, you can opt for a guided fishing trip. The river also offers beautiful hiking trails, especially during the summer.

  • Tana Bru: Tana Bru is a scenic place where you can enjoy the beauty of nature and do some sightseeing. You can take a walk at the suspension bridge and watch the river and the surrounding scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tana

Tana offers numerous hiking and biking trails that offer a chance to explore the wilderness of this region. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Tana River Delta Nature Reserve Trail: This nature reserve trail is an ideal destination for bird lovers or anyone who wants to explore the River Delta. The area includes wetlands, lakes, and rivers, which provide a home to various bird species. You can also see other wildlife like elk, reindeer, and foxes on this route.

Best things to do in Tana: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for some of the best tours to try out in Tana, then here are our top picks:

  • Reindeer sledding: If you want a truly authentic Sami experience, then you can try out reindeer sledding. This tour will take you through scenic forests and snowy landscapes as you enjoy the local customs of reindeer herding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tana

Tana has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained for a week. Here’s a sample itinerary of things to do during your week-long stay:

Food and Drink Scene of Tana

Unlike many tourist towns, Tana has more of a land-based cuisine focusing on reindeer meat and Arctic fish. Here are some local delicacies you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer meat: Reindeer meat is the primary source of protein for Sámi people, and it is a must-try if you are in Tana. You can sample this dish at most local restaurants in town.

Local Beverages

  • Sámi herbal tea: This tea is made from various herbs and berries that grow locally. You can try this tea at most local cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Tana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tana has various accommodation options for visitors, ranging from hotels, hostels to guest houses. Here are some of the best:

  • Tana Hotel: Tana Hotel is the premier hotel in the town of Tana, and it offers high-end rooms for visitors looking for a luxurious stay.

What to Buy in Tana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tana has some unique souvenirs and local products to buy as gifts for loved ones. Some of the top ones include:

  • Sámi jewelry: Sámi jewelry is unique, and you can find some fantastic pieces to take home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Tana

Tana is home to the Sámi people, and they have a rich culture and traditions. The Sámi people value the land, nature, and animals, and their customs revolve around these ideals.

Best Time to Visit Tana

The best time to visit Tana is from May to September, when the weather is mild and there is more daylight. During this period, you can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities and excursions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tana

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind during your Tana visit:

Do:

  • Explore the local culture and traditions.
  • Try out local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take the time to hike the scenic trails, especially during the summer season.

Don’t:

  • Do not disturb the wildlife.
  • Do not litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tana

Tana is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors are always advised to keep their belongings close to them and avoid going to the forest areas alone.

Health in Tana

Tana has excellent health facilities, and visitors can get adequate medical attention in case of emergencies.

Getting to Tana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tana is relatively remote, and to get there, you can opt for the following transport options:

Airports Near Tana

The town of Tana has no airport, and visitors can opt to fly to the nearest airport in Lakselv or Kirkenes, which are about a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Tana

Visitors can also opt for a bus ride to Tana from neighboring towns like Kirkenes, Vadsø, or Vadso.

How to Get Around in Tana

The most convenient way of getting around in Tana is by car or taxi. Many car rental companies operate in the town, and taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Tana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are planning to explore the surrounding areas of Tana, then here are some fantastic destinations and excursions to try out:

  • Kirkenes: Kirkenes is a small town situated on the Russian border, and it offers plenty of outdoor activities and tours.

Conclusion

Tana promises visitors a unique glimpse into the life of the Sámi people while enjoying the beautiful natural scenery. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can know where to stay, what to eat, and all that there is to see and do during their stay. So, pack your bags and head to Tana for a truly unforgettable experience.
