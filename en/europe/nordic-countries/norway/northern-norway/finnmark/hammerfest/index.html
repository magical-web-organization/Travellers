Introduction to Hammerfest

Located in the northernmost part of Norway in Finnmark, Hammerfest is a historic city that dates back to the 16th century. Despite its small size and remote location, Hammerfest offers a surprisingly diverse range of cultural and natural attractions, making it a popular destination for travelers seeking adventure and exploration in the great outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Hammerfest

If you’re planning a trip to Hammerfest, be sure to include these top attractions in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Post-War Reconstruction: Learn about the rebuilding efforts after WWII and Norway’s post-war history in this interactive museum.
  • Hammerfest Church: Built in 1961, this modern church stands out with its impressive glass mosaics and unique architecture.
  • Meridian Column: This monument marks the location of the first accurate measurement of the Earth’s shape.
  • Royal and Ancient Polar Bear Society: Visit the oldest polar bear club in the world and learn about its fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Hammerfest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Salen: Climb to the top of this mountain for stunning panoramic views of Hammerfest and the surrounding fjords.
  • Sørøya Island: Take a ferry to this idyllic island with plenty of hiking trails and opportunities for fishing and wildlife spotting.
  • Gjesværstappan: Go on a boat tour to see thousands of nesting seabirds, including puffins, guillemots, and gannets.
  • Northern Lights: Witness the mesmerizing aurora borealis in the winter months from various vantage points around Hammerfest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hammerfest

Hammerfest is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts with its numerous trails and scenic routes. Here are some must-try routes:

  • The Nordkapp Route: This demanding 432-km route starts and ends in Hammerfest and takes you through breathtaking landscapes and some of the most northerly towns and villages in the world.
  • The Fuglenes Circuit: A moderate 6-km loop trail that takes you along the coast of Hammerfest and offers sweeping views of the ocean and nearby mountains.
  • The Sørøya Circuit: A challenging 92-km route that circles the island of Sørøya and takes you through rugged coastlines, pristine forests, and rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in Hammerfest: Activities and Tours

Hammerfest offers a variety of exciting activities and tours for all ages and interests. Here are some of the best:

  • Husky sledding: Take a thrilling ride on a dog sled and explore the snowy landscapes around Hammerfest.
  • Sea fishing: Hop on a boat and try your luck at catching some of the region’s delicious fish, such as cod and halibut.
  • Snowmobiling: Rent a snowmobile and explore the Arctic wilderness around Hammerfest on your own or with a guide.
  • King Crab safari: Join a tour to catch and cook the world-renowned king crab, a delicacy that thrives in the cold waters of northern Norway.

To make the most of your trip to Hammerfest, consider this sample week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Hammerfest

When it comes to food, Hammerfest has a lot to offer, including fresh seafood, reindeer meat, and other local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer stew: A hearty and flavorful dish made with tender reindeer meat, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Arctic cod: The cold and clear waters around Hammerfest are home to delicious and sustainable cod, which is often served as fillets or in stews.
  • Kveite: A local variant of halibut which is a large, delicious flatfish found in the cold waters of northern Norway.
  • Fårikål: A traditional Norwegian lamb and cabbage stew similar to Irish lamb stew.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A popular Scandinavian distilled spirit that is often flavored with herbs and spices and enjoyed as a shot or mixed in cocktails.
  • Beer: Hammerfest is home to the world’s most northerly brewery, Svalbard Bryggeri, which produces a range of top-quality beers.

Where to Stay in Hammerfest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hammerfest offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Thon Hotel: This centrally located hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a sauna.
  • Skytterhuset Hotel: A 3-star hotel with a restaurant, bar, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Smarthotel Hammerfest: A budget-friendly hotel with modern rooms and a central location.
  • Hammerfest Guesthouse: A cozy and affordable guesthouse with a shared kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Hammerfest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring back some unique souvenirs and local products from Hammerfest, consider these options:

  • Sami handicrafts: Look for handcrafted silver jewelry, leather boots, and traditional woolen clothing made by the indigenous Sami people.
  • Reindeer products: Reindeer meat, antlers, and hides are all popular souvenirs in Hammerfest and make great gifts.
  • Arctic art and design: Many local artists and designers create beautiful pieces inspired by the stunning nature and culture of Hammerfest and the surrounding region.

Local Culture and Customs of Hammerfest

Hammerfest has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the Sami indigenous people and the city’s history as a hub for Arctic exploration and trade. Visitors can learn about this unique culture by attending festivals, visiting museums, and interacting with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Hammerfest

The best time to visit Hammerfest depends on your interests and preferences. Summer months from June to August are ideal for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing, while winter months from December to February offer the chance to see the Northern Lights and enjoy winter sports like skiing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hammerfest

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Hammerfest:

Do:

  • Dress warmly: Even in summer, temperatures can be chilly in Hammerfest, so bring warm clothes and layers.
  • Respect the environment: Help preserve the pristine nature and wildlife around Hammerfest by following eco-friendly practices like picking up your trash and staying on marked trails.
  • Be open to new experiences: Hammerfest offers many unique and exciting activities and cultural experiences, so be adventurous and try something new.

Don’t:

  • Approach wild animals: While it’s possible to see wildlife like reindeer and Arctic foxes in Hammerfest, it’s important to keep a safe distance and not disturb or feed them.
  • Break the law: Norway has strict laws and regulations about hunting, fishing, and other outdoor activities, so make sure to follow the rules and respect local customs.
  • Disrespect the Sami culture: The Sami people have a long and complex history in Norway and are a protected minority, so it’s important to be respectful and learn about their culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Hammerfest is generally a safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to be aware of certain risks and precautions.

Safety in Hammerfest

  • Weather: Norway’s Arctic climate can be unpredictable and severe, so check the weather forecast and be prepared for sudden changes in temperature, wind, and precipitation.
  • Wildlife: While seeing wildlife is one of the highlights of Hammerfest, some animals like polar bears can be dangerous and should be avoided.
  • Driving: Norway has strict traffic laws and regulations, so make sure to observe speed limits and drive carefully on the often narrow and winding roads.

Health in Hammerfest

  • Sun exposure: Even in winter, the Arctic sun can be intense and cause sunburn and other health issues, so wear sunscreen, hats, and protective clothing.
  • Altitude sickness: If you plan to hike or climb in the mountains around Hammerfest, be aware of altitude sickness, caused by the lack of oxygen at high elevations.
  • Medical care: Hammerfest has a modern hospital and several clinics, but medical care can be expensive and travel insurance is recommended.

Getting to Hammerfest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Hammerfest is relatively easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Hammerfest

  • Hammerfest Airport: Located 5 km east of the city, this small airport has daily flights to Tromsø and other Norwegian cities.

Bus Stations Near Hammerfest

  • Hammerfest Bus Station: Serves local and regional bus routes, linking Hammerfest with other towns and cities in Finnmark.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are regular ferries from Hammerfest to other towns and islands in the region, including Sørøya Island and the North Cape.
  • Car: Driving is a popular way to explore Hammerfest and the surrounding areas, with several car rental agencies available.

How to Get Around in Hammerfest

Hammerfest is a small and walkable city, but there are several transportation options available for getting around:

  • Bus: Hammerfest has a local bus system that serves the city and nearby areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available for getting around in and around Hammerfest.
  • Bicycle: Many hotels and shops in Hammerfest offer bicycles for rent.
  • Car: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the surrounding areas and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Hammerfest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to spend in northern Norway, consider visiting these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • North Cape: One of the most popular tourist attractions in Norway, the North Cape is located 210 km from Hammerfest and offers stunning views of the Arctic Ocean and the midnight sun.
  • Alta: A charming town located 200 km from Hammerfest, Alta is known for its unique rock carvings, impressive Northern Lights Cathedral, and Sami culture.
  • Tromsø: Known as the “Gateway to the Arctic,” Tromsø is a vibrant city with lively nightlife, great restaurants, and plenty of outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking.

Conclusion

Hammerfest may be off-the-beaten-path, but it’s a hidden gem for travelers seeking adventure, nature, and cultural experiences in the Arctic Circle. Follow this comprehensive tour guide and explore all that Hammerfest has to offer.
