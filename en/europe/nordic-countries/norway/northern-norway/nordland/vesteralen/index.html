Introduction to Vesterålen

Vesterålen, located in Nordland county of Northern Norway, is a group of islands that boasts of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. This archipelago is surrounded by the Norwegian Sea and the Vestfjorden and is known for its mild climate.

List of Places to Visit in Vesterålen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Andøy Museum in Andenes: This museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of Andøy, from its Sami roots to modern times.
  • Bø Museum in Bø: The museum houses artifacts from the Iron Age and the Middle Ages and also displays a collection of traditional fishing equipment.
  • Hadsel Island Museum in Stokmarknes: The museum displays the cultural history and natural heritage of the Hadsel Islands and also features a gallery showcasing local artists’ works.
  • Hurtigruten Museum in Stokmarknes: This museum is dedicated to the history of the Norwegian Coastal Express, also known as Hurtigruten, and displays the evolution of this iconic ship.
  • Lofoten Aquarium in Kabelvåg: While not technically in Vesterålen, it’s worth a visit while in the area. The aquarium is home to various species of fish and marine life found in the waters around Lofoten and Vesterålen.

Natural Attractions of Vesterålen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Andøy Whale Safari in Andenes: Whale watching is a must-do activity in Vesterålen. Andøy Whale Safari is one of the best places to see different species of whales such as sperm whales and killer whales in their natural habitat.
  • Nyksund in Øksnes: This picturesque village, located on the southern coast of Vesterålen, features colorful houses that date back to the early 1900s. It’s now a hub for artists and musicians.
  • Trollfjord in Hadsel: Taking a boat trip to Trollfjord is highly recommended to experience the stunning scenery of Vesterålen. The narrow fjord is surrounded by steep mountains and is only accessible by boat.
  • Møysalen National Park in Sortland: The park is located on the island of Hinnøya and offers various hiking trails. The standout hike is to the summit of Mount Møysalen, which is the highest peak in Vesterålen.
  • Gaukværøy in Andøy: Another picturesque village in Vesterålen, Gaukværøy features a lighthouse, white sandy beaches, and hiking trails with scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vesterålen

The mountains, valleys, and coastlines of Vesterålen have made it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the hiking and biking trails are:

  • Coastal trail on Andøya: It’s a 40-kilometer coastal trail that offers scenic views of the Norwegian Sea and the mountains in the background.
  • Øksnes Vestbygd Trail: The trail is in Øksnes and offers a moderate hiking experience with stunning views of fjords and mountains.
  • Langøya Ridge Trail in Sortland: The hike up the Langøya Ridge leads to Mount Hinnsteinen, offering unparalleled panoramic views of Vesterålen.

Best Things to Do in Vesterålen: Activities and Tours

  • Whale watching: As mentioned earlier, whale watching is a popular activity in Vesterålen. Several tour companies offer whale safaris, including Andøy Whale Safari and Express boat Whale Safari in Stø in Øksnes.
  • Food experiences: Vesterålen offers numerous food experiences, including fishing trips and traditional Sami food tasting.
  • Midnight sun and Northern lights: During the summer months, Vesterålen experiences midnight sun, while the winter months offer a chance to view the spectacular Northern Lights.
  • Sea eagle safari: Vesterålen has a large population of sea eagles, and taking a sea eagle safari is another exciting adventure.
  • Kayaking: Kayaking is a fun and different way to explore the coastlines of Vesterålen.
  • Beaches and swimming: Vesterålen has several pristine white-sand beaches where visitors can sunbathe, swim, and relax. Some of the popular beaches are Sandsøya Beach in Bø, Bleik Beach in Andøy, and Ballstad Beach in Vestvågøy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vesterålen

Day 1: Arrival in Vesterålen. Settle into your accommodation and explore the local area.

Day 2: Take a whale safari tour with Andøy Whale Safari or Express boat Whale Safari in Stø.

Day 3: Visit the Lofoten Aquarium in Kabelvåg and explore the picturesque village.

Day 4: Hike the Langøya Ridge Trail and experience stunning views of Vesterålen.

Day 5: Take a trip to the famous Trollfjord and experience the scenic fjord surrounded by steep mountains.

Day 6: Experience the local cuisine and visit the Andenes Fishing Museum in Andøy.

Day 7: Explore the village and beaches of Nyksund and enjoy the midnight sun in summer or Northern Lights in winter.

Food and Drink Scene of Vesterålen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stockfish: It’s a traditional Norwegian dish consisting of dried fish that is still popular in Vesterålen today.
  • Reindeer meat: Reindeer meat is another staple food in Vesterålen, and it’s often served with potatoes, carrots, and lingonberry jam.
  • Traditional Sami food: Sami culture is an integral part of Vesterålen’s heritage, and trying traditional Sami dishes such as džájki, a reindeer soup, and bidos, a meat and vegetable stew, is a must.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: Aquavit is a traditional Scandinavian beverage that is often served with a meal, particularly during festive occasions.
  • Beer: Local breweries such as Vesterålen Brewery and Andøy Brewery produce quality beer using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Vesterålen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vesterålen has a range of accommodation options to fit any budget and preference:

  • Thon Hotel Andrikken in Andenes: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • Nyksund Gjestehus: A guesthouse in the picturesque village of Nyksund that provides both hotel and hostel-style accommodation.
  • BW Svolvær Hotel Lofoten: Located in the nearby town of Svolvær, the hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Vesterålen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Sami clothing and accessories: Local artisans produce high-quality Sami clothing and accessories using traditional materials such as reindeer skin, wool, and leather.
  • Stockfish: As mentioned earlier, stockfish is a staple food in Vesterålen and makes a great edible souvenir.
  • Local handicrafts: Local artisans also produce traditional handicrafts such as woodcarvings, pottery, and jewelry, which make for great unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Vesterålen

Sami culture plays an integral role in Vesterålen’s history, and visitors can learn more about it through Sami cultural experiences, handicrafts, and traditional food. The people of Vesterålen are also known for their friendliness and welcoming nature.

Best Time to Visit Vesterålen

The best time to visit Vesterålen depends on your preferences. The summer months, from late May to early August, offer long days with the midnight sun. It’s also the peak tourist season and offers more outdoor activities. On the other hand, winter months, from November to February, offer the chance to view the Northern Lights and experience winter sports such as dog sledding and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vesterålen

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of nature when hiking and exploring the outdoors.
  • Try traditional Sami food and handicrafts.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the environment.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Forget to check the weather and pack accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vesterålen

Vesterålen is a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors must take precautions when hiking and exploring the outdoors, such as checking weather and trail conditions and bringing appropriate equipment.

Health in Vesterålen

Vesterålen has modern hospitals and health clinics that offer medical assistance in case of an emergency. Visitors should also carry their travel insurance and necessary medical documents when traveling.

Getting to Vesterålen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vesterålen

The Harstad/Narvik Airport is the nearest airport and offers flights to various destinations in Norway and other countries.

Bus Stations Near Vesterålen

The nearest bus station is in Sortland, which offers bus connections to major cities in Norway.

Other Options

There is an extensive road network in Vesterålen, and visitors can drive from nearby cities such as Narvik or Tromsø.

How to Get Around in Vesterålen

The best way to get around Vesterålen is by car, as it offers flexibility to explore the archipelago. Alternatively, there are local buses that connect the major towns and villages in Vesterålen.

Where to Go Next from Vesterålen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lofoten: Lofoten is a group of islands located to the south of Vesterålen, known for its dramatic landscape and stunning coastal scenery.
  • Senja: Senja is a unique destination located to the south of Vesterålen that boasts of beautiful beaches, fjords, and mountains.
  • Tromsø: Tromsø is a vibrant city located to the north of Vesterålen, often referred to as the “Gateway to the Arctic.” The city has a lot to offer, from Northern Lights tours to cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Vesterålen is a unique destination that provides a mix of natural beauty and cultural experiences. From whale watching to traditional food tasting, Vesterålen has something for everyone. The people of Vesterålen are friendly and welcoming, making it a must-visit destination in Northern Norway.
