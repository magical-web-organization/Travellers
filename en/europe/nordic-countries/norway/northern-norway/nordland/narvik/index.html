Introduction to Narvik

Narvik, a scenic town in northern Norway, is a perfect getaway for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers. Its stunning fjords, picturesque mountains, charming towns, and wild landscapes make it one of the most sought-after destinations in the country. Narvik has an extensive history and played a significant role during World War II, which makes it a culturally rich town with several museums depicting its turbulent past. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the natural and cultural wonders of this fascinating town.

List of Places to Visit in Narvik

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Narvik is steeped in history and has several museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into its past. One such place is the Narvik War Museum, which tells the story of the town’s involvement in World War II. Other notable museums include the Ofoten Museum, showcasing the town’s history dating back 2,000 years, and the Red Cross War Memorial Museum, which highlights the role of the Norwegian Red Cross in the war.

Natural Attractions of Narvik: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Narvik’s surroundings are a paradise for nature lovers. The town is surrounded by towering mountains, serene lakes, and spectacular fjords. You can take a cable car to the top of the Narvikfjellet mountain and enjoy the sweeping views of the town and the fjord. For an adventurous experience, head to the Narvik Ski Resort, where you can ski on the rugged slopes or go snowboarding. Other natural attractions include the Rombaksfjord, Beisfjord, and the stunning Ballangen Fjord.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narvik

Narvik is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The area boasts several trails for all levels of expertise. The 6 km Tøttadalen trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll surrounded by wildflowers and serene views. If you are looking for a more challenging route, head to the nearby mountainous areas, where you can explore the wild terrain and take in the stunning vistas of the fjord.

Best Things to Do in Narvik: Activities and Tours

Narvik offers several activities and tours to make your trip unforgettable. Take a fishing excursion to Rombaksbotn, where you can catch arctic char and trout. You can also try dog-sledding, snowmobiling, or horseback riding. For a relaxing experience, visit the Narvik Spa and enjoy a sauna or a dip in the jacuzzi.

Week-Long Itinerary for Narvik

Day 1: Explore Narvik town centre and visit the Narvik War Museum.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive to the Ballangen Fjord and explore the area.

Day 3: Enjoy a hiking or biking trip to the Narvikfjellet mountain and take a cable car to the top.

Day 4: Take a fishing excursion to Rombaksbotn and enjoy the serene surroundings.

Day 5: Visit the Ofoten Museum and learn about the town’s history.

Day 6: Take a dog-sledding tour or snowmobiling excursion.

Day 7: Relax at the Narvik Spa and enjoy a sauna and jacuzzi.

Food and Drink Scene of Narvik

Must-Try Dishes

Narvik has a rich culinary heritage, and you should try some of the local delicacies during your trip. One such dish is the traditional “Fiskegrateng,” a fish gratin that’s made with local fish and topped with breadcrumbs. You should also try Reindeer meat, which is a speciality of the region, and the delicious Norwegian waffles served with strawberry jam and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

Narvik also offers several local beverages that you should try. One such drink is the traditional Aquavit, which is distilled from potatoes and flavoured with herbs and spices. You can also try the local beer, “Narvik Øl,” which is brewed in a local microbrewery.

Where to Stay in Narvik: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Narvik offers a range of accommodations including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. If you are looking for luxury, you can stay at the Scandic Narvik hotel, which offers stunning fjord views and excellent amenities. For a more budget-friendly option, check out the Narvik Hostel or the Rica Hotel Narvik.

What to Buy in Narvik: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Narvik has many souvenir shops offering a range of local products. You can buy traditional Sami handicrafts such as reindeer skin products, bracelets, and earrings. You can also buy jarred fish, wild berries, and local cheeses as gifts for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Narvik

Narvik has a rich cultural heritage and a vibrant history. The locals are friendly and hospitable and take pride in their town’s history. You can learn more about their customs and traditions by visiting museums and talking to locals during your trip.

Best Time to Visit Narvik

The best time to visit Narvik is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild, and daylight lasts much longer than other parts of the year. If you are looking for snow activities, then plan your visit between January and April when the snow is abundant, and the skiing season is in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narvik

Do:

  • Take advantage of Narvik’s outdoor activities and scenic surroundings.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural surroundings and ecosystems.
  • Litter or leave behind trash.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Narvik

Narvik is a safe town for tourists with low crime rates. However, you should take precautions when hiking or exploring outdoor areas and be aware of sudden weather changes.

Health in Narvik

Narvik has an excellent healthcare system, and you should take all necessary vaccinations before your trip. You should also carry any necessary medication as some pharmaceuticals may not be available in the town.

Getting to Narvik: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Narvik

The nearest airport to Narvik is Harstad/Narvik Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car. The airport offers direct flights to Oslo, Trondheim, and Tromsø.

Bus Stations Near Narvik

There are several bus stations in Narvik, with regular connections to nearby towns and cities, including Tromsø, Bodø, and Harstad.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Narvik is accessible by car via the E6 highway, which connects it to other towns and cities in northern Norway.

How to Get Around Narvik

Narvik is a small town that’s easily accessible by foot or bike. There are also several bus services that connect the town centre to nearby attractions. Taxis are also available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Narvik: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Narvik is located in a stunning region with several nearby destinations worth exploring. You can visit the Lofoten Islands, a picturesque chain of islands with stunning beaches and fishing villages. If you want to explore more of Northern Norway, take a trip to Tromsø or Bodø, which are popular towns known for their natural beauty and cultural highlights.

Conclusion

Narvik is a picturesque town with a vibrant history and stunning natural beauty. From exploring museums that capture its turbulent past to hiking along rugged trails and taking in the stunning views of the fjords, Narvik has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide to Narvik will help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Norway.
