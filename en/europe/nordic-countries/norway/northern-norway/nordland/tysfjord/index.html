Introduction to Tysfjord

Tysfjord, located in Nordland, Northern Norway, is a hidden gem known for its breathtaking natural beauty, Sami culture, and outdoor activities. The fjord stretches for over 160 kilometers, providing visitors with stunning views of mountains, waterfalls, and crystal-clear water. Tysfjord has a rich history, and it is home to the indigenous Sami people, who have inhabited the area for over 10,000 years. Whether you are looking for a peaceful retreat or a thrilling adventure, Tysfjord has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tysfjord

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sami Centre: A cultural museum that showcases the Sami way of life, their history, and their traditions. Visitors can learn about Sami handicrafts, attend cultural events, and experience traditional Sami food.
  • Skånland Old Church: Built in 1770, this historical church is a well-preserved example of the traditional wooden churches in Northern Norway. The church is open to visitors, and guided tours are available.
  • Vuotos Museum: This museum focuses on the history of Tysfjord and the Sami culture. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the area’s fascinating past.

Natural Attractions of Tysfjord: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tysfjord National Park: The park covers an area of 600 square kilometers and includes glaciers, rivers, waterfalls, wild forests, and wildlife. Visitors can hike, fish, hunt, and take part in guided tours to explore the park’s rugged terrain.
  • Stetind: Known as Norway’s National Mountain, Stetind is a unique-shaped peak that attracts climbers from all over the world. The hike to the top requires experience and proper equipment, but it rewards visitors with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kjøpsvik Bay: This charming fishing village is nestled between mountains and fjords, providing visitors with picturesque views and a chance to experience what life is like in a traditional Norwegian fishing village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tysfjord

Tysfjord offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Northern Norway. From strenuous mountain hikes to leisurely valley walks, there are hiking and biking routes for all abilities. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Narvikfjellet: Known as the “Gateway to the Arctic,” this ski resort in Narvik offers a variety of hiking and biking trails in summer. The trails provide visitors with panoramic views of the surrounding fjords and mountains.
  • Breidvika: This hike takes visitors through a wild and rugged landscape in the Tysfjord National Park. The route is challenging, but the stunning views of glaciers and mountains make it worth the effort.
  • Tysfjorden: A popular biking route that takes cyclists through small fishing villages, along the coast, and past waterfalls. The route is relatively easy and suitable for all abilities.

Best Things to Do in Tysfjord: Activities and Tours

  • Sami Reindeer Sledding: Experience the ancient Sami tradition of reindeer sledding. Visitors can take a ride on a traditional Sami sled through the stunning Tysfjord National Park, surrounded by mountains and forests.
  • Fishing in Tysfjorden: Tysfjorden is a haven for fishing enthusiasts. Visitors can participate in guided fishing tours, which provide everything from the boat to the fishing equipment. The fjord is known for its abundance of cod, salmon, and sea trout.
  • Northern Lights Tour: Witness one of the most breathtaking natural phenomena in the world in Tysfjord. Visitors can take a guided tour and watch the Northern Lights dance across the night sky.

Food and Drink Scene of Tysfjord

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fiskesuppe: A traditional Norwegian fish soup that is made with a variety of local fish, potatoes, and carrots. It is a hearty and delicious meal that is perfect after a long day of hiking or fishing.
  • Reindeer Stew: A Sami specialty, this stew is made with reindeer meat, potatoes, and carrots. It is a flavorful and protein-packed meal that provides visitors with a taste of the local cuisine.
  • Krumkaker: A traditional Norwegian waffle cookie that is crispy and sweet. It is a popular dessert that is perfect with a cup of coffee or tea.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Scandinavian liquor that is made with potatoes, herbs, and spices. It is a popular drink in Norway and is often served with chilled water on the side.
  • Karsk: Also known as “coffee with a kick,” Karsk is a potent mixture made with coffee and moonshine. It is a strong drink that is popular with locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Tysfjord: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tysfjord offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the popular options include:

  • Radisson Blu Hotel Narvik: A modern hotel located in the heart of Narvik, offering comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and conference facilities.
  • Fjellkysten Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse located in Ballangen, offering comfortable rooms, a sauna, and stunning views of the fjord.
  • Tysfjord Turistsenter: A family-friendly camping site located by the Tysfjord National Park, offering cabins, camping spots, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Tysfjord: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sami Handicrafts: Tysfjord is known for its beautiful Sami handicrafts, which include handwoven rugs, wooden spoons, and silver jewelry. Visitors can purchase these unique souvenirs at local markets and shops.
  • Local Seafood: Tysfjord is renowned for its fresh and delicious seafood, including cod, shrimp, and salmon. Visitors can purchase these products at local markets and bring them home to enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Tysfjord

Tysfjord has a rich culture and tradition, heavily influenced by the Sami people. Visitors should respect the local customs and learn about the culture to fully appreciate their experience. Some tips include:

  • Reindeer: Reindeer are a symbol of the Sami culture, and it is important to treat them with respect. Visitors should not approach or disturb the animals.
  • Samisk Flag: The Sami flag is a symbol of the indigenous people, and it is important to understand its significance when visiting the area.
  • Language: Sami is an official language in Norway, and visitors may encounter signage in the Sami language.

Best Time to Visit Tysfjord

The best time to visit Tysfjord is from May through September when the weather is mild, and the days are long. Outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking, are possible during these months. For those who want to witness the Northern Lights, the best time to visit is from late September to early April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tysfjord

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Practice Leave No Trace principles when hiking and camping.
  • Dress in layers and be prepared for varying weather conditions.

Don’t:

  • Approach or disturb reindeer.
  • Leave trash or litter in nature.
  • Damage or remove natural or cultural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tysfjord

Tysfjord is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the natural hazards, including unstable terrain, wildlife, and weather. Visitors should exercise caution when hiking and camping in the Tysfjord National Park, and always check the weather forecast before embarking on outdoor activities.

Health in Tysfjord

Norway has a modern and efficient healthcare system, but visitors should have travel insurance to cover any potential medical costs. It is also important to stay up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Norway.

Getting to Tysfjord: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tysfjord

The closest airport to Tysfjord is Harstad/Narvik Airport, located approximately 70 kilometers from Tysfjord. The airport offers daily flights to Oslo, Bergen, and other destinations in Norway.

Bus Stations Near Tysfjord

Tysfjord can be reached by bus from Narvik and other nearby towns. The main bus station in Tysfjord is located in Drag, which is the largest town in the municipality.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car and drive to Tysfjord. The journey from Narvik takes approximately 90 minutes, and the route offers stunning views of the fjords and mountains.

How to Get Around in Tysfjord

Tysfjord is a small municipality, and the best way to get around is by car or bike. Local buses connect the main towns, and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Tysfjord: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Narvik: A small city located on the shores of the Ofotfjord, known for its skiing, hiking, and beautiful architecture.
  • Lofoten Islands: A picturesque archipelago located west of Tysfjord, known for its fishing villages, stunning scenery, and northern lights.
  • Tromsø: A vibrant city located to the north of Tysfjord, known for its cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Tysfjord is a hidden gem in Northern Norway, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural history, and outdoor adventure. From hiking and fishing to reindeer sledding and Northern Lights tours, there is something for everyone in Tysfjord. Visitors can immerse themselves in the Sami culture, taste the local cuisine, and experience the stunning scenery that makes Tysfjord a must-visit destination.
