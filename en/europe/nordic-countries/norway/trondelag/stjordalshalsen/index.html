Introduction to Stjørdalshalsen

Stjørdalshalsen is a small town situated in Trøndelag, the central region of Norway. It is one of the largest towns in the region, making it the perfect base for exploring the area’s stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Despite its small size, Stjørdalshalsen has a lot to offer, from historical landmarks to scenic hiking routes.

List of Places to Visit in Stjørdalshalsen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hegra Fortress: Located on a hilltop overlooking the town, this well-preserved fortress played an important role in Norway’s history during World War II.
  • Stjørdal Museum: Exhibits here showcase local history and culture, including traditional crafts and Viking artifacts.
  • Værnes Church: This medieval church dates all the way back to the 12th century and is one of the best-preserved examples of medieval architecture in Norway.

Natural Attractions of Stjørdalshalsen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stokkøya Beach: A stunning white-sand beach on the nearby island of Stokkøya, surrounded by crystal-clear waters.
  • Tverråsen: A popular hiking and skiing destination, with breathtaking views over Trøndelag.
  • Fagerhaug Peak: This 604-meter peak offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stjørdalshalsen

  • Stokksjøen–Ridningfjellet Circuit: A 6-kilometer route through the forest, with stunning views over the local lakes and hills.
  • Hellrunden: A challenging 30-kilometer bike route that takes you through rugged terrain and along the coast, passing through several small villages.
  • Prestgardstien Trail: A 15-kilometer hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful countryside near Stjørdalshalsen, passing through forests, farmland, and along the river.

Best Things to Do in Stjørdalshalsen: Activities and Tours

  • Viking Experience: Learn more about Norway’s Viking history with a visit to the Stjørdal Museum, where you can see an impressive collection of Viking artifacts.
  • Northern Lights Watching Tours: During the winter months, this is one of the best places to see the incredible Northern Lights. Join a guided tour to increase your chances of seeing this stunning natural wonder.
  • Rafting and Kayaking: The nearby rivers and lakes offer plenty of opportunities for watersports, including rafting, kayaking, and canoeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Stjørdalshalsen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rakfisk: Fermented fish served with potatoes, sour cream, and flatbread.
  • Fårikål: A traditional Norwegian lamb and cabbage stew.
  • Kjøttkaker: Norwegian meatballs made with beef or pork and served with mashed potatoes and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Scandinavian spirit flavoured with caraway and other spices.
  • Beer: Norway has a thriving craft-beer scene, with local breweries producing a wide range of unique and delicious beers.
  • Fruit Liqueurs: Made with local berries and fruits, including plum, apple, and cloudberry.

Where to Stay in Stjørdalshalsen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quality Hotel Airport Vaernes: A modern hotel located just a few minutes’ walk from Vaernes airport.
  • Scandic Hell: This stylish hotel has a range of comfortable rooms and suites, as well as an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Stjørdalshalsen Vandrerhjem: A cosy hostel with affordable dorm rooms and private double rooms.

What to Buy in Stjørdalshalsen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Knitted Sweaters: These warm woolen sweaters are a popular Norwegian souvenir.
  • Rosemaling: A traditional decorative painting technique born in Norway.
  • Wooden Crafts: Handmade wooden crafts, such as vases, bowls, and figurines, make for unique and long-lasting souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Stjørdalshalsen

  • Respect for nature is incredibly important in Norway, and littering and other forms of environmental damage are highly frowned upon.
  • Tipping is not expected in Norway, as service charges are automatically included in the price of meals and services.
  • Norwegians are generally polite and reserved, but also friendly and open to meeting new people.

Best Time to Visit Stjørdalshalsen

The best time to visit Stjørdalshalsen depends on your preferences. Summer (June-August) is the most popular time to visit, with long sunny days and pleasant temperatures. Winter (December-March) is ideal for skiing, skating, and other winter sports, as well as for witnessing the magical Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stjørdalshalsen

Do:

  • Be respectful of nature and the environment.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather, particularly in winter.
  • Take advantage of the many opportunities for outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas, including bars and restaurants.
  • Drink alcohol in the streets.
  • Leave trash behind when hiking or exploring the great outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stjørdalshalsen

Norway is considered to be one of the safest destinations in the world. Violent crime is extremely rare, and the risks of pickpocketing and theft are low. However, it’s always important to take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on your valuables and avoiding walking alone in deserted areas at night.

Health in Stjørdalshalsen

Norway has a modern healthcare system, and medical care is generally of a high standard. Visitors from the EU and EEA are entitled to free or discounted emergency healthcare cover under the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC). For others, it’s important to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers both medical emergencies and repatriation costs.

Getting to Stjørdalshalsen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stjørdalshalsen

The nearest airport to Stjørdalshalsen is Vaernes Airport, which is located just a few minutes’ drive from the town center. Direct flights from major European cities including London, Berlin, and Amsterdam are available year-round.

Bus Stations Near Stjørdalshalsen

Regular bus services operate to and from Stjørdalshalsen, with connections to Trondheim and other regional centers.

Other Options

Stjørdalshalsen can also be reached by train, with services running from Trondheim and other destinations in Norway.

How to Get Around in Stjørdalshalsen

Stjørdalshalsen is a small town, and most of the main attractions and amenities are within walking distance of each other. However, rental cars and bikes are also available if you want to explore the wider region.

Where to Go Next from Stjørdalshalsen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Trondheim: One of Norway’s largest and most vibrant cities, located just 35 kilometers to the north of Stjørdalshalsen.
  • Røros: A charming mining town with a UNESCO-listed old town, located around 120 kilometers to the south-east of Stjørdalshalsen.
  • The Fjords: Norway’s majestic fjords can be reached within a few hours’ drive from Stjørdalshalsen, and are well worth a visit.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventure, or just soaking up Norway’s stunning natural beauty, Stjørdalshalsen has something for everyone. With its convenient location, friendly locals, and excellent amenities, it’s one of Norway’s best-kept secrets, and a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to the region.
