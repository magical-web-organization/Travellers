Introduction to Røros

Located in the heart of Trøndelag, Røros is a charming town that has retained much of its 17th and 18th-century charm. It’s a popular destination for tourists looking for cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and picturesque sceneries. Founded as a mining town, Røros has a rich history that is evident in its well-preserved wooden buildings and UNESCO World Heritage designation. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Røros, including things to do, places to stay, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Røros

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Røros Museum Smelthytta: The museum, located in an old smelting house, provides an insight into the history of mining and metal production in Røros.

  2. Røros Church: The 17th-century church is an impressive example of baroque architecture and is one of the most visited sites in Røros.

  3. Olav’s Mine: Visitors can explore the underground world of Olav’s mine, which dates back to the 17th century.

  4. Femundsmarka National Park: The park offers a unique opportunity to experience the wilderness of Norway’s forests and mountains.

Natural Attractions of Røros: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Røros Copper Works: The mine and smelting house that is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  2. Skarsvåg Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to a variety of plant and animal species found only in Norway.

  3. Slegghaugan: A popular spot for hiking, biking, and skiing, offering stunning panoramic views of Røros.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Røros

  1. Fjellvandring: A challenging hiking trail that offers a panoramic view of the mountains.

  2. Rondane National Park: This park boasts several hiking trails that range from easy to difficult, accommodating visitors of varying abilities.

  3. Malmvegen: One of the most popular biking routes in Røros, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Røros: Activities and Tours

  1. Visiting a traditional Røros farm: Visitors can explore how people lived in Røros centuries ago, maintaining the farm as it would have been in the old days.

  2. Winter sports: Røros is known for its excellent facilities for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

  3. Craftsmanship and shopping: Røros is also known for its traditional handicrafts, such as knitted mittens and woolen sweaters, which can be purchased in local shops and workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Røros

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Arrival and check-in at a local hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Røros

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Røros sausage: A traditional local dish made of pork, beef, and lamb.

  2. Røros flatbread: The traditional crispbread accompanies most of the local dishes.

  3. Røros butter: A unique Norwegian specialty made using locally sourced cream, produces during summer.

Local Beverages

  1. Røros beer: Handcrafted beer from local Røros brewery.

  2. Rørospols: The local mineral water from Røros is an excellent accompaniment to meals.

Where to Stay in Røros: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Røros Hotell: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city, complete with a spa, restaurant, and top-quality amenities.

  2. Frich’s Hotel & Spiseri: A locally owned historic hotel, recently renovated to offer modern facilities, located in the heart of the city.

  3. Røros Vandrerhjem Bergmannen: A cozy and affordable hostel located in an old copper mining building.

What to Buy in Røros: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Knitwear: Traditional mittens, hats, and sweaters made from wool and sold by local artisans.

  2. Handcrafted souvenirs: Local craftspeople produce unique souvenirs, like decorative art, ceramic, and glassware.

Local Culture and Customs of Røros

Røros has a strong cultural identity, inspired by its mining heritage and maintained today by its residents. among some to observe key holidays and festivals like mining charity and Christmas, experiences ranging from visiting a traditional Røros farm to joining a local ice-hockey game.

Best Time to Visit Røros

The best time to visit Røros is during the winter months, when the city is covered in snow and offers an excellent opportunity for skiing, snowmobiling and ice-skating. However, some festivals and outdoor activities make summer as an excellent alternative.

Do’s and Don’ts of Røros

Do:

  1. Take time to explore the historic sites, museums, and natural beauty of Røros.
  2. Dress appropriately and wear proper gear for outdoor activities.
  3. Try the local cuisine, including the Røros sausage and flatbread.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural landscapes of Røros by leaving trash behind.
  2. Don’t neglect to learn about the cultural heritage and traditions of Røros.

Safety and Health

Safety in Røros

Røros is a safe town for tourists, with low levels of crime, but it’s always recommended to watch your belongings during crowded events. As for outdoor activities, consider the weather conditions and follow the advice given by the local guides.

Health in Røros

The health care system in Norway is top-notch, and Røros has facilities that cater to the medical needs of tourists.

Getting to Røros: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Røros

  1. Røros Airport: Røros flights reach only the top cities in Norway, usually via Oslo, has a handful of domestic flights and good winter connections.
  2. Trondheim Airport: The largest airport near Røros, with a more significant number of international flights.

Bus Stations Near Røros

  1. Røros bus terminal: The main bus terminal in Røros, offering local and regional travel connections.

Other Options

It’s also possible to reach Røros by train from Oslo or Trondheim, via the Rørosbanen railway line.

How to Get Around in Røros

Røros is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, bike, or by car. It also offers a local bus service that connects the city center with the suburbs and nearby communities.

Where to Go Next from Røros: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Trondheim: A vibrant city with stunning architecture and beautiful natural scenery, at less than 2 hours away by train or car.
  2. Femundsmarka National Park: One of Norway’s largest protected natural areas boasts superb hiking and fishing opportunities.

Conclusion

Røros is a unique destination offering a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for winter sports, hiking, or exploring historic mining sites, Røros is a destination that has something for everyone. We hope this guide has given you everything you need to plan your trip to this Nordic treasure.
