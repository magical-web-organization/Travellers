Namsos, a city in Trøndelag region of Norway, is a spectacular destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. Surrounded by fjords, mountains, and forests, Namsos offers breathtaking views of the northern landscapes. Whether you’re looking to relax amidst nature or explore the cultural history, Namsos has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll be your guide to exploring the best that Namsos has to offer.

Introduction to Namsos

Namsos is a city located in the central part of Norway, with a population of around 13,000 people. The city is situated on the shores of Namsenfjorden, surrounded by mountains, forests, and rivers. Namsos is known as the “Gateway to Namdalen,” which is a region rich in natural beauty and cultural history.

List of Places to Visit in Namsos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Namdalsmuseet: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Namdalen region. The exhibits include traditional costumes, tools, and artifacts from the Viking Age.

  • Namsos City Museum: The museum is located in the city center and exhibits the development of Namsos from a small village to a thriving industrial city.

  • Namsos Church: It is a beautiful wooden church built in 1960 which has a unique architecture and is worth visiting.

Natural Attractions of Namsos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rørvik Beach: A beautiful sand beach with crystal clear waters, perfect for a swim or sunbathing.

  • Øyungen Lake: A serene lake surrounded by dense forests and hiking trails. It’s an ideal spot for fishing, kayaking and camping.

  • Skrøyvstadfossen Waterfall: A stunning waterfall located in the Namdal valley, which is easily accessible by car.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Namsos

  • Folla Coastal Route: This route offers breathtaking views of the sea and coastal landscapes. The trail is around 18 km and takes around 3-4 hours to complete.

  • Kjøpmannsstien Trail: An easy hiking trail that leads to the Kjøpmannsbakken viewpoint, with a panoramic view of Namsos and the surrounding landscapes. The trail is 3 km long and takes around 1 hour to complete.

  • Grongstadfjellet Mountain: A challenging trek that rewards you with stunning views of the Namdalen region. This trek takes around 6-7 hours and is only recommended for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Namsos: Activities and Tours

  • Coastal Fishing Tour: A guided tour that takes you on a fishing trip along the Norwegian coast. You can catch fish like mackerel, cod, and pollock and enjoy your catch in a traditional Norwegian-style BBQ.

  • River Rafting: You can enjoy the thrilling experience of river rafting in the Namsen river, which offers a scenic view of the surroundings.

  • Arctic Circle Tour: A guided tour that takes you through the natural beauty of the northern landscape, including the glaciers, and Arctic Circle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Namsos

Day 1-2: Visit the Namdalsmuseet and Namsos City Museum.

Day 3-4: Enjoy the scenic view of Rørvik Beach and hike the Kjøpmannsstien Trail.

Day 5-6: Take a coastal fishing tour followed by an arctic circle tour.

Day 7: Visit the Skrøyvstadfossen waterfall and enjoy the view of the surrounding mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Namsos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Salmon: A special delicacy of the Namdalen region, smoked salmon is known for its unique flavor and texture.

  • Kjøttkaker: Norwegian meatballs served with mashed potatoes, gravy, and lingonberry sauce, is a traditional Norwegian dish.

  • Rakfisk: A fermented fish dish that is served on flatbread and is a unique dish to try in Norway.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Norwegian spirit that is flavored with herbs and spices and is commonly served with fish dishes.

  • Eplemost: A local apple juice made with fresh apples and is a refreshing beverage to try.

Where to Stay in Namsos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Scandic Namsos: A modern hotel located in the heart of Namsos.

  • Rock City Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that is located near the city center.

  • Fru Haugans Hotel: A traditional Norwegian hotel located in Mosjøen, around 40 km from Namsos.

What to Buy in Namsos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Namsos is known for its handcrafted products like wooden bowls, plates, and Viking jewelry.

  • Rakfisk: You can buy jarred rakfisk to take home as a unique gift.

  • Traditional Costumes: The Namdalen region has its traditional dress, which is a unique souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Namsos

  • Respect for nature and environment is important in Namsos, and tourists are expected to maintain cleanliness and preserve the natural surroundings.

  • Norwegian culture emphasizes the importance of punctuality in all aspects of life, and tourists are advised to be punctual for tours and events.

  • It’s customary to remove shoes when entering a house, and it’s regarded as a courtesy to bring a small gift when visiting a Norwegian home.

Best Time to Visit Namsos

The best time to visit Namsos is from June to August when the weather is mild and offers long daylight hours, which is perfect for outdoor activities. The winter season is not recommended due to heavy snowfall and limited daylight hours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Namsos

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings and dispose of waste properly.

  • Dress in layers to be prepared for changing weather conditions.

  • Greet people with a handshake and maintain eye contact while talking.

Don’t:

  • Do not take photographs of people or their property without their permission.

  • Do not smoke in public places, including bars and restaurants.

  • Do not litter or spit in the streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Namsos

Namsos is generally a safe city, with low crime rates. It’s advisable to take basic precautions like not leaving belongings unattended, avoiding carrying large amounts of cash, and staying aware of surroundings in crowded areas.

Health in Namsos

Norway has a robust healthcare system and free emergency medical services for tourists. It’s recommended to have travel insurance that covers emergency medical expenses before visiting Norway.

Getting to Namsos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Namsos

The nearest airport to Namsos is Namsos Airport, located around 3 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Namsos

Namsos bus terminal is located in the city center and is well-connected to major cities in Norway.

Other Options

You can also reach Namsos by car or train.

How to Get Around in Namsos

You can get around Namsos by using public transport, including buses, taxis, and rental cars. In the city center, you can also walk or bike around to explore the city’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Namsos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rørvik: A small fishing village located around 100 km from Namsos, known for its picturesque scenery and traditional seafood restaurants.

  • Tromsø: A city located around 700 km from Namsos and is known as the “Gateway to the Arctic.” It offers the chance to see the Northern Lights and explore the Arctic landscapes.

  • Mo i Rana: Located around 130 km from Namsos, Mo i Rana is home to the famous Arctic Circle Centre and Svartisen Glacier.

Conclusion

Namsos, with its stunning natural landscapes, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities, is a must-visit destination in Norway. We hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Namsos.
