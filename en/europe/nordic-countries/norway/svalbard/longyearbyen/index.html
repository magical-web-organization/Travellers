Longyearbyen, located in Svalbard, Norway, is the northernmost settlement in the world with a permanent population. This small town is home to a stunning landscape, rich history, and unique culture. As a travel destination, Longyearbyen offers a variety of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local delicacies. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best things to do in Longyearbyen, including where to stay, how to get around, and local customs and culture.

Introduction to Longyearbyen

Longyearbyen is situated in the Arctic Ocean, in Svalbard, Norway. With a population of around 2,100 people, it is the largest settlement in Svalbard and serves as the administrative center of the archipelago. The town was founded in 1906 by the American industrialist John Munroe Longyear and has since become an important center for scientific research, education, and tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Longyearbyen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Svalbard Museum: A museum that showcases the history and culture of Svalbard, including exhibits on mining, whaling, and Arctic wildlife.
  • Svalbard Church: The northernmost church in the world, built-in 1958, with beautiful stained-glass windows and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Mine 3: A former coal mine that now offers guided tours, giving visitors an insight into Longyearbyen’s mining history.

Natural Attractions of Longyearbyen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gruvebadet: A popular outdoor swimming pool that is heated by the town’s power plant using geothermal energy.
  • Nordenskiöldtoppen: The highest peak in the Longyearbyen area, offering stunning snow-capped views and a great location for hiking.
  • Adventdalen Valley: A beautiful valley with diverse wildlife, including reindeer and polar foxes, that can be enjoyed through hiking, skiing, or a guided tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longyearbyen

Longyearbyen offers a variety of hiking and biking routes, that lead visitors to the town’s scenic areas, stunning fjords, and glaciers. Some popular routes include:

  • Hiorthfjellet: A scenic hike that starts from the town’s outskirts, leads visitors to the top of a mountain with a panoramic view of the fjords.
  • Mine 7: A biking route that takes visitors through the town’s coal mines, and leads them to the abandoned Russian mining settlement of Pyramiden.
  • Adventdalen Valley: A stunning valley that offers several hiking routes and a great location for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Longyearbyen: Activities and Tours

  • Dog Sledding: An activity that allows visitors to experience the traditional mode of transportation in the Arctic region, and to explore the town’s scenic areas.
  • Northern Lights Hunting: A tour that takes visitors to the outskirts of Longyearbyen, away from the town’s light pollution, to catch a glimpse of the Aurora Borealis.
  • Snowmobiling: An activity that gives visitors the opportunity to explore Longyearbyen’s snow-covered landscape, and to visit the town’s far-flung areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Longyearbyen

Longyearbyen offers a unique food and drink scene that includes local delicacies and international cuisine. Some must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svalbard reindeer: A dish made with local reindeer meat, served with berry sauce, and mashed potatoes.
  • Arctic char: A fish dish caught in the local fjords and served with lemon and local potatoes.
  • Krumkake: A traditional Norwegian sweet that is made with a waffle iron and served with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Svalbard Beer: A local beer that is brewed in Longyearbyen and served in many of the town’s bars and restaurants.
  • Brennivin: A traditional Icelandic schnapps made from potato mash and caraway seeds.

Local Culture and Customs of Longyearbyen

Longyearbyen has a unique culture that is influenced by its Arctic location and its history as a mining town. Some local customs and culture to keep in mind when visiting include:

  • Dressing appropriately for the cold weather and harsh winter conditions.
  • Respecting the local wildlife and following guidelines to prevent disturbing their natural habitats.
  • Being mindful of the town’s history and cultural heritage, and showing respect for the local residents.

Best Time to Visit Longyearbyen

The best time to visit Longyearbyen is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the sun shines 24 hours a day. However, those interested in winter sports and activities like dog sledding and snowmobiling may prefer to visit during the winter months from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longyearbyen

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the cold weather and harsh winter conditions.
  • Respect the local wildlife, and follow guidelines to prevent disturbing their natural habitats.
  • Be mindful of the town’s history and cultural heritage, and show respect for the local residents.
