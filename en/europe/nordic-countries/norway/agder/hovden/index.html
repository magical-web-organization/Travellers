Introduction to Hovden

Hovden is a small, charming village nestled in the heart of the Setesdal Valley, in the Agder region of Norway. The village is known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Hovden is a popular destination for tourists who are looking for a peaceful escape in the countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Hovden

If you’re planning a trip to Hovden, here are the top places you shouldn’t miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hovden Museum: A museum dedicated to the history and culture of Setesdal.
  2. Bykle Old Church: A beautifully preserved wooden church built in the 1600s.
  3. Hylestad Stave Church: One of the oldest stave churches in Norway, dating back to the 12th century.

Natural Attractions of Hovden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hovden National Park: A vast wilderness area with mountains, lakes, and forests, perfect for hiking and skiing.
  2. Hovdenuten Peak: A popular hiking trail that provides stunning views of the Setesdal Valley.
  3. Rauland Ski Resort: A world-class ski resort with over 30 km of slopes for all levels of skiers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hovden

Hovden is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to try.

  1. Hovden-Rysstad Trail: A 27 km trail that takes you through stunning mountain landscapes.
  2. Hovden-Haukeliseter Trail: A 20 km trail that takes you through a national park with breathtaking views.
  3. Hovden-Harefossen-Storegga Trail: A 24 km trail that takes you through lush forests and scenic rivers.

Best Things to Do in Hovden: Activities and Tours

  1. White-Water Rafting: Take a guided tour down the Otra River and experience the thrill of white-water rafting.
  2. Kayaking: Explore the pristine lakes and rivers of Hovden on a kayaking tour.
  3. Skiing and Snowboarding: Hovden is a popular ski resort, and it has slopes for all levels of skiers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hovden

Are you planning to spend a week in Hovden? Here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow.

Day 1: Arrive in Hovden and check into your hotel. Rest day.

Day 2: Visit the Hovden Museum and Bykle Old Church.

Day 3: Go white-water rafting in the Otra River.

Day 4: Explore Hovden National Park and hike the Hovden-Rysstad Trail.

Day 5: Take a kayaking tour of the lakes and rivers of Hovden.

Day 6: Skiing and snowboarding at Rauland Ski Resort.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Hovden.

Food and Drink Scene of Hovden

When in Hovden, make sure to sample these local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fårikål: A delicious lamb and cabbage stew, typically eaten in the fall.
  2. Lutefisk: A traditional dish made with dried and salted cod.
  3. Smalahove: A dish made from a sheep’s head, boiled and served with potatoes and rutabaga.

Local Beverages

  1. Aquavit: A traditional Norwegian spirit made from potatoes and flavored with caraway.
  2. Beer: Norway has a thriving craft beer scene.

Where to Stay in Hovden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hovden has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommendations:

  1. Hovden Fjellstoge: A cozy mountain lodge with beautiful views of the valley.
  2. Hovden Høyfjellshotell: A luxurious hotel with an on-site spa.

What to Buy in Hovden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take a piece of Hovden back home with you, here are some souvenirs and local products to consider:

  1. Knitwear: Hovden is known for its wool and knitting traditions, so be sure to check out the local knitwear shops.
  2. Handmade Wooden Items: The Setesdal region is known for its talented woodworkers.

Local Culture and Customs of Hovden

Hovden has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its music, art, and traditions. The locals are proud of their heritage and are happy to share it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Hovden

The best time to visit Hovden depends on what you want to do. If you’re interested in winter sports, the best time to visit is from December to March. If you want to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking, the best time to visit is from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hovden

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  3. Carry cash as some places may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter.
  2. Don’t be loud and disruptive, especially at night.
  3. Don’t ignore safety warnings when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hovden

Hovden is a safe place to visit. However, as with any outdoor activity, there are risks involved. Make sure to follow all safety rules and regulations when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and white-water rafting.

Health in Hovden

Norway has a high standard of healthcare, and medical facilities in Hovden are top-notch. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance when visiting a foreign country.

Getting to Hovden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hovden

The nearest airport to Hovden is Kristiansand Airport, located about 170 km away.

Bus Stations Near Hovden

There are bus services from Kristiansand, Oslo, and Bergen to Hovden.

Other Options

If you prefer driving, the drive from Oslo to Hovden takes about 5 hours.

How to Get Around in Hovden

The best way to get around Hovden is by car. However, if you don’t have access to a car, there are local buses that run between the main attractions in Hovden.

Where to Go Next from Hovden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Norway, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  1. Kristiansand: A beautiful coastal city with a rich cultural heritage.
  2. Stavanger: A vibrant city with excellent shopping and dining options.

Conclusion

Hovden is a beautiful village that offers a unique taste of Norway’s natural beauty, outdoor activities, culture, and traditions. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to Hovden and make the most of your visit. Happy travels!
