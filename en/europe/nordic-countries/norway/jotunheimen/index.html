Introduction to Jotunheimen

Tucked away in the heart of Norway, Jotunheimen (which translates to “Home of the Giants” in English) is the country’s largest national park. With majestic glaciers, towering mountains, deep valleys, roaring waterfalls, and crystal-clear lakes, it is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Located in the Nordic countries of Europe, Jotunheimen offers breathtaking views that you’ll never forget. It has something for everyone - from culturally rich attractions to outdoor adventures, from scenic cycling routes to thrilling hiking trails to remote glacier hikes.

List of Places to Visit in Jotunheimen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical places in Jotunheimen that you can visit to learn more about its interesting history and unique culture, such as:

  • Lom Stave Church: Located in the village of Lom, this 12th-century wooden church is one of the oldest and most well-preserved in Norway.

  • Jotunheimen National Park Visitor Centre: Located in Lom, this award-winning museum tells the story of Jotunheimen’s natural and cultural heritage through interactive exhibits and multimedia displays.

  • Klimapark 2469: Located in Juvflye, this outdoor museum showcases how the climate and landscapes in Jotunheimen have changed over time and how we can mitigate climate change in the future.

Natural Attractions of Jotunheimen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jotunheimen is a playground for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in Jotunheimen:

  • Gjende: This long and narrow lake is surrounded by steep mountains and is known for its emerald-green water. It is a popular spot for kayaking, canoeing, and hiking.

  • Besseggen Ridge: This iconic hiking trail runs along the ridge of Besseggen, providing stunning views of Gjende and the surrounding mountains. It is considered one of the best hiking trails in Norway.

  • Glacier Hikes: Several glaciers are located in Jotunheimen, such as the Smørstabbtindan Glacier and the Styggebreen Glacier. You can join a guided glacier hike to explore these ancient ice formations.

  • Waterfalls: Jotunheimen is home to many majestic waterfalls, such as Vettisfossen and Hjellefossen. These waterfalls offer beautiful photo opportunities and are easily accessible by hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jotunheimen

Jotunheimen is a hiker’s paradise with a network of well-marked trails that cater to different skill levels and interests. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Jotunheimen:

  • Rondane National Park: This national park is located south of Jotunheimen and offers a multitude of hiking trails that cater to different skill levels and interests, such as Rondane Roundtrip and Dørålseter Roundtrip.

  • Jotunheimen National Park: Jotunheimen is home to several iconic hiking trails, such as Besseggen Ridge, Galdhøpiggen Summit Trail, and Glittertind Summit Trail. The park also offers excellent mountain biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Jotunheimen: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Jotunheimen, whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Jotunheimen:

  • Kayaking/Canoing: Paddle through the emerald-green waters of Gjende and explore the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Glacier Hikes: Join a guided glacier hike and explore the ancient ice formations of Jotunheimen.

  • Fishing: Jotunheimen has several lakes and rivers that offer excellent fishing opportunities, such as trout and Arctic char.

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of Jotunheimen on horseback and discover some of the park’s hidden gems.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jotunheimen

Day 1: Arrive in Lom and visit the Lom Stave Church and the Jotunheimen National Park Visitor Centre.

Day 2: Hike the Besseggen Ridge and take a boat ride on Gjende.

Day 3: Join a guided glacier hike and explore the Smørstabbtindan Glacier.

Day 4: Explore the waterfalls in the area, such as Vettisfossen and Hjellefossen.

Day 5: Go fishing in one of Jotunheimen’s lakes, such as Gjende or Leirvassbu.

Day 6: Visit Klimapark 2469 and learn about the impact of climate change in Jotunheimen and the world.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the views of Jotunheimen, and prepare for your departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Jotunheimen

Must-Try Dishes

Jotunheimen is known for its hearty and flavorful cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Jotunheimen:

  • Fårikål: A traditional Norwegian lamb and cabbage dish that is commonly eaten in the fall.

  • Rakfisk: A fermented fish dish that is a delicacy in Norway. It is typically eaten with flatbread and potatoes.

  • Cloudberries: These tangy and sweet berries grow wild in Jotunheimen and are often used in desserts.

Local Beverages

Jotunheimen has a thriving craft beer scene, and several breweries, such as Berentsen’s Brygghus and Aegir Bryggeri, offer unique and delicious brews. When it comes to non-alcoholic drinks, try “ekte saft,” a traditional Norwegian juice made from berries and fruits.

Where to Stay in Jotunheimen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation in Jotunheimen, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jotunheimen:

  • Leirvassbu Mountain Lodge: Located in the heart of Jotunheimen, this mountain lodge offers comfortable rooms and delicious food.

  • Gjendesheim Turisthytte: This hostel is located on the shores of Gjende and has stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It also offers guided tours and outdoor activities.

  • Bessheim Fjellstue: This charming guesthouse is located in the village of Bessheim and offers cozy rooms, traditional Norwegian cuisine, and a relaxing sauna.

What to Buy in Jotunheimen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jotunheimen offers plenty of opportunities to buy unique and authentic souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Jotunheimen:

  • Traditional Norwegian Sweaters: These warm and cozy sweaters are a must-have for any trip to Norway.

  • Handmade Crafts: Jotunheimen is known for its craft traditions, such as woodworking, knitting, and embroidery. Look for handmade crafts at local markets and souvenir shops.

  • Local Foods: Bring home some of Jotunheimen’s delicious smoked salmon, cured meats, and artisanal cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Jotunheimen

Jotunheimen is proud of its distinct culture and customs. Respect for nature is an important part of the local culture, and visitors are encouraged to follow Leave No Trace principles when exploring the park. Additionally, Jotunheimen has a rich tradition of storytelling and folk music, which you can experience at local cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Jotunheimen

Jotunheimen is open year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Summer (June to August) is the most popular time for hiking and outdoor adventures, while winter (December to February) is ideal for skiing and snowshoeing. Spring and fall are great for fishing and visiting cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jotunheimen

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Be prepared for the weather.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural sites.
  • Disturb local wildlife.
  • Ignore safety signs or warnings.
  • Go off-trail without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jotunheimen

Jotunheimen is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to follow standard safety precautions when exploring the park. Always hike with someone else and be aware of the weather conditions. Additionally, be cautious around glaciers, as they can be dangerous to explore without a guide.

Health in Jotunheimen

Jotunheimen is situated at high altitude, so visitors should be aware of the effects of altitude sickness. Stay hydrated and be prepared for changes in temperature and weather conditions. Additionally, make sure to have the appropriate travel insurance before your visit.

Getting to Jotunheimen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jotunheimen

  • Oslo Airport Gardermoen: This is the closest airport to Jotunheimen, located approximately 250 km to the southwest.

  • Bergen Airport Flesland: This airport is located approximately 300 km to the west of Jotunheimen.

Bus Stations Near Jotunheimen

  • Lom: The village of Lom has a bus station with connections to several cities in Norway, such as Bergen, Oslo, and Trondheim.

  • Otta: The town of Otta has a bus station with connections to several cities in Norway, such as Bergen, Oslo, and Trondheim.

Other Options

  • Car rental: If you want more flexibility in your travels, renting a car is a great option. There are several car rental companies in Norway, such as Avis, Hertz, and Europcar.

  • Train: The Norwegian State Railways (NSB) offer train connections to several cities in Norway, such as Bergen, Oslo, and Trondheim. The closest train station to Jotunheimen is Otta.

How to Get Around in Jotunheimen

The best way to get around Jotunheimen is by car, as it offers the most flexibility. However, there are also several shuttle bus services and taxis available in the area. Additionally, many of the hiking trails and outdoor activities are accessible by foot.

Where to Go Next from Jotunheimen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jotunheimen is located in the heart of Norway, making it a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations, such as:

  • Bergen: Known as the “Gateway to the Fjords,” Bergen is a charming coastal city with a rich history and a vibrant cultural scene.

  • Oslo: Norway’s capital city is packed with cultural attractions, such as museums, galleries, and theaters.

  • Sogn og Fjordane: This region is known for its stunning fjords, glaciers, and mountains, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Jotunheimen is a natural gem that has something for everyone. Whether you’re a hiker, a cyclist, a fisherman, or a culture buff, there are countless opportunities to explore and discover this beautiful national park. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to plan your unforgettable trip to Jotunheimen.
