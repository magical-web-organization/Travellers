Introduction to Hardangervidda

Hardangervidda is a vast, mountainous plateau in Norway, occupying an area of over 8,000 square kilometers. This stunning destination is a paradise for nature lovers, offering breathtaking views, rugged hiking terrain, and wide-open spaces. The region is famous for its dramatic landscapes, wild animal sightings, and fascinating cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Hardangervidda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in learning more about the history and culture of Hardangervidda, there are several must-visit museums and historical places. These include:

  • Hardangervidda Natursenter: This excellent visitor center provides informative exhibits and films about the natural environment of Hardangervidda. It is an excellent starting point for your trip to the region.
  • Hardangervidda Fjellmuseum: This museum contains a fascinating collection of artifacts and displays related to the history of Hardangervidda, from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • Rjukan-Notodden Industrial Heritage Site: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located just outside Hardangervidda and includes several historical factories that helped transform Norway’s economy in the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Hardangervidda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hardangervidda is a nature lover’s paradise, offering endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are just a few of the highlights:

  • Vøringsfossen Waterfall: This stunning waterfall plunges over 180 meters into the Måbødalen Valley and is one of Norway’s most popular attractions.
  • Rjukanfossen Waterfall: This impressive waterfall has many hiking trails that provide stunning views of the cascading water.
  • Hårteigen Peak: This is a popular hiking destination and offers remarkable panoramic views of the plateau and surrounding mountains.
  • Kjeåsen Mountain Farm: This historical farm clings to a steep mountainside and provides unique views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hardangervidda

Hardangervidda is a popular hiking and biking destination, with a range of trails to suit all skill levels. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Hardangervidda Plateau: This area has over 3,000 km of marked trails, including the famous DNT trail network.
  • Hårteigen Peak: This trail provides an impressive view of the plateau and surrounding mountains. It is a moderate to challenging hike.
  • Rjukanfossen Waterfall: This trail is easy to navigate and offers an excellent view of the waterfall in a lush forest atmosphere.

Best Things to Do in Hardangervidda: Activities and Tours

Here are some fun activities and tours to help you get the most out of your trip to Hardangervidda:

  • Join a snowshoeing tour: This is an exciting winter activity that allows you to experience the enchanting beauty of Hardangervidda’s vast snowy landscape.
  • Experience a dog sled tour: This is a perfect way to explore the plateau and the nearby countryside, even in winter.
  • Visit a reindeer farm: This gives you the chance to interact with Norwegian reindeer and learn about Sami culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hardangervidda

If you have a week to explore Hardangervidda, consider following this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Hardangervidda Natursenter and explore the visitor center’s exhibits.
  • Day 2: Hike to Hårteigen Peak and enjoy the magnificent views.
  • Day 3: Take a guided snowshoeing tour across the snowy landscape of Hardangervidda.
  • Day 4: Visit the Rjukan-Notodden Industrial Heritage Site and explore the historic factories.
  • Day 5: Go dog sledding to explore the countryside around Hardangervidda.
  • Day 6: Visit a reindeer farm and learn about Sami culture.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a day of relaxation at a nearby spa or hot spring before your departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Hardangervidda

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the must-try dishes that reflect the local flavor of Hardangervidda:

  • Rømmegrøt: This is a traditional sour cream porridge that is often served with cured meats such as gravlax or reindeer.
  • Smoked Salmon: Hardangervidda is surrounded by fresh mountain waterbodies that are ideal for raising salmon. Expect the smoked salmon to be of exceptional quality!

Local Beverages

Be sure to sample these locally produced beverages during your visit to Hardangervidda:

  • Aquavit: This is a fiery Scandinavian liquor that serves as a great aperitif before meals, and is best enjoyed with hearty Norwegian cuisine.
  • Bjørnebærvin: This is a delicious local blackberry wine that is produced in the region.

Where to Stay in Hardangervidda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options for your stay in Hardangervidda. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hardangervidda Fjellstue: This is a classic mountain lodge that offers cozy rooms and traditional Norwegian cuisine.
  • Rjukan Vertshus: This is a charming hotel located just outside Hardangervidda that provides comfortable rooms and delicious local cuisine.
  • Telemark Camping & Inn: This popular camping facility provides beautiful views of Hardangervidda and comfortable cabins.

What to Buy in Hardangervidda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the souvenir items and local products of Hardangervidda that you should consider bringing home:

  • Sami Handicrafts: Traditional Sami-made handcrafted ornaments and textiles can be found in many markets in Hardangervidda.
  • Reindeer Meat: Reindeer meat is a specialty in many parts of Norway, including Hardangervidda. It is often sold in small shops and souvenir stands.
  • Hardangervidda Souvenirs: Snow globes, keychains, hats, and t-shirts are available in most gift shops in Hardangervidda.

Local Culture and Customs of Hardangervidda

Hardangervidda has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated in various festivals and traditions. The Sami people’s culture and traditional way of life are held in high regard in this region. Other customs include traditional Norwegian music and dance, as well as the celebration of various national holidays.

Best Time to Visit Hardangervidda

The best time to visit Hardangervidda is from June to September when the weather is usually mild, and hiking and biking trails are open to visitors. It is also possible to visit during the winter months for winter sports like skiing, snowmobiling, and dog sledding.

  • Respect local wildlife and avoid disturbing plant and animal life while hiking and touring the region.
  • Participate in local festivals and cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hardangervidda

Hardangervidda is a safe place to visit, but visitors must be aware of the potential hazards associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and mountain biking. It is advisable to follow any recommendations from local authorities and experienced guides to stay safe during your visit.

Health in Hardangervidda

The tap water in Norway is safe to drink. However, visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications and any other medical supplies necessary for their stay. It is also important to take basic precautions like using sunscreen and insect repellent during your visit.

Getting to Hardangervidda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hardangervidda

The nearest airport to Hardangervidda is Bergen Airport Flesland, which is just over 3 hours’ drive from the region. There are also regular bus and train services from Bergen to Hardangervidda.

Bus Stations Near Hardangervidda

Several bus lines provide transportation to Hardangervidda, and it is possible to find direct buses from major cities in Norway. For instance, the bus ride from Oslo to Rjukan city takes approximately 3 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a scenic train ride from Oslo to Rjukan or Bergen to Geilo from where there is a motorable route to the Hardangervidda plateau. Alternatively, visitors can rent a car and drive to Hardangervidda.

How to Get Around in Hardangervidda

The best way to explore Hardangervidda’s vast and scenic landscape is by hiking, biking, or by guided tour. It is also possible to rent a car or hire a taxi for a self-guided tour of the region.

Where to Go Next from Hardangervidda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Hardangervidda, visitors can also consider visiting nearby destinations, including:

  • Rjukan: This is a small municipality outside of Hardangervidda, known for its industrial heritage and skiing opportunities.
  • Geilo: This is a popular ski resort with a wide range of activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and fishing. The area is also known for its stunning natural scenery.
  • Bergen: This is Norway’s second-largest city, known for its charming historic district, vibrant culture scene, and beautiful fjords.
