Introduction to Nakskov

Nakskov is a charming town located in the southern part of the Danish island of Lolland, in the Lolland-Falster region. Nestled along the Nakskov Fjord, the town boasts a picturesque landscape with rolling hills, lush forests, and idyllic beaches. With a population of over 13,000, Nakskov is a bustling town with a rich history and a vibrant culture. In this guide, we’ll be taking a closer look at the top attractions, activities, and experiences that Nakskov has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Nakskov

If you’re planning a trip to Nakskov, you won’t be disappointed with the range of things to see and do in the area. Here is our top list of places to visit in Nakskov:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lolland Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history, culture, and nature of Lolland and its surrounding areas. Here, you’ll find fascinating exhibits showcasing everything from Vikings to 19th-century farming techniques.
  • Nakskov Old Town: This charming, well-preserved neighborhood is a fantastic place to take a stroll. The narrow streets are lined with colorful, traditional buildings, and there are plenty of cafes, shops, and restaurants to check out.
  • Sankt Nikolai Kirke: This beautiful church dates back to the 13th century and features stunning frescoes and other artworks.

Natural Attractions of Nakskov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Knuthenborg Safaripark: This nature park is home to over 1,000 animals from all over the world, including rhinos, giraffes, zebras, and lions. Visitors can drive through the park in their own car or take a guided bus tour.
  • Maribo Lakes Nature Park: Located just a short drive from Nakskov, this park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers a range of activities, including hiking, biking, horseback riding, fishing, and birdwatching.
  • Nakskov Fjord: The fjord is a stunning, natural feature of the area and offers plenty of opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakskov

Lolland is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a variety of routes that showcase the area’s natural beauty and interesting landmarks. Some popular options include the 52-kilometer Lolland pilgrim route, the 29-kilometer Nysted rundt trail, and the 25-kilometer Nakskov-Vindeby coastal trail.

Best Things to Do in Nakskov: Activities and Tours

Aside from the attractions and natural wonders we’ve mentioned, here are a few more ideas for things to do in Nakskov:

  • Visit the local markets and festivals: Throughout the year, Nakskov hosts a range of food and craft markets, as well as music festivals, art exhibitions, and other cultural events.
  • Take a boat tour: There are several boat tour operators in the area that offer guided trips around the fjord and nearby islands.
  • Go on a beer tasting tour: Lolland is home to several microbreweries that produce delicious, locally-made beer. You can take a tour of the breweries, taste their products, and even bring home a few bottles as souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nakskov

If you have a week to spend in Nakskov, here’s one itinerary that will allow you to explore the best of the town and its surrounding areas:

Food and Drink Scene of Nakskov

Nakskov has a wealth of local culinary specialties and traditional dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages when visiting Nakskov:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Flæskesteg: Danish-style roast pork, typically served with potatoes and red cabbage.
  • Æbleskiver: Small, round pancakes often served with jam and powdered sugar.
  • Stegt flæsk: A dish made with crispy fried pork belly and boiled potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Gammel Dansk: A Danish bitter liqueur made with herbs, spices, and citrus.
  • Akvavit: A Scandinavian spirit flavored with caraway, dill, and other spices.
  • Lolland beer: There are several microbreweries in the area producing unique, small-batch beers that are worth trying.

Where to Stay in Nakskov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nakskov has several options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

  • Hotel Harmonien: A centrally located hotel with comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Danhostel Nakskov: A budget-friendly hostel offering private and shared rooms, as well as a range of amenities such as a shared kitchen, laundry facilities, and bike rentals.
  • Pension Elmehøj: A cozy guesthouse located just outside of town, offering clean and comfortable rooms, a large garden, and a hearty breakfast.

What to Buy in Nakskov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs or gifts to take back home? Here are some things to consider buying in Nakskov:

  • Local crafts and artwork: There are several art galleries and craft shops in Nakskov selling handmade jewelry, ceramics, glassware, and other items.
  • Lolland wines: Lolland is home to several vineyards producing high-quality wines made from locally grown grapes.
  • Traditional Danish candies and chocolates: Nakskov has several candy shops selling a range of sweet treats, including licorice, marzipan, and chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakskov

Denmark has a rich cultural heritage, and Nakskov is no exception. Danish people are known for their strong sense of community, egalitarianism, and love of nature. When visiting Nakskov, be sure to respect local customs, such as removing your shoes before entering someone’s home, and always thanking your hosts for their hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Nakskov

The best time to visit Nakskov depends on your interests and preferences. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and exploring the natural beauty of the area, summer is the ideal time to visit. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor events and festivals taking place. If you’re more interested in cultural experiences, consider visiting in the winter months, when there are fewer crowds and plenty of holiday markets and festivities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakskov

Here are a few general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Nakskov:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take advantage of the outdoors and natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disrespectful in public.
  • Assume everyone speaks English (although most people do).
  • Forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent!

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakskov

Denmark is generally a very safe country, with low levels of crime and violence. Nakskov is no exception, and visitors should have no issues with their personal safety as long as they use common sense and take standard precautions.

Health in Nakskov

Denmark has a well-developed healthcare system and high standards of public health. It is recommended that visitors have up-to-date vaccination records and carry the appropriate travel health insurance.

Getting to Nakskov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nakskov is situated in a remote part of Denmark, but there are still several transportation options to get there.

Airports Near Nakskov

The nearest airport to Nakskov is the Copenhagen Airport, located about two hours away by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Nakskov

There is a bus station in Nakskov, which is served by several regional bus companies.

Other Options

The most popular and convenient way to reach Nakskov is by car, which allows visitors to travel at their own pace and explore the surrounding areas at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Nakskov

Nakskov is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi and bus companies serving the area if you need to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Nakskov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time to spend in the area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Møn Island: A picturesque island with stunning white cliffs, historic villages, and quaint seaside towns.
  • The medieval town of Nykøbing Falster: A charming town with a rich history, featuring medieval architecture, museums, and cultural events.
  • The city of Copenhagen: Denmark’s capital city is just a few hours’ drive or train ride from Nakskov, and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Nakskov is a hidden gem in Denmark, with plenty of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, sightseeing, or trying local delicacies, Nakskov is sure to charm and delight. We hope you’ve found this guide helpful and informative, and that it inspires you to plan your own visit to this scenic town.
