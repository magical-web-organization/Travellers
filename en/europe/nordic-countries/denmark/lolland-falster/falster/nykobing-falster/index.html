Introduction to Nykøbing Falster

Located in the heart of Falster region, Nykøbing Falster is one of the most picturesque and historically rich cities in Denmark. Dating back to the Middle Ages, this charming city offers a mix of old-world charm and modern amenities. Visitors will find plenty of things to do, from exploring historic landmarks to hiking and biking in the surrounding countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Nykøbing Falster

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Middelaldercentret: This living museum allows visitors to experience life in the Middle Ages, complete with costumed guides, historic buildings, and interactive exhibits.
  • Nykøbing Falster Local History Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the city through exhibits on local art, industry, and daily life.
  • Højby Church: Dating back to the 12th century, this beautiful church houses several notable works of art, including a rare medieval fresco.

Natural Attractions of Nykøbing Falster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Guldborgsund Zoo and Botanical Gardens: This expansive park features hundreds of animals and plants from around the world, as well as playgrounds, picnic areas, and walking paths.
  • Nykøbing Falster City Park: This lush park is perfect for a leisurely stroll or picnic, with beautiful gardens, fountains, and statues.
  • Knuthenborg Safari Park: Just a short drive from Nykøbing Falster, this wildlife park offers the chance to see exotic animals up close, as well as a variety of rides and attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nykøbing Falster

  • The Masnedø Trail: This easy, 7-mile trail takes visitors across the scenic Masnedø Island, with plenty of opportunities to see local wildlife and historic landmarks.
  • The Møn Trail: This challenging, 25-mile trail offers stunning views of the coastline and countryside, as well as several historic attractions along the way.

Best Things to Do in Nykøbing Falster: Activities and Tours

  • Nykøbing Falster Boat Tour: Take a relaxing cruise along the picturesque Guldborgsund Strait, where you can admire historic landmarks and natural scenery.
  • Tasting Tour of Nykøbing Falster: Sample the local cuisine and beverages at some of the city’s best restaurants and cafes.
  • Art and Architecture Tour: Explore the city’s rich cultural heritage through its stunning buildings and public art installations.

Food and Drink Scene of Nykøbing Falster

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stegt flæsk med persillesovs: This classic Danish dish features crispy pork belly served with a creamy parsley sauce.
  • Røget ål: Smoked eel is a regional delicacy that is often served with potatoes and a rich, buttery sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Æblemost: This refreshing apple juice is a local favorite and pairs well with many traditional Danish dishes.
  • Mjød: This ancient alcoholic beverage is made from honey and water and has been enjoyed in Denmark for centuries.

Where to Stay in Nykøbing Falster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Falster: Located in the heart of the city, this elegant hotel offers spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Danhostel Nykøbing Falster: This affordable hostel offers a variety of lodging options, including private rooms and dormitories, as well as free breakfast and bike rentals.
  • Ny Kirstineberg Manor: This historic guesthouse is located on a beautiful estate just outside the city and offers luxurious accommodations, as well as a pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Nykøbing Falster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Knitwear: Denmark is known for its high-quality woolen goods, and Nykøbing Falster has a variety of shops where you can find beautiful sweaters, hats, and scarves.
  • Ceramics: The city has a rich tradition of pottery and ceramics, and visitors can find a variety of unique and handmade items to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Nykøbing Falster

  • Hygge: This Danish concept of coziness and conviviality is a way of life in Nykøbing Falster, where visitors can enjoy warm, welcoming hospitality and a relaxed pace of life.
  • Respect for Nature: Denmark has a strong environmental ethic, and locals take great pride in keeping their natural surroundings clean and beautiful.

Best Time to Visit Nykøbing Falster

The best time to visit Nykøbing Falster is from May to September when the weather is mild and the city comes alive with festivals and outdoor activities. The winter months can be quite cold and dark, although the city’s Christmas markets and holiday decorations are a highlight of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nykøbing Falster

Do:

  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city’s cobblestone streets and hiking trails.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including traditional Danish dishes and locally brewed beer and cider.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and etiquette, such as taking off your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings, which are highly valued by the local community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nykøbing Falster

Like most Danish cities, Nykøbing Falster is generally very safe for visitors. However, it is always advisable to take common-sense precautions, such as keeping an eye on your personal belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Nykøbing Falster

Denmark has a modern healthcare system that is accessible to visitors in case of emergency. Visitors should consider purchasing travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Nykøbing Falster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nykøbing Falster is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. The nearest airport is Copenhagen Airport, which is approximately 90 miles away. Visitors can also take a ferry from nearby ports in Germany or Sweden. Once in the city, many attractions are within walking distance, and taxis and buses are also available.
