Introduction to Falster

Falster is a beautiful island located in the Baltic Sea, belonging to Lolland-Falster in Denmark. It is a perfect destination for travelers who seek a serene environment, scenic nature, and rich history. With its white-sand beaches, natural wonders, and historic attractions, Falster caters to every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Falster

Falster offers many fascinating tourist attractions to explore. Here are the must-visit places in Falster:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Medieval Centre: A living museum with an authentic representation of life during the Middle Ages.
  • The Guldborgsund Zoo: A beautiful animal park that houses more than 1000 animals.
  • Marielyst Strand: A picturesque beach with long white sand and an enchanting atmosphere.
  • Nykøbing Falster: The largest city in Falster, famous for its architecture and rich history.
  • Stubbekøbing: A historic fishing town that boasts scenic views and rustic charm.

Natural Attractions of Falster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Knuthenborg Safari Park: A wildlife park with diverse animal species of the African continent.
  • Møns Klint: A picturesque cliff that towers over the Baltic Sea with a splendid view of the water.
  • Corselitze Forest: An enchanted forest with tall trees, beautiful ponds, and hiking trails.
  • Nyord: A small island with a wide range of wildlife and stunning views, perfect for a peaceful retreat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Falster

Falster is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following routes are worth a visit:

  • Hiking on Møn: Explore the beauty and nature of Møns Klint on this 4-hour hiking trail.
  • Gedesby to Marielyst langs: 11 kilometers bike path that takes you through beautiful coastlines, forests, and beachfronts.
  • Corselitze-forest Path: The forest path is one of the most beautiful trails in Falster, that takes you through meadows, forests, and fishponds.

Best Things to Do in Falster: Activities and Tours

Some of the must-try activities and tours in Falster are:

  • Sailing and fishing: The coast of Falster is a perfect place to go sailing or fishing for saltwater fish.
  • Horse Riding: Enjoy horse riding through the incredible landscapes and natural sceneries of Falster.
  • Kayaking: Kayaking is a great way to explore the coast of Falster and to spot marine wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Falster

Falster is famous for its delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Falster fish cakes: traditional Danish fish cakes made from locally caught fish.
  • Stjerneskud: a popular dish that consists of white fish, prawn, and caviar on slices of rye bread.
  • Rugbrød: a traditional Danish rye bread that can be eaten with any dish.

Local Beverages

  • Snaps: a Danish alcoholic drink made from distilled spirits and local herbs.
  • Beer: Denmark is known to have a thriving beer industry with plenty of options to choose from.
  • Mead: a honey-based alcoholic drink that has been enjoyed in Nordic countries for thousands of years.

Where to Stay in Falster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Falster has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. The following are some options:

  • Hotel Falster: located in the center of Nykøbing Falster, this is a perfect option for those who want to be close to restaurants and shops.
  • Marielyst Kro: a charming guesthouse located near the beach, ideal for a romantic getaway.
  • Guldborg Camping: a budget-friendly option for those who prefer camping, with excellent facilities and beautiful surroundings.

What to Buy in Falster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Falster provides an opportunity to buy some unique and traditional Danish souvenirs such as:

  • Handwoven carpets: Danish carpets are highly valued for their quality and visual appeal and make a perfect souvenir.
  • Glassware: Denmark is famous for its glassware, and many exceptional glass items can be purchased in Falster.
  • Danish Christmas decorations: Denmark is a famous producer of Christmas decorations, which are a perfect gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Falster

Denmark has a rich cultural heritage and traditions, which is reflected in the everyday life of Falster. Here are some customs to be aware of:

  • Hygge: a term that refers to the practice of creating a warm and cozy atmosphere and enjoying the good things in life.
  • Bicycle culture: Denmark is famous for its cycling culture, and biking is a common mode of transportation in Falster.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory, but it is expected if one receives exceptional service.

Best Time to Visit Falster

The best time to visit Falster is from June to August, which is the summer season. The weather during this time is warm, and the days are long, providing an ideal environment for outdoor activities. The autumn season is also a good time to visit, with fewer tourists and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Falster

Do:

  • Respect the nature and wildlife of Falster.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages of Falster.
  • Be aware of Danish customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment and public places.
  • Be too loud and disruptive in public places.
  • Overlook the importance of cycling to the Danish people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Falster

Falster is generally a safe place to visit, and crime rates remain low. However, travelers are advised to take the usual precautions such as taking care of personal belongings, avoiding dark and deserted areas, and being aware of scams and pickpockets.

Health in Falster

Denmark has a well-established health care system that provides free medical care to all citizens and visitors. There are hospitals and medical clinics located throughout Falster that can provide medical care if necessary.

Getting to Falster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Falster

The nearest airport to Falster is Copenhagen Airport, which is located about three hours by car from the island. From there, travelers can take public transport or rent a car to drive to Falster.

Bus Stations Near Falster

Falster is well connected by bus services, and there are several bus stations throughout the island. The main bus station in Falster is located in Nykøbing Falster, from where travelers can take buses to other cities and towns on the island.

Other Options

Travelers can also access Falster by train, with train stations located in Copenhagen and other major cities in Denmark. Rental cars and taxis are also available for those who prefer more privacy and convenience.

How to Get Around in Falster

Biking is a popular mode of transportation in Falster, and it is an excellent way to explore the island’s beauty. Public transport such as buses and trains are also available and provide convenient and affordable transportation options. Rental cars and taxis are also available for those who prefer more privacy and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Falster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Falster is a perfect base to explore other exciting destinations in Denmark. Here are some of the nearby spots to visit:

  • Zealand: A stunning island famous for Copenhagen and Kronborg Castle, which is only a short train ride from Falster.
  • Odessa: A beautiful city known for its historic sites and vibrant nightlife, located just across from the island of Falster.
  • Møns Klint: A picturesque cliff that towers over the Baltic Sea with a splendid view of the water.

Conclusion

Falster is a perfect destination for travelers who seek a peaceful retreat in the midst of natural beauty. With its scenic views, beautiful landscapes, and rich history, Falster offers a wide range of activities and attractions to suit every tourist. Plan your next vacation in Falster to experience the charm of this beautiful island in Denmark.
