Introduction to Ertholmene

Ertholmene is a small archipelago located off the coast of Bornholm, in the Nordic countries of Europe. Also known as Christiansø, this group of islands consists of two main islets, Christiansø and Frederiksø. With a rich history dating back to the 17th century, Ertholmene is a true hidden gem that offers cultural and natural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Ertholmene

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Fortress of Christiansø: Explore the impressive fortress that was built to protect the Danish kingdom from sea invaders.
  • The Old Garrison Museum: Learn about the history of the archipelago and the life on the fortifications during the 17th and 18th century.
  • The Church of Christiansø: Visit the charming church, built in 1821, and admire the unique interior painted with charming lace decorations.

Natural Attractions of Ertholmene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Windmill and Lighthouse Trail: Enjoy a scenic walk along the old windmill that dominates the island’s skyline and continue towards the lighthouse and the stunning sea view.
  • The Harbour: Stroll around the charming port, admire the cozy atmosphere, and the traditional fishing boats and the colorful huts.
  • The Beaches: Bath in the turquoise waters of the archipelago, and relax on the sandy beaches of Christiansø and Frederiksø.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ertholmene

  • Christiansø Trail: This marked hiking trail takes you around the entire island of Christiansø, with spectacular views on the way.
  • Frederiksø Loop: A short and easy hiking route around Frederiksø, suitable for families with children.
  • Biking on Ertholmene: Rent a bike and explore the archipelago at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Ertholmene: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking Adventure: Discover the beauty of Ertholmene on a unique kayak trip around the island.
  • Fishing Excursion: Join a half-day trip and experience the traditional Danish fishing culture.
  • Sunset Cruise: Enjoy a romantic sunset cruise around the archipelago, and admire the picturesque scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Ertholmene

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Herring: A traditional Danish dish that is served on rye bread, with potatoes and butter.
  • Pork Roast: A classic dish that is served with Danish potatoes and red cabbage.
  • Kærnemælkskoldskål: A refreshing dessert made from buttermilk, sugar, and lemon juice.

Local Beverages

  • Bornholmer Beer: Try the locally brewed beer that is made with pure Bornholm spring water.
  • Akvavit: The traditional Danish schnapps that is served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Ertholmene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Christiansø Gæstgiveri: A cozy guesthouse with a charming atmosphere and stunning sea views.
  • Frederiksø Kro: A cozy inn with a historic interior and excellent food and drink options.
  • Gyldensgårds Hotel & Restaurant: A boutique-style hotel with sea views and a restaurant serving traditional Danish cuisine.

What to Buy in Ertholmene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sea Salt: Take home a bag of locally produced sea salt, handmade with care.
  • Glass Art: Visit the glassblowers that make sophisticated and delicate glass art pieces on Frederiksø.
  • Bornholmer Stone: A unique stone that only comes from Bornholm, usually used as a building material, but also used for jewelry or home decorations.

Local Culture and Customs of Ertholmene

  • The archipelago has its local dialect, and the residents are proud of their heritage.
  • Fishing is an essential part of the culture and the economy of Ertholmene.
  • The Danish Royal Family visits the archipelago annually, and the residents celebrate the occasion with music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Ertholmene

The summer months of June to August are the best times to visit Ertholmene, with warm weather and long daylight hours. However, the archipelago offers unique experiences throughout the year, like visiting during Christmas to experience the traditional Danish Jul.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ertholmene

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the archipelago by foot or bike.
  • Try the local cuisine and drink.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore the safety instructions if taking a guided excursion.
  • Visit outside of the opening hours of attractions and restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ertholmene

  • Keep an eye on the weather, especially during the winter months, when the sea can be rough.
  • Swimming is only allowed on designated beaches.
  • Always follow the guide’s instructions during outdoor activities and tours.

Health in Ertholmene

  • The archipelago has a medical team and a small hospital for emergencies.
  • Bring your medication and medical supplies with you.
  • Beware of ticks that may carry Lyme disease.

Getting to Ertholmene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ertholmene

The nearest airport is Bornholm Airport, with frequent flights from Copenhagen.

Bus Stations Near Ertholmene

There are no bus stations on the island, and most visitors arrive through the harbor.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from Gudhjem on Bornholm Island to Christiansø, operated by Bornholmslinjen.

How to Get Around in Ertholmene

The archipelago is small enough to explore by foot or bike, which can be rented on the island.

Where to Go Next from Ertholmene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bornholm Island: A larger island with forests, beaches, and a rich history and culture.
  • Copenhagen: The capital city of Denmark that offers a trendy food scene and lively nightlife.
  • Skåne, Sweden: Experience the Scandinavian wilderness in the province with vast forest, fields, and lakes.

Conclusion

Ertholmene is a small Danish archipelago that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With plenty of activities, places to visit, and opportunities to relax, this hidden gem is a must-visit destination in the Nordic countries. Plan your trip with our comprehensive travel guide and discover the beauty of Ertholmene.
