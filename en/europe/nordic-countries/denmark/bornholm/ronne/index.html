Introduction to Rønne

Rønne is a charming coastal town located on the eastern side of the Danish island of Bornholm. The town serves as the administrative center of Bornholm and is a popular tourist destination thanks to its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and quaint streets. Rønne boasts a wide range of attractions catering to all visitors, whether they’re interested in history, culture, food, or outdoor adventures. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and local tips to help you make the most of your time in Rønne.

List of Places to Visit in Rønne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • Bornholms Museum: A comprehensive museum showcasing the history and culture of Bornholm island. The museum features exhibitions on art, archaeology, and local life.
  • Hjorths Fabrik: An old ceramics factory turned museum that displays the works of the influential Bornholm artists Johannes and Lizzie Hjorth.
  • Rønne Theater: Built-in 1819, Rønne Theater is an iconic landmark in the town and still functions as a venue for various cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Rønne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hammerknuden: A scenic area located to the north of Rønne that offers hiking and cycling trails, beautiful viewpoints, and stunning landscapes.
  • Ekkodalen: A beautiful valley on the outskirts of Rønne that is famous for its interesting rock formations and tranquil environment.
  • Nordskoven: A forested area on the northern part of the island that is great for hiking and enjoying the natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rønne

  • Hammeren Hiking Trail: A 5 km coastal trail that offers stunning views of the sea, cliffs, and forest.
  • Rønne-Cycle Route: A cycle path that takes you through the charming streets of Rønne town, past beautiful beaches, and stunning countryside.

Best Things to Do in Rønne: Activities and Tours

  • Bornholm Segway Tours: A fun and unique way to explore the town and its surroundings. The tours take you on a Segway through the streets of Rønne and the beautiful countryside of Bornholm.
  • Bornholms Akvarium: A must-visit attraction for families. The aquarium features over 70 species of fish and other marine creatures.
  • Bornholm Golf Club: A beautiful 18-hole golf course surrounded by natural landscapes and stunning coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rønne

Day 1 Visit Bornholms Museum and enjoy a guided tour

Food and Drink Scene of Rønne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sol over Gudhjem: A traditional Bornholm dish made with smoked herring, raw egg yolk, sour cream, and chives.
  • Stegt flæsk: A popular Danish dish made with fried pork belly, brown sauce, and boiled potatoes.
  • Bornholmsk Smørrebrød: An open-faced sandwich native to Bornholm that comes with a variety of traditional toppings such as smoked fish, cold cuts, and pickled vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bornholmer Bryg: A local beer brewed on the island that features a distinct hoppy taste and is brewed using local ingredients.
  • Bornholms Mosteri: A local cider company that produces a range of ciders made with apples and other fruits grown on the island.

Where to Stay in Rønne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Fredensborg: A beautiful hotel located on the outskirts of the town that features a spa, pool, and beautiful gardens.
  • Hotel Abildgaard: A charming hotel located in the heart of Rønne town, offering spacious rooms and a traditional Danish breakfast buffet.
  • Bornholms Vandrerhjem: A hostel located within walking distance of the town center that offers affordable dorm-style accommodation and communal facilities.

What to Buy in Rønne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bornholmsk Ceramics: Handmade ceramics crafted by local artisans and inspired by traditional Bornholm designs.
  • Smoked Fish: Bornholm is known for its smoked herring and salmon, which can be bought at local markets and speciality stores.
  • Bornholmsk Whisky: A unique whisky produced by the Bornholmsk Whisky Distillery, using barley grown on the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Rønne

Bornholm has a unique culture heavily influenced by its remote location and maritime history. The people of Bornholm are known for being friendly, welcoming, and proud of their island’s heritage. Some local customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting Rønne include:

  • “Ullim” and “Tulle” dolls which often represent the citizens of Bornholm, are a popular craft found all over the town.
  • Smoking fish is a popular traditional activity on the island and is often practiced at local fishing villages.
  • Visitors to Bornholm are encouraged to say “grøn” - the Bornholm word for “hello” - when greeting locals.

Best Time to Visit Rønne

The best time to visit Rønne is between May and September when temperatures range from 18°C to 22°C and there is a good chance of sunny weather. This is also the peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodation and tours in advance. The town can be visited throughout the year, however, visitors should expect colder temperatures, shorter daylight hours, and a lower chance of outdoor activities during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rønne

Do:

  • Do rent a bike or scooter to get around the island and enjoy the scenic landscapes
  • Do try traditional Bornholm food and drinks
  • Do respect local customs and traditions
  • Do take advantage of the many free attractions and tours available in Rønne

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb natural environments
  • Don’t rely on public transportation, especially outside the peak season
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for the season and outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Rønne

Rønne is generally a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still exercise caution and be mindful of their surroundings, especially at night or in crowded areas. Visitors should also take care when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling and be aware of any risks or hazards in the natural environment.

Health in Rønne

Visitors to Rønne should have no major health concerns, but those with specific medical needs should consult with their doctor before traveling. It is advisable to obtain appropriate health and travel insurance before embarking on your trip.

Getting to Rønne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rønne

The nearest airport to Rønne is Bornholm Airport, which is served by several airlines including SAS and Norwegian Air Shuttle.

Bus Stations Near Rønne

Rønne Bus Terminal serves as the primary transportation hub for buses serving the town and the surrounding areas. The terminal offers connections to other towns and cities on Bornholm Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Rønne by ferry. Bornholmslinjen operates ferry services to and from the Danish mainland as well as Sweden, Germany, and Poland.

How to Get Around in Rønne

Rønne is a small town that can be easily explored by foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to get around town and explore the beautiful surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Rønne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bornholm Island offers a range of unique attractions and experiences that are well worth exploring. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Gudhjem: A picturesque fishing village known for its smoked fish and stunning natural landscapes.
  • Dueodde: A beautiful beach located on the southern end of the island and known for its fine white sand.
  • Hammershus: The largest fortress in Northern Europe, located on the northern part of the island.

Conclusion

Rønne is a charming town that offers something for everyone, from beautiful beaches and natural landscapes to cultural attractions and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Rønne is sure to delight. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Rønne.
