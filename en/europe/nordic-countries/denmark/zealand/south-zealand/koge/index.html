Introduction to Køge

Located in Southern Zealand, Denmark, Køge is a charming town that offers a unique glimpse into the country’s rich history and natural beauty. Founded in the 13th century, Køge is home to well-preserved medieval buildings, museums, and galleries, as well as outdoor adventures and scenic trails that promise to delight visitors of all tastes and preferences.

Whether you’re interested in immersing yourself in local culture, exploring natural attractions, or indulging in authentic Nordic cuisine, Køge has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Køge.

List of Places to Visit in Køge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Køge has a rich heritage that dates back to the Middle Ages. Visitors can explore the following museums and historical places to learn more about the town’s fascinating history:

  • Køge Museum: Located in the heart of town, the Køge Museum offers a comprehensive overview of the town’s history and culture. The museum features exhibits on local crafts, agriculture, and fishing, as well as an impressive collection of artwork from the 19th century.

  • Køge Skitsesamling: This museum features sketches and drawings from some of Denmark’s most renowned artists. Here, visitors can view sketches by famous Danish painters such as Vilhelm Hammershøi and P.S. Krøyer.

  • Rosendal Palace: Located just outside of town, Rosendal Palace is a beautifully restored 18th-century royal palace that offers a unique glimpse into the lives of Denmark’s monarchs. Visitors can explore the palace’s lavish interiors and formal gardens.

Natural Attractions of Køge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Køge’s natural attractions offer something for everyone, whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, or simply lounging on the beach. Some of the top natural attractions in Køge include:

  • Køge Bay: A beautiful bay located just outside of town, Køge Bay features scenic beaches, rolling dunes, and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, or explore the bay’s numerous hiking and biking trails.

  • Køge Ridge: A scenic hiking and biking trail that runs through Køge Ridge, a stunning natural area that features dense forests, rolling hills, and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is well-marked and accessible to all skill levels.

  • Køge Harbor: A bustling harbor that offers numerous opportunities for boating, sailing, and fishing. Visitors can take a sunset cruise or rent a boat and explore the scenic coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Køge

One of the best ways to explore Køge’s natural attractions is by hiking or biking along one of the town’s many scenic routes. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Køge Ridge Trail: This scenic trail runs through the heart of Køge Ridge, a lush natural area that features dense forests, rolling hills, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Bay Trail: This trail runs along the scenic coastline of Køge Bay and features numerous opportunities for bird-watching and beachcombing.

  • Køge Circular Route: This easy, 12-kilometer route takes visitors on a scenic tour of Køge and its surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Køge: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring local culture or immersing yourself in natural beauty, Køge has a wide range of activities and tours to choose from. Some of the best things to do in Køge include:

  • Rosendal Palace Tour: Take a tour of one of Denmark’s most grand royal palaces, located just outside of Køge.

  • Køge Museum Tour: Explore the fascinating history and culture of Køge on a guided tour of the town’s most renowned museum.

  • Køge Bay Sunset Cruise: Experience the stunning natural beauty of Køge Bay on a sunset cruise along the scenic coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Køge

For visitors who want to experience everything that Køge has to offer, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Køge’s museums and historical sites, including Køge Museum and Køge Skitsesamling.

  • Day 2: Take a scenic hike or bike ride along Køge Ridge.

  • Day 3: Visit Rosendal Palace and explore the palace’s formal gardens.

  • Day 4: Relax on the beach or take a swim in the crystal-clear waters of Køge Bay.

  • Day 5: Take a sunset cruise along the coast or rent a boat and explore the scenic coastline.

  • Day 6: Try local cuisine and drinks at some of Køge’s most renowned restaurants and bars.

  • Day 7: Explore nearby attractions like the town of Stevns Klint or the island of Mon.

Food and Drink Scene of Køge

Køge’s food and drink scene is an important part of the town’s culture and heritage. Visitors can sample some of the best Nordic cuisine and local beverages at the following restaurants and bars:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smørrebrød: Open-faced sandwiches made with a variety of toppings, including pickled herring, smoked salmon, and roast beef.

  • Frikadeller: Traditional Danish meatballs made with pork or beef and served with potatoes and gravy.

  • Rugbrød: Hearty rye bread that’s a staple of Nordic cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Aquavit: A traditional Scandinavian spirit made with herbs and spices.

  • Craft Beer: Køge is home to several microbreweries that produce some of the best craft beer in Denmark.

  • Coffee: Denmark is renowned for its coffee culture, and Køge is no exception. Visitors can sample some of the best coffee in Denmark at local cafes and bakeries.

Where to Stay in Køge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Køge offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs and preferences. From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, Køge has something for everyone. Some of the top accommodations in Køge include:

  • Hotel Niels Juel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of town, offering stylish rooms and top-notch amenities.

  • Køge Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located just outside of town, offering affordable accommodations and easy access to Køge’s natural attractions.

  • Køge Bed & Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse located in the town center, offering comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Køge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to bring home a unique souvenir of their trip to Køge, we recommend the following local products:

  • Danish Design: Køge is home to several boutiques and galleries that sell unique Danish design items, including ceramics, furniture, and textiles.

  • Liquorice: Denmark is famous for its liquorice, and Køge is no exception. Visitors can pick up a bag of locally made liquorice at one of Køge’s many candy shops.

  • Smoked Fish: Køge is located near the sea, making it a prime location for smoked fish. Visitors can pick up a package of locally smoked salmon or herring at a local fish market.

Local Culture and Customs of Køge

Køge is a town with a rich cultural heritage that is heavily influenced by Nordic tradition and customs. Visitors should be aware of the following local customs and etiquette:

  • Hygge: A Danish concept that emphasizes coziness, comfort, and relaxation. Visitors to Køge should embrace this concept by exploring local cafes, bakeries, and taverns.

  • Punctuality: Danes place a high value on punctuality, so visitors should strive to be on time for appointments and meetings.

  • Tipping: It’s customary to tip between 10-15% at restaurants and cafes in Denmark.

Best Time to Visit Køge

The best time to visit Køge depends largely on your interests and preferences. The town offers something for everyone year-round, but some of the top activities and events in Køge include:

  • Summer: The summer months are a popular time to visit Køge, thanks to the town’s many outdoor activities and festivals.

  • Christmas: Køge is known for its charming Christmas markets and festive holiday spirit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Køge

Do:

  • Do embrace local customs and traditions, including hygge and punctuality.

  • Do sample local cuisine and drinks, including smørrebrød, aquavit, and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip at restaurants and cafes.

  • Don’t litter or damage natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Køge is a safe town that welcomes visitors from all over the world. Visitors should be aware of the following safety and health considerations:

Safety in Køge

  • Køge is a safe town, but visitors should take normal precautions to safeguard their belongings and avoid dangerous situations.

Health in Køge

  • Denmark offers a high standard of medical care. Visitors can access medical services at local clinics and hospitals.

Getting to Køge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Køge is easily accessible by a range of transportation options. In addition to driving, visitors can access Køge by:

  • Train: Køge is located on Denmark’s main train line, making it easy to access from Copenhagen and other major cities.

  • Bus: Numerous bus routes connect Køge to surrounding towns and cities.

  • Bicycle: Denmark is a bike-friendly country, and visitors can rent bicycles at numerous locations throughout Køge.

Airports Near Køge

  • Copenhagen Airport: Located just 40 kilometers north of Køge, Copenhagen Airport is Denmark’s busiest airport and a major international hub.

Bus Stations Near Køge

  • Køge Bus Station: Located in the town center, Køge Bus Station offers frequent connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at Copenhagen Airport or at numerous locations throughout Køge.

How to Get Around in Køge

Køge is a small town that is easily navigable by foot or bike. Visitors can also use public transportation options like buses and trains to get around town and the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Køge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors who want to explore more of Southern Zealand and Denmark, we recommend the following nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Stevns Klint: A stunning white cliff that overlooks the sea. Visitors can take a scenic hike along the cliff and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Island of Mon: A picturesque island that features rolling countryside, charming villages, and beautiful beaches. Visitors can explore the island’s many hiking and biking trails, or simply relax on the beach.

Conclusion

Køge is a charming town that offers something for everyone, whether you’re interested in exploring local culture, immersing yourself in natural beauty, or simply relaxing in a cozy cafe. With its rich history, top-notch museums and natural attractions, and vibrant food and drink scene, Køge is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling through Denmark and Southern Zealand.
