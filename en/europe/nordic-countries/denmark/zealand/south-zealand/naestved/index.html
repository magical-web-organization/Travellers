Introduction to Næstved

Næstved is a charming town located in the southern part of Zealand, Denmark. With a population of around 44,000 people, it’s the 14th largest municipality in Denmark and is known for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural landscapes, and thriving local businesses.

A trip to Næstved promises an unforgettable experience, with plenty of things to see, experience, and explore. Whether you’re a history enthusiast, an avid nature lover, or a foodie, Næstved has something to offer to everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll be taking a closer look at some of the best things to do, see, and experience in Næstved. From must-visit attractions to unique local experiences, we’ve got everything you need to plan the perfect trip!

List of Places to Visit in Næstved

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sct. Peders Kirke

Sct. Peders Kirke is a magnificent 14th-century church located in the heart of Næstved. Built in Gothic style, the church is home to a range of beautiful ornaments and decorations, including a stunning altarpiece and a magnificent organ. Visitors can also climb the tower of the church to take in stunning panoramic views over the town.

Gavnø Castle

Gavnø Castle is a magnificent historic castle located on the Gavnø Island, around 5 km from Næstved. The castle dates back to the 12th century and is surrounded by beautiful gardens and parkland. Visitors can explore the castle’s interiors and learn about its fascinating history, or stroll around the park and enjoy the stunning scenery.

BonBon Land

BonBon Land is a family-friendly amusement park located around 20 km from Næstved. The park is famous for its wacky and fun rides, all of which are based on various candy brands. Visitors can also enjoy a range of shows, performances, and events, and take home some delicious candy as souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Næstved: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Møns Klint

Møns Klint is a stunning series of chalk cliffs located around 50 km from Næstved. With heights of up to 120 meters, the cliffs offer breathtaking views over the Baltic Sea and the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike along the trails that wind their way along the cliff tops, or take a boat tour to see the cliffs from the water.

Grib Skov

Grib Skov is a magnificent forest located around 30 km from Næstved. As one of the largest forests in Denmark, it offers an endless array of hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Visitors can also explore the forest’s many lakes and streams, or try their hand at fishing or hunting.

Faxe Limestone Quarry

Faxe Limestone Quarry is a stunning natural wonder located around 20 km from Næstved. The quarry is home to a range of fascinating geological formations, including fossils and unique rock formations. Visitors can take a guided tour of the quarry and learn about its history and geology, or explore the surrounding nature reserve on foot or by bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Næstved

Best Things to Do in Næstved: Activities and Tours

In addition to the town’s many natural and cultural attractions, there are also plenty of fun activities and tours to enjoy in Næstved. Some of our top picks include:

Week-Long Itinerary for Næstved

Day 1: Explore the town’s historic sights, including Sct. Peders Kirke and the Næstved Museum.

Day 2: Visit the stunning Gavnø Castle and its surrounding gardens and parkland.

Day 3: Go hiking or biking in Grib Skov, one of Denmark’s largest forests.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby island of Møn, home to stunning chalk cliffs and a range of outdoor activities.

Day 5: Spend the day at BonBon Land, Denmark’s wackiest candy-themed amusement park.

Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day exploring the town’s many shops, cafes, and restaurants.

Day 7: Take a kayaking or canoeing tour along the Suså River to experience the town’s natural beauty up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Næstved

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Næstved is a gastronomic delight, with plenty of delicious local dishes to try. Some of our top picks include:

  • Flødeboller: A popular Danish sweet made from meringue, covered in chocolate and served with a range of different toppings.
  • Grønsagstærte: A savory vegetable tart made with a range of fresh fruits and vegetables.
  • Stegt flæsk med persillesovs: A classic Danish dish made with fried pork belly and a creamy parsley sauce.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Næstved has plenty to offer, too. Some of our top picks include:

  • Local craft beer from Næstved Bryghus
  • Fruit wines from Vesterhavegaarden
  • Apple juice from the Krenkerup Estate

Local Culture and Customs of Næstved

Næstved is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community and local pride. Some of the town’s unique customs and cultures include:

  • The annual Næstved Market, held every July, which features local food, drinks, crafts, and music.
  • The local tradition of “waving flags” during birthdays and other celebrations, where residents decorate their homes with brightly colored flags.
  • The vibrant local art scene, with a range of galleries, studios, and exhibitions showcasing the work of local artists.

Best Time to Visit Næstved

The best time to visit Næstved is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. However, Næstved is a year-round destination, with plenty to see and do during the spring, autumn, and winter months, too.

Do’s and Don’ts of Næstved

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, including flødeboller, grønsagstærte, and local craft beer.
  • Explore the town’s natural landscapes, including Grib Skov, Faxe Limestone Quarry, and Møns Klint.
  • Take part in local customs and traditions, such as the waving of flags during celebrations.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and biking.
  • Miss out on a visit to Gavnø Castle, one of the town’s most unique and stunning attractions.
  • Underestimate the town’s local art scene, with a range of galleries, exhibitions, and studios to explore.

Safety and Health

Safety in Næstved

Næstved is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a friendly and helpful community. However, like any destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions when traveling, such as staying aware of your surroundings, keeping valuables safe, and avoiding dark or isolated areas at night.

Health in Næstved

Næstved has a modern and well-equipped healthcare system, with a range of hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies located throughout the town and surrounding areas. Travelers are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Denmark to ensure they are covered for any potential medical emergencies.

Getting to Næstved: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Næstved

The nearest airport to Næstved is Copenhagen Airport, located around 100 km from the town center. From the airport, travelers can take a train or taxi to reach Næstved.

Bus Stations Near Næstved

Næstved has a central bus station located in the town center, with regular services to a range of destinations throughout Denmark.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Næstved by car or train, with the town located around 100 km from Copenhagen and connected to the national railway network.

How to Get Around in Næstved

Næstved is a small and compact town that is easy to get around on foot, with many of the town’s main attractions located within walking distance of each other. Alternatively, visitors can also rent bikes or take local buses to explore the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Næstved: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Copenhagen

Located just 100 km from Næstved, the Danish capital of Copenhagen is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city that offers an endless array of attractions, including historic landmarks, world-class museums, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Roskilde

Roskilde is a charming town located around 45 km from Næstved, famous for its impressive cathedral and Viking Ship Museum. Visitors can explore the town’s many historic sights, or attend the annual Roskilde Festival, one of Europe’s biggest music events.

Conclusion

Næstved is a hidden gem in the heart of Denmark, offering visitors a unique and unforgettable travel experience. From stunning natural landscapes to rich cultural heritage, the town has something to offer to everyone, and is a must-visit destination for any traveler heading to Scandinavia.
