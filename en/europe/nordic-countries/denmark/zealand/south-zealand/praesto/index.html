Introduction to Præstø

Located in South Zealand, Denmark, Præstø is a charming coastal town that offers an idyllic escape from the everyday hustle and bustle. This small town boasts of a rich cultural heritage, scenic vistas, and a plethora of outdoor activities, making it a popular destination for tourists.

Whether you want to explore the museums, admire stunning landscapes, go hiking or biking, or indulge in the local cuisine, Præstø has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, exciting activities, local culture, and safety tips, among others.

List of Places to Visit in Præstø

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Præstø Museum

Præstø Museum is a perfect place to learn about the town’s history and the surrounding area. Exhibits showcase the Viking Age, fishing industry, and cultural heritage. Visitors can also explore a recreated 18th-century classroom and the beautiful gardens.

Gåsetårnet

Originally a tower of the Church of Our Lady, Gåsetårnet has become an icon of Præstø over the centuries. This tower has been a lookout, clock tower, and even a prison. Visitors can climb to the top for stunning views of the town and its surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Præstø: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Geocenter Møns Klint

About 30 minutes’ drive from Præstø, Geocenter Møns Klint offers astonishing rock formations, hiking trails, and geological exhibits. The coastal cliffs and the turquoise Baltic Sea create a mesmerizing landscape that you won’t forget.

Præstø Fjord

The 24-kilometer-long Præstø Fjord is an ideal place for kayaking, fishing, and bird watching. Explore the secluded inlets, see the wildlife, and feel the tranquility of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Præstø

Camønoen

Camønoen is a popular long-distance hike with breathtaking views of the countryside and coastline. It is divided into six stages, and each has its own charm and interest.

Præstø Roundtrip

Præstø Roundtrip is a 30 km cycling route that winds through the beautiful landscapes, historic sites, and cultural landmarks of the town.

Best Things to Do in Præstø: Activities and Tours

Dodekalitten

Dodekalitten is an open-air museum of contemporary art that is worth a visit. It is a peculiar collection of twelve concrete monoliths, each forty feet tall, and each representing one month of the year.

Seal Safari

Take a seal safari and watch the seals basking on a sandbank in the sunshine. Book a guided tour and learn about the marine life and coastal ecosystems in this part of Denmark.

Food and Drink Scene of Præstø

Must-Try Dishes

Flæsk & Persille

Flæsk & Persille is a scrumptious dish of pan-fried pork belly, served over boiled potatoes and topped with parsley sauce. This classic Danish dish will leave you feeling satisfied and content.

Rugbrød

Rugbrød is Denmark’s traditional hearty rye bread, made with dark rye flour and fermented slowly. Enjoy it with butter and jam or open sandwich style with different toppings.

Local Beverages

Microbreweries

Experience the craft beer scene in Præstø by visiting the many microbreweries in the area. Tour the breweries, sample the beers, and learn about the brewing process.

Where to Stay in Præstø: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Frederiksminde

This five-star hotel is located in an 18th-century estate alongside the shore of Præstø Fjord. It offers exquisite, luxurious rooms, relaxing spa facilities, and exquisite dining at Restaurant Frederiksminde.

Købmandsgården

Købmandsgården is a guesthouse that provides rustic, cozy accommodations smack in the middle of the town. It features a spacious garden, communal kitchen, and affordable rates. Enjoy the historic charm of the house with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Præstø: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Præstø Røgeri

Præstø Røgeri is a smokehouse that produces mouth-watering smoked fish, including salmon, herring, trout, and eel. You can buy their smoked products as souvenirs or gifts for loved ones with a selection of marmalades, oils, and seasonings.

Gåsetårnet Souvenirs

Buy some of the Gåsetårnet souvenirs for a memory of your trip to Præstø. Find unique designs and locally made handicrafts for a keepsake of this beautiful town.

Local Culture and Customs of Præstø

Præstø is a town steeped in history and culture, and the locals take pride in their traditions. Spend some time wandering through the town to appreciate the beautifully preserved historic buildings, or check out one of the many festivals hosted throughout the year - such as the Kulturmødet where cultural projects and events are held. Remember, the quality of life in Denmark is very high, and the people are pleasant and courteous. Follow the Danish concept of ‘Hygge’ that promotes coziness and quality time with others and enjoy the moment.

Best Time to Visit Præstø

The best time to visit Præstø is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the town also offers captivating winter landscapes, cozy Christmas markets, and snow-covered landscapes to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Præstø

Do:

  • Explore the town’s historic landmarks and scenery.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for your outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be loud and disturb the peace.
  • Underestimate the weather; check the forecasts for activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Præstø

Præstø is known for its low crime rate, and the town is a generally safe place to visit. Follow general safety precautions like being vigilant in crowded areas, protecting your valuables, and locking your accommodation.

Health in Præstø

Denmark has an inclusive health system that covers everyone living in the country. However, it is recommended to have traveler’s insurance to be on the safer side.

Getting to Præstø: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Præstø

Copenhagen Airport (CPH) is the closest airport to Præstø. From there, you can take public transportation or book a taxi to Præstø, which is approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Præstø

Præstø Bus Station is situated in the town center and offers connections to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

You can easily rent a car or a bicycle to explore Præstø and surrounding areas at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Præstø

The best way to get around Præstø is on foot, exploring the cobbled alleys and cozy streets of the town. Bicycles are also a popular option for many tourists, and rental bicycles are available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Præstø: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Copenhagen

Visit the capital of Denmark, located less than 100 km away. Visit famous attractions like Tivoli Gardens, The Little Mermaid, and wander through the cobbled streets of the old town, Nyhavn.

Møn

Møn is only a short drive from Præstø and offers fantastic natural experiences. Discover the stunning chalk cliffs of Møns Klint and explore the countryside of Denmark.

Conclusion

Præstø, Denmark, is a charming town that offers visitors an excellent taste of Danish culture, history, and nature. Whether you choose to hike the Camønoen or relax in a cozy hotel, there’s something for everyone in this wondrous destination. Be sure to check out the local culture, indulge in the local cuisine, and take in the beauty of this unique town, and you’re sure to have a memorable experience.
