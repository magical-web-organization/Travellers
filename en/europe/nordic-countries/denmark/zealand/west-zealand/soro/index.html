Introduction to Sorø

Located in West Zealand, in Zealand, Denmark, Sorø is a small town with a rich history and scenic surroundings. The town is surrounded by forests and lakes that make it an ideal spot for outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and boating.

Sorø is also home to several cultural and historical landmarks, including museums, churches, and old buildings that showcase the town’s history. The town is also known for its food scene, with local restaurants that serve traditional Danish dishes.

In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour through Sorø and help you discover the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Sorø

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sorø Art Museum

Sorø Art Museum is one of the finest art museums in Denmark, with a vast collection of artwork spanning from the 18th century to the present day. The museum’s collection includes artwork from renowned artists such as Christoffer Wilhelm Eckersberg and Vilhelm Hammershøi.

Sorø Klosterkirke

Sorø Klosterkirke is a historic church that dates back to the 12th century. The church is home to an impressive collection of artwork, including impressive frescoes that depict scenes from the Bible.

Sorø Academy

Sorø Academy is a historic boarding school that was established in the 16th century by King Frederik II. The school has educated several prominent Danes, including the philosopher Søren Kierkegaard.

Natural Attractions of Sorø: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sorø Søerne

Sorø Søerne is a group of five lakes located in the heart of Sorø. The lakes offer opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and boating. You can also take a stroll around the lakes and enjoy the scenic views.

Stillinge Strand

Stillinge Strand is a beautiful beach located just a short drive from Sorø. The beach is known for its clean waters and soft sand, making it a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sorø

Sorø Søerne Rundt

Sorø Søerne Rundt is a 12-kilometer long biking and hiking trail that takes you around the five lakes of Sorø. The trail is relatively easy and is suitable for people of all ages and fitness levels.

Skjoldungernes Land National Park

Skjoldungernes Land National Park is a vast national park located in the nearby area. The park offers several hiking and biking trails that take you through the beautiful Danish countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sorø: Activities and Tours

Sorø Golf Klub

Sorø Golf Klub is a popular golf course located just a few kilometers outside of Sorø. The golf course offers stunning views of the surrounding area and is suitable for golfers of all levels.

Sorø Klosterpark

Sorø Klosterpark is a beautiful park located in the heart of Sorø. The park is home to several walking trails and picnic areas, making it an ideal spot for a relaxing day out with family and friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Sorø

Must-Try Dishes

Smørrebrød

Smørrebrød is a traditional Danish dish that consists of open-faced sandwiches. The toppings can vary, but some of the most popular ones include fish, cheese, and cold cuts.

Flæskesteg

Flæskesteg is a traditional Danish roast pork dish that is usually served with potatoes and red cabbage.

Local Beverages

Carlsberg Beer

Carlsberg Beer is one of Denmark’s most famous exports, and it is available in many bars and restaurants in Sorø.

Where to Stay in Sorø: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Comwell Sorø

Comwell Sorø is a four-star hotel located in the heart of Sorø. The hotel offers modern rooms, a spa, and a restaurant that serves delicious Danish cuisine.

The Cottage

The Cottage is a cozy guesthouse located just a few kilometers outside of Sorø. The guesthouse is surrounded by forests and offers a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Sorø: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sorø Upscale Food & Wine

Sorø Upscale Food & Wine is a gourmet store that offers a wide range of Danish delicacies, including cheese, chocolate, and wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Sorø

Danish culture is known for its emphasis on “hygge,” a concept that translates to “coziness.” In Sorø, you’ll find many cozy cafes and restaurants where you can enjoy a warm cup of coffee or tea.

Best Time to Visit Sorø

The best time to visit Sorø is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sorø

Do:

  • Do try some of the local dishes and beverages, such as Smørrebrød and Carlsberg Beer.
  • Do take a bike ride around Sorø Søerne.
  • Do visit Sorø Art Museum and Sorø Klosterkirke.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring a warm jacket and rain gear, as the weather in Denmark can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public spaces.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals, they are friendly and welcoming.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sorø

Sorø is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, it is always best to take precautions and keep an eye on your belongings when in public spaces.

Health in Sorø

Denmark has a high standard of healthcare, and Sorø has several medical facilities available, including a hospital and several clinics.

Getting to Sorø: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sorø

The closest airport to Sorø is Copenhagen Airport, which is located approximately 70 kilometers away. From there, you can take a train or a bus to Sorø.

Bus Stations Near Sorø

Sorø has several bus stations that are serviced by several bus companies, including Flixbus and Movia.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by car, Sorø is easily accessible via several highways. You can also take a train from Copenhagen to Sorø, which takes approximately an hour.

How to Get Around in Sorø

Sorø is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi companies available if you prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Sorø: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roskilde

Roskilde, known for its Viking history, is located just a short drive from Sorø. The town is home to several museums, including the Viking Ship Museum and the Roskilde Cathedral.

Copenhagen

Copenhagen, the capital of Denmark, is located approximately 70 kilometers away from Sorø. The city is known for its beautiful architecture, culture, and culinary scene.

Conclusion

Sorø is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of rich culture and scenic beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore museums and historical landmarks or enjoy outdoor adventures, Sorø has something to offer for everyone. So pack your bags and discover the beauty of Sorø for yourself.
