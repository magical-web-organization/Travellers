Introduction to Amager

Amager is an island located in Copenhagen, Denmark. It is known for its scenic beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities. The island is home to the Copenhagen airport and has excellent connectivity to the city center. This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, local culture, and useful tips for your trip to Amager.

List of Places to Visit in Amager

Amager has a lot to offer for tourists, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Blue Planet aquarium
  • Amager Strandpark beach
  • Christianshavn neighbourhood
  • The Dragør town
  • The Amager Museum

Natural Attractions of Amager: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Amager Nature Park
  • Amager Beach Park
  • The Fields Park
  • The Kastrup Sea Baths
  • The Øresund Bridge - connecting Denmark and Sweden

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amager

Amager’s natural landscape makes it a perfect place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Amager Strandpromenade - a 4.6 km long path along the beach
  • The Amager Nature Trail - a 10 km hiking trail through forests and grasslands, perfect for birdwatching
  • The North-South route - a 21 km biking path from Copenhagen to Dragor, with beautiful views of the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Amager: Activities and Tours

Amager offers a range of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some of the best things to do on the island:

  • Go on a walking or biking tour through the Christianshavn neighbourhood
  • Take a fishing trip from the Kastrup harbour
  • Visit the Blue Planet aquarium
  • Go kayaking in the Amager Nature Park
  • Participate in the annual Amager Beach Race.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amager

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Amager, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Blue Planet aquarium and explore the Amager beach park Day 2: Bike tour through the Christianshavn neighbourhood and visit Amager Museum Day 3: Go on a fishing trip from Kastrup harbour Day 4: Visit the Dragør town and explore the Øresund Bridge Day 5: Hiking in the Amager Nature Park and birdwatching Day 6: Take a kayaking tour through the Amager canals Day 7: Attend the annual Amager Beach Race and enjoy the beach festivities.

Food and Drink Scene of Amager

Amager has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a range of local and international cuisine options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smørrebrød (open-faced sandwiches)
  • Frikadeller meatballs
  • Flæskesteg (pork roast).

Local Beverages

  • Carlsberg beer
  • Aquavit spirit, with a strong fennel flavour.

Where to Stay in Amager: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amager offers a range of accommodation options for tourists, from luxury hotels to backpacker hostels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Crowne Plaza Copenhagen Towers

What to Buy in Amager: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amager has a unique souvenir and local product scene. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hand-painted pottery from Dragør town
  • Øresund Spice - a popular spice mix
  • Danish design products from the Fields shopping center
  • Local beers and aquavit from specialty stores
  • Danish pastries from bakeries.

Local Culture and Customs of Amager

When visiting Amager, it’s important to be aware of the local culture and customs. Some things to keep in mind are:

  • Danes take punctuality very seriously, so always arrive on time for appointments
  • Danes are reserved and formal, so try not to be too outgoing or informal with strangers
  • Several restaurants may include a tip in the bill, so check before tipping
  • Danes are environmentally conscious, so be mindful of sustainability practices.

Best Time to Visit Amager

The best time to visit Amager is late spring to early autumn (May to September) when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. However, Amager offers beautiful scenery and experiences year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amager

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Amager:

Do:

  • Use public transportation, as it’s the most convenient and eco-friendly option
  • Try the traditional Danish cuisine and local beverages
  • Respect Danish customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places
  • Litter or misuse public spaces
  • Touch artifacts in museums and cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amager

Amager is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and well-monitored public spaces. However, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Amager

Amager has excellent healthcare facilities, and emergency services are easily accessible.

Getting to Amager: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amager

The Copenhagen airport is located in Amager, making it easily accessible from anywhere in the world. Other airports nearby include Malmö and Ängelholm airports in Sweden.

Bus Stations Near Amager

Bus routes connect Amager to Copenhagen city center and other destinations in Denmark and Sweden. The most popular bus stations are Amagerbro Station, Sundbyvester Plads, and Tårnby Station.

Other Options

The public transportation system in Copenhagen includes buses, trains, and metro services, making it easy to navigate the city and beyond.

How to Get Around in Amager

Amager has excellent transportation infrastructure, including buses and bikes for rent. Taxis and rental cars are also available but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Amager: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring nearby destinations, here are some recommendations:

  • Copenhagen city center
  • Malmö, Sweden
  • Lolland, Denmark
  • Roskilde, Denmark.

Conclusion

Amager, Copenhagen, is the perfect destination for nature and culture lovers alike. From beaches and parks to museums and historical landmarks, Amager has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your next vacation and make the most out of your trip.
