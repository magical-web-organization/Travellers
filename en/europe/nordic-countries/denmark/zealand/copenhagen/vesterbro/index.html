Introduction to Vesterbro

Known for its eclectic mix of cultural attractions, lively nightlife, and hip restaurants, Vesterbro is a must-visit neighborhood in Copenhagen. Once a working-class district, Vesterbro has undergone a transformation in recent years and is now a trendy hub for fashion, arts, and entertainment.

List of Places to Visit in Vesterbro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Copenhagen Zoo: One of the oldest zoos in Europe, Copenhagen Zoo is home to a wide range of animals including elephants, tigers, and polar bears. Don’t miss the Arctic Ring exhibit, where you can see the resident polar bear up close and personal.
  • Frederiksberg Palace: Built in the 18th century, this stunning palace is surrounded by beautiful gardens and is one of the best places to learn about Denmark’s royal history.
  • Museum of Copenhagen: This museum chronicles the city’s history from its founding to the present day. Their permanent exhibition includes a fascinating display of artifacts from the Viking Age, Medieval Copenhagen, and the industrial revolution.

Natural Attractions of Vesterbro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Enghave Park: A popular spot for picnicking and sunbathing, Enghave Park is a beautiful green space that also offers stunning views of the city.
  • Copenhagen Harbour Baths: Take a dip in the refreshing sea water or relax on the wooden deck at these unique open-air swimming pools located right in the harbor.
  • Søndermarken: Adjacent to Frederiksberg Palace, Søndermarken is a large park with walking paths, bike lanes, and plenty of greenery. Don’t miss the chance to explore the peaceful Frederiksberg Have, a stunning formal garden located within the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vesterbro

For an active way to experience Vesterbro, try one of these hiking or biking routes:

  • Vesterbro City Loop: A popular route that takes you through the buzzing streets of Vesterbro and past some of its most beloved landmarks, including the Meatpacking District and Istedgade.
  • Copenhagen Lakes Tour: This route will take you on a scenic path alongside the many lakes in the city. It’s a great way to experience Copenhagen’s natural beauty while getting your exercise in for the day.
  • Freetown Christiania Tour: This route takes you through the one-of-a-kind community of Freetown Christiania. Learn about the area’s fascinating history and culture as you explore this unique and vibrant neighborhood.

Best Things to Do in Vesterbro: Activities and Tours

  • Copenhagen Street Food: Located in the Meatpacking District, this iconic food market is home to dozens of international food stalls serving up everything from barbecue to sushi.
  • Vesterbro Brewery Tour: Go behind the scenes at local breweries and learn about the beer-making process while sampling some of the best craft beers in Vesterbro.
  • Vesterbro Walking Tour: Discover the history and culture of Vesterbro with a local guide who can show you the hidden gems and must-see sights of the neighborhood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vesterbro

Day 1: Arrive in Vesterbro and take a walking tour of the neighborhood. Day 2: Visit the Copenhagen Zoo and have lunch at Copenhagen Street Food. Day 3: Take the Copenhagen Lakes Tour and have dinner at one of Vesterbro’s top-rated restaurants, such as Kødbyens Fiskebar. Day 4: Explore the Museums of Copenhagen and enjoy a picnic in Enghave Park. Day 5: Go on a Freetown Christiania Tour and enjoy the sunset at Søndermarken. Day 6: Visit Frederiksberg Palace and spend the afternoon shopping in Vesterbro’s trendy boutiques. Day 7: Take a Vesterbro brewery tour and have dinner at one of the beloved gastropubs, such as Mikkeller Bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Vesterbro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smørrebrød: A classic Danish dish, smørrebrød is an open-faced sandwich with various toppings such as herring, smoked salmon, or roast beef.
  • Frikadeller: These Danish meatballs are a delicious and hearty dish, often served with boiled potatoes and red cabbage.
  • Flødeboller: These chocolate-covered marshmallow treats are ubiquitous in Denmark, and you can find them in many of Vesterbro’s bakeries and coffee shops.

Local Beverages

  • Carlsberg Beer: This iconic Danish beer is brewed in Copenhagen and can be found at many local bars and restaurants in Vesterbro.
  • Akvavit: A traditional Scandinavian alcohol, akvavit is a flavored spirit typically consumed with food.

Where to Stay in Vesterbro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Axel Guldsmeden: This eco-friendly boutique hotel is known for its stylish decor and central location.
  • Urban House Copenhagen: This trendy hostel offers a variety of rooms, from private to shared dorms, and has a lively bar and restaurant on the premises.
  • Hotel Ottilia: The newest hotel in Vesterbro, Hotel Ottilia features minimalist-chic rooms and a rooftop bar with views of the city.

What to Buy in Vesterbro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Danish Design: Vesterbro is home to some of Copenhagen’s best design shops, such as Hay House and Normann Copenhagen, where you can find beautiful home decor and furniture.
  • Flea Market Finds: Every Saturday, the outdoor flea market at Halmtorvet is the place to find unique vintage and antique items, such as quirky home decorations and vintage clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Vesterbro

In Vesterbro, the locals are friendly and open-minded. English is widely spoken, and it’s easy to navigate the neighborhood with its many bike lanes and pedestrian-friendly streets. In Denmark, tipping is not expected, as a service charge is typically included in the bill.

Best Time to Visit Vesterbro

The best time to visit Vesterbro is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are many outdoor events and festivals happening throughout the neighborhood.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vesterbro

Do:

  • Do rent a bike and explore the neighborhood like a local.
  • Do try the street food and craft beer scene.
  • Do visit the outdoor swimming pools in the harbor.

Don’t:

  • Don’t jaywalk - it’s illegal in Denmark.
  • Don’t be overly loud or rowdy in public spaces.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for all the walking and biking you’ll be doing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vesterbro

Vesterbro is a relatively safe neighborhood in Copenhagen, but like any city, it’s important to watch your belongings and be aware of your surroundings. Keep an eye out for pickpockets in crowded areas and avoid wandering around alone late at night.

Health in Vesterbro

Denmark has an excellent healthcare system, and there are several clinics and hospitals located in Vesterbro. Be sure to stay hydrated during the summer months and use sunscreen to protect your skin.

Getting to Vesterbro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Vesterbro is easily accessible by public transportation, including buses, metro, and trains. The closest airport is Copenhagen Airport, which is a short train ride away.

Airports Near Vesterbro

  • Copenhagen Airport: Located just 8km from Vesterbro, Copenhagen Airport is the largest airport in the Nordic countries and serves many international flights.

Bus Stations Near Vesterbro

  • DGI Byen Bus Terminal: Located in the heart of Vesterbro, this bus terminal is served by several regional and international bus companies.

Other Options

  • Train: Vesterbro Station is located in the heart of the neighborhood and is served by several regional train lines.

How to Get Around in Vesterbro

The best way to get around Vesterbro is by bike or on foot. Many of the main attractions are within walking distance of each other, and the bike lanes throughout the neighborhood make it easy and safe to explore.

Where to Go Next from Vesterbro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tivoli Gardens: Located just a short bike ride from Vesterbro, Tivoli Gardens is one of the oldest amusement parks in the world and a must-visit attraction in Copenhagen.
  • Nyhavn: This iconic 17th-century waterfront district is known for its colorful buildings and lively bars and restaurants.
  • Malmö: A short train ride away from Copenhagen, the Swedish city of Malmö is a charming and picturesque destination with a vibrant arts scene and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Discover the vibrant and culture-rich neighborhood of Vesterbro with this comprehensive travel guide. From its top-rated restaurants and museums to its beautiful parks and outdoor activities, there’s something for every traveler in this trendy district of Copenhagen.
