Introduction to Frederiksberg

Located just west of Copenhagen, Frederiksberg is a stylish borough known for its lively atmosphere and the beautiful Frederiksberg Gardens. Home to a thriving cultural scene, picturesque neighborhoods, and a myriad of museums and attractions, Frederiksberg is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Denmark.

List of Places to Visit in Frederiksberg

Whether you’re interested in exploring the borough’s history, admiring its green spaces, or diving deep into its cultural offerings, here are some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Frederiksberg Slot: Located at the heart of Frederiksberg, this 18th-century palace is one of the borough’s most iconic landmarks. Take a guided tour to learn about its fascinating history and see its stunning interiors.

  • Cisternerne: Housed in an old water reservoir under Frederiksberg hill, Cisternerne is a unique contemporary art venue that showcases a different exhibition every year.

  • Storm P. Museum: Dedicated to the life and work of the famous Danish cartoonist and humorist, Robert Storm Petersen, this museum features a charming collection of his drawings, books, and personal items.

Natural Attractions of Frederiksberg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Frederiksberg Gardens: Spanning over 60 acres, Frederiksberg Gardens is a lush green oasis in the heart of the city. Take a stroll around its tranquil lakes, historic buildings, and beautiful flower beds, or rent a boat to paddle around its canals.

  • Søndermarken: Adjacent to Frederiksberg Gardens, Søndermarken is a vast forested area that offers a range of hiking and biking trails, as well as several picnic spots.

  • Skydebanehaven: This small but charming park is located in the former shooting range of the Danish army. Today, it’s a popular hangout spot for locals and visitors alike, offering beautiful views of the city from its hilltop location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frederiksberg

With its lush parks and forests, Frederiksberg is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Frederiksberg Runddel to Enghave Station: This relaxing route takes you through the heart of Frederiksberg, past the palace and the gardens, and along the scenic Frederiksberg Alle boulevard.

  • Søndermarken Circuit: This 5 km loop around Søndermarken takes you through forested areas, hills, and open fields, offering stunning views of the city.

  • Frederiksberg to Dyrehaven: If you’re up for a longer ride, this 20 km route takes you from Frederiksberg to Dyrehaven, a beautiful forested area north of the city center.

Best Things to Do in Frederiksberg: Activities and Tours

From museum visits to food tours, there are plenty of things to do in Frederiksberg that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Frederiksberg Food Tour: Take a guided culinary tour of Frederiksberg and discover the borough’s best restaurants, cafes, and food markets.

  • Frederiksberg Palace Tour: Join a guided tour of Frederiksberg Palace and learn about its fascinating history and architecture.

  • Bicycle Rental: Rent a bike from one of the many rental shops in the borough and explore Frederiksberg’s parks and attractions at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frederiksberg

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Frederiksberg, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
Day 1 Take a guided tour of Frederiksberg Palace, explore the palace gardens, and have lunch at one of the nearby restaurants.
Day 2 Go for a walk in Frederiksberg Gardens, visit Cisternerne, and have a picnic in the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Frederiksberg

From traditional Danish fare to international cuisine, Frederiksberg has a vibrant food and drink scene that caters to all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smørrebrød: A Danish classic, smørrebrød is an open sandwich made with rye bread and various toppings like pickled herring, shrimp, or liver pâté.

  • Frikadeller: Danish meatballs made with pork, onions, and spices, served with potatoes and gravy.

  • Wienerbrød: A Danish pastry made with laminated dough and filled with custard, jam, or marzipan.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Denmark is famous for its beer, and Frederiksberg has several excellent breweries and pubs that serve a range of local brews. Check out Mikkeller, Fermentoren, or Ølsnedkeren for a taste.

  • Aquavit: A traditional Scandinavian spirit made with herbs and spices like caraway, dill, and anise, often served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Frederiksberg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Frederiksberg has plenty of choices to suit different needs and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • First Hotel Mayfair: A stylish four-star hotel located near Frederiksberg Gardens, offering spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant.

  • Avenue Hotel Copenhagen: A cozy boutique hotel located in the heart of Frederiksberg, featuring unique decor, a lounge area, and a bar.

  • Hostel Jørgensen: A budget-friendly hostel located near a metro station, offering dormitory-style rooms and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Frederiksberg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to take home from your trip to Frederiksberg? Here are some of the local products and shops to check out:

  • Royal Copenhagen: A famous Danish brand of luxury porcelain and glassware, with a flagship store located on Frederiksberg Alle.

  • Frederiksberg Chokolade: A local chocolatier that makes handmade chocolates, truffles, and pralines using high-quality Belgian chocolate.

  • Frederiksberg Farmers’ Market: Held every Saturday on Gammel Kongevej, this lively market offers a range of fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts from local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Frederiksberg

As a Danish borough, Frederiksberg shares many cultural traditions and customs with the rest of the country. Some of the key elements of Danish culture include:

  • Hygge: A famous Danish concept that refers to a feeling of coziness, comfort, and well-being, often achieved through spending time with loved ones, enjoying good food and drink, and relaxing in a cozy setting.

  • Smiling: Danes are known for their friendliness and positivity, and smiling is a common way to show appreciation and good manners in social situations.

  • Punctuality: Danes value punctuality and expect others to be on time for appointments and meetings.

Best Time to Visit Frederiksberg

The best time to visit Frederiksberg depends on your preferences and interests. Here are some things to consider when planning your trip:

  • Summer: June to August is peak tourist season in Frederiksberg, with warm weather, long days, and lively outdoor events like concerts and festivals.

  • Fall: September to November is a quieter time in Frederiksberg, with cooler temperatures, fewer crowds, and beautiful fall foliage in the parks.

  • Winter: December to February is a magical time to visit Frederiksberg, with cozy Christmas markets, festive decorations, and the possibility of snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Frederiksberg

To make the most of your trip to Frederiksberg, here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try Danish cuisine: From smørrebrød to frikadeller, Danish food is delicious and distinct. Make sure to try some local dishes during your visit.

  • Do rent a bike: Biking is a great way to explore Frederiksberg’s parks and streets, and there are many bike rental shops available.

  • Do respect the parks and gardens: Frederiksberg’s parks and gardens are beautiful and well-maintained. Make sure to dispose of your trash properly and follow the posted rules.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be late: Danes value punctuality, so make sure to arrive on time for appointments, meetings, and tours.

  • Don’t forget to say “tak”: “Tak” is Danish for “thank you,” and it’s a courteous way to show appreciation and respect.

  • Don’t litter: Denmark is known for its clean and green environment, and littering is not tolerated. Make sure to dispose of your trash properly.

Safety and Health

Denmark is a safe and healthy country, with low crime rates and excellent healthcare. Here are some things to keep in mind when it comes to safety and health in Frederiksberg:

Safety in Frederiksberg

  • Frederiksberg is a relatively safe borough, but it’s still important to take common sense precautions like locking your doors, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of your surroundings at night.

  • Denmark has a low level of violent crime, but pickpocketing and petty theft can occur in crowded tourist areas, so keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Frederiksberg

  • Denmark has a high standard of healthcare, and all emergency services are accessible by dialing 112.

  • Remember to pack any necessary prescription medications, and make sure you have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Frederiksberg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Frederiksberg is easy, thanks to its central location and excellent transportation links. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Frederiksberg

  • Copenhagen Airport (CPH): Located just 10 km east of Frederiksberg, Copenhagen Airport is the largest airport in Denmark and serves a range of international and domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Frederiksberg

  • Copenhagen Central Station: Located in the heart of Copenhagen, Copenhagen Central Station services both national and international bus routes.

  • Vesterport Station: Located in Frederiksberg, Vesterport Station services regional bus routes to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station to Frederiksberg is Copenhagen Central Station, which services a range of domestic and international routes.

  • Metro: The Copenhagen Metro serves Frederiksberg with several stops, including Frederiksberg Station and Fasanvej Station.

How to Get Around in Frederiksberg

Frederiksberg is a compact borough that’s easy to navigate on foot, by bike, or by public transportation. Here are some of the best ways to get around:

  • Bike: Biking is a popular and efficient way to get around Frederiksberg, with a range of bike rental shops and bike lanes available.

  • Public Transportation: The Copenhagen Metro and bus system serve Frederiksberg, with several stops located throughout the borough.

  • Walking: Walking is a great way to explore Frederiksberg’s parks, gardens, and neighborhoods, and many attractions are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Frederiksberg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time after exploring Frederiksberg, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Copenhagen: Denmark’s vibrant capital city is just a short Metro ride away from Frederiksberg, offering a range of attractions, shopping, dining, and nightlife options.

  • Helsingør: Located north of Copenhagen, Helsingør is a charming port town that’s home to the famous Kronborg Castle, the setting of Shakespeare’s Hamlet.

  • Roskilde: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Roskilde is an ancient town with a rich cultural history, stunning cathedral, and a range of museums and festivals.

Conclusion

Frederiksberg is a charming, vibrant borough that offers a wealth of attractions, dining options, and outdoor adventures. With its unique mix of historical landmarks, cultural offerings, and natural beauty, Frederiksberg is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Denmark.
