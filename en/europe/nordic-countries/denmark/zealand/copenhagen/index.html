Introduction to Copenhagen

Copenhagen, the capital of Denmark, is a vibrant and picturesque city famous for its blend of modern architecture and rich cultural heritage. The city is known for its design, fashion, art, and food. With its excellent transportation system, it is one of the most walkable cities in the world, making it easy to explore the city on foot. Wondering where to go, what to see, and what to eat? This comprehensive guide to Copenhagen will help you plan your trip and make the most of your time.

List of Places to Visit in Copenhagen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Copenhagen is rich in history and culture, offering plenty of museums and historical sites to visit. Below are some must-visit places.

The National Museum of Denmark

The National Museum of Denmark is the largest museum in the country and offers visitors an opportunity to explore the history and culture of Denmark from the prehistoric era to the present day. The museum has extensive collections of costumes, furniture, weapons, and other objects that reflect the development of Danish society and culture.

Amalienborg Palace

Amalienborg Palace is the home of the Danish royal family and a significant landmark of the city. Visitors can take a tour of the palace, which includes the Royal Reception Room, the Audience Chamber, the Dining Room, and the Royal Chapel. The palace also has an impressive collection of portraits of Danish royalty.

The Little Mermaid Statue

One of the most famous landmarks of Copenhagen, the Little Mermaid Statue, is inspired by Hans Christian Andersen’s fairy tale. The statue is located on the Langelinie promenade, and visitors can enjoy stunning views of the harbor while admiring this iconic statue.

Natural Attractions of Copenhagen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Copenhagen has plenty of natural attractions that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Below are some must-visit natural attractions.

Tivoli Gardens

Tivoli Gardens is one of the oldest amusement parks in the world and a significant tourist attraction in Copenhagen. It offers visitors thrilling rides, beautiful gardens, and various cultural performances, making it a perfect spot for families and couples alike.

The Botanical Garden

The Botanical Garden is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city. Visitors can enjoy the beauty of various plants and flowers, including tropical plants, cacti, and bonsai trees. The garden also houses several greenhouses with exotic plants from around the world.

Nyhavn

Nyhavn is a 17th-century waterfront district with colorful buildings and bustling cafes. It is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, and visitors can take a boat tour to explore the harbor and surrounding neighborhoods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Copenhagen

Copenhagen is a bicycle-friendly city, and exploring it on foot or two wheels is the best way to experience its beauty. Below are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Copenhagen.

The Lakes

The Lakes is a beautiful scenic area in the heart of the city with three artificial lakes that are perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride. The area is surrounded by parks, cafes, and restaurants, making it a perfect spot for a family picnic or a romantic walk.

The Green Path

The Green Path is a 5 km cycling and walking route that takes visitors through some of Copenhagen’s most beautiful parks and gardens. The path is a perfect way to enjoy Copenhagen’s natural beauty and escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

Best Things to Do in Copenhagen: Activities and Tours

Copenhagen has plenty of exciting things to see and do, including unique activities and tours for visitors to experience. Below are some must-try activities and tours in Copenhagen.

Food Tours

Copenhagen has an excellent food scene, and food tours are a great way to discover the city’s culinary delights. Visitors can taste traditional Danish dishes such as Smørrebrød (open sandwiches) and Frikadeller (Danish meatballs) while learning about the country’s food culture.

Canal Tours

Canal tours are a popular way to explore Copenhagen’s harbor and its beautiful architecture. Visitors can relax on a boat while taking in the sights and sounds of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Copenhagen

Denmark has a rich and diverse food culture, and Copenhagen’s food scene is a reflection of that. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages that visitors should not miss out on when in the city.

Must-Try Dishes

Smørrebrød

Smørrebrød is an open sandwich typically made with rye bread and topped with various toppings, including pickled herring, salmon, and meats like roast beef or liver pate. It’s a must-try dish for visitors to experience the Danish food culture.

Frikadeller

Frikadeller is a Danish meatball made with a combination of beef and pork, onions, breadcrumbs, and herbs. They are typically served with boiled potatoes and gravy and are another traditional Danish dish.

Local Beverages

Carlsberg Beer

Carlsberg beer is a world-famous beer and a significant part of Denmark’s beer history. Visitors can enjoy a cold pint at one of the many bars located throughout the city.

Akvavit

Akvavit is a traditional Scandinavian liquor made from potatoes and flavored with various herbs and spices. It’s a local favorite and a must-try for visitors.

Where to Stay in Copenhagen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Copenhagen has a range of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Below are some options for visitors to consider when traveling to Copenhagen.

Hotels

Hotel SKT Petri

Hotel SKT Petri is a modern and elegant hotel located in the heart of the city. It offers visitors comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a fitness center.

Nimb Hotel

Nimb Hotel is a luxurious boutique hotel with excellent views of Tivoli Gardens. It offers visitors comfortable rooms, a spa, and an on-site Michelin-starred restaurant.

Hostels

Danhostel Copenhagen City

Danhostel Copenhagen City is a modern and comfortable hostel located in a great location near popular attractions. It offers visitors clean and affordable rooms, free breakfast, and a common area to meet other travelers.

Generator Copenhagen

Generator Copenhagen is a stylish and trendy hostel located in the heart of the city. It offers visitors a range of room options, including private rooms and shared dorms. They also have on-site bars and restaurants.

What to Buy in Copenhagen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Copenhagen has plenty of unique and local products that visitors can bring back as souvenirs. Below are some local products to consider when shopping in the city.

Pottery

Denmark is famous for its pottery, and visitors can find unique and beautiful pieces in Copenhagen. From traditional designs to modern and contemporary pieces, visitors are sure to find something that catches their eye.

Danish Design

Denmark is known for its design, and visitors can find plenty of unique and stylish items in Copenhagen, including furniture, clothing, and home decor items.

Local Culture and Customs of Copenhagen

Copenhagen is a city with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be aware of some local customs and traditions. Below are some things to keep in mind when visiting Copenhagen.

Hygge

Hygge is a Danish concept that translates to a feeling of coziness and contentment, creating a warm atmosphere for oneself and those around them. When visiting Copenhagen, make sure to experience this cozy and relaxed atmosphere by visiting cafes, galleries, and restaurants.

Tipping

Tipping is not expected in Denmark, as service charges are usually included in the final bill. However, if visitors experience exceptional service, they can leave a small tip as a gesture of appreciation.

Best Time to Visit Copenhagen

The best time to visit Copenhagen is from May to September when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the city is bustling with outdoor activities and cultural events. However, visitors can also consider visiting in the winter months when the city is less crowded and has a cozy atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Copenhagen

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Copenhagen.

Do:

  • Do try traditional Danish food like Smørrebrød and Frikadeller.
  • Do explore the city by bicycle or on foot.
  • Do visit Tivoli Gardens and Amalienborg Palace.
  • Do experience Hygge by visiting cafes and galleries.

Don’t:

  • Don’t jaywalk.
  • Don’t tip unless you receive exceptional service.
  • Don’t forget to wear comfortable shoes to explore the city by foot or bicycle.
  • Don’t forget to respect Danish customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Copenhagen

Copenhagen is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions as they would in any major city. Be aware of petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag-snatching, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Health in Copenhagen

Denmark has a public healthcare system, and visitors can access emergency medical services if needed. Visitors should also ensure they have travel health insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Copenhagen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Copenhagen is easily accessible by air, train, and bus. Below are some options for visitors to consider when traveling to Copenhagen.

Airports Near Copenhagen

Copenhagen International Airport is the biggest airport in Denmark and is located just 8 km from the city center. Visitors can take the metro or train from the airport to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Copenhagen

Copenhagen Central Bus Station is located in the city center and serves international and domestic destinations. Visitors can take the bus to other destinations in Denmark and Europe.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Copenhagen by train or car. The city is well-connected with other European cities via rail, and driving is also an option for visitors who want to explore Denmark’s countryside.

How to Get Around in Copenhagen

Copenhagen is a very walkable city, and visitors can explore most of the city on foot. Visitors can also use the city’s extensive public transportation system, including buses, trains, and the metro. Bicycles are also a popular way to get around, with bike rental services located throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Copenhagen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Copenhagen is a gateway to other fascinating destinations in Scandinavia. Below are some nearby destinations to consider when planning a trip from Copenhagen.

Malmö, Sweden

Malmö is located just across the bridge from Copenhagen and is a vibrant and multicultural city with great food, museums, and art. Visitors can take a train from Copenhagen to Malmö in just 40 minutes.

Roskilde, Denmark

Roskilde is a historic city located just 30 km from Copenhagen. It is home to the famous Roskilde Cathedral and the Viking Ship Museum, making it a perfect day trip destination for visitors.

Conclusion

Copenhagen is a beautiful city with plenty of cultural sites, natural attractions, and exciting activities to offer visitors. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can explore the city’s best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and traditions that make Copenhagen one of the most beautiful cities in the world.
