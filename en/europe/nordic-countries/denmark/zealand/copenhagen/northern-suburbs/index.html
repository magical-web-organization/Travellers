Complete Guide to Northern Suburbs, Copenhagen

If you’re looking to explore one of the hidden gems of Copenhagen, the Northern Suburbs are definitely worth considering. This area offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and stunning views that will leave you in awe. In this complete guide to the Northern Suburbs, we’ll cover the best things to do and see, where to stay and eat, and much more.

Introduction to Northern Suburbs

The Northern Suburbs are located on Zealand, the largest island in Denmark. It’s a cluster of 16 municipalities, each with its own distinctive charm. The area is known for its natural beauty, parks, and lakes, attracting tourists and locals alike. It’s easy to access from Copenhagen’s city center, making it an ideal day trip destination.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Suburbs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Louisiana Museum of Modern Art - This museum boasts a world-renowned collection of contemporary and modern art, with spectacular views of the Øresund Strait.

  2. Kronborg Castle - This UNESCO World Heritage site is known as “Hamlet’s Castle,” as it was the inspiration for William Shakespeare’s play. The castle’s grand halls, hidden passages, and striking architecture make it a must-visit.

  3. Frederiksborg Castle - This Renaissance castle in Hillerød is home to the Museum of National History, showcasing Denmark’s rich history through art and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Northern Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dyrehaven - This deer park is home to 2,000 free-roaming deer and is perfect for hiking, jogging, and cycling.

  2. The Deer Park Lake - Located in Jægersborg Deer Park, this picturesque lake is an excellent spot for picnicking, birdwatching, and boating.

  3. The Hermitage Castle - Set in a stunning forest, this castle offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and plenty of walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern suburbs.

  1. Kongernes Nordsjælland - This magnificent national park has numerous hiking and biking trails. It’s home to several historical landmarks, including Hillerød’s iconic Frederiksborg Castle.

  2. Jægersborg Dyrehave - This park has several walking paths and cycling routes, offering stunning views of the region’s natural landscapes.

  3. Furesø - This vast lake is surrounded by parks, beaches, and biking paths. It’s an ideal spot for a scenic cycle ride, picnic, or boat trip.

Best Things to Do in Northern suburbs: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a canal tour - Experience the Danish capital’s sights and sounds from a unique perspective on a 1-hour Copenhagen canal tour.

  2. Beach hopping - With miles of sandy beaches, you can enjoy a refreshing swim or a relaxing sunbathing session.

  3. Day trip to Sweden - Hop on a ferry and visit the charming Swedish seaside town of Helsingborg.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern suburbs

This sample itinerary covers some of the area’s top attractions:

Day 1: Visit the Louisiana Museum of Modern Art and take a leisure walk through the stunning Sculpture Garden.

Day 2: Explore Kronborg Castle and spend the afternoon in nearby Helsingør.

Day 3: Spend the day hiking in Kongernes Nordsjælland National Park.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Sweden and visit the seaside town of Helsingborg.

Day 5: Discover the hidden gem of Fredensborg Palace and its beautiful gardens.

Day 6: Explore the shores of Furesø Lake and enjoy cycling around its picturesque perimeter.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at the Bellevue Strand beach and indulge in some seafood delicacies.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern suburbs

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Smørrebrød - Classic Danish open-faced sandwiches with unique toppings.

  2. Æbleskiver - Traditional Danish pastries in the shape of balls served with jam and sugar.

  3. Frikadeller - Danish-style meatballs with a crispy exterior and a soft interior.

Local Beverages

  1. Danish beer - Try local brews like Tuborg, Carlsberg, and Mikkeller.

  2. Snaps - Danish schnapps, distilled from various herbs, is a popular liquor in the region.

  3. Aalborg Akvavit - This traditional Danish spirit is infused with dill, coriander, caraway, and other herbs.

Where to Stay in Northern suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Comwell Holte - A charming hotel with a lovely restaurant near Holte station.

  2. Kokkedal Castle - A luxurious hotel with a spa and 18-hole golf course in Hørsholm.

  3. Gentofte Hotel - A cozy yet stylish hotel with free parking and Wi-Fi in Gentofte.

What to Buy in Northern suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Danish design - Pick up unique Danish designs from places like Illums Bolighus and Hay.

  2. Local food products - Explore farmers’ markets and delicatessens for local jams, honey, wine, and cheese.

  3. Danish fashion - Shop for fashionable Scandinavian clothing brands in the region’s boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern suburbs

The Northern Suburbs have an interesting culture and customs. Danes are known for their hygge (cosy) lifestyle and eco-concerns. Visitors can experience this by exploring the numerous parks and lakes, cycling, or sitting in a cozy coffee shop, sipping coffee or Snaps.

Best Time to Visit Northern suburbs

The best time to visit the Northern Suburbs is during the summer months between June and August. The weather is moderate, and the days are longer, making it the perfect time for outdoor adventures, sightseeing, and beach activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern suburbs

Do:

  1. Rent a bike and explore the region’s scenic bike trails.

  2. Try Danish pastries at local bakeries.

  3. Respect the countryside and environment – don’t litter.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke in public places - It’s illegal in Denmark.

  2. Drink and drive - Alcohol limits are strict in Denmark.

  3. Be too casual - Dress smartly when visiting museums, galleries, and restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern suburbs

The Northern Suburbs are generally safe for travelers. However, it’s always important to take precautions like carrying a phone and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Northern suburbs

Medical facilities are easily accessible in the region. Denmark has a highly advanced healthcare system, and emergency services are available 24/7.

Getting to Northern suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern suburbs

The nearest international airports are Copenhagen Airport and Malmö Airport in Sweden.

Bus Stations Near Northern suburbs

There are several bus stations in the region, including Hillerød Station, Birkerød Station, and Hørsholm Station.

Other Options

Trains are the most convenient mode of transportation to the Northern Suburbs. The S-train, operated by DSB, connects Copenhagen and its suburbs.

How to Get Around in Northern suburbs

Biking is the most comprehensive way to explore the Northern Suburbs, given the region’s many bike lanes and trails. You can also use public transport like trains and buses to get around quickly and see more from a new perspective.

Where to Go Next from Northern suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. The Danish Riviera - Explore the beautiful seaside towns of Hornbæk and Gilleleje.

  2. Roskilde - Visit the Viking Ship Museum and explore the historical cathedral town.

  3. Copenhagen - Take the train from central stations like Hillerød or Birkerød to Denmark’s capital for a high dose of modern urban culture, entertainment, dining, and shopping opportunities.

Conclusion

The Northern Suburbs of Copenhagen are an impressive, diverse destination that provides for a perfect break from the hustle and bustle of the city center. Whether you’re seeking cultural attractions or outdoor pursuits, these suburbs have something to offer every type of traveler. Hopefully, this guide has given you plenty of inspiration and practical tips to plan your visit and fully enjoy what this area has to offer.
