Sydfynske Øhav Travel Guide: Explore Denmark’s Hidden Gem

Sydfynske Øhav is a group of beautiful islands and islets located in the southern part of Funen island in Denmark. It comprises 55 islands, including Ærø, Skarø, Drejø, and Avernakø - each with its own unique charm and attractions. From pristine beaches and dense forests to historic towns and cultural landmarks, Sydfynske Øhav offers a plethora of experiences for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see, do, eat, and explore in Sydfynske Øhav, along with practical information to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Sydfynske Øhav

Sydfynske Øhav offers a diverse range of attractions, including museums, historical sites, natural wonders, hiking trails, and more. Here are some of the top places to visit in Sydfynske Øhav:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Danish Railway Museum in Odense: Discover the rich history of Danish railways through interactive exhibits and vintage locomotives.
  • Egeskov Castle: A well-preserved Renaissance castle surrounded by beautiful gardens, vintage cars, and a treetop walk for stunning views.
  • Valdemars Castle: A magnificent Baroque-style castle built in the 17th century and adorned with antique furniture, artworks, and exquisite gardens.
  • The Funen Village: An open-air museum featuring 25 historic buildings that take visitors back to the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Sydfynske Øhav: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Svanninge Bakker: Rolling hills with lush vegetation, grazing cattle, and breathtaking views over the archipelago.
  • Skoven i Skoven: A dense beech forest with hiking trails, picnic areas, and grazing sheep throughout.
  • Ristinge Klint: A stunning cliff formation with white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Bregninge Kirke: A beautiful medieval church with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sydfynske Øhav

Sydfynske Øhav is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many scenic routes that offer a glimpse of the archipelago’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Here are some popular trails to explore:

  • Island Trail: A 220 km-long trail that takes hikers and bikers along the coast of Funen and through Sydfynske Øhav, passing by charming towns, historic landmarks, and stunning natural landscapes.
  • Ærø Trail: A 33 km-long trail that circles the island of Ærø, with spectacular views of the sea, fields, and forests, as well as opportunities to visit historic sites, museums, and art galleries.

Best Things to Do in Sydfynske Øhav: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a solo traveler or a family looking for fun activities, Sydfynske Øhav has plenty to offer. Here are some top things to do in the archipelago:

  • Kayaking in the Archipelago: Rent a kayak or book a tour to explore the secluded coves, hidden beaches, and charming villages of Sydfynske Øhav.
  • Fishing and Crabbing: Try your luck at fishing or crabbing, and enjoy the fresh catch with locals or at one of the many seafood restaurants in the area.
  • Painting and Photography Workshops: Join a workshop to learn painting or photography skills while capturing the stunning landscapes and seascapes of Sydfynske Øhav.
  • Local Market Tours: Visit the many local markets in Sydfynske Øhav to buy fresh produce, artisanal products, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sydfynske Øhav

If you’re planning a longer trip to Sydfynske Øhav, here’s a suggested itinerary for 7 days:

Day 1: Arrive in Odense and explore the Danish Railway Museum and other cultural landmarks.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the historic town of Faaborg and visit the Faaborg Museum of Fine Arts.

Day 3: Visit Egeskov Castle and Gardens, one of Denmark’s most significant Renaissance castles.

Day 4: Hop on a ferry to Ærø and explore the picturesque island on foot or bike.

Day 5: Take a beach day at Ristinge Klint and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Day 6: Go on a kayaking or fishing tour to see the archipelago from a different perspective.

Day 7: Relax in Svendborg, explore the town, and enjoy local food and drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Sydfynske Øhav

Sydfynske Øhav offers a rich culinary scene, with fresh seafood, artisanal products, and locally sourced ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smørrebrød: Open-faced sandwiches made with bread, butter, and various toppings such as shrimp, pickled herring, and roast beef.
  • Stegt flæsk med persillesovs: Pork belly served with parsley sauce and boiled potatoes, a classic Danish dish.
  • Æbleskiver: Small, round pancakes flavored with cardamom and served with jam and powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Svendborg Ølbryggeri: A local microbrewery that produces a range of artisanal beers, including seasonal and limited editions.
  • Ærø Gin: A small-batch gin made with local botanicals and bottled on the island of Ærø.
  • Hyldeblomstsaft: A non-alcoholic elderflower syrup that is a popular and refreshing drink in Denmark.

Where to Stay in Sydfynske Øhav: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sydfynske Øhav offers a range of accommodations options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top picks:

  • Comwell Middelfart: A modern hotel with stylish rooms, a waterfront location, and beautiful views of the Little Belt strait.
  • Broholm Castle: A historic castle turned hotel, with elegant rooms, fine dining, and access to beautiful gardens and parklands.
  • Danhostel Svendborg: A budget-friendly hostel with clean rooms, a central location, and access to a shared kitchen and lounge.

What to Buy in Sydfynske Øhav: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sydfynske Øhav offers many unique and locally made products that are perfect for souvenir shopping. Here are some recommendations:

  • Fynsk Forår: A craft beer made with locally grown hops and malt, and infused with spring flavors such as elderflower and rhubarb.
  • Danish Design: Denmark is known for its iconic and timeless design, and Sydfynske Øhav has many galleries and shops that sell furniture, ceramics, textiles, and other design objects by local artists and designers.

Local Culture and Customs of Sydfynske Øhav

Denmark has a rich cultural heritage, and Sydfynske Øhav is no exception. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Hygge: A Danish concept that translates to coziness, warmth, and conviviality, hygge is a way of life in Denmark, and you’ll find it in the many cafes, restaurants, and homes in Sydfynske Øhav.
  • Cycling: Denmark is a cycling nation, and Sydfynske Øhav is no exception. With many bike lanes and scenic routes, biking is a popular and enjoyable way to explore the area.
  • Sustainability: Denmark is one of the most sustainable countries in the world, and Sydfynske Øhav has many initiatives and projects that aim to protect the environment and promote eco-friendly tourism.

Best Time to Visit Sydfynske Øhav

Sydfynske Øhav is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique experiences. Here’s a breakdown of the best time to visit:

  • Summer (June to August): The peak tourist season, summer is ideal for beach days, kayaking, and outdoor activities, as well as festivals and events.

  • Winter (December to February): Despite the chilly weather, winter in Sydfynske Øhav has its charm, with Christmas markets, mulled wine, and cozy cafes.
