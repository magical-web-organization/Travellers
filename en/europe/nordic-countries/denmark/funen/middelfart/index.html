Introduction to Middelfart

Middelfart, a small town situated in the heart of Funen Island, is a hidden gem of Denmark waiting to be discovered. This charming town nestled between Odense and Kolding is known for its beautiful landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant food and drinks scene. Middelfart is the perfect place for those who want to take a break from the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in the Danish countryside.

Local folklore has it that the town’s name “Middelfart” comes from the Middle Ages when the town was located midway (midt-vejen or mid-way) between two important Danish towns, Odense and Kolding. Despite its small size, Middelfart has something to offer to every traveler. From picturesque coastal drives, exciting outdoor activities, scrumptious local cuisine to historic museums, Middelfart promises a unique Danish experience.

List of Places to Visit in Middelfart

If you are planning a trip to Middelfart, then here is a list of places that should not be missed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Middelfart Museum: This captivating museum is situated in a 17th Century half-timbered house and showcases the town’s cultural heritage, history, and local arts.

  • Kulturoen Middelfart: Middelfart’s iconic cultural center offering year-round art exhibitions, live music, and theater performances.

  • Rebild Bakker National Park: This scenic park located just south of Middelfart, boasts an impressive Viking burial site and offers visitors the chance to immerse themselves in the rich Danish history.

Natural Attractions of Middelfart: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fredericia Vold and Trelde Naes: If you are a fan of scenic drives, then you must take a trip from Middelfart to Fredericia Vold and Trelde Naes. This winding coastal road offers breathtaking views of the ocean and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Lillebaelt Waters: This stunning stretch of coastline offers visitors plenty of opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and swimming. Make sure to visit the famous bridge over the strait and enjoy scenic views of the area.

  • Hindsgavl Nature Reserve: This serene nature reserve is home to a wide array of bird species, wild boars, and deer. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, fishing trips, and picnics in a stunning natural setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Middelfart

Middelfart offers visitors a plethora of hiking and biking routes that traverse the stunning natural landscapes of Funen Island. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes that should not be missed:

  • Lillebaelt Panorama Trail: This scenic 38-kilometer trail runs from Middelfart to Vejle, offering breathtaking views of the ocean and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Kongernes Nordsjaelland Trail: This 65-kilometer trail takes hikers through the stunning landscapes of Northern Zealand, with stops at ancient ruins, picturesque villages, and quiet forests.

  • Odense River Trail: This gorgeous trail runs alongside the river that cuts through Odense, offering hikers an opportunity to explore the city’s rich cultural heritage while enjoying picturesque views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Middelfart: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for exciting things to do while visiting Middelfart, then here are some of the best activities and tours that should be on your bucket list:

  • Whale Watching: Take a whale watching tour from Middelfart Harbor and witness the majestic sea creatures in their natural habitat.

  • Horseback Riding: Saddle up and take a horseback tour of the stunning Funen landscapes, complete with serene forests, flower-filled meadows, and picturesque beaches.

  • Fishing Tours: Enjoy a day-long fishing tour with local fishermen and learn about traditional Danish fishing techniques while catching local seafood like cod and mackerel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Middelfart

If you are planning a week-long trip to Middelfart, then here is a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Middelfart, check-in to your hotel/hostel/guesthouse. Explore the town center, visit the Middelfart Museum, and enjoy local Danish cuisine at a nearby restaurant.

Day 2: Take a day-long Viking history tour of the historical sites of Funen Island, including the Viking burial ground at Rebild Bakker National Park.

Day 3: Rent a bicycle and ride along the scenic Lillebaelt Panorama Trail. Stop for a picnic lunch at a picturesque bay and continue riding until Vejle.

Day 4: Take a kayaking tour of the Lillebaelt waters and explore the surrounding coastline while enjoying scenic views of Middelfart.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Odense and explore the city’s rich cultural heritage, including the Hans Christian Andersen Museum and the Odense River Trail.

Day 6: Go fishing with local fishermen and learn about traditional Danish fishing techniques. Enjoy a seafood lunch.

Day 7: Enjoy a final day in Middelfart by visiting the Kulturoen and participating in cultural events. End the day with a final dinner of Danish delicacies before returning home.

Food and Drink Scene of Middelfart

Middelfart’s food and drinks scene is a vibrant blend of local Danish cuisine and international flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smorrebrod: Open-faced sandwiches topped with fresh seafood, cured meats, and vegetables. Pair it with a cold beer for the perfect Danish lunch.

  • Frikadeller: Traditional Danish meatballs made with pork and veal, served with potatoes and gravy.

  • Stegt Flæsk: Danish-style bacon that is fried to perfection and served with boiled potatoes and parsley sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Akvavit: A Danish liquor distilled from potatoes and flavored with dill. It is typically served chilled and is considered a traditional Danish drink.

  • Carlsberg Beer: One of the most famous Danish beers that is enjoyed both locally and internationally.

  • Glogg: A Danish mulled wine made with spices and red wine, typically enjoyed during the festive season.

Where to Stay in Middelfart: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Middelfart offers visitors a variety of accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Middelfart:

  • Comwell Middelfart: A luxurious hotel situated on the harbor, offering stunning ocean views, and world-class dining options.

  • Middelfart Strand Camping: A family-friendly campground offering comfortable cabins, caravans, and tents alongside plenty of outdoor activities like fishing, biking, and hiking.

  • Frederik VI’s Hotel: A charming 19th-century hotel, brimming with history and elegance, set in the scenic surroundings of Middelfart.

What to Buy in Middelfart: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Middelfart offers a plethora of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family back home. Here are some of the most popular items to buy in Middelfart:

  • Danish Ceramics: Denmark is famous for its pottery and ceramics, and Middelfart has several shops that sell traditional Danish pottery.

  • Larsen Danish Sea Salt: A culinary delicacy made from the pure sea salt harvested from the Lillebaelt waters in Middelfart.

  • Danish Chocolates, Pastries, and Cookies: Satisfy your sweet tooth with local Danish chocolates or traditional Danish pastries and cookies like the iconic Danish butter cookies.

Local Culture and Customs of Middelfart

Middelfart is a small town that prides itself on preserving its Danish heritage, warmth, and hospitality. Locals are welcoming, friendly, and enjoy sharing their culture and traditions with visitors.

Danish culture encourages a hygge (cozy) lifestyle, so don’t be surprised to find locals unwinding in cafes, enjoying outdoor activities, or just spending time with loved ones. Middelfart’s culture is deeply influenced by its maritime heritage, which is reflected in its cuisine, festivals, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Middelfart

Middelfart can be enjoyed throughout the year, with each season offering a unique experience. The peak time to visit Middelfart is from June to August when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you want to experience the winter festivities, then plan a trip in December, when the town turns into a winter wonderland with Christmas markets and mulled wine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middelfart

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Middelfart:

Do:

  • Dress comfortably and appropriately for the weather

  • Try local Danish cuisine and delicacies

  • Participate in outdoor activities and explore the scenic beauty of Funen Island

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces or pollute the environment

  • Ignore local customs and etiquette

Safety and Health

Middelfart is a very safe town and has a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take basic precautions like taking care of their belongings, and staying safe while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety in Middelfart

  • Always wear a helmet while biking or participating in other outdoor activities

  • Keep your valuable belongings in a safe place

  • Follow instructions given by the tour guides for your safety in outdoor activities

Health in Middelfart

Middelfart has a well-developed healthcare system, and visitors are required to have health insurance before entering Denmark. In case of any medical emergency, visitors can contact the nearest hospital or medical facility.

Getting to Middelfart: Transportation Options and Logistics

Middelfart is easily accessible by plane, train, and bus. Visitors have several options to get to Middelfart, including:

Airports Near Middelfart

  • Billund Airport: 68 kilometers away from Middelfart

  • Copenhagen Airport: 200 kilometers away from Middelfart

  • Hamburg Airport: 200 kilometers away from Middelfart

Bus Stations Near Middelfart

  • Middelfart Station

  • Frederica Station

Other Options

  • Take the train from Copenhagen or Odense to Middelfart

  • Drive to Middelfart and rent a car locally

How to Get Around in Middelfart

Middelfart is a small town, and the best way to explore it is on foot or by renting a bike. Visitors can also use public transport options like buses and taxis to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Middelfart: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Middelfart’s location makes it easy to explore the neighboring towns and cities of Funen Island. Some of the popular destinations that can be visited from Middelfart include:

  • Odense: Known as the birthplace of Hans Christian Andersen, Odense is a charming city that is famed for its cultural landmarks, picturesque parks, and historic sites.

  • Kolding: This vibrant city offers visitors a chance to explore its medieval castle, picturesque streets, and modern shopping centers.

  • Aarhus: Denmark’s second-largest city is known for its unique blend of historic landmarks and contemporary architecture, world-class museums, and vibrant cultural events.

Conclusion

Middelfart is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to experience the best of Danish culture, outdoor adventures, and scenic beauty. Whether you want to explore the town’s cultural heritage, indulge in traditional Danish cuisine, or immerse yourself in the stunning natural landscapes, Middelfart promises a unique Danish experience. Plan a trip to Middelfart and discover the warmth, hospitality, and charm of Funen Island.
