Introduction to Ringe

Tucked away in the heart of Funen, Ringe offers a taste of Denmark’s rich cultural heritage combined with stunning natural landscapes. Traditionally a farming community, Ringe is known for its rolling fields, lush forests and tranquil lakes. Despite its small population, the town boasts several cultural attractions, including museums and historic sites. Its location in the center of Funen makes Ringe the perfect base to explore the rest of the island.

List of Places to Visit in Ringe

There are plenty of attractions to keep you busy in Ringe. Check out our top picks for must-see landmarks and natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ringe Museum: Located in the heart of town, Ringe Museum features exhibits on the town’s history and local culture. Don’t miss the old farm buildings and well-preserved interiors that offer a glimpse into historical Danish life.

  • Faaborg Museum: Just a short drive from Ringe, Faaborg Museum features an impressive collection of Danish art, including works by renowned painter Funen Painters.

  • Egeskov Castle: One of Denmark’s best-preserved Renaissance castles, Egeskov Castle is worth a visit for its sprawling gardens and impressive collection of antique cars.

Natural Attractions of Ringe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Egeskov Forest: Located on the grounds of Egeskov Castle, this sprawling forest offers excellent hiking and biking trails with scenic views of the countryside.

  • Skallebaekken Nature Reserve: This tranquil nature reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare birds and plants.

  • Arreskov Lake: Perfect for a summertime swim or paddle, Arreskov Lake is a peaceful spot for a picnic or a leisurely boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ringe

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Ringe offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Enjoy the beauty of the Danish countryside on these beautiful routes.

  • Bike Route to Bøjden: This 20-kilometer route winds through villages and farmlands and ends at the picturesque port town of Bøjden.

  • Svanninge Bjerge Hiking Trail: This challenging 15-kilometer trail takes you through dense forests and up hills for breathtaking panoramic views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Ringe: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, Ringe offers a range of activities for all interests. Don’t miss these top picks for your itinerary.

  • Grønninghoved Beach: Perfect for summer swimming and sunbathing, Grønninghoved Beach is a hidden gem just a short drive from Ringe.

  • Frydenlund Forest: This expansive forest offers a network of hiking trails and beautiful picnic spots. If you’re lucky, you might even spot some local wildlife, such as deer or foxes.

  • Canoeing on the River Odense: Rent a canoe and spend the day paddling along the scenic River Odense.

Food and Drink Scene of Ringe

Danish cuisine is known for its hearty, comfort-food-style dishes that utilize local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to sample during your trip to Ringe.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frikadeller: A staple in Danish cuisine, these meatballs are typically served with boiled potatoes and lingonberry jam.

  • Smørrebrød: Open-faced sandwiches that often feature rye bread, smoked fish, and cured meats.

  • Rødgrød med Fløde: A classic Danish dessert made with a variety of seasonal berries and topped with cream.

Local Beverages

  • Akvavit: A traditional Scandinavian spirit made from potatoes or grain and flavored with caraway or dill.

  • Beer: Denmark is known for its flavorful brews, such as Carlsberg and Tuborg.

Where to Stay in Ringe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite its small size, Ringe offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top picks for where to stay during your visit.

  • Ringe Hostel: A budget-friendly option with dorm-style rooms and communal cooking facilities.

  • Cabin at Arreskov Lake: For a unique experience in nature, rent a cozy cabin overlooking beautiful Arreskov Lake.

  • Hotel Faaborg Fjord: Located just a short drive from Ringe, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the fjord.

What to Buy in Ringe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Ringe, check out these top picks.

  • Rye Bread: A staple in Danish cuisine, rye bread is dense and flavorful and makes a great souvenir for foodies.

  • Danish Wool: Denmark is known for its high-quality wool products, such as blankets and scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Ringe

Danish culture is laid-back and friendly, and the locals in Ringe are no exception. Here are some customs to be aware of during your visit.

  • Hygge: A concept of coziness and warm hospitality that is deeply ingrained in Danish culture.

  • Biking Culture: Denmark is one of the world’s most bike-friendly countries, and cyclists are respected and prioritized on the roads.

Best Time to Visit Ringe

The best time to visit Ringe is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny. Keep in mind that this is also peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ringe

To make the most of your trip and avoid any cultural faux pas, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit.

Do:

  • Hygge: Embrace the Danish concept of “hygge” and enjoy the cozy atmosphere of Ringe’s cafes and restaurants.

  • Rent a Bike: Take advantage of the bike-friendly culture and explore the beautiful countryside on two wheels.

Don’t:

  • Be Punctual: Danish culture is relaxed and punctuality is not always a top priority, so don’t stress if plans are running a little late.

  • Be Loud: Danish culture places a high value on peacefulness and quiet, so be mindful of your noise level in public places.

Safety and Health

Denmark is a safe and healthy country to visit, with a high standard of living and excellent healthcare facilities. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your trip.

Safety in Ringe

  • Traffic Safety: Be aware of traffic, especially when walking or biking in urban areas.

  • Emergency Services: Denmark has a well-established emergency services system, including police and ambulance services.

Health in Ringe

  • Vaccinations: No special vaccinations are required for travel to Denmark.

  • Medical Care: Denmark has an excellent healthcare system, with hospitals and clinics located in most major cities.

Getting to Ringe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ringe is located in the heart of Funen and is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around Ringe.

Airports Near Ringe

  • Odense Airport: Located just 20 minutes from Ringe, Odense Airport offers domestic flights within Denmark.

  • Copenhagen Airport: The largest airport in Denmark, Copenhagen Airport is located about 2 hours from Ringe and offers international flights from around the world.

Bus Stations Near Ringe

  • Ringe Bus Station: Located in the center of town, Ringe Bus Station offers connections to cities throughout Funen.

  • Odense Bus Station: The largest bus station in the region, Odense Bus Station offers connections to destinations throughout Denmark.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Ringe is in Odense, which offers connections to major cities throughout Denmark and Europe.

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the countryside and offers more flexibility in your travel plans.

How to Get Around in Ringe

Ringe is a small town that is easily navigable by foot or bike. If you’re traveling further afield, consider renting a car or taking public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Ringe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ringe is located in the heart of Funen and offers easy access to several nearby attractions. Here are some top picks for where to go next from Ringe.

  • Odense: The largest city on Funen, Odense is known for its charming old town and museums, including the Hans Christian Andersen Museum.

  • Fyns Hoved: Located at the northern tip of Funen, Fyns Hoved offers stunning coastal views and hiking trails.

  • Ærø Island: Just a short ferry ride from Funen, Ærø Island is known for its quaint towns and picturesque landscapes.

Conclusion

Ringe may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of cultural attractions and natural wonders that make it a must-see destination in Denmark. From the tranquility of Arreskov Lake to the sprawling grounds of Egeskov Castle, there’s something for everyone in Ringe. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your itinerary and enjoy an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in the heart of Funen.
