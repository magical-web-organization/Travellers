Introduction to Herning

Herning is a hidden gem in the Jutland region of Denmark. This small but dynamic city is known for its art, culture, sports, and outdoor adventure. From exploring art galleries and museums to hiking in the beautiful landscape, there is something for everyone here. This travel guide to Herning will provide you with a comprehensive overview of all the things to see and do in the city, where to eat and drink, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Herning

When it comes to places to visit in Herning, there is no shortage of options. Here are some of the top spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Herning Museum of Contemporary Art - This modern art museum is a must-visit for art enthusiasts. It has a vast collection of contemporary art from Denmark and around the world.
  2. Carl-Henning Pedersen & Else Alfelt’s Museum - This museum is a tribute to two influential Danish artists, Carl-Henning Pedersen and Else Alfelt. It has a vast collection of their art pieces.
  3. Elia - Herning’s new art museum dedicated to photography and films.

Natural Attractions of Herning: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Klosterhedens Vildtreservat - This nature reserve spans over 6,000 hectares of woodlands, heaths, and meadows, making it perfect for hiking and spotting wildlife.
  2. Skovsnogen Artspace - This unique park features various art installations and sculptures set in nature, providing a fantastic opportunity to explore and enjoy the surroundings.
  3. Herning Hestereservat - Love horses? Explore the domesticated horse sanctuary near Herning and learn more about this amazing animal.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Herning

Herning is an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts because of its many hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular routes to check out include:

  1. The Skjern River - The Skjern River is the longest river in Jutland and is ideal for canoeing, kayaking, and fishing.
  2. Vestjysk Ornitologisk Forening - This birdwatching trail spans over 30 km of idyllic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Herning: Activities and Tours

  1. Jump A Lot Herning - For a fun day out with family or friends, head to Jump A Lot and have a blast at their indoor trampoline park.
  2. Herning Equestrian Centre - Enjoy a horseback riding session at the Herning Equestrian Centre, which offers safe and enjoyable rides for all ages and levels.
  3. Glud Museum - Discover the cultural heritage and history of Herning by visiting the Glud Museum, showcasing the lifestyle and traditions of the past through artifacts and exhibits.

Food and Drink Scene of Herning

Herning has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many delicious local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Stegt flæsk med persillesovs og kartofler - Fried pork belly with parsley sauce and potatoes.
  2. Frikadeller - Danish meatballs served with potatoes, gravy, and pickles.

Local Beverages

  1. Herning Brewery - Try local beer flavors made with local ingredients.
  2. Gudenaaens Bryghus - Enjoy craft beers made with natural spring water and malts at this local brewery in Silkeborg.

Where to Stay in Herning: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Herning has many options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few that we recommend:

  1. Best Western Plus Hotel Eyde - A centrally located hotel offering comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  2. Sov i Herning - A cozy hostel located in the heart of Herning with great facilities, including a garden, free Wi-Fi and a shared kitchen.
  3. Lille Amerika Guesthouse - A lovely bed and breakfast located near the town center.

What to Buy in Herning: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs or some local products to bring back home? Here are some recommendations:

  1. Herning’s Danish rug - Hernandez Danish rug is a timeless classic. These woven rugs have been produced in Herning since the beginning of the 20th century.
  2. Jewelry From Vissing Design - pieces handcrafted from leather, stones and various other natural materials for an exclusive finish.

Local Culture and Customs of Herning

Herning has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions and customs to explore. Here are some important elements of the Herning culture:

  1. Equestrian sport - Herning is a famous place for equestrian sports; you can see a number of equestrian sports event every year.
  2. Danish design - Herning is the brainchild of the Danish Design Centre, which honors well-executed design of all kinds, from art and interior to furniture and textiles.

Best Time to Visit Herning

Herning can be visited year-round, but the summer months typically bring the best weather for exploring the city’s many outdoor attractions. However, if you’re a winter sports fan, the town’s proximity to Gjern Bakker allows for some great skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Herning

Here are some helpful do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Herning:

Do:

  1. Embrace the local culture and customs; be polite and respectful towards locals.
  2. Try new foods and drinks; the Herning region has a rich culinary scene and should be explored to the fullest.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to pack the essentials such as raincoats or umbrellas as the weather can be unpredictable.
  2. Don’t swim in unauthorized areas or leave trash behind in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to ensure you have a safe and healthy experience in Herning:

Safety in Herning

  1. While Herning is generally a safe place to visit, it’s always wise to take precautions like keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding isolated areas.
  2. Be sure to have the emergency number of Herning - 112 ready in case of an emergency.

Health in Herning

  1. In general, the health system in Denmark is top-notch, and you can expect timely and professional medical care if needed.
  2. Avoid drinking tap water unless it’s been boiled or filtered.

Getting to Herning: Transportation Options and Logistics

Herning is easily accessible by both public transport and car.

Airports Near Herning

  1. Billund Airport - 55 km from Herning.
  2. Aarhus Airport - 82 km from Herning.

Bus Stations Near Herning

  1. Herning Bus Station - located in the heart of the city and connecting Herning to neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

  1. Car rentals - available at both airports.

How to Get Around in Herning

Getting around Herning is easy, thanks to its efficient public transport network, including buses, trains, and taxis. Biking is also a great option, as the city has many bike paths and is designed with cyclists in mind.

Where to Go Next from Herning: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding area of Herning, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out:

  1. Silkeborg - A charming town with many cultural and historical attractions, located about 36 km northeast of Herning.
  2. LEGOLAND - This world-famous park is located in Billund, just a short drive from Herning.

Conclusion

Herning is a delightful place to visit, whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in the local culture, explore the great outdoors, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery. We hope this Herning travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip. Happy travels!
