Introduction to Sæby

Sæby is a charming town located in North Jutland, Denmark. It is known for its historic buildings, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant culture. The town sits by the coast of the Kattegat sea and is surrounded by mesmerizing beaches, promenades,and charming countryside.

Sæby is perfect for those who want to take a break from busy city life and relax in the calmness of nature. It is also a great destination for those interested in exploring Denmark’s cultural heritage, as the town houses many museums and historical sites

In this travel guide, we will provide you with a complete itinerary to explore Sæby, including recommendations for places to visit, eat, stay, and things to do.

List of Places to Visit in Sæby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sæby Museum

Sæby Museum showcases the town’s cultural heritage and traditions. The museum is housed in an 18th century, state-of-the-art building and features exhibits ranging from local art, maritime history, and folklore.

Sæby Kirke

Sæby Kirke is a beautiful church that dates back to the 12th century. It features Romanesque-style architecture and intricate decorations such as frescoes and wooden carvings.

Herregården Museum

Herregården Museum is a charming museum that showcases Denmark’s rural lifestyle from the 19th and 20th centuries. It includes exhibits of interior design, textiles, furniture, and working tools.

Natural Attractions of Sæby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sæby Nordstrand

Sæby Nordstrand is a beautiful sandy beach located in the north of the town, with great views of the North Sea.

Dyrehaven

Dyrehaven is a historic attraction known for its rich and diverse wildlife, including fallow deer, red deer, and roe deer. Visitors can take a scenic walk or a horse-drawn carriage ride to explore the park.

Rødhætte Naturområde

Rødhætte Naturområde is a nature reserve with lush greenery and rich birdlife. It is perfect for hiking and bird-watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sæby

Sæby-Gl. Skagen Hiking Route

This popular hiking trail takes you from the center of Sæby to Gl. Skagen. You will see stunning views of the North Sea, Skagen Odde Nature Center, and charming villages.

Mountain Biking Route in Råbjerg Mile

If you’re up for a challenge, check out the mountain biking route in Råbjerg Mile. The trail takes you through massive sand dunes and provides an adrenaline-pumping experience.

Best Things to Do in Sæby: Activities and Tours

Sæby Svoemmehal

Sæby Svoemmehal is a modern swimming facility that includes a 25-meter pool, wellness center, sauna, and steam room.

Sæby Golf Club

Sæby Golf Club is a stunning golf course surrounded by beautiful scenery. It is perfect for first-time golfers, families, and experienced players.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sæby

Day 1: Visit Sæby Museum and explore the town center. Day 2: Hike the Sæby-Gl. Skagen hiking route. Day 3: Take a trip to Råbjerg Mile to hike, picnic or mountain bike. Day 4: Visit Herregården Museum. Day 5: Check out the beaches and wildlife at Dyrehaven. Day 6: Play golf at Sæby Golf Club. Day 7: Relax and unwind at Sæby Svoemmehal.

Food and Drink Scene of Sæby

Must-Try Dishes

Sæby Fiskehus

Sæby Fiskehus is a local fish shop that serves delicious and fresh seafood using traditional recipes.

Vraa Hede Bakery

Vraa Hede Bakery is a bakery that specializes in Danish pastries. They have been producing varieties of fresh pastries using quality ingredients for over 100 years.

Local Beverages

Sæby Bryghus

Sæby Bryghus is a local brewery that produces craft beers. They take great pride in using natural ingredients and traditional brewing techniques, resulting in some of the best beers in the region.

Where to Stay in Sæby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Viking Aqua Spa & Wellness Resort

Hotel Viking Aqua Spa & Wellness Resort is a luxurious hotel with an on-site spa, pool, and wellness center. It is perfect for those looking to unwind and relax.

Danhostel Sæby

Danhostel Sæby is a budget-friendly option for travelers. It has a fully equipped kitchen, cozy rooms, and a terrace with beautiful views of the harbor.

Det Gamle Apotek Guesthouse

Det Gamle Apotek Guesthouse is a charming guesthouse located in a historic building. It features spacious rooms and a beautiful garden to enjoy the tranquility of the surroundings.

What to Buy in Sæby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sæby Glasværk

Sæby Glasværk is a local glassworks shop that produces unique and beautiful glassware, including glasses, vases, and bowls.

Sæby Kirke Stolefabrik

Sæby Kirke Stolefabrik is a furniture shop that specializes in designing and producing quality and stylish chairs. They have been creating handcrafted chairs since 1896 and exported their furniture all over the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Sæby

Sæby is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. As such, it is always a good idea to respect local customs like shaking hands or greeting people politely, especially when visiting historical sites and museums.

Best Time to Visit Sæby

The best time to visit Sæby is between May and September when the weather is mild and a variety of exciting events take place. If you can handle cold temperatures, visiting in the winter months will offer great opportunities for winter sports like skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sæby

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife.
  • Take in local cultural and historic sites.
  • Try Danish cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute nature.
  • Attempt to touch or feed wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sæby

Sæby is known for its safe and low-crime environment. However, it is always a good idea to remain vigilant and secure your belongings to avoid theft. In case of an emergency, call 112.

Health in Sæby

Denmark has an advanced health care system, and Sæby has several medical clinics and hospitals. For non-emergency issues, contact the nearest clinic. In case of a medical emergency, call 112 immediately.

Getting to Sæby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sæby

The nearest airport to Sæby is in Aalborg (57 kilometers), which is served by international airlines such as SAS, Ryanair and Norwegian.

Bus Stations Near Sæby

Sæby bus station is located in the city center and is served by regional buses that connect Sæby to other cities and towns in Denmark.

Other Options

Sæby can also be reached by train or ferry from other Danish cities and neighboring countries like Sweden or Norway.

How to Get Around in Sæby

Sæby is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, you can also hire a bike to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Sæby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Skagen

Skagen is a charming old town located in the northern part of Jutland, known for its distinctive yellow houses and stunning scenery.

Grenen

Grenen is a long and narrow stretch of sand, which marks the northern tip of Denmark. It has stunning views of the North Sea on one side and the Kattegat sea on the other.

Conclusion

Sæby is a hidden gem in Denmark, where travelers can enjoy the best of both worlds: culture and nature. From historic sites, scenic views, delicious food, and outdoor activities, Sæby has something for everyone. Plan your visit to Sæby today to experience the charm and beauty of the town.
