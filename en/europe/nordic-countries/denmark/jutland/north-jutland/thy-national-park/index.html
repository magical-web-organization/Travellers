Introduction to Thy National Park

Located in North Jutland, Denmark, Thy National Park is a breathtaking destination known for its rugged beauty, pristine beaches, and diverse flora and fauna. Spanning over 240 square kilometers, this park is a haven for nature lovers who want to explore the great outdoors. The park is a popular destination for hikers and bikers, as well as for bird watchers, surfers, and other outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Thy National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thy National Park is not just about natural beauty; visitors can also delve into its rich cultural heritage. Some must-visit cultural attractions in the park include:

  • Thy National Museum: Located in Thisted, this museum showcases the cultural and natural history of Thy.
  • St. Thy’s Church: This 13th-century church is a beautiful example of Gothic architecture with a brick-covered façade.
  • Snedsted Museum: This local history museum showcases the life and traditions of the residents of Snedsted.

Natural Attractions of Thy National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Touted as one of Denmark’s wildest places, Thy National Park offers some of the most beautiful natural attractions in Jutland. Here are some of the natural attractions worth visiting:

  • Bulbjerg: This distinctive cliff offers a stunning view of the Danish North Sea. From the top, visitors can see everything from Jutland’s flat terrain to the rugged open coast.
  • Krik Vig: This serene lagoon with its white sandy beaches is a favorite among bird watchers. Visitors can spot a wide range of bird species, including dunlin, wading birds, and geese.
  • Vesterhavet: Denmark’s west coast is well-known for its stunning beach scenery. Vesterhavet is no exception and boasts miles of white sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thy National Park

One of the best ways to explore Thy National Park is by hiking or biking along the many trails that wind through the park. Here are some popular routes:

  • Hiking: The National Park offers several hiking trails ranging from short to long ones. The Red Route (12.1 km) is the most popular, and visitors can experience the park’s forests, wetlands, lakes, and heather-covered hills.
  • Biking: The park has 130 kilometers of bike routes, which take visitors through some stunning scenery. The North Sea Route is especially popular and offers cyclists the chance to view some stunning beaches and sea views.

Best Things to Do in Thy National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Thy National Park offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours, including:

  • Bird Watching: With over 300 bird species, Thy National Park is a haven for bird enthusiasts. Visitors can spot everything from marsh harriers and short-eared owls to common sandpipers.
  • Surfing: Several beaches in the park offer some of the best surfing spots in the country. The small town of Klitmoller near Vorupor Beach is well known as a surfer’s paradise.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore Thy National Park’s many lakes and waterways. Thy Outdoor Center offers guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thy National Park

Visitors to Thy National Park can easily spend a week exploring the park. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival

  • Check into a hotel, hostel, or guesthouse near Thisted.
  • Explore the town of Thisted and visit the Thy National Museum.

Day 2: Hiking

  • Hike the Red Route in Thy National Park.
  • Visit St. Thy’s Church.

Day 3: Beach Day

  • Spend the day at the beach in Vorupor.
  • Attend a surfing lesson or rent a paddleboard or kayak.

Day 4: Bird Watching

  • Visit Krik Vig and go bird watching.
  • Visit Snedsted Museum.

Day 5: Biking

  • Bike the North Sea Route.
  • Visit the Lodbjerg Lighthouse for views of the coastline.

Day 6: Outdoor Adventures

  • Rent a kayak and paddle through the park’s waterways.
  • Visit Bulbjerg, a gorgeous cliff with stunning views.

Day 7: Departure

  • Say goodbye to Thy National Park and head home or to the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Thy National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors to Thy National Park can indulge in some delicious local fare. Some popular dishes include:

  • Sol over Gudhjem: A traditional dish made with smoked herring, mustard, and onions served on a Danish rye bread.
  • Fiskefrikadeller: Fishcakes made with herring, salmon, or cod.
  • Krabbesalat: A salad made with crabmeat, mayonnaise, and dill.
  • Æbleflæsk: A dish made with pork belly and apples.

Local Beverages

Thy National Park is known for its unique local beverages, including:

  • Thisted Bryghus Beer: A local brewery that offers several craft beers, including the popular Thy White Ale.
  • Sømod’s Cider: A local cider company, famous for its organic cider made from hand-picked apples.
  • Møn Is: An ice cream parlor that uses local ingredients in its ice cream and is renowned for its Thybo Cream ice cream.

Where to Stay in Thy National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thy National Park offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the popular options include:

  • Thisted Camping and Cottages: Offers cottages, cabins, and tent sites. Located close to Thy National Park.
  • Thisted Hotel: A charming hotel located in the center of Thisted.
  • Klitmoller Surfhouse: A hostel located in Klitmoller’s surf town, offering affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Thy National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Thy National Park can take home some unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Hand-blown glass: The town of Thyholm has a glassblowing studio that creates unique glassware.
  • Woolen goods: The park has several shops selling woolen items made from locally sourced wool.
  • Local delicacies: Visitors can buy local specialties such as smoked fish, cheese, and locally brewed beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Thy National Park

As with any destination, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs. One of the unique customs in Thy National Park is “Heathland Dance,” a ritual dance that takes place every year on St. John’s Eve. Visitors also must adhere to the park’s rules and regulations to conserve the park’s fragile ecosystem.

Best Time to Visit Thy National Park

The best time to visit Thy National Park is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy the park’s many outdoor activities and attractions. However, if visitors prefer a quieter park, they can visit in the offseason (September- May).

Do’s and Don’ts of Thy National Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment and nature.
  • Adhere to the park’s rules and regulations.
  • Take away trash and litter.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Pick or damage plants and flowers.
  • Leave litter.
  • Light fires outside designated areas.
  • Disturb the wildlife in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thy National Park

Visitors to Thy National Park should exercise caution when hiking or biking along the trails. It’s essential to wear proper footwear and stay on designated trails. Visitors should also be aware of the weather conditions and adhere to park rules and regulations to prevent accidents.

Health in Thy National Park

Thy National Park has several medical facilities in nearby towns such as Thisted. Visitors should bring their medication and consult a doctor before embarking on any strenuous activities, notably hiking and biking.

Getting to Thy National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thy National Park

The closest airport to Thy National Park is Aalborg Airport, which is approximately 80 kilometers away. Visitors can also fly into Billund Airport, which is 170 kilometers away.

Bus and Train Stations Near Thy National Park

Visitors can take a train or bus to Thisted, which is the nearest town to Thy National Park. There are daily train and bus services from Copenhagen and Aarhus.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Thy National Park using Denmark’s excellent road network. From Copenhagen, visitors can take the E45 highway to Herning and then take the state highway 11 to Thisted.

How to Get Around in Thy National Park

The best way to explore Thy National Park is by hiking or biking. Visitors can also rent a car or use public transportation to get to the park’s various attractions.

Where to Go Next from Thy National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Thy National Park can extend their trip and travel to nearby destinations such as:

  • Aalborg: An ancient city with a vibrant cultural scene, museums, and historic sites.
  • Læsø: An island in the Kattegat known for its stunning white beaches and unique culture.
  • Skagen: A picturesque town located at the northernmost point of Denmark, famous for its artists and stunning sand dunes.

Conclusion

Thy National Park is a hidden gem in Denmark, offering visitors a wealth of cultural and natural attractions. From hiking and biking to surfing and bird watching, there’s an activity for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their visit to this stunning national park fully.
