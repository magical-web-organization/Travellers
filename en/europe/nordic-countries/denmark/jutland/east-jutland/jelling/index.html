Food and Drink Scene of Jelling

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Smørrebrød - a traditional Danish food made of buttered rye bread with different toppings such as fish, meat, and vegetables.

  2. Frikadeller - a type of Danish meatball, served with boiled potatoes and gravy.

  3. Stegtflæsk - fried pork belly, served with boiled potatoes, parsley sauce, and beetroots.

Local Beverages

  1. Øl - beer is a must-try local drink. There are many different varieties, from light beers to dark, malty ones.

  2. Akvavit - a Scandinavian brandy, distilled from grains or potatoes. It has a strong herbal flavor, and locals often serve it for special occasions.

  3. Gløgg - a warm, spiced wine, often served during Christmas time. It is made with red wine, cloves, cinnamon, and other spices.

Where to Stay in Jelling: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Fårup Sø Camping - This family-friendly campsite is situated in the Jelling Forest, offering a peaceful and relaxing stay in nature.

  2. Vejle Center Hotel - A four-star hotel located in Vejle town, offering contemporary and luxurious accommodation.

  3. Danhostel Vejle - A hostel located in Vejle, offering simple and affordable accommodation for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Jelling: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Jelling Runes Souvenirs - These souvenirs are generally made from materials like locally sourced wood and stones and bear the inscription of the Jelling Runes.

  2. Danish Handcrafts - These are generally high-quality items that make great gifts - such as blown glass, pottery, woven textiles, leatherware, and hand-carved objects.

  3. Danish chocolates and candies - Denmark has a strong tradition of chocolate-making with several famous producers based around the country, including Anthon Berg, Summer Birds, and Konnerup & Co.

Local Culture and Customs of Jelling

Denmark is known for its tradition of ‘Hygge’- which is a sense of coziness, warmth, contentment, hospitality, and calmness. Danish people are known for their friendliness and welcoming nature. Visitors are expected to be punctual, respectful, and courteous. While dining, it is customary to wait until the host says ‘Velbekomme’ (Bon Appetite) before starting your meal.

Best Time to Visit Jelling

The best time to visit Jelling is during late spring or early summer, from May to June when the weather is mild and comfortable to explore the outdoors. September to October, during the autumn festival, is another great time to visit to enjoy the fall colors and traditional Danish Autumn events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jelling

Do:

  1. Be respectful of Danish culture and customs.

  2. Take time to explore the outdoors and enjoy nature.

  3. Learn a few Danish words and phrases before visiting.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or disrespect the country’s natural environment.

  2. Don’t be overly loud in public areas.

  3. Don’t be insulting or disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jelling

Denmark is considered a safe country for visitors, and crime rates are generally low. Visitors should use discretion and ensure that their belongings are secure.

Health in Jelling

Denmark has a robust healthcare system. Visitors can apply for the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) before traveling to access free or reduced-cost medical care during their stay.

Getting to Jelling: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jelling

The nearest airport to Jelling is the Billund Airport (BLL), located approximately 20 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Jelling

Jelling is well-connected to other parts of Denmark and Europe through national and international bus services. The Jelling bus station is within walking distance of all attractions.

Other Options

Train travel is also an option in Denmark, with Jelling station opening in December 2021.

How to Get Around in Jelling

Jelling is a small village, so you can easily explore it on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent bicycles from local rental shops to explore the area easily.

Where to Go Next from Jelling: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. The city of Vejle – a bustling city with a rich history, it is the perfect destination to explore Danish architecture and shopping districts.

  2. Legoland - A famous theme park, especially for kids, and known for its Lego-themed attractions and activities.

Conclusion

Jelling is an ideal location for exploring Danish culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for natural excursions, cultural experiences, or gastronomic adventures, Jelling has it all. With a strong sense of hospitality, security, and tranquility, it is no wonder that this area is a favorite among tourists who are seeking an authentic Danish adventure.
