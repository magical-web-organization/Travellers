Introduction to Anholt

Anholt is a remote island located in the northern part of the Kattegat Sea, in East Jutland, Denmark. With an area of just 22.5 square kilometers and a population of fewer than 200 people, Anholt is a hidden gem in Denmark that is often overlooked by tourists. However, those who venture to this tiny island will discover a unique landscape, rich history, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Anholt

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anholt has a long and fascinating history that is best explored through its cultural attractions. One of the must-visit destinations on the island is the Anholt Lighthouse, which dates back to 1561 and is one of the oldest lighthouses in Denmark. Another historical attraction is the Anholt Museum, which showcases the island’s history through exhibits on archaeology, geology, and the local flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Anholt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Anholt’s natural landscape is equally impressive and offers numerous opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. The island is home to miles of sandy beaches, including the popular Nordstrand and Sydstrand beaches. Another natural attraction worth exploring is the towering sand dunes that dominate the island’s interior. Visitors can hike through these dunes and soak in the stunning views from the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anholt

Anholt is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The island has several well-marked trails that offer diverse terrain and stunning views of the surrounding sea and landscapes. The most popular hiking trail is the Anholt Rundt, a 12-kilometer loop that takes visitors through forests, dunes, and beaches. Cyclists can also explore the island’s scenic routes by renting a bike from one of the local bike rental shops.

Best Things to Do in Anholt: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in Anholt. Visitors can explore the island’s charming villages and take in the traditional Danish architecture. There are also several water-based activities, such as fishing, kayaking, and snorkeling, that allow visitors to explore the rich marine life of the surrounding waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anholt

To make the most of your trip to Anholt, we recommend a week-long itinerary that includes the island’s best attractions, hiking and biking trails, and local experiences.

Day Itinerary
1 Arrival and check-in at a local guesthouse or hotel.
2 Hike the Anholt Rundt trail and explore the island’s dunes and beaches.
3 Visit the Anholt Lighthouse and Museum, followed by a walk through the charming village of Anholt.
4 Take a guided kayaking tour along the island’s coastline, or go fishing with a local guide.
5 Rent a bike and explore the island’s scenic routes, stopping at the local cafes and restaurants for traditional Danish cuisine.
6 Visit the Nordstrand and Sydstrand beaches before taking a sunset hike through the dunes.
7 Departure day. Relax in one of the island’s cozy cafes before catching the local ferry back to Denmark’s mainland.

Food and Drink Scene of Anholt

Must-Try Dishes

Anholt’s cuisine is a fusion of traditional Danish dishes and locally sourced seafood. One must-try dish is the Smoked Herring, a dish made from the island’s fresh-caught herrings. Another local specialty is the Frikadeller, Danish meatballs made from locally raised pork and beef.

Local Beverages

The island’s microbreweries offer a selection of craft beer, cider, and mead that showcase the local flavors and traditions of Anholt. Visitors can take a brewery tour and sample some of the island’s best drinks.

Where to Stay in Anholt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anholt has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include the Anholt Stue and Hotel Garni, both centrally located within walking distance of the island’s main attractions. For those looking for a more rustic option, the island also has several camping sites and cabins for rent.

What to Buy in Anholt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anholt’s local shops offer a variety of unique and handmade products, including ceramics, glassware, and jewelry. For a taste of the island’s flavors, visitors can also buy locally produced honey, jam, and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Anholt

Despite its small size, Anholt has a rich and unique culture that is deeply connected to its rugged landscape and seafaring traditions. The locals are proud of their heritage and welcoming to visitors, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the island’s history and customs.

Best Time to Visit Anholt

The best time to visit Anholt is during the summer months, from May to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and the island’s outdoor attractions are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anholt

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment by not littering or damaging the landscape.
  • Learn about the island’s history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks to experience the island’s unique flavors.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions of Anholt, such as the seafaring heritage and rural lifestyle.
  • Disturb the local wildlife, such as the seals and seabirds that inhabit the island’s beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anholt

Anholt is a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should still exercise common sense and caution, especially when hiking or exploring the island’s natural landscapes. Pay attention to signs and warnings, and keep a safe distance from any wildlife.

Health in Anholt

There are no major health concerns or diseases on Anholt, and visitors do not need to take any vaccinations or special precautions before traveling to the island. However, it is always a good idea to bring basic first-aid supplies and any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Anholt: Transportation Options and Logistics

To reach Anholt, visitors must take a ferry from Grenaa, which is located in the Jutland peninsula. The ferry operates daily during the summer months, with reduced schedules during the rest of the year. Visitors can also take a bus from Aarhus or Aalborg to Grenaa and then transfer to the ferry.

Airports Near Anholt

The closest airport to Anholt is Aalborg Airport, which is about 88 kilometers from Grenaa.

Bus Stations Near Anholt

The closest bus stations to Anholt are Aarhus and Aalborg, both of which have regular bus services to Grenaa.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Anholt by private boat or helicopter, although these options are much more expensive and less practical than the ferry and bus options.

How to Get Around in Anholt

Anholt is a small island that is best explored on foot or bike. Visitors can rent bikes from several local rental shops or bring their own bikes on the ferry. There are also local buses that operate on the island and connect the main villages and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Anholt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Anholt, visitors can continue their trip to other destinations in Denmark, such as Aarhus, Denmark’s second-largest city, or Skagen, a popular seaside town known for its picturesque beaches and colorful houses.

Conclusion

Anholt may be a small and remote island, but it has a rich culture, stunning scenery, and plenty of activities and experiences for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re hiking through the dunes, sampling local cuisine, or learning about the island’s history, Anholt is an unforgettable destination that should not be missed.
