Introduction to Billund

Nestled in the heart of East Jutland, Billund is a charming town in Denmark that is known for more than just the world-renowned Legoland amusement park. Billund is a hidden gem that offers visitors a blend of rich culture, natural beauty, and exciting activities.

While it may be small, Billund is a town that packs a punch. Visitors to this lovely town can enjoy a range of outdoor adventures, explore museums filled with fascinating exhibits, and sample the delicious local cuisine.

Whether you’re a solo traveler, a family with children, or a group of friends, there’s something for everyone in Billund. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through some of the must-visit places, things to do, best time to visit and much more to make your trip to Billund unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Billund

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Gyttegård Golf Park

Gyttegård Golf Park is a beautiful 9-hole golf course located in Billund. The park spans over an area of 60 acres and offers visitors breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and the impressive Gyttegård Manor. The park also has a restaurant, a putting green, and a driving range.

2. Billund Kirke

Billund Kirke is a stunning church that was built in 1977. The church’s unique design blends modernity with tradition, making it a popular spot for visitors. The church is open to the public and offers guided tours.

3. Grindsted Museum

Grindsted Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum is home to a number of exhibits that explore the town’s fascinating past, from the Viking Age to modern times. The museum is open all year round, and entry is free.

Natural Attractions of Billund: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Givskud Zoo

Givskud Zoo is one of Denmark’s most popular tourist attractions, and for good reason. The zoo offers visitors the chance to see an impressive array of wildlife, including lions, tigers, giraffes, and more. Visitors can also participate in guided tours and animal feedings.

2. Skulpturpark Billund

Skulpturpark Billund is a beautiful outdoor sculpture park located in the heart of Billund. The park features various sculptures from local and international artists and is open all year round. A walk through the park is the perfect way to immerse yourself in the town’s natural beauty.

3. Gyttegård Plantage

Gyttegård Plantage is a beautiful nature reserve located just outside Billund. The reserve spans over an area of 1,000 acres and is home to a number of hiking and cycling trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Billund

Billund is a town that is best explored on foot or by bike. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or just looking for a leisurely stroll, there’s something for everyone in Billund.

1. Gyttegård Plantage Hike

The Gyttegård Plantage Hike is a 4.5 km hike that takes visitors through the beautiful Gyttegård Plantage nature reserve. The hike takes around two hours to complete and is suitable for beginners.

2. Legoland Billund Bike Tour

The Legoland Billund Bike Tour is a fun way to explore the town’s most popular attraction and its surroundings. The tour takes visitors on a 10 km ride through the town and includes stops at Legoland, the Billund Sculpture Park, and more.

Best Things to Do in Billund: Activities and Tours

1. Legoland Billund

A visit to Billund wouldn’t be complete without spending a day at Legoland. Legoland Billund offers visitors a chance to explore the amazing world of LEGO bricks. The park features over 50 rides and attractions, 12 restaurants, and various shops.

2. Vikingeskibsmuseet

Vikingeskibsmuseet is a fascinating museum that explores the history of Viking shipbuilding. The museum’s exhibits include original Viking ships and artifacts, and visitors can even try their hand at traditional Viking crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Billund

Day 1: Billund Kirke and Skulpturpark Billund

Start your trip with a visit to Billund Kirke, followed by a walk through the town’s beautiful Skulpturpark.

Day 2: Gyttegård Golf Park and Givskud Zoo

Spend the day exploring the greenery of the Gyttegård Golf Park before heading to Givskud Zoo to see some of Denmark’s most exciting wildlife.

Day 3: Legoland Billund

Spend the day exploring Legoland’s fun-packed world of LEGO bricks.

Day 4: Grindsted Museum and Gyttegård Plantage

Spend the morning learning about Billund’s fascinating past at the Grindsted Museum before venturing into the beautiful Gyttegård Plantage for an afternoon hike.

Day 5: Vikingeskibsmuseet

Spend the day at Vikingeskibsmuseet learning about the history of Viking shipbuilding. Try your hand at traditional Viking crafts and make your Viking ship.

Day 6: Billund Biking Tour

Explore Billund on a bike tour that takes you through the town’s most popular attractions and scenic views.

Day 7: Relax and Enjoy Billund

Spend your last day in Billund relaxing and taking in the town’s quaint charm before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Billund

Must-Try Dishes

1. Frikadeller

Frikadeller are Danish meatballs and a staple of the country’s cuisine. These delicious meatballs are made from beef and pork and are often served with potatoes, gravy, and lingonberry sauce.

2. Smørrebrød

Smørrebrød is a traditional Danish open-faced sandwich. It consists of a slice of rye bread topped with various meats, fish, vegetables, and sauces.

Local Beverages

1. Carlsberg Beer

Carlsberg is a popular Danish beer brand that is famous in many countries worldwide. It’s a refreshing and light beer perfect for pairing with the local cuisine.

2. Akvavit

Akvavit is a traditional Danish spirit made with herbs and spices, including star anise, fennel, and caraway. It’s usually served as an aperitif or digestif and is a must-try for anyone looking to explore Danish culture.

Where to Stay in Billund: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Zleep Hotel Billund

Zleep Hotel Billund is a modern and affordable hotel located just five minutes from Legoland. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a delicious breakfast buffet.

2. Legoland Holiday Village

Legoland Holiday Village is a great option for families. The village offers different themed rooms, from pirate cabins to wilderness tents. The village is conveniently located near Legoland and has various activities for kids.

What to Buy in Billund: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. LEGO Sets

Of course, the first thing that comes to everyone’s mind when thinking of Billund is the famous LEGO brand. Take a trip to the flagship LEGO store and pick out a few souvenirs or gifts as a reminder of your trip to Billund.

2. Danish Candy

Danish candy is known for its unique flavors and is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth. Pick up some of the local favorites, like salty liquorice or Skippers Pipes, as a sweet souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Billund

As with most places in Denmark, Billund is a town with a unique culture steeped in tradition. Visitors to Billund should take some time to immerse themselves in the town’s customs and traditions, from trying the cuisine to enjoying the local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Billund

The best time to visit Billund is between May and September when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities and the town is at its liveliest. Visitors should note that June and July are the busiest months, with the most crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Billund

Do:

  • Try Danish cuisine, like smørrebrød and frikadeller.
  • Explore the town on foot or by bike.
  • Visit Legoland and the other popular attractions.
  • Immerse yourself in the town’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Expect to see the Northern Lights, as Billund is too far south.
  • Underestimate the sun’s strength, even on cloudy days.

Safety and Health

Safety in Billund

Billund is a safe town with little crime. Visitors should exercise the same level of caution as they would in any other small town.

Health in Billund

Healthcare in Denmark is of a high standard. Visitors should purchase travel insurance that covers medical emergencies before visiting Billund.

Getting to Billund: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Billund

Billund Airport (BLL) is the nearest airport to Billund and is located just 3 km from the town center. The airport offers regular flights to and from various European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Billund

Billund Bus Terminal is located in the center of Billund and offers regular bus connections to various destinations in Denmark.

Other Options

Driving is also a convenient option, with Billund being well-connected to the rest of Denmark.

How to Get Around Billund

Walking and biking are the best way to get around Billund. The town is small and easy to navigate, making it easy for visitors to explore all of its attractions on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Billund: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aarhus

Aarhus is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city that is located just an hour’s drive from Billund. Visitors can explore the old town, relax by the beach, and enjoy a range of cultural attractions.

Legoland Billund Resort

Legoland Billund Resort is an expansive amusement park that is located just a short distance from Billund. The resort offers a range of attractions for visitors of all ages, from thrilling roller coasters to family-friendly rides.

Conclusion

Billund may be small, but it’s a town that offers visitors an exciting and unique experience. From exploring the beautiful outdoors to immersing yourself in the town’s history and culture, there is something for everyone in Billund.

With this ultimate travel guide, you’re now equipped with all the information you need to plan your trip to Denmark’s hidden gem. Get ready to experience all that Billund has to offer and create memories that will last a lifetime.
