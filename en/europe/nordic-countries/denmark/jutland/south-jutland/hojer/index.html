Discover the Beauty of Højer: Your Ultimate Travel Guide

Introduction to Højer

Nestled in South Jutland, Denmark, Højer is a picturesque town famous for its stunning natural beauty, historical landmarks, and vibrant culture. The town boasts a rich history dating back to the Viking era, evident in its well-preserved structures and museums. Additionally, the town is a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts, thanks to its abundant outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and water sports. Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Højer has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Højer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Højer Mill: Højer Mill is a historic Dutch windmill built in the 18th century. This landmark serves as a prime example of the Dutch architectural style and has been impeccably preserved for visitors to admire.
  • Hojer Sluseanlaeg: The Hojer Sluseanlaeg is a complex of locks built in the 19th century to regulate the waters of the Vidå River. Visitors can take a guided tour of the facility or simply soak up the stunning views.
  • Højerup Kirke: The Højerup Kirke is a historic church built in the 13th century. The church boasts some stunning architectural features, including a unique crooked spire that leans towards the west.

Natural Attractions of Højer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wadden Sea National Park: Wadden Sea National Park is a UNESCO-listed reserve along the North Sea coast. The park features diverse landscapes, including dunes, salt marshes, and mudflats. Visitors can go on guided walks and bird-watching tours or visit the picturesque islands that dot the landscape.
  • Vidå Canal: The Vidå Canal is a stunning canal that runs through Højer and connects to the Wadden Sea. The canal is the perfect spot for a relaxing stroll or a leisurely bike ride.
  • Højer Mølle Diget: The Højer Mølle Diget is a breathtaking nature reserve that offers visitors a chance to explore on foot or bike. The reserve features expansive meadows, old trees, and the picturesque Vidå River

Hiking/Biking Routes of Højer

The best way to explore the natural beauty of Højer is on foot or by bike. The town has numerous trails that cater to different skill levels, from simple lakeside walks to more challenging hill hikes. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Løgumkloster Route: This scenic 20 km route passes through rustic farmlands, charming towns, and the historic Løgumkloster Abbey.

  • The Wadden Sea National Park Route: Suitable for experienced hikers and cyclists, this challenging 50 km route takes visitors through the stunning Wadden Sea National Park.

  • The Vidå Route: A picturesque 10 km loop that follows the twisting Vidå River, this route is perfect for families with children or anyone who wants to take a leisurely walk.

Best Things to Do in Højer: Activities and Tours

Højer offers visitors a wide range of experiences, from tranquil walks through nature reserves to adrenaline-pumping water sports. Some of the most popular things to do in Højer include:

  • Bird Watching Tour: Wadden Sea National Park is home to over 10,000 migratory birds. Visitors can take guided bird-watching tours to spot various species that travel to the park every year.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The Vidå River is the perfect spot for kayaking and canoeing enthusiasts. Paddle along the river and admire its stunning natural beauty.

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can go horseback riding through the scenic countryside or take guided tours of the Wadden Sea National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Højer

Day 1 – Explore the historic Højer Mill followed by a leisurely walk through Hojer Scenery Diget.

Day 2 – Take a guided tour of the Hojer Sluseanlaeg, followed by a scenic bike ride along the Vidå Canal.

Day 3 – Visit Højerup Kirke, followed by a picnic lunch by the Vadehavscentret.

Day 4 – Hike along the Wadden Sea National Park route followed by a hearty Danish lunch.

Day 5 – Go on a bird watching tour in the Wadden Sea, followed by a kayaking session in the Baltic Sea.

Day 6 – Explore the charming town of Løgumkloster and visit the Løgumkloster Abbey.

Day 7 – Take a horseback riding tour of the Wadden Sea National Park and enjoy dinner at one of Højer’s many seafood restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Højer

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frikadeller: A traditional Danish meatball dish made from pork and beef served with red cabbage and boiled potatoes.
  • Æbleflæsk: A hearty dish of bacon, apples, onions, and sugar served with boiled potatoes and rye bread.
  • Røget sild: A smoked herring dish usually served with dark rye bread, eggs, and mustard sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Svaneke Bryghus: A small family-owned brewery in nearby Svaneke that produces organic beer.
  • Little Belt: A popular Danish aquavit made from spices and herbs, often served chilled as a digestive.

Where to Stay in Højer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Højer: A charming and family-friendly hotel that offers visitors comfortable rooms with stunning views of the Vidå River.

  • Danhostel Højer: An affordable hostel known for its cozy atmosphere and international clientele.

  • Højer Guesthouse: A lovely guesthouse with spacious rooms and comfortable amenities, located near the historic Højer Mill.

What to Buy in Højer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bodil Nielsen: A local ceramic artist who creates beautiful pottery pieces inspired by the surrounding nature.

  • Flensted Mobiles: A family-owned mobile crafting company based in nearby Tønder.

  • Organic Wadden Sea Salt: Produced in the nearby Rømø Island, this salt is harvested using traditional methods and is renowned for its unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Højer

Højer is a town with a rich cultural heritage that locals take pride in preserving. Visitors should respect the town’s local customs and traditions when partaking in festivities like the annual Musikfest. It is common courtesy to greet people with a handshake or a nod when meeting them.

Best Time to Visit Højer

The best time to visit Højer is from May to August when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. However, visiting in spring (March to April) has its advantages, such as viewing the blooming wildflowers around the Wadden Sea.

Do’s and Don’ts of Højer

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and traditions.
  • Try local delicacies like Frikadeller and Røget Sild.
  • Visit the many museums and historic landmarks that make Højer unique.

Don’t:

  • Ignore signs and designated paths when exploring natural areas.
  • Litter or harm the natural fauna and flora.
  • Disrespect locals and their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Højer

Højer is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution when wandering on hiking trails and when cycling near busy roads. It is also essential to obey signs and warnings in natural reserves like Wadden Sea National Park.

Health in Højer

It is advisable to carry travel health insurance when visiting Højer. The town has a hospital and a range of healthcare professionals in case of an emergency. Visitors should also be up to date with routine vaccinations before traveling to Denmark.

Getting to Højer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Højer

The nearest airports are in Billund (95 km) and Hamburg (131 km). Both airports serve international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Højer

The nearest bus stations are in Tønder and Løgumkloster.

Other Options

Visitors can reach Højer by car or train. Højer is accessible via the E45 highway, and there is a train station in nearby Tønder.

How to Get Around in Højer

The best way to get around Højer is by bicycle and on foot. Bikes are available for rent at various bike rental shops in Højer.

Where to Go Next from Højer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rømø Island: A picturesque island with sandy beaches, dunes, and a vibrant arts and crafts scene.
  • Tønder: A charming town known for its signature ‘Tønder Lace’ and well-preserved 17th-century houses.
  • Esbjerg: A bustling seaport town with a vibrant cultural scene and many family-friendly attractions.

Conclusion

Højer is a town that offers visitors a glimpse into Denmark’s rich history and stunning natural beauty. From exploring historic landmarks to ticking off adrenaline-pumping activities, there is never a dull moment in Højer. We hope our ultimate travel guide helps plan your trip better and makes your time in Højer unforgettable.
