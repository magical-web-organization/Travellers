Introduction to Rømø

Nestled in the southern part of Jutland in Denmark, Rømø is a small island community that stands out with its charming beach town vibes and picturesque countryside landscapes. The island, known for its wide sandy beaches and dunes, provides a perfect getaway for tourists who seek peaceful solitude away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

With a rich history and culture, pristine nature, and various attractions, Rømø offers a variety of activities that caters to everyone, be it a solo traveler, a couple on a romantic getaway, or a family vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Rømø

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rømø Kirke - This church, built in the 1200s, has undergone several restorations over the centuries but still maintains its original Gothic design. A perfect destination for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.

  • Lakolk Beach - The island’s widest beach, perfect for a romantic stroll, and offers picturesque views of the Wadden Sea.

  • Skærbæk Mølle - Visit a historic windmill dating back to 1844, learn about its history, and how it played an essential role in agriculture in Rømø during the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Rømø: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kommandørgård Nature Center - Located in the heart of Rømø’s nature reserve, this center offers nature walks, workshops, and events for visitors to learn about the local flora and fauna.

  • Rømø Dunes - The largest dunes in Northern Europe, towering at seventy-five meters high, provides picturesque views and hiking trails for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rømø

  • Rømø Round - A stunning 16-kilometer circular trail, starting at Lakolk Beach, offers hiking, bicycling routes, and a wealth of scenic overlooks & natural attractions, culminating in the lighthouse’s breathtaking view.

Best Things to Do in Rømø: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding on the Beach - Rømø is home to the world-renowned Rømø Island Horses, offering trail rides on the beach or in the countryside. A perfect adventure for horse lovers and those looking for a unique experience.

  • Sea Kayaking - Paddle through the calm waters of the Wadden Sea while enjoying stunning landscapes and wildlife like seals, migrating birds, and marine life.

Food and Drink Scene of Rømø

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rømø Lobster - Enjoy fresh lobster harvested locally and served at various restaurants throughout the island.

  • Flæskesteg - A traditional Danish dish of roasted pork with crackling served with boiled and roasted potatoes, red cabbage, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Søbogaard beer - Hailed Denmark’s oldest microbrewery with 11 different variations of unfiltered beer and cider, a must-try for beer lovers.

  • Schnapps - Try this traditional Danish brandy in various flavors, including honey and cherry.

Where to Stay in Rømø: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rømø Kurhotel - A luxury hotel situated directly on the Wadden Sea, offering impressive views and various wellness options like a sauna, spa, and fitness center.

  • Rømø Camping - A family-owned campsite located right on the beach and near the Rømø harbor, offering various cabins, trailers, and tents for an unforgettable camping experience.

What to Buy in Rømø: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rømø Baltic Amber - This amber, known as “Liquid Gold,” can be found on the island’s beaches and coast made into stunning jewelry, making it a perfect souvenir for visitors.

  • Wadden Sea Salt - Rømø, located on the Wadden Sea, produces some of Denmark’s finest salts, finishing salts, and seasoning blends perfect for chefs who love flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Rømø

Rømø is known for its rich culture, customs, and traditions, which include fairs, music festivals, and church events.

  • In May, the Rømø Kite Festival, where kites of all shapes and sizes adorn the sky, is a must-see event for visitors.

  • The Rømø Church fair takes place every July, offering traditional festivities with games, food, and music.

Best Time to Visit Rømø

The best time to visit Rømø is between May and October when the weather is warm, and most outdoor attractions are open. However, keep in mind that summers are the busiest and most expensive in terms of travel and accommodation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rømø

Do:

  • Respect nature and follow the “Leave No Trace” principles when hiking or biking.

  • Try the local seafood and cuisine; Rømø has some of the best Danish seafood dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches, or in any other natural areas.

  • Drive on the beaches or dunes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rømø

Rømø is generally safe and crime-free. However, it’s advisable to secure valuables and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Rømø

Rømø has a small health center that can provide necessary medical attention. However, emergency services may require transportation to the mainland’s hospitals.

Getting to Rømø: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rømø

The closest airport to Rømø is Esbjerg Airport (EBJ), which is about a 35-minute drive from the Rømø harbor.

Bus Stations Near Rømø

Rømø is accessible by bus via Sønderborg, Esbjerg, and Tønder. For more comprehensive travel options, it’s best to book a private bus tour or car rental and explore at your own pace.

Other Options

Another option is to drive to one of two ferries that service Rømø: the one from Havneby to List, Germany, or from Esbjerg to Nordby on the island of Fanø, from which Rømø is accessible by road bridge.

How to Get Around in Rømø

The most popular way to get around Rømø is by bike or on foot. Bicycles can be rented from various locations on the island, or guests can bring their own. Taxi services and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Rømø: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sylt Island, Germany - Take a short ferry ride from the Rømø harbor to Sylt and explore the island’s beautiful beaches and quaint towns.

  • Wadden Sea National Park - A vast, pristine landscape consisting of mudflats, salt marshes, and low-lying barrier islands, located on the border between Denmark, Germany, and the Netherlands.

Conclusion

Rømø, Denmark, is a small island that packs a big punch when it comes to natural beauty, exciting activities, rich culture, and flavorful cuisine. Whether it’s a relaxing beach holiday or an adventure-filled exploration, Rømø is an ideal destination for anyone looking to experience the best Denmark has to offer.
