Introduction to Tønder

Welcome to Tønder, a charming medieval town located in South Jutland, Denmark. Founded in the 13th century, Tønder has a rich history and is known for its beautiful architecture, cobblestone streets, and picturesque canals. The city is a popular tourist destination, thanks to its vibrant cultural scene, natural attractions, and excellent restaurants.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ve put together everything you need to know about Tønder - from a list of places to visit to the best hiking and biking spots, from the local food and drink scene to where to stay. You’ll also find tips on local customs, safety, and health. So, let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Tønder

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tønder Museum: Head to Tønder Museum to learn more about the history of the city. The museum is located in a beautiful historic building and features exhibitions on art, history, and culture.

  • Møgeltønder Church: A visit to Møgeltønder Church is a must when in Tønder. Built in the 12th century, this beautiful church is known for its intricate woodwork and stunning frescoes.

  • Schackenborg Castle: This beautiful castle was once owned by the Danish royal family and is now open to the public. Take a guided tour to learn more about the castle’s history, architecture, and art collection.

  • Tønder Festival: If you’re in Tønder in August, don’t miss the Tønder Festival, one of the largest folk music festivals in Europe. The festival brings together musicians, artists, and performers from around the world.

  • Bramminge Bakker: This scenic nature reserve is located just outside of Tønder and is perfect for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

Natural Attractions of Tønder: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wadden Sea National Park: Known for its unique landscape and wildlife, the Wadden Sea National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is a must-visit when in Tønder. Take a guided tour to explore the park’s tidal flats, salt marshes, and dunes.

  • Tønder Marsken: This beautiful wetland area is a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Take a guided tour to learn more about the bird species that call this area home.

  • Sønderjyllands Højderyg: This scenic hill range is located just outside of Tønder and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Hike to the top for panoramic views of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tønder

  • Højer-Polhøje Route: This 20-kilometer route takes you through the beautiful countryside of Tønder and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

  • Wadden Sea Trail: This 120-kilometer hiking trail takes you along the coast of Denmark, Germany, and the Netherlands and is a great way to explore the unique landscape of the Wadden Sea National Park.

  • National Park Route: This 25-kilometer route takes you through the heart of the Wadden Sea National Park and offers stunning views of the tidal flats and salt marshes.

Best Things to Do in Tønder: Activities and Tours

  • Canal Tour: Take a guided tour of the canals of Tønder and learn more about the city’s history and architecture.

  • Tønder Handelsby: This cozy shopping street is known for its unique shops, cafes, and restaurants. Spend an afternoon wandering around and enjoying the local flavor.

  • Tønder Art Museum: Located in a beautiful historic building, the Tønder Art Museum features an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art.

  • Culinary Tour: Take a guided tour of Tønder’s best restaurants and taste some of the city’s most delicious dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tønder

Day 1: Explore the historic center of Tønder and visit the Tønder Museum.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Wadden Sea National Park.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Sønderjyllands Højderyg for stunning views of the countryside.

Day 4: Visit the Møgeltønder Church and take a guided tour of Schackenborg Castle.

Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the Højer-Polhøje Route.

Day 6: Take a culinary tour of Tønder and taste some of the city’s best dishes.

Day 7: Spend a day exploring the nearby towns and countryside of South Jutland.

Food and Drink Scene of Tønder

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smørrebrød: A traditional Danish open-faced sandwich that typically features rye bread, butter, and a variety of toppings, including seafood, meat, and cheese.

  • Frikadeller: A Danish meatball that is typically served with potatoes and gravy.

  • Medisterpølse: A traditional Danish sausage that is usually eaten with potatoes and sauerkraut.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Denmark is known for its excellent beer, and Tønder is no exception. Try some of the local brews at one of the city’s many bars and pubs.

  • Aquavit: This traditional Scandinavian spirit is made from grain or potatoes and is usually served ice-cold.

Where to Stay in Tønder: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Tønderhus: Located in the heart of Tønder, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  • Motel Apartments: These cozy apartments are located just outside of Tønder and offer a peaceful retreat for travelers.

  • Danhostel Tønder: This hostel is perfect for budget travelers and offers comfortable dorm rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Tønder: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tønder Lace: This delicate handmade lace is a local specialty and makes a great souvenir or gift.

  • Københavnerstænger: These sweet Danish pastries are a delicious treat and make a great snack for on-the-go.

  • Hørkarklæde: This traditional Danish linen cloth is a durable and practical item that makes a great gift for any home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tønder

  • Hygge: This Danish term refers to a sense of coziness and contentment. Experience the local culture of Tønder by spending a cozy evening in one of the town’s many cafes or bars.

  • Respect for Nature: Denmark is known for its respect for nature, and this is evident in the way people live and work in Tønder. Be sure to be mindful of your impact on the environment while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Tønder

The best time to visit Tønder is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also the peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodations in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tønder

Safety and Health

Safety in Tønder

Tønder is a safe city, but be sure to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Tønder

Denmark has an excellent healthcare system, and Tønder has several hospitals and clinics. Be sure to have travel insurance that covers healthcare expenses.

Getting to Tønder: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Tønder: The closest international airport to Tønder is Billund Airport, which is located approximately one hour from the city.

  • Bus Stations Near Tønder: Tønder has several bus stations, with connections to nearby towns and cities.

  • Other Options: Tønder is also accessible by train and car.

How to Get Around in Tønder

Tønder is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There is also a reliable bus system that serves the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Tønder: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ribe: This beautiful medieval town is located just 30 minutes from Tønder and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.

  • Esbjerg: This bustling port town is located one hour from Tønder and is a great place to explore the Danish coastline.

  • Legoland Billund: This popular amusement park is located approximately one hour from Tønder and is a great destination for families with children.

Conclusion

With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural attractions, Tønder is a must-visit destination in Denmark.
