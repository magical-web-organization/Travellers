Vårdö, a beautiful island municipality located in the Åland archipelago of Finland, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Known for its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming locals, Vårdö is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the city’s hustle and bustle.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Vårdö, we’ll take you through all the must-visit attractions, things to do, local customs, and everything else you need to know before planning your trip.

Introduction to Vårdö

Vårdö is a small island municipality located in the Åland archipelago, which is situated between Sweden and Finland. This island has a total population of around 500 residents and is known for its spectacular natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

Vårdö has a significant history, dating back to the 12th century when it served as the main harbor for the ancient Vikings. You can still see the traces of Viking settlements in the form of ancient burial sites scattered all over the island.

Visitors to Vårdö can expect to be greeted with stunning ocean views, lush green forests, quaint fishing villages, and a whole lot of peace and quiet.

List of Places to Visit in Vårdö

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Vårdö Hembygdsgård: This cultural center showcases the traditional way of life of the locals and also features a traditional Finnish sauna.

  2. Vårdö Kirka: The church was built in the 14th century and exhibits impressive architectural design and ornate interior artwork.

Natural Attractions of Vårdö: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bockholmssund Nature Reserve: This incredibly scenic natural reserve offers breathtaking views of the archipelago and a place to explore nature by taking hikes or picnics.

  2. Schengen Village: A tiny village, Schengen is the place where the treaty for the implementation of a powerful passport-free zone in Europe, called the Schengen Agreement, was signed. Today, Schengen represents more than its size, its connection to the countries involved in the agreement and historical significance making it a popular attraction for tourists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vårdö

  1. The Österhankmo Hiking Trail: This easy hiking trail is an excellent choice for beginners and covers around 3 kilometers.

  2. The Vallbacken Nature Trail: Along with hiking, visitors can also bike along this scenic trail which traverses through forests and rocky shores.

Best Things to Do in Vårdö: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing Trips: The archipelago offers world-class fishing experiences, with salmon, trout, and pike being among the most popular species to catch.

  2. Kayaking and Canoeing: Vårdö is surrounded by water, making it an ideal place to explore the beautiful archipelago from the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Vårdö

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Raggmunk: A traditional potato pancake dish.

  2. Fried Herring: Freshly caught and fried, a staple in the local cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Ålandsschnaps: A strong alcoholic beverage, commonly enjoyed after meals.

  2. Åland Beer: Known for its crisp taste and refreshing finish, locally brewed beer that can be found in pubs and restaurants across Vårdö.

Where to Stay in Vårdö: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Övernäsgården Guesthouse: A family-run guesthouse with spectacular ocean views.

  2. Mellangården Guesthouse: A cozy and comfortable guesthouse with traditional Finnish architecture and design.

What to Buy in Vårdö: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Traditional Sauna Mugs: These mugs are made of wood and are typically used for sauna drinks.

  2. Åland Knäckebröd: Crispy bread made with rye flour and an essential item in Finnish cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Vårdö

The locals of Vårdö are very friendly and welcoming, and their culture is deeply rooted in their Viking and Finnish heritage. Visitors should be aware of the Finnish custom of taking off their shoes before entering homes and restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Vårdö

The best time to visit Vårdö is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and dry. This is also the time when most of the outdoor activities and attractions are open to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vårdö

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and culture.

  2. Take care of the environment by not littering or damaging flora and fauna.

Don’t:

  1. Be too loud and disruptive, especially in residential areas.

  2. Ignore safety and health precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vårdö

Vårdö is a safe place to visit, with minimal crime rates. Petty theft, however, can occur in tourist-heavy areas, so visitors must take precautions with their belongings.

Health in Vårdö

There are no significant health risks in Vårdö, but visitors should take note of Lyme disease, a bacterial infection transmitted by ticks. The best way to avoid contracting Lyme disease is by wearing adequate clothing and using bug spray.

Getting to Vårdö: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vårdö

The closest airport is Mariehamn Airport, around 35 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or hire a car to reach Vårdö.

Bus Stations Near Vårdö

There are regular bus services between Vårdö and Mariehamn and other destinations in Åland.

Other Options

Ferry services are another popular way to reach Vårdö, with ferries connecting it to Turku and Helsinki in mainland Finland.

How to Get Around in Vårdö

Car and bike rentals are available in Vårdö. Biking and hiking are also popular ways to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Vårdö: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kastelholm Castle: A major historical site of Åland, featuring an impressive medieval castle.

  2. The Åland Maritime Museum: A museum dedicated to the maritime history of Åland and Finland.

Conclusion

Vårdö is a beautiful destination that has something to offer to every traveler. From stunning natural vistas to rich cultural experiences, Vårdö is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan the perfect trip to Vårdö.
