Introduction to Mariehamn

Nestled in the heart of the Baltic Sea lies the stunningly beautiful Åland Islands, with its pearl, the charming town of Mariehamn. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and lively cultural scene, Mariehamn is a destination that has something to offer for everyone.

When exploring this picturesque town, visitors can take a guided tour and learn about its history, marvel at its rugged coastline, or take in the stunning sunset views over the harbor.

List of Places to Visit in Mariehamn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to soak up the rich heritage of Mariehamn is by visiting its museums and historical attractions, such as:

  • The Åland Maritime Museum
  • Pommern, a restored tall ship
  • The Museum Ship S/S Mariehamn
  • The Aland Art Museum

These attractions give visitors a unique insight into the town’s maritime history, local art, and culture.

Natural Attractions of Mariehamn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Mariehamn has plenty of natural attractions to enjoy, including:

  • The stunning Tullarns hill
  • The stunning sea views from the cliffs of Nabben
  • The peaceful Lill Klobb nature reserve
  • The rugged shoreline of the nearby Kobba Klintar islets

Whether hiking, trekking or just taking a leisurely stroll, visitors can enjoy these breathtaking natural wonders in close proximity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mariehamn

Mariehamn boasts of an extensive network of breathtaking hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Mariehamn Promenade
  • The Tullarns Hill Trail
  • The Kungsö Coastal Trail
  • The Lemström Canal Trail

These trails are suitable for all levels of fitness and offer visitors a unique chance to appreciate unique natural landscapes, meet local people and see wildlife in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Mariehamn: Activities and Tours

Whether you want to explore the town and its surroundings, learn about its history and culture, or relax and enjoy its natural beauty, Mariehamn has plenty to offer.

Some of the best things to do in Mariehamn include:

  • Join a guided tour of the Åland Maritime Museum
  • Take a cycling tour along the network of biking paths
  • Explore the rugged coastline and stunning natural landscapes
  • Join a boat tour of the nearby islets of Kobba Klintar

With so many activities and tours to choose from, visitors are guaranteed to make unforgettable memories in Mariehamn.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mariehamn

For visitors staying in Mariehamn for a week or more, it’s advisable to have a plan and to make the most of their stay. Here is a sample itinerary that can help you plan your week in Mariehamn:

Day 1 Visit The Åland Maritime Museum, The Pommern tall ship, and The Museum Ship S/S Mariehamn  
Day 2 Hike the Tullarns Hill Trail

Day 5 Take a cycling tour along the network of biking trails

Food and Drink Scene of Mariehamn

When it comes to sampling traditional local cuisine, Mariehamn delivers some of the best flavors in the Åland archipelago. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The local Åland pancake, topped with whipped cream and jam
  • Smoked fish, such as salmon, rainbow trout, and herring
  • The traditional Palt, or potato dumpling, served with lingonberry jam

Local Beverages

  • The local Åland beer, Stallhagen
  • The famous Åland apple cider

Where to Stay in Mariehamn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mariehamn offers a range of different accommodation options, suiting any budget or preference.

  • For those seeking luxury, the Hotel Arkipelag offers stunning views over the harbor and lavish amenities.
  • If looking for a more budget-friendly option, the Hostel & Guesthouse Kronan is a charming hostel conveniently located in the heart of Mariehamn.
  • For those looking for a cozier accommodation experience, the Degerby Hotel offers a comfortable stay with its charming farmstead character.

What To Buy in Mariehamn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mariehamn is known for its local handicrafts, made with love by local artisans who are proud of their Åland heritage and traditions. Some of the must-buy souvenirs include

  • Local Åland woolen and linen products
  • Artisanal teas and jams
  • Handmade soaps and candles

Local Culture and Customs of Mariehamn

Mariehamn is a friendly town, and locals are often more than happy to share their customs and traditions with visitors. Whether it’s taking a sauna, enjoying a traditional pancake, or learning about local crafts, Mariehamn encourages visitors to engage with the community and to leave with a better understanding of its local culture.

Best Time to Visit Mariehamn

Mariehamn is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy what it has to offer in any season. However, for those looking to experience its mild summers, warm autumns, snowy winters and chilly springs, the best time to visit is between May and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mariehamn

Do:

  • Respect the environment and keep the countryside tidy.
  • Engage with locals and learn about their customs and traditions, including the etiquette of taking a sauna.
  • Admire the rugged coastline and natural scenery of the archipelago’s islets.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave rubbish in the countryside.
  • Disturb wildlife or leave marked trails.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

While Mariehamn is a generally safe destination, visitors are advised to be vigilant, especially around busy areas and during weekends.

Safety in Mariehamn

Some safety tips include

  • Be careful when crossing the roads
  • Keep valuables safe and secure
  • Only use licensed taxis and public transport

Health in Mariehamn

The health system in Mariehamn is exemplary, and travelers can enjoy a high level of medical services.

Getting to Mariehamn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mariehamn is accessible by air, sea, and land.

Airports Near Mariehamn

The nearest airport is the Mariehamn Airport, which regularly hosts regular flights from Helsinki and Turku.

Bus Stations Near Mariehamn

Visitors can also take a bus from Turku to Stockholm via Mariehamn

Other Options

Another option is the ferry that regularly runs from Stockholm and Helsinki to Mariehamn.

How to Get Around in Mariehamn

Getting around Mariehamn is easy, and visitors can choose from public transportation, walking, cycling, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Mariehamn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mariehamn is a great starting point for exploring other nearby attractions and destinations, including:

  • The Archipelago Trail
  • The nearby town of Jomala
  • The nearby Sjöfartsmuseet i Kastelholm, a maritime museum located in Kastelholm Castle.

Conclusion

Mariehamn is perfect for anyone looking for a peaceful, authentic Scandinavian experience. With stunning natural landscapes, a rich cultural scene, and diverse attractions to explore, this town is a destination you don’t want to miss.
