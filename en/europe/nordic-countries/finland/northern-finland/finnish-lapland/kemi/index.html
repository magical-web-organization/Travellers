Introduction to Kemi

Located in Finnish Lapland, Kemi is a picturesque town in Northern Finland, known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and winter activities. With a population of just over 20,000, Kemi is a peaceful and serene destination that attracts travelers from all around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Kemi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kemi has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several museums and historical places that are worth visiting.

Kemi Gemstone Gallery

The Kemi Gemstone Gallery provides a glimpse of the world’s most beautiful minerals and gemstones. The gallery has a collection of over 3000 stones from around the world and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in geology.

Kemi Church

Kemi Church is one of the most iconic landmarks in the town and is known for its exquisite architecture and stunning interior. The church is open for visitors and is an excellent place to learn about the religious significance of the town.

Natural Attractions of Kemi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kemi is blessed with breathtaking natural beauty that offers visitors a chance to connect with nature and explore the great outdoors.

Kemi SnowCastle

The Kemi SnowCastle is one of the most popular attractions in the town and is a testament to the ingenuity of Finnish design. The castle is made entirely out of snow and ice and features stunning ice sculptures, an ice hotel, and a range of winter activities.

Sampo Icebreaker

The Sampo Icebreaker offers a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors to Kemi. This massive icebreaker vessel takes visitors on a voyage through the frozen waters of the Gulf of Bothnia and provides a rare opportunity to experience the breathtaking beauty of the Arctic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kemi

Kemi is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Finland, with routes that cater to every level of fitness and experience.

Keminmaa-Veitsiluoto Trail

The Keminmaa-Veitsiluoto Trail is a 13km hike that takes visitors through some of the most stunning landscapes in the area. The trail offers unmatched views of the Bothnian Bay and is a great way to connect with nature and explore the region’s natural beauty.

Kemi SnowCastle Trail

The Kemi SnowCastle Trail is a 5km hike that takes visitors through the stunning snowy landscapes of the SnowCastle. The trail offers an excellent opportunity to explore the SnowCastle and experience the best of Kemi’s winter activities.

Best Things to Do in Kemi: Activities and Tours

Kemi offers a range of activities and tours that cater to every age group and level of adventure.

Northern Lights Tour

With its proximity to the Arctic Circle, Kemi is an excellent destination to experience the Northern Lights. Visitors can join a guided Northern Lights tour and get a chance to witness this spectacular natural phenomenon.

Husky Safari

A Husky Safari is a thrilling way to explore the snowy landscapes of Kemi. Visitors can take a trip through the beautiful Lapland wilderness on a dog sled, led by a pack of energetic huskies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kemi

Day 1: SnowCastle and Gemstone Gallery

Start your trip with a visit to the iconic SnowCastle, followed by a tour of the Kemi Gemstone Gallery.

Day 2: Sampo Icebreaker and Northern Lights Tour

Take a tour of the Sampo Icebreaker in the morning and embark on a Northern Lights tour in the evening.

Day 3: Kemi Church and Hiking/Biking Trail

Visit the Kemi Church in the morning and spend the afternoon exploring the Keminmaa-Veitsiluoto Trail.

Day 4: Husky Safari

Spend a day with the energetic huskies on a thrilling husky safari.

Day 5: Snowshoe Hike and Ice Fishing

Explore the snowy landscapes of Kemi on a snowshoe hike and try your hand at ice fishing in the afternoon.

Day 6: Shopping and Local Culture

Spend a day shopping for souvenirs and exploring the local culture of Kemi.

Day 7: Relaxation Day

Take some time to relax and soak in the beautiful scenery of Kemi before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Kemi

Must-Try Dishes

Perunamuusi

Perunamuusi is a traditional Finnish dish made from mashed potatoes and served with a variety of meat dishes. It is a hearty and filling meal that is perfect for cold winter days.

Kalakukko

Kalakukko is a Finnish fish pie that is made with rye pastry and stuffed with fish, potatoes, and bacon. It is a delicious and satisfying meal that is perfect for lunch or dinner.

Local Beverages

Lapin Kulta

Lapin Kulta is a popular Finnish beer that is brewed in Lapland. It is a light, crisp, and refreshing beer that is perfect for warmer months.

Leijona Gusto

Leijona Gusto is a traditional Finnish soft drink that is flavored with blackcurrant and other local berries. It is a refreshing and delicious drink that is perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Kemi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Merihovi

Hotel Merihovi is a popular hotel in Kemi that is known for its excellent service and comfortable rooms. The hotel is centrally located and is within walking distance of all major attractions in the town.

Kemi Seaside Glass Villas

Kemi Seaside Glass Villas are a unique and luxurious option for visitors to Kemi. The villas are made entirely out of glass and offer stunning views of the Arctic Ocean.

What to Buy in Kemi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rovaniemi Glass

Rovaniemi Glass is a traditional Finnish glassware that is handmade in Kemi. The glassware is known for its unique designs and high-quality craftsmanship and is a perfect gift or souvenir to take home.

Reindeer Skins

Reindeer skins are a quintessential Finnish souvenir that is perfect for cold winter days. The skins are warm, cozy, and can be used as a rug or throw.

Local Culture and Customs of Kemi

Winter Sports

Winter sports such as skiing, snowboarding, and ice skating are an important part of local culture in Kemi. The town has several ski resorts and ice rinks that offer a range of activities for visitors to enjoy.

Sauna Culture

Sauna culture is an integral part of Finnish culture, and Kemi is no exception. Visitors can enjoy traditional Finnish saunas in several locations throughout the town.

Best Time to Visit Kemi

The best time to visit Kemi is between December and March, when the town is covered in snow and offers a range of winter activities and sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kemi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress warmly and bring appropriate winter gear
  • Try traditional Finnish dishes and souvenirs
  • Explore the outdoors and enjoy winter sports

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the privacy of the locals
  • Litter or damage natural attractions
  • Ignore safety guidelines for winter sports and activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Kemi

Kemi is a safe and peaceful destination for travelers, with low crime rates and excellent infrastructure. Visitors should follow basic safety guidelines for winter sports and outdoor activities to avoid any accidents or injuries.

Health in Kemi

Kemi has excellent healthcare facilities, with several hospitals and clinics located throughout the town. Visitors should have travel insurance and should seek medical help in case of any injuries or illnesses.

Getting to Kemi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kemi

The closest airport to Kemi is the Kemi-Tornio Airport, which is located just 5km from the town center. The airport is served by several airlines that offer regular flights to major destinations across Europe.

Bus Stations Near Kemi

Kemi has several bus stations that offer regular services to major cities in Finland and other European destinations. The bus network in the town is reliable and affordable, making it an excellent option for budget travelers.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kemi by train, with regular services from Helsinki and other major Finnish cities. The train network in Finland is excellent and offers comfortable and affordable services to travelers.

How to Get Around in Kemi

Kemi is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bicycles in several locations throughout the town, and there are several bike paths that offer a safe and scenic way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Kemi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi is a stunning destination located just a few hours away from Kemi. The town is known for its stunning natural beauty, excellent winter sports, and the Santa Claus Village.

Oulu

Oulu is a vibrant and bustling city located just a few hours away from Kemi. The city is known for its excellent food and drink scene, cultural attractions, and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Kemi is a beautiful and serene destination in Northern Finland that offers visitors a chance to explore the great outdoors, experience the local culture, and enjoy a wide range of winter activities. This comprehensive guide provides all the information visitors need to plan the perfect trip to Kemi, Finland.
