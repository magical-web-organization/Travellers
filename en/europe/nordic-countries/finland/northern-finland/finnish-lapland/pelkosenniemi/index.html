Introduction to Pelkosenniemi

Pelkosenniemi is a village located in the Lapland region of Finland, known for its stunning scenery, traditional Finnish culture, and outdoor activities. With its untouched wilderness, picturesque forests, and crystal-clear lakes, Pelkosenniemi offers an abundance of opportunities for outdoor adventures, making it a popular destination for visitors from all over the world.

This travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know about Pelkosenniemi, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. So, whether you’re planning a week-long vacation or just a quick getaway, read on to discover all that this Finnish gem has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Pelkosenniemi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Finnish Lapland is rich in history and tradition, and Pelkosenniemi is no exception. Here are a few highlights:

Pelkosenniemi Church

Pelkosenniemi Church is a stunning wooden church located in the village center, built in 1822. The church is an important cultural and religious landmark and is renowned for its beautiful architecture.

Siida Museum

The Siida Museum is located about an hour’s drive north of Pelkosenniemi in Inari and is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about Sami culture. The museum showcases exhibits on Sami history and culture, as well as contemporary art.

Natural Attractions of Pelkosenniemi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pelkosenniemi offers a remarkable range of outdoor activities with stunning scenery as a backdrop. Here are a few not-to-miss attractions:

Pyhätunturi National Park

Pyhätunturi National Park is a vast expanse of wilderness featuring rugged ravines, mountains, and ancient forests. It is particularly beautiful during the winter months when the snow is on the ground, and the Northern Lights are on full display.

Korouoma Canyon

Korouoma Canyon is a 30-kilometer-long canyon that features stunning waterfalls, crystal-clear streams, and picturesque views. It’s an ideal spot for hiking, camping, and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pelkosenniemi

The Lapland area is known for its spectacular hiking and biking routes. Here are some of Pelkosenniemi’s most popular trails:

Pyhä-Nattanen Trail

The Pyhä-Nattanen Trail is a first-rate hiking route in Pyhä-Luosto National Park. The trail is 60 km long, spanning through beautiful forests, moorland hills, and scenic mountains. The trail leads you to the summit of the legendary Nattanen Hill, providing breathtaking views of the park from a height of 700 meters.

Vanha Kolmiotie Trail

Vanha Kolmiotie Trail is a historical hiking route showcasing the old “Triangle Route” used by gold prospectors and reindeer herders in the 19th century. The route consists of magnificent forests, swamps, and bogs, making it a truly authentic Finnish wilderness experience.

Best Things to Do in Pelkosenniemi: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking to unwind, get your adrenaline pumping, or just soak in the beauty of Northern Finland, Pelkosenniemi offers something for every traveler. Here are a few options:

Skiing

Pelkosenniemi is known for its excellent skiing facilities, including cross-country skiing, downhill skiing, and heli-skiing. The Pyhä-Luosto Ski Resort is one of the best-known places to hit the slopes, with a range of slopes suitable for skiers of all levels.

Snowmobiling

If you’re looking for some excitement, snowmobiling is a great way to explore the Lapland wilderness. There are several tour operators who offer snowmobile safaris, taking you to some of the most beautiful and remote parts of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pelkosenniemi

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you get the most out of your Pelkosenniemi adventure:

Day 1: Experience the local culture at the Siida Museum in Inari. Day 2: Go hiking or snowmobiling in Pyhätunturi National Park. Day 3: Visit the historic Pelkosenniemi Church. Day 4: Take a guided snowshoeing tour around the Pyhä-Luosto area. Day 5: Go skiing or snowboarding at the Pyhä-Luosto Ski Resort. Day 6: Visit the Korouoma Canyon for an unforgettable hiking experience. Day 7: Enjoy a traditional Finnish sauna and relaxing spa treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Pelkosenniemi

Sampling the local cuisine is one of the best ways to experience a new culture. Here are a few must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Karjalanpaisti

Karjalanpaisti is a traditional Finnish stew made with beef, pork, and vegetables, including carrots, onions, and sometimes mushrooms. It’s slow-cooked for hours, making it tender and flavorful.

Sautéed Reindeer

Reindeer meat is a staple of the Sami diet, and sautéed reindeer is a must-try dish when in Lapland. It’s typically served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce and is a delicious, hearty meal.

Local Beverages

Lapin Kulta Beer

Lapin Kulta is a popular Finnish beer brewed in Lapland. It’s a refreshing, light beer with a crisp, clean taste.

Cloudberry Liqueur

Cloudberry Liqueur is a sweet, fruity liqueur made from the local cloudberry fruit. It’s often served as a dessert drink or aperitif.

Where to Stay in Pelkosenniemi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pelkosenniemi offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are a few of the best places to stay:

Santa’s Hotel Tunturi

Santa’s Hotel Tunturi is a cozy, welcoming hotel located a stone’s throw from the Pyhä-Luosto National Park. It boasts a range of amenities, including a restaurant, spa, and sauna.

PyhäSuites

PyhäSuites is a modern, stylish, and comfortable accommodation option that offers an array of apartments and chalets. It’s situated in the heart of Pyhä-Luosto National Park, offering easy access to all the area’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Pelkosenniemi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without bringing home a few souvenirs. Here are a few ideas of what to buy in Pelkosenniemi:

Sami Craftwork

Sami craftwork includes beautiful woven textiles, handicrafts, and jewelry made from natural materials such as reindeer antler, leather, fur, and wood. It’s a great way to take home a piece of Lapland and support local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Pelkosenniemi

Lapland has a rich cultural heritage that’s worth learning about before visiting. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Sami Culture

Sami culture is an essential part of the Lapland region, and visitors should respect their traditions and way of life. Sami people are known for their unique handicrafts and reindeer herding practices, which have been passed down for generations.

Best Time to Visit Pelkosenniemi

The best time to visit Pelkosenniemi depends on your preferences. If you’re looking to hit the slopes, December to April is the best time to visit. If you’re looking for warmer weather and more outdoor activities, summer is an excellent time to explore the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pelkosenniemi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or certain public buildings, such as schools and hospitals.
  • Learn a few Finnish phrases to show respect for the language and culture.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Assume everyone speaks English – learning a few Finnish phrases will go a long way!
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pelkosenniemi

Pelkosenniemi is a safe destination to visit. However, as with any outdoor activity, visitors should take appropriate safety precautions, particularly in outdoor activities like skiing, hiking, or snowmobiling. It’s essential to be prepared for the weather, to have the right gear, and to inform others of your planned route.

Health in Pelkosenniemi

Pelkosenniemi has world-class medical facilities and resources, and visitors can access medical care when needed. It’s also advisable to take basic health precautions like avoiding tap water, washing hands frequently, and bringing any essential medications with you.

Getting to Pelkosenniemi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pelkosenniemi is well connected to the rest of Finland and the world. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Pelkosenniemi

The nearest airport to Pelkosenniemi is Rovaniemi, which is approximately 100 kilometers away. You can also fly into Kittilä airport, which is 120 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Pelkosenniemi

Pelkosenniemi can be reached by bus, with daily bus services to the area from Rovaniemi and Kemijärvi.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Pelkosenniemi is located approximately 100 kilometers north of Rovaniemi along the E75 highway.

How to Get Around in Pelkosenniemi

The best way to get around Pelkosenniemi is by car, especially if you’re planning to explore the region’s scenic routes and national parks. However, for those without a car, there are taxi services and bus routes available.

Where to Go Next from Pelkosenniemi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pelkosenniemi is located in the heart of Lapland, making it an ideal starting point for exploring the region. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi, the capital of Lapland, is a two-hour drive from Pelkosenniemi. It’s known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning scenery, and the world-renowned Santa Claus Village.

Levi Ski Resort

Levi Ski Resort is one of Finland’s best-known ski destinations, and it’s located about 150 kilometers northwest of Pelkosenniemi. Levi offers high-quality downhill skiing, cross-country skiing, and snowmobiling experiences.

Conclusion

Pelkosenniemi is one of Finland’s hidden gems, offering breathtaking scenery, rich cultural heritage, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to hit the slopes, hike through stunning forests, or explore Lapland’s unique culture, Pelkosenniemi is the perfect destination. This travel guide should provide you with everything you need to plan the trip of a lifetime.
