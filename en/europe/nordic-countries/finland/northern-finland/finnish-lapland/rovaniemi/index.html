Rovaniemi, the official town of Santa Claus, is a dream destination for many. Located in the heart of Finnish Lapland, Rovaniemi offers a plethora of unique experiences from visiting Santa Claus himself to going on reindeer safaris, and witnessing the Northern Lights. This travel guide has everything you need to plan your trip to Rovaniemi, Finland.

Introduction to Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi is a magical destination situated in the heart of Finnish Lapland. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique outdoor experiences, Rovaniemi is a dream come true for travelers.

Rovaniemi is the official home of Santa Claus and the Arctic Circle, which makes it a popular destination for families and kids. The town has a population of around 60,000 people and offers a charming atmosphere where you can enjoy a range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi has a lot to offer for tourists, from cultural museums to outdoor adventures, it is never a dull moment in this charming town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The cultural scene in Rovaniemi is vibrant, and there are many museums and historical places you can explore. Some popular cultural attractions include:

  1. Arktikum
  2. Rovaniemi Art Museum
  3. Science Center Pilke
  4. Korundi House of Culture
  5. Santa Claus Village

Natural Attractions of Rovaniemi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rovaniemi is a paradise for nature lovers. With stunning landscapes ranging from forests to rolling hills and rivers, the town has a lot to offer for outdoor adventurers. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  1. Ounasvaara Ski Resort
  2. Husky Park
  3. Ranua Wildlife Park
  4. Pyha-Luosto National Park
  5. Snowmobiling tours

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi has some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Finland. There are many trails that you can explore, ranging from short walks to multi-day hikes. Some popular hiking and biking routes in Rovaniemi include:

  1. Ounasvaara Fell Trail
  2. Auttiköngäs Nature Trail
  3. Vaattunkiköngäs Trail
  4. Syötteen kansallispuisto National Park hiking trails

Best Things to Do in Rovaniemi: Activities and Tours

Rovaniemi has plenty of unique things to offer that really make it stand out from other destinations. Some of the best things to do in Rovaniemi include:

  1. Visit Santa Claus at Santa Claus Village
  2. Witness the Northern Lights with a guided tour
  3. Go on a reindeer or husky safari
  4. Ice fishing on a frozen lake
  5. Snowmobiling with experienced guides

Week-Long Itinerary for Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi offers numerous activities, and it can be overwhelming to decide how to make the most of your time there. Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Rovaniemi city center and visit Arktikum Museum
2 Husky safari and snowmobiling at the Ounasvaara Ski Resort
3 Hiking in Pyhä-Luosto National Park
4 Visit Rovaniemi Art Museum and Science Center Pilke
5 Visit Ranua Wildlife Park and see the local wildlife up close
6 Snowshoeing in the forest and Aurora hunting with a guided tour
7 Visit Santa Claus Village, cross the Arctic Circle and shop for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi’s food and drink scene are something you don’t want to miss. Finnish food is unique and delicious, with a mix of fresh ingredients and flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Reindeer steak with lingonberry jam
  2. Salmon soup
  3. Sautéed reindeer
  4. Arctic char
  5. Lohikeitto (salmon soup)

Local Beverages

  1. Glögi (mulled wine)
  2. Lapin Kulta (local beer)
  3. Cloudberry liqueur

What to Buy in Rovaniemi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rovaniemi is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some popular items to bring back home:

  1. Reindeer leather wallets and gloves
  2. Handmade traditional Sami jewelry
  3. Finnish design items
  4. Puukko knife
  5. Natural beauty products made in Lapland

Local Culture and Customs of Rovaniemi

The Sami people are the main indigenous group in Lapland, and their culture and history are an integral part of the region’s identity. When visiting Rovaniemi, make sure to respect the local customs and traditions, including the Sami people’s beliefs and culture.

Best Time to Visit Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi is a year-round destination, and each season offers unique experiences. However, the best time to visit Rovaniemi depends on what type of activities and experiences you want to enjoy.

  1. Winter season (December-March) - for winter activities, Northern Lights, and Christmas celebrations.
  2. Summer season (June-August) - for hiking, biking, and outdoor adventures in the wilderness.
  3. Autumn (September-October) - for autumn foliage and quiet tourist activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rovaniemi

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Dress warmly and appropriately for outdoor activities.
  3. Book activities and tours in advance.

Don’t:

  1. Chase or disturb wildlife.
  2. Litter the natural environment.
  3. Drive off-road or on snowmobile trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi is generally a safe destination, and the crime rate is low. However, you should take precautions and stay alert, especially during the winter when it gets dark early.

Health in Rovaniemi

Finland has an excellent healthcare system, and Rovaniemi has several public and private hospitals and clinics. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance just in case of accident or illness.

Getting to Rovaniemi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rovaniemi is well connected by air, rail, and road and is easily accessible from many Finnish cities and other European destinations.

Airports Near Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi has its airport, Rovaniemi Airport (RVN), which is around 10 minute-drive away from the city center.

There are also two other airports around two hours away - Kittilä and Ivalo airports.

Bus Stations Near Rovaniemi

There are several bus companies in Finland that operate routes to Rovaniemi.

Other Options

Some other means of transport to consider include rental cars, taxis, or coaches.

How to Get Around in Rovaniemi

Public transport in Rovaniemi is reliable and comfortable, with good connectivity to most parts of the town. Alternatively, you can rent bikes, cars, or snowmobiles for more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Rovaniemi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra days in Rovaniemi, consider exploring some of the nearby destinations and attractions. Some popular options include:

  1. Kemi - home to the SnowCastle, a magnificent ice hotel.
  2. Levi Ski Resort - one of Finland’s largest and most popular ski resorts
  3. Ylläs - a small town famous for its winter sports and Aurora Village.

Conclusion

Rovaniemi is a must-visit destination and offers a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and unique experiences. Whether you are dreaming of a snowy winter wonderland or an adventure in the wilderness, Rovaniemi has something to offer.
