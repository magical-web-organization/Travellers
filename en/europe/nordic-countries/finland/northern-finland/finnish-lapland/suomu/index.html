Introduction to Suomu

Suomu is a hidden gem nestled in the heart of Finnish Lapland, in Northern Finland, one of the Nordic countries in Europe. Suomu is an idyllic destination for holidaymakers looking for serenity, adventure, and captivating natural beauty. The town is surrounded by sprawling forests, crystal-clear lakes, and majestic mountains, providing a breathtaking backdrop for every adventure.

Whether you’re looking to explore the local culture and customs, get lost in nature with outdoor adventures, or simply want to unwind and relax, Suomu is the perfect destination for you. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do in Suomu so that you can plan your dream vacation with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Suomu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Suomu is steeped in history and culture, evident from its numerous museums and historical places. The following are some of the top attractions worth visiting:

The Lapland War Museum

The Lapland War Museum is a must-see for history buffs. It highlights the role of Lapland in World War II and the effect of the war on the region and its inhabitants.

The Saami Museum Siida

The Saami Museum Siida showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the indigenous Saami people of Finnish Lapland. The museum offers an excellent opportunity to learn about the Saami people and their way of life.

Natural Attractions of Suomu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its vast and beautiful wilderness, Suomu is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic views to add to your itinerary:

Hiking in Riisitunturi National Park

Riisitunturi National Park is a hiker’s paradise, with breathtaking landscapes, stunning views, and well-marked trails. The park also boasts the famous Riisitunturi Fell, a scenic spot that provides stunning views of the surrounding area.

Cross-country skiing and snowshoeing

Suomu is a winter wonderland, and cross-country skiing and snowshoeing are some of the most popular winter activities. The town boasts an extensive network of well-groomed trails, where visitors can enjoy the beauty of the Lapland wilderness while staying active.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suomu

Suomu is a paradise for hiking enthusiasts, offering a vast network of well-marked trails. The following are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Suomu:

Riisitunturi National Park Trail

This trail leads hikers through some of the most spectacular scenery in Riisitunturi National Park. The trail weaves between the fell tops and valleys, passing through some of the park’s most beautiful spots.

Suomutunturi Fell Trail

The Suomutunturi Fell Trail provides hikers with another opportunity to explore the beauty of Finnish Lapland. The trail leads hikers to the top of Suomutunturi Fell, which offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Suomu: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to see and do in Suomu. From summer activities like canoeing and hiking to winter activities like skiing and snowmobiling, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Suomu:

Husky Safari

A husky safari is an unforgettable experience, allowing visitors to travel through the snowy wilderness with a team of huskies. The tours take place in winter, and visitors can choose between short or long rides, depending on their interest.

Canoeing

Canoeing is a popular summer activity in Suomu, with plenty of rivers and lakes to explore. Visitors can rent canoes and explore the wilderness at their own pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Suomu

Must-Try Dishes

Finnish cuisine is hearty and wholesome, with some unique dishes that visitors should try. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Suomu:

Reindeer Meat

Reindeer meat is a staple in Finnish cuisine, and Suomu boasts some of the most delicious renditions of this meat. There are various restaurants in the area that serve reindeer steak, soup, and stew.

Gravlax

Gravlax is a cured salmon dish that’s popular in Finland. The dish is made by burying salmon in a mixture of salt, sugar, and dill.

Local Beverages

The craft beer scene is thriving in Suomu, with several local breweries producing delicious, unique flavors. In addition, there are some traditional Finnish beverages that visitors should try, such as:

Koskenkorva

Koskenkorva is a Finnish vodka made from barley that has been distilled multiple times. It’s a common drink in Finland, and visitors can try it at various bars and restaurants in Suomu.

Sima

Sima is a traditional Finnish alcoholic drink made from honey, lemon, and yeast. It’s often consumed during festivals and celebrations, and it’s a delicious and refreshing drink that visitors should try.

Where to Stay in Suomu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Suomu that cater to different budgets and tastes. Here are some of the top options:

Lapland Hotel Luostotunturi

The Lapland Hotel Luostotunturi is a perfect choice for visitors looking for a luxury stay. The hotel has a pool, a spa, and several restaurants on-site.

Riekonkieppi Chalets and Apartments

The Riekonkieppi Chalets and Apartments offer comfortable, homey accommodation options. Visitors can choose from wooden cabins, apartments, or hotel rooms.

What to Buy in Suomu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Suomu offers various souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home with them. Some of the most popular include:

Saami Crafts

The Saami people are famous for their beautiful handicrafts, such as reindeer hides, knives, jewelry, and clothes. Visitors can purchase Saami crafts at the Saami Museum Siida gift shop.

Finnish Sweets

Finnish sweets are popular worldwide, and Suomu offers several sweet treats that visitors can take back home as gifts. Some of the popular Finnish sweet treats include Fazer chocolates and Salmiakki candy.

Local Culture and Customs of Suomu

The indigenous Saami people have a significant influence on the culture and customs of Suomu. The Saami people have a unique way of life, and visitors can learn about it by visiting the Saami Museum Siida.

Best Time to Visit Suomu

Suomu is a great destination year-round, with different seasons offering a unique experience. In winter, visitors can enjoy winter sports, whereas, in summer, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like canoeing and hiking. The best time to visit Suomu is from mid-February to early April, when the weather is less extreme and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suomu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress warmly in winter and wear appropriate hiking gear in summer.
  • Explore the local nature, culture, and customs.
  • Enjoy the unique food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the wilderness or disrespect nature.
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol.
  • Ignore the Northern Lights, a stunning natural phenomenon.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suomu

Suomu is a very safe destination, with a low crime rate. Visitors should be careful when exploring the wilderness, watch out for wildlife, and dress warmly to avoid hypothermia in winter.

Health in Suomu

Visitors should bring any necessary medication with them, as pharmacies in Suomu are uncommon. Visitors should also be careful when hiking and exploring the wilderness to avoid accidents.

Getting to Suomu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Suomu

The nearest airport to Suomu is Kuusamo Airport, which is approximately 50 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Suomu.

Bus Stations Near Suomu

There are several bus stations near Suomu, with regular bus services connecting Suomu to other towns and cities in Finland.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Suomu, although it’s a long journey from most major cities in Europe.

How to Get Around in Suomu

The best way to explore Suomu is by car or bicycle, as public transport services are limited in the area. Visitors can rent cars or bicycles at various locations in Suomu.

Where to Go Next from Suomu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Suomu is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area, and there are several nearby destinations worth visiting. Some of the popular options include:

Rovaniemi

Rovaniemi is the capital of Lapland and boasts several attractions, including Santa Claus Village, the Arktikum Museum, and the Korundi House of Culture.

Pyha-Luosto National Park

Pyha-Luosto National Park is an excellent destination for nature lovers, with stunning landscapes, scenic trails, and fascinating flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Suomu is a magical destination in Finnish Lapland that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a place to unwind, Suomu has it all. Use this guide to plan your dream holiday in Suomu, and discover the beauty of Finnish Lapland.
