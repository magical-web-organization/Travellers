Introduction to Kilpisjärvi

Nestled in the Enontekiö region of Finnish Lapland, Kilpisjärvi is a small village located in northern Finland. This untouched wilderness is surrounded by mountains and pristine lakes and it is a perfect gateway for travelers seeking an adventure in natural paradise. With its cultural heritage, outdoor activities, and stunning landscapes, Kilpisjärvi offers visitors a chance to connect with nature and experience the unique Finnish way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Kilpisjärvi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Historic Places, and Local Architecture

Kilpisjärvi may be small, but it is not short on cultural attractions. Here are some must-visit places in Kilpisjärvi:

  • Sámi Duodji: This gallery exhibits the works of the indigenous Sámi people and their traditional crafts such as clothing, footwear, jewelry, and traditional tools. Visitors can also buy souvenirs and gifts from the gift shop.
  • Halti: This mountain is the highest peak in Finland and is the border between Norway and Finland. Hiking to the top of Halti will give you a breath-taking view of the surrounding landscape.
  • Church of Saint Kilpikaarnanen: This wooden church was built in the year 1952 and is located in the center of the village. Its interior is decorated with local handicrafts and it is an excellent example of local architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kilpisjärvi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kilpisjärvi offers travelers a variety of outdoor activities and scenic views. Here are some of the top things to do in Kilpisjärvi:

  • Kilpisjärvi Lake: The lake is surrounded by hills and a nature reserve, and is located in the village center. The lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, kayaking, and other water sports.
  • Kilpisjärvi Nature Centre: This visitor center provides information about the local flora and fauna found in the nearby nature reserve. They also offer guided tours and exhibitions related to the Arctic nature.
  • Saana Fell: This hill provides a challenging hike for experienced hikers and it is famous for its natural rock formations. The hike provides stunning views of the nearby glaciers and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kilpisjärvi

Kilpisjärvi is famous for its hiking routes, and the trails provide something for every level of hiker or biker. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in Kilpisjärvi:

  • Halti Hiking Trail: This is a challenging trail that takes hikers to the summit of Halti Mountain. It is approximately 55 kilometers long and takes about 4 days to complete.
  • Saana Fell Hiking Trail: This trail takes hikers to the top of Saana Fell. It is approximately 8 kilometers long and takes about 3 hours to hike.
  • Kilpisjärvi-Pallas Hiking Trail: This trail runs from Kilpisjärvi village to Pallas Fell. Visitors can bike or hike this 50-kilometer trail and take in the incredible views.

Best Things to Do in Kilpisjärvi: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to do in Kilpisjärvi. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Reindeer Sledding: Visitors can go on a reindeer sledding tour and experience the traditional way of transportation in the Arctic.
  • Aurora Hunting: During the winter months, the Northern Lights can be seen regularly in Kilpisjärvi. Visitors can book an Aurora hunting tour and experience the stunning light show.
  • Hot Sauna: Visitors can take a relaxing sauna and experience the Finnish way of life. The sauna is usually followed by a dip in the cold lake, which is said to have health benefits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kilpisjärvi

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you plan your Kilpisjärvi adventure:

Day 1-2: Explore the village of Kilpisjärvi, visit the local museums, take a nature trail hike.

Day 3-4: Hike to the top of Saana Fell, visit Kilpisjärvi Nature Centre and take a boat tour on Kilpisjärvi Lake.

Day 5-6: Explore the nearby Pallas-Ounastunturi National Park, experience the traditional Finnish Sauna and take a dip in the lake.

Day 7: Go on a reindeer sled ride and experience the traditional culture of Lapland.

Food and Drink Scene of Kilpisjärvi

Kilpisjärvi has a variety of local cuisine that visitors must try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Manchurian Trout: This is a local dish that is made with the trout found in Kilpisjärvi Lake. The fish is marinated and then served with fried rice and vegetables.

  • Kilpisjärvi Meatballs: These meatballs are served with creamy mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam, and they are a Finnish staple.

Local Beverages

  • Sámi Tea: This is a local tea that is made with floating cloudberry and dwarf birch leaves. It is said to have a soothing effect and health benefits.

  • Sámi Beer: This is a local beer that is brewed with berries from the local forest. It has a unique and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Kilpisjärvi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kilpisjärvi offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Kilpisjärven Retkeilykeskus: This is a guesthouse that offers affordable rooms with shared bathrooms and a communal kitchen. It is centrally located, and visitors can book various tours and activities from here.

  • Lapland Hotel Kilpis: This hotel offers a comfortable stay with private bathrooms and stunning views of the nearby nature reserve. It also has a sauna and a restaurant that serves local cuisine.

What to Buy in Kilpisjärvi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kilpisjärvi offers plenty of souvenir options for visitors to bring back home. Here are some of the best local products to buy:

  • Sámi Handicrafts: Visitors can buy traditional Sámi handicrafts such as leather bags, shoes, and belts.

  • Kilpisjärvi T-Shirt: Visitors can buy a Kilpisjärvi T-Shirt to show off their visit to the stunning village.

Local Culture and Customs of Kilpisjärvi

The people of Kilpisjärvi are known for their love of nature and traditional culture. Here are some customs and traditions you should know about:

  • Respect Nature: The people of Kilpisjärvi have a deep respect for nature. Visitors must follow their example and not litter or damage the environment.

  • Sámi Culture: The indigenous people of Lapland are the Sámi people, and their culture has a significant influence on Kilpisjärvi. Visitors can experience their traditional crafts, music, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Kilpisjärvi

The best time to visit Kilpisjärvi depends on what you want to do while you are there. The winter months are perfect for experiencing the Northern Lights, while the summer months are perfect for hiking and other outdoor activities.

  • Experience the Local Culture and Traditions: Kilpisjärvi has a rich cultural heritage. Visitors must take the opportunity to learn and experience the local way of life.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Kilpisjärvi

Kilpisjärvi is a relatively safe place to visit. However, visitors must be aware of the following:

  • Weather Conditions: The weather in Kilpisjärvi can change quickly, and visitors must be prepared for it.

  • Wild Animals: Kilpisjärvi has plenty of wild animals such as bears and reindeer. Visitors must avoid getting too close to them.

Health in Kilpisjärvi

The healthcare system in Kilpisjärvi is excellent, and visitors can expect quality medical care. However, visitors must be aware of the following:

  • Ticks: Ticks are common in the nearby forested areas, and visitors must take precautions to avoid tick bites.

  • Sunburn: The sun can be strong during the summer months, and visitors must use sunscreen and wear protective clothing.

Getting to Kilpisjärvi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kilpisjärvi may be remote, but it is still relatively easy to access. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Kilpisjärvi

  • Enontekiö Airport: This is the closest airport to Kilpisjärvi, and it is located approximately 60 kilometers away.

  • Kittilä Airport: This airport is located 250 kilometers away from Kilpisjärvi.

Bus Stations Near Kilpisjärvi

  • Kilpisjärvi Bus Station: Kilpisjärvi has a regular bus service that runs to and from the nearby cities.

  • Enontekiö Bus Station: Enontekiö Bus Station is located approximately 67 kilometers away from Kilpisjärvi.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from the nearby cities and drive to Kilpisjärvi.

  • Taxi: Visitors can also take a taxi from the nearby cities to Kilpisjärvi.

How to Get Around in Kilpisjärvi

The best way to get around Kilpisjärvi is by walking or biking. Visitors can also rent a car or take a local taxi service.
