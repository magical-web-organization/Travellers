Introduction to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

The Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area, in Finnish Lapland, is a breathtaking destination for adventure-seekers and nature enthusiasts alike. The area is characterized by its pristine wilderness, vast forests, crystal-clear lakes, and stunning mountain vistas.

Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area covers a vast expanse of over 400,000 hectares, including the Pöyrisjärvi Lake, which is one of the largest lakes in Lapland. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, such as hiking, fishing, canoeing, and skiing. The area is also culturally significant, with numerous museums and historical sites showcasing the unique heritage of the Sami people of Lapland.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything that Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area has to offer, from the best places to visit and things to do, to the food and drink scene, accommodations, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sámi Siida Museum: The Siida Museum is an excellent place to learn about the culture and history of the Sami people of Lapland. Exhibitions showcase traditional Sami handicrafts, clothing, and artifacts.
  • Jyppyrä Chapel: This charming 19th-century chapel is a popular spot for visitors, located on a hill above the village of Hetta. The chapel has a beautiful bell tower and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Keimiöniemi Fishing Hut: This historical fishing cabin, located on the shore of the Pöyrisjärvi Lake, is a perfect spot for a picnic. The cabin dates back to the early 1900s and has been restored to show what life was like for fishermen in Lapland in the past.

Natural Attractions of Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pöyrisjärvi Lake: This massive lake is the highlight of the wilderness area, offering visitors endless opportunities for fishing, boating, and canoeing. Visitors can also hike in the surrounding forests and mountains.
  • Kätkävaara Fell: Kätkävaara is a rugged fell, providing stunning views of the surrounding wilderness. Visitors can reach the top of the fell via a challenging hiking trail.
  • Pyhäkero Fell: Pyhäkero is one of the highest fells in the area, standing at 555 meters tall. Visitors can enjoy a scenic hike to the summit, with views of the Pöyrisjärvi Lake and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, offering thousands of kilometers of trails to explore. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Pöyrisjärvi Round Trail: This 60-kilometer trail circles around the Pöyrisjärvi Lake, offering stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Hetta-Pallas Trail: This 55-kilometer hiking route is famous for its breathtaking landscapes and cultural sites, such as the Sioskuru Gorge and the Pallastunturi Fell.
  • Keimiönjärvi Lake Trail: This 16-kilometer trail circles around the Keimiöjärvi Lake, offering stunning views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area: Activities and Tours

Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area offers visitors a range of fun and exciting activities and tours to choose from, such as:

  • Fishing: The vast Pöyrisjärvi Lake is a perfect spot for fishing enthusiasts, with populations of perch, pike, and trout.
  • Canoeing: Visitors can rent a canoe and explore the pristine wilderness of Pöyrisjärvi at their own pace.
  • Husky Safaris: Tour the wilderness area from the perspective of an energetic husky team. Several different companies offer dog sledding tours.
  • Northern Light Hunting: Experience the incredible Arctic night sky and marvel at the stunning aurora borealis with an expert guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Here’s a recommended itinerary for a week-long trip to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the local history and culture of Lapland by visiting the Sámi Siida Museum, Jyppyrä Chapel, and Keimiöniemi Fishing Hut.
  • Day 3-4: Hike or bike one of the region’s many scenic trails, such as the Pöyrisjärvi Round Trail or the Hetta-Pallas Trail.
  • Day 5: Experience the thrill of dog sledding while taking in the stunning winter landscapes of Pöyrisjärvi.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day of fishing or canoeing on the Pöyrisjärvi Lake.
  • Day 7: Take in the incredible sights of the Aurora Borealis on a guided Northern Lights Hunting tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sautéed reindeer: This traditional dish consists of tender, juicy reindeer meat sautéed with onions and served with mashed potatoes.
  • Kalakukko: This unique Finnish dish is made by stuffing rye bread with fish and pork, then baking it until it is crispy on the outside and moist on the inside.

Local Beverages

  • Cloudberry Liqueur: This delicious, sweet liqueur is made from the rare cloudberry, which grows wild in the Lapland wilderness.
  • Lingonberry Juice: Lingonberries are a common ingredient in Finnish cuisine, and the juice is a refreshing and healthy beverage.

Where to Stay in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area can choose from a range of accommodation options to suit their budget and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • Hotel Hetan Majatalo: This cozy hotel offers comfortable rooms with authentic lapland decor and delicious breakfast and dinner service.
  • Hetta Hostel: This clean and comfortable hostel provides affordable dorm rooms, with a fully equipped shared kitchen for self-caterers.
  • Majava-Mökki Cottage: These cozy lakeside cabins offer privacy and stunning lake views, perfect for honeymooners or couples seeking a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Reindeer Pelts: Reindeer pelts are a common souvenir in Lapland, with their soft and warm fur making them perfect for home decor.
  • Lapland Knives: These traditional Finnish knives are popular among hikers and outdoorsmen, known for their durability and sharpness.

Local Culture and Customs of Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

The Sami people, who are the indigenous people of Lapland, have a rich and unique culture. Visitors to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area can learn about their history and customs by visiting local museums and cultural heritage sites. Visitors should also be mindful of traditional Sami customs, including respecting the land and animals, and asking permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

The best time to visit Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area depends on your preferences. Summer is ideal for hiking, fishing, and outdoor adventures, while winter offers opportunities for skiing, snowboarding, dog sledding, and Northern Lights Hunting. Visitors should be aware that the winters in Lapland can be harsh, with temperatures dipping -30°C or more.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Do:

  • Respect the environment: Visitors should leave no trace and take care to preserve the beautiful wilderness of Pöyrisjärvi for future generations.
  • Experience the culture: Visitors should take the time to learn about traditional Sami customs and heritage, respecting the unique history and traditions of Lapland.
  • Dress appropriately: Visitors should dress in layers and bring appropriate footwear, depending on the time of year and activities planned.

Don’t:

  • Harm the wildlife: Visitors should not disturb or harm the wildlife in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area, leaving them to live in peace in their natural habitat.
  • Leave garbage or litter: Visitors should take all garbage and litter with them, leaving the wilderness area as pristine as they found it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Visitors to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area should be aware of the potential risks associated with hiking and winter sports. It is recommended that visitors carry appropriate equipment and clothing, such as snowshoes, hiking boots, and warm clothing. Visitors should also be mindful of the weather forecast and avoid entering the wilderness area if the conditions are unsafe.

Health in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive health insurance before traveling to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area. There are no hospitals in the immediate wilderness area, so visitors should also take precautions, such as wearing sunscreen, staying hydrated, and avoiding close contact with wildlife.

Getting to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area depends on your starting point. Visitors arriving from further afield will likely fly into Helsinki, and then take a connecting flight to Enontekiö, which is the closest town to the wilderness area. Visitors can also take buses or rent a car to reach the wilderness area.

Airports Near Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

  • Enontekiö Airport is the nearest airport, located about 10 km from the town of Enontekiö.

Bus Stations Near Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

  • Enontekiö Bus Station: This bus station serves the town of Enontekiö and is the best option for visitors traveling to Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to reach the wilderness area. Car rental companies are available at the airports in Helsinki and Enontekiö.

How to Get Around in Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area

Visitors can get around the Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area on foot, by bike, or by car. There are also several local companies that offer guided tours, such as Northern Lights hunting or dog sledding.

Where to Go Next from Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area is just one of many stunning destinations in Finnish Lapland. Visitors can explore other nearby destinations, such as the Pallastunturi National Park or the nearby village of Hetta.

Conclusion

With its jaw-dropping natural vistas, rich cultural heritage, and endless opportunities for adventure, Pöyrisjärvi Wilderness Area is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to experience the beauty and wonder of Lapland. Whether you’re hiking through the forests, dog sledding across the snow-covered landscapes, or simply savoring the local cuisine, Pöyrisjärvi is a destination you won’t soon forget.
