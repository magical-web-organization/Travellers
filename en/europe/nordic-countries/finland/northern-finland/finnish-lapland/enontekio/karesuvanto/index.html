Introduction to Karesuvanto

Karesuvanto is a small village located at the northernmost part of Finnish Lapland, close to the border with Sweden and Norway. This remote and peaceful village is surrounded by stunning nature, including the majestic fells and the Torne River, the largest free-flowing river in Europe. Karesuvanto is an ideal destination for those looking for a genuine Arctic experience and outdoor adventures in a serene and unspoiled environment.

List of Places to Visit in Karesuvanto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sámi Heritage House - This museum showcases the rich culture and traditions of the indigenous Sámi people through exhibitions, workshops, and guided tours.
  • Karesuando Church - This wooden church dates back to the eighteenth century and is the oldest in Enontekiö. It is known for its intricate wooden decorations and stunning frescoes.
  • Lake Kesänkijärvi Stone Age Graves - These ancient burial sites provide a glimpse into the prehistoric times of the area.
  • Hietaniemi War Cemetery - This cemetery commemorates the fallen soldiers of World War II in the northernmost war cemetery in Finland.

Natural Attractions of Karesuvanto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park - This park offers a wide variety of hiking trails that cater to different skill levels and preferences. You can explore the rugged fells, pristine forests, and clear lakes, and encounter a diverse range of flora and fauna along the way.
  • Torne River - You can take a boat trip or go fishing in the Torne River, which is known for its crystal-clear water and abundant salmon and trout.
  • Kilpisjärvi Fell Area - This area boasts some of the most breathtaking sceneries in the northernmost Lapland, including the iconic Saana Fell and the Three-Country Cairn, where you can stand on the border of Finland, Sweden, and Norway.
  • Aavasaksa Hill - This hill provides a panoramic view of the surrounding wilderness and the midnight sun during the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Karesuvanto

  • Hetta-Pallas Trail - This 55-km trail takes you from Hetta to Pallas through the Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park. You can witness stunning landscapes, including the Taivaskero Fell, and stay overnight in wilderness huts.
  • Reindeer Trail - This 21-km trail takes you through the reindeer pastures and forests of Karesuvanto and offers ample opportunities to spot reindeer, the iconic animal of Lapland.
  • Kilpisjärvi-Saana Fell Trail - This 6-km trail takes you to the summit of the Saana Fell, where you can enjoy a panoramic view of the Arctic mountains and lakes.
  • The Finnish Part of Nordkalottruta - This 800-km trail connects the wilderness areas of Finland, Norway, and Sweden, and takes an average of 45 days to complete.

Best Things to Do in Karesuvanto: Activities and Tours

  • Aurora Hunting Tours - You can join guided tours to witness the spectacular Northern Lights, which are visible from September to April in Karesuvanto.
  • Husky and Reindeer Safaris - You can ride a sled pulled by huskies or reindeer and experience the life of traditional Arctic herders.
  • Snowmobiling Adventures - You can explore the pristine winter landscapes in Karesuvanto on a snowmobile and reach remote locations that are inaccessible on foot.
  • Ice Fishing - You can try ice fishing in the frozen lakes of Karesuvanto and enjoy a tranquil and meditative experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Karesuvanto

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat - This is a staple of Lappish cuisine, and you can try it in various forms, including sautéed, stewed, or roasted.
  • Kalakukko - This is a traditional Finnish fish pie made of rye flour and filled with fish and pork fat.
  • Mushroom Soup - This is a creamy soup made of wild mushrooms, onions, and cream and served with rye bread.

Local Beverages

  • Bilberry Juice - This is a refreshing and healthy drink made of bilberries, which are abundant in Lapland.
  • Lappish Tea - This tea is made of herbs and berries, such as lingonberries, juniper, and nettle, and is an ideal beverage to warm you up after a cold day outdoors.
  • Sámi Reindeer Blood Pancake Drink - This is a traditional Sámi drink made of reindeer blood and lingonberry juice and is believed to be a potent energizer.

Where to Stay in Karesuvanto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Karesuando Fjällcenter - This hotel offers comfortable accommodation with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and river. You can also enjoy a sauna, a hot tub, and a restaurant on the premises.
  • K-Market Karesuvanto Hostel - This hostel provides affordable accommodation with clean and basic facilities in the center of Karesuvanto.
  • Roavve Guesthouse - This guesthouse is located in the village of Palojoensuu and provides cozy and rustic accommodation with magnificent views of the fells.

What to Buy in Karesuvanto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sámi Crafts - You can buy handmade crafts made by local Sámi artisans, including traditional silver jewelry, woodcarvings, and reindeer skin products.
  • Lappish Food Products - You can buy various local delicacies, including smoked salmon, reindeer meat, cloudberry jam, and bilberry liqueur.
  • Traditional Finnish Clothing - You can buy woolen socks, hats, and mittens made by local designers and made of high-quality wool or fur.

Local Culture and Customs of Karesuvanto

Karesuvanto is the homeland of the Sámi people, one of the oldest indigenous populations in Europe. Thus, the local culture is heavily influenced by the Sámi traditions, including the reindeer herding and the shamanistic beliefs. The Sámi people valorize the connection with nature, the oral storytelling, and the preservation of the ancestral knowledge and language. Therefore, visitors should respect the Sámi culture and customs and engage in respectful and inclusive interactions with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Karesuvanto

The best time to visit Karesuvanto depends on the traveler’s interests and preferences. The summer months from June to August offer the Midnight Sun phenomenon, with 24 hours of daylight and a vibrant green landscape. The autumn months from September to November offer the chance to witness the Northern Lights and the breathtaking autumnal colors. The winter months from December to March provide opportunities for snow sports, such as skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobiling. The spring months from April to May offer the perfect combination of winter sports and the arrival of spring, with a warmer climate and longer daylight hours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Karesuvanto

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, including the traditional Sámi way of life.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local delicacies and beverages, including the reindeer meat and the Lappish tea.
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their culture and traditions.
  • Take precautions against mosquitos and ticks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the natural environment, including littering and damaging wildlife.
  • Engage in intrusive photography without the consent of the locals.
  • Disturb the reindeer herds and their pastures.
  • Ignore the safety regulations and instructions of the guides and authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Karesuvanto

Karesuvanto is a safe destination with a low crime rate, but visitors should take precautions against the harsh and changeable Arctic environment. Therefore, some safety tips include:

  • Dressing appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Carrying a map, a compass, and a charged mobile phone.
  • Following the safety instructions of the activities and tours.
  • Carrying a first aid kit and medication for common ailments.

Health in Karesuvanto

Karesuvanto has a primary health center that provides basic medical services, but visitors should prepare for the common health risks associated with visiting the Arctic region. Therefore, some health tips include:

  • Getting the recommended vaccinations and medications.
  • Properly hydrating and nourishing the body.
  • Protecting the skin from the sun and the cold.
  • Avoiding excessive consumption of alcohol and drugs.

Getting to Karesuvanto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Karesuvanto is a remote destination that requires careful planning and logistics. However, there are several transportation options available, including:

Airports Near Karesuvanto

  • Enontekiö Airport (ENF) - This is the nearest airport to Karesuvanto, servicing domestic flights from Helsinki and other Finnish cities.

Bus Stations Near Karesuvanto

  • Rovaniemi Bus Station - You can take a bus from Rovaniemi to Karesuvanto, with several daily departures.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - You can rent a car from several locations in Finland and drive to Karesuvanto.
  • Train - You can take the train from Helsinki to Kolari and then transfer to a bus or a taxi to Karesuvanto.

How to Get Around in Karesuvanto

Karesuvanto is a small village that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, for longer distances and remote locations, you can use the following options:

  • Taxi - You can hire a taxi from the village center or book one in advance.
  • Car Rental - You can rent a car from several providers in the village or nearby towns.
  • Snowmobile - During the winter months, you can rent a snowmobile and explore the winter wonderland of Karesuvanto.

Where to Go Next from Karesuvanto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
