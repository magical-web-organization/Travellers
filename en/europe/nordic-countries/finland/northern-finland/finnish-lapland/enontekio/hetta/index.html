Introduction to Hetta

Hetta, located in Enontekiö, in Finnish Lapland, in Northern Finland, in the Nordic countries, and in Europe, is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventurers, and culture buffs alike. Surrounded by pristine wilderness, stunning mountains, and sparkling rivers and lakes, Hetta offers endless outdoor activities and scenic views at any time of year. In addition, Hetta boasts a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical sites showcasing the local life and customs of the Sami people. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Hetta.

List of Places to Visit in Hetta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Samiland Museum and Nature Centre Siida: This museum showcases the culture, history, and traditions of the Sami people. It also features exhibits on Lapland’s flora and fauna as well as a planetarium.
  • Kierikki Stone Age Centre: This open-air museum takes you back in time to the Stone Age and offers a glimpse into the lives of the early inhabitants of Lapland.
  • Jyppyrä Observation Tower: Located in the Pyhä-Luosto National Park, this tower offers panoramic views of the surrounding forests and fells.

Natural Attractions of Hetta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: With its vast wilderness, rugged landscapes, and diverse flora and fauna, this national park is a paradise for hikers, skiers, and nature enthusiasts.
  • Lake Ounasjärvi: This beautiful lake is perfect for fishing, swimming, and kayaking in the summer and ice fishing and skiing in the winter.
  • Pakasaivo: This mysterious lake, surrounded by steep cliffs, is considered sacred by the Sami people and is believed to have mystical powers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hetta

  • Hetta-Pallas Trail: This 55-km long trail is one of the most popular hiking routes in Finland and takes you through the stunning landscapes of the Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park.
  • Pyhä-Luosto National Park: With its network of well-marked trails, this national park offers numerous hiking and biking options for all levels of fitness and experience.
  • Tunturi-Lappi Trail: This 40-km long trail takes you through the picturesque fells and forests of the Enontekiö region and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Hetta: Activities and Tours

  • Reindeer Safari: Experience the traditional Sami way of life and ride a reindeer-drawn sled through the snowy wilderness. Learn about the Sami culture and enjoy a warm meal in a traditional Sami tent.
  • Husky Safari: Hop on a sled pulled by a pack of huskies and glide through the wintry landscapes of Lapland. This adventure is a must-do for dog lovers and thrill seekers.
  • Aurora Hunting Tour: Chase the northern lights and witness the breathtaking spectacle of the aurora borealis. Enjoy a cozy campfire and hot drinks while waiting for the show to begin.

Food and Drink Scene of Hetta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer meat dishes: Reindeer is a staple of the Sami diet and is commonly used in soups, stews, and smoked meats.
  • Rössypottu: This traditional Finnish soup is made with pork, potatoes, carrots, turnips, and onions.
  • Muikku: These small freshwater fish are a local delicacy and are usually pan-fried and served with mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: This traditional Finnish beer is made with malted barley, wheat, and rye and is flavored with juniper berries and spruce shoots.
  • Cloudberry liqueur: This sweet and tangy liqueur is made from the rare and expensive cloudberry fruit, which grows in the Lapland region.
  • Coffee: Finland is known for its love of coffee, and Hetta is no exception. Enjoy a cup of strong and aromatic coffee at one of the local cafes or restaurants.

Where to Stay in Hetta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Hetta: This centrally located hotel offers comfortable rooms, a sauna, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.
  • Fell Centre Kiilopää: This wilderness resort, located in the Pyhä-Luosto National Park, offers a range of accommodation options, from cabins to hotel rooms, and features a spa, restaurant, and outdoor activities.
  • Hetta Huskies Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse, run by a husky breeder and guide, offers comfortable rooms, traditional Finnish sauna, and husky tours.

What to Buy in Hetta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sami handicrafts: Look for traditional Sami handicrafts, such as duodji, which are made by hand using natural materials and techniques that have been passed down for generations.
  • Reindeer products: Reindeer hides, antlers, and meat products make for unique and authentic souvenirs and gifts to bring back home.
  • Lapland-themed gifts: From wooden ornaments to woolen mittens, Hetta offers a range of souvenirs that showcase the beauty and heritage of Lapland.

Local Culture and Customs of Hetta

  • Sami culture: The Sami people are the indigenous people of Lapland and have a rich cultural heritage. Learn about their traditions, language, and way of life by visiting museums and cultural sites and participating in cultural events.
  • Sauna culture: Saunas are an important part of Finnish culture and daily life. Be prepared to experience the heat and relaxation of a traditional Finnish sauna during your stay in Hetta.

Best Time to Visit Hetta

The best time to visit Hetta depends on your interests and preferred activities.

  • Winter (December-March): This is the ideal time for winter sports, such as skiing, dog sledding, and snowmobiling, as well as for seeing the northern lights.
  • Summer (June-August): The summer months are perfect for hiking, fishing, and canoeing, as well as for experiencing the midnight sun, when the sun never sets.
  • Fall (September-November) and Spring (April-May): These transitional seasons offer milder weather, fewer crowds, and stunning autumn colors and spring blooms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hetta

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Sami people.
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles and aim to minimize your impact on the environment.
  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions and pack accordingly.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or damage the natural habitats.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Ignore the safety instructions and guidelines provided by your tour guides and activity providers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hetta

  • Hetta is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it is important to be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking, skiing, and fishing. Always follow safety guidelines and instructions provided by your activity providers.
  • During the winter months, be prepared for extreme weather conditions, including subzero temperatures, snowstorms, and icy roads.

Health in Hetta

  • Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and medical facilities and services are readily available in Hetta and nearby towns. However, it is recommended to have travel health insurance before your trip.
  • Vaccinations are not required for visitors to Finland. However, it is advisable to keep your routine vaccinations up to date.

Getting to Hetta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hetta

  • Enontekiö Airport (5 km)
  • Kittilä Airport (200 km)
  • Rovaniemi Airport (250 km)

Bus Stations Near Hetta

  • Enontekiö Bus Station
  • Muonio Bus Station
  • Kittilä Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train stations are Kolari and Rovaniemi, both of which can be reached by bus from Hetta.
  • Car rental: Car rental is available at the airport and in Hetta town.

How to Get Around in Hetta

  • Walking: Hetta town is relatively small and easy to explore on foot. Many of the attractions and activities are also within walking distance.
  • Bike rental: Bikes are available for rent at several locations in Hetta town.
  • Car rental: Rental car is a convenient option for exploring the surrounding areas and national parks.

Where to Go Next from Hetta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tromsø, Norway: Known as the “Gateway to the Arctic”, Tromsø is a scenic and cultural destination, offering activities such as dog sledding, northern lights tours, and whale watching.
  • Turku, Finland: This historic seaside city is the perfect destination for a day trip or weekend getaway, with its charming old town, medieval castle, and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Stockholm, Sweden: If you have more time and want to explore further, take a trip to Stockholm, the beautiful and cosmopolitan capital of Sweden, with its museums, galleries, and iconic landmarks.

Conclusion

Hetta, in Enontekiö, in Finnish Lapland, in Northern Finland, in the Nordic countries, and in Europe, is a hidden gem, waiting to be discovered. With its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor activities, Hetta offers something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to Hetta and experience the magic of Lapland.
