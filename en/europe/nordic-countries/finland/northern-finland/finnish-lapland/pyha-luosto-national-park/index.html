Introduction to Pyhä-Luosto National Park

Pyhä-Luosto National Park is situated in Finnish Lapland, in Northern Finland, part of the Nordic countries in Europe. It covers a vast area of 142 km², and is home to two fells - Pyhä and Luosto. Pyhä-Luosto National Park is one of the oldest national parks in Finland, established in 1938. It’s a popular destination for both winter and summer outdoor activities and is the perfect spot for hiking, cycling, skiing, and admiring the northern lights.

Best Time to Visit Pyhä-Luosto National Park

It’s best to visit Pyhä-Luosto National Park between November and April to witness the Northern Lights. If you want to hike and enjoy outdoor activities, it’s best to visit during the summer months from June to August.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pyhä-Luosto National Park

  • Wear proper outdoor gear
  • Take necessary precautions to deal with unexpected weather changes

How to Get Around in Pyhä-Luosto National Park

The best way to explore Pyhä-Luosto National Park is on foot, bike or skis. It’s a large area, and the terrain is mostly rugged with trails marked and maintained by park officials for visitors.
