Introduction to Lemmenjoki National Park

Located in Finnish Lapland, Lemmenjoki National Park is renowned for its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. At 2,850 square kilometers, it is the largest national park in Finland and offers a wide range of activities and experiences for nature and adventure enthusiasts.

Some of its main attractions include the Lemmenjoki River, the Kevo Nature Reserve, and the Sami Museum and Nature Center Siida. Visitors can enjoy hiking and biking along the park’s well-marked trails, fishing, canoeing, and river rafting.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at what Lemmenjoki National Park has to offer, so you can plan your trip and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Lemmenjoki National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sami Museum and Nature Center Siida: A state-of-the-art museum that showcases the culture and history of the Sami people, the indigenous inhabitants of Lapland. Exhibits include traditional costumes, handicrafts, and artifacts, as well as multimedia displays about Sami life and traditions.

  • Lemmenjoki Visitor Center: The main information point for the national park, where visitors can get maps, brochures, and hiking tips. The center also has exhibits about the flora, fauna, and geology of the park, as well as a small café.

Natural Attractions of Lemmenjoki National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Lemmenjoki River: The longest free-flowing river in Finland, famous for its gold rush history. Visitors can take guided tours to try their luck panning for gold or explore the river on a canoe or kayak.

  • Kevo Nature Reserve: A rugged wilderness area known for its geological formations, deep gorges, and alpine heaths. It is home to several rare bird and plant species, including the Lapland longspur, the Siberian jay, and the globe flower.

  • Ravadas Falls: A picturesque waterfall located on the Ravadasjoki River, surrounded by lush vegetation and granite boulders. It is a popular spot for picnics and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lemmenjoki National Park

With over 200 kilometers of well-marked trails, hiking and biking are among the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Lemmenjoki National Park. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Lemmenjoki Goldfields Trail: A 22-kilometer hike that takes you through the heart of the gold rush area, passing by abandoned mines, prospecting pits, and cabins. The trail is relatively easy and suitable for beginners.

  • Ravadas Falls Trail: A 10-kilometer round trip hike that leads to the impressive Ravadas Falls, with scenic views along the way. The trail is moderately difficult, with some uphill sections.

  • Kevo Wilderness Trail: A challenging 63-kilometer trek that requires good physical condition and hiking experience. The trail takes you through the rugged terrain of Kevo Nature Reserve, offering stunning views of the Arctic landscape.

Best Things to Do in Lemmenjoki National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Gold Panning: Experience the thrill of gold prospecting in the same way as Finnish gold diggers did in the 19th century. With the help of an experienced guide, you’ll learn how to find and extract gold particles from the river sand.

  • River Rafting: Take a thrilling ride down the rapids of the Lemmenjoki River on a raft or kayak. This activity is suitable for all levels of experience, from beginners to advanced adventurers.

  • Sami Cultural Experience: Learn about the unique culture and way of life of the Sami people, including their reindeer herding traditions, handicrafts, and music. You can also try traditional Sami foods and drinks, such as reindeer meat, smoked salmon, and cloudberries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lemmenjoki National Park

Day 1: Arrive at Lemmenjoki Visitor Center, get information and maps. Take a short hike to the Ravadas Falls.

Day 2: Go gold panning on the Lemmenjoki River, accompanied by a guide.

Day 3: Take a leisurely hike along the Lemmenjoki Goldfields Trail, stopping at the historic cabins and mines.

Day 4: Explore Kevo Nature Reserve on a guided hike, taking in the breathtaking views of the gorge and alpine landscape.

Day 5: Enjoy a relaxing day at the Sami Museum and Nature Center Siida, learning about the history and culture of the Sami people.

Day 6: Take a river rafting excursion on the Lemmenjoki River, experiencing the thrill of the rapids.

Day 7: Depart from Lemmenjoki National Park, taking with you memories of a lifetime.

Food and Drink Scene of Lemmenjoki National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat: A staple food of the Sami people, reindeer meat is lean and tender, with a mild gamey flavor. It is typically served as a stew or grilled with lingonberry sauce.

  • Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish fish pie made with rye bread and stuffed with salmon, perch, or pike. It is a hearty and filling dish, ideal for outdoor picnics.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made with malted barley, juniper berries, and hops. It has a distinctive sweet and spicy flavor and is typically served in wooden cups.

  • Vodka: A popular alcoholic drink in Finland, vodka is typically served chilled and accompanied by pickled cucumber or fish.

Where to Stay in Lemmenjoki National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Inari Wilderness Hotel: A family-owned hotel located in the heart of Lapland, offering comfortable rooms and apartments, a restaurant, and a sauna.

  • Lemmenjoki Wilderness Hotel: A rustic hotel located at the entrance of the national park, offering basic rooms and cabins, a café, and a souvenir shop.

  • Biekka Guesthouse: A traditional Sami guesthouse located on the banks of the Lemmenjoki River, offering authentic accommodations, meals, and activities.

What to Buy in Lemmenjoki National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sami Handicrafts: Traditional Sami handicrafts, such as woven tapestries, wooden bowls, and reindeer antler jewelry, are unique and high-quality souvenirs that reflect the local culture and traditions.

  • Berry Products: Lapland is famous for its berries, such as lingonberries, cloudberries, and blueberries, which are used to make jams, syrups, and liqueurs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lemmenjoki National Park

The Sami people, who have inhabited Lapland for thousands of years, have a unique culture and way of life that is closely connected to the natural environment. They are known for their reindeer herding, handicrafts, and music, as well as their rich oral traditions and folklore.

Visitors to Lemmenjoki National Park can learn about the Sami culture at the Sami Museum and Nature Center Siida and participate in Sami-themed activities and tours.

Best Time to Visit Lemmenjoki National Park

The best time to visit Lemmenjoki National Park is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the days are long. This is also the high season, with plenty of outdoor activities and events available.

During the autumn months, from September to November, the park is quieter but still offers beautiful fall foliage and the possibility to see northern lights.

In the winter months, from December to March, the park is covered in snow and offers unique winter activities, such as dog sledding, snowshoeing, and cross-country skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lemmenjoki National Park

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife, and leave no trace.

  • Follow the park rules and regulations, especially regarding campfires and littering.

  • Bring appropriate hiking gear and clothing, including mosquito repellent and rain gear.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the peace and quiet of the park, especially during the night.

  • Approach wild animals or feed them.

  • Take anything from the park, such as rocks, plants, or artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lemmenjoki National Park

Lemmenjoki National Park is generally safe for visitors, but it is important to be aware of the risks and take appropriate precautions. These include:

  • Be cautious when hiking near cliffs and waterfalls, as the terrain can be slippery and uneven.

  • Keep a safe distance from wild animals, such as bears and moose.

  • Avoid swimming in the river, as the water is cold and fast-flowing.

  • Carry a map and GPS device, and let someone know your itinerary and expected return time.

Health in Lemmenjoki National Park

Lemmenjoki National Park is a remote wilderness area with limited medical facilities and services. Visitors should take the following health precautions:

  • Drink only boiled or filtered water, and bring enough water for your trip.

  • Wear protective clothing and use insect repellent to avoid insect bites.

  • Bring a basic first aid kit and medication for common ailments, such as headaches or stomach upsets.

Getting to Lemmenjoki National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lemmenjoki National Park is located in Finnish Lapland, approximately 200 kilometers north of the Arctic Circle. The nearest airport is Ivalo Airport, which is served by several airlines, including Finnair and Norwegian.

From Ivalo, visitors can take a bus or taxi to the national park, or rent a car and drive along the E75 highway. The park is also accessible by train, with regular services from Helsinki to Rovaniemi, from where you can take a bus or taxi to the park.

How to Get Around in Lemmenjoki National Park

The best way to get around Lemmenjoki National Park is on foot or by bike, using the well-marked trails and designated routes. Visitors can also get around by canoe or kayak on the Lemmenjoki River or by guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Lemmenjoki National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lapland is a vast and diverse region with many other attractions and destinations that are worth visiting. Some of the nearby places include:

  • Inari: A small town on the shore of Lake Inari, known for its Sami culture and handicrafts.

  • Saariselka: A popular ski resort and outdoor destination, offering hiking, skiing, and northern lights viewing.

  • Rovaniemi: The capital of Finnish Lapland, home to Santa Claus Village, Arktikum Museum, and Korundi House of Culture.

Conclusion

Lemmenjoki National Park is a gem of Finnish Lapland, offering stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a wide range of activities and experiences for visitors. Whether you’re a hiker, a history buff, or a nature lover, you’re sure to find something to enjoy and appreciate in this amazing national park. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most of your visit.
