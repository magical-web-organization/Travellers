Introduction to Simo

Simo is a quaint municipality located in the Finnish Lapland region, in the northern part of Finland. It is known for its untouched natural beauty, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant local culture. The region attracts adventure seekers all year round, who come here to witness the famous Northern Lights, explore vast lakes, dense forests, and indulge in outdoor activities like hiking, biking, skiing, and snowmobiling. This comprehensive Simo travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, stay, and eat in the region and provide essential tips for planning a perfect holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Simo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Explore Simo’s rich culture and history at the many museums and historical sites that dot the region. Visit the Pelkosenniemi Museum, housed in a 19th-century log cabin boasting traditional Finnish architecture. Learn about the region’s logging and reindeer herding traditions at the Lapland Agriculture Museum. Additionally, the Sairaalanniemi medical and pharmacy museum is a quirky hidden gem, showcasing the history of healthcare in the region and offering a fascinating glimpse into old medicinal practices.

Natural Attractions of Simo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Simo is a paradise for nature lovers. The pristine wilderness, crystal clear lakes, and stunning landscapes are perfect for outdoor activities. Take a hike in the Pyhä-Luosto National Park and explore the lush forests, rolling hills, and sparkling waterfalls. Or visit Lapland’s largest lake, Inari, where you can take a boat or a canoe and enjoy fishing and camping. Additionally, visit the Simojoki river, one of the best salmon fishing spots in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simo

Simo offers a wide range of trails and routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Talaskankaan Nature Trail is a popular hiking trail that takes you through beautiful forests and gentle hills. For experienced hikers, the Pirunkirkonpolku Trail offers a more challenging terrain, showcasing stunning mountain views. Similarly, Simo also has a network of cycle routes that take you through gorgeous countryside views and picturesque villages. Opt for a guided tour, and you can also learn about the region’s local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Simo: Activities and Tours

There is never a shortage of things to do in Simo. Take a snowmobile tour through the snow-covered forests and witness the northern lights, or try your hand at ice fishing on the frozen lakes. Dog sledding is also a popular activity in the region and provides a unique way to explore the natural beauty of Simo. You can also experience traditional Finnish sauna culture, where locals use a wood-burning sauna, followed by a dip in ice-cold water.

Food and Drink Scene of Simo

Must-Try Dishes

Simo is known for its traditional Finnish cuisine, with many unique dishes that reflect the local lifestyle. Try the mustikkapiirakka, a traditional blueberry pie made with fresh berries sourced from the local forest. The reindeer stew, or poronkäristys, is another classic Finnish dish, made with reindeer meat and served with mashed potatoes. Lastly, the karjalanpiirakka, a savory pastry made with rye flour and filled with rice porridge, is a must-try dish in Simo.

Local Beverages

In Simo, you can indulge in traditional Finnish drinks like cloudberry wine, made from local cloudberry berries, or the famous Finnish beer – Lapin Kulta. For a more unique experience as a coffee lover, you can try the traditional Finnish coffee brewed over an open fire, also known as kahvipannu.

Where to Stay in Simo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Simo provides a range of affordable accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Opt to stay at the Nuorgamin Lomakeskus, a lakeside resort that offers a sauna, spa, and onsite restaurant. Alternatively, if you are looking for a more budget-friendly option, the Hotel Pyhäjärvi and the Rovaniemi Camping are excellent choices.

What to Buy in Simo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Simo is famous for its unique handicrafts made by local artisans. The traditional Finnish knives, or puukko, are a great souvenir to bring back home. You can also buy authentic Sami handicrafts, such as reindeer skin products, traditional Lappish hats, and jewelry made from natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Simo

Simo has a vibrant local culture that is deeply ingrained in Finnish history and traditions. The territory of Lapland is dominated by the indigenous Sami people, who have their unique culture and customs and over the years have blended into Finnish culture. In Simo, you will find many indigenous arts, crafts, festivals, and rituals that showcase the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Simo

Simo’s high season spans from December to February, where you can witness the breathtaking northern lights. Summer is a great time to explore the Simojoki river, where the salmon start to run. Spring and autumn are excellent times to experience the countryside and take part in local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simo

Do:

  • Respect local culture, customs, and traditions.
  • Take care of nature, don’t litter and clean up properly.
  • Carry suitable clothing, including warm jackets, hats, and gloves.
  • Try the local food and drinks, including traditional dishes and Finnish beers.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or interfere with wildlife.
  • Make loud noises or violate quiet hours in residential areas.
  • Disrupt the natural environment or harm any animals.
  • Ignore safety precautions while trying adventure sports and activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Simo

Simo is generally a safe and secure region, and tourists are unlikely to face any specific risks. Still, it’s best to take precautions like carrying a map, being aware of your surroundings, and following instructions if taking a guided tour. Additionally, make sure to carry adequate travel insurance before embarking on your trip.

Health in Simo

The region’s healthcare facilities are of high standard, and there are clinics and hospitals throughout Simo to cater to any medical emergencies. Make sure to carry any necessary medication and familiarize yourself with the emergency numbers before starting your trip.

Getting to Simo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Simo

The nearest international airport to Simo is the Rovaniemi airport, located about 110 km away from Simo, and easily accessible by bus or taxi. Alternatively, you can also fly to the Ivalo airport, located about 170 km away, which has good connectivity with major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Simo

Simo is well connected with the rest of northern Finland by buses. Kemijärvi, located about 150 km away, is the nearest major town, and there are several daily bus services from Kemijärvi to Simo. Additionally, there are direct bus services to Rovaniemi, Oulu, and Tornio.

Other Options

Taxis are available in Simo, but are relatively expensive compared to other public transport options. Car hire is another option for travelers who want to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Simo

Public buses are an affordable and convenient option to get around Simo, especially to explore the national parks and major attractions. Additionally, taxis, rental cars, and bikes are available for hire. For a more unique experience, you can also try dog sledding or snowmobiling tours, which offer the chance to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Simo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kemi

Kemi is a small seaside town located on the Gulf of Bothnia, famous for the impressive Sampo icebreaker ship, which offers an unforgettable floating experience in the ice. You can also visit the world’s largest snow castle, located in Kemi.

Rovaniemi

The vibrant city of Rovaniemi is a short distance away from Simo, and is the gateway to Santa Claus Village, a famous Christmas destination all year round. Additionally, you can visit the Korundi Art Museum, the Arktikum museum, or take part in an aurora borealis safari.

Conclusion

Simo is a hidden gem in Lapland, a region full of natural beauty, vibrant culture, and unique experiences. This Simo travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to the region, taking you through the best places to visit, stay, eat, and explore, as well as providing essential tips for a smooth and comfortable journey. No matter when and how you plan your trip, Simo is sure to leave a lasting impression on you.
