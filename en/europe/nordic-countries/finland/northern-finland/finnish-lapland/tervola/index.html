Introduction to Tervola

Located in Finnish Lapland, Tervola is a charming destination that offers a mix of cultural attractions and natural wonders. Surrounded by beautiful forests and lakes, visitors can hike, bike, and explore the outdoors. Tervola is also home to several museums and historical sites that showcase its rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Tervola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tervola Local Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Tervola with exhibits of traditional crafts, local stories, and an old schoolhouse.
  • Mielakka Ski resort: Known for its excellent ski slopes, Mielakka Ski resort also offers summer activities such as hiking, biking, and canoeing.
  • The Tervola Church: This wooden church was built in the 18th century and is an excellent example of Finnish architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tervola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Ounasjärvi: This large lake is perfect for fishing, kayaking, or simply enjoying the stunning views from the shore.
  • Ostosvaara Hill: Climb this hill for breathtaking panoramic views of Tervola and the surrounding areas.
  • Autti Forest Reserve: This nature reserve is home to unique plant and animal species and offers hiking and birdwatching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tervola

  • Autti Nature Trail: A short yet beautiful hiking trail through the forest and past a small pond.
  • Tervola-Kivitaipale Walking Route: This 22 km trail leads from Tervola to Kivitaipale and passes through beautiful forests and countryside landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Tervola: Activities and Tours

  • Snowmobile Safari: In the winter months, explore the snowy landscapes surrounding Tervola on a snowmobile tour.
  • Aurora Hunting: Tervola is an excellent location for viewing the Northern lights or Aurora borealis, especially in the winter months.
  • Huskies Safari: Take a husky sled ride through the icy landscapes of Tervola.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tervola

day 1-2: Visit Tervola Local Museum, explore the Autti Forest Reserve, and take a short hike on the Autti Nature Trail.
day 3: Go skiing or snowboarding at Mielakka Ski Resort.
day 4: Visit the Tervola Church and climb the Ostosvaara Hill for panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
day 5: Go on a husky safari ride.
day 6-7: Enjoy Aurora hunting or go on a snowmobile safari.

Food and Drink Scene of Tervola

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat: A traditional Finnish delicacy, reindeer meat is often served in stews, roasted, or smoked.
  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs that are often served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam.

Local Beverages

  • Glögi: A hot spiced drink, similar to mulled wine, commonly served during the Christmas season.
  • Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka made from barley and rye that is popular in Tervola.

Where to Stay in Tervola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auttinivaara Apartment: A cozy and scenic apartment located near the Autti Forest Reserve.
  • Martti Cabin: A traditional log cabin that offers privacy and beautiful views.
  • Tervolan Rantamökki: A lakeside cottage with a private beach and sauna.

What to Buy in Tervola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sápmi Crafts: Shop for traditional Sámi handicrafts such as jewelry, clothing, and woodcarvings.
  • Lappish Cheese: A traditional cheese made from reindeer milk that makes a unique and tasty souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tervola

In Tervola, it is customary to remove your shoes before entering a private home. Visitors are expected to be respectful of Finnish culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Tervola

The best time to visit Tervola is during the winter months (November to March) when the Northern lights are visible and the snowy landscapes provide excellent opportunities for winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts in Tervola

Do:

  • Dress warmly in the winter months.
  • Respect Finnish culture and traditions.
  • Try traditional Finnish food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Waste resources or litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tervola

Tervola is a safe destination with little crime and a low risk of natural disasters. Visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities and be aware of the risks associated with winter weather conditions.

Health in Tervola

Finland has a modern healthcare system with high standards of care. It is recommended to purchase travel insurance before visiting Tervola.

Getting to Tervola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tervola

  • Rovaniemi Airport (RVN) - 60 km from Tervola
  • Kemi-Tornio Airport (KEM) - 90 km from Tervola

Bus Stations Near Tervola

  • Tervola Bus Station

Other Options

  • The easiest way to get around Tervola is by renting a car or hiring a taxi.

How to Get Around in Tervola

The best way to explore Tervola is by hiking, cycling, or driving. There are also local buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Tervola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rovaniemi: The “official” hometown of Santa Claus and a popular destination for winter activities.
  • Kemi: Known for its snow castle and icebreaker cruises.
  • Oulu: A lively university town on the coast of Finland.

Conclusion

Tervola is a beautiful and unique destination in Finnish Lapland that offers a mix of cultural attractions and natural wonders. Visitors can explore the outdoors, visit museums and historical sites, and enjoy traditional Finnish food and drink. With its stunning landscapes and friendly locals, Tervola is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Finland.
