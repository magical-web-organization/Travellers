Introduction to Inari

Located in Finnish Lapland, Inari is a hidden gem in Northern Finland. Known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich Sami culture, and wide range of outdoor activities, Inari is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Finnish Lapland.

List of Places to Visit in Inari

Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, natural wonders, or outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Inari. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Siida - This popular museum offers insights into the unique culture, history, and way of life of the Sami people.
  • Sajos - The Sajos building serves as a center for Sami culture and politics, and is a great place to learn more about the Sami people and their traditions.
  • Pielpajärvi Wilderness Church - Located on the shore of Lake Pielpajärvi, this historic wooden church dates back to the 1700s and provides a glimpse into the religious practices of early Finnish settlers.

Natural Attractions of Inari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saariselkä Ski Resort - This popular ski resort offers a wide range of slopes and trails suitable for all levels of skiers and snowboarders.
  • Lemmenjoki National Park - Lemmenjoki is Finland’s largest national park, boasting stunning landscapes and endless opportunities for hiking, fishing, and wildlife spotting.
  • Lake Inari - This stunning lake is Finland’s third-largest and offers beautiful views as well as opportunities for water sports such as kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Inari

Inari is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with numerous trails and routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Some of the best routes in the area include:

  • Inari Hiking Area - This versatile hiking area offers a wide range of trails suitable for all skill levels, with scenic views of Lake Inari and the surrounding forests.
  • Kevo Trail - This challenging 63 km trail takes you through some of Lapland’s most spectacular wilderness areas, including the Kevo Canyon.
  • Ivalo River Trail - This popular trail follows the Ivalo River and offers stunning views of the river valley and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Inari: Activities and Tours

From dog-sledding to Northern Lights tours, Inari has no shortage of activities and tours to keep you busy during your visit. Here are some of the top things to do in Inari:

  • Husky safaris - Experience the thrill of dog-sledding through Lapland’s stunning wilderness areas.
  • Aurora borealis tours - Northern Finland is one of the best places in the world to see the Northern Lights, and there are numerous tour operators in Inari that can take you to the best viewing spots.
  • Reindeer safaris - Learn about the Sami people and their unique way of life while riding on a traditional reindeer sleigh.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inari

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Inari, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the area:

Food and Drink Scene of Inari

Inari and other parts of Lapland offer unique culinary experiences based on local ingredients and traditional cooking methods. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sautéed reindeer - A popular dish in Lapland, this hearty stew features tender reindeer meat, often served with lingonberries and mashed potatoes.
  • Rössypottu - A traditional Finnish stew made with potatoes, onions, and smoked pork.
  • Kalakukko - A Lappish pastry filled with fish and pork.

Local Beverages

  • Sisu - A Finnish energy drink that’s become a cultural icon in Finland.
  • Cloudberry liqueur - A sweet and tart liqueur made from the local cloudberry.

Where to Stay in Inari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options in Inari, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Tradition Hotel Kultahovi - A cozy hotel located in the heart of Inari, with comfortable rooms and a great restaurant.
  • Inarin Poropirtit - A traditional Sami-style accommodation that offers cozy cabins and a unique cultural experience.
  • Hotel Inari - Located next to Lake Inari, this modern hotel offers stunning views and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Inari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Inari, there are several local products that make great souvenirs or gifts. Some popular options include:

  • Sami handicrafts and jewelry - Find unique handmade items made by local Sami artists.
  • Cloudberry products - Cloudberry jam, juice, and other products are a popular souvenir among visitors to Lapland.
  • Reindeer pelts - Take home a high-quality reindeer pelt as a reminder of your trip to Lapland.

Local Culture and Customs of Inari

The Sami people have lived in Lapland for centuries and have a unique culture and way of life. Visitors to Inari can learn more about Sami customs and traditions by visiting museums and cultural centers in the area, experiencing traditional Sami food and drink, and participating in activities such as reindeer safaris.

Best Time to Visit Inari

The best time to visit Inari depends on your interests and what you want to do while you’re there. Winter is a popular season for visitors who want to experience winter sports and see the Northern Lights, while summer offers great hiking and canoeing opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inari

When visiting Inari, there are some important cultural norms to be aware of. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect Sami culture and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a public building.
  • Dress warmly and appropriately for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of Sami people or their belongings without permission.
  • Ignore the signs in nature conservation areas.
  • Speak loudly in public places.

Safety and Health

Inari is generally a safe destination, although visitors should take necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and skiing. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with cold weather and should dress appropriately.

Safety in Inari

  • Be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities and take necessary precautions.
  • Follow hunting and fishing regulations in national parks and other protected areas.
  • Drive safely and obey traffic laws.

Health in Inari

  • Tap water is safe to drink.
  • Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations.
  • Be aware of the risks of hypothermia and frostbite during cold weather.

Getting to Inari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Inari is easily accessible by plane, train, and bus. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around in the area.

Airports Near Inari

  • Ivalo Airport - Located just 40 km away from Inari, Ivalo Airport is the closest airport to the area.

Bus Stations Near Inari

  • Inari Bus Station - Located in the center of Inari, this station is served by regular bus routes from other parts of Lapland.

Other Options

  • Train - The closest train station is located in Rovaniemi, which is approximately 300 km away from Inari.
  • Car - Visitors can rent a car from major cities in Finland and drive to Inari.

How to Get Around in Inari

Inari is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors who want to venture outside of the town center can rent a bike or car, or take a bus or taxi to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Inari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the area around Inari, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Rovaniemi - The capital of Lapland, Rovaniemi is a popular destination for visitors looking to experience the best of Finnish Lapland.
  • Norway - Inari is located near the border with Norway, and visitors can easily cross over to experience the stunning natural landscapes and unique cultural attractions of Norway.

Conclusion

Inari is a unique and fascinating destination that offers a one-of-a-kind travel experience for visitors to Finnish Lapland. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or beautiful scenery, Inari has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the best of Lapland in Inari.
