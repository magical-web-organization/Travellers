Introduction to Ranua

Ranua is a small town located in Finnish Lapland, in northern Finland. Known for its picturesque landscapes and rich wildlife, it’s a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventurer, or a history buff, Ranua has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ranua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ranua Museum: Located in the heart of the town, the Ranua Museum is a must-visit attraction that showcases the history and culture of the area.
  • Rovaniemi Art Museum: Although not technically located in Ranua, the Rovaniemi Art Museum is only an hour’s drive away and offers a glimpse into contemporary Finnish art.
  • Rovaniemi Church: Built in the early 1950s, the Rovaniemi Church is a gorgeous example of modern Finnish architecture.

Natural Attractions of Ranua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ranua Wildlife Park: Home to more than 50 species of animals, the Ranua Wildlife Park is a unique experience that lets you get up close and personal with Arctic wildlife.
  • Ounasvaara Mountain: Known for its breathtaking views and hiking trails, Ounasvaara Mountain is perfect for a day trip or an afternoon hike.
  • Auttiköngäs Waterfall: Located about an hour’s drive from Ranua, the Auttiköngäs Waterfall is a stunning example of Finnish nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ranua

  • Ranua Nature Trail: A 6-kilometer trail that winds through the Finnish countryside and showcases the local flora and fauna.
  • Ounasvaara Mountain Trail: A 10-kilometer hiking trail that leads up to the top of Ounasvaara Mountain and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Iso-Syöte Hiking Trail: A 16-kilometer trail that passes through forests and hills and offers a challenging but rewarding hike.

Best Things to Do in Ranua: Activities and Tours

  • Aurora Borealis Tour: The Northern Lights are a must-see phenomenon that can be observed from Ranua during the winter months.
  • Snowmobile Safari: The perfect activity for adrenaline junkies, a snowmobile safari is a thrilling way to explore the Finnish countryside.
  • Ice Fishing Tour: An authentic experience that lets you experience the Finnish way of life, ice fishing is a relaxing and unique way to spend a winter day.

Food and Drink Scene of Ranua

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat: A staple of Finnish cuisine, reindeer meat can be found in various forms such as sausages, stews, and roasted fillets.
  • Muikku: A small freshwater fish that’s often battered and fried, muikku is a traditional Finnish dish that’s served in restaurants throughout Ranua.
  • Karjalanpiirakka: A thin-crust pastry filled with rice porridge or mashed potatoes, Karjalanpiirakka is a Finnish delicacy that’s popular for breakfast or as a snack.

Local Beverages

  • Koskenkorva: A vodka made from locally grown barley, Koskenkorva is a popular Finnish drink that’s enjoyed on special occasions.
  • Glögi: A hot mulled wine that’s often served during the winter months, Glögi is a festive drink that’s perfect for warming up after a day in the snow.
  • Lonkero: A Finnish long drink made with grapefruit soda and gin, Lonkero is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for a night out in town.

Where to Stay in Ranua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Ilveslinna: A cozy hotel located in the heart of Ranua, Hotel Ilveslinna offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.
  • Lapland Hotel Bear’s Lodge: A stunning hotel located in the middle of the Finnish wilderness, Lapland Hotel Bear’s Lodge is perfect for those who want to get away from it all.
  • Penthouse Polar: A small guesthouse located in Ranua, Penthouse Polar offers cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ranua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Reindeer Pelts: A popular souvenir that’s warm and soft, reindeer pelts can be found in various sizes and colors throughout Ranua.
  • Lappish Knives: Known for their craftsmanship and quality, Lappish knives are a unique and practical souvenir that’s perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Sámi Bracelets: Made by the indigenous Sámi people, Sámi bracelets are a colorful and beautiful souvenir that showcases the local culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Ranua

Ranua is home to a rich and unique culture that’s influenced by its location in Finnish Lapland. The local Sámi people have a strong presence in the area, and visitors can learn about their customs and traditions at the Ranua Museum.

Best Time to Visit Ranua

The best time to visit Ranua depends on what you want to do and see. The winter months, from December to February, are ideal for outdoor activities such as skiing, snowmobiling, and viewing the Northern Lights. The summer months, from June to August, are perfect for hiking and exploring the Finnish countryside.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ranua

Do:

  • Dress warmly during the winter months.
  • Respect the local wildlife and nature.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the local wildlife or their habitat.
  • Leave trash or litter in the Finnish wilderness.
  • Be excessively loud or disrespectful in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ranua

Ranua is a safe town with very low crime rates, but visitors should still take precautions such as locking doors and windows and keeping an eye on their belongings in public places.

Health in Ranua

Visitors to Ranua should be aware of the risks of hypothermia and frostbite during the winter months. It’s important to dress warmly and stay indoors if the temperature drops too low.

Getting to Ranua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ranua

The nearest airport to Ranua is Rovaniemi Airport, which is located about an hour’s drive away. Helsinki Airport is also a popular option, with several daily flights to Rovaniemi.

Bus Stations Near Ranua

Ranua has a small bus station that connects to nearby towns and cities such as Rovaniemi and Oulu.

Other Options

Visitors to Ranua can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Ranua

Ranua is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. For longer journeys, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Ranua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rovaniemi: A larger town located about an hour’s drive from Ranua, Rovaniemi is known for its modern architecture and cultural attractions.
  • Pyhä-Luosto National Park: A stunning national park located about two hours’ drive from Ranua, Pyhä-Luosto offers breathtaking landscapes and hiking trails.
  • Kemi: A coastal town located about three hours’ drive from Ranua, Kemi is home to the famous SnowCastle and seaside views.

Conclusion

Ranua is a hidden gem tucked away in Finnish Lapland, offering stunning natural landscapes, unique cultural experiences, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a winter adventure or a summer getaway, Ranua is the perfect destination for your next trip. Plan your visit now and discover all that this charming town has to offer!
