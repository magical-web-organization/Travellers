Introduction to Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park is situated in Finnish Lapland in Northern Finland, covering an area of over 1000 square kilometers. The park offers a diverse range of natural landscapes, including fells, forests, rivers, and lakes, making it a popular destination for both summer and winter activities. This guide will help you discover the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Samiland exhibition: Explore the history, culture, and traditions of the Sami people, the indigenous people of Lapland, at this exhibition in Ylläs.
  • Jerisjärvi Lake: Take a trip to Jerisjärvi Lake, where you can witness a traditional Finnish smoke sauna and enjoy the views of northern lights in the winter.

Natural Attractions of Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pallastunturi Fell: Enjoy a hike to the highest point of the park, Pallastunturi Fell, and witness spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hetta-Pallas Trail: This 55-kilometer trail offers breathtaking views of the park’s open fells, rugged forests, marshes, and crystal-clear waters.
  • Kesänki Fell: For a more challenging hike, try Kesänki Fell, which boasts amazing views of the park and is a perfect spot for camping.
  • Lainio Snow Village: Experience a unique winter wonderland in the world-renowned Lainio Snow Village, featuring ice sculptures and an ice hotel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park has a range of hiking and biking routes that cater to different skill levels and preferences. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Hetta-Pallas Trail: This famous trail takes you through varying landscapes of the national park, and is a must for hikers and bikers alike.
  • Pallastunturi Nature Trail: This 3-kilometer trail is an easy walk that is suitable for most visitors.
  • Kesänki Fell Trail: A moderate trail that offers spectacular views of the national park and is a great place for camping adventures.

Best Things to Do in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Northern Lights Hunting: During winter, take a guided tour to witness northern lights dancing in the sky.
  • Husky Safaris: Experience the thrill of dog sledding through the stunning wilderness of the national park.
  • Skiing: Pallas-Yllästunturi is home to fantastic ski resorts, which offer downhill and cross-country skiing options for all skill levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Day 1: Visit the Samiland exhibition in Ylläs Day 2: Hike Pallastunturi Fell and enjoy the views of the surrounding landscape. Day 3: Explore the Hetta-Pallas Trail, which offers breathtaking views of the park’s open fells, rugged forests, marshes, and crystal-clear waters. Day 4: Enjoy downhill and cross-country skiing at the resort of Ylläs. Day 5: Take a guided tour to witness northern lights dancing in the sky. Day 6: Go dog sledding through the stunning wilderness of the national park. Day 7: Visit Lainio Snow Village, featuring ice sculptures and an ice hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpaisti: A traditional Finnish stew made with beef and root vegetables.
  • Riisipuuro: A delicious Finnish rice porridge, often served with cinnamon and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made with rye and flavored with juniper.
  • Glögi: A spiced mulled wine - perfect for warming up in chilly winters.

Where to Stay in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous options for accommodation in and around the national park, ranging from luxurious hotels to rustic guesthouses and hostels. Some popular options include:

  • Lapland Hotels Pallas: This hotel is located on the western side of the Pallastunturi Fell and offers spectacular views of the surrounding area.
  • Tunturioravat Cottages: Located near the visitor center, these cottages are perfect for those who prefer a more rustic, authentic experience.
  • Ylläksen Yöpuu: This charming guesthouse is located in the picturesque fishing village of Äkäslompolo.

What to Buy in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sauna products: Finnish sauna culture is an integral part of local life, so consider purchasing some traditional sauna products such as birch whisks, sauna scents, and towels.
  • Local crafts: There are several shops in the area that sell handmade traditional Sami crafts, clothing, and jewelry.
  • Finnish Salmiakki: Finland’s most popular candy, Salmiakki is a salty liquorice treat that you can find in most local shops in Lapland.

Local Culture and Customs of Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

The Sami people have lived in this area for over 9,000 years, and their culture is still an integral part of life in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park today. Visitors can learn about the Sami way of life at the Samiland exhibition in Ylläs. Traditional Sami cuisine, such as reindeer meat and fish caught in the local rivers and lakes, is a staple of the local diet.

Best Time to Visit Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

The national park is open year-round, and each season offers its unique experiences. Summer is ideal for hiking and biking, while winter offers skiing, snowmobiling, and husky safaris. Visitors can witness the northern lights, locally known as the Aurora Borealis, from September to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Follow the marked trails and respect the environment.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and gear for the activity you will be doing.

Don’t:

  • Remove any natural objects from the park.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

  • Always inform someone of your planned route and expected return time.
  • Take great care when hiking or biking in areas of loose rocks or slick surfaces.
  • Ensure you have adequate equipment for your activity before beginning.

Health in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

  • Ensure you are up to date with your vaccinations.
  • Apply insect repellent, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and gear for the weather conditions.

Getting to Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

  • Kittilä Airport
  • Enontekiö Airport

Bus Stations Near Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

  • Kolari Bus Station
  • Muonio Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station is located in Kolari.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available from most nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park

Car rental is the easiest way to explore the park. Alternatively, there are bus and taxi services available for transportation within the area. Walking and cycling are also great options for those who prefer to take it slow and enjoy the scenery.

Where to Go Next from Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Levi: This popular ski resort is just over an hour away from Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park, offering more ski slopes, restaurants, and shopping.
  • Rovaniemi: The capital of Lapland is two and a half hours from the park and is home to the Santa Claus Village, where you can visit Santa and even cross the Arctic Circle.
  • Kilpisjärvi: Continue your exploration of Finnish Lapland by crossing the nearby border into Norway to visit the stunning Kilpisjärvi area.

Conclusion

Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park offers visitors a unique and memorable experience in one of the most untouched regions of Lapland. From breathtaking natural landscapes to traditional Sami culture, skiing, hiking, biking, and dog sledding, there’s something for everyone all year round. We hope our comprehensive guide has helped you plan your visit to this incredible destination. Enjoy your time in Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park!
