A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Kolari, Finland

Are you planning a trip to the enchanting land of Lapland in Finland and seeking a thrilling adventure amidst the rugged wilderness? Look no further than Kolari – a small municipality in Northern Finland that promises to leave you spellbound with its natural beauty, rich culture and outdoor adventures.

Let this comprehensive travel guide to Kolari take you on an excursion through some of the most compelling attractions in the region, provide helpful information on accommodation, transportation, local culture, safety, and health, and help you plan your perfect holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Kolari

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kolari is home to a plethora of museums and historic places that offer a fascinating glimpse into the region’s cultural heritage.

  • The Lappean Loma Nature and Culture Centre: This museum and nature centre showcases an impressive collection of Sami handicrafts, and provides guided tours to help visitors learn more about Lapland’s unique history and nature.
  • The Jäkälä Museum: Located in the beautiful village of Äkäslompolo, this museum features exhibits on the local logging industry and rural life in the 19th century.
  • The Tornionlaakso Museum: This museum is situated in an old Lutheran church in Pahkakumpu and houses exhibits on the history and culture of this remote region.

Natural Attractions of Kolari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From national parks to ski resorts, Kolari has plenty to offer those seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views.

  • Ylläs Ski Resort: With over 60 marked trails, Ylläs Ski Resort is one of the largest ski resorts in Finland. In summer, you can hike or bike around the beautiful slopes.
  • Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park: This UNESCO-listed national park promises unforgettable wilderness adventures, including hiking, kayaking, fishing, and wildlife watching.
  • Kukastunturi Hill: This rocky hill offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kolari

Exploring Kolari’s natural beauty is best done on foot or by bike, and there are plenty of routes to choose from.

  • Könkään kierros Trail: This 5.5 km trail is an easy walk through the beautiful pine forests, over streams and through wetlands. It is located near the village of Kaukonen.
  • Lainio Snow Village Trail: This 7 km trail runs through a unique art installation, Lainio Snow Village, and takes visitors through elaborate ice sculpted figures and buildings.
  • Ylläs Fell Trail: This 14 km trail passes through untamed forests and rugged mountain terrain taking hikers to the top of Ylläs Fell, one of Finland’s most beautiful national park destinations.

Best Things to Do in Kolari: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for unique experiences in Kolari, the town offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different tastes and interests.

  • Reindeer Safaris: Explore the wilderness of Lapland on a reindeer-drawn sleigh, and learn about the fascinating Sami culture.
  • Northern Lights Tours: Indulge in Kolari’s winter magic under the dazzling Aurora Borealis, one of nature’s most mesmerizing spectacles.
  • Dog Sledding Trips: A quintessential Lapland adventure, dog-sledding is a thrilling way to explore the snow-covered landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kolari

Kolari’s alluring natural beauty and historic landmarks are worth exploring throughout the year, whether you’re a nature lover, adventurer or a culture buff. Here’s a seven-day itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day 1 Arrival in Kolari and check-in at hotel
Day 2 and 3 Explore Ylläs National Park, enjoy nature trails, and take part in activities such as skiing, snowshoeing, and hiking
Day 4 & 5 Learn about Sami culture and visit museums in Kolari
Day 6 Go on a reindeer safari or a husky dog sledding ride around Ylläs Fell
Day 7 Relax and explore local food and souvenir markets in the town

Food and Drink Scene of Kolari

Kolari’s rich culinary heritage offers a range of unique and delicious dishes and beverages worth exploring.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpaisti: This traditional Finnish dish is made with beef or pork and is slow-cooked in a stew with root vegetables.
  • Rössypottu: This hearty, traditional soup is made with simple ingredients such as potatoes, barley, peas and onions.
  • Leipäjuusto: A fresh cheese made of cow milk, leipäjuusto is a popular snack for cold Finnish evenings – typically served hot with cloudberry jam.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: Traditional, low-alcohol beer made with rye malt and juniper berries.
  • Sima: A sweet, lightly alcoholic drink made from sugar, water, and lemon juice.
  • Cloudberry liqueur: A sweet liquor made from a unique Nordic berry that grows in the marshy soils of the region.

Where to Stay in Kolari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kolari has a great selection of accommodation options to choose from - ranging from luxurious cabins to budget-friendly hostels.

  • Ylläshumina: A cosy hotel located in the village centre of Ylläsjärvi.
  • Lappalaisen Luontoelämys: A unique guesthouse that offers guests a chance to sleep in a traditional Sami teepee.
  • Toivoniemi Apartment-Hotel: An affordable and comfortable apartment hotel in the heart of Kolari.

What to Buy in Kolari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kolari has plenty of unique souvenirs to offer, from Finnish handicrafts and traditional Sami items to local food and drink products. Here are some of the most popular souvenirs to bring home:

  • Puukko knife: The Finnish traditional utility knife is an iconic souvenir featuring a wooden handle and carbon steel blade.
  • Lapland hats: Made from reindeer skin, these hats keep you warm and make you look stylish.
  • Cloudberry jam: A popular souvenir, cloudberries create a sweet and distinct jam, perfect to spread on toast or cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Kolari

Kolari has a rich cultural heritage, deeply rooted in Sami culture. Here are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting Kolari:

  • Respect Sami culture and traditions by seeking permission before entering private Sami land.
  • Do not disturb the local wildlife by littering or making loud noises.
  • Speaking Finnish is the primary language in the region, so learning some basic Finnish phrases can go a long way.

Best Time to Visit Kolari

Kolari is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on the kind of activities and adventures you are interested in.

  • Winter (December – March): Ideal for winter sports, Northern Lights viewing, and other unique Lapland activities.
  • Summer (June – August): Perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring the beautiful national parks.
  • Autumn (September – November): The best season to see the beautiful autumn foliage with fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kolari

Do:

  • Visit the national parks and other natural attractions.
  • Learn about Sami culture and unique traditions.
  • Try local Finnish and Sami cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Ignore Sami cultural customs or disrespect their land.
  • Forget to dress in warm, waterproof layers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kolari

Kolari is a safe destination, but it’s essential to be aware of the risks associated with Lapland’s rugged wilderness, such as hypothermia, falls, and wild animals. Make sure to dress appropriately, carry essential gear such as a first aid kit, and follow safety precautions while engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Kolari

Finland has an efficient healthcare system. Still, it’s essential to ensure that health insurance policies cover any incidents or illnesses that might occur during your trip.

Getting to Kolari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travelling to Kolari is convenient and accessible, thanks to its excellent transport connections.

Airports Near Kolari

  • The nearest airport is Kittilä Airport, which is around 35 km away from Kolari.
  • Rovaniemi airport is another option, which is located about 200 km away from Kolari.

Bus Stations Near Kolari

  • Kolari bus station is located in the town centre and is well-connected to other cities and towns in Finland.

Other Options

  • Trains: Kolari is well-connected by trains, which makes it an easy destination to reach from Helsinki and other Finnish coastal cities.
  • Rental cars: Another popular option for travelling to Kolari is renting a car – this offers visitors the flexibility to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Kolari

There are plenty of transportation options available in Kolari that allow visitors to explore the region easily.

  • Cars: Rental cars are available in the region, allowing visitors to explore the surrounding villages and countryside.
  • Buses: Local buses connect Ylläs National Park and other villages in the region.
  • Taxis: Taxis are an option for visitors who prefer more convenient and private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kolari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kolari is located in the beautiful region of Lapland, and there are plenty of nearby destinations that promise visitors even more awe-inspiring natural beauty, culture, and adventure. Some popular destinations include:

  • Rovaniemi: Known as the official home of Santa Claus, this beautiful city is located just a few hours from Kolari by train or car.
  • Levi: A hub for winter sports enthusiasts, Levi is a thriving village that boasts top-notch ski slopes and picturesque countryside.
  • Muonio: A small municipality located on the shores of Lake Ounasjärvi, Muonio is famous for its unspoilt natural beauty and scenic hiking routes.

Conclusion

Kolari is a small but charming town with a rich cultural heritage, rugged landscapes, and plenty of opportunities for adventure, exploration, and relaxation. This comprehensive travel guide provides an all-encompassing overview of the best places to visit, things to do, local food and drink, accommodation, transportation, and safety tips to help you plan your perfect trip to Kolari.
