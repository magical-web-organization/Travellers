Introduction to Utsjoki

Utsjoki is a municipality located in the northernmost part of Finland’s Lapland region. It is a place of unparalleled natural beauty, breathtaking landscapes, and rich indigenous culture. Utsjoki’s location near the Norwegian and Finnish borders provides visitors with an opportunity to experience the unique culture and traditions of the Sami people in Lapland.

This guide is designed to help you discover the best of Utsjoki, including popular attractions, local cuisine and customs, outdoor adventures, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Utsjoki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Siida Museum: This museum in nearby Inari is an excellent place to learn about the history and traditions of the Sami people in Lapland. It features exhibits on reindeer husbandry, handicrafts, traditional clothing, and Sami spirituality.
  • Skolt Sámi Heritage House: This cultural museum is dedicated to the Skolt Sami, a subgroup of the Sami people. It showcases the history, customs, and traditions of this indigenous group through exhibitions, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Olmakka Gallery: This contemporary art space offers visitors a chance to experience a mix of local and international art exhibitions in the heart of Utsjoki.

Natural Attractions of Utsjoki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kevo Strict Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is home to an array of flora and fauna, including rare species like the Arctic fox and brown bear. Visitors can go hiking and birdwatching on the nature trails and experience the natural beauty of Lapland.
  • The Ravadas Falls: This is one of the most popular waterfalls in Lapland, located on the Teno River. Visitors can hike to the falls to enjoy the stunning scenery and take a dip in the refreshing water.
  • Santa’s Road: This scenic route is a great way to explore the scenic views of the Utsjoki region, including forests, rivers, and fjords. It is particularly popular during winter when snow blankets the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Utsjoki

  • The Kaldoaivi Wilderness Trail: This is a 180-kilometer-long trail that is ideal for experienced hikers and backpackers. It offers a unique opportunity to experience the remote wilderness of Lapland, with challenging terrain, rugged landscapes, and stunning scenery.
  • Kevon Luonto-Park Path: This is a 15-kilometer-long hiking trail that passes through the pristine wilderness of Kevo Strict Nature Reserve. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the Kevo Canyon and observe the diverse wildlife and plant species that call this area home.

Best Things to Do in Utsjoki: Activities and Tours

  • Northern Lights Hunting: Utsjoki is a prime location to witness the magical Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis). Visitors can book guided tours to go on a Northern Lights hunt, or venture out on their own to find the best places to view the lights.
  • Reindeer Sledding: Reindeer are a central part of Sami culture, and visitors can experience this tradition by booking a reindeer sled ride. The gentle animals take visitors on a magical ride through the snowy forests and offer a unique perspective on the Lapland landscape.
  • Fishing: The Teno River is famous for its salmon fishing, and visitors can book guided fishing trips or try their luck on their own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Utsjoki

Food and Drink Scene of Utsjoki

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat: Reindeer meat is a local specialty and can be found in most restaurants in Utsjoki. It is often served as a reindeer steak or in a stew with potatoes and vegetables.
  • Arctic Char: This freshwater fish is a popular dish in Lapland and is often prepared smoked, grilled, or fried.
  • Cloudberry Jam: Cloudberry jam is a sweet and tangy spread that is made from the berries that grow wild in the Lapland region. It is often served as a side dish with breakfast or dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Sámi Dáhpáh: This is a traditional Sami drink that is used in celebrations and ceremonies. It is made from reindeer milk, herbs, and juice, and has a sweet and creamy taste.
  • Lapin Kulta: This is a popular beer in Finland and is brewed locally in Lapland. It has a crisp and refreshing taste and pairs well with many local dishes.

Where to Stay in Utsjoki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Utsjoki Hotel: This is the only hotel in Utsjoki and offers simple and comfortable rooms with basic amenities. It is located in the town center and provides easy access to local attractions.
  • Kiekerönsalmi Camping: This camping site offers cabins, cottages, and campsites for visitors looking for a more rustic accommodation option. It is located near the Kevo Strict Nature Reserve and offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

What to Buy in Utsjoki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sami Handicrafts: Sami handicrafts are a great souvenir to bring home from Utsjoki. Visitors can find unique items like hand-knitted wool hats, leather bags, and traditional Sami knives.
  • Cloudberry Products: Cloudberry products like jam and liqueurs are a popular souvenir from Lapland. They are ideal for foodies and are a great way to bring a taste of Utsjoki home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Utsjoki

The Sami people have lived in the Lapland region for thousands of years and have preserved their unique culture and traditions. Visitors to Utsjoki can experience Sami culture by attending a traditional reindeer race, going on a Sami tour, or visiting local handicraft markets.

Best Time to Visit Utsjoki

The best time to visit Utsjoki depends on what you want to experience. Winter is ideal for Northern Lights hunting, snowmobiling, and reindeer sledding. Summer offers opportunities for hiking, fishing, and exploring the pristine Lapland wilderness.

Do’s and Don’ts of Utsjoki

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately for the weather
  • Take part in traditional Sami activities and experiences

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or environment
  • Ignore the Northern Lights safety guidelines
  • Cause excessive noise or disturbance in residential areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Utsjoki

Utsjoki is a safe destination with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions like locking doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Utsjoki

There are no major health risks in Utsjoki, but visitors should be prepared for the cold weather and bring appropriate clothing and gear.

Getting to Utsjoki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Utsjoki

The nearest airport is in Ivalo, which is approximately 200 kilometers from Utsjoki.

Bus Stations Near Utsjoki

There is a bus station in Utsjoki that provides regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Utsjoki by rental car or by organized tour.

How to Get Around in Utsjoki

The best way to explore Utsjoki is by rental car or by organized tour. It is also possible to use local buses, but they have limited schedules and routes.

Where to Go Next from Utsjoki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Inari: This nearby town is home to the Siida Museum and offers opportunities for fishing, hiking, and exploring the beautiful natural landscapes of Lapland.
  • Nordkapp: This is the northernmost point of mainland Europe and is a popular excursion from Utsjoki. Visitors can drive or take a bus to the site and enjoy the stunning views of the Arctic Ocean.
  • Karigasniemi: This village is located near the Norwegian border and offers a chance to experience the unique cultural blend of Sami and Finnish traditions.

Conclusion

Utsjoki is a hidden gem in Finnish Lapland, offering visitors a truly unique experience of the Lapland wilderness and Sami culture. From Northern Lights hunting to reindeer sledding to hiking in the pristine wilderness, Utsjoki has something to offer for every type of traveler. Use this guide to help you plan your Utsjoki journey and soak up the magic of Lapland.
