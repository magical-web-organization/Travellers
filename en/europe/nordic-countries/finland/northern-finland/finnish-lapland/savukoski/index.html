Introduction to Savukoski

Nestled in the heart of Finnish Lapland, Savukoski boasts breathtaking natural beauty, unique cultural experiences, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. From world-class hiking and biking routes to charming museums and historical landmarks, this small town has something to offer everyone.

Here, you’ll find pristine forests, crystal-clear lakes, and stunning panoramas of Finland’s northernmost wilderness. It’s an ideal location for winter sports enthusiasts, nature lovers, and anyone seeking a peaceful escape into the heart of nature.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit in Savukoski, the top things to do, must-try dishes, and where to stay. Whether you’re planning a day trip or an extended stay, this guide has everything you need to know about visiting Savukoski.

List of Places to Visit in Savukoski

Savukoski is filled with diverse attractions, ranging from historical landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of our top picks for places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Savukoski Church: Built in 1859, this wooden church is an excellent example of Lapland’s traditional architecture.
  2. Savukoski Museum: Learn about the town’s history at this intimate museum, which features exhibits on local forestry, natural resources, and indigenous Sami culture.
  3. Pirttikoski Rapids: A breathtaking landmark renowned for its fishing opportunities, Pirttikoski rapids are a beloved site for both locals and travelers.

Natural Attractions of Savukoski: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. UKK National Park: One of Finland’s largest national parks, UKK provides ample opportunity for hiking, biking, snowshoeing, and skiing.
  2. Korouoma Canyon: This dramatic canyon is a geologic wonder, featuring steep cliffs, stunning waterfalls, and winding trails that lead to overlooks with panoramic views of the park.
  3. The Pyhä-Luosto National Park: A hiking and skiing paradise, this park also boasts pristine forests and beautiful landscapes. Explore Sami culture on a visit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savukoski

Savukoski is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there’s no shortage of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of our favorites:

  1. Korouoma Canyon Trail: This 14-kilometer trail takes you through some of Korouoma Canyon’s most spectacular scenery, including stunning waterfalls and sweeping views of the area.
  2. Ylä-Luosto Trail: This 12-kilometer trail connects two cabins and offers excellent views of the Pyhä-Luosto National Park and a chance to explore Sami culture.
  3. Savukoski Wilderness Trail: At 50 kilometers long, this trail is a challenging hike through some of the region’s most rugged terrain. It offers wilderness experiences far from the madding crowd.

Best Things to Do in Savukoski: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Savukoski offers a range of other activities for visitors. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Reindeer Farm Visits: Visit a traditional Sami reindeer farm to learn about this unique culture and takes a reindeer ride.
  2. Canoeing: Paddle through some of the region’s crystal-clear lakes and waterways to capture stunning views and experience the pure nature.
  3. Snowmobiling: During winter, explore the Finnish Lapland by snowmobile to witness northern lights and other natural wonders.

Week-Long Itinerary for Savukoski

For visitors looking to spend a week in Savukoski, here’s a sample itinerary to optimize your time:

Day 1: Visit the Savukoski Museum and explore the town.

Day 2: Head to UKK National Park. Hike the 17km Tsarmitunturi fell route or take a shorter 5 km day trip in Urpovaara section.

Day 3: Kayak or canoe on Auvojärvi Lake or participate on a fishing trip to Pirttikoski rapids.

Day 4: Take the legendary Pyhä-Luosto Hiking trail, said to be used by Sami people for over 3,000 years.

Day 5: Snowmobile tour through the Lapland Wilderness. Travel to Urho Kekkonen National Park and take a nature hike.

Day 6: Visit the Korouoma Canyon and take a challenging hike to witness the stunning beauty.

Day 7: End the trip by visiting a traditional Sami reindeer farm, learn about their culture, and take a reindeer ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Savukoski

The culinary scene in Savukoski is characterized by an intense focus on local ingredients found in the surrounding wilderness, forests, and farms. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sauteed Reindeer: A Lapland staple, this dish involves cooking minced reindeer meat with onion, mushrooms, and spices. You can find it in most restaurants in Savukoski.
  2. Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish pastry, this meal contains fish inside a rye bread crust. It’s ideal for taking along on outdoor adventures.
  3. Roe venison: Venison caught in the Finnish forests and cooked by local chefs is a tasty treat to try.

Local Beverages

  1. Sisu: A gin-based aperitif made from pine buds and other Finnish botanicals.
  2. Lapin Kulta: A Finnish beer brewed in Lapland. It is a refreshing and delicious local brew worth trying.
  3. Glogi: A type of Finnish mulled wine made with spices and often served during the festive season.

Where to Stay in Savukoski: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Savukoski offers several types of accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are our top recommendations:

  1. Lapin Satu: Located next to UKK National Park, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent views of the surrounding wilderness.
  2. Korouoma Camping: A budget-friendly option located right on the Korouoma Canyon Trail, the campsite offers a unique opportunity to stay amid the natural beauty of the region.
  3. Savukoski Hostel: Ideally located in the heart of Savukoski, this budget-friendly option offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Savukoski: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Savukoski, there are several souvenirs and local products worth taking home with you:

  1. Handmade Wooden Crafts: This region is known for its traditional handicrafts. Purchase handmade wooden objects inspired by Lapland’s traditional culture and make sure to bring home a piece of Savukoski’s heritage.
  2. Sami Handicrafts: The indigenous Sami people are known for their intricate traditional handicrafts, including silver jewelry, beautiful textiles, and unique knives. You can find many of these offerings in the area’s souvenir shops and markets.
  3. Arctic Berries: Savukoski area is famous for its berries, and visiting during the harvest season can provide the opportunity to try arctic cranberries, cloudberries, blueberries, and lingonberries. You can take home frozen and dried berries from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Savukoski

Savukoski has a rich cultural heritage steeped in its indigenous Sami traditions. These traditions include herding reindeer, a deep connection with nature and sophisticated outdoors skills. Take a guided tour or visit a farm to learn about local customs and the unique way of life associated with northern Finland.

Best Time to Visit Savukoski

The best time to visit Savukoski can depend on the kind of experience you’re looking for. Winter is the best time for winter activities and annual Northern lights season between September to early April. In contrast, summer is great for outdoor activities like hiking and biking and witnessing the Midnight Sun. Overall, the best time for a visit to Savukoski is between late May and early September to enjoy the warmth and various outdoor activities of the Finnish Lapland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savukoski

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the numerous outdoor activities available in the area.
  • Try the delicious local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or cause damage in protected natural areas.
  • Disrespect the Sami culture.
  • Leave trash or waste in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Savukoski

Savukoski is one of the safest destinations in the world, with low levels of crime, and minimal safety concerns. However, it is essential to take the proper precautions when exploring the natural beauty of the region, follow the park rules, and check the weather to avoid any accidents.

Health in Savukoski

Finland has a well-developed healthcare system. There are several hospitals near Savukoski, and you can contact the medical emergency service by calling 112 if required.

Getting to Savukoski: Transportation Options and Logistics

Savukoski is well-connected by air, bus, and car. The closest airport is Ivalo, which serves several international locations, and you can also travel by bus from Helsinki or other Lapland cities. Renting a car is an excellent option for those who want to explore the area at their own pace on their trip.

Airports Near Savukoski

  • Ivalo Airport is the nearest airport, located 80km from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Savukoski

  • Savukoski bus station is located in the town center, with services to several nearby locations.
  • Bus services often connect to other Lapland cities like Rovaniemi, Ivalo, and Tampere.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is the best way to take advantage of Savukoski’s natural beauty and explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Savukoski

The best way to get around Savukoski is by hiking, biking or driving. Most attractions are accessible by car, and bike rental services are available in the town center. Several trails can be accessed on foot, and guided tours are also available for those seeking a more guided experience.

Where to Go Next from Savukoski: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savukoski is in a prime location for exploring the surrounding Lapland region. Here are some of our top picks for nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Rovaniemi: The capital of Finnish Lapland, Rovaniemi boasts world-class museums, unique shopping, and an abundance of winter sports activities.
  2. Inari: This small town is located on the shores of Lake Inari and offers ample opportunities for fishing, hiking, and exploring the surrounding wilderness.
  3. Sodankylä: This area nearby, heritage town celebrates the local culture and hosts the Midnight Sun Film Festival annually in June.

Conclusion

Savukoski is an idyllic destination in the heart of Finnish Lapland, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences. With something for everyone, this small town is a must-visit destination on any trip to Northern Europe. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your next visit to Savukoski with confidence and enjoy an unforgettable adventure in the Lapland region.
