Introduction to Ylläs

Welcome to Ylläs, a breathtakingly beautiful region in Finnish Lapland, known for its pristine natural beauty, snowy landscapes, and thrilling outdoor activities. Located in northern Finland, Ylläs boasts the highest peaks in Finland, and is the perfect destination for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. Sprawling across two villages, Akaslompolo and Ylläsjärvi, Ylläs is a year-round destination that offers a wide range of activities and experiences.

Local Culture and Customs of Ylläs

Ylläs is the home of the Sámi people, and Sámi culture is an integral part of the region’s identity. Visitors are welcome to learn more about Sámi traditions and customs through various cultural tours and experiences.

Best Time to Visit Ylläs

The best time to visit Ylläs depends on your interests. Winter, from November to March, is a popular time for skiing and winter activities, while summer, from June to August, is perfect for hiking and biking. Autumn, from September to October, is an excellent time to view the northern lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ylläs

Do:

  • Dress warmly and in layers
  • Respect the wildlife and natural environment
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Book activities and tours in advance

Don’t:

  • Leave litter behind
  • Approach reindeer or other wildlife
  • Ignore safety instructions while on tours
  • Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Ylläs

Visitors to Ylläs should take care when engaging in outdoor activities, such as skiing, snowmobiling, and husky safaris. Safety instructions should be followed carefully, and proper equipment, such as helmets and safety harnesses, should be worn at all times.

Health in Ylläs

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and medical facilities are available throughout the region. It is recommended that travelers have comprehensive health insurance before arriving in Ylläs.

How to Get Around in Ylläs

The best way to get around Ylläs is by car, although taxis and public buses are available. During winter, ski buses run between villages and the ski resort.
