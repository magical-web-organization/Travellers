Introduction to Levi

Levi is a magical winter destination situated in Finnish Lapland, in the north of Northern Finland, which is known for its rugged landscapes, outdoor winter sports, breathtaking Aurora Borealis, and Sámi culture. It is a tourist destination for all ages, where you can find perfect winter wonderland activities, from cross-country skiing to husky safaris, to chasing the Northern Lights in winter, and hiking and cycling in summer. Levi boasts a small, cozy village, which is surrounded by natural wonders such as forests and snow-capped mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Levi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Samiland Museum - This museum showcases the history and culture of the indigenous Sámi people of Northern Finland. Here you can learn about traditional Sámi lifestyle, including their art, handicrafts, and daily life.
  • Lapland War Museum - The museum is located in the village center and tells the story of the Winter War of 1939-1940 in Lapland.
  • Levi Spirit - Located in the heart of Levi, this museum explores the culture and traditions of the region through immersive, multimedia experiences.

Natural Attractions of Levi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park - This national park is the perfect place for hiking year-round. In winter, snowshoeing and cross-country skiing are also possible.
  • Luvattumaa - Located just outside the village, Luvattumaa is an igloo village that offers guests the chance to experience traditional Arctic accommodation under the Northern Lights.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Levi

Levi has numerous hiking and biking trails, which are often best experienced as part of a guided tour led by local guides. Some of the must-do hiking and biking trails in Levi include the

  • Levi Fell - This is a 1.5 km long trail that takes around 45 minutes to complete. It is a great hiking trail for those looking for scenic views of the village as well as the surrounding forest.
  • Ounasjoki Riverside Trail - This trail starts from the village center and follows the Ounasjoki River, passing through picturesque forests and offering excellent views of the surrounding hillside.

Best Things to Do in Levi: Activities and Tours

  • Cross-country skiing - Levi offers over 230 km of cross-country trails that are maintained and prepared for all experience levels.
  • Snowmobiling - Rent an snowmobile and take a guided tour through snow-covered Lapland forests or up to the mountains.
  • Husky sledding - Take a guided husky safari through unspoiled natural landscapes at a pace-controlled by the dogs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Levi

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Levi and explore the village center
2 Take a husky safari and visit the Samiland Museum
3 Go cross-country skiing along the trails of Levi Fell
4 Try snowmobiling and visit the Lapland War Museum
5 Take a day trip to Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park
6 Go snowshoeing or have a relaxing spa day at Levi Spirit
7 Go Aurora chasing with a local guide and make merry with a traditional Finnish meal

Food and Drink Scene of Levi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sautéed reindeer - This is the signature dish of Lapland and can be found at almost all local restaurants.
  • Grilled salmon - Fresh salmon grilled with a traditional sauce made with wild herbs and spices.
  • Cloudberry tea - A warming hot drink made with wild Finnish cloudberry.

Local Beverages

  • Glögi - Finnish mulled wine made with spices and typically served during winter months.
  • Sisu - A type of Finnish schnapps that is believed to give one courage and fortitude.

Where to Stay in Levi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Levi offers a range of accommodations, including hotels, guesthouses, and holiday apartments. Some of the best options include

  • Levi Hotel Spa – A cozy hotel with a spa that is located in the center of Levi village.
  • Hotel Hullu Poro – This hotel is in the heart of the village, offers suites and rooms with private saunas.
  • Levilehto Apartments – Offers beautifully designed, fully furnished apartments near the village center.

What to Buy in Levi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sámi handicrafts - Visitors can purchase traditional Sámi handicrafts such as hand-woven woolen shawls, hats, and mittens, which are made by local craftspeople.
  • Reindeer skins - Visitors can purchase ethically produced, locally tanned reindeer skins to bring home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Levi

The local culture in Levi is deeply influenced by the Sámi people, who have lived in the region for centuries. Some of the traditions and customs that visitors should be aware of include respecting the local wildlife, learning about traditional Sámi culture, and enjoying local cuisine and beverages.

Best Time to Visit Levi

The best time to visit Levi is during winter from December to March when the snow is at its thickest, and the Northern Lights can often be seen dancing in the night sky. Summer is also a great time to visit for hiking, fishing and more.

Do’s and Don’ts of Levi

Do:

  • Do take a guided tour to learn about Sámi culture.
  • Do try traditional Lappish food and drinks during your stay.
  • Do hire local tour guides for outdoor activities and expeditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Don’t disturb local wildlife while hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Levi

Levi is a safe destination with very low crime rates, including violent crime. However, visitors should take basic precautions like locking doors and windows, keeping valuables secure, and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Levi

Finland has a modern healthcare system, and there are healthcare providers in all major cities. Visitors to Levi should carry adequate travel insurance to cover any medical or emergency expenses.

Getting to Levi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Levi

  • Kittilä Airport - Located about a 25-minute drive from Levi, now with direct international flights during certain seasons.
  • Rovaniemi Airport - located about a 1.5-hour drive from Levi, it is a bigger airport offering direct flights from Helsinki and other European capitals.

Bus Stations Near Levi

  • Levi Bus Station - Public buses connect Levi to surrounding towns in northern Lapland, such as Kittilä and Sirkka.

Other Options

  • Train - Take the train of Lapland from Rovaniemi filled with beautiful winter landscapes and changing scenery.
  • Taxi - Taxis in Levi are reliable, though they can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Levi

The most common ways to get around Levi are

  • By foot - The village is walkable, and all the attractions are close to each other.
  • Public transport - The local buses in Levi run throughout the year.
  • By car - Visitors can rent a car to explore the surrounding areas. In winter, vehicles must be equipped with snow tires and chains.

Where to Go Next from Levi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of other destinations to explore beyond Levi, including:

  • Rovaniemi - also known as the “official home of Santa Claus,” Rovaniemi offers travelers a chance to visit Santa’s village and enjoy winter-themed activities.
  • Ylläs - This beautiful natural setting offers excellent skiing and hiking trails, along with unique culture and cuisine.
  • Inari - Located in the heart of Sámi country, Inari is a great destination to learn about the indigenous culture of northern Finland.

Conclusion

Levi is a must-visit destination for anyone wanting to experience one of the world’s most unique destinations. Visitors to Levi can enjoy scenic views, outdoor activities year-round, great food, and friendly locals. Whether it be the Winter wonderland or during the bright summertime, visitors can be sure to experience an enjoyable and unforgettable trip to this enchanting Lapland destination.
