Introduction to Ivalo

Nestled in the heart of Finnish Lapland, Ivalo is a remote destination that offers a true taste of the wilderness. Known for its rugged landscapes, arctic wildlife, and Northern Lights displays, Ivalo is a top choice for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in nature.

In addition to the stunning natural wonders, Ivalo also has a rich cultural history, with indigenous Sami communities calling the region home for centuries. Today, Ivalo is a vibrant hub for Finnish Lapland’s tourism industry, with a wide range of activities, tours, and accommodations to suit every taste.

List of Places to Visit in Ivalo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to get a sense of Ivalo’s unique cultural history is by exploring its museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Inari Sami Museum and Nature Centre: Learn about the indigenous Sami people and their way of life through exhibits, multimedia displays, and guided tours.

  • Siida - Sámi Museum and Nature Centre: Discover the unique ecology and culture of Finnish Lapland through interactive exhibits and outdoor activities at this museum and nature center in nearby Inari.

  • Pielpajarvi Wilderness Church: Admire the traditional Sami architecture and stunning natural surroundings of this wooden church, which dates back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Ivalo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Of course, one of the main draws of Ivalo is its breathtaking natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for adrenaline-pumping adventures or peaceful hikes through pristine wilderness, Ivalo has something to offer. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Saariselka Ski Resort: Hit the slopes at this popular ski resort, which boasts more than 15 slopes ranging in difficulty from beginner to advanced.

  • Urho Kekkonen National Park: Explore the untouched wilderness of this sprawling national park, which covers more than 2,500 square kilometers and is home to countless species of arctic flora and fauna.

  • aurora borealis Northern Lights: Experience the awe-inspiring beauty of the Northern Lights, which can be seen from Ivalo during the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ivalo

Ivalo is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking in the region. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Ivalo River Trail: Follow the Ivalo River through pristine forests and wetlands, taking in the stunning scenery along the way.

  • Kiilopaa Fell: This scenic route takes you to the top of Kiilopaa Fell, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness.

  • Kaunispaa Mountain: This challenging hike up Kaunispaa Mountain rewards you with stunning views of the Northern Lights during the winter months.

Best Things to Do in Ivalo: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something a bit more structured, there are plenty of tours and activities to choose from in Ivalo. Here are a few options:

  • Reindeer and husky safaris: Experience the traditional Sami way of life by going on a reindeer or husky safari through the wilderness.

  • Ice fishing: Try your hand at ice fishing on one of Ivalo’s many lakes and rivers.

  • Northern Lights tours: Join a guided tour to maximize your chances of seeing the Northern Lights in all their glory.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ivalo

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week in Ivalo:

Day 3 Go skiing at Saariselka Ski Resort

Food and Drink Scene of Ivalo

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine in Ivalo is heavily influenced by the region’s natural resources, with an emphasis on fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Here are a few dishes to try:

  • Reindeer meat: A staple of Sami cuisine, reindeer meat is often served in stews, soups, and roasts.

  • Arctic char: This freshwater fish can be found in many of Ivalo’s rivers and lakes, and is often prepared with traditional Nordic flavors like dill and lingonberries.

  • Leipajuusto: This Finnish cheese is a must-try, with a unique texture that makes it perfect for grilling or frying.

Local Beverages

Ivalo also has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries producing high-quality brews. For something a bit stronger, try the Finnish specialty, Koskenkorva Vodka.

Where to Stay in Ivalo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ivalo has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are a few options:

  • Hotel Ivalo: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, on-site dining, and easy access to the town’s amenities.

  • Wilderness Hotel Nellim: Located on the shores of Lake Inari, this rustic hotel offers cozy, traditional-style rooms and easy access to the surrounding wilderness.

  • Hostel Ivalo: For budget travelers, Hostel Ivalo offers affordable dormitory-style accommodations, as well as private rooms.

What to Buy in Ivalo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring a piece of Ivalo home with you, there are plenty of local products to choose from, including:

  • Traditional Sami handicrafts, such as carvings, jewelry, and woven fabrics.

  • Finnish design and fashion brands, with a focus on minimalist, functional design.

  • Smoked reindeer meat and other gourmet food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Ivalo

Ivalo’s unique culture is influenced by its indigenous Sami population, with a focus on preserving the natural beauty of the region and maintaining a sustainable way of life. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as removing their shoes upon entering a Sami home.

Best Time to Visit Ivalo

The best time to visit Ivalo depends on your interests. For winter sports and Northern Lights viewing, the months of October through March offer the best conditions. For hiking and outdoor activities, the warmer summer months of June through August are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ivalo

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.

  • Dress in warm, layered clothing to combat the cold temperatures.

  • Try local cuisine and products.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the reindeer, which are an important part of Sami life.

  • Litter or pollute the environment.

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ivalo

Ivalo is a very safe destination, but it’s important to be prepared for the harsh winter conditions. Always dress warmly, and be aware of the risks of hypothermia and frostbite.

Health in Ivalo

Ivalo has excellent medical facilities, but it’s recommended that you have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving. In addition, be sure to pack any necessary medication or medical supplies, as they may not be readily available in the area.

Getting to Ivalo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ivalo

The closest airport to Ivalo is Ivalo Airport (IVL), which is served by several domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Ivalo

There are several bus companies that operate services to and from Ivalo, connecting the town with other destinations in Finnish Lapland and beyond.

Other Options

It’s also possible to reach Ivalo by train or car, although these options may be less convenient for international visitors.

How to Get Around in Ivalo

While Ivalo is a relatively small town, it’s best to have your own vehicle in order to explore the surrounding wilderness. Rental cars and snowmobiles are available, as well as guided tours and excursions.

Where to Go Next from Ivalo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Lapland adventure beyond Ivalo, there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore, such as:

  • Rovaniemi: Known as the “official” hometown of Santa Claus, Rovaniemi is a popular destination for families and lovers of holiday cheer.

  • Levi Ski Resort: This popular ski resort in western Lapland offers more than 40 slopes and a lively nightlife scene.

  • Inari: Experience the unique culture and history of the indigenous Sami people at this small town on the shores of Lake Inari.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and exciting activities and adventures, Ivalo is a destination that truly has it all. Whether you’re seeking outdoor thrills, cultural immersion, or a peaceful escape from the city, Ivalo has something to offer. So pack your bags and get ready to explore the wilds of Finnish Lapland!
