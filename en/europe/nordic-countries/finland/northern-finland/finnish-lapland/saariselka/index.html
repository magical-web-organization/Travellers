Introduction to Saariselkä

Saariselkä is a beautiful resort village located in Finnish Lapland, in Northern Finland, in Nordic Countries, in Europe. Known for its natural beauty, Saariselkä is a popular destination for tourists all year round.

In the winter, visitors flock to the village to enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports. In the summer, Saariselkä offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and cycling.

List of Places to Visit in Saariselkä

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Siida Museum: One of the most popular museums in Lapland, Siida Museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the Sami people, the indigenous people of Finland.

  • Gold Prospector Museum: This museum showcases the history of gold mining in the area, including the methods used and the hardships faced by the prospectors.

Natural Attractions of Saariselkä: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kaunispää Hill: At 437 meters, this hill offers a stunning view of Saariselkä and the surrounding areas. It is accessible by cable car or by hiking.

  • Urho Kekkonen National Park: Covering an area of 2,500 square kilometers, this park is perfect for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saariselkä

  • Kiilopää Mountain: This hiking trail is approximately 10 kilometers long, and offers stunning views of the surrounding areas.

  • Fatbike Safari: A guided cycling tour on fatbikes, specially designed for cycling on snow, this tour takes visitors on a scenic ride through the snowy forests surrounding Saariselkä.

Best Things to Do in Saariselkä: Activities and Tours

  • Northern Lights Tour: Saariselkä is one of the best places in the world to witness the magical Northern Lights. Visitors can book a tour for an unforgettable experience.

  • Reindeer Farm Visit: Visitors can learn about the traditional Sami way of life by visiting a reindeer farm and taking a ride on a sleigh pulled by these friendly and gentle animals.

Food and Drink Scene of Saariselkä

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat: A traditional dish in the area, reindeer meat is both delicious and healthy. It can be cooked in a stew, grilled, or served as a steak.

  • Sautéed Chanterelle Mushrooms: This mushroom is found in the forests of Lapland and is a delicacy. The mushrooms are sautéed in butter and seasoned with salt and pepper.

Local Beverages

  • Cloudberry Liquor: This sweet and fruity liquor is made with cloudberries, a native berry to Lapland.

  • Sisu Vodka: Made with pure, natural ingredients in the Finnish countryside, this vodka is a popular choice among locals.

Where to Stay in Saariselkä: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Santa’s Hotel Tunturi: This hotel is one of the most popular in the area. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, a sauna, and a swimming pool.

  • Kiilopää Fell Center: Located in the Urho Kekkonen National Park, this center offers accommodation in traditional Finnish log cabins, as well as a restaurant and sauna.

What to Buy in Saariselkä: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sami Handicrafts: The Sami people are known for their intricate handicrafts, including bracelets, earrings, and traditional clothing.

  • Reindeer Skins: High-quality reindeer skins make for a great souvenir and can be used as a rug, throw, or wall hanging.

Local Culture and Customs of Saariselkä

  • The Sami people have a rich culture and history in the area, and visitors are encouraged to learn about their traditions and customs.

  • Finnish saunas are an important part of everyday life in Finland. Visitors should be aware of proper sauna etiquette before entering.

Best Time to Visit Saariselkä

The best time to visit Saariselkä depends on what activities you want to do. In the winter, from late November to early May, visitors can enjoy winter sports and witness the Northern Lights. In the summer, from June to August, visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, fishing, and cycling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saariselkä

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather, especially in the winter.

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the Sami people.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the pristine natural environment.

  • Ignore the dangers of the wilderness, especially in the winter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saariselkä

  • Always be aware of the weather conditions, especially during the winter when it can be extremely cold and snowy.

  • When hiking or camping in the wilderness, be mindful of wildlife such as bears and wolves.

Health in Saariselkä

  • Visitors should not drink unfiltered water from lakes and rivers.

  • Finish Lapland has high UV exposure during the summer months, so it is important to wear proper sun protection.

Getting to Saariselkä: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saariselkä

  • Ivalo Airport is located approximately 30 kilometers from Saariselkä.

  • Rovaniemi Airport is located approximately 250 kilometers from Saariselkä.

Bus Stations Near Saariselkä

  • There are several daily buses from Rovaniemi and Ivalo to Saariselkä.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Saariselkä

  • Most attractions in Saariselkä are within walking distance of the town center.

  • Visitors can also rent bicycles, snowshoes, or skis to get around more easily.

Where to Go Next from Saariselkä: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rovaniemi, the capital of Finnish Lapland, is located approximately 250 kilometers from Saariselkä.

  • The Arctic Ocean is only a few hours’ drive from Saariselkä.

Conclusion

Saariselkä is a beautiful and unique destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Saariselkä is the perfect place to visit in Finnish Lapland.
