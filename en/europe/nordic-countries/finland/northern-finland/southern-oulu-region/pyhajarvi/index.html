A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Pyhäjärvi, Finland

Located in Southern Oulu region, in Northern Finland, Pyhäjärvi boasts serene natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage that attracts tourists from all around the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-see destinations, activities, food and drink scene, and much more.

Introduction to Pyhäjärvi

Pyhäjärvi is a small municipality located in the Northern part of Finland, and is part of the Southern Oulu region. The town is mostly known for its crystal-clear lake, Pyhäjärvi, and the popular ski resort, Pyhä Ski Resort. Pyhäjärvi has a population of around 5500 individuals and is known for its untouched natural beauty, captivating scenic views, and rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Stone Age.

List of Places to Visit in Pyhäjärvi

Pyhäjärvi has some of the most enchanting and captivating tourist destinations that you would not want to miss out on during your visit. Here are some of the must-visit places of Pyhäjärvi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pyhäjärvi Old Church: A historical wooden church built-in 1753, it is one of the oldest wooden churches in Finland
  • Siuranmaa Museum: A museum that illustrates the history of Pyhäjärvi from the Stone Age to modern times
  • Hartikka Village: A charming village showcasing life in the early 20th century with traditional farmhouses and buildings and a nature trail

Natural Attractions of Pyhäjärvi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pyhä-Luosto National Park: A beautiful park located near the resort offering various hiking trails, wildlife viewing opportunities, and scenic views
  • Iso-Syöte: A natural park known for offering some of the best hiking and mountain biking trails in the area with stunning views of the rolling hills and valleys

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pyhäjärvi

Pyhäjärvi is a haven for hikers and bikers, offering various routes suitable for all levels. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Pyhäjärvi:

  • The Pyhä-Luosto Trail: A 20 km trail that takes you through beautiful forests and streams, and provides stunning views of the fells and valleys
  • Iso-Syöte Fell Trail: A 15 km trail that takes you through thick forests and scenic routes through the fells

Best Things to Do in Pyhäjärvi: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Pyhäjärvi offers a host of activities and tours that you can enjoy during your visit. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Dog sledding: Experience the thrill of dog sledding through beautiful scenery with Pyhä Safaris.
  • ATV safaris: Explore Pyhäjärvi’s vast wilderness on an ATV tour with North Lynx Safaris.
  • Skiing and snowboarding: Hit the slopes at Pyhä Ski Resort, one of the region’s most popular ski resorts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pyhäjärvi

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Pyhäjärvi:

  • Day 1-2: Visit the Siuranmaa Museum and Hartikka Village to learn about Pyhäjärvi’s rich history and culture.
  • Day 3-4: Hike the Pyhä-Luosto Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding fells and valleys.
  • Day 5-6: Go skiing and snowboarding at Pyhä Ski Resort or take an ATV tour with North Lynx Safaris.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Iso-Syöte spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Pyhäjärvi

The Pyhäjärvi region has a rich culinary culture with unique tastes and culinary traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kotamakkara: Traditional sausage made from reindeer meat and other local meats.
  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs served with gravy and mashed potatoes.
  • Poronkäristys: Traditional Finnish food made of reindeer meat, usually served with mashed potatoes, lingonberry jam, and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: Finnish homemade beer made of rye malt, juniper berries, and hops and is a popular drink in Pyhäjärvi.
  • Kilju: A traditional Finnish alcoholic drink made of sugar, water, and yeast

Where to Stay in Pyhäjärvi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pyhäjärvi offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Pyhäjärvi:

  • Pyhäkirnu: A cozy and luxurious hotel located near the Pyhä-Luosto National Park.
  • Pyhän Asteli: A budget-friendly hostel with comfortable rooms and a shared kitchen.
  • Hotel Iso Syöte: A luxurious hotel offering stunning views of Iso-Syöte National Park.

What to Buy in Pyhäjärvi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pyhäjärvi is a great place to shop for unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best items to buy when in Pyhäjärvi:

  • Lemmikki: A local bakery known for its delicious Finnish pastries and bread.
  • Kellokas Handicrafts: A store that offers handmade items such as rugs, clothing, and other handicrafts.
  • Tunturi-Lapin Puukko: A knife maker that offers high-quality knives made using traditional methods.

Local Culture and Customs of Pyhäjärvi

The Finnish culture is known for its love for nature, respect for personal space, and a relaxed way of life. In Pyhäjärvi, locals are warm and welcoming, and it’s common to greet people with a smile.

Best Time to Visit Pyhäjärvi

The best time to visit Pyhäjärvi is during the winter months, from December to March, when the area is covered with snow, and you can enjoy winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding. However, if you prefer warmer temperatures, summer is also a great time to visit, and you can enjoy hiking and camping in the warmer weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pyhäjärvi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress warmly in the winter months.
  • Enjoy the serene nature of Pyhäjärvi.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pyhäjärvi

Pyhäjärvi is a safe destination, and violent crime is rare. However, it’s best to take precautions to avoid pickpockets and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Pyhäjärvi

Pyhäjärvi is a safe destination, and there are no health risks associated with the area. However, it’s always a good idea to carry travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Pyhäjärvi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pyhäjärvi is easily accessible, and there are various transportation options available to get there.

Airports Near Pyhäjärvi

The nearest airport to Pyhäjärvi is Oulu Airport, located around 100km from Pyhäjärvi.

Bus Stations Near Pyhäjärvi

The nearest bus station to Pyhäjärvi is located in Kempele, around 80km from Pyhäjärvi. There are various bus companies that operate services to Pyhäjärvi, including Matkahuolto and Onnibus.

Other Options

You can also rent a car to reach Pyhäjärvi. The journey from Oulu to Pyhäjärvi takes around 1.5 hours by car.

How to Get Around in Pyhäjärvi

Getting around Pyhäjärvi is easy, and there are various options available such as rental cars, taxis, and public transportation. However, it’s best to rent a car if you want to explore the area and its surroundings at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Pyhäjärvi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other destinations near Pyhäjärvi, here are some of our top picks:

  • Rovaniemi: The capital of Lapland, known for its stunning northern lights and Santa Claus Village.
  • Kalajoki: This coastal town is known for its beautiful beaches and natural landscapes.
  • Helsinki: Finland’s capital city and a vibrant cosmopolitan city with an array of cultural and historic sites.

Conclusion

Pyhäjärvi is a town that truly captures the essence of Finnish culture, with its untouched wilderness and stunning natural landscapes. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Pyhäjärvi has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Pyhäjärvi.
