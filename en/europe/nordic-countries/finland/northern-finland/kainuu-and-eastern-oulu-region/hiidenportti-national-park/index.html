Introduction to Hiidenportti National Park

Nestled in the heart of Northern Finland, Hiidenportti National Park is a natural wonderland of rugged landscapes, boreal forests, peat bogs, glistening lakes, and rushing rivers. Covering an area of about 45 square kilometers, the park is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including lynx, bear, wolf, elk, reindeer, and wolverine.

List of Places to Visit in Hiidenportti National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hiidenportti Nature Centre: A modern interactive museum showcasing the park’s flora, fauna, geology, and culture, with exhibition halls, guided tours, and educational programs.
  • The Hiidenkirnu: A huge rock formation shaped like a giant cauldron, which according to Finnish mythology, is the site where the devil used to brew his potions.

Natural Attractions of Hiidenportti National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Korkiakoski Falls: A stunning waterfall cascading over a rocky cliff, with a viewing platform offering panoramic vistas of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Isohieta: A vast plateau with breathtaking views of the rolling hills and valleys, accessible by a hiking trail starting from the Hiidenportti Nature Centre.
  • Värikallio: A unique rock art site with over 60 ancient paintings, depicting humans, animals, and symbols, and offering a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Sami people, who inhabited the area for thousands of years.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hiidenportti National Park

  • Hiidenkierros Trail: A 19 km circular hiking trail that takes you through the park’s most scenic areas, including the Hiidenportti Gorge, Isohieta Plateau, and Värikallio Rock, with several rest stops and campsites along the way.
  • Kellinsalmi Trail: A 6 km circular trail that leads you through the forest and over the Kellinsalmi Suspension Bridge, with a bird-watching tower and a picnic area.
  • Syyrakki Trail: A 4 km trail that takes you to the top of the Syyrakki Hill, with stunning views of Lake Syöte and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Hiidenportti National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and kayaking: Explore the park’s waterways by canoe or kayak, and enjoy the serene beauty of the lakes and rivers.
  • Fishing: Cast a line and try your luck at catching perch, pike, and other freshwater fish in the park’s lakes and streams.
  • Snowshoeing and cross-country skiing: Experience the park’s winter wonderland with a snowshoeing or cross-country skiing trip, and marvel at the snowy landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hiidenportti National Park

Day 1: Arrive in Kajaani, the nearest city, and take a bus or rent a car to Hiidenportti National Park. Check into your accommodation and explore the Hiidenportti Nature Centre.

Day 2-3: Hike the Hiidenkierros Trail, stopping at the Korkiakoski Falls, Isohieta Plateau, and Värikallio Rock. Take a break and enjoy a picnic lunch or a swim in one of the lakes.

Day 4: Rent a canoe or kayak and paddle to the Kellinsalmi Suspension Bridge. Take a short hike to a bird-watching tower and enjoy the views.

Day 5: Go fishing in one of the park’s lakes or streams, and try to catch some freshwater fish for dinner.

Day 6: Experience the park’s winter wonderland with a snowshoeing or cross-country skiing trip. Enjoy the snowy landscapes and the peaceful silence of the forest.

Day 7: Depart from Hiidenportti National Park and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Hiidenportti National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpaisti: A hearty stew made with beef, pork, or reindeer meat, potatoes, carrots, and spices, traditionally cooked in a cast iron pot over an open fire.
  • Kalakukko: A savory pastry filled with fish, pork, and bacon, wrapped in rye dough and baked in a traditional Finnish oven.
  • Leipäjuusto: A local cheese made from cow’s or reindeer’s milk, with a chewy texture and a hint of sweetness.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made from malted rye, barley, and oats, flavored with juniper berries and fermented with wild yeast.
  • Koskenkorva: A clear spirit made from locally grown barley and distilled in a traditional Finnish brewery, with a mild and smooth taste.
  • Karpalo: A refreshing juice made from the local cranberries, known for their health benefits and rich flavor.

Where to Stay in Hiidenportti National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hiidenportti Camping: A cozy campsite with cabins, tents, and caravans, located right at the edge of the park, with easy access to hiking trails and fishing spots.
  • Revontuli Cottage: A charming guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a sauna, surrounded by the forest and offering a relaxing retreat from the hustle and bustle.

What to Buy in Hiidenportti National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Birch Bark Crafts: Handmade baskets, boxes, and bowls made from the local birch bark, with intricate designs and patterns.
  • Reindeer Antler Products: Carved knife handles, buttons, and jewelry made from the renewable resource of reindeer antlers, a staple of Sami culture.
  • Local Honey: Pure and natural honey produced by the bees living in the park’s meadows and forests, with a distinctive taste and aroma.

Local Culture and Customs of Hiidenportti National Park

The Sami people are the indigenous inhabitants of the area, and their rich culture and traditions are reflected in the park’s folklore, art, and cuisine. The park is also home to a variety of endangered plant and animal species, and preserving the fragile ecosystem is a top priority for the Finnish authorities.

Best Time to Visit Hiidenportti National Park

The park is open year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. Summer (June-August) is the peak season, with long days, mild temperatures, and a vibrant nature. Autumn (September-November) brings colorful foliage, berries, and mushrooms, as well as the famous Northern Lights. Winter (December-February) is the snow season, with opportunities for skiing, snowshoeing, and ice fishing. Spring (March-May) is the awakening season, with melting snow, budding trees, and migrating birds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hiidenportti National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s flora, fauna, and cultural heritage.
  • Follow the marked trails and stay on the designated areas.
  • Carry out your litter and leave no trace of your visit.
  • Ask for permission before fishing or hunting in the park.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and wear comfortable shoes.

Don’t:

  • Damage or disturb the park’s natural or cultural features.
  • Light fires outside the designated fireplaces.
  • Pick or damage any vegetation or wildlife.
  • Feed or approach any wild animals.
  • Use motorized vehicles on the trails or off-road.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hiidenportti National Park

  • Always inform someone of your itinerary and expected return time.
  • Carry a map, compass, and GPS device, and know how to use them.
  • Carry a first-aid kit, a mobile phone, and enough food and water.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions and dress appropriately.
  • Watch out for ticks, mosquitoes, and other insect bites.

Health in Hiidenportti National Park

  • Drink only safe water from taps or boiling streams.
  • Take precautions against tick-borne diseases, such as Lyme disease and tick-borne encephalitis.
  • Avoid consuming wild mushrooms or berries unless you have expert knowledge.

Getting to Hiidenportti National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hiidenportti National Park

The nearest airport is Kajaani Airport, which has daily flights to Helsinki and other major cities in Finland.

Bus Stations Near Hiidenportti National Park

Kajaani Bus Station is the main hub for local and regional buses, with connections to Helsinki, Oulu, and other major cities in Finland.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is the most convenient way to get to the park and explore the surrounding area.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Kajaani and can be booked in advance.

How to Get Around in Hiidenportti National Park

The best way to explore the park is on foot, by bike, or by canoe/kayak. There are also several marked trails and forest roads for hiking and biking, as well as a network of waterways for paddling and fishing.

Where to Go Next from Hiidenportti National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kuhmo: A charming town located 45 km from the park, known for its cultural events, music festivals, and art exhibitions, as well as its proximity to the Russian border.
  • Syöte National Park: A neighboring park, located 75 km from Hiidenportti, with its own unique landscapes, wildlife, and hiking trails, including the Syöte Round Tour.
  • Oulanka National Park: A popular park located 160 km from Hiidenportti, with spectacular ravines, rapids, and waterfalls, and the famous Karhunkierros Trail.

Conclusion

Hiidenportti National Park is a true gem of Northern Finland, offering a wonderful combination of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re a nature lover, a culture enthusiast, or an outdoor enthusiast, this park has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the wonders of Hiidenportti National Park!
