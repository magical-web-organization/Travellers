Introduction to Northern Finland

Northern Finland is a region that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. This captivating area of the country is a paradise for those who love experiencing peace, tranquility, and outdoor adventures. With stunning natural landscapes, Northern Finland is home to boreal forests, crystal-clear lakes, and the Northern Lights. It’s a destination that is perfect for those seeking adventure, solace, and an intimate relationship with nature.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Finland

If you’re planning your trip to Northern Finland, there’s no shortage of places to visit. Here are some must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northern Finland has a rich cultural heritage. You can immerse yourself in the region’s history and traditions through its fascinating museums and historical sites.

  • Arktikum in Rovaniemi is a museum that showcases the cultural and social history of Arctic Finland and Lapland.
  • The Sámi Museum Siida in Inari exhibits the Sámi culture and nature of Northern Lapland.
  • Oulu City Museum in Oulu offers an insight into the history and culture of the city and the region.

Natural Attractions of Northern Finland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northern Finland is an adventure lover’s paradise with endless outdoor activities to explore. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Lemmenjoki National Park in Inari is the largest national park in Finland, home to pristine wilderness, deep forests, and free-flowing rivers.
  • Riisitunturi National Park in Posio is a unique place characterized by the old pine forests on its hills and fells.
  • Ylläs in Akaslompolo is a mountain located in the middle of the vast landscape in Lapland. In the winter, it is a ski resort, and in the summer, it is a perfect destination for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Finland

Hiking and biking are some of the best ways to explore Northern Finland’s stunning natural environment. Here are some of the hiking and biking routes you can experience:

  • Hetta-Pallas Trail in Enontekiö is an iconic and challenging trail that passes through the national parks, featuring scenic landscapes with steep, rugged hills and deep and narrow valleys.
  • Rokua Trail in Rokua is a network of hiking and biking trails through a unique and fascinating geopark in Northern Finland.
  • Pyhä-Luosto National Park in Pelkosenniemi offers an extensive network of marked trails suitable for both experienced hikers and beginners.

Best Things to Do in Northern Finland: Activities and Tours

Northern Finland offers endless options for activities and tours, both during summer and winter. Here are some of the best things you can experience:

  • Aurora Hunting is a must-do activity in Lapland, where you can witness the Northern Lights in all its glory.
  • Husky Safari will take you through the breathtaking Lapland wilderness, and you can experience the beauty of nature in every step of the way.
  • Ice Fishing is a unique and popular activity, especially during the winter season, where you can experience the calmness and tranquility of Northern Finland’s frozen lakes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Finland

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Northern Finland, here is a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Finland

The culinary scene in Northern Finland is unique and rich in flavors and traditions.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat: Reindeer is a significant part of Northern Finnish cuisine. It is a lean meat that is rich in flavor, and is often served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam.
  • Karelian Pies: These pies are made from rye flour and filled with rice or potato, and are often served with butter or egg butter.
  • Sautéed Kuusamo Trout: This dish is made from trout from Northern Finland’s rivers and served sautéed with potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Lapin Kulta: This is a popular local beer in Lapland.
  • Sisu: A traditional Finnish energy drink that is said to offer stamina and strength.
  • Cloudberry Liquor: This liquor is made from the berries found only in Northern Finland, and has a unique fruity taste.

Where to Stay in Northern Finland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Finland offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Arctic TreeHouse Hotel in Rovaniemi is a unique and luxurious hotel that offers an immersive experience in Lapland’s nature.
  • Santa’s Hotel Tunturi in Saariselkä is a cozy and comfortable family hotel located in the heart of the village.
  • Hostel Cafe Koti in Rovaniemi is a trendy and affordable hostel located in the city center.

What to Buy in Northern Finland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Finland is home to many unique and traditional products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some of the best products to bring home:

  • Sámi Handicrafts: The Sámi people have a rich cultural heritage in Northern Lapland. You can buy traditional handicrafts such as joiks, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Kilpisjärvi Dry Fish: This fish is a local delicacy that is sold dried and salted.
  • Lapland Cheese: Lapland has many unique cheese varieties, such as Lappi, Reindeer, and Forest Squeaker.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Finland

The Sámi people have a significant presence in Northern Finland, and the region has a distinct cultural identity. The Sámi culture has a rich heritage, with traditions rooted in nature. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Northern Finland

Northern Finland’s tourism season is divided into winter and summer months. The winter season is between December and March, whereas the summer season is between June and August.

The best time to visit Northern Finland depends on what you want to experience. If you want to witness the Northern Lights, the winter season is the best time. However, if you’re looking for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, the summer season is the best time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Finland

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and the outdoor activities you plan to undertake.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave behind any waste in nature.
  • Disturb the wildlife or their habitats.
  • Touch or approach reindeer, as they are not pets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Finland

Northern Finland is a safe destination with minimal crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of the safety and risks associated with the outdoor activities they plan to undertake, such as hiking and biking.

Health in Northern Finland

Northern Finland has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care if needed. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Northern Finland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Finland

The main airport in Northern Finland is Rovaniemi Airport, which offers both domestic and international flights. Other airports in the region include Kittilä Airport and Ivalo Airport.

Bus Stations Near Northern Finland

Northern Finland has extensive bus services operated by Matkahuolto and other bus companies, making it easy to travel within the region. The main bus stations in Northern Finland include Rovaniemi Bus Station and Oulu Bus Station.

Other Options

Northern Finland can be accessed by train, car, or ferry, making it convenient for travelers to choose the mode of transport that suits them best.

How to Get Around in Northern Finland

Getting around Northern Finland is easy with several transportation options, including buses, trains, and car rentals. If you’re planning to explore the wilderness or take a road trip, renting a car is the best option. The bus and train systems are reliable, and offer a cost-effective way to travel within the region.

Where to Go Next from Northern Finland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northern Finland is surrounded by many exciting destinations and attractions. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can explore:

  • Kemi SnowCastle: A stunning castle made entirely out of snow and ice.
  • Syöte National Park: A beautiful national park that offers stunning panoramic views.
  • Ranua Wildlife Park: A park that is home to over 50 different species of Arctic animals

Conclusion

Northern Finland is a unique and beautiful region that offers a wide range of experiences for all types of travelers. From cultural attractions and natural wonders to outdoor adventures and traditional culinary delights, Northern Finland is a paradise waiting to be explored. Start planning your trip and get ready to have an unforgettably stunning experience in Northern Finland.
