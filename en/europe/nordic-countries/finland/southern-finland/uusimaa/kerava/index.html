Introduction to Kerava

Kerava, located in Southern Finland’s Uusimaa region, is a charming and peaceful city of around 38,000 residents. Despite being in close proximity to the bustling cities of Helsinki and Vantaa, Kerava retains its rural charm and breath-taking landscapes. The city offers visitors a variety of activities and sights to explore, including outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, delicious local food, and more. Whether you’re travelling solo or with family, Kerava has something to offer everyone. Read on for our comprehensive guide to Kerava.

List of Places to Visit in Kerava

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kerava Museum: Located in a former firehouse, the Kerava Museum is the perfect place to learn about the city’s history and local culture.
  • Saint Lawrence Church: This beautiful Lutheran church, located in the heart of Kerava, features a stunning altar painting and organ.

Natural Attractions of Kerava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kerava River: Take a stroll or bike ride along the Kerava River and enjoy the picturesque views of the surrounding nature.
  • Koivukylän arboretum park: Located in neighbouring Vantaa, this beautiful park features a variety of trees and plants from around the world and is perfect for a peaceful afternoon walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerava

  • Myllykoski rapids trail: This scenic hiking trail offers beautiful views of the Myllykoski rapids and passes through the lush forests of Kerava.
  • Kerava-Leppävaara bicycle route: This popular bike route runs from Kerava to the neighbouring city of Espoo and features beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kerava: Activities and Tours

  • Keskustan Kuva: Take a guided photography tour of Kerava and capture its beauty through the lens.
  • Play Park: Perfect for families with young children, this indoor playground features a variety of activities including trampolines, ball pits, and slides.

Food and Drink Scene of Kerava

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpaisti: This traditional Finnish stew is made with beef, potatoes, and root vegetables.
  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs made with ground beef, breadcrumbs, and onion.

Local Beverages

  • Salmiakki Koskenkorva: A popular Finnish liquor made with Koskenkorva vodka and salmiakki (salty liquorice).
  • Lapin Kulta: A classic Finnish beer with a rich and smooth taste.

Where to Stay in Kerava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cumulus Resort: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location in the heart of Kerava.
  • Hostel Kerava: Budget-friendly accommodation option with clean and simple rooms.
  • Guesthouse Stranda: Cute and cozy guesthouse featuring a sauna and terrace.

What to Buy in Kerava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Finnish chocolate: Satisfy your sweet tooth with some of Finland’s delicious chocolate brands, including Fazer and Karl Fazer.
  • Finnish wool products: Take home a cozy souvenir, such as a wool blanket or sweater.

Local Culture and Customs of Kerava

Kerava, like the rest of Finland, has a rich culture that’s worth exploring. Finnish culture is rooted in nature, so visitors can expect plenty of opportunities to enjoy the outdoors and learn about local traditions like sauna bathing.

Best Time to Visit Kerava

Summer (June to August) is the ideal time to visit Kerava, especially if you plan on spending time outdoors. The days are long, and the weather is mild, making it easy to explore the city’s attractions and nature.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerava

Do:

  • Do bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city’s nature trails and parks.
  • Do respect Finnish customs, like removing shoes before entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be late for meetings or appointments.
  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothes if visiting during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerava

Kerava is generally a safe city with low crime rates, making it a great destination for solo travellers or families. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as avoiding dark or deserted areas at night.

Health in Kerava

Finland has excellent healthcare, and visitors can easily access medical care in Kerava if needed. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Kerava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kerava

  • Helsinki Airport: Finland’s largest airport is located just 15 km from Kerava.
  • Vantaa Airport: A smaller airport located in neighbouring Vantaa, just a short drive or bus ride from Kerava.

Bus Stations Near Kerava

  • Kerava Bus Station: Located in the city centre, the Kerava Bus Station offers connections to destinations throughout Finland.
  • Tikkurila Bus Station: Located in neighbouring Vantaa, the Tikkurila Bus Station is a major hub for local and intercity buses.

Other Options

  • Train: Kerava has a railway station, which offers frequent connections to nearby cities like Helsinki and Tampere.

How to Get Around in Kerava

Kerava is a walkable city, and most of its attractions and sights are easily accessible on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the city’s nature trails and parks.

Where to Go Next from Kerava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Helsinki: Just a 30-minute train ride from Kerava, Helsinki offers visitors a range of attractions like historic cathedrals, museums, and markets.
  • Porvoo: This charming seaside town, known for its well-preserved old town and quaint boutiques, is just a short drive from Kerava.

Conclusion

Kerava is a hidden Finnish gem, offering visitors a laid-back atmosphere and access to beautiful nature trails and parks. Whether you’re interested in learning about the city’s history and local culture, or simply looking for a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city, Kerava has something to offer every visitor. We hope our comprehensive guide has inspired you to add Kerava to your travel list!
