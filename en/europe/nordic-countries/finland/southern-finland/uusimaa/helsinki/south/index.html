Introduction to South

South is a captivating region in Helsinki, located in Uusimaa, in Southern Finland, in Finland, in Nordic countries, in Europe. It is known for its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie looking to try new cuisines, South has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll give you everything you need to know to plan your trip to South, Helsinki.

List of Places to Visit in South

Are you wondering what places you should visit in South? Well, wonder no more! Below are some of the most popular places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Suomenlinna Fortress: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a must-visit when in South. The fortress is built on six islands and now serves as a popular tourist attraction.
  • National Museum of Finland: Delve into Finnish history and culture at this national museum.
  • Uspenski Cathedral: Visit the largest Orthodox church in Western Europe to witness its stunning architecture and enhance your cultural experience.
  • Helsinki Cathedral: This iconic architectural masterpiece is a symbol of Helsinki and definitely worth a visit.
  • The Ateneum: This art museum is home to some of the best Finnish artwork and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nuuksio National Park: This beautiful national park is perfect for hiking, camping and getting close to nature.
  • Seurasaari Open-Air Museum: Take in the stunning views and learn about Finland’s rural life and traditions.
  • Tervasaari Island: A historic island in the heart of Helsinki where visitors can enjoy a picnic or take in the stunning scenery.
  • Helsinki Zoo: Get up close and personal with animals from around the world here at Helsinki Zoo.
  • Esplanade Park: A beautiful park in the city where you can relax, enjoy live music performances, and grab a bite to eat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

If you’re a hiking or biking enthusiast, you’ll find plenty of breathtaking trails in South.

  • The Nuuksio National Park: From gentle lakeside walks to rugged backcountry hikes, the park offers a variety of trails for all skill levels. The Haukkalampi route is a popular option, as it’s an easy route and only takes about 2 hours to complete.
  • The Helsinki Archipelago: Take a bike ride through the beautiful archipelago of Helsinki and take in the stunning scenery. You can rent bikes from numerous places throughout Helsinki, including Kauppatori and nearby bike rental shops.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do and explore in South – here are some of the best:

  • Helsinki City Tour: Explore the city’s most popular sights, including the Helsinki Cathedral, Senate Square, the Esplanade, and more.
  • Finnish Sauna experience: Visit Löyly, a public sauna located by the seafront, for a truly authentic Finnish sauna experience.
  • Boat Tour of the Archipelago: Take in the stunning views of the archipelago of Helsinki on a boat tour.
  • Visit the Helsinki Market: The Helsinki Market is one of the best places to find local Finnish food, drinks, and crafts.
  • Day Trip to Tallinn: Take a ferry to Tallinn and explore the beautiful old town and its stunning architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Finnish cuisine is a must-try when visiting South. Here are some of the best local dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpiirakka: These are delicious savory pastries made out of rye crust that are filled with rice or potatoes.
  • Kallio-ravintolat: This is a traditional Finnish dish made with pork, liver, and spices, served with mashed potatoes.
  • Ruisleipä: A delicious rye bread that is served with butter, cheese or ham.
  • Leipäjuusto: A traditional Finnish cheese that is often served in cafes and restaurants around Helsinki.

Local Beverages

  • Olut: Finnish beer is a must-try when in Helsinki. Numerous breweries offer guided tours and tastings.
  • Lonkero: This is a local cocktail that is made from gin and grapefruit soda.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodation options in South, Helsinki, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Haven: This luxurious 5-star hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and is located in the heart of Helsinki.
  • Eurohostel: This budget-friendly hostel is a great option for travelers looking for affordable accommodation.
  • Scandic Park Helsinki: This 4-star hotel is located in the beautiful Töölö neighborhood of Helsinki and is known for its great breakfast and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Are you wondering what souvenirs and local products to bring home from Helsinki? Here are some of the best options:

  • Marimekko: A local design house that offers unique clothing and home decor items.
  • Iittala: A Finnish design company known for its iconic glassware.
  • Fazer chocolates: A beloved Finnish chocolate brand known for its delicious flavor and unique packaging.
  • Puukko knives: A traditional Finnish knife that is handmade by skilled craftsmen.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Finnish culture is characterized by its rich history and unique customs. Some of the most important customs to keep in mind are:

  • Respect for nature and the environment
  • The importance of saunas in Finnish culture
  • Punctuality and timeliness are highly valued
  • Finnish cuisine is often centered around fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and pleasant. The long daylight hours during the summer make it the ideal time for outdoor activities and exploring the city. However, winters in South are also a unique experience, as they offer the chance to experience plenty of winter sports and witness the unique Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Bring comfortable shoes as there is a lot of walking involved when exploring the city
  • Respect Finnish culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Be prepared to take off shoes when entering homes or businesses

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places
  • Smoke in prohibited areas
  • Forget to listen and follow the guide’s instructions during tours

Safety and Health

Safety in South

Helsinki is generally a safe place to visit, and violent crime is rare. However, some precautions to keep in mind include:

  • Petty theft can be a problem in tourist areas, so be mindful of your belongings.
  • Be careful when crossing the street, as traffic can be heavy.
  • Ask for assistance from the police or local authorities in case of emergency.

Health in South

Finland has a well-developed healthcare system, and travelers can get medical assistance or consultation if needed. It’s essential to bring your travel insurance, vaccines, and any medication you may need.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to South is easy, thanks to Helsinki’s well-developed transportation network. Here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip:

Airports Near South

Helsinki Airport is the primary airport in South, and it’s located about 20 km north of the city center. It is Finland’s largest airport, offering flights to numerous destinations around the globe.

Bus Stations Near South

If you’re traveling from other parts of Finland, you can take a bus to Helsinki’s central bus station, which is located in the heart of the city. There are also several local buses that serve different areas of South.

Other Options

Travelers can also get around South using Helsinki’s metro lines, trams, taxis, and shared bikes. Many tourists opt to rent a car to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in South

South has an extensive transportation system that includes trains, buses, and the metro. Helsinki’s public transportation offers affordable options to commute around the city and the surrounding regions. It is also bike-friendly, which means you can rent a bike to explore different parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Finland and nearby countries. Here are some of the most popular destinations to visit next:

  • Porvoo: A charming town known for its colorful wooden houses and historic cobblestone streets.
  • Turku: A beautiful city located on the west coast of Finland, known for its stunning architecture, including Turku Castle and Turku Cathedral.
  • Stockholm: Sweden’s vibrant capital is just a ferry ride away from Helsinki and is known for its scenic beauty, culture, and nightlife.

Conclusion

South, Helsinki is undoubtedly a fascinating destination with its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. This comprehensive tour guide should serve as a helpful resource to plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to South. So, pack your bags, and get ready to explore the wonders of Helsinki’s South.
