Introduction to West

Located in the southern part of Helsinki and surrounded by beautiful neighborhoods, West is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves culture, nature, and outdoor activities. With a blend of modern architecture and historical sites, West offers a unique and memorable experience for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in West

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Löyly - This modern waterfront structure offers visitors the chance to relax in a traditional Finnish sauna while enjoying the breathtaking views of the sea and the city.
  • Pihlajasaari Island - One of the most popular summer destinations for locals, this island offers beautiful beaches, nature trails, and historic sites.
  • Design District - This trendy area is home to a plethora of designer shops, art galleries, and museums that showcase Finnish design.
  • Finnish Museum of Photography - If you’re a fan of photography, this museum is a must-visit. It features a permanent exhibition of Finnish photography, as well as groundbreaking international exhibitions.
  • Military Museum - This museum showcases Finland’s military history and the role it has played within the country’s society.

Natural Attractions of West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seurasaari Open-Air Museum - This island museum showcases Finnish culture and traditions through exhibits of traditional houses and buildings that represent various periods of Finnish history.
  • Nuuksio National Park - A perfect spot for hiking, biking, and camping in the wilderness of West. Nuuksio features vast forests, lakes, and rugged terrain that provide visitors with a unique outdoor experience.
  • Hietaniemi Beach - A popular summer destination for both locals and visitors, this beach provides an excellent opportunity to relax and enjoy the cold waters of the Baltic sea.
  • Suomenlinna Fortress - A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Helsinki’s most popular tourist destinations, Suomenlinna offers a glimpse into Finnish history and culture.
  • Helsinki Zoo - Home to over 150 species of animals, including the critically endangered snow leopard, the Helsinki Zoo provides a great opportunity for visitors to learn about conservation and biodiversity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West

West’s outdoor attractions provide some of the best hiking and biking routes in the city. Enjoy trekking in Nuuksio National Park, or take a bike ride along the picturesque shoreline that runs through the heart of the West district.

Best Things to Do in West: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking - Kayaking tours that take visitors around the islands of Helsinki’s archipelago and along the scenic shoreline of West.
  • Boat Tours - Helsinki’s beautiful archipelago is best explored from the water, so if you’re visiting West, don’t miss the boat tour experience.
  • Brewery Tours - One of the most popular local activities in West is Brewery tours. These tours offer the chance to learn about the traditional Finnish beer-brewing process and taste some local craft beers.
  • Hop-on Hop-off Bus Tours - This is an excellent way to explore and get the best out of Helsinki’s top attractions.
  • Arts and Crafts Tours - West district is home to many local artisans, and visiting their studios and workshops is a unique way to experience Finnish culture.

Food and Drink Scene of West

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lingonberry Juice (Punaviini) - A traditional Finnish drink that is made from wild lingonberries. This juice has a bit of sweetness and tartness in it that makes it a perfect drink for all seasons.
  • Karjalanpiirakka - These traditional Karelian pastries are stuffed with rice and milk and served with egg butter on top.
  • Salmiakki - A candy that is made up of ammonium chloride, a unique and strong taste that some people love, and some don’t.
  • Sautéed Reindeer - A delicious dish made with smoked or salt-cured reindeer meat, sautéed with mushrooms, onions, and cream.
  • Fish soup - Finnish cuisine has a long history of seafood, and fish soup is a traditional dish that has been around for centuries.

Local Beverages

  • Lonkero (Long drink) - A popular local beverage made of gin and grapefruit soda, which was introduced in 1952.
  • Sahti - An ancient Finnish beer that is made up of juniper berries, with a slightly sour and sweet flavor.
  • Bilberry Liqueur - A sweet and fruity drink made from wild bilberries.
  • Koskenkorva Vodka - A popular vodka brand that originated from Koskenkorva, a small village in western Finland that has been producing vodka since 1952.

Where to Stay in West: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Radisson Blu Royal Hotel - This luxurious hotel in West has an elegant and contemporary setting. It’s located next to Kamppi, one of Helsinki’s busiest transportation hubs.
  • Hotel Klaus K - A centrally located luxurious hotel that is known for its proximity to designer boutiques, restaurants, and entertainment centers.
  • Hotel Finn - A small yet comfortable budget hotel located near Kamppi Square.
  • Glo Hotel Art - A friendly and modern hotel that is located near the Kamppi metro station.
  • Hostel Diana Park - This hostel is located in the historic building of Kamppi Station, with easy access to all of Helsinki’s main attractions.

What to Buy in West: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Marimekko Products - Marimekko, a Finnish textiles manufacturer, is world-famous for its bright color and vibrant designs.
  • Artek Furnishings - This brand is known for its elegant, modern, and functional furniture products.
  • Fazer Chocolates - Fazer is a Finnish chocolate brand that is known for its high-quality chocolates and confectionery products.
  • Lapland Reindeer Products - Reindeer leather and keratin art are known as some of the most popular souvenirs that you can buy in West.

Local Culture and Customs of West

Finnish customs and traditions have a long and rich history, and visitors to West can experience a number of local customs, including both old and new. In particular, Finland is well known for its sauna culture, with traditional saunas forming an essential part of Finnish life.

Best Time to Visit West

Summer, from June to August, is the best time to visit West, thanks to the warm weather and longer days. But winter is also an excellent time to see the city’s ice-covered waterways and snow-covered landscapes. During this time, the city is illuminated with beautiful lights and decorations, providing a truly magical experience for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of West

Do:

  • Try Finnish food and beverages, including traditional dishes.
  • Experience the sauna culture.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some Finnish greetings and phrases.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive, as it’s strictly forbidden by law.
  • Be loud and disrespectful, especially in public places.
  • Point with your fingers, as it’s considered rude in Finnish culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in West

West district is generally considered safe. However, visitors should take normal precautions against pickpocketing and petty crime. Also, when hiking in Nuuksio National Park, it’s important to stay on designated trails and be aware of wildlife.

Health in West

Finland has a world-class health care system that is accessible to both locals and visitors. The West district has several hospitals and clinics where visitors can seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to West: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West

The Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL) is the main airport serving Helsinki and the West district. It is located about 20 kilometers north of the city, and visitors can easily access West through public transportation or by taxi.

Bus Stations Near West

The Kamppi Bus terminal is the primary bus station in West. It services intercity buses to a variety of locations throughout Finland.

Other Options

  • Train: The Helsinki Central Railway Station connects West and other Finnish cities.
  • Metro: The West district is served by the Helsinki Metro, which provides access to several major attractions.
  • Tram: The tram system is a convenient way to get around Helsinki and the West district.

How to Get Around in West

The West district is well served by public transportation, including buses, trams, and the metro. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the area on their own.

Where to Go Next from West: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West’s central location makes it the perfect base for exploring nearby destinations such as Porvoo, Turku, and Tampere. Visitors can also take day trips to Tallinn, the capital of Estonia, or to the Finnish Lakeland, which is considered one of Europe’s most beautiful natural destinations.

Conclusion

Helsinki’s West district is a unique and charming part of the city that offers a mix of culture, nature, and outdoor activities. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous holiday, West has something for everyone. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
