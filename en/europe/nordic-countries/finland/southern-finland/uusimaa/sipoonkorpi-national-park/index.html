Your Ultimate Guide to Sipoonkorpi National Park

Sipoonkorpi National Park, located in Uusimaa, Southern Finland, is a haven for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Covering an area of 19 square kilometers, the park is home to rich biodiversity, picturesque landscapes, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or exploring the local history, this guide will help you plan your trip to Sipoonkorpi National Park.

Introduction to Sipoonkorpi National Park

Sipoonkorpi National Park was established in 2011 and is the newest national park in Finland. The park’s name comes from the Finnish word “korpi,” which means wilderness, and “Sipoo,” the name of the municipality where the park is situated. The park is easily accessible via public transportation and is a great day trip option from Helsinki, which is only 25 kilometers away.

The National Park offers an array of activities for tourists, including hiking, biking, fishing, and bird watching. Sipoonkorpi has a unique combination of old-growth forests, herb-rich forests, swamps, rocky hills, and streams. This diversity in topography makes it a popular destination among nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Sipoonkorpi National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gumbostrand Konst & Form - Visit the art museum, where you’ll find works of art from local artists

  2. Söderkulla Museum of Local History - Discover the lifestyle of the locals from the late 19th century till the present day

Natural Attractions of Sipoonkorpi National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lakes Porla and Holma - Popular for fishing and swimming, offers mesmerizing views in summer

  2. Oittaa Nature Trail - A 2.5 km trail that takes you through the scenic ridge forest

  3. Glims Farmstead Museum - Discover the lifestyle and history of the 19th century Finnish farmers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sipoonkorpi National Park

Sipoonkorpi has a network of over 50 km of marked trails, which provide you with ample opportunity to experience the beauty of the park. Here are some popular hike and bike routes:

  1. Herrojen maantie nature trail: A 5 km trail that takes you through a lush green landscape.

  2. Kurkijärvi nature trail: A 2 km route around the lake, perfect for bird-watching enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Sipoonkorpi National Park: Activities and Tours

From outdoor activities to art museums, Sipoonkorpi National Park has something for everyone. Here are some of the most popular things to do and tours:

  1. Archipelago tour: Discover the Finnish archipelago in a guided boat tour.

  2. Fishing Tours: Enjoy freshwater fishing in Lake Porkkalanniemi and Lake Holma, both within the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sipoonkorpi National Park

If you have a week to spare, here’s an itinerary for Sipoonkorpi National Park:

Day 1: Visit Jokiniemi nature trail and Porla lakeside

Day 2: Take a guided bike tour and explore the lakes and forests

Day 3: Visit the Gumbostrand Art Museum and explore the Koukkusaari bird-watching tower

Day 4: Hike the Porla-Gunillantie Trail while bird-watching, then visit the Söderkulla Local History Museum.

Day 5-6: Relax in one of the lakeside cabins rented in the park and enjoy the scenic beauty around you.

Food and Drink Scene of Sipoonkorpi National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Karelian pies: Traditional Finnish pies made with rice, milk, and eggs, topped with a dollop of butter.

  2. Grilled Salmon: Fresh salmon grilled over open flames

Local Beverages

  1. Finnish craft beer: Sip on locally brewed craft beer known for its unique flavors and styles.

  2. Cloudberry liqueur: A Finnish delicacy made by mixing berries with vodka.

Where to Stay in Sipoonkorpi National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sipoonkorpi National Park offers a variety of accommodation options, including cottages, hostels, and campsites:

  1. Guesthouses - A great option for those looking for a comfortable and cozy stay.

  2. Cottages - Sipoonkorpi National Park offers well-equipped cottages for rent.

What to Buy in Sipoonkorpi National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sipoonkorpi National Park is well-known for its natural beauty and craft. Here are some souvenirs to bring home:

  1. Honey - Locally produced honey is a must-buy souvenir.

  2. Finnish handcrafted items - Finnish local crafts are known for their elegance and beauty, such as pottery, glassware, or wooden bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Sipoonkorpi National Park

Finnish culture is well-preserved in Sipoonkorpi National Park. When visiting Finland, remember to greet people with a smile, and do visit the museums and cultural places of the National Park.

Best Time to Visit Sipoonkorpi National Park

The best time to visit Sipoonkorpi National Park is during the summer season, from May to August, which is the peak tourist season. However, for winter spor enthusiasts, from the month of November to March, it is a great place for winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sipoonkorpi National Park

Do:

  1. Follow the designated hiking trails for your safety.

  2. Respect the flora and fauna of the park and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t leave the designated trail pathways to preserve the natural beauty of the park.

  2. Don’t exceed the listed maximum occupancy regulations for the park’s cabins and cottages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sipoonkorpi National Park

Sipoonkorpi National Park is considered safe for visitors. However, some safety tips to keep in mind include:

  1. Stay hydrated and bring enough water during your hikes.

  2. Always carry a map of the park area so you won’t get lost.

Health in Sipoonkorpi National Park

Tap water in Finland is safe to drink, and medical facilities offer the highest levels of care for tourists in the country.

Getting to Sipoonkorpi National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sipoonkorpi National Park

Helsinki-Vantaa International Airport is the closest airport to Sipoonkorpi National Park. From there, you can take a bus to the park through the Helsinki bus terminal or a taxi.

Bus Stations Near Sipoonkorpi National Park

There are daily buses from Helsinki Central Railway Station to Sipoonkorpi National Park.

Other Options

Another popular way to reach the park is by cycling from Helsinki.

How to Get Around in Sipoonkorpi National Park

The best way to explore Sipoonkorpi National Park is on foot or by bike. The park offers well-marked trails for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Sipoonkorpi National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

A visit to the capital city, Helsinki, is highly recommended, as it is only a 30-minute drive from the park, and has plenty to check out, such as the modern architecture in the city center and the sunny waterside restaurants by the Helsinki Market Square.

Conclusion

Sipoonkorpi National Park offers a chance to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Finland. This guide provides insights on the best things to do and see in Sipoonkorpi National Park, and we hope it helps you plan your trip to this beautiful destination.
