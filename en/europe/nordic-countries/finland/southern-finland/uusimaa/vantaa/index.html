Introduction to Vantaa

Vantaa is a city in Uusimaa, Southern Finland, which is part of the Nordic countries in Europe. The city is home to around 235,000 people and is the fourth-largest city in Finland, offering a mix of urban and natural attractions that visitors will love. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s rich cultural history or enjoy the great outdoors, Vantaa has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Vantaa

When planning a trip to Vantaa, make sure to include these top attractions in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Finnish Aviation Museum: The museum showcases the history of aviation in Finland with a range of aircraft and items that cover the country’s military and civil aviation.
  • Heureka, The Finnish Science Centre: Heureka offers an interactive experience through the world of science, featuring exhibitions and events that suit visitors of all ages.
  • Jumbo Shopping Centre: The biggest shopping mall in the Nordic countries which holds fashion, electronics, various restaurants and leisure activities.
  • Hämeenkylän Kartano Manor: A beautiful manor house where you can explore the history of Vantaa.
  • St. Lawrence Church: The oldest building in Vantaa that still serves its original purpose as a place of worship. Built in the 1460s and re-built several times since.

Natural Attractions of Vantaa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sipoonkorpi National Park: A perfect destination for hiking and observing wildlife, with a diverse array of flora and fauna.
  • Vanha Rauma: A well-preserved site exhibiting the old Finnish countryside life.
  • The African Safari: The park is located in nearby Lapland and offers an opportunity to observe different animals, including reindeer, polar bears, wolves, and others. If you’re looking for a unique adventure, an African Safari is perfect for you.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vantaa

Vantaa has many trails for hiking and biking that offer stunning views of the local landscape and nature. We recommend taking a walk around the Vantaanjoki river, enjoying sights of lake and forests as well as small alcoves for BBQ and resting. If you’re looking for a more challenging hike, make sure to check out the trails in Sipoonkorpea National Park.

Best Things to Do in Vantaa: Activities and Tours

Besides the natural wonders and historical sites, Vantaa has a diverse range of activities and tours to add to your itinerary. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Visit the Spa and Indoor Waterpark: Flamingo Spa is the perfect place to relax and enjoy indoor and outdoor water activities.
  • Kuusijärvi Lake: One of the most popular destinations in Vantaa. During the summer months, to blend in with the locals, spend a day by the lake swimming, enjoying a BBQ, or sunbathing.
  • Rush Trampoline Park: A fun activity for visitors of all ages, with trampolines, foam pits, ninja obstacle courses, and bouldering walls.
  • Go Ice Skating: For winter travelers in Vantaa, there are a variety of places to go ice skating, including the K-Supermarket Ice Rink.
  • Visit the Vantaa Nigthly Market: Experience Vantaa’s popular Nightly Market for local street food, drinks, and vendors from around the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Vantaa

Vantaa has a range of traditional Finnish foods and international cuisine that visitors will love. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lohikeitto: A traditional Finnish soup made with salmon, potatoes, and cream.
  • Karjalanpaisti: A beef stew made in a cast-iron pot with root vegetables and spices.
  • Ruisleipa: A dark, dense Finnish bread made with rye flour.
  • Karelian pastries/Karjalanpiirakat: pastry made from rye flour and filled with smashed potatoes and typically topped with butter or egg butter.

Local Beverages

  • Finnish Salmiakki Liquor: A sweet and salty alcoholic beverage made with salmiakki, a popular Finnish candy.
  • Hevossalmiakki: A strong liquorice-flavored bittersweet candy.
  • Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka used in various cocktails and drinks.

Where to Stay in Vantaa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vantaa offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers, from hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the most popular hotels include:

  • Clarion Hotel Helsinki Airport: Located in the Helsinki-Vantaa Airport, close to the railway station and Jumbo Mall.
  • Hilton Helsinki Airport: Affordable yet luxurious hotel next to the airport.
  • Forenom Serviced Apartments: A luxurious, sleek, and modern choice which is city-centre situated.
  • Scandic Aviacongress: Located just a few minutes from the Helsinki-Vantaa Airport, offering free shuttle service.

What to Buy in Vantaa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Vantaa, don’t forget to check out these local products:

  • Marimekko: A Finnish design company that specializes in home furnishings, textiles, and fashion.
  • Fazer Chocolate: A world-famous chocolate company that originated in Finland.
  • Nokia Phones: The world-famous Finnish phone manufacturer.
  • Iittala Art Glass: High-quality glassware designed and manufactured in Finland.

Local Culture and Customs of Vantaa

Finland has a unique culture, and it’s essential to respect the locals and their customs when visiting. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect for nature: Finns have a strong connection to nature, so it’s essential to keep the country clean and not to litter.
  • Quietness and personal space: Finns highly value personal space and quietness. Try to avoid being too loud in public places or on public transportation.
  • “Löyly” experience: sauna practices are important in Finnish culture, and visiting saunas is a typical pastime for Finns.

Best Time to Visit Vantaa

The best time to visit Vantaa is in the summer, from June to August. During the summer months, you can enjoy the outdoor activities without worrying about cold weather. However, winter in Vantaa has some magical events during Christmas time. Make sure to check up on the events list of Vantaa when planning your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vantaa

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Vantaa:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes: In Finland, it’s common to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home, sauna, or other indoor spaces.
  • Get a travel card: To use public transportation throughout Vantaa, make sure to get a travel card to save money.
  • Drink tap water: Tap water in Finland is of high quality and safe to drink.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: Tipping in Finland isn’t customary, as servers, guides, or drivers receive a living wage.
  • Be late: Finns are punctual, and it’s essential to keep to schedules in Vantaa.
  • Underdress: Although the weather can be pleasant during the summer months, remember to bring along warm clothes and rain gear as the weather can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Vantaa, it’s essential to know how to stay safe and healthy during your trip.

Safety in Vantaa

Vantaa is generally a safe city for travelers. However, you should still take precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings in busy areas, avoiding walking alone at night, and being aware of your surroundings. Additionally, pay attention to weather forecasts before going outdoors as Finnish weather is known to be unpredictable.

Health in Vantaa

Finland offers high-quality medical care, both public and private, but we recommend having comprehensive travel insurance that includes health coverage. Additionally, make sure to pack any necessary medication and check with your doctor before your trip.

Getting to Vantaa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Overall, Vantaa has a very extensive public transportation system, which is user-friendly and easy to navigate. Here are the options to get to Vantaa:

Airports Near Vantaa

Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL) is the nearest major airport to Vantaa, only 6km away.

Bus Stations Near Vantaa

The main bus terminal in Vantaa is in the Tikkurila suburb, which is easily accessible by commuter trains from Helsinki city centre.

Other Options

Vantaa can also be reached by train from Helsinki city centre, and if you’re coming from Tallinn, you can take a ferry to Helsinki and then make your way to Vantaa.

How to Get Around in Vantaa

Vantaa has an excellent public transportation system, consisting of buses, trains, trams, and metro lines. A travel card can be bought for a day, week, or month and will allow access to all types of transport. Additionally, bikes and e-scooters can be rented at various locations throughout the city if you crave a more mobile exploration.

Where to Go Next from Vantaa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning a longer trip in Finland, make sure to check out these nearby destinations:

  • Helsinki: The capital city of Finland is only a short train ride away from Vantaa and is home to an array of attractions, such as the world-famous Suomenlinna Fortress and many more.
  • Porvoo: A picturesque town situated in the south of Finland, known for its charming wooden architecture, boutique shops, and unique history.
  • Espoo: The second-largest city in Finland, Espoo is situated nearby to Vantaa and is known for its beautiful scenery and architecture.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Vantaa is a fantastic destination for travelers who are looking to explore the beauty of Finland’s cities and nature alike. Whether you want to experience Finnish customs and culture or indulge in Finnish food and drink, Vantaa has something for everyone. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has given you enough information to plan your trip confidently and make your visit to Vantaa a memorable one.
