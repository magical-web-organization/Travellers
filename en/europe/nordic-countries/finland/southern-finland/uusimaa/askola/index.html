Located in Uusimaa, Southern Finland, Askola is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and plenty of outdoor adventures, this town is perfect for travelers who are looking for a peaceful retreat. This comprehensive travel guide covers everything you need to know about Askola, from top attractions to hiking and biking routes, food and drink recommendations, and cultural insights.

Introduction to Askola

Askola is a small town situated on the banks of the Porvoonjoki river, surrounded by lush forests and scenic lakes. The town has a rich history dating back to the Viking era, and its cultural heritage is evident in its museums and historic landmarks. With a population of just over 5,000, Askola is a peaceful and tranquil destination, far from the hustle and bustle of big cities.

List of Places to Visit in Askola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Askola Church: Built-in 1775, Askola Church is a cultural landmark that attracts visitors from all over the world. The church is known for its beautiful architecture, intricate wood carvings, and stunning paintings.
  2. Museum of Askola: The Museum of Askola is a small yet informative museum that showcases the town’s history and cultural heritage. The museum has a collection of artifacts from the Viking era to the present day, including traditional costumes, weapons, and tools.
  3. Havukosken vanha mylly: This picturesque old mill is one of the oldest in Finland, dating back to the 17th century. The mill is still operational, and visitors can see the traditional milling process in action.

Natural Attractions of Askola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Päijänne National Park: This vast national park is home to some of Finland’s most breathtaking natural scenery, including lakes, forests, and wetlands. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, and kayaking in the park.
  2. Itämeren Uniikki Nature Trail: This scenic nature trail is perfect for hiking and biking, offering stunning views of the Porvoonjoki river and surrounding forests. The trail is easy to navigate and suitable for all skill levels.
  3. Hevonlinna: This historic fortification is a popular destination for hikers, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Askola

  1. Porvoonjoki Bike Route: This 12 km bike route follows the banks of the Porvoonjoki river, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. Itämeren Uniikki Nature Trail: This 7 km trail is perfect for hiking and biking, offering breathtaking views of the Porvoonjoki river and surrounding forests.
  3. Kalkkiruukin luontopolku: This 5 km nature trail takes hikers through the beautiful forests surrounding Askola, with plenty of opportunities to see local wildlife and plant life.

Best Things to Do in Askola: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing on the Porvoonjoki River: The Porvoonjoki river is one of the best places in Finland for fishing, with a variety of fish species available, including salmon, trout, and pike.
  2. Kayaking in Päijänne National Park: Päijänne National Park is an excellent destination for kayaking, with plenty of stunning lakes and waterways to explore.
  3. Visiting the Local Markets: Askola has a variety of local markets selling fresh produce, handicrafts, and souvenirs. The markets are a great place to meet locals and get a taste of Finnish culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Askola

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kalakukko: This traditional Finnish dish is a type of pie filled with fish and potatoes, baked inside a rye bread crust.
  2. Poronkäristys: Poronkäristys is a popular Finnish dish made with reindeer meat, served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce.
  3. Vorschmack: Vorschmack is a savory meat stew made with beef, lamb, and herring, served with pickled cucumbers and mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  1. Lonkero: Lonkero is a traditional Finnish drink made with grapefruit soda and gin, and is a popular choice among locals.
  2. Koskenkorva: Koskenkorva is a Finnish vodka made from barley and is a popular choice for cocktails.

Where to Stay in Askola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Hirvihaaran Kartano: This charming hotel is located in a renovated 19th-century manor house, offering guests a unique and luxurious experience in the heart of Askola.
  2. Aittakoli: This rustic guesthouse offers comfortable and affordable accommodation, with easy access to all of Askola’s top attractions.
  3. Pihlajamäen lomamökit: These cozy cottages are perfect for visitors who want a more secluded and peaceful retreat, surrounded by the natural beauty of Askola.

What to Buy in Askola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts: Askola is known for its traditional handicrafts, including wood carvings, knitted goods, and ceramic pottery.
  2. Berries: Finland is famous for its wild berries, including lingonberries, blueberries, and cloudberries. Visitors can buy fresh berries at local markets or pick them themselves in the surrounding forests.
  3. Local Spirits: Finland is home to many excellent breweries and distilleries, and visitors can bring home a bottle of local gin, vodka, or beer as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Askola

Finland is a country with strong cultural traditions, and Askola is no exception. Visitors to Askola should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Removing shoes before entering a home or other buildings
  • Observing silence during saunas
  • Respecting personal space and avoiding physical contact
  • Tipping is not expected in Finland

Best Time to Visit Askola

The best time to visit Askola is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the days are long. The winter months can be quite cold and dark, with limited daylight hours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Askola

Do:

  • Try traditional Finnish food and drink
  • Visit local markets and buy souvenirs
  • Explore the natural beauty of Päijänne National Park
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Make excessive noise, especially late at night
  • Ignore traditional customs and etiquette
  • Disrespect wildlife or the natural environment
  • Leave trash or litter in public spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Askola

Askola is a safe and peaceful town with low crime rates. Visitors should still take basic precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping an eye on personal belongings.

Health in Askola

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and Askola has several medical clinics and hospitals available. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Askola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Askola

The closest international airport to Askola is Helsinki-Vantaa Airport, located approximately 70 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a train or bus to Askola.

Bus Stations Near Askola

There are several bus stations serving Askola, including the Pohjolan Liikenne bus station and the OnniBus stop. Visitors can take buses from Helsinki or other nearby towns to reach Askola.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or bicycle to explore Askola and the surrounding areas. Taxis and ridesharing services are also available.

How to Get Around in Askola

Askola is a small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. Buses and taxis are also available for those who need them.

Where to Go Next from Askola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Porvoo: The charming town of Porvoo is just a short drive from Askola, offering visitors a chance to explore stunning architecture and peaceful waterfront views.
  2. Helsinki: The capital city of Finland is located just over an hour’s drive from Askola, offering a wide range of cultural attractions, museums, and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Askola is a unique and beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Southern Finland. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, historic landmarks, or delicious food and drink, Askola has something to offer every traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re well on your way to planning an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem of Finland.
