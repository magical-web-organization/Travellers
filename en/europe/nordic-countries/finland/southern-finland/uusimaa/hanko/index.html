Introduction to Hanko

Nestled in the southernmost part of Finland lies the charming coastal town of Hanko. Also known as Hangö in Swedish, the town boasts a rich history and a breathtaking natural landscape. Hanko is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and those seeking a peaceful retreat away from the bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Hanko

Here are the top places you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hanko Museum: Learn about the history of Hanko, from its days as a thriving spa town to its role in the Winter War and Continuation War. The museum houses artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that highlight the town’s past.

  • Hanko Coastal Artillery Museum: Explore the underground tunnels, gun emplacements, and other military relics from the 20th century that were once used to protect the town.

  • Bengtskär Lighthouse: Take a day trip to the nearby island of Bengtskär and visit the tallest lighthouse in the Nordic countries. The lighthouse showcases exceptional views of the Baltic Sea and the surrounding landscapes.

Natural Attractions of Hanko: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hanko Beach: Experience relaxation on Hanko’s sandy beach, with a little bit of sun and sea breeze to refresh your day.

  • Tulliniemi Beach: For those looking to explore outside of town, this quiet beach is known for its turquoise waters, golden sand, and tranquil environment.

  • Bellevue Park: A must-visit spot for anyone seeking gorgeous views of the Hanko peninsula, the sea, and the town itself. Don’t forget to pack a picnic and enjoy the moment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanko

Hanko’s nature is best explored on foot or bike, with several fantastic routes to choose from:

  • Tulliniemi Nature Trail: A 5-kilometer trail that winds through the dunes, marshlands, and forests around Tulliniemi Beach.

  • Hankoniemi Trail: A 13-kilometer trail that takes hikers through the woods and along the beautiful rocky coast, where you can spot diverse wildlife and birds such as the Osprey eagle.

Best Things to Do in Hanko: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Sea-kayaking: Explore the caves, beaches, and rugged coastline of Hanko on an adventurous sea-kayaking tour. You can get close and personal with the resident harbor seals and nesting birds.

  • Sailing Tours: Charter a sailboat and explore the archipelago surrounding Hanko, taking in the beautiful scenery from the sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Hanko

Hanko offers an excellent selection of culinary delights highlighting fresh and local ingredients. Here are some dishes and drinks to try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hanko Shrimp Sandwiches: A classic seafood dish of street foods vendors on the Hanko Harbor.

  • Rökeri’s Fish Platter: Sample the daily fresh smoked fish at the famous market straight from the smokehouse.

Local Beverages

  • Hanko Sisu Beer: Produced locally and named after the Finnish spirit of perseverance, this refreshing beer is the perfect choice after a long day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Hanko: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury to budget-friendly, Hanko has plenty of accommodations to suit all types of travelers:

  • Hotel Regatta: A stylish waterfront hotel with modern amenities and secluded rooms with balconies overlooking the sea.

  • Guesthouse Villa Maija: A restored 19th-century seaside villa that provides cozy and comfortable rooms with a homey atmosphere.

  • Hanko Seafront Apartments: Fully equipped, stylishly furnished apartments that offer flexible options for solo travelers, couples, families, and groups.

What to Buy in Hanko: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Herring: A specialty of the region which you should buy directly from the local markets.

  • Marimekko Textiles: A timelessly classy and vibrant fabric manufactured in Hanko and sold around the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanko

The locals of Hanko are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Finnish is the official language, but Swedish is also widely spoken. The culture of Hanko is heavily influenced by its maritime history and the surrounding nature.

Best Time to Visit Hanko

The ideal time to visit Hanko is during summertime, from June to August, where you’ll be able to enjoy the sunny weather and a range of outdoor activities provided by the sea. Hanko’s popularity surges during the mid-July harbor festival, where visitors can enjoy live concerts, food markets, and sales of local produced goods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanko

Do:

  • Respect Finnish customs and traditions, including removing shoes when entering homes and saunas.

  • Take a dip in the refreshing waters of the Baltic Sea, but be cautious of strong waves and rip currents.

Don’t:

  • Be noisy and disturb the peace of Hanko’s small and calm neighborhoods

  • Litter or damage the environment in the beautiful landscapes of Hanko.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanko

Hanko is a safe place for travelers with low crime rates and virtually no violent crimes. However, always take the necessary precautions to secure your belongings, such as keeping your valuables safe, not leaving them unattended, and being cautious in crowded places.

Health in Hanko

Travelers from EU nations and other countries with reciprocal health agreements are entitled to receive free medical treatment in Finland if they are in possession of a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC). The nearest medical center servicing tourists is in Lohja, about an hour drive away.

Getting to Hanko: Transportation Options and Logistics

You can reach Hanko by various modes of transportation from major cities around Finland.

Airports Near Hanko

Helsinki International airport, located about 125 KM away, is the nearest airport from Hanko.

Bus Stations Near Hanko

Regular scheduled buses to Hanko from major cities such as Turku, Tampere, and Helsinki.

Other Options

Rental cars and private taxis services are also available for those looking for a more flexible and private travel experience.

How to Get Around in Hanko

Walking and cycling are the best ways to explore Hanko itself, where the town is small enough to be navigated on foot, and bike rental is available for those exploring the parks and trails further out.

Where to Go Next from Hanko: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore the surrounding area, these are some of the must-see destinations:

  • Turku: A picturesque city that boasts a charming old town, stunning architecture, and excellent food.

  • The Archipelago National Park: A stunning collection of rich landscapes and natural scenery featuring islands, forests, swamps, and rocky cliffs.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an outdoor adventure, Hanko offers the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life. Dive into history, explore nature, and indulge in the culinary delights of this beautiful coastal town.
