Introduction to Loviisa

Nestled in the heart of Uusimaa, in Southern Finland, Loviisa is a charming town that boasts rich cultural and natural attractions. The town is known for its red-brick buildings and picturesque cobblestone streets. It’s a great destination for those who love exploring off-the-beaten-path locations.

In this Loviisa travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, activities to do, where to eat and drink, and where to stay. We’ll also discuss local customs, do’s and don’ts, and important safety considerations.

List of Places to Visit in Loviisa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Loviisa prides itself on its cultural heritage and history. One of the best ways to experience the town’s rich history is by visiting its museums and historical places.

1. Loviisa Maritime Museum

Located on the shore of the Gulf of Finland, the Loviisa Maritime Museum provides a glimpse into the town’s seafaring past. The museum has impressive exhibitions, including a large collection of ship models, paintings, and photographs.

2. Loviisa Museum

Housed in a beautifully restored 18th-century building, the Loviisa Museum tells the story of the town’s history from the 1800s to the present day. Visitors can explore the exhibition rooms, which contain everything from period costumes to vintage photographs.

Natural Attractions of Loviisa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Loviisa’s natural beauty cannot be understated. The picturesque forests, pristine lakes, and rugged coastline make it a paradise for lovers of the great outdoors.

1. Valkom Harbour

Valkom Harbour is a picturesque fishing village located on the coast. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the harbor to enjoy views of the sea and the colorful boats.

2. Strömfors Outdoor Recreation Area

Strömfors Outdoor Recreation Area is a peaceful nature reserve that boasts miles of hiking trails, scenic views, and even a small beach. It’s an ideal spot for a picnic or to enjoy a relaxing day out in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Loviisa

Loviisa has plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer both scenic views and an opportunity to explore the town’s cultural and historical attractions.

1. Kuninkaantie Road

The Kuninkaantie Road is a 17th-century royal road that was built to connect Turku and St. Petersburg. The route covers a distance of 80km and is perfect for a day-long bike ride.

2. Loviisa Coastal Route

The Loviisa Coastal Route is a 40km-long hiking and biking track that winds its way along the town’s coastline. The route offers breathtaking views of the Gulf of Finland and passes by many important cultural and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Loviisa: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities and tours in Loviisa. Here are a few things you can enjoy during your visit:

1. Loviisa Market Square

The Loviisa Market Square is a charming place where visitors can explore the local shops, boutiques, and restaurants. It’s also the venue for the town’s farmers’ market that takes place every Saturday.

2. Kayaking in the Gulf of Finland

Experience the beauty of the Gulf of Finland from a different perspective by kayaking along its picturesque coastlines. There are many guided tours available in Loviisa.

Food and Drink Scene of Loviisa

Loviisa’s food and drink scene is worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Karelian Pies

Karelian Pies are a Finnish speciality made of thin rye crusts that are filled with rice or mashed potatoes. They’re typically eaten warm with a dollop of butter on top.

2. Cabbage Rolls

Cabbage Rolls are another Finnish classic made of boiled cabbage leaves stuffed with ground beef or pork, rice, onions, and sometimes spices.

Local Beverages

1. Lapin Kulta

Lapin Kulta is a Finnish beer that’s brewed in Lapland. It’s a refreshing lager with a light taste and soft bitterness.

2. Long Drink

The Long Drink is a popular alcoholic beverage in Finland that was originally created for the 1952 Summer Olympics in Helsinki. It’s made of gin and grapefruit soda and is both refreshing and potent.

Where to Stay in Loviisa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Loviisa has a good selection of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the town:

1. Hotel Degerby

Hotel Degerby is a stylish hotel located in the heart of Loviisa’s historical town center. The rooms are modern, comfortable, and offer beautiful views of the town.

2. Villa Lovisa

If you’re looking for a more intimate experience, Villa Lovisa is the perfect choice. This charming guesthouse is located in a peaceful neighborhood and offers comfortable rooms with traditional Finnish decor.

What to Buy in Loviisa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Loviisa has a thriving artisan community, and there’s plenty to choose from in terms of souvenirs and local products. Here are some of our recommendations:

1. Loviisa Rye Bread

Loviisa Rye Bread is a local speciality that’s been a staple of the town’s cuisine for centuries. It’s made with rye flour and has a rich, savory flavor that’s a perfect accompaniment to any meal.

2. Handcrafted Glassware

Loviisa’s artisan community also specializes in handcrafted glassware, including beautiful vases, glasses, and other decorative items. They make for great gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Loviisa

Loviisa is a small town with a strong sense of community, and visitors can expect a warm welcome. Finns are generally reserved people, but they’re friendly and polite, so it’s always a good idea to reciprocate the warmth.

Best Time to Visit Loviisa

The best time to visit Loviisa is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, if you’re looking to experience the town’s winter wonderland, the winter months (December-February) are also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Loviisa

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Loviisa:

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Explore the town by foot or bike.
  • Try some of the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive, especially late at night.
  • Leave trash or litter on the streets.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Loviisa

Loviisa is a safe town, and visitors should have no reason to worry about safety during their stay. However, it’s always a good idea to take the usual precautions, such as avoiding dark alleys at night and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Loviisa

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect excellent care in case of any health emergencies. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Loviisa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Loviisa can be easily accessed by various modes of transport including:

Airports Near Loviisa

The closest airports to Loviisa are Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (75km) and Porvoo Airport (35km).

Bus Stations Near Loviisa

There are several bus stations located near Loviisa, including the Porvoo Bus Station (34km) and the Helsinki Bus Station (85km).

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Loviisa is located just off the E18 highway, making it easily accessible by car. Visitors can also take a train to Loviisa from Helsinki, but it’s not a direct route and requires a transfer.

How to Get Around in Loviisa

The best way to get around Loviisa is by foot or bike. The town is small enough for visitors to easily explore on foot, and there are plenty of bike rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Loviisa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Loviisa can also explore the nearby destinations, such as Helsinki (85km), Porvoo (34km), and Kotka (51km).

Conclusion

Loviisa is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be discovered. From its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty to its charming atmosphere and friendly locals, there’s something for everyone in this lovely town. So why not start planning your visit today and experience all that Loviisa has to offer?
