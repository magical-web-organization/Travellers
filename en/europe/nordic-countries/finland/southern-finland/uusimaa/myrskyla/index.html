Introduction to Myrskylä

Myrskylä is a charming municipality located in the Uusimaa region of Southern Finland. This small town is known for its peaceful environment and natural beauty, making it the perfect destination for travelers seeking a break from the bustling city life.

Myrskylä is a combination of traditional Finnish culture and modern amenities, making it a unique destination that caters to all types of travelers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or peaceful relaxation, Myrskylä has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Myrskylä

If you’re planning a trip to Myrskylä, here are some of the top places and attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Myrskylä Local History Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Myrskylä through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Hirvihaaran Kartano: This historic manor house has been restored and turned into a museum, showcasing local history and culture.
  • Myrskylä Church: This beautiful wooden church dates back to the 18th century and is known for its stunning frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Myrskylä: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Repovesi National Park: This national park offers stunning scenery, hiking trails, and opportunities for kayaking and fishing.
  • Huhtiniemi Nature Reserve: A beautiful nature reserve offering a peaceful environment and scenic lake views.
  • Lohjanjärvi Lake: An excellent place for swimming and boating, surrounded by stunning forests and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myrskylä

Myrskylä is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with many stunning hiking trails and bike routes. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Natura Trail: This 5-kilometer hiking trail takes you through the beautiful forests and natural landscapes of Myrskylä.
  • Linnunrata Trail: A 10-kilometer trail that takes you through the stunning countryside of Myrskylä.
  • Tuusula Lake Trail: A 27-kilometer trail that offers fantastic views of the lake and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Myrskylä: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore the calm waters of the nearby lakes and rivers.
  • Sauna Experience: Enjoy the traditional Finnish sauna experience by renting a private sauna or visiting a public sauna.
  • Berry Picking: During the summer season, Myrskylä is filled with delicious berries such as blueberries, lingonberries, and raspberries. Join a local tour to experience berry picking with a local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Myrskylä

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Myrskylä:

Day 1: Arrive in Myrskylä and explore the town center. Day 2: Visit the Myrskylä Local History Museum and Hirvihaaran Kartano. Day 3: Hike the Natura Trail and relax in a traditional Finnish sauna. Day 4: Explore the nearby Repovesi National Park and go kayaking on the lake. Day 5: Bike along the stunning Tuusula Lake Trail and enjoy a picnic by the lake. Day 6: Join a local berry picking tour and enjoy the delicious local produce. Day 7: Depart from Myrskylä and continue your travels in Finland.

Food and Drink Scene of Myrskylä

Finnish cuisine has many delicious specialties that are a must-try when visiting Myrskylä.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish dish made with fish and rye bread.
  • Karjalanpaisti: A meat stew made with beef and pork.
  • Lihapiirakka: A savory Finnish pastry filled with beef or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka distilled from barley and malt.
  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made with juniper berries and malted barley.

Where to Stay in Myrskylä: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Myrskylä has a wide range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences.

  • Hotel Hirvihaaran Kartano: A historic hotel located in a restored manor house.
  • Myrskylän Helmi: A cozy guesthouse located in the town center.
  • Hostel Salmiakki: A budget-friendly hostel located in a tranquil forest setting.

What to Buy in Myrskylä: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Myrskylä, there are several local products that make excellent souvenirs to bring home.

  • Handmade Wood Crafts: Myrskylä is known for its talented craftsmen, who produce beautiful wooden crafts such as bowls, candleholders, and serving trays.
  • Local Berries: During the summer season, there are many delicious berries available for purchase, including blueberries, raspberries, and lingonberries.
  • Juniper Products: Juniper is a traditional Finnish ingredient used in many foods, beverages, and beauty products. Look for juniper soap, candles, and essential oils as a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Myrskylä

When visiting Myrskylä, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions.

  • Sauna Culture: Sauna is an essential part of Finnish culture, and visitors are encouraged to experience a sauna during their stay in Myrskylä.
  • Silence and Respect for Nature: Finnish culture places a high value on silence and respect for nature. When visiting parks and natural areas, visitors are asked to speak softly and avoid littering.

Best Time to Visit Myrskylä

The best time to visit Myrskylä is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is the warmest, and the outdoor activities and attractions are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myrskylä

Do:

  • Respect Finnish culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the stunning natural beauty by taking part in outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and kayaking.
  • Try traditional Finnish cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the peace and quiet of nature by being loud or littering.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Myrskylä

Myrskylä is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a peaceful environment.

Health in Myrskylä

Finland has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical care if needed. Make sure to have travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Myrskylä: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Myrskylä

  • Helsinki-Vantaa airport is the closest airport, located approximately 75 km away from Myrskylä.

Bus Stations Near Myrskylä

  • Myrskylä bus station is located in the town center, and visitors can access regular bus services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also access Myrskylä by car, with ample parking available throughout the town center.

How to Get Around in Myrskylä

The best way to get around Myrskylä is by bike or car. Visitors can rent bikes or cars at local rental companies in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Myrskylä: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lohja: A vibrant city located approximately 50 km away, known for its beautiful lakes and cultural attractions.
  • Porvoo: A charming historic town located approximately 80 km away, known for its picturesque old town and beautiful river views.

Conclusion

Myrskylä is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of Finnish culture, natural beauty, and modern amenities. Whether you’re seeking relaxation, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, this charming town in Southern Finland has something for everyone to enjoy.
