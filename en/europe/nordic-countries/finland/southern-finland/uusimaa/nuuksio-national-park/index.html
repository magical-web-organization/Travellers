Introduction

Nuuksio National Park is a stunning natural paradise located in Uusimaa, Southern Finland. Spread across 53 square kilometers, the park is home to an abundance of wildlife and offers visitors a chance to get up close with nature. The park is a popular destination for hiking, biking and camping enthusiasts, as well as for those seeking a peaceful retreat in the wilderness. In this guide, we’ll explore Nuuksio National Park’s cultural and natural attractions, the best things to do, where to stay, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Nuuksio National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Haltia Nature Centre: This impressive eco-museum features interactive exhibits and displays showcasing the natural and cultural history of Finland.
  • The Finnish Nature Centre Haltia’s Exhibition: This exhibition showcases the Finland’s wildlife, natural environment, and the National Parks of Finland.

Natural Attractions of Nuuksio National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kattila Ponds: A serene spot in the park that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest and landscape.
  • The Haukkalampi Lake: A picturesque lake, which is surrounded by scenic hiking trails and perfect for a refreshing dip in the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nuuksio National Park

  • Iso-Paljo Circuit: This 12.5-kilometer trail through the park takes in some of Nuuksio’s most spectacular scenery and can vary from intermediate to strenuous depending on the trail.
  • Korpinkierros Trail: This 3-kilometer trail winds through the forest and takes hikers past the park’s Korpinkierros Nature Trail and a number of cultural and natural sites.

Best Things to Do in Nuuksio National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Canoe or Kayak: Of the various water sports, canoeing/kayaking is among the most popular activities at Nuuksio National Park as there are many small lakes and streams to explore.
  • Wildlife Watching: Visitors can spot various animals in the park including moose, lynx, and a variety of birds.

Food and Drink Scene of Nuuksio National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpiirakka: A traditional Finnish dish that consists of pastries filled with rice porridge.
  • Kalakukko: A regional specialty that consists of a rye bread crust filled with fish and pork belly.

Local Beverages

  • Kvas: A fermented drink made from bread, yeast, and sugar.
  • Sima: A traditional mead-like drink made from lemon, sugar, and yeast.

Where to Stay in Nuuksio National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Nuuksio: Located in the heart of Nuuksio National Park, this rustic hotel offers comfortable accommodation with a restaurant and bar.
  • Natura Viva: This guesthouse is situated on the shores of Lake Pitkäjärvi and offers a range of accommodation options, including cabins and tents.

What to Buy in Nuuksio National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Recycled Paper: An ideal item for those who want to bring a unique souvenir from their trip.
  • Birch Products: Visitors can buy items made from birch such as jewelry, storage boxes, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Nuuksio National Park

Tourists are expected to behave respectfully towards the natural environment and the wildlife in Nuuksio National Park. It is essential to refrain from disturbing the fauna and flora, and abide by the “leave no trace” principle. It is also customary to dress modestly when visiting places of worship and to remove your shoes before entering.

Best Time to Visit Nuuksio National Park

The best time to visit Nuuksio National Park is in the summer months between May and September when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. Visitors can experience the beautiful surroundings during the season, including the park’s lakes, lush forests of coniferous and deciduous trees, and scenic landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nuuksio National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife, plants, and trees
  • Choose responsible tourism practices such as carrying out your waste when visiting the park
  • Stay on designated trails and pathways

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach the park’s wildlife
  • Light open fires
  • Leave litter

Safety and Health

Safety in Nuuksio National Park

It is important to be prepared when hiking and camping in the park. Visitors should carry a map, water, and appropriate clothing, and should be aware of weather conditions and the potential for rapid changes in weather. Visitors should also protect themselves against ticks, which can carry diseases.

Health in Nuuksio National Park

The park has no shops or medical facilities, so it is crucial to pack basic first aid items such as bandages, antiseptic cream, and insect repellent.

Getting to Nuuksio National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nuuksio National Park

  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport: This airport is about 40km to the east of Nuuksio National Park
  • Turku Airport: This airport is about 160km from Nuuksio National Park.

Bus Stations Near Nuuksio National Park

  • Espoon keskus, Espoo bus station has connections to Helsinki and other parts of Uusimaa.
  • Otaniemi and Leppävaara: Both stations offer direct buses to Nuuksio National Park.

Other Options

  • By train: Helsinki to Espoo (U lines)
  • By car: The park is 30km away from downtown Helsinki

How to Get Around in Nuuksio National Park

Visitors can explore the park on foot, by bike, or by canoe/kayak, depending on their desired level of adventure. Hiking trails are well-marked, and there are several bike rental places in nearby towns for those who prefer to cycle.

Where to Go Next from Nuuksio National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Helsinki

Finland’s capital is just a 40-minute drive from Nuuksio National Park. Helsinki is known for its thriving food scene, stunning architecture, and cultural landmarks such as the Helsinki Cathedral and the Sibelius Monument.

Porvoo

Porvoo is a charming medieval town just an hour’s drive from Nuuksio National Park. The town is home to brightly painted wooden houses, cobblestone streets, and the magnificent Porvoo Cathedral dating back to the 13th century.

Conclusion

Nuuksio National Park is one of the most spectacular outdoor destinations in Finland, and it offers visitors the chance to experience the country’s natural beauty first-hand. With this guide, you have everything you need to plan the perfect trip to the park, from the best places to visit to safety tips and transportation options. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this breathtaking park!
