Introduction to Pyhtää

Pyhtää is a small town located in the Kymenlaakso region of Southern Finland. Despite its size, Pyhtää is a popular tourist destination known for its scenic landscapes, historical sites, and outdoor activities. The town is easily accessible by car or public transport, making it a great destination for a weekend getaway or a longer stay.

List of Places to Visit in Pyhtää

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Pyhtää Church: This 15th-century church is an iconic landmark of Pyhtää and is known for its beautiful frescoes and medieval architecture.
  2. The Ironworks Museum at Langinkoski: This museum tells the story of the ironworks industry that played a significant role in Pyhtää’s history. Visitors can also see the Langinkoski Imperial Fishing Lodge, where the Russian czars used to fish.
  3. The Museum of Local History and Culture: This museum displays artifacts and exhibits related to Pyhtää’s history, from prehistoric times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Pyhtää: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Pyhtää Coastal Route: This scenic route runs along Pyhtää’s coastline, offering breathtaking views of the Baltic Sea and the surrounding countryside.
  2. The Pyhtää Hiking Trail: This 20-kilometer trail winds through Pyhtää’s forests and is a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts.
  3. The Valkmusa National Park: Located on the eastern side of Pyhtää, Valkmusa National Park is home to diverse wildlife and offers excellent opportunities for birdwatching, hiking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pyhtää

The Pyhtää Hiking Trail is the town’s most popular hiking route. This 20-kilometer trail is suitable for hikers of all skill levels and winds through Pyhtää’s forests, fields, and lakes. The trail is marked with blue signs and takes approximately six hours to complete.

For biking enthusiasts, Pyhtää offers several scenic routes that run through the town’s countryside and coastline. The Pyhtää Coastal Route is a particularly popular route among bikers, as it offers beautiful views of the Baltic Sea and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Pyhtää: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing in Langinkoski: Visitors can try their hand at fishing in Langinkoski’s well-stocked river, or rent a boat and explore the area’s many waterways.
  2. Canoeing and Kayaking: Pyhtää’s numerous lakes and rivers offer excellent opportunities for canoeing and kayaking. Rental services are available in the town center.
  3. Nature Walks and Guided Tours: Pyhtää’s many natural attractions and historical sites can be explored through a variety of guided tours and nature walks.

Food and Drink Scene of Pyhtää

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kalakukko: This traditional Finnish pastry is filled with fish, bacon, and barley and is a staple of Pyhtää’s cuisine.
  2. Korvapuusti: These sweet cinnamon buns are popular in Pyhtää and throughout Finland.
  3. Ryynimakkara: This Finnish sausage is made from rye flour and pork and is a must-try when in Pyhtää.

Local Beverages

  1. Lonkero: This Finnish mixed drink is made from gin and grapefruit soda and is a popular beverage in Pyhtää.
  2. Salmiakki Koskenkorva: This vodka-based beverage is flavored with salty licorice and is a local favorite in Pyhtää.

Where to Stay in Pyhtää: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Spa Hotel Casino: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a spa and casino.
  2. The Pyhtää Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse offers affordable accommodations and is located in Pyhtää’s Old Town.
  3. The Kultasimpukka Hotel: This stylish hotel is located in the town center and offers modern amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Pyhtää: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Korvapuusti: These sweet cinnamon buns make a great gift or souvenir.
  2. Ruska Rye Bread: This hearty rye bread is a staple of Pyhtää’s cuisine.
  3. Lonkero and Salmiakki Koskenkorva: These local drinks make a unique souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Pyhtää

Pyhtää is known for its rich cultural heritage, which is evident in the town’s museums, landmarks, and traditional cuisine. Visitors to Pyhtää should also be aware of the Finnish custom of removing shoes before entering homes and certain public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Pyhtää

The best time to visit Pyhtää is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the countryside is at its most beautiful. However, Pyhtää’s natural attractions and outdoor activities can be enjoyed year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pyhtää

Do:

  1. Respect Finnish customs and traditions.
  2. Explore Pyhtää’s natural landscapes and cultural heritage.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Wear shoes indoors.
  2. Litter or harm the environment.
  3. Be disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pyhtää

Pyhtää is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime and a well-maintained infrastructure. Visitors to Pyhtää should take standard precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Pyhtää

Pyhtää has a well-established healthcare system, with several medical clinics and hospital facilities located in the town center. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before visiting Pyhtää.

Getting to Pyhtää: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pyhtää

The nearest airport to Pyhtää is Helsinki Airport, located approximately 100 kilometers away. Visitors can take a train or bus from Helsinki Airport to Pyhtää.

Bus Stations Near Pyhtää

Pyhtää has a bus station located in the town center, with regular services to Helsinki and other towns in the Kymenlaakso region.

Other Options

Pyhtää can also be reached by car or bike, with several major roads and highways running through the town and surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Pyhtää

Pyhtää is a relatively small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Rental cars and taxis are also available for visitors who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Pyhtää: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. The City of Kotka: Located just 25 kilometers from Pyhtää, Kotka is a picturesque seaside town known for its parks, museums, and cultural heritage.
  2. The City of Helsinki: Finland’s capital city is located approximately 100 kilometers from Pyhtää and is home to numerous attractions, including museums, parks, and landmarks.

Conclusion

Pyhtää is a hidden gem in Southern Finland, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Pyhtää has something to offer visitors of all ages and interests.
