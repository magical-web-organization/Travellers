Introduction to Kymenlaakso

Kymenlaakso is a region in Southern Finland located on the Gulf of Finland’s eastern shore. The region is known for its beautiful outdoors, rich culture and history, and friendly locals. It’s a hidden gem that’s waiting to be explored.

Kymenlaakso is easily accessible by car, bus and train and is just a few hours from Finland’s capital Helsinki. The region is perfect for vacationers looking for a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle-bustle. It offers a range of activities from hiking and biking, visiting museums and historical landmarks to experiencing its local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Kymenlaakso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kotka Maritime Museum: One of the largest maritime museums in Finland showcasing Kotka’s rich maritime history.
  2. Langinkoski Imperial Fishing Lodge: A former residence of Russian Emperor Alexander III, where he used to go fishing on the River Kymi.
  3. Maretarium Aquarium: A unique aquarium that showcases the freshwater fish species and aquatic life found in Kymenlaakso.
  4. Hamina Fortress: A well-preserved 18th-century fortress in Hamina that attracts history enthusiasts from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Kymenlaakso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Repovesi National Park: A beautiful national park that offers various outdoor activities such as hiking, canoeing, and camping.
  2. Uuperi Ski Center: The perfect place for skiing in winter, Uuperi Ski Center offers various skiing slopes for all levels of skiers.
  3. Kotka-Hamina Golf Course: One of Finland’s best golf courses that offers a scenic view of the coast.
  4. Pyhtaa Scenic Route: A 35 km scenic drive that offers breathtaking views of the Gulf of Finland, forests, lakes, and fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kymenlaakso

  1. Loviisa Coastal Route: A 35 km cycling route that runs along the Gulf of Finland’s coast.
  2. Saaristotie Route: A 140 km circular cycling route that passes through the archipelago.
  3. Repovesi National Park Hiking Trails: Various hiking trails that take you through forests, hills, and to a scenic viewpoint.
  4. Lake Saimaa Water Route: A 200 km long route that can be traveled by small boats, canoes, or kayaks, which passes through stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Kymenlaakso: Activities and Tours

  1. Kotka archipelago cruise: A cruise that takes you through the beautiful archipelago, where you can witness the stunning scenery, the local seals, and eagles.
  2. Chocolate factory tour: A unique tour where you can learn about chocolate-making and taste different varieties of chocolates and candies.
  3. Sea kayaking tour: Experience Kymenlaakso’s beautiful coastline from a different perspective with a sea kayaking tour.
  4. Cycling or snowshoeing tour in Repovesi National Park: Join a guided cycling or snowshoeing tour in Repovesi National Park and experience nature’s beauty a whole new way.

Food and Drink Scene of Kymenlaakso

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Karelian pies: A traditional pastry filled with rice pudding or mashed potato and served with egg butter.
  2. Salmiakki: A salty liquorice is Finland’s most popular sweet treat and can be found in many local shops and supermarkets.
  3. Kalakukko: A fish-filled pastry that’s a speciality from the region of Kainuu in Finland.
  4. Karjalanpaisti: A hearty stew made with beef, pork, lamb or venison, potatoes, and root vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Long Drink: A refreshing alcoholic drink that’s a mix of gin, grapefruit soda, and tonic water.
  2. Karelian beer: A locally brewed beer that’s hoppy and refreshing and pairs well with the region’s hearty cuisine.
  3. Fazer liquorice vodka: A unique drink that blends Finland’s popular sweets with vodka.

Where to Stay in Kymenlaakso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Santalahti Resort: A beautiful resort that’s located by the sea in Kotka and offers various activities such as kayaking, mini-golf, and sauna.
  2. Kotka Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Kotka and provides easy access to the city’s landmarks and attractions.
  3. Hotel Hamina: A charming hotel located in Hamina’s city center that offers spacious rooms and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Kymenlaakso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Fazer chocolates: A staple Finnish chocolate that’s a perfect souvenir to take home to friends and family.
  2. Lapponia jewelry: Beautifully designed and crafted jewelry that presents the region’s natural beauty.
  3. Marimekko textiles: Marimekko is a Finnish design brand famous for its bright and bold prints. It’s perfect for bringing home a little bit of Finnish design.

Local Culture and Customs of Kymenlaakso

Kymenlaakso, like the rest of Finland, has a rich cultural heritage that’s visible through its art, cuisine, folklore, and historic landmarks. Finnish people value simplicity, honesty, and closeness to nature. These values can be observed in the region’s way of life.

Best Time to Visit Kymenlaakso

The best time to visit Kymenlaakso is during the summer months from May to August when the weather is mild, and the days are long. The region’s natural beauty is in full bloom, and there are a plethora of outdoor activities to indulge in. However, winter in Kymenlaakso has its own charm, with winter sports, winter hikes, and the beautiful winter scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kymenlaakso

Do:

  1. Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  2. Follow the Finnish sauna etiquette.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  4. Respect nature and leave no trace when hiking and biking in the outdoors.

Don’t:

  1. Interrupt someone when they’re speaking.
  2. Be too loud or boisterous in public places.
  3. Be too touchy-feely, as personal space is valued in Finland.
  4. Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kymenlaakso

Kymenlaakso is a safe region with low crime rates. However, it’s important to take basic safety precautions when traveling in any region. Solo travelers, in particular, should avoid walking alone at night and off the beaten path. It’s advisable to keep valuables in a safe place and secure accommodations properly.

Health in Kymenlaakso

Kymenlaakso, like the rest of Finland, boasts of an excellent public healthcare system. Foreign nationals who require medical attention can access public healthcare services directly or through private health providers at their own expense.

Getting to Kymenlaakso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kymenlaakso

  1. Helsinki Airport: The largest airport in Finland is located about 125 km away from Kymenlaakso and provides good flight connections to various international destinations.
  2. Lappeenranta Airport: Located about 70 km away from Kymenlaakso and offers domestic flights to various cities in Finland.

Bus Stations Near Kymenlaakso

  1. Kotka Bus Station: Located in the centre of Kotka, this bus station provides bus connections to many major cities in Finland.
  2. Hamina Bus Station: Located in the centre of Hamina, this bus station provides bus connections to many cities in Finland.

Other Options

  1. Train: The train is an excellent option for those coming from Helsinki or other nearby cities.
  2. Car Rental: Renting a car is an excellent option for those who want more flexibility for their travels.

How to Get Around in Kymenlaakso

Kymenlaakso is an easy region to explore by car, bike, or public transportation. Buses run regularly between cities, and cycle routes of different lengths connect the region’s major attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kymenlaakso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Turku: Finland’s ancient capital city Turku is located about 200 km from Kymenlaakso and is reachable by train, bus, or car.
  2. Helsinki: The Finnish capital Helsinki is just 130 km away from Kymenlaakso and provides excellent connections to international destinations.
  3. Lappeenranta: This city on the shores of Lake Saimaa is about 120 km away from Kymenlaakso and is famous for its historic fortress and beautiful lakeside views.

Conclusion

Kymenlaakso is a region that offers a wealth of peaceful outdoor experiences, cultural landmarks, and unique cuisine. It blends history, tradition, and natural beauty to provide visitors with an unforgettable experience. A must-visit destination for those seeking a peaceful escape from city life.
