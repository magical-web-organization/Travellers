Introduction to Asikkala

Asikkala is a peaceful municipality located in Päijänne Tavastia region, in Southern Finland. It offers a great escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, boasting stunning natural scenery, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities. Tourists visiting Asikkala can experience genuine Finnish culture and way of life while taking on new adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Asikkala

Visitors to Asikkala can explore the following list of places;

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lahti Historical Museum

Lahti Historical Museum takes visitors back in time and showcases the growth and development of Lahti across the centuries. The museum features several exhibits and artifacts, including photographs, documents, and objects used in daily life.

Vääksy Canal

The Vääksy Canal is an immpressive canal that stretches for 1.5 kilometers, connecting Lake Vesijärvi with Lake Päijänne. Visitors can take a boat ride or walk along the canal pathways while enjoying the impressive scenery.

Natural Attractions of Asikkala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Päijänne National Park

The Päijänne National Park is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts, offering vast forests, natural ridges, unspoiled shores, and clear waters. Visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, fishing, boating, and bird watching.

Kelvenne Island Trails

Kelvenne Island Trails are a popular destination for hiking and biking, offering stunning views of the lake and vast open landscapes. The trails are well-maintained and suitable for nature walks, picnic trips or cycling excursions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Asikkala

Asikkala offers several hiking trails, including the Kelvenne Island Trails, ranked among the best in Finland. The Karhunkierros Trail is an excellent option for nature lovers, taking hikers on a scenic journey along rugged and breathtaking landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Asikkala: Activities and Tours

Asikkala offers a plethora of activities and tours, including guided fishing, canoeing, kayaking, and water sports. Visitors can also explore the charming villages and towns in the region, sample local cuisine and take part in cultural festivals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Asikkala

Visitors can spend a week exploring the beauty of Asikkala, starting with a day trip to the Vääksy Canal. The second day can be spent hiking at Kelvenne Island Trails, followed by a visit to Lahti Historical Museum on the third day. The fourth and fifth day can be reserved for lake activities, such as fishing, swimming, and boat rides. On the last two days, visitors can relax and enjoy local cuisine and immerse themselves in the Finnish way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of Asikkala

Asikkala offers a rich culinary scene, with fresh local ingredients used to create authentic Finnish dishes. Visitors can try the following:

Must-Try Dishes

Kalakukko

Kalakukko is a traditional Finnish dish made with fish, potatoes, and pork fat baked into a rye bread crust.

Lihapullat

Lihapullat, or Finnish meatballs, are made of beef or pork, mixed with breadcrumbs and onion.

Local Beverages

Sahti

Sahti is a traditional Finnish beer brewed from malted barley and rye. Some variations also include juniper berries, giving the beer a unique taste.

Koskenkorva

Koskenkorva is a Finnish vodka made from barley and pure Finnish water, containing no artificial flavors or sugars.

Where to Stay in Asikkala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Asikkala offers a multitude of accommodation options, including:

Gustavelund Hotel

Gustavelund Hotel is a charming hotel offering great amenities, stunning lake views, and excellent service. The hotel also features an on-site restaurant, spa, and sauna.

Camping Messilä

Camping Messilä is an excellent budget-friendly option, offering a wide range of outdoor activities and comfortable cabin accommodations. The site has saunas, tennis courts, swimming pools, and a restaurant with stunning lake views.

What to Buy in Asikkala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy souvenirs and local products from shops around Asikkala, including handicrafts, knitwear, and Finnish design items. Other local products include smoked fish, berry jams, and locally produced honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Asikkala

Asikkala offers a unique blend of Finnish customs and traditions, dating back to ancient times, making the region a vibrant cultural destination. Finns have a distinct cultural identity reflected in their family values, social practices, music, and language.

Best Time to Visit Asikkala

The best time to visit Asikkala is during the summer months, from June to August, where the weather is mild, and the days are longer. The late autumn season also offers breathtaking fall foliage views

Do’s and Don’ts of Asikkala

Asikkala prides itself on Finnish customs and traditions. Therefore, visitors should:

Do:

  • Respect the rules and regulations of the National Parks
  • Drink responsibly
  • Try Finnish cuisine
  • Take off your shoes in homes
  • Respect personal space and privacy

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Use excessive profanity
  • Ignore reindeer crossing signs

Safety and Health

Asikkala is generally safe, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always exercise caution and follow safety guidelines.

Safety in Asikkala

Visitors should stay on designated trails and follow directions from park rangers while hiking in National Parks. Those taking part in water activities should wear safety gear and avoid drinking while swimming.

Health in Asikkala

Finland has excellent health care services and clinics. Visitors should bring any prescribed medication with them, and travel insurance is recommended to cover any unexpected medical needs.

Getting to Asikkala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Asikkala is easily accessible by air, rail, and road.

Airports Near Asikkala

The nearest airport is Helsinki Airport, Finland’s largest airport, situated approximately 110 km from Asikkala.

Bus Stations Near Asikkala

Asikkala is serviced by bus operators such as Matkahuolto, offering regular services from major cities to Asikkala.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Helsinki to Asikkala.

How to Get Around in Asikkala

Visitors can get around the region by renting a car, using taxis or using the bus services. For those interested in outdoor activities, a bike is an excellent option for exploring the region at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Asikkala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Asikkala can explore neighboring municipalities, including Lahti, Heinola, and Sysmä. Each town offers a unique set of attractions ranging from cultural and historical landmarks to natural sights and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Asikkala offers visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in Finnish culture and enjoy the beauty of nature. The region boasts scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, fabulous cuisine, and excellent accommodation options, making it a must-visit destination when travelling to Finland.
