Introduction to Hollola

Hollola is a small town located in the region of Päijänne Tavastia, Southern Finland. Known for its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural attractions, this charming town offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs in Hollola. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or just looking for a peaceful getaway, Hollola has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hollola

Hollola is a treasure trove of attractions, from historic landmarks to natural wonders. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hollola Church: Built in the 15th century, this church is one of the oldest in Finland. Admire the stunning medieval architecture and learn about the religious history of the town.
  • Lahti Art Museum: Located in nearby Lahti, this museum showcases some of the best Finnish and international art. From contemporary works to historical pieces, there’s something for everyone.
  • Heinola Art Museum: Set in a beautiful 19th-century building, this museum features an impressive collection of Finnish art. Don’t miss the stunning landscape paintings by Akseli Gallen-Kallela.

Natural Attractions of Hollola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Messilä Ski Centre: Hit the slopes and enjoy some winter fun at this popular ski resort. With a variety of runs suitable for all skill levels and affordable prices, it’s no wonder Messilä is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Vesijärvi Lake: Situated just a short drive from the town center, Vesijärvi is the perfect destination for swimming, boating, or simply enjoying the beautiful views. Take a hike along the lake shore and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Hollola Medieval Church Hill: This historic site is the perfect destination for history and nature lovers alike. Walk along the winding paths and explore the ruins of the medieval church, surrounded by beautiful forest and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hollola

Looking for some outdoor adventures? Hollola offers a variety of hiking and biking routes suitable for all skill levels. Here are some of our favorites:

  • The Hollola Nature Trail: This 5km trail is perfect for hikers looking for a peaceful nature walk. Enjoy the beautiful forest scenery and spot some local wildlife along the way.
  • The Salpakangas Trail: Explore the Salpakangas village and enjoy the scenic views of the Päijänne lake on this 12.7km route. Don’t forget to stop by the historic Salpakangas mill on the way.
  • The Kuoras Trail: This 8km trail takes you through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the region. Enjoy the stunning views of the nearby Härkätunturi mountain and the Päijänne lake.

Best Things to Do in Hollola: Activities and Tours

From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, Hollola offers a variety of activities and tours suitable for all interests. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Hollola Cultural Tour: Immerse yourself in the rich history and cultural heritage of Hollola with this guided tour. From the medieval church ruins to the historic town center, this tour is a must for history enthusiasts.
  • The Vesijärvi Cruise: Take a relaxing cruise along the beautiful Vesijärvi lake and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. With affordable prices and regular departures, this tour is perfect for families and couples.
  • The Messilä Adventure Park: Looking for some adrenaline-pumping fun? Look no further than the Messilä adventure park. With a variety of activities, from ziplining to tree climbing, this park is perfect for thrill-seekers of all ages.

Food and Drink Scene of Hollola

No trip to Hollola is complete without trying some of the local dishes and beverages. Here are some of our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: This traditional Finnish dish is a must-try for foodies. Made with rye crust and filled with fish and pork, it’s a hearty and delicious meal.
  • Ruisleipä: Finland is known for its delicious rye bread, and Hollola is no exception. Be sure to try some freshly baked ruisleipä during your trip.
  • Lihapullat: These Finnish-style meatballs are a popular comfort food in Hollola. Try them with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce for a classic Finnish meal.

Local Beverages

  • Lonkero: This refreshing Finnish beverage is a mix of grapefruit soda and gin. Perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Sahti: This traditional Finnish beer is made with juniper berries and has a unique and complex flavor. A must for beer enthusiasts.
  • Koskenkorva: This Finnish vodka is a popular choice among locals. Try it on the rocks or mix it with cranberry juice for a delicious cocktail.

Where to Stay in Hollola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly hostel or a luxurious hotel, Hollola offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Messilä Manor: This historic manor house offers luxurious rooms and suites with beautiful lake views. With its elegant interiors and stunning location, it’s the perfect choice for a romantic getaway.
  • Hostel Matkakoti Patria: This budget-friendly hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms, perfect for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.
  • Guesthouse Onnenmyyrä: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere. The perfect choice for travelers looking for a home away from home.

What to Buy in Hollola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some unique souvenirs to bring back home? Hollola offers a variety of local products and handicrafts, including:

  • Luumäki Strawberries: Known for their intense flavor and sweetness, these strawberries are a must-try for foodies. Be sure to bring some back home for your friends and family.
  • Pellavavaate Clothing: Made from high-quality linen, these elegant and comfortable clothes are a popular choice among locals. Check out the local boutiques for some unique pieces.
  • Handicrafts: From wooden toys to handmade pottery, Hollola is home to a variety of skilled artisans. Be sure to check out the local markets and souvenir shops for some unique handicrafts to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hollola

Hollola is a town rich in traditional customs and culture. Here are some of the most notable traditions and events:

  • Joulu: The annual Christmas market is a popular tradition in Hollola. Be sure to visit the market to enjoy some traditional food, music, and crafts.
  • Midsummer Celebrations: Midsummer is a big celebration in Finland, and Hollola is no exception. Join the locals in dancing around the maypole and enjoying some delicious food and drinks.
  • Vappu: This traditional spring celebration is a popular holiday in Finland. Join the locals in enjoying some champagne and sima, a traditional Finnish mead.

Best Time to Visit Hollola

The best time to visit Hollola depends on your interests and preferences. For nature lovers, summer (June-August) is the perfect time to explore the beautiful lakes and forests. If you’re into winter sports, January-March is the best time to hit the slopes at the Messilä Ski Centre.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hollola

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Hollola:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Greet people with a handshake or a hug
  • Be punctual for appointments and meetings

Don’t:

  • Interrupt someone when they’re speaking
  • Wear shoes indoors
  • Leave a tip at restaurants (it’s not expected in Finland)

Getting to Hollola: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to get to Hollola, including:

Airports Near Hollola

  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport: Located about 1.5 hours away from Hollola, this airport is the closest international airport.
  • Tampere-Pirkkala Airport: Located about 2 hours away from Hollola, this airport is a popular choice for domestic travelers.

Bus Stations Near Hollola

  • Lahti Bus Station: Located about 20 minutes away from Hollola, this bus station offers regular connections to major cities in Finland.
  • Helsinki Bus Station: Located about 1.5 hours away from Hollola, this bus station offers regular connections to major cities in Finland and other Nordic countries.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option for travelers who want to explore the region at their own pace.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Hollola and can be a convenient option for short trips around town.

How to Get Around in Hollola

Hollola is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. If you’re planning to explore the surrounding area, renting a car is a popular option. Taxis are also readily available for short trips around town.

Where to Go Next from Hollola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to explore more of Southern Finland? Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Lahti: Located just 20 minutes away from Hollola, Lahti is a bustling city known for its cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.
  • Helsinki: Finland’s capital city is just 1.5 hours away from Hollola and offers a variety of attractions, from historical landmarks to modern art museums.
  • Päijänne National Park: This stunning national park is located just a short drive away from Hollola and offers some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in Finland.
