Introduction to Nastola

Located in Päijänne Tavastia, in Southern Finland, Nastola is a charming town with a rich history and natural beauty. Known for its stunning lake views and outdoor adventures, Nastola is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Finland.

Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in local culture and customs or simply relax and take in the breathtaking scenery, Nastola has something for everyone. In this guide, you’ll find everything you need to plan your perfect trip, including the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Nastola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re looking to learn more about Nastola’s rich history and culture, be sure to visit these top museums and historical places:

  • Nastolan Kotiseutumuseo: This charming museum showcases the history and culture of Nastola and its surrounding areas. You’ll find a variety of exhibits and artifacts that paint a vivid picture of life in Nastola throughout the ages.

  • Church of St. Lawrence: Dating back to the 16th century, this beautiful church is a striking example of traditional Finnish architecture. Be sure to check out the stunning frescoes inside.

  • Kärkjärvi Ironworks: Located just outside of Nastola, this historic ironworks site offers a fascinating glimpse into Finland’s industrial past. You’ll learn about the history of iron production in the region and see demonstrations of traditional ironworking techniques.

Natural Attractions of Nastola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nastola is known for its stunning lake views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in the area:

  • Lake Kivijärvi: This picturesque lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. Enjoy a leisurely stroll along the shoreline or rent a boat and explore the lake’s many islands and coves.

  • Päijänne National Park: Just a short drive from Nastola, this beautiful national park offers miles of hiking trails, breathtaking views, and a chance to spot native wildlife like moose and beavers.

  • Lahti Ski Museum and Ski Jump Tower: Located in nearby Lahti, this museum offers a fascinating look into the history of skiing in Finland. You’ll also be able to take a ride to the top of the iconic ski jump tower for stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nastola

Nastola is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the top routes to check out include:

  • Nastola Nature Trail: This easy 5-kilometer trail takes you through some of Nastola’s most beautiful natural landscapes, including forests, lakes, and marshes.

  • Vesijärvi Cycle Route: This picturesque 65-kilometer route takes you along the shores of Lake Vesijärvi, with stunning lake views and beautiful countryside scenery.

  • Päijänne National Park Hiking Trails: With over 20 different trails to choose from, Päijänne National Park offers endless opportunities for hiking and exploring.

Best Things to Do in Nastola: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Nastola has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some top picks:

  • Kayaking on Lake Kivijärvi: Rent a kayak and explore the many islands and coves of this beautiful lake.

  • Sauna Experience: No trip to Finland is complete without a traditional Finnish sauna experience. Book a private sauna rental and enjoy a relaxing soak in the heat.

  • Cycling Tour of Nastola: Join a guided cycling tour of Nastola and explore the town’s many natural and cultural attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nastola

If you have a week to spare, here’s a suggested itinerary for exploring Nastola and its surroundings:

  • Day 1: Visit Nastolan Kotiseutumuseo and the Church of St. Lawrence, and take a stroll around Lake Kivijärvi.

  • Day 2: Explore Päijänne National Park and enjoy a hiking or biking trail.

  • Day 3: Take a guided cycling tour of Nastola and its surrounding villages.

  • Day 4: Visit the Kärkjärvi Ironworks and learn about Finland’s industrial past.

  • Day 5: Enjoy a traditional Finnish sauna experience and take a dip in Lake Kivijärvi.

  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Lahti, visit the Ski Museum and Ski Jump Tower, and enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy your last day in Nastola, perhaps with a leisurely boat ride on Lake Kivijärvi.

Food and Drink Scene of Nastola

Must-Try Dishes

Nastola is known for its hearty, traditional Finnish cuisine. Be sure to try these must-try dishes:

  • Kalakukko: This savory baked pastry is filled with fish, pork, and potatoes, and is a traditional Finnish staple.

  • Lihapullat: Finnish-style meatballs, served with lingonberry sauce and mashed potatoes.

  • Ruisleipä: Traditional Finnish rye bread, often served with butter and cheese.

Local Beverages

Finnish people love coffee and tea, and you’ll find plenty of local cafes and bakeries around Nastola serving up delicious brews. Be sure to try:

  • Koskenkorva: This popular Finnish vodka is made from barley and potatoes.

  • Finnish Beer: Try some of the locally-brewed Finnish beers, such as Koff, Olvi, or Lapin Kulta.

Where to Stay in Nastola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nastola offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to modern hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Kivijärven Kievari: This lakeside guesthouse offers stunning views and comfortable, well-equipped rooms.

  • Cumulus Lahti: Located just a short drive from Nastola, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities, including an indoor pool and sauna.

  • Villa Tyyrpuuri: This charming guesthouse is located on the shores of Lake Kivijärvi and offers cozy, rustic accommodations in a beautiful setting.

What to Buy in Nastola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While Nastola may not be known for its shopping scene, there are still plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home with you. Some ideas include:

  • Finnish Knives: Look for traditional Finnish knives, such as the iconic Puukko, which are known for their high quality and functional design.

  • Finnish Crafts: From hand-carved wooden figurines to intricate Finnish textiles, there are plenty of beautiful crafts to admire and bring home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Nastola

Finland has a rich and unique culture, and there are plenty of customs and traditions to discover in Nastola. Here is a brief overview:

  • Sauna Culture: Saunas are an important part of Finnish culture, and you’ll find them everywhere in Nastola. If you’re invited to a sauna, be sure to follow the traditional customs, like taking a cold dip in the lake afterward.

  • Midsummer Celebrations: Midsummer is a big celebration in Finland, and Nastola is no exception. You’ll find plenty of traditional food and festivities to enjoy on Midsummer’s Eve, which falls on the weekend closest to June 21st.

Best Time to Visit Nastola

The best time to visit Nastola depends on your preferences. If you’re looking to enjoy outdoor activities and natural scenery, then the summer months (June to August) are your best bet. If you prefer cooler temperatures and winter activities, then the winter months (December to February) are ideal. Spring and fall can be beautiful as well, with colorful foliage and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nastola

Do:

  • Be respectful of Finnish customs and traditions, such as sauna culture and Midsummer celebrations.

  • Try the traditional Finnish dishes and beverages, such as Kalakukko and Koskenkorva vodka.

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural attractions in the area.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the Finnish weather, which can be unpredictable and changeable.

  • Be overly loud or boisterous in public places, as Finnish culture values understated behavior and modesty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nastola

Nastola is generally a safe town for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take normal precautions while traveling. Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, especially in crowded places.

Health in Nastola

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and there are plenty of medical facilities and hospitals in Nastola and the surrounding areas. If you need medical assistance, ask your hotel or hostel for recommendations. It’s also a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Nastola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nastola

The closest airport to Nastola is Helsinki Airport, which is located approximately 120 kilometers away. From the airport, you can take a bus or train to Lahti, and then take a local bus or taxi to Nastola.

Bus Stations Near Nastola

Nastola has a bus station in the town center, with frequent connections to Lahti and other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies in Helsinki and Lahti that offer rental cars. It’s also possible to take a taxi or hire a private car service to get to Nastola from the airport or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Nastola

Nastola is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. There is also a local bus system that connects the town center to other nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Nastola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Finland, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Lahti: Just a short drive from Nastola, Lahti is a vibrant city with plenty of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.

  • Helsinki: Finland’s capital city is located approximately 120 kilometers from Nastola and is easily reached by car or public transportation.

  • Lakeland Region: If you’re looking to explore more of Finland’s beautiful lake district, head inland to the lakeland region, which offers miles of hiking and biking trails, charming villages, and beautiful lake views.

Conclusion

Nastola is a hidden gem in Finland, with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure. Whether you’re looking to relax by Lake Kivijärvi, explore the town’s historic sites and museums, or immerse yourself in Finnish culture and customs, Nastola has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your perfect trip and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
