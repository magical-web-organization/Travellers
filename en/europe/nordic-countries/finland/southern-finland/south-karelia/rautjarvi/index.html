Introduction to Rautjärvi

Rautjärvi is a small municipality located in South Karelia, Southern Finland. The name Rautjärvi means “Iron Lake” in Finnish, and the area is known for its rich history dating back to the Iron Age. Rautjärvi consists of several small villages surrounded by idyllic Finnish countryside. It’s a great destination for travelers who want to experience small-town life in Finland and enjoy nature activities.

List of Places to Visit in Rautjärvi

Whether you’re looking to explore cultural landmarks, experience the natural wonders of the area, or get active with outdoor activities, Rautjärvi has plenty to offer visitors.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Käenkoski Village Museum: The museum showcases the life of a Finnish farmer and his family in the 19th and early 20th centuries. You can see traditional Finnish clothing, tools, and furniture. The museum is located in the village of Käenkoski.
  • Rautjärvi Church: The church is a beautiful example of traditional Finnish architecture. It was built in 1775 and is located in the center of Rautjärvi village.
  • Punnaistenniemi: Punnaistenniemi is an Iron Age grave field located near Lake Pyhäjärvi. It’s a great place to explore the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Rautjärvi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pyhäjärvi Lake: Pyhäjärvi is a large lake surrounded by beautiful Finnish countryside. It’s a great place to go fishing, swimming, or boating.
  • Käsämä Canal: The canal was built in the 1870s and connects Pyhäjärvi Lake to Saimaa Lake. You can go boating or kayaking through the canal and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Kummakivi Boulder: Kummakivi Boulder is a unique natural formation located in Rautjärvi. It’s a massive boulder that appears to be balancing on a smaller rock. It’s a great place to take photos and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rautjärvi

Rautjärvi has plenty of great hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the beautiful Finnish countryside. Here are some popular routes:

  • Pyhäjärven lenkki Trail: Pyhäjärven lenkki is a 34-kilometer hiking and biking trail that goes around Pyhäjärvi Lake. It’s a great way to explore the lakeside scenery and natural beauty.
  • Lietjärvi Nature Trail: The trail is 3.5 kilometers long and takes visitors through a beautiful forest and around a pristine lake.

Best Things to Do in Rautjärvi: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Pyhäjärvi Lake is well-known for its excellent fishing opportunities. You can catch perch, pike, and zander.
  • Boating: Rent a boat and explore Pyhäjärvi Lake or Käsämä Canal. You can enjoy the beautiful scenery and the peace and quiet of the Finnish countryside.
  • Sauna: The Finnish sauna is an essential part of Finnish culture. You can rent a traditional sauna and relax in the heat after a long day of hiking or sightseeing.
  • Ice-Fishing: In the winter months, Rautjärvi offers great opportunities for ice fishing on Pyhäjärvi Lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rautjärvi

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Rautjärvi, here’s an itinerary you can use to make the most of your time in the area:

  • Day 1: Visit Käenkoski Village Museum and Rautjärvi Church
  • Day 2: Go hiking on Lietjärvi Nature Trail
  • Day 3: Rent a boat and explore Pyhäjärvi Lake or Käsämä Canal
  • Day 4: Visit Punnaistenniemi and Kummakivi Boulder
  • Day 5: Go fishing on Pyhäjärvi Lake
  • Day 6: Enjoy a traditional Finnish sauna
  • Day 7: Day trip to the nearby city of Lappeenranta to explore the Saimaa Canal and Fortress

Food and Drink Scene of Rautjärvi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpiirakka: A traditional Finnish pastry filled with rice or mashed potatoes. You can find them in most cafes and bakeries.
  • Särki: A local fish species that’s often served marinated or fried.
  • Puolukkamehu: A traditional Finnish drink made from lingonberries.

Local Beverages

  • Olvi Beer: Olvi is a Finnish beer brand that’s popular throughout the country.
  • Koskenkorva Vodka: Koskenkorva is a Finnish vodka that’s made from barley and pure Finnish spring water.

Where to Stay in Rautjärvi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Luostarinmäki: A small hotel located in the center of Rautjärvi village.
  • Käenkoski Countryhouse: A guesthouse located in the village of Käenkoski.
  • Pesäkallio Nature Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse located near Lietjärvi Nature Trail.

What to Buy in Rautjärvi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moomin Products: Moomin is a popular Finnish cartoon character, and you can find Moomin-themed products in most gift shops.
  • Arabia Products: Arabia is a Finnish ceramics brand, and you can find their products in most gift shops.
  • Karelian Pies: You can buy traditional Karelian pies in most bakeries and cafes.

Local Culture and Customs of Rautjärvi

  • Sauna Culture: The Finnish sauna is an essential part of Finnish culture, and it’s customary to visit a sauna at least once a week.
  • Midsummer Celebrations: Midsummer is a major holiday in Finland, and it’s customary to celebrate it by lighting bonfires and spending time with friends and family.
  • Respect for Nature: The Finnish people have a deep respect for nature, and it’s customary to leave nature as you found it when hiking or camping.

Best Time to Visit Rautjärvi

The best time to visit Rautjärvi is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The winter months from December to February are also a great time to visit if you’re interested in winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rautjärvi

Do:

  • Do try traditional Finnish foods and drinks.
  • Do take a sauna.
  • Do respect the Finnish people’s privacy and personal space.

Don’t:

  • Don’t assume that everyone speaks English fluently.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Don’t leave trash or litter in nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rautjärvi

Rautjärvi is a very safe place to visit. Violent crime is almost non-existent, and petty crime is rare.

Health in Rautjärvi

Finland has a good healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the nearby city of Lappeenranta if you need medical attention.

Getting to Rautjärvi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rautjärvi

The closest airport to Rautjärvi is Lappeenranta Airport, which is about 45 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Rautjärvi

There are several bus stations in the nearby city of Lappeenranta that offer connections to Rautjärvi.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Rautjärvi.

How to Get Around in Rautjärvi

The best way to get around Rautjärvi is by car or bike. There are several bike rental companies in Rautjärvi, and most roads are bike-friendly.

Where to Go Next from Rautjärvi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring more of Southern Finland, here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Lappeenranta: Lappeenranta is a nearby city that’s known for its beautiful fortress, Saimaa Canal, and excellent shopping and dining options.
  • Imatra: Imatra is a small town located near the Russian border. It’s a great place to explore Finnish history and culture.

Conclusion

Rautjärvi is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience small-town life in Finland and enjoy nature activities. With its beautiful countryside, friendly locals, and rich history, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Southern Finland.
