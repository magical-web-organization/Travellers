Introduction to Parikkala

Parikkala is a small municipality located in South Karelia, Southern Finland. It is a perfect destination for travellers looking for a peaceful getaway, surrounded by pristine nature and rich cultural heritage. Parikkala is home to several culturally important attractions and natural landmarks, making it an ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Parikkala

Here is a list of must-visit places in Parikkala:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Parikkala Sculpture Park
  • Parikkalan Patsaspuisto Museum
  • Parikkalan Valo
  • Parikkala Church

Natural Attractions of Parikkala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Siikalahti Nature Reserve
  • Lake Simpele
  • Kitee Zoo
  • Linnansaari National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parikkala

Parikkala has some of the most picturesque and well-marked hiking and biking trails in the region. Here are a few routes to consider:

  • Lintulan Luostari Trail
  • Siikalahti Nature Trail
  • Simpeleen Kierros Trail
  • Haapasuo Nature Trail

Best Things to Do in Parikkala: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Parikkala Sculpture Park and discover over 200 remarkable sculptures scattered across a vast park.
  • Explore the Siikalahti Nature Reserve, an exceptional bird-watching spot, and discover the unique combination of nature and art at the Parikkalan Valo.
  • Take a guided tour of Parikkala and uncover the history and culture behind its museums and historical sites.
  • Go on a kayaking tour of the archipelago, admire the beautiful panoramas, and discover hidden gems such as Lake Simpele and Linnansaari National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Parikkala

Day 1: Arrival and exploring the town center
Day 2: Visit Parikkala Sculpture Park and Parikkalan Patsaspuisto Museum
Day 3: Discover the natural beauty of Siikalahti Nature Reserve
Day 4: Go on a biking tour of the countryside
Day 5: Excursion to Kitee Zoo and Lake Simpele
Day 6: Explore Linnansaari National Park
Day 7: Departure from Parikkala

Food and Drink Scene of Parikkala

Parikkala offers an array of local delicacies that visitors should try. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Lohikeitto (Salmon soup)
  • Karjalanpaisti (Karelian stew)
  • Simpele’s Kalakukko (Fish pie)

Local Beverages:

  • Saimaa IPA (Local beer)
  • Leipäjuusto (Traditional cheese)
  • Salmiakki (Salty liquorice candy)

What to Buy in Parikkala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tar products (such as tar soap, shampoo, and candles)
  • Local handicrafts (such as jewelry and pottery)
  • Saimaa-region specialties

Local Culture and Customs of Parikkala

In Parikkala, residents follow the Finnish customs, which include greeting with a handshake, taking shoes off when entering people’s homes, and being punctual for appointments. Clothing in Parikkala is generally casual, with comfortable and practical wear being the norm.

Best Time to Visit Parikkala

The best time to visit Parikkala is during the summer months, between June and August. This time is perfect for hiking, biking, and kayaking, as well as visiting the lakes and national parks. However, if you’re interested in winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding, then you should consider visiting during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parikkala

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Parikkala:

Do:

  • Share your opinions and listen respectfully to others’
  • Participate in the local customs and traditions
  • Respect the local wildlife and environment

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places
  • Feed the wildlife
  • Wear shoes inside people’s homes

Safety and Health

Safety in Parikkala

Parikkala is generally safe for visitors, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of ticks when venturing into nature areas and should take precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and trousers.

Health in Parikkala

There are no major health concerns in Parikkala. It is advised to bring any necessary medication with you and ensure that your insurance covers any medical emergencies.

Getting to Parikkala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parikkala

The nearest international airport to Parikkala is Helsinki Airport, which is located about 4 hours away by train or bus.

Bus Stations Near Parikkala

The Parikkala bus station is located in the town center and offers regular bus service to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Travellers can also reach Parikkala by train, car or ferry.

How to Get Around in Parikkala

It is recommended to have a car to get around Parikkala and explore the surrounding nature reserves and national parks. Biking and hiking are also popular options, and bike rentals are available in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Parikkala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Savonlinna: This beautiful town is known for its Olavinlinna, a medieval castle located on a small island in the middle of Lake Saimaa.
  • Lappeenranta: This historical city is located on the shore of Lake Saimaa and is popular for its fortress, old town, and museums.
  • Helsinki: The capital of Finland is located 4 hours away by train and has many tourist attractions such as cathedrals, museums, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Parikkala offers a unique combination of natural beauty and cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for travellers looking to explore the Finnish countryside. With its rich history, picturesque scenery, and endless outdoor activities, Parikkala is a must-visit destination in Finland.
