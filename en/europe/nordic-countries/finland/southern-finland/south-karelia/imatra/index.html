Imatra is a charming town located in South Karelia, in Southern Finland. This hidden gem is home to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in the region, as well as a wealth of cultural and historical landmarks. Whether you’re looking to soak in the beauty of the great outdoors, explore the town’s rich heritage, or simply relax and enjoy Finnish hospitality, Imatra is the perfect destination for your next vacation. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your dream trip to Imatra.

List of Places to Visit in Imatra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Imatra is a town with a rich cultural heritage, and there’s no shortage of museums and historical landmarks to explore. Some of the must-see attractions in Imatra include:

  • Imatra Museum: This museum covers the town’s history from prehistoric times to the present day. Exhibits include archaeological artifacts, traditional Finnish costumes and textiles, and vintage photographs of Imatra.

  • Imatrankoski Rapids: This natural landmark is not only incredibly beautiful, but also played a key role in the development of the town. The rapids were once used to power a hydroelectric plant, which in turn fueled the growth of the local paper industry.

  • Vuoksenniska Church: This historic church was built in the early 20th century and features stunning Art Nouveau architecture. The church’s bell tower offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Imatra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Imatra is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of outdoor activities and breathtaking vistas to explore. Some of the top natural attractions in Imatra include:

  • Lammassaari Island: This small island is located in the Vuoksi River and is home to a beautiful park and nature reserve. Visitors can explore the island’s forests, fields, and wetlands, as well as enjoy swimming, fishing, and boating.

  • Saimaa Canal: This canal connects Lake Saimaa to the Gulf of Finland and is a popular spot for boat tours and cruises. Along the way, you’ll see stunning scenery, historic landmarks, and perhaps even some local wildlife.

  • Vuoksi Nature Reserve: This vast nature reserve covers an area of more than 20,000 hectares and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, birdwatching, and other outdoor activities in this pristine wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Imatra

Whether you’re an avid hiker or a casual biker, Imatra has plenty of scenic routes to explore. Some of the most popular hiking and biking trails in Imatra include:

  • Imatra Nature Trail: This 7-km trail takes visitors through some of the town’s most stunning natural landscapes, including forests, bogs, and lakes.

  • Saimaa Canal Trail: This 14-km trail follows the route of the Saimaa Canal and offers beautiful views of the waterway and surrounding countryside.

  • Vuoksi River Trail: This 45-km trail follows the length of the Vuoksi River and offers a mix of natural and cultural attractions, including historic landmarks, nature reserves, and scenic overlooks.

Best Things to Do in Imatra: Activities and Tours

Imatra offers a wide variety of activities and tours to suit every taste and budget. Here are some of the top experiences not to be missed:

  • Imatra Spa: This world-class spa offers a range of treatments and services, including massage, facials, and saunas. Visitors can also enjoy the spa’s thermal pools and outdoor jacuzzis.

  • Imatra Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is one of the town’s most iconic landmarks and is best viewed at night when it’s illuminated by colored lights.

  • Churchboat Tour: This unique tour takes visitors along the Vuoksi River on a traditional wooden churchboat. Along the way, you’ll learn about the local history and culture, as well as enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Imatra

If you have a week to spend in Imatra, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s cultural landmarks, including Imatra Museum and Vuoksenniska Church.

  • Day 2: Go on a hiking or biking tour of Imatra’s natural scenery, such as the Imatra Nature Trail or Vuoksi River Trail.

  • Day 3: Take a boat tour along the Saimaa Canal and visit some of the historic landmarks along the way, such as Olavinlinna Castle and the Lusto Forest Museum.

  • Day 4: Relax and unwind at the Imatra Spa, enjoying a range of treatments and services.

  • Day 5: Visit the stunning Imatra Waterfall and take a boat tour of the rapids.

  • Day 6: Take a churchboat tour along the Vuoksi River and learn about the local history and culture.

  • Day 7: Spend a day exploring the nearby town of Lappeenranta, with its beautiful parklands, museums, and markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Imatra

No trip to Imatra would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Karelian Stew: This hearty meat stew is made with beef, pork, and onions, and is a staple of Finnish cuisine.

  • Kalakukko: This traditional Finnish dish is essentially a baked fish pie, made with rye flour, potatoes, and fish (often pike or salmon).

  • Mustikkapiirakka: This blueberry pie is a popular Finnish dessert, made with a shortcrust pastry and a filling of fresh blueberries.

Local Beverages:

  • Finnish Beer: Finland is home to a thriving craft beer scene, with microbreweries producing a wide variety of styles and flavors.

  • Koskenkorva: This Finnish vodka is made from barley and boasts a smooth and velvety taste.

  • Cloudberry Liqueur: This sweet liqueur is made from the unique cloudberry fruit and is a popular Finnish digestif.

Where to Stay in Imatra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Imatra offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Imatra include:

  • Imatran Kylpylä Spa: This luxurious spa hotel offers beautifully appointed rooms and suites, as well as a range of wellness services and activities.

  • Karjalan Portti: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Imatra and offers cozy rooms with traditional Finnish decor.

  • Rantalinna Resort: This lakeside resort boasts a range of accommodation options, from basic cabins to luxury villas. Outdoor activities, such as fishing and boating, are also available.

What to Buy in Imatra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Imatra, here are some ideas:

  • Finnish Crafts: Imatra is home to a vibrant arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans producing handmade ceramics, textiles, and woodwork.

  • Saimaa Caviar: This premium Finnish caviar is produced from roe harvested from local freshwater fish.

  • Finnish Licorice: Finnish licorice has a unique taste and texture, and is available in a wide variety of flavors and styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Imatra

Finland has a rich and unique culture, and there are a few customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of when visiting Imatra. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Finnish Sauna: Sauna is an important part of Finnish culture, and many hotels and spas in Imatra offer sauna facilities.

  • Clothing: Finland has a casual dress code, and visitors should dress in comfortable, practical clothing suitable for the weather.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Finland, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Imatra

Imatra can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit depends on what you’d like to do and see. Summer (June to August) is the most popular time, with long days and warm weather making it ideal for outdoor activities. Winter (December to February) is a great time for skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports, as well as seeing the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Imatra

Do:

  • Do try traditional Finnish cuisine, such as Karelian stew and blueberry pie.

  • Do take a boat tour of the Saimaa Canal and Imatra Waterfall.

  • Do bring comfortable shoes suitable for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the chance to see the breathtaking natural scenery of Imatra.

  • Don’t forget to sample some of the local beverages, such as Finnish beer and koskenkorva vodka.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on what to see and do.

Safety and Health

Safety in Imatra

Imatra is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime and a peaceful atmosphere. However, visitors should still take common sense precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Imatra

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect to receive excellent medical treatment if needed. It’s recommended that visitors obtain comprehensive travel insurance before arriving in Imatra.

Getting to Imatra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Imatra

The nearest airport to Imatra is Lappeenranta Airport, which is located approximately 40 km away. Helsinki Airport, the largest airport in Finland, is located approximately 220 km away.

Bus Stations Near Imatra

Imatra has a bus station located in the town center, offering regional and national connections. Timetables and fares can be found on the Matkahuolto website.

Other Options

Train connections are also available to Imatra’s neighboring cities, such as Lappeenranta and Helsinki. Visitors can check schedules and book tickets on the VR website.

How to Get Around in Imatra

Imatra is a small town with most attractions within walking distance of each other. However, for those who prefer not to walk, there are buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Imatra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Imatra is located in Southern Finland, a region filled with natural beauty and cultural landmarks. Some nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring include:

  • Lappeenranta: This charming lakeside town is located just 25 km from Imatra and features a delightful mix of history and modern amenities.

  • Savonlinna: This historic city, located approximately 115 km from Imatra, is home to Olavinlinna Castle, one of Finland’s most famous landmarks.

  • Koli National Park: This stunning national park is located approximately 200 km from Imatra and offers visitors some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in Finland.

Conclusion

Imatra is a truly special destination, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural history, and Finnish hospitality. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, soak up some local culture, or simply relax and rejuvenate, Imatra has something to offer everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your dream trip to this charming Finnish town and that you’ll come away with memories to last a lifetime.
