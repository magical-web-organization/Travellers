Rautalampi is a small and charming town located in North Savonia, in Finnish Lakeland, and is surrounded by beautiful lakes and forests. It is a perfect destination for those who love nature and outdoor adventures, as well as for those who want to explore Finnish culture and history. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Rautalampi, Finland.

Introduction to Rautalampi

Rautalampi is home to around 3,500 people, and it has a rich history that can be traced back to the Stone Age. The town became more prosperous in the 1800s when iron ore mines were established in the area. Nowadays, Rautalampi is a peaceful town with a friendly atmosphere and offers visitors the opportunity to experience Finnish culture, nature, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Rautalampi

Rautalampi has plenty of places to visit, including culturally important attractions, natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, and more.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Rautalampi Museum is located on the main street of the town and showcases the history of the area. The museum has several exhibitions, including a display of traditional Finnish clothing and artifacts from the iron ore mines that operated in the town. Another culturally important attraction is the Rautalammin Church, which was built in the 1770s and is one of the oldest wooden churches in Finland.

Natural Attractions of Rautalampi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rautalampi is surrounded by beautiful lakes and forests, and you can explore them by taking a boat ride, fishing, or going hiking. One of the top natural attractions in the area is Lake Onkivesi, where you can rent a boat or go fishing. The Kalmoskalmari Nature Trail is a popular hiking trail that offers stunning scenic views of the surrounding forests. Another must-visit attraction in Rautalampi is the Kerkonkoski rapids, which are located on the Keitele River and are known for their natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rautalampi

In addition to the Kalmoskalmari Nature Trail, Rautalampi has several other hiking and biking routes. One of the most popular is the Keitele-Pielavesi route, which is a 60-kilometer-long trail that takes you through beautiful forests and offers stunning views of the lakes. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Rautalampi: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring the natural attractions in the area, there are plenty of other activities and tours to do in Rautalampi. You can take a guided tour of the iron ore mines to learn about the town’s history, or go berry picking and mushroom hunting in the surrounding forests. Another popular activity is horseback riding, and there are several stables in the area that offer horseback riding tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rautalampi

If you have a week to spend in Rautalampi, you can explore the town and its surroundings in-depth. Here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: explore the Rautalampi Museum and the Rautalammin Church
  • Day 2: take a boat ride on Lake Onkivesi and go fishing
  • Day 3: hike the Kalmoskalmari Nature Trail
  • Day 4: take a guided tour of the iron ore mines
  • Day 5: go horseback riding and visit the Kerkonkoski rapids
  • Day 6: relax at one of the town’s public saunas
  • Day 7: take a day trip to one of the nearby towns, such as Kuopio or Jyväskylä

Food and Drink Scene of Rautalampi

Rautalampi has several restaurants and cafes that serve traditional Finnish food, including freshwater fish, mushrooms, and berries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: a traditional Finnish fish pie that is made with freshwater fish and rye flour
  • Karjalanpaisti: a beef stew that is slowly cooked for several hours and served with potatoes and lingonberry jam
  • Mustikkapiirakka: a blueberry pie that is made with a crust of rye flour and served with whipped cream

Local Beverages

  • Salmiakki Koskenkorva: a Finnish vodka that has a salty licorice flavor
  • Lonkero: a traditional Finnish mixed drink that is made with grapefruit soda and gin

Where to Stay in Rautalampi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Rautalampi, ranging from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Rautalampi Hotel: a cozy hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a sauna
  • Guesthouse Onnenmyyrä: a charming guesthouse that is located in the countryside and offers a peaceful atmosphere
  • Rantala Cottages: lakeside cottages that provide a traditional Finnish summer cottage experience

What to Buy in Rautalampi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring back souvenirs from Rautalampi, you can visit the local handicraft shops that sell traditional Finnish goods, such as woolen knitwear and handcrafted wooden items. You can also pick up some locally produced honey or berry products, or Finnish vodka as a gift for someone special.

Local Culture and Customs of Rautalampi

Finnish culture is all about simplicity, nature, and tradition, and Rautalampi exemplifies these traits. Finnish people love spending time in saunas and enjoying the outdoors, and they take pride in their heritage and traditions. When visiting Rautalampi, be sure to respect the local customs and traditions, and take part in the local way of life by visiting the public saunas and enjoying the great outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Rautalampi

The best time to visit Rautalampi is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the days are long, giving you plenty of time to explore the area and enjoy the outdoors. Winter in Rautalampi can be cold and snowy, but it is also an excellent time to visit if you enjoy winter sports, such as cross-country skiing or snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rautalampi

When visiting Rautalampi, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Enjoy the great outdoors and take part in outdoor activities
  • Try the traditional Finnish food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in nature
  • Disturb the wildlife or plants in the area
  • Ignore the beauty of the local scenery and architecture

Safety and Health

Safety in Rautalampi

Rautalampi is a safe place to visit, and the town has a low crime rate. However, it is still important to take standard safety precautions, such as locking your doors and being aware of your surroundings. When hiking or exploring the outdoors, be sure to dress appropriately and carry a map or GPS device.

Health in Rautalampi

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and Rautalampi is no exception. The town has a health center that provides primary medical care. Be sure to bring your European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or other health insurance card when traveling to Finland.

Getting to Rautalampi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rautalampi is located around 400 kilometers north of Helsinki and can be reached by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Rautalampi

The nearest airport to Rautalampi is Kuopio Airport, which is around 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Rautalampi

The closest bus station to Rautalampi is in Suonenjoki, which is around 20 kilometers away. Buses run regularly from Helsinki to Suonenjoki, and from there, you can take a local bus to Rautalampi.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Rautalampi is located just off Highway 9, which runs from Helsinki to Lapland. You can also take a train to either Kuopio or Jyväskylä, and from there, take a bus or rent a car to get to Rautalampi.

How to Get Around in Rautalampi

The best way to get around Rautalampi is by car or bike, as the town is spread out and there are several scenic routes that are best explored by these transportation methods. You can rent a car or bike in town, or bring your own.

Where to Go Next from Rautalampi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rautalampi is located near several other towns and cities that are worth visiting, including Jyväskylä, Kuopio, and Pieksämäki. These towns offer a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and dining options.

Conclusion

Rautalampi is a charming town in Finnish Lakeland that offers visitors the opportunity to experience Finnish culture, history, and nature. From the surrounding lakes and forests to the traditional Finnish food and handicrafts, there is something for everyone in Rautalampi. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Finland.
