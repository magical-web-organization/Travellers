Introduction to Vesanto

Tucked away in the heart of Finnish Lakeland in North Savonia, Vesanto is a charming town offering breathtaking views of tranquil lakes, lush forests, and rolling hills. Renowned for its picturesque landscape, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Vesanto is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking an authentic Nordic experience.

With its wide range of attractions and activities, Vesanto appeals to visitors with diverse interests, from nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts to history buffs and culture vultures.

List of Places to Visit in Vesanto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vesanto church: Built in the 1700s, the church is a masterpiece of Finnish wooden architecture and has beautifully preserved frescoes.
  • Hannula’s House Museum: This traditional Finnish house portrays the life and times of a typical Finn in the early 20th century.
  • Savolaismuseo: This museum houses a valuable collection of artefacts and exhibits that showcase the history and culture of the North Savonia region.

Natural Attractions of Vesanto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lakes: The town is blessed with numerous lakes, with Lake Onkivesi being the largest and most popular. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, boating, and kayaking on these serene waters.
  • Vesanto Forest: Home to a variety of wildlife, hiking trails sprinkle this forest, and nature lovers can marvel at the sights and sounds of this tranquil haven. During winter, visitors can also enjoy cross-country skiing.
  • Metsäkartano Nature and Culture Centre: This center offers a glimpse into Finnish wilderness life. It has a variety of outdoor activities like snowshoeing, canoeing, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vesanto

  • Puhipirtti Trail: This trail cuts through the Vesanto forest and provides a glimpse of Finnish biodiversity.
  • Kumpuvaara Hill Trail: Featuring scenic views of the entire Onkivesi Lake, this hike rewards visitors with an unforgettable panoramic view at its peak.
  • Pyörävaellusreitisto 554: This 22 km trail is popular amongst bikers and offers a mixture of urban and countryside landscape.

Best Things to Do in Vesanto: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: With several lakes to choose from, visitors can experience world-class fishing in the region.
  • Safari tours: Tourists can explore local forests with snowmobiles, dog sleds, and ATVs for a unique cultural experience.
  • Festival: Vesanto hosts annual festivals that celebrate the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Vesanto

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish dish consisting of fish, pork, and rye bread baked inside a loaf of bread.
  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce.
  • Poronkäristys: A reindeer stew served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Koskenkorva: Finnish vodka made from barley and distilled water.
  • Olut: Finnish beer, noted for its potent taste.

Where to Stay in Vesanto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kartano Kievari: A historic mansion turned into a hotel, offering visitors an authentic Finnish experience.
  • Villa Hänninen: A rustic-style villa located on the waterfront of Lake Onkivesi, with idyllic views of the surroundings.
  • Äksyt Ämmät Hostel: Affordable and cozy hostel suitable for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Vesanto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kalevala Jewelry: A jewelry line with a unique local design.
  • Liquorice: Finnish liquorice famous all around the world.
  • Lapland Knives: Finnish knives made by Lapland’s traditional methods using birch wood handles.

Local Culture and Customs of Vesanto

Vesanto’s culture is a mix of Finnish and Karelian traditions that are still intact centuries later. Finns are reserved but friendly people, proud of their culture, and clad in traditional costumes during festivals. The traditional sauna culture is still prevalent here because it’s the best way to relax and spend quality time with family.

Best Time to Visit Vesanto

The best time to visit Vesanto is during summer from June to August. It’s warmer, with lush green scenery, longer days, and the lake district is at its finest. In winter, Vesanto transforms into a winter wonderland, with snowshoeing, cross-country skiing and ice-fishing as popular activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vesanto

Do:

  • Respect Finnish customs and traditions.
  • Always take off your shoes before entering someone’s house.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be too loud or revealing in public areas.
  • Don’t be rude or impolite.
  • Don’t take photographs of others without first seeking their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vesanto

Vesanto is a safe destination, and crime rates are relatively low. However, outdoor enthusiasts should take precautions, such as carrying a map and letting someone know of their planned activities before venturing into the forests.

Health in Vesanto

Finland has excellent healthcare facilities, and Vesanto is no exception. Tourists can rest assured that they can access quality medical services if the need arises.

Getting to Vesanto: Transportation Options and Logistics

It’s easy to get to Vesanto by plane, train, or car.

Airports Near Vesanto

  • Kuopio Airport (KUO) - 108 km
  • Jyväskylä Airport (JYV) - 111 km

Bus Stations Near Vesanto

  • Vesanto Bus Station
  • Rautavaara Bus Station

Other Options

  • Renting a car or bike allows visitors to explore Vesanto’s scenic regions.
  • A train ticket from Helsinki to Vesanto takes four to six hours, and a bus ride from Helsinki takes about seven hours.

How to Get Around in Vesanto

Public transportation is limited in Vesanto, and renting a bike or car is the most convenient way to get around. Taxis are also available around the city.

Where to Go Next from Vesanto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kuopio: A historic city located on the shore of Lake Kallavesi, with magnificent views and shopping options.
  • Savonlinna: Famous for its spectacular castle and festivals, offers a great experience for those in search of unique cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Vesanto is a hidden gem, offering a harmonious blend of nature, culture, and tradition. Its pristine natural landscape and rich cultural history make it a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Finnish experience. So pack your bags and get ready to explore the hidden treasures of Vesanto, Finland.
