Introduction to Suonenjoki

Located in the North Savonia region of Finnish Lakeland, Suonenjoki is a picturesque town known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by lakes, rivers, and forests, Suonenjoki is the perfect destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life and immerse themselves in Finland’s breathtaking outdoor landscape. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical sites, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in nature, Suonenjoki has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Suonenjoki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Suonenjoki Church: This beautiful wooden church was built in 1863 and is one of the oldest buildings in the town. Admire its rustic architecture, gaze at its stunning stained glass windows, and learn about the history of the Lutheran Church in Suonenjoki.
  • Käkisalmen Hovi: Explore the historic manor house of Käkisalmen Hovi, which dates back to the 17th century. Learn about the history of the manor and its owners and admire the stunning architecture and interior design.
  • Suonenjoki Museum: Discover the history and culture of Suonenjoki at this fascinating museum. Exhibits include artifacts from local industry, as well as displays on Finnish agricultural history.

Natural Attractions of Suonenjoki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Kerkonjärvi: Enjoy swimming, fishing, and boating at this beautiful lake, surrounded by lush trees and scenic views.
  • Kolmisoppi Nature Reserve: Hike or bike through this pristine nature reserve and explore its many trails, streams, and forests.
  • Hautalahti Arboretum: Stroll through this lovely arboretum, which boasts a collection of rare and exotic trees from around the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suonenjoki

  • Kolmisoppi Nature Reserve Trail: This 6-kilometer trail winds through the dense forests and scenic landscapes of Kolmisoppi Nature Reserve, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Kerkonjärvi Lake Trail: This easy 2-kilometer trail follows the shore of Lake Kerkonjärvi and takes you through beautiful forests and fields, perfect for a relaxing nature walk or bike ride.
  • Hautalahti Arboretum Trail: This short 1-kilometer trail takes you through the lovely Hautalahti Arboretum, offering a peaceful stroll among rare and exotic trees from around the world.

Best Things to Do in Suonenjoki: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Suonenjoki is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts, with an abundance of lakes and rivers teeming with fish.
  • Boating: Rent a boat and explore the picturesque waterways of Suonenjoki, including Lake Kerkonjärvi and other nearby lakes and rivers.
  • Mushroom and Berry Picking: Forage for wild mushrooms and berries in the surrounding forests and fields, a popular activity among locals and visitors alike.

Food and Drink Scene of Suonenjoki

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: This traditional Finnish dish consists of rye bread pastry filled with fish and pork, then baked in a wood-fired oven. A local specialty in Suonenjoki.
  • Leipäjuusto: Also known as “Finnish squeaky cheese,” this cheese is made from cow or reindeer milk and served warm with cloudberry jam.
  • Karjalanpaisti: A hearty stew made with beef or pork, root vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Karelian beer: Produced in the nearby town of Joensuu, this craft beer is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.
  • Lingonberry juice: Made from locally grown lingonberries, this refreshing juice is a must-try when in Suonenjoki.

Where to Stay in Suonenjoki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Suonenjoki: This cozy hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location in the center of town.
  • Lomakoti Kotoranta: This charming guesthouse is situated right on the shore of Lake Kerkonjärvi, offering stunning views and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Suonenjoen Kievari: This hostel-style accommodation is perfect for budget travelers, offering basic yet comfortable rooms and easy access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Suonenjoki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted textiles: Suonenjoki is known for its traditional Finnish textiles, including woven rugs, blankets, and tablecloths.
  • Local honey: Produced by nearby beekeepers, Finnish honey is a delicious souvenir to bring back home.
  • Natural health products: Suonenjoki is home to a variety of businesses selling natural health products, including herbal remedies and essential oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Suonenjoki

Finland is a nation proud of its customs and traditions. In Suonenjoki, there are a few customs and cultural nuances to keep in mind during your visit.

  • Guests are expected to remove their shoes before entering homes and some public places.
  • Personal space is highly valued in Finnish culture, so it is best to avoid physical contact unless initiated by the other person.
  • Drinking alcohol in public is generally not allowed, except during specific events such as festivals or concerts.

Best Time to Visit Suonenjoki

The best time to visit Suonenjoki is during the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures are mild and the landscape is bursting with colorful flowers and foliage. The winter months, from December to February, can be very cold and snowy, making outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suonenjoki

Do:

  • Respect Finnish customs and traditions.
  • Wear appropriate clothing for the season and outdoor activities.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Suonenjoki.

Don’t:

  • Make loud noises in public places.
  • Smoke in public areas, including outdoor cafes and patios.
  • Ignore local etiquette, such as removing your shoes inside homes and public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suonenjoki

Suonenjoki is generally a safe and peaceful destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, keep in mind the following tips to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip:

  • Use caution when swimming in lakes and rivers, as currents and underwater obstacles can be dangerous.
  • Follow all safety guidelines when hiking or biking in natural areas to avoid accidents or getting lost.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and personal belongings in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Suonenjoki

Finland has a modern and advanced healthcare system, with high-quality medical services available throughout the country. However, it is always recommended to purchase travel health insurance before your trip to ensure peace of mind in case of unexpected illness or injury.

Getting to Suonenjoki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Suonenjoki

The closest airport to Suonenjoki is Kuopio Airport, located approximately 40 kilometers away. From there, you can rent a car or take a bus to Suonenjoki.

Bus Stations Near Suonenjoki

Suonenjoki has a bus station located in the town center, with regular connections to Kuopio and other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for exploring the surrounding area, as it offers more flexibility and freedom to travel at your own pace. Additionally, biking is a great way to explore Suonenjoki and its nearby attractions, with bike rentals available throughout the town.

How to Get Around in Suonenjoki

Suonenjoki is a small town, and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. Additionally, there is a local bus service available, as well as taxi services for longer journeys or transportation to/from nearby airports.

Where to Go Next from Suonenjoki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kuopio: This charming lakeside town is located just 40 kilometers from Suonenjoki and is known for its stunning cathedral, lively marketplaces, and beautiful scenery.
  • Siilinjärvi: Just 25 kilometers away, this tranquil town offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and skiing.
  • Puijo Tower: Located in Kuopio, this tower offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a must-visit attraction for anyone exploring the North Savonia region.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and range of exciting outdoor activities, Suonenjoki is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Finland’s North Savonia region. From hiking and biking through pristine nature reserves to exploring historical sites and sampling delicious local cuisine, Suonenjoki has something to offer everyone, no matter their interests or travel style. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming town has to offer!
