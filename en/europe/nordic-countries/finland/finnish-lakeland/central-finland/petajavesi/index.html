Introduction to Petäjävesi

Petäjävesi is a small municipality located in Central Finland, in the Finnish Lakeland region. This charming town has a lot to offer to tourists looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination.

Petäjävesi is known for its historical wooden church, Petäjävesi Old Church, which was built in the 1760s and is now a UNESCO World Heritage site. The surrounding area is also rich in natural beauty, with many hiking and biking trails, lakes and forests, and opportunities for outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Petäjävesi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Petäjävesi Old Church is the must-visit destination in the town. This historic wooden church dates back to the 18th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The beautiful interior includes details such as a painted pulpit from 1779 and a carved altarpiece with scenes from the Bible.

Another cultural attraction worth a visit is Johannes Linnankoski Museum, which is located near the church. This museum offers interesting insights into the life and works of a Finnish author, Johannes Linnankoski. It’s housed in a charming early 19th-century building, which has been restored to its original condition.

Natural Attractions of Petäjävesi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Petäjävesi has a lot of beautiful natural scenery. One must-visit outdoor attraction is the Saarijärvi-Konnevesi route, which offers a fantastic hiking or cycling experience through beautiful forests, scenic lakes, and tranquil countryside.

Another scenic spot in Petäjävesi is Kalmusaari, which is a beautiful island on the Saarijärvi Lake. The island is part of the Saarijärvi-Konnevesi route, and you can reach there easily by a ferry. You can also rent a paddleboat or a kayak to explore the island and its surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petäjävesi

Petäjävesi offers many hiking and biking trails for all levels of outdoor enthusiasts. One of the most popular routes is the Saarijärvi-Konnevesi route, which is a 120 km trail that takes you through beautiful natural landscapes and stunning landscapes.

For those who want a shorter hike, the Jämsänoro nature trail is another viable option. This trail is 3 km long and provides an opportunity to explore the area’s natural beauty while enjoying hiking through the serene forest.

Best Things to Do in Petäjävesi: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours that you can enjoy while in Petäjävesi, such as:

  • Visit Johannes Linnankoski Museum and learn about the life and works of a famous Finnish writer.
  • Explore the beautiful Saarijärvi-Konnevesi route on a paddleboat or kayak.
  • Join a guided tour of Petäjävesi Old Church and learn about its fascinating history and architecture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Petäjävesi

If you’re planning to stay in Petäjävesi for a week, here’s an itinerary that you can follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Petäjävesi

Must-Try Dishes

The Petäjävesi food scene mainly revolves around traditional Finnish cuisine.

Some of the must-try dishes to sample while in Petäjävesi include:

  • Karjalanpaisti (Karelian Stew) - a savory stew made with beef or pork and vegetables.
  • Leipäjuusto (Finnish Squeaky Cheese) - a traditional Finnish cheese that squeaks when chewed. It’s made from fresh cow or reindeer milk and is usually served with lingonberry jam.
  • Kalakukko - a traditional Finnish pastry filled with fish and porridge.

Local Beverages

Petäjävesi is known for its craft beer, and Alahovi Brewery is the place to visit to sample some local brews. The brewery offers tours that include tastings of their award-winning beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Petäjävesi

Petäjävesi has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their traditions and customs. Some of the cultural events and festivals that take place in Petäjävesi include:

Best Time to Visit Petäjävesi

The best time to visit Petäjävesi is during the summer months from June to August.
