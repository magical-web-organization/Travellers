Introduction to Multia

Located in Central Finland, in the heart of Finnish Lakeland, Multia is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. Situated in the Nordic countries, Multia is a small municipality that is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Here, visitors can explore pristine lakes, dense forests, and rolling hills that provide a perfect backdrop for a range of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to fishing and canoeing. Additionally, Multia is home to an array of museums, historic sites, and cultural attractions, giving visitors a chance to learn more about the region’s fascinating history and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Multia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Multia Museum

Located in the heart of the village, Multia Museum is a must-visit attraction that showcases the region’s rich cultural heritage and history. The museum houses a range of exhibits, including artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays, that offer visitors a fascinating insight into Multia’s past.

Kaunisnurmi Estate

Built in the early 1900s, Kaunisnurmi Estate is an elegant mansion that has been converted into a cultural center. Visitors can explore the mansion and its stunning grounds while learning more about the region’s cultural traditions and heritage.

Alpo Jaakola Statuary Park

Nestled in the heart of Multia’s countryside, Alpo Jaakola Statuary Park is a sculpture park that features more than 50 large-scale sculptures by Finnish artist Alpo Jaakola. The park offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore art in a beautiful natural setting.

Natural Attractions of Multia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Multia Lake

Multia Lake is a picturesque lake that is perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating. Surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills, this lake offers visitors a serene natural setting that is perfect for relaxing and unwinding.

Isojärvi National Park

Located just a short drive from Multia, Isojärvi National Park is a popular destination for hikers, campers, and nature lovers. The park boasts a network of hiking trails that take visitors through stunning forests, lakes, and rugged terrain, offering a glimpse of the region’s natural beauty.

Multia Ridge Trail

The Multia Ridge Trail is a scenic hiking route that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. The trail takes visitors through dense forests and rolling hills, passing by lakes, waterfalls, and other natural attractions along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Multia

The Multia Hiking Trail

The Multia Hiking Trail is a popular hiking route that takes visitors through some of the region’s most scenic landscapes. The trail is well-marked and offers a range of routes to suit all abilities, from short walks to multi-day treks.

The Multia Biking Trail

The Multia Biking Trail is a challenging yet rewarding route that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail takes visitors through dense forests and hills, offering a glimpse of the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Multia: Activities and Tours

Multia Cultural Tour

The Multia Cultural Tour takes visitors on a journey through the region’s rich cultural heritage, showcasing some of its most important cultural attractions. The tour includes visits to museums, historic sites, and cultural centers, giving visitors a chance to learn more about the region’s fascinating history and traditions.

Outdoor Adventure Tour

The Outdoor Adventure Tour is perfect for those who love the great outdoors. The tour takes visitors through Multia’s most scenic landscapes, including lakes, forests, and hills, and includes activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Multia

Day 1: Arrive in Multia and explore the village and Multia Museum.

Day 2: Visit Kaunisnurmi Estate and explore the grounds.

Day 3: Go hiking in Isojärvi National Park and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 4: Take the Multia Ridge Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 5: Embark on the Multia Cultural Tour and learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage.

Day 6: Relax by Multia Lake and enjoy the beautiful natural setting.

Day 7: Go on the Outdoor Adventure Tour and explore the region’s stunning natural landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Multia

Must-Try Dishes

Perunakakkara

Perunakakkara is a traditional Finnish dish that is made from potatoes, flour, and butter. It is often served with lingonberry jam and can be enjoyed as a main dish or a dessert.

Karjalanpaisti

Karjalanpaisti is a traditional Finnish stew that is made with pork, beef, and potatoes. It is often served with lingonberry jam and rye bread.

Local Beverages

Finnish Beer

Finnish beer is a popular drink in Multia, and visitors can enjoy a range of locally-brewed beers at local pubs and restaurants.

Berry Juice

Made from locally-grown berries, berry juice is a refreshing and healthy drink that is popular in Multia and throughout Finland.

Where to Stay in Multia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Kuoreksentie

Hotel Kuoreksentie is a comfortable and modern hotel that is located in the heart of Multia. It offers a range of amenities, including free Wi-Fi, a sauna, and a restaurant.

Multian Matkakoti

Multian Matkakoti is a charming guesthouse that is located in the village of Multia. It offers cozy and comfortable rooms, as well as free Wi-Fi and ample parking space.

What to Buy in Multia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Soaps

Multia is known for its handmade soaps, which are made from natural ingredients and come in a range of scents and flavors.

Local Crafts

Visitors can also find a range of locally-made crafts in Multia, including knitwear, woven textiles, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Multia

Multia is a small and welcoming community that is known for its warm hospitality and rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, which include a strong emphasis on family, community, and nature.

Best Time to Visit Multia

The best time to visit Multia is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. However, Multia is also a popular destination during the winter months, when visitors can enjoy snow sports and other winter activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Multia

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take care of the natural environment
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Multia

Multia is a safe and welcoming destination, but visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any other destination. It is important to lock your doors and windows, keep valuables out of sight, and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in Multia

Visitors to Multia should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance, as well as any necessary vaccinations for travel to Finland. It is also important to bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Multia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Multia

The nearest airport to Multia is Jyväskylä Airport, which is located about 60 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to get to Multia from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Multia

Multia is well-connected by bus to other destinations in Finland. Visitors can take a bus from Jyväskylä or Helsinki to Multia.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car to get to Multia, or take a train to nearby Jyväskylä and then take a bus or taxi to Multia.

How to Get Around in Multia

The best way to get around Multia is by car or bike. Visitors can hire a car or bike from local rental companies, or bring their own. There are also buses that connect Multia to other destinations in the region.

Where to Go Next from Multia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jyväskylä

Jyväskylä is a vibrant university town that is located about 60 kilometers from Multia. Visitors can explore the town’s museums, cultural attractions, and shopping districts, as well as enjoy its vibrant nightlife.

Tampere

Tampere is a bustling city that is located about 150 kilometers from Multia. Visitors can explore its historic sites, cultural attractions, and vibrant food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Multia is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Multia offers visitors a unique and unforgettable travel experience. So whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing holiday in beautiful surroundings, Multia has something for everyone.
