Introduction to Rantasalmi

Rantasalmi is a beautiful town located in South Savonia, in Finnish Lakeland, Finland. The town is situated between two large lakes, Lake Saimaa and Lake Unnukka, making it a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts. The town is also rich in cultural and historical heritage, with several museums and historic places that showcase the town’s past.

List of Places to Visit in Rantasalmi

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply want to relax and unwind, Rantasalmi has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Rantasalmi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lekotti Rural Museum: This museum showcases the rural way of life in the 19th and 20th centuries, with several exhibits showcasing the local handicrafts, tools, and textiles.

  • Rantasalmi Church: This beautiful wooden church was built in the 18th century and features impressive wood carvings and paintings.

  • Kylpyla Museum: This museum tells the story of Rantasalmi’s spa culture, which dates back to the 19th century. Visitors can learn about the town’s spa industry and its impact on tourism.

Natural Attractions of Rantasalmi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Linnansaari National Park: This pristine wilderness area is located on an island in Lake Haukivesi and is home to several rare birds and animals, including the Saimaa ringed seal.

  • Haukivesi Archipelago: This group of islands is a popular destination for kayaking, fishing, and camping. The islands offer stunning views of the surrounding lakes and forests.

  • Rokansaari Island: This island is a unique outdoor attraction that features several unusual rock formations and is home to a variety of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rantasalmi

The surrounding area of Rantasalmi offers many options for hiking and biking. Some of the popular hiking trails in the area include:

  • Lekotti Nature Trail: This 2.5-kilometer nature trail winds through the forest and along the shores of Lake Lekotti.

  • Joutenvesi Trail: This 25-kilometer trail takes hikers through the beautiful Joutenvesi archipelago and offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding forests.

  • Kuvansi-Virmasvesi Trail: This 30-kilometer trail connects two lakes, Kuvansi and Virmasvesi, and takes hikers through a mix of forests and open landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Rantasalmi: Activities and Tours

Rantasalmi offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Rantasalmi:

  • Take a cruise on Lake Saimaa: This is a great way to see the surrounding landscapes and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

  • Visit Rantasalmi Spa: This spa offers a range of treatments and services to help visitors relax and unwind.

  • Try fishing: Rantasalmi is known for its fishing opportunities, and visitors can try their hand at catching some of the local fish species, such as pike and perch.

Food and Drink Scene of Rantasalmi

Rantasalmi is known for its delicious local cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the surrounding forests and lakes. Here are some of the dishes and beverages that visitors should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: This is a traditional Finnish food which is made by wrapping fish in rye dough and baking it in an oven.

  • Riistaruoat: This refers to the local game dishes made from moose and other wild animals found in the forest.

  • Leipajuusto: This is a type of cheese made from cow’s milk, which is baked in the oven until it turns golden brown.

Local Beverages

  • Sima: This is a Finnish mead which is made from fermented water, sugar, lemon, and yeast.

  • Salmiakkikossu: This is a popular Finnish cocktail made by mixing Salmiakki liqueur and vodka.

Local Culture and Customs of Rantasalmi

Rantasalmi has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can learn about the town’s history through its museums and historic places. Finnish culture is known for its sauna culture, which is deeply ingrained in everyday life. Visitors to Rantasalmi can experience the Finnish sauna culture by visiting one of the local saunas.
