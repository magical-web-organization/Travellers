Introduction to Punkaharju

Punkaharju is a small municipality located in the heart of the Finnish Lakeland in South Savonia. Its name comes from the unique and stunning ridge, Punkaharju Esker, which spans 7 km along Lake Saimaa. This geological formation was left behind by a glacier some 12,000 years ago and has since become a beloved destination for nature enthusiasts, hikers, and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Punkaharju

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kerimäki Church

The Kerimäki Church is the largest wooden church in the world, with enough room for roughly 5000 people. Built in 1848, it impresses visitors with its 37-meter-high dome and beautiful frescoes painted by renowned Finnish artist Akseli Gallen-Kallela.

Lusto - The Finnish Forest Museum

Step into the forest and learn about the history, products, and culture of Finnish forestry at Lusto. Explore exhibitions of different tree species and their use, as well as traditional forest livelihoods and handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Punkaharju: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Punkaharju Esker

Take a walk on the Punkaharju Esker and take in the stunning views of Lake Saimaa and the surrounding nature. The path is easy to walk, and those lucky enough to be here on a clear day will be rewarded with breathtaking views.

Linnansaari National Park

Linnansaari is a unique island archipelago and a heaven for those seeking peace and quiet, hiking trails, and unique wildlife spotting opportunities. Explore the lake by boat or kayak and see if you can spot the rare Saimaa ringed seal, which can only be found in this area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Punkaharju

Harju Trail

The Harju Trail is a popular 5 km hiking trail that takes visitors along the ridge and through the beautiful surroundings of Punkaharju. It’s a must-visit if you appreciate stunning natural landscapes.

Puumala Archipelago Trail

Take to the water by bike and explore the Puumala Archipelago Trail, a demanding 40 km bike trail that goes through the beautiful Finnish archipelago. En route, enjoy some of the astonishingly pristine and peaceful nature.

Best Things to Do in Punkaharju: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding at Maatilamatkailu Peräkoski Farm

Experience Finnish rural life and take a horse ride in the stunning nature and beautiful Finnish countryside! This fun activity is ideal for both beginners and experienced riders alike, and will leave you with unforgettable memories.

Guided Fishing Trips

Join a guided fishing trip on Lake Saimaa, and catch some of Finland’s tastiest fish, such as pike, perch, and salmon. Experienced guides will give valuable fishing tips and share their knowledge about the beautiful surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Punkaharju

Day 1: Arrive in Punkaharju and settle into your accommodation. Day 2: Explore the Punkaharju Ridge and visit the Kerimäki Church. Day 3: Visit Lusto - The Finnish Forest Museum and learn more about the country’s forestry heritage. Day 4: Kayak in Lake Saimaa and explore the Linnansaari National Park. Day 5: Take a horseback ride in the countryside at Maatilamatkailu Peräkoski Farm. Day 6: Go on a guided fishing trip on the lake. Day 7: Check out and leave Punkaharju for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Punkaharju

Must-Try Dishes

Karjalanpaisti

Karjalanpaisti is a traditional Finnish meat and vegetable stew with a unique and delicious flavor that everyone should try. It usually contains beef or pork, onions, carrots, and potatoes, and is often seasoned with a combination of salt, allspice, and bay leaves.

Kalakukko

Kalakukko is a Finnish fish pasty that originated in the eastern part of the country. It consists of a pastry filled with fish and bacon, and is a staple dish in the province of Savonia.

Local Beverages

Lonkero

Lonkero is a Finnish alcoholic beverage made of gin and grapefruit soda. It’s a refreshing drink and perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot summer day.

Sahti

Sahti is a traditional Finnish beer made from malted barley, rye, and juniper berries. It’s brewed as a homebrew and is not available in most Finnish bars.

Where to Stay in Punkaharju: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Punkaharju

One of the most popular places to stay at Punkaharju is Hotel Punkaharju. This hotel has a fantastic location near Lake Saimaa and offers comfortable rooms with picturesque views of the lake.

Kerimaa Holiday Centre

Kerimaa Holiday Centre is an excellent choice for those wanting to reconnect with nature while still enjoying modern amenities. The accommodation is located in the beautiful forests of Kerimaa, surrounded by hiking trails and lakes.

What to Buy in Punkaharju: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Savonlinna Marketplace

Savonlinna Marketplace offers a variety of products that are traditional of the Savonia region. You’ll find handmade items, including clothing, jewelry, and wooden handicrafts, as well as Finnish delicacies, such as smoked fish, cloudberry jam, and reindeer sausage.

Local Culture and Customs of Punkaharju

Finland is known for its love of saunas, and Punkaharju is no exception. Don’t be surprised if you’re invited to join a sauna session as part of your stay. Also, it’s customary to remove your shoes when entering private homes and most accommodations, so make sure to pack some cozy socks or slippers.

Best Time to Visit Punkaharju

The summer months from June to August are the most popular due to the warm weather, extended sunlight, and the long list of outdoor activities that this area offers. Winter brings the possibility of experiencing the magical Northern Lights, but the weather can be extremely cold, so be prepared.

Do’s and Don’ts of Punkaharju

Do:

  • Respect Finnish customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy the beautiful nature and try a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Visit cultural and historical attractions, especially the Kerimäki Church and Lusto.

Don’t:

  • Forget to remove your shoes when entering private homes or accommodations.
  • Be rude or impolite to locals.
  • Leave trash behind or damage the beautiful natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Punkaharju

Punkaharju is a safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and relatively good medical facilities. As always, take necessary precautions such as locking your room and car, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Punkaharju

No specific health risks are associated with travel to Punkaharju, but remember to pack accordingly to the weather and insect repellent during summer months.

Getting to Punkaharju: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Punkaharju

The nearest airports are Lappeenranta Airport (LPP) and Savonlinna Airport (SVL), both approximately 50 km from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Punkaharju

Multiple bus lines connect Punkaharju with other cities around Finland. The local bus operator for Punkaharju is Savonlinja.

Other Options

Renting a car is a convenient option to drive around the region and explore the town and countryside.

How to Get Around in Punkaharju

The best way to explore the town of Punkaharju is by bike or on foot. However, renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the surrounding areas and hike nearby trails.

Where to Go Next from Punkaharju: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savonlinna

The picturesque town of Savonlinna, located on the shores of Lake Saimaa, is a must-visit destination. It’s home to the enchanting Olavinlinna Castle, and it’s a great place to try Finnish delicacies and learn more about the culture.

Helsinki

Experience Finland’s cosmopolitan capital, Helsinki, only three and a half hours from Punkaharju by car. Helsinki is known for its beautiful architecture, stunning harbor, and fascinating Nordic culture.

Conclusion

Punkaharju is a hidden gem in the heart of Finnish Lakeland, offering its visitors a unique blend of nature, culture, and Finnish traditions. With so much to see and do, it’s no wonder that the area has become a beloved destination for travelers worldwide. Whether it’s hiking, biking, kayaking, or simply relaxing in a sauna, there’s something for everyone. So start packing your bags, and come discover the magic of Punkaharju!
