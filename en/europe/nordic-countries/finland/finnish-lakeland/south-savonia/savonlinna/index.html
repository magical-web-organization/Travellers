Introduction to Savonlinna

Situated in South Savonia, in Finnish Lakeland, in Finland, in the Nordic countries, and in Europe, Savonlinna is a beautiful town that offers spectacular views of the serene Lake Saimaa. With a rich history, natural beauty and vibrant culture, it is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Finland.

List of Places to Visit in Savonlinna

Savonlinna offers a range of attractions that are perfect for cultural enthusiasts, nature lovers, and adventure seekers looking for an exciting holiday.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Savonlinna is steeped in rich history and culture. Here are some must-visit historical sites and museums that explore the town’s past:

  • Savonlinna Provincial Museum showcases the history and culture of Savonlinna and Lakeland.
  • Olavinlinna Castle is one of the main attractions in Savonlinna and serves as a symbol of Finnish history and culture. It hosts opera performances, guided tours, and exhibitions.
  • Kerimäki Church is the largest wooden church in the world and is a prime example of Finnish architecture.
  • Lusto – The Finnish Forest Museum showcases the country’s natural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Savonlinna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its stunning lake views and lush forests, Savonlinna is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Savonlinna:

  • Lake Saimaa is the fourth largest lake in Europe and offers a range of water activities such as kayaking, canoeing, fishing, and swimming.
  • Oravi Nature Trail is a scenic route that takes you through the lush forests and serene lakefront views.
  • Kolovesi National Park offers hiking trails and canoeing routes, where visitors can spot rare wildlife such as Saimaa seals, ospreys, and golden eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savonlinna

Savonlinna offers an extensive network of hiking and biking routes that cater to both novice and seasoned adventurers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Pikkukili is a 10 km hiking trail that takes you through the forest around Lake Saimaa’s eastern shore.
  • Kolovesi-Kerma route is a 20 km route that takes you through the Kolovesi National Park.
  • Savonranta Circuit is a 70 km cycling route that offers beautiful scenic views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Savonlinna: Activities and Tours

Savonlinna offers a range of activities and tours that are perfect for solo travelers, couples and families. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Opera Festival in July. Enjoy opera performances on Olavinlinna Castle’s outdoor stage.
  • Saimaa Seal Watching Tour. Take a boat tour around Lake Saimaa and spot the rare endangered Saimaa seals.
  • Canoe Safari. Enjoy a scenic canoe safari in the beautiful Kalmonjärvi Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Savonlinna

Savonlinna’s food scene is a delightful mix of Finnish and Karelian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that are unique to Savonlinna:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpaisti: A traditional Karelian pork and beef stew.
  • Muikku: Fried vendace fish, a local delicacy in Savonlinna.
  • Lihapiirakka: Karelian-style meat pasty, a popular street food.

Local Beverages

  • Saimaa Brewery: Savonlinna’s home brewery that produces a range of local beers.
  • Saimaa Gin: A locally produced gin using traditional Finnish botanicals.

Where to Stay in Savonlinna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Savonlinna offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Spahotel Casino is a luxurious hotel situated near the Olavinlinna Castle. It offers a range of amenities such as spa, gym, and sauna.
  • Summer Hotel Tott is a budget-friendly hotel located by Lake Saimaa.
  • Hostel Laituri is a hostel situated in the heart of Savonlinna and is perfect for backpackers.

What to Buy in Savonlinna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Savonlinna offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make for excellent gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Savonlinna:

  • Saimaa Pearl: Handcrafted jewelry made from freshwater pearls from Lake Saimaa.
  • Karelian Pastries: Delicious pastries filled with rice, potatoes, or meat, a traditional delicacy in Karelia.
  • Saimaa Gin: Bring home a bottle of locally produced gin with traditional Finnish botanicals.

Local Culture and Customs of Savonlinna

Savonlinna has a deep-rooted culture and customs that are unique to the region. Here are some cultural facts about Savonlinna:

  • The town is known for its traditional Karelian culture, which is reflected in its food, music, and festivals.
  • The Opera Festival is a significant cultural event in Savonlinna, attracting visitors from around the world.
  • The town celebrates the midsummer festival with bonfires, music, and food.

Best Time to Visit Savonlinna

The best time to visit Savonlinna is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm, and the lakes are crystal clear. Visitors can participate in a range of activities and festivals during this time, making it the perfect time to explore the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savonlinna

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drink.
  • Do experience the outdoors and participate in outdoor activities.
  • Do attend the Opera Festival.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t miss out on the beautiful natural scenery.

Safety and Health

Safety in Savonlinna

Savonlinna is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Savonlinna

Finland has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors to Savonlinna can access medical care through public healthcare centers and private clinics. It is important to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Savonlinna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Savonlinna can be reached via various modes of transport, including air, bus, and train. Here are some options:

Airports Near Savonlinna

Savonlinna Airport is the nearest airport, located about 25 km from the town center. It offers domestic flights to Helsinki.

Bus Stations Near Savonlinna

Savonlinna Bus Station is situated in the town center and offers connections to various destinations across Finland.

Other Options

Savonlinna can also be reached by train from Helsinki.

How to Get Around in Savonlinna

Savonlinna has an extensive public transport system, including buses and trains. Taxis are also readily available. Rental cars are available at the airport or in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Savonlinna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savonlinna’s location in the heart of Finnish Lakeland makes it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some popular nearby destinations:

  • Kerimäki: A small town located about 20 km from Savonlinna, famous for its wooden church.
  • Linnansaari National Park: A stunning area that features a network of islands and waterways, perfect for fishing and hiking.
  • Kuopio: A lively city situated about 150 km from Savonlinna, known for its museums and nightlife.

Conclusion

Savonlinna is a town steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty, offering a range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. With excellent transport links, a range of accommodation options, and a diverse culinary scene, Savonlinna is the perfect destination for an unforgettable holiday in Finnish Lakeland.
