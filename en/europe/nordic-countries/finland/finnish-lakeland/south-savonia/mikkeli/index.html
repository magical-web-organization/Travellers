Introduction to Mikkeli

Mikkeli is a beautiful city in South Savonia, Finnish Lakeland, Finland. It is known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, and warm hospitality. Mikkeli is an ideal destination for travelers who are looking for a peaceful retreat in nature, away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Mikkeli

Here are some of the top attractions in Mikkeli that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mikkeli Cathedral: This iconic Lutheran church is one of the oldest buildings in Mikkeli. It is known for its stunning murals and impressive bell tower.
  • Mikkeli Art Museum: The museum showcases a collection of contemporary and traditional Finnish art, including paintings, sculptures, and installations.
  • Kenkavero Manor: Visit this 19th-century manor to learn about the history and culture of the Finnish aristocracy. You can also enjoy a traditional Finnish meal at the manor’s restaurant.

Natural Attractions of Mikkeli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Visulahti: This adventure park offers a range of activities, including water slides, zip lines, and mini-golf. There is also a beautiful beach to relax on.
  • Kenkavero Forest: Take a nature walk in this beautiful forest and enjoy the fresh air while admiring the native flora and fauna.
  • Lakes and Islands: Mikkeli is surrounded by beautiful lakes and islands. Take a boat tour or rent a kayak to explore the peaceful waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mikkeli

Mikkeli has several scenic hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Pursiala Ridge: This ridge offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest and lakes. The trail is 7.5 km long and takes approximately 2–3 hours to complete.
  • Korpikansa Trail: This trail is 10 km long and passes through a charming forest. You can see various species of birds and wildflowers along the trail.
  • Etelä-Kulju Trail: This trail is 4 km long and takes you through beautiful meadows and fields. It is an excellent option for families with young children.

Best Things to Do in Mikkeli: Activities and Tours

Mikkeli has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Mikkeli:

  • Visit a Sauna: Sauna is an integral part of Finnish culture. Visit one of the many public or private saunas in Mikkeli to relax and rejuvenate.
  • Go Fishing: Mikkeli has some of the best fishing spots in the region. You can catch various freshwater fish, including pike, perch, and trout.
  • Take a Guided Tour: Join a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of Mikkeli. The guides are knowledgeable and friendly, and they can answer any questions you may have.

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Mikkeli:

Food and Drink Scene of Mikkeli

Mikkeli is known for its delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam.
  • Lohikeitto: Creamy salmon soup made with fresh fish, potatoes, and dill.
  • Karjalanpiirakka: Traditional Karelian pastry filled with rice or potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Lonkero: Finnish alcoholic drink made with gin and grapefruit soda.
  • Karelia Konsta: Locally produced craft beer.
  • Salmiakki Koskenkorva: Finnish vodka infused with salty licorice.

Where to Stay in Mikkeli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mikkeli has a range of accommodations to suit various budget and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Scandic Mikkeli: A modern hotel located in the city center. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a sauna.
  • Cumulus City Mikkeli: A cozy hotel located next to the railway station. It has a restaurant, a bar, and a fitness center.
  • Hostel Hermanni: A budget-friendly hostel located in a charming old building. It offers shared and private rooms, a kitchen, and a lounge area.

What to Buy in Mikkeli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mikkeli is known for its locally produced crafts and souvenirs. Here are some of the best options to bring home:

  • Puukko Knives: Traditional Finnish knives made of high-quality steel and birchwood. They are perfect for hunting or camping.
  • Moomin Merchandise: Cute and quirky Moomin-themed souvenirs, including mugs, t-shirts, and plush toys.
  • Kalevala Jewelry: Finnish jewelry inspired by traditional Finnish folklore and mythology.

Local Culture and Customs of Mikkeli

Mikkeli has a rich cultural heritage that is worth exploring. Here are some of the customs and traditions unique to the region:

  • Sauna Culture: Sauna is an integral part of Finnish culture. It is a place of relaxation and socializing, and every Finnish household has a sauna.
  • Midsummer Celebrations: Midsummer is a significant celebration in Finland. It is a time for bonfires, traditional dances, and family gatherings.
  • Finnish Design: Finland is known for its unique design aesthetics, including Marimekko fabrics, Iittala glassware, and Alvar Aalto furniture.

Best Time to Visit Mikkeli

The best time to visit Mikkeli is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and the days are long. You can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, swimming, and fishing. However, if you prefer a quieter atmosphere, consider visiting during the spring or autumn.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mikkeli

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take a sauna and enjoy the beautiful nature.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be disrespectful or rude to the locals.
  • Ignore the safety rules and warnings.

Safety and Health

Mikkeli is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always advisable to take some precautions. Here are some safety and health tips for travelers:

Safety in Mikkeli

  • Always follow the safety rules and regulations.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions, especially if you’re planning outdoor activities.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.

Health in Mikkeli

  • Make sure you have the required vaccinations and medications.
  • Practice proper hygiene and wash your hands regularly.
  • Be aware of the local flora and fauna, especially if you have allergies.

Getting to Mikkeli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mikkeli is well-connected to the rest of Finland. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Mikkeli

  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport: The nearest international airport to Mikkeli, located 220km away. You can travel to Mikkeli by bus or train.
  • Savonlinna Airport: A regional airport located 125km away. You can travel to Mikkeli by bus or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Mikkeli

  • Mikkeli Bus Station: The main bus station in Mikkeli, located next to the railway station. It offers connections to other cities in Finland.

Other Options

  • Train: You can travel to Mikkeli by train from Helsinki or other major cities in Finland.
  • Car: You can rent a car and drive to Mikkeli. The roads are well-maintained and easy to navigate.

How to Get Around in Mikkeli

Mikkeli is a small city that is easy to navigate. You can explore the city on foot or bike. Buses and taxis are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Mikkeli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mikkeli is a great starting point to explore the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations that you can visit:

  • Savonlinna: A charming city located on the shore of Lake Saimaa. It is known for its beautiful castle and opera festival.
  • Lappeenranta: A city located on the Russian border. It is known for its fortress and Hiekkalinna Sandcastle.
  • Kuopio: A lively university town located on the shores of Lake Kallavesi. It is known for its market square and Puijo Tower.

Conclusion

Mikkeli is a hidden gem in Finnish Lakeland. It offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or just a peaceful retreat in nature, Mikkeli has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip to Mikkeli and discover the best of Finland.
