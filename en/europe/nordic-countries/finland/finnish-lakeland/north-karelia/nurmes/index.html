Introduction to Nurmes

Nurmes is a small town located in North Karelia, Finland, and is known for its beautiful natural scenery and cultural attractions. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Nurmes has plenty to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Nurmes.

List of Places to Visit in Nurmes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Pielinen Museum - This museum showcases the history and culture of the Karelian people, including traditional costumes, handicrafts, and photographs.

  2. Lake Pielinen Orthodox Church - Built in the 1940s and featuring traditional Finnish Orthodox architecture, this stunning church is a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts.

  3. Karelia Bastion - This defensive fortress was built in the late 18th century and played an important role in Finnish military history.

Natural Attractions of Nurmes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Koli National Park - Koli National Park is one of the most popular destinations in the region, known for its beautiful forests, rocky hills, and stunning panoramic views of Lake Pielinen.

  2. Ruunaa Hiking Area - Offering dozens of kilometers of scenic hiking and biking trails, this park is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

  3. Hiidenportti National Park - This park is a stunning natural paradise that features rugged rock formations, lush forests, and cascading waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nurmes

  1. Koli Nature Trail - This 2.5 km trail takes visitors on a leisurely stroll through the beautiful forests of Koli National Park.

  2. Pielinen Trail - This 100 km trail offers visitors a challenging hike or bike ride through some of the most beautiful scenery in North Karelia.

  3. Ukko-Koli Hill Trail - This 3.5 km trail leads visitors to the top of Ukko-Koli Hill, offering stunning views of Lake Pielinen along the way.

Best Things to Do in Nurmes: Activities and Tours

  1. Rafting on the Lieksanjoki River - This white-water rafting adventure takes visitors on a thrilling ride down the rapids of the Lieksanjoki River.

  2. Visit to the Bomba House Museum - This beautiful museum showcases traditional Karelian architecture and handicrafts.

  3. Visit to the Koli Relax Spa - This luxurious spa offers visitors a chance to relax and unwind in a beautiful natural setting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nurmes

Day 1: Arrival in Nurmes and check-in at hotel

Day 2: Hiking in Koli National Park

Day 3: Rafting on the Lieksanjoki River

Day 4: Visit to the Pielinen Museum and Lake Pielinen Orthodox Church

Day 5: Visit to Hiidenportti National Park and Ruunaa Hiking Area

Day 6: Visit to the Bomba House Museum and Koli Relax Spa

Day 7: Departure from Nurmes

Food and Drink Scene of Nurmes

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Karjalanpaisti - This traditional Finnish meat stew is made with beef or lamb and slow-cooked with root vegetables and spices.

  2. Kalakukko - This traditional Karelian dish is made with fish and rye dough, and is typically enjoyed as a snack or light meal.

  3. Leipäjuusto - This Finnish cheese is made from cow’s milk and is typically grilled or fried, and served with cloudberry jam.

Local Beverages

  1. Lonkero - This iconic Finnish drink is a mix of grapefruit soda and gin, and is a popular drink among locals.

  2. Karelian beer - This regional Finnish beer is brewed using traditional techniques and local ingredients, and is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Nurmes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Bomba - This beautiful lakeside hotel offers stunning views of Lake Pielinen, and features luxurious rooms and suites, as well as a spa and restaurant.

  2. Holiday Club Nurmeshovi - This modern hotel is located in the heart of town, and offers comfortable rooms and amenities, as well as easy access to local attractions.

  3. Nurmes City Apartments - These fully-equipped apartments offer a comfortable and affordable alternative to traditional hotel stays, with plenty of space and amenities for families and groups.

What to Buy in Nurmes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Karelian pastries - These traditional Finnish pastries are made with rye dough and filled with rice or barley.

  2. Karelian embroidery - This beautiful embroidery style is a traditional Karelian craft, and can be found on clothing, linens, and other souvenirs.

  3. Finnish liquorice - This iconic Finnish candy comes in a variety of flavors and styles, and makes for a tasty and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Nurmes

Local customs in Nurmes are a blend of Finnish and Karelian traditions, with strong influences from Orthodox Christianity. Visitors should be respectful of these traditions and take care to dress appropriately and behave respectfully when visiting local cultural attractions and places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Nurmes

The best time to visit Nurmes is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities and attractions are in full swing. Winter can also be a beautiful time to visit Nurmes, with snow-covered landscapes and a cozy, festive atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nurmes

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of Nurmes, including its parks, lakes, and forests.

  • Do try the local food and drink, especially traditional Karelian dishes and local beverages.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including appropriate dress and behavior when visiting cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.

  • Don’t be disrespectful or disruptive at cultural attractions or places of worship.

  • Don’t forget to try local specialties, like Karelian pastries and Finnish liquorice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nurmes

Nurmes is a safe and welcoming destination for visitors, with low levels of crime and a friendly and helpful local population. Visitors should take standard precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Nurmes

Finland has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors to Nurmes can expect high-quality medical care if needed. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance and familiarize themselves with local emergency services and medical facilities.

Getting to Nurmes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nurmes

The nearest airport to Nurmes is Joensuu Airport, located approximately 85 km away.

Bus Stations Near Nurmes

Nurmes Bus Station is located in the heart of town, with regular bus connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Nurmes by train, with the closest train station located in Joensuu.

How to Get Around in Nurmes

Visitors can get around Nurmes easily on foot or by bike, with many of the town’s attractions located within walking or biking distance of each other. Taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Nurmes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kuopio - This vibrant and cosmopolitan city is located approximately 130 km from Nurmes, and is known for its beautiful architecture, museums, and cultural attractions.

  2. Savonlinna - This charming lakeside city is located approximately 170 km from Nurmes, and is famous for its beautiful medieval castle and exciting cultural events.

Conclusion

Nurmes is a hidden gem in the heart of Finland, with stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and a welcoming local population. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a chance to relax and unwind, Nurmes has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the beauty of North Karelia, Finland.
