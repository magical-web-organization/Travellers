Introduction to Koli National Park

Koli National Park is a hidden gem located in North Karelia, in the Finnish Lakeland region. The park is known for its stunning views of Lake Pielinen and the forested hills of Koli. It covers approximately 30 square kilometers, offering a variety of outdoor activities, natural and cultural attractions, and a glimpse into Finnish culture and customs. With more than 150,000 visitors annually, the park has become one of the most popular tourist attractions in Finland.

List of Places to Visit in Koli National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ukko-Koli Hill: A historical site considered sacred by the ancient Finns, located on the highest point of Koli hill.
  • Koli Nature Centre Ukko: A modern visitor center featuring exhibitions, displays, and a cafe.
  • Pielinen Museum: A cultural museum showcasing the history and culture of the Karelian people in North Karelia.
  • Koli Church: A wooden church built in 1814 and located in the village of Koli.

Natural Attractions of Koli National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Koli Fjell: A chain of forested hills with a height of 347 meters, offering panoramic views of the park and Lake Pielinen.
  • Akka-Koli and Paha-Koli: Two peaks of Koli hill, known for their stunning views and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Pielinen Lake: The fourth-largest lake in Finland, covering a surface area of 891 square kilometers and offering a range of water activities, such as fishing and boating.
  • Koli National Park Trails: A network of hiking and biking trails with varying degrees of difficulty, ranging from easy to advanced, offering stunning views of the park’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Koli National Park

  • Paha-Koli Trail: A 10-kilometer loop trail that rewards hikers with the best views of the park.
  • Ukko-Koli Trail: A 3.5-kilometer trail from Ukko-Koli Hill to the nature center, offering diverse flora and fauna.
  • Herajärvi Trail: A 36-kilometer trail that circles around the beautiful Herajärvi Lake, surrounded by Finnish forests and boulder fields.

Best Things to Do in Koli National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Winter Sports: Skiing, snowshoeing, and ice-fishing on frozen lakes are popular winter activities in the park.
  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat ride on Pielinen Lake, exploring the surrounding scenic beauty from a different perspective.
  • Guided Nature Tours and Workshops: Explore the park with a knowledgeable guide and learn about its history, flora, and fauna.
  • Camping and Picnicking: Enjoy an immersive experience in nature by camping in the park or having a picnic with beautiful views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Koli National Park

Day 1: Arrive at Koli National Park and visit the nature center and Ukko-Koli Hill.

Day 2: Hike Paha-Koli Trail and experience magnificent views of the park.

Day 3: Go on a boat tour of Pielinen Lake and explore the cultural sites around the lake.

Day 4: Take a guided workshop and learn how to fish or make traditional Finnish handicrafts.

Day 5: Go camping in the park or visit Koli Church and nearby historic villages.

Day 6: Take a relaxing day to enjoy the beautiful wilderness with a picnic.

Day 7: Depart from Koli National Park and head on a road trip around North Karelia to explore other nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Koli National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: A regional dish that consists of fish (usually trout or vendace), bacon, and pork wrapped in a rye crust.
  • Karjalanpaisti: A traditional Karelian beef stew, often served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam.
  • Leipäjuusto: A local cheese made from cow’s milk, served hot with cloudberry jam.

Local Beverages

  • Vodka: Finland is known for its vodka, including the popular brands Koskenkorva and Finlandia.
  • Olut: Finnish beer, including the local brewery Pyynikin Käsityöläispanimo.
  • Sima: A traditional low-alcohol fermented drink made from lemons, sugar, and yeast.

Where to Stay in Koli National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kolin Ryynänen: A guesthouse located in the heart of Koli National Park, offering traditional Finnish cuisine and outdoor activities.
  • Hotel Koli Relax Spa: A hotel and spa located near the park, offering various types of accommodations and a range of activities.
  • Kolin Hovi: A hotel and restaurant located in Loma-Koli village, offering cozy rooms and local food.

What to Buy in Koli National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Karelian pastries: A sweet pastry filled with rice or mashed potatoes, usually served with butter or egg butter.
  • Koli Honey: Local honey made from the forest flowers and plants surrounding the park.
  • Sauna Products: Treat yourself with traditional Finnish sauna products, such as sauna hats and birch whisks.

Local Culture and Customs of Koli National Park

Finland is known for its rich cultural heritage, including traditional Finnish sauna culture, ancient mythology, and folklore. The Karelian people, who once inhabited the region, have influenced the local culture and cuisine. Today, the park celebrates these traditions through various cultural events and exhibitions.

Best Time to Visit Koli National Park

The best time to visit Koli National Park is from June to September when the weather is mild and the daylight hours are longer. In winter, Koli becomes a winter wonderland, and the park offers a range of winter activities, including skiing, snowshoeing, and ice-fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Koli National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s nature and leave nothing but footprints.
  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations, including the no-fire policy in protected areas.
  • Participate in park-guided activities and workshops to learn more about the park’s cultural and natural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the park’s natural surroundings.
  • Pick or damage plants and wildlife in the park.
  • Ignore park regulations on camping and fires.

Safety and Health

Safety in Koli National Park

Koli National Park is safe to explore, but it is recommended to stay on the designated trails and follow the park’s rules and regulations. The Finnish weather can be unpredictable, so it’s essential to pack appropriate clothing for all seasons.

Health in Koli National Park

The park does not pose any significant health risks. However, visitors are advised to be cautious of ticks during the summer months, wear appropriate clothing, and use insect repellent to reduce the risk of tick bites.

Getting to Koli National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Koli National Park

The nearest airport is Joensuu airport, which offers regular domestic flights from Helsinki.

Bus Stations Near Koli National Park

There are daily bus services between Joensuu and Koli National Park. The bus station is located in the village of Koli, near the nature center.

Other Options

For visitors with a car, Koli National Park is approximately five hours northeast of Helsinki and two hours north of Kuopio.

How to Get Around in Koli National Park

The best way to get around Koli National Park is on foot or by bike, using the designated trails. The park also offers boat tours of Pielinen Lake, and visitors can rent a car to explore nearby regions.

Where to Go Next from Koli National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Joensuu: A vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage and a vast range of outdoor activities, including water sports and skiing.
  • Vuonislahti: A picturesque village located along the Pielinen Lake, known for its natural beauty and cultural sites.
  • Kuhmo: A town located near the Russian border, offering a range of outdoor activities ranging from hiking to canoeing.

Conclusion

Koli National Park is a stunning destination offering a range of natural and cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and a glimpse into Finnish heritage and traditions. This comprehensive tour guide has provided all the essential information to plan your trip, explore the park’s beauty and understand its history and culture.
