Introduction to Nokia

Nokia is a town located in the Pirkanmaa region of Finland, which is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. The town of Nokia itself is located on the shores of Lake Pyhäjärvi and is surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills. The town’s name comes from the Finnish word “noki,” which means soot, and is a reference to the town’s industrial past.

List of Places to Visit in Nokia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nokia Museum - Explore the town’s rich history and technological innovation at the Nokia Museum, which showcases the town’s industrial heritage from the 19th century to present day.
  2. Kuorevesi Manor - Experience life in an authentic Finnish manor house from the 18th century at the Kuorevesi Manor.
  3. Kallenaution historic area - Visit the Kallenaution historic area, which includes a 19th-century farmstead and an old timber raft.
  4. Teisko Church - Admire the impressive architecture of the Teisko Church, which was built in the 1820s and is known for its unique onion-shaped dome.

Natural Attractions of Nokia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Siuronkallio - Hike to the top of Siuronkallio for breathtaking panoramic views of Lake Pyhäjärvi and the surrounding forests.
  2. Nokianvirta Nature Reserve - Explore the diverse flora and fauna of the Nokianvirta Nature Reserve, which includes over 20 kilometers of marked hiking trails.
  3. Lokalahti Canal - Take a boat tour of the Lokalahti Canal, which is known for its abundant wildlife, including beavers and otters.
  4. Tampereen Pyynikki Observation Tower - Enjoy stunning views of Tampere and the surrounding landscape from the top of the Pyynikki Observation Tower.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nokia

  1. Nokia–Lempäälä Canal Route - This scenic 25-kilometer route follows the historic Nokia–Lempäälä Canal and offers plenty of opportunities for biking, hiking, and birdwatching.
  2. Siuronkallio Nature Trail - This 3-kilometer hiking trail takes you to the top of Siuronkallio and offers stunning panoramic views of Lake Pyhäjärvi and the surrounding forests.
  3. Nokianvirta Nature Trail - Explore the diverse flora and fauna of the Nokianvirta Nature Reserve on this 8-kilometer hiking trail, which includes several birdwatching platforms and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Nokia: Activities and Tours

  1. Sauna Tour - Experience the traditional Finnish sauna culture on a guided sauna tour, which includes visits to several different saunas in and around Nokia.
  2. Ice Fishing Tour - Try your hand at ice fishing on Lake Pyhäjärvi on a guided ice fishing tour, which includes all necessary equipment and snacks and drinks.
  3. Kayaking Tour - Explore the scenic shoreline of Lake Pyhäjärvi on a guided kayaking tour, which includes all necessary equipment and transportation from Nokia to the starting point.
  4. Nokia Beer Tour - Learn about the local beer culture and taste some of Nokia’s famous craft beers on a guided Nokia Beer Tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Nokia

Nokia has a rich culinary heritage, with a focus on fresh, locally-sourced ingredients and traditional Finnish dishes. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kalakukko - A traditional Finnish dish made from a bread dough filled with fish, pork, or beef and baked in an oven for several hours.
  2. Leipäjuusto - A Finnish cheese with a squeaky texture, often served fried with lingonberry jam.
  3. Karjalanpaisti - A hearty meat stew made with beef or pork and root vegetables, which is traditionally slow-cooked in a cast-iron pot for several hours.

Local Beverages

  1. Kaffa Roastery - A local coffee roaster, which offers a variety of freshly roasted coffee beans and blends.
  2. Koskenkorva Vodka - A Finnish vodka made from locally-grown barley and pure spring water.

Where to Stay in Nokia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nokia has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious guesthouses and hotels. Some popular options include:

  1. Scandic Nokia - A modern hotel located in the center of Nokia, with comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Finnish cuisine.
  2. Nokia Camping Viinikanniemi - A budget-friendly camping ground located on the shores of Lake Pyhäjärvi, with a variety of accommodation options, including cabins and tents.
  3. Pyynikki Guesthouse - A charming guesthouse located in the historic Pyynikki district, with cozy rooms and a Finnish sauna.

What to Buy in Nokia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nokia is known for its rich handicraft tradition, and visitors can find a variety of unique local products and souvenirs to bring home. Some popular options include:

  1. Birch bark products - Handmade baskets, candle holders, and other decorative items made from birch tree bark.
  2. Tuohi products - Handcrafted leather items, such as wallets, purses, and belts, made with traditional Finnish materials and techniques.
  3. Juniper berries - A popular Finnish spice, which can be used in cooking and baking.

Local Culture and Customs of Nokia

Finland has a unique cultural heritage, which is reflected in the customs and traditions of the country’s people. Some important customs and cultural practices to keep in mind when visiting Nokia include:

  • Finnish people value personal space and quietness, so try to avoid loud and boisterous behavior in public places.
  • Sauna culture is an important part of Finnish life, and visitors are encouraged to try traditional Finnish sauna, which typically involves alternating between hot sauna rooms and cold dips in the lake or snow.
  • Tipping is not expected in Finland, as service charges are usually included in the bill.

Best Time to Visit Nokia

The best time to visit Nokia is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny and the outdoor activities are at their best. However, if you’re looking to experience traditional Finnish winter activities, such as ice fishing and cross-country skiing, then the winter months from December to March can also be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nokia

Do:

  • Try traditional Finnish sauna on a guided sauna tour.
  • Explore the scenic hiking and biking trails around Nokia.
  • Sample local Finnish dishes, such as Kalakukko and Leipäjuusto.
  • Learn about the town’s rich industrial and technological heritage at the Nokia Museum.

Don’t:

  • Be overly loud or boisterous in public places, as Finnish people value personal space and quietness.
  • Ignore the town’s cultural heritage sites and natural attractions, which are an important part of Nokia’s identity.
  • Forget to pack warm clothing and gear if you’re visiting during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nokia

Nokia is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and a friendly and helpful local population. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as securing valuables and being alert when traveling alone at night.

Health in Nokia

Finland has an excellent healthcare system, which is available to all residents and visitors. There are several hospitals and clinics in the Tampere region, which can provide emergency care and medical assistance in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Nokia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nokia is located approximately 20 kilometers south of Tampere, which is the nearest major city and transportation hub in the region. There are several transportation options available for visitors to reach Nokia, including:

Airports Near Nokia

  1. Tampere-Pirkkala Airport - The nearest airport to Nokia, which is located approximately 20 kilometers north of the town.

Bus Stations Near Nokia

  1. Tampere Bus Station - The main bus station in Tampere, which is located in the city center and offers connections to all major cities in Finland.

Other Options

  1. Train - The nearest train station to Nokia is Tampere Railway Station, which offers regular connections to Helsinki and other major cities in Finland.
  2. Car rental - Renting a car is a convenient and flexible way to explore Nokia and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Nokia

Nokia is a small town, and most attractions and sites are within walking distance. However, renting a bike or a car can be a convenient way to explore the town and its surrounding areas. Taxi and public transportation services are also available for visitors who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Nokia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Tampere - The nearest major city and transportation hub, which offers a variety of cultural attractions, restaurants, and entertainment options.
  2. Pispalan Uittotunneli - A historic log-floating tunnel located in the nearby city of Tampere, which offers guided tours and cultural events.
  3. Hatanpää Arboretum - A beautiful botanical garden located in Tampere, which features over 1500 different plant species.
  4. Hämeenkyrö - A charming rural town located just north of Nokia, which is known for its historic churches and beautiful nature trails.
