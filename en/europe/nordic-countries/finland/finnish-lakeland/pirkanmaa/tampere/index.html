Introduction to Tampere

Tampere, located in the beautiful region of Pirkanmaa, is the largest inland city in the Nordic countries and a fantastic destination for travelers seeking a blend of culture, nature, and adventure. With a rich industrial heritage, museums, galleries, shops, and lively events, Tampere undoubtedly offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tampere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vapriikki Museum Centre: One of the largest museums in Finland, displaying exhibitions on history, technology, and art.
  • Tampere Cathedral: A stunning neo-Gothic cathedral that dates back to 1907.
  • Lenin Museum: Discovered in a period apartment in 1984, this museum tells the story of Lenin’s life and work.
  • Pyynikki Observation Tower: A prominent landmark with stunning panoramic views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Tampere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pyynikki Ridge: A must-visit for nature enthusiasts, Pyynikki Ridge boasts breathtaking views of the city, various hiking trails, and the world-famous Pyynikki observation tower.
  • Näsijärvi Lake: Finland’s second-largest lake, with an abundance of wildlife, picturesque landscapes, and recreational activities.
  • Pispalan Portaat: A historic staircase in the neighborhoods of Pispala that offers a fantastic view of the city.
  • Hatanpää Arboretum: A beautiful park with over 800 species of trees and shrubs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tampere

Tampere is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts and offers numerous hiking and biking trails. Some popular options include:

  • Näsinneula Nature Trail: A 4.5 km hiking route through beautiful natural scenery and past various landmarks.
  • Pispalan Uittotunnelin Polku: A 6 km hiking trail that takes you through a historical canal tunnel.
  • Pyynikki-Jyväskylän Lenkki: A 70 km cycling route that takes you through Pyynikki Ridge and several neighboring towns.

Best Things to Do in Tampere: Activities and Tours

  • Sauna Tour: A relaxing and unique experience, exploring the traditional Finnish sauna culture.
  • Tamperetalo Concert Hall: The brilliant concert hall with a capacity of over 2,000 seats, presenting various cultural and musical events.
  • Tampereen Teatteri: The most extensive theatre in the region, hosting operas, plays, and ballets.
  • Tampere Market Hall: A bustling and vibrant market hall with various fresh and local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tampere

Day 1: Tampere Market Hall, Näsinneula Observation Tower & Pyynikki Ridge

Day 2: Vapriikki Museum Center, Pyynikki-Jyväskylän Lenkki cycling route

Day 3: Lenin Museum, Tampere Cathedral, and Pispalan Portaat

Day 4: Sauna Tour, Tammerkoski Rapids

Day 5: Hatanpää Arboretum, Pyynikki Forest Trail

Day 6: Tampereen Teatteri, Tampere Art Museum

Day 7: Näsijärvi Lake, Viikinsaari Island

Food and Drink Scene of Tampere

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pyynikin Munkit: Traditional Finnish doughnuts best enjoyed when fresh and warm from the famous Pyynikki observation tower cafe.
  • Kalakukko: A potato-based pastry filled with fish and pork and baked for a long time. A local specialty from the Savonian region that Tampere claims its own.
  • Särä: A unique dish of lamb baked in saunas and served with mashed potatoes, lingonberry jam, and pickles.

Local Beverages

  • Koskenkorva Vodka: A well-known Finnish vodka brand with a long history.
  • Nokian Panimo Beer: Local brewery that produces a variety of delicious craft beers.
  • Finnish Gin: The distilled and aromatic gin suitable for cocktails and mixes.

Where to Stay in Tampere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Solo Sokos Tammer Hotel: A historical and luxurious hotel in Tammerkoski Rapids.
  • Radisson Blu Grand Hotel Tammer: A comfortable and centrally-located hotel in an Art Nouveau building.
  • Omena Hotel Tampere: A budget-friendly hotel chain with excellent locations.

What to Buy in Tampere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Finnish Sweets: Try the famous Finnish chocolates, licorice, and other delicious sweets.
  • Marimekko: A well-known Finnish textile brand that sells unique and colorful clothing, home decorations, and accessories.
  • Moomin Products: The beloved Finnish children’s book characters, Moomins, are a popular souvenir option that can be found in many shops throughout Tampere.

Local Culture and Customs of Tampere

  • Finnish Sauna Culture: The traditional Finnish sauna is a significant part of Finnish lifestyle, and experiencing the sauna is a must-try when traveling to Finland.
  • Finnish Social Distancing: Finns usually value their personal space and prefer a comfortable distance in social settings. Finns don’t like small talk and “too personal” conversation subjects, so be mindful of personal boundaries.
  • Finnish Hospitality: Finn’s hospitality is friendly, warm, and hospitable. It’s essential in Finnish culture as the host typically offers his/her guest the best room, best food, and the most comfortable seat in the room.

Best Time to Visit Tampere

The best time to visit Tampere is during the summer between June and August when the weather is milder, and the city comes to life with numerous festivals, events, and outdoor activities such as boating, swimming, and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tampere

Do:

  • Do try the sauna culture and other outdoor activities.

  • Do respect Finnish outdoor cultures by not littering hiking trails, parks and picking only wild berries and mushrooms that you recognize and are safe to eat.

  • Do try the local food and drink scene, keeping in mind Tampere’s unique culinary specialities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be surprised by Finnish social distancing customs; personal space is valued, and “small talk” is less common.

  • Don’t flash big sums of money, jewelry, or other valuable items when walking around the town.

  • Don’t ignore the local speed limits

Safety and Health

Safety in Tampere

Overall, Tampere is a safe city for tourists, with very little violent crime and theft. However, it’s always wise to be careful and avoid leaving valued items unattended in public places. Moreover, bear in mind that winter months can be slippery and icy, be careful when walking on icy sidewalks.

Health in Tampere

Finland’s healthcare system is renowned around the world, with modern facilities and experienced healthcare professionals. The EHIC health card is a must-have when traveling around Europe. Tampere has a 24-hour hospital emergency unit.

Getting to Tampere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tampere

  • Tampere-Pirkkala Airport: located only 18 kilometres from the city center and serves a variety of international and domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Tampere

  • Tampere Bus Terminal: Tampere has excellent bus connections to other Finnish cities and some international destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Finland’s train network is excellent and connects Tampere to other major cities, such as Helsinki, Turku, and Jyväskylä.

How to Get Around in Tampere

Tampere is a walkable city, and you can explore much of it by foot. Nevertheless, trams, buses, and taxis are available throughout the city. You can buy tickets on the bus or at the automatic ticket machines located at most bus stops.

Where to Go Next from Tampere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Helsinki: The vibrant capital city of Finland is only a two-hour train ride away from Tampere.
  • Turku: Turku is a beautiful city located in the archipelago of southwestern Finland, with plenty of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and natural scenery.
  • Lapland: For winter sports and observing the Northern Lights, a visit to Lapland is a unique adventure with snowmobiles, reindeer and husky safaris, and snowshoeing excursions.

Conclusion

Tampere is one of the best destinations to explore for anyone who loves natural landscapes, Finnish heritage, and exploring new cultures. Whether you want to experience traditional Finnish saunas, explore the beautiful lakeside scenery, or visit museums, Tampere has it all in one place. Plan your trip and experience Finland’s beautiful city for yourself today.
