Introduction to Pirkanmaa

Pirkanmaa is a breathtaking region situated in Finnish Lakeland in the Nordic countries of Europe. This picturesque area is known for its diverse outdoor activities, delicious traditional cuisine, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural landscapes. With its prime location in southwestern Finland, Pirkanmaa is easily accessible and attracts travelers from all over the world. In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do, see, and eat in Pirkanmaa, Finland.

  • Pyynikki Observation Tower
  • Näsinneula Observation Tower

  • Lenin Museum

  • Voipaala Art and Cultural Centre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pirkanmaa boasts numerous museums and historical sites that offer visitors a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural heritage. The Vapriikki Museum Centre is a must-visit attraction that showcases cultural and historical exhibitions ranging from archaeology to modern-day art. Another must-visit site is the Lenin Museum, which tells the story of the Russian revolutionary’s role in Finnish history. Step back in time with a visit to the Amuri Museum of Workers’ Housing and explore the restored 20th-century worker’s apartment complex. Discover the region’s art and history at the Tampere Art Museum, which features both Finnish and international works of art.

Natural Attractions of Pirkanmaa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pirkanmaa is home to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Finland. Explore the lush green forests and crystal clear lakes of the Pinsiö Arboretum or head to the Seitseminen National Park for a thrilling hike on one of its many trails. Marvel at the breathtaking views of the city from the Pyynikki Observation Tower or the Näsinneula Observation Tower, which is also the tallest observation tower in the Nordic countries. The Kintulammi Nature Reserve offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, bird watching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pirkanmaa

Pirkanmaa is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts due to its vast network of well-maintained hiking and biking trails. The Pirkan Taival hiking trail is one of the most popular hiking routes in the region, covering over 250 kilometers of stunning natural landscapes and cultural attractions. Another popular option is the Helvetinjärvi National Park, which offers visitors a range of hiking options, including a six-kilometer trail that runs through a deep gorge with stunning views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Pirkanmaa: Activities and Tours

There is never a dull moment in Pirkanmaa, with plenty of activities and tours to enjoy. Take in the picturesque cityscapes on a bike tour through Tampere or explore the region on a guided kayak tour. Unwind with a relaxing boat cruise on lake Pyhäjärvi or head to Yyteri Beach for a day of sun and sand. Fulfill your adrenaline cravings with thrilling activities such as zip-lining, go-karting, or bungee-jumping.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pirkanmaa

Day 1-2: Explore Tampere’s cultural attractions, including the Tampere Cathedral, Vapriikki Museum Centre, and Tampere Art Museum. Day 3-4: Enjoy the great outdoors with a hike in Seitseminen National Park and a trip to the Pyynikki Observation Tower and Näsinneula Observation Tower. Day 5-6: Explore the region’s rich cultural heritage with visits to the Lenin Museum and Amuri Museum of Workers’ Housing. Cap off the cultural tour by exploring Voipaala Art and Cultural Centre. Day 7: Unwind with a relaxing cruise on lake Pyhäjärvi followed by a picnic in the lush green fields of the Pinsiö Arboretum.

Food and Drink Scene of Pirkanmaa

Must-Try Dishes

Pirkanmaa is famous for its traditional cuisine, including savoury fish dishes, hearty meat stews, and delectable pastries. The local specialty is “mustamakkara,” a type of blood sausage that is typically served with lingonberry jam. Other must-try dishes include “kalakukko,” a rye bread filled with fish and “karjalanpaisti,” a delicious meat stew.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with some of the region’s popular beverages, including the locally brewed “Plevna” beer or “Laitila” apple cider. Another popular drink is the “Juhla Mokka” coffee, which is often served with a side of “Tippaleipä,” a type of funnel cake.

Where to Stay in Pirkanmaa: Hotels, hostels, and guesthouses

There is no shortage of accommodations in Pirkanmaa, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Stay in the heart of Tampere at the Scandic Tampere City or experience the lap of luxury at the Lakeside Villas at Pyynikki. Other popular options include the Dream Hotel Tampere and the Solo Sokos Hotel Torni Tampere.

What to Buy in Pirkanmaa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Pirkanmaa with you by purchasing one of the region’s unique souvenirs. Popular options include “Finlayson” textile products, handmade ceramics, and Tampere-themed T-shirts. Foodies will also love the locally sourced honeys, jams, and other preserved foods.

Local Culture and Customs of Pirkanmaa

Pirkanmaa is a region rich in cultural customs and traditions, including the popular Midsummer and Christmas celebrations. The locals are known for their welcoming and friendly nature, with an emphasis on simple living and enjoying the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Pirkanmaa

The best time to visit Pirkanmaa is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is mild, and the days are long. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and swimming, as well as attend numerous festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pirkanmaa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take in the stunning natural landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash in public places
  • Ignore the signs in national parks and protected areas.
  • Be disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pirkanmaa

Pirkanmaa is a safe region to visit, with a low crime rate and excellent healthcare facilities. However, visitors should still exercise caution when walking or hiking in remote areas.

Health in Pirkanmaa

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, with both public and private facilities available. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses in case of emergency.

Getting to Pirkanmaa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pirkanmaa

The Tampere-Pirkkala Airport is the closest airport to Pirkanmaa, offering regional and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Pirkanmaa

Tampere Bus Station is the main bus station in the region, offering regular services to major cities and towns in Finland.

Other Options

Train services are also available, with the Tampere Railway Station located in the heart of the city. Rental cars and taxis are also an option.

How to Get Around in Pirkanmaa

Pirkanmaa is well connected with a range of transportation options, including buses, trains, and taxis. For those who want to explore the region on their own, rental cars and bicycles are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Pirkanmaa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located in the heart of Finland, Pirkanmaa is ideally situated for exploring the surrounding areas, including the vibrant capital city of Helsinki and the stunning natural landscapes of Lapland.

Conclusion

Pirkanmaa is a region that offers something for everyone, from its beautiful natural landscapes to its rich cultural heritage and delectable cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can explore the best things to do, see, and eat in Pirkanmaa, Finland. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing holiday or an adventure-packed getaway, Pirkanmaa has it all.
