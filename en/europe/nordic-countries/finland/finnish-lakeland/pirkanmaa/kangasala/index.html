Introduction to Kangasala

Kangasala is a small but beautiful town located in the heart of Pirkanmaa, in Finnish Lakeland, in Finland. It’s one of the oldest towns in the country and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and plenty of outdoor activities.

Visitors to Kangasala can explore the town’s numerous museums and historical sites, including the Kangasala Church and the Pekkala Manor, which date back to the 18th century. The town is also home to several beautiful parks and nature reserves, including the Sahalahti Nature Reserve and the stunning Kuhmoinen Ridge.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll highlight the best things to do and places to visit in Kangasala, as well as share tips and advice for making the most of your trip to this charming Finnish town.

List of Places to Visit in Kangasala

Kangasala offers a wide range of attractions, from museums and historical sites to stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top places to include on your Kangasala itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kangasala Church: This iconic church dates back to the 15th century and is one of the most visited attractions in Kangasala.

  • Pekkala Manor: This historic 18th-century manor is now a museum that offers visitors insight into Kangasala’s rich cultural heritage.

  • The Finnish Museum of Horology: This museum is dedicated to the history of timekeeping and houses a vast collection of timepieces from around the world.

  • The Serlachius Museum Gustaf: This award-winning museum is housed in an old paper mill and showcases a range of contemporary art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Kangasala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sahalahti Nature Reserve: This stunning nature reserve offers visitors the chance to explore the Finnish wilderness and observe local wildlife such as bears, deer, and lynx.

  • Kuhmoinen Ridge: This beautiful hiking trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and Lake Vesijärvi.

  • Pälkäneen Kanava: This beautiful canal offers visitors the chance to take a boat ride through the Finnish countryside and see the local wildlife up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kangasala

Kangasala is known for its beautiful hiking and biking trails, which offer visitors the chance to explore the stunning Finnish countryside. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Tampere Hiking Area: This scenic hiking trail is located just a short drive from Kangasala and takes visitors through some of the most beautiful scenery in the area.

  • The Kuhmoinen Ridge: This beautiful hiking trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and Lake Vesijärvi.

  • The Kintulammi Nature Reserve: This pristine nature reserve features over 40 kilometers of hiking trails and is home to a wide range of local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Kangasala: Activities and Tours

Kangasala offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Kangasala:

  • Visit Kangasala Church: This iconic church is one of the most visited attractions in the town and offers visitors a glimpse into Kangasala’s rich history and culture.

  • Take a boat tour: There are several companies that offer boat tours around Lake Vesijärvi, allowing visitors to see the local wildlife up close.

  • Visit the Pekkala Manor: This historic manor is now a museum that offers visitors insight into Kangasala’s rich cultural heritage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kangasala

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kangasala, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town center and visit Kangasala Church

  • Day 2: Take a boat tour around Lake Vesijärvi

  • Day 3: Visit the Pekkala Manor

  • Day 4: Explore the Kuhmoinen Ridge

  • Day 5: Visit the Finnish Museum of Horology

  • Day 6: Hike the Tampere Hiking Area

  • Day 7: Visit the Serlachius Museum Gustaf

Food and Drink Scene of Kangasala

No trip to Kangasala would be complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karelian pies: A traditional Finnish pastry filled with rice or potatoes and served with butter.

  • Smoked salmon: A local delicacy that can be found in many restaurants in Kangasala.

  • Finnish meatballs: A classic Finnish dish made with pork and beef and served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made with juniper berries and malted barley.

  • Koskenkorva: A popular Finnish vodka made from barley and purified water.

  • Lonkero: A refreshing Finnish cocktail made with grapefruit soda and gin.

Local Culture and Customs of Kangasala

Kangasala has a rich and vibrant cultural heritage, which is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. The local culture places a strong emphasis on nature and the environment, with many residents enjoying outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

Best Time to Visit Kangasala

The best time to visit Kangasala depends largely on what you want to do. The summer months (June-August) are the most popular time to visit, as the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the winter months (December-February) offer unique opportunities for winter sports and activities, such as skiing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kangasala

To make the most of your trip to Kangasala, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

Getting to Kangasala: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to Kangasala, including air, bus, and car. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Kangasala

The nearest airport to Kangasala is Tampere-Pirkkala Airport, which is located approximately 30 minutes’ drive from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Kangasala

Kangasala is well-serviced by a range of local and regional bus services. The local buses are operated by Nysse, while the regional buses are operated by Matkahuolto or Onnibus, depending on the route.

Other Options

If you’re traveling by car, Kangasala is easily accessible via the E12/E75 and the E63 highways.

How to Get Around in Kangasala

Kangasala is a small town and is easily accessible on foot or by bike. There is also a local bus service operated by Nysse, which connects the town center with surrounding areas and nearby cities.

Kangasala is located in the heart of Finnish Lakeland, which offers plenty of opportunities for further exploration. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:
