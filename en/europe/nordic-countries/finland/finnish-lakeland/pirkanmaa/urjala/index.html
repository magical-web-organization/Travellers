Introduction to Urjala

Tucked away in the heart of Pirkanmaa in Finnish Lakeland, Urjala is a small but vibrant town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. With its sprawling forests, tranquil lakes, and scenic hikes, it is the perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. However, Urjala is not just about natural wonders; it is steeped in culture, from the numerous museums and historical sites to the vibrant local arts scene.

List of Places to Visit in Urjala

Here is a list of top attractions in Urjala that you must not miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Urjala: Located in the heart of the town, the museum is a treasure trove of local history and culture, showcasing everything from traditional handicrafts to archaeological finds.
  • The Old Church of Urjala: This historic church, dating back to the 15th century, is a must-visit for its stunning architecture and intricate stained-glass windows.
  • The Kallenautio Open-Air Museum: This outdoor museum is a must-visit for history buffs, showcasing the traditional way of life in Finland’s countryside during the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Urjala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pyhäjärvi Lake: One of the largest lakes in the Pirkanmaa region, Pyhäjärvi offers an array of activities, from swimming and fishing to sailing and boating.
  • The Toriseva Nature Reserve: Home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, the nature reserve is a great place to go hiking and wildlife spotting.
  • The Pääjärvi Ridge Trail: This scenic trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, with opportunities for birdwatching and mushroom picking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Urjala

Urjala is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Kullaanvuori Trail: This easy 4-kilometer trail offers stunning views of Pyhäjärvi Lake and the surrounding countryside.
  • The Ruonanlahti Hiking Trail: This 8-kilometer trail takes you through the heart of the Toriseva Nature Reserve, with its lush forests and tranquil lakes.
  • The Pajaneva Trail: This challenging 20-kilometer trail takes you through some of the most stunning scenery in the region, from rocky outcrops to dense forests.

Best Things to Do in Urjala: Activities and Tours

  • Paddleboarding on Pyhäjärvi Lake: Rent a paddleboard and explore the crystal-clear waters of Pyhäjärvi Lake.
  • The Urjala Arts Trail: This self-guided tour takes you through the town’s vibrant arts scene, with galleries and studios showcasing a diverse array of local artists.
  • The Lempäälä Golf Course: With its scenic location and challenging holes, the golf course is a great way to spend a lazy afternoon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Urjala

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Urjala:

Day Activity
1 Explore the town center and visit the Museum of Urjala
2 Hike the Kullaanvuori Trail and go paddleboarding on Pyhäjärvi Lake
3 Visit the Old Church of Urjala and the Kallenautio Open-Air Museum
4 Take a scenic bike ride along the Pajaneva Trail
5 Spend the day at the Lempäälä Golf Course

7 Take the Urjala Arts Trail and visit local galleries and studios

Food and Drink Scene of Urjala

Urjala boasts a rich culinary tradition, with a focus on fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish delicacy, kalakukko is a pie filled with fish, bacon, and rice, baked in a rye flour crust.
  • Leipäjuusto: Also known as “squeaky cheese,” this mild cheese is often served grilled and topped with cloudberry jam.
  • Mustamakkara: A sausage made with pork, barley, and blood, mustamakkara is a savory and hearty snack.

Local Beverages

  • Birch sap: A refreshing, light drink made from the sap of birch trees.
  • Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka distilled from barley and infused with natural flavors like cranberry and cloudberry.

Where to Stay in Urjala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Urjala offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

  • The Hotel Urjalankulma: A rustic yet comfortable hotel located in the heart of the town, offering free Wi-Fi, a sauna, and an on-site restaurant.
  • The Honkahovi Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with a traditional Finnish sauna, a garden terrace, and a cozy fireplace lounge.
  • The Kuntoutuskeskus Kankaanpää Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel offering simple yet comfortable accommodation, with free parking and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Urjala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: From traditional textiles to hand-carved wooden items, Urjala is home to a vibrant handicraft scene.
  • Local honey: Finland is known for its delicious honey, and Urjala is no exception. Pick up a jar of honey from the local farmers’ market.
  • Mushrooms: With its vast forests, Urjala is a paradise for mushroom hunters. Buy some dried mushrooms to take home and add to your favorite dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Urjala

Finnish culture is known for its emphasis on simplicity, honesty, and connection with nature. Visitors to Urjala will find a warm and welcoming community that values hospitality and respect for others.

Best Time to Visit Urjala

Urjala is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. Summers are warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities and festivals, while winters offer stunning winter landscapes and the opportunity for activities like skiing and ice-fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Urjala

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a public sauna.
  • Drink tap water, which is safe and delicious.
  • Respect nature and follow the “leave no trace” principle when hiking and camping.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English; try to learn a few basic Finnish phrases.
  • Be too loud or rowdy in public spaces.
  • Be offended if someone doesn’t hug or kiss you; Finns tend to be reserved and prefer to keep their distance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Urjala

Urjala is a safe destination for travelers, but like any other place, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on personal belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Urjala

Finland has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors to Urjala should have no trouble accessing medical care if needed. It is also a good idea to carry travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Urjala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Urjala

The closest international airports to Urjala are Helsinki-Vantaa Airport and Tampere-Pirkkala Airport, both of which offer flights to major cities in Europe and North America.

Bus Stations Near Urjala

Urjala is served by several bus stations, including the Urjala Bus Station and the Lempäälä Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to Urjala from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Urjala

Urjala is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available if you need to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Urjala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tampere: Just a short drive from Urjala, Tampere is a vibrant university town with a lively nightlife scene and a range of cultural attractions.
  • The Finnish Lakeland: With over 168,000 islands and countless lakes, the Finnish Lakeland is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.
  • Helsinki: The Finnish capital is only a few hours’ drive from Urjala and is home to world-class museums, galleries, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Urjala may be a small town, but it offers visitors an array of experiences, from outdoor adventures to cultural immersion. Whether you want to hike in the forests, swim in the lake or sample the local cuisine, Urjala is a hidden gem that is just waiting to be discovered. So why not add it to your travel itinerary and experience the best of Finnish Lakeland yourself?
