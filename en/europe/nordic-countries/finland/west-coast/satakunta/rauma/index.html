Introduction to Rauma

Located in the Satakunta region of the West Coast of Finland, Rauma is a charming town with a rich history and culture. Famous for its UNESCO-listed Old Town, unique architecture, and gorgeous coastline, Rauma offers an unforgettable travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Rauma

There are many incredible attractions and places to visit in Rauma that cater to all types of travelers. Here are our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Rauma: A UNESCO-listed Old Town that dates back to the 18th century and features colorful wooden houses, cobblestone streets, and beautiful gardens.
  • The Rauma Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the town’s history and culture and features exhibitions related to maritime history, art, and handicrafts.
  • The Sammallahdenmäki Bronze Age Burial Site: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this ancient burial site boasts over 30 granite burial cairns and dates back to 1500 BC.

Natural Attractions of Rauma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Archipelago National Park: An archipelago of over 40 islands off the coast of Rauma, featuring stunning landscapes, wildlife, and water sports activities.
  • The Bothnian Sea National Park: A marine conservation area that showcases the unique ecosystem of the Gulf of Bothnia, including rare wildlife and beautiful landscapes.
  • Syväraumankoski waterfall: Located in the outskirts of Rauma, this beautiful waterfall offers stunning views and a peaceful atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rauma

Rauma offers exceptional opportunities for trekking and biking in natural environments such as the national parks. The most popular trails include the Kylmäpihlajanreitti trail, the Willanpolku walking route, and the Raumanmeren Juhannussaaren virkistysreitti trail.

Best Things to Do in Rauma: Activities and Tours

  • Experience the unique charm of the Old Town by taking a guided walk through the historic district.
  • Visit the local handicraft shops to purchase traditional Finnish souvenirs and gifts.
  • Attend the Rauma Lace Week, an annual festival celebrating the art of lace-making and other cultural activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rauma

Day 1: Explore the Old Town and visit the Rauma Museum. Day 2: Visit the Sammallahdenmäki Bronze Age Burial Site and enjoy an outdoor picnic. Day 3: Head to the Archipelago National Park for a day trip, including hiking, kayaking, and swimming. Day 4: Rent a bike and explore Rauma’s scenic routes. Day 5: Take a boat tour around the Bothnian Sea National Park. Day 6: Attend a concert or a play at the legendary Rauma Theater. Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa or sauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Rauma

Rauma is home to an array of delicious cuisine and unique local beverages. Must-try dishes include Kalakukko (a fish-filled pastry), traditional Karelian hotpot, and Linguine with vendance. Local beverages include sahti, a traditional Finnish beer, and berry liquors such as cloudberry and lingonberry liquor.

Where to Stay in Rauma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rauma offers a variety of comfortable lodging options to suit every traveler’s needs. Some of the best places to stay include the Hotel Raumanlinna, Cumulus City Rauma, and the Rauman Meri-Pori Hostel.

What to Buy in Rauma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rauma is known for its unique handicrafts, especially lace-making, pottery, and woodwork. Other popular souvenirs and products include specialty food items such as local honey and mustard.

Local Culture and Customs of Rauma

Finns, in general, are known for their reserved and quiet nature. In Rauma, locals are proud of their unique cultural heritage and are known for their love of music and arts.

Best Time to Visit Rauma

The best time to visit Rauma is during the summer months, from June to August, as the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor adventures. Additionally, the famous Rauma Lace Week takes place in July and offers visitors a chance to experience the distinct cultural heritage of the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rauma

Here are some important tips to keep in mind while traveling in Rauma:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the national parks and coastlines.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Drink and drive.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in water sports or outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Rauma is a generally safe town, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any serious safety concerns. However, it’s important to take normal safety precautions and follow local guidelines. As for health concerns, visitors should have up-to-date vaccinations and carry appropriate travel medical insurance.

Getting to Rauma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rauma is accessible by air, bus, and train. The nearest airport is Pori Airport, around an hour’s drive away. Several bus companies operate daily services between Rauma and other Finnish cities such as Tampere and Helsinki. Additionally, the town is served by several local bus routes and taxis.

How to Get Around in Rauma

Rauma is a compact town and is easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bikes or take public transportation, which includes buses, taxis, and ferries.

Where to Go Next from Rauma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations to visit from Rauma include Pori, Turku, and Helsinki. Visitors can also explore the beautiful Finnish Archipelago by visiting the islands of Kimitoön, Åland, and Kokkola.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a charming coastal town, Rauma has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan your next trip to this beautiful destination today.
