Introduction to Lavia

Lavia is a charming municipality in the Satakunta region of Finland. It is a small town surrounded by beautiful countryside and boasts a rich cultural history. Lavia is ideal for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy the natural surroundings. In this guide, we will give you an insight into the top attractions, things to do, and must-try dishes to make your trip to Lavia an unforgettable one.

List of Places to Visit in Lavia

Culturally Important Attractions

Lavia has a significant historical past, and there are several museums and historical monuments worth visiting while you are here.

Tarvasjoki museum

The Tarvasjoki museum is a must-visit for history buffs. Here, you can explore the history of the municipality through the exhibits that showcase the life and culture of its residents.

Lavia’s Church

Lavia’s Church is an impressive landmark and an essential part of the municipality’s cultural identity. The church was built in the early 19th century and is known for its unique architecture and stunning frescoes.

Natural Attractions

Lavia has an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor activities that you can enjoy.

Lake Köyliönjärvi

Lake Köyliönjärvi is a beautiful lake known for its crystal-clear water. You can spend your day fishing, swimming, or enjoying the view of the lake.

Lavia Nature Trail

The Lavia Nature Trail is a beautiful trail that takes you through the peaceful countryside and forests of Lavia. The path is well-marked, making it easy for hikers to explore the area without a guide.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lavia

Lavia is known for its stunning countryside, and there are several hiking and biking routes you can take to explore it.

Kurjenrahka National Park

Kurjenrahka National Park is located just 20 km away from Lavia and is a great spot for hiking enthusiasts. You can explore the park’s unique wetlands, forests, and lakes, making it an excellent place to take in the natural beauty of Finland.

Best Things to Do in Lavia: Activities and Tours

It can be tough to choose from all the incredible activities and tours available in Lavia. Here are our top recommendations.

Horseback Riding

Explore the area’s surroundings on horseback, taking in scenic views and the peace that comes with it.

Fishing

Finland in itself is a fishing destination and Lavia offers ample opportunity for fishing enthusiasts. Head to Lake Köyliönjärvi if you want to enjoy a day fishing with friends and family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lavia

If you plan on spending a week in Lavia, here is a recommended itinerary for you:

Day 1: Explore Lavia’s Museums

Spend the first day exploring the cultural attractions and museums of Lavia, including the Tarvasjoki museum and Lavia’s Church.

Day 2: Hike Lavia Nature Trail

Explore the stunning natural scenes of Lavia on foot by hiking Lavia Nature Trail.

Day 3: Visit Kurjenrahka National Park

Head out to the nearby Kurjenrahka National Park and spend the day exploring the wetlands, forests, and lakes.

Day 4: Horseback Riding

Enjoy the countryside of Lavia on horseback and take in picturesque views that surround the area.

Day 5: Fishing

Spend a day relaxing by the beautiful Lake Köyliönjärvi and indulge in fishing with friends and family.

Day 6: Cycling

Rent a bike and go round exploring the sights and sounds of Lavia.

Day 7: Rest Day

Relax and unwind in one of the cozy guest houses or hotels in Lavia.

Food and Drink Scene of Lavia

Lavia is a gastronomical delight, and you will find some excellent Nordic cuisine here.

Must-Try Dishes

Mämmi

Mämmi is a traditional Finnish dessert that is a must-try for anyone visiting Lavia. This sweet treat is made with rye flour, water, and dark molasses, and is typically served with milk and sugar.

Kalakukko

Kalakukko is a traditional Finnish dish, a pie filled with fish and pork. It’s the perfect snack to carry around while exploring Lavia’s stunning copper countryside.

Local Beverages

Beer

Finns love their beer, and there are several breweries in the area that produce locally made beer worth trying.

Kossu

Kossu a local spirit, also known as Koskenkorva, is a vodka made locally in Finland. It is a must-try when you’re in Lavia.

Where to Stay in Lavia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lavia has a range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels and guesthouses to suit every travellers pocket.

Guesthouse Onnenmyyrä

Onnenmyyrä offers affordable accommodation without compromising on the comfort and charm of Lavia.

Aulanko Lakeside

Aulanko Lakeside is a luxury hotel located in a picturesque area of Lavia. It’s perfect for anyone looking to experience the tranquility and natural beauty of Finland while enjoying premium hospitality.

What to Buy in Lavia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Lavia, make sure to buy some local products as souvenirs, including:

Rye bread

Rye bread is a staple in Finnish cuisine and Lavia has some of the best. It is a must-buy for those visiting the area.

Finnish Sweets

Finnish sweets such as Fazer chocolates make for an excellent souvenir, so make sure to try and bring back some of these sweet treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Lavia

Finns are proud of their culture and traditions, and locals in Lavia actively participate in preserving this heritage. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as sauna etiquettes and the respect of silence in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Lavia

The best time to visit Lavia is during the summer months, between June and August. The countryside is lush green, and the temperatures are warm, making it a perfect time for outdoor activities and relaxation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lavia

Do:

  • Respect the environment and natural surroundings.
  • Try local delicacies and traditional dishes.
  • Respect the traditions and culture of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Ignore silence in public areas.
  • Litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lavia

Lavia is a safe and peaceful town, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should take precautions, such as locking their vehicles and securing their valuables.

Health in Lavia

Finland has top-notch medical facilities, and Lavia is no different. It is advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Lavia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lavia can be accessed by road or rail, making it an easy destination to reach for tourists.

Airports Near Lavia

The closest airport to Lavia is Tampere-Pirkkala Airport, located about 84 km away. International travelers can opt to arrive at Helsinki-Vantaa airport and then take a train or bus to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Lavia

There are several bus stations in Lavia, making it an ideal place to reach by public transport.

Other Options

Driving yourself to Lavia or hiring a car is the best way to get around the Satakunta region.

How to Get Around in Lavia

Lavia is a town that is easily walkable or by cycling if you opt to rent a bike. Taxis are also available if needed.

Where to Go Next from Lavia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby destinations that can be visited as day trips from Lavia are:

  • Helsinki, 2 hours.
  • Turku, 1 hour.
  • Pori, 45 minutes.

Conclusion

Lavia is a fantastic destination that offers a peaceful escape into the beautiful countryside. The area boasts stunning natural scenery and unique cultural attractions, making it an ideal destination for those looking for an authentic Finnish experience. Use our detailed guide to plan your trip to Lavia and enjoy the many activities and experiences this charming Finnish town has to offer.
