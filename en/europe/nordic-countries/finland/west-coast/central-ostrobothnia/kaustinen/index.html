Introduction to Kaustinen

Kaustinen is a small municipality located in Central Ostrobothnia in Finland’s West Coast region. Encircled by lush forests and scenic landscapes, this charming town boasts rich cultural heritage, vibrant music traditions, and an array of outdoor activities.

The town’s population is around 4,000, with a mix of farming communities, craftspeople, and entrepreneurs. Every summer, Kaustinen comes alive as it hosts one of Europe’s largest folk music festivals, attracting thousands of visitors from across the globe.

In this comprehensive guide, you’ll find everything you need to know to make the most of your visit to Kaustinen.

List of Places to Visit in Kaustinen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kaustinen Folk Music Center - A must-visit for anyone interested in folk music. The center showcases the history and evolution of Finnish folk music and hosts regular concerts and events.

  2. Kaustinen Wooden Church - Built-in 1853, this charming wooden church is one of the oldest standing structures in the area. The interior is adorned with intricate frescoes and wood carvings.

  3. Kaustinen Local Heritage Museum - This open-air museum offers insights into the region’s rural life and culture. It features traditional buildings, such as old farmhouses, barns, and mills.

Natural Attractions of Kaustinen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Härkänevan Moorland Nature Reserve - A beautiful nature reserve boasting diverse vegetation, wildlife, and marshes. It’s an ideal spot for birdwatching, hiking, and nature photography.

  2. Lake Ohtakari - A serene lake surrounded by tranquil forests and pristine landscapes. You can rent kayaks or canoes and enjoy a peaceful paddle around the lake.

  3. Suvantovuori Hill - Climb up to the top of this hill to enjoy a panoramic view of Kaustinen and its surrounding valleys. The hill is popular for hiking and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaustinen

  1. Lahnakoski Nature Trail - A beautiful 6-km trail that winds through forests, meadows, and wetlands. The trail is suitable for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing in the winter months.

  2. Vimpelinvaara Hiking Trail - A challenging 12-km hike that takes you up to Vimpelinvaara Hill, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Kulhuanvaaran Nature Trail - An easy 3.5-km trail that takes you through the Kulhuanvaara Nature Reserve, featuring ancient rock formations, flora, and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Kaustinen: Activities and Tours

  1. Folk Music Festival - Take part in one of Europe’s most vibrant folk music festivals held in Kaustinen every year.

  2. Sauna Experiences - From traditional smoke saunas to modern eco-friendly saunas, Kaustinen offers an array of sauna experiences.

  3. Horseback Riding - Go on a horseback riding tour through Kaustinen’s scenic countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaustinen

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kalakukko - A traditional Finnish fish-filled pastry, usually made with rye bread.

  2. Mämmi - A sweet, porridge-like dish, made from rye flour and malted rye, usually served with cream.

  3. Leipäjuusto - A Finnish cheese made from cow’s milk, usually served with cloudberry jam.

Local Beverages

  1. Sahti - A traditional Finnish beer made from malted barley, rye, and flavored with juniper berries.

  2. Koskenkorva - A local vodka distilled from barley and spring water.

  3. Birch Juice - A refreshing drink made from the sap of birch trees, rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

Where to Stay in Kaustinen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Lomakeskus Revontuli - A lakeside resort with various accommodation options, spa facilities, and outdoor activities.

  2. Mokit Pihlajapuu - Charming wooden cabins situated in a lush forest area, with private saunas and barbecue facilities.

  3. Hostel Tuupala - Hostel-style accommodation located in the center of Kaustinen, ideal for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Kaustinen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handicrafts - Kaustinen is well-known for its thriving handicraft scene, producing unique pieces of pottery, glassware, and woven textiles.

  2. Folk Music CDs - Take home the soulful sounds of Finnish folk music with CDs from local musicians.

  3. Traditional Foods - Stock up on local treats such as rye bread, smoked fish, and cloudberry jam.

Local Culture and Customs of Kaustinen

Kaustinen is a town of proud traditions and customs. Finnish culture places great importance on community and harmony with nature. Visitors can expect a warm, welcoming atmosphere and are encouraged to respect the region’s cultural values.

Best Time to Visit Kaustinen

The best time to visit Kaustinen is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. This is also when the annual Folk Music Festival takes place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaustinen

Do:

  1. Greet people with a handshake or a nod of the head.

  2. Remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a sauna.

  3. Ask before taking photographs of people or their property.

Don’t:

  1. Interrupt people when they are speaking.

  2. Be loud or rowdy in public areas.

  3. Throw trash or litter in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaustinen

Kaustinen is a safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take normal precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and leaving valuables in a secure place.

Health in Kaustinen

Finland has good health care services, with modern facilities and trained professionals. Visitors from EU countries can use their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) to access emergency treatment.

Getting to Kaustinen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaustinen

The nearest airport to Kaustinen is Kokkola-Pietarsaari Airport, located about 50 km away. Another option is Vaasa Airport, situated about 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kaustinen

Kaustinen has a bus station in the town center, with regular services connecting to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kaustinen by train, with the nearest station being in Kokkola, situated about 60 km away.

How to Get Around in Kaustinen

The best way to get around Kaustinen is by car or bicycle. Visitors can rent a car or bike from local providers.

Where to Go Next from Kaustinen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kokkola - A charming coastal town, situated about 50 km away from Kaustinen, known for its beautiful old town, museums, and festivals.

  2. Jyväskylä - A vibrant university town, located about 200 km away, known for its stunning architecture, parks, and waterways.

  3. Oulu - A bustling port town, situated about 250 km away, known for its museums, galleries, and musical events.

Conclusion

Kaustinen is a unique destination that offers a mix of cultural experiences and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in folk music, hiking, or exploring Finnish rural life, Kaustinen has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip now, and get ready to immerse yourself in the warmth and charm of this unspoiled Finnish gem.
