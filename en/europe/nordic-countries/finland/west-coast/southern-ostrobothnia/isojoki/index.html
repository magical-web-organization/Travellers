Introduction to Isojoki

Nestled in the heart of Southern Ostrobothnia in the West Coast region of Finland, Isojoki is a small town that packs a big punch in cultural and natural attractions. Declared as a historical church village, Isojoki offers a unique blend of history, nature, and tradition that is worth exploring. This travel guide highlights the must-visit places in Isojoki and provides you with useful information to help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Isojoki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Isojoki Church:

    One of the highlights of Isojoki is its beautiful wooden church, which dates back to the 18th century. Admire the intricate details of the altar, pulpit, and chandelier, and tune in to the stories of the church’s history.

  • Old Church Hill:

    Located near the church, Old Church Hill is a designated Heritage Site and offers a glimpse into the past with its preserved buildings, such as the Old Worthing House, the Old Parsonage, and the historic schoolhouse.

  • Ilmari Tapiovaara Design Museum:

    For art and design enthusiasts, the Ilmari Tapiovaara Design Museum showcases the works of one of Finland’s most prominent designers. Here you can witness the evolution of design through the years, and appreciate the creativity and innovation that is unique to Finnish design.

Natural Attractions of Isojoki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kauhaneva-Pohjankangas National Park:

    Is your heart craving for a nature adventure? The Kauhaneva-Pohjankangas National Park is the perfect place for you. With its breathtaking landscapes, wetlands, marshes, and forests, it offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city. Some of the activities you can enjoy here include hiking, bird watching, and camping.

  • Yuppo Park:

    Relax and unwind amidst the Yuppovuori hill’s scenic views in Yuppo Park. It is a great place to take a picnic or enjoy the stunning autumn colors of Isojoki in the fall season.

  • Swimming at Kivijärvi:

    On hot summer days, cool off in the refreshing waters of Kivijärvi lake. It is a popular spot for swimming, and there are also saunas and changing rooms available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isojoki

For those seeking a more active experience, Isojoki has numerous hiking and biking trails to choose from. The Old Church Hill Trail provides a chance to explore the historical district while immersing yourself in the natural landscapes. If you want to take a longer hike, the Savikankaan Luontopolku Trail in Kauhaneva-Pohjankangas National Park offers a scenic walk through the beautiful wilderness of Isojoki.

Best Things to Do in Isojoki: Activities and Tours

  • Isojoki Village Festival:

    Visit Isojoki in July and experience the annual Isojoki Village Festival. Be captivated by traditional music, dance, and cuisine, and the joyous atmosphere of the festival.

  • Isojoki Horse Trekking:

    Embark on a scenic horseback ride that takes you through stunning landscapes and hidden trails. The Isojoki Horse Trekking Tour is a fantastic way to enjoy the outdoors.

  • Isojoki Country Store:

    If you’re in search of local products, pay a visit to the Isojoki Country Store. The store offers a selection of handcrafted items, such as wooden toys, pottery, and homemade jams.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isojoki

  • Day 1: Arrive in Isojoki and explore the town.
  • Day 2: Visit the Isojoki Church and the Old Church Hill area.
  • Day 3: Take a hike in Kauhaneva-Pohjankangas National Park.
  • Day 4: Relax at Yuppo Park and swim in Kivijärvi Lake.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a horse trekking tour and visit the Ilmari Tapiovaara Design Museum.
  • Day 6: Try local cuisine, visit the Isojoki Country Store, and attend the Isojoki Village Festival (if available).
  • Day 7: Depart from Isojoki.

Food and Drink Scene of Isojoki

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rönttönen:

    A traditional Finnish dish made of mashed potatoes and barley flour, served with lingonberry sauce and melted butter.

  • Loimulohi:

    A smoked salmon dish that is a favorite in Finland. Try it with crisp rye bread and traditional Finnish cheese.

  • Kalakukko:

    A fish-filled pastry that originated from the Karelian region, it is an ideal snack to bring on your hiking adventures.

Local Beverages

  • Kotikalja:

    A non-alcoholic beverage made from malted grain, it has a sweet, malty flavor that goes well with Finnish cuisine.

  • Mustikkamehu:

    A refreshing blueberry juice that is perfect for the warmer months.

  • Sima:

    A fermented drink made of lemon, honey, and brown sugar, Sima is a traditional Finnish drink served during Vappu (May Day).

Where to Stay in Isojoki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Kivitippu:

    A modern hotel located in Kauhaneva-Pohjankangas National Park, it offers spa services and entertainment activities. It is typically Finnish and also offers many traditional dishes to try.

  • Lepolan Maatilamatkailu:

    A farmstead bed and breakfast that provides a unique experience with its organic farm setting and home-cooked meals.

  • Aava-Isakki Guesthouse:

    A cozy and comfortable guesthouse with scenic views and a quiet atmosphere.

What to Buy in Isojoki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Isojoki Pottery:

    Handcrafted pottery that beautifully captures the essence of Isojoki, such as plates, vases, and jugs.

  • Kauhaneva Knits:

    Woolen items made with traditional patterns and designs that are perfect in the winter season.

  • Pihlajalinna Pottery:

    Award-winning pottery crafted by a local artist that is known for its elegant forms and unique styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Isojoki

In Isojoki, traditions and culture are still celebrated with pride and enthusiasm. Local people are kind, hospitable, and always eager to share their way of life with visitors. Some of the customs that Isojoki is known for include the Vappu celebrations, the Midsummer festivities, and the Christmas markets.

Best Time to Visit Isojoki

The best time to visit Isojoki is during the summer season when the weather is pleasant, and the natural beauty of the town is at its peak. The months of June, July, and August are ideal for hiking, biking, and swimming, as well as attending festivals and events. The fall season also offers a great opportunity to enjoy the autumn colors of Isojoki, while the winter season provides a serene and peaceful atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isojoki

Do:

  • Do try local delicacies and traditional dishes.

  • Do respect the environment in national parks and hiking trails.

  • Do learn some basic Finnish phrases to communicate with the locals.

  • Do carry appropriate gear and equipment for hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t smoke in public areas, such as parks and streets.

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in hiking trails or public areas.

  • Don’t take photographs of strangers without their permission.

  • Don’t touch or disturb wildlife in national parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isojoki

Isojoki is considered a safe tourist destination. However, it is always best to take standard precautions while traveling, such as keeping your belongings safe, avoiding isolated areas, and following traffic rules while driving.

Health in Isojoki

It is recommended to get necessary vaccines and healthcare checkups before traveling to Isojoki. The town has small medical facilities where you can seek help in case of emergency. Additionally, remember to bring appropriate gear such as mosquito repellent, sunscreen, and first-aid kits with you, especially if you plan to engage in outdoor activities.

Getting to Isojoki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Isojoki

The closest airport to Isojoki is Vaasa Airport, which is approximately 70 kilometers from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Isojoki from there. Other airports that are within a reasonable distance are Seinäjoki Airport and Pori Airport.

Bus Stations Near Isojoki

Isojoki has an adequate bus transportation system, with several daily bus services operating to and from the town. The nearest bus stations to Isojoki are located in Seinäjoki, Vaasa, and Pori. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the town and its surroundings conveniently.

How to Get Around in Isojoki

The best way to get around Isojoki is by car as it allows you to explore the town and its surroundings conveniently. Additionally, there are several bike rental services available if you prefer to cycle around. Walking is also an excellent option if you wish to see the town’s old district on foot.

Where to Go Next from Isojoki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Seinäjoki:

    A vibrant town known for its cultural events and attractions and is approximately 40 kilometers from Isojoki.

  • Pori:

    A beautiful coastal city that hosts some of Finland’s largest cultural events and is approximately 80 kilometers from Isojoki.

  • Tampere:

    A charming town that is well-known for its vibrant art scene and its ancient forests. It is approximately 200 kilometers from Isojoki.

Conclusion

Isojoki is a hidden gem in Southern Ostrobothnia that boasts a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. With its museums, churches, national parks, and outdoor activities, Isojoki has something for everyone. By following this travel guide, visitors can experience the best of Isojoki, Finland, and its surrounding regions while enjoying their trip comfortably and conveniently.
