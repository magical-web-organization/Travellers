Introduction to Turku Countryside

Turku countryside is a beautiful and serene region located in the southwest of Finland, about 160km west of the capital, Helsinki. The region covers a vast area of serene forests, rolling hills, breathtaking landscapes, and small towns, making it an ideal getaway destination for those seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. The countryside boasts a unique mix of natural beauty and cultural heritage, which is captivating to discover and explore.

List of Places to Visit in Turku Countryside

Turku countryside offers a range of attractions to explore, from historical sites and museums to tranquil forests and stunning landscapes. Here are just a few of the top places to visit in Turku countryside:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Turku Castle: Turku Castle is a medieval fortress located on the edge of River Aura and considered one of the most significant historical sites in Turku countryside. Built in the 13th century, the castle now stands museum showcasing the history of Turku and its people.
  • Aboa Vetus & Ars Nova: This combination museum showcases the history of Turku along with some modern art exhibitions.
  • Luostarinmaki Handicrafts Museum: This is an open-air museum located in the city center and offers a chance to learn about the history of Turku’s craftsmen and their work.

Natural Attractions of Turku Countryside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Archipelago National Park: Covering 540sq km, Archipelago National Park is a unique paradise for nature lovers. The park consists of thousands of islands, islets, and skerries which visitors can explore by boat or kayak.
  • Kurjenrahka National Park: Kurjenrahka National Park is a beautiful nature reserve located just outside Turku and offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, skiing, and bird watching.
  • Ruissalo Island: A scenic island that can be reached by a short drive from the city center, it is known for its stunning beaches, the Ruissalo fortress and its unique flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Turku countryside

Turku countryside is a paradise for hiking enthusiasts, with many trails to choose from, each offering a unique experience. Here are a few popular hiking/biking routes worth exploring:

  • The Archipelago Trail: This 250 km trail will take visitors around Turku’s archipelago-hopping island to the next island by foot, bike, or car.
  • Hiidenlinnan polku Trail: A long-distance hiking trail passing through the Kurjenrahka national park.
  • Ruissalo Island Trail: A scenic trail passing through the beautiful forested landscape of Ruissalo Island.

Best Things to Do in Turku Countryside: Activities and Tours

Turku countryside offers a range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy, here are some of the best things to do in Turku countryside.

  • Archipelago Cruise: Take a guided tour through the scenic Turku Archipelago by boat, including a pleasant stop at a few popular islands.
  • Finnish Sauna experience: Experience the Finnish sauna the traditional way by taking a sauna boat in the archipelago.
  • Fishing excursion: Go on a fishing trip in Archipelago, with a local guide who knows all the best fishing spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Turku Countryside

Here’s a week-long itinerary for those planning a visit to Turku countryside:

Food and Drink Scene of Turku Countryside

When it comes to food and drink, Turku countryside has a rich culinary scene that visitors can enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Loimulohi: Smoked salmon with a unique flavor is a must-try dish in Turku.
  • Karjalanpiirakka: Originating from the eastern region of Finland, this pastry filled with rice pudding is a popular Finnish treat.
  • Kiisseli: A traditional Finnish dessert made from berries and served with vanilla sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made from rye, barley, and juniper berries.
  • Glögi: A warm spiced drink served in winter, similar to mulled wine.
  • Salmiakki: Finnish licorice with a salty flavor. It can be purchased in many different forms, including candy or as a liqueur.

Where to Stay in Turku Countryside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Turku countryside provides numerous accommodation options with various budgets to suit the needs of its visitors. Here are some of the top options to choose from:

  • Ruissalo Spa Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on Ruissalo Island, featuring a spa, pool, and a scenic view.
  • Hostel Casa Kemi: A budget-friendly hostel located in the center of Turku, featuring private and shared rooms.
  • Maakuntahotelli: A value-for-money hotel located in Salo, that offers free breakfast to its guests.

What to Buy in Turku Countryside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Turku countryside is well-known for its unique crafts and artisanal products, making souvenir shopping an enjoyable experience. Here are some of the best products to bring back home:

  • Finnish handmade glassware and ceramics from Arabia and Iittala.
  • Locally-produced honey and berry jams.
  • Finnish woolen socks and mittens.

Local Culture and Customs of Turku Countryside

The Finnish people are renowned for their laid-back, nature-loving, and artsy culture. Turku countryside’s people embody that laid-back culture, with an appreciation for all forms of art, music and nature. The Finnish people hold great respect for their environment and are eco-conscious, making Finland one of the cleanest countries globally.

Best Time to Visit Turku Countryside

The best time to visit Turku countryside is during the summertime (June-August). During this time of the year, the weather is warm, and the countryside bursts with lush foliage and scenic views. Visitors can enjoy many outdoor activities and bask in the long days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turku Countryside

Do:

  • Respect nature and the environment, ‘jokamiehenoikeus’ allows anyone in Finland to enjoy nature but avoid causing harm to the environment.
  • Try the traditional Finnish sauna the right way by taking sauna breaks and enjoying a cold dip in the lake or sea.
  • Experience local culture by attending a music or arts event.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in nature, and make sure to follow the trash sorting rules.
  • Don’t interrupt someone while they are using the sauna, it is regarded as a sacred ritual for the Finnish people.
  • Don’t ask personal questions from strangers, Finns are known to be polite but a bit reserved in their interactions with strangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Turku Countryside

Turku countryside is one of the safest regions globally, with relatively low crime rates. However, visitors must still take the necessary precautions to avoid any incidents, such as locking valuables in safe spots and following regular safety protocols when out on excursions.

Health in Turku Countryside

Finland, including Turku countryside, is renowned for its high-quality healthcare system, with public and private medical services available to visitors. Visitors from the EU regions can use their European Health Insurance Card for most of the medical treatments, while others from outside of the EU must have proper travel insurance before entering.

Getting to Turku Countryside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Turku countryside is easily accessible, and there are various transportation options available to visitors.

Airports Near Turku Countryside

  • Turku Airport: the nearest airport located about 8km from Turku city center connects with most nearby European cities.
  • Helsinki Airport: A more extensive airport located in the capital, Helsinki, connecting international and national flights.

Bus Stations Near Turku Countryside

  • Turku Bus Station: the central bus station in Turku.
  • Naantali Bus Station: A bus station located in the lovely city of Naantali.

Other Options

  • Ferry services: Ferries connect Turku with Stockholm, Sweden, and Mariehamn in Aland Island.
  • Trains: Fast trains from Helsinki and Tampere serve the city of Turku.

How to Get Around in Turku Countryside

Turku countryside allows visitors to choose their preferred mode of transportation, whether it’s on foot, bike, car or public transport. For those who want a car, renting is a good option, and the significant rental companies are present in Turku city.

Where to Go Next from Turku Countryside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After enjoying Turku countryside, visitors can continue their journey through Finland’s other regions. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Helsinki: Finland’s vibrant and bustling capital city, located about 160km away from Turku.
  • Tampere: A thriving university city located two hours away from Turku.
  • Rovaniemi: The home of Santa Claus, located about 800km away from Turku, in Finland’s Lapland region.

Conclusion

Turku countryside is undoubtedly a dream destination for nature lovers and those keen on immersing themselves in Finnish culture. With its incredible scenery, rich history, and unique culture, visitors can expect to leave Turku countryside feeling refreshed and rejuvenated.
