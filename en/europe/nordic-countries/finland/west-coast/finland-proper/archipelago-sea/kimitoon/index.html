Introduction to Kimitoön

A hidden gem in Finland’s Archipelago Sea, Kimitoön is a stunning destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the beauty and charm of the Nordic countries. From its rich culture and history to its natural wonders and outdoor adventures, Kimitoön has something for everyone.

Located in the West Coast region of Finland Proper, Kimitoön is an island municipality that boasts a diverse landscape, including lush forests, rocky beaches, and rolling hills. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an exciting adventure, this destination is sure to exceed your expectations.

List of Places to Visit in Kimitoön

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dalsbruk Ironworks Village: This historic village is home to one of the oldest ironworks in Finland, dating back to the 17th century. Visitors can explore the museum and learn about the village’s fascinating history.
  • The Bengtskär Lighthouse: This iconic lighthouse is located on a remote island and offers incredible views of the Archipelago Sea. It’s one of Finland’s most famous lighthouses and an important part of the country’s maritime history.
  • Kimitoön Church: Built in the 15th century, this beautiful church is one of the oldest in the area and features stunning architecture and intricate details.

Natural Attractions of Kimitoön: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Teijo National Park: This beautiful park offers visitors the chance to explore Finland’s stunning natural scenery. It boasts over 80 kilometers of hiking trails, as well as opportunities for fishing, cycling, and kayaking.
  • Pähkinäinen Island: This quiet island is the perfect place to relax and enjoy the picturesque views. It boasts several sandy beaches and is a popular spot for picnics and swimming.
  • Kimito Island: This large island offers visitors plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, including hiking, cycling, and horseback riding. The island is also home to several charming villages and picturesque beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kimitoön

Teijo National Park

  • The Teijo Circle Trail (80km): This circular trail takes hikers through the heart of the national park and offers incredible views of the surrounding scenery.
  • The Lounaträsk Circuit (12km): This moderate-level hiking trail meanders around the stunning Lounaträsk Lake, offering hikers a chance to spot wildlife and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
  • The Teijo – Kirjakkala Trail (18km): This challenging hiking trail leads hikers through the forest and offers views of the Archipelago Sea.

Kimito Island

  • The King’s Trail (110km): This long-distance hiking trail runs through Kimito Island and takes hikers through some of the most stunning landscapes in the area.
  • The Archipelago Trail (250km): This scenic cycling trail runs from Turku to Åland and passes through Kimito Island. It offers cyclists stunning views of the Archipelago Sea.

Best Things to Do in Kimitoön: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore Kimitoön’s beautiful coastline and crystal-clear waters with a kayak or canoe tour.
  • Fishing: Kimitoön is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts, offering plenty of opportunities to catch salmon, pike, and perch.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the island’s charming villages and rolling hills on horseback.

Food and Drink Scene of Kimitoön

Must-Try Dishes

  • Muikku: A popular local dish featuring fried freshwater vendace fish.
  • Reindeer Meat: A traditional Finnish dish that is often served with lingonberry sauce.
  • Karelian Pies: These savory pastries are filled with rice or mashed potatoes and served with butter.

Local Beverages

  • Kimitoön Cider: This locally-produced cider is made with apples grown on the island.
  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer that is brewed with juniper berries.
  • Cloudberry Liqueur: This sweet liqueur is made from the rare and delicious cloudberries that grow in the region.

Where to Stay in Kimitoön: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • HavsVidden Resort: This upscale resort offers stunning views of the Archipelago Sea, as well as spa services, a restaurant, and a sauna.
  • Villa Mandala: This charming guesthouse is tucked away in a picturesque village and features cozy rooms and a sauna.
  • Hostel Panget: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Dalsbruk village.

What to Buy in Kimitoön: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Crafts: Kimitoön is home to many talented artisans who create beautiful pottery, textiles, and woodcarvings.
  • Kimitoön Cider: Bring home a bottle (or two) of the island’s famous cider.
  • Cloudberry Jam: This delicious jam is made from the rare and delectable cloudberries that grow in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Kimitoön

Kimitoön is deeply connected to its cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this through traditional events and festivals held throughout the year. These events often feature traditional music, dance, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Kimitoön

The best time to visit Kimitoön is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the island’s natural beauty during the spring and fall months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kimitoön

Do:

  • Respect the local cultural traditions and customs.
  • Enjoy the natural scenery and wildlife, but respect the environment by not littering or disturbing the wildlife.
  • Try the local cuisine and drink.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the national parks and nature reserves.
  • Disturb the peace and quiet of the local residents.
  • Overpack, as there are many opportunities to shop for local products and souvenirs in the villages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kimitoön

Kimitoön is a safe destination with very low crime rates. However, visitors should take usual precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Kimitoön

Finland has a world-class healthcare system, and Kimitoön has several healthcare facilities and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel health insurance before visiting.

Getting to Kimitoön: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kimitoön

  • Turku Airport (60km): Serves domestic and international flights and is the closest airport to Kimitoön.
  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (180km): Serves domestic and international flights and is Finland’s largest airport.

Bus Stations Near Kimitoön

  • Turku Bus Station: Serves regional and national routes to Kimitoön.
  • Helsinki Bus Station: Serves national routes to Kimitoön.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Ferries operate from Naantali and Turku to the islands of Kimitoön.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from Turku or Helsinki and drive to Kimitoön.

How to Get Around in Kimitoön

The best way to explore Kimitoön is by car, as it offers the most freedom and flexibility to visit the various attractions and natural wonders. However, visitors can also use bicycles or public transportation, such as buses and ferries.

Where to Go Next from Kimitoön: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Turku: This historic city is Finland’s oldest capital and offers visitors a glimpse into the country’s fascinating heritage.
  • Helsinki: Finland’s bustling capital is a modern city filled with stunning architecture, world-class museums, and a thriving food scene.
  • Åland: This autonomous region of Finland is located in the Baltic Sea and offers visitors a chance to explore its unique culture and language.

Conclusion

Kimitoön is a hidden gem that should not be missed on any trip to Finland. Its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor adventures make it the perfect destination for any traveler. Explore the island’s charming villages, indulge in the local cuisine, and immerse yourself in the tranquility and beauty of the Archipelago Sea.
