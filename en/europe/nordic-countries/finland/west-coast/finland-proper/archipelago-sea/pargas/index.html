Introduction to Pargas

Pargas is a small town situated on the border of the Turku archipelago in Finland Proper. It is surrounded by a total of 10,000 islands making it the perfect destination for island-hopping and sea-based adventures. Apart from its natural beauty, Pargas also stands out for its rich cultural and historical significance. It was once known as the “marble town” as it was the only place where marble was quarried in Finland. Today, Pargas is a hidden treasure waiting to be explored, offering its visitors a perfect blend of history, nature, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Pargas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pargas Museum

Pargas Museum has exhibitions about the history of Pargas dating back to the Middle Ages, including the marble industry, shipbuilding heritage, and the town’s role during World War II. The museum is open all year round, and it’s free of charge.

The Stone Park

The Stone Park, located in Yttermark, is a beautiful park that features sculptures made from the local marble. The park is open all year round, and you can visit it for free.

Nagu Church

The Nagu Church, located in the neighboring island of Nagu, is said to be one of the most beautiful wooden churches in Finland. It dates back to the 17th century and has a unique Baroque altar.

Natural Attractions of Pargas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pargas Archipelago National Park

The Pargas Archipelago National Park is a paradise for nature lovers. You can take a ferry to the park from Pargas, and explore the beautiful islands, forests, and the Baltic Sea. Hiking trails, camping areas, and kayaking routes are available for visitors.

The Archipelago Trail

The Archipelago Trail is a scenic route that stretches over 200 kilometers. It passes through numerous islands of the Turku archipelago, including Pargas, and offers stunning views of the Baltic Sea. Biking, hiking, and driving are the popular ways to explore the trail.

Kasberget Hill

Kasberget Hill is a popular spot for hikers and photographers who want to capture the panoramic views of Pargas and the surrounding archipelago. It’s an easy hike, and the trail takes around 20-30 minutes to reach the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pargas

Apart from the Archipelago Trail, Pargas offers various hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the most popular routes:

  • The Pargas Water Trail: A 20-kilometer-long trail that passes through the beautiful landscapes of Pargas, including forests, lakes, and the sea.

  • The Nagu Trail: A 30-kilometer-long trail that passes through the Nagu archipelago and offers stunning views of the Baltic Sea.

  • The Kasberget Hill Trail: An easy hiking trail that takes you to the top of Kasberget Hill, offering panoramic views of Pargas and the surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Pargas: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and island-hopping, Pargas offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors that promise a unique experience.

  • Marble Quarry Tour: Take a guided tour of the famous Yttermark Marble Quarry, which supplied marble for several landmarks in Helsinki, such as the Parliament House and Helsinki Cathedral.

  • Sea Kayaking Adventure: Explore the archipelago by kayaking with a guide, discovering the hidden coves and the rich marine life.

  • Fishing Adventure: Experience the traditional Finnish way of fishing with a local guide and learn how to cook your catch the Finnish way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pargas

Here’s a sample itinerary for spending a week in Pargas:

Day 1: Explore the Pargas Museum and Stone Park.

Day 2: Hike the Pargas Water Trail.

Day 3: Visit the Nagu Church and explore the Nagu Trail.

Day 4: Take a ferry to the Pargas Archipelago National Park, and camp overnight.

Day 5: Sea kayaking adventure and fishing tour.

Day 6: Bike the Archipelago Trail and visit the Kasberget Hill.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the pristine beaches of Pargas, such as Högsar Beach or Elvistranden Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Pargas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish dish that consists of fish baked inside a rye bread crust.

  • Munkkikahvit: A Finnish coffee break with munkki (a doughnut-like pastry) and coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made with juniper berries and malted grain.

  • Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka made from barley.

Where to Stay in Pargas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pargas offers a range of accommodations to fit different budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Hotel Kalkstrand: A beach-front hotel that features sea-view rooms and a sauna.

  • Pargas Gästhem: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a shared kitchen.

  • Hostel Panget: A stylish hostel that provides a welcoming atmosphere with a shared lounge and kitchen.

What to Buy in Pargas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Marble: Buy a piece of Pargas’ history with some locally quarried marble.

  • Sahti Beer: Bring home a bottle of the local Finnish beer, made with juniper berries and malted grain.

Local Culture and Customs of Pargas

  • The Finnish Sauna: Finnish culture revolves around sauna, and Pargas offers several locations to experience it.

  • Respect for Nature: As Pargas is surrounded by nature, it’s important to respect it and follow the Leave No Trace principles.

Best Time to Visit Pargas

The best time to visit Pargas is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm, and the archipelago is bustling with activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pargas

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Explore the rich cultural and historical heritage of Pargas.

Don’t:

  • Venture too far out in the sea without a guide.

  • Litter in the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pargas

Pargas is a safe place to visit, with little to no crime. However, it’s always important to keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded places.

Health in Pargas

Pargas has good health care facilities, and emergency services are widely available. Remember to bring necessary medications and wear proper clothing while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Pargas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pargas

The closest airport to Pargas is Turku Airport, which is about 50 kilometers away. Several domestic and international airlines operate flights to Turku.

Bus Stations Near Pargas

  • Pargas Bus Station: Served by regional bus lines

  • Turku Bus Station: Served by several national and international bus lines

Other Options

  • Ferry: Pargas can also be reached by ferry from either Nagu or Korpo.

How to Get Around in Pargas

The best way to get around Pargas is by car, bike, or public transportation. Bikes can be rented from several locations in Pargas, and the public transportation system is efficient and inexpensive.

Where to Go Next from Pargas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Turku: A historical city that offers numerous museums, galleries, and restaurants.

  • Helsinki: The capital city of Finland, known for its vibrant nightlife and modern architecture.

Conclusion

Pargas is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Pargas promises to provide an unforgettable experience. With its beautiful landscape, rich history, and friendly locals, Pargas is a destination that should be on your bucket list. Pack your bags, and get ready to explore the charm of Pargas!
