Introduction to Naantali

Naantali is a charming town situated in the Archipelago Sea, in Finland Proper, in the West Coast region of Finland. It is a popular summer destination for locals and visitors alike, known for its beautiful harbor, quaint old town, and vibrant cultural scene. With plenty of outdoor activities, museums, and picturesque views, Naantali offers a relaxing and refreshing retreat from the hustle and bustle of cities.

List of Places to Visit in Naantali

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Naantali Museum: Take a journey through time as you explore the history and culture of Naantali. The museum has exhibitions on art, local traditions and customs, and the famous Moomin characters.
  • Kultaranta: This magnificent summer residence of Finnish presidents overlooks the Archipelago Sea. Visitors can take a guided tour of the presidential palace to learn about Finnish history and politics.
  • Turku Castle: Just a short drive away, Turku Castle is a must-visit attraction in the region. It is one of the largest surviving medieval castles in Finland and offers a glimpse into the history of the country and the region.

Natural Attractions of Naantali: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kupittaa Park: A large and peaceful park inside the city of Turku, Kupittaa park is the perfect place for a picnic lunch or a leisurely stroll. It boasts a variety of natural landscapes, including forests, meadows and ponds.
  • Archipelago Nature Centre Blue Mussel: Located on the island of Houtskär, Archipelago Nature Centre Blue Mussel is a hub for tourism and bird-watching. Its visitors center displays many of the natural inhabitants of the archipelago.
  • Ruissalo Island: Just a short ferry ride from Naantali, Ruissalo Island is a nature lover’s paradise, offering lush forests, scenic trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naantali

Naantali and the surrounding areas offer several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include the Archipelago Trail, Turku Archipelago Trail, and the Naantali Walkway. These trails showcase the natural beauty of the region, including forests, rocky coastlines, and scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Naantali: Activities and Tours

  • Moomin World: A theme park based on the Moomin books, Moomin World is a must-visit attraction for families with children. The park offers several rides, games and interactive activities that bring the characters to life.
  • Naantali Spa: Relax and unwind in one of the many saunas, pools, and hot tubs at Naantali Spa. The spa offers a range of treatments, from massages to facials, to rejuvenate your mind, body, and spirit.
  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat tour through the archipelago and admire the stunning coastal views. There are several companies operating boat tours from Naantali harbour, including The M/S Aava and The M/S Rudolfina.

Week-Long Itinerary for Naantali

Day Activities
1 Explore the old town of Naantali, visiting the Naantali Museum, the wooden church and the harbor.
2 Head to Turku Castle and Kultaranta Presidential Palace for guided tours.
3 Take a boat tour of the archipelago and Houtskär island.
4 Visit Moomin World theme park.
5 Relax and unwind at Naantali Spa.
6 Hike through Archipelago Trail or Turku Archipelago Trail and enjoy the beauty of nature.
7 Shop for souvenirs and local products at the Naantali market and spend another day exploring the old town.

Food and Drink Scene of Naantali

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam.
  • Kalakukko: A traditional Finnish dish made of fish and pork wrapped in rye pastry and baked.
  • Kaalikääryleet: Cabbage rolls filled with minced meat, rice, and spices, and steamed until tender.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer brewed with juniper berries and malted grains.
  • Lonkero: A popular Finnish mixed drink made of gin and grapefruit soda.
  • Koskenkorva: A famous Finnish vodka distilled from barley and flavoured with natural herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Naantali: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Naantali offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Naantali Spa Hotel: A luxurious hotel located by the sea, Naantali Spa Hotel offers comfortable rooms, spa treatments and wellness programs.
  • Villa Antonius: A charming and cozy guesthouse in the heart of the old town, Villa Antonius offers a unique and authentic Finnish experience.
  • Ruissalo Spa Hotel: Located on the picturesque island of Ruissalo, this hotel offers a tranquil and relaxing atmosphere, delicious food, and easy access to outdoor activities in nature.

What to Buy in Naantali: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Naantali offers a variety of unique and authentic souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home as gifts or keepsakes. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Handmade wooden toys: Naantali is home to many skilled craftsmen who create beautiful and intricate wooden toys that are perfect for children and adults alike.
  • Finnish sweets and chocolate: Finnish sweets are known for their unique flavors and high-quality ingredients. Try Fazer chocolates, Finnish liquorice, and Salmiakki candies.
  • Local crafts and textiles: Naantali has a rich and vibrant history of textile production, and you can find many high-quality crafts and textiles made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Naantali

Finnish culture is known for its quiet and reserved nature, love of nature, and respect for personal space and privacy. Visitors to Naantali should keep in mind the following customs and etiquette:

  • Saunas: Saunas are an important part of Finnish culture, and it is common to take a sauna before or after outdoor activities. Don’t be surprised if you’re invited to join a local in their sauna.
  • Alcohol: Finnish culture is quite relaxed about drinking, but it is important to respect local laws and customs. Drinking in public places is strictly prohibited, and it is not polite to get too drunk in public.
  • Nature: Finns have a strong connection with nature, and it is important to respect the environment and practice responsible tourism. Don’t litter, respect wildlife, and adhere to designated trails and public access points.

Best Time to Visit Naantali

The best time to visit Naantali is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is mild and sunny, and the town is bustling with festive activities and events. The archipelago landscape is at its most beautiful during this season. Winter can be quite chilly, but the town offers several winter activities such as skiing, skating and winter hikes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naantali

Do:

  • Take a sauna: Saunas are an important part of Finnish culture and a great way to relax and unwind.
  • Enjoy nature: Finland is one of the most beautiful countries in the world, with plenty of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery to enjoy.
  • Try local food and drink: Finnish cuisine is unique and tasty, and it’s a great way to experience the culture and local ingredients.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud: Finns are reserved people, and it is important to respect their personal space and privacy. Avoid being too loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Litter: Finns are proud of their clean and pristine environment, and it is important to respect nature by not littering or damaging public spaces.
  • Get too drunk: Finnish culture is quite relaxed about drinking, but it is important to drink responsibly and avoid getting too drunk in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naantali

Naantali is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are relatively low. Visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Naantali

Finland’s healthcare system is highly developed, and visitors can expect high-quality medical care if needed. It is recommended to have comprehensive travel health insurance for emergency medical treatment and evacuation.

Getting to Naantali: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naantali

The closest airport to Naantali is Turku Airport, which is located about 15 km away. Many international airlines fly to Turku from European cities, and it is easy to connect to Naantali by bus, taxi or car rental.

Bus Stations Near Naantali

There are several bus stations near Naantali, including the Turku Bus Station, which offers regular services to Naantali throughout the day.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Naantali by ferry or cruise ship, as the town is a popular stop on many Baltic Sea cruise itineraries.

How to Get Around in Naantali

The best way to explore Naantali and the surrounding areas is by car or bike rental. There are several rental companies in the area, and it is easy to navigate the town and surrounding areas by car. Public transportation is also available, with regular bus services connecting Naantali with nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Naantali: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many beautiful and interesting destinations near Naantali that are worth exploring, including:

  • Turku: Just a short drive away, Turku is a vibrant and historic city known for its medieval castle, lively market square, and iconic landmarks.
  • Åland Islands: Known for its beautiful scenery, peaceful lifestyle, and unique cultural heritage, Åland Islands are a popular destination for nature lovers and history buffs.
  • Helsinki: The capital city of Finland is a must-visit destination for culture, art, and history enthusiasts. Helsinki is home to many museums, galleries and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Naantali is a beautiful and vibrant town that offers something for everyone. From scenic outdoor adventures to rich cultural heritage, there’s no shortage of things to see and do here. With easy access to nearby cities and attractions, Naantali is a perfect destination that can be enjoyed all-year-round.
