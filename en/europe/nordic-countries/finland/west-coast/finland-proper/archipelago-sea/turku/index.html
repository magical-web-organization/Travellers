Introduction to Turku

Turku is a charming city located on the southwest coast of Finland. As the oldest city in Finland, it’s also rich in history and culture, making it a popular destination for travelers from all over the world.

The city is situated in the Archipelago Sea, which means you can enjoy breathtaking views of the sparkling sea, along with the natural beauty of the surrounding islands and forests. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or delicious food and drink, Turku has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Turku

Turku is a city with plenty to offer, and here are some of the must-visit attractions in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Turku Castle: This fortress castle dates back to the 13th century and has served as both a military fortress and a royal residence over the years.
  • Turku Cathedral: This stunning cathedral is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Turku. It’s also the head church of the Evangelical Lutheran Church of Finland.
  • Aboa Vetus & Ars Nova: This unique museum combines two different collections - archaeological artifacts and contemporary art. It’s a fun and interesting way to explore the city’s history as well as modern art.

Natural Attractions of Turku: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ruissalo Island: This stunning island is a perfect location to relax and enjoy nature. It offers beautiful beaches, a botanical garden, and some great walking trails.
  • Vartiovuori Observatory: Climb to the top of the hill and get a bird’s eye view of the city! It’s a great spot to watch the sunset.
  • Archipelago Trail: Rent a bike or a car and explore the stunning views of the Archipelago Sea. It’s a perfect location for a road trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Turku

If you are looking to explore Turku on foot or by bike, we highly recommend the following routes:

  • The Old Town Turku Walk: This walking tour takes you through the historic streets of Turku and introduces you to the city’s fascinating past.
  • Archipelago Trail: This 200-kilometer route takes you through the stunning scenery of the Archipelago Sea, and you can easily explore it by bike or car.
  • Kurjenrahka National Park Trail: This 30-kilometer trail is a perfect location to explore the stunning nature of Turku. It’s ideal for a day trip!

Best Things to Do in Turku: Activities and Tours

  • Turku Food Walk: A fun and unique way to explore the city’s culinary scene. You can taste traditional Finnish dishes, local craft beer, and other delicious treats.
  • Boat tours: There are plenty of boat tours available in Turku that take you along the beautiful Archipelago Sea. You can enjoy the stunning views and take in the natural beauty of the surrounding islands.
  • Turku Art Museum: This museum has a large collection of Finnish art from the 19th century to the present day. It’s a perfect location to explore the local art scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Turku

Finnish cuisine is renowned for its simple, fresh, and natural ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Turku:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karjalanpiirakka: Traditional Karelian pasties made from rye flour stuffed with rice pudding or mashed potatoes.
  • Kalakukko: Finnish fish stuffed inside a rye dough and baked slowly.
  • Rieska: A local bread made of potatoes that can be eaten as a snack or as a side.

Local Beverages

  • Salmiakki: A salty licorice-flavored candy popular with the Finns.
  • Finnish beer: Try some locally brewed craft beers.
  • Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka made from barley and pure water.

Where to Stay in Turku: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Turku has a variety of accommodation options for travelers, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Radisson Blu Marina Palace Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Turku and provides beautiful views of the Aura River.
  • Hostel Harriet: This hostel is close to the city center and offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.
  • Guesthouse Kupittaa: Located near the train station, this guesthouse offers modern rooms and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Turku: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some souvenirs and local products to bring home, we recommend the following:

  • Finnish design: The Iittala and Marimekko shops offer stunning Finnish design products.
  • Finnish chocolate: Try some Fazer chocolate, which is Finland’s oldest and most famous chocolate brand.
  • Sauna products: Finland is famous for its sauna culture, and you’ll find loads of sauna products on the market, like birch whisks, soaps, and oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Turku

Finnish culture is unique and different, but here are some common customs that you should know:

  • Sauna culture: Finns take their saunas very seriously, and it’s an integral part of their culture.
  • Silence: Many Finns value silence and personal space, so they might not be as outgoing and talkative as people from other countries.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in Finland as it’s included in the price of the meal.

Best Time to Visit Turku

The best time to visit Turku is during the summer months (June to August) when the temperatures are mild, and the days are long. However, it’s also the peak tourist season, so be prepared to deal with crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turku

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Turku:

Do:

  • Enjoy the sauna culture.
  • Explore the city’s history and architecture.
  • Try traditional Finnish dishes.

Don’t:

  • Invade personal space.
  • Be loud and talkative in public transportation.
  • Expect to see the northern lights, as they’re rare in Turku due to the city’s location in the south of Finland.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips for travelers visiting Turku:

Safety in Turku

  • Turku is a relatively safe city, but make sure to keep your belongings close to you in crowded areas.
  • Be careful when using public transportation, especially at night, and avoid unlicensed taxis.

Health in Turku

  • Finnish tap water is safe to drink, so there’s no need to buy bottled water.
  • The Finnish healthcare system is reliable and offers high-quality care, so there’s no need to worry about getting sick during your visit.

Getting to Turku: Transportation Options and Logistics

Turku is easily accessible from Helsinki and other major Finnish cities. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Turku

  • Turku Airport: The closest airport to Turku, located approximately 8 kilometers away from the city center.
  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport: Located approximately 160 kilometers away from Turku.

Bus Stations Near Turku

  • Turku Bus Station: Located in the city center, here you can find the local and regional bus lines.
  • Helsinki Bus Station: Located approximately 170 km away from Turku.

Other Options

  • Train: You can easily reach Turku by train from Helsinki and other major cities in Finland.
  • Car: You can rent a car and enjoy the beautiful scenery and drive along the Archipelago Trail.

How to Get Around in Turku

Turku is a relatively small city and easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to travel further, here are some transportation options:

  • Buses: You can use the local bus network that connects the city center with the surrounding areas.
  • Bike: Turku has excellent bike roads, and it’s a cheap, eco-friendly, and fun way to explore the city.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Turku: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore other nearby destinations from Turku, here are some options:

  • Helsinki: The capital of Finland is only a two-hour drive or train journey away.
  • Naantali: This charming town is located just a few kilometers from Turku and is famous for its picturesque old town and Moomin World, a theme park inspired by the famous Moomin characters.
  • Åland: This autonomous archipelago in the Baltic Sea is a great location if you want to experience the unique Swedish-Finnish culture.

Conclusion

Turku is an incredibly charming city, with plenty to offer in terms of culture, natural beauty, and delicious food. We hope this comprehensive guide has given you a good idea of what to expect when planning a trip to Turku, and the best places to visit and things to do in the city.
