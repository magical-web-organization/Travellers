A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Rymättylä

If you’re looking for a peaceful getaway where you can experience the beauty of nature and traditional Finnish culture, Rymättylä is the perfect destination. Located in Finland Proper, on the west coast of Finland, this small municipality is known for its stunning landscapes, cultural attractions, and delicious food. In this guide, we provide an overview of everything you need to know to plan your trip to Rymättylä.

List of Places to Visit in Rymättylä

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pitkäjärven Stone Age Village: This open-air museum lets you experience the daily life of Stone Age people. You can see replicas of their houses, tools, and other artifacts, and learn how they survived in the harsh environment.
  • Rymättylä Church: This charming wooden church dates back to the 18th century and boasts intricate wooden carvings and unique architecture.
  • Rymättylä Local Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the traditional Finnish way of life, with exhibits on handicrafts, agriculture, and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Rymättylä: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Teijo National Park: Just a short drive from Rymättylä, this park features diverse landscapes, including forests, lakes, and rocky shores. You can hike, bike, or kayak to explore the area and see wildlife like moose, deer, and eagles.
  • Rymättylä Archipelago: With over 600 islands, this archipelago offers endless opportunities for fishing, boating, and swimming in pristine waters.
  • Koivuniemi Nature Reserve: This reserve boasts unique geological formations and rare plant species, making it a must-see for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rymättylä

  • Teijo National Park Trails: The park features several trails of varying difficulty, allowing hikers and bikers to explore the diverse natural landscape.
  • Rymättylä Coastal Route: This scenic route follows the coast of the municipality and offers stunning views of the archipelago and the sea.
  • Koivuniemi Nature Trail: This short trail takes you through the unique geological formations of the nature reserve, offering a glimpse into Finland’s ancient past.

Best Things to Do in Rymättylä: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Excursions: Book a fishing tour to explore the abundant waters of the Rymättylä archipelago and catch local fish, such as perch, pike, and whitefish.
  • Canoeing/Kayaking Tours: Paddle your way through the serene waters of Rymättylä’s lakes and archipelago, where you’re likely to spot local wildlife and enjoy scenic views.
  • Lighthouse Tours: Visit the lighthouses of Rymättylä, including the Archipelago Sea National Park’s Bengtskär lighthouse, to experience spectacular views of the archipelago and learn about the history of navigation in Finland.

Food and Drink Scene of Rymättylä

Must-Try Dishes

  • Muikku: A local species of whitefish that is typically fried and served with potatoes and dill sauce.
  • Korvapuusti: A Finnish cinnamon bun that is perfect as a snack or morning coffee treat.
  • Karjalanpiirakka: A traditional Finnish pastry made with rye flour and filled with rice pudding or mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Lonkero: A popular Finnish drink made with grapefruit soda and gin.
  • Sahti: A traditional Finnish beer made with juniper berries and malted grains.
  • Salmiakki Koskenkorva: A Finnish vodka that is infused with salty liquorice and has a distinct salty taste.

Where to Stay in Rymättylä: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rymättylä Camping: This campsite offers a range of accommodations, from tent sites to cottages, and has scenic views of the archipelago.
  • Teijo Bed & Breakfast: This cozy B&B offers comfortable rooms and a homemade breakfast with local ingredients.
  • Hotel Kasnäs: Located just a short drive from Rymättylä, this hotel features a spa, sauna, and restaurant with a view of the sea.

What to Buy in Rymättylä: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Rymättylä is known for its traditional handicrafts such as textiles and ceramics that make great souvenirs.
  • Local Food Products: Take home some local specialties like smoked vendace and Finnish sweets.
  • Local Art: Embrace the spirit of Rymättylä by purchasing unique pieces of Finnish art made with local materials and inspired by local culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Rymättylä

Rymättylä has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through traditional festivals and customs. The Finnish way of life is centered around the importance of nature, community, and family, which is reflected in the everyday life of Rymättylä’s locals.

Best Time to Visit Rymättylä

The best time to visit Rymättylä is during the summer, from May to September when the weather is warm, and the sun shines for long hours. During this time, you can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, boating, and fishing, as well as attend local festivals and markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rymättylä

Rymättylä is a safe destination, with low crime rates and well-maintained infrastructure. However, visitors should still exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Rymättylä

Finland has a high standard of healthcare, and most visitors can access medical care in case of emergencies. However, visitors should still have health insurance and take necessary safety precautions to prevent accidents and injuries.

Public transportation is limited in Rymättylä, so visitors may want to consider renting a car or bike to get around. Taxis are also available but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Rymättylä: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Turku: This historic city is just a short drive from Rymättylä and offers attractions like the Turku Castle, Turku Cathedral, and the Turku Art Museum.
  • Helsinki: Finland’s vibrant capital city is just a few hours’ drive from Rymättylä and offers a vast array of cultural attractions, trendy restaurants and bars, and shopping.
  • The Åland Islands: This unique archipelago is located between Sweden and Finland and is accessible by ferry.

Rymättylä is a hidden gem in Finland, offering visitors a chance to experience traditional Finnish culture and the country’s stunning natural beauty.
