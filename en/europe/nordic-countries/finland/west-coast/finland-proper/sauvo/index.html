Introduction to Sauvo

Located in the southern part of Finland, Sauvo is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of natural beauty. The town dates back to the 13th century and is known for its beautiful countryside, picturesque landscapes, and cultural attractions. With a population of around 4,000 people, Sauvo is a small, cozy town that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. In this guide, we will take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay during your visit to Sauvo.

List of Places to Visit in Sauvo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sauvo’s rich history is evident in the many cultural attractions that the town has to offer. One of the must-see spots is the Sauvo Church, a beautiful medieval church built in the 15th century. The church is surrounded by a cemetery and is a popular spot for photography.

Another interesting place to visit is the Sauvo Museum, which is situated in a charming old school building. The museum showcases the town’s history and culture through exhibits and artifacts, including traditional clothing, utensils, and farming tools. The museum is a great place to learn more about the town’s history and get a glimpse of the Finnish way of life in the past.

Natural Attractions of Sauvo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sauvo is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, and there are several places where you can enjoy the outdoors. One popular destination is the Kurjenrahka National Park, which offers hiking trails, fishing spots, and camping facilities. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery and wildlife, including bears, lynxes, and wolves.

Another must-visit spot is the Teijo National Park, which is located on the southern coast of Finland. The park is a popular destination for hiking, fishing, and bird-watching and offers stunning views of the archipelago and the Baltic Sea. Visitors can also enjoy canoeing and kayaking in the park’s many lakes and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sauvo

Sauvo offers several hiking and biking routes for visitors who want to explore the town and its surroundings on foot or by bike. One popular route is the Teijo-Sauvo trail, which is a 20-kilometer long trail that passes through beautiful forests, lakes, and hills. The trail is suitable for both beginners and advanced hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Another popular route is the Kurjenrahka hiking trail, which is a 35-kilometer long trail that takes visitors through beautiful forests and marshlands.

Best Things to Do in Sauvo: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Sauvo offers several other activities for visitors. One popular activity is fishing, and there are several spots in the town where you can do so, including the Kavilo fishing spot and the River Kurkelanjoki. Visitors can also enjoy kayaking and canoeing in the nearby lakes and rivers.

Another popular activity is horse riding, and there are several stables in and around the town that offer horse riding tours through the beautiful countryside. Visitors can also take part in farm tours and learn more about the Finnish way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sauvo

Day 1: Visit the Sauvo Church and the Sauvo Museum

Day 2: Hike or bike the Teijo-Sauvo trail

Day 3: Visit the Kurjenrahka National Park

Day 4: Go fishing in the Kavilo fishing spot or the River Kurkelanjoki

Day 5: Enjoy a horse riding tour through the countryside

Day 6: Take a farm tour and learn more about the Finnish way of life

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks

Food and Drink Scene of Sauvo

Must-Try Dishes

Sauvo is known for its traditional Finnish dishes, many of which are made from locally sourced ingredients. One of the must-try dishes is the Karjalanpaisti, a hearty stew made with beef, potatoes, and carrots. Another popular dish is the Lihapullat, which are Finnish meatballs served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

Sauvo is known for its beer, and there are several local breweries that you can visit during your trip. One popular brewery is the Sauvon Panimo, which produces a range of craft beers using locally sourced ingredients. Another popular drink is the Finnish berry wine, which is made from a variety of berries, including lingonberries, raspberries, and blueberries.

Where to Stay in Sauvo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sauvo offers several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. One popular option is the Villa Wolax, a cozy guesthouse situated on the shores of Lake Teijo. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a range of activities, including sauna, kayaking, and canoeing.

Another popular option is the Hotel Kasnäs, which is situated on the southwestern coast of Finland. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, restaurants, and spa facilities and is a popular destination for visitors who want to relax and enjoy the beauty of the Finnish archipelago.

What to Buy in Sauvo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sauvo offers several souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home as a reminder of their trip. One popular souvenir is the traditional Finnish knife, which is known as the puukko. Another popular item is the Finnish Sauna Whisk, which is used in the traditional Finnish sauna experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Sauvo

Finnish culture is known for its emphasis on nature, simplicity, and honesty, and these values are evident in the town of Sauvo. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly and welcoming locals who value their traditions and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Sauvo

The best time to visit Sauvo is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural beauty of the town is at its best. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and outdoor activities during this time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sauvo

Sauvo is a safe destination for travelers, and visitors can explore the town without any significant safety concerns. However, it’s always good to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings when traveling.

Health in Sauvo

Sauvo offers excellent healthcare facilities and services, and visitors can access medical care if needed. It’s always good to carry basic medication and first aid supplies.

Getting to Sauvo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sauvo

The closest airport to Sauvo is Turku Airport, which is located around 35 kilometers from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or a bus from the airport to Sauvo.

Bus Stations Near Sauvo

Sauvo is well connected to other Finnish towns and cities through the Finnish bus network. Visitors can take a bus from Helsinki, Turku, and other towns and cities to Sauvo.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or a bike and drive to Sauvo from nearby towns and cities. This is a great option for visitors who want to explore the countryside and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Sauvo

Sauvo is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a car or a taxi to explore the surrounding areas and natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Sauvo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sauvo is a great base for exploring other nearby towns and cities, including Turku, which is located around 50 kilometers from Sauvo. Other nearby destinations include the town of Salo and the Finnish archipelago.

Conclusion

Sauvo is a charming town that offers a great escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals, it’s a great destination for travelers who want to explore the Finnish countryside and experience a traditional way of life. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or just relaxing and enjoying the local food and drinks, Sauvo has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and start exploring this hidden gem in southern Finland.
