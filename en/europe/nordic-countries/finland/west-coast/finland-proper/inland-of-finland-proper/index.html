Introduction to Inland of Finland Proper

Inland of Finland Proper is located in the heart of the Finnish Lakeland, surrounded by forests, lakes, and rivers. This region is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and culture lovers alike. As the name suggests, it is an inland area, making it a perfect destination for a peaceful getaway. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, boutique hotels, local food and drink scene, and useful travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Inland of Finland Proper

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aboa Vetus & Ars Nova

The museum complex contains two buildings - Aboa Vetus, which is a museum of cultural history, and Ars Nova, which is a museum of contemporary art. The former displays archeological findings of the city for more than 700 years, while the latter displays contemporary Finnish art.

Turku Castle

Turku Castle is one of the oldest buildings in Finland. The castle was first built in the 13th century and served as a stronghold for Swedish monarchs. Later, it was converted into a prison and then into a museum. Visitors can take a guided tour through the castle’s various rooms, including a medieval banquet hall and a torture chamber.

Paavo Nurmi Stadium

Paavo Nurmi Stadium is named after Paavo Nurmi, who is widely regarded as the greatest middle-distance runner of all time. Visitors can take a tour of the stadium, which includes a visit to the Paavo Nurmi Museums, which displays memorabilia from the athlete’s life.

Natural Attractions of Inland of Finland Proper: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ruissalo Island

Ruissalo is located in the Turku Archipelago and is known for its beautiful scenery, beaches, and nature trails. Visitors can hike, bike, or walk through the island’s forests and meadows, enjoy the sun on its beaches, or visit the Botanic Garden.

Archipelago National Park

This is an archipelago area consisting of thousands of islands and islets. The park offers a variety of activities, including hiking, kayaking, fishing, and camping. Visitors can take a guided boat tour to explore the different islands.

Kurjenrahka National Park

Kurjenrahka National Park is located near the city of Turku and is known for its unique wetlands and vast forests. Visitors can explore its walking trails, go birdwatching, or rent a canoe to explore the park’s numerous lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Inland of Finland Proper

Kalpa Route

The Kalpa Route is a 60 km hiking trail that passes through the rugged terrain of Lapland. This route is perfect for nature lovers who want to experience the wild nature of Finland.

Ylläs-Levi Hiking Trail

This 130 km long hiking trail is located in the northern part of Finland. The trail starts in Ylläsjärvi and ends in Levi and is one of the most popular hiking routes in Finland. The trail takes hikers through breathtaking landscapes, including forests, fells, and lakes.

Best Things to Do in Inland of Finland Proper: Activities and Tours

Aurora hunting in Lapland

The Northern Lights are a natural phenomenon that can be witnessed in Lapland. Visitors can join a guided Aurora hunting tour, which takes them to the best locations for viewing the Northern Lights.

Sauna Experience

Sauna Culture is deeply rooted in Finnish tradition and is considered one of the most popular leisure activities in Finland. Visitors can enjoy this authentic Finnish experience at the Turku Sauna or Kupittaa Park Sauna, both located in Turku.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inland of Finland Proper

Day Plan of Action
Day 1 Arrival at Turku and check-in at hotel. Visit the Turku Castle for a guided tour.
Day 2 & 3 Explore Ruissalo Island and its Botanic Garden.
Day 4 Visit Paavo Nurmi Stadium and then take a ferry to the Archipelago National Park.
Day 5 Day trip to Helsinki. Visit historical landmarks such as the Helsinki Cathedral and Suomenlinna Fortress.
Day 6 Visit Aboa Vetus & Ars Nova museums and then head to Kurjenrahka National Park for a nature walk.

Food and Drink Scene of Inland of Finland Proper

Must-Try Dishes

Poronkäristys

Poronkäristys is a traditional Lappish dish which is made from reindeer meat and served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam. Visitors can try this dish at the Saana Restaurant, located in the center of Turku.

Kalakukko

Kalakukko is a traditional Finnish food that originated from the Savonia Region. It consists of a rye bread filled with fish and pork fat. Visitors can find this dish at the local Turku Market Hall.

Local Beverages

Glogg

Glogg is a traditional Finnish mulled wine that is consumed during winter months. It is made from red wine, sugar, cinnamon, and cloves. Visitors can find Glogg at the Turku Christmas Market.

Oltermanni

Oltermanni is a Finnish cheese that is mild in taste and rich in texture. Visitors can try Oltermanni at any local food outlet in Turku.

Where to Stay in Inland of Finland Proper: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boutique Hotels

Radisson Blu Marina Palace Hotel, Turku

Radisson Blu Marina Palace Hotel is located in the center of Turku and offers luxurious rooms with scenic views of the Aura River. The hotel also has a spa and a restaurant with a riverside terrace.

Naantali Spa

Naantali Spa is located in the scenic town of Naantali and is known for its spa facilities and wellness services. The hotel also has restaurants, bars, and an outdoor pool.

Hostels

Borea Adventures Hostel

Borea Adventures Hostel is located in the Archipelago National Park and offers comfortable accommodations for budget travelers. The hostel also offers guided tours and activities such as kayaking and fishing.

Hostel Harriet

Hostel Harriet is located in Turku and is known for its cozy atmosphere and prime location. The hostel also offers a terrace, shared kitchen, and a common lounge.

What to Buy in Inland of Finland Proper: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moomin Souvenirs

Moomins are Finnish fictional characters that have become a symbol of Finnish culture. Visitors can find Moomin souvenirs at any local shop in Turku.

Finnish Handicrafts

Finnish handicrafts are unique and handcrafted products that reflect the Finnish way of life. Visitors can buy Finnish handicrafts at the Turku Market Hall or any local market in Turku.

Local Culture and Customs of Inland of Finland Proper

Finnish culture is known for its simplicity, humility, and respect for nature. The Finns take pride in their Sauna culture, which is deeply rooted in their customs. The locals also value punctuality and respecting personal space.

Best Time to Visit Inland of Finland Proper

The best time to visit Inland of Finland Proper is during the summer months from June to August when the temperatures are mild, and days are longer. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and boating. Winter months from December to February are ideal for visitors who want to witness the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inland of Finland Proper

Do:

  • Respect the Finnish culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Keep a safe distance from wild animals.
  • Buy Finnish handicrafts as souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Swim in lakes or rivers without proper attire.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Inland of Finland Proper

Inland of Finland Proper is a safe area to visit. Visitors should always take precautions and stay aware of their surroundings. It is important to have travel insurance that covers emergency medical treatment.

Health in Inland of Finland Proper

Finland has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors are required to have medical insurance. It is recommended to have necessary vaccinations before traveling to Finland.

Getting to Inland of Finland Proper: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Inland of Finland Proper

The nearest airports to Inland of Finland Proper are Turku Airport and Helsinki International Airport. Turku airport is located 8 kilometers from the city center, and Helsinki International Airport is located about 150 kilometers from Turku.

Bus Stations Near Inland of Finland Proper

Turku city center has several bus stations, with regular bus services to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Ferries and boats are an excellent way to explore the archipelago areas of Inland of Finland Proper. There are daily ferry services between Turku and Stockholm, Sweden.

How to Get Around in Inland of Finland Proper

Public transport is well developed in Inland of Finland Proper. Buses are the most convenient way to travel around the area. Taxis are also widely available. Walking and biking are perfect for exploring the cities and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Inland of Finland Proper: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Helsinki

Helsinki is the capital city of Finland and is known for its beautiful architecture and design. Visitors can explore historic landmarks, museums, and experience the unique Finnish culture and way of life.

Tallinn

Tallinn is the capital city of Estonia and is located about 2 hours away from Turku by ferry. Visitors can explore the beautiful old town, visit museums, and enjoy Estonian cuisine.

Conclusion

Inland of Finland Proper is a beautiful destination that offers a perfect combination of Finnish culture, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Visitors can explore its museums and landmarks, hike through its forests and lakes, and enjoy the local food and drink scene. With well-connected public transport and a number of accommodation options, Inland of Finland Proper is perfect for a summer vacation or a winter getaway.
