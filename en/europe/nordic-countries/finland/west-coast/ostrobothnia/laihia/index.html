Introduction to Laihia

Located in Ostrobothnia, in West Coast Finland, Laihia boasts of its charming village atmosphere and the authentic Finnish countryside experience. With its rich cultural and natural heritage, it is an ideal destination for those who seek to explore the unspoiled beauty of Finland and indulge in unique local traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Laihia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Laihia Church: A historic wooden church famous for its iconic crucifix, made by the Finnish artisan Mikael Toppelius.

  • Laihia Local Heritage Museum: This museum displays artifacts from Laihia’s history, including tools used in traditional farming, fishing, and forestry.

Natural Attractions of Laihia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Vaskiluoto Nature Reserve: This unique reserve is an ecologically diverse landscape, housing a variety of plants, birds, and insects. Many walking and hiking trails pass through the area, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers.

  • Laihia Water Tower: This tower, located in the center of the town, offers panoramic views of Laihia and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laihia

  • The Hurskainen Trail: This trail is ideal for hiking and biking, with its picturesque views of the Finnish countryside and cultural landmarks.

  • The Kaskinen Trail: This path winds through the beautiful coastline of Kaskinen, offering stunning views of the Baltic Sea.

Best Things to Do in Laihia: Activities and Tours

  • Laihia Kanuuna: Enjoy a traditional Finnish sauna with an experienced sauna master, who will guide you through the sauna rituals and explain the importance of this beloved Finnish tradition.

  • Laihia Käsityökoulu: Take a crafting class and learn the traditional skills of Finnish handicrafts, such as weaving and felt-making.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laihia

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrival and Check-in. Evening walk around the town.
Day 2 Visit Laihia Church, have lunch at Laihia Kanuuna, and explore Laihia Local Heritage Museum.
Day 3 Hike or bike the Hurskainen Trail, visit the Vaskiluoto Nature Reserve.
Day 4 Learn about Finnish handicrafts at Laihia Käsityökoulu.
Day 5 Drive to Kaskinen Trail and explore the beautiful coastline.
Day 6 Visit Laihia Water Tower and enjoy a Finnish sauna at Laihia Kanuuna.
Day 7 Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Laihia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kalakukko: This traditional Finnish pie is filled with fish and bacon, and baked in rye bread.

  • Lihapullat: Finnish meatballs are typically made with a mixture of ground beef and pork, breadcrumbs, and eggs, and are usually served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sahti: an ancient Finnish beer that is still brewed and enjoyed today.

  • Koskenkorva: a Finnish vodka made from barley and a pure water source, considered one of Finland’s national drinks.

Where to Stay in Laihia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Laihia Seurahuone: This charming hotel offers comfortable rooms and a great restaurant.

  • Laihian Kohina Guesthouse: A guesthouse with a rustic atmosphere, located close to the Vaskiluoto Nature Reserve.

What to Buy in Laihia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Arts and Crafts: Buy handmade Finnish goods like textiles, knitwear, and pottery.

  • Books About Laihia: There are plenty of books available that delve deeper into Laihia’s history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Laihia

  • Finnish locals may come across as reserved, but they are generally very warm and friendly once you get to know them.

  • Laihia has a strong tradition of handicrafts, and visitors can appreciate the beautiful textiles made by local artisans.

Best Time to Visit Laihia

Laihia can be visited at any time of the year, but the summer and autumn months are the most popular. During these months, the weather is mild and outdoor activities like hiking and biking are more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laihia

Do:

  • Do feel free to ask the locals for advice on what to see and do.

  • Do try Finnish sauna and the traditional Finnish cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disturb the peace and quiet of the Finnish countryside, especially by littering.

  • Don’t forget to take off your shoes before entering someone’s home.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laihia

Laihia is generally considered a safe town to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should be aware of the risks of outdoor activities, such as hiking and swimming.

Health in Laihia

Laihia has a small health center that can handle minor illnesses and injuries. For more serious medical cases, visitors should travel to Vaasa, which has a larger hospital.

Getting to Laihia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Laihia

The nearest airport is Vaasa Airport, which is 44 km from Laihia.

Bus Stations Near Laihia

Buses run from Vaasa to Laihia regularly. The bus journey takes around 40 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car in Vaasa and drive to Laihia. The journey takes approximately 30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Laihia

Visitors can walk, bike, or drive around Laihia. Bikes and cars can be rented from various rental companies in Vaasa.

Where to Go Next from Laihia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kokkola: a picturesque old town located 40 km from Laihia.

  • Petajavesi: Explore the UNESCO-listed Petajavesi Old Church, a masterpiece of Nordic wooden architecture.

Conclusion

Laihia offers visitors a unique experience of the authentic Finnish countryside with its diverse range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. With its charming village atmosphere and welcoming locals, Laihia is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore the unspoiled beauty of Finland.
