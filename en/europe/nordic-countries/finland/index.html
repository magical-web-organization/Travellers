Welcome to Finland, a beautiful and fascinating Nordic country. Known for its pristine natural beauty, vibrant cities, and rich history, Finland is a must-see destination for anyone planning a trip to Europe. Whether you’re an outdoor adventurer seeking thrilling experiences, a culture vulture interested in museums and historical sites, or a foodie looking to sample some of the country’s unique cuisine, Finland has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to this amazing country, including the best places to visit, things to do, and practical travel tips.

Introduction to Finland

Finland is a Nordic country located in Northern Europe, bordering the Baltic Sea, Gulf of Bothnia, and Gulf of Finland. It is the eighth largest country in Europe and has a population of over 5 million people. The capital city is Helsinki, which is a vibrant and modern city that is also a cultural and economic hub of the country. Finland has a rich culture, history, and natural environment that make it a great destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Finland

When planning a trip to Finland, there are many places to see and things to do. Here is a list of some of the must-see destinations for any trip to Finland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Finnish National Museum in Helsinki
  • The Ateneum Art Museum in Helsinki
  • The Turku Castle
  • The Suomenlinna Fortress
  • The Arctic Circle in Rovaniemi
  • The Sámi Cultural Center Sajos

Natural Attractions of Finland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Finnish Lakeland, home to over 180,000 lakes and islands
  • The Archipelago of Turku
  • The Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis) in Lapland
  • The Korkeasaari Island Zoo in Helsinki
  • The Koli National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Finland

Finland is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are many beautiful hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the best include:

  • The Kaldoaivi Wilderness Trail, located in the Finnish Lapland
  • The Archipelago Trail, which covers 250km of islands and bridges
  • The Hossa National Park hiking trails
  • The Pallas-Yllästunturi National Park hiking and skiing trails

Best Things to Do in Finland: Activities and Tours

Finland has a range of activities and tours to suit any traveler’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Finland:

  • Visit a traditional Finnish sauna
  • Canoeing and kayaking in the Finnish Lakeland
  • Snowmobiling in Lapland
  • Reindeer and husky rides in Lapland
  • Ice fishing and snowshoeing

Week-Long Itinerary for Finland

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Finland, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Helsinki: Explore the city, visit the Finnish National Museum and the Ateneum Art Museum
Day 2 Turku: Visit the Turku Castle and the Archipelago of Turku
Day 3 Tampere: Visit the Vapriikki Museum and the Pyynikki Observation Tower
Day 4 Kuusamo: Go hiking in the Oulanka National Park
Day 5 Rovaniemi: Visit the Santa Claus Village and the Arctic Circle
Day 6 Kemi: Visit the SnowCastle and take an icebreaker tour in the Bothnian Sea
Day 7 Helsinki: Visit the Sibelius Monument and Temppeliaukio Church

Food and Drink Scene of Finland

The food and drink scene in Finland is unique and varied. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karelian pasty: a traditional Finnish dish made of rye dough and filled with rice or mashed potato
  • Kalakukko: a fish pie made with rye flour and filled with fish and bacon
  • Ruisleipä: a traditional Finnish sourdough rye bread

Local Beverages

  • Lapin Kulta: a Finnish beer
  • Koskenkorva: a Finnish vodka made from barley and spring water
  • Glögi: a Finnish mulled wine served during Christmas season

  • Hotel Kamp in Helsinki
  • Radisson Blu Plaza Hotel in Helsinki

What to Buy in Finland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Finland has a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the top choices include:

  • Marimekko textiles and home goods
  • Iittala glassware and ceramics
  • Fazer chocolate and candy
  • Lapland jewelry and reindeer hides

Local Culture and Customs of Finland

Finnish culture is known for being friendly, reserved, and respectful of personal space. Some local customs to be aware of include:

  • Removing shoes before entering a home
  • Shaking hands with everyone present on arrival and departure
  • Being punctual for meetings and appointments

Best Time to Visit Finland

The best time to visit Finland depends on the activities that you are interested in. For winter activities such as skiing, snowmobiling, and Northern Lights viewing, the best time to visit is between December and February. For hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities, the summer months of June to August are ideal. Fall foliage is also a beautiful time to visit, from September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Finland

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Finland:

Do:

  • Respect the personal space of others
  • Follow social etiquette, such as removing shoes before entering a home
  • Sample traditional Finnish cuisine

Don’t:

  • Touch or speak to strangers on public transportation
  • Expect to see the Northern Lights every night during the winter months
  • Bring up religion or politics in conversation

Safety and Health

Finland is a safe and healthy destination for travelers. However, it’s always best to stay informed about potential risks and hazards. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy while in Finland:

Safety in Finland

  • Be cautious when walking on icy sidewalks or roads during the winter months
  • Be aware of your surroundings when using public transportation
  • Be cautious when swimming in lakes or rivers, as water temperatures can be very cold

Health in Finland

  • Finland has high-quality healthcare facilities, including public and private options
  • The tap water is safe to drink in Finland
  • Mosquitoes can be a nuisance during the summer months, so bring insect repellent

Getting to Finland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Finland is well-connected to the rest of Europe and the world, and there are many transportation options available. Here are some of the best ways to get to Finland:

Airports Near Finland

  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport: located in Helsinki, Finland’s largest airport
  • Turku Airport: located in the city of Turku
  • Oulu Airport: located in the city of Oulu

Bus Stations Near Finland

  • Kamppi Bus Station in Helsinki
  • Turku Bus Station in Turku
  • Tampere Bus Station in Tampere

Other Options

  • Train travel: Finland has an extensive rail network that connects major cities and towns.
  • Ferry travel: There are numerous ferry services between Finland and neighboring countries, such as Sweden and Estonia.

How to Get Around in Finland

Finland has an excellent public transportation system, including buses, trains, and trams. Taxis are also readily available in major cities. For exploring more rural areas, renting a car or bike is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Finland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels after visiting Finland, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Stockholm, Sweden
  • Tallinn, Estonia
  • The Norwegian Fjords
  • The Lapland region in Norway or Sweden

Conclusion

Finland is a unique and fascinating destination that offers something for everyone. With its rich culture, history, and natural beauty, it’s no wonder that Finland is quickly becoming one of the top tourist destinations in Europe. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Finland and provided you with all the information you need for a memorable experience. Safe travels!
