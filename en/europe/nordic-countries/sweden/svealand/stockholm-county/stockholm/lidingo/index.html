Introduction to Lidingö

Lidingö is a beautiful island located in Stockholm, Sweden. It is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and rich history. Visitors can access the island via bridges and tunnels from Stockholm. Lidingö has something for every type of traveler, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Lidingö

Lidingö has a lot to offer in terms of places to visit, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carlbergsgården Museum: A museum that showcases Lidingö’s cultural history and art collection.
  • Lidingö Church: A historical church built in the 15th century, with beautiful architecture and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Lidingö Museum: A small museum with exhibits that reflect Lidingö’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Lidingö: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Millesgården: A sculpture garden and museum that features the works of Carl Milles, nestled in beautiful gardens overlooking the island.
  • Ekholmsnäs Nature Reserve: A nature reserve that is home to several hiking and walking trails through picturesque forests and coastline vistas.
  • Långängen-Elfvik Nature Reserve: This nature reserve features bike trails, hiking paths and a shoreline perfect for picnics with your family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lidingö

Lidingö is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The island offers several trails for outdoor enthusiasts, including:

  • The Lidingöloppet Trail: A 30-km trail perfect for running, walking or biking. It is ideal for those who enjoy challenging terrains.
  • Långängens Bokskogar Trail: A trail that passes through serene forests of oaks and beech trees, providing an idyllic and refreshing natural retreat.
  • Aga Trail: This scenic trail offers panoramic views of Stockholm and the archipelago, ideal for hikers seeking a memorable adventure.

Best Things to Do in Lidingö: Activities and Tours

Lidingö has plenty of things to do, including:

  • Kayaking in Långängen-Elfvik Nature Reserve: Rent a kayak and paddle through the calm waters of the reserve.
  • Visit Lidingö Market: Held every Saturday, visit the street market to experience the local food, art and handmade products.
  • Gustavsberg Porcelain Museum: Learn about porcelain manufacturing, and witness the artistic creations of Gustavsberg artists.

Food and Drink Scene of Lidingö

Lidingö’s cuisine pays homage to its Nordic roots. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Raggmunk: Potato pancakes served with lingonberry sauce and crispy bacon.
  • Toast Skagen: Shrimp on toast with a creamy sauce that is a local specialty.
  • Köttbullar: Swedish meatballs served with mashed potatoes, cream gravy, and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Akvavit: A local spirit with a refreshing taste, perfect for pre-or-post dinner drinks.
  • Järnbrännvin: Also known as “Iron liquor,” this potent drink is a traditional and popular spirit of the area.

Where to Stay in Lidingö: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lidingö offers various types of accommodations for travelers:

  • Villa Brevik: Located in a charming location, with a beautiful garden with a sea view.
  • Blue Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms overlooking the sea.
  • Lilla Hotellet: A budget-friendly hotel, perfect for those seeking a practical and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Lidingö: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home unique souvenirs, including:

  • Local Ceramics: Gustavsberg is the best place to find high-quality ceramics, with an extensive range on offer.
  • Moose and reindeer related products: Moose sculptures, reindeer hides, and horn items are among the most popular local products, and they make wonderful gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Lidingö

Swedes are renowned for their cultural richness, with Lidingö being no exception. Here are a few aspects of local customs that visitors should be aware of:

  • Lagom: This is a Swedish concept of ‘just-right,’ meaning to have enough, but not too much or too little.
  • Fika: A coffee and pastry break that’s meant not only to rejuvenate you but to catch up with family, friends or colleagues.

Best Time to Visit Lidingö

The best time to visit Lidingö is during summer, from June to August, when the weather is the warmest, and the days are longer. This is also peak season for the Stockholm archipelago, which makes Lidingö a convenient stopover destination.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lidingö

Here are some points to keep in mind while visiting Lidingö:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and public spaces.
  • Be punctual - Swedes are known for their promptness and appreciate punctuality.
  • Try to speak a little Swedish, locals will appreciate your efforts.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to remove your shoes while entering a house or someone’s flat.
  • Don’t expect that everyone will speak English because not all Swedes are fluent in English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lidingö

Lidingö is a safe destination, but visitors should practice typical precautions to avoid any unfortunate incidents.

• Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas. • Avoid walking alone in secluded areas at night.

Health in Lidingö

Travelers should have proper health insurance to receive medical services in case of an emergency.

Getting to Lidingö: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lidingö is accessible through different public transport options:

Airports Near Lidingö

The nearest airport is Stockholm Arlanda Airport, which is connected by train and bus to Stockholm city center.

Bus Stations Near Lidingö

A bus service runs from central Stockholm to Lidingö, making it a convenient option to get to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also access the island via car, bike or by taking a water taxi from Stockholm.

How to Get Around in Lidingö

Lidingö is a small island, making it easy to get around on foot or bike rentals. There is also a bus service available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Lidingö: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lidingö serves as a convenient base to visit other nearby destinations in Stockholm, including:

  • Vasa Museum: Located in Stockholm, this museum houses a 17th-century ship which is an integral part of Swedish maritime history.
  • Stockholm Old Town: Known as Gamla Stan in Swedish, historic square boasts some iconic landmarks, including the Royal Palace, Stockholm Cathedral and the Nobel Museum.

Conclusion

Lidingö offers a peaceful retreat from the bustling city of Stockholm. Visitors can experience the island’s cultural richness, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures while relaxing in a serene environment. With this guide, you are sure to have an enjoyable trip that you will not forget anytime soon!
