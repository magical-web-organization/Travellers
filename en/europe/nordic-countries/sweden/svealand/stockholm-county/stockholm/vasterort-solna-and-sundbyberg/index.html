A Complete Travel Guide to Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg, Sweden

Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg are three municipalities situated within Stockholm, Sweden’s capital. They are located in Stockholm County, in Svealand, in the Nordic countries, in Europe. These municipalities have gained popularity among tourists due to their cultural and natural attractions, which showcase the best of Sweden’s beauty and heritage.

Introduction to Västerort, Solna and Sundbyberg

These three municipalities offer a great escape from the hustle and bustle of Stockholm city. They boast of peaceful residential areas, parks, and lakes while still providing access to Stockholm’s rich cultural heritage and bustling dining and entertainment scene. Moreover, Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg have excellent transport links for those who want to visit other parts of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

Gustav III’s Pavilion

Located within Haga Park, this historical pavilion served as a private hideaway for King Gustav III. The pavilion is renowned for its stunning neoclassical architecture and beautifully landscaped gardens, making it a perfect spot for a relaxing walk.

Friends Arena

This is Sweden’s national football arena, located in Solna. Besides hosting football matches, it also hosts other sporting events, music concerts, and cultural festivals.

Mall of Scandinavia

The largest mall in Scandinavia, this complex boasts of over 200 shops, restaurants, and entertainment areas, including a cinema, bowling alley, and a play area for children. It is a perfect spot for those looking to do some shopping or spend an afternoon or evening out.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Råstasjön

Located within Solna, Råstasjön is a serene lake surrounded by a beautiful park that is great for picnics, jogging, and bird watching.

Judarskogens Nature Reserve

Located in Sundbyberg, this reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare birds and animals such as the Red Fox and Roe Deer. The reserve has hiking trails that stretch for several kilometers, which is ideal for nature lovers who want to experience a peaceful getaway from the city’s noise and pollution.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Hagaparken

This is a large park located in Solna, which is known for its extensive network of carefully maintained hiking and biking trails. The park is also home to a golf course and a butterfly house.

Järvafältet

Located in Solna, Järvafältet is a vast natural reserve that boasts of biking and hiking routes that stretch for miles. The reserve is home to several lakes, forests, and parks, which provides a serene and peaceful environment to unwind.

Best Things to Do in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg: Activities and Tours

Stockholm Pass

This pass gives you access to some of the most popular tourist attractions in Stockholm, including the Viking History Museum and The Royal Palace.

Walking Tour of Solna

A guided walking tour of Solna will give you an opportunity to see some of the most iconic landmarks in the area, including Ulriksdal Palace and Friends Arena.

Food and Drink Scene of Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Must-Try Dishes

Gravlax

This is a Swedish dish made up of smoked salmon, dill, and a wide range of herbs and spices. It is usually served with toast.

Köttbullar

Köttbullar are Swedish meatballs that are usually served with lingonberries and cream sauce. This dish is available in most Swedish restaurants in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg.

Local Beverages

Glögg

This is a traditional Swedish hot spicy wine that is usually served during the Christmas holiday season.

Snaps

This is a traditional Swedish alcoholic drink that is commonly taken as a shot before or after meals.

Hostel Dalagatan

Located in Vasastan, this hostel is known for its comfortable rooms and friendly staff that cater to the needs of budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Swedish Handicrafts

Swedish handicrafts, such as traditional wood carvings, glassware, and textiles, can be found in most souvenir shops in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg.

Local Culture and Customs of Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Swedish culture is known for its simplicity, transparency, and social welfare policies. The people in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg are friendly and hospitable and take pride in their cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

The best time to visit Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg is between June and August when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in abundance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg are some of the safest municipalities in Stockholm. However, tourists should exercise caution when in crowded places or when traveling at night.

Health in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Sweden has excellent healthcare facilities, and tourists can access these facilities by purchasing health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

Stockholm-Bromma Airport is the nearest airport to Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg, located within a 10-minute drive from Stockholm city center.

Bus Stations Near Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

The City Bus and Stockholm Metro are the primary modes of transport when moving around Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg. Tourists can purchase travel cards at any of the bus or metro stations and use them for all their travel needs.

Other Options

Tourists can also use boats, taxis, and bicycles to move around Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg. Bicycle rental stations are strategically located throughout the municipalities for convenient access.

How to Get Around in Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg

The City Bus and Stockholm Metro are the most convenient and affordable options when moving around Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg. Tourists can also use bicycles or taxis for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stockholm City Center

Stockholm’s city center is just a few minutes’ drive away from Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg. Here you can visit popular tourist attractions such as The Royal Palace and The Vasa Museum.

Drottningholm Palace

This palace is located on Lovön Island, a few kilometers west of Stockholm city. Here tourists can stroll around the palace’s beautiful gardens and visit the palace’s interiors to experience life as it was like for the Swedish Royal family.

Conclusion

Västerort, Solna, and Sundbyberg are some of the most beautiful and serene municipalities in Stockholm. They offer tourists a perfect blend of natural and cultural attractions, which showcase the best of Sweden’s heritage and beauty. A visit to these municipalities will provide a memorable experience that tourists will cherish for a lifetime.
