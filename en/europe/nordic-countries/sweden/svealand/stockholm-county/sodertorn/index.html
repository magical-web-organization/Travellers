Introduction to Södertörn

Located in Stockholm County, in Svealand, in Sweden, in Nordic countries, in Europe, Södertörn is a picturesque and idyllic destination for travelers seeking natural beauty and rich cultural experiences. Comprising eight municipalities, Södertörn is renowned for its pristine beaches, scenic hiking trails, ancient castles, and charming towns. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Södertörn has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the very best of Södertörn, from its museums and historical places to its hiking and biking routes, from its delicious food and drink scene to its traditional souvenirs and local customs. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Södertörn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Södertörn boasts a rich cultural heritage, reflected in its numerous museums and historical places. These are a few must-visit attractions:

  • Torekällberget Open Air Museum: Experience the 19th-century Swedish countryside at this open-air museum, complete with historic houses, working farms, and traditional crafts.

  • Hågelbyparken: This beautiful park features a 19th-century mansion, an extensive garden, and a museum showcasing local history and culture.

  • Taxinge Slott: Visit this magnificent castle dating back to the 14th century and try their famous cakes in the castle’s café.

Natural Attractions of Södertörn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Södertörn is renowned for its stunning nature reserves, tranquil lakes, and scenic hiking trails. These destinations are perfect for both active travelers and nature enthusiasts:

  • Tyresta National Park: Explore this vast forest with over 60 km of hiking trails, plenty of wildlife, and ancient pine trees.

  • Nynäs Nature Reserve: Spend a day by the picturesque Nynäsviken bay, with countless hiking trails, bird watching, and pristine beaches.

  • Vitträsk Nature Reserve: Take a relaxing walk in the scenic forest and experience the charm of Sweden’s countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Södertörn

For outdoor enthusiasts, Södertörn offers a wealth of scenic hiking and biking trails, showcasing the natural beauty and cultural heritage of the region. Some recommended routes include:

  • Sörmlandsleden: Known as “the pearl of hiking trails,” this 627 km trail takes you through some of the most beautiful natural reserves in Södertörn.

  • Roslagsleden: This 190 km trail connects several charming towns and villages and offers breathtaking coastal views.

  • Mörköleden: If you prefer mountain biking, the 28 km Mörköleden trail offers exciting challenges and stunning lake views.

Best Things to Do in Södertörn: Activities and Tours

Södertörn has plenty of exciting activities and tours for everyone, from families with kids to adventure seekers. Here are a few top picks:

  • Kayaking in Nynäsviken: Take a scenic kayak trip through the picturesque Nynäsviken bay, discovering hidden gems and wildlife.

  • Segway Tour in Tullgarn Palace: Explore the magnificent Tullgarn Palace and its beautiful gardens via Segway.

  • Cycling Tour to Dalarö: Take a cycling tour to the charming fishing village Dalarö, soak up the beautiful scenery and enjoy delicious seafood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Södertörn

With so much to see and do in Södertörn, a week-long itinerary is the perfect way to experience everything this beautiful region has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary to inspire your trip:

Day Activity
1 Explore Södertörn by bike, starting from Tullgarn Palace to the fishing village of Dalarö
2 Visit Taxinge Slott and Tyresta National Park
3 Scenic kayaking tour of Nynäsviken, followed by a walk along Nynäs Nature Reserve
4 Experience the charm of Torekällberget Open Air Museum and Hågelbyparken
5 Explore the medieval town of Södertälje and visit the Tom Tits Experiment Science Center
6 Visit the WW2 fortress of Utö island, followed by relaxing in the nearby beach of Gruvbryggan
7 Visit the scenic Stendörren Nature Reserve and have a picnic on the rocks overlooking the coast

Food and Drink Scene of Södertörn

No trip to Södertörn is complete without savoring some of its delicious local dishes and beverages. Here are a few must-try items:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Västerås Gravlax: A cured salmon dish with a distinctive flavor, traditionally served with dill, mustard, and rye bread.

  • Södertälje Kebab: A local version of the famed Turkish kebab, usually served with a creamy garlic sauce and fresh vegetables.

  • Traditional Swedish Meatballs: These flavorful meatballs are a staple of Swedish cuisine, usually served with creamy gravy, mashed potatoes, and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Snaps: A popular Swedish schnapps typically enjoyed with a traditional midsummer celebration or a festive meal.

  • Södertörn Beer: Craft beer enthusiasts will find plenty of excellent local beers, such as the Södertörns Bryggeri’s Pale Ale.

  • Glogg: A spiced wine that’s a favorite during the winter months, particularly around Christmas and New Year.

Where to Stay in Södertörn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Södertörn offers plenty of accommodation options for all budgets, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Hotell Söder: This hotel, situated in the heart of Södertälje, offers comfortable rooms and is the perfect base for exploring the town’s cultural attractions.

  • Järna Hotell: A cozy eco-friendly hotel, perfect for a relaxing and eco-friendly stay, located in the beautiful countryside of Järna.

  • Camping in Tyresta National Park: For a unique camping experience, head to Tyresta National Park where you can set up camp in the picturesque forest.

What to Buy in Södertörn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for traditional souvenirs and local products to bring back home, Södertörn has plenty of options. Here are a few ideas:

  • Swedish Candy: The candy section in any Swedish grocery store is a must-visit for candy lovers. Some popular Swedish candy brands include Ahlgrens, Cloetta, and Marabou.

  • Handicrafts: Torekällberget Open Air Museum and Hågelbyparken offer a range of traditional handicrafts and locally made products

  • Swedish Design: Sweden is known for its sleek and minimalist design aesthetic. Check out the local design shops for unique and Scandinavian designs

Local Culture and Customs of Södertörn

Swedish culture values simplicity, honesty, and egalitarianism. There are a few local customs and practices to keep in mind:

  • Fika: Fika is a Swedish tradition and break to enjoy coffee and pastries with friends and colleagues

  • Lagom: A Swedish word for “just enough,” lagom embodies the Swedish philosophy that moderation and balance lead to a happier life.

  • Midsummer: A festive celebration on the summer solstice is one of the most significant annual events in Sweden, culminating in traditional dancing and feasting.

Best Time to Visit Södertörn

The best time to visit Södertörn is from May to September when the weather is sunny and warm, and the nature reserves are in full bloom. The winter months can be cold, but they also offer skiing and other winter activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Södertörn

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Södertörn:

Do:

  • Bring your camera: Södertörn’s natural beauty and cultural richness are picture-perfect and worthy of capturing your beautiful memories.

  • Tip fairly: Tipping is not expected or mandatory in Sweden, but it is generally appreciated for excellent service. A tip of 10% is considered generous.

  • Recycle: Sweden is famous for its recycling culture, so make sure you dispose of your waste correctly.

Don’t:

  • Get drunk and disorderly: Drinking alcohol is legal in Sweden, but public drunkenness is not tolerated.

  • Assume everyone speaks English: While many Swedes speak English, it’s polite to learn a few basic Swedish phrases and greetings.

  • Litter: Littering is widely considered disrespectful to the environment and locals, so dispose of your trash responsibly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Södertörn

Södertörn is generally a safe place to visit. Still, it’s always essential to exercise common sense travel precautions, such as staying in well-lit areas at night and being aware of your surroundings when traveling alone. Pickpocketing and petty theft can happen in crowded or touristy areas, so keep your valuables secure.

Health in Södertörn

Sweden has a world-class health care system, so you can rest assured that you are in good hands in case of any medical emergencies. For minor health issues, pharmacies are well-stocked with over-the-counter medications and health products.

Getting to Södertörn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Södertörn

  • Arlanda Airport: Located around 50 km away from central Södertälje. It’s the largest airport in Sweden, serving both domestic and international destinations.

  • Bromma Airport: Located in Stockholm, around 35 km from central Södertälje. It’s a smaller airport and mainly serves domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Södertörn

Södertälje is connected to Stockholm’s extensive bus network, making it easy to navigate the region.

Other Options

Train transfers from Stockholm to Södertälje are frequent and affordable. Alternatively, renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the countryside at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Södertörn

Public transportation is widely available in Södertörn. Buses and trains are reliable and relatively inexpensive, making it easy to explore the region’s major tourist spots. Alternatively, renting a bike is a popular option, given the region’s extensive network of bike paths.

Where to Go Next from Södertörn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time after exploring Södertörn, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Stockholm: The vibrant capital city of Sweden is a must-visit destination, with its charming old town, world-class museums, and stunning archipelago.

  • Gripsholm Castle: Located in the nearby town of Mariefred, this beautiful Renaissance palace is surrounded by a scenic park and offers guided tours.

  • Drottningholm Palace: A picturesque palace and UNESCO World Heritage Site located on an island near Stockholm, offering guided tours, scenic gardens, and nightly performances at the palace theater.

Conclusion

Södertörn offers the perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and exciting activities to make it an unforgettable travel experience. From exploring the local museums and hiking the scenic trails to savoring the delicious local dishes and enjoying the festive celebrations, Södertörn has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today and embark on a journey to discover the very best of Södertörn!
