Introduction to Sigtuna

Located in Stockholm County in Svealand, Sigtuna is a charming medieval town in Sweden known for its rich cultural heritage and remarkable natural beauty. Established in the 10th century, it was once the capital of Sweden and today boasts numerous historical landmarks, stunning natural landscapes, and incredibly scenic views. Being only 30 km north of Stockholm, Sigtuna is one of the most accessible destinations in Sweden and is an ideal spot for travelers seeking historical and cultural experiences. This comprehensive travel guide will help you navigate through this beautiful town and make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Sigtuna

Sigtuna offers a plethora of cultural and natural attractions that are worth seeing.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sigtuna Museum - This museum gives visitors an insight into the town’s fascinating history, culture, and customs. Exhibits include artifacts from the Viking era, pictures, maps, and scale models.

St. Mary’s Church - Considered to be the oldest church in the town of Sigtuna, St. Mary’s Church was built in the 13th century and showcases some of the best medieval architecture in Sweden.

Skokloster Castle - This magnificent castle is located on the outskirts of Sigtuna and presents one of the finest examples of Baroque architecture in the country. The castle houses a vast collection of art, antique furniture, and weaponry from the 16th to 18th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Sigtuna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Malaren - Lake Malaren is the third-largest lake in Sweden and runs through the town of Sigtuna. The lake provides the perfect location for fishing, kayaking, and boating while enjoying the stunning views of the surrounding nature.

Sigtuna Boardwalk - Take a leisurely walk along the boardwalk and enjoy the picturesque views of the lake, park, and an array of other sights in the town.

Sigtuna Hills - Explore the lush countryside and wander through the scenic hills and surrounding forest that provide breathtaking views of the townscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sigtuna

Sigtuna is blessed with splendid bike trails and hiking routes that offer visitors an opportunity to explore the town’s countryside on foot or bike.

Skoklosterleden - This 26 km hiking trail takes hikers through the forest, offering stunning views and beautiful bird-watching opportunities.

Sigtuna Parish Walkabout - A 40 km walking trail that takes you through eight historical churches and burial grounds that showcase the town’s deep cultural heritage.

Best Things to Do in Sigtuna: Activities and Tours

Sigtuna Christmas Market - Held annually in the heart of the town, this is a great event to enjoy some delicious food, shop for crafts, and experience the festive spirit.

Viking Market - Held in June at Sigtuna Museum, this event is not to be missed. Travel back in time and experience Viking culture with food, crafts, and live performances.

Sigtuna Walking Tour - Join a walking tour to learn more about the town’s history and culture from an expert guide who will take you through various historical landmarks and sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Sigtuna

Sigtuna’s food and drink scene offer travelers some fantastic local and traditional cuisine to try and enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

Saffron Pancakes - served with whipped cream and lingonberry jam, this dish is a classic Swedish dessert.

Meatballs - served with potato, gravy, and lingonberry jam, Swedish meatballs are a must-try.

Prinsesstarta - This iconic Swedish cake is a bright green-colored cake with a fluffy filling of whipped cream, custard, and jam.

Local Beverages

Aquavit - This traditional Swedish distilled spirit is typically flavored with a variety of spices, including fennel and caraway seeds.

Glögg - A traditional hot spiced wine served during Christmas time, glögg is a winter favorite in Sweden.

Where to Stay in Sigtuna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sigtuna has numerous hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

Hotell Kristina - a luxurious hotel in the center of Sigtuna with a spa and a restaurant that serves delicious Swedish and international cuisine

Sigtuna Stadshotell – a boutique hotel in the old town of Sigtuna, with a classy atmosphere and a restaurant that serves traditional Swedish cuisine.

Connect Arlanda - an economically priced hotel with modern architecture and clean rooms.

What to Buy in Sigtuna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts - Sigtuna is known for its wide range of handicrafts available in the local markets. These range from handmade ceramics, silverware, and jewelry, to textiles and knitwear sold by local artisans.

Candy and Chocolate - Sweden is renowned for its candy and chocolate. Some popular sweets to bring home include chocolate balls, salty liquorice, and marzipan.

Local Culture and Customs of Sigtuna

Sigtuna is a culturally rich town with deep-seated traditions and customs that visitors should be aware of.

Midsummer Festival - Midsummer is the most important festival in Sweden, traditionally celebrated between June 20th – 25th. It is a time to celebrate Swedish culture with food, drinks, and live music.

Fika - Fika is a Swedish cultural institution that means taking a coffee break accompanied by sweet treats like a cinnamon roll or traditional Swedish pastry like a cardamon bun.

Best Time to Visit Sigtuna

The best time to visit Sigtuna is from May to September when the weather is warm. The snow-capped winter season can also be pleasant for those who enjoy winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sigtuna

Do:

  • Respect the town’s historical landmarks and cultural heritage.
  • Behave politely and respectfully to the locals.
  • Try the traditional Swedish cuisine.
  • Take a walking tour of the town with a local guide.

Don’t:

  • Litter around the town or disturb the natural reserves.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs of private residences without permission.
  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your vehicle.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sigtuna

Sigtuna is a safe town for visitors to explore. The crime rate is low and the locals are friendly and helpful. Despite this, one should take precautions like not leaving valuable items in plain sight, especially in vehicles.

Health in Sigtuna

The health care system in Sweden is of high quality, and visitors are encouraged to obtain travel insurance before visiting Sigtuna.

Getting to Sigtuna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sigtuna

Sigtuna is just 15 minutes away from Stockholm-Arlanda Airport, one of the largest and most accessible airports in Sweden.

Bus Stations Near Sigtuna

The Sigtuna Bus Station connects the town to other major cities and towns in Sweden.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Stockholm’s Central Station to Märsta and then take a local bus to Sigtuna.

How to Get Around in Sigtuna

Sigtuna is a small town, and many of the popular attractions are within walking distance. Bicycles are also a great option for getting around, and visitors can rent them from several shops in the town.

Where to Go Next from Sigtuna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located close to Sigtuna, here are some nearby destinations that can be visited:

Stockholm - The capital city of Sweden is only 30 km away from Sigtuna and can be reached via bus or train. Stockholm is renowned for its rich history, cultural landmarks, and contemporary lifestyle.

Uppsala – Only 35 km north of Sigtuna, this grand university town is famous for its historical buildings, the Uppsala Cathedral, botanical garden, and excellent shopping opportunities.

Conclusion

Sigtuna provides visitors with a perfect blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. The town boasts a range of cultural landmarks, beautiful natural reserves, and authentic gastronomic experiences. No matter what type of traveler you are, Sigtuna has something to offer to everyone. Don’t miss out on exploring this fantastic Swedish destination.
