Introduction to Norrtälje

Nestled along the northern coast of Stockholm County, Norrtälje is a charming town that is often overlooked by travelers in favor of its larger neighbors. However, visitors to Norrtälje are in for a treat with its rich cultural history, stunning natural landscapes, and picturesque old town.

Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, going on outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying the local food and drink scene, Norrtälje has something for every type of traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll dive into the best places to visit, things to do, and tips for your trip to Norrtälje.

List of Places to Visit in Norrtälje

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Norrtälje Konsthall - This contemporary art gallery showcases the works of both local and international artists.
  • Norrtälje Museum - Housed in a historic building, the museum features exhibits on the town’s history, including its role in the timber trade.
  • Pythagoras Industrial Museum - Learn about the industrial history of Norrtälje and see antique machinery and tools in action.
  • Tullporten - This historic gate is one of the most iconic symbols of Norrtälje’s history. Take a stroll around town and spot the gate in its original location.
  • Norrtälje Church - One of the largest wooden churches in Sweden, Norrtälje Church dates back to the 14th century and is still in use.

Natural Attractions of Norrtälje: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Roslagsleden Trail - This hiking trail spans 190 km of beautiful forests, lakes, and coastline in the Roslagen region.
  • Söderarm lighthouse - Take a boat trip to this stunning lighthouse on an island in the Stockholm archipelago.
  • Grisslehamn - This seaside village boasts beautiful beaches, fishing trips, and views of the Baltic Sea.
  • Lohärad Nature Reserve - This preserve is home to several species of birds and wildlife and is a great spot for hiking and picnics.
  • Norrtälje River - Kayak or canoe along this scenic river that meanders through the town and offers a peaceful escape from city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Norrtälje

Norrtälje offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. One of the most popular trails is the Roslagsleden Trail, which spans 190 km through forests, lakes, and coastline in the Roslagen region. Other notable routes include:

  • Kanonrundan - This 8 km trail takes you through rolling hills and forests and offers stunning views of Norrtälje and its surroundings.
  • Lohäradsleden - This 15 km trail takes you through the Lohärad Nature Reserve, where you can spot wildlife and enjoy a peaceful picnic.
  • Sundsta-Monoleden - This 8 km trail takes you through the Sundsta Nature Reserve and offers stunning lake views and sandy beaches.

Best Things to Do in Norrtälje: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour in the Stockholm Archipelago - Take a boat tour from Norrtälje to the Stockholm archipelago and explore the many islands, fishing villages, and lighthouses along the way.
  • Canoeing or Kayaking in Norrtälje River - Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the peaceful Norrtälje River, which winds its way through town and offers a unique perspective on the town.
  • Fishing Trips - Norrtälje is known for its great fishing opportunities, from fly fishing in rivers to deep sea fishing in the Baltic Sea.
  • Local Food and Drink Tours - Take a tour of Norrtälje’s many restaurants, cafes, and breweries and sample the town’s delicious local food and drink offerings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Norrtälje

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Norrtälje:

Day Activity
1 Explore Norrtälje’s historic town center and visit Tullporten and Norrtälje Church.
2 Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the Norrtälje River.
3 Take a day trip to the Stockholm archipelago and visit Söderarm lighthouse or other islands.
4 Go on a fishing trip or take a hiking trail in the nearby Lohärad Nature Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Norrtälje

Must-Try Dishes

Norrtälje’s food scene is heavily influenced by its coastal location, and seafood is a staple on menus throughout the town. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Gravlax - This traditional Swedish dish features salmon that has been cured with salt, sugar, and dill. It is typically served with potatoes and a mustard sauce.
  • Pickled Herring - Another traditional Swedish dish, pickled herring is often served with crispbread, boiled potatoes, and sour cream.
  • Shrimp Sandwich - A classic Swedish sandwich, the shrimp sandwich includes salad, boiled eggs, and fresh shrimp piled high on a slice of bread.

Local Beverages

Norrtälje is home to several breweries and distilleries, producing a range of local beers, ciders, and spirits. Some of the most popular local beverages include:

  • Norrtelje Brenneri - This distillery produces a variety of spirits, including vodka, gin, and whiskey, using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Roslagens Brygghus - This brewery produces a range of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

Where to Stay in Norrtälje: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Norrtälje offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular choices include:

  • Stadshotellet - This historic hotel is located in the heart of Norrtälje and offers elegant rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Swedish dishes.
  • Roslagsvillan - This cozy guesthouse is located on the outskirts of town and offers comfortable rooms and access to a beautiful garden.
  • Herrängens Gårdshotell - This tranquil hotel is situated in the countryside outside Norrtälje and features luxurious rooms and a spa.

What to Buy in Norrtälje: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Norrtälje can find a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Handmade Crafts - Local artisans in Norrtälje produce a variety of handmade crafts, such as pottery, woodcarvings, and textiles.
  • Smoked Fish - Norrtälje is known for its smoked fish, which is typically sold at fishmongers and specialty shops throughout town.
  • Craft Beer - Pick up a pack of locally brewed craft beer as a souvenir or gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Norrtälje

Swedish culture is a unique combination of traditional and modern customs. Visitors to Norrtälje should expect to encounter friendly and welcoming locals who take pride in their town’s history and traditions. Here are a few tips to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Tipping - Tipping is not expected in Sweden, as service charges are typically included in restaurant bills. However, rounding up the bill to the nearest krona is appreciated.
  • Lagom - This Swedish term translates to “just enough” or “moderate” and is a common cultural value in Sweden. Visitors should strive to be considerate of others and not draw too much attention to themselves.
  • Outdoor Etiquette - Sweden has a strong tradition of spending time outdoors, and visitors should respect the natural environment and follow the “allemansrätt” (right of public access) laws when hiking or camping in the countryside.

Best Time to Visit Norrtälje

Norrtälje is best visited between May and September when the weather is mild and the days are long. During this time, visitors can enjoy the many outdoor activities and events, such as the Norrtälje Jazz Festival and the Norrtälje Food Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Norrtälje

Do:

  • Do try local seafood and beer
  • Do explore the natural landscapes of the Roslagen region
  • Do respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public spaces
  • Don’t be surprised if many stores and restaurants close early

Safety and Health

Safety in Norrtälje

Norrtälje is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take care to avoid leaving valuables unattended and stay aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Norrtälje

Sweden has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can access quality medical care if needed. The tap water in Norrtälje is safe to drink, and it is recommended to bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against the sun and insects during outdoor activities.

Getting to Norrtälje: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Norrtälje

  • Stockholm Arlanda Airport - This international airport is the closest to Norrtälje and is located about 50 km south of the town.

Bus Stations Near Norrtälje

  • Norrtälje Bus Station - This bus station is located in the center of town and offers connections to cities throughout the Stockholm region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car from Stockholm Arlanda Airport or other locations in Stockholm and drive to Norrtälje.
  • Train - Visitors can take a train from Stockholm to Rimbo or Hallstavik and then transfer to a local bus or taxi to Norrtälje.

How to Get Around in Norrtälje

Norrtälje is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use local buses for longer distances or rent a car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Norrtälje: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stockholm - Sweden’s capital city is just a short drive or train ride away from Norrtälje and offers numerous cultural attractions, museums, and restaurants.
  • Uppsala - This historic university town is located just 50 km north of Norrtälje and boasts a stunning cathedral and beautiful botanical gardens.
  • Vaxholm - Located in the Stockholm archipelago, Vaxholm is a charming town with a well-preserved old town and castle.

Conclusion

Norrtälje may be a small town, but it offers a rich history and stunning natural landscapes that are often overlooked by travelers. From hiking and biking to local food and drink tours, there are plenty of activities to keep visitors entertained for days. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to add Norrtälje to your travel itinerary.
