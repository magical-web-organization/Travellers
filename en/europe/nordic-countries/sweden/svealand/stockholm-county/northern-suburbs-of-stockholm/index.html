Introduction to Northern suburbs of Stockholm

Located in the Stockholm County, in the Svealand region of Sweden, the Northern suburbs of Stockholm offer visitors a myriad of things to do and see. From natural wonders to rich cultural experiences, this region of Sweden truly has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Northern suburbs of Stockholm

If you’re looking for things to do in the Northern suburbs of Stockholm, there are no shortage of options. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northern suburbs of Stockholm is home to several museums and historical places that are well worth a visit. Here are a few highlights:

  • Bergianska Trädgården - This beautiful botanical garden is located near Stockholm University and features over 9,000 plant species from all over the world.
  • Ulriksdal Palace - This palace was built in the early 1600s and was the home of several Swedish royals. Today, you can take a tour of the palace and its stunning gardens.
  • Drottningholm Palace - This palace is another fantastic example of Swedish baroque architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Take a tour of the palace and grounds, or catch a performance at the Drottningholm Palace Theatre.

Natural Attractions of Northern suburbs of Stockholm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Northern suburbs of Stockholm is simply breathtaking. Here are some of the best natural attractions for you to explore:

  • Rösjöbaden - This beautiful beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or just enjoying a picnic.
  • Järvafältet Nature Reserve - This nature reserve offers over 22 hectares of open grasslands, forests, and lakes. There are several hiking and biking trails to explore, and you might even spot some wildlife along the way.
  • Nationalstadsparken - This park is the perfect place to go for a long walk, jog or bike ride while enjoying the stunning views of Lake Brunnsviken.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern suburbs of Stockholm

The Northern suburbs of Stockholm are a paradise for nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Järvafältet Nature Reserve Loop - This 16 km trail passes through the beautiful Järvafältet Nature Reserve and is perfect for experienced hikers or bikers.
  • Kungsleden Trail - This 450 km trail is a popular destination for hikers and offers stunning views of the Swedish wilderness.
  • Danderyd to Edsviken Trail - This trail runs along the waterfront, and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Northern suburbs of Stockholm: Activities and Tours

When it comes to fun activities and tours, Northern suburbs of Stockholm has plenty to offer. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Kayaking the Archipelago - Get up close and personal with the stunning Stockholm Archipelago by taking a guided kayaking tour.
  • Gamla Stan Walking Tour - Take a guided walking tour of the Gamla Stan, the old town that features cobblestone streets, colorful buildings and a rich history.
  • Vasa Museum Tour - Explore the impressive Vasa Museum and marvel at the intricate details of this well-preserved ship from the 17th century.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern suburbs of Stockholm

Swedish cuisine is famous for its fresh and delicious seafood dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gravlax - Swedish-cured salmon, served with dill and mustard sauce.
  • Köttbullar - Meatballs served with gravy, mashed potatoes, and lingonberry sauce.
  • Smörgåsbord - This traditional Swedish buffet consists of many small dishes such as herring, salmon, potatoes, and bread.

Local Beverages

  • Glögg - A warm, spiced wine that’s perfect for cold winter months.
  • Swedish Schnapps - A strong alcohol made from potatoes and grain, the perfect accompaniment to a Swedish meal.
  • Julmust - A traditional Swedish soft drink consumed during Christmas time.

Where to Stay in Northern suburbs of Stockholm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Scandic Järva Krog - This hotel located in Solna is perfect for families or business travelers and offers free parking.
  • Forenom Aparthotel Stockholm Barkarby - This is a great choice for travelers looking for more space and privacy. Located in Järfälla, these apartments offer kitchens and free WiFi.
  • Good Morning+ Hägersten - This hotel is located near the Stockholmsmassan exhibition centre and is perfect for anyone attending an event in the area.

What to Buy in Northern suburbs of Stockholm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift, here are a few local products worth checking out:

  • Swedish Clogs - These shoes are not only a fashion statement but also practical, durable, and comfortable.
  • Swedish Cheese - Swedish cheese such as Västerbotten, Herrgårdsost, and Prästost make for the perfect edible souvenir.
  • Swedish Chocolates - Swedish chocolates such as Marabou, Fazer, and Plopp are perfect for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern suburbs of Stockholm

Swedish society values equality, and guests are expected to treat others kindly and respectfully. It is also customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a public building. Additionally, smoking in public places is prohibited in Sweden.

Best Time to Visit Northern suburbs of Stockholm

The best time to visit the Northern suburbs of Stockholm is in the summer months, between June and August. This is when the weather is the mildest and the natural beauty of the region is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern suburbs of Stockholm

Here are a few things visitors should keep in mind while traveling to the Northern suburbs of Stockholm:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine - Swedish food is delicious; be sure to try as much as possible during your stay!
  • Do bring comfortable shoes - You’ll likely be doing a lot of walking, so make sure you have footwear that’s comfortable for long periods of time.
  • Do take advantage of the public transportation - It’s easy to navigate, and the Stockholm metro system is considered one of the best in the world.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip - While it’s not mandatory, it’s customary to leave a 10% tip at restaurants and cafes.
  • Don’t be late - Swedes value punctuality, so be on time for any scheduled activities or reservations.
  • Don’t take photos of strangers without permission - It’s always best to ask; Swedes value privacy and respect for others.

Safety and Health

The Northern suburbs of Stockholm are generally a safe destination for travelers. However, as with any place, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety.

Safety in Northern suburbs of Stockholm

While the crime rate in Northern suburbs of Stockholm is relatively low, it’s still essential to take precautions such as keeping your valuables in a safe place and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Northern suburbs of Stockholm

Sweden has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors are entitled to emergency care in the event of an accident or illness. It’s a good idea to have comprehensive travel insurance for the duration of your stay.

Getting to Northern suburbs of Stockholm: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to the Northern suburbs of Stockholm, including air travel, bus, and car.

Airports Near Northern suburbs of Stockholm

The closest airport to the Northern suburbs of Stockholm is Stockholm-Arlanda Airport, located approximately 35 km away. Alternatively, you can fly into Bromma Stockholm Airport, which is located only 11 km from Stockholm city center.

Bus Stations Near Northern suburbs of Stockholm

The Northern suburbs of Stockholm are well-connected by bus; there are several bus stations throughout the region. Some of the most popular include the Stockholm Bus Terminal and the Stockholm Cityterminalen.

Other Options

You can also travel to the Northern suburbs of Stockholm by train or rental car.

How to Get Around in Northern suburbs of Stockholm

The easiest way to get around the Northern suburbs of Stockholm is by public transportation. The Stockholm metro system is efficient, clean, and affordable. Alternatively, you can rent a bike or car, or take a taxi or Uber.

Where to Go Next from Northern suburbs of Stockholm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time and want to explore more of Sweden, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Uppsala - This charming university town features stunning architecture and a rich history.
  • Drottningholm Palace - This palace is another fantastic example of Swedish baroque architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Take a tour of the palace and grounds, or catch a performance at the Drottningholm Palace Theatre.
  • Stockholm City Center - Stockholm has a rich history and vibrant nightlife. You can wander its streets, enjoy dinner in one of its many restaurants, and dance the night away in one of its many clubs and bars.

Conclusion

The Northern suburbs of Stockholm are truly a hidden gem, from its beautiful natural landscape to its rich cultural experiences. With so much to see and do, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring Sweden.
