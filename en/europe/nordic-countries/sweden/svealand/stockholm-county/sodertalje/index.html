Introduction to Södertälje

Located in Stockholm County, Södertälje is a small town in Svealand, Sweden. It is known for its scenic beauty, cultural significance, and burgeoning food and drink scene. Södertälje is only a 45-minute drive from Stockholm and is easily accessible by road and rail.

List of Places to Visit in Södertälje

Södertälje offers visitors a variety of places to explore, from culturally significant attractions to natural wonders. Here is a list of the must-visit places in Södertälje:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tom Tits Experiment: This science center is perfect for families with children. It has more than 600 interactive exhibits and is great for hands-on learning.
  • Södertälje Town Museum: This museum has collections of local artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s history from the Viking era to the present.
  • Torekällberget Open-Air Museum: This outdoor museum showcases life in rural Sweden from the 18th to the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Södertälje: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lina Sandell’s Park: This beautiful park is named after the famous Swedish hymn writer Lina Sandell. It has walking paths around a pond, a playground, and a quaint cafe.
  • Ytterjärna Naturreservat: This nature reserve offers hiking and biking trails, bird watching, and picturesque views of the countryside.
  • Marenviken: This lakeside park is great for swimming, picnicking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Södertälje

Södertälje offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Södertälje:

  • Skottvångs Grufva: This is a beautiful hiking trail situated in a nature reserve just outside Södertälje. It offers stunning views of the countryside and is perfect for a day’s hike.
  • Rönninge Bygdegårdsled: This trail is great for biking. It’s about 10 km long and takes you through idyllic countryside and forests.
  • Sörmlandsleden: This long-distance trail connects Södertälje with other towns and cities in Sörmland. It offers something for everyone, from rugged wilderness to charming villages.

Best Things to Do in Södertälje: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours that you can enjoy in Södertälje:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour along the beautiful Södertälje Canal and experience the town’s waterways.
  • Horse Riding: Experience Sweden’s countryside on horseback with one of the town’s many horseback riding tours.
  • Fishing: Anglers can enjoy fishing in the lakes and streams around Södertälje.

Here’s an itinerary to help you plan a week-long trip to Södertälje:

Food and Drink Scene of Södertälje

Södertälje offers visitors a diverse range of local and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Räkmacka: A traditional shrimp sandwich served with mayonnaise, lemon, and lettuce.
  • Renstek: A popular dish made with reindeer meat and served with potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages:

  • Södermanland’s Oldest Brewery: Located in Södertälje, this brewery produces a variety of craft beer.
  • Äppelmust: A refreshing non-alcoholic cider made from locally sourced apples.

Where to Stay in Södertälje: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Södertälje offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some options:

  • Quality Hotel Park Södertälje City: This modern hotel is centrally located and offers guests a range of amenities, including an on-site restaurant, a gym, and a sauna.
  • Hotell Torpa Pensionat: This historic guesthouse is situated in a peaceful location near Lake Maren.
  • STF Södertälje Hostel: This affordable hostel is located near the town’s transportation hub and is perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Södertälje: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring back a piece of Södertälje, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to take home with you:

  • Swedish candy: Södertälje has a rich candy making heritage and there are plenty of options to choose from.
  • Craft Beer: Södermanland’s Oldest Brewery makes a variety of delicious craft beers that you can take back home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Södertälje

The locals in Södertälje are friendly and welcoming. Here are some things to keep in mind while exploring the town:

  • Respect for the Environment: Swedes are very eco-conscious and make a conscious effort to care for the environment.
  • Quiet Enjoyment: Södertälje residents enjoy peace and quiet and observe “quiet enjoyment” in public places.

Best Time to Visit Södertälje

The best time to visit Södertälje is between June and August when the weather is mild and the days are long. This is also the time when most festivals and events are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Södertälje

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Södertälje:

Do:

  • Take Off Your Shoes: It is customary to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home in Sweden.
  • Embrace the Outdoors: Swedes take their outdoor activities seriously, so don’t be afraid to get out and enjoy nature.

Don’t:

  • Be Late: Swedes are punctual and expect others to be on time as well.
  • Be Loud in Public Places: Södertälje residents enjoy peace and quiet and observe “quiet enjoyment” in public places.

Safety and Health

Södertälje is a very safe town, but like anywhere else, there are a few things to keep in mind for your safety and health.

Safety in Södertälje

  • Mind Your Belongings: Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings.
  • Emergency Services: In case of an emergency, dial 112 for police, ambulance, or fire services.

Health in Södertälje

  • Insurance: Make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.
  • Medical Emergencies: Dial 1177 for non-emergency medical care and 112 for emergencies.

Getting to Södertälje: Transportation Options and Logistics

Södertälje is easily accessible by road and rail. Here are some transportation options:

  • Stockholm Arlanda Airport: The nearest international airport to Södertälje is Stockholm Arlanda Airport, which is located about an hour away from the town.
  • Train: Södertälje has several train stations that are well-connected to other towns and cities in Sweden.

How to Get Around in Södertälje

Södertälje is a walkable town and most attractions are located within a short distance of each other. Cycling and public transport are also good options.

Where to Go Next from Södertälje: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Södertälje:

  • Stockholm: The Swedish capital is only a 45-minute drive from Södertälje and offers visitors a variety of museums, shopping, and dining options.
  • Trosa: This picturesque town is situated on the shores of the Baltic Sea and offers visitors a chance to enjoy the Swedish countryside.

Conclusion

Södertälje is a hidden gem in the heart of Sweden. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or history, this town has something for everyone. Plan your trip, explore the town’s many attractions, and enjoy the beautiful Swedish countryside.
