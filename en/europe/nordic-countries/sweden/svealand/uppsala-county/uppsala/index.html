Introduction to Uppsala

Nestled in the heart of Svealand in Sweden, the city of Uppsala is a charming mix of tradition, culture, and adventure. Known for its picturesque streets, fascinating history, and stunning natural landscapes, Uppsala is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Sweden.

List of Places to Visit in Uppsala

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for new experiences, there is no shortage of things to do in Uppsala. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to experience the rich cultural heritage of Uppsala is by visiting its museums and historical landmarks. Here are a few must-see sites to consider:

  • Uppsala Cathedral: One of the oldest and most magnificent cathedrals in Sweden, Uppsala Cathedral is a masterpiece of Gothic architecture that dominates the city skyline. Explore its stunning interior and learn about its rich history dating back to the 13th century.
  • Vasa Museum: Home to a collection of historic Viking ships, the Vasa Museum gives visitors a glimpse into Sweden’s seafaring past and the archeological excavation and preservation of the salvaged ship Vasa.
  • Linnaeus Museum: Located in the former home of the famous naturalist Carl Linnaeus, the Linnaeus Museum is a must-visit for science enthusiasts. Explore Linnaeus’s laboratory and study the specimens he collected to gain insight into his scientific discoveries.
  • Uppsala Castle: Perched atop a hill overlooking the city, Uppsala Castle is a majestic landmark that dates back to the 16th century. Today, the castle houses an art museum showcasing works by local artists and a series of interactive exhibits highlighting the castle’s history.

Natural Attractions of Uppsala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Uppsala’s lush green hills and sparkling lakes provide plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation and exploration. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in the area:

  • Fyris River: The Fyris River is a picturesque waterway that flows through the heart of Uppsala. Take a stroll along the riverbanks or rent a kayak to explore its tranquil waters.
  • Gamla Uppsala: The historic site of Gamla Uppsala is a must-visit for anyone interested in Viking history and mythology. Walk among the towering burial mounds and learn about the ancient rituals and customs of the people who once lived in the area.
  • Hågadalen Nature Reserve: The Hågadalen Nature Reserve is a massive park on the outskirts of Uppsala that boasts miles of trails winding through beautiful forests and rolling hills. Pack a picnic and spend a relaxing afternoon exploring the natural wonderland.
  • Uppsala Botanical Garden: With over 9,000 plant species and a stunning greenhouse complex, the Uppsala Botanical Garden is an essential stop for nature lovers. Walk through the garden’s carefully curated outdoor displays and immerse yourself in the beauty and diversity of plant life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uppsala

Uppsala offers plenty of opportunities for those who prefer to get around on two feet or two wheels. Here are a few of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Håga Trail: This 9-mile trail winds through the Hågadalen Nature Reserve and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and wildlife.
  • Uppsala Riverside Walk: This scenic trail runs along the banks of the Fyris River and offers a relaxing way to explore the city’s historic architecture and natural beauty.
  • Lake Mälaren Loop: For experienced cyclists looking for a challenge, the 60-mile Lake Mälaren Loop offers breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Uppsala: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking to learn more about local history and culture or simply want to have some fun, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained in Uppsala. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Segway Tour: Take a Segway tour of Uppsala and explore the city’s most famous landmarks and attractions with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Paddle Boarding: Rent a paddleboard and explore the tranquil waters of the Fyris River at your own pace.
  • Swedish Fika Tour: Experience Swedish culture and cuisine on a guided Fika Tour where you’ll enjoy local coffee and pastries with an expert guide in some of the city’s most charming cafes.
  • Uppsala Ghost Tour: Explore the darker side of Uppsala’s history on a ghost tour that takes visitors to some of the city’s most haunted locations.

Food and Drink Scene of Uppsala

Swedish cuisine is a unique blend of traditional flavors and modern innovations, and Uppsala’s food and drink scene reflects this mix. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your trip:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kroppkakor: These traditional Swedish dumplings are made with mashed potatoes and filled with meat, onion, and other savory seasonings.
  • Kanelbullar: A popular Swedish pastry, kanelbullar are sweet and savory cinnamon rolls that are perfect for breakfast or a midday snack.
  • Smörgåsbord: A classic Swedish meal, the smörgåsbord is a buffet-style spread of traditional dishes like pickled herring, meatballs, and various salads.

Local Beverages

  • Glögg: This sweet, spiced mulled wine is a popular wintertime beverage in Sweden, served hot and garnished with raisins and almonds.
  • Ostkaka: A traditional Swedish dessert, ostkaka is a rich, creamy cheesecake often served with lingonberry sauce.
  • Nubbe: A Swedish schnapps served with all traditional meals in Sweden, nubbe is flavoured with caraway, fennel or aniseed.

What to Buy in Uppsala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Uppsala home with you by picking up some unique souvenirs and locally produced products. Here are some ideas to get you started:

  • Handwoven Textiles: Browse the city’s many boutique shops and craft fairs to find one-of-a-kind handwoven textiles and clothing.
  • Swedish Art and Design: From handmade ceramics and glassware to minimalist furniture, Sweden is known for its innovative and stylish design aesthetic.
  • Local Snacks: Sample some of Uppsala’s local delicacies like chocolate-covered licorice and other sweets or local smoked salmon.

Local Culture and Customs of Uppsala

Swedish culture is characterized by a deep respect for nature, an emphasis on egalitarianism and social welfare, and a love of design and innovation. When visiting Uppsala, there are a few local customs to keep in mind:

  • Be on time: Punctuality is highly valued in Sweden, so make sure to arrive on time for appointments or meetings.
  • Remove your shoes: It’s common practice in Swedish households to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home, so be sure to follow this custom.
  • Enjoy Fika: Swedish people love their coffee breaks, or fika in Swedish, so take the time to indulge in this ritual by enjoying coffee and pastries in one of Uppsala’s charming cafes.

Best Time to Visit Uppsala

Uppsala is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Here is what you can expect during each season:

  • Spring: Between March and May, Uppsala begins to thaw after winter, and the vibrant natural landscapes explode with colorful blooms. Temperatures start to warm up, making this season ideal for outdoor activities.
  • Summer: From June to August, Uppsala is at its most vibrant and lively. The days are long, and the weather is warm, making it the perfect time to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.
  • Fall: September and October offer mild temperatures and stunning fall foliage, perfect for hiking and exploring the city’s many green spaces.
  • Winter: From November to February, Uppsala is shrouded in a blanket of snow, and winter sports like skiing and ice skating become popular activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uppsala

When visiting Uppsala, there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure you have a pleasant trip:

Do:

  • Respect nature: Uppsala’s natural landscapes are essential to the city’s identity and culture, so make sure to treat them with respect and care.
  • Be polite: Swedish people are known for their polite and respectful behavior, so make sure to follow their example by being courteous to others in public places.
  • Enjoy Swedish cuisine: Sample local delicacies like kanelbullar and smörgåsbord to get a taste of Sweden’s rich culinary heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Swedish people take great pride in keeping their streets and natural landscapes clean, so make sure to dispose of your waste responsibly.
  • Be too loud: Swedish people tend to be reserved and quiet, so try to keep your voice down in public places and respect the peaceful atmosphere of the city.
  • Be demanding: Swedish people place great value on personal space and privacy, so make sure to be respectful in your interactions with locals and service providers.

Safety and Health

Uppsala is a safe and healthy destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take basic precautions when visiting a new city. Here’s what you should keep in mind:

Safety in Uppsala

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any city, Uppsala has its share of petty crime. Pay attention to your belongings and avoid wandering through unlit areas alone at night.
  • Watch out for bikes: Biking is a popular mode of transportation in Uppsala. Be aware of bikers on shared roadways and sidewalks to avoid accidents.

Health in Uppsala

  • Stay hydrated: Whether you’re hiking through the city’s natural landscapes or exploring its historic sites, it’s essential to stay hydrated. Make sure to carry a water bottle with you at all times.
  • Take precautions against ticks: Ticks are common in Uppsala during the summer months, so make sure to wear long pants and use bug spray when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Uppsala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Uppsala is easily accessible by a variety of transportation modes, including:

  • Air: The closest airport to Uppsala is Stockholm Arlanda Airport, located approximately 20 miles south of the city. From there, you can take a train or taxi directly to Uppsala.
  • Train: Uppsala has regular train service to and from Stockholm and other major Swedish cities.
  • Bus: Uppsala’s bus station, located in the city center, offers regular service to nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Uppsala

Uppsala’s compact size and well-connected transportation network make it easy to get around. Here are a few ways to explore the city:

  • Walking: Many of Uppsala’s top attractions are within easy walking distance of each other, making it the perfect mode of transportation for exploring the city center.
  • Biking: With many bike rental shops located throughout the city, renting a bike is a great way to explore Uppsala’s many bike paths and scenic routes.
  • Public Transit: Uppsala’s public transit system is reliable and affordable, with frequent bus and train service between different areas of the city.

Where to Go Next from Uppsala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Uppsala’s central location makes it an ideal base for exploring other parts of Sweden. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Stockholm: The Swedish capital is located just over an hour away from Uppsala by train, making it a perfect day trip destination.
  • Sigtuna: This charming medieval town is located just a short drive or bus ride from Uppsala’s city center, offering visitors a glimpse into Swedish history and culture.
  • Dalarna: Known for its rustic charm and natural beauty, the region of Dalarna is located just a few hours’ drive from Uppsala and is worth considering for a longer excursion.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring Uppsala’s rich cultural heritage, immersing yourself in its stunning natural landscapes, or simply enjoying local cuisine and experiences, this comprehensive travel guide has everything you need to plan the perfect trip to Uppsala. With its unique blend of tradition, culture, and adventure, Uppsala is guaranteed to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits.
