Introduction to Uppsala countryside

Located in the heart of Svealand, Uppsala countryside is a region of outstanding natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse outdoor adventures. From scenic hiking trails and Nordic ski slopes to historic museums and world-class universities, there’s plenty to explore and enjoy in this idyllic rural landscape.

List of Places to Visit in Uppsala countryside

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Uppsala is home to several world-class museums that showcase the area’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Linnaeus Museum

Step back in time and discover the life and work of the legendary botanist Carl Linnaeus, the “father of modern taxonomy.” This fascinating museum features interactive exhibits, rare artifacts, and stunning botanical gardens.

Upplandsmuseet

Explore the history of Uppsala County from prehistoric times to the present day at the Upplandsmuseet. This impressive museum features exhibits on Viking culture, medieval art, and modern industry, as well as a collection of ancient runes.

The Uppsala Cathedral

One of the most iconic landmarks in Uppsala countryside is the Uppsala Cathedral, a stunning Gothic cathedral built in the 13th century. This awe-inspiring monument features striking stained glass windows, intricate carvings, and a majestic bell tower.

Natural Attractions of Uppsala countryside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Uppsala countryside is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and Nordic skiing.

Fyris River

The Fyris River is a perfect destination for a scenic stroll or a relaxing boat ride through the Uppsala countryside. Take in panoramic views of the countryside and the charming riverbanks, and visit the historic Old Uppsala, the seat of the legendary Viking rulers of Sweden.

Uppland Trails

Uppsala countryside boasts several spectacular hiking and biking routes, including the Uppland Trails. These well-marked and maintained trails lead visitors through dense forests, rolling fields, and picturesque lakes, and offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Wik Nature Reserve

Tucked away in the eastern part of Uppsala county, the Wik Nature Reserve is a hidden gem for nature lovers. This pristine wilderness area features rare flora and fauna, dramatic rock formations, and serene lakes perfect for canoeing and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uppsala countryside

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a leisurely mountain biker, Uppsala countryside has plenty of routes for all levels of experience.

Uppland Trails

The Uppland Trails are a network of well-marked hiking and biking routes that wind through the scenic countryside of Uppsala. From easy walks to challenging mountain bike trails, there’s something for everyone on the Uppland Trails.

Håga Forests

The Haga Forests offer a beautiful natural setting for hiking and biking. The forest is home to several well-marked trails, including the popular Håga Trail, which takes visitors past historic ruins, serene lakes, and abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Uppsala countryside: Activities and Tours

From cultural events to outdoor adventures, Uppsala countryside offers a wide range of activities and tours.

The Viking Market at Gunnes gård

Experience the Viking era firsthand by visiting the Viking Market at Gunnes gård. This annual event features authentic Viking crafts, food, and entertainment, as well as live performances and demonstrations.

The Gästrike–Hammarby Circuit

The Gästrike–Hammarby Circuit is a unique cultural attraction that offers visitors a tour of the hometown of author Erik Bergqvist. This charming village features quaint cottages and stunning gardens, as well as a museum dedicated to Erik Bergqvist’s work.

Week-Long Itinerary for Uppsala countryside

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Uppsala countryside, here’s a sample itinerary that will allow you to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Uppsala city, the university, and the botanical garden.
Day 2 Take a boat ride on the Fyris River and visit the Viking ruins at Old Uppsala.
Day 3 Hike the Uppland Trails or bike the Håga Forests.
Day 4 Visit the Linnaeus Museum and the Upplandsmuseet.
Day 5 Ski the Nordic trails at Gamla Uppsala or Wik Nature Reserve.
Day 6 Go fishing for trout or salmon in the nearby lakes and rivers.
Day 7 Sample local cuisine and buy souvenirs at the Uppsala farmers’ market.

Food and Drink Scene of Uppsala countryside

Uppsala countryside is home to a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases the region’s rich culinary traditions and locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are a few dishes that you should not miss when visiting Uppsala countryside:

Pyttipanna

Pyttipanna is a traditional Swedish dish made from diced potatoes, onions, and meat (typically beef or pork), fried together in a pan and served with fried eggs and pickled beets.

Gravlax

Gravlax is a Nordic specialty made from raw salmon cured in salt, sugar, and dill. It’s typically served with a mustard-dill sauce and crispbread.

Local Beverages

Uppsala countryside offers an abundance of delicious locally brewed beers, including the popular Pilsner from up-and-coming brewer Halla.

Where to Stay in Uppsala countryside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Uppsala countryside offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and taste.

Hostels

If you’re looking for an affordable option, Uppsala Studentbo provides clean and comfortable dormitory-style accommodations for travelers on a budget.

Guesthouses

The charming countryside of Uppsala boasts several guesthouses, including the popular Falsterbo Gästhus, which offers cozy rooms, a beautiful garden and outdoor terrace, and easy access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Uppsala countryside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Uppsala countryside is famous for its locally made handicrafts and unique souvenirs, such as:

Swedish Glassware

Swedish glassware is renowned for its simple elegance and timeless design. Visit the Orrefors Glassworks for a wide selection of handmade glassware and other decorative items.

Handmade Textiles

Uppsala is home to several traditional textile mills that produce high-quality wool, linen, and cotton fabrics. For local souvenirs, check out the small independent shops and boutiques in and around Uppsala.

Local Culture and Customs of Uppsala countryside

Uppsala countryside has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to Viking times. Some of the region’s most notable cultural customs and traditions include:

Midsummer Celebration

Uppsala celebrates the summer solstice with a week-long festival that includes bonfires, traditional dancing, and the raising of the maypole.

Settling Down Ceremony

The settling down ceremony is a traditional Swedish ritual that takes place during the autumn months. It marks the end of the harvest season and the start of the winter months. In Uppsala countryside, this ceremony is celebrated with a feast and traditional folk music and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Uppsala countryside

The best time to visit Uppsala countryside depends on your preferences and activities. The peak summer season, from June to August, is ideal for outdoor adventures and cultural festivals. In the winter months, from December to February, visitors can enjoy Nordic skiing, ice fishing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uppsala countryside

Do:

  • Respect local cultural customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately for the season and weather
  • Always carry emergency supplies, such as a map and a first-aid kit
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles when hiking and camping

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on hiking and biking trails
  • Touch or disturb the local wildlife
  • Ignore the warning signs and safety regulations in national parks and nature reserves

Safety and Health

Safety in Uppsala countryside

Uppsala countryside is generally a safe and welcoming destination. However, visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  • Be aware of ticks during the summer months, as they can transmit Lyme disease.
  • Take necessary precautions when hiking, biking, or skiing in the wilderness areas.
  • Avoid swimming in lakes or rivers unless you’re an experienced swimmer.

Health in Uppsala countryside

Uppsala countryside has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions to prevent illness, such as:

  • Drink bottled or filtered water
  • Avoid raw or undercooked meats and fish
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to Uppsala countryside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Uppsala countryside is easily accessible from major cities in Sweden and the rest of Europe.

Airports Near Uppsala countryside

The closest airport to Uppsala countryside is Stockholm Arlanda Airport, which is located about 30km away.

Bus Stations Near Uppsala countryside

Uppsala has a central bus station, which connects to other major cities in Sweden, such as Stockholm and Gothenburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Uppsala countryside by train, taxi, or rental car.

How to Get Around in Uppsala countryside

The easiest and most convenient way to get around Uppsala countryside is by car or bike. Rental cars are available at the airport and in Uppsala, and bike rentals are available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Uppsala countryside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

Stockholm

Sweden’s capital city, Stockholm, is just a short train ride from Uppsala. The city boasts plenty of historic attractions, world-class museums, and chic shops and restaurants.

Gävle

Located to the north of Uppsala, Gävle is a charming coastal town known for its festivals, museums, and picturesque harbor.

Conclusion

Uppsala countryside has something to offer for every type of traveler, whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie. So pack your bags, grab your hiking boots, and get ready for an adventure of a lifetime!
