Västerås is a picturesque city located in the heart of Sweden. Nestled between stunning lakes, Västerås is the perfect destination for those who love outdoor adventures, scenic views, and cultural experiences. With a vibrant food and drink scene, lively festivals, and a charming old town, Västerås is a great place to spend a week or more.

Introduction to Västerås

Västerås is a city in the Västmanland province, in the central part of Sweden. It is located on the shore of Lake Mälaren, one of Sweden’s largest lakes. The city has a rich history dating back to the Viking Age, and it is filled with cultural landmarks and attractions. Västerås is also a modern city, with great shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

List of Places to Visit in Västerås

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are many museums and historical places to explore in Västerås. The city has a diverse history, with influences from the Vikings, the Middle Ages, and the Industrial Revolution. Here are some of the top cultural attractions:

  • Västerås Castle: This medieval castle was built in the 13th century and is now a museum. Visitors can explore the castle’s history, including its use as a prison and its role in the Swedish Reformation.
  • Anundshög: This ancient burial mound is one of the largest in Sweden, and it dates back to the 1st millennium AD. It is located just outside of Västerås and is surrounded by beautiful nature.
  • Västerås Cathedral: This cathedral was built in the 13th century and is located in the center of the city. It is one of Sweden’s oldest cathedrals and has stunning architecture and artwork.
  • Artipelag: This museum and gallery is located on an island in Lake Mälaren. It features contemporary art exhibitions, a restaurant, and a stunning natural setting.

Natural Attractions of Västerås: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Västerås is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, including lakes, forests, and rolling hills. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in the area:

  • Västerås Archipelago: This group of islands is located in Lake Mälaren and is a popular destination for boating, swimming, fishing, and hiking. The islands are home to charming villages, beautiful nature, and historical landmarks.
  • Hälla Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is located just outside of Västerås and is a great place to explore the Swedish countryside. It has hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views.
  • Kopparberg Park: This park is located in the heart of Västerås and is a great place to relax and enjoy the city’s green spaces. It features a lake, walking paths, and beautiful gardens.
  • Lögaström Nature Reserve: This reserve is located north of Västerås and is known for its beautiful waterfalls, dense forests, and peaceful hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Västerås

Västerås is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the top trails:

  • Mälaren Runt: This bike trail circles Lake Mälaren and is a great way to see the stunning Swedish countryside. It is approximately 600 km in total and can be completed in sections.
  • Ågelsjön Runt: This 11 km hiking trail is located just outside of Västerås and offers stunning views of Lake Ågelsjön and the surrounding forests.
  • Ringsjön Runt: This 23 km biking trail takes visitors through the beautiful countryside around Västerås and features scenic views of Lake Ringsjön.
  • Kopparleden: This 40 km hiking trail takes visitors through the forests and wetlands of Västmanland and features beautiful views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Västerås: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to enjoy in Västerås, from exploring the city’s history to trying out new adventures. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Västerås City Walking Tour: This guided tour takes visitors through the charming streets of Västerås and highlights the city’s history, culture, and architecture.
  • Zoo Night: This unique experience at the Västerås Zoo allows visitors to explore the zoo after dark, with special tours and activities.
  • Erikslund Shopping Center: This shopping center is one of the largest in Sweden and features hundreds of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options.
  • Västerås Summer Games: This annual sports festival takes place in Västerås and features competitions in soccer, handball, floorball, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Västerås

Here is a sample itinerary for a week in Västerås:

Day 1: Explore the Old Town and visit Västerås Castle Day 2: Hike in Hälla Nature Reserve and enjoy lunch at a lakeside cafe Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Västerås Cathedral and visit the Västerås Art Museum Day 4: Bike the Mälaren Runt trail and enjoy a picnic lunch on an island Day 5: Explore the Västerås Archipelago by boat and visit Anundshög Day 6: Shop at the Erikslund Shopping Center and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant Day 7: Go on a hot air balloon ride over Västerås and enjoy a farewell dinner at a scenic restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Västerås

Västerås is known for its diverse food and drink scene, with everything from local specialties to international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Falukorv: This traditional Swedish sausage is made from a mix of beef and pork and is often served with mashed potatoes and lingonberry jam.
  • Raggmunk: This Swedish potato pancake is typically served with crispy bacon and lingonberry jam.
  • Sill: This pickled herring is a staple of Swedish cuisine and can be served in a variety of ways, including with potatoes and sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Västerås has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries producing unique and flavorful brews.
  • Glögg: This Swedish mulled wine is typically served during the winter months and features warm spices like cinnamon, cardamom, and ginger.
  • Birch Sap: This refreshing drink is made from the sap of birch trees and is a popular beverage in Västerås and other parts of Sweden.

Where to Stay in Västerås: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Västerås has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From modern hotels to charming guesthouses, here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Elite Stadshotellet Västerås: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of the city and features luxurious rooms and a restaurant.
  • Clarion Collection Hotel Etage: This boutique hotel is located in a historical building and offers cozy rooms and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • STF Hostel Västerås: This affordable hostel is located just outside of the city center and offers basic accommodations and shared facilities.
  • Västerås Bed & Breakfast: This charming guesthouse is located in a traditional Swedish villa and offers comfortable rooms and a garden.

What to Buy in Västerås: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Västerås offers a range of unique souvenirs and products that make great gifts or keepsakes. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Dala Horse: This traditional Swedish wooden horse is a classic souvenir and can be found in many gift shops in Västerås.
  • Lingonberry Jam: This tart and sweet jam is made from lingonberries, a popular Swedish berry. It is often served with meat dishes or on toast.
  • Swedish Glassware: Västerås is home to several glassworks, and visitors can buy handmade glassware such as vases, bowls, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Västerås

Swedish culture is known for its emphasis on equality, sustainability, and quality of life. Visitors to Västerås can expect to encounter friendly and welcoming locals who value these principles. Some other customs and cultural practices to be aware of include:

  • Fika: This Swedish custom involves taking a coffee break with friends or colleagues, often accompanied by sweets or pastries. Västerås has many charming cafes where visitors can enjoy a fika.
  • Midsummer: This Swedish holiday is celebrated on the longest day of the year and involves dancing around a maypole, eating traditional foods, and enjoying the outdoors.
  • Environmentalism: Sweden is known for its commitment to sustainability, and visitors to Västerås can expect to encounter many eco-friendly initiatives and practices.

Best Time to Visit Västerås

The best time to visit Västerås depends on personal preferences and interests. The summer months of June, July, and August are ideal for outdoor activities and festivals, while the winter months are great for winter sports and Christmas markets. The shoulder seasons of spring and fall offer mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Västerås

Do:

  • Embrace Swedish culture, including fika, environmentalism, and social equality.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Västerås, including its lakes, forests, and parks.
  • Try local food and drink, including traditional dishes and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Interrupt people while they are speaking; Swedes value their personal space and may find interruptions rude.
  • Wear shoes indoors; Swedes typically remove their shoes before entering someone’s home or a public building.
  • Skip the opportunity to talk to locals; Swedes are friendly and welcoming, and they enjoy meeting new people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Västerås

Västerås is a safe city with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should still take normal precautions, such as locking doors and windows, keeping valuables secure, and being aware of their surroundings at night.

Health in Västerås

Västerås has excellent healthcare facilities and services. Visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance and familiarize themselves with local emergency services.

Getting to Västerås: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Västerås

The closest airport to Västerås is Stockholm Västerås Airport, which is located approximately 10 km from the city center. The airport offers domestic and international flights, and there are several transport options to and from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Västerås

Västerås has a central bus station that offers both domestic and international routes. There are also several local bus routes that connect to nearby towns and attractions.

Other Options

Visitors to Västerås can also travel by train, car, or ferry. The city has several train stations, and there are plenty of car rental options. Ferries can be taken to nearby islands and locations.

How to Get Around in Västerås

Västerås is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There are also several bus routes that connect to different parts of the city and surrounding areas. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Västerås: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Västerås can easily explore other parts of Sweden and neighboring countries. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Stockholm, the capital of Sweden, is only 100 km away and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Lake Siljan, one of Sweden’s largest lakes, is located approximately 170 km northwest of Västerås and is famous for its natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Örebro, a charming city located approximately 70 km south of Västerås, offers beautiful architecture, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Västerås is a beautiful and fascinating city that offers something for everyone. Visitors can explore the city’s history and culture, enjoy outdoor adventures, and indulge in great food and drink. With its welcoming locals, eco-friendly initiatives, and stunning natural beauty, Västerås is a great destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Sweden.
