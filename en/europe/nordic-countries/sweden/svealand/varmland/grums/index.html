Introduction to Grums

Grums is a small town located in the province of Värmland, in Svealand, Sweden. Situated in the heart of Nordic countries, Grums is a charming destination that offers natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you want to explore local museums and historical places or enjoy hiking and biking routes, this travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to Grums.

List of Places to Visit in Grums

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Grums boasts several museums and historical places that allow visitors to learn about local culture and heritage.

Grums Church

Grums Church is a beautiful Swedish church that dates back to the 12th century. This centuries-old church has numerous artifacts on display, including a chandelier from the 1600s, a baptismal font from the 1400s, and a pulpit from the 1700s.

Hultet Hembygdsgård

Hultet Hembygdsgård is a well-preserved 19th-century farmstead that showcases the traditional way of Swedish rural life. The farmstead has a museum that displays various artifacts, tools, and machinery used in farming and other daily activities.

Grums Local Heritage Museum

Grums Local Heritage Museum is a small museum that displays the historical items of local significance. The museum has exhibits of photographs, documents, and various everyday items that give a glimpse of the town’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Grums: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grums is surrounded by the natural beauty of lakes, forests, and hills that offers visitors plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Lake Vänern

Lake Vänern is Europe’s third-largest lake and the largest lake in Sweden. With its clear water, stunning scenery, and numerous islands, it is a popular destination for fishing, swimming, and sailing.

Lennart Hellsing Trail

The Lennart Hellsing Trail is a 5 km-long hiking route that follows the scenic shores of Lake Vänern. The trail is named after a Swedish author who loved spending time in Grums. The trail is relatively easy, and it offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding nature.

Kornberget Nature Reserve

Kornberget Nature Reserve is a forested area that offers fantastic hiking trails and scenic views. The reserve is home to various wildlife, including moose, eagles, and foxes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grums

Grums is an excellent destination for those who love hiking and biking, with numerous trails that offer scenic views of the natural beauty of the region.

Fryksdalsleden Trail

Fryksdalsleden Trail is a 280 km-long hiking and biking trail that follows the beautiful forests, lakes, and rivers of Värmland. The trail offers diverse landscapes and several attractions along the way, including Högbergsfältets Nature Reserve, the town of Sunne, and lake Övre Fryken.

Värmlandsleden Trail

Värmlandsleden Trail is another long-distance hiking and biking trail that runs through Värmland. The trail is 800 km long and divided into several sections, with various campsites and cabins along the way.

Best Things to Do in Grums: Activities and Tours

Grums offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences.

Wild Moose Safari

One of the most popular activities in Grums is the Wild Moose Safari. Visitors can join a guided tour to see these majestic animals in their natural habitat.

Grums Art Gallery

The Grums Art Gallery is an excellent place to explore the work of local artists. The gallery exhibits various art forms, including painting, sculpture, and photography.

Lake Vänern Cruise

A great way to explore the beauty of Lake Vänern is to take a cruise. Several companies offer boat tours, which provide visitors with an opportunity to enjoy the lake’s stunning scenery while learning about its history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Grums

Grums has a diverse food and drink scene that showcases local specialties and traditional Swedish dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

Värmland Pancakes

Värmland Pancakes are thin, crispy pancakes that are typically served with preserves or lingonberry sauce. They are a local specialty and are served in most restaurants and cafes in Grums.

Gravlax

Gravlax is a dish made from salmon, which is marinated in salt, sugar, and dill. It is typically served with mustard sauce and potatoes and is a popular dish in Sweden.

Local Beverages

Värmlands Punsch

Värmlands Punsch is a traditional Swedish liquor made from sugar, arrack, and spices. It has a sweet and spicy taste and is typically served as an aperitif or after-dinner drink.

Norrlands Guld

Norrlands Guld is a popular beer in Sweden that is brewed in the northern regions, including Värmland. It has a smooth, rich taste and is a perfect drink to enjoy with local dishes.

Where to Stay in Grums: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grums offers several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Grums Hotell

Grums Hotell is a small, family-run hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a warm welcome. The hotel has a restaurant that serves traditional Swedish dishes and a bar that offers local drinks.

Lövåsgårdens Gästgiveri

Lövåsgårdens Gästgiveri is a charming guesthouse that is set in a beautifully restored 18th-century building. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves local dishes and drinks.

Grums Vandrarhem

Grums Vandrarhem is a budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable beds and shared amenities. The hostel has a communal kitchen, a lounge, and a BBQ area.

What to Buy in Grums: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grums has several stores that sell local products, souvenirs, and crafts.

Värmlandsbryggan

Värmlandsbryggan is a store that sells local crafts and products made by artisans from Värmland. The store has a wide range of items, including pottery, glassware, textiles, and jewelry.

Skoglunds

Skoglunds is a store that specializes in traditional Swedish crafts, including wooden dala horses, knitted sweaters, and glassware.

Local Culture and Customs of Grums

Swedish culture is predominantly egalitarian, and Grums is no exception. Visitors to Grums should be mindful of the local customs, including:

  • It is customary to take off your shoes inside someone’s home or a place of worship.
  • Tipping is not as common as in other countries, but rounding up the bill or leaving a small amount is appreciated.
  • Queuing is part of the Swedish culture, and people will typically wait their turn patiently in lines.

Best Time to Visit Grums

The best time to visit Grums is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and pleasant and the daylight hours are long. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and festivals during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grums

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks, including Värmland pancakes, gravlax, Värmlands Punsch, and Norrlands Guld.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Grums by hiking, biking, or taking a cruise.
  • Respect the local customs and be mindful of others while queuing and taking off your shoes.
  • Visit Grums Art Gallery and other cultural attractions to learn about the town’s history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the pristine natural environment.
  • Ignore the traffic rules or use your phone while driving.
  • Overstep personal boundaries or act disrespectfully towards others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grums

Grums is a safe destination with a low crime rate. Visitors should be mindful of their belongings and keep them in sight in public places. Road safety should be prioritized, as roads can be icy during the winter months.

Health in Grums

Grums has excellent health care facilities, and visitors can access medical assistance if required. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Grums: Transportation Options and Logistics

Grums is easily accessible by car, train, and bus.

Airports Near Grums

The closest airport to Grums is Karlstad Airport, which is located about 50 km from the town.

Bus Stations Near Grums

Grums has a bus station that is served by several local and regional buses.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or join a guided tour to reach Grums. It is also possible to travel to Grums by train, with the nearest train stations being at Kil and Karlstad.

How to Get Around in Grums

Grums is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a car or take local buses to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Grums: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grums is a beautiful destination that offers natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.
