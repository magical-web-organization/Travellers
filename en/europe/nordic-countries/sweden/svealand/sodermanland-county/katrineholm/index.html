Introduction to Katrineholm

Nestled in the heart of Södermanland County, on the eastern coast of Sweden, lies the idyllic town of Katrineholm. Historically known for its paper and timber industry, Katrineholm has transformed into a bustling hub of cultural, recreational, and outdoor activities. The town’s unique charm and stunning landscapes make it an attractive destination for travelers from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Katrineholm

From museums and historical sites to natural wonders and adventure activities, there’s something for everyone in Katrineholm. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Katrineholm City Museum: This museum showcases the town’s rich history, from the Viking era to the present day.

  • Julita Manor: This historic manor and farm give visitors a glimpse into rural Swedish life in the 19th and 20th centuries.

  • Fornsalen: Learn about the ancient history of Södermanland County at this museum, which houses Viking artefacts and exhibitions about the county’s prehistoric past.

  • Katrineholm Church: This beautiful church, located in the town centre, is a prime example of Swedish neoclassical architecture and features stunning frescoes and stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Katrineholm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Flaten Lake: Rent a boat and explore this tranquil lake, or just enjoy a picnic on its shores.

  • Stendammen Nature Reserve: Hike through this beautiful nature reserve, home to numerous bird species, including ospreys and cranes.

  • Tyttbo Falls: Experience the power of nature at these stunning waterfalls, located in the nearby town of Flen.

  • Fiskeby Boardwalk: Take a stroll along this scenic boardwalk, which winds through the picturesque Fiskeby village and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Katrineholm

The town of Katrineholm is surrounded by beautiful forests, lakes and vast open fields, making it a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails:

  • Sörmland Trail: This 1000-kilometer-long trail runs through Södermanland County, passing by some of the most beautiful landscapes in the region, including Katrineholm.

  • Sörmlandsleden: This trail covers 100 stages, each offering unique natural landscapes, and includes 60 nature reserves and over 600 km of hiking routes.

  • Rosvalla Forest: Explore this vast forest located on the outskirts of Katrineholm, which features several hiking and biking trails, as well as picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Katrineholm: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the scenic lakes and rivers surrounding Katrineholm.

  • Golfing: Tee off at one of the many golf courses in the area, such as Katrineholms Golf Club

  • Horseback Riding: Discover the Swedish countryside on horseback with a guided horse riding tour offered throughout the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Katrineholm

Day 1-2: Explore the historical and cultural sites of Katrineholm, including the Katrineholm City Museum, Julita Manor, and Fornsalen.

Day 3-4: Discover the stunning natural landscapes of the area, including Flaten Lake, Stendammen Nature Reserve, and Tyttbo Falls.

Day 5-6: Hike or bike the various trails in the vast forests surrounding Katrineholm, such as Sörmland Trail and Sörmlandsleden.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day on the golf course or on a horseback riding tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Katrineholm

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rabarberpaj: This mouth-watering rhubarb pie is a Swedish classic.

  • Blåbärssoppa: Made from fresh blueberries, this sweet soup is a refreshing treat on a hot summer day.

  • Gravad Lax: This traditional Swedish dish is made from cured salmon and served with dill and mustard sauce.

Local Beverages

  • O.P. Anderson: This traditional Swedish schnapps, made from wormwood and caraway seeds, is a popular drink to toast with.

  • Swedish Cider: Try one of the many varieties of Swedish cider, such as Brännland Cider, made from locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Katrineholm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dufweholms Herrgård: This beautiful 18th-century manor offers luxurious rooms and suites, a restaurant serving local cuisine, and stunning gardens.

  • Best Western Plaza Hotel: Located in the town centre, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a fitness centre.

  • STF Hostel Katrineholm: This affordable hostel is a great option for budget-conscious travelers, offering private and shared rooms, kitchen facilities, and a shared lounge area.

What to Buy in Katrineholm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Knives: The town is famous for traditional handmade knives, perfect for a unique and authentic souvenir.

  • Clogs and Wooden Toys: Take home a piece of Swedish craftsmanship with handmade clogs, wooden toys, and other handcrafted wooden items.

Local Culture and Customs of Katrineholm

The people of Katrineholm are friendly and welcoming, and the town has a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as taking off shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Katrineholm

The best time to visit Katrineholm is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural landscapes are at their most lush and green. The town is relatively quiet during the winter months (December-February) but offers unique winter activities such as ice skating, skiing, and snowshoe hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Katrineholm

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Be friendly and respectful to the locals.

  • Take the time to explore the natural landscapes of the area.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty of the area.

  • Ignore the local customs and culture.

  • Leave trash or litter behind when visiting the natural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Katrineholm

Katrineholm is a safe destination, with low crime rates and a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and should be adequately prepared.

Health in Katrineholm

The health care system in Sweden is of high quality, and Katrineholm has a well-equipped hospital.

Getting to Katrineholm: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Katrineholm

The closest international airport to Katrineholm is Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN), which is about 120 km away from Katrineholm. There are frequent trains that travel from the airport directly to Katrineholm station.

Bus Stations Near Katrineholm

The town is well connected by bus, with regular services from nearby cities such as Stockholm and Örebro.

Other Options

Another convenient option is to rent a car, giving you more flexibility to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Katrineholm

Katrineholm is a small town, and most of the main attractions are within walking distance. Bikes can also be rented in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Katrineholm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stockholm: The Swedish capital is just 100 km away and is easily accessible by train or car.

  • Norrköping: This charming town is known for its art and textile industries and is just an hour’s drive from Katrineholm.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a unique, charming, and welcoming destination, look no further than Katrineholm. With its rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and friendly locals, it’s the perfect place to experience Sweden at its best.
