Introduction to Kumla

Kumla is a charming town located in the Närke region of Svealand in Sweden. Surrounded by picturesque landscapes, Kumla is known for its rich historical heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. The town is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a serene getaway from the bustling cities and a charming town saturated with Scandinavian culture.

List of Places to Visit in Kumla

Kumla has plenty of attractions to offer visitors. Here’s a list of must-see places in Kumla:

Culturally Important Attractions - Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kumla Church: Also known as Kumla Kyrka, this church is one of the oldest and most significant landmarks in Kumla. Its architecture is a blend of Gothic and Romanesque styles, and the interior features beautiful frescoes and a stunning altarpiece.
  2. The Nerikes Allehanda Printing Museum: This museum offers an insight into the history of printing and the evolution of newspapers. The museum houses antique printing presses and equipment dating back to the 1800s.
  3. The Railway Museum: The Kumla Railway Museum showcases the history of Swedish Railways with a comprehensive collection of trains, carriages, and other railway-related equipment.
  4. Kumla Old Town: The Kumla Old Town is like stepping back in time. It has several well-preserved buildings from the 19th century, and it is the perfect place to experience the history and heritage of Kumla.

Natural Attractions of Kumla - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Humsjön Lake: This is an ideal spot for outdoor activities such as fishing and swimming, and there are also several hiking trails around the lake that provide stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  2. Bergsgatan: A walk along Bergsgatan will take you through Kumla’s charming streets and historic architecture, showcasing the idyllic atmosphere of this small Swedish town.
  3. Omberg Nature Reserve: Located just outside Kumla, Omberg Nature Reserve is a paradise for nature lovers. It offers fantastic hiking trails, open green fields, rich flora, and breathtaking views over Lake Vättern.
  4. Ekbacksbadet: This beach located in the heart of Kumla is a perfect place for a quick dip in the waters of Hjälmare Canal or taking a nap in the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumla

Kumla is teeming with stunning hiking and biking routes that take you on an adventure through the beautiful Swedish countryside. Popular hiking/biking routes in Kumla include:

  1. Hiking Trails: Humsjön Lake Trail, Omberg Trail, Bergsgatan Trail, and Kilsbergen Trail.
  2. Biking Routes: Sörmlandsleden Trail, Vättern Runt Trail, and Motala-Laxå Trail.

Best Things to Do in Kumla: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours in Kumla that will satisfy any type of traveler. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning landscapes of Kumla and enjoy magnificent views of the scenery from above.
  2. Golfing: The Kumla Golf Club offers one of the most beautiful and challenging golf courses in Sweden, surrounded by picturesque landscapes and with its unique driving range.
  3. Skiing at Kilsbergen: The Kilsbergen ski resort offers some of the best skiing trails in the region
  4. Swimming: Humsjön Lake and Ekbacksbadet are great destinations for a refreshing swim.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kumla

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for a trip to Kumla:

Day 1: Visit Kumla Church, explore the Old Town, and enjoy some delicious Swedish cuisine at the restaurants in the city’s central square.

Day 2: Visit the Nerikes Allehanda Printing Museum and the Railway Museum.

Day 3: Hiking Tour at Omberg Nature Reserve.

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride over Kumla for a unique perspective of the city

Day 5: Play a round of golf at Kumla Golf Club.

Day 6: Day trip to Lake Vättern and Motala

Day 7: Chill out at Ekbacksbadet or Humsjön Lake and enjoy the natural beauty of the landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Kumla

When in Kumla, you can’t miss out on their fantastic food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Meatballs: An iconic Swedish dish that consists of small meatballs served with mashed potatoes, brown sauce, and lingonberry sauce.
  2. Cinnamon Buns: A beloved Swedish sweet, Cinnamon Buns, are a must-try while you are in Kumla.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: The craft beer scene in Kumla is booming, with several local breweries producing high-quality beers.
  2. Schnapps: Traditional Swedish schnapps and aquavit are both popular choices in Kumla.

Where to Stay in Kumla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kumla has a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the top-rated places to stay in Kumla include:

  1. Kumla Hotel: This cozy hotel combines traditional Swedish-style with modern conveniences and is centrally located in the town center.
  2. Hanåsa Gård: This charming guesthouse is located on the shore of Hjälmare Canal with a fantastic lake view.

What to Buy in Kumla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to buy from Kumla include:

  1. Traditional Swedish Candies: Choose from a wide range of Swedish confectionaries such as polkagris or salmiak toffee.
  2. Handcrafted Items: Wooden ornaments and handicrafts made by local artisans make for fantastic gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumla

The people of Kumla are warm and friendly, and they love visitors who respect their traditions. Some local customs and traditions in Kumla include:

  1. Saying “Hej” for greeting.
  2. Paying with cash instead of credit cards for small purchases

Best Time to Visit Kumla

The best time to visit Kumla is during the summer (June to August) when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities. During the winter months, Kumla’s winter activities, such as skiing and ice skating, are popular among visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumla

Do:

  1. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately, taking the weather into consideration.

Don’t:

  1. Littering or vandalism.
  2. Observing anyone without permission is not acceptable in Kumla.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumla

Kumla is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, it’s always essential to take precautions to ensure your safety, such as keeping your belongings secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Kumla

Kumla has excellent medical facilities, including a state-of-the-art hospital with modern technology and expert medical staff. However, be sure to get comprehensive travel insurance before embarking on your journey to Kumla.

Getting to Kumla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kumla

  1. Stockholm Arlanda Airport: This airport is approximately 2 hours away from Kumla.
  2. Örebro Airport: This airport is about an hour away from Kumla.

Bus Stations Near Kumla

  1. Kumla Bus Station: The bus station is located in the town center and is well-connected to other parts of Svealand.

Other Options

  1. By Train: The most comfortable and convenient way to reach Kumla is by train, with the Kumla station located in the town center.
  2. By Car: Kumla is easily accessible via road from most major cities in Sweden.

How to Get Around in Kumla

Kumla is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or bike. Public transportation is also available in the form of buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Kumla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Örebro: This vibrant city with its beautiful castle and picturesque canal is a 30-minute drive from Kumla.
  2. Tiveden National Park: Tiveden is a stunning nature reserve just under an hour from Kumla, with hiking trails, rock formations, and a beautiful lake.
  3. Glaskogen: An extensive nature reserve north of Kumla, featuring several hiking trails, streams, and lakes.

Conclusion

Kumla may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of history, culture, and nature for travelers to explore. From stunning landscapes to unique cultural experiences, Kumla is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in the beauty of rural Sweden.
