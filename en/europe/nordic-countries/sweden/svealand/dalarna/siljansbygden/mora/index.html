Introduction to Mora

Located in Siljansbygden, in the heart of Dalarna and Svealand in Sweden, Mora is a charming town that attracts visitors from far and wide thanks to its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. The town is most known for its traditional craft of Dalahäst (Dala horse) making, and annually hosts the Vasaloppet, the oldest and largest cross country ski race in the world, which draws in tens of thousands of skiers and spectators. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, sports, or cuisine, Mora has something to offer every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Mora

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mora is home to several museums and historical landmarks that are worth a visit:

  • Zorn Museum: This historical museum celebrates the works of the famous Swedish artist, Anders Zorn, who is known for his portraits, nudes, and genre scenes. The museum features more than 100 of his paintings, sculptures, and textiles.

  • Vasaloppet Museum: Located near the finish line of the Vasaloppet race, this museum showcases the history of the event, including memorabilia and exhibits detailing the race’s origin and evolution.

  • Sollerö Hembygdsgård: This open-air museum consists of several historic buildings and exhibits that showcase the traditional life and customs of Sweden’s rural areas.

Natural Attractions of Mora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mora is famous for its scenic landscapes and outdoor activities that provide visitors with a sense of adventure and rejuvenation. Here are some top outdoor attractions:

  • Orsa Rovdjurspark: One of Europe’s largest wildlife parks, Orsa Rovdjurspark is home to predators such as bears, lynxes, wolves, and tigers. Visitors can also take part in feeding sessions and watch live shows.

  • Siljan: A gorgeous lake located in the center of Dalarna, Siljan offers plenty of activities such as swimming, fishing, and boating. For a romantic experience, take an evening cruise to enjoy the sunset.

  • Nature Reserve Stenåsen: A wildlife reserve that spans over 76 hectares, Stenåsen offers a beautiful hiking trail, picnic areas, and viewpoints that overlook the surrounding forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mora

Mora has a variety of hiking and biking routes to explore the scenic beauty of the town. One must-visit trail is Vildmarksleden (Wilderness Trail), which spans over 90 kilometers across three counties.

Best Things to Do in Mora: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more things to do, here are some exciting activities and tours to consider:

  • Skiing: Mora offers world-class skiing opportunities with groomed trails at ski resorts like Orsa Grönklitt, Gesundaberget, and Idre Fjäll.

  • Dala Horse Factory Tour: Learn about the history of the traditional Dala Horse, watch skilled artisans handcraft the wooden horses, and even paint your own Dala Horse to take home!

  • Mora Brewery Tour: Take a guided tour of the Mora Brewery and learn about the art of beer-making. You can enjoy a sample of their delicious brews at the end of the tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mora

If you’re planning to spend a week in Mora, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Zorn Museum and explore Mora’s town center
2 Take a hike on Vildmarksleden, the famous wilderness trail
3 Learn about the history of the Vasaloppet race at the Vasaloppet Museum
4 Take a bike ride to the charming village of Nusnäs and tour the Dala Horse Factory
5 Go skiing at Orsa Grönklitt, a top-rated ski resort
6 Relax with a scenic boat ride on Lake Siljan and dinner at a lakeside restaurant
7 Visit the Orsa Rovdjurspark for a unique wildlife experience

Food and Drink Scene of Mora

Must-Try Dishes

Mora is famous for its hearty meals and traditional Swedish cuisine. Don’t miss trying these popular dishes:

  • Ärtsoppa and Pannkakor: A delicious pea soup with pancakes is a classic Swedish meal to try when you’re in Mora!

  • Gravlax: A Norwegian dish that’s also popular in Sweden, gravlax is a cured salmon dish that’s served with a dill sauce and bread.

  • Smörgåsbord: A traditional buffet of Swedish foods, including pickled herring, meatballs, and cheeses.

Local Beverages

Don’t forget to try some of the local beverages Mora has to offer, including:

  • Swedish Akvavit: A distilled spirit that’s flavored with herbs and spices, this popular beverage is often served chilled and consumed with a meal.

  • Mora Brewery Beers: The Mora Brewery produces several different types of beer, including an IPA and a pilsner.

Where to Stay in Mora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Mora to fit every budget. If you’re looking for luxury, try the classic Mora Hotell & Spa, which offers beautiful rooms and suites, as well as a relaxing spa. For more affordable options, consider the hostel-like Mora Parken Hotel & Hostel or the cozy Tinas Bed & Breakfast.

What to Buy in Mora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mora is famous for its handmade Dala Horses, which make for a great souvenir or gift for loved ones. Other great souvenirs include Swedish jams and jellies, glassware, and locally-made crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Mora

Mora has strong roots in Swedish folklore and history, with many cultural traditions still alive today. Visitors can experience the culture through local events, including Midsummer celebrations and the annual Vasaloppet ski race.

Best Time to Visit Mora

While Mora is a great destination year-round, the best time to visit depends on what you’re looking for. Winters are perfect for winter sports like skiing, while summers provide plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and boating. If you want to experience Swedish culture, consider visiting during Midsummer’s Eve in June.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mora

Do:

  • Be respectful of local traditions and customs, like taking off your shoes when entering someone’s home.

  • Try traditional Swedish foods and local craft beer.

  • Enjoy the scenic beauty of Lake Siljan and the surrounding forests.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in outdoor areas.

  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public areas, especially at night.

  • Don’t forget to check the public transportation schedules if you’re planning to rely on them during your visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mora

Mora is considered a safe destination for travelers, but as with any trip, it’s important to take precautions by keeping an eye on your belongings and staying alert in public areas.

Health in Mora

Sweden has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can expect high-quality medical care if needed. Visitors from outside of the EU should purchase travel medical insurance before visiting.

Getting to Mora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mora

The closest major airports to Mora are Stockholm-Arlanda Airport (ARN) and Oslo Airport (OSL). From there, travelers can take a train or rent a car to reach Mora.

Bus Stations Near Mora

Mora has a well-connected bus system, with direct connections to major cities like Stockholm, Gothenburg, and Oslo, Norway.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Mora by train through the Mora train station, which offers connections to regional destinations.

How to Get Around in Mora

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Mora and explore the surrounding areas. The town also has a reliable public transportation system, with buses and trains connecting to nearby areas like Orsa and Rättvik.

Where to Go Next from Mora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Mora, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Orsa: A charming village located just 20 kilometers north of Mora, Orsa is home to the beautiful Orsa Rovdjurspark wildlife park and the popular Orsa Grönklitt ski resort.

  • Rättvik: A picturesque town located on the banks of Lake Siljan, Rättvik is known for its beautiful scenery, outdoor activities, and cultural events.

  • Dalhalla: Located just outside of Rättvik, this open-air concert venue offers a unique musical experience among the stunning natural landscapes of Dalarna.

Conclusion

Mora is a wonderful destination for travelers of all ages and interests, with its rich cultural heritage, beautiful scenery, and wide range of outdoor activities. Whether you’re experiencing Swedish culture for the first time or are a seasoned traveler, Mora is sure to leave a lasting impression!
