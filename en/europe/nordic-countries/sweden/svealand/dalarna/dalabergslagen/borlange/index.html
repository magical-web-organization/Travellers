Borlänge is a picturesque town situated in the Dalarna province of central Sweden. Surrounded by lush green forests, magnificent lakes, and mountains, Borlänge is a nature lover’s paradise. It’s a great escape for people looking to break away from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town offers tourists an opportunity to explore the great outdoors, indulge in adventurous activities and learn about local Swedish culture.

Introduction to Borlänge

Borlänge town was never a touristy place until recent years. With its beautiful landscapes, the town has become a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. Borlänge offers many unique experiences that are worth exploring, such as local food, cultural highlights, adventure activities, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Borlänge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Borlänge has some exciting museums and historical places to visit. For those interested in learning about local history and culture, here are our top recommendations:

  • (1) Dalarna Museum: This museum is home to a collection of Dalarna’s cultural history, with various exhibitions showcasing traditional costumes, textiles, handicrafts, and much more.
  • (2) Akerblad’s Museum: This is an open-air museum showcasing the life of a wealthy farmer in the 19th century. You can explore traditional workshops and houses and experience how people used to live in the past.
  • (3) Ornasstugan Museum: This museum is situated in the forest and features a collection of traditional tools and textiles used by the forest community.

Natural Attractions of Borlänge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Borlänge is an outdoor enthusiast’s playground. The town has something for everyone, from pristine lakes to forest trails, and the best part is that nature is just a short drive away. Here are some natural attractions worth exploring:

  • (1) Romme Alpin: This is one of the most renowned ski resorts in Sweden, situated on the southern slopes of Romme Mountain.
  • (2) Frostbrunnsdalen: This nature reserve is a must-visit for hikers. With its lush forests, streams, and wildflowers, Frostbrunnsdalen offers breathtaking scenery that will take your breath away.
  • (3) Runn Lake: If you’re looking for boating, kayaking or fishing, then Runn Lake is the place for you. Known for its crystal-clear water and tranquil environment, this lake is a refreshing escape from the bustling world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borlänge

Borlänge has plenty of hiking and biking trails suitable for all levels of fitness and adventure. Here are some of our top recommended hiking and biking routes:

  • (1) Gammelkroppa Trail: This trail is 16km long and leads hikers through picturesque valleys and alongside lakes, giving hikers fantastic scenic views.
  • (2) Bjurfors Circuit: The Bjurfors Circuit is a network of cycling trails offering varying degrees of difficulty. With well-marked paths and stunning scenery, it’s a must-do for all biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Borlänge: Activities and Tours

Borlänge offers tourists numerous fun-filled activities and tours. Here are some of the top things to do in Borlänge:

  • (1) Snowmobiling in Borlänge’s Powderfields: This is an exciting and unique activity for adrenaline junkies. Zoom across the fresh powder snow on your snowmobile, taking in the stunning countryside scenery.
  • (2) Dalarna Horse Safari: This tour is perfect for animal lovers. Ride a horse through the beautiful countryside of Borlänge and experience life like a Swedish farmer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Borlänge

If you want to explore Borlänge thoroughly, we recommend a week-long itinerary consisting of the following:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Akerblad’s Museum followed by Ornasstugan Museum, and then finish the day with a sunset hike in Frostbrunnsdalen.
2 Explore Romme Alpin and take a skiing lesson to learn from the pros.
3 Have a relaxing day at Runn Lake; go fishing, kayaking or boating.
4 Take a Dalarna Horse Safari tour. The tour will allow you to explore the countryside on horseback and learn about the rich Swedish culture.
5 Go on a snowmobiling adventure in Borlänge’s Powderfields, followed by a warm and delicious traditional Swedish meal at a local restaurant.
6 Explore Dalarna Museum, learn about the region’s cultural history and try traditional costumes for a unique experience.
7 Spend the day cycling through Bjurfors’ circuit, taking in the beautiful scenery and panoramic views of the valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Borlänge

Borlänge is a food lover’s paradise. The town has several restaurants and cafes where you can satisfy your taste buds with a unique blend of local flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you cannot miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Räkstuvning med sparris: This dish is made with shrimp in a creamy asparagus sauce, one of the local favorites.
  • (2) Fläskkorv: This dish is made with a type of sausage, a staple food in Sweden.
  • (3) Köttbullar: Famous all over the world, meatballs in Borlänge are made with a unique blend of spices, and you can taste the difference.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Bärnsten: This is an amber-colored beer, known for its rich, malty taste.
  • (2) Äppelcider: This refreshing apple cider is perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot summer day.
  • (3) Glögg: This is a spiced wine served hot and is perfect for cold winter days.

What to Buy in Borlänge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, then Borlänge has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs:

Local Culture and Customs of Borlänge

Sweden has a unique culture and customs, and it’s always good to know some basic things before visiting any new place. Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Borlänge:

  • (1) Swedes are punctual, so always be on time, especially for meetings or events.
  • (2) Sweden has a drinking culture, and it’s not unusual to have drinks with colleagues after work.
  • (3) Swedes are known for their reserved nature, but they are friendly, welcoming people.

Best Time to Visit Borlänge

Borlänge is beautiful throughout the year. However, the time of year you visit will depend on the kind of activities you’re interested in. The winter months from December to February are perfect for skiing, snowmobiling, and other winter activities. The summer months from June to September are perfect for hiking, fishing, boating, and other outdoor activities.

Getting to Borlänge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Borlänge is easily accessible by bus, train, or car. Here are some transportation options you can consider:

  • (1) Air travel: The closest airport to Borlänge is Dala Airport, but it has limited international flights. It is recommended to fly to Stockholm and take a train or bus to Borlänge.
  • (2) Train: There are regular train services to Borlänge from major Swedish cities like Stockholm and Gothenburg.
  • (3) Bus: There are several bus companies servicing Borlänge, connecting it to other Swedish towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Borlänge

Borlänge is a small town, and most of its attractions are located within walking distance. However, if you want to explore further, renting a car or a bike is recommended.
