Strömstad is a charming coastal town located in the heart of Bohuslän, Sweden. Known for its stunning scenery, cultural significance, and outdoor activities, Strömstad offers a one-of-a-kind experience that visitors will not forget. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have rounded up all the information you need to plan a perfect trip to Strömstad, including recommendations on how to get there, where to stay, and what to do.

Introduction to Strömstad

Strömstad has been a popular tourist destination for both Swedes and international visitors for many years. Its historical buildings, beautiful archipelago, and lively streets have drawn visitors from all around the world. Strömstad’s reputation for excellent seafood, picturesque scenery, and rich heritage makes it a perfect holiday location.

List of Places to Visit in Strömstad

Strömstad offers plenty of attractions for visitors to explore, ranging from culturally significant museums and historical sites, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit places in Strömstad:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Strömstad offers a wealth of cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in history, art, or architecture, there’s something for everyone here. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Strömstad:

  • Strömstad Museum: Explore the rich cultural and historical heritage of Strömstad at this museum. It offers an exclusive insight into the local society and the town as a commercial trading centre.
  • Daftöland Amusement Park: Bring the whole family to this fantastic amusement park where the kids can ride on roller coasters and play in the water park.
  • The fortress of Carlstens Sveaborg 1818-1867: Enjoy a bit of history by visiting the fortress of Carlstens Sveaborg 1818-1867, which stands on an island just outside of the city.

Natural Attractions of Strömstad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its stunning coastal landscape and stunning views, it is no surprise that Strömstad is a popular outdoor destination. Whether it’s hiking, biking, or boating, visitors have plenty of choices. Here are some of the natural attractions that Strömstad has to offer:

  • Kosterhavet National Park: The first marine national park in Sweden, Kosterhavet has an abundance of marine life, seagrass beds, and rocky reefs.
  • Strömstad Boat Tours: Discover the beautiful Swedish archipelago with a guided boat tour from Strömstad. You can see all the way to the Norwegian plateau on a clear day.
  • Nordens Ark: This animal park is located on the hills of the Swedish west coast and holds over 80 species of animals from around the globe.
  • Stromsholmen Beach: Stroll along the beach of this beautiful landing strip complete with sand dunes and boardwalks.
  • Båtfjället: Hike up to the highest point of Strömstad and experience the breathtaking panoramic views of the archipelago.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strömstad

For those who enjoy hiking and biking, Strömstad has plenty of different routes to offer visitors. They vary in length and difficulty, so you can find the perfect one for you. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Svinesundsleden: This 70 km hiking and biking trail stretches from Halden, Norway, to Strömstad, Sweden.
  • Kosterhavet: Explore the Kosterhavet National Park on foot or by bike – this route is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Hiking on Kynnefjäll : Discover the beautiful nature reserve Kynnefjäll and choose between two hiking routes that differ in length.

Best Things to Do in Strömstad: Activities and Tours

There are so many activities to explore in Strömstad. Here are some of the best activities that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Strömstad Fishing Tour: Experience a fishing tour on the Strömstad archipelago and learn about local fishing traditions.
  • Spa-town Havsbads: Pamper yourself at the luxurious spa villas on Havsbads Street, scented with fragrant natural oils, using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Guided city tour: Join one of the guided city tours and explore the rich cultural heritage of Strömstad.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strömstad

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Strömstad, you can follow this itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Strömstad Museum
2 Hike along Kynnefjäll
3 Boat tour in the archipelago

7 Relax on Stromsholmen beach

Food and Drink Scene of Strömstad

Sweden is known for its delicious seafood, and Strömstad is no exception. Visitors have plenty of options to choose from when it comes to food and drink. Here are some of the best dishes and drinks that you should try in Strömstad:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Soup: A creamy soup made with freshly caught lobster and vegetables.
  • Smörgåsbord: A traditional Scandinavian buffet that features a variety of different dishes, including herring, meatballs, and potato salad.
  • Fish and Chips: Locally caught fish is breaded and fried to crunchy perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner: The local Strömstad brewery offers a refreshing pilsner.
  • Schnapps: Kryddsnaps is a tasty beverage made with various herbs and spices and is a favourite among locals.

Where to Stay in Strömstad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Strömstad has various hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay in Strömstad:

  • First Hotel: Centrally located and offers well-furnished rooms and great amenities.
  • Grand Hotel: This luxury hotel is perfect for a romantic getaway or a spa retreat.
  • Hostel Regnbagen: Perfect for budget travellers, this hostel offers comfortable beds and a laid-back atmosphere.

What to Buy in Strömstad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Strömstad offers plenty of souvenirs and local products that you can bring home with you. Here are some of the most popular items:

  • Strömstad Vodka: This locally produced vodka is trendy among visitors.
  • Strömstad Jumper: A stylish jumper that features the Strömstad logo is the perfect souvenir to take home to remember your trip.
  • Shrimp: Bring home some fresh shrimp from the local market and enjoy them with your family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Strömstad

It’s always essential to learn about the local customs and traditions before travelling to a new destination. Strömstad is known for its friendly and relaxed people who value their heritage and culture.

Best Time to Visit Strömstad

The best time to visit Strömstad is from May to September, when the weather is warm, and tourist attractions are open. This is when you can enjoy long days, stunning sunsets and warm water temperatures in the archipelago.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strömstad

Here are some useful tips to help you make the most of your time in Strömstad:

Do:

  • Explore the local archipelago on a guided boat tour.
  • Try the delicious seafood and traditional Swedish dishes.
  • Visit the Kosterhavet National Park.

Don’t:

  • Litter – respect the beautiful environment
  • Walk on bicycle tracks – there are designated walking paths.

Safety and Health

It is always essential to ensure your safety when travelling to a new destination. Strömstad is generally safe and health concerns are minimal. Here are some important things to know:

Safety in Strömstad

  • Watch your belongings when visiting crowded areas.

Health in Strömstad:

  • The tap water in Strömstad is safe to drink. Health services are easily accessible in the city if required.

Getting to Strömstad: Transportation Options and Logistics

You have several transportation options to get to Strömstad, depending on your preferences and budget.

Airports Near Strömstad

The nearest airports serving Strömstad are:

  • Torp Sandefjord Airport – about 45 minutes away by car
  • Landvetter International Airport – approximately 150 minutes away by car
  • Oslo Gardermoen International Airport – about 180 minutes away by car

Bus Stations Near Strömstad

There are several bus stations in Strömstad that offer both local and long-distance connections. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Strömstad Centrum bus station
  • Strömstad Terminalen bus station

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Uddevalla – about 40 minutes from Strömstad.
  • Car: You can drive to Strömstad from Oslo or Gothenburg, following the E6 motorway.

How to Get Around in Strömstad

To get around the city, you can use public transportation, rent bicycles, or simply walk. Strömstad is a relatively small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Strömstad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of the surrounding area, there are plenty of nearby destinations that you can visit, such as:

  • Fjallbacka: This picturesque fishing village is just 40 km south of Strömstad and is perfect for a day trip.
  • Smögen: A popular tourist destination located 80 km south of Strömstad, known for its bustling marina.
  • Hamburgsund: This small village is situated on the coast and is known for its seafood and proximity to the scenic Vättlefjäll nature reserve.

Conclusion

Strömstad is a wonderful destination that offers a unique blend of incredible scenery, rich history, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in hiking, enjoying some of the world’s best seafood, exploring local museums and historical sites, or simply relaxing on the beach, Strömstad has something to offer everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you make the most of your trip to Strömstad.
