Introduction to Söderköping

Söderköping is a charming town in the scenic Östergötland region of southern Sweden. Founded in the 12th century as a trading center, Söderköping flourished during the Middle Ages and the Renaissance, and still preserves many historic landmarks and traditions. Today, Söderköping attracts visitors with its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, outdoor activities, festivals, and local gastronomy.

List of Places to Visit in Söderköping

If you’re planning to explore Söderköping, don’t miss these highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Göta Canal Museum: Learn about the history and engineering of the famous Göta Canal, which passes through Söderköping, and enjoy the interactive exhibitions and boat trips.
  • The Söderköping Town Museum: Discover the town’s past and present through art, artifacts, and stories, and admire the Renaissance-style Town Hall and the 13th-century St. Lawrence Church.
  • The Locks Area: Experience the charm and engineering ingenuity of the Göta Canal locks and bridges, and watch the boats pass through while enjoying a coffee or an ice cream at the nearby cafés.
  • The Väderstad Viking Trail: Follow the footsteps of the Vikings and the legendary king Olaf Skötkonung along this scenic hiking and biking trail, and see the rune stones, graves, and ancient settlements.

Natural Attractions of Söderköping: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bråviken Bay: Marvel at the vastness and diversity of the Baltic Sea and the coast from the Skönvik pier or the Nävekvarn beach, and spot seals, birds, and wildflowers.
  • Tinnerö Eklandskap Nature Reserve: Explore the oak forests, lakes, and meadows of this unique nature reserve, which hosts a variety of flora and fauna, such as orchids, elks, and badgers, and provides excellent hiking, fishing, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Ramunderberget Hill: Hike or bike up this hill, which is located just south of Söderköping, and enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the town, the archipelago, and the forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Söderköping

Apart from the Väderstad Viking Trail and Ramunderberget Hill, Söderköping offers many other scenic trails for hikers and bikers of all levels:

  • The Söderköping Round Trail: This 12 km circular trail goes through forests, fields, hills, and lakes, and offers beautiful views, picnic spots, and resting areas.
  • The S:t Anna Archipelago Trail: This 50 km long trail goes through the picturesque islands and villages of the S:t Anna archipelago, which is located northwest of Söderköping, and offers opportunities for fishing, swimming, boating, and camping.

Best Things to Do in Söderköping: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the cultural and natural attractions of Söderköping, you can also enjoy many activities and tours:

  • The Bråviken Golf Club: Play a round of golf at one of the finest golf courses in Sweden, which is located on the coast of Bråviken Bay, and provides stunning sea views and challenging holes.
  • The Löfstad Castle: Visit this 17th-century castle, which is located just outside Söderköping, and admire the baroque architecture, the art collections, and the local handicrafts, and join one of the guided tours or events.
  • The Söderköping Christmas Market: Experience the coziness and charm of the Swedish Christmas traditions at this annual market, which takes place in December, and offers handmade gifts, mulled wine, and gingerbread houses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Söderköping

If you have a week to spend in Söderköping, you can follow this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Söderköping, check into your hotel, and explore the town center, the Göta Canal locks, and the Söderköping Town Museum.
  • Day 2: Visit the Väderstad Viking Trail, hike or bike along the scenic trail, and have a picnic by the lake.
  • Day 3: Play golf at the Bråviken Golf Club, enjoy the seaside views and the restaurant, and relax at your hotel or hostel.
  • Day 4: Take a boat trip on the Göta Canal, admire the locks and the countryside, and have a coffee or a lunch in a canal-side café.
  • Day 5: Visit the Tinnerö Eklandskap Nature Reserve, explore the oak forests and the lakes, and spot the wildlife.
  • Day 6: Join a guided tour or event at the Löfstad Castle, learn about the history and culture of Östergötland, and buy some local souvenirs or handicrafts.
  • Day 7: Have a leisurely breakfast, check out of your accommodation, and take a bus or a train to your next destination, such as Norrköping, Linköping, or Stockholm.

Food and Drink Scene of Söderköping

Söderköping is known for its traditional and modern cuisine, which features local ingredients and flavors, such as fish, game, berries, mushrooms, and honey. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Söderköping are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gravlax: This classic Swedish dish consists of raw salmon, which is cured in sugar, salt, and dill, and served with mustard sauce, bread, and potatoes.
  • Elk meatballs: These savory meatballs are made of minced elk meat, onions, and spices, and served with lingonberry jam, gravy, and mashed potatoes.
  • Smultronstället: This local dessert consists of whipped cream, sugar, and wild strawberries, which are picked in the nearby meadows and forests, and served with crisp bread or waffles.

Local Beverages

  • Östergötland beer: This tasty beer is brewed by many local microbreweries, such as Bryggeri Munktell, Nääs Gårdsbryggeri, and Gårdsbryggeriet.
  • Söderköpings Brunn mineral water: This famous mineral water is bottled from a natural spring in the nearby village of Ramunderberget, and is known for its purity and health benefits.

Where to Stay in Söderköping: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Söderköping offers many options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to cozy hostels and guesthouses:

  • The Söderköpings Gästgiveri: This historic inn is located in the heart of Söderköping, and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar, and organizes events and concerts.
  • The Söderköpings Brunn: This spa hotel is located in the scenic countryside of Söderköping, and offers elegant rooms, a restaurant, a pool, and a sauna, and provides wellness treatments.
  • The Mangelgården Hostel: This cozy hostel is located in a former laundry house, and offers affordable beds, a kitchen, and a garden, and provides a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Söderköping: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring some authentic and meaningful gifts or souvenirs from Söderköping, you can choose among these options:

  • Handicrafts: Söderköping is known for its skilled artisans, who create beautiful objects of wood, glass, ceramics, and textiles, and sell them in many local shops and markets, such as the Christmas Market.
  • Local products: Söderköping produces many quality products from its natural resources, such as honey, jam, liquorice, and mustard, which you can buy at specialized shops or online.
  • Books and music: Söderköping has also a rich literary and musical culture, which is reflected in its many bookstores and music shops, where you can find works by local authors and artists, or just relax and browse.

Local Culture and Customs of Söderköping

Söderköping has a strong sense of community and tradition, which is expressed in its festivals, folklore, and civic engagement. Some of the local customs and habits of Söderköping are:

  • Midsummer: This is one of the most celebrated festivals in Sweden, and in Söderköping, people gather around the Maypole, dance, sing, and eat traditional food, such as herring, potatoes, and strawberries.
  • Dala horses: These colorful wooden horses are a symbol of Sweden, and in Söderköping, you can find many shops and stalls that sell them in different sizes and designs.
  • Recycling: Söderköping has a strong commitment to environmental sustainability, and encourages its residents and visitors to separate their waste, use public transportation, and conserve energy.

Best Time to Visit Söderköping

Söderköping can be visited throughout the year, and each season has its own charms and activities. However, the best time to visit Söderköping depends on your preferences and interests:

  • Spring (March-May): This is the ideal season for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and birdwatching, and for enjoying the blooming flowers and trees.
  • Summer (June-August): This is the peak season for tourism, and the time when most festivals, concerts, and markets take place, such as the Söderköping Jazz Festival and the Christmas Market.
  • Fall (September-November): This is the harvest season, and the time when you can taste the local delicacies, such as mushrooms, crayfish, and apples, and enjoy the colorful foliage.
  • Winter (December-February): This is the time when you can experience the Swedish Christmas traditions, and enjoy the snow, the ice-skating, and the warming food and drinks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Söderköping

If you want to have a pleasant and respectful experience in Söderköping, follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Greet people politely, and use “hej” or “god dag” as a form of greeting.
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s house or apartment, and respect the Swedish custom of hygge.
  • Use the public transportation, which is efficient and eco-friendly, or bike and walk, which are common and safe activities.
  • Try the local food and drinks, and engage in conversations with the locals, who are generally friendly and open-minded.

Don’t:

  • Be late for appointments, as punctuality is highly valued in Swedish culture.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English, although most Swedes do, and try to learn some basic Swedish phrases, such as “tack” (thank you) and “ursäkta” (excuse me).
  • Be loud or intrusive in public spaces, and respect the privacy and personal space of others.
  • Forget to tip in restaurants or cafes, as the service charge is not included in the bill, and the usual tip is around 10%.

Safety and Health

Söderköping is generally a safe and healthy destination, but some precautions and recommendations should be taken into account:

Safety in Söderköping

  • Watch your belongings, especially in crowded places, such as markets or festivals, and avoid leaving your valuables unattended.
  • Be aware of the traffic rules and signals, and use reflective clothing or lights when walking or biking in the dark.
  • Follow the water safety guidelines, and avoid swimming in the open sea or the canal, which can be dangerous due to strong currents or pollution.

Health in Söderköping

  • Get vaccinated and insured before traveling to Sweden, and carry a valid health insurance card or equivalent.
  • Use sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun, especially during the summer months, when the sun can be strong and harmful.
  • Stay hydrated and carry water with you, especially during outdoor activities, and avoid drinking too much alcohol, which can impair your judgment and health.

Getting to Söderköping: Transportation Options and Logistics

Söderköping is located in the Östergötland region, and can be reached by various means of transportation:

  • By air: The nearest airports are the Stockholm-Arlanda airport (184 km) and the Norrköping airport (25 km), both of which serve international and domestic flights.
  • By train: The Söderköping train station is located in the town center, and provides connections with many Swedish cities and towns, such as Stockholm, Karlstad, and Malmö.
  • By bus: The Söderköping bus station is located next to the train station, and offers regular buses to and from Norrköping and other nearby destinations.
  • By car: Söderköping is also accessible by car, and is located on the E22 highway, which connects the Swedish east coast from Malmö to Stockholm.

How to Get Around in Söderköping

Söderköping is a small and pedestrian-friendly town, and most of its attractions can be reached on foot or by bike. However, if you need to use public transportation or a taxi, you can follow these options:

  • By bus: The ÖstgötaTrafiken bus service operates several lines within Söderköping and its surroundings, such as the line 11 (Söderköping-Norrköping).
  • By taxi: The Söderköping Taxi company provides 24/7 taxi services, and can be hailed on the street, booked online, or reached by phone.

Where to Go Next from Söderköping: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore further the Östergötland region and its neighboring destinations, you can choose among these options:

  • Norrköping: This vibrant city is located 30 km north of Söderköping, and offers many cultural and outdoor attractions, such as the Carl Johans Park, the Arbetets Museum, and the Kolmården Wildlife Park.
  • Linköping: This university town is located 50 km west of Söderköping, and boasts many historic and modern sites, such as the Linköping Cathedral, the Flygvapenmuseum, and the Tinnerö Gardens.
  • Stockholm: This capital city is located 180 km north of Söderköping, and can be reached by train or by car, and offers endless possibilities for culture, shopping, entertainment, and gastronomy, such as the Royal Palace, the Vasa Museum, and the Old Town.
