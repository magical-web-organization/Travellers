Southern Gotland Travel Guide

Sweden’s largest island, Gotland, is home to the picturesque region of Southern Gotland. With its charming towns, stunning coastlines, and fascinating history, Southern Gotland offers a unique experience for travelers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, Southern Gotland has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ve compiled everything you need to know to plan your trip to Southern Gotland.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Gotland

Southern Gotland boasts a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes. Here are some places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Visby City Wall: Constructed in the 13th century, the Visby City Wall is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the best-preserved medieval city wall in Scandinavia.
  • Gotlands Museum: Explore the island’s history, archaeology, and culture at this museum.
  • Lummelunda Cave: Take a guided tour of this limestone cave and discover its natural wonders.
  • Fårö: This island is known for its rugged beauty and connection to Ingmar Bergman, who lived there and filmed several movies.

Natural Attractions of Southern Gotland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gotska Sandön National Park: This remote island in the Baltic Sea is home to sand dunes, pine forests, and a variety of wildlife, including seals and rare birds.
  • Ekstakusten: This scenic coastal area is perfect for hiking, biking, and taking in the breathtaking views of the rocky shoreline and the sea.
  • Langhammars: This nature reserve is home to unique rock formations and a seal colony.
  • Bungenäs: This former military area is now a cultural and natural reserve, with trails for hiking and biking, an art center, and a beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Gotland

Southern Gotland offers several beautiful trails for hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes:

  • The coastal trail: This 60-kilometer trail runs along the eastern coast of Gotland, offering stunning views of the sea and the island’s rugged landscape.
  • The Tjautjas trail: This 10-kilometer trail passes through the Tjautjas nature reserve and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Krusmyntagården trail: This 5-kilometer trail takes you through the Krusmyntagården herb garden and the nearby meadows.

Best Things to Do in Southern Gotland: Activities and Tours

Southern Gotland has plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy during your trip. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Kayaking & SUP: Explore the coastline and its hidden coves by kayak or stand-up paddleboard.
  • Horse riding: Discover the island’s countryside on horseback.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Get a bird’s eye view of Southern Gotland and its stunning landscapes.
  • Photography tours: Hire a professional photographer to show you the best spots for capturing the island’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Gotland

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Southern Gotland, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Visby City Wall and explore the town’s charming alleyways
2 Take a guided tour of the Lummelunda Cave and visit the Gotlands Museum
3 Hike or bike the coastal trail and enjoy the stunning views
4 Spend a day exploring Fårö and its beaches
5 Visit the Gotska Sandön National Park and look for seals and rare birds
6 Take a hot air balloon ride and explore the island from above
7 Relax at one of Southern Gotland’s beaches or go on a horse riding tour

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Gotland

Southern Gotland is known for its delicious seafood, local produce, and unique culinary traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saffron pancake (saffranspannkaka): A sweet, saffron-flavored pancake served with whipped cream and berry jam, usually eaten around Christmas time.
  • Raw herring (rökt eller gravad strömming): A traditional dish made with locally caught herring, cured in salt and sugar, and served with potatoes, onions, and sour cream.
  • Lamb stew (lammstek med rotfrukter): Slow-cooked lamb with root vegetables and herbs, usually served with lingonberry jam.

Local Beverages

  • Gotlandsdricka: A traditional non-alcoholic beverage made with water, malt, and hops, flavored with juniper berries and sugar.
  • Gotlandsbryggeri beer: A local craft brewery producing a variety of beers, including the popular Wisby Klosteröl and the award-winning Saison.

Where to Stay in Southern Gotland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Gotland offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Clarion Hotel Wisby: A stylish, four-star hotel located in the heart of Visby.
  • Hotell Gute: A family-friendly hotel located in the scenic town of Hemse.
  • Kneippbyn Resort: A popular option for families, with a water park, mini-golf, and other activities.
  • B&B in Southern Gotland: For a more authentic experience, consider staying at a cozy bed and breakfast, such as the charming Lärbro Gårdshotell or the historic Stelor B&B.

What to Buy in Southern Gotland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Southern Gotland, there are a few local products you should consider taking home:

  • Sauna whisk (bastukvast): A bundle of birch twigs used for whisking the skin during sauna sessions, a tradition in Swedish culture.
  • Knäckebröd: A type of crisp, thin bread made with rye flour, perfect for snacking or adding to your cheese plate.
  • Limestone candleholder (kalkstensljusstake): These elegant candle holders are made from the limestone found on the island, and come in all shapes and sizes.
  • Mittens and hats: Stay warm and stylish with a pair of hand-knitted mittens or a woolen hat, made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Gotland

Southern Gotland has a rich cultural heritage, with unique traditions and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Language: Swedish is the official language, but many people speak English.
  • Etiquette: Punctuality and modesty are highly valued in Swedish culture. It’s polite to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Holidays: Christmas and Midsummer are two of the biggest holidays in Sweden, and are celebrated with traditional food, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Southern Gotland

Summer (June-August) is the peak season for tourism in Southern Gotland, with warm weather and longer days. Spring (April-May) and Autumn (September-October) are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder weather. Keep in mind that many attractions and restaurants may have limited opening hours during the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Gotland

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts when visiting Southern Gotland:

Do:

  • Do use public transportation or rent a bike to explore the island.
  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the seafood and saffron pancakes.
  • Do respect traditional customs and etiquette, such as removing your shoes when entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment. Be respectful of the island’s fragile ecosystem.
  • Don’t take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Don’t be late for meetings or appointments. Punctuality is highly valued in Swedish culture.

Safety and Health

Southern Gotland is a safe and healthy place to visit, with good medical facilities and low crime rates. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions:

Safety in Southern Gotland

  • Be careful when hiking or biking on uneven terrain. Wear sturdy shoes and bring a map.
  • Be aware of the risks associated with swimming in the sea, such as strong currents and cold water temperatures.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas, such as the Visby City Wall during peak tourist season.

Health in Southern Gotland

  • Tap water is generally safe to drink, but you may prefer to bring bottled water if you have a sensitive stomach.
  • Mosquitoes and ticks can be a nuisance in the summer months. Bring insect repellent and wear protective clothing.
  • Sun protection is important, especially in the summer. Wear sunscreen and a hat to avoid sunburn and heatstroke.

Getting to Southern Gotland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Southern Gotland is easy, with several transportation options available:

  • By air: The Gotland Airport (VBY) offers direct flights from Stockholm, Visby, and other cities in Sweden. International visitors can fly to Stockholm and take a connecting flight or a ferry to Visby.
  • By ferry: Several companies offer ferry services to Visby from Stockholm and other cities.
  • By bus: Several bus companies operate routes to Visby from major cities in Sweden.

Airports Near Southern Gotland

  • Visby Airport (VBY)

Bus Stations Near Southern Gotland

  • Visby bus station

Other Options

  • Ferry: Several companies offer ferry services to Visby from Stockholm and other cities.

How to Get Around in Southern Gotland

The best way to get around in Southern Gotland is by public transportation or by renting a bike or a car. Here are some options:

  • Buses: Gotlands Resebyrå operates a network of buses that connect Visby with other parts of the island.
  • Bikes: Many hotels and local businesses rent bikes to tourists. It’s also possible to bring your own bike on the ferry.
  • Cars: Several rental car companies operate in Visby and at the airport.

Where to Go Next from Southern Gotland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Gotland is a great starting point for exploring other parts of the island, as well as nearby destinations:

  • Northern Gotland: Visit the island’s biggest city, Visby, as well as other attractions such as the Lojsta Castle and the Bunge Museum.
  • Stockholm: Take the ferry or a flight back to the Swedish capital and explore its many museums, restaurants, and historic sights.

Conclusion

Southern Gotland is a unique and beautiful destination that offers a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and stunning natural landscapes. Follow this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time on this charming island.
