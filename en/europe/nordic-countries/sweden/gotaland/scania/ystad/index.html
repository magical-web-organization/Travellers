Introduction to Ystad

Nestled in the southernmost part of Sweden, Ystad is a charming town in the heart of Scania. Known for its picturesque landscapes, rich culture, and fascinating history, Ystad is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Swedish experience. From the cobbled streets of the Old Town to the stunning vistas of the Baltic Sea, Ystad has something for everyone. This travel guide will provide you with insights into the best places to visit, things to do, and local delicacies to try in Ystad.

List of Places to Visit in Ystad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Abbey - The Abbey is one of the most popular landmarks in Ystad, and for good reason. This medieval church is one of the best-preserved examples of Gothic architecture in Scandinavia.
  • The Museum of Ystad - This museum is a must-visit for history buffs. The exhibits cover everything from the Vikings to modern-day Sweden, with a focus on the region of Scania.
  • Per Helsas Gård - Built in the 17th century, this building now houses a museum dedicated to the history of Ystad. The museum features exhibits on local industries, such as fishing and agriculture.

Natural Attractions of Ystad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ales Stenar - Ales Stenar is a mysterious and awe-inspiring monument located in the countryside near Ystad. This ancient stone formation dates back to the Viking Age and is believed to have been used for religious ceremonies.
  • Sandskogens Naturreservat - This nature reserve is the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city. With plenty of hiking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful views, Sandskogens Naturreservat is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Ystad Beach - With its clear water and soft sand, Ystad Beach is an idyllic spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing. During the summer months, this beach is a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ystad

If you enjoy hiking or biking, Ystad is the perfect destination for you. With its beautiful countryside, you’ll find plenty of scenic trails for all levels. Some popular routes include:

  • The Skåneleden Trail - This long-distance hiking trail stretches across the entire province of Scania and offers stunning views of the countryside. The trail passes through Ystad and is a great option for a day hike.
  • The Kattegattleden Trail - This 370-kilometer-long bike path starts in Helsingborg and ends in Gothenburg. If you’re an avid cyclist, you’ll love this route, which passes through Ystad and offers breathtaking coastal views.

Best Things to Do in Ystad: Activities and Tours

  • Ystad Zoo - Located just a few kilometers outside of the town, Ystad Zoo is home to a wide variety of animals, from exotic birds to big cats. This is a great destination for families with kids.
  • Glimmingehus - Visit the best-preserved medieval castle in Scandinavia and learn about the fascinating history of Glimmingehus and the people who lived there.
  • Ystad Studios - Ystad is known for its thriving film industry, and Ystad Studios is at the heart of it. Take a behind-the-scenes tour of the studios to learn more about how movies and TV shows are made.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ystad

Day 1: Explore the Old Town and visit The Abbey. Day 2: Spend the day at Ystad Beach and visit Ales Stenar. Day 3: Take a hike in Sandskogens Naturreservat and have lunch at a local restaurant. Day 4: Visit the Museum of Ystad and spend the afternoon at Glimmingehus. Day 5: Experience the local culture and customs of Ystad by attending a festival or concert. Day 6: Take a bike ride along the Kattegattleden Trail. Day 7: Visit Ystad Studios and have a farewell dinner at one of the town’s many excellent restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Ystad

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gravad lax - A traditional Swedish dish made with cured salmon, served with dill mustard sauce.
  • Wallenbergare - A classic Swedish dish made with minced veal, cream, and breadcrumbs, served with lingonberry jam and mashed potatoes.
  • Smörgåstårta - A savory cake made with layers of bread, cheese, meat, and vegetables, which is often served at special occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Snaps - A type of flavored aquavit, often served with a traditional Swedish meal.
  • Mjöd - A traditional Swedish mead made with honey and water, which has a sweet, rich flavor.

Where to Stay in Ystad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Per Helsas Gård - This historic building was once a farmhouse but has been converted into a charming guesthouse. It’s located in the heart of the Old Town and is within walking distance of many popular attractions.
  • Hotell Continental du Sud - This elegant hotel combines modern amenities with old-world charm. It’s located near the train station and provides easy access to all of Ystad’s attractions.
  • STF Ystad Hostel - If you’re on a budget, this hostel is a great option. It’s located near the beach and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Ystad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery - Ystad is known for its pottery, and you’ll find plenty of shops selling beautiful handmade ceramics.
  • Marzipan - This sweet almond paste is a popular Swedish treat, and you’ll find many variations in Ystad.
  • Lingonberry Jam - Lingonberries are a common ingredient in Swedish cuisine, and the jam makes for a delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ystad

Ystad is a town with a rich cultural heritage, and many local traditions are still alive today. One of the most important events of the year is the Ystad Sweden Jazz Festival, which takes place in August and attracts jazz musicians from all over the world. Another important event is the Ystad Medieval Festival, which is held in June and celebrates the town’s medieval history.

Best Time to Visit Ystad

The best time to visit Ystad is from May to September when the weather is mild and the days are long. During this time, you’ll also have the opportunity to attend many of the town’s festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ystad

Do:

  • Explore the Old Town - Ystad’s charming cobblestone streets are a must-see.
  • Try the local cuisine - From gravad lax to smörgåstårta, Ystad has a rich culinary tradition.
  • Attend a festival - Ystad is known for its vibrant cultural scene, so make sure to attend at least one event during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen - Even in the north, the sun can be strong during the summer months.
  • Wear shoes that are difficult to walk in - Ystad’s streets are made of cobblestones, so comfortable shoes are a must.
  • Ignore the natural beauty - From Ales Stenar to Sandskogens Naturreservat, Ystad has some of the most beautiful landscapes in all of Sweden.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ystad

Ystad is generally a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as not leaving your valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Ystad

Sweden has a high-quality healthcare system, and Ystad has several medical facilities in case of an emergency. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case you need medical treatment while abroad.

Getting to Ystad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ystad is located in the southernmost part of Sweden and is easily accessible by both train and car. If you’re flying into Sweden, you can take a train from Copenhagen or Malmö to Ystad.

Airports Near Ystad

  • Malmö Airport - Located approximately 49 kilometers from Ystad, Malmö Airport is the closest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Ystad

  • Ystad Central Station - This is the main bus station in Ystad and provides connections to other parts of the region.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Renting a car is a great way to explore the countryside around Ystad.
  • Bicycle rental - Ystad is a great town for biking, and there are several places where you can rent a bicycle for a day or more.

How to Get Around in Ystad

Ystad is a small town that is easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to venture outside the town, renting a car or bike is a great option.

Where to Go Next from Ystad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Copenhagen - Just a short train ride away, Denmark’s capital city is a must-visit destination.
  • The Österlen Region - Just north of Ystad, this region is known for its stunning landscapes and charming small towns.
  • Skåne’s West Coast - If you want to explore more of Sweden’s beautiful coastline, head west to Skåne’s West Coast.

Conclusion

Ystad is a hidden gem in the heart of Scania. From its picturesque landscapes to its rich cultural heritage, Ystad has something for everyone. With this travel guide, you’ll be able to experience the best of Ystad, from the must-visit attractions to the local delicacies you have to try. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to this charming Swedish town.
