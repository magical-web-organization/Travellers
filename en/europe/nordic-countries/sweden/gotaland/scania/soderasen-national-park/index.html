Introduction to Söderåsen National Park

Söderåsen National Park is nestled in the beautiful Scania region of Götaland, Sweden. With over 1625 hectares of unspoiled natural beauty, the park offers visitors the opportunity to explore the rich biodiversity of the Nordic countries.

Established in 2001, Söderåsen National Park is home to a vast number of native plant and animal species. The park’s biodiversity and stunning landscapes boast a variety of natural attractions that make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

This comprehensive guide will provide you with an overview of the must-visit locations, hiking and biking routes, natural wonders, local culture, and customs, and much more. Whether you’re a nature lover or an adventure seeker, Söderåsen National Park is a destination that should be on your bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Söderåsen National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Holmängen’s Old Skåne Farm

Holmängen’s Old Skåne Farm is a well-preserved 18th-century working farm that provides visitors with a glimpse into the lifestyles and traditions of Skåne’s rural population.

Visitors can learn about the daily lives of farmers in the area and be fascinated by the exhibits and collections of the historical buildings.

Kopparhatten Nature Reserve

Kopparhatten Nature Reserve is a cultural heritage site that attracts visitors with its stunning scenery and idyllic hiking trails. The reserve is known for its copper mining heritage, which dates back to the 17th century. A guided tour is available to learn all about the history of the copper mines.

Natural Attractions of Söderåsen National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Skäralid Canyon

Skäralid Canyon is the crown jewel of Söderåsen National Park. Its towering cliffs, streaming waterfalls, and lush vegetation offer visitors a breathtaking display of the natural beauty of this region.

Skäralid Canyon also provides an array of hiking trails and thrilling climbing courses. We recommend the 1.6km Skäralidsrundan Trail, which takes you on a magnificent tour of the canyon and its surrounding areas.

Uggleskulle Mountain

Uggleskulle Mountain is a popular spot for hikers, offering stunning panoramic views of the Rönneå Valley. The 3.5km trail is perfect for those looking to catch a glimpse of the beautiful scenery of Söderåsen National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Söderåsen National Park

Söderåsen Trail

The Söderåsen Trail is a 10km linear route, divided into six different stages that take visitors through some of the most picturesque landscapes Söderåsen has to offer.

The trail begins with a climb up to the top of the Söderåsen Plateau, providing an unparalleled view of the park’s beautiful forests. Later, the trail passes through the Skäralid Canyon, where visitors can see it up close and feel its awe-inspiring grandeur.

Biking Routes

Söderåsen National Park also provides thrilling mountain bike trails that are suitable for biking enthusiasts of all skill levels.

The park offers a range of bike trails, from easy to advanced, that vary in length and difficulty. We recommend the Torpåsleden, a 25km challenging route that takes visitors through the scenic and dense forest of Söderåsen.

Best Things to Do in Söderåsen National Park: Activities and Tours

Wildlife Safaris

Take a wildlife safari with an experienced guide, and spot some of the region’s native animals, including deer, elk, beavers, and birds of prey. May to September is the best time to see the animals.

Canoeing and Kayaking

Enjoy a serene paddle down the Rönneå River. Rent a canoe or kayak, and enjoy the peaceful surroundings as you explore the park’s tranquil waterways.

Week-Long Itinerary for Söderåsen National Park

Day 1: Arrival and Exploration

Arrive at Söderåsen National Park and explore the park’s visitor center. Rent a bike or take a hike to discover the park at your pace.

Day 2: Cultural Heritage Tour

Take a guided cultural heritage tour to learn about the area’s rich history, visiting Holmängen’s Old Skåne Farm and Kopparhatten Nature Reserve.

Day 3: Skäralid Canyon Tour

Begin with a hike down to Skáralid Canyon, and take a guided tour around the canyon. Be sure to visit the Kopparhatten Nature Reserve on the way out.

Day 4: Mountain Biking Day

Rent a mountain bike and spend a day exploring the park’s thrilling mountain bike trails.

Day 5: Wilflife Safari

Take a memorable wildlife safari and explore the native wildlife of Söderåsen National Park.

Day 6: Uggleskulle Mountain Hike

Go for a challenging hike up Uggleskulle mountain and be rewarded with stunning panoramic views of the park.

Day 7: Departure

Depart for your next destination, or extend your stay to experience more of the unspoiled beauty of Söderåsen National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Söderåsen National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Sample the delicious traditional dishes of Skåne, such as thinly sliced, salt-cured ham, smoked salmon, and gingerbread biscuits.

Local Beverages

Don’t forget to try the local beverages of Skåne: apple juice, elderberry flower juice, and the crisp, dry Roter Veltliner wine.

Where to Stay in Söderåsen National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Söderåsen National Park offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different travelers’ preferences and budgets.

Färingtofta Hostel

Located in the heart of Söderåsen National Park, Färingtofta Hostel offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price, with a range of facilities that cater to all travelers’ needs.

Röstånga Camping

Röstånga Camping is a family-friendly campground that provides visitors with an opportunity to experience a traditional Swedish camping holiday. The campground is located in Röstånga, just a few minutes’ drive from the park.

What to Buy in Söderåsen National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Söderåsen National Park can purchase a range of souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of their visit.

Snapphane Chocolate

Snapphane Chocolate is a locally made chocolate that comes in a variety of flavors and is available for purchase at the park visitor center. The chocolate is named after the 17th-century resistance fighters, the Snapphane people, who operated in the area.

Handmade Baskets

Handmade baskets made from locally sourced materials, such as willow and birch, are available for purchase at the Kopparhatten Nature Reserve.

Local Culture and Customs of Söderåsen National Park

The local culture of Söderåsen National Park is rooted in rural traditions and customs. The area is known for its outstanding natural beauty, and visitors should be mindful of this when exploring the park. To ensure the park remains unchanged, visitors are required to follow leave no trace principles and stick to designated trails.

Best Time to Visit Söderåsen National Park

The best time to visit Söderåsen National Park is from late spring to early autumn, from May to September. During this time, the park is full of life, and visitors can spot native wildlife and experience the lush forest landscapes in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Söderåsen National Park

Do:

  • Follow Leave No Trace principles
  • Stay on designated trails
  • Follow all park rules and regulations

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb wildlife
  • Venture off-trail or climb trees
  • Build fires or leave unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Söderåsen National Park

Söderåsen National Park is a safe destination. Visitors should follow leave no trace principles, stay on designated trails, and be mindful of any wildlife they may encounter.

Health in Söderåsen National Park

Sweden has an excellent health care system, and visitors can access medical treatment through the country’s public healthcare system. In case of a medical emergency, visitors should call 112, the local emergency hotline.

Getting to Söderåsen National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Söderåsen National Park

MalmöInternational Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 45km south of the park.

Bus Stations Near Söderåsen National Park

The nearest bus station is in Röstånga, located approximately 10km from the park.

Other Options

Driving is an excellent way to get to Söderåsen National Park. Visitors can rent a car and explore the park at their pace.

How to Get Around in Söderåsen National Park

Visitors can explore the park by foot, bike, or car. The park has plenty of footpaths and mountain bike trails to choose from.

Where to Go Next from Söderåsen National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lund

Lund is a charming university town located just a 30-minute drive from Söderåsen National Park. Explore the historic university town and visit its beautiful cathedrals, botanical garden, and museums.

Helsingborg

Helsingborg is a charming coastal town located approximately 40km west of Söderåsen National Park. It’s known for its picturesque coastline, vibrant dining scene, and cultural attractions.
