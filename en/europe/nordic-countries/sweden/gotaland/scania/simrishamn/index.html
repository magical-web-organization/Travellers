Simrishamn is one of the most picturesque towns in the Skåne region of Sweden and is known for its picturesque coastal location. It’s an excellent choice for tourists looking for a peaceful and serene getaway to beautiful nature. Simrishamn has something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and gastronomic delights. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover the best places to explore, things to do, where to stay, and more in Simrishamn, Sweden.

Introduction to Simrishamn

Simrishamn is a coastal town located in Skåne, Southern Sweden, known for its charming atmosphere, fantastic countryside, and stunning coastline with white sandy beaches. The town is quite small, with a population of just over 6000, making it perfect for a weekend trip or a longer stay to explore its surroundings. Simrishamn is a small town with a big heart - it has its own historical background, and it’s known for the traditional harvest festival each year.

List of Places to Visit in Simrishamn

If you’re looking to explore Simrishamn, here is a list of some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Glimmingehus - A medieval castle from the 15th century, it is one of the best-preserved castles in Sweden.
  • Kivik Grave Mounds - A series of prehistoric grave mounds from the Bronze Age, located just a few kilometers outside of Simrishamn.
  • Frimurarehotellet - A beautifully-preserved wooden building with a fascinating history now opened to the public as a hotel.

Natural Attractions of Simrishamn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stenshuvud National Park - A national park with walking trails, beaches, and scenic views of the beautiful coastline.
  • Södra Mellby Beach - A pristine sandy beach with crystal clear water and a beautiful backdrop of scenic cliffs.
  • Mälarhusens Strandbad - A picturesque beach location with greenery, dunes, and stunning white sand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simrishamn

  • Skaneleden Trail - Over 1,200 kilometers of cycling and hiking trails traverse through Skåne - including Simrishamn itself.

Best Things to Do in Simrishamn: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Tours - Enjoy a fishing trip on the coast and try your hand at catching salmon, cod, and other fish.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - There are several local vineyards and wine cellars that offer wine tasting tours for both locals and tourists.
  • Golf-Playing - Simrishamn also provides a picturesque golf course with amazing views along with the coast.

Week-Long Itinerary for Simrishamn

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Simrishamn:

Day Activities
1 Visit the picturesque town of Ystad and tour around

Food and Drink Scene of Simrishamn

Simrishamn has a vibrant food and drink scene with lots of fantastic local dishes, local ingredients, and locally produced beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stekt strömming - Fried herring served with lingonberry sauce is Simrishamn’s culinary specialty.
  • Kryp in malmslätt - It is Simrishamn’s version of a Fika/tea with soft cake and biscuit.
  • Rabarberpaj - Simrishamn’s signature dessert, rhubarb pie, is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Ciders and Lagers - With rich apple and pear fruit farms around Simrishamn, locally brewed drinks are available.
  • Snaps - These are traditional Swedish liqueurs with different flavors and are locally distilled around Simrishamn.

Where to Stay in Simrishamn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy guest house or a luxury hotel, Simrishamn has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are our top recommendations for where to stay:

  • Hotell Turistgården - A centrally located boutique hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Kivikstrand Badhotell - A beachfront retreat with a spa, outdoor pool, and gourmet restaurant.
  • Svedåkra Vandrarhem & Konferens - A friendly hostel surrounded by beautiful nature.

What to Buy in Simrishamn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to buy some souvenir gifts, good news is that Simrishamn has a range of high quality locally handmade goods. Some of the best products to bring back home include:

  • Traditional Skåne Weaving Craft - Pottery and textiles woven from wool and linen are the traditional handicrafts of Skåne, with a genuine unique traditional touch.
  • Sourdough Bread - Locally produced sourdough bread is the staple of Simrishamn as it has a long-standing tradition.
  • Local Wine - The vineyards around Simrishamn have a great climate for winemaking, so you can’t go wrong with a bottle of local wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Simrishamn

Simrishamn, as with the rest of Skåne, is strongly influenced by regional identity and cultural traditions, including food, clothing, language, dance, and music.

Best Time to Visit Simrishamn

The best times to visit Simrishamn are in the spring or summer months from May to September when the weather is mild, and the days are long. But if you’re looking to experience the harverst activities, the autumn months from September to November provide the best opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simrishamn

Here’s a list of things to do and not to do when in Simrishamn to enjoy a seamless trip:

Do:

  • Learn a bit of Swedish - knowing some basic Swedish phrases will help you communicate with locals and show that you respect and appreciate their culture.
  • Explore locally - wander through local shops to see what each of them has to offer, take a journey to the countryside, and explore the beauty that the region has to offer.
  • Try out the traditional cuisine - Simrishamn is especially famous for its traditional cuisine, so be sure to try the local dishes and beverages as soon as you have a chance.

Don’t:

  • Littering - respect the environment and keep your litter secure as you travel.
  • Skip on the opportunity of visiting historical landmarks - Simrishamn is a place that oozes a lot of history; you might not want to miss the chance to explore this history through the historical landmarks.
  • Disrespect the culture - be respectful of the local culture by not making fun of any customs or traditions, and be aware of any taboos or sensitivities that may apply.

Safety and Health

Simrishamn is a generally safe town to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions during your trips.

Safety in Simrishamn

  • Take care around natural hazards - Simrishamn has several natural attractions; e.g., Stenshuvud National Park . However, several cliffs and edges may be dangerous or hazardous. Please take care when visiting such sites and follow all marked rules and regulations.
  • Secure your belongings - always make sure your belongings are secure, and don’t leave your valuables unattended in risky situations.

Health in Simrishamn

There are no significant health risks when visiting Simrishamn or Skane. Be mindful of ticks; the place is known to harbor animal-borne diseases such as Lyme disease.

Getting to Simrishamn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Simrishamn is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are our top recommendations for getting there:

Airports Near Simrishamn

  • Kristianstad Airport - located about 30 kilometers from Simrishamn, providing many domestic and international flights
  • Copenhagen Airport - suits tourists carrying out international travels and is around 173 kilometers away from Simrishamn.

Bus Stations Near Simrishamn

  • Simrishamn Bus Station - this is the primary bus station in town and operates with intercity and domestic bus services

Other Options

  • Car rental - Rentals are available at Kristianstad Airport and other various locations
  • Train - Train services to Simrishamn are available from major cities and other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Simrishamn

Simrishamn is a small town that’s easily navigable by foot or bike, or car if required. You can rent bikes in the city to explore the town and surrounding nature while getting familiar with the locals.

Where to Go Next from Simrishamn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can easily access from Simrishamn:

  • Ystad - A picturesque coastal town and a popular film location with a lively art and culture scene.
  • Malmö - A bustling city with a modern twist mixed with traditional vibes.

Conclusion

Simrishamn is a must-visit stop on any trip to Skåne. With its stunning nature, rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone.
