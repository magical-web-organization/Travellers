Introduction to Oskarshamn

Located in the heart of Sweden’s Småland province, Oskarshamn is a hidden gem that boasts beautiful natural scenery alongside rich cultural landmarks. The town is known for its cozy harbor, which offers picturesque views of the Baltic Sea and the surrounding archipelago. While relatively small, Oskarshamn is packed with things to do and see, making it a perfect destination for travelers looking to explore Sweden off the beaten track.

List of Places to Visit in Oskarshamn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Döderhultarns Museum

Döderhultarn is a world-renowned woodcarver from Oskarshamn that created the work of art known as “St. George and the Dragon”. The museum, housed in the artist’s former workshop, showcases a collection of the woodcarver’s carvings, providing visitors insight into his life and work.

Sjöfartshuset

Sjöfartshuset is a maritime museum situated in the beautiful harbor of Oskarshamn. The museum displays a variety of exhibits from the village’s seafaring past, including a collection of old boats, models of WWII battleships, and navigation equipment. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the building or wander through its exhibits at their own pace.

Ekeberg Wildlife Sanctuary

Ekeberg Wildlife Sanctuary, located just a short drive from Oskarshamn, is an extensive nature reserve home to many different species of animals, including deer, elk, wild boars, and wolves. The reserve also features an exciting adventure trail where visitors can get a taste of the local wilderness.

Natural Attractions of Oskarshamn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blå Jungfrun National Park

Blå Jungfrun National Park is a popular destination for lovers of natural beauty. The park is home to the picturesque island of Blå Jungfrun, known for its narrow coastal paths that meander through boulder fields and deep pine forests. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and boat tours that give a unique perspective of the archipelago.

Badholmen Nature Reserve

Just a short walk from the heart of Oskarshamn lies the Badholmen Nature Reserve, known for its stunning walking trails that provide panoramic views of the surrounding sea. The reserve is also home to a beautiful beach, perfect for a relaxing afternoon of swimming or sunbathing.

Astrid Lindgren’s World

Astrid Lindgren’s World, located 70 kilometers from Oskarshamn, is one of Sweden’s most beloved amusement parks. The park offers visitors a chance to explore the world of the famous Swedish author, whose characters, including Pippi Longstocking, have become icons of popular culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oskarshamn

Oskarshamn is the perfect place to explore Sweden’s beautiful countryside by foot or bicycle. The following are our top recommendations for hikes and bike rides in the area:

  • Hike the coastal path from Badholmen Nature Reserve to the Stensö rest area, where you can relax and soak up the beautiful views of the Baltic Sea.

  • Hike through the heart of the Ekeberg Wildlife Sanctuary, following one of the trails that leads through the reserve’s deep forests and peaceful lakes.

  • Bike through the heart of Oskarshamn, which offers a beautiful stroll through the village’s historic streets, along the harbor, and past the numerous local parks.

Best Things to Do in Oskarshamn: Activities and Tours

With its scenic views and rich cultural landmarks, there’s no shortage of fun things to do in Oskarshamn. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Take a boat trip to the picturesque island of Blå Jungfrun National Park, during which you’ll see unique views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Visit the Saturday market in the city center, where you can buy locally-sourced fruits, vegetables, and other items.

  • Explore the village’s hidden corners by taking a walking tour of Oskarshamn, where you’ll learn about the town’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oskarshamn

For visitors looking for a week-long adventure in Oskarshamn, try the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and relaxation in Oskarshamn. Day 2: Visit the Döderhultarn Museum and explore the Söder Quarter. Day 3: Hike the coastal path from Badholmen Nature Reserve to Stensö rest area and relax at the beach. Day 4: Visit the maritime museum and enjoy a boat tour of Oskarshamn harbor. Day 5: Head over to the Ekeberg Wildlife Sanctuary and enjoy the picturesque hiking trails and wildlife viewing. Day 6: Day trip to Astrid Lindgren’s World. Day 7: Departure from Oskarshamn.

Food and Drink Scene of Oskarshamn

Must-Try Dishes

Oskarshamn boasts a traditional Swedish cuisine, characterized by its focus on fresh, local ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include:

  • Smoked salmon, a locally sourced delicacy that you can find at most restaurants in Oskarshamn.

  • Lingonberry jam, a staple in Swedish cuisine that you’ll find in most breakfast buffets.

  • Meatballs with cream sauce, a dish you can find at most Swedish restaurants in Oskarshamn.

Local Beverages

Oskarshamn and its surroundings offer a variety of locally sourced drinks that should not be missed. Some of the most popular local beverages include:

  • Svartvinbärslikör, a black currant liqueur that is the perfect after-dinner drink.

  • Jägaröl, an adventurous craft beer named after the local Ekeberg Wildlife Sanctuary.

Where to Stay in Oskarshamn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options available in Oskarshamn, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious beach resorts. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Best Western Sjöfartshotellet: a beautiful seaside hotel that offers a variety of comfortable rooms and suites, along with an on-site restaurant and beach club.

  • STF Vandrarhem Oscar: a budget-friendly hostel located right in the heart of Oskarshamn, just a short walk from some of the town’s top attractions.

  • Oscarsgården: a stylish guesthouse featuring beautifully decorated rooms, a garden, and a unique homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Oskarshamn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir from your trip to Oskarshamn, consider some of these locally sourced items:

  • Hand-carved woodwork by Döderhultarn, produced in the artist’s original workshop.

  • A bottle of locally produced Kvastmosse wine, a delicious white wine made from grapes grown on a small vineyard in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Oskarshamn

Oskarshamn is a proud and friendly community that values personal relationships and strong social bonds. It is customary to greet others with a handshake or a hug, depending on the situation, and to accept any invitations to coffee or meals.

Best Time to Visit Oskarshamn

The best time to visit Oskarshamn is during the summer months, from mid-June to early September. The warm weather and long hours of daylight allow visitors to fully enjoy all the village has to offer, from outdoor adventures to leisurely mornings at the beach.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oskarshamn

Do:

  • Show respect for the town’s local customs;
  • Learn some basic Swedish phrases to help you communicate with locals;
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks;
  • Respect the natural beauty of the area and take care not to litter.

Don’t:

  • Be overly loud in public spaces;
  • Use public transportation in an intoxicated state;
  • Fail to respect signs indicating private property;
  • Walk or bike on designated ski trails during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oskarshamn

Oskarshamn is generally a safe and friendly community. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take care not to leave valuables out in public view.

Health in Oskarshamn

Sweden has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors to Oskarshamn can expect to receive excellent medical care should the need arise. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, and travelers with pre-existing conditions should be sure to bring all relevant medical information and contact information for their physicians.

Getting to Oskarshamn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oskarshamn

The nearest major airport to Oskarshamn is the Kallinge Airport, located approximately 133 kilometers away. Visitors can also fly into Stockholm’s Arlanda Airport, which is located approximately 350 kilometers away and offers frequent bus and train connections to Oskarshamn.

Bus Stations Near Oskarshamn

Oskarshamn is served by several bus stations within the town, making it easy for travelers to get around the area and access neighboring towns and villages.

Other Options

For those looking to explore the area on foot or by bike, Oskarshamn is easily accessible from most locations in the region with excellent train and bus connections.

How to Get Around in Oskarshamn

Oskarshamn is a relatively small town and can be explored easily on foot or by bike. However, for those who prefer public transportation, there are several bus routes connecting the town’s different neighborhoods and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Oskarshamn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oskarshamn is surrounded by beautiful countryside, charming villages, and exciting cities that are easily accessible by bus or train. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • Vimmerby, a lively market town located 70 kilometers from Oskarshamn and known for being the birthplace of Astrid Lindgren.

  • Kalmar, a historic town located 70 kilometers from Oskarshamn and home to the famous Kalmar Castle.

Conclusion

Oskarshamn is a unique blend of natural and cultural beauty, offering visitors a chance to explore Sweden’s stunning countryside and rich historical heritage. Whether you prefer relaxing days at the beach or exploring the town’s many museums and landmarks, Oskarshamn has something for everyone.
