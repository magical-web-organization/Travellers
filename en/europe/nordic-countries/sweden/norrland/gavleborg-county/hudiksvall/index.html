Introduction to Hudiksvall

Located in Gävleborg County, in the Norrland region of Sweden, Hudiksvall is a quaint town with rich cultural and natural attractions. It is set on the Gulf of Bothnia, and it’s just a few hours’ drive from Stockholm.

List of Places to Visit in Hudiksvall

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a culture aficionado, Hudiksvall has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Hudiksvall:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hälsingegårdar: These are cultural heritages in Hudiksvall with spectacular unique and decorative wall paintings. Explore the museums inside them to have an in-depth look at their history.
  • Hudiksvalls Museum: Unveil the history and culture of Hudiksvall through art installations, photographs, and documents.
  • Kungsgården Gästgiveri Museum: This former guesthouse is a cultural monument that offers a glimpse into the exciting history of Kungsgården and the surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Hudiksvall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Norra Berget: Hiking, jogging, wildlife watching, or excursions – there are many activities that allow visitors to explore Norra Berget. The view from the top is spectacular.
  • Lillfjärdssjö: This is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, kayaking, and water sports. It’s also a great area for hiking or jogging.
  • Delångersån River: This river runs through the town, providing excellent opportunities for fishing, canoeing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudiksvall

Hudiksvall is surrounded by beautiful nature, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • The Trolldalen Nature Reserve Trail: This is a family-friendly stroll in the forest, where you can enjoy watching birds, bumblebees, and butterflies.
  • The Skålsjögården Trail: It’s a 7-kilometer hike through scenic terrain that ends at Skålsjögården, where you can relax, have a picnic, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Swedish countryside.
  • The Tallberg Trail: This is a 15-kilometer scenic mountain bike trail that winds through the Nordic forest.

Best Things to Do in Hudiksvall: Activities and Tours

  • Guided tour of the Hälsingegårdar: Explore the historical farmhouses and learn about their unique decorations and architecture.
  • Horseback riding: Take a leisurely trip through the forests and the countryside.
  • Fishing: Hone your fishing skills or book an exciting guided fishing tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hudiksvall

Day 1: Visit Hudiksvalls Museum and the Kungsgården Gästgiveri Museum. Day 2: Hike the Trolldalen Trail and visit the Norra Berget. Day 3: Discover the Lillfjärdssjö and cool off in its pristine waters. Day 4: Visit the Hälsingegårdar and connect with Hudiksvall’s cultural history. Day 5: Bike the Tallberg Trail and enjoy the breathtaking views. Day 6: Relax with a fishing day out in the Delångersån River. Day 7: Take a day trip to explore the surrounding areas of Hudiksvall.

Food and Drink Scene of Hudiksvall

Swedish cuisine is influenced heavily by the sea and the fertile, agricultural land. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Råraka: Crispy and buttery potato pancakes served with bacon and lingonberry jam.
  • Raggmunk: Potato pancakes served with bacon and a creamy sauce.
  • Gravlax: Fresh salmon cured with salt, sugar, and dill.

Local Beverages

  • Snaps: This is Sweden’s national drink, a strong alcoholic beverage usually served cold and taken as a shot. Hudiksvall boasts some of the best microbreweries where you can sample local crafts.

Where to Stay in Hudiksvall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hudiksvall has many accommodations to suit travelers’ preferences and budget, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels.

  • Quality Hotel Statt: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Hudiksvall.
  • Hostel Hudiksvall: A budget-friendly and cozy option in the town center.
  • Högbo Brukshotell: A unique and luxurious hotel located in a scenic area of Sandviken.

What to Buy in Hudiksvall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Hudiksvall with these unique souvenirs:

  • Hälsingland’s Tiles: These hand-painted tiles feature colorful images inspired by the Hälsingegårdar.
  • Hand-crafted Wooden Birdhouses: These birdhouses are made by local artisans and are available in different shapes and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudiksvall

Swedes are known to be friendly and welcoming, and Hudiksvall is no exception. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Swedes value punctuality and timeliness, so don’t be late for any appointments.
  • Removing shoes before entering a person’s home or a public indoor is a common custom.

Best Time to Visit Hudiksvall

The best time to visit Hudiksvall is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and the daylight hours are long enough to indulge in outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudiksvall

Do:

  • Do try the traditional Swedish cuisine, including the local delicacies.
  • Do explore Hudiksvall’s natural surroundings, including the forests, rivers, and mountains.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the wildlife when enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Don’t forget to remove your shoes when entering indoor spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hudiksvall

Hudiksvall is a safe place to travel to, with low crime rates. However, common sense and standard safety precautions should always be observed. Be aware of your surroundings and belongings when in crowded areas.

Health in Hudiksvall

Sweden has a well-functioning health care system. If you need medical attention while in Hudiksvall, you can visit the Hudiksvall Hospital.

Getting to Hudiksvall: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hudiksvall

Hudiksvall has a small airport that serves regional flights. Another nearby airport is Sundsvall-Timrå Airport, which is about an hour and a half drive away.

Bus Stations Near Hudiksvall

Hudiksvall Bus Station is the town’s primary transportation hub, serving regional and intercity bus routes.

Other Options

It’s also possible to rent a car or take a train from Stockholm to Hudiksvall.

How to Get Around in Hudiksvall

Hudiksvall is a small town that you can easily walk or bike around. For longer distances, there’s the possibility to rent a car, hail a taxi, or use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Hudiksvall: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sundsvall: The charming town of Sundsvall is located about 100 kilometers south of Hudiksvall and is well-loved for its bustling squares, museums, and cafes.
  • Ljusdal: Ljusdal is a cozy village located about 60 kilometers northwest of Hudiksvall, surrounded by rolling hills, forests, and rivers. It’s a popular spot for hikers and skiers.

Conclusion

Hudiksvall is a hidden gem in Sweden, with a wealth of cultural and natural attractions just waiting to be explored. From the historical farmhouses to the scenic hiking trails, the welcoming locals, and exquisite cuisine, Hudiksvall has something for everyone.
