Introduction to Jokkmokk

Jokkmokk is a small town located in Norrbotten County, in Norrland - the northernmost region of Sweden. It is a historically and culturally rich town and one of the oldest in Norrland. Jokkmokk is known for its unique Sami culture, wild nature, and ancient traditions.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Jokkmokk by providing all the information about the best places to visit, must-try dishes, where to stay, and other useful tips to make your trip memorable.

List of Places to Visit in Jokkmokk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ájtte Museum: A great place to learn about the history and culture of the Sami people. The museum has a comprehensive collection of artifacts, traditional clothes, and other objects that showcase the Sami way of life.
  • Jokkmokk Church: Built-in 1886, the church is a great example of the wooden churches that are unique to Norrland. It is located in the town center and is one of the main landmarks of Jokkmokk.
  • Vitberget Hill: A historical place where you can see the remains of fortifications built during World War II. The hill also offers a great view of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Jokkmokk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sarek National Park: A beautiful national park located in the heart of the Swedish Lapland. It is known for its untouched wilderness, stunning mountain ranges, and wild animals like moose, reindeer, and bears.
  • Stora Sjöfallet National Park: Another national park that is a must-visit in Jokkmokk. It is known for its glaciers, waterfalls, and hiking trails that offer amazing views of the surrounding area.
  • Laponia World Heritage Site: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that encompasses four national parks and is home to the traditional Sami reindeer husbandry sites. It is a great place to learn about Sami culture and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jokkmokk

Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Jokkmokk are:

  • Kungsleden: Also known as the King’s trail, Kungsleden is a 440-kilometer long hiking trail that stretches from Abisko to Hemavan. It is divided into four sections, and each section offers a unique experience of the Swedish Lapland.
  • STF Ritsem: A 13-kilometer long hiking trail that takes you from Ritsem to Akka’s cabin. It is a great trail for hikers who want to explore the wilderness of the Swedish Lapland.
  • Fjällräven Classic: A 110-kilometer long hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful wilderness of the Swedish Lapland. It is a challenging trail and requires proper preparation and equipment.

Best Things to Do in Jokkmokk: Activities and Tours

  • Sami Reindeer Sledding: A unique experience that allows you to ride a sled pulled by reindeer and learn about the traditional Sami way of life.
  • Northern Lights Tour: Jokkmokk is known for its clear skies and is one of the best places to see the Northern Lights. There are various tour operators that offer Northern Lights excursions.
  • Snowmobile Safari: A thrilling way to explore the wilderness of the Swedish Lapland. There are various tours available that take you through the snow-covered forests and frozen lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of Jokkmokk

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gáhkku: A traditional Sami bread made from ground reindeer meat and wheat flour.
  • Renstek: A popular dish made from reindeer meat served with potatoes, lingonberry jam, and sauce.
  • Sautéed Mushrooms: Jokkmokk and the surrounding area has an abundance of mushrooms, and sautéed mushrooms are a local delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Cloudberry Juice: A non-alcoholic drink made from the wild berries that grow in the Swedish Lapland.
  • Birch Sap: A refreshing drink made from the sap of birch trees.

Where to Stay in Jokkmokk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jokkmokk Hostel and Turiststation: A hostel located in the town center. It offers affordable accommodation and is a great place to meet other travelers.
  • Hotel Jokkmokk: A 4-star hotel located in the town center. It offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities like a sauna and fitness center.
  • Guesthouse Sapmi: A guesthouse located just outside of Jokkmokk. It offers a unique experience of staying in a traditional Sami lavvu (tent) and learning about Sami culture.

What to Buy in Jokkmokk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Reindeer Products: Jokkmokk is known for its reindeer products like leather gloves, hats, and bags.
  • Sami Crafts: There are various shops in Jokkmokk that sell traditional Sami crafts like jewelry and clothes.
  • Local Food Products: You can bring home local food products like cloudberry jam, birch syrup, and smoked reindeer meat.

Local Culture and Customs of Jokkmokk

Jokkmokk has a unique culture that is influenced by the Sami people, who have been living in the area for thousands of years. The Sami people have their language, culture, and way of life, which is different from the Swedish culture. It is essential to respect their customs and traditions while visiting Jokkmokk.

Best Time to Visit Jokkmokk

The best time to visit Jokkmokk is from December to April when the Northern Lights are visible, and the town transforms into a winter wonderland. The summer months from June to August are also a great time to visit when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jokkmokk

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress warmly and for the weather
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Learn about the Sami culture

Don’t:

  • Disturb the local wildlife
  • Leave any trash or litter in the natural areas
  • Take any souvenirs from the national parks

Safety and Health

Safety in Jokkmokk

Jokkmokk is a safe place to visit. However, it is important to be prepared for the harsh weather conditions and to follow the safety guidelines of the tour operators while participating in outdoor activities like hiking and snowmobiling.

Health in Jokkmokk

There are medical facilities available in Jokkmokk, but it is important to have proper travel insurance before visiting. It is also recommended to bring proper medication and consult a doctor before participating in any physical activities.

Getting to Jokkmokk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jokkmokk

  • Kiruna Airport: Located 138 km away from Jokkmokk, it has regular flights from Stockholm.

Bus Stations Near Jokkmokk

  • Jokkmokk Bus Station: Located in the town center, it has regular bus connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great option to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.
  • Train: The closest train station is in Murjek, 80 km away from Jokkmokk.

How to Get Around in Jokkmokk

The best way to get around in Jokkmokk is by car or public transport. There are buses that connect the town center to the nearby attractions and towns. Some tour operators also offer transportation services for outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Jokkmokk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kiruna: The northernmost town in Sweden, known for its iron ore mine and Ice Hotel.
  • Lycksele: A small town known for its wildlife park.
