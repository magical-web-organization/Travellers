Introduction to Kiruna

Kiruna, a small town nestled in the Lapland region of Sweden, is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, unique cultural experiences, and endless outdoor activities. Whether you’re seeking a relaxing vacation or an adventure-filled trip, Kiruna has something for everyone. With its proximity to the Arctic Circle, the town enjoys long summer days and magical winter nights under the Northern Lights.

List of Places to Visit in Kiruna

Here are the must-visit attractions in Kiruna:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kiruna Church: A beautiful wooden church built in 1912, known for the unique architecture and stained glass windows depicting scenes from the Bible.
  • Kiruna City Hall: The first city hall made entirely of wood in Sweden, completed in 1958 and declared a national monument in 2001.
  • Kiruna Museum: An indoor exhibition showcasing the history and culture of Kiruna and surrounding areas, from the Sami people to the mining industry.

Natural Attractions of Kiruna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kungsleden Trail: One of the most famous hiking trails in Sweden, spanning over 440 kilometers and offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and lakes.
  • Aurora Sky Station: An observation point located on Mount Nuolja, offering stunning views of the Northern Lights and the surrounding mountain ranges.
  • Abisko National Park: Home to several hiking trails, a picturesque canyon, and the iconic Lapporten mountain gateway offering spectacular views of the northern horizon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kiruna

Kiruna is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with awe-inspiring trails such as:

  • Kungsleden Trail
  • Ráhpavuobme Trail: A 12-kilometer walking trail across the Arctic tundra, providing stunning views and insights into Sami culture.
  • Nikkaluokta – Kebnekaise: A scenic route leading to the highest mountain in Sweden, Kebnekaise.

Best Things to Do in Kiruna: Activities and Tours

To make the most of your stay in Kiruna, try out these activities and tours:

  • Snowmobiling: Explore the Arctic wilderness on a snowmobile and witness the rugged beauty of the Lapland region.
  • Husky Sledding: Experience the thrill of driving your own husky sled through Lapland’s snow-covered forests and landscapes.
  • Ice Fishing: Try your hand at ice fishing, where you can catch Arctic char, trout, and pike while surrounded by stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kiruna

Here’s our recommended itinerary for a week-long trip to Kiruna:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival in Kiruna. Visit Kiruna Church and City Hall.
Day 2 Take a day-trip to Abisko National Park.
Day 3 Hike the Kungsleden Trail or Ráhpavuobme Trail.
Day 4 Snowmobile tour or dog-sledding excursion.
Day 5 Visit the Kiruna Museum and learn about the area’s indigenous Sami culture.
Day 6 Take a trip to the Aurora Sky Station for spectacular views of the Northern Lights.
Day 7 Departure from Kiruna.

Food and Drink Scene of Kiruna

Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages in Kiruna:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meatballs: A traditional dish made with reindeer meat and served with lingonberry sauce.
  • Arctic Char: A popular fish dish, caught fresh from nearby lakes.
  • Gompa: A traditional Sami dish made with reindeer blood, flour, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Cloudberry Juice: Made from the delicious fruit native to Lapland, this juice is a must-try.
  • Beer from Lapland Brewery: Sample some of the unique beers crafted by the Lapland Brewery.

Where to Stay in Kiruna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kiruna offers a variety of accommodation options, including cozy cottages, upscale hotels, and affordable hostels.

  • Icehotel: A unique quirky hotel, which is made entirely out of ice and snow.
  • Camp Ripan: A cabin-style hotel and spa, nestled in nature and offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • STF Kiruna Hostel: An affordable option for those traveling on a budget, located in the city center and offering easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Kiruna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are the best souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Kiruna:

  • Sami Handcrafts: Beautiful handmade Sami crafts such as jewelry, clothing, and traditional tools made by the indigenous Sami people.
  • Lapland Tea: A unique herbal tea made with natural ingredients found only in Lapland.
  • Reindeer Skin: Bring home a piece of Lapland with a soft and cozy reindeer skin rug or blanket.

Local Culture and Customs of Kiruna

Kiruna is a unique blend of modernity and tradition, with strong Sami influences and the mining industry playing a major role in the town’s economy.

  • Sami Culture: The indigenous Sami people have a strong presence in the area and their traditions and customs are visible in the local art, music, and cuisine.
  • Mining Industry: The town’s economy is driven by the mining industry, with the Kiruna mine being the largest underground iron ore mine in the world.

Best Time to Visit Kiruna

The best time to visit Kiruna depends on your interests and the activities you plan to do.

  • Winter (December-February): Ideal for witnessing the Northern Lights and enjoying winter sports such as skiing, snowmobiling, and dog-sledding.
  • Summer (June-August): Ideal for hiking, fishing, and experiencing the midnight sun.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kiruna

Here are the things to do and avoid in Kiruna:

Do:

  • Respect the Sami culture: Learn about the local traditions and customs of the Sami people and respect them.
  • Pack for the weather: Bring warm clothes and appropriate footwear for the winter as temperatures can drop severely.
  • Book accommodations and activities in advance: Kiruna can get crowded during peak season, so it’s best to book in advance.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife: Observe the wildlife from a safe distance and do not disturb their natural habitat.
  • Trash the environment: Leave no traces of garbage in the nature trails or parks.
  • Ignore the weather warnings: Pay attention to weather warnings and avoid going out during extreme conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kiruna

Kiruna is a safe town for tourists. However, it’s essential to keep yourself informed about the weather conditions, especially during the winter season.

Health in Kiruna

Kiruna has excellent medical facilities and healthcare services. However, it’s recommended to have adequate travel insurance while traveling.

Getting to Kiruna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kiruna

Kiruna Airport is the closest airport to the town and is accessible through domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kiruna

Kiruna has bus stations that connect to other towns and cities across Sweden and Norway.

Other Options

You can also reach Kiruna by car, train, or charter bus.

How to Get Around in Kiruna

Kiruna offers various transportation options for tourists:

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in the city center, and the fare is determined by the distance.
  • Buses: Public buses operate within the town and its suburbs.
  • Bicycle: Bicycles can be rented from various rental shops in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Kiruna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore from Kiruna:

  • Jukkasjärvi: A village located 17 kilometers from Kiruna, and famous for the IceHotel.
  • Luleå: A coastal town located 390 kilometers from Kiruna, known for its music, arts, and culture.
  • Tornio: A Finnish town located 56 kilometers from Kiruna, famous for its iconic twin cities.

Conclusion

Kiruna is a magical town located in the heart of Swedish Lapland, offering a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and natural wonders. From hiking the Kungsleden Trail and witnessing the Northern Lights to sampling local delicacies and exploring indigenous Sami culture, Kiruna has it all. So pack your bags and get ready to experience the wonders of the Arctic North in this beautiful town!
