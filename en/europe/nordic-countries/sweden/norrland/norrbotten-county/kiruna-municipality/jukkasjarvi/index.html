Introduction to Jukkasjärvi

Jukkasjärvi, a small village situated in the beautiful Kiruna Municipality, in Norrbotten County, is famous for its Ice Hotel and Northern Lights. Located in the heart of the Nordic countries, Jukkasjärvi is a winter wonderland for travelers seeking adventure. With its pristine nature, rich cultural heritage, and unique experiences, Jukkasjärvi promises to offer an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Jukkasjärvi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jukkasjärvi Church, an iconic wooden church that dates back to 1607.
  • Nutti Sámi Siida, a museum that showcases Sami culture and traditions.
  • Sami camp Giron, a reconstructed ancient Sami camp with reindeer herds.
  • Karesuando Church, a beautiful wooden church located at the Swedish-Finnish border that dates back to 1827.

Natural Attractions of Jukkasjärvi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Torne River, the crystal clear river with its surrounding scenic beauty is perfect for a peaceful stroll or a picnic.
  • Aurora Sky Station, located on Mount Nuolja, it is the perfect spot to watch the Northern Lights.
  • Abisko National Park, the best place for wilderness hiking, skiing, and wildlife watching in the Lapland region.
  • Kiruna, the largest municipality in Sweden, offering visitors an experience of Sami culture, mining tours, and various outdoor activities throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jukkasjärvi

  • The King’s Trail (Kungsleden), a 440 km long-distance trail from Abisko to Hemavan that takes hikers through serene landscapes, wildflower fields, and fantastic mountain views.
  • The Midnight Sun Trail, a shorter hike, that runs along the Torne River, is the perfect way to enjoy the long summer days.
  • The Fatbike Route, a 20 km bike trail, takes you through the frozen river and snow-covered forests while enjoying the beautiful scenery of Jukkasjärvi.

Best Things to Do in Jukkasjärvi: Activities and Tours

  • Ice Hotel, a surreal hotel made entirely of ice that you can stay in.
  • Snowmobile Tours, a thrilling ride across the Arctic wilderness of Lapland.
  • Husky Safari, an unforgettable experience of being pulled on sleds by charming dogs through the most serene lapland scenery.
  • Northern Lights Tour, a guided tour to see the magnificent Aurora Borealis in a comfortable snowmobile-pulled sleigh.
  • Ice Fishing, a winter activity that involves drilling a hole into frozen lake and catching fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jukkasjärvi

Day 1: Arrival in Jukkasjärvi, check into the Ice Hotel, and explore the village.

Day 2: Take a snowmobile tour across the Arctic wilderness of Lapland, and experience the northern lights.

Day 3: Visit the Abisko National Park and take a guided tour to explore the wilderness, go hiking, or skiing.

Day 4: Go on a Husky Safari, an unforgettable experience of being pulled on sleds by charming dogs through the most serene lapland scenery.

Day 5: Visit the Nutti Sámi Siida, a museum showcasing Sami culture and traditions.

Day 6: Take an Ice Fishing tour and enjoy the winter scenery.

Day 7: End the trip with a visit to Karesuando church, a beautiful wooden church located at the Swedish-Finnish border, and departure from Jukkasjärvi.

Food and Drink Scene of Jukkasjärvi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic Char, a local fish of the region that can be found in various restaurants.
  • Reindeer Stew (Janssons Frestelse), a traditional Swedish dish that is popular in Jukkasjärvi.
  • Cloudberry Jam (Hjortron), a deliciously sweet jam made out of berries found in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Norrlands Guld, a lager brewed in the region.
  • Gotlandsdricka, a traditional beverage made from malted barley and hops.

Where to Stay in Jukkasjärvi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ice Hotel, a stunning ice hotel that offers a once-in-a-lifetime experience of staying in a room made entirely out of ice.
  • Camp Ripan, a luxury hotel with cozy rooms, a sauna, and an outdoor hot tub.
  • Jukkasjärvi Lodge, a family-run lodge that offers personal service, comfortable rooms, and traditional Sami cuisine.

What to Buy in Jukkasjärvi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sami handicrafts, like traditional Sami knives, jewelry, and clothes, are great souvenirs to bring home.
  • Local berries and jams make excellent gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Jukkasjärvi

Jukkasjärvi has a rich Sami culture, and visitors can learn more about the traditions, music, and food of the indigenous Sámi people. It is common to greet and thank people in Sami when you are visiting the region.

Best Time to Visit Jukkasjärvi

The best time to visit Jukkasjärvi is from November to April, as it is the winter season that offers guests the chance to experience northern lights, snowmobiling, skiing, and other winter activities. The months of June and July are also popular for the Midnight Sun phenomenon.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jukkasjärvi

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather.
  • Book winter activities and accommodations in advance.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Follow travel rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Northern Lights or Sami culture.
  • Litter or disturb the environment.
  • Forget to carry warm clothes and gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jukkasjärvi

Jukkasjärvi is relatively safe, but visitors should be aware of the cold and snow. Dress appropriately for the weather conditions and carry essential gear like proper shoes, warm jackets, and gloves. When participating in winter activities, always follow safety instructions, and never venture off into unmarked areas.

Health in Jukkasjärvi

Visitors should be aware of the harsh climate of Jukkasjärvi. It is essential to carry any necessary medications and have comprehensive health insurance. As the region is vast, always carry a first aid kit when going for outdoor activities.

Getting to Jukkasjärvi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jukkasjärvi:

  • Kiruna Airport (KRN)

Bus Stations Near Jukkasjärvi:

  • Kiruna Bus Station

Other Options:

Jukkasjärvi can be reached via train, but the majority of visitors fly to Kiruna and take a bus or taxi to the village.

How to Get Around in Jukkasjärvi

To explore the village and attractions, walking or bicycles are the best options. For exploring the surrounding areas, snowmobiles, and four-wheel drive vehicles are popular options.

Where to Go Next from Jukkasjärvi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kiruna, the largest municipality in Sweden, offers experiences of Sami culture, mining tours, and various outdoor activities throughout the year.
  • Narvik, a city in Norway, located by the sea, offers whale watching, snorkeling, and skiing.
  • Kebnekaise Mountain, the highest mountain in Sweden, is a popular destination for hiking and skiing.

Conclusion

Jukkasjärvi offers a unique experience to travelers seeking adventure, culture, and nature. From Ice Hotels and Northern Lights to Sami culture and wilderness hiking, Jukkasjärvi has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, plan your perfect getaway to Jukkasjärvi and experience the magic of Lapland.
