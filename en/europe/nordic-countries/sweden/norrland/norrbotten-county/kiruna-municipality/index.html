Introduction to Kiruna Municipality

Kiruna Municipality is a sprawling region located in Norrbotten County, in the far north of Sweden. This vast territory encompasses the urban center of Kiruna, the world-renowned Icehotel, and a large portion of the Lapland area. It is an ideal destination for anyone seeking to experience the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Sweden.

List of Places to Visit in Kiruna Municipality

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For the cultural enthusiast, Kiruna offers a wealth of museums and historical places to explore. Visit the Kiruna Church, which is one of the largest wooden buildings in Sweden, built-in 1912. Learn about the region’s mining history at the Kiruna City Museum. Or, delve into the Sámi culture at the Jukkasjärvi Sámi Camp.

Natural Attractions of Kiruna Municipality: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its unique location above the Arctic Circle, Kiruna Municipality offers a world of adventure for outdoor enthusiasts. From snowmobiling to dog-sledding, skiing, and snowshoeing, the winter activities here are endless. During the summer months, enjoy the beauty of the Midnight Sun, explore the hiking trails of the Abisko National Park or canoe on the Torne River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kiruna Municipality

Are you looking for some exercise or adventure? Then Kiruna’s hiking and biking routes will excite you. The Abisko National Park is a magnificent area for both experienced trekkers and beginners. The Kungsleden Trail (The King’s Trail) is one of the most famous routes in the world, stretching more than 500 km across arctic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kiruna Municipality: Activities and Tours

Get an incredible insight into the mysterious Northern Lights, take a reindeer sleigh ride experience, or take a snowmobile tour. Some tours provide opportunities to learn about the Sámi culture and history, while others focus solely on outdoor adventures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kiruna Municipality

Day 1: Arrive in Kiruna and explore the town’s cultural heritage. Day 2: Visit the Abisko National Park and hike through scenic landscapes. Day 3: Take a dog-sledding tour and picnicking under the Northern Lights. Day 4: Try out snowmobiling towards the Swedish mountains Day 5: Visit the famous Icehotel and sleep in ice rooms. Day 6: Take a reindeer sleigh ride around the town of Jukkasjärvi. Day 7: Depart from Kiruna after experiencing Arctic wilderness and cultural experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Kiruna Municipality

Must-Try Dishes

Experience the traditional Sámi cuisine by trying the smoked reindeer meat, cured fish, and the Blanda Ball meatball filled with flavors. Västerbotten, the iconic cheese of northern Sweden, is also a must-try. If you love sweets and berries, try the traditional blueberry pie.

Local Beverages

The lingonberry juice, Sámi tea, and the cloudberry liqueur are some of the typical local beverages to try while visiting Kiruna Municipality. The local brewery, Lapplands Bryggeri, offers both beers and ciders made from local wild berries and fruits.

Where to Stay in Kiruna Municipality: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to affordable hostels, Kiruna offers a variety of accommodations to fit your budget and preferences. Stay at the Ice Hotel for a truly unique experience of sleeping in a room made of ice, or choose from the hotels located in the town center or the countryside. For a more authentic experience, stay in a traditional Swedish cabin with the Aurora Sky Station.

What to Buy in Kiruna Municipality: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home something unique and authentic from your visit to Kiruna. The most popular souvenirs include reindeer leather products, Sami handicrafts, jewelry and fine art galleries. Try some Sámi-made cheese or jams and berries from the local farmers’ market. And don’t forget to pick up a bottle of cloudberry liqueur for yourself or to give as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Kiruna Municipality

The Kiruna Municipality is home to both Swedish and Sámi communities, which plays an important role in local customs and culture. The Sámi people have unique customs and traditions, which are expressed through their handicrafts, music and clothing. Respect the culture and customs by taking part in some of the local guided tours, festivals, and Sámi museums in Kiruna.

Best Time to Visit Kiruna Municipality

The best time to visit Kiruna Municipality is from December to April, when the area is covered in snow and the Northern Lights are visible. During this period, you can enjoy activities such as dog sledding, snowmobiling, skiing and take a tour of the frozen sea. Also, in summer from June to August, you can witness the Midnight Sun in all its glory.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kiruna Municipality

Do:

  • Prepare for cold weather and pack warm clothing and footwear.
  • Take part in traditional celebrations, try local dishes, and engage in cultural activities.
  • Book your accommodation and tours in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Respect the Sámi culture and customs during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in natural areas.
  • Never invade someone’s privacy when viewing the Northern Lights.
  • Refrain from making noise pollution during late hours.
  • Do not disturb or harass wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kiruna Municipality

Kiruna Municipality is generally a safe place to visit. However, travelers are advised to be cautious about the weather conditions, especially during the winter months. Make sure to follow safety instructions while taking part in outdoor activities. Also, it is always wise to keep your valuables safe, and avoid areas known for pickpocketing.

Health in Kiruna Municipality

Healthcare in Kiruna is accessible and the region’s hospitals are well-equipped. Make sure to carry essential medication, dress properly for the weather conditions, and stay hydrated while taking part in outdoor activities. Always carry proof of insurance as it will be needed in case of an emergency.

Getting to Kiruna Municipality: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kiruna Municipality

Kiruna Airport is the main airport of the region, offering flights from major airports in Stockholm, Rovaniemi or Tromso. Direct flights to Kiruna take approximately 1.5 hours to 2 hours depending on the departure airport. Transfers from the airport to the town center are available via taxi or a shuttle bus service.

Bus Stations Near Kiruna Municipality

The town of Kiruna is connected to major towns in the region via bus service. Regular buses operate from nearby areas like Gällivare, Luleå, and Jokkmokk.

Other Options

Hiring a car is an alternative option for getting around the region, although it is not recommended for winter driving. Hitchhiking is common among the locals, but it’s important to be cautious before accepting a ride.

How to Get Around in Kiruna Municipality

The most popular method of transport for getting around Kiruna is by renting a car, available at the airport and the town center. Public transportation in Kiruna is limited to the city center, so the most convenient way to explore the region is by car. Taxis and shuttle buses are available for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Kiruna Municipality: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby destinations from Kiruna Municipality include Jukkasjärvi, Gällivare, and Narvik, which offer unique cultural experiences and outdoor activities. You can explore the beautiful mountains and fjords of Norway, experience the midnight sun, or take part in reindeer sledding and Sami cultural tours.

Conclusion

Kiruna Municipality is a true gem of Sweden offering an authentic cultural experience and spectacular natural beauty. With its unique location and vast offerings, it is a truly unforgettable destination for any traveler. Plan your trip well in advance to enjoy the best of the region’s offerings and take home memories to last a lifetime.
