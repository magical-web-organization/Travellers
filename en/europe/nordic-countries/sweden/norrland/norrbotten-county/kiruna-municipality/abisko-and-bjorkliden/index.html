Abisko and Björkliden Travel Guide - Exploring the Best of Norrland

Abisko and Björkliden are two of the most picturesque places in Norrland, Sweden, where nature is at its best. Located in the Kiruna Municipality of Norrbotten County, these towns offer a myriad of activities and natural landscapes to explore. From hiking in the majestic mountains to skiing and dog-sledding during winter, Abisko and Björkliden are a year-round destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more to help you plan the perfect trip to Abisko and Björkliden.

List of Places to Visit in Abisko and Björkliden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Abisko National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Abisko National Park boasts of the Lapland’s highest and most dramatic mountain peaks, waterfalls, and primeval forests. Ripe with indigenous culture and history, Abisko National Park is a popular destination for bird watching and wildflower viewing. In winter, it’s an excellent setting to catch a glimpse of the Northern Lights.

  • Ales Stenar: Located near Kåseberga in Ystad Municipality, Ales Stenar is an ancient monument in Sweden. The quality and size of the stones used for the monument, and the fact that they link the monument to Norse mythology, make it a significant cultural site to visit.

  • Arctic Circle Center: A visitor center located at the Arctic Circle, the Arctic Circle Center features exhibitions on the Arctic environment and culture. The building, which is made of glass walls, allows visitors to enjoy the stunning views of the Arctic Circle.

Natural Attractions of Abisko and Björkliden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Northern Lights: Abisko is known for having some of the world’s most pristine skies, making it one of the best places to witness the aurora borealis in all its glory.

  • Kungsleden Trail (The King’s Trail): This hiking trail is a classic for trekkers. It stretches from Abisko to Hemavan over 450 km and takes about five weeks to hike end-to-end. A shorter 110 km segment from Abisko to Nikkaluokta, is the most popular and offers stunning views of the nearby mountains and glaciers.

  • Dog Sledding: Winter time calls for Dog Sledding, and Abisko is the perfect place to experience it. Visitors can ride dog sleds which take them deep into the forest.

  • Lake Torneträsk: One of Sweden’s largest and deepest alpine lakes and surrounded by mountains, Lake Torneträsk offers spectacular views of its turquoise blue waters surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abisko and Björkliden

  • Kungsleden or The King’s Trail: This 440-kilometer trail from Abisko to Hemavan has it all – beautiful forests, majestic glaciers, pristine lakes, towering mountains, and sweeping valleys. The trail takes five weeks to complete, and hikers can choose to camp or stay at the different mountain huts.

  • Nordkalottleden Trail: This trail follows the Arctic mountains, from Norway through Sweden and Finland, and can take up to eighty days to complete. The trail covers 1,000 kilometers, and hikers get to see some of the most beautiful and diverse landscapes in the region.

  • Sarek National Park: One of Sweden’s most remote and challenging national parks, Scar National Park is home to the largest glacier in Sweden, several high peaks, and rugged valleys.

  • Mountain Biking: The best time to go mountain biking in Abisko and Björkliden is during the summer season. Visitors can rent mountain bikes and explore the different trails around the towns, some of which offer amazing views and take you to places where cars cannot reach.

Best Things to Do in Abisko and Björkliden: Activities and Tours

  • Aurora Sky Station: Situated atop Mount Nuolja, the Aurora Sky Station offers a panoramic 360-degree view of the nearby mountains and skies. In winter, it’s an excellent setting to witness the Northern Lights. Visitors can reach the station by taking a chairlift ride.

  • Snowshoe Walks and Cross Country Skiing: If you’re not up for dog sledding, visitors can take leisurely snowshoe walks through the forest or try cross-country skiing in the winter months.

  • Whitewater Rafting: During Spring and Summer, the rivers of Norrland come alive with an adrenaline-junkie’s dream of white water rapids.

Food and Drink Scene of Abisko and Björkliden

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smorgasbord/Buffet: The smorgasbord is a traditional Swedish buffet where guests sample different dishes, including pickled herring, meatballs, potatoes, and salmon.

  • Reindeer Meat Dishes: Reindeer dishes such as reindeer stew, grilled reindeer, and stewed reindeer meatballs are a must-try in Abisko and Björkliden. The meat is tender and has a distinctively sweet taste.

Local Beverages

  • Breweries: Norrland is home to some of Sweden’s best breweries, including Nils Oscar Bryggeri (Nobel Award Brewery) and Balder Bryggery.

  • Aquavit: This Scandinavian spirit is made from potatoes, barley, and spiced with herbs like caraway and dill. The best way to enjoy it is served ice-cold with a side of pickled herring.

Where to Stay in Abisko and Björkliden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • STF Abisko Mountain Station: This hotel offers a mix of basic and modern rooms, a cozy restaurant, and a bar.

  • Abisko Guesthouse: This guest house provides a more homey and budget-friendly feel and is family-owned. It has a shared kitchen, laundry facilities, and a living room area.

What to Buy in Abisko and Björkliden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sámi Crafts: The Sámi people are known for their handicrafts, including traditional silver jewelry, wood-carvings, leather products, and colorful textiles.

  • Reindeer Pelts and Horns: They make for excellent gifts and souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Abisko and Björkliden

The Sámi culture has a strong presence in Abisko and Björkliden, and visitors should respect their customs and traditions. Visitors should also respect the natural landscapes and leave them as pristine as they found them.

Best Time to Visit Abisko and Björkliden

The best time to visit Abisko and Björkliden depends on one’s preferences. For those who want to witness the Northern Lights, the best time to go is from September to March. Summer is perfect for outdoor activities such as biking and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abisko and Björkliden

Do:

  • Respect the Sámi culture and traditions.
  • Adhere to Leave No Trace principles.
  • Follow the safety protocols and only venture out with professional guides.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural landscapes.
  • Otherwise disrespect the traditions of the Sámi people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abisko and Björkliden

Abisko and Björkliden are generally safe places to visit. Visitors should follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities. Also, visitors should note that severe winter weather can make it difficult to move around the region.

Health in Abisko and Björkliden

Visitors should have travel insurance that covers outdoor activities. Medical facilities are limited in the region and visitors should bring all prescribed medication with them.

Getting to Abisko and Björkliden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abisko and Björkliden

  • Kiruna Airport is the closest airport to Abisko and Björkliden, which is about 90 km away. Scandinavian Airlines and Norwegian Air Shuttle operate flights here.

Bus Stations Near Abisko and Björkliden

  • Länstrafiken is the public bus company that services Abisko and Björkliden.

Other Options

  • Train: Trains run daily to Kiruna Station from Stockholm, with the journey taking around 15 hours.

  • Car: Visitors can also rent cars from Kiruna and drive to Abisko and Björkliden.

How to Get Around in Abisko and Björkliden

Visitors can get around the towns by taxi, bus or rental cars. During the winter season, it’s advisable to use the provided hotel shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Abisko and Björkliden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kiruna: Known for the Kiruna church and the nearby Ice Hotel, Kiruna is a city of Iron Ore mining and also has the charming look of a small town

  • Jukkasjärvi: Visitors can also check out the Jukkasjärvi Chapel, which is one of the oldest wooden buildings in Lapland and its renowned ICEHOTEL.

Conclusion

Abisko and Björkliden are two of the most picturesque and beautiful towns in Sweden. From hiking and biking to dog sledding and witnessing the Northern Lights, visitors can enjoy nature and outdoor activities all year round. The towns have hotels, guesthouses, and hostels to cater to all budgets. Visitors should also be respectful of the Sámi people and customs as they explore the region.
