Introduction to High Coast

Located in Västernorrland County, in Norrland, Sweden, High Coast is a stunning coastal region known for its diverse landscapes, unique culture, and natural beauty. The area was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2000, with its enchanting beauty attracting tourists from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in High Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

High Coast offers a noteworthy selection of museums and historical places that give visitors insight into the region’s rich cultural history.

  • Högbonden Lighthouse
  • The Old Town of Härnösand
  • Nordingrå Old Church
  • Murberget Open-Air Museum

Natural Attractions of High Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

High Coast is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse landscapes, and abundance of flora and fauna. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit in the region:

  • Skuleskogen National Park
  • Ulvön Island
  • The Höga Kusten Bridge
  • Rotsidan Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of High Coast

High Coast has plenty of trails for hiking, biking, and exploring its wilderness on foot. Whether you’re looking to challenge yourself or enjoy a leisurely stroll, these routes cater to all abilities:

  • The Höga Kustenleden trail
  • Skuleberget mountain
  • Smitingen-Höga Kustenleden Coastal Trail

Best Things to Do in High Coast: Activities and Tours

From kayaking and fishing to wildlife watching and exploring the culture and history of the region, there is no shortage of things to do in High Coast.

  • Sea kayaking tours
  • Wildlife safaris
  • Road trips along the picturesque coastal route
  • Excursions with local guides

Week-Long Itinerary for High Coast

Day 1: Arrive to High Coast, explore Härnösand and visit Ulvön Island. Day 2: Visit Skuleskogen National Park and hike Skuleberget Mountain. Day 3: Explore Högbonden Lighthouse and enjoy a seafood dinner. Day 4: Go on a sea kayaking tour, then enjoy a picnic at Rotsidan Beach. Day 5: Head to Murberget Open-Air Museum, then visit Nordingrå Old Church and Höga Kusten Bridge. Day 6: Take a wildlife safari and explore the Skagsudde Nature Reserve. Day 7: Relax and take in the scenery before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of High Coast

Must-Try Dishes

High Coast cuisine is influenced by its coastal location and abundance of fresh seafood. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Surströmming
  • Kalix Löjrom
  • Arctic char
  • Smoked salmon

Local Beverages

High Coast also has a thriving brewing and distilling scene, with the region producing several fantastic beers and spirits. Some of these include:

  • The famous Norrlands Guld beer
  • Bränneriets Brennivin
  • Skånska Spritfabrikens Gin
  • Absolut Vodka

Where to Stay in High Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

High Coast has an excellent range of accommodation options for all budgets and tastes. From luxury hotels to rustic guesthouses, here are some recommendations:

What to Buy in High Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

High Coast has a range of unique products and souvenirs to take home as a memory of your trip. Here are some of the best items to consider:

  • Handcrafted knives from the region’s famous knifemakers
  • Locally produced honey
  • Fine Swedish whisky and other spirits
  • Designer clothing made with locally sourced wool

Local Culture and Customs of High Coast

High Coast is rich in culture and traditions, with unique customs and festivals being celebrated throughout the year. The identity of the region is also prevalent in its cuisine, art, and music.

Best Time to Visit High Coast

The best time to visit High Coast is from May to September. The summer months are ideal for enjoying outdoor activities and taking in the region’s natural beauty, while the fall brings a change in color and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of High Coast

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the region’s diverse landscapes and natural beauty.
  • Embrace the unique cuisine and local products

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or debris when hiking, exploring, or camping in natural areas.
  • Disrespect the local people and their property.
  • Take part in activities that are harmful to the environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in High Coast

High Coast is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should still take general precautions while exploring the region:

  • Respect wildlife and stay at a safe distance.
  • Keep your belongings close and secure.
  • Follow signs and instructions when hiking or exploring natural areas.

Health in High Coast

Sweden has a robust healthcare system, with medical facilities located throughout the country, including High Coast. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before embarking on their trip.
