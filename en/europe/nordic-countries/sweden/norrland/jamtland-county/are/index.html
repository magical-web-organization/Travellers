Introduction to Åre

Nestled in the Jämtland County in Norrland, Sweden, Åre is a picturesque destination best known for its world-class skiing and snowboarding trails. But, there’s more to Åre than just winter sports. With its stunning natural landscapes, fascinating history, and thriving food and drink scene, Åre is a must-visit destination in Sweden.

This comprehensive travel guide to Åre will provide you with all of the information you need to plan a memorable trip to this Nordic gem.

List of Places to Visit in Åre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Åre is rich in cultural heritage with numerous museums and historical places to explore. Here are a few of the top attractions:

  • Åre Old Church: Built-in 1891, this historic wooden church is known for its intricate woodworks and beautiful frescoes.
  • Jamtli Museum: A living history museum showcasing the traditional life of Jämtland residents.
  • Åre Hembygdsgård: A museum dedicated to the heritage of Åre and Jämtland, displaying artifacts, photographs, and cultural exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Åre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Åre’s stunning natural landscapes offer thrilling outdoor activities and breathtaking views. These are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Åreskutan: Rising 1,420 meters above sea level, Åreskutan offers skiing, hiking, and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Tännforsen Waterfall: One of the highest waterfalls in Sweden, Tännforsen is located a short drive from Åre and offers picturesque hiking trails.
  • Lake Åresjön: Perfect for water sports, fishing, and swimming, this beautiful lake is a popular summertime destination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Åre

Åre is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering numerous hiking and biking trails. Here are a few of the best routes:

  • Jämtland Triangle Trail: This 47-kilometer hiking trail takes you through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Sweden.
  • Copper Trail: A popular mountain biking trail with scenic views, challenging terrain, and thrilling descents.
  • Mount Åreskutan Summit Trail: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Åreskutan and rewards you with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Åre: Activities and Tours

Looking for some adventure or relaxation in Åre? Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Åre is one of the best destinations for skiing and snowboarding in Europe, with over 100 slopes to choose from.
  • Snowmobiling: Take a thrilling snowmobiling tour through the snowy forests and trails of Åre.
  • Hot Springs and Spa: Soak in warm mineral waters and indulge in a spa treatment at the Copperhill Mountain Lodge.

Week-Long Itinerary for Åre

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Åre, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Skiing and Snowboarding at Åre Ski Resort
  • Day 3: Visit to Jamtli Museum and Åre Old Church
  • Day 4: Hiking the Jämtland Triangle Trail
  • Day 5: Explore Tännforsen Waterfall and Lake Åresjön
  • Day 6: Hot Springs and Spa Day at Copperhill Mountain Lodge
  • Day 7: Mountain Biking on the Copper Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Åre

When it comes to food and drink, Åre has a lot to offer. Here are a few of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rökt Åreflundra: Smoked Arctic char from Lake Åresjön, served with lingonberries and potatoes.
  • Enbärsgravad Renfile: Juniper-Cured Reindeer filet, served with lingonberry sauce and seasonal vegetables.
  • Messmör: A traditional Nordic sweet cheese served with flatbread and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Öl: Åre has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries such as Åre Bryggcompagni, Östersund bryggeri, and Jämtlands Bryggeri.
  • Äppelmust: A non-alcoholic sparkling apple juice made from locally sourced apples.
  • Norwegian Aquavit: This traditional Scandinavian spirit is made with herbs and spices and has a strong licorice flavor.

Where to Stay in Åre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxurious accommodations or budget-friendly options, Åre has something for everyone. Some of the top places to stay are:

  • Copperhill Mountain Lodge: A luxurious five-star hotel with stunning views, gourmet cuisine, and a relaxing spa.
  • STF Åre Torg Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Åre, with basic amenities and comfortable beds.
  • Åre Bed & Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse with beautiful mountain views, delicious breakfast, and friendly host.

What to Buy in Åre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique souvenirs or local products to bring home from Åre, consider these options:

  • Handcrafted Sami Jewellery: Sami bracelets, necklaces, and other traditional jewelry make a great keepsake from Åre
  • Åre Chocolaterie: Artisan chocolates made with local ingredients, including lingonberries, raspberries, and juniper berries.
  • Åre Oljefabrik: Locally produced oils and skincare products made from natural ingredients such as Arctic sea buckthorn, rosehip, and cloudberry.

Local Culture and Customs of Åre

The Sami people have inhabited the Jämtland region of Sweden for thousands of years, and their culture and traditions still influence life in Åre. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs, including etiquette around reindeer herding, Sami art and music, and traditional Sami cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Åre

The best time to visit Åre depends on your interests. If you love skiing and snowboarding, the winter months from December to April offer the best snow conditions. For hiking and outdoor activities, the summer months from June to September offer warm, mild weather and long daylight hours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Åre

Do:

  • Try local dishes such as Rökt Åreflundra and Enbärsgravad Renfile.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions of the Sami people.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in Åre.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disturb reindeer herds or other wildlife.
  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Åre

Åre is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, travelers should take common-sense precautions such as keeping documents secure, not leaving valuables unattended, and being aware of local customs and etiquette.

Health in Åre

The health care system in Åre is of high quality, and visitors can access medical services at one of the local clinics or hospitals. It’s essential to have travel insurance, including medical coverage, when traveling to Åre.

Getting to Åre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Åre

Åre Östersund Airport is the closest airport to Åre, located about 90 kilometers away. Other nearby airports include Trondheim Airport in Norway and Sundsvall-Timrå Airport.

Bus Stations Near Åre

Regular bus services operate between Åre and nearby towns, including Östersund and Trondheim.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Åre by train, with direct services from Stockholm and Sundsvall. For those driving, the E14 highway connects Åre with Trondheim in Norway and Sundsvall in Sweden.

How to Get Around in Åre

Åre is easy to navigate by foot, bike, or car. Taxis are also available, and buses operate regular services between various destinations in Åre.

Where to Go Next from Åre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time for a day trip or want to explore other destinations in the area, here are a few ideas:

  • Östersund: A lively university town known for its cultural events, shopping, and dining.
  • Storsjön Lake: One of the largest lakes in Sweden, located just 45 minutes from Åre.
  • Lofsdalen: A picturesque mountain village offering outdoor activities and scenic views.

Conclusion

Åre is a beautiful destination that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and relaxation. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan an unforgettable trip to this stunning Nordic gem.
