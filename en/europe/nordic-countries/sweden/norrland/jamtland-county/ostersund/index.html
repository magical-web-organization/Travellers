Introduction to Östersund

Nestled in the heart of Jämtland County in Norrland, Östersund is the bustling city with small-town charm, offering an incredible selection of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, exceptional cuisine, and beautiful architecture. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a more extended adventure, this charming Swedish city has something for everyone. This guide will walk you through the best things to do in Östersund, provide insider tips, and helpful information for planning your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Östersund

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jamtli Museum - An exceptional outdoor museum featuring reconstructed buildings from the 18th to 21st centuries, exhibitions, and activities showcasing the cultural history of Jämtland County.

  • Östersund’s Storsjöteatern - A popular destination for performances of music, comedy, theatre, and other events. The building dates back to the late 19th century and is a splendid example of Swedish national romanticism.

  • Östersunds Konsthall - The local gallery where you can explore contemporary art and exhibitions showcasing works of local and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Östersund: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Winter hiking - Östersund is surrounded by beautiful mountains and forests that offer the best winter hiking experience. Trailing on snowshoes or skis for miles through the scenic forests, spotting wildlife, and enjoying the peacefulness is a one-of-a-kind adventure.

  • Mount Åreskutan - A popular ski resort with slopes for skiers of all levels and sensational panoramic views. Not interested in skiing? You can hop on a cable car to reach the summit and enjoy the magnificent views.

  • Storsjön - The fifth-largest lake in Sweden, is a beloved destination for outdoor lovers year-round. Take a cruise or rent a kayak in the summer, or go walking, skating, or ice fishing in the colder months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Östersund

  • Frösö parkrun - A 5K weekly timed run around the tranquil island is the perfect way to start your day.

  • St. Olavsleden - Known as the Viking trail, it is a popular 580-kilometer hiking trail that winds from Selånger to Trondheim in Norway, one of UNESCO’s cultural heritage routes.

  • Jamtlandstriangeln - A cycling route that takes you through a scenic and cultural 214-kilometer loop route featuring Östersund and the surrounding towns, Jamtland’s triangle is one of the most popular cycling routes in Sweden.

Best Things to Do in Östersund: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the local cuisine- Östersund is famous for the Culinary Olympics, and it is a food lover’s paradise. Discover the local’s favorite eatables such as reindeer meat, fika, and glögg.

  • Visit the local markets - Enjoy shopping in Storsjötorget, one of the largest markets in Sweden that hosts vendors from all over the region. Discover fantastic local handicrafts, artisanal products, and fresh local produce from regional farmers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Östersund

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Jamtli Museum and explore the historical attractions of the region
Day 2 Take a day trip to Mount Åreskutan and spend the day skiing or enjoying the breathtaking views
Day 3 Join a guided city tour of Östersund and experience the fantastic local culture and customs
Day 4 Visit the local galleries and exhibitions, including Östersunds Konsthall and Galleri Z
Day 5 Enjoy a bike tour along Jamtland Triangle or rent an electric boat and explore the local waterways
Day 6 Take a guided tour of the local markets, including Storsjötorget, Njutånger Garden and handicraft market with an outdoor lunch

Food and Drink Scene of Östersund

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reindeer Meat - A traditional local delicacy served in many local restaurants and is a must-try for any food lover.

  • Fika - An essential part of Swedish culture, Fika is a coffee break, usually enjoyed with a sweet buns served in many cafes around the city.

Local Beverages

  • Glögg - Swedish spiced wine traditionally served in winter months, perfect for sipping by the fire.

  • Norrlands Guld - A local beer brewed in Östersund, perfect for unwinding after a long day of adventures.

Where to Stay in Östersund: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotell Östersund - A centrally located hotel, perfect for exploring the city’s attractions, with a restaurant and bar on-site.

  • Scandic Östersund City - A modern hotel located in the heart of downtown, with exceptional amenities such as a fitness center, sauna, and an in-house restaurant.

  • Östersund’s Vandrarhem - A cozy and affordable hostel located close to the city center, offering comfortable rooms for solo travelers or groups.

What to Buy in Östersund: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts - Discover unique and handcrafted gifts and souvenirs at local markets or visiting artisans’ workshops.

  • Cheeses and preserves - The local cheese and fruit preserves are a delicacy to bring home and share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Östersund

The Jämtland County is rooted in deep cultural history and tradition, and it’s worth taking the time to explore its customs and their origins. Residents take pride in their heritage and celebrate it with festivals, events, and parades throughout the year. It’s essential to be respectful of local customs and traditions and, if possible, participate in such events during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Östersund

Östersund has something to offer year-round, depending on your interests. Winter sports enthusiasts will love the skiing and snowboarding that starts in mid-November. The rest of the year offers great opportunities for outdoor excursions, tasting local cuisine, visiting festivals, and enjoying the local cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Östersund

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.

  • Try the local food.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places or natural environments.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions, including environmental and cultural aspects.

  • Ignore safety guidelines or underestimate outdoor adventure risks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Östersund

Östersund ranks as one of the safer cities globally, and violent crimes are rare. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as keeping an eye on personal belongings, following official guidelines, avoiding walking alone in remote areas at night, and preparing adequately for winter outdoor sports.

Health in Östersund

Guests can expect world-class healthcare facilities in Östersund, including public and private hospitals, clinics, and dental surgeries. Medical insurance is essential for visitors, and medical costs are high in Sweden.

Getting to Östersund: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Östersund

Åre Östersund Airport (OSD) is the primary airport serving the region, with domestic and international flights to major cities such as Stockholm, Birmingham, London, Barcelona, and Vilnius.

Bus Stations Near Östersund

Östersund Central Bus Station is located in the city center, and multiple regional and national bus operators serve the region, making it accessible to other major destinations.

Other Options

There are trains connecting cities around the region, but due to Östersund’s location, often it’s better to travel by bus or car.

How to Get Around in Östersund

Bicycles and walking are great ways to explore the city center, particularly during the summer months. Alternatively, the city bus network provides an efficient way of traveling around the city and the surrounding area, with regular services.

Where to Go Next from Östersund: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Åre - A popular ski resort town, located a mere 80 kilometers away from Östersund, offering world-class skiing and panoramic landscapes.

  • Trondheim - Norway’s historic coastal town, provides a wide range of attractions from visiting Nidaros Cathedral to exploring the Ringve Museum of Music History and spending time in museums like the National Museum of Decorative Arts.

Conclusion

Located in the heart of one of the most charming and culture-rich regions in Sweden, Östersund is the perfect place to experience the best of the Swedish countryside and city life. Unique experiences, outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, rich history, and inspiring art and culture all make Östersund a city not to be missed. We hope that this guide has been helpful in planning your trip and helped you in creating unforgettable memories during your time in this beautiful Swedish city.
