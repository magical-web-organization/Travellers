Introduction to Akureyri

Nestled in the heart of North Iceland, Akureyri is a charming, quiet town that boasts of a rich cultural and historical heritage along with spectacular natural landscapes. Despite being Iceland’s second-largest urban area, Akureyri has managed to retain its raw and untouched beauty by setting itself apart from its busy and overcrowded capital city, Reykjavik.

Whether you are looking for beautiful natural attractions, exciting outdoor adventures, rich cultural experiences, or a quiet and peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, Akureyri has it all. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have put together a list of the top places to visit, things to do, and sensational food and drink scene that will make your Akureyri trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Akureyri

Akureyri has a rich history and culture that is evident in its popular tourist attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in Akureyri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Akureyri Art Museum: This museum features the work of international and local artists.
  • Nonni Museum: This museum explores the life of Jón Sveinsson, a famous author.
  • Akureyrarkirkja, the Akureyri Church: This iconic church is located in the heart of the town and is a must-visit place.

Natural Attractions of Akureyri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Goðafoss Waterfall: This awe-inspiring waterfall is one of the most iconic natural attractions in Northern Iceland.
  • Lake Mývatn: It is a shallow eutrophic lake that is famous for its stunning natural beauty.
  • Hverir geothermal area: This highly active geothermal area is renowned for its bubbling mud pools, steaming vents and fumaroles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akureyri

If you’re a fan of the outdoors, Akureyri has got you covered with these popular hiking/biking routes:

  • Sulur: This hiking trail is relatively easy yet rewarding, featuring views of Akureyri from the top of the mountain.
  • Krossdalur valley: This trail takes you through scenic valleys, forests, and mountain ridges.
  • Hlidarfjall Mountain Bike Park: This recently opened bike park has trails for both beginners and pros.

Best Things to Do in Akureyri: Activities and Tours

From whale watching to kayaking to exploring the town’s hidden gems, there’s no shortage of exciting things to do in Akureyri. Here are some other must-do activities:

  • Aurora Borealis: Akureyri is one of the best places to see the Northern Lights from.
  • Snowmobiling: Take a snowmobile tour and explore Iceland’s wild terrain.
  • Whale watching: Spot humpback whales, minke whales, and orcas on a whale watching tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akureyri

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Akureyri, consider these places and activities:

Day 1: Explore Akureyri town and Akureyri Church, followed by a trip to the Botanical Garden.

Day 2: Drive to Goðafoss Waterfall and Lake Mývatn, visit the Dimmuborgir lava fields.

Day 3: Drive to Dettifoss and Selfoss waterfalls.

Day 4: Take a whale watching tour in Skjalfandi Bay, followed by a visit to the Herring Era Museum.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Húsavík, exploring the town’s charming streets and indulging in some whale watching and sea kayaking.

Day 6: A scenic drive to the hot springs at Hverir geothermal area, and top it off by taking a dip in the Mývatn Nature Baths (Blue Lagoon of the North).

Day 7: Hiking day trip to the Sulur mountain or cycling in Hlidarfjall Mountain Bike Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Akureyri

Akureyri has a burgeoning food and drink scene that is undoubtedly worth experiencing. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Icelandic hot dog (Pylsur): A must-try classic dish, the Icelandic hot dog is made with sheep’s intestines and is often served with a variety of toppings.
  • Hákarl: It’s a traditional Icelandic dish, which consists of fermented shark meat.
  • Skyr: A rich Icelandic dairy product that is similar to yogurt.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivin: A clear Icelandic schnapps most commonly drunk with fermented shark.
  • Malt extract: It is a non-alcoholic drink, which is highly popular among the Icelandic population.
  • Icelandic beer: Check out local breweries such as Kaldi and the Icelandic Beer Company.

Where to Stay in Akureyri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Akureyri has a range of options for travelers to choose from. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Kea Akureyri: This iconic hotel with a rich history houses a spa, bar, and restaurant.
  • Akureyri H.I. Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that is centrally located in Akureyri, making it easy to explore the town.
  • Guesthouse Helga: A charming and cozy guesthouse that provides comfortable and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Akureyri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Akureyri, such as:

  • Icelandic woolens and sweaters: Icelandic wool is famous for its quality, and there are many stores in Akureyri that sell traditional woolen clothing.
  • Icelandic chocolate: It’s a perfect gift for your loved ones and is often made using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Icelandic design products: From ceramics to jewelry, there are many beautiful and unique Icelandic design products worth bringing home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Akureyri

When traveling to Akureyri, it’s essential to be mindful of the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the environment: Iceland is famous for its stunning natural landscapes, so it’s essential to be mindful of your impact on the environment.
  • Tipping: Unlike in America, it’s not customary to tip in Iceland; it is considered impolite to tip.
  • Quiet hours: Icelanders take their peace and quiet seriously, so observe the quiet hours from 10.00 pm to 7.00 am.

Best Time to Visit Akureyri

The best time to visit Akureyri is from June to August, as it is warmer with longer daylight hours. June and July offer the famous midnight sun experience, where the sun sets very late, and the natural environment looks stunning amidst the daylight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akureyri

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty: Akureyri is home to many remarkable natural wonders, so take the time to explore the surroundings.
  • Visit local museums and galleries: There are many cultural institutions in Akureyri that offer insight into Iceland’s history and arts.
  • Experience the local food: Be sure to try Icelandic traditional meals while in Akureyri.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate outdoor clothing: Iceland’s weather can be unpredictable, so always dress in warm clothing and bring waterproof jackets and sturdy shoes.
  • Damage natural landscapes: Iceland’s rugged environment is fragile, so it’s essential to take care not to damage it or disturb the wildlife.
  • Swim in unsafe areas: Iceland has many beautiful hot springs, but it’s dangerous to bathe in them if you’re not familiar with the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akureyri

Iceland is one of the safest countries globally, and Akureyri is no exception. However, it is essential to take some precautions, such as:

  • Be mindful of your surroundings: While Akureyri is considered safe, it’s essential to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas.
  • Pay attention to weather conditions: Iceland’s weather can be unpredictable, so always check the forecast before heading out.

Health in Akureyri

Iceland has a world-class healthcare system, and Akureyri boasts of a good hospital and medical clinics. However, it’s always best to have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any unexpected healthcare expenses while in Akureyri.

Getting to Akureyri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akureyri

  • Akureyri Airport: The airport is located just a few kilometers from the town’s center and offers flights to Reykjavik and other domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Akureyri

  • BSI Terminal in Reykjavik: Buses run from Reykjavik to Akureyri several times a day, and the journey takes around five hours.

Other Options

One great way to get to Akureyri is to rent a car and drive from Reykjavik to Akureyri. The journey takes approximately 5-6 hours and allows you to take in the stunning natural scenery.

How to Get Around in Akureyri

Akureyri is a small town, and walking or cycling is an excellent way to explore the town. If you need a mode of transportation other than your feet or bike, then car rentals are widely available in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Akureyri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Húsavík: A charming town that is home to beautiful whale watching tours.
  • Dettifoss waterfall: One of Europe’s most powerful waterfalls, a definite must-see for nature lovers.
  • Jökulsárgljúfur National Park: The canyon is located in the northern part of Vatnajökull National Park.

Conclusion

Akureyri is a small town that packs a punch with its rich cultural heritage, spectacular natural landscapes, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re planning a quiet and peaceful getaway, an action-packed adventure, or a combination of both, Akureyri has something for everyone. Ensure to plan your itinerary well and enjoy every moment exploring this stunning Northern Icelandic gem.
