Introduction to Skagafjörður

If you’re planning a trip to Iceland, be sure to visit Skagafjörður in the northern part of the country. This beautiful region is home to stunning natural scenery, fascinating cultural attractions, and plenty of outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Skagafjörður

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Skagafjörður is rich in history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore during your visit. The following are some must-visit attractions:

  • Skagafjörður Folk Museum: This museum showcases traditional Icelandic farming and fishing methods, as well as artifacts from the area’s history.
  • Glaumbær: This historic turf house is one of the best-preserved examples of traditional Icelandic architecture.
  • Hólar Cathedral: This historic cathedral, which dates back to the 18th century, is a must-visit for anyone interested in Iceland’s religious history.

Natural Attractions of Skagafjörður: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, you’ll find plenty to do in Skagafjörður. The region is home to some of Iceland’s most iconic natural wonders, including:

  • Hofsós Swimming Pool: This infinity pool offers stunning views out over the fjord, and is a must-visit for anyone visiting Skagafjörður.
  • Grettislaug: This natural hot spring offers warm, relaxing waters in a stunning natural setting.
  • Sauðárkrókur Beach: This beautiful black-sand beach is a great place to go for a walk or relax in the sunshine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skagafjörður

For those looking to explore the beautiful Icelandic countryside on foot or by bike, Skagafjörður offers plenty of stunning hiking and biking routes. Some of the best options include:

  • Vatnsdalur Valley: This beautiful valley is home to a number of hiking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Borgarvirki: This historic fortress offers a short but challenging hiking trail, as well as stunning views over the surrounding area.
  • Krossanesborgir Nature Reserve: This nature reserve offers a number of well-marked hiking trails, as well as opportunities for bird watching and wildlife spotting.

Best Things to Do in Skagafjörður: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Skagafjörður has something to offer. Some of the best activities and tours in the area include:

  • Horseback Riding: Skagafjörður is known for its horseback riding tours, which offer a unique way to explore the beautiful Icelandic countryside.
  • Whale Watching: The waters off the coast of Skagafjörður are home to a variety of whale species, and whale watching tours offer a chance to get up close and personal with these beautiful creatures.
  • Glacier Tours: Skagafjörður is located close to several glaciers, and guided glacier tours offer a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to explore these icy landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skagafjörður

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Skagafjörður, the following itinerary will give you a great taste of everything the region has to offer:

Day 1: Visit the Skagafjörður Folk Museum and explore the historic town of Sauðárkrókur.

Day 2: Take a horseback riding tour of the surrounding countryside, and visit the historic turf house at Glaumbær.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring the beautiful valleys and hiking trails of Vatnsdalur Valley.

Day 4: Take a whale watching tour and explore the picturesque fishing village of Húsavík.

Day 5: Visit the Hólar Cathedral and take a guided glacier tour in nearby Vatnajökull National Park.

Day 6: Relax and enjoy the beautiful views at Hofsós Swimming Pool, or take a dip in the warm waters of Grettislaug.

Day 7: Explore the stunning scenery of Borgarvirki and Krossanesborgir Nature Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Skagafjörður

If you’re a foodie, you won’t be disappointed by the culinary delights of Skagafjörður. The region is known for its fresh, locally sourced ingredients and traditional Icelandic cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangikjöt: Smoked lamb is a staple of Icelandic cuisine, and is a must-try during your visit to Skagafjörður.
  • Skyr: This thick, creamy dairy product is a traditional Icelandic staple, and is a great way to start your day.
  • Fiskisúpa: This hearty fish soup is a delicious way to warm up on a chilly Icelandic day.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: This strong, clear Icelandic schnapps is a must-try for anyone looking to sample the local spirits.
  • Opal: This popular Icelandic candy-infused vodka is a fun and unique way to enjoy a drink during your visit.

Where to Stay in Skagafjörður: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Skagafjörður offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hótel Tindastóll: This historic hotel offers comfortable rooms, as well as a restaurant and bar.
  • Hofsós Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located just a short walk from the beautiful Hofsós Swimming Pool.
  • Glaumbær Hostel: This budget-friendly option is located on the grounds of the historic Glaumbær turf house, and offers a unique and memorable experience.

What to Buy in Skagafjörður: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from your visit to Skagafjörður, the following local products are a great choice:

  • Icelandic Wool: Icelandic wool is renowned for its quality and warmth, and you’ll find plenty of beautiful woolen goods to choose from in Skagafjörður.
  • Skyr: If you can’t get enough of the creamy Icelandic dairy product, consider bringing home some skyr to enjoy once you’re back home.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Icelandic jewelry designers are known for using unique materials like lava rock and moss in their creations, and you’ll find plenty of beautiful handmade jewelry to choose from in Skagafjörður.

Local Culture and Customs of Skagafjörður

To make the most of your visit to Skagafjörður, it’s important to familiarize yourself with the local culture and customs. Some important things to know include:

  • Respect for the Environment: Icelanders take great pride in their country’s natural beauty, and it’s important to show respect for the environment by staying on marked trails and not littering.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in Iceland, as service charges are included in most restaurant bills.
  • Greetings: Icelanders are generally reserved and polite, and it’s customary to greet people with a handshake or a nod of the head.

Best Time to Visit Skagafjörður

The best time to visit Skagafjörður depends on your preferences and what you want to experience during your trip. If you’re looking to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and horseback riding, the summer months of June through August are the best time to visit. However, if you’re interested in seeing the Northern Lights, the winter months of September through March offer the best chance of sightings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skagafjörður

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Skagafjörður, it’s important to follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do dress appropriately for the weather, as temperatures can vary greatly.
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Do take advantage of the numerous outdoor activities and opportunities for adventure.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Don’t underestimate the Icelandic weather, and make sure to stay safe and warm during your trip.

Safety and Health

Skagafjörður is generally a safe and healthy place to visit. However, it’s important to take the following precautions during your trip:

Safety in Skagafjörður

  • Stay on marked trails and follow all posted signs and warnings.
  • Be mindful of the weather conditions, which can be unpredictable and change quickly.
  • Take precautions to prevent falls on icy or slick surfaces during the winter months.

Health in Skagafjörður

  • Ensure that all of your vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Iceland.
  • Be prepared for the possibility of motion sickness during whale watching tours or boat trips.
  • Bring any necessary prescription medications with you, as pharmacies may be limited in the area.

Getting to Skagafjörður: Transportation Options and Logistics

Skagafjörður is located in Northern Iceland, and there are several transportation options available for getting there.

Airports Near Skagafjörður

The nearest airport to Skagafjörður is Akureyri Airport (AEY), which is located about 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Skagafjörður

The main bus station in Skagafjörður is located in Sauðárkrókur, and offers connections to several nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

If you prefer to rent a car, several rental agencies operate in Akureyri and Reykjavik, and offer a variety of cars to suit your needs.

How to Get Around in Skagafjörður

The best way to get around Skagafjörður is by car, as this gives you the flexibility to explore the region at your own pace. However, if you prefer not to rent a car, there are several bus and taxi options available.

Where to Go Next from Skagafjörður: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Skagafjörður, the following nearby destinations and excursions are worth considering:

  • Akureyri: This vibrant city is home to a variety of cultural attractions, restaurants, and shopping opportunities.
  • Lake Mývatn: This stunning lake is located about two hours away from Skagafjörður, and is home to some of Iceland’s most iconic natural wonders, including hot springs, mud pools, and volcanic craters.
  • Snaefellsnes Peninsula: This picturesque peninsula is located about four hours away from Skagafjörður, and offers stunning coastal views, beautiful beaches, and unique geological formations.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, fascinating cultural attractions, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure, Skagafjörður is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Northern Iceland. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or explore the rugged Icelandic countryside, Skagafjörður has something to offer for everyone.
