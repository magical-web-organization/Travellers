Introduction to North Iceland

North Iceland is a stunning destination known for its picturesque landscapes, natural wonders, and rich cultural heritage. It is home to some of Iceland’s most popular tourist attractions, including geothermal baths, waterfalls, glaciers, and more. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, nature lover, or history buff, North Iceland has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in North Iceland

Here are some of the top places you should visit in North Iceland:

  • Akureyri: This charming city is the unofficial capital of North Iceland. It is known for its lively cultural scene, vibrant nightlife, and stunning surroundings.

  • Lake Myvatn: This shallow lake is surrounded by geothermal vents, volcanic craters, and lava fields, making it an otherworldly sight to see.

  • Godafoss Waterfall: Known as the “waterfall of the gods,” Godafoss is a must-visit attraction in North Iceland. It is located on the Skjálfandafljót River and is renowned for its beauty.

  • Vatnajökull National Park: This is Iceland’s largest national park, covering roughly 14% of the country. It is home to Europe’s largest glacier and offers some of the best hiking trails in the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North Iceland is rich in history and culture. Here are some of the top museums and historical places you should check out:

  • The Akureyri Museum: Located in the heart of Akureyri, this museum offers an in-depth look at the history and culture of North Iceland.

  • The Herring Era Museum: This museum is located in the town of Siglufjörður and showcases the history of the herring industry in Iceland.

  • The Grimsey Island: This small island is located on the Arctic Circle and is known for its stunning landscapes and unique culture. It is only accessible by boat or plane.

Natural Attractions of North Iceland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Iceland is home to some of Iceland’s most picturesque landscapes. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Dettifoss Waterfall: This powerful waterfall is the most powerful in Europe. It is located in the northern part of Vatnajökull National Park.

  • Hveravellir Nature Reserve: This reserve is known for its unique geothermal landscapes, including hot springs and other natural wonders.

  • Askja: Located in the heart of the Vatnajökull National Park, Askja is an active volcanic area that offers some of the best hiking trails in North Iceland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Iceland

North Iceland offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails that are suitable for all skill levels. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The Laugavegur Trail: This 55-kilometer trail is one of the most popular routes in Iceland. It offers stunning views of the Highlands and glaciers.

  • The East Fjords: The East Fjords are a popular destination for hiking and offer a range of trails that cater to all skill levels.

  • The Jökulsárgljúfur Canyon: This canyon is located in the Vatnajökull National Park and offers a range of hiking trails that take you through surreal landscapes.

Best Things to Do in North Iceland: Activities and Tours

North Iceland offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Whale Watching: North Iceland is known for its abundant whale population. Akureyri is one of the best places to go whale watching.

  • Northern Lights Tour: North Iceland is one of the best places to experience the Northern Lights. There are several tour operators that offer guided tours.

  • Geothermal Baths: North Iceland is known for its geothermal baths, including the famous Blue Lagoon and the Myvatn Nature Baths.

Food and Drink Scene of North Iceland

North Iceland is known for its unique food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangikjöt: Smoked lamb that is a traditional Icelandic delicacy.
  • Hákarl: Fermented shark meat that is a staple in Iceland.
  • Plokkfiskur: A traditional fish stew that is made with mashed potatoes and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: This is a local spirit that is made from potatoes and flavored with caraway seeds.
  • Icelandic Beer: Iceland has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries that offer unique and flavorful brews.

Where to Stay in North Iceland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Iceland has a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Hotel Kea: This hotel is located in the heart of Akureyri and offers comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • Vogafjós Farm Resort: This guesthouse is located near Lake Myvatn and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Hotel Rangá: This hotel is located in the southern part of Iceland and offers luxurious accommodations and stunning views of the Northern Lights.

What to Buy in North Iceland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Iceland is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the top things to bring home:

  • Icelandic Woolen Sweaters: These sweaters are made from Icelandic wool and are known for their warmth and durability.
  • Lava Jewelry: Lava jewelry is made from unique volcanic stones found in Iceland and is a popular souvenir among visitors.
  • Skyr: This is a traditional Icelandic dairy product that is similar to yogurt and is available in several flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of North Iceland

North Iceland has a rich and vibrant culture that revolves around traditions and celebrations. Here are some of the customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Midsummer’s Eve: This is one of the biggest celebrations in Iceland and revolves around the summer solstice. It is celebrated with bonfires, music, and dancing.

  • Christmas in Iceland: Christmas is a big deal in Iceland and is celebrated with a range of festive traditions, including the Yule Lads, who are said to bring gifts to children.

Best Time to Visit North Iceland

The best time to visit North Iceland is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is warmest, and the days are longest, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities and exploration.

############ Do:’s and Don’ts of North Iceland

Here are some do’s and dont’s to keep in mind when visiting North Iceland:

Do

  • Dress in Layers: The weather can be unpredictable, so it’s always best to dress in layers.
  • Bring a Camera: North Iceland is home to some of the most beautiful landscapes in the world, so be sure to bring a camera to capture the scenery.
  • Respect the Environment: North Iceland is known for its pristine landscapes, so be sure to respect the environment by leaving no trace and sticking to designated trails.

############# Don’t:

  • Ignore Road Closures: During the winter months, some roads may be closed due to snow and ice. Be sure to check road conditions before embarking on a trip.
  • Approach Wildlife: North Iceland is home to a range of wildlife, including reindeer and birds. Be sure to keep a safe distance and avoid approaching them.

Safety and Health

North Iceland is generally a safe destination to visit. Here are some tips to ensure a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in North Iceland

  • Be Aware of Weather Conditions: The weather in North Iceland can be unpredictable, so it’s always best to be prepared for changing conditions.

  • Drive Carefully: If you plan to rent a car, be sure to drive carefully, especially during the winter months when roads can be icy and snow-covered.

Health in North Iceland

  • Apply Sunscreen: Even during the winter months, the sun in North Iceland can be strong, so be sure to apply sunscreen.

  • Stay Hydrated: The air in North Iceland can be dry, so be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Getting to North Iceland: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Iceland is easily accessible by plane, car, or bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near North Iceland

  • Akureyri Airport: This is the largest airport in North Iceland and offers daily flights to Reykjavik and other destinations in Iceland.

  • Husavik Airport: This small airport serves the town of Husavik and offers flights to Reykjavik and other destinations in Iceland.

Bus Stations Near North Iceland

  • Akureyri Bus Station: This is the main bus station in North Iceland and offers regular bus service to Reykjavik and other destinations in Iceland.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: If you plan to explore North Iceland on your own, renting a car is a great option.

How to Get Around in North Iceland

The best way to get around North Iceland is by car. If you don’t want to rent a car, there are also several bus tours and private tours available.

Where to Go Next from North Iceland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Iceland is just the beginning of what Iceland has to offer. Here are some of the top destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Golden Circle: This popular route takes you to some of Iceland’s most famous landmarks, including the Geysir geothermal area and Gullfoss waterfall.

  • The South Coast: The South Coast is known for its stunning black sand beaches, glaciers, and waterfalls.

  • West Iceland: This region is home to several hot springs, waterfalls, and lava fields, making it a unique and interesting destination to explore.

Conclusion

North Iceland is a stunning destination that offers a wide range of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to explore the countryside, soak in geothermal baths, or get a taste of local culture, North Iceland has something for everyone. Be sure to take the time to explore this unique and beautiful region of Iceland.
