Introduction to Mývatn

Mývatn is an otherworldly lake located in North Iceland and is one of the most surreal natural phenomena in the country. The lake is surrounded by craters, lava fields, geothermal areas, and birdlife, offering a perfect landscape for hikers, nature lovers, and adventure seekers. Mývatn is a tranquil and secluded region, which makes it an ideal place to escape the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Mývatn

Mývatn has a wealth of attractions that will leave you breathless from cultural museums to natural thermal pools. In this section, we’ll highlight the best places to visit in Mývatn.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best things about visiting Mývatn is the cultural experiences it offers. You can immerse yourself in Icelandic history at The Sigurgeir’s Bird Collection, a unique museum. This museum showcases the vast bird population found in Iceland and the culture of hunting birds.

Natural Attractions of Mývatn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mývatn is a paradise for nature lovers. The scenic beauty of the area is awe-inspiring. You can take a walk along the volcanic craters of Hverfjall, soak in the geothermal baths of the Myvatn Nature Baths, and explore the surreal geological formations of Dimmuborgir.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mývatn

Mývatn is a hiker’s paradise, and there are numerous trails that take you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the region. If biking is your thing, rent a bike and explore the area around Lake Mývatn, taking you through lava fields, pseudocraters, and birdwatching spots.

Best Things to Do in Mývatn: Activities and Tours

Mývatn offers several activities that offer an authentic Icelandic experience. You can take a horse-riding tour through the Mývatn area, go fishing, or take a guided tour to experience the Northern Lights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mývatn

A week spent in Mývatn is the perfect amount of time to explore the scenic beauty of the area. In this section, we’ll provide a detailed itinerary that covers all the main attractions of Mývatn.

Day Activity
1 Arrival and check into accommodation
2 Explore the geothermal areas of Hverir and Krafla

4 Soak in the Myvatn Nature Baths and relax
5 Take a horse-riding tour around the lake

Food and Drink Scene of Mývatn

Mývatn has a unique food and drink scene that showcases Icelandic cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Mývatn.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Icelandic Lamb Stew: A hearty soup made with lamb, potatoes, carrots, and onions.
  • Skyr: A thick and creamy Icelandic yogurt that can be eaten on its own or used as a dip.
  • Puffin: A local delicacy consisting of a seabird that is traditionally hunted, smoked, and served.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: A traditional Icelandic schnapps made from potatoes and flavored with caraway seeds.
  • Viking Beer: A local brew that can be enjoyed while soaking in the geothermal pools.

Where to Stay in Mývatn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mývatn has several options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mývatn.

  • Vogafjós Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located on a farm and offers stunning views of Mývatn.
  • Sel Hotel Myvatn: A luxury hotel offering an onsite restaurant, spa, and sauna.
  • Hlíd Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers an excellent location and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Mývatn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mývatn has many unique souvenirs that you can take back home to remember your trip. Some of the best souvenirs to buy in Mývatn include woolen sweaters, Icelandic chocolate, and handmade ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Mývatn

Icelandic culture and customs are unique, and a trip to Mývatn is an excellent opportunity to learn more about them. The Icelandic people value nature, community, and their language. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a public place.

Best Time to Visit Mývatn

The best time to visit Mývatn is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. You can experience the Midnight Sun, where the sun doesn’t set below the horizon, making for a long day of exploring. However, if you want to witness the Northern Lights, the best time to visit is during the winter from November to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mývatn

When visiting Mývatn, there are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind to ensure a pleasant trip.

Do:

  • Respect the environment and practice Leave No Trace principles when hiking or exploring.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take a dip in the geothermal hot pools.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the area.
  • Disturb the wildlife or damage the vegetation.
  • Ignore safety warnings when hiking or exploring the area.

Safety and Health

When visiting Mývatn, safety and health should be a top priority. Here are some things to keep in mind.

Safety in Mývatn

  • Wear appropriate clothing and gear when hiking, biking, or exploring.
  • Be aware of local weather conditions, especially in the winter months.
  • Follow safety instructions and warnings when participating in activities.

Health in Mývatn

  • Be aware of the geothermal activity in the area and follow safety instructions when bathing.
  • Carry and drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Mývatn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mývatn is located in northern Iceland, and getting there might require some planning. Here are some transportation options to consider.

Airports Near Mývatn

The closest airport to Mývatn is Akureyri Airport, located about 90 kilometers away. Keflavík International Airport is the largest airport in Iceland, and you can take a connecting flight to Akureyri from there.

Bus Stations Near Mývatn

Buses connect Mývatn to other parts of Iceland. The buses run regularly, and you can buy tickets online or at the bus station.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most popular way of getting around Iceland, and you can do the same when visiting Mývatn. You can rent a car from one of the major car rental companies in Iceland or at the airport.

How to Get Around in Mývatn

Getting around Mývatn is easy and comfortable. You can rent a car, take a bus, or join a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Mývatn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mývatn is a gateway to several other exciting destinations in Iceland. Here are some places to visit after exploring Mývatn.

  • Akureyri: A charming town known as the ‘Capital of North Iceland’ that sits on the coast.
  • Dettifoss: Europe’s most powerful waterfall, located about 90 kilometers from Mývatn.
  • Husavik: A beautiful coastal town known for its whale watching tours.

Conclusion

Mývatn is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Iceland. From natural wonders to cultural experiences, there is something for everyone in this serene and beautiful region. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Mývatn and create memories that will last a lifetime.
