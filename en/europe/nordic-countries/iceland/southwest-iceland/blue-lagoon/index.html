Blue Lagoon Travel Guide: Natural Wonders, Activities, and Local Culture

If you’re planning a trip to Iceland, Blue Lagoon should be at the top of your list. This famous geothermal spa is a must-visit destination in Southwest Iceland with its unique blue waters, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Blue Lagoon, including cultural attractions, hiking routes, food and drink scene, transportation options, and safety tips.

Introduction to Blue Lagoon

Blue Lagoon is a geothermal spa located in a lava field in Grindavik, Southwest Iceland. The spa is world-famous for its naturally heated mineral water, which is rich in silica, algae, and minerals known for their healing properties. The temperature of the water is between 37-40°C, making it perfect for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Apart from soaking in the warm blue water, Blue Lagoon offers various outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and kayaking. You can also indulge yourself with spa treatments, massages, and healthy dishes at on-site restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Lagoon

When you visit Blue Lagoon, you’ll have plenty of cultural attractions and natural wonders to explore. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Blue Lagoon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lava Restaurant: Enjoy a fine dining experience with panoramic views of the geothermal waters and volcanic scenery at The Lava Restaurant.
  • Sólheimar Ecovillage: Discover the eco-village community and learn about their sustainable practices and organic agriculture.
  • The Blue Lagoon Silica Hotel: This award-winning hotel is built into the volcanic rock and offers a panoramic view of the lagoon. You can also enjoy an exclusive spa treatment and access to the Blue Lagoon after hours.

Natural Attractions of Blue Lagoon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Blue Lagoon Water: The main attraction of Blue Lagoon, famous for its healing powers.
  • The Blue Lagoon Spa: Relax and rejuvenate with spa treatments and massages.
  • The Reykjanes Peninsula: This volcanic peninsula offers breathtaking views and is home to several natural hot springs.
  • The Viking World Museum: Learn about Viking culture and see a replica of a Viking ship.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Lagoon

Blue Lagoon is a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts. You can explore the surrounding lava fields and experience Iceland’s unique natural beauty in numerous ways. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Blue Lagoon:

  • The Reykjanes Peninsula Hiking Trail: This trail is 43 km long and takes you through the Reykjanes Peninsula’s coastal landscape, volcanic craters, and hot springs.
  • The Blue Lagoon Bike Trail: You can rent a bike at the Blue Lagoon and explore the surrounding volcanic terrain at your own pace.
  • The Fagradalsfjall Hiking Trail: A challenging hike that takes you to the top of Fagradalsfjall Volcano, where you can see the impressive lava fields.

Best Things to Do in Blue Lagoon: Activities and Tours

Apart from enjoying the warm waters of the Blue Lagoon, there are several other activities and tours you can indulge in while in Blue Lagoon. Here are some of our favorite activities:

  • The Northern Lights Tour: Witness the magical northern lights with a guided tour from Blue Lagoon.
  • The Golden Circle Day Trip: Take a day trip from Blue Lagoon and explore Iceland’s famous Golden Circle, which includes Thingvellir National Park, Gullfoss Waterfall, and Geysir.
  • Paddleboard or Kayak on Blue Lagoon: Rent a paddleboard or kayak and explore the lagoon at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blue Lagoon

To fully experience Iceland’s natural beauty, we recommend dedicating at least a week to your Blue Lagoon trip. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip:

Days Itinerary
Day 1 Arrival and check-in at a hotel in Reykjavik
Day 2 Explore Reykjavik city center and take a guided tour of the town
Day 3 Visit the Blue Lagoon Spa and experience the magic of the water
Day 4 Take a guided tour of the Golden Circle
Day 5 Hike the Reykjanes Peninsula Trail
Day 6 Take a kayaking tour of the Blue Lagoon
Day 7 Departure from Reykjavik

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Lagoon

Iceland is famous for its Nordic cuisine, and Blue Lagoon offers some of the best traditional and healthy dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Icelandic Lamb Soup: A hot and flavorful soup made with local vegetables and lamb meat.
  • Skyr: A creamy dairy product similar to Greek yogurt, but with a milder flavor.
  • Grilled Arctic Char: A delicious fish dish served with local vegetables and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: A traditional Icelandic schnapps made from fermented potatoes and caraway seeds.
  • Beer from local microbreweries: Try some of the locally brewed beers, which use unique ingredients like Icelandic moss.

Where to Stay in Blue Lagoon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blue Lagoon offers various accommodation options for all budgets, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Retreat at Blue Lagoon: A luxurious hotel with private lagoon access and exclusive spa treatments.
  • Silica Hotel: A luxury hotel with a private balcony and a view of the lagoon.
  • Hotel Grindavik: A cozy hotel with comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving authentic Icelandic dishes.

What to Buy in Blue Lagoon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Blue Lagoon offers several unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as memories of your trip. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Blue Lagoon Skincare Products: Bring home some of the mineral-rich skincare products only found in Iceland and the Blue Lagoon.
  • Icelandic Wool Products: Icelandic woolen garments like sweaters, gloves, and hats are perfect for keeping you warm in the Icelandic climate.
  • Lava and Volcano Ash Products: Iceland’s volcanic landscape is a treasure trove of unique souvenirs like lava stone jewelry and volcano ash soap.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Lagoon

Iceland has a unique culture and customs that visitors should respect while traveling. Here are some cultural tips for your Blue Lagoon trip:

  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in Iceland, but it is not frowned upon either.
  • Language: Icelandic is the official language, but English is widely spoken.
  • Etiquette: Icelanders are polite and respectful, so make sure to be courteous and respectful to locals and fellow tourists.

Best Time to Visit Blue Lagoon

The best time to visit Blue Lagoon is from May to September, as the weather is mild, and the days are longer. However, visiting in winter is also an excellent option if you want to see the northern lights and enjoy winter sports like snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Lagoon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Blue Lagoon:

Do:

  • Book in advance: Blue Lagoon is a popular destination, so make sure to book your tickets and activities beforehand.
  • Pack wisely: Bring warm clothes, comfortable shoes, and a swimsuit.
  • Respect nature: Iceland is a beautiful country, so make sure to follow the rules and regulations regarding environmental protection.

Don’t:

  • Enter the water without showering: Make sure to take a shower before entering the Blue Lagoon.
  • Forget your towel: Towels are not provided, so make sure to pack your towels and robes.
  • Forget your camera: Blue Lagoon is a photographer’s paradise, so make sure to pack your camera or smartphone.

Safety and Health

Blue Lagoon is a safe and healthy destination for travelers. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Blue Lagoon

  • Always follow the lifeguard’s instructions: There are lifeguards present at all times, so make sure to follow their instructions.
  • Use the walkways and stairs carefully: The area around the lagoon may be slippery, so use caution while walking.
  • Wear sunscreen: The sun in Iceland can be intense, so make sure to wear sunscreen while enjoying outdoor activities.

Health in Blue Lagoon

  • Consult a doctor: If you have any medical conditions, consult a doctor before visiting the Blue Lagoon.
  • Avoid the water if you have open wounds or infections: The mineral-rich water may cause skin irritation if you have open wounds or infections.

Getting to Blue Lagoon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Blue Lagoon is easily accessible from Reykjavik, and there are several transportation options available. Here are some ways to get to Blue Lagoon:

Airports Near Blue Lagoon

  • Keflavik International Airport: Located about 20 minutes away from Blue Lagoon, Keflavik International Airport is the closest international airport to Blue Lagoon.

Bus Stations Near Blue Lagoon

  • Reykjavik Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Reykjavik offers direct buses to Blue Lagoon.

Other Options

  • Private Transfers: You can hire private transfers to Blue Lagoon from your hotel or travel agency.
  • Rent a Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore Iceland, and you can easily drive to Blue Lagoon from Reykjavik.

How to Get Around in Blue Lagoon

Blue Lagoon is a small area, so you can easily explore it on foot. However, if you want to venture further, you can rent a bike or a car from the on-site rental office.

Where to Go Next from Blue Lagoon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Iceland is full of natural wonders and exciting destinations, and there are several nearby places to visit from Blue Lagoon. Here are some top picks:

  • Reykjavik: Iceland’s capital city is famous for its vibrant nightlife, museums, and art galleries.
  • The Golden Circle: Take a day trip from Blue Lagoon and explore Iceland’s famous Golden Circle, which includes Thingvellir National Park, Gullfoss Waterfall, and Geysir.
  • Snaefellsnes Peninsula: Travel further north from Blue Lagoon, and you’ll find the Snaefellsnes Peninsula, famous for its glacier, lava fields, and coastal villages.

Conclusion

Blue Lagoon is a unique destination that offers a perfect blend of relaxation, outdoor adventure, and culture. In this guide, we hope to have provided all the crucial information to plan your trip to Blue Lagoon, including cultural attractions, hiking routes, food and drink scene, transportation options, and safety tips. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable experience in this geothermal oasis in Southwest Iceland.
