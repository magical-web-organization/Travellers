Introduction to Keflavík

Keflavík, located in Southwest Iceland, is a town that offers a unique mix of Icelandic culture and outdoor adventures. It is a popular destination for tourists, especially those who are starting or ending their trip in Iceland due to its proximity to the Keflavík International Airport. Here’s everything you need to know about visiting Keflavík.

List of Places to Visit in Keflavík

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Viking World Museum: An interactive museum that offers a glimpse into the lives of the Vikings.
  • The Reykjanes Heritage Museum: A museum that showcases the unique geology, history, and culture of the Reykjanes Peninsula.
  • Duus Museum: A museum in a historic house that displays items related to the history of the town and fishing industry.

Natural Attractions of Keflavík: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Reykjanes Lighthouse: A scenic lighthouse that offers a spectacular view of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • The Blue Lagoon: A geothermal spa that has become one of the most popular tourist attractions in Iceland.
  • The Bridge Between Continents: A footbridge that connects the European and North American tectonic plates.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keflavík

Keflavík has several hiking and biking routes. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Seltjarnarnes Peninsula Nature Reserve: A beautiful area for walking, hiking, and birdwatching.
  • The Reykjanes Geopark: A unique geothermal area with hiking trails and a visitor center.
  • Bike rentals are also available throughout Keflavík.

Best Things to Do in Keflavík: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Several tour companies offer whale watching tours from Keflavík harbor.
  • ATV Tours: An excellent way to explore the rugged terrain and stunning landscapes around Keflavík.
  • Horseback Riding: A unique way to experience the Icelandic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keflavík

Day 1: Arrive in Keflavík, explore the town, and visit the Viking World Museum. Day 2: Take a whale watching tour, and visit the Reykjanes Heritage Museum. Day 3: Visit the Blue Lagoon, and explore Grindavík. Day 4: Explore the Reykjanes Geopark, hike or bike one of the trails. Day 5: Take an ATV tour, and ride horses in the Icelandic countryside. Day 6: Visit the Duus Museum, walk around the harbor, and enjoy the local food and drink scene. Day 7: Depart from Keflavík Airport.

Food and Drink Scene of Keflavík

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A popular Icelandic dish that is made with freshly caught fish.
  • Icelandic Lamb: Known for its unique flavor, Icelandic lamb is a must-try.
  • Pylsur: Icelandic hot dogs that are a cultural icon.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: A traditional Icelandic schnapps.
  • Viking Beer: A locally brewed beer.
  • Icelandic Glacier Water: Some of the purest water in the world.

Where to Stay in Keflavík: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Keflavík: A four-star hotel with an airport shuttle and a spa.
  • Hotel Jazz: A stylish hotel in the heart of the town.
  • Guesthouse Keflavík: A budget-friendly option close to the airport.

What to Buy in Keflavík: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lopapeysa: An Icelandic sweater that is both warm and stylish.
  • Skyr: A high-protein, low-fat dairy product that is similar to yogurt.
  • Icelandic Wool Products: From blankets to hats, Icelandic wool products are known for their quality and warmth.

Local Culture and Customs of Keflavík

Icelandic culture is deeply rooted in its history and landscapes. It is known for its art, music, literature, and traditions, including:

  • The Icelandic Sagas: These are the classic texts that tell the story of Iceland’s settlement era.
  • The Northern Lights: Iceland is one of the best places in the world to see the Aurora Borealis.
  • Huldufólk: Iceland’s “hidden people,” who are said to live in boulders and caves.

Best Time to Visit Keflavík

The best time to visit Keflavík is from May to September when the weather is the mildest, and the days are longer. However, keep in mind that this is also the busiest time of the year, and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keflavík

Do:

  • Do bring warm clothing, even in the summer.
  • Do try the local food and drink.
  • Do respect Iceland’s unique environment and natural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather.
  • Don’t disrespect the wildlife or natural landmarks.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keflavík

Keflavík is a safe destination for travelers. However, like anywhere else, you should always take basic safety precautions, such as locking your doors and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Keflavík

There are several medical facilities in Keflavík, including a hospital and several clinics. It is essential to have comprehensive travel insurance and keep it with you at all times.

Getting to Keflavík: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keflavík

Keflavík International Airport is the main gateway to Iceland. It is located only 3 kilometers from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Keflavík

Strætó is the public bus company that serves the Greater Reykjavík area, including Keflavík.

Other Options

Several car rental companies operate in Keflavík, and there are also taxi services available.

How to Get Around in Keflavík

The best way to get around Keflavík is by car. However, some tour companies offer transportation for their activities and tours.

Where to Go Next from Keflavík: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Reykjavík: Iceland’s vibrant capital city is only 30 kilometers away from Keflavík.
  • The Golden Circle: One of Iceland’s most popular tourist routes, which includes Þingvellir National Park, Gullfoss waterfall, and Geysir geothermal area.
  • The South Coast: A scenic drive along the south coast of Iceland, which includes several waterfalls, black sand beaches, and glaciers.

Conclusion

Keflavík offers a unique blend of culture and adventure, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic Icelandic experience. From the natural wonders to historic landmarks, Keflavík has something for everyone.
