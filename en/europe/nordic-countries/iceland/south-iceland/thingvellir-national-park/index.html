Introduction to Þingvellir National Park

Þingvellir National Park is an iconic landmark in Iceland and one of the country’s most significant historical and geological sites. The park is located in South Iceland, around 40 kilometers northeast of the capital city Reykjavik. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its unique natural features, including the tectonic rift between the North American and Eurasian plates and impressive lava fields.

List of Places to Visit in Þingvellir National Park

Here are some of the best places to visit in Þingvellir National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Almannagjá Rift: The Almannagjá Rift is an impressive geological formation and a historical site. This rift is where the world’s first parliament, the Althing, was established in 930 AD.
  • Þingvellir Church: The Þingvellir Church is a small church located in the park, and it is one of Iceland’s oldest churches, dating back to the 11th century.
  • Visitor Center Þingvellir: The Visitor Center Þingvellir provides essential information about the park’s history and geology. It is an excellent starting point for your visit as it offers an extensive exhibition showcasing the park as well as documentaries in several different languages.
  • Lögberg: The Lögberg is also known as the Law Rock, which is where ancient Icelandic laws were recited.
  • Nikulásargjá: Nikulásargjá is a beautiful fissure located in the park. It is a popular salmon fishing spot during the summer months.

Natural Attractions of Þingvellir National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silfra: Silfra is a fissure filled with crystal-clear water. It offers some of the best snorkeling and diving experiences in the world.
  • Öxarárfoss Waterfall: The Öxarárfoss Waterfall is a beautiful and serene waterfall located in the park, and visitors can enjoy a nice walk through the surrounding area.
  • Lake Þingvallavatn: Þingvallavatn Lake is the largest natural freshwater lake in Iceland, covering an area of 84 square kilometers. The lake is home to a wide variety of fish, including trout and Arctic char, making it an excellent place for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Þingvellir National Park

Hiking and biking are both popular activities in Þingvellir National Park. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Flosagjá: Flosagjá is a beautiful gorge located in the park, and it is around 500 meters long. This hike is relatively easy, and the gorge provides several great photo opportunities along the way.
  • The path between Thingvellir and Lyngdalsheidi: This route is a little more challenging than some of the other hikes in the park, but it provides an unparalleled view of the surrounding landscape. The trailhead is located in Thingvellir, and the route is around 18 kilometers long.
  • The Thingvellir Lake Loop: The Thingvellir Lake Loop is a relatively easy route that goes around the lake. It provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape, and it is around 4 kilometers long.

Best Things to Do in Þingvellir National Park: Activities and Tours

There are numerous exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Þingvellir National Park, including:

  • Silfra Snorkeling and Diving: As mentioned earlier, Silfra provides some of the best snorkeling and diving experiences in the world.
  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Iceland, and it is an excellent way to explore the surroundings.
  • Golden Circle Tour: The Golden Circle Tour is one of the most popular tours in Iceland, and it includes stops at Þingvellir National Park, Geysir, and Gullfoss.

Food and Drink Scene of Þingvellir National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Icelandic Lamb: Iceland is known for its high-quality lamb, and it is a must-try when in the country.
  • Fish and Chips: Fish and chips are a popular and affordable dish in Iceland and are often made from locally sourced fish.
  • Skyr: Skyr is an Icelandic dairy product that has a similar texture to yogurt, but it is higher in protein and lower in fat.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: Often called “Black Death,” Brennivín is an Icelandic alcoholic beverage made from fermented potatoes.
  • Beer: In past years, beer has only been legal in Iceland since 1989. Today, there’s an excellent selection of Icelandic craft beers to sample.

Local Culture and Customs of Þingvellir National Park

Icelandic culture is unique, and it is essential to respect local customs when traveling to the country. Here are some essential things to keep in mind:

  • Icelandic Naming Conventions: Icelandic surnames often end in “-son” or “-dóttir,” indicating the person’s father’s or mother’s name.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not common in Iceland, and it is not expected in most places.
  • Conservation: Icelanders have a deep respect for their natural environment, and it is essential to be mindful of this when traveling in the country.

Best Time to Visit Þingvellir National Park

The best time to visit Þingvellir National Park is during the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is milder, and the days are longer, providing more time to explore the surrounding areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Þingvellir National Park

Do:

  • Respect the Natural Environment: As previously mentioned, Icelanders have a deep respect for their natural environment, and it is essential to be mindful of this when traveling to the country.
  • Dress for the Weather: The weather in Iceland can be unpredictable, and it is essential to dress in layers and bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities.
  • Explore the Surrounding Areas: The surrounding areas of Þingvellir National Park are beautiful and offer plenty of unique experiences.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Icelanders take great pride in their natural environment, and it is essential not to litter or damage the surroundings.
  • Go Off Trail: Sticking to designated trails helps preserve the natural environment and ensures visitors’ safety.
  • Ignore the Weather Warnings: Weather in Iceland can change quickly, and it is essential to pay attention to weather warnings and advisories.

Safety and Health

Safety in Þingvellir National Park

Þingvellir National Park is safe for visitors, but it is essential to be mindful of the following:

  • Be Prepared for the Weather: As previously mentioned, the weather in Iceland can be unpredictable, and it is essential to be prepared and bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities.
  • Watch Your Step: The park has many rocky and uneven trails, and it is important to pay attention to where you are walking to avoid injury.
  • Swimming Safety: Silfra is a popular snorkeling and diving spot, but it is only suitable for experienced swimmers and divers.

Health in Þingvellir National Park

Iceland has an excellent healthcare system, and emergency services are available throughout the country. It is important to have appropriate health insurance when traveling to Iceland.

Getting to Þingvellir National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Þingvellir National Park

The closest airport to Þingvellir National Park is Keflavik International Airport, which is located around 100 kilometers west of the park. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to the park.

Bus Stations Near Þingvellir National Park

The closest bus station to Þingvellir National Park is the Bus Terminal in Reykjavik, which is located around 40 kilometers southwest of the park. From there, visitors can take a bus to the park.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent option for exploring Þingvellir National Park and the surrounding areas. It provides visitors with the flexibility to explore at their own pace and access more remote areas of the park.

How to Get Around in Þingvellir National Park

The best way to get around Þingvellir National Park is by foot or bike. There are many designated trails and paths throughout the park, and biking is an excellent way to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Þingvellir National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting destinations to explore near Þingvellir National Park, including:

  • Geysir: Geysir is a famous geothermal area in Iceland and is known for its impressive hot springs.
  • Gullfoss Waterfall: The Gullfoss Waterfall is one of Iceland’s most famous waterfalls and is located in the Golden Circle.
  • Reykjavik: Reykjavik, Iceland’s capital city, is a vibrant and colorful city known for its unique culture and nightlife.

Conclusion

Þingvellir National Park is an impressive natural and cultural landmark in Iceland and is an essential destination for any traveler exploring the country. From the beautiful natural attractions to the park’s historical significance, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Þingvellir National Park.
