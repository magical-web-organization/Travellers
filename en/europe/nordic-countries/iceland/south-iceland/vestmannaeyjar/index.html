Introduction to Vestmannaeyjar

Vestmannaeyjar, also known as the Westman Islands, is a group of 15 islands and numerous islets off the south coast of Iceland. Only one of these islands, Heimaey, is inhabited, while the others are uninhabited nature reserves. The volcanic archipelago was formed from an underwater eruption in 1963, which is historically known as the largest and most violent eruption that Iceland has witnessed in the past 1,000 years. Vestmannaeyjar is a must-see destination for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers and culture lovers. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable itinerary to the beautiful island of Vestmannaeyjar.

List of Places to Visit in Vestmannaeyjar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Eldheimar Museum: Located on the site of the 1973 volcanic eruption, Eldheimar Museum tells the story of the eruption and how it affected the people of Heimaey.
  2. Vestmannaeyjar Heritage Museum: This museum provides a wealth of information on Vestmannayjar’s history, culture and geology, with special exhibitions on local legends and the lives of fishermen.
  3. Stórhöfði Nature Reserve: Stórhöfði is an important site for birdwatching, but it also serves as a historic place of pilgrimage, where you can find ruins of the first church built on Heimaey.

Natural Attractions of Vestmannaeyjar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Heimaklettur: Heimaklettur, also called the “Home Rock,” is a steep 283-meter climb from the harbor and provides stunning views of the island and surrounding sea.
  2. Sæheimar Aquarium: This aquarium is home to numerous species of fish and aquatic animals, including puffins, seals, and even a rare red fish that can only be found in the waters around the Westman Islands.
  3. Elliðaey Island: Elliðaey is an uninhabited island with a birdwatching tower and lighthouse. The island is a popular spot for hiking and exploration.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vestmannaeyjar

  1. Eldfell Volcano Hike: Take the Eldfell Volcano Hike and climb to the top of the 200-meter-high volcano that erupted in 1973. From the top, you can see magnificent views of the island and surrounding sea.
  2. Stórhöfði Peninsula Hike: Take the Stórhöfði Peninsula Hike and enjoy the scenic views of the island’s coastline and birdlife.
  3. Biking Trails: There are several biking trails available on the island that offer beautiful views and unique experiences. Renting bikes is easy and cost-effective.

Best Things to Do in Vestmannaeyjar: Activities and Tours

  1. Puffin Watching Tour: Take a boat tour to the nearby islands and observe the cute and charming puffins in their natural habitat.
  2. Fishing Trip: Vestmannaeyjar is famous for its bountiful fish, and many boat tours offer guided fishing trips where you can catch your own fish and enjoy a delicious meal made from your catch.
  3. Kayaking Tour: Exploring the coast and secret coves of Vestmannaeyjar by kayak is an unforgettable experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Vestmannaeyjar

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Soup: A creamy soup made with fresh lobster and vegetables, a local specialty you can find in many restaurants in Vestmannaeyjar.
  2. Fish and Chips: One of the most popular seafood dishes in Vestmannaeyjar, made with fresh fish and a crispy coating.
  3. Puffin: While not everyone’s cup of tea, puffin is a traditional dish in Iceland. Try it grilled with root vegetables and a side of lingonberries for a unique culinary experience.

Local Beverages

  1. Brennivín: A traditional Icelandic schnapps made from fermented potatoes and caraway seeds.
  2. Vikur: A local Viking beer found only in Vestmannaeyjar.

What to Buy in Vestmannaeyjar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Wool Products: Icelandic wool is famous for its softness and durability. Find unique items like hats, scarves, and blankets made from local wool in Vestmannaeyjar.

  3. Icelandic Design Items: Discover a variety of items from local designers and artists in souvenir shops located in town.

Local Culture and Customs of Vestmannaeyjar

In Vestmannaeyjar, fishing is the main industry and has a significant impact on the lifestyle and culture of the locals. The festivals, such as the Vestmannaeyjar Festival, the oldest festival in Iceland, celebrate the fishing industry and folk traditions. Additionally, the Icelandic language is still preserved and widely spoken by the residents. It is always considered respectful to greet the locals in Icelandic when visiting their homes or establishments.

Best Time to Visit Vestmannaeyjar

The most popular time to visit Vestmannaeyjar is between June and August because of the mild weather and long daylight hours. However, the island’s charm is year-round, with winter activities such as skiing and snowmobiling making for a unique experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vestmannaeyjar

Vestmannaeyjar is generally a safe place to travel; however, visitors should exercise caution when hiking and boating. Always follow safety regulations provided by tour operators and avoid unnecessary risks when exploring the natural environment.

Health in Vestmannaeyjar

Vestmannaeyjar has a modern healthcare system with a local hospital. However, visitors should ensure that they have travel health insurance before arriving, as medical costs in Iceland can be high.

Getting to Vestmannaeyjar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vestmannaeyjar

The Vestmannaeyjar airport is located on the island of Heimaey, with regular flights from Reykjavik Domestic Airport.

Bus Stations Near Vestmannaeyjar

Buses are available from Reykjavik to Landeyjahöfn, where a ferry takes passengers to Heimaey.

Other Options

There are also several tour operators that offer guided tours and transportation from Reykjavik to Vestmannaeyjar, providing flexibility and convenience.

How to Get Around in Vestmannaeyjar

The town is small and easily walkable, but renting bikes is an excellent option for exploring the island. Taxis are available, and during the summer months, visitors can take a bus to some of the main attractions.

Where to Go Next from Vestmannaeyjar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Reykjavik: The capital city of Iceland is only a short distance from Vestmannaeyjar and offers a wealth of cultural experiences, including exceptional dining, museums, and shopping.
  2. Vik: Located at the southernmost tip of Iceland, Vik is a charming village known for its black sand beaches and towering cliffs.
  3. Skaftafell National Park: Known for its natural beauty, the Skaftafell National Park offers opportunities for hiking, camping, and exploring glacier lagoons.
