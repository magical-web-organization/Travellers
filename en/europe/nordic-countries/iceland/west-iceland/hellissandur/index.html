Introduction to Hellissandur

Nestled in West Iceland on the Snaefellsnes Peninsula, Hellissandur is a charming small town that offers visitors a glimpse into Iceland’s cultural heritage and natural beauty. The town is situated next to the beautiful Breiðafjörður Bay and surrounded by stunning mountain ranges, making it a prime destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. With a population of just over 400 people, Hellissandur is an ideal place to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and experience the tranquility and calm of rural Iceland.

List of Places to Visit in Hellissandur

From museums and historical sites to breathtaking natural wonders, the town of Hellissandur has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hellissandur Church: This beautiful wooden church, built in 1902, is one of the town’s most iconic landmarks. It features a unique design and an interior filled with natural light.

  • Bárður Snæfellsás Statue: This impressive statue, located near the harbor, commemorates Bárður Snæfellsás, a legendary Icelandic giant who is said to have lived in the nearby Snæfellsjökull glacier.

  • Snæfellsjökull National Park: This stunning national park is home to the iconic Snæfellsjökull glacier, as well as a variety of other natural wonders, such as lava fields and stunning coastline.

Natural Attractions of Hellissandur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kirkjufell Mountain: Known as one of the most photographed spots in Iceland, Kirkjufell Mountain is a must-see for anyone visiting the area. The mountain offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and photography.

  • Lóndrangar: These twin rock pinnacles offer a unique sight, standing tall against the stunning backdrop of the sea. Visitors can hike the nearby trails for a closer look or take in the view from the nearby bird-watching observatory.

  • Rauðisandur Beach: Located just a short drive from Hellissandur, this stunning red sand beach offers a unique and breathtaking photo opportunity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hellissandur

For outdoor enthusiasts, Hellissandur is an ideal destination for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Snæfellsjökull Glacier: This stunning glacier is a popular hiking destination, offering visitors the chance to explore its stunning ice formations and breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

  • Kerlingarfjöll: Located near Hellissandur, this mountain range offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, each with its own unique views of the landscape.

  • Snaefellsness National Park: This stunning national park is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging, and offering a range of stunning views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Hellissandur: Activities and Tours

In addition to its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities, Hellissandur has many other activities and tours to offer its visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • Whale Watching Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour from nearby Grundarfjörður and spot humpback whales, minke whales, and other marine life in their natural habitat.

  • Horseback Riding Tours: Visitors can take a guided horseback riding tour through the stunning Icelandic countryside, with stunning views of the mountains and coastline.

  • Northern Lights Tours: During the winter months, visitors can take a guided tour to see the stunning northern lights, a natural phenomenon that is best witnessed in the dark night skies of Iceland.

Food and Drink Scene of Hellissandur

Although a small town, Hellissandur offers a variety of delicious and authentic Icelandic dishes to try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangikjöt: This traditional Icelandic smoked lamb dish is a must-try for visitors looking to experience local cuisine.

  • Skyr: This Icelandic yogurt is a staple of the local diet, with many delicious flavors to choose from.

  • Kleinur: These traditional Icelandic pastries are a sweet and tasty treat, perfect for dessert or a midday snack.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivín: This traditional Icelandic liquor is made from fermented potato mash and flavored with caraway seeds. A must-try for those looking for a unique, strong drink.

  • Egils Gull: This Icelandic beer is a popular local brew, with a light and refreshing taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Hellissandur

Icelandic culture is rich and diverse, with many unique customs and traditions that are celebrated in Hellissandur and beyond. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect for Nature: Icelanders have a deep respect for the natural beauty and resources of their country, and visitors are encouraged to abide by the principles of leave no trace and sustainable tourism.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not a common practice in Iceland, as workers are well-paid and don’t expect additional gratuities.

Getting to Hellissandur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hellissandur is located on the Westfjords peninsula, which is easily accessible by both car and bus. Air travel is also possible, with flights available to nearby cities such as Reykjavik and Akureyri.

Airports Near Hellissandur

  • Reykjavik Keflavik International Airport: This is the largest airport in Iceland, located approximately 200km from Hellissandur.

  • Akureyri International Airport: This airport is located approximately 338km from Hellissandur and offers flights to several other locations in Iceland.

Bus Stations Near Hellissandur

  • Borgarnes Bus Station: From this station, visitors can take a bus to nearby towns and cities such as Stykkishólmur and Akureyri.

  • Reykjavik Bus Terminal: This is the largest bus terminal in Iceland, offering connections to many towns and cities around the country.

Other Options

  • Taxis and private transportation services are also available, although they can be more expensive than other options.
