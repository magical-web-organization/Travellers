Introduction to Snæfellsjökull National Park

Located in West Iceland, Snæfellsjökull National Park is a breathtaking natural wonder that offers visitors a variety of outdoor adventures, scenic views, and culturally significant attractions. The park spans over 170 square kilometers and is home to Snæfellsjökull glacier, which is often referred to as “the jewel of West Iceland.” The park’s diverse landscape includes lava fields, sandy beaches, sea cliffs, and mountains, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Snæfellsjökull National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Snæfellsstofa Visitor Center: This visitor center is the perfect place to start your journey through Snæfellsjökull National Park. You can learn about the history, geology, and wildlife of the park through interactive exhibits and videos.

  2. Örlygshöfn: This historical site is believed to be the location where the first settlers arrived in Iceland. Visitors can learn about Iceland’s early history and see a replica of a Viking longhouse.

  3. Búðakirkja: This famous black church is a unique and historic landmark in Snæfellsjökull National Park. It was built in 1703 and is surrounded by beautiful lava fields and mountains.

Natural Attractions of Snæfellsjökull National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Snæfellsjökull glacier: A must-see attraction in the park, Snæfellsjökull glacier is over 1,400 meters high and was famously featured in Jules Verne’s “Journey to the Center of the Earth.” Visitors can hike to the top of the glacier or go on a guided tour.

  2. Arnarstapi: This scenic village is located on the coast and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can stroll along the coastline, hike to the nearby cliffs, or take a boat tour to see the nearby sea caves.

  3. Djúpalónssandur Beach: This black volcanic beach is a unique and secluded spot in Snæfellsjökull National Park. It is home to interesting rock formations and is a popular spot for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Snæfellsjökull National Park

  1. Snæfellsjökull Glacier Hike: This challenging hike takes visitors to the top of the glacier and offers panoramic views of the park and surrounding area.

  2. Rauðfeldsgjá Gorge: This moderate hike takes visitors through a narrow canyon and offers views of a waterfall and cliffs.

  3. Hellnar to Arnarstapi Coastal Walk: This easy walk takes visitors along the coast and offers stunning views of the ocean and coastal cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Snæfellsjökull National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Whale Watching: Visitors can take a boat tour from Arnarstapi or Ólafsvík to see whales, dolphins, and other marine life in the Atlantic Ocean.

  2. Caving: Guided tours of the Vatnshellir lava cave offer visitors a unique underground experience.

  3. Northern Lights Viewing: Snæfellsjökull National Park is an ideal place to see the Northern Lights, especially between September and April.

Food and Drink Scene of Snæfellsjökull National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster: The cold waters surrounding Iceland produce some of the world’s best lobster. Many of the restaurants in Snæfellsjökull National Park serve fresh and delicious lobster dishes.

  2. Fish and Chips: Iceland’s traditional fish and chips are a must-try. Many of the local restaurants serve fresh and delicious fish, often caught in the nearby waters.

  3. Skyr: This traditional Icelandic yogurt is a delicious and healthy snack or breakfast option.

Local Beverages

  1. Brennivín: This traditional Icelandic liquor is distilled from potatoes and flavored with caraway seeds. It is often served as a shot.

  2. Icelandic Beer: The craft beer scene in Iceland is booming, and many of the local breweries offer unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Snæfellsjökull National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hellnar Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is located in the village of Hellnar and offers stunning views of the park and coastline.

  2. Fosshotel Hellnar: This modern hotel is located in Hellnar and offers comfortable accommodations and stunning views of the park.

  3. Ólafsvík Guesthouse: This family-run guesthouse is located in the town of Ólafsvík and offers cozy and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Snæfellsjökull National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Wool Products: Iceland’s sheep produce some of the finest wool in the world, and many of the local shops offer hand-knitted wool sweaters, hats, and scarves.

  2. Lava Rock Jewelry: The volcanic landscape of Snæfellsjökull National Park inspires many local jewelers to create unique and beautiful lava rock jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Snæfellsjökull National Park

Icelandic culture is unique and fascinating, and visitors should be aware of some customs and traditions while visiting Snæfellsjökull National Park. It is important to respect the environment and leave no trace, as well as to adhere to the laws and regulations of the park. Icelandic people are generally friendly and welcoming, and visitors should feel free to ask for help or advice during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Snæfellsjökull National Park

The best time to visit Snæfellsjökull National Park is from May to September, when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, visitors can also enjoy the park during the winter months, when they can see the Northern Lights and go skiing or snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Snæfellsjökull National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the environment and wildlife.
  2. Follow the laws and regulations of the park.
  3. Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash or waste in the park.
  2. Disturb wildlife or damage natural resources.
  3. Ignore warning signs or safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Snæfellsjökull National Park

Visitors to Snæfellsjökull National Park should be aware of the dangers of the glacier, including avalanches and crevasses. It is important to always go on a guided tour or to have experience in glacier travel before attempting to hike the glacier alone. Visitors should also be aware of the potential risks of outdoor activities in the park, such as hiking and caving.

Health in Snæfellsjökull National Park

The water in Snæfellsjökull National Park is safe to drink, and there are no major health risks associated with visiting the park. Visitors should be aware of the potential risks of outdoor activities, such as hypothermia and dehydration, and take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Snæfellsjökull National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Snæfellsjökull National Park

  1. Reykjavik Airport (RKV): This airport is located in the capital city of Iceland and offers domestic flights to nearby airports.

  2. Keflavik International Airport (KEF): This airport is located about 2 hours from Snæfellsjökull National Park and offers international flights.

Bus Stations Near Snæfellsjökull National Park

  1. Borgarnes Bus Station: This bus station is located about 50 kilometers from Snæfellsjökull National Park and offers bus services to nearby towns.

  2. Grundarfjörður Bus Stop: This bus stop is located near the town of Grundarfjörður and offers bus services to nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby towns to reach Snæfellsjökull National Park.

How to Get Around in Snæfellsjökull National Park

The best way to explore Snæfellsjökull National Park is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bikes in nearby towns or bring their own. There are also guided tours available that offer transportation to and from the park.

Where to Go Next from Snæfellsjökull National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Reykjavik: Iceland’s capital city is just a few hours’ drive from Snæfellsjökull National Park and offers a variety of cultural attractions and nightlife.

  2. Golden Circle: This popular tourist route is located near Reykjavik and includes some of Iceland’s most famous attractions, including Geysir, Gullfoss waterfall, and Thingvellir National Park.

Conclusion

Snæfellsjökull National Park is a unique and fascinating destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventurers to culture enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the park’s diverse landscape, learn about Iceland’s rich history and traditions, and enjoy delicious local food and drink. With its stunning natural beauty, Snæfellsjökull National Park is a must-see destination for any traveler to Iceland.
