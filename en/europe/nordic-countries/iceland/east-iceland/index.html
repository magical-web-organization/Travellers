Introduction to East Iceland

East Iceland is a hidden gem nestled in the Nordic country of Iceland, known for its breathtaking natural beauty and unique cultural heritage. This region boasts of stunning glaciers, charming fishing villages, and iconic waterfalls that attract visitors from around the globe. With an abundance of hiking trails, outdoor activities, museums, and historical landmarks, East Iceland is a must-visit destination for any adventurer, history buff, or nature lover.

List of Places to Visit in East Iceland

Explore East Iceland’s rich culture and spectacular landscapes by visiting these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seydisfjordur Information Centre: This charming town is home to several historical landmarks and museums, including the Nordic House, which showcases the region’s cultural and artistic heritage.
  • Hengifoss: This awe-inspiring waterfall is a testament to the earth’s geological evolution and is surrounded by colorful basalt rock formations and stunning hiking trails.
  • Storagerdi Fort: This former military fort, now a museum, is a testament to Iceland’s strategic importance and its role in the Cold War era.
  • Minjasafn Austurlands: Discover East Iceland’s rich history and cultural heritage through artifacts and exhibits in this fascinating museum.

Natural Attractions of East Iceland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vatnajokull National Park: Explore the largest glacier in Europe and the stunning landscapes surrounding it, including the famous Diamond Beach and hiking trails.
  • Stokksnes Peninsula: This breathtakingly beautiful peninsula boasts of stunning black sand beaches, majestic mountains, and iconic Viking villages.
  • Hornstrandir Nature Reserve: This remote and stunning nature reserve boasts of stunning wildlife, breathtaking landscapes, and excellent hiking trails.
  • Gufufoss: This hidden gem is a lesser-known waterfall but no less beautiful than others in the area; it is located near the historic Skriduklaustur monastery and is a must-visit for all nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Iceland

East Iceland offers an abundance of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of skill and experience. Some of our top picks include:

  • Kverkfjöll Glacier Trail: This challenging trek offers breathtaking views of the Kverkfjoll Mountains and the Vatnajökull Glacier.
  • Borgarfjordur Eystri Mountain Trail: This moderate hike provides visitors with excellent views of the imposing Dyrfjoll Mountains and the colorful wildflowers of hvalnes.
  • Papey Island Hike: This moderate hike takes visitors on a scenic tour, crossing the waters to the uninhabited Papey island and back.

Best Things to Do in East Iceland: Activities and Tours

East Iceland offers an array of activities and tours that cater to diverse interests and budgets, including:

  • Whale watching: Experience the thrill of the Atlantic Ocean’s waters and get up close and personal with humpback whales, orcas, dolphins, and seals.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore Iceland’s stunning landscapes through one of its unique forms of transportation - Icelandic horses.
  • Northern Lights Tour: Visitors can enjoy the breathtaking views of the aurora borealis from various locations across East Iceland.
  • Kayaking Tours: Explore the fascinating coastline of East Iceland by kayaking through its crystal clear waters and stunning fjords.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Iceland

Day 1: Explore the colorful Seydisfjordur town and visit the Nordic House and Minjasafn Austurlands. Day 2: Visit Hengifoss waterfall and take a hike to its colorful base. Day 3: Explore the Vatnajokull National Park, the largest glacier in Europe. Day 4: Visit the remote and charming fishing villages of Stokksnes and Höfn. Day 5: Hike through the stunningly beautiful Borgarfjardur Eystri Mountain Trail. Day 6: Take a whale watching tour or horseback riding tour. Day 7: Enjoy the Northern Lights Tour.

Food and Drink Scene of East Iceland

East Iceland is known for its unique cuisine, fresh seafood, and locally sourced ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hangikjöt: This traditional Icelandic dish consists of smoked lamb and is usually served with potatoes and peas.
  • Fiskur plokkfiskur: This Icelandic version of fish pie is made with butter, milk, and mashed fish and is often served with rye bread.
  • Skyr: This traditional Icelandic yogurt is often served for breakfast with fresh berries, fruit, and nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Brennivin: This Icelandic spirit made from mashed potatoes and caraway seeds is often referred to as “black death” and is best served chilled.
  • Bjóri: Icelandic beer is gaining popularity worldwide, with many breweries using locally sourced ingredients to create unique flavors.

Where to Stay in East Iceland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Iceland’s accommodation options cater to all budgets and preferences, including luxurious hotels, cozy guesthouses, and affordable hostels. Some of our recommendations include:

  • Hotel Aldan: Located in the charming fishing village of Seydisfjordur, this historic hotel boasts of comfortable rooms, stunning views, and an in-house restaurant.
  • Blabjorg Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse offers affordable accommodation, friendly service, and breathtaking views of the Hornafjordur fjord.
  • Berunes Hostel: This rustic hostel, located in a remote and scenic location, offers comfortable dormitory-style rooms and welcomes visitors with a friendly and inviting atmosphere.

What to Buy in East Iceland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Iceland boasts of unique and locally made souvenirs and products that make for perfect gifts for family and friends, including:

  • Icelandic wool products: From cozy hats and scarves to warm blankets and sweaters, Icelandic wool products are known for their quality and durability.
  • Blueberry Jam: Made from locally sourced blueberries, East Iceland’s Blueberry Jam is a popular and delicious souvenir.
  • Reindeer Antlers: Reindeer is a common sight in East Iceland, and reindeer antlers make for a unique and fascinating souvenir or wall decoration.

Local Culture and Customs of East Iceland

Icelanders value their cultural heritage, and the East region is no exception. Visitors are encouraged to discover and respect Icelandic customs and traditions, including:

  • Respect for Nature: Icelanders have a deep respect for their natural environment and expect visitors to do the same. Visitors are expected to stay on hiking trails and not leave anything behind.
  • Language: Icelandic is the official language of Iceland, with English being widely spoken. Visitors are often encouraged to learn a few basic Icelandic phrases to make communication easier.

Best Time to Visit East Iceland

The best time to visit East Iceland is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, the winter months also have their merits, with the Northern Lights being visible from September to March. Visitors are advised to dress warmly and wear waterproof clothing regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Iceland

Visitors are encouraged to follow these do’s and don’ts when visiting East Iceland:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and follow the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Learn a few basic Icelandic phrases to make communication easier.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Leave trails or litter.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore traffic signs and warnings.

Safety and Health

Iceland is a relatively safe country with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to be mindful of natural hazards, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Additionally, visitors are encouraged to have travel insurance and seek medical attention if needed.

Safety in East Iceland

  • Practice common sense and avoid risky behavior.
  • Follow safety instructions provided by tour and activity providers.

Health in East Iceland

  • Iceland has a robust healthcare system, and emergency services are available 24/7.
  • Visitors are advised to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to East Iceland: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Iceland is easily accessible by air and land, with several options available to visitors.

Airports Near East Iceland

  • Egilsstaðir Airport
  • Húsavík Airport
  • Akureyri Airport

Bus Stations Near East Iceland

  • Seydisfjordur Bus Station
  • Egilsstaðir Bus Station
  • Húsavík Bus Station

Other Options

  • Ferries: Visitors can take a ferry from Denmark and the Faroe Islands to Seydisfjordur.
  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from major cities in Iceland and drive to East Iceland.

How to Get Around in East Iceland

Public transportation in East Iceland is limited, and visitors are encouraged to rent a car to explore the region. Alternatively, tour operators and taxis are available, but visitors should expect higher costs.

Where to Go Next from East Iceland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from East Iceland include:

  • North Iceland: Explore the stunning landscape of North Iceland, including the iconic Lake Myvatn and the otherworldly lava formations.
  • South Iceland: Explore the stunning waterfalls and glaciers of South Iceland, including the famous Seljalandsfoss Waterfall.
  • Westfjords: This remote and untouched region is home to stunning fjords, rugged coastlines, and charming fishing villages.

Conclusion

East Iceland is a stunning and vibrant region that offers visitors an unforgettable and unique experience. From the natural wonders to the cultural heritage, this region will capture your heart and spark your imagination. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to East Iceland today!
