Guelma, a charming city in Northeast Algeria, is a hidden gem that has not yet been fully discovered by the tourist crowd. If you’re looking for a off-the-beaten-path destination that offers a rich cultural experience and natural beauty, Guelma is the right place to go. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a deep dive into what Guelma has to offer and help you plan your perfect trip.

Introduction to Guelma

Guelma is located in the Hodna region of Northeast Algeria, approximately 400 kilometers east of Algiers, the capital city of Algeria. Guelma is known for its vibrant culture, stunning landscapes and amazing outdoor activities. The city has a long history, with evidence of human settlements dating back to prehistoric times.

List of Places to Visit in Guelma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée Public National de Numidie - This museum showcases the rich history of the Numidian civilization. It offers visitors an insight into the Numidian culture and their way of life.
  • Guelma’s Roman Ruins - A must-visit site, the ruins are an important testimony to the Roman presence in North Africa. Walk through the ruins and admire the architectural marvels that have stood the test of time.
  • The Great Mosque of Guelma - Built in the 17th century, the mosque is a fine example of Algerian architecture. It is a religious site and a cultural landmark of the city.

Natural Attractions of Guelma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Guelma National Park - This national park is a nature lover’s paradise. It is home to an extensive range of flora and fauna, and offers visitors the chance to see animals like the Barbary macaque and the golden jackal.
  • El Milia Falls - One of Algeria’s most beautiful waterfalls, El Milia Falls is a natural wonder worth visiting. The falls are surrounded by lush greenery and offer amazing views.
  • Tell Atlas Mountains - For those who love hiking and trekking, the Tell Atlas Mountains are a dream. Explore the mountains and admire the stunning landscapes of Algeria.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guelma

  • Guelma to Aïn Beida Hike - This hike takes you through stunning landscapes and offers breathtaking views of the region.
  • Aïn Beida to Bouhamdane Hike - This hike is perfect for those who love challenging hikes. The route offers amazing views of the region.
  • Bouhamdane to El Ma Labiod Hike - This hike is the perfect way to explore the Atlas Mountains. It is a challenging hike but offers amazing views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Guelma: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the local markets - The local markets are a great place to get a feel for the local culture and pick up souvenirs.
  • Take a cooking class - Learn how to cook traditional Algerian dishes with a local chef.
  • Visit the city’s parks and gardens - Take a stroll through the city’s parks and gardens, and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Guelma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Couscous - A traditional North African dish, couscous is a must-try when in Algeria.
  • Tajine - A slow-cooked stew made of meat, vegetables, and spices, tajine is another popular dish in Algeria.
  • Msemna - A pastry filled with spiced meat or vegetable mix, Msemna is a popular breakfast dish.

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea - A staple in Algerian culture, mint tea is a refreshing beverage that is served at almost every meal.
  • Thé à la menthe glacé - A sweet and refreshing cold mint tea.

Where to Stay in Guelma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel El-Boustene - A 4-star hotel that offers great rooms and amenities.
  • Hotel El Orouba - A budget-friendly option for those who want a comfortable stay without breaking the bank.
  • Dar Mnar - A guesthouse that offers a unique experience for those who want to stay in a traditional Algerian home.

What to Buy in Guelma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Algerian clothing - Buy traditional clothing like a kaftan or djellaba as a souvenir.
  • Handwoven carpets - Handwoven carpets are a popular souvenir in Algeria. You can find them in the city’s markets.
  • Pottery and ceramics - Algeria is known for its beautiful pottery and ceramics. You can find them in local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Guelma

  • Islam is the dominant religion in Algeria, and you should respect the local customs and traditions.
  • It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a mosque or a home.

Best Time to Visit Guelma

The best time to visit Guelma is from March to June and September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant and the landscapes are beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guelma

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Negotiate prices when shopping in the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Photograph people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guelma

Guelma is a safe city to visit, but it is always a good idea to take precautions. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Guelma

Make sure to drink bottled water, and avoid tap water. It is also a good idea to bring insect repellent, as mosquitoes can be a problem in some areas.

Getting to Guelma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Guelma

  • Algiers International Airport - The nearest international airport to Guelma.
  • Constantine International Airport - Another option for those flying to Algeria.

Bus Stations Near Guelma

  • Guelma Bus Station - The main bus station in Guelma.
  • Constantine Bus Station - Another option for those travelling by bus in Algeria.

Other Options

You can also travel to Guelma by train. The city has a train station that is connected to other parts of Algeria.

How to Get Around in Guelma

You can get around Guelma by using taxis, which are affordable and convenient. You can also rent a car, but be aware that the roads can be narrow and winding.

Where to Go Next from Guelma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Constantine - A city located 90 kilometers from Guelma. Constantine is known for its beautiful architecture and stunning views.
  • Timgad - An ancient Roman city located 120 kilometers from Guelma. Timgad is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the best-preserved Roman sites in Algeria.

Conclusion

Guelma is a hidden gem that offers a unique travel experience. From the stunning landscapes to the rich cultural heritage, there is much to discover in this charming city. Use this guide to plan your perfect trip to Guelma and immerse yourself in Algeria’s beautiful culture and history.
