Introduction to Biskra

Biskra is a vibrant oasis city located in the northeastern region of Algeria. Known as the “Queen of the Zibans,” Biskra boasts of a rich and diverse culture, stunning natural beauty, and a warm, welcoming people. Explore this bustling city and immerse yourself in its desert landscapes, cultural heritage, and culinary delights. Use this guide to plan your trip to Biskra and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Biskra

Biskra offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Algeria. Here are the top places to visit in Biskra:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Biskra Museum: The Biskra Museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region, including archaeological artifacts, cultural exhibits, and historical documents.
  • El Kantara Bridge: This iconic bridge, constructed in 1863, spans the Oued El Abiodh river and is a symbol of the city’s history and engineering prowess.
  • Roman Ruins of Timgad: About an hour’s drive from Biskra, the Roman ruins of Timgad are a UNESCO World Heritage site and offer a glimpse into the Roman Empire’s past.

Natural Attractions of Biskra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saharan Oasis: Biskra is a desert city surrounded by lush oases, such as Zibans and Ouled Djellal, and makes for a perfect location for nature lovers.
  • Djebel Sahro: A mountain range that offers hiking opportunities, scenic views, and a chance to experience Berber culture.
  • Le Rocher: Le Rocher, also known as the Finger of God, is a rock formation that provides a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding desert landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biskra

Biskra is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Ghoufi Canyon: A breathtaking canyon located near Biskra that offers hiking, swimming, and camping opportunities.
  • Djebel M’Zab: A mountain range with multiple trails that lead to stunning viewpoints and natural wonders.
  • Oasis Circuit: A 20-kilometer bike tour of Biskra’s lush oases, which provides a glimpse into the region’s agricultural heritage.

Best Things to Do in Biskra: Activities and Tours

Biskra offers a range of activities and tours for tourists, including:

  • Cultural Tours: Guided tours of the city’s cultural attractions, such as the Biskra Museum, El Kantara Bridge, and historic mosques.
  • Desert Tours: Explore the Sahara Desert and experience a camel ride, star-gazing, and camping under the stars.
  • Food Tours: Taste authentic Algerian dishes and explore the city’s culinary scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biskra

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Biskra:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel, explore the city’s markets, and shop for souvenirs.

Day 2: Cultural Tour of Biskra’s historical attractions.

Day 3: Hiking or biking in the Djebel Sahro mountain range.

Day 4: Explore the nearby Ghoufi Canyon and swim in its beautiful natural pools.

Day 5: Take a tour of the Sahara Desert, ride a camel, and camp under the stars.

Day 6: Relax and enjoy the city’s food scene.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Biskra

Algerian cuisine is a delicious blend of Berber, Mediterranean, and Arabic influences. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Biskra:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Couscous: A traditional Algerian dish made of steamed semolina, served with vegetables or meat.
  • Tagine: A slow-cooked stew made with meat, vegetables, and spices.
  • Makroudh: A sweet pastry made by deep-frying a dough filled with dates or almonds.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: A sweet and refreshing tea made with fresh mint and lots of sugar.
  • Algerian Coffee: A strong and aromatic coffee, typically served with dates or sweets.

Where to Stay in Biskra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Biskra offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Biskra Palace Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, offering stunning views and top-notch amenities.
  • Hotel El Kantara: Located near the El Kantara Bridge, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful outdoor pool.
  • Amansou Guesthouse: A traditional Algerian guesthouse that offers a unique cultural experience and personalized service.

What to Buy in Biskra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Biskra is famous for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, carpets, and woven baskets. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy:

  • Pottery and Ceramics: Decorative and functional items made from clay, hand-painted with intricate designs.
  • Carpets: Hand-woven carpets, made with traditional Berber designs and techniques.
  • Dates: A local delicacy, grown in Biskra’s lush oases, and considered one of the world’s best dates.

Local Culture and Customs of Biskra

Algeria is a Muslim country, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: Women should cover their heads and wear loose-fitting clothing that covers their arms and legs.
  • Avoid Public Displays of Affection: Public displays of affection are not acceptable in Algeria.
  • Respect Religious Customs: Remove your shoes before entering mosques and other religious places.

Best Time to Visit Biskra

The best time to visit Biskra is between November and April when the weather is mild, and the oases are in full bloom. During the summer months, the weather can be very hot, and it’s advisable to plan outdoor activities early in the morning or late in the afternoon.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biskra

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Biskra:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly, be respectful of local religious customs, and show cultural sensitivity.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Algerian cuisine is delicious and worth exploring.
  • Bargain at the Markets: Bargaining is a common practice in Algerian markets and souks.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: Stick to bottled water to avoid getting sick.
  • Take Pictures Without Permission: Always ask before taking pictures of people, especially women and children.
  • Travel Alone at Night: It’s safer to travel in groups, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Biskra is generally a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Biskra

  • Avoid Demonstrations: Political demonstrations and protests often occur in Algeria and can turn violent.
  • Be Cautious When Crossing the Street: Drivers may not always follow traffic rules or signals.
  • Stay Alert in Crowded Places: Pickpocketing and other petty crimes can occur in crowded places, so it’s essential to stay vigilant.

Health in Biskra

  • Drink Bottled Water: Algeria’s tap water is not safe to drink, so it’s essential to stick to bottled water.
  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: The sun can be intense in Biskra, so it’s crucial to wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses.

Getting to Biskra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Biskra is easily accessible by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near Biskra

  • Biskra Mohamed Khider Airport: Located about 10 kilometers from the city center, the airport serves national and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Biskra

  • Biskra Central Bus Station: The city’s main bus station, with regular services to national and regional destinations.
  • Biskra Gare Routiere: A smaller bus station, located near the city center, with services to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

  • Train: Biskra has a train station that connects the city to other Algerian cities.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Biskra and are a convenient mode of transportation within the city.

How to Get Around in Biskra

Biskra is a compact city, and most of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis are readily available and are an affordable mode of transportation, especially for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Biskra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Biskra is a perfect base for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Laghouat: A historic city, famous for its traditional handicrafts and stunning architecture.
  • Constantine: Known as the “City of Bridges,” Constantine is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage.
  • Sétif: A region famous for its natural beauty, including national parks and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Biskra is a vibrant city with a rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and warm, welcoming people. Plan your trip to Biskra and explore the city’s cultural heritage, natural wonders, and culinary delights. With this guide, you’re sure to make the most of your visit to this beautiful oasis city in Algeria.
