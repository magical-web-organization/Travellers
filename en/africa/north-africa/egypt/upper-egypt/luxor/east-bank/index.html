Introduction to East Bank

Located in Upper Egypt, Luxor’s East Bank is home to some of the most ancient structures in the world, including the Karnak Temple, the Luxor Temple and the Avenue of Sphinxes. Home to many religious, historical and cultural sites, the East Bank of Luxor draws visitors from around the world for a glimpse into Egypt’s rich history.

List of Places to Visit in East Bank

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Karnak Temple

Built over a period of 2,000 years, the Karnak Temple Complex is the largest ancient religious site in the world. Highlights include the Great Hypostyle Hall, the Sacred Lake, and the Temple of Amun.

Luxor Temple

Built in 1400 BC, the Luxor Temple was created as a place of worship for the god Amun. Highlights include the obelisks and the Avenue of Sphinxes.

Natural Attractions of East Bank: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Luxor Corniche

Take a leisurely stroll along the banks of the Nile River and take in the scenic views of the East Bank.

Banana Island

Take a relaxing ferry ride across the Nile to Banana Island and enjoy fresh banana juice, tea, and other refreshments.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Bank

Cycling Tour

Explore hidden gems of East Bank on a bicycle, while enjoying the fresh air and beautiful scenery, including the rural villages, the Nile river and the Beni Hassan Tombs.

Best Things to Do in East Bank: Activities and Tours

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Enjoy a breathtaking sunrise with a hot air balloon ride over the city of Luxor and the Nile River, taking in the ancient sites like Karnak Temple, Luxor Temple, the West and East banks, and the Valley of the Kings.

Felucca Ride

Take a traditional sailboat cruise along the Nile with views of the city’s stunning landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Bank

Day 1: Karnak Temple Complex and Luxor Temple
Day 2: Luxor Museum and Mummification Museum
Day 3: Valley of the Kings and Queens and Hatshepsut’s Temple
Day 4: Colossi of Memnon and Madinat Habu Temple
Day 5: Abydos and Dendera Temples
Day 6: Hot Air Balloon Ride and Local Villages
Day 7: Relaxing on Banana Island and Felucca Ride

Food and Drink Scene of East Bank

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari: A delicious mix of lentils, rice, macaroni, and caramelized onions, topped with a spicy tomato sauce and chickpeas.
  • Foul: A dish made of cooked fava beans and served with butter, olive oil, and cumin.
  • Ta’amiya: A traditional Egyptian snack made of fava beans, onions, garlic, and herbs, fried and served with pita bread.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus tea: A refreshing tea that is made with dried hibiscus flowers, sugar, and water.
  • Karkadeh: Another tea made with hibiscus flowers, but served cold and often with mint.
  • Saadaya: A type of sugarcane juice that is popular throughout the country.

Where to Stay in East Bank: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sofitel Winter Palace Luxor

A palace-like hotel rich in French history and modern amenities. Located in the city center, a short walk from Luxor Temple.

Hilton Luxor Resort & Spa

A luxurious resort located on the edge of the Nile River with stunning views of the East Bank.

Bob Marley Peace Hotel

A cozy and affordable hotel located close to Luxor Temple, with a chill vibe and friendly staff.

What to Buy in East Bank: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional Clothes

From the embroidered galabiyas (full-length traditional dresses) to handwoven shawls and scarves, traditional Egyptian clothes make for a unique and stylish souvenir.

Spices

Pick up some of the aromatic spices commonly found in Egyptian cuisine, such as turmeric, cumin, cardamom, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of East Bank

The local culture of East Bank is influenced by Islamic and Christian traditions. Engaging with locals and being respectful of their customs is important.

Best Time to Visit East Bank

The best time to explore East Bank is during the winter months from December to February, when temperatures are mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Bank

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Bargain while shopping to get the best prices for souvenir shopping.
  • Carry a bottle of water while exploring the city, as temperatures can be high.

Don’t:

  • Photograph military or police without permission.
  • Smoke in public places, as it is prohibited under the law.
  • Give money to beggars or participate in any illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Bank

East Bank is generally a safe destination to visit, but it’s important to travel with caution and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in East Bank

It’s recommended to have all routine vaccinations up to date before visiting Egypt, including Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and more.

Getting to East Bank: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Bank

Luxor International Airport is the main airport in the East Bank of Luxor.

Bus Stations Near East Bank

The East Bank Bus Station is located on the Nile River and has frequent buses traveling to the rest of the country.

Other Options

Taxi, Uber, and horse-drawn carriages are also popular options for getting around the city.

How to Get Around East Bank

Taxis and bicycles are popular modes of transportation around the city. Rental cars are also available, but be sure to exercise caution while driving.

Where to Go Next from East Bank: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West Bank

Home to the Valley of the Kings, Valley of the Queens, and other popular historical sites.

Aswan

A city located south of Luxor that is known for its charming atmosphere and the iconic Abu Simbel Temple.

Conclusion

East Bank, Luxor is a must-visit destination for any history buff or culture lover. Plan your visit with our comprehensive guide and make unforgettable memories in one of Egypt’s most ancient and enchanting cities.
