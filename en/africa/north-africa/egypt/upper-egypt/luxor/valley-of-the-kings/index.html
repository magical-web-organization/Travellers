If you’re a history buff or simply want to experience ancient Egypt, then Valley of the Kings should definitely be on your bucket list. This striking beauty, located in Luxor, Upper Egypt, is one of the most fascinating and enchanting archeological sites in the world. With its rich cultural significance, natural beauty, and the abundance of activities to do and places to visit, Valley of the Kings is a must-see destination. Here is our comprehensive travel guide to Valley of the Kings, offering you all the information you need to make your trip there unforgettable.

Introduction to Valley of the Kings

Nestled in the hills of Luxor in Upper Egypt, Valley of the Kings was once the burial place of pharaohs during the New Kingdom period. This site features several tombs, each unique and remarkable for its vibrant and intricate artwork lining its walls. Even though only a few tombs are open to the public, the ones which are will leave you in awe with their art, architecture, and historical significance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valley of the Kings

If you want to experience Valley of the Kings actively and immerse yourself in the breathtaking natural beauty of the region, there are plenty of biking routes to choose from. Biking tours are available for all experience levels, so whether you are a beginner or an experienced biker, you will find a tour that suits you. The hiking routes can range from easy to challenging, but they all offer stunning views of the Valley of the Kings’ surrounding hills and unspoiled nature.

Local Culture and Customs of Valley of the Kings

Egyptians are an amiable and hospitable people, and they cherish their traditions and customs. Here are some cultural practices and customs in Valley of the Kings:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully
  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home
  • Use your right hand for eating, greeting and passing objects
  • Be respectful around religious sites and monuments

Best Time to Visit Valley of the Kings

The best time to visit Valley of the Kings is during winter or spring, between December and April when the temperature is moderate, and the skies are clear. Summer is scorching, making outdoor tours uncomfortable, and autumn can be crowded.

Safety and Health

Valley of the Kings is safe for tourists, but it’s still essential to take some precautions. The high temperature and sun exposure can lead to dehydration, heat exhaustion, and sunburns, so make sure to use sunscreen, wear a hat, and drink plenty of water.

Safety in Valley of the Kings
