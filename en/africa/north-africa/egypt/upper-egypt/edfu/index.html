Introduction to Edfu

Located in Upper Egypt, Edfu is a city in the Aswan Governorate. This ancient city, dating back to the Old Kingdom period, is famous for its well-preserved Ptolemaic Temple of Horus, which is the second-largest temple in Egypt after Karnak Temple. Apart from the temple, Edfu has a lot to offer in terms of history, natural beauty, and adventure. This comprehensive travel guide provides all the information you need for planning a trip to Edfu.

List of Places to Visit in Edfu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Temple of Horus: The Ptolemaic Temple of Horus is one of the best-preserved temples in Egypt and is the main attraction in Edfu. Visit this grand structure to see detailed hieroglyphics, intricate carvings, and stunning statues.

  • Edfu Museum: Located near the Temple of Horus, Edfu Museum showcases the history of the city with a focus on the Ptolemaic period. The museum’s collection includes pottery, sarcophagi, and artifacts from various excavations.

Natural Attractions of Edfu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nile River: Take a felucca ride on the Nile river at sunset to capture stunning views of the city.

  • Al-Adiyat Island: Take a guided tour to Al-Adiyat Island to explore its natural beauty, including the oasis and the wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edfu

  • Desert Safari: Book a desert safari tour and explore the beautiful landscape of the Egyptian desert.

  • Biking Tour: Rent a bike and take a tour of Edfu’s countryside, including the farms and the traditional mud-brick houses.

Best Things to Do in Edfu: Activities and Tours

  • Sound and Light Show at the Temple of Horus: Watch the Temple of Horus come to life during the Sound and Light Show that takes place every night.

  • Horse Carriage Ride: Take a horse carriage ride around the city and explore Edfu’s streets and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Edfu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari: A local dish made with rice, lentils, macaroni, and tomato sauce.

  • Ful Medames: A healthy breakfast dish made with fava beans, olive oil, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus Tea: A refreshing drink made with dried hibiscus flowers.

  • Sahlab: A warm and creamy drink made with milk and a type of orchid root.

Where to Stay in Edfu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Iberotel Luxor: A luxury hotel with a private beach, spa, and restaurant.

  • El Mesala Hotel: A budget hotel with clean rooms and a rooftop terrace with views of the Nile river.

What to Buy in Edfu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Papyrus Paintings: Buy handmade papyrus paintings depicting scenes from ancient Egypt.

  • Perfumes: Edfu is famous for its perfumes and essential oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Edfu

  • Dress Modestly: It is important to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.

  • Respect the Culture: Edfu has a conservative culture, and it is important to respect the local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Edfu

The best time to visit Edfu is from October to April when the weather is pleasant and comfortable. The temperature during these months ranges from 20°C to 25°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edfu

Do:

  • Do Dress Conservatively: Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.

  • Do Tip the Service Providers: It is customary to tip the service providers in Edfu.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Drink Tap Water: It is not safe to drink tap water in Edfu. Always drink bottled water.

  • Don’t Photograph People Without Their Consent: Always ask for permission before taking a photo of someone in Edfu.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edfu

Edfu is a safe place for tourists, and the crime rate is low. However, it is important to take precautions and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Edfu

It is advised to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent stomach infections. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and Typhoid before traveling to Edfu.

Getting to Edfu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edfu

The nearest airport to Edfu is Aswan International Airport, which is 77 km away from Edfu.

Bus Stations Near Edfu

Edfu Bus Station is located in the city center and has frequent buses to Luxor, Aswan, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Another option to get to Edfu is by hiring a private car or taxi from Luxor or Aswan.

How to Get Around in Edfu

The best way to explore Edfu is by hiring a taxi or taking a horse carriage ride. Alternatively, you can rent a bike to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Edfu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aswan: Visit Aswan to see the famous Aswan Dam, Philae Temple, and the Nubian Museum.

  • Luxor: Visit Luxor to see the Luxor Temple, Karnak Temple, and the Valley of the Kings.

Conclusion

Edfu is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, and offers many options for adventure and exploration. This comprehensive guide provides all the information you need to plan your trip to Edfu and make the most of your visit.
