Abu Simbel is a small Nubian town located in Upper Egypt, near the border with Sudan. The main attraction of this town is the Abu Simbel temples, which are two massive rock-cut temples constructed during the reign of Ramses II in the 13th century BC. The temples are a UNESCO World Heritage site and are internationally recognized for their historical importance.

Introduction to Abu Simbel

Abu Simbel is a must-visit destination for history buffs, adventure seekers, and culture enthusiasts. The town is situated along Lake Nasser and is surrounded by impressive natural landscapes and breathtaking views. Tourists from all over the world visit to witness the grandeur of the Abu Simbel temples, experience outdoor adventures, and learn about the local culture and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Abu Simbel

Abu Simbel offers a variety of traditional and international cuisines. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ful Medames: a classic Egyptian breakfast dish made with boiled fava beans.
  • Koshari: an Egyptian street food made with rice, lentils, chickpeas, and crispy fried onions.
  • Fiteer Baladi: an Egyptian pastry that resembles pizza, made with unleavened dough and topped with various cheeses, meats, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus tea: a popular non-alcoholic beverage made from the petals of the hibiscus flower.
  • Karkadeh: a sweet and tangy drink made with hibiscus juice, sugar, and water.

Local Culture and Customs of Abu Simbel

Abu Simbel is a vibrant and welcoming community, influenced by Nubian culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local traditions and dress modestly while visiting mosques or other religious sites. Handshaking and greeting the locals is customary, and travelers should avoid showing public display of affection in a conservative area or around locals.

Best Time to Visit Abu Simbel

The best time to visit Abu Simbel is between October and February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The temple complex occasionally closes for restoration during hot summers, and the temperature can reach up to 50°C during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abu Simbel

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and respectfully while visiting religious and conservative areas.
  • Drink plenty of water and use sunscreen while exploring the outdoor attractions.
  • Always ask for permission before taking pictures of the locals or their property.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection in a conservative or religious area.
  • Attempt to touch or climb on the ancient temples or other historical sites.
  • Disrespect local beliefs or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abu Simbel

Abu Simbel is generally a safe place to visit. However, tourists should remain vigilant and aware of their surroundings, especially in busy areas. It is recommended to avoid traveling alone at night or in deserted areas and consider hiring a local guide.

Health in Abu Simbel

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water or water that has been boiled for at least 10 minutes. It is also important to use mosquito repellent and take necessary precautions to avoid contracting diseases like malaria.

Getting to Abu Simbel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abu Simbel

The nearest airport to Abu Simbel is Abu Simbel International Airport, which offers domestic flights to and from Cairo and Aswan.

Bus Stations Near Abu Simbel

Abu Simbel is accessible by bus from Aswan, Luxor, and other major cities in Egypt.

Other Options

Tourists can also take a taxi or hire a private car from Aswan or Luxor to reach Abu Simbel.

How to Get Around in Abu Simbel

Abu Simbel is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, for longer distances, tourists can hire taxis or hitchhike with the locals.

Conclusion

Abu Simbel is a captivating destination that offers a unique blend of history, adventure, and culture. With its stunning temples, breathtaking landscapes, and friendly locals, it is no wonder that this small Nubian town is a top choice for travelers looking to discover the best of Egypt.
