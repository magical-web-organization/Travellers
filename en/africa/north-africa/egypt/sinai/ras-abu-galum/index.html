Introduction to Ras Abu Galum

Located in the Sinai in Egypt, Ras Abu Galum is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience. This stunning natural reserve boasts crystal-clear waters, dramatic mountain ranges, and a diverse array of flora and fauna. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best things to do, see, eat, and experience in Ras Abu Galum, so that you can make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Ras Abu Galum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Catherine’s Monastery: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is the oldest continuously inhabited Christian monastery in the world, and is home to a rich collection of art and manuscripts.
  • Mount Sinai: This iconic mountain is said to be the site where Moses received the Ten Commandments, and offers stunning views from its peak.
  • The Coloured Canyon: A natural wonder of multi-colored rock formations, this site is a wonderland of colors and textures.

Natural Attractions of Ras Abu Galum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ras Abu Galum Beach: This beautiful beach offers crystal-clear waters, coral reefs for snorkeling, and soft sand for lounging.
  • Wadi Baraba: This stunning wadi is great for hiking and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Ras Abu Galum Natural Reserve: This protected area is home to a variety of plant and animal species, including gazelles, hyraxes, and wildcats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ras Abu Galum

  • Mount Sinai Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Mount Sinai and offers stunning views at sunrise.
  • Wadi Arada Hike: This scenic hike takes you through the beautiful terrain of Wadi Arada and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Moiya Al Thalaba Trail: This trail is great for bikers, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Ras Abu Galum: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The clear waters of the Red Sea offer some of the best snorkeling and diving opportunities in the world.
  • Camel Rides: Take a leisurely camel ride along the stunning coastline and enjoy the peace and serenity of the desert landscape.
  • Bedouin Cultural Experience: Immerse yourself in the local Bedouin culture by experiencing traditional music, dance, and cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Ras Abu Galum

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bedouin Tea: A fragrant and refreshing tea made with herbs and spices.
  • Stuffed Camel: A traditional Bedouin dish consisting of a whole camel stuffed with rice, herbs, and spices.
  • Shish Tawook: Grilled chicken skewers marinated in yogurt, garlic, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Arabian Coffee: A strong and fragrant coffee served in small cups, often flavored with cardamom or saffron.
  • Bedouin Tea: A traditional tea made with herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Ras Abu Galum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bedouin Camps: These traditional Bedouin camps offer authentic experiences and stunning views of the natural reserve.
  • Ras Shitan Camp: This beachfront camp offers comfortable accommodation and easy access to outdoor activities.
  • Dahab Hostel: This affordable hostel is located in the nearby town of Dahab and offers easy access to Ras Abu Galum.

What to Buy in Ras Abu Galum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven Rugs: These colorful rugs are made by local Bedouin artisans and are a great way to bring a piece of Ras Abu Galum home with you.
  • Silver Jewelry: Bedouin jewelry is known for its intricate designs and craftsmanship.
  • Spices and Herbs: Take home some of the fragrant spices and herbs that are an integral part of Bedouin cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Ras Abu Galum

Ras Abu Galum is home to a vibrant Bedouin culture, where hospitality and generosity are highly valued. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and dress modestly, particularly when visiting traditional Bedouin camps. It is also customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or tent.

Best Time to Visit Ras Abu Galum

The best time to visit Ras Abu Galum is from October to April, when the weather is mild and pleasant. During the summer months, temperatures can be extremely high, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ras Abu Galum

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Pack sunscreen and plenty of water when venturing outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Take photographs of people without their consent.
  • Drink tap water without filtering or boiling it first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ras Abu Galum

While Ras Abu Galum is generally considered safe for tourists, it is always advisable to take precautions when traveling to a new place. Visitors are advised to stay within designated areas and to avoid traveling alone after dark. It is also advisable to carry a copy of your passport and to keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Ras Abu Galum

Visitors to Ras Abu Galum should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations for Hepatitis A and B, as well as Typhoid. It is also advisable to bring a first aid kit containing essential supplies such as bandages, painkillers, and antiseptic cream.

Getting to Ras Abu Galum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ras Abu Galum is located approximately two hours by car from the town of Dahab. Visitors can reach Dahab by bus from Cairo or Sharm el Sheikh, or by flying into Sharm el Sheikh International Airport.

Airports Near Ras Abu Galum

  • Sharm el Sheikh International Airport: This airport is located approximately two hours by car from Ras Abu Galum.

Bus Stations Near Ras Abu Galum

  • Cairo Bus Station: This bus station offers regular services to Dahab.
  • Sharm el Sheikh Bus Station: This bus station offers regular services to Dahab.

Other Options

  • Private Car: Visitors can hire a private car for the journey from Dahab to Ras Abu Galum.

How to Get Around in Ras Abu Galum

The best way to get around Ras Abu Galum is by foot or by camel. Visitors can also hire a taxi or private car for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Ras Abu Galum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dahab: This coastal town is known for its relaxed vibe and water sports opportunities.
  • Sharm el Sheikh: This bustling resort town offers a wide range of activities and attractions, including water parks and shopping centers.
  • Cairo: Egypt’s capital city is home to a wealth of ancient historical sites, including the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Egyptian Museum.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and abundance of outdoor activities, Ras Abu Galum is a truly unique and unforgettable destination. Whether you’re interested in hiking, diving, or simply soaking up the relaxed atmosphere of a traditional Bedouin camp, Ras Abu Galum has something for everyone. So start planning your trip today, and get ready to experience the best of Egypt’s Sinai region.
