Introduction to Dahab

Tucked away on the southeastern coast of the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt, lies Dahab, a small coastal town that is fast emerging as a popular tourist destination in the country. Known for its stunning natural beauty, beautiful coral reefs, and laidback vibe, Dahab has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

Whether you are looking to soak up some sun, relax on pristine beaches, explore the underwater world, or embark on outdoor adventures like hiking and biking, Dahab will not fail to amaze you. In this travel guide, we have put together all the essential information you need to plan a perfect trip to Dahab.

List of Places to Visit in Dahab

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dahab might be more famous for its natural beauty, but it also holds a rich cultural history that dates back to ancient times. Some of the most popular cultural attractions in and around Dahab include:

  • St. Catherine’s Monastery: Located at the foot of Mount Sinai, St. Catherine’s Monastery is one of the oldest functioning monasteries in the world, dating back to the 4th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and houses a museum that displays ancient manuscripts, icons, and other relics.

  • Nabq Protected Area: Located about an hour’s drive from Dahab, this protected area is home to a range of archaeological sites that date back to the Roman and Byzantine periods. Here, you can explore ancient tombs, temples, and wells.

  • Wadi Gnai: This dry river bed is home to several ancient rock art sites that depict the daily life and cultural practices of the ancient Bedouin tribes.

Natural Attractions of Dahab: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dahab is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in Dahab:

  • Blue Hole: This famous diving spot is a natural underwater sinkhole that plunges to a depth of 100 meters. It is home to a diverse range of marine life and is a paradise for scuba diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

  • Mount Sinai: Rising to a height of 2,285 meters, Mount Sinai is a sacred site for Christians, Jews, and Muslims alike. The hike to the top is a popular activity among tourists and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Ras Abu Galum: This natural protected area is located around 50 km north of Dahab and is accessible only by camel or on foot. It is home to some of the most pristine and secluded beaches in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dahab

Dahab offers an excellent range of hikes and bike routes that vary in difficulty level and scenic beauty. Some of the popular routes to explore include:

  • Wadi Gnai trek: This 2-day trek takes you through the scenic desert landscape of Sinai and ends at Wadi Gnai, where you can explore ancient rock art sites.

  • Safari by bike: This is a guided biking tour that takes you through the Sinai desert and remote Bedouin villages. You can also camp overnight in the desert and enjoy Bedouin hospitality.

Best Things to Do in Dahab: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the cultural and natural attractions, Dahab also offers several other activities and tours that you can enjoy. Some of the popular things to do in Dahab include:

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling: Dahab has some of the most beautiful coral reefs in the world, and diving or snorkeling is a must-do activity here.

  • Kite surfing and windsurfing: The warm and steady winds along the coast of Dahab make it an ideal destination for kite surfing and windsurfing.

  • Bedouin dinner: Enjoy a traditional Bedouin dinner under the stars in the desert and experience the local hospitality and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dahab

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Dahab:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Dahab, relax on the beach, and explore the town center.

  • Day 2: Take a diving or snorkeling trip to the Blue Hole or explore the coral reefs along the coastline.

  • Day 3: Embark on a hike to Mount Sinai and witness the sunrise from the summit.

  • Day 4: Explore the cultural attractions of Dahab, such as St. Catherine’s Monastery, or take a day trip to Nabq Protected Area.

  • Day 5: Go kitesurfing or windsurfing, or take a camel ride to the nearby oasis.

  • Day 6: Explore the various biking and hiking trails, or take a guided safari tour of the Sinai desert.

  • Day 7: Relax on the beach, indulge in some local food and drinks, and depart from Dahab.

Food and Drink Scene of Dahab

Dahab offers a vibrant food and drink scene that caters to every taste and budget. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kushari: A popular Egyptian dish made with rice, lentils, macaroni, and topped with tomato sauce and fried onions.

  • Ful Medames: A breakfast dish made with soaked fava beans, served with parsley, onions, and lemon juice.

  • Shawarma: A popular Middle Eastern dish made with marinated meat (chicken or lamb), vegetables, and sauces wrapped in a pita bread.

Local Beverages

  • Karkadeh: A refreshing hibiscus tea that is popular throughout Egypt.

  • Sahlab: A warm, creamy drink made with orchid root, sugar, and milk, usually served during the winter months.

  • Coffee: Egypt is famous for its Arabic coffee, which is strong, sweet, and usually served with dates.

Where to Stay in Dahab: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dahab offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the popular choices:

  • Luxury Hotels: Some of the popular luxury hotels in Dahab include Hilton Dahab Resort, Swiss Inn Resort Dahab, and Le Meridien Dahab Resort.

  • Budget Hotels and Hostels: Dahab also has several budget hotels and hostels that offer affordable accommodation, such as Dahab Hostel, Bedouin Moon Hotel, and Penguin Village Hostel.

  • Guesthouses: For a more authentic and homely experience, you can also stay at one of the many guesthouses in and around Dahab, such as Shams Hotel & Dive Center, Sheikh Ali Resort, and Jowhara Hotel.

What to Buy in Dahab: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dahab offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products that you can take home as a memento of your trip. Some of the popular items to buy in Dahab include:

  • Bedouin crafts and textiles: You can find a range of handcrafted items made by the local Bedouin tribes, such as rugs, tapestries, and jewelry.

  • Egyptian spices and teas: The markets in Dahab sell a variety of traditional Egyptian spices and teas, such as hibiscus tea, saffron, and coriander.

  • Decorative items: You can also find a range of decorative items that are unique to Dahab, such as artistic lamps, colorful glassware, and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Dahab

Dahab is home to a diverse mix of cultures and has a rich Bedouin heritage. Here are some of the local customs and cultural practices that you should be aware of:

  • Dress Code: While Dahab is relatively liberal compared to other parts of Egypt, it is still a predominantly Muslim country, and it is advisable to dress modestly when in public places.

  • Etiquette: Egyptians are known for their hospitality and politeness, and it is courteous to greet locals and learn basic Arabic phrases.

  • Ramadan: Ramadan is a holy month in Islam, and it is important to respect the customs and traditions during this time, such as fasting during daylight hours and refraining from smoking, drinking, or eating in public.

Best Time to Visit Dahab

The best time to visit Dahab is between October and April, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the water temperature is ideal for swimming and water activities. However, the peak tourist season is between December and February, and prices may be higher during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dahab

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress modestly when visiting public places or religious sites.

  • Try local food and drinks and indulge in the Bedouin hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.

  • Drink alcohol in public places, as it is prohibited in Islam.

  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dahab

Dahab is generally a safe destination for tourists, and violent crime is rare. However, it is advisable to take the usual precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night, not carrying large sums of money, and keeping your belongings secure.

Health in Dahab

Egypt has a high prevalence of waterborne diseases, and it is advisable to drink only bottled water and avoid consuming food that is not cooked or peeled. It is also recommended to carry a first aid kit and prescribed medications.

Getting to Dahab: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dahab

The closest airport to Dahab is Sharm El Sheikh International Airport, which is around 90 km away. It is well-connected to major cities in Europe, Africa, and the Middle East, with several major airlines operating direct flights.

Bus Stations Near Dahab

Dahab also has a bus station that is well-connected to other cities in Egypt. The buses are comfortable and affordable, and there are several options to choose from, including local buses, minibusses, and private coaches.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or rent a car from Sharm El Sheikh to reach Dahab. However, it is advisable to negotiate the fare before getting into the taxi, as the prices can vary.

How to Get Around in Dahab

Dahab is a relatively small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, you can also rent a bike or a scooter to explore the surrounding areas or take a taxi if you need to go further afield.

Where to Go Next from Dahab: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dahab is ideally located, and there are several nearby destinations that you can explore. Some of the popular options include:

  • Sharm El Sheikh: This popular seaside resort town is located just 90 km from Dahab and offers a range of activities such as diving, shopping, and nightlife.

  • Cairo: The capital city of Egypt is around 600 km from Dahab and is a must-visit destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts. Here, you can explore the Pyramids, the Sphinx, and other ancient landmarks.

  • Luxor: Located around 240 km from Dahab, Luxor is another historic city that is famous for its ancient temples and monuments, including the Valley of the Kings and the Karnak Temple Complex.

Conclusion

Dahab is a hidden gem that has something to offer for every kind of traveler. With its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and diverse range of activities and attractions, it is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan an unforgettable trip to Dahab. Happy travels!
