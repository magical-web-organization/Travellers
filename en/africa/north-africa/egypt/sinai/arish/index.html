Introduction to Arish

Arish is a city located in Sinai, Egypt. It is the capital of North Sinai Governorate and is located near the border of the Gaza Strip. Arish is a popular tourist destination for its stunning beaches, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. In this guide, we will explore the top places to visit in Arish, including cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, local cuisine, accommodations, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Arish

When it comes to things to see and do in Arish, visitors are spoiled for choice. Here are some of the must-see sights and attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al-Hasan Ibn Ali Mosque: Considered one of the most beautiful mosques in Sinai, this stunning landmark is known for its colorful ceramic tilework and intricate wooden carvings.
  • Arish Castle: The castle was built in the 16th century and was used as a military fortress by the Ottomans. Today, it serves as a museum and cultural center, showcasing the history and culture of the region.
  • El-Arish Museum: The museum displays a collection of artifacts and exhibits from the Pharaohs to Islamic times. The museum also features a library that houses valuable Islamic and scientific manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Arish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arish Beach: This beautiful public beach features clear blue water and soft sand. It is the perfect place for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Wadi Feiran Oasis: Located to the south of Arish, this oasis is a natural wonder of towering sand dunes and verdant palm groves. Hike through the wadi to enjoy the stunning views and tranquil atmosphere.
  • Mount Sinai: Also known as Jebel Musa, Mount Sinai is the site where Moses received the Ten Commandments from God. The mountain offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding desert landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arish

Arish offers several scenic hiking and biking trails that take visitors through stunning natural landscapes. Here are some popular routes:

  • Wadi Feiran: This 11-kilometer hike is a well-known trail that leads hikers through the stunning Wadi Feiran oasis.
  • Mount Sinai Trail: Also known as the Moses Trail, this trek takes hikers to the summit of Jebel Musa or Mount Sinai, the biblical site of Moses’ revelation.
  • El Tarabin Dunes: This biking trail through the dunes of El Tarabin is perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Best Things to Do in Arish: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Arish has a vibrant marine life, and visitors can explore the underwater world with snorkeling and diving tours.
  • Camel riding: Visitors can take a unique tour and travel through the desert on camelback.
  • Stargazing: Arish’s clear skies offer the perfect opportunity for stargazing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arish

Here is a sample itinerary for travelers planning to spend a week in Arish:

Day Activity
1 Visit Al-Hasan Ibn Ali Mosque and Arish Castle
2 Explore Mount Sinai Trail and St. Catherine’s Monastery

Food and Drink Scene of Arish

Arish has a diverse cuisine with Middle Eastern, African, and Mediterranean influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bamia: This stew features tender lamb, okra, tomatoes, and onions served over rice.
  • Molokheya: A dish made with Jew’s mallow and served with chicken or rabbit.
  • Kofta Kabab: Minced meat grilled on skewers, served with rice and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bedouin tea: This refreshing tea made with mint and herbs is common among Bedouin communities.
  • Sugarcane Juice: A sweet drink made from freshly squeezed sugarcane.

Where to Stay in Arish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arish has a variety of accommodations suitable for all types of travelers. From luxury resorts to budget-friendly options, visitors can choose from the following:

  • Paradise Resort: A five-star resort with beach access, an outdoor pool, and a spa.
  • Arish Yassin Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the city center.
  • People of the Sun Camp: A Bedouin-style guesthouse with traditional décor and stunning views of the desert.

What to Buy in Arish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arish is famous for its handmade crafts and traditional desserts. Visitors can bring home the following:

  • Bedouin rugs and blankets: Handmade from wool by Bedouin women.
  • Dates: Arish is known for producing high-quality dates.
  • Halva: A sweet treat made with sesame seeds.

Local Culture and Customs of Arish

Arish has a rich history and culture influenced by the Bedouin tribes of Sinai. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, particularly when visiting religious sites. Modest dress is also recommended.

Best Time to Visit Arish

Arish has a hot desert climate with little rainfall. The best time to visit is between October and April when temperatures are cooler and more pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arish

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Eat or drink during Ramadan in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arish

Arish is generally a safe place to visit, but travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions. The region has a significant military presence due to its proximity to the Gaza Strip.

Health in Arish

Travelers should drink bottled water and take necessary precautions to avoid foodborne illnesses. Visitors should also bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect themselves from the sun and mosquitos.

Getting to Arish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arish is located approximately 350 kilometers away from Cairo. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Arish

  • Sharm El Sheikh International Airport: Located 190 kilometers away from Arish.
  • Cairo International Airport: Located 350 kilometers away from Arish.

Bus Stations Near Arish

  • El-Merg: Located 30 kilometers away from Arish.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Arish.

How to Get Around in Arish

Arish is a small city, and many attractions are located close to each other. Taxis and buses are the most popular modes of transportation. Buses run regularly, and route information can be obtained from bus stations.

Where to Go Next from Arish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ras Mohammed National Park: Located near Sharm El-Sheikh, this national park is home to some of the best snorkeling and diving spots in Egypt.
  • Dahab: A beach town located approximately 100 kilometers from Arish, popular for its diving and water sports.
  • Taba: A border town located approximately 187 kilometers from Arish and the gateway to the Gulf of Aqaba.

Conclusion

Arish is a breathtakingly beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and a vibrant outdoor adventure scene. With this guide, visitors can explore the top sights and attractions in the region, sample local cuisine, and experience the warm hospitality of this enchanting city.
