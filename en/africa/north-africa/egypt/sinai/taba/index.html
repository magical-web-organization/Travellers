Introduction to Taba

Taba is a small town in the Sinai Peninsula of Egypt, bordered by the picturesque Gulf of Aqaba to the east and Negev Desert to the west. It is a popular destination among tourists for its stunning natural beauty, unique marine life, and rich historical past. Taba offers a plethora of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, snorkeling, and scuba diving, making it an ideal destination for adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Taba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Saladin’s Castle: A 12th-century fortress that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  2. Pharaoh’s Island: An island fortress that was once a trading center between Egypt and the Levant with rich historical significance.
  3. The Colored Canyon: A geological wonder with stunning colors and formations that offers a unique hiking experience.

Natural Attractions of Taba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Taba Heights Beach: A private beach with turquoise waters and white sand that is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and relaxation.
  2. Fjord Bay: A hidden beach with crystal-clear waters, surrounded by towering cliffs and stunning views that can be reached through a hiking trail.
  3. Ras Abu Galum Protectorate: A protected natural area with diverse marine life, coral reefs, and rich flora and fauna that offers a unique snorkeling or diving experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taba

  1. Taba Nature Reserve: A hiking trail that runs through a protected area with stunning mountain views, Bedouin settlements, and local flora and fauna.
  2. Wadi El-Arbein: A hiking trail that leads you through a gorge with stunning rock formations, natural springs, and palm groves.
  3. Serabit El-Khadim: A hiking trail that takes you through the desert to the top of the Serabit El-Khadim mountain, where a temple dedicated to the goddess Hathor stands. It is also famous for Bronze Age mining works.

Best Things to Do in Taba: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Explore the rich marine life and coral reefs in the Red Sea, which is home to over 1,000 species of fish, dolphins and other marine creatures.
  2. Camel Riding: Experience the Bedouin way of travel, and explore the desert by riding a camel.
  3. Sunset Boat Tours: Enjoy a relaxing ride on the Red Sea while watching the stunning sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Taba

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bedouin-style grilled meat: Considered a delicacy in the area, Bedouin-style grilled meat, is typically made with lamb, served with flatbread, and accompanied with Bedouin tea.
  2. Stuffed Vine Leaves: Popularly known as dolma stuffed vine leaves are filled with rice, minced meat, herbs, and spices and are often served as a meze plate in local restaurants.
  3. Shawarma: A Middle Eastern sandwich made with grilled meat stuffed inside pita bread, accompanied with hummus or tahini sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Bedouin Tea: A local hot tea made with black tea leaves, fresh mint, and often sweetened with honey or sugar.
  2. Fresh Juices: Fresh juices, made with seasonal fruits, are the perfect way to quench your thirst during the hot Egyptian summers.

Where to Stay in Taba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Movenpick Taba Resort & Spa: A luxury resort, offering stunning views of the Red Sea and unparalleled comfort and amenities.
  2. Taba Hotel & Nelson Village: A four-star hotel, with a private beach, a full-service spa, and on-site dining options.
  3. Cordoba Resort Taba Heights: A perfect location for a family vacation, with beautiful villas, swimming pools, and a private beach.

What to Buy in Taba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bedouin Jewelry: Bedouin jewelry is handmade with intricate beadwork, silver etchings, and turquoise stones.
  2. Miniature Pyramids: Miniature Pyramids of Giza, a perfect memento to remember your trip, are readily available in local shops, bazaars, and hotels.
  3. Local Spices: Egyptian souks are famous for their spices such as saffron, cumin, and cinnamon, which are excellent take-home options.

Local Culture and Customs of Taba

Egypt has a predominantly Muslim community, and it is essential to respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly while visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Do not drink alcohol on the street or display signs of intoxication.
  • Do not engage in public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Taba

The best time to visit Taba is during the fall and spring months (September-November and March-May) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. Summertime (June-August) can be hot and humid, but it’s the perfect season for water sports and snorkeling trips. Winters (December-February) can be cold, so make sure to pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taba

Do:

  • Experience local customs and traditions, including trying local dishes, and exploring cultural landmarks.
  • Be respectful of local culture and customs, dress appropriately, and adhere to local laws and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection or drink alcohol on the streets.
  • Ignore the rules and regulations of protected natural areas, including Ras Abu Galum Protectorate.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taba

Taba is considered a safe city for tourists, and the crime rates are low. However, it is important to remain vigilant and aware of your surroundings, avoid being out late at night, and keep your belongings safe. It is recommended to hire a local guide or travel in a group while hiking or exploring remote areas.

Health in Taba

Tap water in Egypt may not be safe for consumption, and it is recommended to drink bottled water. It is also recommended to carry insect repellents, sunscreen, and other essential medications.

Getting to Taba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taba

  1. Sharm El Sheikh International Airport: Taba is 188km from Sharm El Sheikh airport.
  2. Taba International Airport: The airport serves Taba and the surrounding areas. Currently, it has limited international flights.

Bus Stations Near Taba

  1. Taba Bus Station: The station is located 2 km from the city center.
  2. Dahab Bus Station: The station is located 80km from Taba and serves buses coming from and going to Cairo.

Other Options

Egypt has a well-developed railway network that connects major cities to each other. Traveling by train is comfortable and affordable, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

How to Get Around in Taba

The best way to get around in Taba is by hiring a taxi or car rental services. Most hotels and resorts offer airport pickup and drop-off services. If you plan to explore the region, you can choose to rent a bike, a quad bike or a jeep.

Where to Go Next from Taba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Saint Catherine’s Monastery: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, located near the town of St. Catherine, and is famous for its historical and religious significance.
  2. Nuweiba: A seaside town with stunning beaches, turquoise waters, and a lively market.
  3. Dahab: A popular destination for diving, windsurfing, and kitesurfing, and is also known for its vibrant nightlife

Conclusion

Taba is a destination that offers something for everyone, including adventure seekers, nature lovers, and history buffs. A perfect blend of stunning natural landscapes and rich historical past, Taba is a must-visit destination for those who want to explore Egypt beyond its famous pyramids.
