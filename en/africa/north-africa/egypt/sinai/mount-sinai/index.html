Introduction to Mount Sinai

Mount Sinai, also known as Gebel Musa, is a peak located in the Sinai Peninsula of Egypt. It is known for its religious significance, as it is believed to be the place where Moses received the Ten Commandments. Tourists from all over the world visit Mount Sinai to hike its rugged terrain, explore the historic sites and bask in its breathtaking natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Sinai

Mount Sinai is home to a variety of attractions that cater to culture enthusiasts, outdoor adventurers, and history buffs. Here are some places to visit in Mount Sinai:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Catherine’s Monastery: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the oldest Christian monasteries in the world and the most famous landmark in the Sinai Peninsula.
  • Moses’ Well: This landmark is a well located in the town of St. Catherine and is believed to be the place where Moses met his future wife, Zipporah.
  • The Burning Bush: Near St. Catherine’s Monastery stands the bush from which God spoke to Moses, making it an essential destination for all visitors.

Natural Attractions of Mount Sinai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Sinai Summit: Hike to the peak of Mount Sinai to witness panoramic views of the surrounding desert and mountains.
  • Mount Catherine Summit: Located nearby, Mount Catherine is Egypt’s highest mountain and an excellent addition to any trekker’s itinerary. Hikers are awarded stunning views from the summit.
  • White Canyon: This scenic natural wonder takes its name from the bright colours of the rocks found in the area. It’s located in the vicinity of Mt. Sinai, and a great option for nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Sinai

Mount Sinai is a haven for trekkers and bikers, with several scenic routes that provide a glimpse of the area’s mesmerizing natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes:

  • Mt. Sinai Camel Trail: A 30km biking trail that lets you experience the stunning scenery of Mt. Sinai via a traditional camel ride.
  • Wadi Feiran: A valley located on the eastern edge of the Sinai Peninsula. The valley is dotted with palm trees and streams that make for a scenic hiking trail.
  • Moses Trail: The traditional hiking route to Mt. Sinai from the St. Catherine Monastery, which is often used by pilgrims.

Best Things to Do in Mount Sinai: Activities and Tours

  • Desert Safari: Take an off-road adventure in Egypt’s iconic desert while exploring its distinct landscape and attractions such as the famed White Canyon.
  • Bedouin Culture Tour: Embrace Bedouin culture by taking a cultural tour of the region, where you will meet and interact with locals.
  • Star-Gazing: Get an unprecedented view of the desert skies through a guided night sky tour, away from the bustle of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Sinai

Egypt’s cuisine is vibrant and offers a lot of options, making it a foodie’s heaven. Mount Sinai has a unique blend of cuisines and flavours influenced by Bedouin and other global cultures. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ful medames: A traditional Egyptian breakfast dish made of cooked fava beans.
  • Kushari: A Cairo street food made from rice, pasta, lentils, chickpeas, and topped with a tomato sauce.
  • Biryani Rice: A Middle Eastern and South Asian flavoured rice dish.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus Juice: A refreshing and tangy beverage often enjoyed after a day’s trek.
  • Mint Tea: A warm cup of mint tea is a popular tradition in Egypt and its neighbours.

Where to Stay in Mount Sinai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mount Sinai has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, where visitors can enjoy a comfortable stay during their trip. Some of the best places to stay in Mount Sinai includes:

  • Saint Catherine Plaza Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in St. Catherine’s town within walking distance to the Monastery.
  • Morgenland Village Hotel: A hotel located in the heart of Wadi el Arbain, perfect for hiking and desert activities.
  • Habiba Village: A great Bedouin-style eco-lodge located near the white canyon, allowing visitors to experience the culture and natural environment of the area.

What to Buy in Mount Sinai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade products to traditional clothing, Mount Sinai has a variety of souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as an excellent reminder of their trip. Here are some unique souvenirs:

  • Authentic Bedouin Clothing: Experience the Bedouin lifestyle out in the desert by purchasing their traditional clothing.
  • Handmade Crafts: Baubles, bags, and other crafts can be great souvenirs to take home to loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Sinai

Mount Sinai has a rich culture and customs influenced by its population of Bedouin tribes. Here are some key aspects to note:

  • Dress Code: Dress modestly, covering shoulders and legs; otherwise, you might cause offence.
  • Bedouin Culture: Bedouins have their way of living that they hold dearly. Show respect and be open to learning about their unique customs.

Best Time to Visit Mount Sinai

The best time to visit Mount Sinai is from late summer to winter (September to February), when the temperature is mostly mild and visibility is clear. Summer months are extreme hot temperatures and not advisable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Sinai

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and show respect to the culture and traditions
  • Take part in desert activities, including stargazing and camel rides
  • Stay hydrated and carry sunscreen to protect from the sun

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter – Help keep Mount Sinai clean by disposing of your garbage properly
  • Don’t cause offence – research the customs and traditional practices of Bedouin people before visiting
  • Don’t wander off – stick to designated hiking trails and stay safe

Safety and Health

Visitors to Mount Sinai must ensure to take precautions, including practising good hygiene, staying hydrated, and using reliable transportation. Here’s some information to note:

Safety in Mount Sinai

  • Hiking advisory: Stay on marked trails to avoid dangerous and isolated areas, trek in groups or with local guides.
  • Crime: Mount Sinai is relatively safe, but petty crime incidents can happen. Keep your personal belongings secure while travelling.

Health in Mount Sinai

  • Stay hydrated: Drink enough water to avoid dehydration in the arid desert climate.
  • Food Safety: Pay attention to food hygiene best practices to ensure you stay free of illness.

Getting to Mount Sinai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mount Sinai can be accessed from various directions either by road, rail, or air. Sharm El Sheikh International Airport is the nearest airport to Mount Sinai. Some other options include:

Airports Near Mount Sinai

  • Sharm El Sheikh International Airport: Located 120km from the Mount Sinai.
  • Taba International Airport: Located 170km from Mount Sinai.

Bus Stations Near Mount Sinai

Besides taxis, public buses or shuttle minibuses are also available in nearby towns.

Other Options

Private shuttles or sedans are available for those who would like some privacy.

How to Get Around in Mount Sinai

While private transportation is available for hire, visitors to Mount Sinai can opt to travel by camel, 4x4s, or safari buses. Bikes and treks are practical modes of exploration within Mount Sinai and offer some of the best opportunities to experience the captivating scenery.

Where to Go Next from Mount Sinai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mount Sinai’s convenient location opens the door to numerous destinations worth exploring while in the area, including:

  • Dahab: A well-known coastal resort town in Egypt with great beach scenery, located close to Mount Sinai.
  • St. Catherine: A neighboring Bedouin town where visitors can shop for souvenirs and get a firsthand experience of life in a Bedouin settlement.
  • Sharm El Sheikh: Exploring the bustling resort is a must-visit at least once when visiting Egypt.

Conclusion

Mount Sinai, with its picturesque terrain and rich cultural heritage, is an excellent destination to explore. Whether it’s a relaxing desert safari or conquering the untamed camels trails, Mount Sinai has something for everyone. Remember to follow the cultures and traditions and stay safe while embracing the region and its Bedouin residents.
