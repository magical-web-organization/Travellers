A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Sharm el-Sheikh

Sharm el-Sheikh is a resort town located on the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt. Known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters, the town attracts thousands of tourists every year. If you’re planning to visit Sharm el-Sheikh, this comprehensive travel guide has got you covered with everything you need to know for an unforgettable journey.

Introduction to Sharm el-Sheikh

Sharm el-Sheikh is a popular tourist destination located in the Red Sea governorate of Egypt. The town is famous for its clear waters, world-class diving and snorkelling sites, and stunning landscapes. Sharm el-Sheikh is also home to some of the most luxurious resorts in Egypt, making it a popular destination for those looking to relax and unwind.

List of Places to Visit in Sharm el-Sheikh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sinai Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of the Sinai Peninsula, from the Bedouin way of life to the ancient Egyptian civilizations that once inhabited the region.
  • The Coptic Orthodox Church of Sharm el-Sheikh: This church is one of the oldest churches in the town and is a must-visit place for history buffs.
  • Old Market: Located in the center of town, the Old Market is a popular shopping and dining destination famous for its vibrant atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Sharm el-Sheikh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ras Mohammed National Park: One of the most beautiful national parks in Egypt, Ras Mohammed is home to diverse marine life, coral reefs, and stunning landscapes.
  • Tiran Island: Tiran Island is a popular spot for snorkelling and diving, with crystal clear waters and colourful coral reefs.
  • The Coloured Canyon: The Coloured Canyon is a unique geological wonder that boasts breathtaking landscapes and natural formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharm el-Sheikh

  • The Sinai Trail: The Sinai Trail is a challenging but rewarding hike that takes visitors through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the Sinai Peninsula.
  • Wadi Qnai: This scenic valley is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking, with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Sharm el-Sheikh: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkelling: Sharm el-Sheikh is known for its world-class diving and snorkelling sites, with diverse marine life and colourful coral reefs.
  • Quad Biking: This adrenaline-packed adventure takes visitors on a thrilling ride through the Sinai Peninsula, with stunning landscapes and desert views.
  • Desert Safaris: A desert safari is a popular activity in Sharm el-Sheikh, with visitors getting the chance to ride camels, explore Bedouin villages, and experience traditional Bedouin cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Sharm el-Sheikh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari: A popular Egyptian street food dish made with rice, lentils, chickpeas, and a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Shakshuka: A breakfast dish made with eggs poached in a spicy tomato and pepper sauce.
  • Ful Medames: A hearty breakfast dish made with fava beans, garlic, and lemon juice.

Local Beverages

  • Egyptian Tea: A traditional tea made with mint leaves and sugar.
  • Sahlab: A popular hot beverage made with milk, sugar, and a flour-based pudding mixture.
  • Hibiscus tea: A refreshing tea made with hibiscus flowers, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Sharm el-Sheikh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sharm el-Sheikh is home to a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the most popular hotels in the town include:

  • Four Seasons Resort Sharm el-Sheikh: A luxurious resort located on the Red Sea beaches.
  • Rixos Sharm el-Sheikh: A five-star hotel with stunning landscapes, sparkling pools, and a private beach.
  • Gafy Resort: A budget-friendly hotel located near the beach and Old Market.

What to Buy in Sharm el-Sheikh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sinai Bedouin Rugs: These traditional handmade rugs are a popular souvenir in Sharm el-Sheikh.
  • Egyptian Spices: A wide variety of exotic spices can be found in the Old Market, and most shops offer a range of pre-made spice blends.
  • Papyrus Art: Papyrus is an ancient form of paper that was used in ancient Egypt. It can be purchased in various forms, including paintings and bookmarks.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharm el-Sheikh

Sharm el-Sheikh is a Muslim-majority town, and visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions. Dress modestly, especially in religious sites, and avoid public displays of affection. Alcohol is available at most hotels and restaurants but is not widely consumed in public places.

Best Time to Visit Sharm el-Sheikh

The best time to visit Sharm el-Sheikh is between October and April when the temperatures are mild and comfortable. Summers can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching up to 45°C, making outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharm el-Sheikh

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially in religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain in the markets and souks.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Photograph people without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharm el-Sheikh

Sharm el-Sheikh is a relatively safe destination, and violent crimes are rare. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas. It is also advisable to avoid walking alone at night and to use licensed taxis.

Health in Sharm el-Sheikh

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid tap water. Mosquitoes are prevalent in the area, so it is advisable to use insect repellent and wear protective clothing.

Getting to Sharm el-Sheikh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sharm el-Sheikh

  • Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport: Located approximately 18 km from the town center, Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport is served by a range of international and domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Sharm el-Sheikh

  • Sharm el-Sheikh Bus Station Terminal: Located in the center of town, Sharm el-Sheikh Bus Station Terminal offers services to other major cities in Egypt, including Cairo and Alexandria.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in the town, and most drivers speak English.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Sharm el-Sheikh and can be easily booked from the airport or online.

How to Get Around in Sharm el-Sheikh

Taxis and minibuses are readily available in Sharm el-Sheikh and can be flagged down on the street. Visitors can also rent cars and bicycles to explore the town and its surroundings. Some hotels and resorts also offer shuttle services to popular attractions.

Where to Go Next from Sharm el-Sheikh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dahab: A popular destination for backpackers and adventure seekers, Dahab is known for its stunning landscapes and laid-back atmosphere.
  • Luxor: A historical city located on the east bank of the Nile River, Luxor is home to some of Egypt’s most iconic ancient temples and monuments.
  • Cairo: The capital of Egypt, Cairo is a bustling city rich in history, culture, and traditions.

Conclusion

Sharm el-Sheikh is a beautiful and vibrant town that offers something for everyone. With its stunning beaches, world-class diving sites, and rich history and culture, it’s no surprise that it’s one of the most popular destinations in Egypt. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or embark on an adventure, Sharm el-Sheikh has got you covered.
