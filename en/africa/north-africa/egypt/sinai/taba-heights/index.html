Introduction to Taba Heights

Located in the Sinai Peninsula of Egypt, Taba Heights is a picturesque resort town that boasts gorgeous beaches, crystal clear waters, and stunning mountain views. The area is perfect for travelers who want to relax and unwind, with plenty of luxurious hotels, restaurants, and spas.

List of Places to Visit in Taba Heights

Taba Heights has a variety of attractions that cater to both nature lovers and history buffs. Here is a list of places that are worth visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castle Zaman: This impressive castle was built in 2004 as a modern interpretation of a historic fortress. It now serves as a museum and cultural center, showcasing the history and culture of Taba Heights.
  • Taba Castle: This restored castle dates back to the Ottoman era and was used as a military fortress. Visitors can explore the castle and learn about its history and significance.
  • St. Catherine’s Monastery: About two hours away from Taba Heights, this UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the oldest working Christian monasteries in the world. It is said to be the site of the Burning Bush from the Bible.

Natural Attractions of Taba Heights: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taba Nature Reserve: Located on the outskirts of Taba Heights, this protected area is home to a variety of wildlife. Visitors can take guided hikes, birdwatching tours, and enjoy the stunning views of the Red Sea.
  • Mount Sinai: This biblical mountain is located about two hours from Taba Heights and is known for its significance in Christianity, Judaism, and Islam. Visitors can hike to the summit and watch the sunrise or sunset.
  • Coral reefs: Taba Heights is known for its beautiful coral reefs, which make it an ideal destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taba Heights

Taba Heights has plenty of hiking and biking trails for those who want to explore the area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • The Colored Canyon: Located about an hour from Taba Heights, this canyon is famous for its vibrant colors and unique rock formations. It is a moderate hiking trail that takes about two to three hours to complete.
  • Mount Sinai Summit: This challenging hike takes about six to eight hours to complete but is worth it for the breathtaking views from the top.
  • Taba Nature Reserve: The reserve is home to several hiking trails that take visitors through stunning landscapes and allow them to spot local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Taba Heights: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural and cultural attractions, Taba Heights has plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • Diving and snorkeling: Taba Heights has several dive sites that offer breathtaking views of coral reefs, sea turtles, and other marine life.
  • Spa treatments: For those who want to unwind, Taba Heights has several luxurious spas that offer a variety of treatments, including massages, facials, and hot stone therapy.
  • Camel rides: Visitors can take a leisurely camel ride through the Sinai desert and enjoy the stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Taba Heights

Taba Heights has a diverse food scene that caters to all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebabs: These grilled meat skewers are a popular street food in Egypt and are often served with pita bread and a variety of dipping sauces.
  • Koshari: This vegetarian dish is made of rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas, topped with a spicy tomato sauce and caramelized onions.
  • Ful medames: This traditional Egyptian breakfast dish is made of mashed fava beans, mixed with spices, vegetables, and served with pita bread.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: This hot beverage is a staple of Egyptian culture and is often served with mint or other herbs.
  • Coffee: Egyptian coffee is strong and bold, often served with a spoonful of sugar and a side of dates.
  • Sahlab: This hot, creamy milk-based beverage is flavored with vanilla and cinnamon and often served with nuts or coconut.

Where to Stay in Taba Heights: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taba Heights has several accommodations that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Taba Sands Hotel & Spa: This luxurious hotel offers stunning sea views, private balconies, and a variety of spa treatments.
  • Cinderella Hotel Taba: This budget-friendly hotel is a great choice for travelers who want to enjoy the views without breaking the bank.
  • The Bay View Taba Heights Resort: This hotel offers a private beach, stunning mountain views, and several activities and tours for guests.

What to Buy in Taba Heights: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taba Heights has several souvenirs and local products that make great gifts and mementos. Here are some of the most popular items:

  • Rugs and textiles: Egyptian rugs and textiles are renowned for their intricate designs and quality.
  • Spices and herbs: The local spice markets offer a variety of herbs and spices that are often used in traditional Egyptian dishes.
  • Jewelry: The local markets offer a variety of jewelry, including gold and silver necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.

Local Culture and Customs of Taba Heights

Egypt has a rich and unique culture that is worth exploring. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Dress code: Egypt is a conservative country, and visitors should dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Ramadan: Ramadan is a month-long fasting period for Muslims, and visitors should be respectful and mindful of the customs and traditions during this time.
  • Greetings: Egyptians often greet each other with a handshake and a smile.

Best Time to Visit Taba Heights

The best time to visit Taba Heights is from September to April when temperatures are cooler, and the humidity is low. The summer months can be uncomfortably hot, with temperatures reaching up to 40 degrees Celsius.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taba Heights

Egypt, in general, is a safe country for travelers. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as watching their belongings and avoiding secluded areas.

Health in Taba Heights

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid consuming raw or undercooked food. Sunscreen and protective clothing should be worn to protect against sunburn and dehydration. Vaccinations for hepatitis A, typhoid, and tetanus are recommended before traveling to Egypt.

Getting to Taba Heights: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taba Heights

The nearest airport to Taba Heights is Taba International Airport, which is located about two kilometers away from the town center. Visitors can also fly into Sharm El Sheikh International Airport or Cairo International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Taba Heights

Taba Heights has several bus stations that operate daily services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and private car services are also available for visitors who want to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Taba Heights

Taba Heights is small enough to explore on foot, but taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who want to venture further.

Where to Go Next from Taba Heights: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taba Heights makes a great base for exploring nearby towns and cities. Here are some popular destinations:

  • Nuweiba: This laid-back beach town is known for its stunning views and crystal clear waters.
  • Cairo: Egypt’s capital city has plenty of ancient monuments, museums, and cultural attractions to explore.
  • Eilat: This Israeli resort town is located across the border from Taba Heights and is known for its beaches, nightlife, and casinos.

Conclusion

Taba Heights is a hidden gem in Egypt that offers a unique and fascinating blend of nature, culture, and relaxation. Whether you prefer to explore ancient monuments or lounge on the beach, Taba Heights has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today!
