Introduction to Siwa

Siwa is a small town located in the Western Desert region of Egypt. Known for its natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture, Siwa has become a popular destination for travelers seeking something a little off the beaten path. The town is also famous for its stunning salt lakes, palm groves, and hot springs. Here, we’ll guide you through the best places to visit, the top things to do, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Siwa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Siwa House Museum

Siwa House is one of the oldest and most well-preserved buildings in the town. The house was constructed in the traditional Siwan style, using local materials such as salt, clay, and palm wood. Today, it serves as a museum and showcases the history, culture, and customs of the Siwan people.

Temple of the Oracle

The Temple of the Oracle is an ancient Egyptian temple located on a hilltop outside of Siwa. It was dedicated to the god Amun and was believed to be the site of an oracle that provided prophesies to the ancient Egyptians. It is one of the most important historical sites in Siwa.

Fatnas Island

Fatnas is an island located in the middle of Siwa Lake. It is famous for its beautiful gardens and the remains of an old Roman settlement. Visitors can take a boat tour of the island and explore its ancient ruins and temples.

Natural Attractions of Siwa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shali Fortress

The Shali Fortress is a ruined fortress situated in the heart of Siwa. It was originally built as a fortified citadel during the medieval period but was later abandoned. Today, it offers visitors a unique glimpse into the town’s rich history and provides breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Siwa Salt Lake

The Siwa Salt Lake is one of the largest salt lakes in Egypt. It is home to a variety of unique plant and animal species and is surrounded by palm groves and sand dunes. Visitors can take a dip in the lake’s healing waters, which are thought to have therapeutic properties.

The Great Sand Sea

The Great Sand Sea is a vast desert region located near Siwa. It features towering sand dunes, rock formations, and scenic vistas. Visitors can take a tour of the desert by camel or ATV and experience the beauty of the Egyptian wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Siwa

Mountain Biking in Siwa

Siwa offers a variety of biking trails for adventurers of all skill levels. Visitors can explore the town’s scenic landscapes by bike and experience the local culture up close. The most popular routes include the Taziry Trail, the Foggini Trail, and the Siwa-Abu Ballas Trail.

Hiking in the Nature Reserve

The Siwa Nature Reserve is a protected area located on the outskirts of the town. It is home to a variety of plant and animal life, including rare species such as the Siwan Palm. Visitors can explore the reserve’s hiking trails and experience the beauty of the Egyptian wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Siwa: Activities and Tours

Siwan Cultural Tour

A Siwan Cultural Tour offers visitors the chance to experience the town’s unique culture first-hand. The tour includes visits to local museums, historical sites, and artisan workshops, where visitors can learn about traditional crafts such as pottery making and carpet weaving.

Siwan Safari Tour

The Siwan Safari Tour is a thrilling adventure that takes visitors deep into the heart of the Great Sand Sea. The tour includes dune bashing, camel rides, and visits to ancient ruins and oases.

Week-Long Itinerary for Siwa

Day 1 Explore the Shali Fortress and the Siwan House Museum
Day 2 Visit the at the Temple of the Oracle and Fatnas Island

Day 7 Relax and unwind in one of Siwa’s many hot springs

Food and Drink Scene of Siwa

Must-Try Dishes

Aish Merahrah

Aish Merahrah is a traditional Siwan dish made from a type of bread that is baked in a clay oven. It is typically filled with meat, tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Siwan Dates

Siwa is famous for its delicious dates, which are grown in the town’s palm groves. Visitors can sample fresh dates from local vendors or purchase them to bring home.

Local Beverages

Siwan Tea

Siwan Tea is a popular local beverage made from a mixture of herbs, including mint and sage. It is typically served sweetened with sugar and is perfect for relaxing after a long day in the desert.

Siwan Juice

Siwan Juice is a refreshing drink made from the town’s abundant supply of fresh fruits, such as oranges, pomegranates, and grapes.

Where to Stay in Siwa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Siwa Villa Hotel

Siwa Villa Hotel is a popular choice for visitors seeking a luxurious stay in Siwa. It features spacious rooms and suites, a swimming pool, and a restaurant that serves traditional Siwan dishes.

Shali Lodge

Shali Lodge is located in the heart of Siwa and offers guests a unique experience of staying in a traditional Siwan building. It features comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace with views of the town and the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Siwa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Siwan Pottery

Siwan pottery is famous for its intricate designs and beautiful colors. Visitors can purchase pottery from local shops or even take a pottery-making workshop to create their own.

Siwan Embroidery

Siwan Embroidery is a traditional craft that is passed down from generation to generation. Visitors can purchase embroidered clothing, bags, and other accessories from local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Siwa

Siwa has a unique culture that is deeply rooted in its history and geography. The town’s residents are known for being friendly, hospitable, and proud of their heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including dressing modestly and avoiding public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Siwa

The best time to visit Siwa is during the winter months (November to February), when the weather is mild and pleasant. During the summer months, temperatures can reach over 100 degrees Fahrenheit, making it too hot to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Siwa

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without their permission
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Speak loudly or disturb the peace of the town

Safety and Health

Safety in Siwa

Siwa is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing. It is also important to avoid exploring remote areas alone and to adhere to local customs and traditions.

Health in Siwa

Siwa is a remote town, so visitors should take appropriate health precautions. The town’s hot springs and natural waters may contain bacteria, so visitors should be sure to drink only bottled water and to avoid consuming food from street vendors.

Getting to Siwa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Siwa

The nearest airport to Siwa is the Siwa Oasis Airport, which is located approximately 20 kilometers from the town center. However, there are limited flights from Cairo and other major Egyptian cities.

Bus Stations Near Siwa

Visitors can also take a bus from Cairo to Siwa, which takes approximately 10 hours. Buses depart from the Turgoman Bus Station in Cairo.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Cairo or other major Egyptian cities. However, driving in Egypt can be challenging due to heavy traffic and poor road conditions.

How to Get Around in Siwa

Siwa is a small town, and most attractions and sites are within walking distance of each other. Bikes and motorcycles are also available for rental, providing a fun and efficient way to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Siwa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bahariya Oasis

Located approximately 4 hours from Siwa, Bahariya Oasis is a popular destination for travelers seeking more adventure and excitement. It is known for its beautiful landscapes, ancient ruins, and hot springs.

Cairo

Cairo is an incredible city with a rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the pyramids, museums, and other historical sites or experience the vibrant nightlife and shopping scene.

Conclusion

Siwa is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors the chance to explore the beautiful Egyptian desert and experience a rich and vibrant culture. With plenty of historical sites, beautiful landscapes, and exciting activities to choose from, Siwa is the perfect destination for travelers seeking something a little off the beaten path.
