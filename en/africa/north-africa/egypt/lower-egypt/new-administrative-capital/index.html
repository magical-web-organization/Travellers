Introduction to New Administrative Capital

Located in Lower Egypt, New Administrative Capital is a planned city built to serve as the new administrative hub of Egypt. With its modern infrastructure and state-of-the-art facilities, the city is attracting thousands of tourists every year. The city boasts of beautiful architecture, scenic views, and rich history, making it an ideal destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in New Administrative Capital

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Grand Egyptian Museum: This spectacular museum is dedicated to the preservation and display of ancient Egyptian artifacts, sculptures and statues.

  • Al-Fattah Al-Alim Mosque: Located in the center of the city, this mosque is one of the largest in the world, with space for over 20,000 worshippers.

  • Martyrs’ Memorial: A beautiful monument that honors the Egyptian soldiers who died for their country in recent wars.

Natural Attractions of New Administrative Capital: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Green River Park: A beautiful park that stretches over 10 kilometers along the city’s outskirts, with bike paths, walking trails, and scenic views.

  • Al-Masa Capital Tower: A futuristic tower that provides stunning views of the city and surrounding desert.

  • Wadi Degla National Park: Located just outside the city, this park offers a great opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Egypt through hiking, biking, and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Administrative Capital

  • Green River Park Bike Path: A 10-kilometer bike path that runs through the scenic Green River Park.

  • Wadi Degla Trail: A 16-kilometer hiking trail that winds through Wadi Degla National Park, offering stunning views of the surrounding desert.

Best Things to Do in New Administrative Capital: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the city from above and take in stunning views of the landscape.

  • Felucca Cruise on the Nile River: Take a relaxing felucca ride down the Nile River and take in the beautiful scenery.

  • Horseback Riding in the Desert: Experience the beauty of the desert on horseback, with experienced guides to ensure your safety.

Food and Drink Scene of New Administrative Capital

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari: A hearty vegetarian dish made with pasta, lentils, chickpeas, and tomato sauce.

  • Ful Medames: A traditional Egyptian breakfast dish made from fava beans, served with various toppings.

  • Ta’miyya: Similar to falafel, this is a common street food in Egypt made with fava beans and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus Tea: A refreshing and popular tea made from dried hibiscus flowers.

  • Shai: The traditional Egyptian tea, is a popular hot drink consumed throughout the country.

  • Karkadeh: Another popular tea made from hibiscus flowers, often served cold.

Where to Stay in New Administrative Capital: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • JW Marriott Hotel Cairo: A luxurious hotel that offers all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

  • SHE Warehouse: A trendy hostel that offers affordable accommodation with a unique industrial design.

  • Royal Maxim Palace Kempinski Cairo: A five-star hotel that offers luxurious rooms and suites, fine dining, and a spa.

What to Buy in New Administrative Capital: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Egyptian Cotton: Known worldwide for its quality, the cotton products of Egypt are a must-have souvenir.

  • Spices: Exotic Egyptian spices like cumin, coriander, and saffron make for flavorful souvenirs.

  • Papyrus: An ancient Egyptian relic, papyrus is still made and sold in Egypt as art and stationary.

Local Culture and Customs of New Administrative Capital

  • Egyptians are known for their friendly and hospitable nature, so don’t be surprised if you’re invited for tea or a meal.

  • Modest dress is recommended, especially if you plan to visit mosques or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit New Administrative Capital

The best time to visit New Administrative Capital is from October to April when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. Summers are extremely hot and can be uncomfortable for sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Administrative Capital

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try local cuisine, including street food.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Display public affection.

  • Criticize local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Administrative Capital

New Administrative Capital is a relatively safe destination, but like any other place, it is recommended to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid visiting unlit areas alone at night.

Health in New Administrative Capital

It is recommended to drink bottled water and take precautions against the sun, especially during summer months when temperatures can reach high levels.

Getting to New Administrative Capital: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Administrative Capital

  • Cairo International Airport (CAI): 45 km away.

Bus Stations Near New Administrative Capital

  • Cairo Central Bus Station (Turgoman Bus Station): 29 km away.

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are available and can be hailed from the street.

How to Get Around in New Administrative Capital

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are the most convenient way to get around the city.

  • Public buses also operate in the city, but they can be crowded and difficult to navigate.

Where to Go Next from New Administrative Capital: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cairo: The capital city of Egypt is just a short drive away and offers endless opportunities for exploring Egyptian history and culture.

  • Alexandria: A beautiful coastal city located on the Mediterranean Sea, known for its history, culture, and ancient landmarks.

Conclusion

New Administrative Capital is an exciting destination with plenty to see and do. Whether you’re interested in exploring history, architecture, or nature, this city is sure to deliver an unforgettable experience. Be sure to follow local customs and take precautions for your safety and health, and you’re bound to have a wonderful time.
