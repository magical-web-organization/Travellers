Introduction to Banha

Banha, located in the Qalyubia Governorate of Lower Egypt, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Despite being one of the largest cities in Egypt, it remains off the beaten path for most tourists. Banha is a perfect destination for travelers interested in Egyptian history, culture, and natural landscapes in a less crowded environment.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Banha, from top attractions to local cuisine, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Banha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Banha Museum

Banha Museumprovides a glimpse into the history of Qalyubia governorate. The museum exhibits fossils, pottery, paintings, and mummies that date back to prehistoric times.

Al-Fath Mosque

Al-Fath Mosque, located in the center of Banha, is a beautiful mosque that boasts a unique architectural style. You can spend time exploring the beautiful interiors and enjoying the peaceful surroundings.

Al-Qanater Al-Khayriyah

Al-Qanater Al-Khayriyahis a historic building that is situated on the Nile river. It has a beautiful architectural style and is surrounded by gardens, including palm trees, which make it a beautiful location to visit.

Kasr El Zaafran

Kasr El Zaafran, formerly known as Kasr El Banat, is a historic fort that dates back to the Mamluk era. It served as a military station and was used to protect the area from enemies. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and a great spot to take beautiful photos.

Natural Attractions of Banha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Banha Botanical Garden

Banha Botanical Garden is a hidden gem that showcases the beauty of Egypt’s natural flora. It is a perfect spot for a relaxing stroll, and you can enjoy the beautiful water features and greenery.

Wadi El-Natrun

Wadi El-Natrun, located about 30 km from Banha, is a spectacular valley that houses four monasteries, St. Pachomius, St. Macarius, St. Bishoy, and El-Surian. The monasteries are open to visitors and provide a glimpse into the Coptic Christian history of Egypt.

Qaraoun Lake

Qaraoun Lake is a beautiful lake located on the northern edge of Qalyubia governorate. It is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and picnicking. You can also rent a boat and explore the lake while enjoying scenic views.

Hike to Gebel Um Shomer

Gebel Um Shomer, located south of Banha, is a perfect spot for hiking enthusiasts. The hike to the top can be challenging, but you will be rewarded with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Banha: Activities and Tours

Banha Culture and Heritage Tour

The Banha Culture and Heritage Tour is a great way to explore the city and learn about its rich history and culture. The tour includes a visit to Banha Museum, Al-Fath Mosque, and Kasr El Zaafran.

Banha Botanical Garden Tour

The Banha Botanical Garden Tour is a relaxing tour that allows you to explore the beauty of the Botanical Garden. It includes a walking tour of the garden and a chance to experience Egypt’s nature up close.

Wadi El-Natrun Monastery Tour

The Wadi El-Natrun Monastery Tour takes you to the four beautiful monasteries located in the valley. You will learn about the Coptic Christian history of Egypt and enjoy the scenic views of the valley.

Local Culture and Customs of Banha

Banha has a rich culture and tradition that you can experience by visiting the historic attractions and interacting with the friendly locals. Dress modestly and be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Banha

The best time to visit Banha is during the winter months (November to February) when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, you can enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views without worrying about the uncomfortable heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banha

Do:

  • Learn some Arabic Phrases
  • Dress modestly
  • Be respectful of local culture and traditions

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission
  • Eat or drink in public during Ramadan
  • Be impolite to locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Banha

Overall, Banha is a safe place to visit. However, it is always wise to take necessary precautions such as being aware of your surroundings, avoiding flashing valuables, and staying in well-lit places at night.

Health in Banha

It is essential to drink bottled water and avoid tap water to avoid digestive issues. Sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses are also necessary to protect yourself from the hot and sunny weather.
