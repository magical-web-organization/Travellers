Introduction to Lower Egypt

Egypt, one of the cradles of civilization, is a land of contradictions that has an interesting history, fascinating culture, and ancient temples. Lower Egypt, located in the northern region of Egypt, is an embodiment of these traits. From the bustling city of Cairo to the Mediterranean coast, Lower Egypt is full of wonders that tourists can explore. It is a region known for its museums, ancient temples, natural wonders, and local cuisine. Here is a comprehensive guide to all that you can see, do, eat, and experience in Lower Egypt.

Food and Drink Scene of Lower Egypt

Must-Try Dishes

Lower Egypt offers a variety of traditional and cultural dishes that visitors can try:

  • Koshari: A mix of macaroni, lentils, fried onions, and chickpeas topped with tomato sauce.
  • Shawarma: A wrap filled with slices of chicken or beef, salad, and tahini sauce.
  • Fatta: Bread pieces soaked in lamb broth, covered in tomato sauce, topped with rice and chickpeas.
  • Molokhia: A soup made of jute leaves and sometimes served with chicken or beef.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus Tea
  • Qamar El-Din: Apricot juice
  • Sahlab: Hot milk drink with orchid root

Local Culture and Customs of Lower Egypt

Egypt has an ancient and deeply-rooted culture that reflects in its architecture, food, and customs. It is essential to be respectful of their culture when exploring the country. It’s best to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and remove shoes before entering mosques. Haggling is a common practice when shopping in the local markets.

Best Time to Visit Lower Egypt

The best time to visit Lower Egypt is between October to April when the temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. However, it can get crowded with tourists. The off-season period is from May to September when the weather is hot and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lower Egypt

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Wear comfortable and modest clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Have loose change for tipping

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water
  • Touch or climb on ancient ruins and monuments
  • Photograph people without asking their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Lower Egypt

Egypt is considered a safe country for tourists. However, it is essential to stay cautious in crowded areas and not to leave valuables unattended.

Health in Lower Egypt

Visitors are advised to take precautions before traveling to Egypt. Immunizations for hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended. It is also essential to drink bottled water and avoid uncooked food or street food.
