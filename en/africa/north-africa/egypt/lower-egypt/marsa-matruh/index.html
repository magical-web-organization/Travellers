Introduction to Marsa Matruh

Marsa Matruh is a coastal city located in the northwest of Egypt, just 290 km from Alexandria, making it an ideal location for a weekend getaway. Its charming location on the Mediterranean Sea is complimented by stunning beaches and warm weather throughout the year. Popular among local and foreign tourists alike, Marsa Matruh boasts of natural beauty, cultural heritage and various other attractions, making it a must-visit destination in Egypt.

List of Places to Visit in Marsa Matruh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marsa Matruh has a rich cultural heritage, making it a fascinating destination for history buffs. For instance, Al Alamein features World War II battlefields and cemeteries that are open to visitors, allowing visitors to learn about the history and sacrifices associated with Egypt’s war involvement. Mareotis Tomb and Qasr Al-Raml archaeological sites are other historically significant sites, which exhibit ancient artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Marsa Matruh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Marsa Matruh is also known for its scenic views and natural beauty. The beaches in the area are a major draw, with Cleopatra Beach being a popular attraction. The area is also home to numerous caves and rock formations, such as the ominous Wadi El-Natrun mountain and the Mersa Matrouh Protected Area all of which are popular spots for hiking, cycling, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marsa Matruh

Marsa Matruh is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The area boasts a variety of hiking and biking routes that accommodate all skill levels. One of the most popular routes is Wadi El-Natrun, a challenging route good for experienced hikers. Alternatively, easier routes such as the Jebel al Zeit’ al Aswad trail and the Salims’ Canyon route are perfect for beginners.

Best Things to Do in Marsa Matruh: Activities and Tours

Marsa Matruh has a variety of activities and tours. The Sea Cave tour is a popular attraction that takes visitors on a boat ride to the surrounding sea caves, offering a stunning view of the oceanic environment. Additionally, horseback riding on the beach at sunset is a favorite pastime activity for many visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marsa Matruh

Day 1: Al Alamein WWII museum and Italian monument. Day 2: Start your day by exploring the Mareotis Tomb and mosque, and visit the excellent Qasr Al-Raml archaeological site. Day 3: Head to the beach and relax by the sea. Day 4: Take a hike in the Wadi El-Natrun Mountains. Day 5: Take a guided sea cave tour and explore the surrounding caves. Day 6: Sunset horseback riding on the beach. Day 7: Visit any spot that you missed or revisit any of your favorite spots.

Food and Drink Scene of Marsa Matruh

Must-Try Dishes

Marsa Matruh is home to authentic Egyptian cuisine. One of the most popular dishes is ‘Ful Mudammas,’ a dish made by mashing fava beans and often eaten for breakfast. Other dishes worth trying are Bassal wa Basa (Onion and Spice Mix) and Molokhiya (a traditional soup).

Local Beverages

Egyptians love tea, and you’ll find it almost everywhere. Additionally, sugar cane juice is a refreshing drink to beat the heat. Other local beverages include Arabic coffee, hibiscus tea, and Karkadeh.

Where to Stay in Marsa Matruh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marsa Matruh offers an array of accommodations for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the most popular hotels include the Jaz Almaza Beach Resort, the Beau Site Hotel, and the Porto Matrouh Resort. On the other hand, budget-conscious travelers can consider more economical options such as Radwan Guest House, Al Tayar Matrouh, and Veracon Aqua Park.

What to Buy in Marsa Matruh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Marsa Matruh, purchase souvenirs for your family and friends. Maakad Saad is an excellent place to visit to purchase local and handcrafted souvenirs such as wooden camel figures, jewelry, and Egyptian cotton textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Marsa Matruh

The predominant religion in Egypt is Islam, which has formed its cultural norms and customs. When entering places of worship, covering and removing footwear is required. Additionally, Egyptian culture also places great emphasis on hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Marsa Matruh

The best time to visit Marsa Matruh is between June and September when temperatures are the highest and the sea is warm. However, spring from March to May or autumn from September to November when temperatures are moderate are also good alternatives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marsa Matruh

Do:

  • Dress modestly when you visit places of worship
  • Try out local food and drink
  • Take sufficient sun protection

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards the local culture and customs
  • Engage in public display of affection (PDA)
  • Venture out alone at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Marsa Matruh

Marsa Matruh is generally safe. However, visitors are advised to take standard precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended on the beach, and also to use licensed taxis and public transport in the city center.

Health in Marsa Matruh

Marsa Matruh is prone to waterborne illnesses such as typhoid and cholera. Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid raw dishes. Standard vaccinations like yellow fever, measles, polio and meningitis are also recommended.

Getting to Marsa Matruh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marsa Matruh

Marsa Matruh International Airport serves international and domestic flights, and it is the fastest way to reach Marsa Matruh. The airport is seven kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Marsa Matruh

Marsa Matrouh has its bus station which mostly serves domestic destinations. Travelers can also find various public transportation options in the form of minivans and taxis.

Other Options

Another way to get to Marsa Matruh is via car, arranging private transportation or renting a car to get to the city independently. However, it’s often a better option to rely on a local guide or fixed-rate taxi service for in-city transportation.

How to Get Around in Marsa Matruh

Transportation options in Marsa Matruh include taxis, minivans, and bicycles. To get around the area, hiring a taxi or using public transportation is a convenient and affordable way. Cycling can also be an enjoyable alternative to explore nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Marsa Matruh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Egypt, here are some nearby destinations that you must see: Alexandria, El-Alamein, Siwa Oasis, and Al-Mersa Matruh.

Conclusion

Marsa Matruh is a hidden gem in Egypt that offers plenty of opportunities to explore its natural beauty, historical attractions, and local culture. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can easily plan their trips, including where to stay, what to do, and how to get around. With its warm weather, great restaurants, and stunning beaches, Marsa Matruh is a must-visit destination in Egypt.
