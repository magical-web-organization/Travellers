Introduction to Tanta

Tanta is a city located in Lower Egypt, in the Nile Delta region. It is the fifth largest city in the country and is known for its vibrant cultural scene, delicious cuisine, and historical landmarks.

Tanta is a must-visit destination for those looking to immerse themselves in Egyptian culture and history. From exploring its museums and historical attractions to hiking or biking its scenic routes, there is something for everyone in Tanta.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the top places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat and drink in Tanta. We will also provide you with some tips on local customs and safety, transportation options, and nearby destinations for your convenience.

List of Places to Visit in Tanta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tanta National Museum

The Tanta National Museum, located in the heart of the city, tells the story of Tanta’s history and heritage. The museum houses a collection of artifacts, including sculptures, pottery, and jewelry, dating back to ancient times. It also features exhibits on a variety of topics, such as the history of the Egyptian cotton industry and the life of Sayyed El-Badawy, one of the most famous Sufi saints in Egypt.

Al-Saeed Mohamed Mosque

One of the most important religious sites in Tanta is the Al-Saeed Mohamed Mosque. The mosque is located in the center of the city and was originally built in the 16th century. It was later expanded in the 18th and 19th centuries and features unique architectural features, such as its beautiful minarets and domes.

Sheik Mansour Al-Tanawy Mosque

Another significant mosque in Tanta is the Sheik Mansour Al-Tanawy Mosque, located in the eastern part of the city. It is named after the famous Sufi saint Mansour Al-Tanawy, and features a beautiful exterior and interior design.

Tanta University

Tanta University is the second largest university in the Nile Delta region and is known for its educational and research excellence. The university offers courses in various fields, including medicine, engineering, and humanities. Visitors can take a guided campus tour and learn about the university’s history and current projects.

Natural Attractions of Tanta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tanta Botanical Garden

The Tanta Botanical Garden is a stunning green oasis in the middle of the city. It features a variety of plants and trees, including rare and exotic species. Visitors can relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings or take a guided tour to learn about the different types of plants and their medicinal properties.

Wadi El Rayan Waterfalls

Located just a short drive from Tanta, the Wadi El Rayan Waterfalls is a beautiful natural attraction that should not be missed. The area features two stunning waterfalls, surrounded by lush greenery and stunning rock formations. Visitors can swim, hike, or simply enjoy the scenic views.

Kafr El-Gesheir

Kafr El-Gesheir is a beautiful village located on the outskirts of Tanta. It is known for its picturesque landscapes and stunning rural architecture. Visitors can take a guided tour of the village, explore the local markets, or simply relax and enjoy the tranquility.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanta

Tanta offers some of the most scenic and picturesque hiking and biking routes in Egypt. Some of the most popular options include the Wadi El Rayan waterfall trail, the Tanta Botanical Garden loop, and the Kafr El-Abd scenic route.

Best Things to Do in Tanta: Activities and Tours

Sufi Music and Dancing

Tanta is known for its vibrant Sufi music and dancing scene. Visitors can attend a Sufi music concert or dance performance, and experience the unique cultural traditions of the city firsthand.

Tanta Cotton Market

The Tanta Cotton Market is a bustling marketplace located in the center of the city. It is known for its high-quality cotton products, such as textiles, clothing, and embroidery. Visitors can shop for souvenirs or simply enjoy the lively atmosphere of the market.

Abla Fahita Museum

The Abla Fahita Museum is a small and quirky museum dedicated to the famous puppet character Abla Fahita. The museum features a collection of Abla Fahita’s costumes, props, and memorabilia, as well as interactive exhibits and games for visitors to enjoy.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tanta

Food and Drink Scene of Tanta

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular dishes in Tanta include:

  • Kobeba: a traditional dish made of cracked wheat stuffed with minced meat, onions, and spices
  • Feteer: a layered pastry that can be sweet or savory, filled with cheese or meat
  • Mahshi: vegetables stuffed with rice, herbs, and spices

Local Beverages

Some of the most popular local beverages in Tanta include:

  • Karkadeh: a sweet and tangy hibiscus tea
  • Sahlab: a creamy, spiced milk pudding
  • Tamr hindi: a sweet and sour tamarind juice

Where to Stay in Tanta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tanta offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, including budget-friendly hostels, cozy guesthouses, and luxury hotels. Some of the most popular choices include the El Salam Hotel, the Green Hotel, and the Zahraa El Maadi Hotel.

What to Buy in Tanta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Tanta include:

  • Local cotton products, such as textiles and clothing
  • Traditional Egyptian spices, such as cumin and coriander
  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics

Local Culture and Customs of Tanta

Tanta is a culturally diverse city, with a strong Islamic influence. Visitors should dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions. It is also important to note that alcohol and pork are not widely available in Tanta due to religious restrictions.

Best Time to Visit Tanta

The best time to visit Tanta is between October and April when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, the city is also less crowded with tourists, and accommodation prices are more affordable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanta

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Learn about the city’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public or bring pork products into the city
  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Engage in any activities that may be considered disrespectful or offensive

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanta

Tanta is generally considered a safe city for travelers. However, visitors should still take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Tanta

It is recommended that visitors to Tanta get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B and typhoid. It is also important to drink bottled or filtered water and use insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever.

Getting to Tanta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tanta

The nearest airport to Tanta is Cairo International Airport, located approximately 90 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or a bus from the airport to Tanta.

Bus Stations Near Tanta

Tanta has two major bus stations, Tanta Station and El Shohadaa Station. Both offer regular services to and from major cities in Egypt.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Tanta by train. The city has a train station located in the city center, with several daily services to and from Cairo.

How to Get Around in Tanta

Tanta is a compact city that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take a taxi or a bus to reach more distant attractions.

Where to Go Next from Tanta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the most popular destinations to visit near Tanta include:

  • Alexandria: a historic coastal city known for its beautiful beaches and ancient landmarks
  • Al Minya: a city located on the banks of the Nile River, known for its archaeological sites and cultural attractions
  • Cairo: the capital of Egypt, home to some of the world’s most iconic landmarks, such as the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx

Conclusion

Tanta is a city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Egypt. From exploring its historical landmarks and museums to hiking its scenic routes and sampling its delicious cuisine, Tanta has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well equipped to make the most of your visit to this fascinating city.
