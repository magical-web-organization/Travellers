Introduction to Giza

Located in Cairo, Lower Egypt, Giza is a city renowned for its mesmerizing pyramids that have stood the test of time for over 4,500 years. The Great Pyramid of Giza, also known as the Pyramid of Khufu, is the last surviving wonder of the ancient world. Its extraordinary features, alongside other impressive structures, massive statues, temples, and museums, have made Giza a top travel destination for tourists globally.

List of Places to Visit in Giza

Giza is a city filled with attractions for globetrotters. From historical marvels to scenic views, this city has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Great Pyramid of Giza: The only surviving wonder of the ancient world, this architectural marvel still lures visitors with its sheer size and symmetry. You can even get an up-close view of this monument by entering the pyramid’s interior and exploring the burial chamber of Pharaoh Khufu.
  • The Sphinx: This famous limestone statue with the head of a human and body of a lion is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Giza. Surrounded by pyramids and temples, this mysterious statue, built over 4,500 years ago, continues to fascinate and awe visitors from around the world.
  • Egyptian Museum: Located in Cairo, this museum houses an extensive collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts, dating back to the Pharaonic period. The museum is home to the world’s largest display of ancient Egyptian antiquities, including the treasures of Tutankhamen and mummies of pharaohs.
  • Saqqara: An ancient necropolis, Saqqara is home to the first known monumental stone structure in Egypt, the Step Pyramid of Djoser. Here, you can explore a complex of tombs and shrines from various periods of Egyptian history.

Natural Attractions of Giza: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Giza Plateau: This vast plateau is an ideal spot to catch panoramic views of the pyramids and the surrounding desert. Visitors can take camel or horseback rides across the plateau at sunset or sunrise.
  • Nile River Cruise: Take a scenic cruise down the Nile and discover some of the picturesque landscapes of Egypt. You can enjoy dinner, dance, and music on board the riverboat while taking in the Nile’s natural beauty.
  • Abu Simbel Temples: Carved out of solid sandstone in Nubia, the two temples of Abu Simbel are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, situated on the banks of Lake Nasser. The temples’ architectural beauty and impressive statues of pharaohs make for a memorable excursion.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Giza

Giza’s landscape of deserts, valleys, and hills make it an excellent adventure destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are the top routes to explore:

  • The Pyramids of Giza to Saqqara Hike: This scenic hike passes by famous landmarks, including the Sphinx, the pyramids of Giza and ends at the ancient complex of Saqqara.
  • Giza Desert Adventure Bike Tour: Take a biking trip into the desert on this guided tour and experience the stunning landscapes at the foot of the Sahara.
  • Sunset Hike to Mount Sinai: You can climb Mount Sinai and take in the breathtaking views of the sunset from the 7,498-foot summit.

Best Things to Do in Giza: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to adventure activities, Giza offers visitors a plethora of exciting activities to indulge in. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Sound and Light Show at the Pyramids of Giza: Enjoy an unforgettable evening at the Pyramids of Giza while watching the ancient wonders come alive through a sound and light show.
  • Luxury Dinner Cruise on the Nile: Embark on a luxury dinner cruise and indulge in delicious local cuisine while floating down the Nile River.
  • Felucca Ride on the Nile: This romantic journey on a traditional Egyptian sailboat is an excellent way to discover the Nile’s beauty and the city’s landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Giza

Here is a week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Giza:

Food and Drink Scene of Giza

Egyptian cuisine is a unique blend of Middle Eastern, African, and Mediterranean influences, and the food scene in Giza offers a range of tasty dishes at affordable prices.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshary: A popular dish made of macaroni, lentils, chickpeas, and topped with a spicy tomato sauce and fried onions.
  • Fiteer: A savory pastry filled with meat or vegetables and served hot.
  • Molokhia: A green leafy vegetable cooked with garlic and coriander and served with rice.
  • Ful Medames: A traditional Egyptian breakfast dish made of fava beans, served with bread and eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Tea is an all-time favorite beverage in Egypt and is served hot and sweetened with sugar.
  • Coffee: Arabic coffee is a strong and potent brew made from roasted coffee beans and served with dates or sweet treats.
  • Karkade: A hibiscus tea served cold, also known as Egyptian tea.

Where to Stay in Giza: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Giza offers a range of accommodation options to fit every budget and travel style. These are some options to consider:

  • Mena House Hotel: Located opposite the Pyramids of Giza, this luxurious hotel is steeped in history and offers breathtaking views of the pyramids.
  • Le Meridien Pyramids Hotel and Spa: This five-star hotel provides an excellent base to explore Giza and is within proximity to the pyramids.
  • Pyramids Loft Homestay: This budget-friendly guesthouse is situated close to the pyramids and offers an authentic home-stay experience.
  • Sphinx Guest House: This guest house offers comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the pyramids.

What to Buy in Giza: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping in Giza offers a range of traditional handicrafts and souvenirs that make great gifts to take home. Here are some popular choices:

  • Papyrus Art: You can purchase ancient Egyptian-style papyrus art or have custom designs created.
  • Alabaster Statues: Alabaster is a natural stone found in Egypt, and artisans skillfully sculpt it into various shapes and sizes.
  • Spices and Teas: Take home some locally sourced spices, teas, and herbs that you can use in your cooking.
  • Egyptian Cotton: Known for its softness, Egyptian cotton is a popular export and makes for comfortable garments and bed linen.

Local Culture and Customs of Giza

The people of Giza are incredibly friendly and hospitable, and their culture is deeply rooted in Egypt’s rich history and traditions. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: While Egypt is generally tolerant, it is advisable to dress conservatively to respect local customs and traditions.
  • Greet Locals: Greetings are an essential part of the culture in Giza, and it is customary to greet locals when entering various establishments.
  • Bargain: Bargaining is a common practice when shopping or taking a taxi, and visitors can often negotiate a better deal.

Best Time to Visit Giza

The best time to visit Giza is during the fall between September and November and the spring between March and May. Temperatures are mild during these months, making it the perfect time to explore the city and its surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Giza

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Giza:

Do:

  • Explore on Foot: Walking is an excellent way to explore Giza’s landmarks and soak up the city’s atmosphere.
  • Respect Local Customs: Be respectful of the local customs and traditions while in Giza.
  • Carry Cash: Giza is a cash-based economy, and it is essential to carry enough cash when shopping or taking taxis.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: It is advisable not to drink tap water as it is not safe for consumption.
  • Photograph Without Permission: Taking photographs of people or their property without permission is considered impolite, and it is advisable to ask for permission first.
  • Wear Revealing Clothes: Avoid wearing revealing clothes or shorts when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Giza is generally a safe destination for visitors, both in terms of personal security and health. However, visitors should be aware of potential scams, pickpocketing, and crowded areas, particularly when visiting tourist sites.

Safety in Giza

  • Avoid Crowded Areas: When visiting tourist sites, be mindful of your surroundings, and avoid crowded areas.
  • Beware of Scams: Be cautious of scams or people trying to sell you counterfeit goods, and always use official vendors.
  • Have Emergency Contact Information: Always carry a copy of your passport and emergency contact information.

Health in Giza

  • Stay Hydrated: It is essential to stay hydrated at all times as Giza is in a desert climate.
  • Use Sunscreen: Protect your skin by using sunscreen and wearing hats or scarves to cover your head.
  • Medical Insurance: It is advisable to have comprehensive medical insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Giza: Transportation Options and Logistics

Giza is located in close proximity to Cairo and is easily accessible by bus, taxi, or car.

Airports Near Giza

  • Cairo International Airport: This airport is located 32 km from Giza and is one of the busiest airports in Egypt.

Bus Stations Near Giza

  • Cairo Transport Station: This bus station is the main transport hub for Giza and is located in Cairo’s downtown area.
  • Turgoman Bus Station: This station is located 4 km from Cairo’s city center and offers regular buses to Giza.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are a convenient option for getting around Giza and Cairo, and the fares are reasonably priced.
  • Car Rental: Car rentals are widely available in Giza and offer an excellent option for visitors who want to explore the city and its surrounds at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Giza

Giza offers a range of transportation modes, making it easy to get around the city and its surroundings.

  • Taxis: Taxis are a convenient and affordable option to get around Giza; however, it is essential to agree on the fare before embarking on the journey.
  • Public Transport: Public transport in Giza includes buses, metro, and trains, making it easy to get around the city.
  • Walking: Walking is an excellent way to explore the city and soak up the atmosphere.
  • Biking: Biking is a fun and eco-friendly way to explore Giza’s landmarks and surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Giza: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Giza is the perfect base to explore other exciting destinations in Egypt. Here are some popular options:

  • Cairo: Cairo is Egypt’s bustling capital city and offers exciting landmarks, museums, and bazaars to explore.
  • Luxor: Located in Upper Egypt, Luxor is known for the Valley of the Kings and Queens, ancient temples, and tombs.
  • Aswan: Aswan is a tranquil city situated on the Nile River and offers beautiful landscapes, temples, and museums.

Conclusion

Giza is one of the world’s most famous travel destinations and is renowned for its impressive pyramids, temples, and museums. Whether you’re interested in history, culture or adventure, Giza has something for everyone. Plan your trip well, follow our travel guide, and experience the best of what this marvelous destination has to offer.
