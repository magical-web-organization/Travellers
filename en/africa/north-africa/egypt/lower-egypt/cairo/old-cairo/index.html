Introduction to Old Cairo

Old Cairo, also known as Coptic Cairo, is an important historical district located in the heart of Cairo, the capital of Egypt. It boasts a rich cultural heritage, fascinating museums, ancient churches, and mosques. Old Cairo is a must-visit destination for those interested in history, art, and architecture.

  4. Hanging Church
  5. Coptic Museum
  6. Ben Ezra Synagogue

Food and Drink Scene of Old Cairo

Egyptian cuisine is famous worldwide for its unique flavors and exotic spices. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Koshari - a vegetarian dish made of rice, lentils, pasta, and tomato sauce
  2. Ful Medames - fava beans cooked with herbs and spices
  3. Shawarma - a sandwich made of marinated meat, vegetables, and sauces in a pita bread
  4. Baklava - a sweet dessert made with layers of filo pastry and honey syrup

Local Beverages

  1. Egyptian Tea - black tea with mint and sugar
  2. Hibiscus Juice - made of dried hibiscus flowers, sugar, and water
  3. Arabic Coffee - strong and brewed with cardamom

Local Culture and Customs of Old Cairo

Egyptians are friendly and welcoming people who are proud of their rich cultural heritage. Islam is the official religion of Egypt, and Arabic is the main language. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  2. Remove your shoes before entering mosques or homes
  3. Greeting someone with a warm handshake and a smile is a sign of respect
  4. Bargaining is common in markets and bazaars

Best Time to Visit Old Cairo

The best time to visit Old Cairo is from October to April when the weather is cool and pleasant. Summers in Cairo can be hot and humid, making it uncomfortable to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Cairo

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions
  2. Try local food and drinks
  3. Learn some Arabic phrases

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing or provocative clothing
  2. Take photos of people without their permission
  3. Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Cairo

Old Cairo is generally safe for tourists. However, it’s always important to take precautions:

  1. Avoid isolated areas and dark alleys at night
  2. Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas
  3. Be careful when crossing roads

Health in Old Cairo

Check with your doctor before traveling to Egypt. Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations and medications. Drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent stomach issues.

Walking and cycling are the best ways to explore Old Cairo. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Old Cairo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Luxor - home to ancient temples and tombs
  2. Aswan - a peaceful city on the Nile River

Conclusion

Old Cairo is a must-visit destination for those interested in history, art, and architecture. With a rich cultural heritage, fascinating museums, and ancient churches and mosques, Old Cairo offers something for everyone. Plus, its location in the heart of Cairo makes it easily accessible and an ideal starting point for exploring other nearby destinations in North Africa.
