Introduction to Islamic Cairo

Located in the heart of Cairo, Islamic Cairo is a historic district that covers the area from Bab Al Futuh gate in the north to the Cairo Citadel in the south. This area is home to some of the most impressive and well-preserved Islamic architecture in the world.

Islamic Cairo is divided into several smaller neighborhoods, each with its unique charm, landmarks, and culture. These neighborhoods include Al-Azhar, Al-Hussein, Suq Al-Ghuri, Moez Street, and Darb Al-Ahmar.

List of Places to Visit in Islamic Cairo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Islamic Art: This museum houses one of the world’s most extensive collections of Islamic art, including ceramics, textiles, manuscripts, and more.
  • The Al-Azhar Mosque: This mosque is one of the oldest universities in the world and a center of Islamic scholarship and learning.
  • The Citadel of Salah El-Din: This imposing fortress sits atop a hill overlooking the city and offers stunning views of Cairo.
  • The Madrasa and Mausoleum of Sultan Hassan: This is one of the most significant examples of Mamluk architecture in the world, featuring intricate designs and detailed stonework.
  • The Al-Hakim Mosque: Built in the 11th century and named after the Fatimid caliph Al-Hakim, this mosque is a beautiful example of Islamic architecture.

Natural Attractions of Islamic Cairo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Al-Azhar Park: This green oasis in the middle of the city offers stunning views of Cairo and is perfect for a relaxing stroll or picnic.
  • Nile River Cruise: Take a leisurely cruise down the Nile and enjoy the breathtaking views of the city skyline from the water.
  • Sound & Light Show at the Giza Pyramids: This mesmerizing show takes place every evening and tells the story of the Pyramids of Giza with a stunning display of lights and sound.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Islamic Cairo

  • Moez Street: This pedestrian-only street is the perfect place for a leisurely stroll or bike ride, showcasing the rich history and architecture of Islamic Cairo.
  • Al-Azhar Park: This park also offers hiking and biking trails that wind their way through the lush greenery and offer breathtaking views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Islamic Cairo: Activities and Tours

  • Islamic Cairo Walking Tour: A guided tour of Islamic Cairo is the perfect way to learn about the history and culture of the area and to see some of the top landmarks up close.
  • Arabian Horse Ride: Take a horseback ride through the desert landscapes surrounding Islamic Cairo.
  • Food Tour: Taste the flavors of Islamic Cairo on a food tour, sampling traditional street food and local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Islamic Cairo

Food and Drink Scene of Islamic Cairo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshary: This classic Egyptian dish consists of rice, lentils, pasta, chickpeas, and crispy fried onions served with a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Ful Medames: A traditional Egyptian breakfast staple made with mashed fava beans and served with bread, cheese, and vegetables.
  • Fatta: A hearty stew made with lamb or beef, bread, and tomatoes, topped with garlic yogurt and crispy fried onions.

Local Beverages

  • Egyptian Tea: A strong black tea brewed with fresh mint or other herbs.
  • Hibiscus Juice: A refreshing drink made from dried hibiscus flowers and sweetened with sugar.

Where to Stay in Islamic Cairo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Arabian Nights Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace with views of Cairo.
  • Kempinski Nile Hotel: This luxury hotel combines traditional Arabic design with modern amenities, including a spa, rooftop pool, and several restaurants.
  • Beit Sabee Guesthouse: This quaint guesthouse is located in the heart of Islamic Cairo and offers beautifully decorated rooms and a peaceful garden courtyard.

What to Buy in Islamic Cairo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade lanterns and lamps: These colorful lanterns are a staple of Islamic Cairo and are perfect for adding a touch of Middle Eastern flair to your home decor.
  • Spices and herbs: Take home a taste of Egypt with local spices and herbs, including cumin, coriander, and saffron.
  • Handwoven carpets and rugs: These unique and beautiful carpets are handmade by local artisans using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Islamic Cairo

Islamic Cairo is a conservative area, and visitors should dress modestly and respect the local customs and beliefs. Women should cover their heads when visiting religious sites, and men should avoid wearing shorts or sleeveless shirts.

Best Time to Visit Islamic Cairo

The best time to visit Islamic Cairo is from December to February when temperatures are milder and more comfortable for sightseeing. Avoid visiting during Ramadan, as many shops and restaurants may be closed during the day.

Do’s and Don’ts of Islamic Cairo

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Bargain with vendors at the markets and street stalls.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water or eat food from street vendors unless you are sure it is safe.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Visit religious sites during prayer times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Islamic Cairo

Islamic Cairo is generally safe, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas. Avoid walking alone at night and be cautious when accepting invitations from strangers.

Health in Islamic Cairo

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations and consider getting additional vaccinations for hepatitis A and B and typhoid. Be cautious when eating at street vendors and drink bottled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Islamic Cairo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Islamic Cairo

Cairo International Airport is the main airport serving Cairo and is located approximately 12 miles from Islamic Cairo. It offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Islamic Cairo

The main bus station serving Cairo is the Cairo Gateway Bus Station, located approximately five miles north of Islamic Cairo. Buses from other cities in Egypt also arrive at this station.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Islamic Cairo by train or taxi from other cities in Egypt.

How to Get Around in Islamic Cairo

The best way to explore Islamic Cairo is on foot or by bike, as many of the streets and alleyways are too narrow for cars. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Islamic Cairo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Pyramids of Giza: One of the most famous landmarks in the world, the Pyramids of Giza are located just a short distance from Islamic Cairo.
  • The Egyptian Museum: Located in the nearby Tahrir Square, the Egyptian Museum is home to an extensive collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts and treasures.
  • Luxor: This ancient city is known for its impressive temples and tombs, including the Valley of the Kings.

Conclusion

Islamic Cairo offers a unique and fascinating window into the rich history and culture of Egypt. With its impressive architecture, beautiful parks, delicious food, and friendly people, it is a must-visit destination for travelers interested in exploring the Islamic world.
