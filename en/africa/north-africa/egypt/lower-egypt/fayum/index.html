If you’re looking for a culturally-rich destination that’s off the beaten path, Fayum, Egypt should definitely be on your travel radar. Located in Lower Egypt, this hidden gem offers visitors a glimpse into the country’s ancient past, as well as a chance to explore its stunning natural scenery.

Introduction to Fayum

Fayyum, also spelled Fayoum, is a city located about 130 km southwest of Cairo in the fertile Nile River delta region of Egypt. It was originally called Shedet in Ancient Egyptian times and later renamed Crocodilopolis by the Greeks. Today, it is known for its archaeological sites, nature reserves, and desert landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Fayum

Fayum is a conservative and deeply religious city, so it’s important for visitors to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some of the things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees
  • Avoid public displays of affection
  • Don’t drink or eat in public during Ramadan
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of people

Best Time to Visit Fayum

The best time to visit Fayum is during the winter months (November to February), when temperatures are milder and more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fayum

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Fayum:

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees
  • Bargain when shopping in markets and souks

Don’t:

  • Drink or eat in public during Ramadan
  • Carry large amounts of cash
  • Touch or remove artifacts or relics from historical sites

Safety and Health

Fayum is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions to ensure a comfortable and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Fayum

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Keep an eye on personal belongings in crowded areas
  • Use licensed taxis or private transportation services to get around

Health in Fayum

  • Bring mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water
  • Pack sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn
