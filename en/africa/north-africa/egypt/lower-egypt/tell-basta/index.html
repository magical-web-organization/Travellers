Introduction to Tell Basta

Nestled in Lower Egypt, Tell Basta is a historic city that offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural and historical heritage. A former capital of ancient Egypt, this city is a treasure trove of artifacts and ruins that date back to thousands of years.

From exploring museums and historical sites to indulging in outdoor adventures and trying out delicious local cuisine, Tell Basta has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through everything you need to know for a perfect trip to Tell Basta.

List of Places to Visit in Tell Basta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tell Basta Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, the Tell Basta Museum showcases the city’s rich heritage through a variety of exhibits and artifacts. The museum houses a vast collection of Pharaonic, Roman, and Coptic antiquities.
  • Bubastis Site: Located on the Nile Delta, the Bubastis Site is home to the ruins of the ancient Egyptian city of Per-Bastet or Bubastis. The archaeological site is home to several temples and other ancient structures that are sure to impress visitors.

Natural Attractions of Tell Basta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wadi Tumilat Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is a wonderful place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and get up close with nature. The reserve spans across 70 km and is home to a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Wadi El-Rayan: This beautiful natural reserve is a must-visit for those looking to indulge in outdoor adventures. The reserve is home to two man-made lakes, several waterfalls, and offers visitors the opportunity to indulge in activities like kayaking and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tell Basta

  • Wadi El-Rayan Hiking and Biking Trail: This beautiful trail offers visitors a chance to explore the scenic beauty of the Wadi El-Rayan reserve while indulging in activities like hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Tell Basta: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tour: Tell Basta is known for its delicious local cuisine, and a food tour is a great way to get a taste of the city’s culinary delights.
  • Balloon Ride: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the city for a unique view of the city’s ruins and the surrounding landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Tell Basta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kofta: This delicious dish is made of minced meat grilled on skewers and seasoned with various spices.
  • Mahshi: This stuffed vegetable dish is a staple in Egyptian cuisine and is usually filled with rice, herbs, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus Tea: A popular beverage in the region, hibiscus tea is made from the dried calyces of the hibiscus flower and is both refreshing and healthy.
  • Saqiya Juice: A refreshing drink made from sugar cane, lime, and other ingredients, Saqiya juice is a must-try for visitors to Tell Basta.

Where to Stay in Tell Basta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Basma Hotel: Located in the heart of the city, the Basma Hotel offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.
  • Club Azur Resort: This upscale resort is located just outside the city and offers visitors a luxurious stay with a variety of amenities.

What to Buy in Tell Basta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry: Tell Basta is known for its exquisite handmade jewelry, made by local artisans using traditional techniques.
  • Spices and Herbs: Visitors can pick up a variety of locally sourced spices and herbs from the city’s many markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Tell Basta

Tell Basta is a predominantly Muslim city, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Tell Basta

The best time to visit Tell Basta is during the winter months (November to February) when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tell Basta

Do:

  • Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain while shopping in the city’s markets.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tell Basta

Tell Basta is relatively safe for visitors, and incidents of violent crime are rare. However, visitors are advised to take basic precautions like avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Tell Basta

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or purified water to avoid water-borne illnesses. It is also recommended that visitors get vaccinated against diseases like Hepatitis A and B and Typhoid.

Getting to Tell Basta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tell Basta

The closest airport to Tell Basta is Cairo International Airport, which is approximately 87 km away.

Bus Stations Near Tell Basta

Visitors can take a bus from Cairo to Tell Basta, with several bus companies offering services to the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or a private car to get to Tell Basta from Cairo.

How to Get Around in Tell Basta

Visitors can get around in Tell Basta by using taxis or renting a car. Bikes and scooters are also available for rent in some parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Tell Basta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cairo: The capital city of Egypt is just a short drive from Tell Basta and is home to several iconic landmarks and tourist attractions.
  • Alexandria: This coastal city is a popular destination among tourists and is home to several museums and historical sites.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Tell Basta is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Lower Egypt region. We hope that this travel guide has helped you plan your perfect trip to Tell Basta.
