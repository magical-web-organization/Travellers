Introduction to Saqqara

Saqqara is an ancient Egyptian burial ground located in Lower Egypt. It is a vast, open-air museum and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Saqqara is home to the Step Pyramid, which was the first stone monument to be constructed in ancient Egypt. It is a must-visit destination for history buffs and anyone interested in ancient Egyptian culture and architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Saqqara

Here is a list of places to visit in Saqqara:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Step Pyramid of Djoser: It is the central feature of Saqqara and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The pyramid is surrounded by a complex of temples, shrines, and other tombs.
  • The Imhotep Museum: It is a small museum that displays artifacts from the Step Pyramid complex and the history of Saqqara.
  • The Tomb of Ti: It is one of the most beautiful tombs in Saqqara, with well-preserved wall paintings of daily life scenes and funerary rituals.
  • The Mastaba of Mereruka: It is the largest mastaba in Saqqara and a great example of the Old Kingdom’s elite tombs.

Natural Attractions of Saqqara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Saqqara Bird Watching Area: It is a protected natural area near the Step Pyramid complex, where birdwatchers can spot a variety of migratory birds.
  • The Saqqara Desert: It is a vast desert with stunning views and beautiful sunrises and sunsets, perfect for a camel ride or a desert safari.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saqqara

  • The Desert Route: It is a desert trail that starts from the Saqqara Bird Watching Area and goes through the Saqqara Desert. The route is perfect for hiking and mountain biking, and it offers stunning views of the desert.
  • The Step Pyramid Complex Circuit: It is a hiking and biking trail that goes around the Step Pyramid complex. The circuit offers a beautiful view of the pyramid and the surrounding temples and tombs.

Best Things to Do in Saqqara: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride over Saqqara: It is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that offers a stunning view of the Step Pyramid, the desert, and the Nile River.
  • Saqqara Sound and Light Show: It is a night-time spectacle that tells the story of Saqqara and its monuments through sound and light effects.

Food and Drink Scene of Saqqara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshary: It is a traditional Egyptian dish made of rice, lentils, pasta, and tomato sauce. It is popular among locals and tourists alike.
  • Ful Medames: It is a spicy Middle Eastern dish made of fava beans, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil. It is usually served with bread.
  • Kebab: It is a grilled meat dish, usually made of lamb or beef, and served with rice or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: It is a popular drink in Egypt, usually served with sugar and mint.
  • Coffee: It is a strong and flavorful drink, usually served with cardamom and sugar.

Where to Stay in Saqqara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Saqqara, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mena House Hotel: It is a luxurious hotel located near the Pyramids of Giza, with stunning views of the pyramids and the Nile River.
  • Djoser’s Shadow Guesthouse: It is a budget-friendly guesthouse located near the Step Pyramid complex, with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Pharaonic Village Hotel: It is a mid-range hotel located near the Saqqara Bird Watching Area, with beautiful views of the Nile River and the surrounding gardens.

What to Buy in Saqqara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Papyrus: It is a traditional Egyptian handicraft made of dried and pressed papyrus reeds, with beautiful hieroglyphic designs.
  • Spices: Egypt is famous for its aromatic spices, such as cumin, coriander, and cardamom.
  • Jewelry: Egyptian jewelry is known for its intricate designs and colorful gemstones, such as lapis lazuli and turquoise.

Local Culture and Customs of Saqqara

Egypt is a Muslim country, and it is important to respect the local customs and traditions. Women are advised to dress modestly, covering their legs, arms, and hair. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Saqqara

The best time to visit Saqqara is from October to April when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer months (May to September) can be extremely hot, with temperatures rising up to 40°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saqqara

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Hire a local guide to learn more about the history and culture of Saqqara.
  • Bargain at local markets and shops, but be respectful and polite.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Insult Islam or the local customs and traditions.
  • Drink tap water, but opt for bottled water instead.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saqqara

Egypt is generally a safe country, but travelers are advised to take standard precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables. It is also recommended to hire a licensed and reputable taxi or transportation service.

Health in Saqqara

Travelers should be up to date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), and hepatitis A and B. They should also consider getting vaccinated against typhoid and rabies, especially if they plan to hike or explore the local wildlife.

Getting to Saqqara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saqqara is located about 30 km from Cairo, and it is easily accessible by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Saqqara

  • Cairo International Airport: It is the closest international airport to Saqqara, located about 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near Saqqara

  • Giza Bus Station: It is the nearest bus station to Saqqara, located about 25 km away.
  • Cairo Bus Station: It is a major bus station in Cairo, located about 30 km away.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Cairo and can be hired to travel to Saqqara.
  • Tour Bus: Many tour operators offer day tours to Saqqara from Cairo and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Saqqara

The best way to get around Saqqara is by hiring a local guide or hiring a taxi. Walking and biking are also great options for exploring the local sites and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Saqqara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Saqqara:

  • Cairo: It is the capital city of Egypt and home to some of the most famous historical and cultural monuments in the world, such as the Pyramids of Giza and the Egyptian Museum.
  • Luxor: It is a city located about 700 km south of Saqqara and home to some of the most impressive ancient Egyptian temples, such as Karnak and Luxor Temple.
  • Aswan: It is a city located about 900 km south of Saqqara and a popular gateway to explore the Nubian culture and the temples of Abu Simbel.

Conclusion

Saqqara is a fascinating destination that offers a glimpse into the ancient Egyptian culture and history. From the stunning Step Pyramid complex to the beautiful desert views, Saqqara has something for everyone. Enjoy the local food, shop for souvenirs, and explore the nearby cities and attractions.
