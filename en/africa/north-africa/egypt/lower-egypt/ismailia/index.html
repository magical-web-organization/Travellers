Ismailia, situated in Lower Egypt, on the west bank of the Suez Canal, is a charming and dynamic city with an intriguing history and diverse culture. With its lush greenery, scenic landscapes, and numerous historical landmarks, Ismailia offers an unforgettable travel experience. Whether you want to explore its natural attractions or delve into its fascinating past, Ismailia has something for every type of traveler. In this complete guide, we will help you discover the top attractions, activities, local delicacies, where to stay, transportation options, and much more.

Introduction to Ismailia

Ismailia was established in 1863 by the Ismail Pasha, the khedive of Egypt, as a strategic city for monitoring the Suez Canal. The city was named after him and quickly became a bustling center for commercial activity. Today, Ismailia is renowned for its well-maintained gardens, picturesque views of Lake Timsah, its numerous museums, and fascinating historical landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Ismailia

Egyptian cuisine is renowned for its flavors and unique combinations. Ismailia is no exception, and its food culture is as vibrant as its inhabitants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari
  • Feteer meshaltet (layered pastry filled with cheese or other ingredients)
  • Shawarma
  • Molokhia (stewed greens with meat)

Local Beverages

  • Sahlab (a warm milk-based drink with nuts and cinnamon)
  • Hibiscus Tea
  • Mango juice
  • Mint tea

Local Culture and Customs of Ismailia

Egyptian culture is rich in traditions, and Ismailia is no exception. Here are a few customs to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Visitors are expected to dress modestly, covering their shoulders and legs in public places
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants and for services
  • Alcohol is not widely available in Ismailia, and it is recommended to drink in moderation
  • Islam is the predominant religion in Egypt, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and religious practices

Best Time to Visit Ismailia

The best time to visit Ismailia is from October to April when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. Summer months can be strenuous, with temperatures above 40 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ismailia

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques or other religious sites
  • Immerse yourself in the city’s culture and tradition
  • Try local food and drinks
  • Bargain when shopping in local markets

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Islam or local customs
  • Drink excessively in public places
  • Take photos of people without their consent, especially women

Safety and Health

Safety in Ismailia

Ismailia is a safe city for visitors, and the people are friendly and welcoming. However, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Ismailia

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid tap water, as it may cause stomach problems. It is also recommended to take precautions against the sun, such as wearing sunscreen and drinking plenty of water.

Taxis, rental cars or chartered boats are the best options for getting around Ismailia. Bikes can also be rented from several locations, making it easy to explore the city at your pace.
