Introduction to El Alamein

El Alamein, located in Lower Egypt in North Africa, is a coastal town that is famous for its significance during World War II. The town sits on the Mediterranean Sea, and its sandy stretches of beaches that contrast against the off-blue crystal-clear waters make it a perfect spot for local and international tourist visits. El Alamein is ideal for people looking for an enjoyable trip filled with adventure, history, and relaxation. This comprehensive guide will help you discover the area’s best cultural, natural, and historical attractions, along with exciting outdoor adventures, hiking, and biking, best things to do and places to visit, the food, the local culture and customs, and transportation options amongst much more.

List of Places to Visit in El Alamein

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. El Alamein War Museum: Displaying a wide range of armaments, military maps, rare photographs, and original uniforms, the museum pays tribute to the fallen soldiers during the Second World War.

  2. Commonwealth War Cemetery: Commemorates over 7,000 Commonwealth and Allied soldiers who died during the famed Battle of El Alamein, one of the significant turning points in the war.

  3. German War Cemetery: A serene, peaceful plot that holds more than 4,000 German soldiers killed in action.

Natural Attractions of El Alamein: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The El Alamein Cove Camping Site: A top destination for camping and basking in the sun.

  2. Agamy Beach: Known for its white sands and crystal-clear waters, ideal for watersports like surfing, windsurfing, sailing, and swimming.

  3. Ras El Hekma Bay: Found close to El Alamein and located around the Ras El Hekma Reserve, is a fantastic spot to swim, relax, and take pictures of the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Alamein

  1. Hiking through Ras El Hekma; the route includes beautiful scenic views, hills, and valleys.

  2. Biking through the El Alamein War Museum: Enjoy a trip on two wheels via beautiful paths lined with trees.

Best Things to Do in El Alamein: Activities and Tours

  1. Wakeboarding: Agamy Beach provides a perfect opportunity to enjoy wakeboarding in El Alamein.

  2. Scuba Diving: Explore the underwater world and its vast marine life with the experienced guides.

  3. Tour the desert plain by Camel: Explore and experience the beautiful Egypt desert terrain and its Bedouin lifestyle.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Alamein

Day Activities
1 Visit the El Alamein War Museum and enjoy a stroll along the serenity of Agamy Beach.
2 Swim with dolphins in the Mediterranean sea, and then visit the Mediterranean town of Marsa Matrouh.
3 Explore the beautiful Ras El Hekma Reserve and dine in the local restaurants afterward.
4 Take a motorcycle tour of the nearby cities and towns, followed by a sunset cruise on the crystal waters.
5 Ride humpback camels through the desert with experienced guides.
6 Explore the multicultural city of Alexandria and its ancient library.
7 Visit the Commonwealth War Cemetery and remember the brave soldiers who died fighting for their future.

Food and Drink Scene in El Alamein

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fattah: Made with bread soaked in meat or chicken broth, then layered with rice, meat, and chickpeas, then topped with roasted nuts and fresh herbs.

  2. Kofta: A grilled meat mixture consisting of ground meat, herbs, and spices that are then grilled on skewers.

  3. Ful: Made by boiling fava beans with olive oil and lemons, and then garnished with chopped tomatoes, onions, and a sprinkle of parsley.

Local Beverages

  1. Sahlab: Served hot and made from a mixture of milk and sugar with a silk-like texture and mild flavour.

  2. Crème brulee; A sweet Egyptian dessert that is rich in texture and made from cream, sugar, and caramel.

Where to Stay in El Alamein: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Porto Marina Resort and Spa: A luxurious hotel close to the beach, great for family vacations.

  2. Marina Lodge: Located in a beautiful marina with a stylish interior, beautiful gardens, and outdoor pool.

  3. Mercure El Alamein: Has a beautiful outdoor pool and stunning views of the sea that make it perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in El Alamein: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Papyrus painted bookmarks and paintings that represent Egypt’s rich culture and history.

  2. Authentic Bedouin-style embroidered clothes and sandals.

  3. Traditional handmade pottery bowls and dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of El Alamein

The local culture and customs revolve around their love for history, art, and food. Egyptians in this region are hospitable and treat visitors with great respect. The language used in El Alamein is Arabic, but most people speak basic English.

Best Time to Visit El Alamein

The best time to visit El Alamein is between September and November and from March to May. During these times, the temperatures are cooler, and the area sees less humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Alamein

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and culture by wearing modest clothing when visiting religious sites.

  2. Visit the area’s attractions during the early morning or late in the afternoon when the sun is cooler.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t engage in public displays of affection.

  2. Don’t take photographs of military sites without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Alamein

The town has good safety records, and the locals are welcoming to tourists visiting their area. However, some pickpockets and petty theft are not uncommon.

Health in El Alamein

Before visiting El Alamein, make sure you have all the necessary vaccinations up to date. Drink only bottled water and ensure that you pack enough sunscreen and mosquito repellent suitable for the climate.

Getting to El Alamein: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Alamein

The nearest airport is Matrouh International Airport, which is 50 kilometers (31 miles) from El Alamein.

Bus Stations Near El Alamein

There is a regular bus schedule with several bus stations between Cairo and El Alamein.

Other Options

There is a train station located in Sidi Gabir, Alexandria, which is approximately 105 kilometers (65 miles) from El Alamein.

How to Get Around in El Alamein

The best way to get around in El Alamein is by taxi, car rentals or motorbike rentals.

Where to Go Next from El Alamein: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Alexandria: Alexandria is about 105 kilometers (65 miles) away from El Alamein and is a popular destination for its ancient history.

  2. Cairo: Cairo is approximately 187 kilometers (116 miles) from El Alamein, where you can experience the country’s vast culture, food, and lifestyle.

Conclusion

El Alamein provides the perfect balance of history, culture, and natural beauty, creating a perfect backdrop for your travel experience. Whether you are a history buff or looking for an opportunity for outdoor adventures, El Alamein has everything to offer. So pack your bags, grab your passport, and take a trip to El Alamein to experience the famous Mediterranean coast and its palpable atmosphere.
