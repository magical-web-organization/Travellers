Introduction to Beni Suef

Beni Suef is a city in Middle Egypt, located on the west bank of the Nile river. It is known for its rich history, landmarks, and natural beauty. The city is surrounded by date palms and agricultural fields, and boasts a scenic landscape. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information for an enjoyable trip to Beni Suef.

List of Places to Visit in Beni Suef

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beni Suef Museum
  • Mosque of Amr ibn al-As
  • Al-Akhdar Mosque
  • Al-Fath Mosque
  • Gebel el-Teir

Natural Attractions of Beni Suef: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beni Suef Waterfalls
  • Wadi Rayan Nature Reserve
  • Valley of the Whales
  • Taha Hussein Museum and Cultural Center

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beni Suef

  • Wadi Rayan Protectorate’s Hiking Trails - this route includes over 200 km of trails for hiking and biking through the valley of Wadi Rayan.
  • Gebel El-Teir hiking trail - starting from Beni Suef, this trail offers a 4-5 hour hike to the top of the west hill.
  • Valley of the Whales - taking a tour through the valley allows you to hike in the desert, and see many fossils along the path.

Best Things to Do in Beni Suef: Activities and Tours

  • Camel riding through the nearby desert
  • Felucca ride along Nile River
  • Visiting the ruins of ancient temples of Egypt
  • Shopping in local markets and souqs

Food and Drink Scene of Beni Suef

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari - a mix of macaroni, lentils, rice, and chickpeas, topped with tomato sauce and fried onions
  • Fatta - a traditional Egyptian dish made with fried bread cubes, rice or macaroni, meat, garlic, and spices
  • Molokhiya - a green leafy vegetable that is stewed and served with rice or bread

Local Beverages

  • Tea with mint or hibiscus
  • Sugarcane juice, fresh coconut milk

Local Culture and Customs of Beni Suef

  • Religion - the majority of the population is Muslim, and it is essential to respect their beliefs and customs.
  • Clothing - it is recommended to dress conservatively, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Social customs - shaking hands, even if you are a woman, is a typical greeting in Beni Suef.

Best Time to Visit Beni Suef

The best time to visit Beni Suef is between November and February when the weather is cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beni Suef

Do:

  • Properly dispose of your trash
  • Respect the local customs and beliefs
  • Take plenty of water

Don’t:

  • Take photos of others without their permission
  • Travel alone at night
  • Eat uncooked food or drink untreated water

Safety and Health

Safety in Beni Suef

Like many places in Egypt, Beni Suef is a safe destination for travel. However, avoid walking alone at night and be mindful of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Beni Suef

It’s important to drink only bottled water, avoid street food, and wear sunscreen to protect yourself from dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Beni Suef: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beni Suef

Cairo International Airport is the closest airport to Beni Suef, and it is about 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Beni Suef

The main bus station in Beni Suef is located near the city center, making it easily accessible to travelers.

Other Options

You can reach Beni Suef by train, which departs from Cairo’s Ramses Station daily. Taxis and private transfers are also available at an additional cost.

How to Get Around in Beni Suef

To get around Beni Suef, taxis and minibusses are readily available at affordable prices.

Where to Go Next from Beni Suef: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fayoum Oasis - Fayoum, southwest of Cairo, is an enchanting region with natural beauty, historic sites.
  • Minya city - Minya, located north of Beni Suef, is a place that still holds an aura of the bygone era.

Conclusion

Beni Suef is a city that combines history, culture, stunning natural attractions, and hospitality.
