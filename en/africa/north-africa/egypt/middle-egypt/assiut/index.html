Assiut Travel Guide - Discover the Best of Middle Egypt

Welcome to Assiut, a fascinating city located in the heart of Middle Egypt that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

When you visit Assiut, you will discover a city with a rich cultural heritage that dates back to ancient times, where you can explore numerous historical sites, museums, and monuments that showcase Egypt’s ancient civilization.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Assiut, we will take you through some of the top places to visit, exciting activities to do, local food and drinks to try, and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Assiut

Here are the top places to add to your itinerary when visiting Assiut:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al-Munira Mosque: A beautifully crafted 19th-century mosque that showcases Islamic architectural design.
  • Assiut National Museum: A museum that displays a rich collection of historical artifacts and ancient relics from the region.
  • Deir El-Muharraq Monastery: A fifth-century Coptic Orthodox monastery that is one of the oldest Christian monasteries in Egypt.
  • Pharaonic Village: A themed park that showcases the lifestyle and culture of ancient Egyptians, complete with a recreated village, a bazaar, and a temple.

Natural Attractions of Assiut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Assiut Barrage: A major dam that provides irrigation to the region and offers stunning views of the Nile.
  • Wadi Al- Assiut: A beautiful valley with a river running through the middle, providing ample opportunities for hiking and scenery watching.
  • Lakes of Al-Azhar Park: A vast man-made park that houses a vast lake, perfect for a relaxing boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Assiut

Assiut is perfect for hikers and bikers who want to explore the St Katherine mountain range. The most popular hiking trails in Assiut include:

  • Hiking in Wadi Al-Assiut: This valley has no hills, but it is suitable for walking, hiking through, and exploring its natural beauty.
  • El-Qala’a Mountain: This mountain range is ideal for experienced hikers or local guides to enjoy the dramatic scenery and challenging terrain.
  • Cycling Around the Nile: The Nile River runs through Assiut, making it easy to rent or bring your bike to explore alongside the banks of the Nile.

Best Things to Do in Assiut: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, there are plenty of other exciting activities and tours in Assiut for visitors to enjoy like:

  • River Boat Ride: Experience the best of Nile’s beauty on a boat ride that takes you through the stunning landscapes and historical sites.
  • Street Food Tour: Visit local street vendors and taste some unique Egyptian cuisine with the help of a knowledgeable food-guide.
  • Visit a Khayamiya: A local market that sells beautifully designed fabrics that are unique to the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Assiut

Here is a sample itinerary for a weeklong trip to Assiut:

  • Day 1: Visit Al-Munira Mosque and walk around the local market.
  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the famous Assiut National Museum.
  • Day 3: Explore Deir El-Muharraq Monastery and witness the exquisite Christian frescoes inside.
  • Day 4: Go hiking through Wadi Al-Assiut Valley
  • Day 5: Discover the beauty of El-Qala’a Mountain
  • Day 6: Experience the beauty of the Nile River on a riverboat ride.
  • Day 7: Go street shopping and visit local restaurants for some authentic Egyptian culinary delights.

Food and Drink Scene of Assiut

When it comes to food, Assiut has its culinary treats to share:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari: a tasty national Egyptian dish consists of rice, macaroni, lentils and topped with fried onions and tomato sauce.
  • Molokhiya: a delicious soup made from fresh leaves of the Molokhia vegetables mixed with minced meat and served with rice or bread.
  • Fatta: a layered dish of bread, rice, meat, and garlic that’s served with a flavorful tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus Tea: a chilled pink tea made from Hibiscus leaves commonly known as Karkade, is a popular beverage in Egypt.
  • Sahlab: a hot sweet Egyptian drink made from the powdered roots of orchid plants that taste delightful and unique.
  • Qamar Al Din Juice: a refreshing juice made from dried apricots mixed with water and sugar syrup.

Where to Stay in Assiut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Assiut offers accommodation options for all budgets. Visitors can choose from a wide range of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to every need and preference.

Some of the recommended places to stay in Assiut include:

  • Assiut Hotels: Sofitel Assiut Barrage, Assiut Hotel, and Hilton Assiut Resort.
  • Assiut Hostels: Lotus Hotel & Restaurant and Jasmine Guest House.
  • Assiut Guesthouses: Assiut Guest House and Al-Masreya Guest House.

What to Buy in Assiut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Assiut offers an array of authentic souvenirs and local products that are perfect as gifts to take back home. Some of the unique items you will find in Assiut include:

  • Baskets and Trays: A wide variety of baskets and trays that are woven by local artisans from palm leaves and other natural materials.
  • Abayas: A trendy traditional Islamic apparel for women that is stylish, comfortable, and affordable.
  • Khayamiya: A unique type of fabric that is embroidered with sequins and bright geometric patterns that locals use for tent and countryside ornaments.

Local Culture and Customs of Assiut

Assiut’s culture is deeply rooted in historical and religious traditions that have been passed down through generations. Despite being modernized, the city still adheres to some old customs, including wearing revealing clothes, especially in religious sites and areas.

The people of Assiut are known for being friendly, hospitable, and open-minded, welcoming visitors from all parts of the world.

Best Time to Visit Assiut

The best time to visit Assiut depends on individual preferences and interests. The summer season is usually hot and humid, while winters are cooler and more comfortable, making it the perfect time to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Assiut

Do:

  • Always ask permission before taking pictures of people or landmarks.
  • Dress respectfully when visiting religious sites and institutions.
  • Try out local cuisine and immerse yourself in the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Display overt displays of affection in public areas.
  • Speak loudly or behave disrespectfully within religious or spiritual premises.

Safety and Health

Safety in Assiut

Assiut is considered a safe city for visitors. However, it’s always best to exercise caution and practice basic safety measures such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep an eye on your valuables and avoid displaying them in public places.
  • Stick to well-lit and popular areas around the city.

Health in Assiut

Visitors should be aware of sun exposure and stay hydrated throughout the day. It’s also important to be mindful of food and drink quality, avoid uncooked foods or water that hasn’t been bottled or purified.

Getting to Assiut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Assiut is easily accessible by air, road, and water. Here are some transportation options available to visitors:

Airports Near Assiut

Assiut International Airport connects Assiut to major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Assiut

The city has several bus stations that connect it to other parts of Egypt, including Cairo, Alexandria, and Luxor.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or use taxis to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Assiut

Getting around Assiut is relatively easy and affordable. Taxis and public transportation are readily available, and you can also rent bikes for touring around the city.

Where to Go Next from Assiut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Assiut is an excellent starting point for exploring other attractions in Southern Egypt, including:

  • Luxor: A city that hosts a vast collection of splendid temples, tombs, and ruins, making it a perfect stop for history buffs.
  • Aswan: A city located in Southern Egypt, which is a gateway to the UNESCO Heritage Sites of Abu Simbel and Philae Temple.
  • Mersa Matruh: This is the ultimate destination for beach lovers that’s famous for its white sandy beaches and deep crystal waters.

Conclusion

Assiut is a fascinating city that combines history, culture, and natural beauty into one place. From culturally important attractions to outdoor adventures, Assiut offers an array of exciting experiences that will make your visit to Middle Egypt unforgettable. We hope this travel guide helps you plan an incredible visit to Assiut!
