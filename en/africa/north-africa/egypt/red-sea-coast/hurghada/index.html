Introduction to Hurghada

Located along the Red Sea Coast of Egypt, Hurghada is the ultimate vacation spot. Founded in the early 20th century, it has transformed from a small fishing village to a large touristic hub for travelers worldwide. The city’s proximity to the Red Sea allows for plenty of exploration and adventure, with activities ranging from snorkeling, diving, and windsurfing, to hiking and biking around the rugged landscape. In addition, Hurghada is home to various cultural, historical, and natural landmarks. Whether you’re looking for a romantic getaway or a family vacation, Hurghada caters to all tastes and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Hurghada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Old Town of El Dahar: Walk through the ancient streets of El Dahar and experience the traditional culture of Hurghada. The old town offers a glimpse into Hurghada’s past and showcases traditional markets, shops, and cafes.

2. Hurghada Museum: The museum is home to several exhibitions that highlight the rich history of Hurghada. Visitors can learn about the city’s ancient civilization and see artifacts from various dynasties.

3. Abdulmoneim Riad Mosque: Built-in the early 20th century, this mosque has been beautifully preserved and maintained. The highlight of the mosque is the massive chandelier in the prayer hall, which weighs several thousand kilograms.

Natural Attractions of Hurghada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Giftun Island: Located off the coast of Hurghada, Giftun Island offers the perfect getaway for beach lovers. Take part in activities such as sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters.

2. Desert Safari: Embark on a thrilling desert safari that includes sandboarding, quad biking, and camel riding. Enjoy the breathtaking views of the vast desert and the red mountains.

3. Makadi Water World: This water park offers fun-filled activities for all ages, from swimming to water slides and more. Enjoy a day full of splashes and laughter.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hurghada

Mount Sinai Trek:

Take a trip to the Mount Sinai area, which is home to several scenic hikes. The popular Mount Sinai trek offers stunning views of the Sinai mountains and the stunning sunrise.

Fat Bike Ride:

For an unforgettable experience, take part in a Fat Bike ride. You’ll get to explore the desert, mountains, and beaches with ease on this comfortable and stable bike that is great for off-road use.

Best Things to Do in Hurghada: Activities and Tours

1. Scuba Diving: Hurghada is known for some of the best underwater experiences around the world. There are diving classes available for all levels, and numerous sites to choose from.

2. Snorkeling: Snorkelers too can enjoy the beauty of the Red Sea with the clear waters and abundance of marine life in Hurghada. Take a snorkeling tour or rent equipment and explore the waters on your own.

3. Glass-Bottom Boat Tour: Take a glass-bottom boat tour and witness the colorful underwater world without getting wet. The vessel travels across coral reefs, allowing passengers to observe different marine creatures.

Food and Drink Scene of Hurghada

Must-Try Dishes

1. Koshari: This traditional Egyptian dish is a hearty meal made with pasta, rice, lentils, chickpeas, and a spicy tomato sauce. It is typically served with crispy fried onions on top.

2. Fiteer: Fiteer is a layered pastry that is typically filled with savory or sweet ingredients. The crust is crispy and flaky, making it a popular choice for breakfast or desserts.

3. Fatteh: This dish is made with pita bread, yogurt, chickpeas, tahini, and spices. It is served hot and is a popular breakfast item.

Local Beverages

1. Hibiscus Juice: Hibiscus tea is a refreshing and sweet drink made from hibiscus flowers that are commonly found in Egypt.

2. Karkadeh: Karkadeh is another popular Egyptian drink made from dried hibiscus flowers. The tea is sweet and tangy and is often served chilled.

3. Sahlab: This hot beverage is made from a combination of milk, cornstarch, and various spices, including cinnamon and cardamom. It is typically served during the winter months.

Where to Stay in Hurghada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hurghada is home to several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Options range from luxury resorts, comfortable hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Some of the top-rated accommodations include the Steigenberger Hotel, the Hilton Hurghada Long Beach Resort, and the Grand Plaza Hotel.

What to Buy in Hurghada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Perfume Oils: Hurghada is known for a wide variety of perfume oils that are made with exotic scents of different flowers, spices, and woods. Pick up a bottle or two as a unique souvenir for yourself or a loved one.

2. Spices: Taking home a blend of the traditional flavors used in Egyptian cuisine is a great idea. The spice market in Hurghada is an excellent place to buy local spices and seasonings-rich with aroma and flavor.

3. Hookahs (Shisha): Hookahs or shishas are popular in Egypt and make a great souvenir for those who love smoking. You can buy a variety of shishas, flavors, and accessories at local markets and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Hurghada

Hurghada is a traditional Muslim city, and visitors are requested to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in public and refraining from public displays of affection. Islam is the primary religion in Hurghada, and visitors can visit the various mosques present in the city.

Best Time to Visit Hurghada

The best time to visit Hurghada is between March and April or October and November, which are the spring and autumn seasons. These months offer the best climate for outdoor excursions, and there’s less crowd if you prefer a quieter vacation. However, due to the city’s popularity, it’s a destination that can be enjoyed year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hurghada

Do:

  • Dress modestly in public places
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bargain when shopping
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Carry a water bottle to stay hydrated

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Drink tap water
  • Take photos of strangers without their permission
  • Wear revealing clothing at public beaches, specifically for women

Safety and Health

Safety in Hurghada

Hurghada is a safe destination to travel, with robust security measures in place to ensure the safety of visitors. Nevertheless, it is always better to use common sense and exercise caution. Be prepared for the cultural differences and be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Hurghada

Hurghada has adequate healthcare facilities, including hospitals and pharmacies, but it’s important to carry travel health insurance. Also, use sunscreen to avoid overexposure to the sun and stay hydrated.

Getting to Hurghada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hurghada

Hurghada International Airport is served by major airlines, including EgyptAir, Turkish Airlines, and Ryanair, and connects to several major cities worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Hurghada

Bus services are available from the city to other major cities in Egypt.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are also available from the airport.

How to Get Around in Hurghada

Taxis and buses are widely available in Hurghada. The city also has a well-developed road network for those who prefer to rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Hurghada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Luxor

The ancient city of Luxor is a short drive from Hurghada, and home to some of Egypt’s most famous landmarks, including the Valley of the Kings, Karnak Temple, and Luxor Temple.

Cairo

The capital of Egypt is an excellent option for a day trip or an extended stay. Cairo has a rich cultural and historical background, including the Pyramids of Giza, the Egyptian Museum, and the Khan El Khalili Bazaar.

Conclusion

Hurghada has something for everyone and provides a unique blend of adventure, culture, and relaxation. Spend your vacation immersed in the beauty of the Red Sea, try local foods and drinks, venture out to historical landmarks or nearby destinations, but most importantly, have an unforgettable experience.
