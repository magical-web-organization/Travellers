Introduction to Soma Bay

Located on the Red Sea Coast of Egypt, Soma Bay is a beautiful holiday resort known for its pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, and world-class amenities. This idyllic destination offers a range of activities for visitors, from water sports to cultural attractions.

In this guide, we will take a closer look at Soma Bay and everything it has to offer. We’ll provide you with a list of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat. We’ll also give you important information about local culture, customs, and safety.

List of Places to Visit in Soma Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Soma Bay can explore a wide range of culturally important sites, including museums and historical landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in the area:

  • Wadi El Gemal National Park: This beautiful protected area is home to a variety of wildlife and natural wonders, including sand dunes, mountains, and coastal lagoons.
  • The Roman Mons Claudianus: One of the best-preserved Roman quarries in the world, this site is located approximately 40km from Soma Bay and is definitely worth a visit.
  • The Temple of Dendera: Considered one of the best-preserved temples in Egypt, the Temple of Dendera is a must-visit site for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Soma Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Soma Bay is also home to a diverse range of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top things to see and do in the area:

  • Coral Reef Diving: Soma Bay is known for having some of the most beautiful coral reefs in the Red Sea where you can also snorkel and see the amazing marine life.
  • Kite Surfing / Wind Surfing: Soma Bay is home to some of the best kiteboarding and windsurfing spots in the world due to its unique weather conditions and nearby spots like Hamata.
  • Sand Dune Bashing: Adventure seekers won’t want to miss the chance to explore the nearby desert with a thrilling sand dune bashing tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soma Bay

Outdoor enthusiasts will also want to explore the area’s hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • The Mountain Bike Trail: This scenic route takes you through the mountains and valleys surrounding Soma Bay, providing breathtaking views of the Red Sea.
  • The Coastal Hiking Trail: Take a stroll along 15km coastal walking trail from Soma Bay to El Gouna, where you will be greeted with stunning views of the Red Sea.

Best Things to Do in Soma Bay: Activities and Tours

Soma Bay offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit every interest. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Guided Snorkeling Tour: Explore the stunning coral reefs and sea life under the guidance of a knowledgeable guide.
  • Sunset Cruise: Enjoy a romantic and relaxing sunset boat tour where you can admire the beautiful sunset views of the Red Sea.
  • Luxury Spa Day: Enjoy a day of pampering at one of the resort’s luxurious spas, which offer a range of treatments and services.

Food and Drink Scene of Soma Bay

No travel experience is complete without a taste of the local cuisine. Soma Bay offers an array of deliciously unique dishes and beverages that any food lover should try.

Must-Try Dishes

Egypt’s rich culinary traditions offer world-famous delicacies with an unparalleled savory taste, and we recommend tasting these dishes while visiting Soma Bay:

  • Koshari: A mix of pasta, lentils, rice, and spicy tomato sauce
  • Ta’meya: Deep-fried chickpea croquettes served with bread and vegetables.
  • Mahshi: Vegetables like bell peppers, cabbage, eggplant, or grape leaves stuffed with herbed rice, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

Egypt offers a wealth of refreshing local beverages. Here are some that you must try while in Soma Bay:

  • Hibiscus Tea: This popular Egyptian beverage is made from dried hibiscus flowers and offers a refreshing and slightly tangy flavor.
  • Fresh Juice: Enjoy the tasty fresh-made orange juice, lemonades, or sugarcane juices that are beloved by locals.
  • Egyptian Beer and Wine: Soma Bay has bars, clubs, and restaurants serving the local beer and wine, including Sakara, Stella, and Omar Khayyam wines.

Where to Stay in Soma Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Soma Bay offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top places to stay in the area:

  • Kempinski Soma Bay: A luxurious five-star resort with stunning views of the Red Sea.
  • Sheraton Soma Bay: A top-rated four-star hotel offering comfortable and affordable rooms.
  • The Breakers Diving & Surfing Lodge: Ideal for adventurers who want an affordable and laid-back stay close to the water.

What to Buy in Soma Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No vacation is complete without some souvenirs to take back home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Soma Bay:

  • Egyptian cotton clothing: Known for its high quality, Egyptian cotton clothing is a popular souvenir among visitors.
  • Local Spices & Tea: Soma Bay is known for its deliciously fragrant spices and tea blends, which make the perfect gifts for foodies.
  • Handicrafts: Local handicrafts and pottery are also popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Soma Bay

Egyptian culture is rich in history and customs, and visitors to Soma Bay should be mindful of local norms and behaviors. Some key points to keep in mind include:

  • Dress Code: While Soma Bay is a resort and dress code is not as strict as in other parts of Egypt, it’s still important to dress modestly in public areas.
  • Ramadan: During the month of Ramadan, locals observe a month of fasting and it is important to be respectful of their customs and traditions.
  • Greeting Etiquette: It is customary to greet locals with “Assalamu Alaikum” (peace be upon you) and respond “Wa Alaikum Assalam” (and peace be upon you).

Best Time to Visit Soma Bay

The best time to visit Soma Bay is between October and March, when temperatures are mild and the weather is comfortable for outdoor activities. Keep in mind that the peak season runs from December to January and prices may be higher during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soma Bay

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while in Soma Bay:

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drinks: Egyptian cuisine is delicious and should be taken advantage of during your trip.
  • Do respect the local culture: Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially during Ramadan.
  • Do enjoy the natural beauty of the area: Soma Bay has gorgeous natural features that should be explored and appreciated.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the locals: Always be respectful to locals and their customs, including dress codes and public displays of affection.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately: While dress codes are not as strict as in other parts of Egypt, it is still important to dress appropriately in public areas.

Safety and Health

Soma Bay is a generally safe destination, and visitors should take the usual precautions while traveling.

Safety in Soma Bay

  • Stay vigilant: As with any tourist destination, there is always a risk of theft, so it is important to keep valuables secure and be aware of surroundings.
  • Respect local customs: Be respectful of local customs and avoid taking photos of locals without their permission.

Health in Soma Bay

  • Sun protection: With its warm weather and sunny climate, it is important to wear sunscreen and protective clothing.
  • Stay hydrated: Be sure to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Medical services: Soma Bay has medical facilities in case of emergency.

Getting to Soma Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Soma Bay

  • Hurghada International Airport: Located approximately 45 minutes from Soma Bay, Hurghada International Airport is the closest international airport to the area.
  • Marsa Alam International Airport: The second closest international airport is Marsa Alam International Airport, which is approximately 30 miles south of Soma Bay.

Bus Stations Near Soma Bay

  • Hurghada Bus Station: Hurghada is the nearest bus station, approximately 45 minutes from Soma Bay.
  • Quseir Bus Station: Quseir Bus Station is also nearby, approximately 30 minutes from Soma Bay.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available and can be convenient for local travel or transportation to and from the airport.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can also rent a car for more flexibility and independence during their trip.

How to Get Around in Soma Bay

Soma Bay is a relatively small and safe destination that can be easily navigated by foot, bike, or taxi. Some resorts and hotels also offer shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Soma Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Soma Bay is ideally located for exploring other nearby destinations, including:

  • Luxor: Home to some of the most iconic landmarks in the world, Luxor is a must-visit destination for history buffs.
  • Aswan: Known for its scenic beauty and rich cultural history, Aswan is another popular destination near Soma Bay.

Conclusion

Soma Bay is a beautiful holiday destination that offers a diverse range of activities and attractions. This comprehensive guide should help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in the area. Whether you’re interested in history, adventure, or relaxation, Soma Bay has something to offer everyone.
