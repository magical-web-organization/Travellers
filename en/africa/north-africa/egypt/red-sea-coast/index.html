Introduction to Red Sea Coast

The Red Sea Coast is a breathtaking destination in Egypt, North Africa, renowned for its abundant natural beauty, stunning coastline, and crystal-clear waters. This area is the perfect playground for outdoor enthusiasts, with world-class diving and snorkeling experiences, diverse wildlife, and endless opportunities for adventure. In this comprehensive tour guide to Red Sea Coast, we’ll explore the must-see attractions and activities, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Red Sea Coast

When planning a trip to Red Sea Coast, there are several notable attractions to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in culture and history, the Red Sea Coast has got you covered. Here are some of the most important places to visit:

  • The Hurghada Museum
  • El-Gouna Museum
  • The Ancient Roman Mons Porphyritis

Natural Attractions of Red Sea Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Red Sea Coast is also known for its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife. Here are some natural attractions you can’t miss:

  • Giftun Island
  • Ras Mohammed National Park
  • Sharm El-Naga Bay
  • Dolphin House Reef

Hiking/Biking Routes of Red Sea Coast

If you love hiking and biking, the Red Sea Coast has several great routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking trails:

  • Wadi Gimal
  • Makadi Bay
  • Jebel Manghoub

Best Things to Do in Red Sea Coast: Activities and Tours

Red Sea Coast offers several activities and tours to make your trip extraordinary. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Snorkeling and diving
  • Glass bottom boat ride
  • Quad biking
  • Camel riding
  • Hot air balloon ride

Week-Long Itinerary for Red Sea Coast

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Red Sea Coast, here’s a sample itinerary to help you maximize your experience:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival in Hurghada, snorkeling at Giftun Island
Day 2 Diving at Ras Mohammed National Park
Day 3 Quad biking excursion in the desert
Day 4 Guided tour of El-Gouna Museum
Day 5 Hot air balloon ride over Luxor, visit to the Valley of the Kings
Day 6 Glass bottom boat ride at Sharm El-Naga Bay
Day 7 Camel ride in the desert to watch the sunset

Food and Drink Scene of Red Sea Coast

Egyptian cuisine offers a diverse range of flavors and spices that make it unique and delicious. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Red Sea Coast:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Koshari
  • Ful Medames
  • Shawarma
  • Kushari
  • Seafood dishes like grilled shrimp and fish

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus tea
  • Mint tea
  • Sobia (made from coconut milk)

Where to Stay in Red Sea Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Red Sea Coast offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay based on your budget:

  • Luxury: Barceló Tiran Sharm Resort, Rixos Seagate Sharm
  • Mid-range: Jasmine Palace Resort and Spa, Nubia Aqua Beach Resort
  • Budget: Royal Albatros Moderna, Sea Garden Resort

What to Buy in Red Sea Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Red Sea Coast with you, here are some souvenirs and local products to consider buying:

  • Papyrus paintings
  • Handmade jewelry
  • Bedouin rugs
  • Spices and herbs

Local Culture and Customs of Red Sea Coast

The Red Sea Coast is known for its friendly locals and laid-back culture. Here are some customs and etiquette to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid taking photos of people without their permission.

Best Time to Visit Red Sea Coast

The best time to visit Red Sea Coast is between October to April, when the weather is moderate and pleasant. However, if you’re interested in water activities, the summertime is the best time to go.

Do’s and Don’ts of Red Sea Coast

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Red Sea Coast:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Stay hydrated in the hot weather.
  • Try local cuisine and products.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Leave your belongings unattended.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Red Sea Coast

Red Sea Coast is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s still essential to take the necessary precautions to ensure your safety:

  • Be mindful of your surroundings.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas.

Health in Red Sea Coast

To ensure a healthy trip, you should take some basic precautions:

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Apply sunscreen.
  • Use bug repellent.

Getting to Red Sea Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to Red Sea Coast is by flying into Hurghada International Airport, which serves flights from Europe and Asia. Alternatively, you can take a bus or drive from Cairo.

Airports Near Red Sea Coast

  • Hurghada International Airport
  • Sharm El Sheikh International Airport
  • Marsa Alam International Airport

Bus Stations Near Red Sea Coast

  • Hurghada Bus Station
  • Sharm El Sheikh Bus Station
  • El Gouna Bus Station

Other Options

Some other transportation options are private taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Careem.

How to Get Around in Red Sea Coast

The easiest way to get around Red Sea Coast is by taxi or rental car. Alternatively, you can also use public transportation, including buses and minibusses.

Where to Go Next from Red Sea Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Egypt, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Cairo
  • Luxor
  • Aswan

Conclusion

Red Sea Coast offers a wonderful destination for travelers looking for a mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, culture, and history. Make sure to follow our guide to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay for a memorable trip to Red Sea Coast.
