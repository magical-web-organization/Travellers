Introduction to Port Suez

Port Suez is an important port city situated in the Red Sea Coast, 60 kilometers (37 miles) south of the Suez Canal. This bustling city is known for its strong cultural and historical significance. The city has a lot to offer to its visitors, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and shopping.

List of Places to Visit in Port Suez

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Suez Canal Museum - Located in the heart of the city, this museum showcases the history of the Suez Canal, from its construction to its important role in maritime trade.
  • Suez War Memorial - This monument dedicated to the heroes who fought in the 1956 Suez War is a must-visit site in Port Suez.

Natural Attractions of Port Suez: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pharaoh’s Island - A beautiful and historically significant island located in the Gulf of Suez that offers scenic views and excellent diving opportunities.
  • Ras Mohamed National Park - A protected area located at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, with spectacular coral reefs and marine life, and several hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Suez

  • Sinai Trail - A 550-kilometer (340-mile) long trail that passes through the Sinai Peninsula, offering trekking and cultural experiences.

Best Things to Do in Port Suez: Activities and Tours

  • Sunset Camel Ride - Explore the desert and capture the beauty of the desert sunset with a camel ride tour.
  • Red Sea Snorkeling tour - Explore the underwater beauty of the Red Sea with a snorkeling tour of its coral reefs and exotic marine life.

Food and Drink Scene of Port Suez

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ful medames - A traditional breakfast dish made with fava beans, olive oil, garlic, and lemon juice.
  • Mahshi - Stuffed vegetables such as tomatoes, cabbage, and eggplant with rice and ground beef or lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Tea with Mint (Shai bi-naana) - A refreshing local tea made with fresh mint and sugar.
  • Qamar Eldin - A sweet juice made from dried apricots.

Where to Stay in Port Suez: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Setty Residence - A modern and luxurious hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Villa 115 - A charming guesthouse located in a quiet area with a pool and garden.

What to Buy in Port Suez: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Egyptian cotton clothes and linens
  • Handmade jewelry and pottery from local markets like the Old Market (Souq)

Local Culture and Customs of Port Suez

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, and always ask permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Port Suez

The best time to visit Port Suez is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild, and the city is not crowded with tourists. Summers are hot and dry, while winters can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Suez

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Visit historical and cultural attractions

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink alcohol in public places
  • Don’t take photographs of people without asking their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Suez

Port Suez is mostly a safe city for tourists. However, it is advisable to take necessary precautions like not flashing valuable items in public places and avoid travelling alone at night.

Health in Port Suez

Travellers to Port Suez should ensure they have up to date vaccinations, and drink bottled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Port Suez: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port Suez

The nearest airport to Port Suez is the Cairo International Airport, located approximately 150 kilometers (93 miles) northwest of the city.

Bus Stations Near Port Suez

  • Microbus Station 2, Suez Regional Transport Authority
  • Suez, Bus Station, Central Station

Other Options

Private taxis and rental cars are also available for transportation within the city.

How to Get Around in Port Suez

The most common modes of transportation in Port Suez are taxis, buses, and rented cars.

Where to Go Next from Port Suez: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cairo - The vibrant capital of Egypt, with its rich history and cultural attractions
  • Alexandria - Known as the “Pearl of the Mediterranean”, this beautiful and historic city is worth visiting.

Conclusion

Port Suez is a fascinating city that has something for everyone to enjoy. With its rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural scenery, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, it is definitely worth a visit for travellers seeking an authentic Egyptian experience. Plan your trip today and discover the wonders of Port Suez!
