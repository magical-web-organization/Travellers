Introduction to Wadi El Gemal National Park

Located in the Red Sea Coast of Egypt, Wadi El Gemal National Park is a breathtaking desert oasis that covers an area of about 5,000 sq km. The park is home to stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and a rich cultural heritage that dates back to ancient Egypt. Wadi El Gemal National Park is an ideal destination for those who wish to witness the beauty of the Egyptian desert and its unique flora and fauna, learn about its history and culture, and experience outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Wadi El Gemal National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Temple of Seti I: an ancient temple dedicated to the god Amun-Ra located on the east bank of the Nile river, south of Luxor.

  • The Valley of the Kings: a significant historical site, home to the tombs of pharaohs and inc and their queens, including Tutankhamun’s tomb.

  • The Red Sea Monastery: this Coptic Orthodox monastery is one of the oldest Christian monasteries in the world.

Natural Attractions of Wadi El Gemal National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Wadi El Gemal Trail: a 42-kilometer long hiking path that takes visitors through deserts, coastlines, and jungles.

  • The Mangrove Forest: filled with numerous bird species, the mangrove forest offers a beautiful landscape along the Red Sea coast. Visitors can kayak through the mangroves or take a boat tour.

  • Sharm El Lulli beach: a secluded beach with crystal-clear waters and stunning coral reefs where visitors can swim and snorkel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wadi El Gemal National Park

Wadi El Gemal National Park offers various hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts of all levels. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • The Wadi El Gemal Trail that offers a four-day hike through a diverse landscape.
  • The Abu Ghusun Trail: a short trail that passes through an abandoned village, leading to a natural pool.
  • The Qulan Trail located at Wadi Lahmi, offering a scenic walk through valleys and freshwater pools.

Best Things to Do in Wadi El Gemal National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and diving: Wadi El Gemal National Park is home to several coral reefs that offer an immersive snorkeling and diving experience with a diverse selection of marine life.

  • Desert Safari: Visitors can experience the desert’s beauty, ride camels or ATV vehicles, visit Bedouin camps and learn about their culture.

  • Horseback riding: Explore the Wadi El Gemal and Red Sea coastline on horseback, taking in the scenic vistas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wadi El Gemal National Park

Day 1: Visit Luxor - East Bank Day 2: Explore the West Bank, including the Valley of the Kings and other ancient sites. Day 3: Arrive at Marsa Alam and check into hotel. Enjoy diving and snorkeling. Day 4: Visit Wadi El Gemal National Park for hiking trails, wildlife sightings, and natural scenery. Day 5: Take a boat tour through the mangrove forest or kayak through the mangroves. Day 6: Enjoy horseback riding along the Red Sea coastline or through Wadi El Gemal National Park. Day 7: Depart from Marsa Alam.

Food and Drink Scene of Wadi El Gemal National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shawarma: a Levantine dish consisting of grilled meat, wrapped in pita bread, and served with fried vegetables and tahini sauce.

  • Koshari: a traditional Egyptian dish made of macaroni, lentils, chickpeas, and fried onions, mixed with tomato sauce and chili vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Hibiscus tea: a popular drink in Egypt, this herbal tea has a sour taste and is believed to be beneficial for health.

  • Turkish coffee: small cups of intense, thick coffee commonly served with almonds and dates.

What to Buy in Wadi El Gemal National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade carpets and kilims: Wadi El Gemal National Park is famous for its carpets and kilims made by the Bedouin nomads.

  • Leather goods: high-quality leather products such as bags, belts, and wallets are commonly found in the nearby markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Wadi El Gemal National Park

The local Bedouin population in Wadi El Gemal National Park has a rich heritage that has been preserved for centuries. Visitors can learn more about the Bedouin’s culture, customs, and lifestyles through cultural tours and experiences.

Best Time to Visit Wadi El Gemal National Park

The best time to visit Wadi El Gemal National Park is from October to March when the weather is mild, and there is less humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wadi El Gemal National Park

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately.
  • Bring plenty of sunscreen and wear a hat.
  • Stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind.
  • Swim or snorkel without a guide.
  • Take photos of local people without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wadi El Gemal National Park

  • Stay on designated paths, especially during hikes.
  • Wear proper footwear and bring a first-aid kit.
  • Avoid hiking alone.
  • Be aware of potential wildlife encounters.

Health in Wadi El Gemal National Park

  • Visitors should drink only bottled or purified water.
  • Visitors should bring all necessary medication with them.

Getting to and Around Wadi El Gemal National Park

Wadi El Gemal National Park can be accessed from the nearby Marsa Alam Airport that services multiple flights from Cairo International Airport. Visitors can either rent a car or hire a taxi from Marsa Alam to travel around the park.

Conclusion

Wadi El Gemal National Park is a must-visit destination in Egypt for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture lovers alike.
