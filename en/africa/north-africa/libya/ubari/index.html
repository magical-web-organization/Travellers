Introduction to Ubari

Ubari is a small city located in the southwestern part of Libya. It is nestled in the Sahara Desert and surrounded by stunning natural wonders, making it a popular destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Ubari is also rich in culture and history, offering visitors the chance to explore ancient ruins and museums.

List of Places to Visit in Ubari

Ubari is a city full of attractions, including its rich culture and natural beauty. Here are the top things to see in Ubari.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tadrart Acacus: A UNESCO World Heritage site that boasts a vast collection of prehistoric rock art.
  • Sabha Museum: A historical museum that showcases the city’s ancient civilization and its development over thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Ubari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ubari Sand Sea: This is a large expanse of sand dunes that provide visitors with a true desert driving experience.
  • Umm al-Maa: A natural lake located at the heart of the Ubari Sand Sea. It is an ideal location for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.
  • Ubari Oasis: A palm tree-lined oasis that offers a perfect place to relax or take a stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ubari

Ubari is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Its natural landscapes offer challenging terrain for experienced hikers and bikers, and leisurely trails for beginners. Here are the top routes to explore in Ubari:

  • Tadrart Acacus Trail: A trail that takes hikers through the Tadrart Acacus Mountains while offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Ubari Oasis Trail: A leisurely trail that takes visitors into the heart of the oasis and allows them to enjoy the natural scenery.
  • Ubari Sand Sea Trail: A challenging trail that takes experienced hikers and bikers through the vast expanse of sand dunes that offer breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Ubari: Activities and Tours

  • Desert Safari: A popular activity that allows visitors to experience the thrill of driving through the Ubari Sand Sea.
  • Swimming and Diving: Umm al-Maa and other natural lakes in the area are ideal locations for swimming, diving and snorkeling.
  • Desert Camping: There is nothing more unique than spending a night under the starry sky in the desert. There is an increasing trend of glamping in Ubari.

Food and Drink Scene of Ubari

Must-Try Dishes

Ubari offers a variety of dishes that are not only tasty but also unique. Some famous dishes to try include:

  • Couscous: A local dish made of finely ground semolina with vegetables or meat.
  • Shorba: A spicy soup made from lamb or chicken with vegetables and various Arabic spices.
  • Lahm-bil-libiya: A traditional Libyan dish made with lamb and potatoes.

Local Beverages

Libya is an Islamic country, and alcohol is strictly prohibited. However, visitors can enjoy a variety of refreshing beverages such as:

  • Sahlab: A creamy hot drink made from powdered orchid roots and served with cinnamon and walnuts.
  • Qaysar: A popular national drink made from fermented millet that has been mixed with spices and sugar.

Where to Stay in Ubari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ubari offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Al-Faida Hotel: offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and serves delicious local cuisine.
  • Al Marwa Hotel: offers air-conditioned rooms and complimentary breakfast. The hotel also has a rooftop terrace with stunning views.
  • Ubari Guest House: offers budget-friendly accommodation with basic amenities and a restaurant serving local dishes.

What to Buy in Ubari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ubari offers a variety of unique gifts and souvenirs to bring back home. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Traditional Arabian jewelry.
  • Hand-woven textiles and clothing.
  • Local pottery and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Ubari

Ubari is a conservative Islamic region. When visiting the city, travelers should dress modestly, respect Islamic customs, and avoid public displays of affection. Visitors should also be aware that alcohol consumption is strictly prohibited within the city limits.

Best Time to Visit Ubari

The best time to visit Ubari is from September to December when the temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Avoid visiting Ubari between June and August when temperatures can reach upwards of 100°F.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ubari

Do:

  • Research local customs and dress appropriately while visiting religious sites.
  • Carry a scarf to cover your head if you’re visiting mosques.
  • Try the local cuisine and experience traditional Libyan culture.
  • Carry enough cash as ATM machines are scarce in Ubari.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Drink alcohol in public areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing or shorts in public areas.
  • Eat during the daylight hours of Ramadan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ubari

Libya has a complex political situation, and Ubari has had some problems with security in recent years. Travelers should avoid areas bordering neighboring countries and large public gatherings. It is advisable to have a local guide and be alert in the city. Ensure you have the proper travel insurance before visiting.

Health in Ubari

There are no major health concerns in Ubari. However, visitors are advised to carry sufficient amounts of medication for any underlying or critical health issues.

Getting to Ubari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ubari can be accessed by air via the Sabha International Airport. There are also buses that run from major cities such as Tripoli and Benghazi to Ubari. Visitors can also opt to rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Airports Near Ubari

  • Sabha International Airport, around 200 km from Ubari.

Bus Stations Near Ubari

  • The bus station in Ubari connects travelers to major cities such as Tripoli and Benghazi.

Other Options

  • Rent a car to explore the region independently.

How to Get Around in Ubari

Taxis are the most common mode of transportation in Ubari, and visitors can easily hail one from the city center. Renting a car is also an option for visitors who want to have a more independent and flexible itinerary.

Where to Go Next from Ubari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ubari is a great starting point for travelers who want to explore other parts of Libya, such as:

  • Tripoli: The capital city of Libya, which features a mix of historical and modern attractions.
  • Benghazi: A bustling port city with a rich history and a vibrant cultural scene.
