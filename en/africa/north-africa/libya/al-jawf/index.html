Introduction to Al Jawf

Located in the northern region of Libya, Al Jawf is a city that is rich in culture and history. For many tourists, this hidden gem is yet to be discovered. However, those who have explored Al Jawf can attest to the breathtaking natural beauty, the diverse outdoors, and the welcoming people. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at what makes Al Jawf worth visiting.

List of Places to Visit in Al Jawf

Here are some of the top attractions that you don’t want to miss when you visit Al Jawf:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Benghazi Museum
  • Omar Al-Mukhtar’s House
  • Bengasi War Cemetery
  • Italian Cemetery

Natural Attractions of Al Jawf: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The White Desert
  • The Black Desert
  • The Sand Sea
  • The Qattara Depression

Best Things to Do in Al Jawf: Activities and Tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Al Jawf

Here’s an itinerary that you can make use of during your stay:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Old Town Shahat, Omar Al-Mukhtar’s House
Day 2 Visit Cyrene and the Apollonia museums
Day 3 Wadi Al-Hayat Hiking Trail, snorkeling at Abughilan Beach
Day 4 Murzuk Sand Sea Camel Trekking, overnight camping
Day 5 Explore Benghazi and its museums
Day 6 Qattara Depression Tour, Visit the Black and White Desert
Day 7 Relax at the Mediterranean coast, visit Lepcis Magna

Local Culture and Customs of Al Jawf

Al Jawf’s customs and traditions are greatly influenced by Islam. The locals are accommodating, respectful, and visitors are expected to reciprocate the same.

Best Time to Visit Al Jawf

From March to May and September to November, the weather in Al Jawf is generally mild, making it the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Al Jawf

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly in holy sites like the mosque.
  • Take off your shoes before entering homes or religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Photograph military personnel, installations or police officers.
  • Speak about politics unless the locals bring the topic up.
  • Drink alcohol in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Al Jawf

Al Jawf is safe to visit, however, tourists must take normal precautions such as avoiding isolated areas, and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Al Jawf

Visitors are advised to have the necessary vaccinations and carry a basic health kit, including malaria prophylaxis.
