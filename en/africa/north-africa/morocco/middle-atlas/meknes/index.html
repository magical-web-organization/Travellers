Introduction to Meknes

Meknes is a city in Middle Atlas, Morocco, known for its rich history and stunning architecture. The city was founded in the 11th century and served as a key center for trade and commerce in North Africa. The city boasts a charming old Medina, ancient ruins, and beautiful buildings that are a testament to its long and varied past.

List of Places to Visit in Meknes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bab Mansour: This is the gateway to the old Medina and one of the most impressive gates in Morocco. It was built in the 18th century.
  • Moulay Ismail Mausoleum: This is the tomb of Moulay Ismail, the Sultan who made Meknes the capital city in the late 17th century.
  • Dar Jamai Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the lavish lifestyle of the wealthy in Morocco’s past with its collection of traditional Moroccan furnishings and art.
  • Volubilis: This ancient Roman city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a peek into the region’s past.

Natural Attractions of Meknes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lalla Aouda Lake: This lake offers a serene place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Meknes’s surroundings.
  • Hassan Tower: A stunning view of the city can be seen from the top of this tower, which dates back to the 12th century.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meknes

  • Middle Atlas Mountains: The rugged terrain of the Middle Atlas Mountains offers numerous hiking and mountain biking trails for enthusiasts of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Meknes: Activities and Tours

  • Camel and Horse Riding Tour: Take a tour of the countryside in Meknes riding a horse or a camel.
  • Cooking Class: Moroccan cuisine is famous around the world. Learn how to make traditional dishes in a cooking class in the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Meknes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Couscous: This popular Moroccan dish is a must-try in Meknes. It is typically served with vegetables and meat.
  • Mint Tea: This sweet tea is a staple in Morocco and is perfect for sipping while taking in the city’s sights.
  • Pastilla: This sweet and savory pastry is a popular street food in Meknes.

Local Beverages

  • Orange Juice: Meknes is known for its fresh and delicious orange juice.

Where to Stay in Meknes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Transatlantique: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers guests a comfortable and elegant stay.
  • Riad Hiba: This traditional guesthouse is perfect for those who want to experience authentic Moroccan hospitality.
  • Palm Appart Club Meknes: This modern, fully-equipped apartment hotel is perfect for families or groups.

What to Buy in Meknes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Meknes is known for its beautiful, hand-painted pottery.
  • Leather goods: Morocco has a long history of producing high-quality leather products, and Meknes is no exception.
  • Moroccan spices: Pick up some of the country’s famous spices to bring home and create your own Moroccan dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Meknes

Meknes is a predominantly Muslim city, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Dress modestly and be aware of prayer times.

Best Time to Visit Meknes

The best time to visit Meknes is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable for exploring the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meknes

Do:

  • Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs.
  • Negotiate prices with shopkeepers in the souks for souvenirs and products.

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are not common in Morocco and should be avoided.
  • Don’t drink tap water. Stick to bottled water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Meknes

Meknes is generally safe for visitors, but it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close.

Health in Meknes

Visitors should drink bottled water and be cautious of street food. It’s also a good idea to visit a healthcare professional before traveling to make sure you are up to date on vaccinations.

Getting to Meknes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Meknes is easily accessible by plane, train, or bus.

Airports Near Meknes

  • Fes-Saiss Airport: This is the closest airport to Meknes, located about 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Meknes

  • Meknes Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Meknes and offers connections to other cities in Morocco.

Other Options

  • Train: Meknes has a train station that connects to major cities in Morocco.

How to Get Around in Meknes

The easiest and most convenient way to get around in Meknes is by taxi. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or by rental car.

Where to Go Next from Meknes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fes: This nearby city is known for its well-preserved medieval architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Chefchaouen: This stunning blue-hued city is located in the Rif Mountains and is a popular destination for its unique charm.

Conclusion

Meknes is a charming and historic city that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Morocco’s past and present. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Meknes is a must-visit destination for those who want to experience the best of Morocco.
