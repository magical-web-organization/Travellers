Introduction to Fez

Fez is a historic city in Middle Atlas, Morocco, known for its rich cultural heritage and diverse architecture. It is divided into two main parts – Fes el Bali, the old fortified city, and Fes Jdid, the newer section developed during the Marinid dynasty. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is a must-visit destination for those interested in exploring the traditional Moroccan way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Fez

Fez is home to numerous attractions that offer travelers the chance to explore the city’s history, architecture, and cultural heritage. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Batha Museum: A former palace that has been converted into a museum showcasing Moroccan arts and crafts.

  • Medersa Bou Inania: One of the finest Islamic educational institutions in Morocco, featuring intricate tilework and carvings.

  • Dar Batha: A palace that is now a museum featuring traditional Moroccan arts and crafts.

  • Al-Attarine Madrasa: A stunning 14th-century Islamic college with intricate tilework and carvings.

  • Jewish Cemetery: Located in the Mellah, this historic cemetery is the final resting place of Jews who lived in Fez.

Natural Attractions of Fez: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ifrane National Park: A 125,000-acre national park that is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including Barbary macaques.

  • Jnan Sbil: A peaceful garden in the heart of the city that offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle.

  • Bou Inania Mosaics: Located in the new town, the Bou Inania Mosaics are a must-visit for those interested in Islamic art.

  • Merinids Tombs: Located on a hill overlooking the city, the Merinids Tombs offer stunning views of the medina and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fez

Fez is surrounded by beautiful mountains and forests, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Tazekka National Park: A popular destination for hiking and trekking, offering beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Bouiblane National Park: A remote national park that provides a challenging and rewarding trekking experience.

  • Azrou Forest: A beautiful cedar forest located near Ifrane, perfect for hiking and nature walks.

Best Things to Do in Fez: Activities and Tours

Fez offers an array of activities and tours for travelers looking to immerse themselves in the local culture and explore the city’s historic sites. Some of the top things to do in Fez include:

  • Take a Guided Tour of the Medina: A must-do for first-time visitors, as it allows travelers to explore the labyrinthine streets of the medina and discover its hidden gems.

  • Visit a Traditional Hammam Bath: Experience the traditional Moroccan spa culture with a visit to a hammam bath.

  • Attend a Traditional Moroccan Dinner: Enjoy a traditional Moroccan meal complete with live entertainment, music, and belly dancing.

  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Moroccan dishes with a local chef.

  • Visit Fez’s Tanneries: Witness the traditional leather tanning process at one of Fez’s famous tanneries.

Food and Drink Scene of Fez

Fez is famous for its traditional Moroccan cuisine, which is both flavorful and diverse. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: A slow-cooked stew made with meat, vegetables, and spices, cooked in a clay pot.

  • Couscous: A North African staple made with steamed semolina grains, served with tender meat and vegetables.

  • Harira: A hearty soup made with lentils, chickpeas, and vegetables.

  • Pastilla: A savory pie made with pigeon or chicken, almonds, cinnamon, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: A traditional Moroccan tea made with fresh mint leaves and sugar.

  • Orange Juice: Freshly squeezed orange juice is a refreshing and healthy choice.

  • Avocado Juice: A creamy and filling drink made with avocado and milk.

Where to Stay in Fez: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fez has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Riad Fes: A luxurious riad featuring traditional Moroccan architecture, a swimming pool, and a rooftop terrace.

  • Dar Elinor: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of the medina, featuring traditional Moroccan decor and a rooftop terrace.

  • Palais Medina & Spa: A modern hotel featuring a swimming pool, spa, and a restaurant serving traditional Moroccan cuisine.

  • Hostel Dar Jannat: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of the medina, featuring a rooftop terrace and a communal lounge.

What to Buy in Fez: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fez is known for its traditional handicrafts, including ceramics, leather products, and textiles. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Ceramics: Handmade pottery and ceramics make for beautiful and unique souvenirs.

  • Leather Products: Fez is famous for its traditional leather tanning process, so it’s a great place to buy leather goods such as bags and shoes.

  • Textiles: From carpets and rugs to blankets and caftans, Fez is a great place to buy traditional textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Fez

Morocco is a conservative Muslim country, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some of the key things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: Women should cover their shoulders and legs, while men should avoid wearing shorts in public.

  • Take Off Your Shoes: It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a mosque, a private home, or even some public spaces.

  • Ask Permission Before Taking Photos: It is considered rude to take photos of people without their permission, so be sure to ask before doing so.

Best Time to Visit Fez

The best time to visit Fez is in the spring (March-May) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and comfortable. The summer months (June-August) can be very hot, while the winter months (December-February) can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fez

Do:

  • Explore the Medina: The medina is the heart and soul of Fez, so be sure to spend plenty of time exploring its winding streets and hidden alleys.

  • Try the Local Food: Fez is famous for its traditional Moroccan cuisine, so be sure to sample some of the delicious dishes on offer.

  • Haggle at the Souks: Bargaining is a part of the shopping experience in Fez, so don’t be afraid to negotiate with vendors.

Don’t:

  • Take Photos of People Without Permission: It is considered rude to take photos of people without their permission, so be sure to ask before doing so.

  • Venture Out Alone at Night: Fez is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always best to be cautious and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Drink Tap Water: The tap water in Fez is not safe to drink, so be sure to stick to bottled water during your visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fez

Fez is generally a safe city, but travelers should still take precautions to avoid becoming the victim of petty crime. Here are some tips for staying safe in Fez:

  • Avoid Carrying Large Amounts of Cash: Only carry the cash you need for the day and leave the rest in a safe place.

  • Be Cautious in Crowded Areas: Pickpocketing is common in crowded areas such as the medina, so keep your valuables close to you.

  • Stay in Well-Lit and Populated Areas at Night: It’s always best to avoid empty streets or poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Fez

Travelers to Fez should take precautions to prevent illness, including:

  • Getting Vaccinations: Check with your doctor before traveling to Fez to ensure that you are up-to-date on your vaccinations.

  • Avoiding Tap Water: Drink only bottled water and avoid ice cubes and salads washed in tap water.

  • Using Sunscreen: Protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen and a hat, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Fez: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fez

The closest airport to Fez is Fes-Saïss Airport, which is located just 15 km from the city center. The airport offers flights to and from several cities in Europe and Africa, including Paris, London, Madrid, and Tunis.

Bus Stations Near Fez

Fez has several bus stations that connect the city with other destinations in Morocco:

  • CTM: Located in the new town, CTM offers comfortable and affordable bus service to several destinations in Morocco.

  • Supratours: Supratours offers bus service to several destinations in Morocco, including Marrakech, Casablanca, and Tangier.

  • Gare Routière: Gare Routière is the main bus station in Fez and offers service to several destinations in northern and eastern Morocco.

Other Options

Travelers to Fez can also reach the city by train or car.

  • Train: Fez is connected to several cities in Morocco via a modern and efficient train system.

  • Car: Travelers can rent a car and drive to Fez from other Moroccan cities, although it is important to note that Moroccan roads can be challenging.

How to Get Around in Fez

Fez is a compact city, and most of the main attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Taxis and horse-drawn carriages are also available for those looking for a more comfortable mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Fez: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fez is well-connected to several other destinations in Morocco, making it an ideal starting point for further exploration. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Meknes: A historic city known for its elaborate palaces and minarets, just an hour’s drive from Fez.

  • Ifrane: A picturesque mountain town known for its Swiss-style architecture and scenic views, located just 45 minutes from Fez.

  • Volubilis: An ancient Roman city located about an hour and a half’s drive from Fez.

Conclusion

Fez is a historic city with a rich cultural heritage, offering countless attractions and activities for visitors. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s history and architecture, hiking and biking in the surrounding mountains, or simply indulging in the local cuisine and culture, Fez is a destination worth exploring.
