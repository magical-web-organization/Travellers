Introduction

Located in the Middle Atlas mountain range of Morocco, Azrou is a charming town that’s filled with natural beauty, rich culture, and plenty of outdoor adventures. This small town is surrounded by stunning cedar forests, rocky terrain, and boasts a wealth of historic and cultural significance. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at Azrou and show you everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Azrou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning more about the history and culture of Azrou, be sure to check out some of the town’s prominent museums and historical sites. The most popular ones are:

  • Tazekka National Park: This stunning national park is located just south of Azrou and is home to a wide array of wild animals, such as mountain monkeys and Barbary macaques.
  • Lac d’Azrou: Take a stroll around this serene lake set amid the glorious middle Atlas mountains - it is the perfect place to relax and unwind.
  • Cedar Forest: Discover the ancient cedar forest of Azrou which is home to over 200 monkeys.
  • Old medina: Close to the market in Azrou, you will find the medina of the town with the ancient mosque and the small trade shops.
  • Alas Mosque: This historic mosque dates back to the 14th century and is a must-visit for anyone who wants to learn more about the religious and architectural significance of Azrou.

Natural Attractions of Azrou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Azrou is famous for its breathtaking outdoor scenery and adventures, and there are plenty of opportunities to explore the mountains, forests, and rivers. Popular attractions include:

  • Azrou Day Trip from Fez: Enjoy a day trip to Azrou from Fes, including time to explore Ifrane, the cedar forest, and Berber villages.
  • The Cedar Forest Circuit: Explore the stunning scenery of the cedar forest and meet the local Barbary apes by choosing the four-hour circuit.
  • Oum El Ala Lake: Located just a few miles west of Azrou, this stunning blue and green lake is a popular spot for picnics and water sports.
  • Mount Toubkal: For experienced hikers in search of a challenge, this is one of the top destinations near Azrou, offering breathtaking views of the Atlas Mountains with an incredible summit experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Azrou

Azrou is a hub for hiking and biking, with many trails suitable for both beginners and experienced adventurers alike. Some of the popular routes in Azrou include:

  • Cedar forest Hike: This easy 3-hour hike is ideal for beginners and takes you through the stunning cedar forest with views of the monkeys.
  • Jbel Ayachi Hike: This challenging 2-day hike takes you to the summit of Jbel Ayachi, which stands at 3,743 meters and offers stunning views of the Atlas Mountains.
  • Ifrane National Park Biking: Rent a bike in Azrou and head to the nearby Ifrane National Park, where you can bike through the lush green forests and admire the local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Azrou: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor adventures and historical attractions, there are many exciting things to do and tours to join in Azrou. These include:

  • Middle Atlas Berber Villages Tour: Visit the local Berber villages and learn more about their traditional way of life, including textile production and bread-making.
  • Horseback Riding: Discover the stunning countryside scenery of Azrou and Ifran on a horseback and discover the mountains in a unique way.
  • Local Cooking Classes: Learn to prepare traditional Moroccan dishes like Mechoui or Tagine.
  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and enjoy the spectacular views of Azrou and surrounding areas from above.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Azrou, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Azrou

If you’re a foodie, you’ll love Azrou. This town is famous for its delicious traditional cuisine that will leave your taste buds craving for more.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the food you have to try includes:

  • Mechoui: A roasted lamb dish that’s smoky and tender, often served with traditional Moroccan bread.
  • Tagine: A slow-cooked stew consisting of meat, vegetables, and spices.
  • Pastilla: A savory Moroccan pastry typically made with pigeon meat, almonds, and cinnamon.
  • Couscous: A staple of Moroccan cuisine - this flavorful dish is typically served with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Moroccan tea is one of the most popular beverages in Azrou, and you can find many tea shops and cafes all around town. Tea is typically served hot and sweetened, along with traditional Moroccan cookies like kaab el ghzal or gazelle horns.

Where to Stay in Azrou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options to choose from when it comes to accommodation in Azrou, ranging from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the options include:

  • Aghbalou Hotel: A comfortable, centrally-located hotel with friendly staff and great views of the town.
  • Auberge Du Moyen Atlas: This rustic guest house is located in the heart of Azrou and boasts a traditional Moroccan decor.
  • Riad Horizons: This elegant 4-star hotel is located in the outskirts of Azrou and offers the perfect combination of comfort, luxury, and natural beauty.

What to Buy in Azrou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Azrou is also known for its local products, including handicrafts, traditional fabrics, and spices. Some of the most popular souvenirs to take home include:

  • Cedarwood Products: Cedarwood is abundant in Azrou and is used to make everything from furniture to decorative carvings.
  • Handmade Carpets: Azrou is also famous for its handmade Berber carpets, woven with natural wool dyes and traditional patterns.
  • Spices: Morocco is known for its flavorful spices, and Azrou is no exception. Some of the most popular spices include Ras El Hanout, cumin, and paprika.

Local Culture and Customs of Azrou

Azrou has a rich cultural heritage, and as a visitor, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are a few things you should keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: The people of Azrou are generally conservative, and it’s recommended to dress modestly in public places like religious sites or public squares.
  • Greetings: Moroccan culture places high value on politeness and hospitality, so be sure to greet people with a smile and say “Salam” or “Hello.”
  • Personal Space: Moroccans also value personal space, so it’s important to be mindful of how close you are standing or sitting to others in public places.

Best Time to Visit Azrou

Azrou is a year-round destination, although some seasons are more suitable for certain activities than others. The best time to visit Azrou depends on your preferences and interests.

  • Spring (April - June): This is a great time to visit Azrou for outdoor activities and hiking as the weather is mild and the scenery is lush and green.
  • Summer (July - September): Summer is peak tourist season in Morocco, and it’s a great time to visit the popular tourist destinations.
  • Autumn (October - November): Autumn is an ideal time to visit Azrou for birdwatchers as the Bald Ibises make nests in the region.
  • Winter (December - February): This is the perfect time to witness the magic of Azrou with snowfall and a warm fireplace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Azrou

When you visit Azrou, here are some do’s and don’ts you should keep in mind:

Do:

  • Interact with locals: Moroccans are friendly and hospitable, and interacting with locals is a great way to learn more about their culture and way of life.
  • Try the local cuisine: Moroccan food is world-famous for its unique flavors and spices, and Azrou is no exception.
  • Respect local customs: Be mindful of local customs and traditions, and dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Bargain: Bargaining is a common practice in Morocco, especially in souks and markets, so be prepared to haggle for a good deal.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: It’s not recommended to drink tap water in Morocco, so be sure to bring bottled water with you or use a filtration system.
  • Take pictures without permission: Always ask permission before taking someone’s photo and be respectful of people’s privacy.
  • Be disrespectful to religion or culture: Be respectful of the local culture and customs, and avoid doing anything that could offend people’s religious beliefs.

Safety and Health

Azrou is a safe place for travelers, and serious crime is rare. However, there are some safety and health precautions you should be aware of.

Safety in Azrou

  • Petty Theft: Petty theft, such as pickpocketing, can occur in crowded places like souks or markets. Be sure to keep your valuables secure and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Road Safety: Road safety can be an issue in Morocco, so be cautious when driving or crossing roads, as reckless driving is common in the area.
  • Outdoor Adventures: If you’re planning on going on any outdoor adventures, such as hiking or biking, be sure to take the necessary precautions to prevent injury, such as wearing proper shoes and bringing plenty of water.

Health in Azrou

  • Vaccinations: It’s recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies before traveling to Morocco.
  • High Altitude: The high altitude of Azrou can cause altitude sickness, so be sure to take your time adjusting to the altitude and consult a doctor if you feel unwell.

Getting to Azrou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Azrou is relatively easy, and there are several transportation options available, including:

  • Air: The nearest airports to Azrou are Mohammed V International Airport in Casablanca and Fes-Saïs International Airport in Fes. From there, you can take a taxi or private transfer to Azrou.
  • Bus: Azrou is well-connected to other parts of Morocco by bus, and there are several bus companies that operate services to and from other major cities like Marrakech, Fes, and Casablanca.
  • Train: Azrou does not have a train station, but the nearest stations are located in Fes and Meknes. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Azrou.

How to Get Around in Azrou

Azrou is a small town, and most of the popular attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you need to travel further afield, there are several transportation options available, including:

  • Taxi: Taxis are one of the most convenient and affordable ways to get around Azrou. You can often find them parked in the town center or outside popular attractions.
  • Car Rental: If you want more freedom to explore the region, you can rent a car from one of the many car rental companies in Azrou.
  • Bicycle: For a more eco-friendly and active way to get around, you can rent a bicycle from one of the local rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Azrou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of exciting destinations and excursions to explore near Azrou, including:

  • Fes: Located less than 80 km from Azrou, Fes is a vibrant and historic city that’s known for its winding alleys, grand palaces, and colorful markets.
  • Ifrane: Located just 13 km south of Azrou, Ifrane is a picturesque mountain town that’s popular for its outdoor adventures and winter sports.
  • Meknes: This imperial city is located around 75 km west of Azrou and is famous for its stunning architecture and historic landmarks.
  • Merzouga: This gorgeous desert town is located around 400 km southeast of Azrou and is famous for its sand dunes and camel treks.

Conclusion

Azrou is a fascinating destination with a wealth of natural beauty, history, and culture to discover. Whether you want to get lost in the cedar forests, explore the local museums, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Azrou. With this travel guide, you’re ready to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming Moroccan town. Bon Voyage!
