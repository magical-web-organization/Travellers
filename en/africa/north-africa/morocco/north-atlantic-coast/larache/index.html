Introduction to Larache

Nestled on the North Atlantic Coast of Morocco, Larache is a charming town that is perfect for those who want a break from the hustle and bustle of the big cities. It is located in the region of Tanger-Tetouan-Al Hoceima, overlooking the sea and surrounded by lush green hills. With a population of nearly 120,000, it’s not too big, nor too small, but just right for travelers who want to experience local culture and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Larache

Larache is full of surprises for travelers, with many culturally significant sites and natural attractions that are worth a visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history, you must visit the El Makhzen Fortress, which was built in the 15th century by the Portuguese. The site is now a museum that displays artifacts from the Phoenician and Roman eras, as well as the Muslim conquest of North Africa. Another must-visit is the Spanish Garden, a beautiful park that was once the residence of the Spanish governor. Finally, don’t forget to visit the French district, with its charming colonial architecture and street cafes.

Natural Attractions of Larache: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Larache is surrounded by natural beauty, with the stunning Oued Loukkos river flowing through it. Take a stroll down its banks and enjoy the cool breeze. Larache is also home to the Moulay Bousselham wildlife reserve, where you can spot numerous bird species, including flamingos, ibis, and herons. For those who love outdoor activities, there are hiking trails that offer scenic views of the hills and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Larache

Larache is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails that cater to all levels of experience. The best way to explore this region is by foot or bike, and the surrounding hills offer some of the most spectacular views of the Atlantic. The popular hiking trails include the Oued Laou Trail and the Toubkal Trail, which are both challenging but rewarding.

Best Things to Do in Larache: Activities and Tours

Larache offers many activities and tours that cater to different interests. You can take a horseback ride along the beach, go on a tour of the Medina, or take a boat ride along the coast. You can also visit the local markets, where you can find unique souvenirs and local handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Larache

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Larache:

  • Day 1: Visit the El Makhzen Fortress Museum, the Spanish Garden, and the French district.
  • Day 2-3: Explore the natural beauty of the Moulay Bousselham wildlife reserve and take a walk along the Oued Loukkos river.
  • Day 4: Take a hiking or biking tour of the hills around Larache.
  • Day 5: Take a tour of the local markets and visit the souks.
  • Day 6-7: Relax on the beach or take a boat trip along the coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Larache

Larache offers a unique culinary experience for foodies, with delicious dishes that are infused with local spices and flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

You must try the Couscous dish, a Moroccan staple that is made with semolina and vegetables. The Tagine dish, made with lamb, chicken or beef, is also a must-try. And for dessert, try the traditional Moroccan almond pastry, the Gazelle Horns.

Local Beverages

Mint tea is the traditional drink of Morocco and is a must-try when in Larache. The local wine and beer are also worth tasting, and the date liquor is a delicious after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Larache: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Larache offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best hotels in Larache are the Hotel Al Kasbah, Hotel Al Yacout, and Hotel Miramar.

What to Buy in Larache: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Larache is known for its unique handicrafts and souvenirs, such as rugs, pottery, and jewelry. You can find them in local markets and small shops in the Medina.

Local Culture and Customs of Larache

Moroccans are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and the people of Larache are no different. Dress modestly while visiting mosques and other religious places. It is also customary to take off your shoes before entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Larache

The best time to visit Larache is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant. It can get hot during the summer months, and some places may close during winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Larache

Do:

  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Bargain in the markets.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Drink tap water.
  • Visit remote areas alone at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Larache

Larache is generally safe for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions to protect your belongings when visiting markets and public places.

Health in Larache

Travelers should take necessary precautions such as drinking bottled water and avoiding street food. It’s also a good idea to have travel health insurance.

Getting to Larache: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Larache

The nearest airport to Larache is Tangier Ibn Batouta International Airport, which is approximately a one-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Larache

Larache is well-connected by bus to other major cities in Morocco, with daily services available from the main bus station.

Other Options

The main train station in Larache connects the town to Tangier, Casablanca, and other major cities in Morocco.

How to Get Around in Larache

The most convenient way to get around Larache is by taxi, although it’s important to agree on a fare before starting your journey. Buses and trains are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Larache: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Larache is the perfect base for exploring the northern region of Morocco. The nearby destinations that are worth visiting include Tangier, Chefchaouen, and Asilah.

Conclusion

Larache is a hidden gem of Morocco that is waiting to be explored by travelers who want to experience the local culture and natural beauty of the North Atlantic Coast. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your trip to Larache and create memories that will last a lifetime.
