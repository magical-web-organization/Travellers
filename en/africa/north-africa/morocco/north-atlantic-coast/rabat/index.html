Introduction to Rabat

Rabat is the capital of Morocco, and a fascinating blend of modernity and traditions. Located on the coast of the North Atlantic, Rabat boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning historical and natural attractions, delicious food and drink scene, and warm hospitality. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Rabat, from the must-visit places to the best activities and tours, from where to stay to what to eat and drink, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Rabat

Below are some of the top attractions you should check out when visiting Rabat:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hassan Tower: an iconic minaret built in the 12th century, with breathtaking views of Rabat from the top
  • Chellah Necropolis: a historic site featuring ancient Roman ruins and tombs
  • Kasbah of the Udayas: a UNESCO World Heritage site with restored historic houses and scenic views of the Bou Regreg River
  • Mohammed VI Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art: a must-visit gallery with over 8,000 art pieces by local and international artists
  • Dar al-Makhzen: a 17th-century palace that serves as the Royal Palace of Morocco
  • Archaeological Museum of Rabat: a fascinating museum showcasing Moroccan artifacts from prehistoric times to the Islamic era

Natural Attractions of Rabat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oudayas Garden: a beautiful park with lush greenery, flowers, and trees, perfect for picnics and relaxing
  • Bouknadel Beach: a popular beach destination with golden sand, clear water, and vibrant atmosphere
  • Rabat Zoo: a family-friendly attraction featuring animals from around the world, including lions, tigers, and monkeys
  • Andalusian Gardens: a charming garden with a maze of trees and flowers, fountains, and water features
  • Foret Hilton: a huge forested park with numerous walking and biking trails, a lake, and picnic areas
  • Bouregreg River: a scenic river that separates Rabat from Salé, offering boat rides and stunning sunset views

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rabat

Rabat offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts, from hiking to biking. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Rabat Medina: a fascinating labyrinth of alleys, shops, and markets perfect for walking and exploring (hiking)
  • Oudayas Kasbah: a scenic fortified fortress overlooking the river, with cobbled streets, narrow alleys, and traditional architecture (hiking)
  • Bouregreg Valley: a stunning valley with traditional villages, olive groves, and river banks, perfect for biking

Best Things to Do in Rabat: Activities and Tours

  • Moroccan Cooking Class: a hands-on culinary experience that will teach you how to make authentic Moroccan dishes such as tagine, couscous, and pastilla
  • Hassan Tower Light Show: a stunning night-time display that illuminates the tower with lasers and lighthearted visual effects
  • Salé Bike Tour: a fun and informative bike tour that explores the charming city of Salé, located across the river from Rabat
  • Traditional Hammam Bath: a relaxing and rejuvenating spa experience that includes a massage, scrub, and steam bath in a traditional Moroccan hammam

Week-Long Itinerary for Rabat

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Rabat:

Day Activities
1 Visit Hassan Tower and Chellah Necropolis
2 Explore the Kasbah of the Udayas and Andalusian Gardens
3 Discover the Archaeological Museum of Rabat and Oudayas Garden
4 See the Mohammed VI Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art and hike in the Foret Hilton
5 Take a bike tour of Salé and relax at Bouknadel Beach
6 Enjoy a traditional hammam bath and see the Dar al-Makhzen Palace
7 Visit the Rabat Zoo and take a sunset boat ride on the Bouregreg River

Food and Drink Scene of Rabat

Moroccan cuisine is famous for its bold flavors, colorful spices, and fresh ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages when in Rabat:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: a slow-cooked stew with meat, vegetables, and spices, served in a cone-shaped clay pot
  • Couscous: a steamed semolina dish served with vegetables, meat, or fish
  • Pastilla: a sweet and savory pie made with pigeon meat or chicken, almonds, and cinnamon
  • Harira: a hearty soup made with lentils, chickpeas, tomatoes, and lamb, served during Ramadan
  • Moroccan mint tea: a sweet and fragrant tea made with fresh mint leaves and sugar, served in a traditional teapot and small glasses

Local Beverages

  • Orange juice: Morocco is famous for its delicious oranges, and you can find freshly squeezed orange juice on every corner in Rabat
  • Avocado juice: a creamy and refreshing drink made with ripe avocados, sugar, and milk or yogurt
  • Mint lemonade: a tangy and cool drink made with fresh lemon juice, sugar, and mint leaves

Where to Stay in Rabat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rabat offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Rabat include:

  • Sofitel Rabat Jardin des Roses: a luxurious hotel with beautiful gardens, swimming pool, spa, and fitness center
  • Riad Kalaa: a charming guesthouse located in the heart of the Medina, featuring traditional architecture and modern amenities
  • Villa Mandarine: a boutique hotel set in a lush garden, with a heated pool, spa, and restaurant
  • Hotel Belere Rabat: a well-equipped hotel located near the train station and other attractions, with a rooftop bar and pool
  • Auberge de la Paix: a cozy and affordable hostel located in the Kasbah of the Udayas, with free breakfast and Wi-Fi

What to Buy in Rabat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rabat has a vibrant market scene, with shops selling handmade crafts, leather goods, jewelry, and spices. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back from Rabat include:

  • Moroccan rugs and carpets: beautiful and colorful rugs and carpets that are handwoven by skilled artisans
  • Pottery and ceramics: unique and intricate clay and ceramic products, including plates, bowls, and tea sets
  • Leather goods: high-quality leather bags, shoes, and belts, often made with traditional techniques
  • Spices and herbs: flavorful and aromatic spices and herbs such as saffron, cumin, and coriander, often sold in colorful markets

Local Culture and Customs of Rabat

Morocco is a Muslim country, and it is important to respect the local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind when visiting Rabat include:

  • Dress modestly: Morocco is a conservative country, and it is important to dress respectfully, especially when visiting religious sites or public places
  • Avoid public displays of affection: physical contact between unmarried couples is not commonly seen in Morocco, and it is best to avoid it in public
  • Tip appropriately: tipping is expected in restaurants, hotels, and other service industries, and the standard rate is around 10%
  • Greet people politely: Moroccans value politeness and hospitality, and it is customary to greet people with “salam alaikum” (peace be upon you) and respond with “wa alaikum salam” (and peace be upon you too)

Best Time to Visit Rabat

The best time to visit Rabat is from March to May or September to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers in Rabat can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy. Ramadan, a month-long religious holiday that involves fasting during the day, can be an interesting time to visit Rabat, but some attractions and restaurants may be closed during the day.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rabat

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Rabat:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Learn some Arabic or French phrases to communicate with locals
  • Bargain at markets and souks to get the best deals

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, always opt for bottled water
  • Take photos of people without asking for their permission first
  • Ignore local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites

Safety and Health

Rabat is a relatively safe city, but some precautions should be taken to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Rabat

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place and avoid leaving them unattended
  • Use licensed taxis or public transportation, and avoid walking alone at night
  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert in crowded places

Health in Rabat

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid food from street vendors
  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as well as any necessary medications
  • Consider purchasing travel insurance before your trip

Getting to Rabat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rabat is easily accessible by plane, train, or bus, and it has a well-connected public transportation network.

Airports Near Rabat

  • Rabat-Salé International Airport: located about 10 km from the city center, with flights to and from major European cities and some African cities

Bus Stations Near Rabat

  • Rabat Central Bus Station: located in the city center, with frequent buses to and from other Moroccan cities
  • Rabat Agdal Bus Station: located in the south of the city, with buses to and from other Moroccan cities and some international destinations

Other Options

  • Train: Rabat has two train stations, Rabat Ville and Rabat Agdal, with frequent trains to and from major Moroccan cities and some international destinations
  • Car rental: rental cars are available at the airport and other locations in the city
  • Taxi: licensed taxis are available throughout the city, and Uber is also available in Rabat

How to Get Around in Rabat

Rabat has a well-connected public transportation system, and it is easy to get around by tram, bus, or taxi. Walking and biking are also great options for exploring the city, especially in the historic Medina and Kasbah.

Where to Go Next from Rabat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rabat is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and taking day excursions. Some of the best options include:

  • Casablanca: Morocco’s largest city, located about 90 km from Rabat, with its famous mosques, shopping centers, and movie theaters
  • Fes: a historic city with a stunning Medina and ancient attractions, located about 200 km from Rabat
  • Chefchaouen: a picturesque blue-painted town nestled in the Rif Mountains, located about 300 km from Rabat
  • El Jadida: a coastal city with a historic fortress and beautiful beaches, located about 100 km from Rabat

Conclusion

Rabat is a fascinating city that offers a mix of historic, cultural, and natural attractions, as well as plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and culinary experiences. Whether you are solo traveling or with family, Rabat is a perfect destination to explore the charm of Morocco. This comprehensive guide provides all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Rabat, from transportation to safety and health tips, to suggested activities and itineraries. Happy travels!
