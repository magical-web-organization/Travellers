Introduction to Asilah

Located on the North Atlantic Coast of Morocco, Asilah is a charming coastal town that offers a blend of Moroccan and European cultures. Its picturesque whitewashed houses and blue-painted walls make it a favorite destination for those seeking a quieter and less touristy atmosphere than nearby Tangier or Chefchaouen. Asilah is home to a rich history, with much of its ancient medina wall and traditional way of life still preserved. Visitors can also enjoy stunning beaches, natural scenery, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Asilah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Raisoul Palace Museum: Built in the 19th century by a Moroccan nobleman, this museum showcases traditional Moroccan art and artifacts.
  • Kamra d’Art Modern et Contemporain: This art gallery is a must-visit for art lovers, featuring contemporary Moroccan and international art exhibitions.
  • Al-Kamra Tower: This 15th-century tower is one of the primary landmarks of Asilah and offers stunning views of the city and coastline.
  • Skala de la Ville: This fortified sea wall dates back to the Portuguese occupation of Asilah in the 15th and 16th centuries and is a symbol of the city’s history.

Natural Attractions of Asilah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paradise Beach: This beach is located three kilometers south of Asilah’s medina and is considered one of the best beaches in the area.
  • Caves of Hercules: These natural caves, 14 kilometers north of Asilah, are believed to be where the Greek god Hercules rested during his travels.
  • Cap Spartel: Visit the lighthouse at the northwestern-most point of Africa and enjoy breathtaking views where the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean sea meet.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Asilah

Asilah is surrounded by idyllic countryside ideal for exploring by foot or bicycle. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Tala’b al-Sadiyyin: a 2-hour walk from Asilah, this trail passes through forests and small villages.
  • Cap Spartel to Asilah: This 7 km bike ride or hike takes about two hours to complete, and offers views of the Mediterranean and Atlantic oceans.
  • Lixus: Located 30 km outside Asilah, this ancient city rests on a hill and has great hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Asilah: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Asilah from strolling through the medina to trying your hand at some water sports. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Water sports: Go windsurfing or kiteboarding at Paradise beach where the wind is suitable, or go kayaking at Elborj lagoon in Sidi Mghait.
  • Walking tours: Guided walking tours through the medina are available, offering insight into daily life and the rich cultural heritage of Asilah.
  • Watch the sunset: Go down to the port or to Al Kamra Tower to witness the beautiful sunset that illuminates the Mediterranean Sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Asilah

Day 1: Arrival and exploring the medina
Day 2: Visit Raisoul Palace Museum and Kamra d’Art Modern et Contemporain
Day 3: Visit Paradise Beach, the Caves of Hercules, and Cap Spartel
Day 4: Hiking/Biking on Lixus
Day 5: Explore Elborj lagoon in Sidi Mghait
Day 6: Relax on the beach, walk around the city
Day 7: Visit Al Kamra tower, shopping, and watch the sunset by the port

Food and Drink Scene of Asilah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: A classic Moroccan dish featuring meat or vegetables, cooked with spices and served with couscous.
  • Harira: A thick soup made of lamb, lentils, chickpeas, coriander, and tomatoes, commonly eaten during Ramadan.
  • Pastilla: A flaky pie filled with spiced meat or seafood and almonds, dusted in powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea: A traditional Moroccan tea, served with fresh mint and sugar.
  • Araq: A locally-produced anise-flavored liquor, served with cold water.
  • Berber Whiskey: Made from figs, this sweet liqueur is commonly found in Asilah and the surrounding area.

Where to Stay in Asilah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Asilah offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended options:

  • Hotel Al Khaima: Located near the medina wall, this four-star hotel offers air-conditioned rooms with balcony views of the ocean.
  • Moroccan House Hotel Asilah: A traditional Moroccan-style guesthouse that features a courtyard, rooftop terrace with ocean views, and a communal lounge.
  • Dar Afram: A boutique guesthouse located in the heart of the medina, offering cozy, individually decorated rooms and a terrace with sea views.

What to Buy in Asilah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Leather products: Asilah is known for its high-quality leather goods, including bags, shoes, and wallets.
  • Ceramics: The medina’s souks offer a range of ceramics and pottery, including brightly colored plates and bowls.
  • Textiles: Handwoven scarves and blankets are a popular souvenir to bring home from Asilah.

Local Culture and Customs of Asilah

Asilah’s culture is a mix of traditional Moroccan and European influences. Visitors should dress modestly, especially when entering religious sites. When wandering through the medina, respect the privacy of locals, ask before taking photographs, and be prepared to barter when shopping.

Best Time to Visit Asilah

The best time to visit Asilah is from April to June or from mid-September to November, when the weather is mild and sunny, and hotels are less expensive than in peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Asilah

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take time to relax on the beach.

Don’t:

  • Visit religious sites during prayer times.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Ignore the local customs when bargaining in the souks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Asilah

Asilah is generally a safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution and take necessary precautions in crowded areas and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Asilah

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and to carry any necessary prescriptions with them. Sunscreen and bug spray are also essential.

Getting to Asilah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Asilah

-The nearest airport to Asilah is Tangier-Ibn Battouta Airport (TNG), located 45 km to the northeast of the city.

Bus Stations Near Asilah

  • CTM and Supratours are the two main bus companies serving Asilah, and their buses run regularly through the city’s main bus stations.

Other Options

-Private taxis from Tangier or other nearby cities can also be arranged.

How to Get Around in Asilah

Walking is the most convenient way to explore the medina, but taxis are readily available for longer distances. Visitors can also rent bicycles by the day or week.

Where to Go Next from Asilah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tangier: A vibrant coastal city, only 45 km northeast of Asilah.
  • Chefchaouen: A charming blue-painted town, located two hours southeast of Asilah, known for its traditional markets and surrounding mountains.
  • Rabat: The Moroccan capital, located about two and a half hours south of Asilah, offers stunning architecture and historical sites, as well as modern attractions.

Conclusion

Asilah is a hidden gem in Morocco that offers a unique combination of history, culture, natural scenery, and adventure. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to this charming coastal town, from exploring the medina, to soaking up the sun on the pristine beaches, and leaving with cherished memories of Morocco.
