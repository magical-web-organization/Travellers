Introduction to North Atlantic Coast

The North Atlantic Coast of Morocco boasts stunning views of the blue ocean, golden beaches, and rocky cliffs. This region is a blend of traditional and modern culture, with authentic local experiences and adventurous outdoor activities. From the cultural attractions to the natural wonders, there is something for every traveler. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to the North Atlantic Coast of Morocco.

List of Places to Visit in North Atlantic Coast

  • Tangier
  • Asilah
  • Larache
  • Tetouan
  • Chefchaouen
  • Martil
  • Cabo Negro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The North Atlantic Coast has a rich history and a vibrant cultural scene. Here are some of the most significant cultural attractions:

  • Kasbah Museum in Tangier: A former residence of the Sultan, the museum offers a glimpse into the traditional Moroccan lifestyle and architecture.
  • Chez Hmad Gueddar Museum in Asilah: The museum is dedicated to showcasing the work of the Moroccan painter and artist Hmad Gueddar.
  • Archaeological Museum in Tetouan: The museum houses a collection of artifacts from the Roman and Islamic periods.
  • Spanish Mosque in the small town of Chefchaouen: With its unique blue and white colors, the mosque offers stunning views of the Rif Mountains and the town.
  • Church of San Francisco in Larache: A beautiful Spanish-style church built in the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of North Atlantic Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The North Atlantic Coast offers stunning natural landscapes and adventurous outdoor activities:

  • Hercules Cave in Tangier: A popular tourist spot with impressive natural formations and an abundance of history.
  • Paradise Beach in Asilah: A beautiful white sand beach, clear blue water, and stunning views.
  • Targha Lake in Tetouan: A serene green-blue lake surrounded by mountain ranges and lush forests.
  • Akchour Waterfalls in Chefchaouen: A series of cascading waterfalls and natural pools, perfect for swimming and hiking.
  • Cape Three Forks in Al Hoceima: Offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Atlantic Coast

The North Atlantic Coast has several excellent hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Rif Mountains in Chefchaouen: A popular spot for hiking and trekking with scenic mountain views
  • Oued Laou Valley in Tetouan: A beautiful valley with a river and green fields to explore on foot or bike
  • Talassemtane National Park in Chefchaouen: A nature reserve with trails for hiking and biking, offering a chance to see native wildlife and plants.

Best Things to Do in North Atlantic Coast: Activities and Tours

  • Go surfing in Taghazout: A popular surf spot for beginners and experienced surfers
  • Take a boat tour to the nearby island in Al Hoceima
  • Explore the colorful markets and souks in Chefchaouen and Tangier
  • Attend a traditional musical performance or a folk festival in Tetouan
  • Enjoy authentic Moroccan cuisine in the many restaurants and cafes in the region

Week-Long Itinerary for North Atlantic Coast

Day 1: Arrive in Tangier, explore the Kasbah, and wander through the Medina. Day 2: Visit the archaeological museum in Tetouan and go hiking in the Rif Mountains. Day 3: Visit the town of Asilah and check out the Chez Hmad Gueddar museum and the Paradise beach. Day 4: Travel to Chefchaouen and attend a traditional musical performance.
Day 5: Go hiking to the Akchour waterfalls and take a dip in the natural pools. Day 6: Relax on the beach in Al Hoceima and take a boat tour to the nearby island. Day 7: Shop at the colorful markets and souks in Tangier and Tetouan before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of North Atlantic Coast

The North Atlantic Coast is known for its delicious Moroccan dishes, fresh seafood, and sweet mint tea. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: A classic Moroccan stew cooked with vegetables, meat, and spices.
  • Couscous: A traditional dish made with tiny steamed balls of semolina, accompanied by vegetables and meat.
  • Pastilla: A sweet-savory pie filled with chicken or fish, almonds, eggs, and spices.
  • Seafood Paella: A Spanish-Moroccan fusion dish with fragrant rice and a variety of seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: A sweet and refreshing tea made with fresh mint leaves and sugar, served in small glasses.
  • Fruit juices: Fresh juices made with seasonal fruits like orange, strawberry, and apricot.
  • Sidi Ali Water: A purified mineral water brand produced in Morocco.

Where to Stay in North Atlantic Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The North Atlantic Coast offers a range of accommodation options to fit every budget and travel style:

  • Luxury hotels: Several luxury hotels offer excellent amenities like swimming pools, spas, and beachfront views like the Sofitel Tamuda Bay Beach & Spa in Tetouan.
  • Budget hotels and hostels: Several budget-friendly options are available, such as the Tangier City Center Hostel in Tangier.
  • Guesthouses and Riads: Experience the traditional Moroccan hospitality in the locally owned guesthouses and riads like the Dar Ouedghiri in Asilah.

What to Buy in North Atlantic Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Ceramics: You can buy hand-painted bowls, plates, and vases, commonly produced in Morocco, at the local souks.
  • Argan Oil: Hailed as “liquid gold,” argan oil is a famous Moroccan beauty product used in cosmetics and cooking.
  • Leather Products: Leather bags, belts, and shoes are famous traditional Moroccan products that can be purchased in the local markets in Tangier, Tetouan, and Asilah.

Local Culture and Customs of North Atlantic Coast

The majority of the population in the North Atlantic Coast is Muslim, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Alcohol is served in most hotels and restaurants, but it is not a common drink in most households. It is not uncommon for Moroccans to greet with a handshake and a kiss on each cheek, which is more common between people of the same gender. Respect the local dress codes for visiting religious sites, and cover arms and legs.

Best Time to Visit North Atlantic Coast

The North Atlantic Coast of Morocco is a year-round destination, with a mild climate that allows visitors to enjoy outdoor activities throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is in the spring and autumn seasons, from March to May and September to November, respectively. During these months, the weather is mild with fewer crowds, making it easy to explore the region at a relaxed pace.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Atlantic Coast

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and in public places.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local Moroccan cuisine, particularly the Tajine, pastilla, and couscous.
  • Bargain at the local markets and souks, but be fair and respectful.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water as it is not potable.
  • Carry large amounts of cash in public.
  • Disrespect the local dress codes or customs.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Morocco is considered a relatively safe country for travelers, but visitors should take the necessary precautions to avoid any incidents. Follow general safety tips like not leaving valuables unattended and locking hotel room doors when going out. Visitors should have all regular vaccination shots before entering the country or consult their healthcare provider for other recommendations. Additionally, visitors should bring adequate sun protection, particularly sunblock for outdoor activities, and stay hydrated.

Getting to North Atlantic Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

The following options are available for traveling to the North Atlantic Coast:

Airports Near North Atlantic Coast

  • Tangier Ibn Battouta Airport: Located in Tangier, it is the nearest airport to the North Atlantic Coast, only 2.5 km away.
  • Tetouan-Sania Ramel Airport: Located in Tetouan, the airport is 25 km away from Tangier.

Bus Stations Near North Atlantic Coast

  • CTM Tangier bus station: Located in Tangier, it is the main bus station, and you can take buses to Tetouan and Chefchaouen.
  • Other smaller bus stations are located throughout the cities and towns in the region, such as Asilah Bus Station, Larache Bus Station, and Tetouan Bus Station.

Other Options

  • High-speed train: You can take the ONCF train from Casablanca or Rabat to Tangier and then travel by bus or taxi to the other cities in the North Atlantic Coast.
  • Taxi: You can hire a taxi from the airport or the train station, which is a convenient and comfortable option to travel around the region.

How to Get Around in the North Atlantic Coast

  • Bus: Local buses operate in most cities and towns, and you can buy tickets at the bus stations or the driver.
  • Taxi: Taxis are a convenient way to explore the region, and visitors can hail them from the streets or book them through apps like Uber or Careem.

Where to Go Next from North Atlantic Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rabat: The capital of Morocco, situated 272 km from Tangier, is known for its historical monuments, palaces, and museums.
  • Fes: One of the largest historic cities in the world, Fes is about 300 km from Tangier and is renowned for its old town charm and Medieval Islamic architecture.
  • Marrakech: The vibrant and colorful city of Marrakech is around 700 km from Tangier and is famous for its vibrant souks, gardens, and historical monuments.

Conclusion

The North Atlantic Coast of Morocco is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the African continent. From its beautiful beaches and natural landscapes to the rich cultural heritage, the region has many unique experiences to offer. Plan your trip to this beautiful region today and discover Morocco’s true beauty.
