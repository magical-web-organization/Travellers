Introduction to High Atlas

Located in Morocco, North Africa, High Atlas is a mountain range known for its picturesque landscapes, rich culture, and historical significance. The range spans over 400 kilometers and is home to several traditional Berber villages, ancient kasbahs, and scenic hiking trails.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you on a journey through the charm and beauty of High Atlas, highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in High Atlas

Whether you’re interested in culture, history, or nature, High Atlas has something to offer for every traveler. Here are some of the top places to visit in High Atlas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ait Benhaddou: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a legendary fortified village located at the foothills of the High Atlas Mountains. It is a popular filming location for Hollywood movies like Lawrence of Arabia, Gladiator, and Game of Thrones.

  • Kasbah Telouet: This 18th-century palace was the residence of the famous Glaoui family, once powerful lords of the pre-colonial Moroccan empire.

  • Toubkal National Park: The park is home to the highest peak in North Africa, Mount Toubkal. It also boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna, including the endangered Barbary macaque.

Natural Attractions of High Atlas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ourika Valley: A lush green valley with clear streams, waterfalls, and terraced gardens. It is perfect for hiking, trekking, and exploring the Berber villages.

  • Tizi n’Test Pass: This scenic pass connects Marrakesh with Taroudant and is famous for its stunning views of the Atlas Mountains.

  • Skiing in Oukaimeden: Oukaimeden is the highest ski resort in Africa, located at an altitude of 3,200 meters. It offers skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of High Atlas

High Atlas is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its diverse terrain, rugged trails, and off-the-beaten-path routes. Here are some of the must-try hiking and biking routes in High Atlas:

  • Toubkal Summit: This is the highest peak in North Africa, and its ascent is a popular challenge for many hikers. The trek takes about two days, and the breathtaking views from the summit are worth the effort.

  • M’goun Circuit: This circular route takes you through the M’goun Massif, the second-highest range in Morocco. You’ll pass through olive groves, Berber villages, and valleys.

  • Tizi n’Tagharat Pass: This is a challenging mountain bike route that takes you through rocky terrain and steep descents. It is not for the faint-hearted, but the thrill of the adventure is worth it.

Best Things to Do in High Atlas: Activities and Tours

High Atlas offers a diverse range of activities and tours, catering to every traveler’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do in High Atlas:

  • Traditional Berber Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Berber dishes with the locals, using local ingredients and traditional methods.

  • Camel Trekking: Explore the desert landscapes of High Atlas on a camelback with a guide. You’ll witness stunning sunsets and have dinner under the stars.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy a bird’s eye view of the Atlas Mountains and its surroundings on a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for High Atlas

To make the most of your trip to High Atlas, here’s a week-long itinerary that covers the best of the region:

Day Activity
1 Explore Marrakesh and visit the Bahia Palace and Djemaa el-Fna Square.
2 Travel to Ouarzazate and visit the Kasbah Ait Benhaddou.
3 Trek to the summit of Mount Toubkal.
4 Head to Ourika Valley and explore its waterfalls and gardens.
5 Visit Telouet Kasbah and travel to the Dades Valley.
6 Explore the Rose Valley and Skoura Oasis.
7 Return to Marrakesh and spend the day at leisure or do some shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of High Atlas

Moroccan cuisine is famous for its flavorful spices, savory tagines, and sweet pastries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages when visiting High Atlas:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: A classic Moroccan stew cooked in a clay pot with meat or vegetables.

  • Couscous: A staple dish made with steamed semolina, vegetables, and meat.

  • Pastilla: A sweet and savory pie made with marinated pigeon or chicken, almonds, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: This is a refreshing tea made with fresh mint leaves and a generous amount of sugar.

  • Argan Oil: This oil is extracted from the argan tree native to Morocco and has several health benefits.

Where to Stay in High Atlas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

High Atlas offers several accommodation options, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in High Atlas:

  • Kasbah du Toubkal: This is a luxurious eco-lodge located in the heart of the Atlas Mountains. It offers stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Hotel Tigmi Ozro: This is a charming guesthouse located in the Berber village of Ait Bouguemez. It offers cozy rooms and excellent food.

  • Riad Zitoune Jdid: This is a budget-friendly riad located in the Marrakesh medina. It offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the city’s attractions.

What to Buy in High Atlas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

High Atlas offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts or mementos. Here are some of the best things to buy in High Atlas:

  • Rugs and Carpets: Berber carpets and rugs are famous for their intricate designs and high quality.

  • Argan Oil: This oil is a popular beauty product and has several health benefits.

  • Pottery and Ceramics: High Atlas is known for its pottery and ceramic wares, including intricate tagine dishes and tea sets.

Local Culture and Customs of High Atlas

High Atlas is home to the Berber people, who have a rich and vibrant culture. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting High Atlas:

  • Hospitality: The Berber people are known for their warm hospitality and generosity. It is common for them to invite visitors for tea and share a meal.

  • Berber Clothing: The traditional Berber clothing is colorful and intricate, with women wearing a headscarf and a kaftan and men wearing a woolen robe and a turban.

Best Time to Visit High Atlas

The best time to visit High Atlas is from March to May and from September to November. The weather is mild during these months, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, the peak season is from June to August, with hot temperatures and crowded tourist spots.

Do’s and Don’ts of High Atlas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting High Atlas:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress modestly, especially in rural areas and religious sites.

  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their permission.

  • Haggle aggressively in the markets.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting High Atlas:

Safety in High Atlas

  • Be vigilant of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.

  • Avoid isolated areas and unlit streets at night.

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and important documents.

Health in High Atlas

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid stomach illnesses.

  • Protect yourself from the sun and heat.

  • Pack a basic first-aid kit with essential medicines.

Getting to High Atlas: Transportation Options and Logistics

High Atlas is accessible by several modes of transportation, including air, road, and rail. Here are some ways to get to High Atlas:

Airports Near High Atlas

The nearest airports to High Atlas are Marrakesh Menara Airport and Ouarzazate Airport.

Bus Stations Near High Atlas

The main bus stations near High Atlas are Marrakesh Bus Station and Ouarzazate Bus Station.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car with a driver or join a guided tour.

How to Get Around in High Atlas

The best way to get around High Atlas is by hiring a car with a driver or by joining a guided tour. Public transport in High Atlas is scarce, and the roads can be challenging to navigate.

Where to Go Next from High Atlas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

High Atlas is a gateway to several popular destinations in Morocco, including:

  • Marrakesh: A vibrant city known for its bustling souks, royal palaces, and lively performances.

  • Essaouira: A picturesque port city, famous for its sandy beaches, blue boats, and vibrant medina.

  • Sahara Desert: An otherworldly landscape of sand dunes, camels, and starry nights.

Conclusion

High Atlas is a hidden gem, waiting to be explored by adventurous travelers. From its scenic landscapes to its rich culture, High Atlas offers a unique and unforgettable experience. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your next trip to High Atlas and makes it an enjoyable and memorable one.
