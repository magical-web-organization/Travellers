Introduction to Zaouiat Ahansal

Located in the High Atlas Mountains of Morocco, Zaouiat Ahansal is a charming village that offers a perfect blend of culture, natural beauty, and adventure. The town is accessible only on foot or by mule, which adds to its allure. Visitors get to experience the traditional way of life and interact with friendly locals while enjoying breathtaking landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Zaouiat Ahansal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zawiya Ahansal Mosque: This is the village’s oldest mosque and a symbol of its religious and cultural history.
  • Maison de la Photographie: The museum showcases old photographs of Morocco, demonstrating the town’s history and the surrounding area.
  • Kasbah Ait Boujane: A historic palace built-in 1735 by the Berkouk tribe, it is worth exploring to soak up the cultural and architectural heritage of the town.
  • Ighrem N’Ougdal: A fortified granary that tells the story of how the locals have preserved their edible crops.

Natural Attractions of Zaouiat Ahansal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tassa Ouirgane National Park: A must-visit for nature enthusiasts who can enjoy hikes, freshwater streams, and waterfalls.
  • Lake Aguelmame Sidi Ali: A natural lake surrounded by mountains, this serene and breathtaking place is perfect for picnics and a swim.
  • Gorges of Anefgou: One of the area’s most stunning natural sites, it also serves as a gateway to other nearby villages and Berber communities.
  • Tizi N’Kerdous: A scenic mountain pass that offers panoramic views of the High Atlas Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zaouiat Ahansal

Zaouiat Ahansal provides numerous routes for trekkers and mountain bikers of all skill levels. You can choose a day hike or a multi-day trek with an experienced guide. Some popular trails include:

  • Toubkal Mountain Trek: Hike the highest mountain in North Africa, which provides unbeatable views of the Atlas range.
  • Siroua Trekking: A trek through diverse landscapes that includes the volcanic rocks of Jebel Siroua.
  • M’Goun Traverse: A challenging trek for experienced hikers, but the magnificent landscapes and panoramic views make it worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Zaouiat Ahansal: Activities and Tours

  • Berber Homestay: A perfect way to experience the Berber way of life by staying with a local family in their home.
  • Traditional Hammam: A locally-operated hammam facility that offers a fantastic experience of the local beauty and wellness culture.
  • Cooking class: Learn to cook traditional Moroccan food over a wood-burning stove with local ingredients and spices.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: See the High Atlas from a unique perspective with a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Zaouiat Ahansal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tajine: The signature Moroccan dish prepared in a cone shape pot. It incorporates a variety of spiced meats or vegetables slow-cooked to create flavorful stews.
  • Couscous: A staple dish in Moroccan culture, it is traditionally served on Fridays, consisting of steamed semolina, meat, and vegetables.
  • Tagraout: A hearty soup made from wheat, chickpeas or peas, and lamb, making it perfect for colder days.
  • Msemen: A doughy square-shaped flatbread folded several times and cooked on a griddle.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: Also known as ‘Moroccan Whiskey’ served in a glass teapot with mint, green tea, and sugar.
  • Argan Oil: A culinary curiosity that is obtained from local fruit and used in salads and bread.
  • Berber Whiskey: A non-alcoholic drink made from natural herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Zaouiat Ahansal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dar El Hana: A delightful guest house with comfortable rooms and excellent service, it offers views of the mountains and is perfect for couples.
  • Kasbah du Toubkal: A boutique hotel at the base of the High Atlas Mountains, it offers an authentic Moroccan experience of food, tradition, and culture.
  • Ait Ben Moro: A traditional Berber house turned into a guest house, offering a quiet atmosphere and exciting food options for visitors.

What to Buy in Zaouiat Ahansal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Berber Rugs and Carpets: Made by local weavers using traditional techniques, this is a must-have souvenir as it portrays the Berber culture.
  • Berber Jewelry: Handmade silver jewelry like necklaces, bracelets, and earrings are fashionable souvenirs of the Berber culture.
  • Argan Oil: Pure argan oil is a valuable souvenir that is used in beauty products for the hair and skin.
  • Spices and Herbs: Local spice markets are full of exotic spices and local herbs that can add an authentic touch to home cooking.

Local Culture and Customs of Zaouiat Ahansal

Respectful behavior is essential to the locals. Stereotyping, open display of affection between couples, and revealing clothing are considered rude and discouraged. Visitors should dress appropriately, cover their heads in religious sites, and ask for permission before taking pictures.

Best Time to Visit Zaouiat Ahansal

The best time to visit Zaouiat Ahansal is from March to May and in October-November due to a conducive weather environment.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zaouiat Ahansal

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures.
  • Try some of the local dishes and drinks.
  • Greet the locals with a ‘Salam’ and a smile.
  • Carry a valid passport and other travel documents.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Show public affection.
  • Dress in revealing clothing.
  • Wisecrack or disrespect the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zaouiat Ahansal

Zaouiat Ahansal is a relatively safe village with very few reports of crime. Visitors must take the usual traveling precautions and keep an eye on their belongings while in crowded areas.

Health in Zaouiat Ahansal

Visitors must ensure they get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and Rabies. Hiring experienced guides and choosing safe food and drink options also helps maintain health while in the town.

Getting to Zaouiat Ahansal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zaouiat Ahansal

The closest airport to Zaouiat Ahansal is Marrakesh Menara International Airport, about 4 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Zaouiat Ahansal

Visitors can take a bus from Marrakech to Beni Mellal. From there, they can take a second bus to Zaouiat Ahansal, which is about 2 hours away.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a private taxi, rent a car or a mule to get to the town.

How to Get Around in Zaouiat Ahansal

The only mode of transport in Zaouiat Ahansal is by foot or mule. Visitors can choose to walk or rent a mule to visit different tourist attractions in the town.

Where to Go Next from Zaouiat Ahansal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marrakech: Morocco’s vibrant city with a blend of modern and traditional lifestyle.
  • Imlil: A village at the foot of the Toubkal Mountain.
  • Essaouira: A charming coastal town known for its seafood, surfing and beach experience.
  • Sahara Desert: A must-do excursion that may take a few days and offers a once-in-a-lifetime desert experience.

Conclusion

Zaouiat Ahansal is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path experience. The town offers a perfect blend of culture, natural beauty, and adventure, making it the perfect getaway from your daily routine. A trip to Zaouiat Ahansal is an unforgettable and worthwhile experience you will always cherish.
