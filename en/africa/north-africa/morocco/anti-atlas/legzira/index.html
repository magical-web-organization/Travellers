Introduction to Legzira

Legzira is a picturesque coastal town located in the Anti Atlas of Morocco. The town is famous for its natural rock arches, reddish cliffs, and stunning beaches. It’s a perfect destination for adventure seekers, beach lovers, and culture enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, and local culture and customs of Legzira.

List of Places to Visit in Legzira

Below is a list of top attractions and must-visit places in Legzira:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dar Souiri: It’s a historical building that used to be a residence for wealthy merchants. Now it’s converted into a cultural center and museum.
  • Sidi Ifni: It’s a colonial town with numerous old buildings and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Legzira: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Legzira Beach: The main attraction of Legzira is the Legzira beach, characterized by the red cliffs and natural rock arches.
  • Paradise Valley: A stunning natural attraction, which is a series of canyons, waterfalls, and natural pools.
  • Imouzzer Waterfalls: A beautiful waterfall that streams down from the High Atlas Mountains.
  • Anti Atlas Mountains: A great destination for hiking, trekking, and exploring the traditional Berber villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Legzira

Legzira is a perfect location for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Below are some of the popular routes:

  • Legzira to Sidi Ifni: It’s a coastal route that takes you through different beaches and stunning landscapes. The distance is around 8 km.
  • Tafraoute to Legzira: A mountainous route that is perfect for experienced hikers. The distance is around 16 km.

Best Things to Do in Legzira: Activities and Tours

  • Quad Biking: Explore the sand dunes and enjoy the adrenaline rush with a quad biking tour.
  • Surfing: Legzira’s beach is a great destination for surfers. Enroll in a surfing lesson or rent a surfboard and hit the waves.
  • Camel Trek: Take a camel ride along the beach and the sand dunes.
  • Fishing: Legzira is an ideal destination for fishing. You can try shore fishing or deep-sea fishing tours.

Here’s a 7-day itinerary for Legzira:

Food and Drink Scene of Legzira

No travel guide is complete without exploring local foods and drinks. Legzira has a diverse food & drink scene. Below are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: A traditional Moroccan dish cooked in a clay pot with meat, vegetables, and spices.
  • Moroccan Mint Tea: A popular hot beverage served with fresh mint, sugar, and green tea.
  • Couscous: A dish made with steamed semolina, vegetables, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Argan Oil: A healthy and unique oil extracted from the argan tree. It’s used in cosmetics, cooking, and dressing.
  • Alcoholic Beverages: Morocco has several local drinks like Boga, Thakira, and Mazagran, which are made from wine and lemonade.

Where to Stay in Legzira: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Legzira has a range of accommodation options that cater to different travel preferences and budgets. Some of the popular choices include:

  • Riad Dar Najmat: A guesthouse located in the center of Legzira beach with comfortable rooms and excellent Arabian decor.
  • Aftas Trip Hostel: A budget-friendly option with views of the Atlantic Ocean and close proximity to Legzira Beach.
  • Ayt Oumghar: A traditional Berber guesthouse located in the mountains with stunning views and hiking trails.

What to Buy in Legzira: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Morocco is famous for its traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. Some popular items include:

  • Argan Products: Argan oil, soap, and cosmetics are popular products that you can bring back home.
  • Moroccan Carpets: Colorful and traditional carpets made with wool or silk.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: Handmade pottery and ceramics with unique patterns and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Legzira

Morocco is a predominantly Muslim country with a rich culture and customs. Here are some useful tips to respect local norms and culture:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques or religious places.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or religious places.
  • Avoid public display of affection.

Best Time to Visit Legzira

The best time to visit Legzira is from March to May and from September to November. The weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds. The daytime temperature ranges from 20 to 30°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Legzira

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling to Legzira:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Carry a scarf or shawl to cover your head or shoulders when visiting religious places.
  • Bargain while shopping at the souks to get the best price.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water or eat uncooked food.
  • Wear revealing clothes or shorts in public places.
  • Enter mosques or religious places with shoes.

Safety and Health

Legzira is a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, it’s always better to be cautious and take preventive measures.

Safety in Legzira

  • Avoid traveling to secluded areas alone or after dark.
  • Keep your valuables and documents in a safe place.
  • Always carry some cash and a copy of essential documents like a passport and visa.

Health in Legzira

  • Drink bottled water and use a water purifier when necessary.
  • Use sunscreen and mosquito repellent when outdoors.
  • Get vaccinated for hepatitis A and typhoid.

Getting to Legzira: Transportation Options and Logistics

Legzira is well-connected, and there are several options to get there.

Airports Near Legzira

  • Agadir-Al Massira Airport: The nearest airport to Legzira is located 90 km away. Several international airlines serve this airport, including Air Arabia, Ryanair, and Transavia.

Bus Stations Near Legzira

  • Tiznit Bus Station: Tiznit is a town located 20 km away from Legzira. Several bus companies like Supratours and CTM serve this route.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: Legzira is easily accessible by car or taxi from nearby towns like Tiznit or Mirleft.

How to Get Around in Legzira

Exploring Legzira can be done on foot or by taxi. It’s advisable to rent a car or motorbike if you plan to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Legzira: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions from Legzira that are worth exploring:

  • Sidi Ifni: A picturesque town located 30 km away from Legzira, famous for its fishing port and colonial buildings.
  • Taroudant: Located 160 km away from Legzira, it’s also known as the ‘Grandmother of Marrakech,’ famous for its souks and traditional handicrafts.
  • Essaouira: A coastal town located 218 km from Legzira, famous for its beaches, music, and traditional handicrafts.

Conclusion

Legzira is a beautiful and unique destination that offers a blend of natural attractions, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. It’s a perfect getaway for anyone looking for a peaceful and authentic experience in Morocco. Hopefully, this travel guide helps you plan your trip to Legzira and make the most of your time there. Happy Travels!
