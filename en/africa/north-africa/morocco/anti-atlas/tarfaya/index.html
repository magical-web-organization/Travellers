Welcome to Tarfaya, a charming coastal town in the southwestern part of Morocco, that offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Tarfaya is a historically and culturally rich place with a handful of must-see sights and activities. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, how to get there, and what to do to make the most out of your trip to Tarfaya Morocco.

Introduction to Tarfaya

Tarfaya is a small town that was initially founded as a Spanish trading post in the early 20th century. The town is best known for its heritage, architecture, and breathtaking nature reserves. Despite being a small settlement, the town offers lots of exciting places to visit and things to do.

List of Places to Visit in Tarfaya

There are plenty of things to see and do in Tarfaya, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Antoine de Saint-Exupery Museum - Tarfaya is famous for being the last stop of the famous French pilot before his mysterious disappearance. The museum features photographs, memorabilia and things related to Antoine de Saint-Exupery’s stay in Tarfaya.
  • The Spanish Fort - It is the iconic landmark of Tarfaya built-in 1928. The fort has been restored and now serves as a historical museum, showcasing the history and culture of Tarfaya.
  • The Sidi Mohamed Ben Abdellah Mausoleum - The mausoleum is a sacred place dedicated to the founder of Tarfaya, whose tomb is located inside. The place is a perfect spot for travelers to explore the local culture and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Tarfaya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laguna de la Muleyya - The lagoon is a serene nature reserve that is home to several bird species. Visitors can take a stroll through the scenic park and appreciate the breathtaking panoramic views of the sea and the lagoon from the observation deck.
  • The Cape Juby Beach - Gorgeous beach with white sand and amazing views of the coastline. It is a popular spot for activities like swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  • The Tarfaya Wind Farm - a unique wind farm that is strategically located on the western coast of Tarfaya. The wind turbines are immense and look stunning against the sunset sky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarfaya

Tarfaya is blessed with a stunning landscape that’s perfect for outdoor activities. The best routes for hiking and biking in Tarfaya are:

  • The Beach and Coastal Route - The route begins at the Cape Juby Beach and leads towards the wind farm. The trail has incredible views of the coastline, the Atlantic Ocean, and the town of Tarfaya.
  • The National Park Route - The route starts from the Laguna de la Muleyya and goes via the dunes towards the sea. The trail offers a panoramic view of the flora and fauna in the conservation area, and an opportunity to breathe fresh air and enjoy the serenity of nature.

Best Things to Do in Tarfaya: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Trekking - A popular outdoor activity in Tarfaya is camel trekking on the Sahara desert. Visitors can enjoy a unique experience and explore the vast desert and pristine sandy dunes.
  • Kitesurfing - Tarfaya’s location on the Atlantic coast allows for some excellent wind conditions, making kitesurfing a popular activity for tourists.
  • Fishing Tours - Fishing tours are available and give visitors the perfect opportunity to experience the region’s traditional fishing methods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarfaya

Here’s a week-long itinerary that includes all the must-see sights in Tarfaya:

Day 1: Visit the Antoine de Saint-Exupery Museum, learn about the town’s history and enjoy local cuisine.

Day 2: Explore the Sidi Mohamed Ben Abdellah Mausoleum then head to Cape Juby Beach, where you can participate in water sports like surfing and kiteboarding, and relax at the end of the day.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Tarfaya wind park and then visit the Spanish fort.

Day 4: Hiking, trekking, or biking on the National Park route.

Day 5: Take a fishing tour in the wide Atlantic ocean, where you can catch some fresh seafood or enjoy a BBQ while overlooking the shoreline.

Day 6: Visit the Laguna de la Muleyya nature conservancy and enjoy the scenic landscapes.

Day 7: Enjoy a free day and explore Tarfaya some more, visit the local markets for souvenirs and dine at local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarfaya

No trip is ever complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are the must-try dishes and beverages during your stay in Tarfaya.

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Tagine - Moroccan specialty, a stew made with vegetables, chicken or beef, and spices, cooked and served in a tagine pot.
  • Seafood platters - fresh catches from the Atlantic ocean, prepared either grilled or fried.
  • Harira - A traditional Moroccan soup made with lentils, chickpeas, vegetables, and meat or chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea - A famous hot tea brewed with fresh mint leaves and sugar, the perfect drink for a chilly morning in Tarfaya.
  • Bottle of Boissons gazeuses - a non-alcoholic carbonated drink that’s available in different flavours, and pairs well with local cuisine.

Where to Stay in Tarfaya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarfaya has an array of accommodation options that cater to every traveler’s needs, ranging from limited-budget hostels to luxurious guesthouses.

  • La Belle Vue Hotel - A charming, comfortable and neat hotel with large and nicely furnished rooms, great amenities, and a friendly staff.
  • Auberge Dar Albatros - Another high-end hotel with a restaurant and spacious rooms, perfect for relaxation and scenic views.
  • Tarfaya Surf and Kite Hostel - A budget and friendly hostel located near the beach, with affordable prices and great services.

What to Buy in Tarfaya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The city of Tarfaya is a treasure trove of unique and traditional handicrafts, local products, and interesting souvenirs. Below are some popular items you can consider buying;

  • Jewelry made from silver - For the perfect keepsake, buy locally elaborated silver jewelry from the traditional artisans.
  • Moroccan Traditional Dress - Kaftans and djellabas, the traditional Moroccan dress is a beautiful souvenir to take home and wear for special occasions.
  • Argan Oil - This cold-pressed oil is not only an excellent souvenir but also perfect for skincare, haircare, and cooking.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarfaya

Morocco, in general, has a diverse and rich culture, and Tarfaya is not any different. Here are some tips to know before travelling there:

  • Dress modestly, particularly when visiting religious buildings.
  • Greet the locals in their language - Tarfaya’s locals are known for their hospitality, and talking Aramaic or Arabic could help improve your connection with the locals.
  • Respect the culture - It’s important to respect the local culture, from their religion, to dress code, language and customs.

Best Time to Visit Tarfaya

Tarfaya experiences a Mediterranean climate with hot summers and relatively mild winters. The best time to visit is in the winter, between October and April, when the temperature is cool and comfortable for all the outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarfaya

Do:

  • Learn a few words of Arabic, Amazigh or Spanish
  • Dress modestly
  • Explore the Sahara Desert and National Parks

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their consent.
  • Get involved with drugs or illegal activities
  • Disrespect the locals’ culture and customs

Safety and Health

Tarfaya is known for its excellent weather and beautiful surroundings, but it’s always good to take precautions to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Tarfaya

  • Avoid isolated areas at night, and don’t walk alone around the town.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place, like your hotel room.
  • Take local transportation instead of using unlicensed taxis.

Health in Tarfaya

  • Ensure you pack any necessary medications you might need.
  • Drink only bottled water or boiled water, and avoid brushing your teeth with tap water.
  • Wash your hands with soap and water often.

Getting to Tarfaya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarfaya is accessible by road, air, or sea, and transportation is available from major Moroccan and international airports you can consider as below:

Airports Near Tarfaya

  • Tarfaya Aerodrome - the nearest airport which flies to and from several major cities on the map.

Bus Stations Near Tarfaya

  • CTM Tarfaya- The main bus station in town.

Other Options

Travellers can also hire a private car to Tarfaya or take a taxi from any other Moroccan city- like Agadir or Laayoune- to their destination.

How to Get Around in Tarfaya

Tarfaya is a small town, so most places are found conveniently within walking distance, but there are a few other forms of transportation available:

  • Taxis - Available for hire, the most convenient option for late nights or if you want to go slightly farther outside town.
  • Bicycle Rental - Rental bikes that are generally available in most guesthouses, hotels, and accommodations.

Where to Go Next from Tarfaya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Laayoune - A nearby coastal city with stunning beaches and traditional markets.
  • Western Sahara - known for its striking landscapes and powerful national heritage
  • Dakhla - A charming port city on the Atlantic coast with excellent seafood and water sports opportunities.

Conclusion

Tarfaya is a hidden gem in Morocco, boasting an array of natural sights and cultural landmarks. From outdoor adventures, picturesque sunsets to traditional foods and handicrafts, Tarfaya has it all. With this ultimate guide, you’re well equipped to journey to Tarfaya and experience everything it has to offer. Have a pleasant and memorable trip!
