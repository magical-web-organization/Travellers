Introduction to Anti Atlas

Located in southern Morocco, Anti Atlas is a stunning mountain range that stretches over 500 km from the Atlantic coast to the Sahara Desert. This region is known for its spectacular landscapes, fascinating culture, and outdoor adventures that attract a growing number of visitors every year.

In this travel guide, we will cover all the essential information you need to know to plan your trip to Anti Atlas. From the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and eat, and more, read on to discover everything about this unique corner of Morocco.

List of Places to Visit in Anti Atlas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tafraoute: A picturesque town located in the heart of Anti Atlas that is famous for its distinctive pink granite boulders and traditional Berber architecture.
  • Aït Benhaddou: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that was once an important stop on the ancient caravan route that linked Marrakech and the Sahara Desert.
  • Taroudant: A fortified city that dates back to the 16th century and is known for its beautiful gardens, historic city walls, and traditional souks.
  • Tiznit: A charming town that is often called the “Silver City” because of its impressive jewelry industry.
  • Agadir: Located on the Atlantic coast, Agadir is a modern city that offers beautiful beaches, a vibrant nightlife, and excellent surfing opportunities.

Natural Attractions of Anti Atlas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dades Gorge: A spectacular canyon that is a popular destination for hiking and rock-climbing enthusiasts.
  • Toubkal National Park: Home to the highest peak in North Africa, Toubkal, this national park is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor adventurers.
  • Draa Valley: A vast palm grove that runs from the Anti Atlas to the Sahara Desert and is dotted with small traditional Berber villages and ancient kasbahs.
  • Zagora: Located at the edge of the Sahara Desert, this town is known for its beautiful desert landscapes, ancient ruins, and traditional mud-brick architecture.
  • Sidi Ifni: A coastal town that offers beautiful beaches, excellent surfing opportunities, and a laid-back atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anti Atlas

  • Jebel Siroua Trek: A challenging circular trek that takes you through the beautiful landscapes of the Siroua Massif range.
  • Toubkal Summit Trek: A popular trek that takes you to the summit of Toubkal, the highest peak in North Africa.
  • Dades Valley Trek: A stunning trek that takes you through the Dades Gorges and the incredible Todra Gorge.
  • The Kasbah Trail: A scenic bike tour that takes you through traditional Berber villages and historic kasbahs.
  • Anti Atlas MTB Challenge: An annual mountain biking event that takes place in the stunning Anti Atlas mountains, attracting bikers from all around the world.

Best Things to Do in Anti Atlas: Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon ride: Enjoy a breathtaking bird’s eye view of the stunning Anti Atlas mountain range and the Sahara Desert.
  • Moroccan cooking class: Learn how to cook traditional Moroccan dishes using local ingredients and spices.
  • Camel trekking: Embark on a camel trek through the Sahara Desert and experience the nomadic way of life.
  • Beach horseback riding: Enjoy a horseback riding experience on the beautiful beaches of Agadir.
  • Surfing lessons: Learn how to surf on the Atlantic coast’s waves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anti Atlas

Day 1: Arrive in Marrakech and take a 4-hour drive to Tafraoute, where you will spend the night.

Day 2: Explore Tafraoute and the surrounding area, including the pink granite boulders and traditional Berber architecture.

Day 3: Drive to the Dades Gorge and spend the day hiking in the stunning canyon.

Day 4: Visit the town of Ouarzazate and the Ait Benhaddou UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Day 5: Trek to the summit of Toubkal, the highest peak in North Africa.

Day 6: Enjoy a camel trek through the Sahara Desert to Zagora.

Day 7: Spend the day in Agadir relaxing on the beach and exploring the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Anti Atlas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine: A traditional Moroccan stew made with meat, vegetables, and spices.
  • Couscous: A dish made with steamed semolina and served with a variety of vegetables and meat.
  • Harira: A hearty soup that is often served during Ramadan and other special occasions.
  • Pastilla: A sweet and savory pie that is usually filled with meat and almonds and dusted with powdered sugar.
  • Mint tea: A traditional Moroccan tea that is brewed with fresh mint and served with sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Sahara beer: A popular Moroccan beer that is brewed using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Argan oil: A nutty oil that is used in Moroccan cuisine for cooking and drizzling over salads.
  • Berber whisky: A non-alcoholic drink made from mint and other herbs.

Where to Stay in Anti Atlas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kasbah Tizimi: A boutique style hotel in Taroudant that is famously known for its beautiful traditional architecture.
  • Kasbah Igoudar: A luxury Moroccan riad style hotel in the Tizi N Tichka region with an outdoor pool and stunning scenery.
  • Dar Sofar: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Ait Ben Haddou, with beautiful views of the Kasbah and the surrounding mountains.
  • Dar Jnane: A stylish guesthouse in Tafraoute with gorgeous views of the Anti Atlas Mountains and a swimming pool.
  • Crocro Deal: A budget-friendly hostel in Zagora that offers comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace with beautiful views.

What to Buy in Anti Atlas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rugs and textiles: Handwoven Berber rugs and textiles are a popular purchase in Anti Atlas.
  • Argan oil: A popular cosmetic product that is made from the nuts of the argan tree, which grows in the region.
  • Pottery and ceramics: Handmade ceramics are a specialty of the Anti Atlas region and make for a beautiful souvenir.
  • Local spices: Stock up on Moroccan spices, such as ras el hanout, cumin, and turmeric, at one of the many souks in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Anti Atlas

The Anti Atlas region is home to a diverse mix of cultures, including the Berbers, Arabs, and Saharan tribes. The Berbers are the indigenous people of the region and have a rich cultural heritage that includes traditional music, dance, and crafts. The majority of the people in the region are Muslim, and it’s important to respect their customs and beliefs when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Anti Atlas

The best time to visit Anti Atlas is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. The summer months (June to August) can be hot, while the winter months (December to February) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anti Atlas

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Learn a few basic Arabic or Berber phrases.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Be respectful of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Take pictures without asking for permission.
  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Disrespect local customs or religious beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anti Atlas

Anti Atlas is considered a safe destination for tourists. Nevertheless, theft, scams and pickpocketing can occur in touristy areas. It’s important to stay vigilant and protect your belongings.

Health in Anti Atlas

There are no specific health risks in Anti Atlas. However, it’s recommended to drink bottled water and avoid eating uncooked or undercooked food. It’s also advisable to have travel insurance and consult a doctor before visiting if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Anti Atlas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anti Atlas

The closest airport to Anti-Atlas is Agadir-Al Massira Airport, which is located about 100 km north of the region. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to your destination.

Bus Stations Near Anti Atlas

There are several bus companies that operate services to Anti Atlas, including CTM and Supratours. The main bus stations in the region are in Tafraoute and Taroudant.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or a driver to take you to Anti Atlas from Marrakech or Agadir. Alternatively, there are tour operators that offer guided tours of the region.

How to Get Around in Anti Atlas

The best way to get around Anti Atlas is by car, preferably a 4x4, since many of the roads in the region are unpaved and rugged. However, there are also bus services and taxis available to take you to the main destinations.

Where to Go Next from Anti Atlas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the beauty of Anti Atlas, here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Marrakech: Discover the vibrant culture, bustling souks, and stunning architecture of Marrakech.
  • Essaouira: Relax on the beautiful beaches of Essaouira, a charming coastal town that is known for its windsurfing and kite surfing.
  • Fez: Explore the historic medina, ancient ruins, and excellent traditional cuisine of Fez.
  • Merzouga: Take a desert safari and camp under the stars in the famous Saharan dunes of Merzouga.

Conclusion

Anti Atlas is a beautiful and unique region in Morocco that offers a mix of natural wonders, cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or visiting Morocco for the first time, Anti Atlas has something for everyone. From hiking and biking expeditions, to relaxing on the beach, exploring traditional souks and eateries, and more, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this corner of North Africa.
