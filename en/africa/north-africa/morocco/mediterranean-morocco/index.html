A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Mediterranean Morocco

If you’re looking for an exotic destination with an interesting mix of culture, history, and natural beauty, then Mediterranean Morocco should definitely be on your list. This region of Morocco is known for its stunning landscapes, picturesque beaches, and rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit places, things to do, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and cultural customs of Mediterranean Morocco.

Introduction to Mediterranean Morocco

Mediterranean Morocco is a region that covers the northern part of Morocco, from the Atlantic Ocean to the Mediterranean Sea. The region is known for its diverse and vibrant culture, which has been shaped by the influence of different empires and dynasties throughout history. You’ll find a fascinating blend of Arabic, Berber, French, and Spanish influences in the culture, architecture, and cuisine of Mediterranean Morocco.

List of Places to Visit in Mediterranean Morocco

If you’re planning a trip to Mediterranean Morocco, here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Kasbah of the Udayas in Rabat
  • The Medina of Tangier
  • The Mausoleum of Mohammed V in Rabat
  • The Hassan II Mosque in Casablanca
  • The Roman ruins of Volubilis

Natural Attractions of Mediterranean Morocco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Rif Mountains in Chefchaouen
  • The Ouzoud Waterfalls in Azilal
  • The Blue Grotto in Cap Spartel
  • The Beaches of Asilah and Al Hoceima
  • The Dades Gorges in Boumalne Dades

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mediterranean Morocco

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll be happy to know that Mediterranean Morocco offers some excellent hiking and biking trails that allow you to immerse yourself in the breathtaking natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Toubkal National Park in the Atlas Mountains
  • The Talassemtane National Park in Akchour
  • The M’goun Massif in Ait Bougmez
  • The Khenifra Plateau

Best Things to Do in Mediterranean Morocco: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with friends, family or alone, there’s always something to do in Mediterranean Morocco. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Camel rides in the Sahara Desert
  • Surfing or windsurfing in Essaouira
  • Wine tasting in Meknes
  • Hammam and spa treatments in Marrakech
  • Shopping in the colorful souks of Fez

Food and Drink Scene of Mediterranean Morocco

Moroccan cuisine is an enticing blend of flavors and spices influenced by various cultures over the centuries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Mediterranean Morocco:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tagine (slow-cooked stew)
  • Couscous (steamed semolina grain with vegetables and meat)
  • B’stilla (savory pie with pigeon, almonds, and spices)
  • Harira (traditional soup with tomatoes, chickpeas, and lentils)
  • Zaalouk (cooked eggplants with tomatoes and spices)

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea
  • freshly squeezed orange juice
  • coffee with spices

Where to Stay in Mediterranean Morocco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mediterranean Morocco offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and travel styles. Here are some recommendations:

  • Luxury hotels: La Mamounia in Marrakesh, Sofitel Casablanca Tour Blanche in Casablanca, Hotel Sahrai in Fez
  • Budget hotels: Riad Dar One in Rabat, Hotel Central in Tangier, Dar Tafouyte in Chefchaouen
  • Hostels: Equity Point Hostel in Marrakesh, Riad Verus in Fez, Yuba Hostel in Tangier
  • Guesthouses: Dar el Khamlia in Merzouga, Kasbah Azul in Ouirgane, Dar Zitouna in Asilah

What to Buy in Mediterranean Morocco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mediterranean Morocco offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make for excellent gifts for family and friends back home. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Moroccan carpets
  • Pottery and ceramics
  • Leather goods
  • Spices and herbs
  • Traditional clothing and accessories

Local Culture and Customs of Mediterranean Morocco

Mediterranean Morocco has a rich and diverse culture that is heavily influenced by Islam. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Greet people with a handshake and a smile
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of locals
  • Use your right hand to eat or pass objects

Best Time to Visit Mediterranean Morocco

The best time to visit Mediterranean Morocco is during the spring (March to May) or the fall (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and mild. Summers can be extremely hot, especially in the desert regions, while winters can be chilly, particularly in the mountainous regions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mediterranean Morocco

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Mediterranean Morocco:

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Be respectful of the culture and customs
  • Learn some basic Arabic or French phrases
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Bargain when shopping in the souks

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without filtering it first
  • Show public displays of affection
  • Take photos of people without their permission
  • Consume alcohol in public places during Ramadan
  • Carry large amounts of cash or valuables

Safety and Health

Safety in Mediterranean Morocco

Mediterranean Morocco is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic precautions to ensure a safe trip:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in quiet or poorly lit areas
  • Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded marketplaces and tourist hotspots
  • Only use registered taxis or public transportation, and avoid sharing a taxi with strangers
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close to you.

Health in Mediterranean Morocco

Before you travel to Mediterranean Morocco, make sure to consult your doctor about necessary vaccinations and medications. Here are some additional health tips:

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water
  • Avoid eating raw or undercooked foods
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent as necessary
  • Pack any necessary prescription medications and bring extra supplies.

Getting to Mediterranean Morocco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mediterranean Morocco is well connected to the rest of Morocco and the world by air, land, and sea. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Mediterranean Morocco

  • Mohammed V International Airport (CMN) in Casablanca
  • Ibn Battuta International Airport (TNG) in Tangier
  • Fes-Saïs Airport (FEZ) in Fes

Bus Stations Near Mediterranean Morocco

  • Bab Doukkala Bus Station in Marrakesh
  • Terminal CTM in Casablanca

Other Options

  • Trains or high-speed rail at the main rail station in Casablanca or Rabat
  • Ferries from Spain to Tangier and Al Hoceima

How to Get Around in Mediterranean Morocco

When it comes to getting around in Mediterranean Morocco, there are several transportation options available:

  • Taxis
  • Buses
  • Trains or high-speed rail
  • Car Rentals
  • Shared taxis

Where to Go Next from Mediterranean Morocco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Oualidia Beaches
  • The Atlas Mountains
  • The Sahara Desert
  • The Portuguese City of El Jadida
  • The UNESCO World Heritage Site of Ait Benhaddou

Conclusion

Mediterranean Morocco is a fascinating and diverse region that has something to offer everyone. From the rich cultural heritage to the breathtaking natural landscapes, this region is a truly unique destination that should be on every traveler’s radar.
