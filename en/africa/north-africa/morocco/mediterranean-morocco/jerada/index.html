Introduction to Jerada

Welcome to Jerada, an off-the-beaten-path destination in Morocco. Located in Mediterranean Morocco, this beautiful town is a hidden gem that offers both cultural and natural attractions. With its spectacular mountain scenery, friendly people, and tasty local cuisine, Jerada is definitely worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Jerada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jerada boasts a rich history that is worth exploring. Some of the must-visit museums and historical sites in Jerada include:

  • The Mining Museum: If you’re interested in Jerada’s mining past, head to the Mining Museum, which is located in the heart of the city.
  • The Museum of Art and History: This museum showcases the region’s rich cultural heritage, including traditional clothing, pottery, and jewelry.
  • The Old Mines: To see the town’s mining ruins up close, take a tour of the old mines, which are located on the outskirts of the city.

Natural Attractions of Jerada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking and mountain biking to bird-watching and star-gazing, Jerada has something to offer for every nature lover. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Jerada include:

  • The Oued Ziz River: This beautiful river runs through the town and offers plenty of opportunities for various water-based activities, such as fishing and swimming.
  • The Tegharghart and Bouiblane Mountains: Step into the mountains and marvel at the lush forests, cascading waterfalls, and stunning views over the city.
  • The Tislit Waterfall: A short hike from the town centre leads you to this impressive waterfall. Take in the natural beauty of the area, relax and dip into the fresh waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jerada

Jerada’s beautiful hiking and biking trails offer an exciting opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region up close. Some of the best routes are:

  • Bouiblane Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of Bouiblane Mountain, where you can marvel at the breathtaking views over the surrounding mountains and towns.
  • Tisjirouine Mountain bike Tour: This mountain biking route is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. It passes through beautiful forests, sparkling streams, and dramatic mountain peaks, including the stunning Tisjirouine Mountain.
  • The Natural Reserve Hike: Wander through the beautiful Natural Reserve, and enjoy the beautiful wilderness while hiking in the beautiful mountain scenery, and perhaps spot some of the native wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Jerada: Activities and Tours

Jerada offers a variety of activities and tours expected to suit everyone’s interests. Here are a few of the best things to do in Jerada:

  • Explore the town’s bustling morning markets managed by the friendly locals providing them with fresh vegetables, fruits, and homemade products.
  • Visit the old mines, where you can learn about the rich mining history of the region and witness how they have adapted into cultural spaces.
  • Take a guided tour of the Natural Reserve to learn about the local flora and fauna and the region’s ecology.
  • Go on a sunset horse ride near Bouiblane mountain, with a panorama all around, a memorable time spent in the natural surroundings is worth it.
  • Experience the Hammam, a traditional Moroccan spa with a unique experience of skin care treatments, massages and other customary services to help you relax and unwind.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jerada

If you are staying in Jerada for a week or more, here is an itinerary to help plan your days efficiently and enjoyably.

Day Activities
Day 1 Take a walking tour of the city and visit the Mining Museum and the Museum of Art and History.
Day 2 Take a day trip to the Oued Ziz River and indulge in swimming, fishing, and other water-based activities.
Day 3 Visit the Old Mines and learn about the town’s mining history. In the evening, attend the local traditional dance and music performance.
Day 4 Take a guided tour of the Natural Reserve and learn about the local ecology and geography.
Day 5 Relax and unwind at the Hammam with traditional Moroccan spa treatments.
Day 6 Head out for a full-day hike or bike tour in the Bouiblane Mountains.
Day 7 Visit the Traditional art and craft center souk, Souk Jdid, choose your favorite piece of handicrafts to take back home, and enjoy the scent of Moroccan spices and local tea.

Food and Drink Scene of Jerada

Must-Try Dishes

Jerada’s cuisine is a delightful mix of Berber, Arab and Mediterranean influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Tajine: A traditional Berber dish prepared with meat, vegetables, and spices, cooked over a low fire.
  • Couscous Royal: A dish composed of lamb or chicken, steamed couscous, and vegetables, typically enjoyed on Fridays as a family meal.
  • Maakouda: A deep-fried potato patty filled with spicy meat or vegetables or served plain as a snack.
  • Harira: A warm and hearty soup traditionally served during the Holy month of Ramadan.

Local Beverages

Tea is the most common drink in Jerada, typically served with mint or sage. For a unique taste, try their sweet orange blossom water or Qata’a, a thickened and sweetened milk and almond drink typically served during Ramadan.

Where to Stay in Jerada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many lodging options in Jerada for different budget ranges. Due to it being an uncrowded destination, many budget-friendly and affordable hotels are available.

  • Riad Dar Moula: A guesthouse located in the Old Town, boasting a peaceful and welcoming atmosphere perfect for travelers who want to experience the Moroccan culture.
  • Hotel Al Boustane: A newly renovated hotel located in the city center, perfect for travelers who like to be at the heart of all activities.

What to Buy in Jerada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jerada has a fantastic range of handcrafted products and souvenirs to bring back home. Here are some recommendations:

-Moroccan Berber rugs and handmade blankets from Souk Jdid, a famous souk that is home to many traditional stores.

  • Local spices as souvenirs, bringing back Jerada’s flavors to put in dishes back home with.

Local Culture and Customs of Jerada

Jerada is a Berber city with a rich culture and history, which locals highly value. Visitors are requested to respect their traditions, customs and cultural values.

  • Attires: Women traveling in Jerada will feel more comfortable wearing modest and covering outfits, while showing bare arms, legs, and shoulders is not a common practice in the region.
  • Islam in Jerada: The majority of the population follows the Islamic religion and will perform prayer five times a day. It’s important to respect the times and locations designated for these prayers as they involve customs and conduct.
  • Music and Dance: Traditionally, the Berbers of Jerada have highly valued artistic performances such as music, dance, and folklore tales. Make sure to experience a musical performance when in town.

Best Time to Visit Jerada

The best time to visit Jerada is during the spring (March to June) and fall (September to November). During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are thinner, providing for a more peaceful environment to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jerada

Do:

  • Respect the local customs, traditions and expectations.
  • Dress conservatively, especially when approaching mosques and public markets.
  • Bargain and negotiate prices while shopping at the local Souks.
  • Try out the traditional Moroccan food culture of sharing dishes between groups, family style.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public, as it is illegal to do so in Morocco.
  • Take photographs of the locals, especially without asking for their consent.
  • Be disrespectful or insensitive of the locals’ religious customs, beliefs and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jerada

Jerada is known for being a safe city, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any critical incidents here. It’s important to practice common sense while navigating through town, especially at night.

Health in Jerada

Visitors are recommended to get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, Tetanus and diphtheria before visiting Jerada.

Getting to Jerada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jerada

The nearest airport to Jerada is Oujda’s Angads International Airport, around 70km away, with flights to more than 15 international destinations, making it very well-connected for international visitors.

Bus Stations Near Jerada

CTM buses are connecting Jerada to most major cities within Morocco, while FOR.MA buses provide transportation from Jerada to other cities in eastern Morocco.

Other Options

If you plan to see nearby towns, a rental car or hiring a private driver is recommended, who will take visitors to Jerada and other cities within the Eastern Morocco region.

How to Get Around in Jerada

Walking around Jerada is a great way to take in the town’s sights, but taxis are also commonly used for transport. Taxis charges are by distance covered but ensure a fair agreement on pricing beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Jerada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jerada is an excellent starting point for exploring the Eastern region of Morocco. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Oujda: A vibrant city in the east of Morocco with a rich culture, history, and cuisine.
  • Tetouan: A traditional walled city on the coast of Morocco, known for its traditional Andalusian architecture and rich arts and crafts.
  • The medieval city of Fez: A popular travel destination known for its well-preserved medieval architecture.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and delicious local cuisine, Jerada is a destination that deserves a spot on your travel list. From hiking and bike tours to cultural landmarks and traditional souks, there’s plenty to see and do in this hidden gem of Morocco.
