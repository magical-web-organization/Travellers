Introduction to Oujda

Oujda is a city located in Mediterranean Morocco, North Africa. It is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Oujda is also the largest city in the Oriental region of Morocco and boasts a population of over 500,000 people. The city is a hub for trade, commerce, and culture and has a fascinating blend of the old and new.

List of Places to Visit in Oujda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sidi Yahya Mosque

Built in the 18th century, the Sidi Yahya Mosque is one of the most important cultural landmarks in Oujda. The mosque has a unique architecture influenced by Andalusian design and boasts intricately designed textiles, woodwork, and tilework.

Dar Sebti Palace

Dar Sebti Palace is an old palace from the 19th century that has now been converted into a museum dedicated to the history and culture of the Oriental region. The museum boasts an impressive collection of traditional Moroccan textiles, pottery, jewelry, and other artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Oujda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tafoghalt Plateau

The Tafoghalt Plateau is a rocky desert plateau located about 30 km away from Oujda. It is a popular destination for hiking and camping due to its scenic beauty and unique flora and fauna.

Beni Snassen Mountain

Beni Snassen Mountain is an impressive mountain range located about 70 km away from Oujda. The peak of the mountain offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding areas and is a popular destination for hiking and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oujda

Tafoghalt Plateau Hiking Trail

The Tafoghalt Plateau hiking trail is a 10-kilometer hike that takes visitors through the scenic desert plateau. The trail is relatively easy, but visitors should still be prepared for the heat and carry plenty of water and sun protection.

Beni Snassen Mountain Trekking Trail

The Beni Snassen Mountain trekking trail is a challenging 2-day trek that takes visitors through the beautiful mountain range. Visitors should be prepared for the altitude and cold temperatures and carry appropriate gear.

Best Things to Do in Oujda: Activities and Tours

Oujda Cultural Tour

The Oujda cultural tour is a guided tour that takes visitors through the city’s historical and cultural landmarks. Visitors will get to see some of the city’s most important mosques, palaces, and museums and learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Moroccan Cooking Class

The Moroccan cooking class is a fun and interactive way to experience the local culture and cuisine. Visitors will learn how to make traditional Moroccan dishes like tagine, couscous, and mint tea.

Food and Drink Scene of Oujda

Must-Try Dishes

Tagine

Tagine is a popular Moroccan dish that consists of slow-cooked meat and vegetables in a spiced sauce. The dish is named after the traditional clay pot it is cooked in and is a must-try when in Oujda.

Couscous

Couscous is a staple Moroccan dish that consists of small steamed balls of semolina served with a variety of vegetables and meat.

Local Beverages

Mint Tea

Mint tea is the national drink of Morocco and is served everywhere in the country. The tea is made with green tea leaves and fresh mint leaves and is typically served with sugar.

Where to Stay in Oujda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Atlas Terminus & Spa

The Hotel Atlas Terminus & Spa is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Oujda. The hotel boasts an impressive spa and wellness center, and guests can indulge in a range of wellness and beauty treatments.

Riad Dar Al Fassia

Riad Dar Al Fassia is a charming guesthouse located in the Medina of Oujda. The guesthouse is known for its impeccable service and traditional Moroccan design.

What to Buy in Oujda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moroccan Carpets and Rugs

Moroccan carpets and rugs are an iconic souvenir to bring home from Oujda. These colorful and intricate rugs are handwoven using traditional techniques and are a beautiful addition to any home.

Argan Oil

Argan oil is a popular cosmetic product made from the kernels of the argan tree. The oil is used for its moisturizing and nourishing properties and is a popular souvenir to bring back from Morocco.

Local Culture and Customs of Oujda

Oujda has a rich cultural heritage influenced by its diverse history and geography. The locals are known for their hospitality and often welcome visitors with open arms. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and refrain from eating or drinking in public during Ramadan.

Best Time to Visit Oujda

The best time to visit Oujda is between March and May or September and November when the temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities. The summer months (June-August) are extremely hot and can be uncomfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oujda

Do:

  • Do dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Do try the local cuisine
  • Do practice common courtesy and show respect to the locals

Don’t:

  • Don’t eat or drink in public during Ramadan
  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission
  • Don’t drink alcohol in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Oujda

Oujda is a relatively safe city, and visitors should exercise common caution when visiting the city. Avoid walking alone in deserted areas or flashing expensive items, such as jewelry or cameras.

Health in Oujda

Visitors should drink only bottled water and take necessary precautions to prevent heat stroke and dehydration due to the high temperatures in the region.

Getting to Oujda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oujda

The Oujda-Angads Airport is located just outside of the city and offers daily flights to international and domestic destinations. Visitors can also fly into the Mohammed V International Airport in Casablanca and then take a connecting flight to Oujda.

Bus Stations Near Oujda

The Oujda Bus Station is the main bus station in the city and offers regular connections to other cities in Morocco, including Fez and Marrakech.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi, rent a car, or use a private transfer service to get to Oujda.

How to Get Around in Oujda

The best way to get around Oujda is by taxi or rental car. Visitors can also use local buses or walk around the city’s Medina.

Where to Go Next from Oujda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fez

Fez is a historic city located about 200 km away from Oujda. The city is known for its centuries-old medina and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Chefchaouen

Chefchaouen is a small town located in the Rif Mountains, about 300 km away from Oujda. The town is known for its blue-painted buildings and is a popular destination for its laidback atmosphere and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Oujda is a fascinating city that is full of history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the city’s cultural landmarks, embark on outdoor adventures, savor the local cuisine and hospitality, and experience the authentic Moroccan way of life. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most out of their trip to Oujda and create unforgettable memories.
