Introduction to Sfax

Sfax is the second-largest city in Tunisia and an important cultural, economic, and commercial hub in the country. Located on the central coast of Tunisia, Sfax boasts a beautiful medina, picturesque beaches, and a rich history that dates back to Phoenician times. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Sfax has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and culinary experiences in Sfax.

List of Places to Visit in Sfax

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sfax’s rich history is reflected in its numerous museums and historical sites, which offer visitors a fascinating glimpse into the city’s past. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Sfax:

  • The Sfax Museum of Archaeology: This museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts from the Phoenician, Roman, and Islamic periods. The exhibits cover everything from pottery and jewelry to coins and tombstones.
  • The Kasbah of Sfax: Located in the heart of the city, the Kasbah is a fortified citadel that dates back to the 9th century. Visitors can explore its narrow streets, impressive gates, and towers, and admire its traditional architecture.
  • The Dar Jellouli Museum: Housed in a beautiful 18th-century mansion, this museum showcases traditional Tunisian crafts and art, including ceramics, textiles, and mosaics.
  • The Ribat of Sfax: This historical Islamic monument is one of the largest in Tunisia and played an important role in defending the city against invaders.

Natural Attractions of Sfax: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sfax is blessed with a stunning natural beauty that is just waiting to be explored. Whether you want to hike, swim, or simply relax on the beach, Sfax has plenty of options. Here are some natural attractions you don’t want to miss:

  • The Sfax Beaches: Sfax is home to some of the most beautiful, unspoiled beaches in Tunisia. The most popular ones are Chaffar, Boukhriss, and Skanes.
  • The Sidi Mansour Lighthouse: This lighthouse is one of the most iconic landmarks in Sfax and offers breathtaking views of the coast.
  • The Kerkennah Islands: This group of islands is located off the coast of Sfax and is a haven for nature lovers. Visitors can explore its pristine beaches, mangrove forests, and salt flats.
  • The Sidi Youssef Dunes: These impressive sand dunes are located just outside of Sfax and are a popular spot for hiking and camel riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sfax

Sfax offers some of the best hiking and biking opportunities in Tunisia, with trails suitable for all levels of fitness and experience. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  • The Kerkennah Islands Trail: This 14-mile trail takes hikers on a scenic journey through the beautiful Kerkennah Islands. Along the way, you’ll see everything from beaches to dunes to salt flats.
  • The Boukornine Mountain Trail: This 20-mile trail takes hikers to the top of Boukornine Mountain, where stunning views of the surrounding landscape await.
  • The Sidi Youssef Dunes Trail: This 5-mile trail takes hikers through the beautiful sand dunes outside of Sfax.

Best Things to Do in Sfax: Activities and Tours

Sfax offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit every interest and budget. Here are some must-try activities:

  • Visit the Medina: The historic medina of Sfax is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the city’s top attractions. Explore its narrow streets, vibrant markets, and stunning architecture.
  • Try local cuisine: Tunisia is famous for its delicious food, and Sfax is no exception. Don’t miss the chance to try local specialties like brik, couscous, and harissa.
  • Take a boat tour: Explore the beautiful Tunisian coastline and surrounding islands with a scenic boat tour.
  • Attend a cultural event: Sfax hosts a range of cultural events throughout the year, including traditional festivals, music concerts, and film screenings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sfax

If you’re planning to spend a week in Sfax, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore the Medina and visit the Sfax Museum of Archaeology.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour to the Kerkennah Islands.
  • Day 3: Hike the Boukornine Mountain Trail.
  • Day 4: Visit the Kasbah of Sfax and the Ribat of Sfax.
  • Day 5: Relax on the beaches of Skanes.
  • Day 6: Explore the Sidi Youssef Dunes and go camel riding.
  • Day 7: Enjoy local cuisine and attend a cultural event.

Food and Drink Scene of Sfax

When it comes to food and drink, Sfax offers a delicious taste of traditional Tunisian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brik: A crispy pastry stuffed with egg, tuna, and harissa.
  • Couscous: A staple dish made from semolina grains served with vegetables, meat, and spicy sauce.
  • Lablabi: A thick soup made from chickpeas, bread, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Mloukhia: A stew made from mallow leaves served with rice or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Boukha: A traditional Tunisian spirit made from figs.
  • Thibarine: A spirit made from dates.
  • Jelab: A refreshing drink made from grape molasses, rose water, and pine nuts.
  • Mint tea: A popular drink served throughout Tunisia.

Where to Stay in Sfax: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sfax offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some popular options:

  • Les Oliviers Palace: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, with a pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • Hotel Ikram Sfax: A budget-friendly hotel located close to the train station, with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Dar Jellouli Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located in a beautifully restored 18th-century mansion in the medina.

What to Buy in Sfax: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sfax is a great place to pick up souvenirs and local products to remind you of your trip. Here are some popular options:

  • Handmade textiles and ceramics: Sfax is famous for its traditional crafts, including woven textiles, pottery, and ceramics.
  • Olive oil: Tunisia is one of the world’s leading producers of olive oil, and Sfax has some of the best.
  • Spices and condiments: Sfax is known for its delicious spices and condiments, including harissa, za’atar, and cumin.

Local Culture and Customs of Sfax

As a predominantly Muslim country, Tunisia has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Sfax:

  • Dress modestly: Tunisia is a conservative country, and visitors should avoid wearing revealing clothing in public.
  • Respect Ramadan: If you’re visiting during Ramadan, be aware that many restaurants and shops may be closed during the day.
  • Learn some basic Arabic: Learning a few Arabic phrases can help you connect with locals and show respect for their culture.
  • Don’t show public displays of affection: Physical contact between members of the opposite sex in public is generally frowned upon in Tunisia.

Best Time to Visit Sfax

The best time to visit Sfax is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer can be very hot, with temperatures soaring above 100°F, while winter can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sfax

To help you have a great trip to Sfax, here are some important do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few basic Arabic phrases.
  • Dress modestly.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photographs of people without asking for permission.
  • Hike or swim alone in remote areas.

Safety and Health

Sfax is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Here are some tips:

Safety in Sfax

  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Sfax

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water.
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Pack any necessary medications in case of emergency.

Getting to Sfax: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sfax is well-connected to the rest of Tunisia by air, road, and rail. Here’s what you need to know about getting to and around Sfax:

Airports Near Sfax

  • Sfax-Thyna International Airport is the main airport serving Sfax, with daily flights to and from major Tunisian cities as well as some international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Sfax

  • Sfax Bus Station is located in the city center and serves as a hub for buses to and from major Tunisian cities.
  • Sidi Mansour Bus Station is located in the suburbs of Sfax and serves as a hub for buses to and from the surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the surrounding areas of Sfax at your own pace.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Sfax and can be a convenient way to get around the city.
  • Train: The Sfax train station connects the city to other destinations in Tunisia, including Tunis and Sousse.

How to Get Around in Sfax

Sfax is a relatively small city, and most of the major attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you need to get around more quickly or easily, here are some transportation options:

  • Taxi: Taxis are affordable and widely available in Sfax.
  • Bus: Local buses run throughout the city and are a great way to get to more distant neighborhoods.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is an option if you want to explore the surrounding areas of Sfax at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Sfax: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Tunisia, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to add to your itinerary. Here are some options:

  • Tunis: The capital city of Tunisia is just a 3-hour drive or train ride from Sfax and offers a wealth of historical and cultural attractions.
  • Kairouan: This UNESCO-listed city is one of the holiest cities in Islam and is known for its impressive mosques and ancient city walls.
  • Douz: This desert oasis town is the gateway to the Sahara and offers camel rides, tours of local date palm groves, and stunning desert landscapes.

Conclusion

Sfax offers visitors a fascinating mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring its ancient medina, hiking its stunning mountains or soaking up the sun on its beaches, Sfax has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Sfax and experience all that this beautiful Tunisian city has to offer.
