Introduction to Matmata

Matmata is located in the Gabes Governorate of Central Coastal Tunisia, in North Africa. The village is known for its distinctive troglodyte architecture that dates back to prehistoric times. The locals here live in traditional underground dwellings that offer an exceptional travel experience. Matmata is also known for its stunning natural beauty that includes mountains, sand dunes, and oases. It is an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience authentic North African culture and stunning landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Matmata

Matmata is full of surprises and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are the must-visit places in Matmata:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Matmata Museum: This small museum is located in the village center and showcases the history and culture of Matmata. It displays traditional clothing, pottery, and other artifacts that date back to prehistoric times.
  2. Ksar Hedada: This is the oldest and most remarkable troglodyte structure in Matmata. It has been beautifully preserved and provides a glimpse into the lives of early settlers in the region.
  3. Douiret: This ancient Roman village is located on a hilltop and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. The village is known for its picturesque ruins and narrow alleys that make it a photographer’s paradise.
  4. Beni M’tir Mosque: This unique mosque is one of the oldest in Tunisia and is located in a troglodyte community of the same name. It has survived earthquakes, invasions, and fires and is an important cultural attraction in Matmata.

Natural Attractions of Matmata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Chaambi: This stunning peak is the highest mountain in Tunisia and offers a challenging hiking experience for adventure lovers. It has picturesque valleys and forests that are perfect for camping.
  2. Chenini: This ancient Berber village is perched on a dramatic cliff that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The village is home to a troglodyte structure called “Ksar Rahou” that is made entirely out of sandstone and dates back to the 4th century BC.
  3. Sebkhet El Melah: This salt lake is located close to Matmata and offers a serene and peaceful atmosphere. Its waters are believed to have therapeutic properties and are perfect for a relaxing swim.
  4. Djerba Island: This beautiful island is located near Matmata and is known for its stunning beaches, palm groves, and fishing villages. It is a popular tourist destination and offers a unique blend of Berber, Jewish, and Arab cultures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matmata

Matmata’s stunning natural beauty offers endless opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes in the region:

  1. Mount Chaambi Trek: This challenging trek takes you to the top of Mount Chaambi and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and forests. It is a 2-day trek and requires prior experience and preparation.
  2. Chenini Trek: This 1-day trek takes you through the picturesque Berber village of Chenini and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is an easy trek and suitable for beginners and experienced hikers alike.
  3. Djerba Island Bike Tour: This bike tour takes you through the palm groves and fishing villages of Djerba Island and offers a unique glimpse into the local lifestyle. It is a half-day tour and is perfect for families and groups.

Best Things to Do in Matmata: Activities and Tours

Matmata offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Matmata:

  1. Troglodyte Village Tour: This tour takes you through the underground dwellings of Matmata and offers a unique perspective on local culture and history. It is a must-do activity for all visitors to Matmata.
  2. Camel Trek: This leisurely trek takes you through the desert landscapes near Matmata and offers an authentic North African experience. It is a half-day tour and is suitable for families and groups.
  3. Cooking Class: This cooking class takes you through the local cuisine of Matmata and teaches you how to prepare traditional dishes like couscous, tagine, and brik. It is a half-day class and is perfect for foodies and culinary enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Matmata

Matmata’s food and drink scene is a blend of traditional Berber and North African cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Couscous: This staple North African dish is made from semolina and is usually served with meat, vegetables, and spicy harissa sauce.
  2. Brik: This savory pastry is made from a thin dough and is usually filled with egg, tuna, and parsley. It is a popular street food in Matmata.
  3. Tagine: This slow-cooked stew is made from meat and vegetables and is seasoned with a blend of spices like cumin, paprika, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  1. Mint Tea: This refreshing tea is a popular drink in Matmata and is made from green tea, fresh mint, and sugar.
  2. Boukha: This traditional distilled spirit is made from figs and is a popular drink in Tunisia. It is usually served as an aperitif.

Local Culture and Customs of Matmata

Matmata has a rich and diverse culture that is influenced by its history, geography, and traditions. Here are some local customs and etiquettes to keep in mind:

  1. Dress Code: Tunisia is a Muslim country, and it is respectful to dress conservatively while visiting Matmata. It is recommended to cover your shoulders and knees while visiting religious sites.
  2. Greetings: Tunisians are warm and hospitable people, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile.
  3. Language: Arabic is the official language of Tunisia, but French is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learning a few basic Arabic phrases can help you communicate better with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Matmata

Matmata can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) seasons. The weather during this time is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are thinner.

Getting to Matmata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matmata is well-connected to other parts of Tunisia and can be reached by various modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Matmata

The nearest airport to Matmata is the Djerba International Airport, which is around 1.5 hours’ drive from Matmata.

Bus Stations Near Matmata

Matmata has its own bus station, and buses from Tunis, Sfax, and other cities in Tunisia operate regularly.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire, and they offer a convenient and comfortable way to reach Matmata.

How to Get Around in Matmata

Matmata is a small village, and walking is the best way to get around the village. Taxis and private cars are also available for hire and can be used to explore nearby attractions.
