Introduction to Gabes

Gabes is a city in Central Coastal Tunisia, Africa. It’s a historic city, founded by the Greeks, and has grown into a modern-day bustling city that blends the best of ancient history with modern-day amenities. Gabes is a gateway to the desert city of Tozeur and the stunning island of Djerba. The city is also famous for its traditions, spice markets, and old dwellings. Let’s explore more about this lovely city.

List of Places to Visit in Gabes

Gabes has a lot of places to visit, and it can be overwhelming to choose. Below are some of the top places you must visit while in Gabes.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gabes is filled with museums and historical places that’ll take you back in time. Here are some of the best destinations:

  • Matmata Caves: Located 70 km from Gabes, are the unique pit houses that served as the filming location for Star Wars. Visitors can see live dwellings and understand how the Berbers lived centuries ago.

  • Gabes Museum: This museum in the center of the city has a collection of artifacts spanning several centuries. It displays old handcrafted textiles, traditional jewelry, agricultural tools and information on the Berber culture.

  • Ksar Ouled Soltane: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a majestic four-story fortified granary where centuries ago people used to store grains to domesticate camels instead of eating them. It is picturesque, beautifully restored and a monument of Berber cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Gabes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gabes is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. Here are the best natural attractions that’ll leave you spellbound:

  • Gabes Oasis: The streets of Gabes are lined by several palm groves where camels roam free, and visitors can go for a camel ride, explore the oasis, or walk on the lanes between the palm trees.

  • Chenini and Douiret: These are two Berber villages less than an hour from Gabes. The villages are tucked away in the winding mountains and showcase traditional Berber homes, Tunisian Berber culture and great views of the desert.

  • Sebkhet el Mellah: An enchanting and mysterious lake located in the largest salt pan in Tunisia. The lake environment is unique, and you may see flamingos and other birds wading in the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gabes

Gabes is undoubtedly an adventure paradise, and for the adventure enthusiasts, here are the best mountains, hikes, and bike tracks to discover:

  • The Sidi Toui Mountain: It’s an impressive 1444m peak located on the outskirts of the city. Hikers can follow the trails that lead to the summit through an unpaved road.

  • Chenini to Douiret Walk: This is a scenic walk that takes visitors through the mountains and valleys that surround Gabes. It passes through the Berber villages of Chenini and Douiret and presents breathtaking views.

  • Off-Road Biking: Biking enthusiasts can rent mountain bikes in Gabes and take the rugged mountain tracks that lead to the ancient Berber villages, lakes, and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Gabes: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the museums, mountains, and hikes, there are several activities to do and tours to join in Gabes, like:

  • Visiting the Souks: Gabes has several spice and street markets, making it a paradise for street shopping enthusiasts. Visitors can try the dates, olive oil, spices, and several fresh Tunisian delicacies.

  • Dolphin Watching Tour: White tuna and dolphins swim below the surface in the coastal areas of Gabes, making it a perfect spot for dolphin watching.

  • Quad Biking: Exploring Gabes’ scenic view via a quad bike tour is a fun way to discover the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Gabes

When in Gabes, you must try the local cuisine that offers some of the unique North African flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Couscous: A traditional Berber staple, made from steamed semolina, and served with lamb stew, veggies and harissa paste.

  • Brik: A traditional Tunisian pastry stuffed with egg, parsley, and tuna.

  • Makrouds: A sweet pastry consisting of semolina dough stuffed with sugared dates and deep-fried.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: Known as the national drink of Tunisia, it is made with green tea, fresh mint leaves, and sugar.

  • Boukha: A traditional Tunisian liquor made from figs.

Where to Stay in Gabes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gabes has several accommodation options that cater to all kinds of budgets, preferences, and locations. Below are the recommended options:

Luxurious Hotels

  • El Mouradi Gabes: A 4-star hotel located in the downtown of Gabes. The hotel has a swimming pool and a large garden space.

  • Chenini Naher: A luxury villa located in the Chettle mountain ranges in a stunning location with views over the Gabes valley.

Budget Hotels

  • Iberostar: Another popular hotel chain that provides a modest and comfortable stay at an affordable price.

  • Green Tower Hotel: A budget hotel located near the downtown that offers clean rooms and a garden.

What to Buy in Gabes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gabes has a rich tradition of craft-making, and visitors can buy some unique Tunisian souvenirs when visiting the city. Some popular items include:

  • Traditional textiles: Gabes’ traditional Berber clothing is famous for intricate embroidery.

  • Dates and olive oil: Gabes is home to several palms, and the dates and olive oil produced in Gabes are some of the best in the region.

  • Pottery: Gabes is known for its white and cobalt blue pottery, which is beautifully handcrafted.

Local Culture and Customs of Gabes

Gabes is a culturally rich city, and visitors must keep certain things in mind when traveling to the city:

  • Dress Code: Tunisia is a Muslim country, and it’s preferable to dress modestly when visiting Gabes.

  • Religious views: Visitors must respect the different religious beliefs, and prayer times in the city.

  • Personal Hygiene: Gabes has strict hygiene laws, and it’s essential to maintain cleanliness while visiting the city.

Best Time to Visit Gabes

The best time to visit Gabes is from March to May or September to November as the climate is mild, and there are fewer crowds in the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gabes

When visiting Gabes, here are some things you should and shouldn’t do:

Do:

  • Try Street Food: The street food in Gabes is lip-smacking, and you’ll miss out if you don’t try Tunisian delicacies.

  • Learn a few Arabic phrases: Tunisian Arabic is widely spoken in Gabes, and learning a few phrases can help in communication.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly: Tunisian culture places a premium on respect and privacy, so it’s best to talk softly.

  • Take pictures of people: It’s essential to ask permission before taking someone’s picture.

Safety and Health

When visiting Gabes, it’s essential to take care of your safety and health.

Safety in Gabes

Gabes is a safe city to travel, and there are only a few petty crimes in the city. Visitors must not travel alone and avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables.

Health in Gabes

Gabes doesn’t have any significant health concerns, but visitors must avoid consuming tap water and follow a proper diet for staying healthy during the trip.

Getting to Gabes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gabes is well-connected to the rest of the country with several transportation options.

Airports Near Gabes

  • Gabes Airport: The Gabes Airport is located a few kilometers from the city center and has several international flights.

  • Monastir Airport: The Monastir Airport is located in the neighboring city and is also known as the Habib Bourguiba International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Gabes

  • Gabes Louage station: The Gabes Louage station connects Gabes to several major cities in Tunisia.

Other Options

  • Taxi service: Taxis are available 24x7 in Gabes and are the preferred mode of transportation in the city.

How to Get Around in Gabes

Gabes is a small city, and it’s best to explore it on foot or by renting a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Gabes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gabes is located in the heart of Tunisia, and there are several destinations that you can explore from Gabes. Here are some of the best:

  • Tozeur: A desert city that offers sand dunes, traditional oasis, and palm groves.

  • Djerba Island: A picturesque island off the southern coast of Tunisia that’s a paradise for beach lovers.

Conclusion

Gabes is a city that seamlessly blends its ancient heritage with modern-day features. The city offers an excellent mix of natural scenery, cultural sites, adventure sports, and a unique culinary experience. This travel guide curated an immersive itinerary for a week-long trip, detailed the best places to stay, eat, visit and shop, and provided tips on cultural customs, safety and health, and transportation options to help you plan your perfect Gabes trip.
