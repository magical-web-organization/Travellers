Introduction to Port El Kantaoui

Port El Kantaoui is a charming seaside resort town located in northern Tunisia, along the Mediterranean Sea. This beautiful destination is popular among tourists for its picturesque marina, pristine beaches, and attractive architecture. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, indulge in delicious Tunisian cuisine, and explore the town’s rich culture and history.

List of Places to Visit in Port El Kantaoui

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Port El Kantaoui Archaeological Museum: This museum houses a collection of ancient artifacts and exhibits from the Roman and Byzantine periods, offering a fascinating insight into Tunisia’s rich history.
  • Ribat of Sousse: This iconic landmark boasts stunning views of the sea and surrounding area. Built in the 8th century, the Ribat is one of the oldest and most well-preserved fortresses in Tunisia.

Natural Attractions of Port El Kantaoui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hergla Beach: This stunning beach offers crystal clear waters and miles of white powder-soft sands. Visitors can swim, surf or sunbath and take in the gorgeous sunset views.
  • Kantaoui Golf Course: Rent clubs, head to the green and enjoy a lovely day of golf. With a variety of terrain including a links-style course and water challenges, this 36-hole golf course can accommodate all levels of players.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port El Kantaoui

  • The Cap Bon Loop: This scenic route offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea and the town. The loop is approximately 70 km long and takes visitors through charming Tunisian villages and along the coast.

Best Things to Do in Port El Kantaoui: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Trekking: Take a unique experience and trek through the landscapes in Tunisia, such as the Sahara Desert, while riding on the back of a camel. A great way to get a unique look at the desert.
  • Island Cruise Adventure: Head out and explore the Kerkennah Islands with an experienced captain on a boat ride tour. Take in the scenic views and go snorkel or swim in the clear waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Port El Kantaoui

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brik: A crispy pastry filled with an egg, parsley, and grated cheese.
  • Chakchouka: A spicy and flavorful dish made with a mixture of onions, tomatoes, garlic, and eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: A sweet and refreshing tea infused with fresh mint leaves.
  • Boukha: A local liquor made from figs and grain.

Where to Stay in Port El Kantaoui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • El Kantaoui Center and Spa: This elegant hotel offers luxurious suites, upscale amenities, and stunning views of the sea.
  • Royal Thalassa Monastir: This luxurious resort features modern rooms, a spa, private beach, and a wide range of activities and tours.

What to Buy in Port El Kantaoui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: The local area boasts a thriving artisan community, and visitors can pick up beautiful handmade rugs, pottery, and jewelry.
  • Olive Oil: Tunisia is one of the leading producers of high-quality olive oil in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Port El Kantaoui

  • Dress appropriately: As Port El Kantaoui is a predominantly Muslim country, it’s recommended to cover up when visiting the cultural and religious sites.
  • Bargain: Negotiating prices is common in Tunisia, and bargaining in shops and street markets is expected.

Best Time to Visit Port El Kantaoui

The best time to visit Port El Kantaoui is during the spring months between March and May or during the fall months between September and November. These seasons offer pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port El Kantaoui

Do:

  • Explore local culture and traditions.
  • Try Tunisian cuisine.
  • Be respectful towards religious and cultural beliefs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t engage in public displays of affection.
  • Don’t take pictures without permission.
  • Avoid drinking tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port El Kantaoui

Port El Kantaoui is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should still take normal safety precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on their belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Port El Kantaoui

Visitors should check with their doctor before traveling to Tunisia to ensure they have the necessary vaccinations. It’s also important to practice basic hygiene such as washing hands frequently and using hand sanitizer.

Getting to Port El Kantaoui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port El Kantaoui

The nearest airport is Monastir Habib Bourguiba International Airport, a 30-minute drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Port El Kantaoui

CTN and SNTRI bus lines are available in Sousse, 13 km away from Port El Kantaoui.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Port El Kantaoui via taxi or car.

How to Get Around in Port El Kantaoui

The town is small enough to explore on foot, but taxis are also readily available to take visitors to nearby attractions and sights.

Where to Go Next from Port El Kantaoui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sousse: Explore this charming coastal town with its beautiful medina and bustling promenade.
  • Monastir: Head towards the South to visit the other major seaside city of Tunisia, with its beautiful marina, and the Narjess and Ribat gardens.
  • El Jem: Visit this UNESCO World Heritage site and marvel at the impressive Roman amphitheater, which is the third-largest in the world.

Conclusion

Port El Kantaoui is a stunning destination that offers plenty to see and do for tourists.
