Introduction to La Goulette

Located in the northern part of Tunisia, La Goulette is a beautiful coastal city with a rich history and culture. This small town is also known as Halq al-Wadi, meaning “the throat of the valley,” and has been recognized as one of the most important port cities in Tunisia. Over the centuries, La Goulette has been under Roman, Islamic, and French occupation, making it a perfect destination for tourists who want to experience diverse cultures and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in La Goulette

La Goulette has numerous attractions that offer visitors the opportunity to explore its history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bardo National Museum: The museum is housed in a 15th-century palace and has a collection of over 100,000 artifacts, including some of the world’s most extensive Roman mosaics.

  • Carthage: This historical site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases the ancient city of Carthage, founded in the ninth century BC.

  • Zitouna Mosque: This mosque is the largest in Tunisia and dates back to the eighth century.

Natural Attractions of La Goulette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Goulette Beach: The golden beaches of La Goulette are one of its main attractions. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and a range of water sports.

  • Korbous Thermal Springs: Located in the mountains, Korbous is a surreal place with hot and cold natural springs that have therapeutic properties for several sicknesses.

  • Sidi Bou Said: Sidi Bou Said is a picturesque village known for its picturesque houses with blue and white facades.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Goulette

La Goulette is surrounded by the sea and the mountains. The city offers several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the sea and mountains, including:

  • Hiking Trail of Ras el Jebel: Visitors can explore the peak of Ras el Jebel, which rises over 300 meters and offers an outstanding view of the Gulf of Tunis.

  • Hiking Trail of Sidi Bou Ali: This hiking trail leads visitors through the hills to the village of Sidi Bou Ali, which offers a panoramic view of the Gulf of Tunis.

Best Things to Do in La Goulette: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting the city’s historical and natural attractions, there is plenty more to do in La Goulette, including:

  • Take a boat tour of the Tunisian coast.

  • Experience traditional Tunisian cooking classes.

  • Horseback riding on the beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Goulette

Here is a seven-day itinerary that visitors can follow to experience the best of what La Goulette has to offer:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Bardo National Museum and explore the medina.
2 Go to the La Goulette Beach and Rades Beach.
3 Head to Sidi Bou Said, explore the village, and walk through the streets.
4 Explore the ancient city of Carthage and view the many ruins.
5 Go surfing, windsurfing, and kitesurfing on the beach.
6 Visit the Korbous thermal springs and take a therapeutic soak.
7 Take a boat tour of the Tunisian coast and enjoy the coastal view.

Food and Drink Scene of La Goulette

La Goulette has a diverse culinary scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brik: This pastry dish is filled with egg, tuna, or meat and is a traditional Tunisian snack.

  • Couscous: A relatively staple dish made from semolina, can be served with chicken or vegetables, seasoned with rich Tunisian spices.

Local Beverages

  • Thé à la menthe: This is a traditional hot mint tea found throughout North Africa.

  • Boukha: A Tunisian alcoholic beverage made from figs.

Where to Stay in La Goulette: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in La Goulette that cater to the needs of travelers, from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel La Goulette: A stylish, modern hotel with an outdoor pool and a private beach area.

  • Dar El Medina: A luxurious guesthouse located in the heart of the medina, designed with great attention to detail.

What to Buy in La Goulette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

La Goulette has several souvenir shops that offer visitors the opportunity to buy a unique range of souvenirs that reflect local culture and traditions. Here are some recommended products to bring home from La Goulette:

  • Tunisian ceramics and pottery

  • Argan oil

Local Culture and Customs of La Goulette

Tunisians are friendly and hospitable people. If you want to experience the local culture while in La Goulette, here are some tips to follow:

  • Dress modestly, especially in religious sites and conservative areas.

  • Ask permission before taking someone’s photo.

Best Time to Visit La Goulette

The best time to visit La Goulette is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. July and August are the busiest months, and many Tunisians go on holiday at this time, so it may be difficult to find accommodations.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Goulette

Here are some things to do and avoid in La Goulette:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Ask before taking someone’s picture.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Goulette

While Tunisia is considered a relatively safe country, be cautious of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas.

Health in La Goulette

When traveling to La Goulette, make sure to update your routine vaccinations or consider additional vaccinations, such as hepatitis A, typhoid, or rabies.

Getting to La Goulette: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Goulette

The Tunis-Carthage International Airport is the closest airport to La Goulette.

Bus Stations Near La Goulette

Tunisia has an extensive bus system, and several buses run between La Goulette and Tunisia’s major cities.

Other Options

Taxi service is available from the airport and around the city.

How to Get Around in La Goulette

There are several transportation options available, including:

  • Taxis

  • Buses

  • Trains

Where to Go Next from La Goulette: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Explore some more of Tunis, take in the beautiful scenery of Sidi Bou Said, or head over to Hammamet, a beautiful resort town on the Mediterranean coast.

Conclusion

La Goulette is a beautiful coastal city in Tunisia that offers visitors a beautiful mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore La Goulette’s best attractions, taste its culinary delights, and experience the local culture and lifestyle while staying safe and healthy.
