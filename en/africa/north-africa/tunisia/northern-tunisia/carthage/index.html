Carthage, located in northern Tunisia, offers a unique blend of historical and natural attractions. Known as one of the most important cities in ancient times, Carthage now welcomes visitors from all over the world with its stunning architecture, museums, and natural beauty. Whether you want to explore the city’s rich history, hike in the hills overlooking the Mediterranean, or sample the local cuisine, Carthage is an excellent destination for travelers.

Introduction to Carthage

Carthage is located on the eastern coast of Tunisia, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea. It was originally founded in the 9th century BC by Phoenician colonists and soon became a major hub of trade and culture. Carthage was also the center of a powerful empire, which vied for control of the Mediterranean with Rome. Today, visitors can explore the ruins of ancient Carthage, including the Punic Ports, Antonine Baths, and ancient amphitheaters.

List of Places to Visit in Carthage

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The National Museum of Carthage: Located on the Byrsa Hill, this museum houses a collection of artifacts from the Punic and Roman periods of Carthage’s history. Visitors can find exhibits showcasing ancient art, pottery, and jewelry.
  2. The Carthage Aqueducts: This structure dates back to the Roman era and can be found on the outskirts of the city. The aqueduct once provided fresh water to the ancient city and is now a popular attraction for visitors who want to learn more about the city’s history.
  3. Carthage Cathedral: This beautiful cathedral was built in the 19th century by the French and is located on Byrsa Hill. It offers stunning views of the surrounding area and is a great place to relax and take in the beauty of Carthage.

Natural Attractions of Carthage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Baths of Antoninus: This is one of the largest bathhouses in the ancient world and offers stunning views of the sea. Visitors can walk around the ruins and also enjoy the scenic views of the Mediterranean.
  2. The Tophet of Salammbô: This is an ancient site where Carthaginians used to make sacrifices to the gods. Today, it is an open-air archeological museum and offers excellent views of the surrounding area.
  3. Gammarth Beach: This beach is a popular destination for visitors who want to enjoy the sun and sea. It is also known for its kite surfing and windsurfing facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carthage

Carthage has several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the surrounding hills and countryside. Some of the most popular include:

  1. Djebel Ressas: This is the highest peak in the region and offers stunning views of the Mediterranean. It is a challenging hike and suitable for experienced hikers.
  2. Sidi Bou Said Marina: This area is popular with locals and tourists alike and offers a number of hiking and biking routes. Visitors can explore the hills overlooking the sea, take in the scenic views, and stop at local cafes and restaurants along the way.

Best Things to Do in Carthage: Activities and Tours

  1. Carthage By Night: This tour takes visitors on a journey through the most important monuments of Carthage, including the Baths of Antoninus, the Punic Ports, and the ancient amphitheaters.
  2. Carthage Medina Tour: This tour takes visitors through the narrow streets of the old medina and offers a glimpse into the daily lives of Carthaginians.
  3. Private Yacht Tour: Visitors can enjoy the Mediterranean in style by booking a private yacht tour. This is a great option for families or groups of friends who want to explore the sea at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carthage

Day 1: Arrival in Tunis and transfer to hotel in Carthage. Day 2: Visit the Tophet of Salammbô and the Baths of Antoninus. Day 3: Explore the National Museum of Carthage and the ancient theater. Day 4: Take a hike in Djebel Ressas. Day 5: Relax at Gammarth Beach and enjoy the sea. Day 6: Go on a private yacht tour and enjoy the Mediterranean. Day 7: Departure from Carthage.

Food and Drink Scene of Carthage

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tunisian Couscous: This dish is a staple in Tunisian cuisine and is made with semolina, vegetables, and meat. It is typically served with harissa, a spicy sauce made from chili peppers.
  2. Brik: This is a fried filo dough pastry typically filled with egg, tuna, or cheese.
  3. Lablabi: This is a traditional Tunisian soup made with chickpeas, bread, and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Mint Tea: This is a popular drink in Tunisia and is often served with sugar and fresh mint leaves.
  2. Sidi Bou Said: This is a local beer that is brewed in Tunis and is known for its crisp, refreshing taste.
  3. Boukha: This is a local brandy that is made from figs and is typically served as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Carthage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Residence Tunis: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the Mediterranean and is located on the outskirts of Carthage.
  2. Dar Marsa Cubes: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Carthage and offers a cozy, intimate setting.
  3. Dar El Maa: This budget-friendly option is located in the old medina and offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Carthage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pottery: Carthage is known for its traditional pottery, which features bright colors and intricate designs.
  2. Olive Oil: Tunisia is one of the largest producers of olive oil in the world, and visitors can find high-quality olive oil in Carthage.
  3. Spices: Tunisian cuisine is known for its use of spices, and visitors can find a variety of high-quality spices in Carthage’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Carthage

  1. Dress Code: Carthage is a predominantly Muslim country, and visitors should dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  2. Language: Arabic is the official language of Tunisia, but English and French are also widely spoken.
  3. Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Tunisia, but it is always appreciated. 10% is a good guideline for restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Carthage

The best time to visit Carthage is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be very hot and humid, making it difficult to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carthage

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or markets.
  2. Try local cuisine, including couscous, brik, and lablabi.
  3. Learn a few Arabic phrases, such as “salaam alaikum” (hello) and “shukran” (thank you).

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water in Tunisia, as it may not be safe.
  2. Take photographs of people without their consent.
  3. Hike alone in remote areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carthage

Carthage is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any major city. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid displaying valuables in public, and be cautious when using ATMs or walking alone at night.

Health in Carthage

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Tunisia. It is also recommended that visitors get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and Typhoid before arriving. Travelers should also avoid drinking tap water and be cautious when eating street food.

Getting to Carthage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carthage

  1. Tunis-Carthage International Airport: This is the main airport serving Carthage and is located approximately 10 km from the city center.
  2. Enfidha-Hammamet International Airport: This airport is located approximately 90 km from Carthage and serves a variety of domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Carthage

  1. Tunis Bus Station: This station is located in the heart of Tunis and serves a variety of domestic and international destinations.
  2. La Goulette Bus Station: This station is located on the outskirts of Tunis and serves a variety of regional destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car, take a taxi, or rent a bicycle or scooter to explore the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Carthage

Visitors can get around Carthage via car, taxi, or public transportation. Taxis are readily available throughout the city, and public transportation is affordable and efficient.

Where to Go Next from Carthage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Tunis: Visitors can explore the capital city, which offers a rich history, bustling markets, and lively nightlife.
  2. Sidi Bou Said: This picturesque village is known for its blue-and-white architecture and offers stunning views of the Mediterranean.
  3. Zaghouan: This town is located approximately 40 km from Carthage and is home to the famous Zaghouan Aqueduct, which once supplied water to ancient Carthage.

Conclusion

Carthage is an excellent destination for travelers who want to explore the history, culture, and natural beauty of Tunisia. With its stunning architecture, museums, and outdoor activities, Carthage offers something for everyone. By following this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their time in the city and create memories that will last a lifetime.
