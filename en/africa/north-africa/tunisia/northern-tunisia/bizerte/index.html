Introduction to Bizerte

Located in northern Tunisia, Bizerte is a coastal city that has a rich history and beautiful natural scenery. The city has been inhabited since ancient times and has been ruled by different empires throughout its history, resulting in a unique cultural blend of Arab, Mediterranean, and African influences.

List of Places to Visit in Bizerte

If you’re planning a trip to Bizerte, here are some must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bizerte Medina: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the medina is the old walled city and a great place to explore on foot.
  • Kasbah of Bizerte: Built in the 17th century, the Kasbah is a fortress that overlooks the harbor and offers stunning views of the sea.
  • Oceanographic Museum: Located in the old town, the museum showcases the marine life of Bizerte and Tunisia.
  • Bardo Museum: Located in Tunis, this museum displays some of the best preserved Roman mosaics in the world.

Natural Attractions of Bizerte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ichkeul National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the park is home to a wetland ecosystem and is famous for its migratory birds.
  • Cap Blanc: A stunning white cliff that offers panoramic views of the Mediterranean.
  • Zamouri Mosque: This mosque is known for its beautiful architecture and serene courtyard.
  • La Grotte: A limestone cave that can be explored on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bizerte

For outdoor enthusiasts, Bizerte offers several options for hiking and biking:

  • Cap Blanc Trail: This 3.6 km trail offers breathtaking views of the sea, and takes about 1.5 hours to complete.
  • Ichkeul Circuit Trail: This 8.5 km trail takes you through the Ichkeul National Park and offers a chance to spot migratory birds and other wildlife.
  • Bizerte Coastal Road: This 16 km scenic road is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or drive, with beautiful views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Bizerte: Activities and Tours

In addition to the places to visit and outdoor activities, here are some other things to do in Bizerte:

  • Visit the Souk: The market in the old medina is a great place to shop for local handicrafts, spices, and food.
  • Go Scuba Diving: The waters off the coast of Bizerte are teeming with marine life and offer great opportunities for scuba diving.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Bizerte’s cuisine is a delicious blend of Mediterranean and North African flavors, with plenty of fresh seafood dishes.
  • Take a Boat Tour: There are several boat tours available in Bizerte, offering a chance to explore the harbor and see the city from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bizerte

If you have a week to spend in Bizerte, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the old town and visit the Kasbah.

Day 2: Take a boat tour and explore the harbor.

Day 3: Visit the Oceanographic Museum and the Bardo Museum in Tunis.

Day 4: Go hiking in Ichkeul National Park.

Day 5: Relax on the beach and try some local food.

Day 6: Go scuba diving in the Mediterranean.

Day 7: Visit Cap Blanc and take in the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Bizerte

Bizerte’s cuisine is a mix of Mediterranean and North African flavors, with lots of fresh seafood dishes. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brik: A fried pastry filled with egg and tuna.
  • Tajine: A slow-cooked stew made with meat or fish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Seafood: Freshly caught seafood such as shrimp, octopus, and sea bass, served grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea: A refreshing tea flavored with mint, often served with dates.
  • Boukha: A strong spirit made from figs.
  • Café Antika: A traditional Tunisian coffee flavored with cardamom and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Bizerte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some options for accommodations in Bizerte:

  • Ramada Plaza: A 4-star hotel located near the beach.
  • Hôtel Sidi Salem: A 3-star hotel with a pool and garden.
  • Auberge de Jeunesse: A hostel located in the old town.
  • Dar Salem: A guesthouse in the Medina area.

What to Buy in Bizerte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider bringing home from Bizerte:

  • Pottery and Ceramic: Bizerte is known for its pottery and ceramic products, including plates, bowls, and vases.
  • Olive Oil: Tunisia is one of the world’s largest producers of olive oil, and you can find high-quality olive oil in Bizerte.
  • Carpet and Textiles: Tunisian carpets and textiles are known for their vibrant colors and intricate patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Bizerte

Tunisia is a Muslim-majority country, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites, and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Bizerte

The best time to visit Bizerte is from March to May or September to November, when the weather is mild and crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bizerte

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Tip restaurant staff if the service was good.
  • Negotiate prices when shopping at the souk.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bizerte

Overall, Bizerte is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always a good idea to take standard precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Bizerte

No specific vaccinations are required for travelers to Bizerte, but it is always a good idea to check with your doctor before travelling. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water and to avoid street food that may cause digestive problems.

Getting to Bizerte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bizerte

The nearest airport to Bizerte is Tunis-Carthage International Airport, which is about 70 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Bizerte

There are several bus stations in Bizerte that connect the city with other destinations in Tunisia, including Tunis and Sousse.

Other Options

The best way to travel around Bizerte is by taxi or on foot.

How to Get Around in Bizerte

In Bizerte, walking is the best way to get around the old town and the Medina. Buses and taxis are also available, but they can be difficult to navigate for non-French speakers. Car rentals are available, but driving in the city can be challenging due to narrow streets and heavy traffic.

Where to Go Next from Bizerte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Tunisia, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Tunis: The capital city of Tunisia is only an hour away from Bizerte and offers plenty of attractions, including the Bardo Museum and the Carthage ruins.
  • Sousse: Located on the east coast of Tunisia, Sousse is known for its beautiful sandy beaches and historic medina.
  • El Kef: A city in northwestern Tunisia, El Kef is known for its ancient Roman ruins and stunning mountain scenery.

Conclusion

Bizerte is a destination that offers a unique mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the old town, hiking through national parks, swimming in the Mediterranean, or trying delicious local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Bizerte. By following this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan a memorable trip to this beautiful Tunisian city.
