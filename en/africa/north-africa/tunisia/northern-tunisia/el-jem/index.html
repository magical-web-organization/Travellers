El Jem Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Northern Tunisia

Envisioning a trip to Northern Tunisia? Look no further and explore the ancient city of El Jem, also spelled “El Djem”. Famous for its grand Roman amphitheater and other cultural, historical, and natural attractions, El Jem promises an unforgettable experience.

Introduction to El Jem

El Jem is a small town located in the Mahdia Governorate of Tunisia, in North Africa. Its population is around 21,000, and the town is situated around 200 km south of the capital, Tunis. Due to its strategic location, El Jem has always been a trading center and a hub for different civilizations.

El Jem is widely known for its ancient Roman amphitheater, the Amphitheatre of El Jem, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The amphitheater is one of the largest in the world, with a seating capacity of around 35,000.

List of Places to Visit in El Jem

El Jem has a lot to offer for travelers, from cultural and historical attractions to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of the top places to visit in El Jem:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Amphitheatre of El Jem: An iconic landmark and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Amphitheatre of El Jem is the main attraction of the town. Built around AD 238-240, it is one of the best preserved Roman amphitheaters in the world.

  2. El Jem Museum: Located near the amphitheater, the museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. It showcases an extensive collection of Roman artifacts, including sculptures, mosaics, pottery, and more.

  3. El Jem Fortress: Constructed in the 17th century, the El Jem Fortress is a majestic structure that stands tall to this day. It was built by the Ottomans to protect the town from invasions.

Natural Attractions of El Jem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Oued el Khadhra Oasis: Located around 10 km away from El Jem, the oasis is a natural wonder that is worth a visit. The palm groves and the water stream create a mesmerizing landscape.

  2. Sidi Ali El Mekki: A small village located near El Jem, Sidi Ali El Mekki is famous for its natural hot springs. The springs are believed to have healing properties and are a popular spot for relaxation.

  3. Quad biking in the Desert: Explore the vast desert around El Jem on a quad bike tour. It’s an exhilarating experience that lets you witness the beauty of the natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Jem

El Jem is surrounded by picturesque landscapes and several hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes are:

  1. Oued el Khadhra: A scenic hike through the palm groves of Oued el Khadhra Oasis.

  2. Jebel Serj National Park: A mountainous terrain that is perfect for a challenging hike or a mountain bike ride.

  3. Ouled Asker: A natural trail that leads you through the charming villages and the lush green landscapes.

Best Things to Do in El Jem: Activities and Tours

Apart from the top attractions, several activities and tours are available in El Jem:

  1. Amphitheater Tour: Take a guided tour of the Amphitheatre of El Jem and learn about its history and architecture.

  2. El Jem Museum Tour: Explore the vast collection of Roman artifacts at El Jem Museum with a guided tour.

  3. Camel Riding: Experience the desert life and take a camel ride in the vast sand dunes.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Jem

If you have a week to spend in El Jem, here’s an itinerary that covers all the top attractions and activities:

Food and Drink Scene of El Jem

El Jem has its unique culinary specialties and local beverages that should be tried during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Brik: A traditional Tunisian pastry that is stuffed with various fillings like egg, tuna, and cheese.

  2. Mloukhia: A stew made from a green leafy vegetable called Jute, served with bread or couscous.

  3. Couscous: A national dish of Tunisia, couscous is a staple that is prepared with steamed semolina.

Local Beverages

  1. Mint Tea: A refreshing drink that is served with mint leaves and sugar.

  2. Boukha: A traditional Tunisian brandy that is made from figs.

  3. Thibarine: A date-based alcoholic beverage that has a sweet taste.

Where to Stay in El Jem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

El Jem has several accommodation options for travelers that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses are:

  1. El Jem Colosseum: A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms and suites with a stunning view of the Amphitheatre of El Jem.

  2. Dar Slim: A boutique hotel that has a traditional Ottoman architecture and a tranquil ambiance.

  3. El Jem Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms with basic amenities.

What to Buy in El Jem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

El Jem has many stores and local markets that offer unique souvenirs and products that represent the Tunisian culture. Here are three things to buy:

  1. Olive Oil Soap: Handmade soap that is made from pure olive oil and is known for its natural benefits.

  2. Tunisian Ceramics: Colorful ceramics with intricate designs that showcase the Tunisian heritage.

  3. Jewelry: Exquisite jewelry with traditional motifs and patterns, like Berber or Arabic calligraphy.

Local Culture and Customs of El Jem

El Jem is a predominantly Muslim town, and travelers should respect the local culture and customs. Here are some tips that can help:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious places.

  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases to communicate with locals.

  • Respect the local traditions and avoid being loud or disruptive.

Best Time to Visit El Jem

The best time to visit El Jem is during the spring or autumn season, from March to May or September to November. The weather is pleasant during this time, and fewer crowds make it easier to explore the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Jem

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages.

  • Carry cash as many small shops and markets do not accept credit cards.

  • Bargain while shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water; always buy bottled water.

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Jem

El Jem is a relatively safe town, with crime rates being low. However, travelers should take precautions like not walking alone at night and not carrying large amounts of money.

Health in El Jem

Travelers should ensure that they have all necessary vaccinations and travel insurance before arriving in El Jem. It is also advisable to carry a basic medical kit for emergencies.

Getting to El Jem: Transportation Options and Logistics

El Jem is well-connected with major cities of Tunisia and can be reached by different means of transportation.

Airports Near El Jem

The nearest airport to El Jem is the Monastir Habib Bourguiba International Airport, which is around 60 km away from the town.

Bus Stations Near El Jem

El Jem has a bus station with frequent connections to Tunis, Sousse, and other neighboring towns.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for transportation within the town and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in El Jem

Walking and cycling are the best ways to explore the town and its surroundings. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from El Jem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

El Jem’s location makes it a perfect base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions.

  • Mahdia: A coastal town with a rich history and a picturesque harbor.

  • Sbeitla: A Roman city with remarkable ruins, including a temple and a theater.

  • Kairouan: The fourth holiest city of Islam and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

El Jem is a hidden gem that has something for everyone. From the ancient Roman amphitheater to the natural wonders of the desert, there are countless reasons to visit this charming town in Northern Tunisia. Don’t miss out on the local food, cultural experiences, and nearby destinations. Plan your trip to El Jem and experience the magic of Tunisia.
