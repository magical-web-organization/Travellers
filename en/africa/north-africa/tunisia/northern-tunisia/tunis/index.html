Introduction to Tunis

Tunis, the capital city of Tunisia, is a charming destination that embodies the cultural diversity of North Africa. With a rich history dating back thousands of years, Tunis offers a unique blend of Arab, Berber, and European influences, resulting in a vibrant and dynamic cityscape.

Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, experiencing outdoor adventures, or indulging in local cuisine, Tunis has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Tunis.

List of Places to Visit in Tunis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bardo Museum: This world-renowned museum houses one of the most extensive collections of Roman mosaics in the world, along with important artifacts from the Carthaginian and Islamic eras.
  • Medina of Tunis: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Medina of Tunis is a historic district full of winding alleyways, traditional shops, and stunning architecture.
  • Carthage: Located just outside of Tunis, this ancient city boasts impressive ruins from the Punic, Roman, and Byzantine eras, including the iconic Carthage Museum and Theatre.

Natural Attractions of Tunis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belvédère Park: This large public park in Tunis is home to lush gardens, an artificial lake, and stunning views of the cityscape.
  • Sidi Bou Said: A quaint village built on a hill overlooking the Mediterranean Sea, Sidi Bou Said is known for its picturesque streets and blue and white architecture.
  • Ichkeul National Park: Located in the north of Tunisia, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to a diverse array of fauna and flora, including rare bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tunis

For those seeking outdoor adventure in Tunis, there are several fantastic hiking and biking routes to explore. These include:

  • Zaghouan Aqueduct Trail: This scenic hiking trail follows the ancient aqueduct system that once supplied water to the city of Carthage.
  • El Haouaria Trail: This biking trail winds through the stunning countryside of northeastern Tunisia, passing by historic villages and spectacular Roman ruins.
  • Bou Kornine Trail: Located within close proximity to Tunis, this trail offers breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Tunis: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Medina: Take a guided tour of the Medina of Tunis to discover the history and culture of this fascinating district.
  • Visit the Bardo Museum: Spend an afternoon exploring the stunning exhibits and galleries of this world-class museum.
  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Tunisian dishes with a local chef.
  • Go Dolphin Watching: Head out on a boat tour of the Gulf of Tunis to spot playful dolphins in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tunis

  • Day 1: Explore the Medina of Tunis and visit the Bardo Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to the ruins of Carthage and discover the ancient history of Tunisia.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Belvédère Park and discover the lush greenery and stunning views of the city.
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour of Sidi Bou Said and discover the quaint streets and blue and white architecture.
  • Day 5: Discover the wildlife and nature of Ichkeul National Park.
  • Day 6: Go hiking or biking along one of the many scenic trails surrounding Tunis.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beautiful beaches of La Marsa and indulge in some fresh seafood.

Food and Drink Scene of Tunis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Couscous: A traditional North African dish made from semolina wheat, vegetables, and meat or fish.
  • Brik: A popular Tunisian street food, Brik is similar to a fried egg roll and is filled with tuna, egg, and harissa.
  • Lablabi: A hearty soup made from chickpeas, spices, and bread.

Local Beverages

  • Thé à la Menthe: This traditional Tunisian mint tea is a refreshing drink enjoyed throughout the country.
  • Boukha: A local distilled spirit made from figs.
  • Mloukhiya: A popular drink made from a type of jute plant, it is believed to have numerous health benefits.

Where to Stay in Tunis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Villa Bleue: A luxury boutique hotel in Sidi Bou Said, offering stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea and luxurious amenities.
  • Dar Ben-Gacem: A quaint guesthouse located in the heart of the Medina of Tunis, offering charming rooms and traditional decor.
  • Hotel Carlton: A grand hotel located just a short distance from the Bardo Museum, offering spacious rooms and stunning panoramic views of Tunis.

What to Buy in Tunis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dates: Tunisia is known for its high-quality dates, which make a delicious and healthy snack or gift.
  • Carpet: Tunisian carpets are known for their intricate designs and high-quality wool and silk fibers.
  • Olive Oil: Tunisia is one of the largest producers of olive oil in the world, making it an excellent souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tunis

Tunisians are known for their hospitality and warm and welcoming nature. When visiting Tunis, it’s important to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. It’s also customary to greet locals with a polite “salam alaykum” (peace be upon you) and to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Tunis

The best time to visit Tunis is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tunis

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect religious customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tunis

Tunis is generally a safe city for tourists, but it’s important to take precautions to avoid being a victim of crime. These include staying in well-lit areas at night, avoiding carrying large amounts of cash or valuables, and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Tunis

It’s important to take precautions when visiting Tunis to avoid getting sick. This includes drinking only bottled water and avoiding raw or undercooked food. It’s also recommended to have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to Tunisia.

Getting to Tunis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tunis

  • Tunis-Carthage International Airport: Located just outside of Tunis, this airport offers direct flights to many major international destinations.
  • Enfidha–Hammamet International Airport: Located approximately 70 kilometers from Tunis, this airport offers direct flights to many popular European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Tunis

  • La Goulette Bus Station: Located in downtown Tunis, this bus station offers local and long-distance bus services.
  • El Kashash Bus Station: Located on the outskirts of Tunis, this bus station offers services to more remote areas in the region.

Other Options

Taxis and trains are also available for transportation within Tunis and to other destinations throughout Tunisia. It’s recommended to use reputable taxi services and book train tickets in advance to avoid long wait times.

How to Get Around in Tunis

Public transportation options in Tunis include buses, metros, and light rails. Taxis and private car rentals are also available. It’s important to negotiate the price of a taxi ride before getting in the car to avoid being overcharged.

Where to Go Next from Tunis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sousse: This coastal city is known for its beautiful beaches and well-preserved historic sites.
  • Kairouan: Considered one of the holiest cities in Islam, this ancient city boasts impressive architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • El Jem: Home to one of the largest and best-preserved Roman amphitheaters in the world, this town is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Tunis is a captivating destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring historic sites, indulging in local cuisine, or experiencing outdoor adventures, Tunis has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this enchanting city.
