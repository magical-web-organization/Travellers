Food and Drink Scene of Kairouan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brik: This is a traditional Tunisian pastry that is usually filled with egg and tuna.
  • Couscous: A staple of Tunisian cuisine, couscous is a type of pasta that is served with meat and vegetables.
  • Harissa: A spicy paste made from chili peppers and garlic. It’s often served alongside bread and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea: This sweet and refreshing tea is a Tunisian favorite.
  • Boukha: A traditional Tunisian liquor made from figs.

Where to Stay in Kairouan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Kasbah: This hotel is located in the Medina of Kairouan and offers traditional Tunisian rooms and courtyard.
  • Kairouan Palace Hotel: This larger hotel offers more modern amenities, including a swimming pool and restaurant.
  • Dar Hassine Allani: A guesthouse located in the Medina of Kairouan, it offers a traditional Tunisian experience.

What to Buy in Kairouan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carpets and textiles: Kairouan is known for its beautiful carpets and textiles. Visitors can find a range of styles and prices.
  • Pottery: There are several shops in the Medina of Kairouan that sell handmade pottery.
  • Dates: Tunisia is famous for its dates, and visitors can find several varieties in Kairouan.

Local Culture and Customs of Kairouan

  • Dress modestly: Kairouan is a conservative city, and visitors should dress modestly out of respect for local customs.
  • Remove shoes before entering mosques or homes: This is a common practice in Tunisia.
  • Ramadan: Visitors should be aware of Ramadan, which is a month-long Muslim holiday. During this time, many shops and businesses may have restricted hours.

Best Time to Visit Kairouan

The best time to visit Kairouan is during the cooler months of November to March. The weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists. Summers in Kairouan can be very hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kairouan

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Try traditional Tunisian food
  • Visit the Great Mosque of Kairouan

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Take photographs of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Kairouan

Kairouan is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to take precautions. Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and taking valuables out in public. Political demonstrations can occur and should be avoided.

Health in Kairouan

Visitors should make sure they are up-to-date on their vaccinations before traveling to Tunisia. Tap water is not safe to drink, so visitors should stick to bottled water. Sunscreen and insect repellent are also important to bring.

Getting to Kairouan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kairouan

The nearest airport to Kairouan is Tunis–Carthage International Airport, which is located about 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Kairouan

The main bus station in Kairouan is located near the Medina.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get to Kairouan.

How to Get Around in Kairouan

The best way to get around Kairouan is on foot or by taxi.

Where to Go Next from Kairouan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sousse: A seaside town located about an hour away from Kairouan.
  • El Jem: This town is about 30 minutes away and is home to the famous Roman amphitheater.
  • Tunis: The capital city is located about 2 hours away and is home to several must-see sights, including the Bardo Museum.

Conclusion

Kairouan is a fascinating city that offers visitors a chance to experience traditional Tunisian life. With its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural landmarks, it’s a must-visit destination for any travel enthusiast.
