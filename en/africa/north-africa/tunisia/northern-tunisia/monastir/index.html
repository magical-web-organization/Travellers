Monastir is a charming coastal city located in northern Tunisia that offers visitors a wide range of attractions and activities to enjoy. With its relaxing beaches, stunning natural scenery, and rich history and culture, this city is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic, diverse Tunisian experience. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and other useful information to help plan your perfect trip to Monastir, Tunisia.

Introduction to Monastir

Monastir is a historic city that dates back to the 8th century, when it was founded by the Arabs. The city has a rich history that can be seen in its many beautiful monuments, mosques and museums. Monastir is also known for its beautiful beaches, where visitors can soak up the sun and enjoy the warm Mediterranean waters. The city boasts a relaxed, laid-back atmosphere, and visitors will find plenty of opportunities to experience Tunisian hospitality and culture during their stay.

List of Places to Visit in Monastir

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, there are several must-see attractions in Monastir that you won’t want to miss. The Ribat of Monastir is a fortress that was built in the 8th century and served as a defensive outpost for the Arabs. Today, it is one of the best-preserved Islamic monuments in North Africa and is well worth a visit. The Mausoleum of Habib Bourguiba, Tunisia’s founding father and first president, is another important historical monument that visitors should see. Here, you’ll find exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of Bourguiba and his role in Tunisia’s history.

Natural Attractions of Monastir: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will be pleased to know that Monastir has plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The city is surrounded by lush green landscapes and boasts stunning mountain and sea views. The El Jem Amphitheatre is a must-visit destination for those who love ancient architecture, as it is one of the best-preserved Roman amphitheatres in the world. Visitors can also hike or bike through the Ouled Khelifa Mountains, which boast stunning panoramic views of the city and the Mediterranean coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monastir

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast looking for adventure, Monastir has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. The Ouled Khelifa Mountains offer excellent opportunities for hiking, with trails ranging from easy to difficult. Visitors can also bike along the coast and take in the breathtaking views of the Mediterranean sea.

Best Things to Do in Monastir: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the city’s history and natural wonders, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to enjoy in Monastir. Visitors can take a camel ride along the beach, book a boat tour of the Mediterranean coastline, or try their hand at windsurfing or kiteboarding. Those looking for a relaxing experience can enjoy a traditional hammam (steam bath) or visit one of the city’s many spas for a massage or other treatments.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monastir

To make the most of your visit to Monastir, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Ribat of Monastir and explore the Medina. Day 2: Take a boat tour of the coastline and enjoy the beautiful views of the Mediterranean sea. Day 3: Visit the Mausoleum of Habib Bourguiba and learn about Tunisia’s history and founding father. Day 4: Go hiking or biking in the Ouled Khelifa Mountains. Day 5: Relax on the beach and soak up the sun. Day 6: Visit the El Jem Amphitheatre and learn about ancient Roman history. Day 7: Try windsurfing, kiteboarding, or book a spa day and enjoy a massage or other treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Monastir

Tunisian cuisine is famous for its delicious flavors and colorful spices, and Monastir is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of local dishes, from traditional couscous and tagines to seafood and grilled meats. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brik: a crispy, pastry-filled dough that’s typically stuffed with egg, tuna, and harissa (a spicy paste made of chili peppers, garlic, and olive oil).
  • Couscous: a dish made from steamed semolina grains, typically served with vegetables and meat or fish.
  • Shakshuka: a dish of poached eggs in a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Lablabi: a chickpea stew that’s typically served for breakfast.

Local Beverages

Tea is a staple drink in Tunisia and is typically served with mint and plenty of sugar. Coffee is also very popular, and visitors can enjoy a traditional Tunisian coffee with spices and sugar.

Where to Stay in Monastir: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monastir has a range of accommodation options to fit any budget. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort, a budget-friendly hostel, or a traditional guesthouse, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some popular hotels in the area include the Regency Hotel and the Sahel Club Hotel.

What to Buy in Monastir: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Monastir will find plenty of opportunities to shop for souvenirs and traditional products, including handmade ceramics, textiles, spices, and olive oil. The city’s Medina is a great place to find unique and authentic souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Monastir

Tunisian culture is heavily influenced by Islam, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Women should dress modestly, and it’s important to remove your shoes before entering a mosque or other religious building. Visitors should also be aware that Tunisians may observe different working hours than those in Western countries, with many businesses closing during prayer times.

Best Time to Visit Monastir

The best time to visit Monastir is during the spring (March - May) or the fall (September - November), when temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. The summer months (June - August) can be very hot and humid, while the winter months (December - February) can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monastir

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Be overly affectionate in public
  • Bring up politics or religion in conversation
  • Take photographs of people without asking for permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Monastir

Monastir is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes. It’s also important to be aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Monastir

Visitors to Monastir should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella, and should also consider getting vaccinated for hepatitis A and typhoid. Visitors should also take care to avoid drinking tap water and should only consume bottled water or other sealed beverages.

Getting to Monastir: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monastir

The closest airport to Monastir is the Habib Bourguiba International Airport, which is located about 8km from the city center. Some airlines that fly to this airport include Tunisair and Ryanair.

Bus Stations Near Monastir

Monastir has a central bus station that offers connections to several other Tunisian cities, including Tunis, Sousse, and Mahdia.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Monastir

Taxis are readily available in Monastir and are an affordable way to get around the city. Visitors can also rent a car or a bicycle if they prefer to explore on their own.

Where to Go Next from Monastir: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting from Monastir, including the coastal city of Sousse and the ancient city of Carthage. Visitors can also take a day trip to the Sahara desert for a truly unique and unforgettable experience.

Conclusion

Monastir is a unique and diverse city that offers visitors a range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions to explore. With its stunning landscapes, beautiful beaches, and warm hospitality, this is a destination that truly embodies the spirit of Tunisia. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Monastir.
