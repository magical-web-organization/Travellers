Introduction to Melilla

Melilla is a small Spanish autonomous city situated in North Africa. It’s an exotic city that boasts of its unique cultural blend of Spanish and Moroccan influence. During the 16th and 17th centuries, Melilla was under the reign of the Spanish empire, so it has numerous historical and cultural monuments from that time.

List of Places to Visit in Melilla

Here are some of the top attractions in Melilla that should be on every traveler’s itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Melilla la Vieja: A fascinating walled city that was the original settlement of the town.
  • Archaeological Museum: Home to several interesting exhibits that tell the story of Melilla’s history.
  • Military History Museum: The museum has an extensive collection of Moroccan and Spanish military artifacts.
  • Plaza de España: The main square of Melilla with a focal fountain and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Ensanada de los Galápagos: A beautiful marina that is a hub for nightlife and entertainment.

Natural Attractions of Melilla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Hernández: A beautiful park with fountains, sculptures, and well-manicured gardens.
  • Playa del Hipódromo: A sandy beach with azure waters, perfect for swimming or sunbathing.
  • Monte Gurugu: A mountain with steep trails leading to breathtaking views of the city and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Melilla

There are numerous routes in and around Melilla that are perfect for biking or hiking enthusiasts. One of the most popular is the Camino de Santiago, which stretches 900km and passes through Melilla on its way to Santiago.

Best Things to Do in Melilla: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of Melilla’s historic sites and learn about the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Visit the various museums in the town, as they hold a wealth of information about Melilla’s past.
  • Go scuba diving or snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters around the town.
  • Try the local seafood; Melilla is famous for its delicious fish dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Melilla

Day 1: Explore Melilla’s historic sites. Day 2: Take a day trip to Monte Gurugu and enjoy dramatic views of the city. Day 3: Take a dip in the turquoise waters of Playa del Hipódromo. Day 4: Visit Melilla’s museums and art galleries. Day 5: Go scuba diving and snorkeling to explore the marine life in the surrounding waters. Day 6: Cycle or hike along the Camino de Santiago trail. Day 7: Relax in Ensanada de los Galápagos, try the local cuisine or enjoy the nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Melilla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maimones: A garlic soup with a poached egg that is perfect for breakfast.
  • Migas: A hearty dish of fried breadcrumbs, chorizo, and peppers, a staple in Melilla.
  • Cuttlefish with potatoes: A succulent seafood dish that is a must-try in any local restaurant.
  • Sweet pastries: Melilla’s bakeries are famous for their delicious breakfast pastries and cookies.

Local Beverages

  • Sangria: A refreshing fruit-infused wine punch that is perfect for summertime.
  • Beer: The town has several local breweries, and their beers are fantastic.
  • Wine: Savor the local wine, which is a blend of Spanish and Moroccan grapes.

Where to Stay in Melilla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Melilla has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best hotels in the town include:

  • Parador de Melilla: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Melilla.
  • Tryp Melilla Puerto Hotel: A great option for a stay near the sea and entertainment venues.
  • Hostal El Dorado: A comfortable guesthouse with modern facilities and great service.

What to Buy in Melilla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and products to buy on your trip to Melilla:

  • Cactus fruit jam: Made from juicy cactus, this jam is a unique and delicious gift.
  • Moroccan-style ceramics: These ceramics have a unique design and make great decorative pieces.
  • Leather goods: Melilla is known for its high-quality leather products, including bags, shoes, and belts.
  • Spices and herbs: Melilla’s markets are filled with exotic spices and herbs that make a great addition to any kitchen.

Local Culture and Customs of Melilla

Melilla is a mix of Spanish and Moroccan culture, and it is common to see both Muslim and Catholic customs and practices. The town is famous for its vibrant and diverse culture, which is reflected in its architecture, art, music, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Melilla

The best time to visit Melilla is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be quite hot, while winters are mild and sometimes rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Melilla

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious places.
  • Respect local customs, especially during Ramadan, which is a holy month for Muslims in the town.
  • Try the local cuisine, which is exquisite and full of flavor.

Don’t:

  • Assume that locals speak English; it is better to learn some basic Spanish.
  • Drink tap water, as it can cause stomach problems for some foreigners.
  • Take photos of locals without their permission; it is considered rude in Melilla.

Safety and Health

Safety in Melilla

Melilla is generally safe, but travelers should take the regular precautions when in a new city, such as avoiding forlorn areas at night, taking care of their belongings and not flashing expensive items.

Health in Melilla

No vaccinations are required for travelers visiting Melilla, but it’s always advisable to consult your doctor for other critical information.

Getting to Melilla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Melilla

Melilla has its airport, the Melilla Airport (MLN). It serves direct flights to Madrid, Almeria, Algeciras, Malaga, and Barcelona all year round.

Bus Stations Near Melilla

Melilla Bus Station is strategically located in the city center, providing the visitors with a cheap and efficient way of traveling.

Other Options

One alternative is by ferry. The town provides ferries crossing daily from Almeria in Spain.

How to Get Around in Melilla

Getting around Melilla is easy due to its compact size. Public transportation links such as buses and taxis are affordable and readily available, while cycling, walking and driving are great options.

Where to Go Next from Melilla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Take a ferry to Spain or Morocco for some exciting day trips.
  • Visit nearby towns like Nador, located in Morocco on the other side of the border, an exciting house of craft markets and bustling streets.

Conclusion

Melilla is a charming and unique city in Africa that offers a combination of Spanish and Moroccan cultures, making it an excellent destination for travelers interested in history, culture, and cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Melilla with confidence and make the most of your time in this exotic location.
