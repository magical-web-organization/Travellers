Introduction to National Park of the Upper Niger

Discover the beautiful and culturally rich National Park of the Upper Niger located in Haute Guinee, Guinea, West Africa. This park is a must-visit for every nature lover and history enthusiast. The park covers an area of 855,000 hectares and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in National Park of the Upper Niger

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The National Park of the Upper Niger is home to several cultural and historical landmarks. To explore the rich culture and heritage of this region, consider visiting:

  • The Musée Nationale de Kankan
  • The Palace of Samory Touré
  • The Toma Village

Natural Attractions of National Park of the Upper Niger: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The National Park of the Upper Niger is blessed with natural beauty and adventure-filled activities. Some of the best outdoor experiences to explore include:

  • The Sankarani River
  • Bird Watching
  • Wildlife Safari
  • Hiking and Biking Trails

Hiking/Biking Routes of National Park of the Upper Niger

The National Park of the Upper Niger is a heaven for mountaineers, hikers, and bikers. Some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore include:

  • The Bandiagara Escarpment Trail
  • Mount Nimba Nature Reserve Trail
  • Konkouré River Trail

Local Culture and Customs of National Park of the Upper Niger

The National Park of the Upper Niger’s people are known for their hospitality and warmth. When visiting, be sure to respect their customs and practices. It’s also a good idea to learn a few basic words in French to communicate with the locals.

Best Time to Visit National Park of the Upper Niger

The best time to visit National Park of the Upper Niger is during the dry season from November to April. During this time, the weather is ideal for outdoor activities, and you can witness plenty of wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of National Park of the Upper Niger

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Carry enough cash in hand, as there are limited ATMs in the area

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the park
  • Don’t take pictures of people without their permission
  • Don’t buy or sell any endangered animal parts or products

Safety and Health

Safety in National Park of the Upper Niger

The National Park of the Upper Niger is relatively safe, but it’s always a good idea to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Traveling in groups
  • Avoiding dark or sketchy areas at night

Health in National Park of the Upper Niger

Visitors should get vaccinated against yellow fever before traveling to National Park of the Upper Niger. It’s also recommended to carry insect repellents and sunscreens for protection against mosquito bites and harsh sun rays.
