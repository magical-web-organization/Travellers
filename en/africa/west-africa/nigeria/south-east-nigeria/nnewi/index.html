Nnewi, located in the South East region of Nigeria, is known as the commercial hub of Anambra State. With its rich history, unique culture, and breathtaking landscapes, Nnewi is a perfect destination for all kinds of travelers. Whether you want to explore the city’s cultural heritage, experience outdoor adventures or indulge in local delicacies, Nnewi has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve compiled a list of the top tourist attractions, best places to stay, and must-try dishes to help you plan a perfect trip to Nnewi.

Introduction to Nnewi

Nnewi, also known as the ‘Japan of Africa’, is a bustling city in southeastern Nigeria, with a population of over half a million people. The city gained prominence during the colonial era and has since become a major industrial and commercial center. Nnewi is home to several powerful business moguls and has produced some of Nigeria’s wealthiest individuals. Despite its modern outlook, the city still retains its traditional roots and boasts of several historical and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Nnewi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Nnewi Museum and Cultural Centre: This museum showcases Nnewi’s rich cultural heritage and features a collection of traditional artifacts, artworks, and historical relics.
  2. Ogbunike Caves: These breathtaking caves hold a lot of historical and cultural significance for the people of Nnewi.
  3. The Palace of the Traditional Ruler of Nnewi: The palace is home to the monarch of the town and is a significant cultural landmark.

Natural Attractions of Nnewi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Owerre Ezukala Hills: This natural wonder offers a spectacular view of Nnewi and its surrounding cities.
  2. Iyi Oji Shrine: This natural spring is believed to have healing powers and attracts several tourists and locals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nnewi

Nnewi has several exciting biking and hiking trails that offer a unique way of exploring the city. Ogbunike Caves and Owerre Ezukala Hills are two excellent hiking sites, while Oji is a great spot to enjoy a leisurely bike ride.

Best Activities and Tours in Nnewi

  1. Visit the Nwafor Festival: This annual festival, held in Nnewi, celebrates the town’s rich cultural heritage and exhibits the best of their traditional customs.
  2. Explore Local Markets: Nnewi is home to several vibrant markets that offer a unique shopping experience, including ‘Nkwo Nnewi,’ the city’s largest market.
  3. Go on a Food Tour: Nnewi is famous for its local delicacies, including ‘akpu,’ a cassava-based dish, and ‘okpa,’ a snack made from ground beans. A food tour of Nnewi is a must-do activity for all foodies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nnewi

Day 1: Visit the Nnewi Museum and Cultural Center

Day 2: Hike the Ogbunike Caves

Day 3: Explore the local markets

Day 4: Take a scenic drive to Owerre Ezukala Hills

Day 5: Visit the Palace of the Traditional Ruler of Nnewi

Day 6: Attend the Nwafor Festival

Day 7: Go on a food tour and indulge in local delicacies

Food and Drink Scene of Nnewi

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Akpu: This cassava-based dish is a staple food in Nnewi and is usually served with soup or stew.
  2. Okpa: A local snack made from roasted and ground beans.

Local Beverages

  1. Palm Wine: A popular alcoholic drink made from fermented palm sap.
  2. Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus leaves.

Where to Stay in Nnewi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nnewi has several accommodation options for all types of travelers, including luxury hotels like the Empire Hotel, budget-friendly options like Ojimba Hotel, and guesthouses like the Obiano Lodge.

What to Buy in Nnewi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nnewi is known for its thriving local industry, and there are several unique products you can purchase as souvenirs, including locally-made pottery, traditional fabrics, and handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Nnewi

Nnewi’s culture is a mix of traditional and modern practices. The people of Nnewi are known for their entrepreneurial spirit and are proud of their cultural heritage. When visiting Nnewi, it is essential to remain respectful of local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Nnewi

The best time to visit Nnewi is during the dry season, which lasts from November to February. The weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nnewi

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Learn a few words of the local language.
  • Indulge in local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect the monarch or any traditional rulers.
  • Wander around at night without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nnewi

Nnewi is generally a safe city to visit, but travelers should remain vigilant and take standard safety precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Nnewi

Travelers should ensure they receive recommended vaccinations before visiting Nigeria. It’s also essential to bring along mosquito repellents and nets, as malaria is prevalent in the region.

Getting to Nnewi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nnewi

The nearest airport to Nnewi is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu.

Bus Stations Near Nnewi

There several bus stations in Nnewi, including the Nnewi Central Motorpark and Powerline bus terminal. Buses run regularly from Nnewi to major cities in Nigeria.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire within the city, and ride-sharing apps like Uber operate in Nnewi.

How to Get Around in Nnewi

Nnewi is a relatively small city, making it possible to explore most of the attractions on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Nnewi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nnewi is surrounded by several exciting destinations that are worth visiting, including:

  1. Onitsha: Known for its bustling markets and rich cultural heritage.
  2. Awka: An emerging city that has several beautiful tourist attractions.

Conclusion

Nnewi is a fascinating destination for both domestic and international tourists. The city’s rich cultural heritage, beautiful landscapes, and delicious cuisine make it a must-visit destination on any tour of Nigeria. This comprehensive travel guide provides you with all the information you need to plan a perfect trip to Nnewi.
