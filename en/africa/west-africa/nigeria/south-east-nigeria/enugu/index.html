Enugu, located in the southeastern region of Nigeria, is a city that offers a unique blend of historical and cultural attractions. It is known as the “coal city” due to its rich mining history, but it has since evolved into a bustling metropolis with vibrant entertainment and a lively art scene.

In this guide, we’ll highlight the must-see places, amazing food and drinks, and exciting activities to do in Enugu. Whether you’re a first-timer or a frequent traveler, this guide will help you curate the perfect itinerary for your visit to Enugu.

List of Places to Visit in Enugu

Here are the top places to visit in Enugu to make your trip unforgettable:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Unity: This is a must-visit for history buffs. It is home to over 2000 artifacts, including sculptures, pottery, and archeological findings. It depicts the national unity of Nigeria and showcases the historical blend of the country.
  • Iva Valley Coal Mines: A historic reminder of the mining industry which helped the city prosper. The mines are now closed, but visitors can see the remains of mining activities and learn about its brutal history.
  • Nike Art Gallery: The gallery showcases stunning works of African art and crafts. It is perfect for art lovers, and visitors can purchase unique African souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Enugu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Milken Hill: The hill offers an incredible bird’s eye view of the city. It’s a perfect spot for hiking, picnics, and taking stunning photographs.
  • Ezeagu Tourist Complex: It is a natural park with waterfalls, swimming areas, caves, and primate conservation center. It’s perfect for tourists who want to engage in outdoor activities such as hiking, swimming, picnicking, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Enugu

One of the best ways to explore Enugu is through hiking and biking. Here are some routes to check out:

  • Ogige Hills: This route has a serene and enjoyable view that is perfect for bikers and hikers. It is known for its steep slopes, which add to the challenge of hiking.
  • Amankwo–Ndeagu Road: A route that is popular among the local population for biking and hiking. It is ideal for trekkers as it combines both the city and rural views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Enugu: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities to engage in while in Enugu. Here are some of the best:

  • Volleyball & Basketball: Enugu is known for its vibrant sports teams, and joining in on some of the team’s practices or games can be an exciting experience.
  • Lifestyle pools: It’s a public pool with cozy changing room facilities and an onsite restaurant. It’s perfect for families, group activities, and a good spot for swimming competitions.

Food and Drink Scene of Enugu

The food and drink scene in Enugu boasts a rich culinary culture. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages to savor while you’re there.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nsala Soup: A delicious local delicacy that consists of chicken, beef or goat meat seasoned with special African herbs and spices.
  • Abacha (African Salad): A mixture of cassava, ugba, and vegetables.
  • Ofe Akwu: It is a soup prepared with fresh pepper, dry akwu, fresh fish, and stockfish.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A traditional alcoholic beverage obtained from the sap of different species of palm trees. It is a must-try if you are lover of alcoholic drinks.
  • Zobo: A non-alcoholic drink made from the dried roselle plant flowers that are then boiled in water. It is a refreshing drink perfect for a summer afternoon.

What to Buy in Enugu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Enugu, don’t miss out on the chance to take home some of the city’s unique souvenirs, arts, and crafts. Here are some suggestions of what to consider buying:

  • Leather Wallet or Bag: Enugu is known for its leather production, and you can find an array of quality and affordable leather bags and wallets perfect for souvenirs.
  • African Fabrics: African fabrics are a popular souvenir item. You can find colorful, unique patterns of traditional West African fabric or contemporary fabrics like Ankara and Kente cloth, making for a great item to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Enugu

When visiting Enugu, it’s important to respect the customs and traditions of the local population. Here are some key cultural insights you should keep in mind:

  • Greetings: The most common traditional greeting in Enugu is “Kedu,” which translates to “How are you?”
  • Dress Code: There are no specific dress codes, but it’s advised to wear appropriate clothing when visiting religious places.
  • Respect for Elders: Respect for the elderly is an essential part of the culture in Enugu. Titled men and women are also revered.

Best Time to Visit Enugu

The best time to visit Enugu is during the dry season, between November and April. During this period, the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are easier to engage in.

Do’s and Don’ts of Enugu

Do:

  • Accept the invitation for a free meal from locals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local street foods.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the elderly or locals.
  • Remove or damage relics, historic sites, and art installations.
  • Wander alone in the rural areas when it is getting late.

Safety and Health

Safety in Enugu

Enugu is relatively safe compared to other Nigerian cities. However, visitors should still take caution and stay vigilant when exploring the city. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t wander into unknown neighborhoods alone.
  • Avoid conspicuous displays of wealth, and keep your valuables secure.
  • Stay informed on local travel advisories.

Health in Enugu

Visitors are required to get vaccinated against Yellow Fever and must carry a valid certificate. Also, visitors should be cautious and take preventative measures against malaria, dengue fever, and other mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Enugu: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get into Enugu. Here are the most common:

Airports Near Enugu

  • Akanu Ibiam International Airport (ENU) is the closest airport to Enugu, located 17 km (10.5 miles) away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Enugu

  • The major bus terminus in Enugu is the Old Park, which offers bus services from major Nigerian cities, including Lagos, Abuja, and Kano.

How to Get Around in Enugu

Some of the most popular ways to get around Enugu are through:

  • Taxis: Taxis are the most common way to get around the city.
  • Keke Napep: This auto-rickshaw is a common means of transportation in Enugu and generally more affordable than taxis. They are easily accessible, and with the help of the driver, you can easily navigate and get around the city.
  • Bus: There are city buses that service several routes around Enugu.

Where to Go Next from Enugu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several places worth visiting in the nearby regions of Enugu. Here are some suggestions:

  • Nigerian National Museum, Lagos: The museum is a rich repository of Nigerian art, history, and culture.
  • Idanre Hills, Ondo State: The hills are a landscape of towering peaks, streams, and valleys. It’s a great adventure for hikers and history enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Enugu is a city with a fascinating culture and history offering an excellent blend of city splendor and nature’s beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you can now plan your travel itinerary and make the most of your visit to Enugu. Remember to keep updated with the latest travel advisories and to stay safe during your visit.
