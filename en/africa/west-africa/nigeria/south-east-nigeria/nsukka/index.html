Introduction to Nsukka

Located in the South East region of Nigeria, Nsukka is a bustling town with a rich cultural heritage. It is home to the prestigious University of Nigeria, as well as several museums, historical sites, and natural attractions. Visitors to Nsukka can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, savour the local dishes and drinks, and discover the unique customs of the local people. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your visit to Nsukka.

List of Places to Visit in Nsukka

Culturally Important Attractions: museums and historical places

University of Nigeria Art Gallery

The University of Nigeria Art Gallery boasts an impressive collection of traditional and contemporary African art. The exhibits include hand-woven textiles, wood carvings, sculptures, and paintings. Visitors can learn about the various Nigerian cultures represented in the gallery and appreciate the artistic talents of local artisans.

Opi Lake Complex

The Opi Lake Complex is a collection of interconnected lakes that are home to a diverse range of wildlife. The complex offers visitors the opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the area by boat, fishing, or birdwatching. It is a peaceful retreat that is ideal for nature lovers.

Onuiyi Haven

Onuiyi Haven is a serene resort that offers visitors a chance to relax and unwind. It features lush gardens, a swimming pool, and several restaurants. The resort also has an impressive collection of traditional Nigerian art and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Nsukka: outdoor adventures and scenic views

Onyema Waterfalls

Onyema Waterfalls is a stunning natural attraction that is tucked away in the hills of Nsukka. It is a popular spot for hikers and nature lovers who enjoy the challenge of trekking through the rocky terrain to reach the falls. Visitors are rewarded with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and the refreshing pools at the base of the falls.

Okpuje Forest Reserve

Okpuje Forest Reserve is a lush and natural forest that provides a serene and peaceful environment for visitors looking to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life. It is home to a wide range of plant and animal species, making it an ideal destination for nature walks and birdwatching.

Ngwo Pine Forest

The Ngwo Pine Forest is a dense pine forest that offers a picturesque setting for picnics, hikes, and outdoor recreation. The forest is located near the Ngwo Cave and Waterfall, another popular natural attraction in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nsukka

Nsukka offers some of the most challenging and rewarding hiking and biking routes in Nigeria. These include:

  • Nsukka-Obollo Afor Hike: This 14km hike takes visitors through the scenic hills and villages of Nsukka, past traditional houses and agricultural lands, and through the Okpuje Forest Reserve.
  • Ozalla-Akpanyi Hike: This 15km hike takes visitors through the Ngwo Pine Forest, past the Ngwo Cave and Waterfall, and through the beautiful countryside of Nsukka.
  • Ohebe-Dim Hike: This 8km hike takes visitors along the banks of the River Ohebe, past the scenic Onyema Waterfalls, and through beautiful forests and villages.

Best Things to Do in Nsukka: Activities and Tours

Campus Tour of University of Nigeria

The University of Nigeria is one of the most prestigious universities in Nigeria, and its sprawling campus offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Nsukka. The campus has several museums, art galleries, and cultural centres that showcase the diverse Nigerian cultures. Visitors can take a guided tour of the campus to learn about its history, architecture, and cultural significance.

Traditional Nigerian Dance and Music Performance

Nsukka is home to several cultural centres that offer visitors an opportunity to experience traditional Nigerian dance and music performances. These performances are a vibrant celebration of the local culture and feature colourful costumes, energetic music, and lively dancing.

Food and Drink Tasting Tour

The food and drink scene in Nsukka is diverse and delicious, and visitors can get a taste of local cuisine by taking a food and drink tasting tour. These tours explore the local markets and eateries and offer visitors a chance to sample traditional dishes like yam porridge, akara, and pepper soup, as well as local drinks like palm wine and zobo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nsukka

Day 1: Arrive in Nsukka and check into your accommodation. Day 2: Take a guided tour of the University of Nigeria Campus and visit the University of Nigeria Art Gallery. Day 3: Visit the Onyema Waterfalls and go for a hike in the Okpuje Forest Reserve. Day 4: Explore the Ngwo Pine Forest and visit the Ngwo Cave and Waterfall. Day 5: Take a food and drink tasting tour of Nsukka. Day 6: Visit the Opi Lake Complex and go on a boat ride or birdwatching expedition. Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Onuiyi Haven Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Nsukka

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yam Porridge: A hearty and nutritious dish made from yam, vegetables, and spices.
  • Akara: A deep-fried bean cake that is a popular breakfast item in Nsukka.
  • Pepper Soup: A spicy and flavourful soup made from meat or fish and a variety of vegetables and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A traditional Nigerian drink made from the sap of palm trees, with a slightly sour and sweet flavour.
  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers and spices, often served chilled.

Where to Stay in Nsukka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nsukka has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Rexon Guesthouse: A comfortable guesthouse located in the heart of Nsukka, with affordable rates and friendly staff.
  • Spring Valley Resort: A luxurious resort that offers guests a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, spa services, and a restaurant.
  • De Meez Guest House: A modern and affordable guesthouse that is popular with budget travellers.

What to Buy in Nsukka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nsukka is known for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, and visitors can find a range of unique and high-quality souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Hand-woven textiles
  • Traditional Nigerian sculptures and carvings
  • Palm wine and zobo

Local Culture and Customs of Nsukka

The people of Nsukka are proud of their cultural heritage and are renowned for their strong sense of community. Some of the most important customs and traditions in Nsukka include:

  • The New Yam Festival: This is a traditional yam festival that is celebrated every year to mark the beginning of the yam harvest. It is a colourful and vibrant celebration of the local culture, featuring music, dancing, and traditional attire.
  • Traditional Masquerade: Nsukka is home to several traditional masquerades, which are a form of cultural expression and entertainment. The masquerades feature colourful costumes and masks and are accompanied by music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Nsukka

The best time to visit Nsukka is between November and February when the weather is mild and dry. It is a popular time for outdoor activities and festivals, and the scenery is at its best. The rainy season, which runs from March to October, can make hiking and outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nsukka

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Visit the natural attractions and interact with the local people.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Wander off into the forest or hike alone without a guide.
  • Take photographs without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nsukka

Nsukka is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard precautions like avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Nsukka

Nsukka has several medical facilities, but visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical treatment. It is also advisable to check with a doctor before travelling to Nsukka to receive any recommended vaccinations.

Getting to Nsukka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nsukka

The nearest airport to Nsukka is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu, which is approximately 1 hour away by car. Visitors can take a taxi or use a car rental service to travel to Nsukka.

Bus Stations Near Nsukka

Nsukka has several bus stations that connect it to other towns and cities in Nigeria, including Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. Some of the most popular bus stations in Nsukka include the Nsukka Park, Otoko Bus Stop, and Ekwegbe Bus Stop.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or use a car rental service to travel to Nsukka. It is advisable to make arrangements in advance to ensure availability.

How to Get Around in Nsukka

Nsukka is a relatively small town, and visitors can explore most of its attractions on foot. Taxis and motorbikes (known locally as okadas) are also available for hire, and visitors can negotiate their rates with the drivers.

Where to Go Next from Nsukka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nsukka is surrounded by several towns and cities that offer visitors an opportunity to experience different aspects of Nigerian culture and heritage. Some of the most popular nearby destinations include:

  • Enugu: Known as the “coal city,” Enugu is a bustling city that is home to several museums, bustling markets, and cultural festivals.
  • Nkwo Nike: Nkwo Nike is a small town known for its indigenous textile industry, where visitors can learn about the traditional methods of weaving, dyeing, and printing textiles.
  • Udi Hills: The Udi hills are located just outside of Enugu and offer visitors stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is a popular destination for hiking and outdoor recreation.

Conclusion

Nsukka is a hidden gem in Nigeria, offering visitors a unique and immersive cultural experience that is unmatched in the country. From its vibrant arts and crafts scene to its stunning natural attractions, there is something for everyone in Nsukka. Use this guide to plan your visit to Nsukka and experience the best that this wonderful town has to offer.
