Introduction to Umunya

Located in Anambra State, in the South East region of Nigeria, Umunya is a beautiful town that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. The town is known for its rich history and cultural heritage, as well as its beautiful outdoor scenery. If you are looking for a peaceful and relaxing getaway, Umunya is definitely worth a visit. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will give you an overview of the town, list the best places to visit, and provide you with tips for making the most out of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Umunya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Umugbalu Ndi-Igbo Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the cultural heritage of the Igbo people. It showcases a collection of artifacts, artworks, and traditional items that date back to ancient times.

Olugwu Palace: A historic palace that was once the residence of the traditional ruler of Umunya, the Olugwu. Today, it has been converted into a museum that houses artifacts and relics from the palace and the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Umunya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mgbowo Waterfall: Located a few minutes outside Umunya, this beautiful waterfall is a hidden gem. It offers a peaceful and serene atmosphere where you can relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the surroundings.

Obiekwe Lake: A serene and beautiful lake that is perfect for fishing and picnicking. You can also take a boat ride across the lake to enjoy the scenery and surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umunya

Ndiokpaleke Hills Trail: This is a popular hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful hills surrounding Umunya. The trail offers panoramic views of the town and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Umunya: Activities and Tours

Visit the Umunya Art Centre: This art center is a hub for local artists and craftsmen. Here, you can buy local arts and crafts, attend workshops, and see exhibitions.

Take a boat ride on the Obiekwe lake: Exploring the lake on a boat is a must-do activity in Umunya. You can rent a boat with a guide, who will show you the best spots for fishing and enjoying the scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Umunya

Must-Try Dishes

Bitterleaf Soup: A traditional Igbo soup made with bitter leaf, meat, fish, and other ingredients. It is a rich and flavorful dish that is a must-try in Umunya.

Nkwobi: A spicy dish made with cow foot, onions, and native spices. It is usually served with a cold drink and is perfect for a hot day.

Local Beverages

Palm Wine: This is a delicious traditional beverage that is popular in Umunya. It is made from the sap of palm trees and has a slightly sweet taste.

Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers, water, and sugar. It is a popular local drink that is perfect for a hot day.

What to Buy in Umunya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Igbo Art and Crafts: The Umunya Art Center is a good place to buy local arts and crafts. You can find paintings, sculptures, and other unique items made by local artists.

Local Spices: The town is known for its delicious local spices, which you can buy in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Umunya

The people of Umunya are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their culture and traditions. It is important to respect their customs and behave appropriately at all times.

Best Time to Visit Umunya

The best time to visit Umunya is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. This is also the time when some of the town’s major cultural festivals are held.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umunya

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Enter private residences without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Umunya

Umuya is generally a safe town, but it is important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep your valuables safe at all times.

Health in Umunya

It is important to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as malaria is common in the region. It is also recommended to drink only bottled or treated water to avoid stomach illnesses.

Getting to Umunya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Umunya

The nearest airport to Umunya is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu, which is about 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Umunya

The town has a bus station that provides regular transportation to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available in Umunya, and can be arranged through your accommodation or local travel agencies.

How to Get Around in Umunya

Taxis and motorcycles are the most common means of transportation in Umunya. It is important to negotiate the fare before starting your journey.

Conclusion

Umuya is a beautiful and charming town that is rich in culture and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in history, art, or outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Umunya. Plan your trip carefully, and you are sure to have a memorable and unforgettable experience.
