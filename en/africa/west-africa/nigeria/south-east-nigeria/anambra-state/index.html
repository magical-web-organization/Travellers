Introduction to Anambra State

Anambra State is a land of history, culture, and tradition in South East Nigeria. It is home to a vibrant mix of ethnic groups, including the Igbo people, who make up the majority of the population. Anambra State is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful landscapes, and bustling urban centers. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or gastronomic delights, Anambra State has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Anambra State

Here are the top attractions and places to visit in Anambra State:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Igbo-Ukwu Archeological Sites: Explore an ancient civilization that spans over 1000 years at this UNESCO World Heritage Site. The site is home to intricate bronze works, terracotta sculptures, and earthenware vessels, some of which date as far back as the 9th century.

  • Ogbunike Caves: This network of caves is believed to possess spiritual significance and is sacred to the Igbo people. Visitors can explore the natural beauty of the caves and learn about the local folklore associated with them.

  • Nnamdi Azikiwe Mausoleum: Pay homage to one of Nigeria’s foremost nationalists and first President at his final resting place in Onitsha.

Natural Attractions of Anambra State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Agulu Lake: Nestled in the serene town of Agulu, this lake is a natural wonder that draws visitors from every corner of the globe. It is fed by underground springs and its crystal-clear waters offer a serene environment for boating or fishing.

  • Oguta Lake: This picturesque lake is located in Oguta, and its peaceful surroundings make it an ideal spot for swimming, picnicking or taking a scenic boat ride.

  • Ezu River: Enjoy the calming ambiance and natural beauty of the Ezu River, located in the heart of the state.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anambra State

  • Ogbaukwu Cave Hike: Embark on a scenic hike through the ancient Ogbaukwu cave system and marvel at the natural rock formations and beautiful waterfalls.

  • Anambra River Trail: Take on this serene river trail, which is great for both hiking and biking. The trail offers breathtaking views of the countryside and traverses through several small towns.

Best Things to Do in Anambra State: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Artisan Markets: Take a stroll through the bustling markets of Onitsha and Nnewi and witness the bustling daily life of the locals.

  • Discover Igbo Culture: Visit some of the cultural centers and learn about the customs, history, and traditions of the Igbo people.

  • Indulge in Local Cuisine: Satisfy your taste buds with authentic Nigerian dishes such as Jollof rice, Banga soup, and Egusi soup.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anambra State

Day Activity
1. Visit the Igbo-Ukwu Archaeological Sites
2. Take a scenic boat ride on Oguta Lake
3. Embark on a hike through Ogbaukwu Cave System
4. Learn about Igbo heritage at the National Museum in Onitsha
5. Take a stroll through artisan markets in Nnewi
6. Visit the historic town of Aguleri and learn about its rich culture
7. Tour the impressive Ogbo-Ugu Caves and enjoy a picnic.

Food and Drink Scene of Anambra State

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice: This flavorful rice dish is a Nigerian staple made with rice, tomato sauce, and a medley of spices.

  • Banga Soup: A delicious soup made with palm nut extract, beef, and an assortment of vegetables, and spices.

  • Egusi Soup: A thick soup made with ground melon seeds, meat, fish, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.

  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus leaves and spices.

  • Lakewood Hotels and Suites: This hotel blends modern amenities with traditional designs, featuring an on-site restaurant and fitness center.

  • Ceekay Suites: A modest hotel with a comfortable atmosphere and friendly staff, located in Nnewi.

What to Buy in Anambra State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Igbo Attire: Check out some of the vibrant, locally made garments and fashion items sourced from the marketplaces.

  • Local Crafts: Support the local artisans by purchasing some of the unique local artwork, sculptures and souvenirs.

  • Egusi Soup Spice Blend: Bring home some of the spices used in the preparation of Anambra state’s signature dish.

Local Culture and Customs of Anambra State

Anambra State is home to the predominantly Igbo people and their culture, customs, and traditions. Some of the most notable customs include prostrating to elders to show respect, giving of kola nuts as a sign of hospitality, and traditional marriage rites involving elaborate ceremonies, gift giving, and negotiation.

Best Time to Visit Anambra State

The best time to visit Anambra State is between November and February when the weather is mild and temperate. However, visitors can enjoy the state’s attractions all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anambra State

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly when visiting religious, cultural or historical sites.

  • Show respect towards elders and local customs.

  • Try out the local food, particularly the signature dishes of Anambra State.

Don’t:

  • Display affluence in public as this may attract unwanted attention.

  • Disrespect local customs, particularly during traditional ceremonies and rituals.

  • Drink tap water, especially when travelling to rural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anambra State

Anambra State is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to travel in groups, avoid walking alone at night and make use of licensed taxis when commuting.

Health in Anambra State

Visitors are encouraged to take prophylaxis against malaria and typhoid fever before visiting Anambra State. It is also advisable to drink bottled water, to ensure optimal health and wellness during the trip.

Getting to Anambra State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anambra State

The closest airport to Anambra State is the Asaba International Airport, located approximately 30 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Anambra State

Anambra State is accessible by bus transport from nearby cities such as Lagos, Abuja, and Onitsha.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for private transportation such as car rentals, cabs or motorcycle taxis.

How to Get Around in Anambra State

The best way to get around Anambra State is by car, taxi, or motorcycle taxi. Private car hires and taxis are also readily available, however, visitors should negotiate the fares before the trip.

Where to Go Next from Anambra State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Delta State: Explore the bustling city of Asaba or visit the historic town of Oshimili for an interesting cultural experience.

  • Imo State: Head to the city of Owerri and explore its striking architecture, serene parks, and gardens.

Conclusion

Anambra State offers something for every traveler, from its rich cultural heritage, to its beautiful landscapes, and diverse culinary traditions. Whether you are seeking an outdoor adventure or a cultural immersion, Anambra State has it all.
