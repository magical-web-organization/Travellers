Ebonyi State, located in South East Nigeria, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. From lush forests to breathtaking waterfalls and historic landmarks, Ebonyi has much to offer for travelers seeking adventure and cultural experiences.

Introduction to Ebonyi State

Ebonyi State, also known as the “Salt of the Nation,” is a landlocked state located in the southeastern part of Nigeria. The state was created in 1996 from the old Abia and Enugu states, and has a diverse ethnic population consisting mainly of Igbo, Izzi, and Ikwo people.

Ebonyi State is known for its rich mineral resources including salt, lead, zinc, limestone, and coal. The state capital, Abakaliki, is a bustling city with a unique blend of modern life and local traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Ebonyi State

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ebonyi State has several museums and historical attractions that provide insight into the state’s cultural heritage. The popular ones include:

  1. National Museum of Unity: The museum is located in Abakaliki and houses a collection of historical artifacts, traditional arts and crafts, and archaeological exhibits.

  2. Amanchore Cave: A historical cave that served as a refuge during the slave trade era.

  3. Abakaliki Township Stadium: A historical site where Nigeria’s first football league match was played in 1972.

Natural Attractions of Ebonyi State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ebonyi State boasts of natural attractions that are popular with locals and tourists alike. Here are some of the best:

  1. Okposi Salt Lake and Salt Village: A scenic lake and traditional salt market that has been in existence for over 400 years.

  2. Awhum Waterfall: A mesmerizing waterfall that is surrounded by lush greenery and perfect for hiking and picnicking.

  3. Ndibe Beach: A serene beach with crystal-clear water and white sand, perfect for swimming and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ebonyi State

Ebonyi State has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and exciting adventures. The most popular ones are:

  1. Nwezenyi Hill: A scenic hill that provides a panoramic view of Abakaliki and its environs.

  2. Ngwo Pine Forest: A natural forest reserve that offers excellent trails for hiking, trekking, and mountain biking.

  3. Uburu Salt Lake Cliffs: A natural cliff with a view of the surrounding landscape that is perfect for hiking and picnicking.

Best Things to Do in Ebonyi State: Activities and Tours

There are several exciting activities and tours to take part in, including:

  1. Canoeing on Oferekpe River: Explore the scenic river while enjoying the serenity of nature.

  2. Iyi Udene natural swimming pool: A hidden gem, the pool offers an opportunity to swim in crystal-clear water surrounded by lush greenery.

  3. Visit a local salt factory: Learn about the traditional process of making salt at one of the many salt factories around the state.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ebonyi State

Day 1: Arrive in Abakaliki, visit the National Museum of Unity and Amanchore Cave.

Day 2: Visit Okposi Salt Lake and Salt Village, and hike around the lake.

Day 3: Trek to Uburu Salt Lake Cliffs and enjoy picnicking.

Day 4: Visit Awhum Waterfall and hike around the waterfall.

Day 5: Go canoeing on the Oferekpe River and relax by the water.

Day 6: Take a nature walk in Ngwo Pine Forest and discover the evergreen vegetation.

Day 7: Visit Ndibe Beach and swim in the crystal-clear water.

Food and Drink Scene of Ebonyi State

Must-Try Dishes

Ebonyi State is known for its delicious local cuisine that is a blend of traditional and modern flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

  1. Ukwa: A porridge made from breadfruit that is usually eaten with roasted or fried fish.

  2. Akpu and Oha soup: A popular dish made from cassava flour and a native soup which has a chlorophyll taste.

  3. Nkwobi: A spicy dish made from cow foot and served with a chilled drink.

Local Beverages

Ebonyi State boasts of several refreshing local beverages that are a delight to the taste buds. The most popular ones include:

  1. Palm wine: A natural wine extracted from palm trees and known for its unique flavor.

  2. Zobo: A refreshing drink made from dried hibiscus flowers.

  3. Kunu: A nutritious drink made from grains and nuts.

Where to Stay in Ebonyi State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ebonyi State has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best include:

  1. Pine Park Hotels: A luxury hotel located in Abakaliki with modern amenities and friendly staff.

  2. Metro View Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in Abakaliki with comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  3. Alexico Hotel: A well-maintained hotel with an outdoor swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.

What to Buy in Ebonyi State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ebonyi State is known for its unique handicrafts and locally made products. Some items to buy as souvenirs or gifts include:

  1. Akwete cloth: A woven cloth made from cotton and silk and used to make traditional attire.

  2. Traditional musical instruments: Ebonyi is known for its traditional musical instruments such as the Ekwe and Ogene.

  3. Handmade pottery: Ebonyi has several skilled potters who make stunning handmade pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Ebonyi State

Ebonyi State has a rich cultural heritage that is unique and colorful. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are encouraged to learn and experience their customs and traditions.

Some cultural practices to note include:

  1. The New Yam festival: a colorful harvest festival that takes place between August and September.

  2. The Ikwokrikwo Masquerade: a traditional masquerade that is performed during festivals and special occasions.

  3. The Afo Festival: a cultural festival celebrated by the Izzi people to herald the new year.

Best Time to Visit Ebonyi State

The best time to visit Ebonyi State is between October and April when the weather is cooler and conducive for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ebonyi State

Do:

  1. Greet the locals warmly and respectfully.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  3. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of people without their permission.
  2. Eat food or drink water from an unknown source.
  3. Wear revealing clothes in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ebonyi State

Ebonyi State is generally safe for tourists, but visitors are advised to take safety precautions such as:

  1. Avoid traveling alone at night.
  2. Keep your valuables secure.
  3. Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Ebonyi State

Ebonyi State has several hospitals and clinics that offer medical services to locals and tourists. Visitors are advised to:

  1. Use mosquito nets and insect repellent to prevent malaria.
  2. Drink bottled water or boiled water to prevent waterborne diseases.
  3. Get vaccinated before traveling to the state.

Getting to Ebonyi State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ebonyi State

The nearest airport to Ebonyi State is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport Enugu, which is about 92km from Abakaliki.

Bus Stations Near Ebonyi State

Several buses operate regular services from major cities to Ebonyi State. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Peace Mass Transit
  • Chisco Transport
  • God is Good Motors

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to get to Ebonyi State from neighboring states or major cities.

How to Get Around in Ebonyi State

Visitors can get around Ebonyi State by hiring a car, taking a taxi, or using motorcycle taxis, also known as “Okadas.”

Where to Go Next from Ebonyi State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Ebonyi State include:

  1. Enugu State: A neighboring state known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic views.

  2. Cross River State: Located south of Ebonyi, the state boasts of lush forests and warm sandy beaches.

  3. Calabar: A popular destination in Cross River State known for its rich history and cultural festivals.

Conclusion

Ebonyi State, with its natural beauty, friendly locals, and rich cultural heritage, is a must-visit destination for tourists looking for a unique African adventure. From exploring the salt markets to swimming in the crystal-clear waters of Ndibe Beach, Ebonyi State has something for everyone. Plan your trip and explore this hidden gem in South East Nigeria.
