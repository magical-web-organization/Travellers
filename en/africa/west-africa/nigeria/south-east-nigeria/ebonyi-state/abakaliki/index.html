Abakaliki is a city located in Ebonyi State, in South East Nigeria, in West Africa. It is rich in history, culture, and natural attractions that make it a popular tourist destination in Nigeria. This comprehensive tour guide for Abakaliki will help you plan your trip to make the most of your stay. We will cover a range of topics from the top places to visit and things to do, to local customs, and transportation options.

Introduction to Abakaliki

Abakaliki is a bustling city full of energy, culture, and history. It’s a great place to immerse yourself in Nigerian culture, try Nigerian cuisine, and experience the beauty of nature. The city is located in a valley and is surrounded by hills, making it a picturesque place to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Abakaliki

Abakaliki has a diverse range of attractions, including museums, historical sites, and natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Akanu Ibiam Federal Polytechnic Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the people of Abakaliki. It has a collection of ancient artifacts, traditional clothing, and other items of cultural significance.

  • Mmanwu Cultural Dance: This is an annual festival that celebrates the culture and heritage of Abakaliki. It features colorful costumes, music, dance performances, and cultural displays.

  • Afikpo Village War Dance: This dance is performed by warriors from Afikpo Village, who wear traditional costumes and weapons. It is a lively and energetic display of culture and heritage.

Natural Attractions of Abakaliki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ndibe Beach: This is a picturesque beach located on the shores of the Cross River. It’s a great place to swim, sunbathe, and relax.

  • Azumini Blue River: This river is known for its clear blue water and sandy beaches. It’s a great place to go for a swim or take a boat ride.

  • Amancho Wildlife Reserve: This reserve is home to a variety of wildlife species, including elephants, monkeys, and antelopes. It’s a great place for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abakaliki

Abakaliki is surrounded by hills and mountains, making it a great location for hiking and biking adventures. Here are some popular routes:

  • Okposi-Umuobo-Uburu: This is a scenic route that takes you through picturesque villages and beautiful scenery.

  • Enugu-Abakaliki Road: This is a popular cycling route that offers stunning views of the countryside.

  • Onicha-Edda-Ugboju Road: This is another scenic route that takes you through some of the most beautiful parts of Abakaliki.

Best Things to Do in Abakaliki: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting attractions, there are many things to do in Abakaliki. Some of the best activities include:

  • Taste the Local Cuisine: Abakaliki is known for its delicious cuisine, which includes dishes like Ofe Akwu, Ukwa, and Nkwobi.

  • Visit Local Markets: Abakaliki has several markets where you can buy local handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

  • Attend Local Festivals: Abakaliki hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Mmanwu Cultural Dance and Afikpo Village War Dance.

Food and Drink Scene of Abakaliki

Abakaliki offers a wide variety of cuisine that reflects the culture and history of the region. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ofe Akwu: A soup made with palm fruit extract, stockfish, and other ingredients. It is often served with pounded yam or fufu.

  • Ukwa: A porridge made from African breadfruit that is boiled and mashed with salt, pepper, and other spices.

  • Nkwobi: A spicy dish made from cow foot cooked in a special sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is a traditional Nigerian drink made from the sap of palm trees.

  • Kunu: A local drink made from a blend of grains and spices, often served chilled.

What to Buy in Abakaliki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Abakaliki has several markets where you can buy local handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs. Some popular items include:

  • Adire Fabric: This is a unique African textile made by tying and dying cloth to create intricate patterns and designs.

  • Hand-woven Baskets: These are made from locally sourced materials and come in a variety of sizes and designs.

  • Wooden Sculptures: These are made by local artists and depict scenes from Nigerian history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Abakaliki

Abakaliki is home to several ethnic groups, each with their unique customs and traditions. It’s important to respect these traditions when visiting the city. Some local customs and culture include:

  • The Atilogwu Dance: This is a dance performed by the Igbo people to celebrate special occasions.

  • The New Yam Festival: This festival is celebrated by several ethnic groups in Abakaliki to mark the end of the harvest season.

Best Time to Visit Abakaliki

The best time to visit Abakaliki is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. The weather is mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable during this time.
