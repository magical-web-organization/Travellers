Abiriba is a beautiful town located in Abia State, in the South East region of Nigeria. Known as the “small London of Africa,” Abiriba boasts a rich cultural heritage, unique traditions, and outdoor adventures that make it a top destination for travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and local customs in Abiriba.

Introduction to Abiriba

Abiriba is a town with a rich cultural heritage, unique traditions, and a history that dates back several centuries. It is located in the Umuahia North Local Government Area of Abia State and is known as the home of the “Ngu” festival, which is celebrated annual. The festival is known for its colorful masquerades, traditional dances, and vibrant cultural displays. In addition to the “Ngu” festival, Abiriba is known for its unique architecture, which includes tall, white-washed buildings with distinct arches and domes. These buildings, which are scattered throughout the town, have given Abiriba the nickname of the “small London of Africa”.

List of Places to Visit in Abiriba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Abiriba has several cultural attractions that offer insight into the town’s history and traditions. The following are some of the must-visit museums and historical sites in Abiriba:

  • The Abiriba Heritage Museum
  • The Ekpo Abiriba Shrine
  • The Ogoja War Memorial
  • The Amaogudu Cave

Natural Attractions of Abiriba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Abiriba is blessed with stunning natural beauty, and these natural attractions showcase the town’s exquisite nature. Some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic views in Abiriba include:

  • The Azumini Blue River
  • The Long Juju Slave Route
  • Ubani Hills

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abiriba

Abiriba offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Nigeria, with trails suitable for all levels of experience. The following are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Abiriba:

Local Culture and Customs of Abiriba

Abiriba is known for its rich cultural heritage, traditions, and customs. Visitors are advised to respect the local culture and customs while in Abiriba.

Best Time to Visit Abiriba

The best time to visit Abiriba is between November and February when the weather is dry and cool. The town is also alive with the colorful “Ngu” festival celebrations in December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abiriba

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Wear skimpy or revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abiriba

Abiriba is generally a safe town, but visitors are advised to take safety precautions like keeping their valuables, passports, and identification documents in a secure place.

Health in Abiriba

Abiriba is relatively low-risk when it comes to health, but travelers should take precautions like wearing mosquito repellent and getting vaccinated against typhoid fever and yellow fever.

Getting to Abiriba

Abiriba is accessible by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near Abiriba

The nearest airport to Abiriba is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu, which is about 145 km away.

Bus Stations Near Abiriba

There are several bus stations in Umuahia, which is about 20 km away from Abiriba.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars from the nearest town, Umuahia, to get to Abiriba.

How to Get Around in Abiriba

Visitors can move around Abiriba by taxi or hire a private vehicle for the duration of their stay.
