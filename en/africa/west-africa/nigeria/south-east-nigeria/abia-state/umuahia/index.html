Introduction to Umuahia

Welcome to Umuahia, a city in Abia State, South East Nigeria. Nestled amidst rolling hills and lush greenery, Umuahia is known for its rich cultural heritage, impressive landmarks, and beautiful scenery. The city is home to a vibrant population and offers a unique blend of modernity and tradition. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or cuisine, Umuahia has something for everyone. Let’s dive into this comprehensive tour guide to explore the best of Umuahia.

List of Places to Visit in Umuahia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National War Museum: This museum features artifacts, weapons, and tanks used during the Nigerian Civil War. It provides a vivid understanding of the war’s impact on the country and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • The Old Residency: Built-in the early 1900s, this beautiful building served as the administration headquarters of the Eastern Region during the colonial era. Today, it houses a museum that showcases the region’s colonial history, and the beautiful surrounding gardens make for a picturesque visit.
  • Books and More Library: Considered the oldest library in Umuahia, this library boasts an impressive collection of books on Nigerian history, government, and politics. The library also serves as a cultural and educational hub for the community.

Natural Attractions of Umuahia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arochukwu Long Juju Slave Route: The Arochukwu Long Juju Slave Route is a historic trail that connects the Arochukwu traditional kingdom to the Atlantic coast. The trail is steeped in history, as it was the route taken by slaves to the coast during the transatlantic slave trade.
  • Azumini Blue River: Azumini Blue River is a beautiful natural body of water with clear, blue water. Visitors can enjoy swimming, canoeing, and fishing while surrounded by the lush greenery of the surrounding landscape.
  • Ngwa Hills: If you’re looking for a hiking or mountaineering adventure, Ngwa Hills is a must-visit destination. These towering hills provide a challenging ascent, but the stunning panoramic views make it worth the effort.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umuahia

  • Eastern Obolo Hiking and Biking Trail: This trail is ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts. It starts from the Eastern Obolo local government area in Akwa Ibom State and passes through scenic landscapes with breathtaking views of the Niger Delta countryside.
  • Katawa Hills to Ohuru Waterfall: This trail takes you through the magnificent Katawa Hills, passing through beautiful forests, and ends with a stunning view of the Ohuru Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Umuahia: Activities and Tours

  • City Tour: A city tour is an excellent way to get acquainted with Umuahia’s unique culture, heritage, and landmarks. You can take guided tours to the city’s museums, markets, and other local attractions.
  • Umuahia Food Tour: If you’re a foodie at heart, you must go on the Umuahia Food Tour. This tour takes you through the city’s streets and markets, introducing you to a diverse range of Nigerian delicacies.
  • Umuahia Craft Market: A tour of the Umuahia Craft Market is a must for anyone interested in indigenous handcrafts. Local artisans create beautiful hand-woven baskets, pottery, and sculptures.

Food and Drink Scene of Umuahia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Okpa: Okpa is a popular snack made from grated seeds of the okra plant, mixed with spices and steamed in banana leaves. It’s a great option for vegans and vegetarians.
  • Abacha and Ugba: A delicious combination of palm fruit, ugba (oil bean), vegetables, and dried fish.
  • Ofe Nsala: A rich and peppery soup made from catfish or chicken and spice.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: Palm wine is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of various species of palm trees. It’s a favorite local drink and is best enjoyed fresh.
  • Zobo: Zobo is a refreshing drink made from boiling dried Rosella calyces till they turn red, and then mixing with ginger and pineapple.

Where to Stay in Umuahia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

  • The Golden Guinea Breweries & Hotel, Umuahia: This luxury hotel offers world-class facilities, including a swimming pool and a restaurant. It’s one of the best hotels in the city and is perfect for those seeking comfort and style.
  • Hotel Domino International: Hotel Domino International is a three-star hotel located in a serene environment with spacious, well-furnished rooms and a pool.

Hostels

  • Let’s Go Backpackers Hostel: A cozy hostel situated in a serene and peaceful part of the city, the Let’s Go Hostel offers affordable shared dorms and private rooms.
  • Emerald Hostel: This hostel offers affordable shared rooms as well as private en-suite rooms. It’s an excellent option for budget-conscious travelers who still want a comfortable and clean place to stay.

What to Buy in Umuahia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts from the Umuahia Craft Market: The Umuahia Craft Market is a treasure trove of handcrafted goods, including handwoven baskets, pottery, and sculptures. These items make for excellent souvenirs and gifts.
  • Local Fabrics: Nigeria is known for its colorful and vibrant fabrics, and Umuahia is no exception. Visitors can bring home beautiful pieces of fabric to create stunning fashion pieces or preserve as keepsakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Umuahia

Umuahia is a melting pot of Western culture and traditional Igbo culture. The Igbo people of Umuahia strongly hold on to their cultural values, which includes food, dress, and language. Visitors should respect and appreciate the cultural diversity of Umuahia and interact with locals politely.

Best Time to Visit Umuahia

The best time to visit Umuahia is between November and February when the weather is cooler and less humid. These months are also good for outdoor activities like hiking and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umuahia

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when in public places.
  • Be open to trying local foods.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Disrespect traditional rulers or religious leaders.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Umuahia

Umuahia is a relatively safe city, though visitors should still exercise caution and be vigilant about their surroundings. Be wary of pickpockets in crowded areas, particularly near markets, and avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Umuahia

Visitors should take precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases when in Umuahia. Be sure to get vaccinated as recommended by a doctor and carry adequate medical supplies.

Getting to Umuahia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Umuahia

The nearest airport to Umuahia is the Sam Mbakwe International Airport in Owerri. Visitors can also fly into the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu, which is about three hours’ drive from Umuahia.

Bus Stations Near Umuahia

Several bus stations serve Umuahia, including the Umuahia Motor Park, where visitors can catch buses to various destinations in Nigeria.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private taxis or rent private cars to get to and around Umuahia.

How to Get Around in Umuahia

Visitors can get around Umuahia by taking taxis or hiring private cabs. Buses and tricycles are also available for those traveling on a budget.

Where to Go Next from Umuahia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Owerri: The capital city of Imo State is known for its hospitality, cultural festivals, and hot springs.
  • Enugu: This bustling city is the capital of Enugu State and is famous for the enchanting Ngwo Pine Forest and the natural rolling hills.
  • Abia State: Visitors can explore other parts of Abia state, such as Aba and Ohafia, both known for their unique cultural heritage.

Conclusion

We hope you have enjoyed this comprehensive tour guide to Umuahia. From historic landmarks to natural attractions, delicious cuisine to cultural immersion, Umuahia has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the hidden gems of this beautiful city.
