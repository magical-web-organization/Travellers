Introduction to Aba

Located in Abia State, in South East Nigeria, Aba is a city known for its thriving commerce and industry. With a population of over one million people, Aba is one of the largest cities in Nigeria.

Aside from its economic significance, Aba is also rich in culture and history. Its museums and historical sites give visitors a glimpse into the city’s past, while its natural attractions allow for outdoor adventures and scenic views. The food and drink scene in Aba offers a unique taste of Nigerian cuisine, and the city’s hotels, hostels, and guesthouses cater to different budgets and travel styles.

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a returning tourist, this comprehensive tour guide to Aba will provide valuable insights and recommendations on how to make the most out of your stay in this Nigerian city.

List of Places to Visit in Aba

When visiting Aba, there’s a plethora of things to see and do. Here’s a list of some of the most popular places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Colonial History: This museum was established to preserve the colonial history of Nigeria. Visitors can see various artifacts, documents, and photographs that document the country’s colonial past.

  • Aba Women’s Riot Monument: The monument commemorates the role of Aba women in the Aba Women’s War of 1929. The war marked a significant moment in Nigeria’s struggle for independence and women’s rights.

  • Ogbor Hill Slave Trade Market: The market was a major slave trade center during the transatlantic slave trade era. Visitors can see the remnants of the market and learn about the history of slavery in the region.

Natural Attractions of Aba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Azumini Blue River: The river is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and other water sports. Visitors can take a picnic and enjoy the scenic views while relaxing by the river.

  • Ngwa Hills: The hills are great for hiking, biking, and sightseeing. Visitors can see the lush vegetation and wildlife, and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Ohanze Isiahia: A natural rock formation that resembles a human face. Visitors can take pictures, go rock-climbing and explore the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aba

Aba is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are many great routes to choose from, including:

  • Umungasi/Umuokirika Hills Hiking Trail: The trail is an excellent spot for nature lovers. The hike is challenging but rewarding as hikers are treated to panoramic views of Aba.

  • Azumini Blue River Bicycle Trail: The trail is perfect for bikers of all skill levels. The route covers a distance of 10km and takes visitors through scenic views of the Blue River and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Aba: Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Aba, the options are almost endless. Here are a few popular activities and tours to consider:

  • Aba City Tour: This tour takes visitors to explore the city and its top attractions, including the National Museum of Colonial History, Aba Women’s Riot Monument, and Ogbor Hill Slave Trade Market.

  • Ngwa Cultural Festival: The festival takes place annually in December and is a celebration of the Ngwa culture. Visitors can witness traditional dances, music, and other cultural festivities.

Food and Drink Scene of Aba

Aba has a vibrant food and drink scene with a plethora of options for all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Abacha: A dish made with cassava, vegetables, and fish or meat.

  • Jollof Rice: A popular West African dish made with rice, tomato sauce, and other seasonings.

  • Ofe Akwu: A soup made with palm kernel nuts, vegetables, and fish or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: Made from the sap of palm trees, palm wine is a popular Nigerian drink.

  • Kunu: A millet-based drink that is sweetened with sugar or honey.

  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from dried hibiscus leaves.

What to Buy in Aba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aba is a great place to buy souvenirs and local products. Here are some items to consider:

  • Traditional Fabrics: Aba is known for its high-quality fabrics made with local cotton. Customers can have the fabrics custom-made into different designs to suit their taste.

  • Local Crafts: Specially crafted items such as wooden masks, woven baskets, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Aba

Aba is a place rich in tradition and culture. It is essential to respect the customs and traditions of the people. Visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering people’s homes.

Best Time to Visit Aba

Aba can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season (November to February). During this time, the weather is mild, and there is little to no rain. It is also an excellent time to attend festivals and cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aba

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while in Aba:

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the people.

  • Dress modestly.

  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection.

  • Make negative comments about religion or politics.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aba

Aba is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid becoming victims of crimes such as pickpocketing and petty theft.

Health in Aba

Visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid contracting ailments, including drinking purified water, avoiding street food, and getting vaccinated for preventable diseases.
