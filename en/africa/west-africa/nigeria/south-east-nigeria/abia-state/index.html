Introduction to Abia State

Located in the South East region of Nigeria, Abia State offers a unique blend of culture and nature. This beautiful state is known for its rich history, vibrant cultural attractions, and scenic outdoor destinations. The state was created in 1991 and is home to over 2 million people. The capital city of Abia State is Umuahia, while the commercial hub of the state is Aba.

List of Places to Visit in Abia State

Abia State has numerous natural and cultural attractions that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the top places to visit in Abia State:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National War Museum, Umuahia: This museum holds collections of weapons, vehicles and other materials used during the Nigerian Civil War.
  2. Arochukwu Long Juju Slave Route: This is a historical monument that dates back to slave trade era. It is believed that the place harbored slaves taken to the coastal regions and beyond by European slave traders.
  3. Ohafia War Dance: This dance is a cultural and traditional display of warriors from Ohafia. It is a spectacular show of strength, power and bravery of the Ohafia people.
  4. Asa Women’s War Museum: This museum commemorates a 1929 women’s riot against the British in colonial times.

Natural Attractions of Abia State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Azumini Blue River: The river is known for its pristine blue water making it a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.
  2. Ugwu-ukwu Caves and Waterfalls: This beautiful natural park features a series of waterfalls, caves, and crystal clear streams.
  3. Apumiri Ubium Cave System: This unique cave system is a natural masterpiece that contains a series of tunnels and passages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abia State

  1. Ogbunike Cave Trail: This trail is located around Ogbunike cave and offers a beautiful natural walk through jungle vegetation with beautiful vantage views along the way.
  2. Umukalu Hills/Arondizuogu: Umukalu Hills is one of the best hiking spots in Abia State with amazing views of the surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Abia State: Activities and Tours

  1. Igbo-Ukwu Museum Tour: This museum features beautiful bronze artwork and other materials that showcase the ancient African civilization of the Igbo tribe.
  2. Visit the Ariaria International Market: This is a popular shopping destination in Aba city that features an impressive variety of affordable local goods.
  3. City Walks and Food Tours: There is no better way to experience the culture and cuisine of Abia State than by taking a walking tour.
  4. Visit the Ngwa Museum: The museum offers an insight into the economic history of the Ngwa people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Abia State

Day 1: Visit the National War Museum and Ngwa Museum, Umuahia

Day 2: Apumiri Ubium Cave System, Arochukwu Long Juju Slave Route

Day 3: City Walk and Food Tour in Aba City

Day 4: Azumini Blue River, Ugwu-ukwu Caves and Waterfalls

Day 5: Ariaria International market, Ohafia War Dance

Day 6: Hiking at Umukalu Hills/Arondizuogu

Day 7: Igbo-Ukwu Museum Tour, Asa Women’s War Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Abia State

Abia State has a rich culinary heritage with delicious and unique dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nkwobi: This is a spicy goat meat dish served with pepper soup.
  2. Abacha and Ugba: This is a delicious Igbo delicacy made from cassava and oil bean seed.
  3. Ukwa: A popular snack made from breadfruit.

Local Beverages

  1. Palm Wine: This is a popular local drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  2. Ngwo Pineapple Drink: A refreshing drink made with locally grown pineapples.

Where to Stay in Abia State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Abia State has several comfortable and affordable accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Bonafide Hotel, Umuahia: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and excellent service.
  2. Sterling Hotel, Aba: A mid-range hotel conveniently located in Aba city.
  3. Excellency Hotel, Umuahia: A budget-friendly hotel with basic amenities and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Abia State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Arochukwu Long Juju Slave Route memorabilia
  2. Handcrafted wooden sculptures and art
  3. Local fabrics and clothing materials

Local Culture and Customs of Abia State

Abia State has a rich cultural heritage with a strong emphasis on family values and social issues. It is not uncommon to see locals engage in traditional customs and practices throughout the state.

Best Time to Visit Abia State

The best time to visit Abia State is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abia State

Do:

  1. Greet locals with a smile and a handshake
  2. Dress modestly in public areas
  3. Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  1. Engage in excessive drinking or smoking in public areas
  2. Visit remote areas without proper guidance and advice

Safety and Health

Safety in Abia State

Abia State is relatively safe for tourists, but it is recommended to exercise caution and follow basic safety guidelines such as avoiding isolated areas, carrying valuable items safely and avoiding late-night outings.

Health in Abia State

Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions such as vaccination against common diseases and carrying medical insurance.

Getting to Abia State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abia State

The nearest airport to Abia State is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu City. From there, visitors can take a taxi to Umuahia or Aba city.

Bus Stations Near Abia State

Visitors can get to Abia State from major Nigerian cities via several bus companies such as God is Good Motors, Chisco Transport, Peace Mass Transit, and Young Shall Grow.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to/from Abia State.

How to Get Around in Abia State

Visitors can easily get around Abia State via taxis, buses, and rented cars.

Where to Go Next from Abia State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Enugu State: This state is known for its lush vegetation, waterfalls, and cultural attractions.
  2. Anambra State: Home to the famous Idemili cultural festival and Ogbunike Cave.

Conclusion

Abia State is a unique destination with an impressive array of natural, cultural, and historical attractions. Visitors can experience the rich culture, cuisine, and hospitality of the Igbo people while enjoying the beautiful outdoor destinations. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Abia State with ease and confidence.
