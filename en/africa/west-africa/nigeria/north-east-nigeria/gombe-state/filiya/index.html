Introduction to Filiya

Nestled in the heart of Gombe State, in northeastern Nigeria, Filiya is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Rich in traditional heritage, the town boasts a plethora of cultural attractions, museums, and historical landmarks that showcase its glorious past. Moreover, its strategic location near the Yankari National Park makes it an excellent base for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

If you’re planning to explore this charming town, read on to discover the top places to visit, best things to do, local culture, and practical tips to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Filiya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Filiya is a treasure trove of historical and cultural landmarks that offer a glimpse into the town’s rich past. Some of the must-see attractions include:

  • Emir’s Palace: Built in the late 19th century, the ornate palace is a fine example of traditional Hausa-Fulani architecture and design. Visitors can explore the sprawling compound, admire the intricate details of the buildings and learn about the Emirate system that governs the town.

  • Filiya Museum: Located inside the Emir’s Palace, the museum showcases a fascinating collection of artefacts and historical exhibits that reflect the town’s cultural and religious heritage. From traditional clothing and jewellery to musical instruments and weapons, the museum offers an immersive experience for history buffs and curious travellers.

  • Old Walled City: Dating back to the 18th century, the old walled city is a UNESCO World Heritage site that boasts stunning architectural features and a vibrant atmosphere. Visitors can stroll the narrow alleys, admire the towering walls and gates, and interact with the friendly locals.

Natural Attractions of Filiya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond the historical and cultural landmarks, Filiya is surrounded by scenic natural beauty that offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and exploration. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Yankari National Park: Located about 80km from Filiya, the Yankari National Park is a wildlife reserve that offers a spectacular display of flora and fauna. Visitors can embark on a safari tour, hike the trails, swim in the natural springs, and camp in the wild.

  • Kalam Lake: A beautiful lake located a few kilometres from Filiya, Kalam Lake is a perfect place to unwind and relax. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful boat ride, go fishing, or simply soak in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Tsalle Hills: Situated on the outskirts of Filiya, Tsalle Hills is a natural wonder that offers a challenging hike and panoramic views of the town and its surroundings. The hills are also steeped in myth and legend, making them a fascinating place to explore from a cultural perspective.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Filiya

For adventure seekers and outdoor enthusiasts, Filiya offers a plethora of hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of experience. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Emir’s Palace Trail: A short and easy hike that takes visitors around the palace compound and offers stunning views of the town and the hills.

  • Tsalle Hills Trail: A moderate to challenging hike that takes visitors to the top of the Tsalle Hills and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surroundings. Be prepared for steep climbs and uneven terrain.

  • Yankari Trail: A multi-day trek that takes visitors deep into the Yankari National Park and offers a chance to spot rare wildlife, swim in natural springs, and camp in the wild. Guided tours are available for safety and convenience.

Best Things to Do in Filiya: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours available in Filiya that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Take a Cultural Tour: Explore the historical and cultural landmarks of Filiya with a knowledgeable guide who can offer insights into the town’s heritage and traditions.

  • Go on a Wildlife Safari: Embark on an exciting safari tour in the Yankari National Park and observe a variety of wildlife, including elephants, baboons, antelopes, and more.

  • Visit a Local Market: Immerse yourself in the vibrant atmosphere of a local market, where you can shop for souvenirs, handicrafts, and traditional clothing.

  • Try Local Delicacies: Sample the delicious local cuisine of Filiya, which includes specialties such as tuwo, miyan kuka, and kuli-kuli.

Week-Long Itinerary for Filiya

If you’re planning to spend a week in Filiya, here’s a sample itinerary that covers all the must-see sights and hidden gems:

Day 1: Explore the Emir’s Palace and museum Day 2-3: Go on a wildlife safari in the Yankari National Park Day 4: Take a cultural tour of the old walled city Day 5: Hike the Tsalle Hills and enjoy the panoramic views Day 6: Relax by Kalam Lake and soak in the natural beauty Day 7: Visit a local market and shop for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Filiya

No trip to Filiya is complete without sampling the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuwo: A staple food in the northern part of Nigeria, tuwo is a thick porridge made from maize, millet, or rice. It is often served with spicy stews or soups.

  • Miyan Kuka: A delicious soup made from baobab leaves, meat, and spices. It is a popular dish during Ramadan and other festive occasions.

  • Kuli-Kuli: A savoury snack made from groundnuts and spices. It is often served as a side dish or appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: A refreshing and nutritious drink made from hibiscus flowers, sugar, and ginger. It is a popular alternative to carbonated drinks.

  • Ginger Beer: A spicy and fizzy drink made from fresh ginger, sugar, and lime. It is a perfect thirst quencher on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Filiya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Filiya offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Emir’s Guest House: Located inside the Emir’s Palace, the guest house offers comfortable and affordable rooms that are steeped in history and tradition.

  • Yankari Safari Lodge: Situated in the heart of the Yankari National Park, the lodge offers luxury tents and cottages with stunning views of the wild.

  • Kalam Lake Resort: A tranquil retreat located by the scenic Kalam Lake, the resort offers comfortable rooms and cottages with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Filiya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Filiya, here are some of the best options:

  • Handicrafts and Artifacts: From traditional pottery and baskets to colourful textiles and jewellery, Filiya offers an array of handicrafts and artifacts that showcase the town’s cultural heritage.

  • Spices and Condiments: Stock up on aromatic spices, such as turmeric, cinnamon, and cloves, which are essential ingredients in the local cuisine. You can also find homemade jams and chutneys that make excellent gifts.

  • Kuli-Kuli and Suya: These savoury snacks are a popular delicacy in northern Nigeria and make excellent gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Filiya

Filiya’s culture is rooted in the Islamic faith and traditional Hausa-Fulani customs. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting religious sites and mosques. It is also customary to greet locals with a handshake and exchange pleasantries.

Best Time to Visit Filiya

The best time to visit Filiya is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this time, the weather is pleasant and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The rainy season, which lasts from May to October, can make the roads muddy and difficult to navigate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Filiya

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Learn some basic Hausa phrases to interact with the locals
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Book guided tours for safety and convenience

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Eat or drink in public during Ramadan

Safety and Health

Safety in Filiya

Filiya is a relatively safe town, but visitors are advised to take precautions and avoid walking alone at night. It is also recommended to carry a copy of your passport and other important documents with you at all times.

Health in Filiya

Filiya is in a malaria-prone region, so visitors are advised to take anti-malaria medication and use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites. It is also recommended to avoid tap water and drink only bottled or boiled water to prevent water-borne diseases.

Getting to Filiya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Filiya

The nearest airport to Filiya is Gombe Airport, which is about 70km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Filiya

Filiya is well connected by road to other cities in Nigeria, and there are several bus stations in the town that offer regular services. Some of the popular operators include ABC Transport and Peace Mass Transit.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or motorcycle to travel to Filiya from nearby cities, such as Bauchi or Jos. However, it is recommended to hire a local driver who is familiar with the terrain and can navigate the roads safely.

How to Get Around in Filiya

Filiya is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and motorcycle taxis are also available for short distances or when travelling to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Filiya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some more time to spare, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Bauchi: A vibrant city located about 100km from Filiya, Bauchi offers a variety of cultural attractions, such as the Yankari Game Reserve and the Wikki Warm Springs.

  • Jos: Known as the “City of the Rocks,” Jos is a bustling town that boasts stunning natural beauty, such as the Jos Plateau and the Shere Hills.

Conclusion

Filiya is a charming town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see in this hidden gem of Nigeria. Follow our travel guide to make the most of your trip and immerse yourself in the rich culture and natural beauty of Filiya.
