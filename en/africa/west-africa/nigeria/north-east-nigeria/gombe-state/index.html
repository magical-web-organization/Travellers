Introduction to Gombe State

Gombe State is a state located in the North Eastern part of Nigeria. It was created in 1996 from part of Bauchi State. Gombe State is bounded to the north by Bauchi and Yobe States, to the east by Adamawa State, to the south by Taraba State, and to the west by Kaduna State. Gombe State covers a land area of about 20,265 square kilometers and has a population of over 3 million people. The state is home to various ethnic groups and the majority of the people are farmers.

List of Places to Visit in Gombe State

Gombe State has a lot of tourist attractions that are worth visiting. Some of the top places to visit in Gombe State are:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gombe Emir’s Palace: The Gombe Emir’s Palace is a historic palace located in the heart of Gombe city. It is an excellent place to learn about the traditional rulership system in Gombe State.

  • Bauchi Slave Route: The Bauchi Slave Route is a historic route used during the transatlantic slave trade. A visit to this place will give you an insight into the history of the slave trade in Nigeria.

  • Tula Ruins and Museum: The Tula Ruins and Museum is an ancient city that was destroyed in the 19th century. The ruins are an important part of Nigeria’s history, and a visit to this place will give you an idea of what ancient Nigeria looked like.

Natural Attractions of Gombe State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gombe National Park: Gombe National Park is a popular park located in the northeastern part of Nigeria. It is known for its chimpanzees, hiking trails, and scenic views.

  • Kalambo Waterfalls: The Kalambo Waterfalls is a spectacular attraction located near the Gombe-Bauchi border. It is one of the highest waterfalls in Nigeria and a great place for picnics and outdoor activities.

  • Gombe River: The Gombe River is one of the most important rivers in Nigeria. It is an excellent place for fishing and boat rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gombe State

Gombe State has several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure lovers. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Gombe State include:

  • Tula to Bojude: This is an easy hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful landscape of Tula.

  • Gombe National Park Trails: Gombe National Park offers several hiking trails that take you through the jungle to see chimpanzees and other wildlife.

  • Gidan Sule to Chinade: This is an adventurous and challenging mountain biking trail that takes you through the hills of Gidan Sule and Chinade.

Best Things to Do in Gombe State: Activities and Tours

Gombe State offers several activities and tours that will make your visit memorable. Some of the best things to do in Gombe State include:

  • Chimpanzee Tracking: Gombe National Park is known for its chimpanzees, and a visit to the park will give you the opportunity to track and observe these amazing animals.

  • Fishing: The Gombe River is a popular fishing spot, and you can catch various species of fish such as tilapia and catfish.

  • Horse Riding: Horse riding is a popular activity in Gombe State, and you can enjoy horse rides through the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Gombe State

Gombe State has a rich culinary culture that is worth exploring. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Gombe State include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moi Moi: Moi Moi is a popular bean cake that is eaten with rice or bread.

  • Fura da Nono: Fura da Nono is a delicious meal made from millet and cow milk.

  • Dambun Shinkafa: Dambun Shinkafa is a traditional rice dish that is usually served with chicken or beef.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: Zobo is a popular drink made from dried hibiscus leaves.

  • Kunu: Kunu is a traditional drink made from millet or sorghum.

Where to Stay in Gombe State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gombe State has several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to different budgets. Some of the top places to stay in Gombe State include:

  • Gombe Guest Inn: Gombe Guest Inn is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Gombe city.

  • Tahir Guest Palace: Tahir Guest Palace is a luxury hotel that offers excellent services and amenities.

  • Nuru Guest House: Nuru Guest House is a mid-range guesthouse that is perfect for business travelers and tourists.

What to Buy in Gombe State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gombe State has several local products that are worth buying as souvenirs. Some of the top products to buy in Gombe State include:

  • Traditional Clothing: Gombe State is famous for its colorful and unique traditional clothing.

  • Mats and Baskets: Mats and baskets are popular crafts in Gombe State, and they make great souvenirs.

  • Local Spices: Gombe State is famous for its spices such as Suya spice and Kilishi spice.

Local Culture and Customs of Gombe State

Gombe State is home to various ethnic groups, and each group has its unique culture and customs. The Hausa-Fulani are the dominant ethnic group in Gombe State, and they are known for their hospitality and warm welcome.

Best Time to Visit Gombe State

The best time to visit Gombe State is during the dry season, which is from November to April. The weather is warm and dry, and outdoor activities such as hiking and fishing are more enjoyable during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gombe State

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious places.

  • Learn a few basic Hausa phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.

  • Eat in public during the month of Ramadan.

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gombe State

Gombe State is generally safe for tourists, but it is important to exercise caution and avoid traveling alone at night. It is also advisable to avoid large crowds and protests.

Health in Gombe State

It is important to take necessary health precautions when visiting Gombe State. Ensure you are vaccinated against diseases such as yellow fever and typhoid fever. It is also advisable to carry a mosquito net as malaria is prevalent in the area.

Getting to Gombe State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gombe State is accessible by road and air.

Airports Near Gombe State

Gombe State is served by the Gombe Lawanti International Airport, which is situated in the city of Gombe.

Bus Stations Near Gombe State

Gombe State is connected to major cities in Nigeria by road, and there are several bus stations that offer transportation services to and from the state.

Other Options

There are also car rental services available in Gombe State.

How to Get Around in Gombe State

Gombe State has a good road network, and it is easy to get around using taxis, buses, or rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Gombe State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Gombe State include:

  • Yankari National Park: Yankari National Park is located in Bauchi State and is famous for its wildlife and hot springs.

Conclusion

Gombe State has a lot to offer tourists who are looking for an authentic Nigerian experience. With its rich cultural history, natural attractions, delicious food, and friendly people, Gombe State is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
