Introduction to Adamawa State

Located in the North East region of Nigeria, Adamawa State is a stunning destination that boasts a wealth of cultural and natural attractions. Known for its rugged terrain and diverse ethnic population, the state is home to over 80 different ethnic groups and has a rich cultural heritage. From the iconic Mambilla Plateau to the popular Gashaka-Gumti National Park, Adamawa State has plenty to offer visitors looking for a truly unique travel experience.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tangale Palace Museum

Located in Gembu, Tangale Palace Museum is a must-visit attraction in Adamawa State. It showcases the rich history and culture of the Tangale people, one of the state’s indigenous ethnic groups. The museum houses artifacts, traditional wears, and other cultural items that give visitors a glimpse into the Tangale way of life.

Sukur Cultural Landscape

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Nigeria, Sukur Cultural Landscape is a unique and remarkable attraction in Adamawa State. It is home to the Sukur people, an ethnic group known for their complex terraced farming system and impressive architecture. The site includes a royal palace, traditional housing, and other cultural landmarks.

Ribadu Cantonment

Ribadu Cantonment is a historic landmark in Adamawa State. It was established in 1963 as a military base and played a significant role in Nigeria’s political history. Today, it has been transformed into a museum that showcases Nigeria’s military history, with a display of antiques, photographs, and weapons.

Natural Attractions of Adamawa State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gashaka-Gumti National Park

Gashaka-Gumti National Park is a natural wonder that covers over 6,000 square kilometers of land. It is the largest national park in Nigeria and is home to an abundance of wildlife, including elephants, chimpanzees, baboons, and antelopes. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, and game drives in the park.

Mambilla Plateau

Located in Sardauna LGA, Mambilla Plateau is one of the most scenic destinations in Adamawa State. It boasts of breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is home to several tourist attractions, including the ChappalWaddi Mountain, Nigeria’s highest peak.

Korup National Park

Korup National Park is another must-visit attraction in Adamawa State, located just across the border in Cameroon. It is a vast expanse of forest with an incredible array of flora and fauna, including primates, crocodiles, and butterflies. The park offers several hiking trails and campsites for visitors.

Best Things to Do in Adamawa State: Activities and Tours

Sukur Cultural Landscape Tour

Take a tour of Sukur Cultural Landscape to see the impressive architecture and terraced farming system of the Sukur people.

Game Drive in Gashaka-Gumti National Park

Go on a game drive to see the diverse wildlife that calls the park home.

Mountain Climbing Expedition

Conquer Nigeria’s highest peak, the ChappalWaddi Mountain, for an unforgettable experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Adamawa State

Day 1 - Arrive in Yola, the state capital, and explore the city’s markets.

Day 2 - Visit Tangale Palace Museum in Gembu and explore the town.

Day 3 - Take a tour of Sukur Cultural Landscape and learn about the unique terraced farming system.

Day 4 - Hike the ChappalWaddi Mountain and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Day 5 - Visit Ribadu Cantonment Museum and learn about Nigeria’s military history.

Day 6 - Explore Mambilla Plateau and its various attractions.

Day 7 - Visit Lamurde Hot Springs and Mayo-Belwa Waterfalls for a relaxing day out.

Local Culture and Customs of Adamawa State

Adamawa is a culturally and ethnically diverse state. Visitors are advised to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as modest dressing and removing of footwear before entering some cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Adamawa State

The best time to visit Adamawa State is from November to April, during the dry season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Adamawa State

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Buy local crafts and products to support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Wander off alone in remote areas or at night.
  • Cause damage to local attractions or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Adamawa State

Visitors should take precautions when traveling in Adamawa State, especially in remote areas. It is essential to stay informed about the current security situation and to avoid political or social gatherings.

Health in Adamawa State

Visitors should ensure that they have all relevant vaccinations before traveling to Adamawa State. It is essential to take precautions against malaria by using mosquito repellent and sleeping under a mosquito net.

The best way to get around Adamawa State is by private car or taxi. Motorcycle taxis (okadas) are also widely available but may not be the safest or most comfortable mode of transportation.
