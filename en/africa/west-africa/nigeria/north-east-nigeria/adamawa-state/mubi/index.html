Travel Guide to Mubi, Nigeria

Surrounded by lush palm trees and scenic mountains, Mubi is a serene Nigerian town that boasts a long history, rich culture and incredible hospitality. Located in the northeastern state of Adamawa, Mubi is a town which few tourists know, but those who do visit, are impressed by its beauty and charm.

This comprehensive guide will provide an overview of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and how to get there, making your trip to Mubi unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Mubi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mubi Residency Museum - This history museum showcases the colonial history of Mubi through artifacts and exhibits.
  2. Madagali Ruins - The Madagali ruins are ruins of an entire town, with buildings and structures dating back to pre-colonial times.
  3. Mubi Emirates Palace - Originally built in the early 1900s as the residence of the Mubi Emir, the palace is a reflection of the architectural style of the era.

Natural Attractions of Mubi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Fahai Hills - Fahai Hills offer breathtaking views of the town and surrounding landscape.
  2. Tombu Waterfalls - Tombu Waterfalls is a natural wonder, surrounded by lush vegetation, and it provides the perfect spot for cooling off on a hot day.
  3. Mandara Mountains - Mandara Mountains’ rugged terrain and hiking trails offer excellent opportunities for nature photography and adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mubi

Mubi’s rich natural environment offers some excellent hiking and biking trails for adventure enthusiasts.

  1. Fahai Hills Trail - This easy-going trail rewards hikers and bikers with gorgeous views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  2. Kwatara Circuit Trail - This moderate trail offers hikers and bikers the chance to explore the diverse landscapes, including forests, waterfalls, and rugged terrains.

Best Things to Do in Mubi: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit a local market - Mubi is home to some of the largest and most vibrant markets in Nigeria where you can buy locally-made souvenirs or sample authentic Nigerian cuisine.
  2. Go on a city tour - Mubi city tour allows you to see the town’s historical sites and learn about its culture and architecture.
  3. Take a cultural dance lesson - Mubi culture is rich in traditional dance, and you can sign up for lessons to try the traditional dance or listen to the music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mubi

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel.

Day 2: Explore Mubi city, and visit the Mubi Residency Museum, Madagali Ruins, the Mubi Emirates Palace and Kwatara Circuit Trail.

Day 3: Hike/bike Mubi’s trails while enjoying the scenery of Fahai Hills and explore the Tombu waterfalls.

Day 4: Cultural dance lesson.

Day 5: City tour of surroundings area of Mubi.

Day 6: Relaxing day at a nearby resort or go fishing at Bama River.

Day 7: Visit the Mandara Mountains and experience the local culture in one of the nearby towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Mubi

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Masa - Masa is a popular street food made of rice flour and often enjoyed with spicy dipping sauce.
  2. Suya - Suya is a spicy kebab made of beef or chicken, marinated in a mixture of spices and roasted over an open flame.
  3. Tuwon Dawa and Miyan Kuka - This traditional dish consists of thick peanut soup served with a side of mashed cassava.

Local Beverages

  1. Zobo - A sweet and tangy drink made from hibiscus flowers.
  2. Ginger Beer - This refreshing drink is seasoned with ginger and is an ideal thirst-quencher in the hot Mubi climate.

Getting to Mubi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mubi

The closest airport to Mubi is the Yola International Airport, approximately 200km away.

Bus Stations Near Mubi

There are numerous bus stations in Mubi that offer connections to other parts of the country.

Other Options

The town is accessible by road and is connected to significant towns in the area through a network of highways.

Where to Go Next from Mubi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Gashaka Gumti National Park - This is an ecological wonder home to wildlife and plants unique to Nigeria and West Africa.
  2. Waza National Park - This park is known for its lion and elephant sightings and is perfect for safari lovers.
  3. Maiha Lake Resort - This resort provides an idyllic ambiance and impressive views of the Maiha Lake.

Conclusion

Mubi is a destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the unique culture and picturesque landscapes of Nigeria. From breathtaking mountain ranges to historical sites, the town has plenty to offer to visitors looking for a remarkable experience. With this guide, you have all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Mubi.
