Introduction to Gashaka Gumti National Park

Gashaka Gumti National Park, situated in North East Nigeria, is the largest in the country and one of the largest protected areas in West Africa. The park is renowned for its diverse vegetation, wildlife, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and adventurers.

Spread over 6,731 square kilometers, the park encompasses the rugged terrain of the Mambilla Plateau and the rugged hills and mountains of the Gashaka region. The park is home to over 100 species of mammals, 500 species of birds, and various primates, including the chimpanzee and Nigeria-Cameroon chimpanzee, which are both endangered.

List of Places to Visit in Gashaka Gumti National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The park boasts of rich cultural heritage and historical monuments that tell the stories of the people who inhabit the area. Some of the cultural attractions that should be on your bucket list include:

  • Mambilla Village: A visit to Mambilla villages, located on the Mambilla Plateau, gives visitors a glimpse of the lifestyle of the local people. The plateau is the highest in Nigeria, and the scenic views of the plateau will take your breath away.

Natural Attractions of Gashaka Gumti National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gashaka Gumti National Park has stunning natural attractions that will awe-inspire the visitors. From hiking and biking trails to scenic waterfalls, the park has something for everyone. Some of the popular natural attractions include:

  • Chappal Waddi: The summit of Chappal Waddi is the highest point in Nigeria and one of the main attractions of the park. Trekking Chappal Waddi is a favorite activity for many visitors who come to the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gashaka Gumti National Park

The park has several hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the opportunity to experience the park’s stunning scenery and natural wonders. Some of the popular hiking/biking trails include:

  • Hiking trail to Chappal Waddi: The trek to Chappal Waddi is not for the faint-hearted, as it takes about six hours to reach the summit. The trail is challenging but rewarding, and hikers can enjoy the scenic views of the park.

Best Things to Do in Gashaka Gumti National Park: Activities and Tours

The park has a range of activities that visitors can engage in, from adventurous outdoor activities to cultural experiences. Some of the best things to do in the park include:

  • Game drives: Visitors can enjoy game drives to see some of the park’s wildlife, including elephants, lions, and buffalos.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gashaka Gumti National Park

A seven-day itinerary in the park can give visitors a wide range of experiences, from cultural tours to hiking and biking adventures. Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in the park:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the park’s accommodation.
  • Day 2: Explore cultural attractions, including Mambilla Village.
  • Day 3 – 4: Trekking and hiking to Chappal Waddi.
  • Day 5: Game drive.
  • Day 6: Relax and take in the park’s natural beauty.
  • Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Gashaka Gumti National Park

The park has a range of local dishes and beverages that visitors can sample. Below are some of the must-try dishes and beverages of the park.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuwon Shinkafa: A popular dish made from locally grown rice, topped with different garnishes like vegetables, peppers, and meats.
  • Kilishi: Spiced and dried beef or chicken, popular as a snack across Nigeria.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A popular alcoholic drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from the hibiscus plant.

Where to Stay in Gashaka Gumti National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The park has various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Below are some of the popular accommodation options in the park.

  • Gashaka Gumti National Park Headquarters: The park has basic guesthouses that are ideal for budget travelers.
  • Serti Hotel: A popular hotel that caters to a more upscale market.

What to Buy in Gashaka Gumti National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The park has a range of locally made products that visitors can buy as souvenirs. Below are some of the top things to buy in the park.

  • Hand-woven baskets: The park has a range of hand-woven baskets made by locals that visitors can buy.
  • Local spices and seasonings: The park has a range of local spices and seasonings that are unique to the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Gashaka Gumti National Park

The park is home to various ethnic groups, each with their unique culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the locals’ customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Gashaka Gumti National Park

The best time to visit the park is during the dry season from November to April when the park’s weather is mild, and outdoor activities are accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gashaka Gumti National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s cultural heritage and customs of local people.
  • Carry appropriate gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter indiscriminately.
  • Disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gashaka Gumti National Park

The park is generally safe, but visitors are encouraged to be vigilant and cautious, particularly when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Gashaka Gumti National Park

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated for yellow fever and typhoid before traveling to the park.

Getting to Gashaka Gumti National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access the park through various transportation options. Below are some of the transport options available.

Airports Near Gashaka Gumti National Park

  • Yola International Airport: The nearest airport to the park, about 245 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Gashaka Gumti National Park

  • Mararaba Mubi Motor Park: Buses from here travel to Gashaka town, which is close to the park.

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire a private car from Yola or Abuja to access the park.

How to Get Around in Gashaka Gumti National Park

Visitors can hire a tour guide from the park’s headquarters or hire a car to explore the park.

Where to Go Next from Gashaka Gumti National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations, including the Mambilla Plateau, the Sukur Cultural Landscape, and the Gashaka Game Reserve.

Conclusion

A visit to Gashaka Gumti National Park is an excellent opportunity to experience the best of West Africa’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. From hiking and biking to game drives and cultural tours, the park has something for everyone.
