Introduction to Kashimbila Game Reserve

Kashimbila Game Reserve is a hidden gem located in the heart of Taraba State, North East Nigeria. The reserve is a great destination for those looking for an extraordinary experience in the natural beauty of Northern Nigeria. The game reserve is home to a wide range of wildlife and ecological habitats, making it a perfect location for a wildlife safari. The reserve sits in a stunning natural setting, surrounded by hills and mountains, forming a perfect backdrop.

List of Places to Visit in Kashimbila Game Reserve

Kashimbila Game Reserve is filled with exciting tourist attractions that cater to both local and foreign visitors. Here are the top places to visit in Kashimbila Game Reserve.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kumbo Museum: This is a great place to learn about the traditional culture and heritage of the Kumbo people of Taraba State. The museum is located near Kashimbila and is a great attraction to visit if you are interested in history.

Natural Attractions of Kashimbila Game Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kashimbila Waterfall: This is one of the main attractions of the park. The waterfall is incredibly scenic and is surrounded by lush vegetation.

  • Wildlife Safari: One of the main attractions in the reserve is the opportunity to encounter the diverse wildlife found within the game reserve. While on a wildlife safari, you can see various animals such as elephants, hippos, baboons, and many more.

  • Mount Gangirwal: Mount Gangirwal is a beautiful mountain range that runs through the game reserve. It is known for its beautiful landscapes, various hiking trails and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kashimbila Game Reserve

Hiking and biking are popular activities among visitors to Kashimbila Game Reserve. The game reserve boasts of beautiful trails that cater to all levels of hikers and bikers. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Gangirwal Hiking Trail: The Gangirwal trail is a challenging hiking route that takes you up the mountain. Hikers are rewarded with unbeatable views over the game reserve.

  • Kashimbila Falls Hiking Trail: This is an easy to moderate 5km trail that takes you through the lush vegetation of the reserve. The highlight of the trail is the breathtaking waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Kashimbila Game Reserve: Activities and Tours

Kashimbila Game Reserve offers numerous activities and tours to visitors. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Wildlife Safari: Explore the diverse wildlife found within the game reserve. You can opt for guided or self-guided safari tours.

  • Bird Watching: With over 100 recorded bird species, Kashimbila Game Reserve is a bird lover’s paradise.

  • Canoeing/Kayaking: Kashimbila Game Reserve offers the option of canoe and kayak rentals to visitors. The experience is fantastic as it allows you to explore the park from a different perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Kashimbila Game Reserve

Kashimbila Game Reserve offers a unique culinary experience, boasting of delicious traditional Nigerian dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kilishi: Kilishi is a dried spicy meat with a unique taste. It is an excellent snack that is perfect for exploring the park.

  • Suya: Suya is another popular Nigerian snack made of spiced grilled meat, generally beef or chicken, which is usually served with pepper sauce and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: Zobo is a chilled drink made from hibiscus flowers. It is delicious and incredibly refreshing.

  • Kunun Zaki: Kunun Zaki is a traditional Nigerian drink made from millet. It is a refreshing drink that is great for hot weather conditions.

Where to Stay in Kashimbila Game Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kashimbila Game Reserve offers various accommodation options to visitors. Here are some of the best options:

  • Kashimbila Motel: The motel is located within the park and offers a great stay for those wanting to be close to nature.

  • Kashimbila Guesthouse: For budget travelers, the guesthouse offers a more affordable stay.

  • Kashimbila Camping: For those looking for a true camping experience, Kashimbila camping is the perfect option.

What to Buy in Kashimbila Game Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kashimbila Game Reserve boasts of unique crafts and products that make for great souvenirs. Here are some of the best products to look out for:

  • Handmade baskets: The park is home to various tribes, and each tribe is known for its unique styles of basket-making.

  • Traditional fabrics: The park is an excellent place to buy traditional Nigerian cloth, especially those that are handmade by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Kashimbila Game Reserve

Kashimbila Game Reserve is home to the Kumbo tribe, who have their unique culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture and dress in a way that is respectful of the local customs.

Best Time to Visit Kashimbila Game Reserve

The best time to visit Kashimbila Game Reserve is during the dry season, which usually runs from November to March. The weather is generally cooler, and the animals are more visible, making it perfect for a wildlife safari.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kashimbila Game Reserve

When visiting Kashimbila Game Reserve, visitors are encouraged to observe the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Bring a pair of binoculars for bird watching.

Don’t:

  • Litter the park.

  • Leave your accommodation at night without your guide’s knowledge.

  • Feed the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kashimbila Game Reserve

While Kashimbila Game Reserve is generally safe, visitors are encouraged to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone, especially at night.

  • Keep valuables securely locked away.

  • Follow the guide’s instructions when on a wildlife safari.

Health in Kashimbila Game Reserve

Visitors are recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to Nigeria. Malaria is prevalent in the region, so it’s important to take necessary precautions by using mosquito repellant, nets and wearing long sleeves and trousers.

Getting to Kashimbila Game Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kashimbila Game Reserve is accessible by air and by road.

Airports Near Kashimbila Game Reserve

  • Yola International Airport: Yola International is the closest airport to Kashimbila Game Reserve, and it is served by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Kashimbila Game Reserve

  • Jalingo Bus Station: Jalingo Bus Station is the best place to take a bus to Kashimbila Game Reserve.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a private driver, taxi or car rental.

How to Get Around in Kashimbila Game Reserve

Visitors can explore Kashimbila Game Reserve by car, bike or foot. Most of the game reserve’s attractions are accessible by car or bike, while hiking trails can be explored on foot.

Where to Go Next from Kashimbila Game Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After your fantastic stay at Kashimbila Game Reserve, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore:

  • Mambila Plateau: The Mambila Plateau is known for its diverse wildlife and unique culture.

  • Gashaka-Gumti National Park: Gashaka-Gumti is the largest national park in Nigeria and is located in the neighboring state of Adamawa.

Conclusion

Kashimbila Game Reserve is a hidden gem located in the heart of Northern Nigeria. The reserve is a great destination for those looking for an extraordinary experience in the natural beauty of Nigeria. With its diverse wildlife, scenic views, and unique culture, Kashimbila Game Reserve has everything to offer its visitors.
