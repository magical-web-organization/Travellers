Introduction to Ido Ekiti

Ido Ekiti is a serene town located in Ekiti State, South West Nigeria. It is known for its beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and a warm welcome from its hospitable people. If you are planning a trip here, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ido Ekiti

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Olosunta Hills: This historic site is located at the north-western part of Ido Ekiti. It is believed to be a worship site for traditional worshippers and has beautiful natural scenery.
  • Ikogosi Warm Spring Resort: It is a natural wonder consisting of two springs that merge and flow side by side, one with cold water and the other with warm water. This attraction features a museum, swimming pool, and other outdoor activities.
  • Iyin Ekiti Hills: This is a significant historical site in Ido Ekiti, as it was the location where the Ekiti Parapo war was fought. The hill is 2,773 feet above sea level, and hiking to the top gives a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Ido Ekiti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olosunta River Valley: This valley is located at the bottom of the Olosunta Hills and provides a picturesque view for visitors. Activities include picnicking, hiking, and camping.
  • Igbo Efon Waterfalls: The waterfall is located in the nearby town of Igbo Efon and is a great place to visit if you are looking for a day trip from Ido Ekiti. Enjoy the serene view while swimming in the cool water.
  • Arinta Waterfall: Located in Idosi, a neighbouring town in Ido Ekiti, the Arinta waterfall is a beautiful sight to see. The waterfall provides a natural setting for picnicking and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ido Ekiti

  • Olosunta Hills hiking trail: This trail is located at the Olosunta Hills and takes hikers on a path through the lush forest and beautiful natural scenery. It is relatively easy and suitable for all levels of hikers.
  • Ikogosi Warm Spring Resort Trail: This trail begins at the Ikogosi Warm Springs Resort and provides hikers with a view of the beautiful natural scenery in Ekiti State.

Best Things to Do in Ido Ekiti: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Local Markets: Visit the popular Oja Oba Market, where you can purchase fresh fruits, handcrafted items, and local cuisines.
  • Attend Festivals: Participate in the Ido-Ekiti Yam Festival, which takes place in August annually.
  • Visit Museums: Learn about the rich and diverse history of Ido Ekiti at the Ekiti State Cultural Center/Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Ido Ekiti

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pounded Yam and Vegetable Soup: This is a local favourite and is made from yam tubers that have been mashed and served with vegetable soup.
  • Ekiti Special Rice: This dish is made with locally grown rice and is cooked with different spices and vegetables.
  • Amala and Ewedu Soup: This is a delicious dish made with yam flour and a green leafy vegetable.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It is a must-try for those looking for a taste of local culture.
  • Zobo: This is a refreshing drink made from dried hibiscus flowers, ginger, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Ido Ekiti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ikogosi Warm Springs Resort: This resort offers visitors the chance to stay in comfortable rooms and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Davido Guest House: This guest house is located in the heart of Ido Ekiti and offers comfortable accommodation equipped with modern amenities.
  • Olasco Guest House: It is another affordable and well-furnished guest house located in the heart of the town.

What to Buy in Ido Ekiti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Adire: This is the traditional Yoruba tie-and-dye fabric used to produce various clothing items such as dresses, skirts, and tops.
  • Aso Oke: This is the traditional Yoruba ceremonial fabric. It is used in most traditional ceremonies such as weddings and funerals.
  • Beads: These are colourful beads made from glass and are often used to make necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.

Local Culture and Customs of Ido Ekiti

The people of Ido Ekiti are predominantly Yoruba and are known for their hospitality, warmth, and rich culture. Visitors to Ido Ekiti should respect local customs and traditions, dress appropriately, and ask for permission before taking pictures of locals.

Best Time to Visit Ido Ekiti

The best time to visit Ido Ekiti is between December and March, which marks the dry season. The weather during this period is usually mild and perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ido Ekiti

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Learn some basic phrases in the Yoruba language to communicate with locals.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their consent.
  • Engage in any illegal activity that may be harmful to the local community.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ido Ekiti

Ido Ekiti is a relatively safe town, and visitors should not be worried about their safety. However, visitors should be aware of petty theft, especially in crowded areas like markets and bus stations.

Health in Ido Ekiti

Visitors to Ido Ekiti should be cautious of eating street food that may have been prepared in unhygienic environments. It is also important to drink clean and purified water to prevent waterborne diseases.

Getting to Ido Ekiti: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ido Ekiti

The nearest international airport to Ido Ekiti is the Murtala Mohammed International Airport in Lagos. From Lagos, travelers can take a flight to Akure Airport, which is a two-hour drive from Ido Ekiti.

Bus Stations Near Ido Ekiti

Major bus stations in Ido Ekiti include Ido Park and Akure Garage. These bus stations connect visitors to other cities in Nigeria.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or a taxi to get to Ido Ekiti from Lagos or other Nigerian cities.

How to Get Around in Ido Ekiti

Ido Ekiti is a small town, and visitors can easily navigate its streets on foot. However, visitors can also hire a taxi or a motorcycle popularly known as “Okada” to move around.

Where to Go Next from Ido Ekiti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Osun Sacred Grove: Osun Sacred Grove is located in Osogbo, a nearby town in Osun State. It is famous for its rich history and cultural significance.
  • Erin-Ijesha Waterfalls: These are seven cascading waterfalls located in Erin-Ijesha, a neighbouring town in Osun State.
  • Ife Museum: Ife Museum is located in Ife, a neighbouring town in Osun State. It houses a vast collection of Yoruba art and artifacts.

Conclusion

Ido Ekiti is a perfect destination for visitors seeking an authentic Nigerian experience. This comprehensive guide will help visitors plan their trip and explore the best that the town has to offer. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and friendly locals, Ido Ekiti is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
