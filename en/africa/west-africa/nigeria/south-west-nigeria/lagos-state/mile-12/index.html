Mile 12 is a vibrant neighborhood located in the heart of Lagos State, Nigeria. While it may not be as popular as some of the more well-known tourist destinations in Nigeria, Mile 12 offers a blend of adventure, culture, and history that is sure to captivate anyone who visits. In this comprehensive guide, we will introduce you to the top attractions in Mile 12, offer valuable tips on traveling to the area, and give you the inside scoop on the best places to eat, sleep, and explore.

Introduction to Mile 12

Mile 12 is situated in the Kosofe Local Government Area of Lagos State, Nigeria. It is known for its bustling market, which is one of the largest food markets in Nigeria. It is famous for the variety of fruits, vegetables, and seafood available here. Mile 12 is also the hub of transportation for many of the surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Mile 12

When it comes to attractions, there is a lot more to Mile 12 than just the market. Here are some of our top picks for the best places to visit in Mile 12:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum Lagos: This museum is located in Onikan, Lagos, just a short drive from Mile 12. It is home to a vast collection of Nigerian art, artifacts, and historical documents, providing a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Nigeria.

  • Badagry Black Heritage Museum: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of the slave trade in Nigeria. It is located in Badagry, about an hour’s drive from Mile 12, and features exhibits and artifacts related to the slave trade.

Natural Attractions of Mile 12: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ikeja City Mall: For some retail therapy, head over to the Ikeja City Mall, located just a few kilometers from Mile 12. The mall has a variety of stores, including high-end fashion outlets, as well as restaurants and a cinema.

  • Ikeja Golf Club: This scenic golf course is located in Ikeja, close to Mile 12. The course is a favorite among golf enthusiasts, and visitors can enjoy the lush green fairways and stunning views of Lagos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mile 12

Mile 12 is well-known for a variety of outdoor adventures, including hiking and biking routes, which offer a great way to explore the surrounding countryside. Here are some of our top choices:

  • Lekki Conservation Centre: This nature reserve is located about an hour’s drive from Mile 12 and offers hiking trails, canopy walkways, birdwatching, and other outdoor activities.

  • Ikorodu Hills: These rolling hills in Ikorodu, just a few kilometers from Mile 12, offer some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area. The views from the top are breathtaking, and the hills are also dotted with waterfalls and small ponds.

Best Things to Do in Mile 12: Activities and Tours

Beyond the outdoor adventures and historical attractions, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Mile 12, including:

  • Visit the Mile 12 Market: This bustling market is a must-visit for anyone looking to experience the local culture and get a taste of Nigeria’s rich culinary heritage.

  • Take a City Tour: A guided city tour will give you an in-depth look at the many historic and cultural landmarks of this vibrant city. Guided tours are available from a variety of local tour operators.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mile 12

If you’re planning a longer stay in Mile 12, here’s an itinerary you can follow to ensure you make the most of your time in this unique and exciting part of Nigeria:

Food and Drink Scene of Mile 12

Food is a big part of the culture in Mile 12, and there are plenty of delicious dishes and local beverages to try. Here’s our list of must-try dishes and drinks to sample while you’re here:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice: This tomato-based rice dish is a staple of Nigerian cuisine and is sure to satisfy anyone’s appetite.

  • Pounded Yam: This dish is made by pounding boiled yam with a mortar and pestle until it reaches a smooth consistency, and is typically served with a variety of soups and stews.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: This refreshing drink is made from boiled hibiscus flowers and is popular throughout Nigeria. It has a sweet-tart flavor and is often served chilled.

  • Palm Wine: This fermented palm tree sap is a popular alcoholic beverage in Nigeria and has a slightly sweet, fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Mile 12: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in and around Mile 12, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Aro Hotel and Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a swimming pool and restaurant.

  • Chrisland Hotel and Suites: This family-friendly hotel features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant on-site.

What to Buy in Mile 12: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back from Mile 12, here are some of the best options:

  • Aso Oke Fabric: This traditional Nigerian fabric is made from brightly-colored yarn and is often used for clothing and home decor items.

  • Wooden Carvings: Hand-carved wooden statues, masks, and other decorative items are a common sight in local markets and make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mile 12

Nigerians are known for their hospitality, and visitors to Mile 12 can expect a warm welcome from the locals. It’s important to dress conservatively and respect local customs and traditions while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Mile 12

The best time to visit Mile 12 is between December and February when the weather is cooler and drier. During the rest of the year, temperatures can be hot and humid, with occasional rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mile 12

When visiting Mile 12, here are some key do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Wear revealing clothing or clothing that is deemed inappropriate by local standards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mile 12

Like any urban area, Mile 12 has its share of petty crime, so it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you when exploring the city. It’s also a good idea to travel with a companion or a reputable tour guide.

Health in Mile 12

Visitors to Mile 12 should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and should ensure they’re up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Nigeria. It is also advisable to drink only bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Mile 12: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mile 12

The nearest airport to Mile 12 is the Murtala Muhammed International Airport, which is located in Lagos, about 20 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Mile 12

Mile 12 is a major transportation hub, and several bus companies operate regular services between the neighborhood and other cities in Nigeria.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services are widely available in Mile 12 and are a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Mile 12

Mile 12 is a small neighborhood, and walking is a great way to explore the area. For longer distances, taxis and ride-sharing services are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Mile 12: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Mile 12, here are a few nearby destinations worth checking out:

  • Victoria Island: This upscale neighborhood in Lagos is known for its high-end shopping, dining, and nightlife.

  • Lekki Conservation Centre: Just a short drive from Mile 12, this nature reserve offers hiking trails, birdwatching, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Mile 12 may not be the most renowned tourist destination in Nigeria, but it offers a unique blend of history, culture, and adventure that makes it well worth a visit. From hiking and biking to exploring local museums and markets, there is truly something for everyone in this fascinating neighborhood.
