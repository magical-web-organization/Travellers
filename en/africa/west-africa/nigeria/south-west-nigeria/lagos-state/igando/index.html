Introduction to Igando

Igando is a bustling suburb of Lagos State, located in South West Nigeria. It is a continually growing and developing area, with a rich cultural heritage and an abundance of attractions and activities to offer visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Igando

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, there are several museums and historical places to visit in Igando:

  • National Museum Lagos - Located just a short distance from Igando, this world-class museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in Nigerian history, culture, and art.
  • Badagry Heritage Museum - This museum is dedicated to documenting the history of Badagry, a nearby town that played a significant role in the transatlantic slave trade.
  • Iga Idunganran Palace - This historic palace was once the residence of some of Nigeria’s most influential rulers and is now open to the public as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Igando: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to explore the great outdoors, Igando has several natural attractions to offer:

  • Eko Tourist Beach Resort - This beautiful beach resort offers a range of activities, including swimming, surfing, kayaking, and more.
  • Igando-Ikotun Local Government Conservation Park - This forest reserve offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city and is home to a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, birds, and more.
  • Oyinbo Fish Pond - This serene pond is a popular spot for fishing and picnicking, and its picturesque surroundings make it the perfect place to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Igando

If you are looking for some hiking or biking trails, there are several scenic routes in and around Igando:

  • LUFASI Nature Park - This beautiful nature reserve offers several hiking trails that wind through lush forests, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bike City Sports - This outdoor recreation center offers several bike trails of varying difficulty, making it a great destination for both experienced and novice riders.

Best Things to Do in Igando: Activities and Tours

There is never a dull moment in Igando, with a range of exciting activities and must-see attractions to keep you entertained:

  • Kayak the Lagos Lagoon - Rent a kayak and explore the beautiful Lagos Lagoon, the largest in Africa, while enjoying the scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Visit The Kalakuta Museum - This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Nigerian music artist, political activist, and Afrobeat legend, Fela Kuti.
  • Explore The Ikeja Shopping Mall - This modern shopping mall offers a wide range of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options, making it a perfect destination for a day out with friends or family.

Food and Drink Scene of Igando

Must-Try Dishes

Nigeria has a rich culinary heritage, and there is plenty to discover in Igando:

  • Egusi Soup - A popular Nigerian soup prepared with melon seeds and served with Pounded Yam or Fufu.
  • Suya - A spicy, skewered street food made with marinated beef, chicken, or goat meat.
  • Jollof Rice - A flavorful West African rice dish made with tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

While in Igando, make sure to try some of the local beverages:

  • Palm Wine - A traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of the oil palm tree.
  • Zobo - A refreshing, sweet-tart hibiscus tea that is a popular beverage across Nigeria.
  • Chapman - A fruity cocktail made with soda, fruit juice, grenadine, and a dash of bitters.

Where to Stay in Igando: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Igando offers several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels:

  • De Santos Hotel and Suites - A modern boutique hotel offering comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Rockview Hotel - A luxurious hotel with beautiful rooms, a pool, and a restaurant.
  • Simplicity Inn - A budget-friendly hostel with friendly staff and clean, comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Igando: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a piece of Igando to take home, consider picking up one of these souvenirs:

  • Adire Fabric - A colorful, hand-dyed fabric that is a traditional textile in Nigeria.
  • Calabash Bowls - Hand-carved wooden bowls that make beautiful decorative items.
  • Local Beads - Vibrant beads made from a range of materials, including glass, plastic, and clay, and often used in traditional Nigerian jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Igando

Nigeria is a country with a rich and varied culture, and while in Igando, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions:

  • Language - The official language of Nigeria is English, but there are several local languages spoken in Igando, including Yoruba and Igbo.
  • Dress Code - Dress modestly and respect local customs when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  • Greeting Customs - Handshakes are a common way to greet people in Nigeria, but it’s important to be aware of local customs and traditions when meeting people for the first time.

Best Time to Visit Igando

The best time to visit Igando is during the dry season, which lasts from November to March. The weather is mild and dry during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Igando

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  • Learn a few phrases in the local language to show respect for the culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water. Drink bottled water or use a water filter.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal activities.
  • Don’t take photographs without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Igando

While Nigeria has its share of security concerns, Igando is generally a safe place to visit. However, it is important to take some precautions to ensure your safety while traveling:

  • Avoid going out alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables out of sight.
  • Use a registered taxi or ride-hailing service for transportation.

Health in Igando

There are several health risks associated with travel to Nigeria, including malaria, yellow fever, and typhoid fever. Make sure to take the necessary precautions to protect your health:

  • Get vaccinated before traveling.
  • Use mosquito repellents and nets to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Drink bottled or filtered water and avoid raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Igando: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Igando

The closest airport to Igando is the Murtala Muhammed International Airport, located in Lagos. From here, you can take a taxi or hire a private car to get to Igando.

Bus Stations Near Igando

Igando is well-connected to other parts of Nigeria through its bus stations. Some of the major bus terminals in the area include the Oshodi Bus Terminal and the Lagos State Transport Management Authority (LASTMA) Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Another option for traveling to Igando is to take a train to Lagos and then take a taxi or hire a private car to get to Igando.

How to Get Around in Igando

There are several transportation options available in Igando, including taxis, buses, and motorbikes. Taxis are the most convenient form of transportation and can be easily hailed on the street or through ride-hailing services like Uber or Bolt.

Where to Go Next from Igando: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in the Lagos area, there are several exciting destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Badagry Town - This historic town was once a major center for the transatlantic slave trade and offers several historical attractions and museums to explore.
  • Lekki Conservation Centre - This nature reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, crocodiles, and more, and offers several hiking trails and canopy walks.
  • Idanre Hills - This scenic hillside village is home to several historic and cultural attractions, including the Idanre Hilltop Shrine and the Orosun Festival.

Conclusion

Igando is an exciting destination that offers a rich blend of culture, outdoor adventures, and historical attractions. From its beautiful beaches to its lush forests, there is something for everyone in this vibrant Nigerian suburb. So pack your bags and get ready to explore!
