Introduction to Snake Island

Located in Lagos City, in Lagos State, Nigeria, Snake Island is an often-overlooked gem in South West Nigeria. The island has a rich cultural history and is famous for its scenic views and outdoor adventures. Discover the best of Snake Island with our comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Snake Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Experience the history and culture of Snake Island by visiting the museums and historical landmarks.

  • Leventis Museum: This museum houses a collection of African arts and crafts, ceramics, and traditional clothing.
  • National Museum Lagos: The museum has an extensive collection of Nigerian art, including traditional sculptures, textiles, and contemporary art pieces.

Natural Attractions of Snake Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Snake Island has breathtaking natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

  • Lighthouse Beach: This is Snake Island’s most famous attraction. The beach has a lighthouse that provides stunning views of Lagos City.
  • Tarkwa Bay Beach: This beach is ideal for swimming and water sports, including kayaking and surfing.
  • Snake Island Mangrove Forest: The mangrove forest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the forest by boat or canoe.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Snake Island

Explore Snake Island’s natural beauty by going on a hike or bike ride along the numerous trails on the island.

  • Snake Island Trail: This trail is perfect for hikers and bikers, with its beautiful views of the mangrove forest and coastline.
  • Tarkwa Bay Trail: This trail takes hikers to Tarkwa Bay Beach and provides picturesque views of the ocean.

Best Things to Do in Snake Island: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to water sports, Snake Island has an activity for every type of traveler.

  • Boat Tour of the Mangrove Forest: Take a boat tour of the mangrove forest to experience the natural beauty of the island.
  • Surf Lessons: Sign up for a surf lesson at Tarkwa Bay Beach to catch some waves.

Food and Drink Scene of Snake Island

Must-Try Dishes

Nigerian cuisine is famous for its bold flavors and unique ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes in Snake Island.

  • Jollof Rice: This one-pot rice dish is made with tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices.
  • Suya: This grilled meat dish is marinated in a blend of spices and served with a peanut sauce.
  • Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup: Pounded yam is a starchy side dish made from boiled yam, which is then mashed and molded into balls. Egusi soup is a savory stew made from melon seeds and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with some of Nigeria’s traditional beverages.

  • Chapman: This drink is made from a mix of fruit juices, soda, and bitters.
  • Zobo: This drink is made from hibiscus flowers and is often sweetened with sugar or honey.
  • Palm Wine: This alcoholic drink is made from the sap of the palm tree.

Where to Stay in Snake Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options in Snake Island, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Lagoon Crest: This boutique hotel is renowned for its waterfront views and comfortable rooms.
  • Kadoma Guest Inn: This affordable guesthouse is located in the heart of Snake Island and offers easy access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Snake Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Snake Island home with you by buying some locally made souvenirs and products.

  • Adire Fabric: This traditional cloth is made using a resist-dye technique and comes in a variety of colors and patterns.
  • Oja, Talking Drum: This percussion instrument is often used in Nigerian music and is made using a hollowed-out log and goat skin.
  • African Print Clothes: These colorful and intricate textiles come in a variety of styles and designs and are perfect for adding some African flair to your wardrobe.

Local Culture and Customs of Snake Island

Nigeria has a rich cultural heritage that is still celebrated to this day. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly when it comes to dress and behavior.

Best Time to Visit Snake Island

The best time to visit Snake Island is between December and February, when the weather is dry and sunny with a nice breeze. The rainy season lasts from May to October, with the heaviest rains in August and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Snake Island

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Try some local foods and drinks.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in the local language.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Snake Island

Like any big city, Lagos has its share of security concerns. Visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding unfamiliar areas at night and keeping valuables out of sight. It is also advisable to use a reputable tour guide when traveling around Snake Island.

Health in Snake Island

Travelers should take the necessary precautions to avoid common travel-related illnesses, such as malaria and traveler’s diarrhea. It is also important to stay hydrated and protect oneself against the harsh sun by wearing a hat and applying sunscreen.

Getting to Snake Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Snake Island

The closest airport to Snake Island is Murtala Muhammed International Airport, which serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Snake Island

Buses run frequently from the interstate bus terminal in Lagos to neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

Hire a taxi or private car to take you to Snake Island, or take a ferry from Lagos to Tarkwa Bay.

How to Get Around in Snake Island

The best way to get around Snake Island is by taxi or hire a private car. Public transport options are also available but can be unreliable at times. It is also possible to explore the island on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Snake Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several interesting places to visit near Snake Island, including:

  • Lekki Conservation Centre: This nature reserve is home to a variety of birds and wildlife and has a canopy walkway.
  • Bar Beach: This beach is located on Victoria Island and is popular for swimming and surfing.
  • Badagry: This historic town is located near the Nigerian and Benin border and is famous for its slave trade history.

Conclusion

Snake Island is a hidden gem in Nigeria that is well worth a visit. From outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and delicious cuisine, Snake Island has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Snake Island and make the most of your stay.
