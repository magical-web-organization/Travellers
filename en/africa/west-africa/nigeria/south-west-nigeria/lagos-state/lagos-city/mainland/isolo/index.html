Introduction to Isolo

Isolo is a bustling city in the heart of Lagos State, Nigeria. It is located on the Mainland and is home to several notable landmarks and attractions. Whether you are a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor to Lagos, Isolo has something to offer. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Isolo.

List of Places to Visit in Isolo

When it comes to tourist destinations, Isolo has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Isolo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history enthusiast, Isolo has several museums and historical places that you can visit to learn more about the city’s past. Some of the popular options include:

  • National Museum Lagos: This museum is located in the city center and showcases a wide range of traditional Nigerian art, including textiles, sculptures, and masks.
  • New Afrika Shrine: The New Afrika Shrine is a music venue and cultural center that pays homage to the legendary African musician, Fela Kuti. It is a must-visit destination for music fans and those interested in African culture.
  • Freedom Park: This park was once a colonial-era prison but has since been converted into a cultural center that hosts various events throughout the year. It is a great place to learn about Nigeria’s struggle for independence from British rule.

Natural Attractions of Isolo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are someone who loves the outdoors, Isolo has plenty of scenic views and outdoor adventures that you can enjoy. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Lekki Conservation Center: This center offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of Lagos State. You can take a canopy walk through the treetops or go bird watching on the grounds.
  • Nike Art Gallery: This gallery is known for its vast collection of African art, textiles, and sculptures. Visitors can take a tour of the gallery and learn about the history and significance of each piece.
  • Tarkwa Bay Beach: This beach is located on a small island just off the coast of Lagos. It offers visitors a chance to enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and other water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isolo

Isolo has several hiking and biking routes that are worth exploring. Some of the best options include:

  • Ikeja Club Golf Course: This 18-hole golf course is surrounded by lush greenery, making it the perfect spot for a leisurely walk or bike ride.
  • Miles 12 to 1 Hike: This hiking trail spans a distance of 11 kilometers and offers stunning views of the Lagos Lagoon.
  • Ojota Pedestrian Bridge: The Ojota Pedestrian Bridge offers a picturesque view of Ikeja, Anthony, and Maryland. Visitors can walk or bike across the bridge and take in the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Isolo: Activities and Tours

Isolo has an endless list of activities and tours that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best options:

  • Lagos City Walking Tour: This tour takes visitors on a journey through the heart of Lagos, showcasing its rich history and culture.
  • Nigerian Cuisine Cooking Class: This class offers visitors a chance to learn how to prepare traditional Nigerian dishes and enjoy a delicious meal afterward.
  • Makoko Canoe Tour: This tour takes visitors through the floating town of Makoko, offering a glimpse of life on the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isolo

If you are planning to visit Isolo for a week, here is a sample itinerary that you can follow to make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Isolo

Must-Try Dishes

Isolo has a diverse food scene that offers visitors a chance to try new and exciting dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Jollof Rice: This dish is a staple in Nigerian cuisine and consists of rice, tomato sauce, and spices.
  • Suya: Suya is a popular street food that is made from spiced and grilled beef or chicken.
  • Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup: This dish is made from yam that has been boiled and mashed until it has a dough-like consistency. It is served with a soup made from melon seeds, spinach, and other vegetables.

Local Beverages

Isolo has several local beverages that are worth trying. Here are some of the best options:

  • Zobo: Zobo is a popular drink made from hibiscus leaves, ginger, and other spices. It is a refreshing drink that is often served chilled.
  • Palm Wine: Palm wine is a traditional drink that is made from the sap of palm trees. It has a sweet and tangy flavor and is often served at social gatherings.

Where to Stay in Isolo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isolo has several accommodation options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Protea Hotel Ikeja: This hotel is located in the heart of Ikeja and offers guests modern amenities, including a fitness center and a swimming pool.
  • Maison Fahrenheit: This boutique hotel is located in the upscale Victoria Island neighborhood and offers guests stylish rooms and a rooftop bar with stunning views of Lagos.
  • Ibis Lagos Airport: This budget-friendly hotel is located close to the airport and offers guests comfortable rooms and easy access to transportation.

What to Buy in Isolo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking to bring home some souvenirs from your trip to Isolo, here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Ankara Fabric: Ankara fabric is a brightly colored and patterned fabric that is used to make clothing and accessories. It is a popular souvenir among visitors to Nigeria.
  • Beaded Jewelry: Isolo has several bead markets where you can buy handmade beaded jewelry.
  • Local Art: Isolo is home to several talented artists who create paintings, sculptures, and other types of art. Visiting a local art gallery is a great way to support the local art scene and bring home a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Isolo

Nigeria has a rich and diverse culture, and Isolo is no exception. Here are some customs and cultural norms to keep in mind when visiting Isolo:

  • Dress Code: Nigerians take their appearance seriously, and it is important to dress conservatively when in public. This means avoiding revealing clothing or anything that may be deemed offensive.
  • Greetings: Nigerians place a high value on greetings and it is customary to greet people when entering a room or meeting someone for the first time. Common greetings include “good morning”, “good afternoon”, and “good evening”.
  • Respect for Elders: In Nigerian culture, elders are respected and it is important to show deference to your elders.

Best Time to Visit Isolo

The best time to visit Isolo is between October and March when the weather is mild and dry. The rainy season runs from April to September, so it is best to avoid visiting during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isolo

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in the local language to communicate with locals.
  • Be respectful of Nigerian culture and customs.
  • Negotiate prices when shopping at markets.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without asking for permission first.
  • Display public affection.
  • Accept food or drinks from strangers.

Safety and Health

Isolo is a safe destination for tourists, but it is always important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Isolo

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation.
  • Keep your personal belongings, particularly your passport and money, in a safe and secure place.

Health in Isolo

  • Drink bottled water to avoid waterborne illnesses.
  • Apply mosquito repellent to protect against malaria.
  • Be cautious when eating street food and make sure it is properly cooked and prepared.

Getting to Isolo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Isolo is easily accessible from various parts of Lagos. Here are the best transportation options to get to Isolo:

  • Air: The Murtala Muhammed International Airport is located in Ikeja and is about a 15-minute drive from Isolo.
  • Bus: Isolo is well-connected to the Lagos bus system, and visitors can take a bus to and from other parts of Lagos.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and Ubers are readily available in Lagos and are an efficient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Isolo

Isolo is a relatively small city, and most places are easily accessible on foot. However, for longer distances, visitors can take a taxi or motorcycle taxi, known as an “okada.”

Where to Go Next from Isolo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Isolo is located in the heart of Lagos and is a great base for exploring other parts of the city. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Victoria Island: This upscale neighborhood is home to several restaurants, bars, and shops and is a great place to spend an afternoon or evening.
  • Lagos Island: Lagos Island is home to several historical sites, including the Brazilian Quarter and the Slave Museum.
  • Badagry: This historic town is located about an hour’s drive from Isolo and is known for its role in the transatlantic slave trade.

Conclusion

Isolo is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. From its rich cultural history to its diverse food scene and outdoor adventures, Isolo is a city worth exploring. With this comprehensive guide, you can make the most of your trip to Isolo and experience all that this city has to offer.
