Introduction to Mainland

Located in the bustling city of Lagos, Mainland is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. Whether you’re looking for cultural experiences, outdoor adventures or simply wish to explore Nigeria’s largest city, Mainland has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to see, do, eat and stay in Mainland.

List of Places to Visit in Mainland

When it comes to traveling to Mainland, there are plenty of attractions to check out. Here are some of the best places to visit in Mainland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mainland has a rich history and there are several sites and museums where you can learn about it. These include:

  • National Museum Lagos: The National Museum Lagos was established in 1957 and has a diverse collection of Nigerian art, archeology, ethnography and natural history.
  • Kalakuta Republic Museum: Dedicated to the life and legacy of legendary musician, Fela Kuti, the Kalakuta Republic Museum offers insight into his music, activism, and political views.
  • Freedom Park Lagos: This former colonial prison recently transformed into a cultural center in 2010 offers live music concerts, art exhibitions, and cultural performances.

Natural Attractions of Mainland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures or scenic views, Mainland has plenty of options. Here are some must-visit natural attractions of Mainland:

  • LUFASI Nature Park: This 20-hectare park is home to hundreds of species of plants and animals, and is perfect for a day trip with family and friends.
  • Lekki Conservation Centre: Located in Lekki Peninsula, just 25 minutes from Mainland, this conservation center is home to diverse wildlife, including monkeys, forest antelopes, and crocodiles.
  • Ikeja City Mall: This modern, large-scale shopping center is a great option for those looking for indoor activities and retail therapy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mainland

Mainland is an excellent place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. These are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Mainland:

  • Laid-Back Bike Rides through Sabo, Yaba: This bike route is perfect for those seeking a casual biking experience, with views of historical landmarks, local homes and roadside sellers.
  • Ikeja GRA: This is a great route for those wanting to test their endurance, offering inclines and flat terrain.

Best Things to Do in Mainland: Activities and Tours

Mainland offers an array of activities and tours, suitable for all age ranges. Here are some of the best things to do in Mainland:

  • Kayaking at LUFASI Nature Park: Enjoy the calm, serene waters of LUFASI Nature Park with kayaking, with rental options are available on site.
  • Food Tours of Mainland: This tour takes you on a culinary journery with diverse cuisine options at various local eateries and street food vendors.
  • Art Classes in Yaba: Experience Nigeria’s vibrant arts scene with several art classes specifically designed for tourists at Nike Art Gallery and Art Twenty One.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mainland

Here is a week-long itinerary that covers some of the best attractions and activities in Mainland:

Day 1 - Arrival and Relaxation

Start your trip on a relaxed note by checking into one of Mainland’s luxurious hotels, or opt for a more budget-friendly guesthouse.

Day 2 - Cultural Attractions

Take a trip back in time with visit to the Kalakuta Republic Museum and the National Museum Lagos.

Day 3 - Outdoor Activities

Visit Lekki Conservation Centre for a leisurely hike, and Isis situated between the Lagoon and the Atlantic Ocean.

Day 4 - Visit The Food Markets of Sabo

Sabo is one of those areas in Mainland where street food is not just a snack but an everyday meal. You’ll get the best freshest and cheapest food on this street.

Day 5 - Shopping Day

Spen your time shopping in Mainland’s numerous malls, with several international-based fashion houses, quality local clothing brands and souvenir shops scattered throughout.

Day 6 - Relaxation in Style

Relax and unwind with a massage or spa session at one of Mainland’s luxury hotels, with various local stores that offer Nigerian-made body oils with unique fragrances.

Day 7 - Departure

Depart Lagos Mainland, Nigeria with fond memories and memorable souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Mainland

When it comes to food and drink, Mainland has a diverse and flavorful scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Mainland:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice: A popular dish in Nigeria, Jollof Rice is a one-pot dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices.
  • Suya: This popular street food consists of skewered, marinated meat (usually beef or chicken) cooked over an open fire.
  • Pounded Yam and Soup: Made from yam and served with soup, this is a staple dish in Nigerian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Sorbitol: This local drink is made from watermelon and is perfect for hot weather.

Where to Stay in Mainland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mainland has a wide range of accommodation options, suitable for all preferences and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mainland:

  • The Moorhouse, Ikoyi Lagos - MGallery Collection: Located in the heart of Lagos, this hotel offers luxury amenities and services.
  • Maison Fahrenheit: This boutique hotel is located in Victoria Island and is known for its Moroccan-inspired decor.
  • Ibis Lagos Ikeja: A budget-friendly option, this hotel offers clean rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Mainland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping in Mainland can be an adventure as it offers a diverse array of products to choose from. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Traditional Fabrics: Mainland sells a diverse range of unique traditional fabrics that make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Local Art: Visit art galleries like Nike Art Gallery and Art Twenty One for some unique, handmade wooden crafts that make for great home décor additions or gifts for friends and family.
  • Local Spices: Bring home authentic Nigerian flavors with local spices like Jamaican Pepper, Nutmeg, and Dawadawa.

Local Culture and Customs of Mainland

It’s important to understand cultural customs and traditions during your visit to Mainland, particularly considering it is a largely conservative society. Here are some important notes related to culture and customs in Mainland:

  • Modesty in Dressing: Dress modestly out of respect for Nigerian culture.
  • Handshaking: Often accompanied by a light embrace, standard greetings in Nigeria involve a firm handshake, direct eye contact and addressing the person by his/her title.
  • Tipping Customs: Tipping is accepted in Mainland, particularly in high-end restaurants and luxury hotels.

Best Time to Visit Mainland

The best time to visit Mainland is between December and April, as the weather is moderate and relatively cooler. The weather is mainly hot and humid for the rest of the year, with occasional rain showers in some months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mainland

To make the most out of your trip to Mainland, here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Interact with locals: Most Nigerians are friendly and helpful, so don’t hesitate to ask for help or recommendations.
  • Respect the culture: Respect Nigeria-British hybrid culture customs and traditions, especially Islamic customs and receive prior information for good understanding of the lifestyle.
  • Experience the Local Life: Explore outside of the main tourist areas and immerse yourself in the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Travel Alone at Night: Avoid traveling alone at night, or taking public transportation when it’s dark outside, as it is not always safe.
  • Cheap Deals: Avoid unkown vendors or those with gueranteed cut-price deals or offers as it can be a scam or fake.
  • Photography and Videography: Always take permission before taking photos or videos of locals, as some people may find it disrespectful.

Safety and Health

It’s important to remain vigilant when in Mainland, especially as a foreigner. Here are some notes on safety and health:

Safety in Mainland

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: Avoid walking alone at night-time, opt for a rideshare of taxi instead.
  • Use a Money Belt: Make use of a money belt as pickpocketing is common in crowded areas.
  • Stay Alert: Take note of your surroundings and trust your instincts, if you feel uncomfortable or in danger try to remove yourself from the dangerous situation as soon as possible.

Health in Mainland

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water as humidity levels in Mainland can be high.
  • Get Vaccinated: It is important to obtain the relevant immunizations prior to traveling to Mainland to minimize the risk of contracting diseases such as Yellow Fever and Malaria.
  • Carry Hand Sanitizer: Personal hygiene is a big deal and locals may not always have the same hygiene habits, so carry hand sanitizer as some restrooms may not have soap or running water.

Getting to Mainland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mainland offers several transportation options available for visitors, these include:

Airports Near Mainland

  • Murtala Muhammed International Airport: Located in Lagos, this is the busiest airport in Nigeria and a central aviation hub that conencts Lagos Mainland to most African countries, as well as Europe, Asia and North America.

Bus Stations Near Mainland

  • Mazamaza Terminus: Minibuses, Keke tricycles, and “okadas” (motorcycle taxis) can all be found at this bus station that takes travelers across Nigeria.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Car rentals are available at the airport, however, the service may be expensive due to high costs of fuel, tolls and customs.
  • Taxis and Rideshares: Taxis are available 24 hours around Lagos, there are also many ridesharing services like Uber and Bolt with good and reliable reviews.

How to Get Around in Mainland

Getting around Mainland is relatively easy and many tourists choose to explore the city via:

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available around Lagos and often offer fixed rates.
  • Rideshare Services: Uber and Bolt offer services in Lagos, and provides relatively affordable and secure rides.
  • Buses: Buses are a popular way to get around Mainland and are very economical, as you will find budget-friendly minibuses and buses.

Where to Go Next from Mainland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mainland is situated perfectly for visitors to have access to other nearby destinations such as:

  • Lekki Peninsula: Located approximately an hour away from Mainland, this area boasts pristine beaches and beautiful resorts.
  • Ikoyi and Victoria Island: These two neighboring islands are the central business districts of Lagos and offer trendy eateries, shopping areas, and beaches.
  • Badagry: This peaceful coastal town is just 40 minutes away from Mainland, and offers an interesting historic architecture and beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Mainland has a lot to offer, and with this comprehensive travel guide in hand, visitors can easily navigate the city’s attractions, food and culture. It’s a perfect city if you are seeking more of a relaxed atmosphere, and want to mix fun, adventure and relaxation all in one destination.
