Introduction to Lekki

Located in Lagos City, in Lagos State, in the southwestern region of Nigeria, Lekki is a bustling city known for its natural beauty, thriving commerce, and fascinating history. Surrounded by pristine beaches, scenic waterways, and verdant forests, Lekki offers visitors a perfect blend of natural wonders, cultural treasures, and contemporary delights.

With this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Lekki’s must-visit places, experiences, and cultural customs. You’ll explore everything the city has to offer and fall in love with its charm.

List of Places to Visit in Lekki

Lekki is brimming with tourist attractions, where the people take immense pride in their heritage and culture. Here are some of the must-visit places in Lekki:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lekki Conservation Centre: Located on Lekki Peninsula, this conservation center features a scenic walking trail through its lush forest and wetlands, an elevation walkway to give you a spectacular view of the reserve, and a treehouse. It is an ideal location for nature lovers and bird watchers.

  • Omenka Gallery: An excellent option for art lovers, this gallery showcases contemporary and historic African art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs.

  • National Museum Lagos: As one of Nigeria’s foremost museums, it features collections of Nigerian art, historical and cultural artifacts from different regions of Nigeria, including royalty regalia, weapons, masks, and musical instruments.

Natural Attractions of Lekki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lekki Beach: The beautiful Lekki Beach is a long stretch of golden sand and blue waters along the Atlantic Ocean. It is the perfect place for picnics, sunbathing, swimming, barbecues, and water sports activities.

  • Alpha Beach: Alpha Beach is a sandy beach that offers an idyllic location for tourists who are looking for relaxed vibe and beautiful scenery. You’ll find beach huts, barbecues, and watersports equipment available for rent.

  • Eleko Beach: Eleko Beach is a picturesque and tranquil beach featuring beautiful rock formations, lagoons, and sandy stretches. Visitors are attracted to its gentle waves with safety fenced areas to protect swimmers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lekki

Lekki is an ideal location for hikers and bikers who love exploring natural trails, off-road cycling, or just taking leisurely strolls. Here are some of the hiking/biking routes you must explore:

  • Nature’s Walk: The Nature Walk is within the Lekki Conservation Center which provides a path that takes visitors into the forest areas of the reserve to experience nature up close.

  • Eleko Beach: Eleko Beach’s stunning landscape with beautiful rock formations and sandy stretches makes for a unique hiking experience.

  • Lekki-Ikoyi Suspension Bridge: The Lekki-Ikoyi Bridge is famous for its beautiful views of the Ikoyi skyline and the Lagos Lagoon. A pedestrian walkway is available for visitors to walk and bike across the bridge.

Best Things to Do in Lekki: Activities and Tours

Lekki has a wide variety of fun activities and exciting tours that are perfect for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Lekki:

  • Surfing: Lekki Beach is the best spot in Nigeria for surfing. You’ll find a range of boards available for rent and can take lessons from experienced instructors.

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour along Lekki’s waterways, including the Lagos Lagoon and Lekki Conservation Centre, and become more involved in the coastal ecology.

  • Quad Biking: Explore Lekki’s landscape in style by taking an adrenaline-filled off-road Quad biking adventure. The Quad bikes are suitable for both adults and children, and safety gear is provided.

Food and Drink Scene of Lekki

Lekki has a thriving food and drink scene with a wide variety of options to satisfy every taste. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Efo Riro: Efo Riro is a popular vegetable soup made with spinach, tomato, meat, and fish. It is a delicious and healthy meal that is best enjoyed with pounded yam or eba.

  • Jollof Rice: Jollof Rice is a flavorful and spicy party rice that is a must-have in West African cuisine.

  • Suya: Suya is a West African specialty made from skewered meat, seasoned with spices, and barbecued over an open flame. It is a tasty and savory snack full of flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is an alcoholic drink made from fermented palm sap. It is also enjoyed as a refreshing and nutritious juice and has a slightly sweet taste.

  • Zobo: Zobo is a refreshing drink made from hibiscus leaves, ginger, and sugar. It is a tangy, refreshing drink that is perfect for hot weather.

  • Chapman: Chapman is a non-alcoholic fruity drink made with orange juice, lemon-lime soda, grenadine syrup, and bitters. It is a sweet and refreshing drink that’s perfect for a hot day.

What to Buy in Lekki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Lekki, you will find a range of unique souvenirs and local products to take home with you. Here are some popular options:

  • Adire: Adire is a unique, locally made textile that uses natural dyes and traditional Yoruba motifs.

  • Wooden Carvings: Wooden carvings are exquisite examples of Nigerian craftsmanship, and there are a variety of unique designs to choose from.

  • Beaded Jewelry: Handmade beaded jewelry in Lekki reflects the local designs and motifs and is available in different styles and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Lekki

Lekki’s culture and customs are vibrant and unique, reflecting the city’s diverse history and cultural heritage. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Greeting: It is customary to greet locals with a “Salam Aleikum” (peace be upon you) or “Bawo Ni” (How are you?) when you meet.

  • Dressing: It is important to dress conservatively and modestly, especially when visiting religious or cultural attractions.

  • Etiquette: Nigeria’s customary etiquette is to address elders respectfully with “Aunty” or “Uncle” and accept generous hospitality with gratitude.

Best Time to Visit Lekki

The best time to visit Lekki is between November and March when the weather is relatively dry and cool, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lekki

Do:

  • Respect the Culture: As with most countries, it is important to respect local culture and customs, including dress codes and social etiquette.

  • Travel Safely: Be aware of your surroundings, take care of your personal belongings and avoid isolated areas at night.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Lekki has a vibrant food scene, and you must try the local delicacies while you’re there.

Don’t:

  • Offend the Locals: Avoid making derogatory comments about the culture, religion, or politics as this may cause offense.

  • Display Public Affection: Public displays of affection are considered inappropriate in Lekki.

  • Underestimate the Heat: It can get quite hot in Lekki, so make sure to stay hydrated and wear protective clothing during the day.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lekki

Lekki is generally safe, but it’s always recommended to be cautious and take precautions. Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in isolated areas, and keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Lekki

It’s important to take precautions to avoid contracting mosquito-borne diseases like malaria. Make sure to use mosquito repellent, sleep with mosquito nets, and dress appropriately to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Lekki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lekki is easily accessible by car or public transport. Here are the transportation options to get to Lekki:

  • Airports Near Lekki: Murtala Muhammed International Airport, located about a 50-minute drive from Lekki.

  • Bus Stations Near Lekki: Lekki is well-connected by buses from Lagos City and other cities within Lagos State.

  • Other Transport Options: Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Uber and Taxify are readily available in Lekki.

How to Get Around in Lekki

Getting around in Lekki can be done via hired and shared taxis and buses. Okadas (motorcycle taxis) are another popular option but can be quite dangerous sometimes. If you’re looking to travel in comfort, you can rent a car, or use ride-hailing apps like Uber and Taxify.

Where to Go Next from Lekki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting destinations and tours to explore near Lekki:

  • Badagry: A historic town located in Lagos State, known for its slave trade history, historic artifacts, and cultural heritage.

  • Lagos Island: A prime destination in Lagos, known for its bustling community and fascinating city lifestyle.

  • Ikeja: An upscale neighborhood in Lagos, known for its attractions, museums, and palaces.

Conclusion

Lekki is a vibrant, diverse city that offers a blend of natural wonders, cultural treasures, and contemporary delights.
