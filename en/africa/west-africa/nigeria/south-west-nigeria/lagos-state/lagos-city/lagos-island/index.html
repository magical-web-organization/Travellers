Introduction to Lagos Island

Lagos Island, with its scenic views and rich cultural heritage, is a popular destination for tourists visiting Nigeria. It is located in Lagos, the largest city and former capital of Nigeria. Lagos Island itself is a small but densely populated area that features a mix of old and modern architecture, cultural heritage sites, bustling markets, and beautiful beaches. Lagos Island is a perfect spot for tourists who are looking to explore the rich history and culture of Nigeria.

List of Places to Visit in Lagos Island

There is so much to see and explore in Lagos Island. Here is our list of top places to visit in Lagos Island.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lagos Island houses several cultural and historical sites that offer a glimpse into Nigeria’s rich history and culture. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions on Lagos Island:

  • National Museum Lagos
  • Freedom Park Lagos
  • The Nigerian Railway Corporation Museum

Natural Attractions of Lagos Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Despite being a small island, Lagos Island has an abundance of natural attractions that outdoor enthusiasts will love. These include:

  • Tarkwa Bay
  • Lighthouse Beach
  • Lagos Yacht Club

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lagos Island

Lagos Island has many hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to see the natural beauty of the island up close. Explore these routes to enjoy some of the spectacular sights of the Island:

  • Ijebu Walkway
  • Lekki-Ikoyi Bridge

Best Things to Do in Lagos Island: Activities and Tours

Lagos Island provides tourists with several exhilarating activities and tours that offer a glimpse of the local culture, history, and attractions. These include:

  • Lagos City Tour
  • Lekki Conservation Centre

Week-Long Itinerary for Lagos Island

If you plan on spending a week in Lagos Island, here is our recommended itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day Activities
1 Visit the National Museum Lagos and Freedom Park
2 Go for a hike at the Lekki Conservation Centre and visit Lighthouse Beach
3 Take the Lagos City Tour, which covers all the major landmarks
4 Relax at Tarkwa Bay or explore Lagos Yacht Club
5 See Eleko Beach or the Elegushi Beach
6 Visit the Nigerian Railway Corporation Museum and explore the Lekki-Ikoyi Bridge
7 Go shopping at local markets or try some authentic Nigerian cuisine

What to Buy in Lagos Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lagos Island is famous for its unique traditional handicrafts, fabrics, and textiles. Some of the must-buy souvenirs include:

  • Adire Fabric
  • African Artifacts
  • Beaded Jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Lagos Island

Nigerian culture is rich and dynamic, and Lagos Island embodies this well. The people of Lagos Island are mainly Yorubas and speak the Yoruba language. The Yoruba culture is deeply rooted in traditional customs, arts, and music.

Best Time to Visit Lagos Island

The best time to visit Lagos Island is during the dry season, from November to March. During this period, the weather is relatively drier and mild, making it perfect for sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lagos Island

Do:

  • Try some authentic Nigerian dishes
  • Visit one of Lagos Island’s beaches
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Go out at night instead travel in a group
  • Take pictures of anyone without their consent
  • Carry too much cash while traveling

Safety and Health

Safety in Lagos Island

While Lagos Island is generally safe for visitors, certain precautions should be taken. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Ensure valuable items are secured
  • Avoid ATMs located in poorly lit areas and crowded places
  • Stay in well-lit and busy areas at night

Health in Lagos Island

Visitors to Lagos Island should be wary of the diseases that are endemic to the region. The following are some of the diseases travelers should get vaccinated against:

  • Yellow Fever
  • Malaria
