Introduction to Surulere

Located in Lagos City, in Lagos State, in South West Nigeria, Surulere is a vibrant and bustling community that boasts of a rich cultural heritage, numerous historical landmarks and exciting outdoor adventures. The name “Surulere” means “Patience is Rewarding” in Yoruba, which is one of the major Nigerian languages spoken in the region. True to its name, Surulere rewards visitors with a unique blend of exciting experiences that make it a top destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Surulere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum Lagos: This museum houses the largest collection of Nigerian art and artifacts in the country, providing an insight into Nigeria’s rich and diverse culture and history.
  • Kalakuta Republic Museum: This museum is a tribute to the famous Nigerian Afrobeat pioneer, Fela Kuti, and serves as a memorial to his life and accomplishments.
  • Lagos City Mall: This mall is a great spot for a day of shopping and entertainment, offering a wide range of stores, restaurants and movie theatres.

Natural Attractions of Surulere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • National Stadium Surulere: A multi-purpose stadium with a seating capacity of over 50,000, it serves as host to various local and international sporting events and concerts.
  • The New Afrika Shrine: A vibrant cultural centre, the Afrika Shrine is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience live music, performances and art, with a strong focus on the African arts scene.
  • Adeniran Ogunsanya Shopping Mall: A go-to spot for shopping, dining and entertainment, the Adeniran Ogunsanya Shopping Mall is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surulere

Surulere offers plenty of hiking and biking routes for the adventurous traveler, including the National Stadium trail, Funke Zainab Usman Street trail and the Adeniran Ogunsanya trail.

Best Things to Do in Surulere: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the National Museum Lagos to learn more about Nigeria’s rich cultural history.
  • Attend a live performance at the New Afrika Shrine for an experience of Africa’s vibrant arts and music scene.
  • Explore Lagos City Mall for a variety of shopping and entertainment options.

Food and Drink Scene of Surulere

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice: A popular Nigerian dish made of rice, tomatoes, onions, and various spices, it is a staple of Nigerian cuisine.
  • Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup: A classic Nigerian dish that consists of mashed yam and a soup made from ground melon seeds, vegetables and spices.
  • Akara: A tasty and filling snack made of deep-fried bean cakes.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A popular alcoholic drink made from fermenting the sap of various species of palm tree.
  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers and spices, it is a favourite among locals.

Where to Stay in Surulere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Nest Hotel Lagos: A cozy and affordable hotel that offers comfortable accommodations and convenient access to local attractions.
  • The Blue Lodge Hotel: A luxury hotel that offers beautiful and spacious rooms, with plenty of amenities and services.
  • Elegant Hospitality Guesthouse: A charming and intimate guesthouse that offers personalised services and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Surulere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Adire Fabric: A beautiful and unique fabric made using a tie-dye technique, it is a must-have souvenir from Surulere
  • Traditional Beaded Jewelry: If you’re looking for a special piece of jewelry, the beaded necklaces and bracelets offered by local artisans are a great option.

Local Culture and Customs of Surulere

The people of Surulere are friendly and welcoming, and their culture is an integral part of Nigerian society. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as dress codes and behavioural norms.

Best Time to Visit Surulere

The best time to visit Surulere is between November and March, during the dry season when the weather is cooler and less humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surulere

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and other cultural attractions.
  • Bargain when shopping at markets and fairs.
  • Sample local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Wander into unfamiliar neighbourhoods without a local guide.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surulere

As with any crowded metropolitan area, visitors should take precautions against pickpockets and theft. Avoid carrying valuables in public and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Surulere

Visitors should be up to date with their vaccinations and take precautions against malaria and other infectious diseases. It is also advisable to drink only bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Surulere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Surulere

Murtala Muhammed International Airport is located approximately a 25-minute drive from Surulere.

Bus Stations Near Surulere

The Jibowu Bus Terminal is located within Surulere and offers transportation to various parts of Lagos City and beyond.

Other Options

Metered taxis and ride-sharing services are widely available in Surulere, providing convenient and safe transportation options.

How to Get Around in Surulere

The most convenient way to get around Surulere is by taxi or ride-sharing service. Public transportation options include commercial buses and tricycles known as keke napep.

Where to Go Next from Surulere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Victoria Island: A popular upmarket destination in Lagos City, known for its beach resorts and nightlife.
  • Lekki Conservation Centre: A wildlife reserve that offers a unique and peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of Lagos City.
  • Nike Art Gallery: A beautiful gallery that showcases Nigerian art and culture, located in the Lekki neighbourhood.

Conclusion

Surulere is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re looking to explore local museums and historical sites, hike through beautiful natural scenery or indulge in delicious local cuisine, Surulere is a must-visit destination that promises to leave you with unforgettable memories.
