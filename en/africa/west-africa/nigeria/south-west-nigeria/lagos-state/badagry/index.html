Introduction to Badagry

Located in Lagos State, South West Nigeria, Badagry is a historic town known for its cultural significance and natural attractions. The town has a rich history of slave trade dating back to the 15th century and is home to several museums and historical landmarks. Badagry also boasts stunning beaches, mangrove forests, and lagoons, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Badagry

When you visit Badagry, here are some of the must-see attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Badagry Heritage Museum

The Badagry Heritage Museum is an excellent place to learn about the history and culture of Badagry. It showcases artifacts and documents related to the slave trade, including the Point of No Return, a spot where slaves were loaded onto ships.

Vlekete Slave Market

This slave market is believed to be one of the largest in Africa, where the enslavement and trading of human beings took place. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Seriki Abass Slave Museum

This museum chronicles the history of the slave trade from a Nigerian perspective. It showcases the lives of the Yoruba people during the slave era and their role in the transatlantic slave trade.

Natural Attractions of Badagry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whispering Palms

Whispering Palms is an eco-friendly resort with sandy beaches, palm trees, and tropical vegetation. It offers a range of activities like swimming, beach volleyball, and kayaking. Guests can also take a boat ride to explore the mangrove forest.

Coconut Beach

Coconut Beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. The beach is lined with coconut trees, and the water is crystal clear.

Suntan Beach

Another beautiful beach with pristine white sand and cool waters where you can relax and unwind. Suntan Beach offers beach chairs, umbrellas, and other amenities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Badagry

Badagry has several hiking and biking trails suited for adventurers who want to explore the town’s natural beauty. One such route is the Badagry Hiking Trail, which takes you through the scenic landscape of Badagry town, including farmland, hills, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Badagry: Activities and Tours

Here are some exciting things to include in your Badagry itinerary:

Boat Tour at the Lagos Lagoon

If you are interested in seeing marine life, taking a boat ride on Lagos Lagoon is the perfect choice. The tour takes you through beautiful lagoons and mangroves.

Badagry Day Tour

Join a guided day tour and explore Badagry’s most popular landmarks, including the Slave Museum, the Brazilian Baracoon, and the First Storey Building.

Tunwase Court

Visit Tunwase Court to watch local craftsmen make beautiful handcrafted works of art from metal and wood.

Food and Drink Scene of Badagry

Badagry has a vibrant culinary scene with street food, restaurants, and cafes serving up delicious dishes influenced by Afro-Brazilian, Yoruba, and Egun cuisines.

Must-Try Dishes

Ewa Agonyin

Ewa Agonyin is a popular street food in Badagry made of mashed beans, palm oil, onions, pepper, and spices.

Moi Moi

Moi Moi is a Nigerian steamed bean pudding made of black-eyed peas.

Pepper Soup

Pepper Soup is a spicy meat soup made with fish, chicken, or goat meat and spices.

Local Beverages

Palm Wine

A traditional alcoholic drink made from the sap of palm trees, fermented naturally without added yeast or bacteria.

Kunu

Kunu is a Nigerian non-alcoholic drink made from grains like millet, rice or sorghum. It is sweetened with sugar, spices and sometimes mixed with milk.

Where to Stay in Badagry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Badagry has several accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some suggestions:

Whispering Palms Resort

This eco-friendly resort offers spacious rooms with a garden view, perfect for families and couples. It also has beach access, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

Badagry Coral Hotel

A modern hotel that features air-conditioned rooms, a bar, and restaurant. It also provides airport transfer and tour services to guests.

La Campagne Tropicana Beach Resort

This resort is located on the Atlantic coast of Lagos and features beachfront rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool. It also offers spa treatments and organized tours.

What to Buy in Badagry: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to take home a piece of Badagry? Here are some souvenirs and local products you can buy:

Wooden Artifacts

Wooden artifacts are an essential product of Badagry. They are handcrafted by local artisans and feature intricate carvings of humans, animals, and scenery.

Basketry

Basketry is another product of Badagry that showcases the town’s rich history and tradition. Each basket has unique patterning, decorative designs and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Badagry

In Badagry, it is customary to respect the elderly and show hospitality to guests. It is also important to dress modestly when visiting cultural landmarks or attending traditional events.

Best Time to Visit Badagry

The best time to visit Badagry is between December and February, when the weather is cool and dry. During this period, the town holds several cultural events, including the Badagry Heritage Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Badagry

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures or videos of people without consent.
  • Litter around public spaces.
  • Pay inflated prices for goods or services.

Safety and Health

Safety in Badagry

Badagry is generally safe; however, it is advisable to keep your belongings close and be cautious when venturing out at night. Avoid taking drinks from strangers and be aware of pickpockets.

Health in Badagry

Malaria is a common ailment in Badagry, so it is essential to take preventative measures like mosquito nets, insect repellent and vaccines before you visit.

Getting to Badagry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Badagry

The closest airport to Badagry is the Murtala Muhammed International Airport in Lagos, which is about an hour’s drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Badagry

Major Bus stations in Lagos that connect to Badagry include CMS Bus Terminal and Mile 2 Bus Terminal. Buses and taxis are available for transportation.

Other Options

Taxis, Hire cars and Uber services are available within Lagos city that go as far as Badagry.

How to Get Around in Badagry

The best way to get around Badagry is by car or taxi. Public transportation is also available within the town, as well as organized tours.

Where to Go Next from Badagry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Badagry is located near several destinations worth a visit, including the Lagos Island, Lekki Conservation Center, and National Park.

Conclusion

Badagry offers a unique and memorable travel experience, given its rich cultural heritage and natural attractions. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect itinerary and immerse yourself in the beauty and history of this town.
