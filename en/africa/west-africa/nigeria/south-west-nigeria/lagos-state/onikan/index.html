Introduction to Onikan

Onikan is a vibrant suburb of Lagos State in South West Nigeria, known for its rich cultural heritage, famous museums, and historical landmarks. Located on the Atlantic Ocean and surrounded by lush greenery, Onikan attracts visitors from all over the world who want to experience the inviting atmosphere and witness its local historic sites.

In this travel guide, we will provide detailed information on the top things to do, see, and eat in Onikan, and all you need to know about the best time to visit, where to stay, how to get around, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Onikan

Onikan has something for everyone, including history buffs, culture enthusiasts, and nature lovers. Here is a list of places to visit in Onikan that are worth exploring:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Historical monuments and museums in Onikan boast insights into the country’s past and its role in the African continent. Some of the must-visit sites in Onikan include:

  • National Museum of Nigeria: Explore the country’s rich and diverse history and art at the National Museum. The museum features exhibit galleries with artifacts ranging from traditional Oduduwa sculptures to modern art from contemporary Nigerian artists.

  • Tinubu Square: This public square is dedicated to the famous political figure, Madam Efunroye Tinubu. Visitors can explore the nearby government buildings or take a stroll around the square, which also has a statue of the Tinubu herself.

Natural Attractions of Onikan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Onikan is the perfect place to experience nature and its wonders. The suburb has several outdoor attractions that offer scenic views and outdoor activities, including:

  • Onikan waterfront: Enjoy a leisurely boat ride or a scenic walk along the oceanfront. It’s a perfect place to watch the sunrise or sunset.

  • Freedom Park: This scenic park is a peaceful oasis in a busy city. It features stunning gardens, fountains, and artwork installations. Visitors can enjoy live music performances and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Onikan

Onikan is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with tons of scenic hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Some of the top routes in Onikan include:

  • Lekki Conservation Centre: This conservation area offers a 401-meter-long canopy walkway, bird-watching, and an abundance of wildlife. You can also take a leisurely walk through the forest, through the raised bridges and sandy paths.

  • Tarkwa Bay: Located in Apapa, Lagos, this beach is a popular spot for activities such as swimming, surfing, sunbathing, and more.

Best Things to Do in Onikan: Activities and Tours

Onikan is the perfect place for activities and tours that offer a glimpse into the region’s unique history and culture. Here are some of the top activities and tours in Onikan:

  • Lagos City Tour: This guided tour allows visitors to discover the highlights of Lagos, including the idyllic Tafawa Balewa Square, the markets, and the numerous cultural attractions of the suburb.

  • Lekki Art Market: The Lekki Art Market is the perfect place to experience the region’s unique crafts, arts, and textiles. Visitors can browse through the hand-carved masks, traditional clothing items, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Onikan

To get the most out of your visit to Onikan, we recommend staying for a week to explore the region’s unique culture and history. Here’s a suggested itinerary for seven days in Onikan:

Day 1: Arrive in Lagos and settle into accommodation.
Day 2: Take a Lagos City Tour and visit the National Museum of Nigeria
Day 3: Visit the Lekki Conservation Centre for a hike along the canopy walkway.
Day 4: Explore the Freedom Park Lake
Day 5: Take a walking tour of Tinubu Square and other historical landmarks
Day 6: Visit the Lekki Art Market
Day 7: Relax at the Onikan seashore, explore Tarkwa Bay and enjoy the sunset views.

Food and Drink Scene of Onikan

When it comes to food and drink, Onikan offers a wide range of options to suit all taste preferences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Onikan.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ofada rice and sauce: A traditional Nigerian delicacy made with local rice and a spicy pepper sauce.

  • Suya: A popular Nigerian street food made with skewered meat (chicken or beef) that’s rubbed with a special spice mix and grilled over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: An alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.

  • Zobo: Also known as Hibiscus tea, it’s a popular and refreshing drink made from Hibiscus flowers and sugar.

Where to Stay in Onikan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Onikan offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses located in the region include:

  • Federal Palace Hotel Lagos: A five-star hotel located in Victoria Island, offering guests stunning ocean views and comfortable accommodation.

  • Lagos Continental Hotel: A luxurious hotel in Victoria Island, offering guests world-class amenities and panoramic views of Lagos.

What to Buy in Onikan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Onikan has a fantastic shopping scene, offering a variety of local products and crafts that make perfect souvenirs or gifts. Some popular items to bring include:

  • Traditional Clothing: You can find traditional clothing items made by local artisans in Onikan.

  • Wooden masks and carvings: Wooden masks and carvings are beautifully made and serve as perfect decorative items for your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Onikan

Onikan’s culture is a unique blend of history, tradition, and modern influences. Visitors to Onikan are encouraged to respect local customs, including dressing modestly, and always asking for permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Onikan

As with most destinations in West Africa, the best time to visit Onikan is during the dry season, from November to February. This period offers pleasant weather conditions and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Onikan

Here are some important dos and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Onikan:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully while exploring the region.

  • Learn some basic Yoruba phrases and greet locals in their local language.

  • Explore the region’s cultural landmarks and museums.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of people without asking their permission

  • Avoid public displays of affection.

  • Don’t wander around the streets at night by yourself.

Safety and Health

Safety in Onikan

Onikan is a relatively safe area, but visitors should still take basic precautions, such as avoiding secluded and poorly lit areas, and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Onikan

It’s recommended to get vaccinated against yellow fever and to carry a first-aid kit when traveling. Also, visitors should stick to bottled water and avoid street food when possible.

Getting to Onikan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Onikan is easily accessible from other parts of Lagos State and neighboring cities. Here are some transportation options to consider when traveling to Onikan:

  • Air travel: The nearest airport to Onikan is the Lagos Murtala Muhammed International Airport.

  • Bus travel: Buses are the most common mode of travel within Lagos and across the country. Visitors can take a bus from the city center to Onikan.

  • Taxis: Taxis are available throughout Lagos and can be hailed easily from the street.

How to Get Around in Onikan

Getting around Onikan is relatively easy, thanks to its well-planned road networks and public transportation services. Visitors can use buses, taxis, and motorcycle taxis (okadas), or hire a car for more significant convenience.

Where to Go Next from Onikan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Onikan’s location makes it a perfect starting point to explore other exciting destinations in Lagos State and the surrounding regions. Some of the top destinations include:

  • Victoria Island: An upscale neighborhood known for its high-end restaurants, shopping malls, and stunning nightlife.

Conclusion

Onikan is an exciting destination offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This travel guide should help you plan your trip, with information on the top things to do, see, and eat in Onikan, where to stay, how to get there, and other important tips for your visit.
