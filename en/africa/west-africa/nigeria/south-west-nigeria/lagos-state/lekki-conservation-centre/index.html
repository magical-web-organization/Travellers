Introduction to Lekki Conservation Centre

Lekki Conservation Centre is a hidden gem in South West Nigeria, offering visitors a serene and tranquil escape from the bustling city of Lagos. The conservation centre spans over 78 hectares of lush green forest, home to a variety of exotic fauna and flora.

Founded in 1990 by the Nigerian Conservation Foundation (NCF), the Lekki Conservation Centre is dedicated to the preservation of wildlife and the environment. The centre features various nature trails, canopy walkways, bird hides, and viewing platforms, providing visitors with the opportunity to get up close and personal with nature.

Best Time to Visit Lekki Conservation Centre

The best time to visit Lekki Conservation Centre is during the dry season, between November and March. The weather is less humid and there’s less rainfall, making it easier to explore the outdoors. However, if you’re looking to experience the lush greenery of the forest, visit during the rainy season between April and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lekki Conservation Centre

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Lekki Conservation Centre:

Do:

  • Follow the instructions of your tour guide

  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Feed the wildlife
  • Use flash photography

Safety and Health

Safety in Lekki Conservation Centre

Overall, Lekki Conservation Centre is a safe and secure destination for tourists. However, it’s always recommended to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring the outdoors.

Health in Lekki Conservation Centre

When visiting Lekki Conservation Centre, it’s important to take precautions against malaria, as the region is prone to the disease. Make sure to use insect repellent, wear protective clothing, and take antimalarial medication if recommended by your doctor.
