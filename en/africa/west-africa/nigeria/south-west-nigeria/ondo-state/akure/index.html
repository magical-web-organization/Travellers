Introduction to Akure

Akure is a serene and bustling town located in the south-western part of Nigeria, in the Ondo state. Akure is surrounded by mountains and rolling hills which make it a perfect destination for adventurers, nature lovers, and sightseers. The town is rich in culture and diversity, and visitors can explore its historical sites, natural attractions, and local food scene.

List of Places to Visit in Akure

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Owo Museum of Antiquities

The Owo Museum of Antiquities contains a diverse collection of bronze, ivory, and wooden artifacts from the ancient Owo kingdom. The museum provides a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Yoruba people.

Idanre Hills

The Idanre Hills are a group of ancient and mystical hills located about 24 kilometers southwest of Akure. The hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and a glimpse of the ancient Yoruba culture.

Natural Attractions of Akure: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Owo Forest Reserve

The Owo Forest Reserve is a protected area that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the forest on foot or on bike trails, camp in its tranquil surroundings, and discover the diverse range of wildlife.

Owena River

The Owena River is a spectacular natural attraction in Akure. Visitors can go for boat rides or fishing, admire the scenic views along the river, and explore the nearby waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akure

Akure Trail

The Akure Trail is a 6-kilometer trail that takes visitors through the town’s stunning scenery, including mountain ranges, rolling hills, and a diverse range of wildlife. The trail is perfect for hikers and bikers who want to get an authentic African outdoor experience.

Best Things to Do in Akure: Activities and Tours

Araromi Mangrove Tour

The Araromi Mangrove Tour is an eco-tourism experience that takes visitors through the stunning mangrove forests along the coast of Akure. The tour provides an opportunity to see and learn about the unique ecosystem and the wildlife that calls it home.

Cultural Dance and Music Escape

The Cultural Dance and Music Escape is a showcase of the rich cultural heritage of the Yoruba people. It features traditional dance, music, and cultural performances that provide a glimpse into the unique culture of Akure.

Food and Drink Scene of Akure

Must-Try Dishes

Amala and Ewedu Soup

This traditional Yoruba dish consists of mashed yam flour served with a slimy soup made from the jute leaf. It is a highly nutritious and satisfying meal.

Ondo Banga Soup

Ondo Banga Soup is a stew made from palm kernels and assorted meat. It is a highly nutritious and flavorful dish that showcases the unique culinary heritage of the Ondo people.

Local Beverages

Palm Wine

Palm wine is an alcoholic beverage made from the fermented sap of palm trees. It is a popular drink in Akure and is served fresh and tasty.

Zobo

Zobo is a non-alcoholic drink made from hibiscus flowers, ginger root, and other local ingredients. It is a refreshing and healthy option for those who want to try something new.

Where to Stay in Akure: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Royal Birds Hotel

The Royal Birds Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Akure. It provides guests with comfortable rooms, modern amenities, and excellent services.

Ritz-Carlton Guesthouse

The Ritz-Carlton Guesthouse is a cozy and affordable guesthouse located in a quiet neighborhood in Akure. It provides guests with clean and comfortable rooms, excellent services, and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Akure: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Adire Textiles

Adire Textiles are traditional Yoruba fabrics that are hand-dyed using a resist-dyeing technique. Visitors can buy unique and colorful fabrics for souvenirs or gifts.

Akure Beads

Akure Beads are traditional Yoruba beads that showcase the local craftsmanship and artistry. Visitors can buy unique and intricately designed beads as a perfect gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Akure

The people of Akure are known for their hospitality, warmth, and openness. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs, dress modestly, and avoid sensitive subjects such as politics and religion.

Best Time to Visit Akure

The best time to visit Akure is during the dry season, from December to March. The weather is warm and sunny, and visitors can explore the town’s attractions without the hassle of rain or heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akure

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the town’s natural attractions and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Engage in political or religious discussions.
  • Offend the locals by taking photographs without permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Ignore the safety precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akure

Akure is a relatively safe town, and visitors are unlikely to experience any security issues. However, visitors are advised to stay vigilant, avoid isolated areas, and take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows and keeping valuable items out of sight.

Health in Akure

Visitors are advised to take suitable health precautions before visiting Akure, such as getting vaccinations and taking anti-malaria medication. Visitors should also be cautious about food and water hygiene and avoid street food stalls and unsafe drinking water.

Getting to Akure: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akure

The Akure airport is the closest airport to the town, with daily flights from Lagos and other major Nigerian cities.

Bus Stations Near Akure

Visitors can take a bus from Lagos, Abuja, or other major Nigerian cities to Akure. The bus journey takes about six hours and is a relatively comfortable and affordable option.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi from Lagos or other Nigerian cities to Akure. It is a more expensive but more convenient option for those who want to travel in comfort and style.

How to Get Around in Akure

Visitors can explore Akure by foot, bike, or taxis. Taxis are abundant and relatively cheap, and visitors can hire a taxi for a few hours or a full day. Bikes and motorbikes are also available for hire, and are a popular option for visitors who want to explore the town’s natural attractions and scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Akure: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oshogbo

Oshogbo is a historic and cultural town located about 100 kilometers from Akure. It is home to the Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and other cultural and natural attractions.

Ondo City

Ondo City is a picturesque and historic town located about 45 kilometers from Akure. It is home to the Ondo State University and other cultural and natural attractions, including the Olukare’s Palace and the Ondo Forest Reserve.

Conclusion

Akure is a fascinating and diverse town that has something to offer for everyone. Whether visitors want to explore the town’s natural attractions, cultural sites, or local food scene, Akure has something for everyone. Visitors can experience the town’s unique culture and customs, meet friendly and hospitable locals, and enjoy an authentic African adventure.
