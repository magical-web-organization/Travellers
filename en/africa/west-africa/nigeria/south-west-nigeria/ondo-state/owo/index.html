Introduction to Owo

Owo is a culturally and historically rich town located in Ondo State, South West Nigeria. The town is home to many fascinating attractions that showcase its rich heritage and natural beauty. Owo is known for its impressive museums, historical sites, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and hospitality.

In this comprehensive travel guide for Owo, we will take you on a journey to the best places to visit, top things to do, local culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Owo

Whether you are a first-time visitor or a frequent traveler, Owo has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Owo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Owo is home to many historically significant places that showcase its rich culture and heritage. Some of the top museums and historical places to visit include:

  • Palace of Olowo: It is one of the oldest landmarks in Owo, and it is the official residence of the traditional ruler of Owo Kingdom. The palace’s architecture dates back to the 15th century, and it is an impressive sight to behold.

  • Owo Museum of Antiquities: This museum houses collections of traditional artifacts and crafts that showcase the town’s history and culture. It is an excellent place to learn about the Yoruba traditions and customs.

  • Igbo Olodumare: This mysterious and historical site is believed to be a sacred place where the creation took place. It is a must-visit place for history enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Owo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views, Owo is a perfect destination. The town is blessed with pristine forests, waterfalls, and rocky terrains. Here are some of the best natural attractions of Owo:

  • Owo Forest Reserve: This lush forest reserve provides excellent opportunities for hiking, bird watching, and wildlife viewing. It is home to many species of birds, monkeys, antelopes, and other animals.

  • Igbodigo Forest: This lovely forest is located about 20 km from Owo town. It is an excellent place to experience nature, walk, and hike while taking in the beauty of the forest.

  • Omi-Ekun Waterfalls: This waterfall is one of the most beautiful spots in Owo. It is located about 30 km from the town and is known for its scenic beauty and tranquility.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Owo

Hiking and biking are two enjoyable outdoor activities in Owo. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes of Owo:

  • Owo Forest Reserve Hiking Trail: This trail stretches through the forest reserve, providing hikers with an opportunity to connect with nature and take in the beauty of the forest.

  • Cycling in Owo Town: The town’s streets provide an ideal atmosphere for biking enthusiasts. The town’s quiet and peaceful streets make it a perfect place to enjoy a leisurely ride.

Best Things to Do in Owo: Activities and Tours

Owo offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Owo:

  • Visit the Owo Museum of Antiquities: A guided tour of the museum is an excellent way to learn about the town’s history and culture.

  • Explore the Forest Reserve: An adventurous hike or bird watching excursion in the Owo Forest Reserve is a must-do activity.

  • Attend a Cultural Festival: Owo is known for its vibrant cultural festivals, such as the Igogo Festival, which is held annually in the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Owo

Here is a week-long itinerary for Owo:

Day 1: Palace of Olowo, Owo Museum of Antiquities

Day 2: Igbodigo Forest, Cycling around Owo Town

Day 3: Omi-Ekun Waterfalls, Bird Watching

Day 4: Igbo Olodumare, Olowo’s Palace

Day 5: Owo Forest Reserve, Wildlife Viewing

Day 6: Traditional Market, Cultural Tours

Day 7: Attend a Cultural Festival

Food and Drink Scene of Owo

Owo is home to many mouth-watering dishes and refreshing drinks that will leave a lasting impression on visitors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iyan and Egusi Soup: A traditional meal made from pounded yam and melon soup, a staple in many Yoruba households.

  • Amala and Ewedu Soup: Another signature dish of Owo, which consists of a dough made from yam flour and served with blended leaf soup.

  • Owo Pepper soup: This is a spicy soup made with fish or goat meant to warm you up on a chilly night.

Local Beverages

  • Ogi: A traditional beverage made from corn or rice and served with groundnut or milk.

  • Palm Wine: This is an alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It is a popular local drink in Owo and is very refreshing.

Local Culture and Customs of Owo

The culture and customs of Owo are steeped in tradition and heritage. Visitors to Owo should take the time to learn about the town’s history, people, and traditions. Music, dance, and festivals are an integral part of the town’s culture, and visitors are welcome to join in the celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Owo

The best time to visit Owo is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this time, the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Owo

Visitors to Owo should observe the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Be polite and friendly to the locals

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission

  • Disrespect the traditional ruler or elders

  • Engage in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Owo

Owo is a safe town to visit, but visitors should still exercise caution and follow safety precautions. Avoid carrying large amounts of money or valuables, and be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Owo

Visitors to Owo should take the necessary health precautions, such as wearing insect repellent, to prevent malaria and other illnesses.

Getting to Owo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Owo town is accessible by road and air. Here are some options for getting to Owo:

  • Air: The nearest airport to Owo is Akure Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from the town.

  • Bus: Owo is well-connected by road, with several bus companies providing transportation to and from the town.

How to Get Around in Owo

Getting around Owo is relatively easy, with many transportation options available, such as buses, taxis, and motorcycles.

Where to Go Next from Owo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Owo is a fascinating destination with a unique culture, rich history, and many natural attractions. This guide will hopefully help you plan your trip to Owo and experience all the town has to offer. Whether you are an adventure seeker, culture enthusiast, or just looking to relax and unwind, Owo has something for everyone.
