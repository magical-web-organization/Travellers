If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Nigeria, look no further than Ikare. Located in Ondo State, in South West Nigeria, Ikare is a hidden gem that’s sure to delight any traveler. With its rich cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, and breathtaking natural beauty, Ikare is a destination that’s not to be missed. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, hiking trails, best things to do, where to stay, local food scene, and more.

Introduction to Ikare

Ikare is a small town in Ondo State, Nigeria. It has a rich heritage and culture that’s shaped by its people and their traditions. The town is surrounded by lush forests and hills, providing stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors to Ikare will be amazed by the warm hospitality of the locals, who are always ready to introduce travelers to their way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Ikare

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arinta Waterfalls: This natural wonder is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Ikare. The waterfall is located in a serene environment, surrounded by lush vegetation and rocky landscapes. It provides a great spot for picnicking, swimming, and recreation.

  • Owa Waterfalls: Another must-see destination in Ikare is Owa Waterfalls. The waterfall is located in a picturesque setting and is known for its crystal clear waters, which originate from the Okitipupa hills. It’s perfect for sightseeing, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Ile-Ife Museum of Antiquities: This museum is dedicated to preserving the cultural heritage of the Yoruba people. It houses a collection of artifacts ranging from ancient pottery, bronze sculptures, and ritual objects, among others.

Natural Attractions of Ikare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Idanre Hills: This range of hills boasts an impressive landscape, natural caves, and waterfalls. Visitors can explore the rugged terrain while enjoying the stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Owena Dam: The Owena Dam is an artificial dam that provides water to communities around Ikare. It also provides a great spot for sightseeing and recreation, with ample opportunities for fishing, bird watching, and nature walks.

  • Oluwa River: The Oluwa River is another natural attraction in Ikare. It’s a great spot for fishing, swimming, or just enjoying the serene environment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ikare

  • Agbele Hill Hiking Trail: This hiking trail provides stunning views of the surrounding forests, hills, and streams. It offers a moderate challenge, making it perfect for hikers of all skill levels.

  • Igba-Owa Trail: This trail leads hikers through the rugged terrain of Owena Dam and provides breathtaking views of the dam and its surroundings.

  • The Road to Arinta Waterfalls: This route leads visitors to one of the most scenic spots in Ikare, the Arinta Waterfalls. The hike is relatively easy and provides a great way to experience the natural beauty of Ikare.

Best Things to Do in Ikare: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the local market and try some of the local delicacies.

  • Take a guided tour of the cultural attractions and landmarks.

  • Visit the local art gallery and meet the local artists.

  • Experience the local nightlife and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ikare

Day 1: Visit Owa Waterfalls in the morning, followed by a tour of the Ile-Ife Museum of Antiquities in the afternoon.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking Agbele Hill Trail.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the local market and try some of the local delicacies.

Day 4: Go on an adventure in Owena Dam and its surrounding areas.

Day 5: Visit the Arinta Waterfalls and its surroundings.

Day 6: Take a road trip to Idanre Hills and explore the natural caves and waterfalls.

Day 7: Visit the local art gallery and meet the local artists.

Food and Drink Scene of Ikare

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pounded yam and egusi soup

  • Efo riro (vegetable soup)

  • Amala and ewedu (yam flour and jute soup)

Local Beverages

  • Palm wine

  • Zobo drink (hibiscus flower drink)

  • Kunu drink (a fermented drink made from corn, millet, or sorghum)

Where to Stay in Ikare: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sifton Guest House: A comfortable and affordable guest house located in the heart of Ikare.

  • Owena Motel: A budget-friendly motel that provides basic amenities for travelers.

  • Grandville Guest House: A luxury guest house that provides top-notch amenities and services.

What to Buy in Ikare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yoruba textiles and crafts

  • Local musical instruments

  • Beads and jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Ikare

The people of Ikare are predominantly Yoruba, and they have a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in their traditions, customs, and beliefs. Visitors are advised to respect the culture and customs of the locals and to dress modestly to avoid causing offense.

Best Time to Visit Ikare

The best time to visit Ikare is between November and February, during the dry season. The weather is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ikare

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.

  • Disrespect the local culture.

  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ikare

While Ikare is generally a safe destination, travelers are advised to take the usual precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night, keep valuables safe, and be vigilant of your surroundings.

Health in Ikare

Travelers are advised to take precautions against malaria, which is common in the region. Use mosquito nets, wear long-sleeved clothing, and use mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Ikare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ikare

The closest airport to Ikare is the Akure Airport, which is about 1.5 hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Ikare

There are several bus stations in Ikare, including the Owena Bus Station and the Idanre Bus Station. Travelers can take buses from Lagos or Akure to these stations.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a car or taxi to get to Ikare from Akure or Lagos.

How to Get Around in Ikare

Travelers can get around Ikare on foot or by taxi. Taxis are readily available and affordable, making it easy to explore the town and its surrounding areas.

Conclusion

Ikare is a unique and fascinating destination that’s sure to delight any traveler. With its rich culture, outdoor adventures, and natural beauty, Ikare is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Ikare and experience all the wonder it has to offer.
