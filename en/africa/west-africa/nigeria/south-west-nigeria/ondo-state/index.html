If you’re looking for a destination that offers a mix of cultural diversity and natural beauty, Ondo State in South West Nigeria is the place to be. The region is home to a wealth of attractions, including museums, historical sites, natural wonders, hiking and biking trails, and vibrant markets. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best things to do, see and experience on your trip to Ondo State.

Introduction to Ondo State

Ondo State is located in the south-western part of Nigeria and has been historically known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful landscapes, and hospitable people. The state is a melting pot of various ethnic groups, including the Yoruba, Ikale, Ijaw, and Ondo. Its capital, Akure, is the largest city and commercial hub of the state, with a bustling downtown area and a lively market.

List of Places to Visit in Ondo State

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the rich cultural heritage of Ondo State, head to one of the many museums and historical sites in the area. Some of the most popular attractions include:

  • Owo Museum of Antiques: This museum houses a collection of artefacts and antiquities from the ancient Owo Kingdom. It is one of the best places to learn about the Yoruba art and culture.

  • Idanre Hills: This is an iconic UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Idanre town. It features a cluster of hills with peculiar flora and fauna, scenic valleys, and impressive ancient architecture.

  • Oba’s Palace, Akure: This is the traditional residence of the Oba of Akure, and it’s an impressive structure with a long history. Visitors can tour the palace and learn about local customs and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Ondo State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whether you’re a nature lover or an avid adventurer, Ondo State has plenty of outdoor activities to offer. The state is blessed with diverse topography, from lush rainforests to beaches. Below are some excellent destinations to experience the natural beauty of the area:

  • Arinta Waterfall, Ikare-Akoko: This natural wonder is a hidden gem located in the deep forests of Ikare-Akoko. It’s a perfect spot for hiking, bird watching, and photoshoots.

  • Ebomi Lake, Arogbo: An idyllic lake-fringed by thick green vegetation and home to a wide variety of aquatic life. Visitors can enjoy fishing, swimming, and canoeing.

  • Igbo-Olodumare Forest Reserve: Situated on the outskirts of Ondo City, this reserve is an excellent place to explore lush forests, enjoy bird watching and encounter a variety of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ondo State

Ondo State boasts an extensive network of trails that are suitable for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers. Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Oke Maria Trail: Located in Akure, this trail is one of the best places to explore the dense forests, secluded waterfalls and picturesque creeks in the region.

  • Aribisco Trail, Oniparaga: Explore the Aribisco Forest Reserve with this scenic trail. Highlights include views of the ocean and lush greenery.

  • Idanre Hill Trail: This well-maintained journey starts from the base of the hills and offers hikers an opportunity to explore the historic settlements of the Idanre people.

Best Things to Do in Ondo State: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to outdoor activities, there is no shortage of things to do in Ondo State. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit the Oluwa Glass Palace: This a glass-making village located in Ondo city. Tourists can watch skilled workers craft beautiful objects and sculptures from molten glass.

  • Attend the Ogun Festival: Held every year in Idanre during August, this festival features cultural dances, music, and performances.

  • Visit the Owo Rock Art site: This is a historic site that features ancient rock engravings and carvings that date back several centuries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ondo State

Here’s an itinerary that covers the best of Ondo State in a week:

Day 1: Arrival in Akure, visit Oba’s Palace, and explore the city. Day 2: Visit the Owo Museum of Antiques and experience the rich cultural history of Ondo State. Day 3: Explore the Idanre Hills, hike the trails, and take in the scenic views. Day 4: Visit the Arinta Waterfall and Ebomi Lake for nature activities. Day 5: Visit Oluwa Glass Palace and indulge in shopping. Day 6: Visit the Ogun Festival in Idanre. Day 7: Depart for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Ondo State

Must-Try Dishes

Ondo State is home to many delicious and mouth-watering delicacies that you must try. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Egunsi soup: This healthy, spicy soup is made of Egusi or melon seeds, vegetables, meat or fish.

  • Ikale Banga Soup: One of the well-known and popular soups in Ondo State made with Banga fruit, popular in many West African cuisines.

  • Obe-Eja: Made of fish, tomatoes, onions, palm oil, and pepper, this soup is the most popular soup in the state.

Local Beverages

Ondo state is famous for its palm tree plantations, which provide the essential ingredient for many local drinks. Try these beverages on your visit:

  • Palm Wine: A popular, locally brewed alcoholic drink made from fermented or distilled palm tree sap.

  • Zobo Drink: A popular drink in many parts of West Africa, zobo is made from a local hibiscus flower that is brewed with spices like ginger and cloves.

Where to Stay in Ondo State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ondo State has numerous hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Sunview Hotel, Owo: Boasting excellent facilities and services, this hotel is a great option in Owo.

  • Viss Luxury Suites, Akure: A modern hotel with 24-hour room services, gym and swimming pool.

  • Bash Hotel and Restaurant, Ondo: An affordable and elegant option with sleek interiors and scenic views.

What to Buy in Ondo State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted souvenirs to local textiles, there are plenty of items to take home from Ondo State. Here are some ideas:

  • Baskets made from locally-sourced materials (palm, bamboo, or rattan).

  • African print textiles (Ankara) and clothes made in vibrant patterns.

  • Locally made woodcarving (Masks, figurines, and sculptures).

Local Culture and Customs of Ondo State

Ondo State is known for its rich cultural traditions, festivals, and rituals. Respect for elders and community values is essential in the state, which is evident in daily life. Also, body art (tattoos, scarf markings) is prevalent in the area and symbolizes cultural and traditional practices.

Best Time to Visit Ondo State

The best time to visit Ondo State is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is cooler, and road conditions are better during this time. However, the region is beautiful all year round, and it’s possible to visit during the wet season too.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ondo State

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, particularly when visiting religious sites and cultural areas.

  • Greet elders with a handshake or a nod of respect.

  • Try to learn and speak a few words of local dialects.

Don’t:

  • Do not take pictures of people without their consent, particularly women and children.

  • Avoid eating in public places during Ramadan.

  • Do not litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ondo State

Ondo State is generally safe, but as with anywhere in the world, there are risks associated with traveling. Here are some tips to stay safe:

  • Stick to populated and well-lit areas, particularly at night.

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables in public.

  • Use licensed taxis or private cars when moving around the state.

Health in Ondo State

Visitors are advised to take appropriate medical precautions before visiting Ondo State. Here are some tips to ensure health and safety on your trip:

  • Make sure to get necessary shots and vaccinations before arrival.

  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid waterborne illnesses.

  • Seek medical attention if you fall ill while traveling.

Getting to Ondo State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ondo State

Akure Airport is the primary airport serving Ondo State, with regular domestic flights to and from Lagos, Abuja, and other major cities in Nigeria.

Bus Stations Near Ondo State

The major bus terminals in the state are located in Akure, Owo, and Ondo. You can take a wide range of bus brands such as ABC, GIGM, and Agofure to get to the state from other parts of Nigeria.

Other Options

There is no train service to Ondo State at present, but visitors can use private cars or hire taxis to get around the state. Alternatively, one can get a ride through ride-sharing apps like Bolt and Uber.

How to Get Around in Ondo State

Minibusses, taxis, motorbikes, and buses are the most common means of transportation within the state. Bike rental services are also available for travelers who prefer cycling around.

Where to Go Next from Ondo State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ondo state is in a great location for exploring the surrounding area, from bustling cities to serene national parks. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Lagos: Nigeria’s largest city is just a few hours away by car, and its attractions include museums, beaches, restaurants, and markets.

  • Yankari National Park: Located in Bauchi State, it’s one of the most visited national parks in Nigeria with a variety of wildlife and natural resources.

Conclusion

Ondo State is a great travel destination with unique cultural history, natural wonders, and stunning locales. It offers a mix of outdoor tours, cultural experiences, and delicious food options. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has been useful in planning your trip to Ondo State, Nigeria. Happy travels!
