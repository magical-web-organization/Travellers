Are you ready to explore one of the hidden treasures of Nigeria? Old Oyo National Park is a must-visit destination for those seeking outdoor adventure and cultural experiences. Found in Oyo State, South West Nigeria, the park boasts of museums, historical places, scenic views, hiking routes, and many more. This comprehensive guide will help you plan a successful tour of Old Oyo National Park.

Introduction to Old Oyo National Park

Founded in 1952, Old Oyo National Park covers an area of 2,512 square kilometers. It is home to a diverse ecosystem comprising savannah woodland, gallery forest, and patches of riparian forests. The Owu, Oyo, and Ogun rivers cut through the park, providing a beautiful landscape.

Apart from its distinctive plant and animal life, Old Oyo National Park is also rich in cultural history. The park’s history dates back to the Oyo Empire’s reign in the 14th century, making it a culturally significant destination.

List of Places to Visit in Old Oyo National Park

There are many things to see and do in Old Oyo National Park. Here is a list of some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Koso Hill - visit this place to see the remains of the old Oyo Empire.
  • Old Oyo National Museum - houses artifacts and historical collections from Oyo Empire.
  • Tilapia Island - a rock island that serves as a natural reserve for diverse species of flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Old Oyo National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Igboho Waterfalls - the waterfalls provide a serene atmosphere for picnics and outdoor activities.
  • Oyo River - explore the river while enjoying the lush vegetation along its banks.
  • Wildlife spotting - spot wildlife including baboons, hippos, and buffalos on Safaris and drives.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Oyo National Park

For those seeking out intense outdoor adventures, Old Oyo National Park offers the opportunity to hike, bike or trek. Here are some hiking/biking routes:

  • Igboho Waterfalls trek - offers stunning views of the waterfalls and the surrounding vegetation.
  • Odo-oba trail - offers an excellent opportunity for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Old Oyo National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many things to do in the park. Here are some of the top activities:

  • Safari - take a day trip to explore the park and spot some of its wild inhabitants.
  • Photography - the park offers a stunning landscape to take some amazing shots.
  • Boat ride - rent a boat and explore the rivers that run through the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old Oyo National Park

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a hotel

Day 2: Visit the Old Oyo National Museum and explore the Koso Hill

Day 3: Take a hike to Igboho waterfalls and have a picnic

Day 4: Go on a boat ride down the Oyo River

Day 6: Go on a safari or wildlife drive

Food and Drink Scene of Old Oyo National Park

The local food and drink scene are an essential aspect of any travel experience. Here is a list of the food and drinks to try in Old Oyo National Park:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pounded Yam - a Nigerian dish made from yam flour, usually served with palm oil soup.
  • Fried rice - a dish made from rice, vegetables, and spices.
  • Ewedu and Amala - another Nigerian dish made by combining Amala flour with Ewedu soup.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo - This drink made from hibiscus flowers and spices is a Nigerian favorite.
  • Palm wine - a traditional alcoholic drink made from the sap of the palm tree.
  • Sobo - a refreshing drink usually made from ginger and pineapple.

Where to Stay in Old Oyo National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options to choose for accommodation, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious lodges. Some of the top options include:

  • Celemoor Hotel - located close to the park, this hotel offers comfortable accommodation and excellent service.
  • Oyo State Hotel - located in the town of Oyo, this hotel is perfect for those on a budget.
  • Bovina Hotel - located in the Gbongan area, this hotel is perfect for travelers looking for a luxurious stay.

What to Buy in Old Oyo National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Old Oyo National Park by purchasing local souvenirs and products such as:

  • Traditional Cloth - handwoven by locals, these cloth pieces feature colourful designs and patterns.
  • Beaded Artworks - beaded artworks handmade by the locals.
  • Headrests - made from wood, these headrests are a unique and practical souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Old Oyo National Park

Old Oyo National Park is rich in cultural history, and it’s advisable to respect local cultures and customs. Here are a few tips:

  • Dress sensibly and modestly.
  • Ask for permission before taking someone’s photo.
  • Be respectful of the local’s religious beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Old Oyo National Park

The best time to visit Old Oyo National Park is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is mild, and it’s the perfect time for wildlife spotting and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Oyo National Park

Do:

  • Carry sunblock, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Carry a camera to capture beautiful moments and memories.
  • Learn and respect the park rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife or attempt to touch them.
  • Litter or leave any trash behind.
  • Stray off the designated hiking or biking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Oyo National Park

As with any trip, safety is essential, and it’s advisable to take the necessary safety precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Ensure that you have appropriate insurance cover.
  • Avoid walking around at night.
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure.

Health in Old Oyo National Park

It’s essential to take health precautions, as the park is located in a region with possible health risks, such as malaria. Here are some health safety tips:

  • Consult with medical professionals before taking the trip.
  • Wear protective clothing and use mosquito repellent.
  • Carry a first aid kit.

It’s easy to get to Old Oyo National Park, and there are different transportation options available. Here are some of the most common ways to get there:

Bus Stations Near Old Oyo National Park

Other Options

How to Get Around in Old Oyo National Park

To explore the park, it’s advisable to hire a car or take a guided tour. These options provide flexibility and enable visitors to cover more ground and see more of the park’s treasures.

Where to Go Next from Old Oyo National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, consider exploring other nearby attractions such as:

  • Oke-Ado Mountain - a mountain range that provides a magnificent view of Ibadan city.
  • Ibadan Cultural Center - home to artifacts, musical instruments, and masks used in traditional Yoruba festivals.
