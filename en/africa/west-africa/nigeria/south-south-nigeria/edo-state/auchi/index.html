Introduction to Auchi

Auchi, located in Edo State, South South Nigeria, is a vibrant and culturally rich city that offers visitors a unique experience. Known for its skilled artisans and thriving market places, the city is a hub for trade and commerce in the region. Auchi is also famous for its world-class polytechnic, the Federal Polytechnic, Auchi, which attracts students from across the country.

Visitors to Auchi can take in the rich culture and history of the city by visiting its museums and historical sites, or take advantage of the natural beauty by exploring the hiking and biking routes or enjoying outdoor activities. The city also offers a variety of accommodation options, from hotels to hostels and guesthouses, making it easy for travelers to plan their visit.

List of Places to Visit in Auchi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Auchi Palace Museum: This is the former palace of the Otaru of Auchi, which was converted into a museum that showcases the history and culture of the people of Auchi. Visitors can see the regalia of the Otaru, traditional musical instruments, and other artifacts.
  • Ibiebele Hill: This landmark is where the founder of Auchi, Momodu, is believed to have been buried. The hill offers visitors a panoramic view of the city and surrounding landscape.
  • The Auchi Culture Centre: This centre hosts cultural events and performances, including dance and theatre shows, art exhibitions, and craft fairs.

Natural Attractions of Auchi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Auchi Rock Formations: The rock formations in Auchi are a popular attraction for visitors who want to take in the scenic beauty of the region. The formations have been natural attractions for ages and are a great place for photography and exploration.
  • Ogba Hills: Located on the outskirts of Auchi, these hills offer a natural escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can hike, bike or just take in the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auchi

Auchi offers a great network of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Auchi-Benin Road Trail: This trail offers a scenic journey between Auchi and Benin City, taking visitors through traditional villages and along the breathtaking Imane-Ise River.
  • Auchi Polytechnic Trail: This trail is located within the campus of the Federal Polytechnic Auchi and offers a glimpse into the natural environment of the region.

Best Things to Do in Auchi: Activities and Tours

  • Craft Village: Located in the heart of Auchi city, the craft village is a must-visit destination for art enthusiasts. Visitors can witness artisans creating intricate works of art like bronze castings and textile weaving.
  • Dongo Hill Tour: Dongo Hill is a historical site where one of the early settlers in Auchi lived. The hill is surrounded by scenic views and is perfect for nature walks.
  • Ojirami Dam: For those who love fishing, this dam is a perfect spot to visit. Visitors can also enjoy boat rides and picnics around the dam.

Food and Drink Scene of Auchi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Owo Soup: this soup is made with palm oil and is popular among the Esan tribe of Nigeria. It is usually eaten with fufu or eba.
  • Banga Soup: This soup is made with palm fruit extract, meat or fish, and spices. It is served with starch or eba and is often enjoyed at weddings or other special events.

Local Beverages

  • Pito: This is a local beer made from fermented sorghum or millet. It is often served at festivals and other cultural events.
  • Zobo: This refreshing drink is made from hibiscus flowers and is often accompanied with spicy pepper and ginger. It is a popular beverage in Nigeria.

Where to Stay in Auchi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auchi offers a range of accommodation options to cater to different budgets.

  • Charvet Hotel: This is a luxurious hotel offering room service, free Wi-Fi, and a restaurant.
  • Coral Executive: This is a budget-friendly hotel with rooms featuring air conditioning, free Wi-Fi, and a restaurant.
  • Rainbow Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with air conditioning, free Wi-Fi, and a 24-hour reception service.

What to Buy in Auchi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Auchi is famous for its skilled artisans who create beautiful works of art. Visitors can take home souvenirs such as:

  • Brass Works: These are brass works made in the ancient lost-wax art form.
  • Textiles: Visitors can buy beautiful textiles, including hand-woven fabrics, tie-dye, and batik works.

Local Culture and Customs of Auchi

Auchi is a city with a rich cultural heritage. The people of Auchi celebrate different festivals, including the New Yam Festival, which marks the beginning of the harvesting season. Visitors should respect the cultural and religious practices of the inhabitants, as well as their dress codes in public places.

Best Time to Visit Auchi

The best time to visit Auchi is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auchi

Do:

  • Respect the customs and cultural practices of the inhabitants.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting public places.
  • Try out the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their consent.
  • Wander around late at night in unfamiliar places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Auchi

Auchi is generally safe, but visitors should take the necessary precautions.

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash around.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation.
  • Use a registered taxi service.

Health in Auchi

Visitors should take standard health precautions such as getting vaccinated against yellow fever and typhoid fever, and drinking bottled water.

Getting to Auchi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Auchi

The nearest airport to Auchi is the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu, which is about 190km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Auchi

Visitors can take buses from the Lagos or Abuja bus parks to Auchi.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Auchi by private car, or take a taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Auchi

Visitors can get around Auchi on foot or by using private taxis, which are affordable and easily accessible.

Where to Go Next from Auchi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Benin City: Visitors can explore the rich cultural heritage of this ancient city and visit wonderful sites like the Benin Bronzes, the Oba’s Palace, or the National Museum.
  • Okomu National Forest Reserve: This reserve is home to over 200 species of birds and hundreds of other animals such as elephants, buffaloes, and antelopes. It’s an ideal destination for nature lovers.

Conclusion

Auchi is a city worth exploring for anyone interested in the vibrant culture and history of Nigeria. With its rich heritage, museums, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and accommodation options, there are plenty of reasons to visit Auchi. Visitors will be able to experience the warmth of Nigerian hospitality and take home unforgettable memories.
