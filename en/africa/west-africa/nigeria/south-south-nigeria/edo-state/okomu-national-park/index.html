Introduction to Okomu National Park

Located in Edo State, in South South Nigeria, Okomu National Park is a stunning natural reserve offering unique experiences to travelers from around the world. The park stretches over 181 square kilometers, housing a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several endangered species.

Okomu National Park was established in 1935 and has since become a vital conservation and research center. The park was named after the Okomu Forest Reserve, which was created in 1917. The park’s dense rainforest, savannahs, and rivers create an excellent habitat for different wildlife species.

List of Places to Visit in Okomu National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Here are some culturally important attractions in Okomu National Park that you should visit to learn more about the region’s history and culture:

  • Okomu Museum: Here, you will find several exhibits showcasing the culture and traditions of the Edo people.
  • Iyere Rock Shrine: This is a sacred site that contains many spiritual artifacts and is an essential part of the Edo people’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Okomu National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Okomu National Park is full of natural attractions. Here are some of the most popular outdoor adventures and scenic views you should not miss:

  • Canopy Walkway: This is a 40-meter high walkway that offers an amazing view of the forest canopy and the surrounding landscape.
  • Okomu River: The Okomu River is a great place to enjoy water-based activities such as fishing and canoeing.
  • Ogbodo Bridge: This is a suspension bridge that spans over the Ogbodo River, offering visitors scenic views of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okomu National Park

The park has several hiking and biking routes, and there are many options available for adventure seekers. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Gbaga Waterfall Trek: This trek takes visitors to the breathtaking Gbaga waterfall.
  • Rufus Creek Trek: This trek takes visitors to the Rufus Creek, which is full of crystal clear water and lush green foliage.
  • Okomu Circular Trail: This trek takes visitors through the park’s different landscapes, offering scenic views and an opportunity to spot different wildlife species.

Best Things to Do in Okomu National Park: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours that you can enjoy in Okomu National Park:

  • Bird Watching: Okomu National Park is home to over 150 bird species, making it an excellent destination for bird watching enthusiasts.
  • Wildlife Safaris: Visitors can take wildlife safari tours to explore the park’s diverse range of wildlife species and their habitats.
  • Night Walks: Visitors who love the outdoors can take night walks to experience the park’s nocturnal wildlife species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Okomu National Park

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Okomu National Park:

Day 1: Visit the Okomu Museum and explore the park’s culture and history.

Day 2: Take the Rufus Creek Trek and enjoy the stunning views of the Rufus Creek.

Day 3: Go on a wildlife safari tour and spot different wildlife species, such as elephants, monkeys, and antelopes.

Day 4: Enjoy water-based activities such as canoeing and fishing in the Okomu River.

Day 5: Take the Okomu Circular Trail and experience the park’s different landscapes, wildlife, and vegetation.

Day 6: Visit the Iyere Rock Shrine and learn more about the Edo people’s spiritual practices.

Day 7: Take a night walk tour to experience the park’s nocturnal wildlife species.

Food and Drink Scene of Okomu National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the must-try dishes in Okomu National Park:

  • Owo Soup: This local soup is made with smoked fish and vegetables and is a delicacy among the Edo people.
  • Bitterleaf Soup: This soup is made with bitter leaf, fish, and other ingredients.

Local Beverages

Here are some of the local beverages that you should try in Okomu National Park:

  • Palm Wine: This wine is made from the sap of the palm tree and is a popular beverage in the region.
  • Zobo: This is a popular local drink made from the hibiscus calyces and is refreshing.

Where to Stay in Okomu National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodation options in Okomu National Park:

  • Okomu National Park Guest House: The national park has a guest house with private rooms, restaurant, and bar.
  • Bini River Lodge: This lodge offers comfortable and luxurious accommodation options with excellent views of the park.

What to Buy in Okomu National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back from Okomu National Park:

  • African Masks: Edo State is known for its beautiful and intricate african masks, which are popular souvenirs.
  • Carvings: Visitors can buy hand-carved sculptures and other art pieces made from wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Okomu National Park

The Edo people of Nigeria are the primary ethnic group living around Okomu National Park. Visitors should note that there are cultural customs to respect when visiting the region, such as dressing conservatively and not photographing people without permission.

Best Time to Visit Okomu National Park

The best time to visit Okomu National Park is during the dry season, which starts in November and ends in April. This is the best time to spot wildlife species, take outdoor adventures, and enjoy the park’s natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okomu National Park

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts when visiting Okomu National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the cultural customs and practices of the Edo people.
  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting cultural and historical sites.
  • Hire local guides when taking outdoor adventures to explore the park safely.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park.
  • Feed or touch the wildlife species in the park.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Okomu National Park

While the park is relatively safe, visitors should note that there can be some hazards in the park, such as wildlife encounters and slipperiness on hiking trails. Visitors should always hire a local guide when exploring the park to ensure their safety.

Health in Okomu National Park

Visitors should be up-to-date on their vaccinations, including yellow fever, typhoid, and hepatitis A and B, before traveling to Okomu National Park. Travelers should also practice good hygiene such as washing hands regularly and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Okomu National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okomu National Park

The nearest airport to Okomu National Park is Benin Airport, which is about 100 kilometers away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Okomu National Park

The park is accessible via road transport from major cities such as Lagos and Port Harcourt, with different bus services available.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car, motorcycle, or bicycle to explore the park and surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Okomu National Park

The park area is accessible by foot, bike or car. A local guide can help visitors navigate the different trails and paths.

Where to Go Next from Okomu National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the best places to visit near Okomu National Park:

  • Benin City: The city is famous for its rich history and culture, including the Benin Bronze art and the Royal Palace.
  • Ibadan: Ibadan is a bustling city, famous for its universities, museums, and local markets.

Conclusion

Okomu National Park is an excellent destination for travelers who love outdoor adventures, wildlife, and culture. Whether you want to take a wildlife safari tour, trek through lush forests, or explore the Edo people’s culture, Okomu National Park has something for everyone. Come for a visit, enjoy the park’s natural beauty, and take lasting memories home.
