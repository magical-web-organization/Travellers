Introduction to Okpekpe

Okpekpe is a small town in the Etsako East Local Government Area of Edo State, Nigeria. It is known for its historic significance, stunning natural landscapes, and unique cultural attractions. With a population of just over 10,000 people, this sleepy town offers travelers a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in Nigerian culture and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Okpekpe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Okpekpe Shrine: This sacred site is the centerpiece of Okpekpe’s cultural heritage. It is believed to have been built in the 15th century and is the largest shrine in Edo State. Visitors can learn about the town’s rich history and traditions by exploring the various monuments and artworks on display.
  • Achilles Art Gallery: If you are interested in contemporary Nigerian art, then a visit to Achilles Art Gallery is a must. The gallery showcases the work of local artists, including painting, sculpture, photography, and mixed media.
  • Ivie Palace: The Ivie Palace is the traditional home of the Okpekpe Chief. This historic building features traditional architecture and is a great place to learn about the town’s royal history.

Natural Attractions of Okpekpe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okpekpe Hills: The Okpekpe Hills is a series of undulating hills that offer spectacular views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike, camp or undertake an eco-tour through the hills to discover the natural abundance that exists in the area.
  • Afuze River: The Afuze River is a scenic waterway that offers several recreational activities such as fishing, swimming, and kayaking. It is a unique spot to relax and take in the beautiful surroundings.
  • Ogbido Fishing Festival: Held annually in August, the Ogbido Fishing Festival is a cultural event that celebrates the town’s fishing heritage. The festival usually features traditional dances, music, and lots of delicious local cuisine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okpekpe

  • Okpekpe Hills Hike: This 5.5km hike is perfect for those looking to explore the scenic views of the Okpekpe Hills. It takes around 2-3 hours to complete depending on the fitness level of the participant. The hike is graded as moderate, but the views from the top make the effort more than worth it.
  • Afuze River Bike Ride: Enjoy a scenic bike ride through the beautiful Afuze River area. The ride is easy and suitable for all ages and fitness levels. The route takes you through several small villages, and visitors can stop for refreshments at local shops and restaurants.

Best Things to Do in Okpekpe: Activities and Tours

  • Cultural Tour: Take a guided tour of the town’s most important cultural and historical sites, including the Okpekpe Shrine, Ivie Palace, and the Achilles Art Gallery.
  • Eco-tour: Explore the natural beauty of the Okpekpe hills in a guided eco-tour. Participants can learn about the local flora and fauna whilst hiking, and camping under the stars.
  • Fishing: Head down to the Afuze River and try your hand at fishing. Several local guides are available to assist and provide a unique insight into the local fishing techniques.
  • Local Cooking Class: Discover the secrets of Nigerian cuisine by taking a cooking class with a local chef. Participants can choose from several traditional dishes and learn how to prepare them in the traditional manner.

Food and Drink Scene of Okpekpe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banga Soup: A traditional palm nut soup made with meat or fish, served with pounded yam or fufu.
  • Owo Soup: Made with ground melon seeds, vegetables, and spices, Owo soup is a hearty vegetarian dish that packs a punch of flavor.
  • Edikaikong Soup: A delicious spinach and vegetable soup with meat or seafood, commonly served with fufu or eba.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A popular local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It is usually served fresh and can be enjoyed on its own or mixed with other drinks.
  • Zobo: A refreshing and tangy drink made from the hibiscus flower, often sweetened with sugar and spices.
  • Kunu: A non-alcoholic drink made from grains like millet, maize, or sorghum, often flavored with ginger or cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Okpekpe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Heritage Palace: A budget-friendly guesthouse with traditional architecture and comfortable rooms.
  • Okpekpe Motel: A mid-range hotel located in the heart of town, with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Fortune Suites: A high-end hotel with modern amenities, located on the outskirts of Okpekpe town.

What to Buy in Okpekpe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wooden carvings: Okpekpe is known for its wooden carvings, featuring intricate designs such as masks, statues, and figures.
  • Adire Fabric: This traditional West African fabric is dyed using a resist-dyeing technique and comes in various patterns and colors.
  • Palm wine or Kunu: Take home a bottle of the local palm wine or kunu, or even better, learn how to make the drink yourself.

Local Culture and Customs of Okpekpe

The people of Okpekpe are known for their rich history and cultural customs. In Okpekpe, it is customary to greet elders by kneeling down, and it is considered a sign of respect to avoid eating or drinking in the presence of an elder. Visitors to Okpekpe are encouraged to learn about and respect local cultural customs and traditions, including dressing appropriately in public places and being mindful of local taboos.

Best Time to Visit Okpekpe

The best time to visit Okpekpe is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. During this time, the weather is ideal for outdoor activities and tours, and the town is vibrant with cultural events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okpekpe

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area
  • Support local businesses and markets

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Disrespect elders or local customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Okpekpe

Okpekpe is a relatively safe town, though travelers should take necessary precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables safe. Visitors should also be aware of street hawkers who may try to scam or deceive them.

Health in Okpekpe

Visitors to Okpekpe should take precautions to prevent common illnesses, such as malaria and typhoid. It is recommended to consult your doctor prior to traveling for relevant vaccinations and to bring mosquito repellent and antimalarial medication.

Getting to Okpekpe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okpekpe

The closest airport to Okpekpe is the Benin City Airport, which is approximately 90 minutes’ drive away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Okpekpe.

Bus Stations Near Okpekpe

Several buses run from Lagos to Okpekpe daily, with a journey time of approximately 6-7 hours. Visitors can also take a public bus from Benin City to Okpekpe, with a journey time of approximately 90 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi from Benin City or Lagos to Okpekpe.

How to Get Around in Okpekpe

The best way to get around Okpekpe is by foot, as the town is small and easily navigable. Bicycles and motorbikes can also be rented from several local vendors for those looking to cover more distance.

Where to Go Next from Okpekpe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Owu Waterfall: A stunning waterfall located in the nearby Obudu area, approximately 2 hours’ drive away from Okpekpe.
  • Ososo Tourist Center: A scenic hilltop resort located in the nearby Ososo area, approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes’ drive from Okpekpe.
  • Igarra: Another historic town located approximately 1 hour’s drive from Okpekpe, known for its cultural and historical significance.

Conclusion

Okpekpe is a hidden gem in southern Nigeria, offering travelers a unique opportunity to immerse themselves in Nigerian culture and traditions. With its wealth of natural attractions, historic landmarks, and delicious local cuisine, Okpekpe is an ideal destination for anyone looking to experience the true spirit of West Africa. Plan your trip today and discover the wonders of this beautiful town for yourself.
