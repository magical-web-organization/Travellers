Introduction to Ukhun

Ukhun, located in Edo State, Nigeria, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This small town is bursting with natural beauty, a rich cultural heritage, and a deep history that dates back centuries. Home to friendly locals, Ukhun is the perfect place to relax and experience the vibrant lifestyle of rural Nigeria. In this travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the must-see places, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, cultural treasures, and mouth-watering cuisine that this amazing destination has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ukhun

There are many exciting places to visit in Ukhun, each one showcasing unique natural beauty, awe-inspiring historical sites, and local culture. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Igodomigodo Palace Ruins - this historical site is where the famous Benin-Brass trade took place between 16th – 17th centuries. The ruins of the palace are well preserved and showcase the rich history of the ancient Kingdom of Benin.
  • Ososo Tourist Centre – located at the top of a scenic hill, the Ososo Tourist Centre provides spectacular views of the town and its surroundings. The Centre also features a museum, a mini zoological garden, as well as natural swimming pools.

Natural Attractions of Ukhun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ukhun Rock Formation – an ideal spot for rock climbing, the Ukhun Rock Formation is a series of stunning rock formations that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding valley.
  • Adu Waterfalls – If you are eager to experience a natural wonder, the Adu Waterfalls is the place to go. It’s a perfect natural pool that you can dive, swim and explore while listening to the soothing sound of the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ukhun

Ukhun is a destination that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of our favorite hiking/biking trails.

  • Amedokhian Beadle Trail – This trail is an excellent option for anyone who likes hiking, allowing you to relax and explore the natural beauty of the forest.
  • Ososo Hills Hiking Trail – This is a challenging hike that is ideal for more experienced hikers. The trail provides stunning views of the natural landscape and its surroundings.
  • Nihin Hill Bike Trail – If you prefer biking, this trail is perfect for you. It cuts through the natural terrain, providing a thrilling experience for bikers.

Best Things to Do in Ukhun: Activities and Tours

With a diverse range of activities and tours such as agro-tourism, village tours, and cultural experiences, Ukhun has something for everyone. Below are some of the best things to do while you’re in Ukhun.

  • Agro-tourism – This tour highlights the village’s agro-industry and the production of farm produce such as cassava, yams, and maize.
  • Village Tours - The village tour is an excellent opportunity to experience the local culture, learn about the customs and daily life of the locals, and sample the delicious traditional food.
  • Cultural Experiences – Ukhun is a cultural hub, and visitors have the opportunity to learn about the traditional Beliefs and Medicinal Practices amongst others.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ukhun

If you’re planning on visiting for a week, here is an itinerary that will cover all of the top attractions:

Day 1: Visit the Adu Waterfalls and take a refreshing swim in the natural pool.

Day 2: Head to the Ososo Tourist Centre to enjoy the scenic views and check out the museum.

Day 3: Take a walking tour of the village learn the history and culture of the locals.

Day 4: Visit Igodomigodo Palace Ruins to learn about the Benin-Brass trade and its significance.

Day 5: Check out the Ukhun Rock Formation and go rock climbing or hiking.

Day 6: Explore the hills of Ososo and go for a hike or a bike ride on the Nihin Hill Bike Trail.

Day 7: Experience the agro-tourism or attend a cultural experience to round out your stay in Ukhun.

Food and Drink Scene of Ukhun

Ukhun has a rich culinary heritage that offers you the chance to taste a variety of traditional delicacies. The food in Ukhun is a fusion of different cultures, including the Yoruba, Bini, and Esan People. Here are our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ekpoma Soup – A traditional vegetable soup, Ekpoma is made with Ugu leaves and coated with a healthy amount of palm oil.
  • Eguisi Soup – This is a pumpkin seed soup that’s slow-cooked with spices and served with steamed rice or pounded yam.
  • Bitterleaf Soup – Made from the bitterleaf plant, this soup is cooked with onions, ogiri or iru, and seasonings.

Local Beverages

  • Palm wine – a non-alcoholic drink made from palm trees, it’s a must-try if you’re visiting Ukhun.
  • Zobo – made from dried Roselle flowers, Zobo is a popular drink in West Africa that is usually served chilled with ice.

Where to Stay in Ukhun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ukhun has many accommodations ranging from luxury hotels to guesthouses, and hostels. Below is a list of our top picks:

  • Ososo Tourist Centre Resort – a luxury hotel featuring modern amenities and scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Usenmisan Guesthouse – a cozy guesthouse with an on-site restaurant, situated in a quiet neighborhood.
  • Amedokhian Leisure Resort – a high-quality resort surrounded by lush vegetation that offers a range of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Ukhun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Ukhun, you’ll find many handcrafted items that are unique to the local culture. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  • Adire fabrics - handmade decorative fabrics that come in vibrant colors and patterns.
  • Beads – These are a popular souvenir to take home as they are intricately designed.

Local Culture and Customs of Ukhun

Ukhun boasts a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their customs and traditions. The residents are known for their hospitality, and visitors are welcomed with open arms. The community’s traditional dances are an essential aspect of the local culture, and visitors can witness these dances and learn about the cultural significance of each.

Best Time to Visit Ukhun

The best time to visit Ukhun is between November and April as the weather is dry and perfect for outdoor activities. The rainy season, which starts in May and ends in October, can make some of the roads impassable and limit outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ukhun

Do:

  • Take a village tour to learn about the locals’ customs and way of life.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the indigenous soups.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Enter sacred or restricted areas without permission.
  • Take photographs of people without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ukhun

Ukhun is generally safe for visitors, but travelers should take standard precautions, such as avoiding traveling alone at night and keeping valuables in a secure place. Visitors should also keep an eye on their surroundings and take extra care when walking near bodies of water and hiking trails.

Health in Ukhun

Ukhun does not have any significant health risks, but visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as getting vaccinated against common diseases such as yellow fever, tetanus, and typhoid fever. Visitors should also bring insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and carry a first-aid kit with them.

Getting to Ukhun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ukhun

The nearest airport to Ukhun is Benin City Airport, which is roughly 1.5 hours’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near Ukhun

The most accessible bus station near Ukhun is the Ekpoma Bus Station, which is a mere 25 minutes’ drive away.

Other Options

Taxis and cars for hire are also available throughout Edo State, making it easy to hire a driver or car service to get to Ukhun.

How to Get Around in Ukhun

The most common method of transportation in Ukhun is by motorbike or foot, and visitors can easily rent bikes to get around the town. If you prefer to explore farther afield, car rentals are also available in Edo State.

Where to Go Next from Ukhun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Nigeria, Ukhun provides easy access to many fantastic destinations, including:

  • Benin City – Located just two hours away from Ukhun, Benin City is an ancient kingdom that is rich in history and culture, with plenty of exciting attractions to explore.
  • Lagos City – Lagos is Nigeria’s largest city and is famous for its thriving art and music scene, stunning beaches, and modern shopping malls.

Conclusion

Ukhun is truly a destination that is off the beaten track, a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of modern city life. From natural wonders to cultural heritage, Ukhun offers visitors the chance to experience all that Nigeria has to offer in a peaceful and relaxing setting.
