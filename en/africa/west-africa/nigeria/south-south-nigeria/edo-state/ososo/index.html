Introduction to Ososo

Located in the heart of Edo State, in South South Nigeria, Ososo is a hidden gem that offers visitors the chance to explore Nigeria’s natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures. The town is surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and winding rivers, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the must-see attractions, best things to do, where to eat and sleep, local culture, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Ososo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ososo Cultural Centre: This centre is dedicated to promoting the rich cultural heritage of the Esan people. Visitors can learn about the history, customs, and traditions of the local people through displays of art, music, dance, and traditional dress.

Natural Attractions of Ososo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Awhum Waterfall: Awhum Waterfall is one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Nigeria, located just a short drive from Ososo. With cascading waterfalls and crystal-clear pools, it’s perfect for swimming, picnicking, or taking a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ososo

  • Ososo Hills: Ososo Hills is one of the top attractions of Ososo, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The hills are a popular destination for hikers and mountain bikers, and visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the hills during the day, or watch the sunset from the top of the hills in the evening.

Best Things to Do in Ososo: Activities and Tours

  • Ososo High Ropes: For adventure seekers, Ososo High Ropes offers a range of adrenaline-pumping activities, including zip-lining, rope climbing, and bridge crossings. With different levels of difficulty, it’s perfect for the whole family.

Day 1 Arrive in Ososo and check-in to your hotel
Day 2 Explore the Ososo Hills and surrounding areas

Day 4 Experience Ososo High Ropes and adventure park

Food and Drink Scene of Ososo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banga Soup: A local delicacy made from palm fruits, Banga soup is typically served with rice or fufu, a popular West African dish made from cassava.
  • Edikang Ikong: Another popular dish in Ososo, Edikang Ikong, is a nutritious soup made from a blend of vegetables and meat, usually, goat or beef.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: Palm wine is a popular alcoholic beverage in Nigeria made from fermented palm sap. It’s a must-try when visiting Ososo and other parts of Nigeria.
  • Zobo: Zobo is a refreshing, non-alcoholic beverage made from hibiscus leaves and is commonly found throughout West Africa.

Where to Stay in Ososo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Elwood Suites: Luxury suites with balconies overlooking the hills.
  • Delcam Suites: Budget-friendly guesthouse with comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Ososo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bead Jewelry: Visitors can buy traditional African beaded jewelry made by locals from Ososo.
  • Pottery: The town also has pottery markets selling locally produced pottery by the people of Ososo.

Local Culture and Customs of Ososo

The people of Ososo are traditionally farmers, and their customs and beliefs are integral to their way of life. Visitors can learn about the music, dance, art, and dress of the Esan people, who are known for their rich cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Ososo

The best time to visit Ososo is during the dry season, which runs from November to February, providing visitors with perfect hiking and outdoor adventure weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ososo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the people
  • Try the local delicacies

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Take photos of people in the market without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Ososo

Ososo is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors are advised to be careful when hiking on the hills and engaging in outdoor activities. It’s important to keep an eye on the weather because the rainy season can make some activities like hiking challenging.

Health in Ososo

Visitors are advised to take precautions against malaria and other tropical diseases by taking anti-malarial medication and using insect repellent.

Getting to Ososo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ososo

The nearest airport to Ososo is the Benin City Airport, which is approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ososo

Several bus companies operate buses to Ososo from Benin City, Warri, and Lagos.

Other Options

Visitors can hire private cars or taxis for transportation during their stay in Ososo.

How to Get Around in Ososo

The best way to get around Ososo is by car or foot. Visitors can also hire motorbikes or bicycles for their transportation needs in town.

Where to Go Next from Ososo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oshogbo: The city is famous for its annual Osun-Osogbo festival, which celebrates the river goddess Osun.
  • Idanre Hills: Located in Ondo State, Idanre Hills is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features stunning landscapes and historical sites.

Conclusion

Ososo is a beautiful town in southern Nigeria, offering visitors a range of adventures and cultural experiences. With its natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures, it’s a must-visit destination in Nigeria.
