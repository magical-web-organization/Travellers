Introduction to Upenekang

Located in Akwa Ibom State, in the South South region of Nigeria, Upenekang is a serene village known for its rich cultural heritage and spectacular natural wonders. The friendly locals, warm weather, and exciting activities make it an excellent destination for solo travelers, couples, and families alike – providing an experience that caters to everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll share everything you need to know about Upenekang, from the best things to do and see, where to stay and eat, cultural customs to be aware of, and important safety information to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Upenekang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

To witness an insight into the cultural heritage of Upenekang, you can visit the following places:

  • Akwa Palace: An 18th-century palace that represents traditional African architecture

  • Etinang Archaeological Site: A historical site dating back to the 11th century with carvings and statues in the area.

  • Ibibio Museum: Exhibits the rich culture of the Ibibio tribe, including various artifacts and cultural activities.

Natural Attractions of Upenekang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The village boasts of several natural wonders that are a must-visit, including:

  • The Ikot Abasi Beach: Enjoy the spectacular view of the ocean or indulge in water activities.

  • Idua River: A popular spot for swimming, fishing or kayaking.

  • Oron Beach: A white sand beach that offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Upenekang

Nature lovers can get their fill by hiking/biking through these routes:

  • Ekporo Trail: A 7km jungle trail that leads to a beautiful waterfall.

  • Upenekang Hill Climb: This trail leads up a hill and offers stunning panoramic views.

  • ** Iko Akwa Ibom Trail:** A bike trail that winds from Upenekang to neighboring towns.

Best Things to Do in Upenekang: Activities and Tours

Experience the best activities and tours in Upenekang by indulging in the following:

  • Iyak River Canoeing: Embark on a canoe trip down the Iyak River, spotting beautiful natural scenery while on your ride.

  • Traditional Dance Classes: Join a local dance class and learn the traditional dances of Upenekang, & other areas.

  • Adiren Cultural Festival: Attend Upenekang’s Adiren Cultural Festival - showcasing food, dance, music, and traditional sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Upenekang

Must-Try Dishes

Upenekang boasts a unique cuisine that you must indeed try:

  • Afia Efere: White soup made with fish or chicken, served with pounded yam.

  • Nsibidi: A local stew made with several types of meat and vegetables.

  • Ikot Ekpene Kpomo Soup: A spicy, hearty soup made with tripe, cow hoof, and assorted meats.

Local Beverages

Wash these delectable meals down with one of these refreshing beverages:

  • Palmwine: A traditional vinous drink extracted from palm trees.

  • Soborodo: A drink made from the hibiscus plant, it’s cranberry in color, and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Upenekang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Upenekang has several decent accommodation options for every traveler’s unique needs, including:

  • Akadua Boutique Hotel: located in town, this hotel offers comfortable rooms with great amenities, including a pool.

  • Essien Udim Guesthouse: This guest house is located near the airport and is ideal for business travelers.

  • Olofeng Guest Lodge: This property is located in a serene environment while still near major attractions in the area.

What to Buy in Upenekang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Upenekang’s main street is lined with shops that sell traditional crafts, jewelry, and fabrics. Local products to bring back home include:

  • Akan Fabric- This fabric with its bright colors and patterns is distinct to the area and can be used to make clothing or upholstery.

  • Afia Efere - While easier to buy ready-made, you can buy local spices and ingredients to make the white soup at home.

Religious Customs and Cultural Significance of Upenekang

Uhteh ha Owo is one of the unique cultures that thrives in Upenekang. They believe they have special powers from their ancestors, which leads them to practice various secret rituals. Respect the locals and their practices.

Best Time to Visit Upenekang

The best time to visit Upenekang is during the dry season, which is typically from November to early April, allowing visitors to enjoy most of the outdoor activities without rain getting in the way.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upenekang

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Wear comfortable and modest clothing, especially when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Do not engage in any activity that compromises local customs and beliefs.

  • Avoid taking pictures of the locals without their consent.

  • Never litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Upenekang

Upenekang is generally safe, with little to no cases of violent crime. Still, we recommend that travelers take the standard precautions and avoid unsafe areas at night.

Health in Upenekang

As with most West African countries, there is a significant risk of malaria in Upenekang. It would be best if you were prepared and take the necessary precautions to avoid being bitten by mosquitos, including sleeping in mosquito nets and using DEET insect repellent.

Getting to Upenekang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Upenekang

The nearest airport to Upenekang is the Akwa Ibom International Airport, located one hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Upenekang

Travelers can catch a bus from popular cities such as Lagos, Port Harcourt, and Calabar to Abak, which sits just three miles from Upenekang.

How to Get Around Upenekang

Travelers can move around Upenekang using buses, tricycles or using a rental car. Most attractions are situated closeby.

Where to Go Next from Upenekang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Explore more of Nigeria by visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Uyo City: A bustling city just 45 minutes south of Upenekang.

  • Obudu Cattle Ranch: Located nearby, this ranch is home to stunning views, wildlife, and delicious produce, perfect for a day trip.

  • ** Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary**Situated in Cross River, Afi Mountain is 120km and 1h:20m away from Ikot Abasi.

Conclusion

Upenekang is a great destination for adventure, nature, and culture enthusiasts. With its natural beauty, rich culture, and unique cuisine, Upenekang provides an unforgettable travel experience. Be sure to follow our guide to discover the best that this Nigerian village has to offer.
