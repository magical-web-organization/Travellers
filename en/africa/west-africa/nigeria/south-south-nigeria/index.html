Introduction to South South Nigeria

Located in West Africa and bordered by Benin to the west and Cameroon to the east, South South Nigeria is a region known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure tourism. Home to several ethnic groups, including the Ijaw, Efik, and Itsekiri, this region is famous for its dazzling cultural festivals, including the Calabar Carnival and the Ekpe Masquerade. South South Nigeria is also home to some of Nigeria’s largest oil and gas reserves, which contribute to the region’s vibrant economy.

List of Places to Visit in South South Nigeria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about South South Nigeria’s rich cultural history, there are several museums and historical sites you should consider adding to your itinerary:

  • National Museum of Nigeria, Oron: This museum houses an impressive collection of Nigerian art, fine art, and archaeological objects from various parts of the country.
  • National War Museum, Umuahia: A significant site highlighting Nigerian’s military history is a must-visit. Here, you can experience exhibits and artifacts from the Nigerian Civil War.
  • Akwa Akpa Ancient City, Calabar: Tucked away in the city of Calabar is Akwa Akpa, an ancient town with roots dating back to the 1500s.

Natural Attractions of South South Nigeria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Visitors will be pleasantly surprised at the region’s natural beauty and attractions. Take in the magnificent views and experience the region’s natural flavor by visiting the following locations.

  • Obudu Mountain Resort, Obudu: This beautiful mountain resort destination offers various outdoor activities like hiking, swimming, and exploring a forest preserve.
  • Ibeno Beach, Akwa Ibom: The longest sand beach in West Africa is ideal for water sports, sunbathing.
  • Kwa Falls, Cross River: A beautiful spectacle of nature at its best, it is an excellent location for taking photographs and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South South Nigeria

The vast expanse of South South Nigeria makes it an ideal destination for outdoor adventure activities like hiking and biking. Visitors can take advantage of the many nature trails, including the following:

  • National Park Cross River, Cross River: This park offers an amazing hiking experience covering about a 5km trail with impressive scenic beauty.
  • Ogbunike Caves, Anambra: Interesting caves located in the town of Ogbunike has pathways that lead to a small stream.
  • Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary, Cross River: Spot endangered primate species like the Drill Monkey while enjoying robust hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in South South Nigeria: Activities and Tours

South South Nigeria is famous for the Calabar Carnival and other events, where visitors can partake in numerous activities like sightseeing tours, cultural festivals, and sporting events.

  • Calabar Carnival, Calabar: A festival celebrating culture and heritage with a night of dance, music, and talent shows.
  • Okpekpe Road Race, Edo: A popular marathon race held annually in the state attracting athletes from different parts of Africa.
  • Niger Delta Boat Regatta, Bayelsa: An event highlighting the traditional craft of boat racing.

Food and Drink Scene of South South Nigeria

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to South South Nigeria is complete without trying some of the regional cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Edikang Ikong: A vegetable soup made with pumpkin leaves, water leaves, and lots of spices.
  • Banga soup: A flavorsome soup made with palm fruit and assorted meat.
  • Bole: A famous street food in the region, roasted plantain usually paired with fish or chicken.

Local Beverages

South South Nigeria also has a rich drinking culture, and some must-try beverages include:

  • Palm Wine: A milky, wine-like alcoholic drink made from palm trees.
  • Orijin: Popular refreshing alcoholic drink found in bars, clubs, and restaurants across the region.
  • Kunu: Non-alcoholic drink made from millet and spices.

Where to Stay in South South Nigeria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options to choose from in South South Nigeria, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Le Meridien, Ogeyi Place: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Port Harcourt with excellent amenities.
  • Obudu Mountain Resort: A peaceful retreat with fantastic scenic views, this resort offers excellent accommodation options.
  • Atalaia Hostel, Calabar: A budget-friendly hostel located in Calabar within walking distance from the beach and cultural center.

What to Buy in South South Nigeria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of South South Nigeria’s unique culture and tradition back home with you by purchasing any of the following souvenirs:

  • Traditional beads and jewelry
  • Handmade baskets and trays
  • Carved wooden masks

Local Culture and Customs of South South Nigeria

The region is renowned for its rich cultural diversity, and visitors are encouraged to respect the customs and traditions of the locals. The Efik people, in particular, have their traditional system of governance, and visitors can experience this firsthand by attending the Ekpe masquerade festival.

Best Time to Visit South South Nigeria

The best time to visit South South Nigeria is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is more pleasant, and the water bodies in tourist attractions are more serene and clear to explore. The rainy season is usually from May to October, characterized by heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of South South Nigeria

Do:

  • Dress appropriately
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the culture and traditions of the locals

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the customs and traditions of the locals
  • Don’t wander around unfamiliar areas at night
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended while in town

Safety and Health

Safety in South South Nigeria

South-South Nigeria is generally safe, but visitors are advised to take reasonable security measures.

  • Keep cash and valuables safe and discreet
  • Avoid walking alone in unfamiliar locations, especially at night.
  • Use Journeys only if necessary.

Health in South South Nigeria

Visitors are advised to have their vaccines up-to-date prior to arrival. You should also take mosquito nets and repellants because malaria could be prevalent in some areas.

Getting to South South Nigeria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South South Nigeria

The region has a few airports that visitors can access. Some of the international airports include:

  • Ibom International Airport - Uyo, Akwa Ibom
  • Margaret Ekpo International Airport - Calabar,Cross River

Bus Stations Near South South Nigeria

Visitors can take buses to South-South Nigeria from Lagos and other neighboring states. The state’s long-distance bus station is on the outskirts of the state capitals:

  • Yoruba Road Bus-stop in Warri, Delta state
  • Mararaba Motor Park, Calabar

Other Options

Ferry and boat transportation services are available for visitors in some areas within South South Nigeria. Visitors can also take personal or taxi transportation.

How to Get Around in South South Nigeria

The most convenient way to move around the region is by road or air transport. Visitors can hire private taxis and cars or make use of public transportation buses. Personal or rental vehicles are other alternatives. Ensure to use reputable transportation companies.
