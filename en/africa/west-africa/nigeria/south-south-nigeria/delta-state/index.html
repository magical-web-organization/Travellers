Delta State is a beautiful destination located in Nigeria’s South-South region, where the Niger Delta meets the Atlantic Ocean. Known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and friendly locals, Delta State is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Nigerian experience.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, and everything you need to know about Delta State in Nigeria.

Introduction to Delta State

Delta State is a diverse region that is home to several ethnic groups, including the Urhobo, Isoko, Itsekiri, Ijaw, and Ndokwa people, each with its unique culture and traditions. The state’s capital is Asaba, located in the northern part of the state, while Warri is the commercial center of the state.

List of Places to Visit in Delta State

Delta State is home to several exciting tourist attractions, ranging from natural wonders to culturally significant landmarks. Here are a few must-visit places to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Asaba Museum: This museum displays artifacts and exhibits related to the history and culture of the Asaba people. A visit to this museum will provide unique insights into the cultural heritage of the region.

  • Nana Living History Museum: The Nana Living History Museum in Warri is one of the must-visit places for history enthusiasts. The museum displays the Itsekiri people’s cultural artifacts, including clothing, jewelry, pottery, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Delta State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Abraka Turf and Country Club: This 18-hole golf course is a perfect getaway for golf enthusiasts. The country club also features a swimming pool, tennis court, and other recreational facilities.

  • Warri Kingdom Royal Cemetery: This historical cemetery is a burial ground for the monarchs of Warri. The cemetery features intricately carved headstones and tombs, and it provides a glimpse into the region’s history and culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delta State

Delta State is blessed with stunning landscapes that provide excellent opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are a few popular routes to explore:

  • Oshimili Hill Trail: This hiking trail in Asaba takes you on a scenic journey through lush vegetation and offers stunning views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Delta State: Activities and Tours

Delta State offers a myriad of things to do, from exploring historical sites to experiencing the region’s unique culture. Here are a few must-do activities:

  • Boat Cruise Along The Niger Delta: Take a boat cruise along the Niger Delta for an opportunity to see the region’s biodiversity up close. The cruise also provides an excellent opportunity to learn about the region’s unique history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delta State

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit to Delta State:

Day 1: Visit the Asaba Museum and explore the city of Asaba.

Day 2: Visit Warri Kingdom Royal Cemetery and learn about the history of Warri.

Day 3: Take a boat cruise along the Niger Delta and explore the region’s biodiversity.

Day 4: Hike the Oshimili Hill Trail and enjoy stunning views of the city.

Day 5: Visit the Nana Living History Museum and explore the Itsekiri culture.

Day 6: Enjoy a round of golf at Abraka Turf and Country Club.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as the Ososo Tourist Center or the Nike Lake Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Delta State

Delta State is home to a diverse culinary scene that offers a mix of local and international flavors. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banga Soup: This soup is made with palm fruit extract, and it is typically served with starch or fufu.

  • Ofe Owerre: This dish is a staple in Delta State and is made with a mix of vegetables, meat, and fish, flavored with traditional spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This beverage is made by fermenting the sap of a palm tree, and it is a popular local drink in Delta State.

Where to Stay in Delta State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delta State has a wide range of accommodation options to suit various budgets and preferences. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Transcorp Hotels: This hotel is located in the heart of Asaba and features luxurious rooms and suites, restaurants, bars, and other facilities.

  • Ibis Hotel Warri: This hotel is centrally located in Warri and offers modern rooms and suites, a restaurant, a bar, and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Delta State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Delta State is famous for its traditional crafts, including pottery, basketry, and woodcarving. Here are a few places to buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Ogbogolo Market: This bustling local market in Asaba is the perfect place to buy souvenirs and unique handicrafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Delta State

The people of Delta State are warm and hospitable, with a rich cultural heritage. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Delta State:

  • Respect Local Customs: Delta State is a diverse region with several ethnic groups, each with its traditions and customs. It is essential to respect these customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Delta State

The best time to visit Delta State is between November and April when the weather is mild and dry.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals’ customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Delta State

Delta State is generally safe for tourists, but it is always advisable to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Delta State

Ensure you have up-to-date vaccinations, and carry mosquito repellent to prevent malaria.
