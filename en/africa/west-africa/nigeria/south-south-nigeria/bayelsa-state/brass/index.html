Introduction to Brass

Located in Bayelsa State, in Nigeria’s South South region, Brass is a picturesque town that’s rapidly becoming a popular destination among seasoned travelers. Brass is famous for its lush greenery, beautiful waterways, and breathtaking scenery, making it the perfect place for relaxation and outdoor adventures. With a rich cultural heritage, visitors to this town can’t help but fall in love with its unique blend of history and beauty.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the various tourist attractions and give you expert tips and advice to help you plan your trip to Brass, Nigeria. From the best places to visit to the must-try local dishes and where to stay, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Brass

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brass Kingdoms Museum - The museum showcases an impressive collection of artifacts that captures the history and culture of the people of Brass. Visitors get a glimpse of life in the Kingdoms of Brass through the centuries.
  • Nembe-Brass Warf - The warf, built-in 1927, was a crucial hub for trade and commerce between Brass and other coastal towns. Visitors can learn about the history of the area while enjoying a scenic view of the Brass River.
  • Kolokuma-Opokuma National Park - The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare primate species, butterflies, and birds, among others. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, nature walks, and bird watching.

Natural Attractions of Brass: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Twon Brass Beach - The beach is known for its serene beauty and miles of crystal-clear waters, making it a perfect spot for surfing, swimming, and sunbathing.
  • Nembe Creek - Nestled amid mangrove forests and serene waters, Nembe Creek is renowned for its lush scenery and breathtaking views.
  • Nembe Creek Oil and Gas Estate - The Oil and Gas Estate is a unique facility that provides visitors with an in-depth look at the operations of the oil and gas industry in the Niger Delta.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brass

  • Brass - Okpoama Forest Reserve - The reserve is a beautiful hiking trail, featuring lush greenery and a variety of birdlife.
  • Brass - Akassa - This route offers a challenging yet exhilarating biking experience, leading cyclists through scenic landscapes dotted with numerous historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Brass: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours of the Brass River - Explore the beautiful Brass River aboard a traditional Nigerian canoe or speedboat.
  • Fishing - Brass is known for its rich marine life, making it an excellent spot for local and international anglers to fish.
  • Bird Watching - The town is home to hundreds of species of birds, including the rare white throated blue swallow, black-capped kingfisher, and yellow-billed kite.

Food and Drink Scene of Brass

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banga Soup - a popular spicy soup made from palm nuts, meat, and assorted vegetables. It is typically eaten with locally grown rice.
  • Pepper Soup - a flavorful soup made with local spices and typically served with goat meat or fish.
  • Suya - a popular Nigerian street food, consisting of grilled meat seasoned with local spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine - a sweet alcoholic beverage made from fermented palm tree sap.
  • Zobo - a refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers, sugar, and spices.

Where to Stay in Brass: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nembe Creek Hotel - A modern-day hotel located within the Nembe Creek Oil and Gas Estate. It boasts modern facilities, including a gym, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Brass Hotel - This hotel offers affordable accommodation with great views of the Brass River.
  • Aglowoo Guest House - The perfect budget-friendly option offering clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Brass: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Crafts - Brass is home to skilled artisans who create a range of traditional crafts, including pottery, baskets, and woodcarvings.
  • Oil Paintings - The town is known for its talented artists who create stunning oil paintings inspired by the natural beauty of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Brass

Brass is known for its rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions. The town’s people are proud of their unique history, and love to share it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Brass

The best time to visit Brass is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this period, the weather is warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brass

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region by embarking on outdoor adventures.
  • Indulge in the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the wildlife or the environment.
  • Engage in any form of illegal activity while in Brass.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brass

Although Brass is a relatively safe town, visitors are advised to exercise caution and to be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Brass

Visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions, including vaccinations, to protect against diseases such as malaria and yellow fever.

Getting to Brass: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brass

The nearest airport to Brass is the Port Harcourt International Airport, which is approximately 200 km away. Visitors can use the airport taxis or private hire cars to reach Brass.

Bus Stations Near Brass

The town is served by several major bus stations, including the Port Harcourt Motor Park and the Bayelsa Transport Corporation.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to hire private cars or use ride-sharing services such as Uber to reach Brass.

How to Get Around in Brass

The best way to get around in Brass is by using motorcycles or taxis. Visitors can also opt to rent cars from reputable car rental firms.

Where to Go Next from Brass: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yenagoa - The capital of Bayelsa State, Yenagoa is known for its historic landmarks, including the Isaac Boro Park and the Bayelsa State Peace Park.
  • Nembe Kingdom - Located along the coast of the Atlantic Ocean, Nembe Kingdom is famous for its rich cultural heritage and traditional festivals.
  • Amassoma - A quiet university town located on the banks of the Nun River.
