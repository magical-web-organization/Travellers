Introduction to Rivers State

Rivers State is a coastal state in the South-South geopolitical zone of Nigeria. It’s known for its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. The state is home to some of the country’s most important landmarks and tourist destinations.

The capital of Rivers State is Port Harcourt, which is considered the economic capital of the South-South. The state is habited by various ethnic groups, including Ikwerre, Ogoni, Ijaw, and Kalabari, each with its unique traditions and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Rivers State

Rivers State has many places of interest to tourists. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, the state has something to offer for every kind of traveler. Here are some of the top places to visit in Rivers State:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum, Port Harcourt

The National Museum, located in Port Harcourt, is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history and culture of Nigeria. The museum has a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the country’s pre-colonial and colonial eras.

King Jaja of Opobo’s Monument

King Jaja of Opobo was a legendary figure in the history of the Opobo Kingdom, and his legacy is preserved in the form of a monument in Opobo. The monument is a popular destination for tourists seeking to learn more about the culture and traditions of the region.

Bonny Island

Bonny Island is located in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria and is known for its rich history and culture. It’s home to the famous Bonny Kingdom, which has a long and storied past. Tourists can visit the Bonny Museum located on the island, which offers insight into the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Rivers State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Port Harcourt Tourist Beach

Port Harcourt Tourist Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Rivers State. It’s located in Port Harcourt and offers visitors the chance to relax and soak up the sun. The beach has a range of activities, including volleyball, beach soccer, and horseback riding.

Isaac Boro Park

Isaac Boro Park is located in the heart of Port Harcourt and offers visitors the chance to enjoy a serene environment in the middle of the bustling city. The park is home to a variety of flora and fauna and has play areas for children and benches for relaxation.

Rivers State National Park

Rivers State National Park is one of the largest parks in Nigeria and is home to a diverse range of animal and plant species. Tourists can enjoy a range of activities, including hiking, bird watching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rivers State

Rivers State has several hiking and biking routes for tourists who enjoy outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

Port Harcourt City Walk

Port Harcourt City Walk is a guided tour that takes visitors through the streets of Port Harcourt. The walk is designed to showcase the history and culture of the city and includes visits to landmarks and attractions.

Ogbunabali Forest Reserve

Ogbunabali Forest Reserve is a natural forest reserve located in Port Harcourt. The reserve has several hiking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the lush vegetation and spot birds and animals.

Okomu National Park

Okomu National Park is a nature reserve located in Edo State, near the border with Rivers State. The park has hiking and biking trails that take visitors through the dense forest and past streams and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Rivers State: Activities and Tours

Beyond the standard tourist destinations, Rivers State has several unique activities and tours that tourists can enjoy. Here are some of the best:

Boat Tours of the Creeks and Rivers

Rivers State is known for its sprawling network of creeks and rivers, which offer breathtaking views and unique experiences. Tourists can book boat tours to explore these waterways and discover the hidden gems that lie within.

Chimpanzee Sanctuary and Wildlife Conservation Centre

The Chimpanzee Sanctuary and Wildlife Conservation Centre is located in Port Harcourt and is home to several orphaned chimpanzees. Tourists can visit the center and learn more about the conservation efforts underway to protect these endangered animals.

Nigerian Carnival

The Nigerian Carnival is an annual celebration of the country’s culture and traditions. The event takes place in Port Harcourt and features parades, music, and dancing.

Food and Drink Scene of Rivers State

Nigerian cuisine is known for its spice, flavor, and variety. Rivers State is no exception, and tourists can indulge in a range of local delicacies while exploring the region.

Must-Try Dishes

Banga Soup

Banga Soup is a staple of Rivers State cuisine. It’s made with palm fruit extract, fish, and a range of spices, and is typically served with a side of white rice or fufu.

Oha Soup

Oha Soup is a traditional soup made with shredded oha leaves, cocoyam, and assorted meat. It’s known for its rich flavor and is often served with garri or semolina.

Pepper Soup

Pepper Soup is a spicy soup made with meat, fish, or chicken, and a range of spices. It’s a popular dish in Rivers State and is often served with yam or plantain.

Local Beverages

Palm Wine

Palm Wine is a popular traditional drink in Rivers State. It’s made from the sap of palm trees and has a sweet, slightly sour taste. It’s often served chilled in calabash gourds.

Ogogoro

Ogogoro is a popular local spirit made from palm wine. It’s a strong drink and is often served with kola nuts or bitter kola.

Where to Stay in Rivers State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rivers State has several accommodation options for tourists, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top options:

Le Meridien Ogeyi Place

Le Meridien Ogeyi Place is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Port Harcourt. The hotel has several amenities, including an outdoor pool, fitness center, and a range of dining options.

Presidential Hotel

Presidential Hotel is another luxury hotel located in Port Harcourt. The hotel has several rooms and suites, a restaurant, a bar, and a fitness center.

Swiss Spirit Hotel and Suites

Swiss Spirit Hotel and Suites is a budget-friendly option located in Port Harcourt. The hotel has several rooms, a restaurant, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Rivers State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Rivers State can bring home a range of souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the top options:

Traditional Handicrafts

Rivers State is home to several ethnic groups, each with its unique traditions and crafts. Tourists can purchase local handicrafts, including pottery, woodcarvings, and woven baskets.

Beads

Beads are a significant part of Rivers State culture, and visitors can purchase locally made bead necklaces, bracelets, and other accessories.

Local Fabrics

Rivers State is known for its beautiful fabrics, including the popular Akwete and Adire prints. Tourists can purchase locally made fabrics and have them made into traditional dresses and other clothing items.

Local Culture and Customs of Rivers State

Rivers State has a vibrant and diverse culture, with each ethnic group having its unique customs and traditions. Some of the significant cultural practices in the state include masquerade festivals, traditional dances, and storytelling sessions.

Visitors to Rivers State should be respectful of these customs and avoid offending the locals’ sensitivities. It’s also essential to dress modestly when visiting culturally significant places, such as mosques and traditional shrines.

Best Time to Visit Rivers State

The best time to visit Rivers State is between December and February when the weather is mild and dry. The rainy season runs from March to November, with heavy rainfall and storms common during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rivers State

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting culturally significant places.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Book tours and activities with registered and reputable providers.

Don’t:

  • Wander into unknown neighborhoods alone, especially at night.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Carry large sums of money or expensive jewelry.
  • Bring prohibited items to the country, such as narcotics and firearms.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rivers State

While Rivers State is generally safe for tourism, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. The state has a history of kidnapping and armed robbery, especially in some parts of the city.

Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and should always be vigilant when interacting with strangers. It’s advisable to book tours and activities through registered and reputable providers.

Health in Rivers State

Visitors to Rivers State should ensure they are up to date with their vaccinations before traveling. Malaria is prevalent in the region, and visitors should take necessary precautions, such as using mosquito nets and taking anti-malaria medication.

Getting to Rivers State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rivers State

The major airport serving Rivers State is the Port Harcourt International Airport. Several domestic and international airlines operate flights to and from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Rivers State

Several bus companies operate services to Rivers State from other parts of Nigeria. Examples of these bus services include God is Good Motors, Chisco, and ABC (Associated Bus Company).

Other Options

Visitors to Rivers State can also book taxi services through ride-hailing apps such as Uber and Bolt or hire private drivers.

How to Get Around in Rivers State

Several transportation options are available to visitors to Rivers State. Taxis and motorcycle taxis (known as okadas) are readily available and are one of the most popular means of transport.

Tourists can also rent cars through reputable rental companies, but should note that driving in Nigeria can be challenging and requires a valid International Driving Permit.

Where to Go Next from Rivers State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Rivers State can easily travel to other parts of Nigeria from the state. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Lagos, Nigeria’s largest city and commercial capital
  • Abuja, Nigeria’s capital city
  • Cross River State, known for its pristine beaches and wildlife reserves

Conclusion

Rivers State is an incredibly diverse and exciting destination for tourism. From its natural wonders to its rich cultural heritage, the state has something to offer for every type of traveler. By following the tips and advice in this guide, tourists can get the most out of their visit to Rivers State and leave with unforgettable memories.
