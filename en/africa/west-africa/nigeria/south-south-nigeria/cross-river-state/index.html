Introduction to Cross River State

Cross River State is a Nigerian state located in the South South region of Nigeria. It is bordered by Benue State to the north, Ebonyi and Abia States to the west, Cameroon to the east, and the Gulf of Guinea to the south. With a population of over 4 million people, Cross River State is known for its rich culture, natural beauty, and exciting tourist attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Cross River State

Make sure you visit these top attractions in Cross River State:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cross River State has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore this through its museums and historical places. Some of the most notable cultural attractions include:

  • The National Museum of Colonial History
  • The Slave Museum
  • Mary Slessor’s House

Natural Attractions of Cross River State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cross River State is home to some of the most breathtaking natural attractions in Nigeria. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or scenic views, Cross River State has everything to offer. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Obudu Mountain Resort
  • Agbokim Waterfalls
  • Kwa Falls
  • Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Cross River National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cross River State

Cross River State is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. With its scenic countryside and undulating terrain, Cross River State offers visitors unparalleled opportunities for outdoor recreation. Some of the most popular hiking/biking trails in Cross River State include:

  • Obudu Cattle Ranch Trail
  • Canopy Walkway Tour in Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Biking Trails in Ugep, Ikom and Okpoma.

Best Things to Do in Cross River State: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re travelling alone, with friends, or family, there’s always something to do in Cross River State. Some of the best things to do in Cross River State include:

  • Visiting the Obudu Cattle Ranch Resort for relaxation and adventure activities
  • Embarking on a boat ride on the Rivers, Creek and Waterfalls.
  • Touring Tinapa Business and Leisure Resort for fun, shopping, eating and movie watching.
  • Attending the Calabar Festival

Local Culture and Customs of Cross River State

The people of Cross River State have a rich and diverse culture with many customs and traditions. The Ibibio and Efik tribe, predominantly occupy the state. Visitors are encouraged to learn and respect the local customs and traditions when visiting Cross River State.

Best Time to Visit Cross River State

The best time to visit Cross River State is from November to April when the weather is cooler and drier. This time period is ideal for outdoor activities, festivals and tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cross River State

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Experience the Calabar Festival
  • Practice caution in crowded areas

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately
  • Refuse offers of hospitality
  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Choose a remote area after dusk

Safety and Health

Cross River State is generally safe for visitors. However, travel insurance is recommended when travelling to Cross River State. Visitors are also advised to take appropriate precautionary measures to prevent any health concerns such as eating unhealthy foods and leaving valuables in the open.

Safety in Cross River State

  • Always lock your car when driving
  • Stay alert when travelling in crowded areas
  • Avoid walking alone at night

Health in Cross River State

  • Drink bottled or purified water
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites
  • Visit a healthcare facility if you feel unwell

How to Get Around in Cross River State

Taxis are the most common mode of transportation within Cross River State. Car rentals are also available for travelers that want more flexibility in their travels. There are no train or subway services available in Cross River State.
