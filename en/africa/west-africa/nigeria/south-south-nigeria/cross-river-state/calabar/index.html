Introduction to Calabar

Calabar is a vibrant and colorful city situated in Cross River State, South-South Nigeria. This historic city has preserved its rich culture, traditional artifacts, and landmarks for generations. Calabar is a true gem that attracts tourists from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Calabar

Looking for an adventure? Here are the top attractions to visit in Calabar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calabar is full of historical landmarks that tell stories of its past. The city is home to numerous museums and ancient buildings such as:

  • The National Museum of Calabar
  • Tinapa Business Resort
  • Mary Slessor’s House
  • Slave History Museum
  • Drill Monkey Rehabilitation Centre

Natural Attractions of Calabar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calabar features a variety of scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone. Visit these natural wonders:

  • Agbokim Waterfall
  • Cross River National Park
  • Obudu Cattle Ranch
  • Kwa Falls
  • Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calabar

Calabar is known for its hiking and biking trails such as the Obudu Plateau, Tinapa Jogging Trail, and the scenic Cross River State Park.

Best Things to Do in Calabar: Activities and Tours

Calabar is filled with exciting activities and tours, suitable for all ages. Some of the best include:

  • Zipline at Tinapa Business Resort
  • Canoeing on Cross River
  • Black and White Sands Beach
  • Kayaking at Marina Resort
  • Boat ride to UNESCO Slave History Museum

Week-Long Itinerary for Calabar

To make the most of your visit to Calabar, plan for a week-long itinerary that includes:

Day Activity
1 Visit Old Residency Museum and Slave Museum
2 Tour Mary Slessor’s House and drive to Agbokim Waterfalls
3 Adventure at Kwa falls
4 Obudu Cattle Ranch visit and cable car ride
5 Tour Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary or drill monkey rehabilitation center
6 Explore Tinapa Business Resort and Canopy Walk
7 Relax at Marina Resort or Calabar International Conference Centre

Food and Drink Scene of Calabar

Get your taste buds ready to savour the delicious traditional dishes and beverages of Calabar:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Afang Soup
  • Edikang Ikong Soup
  • Abak Atama Soup
  • Efere Ikon Soup
  • Ekang Nkukwo

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine
  • Zobo Drink
  • Kunu Drink
  • Suya

Where to Stay in Calabar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calabar is home to a range of quality accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Calabar include:

  • Transcorp Hotels
  • Tinapa Lakeside Hotel
  • Channel View Hotels
  • Calabar Harbour Resort and Spa
  • Obudu Mountain Resort

What to Buy in Calabar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Do not leave Calabar empty-handed. Bring home unique souvenirs and local products such as:

  • Calabar woven baskets
  • Traditional Attires
  • Wooden art and crafts
  • Calabar black soap
  • Beaded jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Calabar

Calabar has a rich and unique culture, with traditional practices such as Ekpe masquerade, Efik Coronation, and Ndem Ambassadors. Their traditional folklore and music—Highlife and Ekombi—are thriving today.

Best Time to Visit Calabar

Calabar has a tropical climate and the best time to visit is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. Time your visit during the Calabar Carnival in December, a colorful celebratory event that parades the streets for weeks on end.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calabar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Visit museums and historical sites
  • Try the traditional cuisine and beverages
  • Take your time to explore the city
  • Attend the Calabar Carnival

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without permission
  • Disrespect the local people and culture
  • Litter in public spaces
  • Dress inappropriately

Safety and Health

Safety in Calabar

Calabar is generally safe; however, it is important to maintain caution in crowded areas and public spaces. Seek guidance from trusted locals and licensed tour operators.

Health in Calabar

Vaccinations such as yellow fever are highly recommended before visiting Calabar. Drink only bottled water and carry your medications for common illnesses like malaria and typhoid fever.

Getting to Calabar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calabar

Calabar is served by Margaret Ekpo International Airport, which is about 10 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Calabar

From Lagos, the most reliable bus companies include ABC Transport, and Cross Country. You can also access Calabar by road from neighbouring states such as Port Harcourt and Abia.

Other Options

Taxis and private hire companies like Uber and Bolt operate in Calabar but are relatively sparse.

How to Get Around in Calabar

Transportation options in Calabar include taxis, buses, and private rental cars. You can also rent bicycles in designated rental facilities within the city.

Where to Go Next from Calabar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Calabar, explore other nearby destinations such as:

  • Obudu Cattle Ranch
  • Accra, Ghana
  • Yaounde, Cameroon

Conclusion

Calabar is a unique city overflowing with history, culture, and natural beauty. Explore this colorful city’s rich cultural heritage, stunning scenery, and outdoor adventures, and delight in traditional cuisine and beverages. Bring home some of its local artifacts and souvenirs to remember your exciting trip to Calabar.
