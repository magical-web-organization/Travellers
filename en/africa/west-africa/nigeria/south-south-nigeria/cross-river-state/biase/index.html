Introduction to Biase

Biase is a local government area in the southern part of Nigeria, located in Cross River State. It is home to a number of famous tourist attractions and natural wonders that make it a popular destination for travelers. Its unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty makes it an ideal place for a vacation.

In this guide, we will guide you through the various attractions, things to see and do, places to stay, and what to eat in Biase.

List of Places to Visit in Biase

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Obudu Ranch: This is a ranch located in the heights of the Sankwala Mountains. The Obudu Mountain Resort is a must-visit attraction in Biase. Aside from the lush landscape of the mountains and waterfalls, Obudu mountain is a thrilling and invigorating natural tourist spot.

  • Tinapa Business and Leisure Resort: Tinapa is located in Calabar and is one of the largest business and leisure resorts in West Africa. It comprises a hotel, a standard-sized Cineplex, slot machines, restaurants, and gaming arcades.

  • Slave Trade Route: The slave trade in Nigeria was abolished in the 19th century, but its legacy lives on. Tourists with an interest in history can take a guided tour along the slave trade route which passes through Biase. Along the way, you will learn about the history of the slave trade and visit significant historical sites.

Natural Attractions of Biase: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Agbokim Waterfalls: Agbokim waterfall is located in Etung, about an hour’s drive from Biase. This waterfall consists of seven streams cascading over a cliff, creating a stunning visual display.

  • Oban Hills: A trip to Biase is not complete without visiting the Oban hills. This natural attraction is home to a vast array of rare plants and animals, including the Cross River gorilla, which can only be found in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biase

  • Obudu Mountains: Hiking in Obudu Mountains is recommended for experienced hikers. The trek can take up to 3 days, and the mountainous terrain and changing weather conditions require you to be physically fit and well-prepared.

  • Oban Hills: The Oban hills are home to a vast network of hiking trails that offer both easy and challenging terrain. Along the way, you will come across rare plants and animals, including the Cross River gorilla.

Best Things to Do in Biase: Activities and Tours

  • Canopy Walk in Kakum National Park: Kakum National Park is about 2 hours’ drive from Biase, but it is worth the trip. It is home to the world-famous canopy walkway, which is suspended 40 meters above the ground and spans more than 350 meters through the forest.

  • Cultural Festivals: Biase is rich in culture, and there are several cultural festivals held throughout the year. The most famous of them is the New Yam Festival, which is usually held in August. This is an excellent opportunity to learn about the customs and traditions of the Biase people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biase

Day 1 Arrive in Biase and check into your hotel
Day 2 Visit the Oban Hills and Akwa Falls
Day 3 Take a guided tour of the slave trade route and visit the historical sites
Day 4 Explore the Tinapa Business and Leisure Resort
Day 5 Hike to the top of the Obudu Mountains
Day 6 Visit the Agbokim waterfall, then return to your hotel
Day 7 Depart Biase and head to your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Biase

Must-Try Dishes

  • Afang Soup: this is a popular dish in Biase and is made with cocoyam leaves, water leaves, and various meats.

  • Ekom Nkukwo: this dish is made with seafood and coconut milk and is best eaten with pounded yam.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: Palm wine is a popular drink in Biase, made by tapping the sap from palm trees.

  • Hibiscus Tea: This is a refreshing drink made from the hibiscus flower, which is boiled in water with sugar and served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Biase: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Obudu Mountain Resort: This is one of the best places to stay in Biase. It is located on the Obudu Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Benis Hotels, Calabar: This hotel is located in Calabar, a few minutes’ drive from Biase. It offers comfortable rooms and excellent customer service.

What to Buy in Biase: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Artifacts: Biase is rich in culture, and there are several local artifacts that you can buy as souvenirs. These include traditional masks, pottery, and sculptures.

  • Handwoven Fabrics: You can also buy handwoven fabrics, which are made from locally grown cotton and dyed with natural dyes.

Local Culture and Customs of Biase

  • Clothing: Biase people are known for their brightly colored clothing, which usually consists of a wrapper and a blouse or shirt.

  • Language: The people of Biase speak Ejagham, one of the many dialects spoken in Cross River State.

Best Time to Visit Biase

The best time to visit Biase is between November and March when the weather is dry and cool. This is the perfect time to go hiking and explore the natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biase

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drinks

  • Do learn about the culture and customs of the Biase people

Don’t:

  • Do not litter

  • Do not disrespect the locals or their customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Biase

Biase is a relatively safe place to visit, but tourists should be cautious and avoid walking alone at night in remote areas.

Health in Biase

Tourists should take precautions when drinking tap water and be sure to use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Biase: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biase

The nearest airport to Biase is Margaret Ekpo International Airport, which is located in Calabar.

Bus Stations Near Biase

The most common bus station in Calabar is the Ayade Road Bus Station.

Other Options

Car rental services are also available in Calabar.

How to Get Around in Biase

The best way to get around Biase is by car or taxi. Car rental services are available in Calabar, or you can hire a taxi for the duration of your stay.

Where to Go Next from Biase: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Calabar: Calabar is about an hour’s drive from Biase and is a popular destination for tourists. It is home to the Calabar Carnival, one of the biggest cultural events in Africa.

  • Akwa Ibom: Akwa Ibom is another neighboring state famous for its natural beauty and has many tourist attractions worth visiting.

Conclusion

Biase is a fantastic destination for travelers looking to experience the culture, history, and natural beauty of Nigeria. With so many natural landscapes and cultural landmarks, there is never a shortage of things to do and see in Biase.
