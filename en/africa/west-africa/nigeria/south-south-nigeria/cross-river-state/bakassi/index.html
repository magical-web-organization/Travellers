Introduction to Bakassi

Bakassi is a small peninsula in Cross River State, Nigeria, located in the South-South region of Nigeria. Despite its small size, Bakassi is known for its exotic flora and fauna, sandy beaches, and waterways. It is home to several fishing communities and is known for its unique culture and traditions. The area was once a source of dispute between Nigeria and Cameroon but has been under Nigerian control since 2008.

List of Places to Visit in Bakassi

Here is a list of places to visit in Bakassi during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mary Slessor’s House: This historical site was the residence of Mary Slessor, a Scottish missionary who worked in Nigeria from 1876 until her death in 1915.
  • The Calabar Museum of Slavery: This museum showcases the dark history of the transatlantic slave trade in Nigeria, including the role of Cross River Province in the trade.
  • Obutong Beach: This beach is known for its stunning scenery, and it is perfect for swimming, picnics, and relaxation.
  • Ibeno Beach: This beach is an ideal location for outdoor activities such as surfing, swimming, and sunbathing.

Natural Attractions of Bakassi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cross River National Park: Located in the southeast of Nigeria, this park is known for its unique biodiversity, with over 1,500 plant species, 300 bird species, and 40 mammal species.
  • Kwa Falls: This beautiful waterfall is located in Akamkpa, Nigeria, and is a popular spot for picnics and swimming.
  • The Drill Ranch: This sanctuary is home to the endangered Drill monkey, and visitors can learn about the work of conservationists in protecting the rare primate.
  • The Caves of Akampka: These natural caves are located in Akamkpa and are a popular spot for tourists looking to explore.

Food and Drink Scene of Bakassi

Nigeria is known for its diverse cuisine, and Bakassi is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice: This dish is a staple in Nigerian cuisine and is made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a unique blend of spices.
  • Suya: This is a popular street food made of grilled meat seasoned with a spicy blend of ground peanuts, cayenne pepper, and other spices.
  • Afang Soup: This delicious soup is made with Afang leaves and a variety of meats and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This drink is made from the sap of the Palmyra palm tree
  • Zobo: This drink is made from the calyx of the Hibiscus plant and is served cold.

Local Culture and Customs of Bakassi

The people of Bakassi are primarily Efik and Ibibio, with a mix of other cultures due to its history as a trading hub. The people of Bakassi are known for their hospitality, and their festivals and cultural celebrations, including the Calabar Carnival, which is a major attraction in Nigeria.

Best Time to Visit Bakassi

The best time to visit Bakassi is during the dry season, which runs from November to February. The weather is usually sunny, and there are no rains to disrupt travel activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bakassi

Bakassi is generally safe for tourists, but you should always take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. It is advisable to avoid traveling alone at night and to keep valuables in a safe place.

Health in Bakassi

Visitors to Bakassi should ensure they have all necessary vaccinations and medications before traveling. It is also advisable to drink bottled or purified water during your stay.
