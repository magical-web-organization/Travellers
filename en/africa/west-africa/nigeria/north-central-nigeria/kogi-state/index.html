Introduction to Kogi State

Kogi State is located in the North Central region of Nigeria and shares borders with nine other states in Nigeria. It is home to a variety of cultural and natural attractions that attract visitors from all over the world. The state is home to many ethnic groups, including Ebira, Bassa, Nupe, and Igala, and their cultures are reflected in the state’s food, festivals, and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Kogi State

If you’re planning a trip to Kogi State, be sure to add these attractions to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ibrahim Babangida Square: This public square is located in the heart of Lokoja, the capital city of Kogi State. It is named after Ibrahim Babangida, a former military head of state, and serves as a venue for events and celebrations.
  • Lokoja Museum: The Lokoja Museum is a small but interesting museum that showcases the history and culture of Kogi State. It is located in an old colonial building in Lokoja.
  • Lord Lugard’s Residence: Lord Lugard’s Residence is a historic building in Lokoja that was once home to Frederick Lugard, a British colonial administrator who became the first governor-general of Nigeria.

Natural Attractions of Kogi State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Patti: Mount Patti is a scenic mountain that offers a panoramic view of Lokoja and the surrounding countryside. It is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Idris Malikawa’s Tomb: Idris Malikawa’s Tomb is an important cultural and historical site located in the town of Idah. It is the final resting place of a legendary warrior who fought against the British colonialists in the 19th century.
  • River Niger: The River Niger is a major river that flows through Nigeria and West Africa. It is an important source of water and is home to many species of aquatic animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kogi State

Kogi State offers several hiking and biking trails that offer an opportunity to explore the state’s natural beauty.

  • Mount Patti Trail: This trail leads up to the summit of Mount Patti, which offers a panoramic view of Lokoja and the surrounding countryside.
  • Awo Tunnel Trail: This trail leads through a historic tunnel that was used by British colonialists during their occupation of Nigeria.
  • River Niger Trail: The River Niger Trail is a scenic route that follows the course of the river through Kogi State.

Best Things to Do in Kogi State: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the local food scene: Kogi State is known for its delicious cuisine, and there are many local restaurants and markets where you can sample the local dishes.
  • Visit local festivals: Kogi State is home to several festivals throughout the year, such as the Osuntokun Festival and the Igala Cultural Festival.
  • Take a river cruise: River Niger offers several scenic river cruises that take you through the heart of Kogi State.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kogi State

Day 1: Visit the Lokoja Museum and explore the historic sites in Lokoja Day 2: Hike up Mount Patti and enjoy the scenic views Day 3: Visit the town of Idah and explore the Idris Malikawa’s Tomb Day 4: Take a river cruise along the River Niger Day 5: Visit the local markets and sample the local cuisine Day 6: Attend a local festival or cultural event Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local spa or wellness center

Food and Drink Scene of Kogi State

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suya: Suya is a popular street food in Nigeria that consists of skewered meat (usually beef or chicken) that is seasoned with spices and grilled over an open flame.
  • Pounded Yam: Pounded Yam is a staple food in Nigeria that is made by pounding boiled yam until it becomes a smooth, dough-like consistency.
  • Egusi Soup: Egusi Soup is a hearty soup made with ground melon seeds and various vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: Palm Wine is a traditional Nigerian alcoholic drink that is made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Zobo: Zobo is a popular non-alcoholic drink made from hibiscus leaves that are boiled and sweetened with sugar.
  • Kunu drink: Kunu drink is a local drink made from millet, spices, and sometimes ginger.

Where to Stay in Kogi State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • New Pinnacle Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Lokoja and offers modern amenities and comfortable accommodations.
  • Kogi Hotels Limited: Kogi Hotels Limited is a popular hotel located in Idah that offers affordable accommodations and friendly service.
  • Niger Motel: The Niger Motel is a budget-friendly option located in Lokoja that offers basic accommodations and clean facilities.

What to Buy in Kogi State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Fabric: Kogi State is home to many textile markets that sell locally-made fabrics and clothing.
  • Pottery: There are several pottery workshops in Kogi State that sell unique and handmade pottery items.
  • Local Crafts: Kogi State is home to many talented artisans who create beautiful and unique crafts like baskets, carvings, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Kogi State

  • Respect for elders: In Kogi State, respect for elders is highly valued, and it is customary to greet them with a bow or a curtsy.
  • Traditional festivals: Kogi State is home to many traditional festivals that celebrate the state’s culture and history.
  • Hospitality: The people of Kogi State are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Kogi State

The best time to visit Kogi State is during the dry season, which typically runs from November to March. The weather is cooler and there is less rain during this time, which makes it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kogi State

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in the local language
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Disrespect elders or local authorities
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Kogi State

While Kogi State is generally safe for visitors, there have been sporadic incidents of violence and crime. It is important to exercise caution and avoid travelling alone at night. Additionally, be aware of your surroundings and avoid showing off valuables in public.

Health in Kogi State

Visitors to Kogi State should take precautions to protect themselves against mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria. It is also important to drink only bottled or purified water and to avoid eating street food from unhygienic vendors.

Getting to Kogi State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kogi State

The nearest airport to Kogi State is the Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport in Abuja, which is located approximately two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kogi State

There are several bus stations located throughout Kogi State that offer services to nearby cities and states.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Kogi State from nearby cities or states.

How to Get Around in Kogi State

The best way to get around Kogi State is by car, either by renting one or hiring a private driver. Buses and shared taxis are also available, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable for long distances.

Where to Go Next from Kogi State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abuja: The capital city of Nigeria is located just two hours away from Kogi State and is known for its modern architecture and vibrant nightlife.
  • Kaduna: Kaduna is a historical city located north of Kogi State that is home to several cultural and architectural attractions.
  • Nassarawa: Nassarawa is a state located to the east of Kogi State that is known for its natural beauty and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

Kogi State is a hidden gem in Nigeria that is home to a rich history, a diverse culture, and stunning natural attractions. From the scenic views on Mount Patti to the local cuisine and traditional festivals, there is something for everyone to enjoy during a visit to this beautiful state. With this comprehensive travel guide, you will be well-equipped to plan your perfect trip to Kogi State.
