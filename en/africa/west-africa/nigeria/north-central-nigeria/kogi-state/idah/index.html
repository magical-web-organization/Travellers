Introduction to Idah

Idah is a historic town in Kogi State, North Central Nigeria. It is situated on both banks of the Niger River, which makes it a popular destination for tourists who want to experience the natural beauty of Nigeria. The town is known for its rich cultural heritage and historical importance, with many museums, monuments, and palaces that date back centuries.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Idah. Whether it’s your first time visiting or you’re a frequent traveler, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Idah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Idah is blessed with an abundance of rich cultural attractions, ranging from museums to historical places. These are some of the most significant places to visit:

  • The Attah’s Palace: This is the residence of the Attah of Idah, the traditional ruler of the town. The palace is a major cultural site and is a place where visitors can learn about the traditional customs and beliefs of the Igalas.
  • The Idah Museum: The museum is home to a diverse collection of artifacts from the town’s history, including traditional artworks, pottery, and textiles.
  • Ozioko Hill: A significant historical site, Ozioko Hill is where the Igalas are said to have first settled.

Natural Attractions of Idah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Idah is blessed with breathtaking natural scenery and outdoor activities that will excite nature lovers. These are some of the natural attractions visitors should explore:

  • Niger River: The Niger River offers some of the best scenic views in the town. Visitors can go on boat rides and fishing trips or simply relax and take in the stunning scenery.
  • Mount Patti: For the more adventurous, Mount Patti offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding landscape. Hiking to the top of the mountain is a popular activity for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Idah

Idah has several trails that offer visitors a chance to explore the natural and cultural beauty of the town. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Idah Escarpment Trail: This trail leads hikers through the Idah hills, offering stunning views of the Niger River and surrounding villages.
  • Idah-Benue River Trail: This trail passes through the scenic Benue River, and offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Idah: Activities and Tours

Aside from the popular attractions in Idah, there are several activities and tours that visitors can engage in to create some unforgettable experiences. These are some of our top picks:

  • Explore the Local Markets: Visit the local markets in Idah to experience the hustle and bustle of traditional Nigerian markets. Visitors can buy souvenirs, food, and other local products.
  • Boat Tour on the Niger River: A boat tour of the Niger River is a fantastic way to take in the stunning scenery of the region.
  • Attend a Cultural Festival: Visitors can attend a cultural festival in Idah such as the Igala New Yam Festival, which takes place in August each year, to experience the traditions, culture, and customs of the Igalas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Idah

We’ve put together an ideal week-long itinerary for visitors to Idah. This itinerary includes some of the most significant attractions and activities, as well as some hidden gems in the region.

Day 1: Visit the Attah’s Palace and the Idah Museum.

Day 2: Hiking on the Idah Escarpment Trail.

Day 3: Visit Ozioko Hill, the site of the first settlement of the Igalas.

Day 4: Boat tour on the Niger River.

Day 5: Explore the local markets in Idah and attend a cultural festival.

Day 6: Visit the Mount Patti.

Day 7: Relax at one of the town’s many cafes or restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Idah

Idah is a foodie’s delight. With a diverse range of cuisines, visitors can find a delightful array of local and international dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Eba and Egusi Soup: A favorite local dish made with cassava flour and a rich soup made from ground melon seeds.
  • Suya: A popular Nigerian street food made from skewered and grilled meat, usually served with a spicy sauce.
  • Pounded Yam and Efo Riro Soup: A popular Nigerian dish made from yam flour, served with a vegetable soup.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A traditional drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Zobo: A drink made from dried hibiscus leaves and infused with ginger, usually served chilled.

Where to Stay in Idah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Idah has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. These are some of the best places to stay:

  • Bachar Royal Hotel: A luxurious hotel with well-appointed rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Royal Choice Inn: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms and basic amenities.
  • Kofa Hotel and Suites: A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Idah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Idah is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional crafts. Visitors can purchase handmade items, artworks, and other souvenirs to take home as a memory of their visit.

Some of the popular souvenirs include:

  • Pottery: Beautiful handmade pottery is a popular souvenir in Idah.
  • Textiles: Visitors can buy traditional textiles such as hand-woven baskets and fabrics.
  • Wood Carvings: Unique wood carvings, including masks and figurines, are popular among visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Idah

Idah is deeply rooted in its cultural heritage, and visitors can learn about the customs and beliefs of the Igalas by exploring the town’s museums, palaces, and historical sites. Visitors to Idah should be respectful of local customs, dress modestly, and ask permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Idah

The best time to visit Idah is between October and March when the weather is relatively cooler and more comfortable. The rainy season is between April and September, and it can be humid and uncomfortable for some visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Idah

Do:

  • Dress modestly to show respect to local customs.
  • Ask permission before taking photographs.
  • Explore the local markets and try the local food.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Idah

Idah is generally safe for visitors, but it is always advisable to take precautions to ensure your safety, especially at night. Visitors should avoid carrying large sums of cash and should be wary of pickpocketing.

Health in Idah

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure they remain healthy throughout their trip. These include drinking bottled water, using mosquito nets, and avoiding street food.

Getting to Idah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Idah

The nearest airport to Idah is the Ajaokuta airport, which is approximately 30km from the town. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Idah.

Bus Stations Near Idah

Idah has several bus stations that serve different regions in Nigeria. Some of the popular bus terminals include:

  • Kogi Transport Company: This terminal offers services to most parts of Nigeria.
  • Sikera Motors: This terminal offers services to Northern Nigeria.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi to Idah from any of the neighboring cities. Taxis are readily available and are a safe and convenient way to travel within the region.

How to Get Around in Idah

Visitors can get around Idah on foot, by taxi or car hire. Taxis are readily available and can be hailed from most street corners. Visitors can also rent cars to use during their stay in Idah.

Where to Go Next from Idah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Idah is surrounded by several exciting destinations that visitors can explore, including:

  • Lokoja: A historic town known for its significance in Nigeria’s colonial history.
  • Koton-Karfe: A small town known for its ancient walls and fortress.
  • The Gurara Waterfalls: A popular natural attraction that is renowned for its stunning scenery and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Idah is an exciting destination for those seeking to experience the cultural and natural heritage of Nigeria. With a rich diversity of attractions and activities, visitors are bound to have an unforgettable experience in this historic town. The guide above provides a framework to help you make the most of your trip.
