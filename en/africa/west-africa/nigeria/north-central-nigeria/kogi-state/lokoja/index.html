Introduction to Lokoja

Located in the North Central region of Nigeria, Lokoja is a city with rich cultural heritage and historical significance. This ancient city was the first administrative capital of Nigeria and is known for its scenic beauty and diverse natural attractions. In this travel guide, we will explore the top places to visit, things to do, local culture and customs, food and drink scene, and much more, to help you plan your visit to Lokoja.

List of Places to Visit in Lokoja

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Museum, Lokoja: The National Museum houses important archaeological and ethnographic collections from the region, showcasing the prehistoric and historical heritage of Nigeria.
  • Mount Patti: This is the highest point in Lokoja and offers stunning panoramic views of the city and its surroundings.
  • Lord Lugard’s Residence: This was the home of Sir Frederick Lugard, a British colonial administrator who founded Lokoja as the administrative capital of Nigeria.

Natural Attractions of Lokoja: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Confluence of River Niger and Benue: This is where the two great rivers, Niger and Benue meet, and is a unique spectacle of swirling water currents.
  • Ogidi Gorge: This natural wonder is a beautiful canyon created by a river that winds through the rocky terrain and is a popular spot for adventure activities like hiking, climbing and swimming.
  • Kpata Hill: This hill is a popular spot for hiking and offers scenic views of the city and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Lokoja

Lokoja has several beautiful hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Confluence Beach to Mount Patti: This is a challenging and scenic route that takes you along the River Niger and up to the top of Mount Patti for stunning views.
  • Lokoja Housing Estate to Kpata Hill: This is an easier hike that takes you from the modern housing estates of Lokoja to the summit of Kpata Hill.

Best Things to Do in Lokoja: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of the Confluence: Take a boat tour of the confluence of River Niger and Benue for a unique water experience and to learn about the historical significance of the area.
  • Visit a Local Market: Lokoja has several colorful local markets, where you can shop for local fabrics, handicrafts, and fresh produce.
  • Experience Local Culture: Attend a traditional festival or ceremony in Lokoja and experience the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lokoja

  • Day 1: Visit the National Museum and the Lord Lugard’s Residence.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Confluence and enjoy a picnic lunch by the riverside.
  • Day 3: Visit Mount Patti and hike to the summit for stunning views.
  • Day 4: Explore the Ogidi Gorge and enjoy a day of adventure activities.
  • Day 5: Visit the Kpata Hill and hike to the summit for sunset.
  • Day 6: Visit a local market and shop for souvenirs.
  • Day 7: Experience local culture by attending a traditional festival or ceremony.

Food and Drink Scene in Lokoja

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup: This is a popular local dish made from yam flour and a soup made from ground melon seeds, vegetables, and meat or fish.
  • Grilled Fish: Fresh fish grilled over an open fire is a local specialty and is served with spicy pepper sauce and sides like plantains and cassava.
  • Suya: This is skewered and grilled meat, seasoned with ground peanuts, cayenne pepper, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Zobo: This is a sweet and tangy drink made from hibiscus flowers, sugar and spices.
  • Kunu: This is a millet-based drink that is sweetened with sugar or honey and flavored with spices like ginger.

Where to Stay in Lokoja: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • New General Hospital Guest House: This guesthouse is located in a serene environment, with comfortable rooms, and good customer service.
  • The Circles Hospitality: This luxurious hotel has modern facilities, including free wifi, a swimming pool, a fitness center, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Lokoja: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Fabrics: Lokoja is known for its colorful fabrics, such as aso-oke, adire, and ankara. These fabrics are used to make clothes, bags, and other accessories.
  • Handicrafts: Local artisans create beautiful handicrafts, such as pottery, woodcarvings, and beadwork.
  • Spices and Herbs: Lokoja is known for its aromatic spices and herbs, such as black pepper, garlic, ginger, and turmeric. These can be bought from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Lokoja

Lokoja has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of traditions from several ethnic groups. Respect for elders and communal living are important values in Lokoja. Greetings are an important custom and should be shown by shaking hands and exchanging pleasantries.

Best Time to Visit Lokoja

The best time to visit Lokoja is during the dry season between December to March. The weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are most enjoyable. However, be prepared for the Harmattan, a dry and dusty wind that can affect visibility and respiratory health.

Do’s and Don’ts in Lokoja

Do:

  • Dress modestly: Lokoja is a conservative city, and it is respectful to dress conservatively.
  • Show respect: Show respect to elders and locals, and always ask before taking photos.
  • Use greetings: Use greetings such as “Sannu” and “Salaam” to show politeness when meeting locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: Drink only bottled or purified water to avoid water-borne illnesses.
  • Take photographs without permission: Always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.
  • Disrespect local customs: Respect local customs and traditions, and seek advice from locals if unsure about acceptable behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lokoja

Lokoja is generally a safe city for tourists, but it is advised to take standard precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night, not flashing valuables, and being aware of scams and pickpockets.

Health in Lokoja

Before traveling to Lokoja, it is advisable to consult a travel health specialist who will advise on vaccinations and other necessary medications. Medical facilities are limited, and it is recommended that tourists carry a first-aid kit and basic medicines.

Getting to Lokoja: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lokoja

Lokoja is serviced by the local airport, which has limited connections to other cities in Nigeria.

Bus Stations Near Lokoja

There are several bus stations in Lokoja, including the Kogi Express Terminal, from where you can access buses to major cities in Nigeria.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire to get around Lokoja.

How to Get Around in Lokoja

The best way to get around Lokoja is by taxi, which can be easily flagged down on the street. Alternatively, motorbikes or okadas can be used for shorter distances but only if you are comfortable on them.

Where to Go Next from Lokoja: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abuja: The Nigerian capital city is only 2 hours away from Lokoja and has plenty of cultural sites and modern amenities.
  • Idah: Idah is located on the banks of River Niger, and its Royal Palace is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Lokoja is an ideal destination for tourists who appreciate culture, adventure, and natural beauty. With its rich historical heritage, natural wonders, and delicious food, Lokoja is a must-visit location in Nigeria. This travel guide provides comprehensive information on the top places to visit, things to do, local culture and customs, food and drink scene, and logistics of visiting Lokoja.
