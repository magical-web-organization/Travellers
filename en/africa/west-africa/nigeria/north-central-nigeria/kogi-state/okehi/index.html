Introduction to Okehi

Okehi is located in Kogi State, in North Central Nigeria, offering a range of interesting attractions, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s something for everyone in this destination. Whether it’s a solo trip or a family vacation, Okehi has got it all. This guide aims to provide you with all the information and insights that you need to plan a fantastic trip to Okehi.

List of Places to Visit in Okehi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Igala Museum: Located in Idah, this museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It showcases the rich cultural heritage and traditional civilization of the Igala people, including masquerades, traditional attire, weapons, and other artifacts.
  • Idah Fort: Constructed in 1904, this fort is a historical landmark in Idah that played a significant role in the colonial era. It showcases British imperial architecture and is worth checking out for its strategic military importance.
  • Okehi Art and Cultural Centre: Located in Okehi Local Government Area, the Art and Cultural Centre is dedicated to preserving the traditional arts and crafts of the Igala people. It houses a range of art and crafts, including pottery, weaving, carving, and more.

Natural Attractions of Okehi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Patti: At 1,800 meters, Mount Patti offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape. It’s a great spot for adventure enthusiasts who enjoy trekking, hiking, and rock climbing.
  • Osuku Rock Formation: A unique natural rock formation that resembles a human face, Osuku Rock is a geological wonder that has religious significance to locals.
  • River Niger Delta Boat Cruise: A boat ride on the Niger Delta is an amazing experience for nature lovers. The river boasts a wide range of flora and fauna, and it’s not unusual to spot crocodiles, hippopotamuses, and other wild animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Okehi

  • Mount Patti Trekking Route: The trek to Mount Patti takes approximately 6-7 hours and is a challenging hike. The trek is best undertaken in the morning or late afternoon when the sun is less intense.
  • Ojuwo Ayegbetu Trail: The Ojuwo Ayegbetu Trail is a 4-hour hiking route that takes you through some of the most picturesque forests and hills of Okehi. It’s an excellent way to experience the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Okehi: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local markets: No trip to Okehi is complete without visiting the local markets. The markets offer a range of locally made products, including clothing, art, crafts, and food.
  • Osuku Rock Festival: This cultural festival takes place annually in December and is a celebration of the Osuku Rock Formation. It’s a great way to experience the local culture of Okehi and enjoy traditional music, dance, and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Okehi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup: A local favorite, Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup is a traditional Nigerian dish made from pounded yam (a starchy vegetable) and Egusi (a type of melon seed) blended together in a stew with meat or fish.
  • Suya: Suya is a popular West African street food made with skewered meat (beef, chicken or goat), seasoned with spices and groundnut powder.
  • Akara: Deep-fried bean cakes made from black-eyed peas, Akara is a popular breakfast item in Okehi.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees, Palm Wine is a refreshing drink with a sweet taste.
  • Zobo: Made from the hibiscus flower, Zobo is a popular non-alcoholic drink in Okehi that’s known for its health benefits.
  • Kunu: A nutritious and filling drink made from millet, Kunu is a popular drink in Nigeria.

Where to Stay in Okehi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sunshine Hotels and Suites: Located in Idah, Sunshine Hotels and Suites is a popular choice for tourists. The hotel offers comfortable accommodation and great facilities, including a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.
  • Nkoyo Absolute Hotel: Nkoyo Absolute Hotel is located in the heart of Okehi and offers excellent value for money. The hotel has comfortable rooms, a bar, and a restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in Okehi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art and Crafts: Okehi is home to some of the best local art and crafts, including pottery, weaving, carving, and more. These products make great souvenirs that you can bring home to remember your trip.
  • Kola Nut: Kola Nut is a traditional African nut that has social and cultural significance. You can find them in the local markets and they make a great gift for friends and family.
  • Local Fabrics: Okehi is known for its beautiful local fabrics, including Ankara and Adire. These fabrics can be found in the local markets and make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Okehi

The Igala people of Okehi are renowned for their rich culture and traditions. One of the things you’ll notice about the local people is their hospitality and warmth towards visitors. Respect for elders is a fundamental value, and it’s important to show courtesy and respect towards them. Dressing conservatively is also advised, particularly in rural areas.

Best Time to Visit Okehi

The best time to visit Okehi is during the dry months, between December and February, and June to September. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without any hassles.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okehi

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Experience the local culture and custom
  • Respect elders and local customs
  • Dress conservatively

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Wear revealing clothes in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Okehi

Okehi is a relatively safe destination, and incidents of violent crimes are minimal. However, it’s essential to exercise caution and common sense, particularly when venturing into rural areas. It’s advisable to avoid traveling at night and to stay in well-lit areas where possible.

Health in Okehi

It’s essential to take necessary health precautions when traveling to Okehi. It’s advisable to get vaccinated for diseases such as yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever before arriving. It’s also important to carry insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to minimize the risk of mosquito-born diseases.

Getting to Okehi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okehi

The nearest airport to Okehi is the Aminu Kano International Airport in Kano (299 km). There are several flights connecting Kano to major cities in Nigeria, such as Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt.

Bus Stations Near Okehi

Okehi is well-connected by roads, and there are several bus stations and terminals in the area. Some of the popular bus stations include Lokoja Bus Station and Idah Bus Station.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available in Okehi, and they offer a convenient way to get around the city and explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Okehi

Buses and taxis are the most common modes of public transportation in Okehi. However, they can be crowded and uncomfortable, particularly during peak hours. It’s advisable to negotiate the fare before embarking on a journey.

Where to Go Next from Okehi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abuja: The capital city of Nigeria is located approximately 6 hours from Okehi and is a popular destination for sightseeing and shopping.
  • Kano: Known for its ancient city walls and mosques, Kano is a city rich in history and culture, located approximately 6 hours from Okehi.

Conclusion

Okehi is a hidden gem in Kogi State, and this travel guide aims to provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to this unique destination. From natural wonders to rich cultural experiences, Okehi has something for everyone.
