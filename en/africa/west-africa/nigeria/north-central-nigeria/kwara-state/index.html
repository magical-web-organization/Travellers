Introduction to Kwara State

Located in the North Central region of Nigeria, Kwara State is a state with a rich cultural heritage, diverse natural attractions, and a vibrant economy. The state is bordered by Niger State to the North, Kogi State to the West, Ekiti State to the South, and Ondo State to the South-West. The state capital is Ilorin, which is known for its unique blend of Yoruba, Hausa, and Nupe cultures. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or just looking for a getaway, Kwara State has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kwara State

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Esie Museum
  • Owu Waterfall
  • Owo-Isin Hills
  • Alfa Alimi Mosque
  • Sobi Hill

Esie Museum

Located in Esie town, the Esie Museum is one of the most popular cultural attractions in Kwara State. The museum is home to over 1,000 soapstone figures that were excavated from the town in 1775. Each figure has a unique expression, which has intrigued visitors for generations.

Owu Waterfall

Located in Owa-Onire town, the Owu Waterfall is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Nigeria. The waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Owo-Isin Hills

The Owo-Isin Hills are a series of small hills located in Owo-Isin town. The hills offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside and are a great place to go for a hike or a picnic.

Alfa Alimi Mosque

The Alfa Alimi Mosque is located in Ilorin, the state capital of Kwara. The mosque is one of the oldest structures in the city and is an important symbol of the state’s Islamic heritage.

Sobi Hill

Sobi Hill is located in the outskirts of Ilorin and is a popular hiking spot for locals and tourists alike. The hill offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is a great place to go for a picnic.

Natural Attractions of Kwara State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Owu Falls
  • Kainji Lake National Park
  • Arinta Waterfall
  • Kampe Hill
  • Patigi River

Owu Falls

Owu Falls is located in Owu-Isin town and is one of the highest waterfalls in Nigeria. The waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a great spot for hiking and picnicking.

Kainji Lake National Park

Kainji Lake National Park is located to the north of Kwara State and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, baboons, and antelopes. The park also features the famous Kainji Dam, which is the largest dam in Nigeria.

Arinta Waterfall

The Arinta Waterfall is located in Okutala-Mbari town and is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Kwara State. The waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a great place to go for a hike or a picnic.

Kampe Hill

Located in Kampe town, Kampe Hill is a popular hiking spot that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The hill is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a great place to go for a picnic.

Patigi River

The Patigi River is located in Patigi town and is a great spot for fishing and boating. The river is home to a diverse range of fish species, including tilapia and catfish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kwara State

Kwara State is home to a number of scenic hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Some popular hiking and biking routes in Kwara State include the Owu Falls Trail, the Kampe Hill Trail, and the Owo-Isin Hills Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Kwara State

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amala and Ewedu
  • Tuwo Masara and Miyan Kuka
  • Suya

Amala and Ewedu

Amala and Ewedu is a popular dish in Kwara State made from yam flour and a stew of jute leaves. The dish is usually served with pepper sauce and fried fish.

Tuwo Masara and Miyan Kuka

Tuwo Masara and Miyan Kuka is a delicious dish made from corn flour and a stew of baobab leaves. The dish is usually served with a variety of meat and fish.

Suya

Suya is a popular snack in Kwara State made from skewered beef or chicken that has been marinated in a blend of spices and grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Pito
  • Kunu

Pito

Pito is a local beer made from fermented millet. The beer has a slightly sweet and sour taste and is a popular drink among locals and tourists alike.

Kunu

Kunu is a non-alcoholic beverage made from a blend of millet, ginger, and sugar. The drink is usually served cold and is perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Kwara State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kwara State offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Some popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the state include the Kwara Hotel, Hotel Seventeen, and the Amani Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Kwara State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Kwara State include handicrafts, textiles, pottery, and leather goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Kwara State

Kwara State has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, with many different tribes and ethnic groups represented. The state is known for its vibrant music and dance traditions, as well as its unique blend of Islamic and traditional African culture.

Best Time to Visit Kwara State

The best time to visit Kwara State is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this time, the weather is cooler and drier, making it the perfect time to explore the state’s many outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kwara State

  • Dress conservatively in public places
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of people or places

Don’t:

  • Distract drivers by walking in the middle of the road
  • Drink or use drugs in public places
  • Engage in public displays of affection

Safety and Health

Safety in Kwara State

Kwara State is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. It is advisable to avoid traveling alone at night or in areas that are unfamiliar. Visitors should also keep their valuables out of sight and be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Kwara State

Visitors to Kwara State should ensure that they are up to date on their vaccinations before traveling. Malaria is prevalent in the state, so it is advisable to take anti-malaria medication and to use mosquito nets when sleeping.

Getting to Kwara State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kwara State

The Ilorin International Airport is the main airport serving Kwara State, with daily flights to major cities in Nigeria and West Africa.

Bus Stations Near Kwara State

Kwara State is well connected by road, with a number of bus stations serving the state. Some popular bus stations include the Ilorin Bus Station and the Offa Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors to Kwara State can also reach the state by train or by car from neighboring states.

How to Get Around in Kwara State

Kwara State is well connected by road, with a number of taxis, buses, and motorbikes available for hire. Visitors can also rent cars if they prefer to drive themselves.
