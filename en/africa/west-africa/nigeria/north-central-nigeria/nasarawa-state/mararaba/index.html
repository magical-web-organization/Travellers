Introduction to Mararaba

Mararaba is a bustling municipality in Nasarawa State, North Central Nigeria. Edged by the Abuja-Keffi expressway, it provides a strategic location and an easy gateway to Lagos and other destinations in Nigeria. With its unique cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, vibrant food and drink scenes, and a plethora of enjoyable activities, Mararaba is undoubtedly worth visiting.

List of Places to Visit in Mararaba

Mararaba boasts of numerous attractions that will leave any traveller in awe. Whether you are a history buff, adventure seeker, or nature lover, Mararaba has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit places in Mararaba:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Nasarawa State Museum: This museum is just an hour’s drive from Mararaba town and exhibits Nasarawa’s history, customs, culture, and traditions.

  • Obiokpu Eggon Cultural Centre: A historical landmark, this centre showcases the customs and traditions of the Eggon people, one of the major ethnic groups in Nasarawa state.

Natural Attractions of Mararaba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Keffi Polo Ranch: The Keffi Polo Buffalo Ranch is a scenic ranch where visitors can engage in outdoor activities like horseback riding, polo, and golf.

  • The Farin Ruwa Waterfalls: It is a breathtaking waterfall with a height of over fifty meters, located in Wamba Local Government Area. It is accessible from Mararaba by road and is a perfect destination for an adventure lover.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mararaba

Mararaba is blessed with a variety of mountain ranges and lush green forests that provide endless opportunities for hiking and biking. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced hiker or biker, there is a trail for you. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Kuduru Mountain: An hour’s drive from Mararaba town, this mountain range provides a perfect place for hiking and nature exploring.

  • Keffi Hills: Located in the Keffi Local Goverment Area and about 20 minutes from Mararaba, the Keffi Hills are ideal for hiking or biking, providing panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Mararaba: Activities and Tours

Mararaba has plenty of activities to keep tourists busy. Here are some of the best things to do in Mararaba:

  • Visit the Nasarawa State Museum: Learn about the history, customs, and culture of Nasarawa State by visiting this museum.

  • Explore the Obiokpu Eggon Cultural Centre: Another must-visit, this cultural centre showcases the customs and traditions of the Eggon people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mararaba

If you’re looking to spend a week exploring Mararaba, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive and check into your accommodation. Explore the city’s vibrant food scene
Day 2 Visit the Nasarawa State Museum and the Obiokpu Eggon Cultural Centre
Day 3 Go hiking or biking in Kuduru mountain, followed by a visit to the Keffi Polo Ranch
Day 4 Take a day trip outside Mararaba to Farin Ruwa Waterfalls
Day 5 Spend time exploring the markets and buying souvenirs and local goods
Day 6 Enjoy a walking tour of the city, taking in the architecture and street food
Day 7 Depart Mararaba

Food and Drink Scene of Mararaba

Mararaba has a rich food culture, and offers visitors a range of delicious dishes to try. Food in Mararaba is influenced by the culture and traditions of the Eggon people. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Masa: This is a traditional Eggon dish made from rice flour and yeast. A variation of the dish is also popular in the northern parts of Nigeria.

  • Kuli-Kuli: It is a popular West African snack made from groundnut with flavors of garlic and cayenne pepper.

Local Beverages

  • Pito: This is a popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented sorghum. It is a popular drink among the Eggon people.

Where to Stay in Mararaba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mararaba offers visitors a range of accommodation choices, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some options:

  • Noble Vista Hotel: This hotel offers a range of budget-friendly rooms and is located within the heart of Mararaba.

  • Drasha Suites: This hotel is located close to the Keffi Polo Ranch and offers guests a range of luxury rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Mararaba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mararaba is a commercial hub with a thriving market scene. Some local products that make for great souvenirs include:

  • Handwoven baskets and mats: Eggon women weave beautiful baskets and mats that are perfect for home decor.

  • Traditional clothing: The Eggon people have unique and colorful traditional clothing that makes for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mararaba

Mararaba is home to the Eggon people, one of the major ethnic groups in Nasarawa state. The Eggon people are known for their love for music, dance, and festivals. Visitors are advised to respect the local customs and dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Mararaba

The best time to visit Mararaba is from November to February when the temperature is milder and rainfall is lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mararaba

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect the local culture.

  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any public display of affection.

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mararaba

Mararaba is a safe destination for tourists with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to exercise caution and be mindful of their belongings to avoid petty theft.

Health in Mararaba

Visitors are advised to take precautions against malaria, by taking anti-malaria pills, and seeking medical attention if they feel ill.

Getting to Mararaba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mararaba is easily accessible by road and air. Visitors can reach Mararaba by:

Airports Near Mararaba

The closest airport to Mararaba is the Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport in Abuja.

Bus Stations Near Mararaba

Mararaba is well connected by road, and visitors can take a bus from Lagos or Abuja to reach Mararaba.

Other Options

Taxis and car rental services are also available.

How to Get Around in Mararaba

Visitors can get around Mararaba by taxis, motorbikes or using public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Mararaba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations for visitors to explore include

  • Akwanga: This is a small town in Nasarawa State and is famous for its historical and cultural significance. Located just an hour’s drive from Mararaba, it is a perfect day trip destination.

  • Gurara Waterfalls: Located in Niger State, Gurara waterfall is another breathtaking waterfall located two hours away from Mararaba. Visitors can engage in hiking and water activities around the waterfall.

Conclusion

Mararaba may be a small town, but it is packed with cultural, historical, and natural treasures waiting to be explored. Whether you are an adventure-seeker, foodie or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Mararaba is an excellent destination to visit. Wear your hiking boots, pack your bags, and get ready to explore the hidden gems of Mararaba.
