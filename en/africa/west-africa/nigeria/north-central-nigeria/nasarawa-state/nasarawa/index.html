Introduction to Nasarawa

Nasarawa State is a rich and fascinating destination in North Central Nigeria. It’s known for its diverse cultural heritage, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. The state offers a perfect blend of modernity and tradition. Nasarawa’s capital is Lafia, and the state’s tagline is “Home of Solid Minerals”. The state is colorful, with a unique blend of stunning landscapes, forests, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Nasarawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nasarawa is rich in history and culture, which is reflected in its various museums and historical sites, such as:

Agwai Cultural Heritage Museum

The Agwai Cultural Heritage Museum is located in Lafia, the capital of Nasarawa State. It showcases the rich cultural heritage and traditions of the people of Nasarawa State. The museum boasts of collections of diverse and unique artifacts, traditional costumes, and other historical objects.

Doma Dam

Doma Dam is a popular recreation destination in Nasarawa, where tourists go to relax and have fun. The dam’s serene environment provides visitors with an ideal spot for picnics, swimming, and boating.

Natural Attractions of Nasarawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nasarawa State offers a variety of natural attractions that let you explore and experience the state’s natural beauty.

Farin Ruwa Falls

Farin Ruwa Falls is one of the highest waterfalls in Nigeria, standing at about 150 meters high. The waterfall, which is located in Wamba Local Government Area, is an impressive sight to behold. The trek to the falls requires hiking skills and good fitness levels, but it’s well worth the effort.

Panda Forest Reserve

Panda Forest Reserve is one of the most beautiful forests in Nasarawa. It’s situated in the Panda Development Area of the state and covers about 546 square kilometers. The reserve is home to different wildlife species such as elephants, baboons, monkeys, hyenas, and antelopes. Visitors engage in outdoor activities such as camping, hiking, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nasarawa

Nasarawa State is a great location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The state offers several hiking and biking options that let you explore the natural wonders of the region.

Arafu Hills

Arafu Hills is a hilly area in the southern part of Nasarawa state, with a height of over 1000 meters above sea level. It’s one of the top hiking destinations in Nasarawa, with numerous stunning views that stretch across the surrounding businesses and communities.

Best Things to Do in Nasarawa: Activities and Tours

Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or thrill-seeker, Nasarawa state has something for everyone.

Ajaokuta Steel Company Tour

The Ajaokuta Steel Company Tour is a popular tour in Nasarawa, which lets you explore one of the largest steel plants in Africa. The tour takes you through the company’s production process and the history of the steel industry in Nigeria.

Cultural Festivals

Nasarawa State celebrates various cultural festivals throughout the year, which are a great way to learn about the state’s cultural heritage. One of the notable festivals is the New Yam Festival, which occurs annually in August.

Food and Drink Scene of Nasarawa

To understand Nasarawa, you need to taste its local cuisine. Nasarawa State serves a variety of dishes, from the traditional soups to the different kinds of meat.

Must-Try Dishes

Miyan Taushe

Miyan Taushe is a popular soup in Nasarawa that’s made from boiled pumpkin, tomato, and meat. It’s usually served with tuwo shinkafa (mashed rice).

Dambu Nama

Dambu Nama is a spiced dry-meat snack that’s popular in Nasarawa. The snack is made from shredded smoked meat mixed with spices.

Local Beverages

Kunu Aya

Kunu Aya is a refreshing drink made from tiger nuts and dates. It’s a perfect drink to relax with after a long day of outdoor activities.

Where to Stay in Nasarawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nasarawa State offers various options for accommodations, depending on your travel style and budget. Here are some of the best options:

Mariam Hotel

Mariam Hotel is located in the heart of Lafia, Nasarawa State’s capital. The hotel features clean rooms and excellent service that leaves you feeling at home.

Akwanga Guest House

Akwanga Guest House is another excellent place to stay, with affordable rooms that are clean and comfortable. It’s located close to various attractions, making it an ideal spot for tourists.

What to Buy in Nasarawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring a piece of Nasarawa State with you, here are some souvenirs and products you can buy:

Pottery

Pottery is a popular art form in Nasarawa, with locals using unique techniques to create beautiful and functional pieces that you can take home.

Woven Baskets

The state is known for its woven baskets, which are crafted from locally sourced materials. They make for excellent gifts or home décor items to remind you of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Nasarawa

Nasarawa is home to various tribes, each with its rich cultural heritage and practices. One thing that is common across the different tribes is their hospitality and friendliness to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Nasarawa

The best time to visit Nasarawa is during the dry season, between November and April. During this period, the weather is mild and pleasant, which makes outdoor activities and exploring more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nasarawa

Do:

  • Take time to learn about the various cultures and customs of Nasarawa State.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Book your accommodations in advance.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately or indecently.
  • Disrespect the locals’ cultural norms and customs.

Safety and Health

Nasarawa State is generally safe but, tourists and travelers are advised to follow the necessary safety tips.

Safety in Nasarawa

  • Be aware of your surroundings.
  • Be cautious of pickpockets and other petty crimes.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Nasarawa

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Malaria is prevalent in Nasarawa, so take preventative measures like mosquito nets, repellants, and antimalaria drugs.

Getting to Nasarawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nasarawa State is easily accessible by air, road, or rail.

Airports Near Nasarawa

The nearest airport to Nasarawa is Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport in Abuja, which is about two hours away from Lafia by road.

Bus Stations Near Nasarawa

You can get to Nasarawa by buses that run from various points in Nigeria such as Lagos, Abuja, and Kano.

Other Options

You can also travel to Nasarawa by train, with the nearest station being in Kafanchan.

How to Get Around in Nasarawa

The easiest way to get around Nasarawa is by road. Taxis, buses and tricycles (locally known as keke NAPEP) are the most common means of public transportation in Nasarawa.

Where to Go Next from Nasarawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Abuja, Nigeria’s capital city
  • Jos, the Plateau State capital and home to various landmarks and tourist attractions

Conclusion

Nasarawa State is a destination that offers something for everyone, from historical sites and culture to natural attractions and outdoor activities. With this comprehensive guide, you can now plan your trip to Nasarawa with ease and confidence, knowing what to expect and where to go.
