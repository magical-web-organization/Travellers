Lafia is a hidden gem located in the North Central region of Nigeria. While it may not be as popular as other destinations in the country, Lafia offers a unique experience for travelers looking to explore the cultural and natural attractions of Nigeria. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to Lafia, featuring top attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and more.

Introduction to Lafia

Lafia is the capital of Nasarawa State and is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. The city is surrounded by hills and valleys, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

The people of Lafia are known for their hospitality and friendly nature, and visitors are always welcome. The best time to visit Lafia is during the dry season, which runs from November to March.

List of Places to Visit in Lafia

Here are some of the top places to visit in Lafia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ta’al Cultural Complex: This museum houses various artifacts from the Doma and Lafia kingdoms and is a great place to learn about the history and culture of the area.

  • Emir’s Palace: This is the traditional residence of the Emir of Lafia and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate designs.

Natural Attractions of Lafia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Farin Ruwa Falls: This waterfall is located in Wamba Local Government Area, 120km from Lafia. It is one of the highest waterfalls in Nigeria, and the view from the top is breathtaking.

  • Awe Lake: This lake is located in Awe Local Government Area, 75km from Lafia. It is a great place to relax and enjoy the picturesque view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lafia

  • Akwanga Hills: This is a great spot for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. The hills are located approximately 45km from Lafia and offer scenic views of the surrounding area.

  • Farin Ruwa Falls: In addition to the waterfall itself, there are also several hiking trails in the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Lafia: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local markets: Lafia is known for its vibrant markets, where you can find a variety of traditional crafts, clothing, and food.

  • Take a city tour: A guided tour of Lafia is a great way to learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Lafia

Lafia is known for its delicious and traditional cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuwo Shinkafa: This is a traditional rice dish that is popular in Lafia and is usually served with soup and meat.

  • Miya Kuka: This is a soup made with powdered Baobab leaves and is a local delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Kunu: This is a non-alcoholic beverage made from millet and spices and is a popular drink in Lafia.

  • Bura: This is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet and is usually served during special occasions.

Local Culture and Customs of Lafia

Lafia is a multicultural city with several ethnic groups. The people are known for their hospitality and friendly nature. When visiting Lafia, it is important to respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Lafia

The best time to visit Lafia is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this time, the weather is pleasant and temperatures are mild.
