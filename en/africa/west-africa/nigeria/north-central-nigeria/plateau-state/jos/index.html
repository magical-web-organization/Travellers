Introduction to Jos

Located in North Central Nigeria, Jos is a bustling city that is renowned for its natural beauty, cool climate, and vibrant culture. The city boasts a rich history and is home to a diverse blend of people, including the Berom, Anaguta, and Afizere tribes. Visitors to Jos will find that it is a city that is full of surprises, with plenty of exciting attractions and activities to experience.

List of Places to Visit in Jos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in Nigerian history and culture. It features exhibits on ancient Nigerian civilizations, traditional tribal customs, and contemporary art.

  • Jos Museum: Housed in a beautiful colonial-era building, the Jos Museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts that reflect the city’s rich cultural and historical legacy.

  • Rayfield Golf Club: This historic golf club is a place where visitors can experience the city’s colonial past while enjoying a game of golf or a drink at the club’s bar.

Natural Attractions of Jos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jos Wildlife Park: This wildlife park is home to a range of African animals, including lions, leopards, and baboons. Visitors can go on a guided tour through the park, take a hike along one of the walking trails, or have a picnic in the park’s beautiful grounds.

  • Shere Hills: At over 1,800 meters, Shere Hills is the highest point around Jos, offering breathtaking views of the city, sprawling hills, and plateaus that surround it.

  • Jos Plateau: The Jos Plateau offers visitors the chance to experience the natural beauty of the region up close. Hiking, camping, and picnicking are popular activities in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jos

  • Wildlife Park Circuit: This hiking and biking trail winds through the Jos Wildlife Park, taking visitors on a journey through the park’s lush forests and scenic landscapes.

  • Shere Hills Trail: This challenging hiking and biking trail takes visitors to the top of Shere Hills, rewarding them with panoramic views of the city and surrounding countryside.

  • Jos Plateau Trail: This gentle hiking and biking trail weaves through the gentle hills and valleys of the Jos Plateau, providing visitors with a chance to appreciate the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Jos: Activities and Tours

  • City Tour: A guided city tour is a great way to experience the best of Jos. Visitors can explore historic landmarks, learn about the city’s culture and traditions, and visit local shops and markets.

  • Wildlife Safari: Taking a wildlife safari through Jos Wildlife Park is an exciting way to see some of Africa’s most iconic animals up close and in their natural habitat.

  • Cave Exploration Tour: Jos is home to a network of caves that are open to visitors. A guided tour of the caves offers insights into the geology and history of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Jos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuwo Shinkafa: A popular Northern Nigerian dish made from rice flour and served with a flavorful soup.

  • Miyan Kuka: A traditional soup made from baobab leaves, meat, and spices.

  • Suya: Thinly sliced beef, chicken or lamb skewered and grilled over an open flame, and served with spicy sauce and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus leaves, sugar, and spices.

  • Palm Wine: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented palm tree sap.

  • Kunu: A non-alcoholic drink made from millet, peanuts, and spices.

Where to Stay in Jos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rockview Hotel: A luxurious hotel that boasts stunning views of Jos’ surrounding hills and landscapes.

  • Vom Suites: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean rooms and comfortable amenities, including Wi-Fi and air conditioning.

  • Sol B&B Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Jos and offers simple rooms, friendly staff, and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Jos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery and Ceramics: Jos is known for its beautiful and intricate pottery and ceramics, which make great souvenirs.

  • Jewelry: Local artisans create unique pieces of jewelry using traditional Nigerian techniques.

  • Leather Products: Jos is home to many talented leatherworkers who create everything from bags and belts to shoes and sandals.

Local Culture and Customs of Jos

Jos is a city that is proud of its culture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of these customs, including dressing modestly, asking permission before taking photos, and being courteous to locals.

Best Time to Visit Jos

The best time to visit Jos is between October and March, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The rainy season is between April and September, and visitors should be prepared for occasional rain and thunderstorms during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jos

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites

  • Bargain when shopping in markets

Don’t:

  • Take photos without asking permission

  • Engage in public displays of affection

  • Disrespectful of local beliefs or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jos

Jos is generally a safe city for travelers, but visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. This includes avoiding walking alone at night and not flashing valuables or large sums of money around.

Health in Jos

Visitors to Jos should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and take precautions against malaria. They should also be careful when consuming food and water, sticking to bottled or boiled water and avoiding street food that may be contaminated.

Getting to Jos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jos

Jos has its airport, Yakubu Gowon Airport, which is serviced by a few national carriers. However, travelers can fly into Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport in Abuja or Mallam Aminu Kano International Airport in Kano and take a bus or car to Jos.

Bus Stations Near Jos

The popular bus stations that travellers can use for getting in and out of Jos include, Gembu Motor Park, Jos Terminus, and the Central Bus Station.

Other Options

Travelers can take a taxi or hire a private driver to get around Jos and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Jos

Taxis and private cars are the most common way to get around Jos. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to navigate the city’s streets.

Where to Go Next from Jos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abuja: The capital city of Nigeria is located just three hours from Jos and boasts a range of exciting attractions, including the National Mosque and the Nigerian National Museum.

  • Kagoro Hills: Located less than an hour from Jos, the Kagoro Hills offer visitors the chance to explore ancient rock art and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Conclusion

With its natural beauty, rich culture, and exciting attractions, Jos is a destination worth adding to your travel itinerary. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to this fascinating Nigerian city.
