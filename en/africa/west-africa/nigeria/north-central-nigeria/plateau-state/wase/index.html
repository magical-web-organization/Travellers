Introduction to Wase

Wase is a historical city located in Plateau State, Nigeria, known for its rich cultural and natural heritage. The city serves as a gateway to several natural attractions, including waterfalls, forests, hills, and valleys.

With its welcoming people and pristine natural beauty, Wase is a travel destination that should not be missed. This travel guide will highlight some of the top attractions, activities, and cultural experiences to enjoy in this enchanting city.

List of Places to Visit in Wase

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wase Rock: One of the top tourist attractions in Wase, the Wase Rock is a towering rock formation that serves as a symbol of the city’s natural beauty. The site features intriguing legends and myths and is considered a sacred spot by the locals.

  • The Emir’s Palace: Built-in the 18th century, the Emir’s Palace is a historical and cultural landmark in Wase. The palace features traditional architecture, elaborate furnishings, and colorful gardens.

  • Ungwar Noh Culture and Heritage Center: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of Wase and its surrounding areas. Featuring artworks, traditional clothing, and other artifacts, visitors can learn more about the customs, traditions, and history of the people of Wase.

Natural Attractions of Wase: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Kwall Falls: The Kwall Falls is a breathtaking waterfall located on the Jos Plateau. Surrounded by lush vegetation, the falls offer an ideal spot for picnics and relaxation.

  • The Pandam Game Reserve: The Pandam Game Reserve is a wildlife sanctuary located in Wase. The reserve is home to various species of animals, including baboons, hyenas, antelopes, and more. Visitors can take a guided tour of the reserve and view the animals in their natural habitat.

  • Lamingo Lake: The Lamingo Lake is a stunning natural lake that serves as a hotspot for tourists. The lake offers a serene environment for relaxation and views of different bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wase

  • Kumbul-ei Hills: Kumbul-ei Hills is a scenic location located about 15 kilometers from Wase town. The destination is suitable for tourists who want to engage in some physical activities like hiking, biking, and sightseeing.

  • Langai Hills: Langai Hills are located near Wase Bakin Rijiya. The potential visitor would require a trekking guide to get there, and the hills are suitable for tourists who love outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Wase: Activities and Tours

  • Cultural Tour of Wase: Take a guided tour of Wase and learn more about the local culture, traditions, and customs of the people.

  • Waterfall Adventure: Adrenaline-junkies can take a thrilling tour of the Kwall Falls and try out exciting activities like swimming, rafting, and hiking.

  • Wildlife Tour: Visitors can take a guided wildlife tour of the Pandam Game Reserve and view different species of animals in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wase

Day 1: Arrival in Wase and visit to the Emir’s Palace

Day 2: Trip to the Kwall Falls and swim/rafting activities

Day 3: Wildlife tour of the Pandam Game Reserve

Day 4: Cultural and Heritage tour of Wase

Day 5: Visit to the Wase Rock

Day 6: Hiking/biking tour of the Langai Hills

Day 7: Relaxation at the Lamingo Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Wase

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuwo Shinkafa: A local delicacy made from rice and enjoyed with various soups and stews

  • Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup: Made from yam and melon seeds, this dish is a popular choice in Wase

  • Miyan Taush: A flavorful soup made with groundnut paste and meat

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers

  • Palm Wine: An alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees

Where to Stay in Wase: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Wase can find a range of accommodation options, including:

  • Kati-Lom Hotel and Suites: A modern hotel located in Wase offering different guest room choices, a relaxing environment, and maximum customer satisfaction.

  • Born-Sunrise Hotel: This hotel is situated at Wase’s heart and offers comfortable and affordable accommodation, especially for tourists and families.

What to Buy in Wase: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kalabash: An intricately designed gourd used for drinking and serving beverages in traditional settings.

  • Traditional Weaving: Hand-woven fabrics and clothing that showcase the unique designs and patterns of the Wase people

Local Culture and Customs of Wase

The people of Wase are known for their rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in their music, art, and traditional festivals. Visitors to Wase can participate in cultural events and experience the warmth and hospitality of the local people.

Best Time to Visit Wase

The best time to visit Wase is from October to March when the climatic conditions are mild and conducive for outdoor activities and tourism.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wase

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions

  • Dress modestly

  • Carry enough drinking water during tours and hikes

Don’t:

  • Consume illicit drugs

  • Disrespect the local culture or people

Safety and Health

Safety in Wase

Although Wase is relatively peaceful, visitors are advised to take precautions and avoid moving at night. Also, tourists are advised to avoid walking alone in isolated areas.

Health in Wase

Visitors to Wase are advised to get vaccinated against diseases such as yellow fever, typhoid, and Hepatitis A and B. It’s also important to carry appropriate medication and to follow the recommended safety precautions while on tours and hikes.

Getting to Wase: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wase

Bus Stations Near Wase

Other Options

  • Chartered Cars or Taxis

How to Get Around in Wase

Visitors can move around Wase town in Taxis or Keke Napep (tricycles).

Where to Go Next from Wase: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the neighboring cities and towns, including Jos, Lafia, and Akwanga, to experience more of the region’s cultural and natural heritage.

Conclusion

Wase is a hidden gem worth exploring by travelers who enjoy cultural tourism and outdoor activities. From its natural attractions to its rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone in Wase. This comprehensive travel guide will help make your visit to Wase memorable and enjoyable.
