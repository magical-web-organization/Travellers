Plateau State is a North-Central region in Nigeria, known for its scenic landscapes, outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, and vibrant local culture. From museums to hiking routes, this travel guide will delve into the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay when traveling to Plateau State.

Introduction to Plateau State

Located in the middle belt of Nigeria, Plateau State is called the “Home of Peace and Tourism” due to its diverse cultural heritage and abundance of natural resources.

The capital of Plateau State is Jos, a city that sits at an elevation of 1,238 meters above sea level. This high altitude makes Jos a popular destination for holidaymakers seeking cooler weather and a relaxed atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Plateau State

From museums to natural landmarks, Plateau State offers travelers a range of attractions to visit. Here are some of the must-see places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum Jos: home to the largest collection of Nok terracottas in Nigeria, and a range of indigenous art and crafts.
  • Jos Museum & Zoo: This museum showcases the cultural heritage of Plateau State, featuring traditional clothing, arts, and crafts. The onsite zoo offers a chance to see over 800 animal species from around the world.
  • Riyom Rock Formation: A stunning natural wonder located in Riyom Local Government Area, Riyom Rock Formation is a must-see for outdoor enthusiasts and geologists.

Natural Attractions of Plateau State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shere Hills: These hills offer spectacular views of the surrounding countryside, and a range of outdoor activities including hiking, camping, and bird watching.
  • Assop Falls: Located in the Jos East Local Government Area, these falls offer an idyllic spot to swim, have a picnic or take photographs.
  • Pandam game reserve: Spanning over 233 km², this game reserve offers an unforgettable wildlife experience with over 30 animal species to see including lions, elephants, and buffalos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plateau State

Plateau State is home to several epic hiking and biking routes that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Farin Ruwa Falls Route: This route takes you to the popular Farin Ruwa Falls, and is perfect for scenic hiking and photography.
  • Riyom Rock Route: Climb the stunning rock formations of Riyom, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Jos Wildlife Park Route: This route takes you through the Jos Wildlife Park, where you can spot a range of wildlife species including baboons, elephants, and monkeys.

Best Things to Do in Plateau State: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Plateau State, from cultural experiences to wildlife safaris. Here are some of the best:

  • Village Tour: Explore local cultures and lifestyles by taking a guided tour of a traditional village.
  • Bird Watching: With over 800 bird species in Plateau State, bird watching is a popular activity for nature lovers.
  • Safari Tours: Take a wildlife safari tour through the Pandam Game Reserve or the Jos Wildlife Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plateau State

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for your visit to Plateau State:

Day 1: Arrival in Jos, check-in at your hotel and relax. Day 2: Explore Jos Museum & Zoo, and visit the National Museum Jos. Day 3: Hike Riyom Rock formation, and visit other nearby attractions such as Kurra Falls or the B-Shere Hills. Day 4: Visit the Pandam Game Reserve for a wildlife safari tour. Day 5: Take a tour of a traditional village and learn about the local cultures and lifestyles. Day 6: Visit Assop Falls and explore other nearby attractions such as the Wase Rock and the Jos Wildlife Park. Day 7: Departure from Jos

Food and Drink Scene of Plateau State

Plateau State is well-known for its delicious culinary offerings, from stews to soups and refreshing beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yam Porridge: A hearty dish made from yam, tomatoes, onions, and seasoning.
  • Nkwocha: A traditional soup made with local ingredients such as okra, beans, and ugu.
  • Jollof Rice: A popular West African dish made with flavored rice, tomato sauce, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Kunu: A refreshing drink made from groundnuts, millet, or rice.
  • Palm Wine: A fermented drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Zobo: A non-alcoholic drink made from the hibiscus plant, often served with spices and sugar.

Where to Stay in Plateau State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Plateau State, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hillstation Hotel and Resort: Located in Jos, Hillstation offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Panda Guest House: A budget guesthouse located in Jos, with a range of amenities such as free Wi-Fi and breakfast.
  • Rockview Hotel: A luxurious hotel in Jos, featuring an onsite restaurant, a pool, and a spa.

What to Buy in Plateau State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Plateau State is famous for its arts and crafts, from pottery to textiles and jewelry. Here are some of the local products to bring back home as souvenirs:

  • Pottery: Choose from a range of beautifully crafted pottery products, such as vases, jars, and bowls.
  • Textiles: Pick up some locally woven fabrics, such as the popular Asoke or Adire cloth.
  • Handicrafts: Choose from a range of handmade crafts, such as beaded jewelry, wood carvings or sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Plateau State

Plateau State is home to a variety of ethnic groups and languages. The dominant group is the Berom people, who are known for their agricultural practices and festive celebrations. Visitors should dress modestly, respect local customs and traditions, and seek permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Plateau State

The best time to visit Plateau State is between October and February when the weather is cool and dry. The rest of the year can be humid and wet, with occasional rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plateau State

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local foods and beverages.
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of locals.
  • Explore the natural landmarks of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t wander off alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural or cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plateau State

Plateau State is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions. It is advisable to avoid traveling to certain areas in the region due to occasional instances of violence and unrest.

Health in Plateau State

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as malaria. It’s also advisable to carry any necessary medications, and drink bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Plateau State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plateau State

The nearest airport to Plateau State is Jos International Airport, located in the capital city of Jos. The airport is served by several airlines, including Arik Air, Dana Air, and Air Peace.

Bus Stations Near Plateau State

There are several bus stations in Jos and the neighboring cities of Bauchi and Abuja. Some of the popular bus companies operating in the region include God Is Good Motors, ABC Transport, and Young Shall Grow Motors.

Other Options

Taxis and car rental services are also available in Jos and the neighboring cities, and can be arranged through hotels or online services. However, visitors should be cautious when taking taxis and hire only reputable drivers.

How to Get Around in Plateau State

Visitors can get around Plateau State using taxis, private hire cars, or by renting a car or motorbike. Motorcycles are also common in the region and offer affordable and convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Plateau State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Plateau State is located in the middle belt of Nigeria, and there are several cities and destinations in the region that are worth a visit. Here are some of the popular nearby destinations:

  • Abuja: The capital city of Nigeria, known for its modern architecture, museums, and restaurants.
  • Bauchi: A historic city, home to several cultural landmarks, including the Yankari National Park and Tafawa Balewa Tomb.
  • Kaduna: A vibrant commercial city, with a range of attractions, including art galleries, museums, and a wildlife park.

Conclusion

Plateau State offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. From hiking trails to wildlife safaris and traditional village tours, there’s something for everyone in this North-Central region of Nigeria. With this guide, you’re well equipped to plan your trip to Plateau State and make the most out of your visit.
