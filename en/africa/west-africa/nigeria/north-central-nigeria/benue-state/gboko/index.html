Introduction to Gboko

Gboko is a small town located in the Benue State of Nigeria, in North Central Nigeria. It is a culturally rich town with a population of approximately 263,065 people. Gboko enjoys a semi-tropical climate with wet and dry seasons. The town is known for its agricultural prowess, producing crops such as yam, cassava, and maize.

Gboko is an unexplored tourist destination, but the town has a lot to offer to visitors, from historical monuments to natural landmarks, cultural artifacts to culinary delights. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, what to buy, and more in Gboko.

Best Things to Do in Gboko: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the cultural museum and learn about the rich history and culture of the Tiv people.
  • Take a hike through the scenic trails of the Tiv Waterfalls, Agena Hills, Gboko Hills, Otupko Forest Reserve, and Tunga Farmland.
  • Explore the Katsina Ala River located in Tunga Village and enjoy a refreshing swim or boat ride.
  • Visit the Tomb of Mbakunu and learn about the legend of the deceased’s heroic feats.
  • Experience the scenic views of the town at the Ikwe Holiday Resort.

Local Culture and Customs of Gboko

The Tiv people of Gboko have a rich cultural heritage that they proudly preserve. They are skilled farmers who produce fresh crops from the fertile farmlands. Visitors will notice their unique customs and traditions as they tour the town, and they will discover the remarkable friendliness of the people.

Best Time to Visit Gboko

The best time to visit Gboko is between November and February, as the weather is cool and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gboko

Do:

  • Respect the cultural heritage and the customs of the Tiv people.
  • Go with a tour guide when exploring natural landmarks.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Take pictures without permission from locals.
  • Disrespect the Tiv culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gboko

Gboko is a relatively safe town, but like any other place, visitors should exercise caution when traveling. Tourists are encouraged to leave expensive belongings in their hotel rooms, stay in public places, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Gboko

Visitors should be up to date on their routine vaccinations and take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid malaria.

Getting to Gboko: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gboko

The closest airport is the Makurdi Airport, located 44 km away from Gboko.

Bus Stations Near Gboko

Visitors can catch buses from Makurdi’s Jolly B Motor Park straight to Gboko.

Other Options

Taxis and minivans are popular modes of transport around Gboko.

How to Get Around in Gboko

Visitors can take taxis, motorbikes, minivans, or bike rides to navigate around Gboko easily.

Conclusion

Gboko is a beautiful and captivating town with so much to offer. Tourists will not regret taking their time to explore this hidden gem in the heart of West Africa.
