Introduction to Makurdi

Makurdi, the capital city of Benue State, is situated in the North Central Region of Nigeria. Nicknamed the “Food Basket of the Nation,” Makurdi is a bustling metropolis, known for its diverse culture, rich history, and scenic beauty. The city boasts of a blend of traditional and modern architecture, offering visitors a unique cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Makurdi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. The National Museum of Benue: The National Museum is located in the city center and has a collection of traditional artifacts, including crafts, masks, and pottery.

2. The Makurdi Central Mosque: The mosque is a beautiful piece of architecture and one of the oldest structures in the city.

3. Tor Tiv Palace: The traditional home of the Tiv king, Tor Tiv Palace, is an excellent spot to experience traditional culture and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Makurdi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Gboko Lagoon: A natural ecosystem with a diverse range of birds and fish, Gboko Lagoon is an ideal spot for picnics, fishing, boating, and other outdoor activities.

2. Naka Hills: Naka Hills is the perfect spot for hiking and offers beautiful views of the city.

3. Solomon Lar Amusement Park: This park offers a range of outdoor activities, including go-karting, zip lining, paintball, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Makurdi

1. Naka Hills: The trail at Naka Hills offers a 3-mile hike through beautiful scenery and takes about 1-2 hours to complete.

2. Gboko Lagoon: The trail around Gboko Lagoon offers a beautiful and serene hiking experience around the water body.

3. Wurukum Hills: The Wurukum Hill is an excellent spot for exploring the natural beauty of the city on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Makurdi: Activities and Tours

1. Explore Akure Ranch: Akure Ranch offers an opportunity to experience a safari without leaving the city. Visitors can see herds of cattle and antelopes on a horseback tour of the ranch.

2. Try Local Cuisine: A culinary tour of Makurdi isn’t complete without trying the popular local dishes like “Pounded yam and Egusi soup,” “Gbegiri soup and Amala,” and “Tiv Kpamin.”

3. Visit the Artisan Market: The artisan market in Makurdi is famous for its traditional crafts and artwork, including carvings, beadwork, and mats.

Food and Drink Scene in Makurdi

Must-Try Dishes

1. Pounded yam and Egusi soup: Pounded yam is a popular staple made from yam and served with Egusi Soup, which is made from finely ground melon seeds.

2. Gbegiri soup and Amala: Gbegiri soup is made from beans, and Amala is a type of dough made from yam. The combination is a common local delicacy.

3. Tiv Kpamin: A Tiv dish made from brown beans and served with yam, Tiv Kpamin is a must-try in Makurdi.

Local Beverages

1. Kunun Aya: A refreshing drink made from tiger nuts, Kunun Aya is a non-alcoholic sweet beverage and a local favorite.

2. Palm Wine: A popular alcoholic beverage made from palm tree sap, Palm Wine is a must-try for visitors to Makurdi.

Where to Stay in Makurdi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Benue Hotels and Resort: A luxury hotel located just outside the city, Benue Hotels and Resort offer well-furnished rooms and excellent service.

2. Clearview Hotels and Suites: A budget-friendly option located in the city center, Clearview Hotels and Suites offer clean rooms and basic amenities.

3. Hotel Rosebud Limited: A mid-range hotel located near the city center, Hotel Rosebud Limited offers comfortable rooms and good service.

What to Buy in Makurdi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Traditional Crafts: Makurdi is famous for its traditional craftsmanship, including carvings, beadwork, and mats.

2. Tiv Cloth: Tiv Cloth is a traditional fabric made from locally sourced cotton and is a popular souvenir in Makurdi.

3. Coffee and Spices: Benue State is known for its coffee and spices, which make for an excellent souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Makurdi

Makurdi is a culturally diverse city with a significant population of Tiv, Idoma, and other ethnic groups. Visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious or cultural monuments.

Best Time to Visit Makurdi

The best time to visit Makurdi is between December and February when the weather is mild and dry. The rainy season runs from March to November, and the roads may be impassable during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Makurdi

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the local culture and heritage

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission
  • Disrespect cultural or religious monuments
  • Travel alone at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Makurdi

Makurdi is generally safe for visitors. However, there have been reported cases of petty theft, so visitors are advised to be cautious with their belongings.

Health in Makurdi

Make sure to drink only bottled water and use mosquito repellent to protect against malaria.

Getting to Makurdi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Makurdi

Makurdi Airport is located about 10 kilometers from the city center and serves domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Makurdi

Makurdi has a central bus station, where travelers can find buses to various cities in Nigeria.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Makurdi

Makurdi has several transportation options, including taxis, buses, and motorcycles. Taxis are the most popular and convenient mode of transportation in the city.

Where to Go Next from Makurdi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can head to Jos, the capital city of Plateau State, known for its magnificent waterfalls, rock formations, and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Makurdi offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience with its blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and urban charm. Plan your trip to this hidden gem in North Central Nigeria with our comprehensive guide.
