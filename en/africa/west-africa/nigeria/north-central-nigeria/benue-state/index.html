Introduction to Benue State

Nestled in the heart of Nigeria, Benue State is a breathtaking destination that boasts an incredible blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. This North Central state, bordered by Nassarawa, Taraba, Cross River, Ebonyi, Enugu, Kogi, and Abuja (the Federal Capital Territory), is known for its agricultural prowess, with the famous Benue River running through it, which is the reason for its moniker, “The Food Basket of the Nation.”

The state is home to a rich cultural heritage, vibrant markets, natural wonders, and warm, welcoming people, making it an excellent destination for travelers looking to explore Nigeria beyond the bustling metropolises. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, Benue State has something to offer everyone. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Benue State

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Benue State:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ikyogen Hills: This stunning hill, located in Kwande Local Government Area, is surrounded by myths and legends that trace back to the Tiv people. The hills were once a fortress for warriors, with six natural caves used as hideouts for the fighters.
  • Benue State University Zoo: Located in Makurdi, the Benue State University Zoo is home to a variety of animals, including lions, elephants, antelopes, and many more. Take a guided tour or explore the zoo’s trails by yourself.

Natural Attractions of Benue State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kucha Hills: These hills are located in Tarka Local Government Area and offer a breathtaking view of the Benue River. The hills are a favorite spot for hiking and picnicking, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Gbeji Hills: If you’re looking for a challenging hike, Gbeji Hills is the place to be. This location is a fantastic spot to explore local wildlife and to immerse yourself in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benue State

Benue State has some fantastic hiking and biking routes that provide an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the state.

  • Mount Okwale: Located in Konshisha Local Government Area, Mount Okwale is the highest peak in Benue State, standing at over 2,435 feet. Hikers must be prepared for a strenuous hike, but the views from the Summit are worth it.
  • Gbeji Hills Trail: With a distance of over 15 kilometers, this trail is suitable for both hiking and biking. Along the way, you will be able to explore natural wonders and local culture.

Best Things to Do in Benue State: Activities and Tours

From wildlife viewing to cultural immersion, there’s something for everyone in Benue State.

  • Tiv Dance: The Tiv dance is a popular cultural dance in Benue State, and learning the dance moves can be a fun and unique experience.
  • ** Idoma Boat Regatta:** This cultural festival, held annually during the Easter period, is a celebration of the Idoma people’s rich heritage, with boat races on the Benue River, masked dancers, traditional musicians, and local delicacies on offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Benue State

Benue State is widely known for its agricultural prowess, and as such, the state boasts a range of tasty local cuisine, from delicious soups to mouth-watering snacks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikwokirikwo: This is a traditional Tiv soup containing a variety of fresh vegetables and assorted meats. The dish is cooked in a clay pot and has a unique taste.
  • Swallow with Efere: This locally made soup is paired with yam or cassava flour and is traditional food among the Idoma people.

Local Beverages

  • Pito: Made from fermented millet or sorghum, this locally made beer is refreshing and has a unique taste.
  • Tamarind Juice: This refreshing drink is made from the sap of the tamarind tree, and it’s perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Benue State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Benue State, which cater to all budgets.

  • Hotel Benue: Located in the heart of Makurdi, Hotel Benue is a luxury hotel with top-notch amenities.
  • Benue Guest House: This guest house in Otukpo offers cozy rooms at an affordable price point.
  • Gidi Hotel Limited: This hotel, located near the downtown area of Makurdi, is a great option for travelers looking for affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Benue State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From traditional crafts to agricultural products, here are a few souvenirs you shouldn’t miss out on:

  • Crafts: Benue State is known for its traditional pottery, wood carving, and weaving products that make excellent souvenirs.
  • Agricultural Products: Being the food basket of the nation, Benue State is well-known for its agricultural products such as yams, nuts, cassava, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Benue State

Benue State boasts a rich and diverse culture, with over 30 ethnic groups residing in the state. The Tiv, Idoma, and Igede are the main ethnic groups, each with their unique cultural practices and traditions, including their music, dance, and art.

When interacting with locals, it’s essential to be respectful and courteous. Consider learning a few words of the local language, and wear appropriate clothing when visiting cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Benue State

The best time to visit Benue State is between November and March, when the weather is mild and the skies are clear. It’s essential to avoid visiting during the rainy season, which runs from June to October, as heavy rainfalls can make outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Benue State

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural sites.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Do not engage in illegal activities.
  • Do not disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Do not litter the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Benue State

Benue State is relatively safe for tourists, but it’s essential to take precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Benue State

It’s recommended that tourists visiting Benue State take necessary health precautions, such as being up to date with vaccinations and taking measures to avoid malaria.

Getting to Benue State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Benue State is accessible by road, air, and rail.

Airports Near Benue State

The nearest airport to Benue State is the Makurdi Airport, which receives daily flight from Abuja and Lagos.

Bus Stations Near Benue State

Benue State is accessible by bus, with several bus companies providing daily services to various locations.

  • ABC Transport
  • Park Way Transport Service
  • God Is Good Motors Limited

Other Options

For travelers seeking a more personalized experience, hiring a car is an excellent option to explore Benue State and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Benue State

There are several options for getting around Benue State, including taxis, buses, and private cars. Taxis are the most convenient option, offering both shared and private rides.

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Benue State is a destination that should be on the top of your travel list.
