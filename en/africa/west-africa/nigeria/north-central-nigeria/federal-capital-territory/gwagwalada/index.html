Located in the Federal Capital Territory of Nigeria, Gwagwalada is a hidden gem that offers plenty of activities and attractions for travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve put together everything you need to know to make the most out of your trip.

Introduction to Gwagwalada

Gwagwalada is a growing city that boasts a rich history, vibrant culture, and breathtaking landscapes. The city is located in the northern part of Nigeria, in the West African region, and serves as a hub for nearby towns and villages. The area is known for its agricultural productivity, with yam and cassava being its major crops. Additionally, Gwagwalada also has several archaeological sites where ancient artefacts are found. The city is easily accessible from Abuja, which makes it a great destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Gwagwalada

There are several places to visit and things to do in Gwagwalada that reflect its rich historical and cultural heritage as well as natural wonders. Here are some worth mentioning:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gwagwalada Central Mosque: This mosque is an excellent example of modern Islamic architecture and is one of the largest mosques in Nigeria. The mosque is a symbol of the city’s devotion to the Muslim faith.
  • Usman Danfodio University Archaeological Museum: This museum showcases ancient artefacts such as pottery, weapons, textiles, and other relics from Nigeria’s past.
  • The Sahara Desert Dust Plume Observatory: Located within the Nigerian Meteorological Agency’s premises, this place is a hub for research and study of dust storms in the Saharan region, which affects West Africa annually.

Natural Attractions of Gwagwalada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Giri Hills: This is a mountain range located in the southwestern part of Gwagwalada. The hills offer a scenic view of the city as well as an opportunity to hike.
  • Gurara Waterfalls: One of the hidden gems of Gwagwalada, Gurara Waterfalls is a stunning natural attraction located in the nearby town of Suleja. The waterfalls offer a calm and serene environment for visitors to relax or go for a refreshing swim.
  • Gamji National Park: This national park is located in nearby Minna and is home to several species of wildlife such as elephants, giraffes, and baboons. The park is perfect for hiking, picnicking, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gwagwalada

Gwagwalada is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts as the city is surrounded by hills and mountains that offer stunning views of the city and beyond. Some popular hiking and biking routes in and around Gwagwalada are:

  • Giri Hills Hike: This hike is perfect for beginners and offers an easy climb to the top of Giri Hills, from which visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the city.
  • Shere Hills Bike Trail: This is an intense bike trail that runs from Jos to Bauchi and passes through Gwagwalada. The trail offers a challenging yet exciting experience for cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Gwagwalada: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the city’s cultural and natural attractions, Gwagwalada has several activities and tours that travelers can do. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Visit Dutsen-Alhaji Market: This market is an excellent place to experience the local culture and buy souvenirs such as textiles, jewellery, and art.
  • Take a food tour: Nigerian cuisine is known for its spice, variety and flavors. Take a food tour and try different local dishes such as Suya, Jollof rice, and Akara.
  • Visit nearby towns and villages: Gwagwalada is surrounded by several towns and villages that offer a glimpse into the country’s rural life. These towns include Zuba, Sheda, and Tungan-Maje among others.

Food and Drink Scene of Gwagwalada

Nigerian cuisine is diverse, and Gwagwalada is no exception. Here are some of the best dishes and beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suya: This is a local delicacy made of grilled meat that is marinated in spices such as ginger and garlic.
  • Jollof Rice: This is a staple dish in Nigeria that is made of rice, tomatoes, onions, and spices.
  • Akara: This is a fried bean cake that is usually served as a breakfast dish.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This drink is made from the sap of palm trees and is a popular drink in Nigeria.
  • Zobo: This is a refreshing drink made of hibiscus flowers, sugar, and other spices.

  • Bernice Guest House: This guesthouse is perfect for budget travelers and offers basic amenities such as a TV, Wi-Fi, and a private bathroom.

What to Buy in Gwagwalada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs you can buy in Gwagwalada that reflect the city’s rich culture and heritage. Some unique items you can buy include:

  • Kente Fabric: This is a colorful and vibrant fabric that is commonly worn in West Africa, and is popular in Nigeria.
  • Wooden masks: These masks are a symbol of the city’s cultural heritage and make for great wall decorations.
  • Local art: Gwagwalada is home to several artists who produce unique and modern art pieces that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Gwagwalada

Gwagwalada is a community that values its cultural heritage and customs. Visitors should respect local customs such as dressing conservatively, removing their shoes before entering mosques and temples, and refraining from public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Gwagwalada

The best time to visit Gwagwalada is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. The weather during this time is mild, with clear skies and little rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gwagwalada

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local cuisine.
  • Bargain when shopping for souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Display affection publicly.
  • Disrespect religious sites and customs.
  • Visit during the rainy season (April to October) without proper gear.

Safety and Health

As with all travel destinations, it’s important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings when in Gwagwalada. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Gwagwalada

  • Stay in well-lit areas especially at night.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables close and avoid wearing expensive jewelry or watches in public.

Health in Gwagwalada

  • Drink bottled water or water that has been boiled or disinfected.
  • Wear protective clothing and use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito bites, which could lead to malaria.
  • Seek medical attention if you fall ill.

The best way to get around Gwagwalada is by taxi, which can be found easily around the city. The city is relatively compact, so walking or biking is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Gwagwalada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Gwagwalada. Here are some worth considering:

  • Zuma Rock: This natural formation is located in nearby Niger State and is known for its incredible height and beauty.
  • Abuja: The capital city of Nigeria is only a short distance away and is home to several attractions such as the National Mosque and Aso Rock.
  • Minna: This city is in Niger state and is home to several natural attractions such as the Gurara Waterfalls and the Gamji National Park.

Conclusion

Gwagwalada is an incredible travel destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning landscapes, welcoming locals, and rich cultural heritage, Gwagwalada is a destination that should not be missed. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, food, or just relaxing, Gwagwalada has something for everyone.
