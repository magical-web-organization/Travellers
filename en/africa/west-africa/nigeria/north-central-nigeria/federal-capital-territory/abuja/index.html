Abuja Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Abuja

Located in Federal Capital Territory in North Central Nigeria, Abuja is the capital city of Nigeria. It is a modern city with a rich cultural heritage, and it is also one of the most developed cities in Africa. Abuja is the perfect place to visit for history lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and foodies alike. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options and logistics, and nearby destinations and excursions in Abuja.

Local Culture and Customs of Abuja

Abuja has a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to show respect to the local traditions and customs while visiting the city. Here are some local customs to keep in mind while visiting Abuja:

  1. Dress appropriately

Best Time to Visit Abuja

The best time to visit Abuja is in the dry season from November to March, which is the best time for outdoor adventures and sightseeing. The rainy season from May to October can be challenging for some activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abuja

When visiting Abuja, it is essential to be aware of local customs and follow the basic etiquette for the country. Here are some do’s and don’ts of Abuja:

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately
  2. Respect local customs and traditions
  3. Use the left hand for only toilet functions
  4. Smile and be friendly

Don’t:

  1. Touch people’s heads without their consent
  2. Drink alcohol in public
  3. Eat with your left hand
  4. Photograph government buildings or officials without permission

Safety in Abuja
