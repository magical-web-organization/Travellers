Introduction to Kebbi State

Kebbi State is one of the states in the North West region of Nigeria. Known as the “Land of Equity,” Kebbi state is a serene and peaceful state with a rich cultural, historical and natural heritage. The state is home to several tourist attractions that offer unique experiences, such as hiking, biking, outdoor adventures, and cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Kebbi State

If you are planning to visit Kebbi state, you should plan your itinerary wisely. Here’s a list of places to visit in Kebbi State to get the most out of your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kebbi State is steeped in rich cultural and historical heritage. A visit to the prominent museums and historical places in the state will give you an insight into the state’s past and present.

  • Argungu Fishing Festival: This festival is one of the most significant cultural events in the state, and it is held annually to celebrate the rich and diverse culture of the people of Kebbi state.
  • Kanta Museum: Here, you’ll find artifacts, relics, and documentation from the pre-colonial and colonial era of the state.
  • Emir’s Palace: Home to the emir of Gwandu, one of the prominent traditional rulers in Kebbi state. The palace offers a glimpse of the state’s traditional architecture and serves as a significant cultural and historical landmark.

Natural Attractions of Kebbi State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kebbi state’s natural attractions offer visitors the chance to explore scenic views and engage in outdoor activities that offer a unique experience.

  • Kambari Forest Reserve: This reserve features savannah woodland and grassland that is home to an array of wildlife like baboons, antelopes, and birds.
  • Mungo Park Cenotaph: This cenotaph is in commemoration of an explorer who discovered the River Niger. It offers a breathtaking view of the river.
  • Girmache Shrine: This natural rock formation is located in Aleiro town in Kebbi state, and it’s a significant cultural and spiritual landmark.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kebbi State

Kebbi state’s unique terrain offers hiking and biking enthusiasts various options to explore the natural beauty of the state. Here are the top hiking/biking routes in Kebbi State:

  • Kambari Forest Reserve Hiking Trail: This trail is perfect for those who want to hike and bird-watch. It passes through the forest reserve, allowing you to take in the beautiful scenery and wildlife.
  • Gwazunu Caves Biking Trail: This trail takes you through the scenic landscape of Kebbi state and passes through Gwazunu Caves. It’s one of the most adventurous bike trails in the state.

Best Things to Do in Kebbi State: Activities and Tours

Kebbi state offers visitors the chance to participate in various activities and tours, allowing them to immerse themselves in the state’s culture and natural heritage.

  • Argungu Fishing Festival Tour: This tour is perfect for those who want to experience the state’s cultural heritage. The festival features a boat race and fish-catching competition, cultural dances, and local crafts.
  • Kambari Forest Reserve Safari Tour: This tour allows tourists to explore the Kambari Reserve, a protected forest reserve that houses various animal species.
  • Kangigi Rocks Trekking Tour: This tour involves trekking through the beautiful Kangigi Rocks, surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, flora, and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Kebbi State

Kebbi state’s food and drink scene is a reflection of its diverse cultural heritage, with northern and southern influenced dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuwo Shinkafa: A northern Nigerian dish, Tuwo is made from rice flour and is usually eaten with a soup, stew or sauce.
  • Fura da Nono: This is a popular Northern Nigerian drink made from cow’s milk and millet.
  • Suya: This is a popular Northern Nigerian snack made from beef, chicken, or fish, marinated in a spicy sauce and grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Zobo: A refreshing drink made from the dried flowers of the Roselle plant, usually served chilled.
  • Gari/Naussa: A drink made from fermented grains, usually millet or sorghum. It’s a popular drink in Northern Nigeria.

Where to Stay in Kebbi State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kebbi state has several accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly options to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the recommended options for tourists:

  • Riba Hotels, Birnin Kebbi: A luxury hotel located in the central business district of Birnin Kebbi.
  • Secured Court Hotel, Birnin Kebbi: A budget-friendly hotel located close to the central market and other attractions in Birnin Kebbi.
  • Awawa Hotel and Suites, Yauri: A comfortable option located in Yauri, with several amenities for tourists.

What to Buy in Kebbi State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Kebbi state can take home unique souvenirs and local products as a reminder of their visit to the state. Here are some popular options:

  • Baba Ngege Hat: A traditional hat worn by the people in Kebbi state.
  • Kakaki: A traditional musical instrument made from brass or iron.
  • Kebbi State Fabric: Unique and colorful fabrics made by local artisans in Kebbi state.

Local Culture and Customs of Kebbi State

Kebbi state is renowned for its vibrant and diverse culture. The people of Kebbi state are hospitable and friendly, and they hold their cultural values and customs in high esteem.

Some of the customs and traditions include:

  • Hospitality: The people of Kebbi state are hospitable, and visitors are usually welcomed warmly.
  • Conservative Dressing: While Kebbi state is relatively liberal in its dressing culture, visitors are advised to dress conservatively to show respect for the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kebbi State

The best time to visit Kebbi state is between October and April. During this period, the weather is pleasant, and there are numerous events and festivals to attend.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kebbi State

Here are some essential guidelines to adhere to while in Kebbi state:

Do:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting cultural or religious sites.
  • Try the traditional dishes and drinks of the state.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Get involved in any form of public disturbance as the state promotes a peaceful and serene environment.

Safety and Health

It’s essential to stay safe and healthy while visiting Kebbi state.

Safety in Kebbi State

Kebbi state is generally a safe place to visit. Still, visitors are advised to exercise caution while traveling and avoid traveling at night.

Health in Kebbi State

Visitors to Kebbi state are advised to take necessary vaccinations and be cautious of their eating and drinking habits. They should also adhere to hygiene practices to avoid any food or water-borne diseases.

Getting to Kebbi State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kebbi state is accessible by various means of transportation, including road, air, and water.

Airports Near Kebbi State

  • Sir Ahmadu Bello International Airport, Sokoto: Located in the neighboring Sokoto state, this airport is the closest airport to Kebbi state.

Bus Stations Near Kebbi State

  • God is Good Motors, Birnin Kebbi: A popular bus station located in Birnin Kebbi.
  • ABC Transport, Yauri: A reliable bus station located in Yauri.

Other Options

  • Waterway: Visitors can travel to Kebbi state by water through the River Niger.

How to Get Around in Kebbi State

The best way to get around Kebbi state is by car or taxi. Visitors can also hire a bike or bicycle to explore the state’s natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kebbi State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kebbi state is well-located for visitors to explore the neighboring states and countries. Recommended nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Sokoto State: Sokoto state is home to fascinating cultural and historical landmarks, including the Shehu Palace, Sokoto Museum, and the Goronyo Dam.
  • Niger Republic: Visitors can explore the neighboring Niger Republic, known for its stunning natural beauty, such as the Air Mountains, the W National Park, and the Niger River.

Conclusion

Kebbi state is an extraordinary destination with diverse natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage and friendly locals. Visitors can immerse themselves in the state’s culture by trying the local dishes, buying souvenirs, and exploring the natural beauty of the state. By following the guides and suggestions provided in this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can make the most out of their visit to Kebbi state.
