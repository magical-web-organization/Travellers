Introduction to Zonkwa

Zonkwa is a beautiful town in the heart of Kaduna State, Nigeria. Located in North West Nigeria, Zonkwa is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply looking for a relaxing escape in a beautiful setting, Zonkwa is sure to delight. In this travel guide, we’ll take a detailed look at the best things to do and see in Zonkwa, as well as where to stay and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Zonkwa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Emir of Zaria Palace
  • Kufena Hills
  • National Museum Kaduna -Leventis Museum

Natural Attractions of Zonkwa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kamuku National Park
  • Kaduna River
  • Gidan Jaja Rock Formation -Nok village

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zonkwa

Zonkwa is an excellent place for hikers and bikers to explore, with numerous trails of varying difficulty and terrain. Some popular routes include:

  • Kufena Hills
  • Kagoro Hills
  • Gidan Jaja Rock Formation Trail
  • Zamani Lagoon

Best Things to Do in Zonkwa: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Kamuku National Park
  • Take a tour of the Emir of Zaria Palace
  • Try out horseback riding at Almber Ranch Resort
  • Learn some architecture at Nok village
  • Relax and enjoy the peace while visiting the Kagoro Hills.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zonkwa

Day 1: Explore Kamuku National Park Day 2: Visit the Emir of Zaria Palace and Kufena Hills Day 3: Take a bike tour of Gidan Jaja Rock Formation and Kagoro Hills Day 4: Learn about the Nok culture at Nok village Day 5: Enjoy some leisure at the Zamani Lagoon Day 6: Visit Pambegua Hills Day 7: Take a river cruise on the Kaduna River

Local Culture and Customs of Zonkwa

Zonkwa is home to various cultural practices and beliefs, including festivals such as the Kaduna Art and Book Festival, the Zonkwa Festival, and the Laraban Tara.

Best Time to Visit Zonkwa

The best time to visit Zonkwa is between November and February. During this period, the weather is cool and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zonkwa

Do:

  • try out the local food and drinks

    Don’t:

  • Smoke in public
  • Drink in public
  • Insult or disrespect the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Zonkwa

Zonkwa is a safe town to visit. However, it is recommended to take precautions such as avoiding dark and isolated areas at night and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Zonkwa

Travelers to Zonkwa are advised to take precautions such as drinking only bottled water, wearing insect repellent to avoid malaria, and getting vaccinated against yellow fever.
