Cap Vert-Thies Travel Guide

If you’re in search of an authentic African experience, Cap Vert-Thies in Senegal is worth considering. Located in West Africa, the region is known for its cultural and ecological richness. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your Cap Vert-Thies trip, including where to visit, what to do, where to stay, local customs, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Cap Vert-Thies

Cap Vert-Thies is a region in Senegal, West Africa. It is made up of four main areas: Dakar, Rufisque, Thiès, and Mbour. Toubab Dialao, located in Dakar, is a small fishing village that offers a scenic view of the ocean with impressive cliffs. The city of Thiès is known for its colonial architecture as well as its famous Ndiambour wrestling. Mbour is often regarded as a gateway to the national park, and Rufisque is known for its slave trade history.

Cap Vert-Thies has a tropical climate, with average temperatures ranging from 22-34°C. The rainy season occurs between June and October, so the best time to visit is between November and May.

List of Places to Visit in Cap Vert-Thies

When in Cap Vert-Thies, you won’t run out of places to visit, from culturally significant sites to natural attractions. Below are some of the must-visit places in Cap Vert-Thies:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gorée Island: A UNESCO World Heritage site located off the coast of Dakar, Gorée Island was a major hub for the Atlantic slave trade. Today, visitors can explore the House of Slaves and learn about the history of the region.
  • IFAN Museum of African Arts: A museum in Dakar that houses a vast collection of African art, including masks, sculptures, textiles, and more.
  • Ndiambour Wrestling: A unique form of wrestling, Ndiambour wrestling is the pastime of many young men in Thiès. The sport has become a significant cultural attraction for the region.
  • Mausoleum of Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba: Also known as the “Touba Mosque,” this mosque in Touba is the final resting place of Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba. He played a significant role in the spread of Islam in Senegal and West Africa.

Natural Attractions of Cap Vert-Thies: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pink Lake or Retba: As the name suggests, the lake has a unique pink hue due to the high salt content. Visitors can take a boat ride or take a dip in the lake.
  • Bandia Nature Reserve: A nature park located in Mbour, you can see African wildlife such as giraffes, zebras, rhinoceros, and more. Visitors can enjoy a game drive or go on a walking tour.
  • Somone Lagoon: A picturesque lagoon located in the Petite-Côte area, Somone Lagoon is a protected bird area. Visitors can take a boat ride, hike, or simply relax.
  • African Renaissance Monument: A 160-foot (49 m) monument located on the outskirts of Dakar which commemorates independence and growth, visitors can climb the tower and enjoy aerial views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cap Vert-Thies

  • Goree Island walking tour: A walking tour in Gorée Island, you can explore the narrow streets, visit historical buildings, and enjoy a scenic view of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Dakar city bike tour: With the help of guides, visitors can bike around the Dakar and see its bustling markets, colorful street art, beaches, and remarkable landmarks.
  • Mbour to Popenguine Coastal Walk: This walk takes you from Mbour all the way to Popenguine, a quiet seaside town with beautiful beaches, overlooked by an old church, and a famous rock formation.

Best Things to Do in Cap Vert-Thies: Activities and Tours

  • Learn to surf: Dakar is regarded as one of the best surfing spots in West Africa. There are many surf schools around the area and it’s a great way to explore the coastline.
  • Enjoy a boat ride to the Island of Goree: Visitors can take a boat ride from Dakar to Gorée Island and explore the various museums, art galleries, and historic buildings
  • Take a camel ride on the beach: Camel riding is a popular attraction on the beaches of Cap Vert-Thies, and a great way to explore the region.
  • Visit the African Renaissance Monument: Located in Dakar, visitors can climb to the top of the monument and enjoy the panoramic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cap Vert-Thies

If you have a week to spend in Cap Vert-Thies, you can explore the region in the following itinerary:

  • Day 1 – Explore Gorée Island and Dakar City
  • Day 2 – Visit the African Renaissance Monument and Hope Side Street Art
  • Day 3 – Visit the Bandia Nature Reserve and go on a game drive
  • Day 4 – Take a camel ride on the beach and visit Somone Lagoon
  • Day 5 – Drive to Thiès and explore Ndiambour wrestling culture and colonial architecture
  • Day 6 – Visit the Touba Mosque and take a boat tour to Pink Lake
  • Day 7 – Relax and enjoy a free day at one of the beaches in Cap Vert-Thies

Food and Drink Scene of Cap Vert-Thies

When traveling to Cap Vert-Thies, you must try the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some dishes and drinks to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thiéboudienne: A flavorful dish made with fish, vegetables, and rice, usually served with a spicy sauce.
  • Yassa Poulet: Chicken cooked in onions and lemon juice, served with rice.
  • Mafe: A traditional West African peanut stew made with meat, fish, or vegetables, served with rice or bread.
  • Couscous: A West African staple food made with grains typically served alongside fish or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Bissap: A refreshing drink made from the hibiscus plant, mixed with sugar and water.
  • Touba Coffee: A mild, sweet coffee that’s often spiced with cloves and other ingredients, and has a unique flavor.
  • Thiof Fish: A popular fish that is roasted over hot coals and is often washed down with local beer.

Where to Stay in Cap Vert-Thies: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cap Vert-Thies offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • King Fahd Palace: A 5-star luxury hotel in Dakar with ocean views, swimming pools, and private beaches.
  • Hotel Le Djoloff: A boutique hotel in Dakar with modern décor and air-conditioned accommodations.
  • Keur Dada: A family-run guest house located in the Somone Lagoon with a beautiful garden and outdoor swimming pool.
  • Hotel Sokhamon: A budget-friendly hostel in Thiès.

What to Buy in Cap Vert-Thies: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, check out:

  • African fabrics: African prints and fabrics are a beautiful souvenir that you can use for clothing or home decor.
  • Sand paintings: A unique form of art made with different colored sand and depicting African landscapes, wildlife, and more.
  • Wooden carvings: Senegal is known for its intricate wooden carvings, such as masks, figures, and jewelry boxes.

Local Culture and Customs of Cap Vert-Thies

Senegal is predominantly a Muslim country, and it’s important to respect the local customs and dress modestly. When visiting mosques or religious sites, ensure to dress appropriately, such as covering your arms, legs, and head.

It’s also essential to greet people when you meet them by saying, “Salaam Alaikum,” meaning “peace be upon you,” before commencing a conversation. Senegalese people are generally welcoming and friendly, and it is appropriate to acknowledge their presence.

Best Time to Visit Cap Vert-Thies

The best time to visit Senegal and Cap Vert-Thies is between November to May, during the dry season. The weather is hot but more comfortable, and you’ll have more opportunities to explore the region without rain interruptions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cap Vert-Thies

When visiting Cap Vert-Thies, here are some proposals on what to do and what not to do:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Greet people respecting their customs and traditions
  • Learn some French and Wolof (local language)
  • Explore the region with local guides

Don’t:

  • Dress immodestly when visiting religious sites
  • Take photographs of people without permission
  • Discuss religion or politics unless asked to
  • Litter or damage the natural scenery

Safety and Health

Safety in Cap Vert-Thies

Cap Vert-Thies is generally considered a safe place for visitors. Nevertheless, it’s essential to take the usual precautions while traveling, such as taking care of your valuable belongings, especially in crowded and touristy areas.

Health in Cap Vert-Thies

When traveling to Cap Vert-Thies, it’s advisable to get immunizations against yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and meningitis. You might also want to take antimalarial tablets to prevent contracting malaria. It’s essential to use insect repellant, wear long-sleeve shirts, and pants to reduce the risk of mosquito bites.

Getting to Cap Vert-Thies: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cap Vert-Thies

The best airport to fly into is Leopold Sedar Senghor International Airport in Dakar. Other airports include Blaise Diagne International Airport near Mbour, and Saint-Louis Airport located in Saint-Louis.

Bus Stations Near Cap Vert-Thies

There are many bus stations in Dakar, where you can board a bus for various destinations.

Other Options

Taxis are prevalent in Dakar, especially in touristy areas. Private taxis are more expensive than the shared taxi, but they’ll get you to your destination more quickly. You can also opt to rent a car, hire a driver or rent a bike to explore the region.

How to Get Around in Cap Vert-Thies

In Cap Vert-Thies, you can opt to travel by taxi, bus, car rental, bike rental, or hire a driver. Getting around the region independently may require some French, and it’s advisable to have a guide, especially when visiting remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Cap Vert-Thies: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Cap Vert-Thies has a lot to offer, nearby destinations worth visiting and can be easily accessible are:

  • Saint-Louis: A quiet and relaxed colonial town located in the north of the country, famous for its lack of traffic and horses roaming freely on the streets.
  • Dakar: Dakar, the capital city, is a bustling metropolis and offers an abundance of historical and cultural attractions.
  • Pink Lake: Also known as Lac Retba, a pink lake located close to Saint Louis, is an ideal place to enjoy freshwater, salt-water experience.
  • Saly: Saly is a charming coastal town located 20 miles south of Mbour that has served as a popular tourist destination for the past few decades.
