Introduction to Thies

Thies is a charming city located in the west of Senegal, Africa. It offers a rich culture, breathtaking landscapes, and a vibrant arts scene. Visitors can enjoy everything from local markets and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and delicious food. In this guide, we will provide a comprehensive list of things to do, see, and eat in Thies, as well as travel tips and safety advice.

List of Places to Visit in Thies

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Leopold Sedar Senghor Museum

One of the most important cultural attractions in Thies is the Leopold Sedar Senghor Museum. The museum is a tribute to the famous Senegalese poet and first president, Leopold Sedar Senghor. Visitors can explore the beautifully preserved colonial house where he was born, as well as an exhibition on his poetry, politics, and philosophy.

Abbaye de Keur Moussa

The Abbaye de Keur Moussa is a stunning monastery located in the outskirts of Thies. The monastery is a unique blend of traditional African and European styles and contains stunning architectural features. Visitors can take guided tours of the grounds and learn about the history and religious practices of the monastery.

Faidherbe Bridge

The Faidherbe Bridge is a historical landmark in Thies that connects the city to Saint-Louis. The bridge was built in the 19th century by French engineers and has become a symbol of Thies. Visitors can enjoy a walk over the bridge and admire the surrounding scenery.

Natural Attractions of Thies: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lac Rose

Lac Rose, also known as the Pink Lake, is a must-visit natural attraction in Thies. This stunning natural wonder gets its name from the unique pink color of its water, which is caused by the high salt content and unique bacteria in the lake. Visitors can take a traditional pirogue boat ride on the lake and witness the local salt harvesters at work.

Parc forestier et zoologique de Hann

If you’re looking for wildlife and outdoor adventure, don’t miss the Parc forestier et zoologique de Hann. This expansive park is home to a variety of animal species, including giraffes, elephants, and lions. Visitors can also enjoy hiking and picnicking in the park’s beautiful natural setting.

Mamelles Lighthouse

For spectacular views of the Thies coastline, head to the Mamelles Lighthouse. This historical lighthouse was built in the 20th century and offers panoramic views of the city and the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thies

Randorando

Randorando offers guided hiking and biking tours of the Thies region. Visitors can explore the stunning natural landscapes, learn about the local culture and history, and enjoy outdoor adventure in a safe and sustainable way.

Best Things to Do in Thies: Activities and Tours

Dance and Drumming Classes

If you want to experience the local culture of Thies in a unique way, take dance and drumming classes. Many local organizations and dance groups offer classes to visitors. The classes are a fun and engaging way to learn about the history and traditions of Senegal.

Thies Market

No visit to Thies is complete without visiting the local market. Thies Market is a vibrant and bustling hub of activity, where visitors can find everything from local crafts and souvenirs to fresh produce and spices. Visitors should not miss the opportunity to taste traditional snacks and street food.

Senegal River Cruise

To experience the scenic beauty and rich history of the Senegal River, take a river cruise. Many boat tours are available in the region, offering visitors the chance to witness stunning sunsets, visit local fishing villages, and see historical landmarks along the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Thies

Must-Try Dishes

Thieboudienne

Thieboudienne, also known as CeebuJen in Wolof, is the national dish of Senegal. It is a flavorful and hearty rice and fish stew that is typically served with vegetables, plantains, and a spicy sauce. You can find Thieboudienne at many local restaurants and street food vendors.

Yassa

Yassa is a popular Senegalese dish made with marinated chicken or fish, onions, and lemons. The dish is typically served with rice and vegetables and has a savory, tangy flavor.

Local Beverages

Bissap

Bissap is a refreshing and sweet drink made from dried hibiscus flowers. The drink has a deep red color and a fruity, slightly sour taste. It is a staple at most local cafes and restaurants in Thies.

Touba Coffee

Touba coffee is a specialty coffee that is unique to Senegal. The coffee is infused with a blend of spices, including cloves and ginger, giving it a warm and aromatic flavor. Be sure to try a cup at a local cafe.

Where to Stay in Thies: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Royal Thies

Hotel Royal Thies is a comfortable and modern hotel located in the heart of Thies. The hotel features clean and spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

La Maison Rose

La Maison Rose is a quaint and charming guesthouse in Thies. It features cozy rooms with traditional African decor, a courtyard garden, and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Thies: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional Crafts

Thies is known for its traditional crafts, including pottery, textiles, and jewelry. Visitors can find unique and beautiful crafts at local markets and souvenir shops.

Local Spices

Senegal is known for its unique and flavorful spice blends. Visitors can take home a variety of spices, including thyme, coriander, and black pepper.

Local Culture and Customs of Thies

Family Values

Family is an important part of Senegalese culture, and visitors should be aware of the importance of respecting family values. Visitors should take care not to offend or disrespect family members, elders, or the local community.

Religion

Islam is the dominant religion in Senegal, and visitors should be respectful of local religious customs and practices. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and be mindful of the local customs and rituals.

Best Time to Visit Thies

The best time to visit Thies is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. During this time, temperatures are comfortable, and there is little to no rain. However, visitors should be aware of the harmattan, a dry and dusty wind that blows from the Sahara Desert that can sometimes affect air quality during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thies

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect family values or offend community members.
  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Engage in aggressive or disrespectful behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thies

Thies is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. It is recommended to avoid walking alone after dark, keep valuables hidden from view, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Thies

Visitors to Thies should take precautions to protect their health. It is recommended to drink bottled water, use mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as malaria, and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Thies: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thies

The nearest airport to Thies is Blaise Diagne International Airport, located in Dakar. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a private shuttle to Thies.

Bus Stations Near Thies

Thies has a central bus station that offers regional and national connections. Visitors can take a bus from Dakar or other major cities in Senegal.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or a private shuttle from Dakar to Thies, which is a distance of approximately 70 kilometers.

How to Get Around in Thies

Visitors can get around Thies by taxi, public transportation, or on foot. Taxis are readily available and offer a convenient way to get around the city. Public transportation is available but can be crowded and slow. Walking is also a great way to explore the city and its many attractions.

Where to Go Next from Thies: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dakar

Dakar is the capital city of Senegal and is located just 60 kilometers from Thies. It is a vibrant and bustling city, filled with historical landmarks, markets, and restaurants.

Saint-Louis

Saint-Louis is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is located approximately 200 kilometers from Thies. This charming city offers a mix of African and French colonial architecture and a vibrant music and arts scene.

Conclusion

Thies is a fascinating and beautiful city that offers visitors a unique and authentic experience of Senegalese culture and history. From natural wonders and outdoor adventures to traditional crafts and delicious cuisine, Thies has something for everyone. Visitors can explore the city’s many attractions, learn about the local culture, and create unforgettable memories.
