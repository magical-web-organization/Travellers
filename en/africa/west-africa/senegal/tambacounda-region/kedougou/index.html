Introduction to Kedougou

Located in the Tambacounda Region of Senegal, Kedougou is an off-the-beaten-path gem that is often overlooked by tourists. The region borders Guinea and Mali, and its diverse landscape includes mountains, forests, and the majestic Senegal River. Kedougou is home to various ethnic groups, each with its own unique culture and traditions.

Despite its remote location, Kedougou offers a range of activities and attractions for travelers seeking adventure, culture, and relaxation. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Kedougou.

List of Places to Visit in Kedougou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Moussala Mound: Located in the town of Kedougou, Moussala Mound is a historical site that dates back to the 14th century. It is believed to have been the capital of the Mali Empire before it was taken over by the Songhai Empire.

  2. Ethnographic Museum of Kedougou: This museum showcases the traditions and customs of the various ethnic groups in the region, including the Bedik, Bassari, and Peuhl. Visitors can explore traditional huts, clothing, and musical instruments.

Natural Attractions of Kedougou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dindefelo Falls: Located in the Dindefelo Natural Reserve, these stunning waterfalls are a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can hike through the lush forest to reach the falls, which cascade down a steep cliff.

  2. Fouta Djallon Mountains: This mountain range spans across West Africa, and the section in Kedougou is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts. The trails offer breathtaking views of the landscape and abundant wildlife, including baboons and chimpanzees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kedougou

Kedougou is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with its rugged mountains, dense forests, and vast savannahs. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  1. Mount Assirik: This 800-meter high mountain offers a challenging hike and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Biking through Bedik Villages: Explore the traditional Bedik villages on a bike, passing through rice paddies and lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Kedougou: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit a traditional village: Learn about the customs and traditions of the Bedik or Bassari people by visiting one of their villages.

  2. Canoeing on the Senegal River: Take a leisurely canoe ride down the Senegal River, and enjoy the tranquil setting and stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kedougou

Day 1-2: Explore the town of Kedougou and visit the Ethnographic Museum.

Day 3-4: Hike to Dindefelo Falls and explore the Dindefelo Natural Reserve.

Day 5-6: Hike through the Fouta Djallon Mountains and visit traditional Bedik villages.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing canoe ride down the Senegal River.

Food and Drink Scene of Kedougou

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Thiéboudienne: This is the national dish of Senegal, and it is a flavorful rice dish with fish and vegetables.

  2. Yassa Poulet: This dish features marinated chicken served in a sauce of onions, lemon juice, and mustard.

Local Beverages

  1. Bissap: This refreshing drink is made from hibiscus flowers and is sweetened with sugar.

  2. Gin and Juice: A popular alcoholic beverage that combines the local gin with tropical juices.

Where to Stay in Kedougou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Le Relais de Kedougou: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a swimming pool, located in the heart of Kedougou.

  2. Auberge Campement de Mare de Bedik: This guesthouse offers traditional Bedik huts and is located near the Dindefelo Falls.

What to Buy in Kedougou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handwoven Fabrics: The Bassari and Bedik people are known for their intricate weaving skills, and visitors can purchase colorful fabrics and textiles.

  2. Crafts: The local markets offer a wide range of handicrafts, including wooden carvings, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Kedougou

Kedougou is home to various ethnic groups, each with its own unique culture and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including dress codes and avoiding taking photos without permission.

Best Time to Visit Kedougou

The best time to visit Kedougou is from November to February, when the weather is cooler and drier. It is also the time when the region’s festivals take place, including the Bassari Festival and the Kankourang Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kedougou

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Learn some basic French or Wolof to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting traditional villages.
  • Engage in any form of illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kedougou

Kedougou is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and securing valuables.

Health in Kedougou

Visitors should speak with a healthcare provider before traveling to Kedougou to receive necessary vaccinations, including yellow fever and hepatitis A. Malaria is also present in the region, so visitors should take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Kedougou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kedougou

The nearest airport to Kedougou is the Tambacounda Airport, which is located approximately 150 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi to Kedougou.

Bus Stations Near Kedougou

Kedougou is connected to neighboring towns and cities via a network of buses. Bus stations are located in the center of town.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a shared taxi to Kedougou.

How to Get Around in Kedougou

Many of the attractions in Kedougou are located outside of the town, so visitors should plan to rent a car or hire a taxi to get around. Bikes are also available for rent in the town.

Where to Go Next from Kedougou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Niokolo-Koba National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located approximately 250 km from Kedougou and is home to various wildlife species, including elephants, lions, and hippos.

  2. Dakar: Senegal’s vibrant capital city is located approximately 800 km from Kedougou and offers a range of attractions, including museums, markets, and beaches.

Conclusion

Kedougou is an undiscovered gem in the heart of Senegal, offering a unique blend of culture, nature, and adventure. This comprehensive guide has provided information on the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Kedougou. Whether you’re seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure or a relaxing getaway, Kedougou is sure to provide a memorable experience.
