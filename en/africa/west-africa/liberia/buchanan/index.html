Introduction to Buchanan

Buchanan, the second largest city in Liberia, is a hidden gem in West Africa, offering a perfect balance between a rich cultural heritage and outdoor adventures. Located on the Atlantic Coast at the mouth of the Saint John River, this city is a gateway to a world of natural wonders, including waterfalls, wildlife reserves, and beaches.

Whether you are a history buff, a culture enthusiast or an adventure seeker, Buchanan has something to offer. This comprehensive travel guide outlines the must-visit places in Buchanan, things to do, where to stay, and what to expect while enjoying your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Buchanan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Buchanan has a rich history and culture, and visiting its museums and historical landmarks is one way to appreciate its heritage. The following are the top places to visit in the city:

  • National Museum of Liberia: This museum located in Monrovia, features artifacts that detail the country’s cultural and social history.
  • SSVC Cape Palmas Station: Take a trip back in time with this historic station built in 1907. Visitors can learn about the railroad and its history in Liberia.
  • Sapo National Park: Wildlife enthusiasts would enjoy the protected rainforest that is home to chimpanzees, hippos, and several bird species.

Natural Attractions of Buchanan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Buchanan boasts of several natural attractions for outdoor enthusiasts. Its beaches, waterfalls, and wildlife reserves offer memorable experiences for visitors. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Yekepa Waterfalls: Located in Nimba County, the Yekepa Waterfalls is a stunning display of nature. Visitors can hike through the forest trail to the waterfalls and enjoy a refreshing swim.
  • Lake Piso: The second largest lake in Liberia, Lake Piso, offers a scenic view of the surrounding mountains and a chance to see the community life of the locals.
  • Kpatawee Waterfall: A trip to Buchanan is incomplete without a visit to Kpatawee Waterfall in Bomi County. This waterfall is a serene encounter with nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buchanan

Buchanan is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The following routes offer some of the most scenic views and exercise opportunities in the city:

  • Buchanan Coastal Highway: This route runs along the coast and has stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Buchanan Agriculture road: This route runs through the plantations, passing through several villages along the way.
  • Buchanan to Bopolu: This route is a challenging one that takes several days to complete. However, it offers picturesque views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Buchanan: Activities and Tours

Buchanan offers several things to do, from cultural tours to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Visit the historical landmarks and museums to learn about the city’s history and culture.
  • Go on a boat tour or a fishing trip to explore the coast.
  • Take a day trip to the wildlife reserves to see chimpanzees and hippos.

Week-Long Itinerary for Buchanan

A week in Buchanan offers a chance to visit the city’s best sites and take part in various activities. Here’s an ideal itinerary:

Day 1:

  • Arrive in Buchanan in the morning.
  • Visit the National Museum of Liberia.

Day 2:

  • Take a boat tour along the coast.
  • Explore the historic SSVC Cape Palmas Station.

Day 3:

  • Visit Lake Piso.
  • Enjoy a hike to Kpatawee Waterfall.

Day 4:

  • Visit the Port of Buchanan.
  • Take a city tour.

Day 5:

  • Go on a fishing trip.
  • Relax at the beach.

Day 6:

  • Hike along the Buchanan Coastal Highway.

Day 7:

  • Visit Sapo National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Buchanan

Buchanan offers a variety of local dishes and beverages that are worth trying. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice: a popular West African dish made with rice, tomato, onion, and spices.
  • Fufu and Soup: a dish made with cassava, plantain, or yam, served with soup.
  • Pepper Soup: a spicy soup made with meat or fish.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: a traditional alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Ginger Beer: a refreshing drink made with fresh ginger and sugar.

Where to Stay in Buchanan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buchanan offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Buchanan Log House: a cozy guesthouse located in the center of the city.
  • Royal Hotel: an affordable hotel located near the port.
  • Bay Beauty Guesthouse: a modern guesthouse located in a quiet neighborhood.

What to Buy in Buchanan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Buchanan offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that are worth getting. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Handmade crafts: Wooden sculptures, masks, and carvings are available in the city’s markets.
  • Liberian coffee: Liberia has a thriving coffee industry, and local coffee is a perfect souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Buchanan

Buchanan has a diverse culture and customs that are worth learning about. Here are some key aspects of the local culture:

  • Language: Liberians speak English, but there are also several indigenous languages spoken in the country.
  • Dress code: Dressing modestly is encouraged as Liberians are generally conservative.
  • Religion: Christianity is the dominant religion in Liberia, with Muslims making up a significant minority population.

Best Time to Visit Buchanan

The best time to visit Buchanan is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. This period offers dry and sunny weather, ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buchanan

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Learn a few words of the local language.
  • Respect the locals and their culture.

Don’t:

  • Use the flash while taking photos of the locals without their permission.
  • Purchase wildlife products, as it is illegal and contributes to the exploitation of wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buchanan

Liberia has a generally high crime rate, but Buchanan is a relatively safe city for travelers. However, it is advisable to take precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night, carrying valuables discreetly, and not drawing attention to yourself as a tourist.

Health in Buchanan

It is advisable to take precautions against illnesses such as malaria, yellow fever, and typhoid before traveling to Liberia. It is also essential to have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Buchanan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buchanan is accessible by air, road, and sea. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Buchanan

  • Roberts International Airport (ROB)
  • James Spriggs Payne Airport (MLW)

Bus Stations Near Buchanan

  • The main bus station in Buchanan is located on Tubman Boulevard.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the city center and can be hired for a day.

How to Get Around in Buchanan

Buchanan is a relatively small city, and the best way to get around is by taxi or by renting a bike. Hiking is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Buchanan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Liberia offers several destinations within reach of Buchanan. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Monrovia: the capital city of Liberia.
  • Nimba County: home to wildlife reserves and stunning waterfalls.
  • Robertsport: a coastal town with beautiful beaches and surfing spots.

Conclusion

Buchanan, Liberia, offers a perfect mix of cultural experiences and outdoor adventures. Whether you are exploring the city’s museums and landmarks, enjoying the scenic views, or trying the local cuisine, a visit to Buchanan promises to be a memorable one. This comprehensive travel guide has provided insights on the must-visit sites, things to do, and practical tips to help plan your visit.
