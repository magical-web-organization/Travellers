Introduction to Aného

Aného is a charming coastal town located in southeastern Togo, West Africa. It served as the country’s capital during the pre-colonial and early colonial periods and has since been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its rich cultural and historical heritage. Today, Aného is a popular destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and beachgoers who are looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Aného

Whether you’re interested in exploring culturally important attractions or natural wonders, Aného has plenty of sights to offer. Here are some places you shouldn’t miss during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musee International du Golfe de Guinee: This ocean-based museum showcases the history of Togolese maritime traditions, including fishing techniques, boat building, and trade routes.
  • Palace of King Mlapa: Once the residence of one of the most powerful kings of the region, this palace is now an iconic site that represents the cultural heritage of Aného.
  • Slave Route: The Slave Route is a historical trail that takes you through the paths where slaves were once brought to the coast to be sold to European traders. It’s a powerful reminder of Togo’s dark history.

Natural Attractions of Aného: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aného Lagoon: This stunning lagoon is a natural oasis for many species of birds, fish, and other aquatic life. You can enjoy a boat ride, bird watching or a picnic by the shore.
  • Mount Agou: As the highest peak in Togo, Mount Agou welcomes adventurous hikers that seek a challenging trek and breathtaking views of Togo’s lush landscape.
  • The Beach: The pristine beach in Aného provides the perfect sunny ambiance for beach-goers and is home to one of the most visited surf breaks in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aného

Aného is surrounded by breathtaking landscapes that offer mesmerizing scenic routes for hikers and bikers. Here are some recommendations:

  • Agou Trek: This challenging hike that leads to the summit of Mount Agou is a popular activity among experienced hikers.
  • Forest of Missahohe: Biking enthusiasts can explore the well-marked trails that run through this lush forest.

Best Things to Do in Aného: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and touring cultural sites, there are plenty of other things to do in Aného. Here are some must-try activities and tours:

  • Surfing: Aného is known for its world-class surf breaks on the beach, making it a popular surfing destination.
  • Guided Tours: Take a guided tour of the historical sites or hike Mount Agou for a knowledgeable local experience.
  • Local Festivals: The town has various festivals throughout the year showcasing the unique cultural heritage of the town.

If you’re planning on staying in Aného for a week or more, you can follow this suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Aného

Togolese cuisine is full of delicious and exotic flavors, and Aného has plenty of options to explore. Here are some must-try dishes and must-have local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Akoume: A popular local dish that resembles a savory porridge made with corn or cassava flour.
  • Poyo: A beer-like alcoholic beverage brewed from fermented corn, or millet, and is a favorite amongst locals.
  • Grilled fish or seafood that is fresh and locally caught.

Local Beverages

  • Tchoukoutou: a light local beer made from fermented cassava or cornflour.
  • Bone relaxing teas, made from leaves or bark of various local trees and shrubs.

What to Buy in Aného: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aného is renowned for its handmade crafts, most notably its wood carving and local textiles. Some recommended items to purchase include:

  • Handmade textiles, such as Kente and wood-carved figurines, bowls, and other trinkets.
  • Handbeaten metal sculptures, featuring designs inspired by local folklore.

Local Culture and Customs of Aného

Togo’s diverse ethnic groups have various customs and cultural beliefs. In Aného, the primary ethnic group is the Ewe-fon, making up about 40% of the town’s population. It’s advisable to research and respect the local customs to avoid any cultural misunderstandings.

Best Time to Visit Aného

The best time to visit Aného is between November and February when the weather is cooler and less humid. The temperature averages at around 25 degrees celsius, which is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking and exploring the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aného

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Aného:

Do:

  • Keep appropriate clothing when visiting cultural sites, such as covering arms and legs.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try to learn some basic words or phrases in the local language and use them when possible.

Don’t:

  • Consume tap water; it’s recommended to drink bottled water.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Give money or gifts directly to children as it creates a dependency on begging.

Safety and Health

As with any destination, it’s necessary to take appropriate measures to ensure your safety and well-being. Here are a few things to consider:

Safety in Aného

  • Be cautious of petty theft and pick-pocketing.
  • Avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night.
  • Be aware of the ocean’s waves because of the strong currents and avoid swimming alone.

Health in Aného

  • Contaminated water can lead to illnesses. It’s advised to drink only bottled water.
  • Anti-malaria medication is recommended for these regions due to the high risk of the disease.
  • Wear mosquito repellent and protective clothing to avoid insect bites.

Getting to Aného: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aného is accessible by various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Aného

  • Lome-Tokoin International Airport is the primary airport serving Togo, and it’s around 45 kilometers from Aného.

Bus Stations Near Aného

  • The bus system in Togo is extensive, and various companies travel to Aného from Lomé.

Other Options

  • Hire a taxi or private driver directly from Lomé is the ideal option for those who prefer privacy and less time constraints.

How to Get Around in Aného

To get around Aného, travelers can rent bikes, motorbikes or can hire a private car. Additionally, taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Aného: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aného is an excellent starting point for further exploring Togo’s cultural and natural attractions, such as Lomé, Lake Togo, and Kpalime reserve.

Conclusion

Aného offers a unique and memorable experience that combines cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and beautiful beaches. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a culture enthusiast, Aného has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the town, enjoy its amazing local cuisine, engage in outdoor activities, and experience its culture to the fullest.
