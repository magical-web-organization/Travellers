Introduction to Togo

Togo is a small country located in West Africa, bordered by Ghana to the west, Benin to the east, and Burkina Faso to the north. Despite its size, Togo offers a unique blend of cultures, history, and nature that make it a hidden gem of West Africa. From bustling markets and museums to stunning landscapes and wildlife, Togo has something to offer to every type of traveler. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of Togo and plan your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Togo

If you’re planning a trip to Togo, make sure to add these destinations to your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Togo: Located in the capital city of Lomé, the National Museum of Togo showcases the country’s history and culture through art, artifacts, and exhibitions.
  • Voodoo Market: The Akodessewa Fetish Market in Lomé is one of the largest voodoo markets in the world, where you can find traditional voodoo remedies, charms, and other artifacts.
  • Tamberma Valley: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to the Tamberma people, who live in traditional mud houses and practice unique architectural techniques.

Natural Attractions of Togo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Koutammakou Landscape: This stunning landscape in northern Togo is home to the Batammariba people, who live in unique fortress-like houses made of clay and straw.
  • Fazao-Malfakassa National Park: This national park in central Togo is home to diverse wildlife, including elephants, monkeys, and antelopes.
  • Mount Agou: The highest peak in Togo, Mount Agou offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Togo

Togo offers several hiking and biking routes that allow you to explore its stunning natural beauty. Some popular routes to consider include:

  • The Togo Mountain Trail: This 120 km trail crosses the Atakora Mountains in northern Togo, passing through remote villages and offering stunning vistas of the landscape.
  • The Kpalimé Loop: Located in southern Togo, this 30 km loop takes you through lush rainforests, waterfalls, and farms, and is perfect for both hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Togo: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Togo, here are some activities and tours to consider:

  • Visit a traditional market: Togo has several colorful and bustling markets where you can find local crafts, food, and other products. Some popular markets to visit include the Lomé Grand Market and the Kara Market.
  • Take a voodoo tour: Voodoo is a traditional religion in Togo, and several tours are available that allow you to learn about its practices and rituals.
  • Attend a traditional festival: Togo has several vibrant cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Epe Ekpe festival in Kpalimé and the Evala wrestling festival in Sokodé.

Food and Drink Scene of Togo

Togo has a rich culinary tradition, with a variety of dishes and flavors influenced by its neighboring countries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pâte: A local staple made of boiled cassava or yam, mashed into a starchy paste.
  • Tchakpalo: A spicy chicken and okra stew, served with rice.
  • Fufu: A starchy dough made of cassava or plantain, usually served with soup or stew.

Local Beverages

  • Bissap: A refreshing drink made of hibiscus flowers, served cold.
  • Tusker beer: A popular local beer, brewed in neighboring Kenya and Uganda
  • Sodabe: A local palm wine, made from the sap of palm trees

Where to Stay in Togo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Togo offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Onomo Lomé: A five-star hotel located in the heart of Lomé, with panoramic views of the city and the sea.
  • Auberge du Lac: A mid-range guesthouse located near Lake Togo, offering stunning views of the lake and the surrounding landscape.
  • Hotel Kara: A budget-friendly hotel located in Kara, with simple rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

What to Buy in Togo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Togo offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts and mementos. Some popular options include:

  • Batik fabrics: Togo is known for its vibrant and colorful batik fabrics, made using traditional techniques.
  • Wooden carvings: Togo has a rich tradition of woodcarving, with unique and intricate designs available at many markets.
  • Spices and herbs: Togo offers a variety of local spices and herbs, including cloves, nutmeg, and ginger, which make great additions to your kitchen at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Togo

Togo has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by its history and its neighboring countries. Some important customs and traditions to keep in mind while traveling in Togo include:

  • Dress modestly: Togo is a predominantly Muslim country, and it’s recommended to dress modestly, especially in rural areas.
  • Respect local beliefs: Voodoo is an important part of Togo’s culture and history, and it’s important to respect its practices and rituals.
  • Greet locals: Togolese people are generally friendly and hospitable, and it’s customary to greet people with a handshake or a nod.

Best Time to Visit Togo

The best time to visit Togo is during the dry season, which lasts from November to February. During this time, the weather is pleasant and there is less chance of rain, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Togo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling in Togo:

Do:

  • Learn some French: French is the official language of Togo, and knowing some basic phrases can be helpful while navigating the country.
  • Bargain at markets: Bargaining is common practice in Togo, so don’t be afraid to negotiate prices at markets and shops.
  • Be respectful: Togolese people are generally friendly and welcoming, and it’s important to show respect and gratitude towards locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: It’s recommended to drink bottled water in Togo, as tap water may not be safe.
  • Photograph locals without permission: It’s considered rude to take photos of people without their permission, so always ask before taking pictures.
  • Leave valuables unattended: Petty theft can be a problem in Togo, so it’s important to keep your belongings close and secure.

Safety and Health

Togo is generally a safe country to travel to, but it’s important to take some precautions to ensure your safety and health while on the road.

Safety in Togo

  • Avoid political demonstrations: Political tensions can be high in Togo, and it’s recommended to avoid demonstrations and public gatherings.
  • Use common sense: As with any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and use common sense, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Check travel advisories: Before traveling to Togo, check your government’s travel advisory for any updates or warnings.

Health in Togo

  • Get vaccinated: It’s recommended to get vaccinated against typhoid, hepatitis A, and yellow fever before traveling to Togo.
  • Malaria prevention: Togo is a malaria-endemic country, and it’s important to take measures to prevent mosquito bites, such as wearing long sleeves and using insect repellent.
  • Safe food and water: To avoid getting sick, be mindful of what you eat and drink, and avoid eating street food and drinking tap water.

Getting to Togo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Togo is accessible by air, land, and sea, and there are several options to consider when planning your trip.

Airports Near Togo

  • Lomé-Tokoin International Airport: The main airport in Togo, located in the capital city of Lomé.
  • Niamtougou International Airport: Located in northern Togo, near the city of Kara.

Bus Stations Near Togo

  • Cotonou Bus Station: Located in neighboring Benin, Cotonou is a major transportation hub with buses to many destinations in Togo.
  • Accra Bus Station: Located in neighboring Ghana, Accra also has several buses that go to Togo.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are several ferries that operate between Lomé and neighboring countries such as Ghana and Benin.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is also an option, although it’s important to be aware of the condition of the roads and the local driving customs.

How to Get Around in Togo

Togo has several transportation options for getting around, including taxis, motorcycles, and buses.

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Togo, and it’s recommended to negotiate the fare before getting in.
  • Motorcycles: Motorcycles or “motos” are a popular and inexpensive way to get around, especially in rural areas.
  • Buses: Buses are a cheap and convenient way to travel between cities, although they can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Togo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Ghana: Togo shares a border with Ghana, and it’s easy to cross over and explore its vibrant cities and historical sites.
  • Benin: Located to the east of Togo, Benin offers a rich cultural experience, with its own voodoo traditions, historical sites, and nature attractions.
  • Ivory Coast: To the west of Togo, Ivory Coast offers stunning beaches, natural parks, and cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Togo may be small in size, but it offers a treasure trove of adventures, experiences, and cultural gems. This comprehensive travel guide has provided an overview of the must-visit attractions, hiking/biking routes, and best things to do in Togo. Remember to immerse yourself in the local culture and customs, respect the history and traditions of the country, and take precautions to ensure your safety and health while traveling. With these tips and recommendations, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Togo.
