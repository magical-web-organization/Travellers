Introduction to Lomé

Situated on the Gulf of Guinea, Lomé is the capital and largest city of Togo. The city’s eclectic architecture tells tales of its colonial past, while its vibrant markets and stunning coastlines showcase its present-day charm. With a bustling port and a lively nightlife, Lomé offers something for everyone.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you uncover the vibrant culture and scenic beauty of Lomé, providing insider tips on the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Lomé

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lomé boasts a rich cultural heritage, with museums and historical sites offering a glimpse into its past.

  • National Museum: This museum is dedicated to Togo’s history and showcases a range of art and artefacts, including religious shrines, pottery, and tribal masks.
  • Palais des Congrès: This impressive building, formerly a palace, is now used for conferences, shows and other events but also houses the National Museum of Togo and Art Center.
  • Independence Square: Located in the city center, this square serves as a reminder of Togo’s independence from France in 1960.
  • Slave House: The Slaves’ House is a local museum that gives a sobering insight into Togo’s slave trade history.

Natural Attractions of Lomé: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond its historical attractions, Lomé also boasts numerous natural wonders that are sure to leave visitors in awe.

  • Togo-Atakora Mountains: These mountains are a great destination for hiking and outdoor adventures.
  • Beaches: Lomé’s coastline is dotted with beaches and resorts, with popular ones like Plage de Lomé and Robinson Plage.
  • Marche des Feticheurs: Lomé’s voodoo market is a unique experience for any visitor looking for authentic and deeply spiritual culture.
  • Sacré-Cœur Cathedral: Located in the heart of Lomé, this Roman Catholic cathedral is a symbol of Togo’s religious heritage and features beautiful stained glass windows and an impressive organ.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lomé

Lomé’s natural beauty makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Togo-Atakora Mountains provide plenty of opportunities for hiking, with scenic routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. Cycling is another popular way to explore the city’s sights and sounds, with many bike rentals available around town.

Best Things to Do in Lomé: Activities and Tours

Lomé has a vibrant cultural scene, with a range of activities and tours that cater to all interests.

  • Lomé Art and Craft Market: The market is a great place to explore local handicrafts, from traditional textiles and beaded jewelry to carved wooden souvenirs.
  • Voodoo Tour: Visitors curious about this unique West African religion can take a guided tour to learn more.
  • Ganvie Stilt Village: This fascinating fishing village built entirely on stilts is just a short drive from Lomé.

Food and Drink Scene of Lomé

Must-Try Dishes

Togolese cuisine is a fusion of African and European influences, with a variety of ingredients and flavors.

  • Fufu and groundnut soup: A traditional and popular dish made with a dough of cassava, yam, or plantains.
  • Sauce gombo: This is a local soup made with an okra sauce served with fish or meat.
  • Koklo meme: This is a popular street food that is marinated and grilled chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Palm wine: Alcohol brewed from palm tree sap with a unique tangy taste.
  • Bissap: Made from hibiscus flowers, it is a sweet, tangy and refreshing drink perfect for Togo’s hot weather.

Where to Stay in Lomé: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury stay or a budget-friendly option, Lomé has plenty of choices for visitors.

  • Hotel 2 Fevrier: One of the most notable hotels in Lomé, this grand hotel is located in the city center.
  • Le Patio: A mid-range hotel located near the beach.
  • Auberge de la Plage: A budget-friendly option with a laid-back atmosphere, located on the coast.

What to Buy in Lomé: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kente Cloth: This is a brightly colored woven cloth that denotes in Ewe Ghana and Togo traditional life cycle events.
  • Wooden Handicrafts: Made by local artisans and featuring intricate designs.
  • Beaded Jewelry: Handmade beaded necklace or bracelet from Lomé’s many artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Lomé

Togo is a diverse West African country, with over 40 different ethnic groups, and Lomé reflects this diversity in its rich cultural heritage.

  • Attire: Togolese tend to dress modestly, with women covering their legs and often their head for religious reason.
  • Religion: Vodou is a prominent religion, and Togo is home to one of the most revered voodoo markets in West Africa.
  • Hospitality: Togolese are known for their warmth and friendliness. Arriving guests are frequently greeted with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Lomé

The best time to visit Lomé is during the dry season, which runs from November to February. The weather is cooler, and there is less rainfall in these months, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lomé

Do:

  • Learn some basic French phrases.
  • Dress respectfully when visiting religious sites.
  • Be polite and respectful to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Get involved with scams and fraudsters.
  • Dress inappropriately in public places or beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lomé

Lomé is generally a safe city, but visitors should be cautious when traveling at night and avoid isolated areas.

Health in Lomé

It is recommended to consult a travel clinic before traveling and to get vaccinations a few weeks before travel. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito bites due to malaria risks.

Getting to Lomé: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lomé

The Lomé–Tokoin Airport serves Lomé, with flights from major cities in Africa and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Lomé

Buses from Accra, the capital of neighboring Ghana, travel to Lomé regularly.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are available around Lomé, but visitors should exercise caution when using these options.

How to Get Around in Lomé

Taxis, buses and tro-tros(local mini-buses), and bike rentals are the common modes of transport available in Lomé. Visitors should set the taxi fare before starting any journey to avoid overpricing.

Where to Go Next from Lomé: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lomé’s location at the crossroads of West Africa makes it a perfect gateway to explore the surrounding regions, including

  • Kpalimé: A picturesque town known for its beautiful surrounding mountains popular for hikers and trekker.
  • Lake Togo: A popular retreat for watersports and fishing.
  • Aneho: A historic town known for its slave trade history and its annual festival.

Conclusion

Lomé is a vibrant West African city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most of your visit to Lomé, experiencing the best places to stay, things to do, and where to eat. Plan your trip well from start to finish, and you’ll have a memorable holiday in Lomé, Togo.
