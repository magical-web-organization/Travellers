Are you planning a trip to Africa and looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination? Look no further than Northern Benin! This region, located in West Africa, boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. In this comprehensive guide, we’ve compiled everything you need to know to plan your trip to Northern Benin.

Introduction to Northern Benin

Northern Benin is situated in the northern part of the country and is home to several ethnic groups, including the Bariba, Dendi, and Fulani. The region is known for its traditional crafts, music, and dance, making it a fascinating cultural destination. You’ll find Baobab trees dotting the landscape along with a mix of Sudanese and Sahelian flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Benin

There are many exciting destinations to explore in Northern Benin, so we’ve compiled a list of the most popular places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Palace of Abomey: This UNESCO World Heritage Site was once the capital of the Dahomey Kingdom and features several royal palaces that showcase the history and culture of the local people.
  • Museum of Ethnography of Porto-Novo: A museum showcasing the cultures of the indigenous peoples of Benin.
  • Taneka Village: Experience traditional village life and learn about local weaving and blacksmith work.

Natural Attractions of Northern Benin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pendjari National Park: A safari into the park offers visitors a chance to see lions, buffalo, elephants, hippos, crocodiles and over 350 bird species.
  • W National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that extends into Benin, Burkina Faso, and Niger. It’s the habitat of a wide variety of flora, fauna, and wildlife.
  • Tanguieta Waterfall: A stunning waterfall with a pool below, where you can swim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Benin

There is no shortage of opportunities to trek and bike in Northern Benin, with trails catering to all levels of fitness. Popular routes include:

  • Pendjari National Park: Within the national park, there are short and long hiking routes and cycling routes, allowing visitors to take in the scenery.
  • Koutammakou: Trek in this area, which is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its unique architecture and natural beauty.
  • Tanguieta Waterfall: A gentle hike up a track will lead you through brush and woodland to this beautiful waterfall and its pool.

Best Things to Do in Northern Benin: Activities and Tours

Here are some exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy in Northern Benin:

  • Music and Dance: You can enjoy traditional music and dance performances throughout the region.
  • Craftsmanship Tours: Northern Benin has many craftsmen working with traditional techniques. You can learn about their techniques while watching them at work.
  • Horseback Safari: Take a horseback safari tour to explore the parks and other natural habitats and see the wildlife roaming free in natural habitats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Benin

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Northern Benin that will allow you to experience the best of the region:

Day 1: Arrival in Cotonou; visit the Dantokpa Market and the Museum of Ethnography of Porto-Novo.

Day 2: Drive to Abomey; visit the Royal Palace of Abomey and learn about the history of the Dahomey Kingdom.

Day 3: Drive to Taneka village and learn about weaving and blacksmith work.

Day 4: Visit Tanguieta Waterfall and trek in the nearby afforested area.

Day 5: Explore Pendjari National Park and go on a safari to see lions, elephants, and other exotic animals.

Day 6: Drive to Koutammakou and trek to see the UNESCO World Heritage Site unique architecture.

Day 7: Departure from Cotonou.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Benin

One of the best things about traveling to Northern Benin is experiencing its incredible cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peanut butter soup: A hearty soup made from peanut butter and vegetables.
  • Akpan: A maize-based dish with tomato sauce, onion, and smoked fish.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A sweet drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Sodabi: A local liquor made from palm wine and distilled into a clear liquid.

Where to Stay in Northern Benin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Benin has a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Hotel du Lac: A budget-friendly option in Dantokpa.
  • Hotel Cortez: A mid-range hotel in Cotonou.
  • Pendjari Lodge: A luxury game lodge in Pendjari National Park.

What to Buy in Northern Benin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Northern Benin, look for these souvenirs and local products:

  • Woven baskets: Traditional baskets woven from local materials make for a unique and practical souvenir.
  • Wooden sculptures: The craftsmanship of wooden sculptures found in the region are made from multiple types of wood.
  • Cloth: The region is famous for its wax prints, which can be found in various shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Benin

Northern Benin is home to several ethnic groups, each with its unique customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly and respect the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Northern Benin

The best time to visit Northern Benin is from November to February, when temperatures are less humid and more pleasant. During this time, visitors can also experience the traditional festivals and ceremonies that take place throughout the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Benin

When traveling to Northern Benin, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts of the region:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.
  • Ask permission before taking photographs.
  • Bargain when shopping at markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water; only drink bottled water to prevent water-borne diseases.
  • Wear revealing clothing; dress modestly.

Safety and Health

Northern Benin is a safe destination; however, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take precautions such as carrying their belongings with them. Additionally, visitors should also take steps to protect their health by avoiding tap water, bringing insect repellent, and taking malaria prophylaxis.

Getting to Northern Benin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Northern Benin can arrive by air or overland transportation options like buses taxi or car rentals.

Airports Near Northern Benin

  • Cadjehoun Airport in Cotonou.

Bus Stations Near Northern Benin

  • Porto Novo Bus Stations.

Other Options

  • Rental cars and taxis are available to move around the region.

How to Get Around in Northern Benin

Visitors can explore Northern Benin in rental cars, taxis, or public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Northern Benin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Consider these nearby destinations and excursions when planning your trip out of Northern Benin:

  • Ouidah: A nearby coastal town known for its voodoo culture and history of slave trade.
  • Wli Waterfall: A set of stunning waterfalls located in the nearby destination Ghana.

Conclusion

Northern Benin offers a unique travel experience with its vibrant culture, breathtaking landscapes, and diverse wildlife. Whether you’re looking to explore the historic sites or take in the natural beauty of the region, this comprehensive guide has everything you need to know to plan your trip. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in the heart of West Africa.
