Introduction to Gambia

Gambia is a small country in West Africa known for its beautiful beaches, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. Formerly known as the “Smiling Coast of Africa,” Gambia is a destination that offers a unique blend of traditional African culture and modern amenities. With its warm weather, friendly locals, and stunning scenery, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Gambia

If you’re planning a trip to Gambia, there are several must-visit attractions that you shouldn’t miss. We’ve compiled a list of the top places to visit in Gambia, organized by category.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Gambia: Located in Banjul, the National Museum of Gambia is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in the country’s history and culture. The museum houses a collection of artifacts, including traditional clothing, musical instruments, and ceremonial masks.

  • Arch 22: Located in the heart of Banjul, Arch 22 is a historic triumphal arch that commemorates Gambia’s independence from Britain. Visitors can climb to the top of the arch for a stunning view of the city.

  • James Island: Also known as Kunta Kinte Island, James Island was once a major hub of the African slave trade. Today, visitors can tour the island and learn about its history and impact on the region.

Natural Attractions of Gambia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Abuko Nature Reserve: Located in the Western Division, Abuko Nature Reserve is a lush tropical forest home to a variety of birds, monkeys, and other wildlife. Visitors can hike through the reserve or take a guided tour.

  • Tanji Fish Market: One of the biggest fish markets in West Africa, Tanji Fish Market is a vibrant and bustling attraction where visitors can watch fishermen bring in their daily catch and haggle for fresh seafood.

  • Kachikally Crocodile Pool: Located in Bakau, Kachikally Crocodile Pool is a sacred site where visitors can see crocodiles up close and learn about their role in traditional Gambian culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gambia

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll be pleased to know that Gambia has plenty of hiking and biking trails worth exploring. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Tanji-Kartong Nature Trail: This 10-kilometer trail takes you through the Tanji Bird Reserve and the coastal village of Kartong.

  • Kiang West National Park: This park is located in the Lower River Division and offers several hiking trails through the savannah landscape.

  • Footsteps Eco-Lodge Trail: This trail is located near Gunjur and takes you through a forested area filled with birdlife and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Gambia: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Gambia. Some of the best include:

  • River Safari: Take a boat tour of the Gambia River and see crocodiles, hippos, and other wildlife up close.

  • Juffureh Walking Tour: This tour takes you through the village of Juffureh, the ancestral home of Kunta Kinte, the main character in Alex Haley’s Roots.

  • West African Cooking Class: Learn how to make traditional Gambian dishes like domoda and benechin in a cooking class.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gambia

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Gambia, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Gambia

Gambian cuisine is a unique blend of West African and European influences, with dishes like benachin (rice and fish) and yassa (chicken or fish in a spicy sauce) being popular staples. In addition to local cuisine, there are also plenty of international restaurants and cafes to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Domoda: A peanut stew made with vegetables and either beef, chicken, or fish.

  • Superkanja: A one-pot meal made with cassava leaves, onions, and either beef, chicken, or fish.

  • Tapalapa: A soft, chewy bread often used in sandwiches and as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Attaya Tea: A traditional tea made with loose-leaf tea, mint, and sugar.

  • Baobab Juice: A refreshing juice made from the fruit of the baobab tree.

Where to Stay in Gambia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget hostels, there are plenty of accommodation options available in Gambia. Some of the best hotels include:

  • Kololi Beach Resort: A luxury beachfront resort with a pool, spa, and multiple dining options.

  • Gambia Coral Beach Hotel and Spa: Another luxury beachfront option with a spa and outdoor pool.

  • Tafbel Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in Serrekunda, close to many of Gambia’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Gambia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back from Gambia, there are several unique items to choose from. Some of the best include:

  • Beadwork: Gambia is known for its colorful beadwork, which is often used to create jewelry and other accessories.

  • Music CDs: Gambia has a lively music scene, and many local musicians sell CDs at markets and on the streets.

  • Clothing: Traditional Gambian clothing, like the boubou and kente cloth, make for unique and colorful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Gambia

Gambia is a predominantly Muslim country, and visitors should dress modestly out of respect for local customs. It’s also important to ask for permission before taking photos of people, as some may be hesitant to have their picture taken.

Best Time to Visit Gambia

The best time to visit Gambia is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. However, because of the country’s tropical climate, temperatures remain warm year-round, making it a great destination for winter getaways.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gambia

Do:

  • Dress modestly out of respect for local customs.

  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people.

  • Try the local cuisine and attend cultural events.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of government buildings or military personnel.

  • Engage in public displays of affection, as this is considered inappropriate in Gambian culture.

  • Swim in the ocean at night, as there are often strong currents and rip tides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gambia

Gambia is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions like avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables out of sight. Visitors should also be aware of scams and should take extra care in crowded areas.

Health in Gambia

Travelers to Gambia should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations like measles, mumps, and rubella. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever by using insect repellent and covering up exposed skin.

Getting to Gambia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gambia is relatively easy to get to, with several options for transportation. Some of the most popular include:

Airports Near Gambia

  • Banjul International Airport: Located in the capital city of Banjul, this airport serves multiple international carriers and offers direct flights to several European cities.

  • Dakar International Airport: Located in Senegal, just across the border from Gambia, this airport serves as a major hub for international flights to West Africa.

Bus Stations Near Gambia

  • Bissau Bus Station: Located in the capital city of Bissau, Guinea-Bissau, this bus station offers transportation to multiple cities in Gambia.

  • Ziguinchor Bus Station: Located in the capital city of Ziguinchor, Senegal, this bus station offers transportation to several cities in Gambia.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are several ferry services that operate between Banjul and the Senegalese port of Dakar.

  • Private Car: Private cars can be hired in Senegal and Guinea-Bissau for transportation to Gambia.

How to Get Around in Gambia

Taxis and shared minivans, known as bush taxis, are the most common form of transportation in Gambia. Visitors should negotiate prices for taxis in advance and should expect to pay slightly more than locals for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Gambia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore West Africa, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Some of the best include:

  • Dakar, Senegal: Just across the border from Gambia, Dakar is a vibrant capital city with a rich history and culture.

  • Sine-Saloum Delta National Park: Located in Senegal, this national park is home to a diverse array of wildlife and natural scenery.

  • Bijagos Islands, Guinea-Bissau: A collection of 88 islands off the coast of Guinea-Bissau, the Bijagos Islands offer a unique glimpse into West African culture and wildlife.

Conclusion

With its welcoming locals, stunning natural scenery, and unique cultural heritage, Gambia is a must-visit destination for any traveler to West Africa. Whether you’re interested in hiking, wildlife spotting, or cultural activities, there’s something for everyone in Gambia. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of this “Smiling Coast of Africa” for yourself.
