A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Janjanbureh, Gambia

Janjanbureh, formerly Georgetown, is a lively town on an island in the middle of the Gambia River. As one of the oldest and most accessible towns in The Gambia, it attracts visitors from all over the world who come to discover its culture, history, and natural beauty. This comprehensive travel guide will help you get to know Janjanbureh and make your trip as amazing as possible.

Introduction to Janjanbureh

Janjanbureh is a town on an island in the middle of the river Gambia. The town is part of the Central River Region (CRR) of The Gambia, which covers an area of approximately 3,900km². It sits 108 kilometres east of the capital, Banjul.

Janjanbureh is steeped in history and culture. It was a strategic trading port for the slave trade during colonial times. Today, it is a vibrant town and home to many cultural and natural tourist attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Janjanbureh

Janjanbureh has many tourist attractions, both natural and cultural. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Janjanbureh.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Janjanbureh Island Museum: The museum is located in the heart of Janjanbureh town and is housed in a restored colonial building. It displays exhibits on the history of the slave trade, the colonial era, and The Gambia’s independence struggle.
  • James Island: James Island is located near Janjanbureh on the river Gambia. It is where slaves were held captive before being sent across the Atlantic. Today, it is a powerful reminder of the brutality of the slave trade.
  • Janjanbureh’s Colonial Architecture: The town has many colonial-style buildings from the 19th century when the British were in control. Walk around the streets with a guide to see the various architectural designs.

Natural Attractions of Janjanbureh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baboon Island National Park: The park sits a short boat ride from Janjanbureh’s shores. It boasts of rare chimps, baboons, colobus monkeys, and other bird species. A visit to the park is a must for nature lovers.
  • River Gambia National Park: It covers a vast area of the Gambia’s river landscape—ideal for birdwatchers, anglers, and boating enthusiasts. The park’s central point is Kunta Kinteh Island, which has significant historical importance in the slave trade era.
  • Denton Bridge: This is a scenic stop while boating along River Gambia. The bridge connects the north and south banks of the river Gambia. You can catch a glimpse of fishing boats, ferries, and even dolphins from the bridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Janjanbureh

Janjanbureh’s landscape is ideal for outdoor enthusiasts who may be interested in hiking or biking. Here are some of the popular routes.

  • Kunta Kinteh Boating Safari – Wandifa’s Trail: The trail is a 40-kilometre scenic hike that takes place over three days. Along the way, visitors can experience the lush rainforest, historic sites, and visit several villages.
  • Baboon Island National Park – Chimpanzee Trek: A hiking and biking trip to the Baboon Island National Park offers a chance to discover an elusive chimp. The trail passes through thick and pristine forest and offers breathtaking views of the river.

Best Things to Do in Janjanbureh: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Janjanbureh beyond visiting the top tourist attractions. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Janjanbureh.

  • Birdwatching: The Gambia is a paradise for birdwatchers, and Janjanbureh is no exception. Birders can see different species of birds including kingfishers, sunbirds, and bee-eaters.
  • Scenic River Tours: A popular activity in Janjanbureh is a river tour. You can catch a glimpse of dolphins, hippos, or crocodiles while taking in the beautiful scenery along the river.
  • Indulge in Local Cuisine: No trip to Janjanbureh is complete without sampling the local food. Visitors can go into town to try local dishes like domoda, benachin, and akara.

Food and Drink Scene of Janjanbureh

Janjanbureh’s food and drink scene is a mix of local and international cuisines. However, visitors should try the local dishes and beverages to get a taste of the local culture.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Domoda: It is a stew made with peanut butter and served with rice. The dish is usually made with beef, but chicken, lamb, or fish can also be used.
  • Benachin: It is a one-pot dish with rice, vegetables, and meat. The dish is cooked with tomato, onion, and green pepper to make a flavorful and hearty meal.
  • Akara: It is a deep-fried dumpling made from black-eyed peas or beans. The dish is served as a snack or breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Attaya: It is a strong tea made from green tea leaves, sugar, and mint leaves. It is usually served three times, and drinking it is a social event.
  • Palm Wine: It is an alcoholic drink made from the fermented sap of palm trees. It has a slightly acidic taste and is served cold.

Where to Stay in Janjanbureh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Janjanbureh has several accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

  • Campama Riverside Lodge: The lodge offers stunning views of the river Gambia and provides boat tours, bike hire, and more.
  • Tendaba Camp: Tendaba Camp has many facilities, including a restaurant, bar, laundry, and Wi-Fi.
  • Janjanbureh Guesthouse: The guesthouse is in the heart of the town and offers many amenities, including a restaurant, a bar, and a garden.

What to Buy in Janjanbureh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy many local products and souvenirs in Janjanbureh. Here are some of the top items to purchase.

  • Batiks: Handmade batiks are a popular souvenir in Janjanbureh. They are colorful and display different designs and patterns.
  • Wood Carvings: The Gambia is known for its intricate wood carvings. Visitors can buy masks, statues, and other items carved from wood.
  • Beaded Jewelry: Colorful beaded jewelry is handmade in Janjanbureh. Visitors can buy necklaces, bracelets, or earrings.

Local Culture and Customs of Janjanbureh

Respect for local culture and traditions is an important part of any trip to Janjanbureh. Visitors should know that the native language is Mandinka, and greetings, like “Salaam Alaikum,” are essential.

Janjanbureh’s people are friendly and welcoming, and visitors will experience the warmth of their hospitality. Visitors should avoid excessive public display of affection, wearing revealing clothing, or drinking in public.

Best Time to Visit Janjanbureh

The Gambia is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit Janjanbureh is from November to May. During this period, the weather is dry and provides an excellent opportunity to visit the national parks and nature reserves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Janjanbureh

While visiting Janjanbureh, there are some things visitors should do and avoid doing.

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and peoples’ privacy.
  • Dress conservatively outside of the beach resorts.
  • Wear insect repellent and appropriate clothing when hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission.
  • Litter or disturb the wildlife.
  • Show excessive public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety and health should always be a priority when traveling to Janjanbureh. Here are some guidelines for staying healthy and safe.

Safety in Janjanbureh

  • Visitors should carry identification documents, such as a passport.
  • Visitors should only carry their essential possessions around town.
  • It is best to avoid walking alone after sunset.

Health in Janjanbureh

  • Visitors should be up to date with routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, rubella, and tetanus.
  • Malaria is prevalent in the region, and visitors should use mosquito repellents and mosquito nets.
  • Visitors should only drink bottled water or filtered water.

Getting to Janjanbureh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Janjanbureh requires flying into Banjul International Airport and then taking a taxi or bus to the town. Visitors can also take the local ferry from Banjul to Janjanbureh.

Airports Near Janjanbureh

  • Banjul International Airport: The airport is 112 kilometres from Janjanbureh. Visitors can take taxis or the public transport available at the airport to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Janjanbureh

  • Georgetown Bus Terminal: The terminal is in the heart of the town, and visitors can access it easily. Buses from Banjul and other major towns also get to this terminal.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Visitors can take a short ferry ride from Banjul to Janjanbureh. It is an excellent and serene way to enjoy the area’s natural beauty.

How to Get Around in Janjanbureh

Getting around Janjanbureh is relatively easy. Taxis and buses are available, but visitors can hire bicycles or walk around the town.

Where to Go Next from Janjanbureh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations visitors can explore from Janjanbureh. Here are a few places worth visiting.

  • Kuntaur: A small town known for its scenery and as a bird watching site.
  • Makasutu: A tropical reserve with a range of diverse ecosystems, including forest, savannah, and wetlands.
  • Sanyang: A popular beach destination in Gambia. Visitors can enjoy water sports or relax on the sandy beaches while watching the sunset.

Conclusion

Janjanbureh is a fascinating town with a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring the colonial architecture, outdoor adventures, and sampling local cuisine, there is something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their stay in Janjanbureh and bring back unforgettable memories of their time in The Gambia.
