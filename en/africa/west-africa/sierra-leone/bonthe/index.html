Introduction to Bonthe

Bonthe is a charming island town located in the Sherbro River Estuary in southern Sierra Leone. Founded in 1821 as a British colonial settlement, Bonthe has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and local traditions. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, lush vegetation, and opportunities for outdoor adventures like hiking, biking, and fishing.

List of Places to Visit in Bonthe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bonthe Museum

Bonthe Museum is located in the heart of the town and showcases the history and culture of Bonthe. The museum has a collection of artifacts and exhibits that portray the daily life and traditions of the Sherbro people, including their arts and crafts, music, and religion.

St. John’s Anglican Church

St. John’s Anglican Church is a historic building that dates back to the 19th century. The church is built in a colonial style and has beautiful stained-glass windows and a bell tower. It is a popular attraction for its unique architecture and its significance in the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Bonthe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bonthe Beach

Bonthe Beach is a long stretch of golden sand that offers stunning views of the river and the ocean. The beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and beach walks. Visitors can also enjoy water sports like surfing, kayaking, and snorkeling.

Bonthe Island Nature Reserve

Bonthe Island Nature Reserve is a protected area that covers 3,000 hectares of land and water. The reserve is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, including mangrove trees, marine turtles, and tropical fish. Visitors can explore the reserve on foot, by boat, or by bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bonthe

Bonthe is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. There are several scenic trails and routes that offer breathtaking views of the river, the ocean, and the surrounding countryside.

Sherbro River Trail

The Sherbro River Trail is a popular hiking and biking trail that follows the course of the Sherbro River. The trail offers stunning views of the river and the mangrove forests that line its banks. Visitors can also spot a variety of birds and wildlife along the way.

Bonthe Island Trail

The Bonthe Island Trail is a scenic route that takes visitors along the coastline of Bonthe Island. The trail offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding villages. Visitors can also see local fishermen at work and learn about the local fishing industry.

Best Things to Do in Bonthe: Activities and Tours

Sherbro River Tour

A Sherbro River Tour is an exciting way to explore the beauty and culture of Bonthe. Visitors can take a boat ride along the river and visit local fishing villages, see the mangrove forests, and spot wildlife such as crocodiles, sea turtles, and dolphins.

Beach Day

A day spent at Bonthe Beach is a great way to soak up the sun and enjoy the beautiful scenery. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, and enjoy water sports like surfing and kayaking. The beach is also a great spot for beach walks and picnics.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bonthe

For visitors who want to spend a week in Bonthe, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Bonthe and explore the town.
  • Day 2: Visit the Bonthe Museum and St. John’s Anglican Church.
  • Day 3: Take a Sherbro River Tour and visit local fishing villages.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at Bonthe Beach.
  • Day 5: Go hiking or biking along the Sherbro River Trail.
  • Day 6: Explore Bonthe Island and visit local villages.
  • Day 7: Depart Bonthe.

Food and Drink Scene of Bonthe

Must-Try Dishes

Cassava Leaves

Cassava Leaves is a popular dish in Bonthe that is made from cassava leaves, palm oil, onions, and fish or meat. The leaves are ground into a paste and cooked with the other ingredients to create a rich and flavorful stew.

Groundnut Soup

Groundnut Soup is a soup made with peanut butter, tomatoes, onions, and fish or chicken. It is served with rice or fufu and is a popular dish in Bonthe.

Local Beverages

Palm Wine

Palm Wine is a traditional alcoholic beverage in Bonthe that is made from fermented sap taken from palm trees. It has a sweet and sour taste and is often served chilled.

Ginger Beer

Ginger Beer is a non-alcoholic beverage made from fresh ginger, sugar, and water. It is a refreshing drink that is popular in Bonthe.

What to Buy in Bonthe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Woodcarvings

Woodcarvings are a popular souvenir in Bonthe and can be found in local markets and shops. They are usually made from local woods like mahogany and ebony and feature designs of local wildlife and cultural symbols.

Batik Fabrics

Batik Fabrics are handmade textiles that are dyed using a wax-resist technique. They come in a variety of colors and designs and are a popular souvenir in Bonthe.

Local Culture and Customs of Bonthe

The Sherbro people of Bonthe are known for their rich culture and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and follow some basic etiquette:

  • Dress modestly when visiting local villages or religious sites.
  • Greet people with a handshake and ask for permission before taking photos.
  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.

Best Time to Visit Bonthe

The best time to visit Bonthe is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. The weather is warm and sunny, and the water is calm and clear. The rainy season, which runs from May to November, can make travel difficult and some attractions may be closed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bonthe

Do:

  • Try local cuisine like cassava leaves and groundnut soup.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting local villages or religious sites.
  • Take photos without permission.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bonthe

Bonthe is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take some precautions:

  • Avoid walking alone late at night.
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Follow local advice regarding security issues.

Health in Bonthe

Visitors to Bonthe should take some health precautions:

  • Get recommended vaccinations before traveling.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent malaria and other insect-borne diseases.

Getting to Bonthe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bonthe

The closest airport to Bonthe is Lungi International Airport in Freetown. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight to Bonthe Airport or a ferry to Bonthe Island.

Bus Stations Near Bonthe

There are no bus stations in Bonthe. Visitors can take a taxi or hire a private car to get around the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bonthe by taking a ferry from the town of Tombo or by hiring a private boat from Freetown.

How to Get Around in Bonthe

Visitors can explore Bonthe on foot, by bike, or by taxi. Bikes and taxis are available for hire in the town.

Where to Go Next from Bonthe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tiwai Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Tiwai Island Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can take a guided tour and spot monkeys, birds, and other animals. The sanctuary is located about 70 km from Bonthe.

Outamba-Kilimi National Park

Outamba-Kilimi National Park is a national park that is home to a variety of wildlife such as primates, elephants, and leopards. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the park’s forests and savannahs. The park is located about 150 km from Bonthe.

Conclusion

Bonthe is a hidden gem in Sierra Leone that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the town’s museums and historical sites, enjoy outdoor adventures like hiking and biking, and sample the delicious local cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to this charming island town.
