Introduction to Burkina Faso

Located in West Africa, Burkina Faso is a vibrant and welcoming country that offers visitors a rich blend of traditional and modern culture, stunning natural scenery, and a unique taste of African life. From bustling marketplaces and ancient historic sites to breathtaking waterfalls and wildlife reserves, there is no shortage of things to see and do in Burkina Faso. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the best places to visit, the cultural and natural attractions, local customs and traditions, safety tips, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Burkina Faso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

A visit to Burkina Faso wouldn’t be complete without exploring some of the country’s significant cultural landmarks and historic sites. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • The Grand Mosque of Bobo-Dioulasso: Located in the country’s second-largest city, this stunning mosque is one of the largest in West Africa and a masterpiece of Sudano-Sahelian architecture.

  • The Ruins of Loropéni: This UNESCO World Heritage Site features the remains of an ancient fortress city that dates back over 1,000 years.

  • The Moro-Naba Palace: This traditional palace in Ouagadougou is a testament to the country’s history and culture and is home to the King of the Mossi people.

Natural Attractions of Burkina Faso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burkina Faso is not only culturally rich, but also offers an abundance of natural beauty waiting to be explored. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • The Waterfalls of Banfora: These stunning cascades are a popular spot for swimming, picnicking, and hiking.

  • The National Park of Bangr Weogo: This wildlife reserve is a haven for bird watchers and offers visitors the chance to spot elephants, jackals, and antelopes in their natural habitat.

  • The Gorges of Sindou: This unique geological formation features stunning rock formations that are perfect for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso offers visitors plenty of opportunities to stretch their legs and explore the country on foot or by bike. Here are some of the top routes:

  • The Cascades Loop: This picturesque route takes visitors through stunning countryside and past some of the country’s best waterfalls, including the Karfiguéla and Banfora falls.

  • The Parc National de la Pendjari route: This scenic route is perfect for wildlife enthusiasts looking to spot native species such as buffalo, hippos, and lions in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Burkina Faso: Activities and Tours

Burkina Faso has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors, including:

  • Visiting local markets for a taste of traditional African life.

  • Participating in traditional music and dance performances.

  • Enjoying a scenic hot air balloon ride for breathtaking views of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burkina Faso

If you’re wondering how to make the most of your time in Burkina Faso, here’s a sample itinerary to get you started:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Ouagadougou and explore the city’s markets and cultural landmarks.

  • Day 2: Take a day trip to the Gorges of Sindou for some hiking and stunning views.

  • Day 3-4: Explore the National Park of Bangr Weogo and spend some time spotting wildlife and enjoying nature walks.

  • Day 5-6: Head to Bobo-Dioulasso to explore the city’s Grand Mosque and other cultural landmarks.

  • Day 7: Take a scenic hot air balloon ride for one last look at the country’s natural beauty before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Burkina Faso

Must-Try Dishes

Burkina Faso is famous for its flavorful and spicy cuisine, which centers around traditional African dishes such as:

  • Poulet Yassa: A grilled chicken dish marinated in spicy lemon sauce.

  • Riz Gras: A rice pilaf dish cooked with meat, vegetables, and spices.

  • Bananes Plantains: Fried plantains that are a popular local snack.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Burkina Faso offers an array of refreshing drinks, such as:

  • Bissap: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers and sugar.

  • Zoom-kom: A traditional fermented drink made from millet.

  • Tchapalo: A local beer made from wheat or millet.

Where to Stay in Burkina Faso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burkina Faso offers visitors a range of comfortable and affordable accommodations, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Ouaga Beach: A beachfront hotel located near Ouagadougou’s airport that offers comfortable rooms and a swimming pool.

  • Guesthouse Chez Vincent: A popular guesthouse located in Bobo-Dioulasso that offers spacious rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

  • Hotel Palm Beach: A beachside hotel located in the popular tourist destination of Banfora that offers comfortable rooms and a spa.

What to Buy in Burkina Faso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burkina Faso is known for its handicrafts and unique souvenir items, such as:

  • Handwoven baskets and bags.

  • Intricately carved wooden masks.

  • Traditional clothing and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso is a friendly and welcoming country with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should show respect for local customs, such as dressing conservatively and removing shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Burkina Faso

The best time to visit Burkina Faso is during the dry season from November to February when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities and exploring. However, temperatures can be high during the day, so visitors should prepare accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burkina Faso

Do:

  • Dress conservatively to show respect for local customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Learn a few phrases in the local language to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people or their belongings without asking for permission first.

  • Show public displays of affection, as this is considered disrespectful.

  • Get too drunk in public, as this is frowned upon in the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso is generally a safe country, but visitors should exercise caution and avoid travelling alone at night or in isolated areas. It is also advisable to only use licensed taxis and buses for transportation.

Health in Burkina Faso

Visitors should take appropriate measures to protect their health, including:

  • Getting vaccinated against diseases such as yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid.

  • Drinking only bottled or purified water.

  • Using mosquito repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Burkina Faso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burkina Faso

The main airport for international flights to Burkina Faso is the Ouagadougou International Airport. There are also several domestic airports in the country, including the Bobo-Dioulasso Airport.

Bus Stations Near Burkina Faso

There are several international bus companies that operate in Burkina Faso, including ABC Transport and Rimbo Transport. There are also several local bus companies that offer transport within the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to arrive in Burkina Faso by car or train from neighboring countries such as Ghana, Ivory Coast, and Togo.

How to Get Around in Burkina Faso

The most common and convenient way to travel around Burkina Faso is by taxi or bus. Visitors should only use licensed taxis and bus companies and should negotiate prices in advance.

Where to Go Next from Burkina Faso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Burkina Faso is surrounded by several neighboring countries that offer plenty of exciting places to explore, including:

  • Ghana: Featuring stunning beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant cities, Ghana is a must-see destination for tourists.

  • Ivory Coast: Visitors can visit the gorgeous Assinie beach or explore the vibrant city of Abidjan.

  • Benin: This coastal country is home to the awe-inspiring Somba people and a range of fascinating cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Burkina Faso offers visitors an unforgettable glimpse into West African culture and traditions, all while surrounded by stunning natural scenery and diverse wildlife. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re well equipped to plan an unforgettable trip to Burkina Faso that will leave you with memories to last a lifetime.
