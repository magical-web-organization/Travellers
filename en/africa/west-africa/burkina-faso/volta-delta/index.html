Introduction to Volta Delta

Located in the heart of Burkina Faso, West Africa, Volta Delta is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. This region is home to traditional villages, breathtaking landscapes, and cultural attractions that offer a glimpse into the country’s rich history and vibrant culture.

If you’re planning a trip to Volta Delta, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most out of your visit. From exploring natural wonders to indulging in local cuisine, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Volta Delta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musee de la Musique de Ouagadougou: This museum showcases a wide array of traditional musical instruments, costumes, and artifacts from the country’s various ethnic groups.

  • Grande Mosquee de Ouagadougou: One of the largest mosques in West Africa, this historic landmark boasts impressive Islamic architecture and intricate stonework.

  • Moro-Naba Palace: The former palace of the Gurunsi Kingdom, this sprawling complex now serves as a museum showcasing traditional Burkina Faso architecture and exhibits on traditional life.

Natural Attractions of Volta Delta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karfiguela Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is located in Karfiguela village and offers a refreshing escape from the hot West African sun. The surrounding landscape is also ideal for hiking and picnicking.

  • Nazinga Game Reserve: This wildlife sanctuary offers visitors a chance to view Burkina Faso’s native wildlife, including elephants, hippos, and baboons.

  • Cascade de Sissili: Situated in the heart of the Sissili province, this waterfall offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness and is a popular spot for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volta Delta

  • Bobo-Dioulasso to Banfora: This scenic route takes travelers through picturesque villages and natural landscapes, including forests, mountains, and rivers.

  • Gorom-Gorom to Dori: This popular hiking route winds through the desert landscape and takes travelers past remote villages and ancient ruins.

Best Things to Do in Volta Delta: Activities and Tours

  • Tour of Traditional Villages: Discover the unique traditions and way of life of the Mossi people with a tour of one of the many traditional villages in the region.

  • Hot Air Balloon Safari: Take to the skies and explore Burkina Faso’s landscapes from a different perspective with a hot air balloon tour.

  • Local Craft Markets: Shop for locally made handicrafts, including pottery, textiles, and jewelry, at one of the many bustling markets in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Volta Delta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poulet Bicyclette: This popular dish features roasted chicken cooked over an open flame.

  • Bissap Juice: Made from the hibiscus flower, Bissap juice is a refreshing, tart beverage that is popular throughout Burkina Faso.

  • Peanut Sauce: A staple in West African cuisine, peanut sauce is often served over rice and features a blend of spices and ingredients unique to each region.

Local Beverages

  • Dolo: A type of traditional beer made from fermented millet, Dolo is a popular beverage in Burkina Faso and can be found at local roadside stalls.

  • Palm Wine: Made from the sap of palm trees, palm wine is a slightly sweet alcoholic beverage that is popular throughout West Africa.

Where to Stay in Volta Delta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Laico Ouaga 2000: This luxurious hotel in Ouagadougou features an outdoor pool and spa, as well as several on-site dining options.

  • El Farik Hotel: With stunning views of the nearby Karfiguela Waterfall, El Farik Hotel is a popular accommodation for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Nazinga Game Ranch: This wildlife reserve offers comfortable accommodations in traditional rondavels and chalets.

What to Buy in Volta Delta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Handcrafted pottery is a popular souvenir and can be found at local markets and workshops.

  • Textiles: Brightly colored textiles featuring traditional West African prints make for a vibrant and unique souvenir.

  • Jewelry: Local artisans create intricate, one-of-a-kind jewelry pieces featuring beads and semi-precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Volta Delta

  • Greeting: It is customary to greet locals with a handshake and a smile.

  • Dress Code: Burkina Faso is a conservative country, and visitors should dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Volta Delta

The best time to visit Volta Delta is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this time, temperatures are cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volta Delta

Do:

  • Learn a few French phrases: French is the official language of Burkina Faso and knowing a few basic phrases will go a long way in communicating with locals.

  • Respect local customs: Burkina Faso is a conservative country with strict societal norms. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission: It is considered rude to take photos of people without their consent.

  • Eat with your left hand: The left hand is considered unclean, and it is considered impolite to eat with it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Volta Delta

While Burkina Faso is generally safe, travelers should be aware of the risks of petty theft and street crime. It is also advisable to avoid traveling after dark and to stick to well-lit areas in cities.

Health in Volta Delta

Visitors to Burkina Faso should take precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne illnesses. It is also advisable to drink only bottled water and to seek medical advice before traveling to the region.

Getting to Volta Delta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Volta Delta

  • Ouagadougou International Airport

Bus Stations Near Volta Delta

  • Ouagadougou Central Bus Station

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Ouagadougou for travel within the region.

How to Get Around in Volta Delta

Public transportation options in Volta Delta are limited, and most visitors choose to hire a private driver or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Volta Delta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Niamey, Niger: Just across the border, Niamey is the capital of Niger and is known for its colorful markets and traditional architecture.

  • Ghana: Head south to explore the charming capital city of Accra and the nearby natural wonders of Cape Coast and Kakum National Park.

Conclusion

Volta Delta may not be the most well-known travel destination, but for those seeking an authentic West African experience, it is nothing short of magical. With its vibrant culture, breathtaking landscapes, and warm hospitality, Burkina Faso is a must-visit for any adventurous traveler.
