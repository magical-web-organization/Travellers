Introduction to Ouagadougou

Ouagadougou is the capital of Burkina Faso, located in the Volta Delta region of West Africa. Known for its vibrant culture, street markets, and warm hospitality, Ouagadougou is a popular destination for tourists seeking an authentic African experience.

List of Places to Visit in Ouagadougou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of Burkina Faso

The National Museum of Burkina Faso houses a vast collection of traditional and contemporary art, including masks, sculptures, and textiles from various ethnic groups across the country. It also features exhibitions on Burkinabe history and culture, including the colonial period and the country’s struggle for independence.

Laongo Sculpture Symposium

The Laongo Sculpture Symposium is a unique outdoor art exhibition that brings together sculptors from across Africa and the world. Located in the granite massifs near Ouagadougou, it features dozens of large-scale sculptures that are integrated into the surrounding landscape.

Moro-Naba Palace

The Moro-Naba Palace is the official residence of the chief of the Mossi people, the largest ethnic group in Burkina Faso. Built in the 15th century, it features a complex of courtyards, huts, and shrines that reflect traditional Mossi architecture and design.

Natural Attractions of Ouagadougou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ouagadougou Forest Reserve

The Ouagadougou Forest Reserve is a lush, green oasis in the heart of the city. It covers over 2,000 acres and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including monkeys, squirrels, and over 150 species of birds. Visitors can go for a hike or a picnic in the shade of the trees.

Ziniaré Lake

Ziniaré Lake is a popular destination for families and nature lovers. Located just outside the city, it offers opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating. Visitors can also rent a bike and explore the scenic trails around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ouagadougou

Hiking Trails

Dôgô Village Trail

The Dôgô Village Trail is a short hike that takes visitors through the traditional Mossi village of Dôgô, located just outside the city. Along the way, visitors can see how locals live and work, including traditional crafts such as pottery and weaving.

Biking Trails

Laongo and Tanghin Bike Tour

The Laongo and Tanghin Bike Tour is a scenic route that takes visitors through the Laongo Sculpture Symposium and the Tanghin Reserve. Along the way, bikers can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and stop to see sculptures created by artists from around Africa.

Best Things to Do in Ouagadougou: Activities and Tours

City Tour

A City Tour is the perfect way to get an overview of Ouagadougou’s history and culture. A guide will take visitors around the city, showing them landmarks such as the National Museum, Moro-Naba Palace, and the Grand Mosque, while providing insight into the city’s unique character and local customs.

Village Tour

A Village Tour allows visitors to experience the traditional way of life in Burkina Faso. Visitors can see how locals cultivate crops, create traditional handicrafts, and celebrate their customs and beliefs during festivals and ceremonies.

Food and Drink Scene of Ouagadougou

Must-Try Dishes

Poulet Yassa

Poulet Yassa is a popular dish in Burkina Faso made with marinated chicken, onions, peppers, and lemon juice. It is usually served with rice or couscous.

Riz Gras

Riz Gras is a traditional Burkinabe dish made with rice, vegetables, and meat, usually chicken or beef. It is seasoned with ginger, garlic, and onions, and is a staple in many households.

Local Beverages

Bissap

Bissap is a traditional Burkinabe drink made from the dried petals of hibiscus flowers, which gives it a tangy, fruity flavor. It is often served cold and sweetened with sugar.

Dolo

Dolo is a locally brewed beer made from millet or sorghum. It has a unique flavor and is a popular drink in Burkina Faso.

Where to Stay in Ouagadougou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Laïco

Located in the heart of Ouagadougou, Hotel Laïco is a luxurious option for travelers seeking comfort and convenience. It boasts an outdoor pool, fitness center, and spa, as well as a rooftop restaurant with panoramic views of the city.

Auberge le Pélican

Auberge le Pélican is a charming guesthouse located in a quiet neighborhood just outside the city center. It offers spacious rooms and a peaceful garden, as well as an on-site restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

What to Buy in Ouagadougou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burkina Faso Masks and Sculptures

Burkina Faso is known for its traditional masks and sculptures, made by hand using wood, metal, and other materials. They are unique and beautiful mementos of a trip to the country.

Shea Butter Products

Shea butter is a popular ingredient in Burkinabe skincare products, known for its moisturizing and restorative properties. Visitors can buy locally made soaps, lotions, and balms to take home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Ouagadougou

Burkinabe culture is a rich blend of traditional and modern influences. Visitors should be aware of local customs when traveling in the country, such as dressing modestly and asking permission before taking photos of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Ouagadougou

The best time to visit Ouagadougou is during the dry season, from November to April, when the weather is warm and sunny. The rainy season, from May to October, can be hot and humid, with frequent downpours and occasional flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ouagadougou

Do:

  • Take the time to learn about local customs and etiquette
  • Respect the culture and beliefs of the Burkinabe people
  • Bargain politely at markets and vendors

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission
  • Wear revealing clothing or attire that is considered disrespectful
  • Drink tap water or eat uncooked food that may cause health problems

Safety and Health

Safety in Ouagadougou

Burkina Faso is generally a safe country, but visitors should be aware of the threat of terrorism and take appropriate precautions, such as avoiding crowded areas and following the advice of local authorities.

Health in Ouagadougou

Visitors should be aware of the risk of malaria in Burkina Faso and take preventative measures, such as using insect repellent and taking anti-malarial medication. Tap water is not safe to drink, so visitors should stick to bottled or purified water.

Getting to Ouagadougou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ouagadougou

Ouagadougou Airport is the main international gateway to Burkina Faso, with flights to a variety of European and African destinations.

Bus Stations Near Ouagadougou

The main bus terminal in Ouagadougou is the Gare Routière, located in the city center. From here, visitors can take buses to destinations across Burkina Faso and beyond.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available in Ouagadougou, although visitors should be aware of the state of the roads and the risk of traffic accidents.

How to Get Around in Ouagadougou

Motorbike Taxis

Motorbike taxis, known as “motos”, are a popular and affordable way to get around Ouagadougou. Visitors should always wear a helmet and negotiate the fare before getting on.

Car Rentals

Car rentals are available, but visitors should be aware of the challenges of driving in the city, including poor road conditions and heavy traffic.

Where to Go Next from Ouagadougou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Banfora

Banfora is a town located in southwestern Burkina Faso, known for its stunning natural beauty, including waterfalls, lakes, and canyons. Visitors can take a day trip or spend a few days exploring the area.

Niangoloko

Niangoloko is a small town located on the border with Ghana, known for its traditional handicrafts and markets. Visitors can spend a day shopping for souvenirs and meeting local artisans.

Conclusion

Ouagadougou is a vibrant and welcoming city, rich with history and culture. Whether visitors are interested in museums, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming capital city of Burkina Faso. With our comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip with confidence and enjoy all that Ouagadougou has to offer.
