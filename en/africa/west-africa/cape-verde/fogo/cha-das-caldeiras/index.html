Introduction to Chã das Caldeiras

Located in Fogo, Cape Verde, Chã das Caldeiras is a one-of-a-kind volcanic valley situated on the Fogo island’s slopes. The village also offers breathtaking views of the island, which has been nicknamed “The Island of Fire.” This picturesque location’s crater reveals that it is a vibrant agricultural centre with a unique microclimate that allows for cultivation on the lava fields. There are many reasons to visit Chã das Caldeiras, but the most notable is to experience the simple and quiet life of Cape Verdean locals who have made ingenious use of their harsh surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Chã das Caldeiras

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Museu - A museum located in the centre of the village with exhibitions featuring the local history of Fogo, the island’s eruptions, and traditional customs.
  • Pico do Fogo - The highest peak on the island, offering climbers breathtaking views, and a challenging climb to the summit.
  • Capelinha - A religious centre located at the centre of the village, where locals and visitors come to pray and find solace.
  • Boca de Forno - A local landmark where visitors can experience an active lava flow after sunset.

Natural Attractions of Chã das Caldeiras: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lava Fields - Take a walk across the vast lava fields and witness the regrowth of vegetation from the soils.
  • Gato - A rare location found on the Chã das Caldeiras slope that still has geysers and holes that release gases that make hissing sounds.
  • Serra Malagueta National Park - This park has footprints left by African slaves and is home to many wildlife species.
  • Forest of Monte Velho - The only tropical dry forest with endemic species in Cape Verde.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chã das Caldeiras

Chã das Caldeiras is an ideal location for hikers, nature lovers, and adventure seekers. It’s surrounded by nature, and visitors can take a walk to the top of Pico do Fogo, bike to Santa Catarina or enjoy a pleasant hiking trip through this unique landscape. There are many paths for people of all fitness levels, making the valley accessible to everyone.

Best Things to Do in Chã das Caldeiras: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Fogo Island - Discover Fogo island which has a 21 km long coatline, is home to rare wildlife, and is surrounded by vast multicoloured landscapes.
  • Visit Coffee Plantations - Coffee is the primary crop of the valley, and people come from worldwide to inspect the production process in Chã das Caldeiras.
  • Local Tours - Guides are available to take visitors around the village to see how life is in Chã das Caldeiras.

Food and Drink Scene of Chã das Caldeiras

Must-Try Dishes

  • Catchupa - A traditional stew made with corn, beans, and vegetables; a staple food in Chã das Caldeiras.
  • Feijão - A nutritious bean soup made with beans, meat, vegetables and maize.
  • Fogo Wolfish - A local specialty made with fresh fish and served together with cilantro, tomato, onions, and peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Grogue - A rum-like drink made using sugarcane.
  • Ponche - A sweet, fruit-based alcoholic drink made with rum.

Where to Stay in Chã das Caldeiras: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various options for accommodations in Chã das Caldeiras, with options ranging from guesthouses to luxury hotels.

  • Casa Marisa - A family guesthouse in the centre of the village;
  • Crater Rim House - A unique guesthouse located close to the Pico do Fogo trailhead;
  • Hotel Colonial - A budget-friendly colonial-style hotel in São Filipe.

What to Buy in Chã das Caldeiras: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee - Chã das Caldeiras is renowned for its coffee, which is described as being of a unique quality.
  • Artisanal weavings - This local handicraft is made using colourful and traditional materials, making it a perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Handicrafts - The village is home to many skilled artists who produce beautiful pottery pieces, jewellery, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Chã das Caldeiras

Chã das Caldeiras is renowned for its rich Cape Verdean culture, with its residents holding on to traditional customs such as handcrafted weavings, pottery, and carpentry. The locals of Chã das Caldeiras are friendly and more than willing to share a laugh or two with visitors, but it’s important to adhere to the local customs.

Best Time to Visit Chã das Caldeiras

The best times to visit Chã das Caldeiras are between October and April when the climate is cool, and the vegetation is lush.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chã das Caldeiras

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions - The locals of Chã das Caldeiras hold onto their traditions, and it’s important to respect them.
  • Pack sensible clothing items - The weather can vary, and it’s best to dress in layers.
  • Try the local cuisine - Cape Verdean cuisine is a treat to the taste buds, so it’s important to try local dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter - The locals take care of their surroundings, and visitors should respectfully not litter.
  • Waste water - The local community relies on a limited water supply, so it’s important to conserve water when possible.
  • Take pictures of people without permission - It’s important to respect people’s privacy and get permission before taking their pictures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chã das Caldeiras

Chã das Caldeiras is generally considered a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions. It’s important to carry a map, tell someone where you’re going, and avoid carrying valuables around.

Health in Chã das Caldeiras

Visitors should carry a mosquito repellent and sunscreen as the island can be hot and humid. Medical services may be limited, so it’s necessary to have comprehensive travel insurance.

Getting to Chã das Caldeiras: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chã das Caldeiras

The closest airport to Chã das Caldeiras is São Filipe, which is approximately 10 km away.

Bus Stations Near Chã das Caldeiras

Public buses do not run regularly, and visitors may need to hire a car or take a taxi to reach Chã das Caldeiras.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car from São Filipe to reach Chã das Caldeiras.

How to Get Around in Chã das Caldeiras

Chã das Caldeiras is small enough to explore on foot, and bicycles are available for hire to go to other villages.

Where to Go Next from Chã das Caldeiras: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • São Filipe - A port town with a tropical atmosphere and winding cobbled streets.
  • Monte Grande - A village located on the slopes of the Pico do Fogo, and home to a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Cova Figueira - A quiet oasis, defined by a sparsely populated area with a few homes at the base of Fogo’s rocky mountains.

Conclusion

Chã das Caldeiras is a remarkable location, with its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty and friendly people, providing an immersive experience for visitors. The village offers various attractions, outdoor activities and different accommodation options making it a unique travel destination for everyone.
