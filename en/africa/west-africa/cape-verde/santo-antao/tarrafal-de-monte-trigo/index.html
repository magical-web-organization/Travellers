Introduction to Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

Tarrafal de Monte Trigo is a picturesque village that sits in the breathtaking Santo Antão island of Cape Verde. This hidden gem may be small, but it offers visitors stunning scenery, delicious local cuisine, and friendly locals. If you’re a nature lover, thrill-seeker or history buff, this is the ideal spot to visit for an authentic African experience.

List of Places to Visit in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tarrafal de Monte Trigo Church

Located right in the heart of the village, the Church of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo is a significant landmark that dates back to the 18th century. The church features intricate stonework and murals depicting Biblical scenes.

Ethnographic Museum

The Ethnographic Museum is a small yet fascinating attraction in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo, which showcases the local history and culture of the island through ancient tools, clothing, and musical instruments.

Natural Attractions of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Monte Trigo Viewpoint

Located about 1.5 km from the village, the Monte Trigo Viewpoint offers a stunning 360-degree panoramic view of the scenic valley and coastal landscape. Get your camera ready!

Ribeira Grande Valley

For hikers, this a must-visit destination. A challenging trek from the village to the beautiful Ribeira Grande valley offers unparalleled views of the mountainous terrain, and for the more adventurous, there are enormous rock formations to climb.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

There are plenty of stunning trails that offer hikers and bikers a chance to explore the beautiful countryside of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Ribeira Grande Valley Trails
  • Monte Trigo to Cruzinha Trail
  • Monte Trigo to Porta do Sol

Best Things to Do in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo: Activities and Tours

  • Catamaran Tours: Take a relaxing and scenic catamaran tour featuring stunning views of the coastline and the chance to spot whales and dolphins.
  • Surfing: Tarrafal de Monte Trigo offers a great surf spot for beginners and advanced surfers alike, with a variety of surf schools available to take day or week-long lessons.
  • Snorkelling: The crystal-clear waters of the Atlantic offer excellent opportunities for snorkelling and discovering the coral below.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

Day 1- Arrive in Santa Antão Island and settle at your lodgings.

Day 2- Visit the Tarrafal de Monte Trigo Church then head to the Ethnographic Museum.

Day 3- Hike the Ribeira Grande Valley Trails.

Day 4 - Enjoy a Catamaran Tour of the coast.

Day 5- Surfing lessons at the nearest surf school.

Day 6- Snorkelling in the Atlantic waters.

Day 7- Relax for a day by the beach, then depart for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cachupa: a popular dish served with beans, vegetables, and meat or fish.

  • Grilled Fish: Fresh fish served up grilled or pan-fried with a side of vegetables and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Grogue: A local spirit made from sugarcane and traditionally distilled in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo. A must-try for adults.

  • Fogo Coffee: A high-quality coffee grown in the nearby Fogo Island that is sure to satisfy coffee enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

The culture here is shaped by African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences. One of the most interesting cultural experiences is the local ‘Batuko’ music, a drum sound that is often played during village festivities. Visitors must respect the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

The best time to visit Tarrafal de Monte Trigo is between November to June, as these months offer the most pleasant weather conditions. It’s especially enjoyable from December to May, and the surrounding valleys are lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Bring your sunglasses and sunscreen for the sunnier days

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Litter on the beaches or hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

Tarrafal de Monte Trigo has a reputation for being a safe destination for tourists. As with any destination, visitors should take care of their belongings, stay on designated hiking trails and avoid venturing in areas not generally known for hiking. Exercise caution and be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

We recommend getting vaccinated before venturing to Cape Verde. Wear sunscreen to avoid sunburn since the tropical sun can be extremely harsh.

Getting to Tarrafal de Monte Trigo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

The closest international airport to Tarrafal de Monte Trigo is the Amilcar Cabral International Airport, located in Sal Island. From there, you can take a domestic flight to Rabil Airport in Santo Antão Island.

Bus Stations Near Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

The bus station is located just outside the airport, and you can take a taxi or shared four-wheel drive car to Tarrafal de Monte Trigo.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can hire a car or take a private transfer from the airport. Bear in mind that the island routes can be extremely bumpy, and 4WD vehicles are recommended.

How to Get Around in Tarrafal de Monte Trigo

The village is relatively small and offers many fantastic walking trails. However, if you prefer, there are three-wheeled taxis (called Gatos) available. They are a fun way to travel from the guesthouses, and many drivers also offer tours.

Where to Go Next from Tarrafal de Monte Trigo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Paul Valley: A neighbouring village that offers stunning hikes and views.
  • Ribeira Grande: The most extensive valley in the island, and yet another hiking paradise.
  • Porto Novo: A historic town located south of Santo Antão Island that also offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Conclusion

Tarrafal de Monte Trigo, with its natural beauty, friendly locals and rich culture, is an excellent escape for anyone looking for a unique African experience. Be respectful of the culture and customs, hiking safely and respectfully of the land, and with these tips, you can plan your visit to make the most of what Tarrafal de Monte Trigo has to offer.
