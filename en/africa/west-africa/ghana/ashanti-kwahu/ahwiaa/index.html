Introduction to Ahwiaa

Located in the Ashanti-Kwahu region of Ghana, Ahwiaa is a small town with a rich cultural history. Known for its talented woodcarvers who create intricate and beautiful carvings for both local use and export, Ahwiaa is a must-visit for anyone interested in art and craft. Apart from its elaborate woodcarvings, Ahwiaa also boasts a warm and welcoming community, delicious cuisine, and natural beauty that will take your breath away.

List of Places to Visit in Ahwiaa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ahwiaa Woodcarving Center: The Woodcarving Centre in Ahwiaa offers an opportunity to witness the skill and creativity of the local woodcarvers. Visitors can watch as the artisans create beautiful sculptures and masks from a variety of woods, such as ebony, mahogany, and cedar.
  • Ahwiaa Museum: The Ahwiaa Museum is a treasure trove of traditional art and crafts. It showcases the rich cultural heritage of Ahwiaa, including ancient artifacts such as ceramics, textiles, and other traditional materials. The museum is an excellent place to visit to learn more about the history of the town.
  • Besease Shrine: This historic shrine is located just outside Ahwiaa and is dedicated to a local deity. Many people believe that the shrine has healing powers, and it is a popular pilgrimage site for locals and tourists.

Natural Attractions of Ahwiaa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Bosomtwe: Just 30 minutes away, Lake Bosomtwe is a beautiful lake surrounded by rolling hills. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing and relaxing on the lake’s shore while soaking in the beauty of the surroundings.
  • Ahwiaa Forest Reserve: The Ahwiaa Forest Reserve is a beautiful natural reserve home to a wide variety of indigenous plant and animal species. Visitors can explore the reserve on hiking trails, which provide a great opportunity to experience the region’s natural beauty up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ahwiaa

There are several excellent hiking and biking routes in Ahwiaa, each offering a unique way to explore the area’s natural beauty. Some popular routes include hiking to the top of Mount Afadjato, the highest peak in Ghana, and taking a bike tour through the hills around the town.

Best Things to Do in Ahwiaa: Activities and Tours

  • Woodcarving Workshops: Take a woodcarving workshop to learn how to make your own traditional African art pieces. The Ahwiaa Woodcarving Centre offers classes for all skill levels, and it is a must-do activity while in town.
  • Traditional Dance and Drumming Shows: Enjoy a traditional African dance and drumming performance in Ahwiaa. The energetic performances will keep you entertained throughout the night while also giving you a chance to learn more about local culture and heritage.
  • Lake Tours: Take a boat tour at the beautiful Lake Bosomtwe, where you can enjoy the gorgeous scenery and fresh air while learning about the history and culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ahwiaa

Day 1: Arrive in Ahwiaa, and visit the Woodcarving Centre Day 2: Take a traditional dance and drumming class Day 3: Visit the Ahwiaa Museum and Besease Shrine Day 4: Go on a hike in the Ahwiaa Forest Reserve Day 5: Take a bike tour in the hills around Ahwiaa Day 6: Take a boat tour on Lake Bosomtwe Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Ahwiaa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fufu and Soup: A local favorite, Fufu and soup is made from cassava and served with a rich, flavorful soup.
  • Jollof Rice: This rice and tomato-based dish is a staple throughout Ghana and is a must-try while in town.
  • Banku and Tilapia: Banku is a corn-based food that is typically eaten with grilled fish, such as tilapia.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: Made from the sap of palm trees, Palm wine is a local delicacy and a must-try for anyone visiting Ahwiaa.
  • Sobolo: A refreshing hibiscus tea that is served chilled.

Where to Stay in Ahwiaa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kongo Hotel: A comfortable and affordable hotel in the heart of Ahwiaa, offering both single and double rooms.
  • Lakeside Guesthouse: A beautiful guesthouse overlooking Lake Bosomtwe, with comfortable rooms and stunning views.

What to Buy in Ahwiaa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woodcarvings: Ahwiaa is known for its beautiful woodcarvings, ranging from traditional masks and sculptures to household items.
  • Textiles: The town is also home to many skilled weavers who produce beautiful fabrics and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Ahwiaa

  • Greetings: Traditional greetings in Ahwiaa are an important aspect of local culture. It is customary to ask about family and health before engaging in other conversations.
  • Dress Code: Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully when in town, avoiding revealing clothing.

Best Time to Visit Ahwiaa

The best time to visit Ahwiaa is during the dry season, which runs from November to March when temperatures are cooler and rainfall is less frequent.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ahwiaa

Do:

  • Be respectful of local culture and customs
  • Try new foods and experiences
  • Learn some basic phrases in the local language

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Take photos without permission
  • Be disrespectful to local traditions and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Ahwiaa

Ahwiaa is a relatively safe town, with low levels of crime. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should watch their belongings and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ahwiaa

Visitors should take precautions to avoid malaria, which is common in the region. It is recommended to take antimalarial medication and use mosquito nets.

Getting to Ahwiaa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ahwiaa

Kumasi International Airport is the closest airport to Ahwiaa, located about an hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Ahwiaa

There are several bus stations nearby, including the Kumasi Bus Station, which offers regular services to Ahwiaa.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired for transportation.

How to Get Around in Ahwiaa

The best ways to get around Ahwiaa are on foot, bicycle or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Ahwiaa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kumasi: Just an hour away, Kumasi is Ghana’s second-largest city and a great place to learn more about Ghana’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Kintampo Waterfalls: A stunning waterfall located just a few hours away from Ahwiaa, perfect for a day trip.

Conclusion

Ahwiaa is a town that is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. With plenty of activities to do and sights to see, it is the perfect destination for anyone looking for an authentic Ghanaian experience. From woodcarving workshops to traditional dance and drumming performances to stunning hikes and scenic drives, there is something for everyone in Ahwiaa.
