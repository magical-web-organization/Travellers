Introduction to Suhum

Suhum is a small town located in Ashanti-Kwahu, Ghana. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures. Visitors to Suhum can enjoy a mix of historical attractions, natural landscapes, and local Ghanaian culture. Whether you are into outdoor exploring or cultural sightseeing, there is plenty to see and do in Suhum. This guide will help you plan your trip to Suhum with suggestions for places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Suhum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Suhum Palace Museum: This museum is located in the Suhum Palace, which served as the residence of Suhum’s chiefs. Visitors can learn about the rich cultural history of Suhum through exhibits of traditional clothing, artifacts, and historical documents.

  • Gyaase Shrine: This shrine is located in the heart of Suhum and serves as a spiritual center for the local Kumasi people. Visitors can learn about the culture and traditions of the Kumasi people and see the traditional shrines and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Suhum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boti Falls: Boti Falls is a beautiful waterfall located near Suhum. Visitors can hike through the lush forest to reach the falls and enjoy the breathtaking views.

  • Aburi Botanical Gardens: The Aburi Botanical Gardens are located just outside of Suhum and are home to a variety of plant species from around the world. Visitors can take a leisurely walk through the gardens and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suhum

  • The Suhum to Boti Falls Trail: This trail is a moderately difficult hike that takes visitors from Suhum to Boti Falls through the lush forest of Ghana. The trail is marked with signs and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • The Aburi to Peduase Lodge Trail: This trail is an easy walk through the Aburi Forest Reserve, with a stop at the Peduase Lodge for a refreshing drink and scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Suhum: Activities and Tours

  • Suhum Cultural Tour: This tour takes visitors on a journey through the cultural history of Suhum, including visits to the Suhum Palace Museum and the Gyaase Shrine.

  • Boti Falls Adventure: This adventure includes a hike to the Boti Falls, followed by a refreshing swim in the natural pool below the falls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Suhum

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Suhum and check-in at your hotel. Take a leisurely walk around the town center and sample local foods.
Day 2 Visit the Suhum Palace Museum and the Gyaase Shrine for a cultural experience.
Day 3 Take the Suhum to Boti Falls Trail for a hike to the spectacular waterfall.
Day 4 Visit the Aburi Botanical Gardens and take in the natural beauty.
Day 5 Explore the local markets and buy souvenirs to take home.
Day 6 Take a cultural tour of nearby villages and learn about the customs and traditions of the Kumasi people.
Day 7 Relax at your hotel or take an optional tour depending on your interests.

Food and Drink Scene of Suhum

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fufu: Fufu is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from cassava and plantains. It is usually served with a soup or sauce.

  • Jollof Rice: Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made from rice, tomatoes, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Pito: Pito is a traditional Ghanaian beer made from millet or sorghum. It is a popular drink in Suhum.

  • Palm Wine: Palm Wine is a sweet, fermented drink made from the sap of palm trees. It is a popular drink in Suhum and other parts of Ghana.

Where to Stay in Suhum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hillside Guesthouse: The Hillside Guesthouse is a comfortable and affordable option in Suhum. It is located on a hillside with scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Suhum Presbyterian Guesthouse: The Suhum Presbyterian Guesthouse is a cozy and peaceful option in Suhum. It is located near the town center and offers easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Suhum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kente Cloth: Kente Cloth is a traditional Ghanaian fabric made from interwoven strips of silk and cotton. It is commonly used for clothing and other decorative purposes.

  • Adinkra Cloth: Adinkra Cloth is a traditional fabric made from cotton and decorated with Adinkra symbols. It is commonly used for clothing and other decorative purposes.

Local Culture and Customs of Suhum

The Kumasi people of Suhum have rich cultural traditions that are evident in their dress, music, and dance. Visitors to Suhum should respect local customs and traditions, which include wearing appropriate clothing and avoiding loud music or behavior.

Best Time to Visit Suhum

The best time to visit Suhum is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. The weather is mild and comfortable during this time, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suhum

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the Kumasi people.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and local customs.

  • Sample local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Engage in loud or disruptive behavior.

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

  • Carry large amounts of cash or valuable items.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suhum

Suhum is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. This includes avoiding areas that are known to be unsafe and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items.

Health in Suhum

Visitors to Suhum should take precautions to avoid illness, including drinking bottled or boiled water and avoiding undercooked or unhygienic food.

Getting to Suhum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Suhum

The nearest airport to Suhum is Kotoka International Airport in Accra, which is approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Suhum

The Suhum Bus Station is located in the town center, offering regular buses to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are available for hire in Suhum.

How to Get Around in Suhum

The best way to explore Suhum is on foot or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Suhum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kumasi: Kumasi is the capital of the Ashanti region and is known for its traditional crafts, markets, and cultural heritage.

  • Cape Coast: Cape Coast is a coastal town known for its slave trade history and colonial architecture.

Conclusion

Suhum is a wonderful destination for travelers looking to explore Ghana’s cultural heritage and natural beauty. By following our guide, you can plan your trip to Suhum and make the most of your visit. From hiking to cultural tours, you can explore this small town and surrounding area at your own pace.
