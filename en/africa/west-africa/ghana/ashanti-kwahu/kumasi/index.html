Introduction to Kumasi

Nestled in the heart of the Ashanti-Kwahu region of Ghana, Kumasi is the cultural and historical hub of the country. The city offers a perfect blend of ancient traditions, modern experiences, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique African experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kumasi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Manhyia Palace Museum

The Manhyia Palace Museum is a must-visit attraction in Kumasi. It is the official residence of the Asantehene, the king of the Ashanti Kingdom, and a great place to learn about the history, culture, and traditions of the Ashanti people. The museum houses several exhibits, including royal paraphernalia, photographs, and artifacts that showcase the rich history and heritage of the Ashanti Kingdom.

2. Adanwomase Kente Weaving Village

Visitors to Kumasi should take a trip to Adanwomase Kente Weaving Village, which lies about twenty minutes away from the city center. It is a place where locals produce Kente cloth, which is the traditional fabric of the Ashanti people. Here, you can observe the weaving process, interact with locals, and buy souvenirs.

3. Bonwire Kente Weaving Centre

Bonwire Kente Weaving Centre is another significant cloth-weaving village in Kumasi. It is home to several skilled craftsmen who create exquisite Kente designs. Tourists who visit the center can learn about the history of Kente weaving, observe the art in practice, and shop for authentic Kente designs.

4. Asante Traditional Buildings

For visitors looking for a less commercial attraction, Asante Traditional Buildings is a must-visit. The historical buildings are located in Barekese, which is a 45-minute drive out of the city center. Here, you can see traditional Ashanti homes, shrines, and even palaces. It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience the traditional architecture of Ghana.

Natural Attractions of Kumasi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Lake Bosomtwe

Lying on the periphery of Kumasi, Lake Bosomtwe is the largest natural lake in Ghana. Tourists can enjoy swimming, fishing, and boating in the serene waters of the lake. The area around the lake boasts of steaming geysers, breathtaking waterfalls, and picturesque landscapes, making it the perfect spot for nature lovers.

2. Owabi Wildlife Sanctuary

The Owabi Wildlife Sanctuary is located just outside of Kumasi, and it is an excellent spot for bird watching and nature walks. Visitors can expect to see a wide range of birds such as kingfishers, ospreys, kites, and several species of ducks. The Sanctuary also has some small game such as bushbacks, birds, opossums, and several others.

3. Bobiri Forest Reserve

Bobiri Forest Reserve is a great destination for camping, bird watching, and nature walks. The forest is home to over 500 different species of flora and fauna, including rare species of butterflies and birds. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, nature walks, and camp out in the forest for an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumasi

Hikers and bikers in Kumasi can opt to experience the rich historical landmarks within the city center, including the Manhyia Palace and the Kumasi Central Market. Those looking to go off the beaten path should head out to the nearby hills and mountains, such as the Owabi Mountains, which can be challenging for both beginners and seasoned hikers.

Best Things to Do in Kumasi: Activities and Tours

1. Culture and Heritage Tour of Kumasi

Visitors to Kumasi can get the best view of the city’s history and culture by taking a cultural heritage tour. The tour usually starts at the Manhyia Palace and takes visitors through the Adanwomase and Bonwire Kente weaving villages, Asante Traditional Buildings, and Kumasi Central market.

2. Street Food Tour

Street Food Tours are an excellent way to sample the best of Kumasi’s cuisine. Visitors can sample various local dishes such as fufu, jollof rice, and kenkey. The tour also includes a stop at a local bar, where visitors get to try out the locally brewed beer while listening to traditional Ghanaian music.

3. Lake Bosomtwe Boat Cruise

A boat cruise on Lake Bosomtwe is a relaxing way to spend an afternoon in Kumasi. The cruise offers a breathtaking view of the serene waters of the lake and the surrounding hills. Visitors can enjoy fishing, swimming, or simply enjoy the magnificent views.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumasi

The Ashanti people are proud of their culture and traditions. Visitors to Kumasi should familiarize themselves with the local customs, such as dressing modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting the palace or other traditional sites. Visitors should also respect local cultural norms with regard to displays of affection, especially in public.

Best Time to Visit Kumasi

Kumasi enjoys a tropical climate, with the rainy season running from April through to September, and the dry season running from December through to February. The best time to visit Kumasi is between December and April, when the weather is favorable, and most outdoor activities are accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumasi

Do:

  • Do respect local customs and traditions
  • Do try the local cuisine
  • Do carry enough cash for transactions
  • Do wear comfortable shoes when hiking or biking

Don’t:

  • Don’t engage in public displays of affection
  • Don’t photograph locals without permission
  • Don’t wander around at night in remote areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumasi

Kumasi is generally safe for tourists, with low rates of violent crime. However, visitors should always be on the lookout for pickpockets and scams in the busy marketplaces. It is advisable to carry just enough cash for transactions and avoid walking alone at night in remote areas.

Health in Kumasi

Before traveling to Kumasi, it is advisable to seek medical advice and get vaccinated against yellow fever and other tropical diseases. Visitors should also be cautious of the malaria risk and take precautions such as applying insect repellant, using bed nets, and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

How to Get Around in Kumasi

Kumasi is a relatively small city, and most of the attractions are within easy reach. Visitors can get around the city using taxis, tro-tros (minibusses), and car rentals. Biking and hiking are also great ways to explore the city’s scenic sights and off-beat areas.

Conclusion

Kumasi is a destination that offers a glimpse into the rich African heritage of the Ashanti people. With scenic natural beauty, friendly locals, and a wide range of activities, Kumasi should be on every traveler’s itinerary. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan your next visit to Kumasi with ease.
