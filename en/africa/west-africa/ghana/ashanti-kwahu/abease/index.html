Introduction to Abease

Located in the Ashanti-Kwahu region of Ghana, Abease is a small village brimming with natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance. Despite its size, Abease offers a plethora of exciting activities and experiences for visitors to indulge in. From hiking and biking to exploring historic landmarks and museums, this hidden gem of Ghana is a must-visit for any traveler seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. This travel guide will take you through everything you need to know to plan your dream trip to Abease.

List of Places to Visit in Abease

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Abease Museum: This museum is dedicated to exhibiting the history and culture of the Ashanti-Kwahu people. It features artifacts, artworks, and other displays showcasing the local traditions and way of life.
  • The Nkwatia Presbyterian Church: Built by the Presbyterian Church of Ghana in 1890, this historic church is an architectural marvel and a testament to the Christian faith of the local people.
  • The Tetteh Quarshie Cocoa Farm: Named after the man who brought cocoa beans to Ghana, this farm provides a unique opportunity to learn about the history of Ghana’s cocoa industry and how it has impacted the country’s economy over time.

Natural Attractions of Abease: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Boti Falls: One of the most impressive waterfalls in the region, the Boti Falls is a MUST-SEE attraction in Abease. The waterfall is surrounded by lush, green forests and is an ideal destination for hiking and picnicking.
  • The Umbrella Rock: This massive rock formation sits atop a hill and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike up to the rock and enjoy the scenery while learning about its cultural significance.
  • The Kwahu Scarp: A long stretch of cliffs that offer a panoramic view of the villages and towns that lie below. This attraction is perfect for adventurous travelers who enjoy hiking or rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abease

Abease has a variety of hiking and biking routes that showcase its natural beauty and cultural significance. Some popular routes include:

  • The Boti Falls Trail: This trail takes visitors on a scenic hike to the Boti Falls, offering stunning views of the rainforest and the falls themselves.
  • The Kwahu Scarp Trail: This trail takes hikers through the cliffs of the Kwahu Scarp, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Abease Village Trail: This route takes visitors on a tour of the village, allowing them to experience the local culture and to interact with villagers in their daily life.

Best Things to Do in Abease: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Guided Tour of the Abease Museum: An experienced guide will lead you through the museum and share interesting facts about the local culture and traditions.
  • Explore the Boti Falls: Spend a day hiking in the rainforest and taking a refreshing swim in the Boti Falls.
  • Attend the Odwira Festival: This annual festival is held in September and celebrates the Ashanti Kwahu people’s New Year. Visitors can enjoy traditional music and dance performances and taste local cuisine.
  • Visit the Umbrella Rock Cultural Center: Learn about the cultural significance of the Umbrella Rock while enjoying local entertainment and interacting with villagers.

Food and Drink Scene of Abease

Ghana is known for its delicious cuisine, and Abease is no exception. Visitors can enjoy local dishes and beverages, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fufu and Soup: Fufu is a starchy staple made from cassava, plantains, or yams. It is typically eaten with a soup made from goat or chicken, seasoned with local spices.
  • Jollof Rice: A spicy rice dish that is a staple in Ghanaian cuisine. It is typically eaten with grilled chicken or fried fish.
  • Banku: A doughy ball that is made from a mixture of cassava, maize, and water. It is typically eaten with soup or stew.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A sweet and sour alcoholic drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Sobolo: A refreshing drink made from dried hibiscus leaves, ginger, and pineapple.
  • Pito: A locally brewed beverage made from fermented millet.

Where to Stay in Abease: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Abease is a relatively small village, there are numerous accommodation options to choose from, ranging from economic guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Nyame Bekyere Hotel: A popular hotel that offers comfortable rooms and exceptional service.
  • Peacehaven Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse that is perfect for travelers on a budget.
  • Sasakawa Lodge: A luxurious lodge set amidst lush tropical gardens.

What to Buy in Abease: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers can bring home a variety of local products and souvenirs, including:

  • Kente Cloth: A colorful fabric that is woven by local artisans and is a symbol of Ghanaian culture.
  • Beaded Jewelry: Handmade jewelry made from colorful beads that are used to create intricate patterns and designs.
  • Hand-carved Wooden Masks: These masks are carved by local artisans and are often used in traditional celebrations and cultural performances.

Local Culture and Customs of Abease

The Ashanti-Kwahu people are known for their rich and unique culture, characterized by traditional religion, arts, and crafts. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions and to engage with the villagers in a respectful and dignified manner.

Best Time to Visit Abease

The best time to visit Abease is between September and December, during the dry season. This is when the weather is mild, and there are fewer mosquitos and other pests. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abease

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash with you, as many villagers do not accept credit cards.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Engage in any behavior that could be deemed disrespectful or insensitive to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abease

Abease is a relatively safe village. However, travelers should still take basic safety precautions, such as safeguarding their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Abease

Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from malaria and other diseases, such as yellow fever and cholera. It is recommended that travelers consult their healthcare provider before visiting Abease and take appropriate vaccines and medications.

Getting to Abease: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abease

The nearest airport to Abease is the Kumasi International Airport, which is located approximately 65 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Abease

There are several bus stations in the nearby towns of Kwahu Tafo and Mpraeso, from which visitors can catch a bus to Abease.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire in the nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Abease

The best way to get around Abease is on foot or by bicycle, as the roads in the village are narrow and winding. Taxis and private cars are also available for hire for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Abease: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Kintampo Waterfalls: Located approximately 120 kilometers north-west of Abease, this natural wonder features several waterfalls and swimming holes.
  • Kumasi: Known as the “Garden City,” Kumasi is the second-largest city in Ghana and an important cultural and economic center.
  • Lake Bosomtwe: Located approximately 90 kilometers south-west of Abease, this crater lake is a popular destination for fishing, swimming, and cultural tours.

Conclusion

Abease is truly a hidden gem of Ghana, offering rich cultural experiences and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Abease has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide has provided you with everything you need to know to plan your dream trip to Abease, Ghana. Happy travels!
