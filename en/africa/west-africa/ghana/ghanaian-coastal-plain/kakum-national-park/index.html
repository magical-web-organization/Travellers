Introduction to Kakum National Park

Kakum National Park is a protected forest area located in the Central Region of Ghana, West Africa. It covers about 375 square kilometers and is known for its diverse flora and fauna. The park is home to over 600 species of birds, 40 species of mammals, and many reptiles and insects.

Established in 1931, the park was officially opened to the public in 1994. Kakum National Park is the perfect destination for adventure seekers, nature enthusiasts, and anyone looking to experience the unique culture and traditions of Ghana.

List of Places to Visit in Kakum National Park

There are many places to visit in Kakum National Park, each offering a unique experience. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cape Coast Castle: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a must-see for anyone interested in Ghana’s history. It was one of the main ports of the Atlantic slave trade and is now a museum that tells the story of the enslaved Africans and their descendants.

  2. Elmina Castle: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, Elmina Castle was built by Portuguese traders in the 15th century. It was also used as a slave trading post and is now a museum.

  3. Posuban Shrines: These colorful buildings are traditional shrines that serve as protective houses for local communities.

Natural Attractions of Kakum National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Canopy Walkway: The canopy walkway is a must-do experience in Kakum National Park. The walkway is suspended 30 meters above the ground and offers breathtaking views of the forest canopy.

  2. Wildlife Viewing: Take a guided tour of the park to spot elephants, antelopes, monkeys, and other wildlife.

  3. Nature Hikes: The park has many hiking trails to choose from, each offering unique views of the flora and fauna of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kakum National Park

The park has many hiking and biking routes, ranging from easy to difficult. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in Kakum National Park:

  1. Old Wired Road Trail: This trail is 6.5 kilometers long and is rated moderate. It’s perfect for hiking or mountain biking.

  2. Kakum Canopy Walkway Trail: This trail is only 2.5 kilometers long and is rated easy. It’s perfect for anyone looking for an easy hike with stunning views.

  3. Antelope Trail: This trail is 21 kilometers long and is rated difficult. It’s perfect for experienced hikers looking for a challenge.

Best Things to Do in Kakum National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Canopy Walkway Experience: Take a guided tour of the canopy walkway for an unforgettable experience.

  2. Guided Wildlife Tour: Take a guided tour of the park to spot elephants, antelopes, monkeys, and other wildlife.

  3. Cultural Tour: Learn about the local culture and traditions of Ghana with a cultural tour of the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kakum National Park

Ghanaian cuisine is known for its bold flavors and use of local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Jollof Rice: This spicy tomato rice dish is a staple in Ghanaian cuisine.

  2. Banku and Tilapia: This dish consists of fermented corn dough and grilled tilapia fish and is a favorite in the coastal region.

  3. Kelewele: Spiced fried plantains are a popular snack in Ghana.

Local Beverages

  1. Palm Wine: This alcoholic drink is made from fermented palm tree sap.

  2. Sobolo: This drink is made from hibiscus flowers and is known for its refreshing taste.

  3. Bissap: This drink is made from dried hibiscus flowers and is also known for its refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Kakum National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in and around Kakum National Park, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Rainforest Lodge: This eco-friendly lodge is located within the park and offers comfortable rooms with stunning views.

  2. Hans Cottage Botel: This unique hotel is built around a man-made lake and offers comfortable rooms with beautiful views of the surrounding nature.

  3. Oasis Beach Resort: This beachfront resort is located about 20 kilometers from the park and offers luxury rooms with ocean views.

What to Buy in Kakum National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kakum National Park is known for its unique local products and souvenirs. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Kente Cloth: This brightly colored traditional African cloth is a must-buy souvenir from Ghana.

  2. Adinkra Symbols: These are traditional symbols that represent different concepts, and they are often used to decorate fabrics and other items.

  3. Shea Butter: This natural beauty product is made from the nuts of the shea tree and is known for its moisturizing properties.

Local Culture and Customs of Kakum National Park

Ghana is known for its rich cultural heritage, and Kakum National Park is a great place to immerse yourself in the local culture. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of:

  1. Dress Code: It’s important to dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting cultural sites and places of worship.

  2. Handshakes: Handshakes are a common greeting in Ghana, and it’s considered impolite to refuse a handshake.

  3. Language: English is the official language of Ghana, but many locals also speak or understand one or more of the local dialects.

Best Time to Visit Kakum National Park

The best time to visit Kakum National Park is from August to March, which is the dry season. The weather is sunny and relatively cool during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kakum National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions.

  2. Hire a guide for wildlife tours and outdoor activities.

  3. Observe the park’s rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  1. Feed or approach wildlife.

  2. Litter or damage the park’s natural resources.

  3. Venture off hiking trails or biking routes alone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kakum National Park

Kakum National Park is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety.

  1. Always hire a guide for outdoor activities.

  2. Avoid walking alone at night.

  3. Observe the park’s rules and regulations.

Health in Kakum National Park

The park is located in a malaria-endemic area, so it’s important to take precautions and carry insect repellent and antimalarial medication.

Getting to Kakum National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kakum National Park

  1. Kotoka International Airport in Accra.

  2. Kumasi International Airport in Kumasi.

Bus Stations Near Kakum National Park

  1. Cape Coast Bus Station in Cape Coast.

  2. Takoradi Bus Station in Takoradi.

Other Options

  1. Private Car: You can rent a car and drive to the park, but it’s recommended to hire a driver or take a guided tour.

  2. Taxi: There are many taxis available in the region, but be sure to negotiate the fare before getting in.

How to Get Around in Kakum National Park

The best way to get around in the park is by hiring a guide or taking a guided tour. You can also walk, hike, or bike the park’s trails on your own, but it’s recommended to stick to the designated routes.

Where to Go Next from Kakum National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations to explore after visiting Kakum National Park. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Cape Coast: Explore the historic Cape Coast Castle and other cultural sites in the city.

  2. Takoradi: Visit the nearby beaches and enjoy the relaxed coastal vibe.

  3. Accra: Ghana’s capital city is only a few hours’ drive away and offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions.
