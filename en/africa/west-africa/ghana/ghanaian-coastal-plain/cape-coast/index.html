Introduction to Cape Coast

Cape Coast is a historic city located in the Ghanaian Coastal Plain, in Ghana, West Africa, and it is the capital of the Central region. Cape Coast played a crucial role in the transatlantic slave trade and evidence of slavery is ever present. Nowadays, Cape Coast offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination on any trip to Ghana.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, hiking/biking routes, activities and tours, where to eat and drink, where to stay, what to buy, local customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Cape Coast

Cape Coast is a city full of incredible landmarks, museums, and historical sites. Here are some of the most popular places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cape Coast Castle: This castle was one of the major slave trade hubs and is now a museum where visitors can explore the dark history of the slave trade.

  • Elmina Castle: This castle was also used for the slave trade and is a must-visit for those interested in Ghana’s history.

  • Fort Victoria: This fort was built by the British in the 19th century to stop human trafficking through the coast.

  • Cape Coast University Museum: This museum showcases the culture and history of Ghana with exhibits on traditional musical instruments, textiles, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Cape Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kakum National Park: Kakum National Park is a protected rainforest with a canopy walkway that offers breathtaking views of the forest, unique wildlife, and bird watching.

  • Brenu Beach: Brenu Beach is a beautiful and serene beach that is perfect for relaxing under the sun and watching the waves.

  • Abrafo Odumasi Butterfly Sanctuary: This sanctuary features a variety of butterfly species and is perfect for wildlife enthusiasts or anyone looking for a peaceful escape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cape Coast

Cape Coast has several hiking/biking routes showcasing its beautiful scenery.

  • Hans Cottage Botel to Kakum National Park: This 30km route offers stunning views of nature and the opportunity to visit Kakum National Park.

  • Cape Coast to Elmina: This 14km route on the coast offers scenic views and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Cape Coast: Activities and Tours

  • Kakum Canopy Walk: Experience the thrill of walking in the jungle treetops on this canopy walk that is suspended over 30 meters above the ground.

  • Visit the Cape Coast / Elmina Castle: Explore the history of the slave trade in Ghana and learn more about the African Diaspora.

  • Attend a drumming and dancing performance: Immerse yourself in traditional Ghanaian culture with a drumming and dancing performance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cape Coast

For visitors planning a one-week stay, here is a sample itinerary to make the most of your visit:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Cape Coast Castle and explore the city’s history
Day 2 Explore Kakum National Park and go on the canopy walk
Day 3 Experience traditional Ghanaian drumming and dancing performance

Food and Drink Scene of Cape Coast

Cape Coast has a unique and tasty food scene that visitors should explore.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fufu: A doughy ball made of cassava, plantains or yams that is often dipped in soup or stew.

  • Kenkey: A fermented corn dough that is often served with soup or stew.

  • Banku: A doughy ball made of cassava and corn that is often paired with soup or stew.

  • Jollof Rice: A popular West African dish made with rice, vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A sweet and mild alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees.

  • Sobolo: A refreshing drink made with dried hibiscus flowers, ginger, and sugar.

  • Pito: A fermented beverage made of millet and is often served with ice.

Where to Stay in Cape Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cape Coast caters to all budgets with a range of accommodation options.

  • Oasis Beach Resort: This beachfront resort offers a range of rooms and amenities, including a pool, garden, and lounge.

  • Mfantsipim Guest House: This guest house is located in the heart of Cape Coast and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  • Rain Forest Lodge: This eco-lodge located near Kakum National Park offers unique rooms and a mix of indoor and outdoor living spaces.

What to Buy in Cape Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kente Cloth: A colorful and intricate woven cloth that is often worn on special occasions.

  • Crafts: There is a range of crafts on offer throughout Cape Coast, including wooden carvings, baskets, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Cape Coast

Ghanaian culture is rich, diverse, and welcoming. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions such as removing shoes before entering a home or place of worship and dressing modestly.

Best Time to Visit Cape Coast

The best time to visit Cape Coast is from November to March when the weather is dry and mild, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape Coast

Do:

  • Try new foods and drinks: Cape Coast has a unique and tasty food scene that is worth exploring.

  • Learn about the history and culture: Cape Coast has a rich history and culture that visitors should learn and experience.

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Be respectful when visiting local communities, homes, and places of worship.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs: Visitors should avoid behavior that may offend local customs and traditions.

  • Take photographs without permission: Visitors should ask permission before taking photos of people or their property.

  • Litter: Keep Cape Coast clean and respect the environment by properly disposing of trash.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape Coast

Cape Coast is a generally safe city, but visitors should take safety precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night, securing their valuables, and remaining vigilant.

Health in Cape Coast

Visitors should consult their doctor and take appropriate health precautions, such as getting vaccinations and taking anti-malaria medication, before traveling to Cape Coast.

Getting to Cape Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cape Coast is well-connected to other major cities in Ghana.

Airports Near Cape Coast

The closest airport to Cape Coast is the Kotoka International Airport in Accra, which is approximately 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Cape Coast

Cape Coast has several bus stations that offer transportation to other cities in Ghana.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber are available throughout Cape Coast and are a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Cape Coast

The best way to get around Cape Coast is by taxi or by renting a car if you want to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Cape Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations near Cape Coast that visitors may be interested in exploring:

  • Accra: The capital city of Ghana, located approximately 150 km from Cape Coast.

  • Takoradi: A bustling coastal city, located approximately 70 km from Cape Coast.

Conclusion

Cape Coast is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. With its unique food scene, historic landmarks, and outdoor adventures, Cape Coast offers something for everyone. Plan your visit with this comprehensive guide and experience the best that Cape Coast has to offer.
