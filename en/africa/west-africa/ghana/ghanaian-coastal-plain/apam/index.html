Introduction to Apam

Nestled in the coastal plain of Ghana in West Africa, Apam is a charming town that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. Home to about 20,000 people, this relaxed town is located in the central region of Ghana and boasts a stunning coastline, lush vegetation, and rich cultural heritage.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through some of the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, local cuisine, where to stay, and much more. Here are some of the fantastic things to do and see in and around Apam.

List of Places to Visit in Apam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Fort Patience: This historical landmark was built in 1667 by the Dutch to serve as a trading post.
  • Sankofa Beach House: The Sankofa Beach House serves as a memorial for the transatlantic slave trade and the return of Africans to their ancestral lands.
  • The Apam Cultural Centre: The Cultural Centre displays the rich cultural heritage of the Apam people through various exhibits and interactive displays.

Natural Attractions of Apam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Apam Beach: This is the place to be for a relaxing experience by the sea. Take a dip in the calm waters, soak up some sun, or take a leisurely stroll on the beach.
  • The Kakum National Park: This park offers an incredible canopy walk, which is an excellent way to view the lush vegetation and wildlife in the park.
  • The Abrafo Forest Reserve: Hike or bike through this reserve that is home to many bird species, monkeys, and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apam

  • The Abrafo Forest Reserve Trail: This trail will take you through the lush vegetation of the reserve, providing an opportunity to view various plant and animal species.
  • The Kakum National Park Trail: This scenic walking trail takes you through the canopy of the forest, which is home to various bird species and monkeys.
  • The Coastal Trail: This bike trail provides you with a scenic view of the coastline and the serene environment of the town.

Best Things to Do in Apam: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Fort Patience and learn about its important role in the slave trade and colonial history of Ghana.
  • Visit the Sankofa Beach House and learn about the history of the transatlantic slave trade.
  • Take a dip in the Apam Beach and relax in the serene environment by the sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Apam

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fufu: A traditional Ghanaian dish made of cassava and plantain flour. Typically accompanied by soup.
  • Banku: Similar to fufu, made of fermented corn, cassava, or yam dough. Usually served with soup, stew, or fish.
  • Jollof Rice: A one-pot rice dish made with tomatoes, spices, and meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A locally made alcoholic beverage that is made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Sobolo: A refreshing beverage made from hibiscus flowers.
  • Pito: A locally brewed beer made from millet or sorghum.

Where to Stay in Apam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Coconut Grove Beach Resort: This resort offers direct access to the beach, spacious rooms, and excellent amenities.
  • Hilltop Hotel: An affordable hotel in a serene location with good food, comfy rooms, and friendly staff.
  • Apam Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option that offers clean rooms, friendly service, and a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Apam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kente Cloth: This is a colorful fabric that is hand-woven by local artisans, and it is one of the most popular souvenirs in Ghana.
  • Beaded Jewelry: Apam is known for its fantastic beadwork, and you can find a variety of jewelry made of beads.

Local Culture and Customs of Apam

The Apam people are known for their rich cultural heritage and warm hospitality. Their customs and traditions are evident in various aspects of their lives, including music, dance, and festivals. One of the more popular festivals is the Apam Kundum Festival, which celebrates the harvest season and involves drumming and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Apam

The best time to visit Apam is between November and March when the weather is relatively cooler and drier. This period is perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the town’s cultural heritage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apam

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural attractions and events.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apam

Apam is a generally safe town to visit. However, it is still wise to take precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night, not leaving valuables in plain sight, and being vigilant in crowded areas.

Health in Apam

It is essential to take necessary health precautions when visiting Apam. This includes getting vaccinations, drinking bottled water, and wearing mosquito repellant to prevent malaria.

Getting to Apam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apam

The nearest airport to Apam is the Kotoka International Airport in Accra, which is about a three-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Apam

Several bus companies operate routes to Apam, including VIP and STC Bus Company.

Other Options

Taxis and private car rental options are also available for visitors to Apam.

How to Get Around in Apam

The best way to get around Apam is by using a taxi or hiring a private car. Buses and tro-tros are also available, but they tend to be crowded and are not always reliable.

Where to Go Next from Apam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cape Coast: Visit the Cape Coast Castle, Elmina Castle and learn about the colonial history of Ghana.
  • Accra: Explore the vibrant capital city of Ghana, known for its beaches, markets, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Apam is an excellent destination for visitors interested in getting a taste of Ghanaian culture and natural beauty. With its cultural landmarks, breathtaking scenery, and mouth-watering cuisine, Apam is an exciting place to explore for anyone looking for a relaxed yet memorable travel experience.
