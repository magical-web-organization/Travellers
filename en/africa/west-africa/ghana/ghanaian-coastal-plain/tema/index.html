Introduction to Tema

Tema is a vibrant city in the Ghanaian Coastal Plain, located in the southeastern part of Ghana. It is renowned for its vast industrial zone and bustling harbor, making it an ideal destination for those who want to have a glimpse of Ghana’s economic hub.

However, Tema is more than just an industrial hub; it is also home to various tourist attractions, cultural sites and outdoor activities. Above all, it is the perfect destination for travelers who want to immerse themselves in the local culture.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, where to stay, local food and culture, safety tips, and transportation options to help you plan a perfect trip to Tema.

List of Places to Visit in Tema

Tema boasts several attractions, from cultural sites and historic landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic spots. Here are some of the top places to visit in Tema:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tema Heritage and Squares Museum: This museum houses several artifacts and exhibits, illustrating the history and culture of Tema and Ghana as a whole.
  • Centre for National Culture: This cultural center features numerous artworks, crafts, music and dance performances, and architecture that reflects Ghana’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Cenotaph: This cenotaph was erected in honor of Ghanaians who fought and died during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Tema: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mankoadze Beach: This popular beach features several restaurants, bars, and recreational activities, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and water sports.
  • Sakumo Lagoon: This Ramsar site is a haven for bird-watchers, with numerous bird species present in the area.
  • Tema Manhean Fishing Village: This colorful fishing village is home to several fishermen and their families, providing a glimpse of traditional Ghanaian life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tema

Tema offers several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Tema include:

  • Tema Hills: This rugged terrain is ideal for hiking enthusiasts, offering a unique perspective of Tema’s skyline.
  • Tema Motorway: This road offers a smooth ride, with little to no traffic, and stunning views of Tema.

Best Things to Do in Tema: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff or adventure enthusiast, Tema offers various activities and tours that cater to all interests. Some of the best things to do in Tema include:

  • Boat ride at Sakumo Lagoon: This scenic boat ride offers visitors a unique opportunity to view the numerous bird species that inhabit the lagoon.
  • Visit to the Centre for National Culture: This cultural center offers visitors an immersive experience into Ghanaian culture, with various workshops, music, and dance performances.
  • Visit to Mankoadze Beach: This beautiful beach offers several water sport activities, volleyball courts, and restaurants, perfect for a day of relaxation or fun.

Food and Drink Scene of Tema

Tema’s food and drink scene comprises a blend of local and international cuisines. There are several dishes and drinks that you shouldn’t miss during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banku and Tilapia: This dish comprises cooked corn dough and grilled tilapia fish, a Ghanaian favorite.
  • Jollof rice: This rice dish is prepared with tomato sauce, spices, and vegetables, and is a staple food in Ghana.

Local Beverages

  • Palm wine: This drink is made from the sap of palm trees and is available in various locations throughout Tema and Ghana as a whole.

Where to Stay in Tema: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tema offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay in Tema:

  • Royal Nick Hotel: This hotel is an excellent option for those who want to experience luxury accommodations.
  • Arden International Hotel: This mid-range hotel offers comfortable rooms and various amenities that cater to both business and leisure travelers.
  • Yegoala Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and affordable rooms, ideal for travelers on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Tema: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tema offers several souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a reminder of their trip. Some of the best things to buy in Tema include:

  • African fabrics and clothing: Visitors can buy local Ghanaian fabrics and designs from the Centre for National Culture.
  • Hand-carved wood crafts: These crafts are available in various locations throughout Tema and are an excellent souvenir for art enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tema

Ghana is home to various cultures and customs, and Tema is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and Etiquettes that visitors should be aware of:

  • Avoid using your left hand: In Ghana, using your left hand for anything other than washing is considered impolite.
  • Dress modestly: Ghanaians value modest dressing, especially in public places or when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Tema

The best time to visit Tema is between October and April when the weather is dry and sunny. This time of year also coincides with Ghana’s festivals and cultural events, providing visitors with more immersive experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tema

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts when visiting Tema:

Do:

  • Greet people: In Ghana, greeting people, especially elders, is considered respectful.
  • Try local food: Ghanaian food is delicious and diverse, with many signature dishes you shouldn’t miss during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission: It is considered impolite to take photos of people or places without first seeking permission.
  • Display public affection: Public displays of affection are not the norm in Ghana and can be deemed inappropriate.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tema

Tema is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some safety tips to consider:

  • Avoid walking alone after dark: As with any city, it is best to avoid walking alone after dark, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Watch for pickpockets: Pickpocketing is prevalent in crowded areas, so it is best to keep your valuables safe during your visit to Tema.

Health in Tema

Visitors should take preventive measures to protect their health while in Tema, including:

  • Drink bottled or filtered water: It is best to avoid tap water, which may not be safe for drinking or brushing teeth.
  • Vaccinations: Visitors should check with their healthcare providers to ensure that their vaccinations are up-to-date.

Getting to Tema: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tema is accessible by various modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Tema

  • Kotoka International Airport: This airport is located in Accra, about 30 kilometers away from Tema.

Bus Stations Near Tema

  • VIP Bus Station: Located in Tema and offers daily bus services to various destinations in Ghana.

Other Options

  • Taxis and car rentals: Visitors can also hire taxis or rent cars to explore Tema and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Tema

Visitors can use taxis or car rentals to get around Tema. Alternatively, they can use public transportation, such as buses and tro-tros, which are the most affordable option.

Where to Go Next from Tema: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations to explore near Tema, including:

  • Accra: This city is Ghana’s capital and is renowned for its museums, historic landmarks, and lively nightlife.
  • Cape Coast: This city is home to the Cape Coast Slave Castle and several other historic sites.

Conclusion

Tema is a unique travel destination that offers a blend of culture, history, and adventure. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring historic sites, or trying local cuisines, Tema has something for everyone. Armed with this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan a memorable trip to this bustling Ghanaian city.
