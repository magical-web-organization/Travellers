Introduction to Accra

Accra, the capital city of Ghana, offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity. Located in the south of the country, Accra boasts a rich historical and cultural background, making it one of the top tourist destinations in West Africa. The city features an array of attractions, including museums, historical landmarks, natural reserves, and exceptional cuisine that make it a must-see destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Accra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of Ghana

The National Museum of Ghana is an ideal place to start your journey of exploring the country’s cultural heritage. Located in the city center, the museum has four exhibition galleries showcasing Ghana’s history, ethnography, archaeology, and arts.

Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park

Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park is a historical site that commemorates Ghana’s first President, Dr. Kwame Nkrumah. The park features a mausoleum, museum, and statues of notable figures in Ghana’s history, including the country’s first President and other prominent leaders.

Natural Attractions of Accra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Labadi Beach

Labadi Beach is a perfect place to unwind and enjoy scenic views of the Atlantic Ocean. The beach offers countless activities such as swimming, volleyball, and various water sports. There are also vendors selling local foods and drinks along the shore.

Aburi Botanical Gardens

Aburi Botanical Gardens is a serene escape from the bustling city. Located on a hilltop, the gardens feature various tree species, orchids, and a canopy walk. The gardens also offer picnic spots and guided tours for the visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Accra

Achimota Forest Reserve

Achimota Forest Reserve is a perfect spot for hiking and biking, located just outside of the city. The reserve boasts a variety of flora and fauna, including monkeys, birds, and exotic plants, making it an ideal place for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Accra: Activities and Tours

Nima Tour

The Nima tour is a guided tour of the Nima district, a vibrant suburb in Accra known for its lively markets, food stalls, and street art. The district tour offers a unique insight into Ghana’s culture and traditions.

Jamestown Tour

Jamestown Tour is another must-visit destination characterized by its vibrant art scene and stunning colonial architecture. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour through the district while exploring the many art galleries, food stalls, and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Accra

Must-Try Dishes

Banku and Tilapia

Banku and Tilapia is a traditional dish in Ghana, consisting of a fermented corn dough and grilled, marinated tilapia fish. The dish is usually served with soup or pepper sauce.

Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and spices. The dish can be served with grilled chicken, goat meat, or fish.

Local Beverages

Pito

Pito is a local beverage made from fermented millet, and it’s commonly served at social gatherings like weddings, festivals, and funerals.

Palm Wine

Palm wine is a beverage made from the sap of various species of palm trees. The beverage can be enjoyed fresh, or it can be fermented into a popular alcoholic drink.

Where to Stay in Accra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accra offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Accra include:

Kempinski Hotel Gold Coast City

The Kempinski Hotel is a luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Accra. The hotel boasts 269 rooms, six restaurants, and various other amenities that make it one of the best places to stay in the city.

Cozy Accommodation

Cozy Accommodation is a budget-friendly hostel located near the Labadi Beach. The hostel offers air-conditioned rooms, internet access, and a shared kitchen for the guests.

Villa Monticello

Villa Monticello is a boutique hotel that offers luxurious accommodations and world-class dining experiences. The hotel features 14 rooms, a spa, a fitness center, and a rooftop terrace for the guests.

What to Buy in Accra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kente Cloth

Kente Cloth is a traditional fabric that is hand-woven by the Ashanti people of Ghana. The cloth comes in various colors and patterns and can be bought at the local markets.

Adinkra Symbols

Adinkra symbols are traditional symbols used in Ghana that represent different meanings. The symbols are usually stamped onto clothes and other textiles for decorative purposes.

Local Culture and Customs of Accra

Ghana is a culturally diverse country with many different customs and traditions. Some of the things to keep in mind when visiting Accra include:

  • Dress modestly in public places, especially when visiting temples or religious sites.
  • Greet people with respect.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Accra

The best time to visit Accra is between November and March when the weather is cool and dry. The temperature can rise to over 30°C during the rest of the year, making it uncomfortable for some travelers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Accra

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the cultural attractions and landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Hesitate to ask for help if you need it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Accra

Accra is a relatively safe city, but travelers should be cautious, especially when walking alone at night. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or wearing expensive jewelry.
  • Try to take official taxis instead of using public transportation.
  • Stay away from isolated places or neighborhoods.

Health in Accra

Visitors to Accra should take some health precautions, including:

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Get vaccinated against yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever.
  • Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Accra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Accra

The Kotoka International Airport is the main airport serving Accra, and it’s located about 6 miles from the city center. The airport serves various airlines flying to destinations all around the world.

Bus Stations Near Accra

The main bus terminal in Accra is the STC Bus Station, which serves various destinations in Ghana and neighboring countries like Togo, Ivory Coast, and Burkina Faso.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for private taxis or car rentals to get around Accra if they prefer private transportation.

How to Get Around in Accra

Accra offers various transportation options, including taxis, buses, and shared taxis (tro-tros). Visitors can also use ride-hailing apps like Uber and Bolt, which are available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Accra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations that are worth visiting from Accra include:

  • Cape Coast and Elmina
  • Kokrobite
  • Ada Foah

Conclusion

Accra is a city full of surprises and adventures, offering visitors an experience unlike any other. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or just relaxation, Accra has something for every type of traveler. Use this guide to plan your next visit to this bustling city and explore everything it has to offer.
