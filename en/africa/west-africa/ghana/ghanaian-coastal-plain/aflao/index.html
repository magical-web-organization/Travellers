Introduction to Aflao

Aflao is a town located in the Ghanaian Coastal Plain, in the Volta Region of Ghana. It is a border town, and serves as an entry point for travelers coming from Togo into Ghana. Because of its location, Aflao is a popular transit town for travelers heading to other destinations in Ghana such as Accra, Kumasi, and Cape Coast.

Despite being known mainly as a transit town, Aflao has its own appeal as a tourist destination. It boasts a rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural landscapes, and a unique food and drink scene. This guide will give you all the information you need to plan your trip to Aflao.

List of Places to Visit in Aflao

If you’re wondering what to do in Aflao, there are plenty of options for things to see and places to visit. Here are some of the best places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you want to learn about the history and culture of Aflao, there are several museums and historical sites to explore.

  • The Aflao Museum: This museum showcases the culture and traditions of the Ewe people, who are the dominant ethnic group in Aflao.
  • Aflao Border Post: This is where the border between Ghana and Togo is located. It’s a bustling place, with lots of activity as people cross between the two countries.
  • Fort Prinzenstein: Located in nearby Keta, Fort Prinzenstein is a historic fort that was used in the transatlantic slave trade. It is now a museum that educates visitors about the brutal history of the slave trade.

Natural Attractions of Aflao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the outdoors, Aflao has plenty to offer. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Aflao Beach: Aflao has a beautiful beach where you can relax and soak up the sun. It’s a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.
  • Wli Waterfalls: Located about two hours from Aflao, Wli Waterfalls is the highest waterfall in West Africa. It’s a stunning sight and a must-visit destination for nature lovers.
  • Mount Afadja: This is the highest mountain in Ghana, and is located just a few miles from Aflao. It’s a great spot for hiking and enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aflao

If you’re an avid hiker or biker, Aflao has some great options for exploring the surrounding landscape. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Mount Afadja Hike: This hike takes you to the summit of Mount Afadja, the highest point in Ghana. The trail is about 6 miles long and takes about 4-5 hours to complete.
  • Aflao Beach Trail: This is a leisurely hike along the Aflao Beach. It’s about 3 miles long and takes about 2 hours to complete.
  • Keta Lagoon Trail: This is a scenic trail that takes you through the Keta Lagoon area. It’s about 5 miles long and takes about 3-4 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Aflao: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of other things to do in Aflao. Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Togo and Benin Excursion: You can take a day trip to neighboring countries Togo and Benin, and explore the local markets, museums, and cultural sites.
  • Aflao Market Tour: This is a guided tour of the local market in Aflao. You’ll get to see the colorful stalls and learn about the different products sold here, including textiles, crafts, and food.
  • Ewe Culture Tour: This is a tour that takes you to local Ewe villages, where you can experience the traditional way of life and learn about the culture and customs of the Ewe people.

Food and Drink Scene of Aflao

Aflao has a unique food and drink scene, with plenty of local dishes and beverages to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banku: This is a staple dish in Ghana, and is made from fermented corn and cassava dough. It’s usually served with soup or stew.
  • Fufu: Another popular Ghanaian dish, fufu is made from boiled cassava and plantains, and is usually served with a soup or stew.
  • Jollof Rice: This is a spicy rice dish that is popular throughout West Africa. It’s made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is an alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It’s a popular drink in West Africa, and is often served at social gatherings.
  • Sobolo: This is a refreshing drink made from hibiscus leaves. It’s sweet, tangy, and perfect for hot days.
  • Pito: This is a local beer made from millet or sorghum. It’s low in alcohol content and has a slightly sour taste.

Where to Stay in Aflao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, there are plenty of places to stay in Aflao. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to consider:

  • Green Tree Hotel: This is a budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • Holy Trinity Spa and Health Farm: This is a luxury spa and resort with beautiful rooms, a swimming pool, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Aflao Guesthouse: This is a basic guesthouse with clean rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Aflao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring home from Aflao, here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Kente Cloth: This is a traditional fabric that is made by the Ewe people. It’s brightly colored and comes in a variety of patterns.
  • Beaded Jewelry: Aflao is known for its intricate beaded jewelry, which is made by local artisans.
  • Wooden Carvings: There are many talented woodcarvers in Aflao, and you can find a variety of beautiful wooden carvings to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Aflao

The dominant ethnic group in Aflao is the Ewe people, who have a rich cultural heritage. Some customs and traditions to be aware of when visiting Aflao include:

  • Handshakes: In Aflao, handshakes are an important part of greetings. Make sure to offer a handshake when meeting someone for the first time.
  • Dress Code: Dressing modestly is important in Aflao, especially when visiting cultural or religious sites.
  • Respect for Elders: Respect for elders is highly valued in Aflao, so make sure to show deference and courtesy to older people.

Best Time to Visit Aflao

The best time to visit Aflao is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. This is when the weather is cooler and less humid, making it more comfortable for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aflao

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Aflao:

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Be polite and respectful towards locals.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the culture and history of Aflao.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Be overly aggressive or argumentative.

Safety and Health

Aflao is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s important to take precautions to stay healthy and secure. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Aflao

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.
  • Keep your valuables locked up in your hotel room or a safe.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation, and avoid taking unmarked taxis or buses.

Health in Aflao

  • Make sure to drink bottled water to avoid getting sick from tap water.
  • Bring mosquito repellent to protect yourself from mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria.
  • Consider getting vaccinated before your trip.

Getting to Aflao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aflao is located about 2 hours from Accra, the capital of Ghana. Here are some transportation options to consider when getting to Aflao:

Airports Near Aflao

The closest airport to Aflao is Kotoka International Airport in Accra. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Aflao.

Bus Stations Near Aflao

There are several bus stations in Accra that offer services to Aflao, including the STC Bus Terminal and the VIP Bus Terminal.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or hire a private driver to take you to Aflao from Accra.

How to Get Around in Aflao

The best way to get around in Aflao is by taxi or private car. There are also local buses and tro tros (minibuses) available, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Aflao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels after Aflao, there are plenty of nearby destinations to consider. Some popular options include:

  • Accra: The capital of Ghana, Accra is a bustling city with plenty of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Cape Coast: Located on the coast of Ghana, Cape Coast is known for its beautiful beaches and historic fortresses.
  • Lomé: The capital of Togo, Lomé is a vibrant city with lots to see and do.

Conclusion

Whether you’re passing through on your way to other destinations in Ghana, or specifically seeking out the attractions of Aflao, this guide is a great starting point to plan your trip. With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique food and drink scene, Aflao is definitely worth a visit for any traveler to Ghana.
