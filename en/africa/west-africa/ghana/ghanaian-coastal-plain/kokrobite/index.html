Complete Guide to Kokrobite - Best Places to Visit, Stay and Eat

Kokrobite is a small town located in the coastal plain of Ghana, a West African nation renowned for its vibrant culture, lush landscapes, and incredible wildlife. This small town is known for its beautiful beaches and has been attracting tourists from all over the world for several years. In this comprehensive guide to Kokrobite, we will walk you through the best places to visit, stay, eat, and explore during your trip.

Introduction to Kokrobite

Kokrobite is a bustling town that enjoys a unique blend of traditional and modern cultures. The town is home to a vibrant community that thrives on fishing, farming, and handmade crafts. The town’s location near Accra makes it an accessible destination for tourists who are looking for a relaxing and peaceful vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Kokrobite

Kokrobite has plenty of attractions for tourists of all ages. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Kokrobite:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park- This is one of the most important historical sites in Ghana, as it commemorates the life and legacy of Ghana’s first president, Kwame Nkrumah. The park features an impressive mural portraying Nkrumah’s life, a bronze statue of his likeness, and several other monuments.

  • The Arts Center- This is one of the largest marketplaces in Accra, where you can find a wide range of handmade crafts, including clothing, jewelry, and wood carvings. If you are looking for a unique souvenir to take home, this is the perfect place to shop.

  • The National Museum- This museum houses a wide collection of Ghanaian art, cultural artifacts, and historical documents. The museum’s main attraction is the exhibit on the Gold Coast, which showcases the history of the Ashanti kingdom and the region’s trade relations with Europeans.

Natural Attractions of Kokrobite: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Kokrobite Beach- One of the most popular attractions, the water is perfect for swimming, surfing or just relaxing by the beach. The beach is lined with coconut trees and makes for a serene and peaceful atmosphere, perfect for late afternoon walks as well.

  • The Wli Waterfalls- Known as the highest waterfalls in West Africa, this is a sought-after destination for thrill-seekers who want to experience the area’s natural beauty up-close. The trek is challenging, but worth the effort, as you will be rewarded with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Kakum National Park- A large conservation area, the park is home to a wide range of plant and animal species. The park features a network of canopy walkways that allow visitors to see the park’s canopy, where you can enjoy beautiful scenic views, butterflies and over 100 documented bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kokrobite

Kokrobite is an excellent place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kokrobite:

  • Mokala Hill- This route will take you to the top of the hill, where you will be rewarded with an unparalleled view of the town and its beautiful surroundings.

  • Kokrobite and Beyond: Bike Tour- A bike tour of the town, complete with leisurely stops at local eateries and shops, this is an excellent way to experience the culture of the town.

Best Things to Do in Kokrobite: Activities and Tours

Kokrobite offers plenty of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some of the best things to do in Kokrobite:

  • Sunset and Boat Cruise- You can relax and marvel at the beautiful sunsets from the ocean while enjoying some happy hour drinks.

  • Surfing Lessons- Kokrobite is perfect for surfing, with its strong waves, you can learn to surf with some of the best instructors in the region.

  • Craft Workshops- Kokrobite is well known for its arts and crafts. You can learn how to create some of the traditional Ghanaian arts and crafts at one of the local workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kokrobite

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Kokrobite:

Day 1 Arrival in Kokrobite and check-in at your hotel
Day 2 Visit Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park and The Arts Centre
Day 3 Explore the National Museum and shop for handmade crafts
Day 4 Hike or bike to Mokala hill and take in the beautiful scenery
Day 5 Spend the day surfing, either taking a lesson or practicing your surfing skills
Day 6 Take part in a craft workshop and create your own souvenirs
Day 7 Spend your last day relaxing at Kokrobite beach and catching the sunset on a boat cruise

Food and Drink Scene of Kokrobite

Kokrobite boasts of an excellent food and drink scene that showcases traditional Ghanaian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jollof Rice- This is a signature dish of Ghana, which features rice cooked with tomatoes, onions, and spices. It’s typically served with grilled chicken, fish, or vegetables.

  • Banku and Tilapia- Banku is a starchy cornmeal dough that’s typically served with spicy gravy and grilled tilapia fish.

  • Kelewele- This is a popular street food snack that features ripe plantains that are deep-fried and then seasoned with spices, including cinnamon, chili powder, and ginger.

Local Beverages

  • Sobolo- This is a refreshing, non-alcoholic drink made from hibiscus flowers that are boiled with ginger and cloves, then sweetened with sugar.

  • Palm Wine- A sweet and tangy wine made from fermented palm sap. It’s typically served in calabash bowls and is perfect for hot days.

  • Star Beer- This is Ghana’s most popular beer, it’s a lager that’s brewed with cassava and has a light, crisp flavor.

Where to Stay in Kokrobite: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kokrobite has plenty of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Kokrobite:

  • Biriwa Beach Hotel- This beachfront hotel offers well-appointed rooms, a restaurant, and several recreational amenities.

  • Big Milly’s Backyard- A budget hostel located close to the beach, with a restaurant, bar and outdoor lounges that will guarantee a memorable stay.

  • Kokrobite Garden Guesthouse- A welcoming and friendly guest house with a beautiful garden, tropical views and outdoor seating areas that provide warmth and comfort all year round.

What to Buy in Kokrobite: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kokrobite is an excellent place to shop for traditional Ghanaian crafts and products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to take home:

  • Kente Fabric- This is a traditional textile that’s made from silk and cotton. It’s typically used to create clothing or accessories and features bold, colorful designs.

  • Wood Carvings- Ghana is known for its intricate wood carvings, which feature a variety of animals, people, and cultural symbols.

  • Beads and Jewelry- Ghana is famous for its handmade beaded jewelry, which features colorful glass beads, brass, and other materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Kokrobite

Kokrobite has a rich culture that is characterized by music, dance, and traditional rites of passage. Visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture by attending cultural events, learning about the history of the town, and participating in local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kokrobite

The best time to visit Kokrobite is between October and April, which is typically the dry season. During this period, the weather is sunny and warm, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kokrobite

Here are some important tips to keep in mind while exploring Kokrobite:

Do:

  • Dress Conservatively- While Kokrobite is a laid-back town, it’s important to remember that it’s still a conservative society. To avoid offending the locals, dress modestly.

  • Respect Local Customs- Ghana has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s essential to respect local customs and traditions.

  • Hire Local Tour Guides- Hiring a local tour guide can provide you with valuable insight into the history and culture of the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter- Ghana is known for its pristine natural beauty, and it’s essential to keep it that way by not littering.

  • Take Photographs of Strangers Without Permission.- It’s respectful to ask someone’s permission before taking their photograph.

  • Shake Hands With Your Left Hand- Ghanaian culture considers the left hand unclean, so always use your right hand to shake hands, point, or touch someone.

Safety and Health

Kokrobite is a safe and friendly town, but it’s still essential to keep safety in mind while exploring the area.

Safety in Kokrobite

  • Avoid walking alone at night- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially at night. Walk in groups or use a reputable taxi service to get around.

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and travel documents.- In case of an emergency or lost documents, having copies of your identification is essential.

  • Be vigilant with your belongings- Pickpocketing can occur in crowded places or markets, so keep a close eye on your belongings.

Health in Kokrobite

  • Drink bottled water- Always drink bottled water and avoid tap water, even in restaurants.

  • Use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved shirts and pants- Malaria is prevalent in Ghana, and it’s essential to take precautions against mosquitoes.

  • Get vaccinated before traveling.- Talk to a healthcare provider about getting vaccinations before traveling to Ghana.

Getting to Kokrobite: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kokrobite is accessible via several transportation options. Here’s what you need to know:

  • Airports Near Kokrobite

    The nearest airport to Kokrobite is Kotoka International Airport, which is located in Accra, approximately 30km away. You can take a taxi from the airport to Kokrobite.

  • Bus Stations Near Kokrobite

    The main bus station in Accra is the Kwame Nkrumah Circle station. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to Kokrobite.

  • Other Options

    You can also hire a private car or a taxi to take you to Kokrobite from Accra or other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Kokrobite

Kokrobite is a small town that can easily be navigated on foot, but if you prefer to use transportation, you can hire a taxi, a motorbike, or a bicycle from any of the local rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Kokrobite: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kokrobite is located near several other popular destinations in Ghana. Here are some of the best places to check out after your Kokrobite trip:

  • Cape Coast- This historic city is located about 100km from Kokrobite and is home to several cultural and historical landmarks, including the Cape Coast Castle and the Kakum National Park.

  • Accra- Ghana’s capital city is located about 25km from Kokrobite and is known for its vibrant culture, food, and nightlife.

  • Elmina- This picturesque town is located about 80km from Kokrobite and is known for its beautiful beaches, traditional fishing villages, and ancient castles.

Conclusion

Kokrobite is a unique and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in the local culture, explore the area’s natural beauty or simply relax and unwind, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience. With this comprehensive guide to Kokrobite, we hope you’re inspired to plan your next trip to this incredible destination.
