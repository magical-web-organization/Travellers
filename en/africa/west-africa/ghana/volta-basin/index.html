A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Volta Basin, Ghana

Located in the eastern region of Ghana, Volta Basin is a hidden jewel in West Africa. It is home to a range of natural and cultural attractions, including spectacular waterfalls, scenic views, ancient historical sites, and diverse local culture. In this comprehensive guide, we have compiled everything you need to know to plan your trip to Volta Basin, including where to stay, what to do, what to eat, and what to avoid. So, let’s dive into the heart of Ghana and discover the secrets of Volta Basin.

Introduction to Volta Basin

Volta Basin is a large drainage basin that covers over 400,000 square kilometers of land in West Africa. It is a major source of water supply to several countries, including Benin, Burkina Faso, Cote d’Ivoire, Ghana, Mali, and Togo. The basin is named after the Volta River, a major river that runs through Ghana and empties into the Atlantic Ocean. It is also home to the world’s largest man-made lake – Lake Volta.

Local Culture and Customs of Volta Basin

The culture of Volta Basin is diverse, with many influences from the Ewe, Ashanti, and Fante people. Respect for elders and authority is an essential part of the local culture, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and follow local customs.

Best Time to Visit Volta Basin

The best time to visit Volta Basin is from October to March when the weather is dry and mild. The rainy season (April to September) can be challenging for outdoor activities, but it is a great time to see the waterfalls and enjoy the lush green scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volta Basin

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Negotiate prices when buying souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Disrespect elders or authority figures
  • Take photos without asking for permission
  • Engage in any illegal activities
  • Touch the animals in the monkey sanctuary or crocodile pond

Safety and Health

Safety in Volta Basin

Volta Basin is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is always advisable to take precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Use authorized taxi services
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times

Health in Volta Basin

Visitors to Volta Basin should take precautions to prevent malaria and other tropical diseases. Here are some recommendations from the CDC:

  • Get vaccinated for yellow fever and other diseases
  • Take antimalarial medication as prescribed
  • Use insect repellent and mosquito nets
  • Drink only bottled or purified water

Getting to Volta Basin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Volta Basin is easily accessible from Accra, the capital of Ghana. Here are some options for getting to Volta Basin:

Airports Near Volta Basin

Bus Stations Near Volta Basin

  • STC Bus Station, Accra
  • VIP Bus Station, Accra

Other Options

How to Get Around in Volta Basin

The best way to explore Volta Basin is by hiring a car or taxi. There are also motorcycle taxis (okada) available for short journeys. Bicycle rentals are available in some areas for those who prefer eco-friendly transportation.
