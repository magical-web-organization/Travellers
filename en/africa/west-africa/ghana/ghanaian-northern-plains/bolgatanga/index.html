Introduction to Bolgatanga

Bolgatanga, located in the Northern Plains of Ghana, is a vibrant city that is steeped in culture and heritage. This city, also known as Bolga, is the capital of the Upper East Region of Ghana and is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the best things to do and see in Bolgatanga.

List of Places to Visit in Bolgatanga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bolgatanga is home to many museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the rich culture and heritage of the region. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Bolgatanga Regional Museum: This museum showcases the region’s cultural heritage through a collection of traditional artifacts and photographs.
  • Paga Crocodile Pond: This pond is home to sacred crocodiles that are believed to be the reincarnations of the ancestors of the local people.
  • Tongo Hills: These holy hills are home to the Tongo Shrine, where the local people offer sacrifices to their ancestors.

Natural Attractions of Bolgatanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bolgatanga is surrounded by beautiful nature and offers many outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Tenzug Shrine: This sacred shrine is an important religious site for the local people and offers a stunning view of the surrounding hills.
  • Navrongo Rock: This massive rock formation is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Bolgatanga and offers a panoramic view of the city.
  • Pikworo Slave Camp: This historical site is an important reminder of the slave trade and offers a glimpse into the dark history of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bolgatanga

Bolgatanga offers many hiking and biking routes that traverse the hills and valleys of the region. Some of the best routes include:

  • Tali Hills: This route takes you through the Tali Hills and offers a stunning view of the region’s natural beauty.
  • Sirigu Village: This bike tour takes you through the picturesque Sirigu village, known for its traditional mud houses and vibrant cultural festivals.

Best Things to Do in Bolgatanga: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do and see in Bolgatanga, ranging from cultural immersion to outdoor adventures. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Visit a Local Market: Bolgatanga is famous for its colorful marketplaces, where you can buy local produce, textiles, and crafts.
  • Traditional Music and Dance: Experience the vibrant culture of the region by watching a traditional music and dance performance.
  • Rock Climbing and Abseiling: Embark on an adrenaline-filled adventure by climbing the Navrongo Rock or abseiling down the Tenzug Shrine.

Food and Drink Scene of Bolgatanga

Must-Try Dishes

Bolgatanga is famous for its diverse cuisine that offers a mix of traditional Ghanaian and international flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Tuo Zaafi: This is a popular dish made from boiled corn flour served with soup and meat.
  • Fufu: This dish is made from cassava or plantain and is served with soup or stew.
  • Jollof Rice: This spicy rice dish is a staple in many West African countries and is served with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Bolgatanga is known for its traditional drinks that are made from local ingredients. Some of the popular beverages include:

  • Pito: This is a traditional beer made from fermented millet and is a popular drink during festivals and celebrations.
  • Sobolo: This is a hibiscus tea that is popular in Ghana and is served cold with sugar and ice.
  • Zom-Kom: This is a refreshing drink made from the pulp of the baobab fruit and is served cold.

Where to Stay in Bolgatanga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bolgatanga offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Upland Hotel: This 3-star hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as a bar, restaurant, and swimming pool.
  • Kambe Hotel: This boutique hotel offers stylish rooms and suites with modern amenities such as free Wi-Fi and a 24-hour front desk.
  • Guest Beza Hotel: This budget-friendly guesthouse offers clean and comfortable rooms and is located in a quiet neighborhood.

What to Buy in Bolgatanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bolgatanga is famous for its traditional crafts and textiles that make for perfect souvenirs to bring home. Some of the popular products to buy include:

  • Bolga Baskets: These hand-woven baskets are made from locally sourced straw and are popular for their durability and simplicity.
  • Kente Cloth: This is a colorful fabric that is hand-woven by local artisans and is a symbol of Ghanaian cultural heritage.
  • Smocks: These are traditional garments worn by the people of Ghana and are made from hand-loomed cotton or silk.

Local Culture and Customs of Bolgatanga

Bolgatanga is home to many ethnic groups, each with its own unique customs and traditions. Some of the common customs and traditions include:

  • Greetings: It is customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile.
  • Dress Code: The people of Bolgatanga are generally conservative, and it is advisable to dress modestly in public.
  • Respect for Elders: The local people have a great respect for their elders and it is customary to greet them first in a group.

Best Time to Visit Bolgatanga

The best time to visit Bolgatanga is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities without any interruptions. The rainy season, which runs from April to October, can be quite hot and humid, making it less enjoyable for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bolgatanga

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain when shopping at the market.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without asking for permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Use your left hand to eat or to pass items to others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bolgatanga

Bolgatanga is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe in a hotel safe or secure location.
  • Be cautious when accepting food or drinks from strangers.

Health in Bolgatanga

Visitors to Bolgatanga should take precautions to protect their health such as:

  • Carry a mosquito repellent and use it at all times.
  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Get vaccinated for yellow fever and other illnesses before traveling.

Getting to Bolgatanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bolgatanga is easily accessible through various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Bolgatanga

Bus Stations Near Bolgatanga

How to Get Around in Bolgatanga

The best way to get around Bolgatanga is by taxi, as they are readily available and relatively affordable. Biking and hiking are also popular modes of transportation, especially for those who want to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Where to Go Next from Bolgatanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many destinations and excursions that you can explore from Bolgatanga, including:

  • Mole National Park: This park is located approximately 5 hours from Bolgatanga and offers visitors the chance to see elephants, antelopes, and other wildlife.
  • Tamale: This city is located approximately 5 hours from Bolgatanga and is home to various markets, museums, and historical sites.
  • Yendi: This town is located approximately 2 hours from Bolgatanga and is known for its cultural festivals and traditional crafts.

Conclusion

Bolgatanga is a vibrant city that is full of cultural heritage and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the best of Bolgatanga, including its must-see attractions, delicious food, and diverse culture. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, Bolgatanga has something for everyone. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore this beautiful city.
