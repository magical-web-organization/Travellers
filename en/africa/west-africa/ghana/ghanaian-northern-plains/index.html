Introduction to Ghanaian Northern Plains

Ghana is a country of diverse landscapes, vibrant cultures, and rich histories. One of the most breathtaking areas is Ghanaian Northern Plains, the northernmost region of the country. This area is filled with rolling hills, open savannah, and stunning waterfalls, as well as archaeological sites and cultural museums that offer insight into Ghana’s deep history. Whether you’re an avid hiker, history buff, or culture lover, Ghanaian Northern Plains has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Ghanaian Northern Plains

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Larabanga Mosque: one of the oldest mosques in Ghana
  • Paga Crocodile Ponds: home to sacred crocodiles that walk freely with locals
  • Boabeng-Fiema Monkey Sanctuary: a protected forest where you can spot various species of monkeys
  • Navrongo Catholic Cathedral: an impressive example of European-Gothic architecture
  • Sirigu Women’s Pottery: a center for traditional pottery-making by local women
  • Bolgatanga Market: a vibrant local market with craft, food, and textile stalls
  • Wechiau Hippopotamus Sanctuary: a protected area for hippos and other wildlife

Natural Attractions of Ghanaian Northern Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mole National Park: a wildlife reserve with elephants, antelopes, baboons, and other savannah animals
  • Kintampo Waterfalls: a stunning series of waterfalls and cascading pools in a forested area
  • Tongo Hills: a group of granite hills with unique rock formations and a local village
  • Gambaga Scarp: a scenic cliff with views over the plains
  • Ankasa Conservation Area: a biodiverse area with rainforest, rivers, and wildlife
  • Nakpanduri Mystic Stone: a mysterious rock formation on a hill

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ghanaian Northern Plains

If you are looking for adventure, hiking and biking are popular activities in Ghanaian Northern Plains. Here are the most well-known routes:

  • Larabanga-Bui National Park: a two-day trek that takes you from the Larabanga Mosque to the Bui National Park
  • Mole National Park: several hiking routes that allow you to get closer to the wildlife, such as the 5km Savanah Trail or the 11km Wannasee Trail
  • Tongo Hills: a two-hour hike that takes you to a local village, with views over the hills
  • Gambaga Scarp: a short but steep climb to the top of the cliff
  • Nakpanduri: a trek to the mysterious Mystic Stone

Best Things to Do in Ghanaian Northern Plains: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Ghanaian Northern Plains, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a tour of the Larabanga Mosque, and learn about Islam in Ghana
  • Visit the Paga Crocodile Ponds, and watch the crocodiles being fed
  • Go on a safari in Mole National Park, and see elephants, antelopes, baboons, and warthogs
  • Visit the Boabeng-Fiema Monkey Sanctuary, and spot various species of monkeys
  • Learn how to make pottery at Sirigu Women’s Pottery
  • Shop for crafts and textiles at Bolgatanga Market
  • Take a scenic drive to Tongo Hills, and visit the village at the top of the hills
  • Swim in the pools of Kintampo Waterfalls
  • Go bird-watching in Ankasa Conservation Area
  • Visit the Nakpanduri Mystic Stone, and hear the local legends

Week-Long Itinerary for Ghanaian Northern Plains

If you have a week to explore Ghanaian Northern Plains, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Larabanga Mosque and Paga Crocodile Ponds
  • Day 2: Mole National Park safari and the Savanah Trail
  • Day 3: Tongo Hills and the village tour
  • Day 4: Sirigu Women’s Pottery and Bolgatanga Market
  • Day 5: Kintampo Waterfalls and Gambaga Scarp
  • Day 6: Ankasa Conservation Area and bird-watching
  • Day 7: Nakpanduri Mystic Stone and local legends tour

Food and Drink Scene of Ghanaian Northern Plains

Must-Try Dishes

Ghanaian cuisine is delicious and hearty, with lots of spices and herbs. Here are some of the best dishes to try in Ghanaian Northern Plains:

  • Waakye: a dish made of rice and beans, served with spicy sauce and fried plantain
  • Fufu and soup: a staple meal made of mashed yams or cassava, served with soup
  • Jollof rice: a popular rice dish with tomatoes, onions, and spices
  • Tuo zaafi: a porridge-like dish made of millet or sorghum flour, served with soup
  • Kenkey: a fermented cornmeal dish, often served with fish or stew

Local Beverages

Ghanaian Northern Plains is also known for its refreshing beverages, made from local ingredients. Here are some drinks to try:

  • Sobolo: a hibiscus tea with ginger, often served chilled
  • Pito: a fermented drink made from millet or sorghum, similar to beer
  • Bissap: another hibiscus drink, often sweetened with sugar or honey
  • Palm wine: a traditional drink made from the sap of palm trees

Where to Stay in Ghanaian Northern Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ghanaian Northern Plains has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to simple guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mole Motel: located inside Mole National Park, with views over the savannah and a swimming pool
  • Bui Village Lodge: a charming lodge near the Bui National Park, with comfortable rooms and a restaurant
  • Safari Hotel: a budget-friendly hotel in Tamale, with basic rooms and a rooftop terrace
  • Crocodile Island: a unique eco-lodge on the Paga Crocodile Ponds, with traditional huts and cultural activities
  • Paga Hill Guest House: a guesthouse in the heart of Paga, with comfortable rooms and a restaurant

What to Buy in Ghanaian Northern Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ghanaian Northern Plains is a great place to shop for crafts, textiles, and local products. Here are some ideas:

  • Bolga baskets: colorful woven baskets made by local artisans, available at Bolgatanga Market
  • Batik cloth: hand-dyed fabric with vibrant patterns, available at Sirigu Women’s Pottery
  • Shea butter: a natural skin moisturizer made from the nuts of shea trees, available at local markets and shops
  • Beaded jewelry: necklaces and bracelets made with colorful beads, available at craft stalls and markets

Local Culture and Customs of Ghanaian Northern Plains

Ghanaian Northern Plains is a region with a proud cultural heritage, and it is important to respect local customs and manners. Here are some tips:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially near mosques and religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people, especially in rural areas.
  • If someone offers you food or drink, it is polite to accept.
  • Handshaking and greeting someone by their name is customary, especially in the Muslim north.

Best Time to Visit Ghanaian Northern Plains

The best time to visit Ghanaian Northern Plains is during the dry season, from November to March. The weather is cooler and less humid, making outdoor activities more comfortable. However, wildlife sightings are more difficult during the dry season, so keep that in mind. The rainy season, from April to October, can be very wet and muddy, but it brings lush greenery and beautiful waterfalls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ghanaian Northern Plains

Do:

  • Try local food and drink
  • Respect local customs and manners
  • Learn some basic phrases in Dagbani or other local languages
  • Haggle politely at markets and stalls

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission
  • Wear revealing clothes in public places
  • Disrespect religious traditions and beliefs
  • Make assumptions about local customs and practices

Safety and Health

Safety in Ghanaian Northern Plains

Ghana has a low overall crime rate, but it is important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in urban areas
  • Keep your valuable belongings out of sight
  • Be cautious of scams and pickpockets in crowded areas
  • Check the travel advisories before your trip

Health in Ghanaian Northern Plains

It is recommended to have up-to-date vaccinations for various diseases, such as yellow fever, malaria, and typhoid. It is also important to drink bottled water, wear insect repellent, and use sunscreen to avoid mosquito bites and sunburn. Medical facilities are available in major towns and cities, but it is advisable to have travel insurance.

Getting to Ghanaian Northern Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ghanaian Northern Plains

The main airport near Ghanaian Northern Plains is Kotoka International Airport in Accra, the capital city. There are several airlines that fly to Accra from major cities in Africa, Europe, and North America. From there, you can take a domestic flight to Tamale Airport or Wa Airport, which are the gateways to Ghanaian Northern Plains.

Bus Stations Near Ghanaian Northern Plains

Buses are a popular mode of transport in Ghana, and there are several bus stations that connect Accra and Tamale or Wa. Some of the major bus companies include STC, VIP, and Metro Mass. It is recommended to book your tickets in advance.

Other Options

Taxis, minibusses, and private cars are also options for getting around Ghanaian Northern Plains. However, it is important to negotiate the fares and ensure that the vehicle is in good condition.

How to Get Around in Ghanaian Northern Plains

The best way to get around Ghanaian Northern Plains is by hiring a car with a driver or taking a guided tour. This allows for flexibility and safety, especially if you are not familiar with the roads or culture. Taxis and minibusses are also available in towns and cities, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Ghanaian Northern Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Ghana, or if you want to visit nearby countries, here are some suggestions:

  • Cape Coast and Elmina: historic coastal towns with castles and forts, about a 6-hour drive from Accra
  • Kumasi: the cultural capital of Ghana, with a palace museum and traditional markets, about a 8-hour drive from Tamale
  • Burkina Faso: a neighboring country with a vibrant cultural scene, about a 6-hour drive from Tamale

Conclusion

Ghanaian Northern Plains is a must-visit destination for nature-lovers, culture-seekers, and adventure enthusiasts. With its breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and friendly locals, this region offers a unique and unforgettable experience. Follow our guide to plan your trip, discover the best places to visit, and immerse yourself in Ghanaian culture.
