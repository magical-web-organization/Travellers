Introduction to Wa

Wa is a hidden gem of the Northern Plains in Ghana, located in West Africa. It is a city with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. If you are an adventurous traveler who loves exploring off-beat destinations, Wa is the place for you. In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and other helpful tips for your travel itinerary to Wa.

List of Places to Visit in Wa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wacambo Shrine: This shrine is a National Monument and is known for its unique architecture and historical importance.
  2. Lobi House Museum: This museum showcases the traditional architecture, art, and culture of the Lobi people.
  3. Wa Naa’s Palace: The palace is home to the Wa Naa (Paramount Chief) and offers a glimpse into the traditional Ghanaian royalty and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Wa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wechiau Hippo Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to a large population of hippos and offers opportunities for river safaris, bird watching, and fishing.
  2. Gwollu Hills: These hills provide scenic views of the surrounding landscapes and are believed to have played a role in the defense against slave raiders.
  3. Bulenga Crocodile Pond: This pond is home to dozens of crocodiles and offers a unique opportunity to get up close with these reptiles.
  4. Arigu Lagoon: This lagoon is a bird-watcher’s paradise and offers a peaceful escape in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wa

  1. Nandom to Lawra Trail: This trail covers a distance of 28 km and offers scenic views of villages and waterfalls.
  2. Wa to Wechiau Trail: This trail covers a distance of 170 km and is suitable for experienced bikers.

Best Things to Do in Wa: Activities and Tours

  1. Hippopotamus River Safari and Bird Watching Tour: Take a safari down the Black Volta River and spot hippos, crocodiles, and over 235 bird species.
  2. Traditional Dance Performance: Watch a traditional dance performance and learn the history and culture behind the dance.
  3. Wa Market Tour: Explore the vibrant Wa market and shop for locally-made handicrafts and products.
  4. Motorbike Tour of Wa: Explore the city of Wa and its surroundings on a motorbike tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wa

Day 1: Explore the city of Wa and visit Wacambo Shrine and the Wa Naa’s Palace.

Day 2: Take a river safari and bird watching tour in Wechiau Hippo Sanctuary.

Day 3: Hike the Nandom to Lawra Trail and explore the villages and waterfalls along the way.

Day 4: Visit Lobi House Museum and learn about the art and culture of the Lobi people.

Day 5: Take a traditional dance performance tour and learn about the history and culture behind the dance.

Day 6: Explore the Wa market and shop for locally-made handicrafts and products.

Day 7: Visit Bulenga Crocodile Pond and get up close with the crocodiles.

Food and Drink Scene of Wa

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tuo Zaafi: A popular dish made from corn flour and served with a soup or stew.
  2. Waakye: Rice and beans cooked together and served with a variety of sauces and sides.
  3. Banku: A fermented corn and cassava dough, cooked and served with soup, stew or sauce.
  4. Fura da Nono: This is a mixture of fermented milk and ground millet that creates a yogurt-like substance.

Local Beverages

  1. Pito: A locally brewed alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet or sorghum.
  2. Zoomkoom: A refreshing ginger and lemon drink.

Where to Stay in Wa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Nafana Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse offering clean and comfortable rooms.
  2. Wa East Guesthouse: A guesthouse with attentive staff and a beautiful garden.
  3. Uweto Guesthouse: A family-owned guesthouse located outside of the city and offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Wa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handwoven baskets: These baskets are made by local artisans and come in a variety of sizes and designs.
  2. Shea butter: This locally-made moisturizer is used for skin and hair care.
  3. Beads and fabric: Wa is known for its colorful beads and fabric that are used to make beautiful jewelry and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Wa

The people of Wa are friendly and welcoming, but it is important to be respectful of their culture and customs. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites, and ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Best Time to Visit Wa

The best time to visit Wa is during the dry season, from November to April. The weather is pleasant, and the roads are more accessible than during the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wa

Do:

  1. Learn some basic phrases in the local languages.
  2. Respect the local culture and customs.
  3. Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing or dress inappropriately.
  2. Take photographs without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wa

Wa is generally considered safe for travelers, but it is important to take basic precautions such as avoiding empty streets at night and not flashing valuable items. Keep your valuables safe and secure at all times.

Health in Wa

Travelers to Wa should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and may need additional vaccinations depending on the length and nature of their trip. Mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria are prevalent in Ghana, so it is important to take preventive measures such as wearing insect repellent and sleeping under mosquito nets.

Getting to Wa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wa

Wa has a small airport, but the majority of international travelers arrive at Kotoka International Airport in Accra, which is about an 11-hour drive from Wa.

Bus Stations Near Wa

There are several bus stations in the city of Wa serving routes to nearby towns and cities in Ghana.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are available in Wa for getting around the city and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Wa

Walking is the best way to explore the city of Wa, but taxis and motorbikes are also available for getting around the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Wa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mole National Park: A national park with a large elephant population, located about a 3-hour drive from Wa.
  2. Navrongo: A town with a unique architectural style and cultural attractions, located about a 4-hour drive from Wa.

Conclusion

Wa is a hidden gem in Ghana, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With our comprehensive travel guide, you will be able to explore the best places to visit, activities to do, and cultural attractions to experience in Wa, making your trip a memorable one.
