Introduction to Abengourou

Abengourou is a city located in Eastern Plantations, in Côte d’Ivoire, in West Africa, in Africa. This city is known as a hub for cocoa and coffee production, and it is bordered by lush green fields and dense forests. Despite being off the beaten path, Abengourou’s unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality make it a hidden gem that is worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Abengourou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Abengourou boasts a rich cultural history that can be seen in its numerous museums and historical landmarks. Start your cultural tour with a visit to the Palais de Justice, a colonial-era building that was once the seat of the French justice system. Explore the nearby Basilique Notre-Dame de la Paix, a beautiful church that showcases a unique blend of African and European architectural styles. Next, head to the Musée du Masque, which is dedicated to the art of mask-making and features a collection of over 300 masks from different regions of West Africa.

Natural Attractions of Abengourou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Abengourou’s natural attractions offer an array of outdoor adventures and scenic views. For a great outing, visit the Cascade Waterfalls which are located just outside the city. You can swim in the cool waters or have a picnic on the banks. The city is also surrounded by dense forests that offer great hiking opportunities. You can explore the Mont Nimba Strict Nature Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 100 miles from Abengourou. Trek through the forests and come face to face with different species of animals and birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abengourou

Abengourou has several hiking and biking routes that are ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. One of the most scenic routes is the Yamoussoukro-Bouaké trail that stretches for over 300 miles. This route cuts across the lush fields and dense forests of the region, offering stunning views of the scenery that can only be experienced on foot or on a bike. Several local tour operators provide guided bike tours, which can be booked for a day or longer.

Best Things to Do in Abengourou: Activities and Tours

Beyond exploring the city’s cultural and natural attractions, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy. One such activity is the coffee and cocoa tour, which teaches visitors about the process of harvesting coffee and cocoa beans from the fields to the cup. You can sample freshly brewed coffee or chocolate, and even buy some to take home. For a real adventure, try the canopy walk, which is a walk among the treetops of the forest, above the leafy canopy. This walk offers a bird’s eye view of the surrounding landscape, and you can spot different species of birds, animals and insects from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Abengourou

Must-Try Dishes

Abengourou is known for its delicious local cuisine that blends African and European flavours. The city’s signature dish is Attiéké, made from cassava couscous and served with grilled fish or chicken. Another popular dish is Foutou, mashed yam or plantains served with a sauce of your choice. Poulet braisé, grilled chicken, is a delicious delicacy that locals and tourists enjoy.

Local Beverages

Palm wine is a traditional beverage that is very popular in Abengourou. It is made from the sap of the Palmyra palm tree and has a sweet and slightly sour taste. You can also try Bangui, a traditional locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Abengourou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Abengourou has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. You can stay at upscale hotels such as Hôtel L’Animato or Hotel Prestige, which offer comfortable rooms and excellent service. If you prefer a more budget-friendly option, there are several guesthouses and hostels that offer clean and affordable rooms, such as Chez Lionel Guesthouse and B&B chez Lamy.

What to Buy in Abengourou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Abengourou offers several local products and souvenirs that are perfect to take home. You can buy locally-made crafts such as wooden carvings, jewelry, and textiles. You can also buy locally grown coffee and cocoa beans, which make great gifts for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Abengourou

Abengourou’s local culture is rich and diverse. The local people are warm and friendly, and they place a high value on community and hospitality. Dress modestly and respect their customs and traditions. You can experience the local culture during the annual festival that takes place in November or December. During this festival, there are music and dance performances, traditional games, and food and drink stalls.

Best Time to Visit Abengourou

The best time to visit Abengourou is during the dry season, which is between November and February. The rainy season, between March and October, makes it difficult to explore the natural attractions of the city as most activities are outdoor-based.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abengourou

Do:

  • Learn some basic French before you go.
  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Respect their customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s historical and cultural heritage.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Take photos of locals without their permission.
  • Leave valuables in plain view.
  • Walk alone at night or in sketchy areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abengourou

Abengourou is a safe city, but it’s important to be cautious and use common sense. Don’t leave valuables in plain sight and be cautious when walking in the evening or in unfamiliar areas. It is always a good idea to travel with a local or stay informed about the city’s safety situation before setting out on your tour.

Health in Abengourou

Abengourou has a tropical climate, so it’s important to protect yourself from insect bites and avoid dehydration. Carry a bottle of water with you at all times and use mosquito repellent. It’s also a good idea to get vaccinated against yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid before traveling to Abengourou.

Getting to Abengourou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Abengourou is well connected to other major cities in Côte d’Ivoire. There are several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and private cars. The nearest airport, Abidjan International Airport, is located approximately 135 miles from Abengourou, but you can hire a taxi or take the bus to reach the city. Once you arrive in Abengourou, it’s convenient to get around on foot or by motorcycle.

How to Get Around in Abengourou

Getting around Abengourou can be done on foot or by shared taxis or motorcycles. Motorcycle taxis are a popular mode of transportation, especially for shorter distances. They are easily identifiable by their brightly coloured yellow and green vests.

Where to Go Next from Abengourou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that you can explore from Abengourou, including Bouaké, the second-largest city in Côte d’Ivoire, and Yamoussoukro, the country’s capital. Bouaké offers many cultural and historical landmarks, such as the Mosque de Dioulakro and the Maquis des Artistes. Yamoussoukro is home to the impressive Basilique de Notre-Dame-de-la-Paix, which is one of the largest Catholic churches in the world.

Conclusion

Abengourou is a hidden gem that is full of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re on a solo trip, a romantic getaway, or a family vacation, there is something for everyone in Abengourou. Take a step off the beaten path and explore this unique city that is sure to leave a lasting impression on you.
