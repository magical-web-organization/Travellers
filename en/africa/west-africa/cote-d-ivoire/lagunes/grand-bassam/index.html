Introduction to Grand-Bassam

Grand-Bassam is a beautiful coastal town on the outskirts of Abidjan, the largest city in Cote d’Ivoire. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches, and stunning colonial architecture that has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The town has deep cultural and historical roots that date back to the late 19th century when it was the first capital of Cote d’Ivoire before it was moved to Abidjan. Grand-Bassam is a destination that offers visitors an immersive experience through its museums, historical sites, and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Grand-Bassam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Costume Museum

The National Costume Museum is a must-visit for anyone looking to dive into the cultural heritage of Cote d’Ivoire. The museum displays a vast collection of traditional costumes from different ethnic groups in the country. Visitors can learn about the different fabrics, motifs, and designs used to create these traditional garments while immersing themselves in the country’s culture.

Historical Colonial Buildings

Grand-Bassam is known for its beautiful colonial architecture, which provides a snapshot of the town’s rich history. Examples of the colonial buildings include the French Governor’s Palace, the Old Post Office, and the Assini Palace. These buildings are a testament to the town’s unique architectural heritage, a must-visit for anyone who loves history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Grand-Bassam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Beaches

Grand-Bassam is home to several beautiful beaches. Visitors can relax and soak up the sun while enjoying the views of the Atlantic Ocean. Some of the most popular beaches include the Bassam Beach, the Assinie-Mafia Beach, and the Kossou Dam that features crystal-clear water and is perfect for swimming.

Comoë National Park

The National Park is perfect for those who love nature and outdoor adventures. It’s home to several species of animals such as elephants, baboons, monkeys, and leopards. Visitors can choose to take either a walking or driving tour to explore the park while taking in the views of the lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand-Bassam

Grand-Bassam is perfect for those who love hiking and biking. The town’s landscapes offer different levels of difficulty, whether you are looking to have a leisurely walk or a heart-pumping workout. The most popular routes include the Comoë National Park and the Assinie Area Circuit, which takes visitors past the coast, lagoon, and forest areas.

Best Things to Do in Grand-Bassam: Activities and Tours

Cultural Tours

Cultural tours are perfect for visitors that want to explore the town’s history, culture, and heritage. Offered by several tour companies in the area they provide the opportunity to explore landmarks like the Old French Colonial Quarter, the Palais de Justice, and the Costume Museum. This is a unique way to learn about local culture and history.

Water Sports

Water sports like surfing, kayaking, and jet skiing are offered at the beaches in Grand-Bassam. Visitors can also book a yacht tour to see the town from the sea, which provides a unique perspective of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grand-Bassam

Day 1 – Arrival and explore the Colonial Quarter Day 2 – Visit the Costume Museum and Assini Palace Day 3 – Visit the Comoë National Park Day 4 – Spend the day at the beach Day 5 – Take part in water sports or book a yacht tour Day 6 – Visit the French Governor’s Palace and the Old Post Office Day 7 – Souvenir shopping and relaxing by the beach

Food and Drink Scene of Grand-Bassam

Must-Try Dishes

Alloco

Alloco is a popular dish in Cote d’Ivoire; It is made from fried plantains served with a spicy tomato or chili sauce. It’s a perfect lunch or dinner option that is both affordable and delicious.

Attiéké

Attiéké is a staple food in Cote d’Ivoire made from cassava. It is similar to couscous and can be served with fish, chicken, or beef stew, and it’s readily available in most restaurants in the town.

Local Beverages

Bissap

Bissap is a refreshing and sweet drink made from the hibiscus plant. It is served chilled and provides a perfect escape from the town’s warm weather. It’s a perfect drink to try, especially during the hot summer months.

Bangui

Bangui is a local beer brewed in Cote d’Ivoire; it is a perfect choice for those looking to explore the local beer scene while in Grand-Bassam.

Where to Stay in Grand-Bassam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grand-Bassam has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the most popular places to stay in the town include the Ibis Hotel, the Villa 17, and the Le Wharf Hotel & Spa. These accommodation options offer a range of amenities, including swimming pools, WiFi, and onsite restaurants.

What to Buy in Grand-Bassam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find several souvenirs and local products to take home as gifts or mementos. These items include traditional fabrics, carvings, and jewelry. There are several markets in the town where visitors can purchase these products, including the Grand-Bassam Artisanal Market and the Quartier France Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand-Bassam

The local people in Grand-Bassam are known for their warm and welcoming nature. Visitors should always respect the local culture and customs, dress appropriately, and not take photos without permission. It’s also essential to learn a few French words as many locals speak French, the official language of Cote d’Ivoire.

Best Time to Visit Grand-Bassam

The best time to visit Grand-Bassam is between November and March when the weather is cooler and less humid. The temperature during this period averages around 25°C, and there’s less rainfall, making it perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grand-Bassam

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few French words

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission
  • Disrespect the local culture
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Grand-Bassam

Grand-Bassam is relatively safe; however, visitors should take basic precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Grand-Bassam

Visitors should be up to date with routine vaccinations and prepare for the risk of malaria. They should take measures to prevent mosquito bites by using repellent and wearing long-sleeved shirts and pants.

Getting to Grand-Bassam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grand-Bassam

The nearest airport to Grand-Bassam is the Félix Houphouët-Boigny International Airport in Abidjan, located approximately 32 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or private shuttle to the town’s center.

Bus Stations Near Grand-Bassam

There are several bus stations near Grand-Bassam that offer transport to different cities and towns across Cote d’Ivoire. However, travelers should note that road conditions can be bumpy or unpredictable, and it’s advisable to book tickets in advance.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to hire a car to explore the town or take a shared taxi for a cheaper option.

How to Get Around in Grand-Bassam

The most common form of transport in the town is shared taxis, which are widely available and affordable. Alternatively, visitors can opt for private taxis or hire a car for self-driven tours.

Where to Go Next from Grand-Bassam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can opt to travel to nearby destinations such as Abidjan, the country’s largest city, which is an hour’s drive from Grand-Bassam. Other destinations include the Comoë National Park or the Assinie-Mafia beach area.

Conclusion

Grand-Bassam is one of the hidden gems of Cote d’Ivoire, providing a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. It’s an ideal destination for those who want to immerse themselves in local culture and explore the town’s breathtaking natural scenery. Visitors are sure to experience unforgettable moments while in Grand-Bassam.
