Introduction to Anjouan

Anjouan is one of the four islands that make up the Comoros, a volcanic archipelago situated off the coast of East Africa. Often referred to as the “Perfume Island”, Anjouan is known for its fragrant plantations of ylang-ylang, vanilla, and cloves. The island boasts a rich cultural heritage, diverse wildlife, and stunning natural landscapes that make it worth exploring. In this comprehensive tour guide to Anjouan, we will take a closer look at everything this beautiful island has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Anjouan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Anjouan is steeped in history and culture, with numerous landmarks and museums showcasing the island’s past. The following are some of the must-visit places for history buffs and culture enthusiasts:

  • Domoni Mosque: The oldest mosque on the island, built in the 15th century using local materials, such as coral and lava stones. It’s a beautiful example of Swahili architecture and is still in use today.

  • The Old Customs House: Located in Mutsamudu, this historical building was once the center of trade and commerce in Anjouan. Today, it is a museum that provides insights into the island’s history and economy.

  • The Sultan’s Palace: Another popular attraction in Mutsamudu, the Sultan’s Palace is a testament to Anjouan’s rich culture and heritage. Visitors can explore the beautifully preserved palace and learn about the life of the sultans who once ruled the island.

Natural Attractions of Anjouan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Anjouan is known for its scenic views and diverse wildlife, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Below are some of the best natural attractions on the island:

  • Mount Ntringui: Anjouan’s highest peak stands at 1,595 meters and offers stunning views of the island’s landscape. Hiking to the top is a popular activity, but it requires a guide and is only recommended for experienced hikers.

  • Lokoni Forest: Anjouan’s largest forest reserve spans over 3,000 hectares and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the forest and spot rare species like the Anjouan Scops Owl.

  • Nosy Tanikely Marine Reserve: While technically not on the island of Anjouan, this marine reserve is easily accessible by boat and is a must-visit for snorkelers and scuba divers. It’s home to an abundance of tropical fish, sea turtles, and other marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anjouan

Anjouan’s rugged landscape makes it a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails include the trek to the top of Mount Ntringui, which is best done with a guide, and the hike to Bouni Beach, which offers stunning coastal views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Anjouan: Activities and Tours

Anjouan offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Popular options include:

  • Visiting the Chiroroni Natural Reserve: This wildlife sanctuary is home to several endangered species, including the Livingstone’s fruit bat and the Coelacanth fish.

  • Exploring the island’s many beaches: Anjouan is home to several beautiful beaches with crystal-clear waters, including Moya Beach and Bouni Beach.

  • Taking a plantation tour: Anjouan’s plantations are famous for their spices and fragrant flowers. Visitors can take tours to learn more about ylang-ylang, vanilla, and nutmeg cultivation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anjouan

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Anjouan, here’s how you can make the most of your time on the island:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the historical landmarks in Mutsamudu, such as the Old Customs House and the Sultan’s Palace.

  • Day 3-4: Hike to the top of Mount Ntringui and explore Nosy Tanikely Marine Reserve.

  • Day 5-6: Relax on Anjouan’s beautiful beaches, such as Moya Beach and Bouni Beach.

  • Day 7: Take a plantation tour and learn more about the island’s spice cultivation.

Food and Drink Scene of Anjouan

Anjouan’s cuisine reflects the island’s diverse cultural heritage, with Arab, African, and French influences. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boko Boko: A local dish made with chicken, spinach, and coconut milk.

  • Langouste a la Vanille: Grilled lobster served with a vanilla sauce.

  • Pilaou: A rice dish flavored with spices and served with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Mataba: A refreshing drink made with coconut water, sugar, and lime juice.

  • Mawaba: A local beer brewed with cassava.

Where to Stay in Anjouan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anjouan offers a range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the popular choices include:

  • La Gazelle Hotel: A mid-range hotel in Mutsamudu that offers comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the harbor.

  • Le Papillon Hotel: A luxury hotel in Bambao that offers spacious rooms and villas with private pools and ocean views.

  • Chez Mema: A budget guesthouse in Mutsamudu that offers basic rooms and a shared kitchen for self-catering.

What to Buy in Anjouan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anjouan is famous for its spices and fragrances, making these excellent souvenirs to bring back home. Other popular items include woven baskets, traditional jewelry, and vanilla pods.

Local Culture and Customs of Anjouan

Anjouan’s culture is influenced by its history and diverse religious traditions, with Islam being the dominant religion. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Anjouan

The best time to visit Anjouan is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The island can get hot and humid during this time, but it’s also the best time for outdoor activities and exploring the island’s natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anjouan

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

  • Learn a few basic phrases in the local language, Shikomori.

  • Try the local cuisine and spices.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear outside of the beaches.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anjouan

Anjouan is generally a safe destination for visitors, but it’s always recommended to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Anjouan

It’s recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before traveling to Anjouan and to make sure you’re up-to-date with all necessary vaccinations. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses and dehydration.

Getting to Anjouan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anjouan

The main airport on the island is the Anjouan Ouani Airport, which is served by several airlines that offer flights to other destinations in the Comoros.

Bus Stations Near Anjouan

There is no public transport system on the island, but visitors can hire taxis or private cars to get around.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Anjouan by ferry from other islands in the Comoros or from Tanzania.

How to Get Around in Anjouan

The best way to get around Anjouan is by taxi or private car. Visitors can also hire bicycles or motorbikes to explore the island at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Anjouan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anjouan is part of a larger archipelago that includes the islands of Grande Comore, Mohéli, and Mayotte. Visitors can take ferries or flights to these destinations to continue their travels in the region.

Conclusion

Anjouan is a beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone on this fragrant island. Plan your trip well, dress modestly, and respect local customs and traditions, and you’re sure to have a memorable experience in Anjouan.
