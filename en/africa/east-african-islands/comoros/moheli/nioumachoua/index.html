Nioumachoua is a picturesque town in Mohéli, one of the three islands that make up the Union of Comoros. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, stunning vistas, and friendly locals. If you’re planning a trip to Nioumachoua, our comprehensive travel guide has got you covered. We’ve put together a list of must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do and see, where to stay, what to buy, and much more.

Introduction to Nioumachoua

Nioumachoua is a charming town located on the southern coast of Mohéli, the smallest of the three Comorian islands. The town is known for its laid-back atmosphere, friendly locals, and beautiful natural scenery. Nioumachoua is a great place for travelers who want to experience authentic Comorian culture while also enjoying stunning beaches, outdoor adventures and exploring the island’s history and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Nioumachoua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moheli National Park - A protected area on Moheli that is home to several endemic and endangered species, such as the Moheli scops owl and the Comoros livingstone fruit bat.
  • Itsandra Mosque - The oldest mosque on the island, built in the 15th century.
  • Mouale Mosque - A beautiful white mosque in the center of Nioumachoua, dating back to 1939.

Natural Attractions of Nioumachoua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plages de Nioumachoua - A long stretch of white sand beaches with crystal-clear water, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.
  • Mount Ntingui - A challenging hike up to the summit of the highest peak on Moheli, with breathtaking views of the island.
  • Tanjona Beach - A secluded beach with pristine waters, only accessible by boat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nioumachoua

  • Mount Ntingui Hike - A challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Moheli’s highest peak, with stunning panoramic views of the island.
  • Nioumachoua Coastal Trail - A scenic coastal trail that takes you along the beaches and rocky cliffs of Nioumachoua.

Best Things to Do in Nioumachoua: Activities and Tours

  • Dolphin Watching - Take a boat tour to spot some of the many dolphins that are found in the waters around Moheli.
  • Snorkeling and Diving - Discover the island’s rich underwater life with a snorkeling or diving tour.
  • Traditional Boat Tour - Take a traditional dhow boat tour around the island to explore its many coastline highlights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nioumachoua

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Nioumachoua, relax at the beach.
Day 2 Hike to the summit of Mount Ntingui.
Day 3 Explore the Moheli National Park.
Day 4 Take a boat tour to spot dolphins.
Day 5 Visit Itsandra Mosque and the surrounding area.
Day 6 Take a traditional dhow boat tour around the island.
Day 7 Relax on the beach and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Nioumachoua

Must-Try Dishes

  • Langouste - A local lobster dish, often served grilled with garlic butter or in a curry.
  • Papayes farcies - Stuffed papayas with beef, spices, and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Mkatra Mora - A traditional Comorian drink made from fermented honey and water.
  • Banga - A local beer made from cassava.

Where to Stay in Nioumachoua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Moheli Laka Lodge - A charming eco-resort on the beach, offering comfortable bungalows with private balconies.
  • L’Heure Bleue - A colonial-style hotel that features an outdoor pool, a spa, and a restaurant serving Creole cuisine.
  • Chez Hakim Hotel - A guesthouse located in the heart of Nioumachoua, offering affordable rooms with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Nioumachoua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Comorian Ylang-Ylang Perfume - A fragrant and popular perfume made from the flowers of the ylang-ylang tree.
  • Hand-crafted Wooden Bowls - Beautiful hand-made wooden bowls, often used for serving food.
  • Hand-woven Raffia Mats - Colorful and durable mats made from woven raffia leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Nioumachoua

The Comorian culture is made up of a mix of influences from African, Arab, and French traditions. The locals are warm and friendly, and it’s important to show respect for their customs.

Best Time to Visit Nioumachoua

The best time to visit Nioumachoua is during the dry season, which runs from May to November. The weather is sunny and pleasant during this time, and the sea is calm, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nioumachoua

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when entering religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of people.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water. Stick to bottled water.
  • Don’t touch or disturb the wildlife in the national park.
  • Avoid discussing politics or religion, as it can be sensitive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nioumachoua

Nioumachoua is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to take the usual precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Nioumachoua

Visitors to Nioumachoua should take precautions against malaria and dengue fever, both of which are prevalent in the Comoros. It’s essential to use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid being bitten.

Getting to Nioumachoua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nioumachoua

The closest airport to Nioumachoua is Prince Said Ibrahim International Airport (HAH) in Moroni, the capital of the Comoros. From there, you can take a domestic flight or a boat to Moheli.

Bus Stations Near Nioumachoua

There are regular buses that run between the towns on Moheli Island. Taxis are also available for hire.

Other Options

Many visitors to Moheli choose to take a boat from Grande Comore, one of the other islands in the Union of Comoros.

How to Get Around in Nioumachoua

The best way to get around the town is on foot, as it is small and easy to navigate. Taxis and motorbikes are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Nioumachoua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fomboni - The capital of Moheli, home to the historic Moheli Palace and the bustling market.
  • Anjouan - The second-largest island in the Union of Comoros, known for its beautiful beaches and traditional spice markets.
  • Grande Comore - The largest island in the Union of Comoros, home to the endangered Coelacanth fish and the volcanic Mount Karthala.

Conclusion

Nioumachoua is a hidden gem on the Comorian islands, offering visitors a mix of beautiful nature, stunning beaches, and fascinating culture. With our comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to explore the town and everything it has to offer!
