Introduction to Toliara Province

Toliara Province, also known as Atsimo-Andrefana, is located in southwestern Madagascar. It is a diverse and fascinating region, home to stunning landscapes, cultural treasures, and breathtaking marine life. Toliara Province boasts a unique blend of African and Asian influences, as it was a major trading center for centuries.

Food and Drink Scene of Toliara Province

The local cuisine of Toliara Province is heavily influenced by French and Asian flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Romazava - A traditional Malagasy dish made with beef or pork, leafy greens, and ginger.
  • Lasopy - A soup made with tomato, meat, and local vegetables.
  • Ravitoto - A dish made with cassava leaves, pork, and peanut butter.

Local Beverages

  • Three Horses Beer - This is the most popular beer in Madagascar, brewed by THB (Three Horses Brewery).
  • Ranovola - A popular drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Rum - Madagascar produces some of the finest rum in the world, made with sugar cane.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toliara Province

Do:

  • Learn some Malagasy phrases - Locals appreciate it when visitors try to speak their language.
  • Respect local customs and traditions - Non-verbal communication plays a big role in Malagasy culture, so be mindful of your body language.
  • Enjoy the outdoors - Toliara Province is home to some of the most beautiful beaches and landscapes in Madagascar.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing - Malagasy culture is conservative, and showing too much skin is considered disrespectful.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission - Always ask for permission before taking someone’s photograph.
  • Buy products made from endangered species - It is illegal to buy, sell, or trade products made from endangered species in Madagascar.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toliara Province

Toliara Province is generally safe to travel, but it is always advisable to take basic precautions:

  • Be alert in crowded areas, especially at night.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Do not walk alone in poorly-lit or isolated areas.

Health in Toliara Province

Malaria is a common disease in Madagascar, so it is essential to take precautions such as using mosquito nets and repellent. It is also advisable to check with your doctor regarding vaccinations.

Getting to Toliara Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Toliara Province

The main airport serving Toliara Province is the Toliara Airport (TLE).

Bus Stations Near Toliara Province

Buses are a popular and economical way to travel around Madagascar. The main bus station in Toliara is located in the city center.

Other Options

Taxis, tuk-tuks, and rental cars are also available in Toliara Province, but it is advisable to use a reputable provider.

How to Get Around in Toliara Province

The best way to get around Toliara Province is by car or on foot. Walking is a great way to explore the city center, while renting a car is a convenient way to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Toliara Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Ifaty Beach - Located just south of Toliara, Ifaty Beach is famous for its stunning coral reefs and marine life.
  • Anakao - This small fishing village is located 30 km south of Toliara and is known for its unspoiled beaches and laid-back vibe.
  • Isalo National Park - Approximately 250 km north of Toliara, Isalo National Park is a stunning reserve that features canyons, waterfalls, and stunning scenery.
