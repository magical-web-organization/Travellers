Introduction to Nosy Be

Nosy Be is a small island located in the northern part of Madagascar, resting in the Indian Ocean. Known as the “Perfumed Island,” Nosy Be is a paradise for tourists looking to experience the beauty of nature combined with the tropical and exotic elements of the Madagascar. With pristine beaches, lush forests, rare species, a rich culture and history, Nosy Be is an extraordinary destination for relaxation and exploration. This comprehensive tour guide will help visitors that want to experience the best of Nosy Be by providing directions on how to travel around the island and lists of places to visit, activities to do and must-try local dishes.

List of Places to Visit in Nosy Be

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lokobe Reserve. This is a 450-acre nature reserve and forest, which is home to many of Madagascar’s endemic species. It’s one of the last areas of lowland rainforest remaining in Madagascar and is home to over 50 species of birds, nine species of lemurs, and various reptiles and amphibians.

  2. Nosy Komba Island. This small island is located only 20 minutes away from Nosy Be by boat. It serves as the home to the tragic spirit-worshipped Macaque monkey species. You can spend hours hiking through the well-blazed trails of the island and enjoy the sight of these monkeys in their natural habitat. You can also visit the village and see the traditional Malagasy way of life.

  3. Mount Passot. This 329-meter dormant volcano provides panoramic views of the island and its surroundings. You can climb the stairs reaching to the top and enjoy breathtaking views of the entire island, the Indian Ocean and the mainland of Madagascar.

Natural Attractions of Nosy Be: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Nosy Tanikely. This tiny marine reserve is widely recognized for its excellent diving opportunities, snorkeling and its stunning coral reefs. The reserve is home to many different species of tropical fish, turtles, rays and even some larger predators such as sharks.

  2. Nosy Iranja. This is a gorgeous uninhabited island, located around 45 minutes by boat from Nosy Be, with sun-kissed white sand beaches and crystal clear waters. It is a perfect place to bask in the sun, swim and go snorkeling. During low tide, there is a natural sand beach connecting the two islets, forming a picturesque and unique landscape.

  3. Sacred Tree of Mahatsinjo. This ancient Baobab tree is nearly 1000 years old and is known for having medicinal powers. Many Malagasy come to pray and pay their respects for bringing blessings to their lives. It’s located on the road from Hell-Ville to Dzamandzar and is often overlooked by tourists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nosy Be

  1. Montagne d’Ambre National Park. Located in the surrounding area of Diégo Suarez, Montagne d’Ambre National Park is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, making it an ideal place for hiking and guided treks.

  2. Nosy Be Botanical Gardens. These gardens are spread over a nine-acre plot of land, with more than 120 tropical plant species. Visitors can explore the garden through self-guided walks and tours.

  3. Cascade d’Amby. This waterfall is located in the center of the island, and the hike to the waterfall takes around an hour. It’s a perfect place for swimming, surrounded by the beauty of the waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Nosy Be: Activities and Tours

  1. Whale Watching Tour. This island is famous for its annual whale migration. Between July and September, tourists can experience whale watching tours, where they can see and hear these giant creatures from up close.

  2. Dolphin Tour. Swim and play alongside dolphins on this aquatic adventure tour. You’ll have the opportunity to see many different species of these friendly mammals that live in the Nosy Be waters.

  3. Kitesurfing. The windy season starts from April to November, which makes Nosy Be one of the best kitesurfing spots in the world. With consistent winds and warm water, you can experience a breathtaking ride on the waves all day long.

Food and Drink Scene of Nosy Be

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Zebu Steak. This dish consists of steak made from a special type of cattle particular to Madagascar’s highlands served with garlic and black pepper.

  2. Lobster. Nosy Be is known for its exotic seafood. You can find fresh lobsters, grilled to perfection in local restaurants and cafes or try them in local dishes.

    • Pro tip: Le Bateau Ivre and Madirokely beach are the best places for seafood lovers.

  3. Romazava. This traditional Madagascar dish is a stew made from meat, local greens, and spices. It is often served with rice and can be found in every restaurant around the island.

Local Beverages

  1. Three Horses Beer. This beer is a must-try when in Nosy Be. It’s produced in Madagascar and is one of the most popular beers in the country.

  2. Rum Infusions. Madagascar is the home of exotic flavors, and their rum flavors are no exception. Try the banana, mango, or vanilla flavors for an authentic experience.

Where to Stay in Nosy Be: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Manga Soa Lodge. A luxury lodge located within walking distance to Ambatoloaka beach. It features outdoor pools, Jacuzzi, great views and outdoor activities like scuba diving and snorkeling.

  2. Nosy Lodge. A budget-friendly lodge located at the heart of Ambatoloaka, the most lively part of Nosy Be. They have a well-maintained garden, two outdoor pools, and furnished bungalows.

  3. Le Jardin d’Antanimena. The colonial house is located 200 meters from Antanimena market and offers breezy and cozy rooms with excellent breakfast, service and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Nosy Be: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ylang-ylang Fragrances. Ylang-ylang is a flower grown only in Madagascar, which is aromatic and is used as a base for perfumes. From soaps, oils to perfumes, you can bring this exotic fragrance to gift your loved ones.

  2. Handwoven Raffia Goods. Purchase locally made hats, bags, and mats made from raffia as perfect souvenirs from Nosy Be.

Local Culture and Customs of Nosy Be

  1. Music and Dance. Madagascar’s music and dance culture play a significant role in showcasing the diversity of its unique heritage. Most of their traditional music is accompanied by its cultural dance moves performed by both men and women.

  2. Religion. The local Anjanas worship their ancestral spirits, and the westerners’ hold mostly Christian beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Nosy Be

The best time to visit Nosy Be is between April and December, where the weather ranges from warm to hot, and precipitation is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nosy Be

Do:

  1. Dress comfortably and modestly

  2. Bargain when purchasing items and food

  3. Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  1. Wander around the town after dark.

  2. Take photographs of individuals or their houses without permission.

  3. Touch lemurs as they are protected

Safety and Health

Safety in Nosy Be

Nosy Be is generally safe, but like many tourist destinations, theft can occur. Visitors should take precautions to keep their belongings safe, particularly in crowded areas such as markets and the beach.

Health in Nosy Be

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as malaria and dengue fever, particularly during the rainy season. It is recommended to carry and use mosquito repellent, avoid mosquito breeding grounds like pools of water and seek medical attention if symptoms like high fever, headache and muscle pain persist.

Getting to Nosy Be: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nosy Be

Fascene Airport (NOS) is the primary airport for flights to and from Nosy Be, providing efficient and reliable services from different cities in Madagascar and other African Countries.

Bus Stations Near Nosy Be

Bus transport is available from Antananarivo to Nosy Be 18 hours away. The journey can be booked through an agency in town.

Other Options

Boats and Ferries are also available from the mainland and other neighboring islands; private charters can also be rented.

How to Get Around in Nosy Be

The most common way to get around the island is by taxi-brousse, motorcycle taxi, or bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Nosy Be: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Diego Suarez; this is the largest town in the north and a great spot to explore. You can find various tours showcasing the amazing wildlife while exploring the ancient pirate history of Madagascar.

  2. The Tsingy Red Stone Park, Ankify; Located just south of Ankify, the Tsingy Park is a unique geological formation of red stone, carved by erosion and wind over decades.Visitors can experience trekking to the top for panoramic views of the ocean and village.
