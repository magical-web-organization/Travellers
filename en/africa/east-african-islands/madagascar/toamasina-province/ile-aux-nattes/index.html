Introduction to Ile aux Nattes

Nestled in the Toamasina Province of Madagascar, Ile aux Nattes is a hidden gem that boasts pristine, white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and an abundance of marine life. Also known as Nosy Nato or “the Island of the Fortunate Ones,” Ile aux Nattes has a relaxed atmosphere that allows visitors to immerse themselves in the island culture.

The island is small, only 2.5 kilometers long, and can be explored by foot, bicycle, or local boat. Its idyllic setting, rich history, and vibrant culture make Ile aux Nattes a must-visit destination in Madagascar.

List of Places to Visit in Ile aux Nattes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Village d’Ankazoberavina

Ankazoberavina village is located on the southwest coast of Ile aux Nattes. It is an excellent place to experience the island’s unique culture and way of living. You can wander through the village’s narrow alleys to explore traditional houses and meet the friendly locals.

Le Phare Jaune

Le Phare Jaune, located on the southern side of the island, is a lighthouse that has been in operation since 1901. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse to enjoy panoramic views of the island and the Indian Ocean.

Natural Attractions of Ile aux Nattes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Plage des Anglais

Plage des Anglais is a stunning beach located on the northeast coast of Ile aux Nattes. It is one of the most beautiful beaches on the island and features crystal-clear waters and a peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can relax on the beach, swim, snorkel or paddleboard.

Grand Sable Bays

Located on the western side of the island, Grand Sable Bays is a hidden gem that is accessible by boat. Surrounded by cliffs, the beach is a natural marvel with its stretch of white sand and turquoise waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ile aux Nattes

Ile aux Nattes is a small island, and hiking and biking are great ways to explore the island’s beauty. Visitors can rent bicycles from the island’s hotels and ride around the island’s paved and dirt roads. Routes include:

Le Chemin des Coquillages

This route follows the island’s coastline and passes through various beaches, coconut groves, and mangrove forests. It is a flat, scenic ride that is suitable for all skill levels.

Morcellement Parc

This route takes visitors through the interior of the island’s nature reserve, passing through forested areas and small villages. It is a challenging route and is suitable for experienced hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Ile aux Nattes: Activities and Tours

Snorkeling

Ile aux Nattes is surrounded by coral reefs, making it an ideal location for snorkeling. Visitors can explore the pristine waters and encounter a variety of sea creatures, including sea turtles and colorful fish species.

Boat Tours

Boat tours are a great way to explore the surrounding areas of Ile aux Nattes. Tours can vary from dolphin spotting to beach hopping, or simply enjoying the sunset from the Indian Ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ile aux Nattes

Day 1: Explore the village of Ankazoberavina and learn about the island’s culture and history.

Day 2: Spend your day on the idyllic beach of Plage des Anglais.

Day 3: Rent a bicycle and explore the island’s different beaches, coconut groves, and mangrove forests.

Day 4: Sign up for a boat tour, where you can see dolphins, have lunch on a secluded island and snorkel in the surrounding clear waters.

Day 5: Take a picnic to Grand Sable Bay, one of the most beautiful and secluded beaches on the island.

Day 6: Visit the Le Phare Jaune lighthouse and enjoy panoramic views of the island and the Indian Ocean.

Day 7: Spend your last day relaxing on the beach or trying one of the island’s water sports activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Ile aux Nattes

Must-Try Dishes

Romazava

Romazava is a traditional Madagascar vegetable soup that is made from leafy greens, meat, and various spices. It is considered a national dish in Madagascar and is a must-try when visiting Ile aux Nattes.

Local Beverages

Three Horses Beer

Three Horses Beer, also known as THB, is the most popular beer in Madagascar. It is a light-bodied, lager-style beer that is refreshing in the island’s tropical climate.

Where to Stay in Ile aux Nattes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Ile aux Nattes have several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts.

Hotel Natura Lodge

Hotel Natura Lodge is a luxurious hotel located on the northern side of Ile aux Nattes. The hotel offers bungalows that feature sea views, private terraces, and outdoor showers. Guests can also enjoy the hotel’s infinity pool and seaside restaurant.

L’Oasis de Nosy Be

L’Oasis de Nosy Be is a charming guesthouse located on the southern side of the island. The guesthouse features several detached bungalows and offers a peaceful atmosphere and proximity to the beaches.

What to Buy in Ile aux Nattes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Vanilla

Madagascar is known for its high-quality vanilla beans, and Ile aux Nattes is no exception. Visitors can bring home vanilla beans or locally made vanilla products like vanilla sugar or vanilla extract.

Local Culture and Customs of Ile aux Nattes

The people of Ile aux Nattes are friendly and welcoming. Visitors are encouraged to engage with locals to learn about the island’s unique culture and customs. The island’s culture is a mix of African and Asian influences and is reflected in its music, food, and architecture.

Best Time to Visit Ile aux Nattes

The best time to visit Ile aux Nattes is from May to November when the climate is dry, and the sea is calmer for water-related activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ile aux Nattes

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try traditional dishes and attend local festivals.
  • Engage in sustainable tourism practices by not littering and conserving water and energy.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the coral reefs or marine life.
  • Bring outside plants or animals onto the island.
  • Disrespect the local culture or way of life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ile aux Nattes

Ile aux Nattes is a safe destination. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Ile aux Nattes

Visitors to Ile aux Nattes should take basic health precautions, such as drinking only bottled water and applying insect repellent daily to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Ile aux Nattes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ile aux Nattes is accessible from several cities in Madagascar.

Airports Near Ile aux Nattes

The closest airport to Ile aux Nattes is Fascene Airport (NOS) in Nosy Be, where visitors can take a local flight to Toamasina Airport (TMM). From Toamasina, visitors can take a taxi or private transfer to the port and take a boat to Ile aux Nattes.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the island from the city of Mahambo, where local boats are available to take visitors to Ile aux Nattes.

How to Get Around in Ile aux Nattes

Visitors can explore the island by foot, bicycle, or local boat. Bicycles can be rented from the hotels, and local boats can be hired for exploring the surrounding islands and waters.

Where to Go Next from Ile aux Nattes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Ile aux Nattes can continue their exploration of Madagascar by visiting the nearby city of Toamasina or the Analamazaotra Special Reserve, home to various lemur species.

Conclusion

Ile aux Nattes offers a unique experience that combines natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and relaxation in an idyllic island setting. Plan your visit with our comprehensive guide and immerse yourself in Madagascar’s island paradise.
