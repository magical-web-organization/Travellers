Introduction to Toamasina Province

Located on the Eastern coast of Madagascar, Toamasina Province is a beautiful destination that boasts of natural beauty, rich culture, and a variety of exciting outdoor activities. The capital city of the province, Toamasina, is the largest port city in Madagascar and serves as a gateway to explore the province’s scenic wonders. This travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and other essential travel information for a memorable vacation in Toamasina Province.

List of Places to Visit in Toamasina Province

Toamasina Province has a rich history and diverse topography that guarantees an unforgettable vacation. Here is a list of some of the top attractions that should not miss while in the province:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Musee de la Fondation Bernheim: This museum gives travelers a glimpse into the history of the province and Madagascar at large, with exhibits that showcase the cultural and social aspects of the Malagasy people.
  2. Lighthouse of Pointe des Milles: This historic lighthouse is an essential landmark in Toamasina and provides unparalleled views of the Indian Ocean.
  3. Rova of Toamasina: This palace was built in the 19th century by Queen Ranavalona III and is an excellent representation of Malagasy architecture.

Natural Attractions of Toamasina Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ile aux Prunes: This island is the perfect destination for a relaxing day trip. With its crystal-clear waters and sandy beaches, it is great for swimming and sunbathing.
  2. Parc Ivoloina: This reserve offers a unique opportunity to see the incredible biodiversity of Madagascar. Visitors can view various animal species and learn about conservation efforts.
  3. Canal des Pangalanes: This canal stretches over 645 km, running parallel to the Eastern coast of Madagascar, from Farafangana to Tamatave. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the stunning landscapes, including lagoons, mangroves, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toamasina Province

  1. Analamazoatra Reserve: This nature reserve has a variety of hiking trails, taking visitors through lush vegetation and diverse wildlife.
  2. Andasibe-Mantadia National Park: This park is home to many indigenous species of flora and fauna, providing unique exploration opportunities.
  3. Masoala National Park: This park is among the largest protected reserves in Madagascar and has several biking and hiking routes that take visitors through majestic forests and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Toamasina Province: Activities and Tours

  1. Wildlife Tours: Toamasina Province is home to many endemic species and a variety of animals, including lemurs, chameleons, and more. Take a guided tour to learn more about these beautiful creatures.
  2. Beach activities: The province has beautiful beaches that offer plenty of activities such as surfing, paddleboarding, and kite surfing.
  3. Cultural Tours: Take a cultural tour to learn about the traditions and customs of the Malagasy people, including music, dancing, and food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Toamasina Province

Day 1: Visit the Musee de la Fondation Bernheim and the Lighthouse of Pointe des Milles. Day 2: Explore the Ile aux Prunes and enjoy a relaxing day at the beach. Day 3: Take a boat tour along Canal des Pangalanes, passing through scenic landscapes and beautiful lagoons. Day 4: Visit the Analamazoatra or Andasibe-Mantadia Reserve and hike through the beautiful rainforests. Day 5: Enjoy the beautiful views at Masoala National Park, including its beaches and large expanse of lush greenery. Day 6: Take a cultural tour, where you can learn about Malagasy traditions and customs, including food and music. Day 7: Take a relaxing bike ride or explore some of the lesser-known attractions in Toamasina.

Food and Drink Scene of Toamasina Province

Madagascar has a rich culinary heritage, and Toamasina is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ravitoto: This dish is a staple in Madagascar, made with manioc leaves, pork, and ginger.
  2. Romazava: made with beef, this stew is seasoned with local spices and served with rice.
  3. Akoho sy voanio: This hearty chicken and coconut milk dish is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  1. Three Horse Beer: This is Madagascar’s national beer, known for its refreshing taste and popularity among locals.
  2. Toaka gasy: an alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane juice and allowed to ferment.

Where to Stay in Toamasina Province: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Java: Situated in the center of Toamasina city, this hotel is renowned for its luxurious amenities and location.
  2. Hotel Coco Lodge: Positioned along the Pangalanes Canal, this lodge offers stunning views and comfortable accommodation.
  3. Hotel Ocean Whisper: Located along the Eastern Coast of the province, this oceanfront hotel offers scenic views and a serene environment for relaxation.

What to Buy in Toamasina Province: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Toamasina province is known for its local handicrafts, including textiles and wooden crafts. Some of the best items to buy when on vacation in the province include:

  1. Local textiles like raffia fibres and silk fabrics
  2. Wood carvings like zebu horns, walking sticks, and carved souvenirs
  3. Spices like Madagascan vanilla, cloves, and green pepper

Local Culture and Customs of Toamasina Province

Malagasy culture is diverse and rich, characterized by different traditional practices, including music, dances, and other rituals. Toamasina province hosts a mix of these practices, and visitors can experience some of these during cultural tours or by interacting with locals. It’s important to be respectful of the culture and customs during interactions with locals.

Best Time to Visit Toamasina Province

The best time to visit the province is during winter (May to November), when there is less rainfall, and the weather is cooler. During this period, tourists can partake in various outdoor activities, including hiking and beach activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toamasina Province

Do:

  1. Respect the locals’ culture and customs, especially during your interactions with them.
  2. Carry sunscreen, insect repellent and appropriate clothing like light clothing, hiking shoes, and headwear.
  3. Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Do not wear military-style clothing.
  2. Do not litter the environment, dispose of waste properly.
  3. Do not attempt to feed the animals in the parks and reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toamasina Province

Toamasina Province has a relatively high crime rate, especially in the city center. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and keep their valuables safe. It’s also essential to avoid isolated areas and walking alone at night.

Health in Toamasina Province

Travelers should take necessary precautions, including getting vaccinated against yellow fever and cholera before traveling to Madagascar. Visitors should also drink bottled or purified water and practice proper hygiene.

Getting to Toamasina Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Toamasina Province

The Toamasina Airport is the main airport serving the province, connecting the region to other cities within Madagascar, including Antananarivo, Morondava and Sambava amongst others.

Bus Stations Near Toamasina Province

Several bus companies operate services to Toamasina Province in Madagascar. The most popular are JIRAMA, Zuba Bus, and Cotisse.

Other Options

Travelers can also get to Toamasina Province by car. Car rentals are available at the airport or in the city center, with a chauffeur option available.

How to Get Around in Toamasina Province

The best way to get around Toamasina is by car rental, allowing visitors flexibility and convenience when navigating through the province. Buses, taxis and bike rentals are also available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Toamasina Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mahavelona: A small fishing village, featuring some of Madagascar’s most striking beaches.
  2. Andasibe: A nearby town with the Andasibe-Mantadia National Park and the Vakona Forest Lodge, which offers diverse outdoor activities and wildlife exploration options.
  3. Ambatondrazaka: A town in the province, known for its vibrant traditional Malagasy festivals and celebrations.

Conclusion

Toamasina Province serves as a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture and diverse wildlife, making it a travelers’ delight. The destination presents numerous outdoor activities, fantastic dining options, scenic landscapes, and unforgettable cultural experiences, providing a truly memorable vacation. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you in planning an adventure in the beautiful Toamasina Province and that you have a brilliant time exploring the natural wonders of Madagascar.
