Introduction to Seychelles

Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands situated in the Indian Ocean, off the eastern coast of Africa. It is famous for its crystal clear blue waters, white sand beaches, lush green forests, and diverse marine life. Seychelles has a rich history, culture, and cuisine that is worth exploring.

Local Culture and Customs of Seychelles

Seychelles has a diversity of cultures, including Creole, African, Indian, and European. The official language is Seychellois Creole, but English and French are also spoken. Seychelles has a relaxed and laid-back culture, where one can enjoy the beautiful beaches and warm weather.

Best Time to Visit Seychelles

The best time to visit Seychelles is from April to May and October to November. The weather is comfortable, and the tourists are relatively fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seychelles

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites
  • Protect the environment and marine life
  • Try the local seafood cuisine and beverages
  • Enjoy the serene beaches

Don’t:

  • Take coral or seashells as souvenirs from the beach
  • Disturb the marine life when scuba diving or snorkeling
  • Wear camouflage clothing, as it is only authorized for military personnel

Safety and Health

Safety in Seychelles

Seychelles is a safe country but like any other tourist destination, one has to be vigilant about their belongings and avoid going to secluded areas at night.

Health in Seychelles

Seychelles does not have any endemic diseases, and the water is safe to drink. However, travelers should consult their healthcare provider before traveling and get the necessary vaccinations.
