Introduction to La Digue

La Digue is a small island located in Seychelles, East African Islands, Africa. With stunning beaches, hidden coves, and granite boulders, La Digue is a popular destination for tourists looking to relax and unwind in paradise. The island is largely undeveloped—there are no high-rise hotels or fast-food chains—so visitors can expect a serene, laid-back atmosphere here.

List of Places to Visit in La Digue

If you’re planning to visit La Digue, you won’t be disappointed with the abundance of natural beauty, wildlife, and cultural landmarks. Here is a list of some of the best places to visit on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • L’Union Estate: A former coconut plantation that now serves as a park and museum. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of La Digue here, and walk through the plantation’s old buildings and cemetery.
  • Heritage Craft Village: This small museum showcases traditional Seychellois crafts, such as basket weaving and pottery. Visitors can also purchase handmade souvenirs and watch artisans at work.

Natural Attractions of La Digue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anse Source D’Argent: This is one of the most photographed beaches in the world, with clear turquoise water and towering granite boulders. Visitors can relax on the sand, snorkel, or explore the nearby coves.
  • Anse Cocos: This secluded beach is accessible only by hiking, but the effort is definitely worth it. With crystal-clear water and a rocky shoreline, this is one of the most picturesque spots on the island.
  • Veuve Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to the endangered black paradise flycatcher bird, known locally as the “Veuve”. Visitors can hike through the reserve, spotting unique flora and fauna and bird watching along the way.
  • Nid d’Aigle: This spot offers an amazing panoramic view of the island from the top of a cliff.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Digue

La Digue is a small island, so visitors can explore it on foot or by bike. Here are some routes to try out:

  • Anse Marron Hike: A challenging hike that takes you through the lush, tropical interior of the island to a secluded cove. Be prepared for some steep climbs and rocky terrain.
  • Petite Anse to Grand Anse Beach: A relatively easy hike that leads you to two beautiful beaches, known for their clear waters and white sand.
  • Biking around the island: Rent a bike and explore the island at your own pace. Make sure to see Anse Source D’Argent, one of the most stunning beaches in the world.

Best Things to Do in La Digue: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the natural and cultural attractions of the island, there are plenty of activities to keep visitors busy:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: La Digue is home to a variety of marine life, including colorful fish and coral. Visitors can rent equipment and take lessons from local dive centers.
  • Fishing Trips: Visitors can take a fishing trip with a local guide and try their hand at catching some of the island’s abundant fish species.
  • Sunset Cruises: Take a romantic sunset cruise around the island, and marvel at the stunning colors of the sky.
  • Island Hopping: Take a day trip to nearby islands such as Praslin or Curieuse, and experience their unique beauty and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Digue

For visitors who want to experience the best of La Digue in a week, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of La Digue

The food scene in La Digue is heavily influenced by the fresh seafood and local fruits and vegetables. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled fish with Creole Sauce: Fish is the staple dish of Seychelles, and this dish is a must-try. The fish is seasoned with a blend of local spices and served with a spicy tomato-based creole sauce.
  • Octopus Curry: Tender pieces of octopus cooked in a rich, flavorful curry sauce.
  • Fruit Bat: Not for the faint-hearted, fruit bat is a traditional Seychellois dish that is now a delicacy. It is usually cooked in a spicy sauce and served with rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink that comes straight from the coconut tree. It is a popular local delicacy and is known for its hydrating properties.
  • Toddy: A mildly alcoholic drink made from the sap of coconut palms. It is a popular local beverage and is usually fermented for a few hours to make it sweeter.

Where to Stay in La Digue: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Digue has a range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay on the island:

  • La Diguoise Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden. It is located in a quiet area of La Digue and is within walking distance of the beach.
  • Domaine de l’Orangeraie Resort and Spa: A luxury resort with a spa, swimming pool, and restaurant. It is located on the hillside and offers a stunning view of the sea.
  • Chez Marston Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and spacious rooms. It is located near the village and is within walking distance of the beach.

What to Buy in La Digue: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors who want to bring home a piece of La Digue, here are some local products and souvenirs to look out for:

  • Coconut oil: This natural oil is made from the meat of the coconut and is known for its moisturizing properties. It is a popular local product and can be found in most shops and markets.
  • Seychellois spices: The local spice blends used in Seychellois cuisine are a must-have for foodies. Look out for blends of cinnamon, turmeric, ginger, and other local spices.
  • Handmade crafts: There are many small shops and markets on the island selling handmade crafts such as baskets, hats, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of La Digue

The people of La Digue are known for their friendly and hospitable nature. Here are some customs and cultural practices to be aware of:

  • Respect local customs: Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and practices of the island. Dress appropriately when visiting cultural landmarks or attending local events.
  • Learn some Creole: The local language, Seychellois Creole, is a mix of French, English, and African languages. Learning a few basic phrases can go a long way in making connections with locals.
  • Bartering: Bartering is a common practice in local markets, so visitors should be prepared to negotiate prices.

Best Time to Visit La Digue

The best time to visit La Digue is between March and May, when the weather is dry and mild. The months of October and November can be rainy, but visitors can still enjoy the island’s natural beauty during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Digue

Do:

  • Respect wildlife: La Digue is home to a variety of unique wildlife, such as giant tortoises and black paradise flycatchers. Visitors should be respectful of their habitats and avoid disturbing them.
  • Protect the environment: Visitors should be mindful of their impact on the environment and avoid littering or damaging coral reefs.
  • Try local cuisine: Seychellois cuisine is a blend of African, French, and Indian influences, and visitors should take the opportunity to try some of the delicious dishes.

Don’t:

  • Take coral: Visitors should not take any coral from the island, as it is illegal and detrimental to the ecosystem.
  • Wear revealing clothing: Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting cultural landmarks or attending local events.
  • Use plastic bottles: Visitors should avoid using single-use plastics on the island, as they can be harmful to the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Digue

La Digue is a safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid isolated areas at night: Visitors should stay in well-lit and populated areas when walking at night.
  • Watch out for ocean currents: The currents around the island can be strong, so visitors should swim only in designated areas.
  • Secure valuables: Visitors should secure their valuables in a safe place when not in use.

Health in La Digue

There are no major health concerns in La Digue, but visitors should take precautions, such as:

  • Drink bottled water: Visitors should drink only bottled water or water that has been boiled or treated.
  • Use insect repellent: Visitors should use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases such as dengue fever or malaria.

Getting to La Digue: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Digue

The closest airport to La Digue is the Seychelles International Airport on Mahe Island. From there, visitors can take a ferry or a small plane to La Digue.

Bus Stations Near La Digue

There are no bus stations on La Digue. However, visitors can take a bus from the Seychelles International Airport on Mahe Island to the Baie Ste Anne jetty, and then take a ferry to La Digue.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private helicopter or boat to La Digue, depending on their budget and preferences.

How to Get Around in La Digue

The best way to get around La Digue is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent a bike for a day or a week, and explore the island at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from La Digue: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take day trips or longer excursions to nearby islands such as:

  • Praslin: This island is known for its stunning beaches and natural reserves, such as Vallée de Mai, home to the rare coco de mer palm tree.
  • Curieuse: This small island is home to a population of giant tortoises and is known for its natural reserves and abundance of marine life.

Conclusion

La Digue is a paradise island that has something to offer to every type of traveler. From secluded coves to stunning beaches, from challenging hikes to relaxing spas, La Digue has it all. Follow this week-long itinerary, explore the island’s natural and cultural attractions, and create unforgettable memories in one of the most beautiful places on earth.
