Introduction to Praslin

If you’re looking for a truly tropical paradise, Praslin Island is where you need to be! Located in the Seychelles, part of East African Islands, Praslin offers an unparalleled and distinct vacation experience. Known for its stunning white sand beaches, turquoise waters, and lush greenery, this island is the perfect destination for adventurers, nature lovers, and sun-seekers alike.

But, Praslin is more than just a beautiful beach destination. It is also home to a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural wonders, and unique local cuisine that will tantalize your taste buds. Read on to discover the top places to visit and things to do on your next trip to Praslin.

List of Places to Visit in Praslin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As a former French colony, Praslin has a rich cultural heritage that is best explored through its museums and historic sites. Here are some places you won’t want to miss:

  • Praslin Museum - This museum showcases the island’s history, culture, and biodiversity through exhibits ranging from giant tortoise skeletons to traditional fishing gear. You’ll get a glimpse into the way of life of the island’s earliest inhabitants to present day.
  • Vallée de Mai Nature Reserve - Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, this nature reserve is home to the famous Coco de Mer palm, which has the largest seed in the world. The reserve is also home to several other endemic species and is a must-visit for nature lovers and photographers.
  • St. Anne Church - Located in the heart of Praslin, this charming church is the oldest religious building on the island dating back to the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Praslin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While there is plenty of cultural history to explore, Praslin is predominantly known for its natural beauty. With such stunning sights all around you, you’ll want to hit up some of these natural attractions:

  • Anse Lazio Beach - This gorgeous beach is one of the most popular on the island, and for good reason. Turquoise waters, picturesque granite rock formations, and soft white sand make Anse Lazio a true beach lover’s paradise.
  • Anse Georgette Beach - Another beautiful beach located on the north-west of the island, Anse Georgette is a peaceful oasis that is perfect for a relaxing day out.
  • Fond Ferdinand Nature Reserve - This massive nature reserve is home to several endemic and endangered species including the Black Parrot, and the Seychelles Bulbul, as well as several endemic plants. The reserve offers several hiking trails ranging from easy to moderate, so pack some comfortable shoes and your camera and get out there.
  • Curieuse Island - Take a day trip to Curieuse Island and explore the famous giant tortoise sanctuary. You can also visit the remains of the old leper colony, go snorkeling in the crystal clear waters, or simply relax on one of the island’s secluded beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Praslin

From forest reserves to coastal paths, Praslin offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails. Here are some popular routes:

  • Anse Mondon Trail - A short 2.2km trail that follows the northwest coast of Praslin. Offering stunning views of the ocean, this trail takes you through a mangrove swamp and ends at Anse Mondon Beach.
  • Vallée de Mai Nature Reserve Trail - A relatively easy 2km trail that meanders through the reserve’s forest. Along the way, you’ll see the famous Coco de Mer palm trees and several other endemic species.
  • Fond Ferdinand Trail - A moderate 4km trail that winds through the reserve’s hills and meanders through a forest of endemic trees.

Best Things to Do in Praslin: Activities and Tours

When you’re done soaking up the beauty of Praslin, there are several activities and tours to keep you occupied. Here are some popular ones:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving - With crystal clear waters teeming with exotic marine life, it would be a shame not to take a dive into the water.
  • Boat Tours - Discover the surrounding islands like La Digue, Cousin, and Cousine with boat tours that often include lunch and a stop for swimming.
  • Fishing Trips - Praslin has a rich fishing culture, so why not experience it for yourself? Several local tours offer half day or full day fishing trips, so you can catch your own dinner.
  • Sailing and Windsurfing - The trade winds of the Seychelles islands make windsurfing and sailing a fun and thrilling activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Praslin

Here’s a sample itinerary to help plan your trip to Praslin:

  • Day 1: Visit the Vallée de Mai Nature Reserve and immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the island.
  • Day 2: Spend the day exploring the island’s beaches, with stops at Anse Lazio, Anse Georgette, and Anse Kerlan.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Curieuse Island, explore the giant tortoise sanctuary and snorkel in the clear waters.
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and explore the island’s various hiking trails including the Anse Mondon Trail or take a fishing trip.
  • Day 5: Visit St. Anne Church and the Praslin Museum to experience the island’s history and culture.
  • Day 6/7: Spend the last few days of your trip experiencing some of the island’s adventurous activities, like scuba diving, sailing, or windsurfing.

Food and Drink Scene of Praslin

When it comes to local cuisine, Praslin has a lot to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Fish - Being an island with plenty of fishes in the sea, grilled fish is a staple dish in Praslin, especially the Red Snapper is a popular option.
  • Octopus Curry - This dish is a favorite among locals and visitors alike. Tender pieces of octopus are cooked slowly in a creamy, spiced coconut sauce, and served with rice or bread ensures to satisfy your taste buds.
  • Ladob - This sweet dish is made with bananas, sweet potatoes or cassava, and coconut milk with brown sugar. It is often served as a dessert and is perfect after a delicious meal.

Local Beverages

  • Takamaka Bay Rum - Made locally on the Seychelles islands, Takamaka Bay rum is a smooth, flavorful rum made with local spices and sugar cane.
  • Seybrew and Ekyu - Seybrew is the national beer of Seychelles, it is a light lager with a crisp taste. Ekyu, on the other hand, is a locally made wine that is sweet and tangy.

Where to Stay in Praslin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Praslin offers a wide range of accommodations to fit every budget and preference. Here are some popular options:

  • Coco de Mer Hotel - This hotel is on the edge of a gorgeous beach with rooms overlooking the sea. Coco de Mer Hotel also has a few good restaurants serving local and international cuisine.
  • Palm Beach Hotel and Spa - This hotel features beautiful rooms with sea or garden views. It also has an outdoor pool, international restaurant and a luxurious spa that offers many treatments.
  • Villa Anse La Blague - This beautiful villa is located in a quiet area in Praslin that is 5km north of Baie St. Anne. It has a beautiful garden with a private pool where you can enjoy the beautiful scenery in peace.

What to Buy in Praslin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Praslin with you by browsing through local shops and boutiques to find souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Some great gift ideas include:

  • Coco de Mer - This iconic palm tree grows only in the Seychelles and Praslin is one of the only places where it can be found. Take a small souvenir back home in the form of a keychain, or invest in a larger piece like a carving made from the sea coconut.
  • Creole Crafts - Many local craft makers make beautiful traditional items, such as banana leaf products, woven baskets, and other decorative pieces.
  • Takamaka Rum - This rum is made in the Seychelles, is aged in oak barrels and is available in a range of flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Praslin

Praslin’s culture is a unique blend of African, European, and Asian influence. Its people are friendly and welcome tourist warmly. Seychellois Creole is an official language with French and English being commonly spoken. Dress conservatively while staying in public areas and always ask for permission before taking photographs of local people.

Best Time to Visit Praslin

The best time to visit Praslin is during the dry season from May to October, when it is less hot and humid. It is also the best time for diving and swimming as the waters are calm and clear, but it can be windy in July/August. If you want to avoid the crowds of tourists, visit during the shoulder season from April to May, and October to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Praslin

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately in public places, especially religious areas.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear a hat to avoid sunburn.
  • Plan ahead and book accommodation in advance.

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe topless or nude on the beaches, it is illegal and disrespectful.
  • Litter on the island, this includes cigarette butts and plastic. Always use designated litter bins.
  • Swim outside of designated areas, Praslin has strong sea currents.
  • Take any endangered plants or animals from the island as souvenirs.
  • Walk on the coral or touch the marine life, this could kill the coral and harm the marine life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Praslin

Generally, Praslin is a safe place for tourists. However, it’s important to take standard safety precautions such as not walking alone at night, not flashing valuables and following all safety regulations during outdoor activities.

Health in Praslin

No vaccines are required to travel into Seychelles, but it’s worth making sure that you are up to date with routine vaccinations before travelling. Tap water is safe to drink but bottled water is also readily available. Protect yourself from mosquito bites by wearing protective clothing, using insect repellent, and sleeping under mosquito nets.

Getting to Praslin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Praslin

  • Seychelles International Airport (SEZ) is located on Mahé Island, which is the closest airport to Praslin.
  • After arriving at SEZ, you can take a domestic flight to Praslin or take a ferry from Victoria Jetty.

Bus Stations Near Praslin

There are no bus stations located on Praslin, however, local buses serve most of the island’s small towns.

Other Options

  • You can hire a car or bike to explore the island or book a taxi for a more comfortable ride.
  • Several local tour companies offer tours and day trips that include transportation.

How to Get Around in Praslin

Praslin is a small island that is easy to navigate by car, bike or foot. There is no public transport system but you can hire a taxi, car or bike to move easily around the island. Local tours also offer transport as part of their tour package.

Where to Go Next from Praslin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

With Seychelles being home to over a hundred islands, there are several destinations that you can visit from Praslin. Here are some popular options:

  • La Digue - Known for its stunning beaches and unique rock formations, this island is a must-visit.
  • Mahé - The largest island in Seychelles, Mahé is home to the capital city Victoria and has a lively restaurant scene, casinos, and the famous Beau Vallon Beach.
  • Cousin Island - A wildlife reserve that is home to hundreds of species such as giant tortoises, seabirds and geckos.

Conclusion

Praslin is an island paradise that offers a truly unforgettable vacation experience. With its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventurous spirit, it is no wonder that this island attracts visitors from around the world. Whether you’re looking to relax on a stunning beach, explore lush jungles or indulge in a local cuisine, Praslin has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable journey to Seychelles.
