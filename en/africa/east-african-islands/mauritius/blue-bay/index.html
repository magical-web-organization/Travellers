Introduction to Blue Bay

With its pristine beaches, breathtaking coral reefs and turquoise lagoon, Blue Bay is a natural paradise for beach lovers and snorkeling enthusiasts. Located on the southeastern coast of Mauritius, Blue Bay is an ideal getaway for a relaxing holiday, romantic escapades or adventurous exploring. Its exquisite blend of azure skies, lush greenery, and turquoise waters create a stunning tropical haven.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Bay

Blue Bay offers a wealth of attractions that cater to different tastes and interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in Blue Bay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mahebourg Museum showcases the history and culture of the island, from the colonial period to the present day.
  • Frederik Hendrik Museum is a historical home that dates back to the French colonial era, exhibiting furniture and clothing from that period.
  • Saint Aubin House is a plantation house that showcases the lifestyle of wealthy sugar barons of the island.

Natural Attractions of Blue Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Bay Marine Park offers a glimpse into the fascinating marine ecology of the island. Explore the rich, diverse ecosystem of coral reefs, fish, and other marine creatures.
  • Ile aux Aigrettes is a nature reserve that showcases the unique flora and fauna of Mauritius. A rare opportunity to see some of the last surviving species of native flora and fauna, including the pink pigeon and the giant tortoise.
  • Lion Mountain is a natural landmark and the highest point in the Grand Port region, offering spectacular views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Bay

Blue Bay offers a range of hiking and biking routes for exploring the natural wonders of the region. Some of the favorite routes include:

  • Bras d’eau Trail offers a tranquil, forested hike that passes through rolling hills, waterfalls, and green landscapes.
  • Ferney Valley Trail takes you through forested landscapes and offers stunning views of the coast.
  • Mare Aux Vacoas Trail offers a challenging hike that goes through lush green hills, valleys, and forested paths.

Best Things to Do in Blue Bay: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling: Blue Bay Marine Park is a perfect place for snorkeling enthusiasts.
  • Glass-bottom boat tours: Explore the marine life of Blue Bay without getting wet.
  • Island-hopping: Take a boat trip to Ile aux Cerfs, an idyllic island off the coast of Mauritius.
  • Kiting: Experience the thrill of kiteboarding with the help of trained professionals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blue Bay

Day Itinerary
1 Relax on the beach and soak up the sun. Take an evening stroll along Blue Bay beach and catch a stunning sunset over the azure sea.
2 Explore the cultural sites of Mahebourg Museum and Saint Aubin House.

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Bay

Must-Try Dishes

Mauritian cuisine reflects a mix of cultural influences, including Indian, Chinese, African, and European. Some of the must-try dishes in Blue Bay include:

  • Mauritian-style seafood curry
  • Dholl puri - a popular street food consisting of a flatbread served with curried beans, chutney, and other sides.
  • Gateau patate - a dessert made with sweet potato, coconut milk, and nutmeg.
  • Boulettes - small dumplings filled with meat or seafood, often served with broth.

Local Beverages

  • Phoenix Beer - a refreshing and light beer that goes well with spicy food.
  • Rum - Mauritius is famous for its rum, which comes in a range of flavors, including vanilla, banana, and coconut.
  • Alouda - a sweet, milk-based drink flavored with agar jelly, tapioca pearls, and syrup.

Where to Stay in Blue Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blue Bay offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay in Blue Bay:

  • Shandrani Beachcomber Resort and Spa: featuring three private beaches, kids’ club, gym, and tennis court.
  • Preskil Island Resort: nestled in a tropical garden, offering water sports, a spa, and breathtaking views of the sea.
  • Chantauvent Guesthouse: an eco-friendly guesthouse that offers sea-facing rooms, breakfast, and free access to Blue Bay Marine Park.

What to Buy in Blue Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mauritius is known for its spices, textiles, and handicrafts. Some of the unique products to buy in Blue Bay include:

  • Vanilla pods
  • Curcuma (turmeric) powder
  • Handmade textiles
  • Pottery products

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Bay

Mauritians are warm and friendly people who are proud of their cultural heritage. Some local customs to keep in mind include:

  • It is customary to remove shoes when entering a home or place of worship.
  • Avoid public displays of affection, which may be viewed as inappropriate.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions and dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Blue Bay

Mauritius has a tropical climate, with warm and humid weather year-round. The best time to visit Blue Bay is from May to December, which is considered the winter season. The weather is cooler and less humid during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Bay

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Stay hydrated

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Swim when the sea is rough
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Ignore safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blue Bay

Mauritius is considered a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always wise to take safety precautions, such as:

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.
  • Be cautious when swimming, as the sea can be rough and unpredictable.

Health in Blue Bay

Mauritius has a good healthcare system, and medical facilities are available in major towns and cities. However, it is recommended that travelers get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B, and typhoid fever before visiting Blue Bay.

Getting to Blue Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blue Bay

The closest airport to Blue Bay is Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport, which is located 9 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Blue Bay

The closest bus station to Blue Bay is Mahebourg Bus Station, which is situated in the town center and offers connections to other parts of the island.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are readily available in Blue Bay, offering travelers the convenience and flexibility to explore the region at their own pace.

How To Get Around in Blue Bay

Blue Bay is a small town and can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Taxis, buses, and car rentals are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Blue Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mauritius offers a wealth of attractions beyond Blue Bay, including:

  • Port Louis, the capital city of Mauritius
  • Le Morne Brabant, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Chamarel, home to a unique and colorful landform known as the Seven Colored Earth.

Conclusion

Blue Bay is a destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a laid-back atmosphere. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set to plan a memorable trip to Blue Bay, whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or both. Embrace the beauty of the region and its warm, welcoming people, and return home with memories that will last a lifetime.
