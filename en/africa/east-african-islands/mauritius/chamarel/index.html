Introduction to Chamarel

Chamarel is a small village located in the district of Rivière Noire on Mauritius Island, east of Africa. The village is known for its scenic views, natural attractions, and cultural significance, making it the perfect destination for travelers seeking a peaceful and authentic experience.

List of Places to Visit in Chamarel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Rhumerie de Chamarel: A traditional rum distillery where visitors can learn about the history of rum production in Mauritius and taste different varieties of rum.
  • The Seven Colored Earth: A geological formation famous for its unique sand dunes with seven different colors.
  • The Black River Gorges National Park: A large natural park with stunning waterfalls, endemic flora and fauna, and breathtaking views.
  • The Chamarel Waterfall: The highest waterfall in the country with a height of 100 meters.
  • The Curious Corner of Chamarel: An entertainment center with various interactive exhibits and mind-bending illusions.

Natural Attractions of Chamarel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Chamarel Viewpoint: A scenic spot from where visitors can admire panoramic views of the village and the surrounding mountains.
  • The Tamarin Bay: A beach known for its crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • The Le Morne Brabant Mountain: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with a rich history and stunning views.
  • The Black River Gorges Waterfall: A hidden waterfall hidden in the Black River Gorges National Park.
  • The Casela World of Adventures: A nature park with various activities, including zip-lining, and animal encounters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chamarel

Chamarel is perfect for hikers and bikers, with several adrenaline-pumping routes to explore, including:

  • The Le Morne Coastal Trail: A hiking trail that leads to Le Morne Brabant, with views of the ocean and surrounding mountains.
  • The Black River Peak Trail: A challenging and demanding hiking route that leads to the highest peak of Mauritius.
  • The Tamarin Falls Trail: A scenic hiking trail that leads to Tamarin Falls.

Best Things to Do in Chamarel: Activities and Tours

Chamarel offers a range of activities for all ages, including:

  • Catamaran Cruise: A relaxing cruise around the coast of Mauritius, with opportunities for snorkeling and swimming.
  • Quad Biking Safari: A thrilling way to explore the village and its surroundings.
  • Scuba Diving: An opportunity to explore the underwater world of Mauritius, with various dive sites for all levels.
  • Skydiving: An adrenaline-pumping experience with stunning aerial views of the island.
  • Horseback Riding: An authentic way to explore the village and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chamarel

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Visit Rhumerie de Chamarel. Day 2: Hike to Le Morne Brabant Mountain. Day 3: Scuba diving in Tamarin Bay. Day 4: Visit the Seven Colored Earth and Chamarel Waterfall. Day 5: Horseback riding around the village and surroundings. Day 6: Catamaran cruise around the coast of Mauritius. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Chamarel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dholl Puri: A traditional street food made with lentils, served with different accompaniments.
  • Boulettes: A Mauritian delicacy with Chinese influence, made with fish, meat, or vegetables.
  • Fresh seafood: Mauritius Island is known for its fresh seafood, and Chamarel does not disappoint.

Local Beverages

  • Rum: Chamarel is famous for its rum production, with several distilleries offering tours and tastings.
  • Phoenix Beer: A local beer that pairs well with seafood and sunset views.
  • Alouda: A refreshing drink made with milk, basil seeds, and vanilla.

Where to Stay in Chamarel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chamarel offers various accommodation options, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and cozy guesthouses, including:

  • Lakaz Chamarel: A luxury eco-lodge surrounded by lush nature, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • La Vieille Cheminee: A traditional Creole cottage with cozy rooms and authentic cuisine.
  • Vanilla House: An affordable and cozy guesthouse with a beautiful garden setting.

What to Buy in Chamarel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rum: A bottle of locally produced rum makes for an excellent souvenir for friends and family back home.
  • Traditional textiles: Mauritius Island is known for its textiles, including colorful sarongs, dresses, and shirts.
  • Handicrafts: Chamarel offers a range of authentic handicrafts, including wood carving and basket weaving.

Local Culture and Customs of Chamarel

Mauritian culture is a unique blend of African, Indian, and European influences, reflecting the island’s long history of colonialism and migration. The locals are friendly and proud of their cultural heritage and always welcome travelers who respect their values and customs.

Best Time to Visit Chamarel

The best time to visit Chamarel is between May and December, when the weather is dry and mild, with less humidity and rainfall. The village can be visited all year round but avoid visiting between January and March when the weather is hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chamarel

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take care of the natural environment.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities.
  • Disrespect the locals and cultural traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Swim outside designated areas on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chamarel

Mauritius Island is considered safe for travelers, and crime rates are low compared to neighboring countries. It is advisable to take precautions against petty theft, such as not carrying valuable items in plain sight and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Chamarel

Mauritius Island has good medical facilities and services, but travelers should take precautions against mosquitos and other insects, as they can transmit diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It is advisable to consult a travel doctor before visiting Chamarel to get the appropriate vaccinations and medications.

Getting to Chamarel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chamarel

The nearest airport to Chamarel is Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport, located approximately 50 kilometers away. Taxis and rental cars are available at the airport, and pre-booking is advisable.

Bus Stations Near Chamarel

The nearest bus stations to Chamarel are located in nearby villages, including Black River and Petit Gabriel. Buses are a cheap and convenient way to travel around Mauritius Island, with several daily departures to major destinations.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available in Chamarel, but it is advisable to pre-book to avoid any inconvenience or overcharging. Additionally, several tour operators offer guided tours and transportation services to Chamarel and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Chamarel

Chamarel’s small size makes it easy to explore on foot or bicycle, but a rental car or taxi is recommended for more extended trips and excursions. Additionally, several tour operators offer guided tours of the village and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Chamarel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mauritius Island offers several other tourist destinations and attractions, including:

  • Port Louis: The capital city of Mauritius, known for its vibrant street life, historical landmarks, and traditional markets.
  • Flic en Flac: A popular beach destination with white sand beaches, clear waters, and sunset views.
  • Grand Baie: A bustling town with several shopping and dining options, nightlife, and water sports.

Conclusion

Chamarel is a hidden gem on Mauritius Island, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and authentic experiences. With its stunning landscapes, adventurous activities, and welcoming locals, Chamarel is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a peaceful and unforgettable getaway.
