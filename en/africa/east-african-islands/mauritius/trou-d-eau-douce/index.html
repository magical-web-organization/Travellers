Introduction to Trou d’Eau Douce

Trou d’Eau Douce is a beautiful coastal village located in the east of Mauritius island. The name translates to “sweet water hole”, referring to the freshwater reservoir that feeds into the sea. The village is a popular destination for tourists looking to relax on pristine beaches and enjoy outdoor activities. This travel guide will provide a comprehensive overview of Trou d’Eau Douce, including the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore.

List of Places to Visit in Trou d’Eau Douce

When visiting Trou d’Eau Douce, there are plenty of places to visit that offer a diverse range of experiences. From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures, here are the must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lighthouse The historic lighthouse is an impressive structure that offers stunning views of the surrounding area. Visitors can climb to the top and take in the breathtaking views of the coast and the sea.
  • Cathedral de l’Immaculee Conception This beautiful cathedral is located in the heart of the village and is a popular spot for visitors to take pictures. The architecture and design of the cathedral are awe-inspiring.
  • Domaine de l’Etoile This historical estate offers visitors an opportunity to explore the history and culture of the island. Visitors can walk through the museum and view artifacts and exhibits that detail the history and culture of Mauritius.

Natural Attractions of Trou d’Eau Douce: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ile aux Cerfs This beautiful island is located just off the coast of Trou d’Eau Douce and is a popular spot for tourists. Visitors can take a boat to the island and spend the day exploring the beaches and natural scenery.
  • Grand River South East Waterfall This stunning waterfall is located in the southeast of Mauritius and is a popular spot for visitors to take a dip in the refreshing waters. Visitors can also take in the breathtaking views of the waterfall and the surrounding landscape.
  • Blue Bay Marine Park This marine park is home to a diverse range of marine life, including coral reefs, fish, and sea turtles. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the park and enjoy snorkeling or diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trou d’Eau Douce

Exploring the coastline and interior of Trou d’Eau Douce by foot or bike is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the island. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Ile aux Cerfs Trail This trail takes visitors through the interior of the island and offers stunning views of the landscape and coastline. The trail is accessible by foot or bike and is a popular spot for nature lovers.
  • The Three-Headed Trail This hiking trail offers visitors the opportunity to explore the lush vegetation and natural wonders of the area. Visitors can take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and enjoy the peace and solitude of the trail.
  • Coastal Bike Trail This scenic coastal trail takes visitors along the coast and offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can stop along the way to explore the beaches and natural attractions of the area.

Best Things to Do in Trou d’Eau Douce: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours available for visitors to Trou d’Eau Douce. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Boat Tour Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the coastline and nearby islands. The tours offer visitors the opportunity to see marine life, enjoy the beaches, and soak in the stunning scenery.
  • Water Sports Trou d’Eau Douce is a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of water sports, including snorkeling, diving, kite surfing, and windsurfing.
  • Golf The area is home to the renowned 18-hole golf course, designed by golf legend Bernard Langer.

Food and Drink Scene of Trou d’Eau Douce

The food and drink scene in Trou d’Eau Douce is a fusion of African, Indian, and Chinese cuisines. Visitors can savor a wide range of dishes and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Curry Mauritius is famous for its spicy curries, which are made with a variety of meats or seafood and served with rice or bread.
  • Daube This slow-cooked beef stew is flavored with spices and red wine and served with mashed potatoes.
  • Gateau Piment These deep-fried lentil fritters are a popular snack in Mauritius and often served with chutney.

Local Beverages

  • Rum Mauritius is known for producing some of the best rum in the world. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of local rum varieties, including dark, spiced, and white.
  • Phoenix Beer This popular beer is made in Mauritius and can be found in most restaurants and bars.
  • Alouda This sweet milk-based drink is flavored with rose syrup and topped with basil seeds.

Where to Stay in Trou d’Eau Douce: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options available in Trou d’Eau Douce, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Shangri-La’s Le Touessrok Resort & Spa This 5-star resort is located on its own private island, just off the coast of Trou d’Eau Douce. The resort offers luxury accommodation, dining, and amenities.
  • Bubble Lodge This unique accommodation option offers visitors the opportunity to stay in transparent domes in the heart of nature.
  • Chez Tino This budget-friendly guesthouse is located in the village and offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Trou d’Eau Douce: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Trou d’Eau Douce

Mauritian culture is a unique fusion of African, Indian, and Chinese traditions. Visitors to Trou d’Eau Douce can experience the local culture by trying the food, listening to the music, and participating in local festivals and events. It’s important to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and removing shoes before entering a home or temple.

Best Time to Visit Trou d’Eau Douce

The best time to visit Trou d’Eau Douce is during the dry season, from May to December. During this time, visitors can enjoy sunny weather and comfortable temperatures. The wet season, from January to April, can be quite humid and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trou d’Eau Douce

Visitors should keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts when visiting Trou d’Eau Douce:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the environment by littering or damaging natural attractions.
  • Take unauthorized photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drugs or prostitution.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Trou d’Eau Douce should keep in mind the following safety and health tips:

Safety in Trou d’Eau Douce

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure.
  • Swim only in designated areas and follow beach safety guidelines.
  • Be cautious when engaging in water sports, such as diving or kite surfing.

Health in Trou d’Eau Douce

  • It’s important to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and the risk of mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and malaria.
  • Visitors should drink bottled water or filtered water.
  • Take care when trying spicy or unfamiliar foods to avoid stomach issues.

Getting to Trou d’Eau Douce: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Trou d’Eau Douce can arrive by air, sea, or land. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Trou d’Eau Douce

  • Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport This is the main international airport in Mauritius and is located approximately 45 minutes from Trou d’Eau Douce.

Bus Stations Near Trou d’Eau Douce

  • Trou d’Eau Douce Bus Station This station is located in the center of the village and offers connections to other parts of the island.

Other Options

  • Taxis Visitors can hire a taxi to take them from the airport to Trou d’Eau Douce. Taxis are also available for local transport and tours.
  • Car Rental Visitors can rent a car to explore the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Trou d’Eau Douce

Visitors can get around Trou d’Eau Douce by foot, bike, taxi, or car rental. Biking and walking are great ways to explore the village and its surroundings, while taxis are a convenient option for longer journeys. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the island at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Trou d’Eau Douce: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Trou d’Eau Douce can explore other nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Grand Port This historical town is located nearby and is home to several museums and cultural attractions.
  • Mahebourg This coastal town is located nearby and offers visitors the opportunity to explore local markets, museums, and natural attractions.
  • Port Louis The bustling capital of Mauritius is located on the other side of the island and is a great destination for shopping, dining, and cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Trou d’Eau Douce is a beautiful destination that offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty and culture of the east coast of Mauritius. Whether you’re looking for relaxation, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, Trou d’Eau Douce has something for everyone.
