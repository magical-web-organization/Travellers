Introduction to Curepipe

Curepipe is a charming town located in the heart of Mauritius, which is one of the East African Islands, in Africa. The town has a rich cultural heritage and a myriad of natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking adventure and relaxation. Curepipe is known for its cool and misty climate, so be sure to pack a light jacket.

List of Places to Visit in Curepipe

Here are some must-visit places in Curepipe that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Domaine des Aubineaux: It is a stunning colonial mansion that was built in 1872. You can take a guided tour of the house and the beautiful gardens surrounding it.

  • Mauritius Glass Gallery: It is a museum and a workshop that showcases the art of glassblowing. You can take a tour and watch the skilled artisans create unique glassware.

  • St. Therese Church: It is a beautiful Catholic church that was built in the 19th century. The church has intricate stained-glass windows and a peaceful atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Curepipe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Trou aux Cerfs: It is a dormant volcano that offers panoramic views of the town and the surrounding landscape. You can hike to the top of the volcano and enjoy the breathtaking vista.

  • Black River Gorges National Park: It is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. You can hike, bike, or go birdwatching in the park.

  • Le Petrin Information Centre: It is a nature reserve that has a network of hiking trails. You can see rare bird species, lizards, and other animals while hiking in the reserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Curepipe

Curepipe has some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Mauritius. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Crest Trail: It is a challenging 10-km hike that takes you through the Black River Gorges National Park. You can see stunning waterfalls and panoramic views of the valley.

  • The Ferney Trail: It is a 12-km circular route that starts and ends at the Ferney Nature Reserve. You can see rare birds, giant tortoises, and other wildlife on this trail.

  • The Forest Playground Trail: It is a 5-km bike trail that takes you through a lush forest. You can see beautiful waterfalls, exotic birds, and other animals on this trail.

Best Things to Do in Curepipe: Activities and Tours

Curepipe has plenty of activities and tours that cater to various interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the Chamarel Seven Coloured Earth: This stunning geological formation has sand dunes with seven different colors.

  • Take a guided tour of the rum distilleries: Mauritius is known for its rum, and you can learn about the rum-making process and taste some of the best rum in the country.

  • Attend the weekly market: The weekly market in Curepipe is a bustling affair where you can buy fresh fruits, vegetables, and local handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Curepipe

Curepipe has a vibrant food and drink scene that you can’t miss. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gateau Piment: It is a spicy snack made with lentils and peppers.

  • Octopus Curry: It is a popular Mauritian dish made with fresh octopus, spices, and coconut milk.

  • Dholl Puri: It is a flatbread made with yellow split peas and served with various curries.

Local Beverages

  • Alouda: It is a refreshing drink made with milk and basil seeds.

  • Phoenix Beer: It is a pale lager that is popular in Mauritius.

  • Rum: Mauritius is known for its rum, and you should try some of the best locally made rums.

Where to Stay in Curepipe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Curepipe has a range of accommodation options to suit various budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  • Villa Le Tamarinier: It is a luxury villa that has a beautiful garden and a pool.

  • Gîte des Vacoas: It is a guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a communal kitchen.

  • La Vallee de Couleurs Nature Park and Lodge: It is a lodge that is located in a nature reserve and offers stunning views of the landscape.

What to Buy in Curepipe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should buy in Curepipe:

  • Handwoven Baskets: These baskets are made with natural fibers and come in various shapes and sizes.

  • Local Spices: Mauritius is known for its unique blend of spices, which you can buy at the local markets.

  • Handmade Jewelry: You can buy beautiful handmade jewelry made with local materials like shells and beads.

Local Culture and Customs of Curepipe

Curepipe has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and you should respect their customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Appropriately: Mauritians dress conservatively, and you should respect their culture by dressing modestly.

  • Show Respect to Elders: In Mauritian culture, showing respect to elders is crucial, so be polite and courteous when interacting with elderly people.

  • Learn Some Basic French and Creole: French and Creole are the languages spoken in Mauritius. You should learn some basic phrases to communicate with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Curepipe

The best time to visit Curepipe is between May and December when the weather is mild and dry. The peak season is from November to April when the weather is warm and humid. However, be prepared for occasional rain showers during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Curepipe

Here are some do’s and don’ts of Curepipe that you should keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do carry cash as some local markets may not accept credit cards.

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

  • Do try different types of food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.

  • Don’t touch the corals while snorkeling or diving as they are fragile.

  • Don’t take pictures of the locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

While Curepipe is generally safe, it is always wise to take some precautions:

Safety in Curepipe

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night, especially if you are traveling alone.

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables while sightseeing.

  • Beware of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Curepipe

  • Drink only bottled water.

  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

  • Seek medical attention immediately in case of an emergency.

Getting to Curepipe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Curepipe is easily accessible by air, bus, or taxi. Here are some options:

Airports Near Curepipe

  • Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (MRU): It is the main gateway to Mauritius and is located about 45 km from Curepipe.

  • Sir Charles Gaëtan Duval Airport (RRG): It is a domestic airport that serves flights to and from Rodrigues Island.

Bus Stations Near Curepipe

  • The Curepipe Bus Station is the main hub for connecting bus services to other parts of Mauritius.

  • The Rose Hill Bus Station is located near Curepipe and offers frequent services to the capital city, Port Louis.

Other Options

  • Taxis and car rentals are readily available in Curepipe.

  • Motorcycle rentals are also available for adventure seekers.

How to Get Around in Curepipe

Curepipe is a small town, and you can explore it on foot. However, there are other options for getting around:

  • Buses are a cheap and convenient way to get around the town and the surrounding areas.

  • Taxis are readily available, but they can be expensive.

  • Car and motorcycle rentals are available for those who want to explore the town at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Curepipe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Curepipe:

  • Grand Bassin: It is a sacred lake that is considered holy by Hindus in Mauritius.

  • Le Morne: It is a peninsula that offers stunning beaches and water sports.

  • Port Louis: It is the capital city of Mauritius and offers a glimpse into the country’s colonial past.

Conclusion

Curepipe is a unique destination that offers a blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your time in this lovely town. Enjoy your trip to Curepipe!
