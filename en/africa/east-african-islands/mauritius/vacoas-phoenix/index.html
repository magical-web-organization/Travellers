Introduction to Vacoas-Phoenix

Vacoas-Phoenix is a scenic town located in the Plaines Wilhems district in the central part of Mauritius. Despite being one of the smallest towns in Mauritius, Vacoas-Phoenix is home to some of the island’s most beautiful natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and adventurous activities. In this travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know before planning your trip to Vacoas-Phoenix, from the best things to do, places to stay, and where to eat to hiking routes, nearby excursions, and cultural customs.

List of Places to Visit in Vacoas-Phoenix

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Quatre Bornes Historical and Heritage Museum

Located in Quatre Bornes, just a few miles from Vacoas-Phoenix, the Quatre Bornes Historical and Heritage Museum is an excellent place to learn about the island’s history, art, and culture. The museum showcases a wide range of artifacts, photographs, and documents that date back to the early days of the island’s colonization.

Phoenix Cultural Centre

The Phoenix Cultural Centre is a multipurpose cultural center located in Phoenix, Mauritius. The center hosts a variety of exhibitions, concerts, cultural events, and festivals throughout the year, providing visitors with a glimpse into the rich and diverse cultural heritage of Mauritius.

Natural Attractions of Vacoas-Phoenix: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trou aux Cerfs

Trou aux Cerfs is an extinct volcanic crater located in Curepipe, a few minutes’ drive from Vacoas-Phoenix. The crater is one of the most visited natural attractions in Mauritius, and for good reason—the panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and the island’s central plateau are simply breathtaking.

Tamarind Falls

Tamarind Falls, also known as 7 Cascades, is a series of seven waterfalls located in the nearby town of Henrietta. The falls are considered one of the island’s most beautiful natural spots, and visitors can embark on a hiking tour to explore the surrounding rainforest and freshwater pools.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vacoas-Phoenix

Black River Peak Trail

The Black River Peak Trail is a challenging hiking trail that takes visitors through the lush rainforest of the Black River Gorges National Park, the largest national park in Mauritius. The trail offers stunning views of the island’s western coast and is home to a variety of endemic bird and plant species.

Montagne Ory Trail

The Montagne Ory Trail is a relatively easy hiking trail that starts from the town of Vacoas-Phoenix and leads to the top of Montagne Ory, one of the highest peaks in the region. The trail offers panoramic views of the island’s central plateau and is a popular spot for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Vacoas-Phoenix: Activities and Tours

Mauritius Glass Gallery

The Mauritius Glass Gallery is a unique art gallery located in Phoenix, Mauritius, showcasing the work of internationally renowned glass artist, Ode la Forge. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the gallery, watch live glassblowing demonstrations, and even take part in a glassblowing workshop.

Horse Riding Tours

Horse riding tours offer a unique way to explore the island’s natural beauty, offering visitors stunning views of tropical forests, mountains, and beaches. There are many horse riding tours available in Vacoas-Phoenix and the surrounding regions, catering to riders of all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Vacoas-Phoenix

Must-Try Dishes

Dholl Puri

Dholl Puri is a popular Mauritian street food made from a thin, flatbread stuffed with yellow lentils, curry sauce, and chutney. It is considered a staple food in Mauritius and can be found in many local restaurants and street vendors.

Fish Vindaye

Fish Vindaye is a traditional Mauritian dish made with fried fish, mustard seeds, turmeric, and vinegar. It is typically served with rice and pickles and is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

Phoenix Beer

Phoenix Beer is a local beer brand that has been brewed in Mauritius since 1963. The beer is light, refreshing, and pairs well with spicy Mauritian cuisine.

Alouda

Alouda is a sweet, creamy drink made with milk, basil seeds, agar-agar, and vanilla. It is typically served cold and is a popular drink to enjoy on a hot day in Mauritius.

Where to Stay in Vacoas-Phoenix: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Le Domaine des Aubineaux

Le Domaine des Aubineaux is a colonial-style guesthouse located in Curepipe, just a few miles from Vacoas-Phoenix. The guesthouse combines French elegance with Mauritian charm, offering visitors a comfortable stay and easy access to nearby natural attractions and cultural landmarks.

Casa Florida Hotel and Spa

Casa Florida Hotel and Spa is a relaxing retreat located in Ebene City, just a short drive from Vacoas-Phoenix. The hotel features a spa, outdoor pool, and restaurant, and offers visitors easy access to nearby shopping and entertainment venues.

What to Buy in Vacoas-Phoenix: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mauritian Rum

Mauritian rum is one of the island’s most popular exports, and visitors can pick up a bottle at one of the many local distilleries or souvenir shops. The rum comes in a variety of flavors, from coconut and vanilla to cinnamon and coffee.

Ship Models

Ship models are a traditional Mauritian craft and make for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home. Visitors can find ship models in various sizes and styles, ranging from simple sailboats to intricate replicas of famous historical vessels.

Local Culture and Customs of Vacoas-Phoenix

Mauritius is a culturally diverse island, and visitors should take the time to learn about local customs and traditions before they arrive. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting cultural landmarks, remove their shoes before entering someone’s home, and ask for permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Vacoas-Phoenix

The best time to visit Vacoas-Phoenix is from May to December, when the weather is dry, sunny, and comfortable. January to April can be rainy, and the island is more crowded during the holiday season in December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vacoas-Phoenix

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Dholl Puri, Fish Vindaye, Phoenix Beer, and Alouda.
  • Take the time to learn about local culture and customs.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing when exploring nature trails and hiking routes.
  • Be respectful of local traditions and customs.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural landmarks or religious sites.
  • Litter or damage natural attractions and wildlife habitats.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Be loud and disrespectful in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vacoas-Phoenix

Vacoas-Phoenix is a safe town for visitors, but like any tourist destination, visitors should take precautions to avoid becoming a victim of petty theft or scams. Visitors should keep their valuables in a safe place and be wary of strangers who approach them with unsolicited offers or deals.

Health in Vacoas-Phoenix

There are no major health risks in Vacoas-Phoenix, but visitors should take precautions to avoid tropical diseases such as dengue fever and chikungunya. Visitors should also be sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Vacoas-Phoenix: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vacoas-Phoenix

The Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport is the main airport in Mauritius, and visitors can easily reach Vacoas-Phoenix by taxi or bus from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Vacoas-Phoenix

The Rose Hill Bus Station is the main bus station in the region and offers frequent connections to nearby towns and cities, including Vacoas-Phoenix.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to explore the island at their own pace, but should be prepared to navigate narrow roads and heavy traffic.

How to Get Around in Vacoas-Phoenix

The easiest way to get around Vacoas-Phoenix is by bus or taxi. Buses are inexpensive and run frequently, while taxis are more expensive but offer more flexibility and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Vacoas-Phoenix: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Port Louis

Port Louis is the capital city of Mauritius and is located just a short drive from Vacoas-Phoenix. The town is home to many cultural landmarks and historic sites, including the Aapravasi Ghat and the Caudan Waterfront.

Grand Baie

Grand Baie is a popular tourist destination located in the northern part of Mauritius, known for its beautiful beaches, shopping, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Vacoas-Phoenix is a hidden gem in Mauritius, offering visitors a perfect combination of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and fun-filled activities. With our comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their perfect Mauritius vacation and discover everything this charming town has to offer.
