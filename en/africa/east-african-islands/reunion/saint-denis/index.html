Introduction to Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is the capital city of Réunion, a French overseas department located in the Indian Ocean. The city is known for its stunning architecture, rich history, and vibrant culture. As the largest city in Réunion, Saint-Denis offers plenty of opportunities for travelers to explore and immerse themselves in the local way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Denis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Le Barachois - a historical site featuring the first landing place of French explorers in Réunion
  • La Maison de La Vanille - a museum dedicated to the history and production of vanilla in Réunion
  • Le Musée Léon Dierx - a fine arts museum featuring paintings and sculptures from around the world
  • La Roche Écrite - a geological site with breathtaking views of the city and sea

Natural Attractions of Saint-Denis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Le Jardin de l’État - a botanical garden featuring over 12,000 plant species including palm trees, orchids, and fruit trees
  • Le Piton de la Fournaise - one of the most active volcanoes in the world, offering an adventurous trek for hikers
  • La Cascade Niagara - a breathtaking waterfall located in the heart of Saint-Denis
  • Le Trou de Fer - a stunning canyon featuring waterfalls and wildlife, perfect for nature enthusiasts

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that cater to beginners and experienced adventurers.

  • Le Sentier de La Roche Écrite - a challenging 7-mile hike that provides unparalleled views of the city and sea
  • La Route des Tamarins - an easy 12-mile bike ride along the west coast of Saint-Denis
  • Le Sentier de la Chapelle - a moderate 4-mile hike that leads to a historic chapel and stunning views of the city

Best Things to Do in Saint-Denis: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the city’s colonial architecture - take a walking tour of Saint-Denis and discover the city’s rich history and culture
  • Experience the local cuisine - indulge in traditional Creole dishes such as curry, rougail saucisse, and samosas
  • Visit the local markets - explore the vibrant markets of Saint-Denis to purchase local produce, spices, and handicrafts
  • Take a helicopter tour - experience the city from a different perspective with an exhilarating helicopter tour

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Denis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Curry - a popular dish in Réunion made with meat, seafood, or vegetables served with rice and beans
  • Rougail Saucisse - a spicy dish made with sausages, tomatoes, onions, and spices
  • Samosas - a savory pastry filled with vegetables or meat

Local Beverages

  • Rhum arrangé - a locally-made rum infused with fruits, spices, and herbs
  • Dodo Beer - a light and refreshing beer made in Réunion

Where to Stay in Saint-Denis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Denis offers a range of accommodation options for travelers.

  • Le Juliette Dodu - a historic hotel located in the heart of Saint-Denis
  • La Villa Angelique - a luxurious guesthouse offering stunning views of the city
  • Le Central Hotel - a modern hotel located in the city center

What to Buy in Saint-Denis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vanilla - Réunion is known for its high-quality vanilla, which can be found in markets throughout the city
  • Rhum arrangé - a unique and flavorful souvenir that can be enjoyed back home
  • Handicrafts - Réunion is known for its vibrant handicrafts, including basketry, embroidery, and wood carving

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is a melting pot of cultures and traditions. The city’s cultural diversity is reflected in its architecture, food, music, and art. It is important for travelers to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Denis

The best time to visit Saint-Denis is between May and October, when the weather is dry and sunny. The city can be visited throughout the year, but be aware that the rainy season is from December to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Denis

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and drink
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Swim in the sea without local guidance

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Denis

Saint-Denis is a safe city, but travelers should exercise caution in crowded areas and be aware of their belongings. It is also important to follow local safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Saint-Denis

Travelers should visit their doctor before traveling to Réunion to ensure they are up-to-date with their vaccinations. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue and Zika are present in the region, so it is important to take precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Saint-Denis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Denis

Roland Garros Airport is located just outside of Saint-Denis and is the main airport serving the region. There are multiple flights a day from Paris and other major cities within France.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Denis

The city’s main bus station is located in the city center and provides regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available in Saint-Denis for those who wish to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Saint-Denis

Walking is the best way to explore the city center of Saint-Denis, and public transportation is available for trips outside of the city. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who wish to explore the surrounding areas at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Denis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Réunion is a stunning island with plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Some popular options include:

  • Cirque de Salazie - a stunning natural amphitheater located just a short distance from Saint-Denis
  • Saint-Pierre - a beautiful beach town located on the west coast of Réunion
  • Cilaos - a stunning mountain town perfect for hiking and outdoor adventures

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and vibrant local culture, Saint-Denis is an incredible destination for travelers seeking an unforgettable adventure. Use our comprehensive travel guide to plan the perfect trip and experience all that this incredible city has to offer.
