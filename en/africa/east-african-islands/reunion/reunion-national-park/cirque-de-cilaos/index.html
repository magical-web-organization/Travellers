Introduction to Cirque de Cilaos

Nestled in the heart of Réunion National Park, Cirque de Cilaos boasts some of the most spectacular natural scenery in the world. The landscape is unique, with towering cliffs, deep ravines, and winding roads leading to picturesque towns and villages. Here’s your ultimate guide to exploring Cirque de Cilaos.

List of Places to Visit in Cirque de Cilaos

From historical landmarks to outdoor adventures, Cirque de Cilaos has plenty of destinations to explore. Here’s a list of must-see attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cilaos Church: A beautiful and historic church constructed in the early 1900s.
  • Maison de la Broderie: A museum dedicated to the art of embroidery.
  • Tapcal, a powerful waterfall said to have healing properties.

Natural Attractions of Cirque de Cilaos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Piton des Neiges: The highest point on the island, and an excellent location for hiking.
  • Le Bras de Cilaos Trail: A challenging hike that takes you through the heart of Cirque de Cilaos.
  • La Chapelle Trail: A relatively easy walk that offers stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cirque de Cilaos

Cirque de Cilaos is a hiker’s paradise, with trails ranging from easy to expert. Biking is also popular, with miles of roads that will take you through breathtaking scenery. Here are some routes to try:

  • Trail to Piton des Neiges: An expert-level hike up Réunion’s highest peak.
  • La Chapelle Trail: A moderately challenging walk through lush forests and stunning vistas.
  • Piton de Sucre: A beginner-friendly hike with incredible views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Cirque de Cilaos: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Cirque de Cilaos has something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Relax and unwind at one of the many hot springs and spas in the area.
  • Explore the vibrant arts and crafts scene in Cilaos and surrounding areas.
  • Take a scenic helicopter tour of the island to experience Cirque de Cilaos from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cirque de Cilaos

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Cirque de Cilaos, here’s an itinerary to help you maximize your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Cilaos and explore the town, including the Maison de la Broderie museum.
Day 2 Hike the La Chapelle Trail in the morning, followed by an afternoon soak in the Cilaos hot springs.
Day 3 Rent a mountain bike and explore the surrounding trails.
Day 4 Take a scenic drive around the Cirque de Cilaos and visit the Tapcal waterfall.
Day 5 Tour the island by helicopter for a different perspective on the stunning scenery.
Day 6 Hike the Piton des Neiges trail, starting early in the morning to catch the sunrise.
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and enjoy a final soak in the hot springs before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Cirque de Cilaos

Réunion cuisine is a mix of African, French, Indian, and Chinese influences, making the food scene in Cirque de Cilaos unique and delicious. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rougail Saucisse: A spicy sausage and tomato stew, typically served with rice.
  • Cari: A curry made with chicken, pork, or fish, served with rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Rhum Arrange: A rum-based drink infused with fruit and spices.
  • Bourbon Pointu Coffee: A rare and highly prized coffee grown only on Réunion Island.

Where to Stay in Cirque de Cilaos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for lodging in and around Cirque de Cilaos, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the most highly recommended options:

  • Hotel Tsilaosa: A stunning and luxurious hotel that provides stunning mountain views.
  • La Reunion Resort: A family-friendly resort with great amenities like a pool and spa.
  • Auberge de Cilaos: A budget-friendly option with clean, basic rooms.

What to Buy in Cirque de Cilaos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Réunion is known for its beautiful embroidery and artisanal crafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Embroidered handkerchiefs or tablecloths from Maison de la Broderie.
  • Bourbon Pointu Coffee, which can only be grown on Réunion Island.
  • Spices and condiments, like the locally made curry powder and vanilla.

Local Culture and Customs of Cirque de Cilaos

Réunion Island has a diverse cultural heritage, which is reflected in the music, dance, and food of the region. The local Creole language is also widely spoken. When visiting Cirque de Cilaos, it’s important to be respectful of the culture and traditions of the people who call the island home.

Best Time to Visit Cirque de Cilaos

The best time to visit Cirque de Cilaos is during the dry season, which runs from May to November. This is when the weather is cooler and less humid, making it easier to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cirque de Cilaos

When visiting Cirque de Cilaos, it’s important to be mindful of local customs and etiquette. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local delicacies and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Creole phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Take photos of locals without their permission.
  • litter in the natural reserves or important historical sites

Safety and Health

When traveling to Cirque de Cilaos, it’s important to take precautions to stay safe and healthy.

Safety in Cirque de Cilaos

  • Be aware of the potential for flash floods and landslides during the rainy season.
  • Always hike with a partner and let someone know where you’re going.
  • Avoid leaving valuables in your car, as break-ins are not uncommon.

Health in Cirque de Cilaos

  • It’s important to stay hydrated and use sun protection when hiking or exploring outdoors.
  • Check with your doctor prior to travel to ensure all necessary immunizations and medications are up-to-date.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you experience symptoms of heat exhaustion or dehydration.

Getting to Cirque de Cilaos: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to Cirque de Cilaos.

Airports Near Cirque de Cilaos

  • Réunion Roland Garros Airport (RUN): Located in Saint-Denis, approximately 47 miles from Cirque de Cilaos.

Bus Stations Near Cirque de Cilaos

  • Le Port: Most buses to Cirque de Cilaos leave from here.

Other Options

  • Taxis and rental cars are both available options.

How to Get Around in Cirque de Cilaos

The best way to get around Cirque de Cilaos is by car, either by renting one or hiring a driver. Additionally, hiking and biking are popular options for exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from Cirque de Cilaos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time after exploring Cirque de Cilaos, consider visiting some of these nearby destinations:

  • Le Piton de la Fournaise: An active volcano on the east side of the island.
  • Saint-Denis: The capital city of Réunion Island, with a vibrant cultural scene and historic sites to explore.

Conclusion

Cirque de Cilaos is a stunning destination that’s well worth the trip. With incredible natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious food, it’s a must-see location for any traveler in Réunion National Park. Follow this guide to help you plan the ultimate trip to Cirque de Cilaos.
