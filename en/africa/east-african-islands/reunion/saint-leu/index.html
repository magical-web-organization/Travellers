Introduction to Saint-Leu

Saint-Leu is a picturesque coastal town located on the western coast of Réunion, a French department in East African Islands. Brimming with natural beauty, cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor activities, it’s a popular tourist destination for travelers looking for a unique tropical getaway. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and more while exploring Saint-Leu.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Leu

Being a small town, Saint-Leu is packed with picturesque places that are worth exploring. Below are some of the must-visit sites in Saint-Leu.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kelonia: Located near the lagoon in Saint-Leu, Kelonia is an interesting place that specializes in marine turtle conservation. It’s a must-visit for animal lovers and anyone interested in learning more about the conservation of these fascinating creatures.

  • Le Musée Stella Matutina: A museum dedicated to the history of Réunion and the sugarcane industry, Le Musée Stella Matutina is housed in a historic sugar factory. It boasts informative exhibits, multimedia presentations, and a delightful open-air exhibition.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Leu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maïdo: Rising over 2,000 meters, Maïdo is one of the most stunning peaks in Réunion, attracting outdoor enthusiasts from all over the world. You can indulge in breathtaking views of the coastline, lush forests, and remarkable rock formations.

  • L’Etang-Salé Forest: A verdant oasis nestled between the beach and the mountains, L’Etang-Salé Forest is a haven for nature lovers. It’s home to a range of flora and fauna, including the endemic Tuit-Tuit and Pic Vert birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Leu

Saint-Leu is a paradise for adventure enthusiasts, from hiking to mountain biking trails. Below are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the town.

  • Cilaos: A charming mountainous village, Cilaos boasts an array of hiking and biking trails. You’ll see stunning waterfalls, scenic views, and impressive canyons.

  • Piton de la Fournaise: One of the world’s most active and accessible volcanoes, Piton de la Fournaise is a must-visit for adventure enthusiasts in Saint-Leu. You can catch sights of awe-inspiring lava flows, rocky terrains, and otherworldly landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Leu: Activities and Tours

Whether it’s adrenaline-fueled activities, cultural tours, or relaxing on the beach, Saint-Leu has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Saint-Leu.

  • Horseback Riding on the Beach: You can explore Saint-Leu’s beautiful coastline on horseback with an experienced guide. It’s the perfect way to admire the stunning sunset views of the town’s beaches.

  • Paragliding over the Coastline: For an unforgettable experience, try paragliding over the stunning coastline of Saint-Leu. You’ll get breathtaking aerial views of the town’s pristine beaches, mountain ranges, and picturesque landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Leu

With so much to see and do in Saint-Leu, one could easily spend a week exploring the town and its surroundings. Here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Leu

Saint-Leu has a vibrant culinary scene, boasting traditional Réunionese cuisine that influences its flavors. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Saint-Leu.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rougail Saucisse: A signature lentil stew seasoned with sausage, onions, and tomato paste, Rougail Saucisse is a staple in Réunionese cuisine.

  • Carri Poulet: A mouthwatering chicken stew with saffron and turmeric, Carri Poulet is a classic Réunionese dish. It’s usually served with rice, beans, and other side dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Rum: Réunionese rum is a specialty of the island and is renowned worldwide for its unique flavor. You must try the different varieties of rum on your visit to Saint-Leu.

  • Dodo Beer: A light and refreshing beer brewed on the island, Dodo Beer is a favorite among locals and tourists.

Where to Stay in Saint-Leu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Leu has accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Below are some of the best places to stay in Saint-Leu.

  • Le Boucan Canot Hotel: An elegant hotel that offers luxurious accommodation with an outdoor pool and a private beach.

  • L’Orky-Mel: A charming guesthouse boasting comfortable rooms, beautiful gardens, and a warm atmosphere.

What to Buy in Saint-Leu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While in Saint-Leu, you can purchase a range of amazing souvenirs and local products. Below are some unique offerings of the town.

  • Vanilla: Réunion is known for producing some of the world’s finest vanilla. You can buy different varieties of vanilla, including Bourbon vanilla, at local markets.

  • Handmade Crafts: The craft markets in Saint-Leu offer an impressive range of locally made crafts, including pottery, masks, baskets, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Leu

Réunion is a culturally diverse island. Its people have a rich heritage influenced by numerous cultures, including French, African, and Indian. Religion is also a significant aspect of the culture on the island, with Christianity being the dominant religion, followed by Hinduism and Islam.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Leu

The best time to visit Saint-Leu is from May to December when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the town’s climate is tropical, and occasional rain showers can occur throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Leu

When visiting Saint-Leu, it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Below are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind.

Do:

  • Learn some basic French phrases to communicate with the locals.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear excessively revealing clothing in public places.

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural beauty of Saint-Leu.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are essential considerations when touring Saint-Leu. Here are some basic guidelines to keep you safe and healthy during your visit.

Safety in Saint-Leu

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially when venturing out on your own.

  • Leave valuable items in your hotel safe or secured place.

Health in Saint-Leu

  • While in Saint-Leu, stay hydrated as much as possible, especially if you’re doing outdoor activities.

  • Obtain travel insurance before your trip to Réunion to cover any healthcare needs.

Getting to Saint-Leu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saint-Leu is accessible by air and other transportation modes. Below are some options to consider when getting to the town.

Airports Near Saint-Leu

  • Roland Garros Airport: The nearest international airport to Saint-Leu is Roland Garros Airport in Saint-Denis. It’s located about 42 kilometers from Saint-Leu.

  • Pierrefonds Airport: Located 14 kilometers south of Saint-Pierre, Pierrefonds Airport serves Réunion’s southern coastline.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxi services are available from the airport to take you to your hotel in Saint-Leu.

  • Bus: Regular bus service is available to Saint-Leu from Saint-Pierre and Saint-Denis.

How to Get Around in Saint-Leu

While in Saint-Leu, you can get around using various transport modes, including taxis, buses, and rental cars. You can also rent a bike to explore the coastline and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Leu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint-Leu is an excellent hub for exploring Réunion’s western coast. Below are some of the nearby destinations and excursions to consider.

  • Saint-Gilles-Les-Baines: An attractive seaside resort on the west coast of Réunion, Saint-Gilles-Les-Baines is known for its stunning beaches, lively atmosphere, and excellent dining and shopping options.

  • Saint-Pierre: A gorgeous town located on the southern coast of the island, Saint-Pierre boasts palm-fringed beaches, historic architecture, and a bustling nightlife scene.

Conclusion

From its pristine beaches to its nature reserves and cultural attractions, Saint-Leu is a true gem in the heart of Réunion. Our comprehensive tour guide provides all the necessary information you need to explore this beautiful town fully. Whether you’re a culture buff, nature enthusiast, or just looking for an exotic tropical getaway, Saint-Leu is the place to be.
