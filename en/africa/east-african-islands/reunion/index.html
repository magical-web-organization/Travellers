Introduction to Réunion

Réunion is a volcanic island located in the Indian Ocean, specifically in the East African Islands. It is a French overseas department, which offers a unique blend of French and Creole culture. The island boasts of lush tropical forests, stunning beaches, and towering mountains offering visitors a chance to enjoy a perfect holiday. With so much to offer, Réunion is undoubtedly a haven for adventure lovers, nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and anyone looking to unwind in a stunning paradise.

Food and Drink Scene of Réunion

As a melting pot of cultures, Réunion’s cuisine is a fusion of French, African, Indian, and Chinese influences, with a Creole twist. Fresh seafood, ginger, and vanilla are some of the staple ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rougail Saucisse
  • Carry Poulet
  • La rougail boucané

Local Beverages

  • Rhum Agricole
  • Bourbon Pointu coffee

Local Culture and Customs of Réunion

Réunion’s culture is a blend of Creole, French, and Asian traditions, influenced by the island’s history and natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Réunion

The best time to visit Réunion is during the dry season (May to October). The weather is warm, and there are fewer chances of rain, making it a perfect time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Réunion

Do:

  • Respect the island’s culture
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Take part in outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Litter on the island
  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the national parks

Safety and Health

Safety in Réunion

Réunion is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas and not leaving valuables in their cars.

Health in Réunion

As a precaution against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and chikungunya, it is advisable to use mosquito repellent and cover your skin with long sleeves and pants.
