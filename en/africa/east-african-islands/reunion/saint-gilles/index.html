Introduction to Saint-Gilles

Saint-Gilles is located on the west coast of Réunion Island, an overseas territory of France situated in the Indian Ocean. As one of the most popular tourist destinations on the island, Saint-Gilles offers visitors a wide variety of activities, from hiking and biking to cultural attractions and scenic views. The town is famous for its beautiful beaches, lively nightlife, and French colonial architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Gilles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • “La Maison de la Vanille” Museum – This museum is dedicated to the history of vanilla cultivation in Réunion Island and the world. Visitors can learn about the cultivation and extraction of vanilla and sample vanilla-infused treats.
  • “La Villa Rivière” Museum – This colonial mansion was built in 1820 and now houses a museum dedicated to the history of Saint-Gilles.
  • “Temple du Colosse” – A Hindu temple located in Saint-Gilles-Les-Bains, known for its impressive statue of the goddess Shiva.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Gilles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Le Maïdo – A mountain peak in the Réunion National Park that offers breathtaking views of the western coast of Réunion Island.
  • La Saline Beach – A beautiful white sand beach with calm waters, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Kelonia – A sea turtle observatory and rehabilitation center where visitors can learn about the endangered species and their conservation efforts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Gilles

Saint-Gilles offers a variety of hiking and mountain biking trails for all levels of experience. Some popular routes include:

  • Roche Ecrite – A challenging hike that takes you to the highest point on Réunion Island with panoramic views of the east and west coasts.
  • Sentier Littoral – A coastal trail that takes you along the rugged coast of Saint-Gilles, passing through mangroves and cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Gilles: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching – Between June and September, visitors can go on a whale watching tour and spot humpback whales as they migrate through the Indian Ocean.
  • Paragliding – Take in the stunning views of Saint-Gilles from above with a paragliding tour.
  • Jet Skiing – Explore the beautiful coastline of Saint-Gilles on a jet ski tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Gilles

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rougail Saucisse – A spicy sausage dish served with rice, tomatoes, and onions.
  • Cari – A curry dish made with meat or fish and local spices, typically served with rice or lentils.
  • Palmiste – Young palm tree hearts, a delicacy in Réunion cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Rhum Arrangé – Rum infused with local fruits and spices.
  • Dodo Beer – A local beer produced on Réunion Island.

Where to Stay in Saint-Gilles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lux* Saint Gilles – A luxury beach resort with a lagoon-style pool, spa, and restaurants.
  • Les Filaos – A budget-friendly hotel located close to the beach and city center.
  • Kaz Ocean – A guesthouse with a tropical garden and outdoor pool, located in a quiet residential area.

What to Buy in Saint-Gilles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vanilla – Réunion Island is known for its high-quality vanilla, which can be bought in various forms such as extracts, beans, and powders.
  • Artisanal Crafts – Local crafts such as woven baskets, pottery, and woodcarvings make great souvenirs to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Gilles

Réunion Island has a rich cultural heritage and a diverse population that includes French, African, Indian, and Chinese communities. The island is famous for its music, dance, and cuisine, which reflect this cultural diversity. Visitors to Saint-Gilles should respect local customs, dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Gilles

The best time to visit Saint-Gilles is from May to October when the weather is cooler and drier. June to September is also the best time for whale watching tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Gilles

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Public displays of affection are frowned upon.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Gilles

Saint-Gilles is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise caution when walking alone at night and avoid walking in secluded areas.

Health in Saint-Gilles

Visitors to Réunion Island should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles-mumps-rubella (MMR), tetanus, and hepatitis A. They should also take precautions to prevent mosquito bites to reduce the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Chikungunya fever.

Getting to Saint-Gilles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Gilles

The closest airport to Saint-Gilles is Roland Garros Airport in Saint-Denis, approximately 40 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a bus or rental car to Saint-Gilles.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Gilles

Saint-Gilles has a bus station with frequent connections to other towns on Réunion Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around Saint-Gilles and Réunion Island.

How to Get Around in Saint-Gilles

Saint-Gilles is a walkable town with most attractions located within walking distance. Visitors can also take a bus or rent a car to explore the town and surrounding area further.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Gilles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saint-Paul – A historic town located 8 kilometers north of Saint-Gilles, known for its colorful market and traditional Creole architecture.
  • Cilaos – A picturesque mountain village located in the Réunion National Park, known for its hot springs and hiking trails.
  • Piton de la Fournaise – An active volcano located in the Réunion National Park, accessible by car or hiking trails.

Conclusion

Saint-Gilles is a beautiful and diverse town that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and stunning beaches. From hiking to paragliding, whale watching to museum hopping, there are plenty of activities to keep visitors busy during their stay. By following local customs and staying safe, visitors can immerse themselves in the rich culture and natural beauty of Saint-Gilles and Réunion Island.
