Introduction to Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe is a landlocked country located in Southern Africa, bordered by Zambia to the north, Mozambique to the east, Botswana to the west, and South Africa to the south. The country is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and abundant wildlife, making it a popular destination for travelers around the world. In this travel guide of Zimbabwe, we’ll take a closer look at the must-see attractions, best things to do, hiking and biking routes, local food and drink scene, where to stay, and more. Let’s begin!

Food and Drink Scene of Zimbabwe

Zimbabwean cuisine is a blend of African and colonial influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sadza - a staple maize meal served with meat, vegetables, and sauces
  • Mazondo - beef hooves and trotters slow-cooked with tomatoes and onions
  • Kapenta - small freshwater fish fried and served with vegetables
  • Peanut Butter and Spinach Stew - a flavorful vegetarian dish made with peanut butter and spinach
  • Mopani Worms - a local delicacy consisting of caterpillars cooked with onions and tomatoes

Local Beverages

  • Castle Lager - a popular local beer
  • Chibuku - a traditional sorghum beer often sold in cartons
  • Mazoe Orange Juice - a refreshing juice made from local oranges
  • Rooibos Tea - a caffeine-free herbal tea

What to Buy in Zimbabwe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zimbabwe is known for its arts and crafts, particularly stone sculptures, baskets, and wood carvings. Here are some other souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Mbira - a traditional Zimbabwean musical instrument

  • Baobab Oil - a natural oil used for skin and hair care
  • Zambezi Honey - a sweet and delicious honey produced in Zimbabwe

Local Culture and Customs of Zimbabwe

Zimbabweans are friendly and welcoming people who value their culture and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Greetings are important in Zimbabwe, so take the time to say hello and introduce yourself properly.
  • Dress modestly and avoid wearing revealing clothing in public places.
  • Respect local customs, particularly around religious and cultural festivals.
  • In rural areas, it is customary to ask permission before taking photographs of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Zimbabwe

The best time to visit Zimbabwe is from May to October when the weather is dry and sunny. The country can be very hot during October and November, so it’s best to avoid those months if you are sensitive to heat. The rainy season usually begins in November and ends in April, and during this time some roads may become impassable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zimbabwe

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Zimbabwe:

Do:

  • Try the local dishes and beverages.
  • Respect the wildlife and stay in designated areas.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid insect bites.
  • Negotiate prices when shopping at markets.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without permission.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Ignore wildlife warnings or safety rules.
  • Hesitate to ask for help or advice if you need it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe is generally a safe country for travelers. However, it’s important to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure, avoiding isolated areas at night, and being aware of your surroundings. It’s also recommended to avoid political demonstrations and public gatherings.

Health in Zimbabwe

Travelers to Zimbabwe should take appropriate health precautions. It’s recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, yellow fever, and influenza. Malaria is common in Zimbabwe, so it’s important to take anti-malaria medication and use insect repellent when necessary. HIV/AIDS is also prevalent in the country, so it’s important to practice safe sex and avoid sharing needles.

How to Get Around in Zimbabwe

Travelers in Zimbabwe can get around by car, bus, or taxi. It’s recommended to use licensed taxis or car services, as some unlicensed operators may not be reliable or safe. Tour operators also offer guided tours and transportation to popular attractions and destinations.
