Introduction to Kwekwe

Nestled in southeastern Zimbabwe, Kwekwe is a city endowed with both cultural and natural attractions. The city owes its existence to gold mining and traces its history to the early 1900s, when the first gold mines were established here. Today, Kwekwe is a bustling city that attracts thousands of tourists every year. Whether you are interested in its rich cultural heritage or want to explore its natural beauty, Kwekwe has a lot to offer. This travel guide aims to help you plan your trip to Kwekwe by covering everything from places to visit, food and drink scene, accommodation, safety, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Kwekwe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kwekwe is steeped in history and culture, and there is no shortage of museums and historical places to visit here. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • The National Mining Museum. Located at the heart of the city, the National Mining Museum is an excellent place to learn about the city’s mining history. The museum houses a vast collection of mining artifacts, including machinery, tools, and equipment, as well as an array of geological specimens.

  • The Kwekwe Nature Sanctuary. If you love nature, then a visit to the Kwekwe Nature Sanctuary is a must-do. The sanctuary is home to several endangered species, including the black rhinoceros, and offers a fantastic opportunity to get up close with the wildlife.

  • The Iron Smelting Museum. The Iron Smelting Museum is another place that should be on your itinerary when visiting Kwekwe. The museum is situated at the ancient iron-smelting site known as “Lalapanzi,” and you can see how iron was made in ancient times.

Natural Attractions of Kwekwe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who enjoy the great outdoors, Kwekwe has plenty of natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • The Zinyama Cave. The Zinyama Cave is an excellent place to explore if you’re into caving. The cave system is vast, with more than four kilometers of underground passages to explore.

  • The Driefontein Recreational Park. The Driefontein Recreational Park is located on the outskirts of Kwekwe. It’s an excellent place to picnic, relax, or take a hike. The park also has several walking trails, and you can expect to see a variety of wildlife as you explore the park.

  • The Zhombe Mountains. If you’re up for a challenge, then hiking the Zhombe Mountains should be on your list of things to do in Kwekwe. The mountains offer some of the best hiking experiences in Zimbabwe, and you can expect to see stunning views of the surrounding countryside from the summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kwekwe

Kwekwe is a city that caters to outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re into hiking or mountain biking, there are several trails and routes to explore in and around the city. Some of the best hiking and biking destinations in Kwekwe include:

  • The Zinyama Cave Hiking Trail. This trail takes you through the beautiful wilderness of the Zinyama Cave. The trail is challenging but rewarding, with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Driefontein Recreational Park Hiking Trail. This trail starts at the Driefontein Recreational Park and takes you through the beautiful park. The trail is an excellent option for those who are looking for a relaxing hike.

Best Things to Do in Kwekwe: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Kwekwe has a plethora of activities and tours available for tourists to try. Here are some of the best things to do in Kwekwe:

  • Take a Gold Mine Tour. Kwekwe owes its existence to gold mining, and taking a gold mine tour is an excellent way to learn about the city’s history.

  • Visit the River God. The River God is a massive rock formation that bears a striking resemblance to a human face. The formation is believed to be sacred and is a must-see attraction when visiting Kwekwe.

  • Explore the Kwekwe Central Business District. The central business district of Kwekwe is a lively and bustling area with plenty of shops, bars, and restaurants. It’s an excellent place to spend an afternoon exploring the local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Kwekwe

Must-Try Dishes

Kwekwe has a fantastic food scene, with a variety of dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Sadza and Stew. Sadza and stew is a staple dish in Zimbabwe and consists of a thick maize meal and a tomato-based stew.

  • Mopane Worms. Mopane worms are a delicacy in Zimbabwe and are a common snack food. The worms are high in protein and have a nutty flavor.

  • Biltong. Biltong is dried meat that is similar to beef jerky. It’s a popular snack food in Zimbabwe.

Local Beverages

Kwekwe has several local beverages that you should try, including:

  • Chibuku. Chibuku is a popular traditional beer made from maize, sorghum, and water. It’s a sour and refreshing drink that is perfect for a hot day.

  • Mazoe Orange Crush. Mazoe Orange Crush is a popular soft drink in Zimbabwe. It’s made with real orange juice and has a sweet and tangy flavor.

  • Rooibos Tea. Rooibos tea is a popular herbal tea in Zimbabwe. It’s made from the rooibos plant, which is native to South Africa, and has a sweet and nutty flavor.

Where to Stay in Kwekwe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kwekwe has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to stay in Kwekwe include:

  • The Golden Mile Hotel. The Golden Mile Hotel is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Kwekwe. The hotel offers stylish rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • The Kwekwe Hotel. The Kwekwe Hotel is a budget-friendly option located in the city center. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • The Amaveni Guesthouse. The Amaveni Guesthouse is a charming guesthouse located on the outskirts of Kwekwe. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, a garden, and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Kwekwe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kwekwe has several souvenirs and local products that you can bring home, including:

  • Gold Jewelry. Kwekwe is known for its gold mining, and you can find a variety of gold jewelry in the city.

  • Stone Carvings. Stone carvings are a popular souvenir in Zimbabwe, and Kwekwe has several artists that produce beautiful stone carvings.

  • Arts and Crafts. Kwekwe has several artists that produce exquisite arts and crafts, including pottery, baskets, and woven blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kwekwe

Kwekwe has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Zimbabweans are generally friendly and hospitable, and it’s polite to greet people with a smile and a handshake.

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites.

  • It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering some homes and businesses.

Best Time to Visit Kwekwe

The best time to visit Kwekwe is between May and September when the temperatures are cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kwekwe

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Explore the city’s rich cultural and natural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Buy products made from endangered species.

  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kwekwe

Kwekwe is a safe city to visit, but travelers should take precautions such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables out of sight.

  • Use taxis or private cars for transportation after dark.

Health in Kwekwe

Visitors to Kwekwe should be up-to-date on their vaccinations, including hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever. It’s also essential to take precautions against malaria by using mosquito nets and insect repellents.

Getting to Kwekwe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kwekwe

The closest airport to Kwekwe is the Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport, located in Harare, about 200 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kwekwe

Kwekwe has several bus stations, including the Kwekwe Bus Station and the Mbizo Bus Stop. Buses are available for transportation to and from Harare and other major cities in the region.

Other Options

Car rental services are available in Kwekwe, and it’s possible to hire a car for transportation during your stay.

How to Get Around in Kwekwe

Kwekwe has a well-developed public transportation system, including minibusses and taxis. Taxis are available for hire at the airport and bus stations.

Where to Go Next from Kwekwe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kwekwe is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding region. Some nearby destinations that you could consider include:

  • Gweru. Gweru is a city located about 70 kilometers from Kwekwe. The city is well known for its beautiful gardens and historic buildings.

  • Matobo National Park. Matobo National Park is located about 200 kilometers from Kwekwe and is home to a vast array of wildlife, including rhinos, zebras, and giraffes.

  • The Great Zimbabwe Ruins. The Great Zimbabwe Ruins are located about 300 kilometers from Kwekwe and provide a fascinating glimpse into Zimbabwe’s rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Kwekwe is a hidden gem in southeastern Zimbabwe, with something for everyone, from nature lovers to culture enthusiasts. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re well-equipped to plan your trip and discover all that Kwekwe has to offer.
