Introduction to Southeastern Zimbabwe

Southeastern Zimbabwe may not be as popular as other regions in the country, but it has a lot to offer to the intrepid traveler. From breathtaking natural wonders to culturally rich museums, Southeastern Zimbabwe boasts a diverse range of destinations that cater to every traveler’s interests. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, you will find something to love in Southeastern Zimbabwe.

List of Places to Visit in Southeastern Zimbabwe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Great Zimbabwe National Monument: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a stunning ancient city built in the 11th century and is one of the largest stone structures in ancient Africa.

  • Chimanimani National Park: Explore the rich cultural heritage of the Bantu people through rock paintings and cave art scattered throughout the park.

  • Mutare Museum: Experience the historical significance of this bustling city and learn about the local customs and traditions of the people.

Natural Attractions of Southeastern Zimbabwe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bvumba Mountains: Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape from the highest peaks in the region, take a nature walk or engage in a horseback riding tour.

  • Nyanga National Park: Climb the highest peak in Zimbabwe, Mount Inyangani, take a swim in the spectacular natural pools, or visit the lovely Pungwe Falls.

  • Tanganda Tea Estate: Experience tea picking, processing, and tasting tours in one of the country’s most scenic tea plantations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southeastern Zimbabwe

  • Chirinda Forest: Discover the biodiversity of the rainforest on this walking trail.

  • Inyangani Mountain: Challenge yourself with a hike up one of the highest peaks in the country.

  • Bvumba Botanical Gardens: Take a leisurely stroll through the lush gardens and enjoy the tranquil setting.

Best Things to Do in Southeastern Zimbabwe: Activities and Tours

  • Arts and Crafts Market in Mutare: Get a unique shopping experience while supporting local artists and vendors.

  • Fishing in Nyanga: Enjoy the thrill of fishing in some of the most beautiful rivers and lakes of the region.

  • Canopy Tour in Mutarazi Falls: Feel the adrenaline rush and discover the beauty of the Mutarazi Falls from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Southeastern Zimbabwe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nyama Choma: grilled meat, usually beef, served with vegetables

  • Sadza: a maize porridge and staple food in Zimbabwe

  • Matemba: a traditional dish made of tilapia fish and served with sadza.

Local Beverages

  • Chibuku Shake Shake: a homebrewed beer made from maize

  • Maheu: a refreshing and nutritious drink made from fermented grains

  • Zambezi Lager: a locally brewed beer

Where to Stay in Southeastern Zimbabwe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hillside Manor: a boutique hotel with stunning views of the Mutare City

  • Musangano Lodge: a cozy lodge located in the heart of the Chimanimani Mountains

  • The Inn on the Vumba: a luxurious retreat in the Bvumba Mountains with a swimming pool and traditional cuisine.

What to Buy in Southeastern Zimbabwe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carvings and Sculptures: These are made from wood or stone and include animal figurines, masks, and traditional ornaments.

  • Textiles: Traditional fabrics include Shweshwe or geNdonga.

  • Tea: Buy premium tea brands from the Tanganda Tea Estate or other local tea plantations.

Local Culture and Customs of Southeastern Zimbabwe

  • Greeting: When visiting someone’s home, it’s polite to greet them with a “Makadii?” which means “How are you?”

  • Attire: Dress modestly when visiting rural areas or cultural sites and remove shoes before entering some places.

  • Gift Giving: When staying with a local family or visiting a person’s home, it’s customary to bring a small gift such as fruit, sweets or a small souvenir.

Best Time to Visit Southeastern Zimbabwe

The best time to visit Southeastern Zimbabwe is during the dry season, from April to September. The weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are more comfortable to undertake.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southeastern Zimbabwe

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions

  • Try the local cuisine and support local businesses

  • Be aware of the wildlife in the parks and follow park rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos of people without their consent.

  • Don’t litter in national parks and conservation areas.

  • Don’t engage in drug use or any illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southeastern Zimbabwe

As with any foreign destination, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to prevent theft and other crimes. It is essential to keep track of your belongings, be mindful of pickpockets, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Southeastern Zimbabwe

Travelers should consult with their healthcare provider before traveling to Southeastern Zimbabwe, to get up to date on recommended vaccinations and medications. It is advisable to drink bottled water, use sunscreen, and protect oneself from mosquito bites.

Getting to Southeastern Zimbabwe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southeastern Zimbabwe

  • Harare International Airport: located 263 kilometers from Mutare

  • Beira International Airport: located 192 kilometers from Mutare

Bus Stations Near Southeastern Zimbabwe

  • Mutare Central Bus Station: located in the city center

  • Nyanga Bus Station: located in the Nyanga township

  • Vumba Bus Station: located in the Vumba Mountains

Other Options

  • Vehicle Rental: Car hire is available in Harare and can be a convenient way to explore the Southeastern region.

  • Taxi Services: Metered taxis are available in the cities and towns, and they are the safest option for getting around.

How to Get Around in Southeastern Zimbabwe

The region is serviced by an extensive network of paved and unpaved roads. Visitors can rent a vehicle or take public transport like minibusses for an authentic Zimbabwean experience. Taxis and hired cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Southeastern Zimbabwe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Victoria Falls: Explore one of the seven natural wonders of the world, located about 1,000 km from Harare.

  • Lake Kariba: Enjoy a scenic cruise or go fishing in this enormous man-made lake on the northern border.

  • Great Zimbabwe Ruins: Discover the fascinating structures and rich history of the Great Zimbabwe ruins located in the Masvingo province.

Conclusion

Southeastern Zimbabwe has something to offer every traveler, from the historical wonders of the Great Zimbabwe National Monument to the breathtaking views in the Bvumba Mountains. Explore the local culture, enjoy exhilarating outdoor activities, and indulge in the mouth-watering cuisine. Come and experience the hidden gems of this incredible region of Zimbabwe.
