Explore Gweru: Your Ultimate Travel Guide

If you’re planning a trip to Zimbabwe, be sure to add Gweru to your list of destinations. Located in Southeastern Zimbabwe, this charming city is known for its fascinating history, stunning natural attractions, and vibrant cultural scene.

In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at Gweru’s top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Gweru

Gweru is home to several must-see attractions, including historical landmarks, museums, and natural wonders. Here are a few top locations to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gweru is rich in history and culture, and its many museums and historical landmarks are a testament to this fact. Here are some key places to visit:

  • The Antelope Park: This wildlife preserve is home to an array of exotic animals, including zebras, giraffes, and lions. Visitors can take a guided tour of the park or even book an overnight stay in one of the luxurious lodges.
  • The National Museum of Zimbabwe: This museum houses a vast collection of Zimbabwean art, including ancient Shona sculptures and contemporary paintings.
  • The Kwekwe National Park: Located just a short drive from Gweru, this sprawling park is home to an abundance of wildlife species, including elephants, lions, and rhinos.

Natural Attractions of Gweru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking and biking to bird watching and fishing, Gweru is a nature lover’s paradise. Here are some of the best outdoor activities to explore in and around the city:

  • The Matobo Hills: A popular destination for rock climbers and hikers, this stunning range of hills boasts breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • The Midlands State Park: This park is a favorite among cyclists, thanks to its well-maintained biking trails and stunning natural beauty.
  • The Gweru River: This scenic river winds its way through the heart of Gweru and is a popular spot for fishing and rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gweru

If you’re looking to explore Gweru’s natural beauty by foot or bike, there are several routes worth checking out. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • The Matobo National Park Hike: This day-long hike takes you through the heart of Matobo National Park, with stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • The Midlands Cycling Trail: This well-marked trail spans over 200 kilometers and takes cyclists through some of the region’s most scenic landscapes.
  • The Gweru River Walk: This leisurely stroll along the banks of the Gweru River is a great way to take in the city’s natural beauty at a more relaxed pace.

Best Things to Do in Gweru: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many natural wonders, Gweru is home to a range of activities and tours that are sure to appeal to visitors of all ages. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Camelot Spa: This luxurious day spa offers a range of treatments, including massages, facials, and body wraps.
  • The Gweru Golf Course: This 18-hole golf course is a favorite among locals and visitors alike, with spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Antelope Park Night Safari: This unforgettable tour takes you on a nighttime adventure through the park, where you’ll see lions, leopards, and other nocturnal animals in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gweru

If you’re planning to spend a full week in Gweru, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 Visit the National Museum of Zimbabwe
Day 2 Take a hike in the Matobo Hills

Food and Drink Scene of Gweru

No trip to Gweru is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sadza: Made from maize meal, this traditional Zimbabwean dish is usually served with meat or vegetables.
  • Mopane worms: High in protein, these edible caterpillars are often fried or dried and eaten as a snack or added to stews.
  • Chikendeya: This popular local dish consists of chicken cooked in peanut butter sauce and served with sadza.

Local Beverages

  • Chibuku: A fermented corn-based drink, Chibuku (also known as ‘shake shake’) is a popular local brew with a strong taste and high alcohol content.
  • Lemon Beer: Made from freshly squeezed lemons and local beer, this refreshing drink is a must-try for any visitor to Gweru.

Where to Stay in Gweru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gweru has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and tastes. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

  • Gweru Guest House: This charming guesthouse is centrally located in the heart of the city and offers spacious rooms and excellent service.
  • Antelope Park: For those looking for a more luxurious experience, Antelope Park offers a range of lodges and suites with stunning views of the surrounding wildlife preserve.
  • The Village Lodge: This budget-friendly hostel is a great option for backpackers and travelers on a tight budget, with clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Gweru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handicrafts to local delicacies, Gweru offers plenty of options for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring back home. Here are a few items worth checking out:

  • Handmade baskets: Woven from local grasses and fibers, these rustic baskets make for a unique and eco-friendly souvenir.
  • Shona sculptures: These intricate stone sculptures are a staple of Zimbabwean art and are available in shops and galleries throughout Gweru.
  • Local coffee: Made from locally sourced beans, Gweru coffee is known for its rich flavor and smooth finish.

Local Culture and Customs of Gweru

In Gweru, locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and the city has a relaxed and laid-back atmosphere. However, it’s important to be mindful of local customs and etiquette. Here are a few key things to keep in mind:

  • Handshakes: In Zimbabwe, it’s common to greet someone with a firm handshake and a smile.
  • Dress code: While Gweru is a relatively relaxed city, it’s important to dress modestly when visiting places of worship or formal events.
  • Respect elders: In Zimbabwean culture, elders are highly respected, so be sure to show deference and respect to older individuals.

Best Time to Visit Gweru

The best time to visit Gweru is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gweru

To make the most of your trip to Gweru, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Take guided tours: Local tour guides can offer invaluable insights into the region’s history and culture.
  • Try local cuisine: Zimbabwean cuisine is unique and delicious, so be sure to try some of the local dishes and beverages.
  • Be respectful: Always show respect and courtesy to locals, especially when visiting places of worship or cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep Gweru’s natural beauty intact by disposing of your trash responsibly.
  • Drink tap water: It’s recommended to only drink bottled water to avoid the risk of waterborne illnesses.
  • Ignore safety precautions: While Gweru is generally safe, it’s still important to take precautions against theft and other common crimes.

Safety and Health

Like most cities in Zimbabwe, Gweru is relatively safe, but visitors should still exercise caution and take common-sense safety measures. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Gweru

  • Avoid walking alone late at night: While violent incidents are rare, it’s still important to be cautious when walking after dark.
  • Take taxis or ride-sharing services: It’s generally safer to take a taxi or ride-sharing service than to walk alone or use public transportation.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye out for pickpockets and other common criminals, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Gweru

  • Get vaccinated: Make sure you’re up-to-date on all necessary vaccinations, including hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever.
  • Avoid tap water: To prevent waterborne illnesses, only drink bottled or purified water.
  • Bring insect repellent: Mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever are prevalent in Zimbabwe, so make sure to bring insect repellent, long-sleeved shirts, and pants.

Getting to Gweru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gweru is easy to reach via several transportation options, including flights, buses, and trains. Here’s a closer look at each option:

Airports Near Gweru

  • Gweru-Thornhill Airfield: Located just outside the city, this small regional airport offers flights to nearby destinations.
  • Harare International Airport: Zimbabwe’s largest airport is located in the capital city of Harare, which is a 3-hour drive from Gweru.

Bus Stations Near Gweru

  • Gweru Intercity Bus Terminal: This centrally located terminal offers frequent buses to major Zimbabwean destinations like Harare, Bulawayo, and Victoria Falls.

Other Options

  • Trains: Regular trains connect Gweru with other Zimbabwean destinations, including Harare and Bulawayo.
  • Private car or taxi: For added convenience and flexibility, you can also hire a private car or taxi to take you to Gweru from elsewhere in Zimbabwe.

How to Get Around in Gweru

Once you’ve arrived in Gweru, there are several transportation options for getting around the city, including taxis, buses, and shared ‘kombis’.

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Gweru and are a convenient way of getting around the city.
  • Buses: For longer distances and intercity travel, buses are a popular and affordable option.
  • Shared kombis: Similar to minibusses, shared kombis are a common mode of transportation in Gweru and are often used for short trips within the city.

Where to Go Next from Gweru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Zimbabwe, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out, including:

  • Great Zimbabwe: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to some of the best-preserved ruins of ancient Africa and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Hwange National Park: This sprawling nature reserve is known for its abundant wildlife, sprawling vistas, and stunning sunsets.
  • Bulawayo: Zimbabwe’s second-largest city is located just a short drive from Gweru and is home to several cultural landmarks and modern amenities.

Conclusion

Gweru is a truly remarkable destination, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a culture enthusiast, or simply looking to relax and unwind, Gweru is sure to exceed your expectations. Book your trip today and get ready to explore this incredible city for yourself!
