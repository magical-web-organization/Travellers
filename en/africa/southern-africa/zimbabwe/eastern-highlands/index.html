Introduction to Eastern Highlands

The Eastern Highlands is a mountainous region in Zimbabwe, Southern Africa. The region is known for its scenic beauty, diverse flora and fauna, and outdoor adventures. Eastern Highlands comprises three main mountain chains, including Nyanga, Bvumba, and Chimanimani. Nyanga is the tallest mountain and offers visitors multiple natural and cultural attractions. Bvumba is known for its vast tea plantations, natural forests, and botanical gardens. Chimanimani is a popular hiking destination with its rugged mountain peaks, waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Highlands

Eastern Highlands has a rich cultural heritage influenced by various ethnic groups, including Shona, Ndebele, and Tonga. Greetings are an essential part of Eastern Highlands’ culture. When greeting an elder or stranger, place your right hand on your left elbow while bowing slightly. Also, it’s customary to offer a small gift or token when visiting someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Highlands

The best time to visit Eastern Highlands is from August to December when the temperatures are mild and rainfall is minimal. The region experiences a wet season from December to April, which may make hiking and outdoor activities challenging.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Highlands

Eastern Highlands is generally a safe travel destination. However, visitors should be aware of petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft. It’s advisable to avoid walking alone at night, and always lock your accommodation’s doors and windows.

Health in Eastern Highlands

Before visiting Eastern Highlands, visitors should ensure they are up-to-date with their routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, rubella, and hepatitis A&B. Malaria is present in the region, and visitors should take necessary precautions, such as taking antimalarial medication and using insect repellent.
