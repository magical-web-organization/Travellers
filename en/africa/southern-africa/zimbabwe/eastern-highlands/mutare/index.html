Introduction to Mutare

Mutare is a city in the Eastern Highlands of Zimbabwe, located in the Manicaland Province. It is known for its scenic beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse wildlife. Its strategic location, at the border with Mozambique, makes it a gateway to other African countries.

List of Places to Visit in Mutare

Mutare offers an extensive range of attractions, both natural and cultural. The following are some of the must-see places in and around the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The city has a rich cultural heritage, and there are several museums and historical places you can visit to learn about the history of Mutare and Zimbabwe.

  • The Mutare Museum: This museum provides an overview of the history and culture of Zimbabwe, from pre-colonial times to the present. It has exhibits on the country’s geology, archaeology, anthropology, and botany.

  • The Vumba Botanical Gardens: Located just outside of the city, the gardens are home to an incredible array of plant species, some of which are native to the region.

  • The Utopia House Museum: This house was built in the 1900s and is a showcase of the colonial architecture of the time. It is now a museum and art gallery featuring local artists.

Natural Attractions of Mutare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love outdoor adventures and exploring nature, Mutare has a lot to offer. The region is home to breathtakingly beautiful landscapes and stunning natural wonders.

  • Nyanga National Park: Located about 80 km from the city, the park is the oldest park in Zimbabwe and one of the most popular tourist attractions. It has several hiking trails, waterfalls, and great scenic views.

  • Bvumba Mountains: The Bvumba Mountains, located on the border with Mozambique, are home to some of Zimbabwe’s most scenic views. The area is known for its tea plantations, botanical gardens, and nature reserves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mutare

Mutare is also an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several trails and routes to choose from, with varying levels of difficulty.

  • Pungwe Gorge: This is a popular hike, located in the Nyanga National Park. The trek follows the Pungwe River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Chimanimani Mountains: This is one of the most challenging hikes in the region, but also one of the most rewarding. The trail takes you through pristine wilderness, and you can see some of Zimbabwe’s most spectacular views.

Best Things to Do in Mutare: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and exploring the city’s natural and cultural attractions, there are also other exciting activities you can engage in while in Mutare.

  • Game Drives: Mutare is home to several game reserves, where you can enjoy game drives and see some of Africa’s most iconic wildlife.

  • Canopy Tours: The Mutarazi Falls, located just outside the city, offer an epic canopy tour experience, with panoramic views of the waterfalls and surrounding landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Mutare

No trip to Mutare is complete without enjoying some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the local dishes and drinks you must try while in the city.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sadza: This is a staple food in Zimbabwe, and it is made from maize meal. It is often served with meat and vegetables.

  • Mbudzi Nematumbu: This is a traditional dish made from goat meat and liver, and it is often served with maize meal.

Local Beverages

  • Mazoe Orange Crush: This is a popular soft drink in Zimbabwe, and it is made from locally grown oranges.

  • Castle Lager: This is a locally brewed beer, and it is one of the most popular brands in Zimbabwe.

Where to Stay in Mutare: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mutare offers a wide range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to backpacker hostels. Here are some of the best options to consider.

  • The Golden Peacock Hotel: This is a luxury hotel located in the city center. It has spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

  • African Sun Amber Hotel: This hotel offers excellent value for money, with comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local and international dishes.

What to Buy in Mutare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Mutare, there are several souvenirs and local products you can buy. The city is known for its intricate handcrafts and artisanal products.

  • Handmade baskets and pottery: These are crafted by local artisans and are excellent souvenirs to take home.

  • Coffee: Zimbabwe is known for its high-quality coffee, which is grown in the region. You can get freshly roasted coffee beans to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mutare

Zimbabwe has a rich cultural heritage, and Mutare is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions you should be aware of while in the city.

  • Greetings: It is common to greet people with a handshake, and it is always considered polite to ask about each other’s well-being.

  • Dress Code: Zimbabweans dress conservatively, and it is best to dress modestly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Mutare

The best time to visit Mutare is between May and September, during the dry season. However, the region is beautiful all year round, and it depends on the activities you plan to do.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mutare

Here are some basic Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while in Mutare.

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions

  • Dress appropriately in public places

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission

  • Talk politics, unless it is a safe and neutral topic.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are the top concerns when traveling to any destination. Here is some essential information to keep in mind while in Mutare.

Safety in Mutare

Mutare is generally considered safe for tourists, but it is always best to take precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking in poorly lit areas.

Health in Mutare

It is essential to take the necessary health precautions before traveling to Mutare. Check the CDC website for any necessary vaccinations or medications you may need before your trip.

Getting to Mutare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mutare is easily accessible by various means of transport. Here are the most commonly used transport modes to get to the city.

Airports Near Mutare

The nearest airport to Mutare is the Harare International Airport, which is about 260 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mutare

Mutare has a bus terminal, which offers both intercity and international bus services.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or take a taxi from Harare to Mutare.

How to Get Around in Mutare

The best way to get around in Mutare is by private car or taxi. You can also use public transport, but it can be challenging to navigate if you do not speak the local language.

Where to Go Next from Mutare: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Zimbabwe, there are several destinations and excursions you can undertake from Mutare.

  • Great Zimbabwe: This is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located about 300 km from Mutare. It is one of the largest stone ruins in Southern Africa and is a significant historical and cultural attraction.

  • Lake Kariba: This is one of the largest man-made lakes in the world, located about 500 km from Mutare. It is a popular tourist destination, with endless opportunities for fishing, game viewing, and water sports.

Conclusion

Mutare is a great tourist destination for nature lovers, cultural enthusiasts, and adventure seekers. The city and its surroundings offer an extensive range of attractions, hiking trails, and outdoor adventures. The region’s culinary scene and accommodations are also excellent. With a comprehensive understanding of the local customs and traditions, you’re guaranteed a fantastic and safe travel experience in Mutare.
