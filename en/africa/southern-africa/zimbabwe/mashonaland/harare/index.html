Introduction to Harare

Harare, the capital city of Zimbabwe, is a bustling metropolis with a unique blend of historical landmarks, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. Situated in Mashonaland, in Southern Africa, Harare offers visitors a glimpse into the rich diversity of Zimbabwe.

From fascinating museums and historical sites to picturesque outdoor adventures and bustling markets, Harare has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an action-packed getaway, Harare’s plethora of attractions provide a wide range of options.

To help you plan your trip, we’ve put together a comprehensive guide to the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture in Harare.

List of Places to Visit in Harare

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. National Gallery of Zimbabwe

The National Gallery of Zimbabwe features a diverse collection of contemporary African art and is a must-see for anyone interested in the arts. With rotating exhibits featuring the works of both established and up-and-coming artists, the gallery is a hub of creativity and inspiration.

2. Zimbabwe Museum of Human Sciences

The Zimbabwe Museum of Human Sciences houses the country’s largest collection of ethnographic and historical artifacts, including Stone Age tools, traditional textiles, and musical instruments. Take a walk through Zimbabwe’s rich history and learn about the country’s cultural heritage.

3. Mbare Musika Market

Mbare Musika Market is one of the largest open-air markets in Harare, offering visitors a chance to experience the hustle and bustle of daily life in Zimbabwe. You’ll find everything from fresh produce and spices to handmade crafts and clothing.

Natural Attractions of Harare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Kuimba Shiri Bird Sanctuary

Located on Lake Chivero, the largest lake in Zimbabwe, the Kuimba Shiri Bird Sanctuary is home to a vast array of birds and other wildlife. The sanctuary offers guided tours, bird shows, and canoe trips, making it a perfect outing for nature lovers.

2. Mukuvisi Woodlands

The Mukuvisi Woodlands is a natural preserve located in the heart of Harare. Here, you can spot wildlife such as giraffes, zebras, and ostriches. It’s a perfect spot for a relaxing picnic or a leisurely stroll along the walking trails.

3. Ngoma Kurira Munda

Ngoma Kurira Munda is a stunning waterfall located 30 km from Harare. The waterfall offers visitors a chance to experience the beauty of Zimbabwe’s natural landscapes and is perfect for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harare

From city walks to nature trails, Harare’s diverse landscape offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the must-visit trails include:

  • The Harare Gardens Walking Trail
  • The Rhino Walk at the Mukuvisi Woodlands
  • The Gorge Trail at the Chivero Nature Reserve

Best Things to Do in Harare: Activities and Tours

1. City Tour of Harare

Explore Harare’s architectural landmarks, cultural sites, and vibrant markets with a guided city tour. Visit iconic landmarks such as the National Heroes Acre, and learn about the city’s history and culture through informative commentary by local guides.

2. Zimbabwean Cooking Class

Experience the unique flavors of Zimbabwean cuisine by taking a cooking class. Learn to make iconic dishes such as sadza (a type of porridge), relish, and traditional meat dishes, while enjoying a fun and interactive cultural experience.

3. Wine Tasting at The Antelope Park

If you’re a wine enthusiast, head to The Antelope Park and indulge in a wine tasting session. The restaurant offers a wide selection of local and international wines, paired with delicious snacks and meals.

Food and Drink Scene of Harare

Must-Try Dishes

1. Sadza

Sadza is a staple food in Zimbabwe, made from cornmeal and served with a variety of relishes such as gravy or vegetable stew. It’s a must-try dish that is flavorful and filling.

2. Boerewors

A type of sausage with a unique flavor, boerewors is a popular snack in Harare. It’s made from beef, pork, and spices and served with a side of tomato sauce.

3. Zambezi Bream

Zambezi bream is a freshwater fish found in the Zambezi River. It’s a popular food in Zimbabwe and prepared in a variety of ways, including fried and grilled.

Local Beverages

1. Chibuku

Chibuku is a traditional brew made from maize and sorghum, and is a favorite among locals. It’s usually served in large, colorful containers that are shared among friends.

2. Maheu

Maheu is a sweet, non-alcoholic beverage made from millet and often flavored with fruits and spices. It’s a refreshing drink that is perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot day.

3. Mazoe

Mazoe is a popular soft drink in Zimbabwe, made from concentrated fruit juice and water. It’s available in a variety of flavors, including orange, blackcurrant, and apple.

Where to Stay in Harare: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harare boasts a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the recommended places include:

  • Cresta Lodge Harare
  • The Monomotapa Hotel
  • Meikles Hotel

What to Buy in Harare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Handwoven Baskets

Handwoven baskets are a popular souvenir in Harare, and make for a great home décor item. These baskets are typically made from natural materials and come in a variety of sizes and designs.

2. Stone Sculptures

Zimbabwe is famous for its stone sculptures, which are hand-carved by local artists using traditional methods. These sculptures come in a variety of shapes and sizes, ranging from small figurines to large, intricate pieces.

3. Shona Masks

The Shona people are renowned for their vibrant masks, which are used in traditional dances and ceremonies. These masks come in a variety of designs and are made from a range of materials, including wood, metal, and cloth.

Local Culture and Customs of Harare

The people of Harare are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Zimbabwean culture is deeply rooted in tradition and values such as respect for elders and communal living.

When visiting Harare, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or cultural landmarks, and always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Best Time to Visit Harare

The best time to visit Harare is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is mild and pleasant, with clear skies and plenty of sunshine. October to April is the rainy season, which can make travel and outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harare

Do:

  • Do learn some basic phrases in Shona or Ndebele to help you interact with locals.
  • Do dress modestly when visiting religious sites or cultural landmarks.
  • Do try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of people or places without permission.
  • Don’t get into political discussions with locals.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harare

Harare is generally a safe city. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Harare

Visitors to Harare should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as malaria is prevalent in some areas. It’s also recommended to get vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever before traveling to Zimbabwe.

Getting to Harare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harare

Harare International Airport is the main airport serving the city, with regular flights to and from major cities in Africa and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Harare

Harare’s main bus station is Road Port Bus Terminus, which connects the city to other destinations in Zimbabwe and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or book a private transfer to get to Harare from nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Harare

Harare has a reliable public transportation system, with buses, minibusses, and taxis available throughout the city. Visitors can also rent a car or use ride-sharing services to get around.

Conclusion

Harare is a city full of surprises, offering visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and nature. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be equipped to plan the perfect trip to Harare, and experience everything this vibrant city has to offer.
