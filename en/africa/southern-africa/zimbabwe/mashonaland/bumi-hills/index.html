Introduction to Bumi Hills

Nestled in Mashonaland, Zimbabwe, Bumi Hills is a picturesque destination that boasts stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Situated on the southern edge of Lake Kariba, the largest man-made lake in the world, Bumi Hills is an ideal getaway for travelers looking for outdoor adventures, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. This guide will take you through the must-visit places, activities, food, and culture of Bumi Hills.

List of Places to Visit in Bumi Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bumi Hills is home to several historically and culturally significant places that serve as a window into Zimbabwe’s past. One such place is the Kariba Dam, which is the world’s largest man-made dam. It is a fascinating piece of engineering that has brought electricity and water to the region. Another must-visit site is the Baobab tree, which is believed to be over 1,000 years old and is a sacred site for the local tribes. Additionally, the Bumi Hills Foundation is dedicated to promoting the conservation of the area’s wildlife and natural resources and has several educational institutions that can be visited.

Natural Attractions of Bumi Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will have plenty to do in Bumi Hills. The wildlife in the area is diverse and includes elephants, hippos, lions, and crocodiles. Visitors can take guided safaris or game drives to observe the animals in their natural habitat. The nearby Matusadona National Park for a chance to see some of Africa’s most impressive game animals, while the Ume River provides an opportunity for fishing, kayaking, or canoeing. For bird lovers, the Matusadona Bird Trust has a research and education center that offers birdwatchers the chance to learn about the area’s unique bird population.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bumi Hills

Bumi Hills is a natural playground with scenic hiking and biking routes dotted throughout the region. One of the most popular routes is the Chizarira Mountain Range, which offers breathtaking views of Lake Kariba and the Zambezi valley. Another great option is the Ntaba Nkulu Trail, which takes hikers through an untouched wilderness of hardwood forests, rocky outcroppings, and riverine vegetation. For a more laid-back experience, visitors can rent a mountain bike and explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Bumi Hills: Activities and Tours

Besides fishing, game drives, and birdwatching, there are many other fun activities to try in Bumi Hills. Visitors can take a sunset cruise on Lake Kariba to witness the beauty of the region’s landscapes. Alternatively, travelers can take a guided nature walk to learn about the local flora and fauna. For a cultural experience, the nearby Tonga village is an excellent destination to learn about the Tonga people’s customs, beliefs, and way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bumi Hills

Day 1: Arrival and check into local guesthouse. Day 2: Guided nature walk and sunset cruise on Lake Kariba. Day 3: Game drive in Matusadona National Park. Day 4: Tour of culturally important sites - Baobab tree and Kariba Dam Day 5: Free day to hike, bike, or explore nearby Tonga village Day 6: Fishing or kayaking adventure on Ume River Day 7: Departure from Bumi Hills.

Food and Drink Scene of Bumi Hills

Must-Try Dishes

Zimbabwe has a rich culinary tradition, and Bumi Hills offers an excellent opportunity to sample some of the region’s most delicious dishes. One such dish is Sadza, a traditional staple made from ground maize, which is often served with meat, vegetable stews, or relishes. Another must-try dish is Kapenta, which is a small freshwater fish commonly served fried or grilled. The area also offers a wide range of vegetarian and vegan meals, including healthy meat-free bowl dishes and vegetarian stews.

Local Beverages

Beer and wine are popular libations, but the region’s specialty is the traditional beer made from locally grown grains. Popular brands include Chibuku and Mazoe Orange Juice, a staple drink in Zimbabwe. Fresh juices made from locally grown fruit, such as papayas, mangoes, and oranges, are also widely available.

Where to Stay in Bumi Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bumi Hills has a range of accommodations to suit different preferences and budgets. For travelers who enjoy luxury, the Bumi Hills Safari Lodge offers breathtaking views and five-star amenities. For budget-conscious travelers, there are several guesthouses and backpacker hostels in the nearby town of Kariba. Choosing to stay in a locally owned and operated guesthouse is not only budget-friendly but also allows visitors to experience Bumi Hill’s true essence and charm from a local’s perspective.

What to Buy in Bumi Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Bumi Hills can find a wide range of souvenirs and local products to bring back home. The area is famous for its woodcrafts, pottery, and beadwork that reflect the region’s culture and heritage. Other items to consider include traditional tribal masks, artwork, and batiks, as well as locally produced jams, chutneys, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Bumi Hills

Bumi Hills is home to the Tonga people, who have a rich cultural heritage and close ties to Lake Kariba. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and not taking photographs without permission. Engaging with the locals is an excellent opportunity to learn about the region’s history, culture, and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Bumi Hills

The best time to visit Bumi Hills is between May and October when the weather is warm and dry, and the wildlife is abundant. December through March is also a great time to visit when the landscape is lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bumi Hills

Do:

  • Do carry Sun protection items such as hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen
  • Do dress in lightweight, comfortable clothing when going on safari.
  • Do carry insect repellent
  • Do be respectful of cultural and traditional practices

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs without permission.
  • Don’t hunt or take any wildlife or plants without permission.
  • Don’t throw trash or litter throughout the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bumi Hills

Although Bumi Hills is relatively safe, travelers should exercise caution when venturing into the wilderness or exploring remote areas. Always travel with a guide and inform someone of your itinerary before leaving for a hike, safari or outdoor adventure. Travelers should also carry basic first aid supplies, additional water supplies, and a charged mobile phone.

Health in Bumi Hills

Travelers should always consult their doctor for vaccination recommendations when traveling to Zimbabwe. Additionally, visitors should take measures to prevent insect bites, such as using insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing. Tap water is not always safe to drink, so travelers should ensure they have enough bottled or purified water to last their trip.

Getting to Bumi Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bumi Hills

The nearest airports to Bumi Hills are Harare International airport, which is about 250 km away, and Victoria Falls International airport, which is about 600 km away. Domestic flights from Harare or Victoria Falls to Kariba are available from several local airlines.

Bus Stations Near Bumi Hills

Intercity buses and coaches run between Harare and other major cities and towns in Zimbabwe, including Kariba, which is the nearest town to Bumi Hills. From Kariba, you can take a taxi or a shared minibus taxi to Bumi Hills.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are available in Harare and Victoria Falls if you prefer to drive yourself to Bumi Hills. The route from Harare follows the Harare-Kariba highway, while the route from Victoria Falls passes through Hwange National Park en route to Kariba.

How to Get Around in Bumi Hills

To get around Bumi Hills, travelers can either hire a car or opt for a guided tour or safari. Most safari lodges and guesthouses offer a variety of transport options, including private transfers, or shared minibus taxis. Alternatively, travelers can rent a bicycle to explore the region at their pace.

Where to Go Next from Bumi Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Bumi Hills, tourists can explore other destinations and attractions nearby, including Victoria Falls, Hwange National Park, and Mana Pools National Park. These destinations offer a wide range of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Bumi Hills is a captivating destination that is sure to delight travelers looking for adventure, beauty, and culture. With its breathtaking scenery, vibrant cultural heritage, and wealth of outdoor activities, Bumi Hills should be on every traveler’s list of must-visit destinations. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an active adventure, Bumi Hills has something to offer everyone.
