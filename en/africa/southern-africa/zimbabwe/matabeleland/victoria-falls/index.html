Introduction to Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls, also known as ‘Mosi-oa-Tunya,’ meaning “the smoke that thunders,” is one of the most iconic natural wonders in the world. It straddles the border between Zimbabwe and Zambia, attracting millions of visitors each year. The waterfall is over a kilometer wide and drops almost 108 meters into the Zambezi River. The surrounding region is home to diverse wildlife, beautiful parks, museums, and historical sites. Whether you are an adventure-seeker or looking for a laid-back holiday, Victoria Falls has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Victoria Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Victoria Falls is not only famous for its natural beauty but also has a rich history and cultural heritage. The following are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Victoria Falls:

  • Victoria Falls Bridge: Built in 1905, this historic bridge was designed by Sir Ralph Freeman and is a marvel of engineering. Visitors can do a bungee jump or take part in a thrilling swing from the bridge, and enjoy magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Victoria Falls Matabeleland North Heritage Centre: A great place to learn about the local culture and history of the Victoria Falls region. The center showcases the region’s arts, history, and traditional customs.
  • Railway Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of Victoria Falls railway and the Victorian-era steam trains that once serviced the region. Visitors can see a range of locomotives, carriages, and photographs.
  • Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this national park is located on the banks of the Zambezi River, covering an area of about 66 square kilometers. The park is home to a wide range of wildlife, including elephants, zebras, giraffes, buffalos, and antelopes. Visitors can take a walking or driving safari, which is an unforgettable experience.

Natural Attractions of Victoria Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Victoria Falls boasts incredible natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Listed below are some of the must-visit natural attractions that you can experience while in Victoria Falls:

  • Victoria Falls National Park: A protected area that encompasses the waterfalls and the surrounding rainforest, home to unique flora and fauna. One can take a guided tour of the falls, hike or go bird-watching.
  • Devils Pool: An iconic natural infinity pool on the edge of the Victoria Falls, offering breathtaking views of the waterfall. Visitors can only access the pool during the dry season, from May to November.
  • Livingstone Island: Named after the famous explorer David Livingstone, this island is located in the middle of the Zambezi River, on the edge of the Victoria Falls. Visitors can take a canoe ride or swim in the river, and enjoy a stunning view of the falls.
  • Zambezi River Cruise: A chance to relax and unwind while sailing down the Zambezi River. Visitors can get a great view of the African wildlife and birdlife, while enjoying dinner and drinks onboard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Victoria Falls

For the adventurous traveler, Victoria Falls offers some fantastic opportunities for hiking and biking. The following are some of the best trails that you can explore while in Victoria Falls:

  • The Batoka Gorge trail: A challenging 16 km trail that takes you through the Batoka Gorge. It offers magnificent views of the Zambezi River, the wildlife, and the vegetation.
  • The Victoria Falls walk: A leisurely walk along the falls, offering different vantage points of the waterfall.
  • Zambezi River Trail: A scenic trail along the Zambezi River, perfect for mountain biking, hiking, or horse riding.

Best Things to Do in Victoria Falls: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Victoria Falls, offering something for everyone. Here are the must-try activities and tours:

  • Bungee Jumping: One of the most popular adrenaline activities in Victoria Falls, jump off the Victoria Falls Bridge for a thrilling experience.
  • White Water Rafting: River rafting down the Zambezi River is another exciting challenge for the adventure-seekers.
  • Helicopter Ride: A helicopter ride over the Victoria Falls offers breathtaking views and an unforgettable experience.
  • Cultural Tours: Take a cultural tour to the local villages to learn about the customs and daily lives of the locals.
  • Night Safari: A guided tour at night in the national park offers a unique perspective on nocturnal wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Victoria Falls

Must-Try Dishes

The Gastronomy culture of Victoria Falls is diverse, with plenty of dishes to sample. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Sadza: A staple food made of maize or cornmeal, usually served with vegetables, meat, or soup.
  • Mopane Worms: A local delicacy made from the caterpillars of a moth, high in protein and traditionally dried or cooked.
  • Kapenta: Small fish, usually deep-fried and served with vegetables or a side salad.
  • Biltong: A dried meat that is usually eaten with salt and pepper.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Victoria Falls has a variety of locally made drinks that you should try. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Zambezi Lager: A locally brewed beer widely available in bars, restaurants, and hotels.
  • Mazoe: A popular fruit juice brand found throughout Southern and Eastern Africa.
  • Amarula: A creamy liqueur made from the fruit of the Marula tree, usually consumed as a dessert.

Where to Stay in Victoria Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Victoria Falls has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Victoria Falls Hotel: A historic colonial-style hotel offering panoramic views of the falls.
  • Victoria Falls Backpackers: A budget-friendly hostel with dorms and private rooms.
  • A’Zambezi River Lodge: A riverside lodge that offers luxury rooms and exclusive river cruises.

What to Buy in Victoria Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Victoria Falls has plenty of souvenir shops and markets that sell a wide range of locally produced arts and crafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should consider buying:

  • Hand-carved wooden hippopotamuses.
  • Tribal masks and other African-inspired artworks.
  • Baskets and woven handicrafts.
  • Traditional textiles and garments.

Local Culture and Customs of Victoria Falls

The locals in Victoria Falls are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their culture and traditions. Here are some of the customs to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Shaking hands is the most common form of greeting.
  • Dress conservatively in public places and when visiting cultural sites.
  • Avoid discussing politics or making derogatory comments about the locals.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Victoria Falls

The most favorable time to visit Victoria Falls is from May to September, during the dry season. The weather is mild, and the water levels are low, allowing for better views of the falls. The best time for adventure activities like rafting or bungee jumping is from August to December, when the water levels are higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Victoria Falls

Do:

  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities, as the heat can be intense.
  • Keep your passport and valuables in a safe place at all times.
  • Take malaria prophylaxis before and after visiting Victoria Falls.
  • Bargain when shopping in the markets and souvenirs shops

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in the national parks or public places.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Do not take pictures of the locals or natural landmarks without their permission.
  • Do not swim in the Victoria Falls, as it is extremely dangerous and has strong currents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls is a safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Avoid walking alone in isolated or unfamiliar areas, especially at night.
  • Do not carry large amounts of cash around with you.
  • Remain aware of your surroundings to detect any possible security threats.
  • Only use reputable and licensed tour operators for adventure activities.

Health in Victoria Falls

Some health concerns visitors should be aware of when traveling to Victoria Falls include:

  • Malaria is prevalent in the region. It’s advised to take a prophylactic 2 weeks before your visit.
  • Dehydration is a common concern due to the intense heat. Drink plenty of water, and avoid staying outside for extended periods.
  • Consult with a travel doctor for any recommended vaccinations

Getting to Victoria Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls International Airport is the closest airport to the falls and is serviced by several international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Victoria Falls

Buses are a comfortable and affordable way to travel to Victoria Falls. The Zimbabwean bus company, Intercape Mainliner, runs regular services to and from Harare, Bulawayo, and Johannesburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive by train or car.

How to Get Around in Victoria Falls

Getting around Victoria Falls is relatively easy. Taxis, buses, and rental cars are available for visitors. Alternatively, visitors can take part in guided tours, where transportation is provided.

Where to Go Next from Victoria Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations visitors can explore after visiting Victoria Falls, including:

  • Hwange National Park: Home to a diverse range of wildlife, with plenty of opportunities for game drives and safaris.
  • Chobe National Park: Located in Botswana, this park is famous for its herds of elephants and is easily accessible from Victoria Falls.
  • Livingstone: Across the border in Zambia, Livingstone is home to several museums and cultural centers, including the Livingstone Museum and the Maramba Cultural Museum.

Conclusion

Victoria Falls has so much to offer visitors, from the natural beauty of the falls, the cultural attractions and museums, the adventure and outdoor activities, and the delicious traditional cuisine. Visitors are guaranteed an unforgettable experience in this iconic destination in Southern Africa.
