Introduction to Bulawayo

Bulawayo is the second-largest city in Zimbabwe, and it is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and natural beauty. The city is located in the southern part of Zimbabwe and is a popular tourist destination owing to its numerous attractions, hospitable people, and vibrant art and culture. Bulawayo is the perfect destination for adventure seekers, history buffs, and anyone looking for a unique African experience.

List of Places to Visit in Bulawayo

Bulawayo has numerous attractions, ranging from cultural landmarks and historical sites to scenic natural wonders. Here are some of the must-see places in Bulawayo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bulawayo has a rich history that is worth exploring, and the city boasts several museums and historical sites that give visitors an insight into the city’s past. Some of the most notable cultural attractions in Bulawayo include:

  • National Museum of Zimbabwe - this museum offers visitors an opportunity to learn about the history, culture, and heritage of Zimbabwe through various artifacts, exhibits, and displays.
  • Matobo Hills - a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers visitors a glimpse into the ancient rock engravings and paintings of the San people.
  • Khami Ruins - this archaeological site showcases the history and culture of the Torwa and Kalanga civilizations and gives visitors a chance to explore the ruins of an ancient city.

Natural Attractions of Bulawayo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bulawayo’s natural beauty is unparalleled, and the city is home to numerous outdoor adventure activities and scenic attractions. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in Bulawayo:

  • Matobo National Park - a wildlife reserve that is home to numerous animal species, including leopards, zebras, and rhinos.
  • Victoria Falls - one of the seven Natural Wonders of the World, located just a few hours’ drive from Bulawayo.
  • Tshabalala Wildlife Sanctuary - a nature reserve that offers visitors an opportunity to see a wide variety of animal species, including lions, giraffes, and cheetahs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bulawayo

For adventure seekers and outdoor enthusiasts, Bulawayo has several hiking and biking trails that offer incredible views and a chance to experience the natural beauty of the city. Some popular hikes and biking routes in Bulawayo include:

  • Matobo Hills National Park - This park features several hiking trails that take visitors through lush forests and offer breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.
  • Tshabalala Game Sanctuary - this wildlife sanctuary has several biking routes that take visitors around the park, offering an opportunity to see wildlife up close.

Best Things to Do in Bulawayo: Activities and Tours

Bulawayo offers numerous fun-filled activities and tours, allowing visitors to experience the city’s unique culture, nature, and history. Here are some of the best things to do in Bulawayo:

  • Visit the Natural History Museum
  • Go on a game drive in Hwange National Park
  • Explore the ruins of Khami
  • Go on a city tour of Bulawayo

Week-Long Itinerary for Bulawayo

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Bulawayo:

Day 1: Visit the National Museum of Zimbabwe and explore the city center.

Day 2: Go on a game drive in Hwange National Park.

Day 3: Visit Matobo Hills National Park and explore the ancient rock engravings and paintings.

Day 4: Explore the ruins of Khami and learn about the history of the ancient city.

Day 5: Go on a city tour and visit the most historic sites and landmarks of Bulawayo.

Day 6: Spend the day at Tshabalala Wildlife Sanctuary and see various animal species.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the city’s vibrant nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Bulawayo

Bulawayo has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects the city’s diverse cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sadza - a traditional Zimbabwean dish made of maize meal and served with a variety of meat and vegetable stews.
  • Matemba - a smoked fish dish that is popular in the region.
  • Mopani worms - a local delicacy that is high in protein and often served with peanut butter and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Chibuku - a traditional Zimbabwean beer made from fermented maize or sorghum.
  • Mazoe - a popular fruit juice made from local fruits.

Where to Stay in Bulawayo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bulawayo has numerous accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bulawayo:

  • The Nesbitt Castle - a luxurious hotel that offers guests a chance to experience the city’s colonial history.
  • Holiday Inn Bulawayo - a budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • The Musketeers Lodge - a family-friendly guesthouse that offers a tranquil and peaceful environment.

What to Buy in Bulawayo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that visitors can buy in Bulawayo as a keepsake of their trip. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Handmade baskets and pottery made by local artisans
  • Carved wooden sculptures depicting African wildlife
  • Traditional clothing made from local fabrics such as Shweshwe

Local Culture and Customs of Bulawayo

Bulawayo is a diverse city with a rich cultural heritage that is worth exploring. As a visitor, it’s important to respect the local customs and culture. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Greeting people with a handshake is customary.
  • When entering a household, it is courteous to remove your shoes.
  • Keep in mind that homosexuality is illegal in Zimbabwe, and public displays of affection are not common.

Best Time to Visit Bulawayo

The best time to visit Bulawayo is between the months of May and September when the weather is cooler and drier. However, visitors can still enjoy Bulawayo’s attractions throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bulawayo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Keep a respectful and open-minded attitude towards locals.

Don’t:

  • Engage with wild animals without proper guidance and supervision.
  • Take photographs without asking permission.
  • Engage in any illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bulawayo

Bulawayo is generally safe for tourists, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take common sense precautions to avoid petty theft, such as avoiding carrying large amounts of cash or wearing expensive jewelry.

Health in Bulawayo

Visitors should consult with their physician before traveling to Bulawayo, as some vaccines are recommended. It’s also advised to drink bottled water and avoid street food to avoid getting sick from contaminated food.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Bulawayo

Visitors can explore Bulawayo’s attractions using public transportation or by hiring a taxi. It is also recommended to hire a car to fully experience the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Bulawayo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bulawayo is located in a region rich in tourist destinations, and there are several nearby destinations worth exploring. Here are some recommended nearby excursions:

  • Victoria Falls - a UNESCO World Heritage Site located a few hours’ drive from Bulawayo.
  • Mana Pools National Park - a wildlife reserve located on the Zambezi River that offers incredible safari experiences.
  • Chobe National Park - a game reserve located in Botswana that offers visitors an opportunity to see a wide variety of animals up close.

Conclusion

Bulawayo is a destination worth exploring, with its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and vibrant art and culture. This guide provides visitors with all they need to plan their trip and explore the best that Bulawayo has to offer. Plan your itinerary, pack your bags, and prepare to experience a unique Zimbabwean adventure in Bulawayo!
