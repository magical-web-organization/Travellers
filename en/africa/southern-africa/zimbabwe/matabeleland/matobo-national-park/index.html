Introduction to Matobo National Park

Matobo National Park is a stunning destination located in the heart of Southern Africa, in Zimbabwe’s Matabeleland region. Covering over 44,500 hectares, the park is blessed with an incredible range of wildlife, stunning landscapes, and amazing cultural sites.

The park is named after the Matobo Hills, a series of magnificent granite formations that are estimated to be over 2 billion years old. These hills are one of the park’s main attractions, but the area also boasts an incredible range of flora and fauna, as well as culturally important sites.

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural tours, or just soaking up the stunning landscapes, Matobo National Park is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Matobo National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Matobo National Park Museum: This small but informative museum provides visitors with a comprehensive overview of the park’s history, geology, and cultural significance.

  • World’s View: This incredible site provides stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and is believed to have been a favorite spot of Cecil Rhodes.

  • Ndebele Village: Learn about the local Ndebele culture and traditions by visiting this traditional village. Visitors can watch demonstrations of traditional arts and crafts and learn about the history and culture of this fascinating people.

Natural Attractions of Matobo National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rhino Walk: Join a guided walk to see the park’s incredible population of white and black rhinos up close.

  • Dwala Caves: Explore the park’s stunning cave system, filled with ancient rock paintings and carvings that date back thousands of years.

  • Maleme Dam: Take a relaxing boat ride on the dam and enjoy the stunning scenery and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matobo National Park

  • Toghwana Dam Trail: A challenging hike that leads you to the park’s famous Toghwana Dam.

  • The Rock Art Trail: A scenic hike that takes you to some of the park’s most impressive rock art sites.

  • World’s View Trail: A leisurely hike that leads you to the park’s famous World’s View site, where you can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Matobo National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Game Drives: Take a guided game drive to see the park’s incredible wildlife up close, from elephants and giraffes to lions and leopards.

  • Birdwatching: Matobo National Park is home to over 350 species of birds, making it an ideal destination for birdwatchers.

  • Cultural Tours: Learn about the park’s rich cultural heritage by taking a guided tour of the local villages and historical sites.

Food and Drink Scene of Matobo National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sadza: A staple food made from maize meal and often served with vegetables and meat dishes.

  • Biltong: A South African-style beef jerky that is a popular snack throughout the region.

  • Chakalaka: A spicy vegetable dish that is often served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Chibuku: A sour maize beer that is a popular drink throughout Zimbabwe.

  • Rooibos: A traditional South African tea that is known for its health benefits.

Where to Stay in Matobo National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Amalinda Lodge: A luxurious lodge that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and a range of amenities, including a spa and restaurant.

  • Big Cave: This eco-friendly lodge is built into the rocky hills and offers a unique and immersive experience.

  • Camp Amalinda: A tented camp that offers comfortable accommodations and stunning views of the park.

What to Buy in Matobo National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beadwork: The local Ndebele people are known for their intricate beadwork, which makes for a unique and colorful souvenir.

  • Woodcarvings: Hand-carved wooden sculptures and other crafts are a popular souvenir in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Matobo National Park

The local Ndebele people have a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the region. It is important to respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and customs around food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Matobo National Park

The best time to visit Matobo National Park is during the dry season, which runs from June to October. This is the best time for game viewing and outdoor activities, as the weather is mild and there is minimal rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matobo National Park

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: This includes dress codes and customs around food and drink.

  • Take precautions against malaria: The park is located in a high-risk malaria area, so it is important to take precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife: Feeding the wildlife can disrupt their natural behavior and can be dangerous for both visitors and animals.

  • Litter: It is important to take care of the park’s natural environment by not littering and disposing of waste responsibly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matobo National Park

While Matobo National Park is a safe destination for travelers, it is important to take precautions such as not walking alone at night and following park rules and regulations.

Health in Matobo National Park

The park is located in a high-risk malaria area, so it is important to take precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants. Visitors should also ensure that they are up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella, and should consider getting vaccinated for hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Matobo National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matobo National Park

The nearest international airport to Matobo National Park is Joshua Mqabuko Nkomo International Airport, located in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe.

Bus Stations Near Matobo National Park

There are several bus stations in Bulawayo, including the Bulawayo City Centre Bus Station and the Bulawayo Main Bus Station, which offer connections to other destinations in Zimbabwe and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange private transfers or rent a car to travel to Matobo National Park.

How to Get Around in Matobo National Park

The park is easily explored by car, and visitors can also arrange guided tours and transfers through their accommodations.

Where to Go Next from Matobo National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hwange National Park: Zimbabwe’s largest national park, home to an incredible range of wildlife including elephants and lions.

  • Great Zimbabwe: A UNESCO World Heritage site that provides insight into the region’s fascinating history and culture.

Conclusion

Matobo National Park is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. From its stunning landscapes and incredible wildlife to its rich cultural heritage, there is something here to appeal to every traveler. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural enrichment, Matobo National Park is a destination that delivers.
