Introduction to Matabeleland

Matabeleland is a region located in southern Zimbabwe, known for its rich cultural and natural heritage. The region is home to the Ndebele people, who have a unique history and culture that is worth exploring. Visitors can expect warm hospitality, stunning landscapes, and a variety of outdoor activities in Matabeleland.

List of Places to Visit in Matabeleland

Here are some of the must-visit places in Matabeleland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National Museum of Zimbabwe - Bulawayo
  2. Mzilikazi Arts and Crafts Centre - Bulawayo
  3. Khami Ruins - Bulawayo
  4. Tshabalala Game Sanctuary - Bulawayo
  5. Railway Museum - Bulawayo
  6. Matobo Hills - Matobo

Natural Attractions of Matabeleland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Matobo National Park - Matobo
  2. Hwange National Park - Hwange
  3. Victoria Falls - Victoria Falls

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matabeleland

Local Culture and Customs of Matabeleland

Visitors to Matabeleland should be respectful of the local Ndebele culture and customs. The Ndebele people are known for their vibrant and colorful art, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Matabeleland

The best time to visit Matabeleland is during the dry season (June to September) when the weather is mild, and wildlife is easier to spot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matabeleland

Do:

  1. Be respectful of the local culture and customs
  2. Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites
  3. Buy locally made products to support the community

Don’t:

  1. Take photos without permission
  2. Be disrespectful to elders or community leaders
  3. Damage or remove cultural artifacts

Safety and Health

Safety in Matabeleland

Matabeleland is a generally safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. It is advised to avoid walking alone at night and to use a reputable taxi service.

Health in Matabeleland

Yellow fever vaccination is required for travelers coming from certain countries. It is also recommended to take precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases. Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent illnesses.
