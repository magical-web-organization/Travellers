Introduction to Moremi Game Reserve

Moremi Game Reserve is a protected area located in the Okavango-Chobe region of Botswana, Southern Africa. It is one of the top wildlife destinations in Africa, and the first wildlife sanctuary in Africa to be created by an indigenous tribe. Spanning over 5000 square kilometers, the park offers a range of habitats that support diverse wildlife, including elephants, lions, leopards, rhinos, buffalos, and a variety of antelopes.

List of Places to Visit in Moremi Game Reserve

There are numerous attractions to explore in Moremi Game Reserve, including culturally important sites, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. Here are just a few:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khwai village: A traditional village that offers an opportunity to learn about the local culture and way of life.
  • Seronga village: Another traditional village where visitors can experience local customs and traditions.
  • Chief’s Island: The largest island in the Okavango Delta, and a place of cultural and historical importance.

Natural Attractions of Moremi Game Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Xakanaxa: A popular area for game drives and spotting wildlife, including elephants and buffalo.
  • Third Bridge: A scenic area for birdwatching and observing hippos and crocodiles.
  • Mboma Island: A picturesque location for boat safaris and photography opportunities.
  • Moremi Game Reserve Safari: Guides offer breathtaking views of this wildlife-rich reserve as you wind your way through lush forested areas teeming with life.
  • Moremi Game Reserve Scenic Flights: For those looking to see the park from a different perspective, scenic flights offer an unforgettable experience with breathtaking aerial views of the Okavango Delta.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moremi Game Reserve

With dense vegetation and large wildlife populations, hiking and biking in Moremi Game Reserve may not be allowed, as it can be dangerous. However, guided walks are available with experienced guides to explore the park on foot in a safe and secure environment.

Best Things to Do in Moremi Game Reserve: Activities and Tours

  • Game drives: One of the most common activities, game drives offer the opportunity to observe wildlife at close range, as well as the park’s stunning scenery.
  • Boat safaris: An ideal way to explore the Okavango Delta and its waterways.
  • Mokoro rides: Authentic dugout canoe rides through the delta which allows you to explore the winding waterways in peace and tranquility.
  • Walking safaris: Led by experienced guides, walking safaris provide a unique opportunity to track wildlife on foot, in a safe and controlled environment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moremi Game Reserve

A week in Moremi Game Reserve provides plenty of time to explore the park’s diverse offerings.

  • Day 1 & 2: Arrive at the park, set up camp, and go on game drives to explore the park like Khwai village and Seronga village.
  • Day 3 & 4: Take a boat or mokoro ride, and visit Mboma Island for birdwatching or see Third Bridge for some nature photography.
  • Day 5 & 6: Go on a guided walking safari to track wildlife on foot and experience the park up close.
  • Day 7: On the last day, take a scenic flight for breathtaking aerial views of the Okavango Delta.

Food and Drink Scene of Moremi Game Reserve

The park mostly operates on self-catering. It is advisable to carry your own food and drinks. There are a few food markets and stores within the borders of Moremi Game Reserve, but for a bigger selection, it’s recommended to search in nearby towns.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seswaa: A traditional Botswana dish made of slow-cooked beef and served with pap (maize porridge).
  • Vetkoek: A popular snack consisting of deep-fried dough stuffed with a variety of fillings, from jam to curried mince.
  • Chobi Chicken: A local delicacy made with tender chicken pieces cooked in a traditional pot.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The most popular local beer is St Louis, followed by Castle.
  • Local spirits: Mampoer and Amarula are two popular local spirits.

Where to Stay in Moremi Game Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moremi Game Reserve offers several accommodation options, ranging from luxury lodges to campsites. Visitors can choose to stay inside the park or outside, with the latter being more affordable. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Camp Moremi: A luxurious tented camp with a swimming pool and a private concession.
  • Third Bridge Camp: An affordable camping and self-catering option, located on an island in the delta.
  • Xomae Group Camp: This is a private campsite that provides game viewing opportunities for guests.

What to Buy in Moremi Game Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts: Traditional basket weaving and pottery are among the most popular handicrafts available in the park.
  • Authentic with Jewelry: A popular souvenir amongst visitors to the region.
  • Traditional Cloth: Known as Shweshwe, this is an iconic South African textile and is sold at most parks’ curio shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Moremi Game Reserve

The people of Moremi Game Reserve are overwhelmingly Tswana, one of Botswana’s largest ethnic groups. Visitors are encouraged to respect Tswana culture and customs, including avoiding sensitive topics like politics, and removing shoes before entering homes or places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Moremi Game Reserve

The best time to visit Moremi Game Reserve is during the dry season (May to October), as this is when wildlife congregates around water sources and is more accessible to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moremi Game Reserve

Do:

  • Do hire local guides and support the local economy.
  • Do respect wildlife and their habitat by keeping a safe distance.
  • Do adhere to park rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the park.
  • Don’t feed the animals.
  • Don’t pick flowers or disturb vegetation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moremi Game Reserve

The park has restricted access to specific areas deemed too dangerous for tourists. Visitors should be careful when venturing off into areas that have not been designated safe for tourists. It is also vital to keep safety procedures in mind when it comes to wildlife encounters to avoid unwanted conflict.

Health in Moremi Game Reserve

Malaria is prevalent in the area, and tourists should get vaccinated and take prophylaxis medication well in advance. Tsetse flies are also common and can spread Sleeping Sickness in case of bites. Be sure to protect yourself and wear appropriate protective gear to avoid bites.

Getting to Moremi Game Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

The park is accessible via Maun, the nearest town with an airport. From Maun, visitors can take either a private or a scheduled tour, in addition to self-driving.

Airports Near Moremi Game Reserve

  • Maun International Airport: The nearest airport to Moremi Game Reserve, located about 90 km from the park. Flights to Johannesburg, Cape Town, and other major cities in South Africa are available from Maun.
  • Kasane International Airport: The secondary airport located in Chobe, Botswana, a few hours away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Moremi Game Reserve

The park has no bus stations. Still, visitors can take a bus ride from Gaborone or Johannesburg to Maun, where they can pick up rental cars or hop onto charters and tours.

Other Options

Self-driving is a popular option amongst visitors to Moremi Game Reserve, but it is recommended to rent a 4x4 vehicle, as the roads can be challenging to navigate.

How to Get Around in Moremi Game Reserve

There are numerous means of getting around Moremi Game Reserve, including guided game drives, boat safaris, and walking tours. Visitors can take either self-guided or expert-guided tours with options in the language of the visitor’s choice.

Where to Go Next from Moremi Game Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Moremi Game Reserve is relatively isolated, with Maun being the nearest town. Still, visitors can explore other parks around the region such as Chobe National Park, Makgadikgadi Pans National Park, and Central Kalahari Game Reserve.

Conclusion

Moremi Game Reserve offers a one-of-a-kind opportunity to experience wildlife up close and personal, in a beautiful and expansive protected area. With so much to see and do, visitors to this exciting park are sure to have a memorable visit. Remember to protect and respect the wildlife and their environment while immersing yourself in the park’s beauty by studying the ecosystem and following the wildlife’s behaviors.
