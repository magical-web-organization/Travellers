Introduction to Okavango-Chobe

Okavango-Chobe is a popular tourist destination located in the northern part of Botswana, Southern Africa. It is home to diverse wildlife, beautiful natural landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage. The region comprises two main areas: the Okavango Delta, a unique wetland ecosystem; and Chobe National Park, which boasts some of the best game viewing experiences in Africa. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive tour guide for Okavango-Chobe, including a list of places to visit, things to do, and tips for your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Okavango-Chobe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Makgadikgadi Pans: This ancient lake bed is home to one of the largest salt pans in the world. Visitors can explore the ruins and learn about the region’s history and culture.

  • Kasane: A small town located near Chobe National Park, it is a popular starting point for a safari adventure.

  • San People: The San people are an indigenous tribe that has lived in the area for thousands of years. Visitors can experience their way of life, including hunting and gathering techniques, dances, and music.

  • Tsodilo Hills: These ancient rock formations are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and hold significant religious and cultural value for the San people.

Natural Attractions of Okavango-Chobe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okavango Delta: A unique wetland ecosystem that is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, buffalo, lions, and leopards. Visitors can explore the delta on a mokoro (traditional canoe) or take a walking safari.

  • Chobe National Park: One of the best game viewing destinations in Africa. Home to elephants, lions, leopards, cheetahs, wild dogs, and over 400 bird species. Visitors can take a game drive or a river safari to see the wildlife up close.

  • Moremi Game Reserve: The first wildlife reserve in Botswana and a prime location for wildlife viewing, bird watching, and scenic drives.

  • Victoria Falls: While not technically part of Okavango-Chobe, Victoria Falls is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the region. Located on the border of Zimbabwe and Zambia, the falls are one of the largest and most spectacular waterfalls in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okavango-Chobe

  • Chobe Riverfront: A scenic drive along the Chobe River, where visitors can spot wildlife and birdlife.

  • Khama Rhino Sanctuary: A community-driven wildlife sanctuary where visitors can go on self-guided walking trails or arrange guided game drives and night drives.

  • Susunia Hill: A challenging hiking trail that rewards visitors with stunning views of the Okavango Delta.

Best Things to Do in Okavango-Chobe: Activities and Tours

  • Game Viewing: Take a game drive, river safari, or walking safari to see the diverse range of wildlife in the area.

  • Mokoro Safari: Experience the Okavango Delta’s unique ecosystem on a traditional mokoro canoe.

  • Bird Watching: Okavango-Chobe is home to over 400 bird species, making it a paradise for bird watchers.

  • Cultural Experiences: Learn about Botswana’s rich culture and history by visiting museums and cultural centers, or experiencing the way of life of the San people.

Food and Drink Scene of Okavango-Chobe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seswaa: A traditional Botswana dish made of beef or goat meat and served with pap (maize porridge).

  • Morogo: A traditional vegetable dish made of various indigenous greens.

  • Vetkoek: A type of fried bread that is often filled with savory or sweet fillings.

Local Beverages

  • Mopane Worm: A popular local delicacy that is served as a crunchy snack or used as a garnish for dishes.

  • Milk Tart: A creamy dessert made of milk, eggs, and sugar.

  • Chibuku: A popular locally brewed beer that is made from maize and sorghum.

Where to Stay in Okavango-Chobe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chobe Safari Lodge: Located on the banks of the Chobe River, this lodge offers luxury accommodation and stunning views of the river.

  • Ngoma Safari Lodge: A luxury lodge located in Chobe National Park, offering stunning views of the floodplains and wildlife.

  • Backpackers Paradise: An affordable and friendly hostel located in Kasane, offering comfortable dorms and private rooms.

  • Sango Safari Camp: A luxurious tented camp located in Khwai Private Reserve, offering guided safaris and scenic views of the Okavango Delta.

What to Buy in Okavango-Chobe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wood Carvings: Hand-carved wooden sculptures and products are a popular souvenir in Botswana.

  • Beadwork: Intricately beaded jewelry, baskets, and other decorative items are commonly found in local markets.

  • Crafts: Handmade crafts such as pottery, textiles, and paintings are a great way to bring a piece of Botswana home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Okavango-Chobe

  • Respect for Elders: In Botswana, elders are highly respected and considered the wise and elderly leaders of the community.

  • Traditional Dress: Many tribes in Botswana wear traditional clothing as a symbol of their heritage and customs.

  • Greetings: Handshakes are the standard form of greeting in Botswana. It is also customary to ask about one’s well-being and family before engaging in small talk.

Best Time to Visit Okavango-Chobe

The best time to visit Okavango-Chobe is during the dry season (May to October), when the weather is mild, and wildlife is abundant. However, this is also the peak season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices. The wet season (November to April) is a good time for bird watching, but roads can be impassable due to heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okavango-Chobe

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting cultural sites or interacting with local communities.

  • Carry plenty of water and sunscreen during outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb wildlife.

  • Take photos or videos of locals without their permission.

  • Engage in illegal or unethical activities, such as poaching or littering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Okavango-Chobe

Botswana is a relatively safe country, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take necessary precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Okavango-Chobe

Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations for diseases such as malaria, typhoid, and hepatitis A and B. It is also recommended to carry a basic medical kit and purchase travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Okavango-Chobe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okavango-Chobe

The main airport near Okavango-Chobe is Kasane Airport (BBK), which offers domestic flights to major cities in Botswana.

Bus Stations Near Okavango-Chobe

There are several bus stations in Botswana, including Gaborone Bus Station and Francistown Bus Station. From these stations, visitors can take a local bus or minibus to Kasane.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car and drive to Kasane or join a guided tour that includes transportation.

How to Get Around in Okavango-Chobe

The best way to get around Okavango-Chobe is by hiring a local guide or taking a guided tour. This ensures you have an experienced driver who can navigate the roads and wildlife safely.

Where to Go Next from Okavango-Chobe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Namibia: A neighboring country with stunning natural landscapes, such as the Skeleton Coast and Sossusvlei Dunes.

  • Zimbabwe: Home to the world-famous Victoria Falls and the historic town of Bulawayo.

  • South Africa: A neighboring country with diverse wildlife, world-class wineries, and cosmopolitan cities.

Conclusion

Okavango-Chobe is a unique and exciting travel destination that has something for everyone. Whether you are a nature lover, cultural enthusiast, or adventure seeker, the region’s diverse attractions and activities are sure to please. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip with confidence and make the most of your time in Okavango-Chobe.
