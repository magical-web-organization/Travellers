Introduction to Okavango Delta

Okavango Delta is a unique and highly diverse wetland system located in Okavango-Chobe, Botswana, Southern Africa. This wetland system covers an area of about 15,000 square kilometers and is made up of channels, lagoons, and islands that are home to remarkable flora and fauna. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Natural Wonders of Africa. This guide will provide you with all the essential information to make your trip to Okavango Delta a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Okavango Delta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Okavango Delta Cultural and Research Centre: The center showcases the culture and history of the Okavango Delta.

  2. The Tsodilo Hills: The hills have rock paintings that date back to the Stone Age.

  3. Lekhubu Island: The island has many artifacts from ancient times.

Natural Attractions of Okavango Delta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Okavango Delta Wildlife Reserve: Home to many exotic wildlife species like elephants, buffalos, giraffes, lions, and cheetahs.

  2. Moremi Game Reserve: One of the most beautiful and scenic regions of the Okavango Delta that offers game drives, guided walks, and boat safaris.

  3. Chobe National Park: A tourist hotspot known for its large elephant populations, lions, leopards, and other big-game animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okavango Delta

  1. Xugana Island Lodge: It offers walking and guided trekking trails that range from a few hours to a complete day.

  2. Kanana Camp: A place that offers bike tours through the delta and various walking trails.

  3. Nxabega Okavango Tented Camp: Offers private walks and guided biking tours.

Best Things to Do in Okavango Delta: Activities and Tours

  1. Game Drive at Moremi Game Reserve: The best way to see the wildlife of Okavango Delta is by taking a game drive.

  2. Boat Safari at the Okavango Delta Wildlife Reserve: Take an early morning or late afternoon boat safari to get closer to the wildlife.

  3. Mokoro Safari: A traditional dug-out canoe safari is an incredible way to explore the beauty of Okavango Delta.

Food and Drink Scene of Okavango Delta

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beef Potjie: A traditional stew made of beef, vegetables, and spices cooked over an open fire.

  2. Seswaa: A traditional Botswana dish made from beef that is slow-cooked and served with a maize meal pap.

  3. Vetkoek: A pastry dough filled with curried mince or jam.

Local Beverages

  1. Chibuku: A traditional sorghum beer.

  2. Majekete: A non-alcoholic drink made from hibiscus leaves.

  3. South African Wine: South African wines are widely available at lodges and campsites.

Where to Stay in Okavango Delta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Xugana Island Lodge: A luxury lodge that offers game drives, walking and trekking trails, and water-based activities.

  2. Kanana Camp: A campsite that offers outdoor activities like bike tours, walking trails, and boat safaris.

  3. Nxabega Okavango Tented Camp: A campsite that offers a variety of outdoor activities like walking trails, bike tours, and private walks.

What to Buy in Okavango Delta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Traditional Handicrafts: An extensive collection of handicrafts made from wood, stone, and other natural materials.

  2. Wild Animal Souvenirs: African masks, sculptures, and other souvenirs made from wood or soapstone.

  3. Local Art and Paintings: A wide range of art and paintings that showcases the beauty of Okavango Delta.

Local Culture and Customs of Okavango Delta

The people of Okavango Delta have a rich culture and tradition. Most people practice subsistence farming and fishing. Respect for elders is an essential facet of their culture.

Best Time to Visit Okavango Delta

The best time to visit the Okavango Delta is during the dry season from May to October. This is the period when the wildlife converges at the available water sources.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okavango Delta

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and dress conservatively.

  2. Engage in outdoor activities only in the company of a knowledgeable guide.

  3. Follow the instructions of your guide during game drives and boat safaris.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter in the park.

  2. Don’t attempt to approach animals on foot.

  3. Don’t feed the animals.

Safety and Health in Okavango Delta

Safety in Okavango Delta

  1. Be aware of your surroundings and do not walk alone at night.

  2. Keep a safe distance from wildlife.

  3. Ensure you have adequate travel insurance before your trip.

Health in Okavango Delta

  1. Take precautions against malaria by taking medication and protecting yourself from mosquito bites.

  2. Drink only bottled water or water that has been properly filtered or boiled.

  3. Ensure you have adequate medical insurance before your trip.

Getting to Okavango Delta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okavango Delta

  1. Maun International Airport: The biggest and most popular airport that serves Okavango Delta.

  2. Kasane Airport: The airport serves the Northern region of Okavango Delta.

Bus Stations Near Okavango Delta

  1. Johannesburg - Maun Bus Station: The station offers direct bus services to Maun.

  2. Windhoek - Maun Bus Station: The station offers direct bus services from Windhoek to Maun.

Other Options

  1. Private transfer service: Offers a more personalized experience.

  2. Car rental: Perfect for independent travelers.

How to Get Around in Okavango Delta

  1. Walking and trekking trails: Ideal for exploring small areas and taking in the scenery.

  2. Biking Tours: Ideal for more extended tours and for exploring larger parts of the Delta.

  3. Boat safaris: Perfect for exploring the Delta’s waterways and seeing wildlife in their natural habitat.

Where to Go Next from Okavango Delta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Zambezi River: Take an excursion and experience one of the world’s great rivers.

  2. Victoria Falls: Visit the awe-inspiring Victoria Falls, one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.

Conclusion

Okavango Delta is a unique and remarkable destination that offers breathtaking scenery, exotic wildlife, and a rich cultural experience. With this guide, you can plan your trip efficiently and make the most of your Okavango Delta experience.
