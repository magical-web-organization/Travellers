Travel Guide to Manzini, Eswatini: All You Need to Know

Manzini is a city located in the northern region of Eswatini, in Southern Africa. With its bustling markets, lively culture, and breathtaking natural beauty, Manzini is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Eswatini. In this guide, we will take you on a virtual tour of Manzini and help you plan your dream vacation with tips on where to go, what to do, what to eat, and how to get around.

Introduction to Manzini

Manzini is the second most populous city in Eswatini after the capital, Mbabane. It is also the country’s commercial and industrial center, with a vibrant market scene and bustling streets filled with shops and restaurants selling traditional wares and delicious food. Visitors to Manzini can expect a warm and friendly welcome, with locals eager to share their cultural heritage and natural wonders with travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Manzini

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a food lover, Manzini has something to offer everyone. Here is a list of must-visit attractions to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • King Sobhuza II Memorial Park: a tribute to the former king of Eswatini, with exhibits on his life and reign, as well as a mausoleum where he is buried
  • Mantenga Cultural Village: a living museum that showcases traditional Swazi culture, with demonstrations of dance, music, and crafts
  • Eswatini National Museum: a museum that houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits on the history and culture of Eswatini

Natural Attractions of Manzini: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malolotja Nature Reserve: a protected wilderness area that boasts stunning scenery, hiking trails, and wildlife such as mountain zebras, baboons, and eagles
  • Hawane Nature Reserve: a wildlife sanctuary that allows visitors to see animals such as eland, zebras, wildebeest, and giraffes in their natural habitat
  • Maguga Dam: a man-made lake with a picturesque dam wall that offers great views and photo opportunities

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manzini

  • The Hlane Royal National Park: A park with excellent mountain bike and hiking trails, it is a wild, rugged wilderness with a healthy population of lion, elephant, and white rhino

Best Things to Do in Manzini: Activities and Tours

  • Walking tour of Manzini’s bustling markets and shops: take a stroll through Manzini’s vibrant markets and shops and discover traditional Swazi crafts, clothing, and food
  • Cultural Tour to Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary: A guided tour of the sanctuary, and activities like hiking, horse riding and mountain biking.
  • Safari at Hlane Royal National Park: An open-vehicle game drive through the park’s wilderness area to spot the famous big five (elephant, lion, rhino, leopard, and buffalo)

Week-Long Itinerary for Manzini

Day 1: King Sobhuza II Memorial Park, Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary Day 2: Eswatini National Museum, Mantenga Cultural Village Day 3: Hawane Nature Reserve, Malolotja Nature Reserve Day 4-5: Hike the Hlane Royal National Park Day 6: Maguga Dam, Walking tour of Manzini’s bustling markets and shops Day 7: Safari at Hlane Royal National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Manzini

Swazi cuisine is a mix of South African, Zulu, and European influences, with dishes such as grilled meats, stews, and pap (a type of cornmeal porridge). Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample while in Manzini:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braaivleis (barbecue meat)
  • Umngqusho (dish made from corn and beans)
  • Potjiekos (stew cooked in a cast-iron pot)
  • Bunny Chow (a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry)

Local Beverages

  • Sibebe Premium Lager: a local beer brewed in Eswatini using locally sourced ingredients
  • Amarula: a creamy liqueur made from the fruit of the marula tree, a native plant to Africa

Where to Stay in Manzini: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manzini has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Manzini:

  • The George Hotel: a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Manzini, with elegant rooms and fine dining
  • Lugogo Sun Hotel: a mid-range hotel with excellent facilities, including a swimming pool, bar, and restaurant
  • Ekhaya Guesthouse: a budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a relaxed, homely atmosphere

What to Buy in Manzini: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Manzini’s markets are the perfect place to find unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some suggestions:

  • Traditional Swazi crafts, such as woven baskets, beaded jewelry, and woodcarvings
  • Local foods such as biltong (dried meat), dried fruit, and Swazi honey
  • Local textiles, such as colorful printed fabrics and shawls.

Local Culture and Customs of Manzini

Eswatini has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through music, dance, and art. Visitors to Manzini can experience this culture firsthand by attending traditional ceremonies and festivals such as the Umhlanga Reed Dance. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and traditions, particularly regarding dress code and behavior in public places.

Best Time to Visit Manzini

The best time to visit Manzini is during the dry season from May to October, which offers cooler temperatures and the best wildlife viewing opportunities. The wet season from November to April can be hot and humid, with heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manzini

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local food and drinks
  • Bargain for goods and services in the markets

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are frowned upon in Swazi culture
  • Take or use photos without asking for permission
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Manzini

Eswatini is generally a safe and stable country, with low rates of crime against tourists. Visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuable belongings safe.

Health in Manzini

Visitors to Manzini are advised to take measures to protect themselves against common health risks such as malaria and typhoid fever. It is recommended to speak to your doctor before traveling and to obtain necessary vaccines and medication.

Getting to Manzini: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manzini

Eswatini does not have its own international airport, the closest airport is King Mswati III International Airport (KMIII) located in Manzini.

Bus Stations Near Manzini

Manzini is connected to many cities in Southern Africa by bus, with regular services from Johannesburg, Maputo, and Durban.

Other Options

Driving from Johannesburg or Nelspruit is also an option. Visitors to Manzini can rent a car from a reputable car rental company in South Africa or Eswatini.

How to Get Around in Manzini

The easiest and most convenient way to get around Manzini is by car. There are also taxis and buses available but bear in mind the city could be difficult to navigate.

Where to Go Next from Manzini: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When visiting Manzini, there are several nearby destinations that are worth a visit. A few suggestions are:

  • Mbabane: Capital city and cultural center of Eswatini, home to museums, markets, and traditional Swazi crafts
  • Ezulwini Valley: A picturesque valley that is home to resorts, restaurants, and casinos
  • Kruger National Park: Located just on the other side of the border with South Africa, it is one of the world’s leading wildlife reserve.

Conclusion

Manzini is a city that has something for every kind of traveler, from adventure seekers and nature lovers to history buffs and foodies. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope to inspire you to explore the unique culture, natural beauty, and vibrant life of Manzini, Eswatini. Plan your dream vacation now and make unforgettable memories in this beautiful country!
