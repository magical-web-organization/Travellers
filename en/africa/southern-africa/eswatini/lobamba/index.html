Introduction to Lobamba

Located in the southern part of Africa, Lobamba is the traditional and legislative capital of Eswatini. It is an ancient city that has been inhabited for more than 200 years and is known for its cultural heritage and natural beauty. With its fascinating history, scenic views, and rich culture, Lobamba is a must-visit destination for any traveler who wants to explore Africa.

List of Places to Visit in Lobamba

To help you make the most out of your visit to Lobamba, here’s a list of top attractions that you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lobamba Royal Village - This place is the official residence of King Mswati III and is an important cultural site that showcases the traditional way of life of Swazi people. It features traditional huts, a royal kraal, and the Royal Cemetery.

  • Swazi Cultural Village - This living museum is a popular attraction that displays and educates visitors on Swazi tradition, culture, and lifestyle through interactive events, such as dancing, singing, and artwork.

  • Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary - This beautiful wildlife sanctuary is home to an array of animals like zebras, giraffes, and warthogs. It is a significant cultural site as well, with the remains of the Iron Age and traditional Swazi homesteads.

Natural Attractions of Lobamba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mantenga Nature Reserve - This nature reserve is a great spot to explore nature, and you will get to see beautiful birds and animals like birds of prey, antelope, and more while enjoying a scenic view.

  • Maguga Dam - Located just outside the city center, this dam is a beautiful landscape that provides peaceful relaxation and stunning views of surrounding mountains.

  • Mlawula Nature Reserve - This reserve is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and adventurous challenges. Here, you can hike through towering peaks, flowing rivers, and see wildlife, such as baboons and antelopes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lobamba

Lobamba is a great place for those who love an outdoor adventure. The city has some of the best hiking and biking routes, as the natural landscape is both appealing and challenging to travelers. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Lugogo Mountain: This mountain is popular among hikers as it offers a great challenge and stunning views at its peak.

  • Sibebe Rock: This enormous granite rock is the world’s second-largest monolith and is a popular hiking spot for adventurous hikers.

  • Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary: The wildlife sanctuary has a diverse range of hiking trails that cater for different skill levels, and visitors will see wildlife during their hike through the park.

Best Things to Do in Lobamba: Activities and Tours

There’s always something to do in Lobamba. Whether you enjoy an outdoor adventure or would rather have a taste of the city’s cultural heritage, there is something for everyone. Here are some must-do activities and tours:

  • Traditional dance performances: You can watch traditional Swazi dance performances at Swazi Cultural Village and other venues. These are lively and colorful performances that are an essential part of Swazi culture.

  • Wildlife safaris: You can enjoy guided wildlife tours through various wildlife reserves around the city, such as Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary and Mlawula Nature Reserve, that will give you the chance to see a diverse range of animals.

  • Canopy Tours: Enjoy the thrill of zip-lining with scenic views of the forest and nature around you.

  • Horse Riding: Horse Riding in Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary is a unique way to see swazi culture, wildlife, and birdlife.

  • Visit local Markets: Markets such as the Manzini market and Lobamba craft market have a wide variety of swazi souvenirs to bring back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Lobamba

Lobamba has diverse food and drink scenes due to its rich cultural heritage. From local cuisine, international dishes, local beer, and imported beverages, there is always something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biltong: a south african dried meat usually made of beef or game, which is a famous snack in Lobamba.
  • Siswati cuisine: Siswati cuisine includes dishes such as phutu pap or maize, roasted meat, imithiyane/hoppolo or cowpeas, sisidibo, and more.

Local Beverages

  • Sibebe Beer: A local beer brewed in Mbabane, Eswatini, and made out of locally grown maize and hops.

  • Makhewu: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from maize or sorghum, popular among locals.

  • Tshwala: A traditional beer brewed mainly from maize, it is also famous among the people and is part of their culture.

Where to Stay in Lobamba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lobamba offers various accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious, catering to individual travelers, families, or groups. Here are some top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city:

  • Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary Rest Camp: This wildlife sanctuary is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable accommodation in huts or cottages with stunning views of the wildlife sanctuary.

  • Lidwala Backpackers: This hostel is located in a beautiful environment and offers a comfortable stay with fellow travelers.

  • Royal Swazi Spa: This luxurious lodge offers world-class amenities, such as a golf course, a spa, and several dining options.

What to Buy in Lobamba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lobamba has many souvenir shops, markets, and craft makers that sell unique and authentic Swazi products that are worth taking home. Here are some popular souvenirs and local products to consider buying:

  • Sisal baskets: Made from Sisal, they are usually handmade and come in various shapes and sizes, mostly decorated with bright colors.

  • Woolen rugs and carpets: Woven by rural weavers, these carpets come in different sizes, shapes, and designs.

  • Swazi Candles: These are colorfully handmade candles shaped like animals, birds, and other novelty shapes that make for great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Lobamba

The culture and customs of the Swazi people are prevalent in Lobamba. The people are friendly and welcoming, and it is essential to respect their customs, values, and norms. Here are some vital cultural and customs practices to keep in mind while in Lobamba:

  • Greeting custom: The Swazi people like to greet visitors warmly, so it is recommended to master the basic greetings, such as “Sawubona,” which means “I see you.”

  • Dress code: The Swazi people dress conservatively, so it’s recommended to dress in modest clothing, especially when visiting shrines and cultural sites.

  • Respect the Royal Family: Swazis maintain great respect and admiration for their king, so it’s important to be respectful when interacting with the royal family or visiting royal sites.

  • Do not take pictures without permission: It’s essential to ask for permission before taking pictures, especially when visiting cultural and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Lobamba

Lobamba has a warm and mostly tropical climate, so it is open to visitors all year round. However, the best time to visit is between May and September when the temperatures are cooler and conducive for outdoor activities. These months are considered to be dry months, and the vegetation is also less dense.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lobamba

To ensure an enjoyable visit to Lobamba, here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions
  • Do bring sunscreen and insect repellent if traveling during the summer
  • Do bring comfortable and modest clothing
  • Do carry cash, as some establishments do not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Don’t display physical affection in public
  • Don’t take pictures without seeking permission
  • Don’t make offensive comments or gestures

Safety and Health

While Lobamba is a generally safe destination, visitors should take certain precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Lobamba

  • Always be vigilant when walking alone or in dimly lit areas.
  • Safeguard your valuables and avoid displaying them in public areas.
  • Be cautious when hiring taxis or cars and always confirm that they are from licensed operators.
  • Do not leave your belongings unattended to avoid theft.

Health in Lobamba

  • Ensure that you have updated vaccinations before traveling to Lobamba.
  • Drink bottled water or boiled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.
  • Take precautions against insect-borne diseases by applying insect repellent, wearing long sleeves and pants, and sleeping under insecticide-treated beds nets.

Getting to Lobamba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re flying into Swaziland or driving from neighboring countries like South Africa, there are various transportation modes to get to Lobamba.

Airports Near Lobamba

  • King Mswati III International Airport: Lobamba is about 63 km away from the King Mswati III International airport, which is the nearest airport to Lobamba.

Bus Stations Near Lobamba

  • Manzini Bus Station: Manzini Bus Station is 8 km away from the city center and is a critical transport hub for various routes.

Other Options

  • Renting a Car: Car rentals are widely available and are a convenient option for getting around Lobamba.

How to Get Around in Lobamba

Getting around Lobamba is relatively easy and affordable. Here are some transportation options that you can consider:

  • Local taxis: Local taxis are available in Lobamba, and visitors can use them to move around the city.

  • Car rentals: Rental cars are available, but visitors must have valid driving licenses.

  • Bicycle rentals: Bicycle rentals are also available for those who prefer to explore the city on two wheels.

  • Walking: Visitors can get around Lobamba on foot, as some of the city’s attractions are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Lobamba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have maximized your time in Lobamba, you can explore nearby destinations and excursions such as:

  • Mbabane: The capital city of Eswatini, Mbabane, is just 15km away from Lobamba.

  • Kruger National Park: This world-famous park is about 150km away from Lobamba and is rich in wildlife and landscapes.

Conclusion

Lobamba is a city that is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it the perfect destination for travelers who seek to explore Africa. With so many attractions, activities, and cultural experiences, guests will be amazed at what Lobamba has to offer.
