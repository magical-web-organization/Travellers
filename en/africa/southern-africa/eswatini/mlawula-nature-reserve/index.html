Are you a nature lover looking for a unique African safari experience? Look no further than Mlawula Nature Reserve, a hidden gem nestled in the heart of Eswatini. This reserve boasts breathtaking landscapes, stunning wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. In this guide, we will take you through everything you need to know for your Mlawula adventure.

Introduction to Mlawula Nature Reserve

Mlawula Nature Reserve is a protected area spanning over 16,500 hectares and located in southeastern Eswatini. It was established in 1983 to protect the diverse flora and fauna of the Lubombo Conservancy. The reserve is home to over 95 species of mammals, including giraffes, zebras, and rhinos, as well as over 300 species of birds.

List of Places to Visit in Mlawula Nature Reserve

There are many must-visit places in Mlawula Nature Reserve that will make your trip a memorable experience. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Learning about the history and culture of Eswatini is a must when visiting the Mlawula Nature Reserve. Make sure to visit the following cultural attractions:

  • Emlembe Emcimbini Museum: Built-in 2008, this museum showcases the traditional lifestyles and customs of the Swazi Nation. Visitors here can learn about Swazi cultural practices, including food, music, traditional medicines, and spiritual beliefs.

  • Usuthu Gorge: This is a historical site where the Swazi warriors led by King Sobhuza I defeated the British troops in 1883. Here you can experience a natural scenic view of the surrounding mountains, including the world-famous Lebombo Mountains.

Natural Attractions of Mlawula Nature Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mlawula Nature Reserve boasts some of the most beautiful natural attractions. These include:

  • Siphiso Waterfall: The Siphiso Waterfall is a hidden gem located inside the reserve, surrounded by lush green mountains. A hike to the waterfall takes about an hour, but it’s worth it once you get there.

  • Mbuluzi Gorge: This is a gorgeous gorge with a natural pool perfect for swimming. The Mbuluzi River runs through it, and you can spend hours relaxing and enjoying the sounds of the surrounding nature.

  • Ndzindza Nkulu: Ndzindza Nkulu is a granite outcrop that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It’s an excellent place for a picnic and enjoying the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mlawula Nature Reserve

If you love hiking or biking, Mlawula Nature Reserve has got plenty of options for you. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Nyonyane and Sheba’s Breasts: This route takes you up the Nyonyane mountain, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the Mlawula Nature Reserve. Then, the path continues to Sheba’s Breast, offering even more scenic views of the surrounding environment.

  • Mbuluzi Reserve Circle: This route is an excellent choice for those who prefer biking. It’s a 16 km track that takes three to four hours to complete, covering grasslands, mountains, and riverine forests.

Best Things to Do in Mlawula Nature Reserve: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities to do in Mlawula Nature Reserve. Here are some of our recommended things to do:

  • Game Drives: Game drives are a must-do activity while in Mlawula Reserve. There are experienced guides who can take you on a safari to spot the Big Five game animals ― elephants, lions, leopards, rhinos, and buffalos.

  • Bird-watching: Mlawula Nature Reserve is a haven for bird lovers. You can spot many different bird species in the reserve throughout the year.

  • Visiting the local community: A visit to the local Swazi community is a great way to learn about their culture and traditions. You can book a tour to visit schools, churches, and other cultural sites in the local community.

Food and Drink Scene of Mlawula Nature Reserve

Eswatini’s rich cultural heritage is also present in its food and drinks. These are the standout dishes and drinks that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braai: Braai is a popular dish in Eswatini that features grilled meats, including beef, chicken, and pork.

  • Siswati cuisine: This traditional cuisine includes dishes such as samp and beans, phutu pap, and umnqusho.

Local Beverages

  • Sibebe Beer: This popular beer brewed in Eswatini has a light and smooth taste.

  • Mkhamba: Mkhamba is a traditional Swazi beer made from fermented cornmeal.

Where to Stay in Mlawula Nature Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Mlawula Nature Reserve offers a wide range of accommodation options, depending on your preferences and budget. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Mlawula Nature Reserve Camping: This offers a unique experience of sleeping under the stars in the heart of the reserve.

  • Mlawula Nature Reserve Lodges: There are several lodges located inside the reserve, including Bulembu Lodge, Mlawula Nature Retreat, and Mkhaya Game Reserve.

What to Buy in Mlawula Nature Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from your trip:

  • Traditional crafts: You can purchase handmade baskets, masks, and other items made by the local Swazi community.

  • Local produce: You can buy locally produced honey, jams, and other farm products from markets in the local community.

Local Culture and Customs of Mlawula Nature Reserve

The Swazi culture is rich and deeply ingrained in daily life in Mlawula Nature Reserve. Here are some customs and cultural practices to keep in mind:

  • Respect the locals: It’s essential to show respect to the local community and their customs.

  • Dress appropriately: Swazis are generally conservative, and it’s best to dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Mlawula Nature Reserve

The best time to visit Mlawula Nature Reserve is during the winter months (May to September) when the weather is cooler and dry. This makes it easier to spot game animals while on safari.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mlawula Nature Reserve

Here are some general tips about what to do and what not to do while visiting the reserve:

Do:

  • Follow the rules: Always follow the rules and regulations of the reserve to ensure your safety and that of the wildlife.

  • Tip the guides: Tipping the guides is a common practice in Eswatini and is appreciated for their hard work.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife: It’s crucial to observe the wildlife from a distance and avoid actions that may disturb them.

  • Litter: Keep the reserve clean by not littering or leaving trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mlawula Nature Reserve

Mlawula Nature Reserve is a safe destination for travellers. However, it’s essential to take standard precautions, such as not walking alone in isolated areas and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Mlawula Nature Reserve

It’s essential to take malaria medication when visiting Mlawula Nature Reserve since it’s a malaria-endemic area. Also, make sure to use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing during mosquito-infested areas.

Getting to Mlawula Nature Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mlawula Nature Reserve

The nearest international airport to Mlawula Nature Reserve is located in Johannesburg, South Africa. From there, you can either drive or book a transfer to Mlawula Nature Reserve.

Bus Stations Near Mlawula Nature Reserve

The closest bus station is located in Mbabane, the capital of Eswatini. From there, you can take a taxi or rent a car to Mlawula Nature Reserve.

Other Options

Driving to Mlawula Nature Reserve is another option for those who prefer to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Mlawula Nature Reserve

The best way to get around Mlawula Nature Reserve is by hiring a car. However, there are also tour operators and guides that offer tours of the reserve.

Where to Go Next from Mlawula Nature Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eswatini has many other highlights and destinations worth exploring after visiting Mlawula Nature Reserve. These include:

  • Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in the middle of Eswatini, this sanctuary offers a unique experience of seeing wildlife up close in the parks.

  • Hlane Royal National Park: This park is one of Eswatini’s largest parks and is home to elephants, lions, cheetahs, and rhinos.

Conclusion

Mlawula Nature Reserve is a fantastic destination for travellers who want to experience the best of Eswatini’s natural beauty and culture.
