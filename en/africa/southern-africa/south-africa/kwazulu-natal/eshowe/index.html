Introduction to Eshowe

Located in KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, Eshowe is a quaint town surrounded by lush greenery and breathtaking natural beauty. The town is known for its rich history, culture, and traditions, making it a popular destination for travelers. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Eshowe has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ve highlighted the top places to visit, things to do, and local customs to help you plan your trip to Eshowe.

List of Places to Visit in Eshowe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fort Nongqayi Museum Village

A must-visit site and a landmark of Eshowe, Fort Nongqayi Museum Village houses various artifacts, documents, and photographs that shed light on the Zulu culture, history, and traditions. The museum village also includes a traditional Zulu village that showcases the local way of living.

Dlinza Forest Aerial Boardwalk

Get a bird’s eye view of Eshowe’s lush natural beauty by visiting the Dlinza Forest Aerial Boardwalk. The elevated walkway offers a 125-meter long path through the forest canopy, where visitors can keep an eye out for the local fauna and flora.

Natural Attractions of Eshowe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Ongoye Forest Reserve

The Ongoye Forest Reserve offers visitors a chance to explore the dense indigenous forest, where you can take a guided tour or hike on your own. Besides hiking, visitors can also indulge in bird watching, photography or swimming in the natural streams of the forest.

Nkandla Forest

Nkandla Forest is a spectacular site where visitors can see rare bird species, natural waterfalls, and spectacular views of the surrounding countryside. Hiking trails and camping facilities are available, making it the perfect retreat for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eshowe

Entonjeni Nature Reserve

The Entonjeni Nature Reserve boasts several stunning hiking and biking trails that can accommodate different levels of difficulty. The paths offer breathtaking views, serene picnic spots and are home to an array of wildlife and bird species.

Best Things to Do in Eshowe: Activities and Tours

Zulu Cultural Village

Visitors can immerse themselves in the rich Zulu culture and traditions by visiting the Zulu Cultural Village. The village offers visitors a chance to experience cultural dances, try traditional dishes, and learn about the history and way of life of the Zulu people.

*Shrine of the Zulu Martyrs

The Shrine of the Zulu Martyrs is a historical site that played a significant role in the history of South Africa’s struggle for freedom. Visitors can learn about the story of these brave warriors and honor their efforts by placing a wreath or flowers at the monument.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eshowe

Here’s a suggested itinerary for your week-long trip to Eshowe:

Day 1: Visit Fort Nongqayi Museum Village and the Zulu Cultural Village. Day 2: Hike/bike the Entonjeni Nature Reserve. Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Dlinza Forest Aerial Boardwalk. Day 4: Explore Ongoye Forest Reserve and go bird watching. Day 5: Visit the Shrine of the Zulu Martyrs. Day 6: Relax by the natural pool at Nkandla Forest. Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as the Hluhluwe Umfolozi Game Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Eshowe

Must-Try Dishes

Bunny Chow

This classic South African dish consists of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with spicy curry. It’s a hearty meal that is perfect for a chilly evening.

Boerewors

A traditional South African sausage made from beef, lamb, and pork, Boerewors is an excellent option for BBQs.

Local Beverages

Umqombothi

A traditional African beer made from maize, sorghum, and yeast, Umqombothi has a sweet and sour taste and is often served at cultural events or ceremonies.

Rooibos Tea

A herbal tea made from the leaves of the rooibos plant, Rooibos Tea is a South African favorite known for its sweet, nutty flavor and numerous health benefits.

Where to Stay in Eshowe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sugar Hill Manor Guesthouse

Located in a peaceful suburb of Eshowe, Sugar Hill Manor Guesthouse offers comfortable and affordable accommodation with modern amenities.

Eshowe Guesthouse

A homely bed, and breakfast guesthouse with a tranquil surrounding, Eshowe Guesthouse is perfect for families or romantic getaways.

What to Buy in Eshowe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wooden Crafts and Sculptures

Eshowe is known for its exquisite handmade wooden crafts and sculptures. Local artisans create hand-carved benches, frames, and wooden figures, making these products an excellent souvenir.

Zulu Beadwork

Zulu beadwork is a significant part of the local culture, and visitors can purchase necklaces, earrings, and other jewelry items made from colorful beads.

Local Culture and Customs of Eshowe

Eshowe is predominantly Zulu, and it is essential to respect their customs and traditions. Visitors should dress appropriately and be mindful of their behavior when interacting with locals. It is customary to greet people with respect and courtesy, and visitors should try their hand at a typical greeting or two to show appreciation of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Eshowe

The best time to visit Eshowe is between March and October when the weather is cooler and milder.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eshowe

Do:

  • Be respectful and courteous when interacting with locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the Zulu culture.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Wander around at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Leave your valuables in a visible place.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eshowe

Eshowe is relatively safe, but visitors should exercise caution and be vigilant at all times, especially at night. Avoid walking alone in the dark and keep an eye out for any suspicious activities.

Health in Eshowe

Visitors traveling to Eshowe should ensure they have all the necessary vaccinations before their trip. Malaria is prevalent in the region and visitors should take appropriate measures to prevent infection.

Getting to Eshowe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eshowe

The closest airport to Eshowe is the King Shaka International Airport, which is approximately a 2-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Eshowe

There are several bus companies in South Africa that operate to and from Eshowe. Visitors can book their tickets online for easy travel arrangements

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private taxi to explore Eshowe and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Eshowe

The best way to explore Eshowe is by car or taxi. Visitors can rent a car or hail a taxi from the town center.

Where to Go Next from Eshowe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Eshowe, including:

  • Hluhluwe Umfolozi Game Reserve
  • The Beaches of Sodwana Bay
  • St. Lucia Wetlands Park

Conclusion

Eshowe is undoubtedly an excellent destination for adventurers and cultural explorers alike. From natural sites to historical landmarks and cultural experiences, the small town has a lot to offer its visitors. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip to Eshowe and have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful part of South Africa.
