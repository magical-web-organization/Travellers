Introduction to Scottburgh

Located in the picturesque South Coast of KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, Scottburgh is known for its beautiful beaches, rich history, and outdoor adventures. This charming town is a popular destination among travelers who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and relax in a coastal oasis. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, a history buff, or simply looking for a peaceful getaway, Scottburgh has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll provide you with everything you need to know to plan your visit to Scottburgh.

List of Places to Visit in Scottburgh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Scottburgh Museum: Discover the fascinating history of Scottburgh and its surrounding areas at this small museum. Exhibits include displays on the sugar cane industry, shipwrecks, and a recreation of a 19th-century settler’s cottage.

  • Empisini Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is home to the remains of a Zulu homestead that dates back to the 1800s. The homestead was the birthplace of Inkosi Albert Luthuli, the first African to win a Nobel Peace Prize. Visitors can take a guided tour of the reserve and learn about Zulu culture and history.

  • Aliwal Shoal: Located just off the coast of Umkomaas, this world-famous dive site is a must-visit for diving enthusiasts. The shoal is home to a diverse range of marine life, including sharks, rays, and turtles.

Natural Attractions of Scottburgh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scottsburgh Beach: With its clear turquoise waters and wide stretch of sand, Scottburgh Beach is the perfect place to relax and soak up the sun. Visitors can go swimming, surfing, or fishing, or simply enjoy a leisurely stroll along the beach.

  • Crocodile Creek: Get up close and personal with one of Africa’s most feared predators at Crocodile Creek. This wildlife park offers guided tours of its crocodile enclosures, as well as a chance to see other animals such as snakes, iguanas, and tortoises.

  • Vernon Crookes Nature Reserve: If you want to explore the great outdoors, head to the Vernon Crookes Nature Reserve. The reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including zebra, giraffe, and numerous bird species. Visitors can go hiking, birdwatching, or even mountain biking on the reserve’s trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scottburgh

  • Umkomaas Hiking Trail: This 12-kilometer trail winds through the lush vegetation of the Umkomaas Valley, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Lake Eland Game Reserve Hiking Trail: This trail takes you through the dense forest and grasslands of the Lake Eland Game Reserve, where you can spot a range of wildlife, including zebra, wildebeest, and baboons.

  • Scottburgh Mountain Biking Trails: With its varied terrain and scenic views, Scottburgh is a popular destination for mountain bikers. There are several bike trails in and around the town, including the Scottburgh MTB Trails, which offer a challenging but rewarding ride.

Best Things to Do in Scottburgh: Activities and Tours

  • Shark Cage Diving: For an adrenaline-pumping adventure, why not try shark cage diving? Scottburgh is a popular destination for this thrilling activity, which allows you to get up close and personal with sharks in their natural habitat.

  • Whale Watching: Between June and November, humpback whales migrate along the South African coast, and Scottburgh is the perfect place to spot them. Several tour operators offer whale-watching trips, which give you the chance to see these magnificent creatures up close.

  • Sardine Run: Every winter, millions of sardines migrate up the South African coast, followed by predators such as dolphins, sharks, and birds. The annual sardine run is a spectacle not to be missed, and Scottburgh is one of the best places to witness it.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scottburgh

Day 1 Check into your accommodation and spend the day relaxing on Scottburgh Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Scottburgh

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bunny chow: A popular South African dish that originated in Durban, bunny chow is a loaf of bread filled with curry. The dish is now popular throughout South Africa, and Scottburgh has several restaurants that serve it.

  • Braai: A traditional South African barbecue, braai is a must-try for any meat lover. Visitors can enjoy a braai at one of the town’s many beaches or parks, or at a local restaurant.

  • Biltong: Similar to beef jerky, biltong is a popular South African snack made from dried meat. Visitors can sample a range of biltong flavors at local shops and markets.

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos tea: A caffeine-free herbal tea made from the leaves of the rooibos plant, which is native to South Africa. Visitors can enjoy a cup of rooibos tea at local cafes and restaurants.

  • South African wine: South Africa is known for its world-class wines, and visitors to Scottburgh can sample a range of local wines at nearby vineyards.

Where to Stay in Scottburgh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Blue Marlin Hotel: Located right on Scottburgh Beach, this hotel offers stunning ocean views and a range of amenities, including an outdoor pool, a spa, and a restaurant.

  • Milkwood Beach Apartments: These self-catering apartments are located just a short walk from Scottburgh Beach and offer everything you need for a comfortable stay, including a fully equipped kitchen, air conditioning, and satellite TV.

  • Umzumbe Surf House: For budget-friendly accommodations, check out the Umzumbe Surf House. This hostel offers dorm rooms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen, lounge, and garden.

What to Buy in Scottburgh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Zulu crafts: Visitors can purchase a range of handmade crafts from local Zulu artisans, including beadwork, baskets, and ceramics.

  • Sugar cane products: KwaZulu-Natal is known for its sugar cane industry, and visitors to Scottburgh can purchase a range of sugarcane products, including sugar, syrup, and sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Scottburgh

The Zulu culture has a strong presence in Scottburgh and the surrounding area, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the customs and traditions of this fascinating culture. Visitors should also respect local customs, dress modestly when visiting cultural sites, and always ask for permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Scottburgh

The best time to visit Scottburgh is between April and September, when the weather is mild and dry. This is also the best time for outdoor activities such as hiking and whale watching. December and January can be crowded and hot, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scottburgh

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local foods and beverages.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor activities that Scottburgh has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scottburgh

Overall, Scottburgh is a safe destination for visitors, but it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables. It’s also advisable to avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.

Health in Scottburgh

There are no specific health risks associated with visiting Scottburgh, but visitors should ensure they are up to date on routine vaccinations. Visitors should also take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as malaria is present in some parts of South Africa.

Getting to Scottburgh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scottburgh

The closest airport to Scottburgh is King Shaka International Airport, which is located approximately 90 kilometers north of the town. Visitors can take a shuttle or taxi from the airport to Scottburgh, or rent a car.

Bus Stations Near Scottburgh

Several bus companies operate services to Scottburgh from major cities such as Durban and Johannesburg. The nearest bus station to Scottburgh is located in Umkomaas, approximately 8 kilometers away.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Scottburgh from major cities in South Africa, or take a train from Durban to Umkomaas, followed by a short taxi ride to Scottburgh.

How to Get Around in Scottburgh

The best way to get around Scottburgh is by car, as many of the town’s attractions are located outside of the town center. Visitors can rent a car, or take a taxi or shuttle to their desired destination.

Where to Go Next from Scottburgh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Durban: South Africa’s third-largest city is located just 60 kilometers north of Scottburgh and is known for its beaches, museums, and nightlife.

  • Oribi Gorge: Located approximately 50 kilometers west of Scottburgh, Oribi Gorge is a popular destination for outdoor activities such as bungee jumping, white water rafting, and hiking.

  • Port Shepstone: Located approximately 30 kilometers south of Scottburgh, Port Shepstone is a charming coastal town known for its beautiful beaches and rich history.

Conclusion

With its stunning beaches, rich history, and outdoor adventures, Scottburgh is a destination not to be missed. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an action-packed adventure, this coastal town has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit to Scottburgh and make the most of your time in this picturesque South African town.
