Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park: A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park is a sprawling natural wonder of South Africa, with peaks that rise over 3,000 meters and waterfalls tumbling down precipices into idyllic valleys. The park is incredibly diverse and is home to an enormous range of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species. This comprehensive tour guide will make it easy to plan your visit, with information on attractions to visit, hikes to take, delicious foods to try, and more.

Introduction to Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in KwaZulu-Natal Province in South Africa. The park is characterized by its rugged beauty, with towering mountains, deep valleys, and cascading waterfalls. The Drakensberg range, from which the park gets its name, extends over 1,000 km through South Africa, Lesotho, and Swaziland. The park is home to a large variety of fauna and flora, some of which are rare and can only be found here. It is a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers.

List of Places to Visit in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

When it comes to places to visit in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park, there is no shortage of options. Here are some of the most popular:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Didima San Rock Art Centre: A museum that provides an insight into the rich cultural heritage of the San people who lived in these mountains for thousands of years. The museum features a vast collection of rock art pieces and artifacts.
  • Kamberg Nature Reserve: The Kamberg Rock Art Center is situated in the Kamberg Nature Reserve on the foothills of the majestic Drakensberg Mountains. It features an extensive collection of San rock art found in natural caves and overhangs.
  • Nelson Mandela Capture Site: This historical site in Howick marks where Nelson Mandela was arrested by the apartheid-era police on August 5th, 1962.

Natural Attractions of Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Drakensberg Amphitheatre: The Drakensberg Amphitheatre is an impressive rock face in the Northern Drakensberg - one of the most awe-inspiring sights in the park.
  • Giant’s Castle Game Reserve: A large reserve in the foothills of the Drakensberg mountains that offers exceptional bird-watching opportunities, trout fishing, and hiking trails.
  • Tugela Falls: One of the highest waterfalls in the world, with a total drop of 948 meters over five drops.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

Hiking and biking are two of the most popular activities in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg National Park. Here are some of the best trails to consider:

  • Sentinel Peak & Chain Ladders: A hiking trail that leads to the top of the Sentinel Peak, a 3,200-meter high mountain that offers breathtaking views of the park.
  • Royal Natal National Park: This park includes several breathtaking trails, including the Amphitheatre Trail, Cascades Trail, and Tugela Gorge trail.
  • Cathedral Peak: This peak is situated in the central Drakensberg and offers a challenging hike that rewards you with jaw-dropping views once you reach the top.

Best Things to Do in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy at Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park. Here are a few:

  • Helicopter Tour: Witness the splendor of the Drakensberg Mountains from a unique perspective on a helicopter tour.
  • Horseback Riding: Horse trails are available for adventurers of all levels.
  • White Water Rafting: This activity is an exhilarating way to explore the park and take in its natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

No trip to Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park is complete without trying the local foods and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bunny chow: A dish consisting of curry served in a hollowed-out loaf of bread
  • Boerewors & Pap: A signature South African dish consisting of thick, juicy sausages and grinding maize
  • Biltong: A snack beef or game meat dried and spiced

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos Tea: A popular herbal tea, made from the Rooibos plant.
  • Cape Malay Coffee: Coffee infused with cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves.
  • South African Wine: South Africa is known for producing some of the finest wines globally.

Where to Stay in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of options for accommodation at Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Whatever your preference or budget, there is an option for you.

  • The Cavern Resort & Spa: A luxury mountain resort nestled in the heart of the Drakensberg Mountains and offering authentic experiences.
  • Drakensberg Mountain Retreat: A charming, self-catering farmhouse with stunning views of the Drakensberg mountains.
  • Amazian Mountain River Lodge: A tranquil lodge situated on the banks of the Umzimkulu River.

What to Buy in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Locally produced handicrafts and art pieces are perfect souvenirs to bring home from Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park. Local craftspeople produce everything from pottery to traditional beadwork to hand-carved walking sticks.

Local Culture and Customs of Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

The people of the Drakensberg mountains are diverse and include various indigenous groups, including the San, who have a long and fascinating history in the area. Some members of these communities work as guides, offering a unique perspective on the park’s ecology and culture.

Best Time to Visit Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

The best time to visit Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park is between May and September when temperatures are mild, and the weather is usually clear, making it ideal for hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

Do:

  • Respect the local people and their customs.
  • Respect the environment and the wildlife.
  • Follow hiking protocols and always stay on marked trails.

Don’t:

  • Wear brightly colored clothing when hiking, as this can attract unwanted attention from wildlife.
  • Do not litter.
  • Do not remove artifacts from historical and cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

South Africa is generally safe, but tourists should always take the standard safety measures when traveling to unfamiliar areas.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking at night
  • Do not leave valuables unattended
  • Do not show off large amounts of cash
  • Always secure your belongings

Health in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

South Africa has a high prevalence of HIV/AIDS, and tourists should take standard precautions when engaging in sexual activities. There is no malaria risk in Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park. However, it is always advisable to speak with your doctor before traveling abroad.

Getting to Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

The two closest airports to Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park are:

  • King Shaka International Airport Durban: The closest and most convenient airport to access the park, located approximately 230 km away.
  • O. R. Tambo International Airport Johannesburg: Located about 450 km away, this airport provides more extensive international and domestic flight options.

Bus Stations Near Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park

Although bus services operate in the area, it is not widely used to access the park. For more convenient transportation, it is advisable to hire a car.
