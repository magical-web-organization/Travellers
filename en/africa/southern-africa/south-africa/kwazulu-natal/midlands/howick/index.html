Introduction to Howick

Located in the heart of Midlands in KwaZulu-Natal, Howick is a charming town known for its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Surrounded by rolling hills and scenic landscapes, Howick offers visitors an array of exciting things to see and do. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply in search of a relaxing getaway, Howick is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Howick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Howick Museum: Learn about the history of Howick and its people at this local museum, which showcases a range of exhibits and artifacts related to the town’s past.
  • Nelson Mandela Capture Site: Explore the historic site where Nelson Mandela was captured in 1962, and visit the monument erected in his honor.
  • The Falls Retreat: Discover the cultural heritage of the Midlands at this charming retreat, which offers a range of activities and events focused on the local arts and crafts scene.

Natural Attractions of Howick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Howick Falls: Admire the stunning 330-foot waterfall located in the heart of Howick, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Karkloof Nature Reserve: Explore the natural beauty of KwaZulu-Natal at this sprawling nature reserve, which boasts miles of hiking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and stunning scenery.
  • Midmar Dam: Enjoy a day of swimming, fishing, or boating at this popular freshwater reservoir, which offers a wide range of recreational activities for visitors of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Howick

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or prefer a scenic bike ride, Howick offers an array of exciting routes and trails to explore. Some of the best options include:

  • The Karkloof Trail: This 8km trail winds through the stunning Karkloof Nature Reserve, offering hikers and bikers the chance to spot a range of native wildlife and admire the region’s natural beauty.
  • The Nelson Mandela Capture Site Trail: This scenic 5km trail takes visitors through the lush Midlands countryside, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and the historic Nelson Mandela Capture Site monument.

Best Things to Do in Howick: Activities and Tours

  • Midlands Meander: Explore the unique arts, crafts, and food scene of the Midlands on this self-guided tour, which takes visitors through a range of local studios, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Soar high above the Midlands and enjoy stunning panoramic views of the region on this unforgettable hot air balloon ride.
  • Zipline Adventure: Get your adrenaline pumping on this thrilling zipline adventure, which takes visitors through the treetops of the Karkloof Nature Reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Howick

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Howick, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Howick

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bunny Chow: This traditional South African dish consists of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curried meat or vegetables.
  • Biltong: A popular South African snack, biltong is a type of dried meat that’s similar to beef jerky.
  • Boerewors: A type of sausage made from beef and pork, boerewors is a South African staple that’s often served at braais (barbecues).

Local Beverages

  • Safari: A popular local soda that comes in a range of flavors, including grape, lemon, and cream soda.
  • Cape Velvet: A creamy liqueur made from a blend of chocolate, wine, and cream.
  • Rooibos Tea: A caffeine-free herbal tea that’s grown in South Africa and is said to have a range of health benefits.

Where to Stay in Howick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Howick offers a range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Some of the best options include:

  • The Howick Falls Hotel: Located in the heart of Howick, this charming hotel offers stunning views of the falls and a range of luxurious amenities.
  • Beverley Country Cottages: This family-run guesthouse offers cozy self-catering cottages and warm hospitality in a peaceful countryside setting.
  • Thistledown Country House: Nestled amidst lush gardens and rolling hills, this elegant guesthouse offers a range of comfortable rooms and gourmet dining options.

What to Buy in Howick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Howick, here are some of the best options:

  • Ceramics: The Midlands is famous for its high-quality ceramics, which are produced by local artisans and sold in a range of shops and galleries throughout the region.
  • Beadwork: Another popular local craft, beaded jewelry and accessories are sold in a range of stores and markets throughout Howick and the surrounding area.
  • Honey: KwaZulu-Natal is known for its delicious honey, which is produced by local beekeepers and sold in stores and markets throughout the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Howick

The people of Howick are known for their warm hospitality and strong sense of community. Visitors can expect to be greeted with a friendly smile and treated to an array of local traditions and customs during their stay. Some important customs to note include:

  • Respecting Elders: In South African culture, respecting elders is an important value that’s instilled from a young age.
  • Braais: A cornerstone of South African culture, braais (barbecues) are a social event that are often accompanied by music, drinks, and good company.
  • Shosholoza: This popular South African song is often sung at sporting events and other gatherings, and is considered a symbol of national unity and pride.

Best Time to Visit Howick

The best time to visit Howick is during the summer months from December to February, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should be aware that this is the peak tourist season, and prices and crowds can be high. For those looking to avoid the crowds, the shoulder seasons of March to May and September to November offer mild weather and fewer visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Howick

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions: South African culture is diverse and rich, and visitors should take the time to learn about and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try Local Cuisine: South African cuisine is known for its unique flavors and ingredients, and visitors should take the time to try some of the local dishes during their stay.
  • Pack for the Weather: The weather in Howick can be unpredictable, and visitors should be sure to pack for a range of temperatures and conditions.

Don’t:

  • Rely on Public Transportation: While there are public transportation options available in Howick, they can be unreliable and infrequent.
  • Underestimate Sun Exposure: The sun in South Africa can be strong, and visitors should be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and dehydration.
  • Display Valuables: Petty theft can be a problem in some areas of South Africa, and visitors should avoid displaying expensive jewelry or electronics in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Howick

While Howick is generally a safe destination for visitors, there are some precautions that travelers should take to ensure their safety:

  • Exercise Caution at Night: Petty crime can be a problem in some areas of Howick, especially at night. Visitors should avoid walking alone after dark and should stay in well-lit areas.
  • Be Aware of Wildlife: Howick is home to a range of native wildlife, including snakes, spiders, and baboons. Visitors should exercise caution and avoid approaching or disturbing any animals they encounter.

Health in Howick

Travelers to Howick should take the following health precautions:

  • Get Vaccinated: Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to South Africa. Additional vaccinations may be recommended depending on the traveler’s itinerary.
  • Take Precautions Against Mosquito Bites: Malaria is present in some areas of South Africa, including KwaZulu-Natal. Visitors should take precautions against mosquito bites, including wearing long sleeves and using insect repellent.
  • Drink Bottled Water: Tap water in South Africa may not be safe to drink. Visitors should stick to bottled water and avoid consuming ice cubes or consuming food or beverages from street vendors.

Getting to Howick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Howick

The closest airport to Howick is Pietermaritzburg Airport (PZB), which is located approximately 20km away. Durban International Airport (DUR) is another option, located approximately 110km away.

Bus Stations Near Howick

The closest bus station to Howick is located in Pietermaritzburg, approximately 20km away. Greyhound and Intercape offer regular bus services to Pietermaritzburg from major cities throughout South Africa.

Other Options

Visitors to Howick can also rent a car, take a taxi, or use a ride-sharing app like Uber to get around the town and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Howick

While public transportation options are available in Howick, they can be unreliable and infrequent. Visitors may prefer to rent a car or use a ride-sharing app like Uber to get around the town and explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Howick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your South African adventure after your visit to Howick, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Pietermaritzburg: Explore the capital city of KwaZulu-Natal, which is home to a range of historic landmarks, cultural attractions, and natural wonders.
  • Drakensberg Mountains: Head to the stunning Drakensberg Mountains, which offer some of the most breathtaking scenery in South Africa as well as world-class hiking and adventure sports.
  • Durban: Visit the vibrant seaside city of Durban, which boasts beautiful beaches, a bustling downtown, and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply soaking up the local culture, Howick offers something for everyone. With its stunning scenery, warm hospitality, and vibrant community, Howick is the perfect destination for travelers looking to experience the best of KwaZulu-Natal.
