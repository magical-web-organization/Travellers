Introduction to Umhlanga

Umhlanga, located in the North Coast of KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, is a beautiful seaside resort town that offers the perfect mix of sandy beaches, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.

The town is well-known for its golden beaches, protected by shark nets and lifeguards, and for the iconic Umhlanga Lighthouse, a historic landmark with breathtaking views.

Whether you’re looking for a romantic weekend getaway, a family-friendly vacation or an adrenaline-packed holiday, Umhlanga has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll dive deep into the best places to visit, local culture and customs, the food, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Umhlanga

Umhlanga has a wide range of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in Umhlanga:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Umhlanga Rocks Promenade - The Umhlanga Rocks Promenade offers a scenic walk and cycle path along the coast. You can also see the impressive bronze statue of Bruce Brown, a British soldier who fought in the Anglo-Zulu War.

  • Umhlanga Lighthouse - This iconic lighthouse stands tall at 21 meters and is located at the edge of the Umhlanga Rocks promenade. It was built in 1954, and to this day, it still lights up Umhlanga Bay.

  • The KwaZulu-Natal Sharks Board - Founded in 1962, is the only organization in the world that provides bather protection against sharks while minimizing environmental impact. The Sharks Board provides informative presentations on marine life in KwaZulu-Natal.

  • The Phansi Museum - Phansi Museum celebrates the culture of Southern African people through an impressive collection of art and crafts, household and farming utensils, furniture, weapons, and attire.

Natural Attractions of Umhlanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Umhlanga Nature Reserve - Situated in the heart of Umhlanga, the nature reserve has scenic forest and grasslands that are home to a variety of bird species.

  • Hawaan Forest - Hawaan Forest is one of the few remaining forests in KwaZulu-Natal. Visitors can enjoy horse riding or taking leisurely walks along the forest trails.

  • Umhlanga Beaches - Beach lovers should look no further than Umhlanga for a perfect vacation. The town’s beaches, such as Umhlanga Main Beach and Bronze Beach, are the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and beach games.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umhlanga

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Umhlanga has plenty of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Here are a few popular ones:

  • Umhlanga Lagoon Nature Reserve Trail – Umhlanga Lagoon Nature Reserve Trail is a relatively easy hike and is the perfect day outing amidst a natural coastal forest.

  • Hawaan Forest - Hawaan Forest re-opened a hiking trail that takes you into one of the last coastal dune forests in the area. The trail takes you through the Hawaan Nature Reserve and the Umhlanga Lagoon Nature Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Umhlanga: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural and cultural attractions, Umhlanga also offers exciting activities and tours. Some of the popular ones are:

  • Deep-Sea Fishing - Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced angler, there are plenty of opportunities for deep-sea fishing tours in Umhlanga.

  • Water Sports - Umhlanga is a paradise for watersports enthusiasts. You can enjoy surfing, kiteboarding, stand-up paddle boarding, and many more.

  • Wine Tasting Tour and Safari - The Shongweni Farmers Market offers wine-tasting tours that take you through the beautiful vineyards of the Midlands Meander. After the tour, you can enjoy a game drive in one of the nearby wildlife parks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Umhlanga

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to fully explore and enjoy Umhlanga:

Day 1: Take a leisurely stroll along the Umhlanga Promenade and take in the stunning views. Spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach.

Day 2: Visit the Umhlanga Lighthouse and Shark Board Museum. In the afternoon, explore the Phansi Museum.

Day 3: Go for a hike in the Umhlanga Lagoon Nature Reserve Trail. In the evening, have a traditional braai (barbecue) with locals.

Day 4: Spend the day discovering the Hawaan Forest & Umhlanga Lagoon Nature Reserve, and then relax with a sundowner on one of Umhlanga’s beautiful beaches.

Day 5: Get your adrenaline pumping with a day of action-packed watersports or a surfing lesson.

Day 6: Take a day tour to the Midlands Meander for a wine tasting tour and a game drive.

Day 7: Depart for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Umhlanga

No travel experience is complete without sampling the local food and drinks. Umhlanga offers unique tastes that you won’t find anywhere else.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bunny Chow - Bunny Chow is a spicy curry that is served in a bread loaf, a traditional Durban dish.

  • Braai - Known as South Africa’s version of the BBQ, Braai is a smoky, flavorful meal cooked over an open flame.

  • Samoosa - Samoosas are delicious snacks filled with vegetables, meat or curry that are perfect for a quick snack or appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Umqombothi - Umqombothi is a traditional beer that is made from fermented maize or sorghum.

  • Rooibos Tea - Rooibos tea is a flavored tea that is native to South Africa and is known for its medicinal and health benefits.

  • Oyster Box Hotel - A luxury hotel that overlooks the seaside and beautifully designed with African-inspired decor.

  • Umhlanga Sands Resort - A family-friendly resort with plenty of activities, situated on the beach front.

What to Buy in Umhlanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir, Umhlanga has lots of options for you. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs that you can take home:

  • Zulu Beadwork and Crafts - Zulu beadwork is a traditional craft that produces beautiful, handcrafted jewelry, and other accessories.

  • Spices and Seasonings - Durban is known for its blend of spices and seasonings that give South African cuisine its unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Umhlanga

Umhlanga is a multicultural town with a mix of African, Asian, and European influences. The locals are hospitable and welcoming, and the town celebrates a wide range of cultural festivals and events throughout the year.

When visiting Umhlanga, it’s essential to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Umhlanga

The best time to visit Umhlanga is from December to February, which is summer season in South Africa. The weather is warm and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and beach vacations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umhlanga

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine – Umhlanga is a food lover’s paradise with an endless variety of delicious dishes to try.

  • Wear sunscreen - The sun can be intense, so make sure you apply sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

  • Pack light - Umhlanga is a beach town, and you won’t need too many formal clothes, so pack light and comfortable clothes.

Don’t:

  • Misinterpret the cultural practices - Umhlanga has different cultural practices, so it’s essential to respect them and not to misinterpret them.

  • Litter or misuse natural resources - Umhlanga’s natural resources are its most valuable asset, so it is important to keep the environment clean and protected.

Safety and Health

Safety in Umhlanga

Umhlanga is relatively safe to visit, and crime is generally not a significant problem in the area. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and avoid carrying valuables on you.

Health in Umhlanga

Umhlanga has private and public health facilities that offer high-quality care. Visitors are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria and yellow fever.

Getting to Umhlanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Umhlanga

  • King Shaka International Airport - King Shaka International Airport is the main airport serving Umhlanga.

Bus Stations Near Umhlanga

  • Durban Station - Durban Station is the major bus station serving Umhlanga.

Other Options

Car hire can be a great option if you want to explore Umhlanga and the surrounding area on your own. Alternatively, you can hire a taxi or use a ride-hailing app such as Uber.

How to Get Around in Umhlanga

Umhlanga is a walkable town, and you can easily explore it on foot. Alternatively, there are taxis, buses, and car rental services available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Umhlanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Umhlanga is located in the heart of KwaZulu-Natal, making it an excellent base for exploring other destinations such as:

  • Durban - A vibrant city with plenty of cultural attractions, food, and nightlife.

  • Ballito - A vibrant beach town with excellent water sports and outdoor activities.

  • PheZulu Cultural Village - An informative and entertaining cultural tour that offers a glimpse into the daily lives, traditions, and customs of Zulu people.

Conclusion

Umhlanga is a hidden gem in South Africa, with plenty of natural beauty and cultural treasures that are waiting to be discovered. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this idyllic seaside resort town.
