Introduction to Newcastle

Newcastle is a city located in Battlefields, KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, and is the third-largest urban centre in this province. Steeped in history and surrounded by natural beauty, Newcastle boasts a host of attractions that will captivate visitors of all ages and interests.

From the magnificent Drakensberg Mountains to the rich Zulu culture that permeates every aspect of daily life, Newcastle is a unique destination that combines history, culture, and natural beauty.

In this extensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything you need to know about visiting Newcastle, including the must-visit places, the best things to do, where to stay, and how to get there. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Newcastle

When visiting Newcastle, there are several places you should not miss. Here are our top picks for attractions in Newcastle:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Fort Amiel Museum: This historic fort serves as a museum of Newcastle’s history, including the war between the Zulu Kingdom and the British Empire.

  2. Talana Museum: Learn about the Anglo-Boer war and its impact on the region at this museum, which also includes exhibits on local cultural traditions.

  3. Zulu Cultural Museum: This museum showcases the rich Zulu heritage and cultural traditions in Newcastle and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Newcastle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Amajuba Nature Reserve: For an up-close look at the region’s flora and fauna, explore this stunning reserve, home to a variety of animal species and several scenic hiking trails.

  2. Ingogo Valley: Take a scenic drive through Ingogo Valley and stop for a picnic lunch surrounded by breathtaking mountain views.

  3. Chelmsford Nature Reserve: Named after Lord Chelmsford, who led the British forces during the Anglo-Zulu War, this reserve is home to impressive rock formations, hiking trails, and historic sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newcastle

If you love hiking or cycling, there are several fantastic routes to explore in Newcastle. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  1. The Waterfall Hike: Follow the trail from the edge of town to the top of a gorgeous waterfall, where you’ll have spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.

  2. Sarel Cilliers Memorial Hike: This trail commemorates the Boer War, and takes hikers past historic sites and through scenic countryside.

  3. Chelmsford Nature Reserve Trail: This trail takes hikers through the reserve mentioned earlier, with plenty of opportunities to see wildlife and stunning rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Newcastle: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, there are a variety of activities and tours you can enjoy in Newcastle, including:

  1. Mountain biking: Rent a mountain bike and explore the stunning countryside on two wheels.

  2. Horse riding: Take a leisurely ride through the countryside, stopping for a picnic in a scenic spot.

  3. Hot air balloon rides: Get a bird’s eye view of Newcastle and the stunning scenery from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Newcastle

Newcastle boasts a wide variety of culinary delights, with something for every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bunny Chow: A uniquely South African dish, Bunny Chow consists of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curried meat or vegetables.

  2. Boerewors: Traditional South African sausage made with beef or pork sausage seasoned with coriander and other spices.

  3. Potjiekos: A stew typically cooked in a cast-iron pot over an open fire, Potjiekos can be made with various meats and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Rooibos Tea: This caffeine-free herbal tea is a South African classic, with a slightly sweet, nutty flavour.

  2. Pinotage Wine: A type of red wine produced mainly in South Africa, Pinotage is a unique and delicious blend of Pinot Noir and Cinsaut grapes.

  3. Savanna Cider: A popular local cider, Savanna is known for its crisp, refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Newcastle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation in Newcastle, there are myriad options available, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Black Horse Brewery and Lodge: This charming lodge is located on a working brewery, with beautifully appointed rooms, excellent food, and delicious beer.

  2. The Sands at St Francis: Located near the beach in St. Francis Bay, this elegant resort offers spacious suites, incredible views, and plenty of activities.

  3. Fernhurst Lodge: With stunning views of the Drakensberg Mountains, Fernhurst Lodge offers comfortable guest rooms and excellent food.

What to Buy in Newcastle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Newcastle, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from, including:

  1. Crafts and Artwork: The region is known for its intricate beadwork, wood carvings, and other traditional crafts.

  2. Rooibos Tea: Stock up on this delicious herbal tea to enjoy back home.

  3. Wines and Spirits: Pick up a bottle of locally-produced wine or cider to enjoy back home with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Newcastle

Newcastle is a melting pot of cultures, with a strong Zulu influence. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Greeting people with a handshake or nod of the head, always using the right hand.
  • Removing shoes before entering homes or mosques.
  • Dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Newcastle

The best time to visit Newcastle is between March and May, or between September and November, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newcastle

Here are some general Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Newcastle:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine: Newcastle offers a unique blend of traditional South African and Zulu cuisine.

  • Do take in the natural beauty: Between the Drakensberg Mountains and the stunning waterfalls, the region is full of natural wonders to explore.

  • Do respect local traditions and customs: Be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t travel alone at night: Take precautions if you’re walking or driving at night.

  • Don’t litter: Help keep the region’s natural beauty pristine by disposing of your waste properly.

  • Don’t assume everyone speaks English: While English is widely spoken, Zulu is the predominant language in Newcastle.

Safety and Health

When it comes to safety and health in Newcastle, visitors should take the usual precautions, including:

Safety in Newcastle

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when travelling alone or after dark.

  • Do not leave your valuables unattended or visible in your vehicle.

Health in Newcastle

  • The region is considered low-risk for malaria, but visitors should still take precautions, including using mosquito nets and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

  • Make sure to drink bottled water, especially if you’re unsure about the water quality in a particular area.

Getting to Newcastle: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you’re travelling to Newcastle, here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Newcastle

The nearest airport to Newcastle is the Newcastle Airport, which offers daily flights to and from Johannesburg.

Bus Stations Near Newcastle

Greyhound and Translux are two of the main bus companies that offer services to and from Newcastle.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or organise a private transfer to get to Newcastle from nearby cities and airports.

How to Get Around in Newcastle

While many of the main attractions in Newcastle are walkable, visitors may also want to use public transportation or hire a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Newcastle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Drakensberg Mountains: This stunning mountain range is a must-see destination, located just a couple of hours from Newcastle.

  2. Battlefields Route: Follow the trail of historic battlefields in the region, including the famous battle of Isandlwana.

  3. St. Lucia Estuary: Located on the coast, the St. Lucia Estuary is home to a wide array of wildlife, including hippos, crocodiles, and flamingos.

Conclusion

Newcastle, South Africa, is a destination that offers something for everyone, from history buffs to nature enthusiasts. With a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of activities to keep you entertained, it’s no wonder that so many visitors are drawn to this one-of-a-kind destination.

Whether you’re visiting for a week or just a few days, make sure to get out and explore everything this unique region has to offer.
