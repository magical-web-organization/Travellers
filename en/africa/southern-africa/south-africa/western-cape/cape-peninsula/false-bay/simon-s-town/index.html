Introduction to Simon’s Town

Located in False Bay, which is part of the Cape Peninsula in the Western Cape province of South Africa, Simon’s Town is a charming coastal town with a rich history dating back to the 18th century. Originally established by the Dutch East India Company as a halfway point between Europe and Asia, Simon’s Town has evolved into a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the world.

With its picturesque harbor, stunning beaches, and unique attractions, Simon’s Town offers a perfect combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best things to do, see, and eat in Simon’s Town. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming seaside town.

List of Places to Visit in Simon’s Town

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Simon’s Town has a rich history, and some of the best places to explore this history include:

  • The Simon’s Town Museum: This is a small but fascinating museum that houses exhibits on the history of Simon’s Town, including displays on the naval history of the town, the fishing industry, and the development of the town over the years.

  • The Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve: This reserve, which is part of the Table Mountain National Park, is home to a wealth of historical sites, including old forts, lighthouses, and shipwrecks. Visitors can explore these sites on foot or by car and experience the history of the Cape Peninsula up close.

  • The Just Nuisance Memorial: Just Nuisance was a much-loved Great Dane who became the only dog ever to be enlisted in the Royal Navy. He is a beloved symbol of Simon’s Town and his memorial is well worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Simon’s Town: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Simon’s Town is blessed with some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in South Africa. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Boulders Beach: This stunning beach is home to a colony of African Penguins, making it one of the only places in the world where you can get up close and personal with these incredible creatures.

  • Cape Point Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to a vast array of indigenous flora and fauna, as well as some of the most stunning views in South Africa. Visitors can hike or drive to the top of the Cape Point lighthouse and take in the breathtaking views of the ocean and mountains.

  • Seal Island: Just a few kilometers off the coast of Simon’s Town is Seal Island, home to thousands of Cape Fur Seals. Visitors can take a boat tour to the island and watch these incredible creatures in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simon’s Town

Simon’s Town is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Table Mountain National Park: This park contains a huge network of hiking and biking trails, catering to all skill levels.

  • Silvermine Nature Reserve: This reserve is home to a range of hiking and biking trails that wind through beautiful forests and offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve: This reserve boasts some of the most scenic hiking trails in South Africa, with stunning views of the ocean and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Simon’s Town: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Simon’s Town. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation or culture, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the False Bay and watch the whales, dolphins, and seals in their natural habitat.

  • Shark Cage Diving: If you’re feeling brave, take a dive in the cage and come face to face with one of the world’s most fearsome predators, the Great White Shark.

  • Wine Tasting: Explore the nearby Cape Winelands and indulge in some of South Africa’s best wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Simon’s Town

Must-Try Dishes

Simon’s Town has a thriving food scene with plenty of restaurants and cafes to choose from. Here are some of the classic dishes you should try:

  • Cape Malay Curry: This aromatic curry is a traditional dish that originated in Cape Town and is made with a blend of spices, coconut milk, and meat or vegetables.

  • Fish and Chips: Given Simon’s Town’s location near the ocean, it’s no surprise that fish and chips is a popular dish here. Try it with a side of mushy peas and tartar sauce for the ultimate British-style meal.

  • Boerewors: A popular South African sausage that is perfect for a quick snack or as the main ingredient in a BBQ.

Local Beverages

South Africa is known for its wine, and Simon’s Town is located near some of the best vineyards in the country. Here are some of the local beverages you should try:

  • Wine: The Cape Winelands are just a short drive from Simon’s Town, and they produce some of the best wines in the world.

  • Craft Beer: The craft beer scene has been on the rise in South Africa, and there are several excellent breweries in the Cape Town area.

Where to Stay in Simon’s Town: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Simon’s Town has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best:

  • The Waterfront Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the heart of Simon’s Town, right next to the town’s vibrant waterfront.

  • The Old Village Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Simon’s Town and offers a unique glimpse into the town’s past.

  • The Grosvenor Guest House: This stunning guest house is located in a tranquil residential area and offers beautiful views of the ocean.

What to Buy in Simon’s Town: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back home, here are some of the best options:

  • African Art and Crafts: Simon’s Town has a range of craft markets, where you can buy traditional African art and crafts, including beadwork, pottery, and wooden carvings.

  • Local Souvenirs: There are plenty of shops in Simon’s Town that sell souvenirs, including t-shirts, keychains, and postcards.

Local Culture and Customs of Simon’s Town

South Africa is a melting pot of cultures, and Simon’s Town is no exception. Here are some of the things you should know about the local culture and customs:

  • Etiquette: South Africans are generally friendly and informal, but it’s important to show respect for local customs and traditions.

  • Languages: The official languages of South Africa are English, Afrikaans, and Xhosa, but there are many other languages spoken in the country.

  • Cuisine: South African cuisine is a blend of international flavors, with a strong Indian and British influence.

Best Time to Visit Simon’s Town

The best time to visit Simon’s Town is during the summer months (December to February), when the weather is warm and dry. However, this is also the busiest time of the year, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices. The winter months (June to August) are cooler and wetter, but the town is quieter and prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simon’s Town

Do:

  • Explore the Natural Beauty: Simon’s Town is home to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in South Africa. Take the time to explore them and appreciate their beauty.

  • Interact with the Locals: South Africans are friendly and welcoming people. Take the time to chat with the locals and learn about their culture and traditions.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: South African cuisine is a blend of international flavors, with a unique twist. Be sure to try some of the local dishes and discover the flavors of the country.

Don’t:

  • Leave Valuables Unattended: As with any tourist destination, it’s important to be aware of your belongings and keep them close at hand.

  • Ignore Safety Precautions: While Simon’s Town is generally a safe place, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night.

  • Disrespect Local Customs and Traditions: South Africa is a multicultural society, and it’s important to show respect for local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Simon’s Town

Simon’s Town is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, as with any tourist destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Be Vigilant in Crowded Places: Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places, such as markets and tourist attractions.

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: While Simon’s Town is generally a safe place, it’s always best to avoid walking alone at night, especially in unlit areas.

  • Take a Licensed Taxi: If you need to travel at night, take a licensed taxi rather than walking or taking public transportation.

Health in Simon’s Town

South Africa is a country with a high prevalence of HIV/AIDS, so it’s important to take precautions to protect yourself. Here are some tips:

  • Practice Safe Sex: Always practice safe sex, using condoms or other forms of protection.

  • Take Malaria Prophylaxis: There is a risk of malaria in some parts of South Africa, so it’s important to take appropriate prophylaxis if you’re traveling to these areas.

  • Drink Safe Water: It’s important to drink safe, bottled water or water that has been purified.

Getting to Simon’s Town: Transportation Options and Logistics

Simon’s Town is located about 40 kilometers south of Cape Town, and there are several transportation options available to get there:

Airports Near Simon’s Town

The closest airport to Simon’s Town is Cape Town International Airport, which is located about 40 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Simon’s Town

The main bus station in Cape Town is the Cape Town Bus Terminal, which is located in the city center. From here, you can take a local bus or taxi to Simon’s Town.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Simon’s Town is easily accessible by car via the M3, M42, and M64 highways.

How to Get Around in Simon’s Town

Once you’re in Simon’s Town, there are several options for getting around:

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies in Simon’s Town, making it easy to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

  • Local Buses: Mini-buses and buses run on all main routes in Simon’s Town and are a cheap and convenient way to get around.

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Simon’s Town, but it’s always best to take a licensed taxi to ensure your safety.

Where to Go Next from Simon’s Town: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Simon’s Town is located in a prime location for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you might want to consider:

  • Cape Town: Just 40 kilometers north of Simon’s Town is Cape Town, one of the most popular tourist destinations in South Africa.

  • Stellenbosch: Located in the heart of the Cape Winelands, Stellenbosch is a charming university town famous for its wine and stunning scenery.

  • Hermanus: Known as the whale watching capital of South Africa, Hermanus is a popular destination for whale watching tours.

Conclusion

Simon’s Town is a captivating destination that offers a perfect mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re exploring the charming town center, soaking up the sun on the beach, or hiking in the nearby nature reserves, there’s something for everyone in this delightful seaside town. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Simon’s Town and inspired you to explore all that this fascinating destination has to offer.
