Introduction to Nature’s Valley

Nature’s Valley is a small village nestled between the sea and the Tsitsikamma Mountains, located in the heart of the Garden Route in the Western Cape, South Africa. The village boasts a pristine long beach, scenic walks, world-renowned forests and tall mountains, making it an ideal location for adventurers and nature lovers alike. Here is a comprehensive guide to help you explore Nature’s Valley.

List of Places to Visit in Nature’s Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Big Tree: A huge yellowwood tree estimated to be more than 800 years old and standing 36.6m tall.
  • The Millwood Gold Fields: This abandoned mining town was once a bustling hub for gold miners. The town is now a part of the Garden Route National Park and is popular for its hiking trails.
  • Fishing at Groot River: Fish for kob, grunter, and shad in the Groot River mouth.

Natural Attractions of Nature’s Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaches: Nature’s Valley has one of the most pristine long beaches in South Africa. The Plettenberg Bay Nature Reserve borders the eastern side of the beach, providing hiking trails with stunning views.
  • Hiking Trails: With a variety of trails ranging from easy to challenging, visitors can explore the Tsitsikamma Mountains and indigenous forests. Popular routes include the Otter Trail, Dolphin Trail, and the Tsitsikamma Forest Trail.
  • Mountain Biking: The routes around Nature’s Valley offer an unparalleled mountain biking experience, including picturesque views of the mountains and sea.
  • Kayaking: Explore the Groot River Lagoon and the rugged coastline in a kayak.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nature’s Valley

  • The Otter Trail: A 5-day hike along a 42km coastline from Storms River Mouth to Natures Valley.
  • The Dolphin Trail: A 2-5 day guided hike covering 17km to 40km depending on the chosen route.
  • Tsitsikamma Forest Trail: A 2-6 day hike through indigenous forests with beautiful views and serene environments.
  • Tours and Guides: There are a variety of tour and guide companies that offer hiking and biking tours.

Best Things to Do in Nature’s Valley: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Encounters: Visit Birds of Eden, Monkey Land or Elephant Sanctuary for a thrilling wildlife encounter.
  • Kayaking Adventure: Paddle down the Groot River Lagoon or along the coastal shores.
  • Zip-lining: Get an aerial view of the beautiful forest and mountains on this thrilling adventure.
  • Golfing: Play a round of golf at the local course in Plettenberg Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nature’s Valley

Day Activities
1 Arrive and settle in

Food and Drink Scene of Nature’s Valley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Fresh seafood dishes are a must-try when visiting Nature’s Valley. Try the seafood platter at Moons Country Kitchen.
  • Bunny Chow: A unique and delicious South African dish.
  • Malva Pudding: A fluffy and sweet traditional South African dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: South Africa is known for its world-renowned, award-winning wines. Try a glass from the local vineyard such as Bramon Wine Estate.
  • Craft Beer: Sample some of the local beers at the Jakkalsvlei Private Cellar.

Where to Stay in Nature’s Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodations to suit any traveler, from luxurious hotels to more budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses.

  • Kurland Hotel: A luxury boutique hotel located on a private estate.
  • Wild Spirit Backpackers: A lively backpackers hostel with a range of accommodations from cabins to tents.
  • Natures Valley Rest Camp: A family-friendly, self-catering camp located in the Garden Route National Park, offering cabins and camping spots.

What to Buy in Nature’s Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Souvenirs: Purchase hand-crafted, unique souvenirs at local markets.
  • Wines: Take home a bottle of South African wine as a remembrance of your trip.
  • Local Artwork: Support local artists by purchasing some of their artwork as a keepsake.

Local Culture and Customs of Nature’s Valley

The local customs revolve around nature and the environment, with conservation being a top priority. It is customary to respect the sanctity of nature and the indigenous fauna and flora.

Best Time to Visit Nature’s Valley

The best time to visit Nature’s Valley is during the summer season (October-March) when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. Wildlife encounters and seasonal festivals are also popular during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nature’s Valley

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife
  • Follow the code of conduct while hiking the trails
  • Tip restaurant staff if they provide exceptional service

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural environment
  • Feed the wildlife
  • Engage in activities that could harm the environment or wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Nature’s Valley

Overall, Nature’s Valley is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take certain precautions such as staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Nature’s Valley

It is important to use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and mosquito bites, respectively. It is recommended to bring any necessary medication and seek medical attention in case of an emergency.

Getting to Nature’s Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nature’s Valley

  • George Airport: About a 1.5-hour drive from Nature’s Valley.
  • Port Elizabeth Airport: About a 2.5-hour drive from Nature’s Valley.

Bus Stations Near Nature’s Valley

  • Plettenberg Bay Bus Station
  • Knysna Bus Station

Other Options

Taxis, car rentals, and private transfers are also available.

How to Get Around in Nature’s Valley

It is best to rent a car or bike to get around Nature’s Valley comfortably. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Nature’s Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Plettenberg Bay: A seaside town with stunning beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Tsitsikamma: A nature lover’s paradise with hiking trails, forest tours, and exciting activities.
  • Knysna: A historical town with scenic views and exciting activities.

Conclusion

Nature’s Valley offers an exceptional combination of scenic beauty, natural habitats, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are a nature lover or an adrenaline junkie, there is something for everyone in this small village in the Garden Route of South Africa.
