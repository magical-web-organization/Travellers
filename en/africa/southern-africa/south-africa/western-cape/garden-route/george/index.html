Introduction to George

George is a beautiful city located in Western Cape, South Africa. It is the sixth-largest city in the province and has been named after King George III of England. George is situated on the famous Garden Route, a scenic stretch of coastline situated between the Outeniqua Mountains and the Indian Ocean.

George is famous for its lush greenery, beautiful beaches, and tranquil atmosphere. The city boasts activities that cater to everyone, from nature lovers to adventurers, making it an ideal tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in George

There are many places to visit in George that cater to different interests. Here are some of the top attractions you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Outeniqua Transport Museum: This museum showcases the history of transport in South Africa, with a particular focus on railway history. Visitors can take a ride on a steam locomotive and view vintage cars and wagons.

  2. George Museum: George Museum is housed in the historic old Drostdy building, which was once home to the magistrates and the district administrator. This museum showcases the rich history of George, including its early settlers and the indigenous people who lived in the area.

  3. The Slave Tree: Legend has it that the tree was used as a whipping post for slaves in the past. Today, visitors can view the tree and learn about the dark history of slavery in the region.

Natural Attractions of George: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Outeniqua Mountains: Forming part of the Cape Fold Belt, the Outeniqua Mountains are a hiker’s paradise. Visitors can take a guided hike or a self-guided hike in the mountains, which boasts stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  2. Garden Route National Park: This national park spans over 150 km and is known for its rich biodiversity, including the famous Knysna Lagoon, fynbos, and forest. Visitors can indulge in activities like hiking, mountain biking, and bird-watching.

  3. Victoria Bay: Victoria Bay is a beautiful crescent-shaped beach located just outside of George. Visitors can indulge in activities like swimming, surfing, and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of George

  1. Craddock Peak Trail: This challenging hike takes visitors up to the peak of Craddock Peak, which offers panoramic views of the countryside below.

  2. Outeniqua Hiking Trail: This is a four-day hike that covers 108 km, starting from George and ending in Harkerville. The trail is graded as moderate to difficult, with diverse landscapes and stunning views of the Garden Route.

  3. George Peak: The peak lies within the Outeniqua Mountains and is the highest point in the George area. The hike to the peak is graded as moderate, offering views of the surrounding mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in George: Activities and Tours

  1. Golfing: George is known as the “Golfing Mecca” and is home to some of the best golf courses in the country, including Fancourt and Oubaai.

  2. Whale watching: Visitors can take a boat tour from nearby towns like Mossel Bay and Knysna to see Southern Right Whales and Humpback Whales during the migration season.

  3. Paragliding: Soar over the Garden Route with a paragliding tour that offers breathtaking views of the forests, mountains, and ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for George

Day 1: Arrive in George and settle into your accommodation.

Day 2: Start the day with a hike in the Outeniqua Mountains, followed by a visit to the Outeniqua Transport Museum.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive along the Garden Route and visit Victoria Bay, Wilderness, and Knysna.

Day 4: Visit the Garden Route National Park, indulge in hiking or mountain biking, and take in the spectacular views of the forest and lagoon.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at one of the prestigious golf courses in the city.

Day 6: Take a boat tour to see the whales, followed by an afternoon of paragliding.

Day 7: Relax on the beach in Victoria Bay or explore the city’s many shops and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of George

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Potjiekos: This is a hearty stew cooked in a traditional cast-iron pot over an open fire.

  2. Bunny Chow: A popular street food, bunny chow is a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry.

  3. Cape Malay curry: A fragrant, spicy curry that features a fusion of South African and Southeast Asian flavors.

Local Beverages

  1. Rooibos tea: A herbal tea native to the Western Cape, rooibos boasts many health benefits and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

  2. Wine: Western Cape is recognized as one of the best wine-growing regions in the country, and visitors should sample wines from the local vineyards.

Where to Stay in George: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Fancourt Hotel: A five-star hotel situated on the prestigious Fancourt Estate, this hotel offers luxury accommodation, world-class golf courses, and a spa.

  2. The Backpackers: A budget-friendly option, this hostel offers comfortable dorm rooms and private en-suite rooms.

  3. Acorn Guest House: This cozy guesthouse is situated in a quiet residential area, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to the city’s main attractions.

What to Buy in George: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Local Artwork: George is home to many artists, and visitors should explore the local galleries and markets to find unique pieces of art.

  2. Rooibos Tea: Visitors should stock up on the locally produced rooibos tea, which boasts many health benefits.

  3. Wine: George is located in the heart of the Garden Route wine region, and visitors should take advantage of the opportunity to purchase wines from local vineyards.

Local Culture and Customs of George

The locals in George are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should respect the region’s cultural diversity. It is customary to greet people with a handshake, and visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit George

The best time to visit George is during the summer months, between November and February when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also plan their visit around the whale migration season, which runs from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of George

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine, including rooibos tea and Cape Malay curry.
  3. Indulge in outdoor activities like hiking and paragliding.

Don’t:

  1. litter or damage the natural environment.
  2. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  3. Engage in illegal activities like drug use or poaching.

Safety and Health

Safety in George

George is a safe city to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take precautions like not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in George

Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to South Africa. Important vaccinations include Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and Yellow fever.

Getting to George: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near George

The nearest airport to George is George Airport, which is located about 7 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near George

There are several bus stations located in the center of George, offering connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach George by car, taxi, or private transfer from nearby towns and cities like Mossel Bay, Knysna, and Cape Town.

How to Get Around in George

Visitors can get around George using public transportation, including buses and taxis. However, the city is small enough to explore by foot, and visitors should consider renting a car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from George: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mossel Bay: A coastal town situated about an hour’s drive from George, Mossel Bay boasts a rich history and a range of outdoor activities like hiking and surfing.

  2. Knysna: Located about an hour’s drive from George, Knysna is famous for its lagoon and oyster farms.

  3. Cape Town: South Africa’s most famous city, Cape Town is situated about four hours’ drive from George.

Conclusion

George is a charming city that offers a range of activities and attractions for visitors. With its lush greenery, scenic views, and friendly locals, George is an ideal destination for anyone looking for a taste of South African life. Be sure to explore the city’s museums, try the local cuisine, and indulge in outdoor activities like hiking and paragliding.
