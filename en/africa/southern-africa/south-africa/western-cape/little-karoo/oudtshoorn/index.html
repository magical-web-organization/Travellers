Introduction to Oudtshoorn

Located in the Little Karoo region of Western Cape, Oudtshoorn is a charming town surrounded by scenic landscapes, wildlife and vast ostrich farms. It’s a great destination for those seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Oudtshoorn is a popular tourist destination and is well known for its scenic beauty, history, and cultural significance.

List of Places to Visit in Oudtshoorn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oudtshoorn is rich in history and culture, and there are numerous museums and historical places to visit. Here are some of the must-visit places:

1. Cango Caves

Cango Caves are the largest caves in Africa and one of the country’s most prominent natural wonders. These caves boast an amazing variety of limestone formations, including underground lakes and stalactites.

2. CP Nel Museum

The museum is home to a range of exhibits, including the town’s history, ostrich industry, and unique Karoo fauna.

3. Swartberg Pass

The Swartberg Pass is a scenic mountain pass that connects Oudtshoorn and Prince Albert. It’s regarded as one of the most picturesque routes in South Africa.

Natural Attractions of Oudtshoorn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oudtshoorn is surrounded by a breathtaking natural landscape, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the natural attractions of Oudtshoorn that tourists must visit:

1. Ostrich Farm

Ostrich farming is the backbone of the local economy in Oudtshoorn. Therefore, visiting an ostrich farm is a must-do activity if you’re in the town. Get ready to explore the biggest farm, where you can also try ostrich rides and dine on ostrich meat.

2. Cango Wildlife Ranch

Cango Wildlife Ranch is home to a variety of animals, including crocodiles, tigers, cheetahs, and more. It’s an incredible opportunity to get up-close and personal with these magnificent creatures.

3. Meerkat Adventures

Meerkat Adventures will give you the experience of the lifetime! Rise early, leave luxury behind and travel about a 30-minute drive from the Haus to an open field where you’ll experience the magic of a meerkat family emerging from their burrows, and bask in the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oudtshoorn

If you’re looking to explore Oudtshoorn’s countryside, hiking or biking is a great option. Here are a few incredible routes for both activities:

1. Swartberg Pass

The Swartberg Pass offers undeniably scenic views which are best enjoyed on a cycling tour of this heritage site.

2. Gamkaskloof

Gamkaskloof, also known as “The Hell,” is a secluded valley that remained cut off from the outside world until the 1960s. Hikers can take a fantastic two-day trek to the valley from Oudtshoorn.

3. Swartberg Nature Reserve

The Swartberg Nature Reserve offers a range of hikes and trails for all levels of fitness, each with breathtaking views of the valley below.

Best Things to Do in Oudtshoorn: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained in Oudtshoorn. Here are our top picks:

1. Hot Air Balloon Ride

A hot air balloon ride is an excellent way to explore the stunning landscapes of Little Karoo. Visitors can experience the incredible views while floating in the sky.

2. Wine Tasting

Oudtshoorn is not known for wine, but the region is famous for some of the best boutique wineries in South Africa. Visitors can enjoy wine tasting at Karusa Vineyards and other vineyards in the area.

3. Safari Game Drive

Visitors can witness Africa’s Big Five and other exquisite wildlife on safari tours through private game reserves near Oudtshoorn. These safari drives are a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oudtshoorn

Although Oudtshoorn has a lot to offer, a week-long itinerary will ensure that you get to explore all the prominent places without any rush. Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 1 Cango Caves, Wildlife Ranch
Day 2 Hiking on the Swartberg Pass

Food and Drink Scene of Oudtshoorn

Oudtshoorn offers a unique food and drink scene with traditional Karoo cuisine and wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Ostrich Meat

As Oudtshoorn is known for its ostrich farming, visitors should try ostrich meat. It’s one of the best culinary delights of Oudtshoorn.

2. Karoo Lamb

Karoo lamb is a staple food of the region, and you’ll find it in many restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

1. South African Wine

South African wine is known for its unique taste because of the favorable weather in the Western Cape. Visitors should try Shiraz, Pinotage, and Sauvignon Blanc.

2. Craft Beer

Craft beer has become popular in Oudtshoorn recently, and many small breweries can be found in and around the town.

Where to Stay in Oudtshoorn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oudtshoorn offers a range of accommodation options to fit every budget. Here are some of the top places to stay in Oudtshoorn:

1. De Zeekoe Guest Farm

De Zeekoe Guest Farm is an amazing guesthouse with a spa, swimming pool, hiking trails, and a fantastic restaurant serving local cuisine.

2. La Pension Guest House

La Pension Guest House is a stunning guesthouse with a pool, luxury rooms, and access to many outdoor activities around Oudtshoorn.

3. Backpackers Paradise

Backpackers Paradise is a budget-friendly hostel with a lively bar and various activities for visitors.

What to Buy in Oudtshoorn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oudtshoorn is known for its traditional ostrich products, including meat, feathers, and leather. Visitors can buy these and other souvenirs at gift shops around the town. Here are some of the recommended souvenirs:

1. Ostrich Leather Handbags and Wallets

Oudtshoorn is famous for producing high-quality ostrich leather products. Visitors should consider buying a handbag or purse for a unique souvenir.

2. Ostrich Eggs

Ostrich eggs are among the biggest in the world, and many shops sell painted ostrich-eggshell art, making for a good home decor idea.

Local Culture and Customs of Oudtshoorn

Oudtshoorn boasts a rich cultural history that visitors can experience all around town. Visitors must be respectful of the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Oudtshoorn

The perfect time to visit Oudtshoorn is from May to September. These months have the most refreshing temperatures, making it the ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oudtshoorn

Do:

  • Explore the Cango caves and wildlife ranch.
  • Visit the ostrich farms and experience the unique scenery.
  • Buy local souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Travel alone or walking alone at night in the town.
  • Disrespect the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oudtshoorn

Oudtshoorn is generally a safe place for visitors, but as with anywhere, there is always some risk. Visitors should avoid leaving their belongings unattended in public places and make sure their accommodation is secure.

Health in Oudtshoorn

Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance for their trip and talk to their doctor regarding vaccinations or health risks in the region.

Getting to Oudtshoorn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oudtshoorn is located between major cities, making it easy to get to.

Airports Near Oudtshoorn

The nearest airport to Oudtshoorn is George Airport, which is about 50 kilometers from the town.

Bus Stations Near Oudtshoorn

Visitors can take a bus from Cape Town, Port Elizabeth, or Johannesburg to Oudtshoorn.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a self-drive to Oudtshoorn from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Oudtshoorn

Visitors can get around Oudtshoorn by using a taxi or car rental service. Visitors can also get around the town on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Oudtshoorn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Knysna

Knysna is a charming coastal town just an hour’s drive from Oudtshoorn. It offers beautiful beaches, fantastic food, and a stunning lagoon.

2. Garden Route

Tourists can drive through the Garden Route which stretches from Mossel Bay to Port Elizabeth. A beautiful and winding coast makes this famous route one for those who like to see idyllic scenery.

Conclusion

Oudtshoorn is a fantastic town to visit in South Africa, offering a mix of natural beauty, unique cultural experiences, and lots of outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for an adventure, a cultural experience, or a relaxing destination, Oudtshoorn has it all.
