Introduction to Port St Johns

Tucked away in the Wild Coast region of Eastern Cape, South Africa, Port St Johns is a hidden gem that offers visitors a taste of authentic African culture and adventure. Endowed with pristine nature, stunning beaches, and jagged cliffs, Port St Johns provides a unique experience that is both enriching and fulfilling. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker, or history buff, there is something here for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight some of the best places to visit, eat, explore, and experience during your trip to Port St Johns.

List of Places to Visit in Port St Johns

Whether you want to learn about the local culture, hit the trails for some outdoor adventure, or simply take in the scenic views, Port St Johns has something to offer. Here are some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss during your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in learning about the local culture, Port St Johns has a series of cultural attractions that give insight into the region’s rich history and heritage. The must-visit places include:

  • The Umzimvubu Retreat Guest House: This is where Nelson Mandela stayed after he was released from prison in 1990.

  • Silaka Nature Reserve: The reserve has two waterfalls that make for stunning views and is a great spot for bird watching.

  • Mthatha Dam: The dam is great for picnicking, fishing, and sailing.

Natural Attractions of Port St Johns: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will be thrilled to explore the natural wonders that Port St Johns boasts of. From hiking trails to water sports, there is something for everyone. Some natural attractions worth exploring include:

  • Second Beach: A long stretch of white sand and shallow waters, it is great for swimming and surfing.

  • Third Beach: This beach is for the more experienced surfers who can handle stronger currents.

  • Breachaine Gorge: A popular hiking trail along the Umzimvubu River that leads to a beautiful waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port St Johns

Port St Johns has some of the most stunning hiking trails in South Africa. These trails cut through pristine forests and along the coast, offering breathtaking views along the way. Some popular routes include:

  • Silaka Nature Reserve Hiking Trail: This 8 km trail passes through the reserve and along the river bank.

  • Cape Hermes Trail: This 11 km trail takes you through a forest and on to a cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean.

  • Umzimbuvu River Walk: This easy 3 km trail leads to a waterfall and is perfect for beginner hikers.

Best Things to Do in Port St Johns: Activities and Tours

Port St Johns is full of opportunities for fun activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Fishing: Port St Johns is renowned for its superb fishing opportunities. Go out with a local guide and catch your own dinner.

  • Kayaking: The Umzimvubu River is perfect for kayaking enthusiasts, offering a chance to explore the unspoiled nature of the Wild Coast.

  • Sunset Safari: Spend an evening at the Mzantsi Sunrise Lodge watching the sunset while enjoying a game drive.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port St Johns

If you’re planning to stay for a week or longer, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most out of your trip.

Day Activity
1 Explore Second Beach, one of the most popular beaches in the area
2 Take a guided hike through the Silaka Nature Reserve
3 Go fishing with a local guide and catch your own dinner
4 Go kayaking along the Umzimvubu River
5 Take a tour of the Nelson Mandela Museum in Mthatha
6 Visit the Breachaine Gorge for a stunning waterfall view
7 End your trip with an afternoon game drive at Mzantsi Sunrise Lodge

Food and Drink Scene of Port St Johns

No trip to Port St Johns is complete without sampling some of the local dishes and drinks. Here are some of the must-try foods during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Umqombothi: A traditional Xhosa beer made from corn, malt, and sorghum.

  • Bunny Chow: A dish that originated in Durban and is a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry.

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos Tea: Grown only in South Africa, Rooibos tea is known for its health benefits and soothing properties.

  • Cane Spirits: Made from sugar cane, these spirits pack a punch and are perfect for those who love an authentic African experience.

Where to Stay in Port St Johns: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Port St Johns has various accommodation options for travelers ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are a few options:

  • Jungle Monkey: A backpacker’s hostel that offers dorms and private rooms at affordable prices with access to a swimming pool.

  • Umzimvubu Retreat Guest House: This guesthouse has various rooms available, including a honeymoon suite.

  • Mzantsi Sunrise Lodge: This luxury lodge offers a chance to view wild animals from your balcony.

What to Buy in Port St Johns: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Port St Johns by buying some of the local products and souvenirs. Here are some must-buy items from Port St Johns:

  • Beaded Jewelry: The Xhosa beadwork is a traditional art form and makes for a unique souvenir.

  • Hunting Knife: Used by the locals for hunting, these knives make for an excellent purchase and are great for outdoor enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Port St Johns

Port St Johns is rich in culture and history, and visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local customs and traditions. Some important things to take note of include:

  • It is customary to greet people with “Molo” or “Hello” in Xhosa.

  • Respect the local customs and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Port St Johns

The best time to visit Port St Johns is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. This is when the weather is mild, and the risk of malaria is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port St Johns

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Port St. Johns:

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Take care of the environment and keep it clean.

Don’t:

  • Don’t swim in the ocean alone.

  • Don’t walk around at night alone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port St Johns

Port St Johns is a relatively safe town, but it’s always advisable to take precautions to ensure your safety.

  • Avoid walking around alone at night.

  • Take precautions against mosquitoes and malaria.

Health in Port St Johns

Port St Johns has a medical clinic that provides basic medical services. However, it’s advisable to bring your own medication and consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Port St Johns: Transportation Options and Logistics

Port St Johns is a somewhat remote destination, but there are various transportation options to get you there. Some of the options include:

Airports Near Port St Johns

  • Mthatha Airport (also known as the Umtata Airport) is the closest airport located at about 120 km from Port St Johns.

  • East London Airport is located about 300 km from Port St Johns.

Bus Stations Near Port St Johns

  • Greyhound and Intercape bus services provide regular trips to Mthatha.

Other Options

  • Private taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Port St Johns

The best way to get around Port St Johns is by car, and there are car rental services available at the airport. However, if you’re not comfortable driving on the other side of the road, you can always take a taxi or shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Port St Johns: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Port St Johns is surrounded by various destinations worth visiting, including:

  • Mthatha: Visit the Nelson Mandela Museum and learn more about the history of South Africa.

  • Coffee Bay: A beautiful secluded bay with great surfing opportunities.

Conclusion

Port St Johns is a hidden gem of South Africa, offering visitors an authentic African experience. With natural beauty, stunning beaches, and rich cultural heritage, there is something here for everyone. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or history buff, you’ll find something to your liking in Port St Johns.
