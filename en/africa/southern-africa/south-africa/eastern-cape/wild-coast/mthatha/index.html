Introduction to Mthatha

Mthatha, also known as “Umtata,” is a city in the Eastern Cape Province of South Africa, located on the banks of the Mthatha River. The city is the cultural and economic hub of the Wild Coast region, home to several museums, historical sites, and natural attractions that attract tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Mthatha

If you’re planning a trip to Mthatha, make sure to visit these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nelson Mandela Museum: Dedicated to the life and legacy of Nelson Mandela, the museum is housed in two locations in Mthatha; the Bhunga Building and Qunu. Learn about Mandela’s childhood, political activism, imprisonment, and presidency.
  • Mthatha Dam: Located in the heart of the city, the dam offers a beautiful view of Mthatha, and it’s a popular spot for picnics, fishing, and camping.
  • Mthatha Museum: The museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Eastern Cape through art, crafts, and artifacts.
  • Umtata Airport: Established in 1959, this airport is one of the oldest in South Africa, and it has played a significant role in the country’s aviation history.

Natural Attractions of Mthatha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hluleka Nature Reserve: The reserve boasts rocky shores, lush forests, and beautiful beaches, making it a perfect spot for bird watching, hiking, fishing, and camping.
  • Wild Coast Sun: Located in the nearby town of Port Edward, the resort offers a range of outdoor activities, including golf, horse riding, and water sports.
  • The Silo: A popular lookout point in Mthatha that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mthatha

Mthatha is a great destination for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Mthatha to Coffee Bay: A challenging route that offers stunning views of the Wild Coast Region.
  • Silaka Nature Reserve: A nature reserve that offers several hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult.
  • Waterfall Bluff Trail: A challenging trail that takes you to the majestic Waterfall Bluff, offering stunning views of the waterfall and the Indian Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Mthatha: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the attractions mentioned above, here are some other things you can do in Mthatha:

  • Experience Xhosa Culture: Visit a local Xhosa village and learn about their traditions, language, and way of life.
  • Go on a Game Drive: Explore the nearby Inkwenkwezi Private Game Reserve and spot the “Big Five.”
  • Join a Township Tour: Discover the vibrant culture and history of Mthatha’s townships by joining a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mthatha

Day 1: Visit the Nelson Mandela Museum. Day 2-3: Explore the Hluleka Nature Reserve and relax at one of the beaches. Day 4: Take a hiking/biking tour in Silaka Nature Reserve. Day 5: Join a Township Tour. Day 6-7: Visit the nearby Wild Coast Sun Resort for golf, horse riding or water sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Mthatha

Mthatha has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Umngqusho: A traditional Xhosa dish made from beans and maize.
  • Chakalaka: A spicy relish made from onions, tomatoes, and peppers.
  • Biltong: Dried and cured meat.

Local Beverages

  • Amarula: A creamy liqueur made from the fruit of the Marula tree.
  • Rooibos Tea: A herbal tea native to South Africa.

Where to Stay in Mthatha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mthatha has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Mayfair Hotel: A luxurious 4-star hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Riverview Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse with stunning views of the Mthatha River.

What to Buy in Mthatha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mthatha is known for its traditional arts and crafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products you can buy:

  • Beadwork: The Xhosa people are known for their intricate beadwork, which you can find in local markets.
  • Woodcarvings: Local artisans create beautiful sculptures and carvings from wood.
  • Xhosa Blankets: Colorful blankets with geometric patterns that are a traditional Xhosa garment.

Local Culture and Customs of Mthatha

The Xhosa people are the predominant ethnic group in Mthatha, and their culture and traditions play a significant role in the city’s identity. Respect their customs and traditions, and try to learn a few words or phrases of the Xhosa language.

Best Time to Visit Mthatha

The best time to visit Mthatha is between April and October when the weather is dry and mild. Avoid visiting during December and January, as it is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mthatha

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Learn a few words of the Xhosa language.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Don’t buy or consume drugs or engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mthatha

Mthatha is a relatively safe city, but it’s always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables secure.

Health in Mthatha

Make sure to take all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Mthatha, especially if you plan to visit rural areas. Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and prevent malaria.

Getting to Mthatha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mthatha

The Umtata Airport is the only airport in Mthatha, located a few kilometers outside the city center.

Bus Stations Near Mthatha

Several bus companies operate in Mthatha, including Intercape and Greyhound.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or arrange for a private transfer to Mthatha from nearby cities like East London or Port Elizabeth.

How to Get Around in Mthatha

Mthatha has a good public transportation system, including mini-buses and taxis. You can also hire a car or a bicycle to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Mthatha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Coffee Bay: A small town known for its beautiful beaches and popular surfing spots.
  • Port St. Johns: A picturesque town located at the mouth of the Umzimvubu River, offering fishing, hiking, and kayaking opportunities.
  • Inkwenkwezi Private Game Reserve: A private game reserve located near Mthatha, home to several species of wildlife.

Conclusion

Mthatha is a vibrant city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and modern amenities. Use this guide to plan your perfect Mthatha adventure and discover all that this beautiful city has to offer.
