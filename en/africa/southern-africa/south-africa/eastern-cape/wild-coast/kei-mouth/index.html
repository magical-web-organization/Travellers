Your Comprehensive Travel Guide to Kei Mouth, South Africa

Kei Mouth is a small town located on the Wild Coast of Eastern Cape, South Africa. This hidden gem offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions, making it an ideal destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Kei Mouth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kei Mouth Museum: This small museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of Kei Mouth and its surroundings. The exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and documents related to the town’s colonial and indigenous past.
  2. Cathcart Cemetery: This historical graveyard is the final resting place of many prominent figures from the Eastern Cape’s past, including Sir Thomas Maclear, a British astronomer who lived and worked in the region in the 19th century.
  3. Kraalbosdam Methodist Church: This small church was first built in the 1860s and serves as a testament to the influence of Christianity on the Xhosa people of the Eastern Cape.

Natural Attractions of Kei Mouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kei River Mouth: This stunning estuary offers a range of activities, including boating, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can take a boat trip upriver to explore the pristine, forested valley.
  2. Hamburg Dunes: Located just a short drive from Kei Mouth, these amazing sand dunes are a natural wonder that attracts visitors from all over the world.
  3. Kruger Nature Reserve: This small private reserve offers a chance to spot a variety of wildlife, including zebra, giraffe, and wildebeest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kei Mouth

  1. Coastal Hiking Trail: This trail runs along the coastline from Kei Mouth to Morgan Bay and offers spectacular views of the ocean, coastal forests, and rocky cliffs.
  2. Kei River Valley Hiking Trail: This trail winds through the lush forests and open grasslands of the Kei River Valley and offers opportunities to spot rare bird species, such as the Knysna Lourie.
  3. Wild Coast Meander: This scenic, self-drive route takes visitors through the heart of the Wild Coast region and offers stunning views of the rugged coastline and rural countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kei Mouth: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing Charters: Kei Mouth offers some of the best fishing opportunities in South Africa. Visitors can join a fishing charter and catch a variety of species, including blacktail, kob, and yellowfish.
  2. Horseback Riding: Explore the rugged terrain of Kei Mouth on horseback and experience the natural beauty from a unique perspective.
  3. Surfing Lessons: The Wild Coast is known for its amazing surf spots, and Kei Mouth is no exception. Take a surfing lesson and catch some waves in one of South Africa’s most beautiful locations.

Food and Drink Scene of Kei Mouth

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Braai: South Africa’s traditional barbeque, featuring grilled meat, boerewors (a type of sausage), and pap (a type of porridge).
  2. Bunny chow: A South African fast food dish that originated in Durban, consisting of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry.
  3. Potjiekos: A traditional South African stew that is cooked over an open fire in a cast-iron pot.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: South Africa has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors to Kei Mouth can sample a range of locally brewed beers, such as Darling Brew and Drifter Brewing Company.
  2. Wine: The Eastern Cape is home to some of South Africa’s best wineries, including Bouchard Finlayson and Hamilton Russell Vineyards.
  3. Rooibos Tea: This caffeine-free tea is grown in the Western Cape and is known for its unique, naturally sweet flavor.

Where to Stay in Kei Mouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Trennerys Hotel: This beachfront hotel offers comfortable accommodation in a stunning location.
  2. Kei Mouth Guest Lodge: A cozy, intimate guesthouse located in the heart of Kei Mouth.
  3. Cottages at the Village: Self-catering cottages set in a tranquil garden setting, just a short walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Kei Mouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Xhosa Crafts: The Xhosa people are famous for their beautiful beadwork and woven baskets.
  2. Wild Coast Wines: Bring home a bottle of wine from one of the award-winning wineries of the Eastern Cape.
  3. Local Artwork: Explore the local galleries and art shops to find unique pieces of artwork that capture the spirit of Kei Mouth.

Local Culture and Customs of Kei Mouth

The Eastern Cape is home to many different cultures and people, including the Xhosa, Afrikaans, and British. Visitors to Kei Mouth should take the time to learn about the local customs and traditions, such as the importance of cattle to the Xhosa people and the history of the British colonial period.

Best Time to Visit Kei Mouth

Kei Mouth enjoys a moderate, Mediterranean climate, with warm summers and mild winters. The best time to visit is during the summer months (December to February), when the temperatures are warm, and the beaches are busy. However, visitors can enjoy Kei Mouth’s natural beauty and cultural attractions year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kei Mouth

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions of the different cultures in the region.
  2. Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the region, including the stunning coastline and majestic forests.
  3. Be open to trying new foods, such as the traditional South African dishes mentioned above.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in any activities that are harmful to the environment, such as littering or disrupting fragile ecosystems.
  2. Be disrespectful to wildlife or domestic animals, especially those that are culturally significant.
  3. Ignore the importance of South Africa’s complicated history and ongoing social issues.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kei Mouth

Kei Mouth is generally a safe destination for visitors, but travelers should still take common-sense precautions, such as locking their doors and securing their belongings. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with swimming in the ocean or hiking in remote areas and take appropriate safety measures.

Health in Kei Mouth

Visitors to Kei Mouth should ensure that they are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations and should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as malaria is present in some areas of South Africa. Travelers should also be aware of the risks associated with drinking tap water and should stick to bottled water whenever possible.

Getting to Kei Mouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kei Mouth

The closest major airport to Kei Mouth is East London Airport (ELS), which is approximately 100 kilometers away. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or take a shuttle or taxi to Kei Mouth.

Bus Stations Near Kei Mouth

Intercape and Greyhound are the two main South African intercity bus companies that operate in the Eastern Cape. Visitors can take a bus from East London or Port Elizabeth to Kei Mouth or a nearby town, such as Umtata or Queenstown.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Kei Mouth from East London or Port Elizabeth. The N2 highway runs through the Eastern Cape and offers a scenic and relatively safe driving experience. Alternatively, visitors can take a guided tour of the area, which includes transportation to and from Kei Mouth.

How to Get Around in Kei Mouth

Visitors can explore Kei Mouth and its surroundings on foot, by bike, or by car. There are several car rental companies located in nearby towns, such as East London and King William’s Town, and visitors can also rent bikes from local rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Kei Mouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Morgan Bay: This charming seaside town is located just a short drive from Kei Mouth and offers a range of activities, including hiking, swimming, and fishing.
  2. Haga Haga: This small fishing village is another popular destination for visitors to the Wild Coast and is known for its stunning beaches and relaxed atmosphere.
  3. Wild Coast Sun: This resort and casino complex is located on the border between the Eastern Cape and KwaZulu-Natal and offers a range of activities, including golf, spa treatments, and gaming.

Conclusion

Kei Mouth is a unique and beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and rich cultural heritage of the Eastern Cape. With its stunning beaches, amazing outdoor adventures, and vibrant cultural scene, Kei Mouth is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to South Africa.
