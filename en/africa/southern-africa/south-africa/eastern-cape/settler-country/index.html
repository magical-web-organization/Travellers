Introduction to Settler Country

Settler Country is a region located in the Eastern Cape province of South Africa. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. The area was named after the British Settlers who arrived in the Eastern Cape during the early 19th century.

List of Places to Visit in Settler Country

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • 1820 Settlers National Monument - A tribute to the British Settlers who arrived in 1820.
  • The British Settlers Museum - A museum dedicated to the history and heritage of British Settlers.
  • Ox Wagon Trail - A historical route that takes visitors through the landscapes and cultural heritage of the region.
  • Frontier Country - A collection of historical towns located in the Eastern Cape.

Natural Attractions of Settler Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baviaanskloof Nature Reserve - A natural wonderland that stretches over 200 km with picturesque views, hiking trails, and mountain passes.
  • Mountain Zebra National Park - A conservation park boasting small game and majestic mountain views.
  • Grahamstown - A historic university town offering parks and nature reserves on its outskirts.
  • Wild Coast - A rugged stretch of coastline acclaimed for its unique beauty, unspoiled beaches, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Settler Country

  • Baviaanskloof Trail - A 13-day hiking trail that crosses through the Baviaanskloof Nature Reserve.
  • Donkin Heritage Trail - A scenic walking route that takes visitors through historic sites and buildings in Port Elizabeth.
  • Otter Trail - A 42.5 km hiking trail that follows the rugged Garden Route coastline.

Best Things to Do in Settler Country: Activities and Tours

  • Shamwari Game Reserve - A premier wildlife rehabilitation and conservation park offering safari tours.
  • Kirkwood Wildlife Festival - A celebration of Africa’s wildlife featuring game drives, bird watching, and live music.
  • Addo Elephant National Park - A national park featuring a diverse range of fauna and flora.

Food and Drink Scene of Settler Country

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braaivleis - South African grilled meat
  • Biltong - Dried and cured meat, similar to beef jerky
  • Chakalaka - A spicy vegetable relish

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos Tea - A caffeine-free herbal tea with numerous health benefits.
  • Wine - South Africa has a booming wine industry, with Settler Country being no exception.
  • Beer - South Africa has a diverse range of beers, with local breweries located throughout the country.

Where to Stay in Settler Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Boardwalk Hotel and Casino - A 5-star hotel with a top-notch casino and scenic sea views.
  • The Royal St Andrews Hotel and Spa - A historic hotel located in Grahamstown with a spa, restaurant, and bar.
  • Tsitsikamma Village Inn - A charming inn located in the heart of Tsitsikamma with cozy rooms and a garden.

What to Buy in Settler Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beadwork - Settler Country offers beaded crafts and jewelry made by locals.
  • Local Wine - South African wine is highly regarded, with many vineyards in Settler Country.
  • Biltong - A popular South African snack made from cured meat.

Local Culture and Customs of Settler Country

  • Respect the elders and the community’s customs and traditions.
  • Do not bargain in local markets, as it might be considered rude.
  • Always ask before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Settler Country

The best time to visit Settler Country is from March to May and September to November. The weather is moderate during these months, and you can avoid the peak tourism season from December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Settler Country

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Keep a safe distance from wild animals in parks and reserves.
  • Respect local customs, beliefs, and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t walk around alone in isolated areas.
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car or unattended.
  • Don’t take photographs of military installations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Settler Country

  • Avoid walking around alone at night.
  • Use reputable taxi services and public transportation.
  • Keep valuables in a secure place.

Health in Settler Country

  • Make sure to get all the necessary vaccinations before traveling.
  • Be wary of consuming tap water.
  • Apply sunscreen before going outdoors and bring mosquito repellent.

Getting to Settler Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Settler Country

  • Port Elizabeth Airport - The main airport in the region, with domestic and international flights.
  • East London Airport - Located in the city of East London.

Bus Stations Near Settler Country

  • Greyhound - Offers bus services from Johannesburg to Port Elizabeth and other major cities in South Africa.
  • Intercape - Provides bus services from Cape Town, Johannesburg to Port Elizabeth and other major cities in South Africa.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - Many car rental companies are located in South Africa.
  • Train - The Shosholoza Meyl Train offers a scenic ride from Johannesburg to Port Elizabeth.

How to Get Around in Settler Country

The best way to travel around Settler Country is by renting a car. Public transportation is available but not as frequent. Taxis and shuttle services can be arranged through hotels and tour operators.

Where to Go Next from Settler Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Elizabeth - A coastal city known for its beautiful beaches.
  • Garden Route - A scenic coastal drive that spans across the Western and Eastern Cape.
  • Kruger National Park - A world-renowned game reserve located in the Limpopo and Mpumalanga provinces.

Conclusion

Settler Country is a hidden gem in South Africa that offers nature, culture, and history in one destination. From hiking trails to wildlife reserves, Settler Country has something for everyone. With its vibrant food and drink scene and welcoming locals, it’s a place that needs to be added to your travel bucket list.
