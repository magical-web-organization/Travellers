Introduction to Kenton-on-Sea

Kenton-on-Sea is a picturesque coastal town located in Sunshine Coast, Eastern Cape, South Africa. With its pristine beaches, fascinating history, and various outdoor adventures, the town attracts travelers from all over the world looking to soak up some sunshine and discover the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Kenton-on-Sea

There’s no shortage of things to do and see in Kenton-on-Sea. Here is a list of the top places to visit in town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Great Fish Point Lighthouse: Built-in the 18th century, the lighthouse provides a glimpse of the town’s history and affords beautiful views of the shoreline.
  • Albany Museum: This museum showcases the cultural diversity of South Africa from ancient times to modern days, with exhibits of historical artifacts, artworks, and lifestyle objects.
  • Fort D’ Acre Reserve: This fort dates back to the 19th century and provides a history lesson on the British colonialism era in Eastern Cape.

Natural Attractions of Kenton-on-Sea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kariega Game Reserve: With its Big 5 wildlife and other wild animals, this reserve is perfect for a game drive on a safari tour or relaxing on the river cruise.
  • Boat Tours: Local boat tours are a great way to explore the Kenton-on-Sea coast and take advantage of the beauty.
  • Beaches: The town has many clean, unspoiled beaches offering a variety of outdoor activities, including swimming, surfing, snorkeling, and paddling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kenton-on-Sea

Kenton-on-Sea is a walker’s paradise with several nature walks and trail tours to select from, including:

  • Kowie River trail: This trail offers an opportunity to hike and bike lush riverside vegetation, while enjoying the wildlife and natural scenery.
  • Bushman’s River Trail: Take in the scenic views of the river and mountains from this hike.

Best Things to Do in Kenton-on-Sea: Activities and Tours

  • Deep-Sea Fishing: Get an adrenaline rush while deep-sea fishing with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: With the clear, calm waters of the Kariega and Bushman’s River, kayaking and canoeing are popular pastimes.
  • Local Festivals: Experience local cultural festivals like the Mantis and Moon backpackers’ festival and Grahamstown National Arts Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kenton-on-Sea

Day 1 – 3: Wildlife and game drives at Kariega Game Reserve and the surrounding areas.

Day 4 – 5: Hiking and biking tours along a network of nature trails around the Kenton-on-Sea coast.

Day 6: Visit the Great Fish Point Lighthouse, Albini Museum, and Fort D’ Acre Reserve.

Day 7: Relax at some of the pristine beaches around town.

Food and Drink Scene of Kenton-on-Sea

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braai: South Africa’s signature dish, a BBQ meat feast.
  • Boerewors: This traditional South African sausage is great for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.
  • Bunny Chow: A spicy and unique dish consisting of a bread loaf filled with meat curry.

Local Beverages

  • South African Wines: South Africa’s wine is known for its variety of sweet and dry wine types.
  • Beer: Irrespective of the multicultural flavors, beer is the most preferred beverage.
  • Rooibos Tea: Try the locally grown Rooibos tea for a caffeine-free taste.

Where to Stay in Kenton-on-Sea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kenton-on-Sea has different accommodation properties to fit various budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Middle Beach Lodge: This lodge has a fantastic seafront view, luxurious rooms, and continental breakfast.

  • Kenton-on-Sea Holiday House: This holiday home has a scenic view of the Indian Ocean and the surrounding coastal vegetation.

  • Riverside Lodge: This lodge is great for family holiday accommodation because family room options are available for comfort and luxury.

What to Buy in Kenton-on-Sea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woven Crafts: Locally made woven crafts like baskets and bags are a must-buy experience.
  • Wooden Sculptures: Buy carved sculptures and ornaments at the local market as souvenirs.
  • Jewelry: Even if souvenir shopping is not on your mind, you will love the unique jewelry designs at the artisanal boutiques in town.

Local Culture and Customs of Kenton-on-Sea

Kenton-on-Sea is a multi-cultural melting pot, and it’s essential to know the following customs:

  • Traditional Dress: Zulu traditional dress is the most recognizable in Eastern Cape.
  • Music and Dance: Music and dance are an integral part of South African culture and are central to most of the country’s festivals.
  • Language: There are eleven official languages in South Africa, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas like Kenton-on-Sea.

Best Time to Visit Kenton-on-Sea

The best time to visit Kenton-on-Sea is from December to February when it is summer in South Africa, and the weather is warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kenton-on-Sea

Do:

  • Learn how to say “hello” and “thank you” in the local languages.
  • Try the native cuisine and attend the local festivals to explore the culture.
  • Always wear sunblock as the sun can be harsh.

Don’t:

  • Do not take photos or videos of the locals without permission.
  • Do not touch the wildlife in game reserves or other natural habitats.
  • Do not leave your valuables unattended on beaches, on the street, or other public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kenton-on-Sea

Kenton-on-Sea is a safe tourist destination, but visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Keep your valuables and passport secure while traveling.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Kenton-on-Sea

  • Malaria is not a problem in Kenton-on-Sea but make sure to get vaccinated before traveling from your home country..
  • The town has a sufficient number of medical facilities, but it’s still best to seek medical advice before travel.

Getting to Kenton-on-Sea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kenton-on-Sea

The closest airport is Port Elizabeth International Airport, which is about 120 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kenton-on-Sea

The closest bus stations are in Port Elizabeth, which connects to Kenton-on-Sea by a cab, taxi, or shuttle service.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent way to get around and explore the surrounding areas at your pace.

How to Get Around in Kenton-on-Sea

Walking or biking are the best options to explore the town, but there are also shuttle services available to get around the adjacent areas.

Where to Go Next from Kenton-on-Sea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • South African Seaside Towns: Discover other seaside towns like Port Elizabeth, Jeffrey’s Bay, and Cape St. Francis.
  • Grahamstown: Known for its colonial architecture and rich history, it’s an hour’s drive from Kenton-on-Sea.
  • Addo Elephant National Park: Get to see the Big 7 wild animals, including the newly introduced killer whales.

Conclusion

To get a taste of South African culture and outdoor adventures, Kenton-on-Sea is the place to go. From getting lost in local culture, discovering the natural beauty of Eastern Cape, or enjoying a tranquil getaway, Kenton-on-Sea is a town worth visiting. Ensure to check off places to visit, local cuisine, traditional customs, and anything that captures your interest.
