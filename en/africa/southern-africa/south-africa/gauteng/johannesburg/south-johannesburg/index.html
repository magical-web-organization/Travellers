Introduction to South Johannesburg

South Johannesburg, also known as Soweto, is a vibrant township in Johannesburg, Gauteng, South Africa. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the city, offering a rich cultural and historical experience. The township played a significant role in the anti-apartheid movement in the country, making it even more appealing to those interested in learning about the past.

List of Places to Visit in South Johannesburg

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, South Johannesburg has something to offer. Here is a list of some of the top attractions in the township:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apartheid Museum The Apartheid Museum is one of the most significant museums in South Africa, providing a comprehensive look at the country’s history of apartheid. The museum features exhibits on the rise and fall of apartheid, with interactive displays and audio-visual presentations.

  • Hector Pieterson Memorial and Museum The Hector Pieterson Memorial and Museum is named after a young boy who was killed during the Soweto Uprising of 1976. The museum showcases the events of that day and the children who died in the protests.

  • Nelson Mandela House The Nelson Mandela House is a modest dwelling where Nelson Mandela lived from 1946 to the early 1960s. The house is now a museum that displays the belongings and photographs of Mandela and his family.

  • Walter Sisulu Square Walter Sisulu Square is a historic site in Soweto where the Freedom Charter was adopted in 1955. Today, the square is a lively cultural hub that hosts various events and festivals.

Natural Attractions of South Johannesburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Klipspruit Valley Klipspruit Valley is a serene area just outside of Soweto, surrounded by rolling hills and forests. It’s a great place to picnic, hike, or ride a bike while enjoying the beautiful views.

  • Diepkloof Park Diepkloof Park is an urban park in Soweto that offers recreational activities such as jogging, cycling, and picnicking. It’s also a popular spot for birdwatching.

  • Kopjeskraal Kopjeskraal is a nature reserve just a short drive from Soweto, featuring an array of wildlife and hiking trails. It’s the perfect place for a day trip and a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Johannesburg

Soweto also boasts several hiking and biking trails that offer a unique way to explore the area. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Walter Sisulu Trail The Walter Sisulu Trail is a moderate hiking trail that takes you through the scenic Klipspruit Valley. The trail is 10 kilometers long, and you can expect to see stunning views of the surrounding hills and landscape.

  • Mofolo Park Mofolo Park is a popular spot for jogging, cycling, and hiking. The park has various trails that take you through the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in South Johannesburg: Activities and Tours

  • Soweto Bicycle Tour A bike tour is a great way to explore Soweto, soaking in the vibrant atmosphere, and learning about the local culture and history with a knowledgeable guide. Some of the most popular bike tours in Soweto include the Soweto Bicycle Tour and the Soweto Heritage Tour.

  • Soweto Theatre The Soweto Theatre is a cultural hub that features various performances throughout the year, showcasing local talent across different genres.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Johannesburg

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to South Johannesburg:

  • Day 1: Visit the Apartheid Museum and the Hector Pieterson Memorial and Museum.
  • Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Kopjeskraal.
  • Day 3: Hike the Walter Sisulu Trail or bike through Soweto.
  • Day 4: Visit the Nelson Mandela House and learn about his life and legacy.
  • Day 5: Relax in Diepkloof Park.
  • Day 6: Watch a performance at the Soweto Theatre.
  • Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products in the township.

Food and Drink Scene of South Johannesburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mogodu Mogodu is a hearty and flavorful dish made with tripe, onions, and spices. It’s a popular dish in South Africa, and you can find it in most local restaurants in Soweto.

  • Chakalaka Chakalaka is a spicy vegetable relish and a staple dish in South Africa. It’s usually served with pap (maize porridge) or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Umqombothi Umqombothi is a traditional beer made with maize or sorghum, yeast, and water. It has a distinctive taste and is usually served at cultural events and ceremonies.

  • Amarula Amarula is a cream liqueur made with the fruit of the African marula tree. It’s a popular drink in South Africa and is often served over ice as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in South Johannesburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Soweto offers a range of accommodations to cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Soweto Hotel and Conference Center The Soweto Hotel and Conference Center is a luxurious hotel with excellent amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and outdoor pool.

  • Lebo’s Soweto Backpackers Lebo’s Soweto Backpackers is a popular hostel in the heart of Soweto that offers affordable accommodation and tours.

What to Buy in South Johannesburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Crafts Many local artists and crafters sell their handmade items in the township. You can find everything from beaded jewelry and baskets to wood carvings and traditional clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of South Johannesburg

Iziko (respect) and Ubuntu (humanity) are important cultural values in Soweto. Visitors should show respect for the community and be mindful of local customs, particularly when visiting sacred or significant places.

Best Time to Visit South Johannesburg

The best time to visit South Johannesburg is during the dry season which runs from May to September. The weather is mild, and there is less chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Johannesburg

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Learn a few words of the local language (Zulu, Xhosa, or Sotho).
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Wander around the township at night alone.
  • Make assumptions or stereotypes about the local people and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Johannesburg

Soweto is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s always advisable to take precautions, such as avoiding walking around the township alone at night and carrying valuables with you at all times.

Health in South Johannesburg

It’s essential to check with your doctor before traveling to South Africa to ensure you have all necessary vaccinations. It’s also wise to take precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases if you’re spending time in rural areas.

Getting to South Johannesburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Soweto is located just a 30-minute drive from the Johannesburg city center. There are various transportation options to reach the township, including:

  • Taxi You can take a taxi from anywhere in Johannesburg to Soweto. It’s also possible to use the metered taxi services in the area.

  • Soweto Bicycle Tour The Soweto Bicycle Tour is a fun and eco-friendly way to reach the township while exploring the local surroundings.

Airports Near South Johannesburg

The nearest airport to Soweto is the O.R Tambo International Airport, about a 40-minute drive away. Several taxi and shuttle services operate between the airport and Soweto.

Bus Stations Near South Johannesburg

There are several bus operators that offer services to Soweto, including Gautrain and Intercape.

Other Options

Car rental is also available from the O.R Tambo International Airport or from the Johannesburg city center.

How to Get Around in South Johannesburg

There are various ways to get around Soweto, including:

  • Taxi Taxis are the most popular form of public transportation in Soweto. However, it’s necessary to negotiate the price beforehand.

  • Bus There are several bus services in Soweto that run between different parts of the township.

  • Bike Biking is becoming a popular way to explore Soweto, with many bike tours available in the area.

Where to Go Next from South Johannesburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gold Reef City Gold Reef City is an amusement park and entertainment complex in Johannesburg that offers fun activities for the whole family, including roller coasters, a casino, and a theater.

  • Pilanesberg National Park Pilanesberg National Park is a wildlife reserve located about two hours from Johannesburg. It’s home to the Big Five (lions, elephants, rhinos, buffaloes, and leopards) and a must-visit for nature lovers.

Conclusion

Soweto is a vibrant and culturally significant township that offers a unique experience of South African history and culture. With its stunning natural scenery, diverse food and drink scene, and friendly locals, Soweto is a destination that is sure to captivate any visitor.
