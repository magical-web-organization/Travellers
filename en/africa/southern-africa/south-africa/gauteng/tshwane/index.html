Introduction to Tshwane

Tshwane is the executive capital of South Africa and is located in the north of Gauteng Province. It is a vibrant, cosmopolitan city that offers visitors a unique blend of tradition, culture, and history. From its museums and historical sites to its natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Tshwane has something to offer everyone.

Planning a trip to Tshwane? Look no further! In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Tshwane

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tshwane has a rich cultural and historical heritage that can be explored in its museums and historical sites.

  • Voortrekker Monument: The Voortrekker Monument is a national heritage site and commemorates the Great Trek of the Afrikaner people. It offers a fascinating insight into the nation’s history and has a museum, art gallery, and restaurant on the premises.
  • Freedom Park: A must-visit for all visitors, Freedom Park is dedicated to the nation’s struggle for freedom and offers a history of South Africa from its earliest times. It is built on the site where the apartheid government imprisoned political activists and has a museum, art gallery, and restaurant.
  • Kruger House Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the life of the former president of South Africa, Paul Kruger. Visitors can see his personal belongings and get an up-close look at the history of South Africa during this time period.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tshwane boasts a range of natural attractions that can be enjoyed by nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

  • Cullinan Diamond Mine: The mine offers visitors the opportunity to learn about the diamond industry, the history of the Cullinan Diamond, and can even take a tour into the mine itself.
  • Jan Cilliers Park: Jan Cilliers Park is a beautiful park in the heart of Tshwane that is perfect for a picnic or a quiet walk. It boasts a range of flowers, trees, and birds and offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Union Buildings: The Union Buildings are an iconic landmark in South Africa and offer breathtaking views of Tshwane. Visitors can explore the gardens surrounding the building and learn about the history of South Africa.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tshwane

Tshwane has a range of hiking and biking routes that offer spectacular views of the city and surrounding countryside.

  • Hennops Hiking Trail: The Hennops Hiking Trail is a popular trail that offers both easy and more challenging routes. The trail follows the Hennops River and takes hikers through a range of terrain, including river crossings and rocky terrain.
  • Groenkloof Nature Reserve: The Groenkloof Nature Reserve offers a range of hiking and biking trails and is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including giraffes, zebras, and antelope.

Best Things to Do in Tshwane: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Tshwane - from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone.

  • Soweto Tour: A tour of Soweto is a must-do when in Tshwane. Visitors can see and learn about the history of the township, including a visit to Nelson Mandela’s former home.
  • Pretoria Zoo: The National Zoological Gardens of South Africa, also known as the Pretoria Zoo, is a must-visit attraction for animal lovers. The zoo is home to a range of animals from all over the world, including big cats, primates, and reptiles.
  • Tswaing Meteorite Crater: Located just outside Tshwane, the Tswaing Meteorite Crater is a fascinating natural attraction that lets visitors explore the impact of a meteorite strike that happened over 200,000 years ago.
  • Hot air balloon ride: A hot air balloon ride is an unforgettable way to see Tshwane and the surrounding countryside. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the city and its surroundings while floating above the clouds.

Food and Drink Scene of Tshwane

Must-Try Dishes

South Africa has a rich culinary heritage, and Tshwane is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a range of traditional dishes that are delicious and unique to the area.

  • Bunny Chow: A popular street food, bunny chow consists of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry.
  • Boerewors: A traditional South African sausage that is enjoyed with pap, a maize-based porridge.
  • Potjiekos: A stew that is cooked over an open fire, potjiekos typically includes meat and vegetables and is a popular dish for social gatherings.

Local Beverages

Tshwane has a range of local beverages that visitors should try.

  • Rooibos tea: Made from the leaves of the rooibos plant, rooibos tea is a popular South African beverage that is enjoyed for its health benefits and delicious taste.
  • Amarula: A cream liqueur made from the fruit of the African marula tree, Amarula is a popular drink in South Africa.
  • Castle Lager: A popular beer in South Africa, Castle Lager is brewed in Tshwane and is the perfect accompaniment to a traditional South African meal.

Where to Stay in Tshwane: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tshwane has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

  • Protea Hotel by Marriott Pretoria Hatfield: A modern, stylish hotel located in the heart of the city, the Protea Hotel by Marriott Pretoria Hatfield offers guests easy access to the city’s attractions and a range of amenities.
  • The Backpack: A popular backpacker hostel that offers both private and dormitory-style accommodation, The Backpack is located in the trendy suburb of Hatfield and has a relaxed, welcoming atmosphere.
  • The Nobleman Boutique Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the upmarket suburb of Brooklyn, The Nobleman Boutique Hotel offers guests a range of high-end amenities and services.

What to Buy in Tshwane: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring back home? Here are some locally made products that make great gifts.

  • Beadwork: The traditional art of beadwork is a major part of South African culture and offers visitors an opportunity to buy unique, handmade pieces.
  • African Crafts: From carvings to paintings, Tshwane is home to many amazing local artists and crafters who sell their work in markets and shops around the city.
  • Wine and Spirits: South Africa is known for its wine and spirits, and Tshwane has a range of local products that make great gifts for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tshwane

South Africa has a rich and diverse culture that is influenced by its history and the many different ethnic groups that make up its population. Visitors should take the time to learn about and respect the local culture and customs while in Tshwane.

Best Time to Visit Tshwane

Tshwane enjoys a temperate climate with warm, sunny summers and cool, dry winters. The best time to visit depends on your preferences and what you want to see and do.

  • Summer (December to February): Summer is the peak tourist season in Tshwane, and visitors can expect warm temperatures, sunny skies, and long days.
  • Autumn (March to May): Autumn is a beautiful time to visit Tshwane, with mild temperatures and beautiful foliage.
  • Winter (June to August): Winter can be cold and dry in Tshwane, but visitors can still enjoy a range of activities like hiking and wildlife spotting.
  • Spring (September to November): Spring is a beautiful time to visit, with mild temperatures and beautiful wildflower displays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tshwane

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Learn some of the local language, like isiZulu or Xhosa.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take precautions to stay safe.

Don’t:

  • Engage in behavior that is culturally inappropriate or disrespectful.
  • Leave your belongings unattended.
  • Walk alone at night in areas that are unfamiliar to you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tshwane

Tshwane is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to stay safe.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables.
  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas at night.

Health in Tshwane

Visitors should take precautions to protect their health while in Tshwane.

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Get vaccinated before traveling, especially for diseases like malaria and yellow fever.
  • Carry insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Tshwane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tshwane

Tshwane is served by two major airports.

  • OR Tambo International Airport: Located in Johannesburg, OR Tambo International Airport is the nearest international airport to Tshwane.
  • Lanseria International Airport: Located in the north of Johannesburg, Lanseria International Airport is a smaller airport that is often used for domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Tshwane

Tshwane is well-connected by bus, with several bus stations located throughout the city.

  • Pretoria Gautrain Station: The Gautrain is a rapid transit railway system that connects Tshwane with Johannesburg and other major cities in Gauteng.
  • Pretoria Mamelodi Bus Station: Located in the Mamelodi township, the Mamelodi Bus Station offers connections to other major cities in South Africa.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at OR Tambo International Airport or Lanseria International Airport and drive to Tshwane.
  • Taxis and Uber: Taxis and Uber are a convenient way to get around Tshwane and can be easily arranged through the hotel or a mobile app.

How to Get Around in Tshwane

Tshwane is a large city, but there are several convenient and affordable ways to get around.

  • Gautrain: The Gautrain is a convenient and fast way to travel between major cities in Gauteng.
  • Taxis and Uber: Taxis and Uber are readily available and can be arranged easily.
  • Bus: Tshwane has a reliable bus system that offers connections to major attractions and neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Tshwane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tshwane is surrounded by several exciting destinations that make great day trips or longer excursions.

  • Johannesburg: South Africa’s largest city, Johannesburg is just a short drive from Tshwane and offers a range of attractions, including the Apartheid Museum and Gold Reef City.
  • Sun City: Located just two hours from Tshwane, Sun City is a popular vacation destination that offers a range of activities and attractions, including game drives and a water park.
  • Hartebeespoort Dam: Located just north of Tshwane, Hartebeespoort Dam is a popular spot for water sports and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Tshwane is a vibrant and diverse city that has something to offer everyone, from its rich cultural and historical heritage to its range of outdoor adventures and natural attractions. By following this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can make the most of their time in Tshwane and experience all that this amazing city has to offer.
