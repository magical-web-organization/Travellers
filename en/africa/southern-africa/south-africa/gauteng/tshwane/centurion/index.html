Introduction to Centurion

Centurion is a gorgeous suburb located at the heart of Tshwane, in Gauteng province, South Africa. It is famous for its latest shopping center, Mall@Reds, and the tranquil lifestyle that it offers. This bustling urban area is perfect for travelers who are seeking adventure, cultural excursions, and urban landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Centurion

Here is an itinerary perfectly suited for first-time travelers to Centurion:

Day 1: Visit the Smuts House Museum and then proceed to the Irene Dairy Farm. Day 2: Hike at the Rietvlei Nature Reserve and enjoy lunch at the Big Red Barn. Day 3: Visit the Voortrekker Monument, and then head over to the Irene Village Market. Day 4: Take a tour of the South African Mint Company, and then visit the Rooihuiskraal Historical Terrain. Day 5: Visit the Groenkloof Nature Reserve and take a picnic lunch. Day 6: Take a cycling tour of the Jan Smuts Hiking Trail, and then visit the Let’s Go Bowling. Day 7: Take a local food tour, visiting some of the top restaurants in Centurion.

Local Culture and Customs of Centurion

Centurion is a multicultural city, and residents take pride in their unique cultural heritage. There are a few customs and traditions to be aware of. Shaking hands is a common greeting, and punctuality is highly valued in business meetings.

Best Time to Visit Centurion

The best time to visit Centurion is during the winter months, between June and August, when the weather is cooler. This is also the offseason, and deals on accommodation can be found. The summer months, between November and February, are the busiest and the warmest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centurion

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly in public places
  • Always carry cash, as not all shops and restaurants accept credit cards
  • Learn some basic Afrikaans or Zulu phrases

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables visible in a parked car
  • Smoke in public places, including bars and restaurants
  • Swim unsupervised in rivers, lakes, or dams

Safety and Health

Safety in Centurion

Centurion is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding areas that are known to be unsafe at night.

Health in Centurion

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Centurion, but visitors may prefer to drink bottled water, which is readily available. Visitors should also ensure they are up to date on their vaccinations before traveling to South Africa.

How to Get Around in Centurion

Walking is the best way to explore Centurion’s central area. Public transport options include buses and the Gautrain, which provides a fast and reliable mode of transportation between major cities.
