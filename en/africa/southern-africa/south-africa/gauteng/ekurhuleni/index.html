Introduction to Ekurhuleni

Ekurhuleni is a metropolitan municipality situated in the province of Gauteng, in the northern region of South Africa. This bustling, vibrant city is located in the heart of the country and offers visitors a mixture of historic and modern attractions.

The name Ekurhuleni means “place of peace” in the Xitsonga language, and it lives up to its name by offering a tranquil atmosphere alongside a buzzing city scene. This guide will take you on a journey of discovery through the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs of Ekurhuleni.

List of Places to Visit in Ekurhuleni

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Apartheid Museum

The Apartheid Museum in Johannesburg, a 20-minute drive from Ekurhuleni, tells the story of South Africa’s dark past. The comprehensive exhibitions cover the apartheid era from its inception in 1948 until its demise in 1994.

2. Germiston Historical Museum

The Germiston Historical Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It houses collections of photographs, artifacts, and documents that tell the story of Germiston, a city founded in the late 1800s during the gold rush.

3. Wattlespring Cultural Village

Wattlespring Cultural Village is a fascinating cultural center that showcases the traditional lifestyle of the Bapedi people. Visitors can learn about the customs, history, and beliefs of the Bapedi, and even participate in traditional dancing and singing.

Natural Attractions of Ekurhuleni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Suikerbosrand Nature Reserve

Located just an hour from Ekurhuleni, Suikerbosrand Nature Reserve is a paradise for nature lovers. The reserve offers hiking and biking trails that wind through rolling grasslands, rocky hills, and wooded valleys. It is also home to wildlife such as baboons, antelopes, and zebras.

2. The Bunny Park

The Bunny Park is a popular attraction for families with young children. The park provides a safe and fun environment for kids to interact with animals such as rabbits, ducks, and goats. Visitors can also enjoy a picnic or take a stroll through the park’s beautiful gardens.

3. Germiston Lake

Germiston Lake is a picturesque lake that offers visitors a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The lake is surrounded by lush gardens, and visitors can enjoy a picnic while taking in the beautiful views. The lake also provides opportunities for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ekurhuleni

Ekurhuleni boasts some of the best hiking and biking trails in South Africa. Here are the top routes to explore:

  • Rondebult Bird Sanctuary Trail: This 3.6 km trail weaves through the Rondebult Bird Sanctuary, where birdwatchers can spot a variety of species.

  • Modderfontein Reserve Trail: This 7.6 km trail winds through the Modderfontein Reserve, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Klipriviersberg Nature Reserve Trail: This 10.9 km trail is a challenging hike through the rugged terrain of the Klipriviersberg Nature Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Ekurhuleni: Activities and Tours

Ekurhuleni offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are our top picks:

  • Hot Air Balloon Safari: Soar above the beautiful landscape of Ekurhuleni in a hot air balloon and take in breathtaking views of the city.

  • Soweto Bicycle Tour: Explore Soweto, a nearby township that played a significant role in the struggle against apartheid, on a guided bicycle tour.

  • Weekend Market at Bruma Lake: Discover the vibrant local culture and enjoy a unique shopping experience at the Weekend Market at Bruma Lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ekurhuleni

Day 1 - Explore the Germiston Historical Museum and enjoy a picnic at Germiston Lake.

Day 2 - Experience the vibrant local culture at the Weekend Market at Bruma Lake.

Day 3 - Take a hot air balloon safari over the beautiful landscape of Ekurhuleni.

Day 4 - Visit the Apartheid Museum in Johannesburg.

Day 5 - Explore the natural beauty of Suikerbosrand Nature Reserve on a hiking or biking trail.

Day 6 - Discover the traditional lifestyle of the Bapedi people at Wattlespring Cultural Village.

Day 7 - Take a guided bicycle tour of Soweto, a nearby township that played a significant role in the struggle against apartheid.

Food and Drink Scene of Ekurhuleni

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pap and wors: A staple dish in South Africa, consisting of a maize porridge and a spicy sausage.

  • Biltong: Dried meat that is similar to beef jerky.

  • Vetkoek: A fried dough or pastry filled with savory or sweet ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos tea: A caffeine-free herbal tea made from the leaves of the rooibos plant.

  • Amarula: A cream liqueur made from the fruit of the marula tree.

  • Castle Lager: A popular South African beer.

  • Gemini Backpackers Lodge: This hostel is perfect for budget travelers and offers a relaxed and friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ekurhuleni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • African art and crafts: Ekurhuleni is home to a thriving arts and crafts scene, with many local artisans producing beautiful sculptures, paintings, and textiles.

  • Rooibos tea: As mentioned earlier, rooibos tea is a popular local beverage, and it makes a great souvenir for tea lovers.

  • Beaded jewelry: Beaded jewelry is a traditional craft in South Africa, and there are many gorgeous pieces to choose from in Ekurhuleni.

Local Culture and Customs of Ekurhuleni

South Africa is a diverse country with many different cultures and customs. In Ekurhuleni, the Xitsonga and Bapedi cultures are prominent. Visitors are encouraged to respect the customs and traditions of the local people and to ask permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Ekurhuleni

The best time to visit Ekurhuleni is during the dry season, between May and September. The weather is mild, and outdoor activities are enjoyable. December and January are also popular times to visit due to the summer holiday season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ekurhuleni

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take precautions against crime, such as using a taxi service at night.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities such as drug use.

  • Take photographs of people without their consent.

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ekurhuleni

Ekurhuleni is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should take precautions against petty theft and other crimes. It is recommended to use a taxi service at night and to keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Ekurhuleni

Ekurhuleni has good healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical services at private hospitals and clinics. It is recommended to take precautions against malaria if traveling to nearby Kruger National Park.

Getting to Ekurhuleni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ekurhuleni

  • O.R. Tambo International Airport: This is the closest airport to Ekurhuleni and is located approximately 5 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Ekurhuleni

  • Johannesburg Park Station: This is the main bus terminal in Johannesburg and is located approximately 15 km from Ekurhuleni.

Other Options

  • Uber: Uber is available in Ekurhuleni and is a safe and convenient way to get around.

  • Car rental: Car rental is available at O.R. Tambo International Airport and other locations throughout Ekurhuleni.

How to Get Around in Ekurhuleni

The best way to get around Ekurhuleni is by car or taxi. Public transportation options are limited, and walking is not recommended due to safety concerns.

Where to Go Next from Ekurhuleni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ekurhuleni is located in a central location, making it a great base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are our top picks:

  • Kruger National Park: Located approximately a 4-hour drive from Ekurhuleni, Kruger National Park is home to a vast array of wildlife, including the Big Five.

  • Pretoria: Located approximately a 1-hour drive from Ekurhuleni, Pretoria is the administrative capital of South Africa and offers a range of historical and cultural attractions.

  • Magaliesberg: Located approximately a 2-hour drive from Ekurhuleni, the Magaliesberg region offers breathtaking scenery and outdoor activities such as hiking and hot air ballooning.

Conclusion

Ekurhuleni is a dynamic city that offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring museums and historical sites to hiking through nature reserves, there is something for every type of traveler in Ekurhuleni. With its good healthcare facilities, transportation options, and range of accommodations, planning a trip to Ekurhuleni is easy and hassle-free.
