Introduction to Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a small town located in the Free State province of South Africa. Founded in 1864, Bethlehem is a picturesque town with many cultural and natural attractions. The town is known for its sandstone buildings and its surrounding mountains, rivers, and parks, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything there is to know about planning your trip to Bethlehem.

List of Places to Visit in Bethlehem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bethlehem is home to a variety of museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s rich history and culture. A few must-see attractions include:

  • Bethlehem Museum: This museum showcases the town’s history, from pre-colonial times to the present day. Exhibitions include traditional crafts, clothing, and photographs.

  • Sol Plaatje House: The birthplace of famous South African activist and writer Sol Plaatje, this museum provides insight into his life and legacy.

  • Dihlabeng Municipal Cultural Centre: This cultural center features exhibitions and workshops that celebrate the arts and crafts of the local Basotho culture.

Natural Attractions of Bethlehem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bethlehem is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, including mountains, rivers, and parks. Some of the top natural attractions in Bethlehem include:

  • Rooiberg Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including antelope, zebras, and baboons. Visitors can also hike to the top of Rooiberg for breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Golden Gate Highlands National Park: This national park is located just outside of Bethlehem and is known for its sandstone cliffs, stunning vistas, and hiking trails.

  • Bethlehem Sandstone Art Mine: This unique geological formation is home to colorful sandstone formations that have been shaped by rain and wind over millions of years.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the top trails include:

  • Thaba Ntlenyana: This is the highest peak in South Africa, offering a challenging but rewarding hike with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Eureka Trail: This easy-to-moderate trail winds through the Golden Gate Highlands National Park, offering picturesque views of the sandstone cliffs and valleys.

  • Sentinel Peak Hike: This strenuous hike to the top of Sentinel Peak offers views of the Tugela Falls, the second highest waterfall in the world.

Best Things to Do in Bethlehem: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do and see in Bethlehem. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Hot air balloon rides: Get a bird’s eye view of Bethlehem and the surrounding landscapes on a hot air balloon ride.

  • White water rafting: Take a thrilling ride down the Ash River rapids, located just outside of Bethlehem.

  • Basotho Cultural Village tour: Learn about the culture and traditions of the local Basotho people on a guided tour of the Basotho Cultural Village.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bethlehem

Day 1: Explore Bethlehem’s museums and historical sites, including the Bethlehem Museum, Sol Plaatje House, and the Dihlabeng Municipal Cultural Centre.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Thaba Ntlenyana, the highest peak in South Africa.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over Bethlehem and the surrounding landscapes.

Day 4: Explore the Golden Gate Highlands National Park on foot or by bike.

Day 5: Go white water rafting down the Ash River rapids.

Day 6: Learn about the culture and traditions of the Basotho people on a guided tour of the Basotho Cultural Village.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of Bethlehem’s local cuisine and beverages. Check out some local shops for souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Bethlehem

Must-Try Dishes

Bethlehem is known for its hearty and savory dishes, many of them influenced by the Basotho culture. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Pap en vleis: A traditional South African dish consisting of maize porridge served with a meat stew.

  • Potjiekos: A stew made with meat and vegetables that is slow-cooked in a cast-iron pot over open flames.

  • Braai: A South African-style barbecue that is highly social, typically involving a large fire and plenty of meat and sides.

Local Beverages

South Africa is known for its wines and beers, and Bethlehem is no exception. Local and national favorites include:

  • Castle Lager: A popular South African beer that is light and refreshing.

  • Pinotage: A red wine that is unique to South Africa, with distinct notes of plum and smoky flavors.

  • Rooibos tea: A caffeine-free herbal tea that is unique to South Africa, with a nutty and slightly sweet flavor.

Where to Stay in Bethlehem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bethlehem has a variety of accommodations available depending on your budget and preferences. A few options include:

  • Green Olive Guesthouse: A cozy and comfortable guesthouse with modern amenities and friendly hosts.

  • Birch Bush B&B: A charming bed and breakfast located in a historic sandstone house.

  • Mont d’Or Hotel: A boutique hotel with elegant rooms and on-site restaurant and bar.

What to Buy in Bethlehem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring home from Bethlehem? Some popular options include:

  • Mohair products: Made from the wool of Angora goats, Mohair products like scarves and blankets are a specialty in Bethlehem.

  • Sandstone art: Bethlehem’s unique sandstone formations are often turned into beautiful pieces of art and decor.

  • Local wines and beers: Bring home a taste of South Africa with a bottle of locally-produced wine or beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Bethlehem

Bethlehem is home to a diverse range of cultures, including the Basotho and Afrikaans communities. When visiting Bethlehem, it is important to respect local customs and traditions. Some tips include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or cultural events.

  • When meeting someone new, it is customary to shake hands and make eye contact.

  • Avoid discussing controversial or sensitive topics, such as politics or religion, unless you are well-acquainted with the person you are speaking to.

Best Time to Visit Bethlehem

The best time to visit Bethlehem is during the summer months (December-February) when the weather is warm and conducive to outdoor activities. Additionally, Bethlehem hosts a variety of cultural events and festivals throughout the year, including the Bethlehem Bonanza and the Bethlehem Airshow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bethlehem

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try some of the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take advantage of the opportunity to explore the great outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Engage in potentially offensive behavior, such as public displays of affection or aggressive behavior towards others.

  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Discuss controversial or sensitive topics unless you are well-acquainted with the person you are speaking to.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a safe town for visitors, but it is still important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Some tips include:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables securely locked away when not in use.

  • Be cautious when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking and white water rafting, and always follow safety protocols.

Health in Bethlehem

It is important to take necessary health precautions before visiting Bethlehem. Some tips include:

  • Make sure your vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to South Africa.

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.

  • Bring any necessary medications with you, as medical facilities in Bethlehem may be limited.

Getting to Bethlehem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bethlehem

The nearest airport to Bethlehem is the Bram Fischer International Airport in Bloemfontein, located approximately 240 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or hire a car to travel to Bethlehem.

Bus Stations Near Bethlehem

Bethlehem has its own bus station, but visitors can also take a bus from other major cities in South Africa, such as Johannesburg or Durban.

Other Options

Visitors may also choose to hire a car and drive to Bethlehem. The town is located on the N5 highway and is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Bethlehem

Public transportation in Bethlehem is limited, so visitors may choose to hire a car or take a taxi to get around. Biking and walking are also viable options, particularly for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Bethlehem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bethlehem is located in the heart of the Free State province, with plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Some options include:

  • Clarens: A charming town located just 30 minutes from Bethlehem, known for its art galleries and outdoor adventures.

  • Ficksburg: A town located on the border of Lesotho, famous for its annual Cherry Festival.

  • Golden Gate Highlands National Park: Located just outside of Bethlehem, the national park can easily be explored as a day trip or overnight excursion.

Conclusion

Bethlehem, South Africa is a beautiful and unique destination with plenty to see and do. Whether you’re interested in exploring local culture, hiking in the stunning natural scenery, or simply relaxing and enjoying some local cuisine and beverages, there is something for everyone in Bethlehem. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has helped you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming South African town.
