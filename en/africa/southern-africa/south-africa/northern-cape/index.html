Introduction to Northern Cape

Northern Cape is a province nestled in the heart of South Africa. This sprawling region offers a diverse range of landscapes, from rugged mountains to vast deserts, and is home to a rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a history buff, Northern Cape has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Cape

To make the most of your time in Northern Cape, here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kimberley Mine Museum: Explore the history of diamond mining and learn about the infamous Big Hole in Kimberley.
  • Riemvasmaak Hot Springs: Soak in the natural hot springs and view the rock art of the indigenous San people.
  • Augrabies Falls National Park: Marvel at the stunning waterfalls and learn about the San rock art.
  • Orange River Cellars: Visit the largest wine co-operative in South Africa and sample some of the finest wines of the region.
  • William Humphreys Art Gallery: Admire the works of local and international artists at this art museum in Kimberley.

Natural Attractions of Northern Cape: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park: Go on a safari and spot lions, leopards, and cheetahs in their natural habitat.
  • Tankwa Karoo National Park: Explore the iconic red hills, unique flora, and fauna of this desert region.
  • Witsand Nature Reserve: Sandboard down the dunes or hike the trails of this wilderness reserve.
  • Namaqua National Park: Experience an explosion of colorful wildflowers in the spring and indulge in outdoor activities.
  • Goegap Nature Reserve: Take a scenic drive, spot wildlife, and explore desert terrain in this reserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Cape

Northern Cape is home to some of the most breathtaking hiking and biking routes in South Africa. Here are the top ones to explore:

  • Orange River Trail: Hike the stunning Orange River trail, which offers views of waterfalls, gorges and in some regions, wildlife.
  • Kokerboom Trail: Bike the rugged terrain of the Kokerboom Trail and enjoy spectacular views of the Richtersveld Nature Reserve.
  • Diamond Coast Hiking Trail: Hike the 3-day trail that takes you along the coast, where you’ll spot whales, dolphins, and seals.
  • The Kalahari Traverse: Hike the 7-day trail and discover the beauty of the desert terrain, wildlife, and local communities.
  • Fish River Canyon: Hike the famous 4-day Fish River Canyon trail and be thrilled by panoramic canyon views, wildlife, and hot springs.

Best Things to Do in Northern Cape: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in Northern Cape:

  • Star Gazing: Take a night-time tour and experience the vast open skies that Northern Cape is known for.
  • Horse Riding: Explore the stunning Terrestrial Eco-zone on horseback and discover unique flora and fauna.
  • Hot Air Balloon: Take a romantic hot air balloon ride over the stunning landscape of Northern Cape.
  • Surfing: Encounter the unique waves of the West Coast and indulge in the best surfing South Africa has to offer.
  • Game Drives: Take a game drive and have the opportunity to spot some of the most exotic wildlife and birds on a safari.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Cape

The Food and drink scene of Northern Cape is bursting with local delicacies and produce which make for a must-try culinary experience.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boerewors: a traditional South African sausage made with a blend of beef, pork, and spices.
  • Biltong: meat jerky made from various derivatives of game, such as Kudu, Ostrich, and Springbok meat.
  • Sosaties: marinated meat skewers with a touch of chutney that are cooked over an open flame.
  • Pap: a staple food made from maize that’s served with relish, chakalaka, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos Tea: a popular caffeine-free tea that’s native to the Cape region.
  • Honey-bush Tea: Another indigenous tea to South Africa made from a plant grown in the fynbos region.
  • Vaaljapie: A locally brewed beer, which gets its name from the notorious dust devils that are common in the Northern Cape.

Where to Stay in Northern Cape: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

No matter your budget or travel preference, Northern Cape has accommodation options ranging from budget to luxury. Here’s a list of suggested places to stay in Northern Cape:

  • Kgalagadi Lodge: A cozy guesthouse located in the Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park.
  • Die Kokerboom Gastehuis: A quaint guesthouse located in the heart of Springbok catering to travelers looking to experience local culture.
  • The Kimberley Club: A historic hotel located in the heart of Kimberley, with comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities.
  • Dundi Lodge: A luxurious lodge that offers excellent facilities and amenities such as outdoor pools, spa treatments, and fine dining.

What to Buy in Northern Cape: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or mementos to take home from Northern Cape then here is what to buy:

  • Diamonds: The Northern Cape is known for its diamond mines, and a diamond is a good investment.
  • Kalahari Truffles: A rare and exotic truffle that’s found in the Kalahari Desert.
  • Rooibos Tea and Honey-bush Tea: Take home a box of the famous tea types, which you can’t find easily outside of South Africa.
  • San Art and Crafts: Purchase authentic San people’s artwork, such as pottery, beadwork, and carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Cape

The locals in Northern Cape are friendly and welcoming people. A few things to keep in mind to respect their culture and customs include:

  • Cultural festivals and events: Respect the customs and traditions of the local people by dressing appropriately and not imposing on their way of life.
  • Greeting customs: In many cultures in Northern Cape, it is customary to shake hands with the right hand, and sometimes women may embrace each other as a sign of respect.
  • Respect wildlife: Northern Cape has an abundance of wildlife, respect their space as they are not domesticated animals.
  • Language: English and Afrikaans are the most widely spoken language in the Northern Cape. Try to learn greetings and basic phrases in the local language as a sign of respect for their native culture.

Best Time to Visit Northern Cape

The best time to visit Northern Cape is during the spring (August to mid-October) when the wildflowers are in bloom, and the temperature is mild. The fall (April and May) is also a great time to visit, as the temperatures are cooler, and there are fewer tourists. However, if you plan to visit the Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park, winter (May to August) is the best time as wildlife sightings are more frequent during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Cape

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to consider for your trip to Northern Cape:

Do:

  • Research the local culture: Learn about the local customs and traditions, it will help you avoid offending anyone.
  • Pack appropriately: Make sure to pack for the season and the activities you plan to do, which may include sunscreen, insect repellent, and protective gear.
  • Hire a local guide: Consider hiring a local guide to show you around the region or to help with the translation of local languages.
  • Drink bottled water: Always drink bottled water in Northern Cape to avoid any health concerns.

Don’t:

  • Photograph the locals without permission: Always ask permission before taking someone’s photo, and be respectful if they decline.
  • Litter: Keep Northern Cape clean by disposing of your waste correctly.
  • Impose your beliefs: Do not impose your beliefs, culture or preferences onto the locals, as it could cause offense.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Cape

Northern Cape is relatively safe for travelers, and violent crimes are rare. However, as with any travel destination, it’s essential to stay alert, especially at night, and to take precautions to avoid petty theft or scams.

Health in Northern Cape

Northern Cape is not a high-risk area for malaria or other major diseases. However, it’s always best to take precautions by wearing insect repellent and protective clothing. Also, be sure to get vaccinated before your trip and have travel insurance.

Getting to Northern Cape: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northern Cape is accessible by both air and land. Here are some options to consider for getting to Northern Cape:

Airports Near Northern Cape

  • Kimberley Airport: Served by several domestic airlines, including South African Airways, and British Airways.
  • Upington Airport: Served by South African Airways, British Airways and SA Airlink.

Bus Stations Near Northern Cape

  • Kimberley Bus Station: Bus services connect to major cities such as Johannesburg, Bloemfontein, and Cape Town.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Car rental services are available in major cities, including Cape Town and Johannesburg.
  • Train: The Shosholoza Meyl train service connects cities such as Johannesburg, Cape Town, and Port Elizabeth, with stops in Kimberley.

How to Get Around in Northern Cape

The best way to explore Northern Cape is by car or a hired private tour guide. There are regular bus and taxi services available through major towns and cities

Where to Go Next from Northern Cape: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Northern Cape:

  • Cape Town: Experience the beauty of Cape Town and discover the Cape of Good Hope.
  • The Garden Route: Explore the scenic beauty of the Garden Route with its rolling hills, stunning beaches, and world-class wines.
  • The West Coast: Discover the West Coast’s unspoiled coastline with tranquil fishing villages, wildflowers, and birdlife.
  • The Namibian Border: Bordering Namibia, travelers can cross and explore the natural beauty of the Namibian regions.

Conclusion

Northern Cape is a stunning province in South Africa that has something to offer to every type of traveler. From the sprawling wilderness to vibrant cultural attractions, Northern Cape is a destination everyone should experience.
