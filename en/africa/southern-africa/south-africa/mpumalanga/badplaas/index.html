Introduction to Badplaas

Located in the beautiful province of Mpumalanga, Badplaas is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural diversity. The town is surrounded by the picturesque Nkomazi valley and is home to some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the region. This town is a perfect spot for getting away from the hustle of city life and indulging in outdoor activities, exploring local culture, and enjoying delicious food. In this travel guide, we will take a closer look at the things that make Badplaas a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Badplaas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Rock Art Trail - Take a stroll through the hills of Badplaas, and you will find ancient rock art dating back to the San people.

  2. Eureka City - This museum showcases the history of mining in the region and its impact on the local community.

  3. Ngwenya Glass Village - Witness master glassblowers at work while learning about the history of glass in the region.

Natural Attractions of Badplaas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Venetia State Forest - This beautiful and serene forest offers hiking trails, birdwatching, and camping opportunities.

  2. Badplaas Hot Springs - Relax in natural hot springs, high in mineral content, that offer a rejuvenating experience.

  3. Kruger Park - One of the most iconic national parks in South Africa, offering the chance to spot the Big 5.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Badplaas

Badplaas offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in South Africa. Some of the top hikes include the Madlala Trail, the Vreloef Pass Trail, and the Ncibidwane Trail. These trails offer sweeping vistas of the valleys below and a chance to experience the natural beauty of the region up close.

Best Things to Do in Badplaas: Activities and Tours

  1. Experience local culture with the Swazi Cultural Tour that takes you on a journey through the history and traditions of the Swazi people.

  2. Go horseback riding in the beautiful surroundings of Badplaas and explore the breathtaking landscapes while enjoying the fresh air.

  3. Take a tour of the Kruger National Park and experience the African wildlife up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Badplaas

If you’re planning a week-long vacation to Badplaas, we suggest the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the many hiking and biking trails that Badplaas has to offer.
  • Day 2: Head to the Badplaas Hot Springs for a relaxing day of soaking in warm mineral-rich waters.
  • Day 3: Take a tour of the nearby Kruger National Park and experience some of Africa’s iconic wildlife.
  • Day 4: Visit the Ngwenya Glass Village and see master glassblowers at work.
  • Day 5: Go horseback riding through the beautiful surrounds of Badplaas.
  • Day 6: Experience the local culture with a Swazi Cultural Tour.
  • Day 7: Visit the Eureka City museum and learn about the impact mining has had on the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Badplaas

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Biltong: A dried meat that is a local favorite and perfect for snacking.

  2. Boerewors: A traditional South African sausage, usually served with pap (maize meal).

  3. Cape Malay Curry: A spicy curry with a unique blend of herbs and spices, commonly served with rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Rooibos Tea: A popular herbal tea grown in South Africa, known for its health benefits and unique flavor.

  2. Amarula: A cream liqueur made from the fruit of the African marula tree and enjoyed as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Badplaas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Badplaas, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the top-rated places to stay include Badplaas Resort, Riverman Cabin, Kierieklapper Lodge, and Sycamore Avenue Treehouse.

What to Buy in Badplaas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade pottery: Badplaas has a thriving artisan community that offers pottery, jewelry, and other handmade crafts.

  2. Native herbs and spices: The region offers a range of unique herbs and spices, such as the African wild rosemary.

Local Culture and Customs of Badplaas

Badplaas is home to a diverse population, including Swazi, Zulu, and Afrikaans-speaking people. The culture is a blend of traditional and modern elements, with a focus on appreciation for the local environment and respect for the region’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Badplaas

The best time to visit Badplaas is during the cooler months between May and October. During this time, the weather is mild, and the risk of rain is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Badplaas

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Take care of the natural environment and avoid littering.

  3. Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in any form of discrimination or racism.

  2. Take photographs of people without their permission.

  3. Disturb the natural wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Badplaas

Badplaas is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take precautions like they would in any other unfamiliar area. Always keep an eye on your belongings, avoid walking alone at night, and stay away from dangerous areas.

Health in Badplaas

The health infrastructure in Badplaas is good, but it’s always wise to take necessary health precautions before traveling. Visitors should consider getting immunized against common illnesses like typhoid and hepatitis A.

Getting to Badplaas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Badplaas

The nearest airport to Badplaas is Kruger Mpumalanga International Airport, which is located about 114km away.

Bus Stations Near Badplaas

The closest bus station to Badplaas is Nelspruit, which is located about 90km away.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, taking advantage of many car rental providers in the area.

How to Get Around in Badplaas

The best way to get around in Badplaas is by car. However, visitors who do not have access to private transportation can use taxis, minibusses, or scheduled bus services.

Where to Go Next from Badplaas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Nelspruit - located only 88km from Badplaas and is known for its beautiful botanical gardens.

  2. Barberton - a small town only 35km from Badplaas, famous for its gold-mining history and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Badplaas has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, you’re sure to find it here. From the picturesque landscapes to the rich history, Badplaas is a destination worth visiting, and we hope this guide has helped you plan your perfect getaway.
