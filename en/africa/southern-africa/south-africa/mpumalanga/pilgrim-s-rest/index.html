Introduction to Pilgrim’s Rest

Pilgrim’s Rest is a fascinating town set in Mpumalanga province in South Africa. Known for its historic significance and natural beauty, Pilgrim’s Rest attracts thousands of tourists every year. It is located approximately 400 km east of Johannesburg, and it is one of the most visited towns in Mpumalanga.

Pilgrim’s Rest was declared a national monument in 1986. The town preserves the architecture and lifestyle of the late 1800s and early 1900s. The visitor will get to experience living history on the streets, and in the buildings that have been preserved immaculately.

In this guide, we will help you explore the best places to visit in Pilgrim’s Rest, enjoy outdoor activities, sample the local cuisine, know where to stay, and learn about the culture and customs of the town.

List of Places to Visit in Pilgrim’s Rest

If you are planning a trip to Pilgrim’s Rest, there are many places you should add to your itinerary. Here are some of the top destinations to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Royal Hotel: This is the oldest hotel in South Africa that has been continuously in operation since 1876. The hotel has been refurbished but still boasts authentic Victorian decor and charm.

  • The House Museum: This museum gives visitors an idea about what life was like for wealthy people during the gold rush. The house has a range of antique furniture, crown moldings, imported wallpapers, and ornate ceilings.

  • The Diggings Museum: The Diggings showcase the past gold-mining era with a range of dummies depicting scenes of how it was to work the mines during the gold rush.

  • Alanglade House Museum: This is a well-preserved example of an Edwardian mansion that was owned by the mine manager.

Natural Attractions of Pilgrim’s Rest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Sheba Nature Reserve: The reserve boasts a lush forest, waterfalls, and scenic views. Hiking trails will take you on an adventure through the forest, spotting birds and monkeys in the trees.

  • Blyde River Canyon Reserve: This is the third-largest canyon in the world and has stunning views of the cliffs that stretch out into the distance and a river far below. Visitors can go on scenic drives or hikes and take boat tours on the Blyde Dam.

  • Panorama Route: Drive along the Panorama Route for a breathtaking view of several landmarks, including the Three Rondavels, God’s Window, and Bourke’s Luck Potholes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pilgrim’s Rest

Pilgrim’s Rest has several hiking trails and biking routes that visitors will enjoy. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking:

  • Pilgrim’s Rest History Walk: This tour is an excellent option for history buffs, as it takes visitors through the town’s historical areas while providing valuable insights into the town’s rich history.

  • The Graskop Gorge Hiking Trail: This trail is approximately 10 km long and takes you through forest, river crossings, and waterfalls.

  • Mountain Bike Trails: The forest offers an opportunity for mountain bike enthusiasts to ride their bicycles on the dirt trails.

Best Things to Do in Pilgrim’s Rest: Activities and Tours

Pilgrim’s Rest offers a range of activities to keep visitors busy during their stay. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Gold Panning: You can’t visit Pilgrim’s Rest without trying your hand at gold panning. Several tour operators offer gold panning experiences.

  • Steam Train Rides: Take a ride on the steam train that runs between Pilgrim’s Rest and Grootvlei.

  • Heritage Walks: Take a walk around the town to explore Pilgrim’s Rest’s rich history.

Food and Drink Scene of Pilgrim’s Rest

The town has a reasonable number of restaurants and coffee shops. Here are some recommended dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Potjiekos: A stew of meat and vegetables cooked in a cast-iron pot over the fire. Variations of this dish may include chicken, beef, lamb, and vegetables.

  • Boerewors: A traditional sausage made of beef and pork seasoned with spices.

  • Bunny Chow: A curry served in a hollowed-out bread roll, a popular dish in South Africa.

Local Beverages

  • Amarula: A cream liqueur with flavors of marula fruit that is native to South Africa.

  • Castle Lager: A South African beer that is popular throughout the country.

Where to Stay in Pilgrim’s Rest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pilgrim’s Rest has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Royal Hotel: The oldest hotel in South Africa, this hotel has been in operation since 1876 and is still a popular choice among tourists.

  • Pilgrim’s Rest Backpackers: This hostel offers affordable accommodation for backpackers and travelers on a budget.

  • Misty Mountain: This guest house is located in the forest around 20 kilometers from Pilgrim’s Rest and offers a romantic and upscale option for travelers.

What to Buy in Pilgrim’s Rest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pilgrim’s Rest is home to several shops selling souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommended items to buy:

  • Gold Nuggets: You can purchase gold nuggets as a souvenir, a remnant from the town’s gold-mining era.

  • Handmade Crafts: Various stores in the town sell a range of handcrafted goods, including pottery, beadwork, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Pilgrim’s Rest

Like any other South African town, Pilgrim’s Rest has unique customs and local culture. The people here are typically friendly, and the culture is a fusion of African and European influences.

Best Time to Visit Pilgrim’s Rest

The best time to visit Pilgrim’s Rest is undoubtedly in the summer months between November and February when the weather is warm, and the precipitation is minimal. However, visiting during the winter season between May and August also has its perks. The views are more beautiful in winter with the lush greenery and natural waterfalls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pilgrim’s Rest

If you are planning to visit Pilgrim’s Rest, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture

  • Explore local towns and attractions

  • Take a walk around town

Don’t:

  • Swim in the rivers without knowing the water quality

  • Litter in public areas

Safety and Health

Pilgrim’s Rest is a generally safe place, but it is always wise to take precautions as with any town.

Safety in Pilgrim’s Rest

  • Do not display ostentatious wealth

  • Always keep valuable items locked up in your room

  • Do not walk alone when it is dark

Health in Pilgrim’s Rest

  • Check for ticks when hiking, as they can cause several diseases

  • Make sure you have the necessary vaccinations before visiting South Africa

Getting to Pilgrim’s Rest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pilgrim’s Rest is roughly 400 kilometers from Johannesburg, and there are several ways to get there. Some of the transportation options include:

Airports Near Pilgrim’s Rest

  • Kruger Mpumalanga International Airport

  • O.R. Tambo International Airport (Johannesburg)

Bus Stations Near Pilgrim’s Rest

  • Greyhound and Translux are two of the most reliable and popular bus services to take to the town.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available from major companies, and self-driving is an option, though be careful of animals on the roads.

How to Get Around in Pilgrim’s Rest

Walking is the best way to get around Pilgrim’s Rest, given its compact size. Alternatively, you could take mule carts or donkeys around the place.

Where to Go Next from Pilgrim’s Rest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some recommended destinations and excursions you can do next after visiting Pilgrim’s Rest:

  • Kruger National Park: One of South Africa’s most famous nature reserves, it is home to thousands of animals and birds.

  • Sabi Sands: A private game reserve that offers the opportunity to spot wildlife up close, including the Big Five.

Conclusion

Pilgrim’s Rest is a charming town with so much to offer. Whether you are a history buff or nature lover, there’s something for everyone in this town. Take this comprehensive tour guide along, and have an unforgettable trip to Pilgrim’s Rest.
