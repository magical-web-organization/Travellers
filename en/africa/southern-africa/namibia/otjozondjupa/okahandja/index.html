Your Complete Guide to Okahandja, Namibia

Located in the Otjozondjupa region of Namibia, Okahandja is a hidden gem known for its historical landmarks and scenic beauty. Surrounded by mountains and lush greenery, this small town attracts visitors seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. In this comprehensive guide, we will cover everything you need to know when planning a trip to Okahandja, Namibia.

Introduction to Okahandja

Founded in the mid-1800s, Okahandja is a town steeped in history and culture. It derives its name from the Herero language, meaning “the place where two rivers flow into each other.” Today, it is a growing town with a population of around 24,000, an eclectic mix of modern buildings and traditional homesteads, and a vibrant arts and crafts scene.

List of Places to Visit in Okahandja

When in Okahandja, there are a few places you cannot afford to miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Herero Graves: These graves belong to the Herero warriors who died fighting against the Germans in the early 1900s. They are located on the outskirts of town and are a significant historical landmark.
  2. The Woodcarvers Market: The market is an open-air market that sells handmade wooden crafts and souvenirs. It is a great place to soak up the local culture.
  3. Okahandja Open Market: The largest open-air market in Namibia, this market sells everything from fresh produce to traditional garments and curios.

Natural Attractions of Okahandja: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Von Bach Dam: A massive man-made lake perfect for fishing, water sports, and picnics.
  2. Gross Barmen Hot Springs: Located about 20km from Okahandja, this natural spa is known for its therapeutic properties and scenic surroundings.
  3. Waterberg Plateau Park: An hour’s drive from Okahandja, this park is famous for its ancient red sandstone cliffs and rich wildlife, including rhinos, cheetahs, and buffaloes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okahandja

For outdoor enthusiasts, Okahandja has several hiking and biking trails that are worth exploring. These include:

  1. The Fertility Trail: A 12km hike that meanders through the hills and valleys surrounding the town.
  2. The Otjihinamaparero Trail: A 30km bike ride that takes you through the beautiful landscapes of the area.
  3. The Okahandja Dam Hike: A 5km hike with beautiful views of the Dam and the surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Okahandja: Activities and Tours

  1. Okahandja Tour: A guided tour of the town’s historical monuments and cultural sites.
  2. San Painting Tour: A tour of the ancient rock art sites located in the Otavi Mountain Range.
  3. Conservation Tour: A guided tour of the local nature reserves, including the Waterberg Plateau Park and the Daan Viljoen Game Reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Okahandja

Day 1-2: Explore the town’s historical landmarks and cultural sites, including the Herero Graves, the Woodcarvers Market, and the Open Market.

Day 3: Visit the Von Bach Dam for some fishing and outdoor activities.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the Gross Barmen Hot Springs.

Day 5: Explore Waterberg Plateau Park and its wildlife.

Day 6: Take a hike or bike ride along one of the town’s scenic trails.

Day 7: Relax at your hotel or guesthouse, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy a traditional meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Okahandja

The town’s culinary scene reflects its rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Oshifima: A traditional maize meal that is a staple in the local diet.
  2. Kapana: Grilled meat served with traditional spices and vegetables.
  3. Mopane Worms: Considered a delicacy in the local cuisine, these caterpillars are high in protein and eaten fried or dried.

Local Beverages

  1. Omagongo: A popular traditional drink made from fermented wild grapes.
  2. Munkoyo: A non-alcoholic drink made from fermented roots.

Where to Stay in Okahandja: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Okahandja has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Here are some top picks:

  1. Omapala Lodge: A luxurious hotel situated on the outskirts of town, offering stunning panoramic views and modern amenities.
  2. Okahandja Country Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of town with a colonial-style decor.
  3. Otjiruze Guesthouse: A homely guesthouse located in a quiet residential area.

What to Buy in Okahandja: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The town’s markets and curio stalls offer a range of unique souvenirs and handcrafted items. Here are some popular items to look out for:

  1. Wooden carvings: Hand-carved sculptures and figurines depicting local wildlife, people, and objects.
  2. Himba bracelets: Beaded bracelets made by the Himba people, a local tribe known for their traditional way of life.
  3. Handmade jewelry: Earrings, necklaces, and bracelets made with local gems and stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Okahandja

Namibia is known for its diverse ethnic groups and unique traditions. In Okahandja, the Herero people are the largest ethnic group, and their traditional dresses are a must-see. Visitors are encouraged to respect the locals’ cultural beliefs and practices and avoid taking pictures without permission or disturbing their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Okahandja

The best time to visit Okahandja is during the dry season, which runs from April to October, when temperatures are moderate and rainfall is scarce. This is also the best time for outdoor activities and wildlife safaris.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okahandja

Do:

  1. Respect the locals’ cultural beliefs and practices.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  3. Explore the town’s historical landmarks and scenic trails.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures without permission.
  2. Buy products made from endangered animals (e.g., ivory).
  3. Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Okahandja

Okahandja is a safe destination for tourists, but it is still advisable to take necessary precautions. Visitors are encouraged to avoid walking alone at night and to keep their valuables safe.

Health in Okahandja

Okahandja has a few private clinics and pharmacies, but medical facilities are limited. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication and get comprehensive travel insurance.

Getting to Okahandja: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okahandja

The nearest airport to Okahandja is the Hosea Kutako International Airport, located 70km away.

Bus Stations Near Okahandja

Okahandja has a bus station that connects it to other major towns and cities in Namibia.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to reach Okahandja from Windhoek.

How to Get Around in Okahandja

The town is relatively small and easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also hire a bike or car.

Where to Go Next from Okahandja: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Okahandja, there are plenty of exciting destinations to explore in Namibia, including:

  1. Windhoek: The capital city of Namibia is a bustling metropolis with plenty of cultural landmarks and modern facilities.
  2. Swakopmund: A coastal town famous for its sandy beaches, adventure sports, and German-inspired architecture.
  3. Etosha National Park: One of Africa’s largest wildlife reserves, where you can spot lions, elephants, and many other wild animals.

Conclusion

Okahandja is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique combination of culture, history, and nature. Whether you are interested in exploring the town’s historical landmarks, hiking its scenic trails, or trying its flavorful cuisine, Okahandja is sure to leave a lasting impression. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty of Namibia for yourself.
