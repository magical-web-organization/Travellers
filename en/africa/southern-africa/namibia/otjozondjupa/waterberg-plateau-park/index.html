Introduction to Waterberg Plateau Park

Waterberg Plateau Park is a hidden gem situated in the Otjozondjupa region of Namibia in Southern Africa. This national park spans over 400 square kilometers and is known for its incredible landscape, diverse wildlife, and cultural heritage. The park is named after the Waterberg Plateau, which rises 200 meters above the surrounding area, creating a breathtaking sight.

List of Places to Visit in Waterberg Plateau Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Waterberg Plateau Park is rich in history and culture, making it a must-visit destination for tourists. Some of the culturally important attractions to see in the park include:

  • Okakarara Cattle Auction: Every Friday, farmers from different parts of Namibia converge here to sell their animals, providing a unique opportunity to experience the country’s rural life.
  • Herero Graves: Found in a small cemetery, the graves of early Herero leaders offer insights into the tradition and history of the Herero people.
  • Herero Memorial: A granite obelisk, also known as the Herero War Memorial, was erected in memory of the Herero who lost their lives in the 1904-1908 German-Herero War.
  • Waterberg Plateau Rest Camp Museum: Located in the rest camp, the museum displays the history and culture of the local Herero and San people.

Natural Attractions of Waterberg Plateau Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its cultural riches, Waterberg Plateau Park boasts a diverse range of fauna and flora, hiking trails, and spectacular views. Here are some of the park’s key attractions:

  • Hiking Trails: The park has several hiking trails for visitors to enjoy, ranging from easy to challenging. One of the most popular trails is the Waterberg Plateau Trail, which stretches for 14 kilometers and leads up to the plateau.
  • Wildlife: Visitors to the park can see a range of wildlife, including cheetahs, leopards, giraffes, sable antelopes, klipspringers, and many more. Guided game drives are also available.
  • Waterberg Plateau: The towering plateau is a sight to behold, giving visitors panoramic views of the surrounding area. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the plateau and learn about its unique ecosystem.
  • Waterberg Wilderness: The Waterberg Wilderness is the southernmost extension of the world-renowned Okavango Delta, a natural wonder that covers 22,000 square kilometers across the neighboring Botswana. The area is home to pristine rivers and other scenic natural features, such as towering sand dunes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waterberg Plateau Park

The Waterberg Plateau Park offers a range of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore the park’s natural beauty. Some popular routes include:

  • Waterberg Plateau Trail: This trail stretches for 14 kilometers and leads up to the plateau. It is a challenging hike that takes approximately six hours to complete.
  • Hiking trails around the former German military base: The trails lead through the bush, providing opportunities to hike and view wildlife.
  • Sable Walking Trail: The trail is approximately 4 kilometers and leads through the sable antelope area.

Best Things to Do in Waterberg Plateau Park: Activities and Tours

Waterberg Plateau Park offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in the park include:

  • Game Drives: Visitors can enjoy guided game drives, where they can spot the park’s wildlife and learn more about the park’s ecosystem.
  • Cultural Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the local villages and gain insights into the lives and customs of the San and Herero people.
  • Bird Watching: Bird lovers can watch over 200 species of birds in the park, including the rare white-tailed shrike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Waterberg Plateau Park

Waterberg Plateau Park offers visitors a lot to see and do. A recommended week-long itinerary includes:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Waterberg Plateau Park and check into the rest camp.
  • Day 2: Take a guided cultural tour of the park’s cultural sites and the local villages.
  • Day 3: Spend the day hiking or biking on the park’s trails.
  • Day 4: Take a guided game drive to spot the park’s wildlife and learn more about the park’s ecosystem.
  • Day 5: Visit the Waterberg Plateau and enjoy the stunning views it offers.
  • Day 6: Learn about the park’s history and culture at the Waterberg Plateau Rest Camp Museum.
  • Day 7: Depart Waterberg Plateau Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Waterberg Plateau Park

Namibia has a rich food and drink culture that visitors to the Waterberg Plateau Park can enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biltong: A dried meat delicacy, biltong is a Namibian favorite and can be found in most local shops and markets.
  • Potjiekos: A stew cooked over an open fire, featuring meat or vegetables and flavored with a range of spices.
  • Oysters: Namibia is known for its oysters, which are harvested from the Namibian coast and served in many restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Windhoek Beer: A locally brewed beer, Windhoek is a popular choice for locals and tourists alike.
  • Mopani Tea: A traditional tea made from the leaves of the mopani tree, a local hardwood species.
  • Omalovu: This is a traditional Ovambo beer made from sorghum.

Where to Stay in Waterberg Plateau Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Waterberg Plateau Park offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Campers can take advantage of the park’s campsites, while those looking for a bit more luxury can opt for a stay at one of the lodges.

  • Waterberg Plateau Rest Camp: The camp offers camp sites and bungalows with stunning views of the park.
  • Waterberg Wilderness Lodge: Located on the lower slopes of the Waterberg Plateau, the lodge gives visitors breathtaking views of the valley and surrounding hills.
  • Frans Indongo Lodge: The lodge is situated on a private game reserve, providing visitors with the opportunity to spot game in a natural habitat.

What to Buy in Waterberg Plateau Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to the park can buy souvenirs and local products from many shops and markets in the area. Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Crafts and Artifacts: Visitors can purchase traditional crafts such as baskets, pottery, and woodwork.
  • Leather Goods: Namibia is known for its high-quality leather goods, and visitors can purchase products such as belts, wallets, and shoes.
  • Omusati Cheese: A local dairy product, Omusati cheese is made from cow’s milk and is available in most markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Waterberg Plateau Park

Namibia is a diverse country known for its cultural richness. Visitors to the Waterberg Plateau Park can expect to encounter different cultures and ethnic groups, each with their unique customs and traditions. It is essential to respect these customs when visiting the park.

Best Time to Visit Waterberg Plateau Park

The best time to visit Waterberg Plateau is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is mild and dry during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities and game viewing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waterberg Plateau Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Observe park regulations at all times.
  • Carry enough drinking water and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Feed or disturb wildlife.
  • Litter or leave trash behind in the park.
  • Take anything from the park, including flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waterberg Plateau Park

Waterberg Plateau Park is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions against petty crime, such as keeping valuable items close and not wandering off the marked trails.

Health in Waterberg Plateau Park

Visitors are advised to take precautions against malaria when visiting the park. It is recommended to take prophylactic medication before entering the park.

Getting to Waterberg Plateau Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Namibia has a range of transportation options for visitors to get to Waterberg Plateau Park. Some of the options include:

  • Air Travel: The closest airport to Waterberg Plateau Park is Hosea Kutako International Airport, which is approximately 300 km away.
  • Bus Travel: The park is accessible by bus, with regular services running from Windhoek.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car and drive to the park. The park has well-marked roads, and driving is safe and easy.

How to Get Around in Waterberg Plateau Park

Waterberg Plateau Park has excellent infrastructure and is easy to navigate. Visitors can rent a car, use public transport or hire a tour guide to explore the park further.

Where to Go Next from Waterberg Plateau Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors looking to explore more of Namibia can take excursions to nearby destinations such as:

  • Etosha National Park: A world-renowned national park known for its abundant wildlife and spectacular landscape.
  • Windhoek: Namibia’s capital city, Windhoek boasts a rich cultural heritage and fascinating history.
  • Sossusvlei: Home to some of the world’s highest sand dunes, the Sossusvlei area is a must-visit destination for adventure seekers.

Conclusion

Waterberg Plateau Park is a hidden gem that offers a range of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether you are interested in culture, history, wildlife or outdoor adventures, Waterberg Plateau Park has something unique to offer everyone. With its diverse range of accommodation options, excellent infrastructure, and easy accessibility, the park is a perfect destination for travelers looking to experience Namibia’s natural and cultural beauty.
