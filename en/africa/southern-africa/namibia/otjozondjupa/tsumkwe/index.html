Introduction to Tsumkwe

Nestled in the heart of the Otjozondjupa region of Namibia, Tsumkwe is a peaceful town surrounded by the striking landscapes of southern Africa. Known for its diverse culture, rich history, and awe-inspiring natural wonders, Tsumkwe is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the true essence of Namibia. From hiking and biking routes to cultural attractions and delicious local cuisine, there is plenty to see and do in Tsumkwe.

List of Places to Visit in Tsumkwe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Juhoansi Living Museum - Learn about the local Ju/’hoansi tribe’s traditional way of life by touring their village and participating in activities such as fire-making, hunting, and gathering.

  • Nyae Nyae Conservancy - This 9,800 sq km conservancy is home to indigenous communities like the San and their land includes diverse wildlife, sand dunes and vegetation.

  • Bushman Art Gallery - Beautiful bushman paintings by the Ju/’hoan and ‡Khomani San people, and other artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Tsumkwe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nyae Nyae Pans - These natural salt pans are a must-visit for birding enthusiasts, boasting more than 240 species of birds including the majestic flamingos.

  • Khaudum National Park - Considered one of the wildest and remote game parks in Namibia, Khaudum is a haven for wildlife such as elephants, lions, cheetahs, and more.

  • Omuvera Cave - A unique geological formation that features breathtaking stalactite and stalagmite formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsumkwe

  • Nyae Nyae National Reserve Hiking Trail - The 30km trail offers an unforgettable experience of dry savannah habitats with great wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in Tsumkwe: Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon tour - Take a hot air balloon ride and view the vast wilderness of Namibia’s landscapes inside a scenery that will become etched on your memories forever.

  • Visit a Himba Village - Experiencing Himba tribe way of life and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tsumkwe

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Tsumkwe, explore the town, and settle into your accommodation.
2 Visit the Juhoansi Living Museum and learn about the tribe’s traditions and customs.
3 Go on a hot air balloon tour for breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
4 Take a hike or bike ride along the Nyae Nyae National Reserve Trail.
5 Visit the Nyae Nyae Pans for bird watching and photography.
6 Venture to Khaudum National Park for wildlife viewings.
7 Spend your last day enjoying the local cuisine, shopping for souvenirs, and reflecting on your memorable trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsumkwe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seswaa - A traditional Botswana meat stew made with beef, goat or lamb and sefub (sorghum or maize meal).

  • Mopane Worms - A delicacy in Namibia, mopane worms are deep fried or dried and seasoned to be eaten like snacks.

Local Beverages

  • Oshikundu - A local drink made from fermented maize or millet that has a slightly sour taste and is popular among the Himba and Herero tribes.

Where to Stay in Tsumkwe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nhoma Camp - This eco-friendly camp is located near the Ju/’hoansi Living Museum and offers unique cultural experiences and comfortable accommodation.

  • Khaudom Lodge - Featuring six luxury thatched chalets and a family unit, Khaudom Lodge offers a comfortable and luxurious stay in the heart of Namibia’s wildlife reserves.

What to Buy in Tsumkwe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jewelry - Handcrafted jewelry made from ostrich eggs, wooden beads, and copper wire.

  • Basketware - Basketware is woven from the soft grasses, called otjize, that grow in the swamps found in the northeast of Namibia, and is known for its fine texture.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsumkwe

Tsumkwe’s diverse culture is best experienced through interaction with the various indigenous tribes, such as the Ju/’hoansi and Himba.

Best Time to Visit Tsumkwe

The best time to visit Tsumkwe is during its dry and cooler winter season, from May to September. During this time, wildlife sightings are abundant and the weather is mild for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsumkwe

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Learn about the traditional way of life of the indigenous communities and respect their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of the locals without asking for their permission.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment or cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsumkwe

While Tsumkwe has a low crime rate, it is still advisable to take precautions such as not walking alone at night and protecting your valuable belongings.

Health in Tsumkwe

It is recommended that visitors obtain vaccinations for typhoid, hepatitis A and B, and tetanus before traveling to Tsumkwe. Visitors should also take precautions against malaria and drink only bottled or purified water.

Getting to Tsumkwe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsumkwe

The nearest airport to Tsumkwe is the Eros Airport in Windhoek, which is approximately 600 km away.

Bus Stations Near Tsumkwe

Tsumkwe does not have a bus station, but one can take a bus from Windhoek to Tsumkwe with Intercape Mainliner.

Other Options

One can also hire a car and drive to Tsumkwe, but it is advisable to take a sturdy vehicle as the roads can be challenging.

How to Get Around in Tsumkwe

The best way to get around in Tsumkwe is by hiring a car or taking a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Tsumkwe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Etosha National Park - One of Africa’s largest game reserves, known for its abundance of wildlife and stunning landscapes.

  • Waterberg Plateau Park - A national park with diverse flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species.

Conclusion

Tsumkwe is an exciting destination for those looking to experience the true essence of southern Africa. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and vibrant natural wonders, it is a must-visit destination for any traveler seeking an unforgettable adventure.
