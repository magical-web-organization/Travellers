Introduction to Caprivi

Located in the northernmost part of Namibia, Caprivi is a narrow stretch of land that borders Angola, Botswana, and Zambia. Often referred to as Namibia’s “panhandle,” Caprivi is renowned for its abundant wildlife, diverse cultures, and stunning natural scenery. The region spans over 400 km from the Kavango River to the Zambezi River and is home to several national parks and reserves.

List of Places to Visit in Caprivi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mbunza Living Museum - a traditional village that offers an immersive experience into the culture of the Mbunza people. Visitors can learn about traditional crafts and skills and enjoy a cultural performance.

  2. Lizauli Traditional Village - a living museum that showcases the traditional lifestyle of the Mafwe tribe. Visitors can experience traditional food, dance, and music.

  3. Fort Sesfontein - a former German fort that dates back to the colonial era. The fort offers tours that detail its history and the role it played in Namibia’s struggle for independence.

Natural Attractions of Caprivi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bwabwata National Park - known for its abundant wildlife, including elephants, buffalo, lions, and leopards. Visitors can enjoy game drives and guided walks.

  2. Mahango Game Reserve - a small reserve located on the western banks of the Okavango River, offering abundant wildlife viewing opportunities.

  3. Zambezi Region - home to the famous Zambezi River, the region offers river boat cruises and fishing tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caprivi

  1. Popa Falls Hiking Trail - a scenic trail that stretches over 10 km, providing excellent views of the Okavango River and its falls.

  2. Buffalo Core Area Hiking Trail - a challenging trail that takes visitors through the heart of Bwabwata National Park, providing fantastic opportunities to see elephants, lions, and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Caprivi: Activities and Tours

  1. Chobe National Park Day Trip - a full-day guided safari in Botswana’s Chobe National Park, known for its large herds of elephants and buffalo.

  2. Caprivi Cultural Tour - a guided tour through Namibia’s Caprivi region, offering an opportunity to experience local cultures and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Caprivi

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kapana - a popular street food made of grilled meat, often paired with maize meal.

  2. Mopane Worms - a traditional delicacy made of edible caterpillars, high in protein and low in fat.

Local Beverages

  1. Oshikundu - a traditional millet-based drink that is slightly bitter but refreshing.

  2. Tafel Lager - a popular Namibian beer that is light and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Caprivi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Caprivi Mutoya Lodge & Campsite - a luxury campsite located in the heart of Caprivi, offering affordable accommodation and various activities.

  2. Mazambala Island Lodge - a five-star lodge located on an island in the Zambezi River, offering luxury accommodation and various activities.

What to Buy in Caprivi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Traditional Crafts - Caprivi is known for its traditional crafts, including wooden carvings and baskets.

  2. Wildlife Art - various artists in the region create artwork inspired by the wildlife that inhabits Caprivi, including elephants, lions, and buffalo.

Local Culture and Customs of Caprivi

Caprivi is home to many diverse and unique cultures. Visitors should respect the traditions and customs of the local communities by asking before taking photographs or entering private property.

Best Time to Visit Caprivi

The best time to visit Caprivi is during the dry season, from May to October. The weather is mild, and wildlife viewing is optimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caprivi

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and environment.

  • Ask before taking photographs or entering private property.

  • Learn about the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife.

  • Take souvenirs from the national parks or wildlife reserves.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caprivi

Caprivi is relatively safe for tourists. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as not displaying valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Caprivi

Visitors should take standard health precautions when visiting Caprivi. It is recommended to have a yellow fever vaccination certificate if traveling from an endemic country. Malaria prophylaxis is also recommended.

Getting to Caprivi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caprivi

  1. Katima Mulilo Airport (MPA) - located in the town of Katima Mulilo, Caprivi’s largest town.

  2. Ngoma Airport (GZM) - located in Botswana, a short distance from the Namibian border.

Bus Stations Near Caprivi

There are no bus stations in Caprivi. However, several bus companies operate regular services between major towns in Namibia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Car rentals are available in Windhoek, Namibia’s capital city. It is advisable to rent a 4x4 vehicle for travel in Caprivi, as many roads are unpaved and require a high clearance vehicle.

How to Get Around in Caprivi

The most convenient transportation option for getting around in Caprivi is via private vehicle. Car rentals are available in Katima Mulilo and Windhoek.

Where to Go Next from Caprivi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe - a world-renowned waterfall just across the border from Caprivi.

  2. Etosha National Park, Namibia - one of Namibia’s premier game reserves and only a few hours drive from Caprivi.

Conclusion

Caprivi is a hidden gem of Namibia, offering incredible natural scenery, abundant wildlife, and unique cultural experiences. Follow our comprehensive guide to Caprivi to plan a memorable trip and create unforgettable memories.
