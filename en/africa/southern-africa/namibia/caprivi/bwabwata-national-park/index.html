Bwabwata National Park is a stunning destination tucked away in the Caprivi Strip of Namibia in Southern Africa, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural significance. Known for its rich animal wildlife and diverse landscapes, this park covers over 6,100 square kilometers from the Kavango River in the west to the Kwando River in the east. If you’re planning a visit to Bwabwata National Park, read on to discover the must-visit places, the best hiking trails, the delicious local cuisine, top accommodations, and more.

Introduction to Bwabwata National Park

Bwabwata National Park is a nature sanctuary that attracts plenty of wildlife, from herds of elephants and rhino to lions and leopards. However, the park was not always a safe haven for animals. It was once a war zone during the Namibian War of Independence in the 1970s and ’80s. Nowadays it serves as a testament to the potential of conservation efforts in Africa.

List of Places to Visit in Bwabwata National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museums and historical sites can be found in abundance in and around Bwabwata National Park.

  1. Kwando Core Area: The Kwando Core Area is a vital game-viewing area bordered by the floodplains of the Kwando River, and it’s also a great place for fishing.

  2. The Kwando River: The northern boundary of Bwabwata National Park is formed by the Kwando River. The best way to experience the river is on a boat safari.

  3. Popa Falls: Popa Falls is a series of small waterfalls that can be found on the upper reaches of the Okavango River. The falls are located in the Bwabwata National Park, and they’re a great place for fishing, birdwatching, and hiking.

Natural Attractions of Bwabwata National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bwabwata National Park showcases some of the most breathtaking landscapes and outdoor adventures in Namibia. Here are a few of the best natural attractions to explore:

  1. Buffalo Core Area: The Buffalo Core Area is the largest protected area and has the densest herds of buffalo.

  2. Mahango Core Area: The Mahango Core Area, located in the southeastern corner of the park, is a true wilderness area with an incredibly rich and diverse birdlife.

  3. Horseshoe Lagoon: The Horseshoe Lagoon is a popular spot for birdwatchers because of its stunning birdlife. Visitors can spot rare bird species like the slaty egret, wattled crane, and Hartlaub’s babbler.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bwabwata National Park

These are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Bwabwata National Park:

  1. Buffalo Core Area Trail: The 35-kilometer Buffalo Core Area Trail takes hikers through the heart of the park and showcases some of the most outstanding wildlife sightings.

  2. Mahango Core Area Trail: The Mahango Core Area Trail is a 24-kilometer walking trail through the Mahango Core Area.

Best Things to Do in Bwabwata National Park: Activities and Tours

If you visit Bwabwata National Park, here are some essential things to do and possible tours:

  1. Game Drives to the Kwando Core Area: A game drive to the Kwando Core Area is a must for those interested in seeing elephant and buffalo herds through the expansive landscape.

  2. Guided Hiking and Biking Tours: Whether you want to hike through the Buffalo or Mahango Core Areas or bike along the river, guided tours can help you get the most out of your outdoor adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bwabwata National Park

Here’s a week-long itinerary for seeing all that Bwabwata National Park has to offer:

Day 1: Arrive at the park and settle into your accommodation.

Day 2: Game drive in the Kwando Core Area in the morning and afternoon.

Day 3: Guided hike or bike tour of the Buffalo Core Area.

Day 4: Boat safari on the Kwando River in the morning and afternoon.

Day 5: Visit Popa Falls and continue to explore the Buffalo Core Area.

Day 6: Guided walking tour in the Mahango Core Area.

Day 7: Depart from the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Bwabwata National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Namibian cuisine is heavily influenced by its German colonial past. These are some popular dishes to try:

  1. Potjiekos: A traditional meat and vegetable stew.

  2. Braaivleis: A barbecue dish that is popular in South Africa and Namibia.

Local Beverages

  1. Windhoek Lager: A popular Namibian beer.

  2. Mopane tea: A caffeine-free tea made from the leaves of the mopane tree.

Where to Stay in Bwabwata National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Nambwa Tented Lodge: This lodge is set in a private concession within the Bwabwata National Park and offers luxury tented accommodation.

  2. Camp Kwando: Camp Kwando is a charming and authentic lodge with stunning views of the Kwando River.

What to Buy in Bwabwata National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade artisan crafts: The region is known for its exceptional crafts, from woven baskets and pottery to intricate beadwork.

  2. Local jewelry: You can find locally made pieces of jewelry that are extraordinary.

Local Culture and Customs of Bwabwata National Park

People in Namibia are generally welcoming and hospitable. When visiting Bwabwata National Park or any other place in Namibia, respecting local culture and customs can help you have a more enjoyable stay.

Best Time to Visit Bwabwata National Park

The best time to visit Bwabwata National Park is during the winter months, from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bwabwata National Park

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

  2. Take care of the environment and do not throw trash.

Don’t:

  1. Feed animals or disturb their natural habitats.

  2. Approach wildlife too closely.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bwabwata National Park

The park is a safe place to visit, as poaching has decreased over the years. However, visitors are advised to stay away from dangerous animals such as lions and hippos.

Health in Bwabwata National Park

It’s recommended to get vaccinated against diseases such as Yellow Fever and Malaria before entering Namibia.

Getting to Bwabwata National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bwabwata National Park

The nearest airport to Bwabwata National Park is the Katima Mulilo Airport in Katima Mulilo.

Bus Stations Near Bwabwata National Park

Buses traveling to and from Windhoek stop in Rundu and Katima Mulilo, which are the two major towns close to Bwabwata National Park.

Other Options

You can drive to Bwabwata National Park from either Rundu, which is approximately 200 km away, or Katima Mulilo, which is approximately 150 km away.

How to Get Around in Bwabwata National Park

The best way to experience Bwabwata National Park is to rent a car from one of the nearby towns, Rundu or Katima Mulilo. Alternatively, some accommodations provide private transport for their guests.

Where to Go Next from Bwabwata National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kavango Region: The Kavango Region is an unexplored area where nature, culture, and adventure are combined.

  2. Chobe National Park: Chobe National Park is one of the largest national parks in Botswana and is famous for its lush marshes, dry plains, and bountiful wildlife.

Conclusion

Bwabwata National Park is a natural paradise with enough outdoor adventures and cultural experiences to fill anyone’s itinerary. With this guide, you’re sure to have a fulfilling and memorable time in Namibia’s Bwabwata National Park.
