Introduction to Katima Mulilo

Located in the Caprivi region of Namibia, Katima Mulilo is a vibrant town full of rich cultures and diverse landscapes. It serves as the gateway to the Victoria Falls and is situated on the banks of the mighty Zambezi River. This travel guide will explore the best of what Katima Mulilo has to offer, from cultural attractions and natural wonders to hiking/biking trails, must-do activities, where to stay, safety tips, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Katima Mulilo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mpacha Memorial Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Caprivi Strip and the role it played in Namibia’s fight for independence.
  • Old House Museum: Built in 1904, this historic building was once a German colonial governor’s residence and now houses a museum exhibiting the history of Katima Mulilo and the Caprivi Region.
  • King’s Palace Museum: Located in the nearby village of Luhonono, this museum features exhibits about the Lozi tribe’s culture and their traditional leadership.

Natural Attractions of Katima Mulilo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zambezi River: Take a cruise down the river and enjoy its stunning scenery and wildlife.
  • Katima Mulilo Forest Reserve: This reserve is a natural wonderland that features a range of game species and birdlife.
  • Mudumu and Mamili National Parks: These two national parks provide an opportunity to witness the wildest areas of Caprivi, with diverse fauna and flora that you can only find in Africa.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Katima Mulilo

Famous Hiking/Biking Routes

  • Caprivi Adventure Trail: A 700km self-drive and walking trail that crosses the Caprivi region of Namibia and winds through Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Botswana.
  • Nambwa Tented Lodge Walking Trail: This walking trail takes you through the lush forests and wetlands of the Mudumu National Park, where you can see a variety of animals and birds in their natural habitats.

Best Things to Do in Katima Mulilo: Activities and Tours

  • Water Rafting and Kayaking: Zambezi River is an ideal place to experience a thrilling water rafting or kayaking expedition.
  • Fishing: The Zambezi River provides fishing opportunities for anglers of all abilities, with tigerfish being the most sought-after species.
  • Cultural Tours: Explore the fascinating cultures of the Caprivi region by taking a guided tour of local villages or attending traditional ceremonies.

Food and Drink Scene of Katima Mulilo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kapana: This dish is a popular street food in Namibia and is essentially grilled meat with onions and chili.
  • Mopane Worms: These are a local delicacy and are often served fried, cooked with tomatoes, or pounded into a paste.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is a traditional alcoholic drink made by fermenting sap from palm trees.
  • Kapana Beer: This is a popular local beer that is brewed from maize.

Where to Stay in Katima Mulilo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Old House Guesthouse: This guesthouse is in the heart of Katima Mulilo and offers comfortable, affordable rooms with lovely garden surroundings.
  • Chundukwa River Lodge: This lodge is located on the banks of the Zambezi River and offers luxury accommodation with breathtaking river views.
  • Caprivi Houseboat Safaris: This unique experience provides an opportunity to stay aboard a houseboat while exploring the Zambezi River, complete with guided tours and game viewing.

What to Buy in Katima Mulilo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crafts: You can find a range of handmade crafts, such as baskets and beadwork, depicting local traditions and cultures.
  • Carvings: Beautiful carved wooden animals, masks, and other items can be found at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Katima Mulilo

  • Greetings: The Lozi tribe uses a unique greeting system that involves a handshake accompanied by a special clap and following with an ask of “Lima pe?” (meaning, “how is your farming going?”).
  • Clothing: Dress modestly in public places, covering your shoulders and knees.

Best Time to Visit Katima Mulilo

The best time to visit Katima Mulilo is from May to October when the weather conditions are dry and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Katima Mulilo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Keep eyes peeled for wild animals and ride only in designated areas.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without permission.
  • Litter, and pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Katima Mulilo

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Secure your belongings and valuables in the physical storage.
  • Do not carry large amounts of cash on you.

Health in Katima Mulilo

  • Anti-malaria medication is recommended.
  • Drink bottled water or boiled, filtered water.

Getting to Katima Mulilo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Katima Mulilo

  • Mfuwe International Airport: Mfuwe International Airport is in Zambia and is the gateway to the South Luangwa National Park.
  • Livingstone Airport: Livingstone airport is in Zambia and is the closest airport to the Victoria Falls.

Bus Stations Near Katima Mulilo

  • Wenela Bus Station: The Wenela Bus Station is in the heart of Katima Mulilo and serves as a hub for buses traveling to/from Botswana, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.

Other Options

  • Car Hire: Car hire is available in Katima Mulilo from rental companies like Avis, Europcar, and Hertz.
  • Transfers: Private transfers can be arranged in advance by your hotel/lodge.

How to Get Around in Katima Mulilo

The easiest way to get around Katima Mulilo is by car, bicycle, or walking.

Where to Go Next from Katima Mulilo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Victoria Falls: Visit the gorgeous Victoria Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Botswana: Take a trip across the border and explore the Chobe National Park, home to large herds of elephants and other wildlife.
  • Zimbabwe: Take a short flight or drive and visit the Hwange National Park, the largest natural reserve in Zimbabwe.
