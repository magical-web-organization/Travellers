Introduction to Omaheke

Located in the eastern part of Namibia, Omaheke is a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience of Africa. It is home to a diverse range of cultures, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, making it an ideal place for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike.

Because of its location near the Kalahari Desert and the Gaborone-Mamuno Trans-Kalahari Highway, the region has become an important stop for those traveling by road from Botswana to Namibia.

List of Places to Visit in Omaheke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Traditional Himba Village: Get a glimpse into the lives of the Himba people, an indigenous group famous for their red ochre skin lotion and unique style of dressing.
  2. The Ovaherero Cultural Center: Learn all about the history and customs of the Ovaherero people, one of the largest ethnic groups in Namibia.
  3. The Torgos Lodge Museum: Discover the natural history of the area at this museum, which houses a collection of fossils and displays on the flora and fauna of the region.

Natural Attractions of Omaheke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Waterberg Plateau: Hike through the lush plateau, home to various wildlife including rhinos, cheetahs, and vultures.
  2. Spitzkoppe: This iconic landmark features stunning rock formations and is perfect for hiking, climbing, and stargazing at night.
  3. Ghaub Cave: Explore Namibia’s longest cave system, which features stunning stalactites and stalagmites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Omaheke

  1. Kalahari Red Dunes Trail: This 6-day hike takes you across the Kalahari Desert, where you’ll witness stunning red sand dunes and remote Himba villages.
  2. Eastern Escarpment Trail: This challenging 8-day hike takes you through the Waterberg Plateau and along the eastern escarpment of the Kalahari Basin.
  3. Dorsland Trek: Trace the route of the Dorsland Trekkers, a group of Boers who migrated northward from South Africa in the late 1800s.

Best Things to Do in Omaheke: Activities and Tours

  1. Go on a safari to see black rhino, giraffe, and other wildlife at the Waterberg Plateau Park.
  2. Visit a traditional Himba village and learn about their daily life and customs.
  3. Explore the Torgos Lodge Museum and its display of fossils and the flora and fauna of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Omaheke

Day Activity
1 Visit the Himba Village and learn about the traditions of the indigenous peoples in the region.
2-3 Go on a safari through the Waterberg Plateau to witness stunning wildlife, and hike through the area in the evening.
4-5 Hike along the Eastern Escarpment Trail and experience the vast natural beauty of the region.
6-7 Go on a guided tour of the Torgos Lodge Museum to learn about the natural history of Omaheke.

Food and Drink Scene of Omaheke

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kalahari Oysters (Jewels of the Desert): These unusual delicacies are actually pickled and sliced wild watermelon.
  2. Mopane Worms: A local delicacy that is high in protein, these edible caterpillars are often dried and eaten as a snack.
  3. Ostrich Steak: A lean and healthy meat option that is locally farmed and served in many restaurants in the region.

Local Beverages

  1. Windhoek Lager: This locally brewed beer is popular throughout Namibia, and a must-try for beer lovers.
  2. Omaheke Wine: A selection of red and white wines produced in Omaheke, made from locally grown grapes.
  3. Rooibos Tea: This caffeine-free herbal tea is a great way to relax and enjoy the view in the afternoon.

Where to Stay in Omaheke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Waterberg Plateau Lodge: This lodge offers stunning panoramic views of the Omaheke region, and is just a few kilometers from the popular Waterberg Plateau.
  2. Ghaub Guest Farm: Located in the heart of the Kalahari Basin, this cozy guest farm offers unique accommodations and a great atmosphere.
  3. Torgos Lodge: This lodge is perfect for those interested in experiencing the stunning natural beauty of the region, as it is located on the edge of the Kalahari Desert.

What to Buy in Omaheke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Namibian Tribal Crafts: From woven baskets to handmade jewelry, there are many beautiful handicrafts available in the region.
  2. Oryx Jerky: A famous snack in Namibia, oryx jerky is made from locally hunted game and is a great souvenir to bring home.
  3. Kalahari Salt: A gourmet salt that is hand-harvested from the Kalahari Basin, this salt is a great choice for foodies and chefs.

Local Culture and Customs of Omaheke

The region is home to several ethnic groups, including the Herero, Himba, Damara, and San Bushmen. Visitors can learn about their traditions and customs by visiting cultural centers and attending cultural performances.

Visitors should also be aware of cultural norms such as shaking hands and using formal titles when addressing elders.

Best Time to Visit Omaheke

The best time to visit Omaheke is between May and October, when the weather is dry and temperatures are mild. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and safari tours without worrying about rain and humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Omaheke

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions by dressing modestly and using formal titles when addressing elders.
  2. Try the local food and beverages, including the local beer and wine.
  3. Learn about the local flora and fauna, and support conservation efforts by following established trails and avoiding disturbing wildlife.

Don’t:

  1. Buy souvenirs made from endangered species or looted antiquities.
  2. Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent when venturing out in the sun.
  3. Take pictures of individuals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Omaheke

While the region is generally safe for travelers, visitors should be aware of the threat of wildlife, especially in the Waterberg Plateau Park. Visitors should also take precautions when driving in remote areas, and avoid driving at night.

Health in Omaheke

Visitors to Omaheke are at risk of contracting malaria and should take precautions such as wearing protective clothing and using mosquito nets and insect repellent. Visitors should also ensure they are up to date on routine vaccinations.

Getting to Omaheke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Omaheke

The nearest airports to Omaheke are Windhoek Eros Airport and Hosea Kutako International Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Omaheke.

Bus Stations Near Omaheke

The nearest bus station to Omaheke is in Windhoek, which offers daily bus services to and from major cities in Namibia and South Africa.

Other Options

Travelers can also take self-driven tours or hire a private guide to explore the region.

How to Get Around in Omaheke

The best way to get around Omaheke is by renting a car or going on guided tours. Visitors can also use public transportation such as taxis and buses, but should be aware that they are not always reliable and may be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Omaheke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Etosha National Park: A world-renowned safari destination that is home to some of Africa’s most famous wildlife.
  2. Sossusvlei: Explore the stunning red sand dunes and unique landscapes of this iconic Namibian destination.
  3. Swakopmund: A beautiful coastal town known for its German architecture and outdoor activities, such as sandboarding and skydiving.

Conclusion

Omaheke offers travelers a unique and authentic experience of Africa, with its diverse range of cultures, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in hiking, wildlife safaris, or local culture, there is something to do and see in Omaheke for everyone.
