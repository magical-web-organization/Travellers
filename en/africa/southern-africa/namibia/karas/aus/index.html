Introduction to Aus

Located in the ǁKaras region of Namibia, Aus is a small town that offers a blend of historical significance and natural beauty. The town itself is steeped in history, with a notable focus on colonialism and war. Additionally, the town’s location is perfect for exploring outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and animal spotting. Its proximity to several national parks and game reserves makes it a must-visit destination for those who love wildlife and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Aus

Als, although a small town, Aus has several places to visit. Here are some of the most important ones.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Keetmanshoop Museum

Founded in 1974, Keetmanshoop museum features a wide range of historical items, ranging from ancient times to the colonial era. You can explore various exhibits on offer about the town’s history, along with Namibian culture.

2. Lüderitz Museum

The Lüderitz museum was set up over 2000 years ago and largely focused on the colonial history of the town. There are many exhibits that detail the town’s past, along with pieces of furniture and antiques.

Natural Attractions of Aus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Kolmanskop Ghost Town

Once a thriving mining town in the early 20th century, the Kolmanskop Ghost Town is now an abandoned settlement shrouded in sand. Visitors can explore the town’s hospital, the grand casino, and other places, and it is a perfect place for photography.

2. Aus Wildlife Sanctuary

The Aus Wildlife Sanctuary is a conservation area that offers visitors a chance to spot various endemic and indigenous species. It is home to several animal species such as the Hartmann’s Mountain Zebra, Oryx, and Springbok.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aus

There are several hiking and biking routes in and around Aus, offering visitors a chance to explore the town’s natural beauty.

1. Naute Dam

Dotted along the road between Aus and Keetmanshoop, Naute Dam offers many hiking and biking trails. The dam itself is fascinating, and visitors can watch as local fishermen catch their daily haul.

2. Sperrgebiet National Park

Sperrgebiet National Park is a protected reserve that offers several trails for visitors to explore. The park’s landscape is incredible, and you can spot a range of wildlife from small reptiles to large antelopes like Gemsbok, Wildebeest, and Eland.

Best Things to Do in Aus: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the town’s natural beauty and cultural significance, Aus offers several activities and tours for visitors to experience.

1. NamibRand Nature Reserve

The NamibRand Nature Reserve is located in the southern part of Namibia, and it offers several safari tours for visitors to experience. You can see majestic animals such as lion, cheetah, and rhinos in these safaris.

2. Fish River Canyon

Fish River Canyon is one of the main attractions in Namibia and a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Visitors can take a day trip to explore its beauty and take in the stunning views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aus

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit to Aus:

Day 1 Keetmanshoop Museum
Day 2 Kolmanskop Ghost Town
Day 3 Aus Wildlife Sanctuary
Day 4 Naute Dam
Day 5 Sperrgebiet National Park
Day 6 NamibRand Nature Reserve
Day 7 Fish River Canyon

Food and Drink Scene of Aus

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Aus is simple with several street vendors and marketplaces offering local delicacies. Popular dishes include

  • Potjiekos: a traditional meat and vegetable stew
  • Biltong: cured meat like beef or kudu
  • Vetkoek: fried doughy savory donut

Local Beverages

Local beverages to try in Aus include:

  • Windhoek Lager
  • Amarula Cream

What to Buy in Aus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the souvenirs you can buy in Aus include:

  • Himba Jewelry
  • Sand-filled eggs: these are beautiful and unique carvings of animals and birds, filled with desert sand.
  • Oryx jerky: a must-try snack popular in Namibia

Local Culture and Customs of Aus

Nomadic Himba tribespeople are prevalent in the southern regions of Namibia. In Aus, visitors can have the opportunity to meet the Himba and learn about their culture and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Aus

The best time to visit Aus is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. The weather is mild, and visitors can avoid the heat of the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aus

Do:

  • Respect the local cultures and customs
  • Bring appropriate clothing, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the Himba without permission
  • Disrespect any cultural or religious events.
  • Drive on the desert floor without proper gear and guides

Safety and Health

Safety in Aus

Aus is largely safe for travelers. Still, it is essential to take regular safety precautions to avoid any possible theft cases, stay safe at night, or avoid dangerous areas.

Health in Aus

Travelers need to take basic health precautions such as ensuring they are up to date with their vaccines and taking anti-malaria tablets when necessary. Medical assistance is available in the larger towns, but travelers are advised to bring any necessary medication before arrival.

Getting to Aus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aus

The Hosea Kutako International Airport is the closest airport to Aus, situated about 1,000 kilometers from Aus. It provides flights to all major African cities.

Bus Stations Near Aus

The luxury bus service, Intercape, connects Aus to other destinations throughout Namibia, South Africa, and Zimbabwe.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel by car as the tarred road between Keetmanshoop and Lüderitz is in good condition.

How to Get Around in Aus

Getting around in Aus is mostly done by foot, although there are taxis available. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding areas conveniently.

Where to Go Next from Aus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some destinations that visitors can explore from Aus:

  • Lüderitz: a coastal town located about 100 kilometers from Aus, perfect for exploring Namibian colonial history.
  • Sossusvlei: a popular sand dune range located about 350 km from Aus that attracts visitors from all over the world.
  • Windhoek: the capital city of Namibia, located about 560 km from Aus.

Conclusion

Aus combines a rich history with natural beauty to offer visitors a unique experience. From hiking and animal viewing to cultural experiences and food delicacies, there is something for everyone in this Namibian town. With a detailed itinerary of what to do, see, and experience in Aus, visitors can make the most of their time in this exceptional destination.
