Introduction to Outjo

Outjo, located in the Kunene region of Namibia, is a hidden gem that often gets overlooked by travelers. Known for its stunning natural beauty, cultural significance, and adventurous outdoor activities, Outjo is the perfect destination for a unique vacation experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Outjo, Namibia.

List of Places to Visit in Outjo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Outjo Museum: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs, showcasing a comprehensive collection of artifacts and exhibits that reveal the rich cultural heritage of the area.

Natural Attractions of Outjo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Etosha National Park: Etosha is one of the most popular national parks in Namibia, famous for its vast open spaces, diverse wildlife, and stunning landscapes.
  • Waterberg Plateau Park: The Waterberg Plateau Park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, bird watching, and game drives.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outjo

Outjo offers a range of hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventurous travelers looking for a challenge. Some of the best routes include:

  • Mount Etjo: This challenging hike rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding hills and countryside.
  • Grootberg Pass: This biking route takes you through the rugged wilderness of the Kunene region and is an unforgettable adventure.

Best Things to Do in Outjo: Activities and Tours

  • Game Drives: Take a guided game drive through Etosha National Park and experience the thrill of spotting some of Africa’s most iconic wildlife.
  • Cultural Tours: Discover the rich cultural history of Outjo with a guided tour of the town’s museums and historical sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Outjo

Day 1: Arrival and check-in Day 2: Etosha National Park Day 3: Waterberg Plateau Park Day 4: Hiking or biking Day 5: Cultural activities Day 6: Game drives Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Outjo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biltong: This delicious and chewy treat is a popular snack in Namibia, made from dried and cured meat.
  • Oshifima: This traditional staple food is made from maize meal and is often served with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tafel lager: This locally brewed beer is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.
  • Mopani worm tea: This exotic tea is made from the dried shells of Mopani worms, a local delicacy.

Where to Stay in Outjo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mid-Range Hotels

  • Etotongwe Lodge: This lodge is located just outside of Outjo and offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

Budget Lodging

  • Etosha Safari Camp: This budget-friendly camp is located just outside of Outjo and offers basic but comfortable accommodation in traditional-styled huts.

What to Buy in Outjo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ovambo dolls: These handcrafted dolls are a unique souvenir and a great way to support the local community.
  • Mopani worm crisps: These crunchy snacks are made from the larvae of the Mopani moth, and are a local delicacy.

Local Culture and Customs of Outjo

Outjo is home to a diverse range of cultures, including the Himba, Herero, and Ovambo people. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and seeking permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Outjo

The best time to visit Outjo is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. This time of year offers cooler temperatures and clear skies, making it ideal for outdoor activities and wildlife viewing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outjo

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough cash, as ATMs and credit card facilities may not be available in all places.
  • Be mindful of the environment and the impact of your actions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore basic safety precautions such as locking your car and keeping valuable items out of sight.
  • Take photographs of people without permission.
  • Engage in any activities that harm wildlife or the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Outjo

Outjo is a safe destination for travelers, but basic safety precautions should be taken, such as not leaving valuables in plain sight and locking car doors.

Health in Outjo

It is recommended to get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B before visiting Outjo. Mosquitoes are prevalent in the region, so it is also advisable to take necessary precautions against malaria.

Getting to Outjo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Outjo

  • Hosea Kutako International Airport: This is the nearest international airport, located approximately 300 kilometers from Outjo.

Bus Stations Near Outjo

  • Outjo Bus Station: This bus station is located in the town center and offers regular connections to other towns in Namibia.

Other Options

  • Car rental is a popular option for travelers who want to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Outjo

There are several transportation options for getting around Outjo, including car rental, taxis, and public transport.

Where to Go Next from Outjo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Damaraland: This rugged region is home to ancient rock art, geological wonders, and a unique array of wildlife.
  • Skeleton Coast: Explore the desolate yet beautiful coastline of Namibia, known for its shipwrecks and roaming wildlife.

Conclusion

Outjo is a destination that offers something for everyone, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Plan your trip now and explore this hidden gem in Namibia.
