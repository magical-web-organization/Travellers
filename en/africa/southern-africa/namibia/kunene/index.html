Introduction to Kunene

Kunene is a region located in the north-western part of Namibia, bordering Angola. This rugged region is known for its diverse landscapes, rich wildlife, and unique culture. Kunene is home to the Himba, one of the few remaining semi-nomadic tribes in Africa, who have captivated travelers with their traditional attire and way of life. The region is beloved by adventurers for its scenic views, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Kunene

Kunene offers a range of cultural and natural attractions, from museums to outdoor adventures, that are worth visiting.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kunene Museum: Learn about the region’s rich history and traditional way of life.
  • Onduruquea Rock Paintings: Admire the ancient rock art that depicts the daily life and spiritual beliefs of the San people.
  • Himba Village Tours: Experience firsthand the Himba people’s way of life and their vibrant culture.

Natural Attractions of Kunene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Epupa Falls: Admire the stunning cascading waterfalls, one of Namibia’s greatest natural wonders.
  • Skeleton Coast National Park: Explore the otherworldly coastline that showcases the harsh beauty of Namibia’s desert landscapes.
  • Damaraland: Enjoy hiking or bike riding in this rugged landscape that is home to Namibia’s famous desert-adapted elephants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kunene

Kunene is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many trails that showcase the region’s diverse landscapes. Here are some must-try hiking and biking routes of Kunene:

  • The Kunene River Hike: This 7-day hike takes you along the Kunene River, and offers stunning views of wildlife, waterfalls and dramatic drops.
  • The Otjihipa Mountain Trail: This mountain track is excellent for those looking for a challenging and picturesque hike.
  • The Etendeka Mountain Trail: Hike around volcanoes, climb to the rim of extinct craters or explore valleys to uncover hidden springs.

Best Things to Do in Kunene: Activities and Tours

Kunene offers a range of unique and memorable experiences that cater to everyone’s interests, like:

  • Himba Village Tours: Experience the Himba culture first-hand and witness their daily life.
  • Scenic Flights: Get lost in the stunning view of the region from above, an excellent experience for thrill-seekers.
  • Stargazing: Experience a mesmerizing sky full of stars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kunene

Kunene has a lot to offer to its visitors, and planning a week-long itinerary ensures you make the best of your trip. Here’s an example of a 7-day itinerary in Kunene:

Day 1-2 Visit the Epupa falls, take a guided tour, and stay around in Opuwo town.
Day 3-4 Book a Himba Village Tour experience, dropping by different villages and experiencing the life of the Himba people.
Day 5-6 Engage in outdoor adventures such as hiking and bike riding in the Damaraland region.
Day 7 Finish your tour by taking a Scenic Flight of the region while admiring the Geographical landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Kunene

Namibia has a unique cuisine, reflecting its diverse history and culture. Kunene, like other regions in Namibia, has several local dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks for foodies:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kapana: Pronounced “cup-anna,” this traditional delicacy is made from grilled beef or goat meat and is typically served with spices and chili.
  • Mopani Worms: Abundant in the region’s mopani trees, these worms are rich in protein and are considered a delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Omagongo: This tasty marula fruit beverage has high levels of Vitamin C and is also considered a traditional drink for special occasions like weddings.
  • Tafel Lager: A locally produced beer that is a must-try for beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Kunene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kunene has several accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences, including luxury lodges, guesthouses, and campsites. Here are some recommended options:

  • Epupa Camp: A luxurious campsite that offers brilliant views of the Epupa Falls and the Kunene River.
  • Opuwo Country Lodge: A comfortable lodge with clean and cozy rooms, located in a picturesque setting.
  • Otjitotongwe Cheetah Guest Farm: A unique experience of living near Cheetahs, this farm offers services that cater to its visitors.

What to Buy in Kunene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kunene produces several unique products that serve as memorable souvenirs. Here are some worth buying:

  • Himba Jewelry: The Himba people are famous for their traditional jewelry made from ostrich eggshells, copper, and leather.
  • Carvings: Hand-carved wooden animals and other decorative items are popular souvenirs in the region.
  • Karosse- a type of traditional clothing typically made leather and ostrich feather.
  • Ehi-Maize- is the region’s traditional porridge that can be bought as a food product to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kunene

The region is home to the Himba people, known for their traditional way of life and colorful attire. Their dress code and jewelry are not only functional, but they also symbolize specific values and beliefs. Visitors to the region should respect their cultural practices and ask permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Kunene

Kunene can be visited year-round, but the best time to explore the region is during the cooler and drier months of May to October. The weather is pleasant for outdoor activities like hiking and biking, and this period witnesses several wildlife sightings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kunene

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, respect the Himba people and seek permission before taking pictures.
  • Be prepared for a rugged trip. Kunene is a remote area where proper infrastructure might be limited.
  • Engage in outdoor activities to witness the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Do not abandon your car or wander off on foot because it’s easy to get lost in the desert terrain.
  • Don’t litter, as this can harm the environment and the wildlife living in it.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal activity like game poaching—Namibia vehemently prosecutes game poachers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kunene

Kunene is a safe region to visit, but like every tourism destination, there are still advisable precautions visitors should take, including:

  • Don’t leave valuables visible in cars.
  • Don’t hitchhike. Instead, choose registered taxi services and public transportation.
  • Avoid visiting some areas at night or wandering off alone.

Health in Kunene

Visitors are advised to take malaria prophylaxis before visiting the region, as it is a high-risk area. Use mosquito repellent, wear long-sleeve and long-pants, and sleep under mosquito nets. It’s also essential to carry a comprehensive first-aid kit to tackle minor medical emergencies.

Getting to Kunene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Namibia has good infrastructure and transport networks despite its remote locations. The primary mode of transportation is road transport, and visitors can also choose chartered flights into the region. Here are some transportation options to help you get to Kunene:

Airports Near Kunene

  • Eha Pahala is the region’s primary airport, located in Opuwo town
  • Sesfontein Airstrip, popular for tourists visiting the Damaraland region.

Bus Stations Near Kunene

  • Minibus taxi: This shared transport is a common mode of transportation in Namibia, but the route to Kunene is limited.

Other Options

  • Chartered flight: Fly into the region with flights chartered from Eros Airport in Windhoek.

How to Get Around in Kunene

The beauty of Kunene and its unique landscapes can best be appreciated through a self-drive road trip. Renting a car in Opuwo town is the most convenient option. Visitors can also use taxi services or public transportation, but it might be more challenging as the region is remote, and public transportation might be limited.

Where to Go Next from Kunene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Namibia offers a variety of unparalleled locations that visitors can explore after visiting Kunene. Here are some nearby destinations visitors can consider:

  • Etosha National Park: A popular wildlife reserve, renowned for its diverse animal population.
  • Twyfelfontein: A UNESCO World Heritage site that features ancient rock art and engravings that depict the history of the San people, dating back to thousands of years ago.
  • Swakopmund: A beach resort town, offers diverse excursions like dune bashing or exquisite dining experiences.

Conclusion

Kunene’s captivating landscapes, rich cultural history, and unique wildlife are worth exploring. The region is home to unique tribes that showcase their way of life. Visitors seeking an adventure will not be disappointed by opting to hike, bike ride or going for scenic flights. With proper planning, visitors can have a memorable trip, taking in the natural beauty of Kunene while experiencing the culture of Himba people.
