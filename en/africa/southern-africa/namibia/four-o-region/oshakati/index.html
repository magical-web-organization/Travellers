Introduction to Oshakati

Oshakati is a city in the Four-O region of Namibia, located in Southern Africa. It is the capital of the Oshana region and the fourth-largest city in the country. Oshakati is a bustling city known for its vibrant culture, rich history and beautiful landscapes. This tour guide will provide you with everything you need to know when planning a trip to Oshakati.

List of Places to Visit in Oshakati

Oshakati offers a range of attractions for travellers to explore, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of some of the best places to visit in Oshakati:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oshakati Open Market: This bustling market is a great place to experience authentic Namibian culture. It is home to various vendors selling a wide range of local produce, arts, and crafts.
  • Eenhana Memorial: This monument is located in the nearby town of Eenhana and honours the fallen soldiers of the Namibian War for Independence.
  • Oshakati Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Oshana region, featuring exhibits on traditional clothing, tribal customs, and local artwork.

Natural Attractions of Oshakati: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Etosha National Park: One of the most popular national parks in Africa, Etosha is home to diverse wildlife including lions, elephants, and cheetahs. It is located just a few hours’ drive from Oshakati.
  • Oshana Region Dam: This artificial lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking. The surrounding landscapes boast scenic views of the nearby mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oshakati

The Oshana region offers a range of hiking and biking trails for adventurers looking to explore the outdoors.

  • Oshakati to Ondangwa Trail: This scenic trail stretches over 20km and features waterfalls, caves, and stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Ongwediva Cycling Trail: This 40km trail offers a challenging yet rewarding experience for cycling enthusiasts. It offers impressive views of the region’s unique flora and fauna as well as the stunning Oshana Region Dam.

Best Things to Do in Oshakati: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do and unique tours to take in Oshakati. Whether you’re looking to dive into the local culture or experience the outdoors, there is something for everyone.

  • Oshana Cultural Tour: This immersive experience takes travellers through the heart of the traditional Oshana culture. Visitors will learn about local customs and traditions, view traditional dances, and learn how to prepare local dishes.
  • Etosha National Park Safari: This exciting safari experience takes visitors on a guided tour of Etosha National Park. The tour offers an opportunity to see the park’s wildlife up close and personal.

Food and Drink Scene of Oshakati

The Oshana region is famous for its unique and delicious cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the traditional Oshana culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oshifima: A traditional Oshana dish made from maize meal, often served with beef, chicken or fish.
  • Kapana: Grilled meat skewered on sticks, served with a variety of traditional dipping sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Oshikundu: A traditional drink made from fermented millet or sorghum.
  • Mahangu Beer: A traditional beer made from mahangu grains, and is a favourite of locals.

Where to Stay in Oshakati: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options in Oshakati, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Protea Hotel Ondangwa: A luxurious and comfortable hotel located just 30 minutes outside of Oshakati.
  • Oshikango Bed and Breakfast: This warm and inviting guesthouse is located just a few blocks from the centre of Oshakati.

What to Buy in Oshakati: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oshakati offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or reminders of your trip. Here are some popular options:

  • Handcrafted jewellery: Local artisans create beautiful jewellery from natural materials, such as bone, wood, and shells.
  • Traditional textiles: Vibrant textiles, such as shawls and blankets, are made by hand and feature traditional patterns and colours.

Local Culture and Customs of Oshakati

The Oshana region is rich in culture and traditions. Visitors can expect to see traditional celebrations, such as dances and feasts, throughout the year. However, it is important to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly and avoiding taking photos without asking for permission.

Best Time to Visit Oshakati

The best time to visit Oshakati is during the cooler months, from June to September. During this time, temperatures are mild, making outdoor activities more comfortable. It is also the peak tourist season, so travellers can expect more crowds during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oshakati

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Oshakati:

Do:

  • Respect traditional customs and dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.
  • Try local dishes and beverages, they are delicious and unique.
  • Take plenty of water when hiking or doing outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without asking for permission, as some locals may be uncomfortable with their picture being taken.
  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Leave items unattended, pickpocketing and theft can occur in busy tourist areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oshakati

Oshakati is a relatively safe city, but visitors should exercise caution, especially in crowded areas. It is recommended to avoid walking alone at night and to keep valuables locked up.

Health in Oshakati

Travellers should be up to date on all routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), tetanus, and influenza. It is also recommended to seek advice from a healthcare professional before travelling to ensure all necessary travel vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Oshakati: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oshakati

The closest airport to Oshakati is the Ondangwa Airport, which is located just 30 minutes outside of the city. It offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Oshakati

Oshakati is serviced by the City Liner bus service, which offers convenient transport to all major cities in Namibia.

Other Options

Travellers can also take a taxi, rental car, or private shuttle to get to and from Oshakati.

How to Get Around in Oshakati

Travellers can get around Oshakati by taxi, car rentals, or by walking. Taxis are widely available and are a convenient way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Oshakati: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth checking out:

  • Windhoek: Namibia’s capital city is located just a few hours’ drive from Oshakati and is home to various cultural sites and shopping outlets.
  • Swakopmund: This beautiful coastal town is located on Namibia’s Atlantic coast and is known for its stunning beaches, beautiful architecture, and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

Oshakati is a must-visit destination for travellers looking to experience the best of Namibian culture, history, and natural beauty. With excellent accommodation options, exciting activities, and delicious local cuisine, Oshakati is the perfect destination for your next vacation.
