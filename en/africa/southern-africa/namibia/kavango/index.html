Introduction to Kavango

Kavango is a region located in the north-eastern part of Namibia, bordered by Angola to the north and Botswana to the east. It’s an area well-known for its diverse wildlife, scenic landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Kavango can expect a unique travel experience that combines outdoor adventure, local culture, and authentic cuisine. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do in Kavango, including recommended places to visit, hiking routes, food and drink options, where to stay, and travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Kavango

Kavango has many attractions that cater to different interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in Kavango:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nkurenkuru Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Kavango’s people through artefacts, photographs, and exhibitions.

  • Lizauli Traditional Village: Visitors can learn about the traditional lifestyle and practices of the local Kavango people, including music, dance, and food.

  • Kavango Craft Centre: This centre is a hub for local artisans who sell their crafts, including pottery, beadwork, and wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Kavango: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Popa Falls: A series of cascading waterfalls along the Kavango River provides a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities such as fishing, birdwatching, and boat tours.

  • Mahango Game Reserve: Visitors can spot over 70 species of mammals, including elephants, hippos, and antelopes, as well as a variety of bird species.

  • Kavango River: The mighty river provides opportunities for water activities such as canoeing, kayaking, and river cruises.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kavango

Kavango’s natural landscapes are ideal for hiking and biking. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Bwabwata Hiking Trail: This 4-day trail takes visitors through the Bwabwata National Park, home to elephants, lions, and other wildlife.

  • Nkasa Rupara National Park: Visitors can take a guided bike tour on the park’s 50km of trails, which wind through lush vegetation and provide views of the Kwando River.

Best Things to Do in Kavango: Activities and Tours

  • Take a cultural tour: Visitors can learn about the local Kavango culture and traditions with a guide who can help them interact with local people and experience the traditional cuisine.

  • Go on a river cruise: From a relaxing sunset cruise to an adventure-packed fishing trip, visitors can take in the beauty of Kavango’s river landscapes in a unique way.

  • Visit a game reserve: From Mahango Game Reserve to Bwabwata National Park, visitors can see some of Africa’s most iconic animals and learn about conservation efforts in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kavango

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Kavango:

Day Activity
1 Visit Nkurenkuru Museum, and enjoy local cuisine
2 Drive to Popa Falls and go on a boat tour, and visit a nearby traditional village
3 Visit Kavango Craft Centre and try your hand at pottery
4 Go on a river cruise, try fishing, and enjoy a braai (BBQ)
5 Take a guided tour of Mahango Game Reserve
6 Go on a hiking trail in Bwabwata National Park
7 Chill out by the river, enjoy local drinks, and soak up the African sun

Food and Drink Scene of Kavango

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kapana: Grilled meat served with hot chili sauce and local beer.

  • Oshifima: Maize porridge served with stew or beef.

  • Mopane Worms: A local delicacy served fried or cooked in a soup.

Local Beverages

  • Mahangu Beer: A traditional beer made from mahangu (millet) and brewed by locals.

  • Oshikundu: A fermented drink made from mahangu and served cold.

Where to Stay in Kavango: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kavango has a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and needs. Some recommended places to stay include:

  • Hakusembe River Lodge: A luxury lodge on the banks of the Kavango River that offers river cruises, fishing trips, and game drives.

  • Nunda Safari Lodge: A rustic lodge that offers guided tours of Mahango Game Reserve and sunset river cruises.

  • Rainbow River Lodge: A budget-friendly option that offers self-catering rooms and river activities.

What to Buy in Kavango: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wood carvings: Hand-carved wooden sculptures and artefacts are popular souvenirs.

  • Beadwork: Traditional beadwork jewellery and art is sold at local markets.

  • Pottery: Handmade pottery is sold at Kavango Craft Centre.

Local Culture and Customs of Kavango

The locals in Kavango are friendly and welcoming. Visitors should dress modestly and ask for permission before taking photos of people or their homes. It’s important to respect local customs and traditions, such as giving greetings and showing respect for elders.

Best Time to Visit Kavango

The best time to visit Kavango is during the dry season between May and October. This is when wildlife is more visible, and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kavango

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

  • Do interact with the local people and learn about their culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t give money to beggars or children asking for money.

  • Don’t buy ivory or wildlife products.

  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kavango

Kavango is a safe destination for travellers, but visitors should take normal precautions such as not walking alone at night and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Kavango

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and malaria prophylaxis is recommended for all travellers. It’s important to stay hydrated and practice good hygiene to prevent illness.

Getting to Kavango: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kavango

  • Rundu Airport is the nearest airport to Kavango region, and is located about 75km away.

  • Katima Mulilo Airport is another option and is located 300km from Kavango.

Bus Stations Near Kavango

  • Kavango is well connected by bus to major cities such as Windhoek and Swakopmund.

Other Options

  • Visitors can rent a car and drive to Kavango, as the roads are well maintained.

  • Private transfers and guided tours are also available.

How to Get Around in Kavango

Visitors can hire a car or take a taxi to get around Kavango. Some lodges and guesthouses offer guided tours and shuttles.

Where to Go Next from Kavango: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Etosha National Park: One of the largest game reserves in Africa, famous for its wildlife and stunning scenery.

  • Caprivi Strip: A narrow strip of land that extends between Botswana and Namibia, home to a variety of game reserves, wildlife, and the Okavango River.

Conclusion

Kavango offers a unique travel experience that combines outdoor adventure, local culture, and authentic cuisine. Visitors can explore the natural landscapes, spot iconic wildlife, and interact with friendly locals. From river cruises to guided tours, Kavango has something to offer for every type of traveller.
