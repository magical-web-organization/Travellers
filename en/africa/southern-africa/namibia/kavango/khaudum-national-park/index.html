Introduction to Khaudum National Park

Khaudum National Park is a nature lover’s paradise located in Namibia’s Kavango region. Spanning 4,500 square kilometers, it is one of the largest game reserves in the country. This national park is known for its isolated beauty and abundant wildlife, making it a great destination for a safari adventure. The park’s unique location, fauna, and flora make it a hidden gem worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Khaudum National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khaudum Traditional Village: Authentic recreation of a traditional African village, showcasing the Kavango way of life.
  • Kapangolo Lookout: A historical landmark where you can witness the local’s traditional rituals.

Natural Attractions of Khaudum National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khaudum Game Reserve: Home to over 300 species of wildlife, including lions, elephants, cheetahs, and more.
  • Thunge Dam: A breathtaking scene surrounded by trees, a perfect location for camping, and scenic walks.
  • Onankali Pan: A popular location for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khaudum National Park

  • Khaudum Pan Trail: A hiking trail that leads to the heart of the park.
  • Bushman Trail: A cycling trail that passes the park’s excellent game viewing spots.

Best Things to Do in Khaudum National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife tours: An exciting way to get up and close with Khaudum’s diverse wildlife.
  • Camping: The park offers various campsites, each with its attractive scenery and wildlife sightings.
  • Guided bush walks: An excellent way to learn about the Kavango people and the park’s flora and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Khaudum National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ompass: A local fish dish wrapped in banana leaves and cooked over an open fire.
  • Biltong: A dried and cured meat snack known to be a traditional delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Oshikundu: A traditional drink of fermented mahangu (pearl millet), with a tangy taste.
  • Windhoek Lager: A popular Namibian beer.

Where to Stay in Khaudum National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sikereti Campsite: The park’s only campsite, offering basic facilities in the park’s heart.
  • Nkasa Lupala Lodge: A luxury lodge situated outside the park, providing all-inclusive meals and game drives.

What to Buy in Khaudum National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts: Kavango people are well-known for their beadwork, textiles, pottery, and baskets.
  • Ombu Cream: A luxurious skincare product made from wild-growing Ombu berries in the Kavango region.

Local Culture and Customs of Khaudum National Park

The Kavango people live in the region surrounding Khaudum National Park. Their way of life is grounded in traditional agricultural practices and fishing, which they have passed down from generations to generations.

Best Time to Visit Khaudum National Park

The dry season (April to October) is the best time to visit Khaudum National Park, as there is less vegetation, allowing for better wildlife sightings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khaudum National Park

Do:

  • Arrive equipped for camping, as the park has limited facilities.
  • Respect wildlife and the environment.
  • Engage with the locals; they have a wealth of valuable information.

Don’t:

  • Go for bush walks without a guide, who can lead you to safer areas.
  • Feed or disturb wildlife.
  • Use heavy-load vehicles, as the terrain can be challenging.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khaudum National Park

Khaudum National Park is a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should maintain a level of caution and avoid travelling alone, especially at night.

Health in Khaudum National Park

Khaudum National Park lies in a malaria endemic region; travellers are advised to take precautionary measures such as taking anti-malaria medication before arrival and packing mosquito repellents.

Getting to Khaudum National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The best way to get to Khaudum National Park is by car from the nearest airport, which is Rundu Airport. Visitors can either hire a rental car or use shuttle services to the park.

Airports Near Khaudum National Park

  • Rundu Airport

Bus Stations Near Khaudum National Park

There are no major bus stations near Khaudum National Park. Still, there are small local buses, referred to as “combis,” that visitors can use to get to Kavango’s smaller cities.

Other Options

Visitors can choose to fly or book a guided tour that includes ground transportation, accommodation and activities.

How to Get Around in Khaudum National Park

The park’s roads are challenging and recommended for 4x4 vehicles only. Visitors can also hire guides or use the park’s bus services for easier access to the various sites.

Where to Go Next from Khaudum National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bwabwata National Park: A neighbouring park, known for having the highest population density of African wild dogs on the continent.
  • Popa Falls: A waterfall found near the boundaries of Bwabwata national park; it is known for its dramatic beauty.
