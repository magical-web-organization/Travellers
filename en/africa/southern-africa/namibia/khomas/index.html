Introduction to Khomas

Located in the central part of Namibia, Khomas is a region that boasts stunning natural landscapes, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. It is home to the capital city of Windhoek, which serves as the gateway to the entire country.

List of Places to Visit in Khomas

From museums and historical sites to breathtaking mountains and natural parks, Khomas has something to offer to every traveler. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Namibia: This museum showcases the rich history and diverse cultures of Namibia, including exhibits on the country’s natural history, geology, and anthropology.

  • Christ Church: Built in the early 1900s, this historic church boasts beautiful stained-glass windows, intricate designs, and a tranquil atmosphere inside.

  • Independence Memorial Museum: This museum tells the story of Namibia’s fight for independence against South Africa and highlights the country’s accomplishments since achieving independence in 1990.

Natural Attractions of Khomas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Daan Viljoen Game Reserve: Set in the Khomas Hochland Mountains, this nature reserve boasts a variety of wildlife and stunning views of the rugged wilderness.

  • Gross Barmen Hot Springs: This natural hot spring provides visitors with a relaxing and rejuvenating experience amid picturesque surroundings.

  • Avis Dam Nature Reserve: This is a perfect spot for bird-watching and hiking, offering beautiful panoramic views of the city of Windhoek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khomas

Khomas’ mountain ranges and desert landscapes offer excellent hiking and biking opportunities for adventure seekers. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Auas Mountains : These mountains, located south of Windhoek, offer scenic trails of varying difficulty levels.

  • Khomas Hochland Trail: This multi-day hiking trail winds through the mountains and offers the chance to spot wildlife.

  • Jockl’s View: This biking trail offers stunning views of the Namibian savannah, ending at the top of a hill for a panoramic view of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Khomas: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Khomas, whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or culture. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a city tour of Windhoek to learn about the history and culture of Namibia’s capital city.

  • Skydiving over the city of Swakopmund is the perfect adventure for thrill-seekers.

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over Sossusvlei for a breathtaking view of the Namib Desert.

  • Visit a Himba or Herero village and learn about their unique culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Khomas

Day 1: Explore the city of Windhoek and visit the National Museum of Namibia and Independence Memorial Museum.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the Daan Viljoen Game Reserve for a wildlife safari and nature hike.

Day 3: Visit the Christ Church and explore the city’s vibrant art scene.

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride over Sossusvlei and witness the unparalleled beauty of Namib Desert.

Day 5: Hike the scenic trails of the Auas Mountains and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Day 6: Take a tour of a Himba or Herero village and learn about their unique culture and traditions.

Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate at the Gross Barmen Hot Springs before departing Khomas.

Food and Drink Scene in Khomas

Namibian cuisine is influenced by the country’s German and British colonial history, also adding a unique African twist. Here is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages in Khomas:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biltong - Dried cured meat that is served as a snack or side dish.

  • Potjiekos - Stewed meat and vegetables made in a cast-iron pot.

  • Kapana - Local street food consisting of grilled meat and chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Windhoek Lager: A locally brewed beer that is refreshing and crisp.

  • Amarula: A creamy liqueur made from the fruit of the marula tree.

Where to Stay in Khomas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Khomas offers a range of accommodation options for visitors of all budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  • Arebbusch Travel Lodge: A spacious and comfortable lodge located in a peaceful setting with easy access to the city.

  • Urban Camp: A unique camping experience in the heart of the city, featuring luxurious tents and modern amenities.

  • Hilton Windhoek: A luxury hotel located in the city center, offering top-notch amenities and excellent service.

What to Buy in Khomas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Khosmas is home to local artisans and craftspeople, offering a wide array of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts. Here are some recommendations:

  • Namibian jewelry made from semi-precious stones.

  • Hand-woven baskets and mats.

  • Carvings and sculptures made from African hardwood.

Local Culture and Customs of Khomas

Namibia, and Khomas in particular, has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of influences from indigenous tribes and European colonialism. Here are a few things to know about local customs:

  • Greetings are important in Namibia and it is customary to shake hands and ask about each other’s well-being.

  • Tipping is usually expected for good service in restaurants and hotels, usually around 10% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Khomas

The best time to visit Khomas is during the dry season, from April to October, when temperatures are mild and rainfall is at its lowest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khomas

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly and appropriately in public places.

  • Carry cash as not all establishments accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Photograph locals without their permission.

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

  • Wear camouflage clothing as it is reserved for the military.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khomas

Namibia is considered a safe country for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, it is advisable to avoid walking alone at night and to be aware of your surroundings, especially in urban areas.

Health in Khomas

It is recommended to have up-to-date vaccinations for tetanus, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid before visiting Khomas. Malaria is prevalent in some areas of Namibia, and it is recommended to take prophylactic medication and wear mosquito repellent when traveling to those regions.

Getting to Khomas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khomas

The Hosea Kutako International Airport is the main airport serving Khomas and Namibia.

Bus Stations Near Khomas

The Windhoek Central Bus Station is the major hub for buses and taxis within the city.

Other Options

It is also possible to rent a car or hire a private taxi for transportation within Khomas.

How to Get Around in Khomas

The best way to explore Khomas is to rent a car or hire a private taxi. Public transportation is also available, but it can be unreliable and infrequent.

Where to Go Next from Khomas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Namibia is a vast and beautiful country, and Khomas is an excellent starting point for exploring other regions. Here are some destinations worth visiting:

  • Swakopmund: A coastal city known for its German colonial architecture and adventure activities.

  • Sossusvlei: A famous attraction in the Namib Desert with towering red sand dunes and unique fauna.

  • Etosha National Park: A wildlife sanctuary that boasts a large array of animals, including lions, elephants, and rhinos.

Conclusion

Khomas is a beautiful region in Namibia that offers an incredible mix of cultural heritage and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to delve into local traditions, bask in outdoor adventures, or simply relax in beautiful surroundings, Khomas has something for you. Use this guide to plan your journey to this beautiful country and make the most of your trip.
