Are you planning a trip to Southern Africa and looking for the perfect destination to explore? Look no further than Walvis Bay in Namibia, a port town nestled on the Atlantic coastline. Walvis Bay is a charming city with a rich history and natural beauty, offering something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll help you plan your trip by highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, and cultural customs to be aware of.

Introduction to Walvis Bay

Walvis Bay, meaning “Whale Bay” in Afrikaans, is a city on the western coast of Namibia. The town is surrounded by sand dunes and is situated near the Namib Desert, providing a spectacular backdrop for any adventure. Walvis Bay is a hub for international shipping and fishing which makes it a vital commercial area. The city is also known for its colonies of pink flamingos, pelicans, and other wildlife, which can all be seen in the Walvis Bay Lagoon.

List of Places to Visit in Walvis Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you want to explore the cultural and historical aspects of Walvis Bay, there are several places to visit. The Swakopmund Museum is a great place to learn about the colonial history of the area, dating back to the early 1900s. The museum houses a collection of artifacts from ancient Namibia, as well as from the German occupation in the early 20th century. Another notable museum is the Walvis Bay Museum, which highlights the marine life and history of the town.

For those who are interested in visiting historical buildings, the Rhenish Mission Church and the Woermannhaus are the places to go. The Rhenish Mission Church is a historical monument from the 1800s, built by German missionaries. Meanwhile, the Woermannhaus was built as a trading post by the Woermann family in the early 1900s and now serves as a community center.

Natural Attractions of Walvis Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Walvis Bay is home to some of the most breathtaking natural wonders in Namibia. The Walvis Bay Lagoon is a picturesque body of water, and a popular spot for bird-watching, kite-surfing, and windsurfing. Pelican Point, a sandbar at the southern end of the lagoon, offers an opportunity to spot dolphins, seals, and whales.

If you’re looking for more active outdoor pursuits, the Dune 7 sand dune is nearby and is the highest in the Namib Desert. It’s a perfect destination for sandboarding and quad biking. You can also go kayaking or canoeing in the lagoon, or take a scenic flight over the town and desert landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walvis Bay

Walvis Bay has plenty of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The Swakopmund Trail is a 58-kilometer mountain bike trail through the dunes of the desert environment, offering stunning views of the ocean and land. The Vogelstrand Trail is another popular hiking and biking route, offering views of the Atlantic coastline and access to the Desert Golf Course.

Best Things to Do in Walvis Bay: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, there are many things to do in Walvis Bay. A boat tour on the lagoon and harbor is a popular choice, and offers a chance to see dolphins, seals, and pelicans up close. Another popular activity is a 4x4 tour of the sand dunes, providing a memorable off-road experience.

Walvis Bay is also a great place for fishing, with opportunities to catch yellowtail, kabeljou, and steenbras. For those who want to stay on land, a guided tour of the town is highly recommended to learn about Walvis Bay’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Walvis Bay

Here’s our recommended itinerary for a week-long trip to Walvis Bay:

Day 1: Explore the Walvis Bay Lagoon and Pelican Point, and take a boat tour. Day 2: Take a guided tour of the town and visit historical attractions like the Rhenish Mission Church and the Woermannhaus. Day 3: Go sandboarding or quad biking at Dune 7. Day 4: Take a scenic flight over the town and desert landscapes. Day 5: Go kayaking or canoeing on the lagoon. Day 6: Take a 4x4 tour of the sand dunes or visit a nearby game reserve. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Walvis Bay

No trip to Walvis Bay is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Namibian cuisine is influenced by German and South African cooking, and it’s a perfect blend of exotic meats, seafood, and fresh vegetables. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Kapana (grilled beef)
  • Swakopmund oysters
  • Biltong (dried meat)
  • Potjiekos (stew cooked in a pot over an open flame)
  • Braaivleis (barbecued meat)

Local Beverages:

  • Windhoek Lager (beer)
  • Tafel Lager (beer)
  • Mampoer (fruit brandy)
  • Amarula (cream liqueur)
  • Rooibos tea

What to Buy in Walvis Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, Walvis Bay has plenty of options. Local artisans sell items like handmade pottery, beaded jewelry, and textiles. Biltong is a popular snack to bring back home, as well as Amarula.

Local Culture and Customs of Walvis Bay

Namibians are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. It’s customary to greet people before starting a conversation, using “good day” or “good morning.” When invited to someone’s home, it’s respectful to bring a small gift like wine or flowers. Tipping isn’t expected, but it’s appreciated to show gratitude for good service.

Best Time to Visit Walvis Bay

The best time to visit Walvis Bay is during the cooler months of May to October when the temperature ranges from 15-25 degrees Celsius. The winter season is also the peak season for whale and dolphin watching. The summer months, from November to April, can be very hot, with temperatures reaching above 35 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walvis Bay

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting public places or religious sites.
  • Use sunscreen and bring plenty of water when exploring outdoors.
  • Learn some basic Afrikaans phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their consent.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas.
  • Walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Drink tap water without boiling or filtering it first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walvis Bay

Walvis Bay is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, it’s still important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables hidden from view. It’s also important to follow safety guidelines when participating in adventure activities like sandboarding and quad biking.

Health in Walvis Bay

Make sure to take precautions to avoid heat-related illnesses by drinking plenty of water and wearing sunscreen. Consult with a healthcare professional about specific health risks in the area and get any necessary vaccinations before visiting. Mosquito-borne diseases like malaria and dengue fever are not prevalent in Walvis Bay but can be common in other parts of Namibia.

Getting to Walvis Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Walvis Bay

The closest airport to Walvis Bay is the Walvis Bay Airport, which is about 15 minutes away from the city center. You can also fly into Hosea Kutako International Airport, which is about 45 minutes away by plane.

Bus Stations Near Walvis Bay

There are several bus routes that connect Walvis Bay with other cities in Namibia. Big Sky Coaches and Intercape are two of the most popular bus companies operating in the region.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a taxi is a convenient way to get around Walvis Bay and explore the surrounding areas. It’s also possible to take a train from Windhoek to Swakopmund and then travel by bus or taxi to Walvis Bay.

How to Get Around in Walvis Bay

Walvis Bay is a small city with most attractions within walking distance. Taxis are available for hire if needed or renting a car is also an option for those who prefer driving themselves.

Where to Go Next from Walvis Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Namibia after visiting Walvis Bay, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Sossusvlei, known for its towering sand dunes and stunning sunsets.
  • Etosha National Park, famous for its diverse wildlife and vast salt pan.
  • Cape Cross Seal Reserve, home to a large colony of Cape Fur Seals.
  • Swakopmund, another coastal city known for its German heritage and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Walvis Bay is a hidden gem in Namibia, offering a perfect blend of history, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re interested in exploring the sand dunes or trying local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your perfect trip to Walvis Bay, and we can’t wait for you to experience all that this beautiful city has to offer.
