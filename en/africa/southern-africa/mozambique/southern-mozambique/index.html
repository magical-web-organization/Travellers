Introduction to Southern Mozambique

Located in Southern Africa, Mozambique is known for its long stretches of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life. Southern Mozambique, in particular, offers a unique blend of cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures that attract visitors from around the world.

If you’re planning a trip to Southern Mozambique, our comprehensive guide can help you make the most of your visit. Read on for a list of must-visit places, best things to do, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Mozambique

Southern Mozambique has a range of culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and iconic landmarks that are worth exploring. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Nacional de Arte – This museum in Maputo showcases a wide range of contemporary and traditional African art, making it a top attraction for art enthusiasts.
  • Fortaleza de Maputo – This historical fort in Maputo is a great place to soak up Mozambique’s colonial past and enjoy panoramic views of the city.
  • Ilha de Moçambique – A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this island is home to centuries-old architecture that showcases Mozambique’s rich history and unique cultural mix.

Natural Attractions of Southern Mozambique: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bazaruto Archipelago National Park – This national park is known for its diverse marine life, including sea turtles, dolphins, and whale sharks. Visitors can snorkel, dive, and swim in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Inhaca Island – Located just off the coast of Maputo, Inhaca Island is a popular spot for birdwatching, hiking, and beachcombing.
  • Ponta do Ouro Partial Marine Reserve – This reserve is home to a variety of marine species, including dugongs, dolphins, and humpback whales. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling and diving in its clear, warm waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Mozambique

For outdoor enthusiasts, Southern Mozambique offers a range of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to try:

  • Ponta do Ouro Coastal Hike – This 7km hike offers stunning views of the Indian Ocean and includes a dip in a natural tidal pool.
  • Machangulo Peninsula Bike Trail – This 12km trail takes visitors through lush vegetation and past deserted beaches.
  • Maputo Special Reserve – This reserve offers opportunities for guided hikes through its rich wildlife areas.

Best Things to Do in Southern Mozambique: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Southern Mozambique offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Dolphin and Whale Watching – Several companies offer dolphin and whale watching tours in the Bazaruto Archipelago National Park and Ponta do Ouro.
  • Safari Tours – Visitors can enjoy guided safari tours in the Maputo Special Reserve and Machangulo Private Nature Reserve.
  • Fishing – Southern Mozambique is known for its world-class fishing, with opportunities to catch barracuda, kingfish, and tuna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Mozambique

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Southern Mozambique, here’s a sample itinerary to consider:

Day Activity
1 Visit Museu Nacional de Arte and explore Maputo
2 Take a day trip to Inhaca Island for birdwatching and beachcombing
3-4 Enjoy snorkeling and diving in the Bazaruto Archipelago National Park
5 Take a guided safari tour in the Maputo Special Reserve
6 Try your luck at fishing in the Indian Ocean
7 Relax on the beach and soak up Southern Mozambique’s natural beauty

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Mozambique

No trip to Southern Mozambique is complete without tasting the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Piri-Piri Chicken – This spicy grilled chicken is a signature dish in Mozambique and can be found in most restaurants.
  • Matapa – A traditional Mozambican dish made with cassava leaves, coconut, and ground peanuts.
  • Chamussas – Similar to Indian samosas, chamussas are filled with vegetables or meat and fried until crispy.

Local Beverages

  • R&R – A refreshing cocktail made with 2M beer and raspberry syrup.
  • Vinho Verde – A light, crisp white wine that pairs well with seafood.
  • Xai-Xai – A popular Mozambican beer made with sorghum and maize.

Where to Stay in Southern Mozambique: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels, Southern Mozambique offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some top picks:

  • Anantara Bazaruto Island Resort – A luxury resort located on the shores of Bazaruto Island.
  • Polana Serena Hotel – A historic hotel in Maputo that offers a blend of colonial and contemporary charm.
  • Fatima’s Nest – A budget-friendly hostel in Tofo that offers a lively atmosphere and close proximity to the beach.

What to Buy in Southern Mozambique: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a souvenir or unique gift to bring back home? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Capulanas – Traditional Mozambican cloth that comes in a wide range of vibrant colors and patterns.
  • Mozambican Crafts – Handmade crafts, such as woven baskets and wooden carvings, can be found in local markets throughout Southern Mozambique.
  • Cashews – Mozambique is one of the world’s top cashew producers, and visitors can find fresh, high-quality cashews to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Mozambique

Mozambique has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous, Portuguese, and African traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as shaking hands when greeting someone and removing shoes when entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Southern Mozambique

The best time to visit Southern Mozambique is between May and October, during the dry season. Temperatures are cooler, and the water is clear, making it the perfect time for snorkeling and diving.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Mozambique

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious and cultural sites.
  • Carry a hat, sunscreen, and water when going outdoors.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people or religious sites without permission.
  • Swim outside designated areas, as tides can be strong.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Southern Mozambique should take precautions to ensure their safety and health during their trip.

Safety in Southern Mozambique

  • Avoid isolated areas and traveling alone at night.
  • Keep valuables hidden and secure.
  • Be cautious of pickpockets and petty theft.

Health in Southern Mozambique

  • Get vaccinated, especially for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever.
  • Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria.

Getting to Southern Mozambique: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southern Mozambique is easily accessible by air and land, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Southern Mozambique

  • Maputo International Airport – Located in Maputo, this airport offers both domestic and international flights.
  • Vilankulo Airport – Located in Vilankulo, this airport offers flights to several Southern African destinations.

Bus Stations Near Southern Mozambique

  • Johannesburg Park Station – Located in Johannesburg, this bus station offers several routes to Mozambique.
  • Mbabane Bus Station – Located in Mbabane, this station offers routes to Maputo.

Other Options

  • Car Rental – Several car rental companies offer rentals in Maputo and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi Services – Taxis are available in most major cities and tourist areas.

How to Get Around in Southern Mozambique

As a tourist, getting around Southern Mozambique can be a bit challenging, especially for first-timers. However, several options are available, including:

  • Taxis – Taxis are available in most major cities and tourist areas, and prices should be negotiated before the ride.
  • Public Transport – Minibusses and shared taxis are common in Mozambique, and they can be an affordable option for getting around.
  • Car Rental – Several car rental companies offer rentals in Maputo and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Southern Mozambique: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, Southern Mozambique offers several nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are some top picks:

  • Kruger National Park – Located just across the border in South Africa, this world-renowned wildlife reserve is a must-visit for safari enthusiasts.
  • Swaziland – A small, landlocked country in Southern Africa, Swaziland offers a unique blend of culture, adventure, and wildlife.
  • Victoria Falls – Located on the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe, Victoria Falls is one of the world’s largest waterfalls and a top tourist attraction in Southern Africa.

Conclusion

With its beautiful beaches, diverse culture, and range of outdoor adventures, Southern Mozambique is a must-visit destination for travelers to Africa. Use this comprehensive guide to help plan your trip and make the most of your visit.
