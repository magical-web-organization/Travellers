Mokhotlong, located in the Lesotho Highlands of Southern Africa, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With deep cultural roots, breathtaking scenery, and an array of outdoor adventures, Mokhotlong offers travelers a unique and enriching experience. In this Mokhotlong travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and what to expect during your trip.

Introduction to Mokhotlong

Mokhotlong is a small town located in Lesotho, a mountainous country known as the “Kingdom in the Sky”. It is situated near the border of South Africa and therefore accessible by car or bus. The town has a rich cultural history, with a mix of Sesotho and European influences. Visitors can experience traditional Basotho lifestyle, which includes wearing traditional Basotho blankets and hats.

List of Places to Visit in Mokhotlong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mokhotlong has various museums and historical places that visitors can explore. These include:

  • Sehlabathebe National Park
  • The Hlotse River
  • Kings Chapel
  • The Dinosaur Footprints
  • The Moteng Valley

Natural Attractions of Mokhotlong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mokhotlong offers an array of outdoor activities and scenic views for visitors to enjoy. Some of these include:

  • Hiking or Biking the Sani Pass
  • Seeing the Maletsunyane Waterfalls
  • Trekking the Mafika Lisiu Pass
  • Climbing Thabana Ntlenyana (the highest peak in Lesotho)
  • Enjoying a horse-riding adventure

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mokhotlong

Mokhotlong is a great place to go hiking or biking. There are several routes to choose from, including:

  • The Sky Route
  • The Ha Baroana Route
  • The Limestone Route
  • The Valley Route

Best Things to Do in Mokhotlong: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities and tours for visitors to participate in while in Mokhotlong. Some popular options include:

  • Visiting Liphofung Caves
  • Checking out the traditional Basotho blanket and hat-making workshops
  • Trying local traditional beer and food
  • Experiencing the Lesotho traditional dances and music
  • Exploring the Tsehlanyane National Park

Week-Long Itinerary for Mokhotlong

To make the most of your trip to Mokhotlong, we suggest the following itinerary:

Day 1 - Arrival, settling in, and exploring the town Day 2 & 3 - Hiking or Biking Day 4 - Cultural Tours Day 5 & 6 - Horse Riding and National Park Exploration Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene in Mokhotlong

Must-Try Dishes

Lesotho’s cuisine is similar to other African nations, but with a twist. Try some of these dishes when visiting Mokhotlong:

  • Pito - A traditional beer made from fermented maize or sorghum
  • Pap - A porridge-like dish made from maize meal
  • Moroko - Spinach or collard greens cooked with onions and peppers
  • Roast Chicken or Beef - Grilled meat served with pap or rice

Local Beverages

Lesotho has several traditional drinks, including:

  • Pito - A traditional beer made from fermented maize or sorghum
  • Maluti Beer - A locally-brewed beer named after the Maluti Mountains

Where to Stay in Mokhotlong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Mokhotlong, including:

  • Phamatseng Guest House
  • Top Mountain Guest House
  • Maletsunyane Lodge

What to Buy in Mokhotlong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mokhotlong is known for its traditional textiles, including blankets and hats. Other souvenirs to consider include traditional Basotho jewelry or pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Mokhotlong

The Basotho people are a friendly and tightly-knit community, with proud traditions and customs. Visitors to Mokhotlong should respect local culture and dress appropriately. Basic greetings like ‘Dumela’ or ‘Lumela’ (hello in Sesotho) will earn tourists respect, as the local people appreciate efforts to place themselves on a level playing field.

Best Time to Visit Mokhotlong

The best time to visit Mokhotlong is between March and May and from September to November when the weather is mild and dry. However, if visitors are interested in experiencing Lesotho’s traditional cattle grazing festival, the festival is held every year in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mokhotlong

Do:

  • Respect Local Culture and Customs
  • Dress modestly
  • Learn basic Sesotho phrases

Don’t:

  • Engage in Public displays of affection
  • Disrespect the Basotho people, culture, or customs
  • Venture out of town without local guides or knowledge

Safety and Health

Mokhotlong is a relatively safe place for visitors. However, as with any travels, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Mokhotlong

  • Always listen to local guides or travel officials
  • Don’t walk alone at night
  • Avoid a hike or stroll out of town without first-hand knowledge or highly recommended guides

Health in Mokhotlong

Visitors should make sure they have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to Mokhotlong. Visitors should be cautious of drinking tap water and consider purchasing bottled water instead.

Getting to Mokhotlong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travelers can get to Mokhotlong by bus or car. The town isn’t equipped with an airport, but the closest airport is Moshoeshoe I International Airport in Maseru.

Airports Near Mokhotlong

  • Moshoeshoe I International Airport

Bus Stations Near Mokhotlong

  • Butha-Buthe Bus Station
  • Maseru Bus Station

Other Options

Travelers can rent a car to drive to Mokhotlong, or they may opt for a private shuttle service.

How to Get Around in Mokhotlong

Visitors can travel around Mokhotlong by taxi, bus, or private car hire. It is recommended to go with highly-rated shuttle or car hire drivers who are familiar with the terrain and weather conditions.

Where to Go Next from Mokhotlong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors may consider extending their travels to neighboring attractions after visiting Mokhotlong. Some nearby destinations to consider include:

  • Semonkong
  • Malealea
  • Katse Dam

Conclusion

Mokhotlong is a hidden gem that tourists will not want to miss. With its rich cultural history, stunning scenery, unique trips and tranquil environment, it offers a one-of-a-kind African travel experience. Use this travel guide to make the most of your trip to Mokhotlong.
