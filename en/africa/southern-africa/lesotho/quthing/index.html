Introduction to Quthing

Nestled in the southern part of Lesotho, Quthing is a small town that offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of other cities in Africa. It boasts of magnificent landscapes, towering peaks, lively rivers, and expansive green fields, which is why it is often called the “Roof of Africa.”

If you’re looking for an idyllic destination to explore nature and soak in unique local culture and traditions, you’ll love Quthing. This comprehensive guide will introduce you to everything you need to know about visiting Quthing, including the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, local food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Quthing

Basotho people’s culture is an integral part of life in Quthing. The locals highly value their traditions, and it’s essential to respect them when visiting. Some of the local customs include:

  1. Wearing Traditional Clothing
  2. Honoring the Chiefs
  3. Greeting People
  4. Sharing Meal with loved ones

Best Time to Visit Quthing

Quthing can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is from September to April when the weather is dry and sunny, making it easier to explore the outdoor attractions.

Getting to Quthing: Transportation Options and Logistics

To travel to Quthing from the capital, Maseru, Here are different transportation options available.

Airports Near Quthing

The nearest airport is Moshoeshoe I International Airport, which is located in Maseru.

Bus Stations Near Quthing

To get to Quthing, you can take a bus from Maseru to Quthing Terminal.

Other Options

You can rent a car if you want to explore Quthing on your own. However, public transportation is the most common and affordable way to travel.

How to Get Around Quthing

The locals mainly use taxis to get around Quthing, but you can also rent a car or scooter.
