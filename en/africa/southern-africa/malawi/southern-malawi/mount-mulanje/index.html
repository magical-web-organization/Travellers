Introduction to Mount Mulanje

Mount Mulanje is a majestic mountain massif located in Southern Malawi, in the southernmost region of Africa. It is a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts due to its breath-taking scenery, awe-inspiring views and its culturally rich surroundings. The granite massif rises spectacularly from the surrounding plains and forested foothills, reaching a peak height of 3,002 metres above sea level at its highest point, Sapitwa Peak. The mountain and its surrounding forests, rivers, and valleys create a unique micro-environment with a diversity of flora and fauna, making it an ideal destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Mulanje

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mount Mulanje is known not only for its natural beauty but for its rich cultural heritage as well. The mountain and its surrounding areas are home to many museums and historical places that give visitors an insight into Malawi’s history and culture. Some noteworthy attractions include:

  • Mulanje Mission Museum – located at the foot of the mountain, the museum showcases the history of Protestant missions in Malawi and their role in education, health and social reform in Southern Malawi during the colonial era.

  • Likhubula Forest Station – a historical site that was established in the early 1900s to protect and manage the natural resources of the Mulanje Massif. It also served as a centre for scientific research on the mountain’s flora and fauna.

  • Mandala House – located in the city of Blantyre, the house is a unique example of British colonial architecture and it’s now a museum focusing on the history of commerce and trade in Malawi during the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Mount Mulanje: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Mulanje is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts as it offers a plethora of outdoor activities such as hiking, rock climbing, mountain biking, trekking and camping. Some of the most popular natural attractions of Mount Mulanje include:

  • Sapitwa Peak – a challenging hike to the highest point of the mountain that offers spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Likhubula Falls – a beautiful waterfall that is located within the Likhubula Forest Reserve.

  • Dziwe La Nkhalamba – a natural rock pool that is located in the Chisepo Forest Reserve. It is perfect for swimming and relaxing after a long day of hiking.

  • Chambe Peak – a shorter and more accessible climb than Sapitwa peak. It offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is perfect for a day hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Mulanje

Hiking on the Mulanje Massif is a popular activity for tourists, and several well-established trails cover a range of distances and difficulties through the forests and mountain landscapes. The most popular trails are found around the eastern side of the mountain and offer varied experiences.

Four Day Hiking Trail

This is perhaps the most popular trail for visitors to Mulanje. The trail, which is fully equipped with lodges, is known as the Four Day Hiking Trail and can be done either clockwise or anti-clockwise. It offers incredible views of the surrounding landscape and passes through beautiful forests and valleys.

Mountain Biking Trail

A 34-kilometer trail that starts and ends in Likhubula and takes riders through beautiful forests, villages, farmlands and beautiful scenic spots in Mount Mulanje.

Best Things to Do in Mount Mulanje: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, climbing, and mountain biking, other exciting activities to do in Mount Mulanje include:

  • River Rafting on the Mulanje Boma river: This exciting activity offers a thrilling experience through rapids that is safe for all ages.

  • Sunset Cocktail Cruise on Lake Malawi: Take a breathtaking cruise on Lake Malawi and enjoy a sunset cocktail against the backdrop of spectacular mountains.

  • Wildlife Game Drive in Liwonde National Park: Enjoy a game drive in the beautiful Liwonde National Park, which offers a stunning and an excellent vantage point of wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Mulanje

The food and drink scene in Mount Mulanje is a combination of Malawian traditional cuisine and contemporary styles, making it an exciting and unforgettable culinary experience.

Must-Try Dishes

African cuisine comprises of a rich combination of spices, herbs and flavours, and Malawian cuisine is not an exception:

  • Chambo: a popular freshwater fish that is commonly grilled, baked or fried.

  • Nsima: considered the country’s staple food, it’s a dough-like substance made from maize flour and hot water.

  • Mandasi: a fried doughnut-like snack that is traditionally eaten for breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Malawi Gin: locally brewed gin that has a unique fruity taste.

  • Malawi Coffee: organic coffee grown in the country, it is considered one of the best in the world and is available in various products.

Where to Stay in Mount Mulanje: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mount Mulanje offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses:

  • Likhubula Forest Lodge: Located in Likhubula Forestry Reserve, the lodge offers nice, clean and well-maintained accommodation.

  • The Mushroom Farm: A popular choice for backpackers, offering camping or private rooms, an on-site restaurant with a big menu and other activities.

  • Kara O’Mula Country Lodge: A boutique hotel known for its beautiful surroundings, it offers a luxurious and comfortable stay with a unique African style décor.

What to Buy in Mount Mulanje: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Going back home with a few unique and authentic souvenirs is one of the most memorable parts of any trip. Visitors to Mount Mulanje can shop for locally made crafts, jewellery, and artifacts such as:

  • Hand-woven baskets and mats made by Malawian women.

  • Wooden art pieces and sculptures, such as figures of animals and tribal masks carved by local artists.

  • Malawi Gin and Coffee, as mentioned above, these beverages are locally grown and brewed, making them an appealing item to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Mulanje

Malawi is a culturally rich country, with a unique blend of African traditions and colonial influence. Visitors to Mount Mulanje are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Greeting locals: A friendly greeting of “Muli bwanji” (How are you?) is customary in Malawi, and visitors are often met warmly when using this greeting.

  • Dress Code: It is considered impolite to wear revealing clothes in Malawi, visitors should dress modestly.

  • Photography: Always ask for permission before taking photos, some places and people may not allow their photos to be taken.

Best Time to Visit Mount Mulanje

The best time to visit Mount Mulanje is from May to September. During these months, the weather is dry and sunny, making it easier to hike and explore the mountain. The temperature during this period ranges between 22-28°C, which is pleasant and comfortable. Visiting during the off-peak season, October to April, should be avoided due to heavy rainfall and flash floods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Mulanje

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Carry appropriate hiking gear and equipment.

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated throughout the day.

Don’t:

  • Litter, the environment should be respected and preserved by properly disposing of waste.

  • Disturb local wildlife, visitors are encouraged not to interfere or interfere with any animals they may come across while hiking.

  • Explore the mountain alone or without a guide, visitors are advised to use local guides when exploring the mountain trails.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Mount Mulanje are advised to take the following health and safety precautions:

Safety in Mount Mulanje

  • Always hike with a guide or in a group.

  • Do not venture off-trail as it is easy to get disorientated and lost.

  • Take care when swimming and avoid dangerous undertows in rivers and waterfalls.

Health in Mount Mulanje

  • Visitors are advised to get up-to-date vaccinations before travel.

  • Drink bottled or filtered water only.

  • Use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites, which can carry diseases such as malaria.

Getting to Mount Mulanje: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mount Mulanje is easily accessible by air and road, and there are several transport options available to travelers:

Airports Near Mount Mulanje

  • Chileka International Airport: located in Blantyre, is the closest airport to Mount Mulanje, at about 45 kilometers away.

  • Lilongwe International Airport: located in the capital city of Lilongwe, is about 350 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Mount Mulanje

  • Blantyre Bus Station: located in the city of Blantyre, offers bus services to Mulanje town.

  • Limbe Bus Station: located in the suburb of Limbe, offers bus services to Mulanje town.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: A private taxi or hired car is a great way to travel around Mount Mulanje area.

  • Mini-Bus: A cheap mode of transport, but can be uncomfortable and overcrowded at times.

How to Get Around in Mount Mulanje

The most convenient and reliable mode of transport around Mount Mulanje is via a hired vehicle, car or a taxi. Visitors can also use public taxis, but these can be uncomfortable and overcrowded.
