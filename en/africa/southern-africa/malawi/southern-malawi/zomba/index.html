Zomba, a city in southern Malawi, offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your next trip to Zomba, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Zomba

Zomba is a city in southern Malawi and is the former capital of the country. With a population of over 80,000 people, it is one of the largest cities in Malawi. The city is located in the Shire Highlands and is surrounded by hills and forests, making it a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Zomba

Zomba has a variety of attractions to visit, from culturally important museums to scenic outdoor locations.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zomba Plateau: Offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside, this plateau is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Zomba.
  • Chancellor College: This college is part of the University of Malawi and is located on the Zomba Plateau. It is home to the Museum of Malawi, which displays collections of culture, history, and natural history.
  • Zomba Central Prison: A historical site with an impressive architecture style. Planned in the early 20th century, its design is inspired by the Eastern State Penitentiary in the USA.
  • Zomba Market: A vibrant market that is full of life, color, and diversity. It is an excellent place to purchase souvenirs and local products.

Natural Attractions of Zomba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zomba Plateau Forest Reserve: A popular hiking and picnic destination with several trails.
  • Williams Falls: A stunning waterfall located on the Zomba Plateau with a drop of over 30 meters.
  • Brachystegia Woodland: A woodland area on the slopes of the Zomba Plateau.
  • Lake Chilwa: A large lake in south-eastern Malawi near the border of Mozambique. It is home to over 200 species of birds and is a popular fishing destination for locals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zomba

Zomba is a haven for hiking enthusiasts, with a variety of trails suitable for beginners and experienced hikers alike. Some popular hiking and biking trails which may suit one’s preferences include:

  • Plateau Trail: A 5-kilometer trail on top of the Zomba Plateau, with stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Williams Falls Trail: A 3-kilometer trail that leads to the stunning Williams Falls on the Zomba Plateau.
  • Brachystegia Trail: A 5-kilometer trail on the slopes of the Zomba Plateau through Brachystegia woodland.

Best Things to Do in Zomba: Activities and Tours

  • Hiking and Biking: Discover the natural wonders of Zomba on one of the many hiking and biking trails.
  • Chancellor College Museum Tour: Explore the Museum of Malawi and learn about the culture, history, and natural history of the country.
  • Market Tour: Visit Zomba Market and immerse yourself in the vibrant atmosphere of the market.
  • Lake Chilwa Fishing Tour: Experience local fishing methods on Lake Chilwa and catch your own dinner.

Week-Long Itinerary of Zomba

Day 1: Arrive in Zomba and visit the Museum of Malawi at Chancellor College. Day 2: Go hiking on the Brachystegia Trail. Day 3: Take a tour of Zomba Market. Day 4: Visit the stunning Williams Falls on the Zomba Plateau. Day 5: Go on a fishing tour on Lake Chilwa. Day 6: Explore the city of Zomba and visit the historical Zomba Central Prison. Day 7: Conclude the trip with a picnic on the Zomba Plateau.

Food and Drink Scene of Zomba

Zomba has a variety of local dishes and beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nshima: A maize-based dish that is a staple of Malawian cuisine.
  • Chambo: A fish commonly found in Lake Malawi and Lake Chilwa, usually served grilled or fried.
  • Nsima with Peanut Sauce: A version of nshima served with a rich peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Malawi Gin: A gin made from sugar cane, produced by the Malawi Distilleries Limited company.
  • Malawi Coffee: A rich and flavorful coffee grown in the northern regions of Malawi.

Where to Stay in Zomba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zomba offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some popular choices include:

  • Ku Chawe Inn: A luxury hotel located on the Zomba Plateau with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Zomba Forest Lodge: A lodge located in a forested area of the Zomba Plateau, offering a peaceful and tranquil environment.
  • Zomba Community Center: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Zomba, with comfortable and clean rooms.
  • Hostel Samuel: A guesthouse located close to Chancellor College, offering affordable accommodation for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Zomba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woodcarvings: Handcrafted wooden carvings of animals and other objects are a popular souvenir in Zomba.
  • Malawi Gin: A bottle of Malawi Gin is a great souvenir to bring home.
  • Textiles: Zomba is known for its traditional textile crafts, with brightly colored fabrics and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs in Zomba

The people of Zomba are friendly and welcoming to visitors. However, it is important to respect the local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress Modestly: The people of Zomba dress conservatively, and visitors should do the same.
  • Ask for Permission: Before taking photographs of individuals or religious sites, it is polite to ask for permission.
  • Respect Religion: Malawi is predominantly Christian, and visitors should show respect for religious customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Zomba

The best time to visit Zomba is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The temperatures during this time are cooler, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. The wet season, from November to April, can be hot and humid, with frequent rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zomba

Do:

  • Stay Hydrated: The weather in Zomba can be hot and humid, so it is important to stay hydrated.
  • Take a Guide: Some of the hiking trails can be challenging, so it is recommended to take a guide.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Malawian cuisine is unique and flavorful, so be sure to try some of the local dishes during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Leave Trail: It is important to stay on the established trails when hiking on the Zomba Plateau to minimize the impact on the environment.
  • Litter: Visitors should not litter on the hiking trails or other natural areas in Zomba.
  • Ignore Safety Warnings: Visitors should keep an eye out for safety warnings or signs while hiking, and should not attempt trails that are beyond their skill level.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zomba

Zomba is a safe destination for visitors, but it is recommended to take precautions such as:

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: It is recommended to walk with others or take transportation at night.
  • Secure Valuables: Visitors should secure their valuables and avoid carrying large sums of cash.
  • Follow Local Customs: Visitors should respect local customs and traditions to avoid offending the local population.

Health in Zomba

Visitors to Zomba should take the following precautions:

  • Get Immunized: It is recommended to get immunized before visiting Zomba to prevent common diseases such as malaria and yellow fever.
  • Drink Bottled Water: Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and instead opt for bottled water.
  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: The climate in Zomba can be hot and humid, so it is important to wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Zomba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zomba

The nearest airport to Zomba is Chileka International Airport (BLZ) in Blantyre, which is approximately 22 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Zomba

Zomba has a variety of bus stations, including:

  • Zomba Bus Station: Located in the city center and offers connections to cities across Malawi.
  • Zomba Minibus Station: Located near Chancellor College and offers connections to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for transportation within and around Zomba.

How to Get Around in Zomba

The best way to get around in Zomba is by taxi or rental car. Visitors should negotiate the price beforehand and ensure that the driver is licensed and insured. Some hotels also offer shuttle services for their guests.

Nearby Destinations and Excursions from Zomba

Zomba is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Blantyre: The second-largest city in Malawi, home to museums, markets, and an international airport.
  • Lake Malawi: A large lake in Malawi that is home to several beach resorts and offers a variety of water activities.
  • Mulanje Mountain: A mountain in southern Malawi that offers hiking trails and stunning views.
