Introduction to Northern Malawi

Northern Malawi is a picturesque region blessed with impressive landscapes, rich culture, and hospitable people. The area lies in the northern part of Malawi, nestled between Zambia and Tanzania, and offers visitors a one-of-a-kind experience. From magnificent national parks to vibrant markets, Northern Malawi has something to please every traveler. In this guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, where to stay, what to eat, and the best way to get around in Northern Malawi.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Malawi

For a memorable excursion in Northern Malawi, be sure to put these attractions on your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Livingstonia Mission: Established by Scottish missionaries in the late 1800s, Livingstonia Mission provides a glimpse into the colonial history of Malawi. The mission has an impressive church, an old printing press, and a heritage center with exhibitions on the history of the mission.
  • Karonga Museum: This museum is dedicated to displaying the fossils of the early humans that were found in the region. The museum is a must-visit for history buffs and those interested in prehistoric anthropology.
  • Chongoni Rock Art Area: This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts over 100 sites featuring rock paintings that date back over 2000 years. The artwork provides insights into the culture and lifestyle of the indigenous people that once lived in the area.

Natural Attractions of Northern Malawi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Malawi: The third-largest lake in Africa is one of the jewels of Northern Malawi. The lake is known for its crystal-clear waters, beautiful beaches, and the diverse array of fish species within it. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, snorkeling, and swimming in the lake.
  • Nyika National Park: The park is known for its rolling hills, expansive grasslands, and diverse wildlife. Wandering through the Nyika National Park, visitors can spot leopards, antelopes, and zebras, among others.
  • Vwaza Marsh Wildlife Reserve: Covering over 1000 square kilometers, this reserve is home to over 200 bird species and wild animals such as elephants, lions, and buffalos. Visitors can enjoy an unforgettable safari experience in the reserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Malawi

Northern Malawi boasts incredible terrain ideal for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Chelinda Camp Hike: This 2km hike offers spectacular views of the highlands of Nyika National Park, and visitors may have the chance to spot eland and zebra.
  • Mulanje Massif: This mountain range has various hiking trails, including the famous path to Sapitwa peak, which is the highest peak in Malawi. The climb takes at least two days, and visitors can enjoy the sweeping views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Liwonde National Park: The park has two mountain bike trails that give visitors a different perspective of the wildlife that roams in the reserve. The trails are graded based on difficulty and are accessible to both novice and experienced bikers.

Best Things to Do in Northern Malawi: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and kayaking along Lake Malawi: The lake’s clear waters make it an excellent spot for snorkeling and kayaking, allowing visitors to get up close with colorful fish species and unique rock formations.
  • Masasa Beach Day Trip: This beach in Senga Bay is the perfect day trip for visitors seeking a beach holiday while in Northern Malawi. Guests can relax, swim, and take in the beautiful scenery of the lake.
  • Chinteche Craft Market: This market has various stalls selling authentic Malawian handcrafted items, including baskets, jewelry, and woodcarvings.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Malawi

The food and drink scene in Northern Malawi is diverse, with traditional dishes and drinks offered alongside international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nkhukuyapezi: This dish is primarily served at large traditional gatherings by the Tumbuka tribe. It consists of ground cassava leaves mixed with peanuts, tomatoes, and groundnut powder, and is cooked over a fire.
  • Kondowole: This dish comprises of maize meal and beans cooked in a leafy green sauce. It’s a staple for most Malawians.
  • Chambo Fish: This tilapia fish is indigenous to Lake Malawi and is often grilled or fried and served with Nsima (a maize staple).

Local Beverages

  • Masese: A flavorful and potent drink made from maize from which the alcohol is distilled.
  • Thobwa: This non-alcoholic drink is made from maize and is typically flavored with pineapple juice, cassava, or bananas.

Where to Stay in Northern Malawi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Malawi has numerous accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best:

  • The Makuzi Beach Lodge: Located along the shores of Lake Malawi, Makuzi Beach Lodge offers guests a relaxed and comfortable stay with its striking beach views and contemporary rooms.
  • Chelinda Camp: This luxury camp, situated within the Nyika National Park, offers an exclusive experience with comfortable tents that provide a unique blend of luxury and wilderness stay.
  • Luwawa Forest Lodge: This lodge is located in Viphya Mountains and is ideal for nature lovers. The lodge offers a serene ambiance and guest can enjoy wildlife sightings such as sycamore figs and samango monkeys.

What to Buy in Northern Malawi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Northern Malawi, visitors can purchase a variety of souvenirs to take back home as memories. Here’s what to look for:

  • Traditional Malawian Clothing: Visitors can buy traditional dresses, shirts, and skirts made with distinct batik patterns.
  • Baskets and Craft Products: Northern Malawi is known for its basketry, with intricate baskets, mats, and other crafts available in the markets.
  • Nkhalakalo: Visitors can purchase a variety of hand-carved wooden products such as masks, figurines, and other artisanal items.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Malawi

The traditional culture of Northern Malawi is diverse, with various tribes practicing different customs and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions, including dress codes and appropriate behavior.

Best Time to Visit Northern Malawi

The best time to visit Northern Malawi is from May to October, which is the dry season. The weather during this period is mild, with temperatures ranging between 18°C to 28°C. The dry season offers visitors better opportunities to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, wildlife safaris, and beach outings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Malawi

Visitors should adhere to the following guidelines to ensure a pleasant and safe stay in Northern Malawi.

Do:

  • Dress modestly and politely when visiting villages or holy sites.
  • Try to learn a few words in Chichewa (local language).
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local culture or traditions.
  • Buy souvenirs made from endangered animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Malawi

Northern Malawi is relatively safe, and violent crimes against tourists are rare. However, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling late at night.

Health in Northern Malawi

Visitors should ensure that their vaccines are up to date and take the necessary anti-malaria medication before traveling to Northern Malawi. They should also prioritize safe drinking water and avoid consuming raw foods or fruits.

Getting to Northern Malawi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Northern Malawi by air, road, or water.

Airports Near Northern Malawi

  • Mzuzu Airport: Located in Mzuzu City, Mzuzu Airport is Malawi’s second-largest airport and offers direct flights to Lilongwe.
  • Chileka International Airport: The main international airport in Malawi, Chileka International Airport, is approximately four hours away from Northern Malawi.

### Bus Stations Near Northern Malawi

  • Mzuzu Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Mzuzu, and visitors can take buses to destinations such as Lilongwe and Blantyre.
  • Lilongwe Bus Station: Visitors can take buses from Lilongwe Bus Station to several destinations in Northern Malawi, including Mzuzu and Nkhata Bay.

Other Options

  • Water Transportation: Visitors can access Northern Malawi via ferry from Monkey Bay to Likoma Island and even to the nearby countries of Tanzania and Mozambique.
  • Car Rental: Tourists can rent cars in Lilongwe or Mzuzu and explore the region at their pace.

How to Get Around in Northern Malawi

Public transportation in Northern Malawi typically includes minibusses, shared taxis, and larger coaches. Visitors can also hire cars, motorbikes, or bicycles.

Where to Go Next from Northern Malawi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northern Malawi is well connected to other nearby destinations, which visitors can explore after their stay in the region.

  • Liwonde National Park: Located in Southern Malawi, the park boasts diverse wildlife and is ideal for visitors seeking a safari experience.
  • Zomba Plateau: Visitors can hike to the top of this plateau to enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the Ozark Mountains.
  • Mulanje Massif: Visitors can continue their hiking adventure by climbing the Mulanje Massif.

Conclusion

Northern Malawi offers visitors a unique and unforgettable African experience, from its stunning landscapes and natural wonders to its rich culture and hospitable people. By following our guide, visitors can make the most of their stay in Northern Malawi and create memories to last a lifetime.
