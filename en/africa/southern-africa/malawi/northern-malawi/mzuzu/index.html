Located in Northern Malawi, Mzuzu is a vibrant city that offers a unique blend of culture, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Whether you’re looking to explore historical landmarks, hike through the countryside, learn about the local customs, or simply relax and enjoy the local food and drinks, Mzuzu has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and tips for safety and health in Mzuzu, Malawi.

Introduction to Mzuzu

Mzuzu is one of the largest cities in Malawi, with a population of over 200,000 people. It’s an important economic and commercial center, with a bustling market, shops, restaurants, and a variety of businesses. The city is surrounded by beautiful hills, and it’s known for its pleasant climate and friendly people. Mzuzu is a great destination for culture, adventure, and relaxation, and it’s an ideal gateway to the northern region of Malawi.

List of Places to Visit in Mzuzu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the history and culture of Malawi, Mzuzu has several museums and historical landmarks that are worth visiting. These include:

  • Mzuzu Cultural Museum: This museum celebrates the culture and traditions of the Northern Region of Malawi through exhibits, workshops, and events. It’s a great place to learn about the history, music, dance, and art of the local communities.

  • Livingstonia Mission: This Christian mission was established by Scottish missionaries in the late 19th century and is located high on a hill overlooking Lake Malawi. It’s a beautiful and historic place that played a crucial role in the spread of Christianity and education in the region.

  • Mzuzu University: Founded in 1997, this university is one of the best in Malawi and is known for its academic excellence and commitment to community development. Visitors can tour the campus and attend events and lectures.

Natural Attractions of Mzuzu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mzuzu is surrounded by beautiful hills, forests, and rural communities that offer a variety of outdoor activities and scenic views. These include:

  • Viphya Forest Reserve: This forest reserve covers over 50,000 hectares and is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, antelopes, and hyenas. Visitors can go hiking, bird watching, camping, and picnicking in the forest.

  • Luwawa Forest Reserve: This forest reserve is located on the eastern escarpment of the Viphya Plateau and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Visitors can go hiking, camping, and fishing in the forest.

  • Nkhata Bay: This scenic bay is located on the shore of Lake Malawi and is a popular destination for swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking. Visitors can also enjoy fresh fish and local drinks at the many restaurants and bars along the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mzuzu

Mzuzu and its surroundings have a network of well-maintained and scenic hiking and biking trails that offer great opportunities for outdoor adventure. Some popular routes include:

  • Livingstone Trail: This trail follows the route used by Dr. David Livingstone during his exploration of the region in the 19th century. It’s a challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

  • Viphya Plateau Trail: This trail takes hikers through the Viphya Forest Reserve, with its beautiful scenery, waterfalls, and wildlife. It’s a moderate hike that can be completed in a day.

  • Mzuzu City Trail: This trail takes visitors on a tour of the city’s landmarks and attractions, including the market, the university, the museum, and the scenic viewpoints. It’s an easy walk that can be completed in a few hours.

Best Things to Do in Mzuzu: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and sightseeing, there are several other activities and tours available in Mzuzu, including:

  • Cultural dances and music shows: Visitors can attend traditional dances and music performances at the cultural center or local restaurants.

  • Visit rural communities: Visitors can get a glimpse of rural life by visiting traditional villages and homesteads and interacting with the locals.

  • Shopping: Mzuzu has a variety of shops and markets that offer a range of goods, including clothes, souvenirs, and local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mzuzu

For visitors who want to spend more time in Mzuzu, here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay:

Day 1: Arrival and city tour. Day 2: Visit Mzuzu Cultural Museum, Livingstonia Mission, and Mzuzu University. Day 3: Hike Livingstone Trail and explore the Viphya Forest Reserve. Day 4: Visit Nkhata Bay, swim, and enjoy local drinks and fresh fish. Day 5: Bike around Mzuzu and visit rural communities. Day 6: Visit Luwawa Forest Reserve, go hiking and camping. Day 7: Shopping and departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Mzuzu

Mzuzu has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects the city’s cultural diversity and local products. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nsima: This is Malawi’s staple food, made from maize flour and typically served with a variety of stews, vegetables, and meats.

  • Chambo: This is a type of fish that’s endemic to Lake Malawi and is commonly served grilled or fried.

  • Mandasi: This is a type of deep-fried bread that’s often eaten as a snack or breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Malawi Gin and Tonic: This is a popular local drink that combines gin, tonic water, and a slice of lime.

  • Masese: This is a locally brewed beer that’s made from maize or sorghum and is often served in traditional clay pots.

Where to Stay in Mzuzu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mzuzu has a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular ones include:

  • Sunbird Mzuzu: This is a luxury hotel that offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, a restaurant, and other amenities.

  • Joy’s Place Lodge: This is a mid-range lodge that offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.

  • Mzuzu Backpackers: This is a budget hostel that offers dorms and private rooms, a shared kitchen, and a garden.

What to Buy in Mzuzu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors who want to bring home a piece of Mzuzu, there are several souvenirs and local products that are worth buying, including:

  • Wood carvings and sculptures: Mzuzu is known for its traditional wood carvings and sculptures that depict local animals and people.

  • Textiles: There are several shops and markets that sell traditional Malawian fabrics, including chitenjes and khangas.

  • Coffee: Malawi produces some of the best coffee in Africa, and visitors can buy freshly roasted beans at the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Mzuzu

Mzuzu has a rich and diverse culture that’s shaped by its history, geography, and local traditions. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the local customs and beliefs, which include:

  • Greetings: It’s customary to greet people with a handshake or a wave, and to ask about their health and family before starting a conversation.

  • Dress code: Malawians are generally conservative dressers, and visitors are advised to cover their shoulders and knees in public places.

  • Religion: Malawi is a predominantly Christian country, with a significant Muslim minority. Visitors are advised to respect the local religious practices and institutions.

Best Time to Visit Mzuzu

Mzuzu has a subtropical climate with two distinct seasons: the dry season (May to October) and the rainy season (November to April). The best time to visit is during the dry season, when the weather is mild and sunny, and there’s less chance of rain. However, visitors should be prepared for cold nights, especially in the mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mzuzu

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mzuzu:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Chichewa phrases, such as “Moni” (hello), “Zikomo” (thank you), and “Mulibwanji” (how are you).

  • Carry cash, as many places don’t accept credit cards or mobile payments.

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting religious sites or rural communities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos without asking for permission, especially of people and religious sites.

  • Don’t litter or destroy the environment, especially when hiking or camping.

  • Don’t travel alone at night, especially in unfamiliar places.

Safety and Health

Mzuzu is generally a safe and friendly place, but visitors should take some precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Mzuzu

  • Avoid carrying valuables or wearing flashy jewelry in public places.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dark or deserted areas.

Health in Mzuzu

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water and avoid ice or raw vegetables and fruits.

  • Carry mosquito repellent and use it regularly, especially at night.

  • Get vaccinated against common diseases such as hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever.

Getting to Mzuzu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mzuzu is easily accessible by several means of transport, including:

Airports Near Mzuzu

  • Mzuzu International Airport: This is the main airport in the city, with daily flights to and from Lilongwe and Blantyre.

Bus Stations Near Mzuzu

  • Mzuzu Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the city, with daily connections to different parts of Malawi and neighboring countries.

Other Options

  • Private cars: Visitors can hire private cars or taxis to take them to Mzuzu from Lilongwe or Blantyre.

How to Get Around in Mzuzu

Mzuzu has several means of transport that visitors can use to get around the city, including:

  • Taxis: There are several taxi companies in Mzuzu that offer reliable and affordable services.

  • Matatus: These are minivans that serve as public transport and run along several routes in the city.

  • Bicycles: Visitors can rent bicycles from several shops and guesthouses in Mzuzu and explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Mzuzu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mzuzu is a great base from which to explore other parts of the Northern Region of Malawi, including:

  • Nyika National Park: This park is located on the Nyika Plateau and offers great opportunities for wildlife watching, hiking, and camping.

  • Likoma Island: This island is located on Lake Malawi and is known for its beautiful beaches, traditional architecture, and friendly people.

  • Nkhata Bay: This scenic town is located on the eastern shore of Lake Malawi and is a popular destination for swimming, snorkeling, and fishing.

Conclusion

Mzuzu is a hidden gem in Northern Malawi, with a unique blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, food, or people, Mzuzu has something for everyone.
