Introduction to Livingstone

Livingstone is a city located in the southern region of Zambia, in the Zambezi Basin. This culturally-rich city was named after the famous explorer Dr. David Livingstone and is known as the gateway to the Victoria Falls - one of the natural wonders of the world. With a variety of things to see and do, Livingstone provides a memorable travel experience and an insight into African culture and wildlife.

List of Places to Visit in Livingstone

When visiting Livingstone, there are several places that shouldn’t be missed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Livingstone Museum is the oldest and largest museum in Zambia that showcases the natural and cultural history of the country. It includes displays of traditional Zambian artifacts, colonial-era exhibits, and Dr. David Livingstone’s belongings. Another historic attraction is the Railway Museum, which exhibits old steam engines and historical railway cars.

Natural Attractions of Livingstone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Livingstone is a paradise for nature lovers, which offers a variety of outdoor adventures and scenic views. Victoria Falls, with its stunning scenic views, is undoubtedly the main attraction of Livingstone. Visitors can view the falls from various vantage points, including the Knife-Edge Bridge or the Lookout Tree. Other outdoor activities include game drives in Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park, bungee jumping, whitewater rafting, and helicopter rides over the falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Livingstone

Livingstone has several beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer visitors breathtaking panoramas. One of the most popular routes is the Livingstone Mountain Trail, which takes hikers to the top of Livingstone Mountain, where one can see stunning views of Victoria Falls and the city of Livingstone below.

Best Things to Do in Livingstone: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours that visitors can enjoy while in Livingstone. Some popular activities include sunset boat cruises, walking safaris, horseback safaris, and cultural tours. There’s also a chance to meet the locals, learn their traditions, and gain insight into the Zambian culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Livingstone

For those spending a week in Livingstone, here’s a suggested itinerary to see everything the city has to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Victoria Falls Tour, including the Knife-Edge Bridge and Lookout Tree
Day 2 Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park Game Drive
Day 3 Whitewater Rafting on the Zambezi River
Day 4 Livingstone Mountain Trail and Hike to the Top
Day 5 Horseback Safari through Zambezi Riverbank
Day 6 Culture Tour of the Village, including traditional Zambian crafts
Day 7 Rest and Relaxation; catch a sunrise or sunset on the Zambezi River

Food and Drink Scene of Livingstone

If you’re a food lover, Livingstone has a lot to offer. Here are some traditional dishes and drinks you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

Nshima is Zambia’s national dish and is served with varying accompaniments such as vegetables, meats, or relishes. Another must-try is Biltong, a spiced and dried meat snack that has a unique flavor. If you’re feeling adventurous, try caterpillars, a popular Zambian delicacy.

Local Beverages

One of the most popular local drinks is Munkoyo, a fermented drink made from the roots of the African baobab tree. Chibuku is another local drink made from fermented maize and is found widely in Zambia.

Where to Stay in Livingstone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Livingstone has a variety of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some notable places include the Avani Victoria Falls Resort, The Royal Livingstone Hotel, and the Zambezi Sun Hotel.

What to Buy in Livingstone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking for souvenirs and local products, Livingstone has a lot to offer. Some popular items include traditional Zambian crafts such as hand-woven baskets, pottery, and wood carvings. Local textiles such as chitenge or kitenge, a colorful fabric used for wrap-around skirts, are also popular.

Local Culture and Customs of Livingstone

When visiting Livingstone, it’s important to be respectful of the local culture. Greetings are important, and visitors should follow the lead of the locals in how to greet the people they meet. Dress modestly and avoid wearing revealing clothing, especially when visiting a village or a traditional ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Livingstone

The best time to visit Livingstone is during the dry season between May and October when the Victoria Falls are at their peak flow. This is also the best time for outdoor activities such as game drives and river cruises.

Do’s and Don’ts of Livingstone

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals
  • Wear sturdy shoes for outdoor adventures
  • Carry sunscreen and plenty of water

Don’t:

  • Swim in the Zambezi River, as there is a high risk of crocodiles and hippos
  • Feed the wildlife and monkeys in the Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park

Safety and Health

Safety in Livingstone

Livingstone is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should be cautious when walking alone or at night. Don’t carry too much cash or valuable items with you, and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Livingstone

Be sure to have updated vaccinations and take anti-malaria medication. Also, carry mosquito repellent and protect yourself from insect bites.

Getting to Livingstone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Livingstone

The Harry Mwanga Nkumbula International Airport is the main airport for Livingstone and has regular flights from Johannesburg and Lusaka.

Bus Stations Near Livingstone

The Livingstone Inter-City Bus Terminus connects Livingstone to other cities in Zambia and neighboring countries such as Zimbabwe and Namibia.

Other Options

Taxis are readily available in Livingstone, and there are many car rental companies one can rent a car from as well.

How to Get Around in Livingstone

Taxis are the most popular mode of transportation in Livingstone, and rates are affordable. Car hire is also available. It’s advisable to negotiate the fare before commencing your journey.

Where to Go Next from Livingstone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations near Livingstone, including the Kazungula border town, where one can take a ferry across the Zambezi River to Botswana or Namibia. Chobe National Park is another nearby destination that offers game drives and river safaris.

Conclusion

Livingstone is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a unique immersion in African culture and wildlife. With its cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and exotic cuisine, Livingstone is sure to provide a memorable experience. Whether you want to hike to the top of Livingstone Mountain or bungee jump from the Victoria Falls Bridge, Livingstone has something for everyone.
