Introduction to Kapiri Mposhi

Kapiri Mposhi is a small town located in the central part of Zambia, and it serves as a transportation hub for most of the country’s northern and central provinces. Most tourists use Kapiri Mposhi as a transit point to other popular destinations, but the town itself is a hidden gem that is worth exploring. The town is named after the Kapiri tree, which is native to the area, and Mposhi means ‘large pool’ in the local Bemba language.

List of Places to Visit in Kapiri Mposhi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kapiri Mposhi Museum

The Kapiri Mposhi Museum is a small museum that houses a collection of cultural artifacts, photographs, and documents that tell the story of the town’s history and its significance as a transportation hub. The museum is open from Monday to Friday from 08:00 to 16:30 hours, and the entrance fee is affordable.

Kabwe Skull Site

Kabwe Skull Site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 60 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi. The site is significant because it is one of the world’s largest deposits of fossils of ancient humans, and it provides an insight into human evolution. The entrance fee is reasonable, and visitors are provided with a guide to take them around the site.

Natural Attractions of Kapiri Mposhi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chimanuka Game Management Area

Chimanuka Game Management Area is a wildlife-filled expanse that is located about 50 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi. The park is home to a variety of wild animals, including elephants, lions, leopards, hippos, and crocodiles. The park also offers a variety of activities, including game drives and boat safaris.

Kapishya Hot Springs

Kapishya Hot Springs is located about 90 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi, and it is a popular destination for relaxation and rejuvenation. The hot springs are situated in a beautiful valley surrounded by hills and forests, and they offer a serene environment for bathing and unwinding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapiri Mposhi

Kapiri Mposhi is surrounded by a beautiful landscape of hills, valleys, and forests that are ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside, and visitors can organize guided tours with local guides or rent bikes from local shops.

Best Things to Do in Kapiri Mposhi: Activities and Tours

Railway Museum

The Railway Museum is located in Kabwe, which is about 60 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi, but it is worth the trip. The museum houses a collection of historic locomotives, carriages, and other railway memorabilia, and it is an excellent place to learn about the history of railways in Zambia.

Kundalila Falls

Kundalila Falls is a beautiful waterfall located about 80 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi. The falls can be accessed via a short hike through the forest, and the views from the top are stunning. Visitors can also swim in the pool at the base of the falls.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapiri Mposhi

Must-Try Dishes

Nshima and relish

Nshima is a staple food in Zambia, and it is made from pounded maize served with a vegetable stew called relish. The dish is hearty, filling, and flavorful. Visitors can sample Nshima and relish at local restaurants and street vendors.

Kapenta

Kapenta is a small, dried fish that is a popular snack in Zambia. The fish is crispy and salty and is usually served with Nshima.

Local Beverages

Chibuku

Chibuku is a traditional beer that is brewed from maize or sorghum. The beer is opaque, sour, and has a low alcohol content. Chibuku is sold in large containers and is usually shared among a group of people.

Shake-Shake

Shake-Shake is a popular soft drink in Zambia that is made from fermented maize. The drink is sweet, tangy, and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Kapiri Mposhi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kapiri Mposhi has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Kapiri Mposhi include The Lotus Guest House, Kapiri Sunset Guest House, and Kapiri Executive Lodge.

What to Buy in Kapiri Mposhi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Kapiri Mposhi can buy a variety of local products and souvenirs, including handcrafted wooden carvings, baskets, and textiles. Most of these products can be found at local markets and street vendors, and visitors are advised to bargain for the best prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Kapiri Mposhi

The primary language spoken in Kapiri Mposhi is Bemba, but English is also widely spoken, especially in tourist areas. Zambia is a conservative country, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kapiri Mposhi

The best time to visit Kapiri Mposhi is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather during this period is sunny and warm, and wildlife viewing is excellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kapiri Mposhi

Kapiri Mposhi is a relatively safe town, but visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables safe. It is also recommended to use licensed tour operators for activities such as hiking and wildlife safaris.

Health in Kapiri Mposhi

Visitors to Kapiri Mposhi should take standard health precautions, such as drinking bottled water, using insect repellent, and avoiding raw or undercooked food. Travelers should also ensure they have up to date vaccinations for diseases such as yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid fever.

Getting to Kapiri Mposhi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kapiri Mposhi

The nearest airport to Kapiri Mposhi is Kenneth Kaunda International Airport in Lusaka, which is about 170 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight, hire a car or use public transportation to get to Kapiri Mposhi.

Bus Stations Near Kapiri Mposhi

Kapiri Mposhi has a bus station that is serviced by several long-distance bus companies. Visitors can take a bus from popular destinations such as Lusaka, Livingstone, and Ndola.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or arrange for a taxi to take them to Kapiri Mposhi from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Kapiri Mposhi

The best way to get around Kapiri Mposhi is by hiring a car or using a taxi. Visitors can also explore the town on foot or rent a bike from local shops.

Where to Go Next from Kapiri Mposhi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Livingstone

Livingstone is a popular destination located about 500 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi. The town is famous for the Victoria Falls, which is one of the seven natural wonders of the world. Visitors to Livingstone can also take part in various adventurous activities such as bungee jumping, white water rafting, and helicopter tours.

Lusaka

Lusaka is the capital city of Zambia, and it is located about 170 kilometers from Kapiri Mposhi. The city is a center of commerce and administration, and it offers plenty of cultural, historical, and natural attractions for visitors to explore.
