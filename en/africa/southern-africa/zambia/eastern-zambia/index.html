Introduction to Eastern Zambia

Eastern Zambia, part of the larger Zambia, is a landlocked country located in Southern Africa. It is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. The region is well-known for its wide range of wildlife, warm and welcoming people, natural wonders, and vibrant cities. Whether you are an adventurer, a history buff, or a nature lover, there is something in Eastern Zambia that will fascinate you.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Zambia

From historical landmarks to breathtaking natural attractions, Eastern Zambia is abundant with magnificent sites to explore. Here are the top places to visit during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Munda Wanga Environmental Park: A wildlife sanctuary located in Lusaka, the capital city of Zambia. The park is home to over 49 species of mammals and 250 bird species.
  • Bushman Art Gallery: The gallery is located in the town of Mfuwe and showcases a diverse collection of the art of the local tribes of Eastern Zambia.
  • Chimfunshi Wildlife Orphanage: A sanctuary for chimpanzees located in Northwestern Zambia. It was established in 1983 by a Dutch woman who rescued a group of chimpanzees from a smuggler.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Zambia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South Luangwa National Park: One of the top national parks in Africa with abundant wildlife and scenic landscapes. It is home to over 60 species of mammals, 400 bird species and numerous reptiles and insects.
  • Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park: Located in Southern Zambia, this park is known for the magnificent Victoria Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in the world.
  • Nyika Plateau National Park: Located in the northern part of Eastern Zambia, this park is a hiker’s paradise, with beautiful landscapes and a wide variety of bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Zambia

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a beginner, Eastern Zambia offers numerous opportunities for outdoor adventure. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Mafinga Hills Forest Reserve: A series of forest covered hills located near Mafinga town, providing excellent photo opportunities for visitors.
  • Victoria Falls Trail: Hike along the Zambezi River and appreciate the breathtaking views of Victoria Falls.
  • North Luangwa Walking Safaris: Take an adventurous walking safari in the North Luangwa National Park and be amazed by the diverse landscape of the park.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Zambia: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do in the Eastern Zambia region:

  • Bush Camping: Enjoy a night under the stars in the heart of the wild in one of the many campsites that are available in the national parks.
  • White Water Rafting: Take an exhilarating ride downstream in the rapids of the Zambezi River.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy a unique adventure and get an amazing aerial view of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Zambia

Here is a sample itinerary to make the most out of your week-long visit to Eastern Zambia:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Munda Wanga Environmental Park
Day 2 Explore the Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park, home of the Victoria Falls
Day 3 Go on a walking safari in the South Luangwa National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Zambia

Eastern Zambia is known for its diverse and flavorful cuisine, influenced by local and international cultures. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nshima: A staple food made of maize flour, usually served with relish.
  • Kapenta: A type of dried/smoked fish that is usually eaten with Nshima.
  • Chicken and Peanut Stew: A traditional dish made from chicken, peanuts, and local spices.

Local Beverages

  • Chibuku Shake Shake: A popular traditional beer made from maize or sorghum.
  • Munkoyo: A refreshing drink made from fermented maize or cassava.

Where to Stay in Eastern Zambia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastern Zambia offers different accommodation options for tourists, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here is a list of some of the top-rated places to stay:

  • Mfuwe Lodge: A luxurious lodge located on the doorstep of the South Luangwa National Park.
  • Tribal Textiles Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located near the town of Mfuwe.
  • Chaminuka Lodge: A beautiful lodge in Lusaka surrounded by stunning gardens and wildlife.

What to Buy in Eastern Zambia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eastern Zambia has a wide variety of unique handicrafts that are perfect souvenirs to bring home. Here are some of the best products to buy:

  • Textiles: Ethnic textiles are woven by locals that depict the traditional stories and designs of Zambia.
  • Woodcarvings: Wooden figurines and sculptures carved by local craftsmen.
  • Pottery: Unique and colorful pottery made by traditional Zambian potters.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Zambia

Eastern Zambia has a rich and fascinating cultural heritage. Here are some cultural customs and traditions to look out for:

  • Greeting: Handshakes are the most common form of salutation in Eastern Zambia. However, in rural areas, people will often bow their heads to show respect.
  • Music and Dance: Traditional music and dance are an essential part of Zambian culture. The drums are the most prized instrument.
  • Attire: Zambian clothing is often bright and colorful, with traditional patterns and designs.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Zambia

The best time to visit Eastern Zambia is from May to October, which is the dry season. The weather is cooler during this time, and the animals in the national parks can be easily spotted as they gather around waterholes. The wet season, which is from November to April, is also a great time to visit if you’re interested in bird watching, but the weather can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Zambia

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately and modestly when visiting villages and religious sites.
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of locals and their property.

Don’t:

  • Give money or gifts to children, as it encourages begging.
  • Use your left hand for eating or touching someone else as it is considered impolite.
  • Disrespect the wildlife by getting too close to them or interfering with their natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Zambia

Like any other destination, travelers should be cautious and take necessary precautions, especially when exploring the national parks. Use common sense and follow safety measures such as not walking alone in secluded areas and avoiding public transportation at night.

Health in Eastern Zambia

Before traveling, it is advisable to receive vaccinations and malaria prophylaxis, as malaria is prevalent in Zambia. It’s also essential to take necessary measures, such as wearing protective clothing, using insect repellent, and staying in screened-in accommodations to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Eastern Zambia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Zambia

  • Lusaka International Airport: Travelers can fly in from several locations around the world and will land 28 km from Lusaka.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Zambia

  • Lusaka City Bus Terminus: This is the main bus station in Lusaka and provides transportation to various destinations in Eastern Zambia.

Other Options

  • Travelers can also hire private taxis or helicopters to commute to different parts of Eastern Zambia.

How to Get Around in Eastern Zambia

Within towns and cities, taxis and local buses are readily available to navigate around Eastern Zambia. For longer distances, bus services and private car hires are recommended.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Zambia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations to continue your African adventure:

  • Zimbabwe: Home of the magnificent Victoria Falls and a great destination for wildlife tours.
  • Malawi: Visit the beautiful Lake Malawi and explore the local cultural heritage.
  • Tanzania: Travel to the world-famous Serengeti National Park and witness the largest migration of wildebeest and zebra in the world.

Conclusion

Eastern Zambia is an African gem with much to offer any traveler. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply looking for a unique adventure, Eastern Zambia is the perfect destination for your next vacation!
