A Comprehensive Guide to Kasama: Exploring the Wonders of Northern Plateau, Zambia

Situated in the Northern Plateau of Zambia, Kasama is a charming destination for anyone seeking to experience natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure. The city is renowned for its scenic landscape of rolling hills and awe-inspiring waterfalls. In this guide, we will explore the wonders of Kasama and offer a detailed itinerary to make the most of your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Kasama

Kasama offers an array of culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, and exciting activities to explore during your stay. Here’s our list of must-visit places in Kasama.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kasama Museum: Explore the historical artifacts and cultural objects at the Kasama Museum. It’s a great place to learn about the Bemba tribe’s language, traditions, and way of life.
  2. Kasama Crafts Village: If you’re a fan of arts and crafts, don’t miss out on the Kasama Crafts Village. You can find handmade items such as jewelry, baskets, and pottery produced by local artisans.
  3. Chambeshi Monument: The Chambeshi monument commemorates the spot where the first shots of World War I in Africa were fired back in 1914.

Natural Attractions of Kasama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kundabwika Falls: Kundabwika Falls is a breathtaking waterfall located 35 km away from Kasama. Visitors can hike through the forest and enjoy stunning views along the way.
  2. Chishimba Falls: Located 33 km north of Kasama, Chishimba Falls is a popular tourist destination for its forest walks, birdwatching, and scenic waterfalls.
  3. Nsalu Caves and Waterfalls: Nsalu Caves and Waterfalls offer a splendid view of water cascading through rocks and boulders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kasama

Kasama is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The city’s scenic landscape and rugged terrain offer endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. Here are some popular hiking and biking trails in Kasama.

  1. Kasanka Hiking Trail: Kasanka National Park has several hiking trails, including the Chimfunshi trail, which is a 65km-long trail through pristine wilderness. It takes about three days to complete.
  2. Kundabwika Falls Hiking Trail: You can enjoy a two-hour trek through the Kasama forest to reach the Kundabwika Falls.
  3. Kasama Cycling Route: You can rent a bike and take the Kasama cycling route, which is a 50km loop that takes you through the countryside and offers stunning views of the Lingwa Escarpment.

Best Things to Do in Kasama: Activities and Tours

Kasama offers a wide range of exciting activities, making it an ideal destination for adventure seekers. Here are some must-try activities and tours in Kasama.

  1. Game Drives at Kasanka National Park: Kasanka National Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. You can take a game drive to spot the famous Bat Migration and other wildlife.
  2. Bird watching in Lunga River: The Lunga River is a bird watcher’s paradise, and visitors can hire a guide to experience the best birding.
  3. Bemba Cultural Experience: The Bemba tribe has a unique culture and way of life. Visitors can learn about their traditions, music and dances and even participate in traditional ceremonies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kasama

Here is our suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Kasama:

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene of Kasama

No trip to Kasama is complete without experiencing the local food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nshima: Nshima is a staple food in Zambia that is made from maize flour and served with vegetables, beans or meat.
  2. Kapenta: Kapenta is a type of fish that’s popular in Zambia. It’s usually served fried or in a relish.
  3. Chikanda: Chikanda is a Zambian food made from the roots of a wild orchid. It’s a vegetarian dish that’s served with a tomato relish.

Local Beverages

  1. Munkoyo: Munkoyo is a traditional Zambian beverage made from maize meal and water. It’s often sweetened with sugar or honey.
  2. Chibwantu: Chibwantu is a traditional beer made from maize and sorghum.

Where to Stay in Kasama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kasama offers a range of accommodation options that suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some options to consider.

  1. Protea Hotel: Protea Hotel is located in the heart of Kasama and offers comfortable accommodation with a restaurant, bar, and swimming pool.
  2. Kasama Lodge: Kasama Lodge offers affordable accommodation with modern amenities and comfortable rooms.
  3. Lukupa Lodge: Lukupa Lodge is situated in a tranquil environment beside a mountain with comfortable and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Kasama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kasama crafts village is a great place to shop for souvenirs and local crafts. You can buy handcrafted jewelry, baskets, pottery, and other unique items produced by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Kasama

The people of Kasama are mostly Bemba and have a unique culture that may be different from other parts of Zambia. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect cultural traditions and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Kasama

The best time to visit Kasama is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is pleasant during this period, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities and tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kasama

While visiting Kasama, visitors should keep in mind the following Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry and drink bottled water to avoid water-borne diseases
  • Take precautions against malaria, especially if visiting rural areas

Don’t:

  • Overstay your visa
  • Take photographs of military installations or personnel without permission
  • Purchase goods made from endangered species, like ivory, rhino horns, or pangolin scales

Safety and Health

Kasama is generally safe, but visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Kasama

  • Avoid walking alone at night and in dimly lit areas
  • Keep valuables, including money and passports, in a secure place
  • Be cautious when using public transportation, especially at night

Health in Kasama

  • Visitors are advised to take malaria prophylaxis before traveling to Kasama
  • Seek medical attention immediately in the event of illness or injury

Getting to Kasama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kasama

Taxis from Simon Mwansa Kapwepwe International Airport in Ndola to Kasama can be arranged with the hotel or booked independently.

Bus Stations Near Kasama

Kasama bus station offers transport to other towns in Zambia, such as Lusaka, Ndola, Kitwe, and Mpika. Motorbikes are very popular for short distance transport in Kasama.

Other Options

Car hires can be arranged through travel agencies or a hotel booking.

How to Get Around in Kasama

Taxis, mini-buses, and motorbikes (known as Boda bodas) are the primary modes of transportation in Kasama. Taxis and boda bodas are the most popular form of transportation for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Kasama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you decide to venture further and explore other nearby destinations, here are some suggested places to visit:

  1. Mweru Wantipa National Park: Located 140km Northeast of Kasama, Mweru Wantipa National Park is famous for its awe-inspiring birdlife and herds of lechwe and impala.
  2. Shiwa Ng’andu: Shiwa Ng’andu is a manor house located in the north of Zambia that offers a glimpse into Zambia’s colonial past.
  3. Bangweulu Wetlands: The Bangweulu Wetlands are an impressive wildlife sanctuary located 300km Northeast of Kasama.

Conclusion

Kasama is a hidden-gem destination in Zambia that boasts stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. This comprehensive guide can help you plan a memorable trip to Kasama, explore its most scenic sites, enjoy its delicious cuisine, and experience its unique culture and traditions. Happy travels!
