Introduction to Chingola

Chingola is a charming town located in the heart of Copperbelt, Zambia’s copper mining region. With its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty, Chingola offers an unforgettable experience to visitors from around the world. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, the town has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay and eat, local culture and customs, and how to get to and around Chingola.

List of Places to Visit in Chingola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chimfunshi Wildlife Orphanage: Founded by David and Sheila Siddle, the Chimfunshi Wildlife Orphanage is a sanctuary for injured and orphaned animals, including chimpanzees. Visitors can take guided tours of the sanctuary and learn about the life and behavior of these primates.
  • Nchanga Open Pit Mine: The largest open cast mine in the world, Nchanga Open Pit Mine is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in the history and economics of Copperbelt. Visitors can take guided tours of the mine and witness the operations up close.
  • Chingola Museum: The Chingola Museum is a treasure trove of local history and culture with exhibits spanning from pre-colonial times to the present day. The museum is known for its collection of traditional indigenous art and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Chingola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nchanga South National Park: Situated just outside Chingola, the Nchanga South National Park is home to various animal species, including kudu, buffalo, and warthog. Visitors can take guided safari tours of the park, go birdwatching or explore the walking trails.
  • Kafue River: One of Zambia’s longest rivers, the Kafue River offers an array of water activities such as fishing, canoeing, river rafting, and boat tours. Visitors can catch a glimpse of the abundant wildlife along the river, such as hippos, crocodiles, and various bird species.
  • Lake Kashiba: Located near Chingola, Lake Kashiba is a stunning freshwater lake that is surrounded by beautiful mountains and lush vegetation. Visitors can enjoy activities such as fishing and boating, or simply relax and take in the serene atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chingola

  • Chingola Nature Trail: The Chingola Nature Trail is a 13km trail that is ideal for hiking and mountain biking. The trail winds through the beautiful Chikola Hills, offering breathtaking views of the Copperbelt region.
  • Kasompe Forest Reserve: The Kasompe Forest Reserve is a protected forest area that is teeming with wildlife and offers an array of hiking and biking routes. Visitors can explore the dense woodlands, streams, and hills while spotting numerous bird species and other wildlife.
  • Nchanga North Forest Reserve: The Nchanga North Forest Reserve is a wetland area that offers scenic walking paths, boardwalks, and bird-watching opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Chingola: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Local Copper Mine: Copper mining is the backbone of the Copperbelt economy, and visitors can take a guided tour of a copper mine to learn about the mining process and its impact on the local economy.
  • Experience Local Culture: Chingola has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can engage in cultural activities such as attending traditional dances, visiting local markets, and trying out regional cuisine.
  • Go on a Safari: With its abundance of wildlife, Chingola offers numerous options for safari tours. Visitors can experience the thrill of seeing big cats, elephants, giraffes, and many other animals up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Chingola

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nshima: Zambia’s national dish, Nshima is a cornmeal-based dish that is usually served with vegetables or meat.
  • Mandasi: Mandasi is a deep-fried doughnut-like pastry that is a popular breakfast food in Chingola.
  • Chikanda: Chikanda is a type of dumpling made from ground nuts, cassava, and spices. It is a popular snack food in Zambia.

Local Beverages

  • Munkoyo: Munkoyo is a fermented drink made from maize flour, sorghum or cassava. It is a refreshing and healthy alternative to soda.
  • Tujilijili: Tujilijili is a locally brewed alcoholic beverage that is made from sugar, water and yeast.

Where to Stay in Chingola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Protea Hotel: A popular hotel in Chingola, the Protea Hotel is situated near all major attractions and offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining facilities.
  • Chingola Guesthouse: The Chingola Guesthouse is a budget-friendly option for travelers. It offers clean rooms and basic amenities.
  • Mokorro Game Ranch and Lodge: Located just outside Chingola, Mokorro Game Ranch and Lodge is a luxurious accommodation option that offers exceptional views of the surrounding wildlife and nature.

What to Buy in Chingola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Copper products: Chingola is known for its copper mining industry, and visitors can purchase an array of copper products, including jewelry and artwork.
  • Traditional textiles: Zambia is renowned for its beautiful textiles, including chitenge and kapenta. Visitors can purchase unique and colorful pieces of fabric to take home as souvenirs or gifts.
  • Mukuni beads: Mukuni beads are a traditional craft of the Tonga people and have been featured in museums and galleries around the world. Visitors can purchase these locally made beads as a unique and beautiful reminder of their trip to Chingola.

Local Culture and Customs of Chingola

  • Greeting: It is customary to greet people with a handshake or a nod of the head.
  • Dress code: Zambia has a conservative culture, and visitors should dress modestly, especially when visiting cultural or religious attractions.
  • Respect for elders: Elders are highly respected in Zambian culture, and visitors should show appropriate deference and respect to older people.

Best Time to Visit Chingola

The best time to visit Chingola is during the cooler, drier months of May to October. However, visitors can enjoy the town’s attractions year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chingola

Do:

  • Respect local cultures and customs: Always show respect to local traditions and customs.
  • Stay hydrated: Zambia is a hot country, and visitors should drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Practice good hygiene: To prevent illness, visitors should practice good hygiene, such as washing their hands frequently and avoiding uncooked food.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect wildlife: Visitors should not harm or disturb the local wildlife in any way.
  • Act inappropriately: Visitors should not engage in any behavior that is considered disrespectful, aggressive or inappropriate.
  • Waste water: Water is a precious resource, and visitors should not waste it needlessly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chingola

Chingola is a fairly safe town, but visitors should be vigilant and take precautions to prevent petty crimes such as pickpocketing or theft. Visitors should also be cautious when traveling outside the town at night.

Health in Chingola

Visitors should take precautions to prevent malaria, as it is prevalent in the region. They should also be up to date on routine vaccinations and consult their doctor before traveling to Chingola.

Getting to Chingola: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Chingola: The closest airport to Chingola is Simon Mwansa Kapwepwe International Airport in Ndola, which is located about 60km away. Visitors can get to Chingola via taxi or car rental.
  • Bus Stations Near Chingola: Visitors can travel to Chingola by bus from major cities in Zambia, such as Lusaka, Kitwe, and Ndola.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also choose to hire a private car or take a taxi to Chingola from other destinations in the region.

How to Get Around in Chingola

Visitors can get around Chingola easily via taxi or car rental. Bicycles are also available for rent for those who prefer a more active mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Chingola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kafue National Park: Located a few hours’ drive from Chingola, the Kafue National Park is Zambia’s largest national park and offers an abundance of wildlife and safari trails.
  • Victoria Falls: One of the world’s most iconic natural wonders, Victoria Falls is located about 500km from Chingola and can be reached via car or bus.
  • South Luangwa National Park: One of Zambia’s most popular national parks, South Luangwa is located about 800km from Chingola and offers an array of wildlife and safari opportunities.

Conclusion

Chingola is a vibrant, culturally rich, and naturally stunning town that offers an unforgettable experience to visitors from around the world. From its bustling markets and traditional dances to its lush forests and abundant wildlife, Chingola has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip to this beautiful town in Zambia.
