Ndola Travel Guide: Your Ultimate Tourist Handbook

Ndola, the third-largest city in Zambia, is a bustling metropolis in the Copperbelt Province that serves as a gateway to the region’s mining industry. In addition to its commercial significance, Ndola is home to an array of cultural and natural attractions that make it a popular tourist destination. This guide will help you discover Ndola’s best offerings, from its renowned museums and historical sites to its breathtaking scenery and thrilling outdoor adventures.

Introduction to Ndola

Ndola is situated in the northern part of Zambia, in the Copperbelt Province, and is part of the African Great Lakes region. The city has a rich and diverse culture that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Ndola is a vibrant city that is growing fast and is becoming an important economic center in the country.

List of Places to Visit in Ndola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Copperbelt Museum: This museum showcases the history and significance of the Copperbelt region’s mining industry. It features exhibits on mining equipment, labor history, and the region’s social and economic development.

  2. Dag Hammarskjöld Memorial: This monument is dedicated to Dag Hammarskjöld, the former Secretary-General of the United Nations. He died in a plane crash near Ndola in 1961 while on a peace mission to Congo. You can visit the monument to pay tribute to one of the greatest peacekeepers in history.

  3. Mokambo Cultural Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the traditional culture of the Kaonde and Lunda people, who are indigenous to the Copperbelt Province. Visitors can see exhibitions of local art, music, and dance.

Natural Attractions of Ndola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ndola Golf Club: This 18-hole golf course is set in serene surroundings and boasts of a challenging course. It is a great place to relax, unwind, and enjoy the beauty of the city.

  2. Kafubu River: This river flows through Ndola and is a popular spot for fishing, canoeing, and picnicking. Visitors can take a walk along the riverbanks and admire the stunning scenery.

  3. Ndola Lime Nature Reserve: This reserve is located on the outskirts of Ndola and is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can see a variety of birds, baboons, and other animals that roam freely in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ndola

  1. Mukuyu Slave Tree Trail: This trail takes you through a scenic route that showcases Ndola’s rich history. Along the way, you’ll see the famous Mukuyu Slave Tree, which is believed to have been used as a hiding place by slaves who were being transported through the area.

  2. Ntumbachushi Falls: This hiking trail takes you to Ntumbachushi Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in Zambia. The trail is around 5 kilometers in length, but the stunning views of the waterfall make it worth the effort.

  3. Bwana Mkubwa Trail: This is a great biking route that takes you through the Bwana Mkubwa Copper Mine. The trail is around 10 kilometers long and is suitable for riders of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Ndola: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit Copperbelt Museum: Learn about the rich history of the Copperbelt region and its significance to Zambia’s economy.

  2. Take a tour of Dag Hammarskjöld Memorial: Pay your respects to the former Secretary-General of the United Nations and one of the greatest peacekeepers in history.

  3. Go wildlife spotting at Ndola Lime Nature Reserve: Get a chance to see baboons and various bird species that inhabit the reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ndola

Day 1: Visit Copperbelt Museum Day 2: Take a walking tour of the city Day 3: Explore Mokambo Cultural Museum Day 4: Go fishing at Kafubu River Day 5: Do some hiking at Ntumbachushi Falls Day 6: Play golf at Ndola Golf Club Day 7: Go on a safari trip to nearby safari reserves

Food and Drink Scene of Ndola

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nshima: This is the staple food of Zambia and is made from maize flour. It is usually served with a relish of meat, fish, or vegetables.

  2. Kapenta: This is a small fish that is usually eaten either dried or fried. It is a popular delicacy in Zambia, and Ndola is no exception.

  3. Ifisashi: This is a vegetable stew made with pumpkin leaves, peanuts, and tomatoes. It is usually served with Nshima.

Local Beverages

  1. Mosi: This is a popular locally brewed beer that is brewed from maize and sorghum. It is available in most bars in Ndola.

  2. Maheu: This is a non-alcoholic drink made from maize meal, sugar, and water. It is a refreshing drink that is available in most food markets.

Where to Stay in Ndola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Protea Hotel Ndola: This is a luxurious hotel that is located in the heart of Ndola. It offers world-class amenities and is perfect for both business and leisure travelers.

  2. Mukwa Lodge: This is a budget-friendly lodge that offers clean and comfortable accommodation. It is located near Ndola Airport and is perfect for a short stay.

  3. Chimwemwe Lodge: This is a guesthouse that offers a homely feel and is perfect for long-term stays. It is located in a quiet and peaceful neighborhood in Ndola.

What to Buy in Ndola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Copper crafts: Copper is an important export product of Zambia, and Ndola has a variety of locally-made copper crafts that would make excellent souvenirs.

  2. Traditional artwork: Ndola is home to many talented artists, and their work is on display in local art markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ndola

Ndola is a culturally diverse city that is home to a variety of ethnic groups that have their own traditions and customs. It is important to dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural sites and respect local customs.

Best Time to Visit Ndola

The best time to visit Ndola is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is pleasant and mild, and there is very little rainfall. However, it is worth noting that Ndola has a relatively stable climate, with temperatures averaging between 20 and 25 degrees Celsius throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ndola

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress modestly.
  3. Learn a few phrases in the local language.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of people without their consent.
  2. Disrespect the local culture.
  3. Carry large amounts of cash around.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ndola

Ndola is generally safe for tourists, but it is still important to take precautions. You should avoid walking alone at night and stay in well-lit areas. It is also recommended that you carry a copy of your passport and keep valuable items in a safe place.

Health in Ndola

It is important to take precautions when it comes to your health in Ndola. You should drink bottled water and avoid consuming food and drink from street vendors. You should also make sure to get vaccinated before your trip and carry a first aid kit with you at all times.

Getting to Ndola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ndola

The main airport serving Ndola is Simon Mwansa Kapwepwe International Airport, which is located around 5 kilometers from the city center. It offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Ndola

Ndola has a central bus station located in the city center, which offers various bus services to different parts of the country.

Other Options

One can also hire a private car or taxi to explore the city or take a tour of nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Ndola

Ndola is a relatively small, compact city, and most of its attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Taxis and buses are also available to access other parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Ndola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Livingstone: This town is located approximately 500 kilometers from Ndola and is home to the famous Victoria Falls, one of the top tourist attractions in Zambia.

  2. Lusaka: Zambia’s capital city is only a few hours’ drive from Ndola and is a hub of culture and commerce.

Conclusion

Ndola is a city that is full of surprises, whether it’s breathtaking scenery, fascinating history, or vibrant culture. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Ndola effectively, and experience everything this charming city has to offer. By following this guide and taking precautions, you’ll be able to enjoy a pleasant and memorable trip to Ndola.
