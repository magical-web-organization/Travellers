Introduction to Copperbelt

Copperbelt is a province in Zambia located in the northern part of the country. Known as the country’s economic hub, the region is named after the rich deposits of copper that can be found in the area. Apart from being a commercial center, Copperbelt is also home to a rich culture and history, beautiful scenery, and adventurous activities that attract tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Copperbelt

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to culturally significant places in Copperbelt, there are a few that should definitely be on your list. The Copperbelt Museum showcases the region’s rich mining history, while the Chisokone Market is a vibrant bazaar where you can buy anything from clothing to souvenirs. You can also explore the Kabwata Cultural Village, which provides a glimpse into the life of the traditional Zambian people. Another must-visit attraction is the Chingola Open Pit Mine, one of the largest copper mines in the world.

Natural Attractions of Copperbelt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Copperbelt is a paradise for nature lovers, with several natural attractions that offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. The Chimfunshi Wildlife Orphanage is home to several species of monkeys, chimpanzees, and other rescued animals. You can also visit the Kafue River, which offers stunning scenery and is a perfect spot for fishing and boat cruising. Additionally, the Nsongezi Wildlife Reserve boasts great wildlife sightings and beautiful views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Copperbelt

Copperbelt is home to several hiking and biking trails, which offer a unique way to explore the region’s natural beauty. The Mwekera Trail is one of the most popular trails, featuring a scenic route through the forest and the opportunity to spot local wildlife. You can also opt to hike or bike the Kafue River Bridge Loop, a 10-kilometer trail that crosses the Kafue River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Copperbelt: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring museums and natural attractions, Copperbelt also offers plenty of thrilling activities for visitors. You can take a hot-air balloon ride, go quad biking, or even try your hand at skydiving. In addition, several tour providers offer cultural tours of the area, which provide insight into the local culture and customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Copperbelt

If you’re planning to spend a week in Copperbelt, here’s an itinerary that covers some of the must-visit places in the region:

  • Day 1: Visit the Copperbelt Museum, the Chisokone Market, and the Kabwata Cultural Village.
  • Day 2: Go hiking or biking on the Mwekera Trail, followed by a visit to the Chimfunshi Wildlife Orphanage.
  • Day 3: Explore the Chingola Open Pit Mine and the Kafue River Bridge Loop.
  • Day 4: Take a hot-air balloon ride over Copperbelt or go quad biking.
  • Day 5: Visit the Nsongezi Wildlife Reserve and go on a boat cruise on the Kafue River.
  • Day 6: Take a cultural tour of the region and visit the local villages.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the local spas or restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Copperbelt

Must-Try Dishes

Copperbelt’s food scene is a mix of traditional Zambian cuisine, Indian and British influences. Some of the must-try dishes include nshima, a type of cornmeal porridge served with relish, and bream fish, a local fish that’s often grilled or fried. Other popular dishes include boerewors, a type of sausage, and chikanda, a Zambian delicacy made from groundnuts.

Local Beverages

Zambia is known for its wide range of local beverages, including mosi beer, a locally brewed beer that’s popular among locals and visitors alike. If you prefer non-alcoholic drinks, try a maheu, a traditional Zambian drink made from fermented maize.

Where to Stay in Copperbelt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Copperbelt offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the popular luxury hotels in the area include the Protea Hotel and Radisson Blu, while budget travelers can opt for backpacker hostels or guesthouses, such as the Fawlty Towers Lodge and Moba Hotel.

What to Buy in Copperbelt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some unique souvenirs, Copperbelt has you covered. Some of the popular items include traditional handicrafts, such as baskets and pottery, as well as copper and brass ornaments. You can also purchase traditional clothing, such as chitenge fabric and African prints.

Local Culture and Customs of Copperbelt

Copperbelt is home to a diverse population, with over 70 different ethnic groups residing in the region. The Bemba people are the largest ethnic group and their language is widely spoken in the area. When it comes to customs, Zambians are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Copperbelt

The best time to visit Copperbelt is during the dry season, which runs from May to August. During this time, the weather is pleasant and there’s little to no rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Copperbelt

Do:

  • Greet people with a handshake and a smile.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting rural areas or religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Flush toilet paper down the toilet, as the plumbing system may not be able to handle it.
  • Touch people without permission, as this may be considered rude or offensive.
  • Leave your valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Copperbelt

Copperbelt is relatively safe for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions. Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas and keep your valuables hidden or locked away. Additionally, be wary of scams or pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Copperbelt

Before visiting Copperbelt, it’s recommended to get vaccinated against yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid. It’s also important to take precautions against mosquito bites, as malaria is prevalent in the region.

Getting to Copperbelt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Copperbelt

The main airport in Copperbelt is Simon Mwansa Kapwepwe International Airport, which serves several domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Copperbelt

There are several bus stations in Copperbelt, including the Ndola Bus Station, Kitwe Bus Station, and Chingola Bus Station, which provide connections to various destinations in Zambia.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can also hire a car and drive to Copperbelt, though it’s recommended to have an experienced driver as the roads can be difficult to navigate.

How to Get Around in Copperbelt

The most convenient way to get around Copperbelt is by car or taxi. Several car rental companies operate in the area, or you can opt to take a taxi or minibus, which are widely available.

Where to Go Next from Copperbelt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Zambia, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering. You can visit the majestic Victoria Falls, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, or travel to the Lower Zambezi National Park, which offers great wildlife sightings and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Copperbelt is a hidden gem in Zambia, offering a mix of adventure, culture, and stunning scenery. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now equipped to plan your trip to Copperbelt and make the most of your visit.
