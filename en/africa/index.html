Introduction to Africa

Africa is the second most populated continent in the world and is known for its diverse cultures, wildlife, and natural beauty. It’s a destination that appeals to many types of travelers, from adventure seekers to those seeking cultural experiences. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll provide a detailed outline of the must-visit places, cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures in Africa.

List of Places to Visit in Africa

Africa is a large continent, and there are many places to see and things to do. Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Africa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Africa has a rich cultural history that spans thousands of years. Some of the top cultural attractions to visit in Africa include:

  • Cape Town, South Africa: This city is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, museums, and historic landmarks.
  • Robben Island, South Africa: This island served as a political prison during apartheid and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Marrakesh, Morocco: This city is known for its traditional markets, mosques, and palaces.
  • Lalibela, Ethiopia: This town is known for its rock-hewn churches, many of which date back to the 12th century.
  • Timbuktu, Mali: This ancient city is known for its historic mosques and libraries.

Natural Attractions of Africa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Africa is home to some of the most beautiful landscapes and natural wonders in the world. Here are some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic views to experience in Africa:

  • Serengeti National Park, Tanzania: This park is known for its annual wildebeest migration and abundance of wildlife.
  • Victoria Falls, Zambia/Zimbabwe: This waterfall is one of the largest in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Mount Kilimanjaro, Tanzania: This mountain is the highest in Africa and a popular destination for hikers.
  • Okavango Delta, Botswana: This delta is the world’s largest inland delta and is home to a variety of wildlife.
  • Sahara Desert, North Africa: This vast desert covers a large area of North Africa and is a popular destination for desert safaris and camel treks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Africa

Africa is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes to take:

  • Table Mountain Hike, South Africa: This hike provides stunning views of Cape Town and the surrounding coastline.
  • Fish River Canyon Hiking Trail, Namibia: This trail is known for its beautiful canyon views and diverse wildlife.
  • The Atlas Mountains, Morocco: The Atlas Mountains offer a variety of hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Usambara Mountains, Tanzania: These mountains offer some of the most beautiful and challenging hiking trails in Africa.
  • Victoria Falls Bike Tour, Zambia/Zimbabwe: This bike tour allows you to see Victoria Falls and the surrounding area in a unique way.

Best Things to Do in Africa: Activities and Tours

Africa offers a wide range of activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Africa:

  • Safari: Going on a safari is a must-do experience in Africa. Whether you want to see lions, giraffes, or elephants, there are many parks and reserves to choose from.
  • Gorilla Trekking: Gorilla trekking is a unique and exciting experience that allows you to see these endangered animals up close.
  • Nile River Cruise: A Nile River cruise is a relaxing way to see some of the stunning scenery and historic sites in Egypt.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: Hot air ballooning is a unique way to see the stunning landscapes of Africa, such as the Serengeti.
  • Whale Watching: Between the months of June and November, you can see whales migrate along the coast of South Africa.

Week-Long Itinerary for Africa

If you’re short on time, a week-long itinerary can still allow you to experience the best of Africa. Here’s an example:

  • Day 1-2: Cape Town, South Africa. Explore the city, visit museums, and take a hike up Table Mountain.
  • Day 3-4: Kruger National Park, South Africa. Go on a safari and see the Big Five.
  • Day 5-6: Victoria Falls, Zambia/Zimbabwe. See the waterfall and go on a sunset cruise on the Zambezi River.
  • Day 7: Marrakesh, Morocco. Explore the markets and visit a traditional hammam.

Food and Drink Scene of Africa

Africa offers a unique and diverse culinary experience. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bobotie: A South African dish of spiced meat baked with an egg-based topping.
  • Tagine: A Moroccan stew made with meat, vegetables, spices, and dried fruits.
  • Injera: A spongy Ethiopian bread used to scoop up stews and curries.
  • Bunny Chow: A South African fast food dish made with curry that is served in a hollowed-out loaf of bread.
  • Jollof Rice: A West African rice dish with tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Rooibos Tea: A South African tea made from the rooibos plant that is often served with milk and sugar.
  • Amarula: A South African cream liqueur made from the fruit of the marula tree.
  • Berber Tea: A Moroccan tea made with mint and sugar.
  • Palm Wine: A popular alcoholic beverage in West Africa made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Castle Lager: A popular South African beer.

Where to Stay in Africa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Africa has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Cape Grace, Cape Town, South Africa: A luxury hotel located on the V&A Waterfront.
  • The Victoria Falls Hotel, Zambia/Zimbabwe: An iconic hotel that overlooks the falls.
  • The Rufiji River Camp, Tanzania: A tented camp located in the Selous Game Reserve.
  • Riad Kniza, Marrakesh, Morocco: A traditional Moroccan riad with beautiful courtyard gardens.
  • The Backpack, Cape Town, South Africa: A hostel located in the heart of Cape Town.

What to Buy in Africa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Africa offers a range of unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Africa:

  • African textiles and fabrics.
  • Handmade baskets and crafts.
  • Carved wooden masks and sculptures.
  • Beadwork and jewelry.
  • Spices and tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Africa

Africa has a rich and diverse cultural heritage. It’s important to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques, churches, or other religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people.
  • Use your right hand for greeting, eating, and giving or receiving items.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Africa

The best time to visit Africa depends on what you want to see and do. Generally, the dry season from June to October is the ideal time for wildlife viewing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Africa

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Africa:

Do:

  • Research local customs and traditions before you go.
  • Get vaccinated before you travel to Africa.
  • Stay informed about political situations and security threats.
  • Respect the environment and avoid littering.
  • Support local businesses and communities.

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large amounts of cash.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or ignore their customs.
  • Wander around at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Touch or disturb wildlife.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are important considerations when traveling to Africa. Here are some tips:

Safety in Africa

  • Research travel warnings and security issues before you go.
  • Avoid walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep your valuables and documents in a secure location.
  • Use reputable transportation services.
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Africa

  • Get vaccinated before you travel to Africa.
  • Take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, which can spread diseases such as malaria and Zika.
  • Use sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and skin damage.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid contamination.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues while in Africa.

Getting to Africa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Africa is well connected to the rest of the world, and there are many ways to get to the continent. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

  • Airlines: Many airlines fly to major cities in Africa, including South African Airways, Ethiopian Airlines, and Air Morocco.
  • Bus: Bus services are available in many African countries.
  • Train: Some countries, such as South Africa, offer long-distance train services.
  • Visa Requirements: Many countries in Africa require visas for entry, so be sure to research this before you go.

Airports in Africa

  • OR Tambo International Airport, Johannesburg, South Africa
  • Cairo International Airport, Egypt
  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, Nairobi, Kenya
  • Addis Ababa Bole International Airport, Ethiopia
  • Mohammed V International Airport, Casablanca, Morocco

How to Get Around in Africa

Getting around in Africa depends on where you are and what you want to do. Here are some options to consider:

  • Public Transportation: Public transportation services such as buses and trains are available in many African cities.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in many cities, but be sure to negotiate a price beforehand.
  • Car Hire: Rental cars are available in some cities, but be aware that roads can be challenging.
  • Private Tours: Private tours with a guide or driver can be a great way to see the sights.

Conclusion

Africa is a diverse and fascinating continent that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in wildlife, culture, history, or outdoor adventures, Africa has it all. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your time in Africa. Remember to stay safe, respect local customs, and enjoy the journey!
