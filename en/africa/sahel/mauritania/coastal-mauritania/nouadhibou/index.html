Introduction to Nouadhibou

Located on the west coast of Mauritania, Nouadhibou is a coastal city known for its stunning landscapes, wildlife, and cultural heritage. With vast expanses of desert and coastline, the city offers a unique blend of natural beauty and man-made wonders. Home to a thriving fishing industry, Nouadhibou attracts visitors from around the world for its beaches, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. This travel guide is designed to help you navigate the city and discover its many treasures.

List of Places to Visit in Nouadhibou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Nouadhibou: The museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the city. It showcases various artifacts, including traditional clothing, tools, and weapons.
  • Darga: Located in the city center, the darga is a sacred place for Muslims. The site is known for its beautiful architecture and peaceful atmosphere.
  • Monoliths of Ben Amira: These prehistoric monuments have been standing for over 4,000 years and are considered an important part of Mauritanian heritage.

Natural Attractions of Nouadhibou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Banc d’Arguin National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Banc d’Arguin National Park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including migratory birds, dolphins, and sea turtles. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the park.
  • Cap Blanc: This stunning peninsula is known for its scenic coastline, crystalline waters, and white sand beaches. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports such as windsurfing and kitesurfing.
  • Desert of Nouadhibou: Extending over 10,000 square kilometers, the desert is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to go on camel or ATV rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nouadhibou

  • Cap Blanc Trail: This trail follows the coastline to Cap Blanc and offers breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.
  • Parcours de Santé: Located near the city center, this hiking trail is perfect for those who want to explore the local terrain on foot. The trail also includes outdoor exercise equipment.

Best Things to Do in Nouadhibou: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing trips: With a bustling fishing industry, Nouadhibou is the perfect destination for seafood lovers. Visitors can take a boat trip for a chance to catch their own fish and enjoy an authentic local meal.
  • Lighthouse tour: The Nouadhibou lighthouse is one of the tallest in Africa, standing at 60 meters. Visitors can take a tour to the top for a stunning view of the city and the coastline.
  • Camel riding: Experience the beauty and tranquility of the desert of Nouadhibou on a camel ride, where you can take in the stunning scenery and enjoy a unique adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Nouadhibou

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thiéboudienne: This national dish is a flavorful rice and fish stew, cooked with vegetables and spices.
  • Couscous: Made with semolina wheat, couscous is a staple in Mauritanian cuisine and can be served with meat, vegetables, or fish.
  • Aseeda: A sweet dessert made with millet flour, water, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Green tea: A refreshing tea served with mint and sugar.
  • Sahara beer: The locally brewed beer is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Nouadhibou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ibis Nouadhibou: Located near the city center, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving international cuisine.
  • Hôtel Continental: This hotel offers panoramic views of the sea, a restaurant, and a pool.
  • Auberge Evasion: A cozy guesthouse with a colorful decor and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Nouadhibou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jewelry: Handmade jewelry made from camel bone and ebony wood is a popular souvenir.
  • Ethnic clothes and fabrics: Colorful fabrics and traditional garments are a must-buy for fashion enthusiasts.
  • Spices and tea: Spices such as cumin and saffron and delicious green tea blends can be found in local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Nouadhibou

  • Islam is the predominant religion in Nouadhibou, and visitors are expected to show respect for Muslim customs and dress modestly.
  • Traditional clothing includes brightly colored robes and headwear for men, and colorful dresses and headscarves for women.

Best Time to Visit Nouadhibou

The best time to visit Nouadhibou is from October to April when the weather is warm and sunny with little rainfall. During the summer months, the temperatures can reach up to 50 degrees Celsius, making outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nouadhibou

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photographs without asking for permission.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nouadhibou

  • Visitors are advised to avoid traveling alone at night and to be aware of their surroundings in crowded areas.
  • Petty theft can be a problem, so it’s recommended to keep valuables safe and secure.
  • Visitors should also avoid crossing the border to Western Sahara due to the risk of landmines.

Health in Nouadhibou

  • Visitors should drink only bottled water and avoid uncooked food.
  • It’s recommended to get vaccinations for yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies before traveling to Mauritania.
  • Sunscreen and appropriate clothing are also essential to avoid sunburn and heat exhaustion.

Getting to Nouadhibou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nouadhibou

  • Nouadhibou International Airport is the main airport serving the city, with flights from major cities in the region, including Dakar and Casablanca.

Bus Stations Near Nouadhibou

  • There are no direct domestic or international bus routes to Nouadhibou.

Other Options

  • Taxis and minivans are the main form of transport within the city, and visitors can negotiate prices with drivers.

How to Get Around in Nouadhibou

  • Walking, cycling, and taxis are the most common means of getting around the city.
  • Visitors can also hire a car or a 4x4 to explore the surrounding region.

Where to Go Next from Nouadhibou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Parc National du Banc d’Arguin: Located 60 kilometers south of Nouadhibou, this national park boasts one of the world’s largest bird sanctuaries.
  • Atar: Located 400 kilometers inland, Atar is a desert city known for its stunning landscapes and ancient architecture.
  • Chinguetti: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Chinguetti is a historic settlement in the heart of the Sahara desert, known for its unique architecture and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Nouadhibou is a fascinating destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. With its stunning coastline, vibrant markets, and friendly locals, it’s a must-visit for those looking to explore the lesser-known treasures of Africa. Use this travel guide to help plan your trip and discover the many wonders of Nouadhibou.
