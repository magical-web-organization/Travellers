Introduction to Choum

Located in the heart of the Sahara Desert, Choum is a small town in the Sahel region of Mauritania. Despite its remote location, Choum offers a unique travel experience for adventure seekers and cultural enthusiasts alike. From hiking and biking routes to historic landmarks and cultural festivals, Choum is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered.

List of Places to Visit in Choum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • El-Ghallaouiya Mosque: The oldest and most iconic mosque in Choum, featuring beautiful architecture and intricate mosaics.
  • Chinguetti Library: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that houses rare and ancient Islamic manuscripts.
  • Maadar en Nayair Museum: An interactive museum that showcases traditional Mauritanian crafts and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Choum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Terjit Oasis: A stunning natural oasis located in a canyon, perfect for swimming and relaxing.
  • Krechet El-Ghazal: A natural rock formation that resembles a gazelle located in the middle of the desert.
  • Oum El Mahassin Palm Grove: An expansive palm grove that provides shade and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Choum

  • Tidjikja Loop: A challenging 90-kilometer loop that passes through the desert, mountains, and palm groves.
  • Desert Trek: A multi-day trek that takes you through rugged desert terrain and past traditional nomadic settlements.

Best Things to Do in Choum: Activities and Tours

  • Fat Bike Tour: Take a guided tour on a fat bike, exploring the natural beauty of Choum’s desert and mountains.
  • Fishing Tours: Book a tour to go fishing with traditional Mauritanian fishermen in the Oum El Mahassin River.
  • Henna Tattooing: Get a traditional henna tattoo from local women using natural ingredients and ancient techniques.

Week-Long Itinerary for Choum

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Choum’s historic landmarks like the El-Ghallaouiya Mosque and Chinguetti
Day 2-3 Take a multi-day desert trek and campout under the stars
Day 4 Relax at the Terjit Oasis and experience traditional bathing ceremonies

Food and Drink Scene of Choum

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thieboudienne: A traditional Senegalese rice and fish dish, popular in Choum’s local restaurants.
  • Bread and Couscous: A staple food in Choum, typically eaten with meat or vegetable stew.
  • Mint Tea: A refreshing and traditional Mauritanian tea that is served throughout the day.

Local Beverages

  • Kinkeliba: A tea made from the dried leaves of the kinkeliba plant, believed to have health benefits.
  • Lemonade: Freshly made lemonade is a popular and refreshing beverage in Choum.
  • Bissap Juice: A traditional West African juice made from hibiscus flowers.

Where to Stay in Choum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chez Ali: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the El-Ghallaouiya Mosque.
  • Auberge Sahara: A mid-range hotel featuring a restaurant, free Wi-Fi and air conditioning.
  • Lodge Kameliya: A luxurious eco-lodge that offers a unique and comfortable desert experience.

What to Buy in Choum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Traditional and unique jewelry made from silver or camel bone.
  • Toureg Headscarves: Brightly colored scarves, commonly worn by the Toureg people in the Sahara.
  • Mauretanian Blankets: Traditional blankets that are hand-woven and feature intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Choum

Choum is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from Mauritania’s Arab, Berber, and African heritage. The local people are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors. It is common to greet someone with “Salem” (peace) or “Salamu Calaykum” (peace be upon you) and it’s customary to take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or mosque.

Best Time to Visit Choum

The best time to visit Choum is during the cooler months, from November to February. The temperatures are mild enough to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking, and it’s also the dry season when the sandstorms are less frequent.

Do’s and Don’ts of Choum

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration in the desert heat.
  • Cover your body when visiting sacred places like mosques.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing or swimsuits in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Choum

Choum is generally a safe place for travelers. However, it’s important to be cautious when travelling in remote areas and to avoid large gatherings during local celebrations.

Health in Choum

It’s recommended to get vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, yellow fever, and typhoid before travelling to Choum. It’s also important to bring and drink only bottled water.

Getting to Choum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Choum

The nearest airport to Choum is the Atar International Airport (ATR), which is about 200 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Choum

The closest bus station to Choum is in Atar, where you can take a local bus or shared taxi to Choum.

Other Options

You can book a private driver or hire a car to get to Choum, but it’s recommended to hire a local guide for the best experience.

How to Get Around in Choum

The best way to get around in Choum is by foot or on a guided tour. A local guide can help you navigate the desert terrain and ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

Where to Go Next from Choum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atar: A charming town located in the Adrar Region, known for its white-washed buildings and traditional markets.
  • Nouakchott: The capital city of Mauritania, with a vibrant local culture and historic landmarks.
  • Banc d’Arguin National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring diverse bird species and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Choum might not be on every traveler’s radar, but it’s a unique travel destination that offers a blend of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and peaceful serenity. From historic landmarks and natural attractions to exotic cuisine and traditional souvenirs, Choum is a hidden gem worth exploring.
