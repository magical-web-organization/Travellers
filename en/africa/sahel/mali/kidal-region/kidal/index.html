Introduction to Kidal

Kidal is a town and region located in the northeastern part of Mali, which is in the heart of the Sahel region of Africa. It is known for its rich history, beautiful desert landscapes, and unique cultural heritage. Despite years of conflict and instability, Kidal has recently opened up to tourists seeking a truly unforgettable travel experience. Here is your comprehensive guide to exploring Kidal.

List of Places to Visit in Kidal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Tomb of Askia - a 15th-century masterpiece of West African architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • The Kidal Museum - displaying artefacts of the area’s past, including rock paintings and traditional Touareg crafts.
  • The Great Mosque of Kidal - an iconic landmark of the town, with ornate brickwork and beautiful minarets.
  • The Hat of Mossi - a mysterious prehistoric ruin that looks like a giant hat from afar.

Natural Attractions of Kidal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tassili N’Ajjer - a beautiful mountain range with stunning views of the surrounding desert landscapes.
  • Tin Hinan Desert - a bewitching desert dotted with green oases and ancient camping grounds.
  • Erg Admer - a beautiful sand-dune sea with high winds reputed for windboarding and sandboarding.
  • Tassili n’Aglers - a magical place with awesome rock formations that you can’t miss.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kidal

  • The Adrar des Ifoghas Trek - a challenging trek through the beautiful Adrar des Ifoghas mountain range that takes about 10 days to complete.
  • The Kidal - Iforas mountain biking trail - a scenic bike ride from the town of Kidal to the Iforas mountains, passing through beautiful desert landscapes and traditional villages.

Best Things to Do in Kidal: Activities and Tours

  • Camel treks to Timbuktu - an exciting adventure that takes several days, passing through beautiful desert landscapes and traditional Berber villages.
  • Exploring the Touareg culture - experience the unique culture of the Touareg people with a visit to a local village and learning about their traditional customs and crafts.
  • Star-gazing at night - stargaze in the beautiful desert sky with a knowledgeable guide to show you the constellations.

Food and Drink Scene of Kidal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Couscous - a traditional Berber dish made of steamed semolina grains served with meat, vegetables, and chickpeas.
  • Thieboudienne - a flavorful rice dish with fish, tomatoes, and vegetables, typical of Senegal, but common in Kidal.
  • Chakaluka - a spicy Touareg dip made from tomatoes, chili peppers, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Mint tea - a refreshing and traditional drink in Mali.
  • Touareg tea - a strong green tea with mint and lots of sugar, served in three rounds as a sign of hospitality.

Where to Stay in Kidal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Amassakoul - a hotel with a combination of modern comfort and a traditional style, offering ensuite rooms.
  • Hotel La Colombe - cozy, best-tier hotel with restaurant, bar, lively garden, a sun terrace, and 24-hour front desk service.
  • Camping Evasion - budget-friendly camping accommodation, offering both individual tents and traditional Touareg tents.

What to Buy in Kidal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade jewellery - Kidal is known for its traditional Berber jewellery crafted by the Touareg people.
  • Traditional Touareg textiles - colorful handmade cloths and clothing with intricate designs and patterns.
  • Handmade leather products - intricately designed and crafted leather products, from sandals to diaries.

Local Culture and Customs of Kidal

Kidal is mainly inhabited by the Touareg people, who are known for their unique cultural customs and traditions. It is important to respect their religion, customs, and way of life when exploring Kidal.

Best Time to Visit Kidal

The best time to visit Kidal is between November and February when the weather is mild, and the desert landscapes are at their most beautiful. In the summertime, the temperatures can be intense and uncomfortable for exploring the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kidal

Do:

  • Respect the local culture by dressing modestly and covering your head when visiting mosques or other holy sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the traditional Berber dishes.
  • Respect the local customs and way of life.
  • Take plenty of water, sunscreen and wear comfortable shoes when exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs or film people without obtaining their permission first.
  • Purchase cultural artefacts or items of cultural significance without verifying their authenticity.
  • Fail to obtain the necessary visas and travel documents before arriving in Kidal.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kidal

Unfortunately, Kidal and the surrounding region have been subject to bouts of instability and conflict, so it is important to stay up to date on the situation before planning your trip. It is recommended to seek information from reputable sources, such as government travel advisories, and to stay in well-traveled areas and avoid traveling alone.

Health in Kidal

It is important to take the necessary health precautions when travelling to Kidal, including vaccinations for yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid. Malaria is also present in the region, so take measures to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Kidal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kidal

The Kidal Airport serves both domestic and international flights. However, it is advisable to check regional travel advisories before planning your visit.

Bus Stations Near Kidal

There are several regional bus services that connect to Kidal, including from Gao in the east or Tamanrasset in Algeria to the west.

Other Options

Hiring a private driver is also possible to reach Kidal from neighbouring countries in the region, which can be arranged through local tour companies.

How to Get Around in Kidal

Taxis and hire cars are available in Kidal for short trips, while for longer trips a private driver or a tour service is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Kidal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The city of Gao - a historic city located on the banks of the Niger River.
  • The Salt Mines of Taoudenni - located in the heart of the Sahara Desert and home to some of the oldest known salt mines in the world.
  • The city of Timbuktu - a UNESCO World Heritage Site with a rich cultural history.

Conclusion

Kidal is one of the most captivating and unforgettable destinations in the Sahel region. This comprehensive guide has covered everything you need to know, from the best places to visit, accommodation options, and the local culture and customs, ensuring you have a safe and enjoyable adventure in Kidal, Mali.
