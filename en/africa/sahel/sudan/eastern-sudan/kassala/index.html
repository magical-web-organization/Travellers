Introduction to Kassala

Kassala is a city located in eastern Sudan in the Sahel region of Africa. This historic city offers tourists a chance to explore a rich blend of cultures, traditions, monuments, and outdoor adventure activities. Kassala is home to several museums that showcase the history and culture of the Nubians and other ethnic groups that have inhabited this region.

In this guide, we will take you through the best things to do and see in Kassala, along with recommendations for where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to stay safe while exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Kassala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Khatmiyya Mosque

This historic mosque is one of the oldest mosques in Kassala, built by the Hamed family during Ottoman reign. It is a beautiful example of Islamic architecture with its white-and-blue colors and intricate details.

The Museum of Eastern Sudan

The Museum of Eastern Sudan offers a glimpse into the diverse history of Sudan’s eastern region. The museum features exhibits on Nubian culture, ancient Egyptian history, and Sudan’s Islamic heritage. You can also see traditional tools, pottery, and jewelry from the region.

Doko Village

Take a trip to Doko Village and explore the traditional lifestyles of the Nubian people. Located at the foothills of the Kassala Mountains, Doko offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of city life. You can also visit the local market to buy traditional Nubian crafts and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Kassala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kassala Mountains

The Kassala Mountains offer a spectacular backdrop to the city. Take a hike to enjoy its scenic beauty and rugged terrain. You can also hire a guide to go on a more extended hike that includes cultural and historical sites.

Gash River

The Gash River is a great place to enjoy some outdoor activities. Take a kayak or canoe ride down the river and enjoy the stunning views. You may also spot a variety of birds and wildlife on your way.

National Park of Sanganeb

Located off the coast of Sudan, the National Park of Sanganeb is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Here, you can scuba dive and snorkel amid the vibrant coral reefs and marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kasala

Kassala is a great destination for hikers and bikers. A hike to the top of Kassala Mountain is perfect for anyone wanting a good workout and breathtaking views. Those who prefer biking can head towards the Red Sea Hills, where you can easily hire a bike and explore on your own or with a guide.

Best Things to Do in Kassala: Activities and Tours

Camel Rides

Experience a camel ride in the desert with an experienced guide. You can explore the cultural and historical sites on this four-hour tour.

Sudanese Cooking Classes

Learn how to cook Sudan’s traditional dishes like ful and fata on this half-day cooking course. You will also pick exotic spices from the local market and learn about the ingredients used in Sudanese cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kassala

Day 1: Arrival and Sightseeing

Start your visit to Kassala with a tour of the city. Visit the grand Khatmiyya Mosque, The Museum of Eastern Sudan, and the Doko village.

Day 2: Hiking or Biking

Explore the Kassala mountain range with a hike or bike ride. Take in the breathtaking views and immerse yourself in the ethereal beauty of these mountains.

Day 3: Gash River

Take a kayak or canoe ride down the Gash River while enjoying the surrounding greenery and spotting wildlife along the banks.

Day 4: Cultural and Gastronomic Experience

Indulge in a Sudanese cooking class in the afternoon, and enjoy a camel ride in the evening. The four-hour tour includes a visit to a local market, where you will pick exotic spices, and cook Sudanese dishes like ful and fata.

Day 5: Excursion to Red Sea Hills

Take a day-long excursion to the Red Sea Hills and embark on a self-guided bike tour. Enjoy the picturesque landscape and explore the local culture and heritage.

Day 6: National Park of Sanganeb

Experience the aquatic beauty of Sudan by scuba diving or snorkeling through the National Park of Sanganeb.

Day 7: Departure

Take in the last sights of Kassala before catching a bus or flight back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Kassala

Must-Try Dishes

Ful

Ful is a classic Sudanese dish served with bread. It is a hearty breakfast dish that is filling and flavorful.

Fata

Fata is a dish made of bread, milk, and spices. It is a unique and delicious dish, perfect for lunch or dinner.

Local Beverages

Shai

Shai is a sweet tea that is often flavored with mint and other spices. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for enjoying in the hot weather.

Aradaib

Aradaib is a traditional Sudenese dessert made from ground nuts, sesame seeds, and honey. It is a sweet and satisfying treat that is perfect for after-dinner.

Where to Stay in Kassala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kassala Tourist Hotel

Located in the heart of Kassala, Kassala Tourist Hotel is one of the best places to stay in the city. The hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms with modern amenities and a friendly staff.

Alshamaal Hotel

Located near the Kassala bus station, Alshamaal Hotel is a budget-friendly option for travelers. The hotel offers basic amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Kassala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Crafts

Handmade crafts like pottery, traditional clothing, and jewelry are great souvenirs from Kassala. The local market in Doko Village is a great place to find such items.

Local Culture and Customs of Kassala

Sudan is an Islamic country that observes strict societal norms. It is important to respect local customs and traditions, while visiting Kassala. Dress conservatively, especially when visiting mosques and other religious places. Follow local rules and regulations, and take permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Best Time to Visit Kassala

The best time to visit Kassala is during the cooler months of November to February. The temperature is between 15-20°C, making it ideal for outdoor activities like hiking and exploring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kassala

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting mosques.
  • Take permission before taking photographs.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Disrespect religious or cultural norms.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kassala

Sudan has a negative travel advisory, and travelers must be aware of the risks before visiting the country. Exercise caution at all times and maintain a low profile, especially after dark. Avoid large gatherings and protests.

Health in Kassala

It is important to take necessary precautions while traveling to a foreign country. Consult with a physician before traveling and take necessary vaccinations. Carry the necessary medication and prescription with you in case of any emergency.

Getting to Kassala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kassala

Kassala International Airport is the closest airport, catering to domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Kassala

The Kassala Bus Station offers a variety of transportation options, from local buses to private taxis, to travel to and from the city.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a private taxi, or take a rental car to reach Kassala.

How to Get Around in Kassala

Kassala is a walkable city with most popular tourist destinations located in the city center. Visitors can also hire a private taxi or take public buses to reach farther locations.

Where to Go Next from Kassala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Port Sudan

Port Sudan is a beautiful and lively port city, and a popular destination for beach lovers. This city is located on the north coast of the Red Sea and offers world-class diving spots.

The Nile River

A trip to the Nile River is an absolute must-visit for nature lovers. You can explore the famous ancient sites of Meroe along the Nile River.

Conclusion

Kassala offers a blend of historic and cultural sites, scenic beauty, outdoor adventure activities, and delicious cuisine. Despite the risks, Kassala is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in African culture and history. Be sure to exercise caution and respect local customs to ensure a memorable and safe visit.
