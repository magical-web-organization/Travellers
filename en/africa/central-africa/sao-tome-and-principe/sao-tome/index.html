Introduction to São Tomé

São Tomé is a small and beautiful island nation located in Central Africa, known for its rich Portuguese colonial history, unspoiled jungles and beaches, and friendly locals. São Tomé is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves adventure, nature, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in São Tomé

If you’re planning a trip to São Tomé, here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Nacional - A museum that showcases the history and culture of São Tomé and Príncipe.
  • Fort of São Sebastião - A 16th-century Portuguese fort that now houses a museum art gallery.
  • Catedral de São Tomé - A historic cathedral that was built in the 1570s.

Natural Attractions of São Tomé: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pico Cão Grande - A towering volcanic rock formation that’s popular for hiking and rock climbing.
  • Lagoa Azul - A beautiful blue lagoon located in the heart of the island.
  • Praia Piscina - A secluded beach with crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of São Tomé

São Tome is an island town that offers a lot of hiking/biking routes. Here are some of the best ones to try:

  • Pico Cão Grande - A challenging hike that offers stunning panoramic views of the island.
  • Obo National Park - A nature reserve that features several trails, including a 2-hour hike to Cascata de São Nicolau, a waterfall.
  • Roça Monte Café - One of the most popular coffee plantations in São Tomé, offers scenic hiking routes through coffee plantations.

Best Things to Do in São Tomé: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some exciting and unique things to do in Sao Tome, here are a few ideas:

  • Coffee Plantation Tour - Visit one of the many coffee plantations in São Tomé and learn about the coffee-making process.
  • Diving and Snorkeling - Try diving or snorkeling in the crystal clear waters around the island and discover the coral reefs’ beauty and marine life of the place.
  • São Toméan Cultural Experience - Get a glimpse of Sao Tome’s rich culture and customs at a traditional dance performance or local festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for São Tomé

Here’s a week-long itinerary for São Tomé that will help you to make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in São Tomé, explore the city by visiting Museu Nacional, Catedral de São Tomé, and Fort of São Sebastião.
  • Day 2: Hike to Pico Cão Grande, visit nearby historic Roça Monte Café.
  • Day 3: Explore Lagoa Azul or bounce between untouched beaches across the island, including Praia Piscina and Praia Jalé.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of coffee plantations to see how coffee is grown in Sao Tome.
  • Day 5: Visit Cascata de São Nicolau, the popular waterfall or go hiking in Obo National park, pack a picnic and relax amidst the scenic views of the park.
  • Day 6: Go diving or snorkeling around the island.
  • Day 7: Depart from Sao Tome.

Food and Drink Scene of São Tomé

No trip to São Tomé is complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Caril de Cabrito - A traditional dish of Sao Tome and Principe which is a curry of goat meat made with vegetables and coconut milk.
  • Calulu - A boiled fish or meat stew which is typically served with white rice.
  • Bananas da terra - Fried plantains, a crunchy and sweet snack, perfect on beaches.

Local Beverages

  • Cerveja Rosema - A locally brewed beer that has a rich caramel flavor.
  • Cocoa Liquor - Sao Tome and Principe have one of the finest cocoa in the world. Try the cocoa liquor that is made by mixing a blend of cocoa with goats’ milk and sugar.

Where to Stay in São Tomé: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

São Tomé has a range of accommodation options available to meet every budget.

  • Omali Lodge - A four-star luxury resort that has a private beach, infinity pool, and a spa.
  • Pestana Sao Tome - A five-star hotel located on the edge of the city. It has a pool, gym and many other amenities.
  • Casa Vitoria - A charming guesthouse located in the heart of the city, which is known for its warm hospitality.

What to Buy in São Tomé: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the souvenirs and local products you should consider purchasing when you visit São Tomé:

  • Coffee - São Tomé produces some of the world’s finest coffee, get your fix and pack them for home.
  • Cocoa - Chocolate made from Sao Tome’s cocoa is an exceptional delicacy that’s a must-try.
  • Wooden Carvings - The island is famous for its hand-carved wooden sculptures, from historical colonial figure replica to animals.

Local Culture and Customs of São Tomé

São Tomé is a melting pot of cultures and ethnicities, with Portuguese Colonial and African influences. The locals value their traditions and customs, which contribute to the island’s unique cultural identity. It’s important to respect their culture and be open to new experiences and customs.

Best Time to Visit São Tomé

The best time to visit São Tomé is from May to September, where the weather is drier, making it perfect for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of São Tomé

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Pack a swimsuit and hat for the beach
  • Carry sunscreen and insect repellent when going on hikes

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing, particularly when visiting spiritual sites
  • Judge the locals for their ways of life
  • Litter in the city or the countryside

Safety and Health

Safety in São Tomé

São Tomé is a safe country to visit, while crime rates in the city are quite low, a certain amount of caution and common sense is always required when traveling.

Health in São Tomé

São Tomé has an excellent healthcare system, and hospitals are well-equipped with modern facilities. Yet, it is ideal to pack basic first-aid kit and GPS enabled mobile phone in case of emergency.

Getting to São Tomé: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near São Tomé

São Tomé International Airport is the main gateway for travel to the island. It is connected by daily international flights to major airports across the world.

Bus Stations and Taxis Near São Tomé

There are no bus or train services in São Tomé. Taxis and private cars are the most common modes of transport around the city.

Other Options

Private boats and yachts can be rented for island hopping or sea adventures around the island for a unique experience.

How to Get Around in São Tomé

There are several transport options available to explore the city, including taxis and rental cars. Those with adventure in their heart can rent bicycles or go hiking for an adventure with nature.

Where to Go Next from São Tomé: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ilhéu das Rolas - A small island off the southwest coast of São Tomé which is perfect for diving and snorkeling.
  • Principe - An island nation close to São Tomé with beautiful landscapes and wildlife.
  • Gabon - A neighboring African nation with rich biodiversity and national parks.

Conclusion

São Tomé is an ideal destination for those seeking an exciting and unique travel experience. Exploring the island’s beautiful national parks, beaches, and historical sites will be an unforgettable experience. Indulging in the local cuisine and culture, and mingling with the friendly locals will make you feel at home while in São Tomé.
