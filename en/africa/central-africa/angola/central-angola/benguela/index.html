Introduction to Benguela

Benguela is a coastal city located in Central Angola, in Africa. Founded in the 17th century, it is one of the oldest cities in Angola and is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, stunning landscapes, and pristine beaches. Benguela is a perfect destination for travelers looking for an immersive African experience, away from the typical tourist hubs.

List of Places to Visit in Benguela

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Benguela is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural heritage. The following are a few must-visit sites:

  • Fort of Sao Felipe: Built in the 17th century, the historic fort is one of the most impressive landmarks in Benguela. It offers fantastic views of the city and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Benguela Railway Museum: The museum showcases the history of the railway system in Angola and the significant role it played in the country’s development.
  • Museum of the People’s Resistance: The museum provides an overview of the armed struggle against colonialism in Angola and honors the brave men and women who fought for independence.
  • Cihango Monument: Located at the entrance of the city, the monument is a tribute to the first Portuguese expedition that explored the region in the 15th century.

Natural Attractions of Benguela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Benguela is blessed with a diverse array of natural attractions – from rugged mountain ranges to pristine beaches. Below are a few not-to-be-missed outdoor destinations:

  • Baia Azul: Located south of Benguela, Baia Azul features stunning white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Mount Leba: The mountain range is a hiker’s paradise, offering numerous hiking trails, towering peaks, and breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Iona National Park: The national park is one of the most biodiverse regions in Angola, with a wide range of flora and fauna, including elephants, lions, hyenas, and giraffes.
  • Tundavala Gap: The Tundavala Gap is a geological phenomenon that offers sweeping views of the surrounding landscape, including mountains, canyons, and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benguela

Benguela offers excellent opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the region’s rugged terrain. Here are a few hiking and biking routes:

  • Mount Leba Hike: The hike up Mount Leba is a challenging but rewarding experience. You will pass through lush forests, rocky ridges, and breathtaking vistas.
  • Cubal Canyon Trek: The Cubal Canyon Trek is a moderate hike that passes through picturesque landscapes and offers stunning views of the Cubal River.
  • Casa Tua Trail: The Casa Tua Trail is a popular mountain biking route that offers a mix of terrains, from rocky hills to smooth dirt tracks.

Best Things to Do in Benguela: Activities and Tours

Benguela offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to a wide range of interests. Here are a few top-rated activities:

  • Whale Watching: Benguela is one of the best places in Africa to go whale watching, with humpback whales, orcas, and dolphins frequently spotted along the coast.
  • Fishing Trips: Benguela’s waters are teeming with fish, making it an ideal destination for fishing trips.
  • Wine Tasting: Benguela boasts a burgeoning wine industry, with several vineyards offering wine tastings and guided tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Benguela

Angola has an exciting and diverse culinary scene, and Benguela is no exception. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moamba: A traditional Angolan stew made with meat, vegetables, and palm oil.
  • Calulu: A popular dish made with fish or chicken cooked in palm oil with onions, garlic, chili, and vegetables.
  • Frango Grelhado: Grilled chicken marinated in spices and served with a side of rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • N’Gola: A popular Angolan beer that is light and refreshing.
  • Palm Wine: A traditional African drink made from fermented palm sap.
  • Ginger Beer: A non-alcoholic drink made from fresh ginger root and sugar syrup.

Where to Stay in Benguela: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Benguela offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few top-rated choices:

  • Hotel Praia Morena: Located on the beachfront, the hotel offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and modern amenities.
  • Aparthotel Mil Cidades: The aparthotel offers spacious and well-appointed rooms, with cooking facilities.
  • Hotel Luso: The hotel is located in the heart of Benguela and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Benguela: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, Benguela has plenty of options. Here are a few ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts: You can find an array of handmade crafts, including baskets, pottery, and wooden carvings.
  • Local Spices: Angola is known for its incredible spices, and Benguela’s markets stock a wide range of flavors.
  • Artwork: Benguela has a thriving art scene, with many local artists creating striking paintings and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Benguela

Angolans are generally friendly and welcoming to foreigners. However, it’s essential to respect local customs and traditions. Here are a few cultural norms to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: It’s customary to greet people with a handshake or hug, depending on the nature of your relationship.
  • Respect: Angolans pride themselves on their traditions and customs, and it’s crucial to respect these values.
  • Dress Code: Dress modestly when visiting places of worship or government buildings, and avoid wearing revealing clothing.

Best Time to Visit Benguela

The best time to visit Benguela is during the dry season, from June to September. The weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities without worrying about rain or humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Benguela

Do:

  • Learn Basic Portuguese Phrases: Knowing a few key phrases in Portuguese can go a long way in making your trip more enjoyable.
  • Eat Locally: Angolan cuisine is delicious, and it’s an excellent way to experience the local culture.
  • Be Open-Minded: Angolan culture is different from Western cultures, so keep an open mind and embrace new experiences.

Don’t:

  • Take Photographs Without Permission: Always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places of worship.
  • Disrespect Customs and Traditions: Be respectful of local customs and traditions, even if they are unfamiliar to you.
  • Travel Alone at Night: It’s generally not safe to travel alone at night, so plan your activities accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Benguela

Benguela is a relatively safe city, but it’s still essential to take precautions while traveling. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: Stick to well-lit areas and travel in groups at night.
  • Be Vigilant with Your Valuables: Keep your valuables, including cash, passport, and electronics, in a secure place.
  • Use Licensed Taxis: Only use licensed taxis, and negotiate the fare before starting your journey.

Health in Benguela

It’s crucial to take health precautions when traveling to Benguela. Here are a few tips:

  • Get Vaccinated: Make sure you are up to date on all your vaccinations before traveling to Angola.
  • Drink Bottled Water: Only drink bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.
  • Protect Yourself from Mosquito Bites: Use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to protect yourself from mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Benguela: Transportation Options and Logistics

Benguela is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here are a few transportation options:

Airports Near Benguela

  • Benguela Airport: The airport is located 3 km from the city center and offers daily flights to and from Luanda.

Bus Stations Near Benguela

  • Benguela Bus Terminal: The bus terminal is located in the city center and offers connections to other cities in Angola, including Lubango and Luanda.

Other Options

  • Train: Benguela is connected to other cities in Angola via a reliable rail network.
  • Rental Car: Rental cars are available in Benguela, but it’s essential to have an international driving permit.

How to Get Around in Benguela

Benguela is a relatively small city, and you can easily explore it on foot or by taxi. Taxis are widely available and reasonable, but it’s essential to negotiate the fare before starting your journey.

Where to Go Next from Benguela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Benguela is a fantastic base to explore other parts of Angola. Here are a few nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Lobito: A coastal city located 30 km north of Benguela, known for its beautiful beaches and Portuguese colonial architecture.
  • Lubango: A city located 180 km southeast of Benguela, famous for its mountain landscapes and cascading waterfalls.
  • Huambo: A city located 280 km east of Benguela, known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant street markets.

Conclusion

Benguela is a unique and vibrant African destination, with plenty of attractions and activities to offer visitors. From its rich cultural heritage to its pristine beaches and awe-inspiring mountain ranges, Benguela provides a memorable experience that will live with you for years to come. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this gem of a city in Angola.
