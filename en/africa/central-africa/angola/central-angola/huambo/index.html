Introduction to Huambo

Are you looking for an authentic African experience? Look no further than Huambo, the second-largest city in Angola. Located in the central region of the country, Huambo offers a unique blend of rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. From its museums and historical landmarks to its outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in Huambo. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Huambo.

List of Places to Visit in Huambo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Huambo has a rich history dating back to the colonial era. Visitors can explore the city’s past at several museums and historical places, such as:

  • Jardim da Cultura: A beautiful park built in the early 20th century, displaying a range of indigenous plant species, and home to the Museum of Huambo
  • Fort Huambo: A former military stronghold from the colonial era, now used as a museum dedicated to Angola’s struggle for independence
  • Nossa Senhora do Monte Church: A stunning Catholic church built in the late 19th century, featuring unique architectural details and a fascinating history

Natural Attractions of Huambo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Huambo is home to many natural wonders, including:

  • Tundavala Gap: A breathtaking canyon with stunning panoramic views, located near the town of Lubango, about 170 km from Huambo
  • Calandula Waterfalls: A magnificent waterfall system located in the north of Angola that cascades in crystal clear pools
  • Quiçama National Park: A vast wilderness area that is home to several species of endangered animals, including elephants, hippos, and crocodiles

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huambo

Huambo offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Angola. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Mount Moco: The highest peak in Angola, it provides breathtaking views, and it’s a perfect spot for hikes
  • Flamingo Route: A bird-watching trail located in the Cunene river and the Namib Desert
  • Sobas Route: An off-road trail that passes through several picturesque villages

Best Things to Do in Huambo: Activities and Tours

Huambo has plenty of things to do for all interests, including:

  • Visit a traditional market: Head to the central market and shop for local crafts and souvenirs
  • Try the local food: Sample some of the delicious Angolan cuisine, including beans and rice, fish, and the national dish, “muamba de galinha”
  • Take a city tour: Explore the city’s landmarks, museums, and historical sites with a local guide
  • Join a cultural festival: Experience the colorful cultural festivals that take place throughout the year, including the national Independence Day celebration, which is held on 11 November

Week-Long Itinerary for Huambo

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Huambo, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the city’s museums, parks, and historical landmarks
  • Day 3-4: Embark on a scenic hike or bike ride and explore the stunning natural beauty around Huambo
  • Day 5-6: Join a cultural festival or experience the local cuisine and nightlife
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Lubango and visit Tundavala Gap

Food and Drink Scene of Huambo

Must-Try Dishes

Angolan cuisine is a fusion of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences. Some of the must-try dishes in Huambo include:

  • Muamba de Galinha: Angola’s national dish made with chicken, peanut butter sauce, and local vegetables
  • Mufete: Grilled fish served with traditional cassava bread and vegetables
  • Calulu: Stewed fish or chicken with okra, palm oil, and beans

Local Beverages

Angola produces its wine, beer, and soft drinks. Some of the popular local drinks include:

  • Cuca and Nocal: The two most popular beers in Angola
  • Catembe: A local brandy made from distilled palm wine

Where to Stay in Huambo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huambo has several accommodation options to suit different budgets, including:

  • Hotel Ekuikui: A four-star hotel with an outdoor pool, spa, and fitness center
  • Hotel Roma Ritz: A boutique hotel located in the city center, offering modern amenities and comfortable rooms
  • Hostel Huambo: A budget-friendly hostel with clean dormitories and private rooms

What to Buy in Huambo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huambo has many unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home, such as:

  • Handcrafted pottery and weaving: Visit the central market and pick up locally made pottery, baskets, and weaving
  • Coffee and cocoa: Angola is one of Africa’s leading coffee and cocoa producers, so make sure to try and bring some home.

Local Culture and Customs of Huambo

The natives of Huambo are predominantly Ovimbundu people who live in rural areas within the province. They primarily speak Kimbundu and Portuguese. Traditional music, and dances correlate with the Ovimbundu people’s daily social life are also significant parts of cultural practices in Huambo.

Best Time to Visit Huambo

The best time to visit Huambo is from June to September, which is the dry season with milder temperatures. Visitors can also visit between November to February, which is the rainy season, but the temperatures can be stifling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huambo

Do:

  • Wear comfortable, lightweight clothing
  • Carry enough cash, as credit cards may not be accepted everywhere
  • Respect local customs, especially during festivals
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Don’t carry valuables in public as pickpocketing can be common.
  • Don’t take photographs of people or properties without explicit permission.
  • Don’t make derogatory remarks about Angola, its culture, or its politics.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huambo

Huambo is generally safe, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as:

  • Avoid displaying obvious signs of wealth
  • Avoid walking alone in secluded areas, particularly at night
  • Stick to well-lit roads and avoid shortcuts through alleyways or backstreets
  • Be aware of scams and hustlers

Health in Huambo

Visitors to Huambo should take standard health precautions, including:

  • Ensuring that all vaccinations are up to date
  • Drinking bottled water to avoid waterborne diseases
  • Using insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites that can carry illnesses such as malaria

Getting to Huambo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huambo

The nearest airport to Huambo is Albano Machado Airport, located about 8 km from the city center. Some airlines that fly to Albano Machado include:

  • TAAG Angola Airlines
  • Ethiopian Airlines
  • South African Airways
  • Turkish Airlines

Bus Stations Near Huambo

Huambo has several bus stations that offer service to nearby towns and cities, including:

  • Rodoviario Huambo
  • Huambo Expresso

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars, taxis, or rental cars to travel around the city and its environs.

How to Get Around in Huambo

The most convenient way to get around Huambo is by taxi, with fares depending on the distance traveled. Visitors can also take minivans, called “candongueiros,” which are cheaper but can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Huambo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Huambo include:

  • Lubango: A scenic town located about 170 km south of Huambo, featuring Tundavala Gap
  • Benguela: A coastal city located about 300 km west of Huambo, featuring stunning beaches, colonial architecture, and museums.
  • Namibe: A city featuring beautiful desert landscapes, located about 500 km south of Huambo.

Conclusion

Huambo is an excellent destination for tourists looking for an authentic African experience. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty, there is something for everyone in Huambo. The above guide provides insights into the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay, making your trip planning easier. Nevertheless, travelers should always take care of their safety and health while exploring this beautiful destination.
