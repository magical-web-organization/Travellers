Introduction to Lubango

Nestled in the heart of Southwest Angola, Lubango is a charming city that beckons travelers with its inviting blend of natural wonders and cultural attractions. Known as “the forgotten city,” Lubango was once a thriving hub for coffee and diamond production, but it suffered immensely during the civil war that ravaged the country for decades. Today, Lubango is enjoying a renaissance, thanks to its rich heritage, stunning landscapes, and welcoming locals.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Lubango has something to offer. With this comprehensive guide to Lubango, you’ll discover the top things to do and see, along with practical tips and insider recommendations to make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Lubango

To make the most of your trip to Lubango, we’ve compiled a list of must-visit destinations you won’t want to miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or a culture vulture, Lubango has plenty of fascinating landmarks and museums to explore.

Tunda Vala Fissure

This natural wonder is one of the city’s most iconic landmarks, a massive split in the rock that you can venture through on foot or by car. It offers spectacular views of the landscape, including the surrounding mountains, valleys, and rivers.

Christ the King statue

At 26 meters tall, the Christ the King statue is one of Angola’s tallest religious monuments. It’s a striking sight, perched atop a hill overlooking the city, and is illuminated at night for an even more dramatic effect.

Lubango Cathedral

Built in the early 20th century, Lubango Cathedral is an impressive neo-Gothic structure that has become a symbol of the city’s rebirth. The cathedral’s ornate interiors include beautiful stained glass windows, high ceilings, and towering pillars.

Ebro Observatory

Famous for its impressive telescope, the Ebro Observatory is a fascinating site for astronomers, astrophysicists, and curious travelers alike. It’s located on a hilltop outside the city, offering stunning views of the sky and the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Lubango: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lubango is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, from mountains and waterfalls to forests and caves. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit.

Serra da Leba Pass

One of the most famous routes in Angola, Serra da Leba Pass is a winding road that zigzags through the stunning mountain range. Along the way, you’ll be treated to magnificent views of the valley below, dotted with colorful fields, farms, and small villages.

Tundavala Gap

Tundavala Gap is another incredible natural formation, a vast canyon that offers breathtaking views of the valley below. It’s a popular spot for hiking, picnicking, and camping, and is also home to a small village where you can learn about the local culture and traditions.

Humpata Plateau

The Humpata Plateau is a vast expanse of highland grasslands that offers some of the best views in Angola. From here, you can see the Cunene River, the mountains, and the desert. The plateau is also rich in wildlife, including antelopes, wildebeest, and zebras.

Tchitundo-Hulu Rock Engravings

For those interested in Archeology, Tchitundo-Hulu is a must-visit site. These ancient rock engraving are considered to be some of the most important historic sites in Angola, dating back approximately 2,000 years ago, and offer insight into the life and culture of Angola’s early inhabitants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lubango

For outdoor enthusiasts, Lubango offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. Here are some of the top routes to explore.

Serra da Leba Trail

The Serra da Leba Trail is a popular hiking route that takes you through stunning landscapes, including waterfalls, forests, and hills. The trail is considered moderately difficult and is suitable for hikers with some experience.

Humpata Plateau Hike

The Humpata Plateau Hike is another popular route that takes you through rolling hills, colorful fields, and small villages. It’s a fantastic way to experience the local culture and learn about the traditional ways of life in Angola.

Tundavala Gap Bike Route

The Tundavala Gap Bike Route is a relatively easy ride that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valley. It’s a great way to explore the region and experience its natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Lubango: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with friends and family, Lubango has plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy. Here are some of our top recommendations.

Visit the Lubango Shoprite and Mercado de Capulana

These markets feature a vast range of locally-produced products and handicrafts. Whether you want to bring home a souvenir or try some local cuisine, these markets are the perfect place to explore and experience the local culture and traditions.

Take a Walking Tour of Lubango

If you enjoy walking, there’s no better way to see the city than with a guided walking tour. Discover hidden gems such as street art, specialty coffee shops or stop by popular spots like a local craft market and the local food stalls.

Enjoy a Sunset Over Tundavala Gap

Sunsets in Lubango are a thing of beauty, and the best place to experience one is at Tundavala Gap. As the sun sets, the valley below is bathed in a warm glow, creating a truly breathtaking sight.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lubango

With so much to see and do in Lubango, it can be challenging to plan your trip. Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit.

Day 1 - Arrive in Lubango and explore the city center, including Lubango Cathedral, the Fort of São Bento, and Mercado de Capulana.

Day 2 - Head out of the city to the Serra da Leba Pass, where you can hike or bike through stunning landscapes and enjoy panoramic views of the valley below.

Day 3 - Visit the Ebro Observatory for a fascinating glimpse into the cosmos, then head to Cristo Rei for breathtaking views of the city.

Day 4 - Take a guided tour of the Tchitundo-Hulu rock engravings to discover Angola’s rich cultural heritage.

Day 5 - Embark on a hiking trip to Tundavala Gap, enjoying the scenic beauty of the valley below.

Day 6 - Spend a day exploring the Humpata Plateau, with its stunning views, abundant wildlife and rolling hills.

Day 7 - Visit the Mercado Municipal for some local cuisine and souvenirs before departing Lubango.

Food and Drink Scene of Lubango

Lubango is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with a wide range of dishes and delicacies to savor. Here are some of the must-try foods and beverages you won’t want to miss.

Must-Try Dishes

Calulu

Calulu is an Angolan dish that consists of stewed fish, meat or beans, served with various vegetables, bathed with a delicious sauce and a side of funge, a porridge made from cassava flour.

Muamba Chicken

Muamba Chicken is a popular Angolan dish that can be found at most restaurants in Lubango. This dish is made with chicken, hot peppers, okra, palm oil, and spices, giving it a unique flavor.

Fish Dish

As Lubango is close to the coast, it’s no surprise that fish is a staple in its seafood dishes. Try some grilled or stewed fish at local restaurants with an array of side dishes.

Local Beverages

Coffee

Angola produces some of the best coffee in the world, and Lubango is no exception. Enjoy a cup of locally sourced coffee from the Porto Alexandre region while enjoying the majestic region.

Palm Wine

Palm wine is a traditional beverage made from fermented palm tree sap. It’s a sweet, tangy drink that’s sure to refresh you on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Lubango: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lubango offers a range of accommodation options to suit every need and budget. Here are some of our top recommendations.

Hotel Serra da Chela

Hotel Serra da Chela is a modern and elegant hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It’s conveniently located near the city center and is a great choice for those who want to be close to the action.

Hotel Victoria Garden

This historical establishment is located in downtown Lubango and is one of the most famous hotels in town. It has 46 rooms, each with its private balcony, restaurant, bar, and garden perfect for relaxing after a long day of exploring the city.

Guesthouse Girassol

For a more affordable stay, Guesthouse Girassol is the perfect option. This modest guesthouse offers comfortable rooms with basic amenities and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lubango: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lubango is famous for its handicrafts, including woven baskets, pottery, and wood carvings. You can also find jewelry made from locally sourced gems and minerals, including diamonds, emeralds, and tourmalines.

Local Culture and Customs of Lubango

The people of Lubango are warm, friendly, and welcoming. The culture is heavily influenced by the Portuguese, with African and European traditions intertwined. It’s customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile, and it’s always best to ask permission before taking photographs of people or cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Lubango

Lubango has a dry, sunny climate year-round, with temperatures averaging between 20 and 25 degrees Celsius. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from May to October when the landscape is drier, and visibility is clearer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lubango

Do:

  • Dress comfortably for outdoor activities, particularly for hiking or biking routes.
  • Take time to learn about the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Visit local markets and shops for souvenirs.
  • Respect the environment, and avoid littering or damaging natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t travel alone at night in unknown areas, particularly outside the city.
  • Don’t buy wildlife products or contribute to illegal wildlife trafficking.
  • Don’t wander off marked routes or into restricted areas in natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lubango

Lubango is generally a safe city, but as with any destination, visitors should take precautions to stay safe. Here are some top tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, particularly in areas outside the city center.
  • Always carry a copy of your passport and travel documents with you, and keep the originals secured in the hotel safe.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, particularly in crowded areas or marketplaces.
  • Always use reputable taxi companies.

Health in Lubango

Visitors to Lubango should take precautions to protect their health, including:

  • Carry a first aid kit with basic medications and supplies, including insect repellent.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing when outdoors.
  • Drink only bottled or purified water to avoid waterborne illnesses.
  • Be aware of diseases such as malaria and get vaccinated before travel.

Getting to Lubango: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lubango

Lubango is served by the Lubango Airport (SDD), which is located about 8km north of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Lubango

There are several bus stations in Lubango, including the main bus station, where you can find connections to other cities in the region.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are also available and provide more flexibility and convenience, particularly if you’re traveling with a group or have a lot of luggage.

How to Get Around in Lubango

Lubango is a small city and easily navigable on foot, particularly in the city center. Taxis are also readily available and are a good option for longer distances or when you need to carry luggage. Motorbike taxis, or “kupapata,” are another popular form of transport.

Where to Go Next from Lubango: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more of Angola, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Namibe: This small, coastal town is famous for its stunning beaches, including the famous Baía das Tigres.

  • Menongue: Located in the southeast of Angola, Menongue is a small and quiet city that offers a glimpse into rural Angolan life.

  • Kissama National Park: Located just north of Luanda, Kissama National Park boasts an abundance of wildlife, including Elephants, Lions, and Antelope.

Conclusion

Lubango is a hidden gem, a city that has reinvented itself after a difficult past and now welcomes visitors with open arms. With its natural wonders, cultural attractions, and warm hospitality, Lubango is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. Whether you want to explore history and culture, hike through stunning landscapes, taste incredible food, or simply relax and take it all in, Lubango offers an unforgettable experience that will stay with you long after you leave.
