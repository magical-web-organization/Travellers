Introduction to Great Escarpment

Welcome to the Great Escarpment, a magnificent natural wonder located in Southwest Angola. The Great Escarpment offers visitors an opportunity to experience breathtaking views of its rugged terrain, stunning waterfalls, and wildlife. This guide will provide you with essential information to plan your trip to this spectacular destination.

List of Places to Visit in Great Escarpment

There are a wide variety of culturally and naturally significant attractions in Great Escarpment. Here are some of the must-visit places while you’re there:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tchitundo-Hulu Rock Art site
  • Cangandala National Park
  • Kalandula Waterfalls
  • Mupa National Park

Natural Attractions of Great Escarpment: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Moco
  • Bicuar National Park
  • Pedras N’gandu Waterfalls
  • Black Rocks of Pungo Andongo

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Escarpment

Great Escarpment offers an array of hiking and biking trails for all levels of adventurers. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Mount Moco Summit Trail
  • Pedras N’gandu Waterfalls Trail
  • Black Rocks of Pungo Andongo Trail
  • Cangandala National Park Trail

Best Things to Do in Great Escarpment: Activities and Tours

Great Escarpment offers visitors a chance to experience some thrilling activities and tours. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Wildlife Safari at Bicuar National Park
  • Camping at Kalandula Waterfalls
  • Rock Climbing at Black Rocks of Pungo Andongo
  • Village Tourism at Mupa National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Great Escarpment

Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine of Great Escarpment is rich in flavour and culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Chicken Muamba: Chicken cooked with various spices and served with rice
  • Calulu: Stewed fish or chicken with peanuts, cassava flour, and vegetables
  • Funje: A dough-like side dish made from cassava flour
  • Xima: A porridge made from maize flour and water

Local Beverages

Great Escarpment is famous for its colourful and refreshing drinks. Here are some of the local beverages you must try:

  • Ginguba: A peanut-flavoured beer
  • Pito: A traditional beer made from fermented maize
  • Cassava Beer: A beer made from cassava roots

Where to Stay in Great Escarpment: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Great Escarpment boasts a range of accommodation options catering to travellers of all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Victória Garden
  • Hotel Serra da Chela
  • Bicuar National Park Campsite
  • Luchaze Self-Catering Guesthouse

What to Buy in Great Escarpment: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Beautiful artisanal crafts like baskets, pottery, and textiles
  • Coffee: The region produces some of the best coffee in the world.
  • Basketry: Made from intricately woven material, these baskets are a great souvenir
  • Tribal Artifacts: A variety of colorful carvings and masks.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Escarpment

Great Escarpment is home to various historical and cultural sites, and it is essential to remain respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some noteworthy cultural experiences to have:

  • Attend traditional tribal dance ceremonies
  • Learn about the local Tchokwe culture and their artistic expression
  • Visit the historic landmarks of Ramiro and Kiangombe
  • Visit the local markets and purchase locally made crafts and souvenirs

Best Time to Visit Great Escarpment

The Great Escarpment is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from May to October, when the weather is cooler and drier. Visitors should be aware that the rainy season typically lasts from November through to April and can be quite challenging and dangerous.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Escarpment

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress in conservative clothing while visiting religious or traditional sites.
  • Keep the environment clean and free from litter
  • Learn some Portuguese phrases to enhance your communication.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt the wildlife and their habitat
  • Take photographs without seeking permission from locals
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Wander off into unknown territory without a tour guide

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Escarpment

Great Escarpment is generally considered safe for tourists. It is always wise to avoid any activity that poses a potential risk. Visitors should exercise caution when out and about and should avoid displaying signs of affluence to prevent robbery or theft.

Health in Great Escarpment

Travellers to Great Escarpment should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations. Visitors should seek medical advice from their doctor to ensure that they are adequately protected against infectious and tropical diseases. It is advisable to bring insect repellent, sunscreen and Loperamide for stomach discomforts.

Getting to Great Escarpment: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Escarpment

The closest international airports to Great Escarpment are the Quatro de Fevreiro International Airport in Luanda and Namibe Airport. Namibe airport is the closest airport to Great Escarpment, 690km away.

Bus Stations Near Great Escarpment

There are plenty of bus companies that operate within Angola. The most affordable, though slowest way to reach a destination, is by taking a bus from Luanda to Lubango.

Other Options

Another way to reach Great Escarpment is by private car or hire a taxi from Lubango.

How to Get Around in Great Escarpment

The best way to explore Great Escarpment is by private car or with a hired driver. Visitors can also choose to use public transportation, but it is often overcrowded and unreliable. Motorcycles, known as “candongueiro,” are a popular mode of transport and are most suitable for short trips.

Where to Go Next from Great Escarpment: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lubango, Angola’s 2nd Largest city
  • Namibe, a town situated on the Atlantic Coastline with beautiful beaches
  • Ombadja, a town famed for its exotic Baobab trees

Conclusion

Explore the rugged beauty and breathtaking landscapes of Great Escarpment, a unique destination in Central Africa that offers a truly authentic cultural and natural experience. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip, and make unforgettable memories in Angola’s premier destination.
