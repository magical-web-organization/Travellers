Introduction to Greater Luanda

Greater Luanda is the capital city of Angola and located in Central Africa. It is comprised of seven municipalities and is the most populated city in the country. Greater Luanda boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, there’s something for everyone in Greater Luanda.

List of Places to Visit in Greater Luanda

  • Ilha de Luanda: a beautiful island with historic architecture and stunning beaches
  • Fortaleza de São Miguel: a 16th-century fort with a museum about Angola’s history and colonization
  • National Museum of Slavery: the only museum in Angola dedicated to the history of slavery
  • Miradouro da Lua: a majestic rock formation with stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean
  • Kwanza River: a beautiful river with stunning views, fishing opportunities, and boat tours
  • Benfica Market: a bustling market with local crafts, souvenirs, and fresh produce

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of Angola

The National Museum of Angola exhibits artifacts of Angolan history including bronze sculptures from the ancient Kingdom of Kongo kingdom, and relics of Angola’s independence war. The museum is housed in a stunning 19th-century palace, with beautiful gardens and lots of shady trees.

Fortaleza de São Miguel

The Fortaleza de São Miguel is a fortress built in 1576 to defend the port of Luanda. It now acts as a museum, showcasing thick walls, cannons, and turrets, and provides valuable insights into Angola’s colonial history.

National Museum of Slavery

The National Museum of Slavery is a sobering reminder of Angola’s dark history. The museum is home to exhibits of several paintings, photographs, and sculptures related to the slave trade. It is located in Muxima, a small town about two hours from Luanda.

Natural Attractions of Greater Luanda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Miradouro da Lua

The Miradouro da Lua, or Moon Viewpoint, is a fantastic clay and rock formation that looks like a lunar landscape. The viewpoint offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean, rocky cliffs, and lush vegetation.

Kissama National Park

Kissama National Park is about 75 km south of Luanda and has recently opened its gates to tourists. The park provides eco-tourism opportunities such as game drives, walking safaris, and bird-watching. Visitors have a chance to see elephants, buffaloes, antelopes, and other wild animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greater Luanda

Cabo Ledo

Cabo is a beautiful and secluded beach which can only be reached by foot or bike. Adventurers can navigate sandy dunes and remote villages whilst heading to the stunning beachfront.

Avenida Marginal

This is a six-kilometre long walkway with panoramic views of Luanda Bay. Avenida Marginal is perfect for walkers and runners looking to burn some calories whilst taking in the coastal views.

Best Things to Do in Greater Luanda: Activities and Tours

Explore the Beaches

Greater Luanda is home to many beautiful and secluded beaches. Easily accessible beaches are Kinaxixe Beach and Cabo Ledo, but Nanuka Beach is particularly well-known for its excellent surfing sessions.

Explore the local markets

Luanda is home to thriving local markets filled with crafts, souvenirs, fresh foods, and local experiences. Benfica Market is the most famous and is located near upmarket neighbourhoods of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Greater Luanda

Must-Try Dishes

Angolan cuisine is robust and spicy, a mix of African and Portuguese flavors. Here are some must-try dishes to include on the itinerary:

  • Calulu: a stew featuring dried fish, okra, onions, and greens, cooked in palm oil
  • Moamba de Galinha: a chicken dish served with a spicy, African-inspired sauce
  • Farofa: a staple side dish, consisting of toasted cassava flour with onions, olive oil, garlic, and spices
  • Papas de Milho: a traditional Angolan porridge made from maize flour and served with a variety of different foods.

Local Beverages

Angola boasts flavorful and vibrant drinks that will complement any meal. Here are some local drinks to explore:

  • Cuca Beer: the go-to beer for locals and tourists alike
  • Laranjada: a must-try refreshing soft drink blended with orange juice
  • Ginguba: an alcoholic drink made with peanut rum

Where to Stay in Greater Luanda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greater Luanda has several accommodations from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Skyna Luanda: a contemporary and affordable hotel with spacious rooms and delicious dining options.
  • Epic Sana: a luxurious 5-star hotel offering a swimming pool, fitness centre, and excellent dining options
  • Solar de Condes de Resende: a cozy guesthouse located in the historic centre of Luanda

What to Buy in Greater Luanda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Benfica Market offers a wide range of locally-made products and souvenirs such as wood carvings, clothes, fabrics, baskets, and jewelry. Here are other souvenir ideas to bring home:

  • Coffee Beans: Angolan coffee beans are excellent, with a balanced combination of acidity and sweetness.
  • Handmade Crafts: Beautiful traditional crafts are available for purchase such as pottery, wooden carvings, and souvenirs with the Angolan flag.

Local Culture and Customs of Greater Luanda

The people of Angola are friendly and hospitable, and their culture is deeply rooted in tradition. Visitors should dress modestly in public places, particularly to religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Greater Luanda

The best time to visit Greater Luanda is between June and November. The weather is cooler during June to August, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and wildlife tours. In November, the rain will begin, but this period presents a unique opportunity to witness bird migrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greater Luanda

Do:

  • Try to learn some Portuguese, as while English is spoken in some tourist locations, Portuguese is the official language.
  • Bring mosquito repellent; mosquitos are known to carry malaria in the country.
  • Drink plenty of fluids, especially if you’re out in the sun.

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or flaunt lots of cash in public to avoid being a target of theft.
  • Smoke in public spaces, it is illegal in Angola, and fines may be imposed.
  • Take photos of people without asking permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greater Luanda

As with most cities, certain areas may be considered unsafe. It is best to avoid walking alone in the city centre at night or in unfamiliar neighbourhoods. Visitors should be careful with public transport, as there may be pickpocket attacks. It is advisable to avoid travelling alone to remote areas, especially after dark.

Health in Greater Luanda

Visitors should consider getting vaccinations against common diseases, including yellow fever, cholera, hepatitis A and B, typhoid fever, and rabies. Visitors should also bring along their required medications, sunscreen, insect repellent, and stay hydrated.

Getting to Greater Luanda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greater Luanda

The best airport to connect flights to Greater Luanda can be found at Quatro de Fevereiro International Airport. This airport has regular flights, making it the busiest in the country. It is located just outside of the city, however, and visitors will have to catch a taxi or shuttle to get to the centre of Luanda.

Bus Stations Near Greater Luanda

The main bus station is Rodo Luanda that connects visitors to several other towns, however, it is not considered as reliable for tourists. Transport services from local minibuses are often the cheapest and easiest options for travelling within the city.

Other Options

Angola’s transport system continues to expand and improve, with better infrastructure planned. It is recommended to hire a tour guide before travelling to Angola to ensure good transportation options during your trip.

How to Get Around in Greater Luanda

The most popular mode of transport in Greater Luanda is taxi services, which can be found throughout the city. It’s imperative to remember that always agreeing on a fare before starting a ride is essential. Alternative options such as motorcycle or bike-hire may also be available, although bike options are limited to the bay walk strip or tour companies outside of the city.

Where to Go Next from Greater Luanda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Luanda’s prime location in Angola allows for easy access to fantastic road trip experiences. Here are three destinations that can be reached within a single day:

  • Caxito: Known as the “Green City,” Caxito is the capital of the Bengo Province, and it features plenty of watersports and tourist attractions.
  • Sumbe: Sumbe is known for its seafood and variety of beaches.
  • Kwanza Sul: Kwanza Sul is the largest province of Angola with lakes, mountains, and valleys.

Conclusion

Greater Luanda is a travel destination that caters to multiple interests, from visiting its beaches to exploring its historical sites, museums, and national parks. By following the tips and recommendations provided in this guide, visitors can get the most out of their trip to Greater Luanda, Angola.
