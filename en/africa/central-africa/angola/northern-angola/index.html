Introduction to Northern Angola

Northern Angola is a beautiful region with a deeply rich culture and history. The region is inhabited by various ethnic groups, including Ambundu, Kikongo, Chokwe, and Ovimbundu. It is a fantastic travel destination with breathtaking views, rich wildlife, and fascinating historical sites.

In this tour guide, we will explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, the food and drink scene, the local culture and customs, safety and health, and transportation options in Northern Angola.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Angola

Here are the top places you don’t want to miss in Northern Angola:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Anthropology is located in Luanda, Angola’s capital city. It is home to numerous ethnographic and archeological artifacts that offer an insight into the country’s traditional cultural practices.
  • Fortress of São Miguel located in Luanda is a UNESCO world heritage site known for its historical significance. It was originally built by the Portuguese in 1575 to protect the city from attacks.
  • Kalandula Falls, situated in the Kalandula municipality, is one of Angola’s most famous tourist destinations. It is the second most voluminous waterfall in Africa (after Victoria Falls) and is a sight to behold.

Natural Attractions of Northern Angola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cangandala National Park, located in the Malanje province, offers a fantastic opportunity for wildlife viewing. The park is home to various endemic and endangered species, including the giant sable antelope.
  • Quiçama National Park, situated in the Bengo province, is Angola’s largest national park. It offers fantastic game-viewing opportunities, including elephants, lions, antelopes, and hippos.
  • Cabinda Province is located in the northwestern part of Angola, bordering the Republic of Congo and the Democratic Republic of Congo. It offers a peaceful coastal environment, fresh seafood dishes, and an opportunity to witness the traditional ways of life of some ethnic groups in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Angola

Northern Angola offers a range of hiking and biking routes for travelers.

  • Pungo Andongo Route, located in the Malanje province, is a 10km circular route that takes hikers through breathtaking landscapes.
  • Kalandula Falls Route, located in the Kalandula municipality, offers a 20km hiking trail from the base of the falls to the Quinaxixe River.
  • Quiçama National Park Route, located in Bengo province, offers an opportunity to bike through the park, enjoying spectacular views of wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Northern Angola: Activities and Tours

Northern Angola offers a range of activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Game drives in Quiçama National Park offer a fantastic opportunity to see Angola’s amazing wildlife.
  • Visit the Cabo Ledo Beach situated 120 km southwest of Luanda has a beautiful sandy coastline offering an excellent picturesque view of the ocean.
  • Angola Railway Tour, is a seven-day excursion from Luanda to Malanje, it offers passengers with an opportunity to witness panoramic vistas and the cave paintings of Tchitundu-Hulu.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Angola

Angolan cuisine is rich and diverse, with influences from Portugal, Brazil, and Africa. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Northern Angola:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moamba de Galinha is a traditional dish made from chicken, palm oil, and fresh vegetables.
  • Calulu is a dish made from fish or chicken stew with vegetables, often served with funge, a type of cornmeal porridge.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine is a traditional drink made from fermented palm tree sap.
  • Tchinda is a drink made from a mixture of maize, sorghum, and cassava flour.

Where to Stay in Northern Angola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Angola has a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Talatona Convention Hotel located in Luanda, is a five-star hotel featuring an outdoor pool and a fitness center.
  • Hotel Camões Malange, located in the Malanje province has affordable rooms, swimming facilities, and onsite bar and restaurant.
  • Cabila Punta Negra guest house, located in Cabinda Province, offers ocean view accommodations close to the beach.

What to Buy in Northern Angola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Angola has many locally made products that you can take home as souvenirs. Some of the best include:

  • Capulana is a traditional fabric used to make clothing, bags, and blankets.
  • Bonga Oil used for skin care and cosmetic, Also used for cooking and dressing salads.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Angola

Northern Angola is an ethnically diverse region with many cultural practices, customs, and traditions. Some of the most notable include:

  • The Umbundu people, the largest ethnic group in the region, are famous for their social organization and dance traditions.
  • The Kisama Museum, located in Luanda, displays collections of traditional artifacts and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Northern Angola

The weather in northern Angola is tropical, with distinct dry and rainy seasons. The best time to visit Northern Angola is during the dry season that falls between May and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Angola

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts when traveling to Northern Angola:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases to help you communicate with locals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting historical and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Display wealth or valuable possessions, which can attract unwanted attention.
  • Take photographs, it’s illegal to take photographs of government buildings, streets, and bridges.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Angola

Northern Angola has historically been an unsafe region due to civil unrest, military conflicts, and landmines. However, the situation has improved significantly over the last few years. Travelers are advised to stay vigilant and aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Northern Angola

Northern Angola has a high prevalence of water-borne diseases like cholera and typhoid fever. Travelers are advised to drink bottled water and avoid eating raw foods. It is also recommended to seek medical advice before traveling.

Getting to Northern Angola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Angola

The nearest airport to Northern Angola is Quatro de Fevereiro International Airport, located in Luanda.

Bus Stations Near Northern Angola

The major bus station in Luanda is Luanda Provincial Bus Terminal covering both local and international destinations.

Other Options

The railway system in Angola connects the capital city, Luanda, with other towns and cities, however, It is often unreliable.

How to Get Around in Northern Angola

The most convenient way to get around Northern Angola is by car rental. There are car rental services available in Luanda & its province including a shuttle service. Also, for travelers who are feeling more adventurous, motorbike rental is also available.

Where to Go Next from Northern Angola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Angola is a vast country with many attractions outside the Northern region. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Benguela province is situated southwest of Angola, long and historic coastline as well as mountains and parks.
  • Lubango province Situated south-western Angola offers breathtaking views like the viewpoint of the Tundavala and Natural Parks.

Conclusion

Northern Angola offers a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking landscapes, wildlife, and adventure opportunities. With careful planning and preparation, travelers can have a fantastic experience exploring this fantastic region of Angola.
