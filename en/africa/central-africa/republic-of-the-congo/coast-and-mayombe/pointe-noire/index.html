Introduction to Pointe-Noire

Located in the southern part of Republic of the Congo, Pointe-Noire is a port city and gateway to the country’s coastline known for its beautiful beaches and warm climate. The city is often referred to as the country’s economic capital, and it’s a hub for trade and industry. Despite its bustling nature, Pointe-Noire is also rich in culture, history, and nature. This travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, how to get around, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Pointe-Noire

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Congo Beach Hotel

The Congo Beach Hotel is located along the beautiful Congo coast with stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. This historical landmark dates to the 1960s and features elegant architecture and decor that take you back in time. The iconic hotel is also home to a renowned restaurant, a bar, and an art gallery.

The Pierre Savorgnan de Brazza Memorial

Located in the city center, this memorial honors the founder of Brazzaville and some of the country’s most significant historical figures. The museum features a vast collection of artifacts, including weapons, traditional art, and photographs.

The Mâ Loango Museum

The Mâ Loango Museum is a fascinating museum dedicated to the preservation of the local culture, traditions, and heritage of the Kouilou region. The museum showcases a collection of sculptures, fabrics, and traditional musical instruments.

Natural Attractions of Pointe-Noire: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lesio-Louna Nature Reserve

This nature reserve is located north of the city and covers over 500 square kilometers of rainforest. It’s home to various endemic species, including gorillas, chimpanzees, and mandrills. Visitors can take guided tours of the park, learn about the conservation efforts, and enjoy hiking trails.

Loango National Park

Loango National Park is a pristine park located on the coastline of the Atlantic Ocean. The park is home to various species of wildlife, including elephants, buffalos, and hippos. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, hiking trails, and birdwatching.

The Coastal Road

The coastal road that stretches from Pointe-Noire to the border of Angola is an excellent way to explore the country’s coastline. The route is characterized by stunning beaches, fishing villages, and beautiful landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pointe-Noire

Gorges of Diosso

Located just a few kilometers from Pointe-Noire, the Gorges of Diosso is an excellent spot for hiking and picnicking. The area is defined by stunning landscapes, rich vegetation, and beautiful waterfalls.

Plateau des Cinq Chutes

The Plateau des Cinq Chutes is a beautiful hiking route that takes visitors through some of the country’s best scenic landscapes. Hikers can expect to encounter beautiful waterfalls, dense vegetation, and rare wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Pointe-Noire: Activities and Tours

Congo River and Beach Excursion

This guided tour takes visitors through the Congo River and ends at one of the most beautiful beaches in Pointe-Noire. Along the way, visitors can enjoy stunning views of the coastline, spot dolphins and whales, and learn about the local culture and history.

City Tour

A City tour is an excellent way to explore Pointe-Noire’s culture and history. Visitors can take guided tours of the city’s landmarks, museums, and markets. They can also taste local cuisine and interact with the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Pointe-Noire

Must-Try Dishes

Poulet à la Moambé

Chicken cooked in a spicy sauce made of palm nut cream, spinach, cassava leaves, and onions.

Fufu and Ndombolo

A traditional Central African dish made of cassava flour dough and an entrée of boiled beans.

Local Beverages

Primus Beer

Primus beer is the most popular beer in Republic of the Congo, and it’s served in several bars and restaurants throughout Pointe-Noire. The beer is light and easy to drink.

Palm Wine

Palm Wine is a popular alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It’s served fresh and fermented.

Where to Stay in Pointe-Noire: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Radisson Blu M’Bamou Palace Hotel

Located in the heart of Pointe-Noire, Radisson Blu M’Bamou Palace hotel features luxury rooms, a swimming pool, a fitness center, a restaurant, and a bar.

Atlantic Palace Hotel

Atlantic Palace Hotel is an affordable alternative to the more expensive hotels in the city center. The hotel features rooms, a swimming pool, a restaurant, and a bar.

Residence de la Pointe

Residence de la Pointe is a comfortable guesthouse located on the coast. The guesthouse features spacious rooms, stunning views of the ocean, and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Pointe-Noire: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenirs

Traditional Artworks

Pointe-Noire is home to several communities that produce traditional artwork. The artwork includes masks, sculptures, and textiles.

Handicrafts

Handicrafts such as baskets, pottery, and jewelry make for great souvenirs.

Local Products

Palm Oil

Palm oil is a popular ingredient in Congo’s cuisine. Visitors can buy fresh palm oil in local markets.

Cassava Flour

Cassava flour is a staple food in Central Africa. It’s used to make Fufu, one of the local delicacies.

Local Culture and Customs of Pointe-Noire

Pointe-Noire is a diverse city with several ethnic groups and religions. The predominant religion in the city is Christianity, followed by Islam. Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Pointe-Noire

The best time to visit Pointe-Noire is from June to September when the weather is sunny and dry. The rainy season starts in October and ends in May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pointe-Noire

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Try local cuisine
  • Learn about the culture

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without their permission
  • Politely refuse offers from strangers in some parts of the city

Safety and Health

Safety in Pointe-Noire

Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and tourist should take extra precautions due to the high risk of crime in the city. Visitors are advised to take taxis at night and avoid being out after dark.

Health in Pointe-Noire

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated for Yellow fever, Malaria, Typhoid, and other diseases before visiting Pointe-Noire. Visitors should also exercise caution when consuming local food and water.

Getting to Pointe-Noire: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pointe-Noire

Pointe-Noire Airport is located less than 5 km from the city center. Visitors can take taxis to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Pointe-Noire

Pointe-Noire has several bus stations, and visitors can take buses to several destinations in the city and other parts of the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also take ferries and private chartered flights to Pointe-Noire.

How to Get Around in Pointe-Noire

Visitors can use taxis, rental cars, and bikes to get around the city. Taxis are the most popular mode of transport but should be booked in advance.

Where to Go Next from Pointe-Noire: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brazzaville

Brazzaville is the capital city of Republic of the Congo and is located approximately 500 km east of Pointe-Noire. Brazzaville features several museums, landmarks, and cultural attractions.

Kinshasa

Kinshasa is the capital city of the neighboring Democratic Republic of the Congo and is located across the Congo River from Brazzaville. Visitors can take a ferry to Kinshasa and explore its cultural attractions and landmarks.

Conclusion

Pointe-Noire is a beautiful destination that offers visitors the opportunity to explore its natural and cultural attractions. Visitors can enjoy outdoor adventures, learn about the tradition and customs, taste local cuisine, and interact with the locals. Use this travel guide to plan your next trip to Pointe-Noire, and discover all the city has to offer.
