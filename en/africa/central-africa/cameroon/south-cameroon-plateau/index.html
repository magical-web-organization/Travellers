Introduction to South Cameroon Plateau

South Cameroon Plateau is located in the southwestern part of Cameroon, in Central Africa. It is a highland region with diverse landscapes, from lush tropical rainforests to rolling grasslands and volcanic mountains. The plateau is home to various ethnic groups, and this has contributed to a rich and vibrant cultural experience for visitors. South Cameroon Plateau offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure activities that will leave any traveler mesmerized.

List of Places to Visit in South Cameroon Plateau

Here are some of the top places you should add to your itinerary when visiting South Cameroon Plateau:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bafut Palace: This historic palace is one of the most significant cultural attractions in Cameroon. It was built in the 18th century and is home to the Bafut people, one of the largest ethnic groups in the region. Visitors can learn about the history, culture, and traditions of the Bafut people and see the intricate carvings and decorations of the palace.
  • The Mankon Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the traditions and cultural heritage of the Mankon people. It features a collection of artifacts, handicrafts, and traditional clothing that showcase the history and way of life of the Mankon people.
  • Mount Cameroon: This volcanic mountain is the highest peak in West Africa and a favorite destination for hiking enthusiasts. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of South Cameroon Plateau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Korup National Park: It is one of the most biodiverse regions in Africa, with dense rainforests, rivers, and waterfalls. The park is home to various animal species, including gorillas, chimpanzees, elephants, and over 400 species of birds.
  • Lobe Waterfalls: Lobe Waterfalls is located near Kribi, a coastal town in South Cameroon. It is a stunning waterfall that drops from a 50m high cliff directly into the Atlantic Ocean below.
  • Bomana Beach: This beach is situated near Limbe and offers a serene retreat from the bustling city life. It is ideal for beach lovers and offers panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Cameroon Plateau

South Cameroon Plateau is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes:

  • The Mount Cameroon Trail: This trail leads to the summit of Mount Cameroon and takes around 3-4 days to complete. Along the way, hikers will pass through rainforests, savannas, and volcanic ash fields, and enjoy fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Korup National Park Hiking Trail: Visitors can explore the dense rainforests of Korup National Park along designated hiking trails, with experienced guides leading the way.
  • The Lobe Waterfalls Trek: This trail takes visitors through scenic rainforests to see the stunning Lobe Waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in South Cameroon Plateau: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in South Cameroon Plateau:

  • Take a Cultural Tour: Visitors can explore the rich cultural heritage of the region, visit historic sites and learn about the customs and traditions of the local people.
  • Visit the National Parks: South Cameroon Plateau boasts several national parks, offering visitors the opportunity to see a vast array of flora and fauna.
  • Explore the Beaches: Visitors can relax on the pristine beaches, enjoy watersports or take a dip in the Atlantic Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of South Cameroon Plateau

Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages you should sample when visiting South Cameroon Plateau:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ndole: This is a signature Cameroonian dish that consists of stewed bitter leaves, peanuts, and meat or fish.
  • Eru: This is a popular vegetable soup made with beef or fish, and it is served with plantains or fufu.
  • Grilled Fish: Fresh fish is a staple in South Cameroon Plateau, and it is usually served grilled with a side of spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is a popular alcoholic beverage in Cameroon, and it is made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Hibiscus Tea: This tea is made from the flowers of the hibiscus plant and is popular across Cameroon.
  • Bissap: This beverage is made from the dried calyxes of the hibiscus sabdariffa plant and is served cold.

  • Le Paradisier Hotel Kribi: This beachfront hotel is located in Kribi and offers visitors stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

What to Buy in South Cameroon Plateau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting South Cameroon Plateau, here are some of the products you should consider bringing home:

  • Wooden Masks and Carvings: The region is known for its intricate wood carvings, and visitors can purchase masks, statues, and other decorative items.
  • Beaded Jewelry: The local markets offer a range of beaded jewelry, from necklaces and bracelets to earrings and rings.
  • Bags and Baskets: Hand-woven bags and baskets made from local materials like raffia, palm leaves, and bamboo are another popular item to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of South Cameroon Plateau

South Cameroon Plateau is home to over 50 ethnic groups, each with its unique culture and customs. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as shaking hands with the elders or asking for permission before taking photographs. Visitors should also dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit South Cameroon Plateau

The best time to visit South Cameroon Plateau is during the dry season, which runs from November to February. The weather is pleasant during this time, and visitors can enjoy hiking trails and other outdoor activities without the risk of being rained out.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Cameroon Plateau

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting South Cameroon Plateau:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Carry water and a first-aid kit when hiking or exploring the national parks.
  • Learn some French or local languages like Pidgin to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of people or their property without their permission.
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs, traditions, or religious beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Cameroon Plateau

South Cameroon Plateau is considered safe for visitors, but it’s always advisable to exercise caution and take necessary precautions. Visitors should avoid traveling at night, stick to designated trails when hiking, and stay alert in crowded areas. It’s advisable to check travel alerts before traveling to the region.

Health in South Cameroon Plateau

Visitors to South Cameroon Plateau should ensure that they get vaccinated against yellow fever before arriving as it’s a requirement for entry. They should also carry mosquito repellent as the region has high malaria cases. Visitors should keep hydrated, avoid ice and uncooked food, and carry a first aid kit when heading out on trails. Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any unpleasant symptoms.

Getting to South Cameroon Plateau: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to South Cameroon Plateau from different parts of Cameroon and the world, including:

Airports Near South Cameroon Plateau

The Yaounde Nsimalen International Airport offers international flights, and the Douala International Airport offers domestic and international flights. Visitors can take a connecting flight or a cab from the airport to their destination.

Bus Stations Near South Cameroon Plateau

The region has several bus terminals like Dschang, which offers transport to all parts of the region. The transport buses offer services like intercity and cross-border transportation.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private driver, rent a car, or use ride-sharing apps to get to their destination. Make sure to negotiate prices with the vendor before commencing any trip.

How to Get Around in South Cameroon Plateau

Visitors can get around South Cameroon Plateau using various transportation modes. There are buses and minivans that offer intercity transportation. There are taxi services and ride-sharing services that are also available in the region. Visitors may also opt to rent a car or a motorbike from rental operators.

Where to Go Next from South Cameroon Plateau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Cameroon Plateau offers several popular destinations to visitors. Here are some recommended nearby destinations:

  • Kribi: This coastal town lies on the Gulf of Guinea and offers beaches, waterfalls, and a lighthouse.
  • Limbe: Limbe is known as the ‘Botanical Garden City’ and is a welcoming coastal town edged with thrilling waterfalls and black sandy beaches.
