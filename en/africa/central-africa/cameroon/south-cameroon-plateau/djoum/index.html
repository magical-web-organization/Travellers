Introduction to Djoum

Located in the South Cameroon Plateau region, Djoum is a small town with a rich culture and history. It is known for its stunning landscapes, natural attractions, and unique cultural experiences. Djoum is filled with exciting activities for all types of travelers, from hiking and wildlife watching to exploring local markets and museum exhibits.

List of Places to Visit in Djoum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Djoum is a town steeped in history and culture, and there are several sites that visitors should not miss. The Mvet museum displays traditional masks and other cultural artifacts from the region, providing insight into the local way of life. The wooden palace of the local chieftain, or “Fon”, is also an essential visit, showcasing the town’s traditional architecture and housing a mini-museum that details the history of Djoum.

Natural Attractions of Djoum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Djoum boasts stunning natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Dja Reserve, in the nearby forest, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and home to various wildlife like forest elephants, gorillas, chimpanzees, mandrills, and other animals. You can go for a guided nature walk, see various bird species, or go on a game drive through Dja Reserve. The Lake Oguemoue is also a popular attraction, with scenic views and opportunities for fishing, bird watching and boat rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Djoum

Djoum offers great opportunities for hiking and biking. There are guided mountain biking tours through the spectacular landscapes of the region, in addition to more leisurely bike rides around the town. The forests around Djoum offer captivating hikes; adventure to discover wildlife, rock formations and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Djoum: Activities and Tours

Guided bird-watching tours are a popular activity, where you have the chance to spot various bird species in the region. Take a cultural heritage tour of the town, explore behind the religious sacred places, meet local artisans and learn about their crafts or embark on a wildlife-watching experience. Other activities include visiting botanical gardens, shopping for souvenirs, and getting to know the local communities’ way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Djoum

Day 1: Arrive in Djoum and settle into accommodation. Day 2: Take a cultural heritage tour of the town and visit the Mvet Museum. Day 3: Venture out into the Dja Reserve for a wildlife-watching experience. Day 4: Go on a guided hike or bike tour through the forests of Djoum. Day 5: Visit the wooden palace of the Fon and explore the mini-museum inside. Day 6: Take a boat ride, fish or engage in bird watching on Lake Oguemoue. Day 7: Depart Djoum and move on to the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Djoum

Must-Try Dishes

The food in Djoum is a blend of traditional African and European cuisine. Some must-try dishes include Ndole, cooked with peanuts or palm butter and an array of greens, Fufu and Eru, a traditional combination of cassava and plantain with spinach-like leaves.

Local Beverages

Local brews such as “palm wine” and “matango” are must-copies toasting to the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Where to Stay in Djoum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodations in Djoum cover a variety of budgets and preferences, and the town has various campsites, hotels, lodges, cottages, and guesthouses. Stay at Dja Ecolodge is an excellent option, with comfortable rooms, various activities, and a restaurant on site.

What to Buy in Djoum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No visit to Djoum is complete without a visit to the local markets, where visitors can find an array of products from intricate traditional crafts to organic foods and fresh produce. Other items that make perfect souvenirs include local fabrics, wooden carvings, and pottery. Don’t forget to pick up some of the locally produced products like palm wine or matango.

Local Culture and Customs of Djoum

The people of Djoum are colorful, and their culture and customs are essential parts of the town’s heritage. Be respectful when visiting local communities and adhere to their customs and traditions. Women should avoid wearing revealing clothing and wear a wrap (sarong) to cover their legs when entering sacred areas or cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Djoum

The dry season, from November to March, is the best time to visit Djoum, when many activities like bird watching, hiking, biking, and game drives are possible. In the running season, lots of outdoor activities are often unavailable or require advance bookings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Djoum

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothing and shoes.
  • Respect the local traditions and cultures.
  • Carry an insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local beliefs and customs.
  • Engage in activities that harm local wildlife, like hunting or damage habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Djoum

Djoum is relatively safe, but visitors should take precautions when moving around the region. Be careful when walking at night and avoid carrying valuables in crowded areas.

Health in Djoum

Visitors to Djoum should make sure they have all the required vaccinations before arriving in Cameroon. Drink bottled water and avoid street food that may cause stomach upsets.

Getting to Djoum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Djoum

Djoum doesn’t have an airport, but the closest airport is Nkongsamba Airport in Limbe, which is about 3 hours away. Flights come in various times a week; mainly from the capital city, Yaoundé.

Bus Stations Near Djoum

Buses are the most popular way to get to Djoum. Visiting Douala or Yaoundé, you can catch a bus and make your way down to Djoum. There are regular bus routes that ply this route.

Other Options

Visitors can opt to hire private cars or taxis to travel to Djoum from surrounding towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Djoum

Getting around Djoum’s small town is best done on foot, with local guides or taxis available to take visitors to various attractions outside the town.

Where to Go Next from Djoum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Limbe and Yaoundé are nearby cities, where visitors can continue their journey exploring the vast beauty of Cameroon, including taking on hiking expeditions, visiting more cultural attractions or National parks like the Lobeke National Park.

Conclusion

Djoum is an incredible destination with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural environs. Visitors can make the most of their time in Djoum by exploring museums, engaging in outdoor activities, enjoying local food and drinks, and interacting with friendly locals. Ensure to observe the customs while enjoying the attraction and have a memorable experience.
