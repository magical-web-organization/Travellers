Introduction to Cameroon

Cameroon is a country located in Central Africa, bordered by Nigeria to the west, Chad to the northeast, the Central African Republic to the east, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, and the Republic of Congo to the south. It is known for its diverse culture, natural beauty, wildlife, and vibrant music scene. Cameroon has something for everyone, from beach lovers to adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Cameroon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée National de Yaoundé: This museum showcases the history, art, and culture of Cameroon.
  • Bafia Art Center: A great place to buy authentic souvenirs and arts and crafts that reflect the local culture.
  • Lamidats of the North: These are traditional palace complexes worth visiting, located in the northern part of Cameroon.
  • Bafut Palace: Another beautiful traditional palace located in the western part of Cameroon.
  • Duala Edea: A beautiful city worth visiting, where you can learn about the colonial history of Cameroon.

Natural Attractions of Cameroon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Cameroon: The highest point in West Africa and a popular destination among hikers and climbers.
  • Rhumsiki Peak: A scenic rock formation and agricultural village located in the northern part of Cameroon.
  • Waza National Park: A popular wildlife reserve in the northern part of Cameroon that is home to elephants, giraffes, lions, and many more animals.
  • Kribi Beach: A beautiful coastal town located in the southern part of Cameroon, famous for its beaches, waterfalls, and seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cameroon

  • Mount Cameroon: One of the most challenging hikes in Cameroon, that can take up to four days to complete.
  • Hiking in the Mandara Mountains: A difficult hike that offers beautiful views of the landscapes and the local culture.
  • Biking along the Cameroon-Nigeria Border: A scenic bike route with stunning views of the landscape and passing through local villages.

Best Things to Do in Cameroon: Activities and Tours

  • Take a tour of the Limbe Wildlife Centre and Botanic Garden.
  • Visit the Ekom-Nkam Waterfall and have a picnic.
  • Take a Wildlife Safari tour in Waza National Park.
  • Go kayaking or surfing along the Kribi beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Cameroon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ndole: A classic dish made with bitter leaf, peanuts, and meat.
  • Koki: A tasty dish made with black-eyed peas, plantains and spinach.
  • Mbongo: A spicy stew made with chicken, spices, tomatoes, and palm oil.
  • Soya: A popular street food made with grilled skewered meat seasoned with spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: A popular traditional alcoholic drink made from the sap of various species of palm trees.
  • Bissap: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Cameroon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel Mont Fébé, Yaoundé: A luxury hotel with excellent facilities and breathtaking views of Mount Cameroon.
  • Hôtel des Deputes, Douala: A budget-friendly accommodation in Cameroon’s business capital city.
  • Reps Backpacker Hostel, Bamenda: A great hostel for backpackers in the northwestern part of Cameroon.
  • Le Meridien, Douala: A luxury hotel located near the airport and the city center.

What to Buy in Cameroon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kente Cloth: A colorful fabric used for clothing or decorating.
  • Bamileke Juju Hats: A symbolic hat made of feathers that represents power and wealth.
  • Tribal Masks: Beautiful decorative masks made by the local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Cameroon

Cameroon is a country with diverse ethnic groups, each with their own unique customs and traditions. One should be respectful of different tribes and observe the dress etiquettes when visiting the traditional palaces. Business attire is preferred when visiting government offices.

Best Time to Visit Cameroon

The best time to visit Cameroon is from November to February, as the temperature is cooler and the air is less humid. During this period, there are also many festivals and events in the country, creating a vibrant atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cameroon

Do:

  • Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn a few phrases in French as it is one of the country’s official languages.
  • Greet people in the local way with “Bonjour” and “Bonsoir.”

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local customs and personal space.
  • Bargain aggressively in street markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cameroon

Petty theft and pickpocketing are known to occur in urban areas. It’s advisable to be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone after dark. Also, it’s suggested to stay informed with regards to the current security situation in the regions you plan to visit.

Health in Cameroon

Malaria is prevalent in Cameroon, so it’s advisable to take necessary precautions such as taking anti-malaria medication and using insect repellent.

How to Get Around in Cameroon

Taxis and buses are the most common ways to get around Cameroon’s major cities. Motorbikes and bicycles are also common in the rural areas. One can also rent a car, though it’s recommended for those who are familiar with the country’s roads and language.

Where to Go Next from Cameroon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cameroon has many nearby destinations worth exploring, including:

  • Brazzaville, Republic of Congo: A neighboring country with a variety of natural and cultural attractions.
  • Kahuzi-Biega National Park, Congo: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with gorilla tracking and scenic views.
  • Lobéké National Park, Cameroon: A large rainforest nature reserve.

Conclusion

Cameroon is a destination that should not be missed on your African adventure. With its diverse culture, natural beauty, and exciting outdoor activities, there is something for everyone. Following the local customs, taking security and health precautions, and exploring nearby destinations will make your trip unforgettable.
