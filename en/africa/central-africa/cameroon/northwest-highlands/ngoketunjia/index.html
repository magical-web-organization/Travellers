Ngoketunjia Travel Guide

Ngoketunjia is a region located in the Northwest Highlands of Cameroon in Central Africa. The region is known for its beautiful landscapes, outdoor adventures, and unique cultural experiences. Introduction to Ngoketunjia

Ngoketunjia is a region that is located in the northwestern part of Cameroon. It is bordered by the regions of Bui to the north, Boyo to the east, Mezam to the south, and Menchum to the west. The region is home to many ethnic groups, including the Widikum, the Bamileke, and the Fulani. The region receives a lot of rainfall, making it a lush and green area with beautiful landscapes and scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Ngoketunjia

Ngoketunjia has a lot of great places to visit. Here are some of the must-see attractions in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wum Ngiep Cultural Festival: This annual festival is held in the town of Wum and is a celebration of the region’s cultural heritage.
  • Widikum Palace: The palace is located in Nkambe and is home to the traditional ruler of the Widikum people.
  • Bamenda Museum of Arts and Crafts: The museum is located in Bamenda and houses a collection of traditional art and crafts from the region.

Natural Attractions of Ngoketunjia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Nyos: This crater lake is located in the western part of the region and is known for its beautiful scenery and unique ecosystem.
  • Mount Oku: This mountain is the highest peak in the region and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Menchum Falls: These waterfalls are located in the western part of the region and are a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ngoketunjia

Ngoketunjia is a great region for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Mount Oku Trail: This trail takes you to the summit of Mount Oku and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lake Nyos Trail: This trail takes you around the crater lake and allows you to see the unique ecosystem up close.
  • Bamenda Ring Road: This 367 km road takes you through the region’s countryside and offers beautiful views of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Ngoketunjia

Ngoketunjia has a unique food and drink scene that is influenced by the region’s cultural diversity. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fufu and Njama-Njama: This dish is made of pounded cassava and served with a leafy green vegetable.
  • Mbanga Soup: This soup is made with palm oil, crayfish, and vegetables and is a staple dish in the Bamileke culture.
  • Poulet DG: This dish is made of chicken, plantains, and vegetables and is a popular dish in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: This is an alcoholic drink made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Milo: This is a hot chocolate drink that is a popular beverage in Cameroon.
  • Sodabi: This is a type of locally distilled liquor that is made from palm sap.
