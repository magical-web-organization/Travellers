Introduction to Congo Basin

The Congo Basin is a vast area of Central Africa that covers more than 1.5 million square miles. It is home to one of the world’s largest rainforests and is known for its stunning biodiversity, including endangered species such as gorillas and chimpanzees. The area is also significant historically, as it was once a major center of African trade and slave routes.

1 Arrival in Kinshasa, National Museum of Kinshasa

Food and Drink Scene of Congo Basin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Matoke: a dish made with plantains and vegetables
  • Fufu: a starchy dish made from cassava or yams
  • Groundnut Stew: a spicy stew made with peanut sauce and meat or fish

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine: a sweet, fermented drink made from palm tree sap
  • Primus Beer: a popular lager produced in Congo
  • Ginger Beer: a non-alcoholic, spicy drink

Local Culture and Customs of Congo Basin

Congo Basin is a culturally rich and diverse area, with many different ethnic groups and languages. It is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering someone’s home or a sacred site.

Best Time to Visit Congo Basin

The best time to visit Congo Basin is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. The weather is mild, and wildlife sightings are more frequent. However, note that this is also peak tourist season, so prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Congo Basin

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry cash with you, as some places may not accept credit cards
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in public areas
  • Walk around at night alone or in isolated areas
  • Take photographs without permission from locals or in sacred sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Congo Basin

Congo Basin is generally safe for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions when traveling in this region. Be sure to research safety precautions before your trip, and always trust your instincts.

Health in Congo Basin

Malaria and yellow fever are prevalent in the region, and it’s essential to take the necessary precautions before your trip. Consult a travel doctor before your departure to get the necessary vaccines and medications.
