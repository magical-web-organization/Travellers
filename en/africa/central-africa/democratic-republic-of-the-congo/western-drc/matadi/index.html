Introduction to Matadi

Located in Western DRC, Matadi is the capital city of Kongo Central province, situated around 148 km South-West of Kinshasa, the national capital. The city is an important port on the Congo River and serves as a gateway linking Congo to the Atlantic Ocean via its deepwater port. Matadi boasts an abundance of natural and man-made attractions, including natural wonders like the scenery of the Congo River, opportunities for outdoor adventure, historical sites, and museums. This comprehensive travel guide will provide an overview of Matadi and help you plan your itinerary to this exciting and underrated African destination.

List of Places to Visit in Matadi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Matadi is home to several significant museums and historical landmarks that showcase the region’s rich cultural heritage. The most notable are:

1. Museum of Bakongo

The Museum of Bakongo in Matadi is a cultural center that displays fascinating exhibits on the region’s cultural history, artifacts, and ancient artifacts. The museum is dedicated to the powerful Kongo Empire, and it is Africa’s first modern museum.

2. Palais de la Justice

Palais de la Justice is a stunning colonial courthouse in the heart of Matadi, completed in 1925. The magnificent architecture and grandeur of the building reveal a glimpse of the district’s past.

3. Cathédrale Sainte-Anne

The Cathédrale Sainte-Anne is one of the most iconic landmarks in Matadi, known for its striking architecture and grandeur. The cathedral is named after Saint Anne, the patron saint of Congo, and it is the largest Catholic church in the region.

Natural Attractions of Matadi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural wonders of Matadi are one of the main reasons why the city is a popular destination in Central Africa. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities and scenic views, such as:

1. Mpozo River

Mpozo River is a beautiful tributary of the Congo River that flows along the Matadi-Kinshasa road. The river is a popular spot for picnics, fishing, and camping, surrounded by lush greenery and stunning views.

2. Kwilu River

The Kwilu River is another scenic river in Matadi that offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in nature, surrounded by breathtaking vistas, wildlife, and waterfalls.

3. Mont Ngaliema

Mont Ngaliema is the highest peak in Matadi, standing at 550 meters, and it is popular for hiking and bird watching. The mountain also boasts the best views of the Congo River and the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matadi

Matadi is a haven for adventure enthusiasts, with several challenging and exciting routes to explore by bike or foot, such as:

  • Congo River Trail: This hiking route takes you through the local villages along the Congo River, allowing you to witness the traditional life of the Bakongo people.

  • Matadi to Kinshasa Road: This cycling route is equally challenging and rewarding, taking you through dense forests, alongside rivers, and up hills, providing ample opportunities to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Matadi: Activities and Tours

There are numerous things to do in Matadi to keep visitors engaged and entertained, such as:

  • Boat Rides: Enjoy a scenic boat ride along the Congo River and take in the beautiful views of the city’s landscape from the water.

  • Fishing: Head to Mpozo River and catch tilapia or the black bass embracing traditional fishing styles practiced by the locals.

  • City Tours: Discover the diverse neighborhoods, cathedrals, and historical sites through guided tours conducted by the Matadi Tourism Board.

Food and Drink Scene of Matadi

Matadi is home to a variety of local dishes and traditional beverages you must try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

Matadi cuisine is a fusion of African and European tastes, and visitors can indulge in dishes such as:

  • Moambe Chicken: A popular dish made from chicken served with a traditional sauce of palm oil and vegetables.

  • Madesu: A popular street food snack made of fried cassava, plantains, and potatoes, a very tasty snack to indulge in.

  • Poulet Ya Malamba: A popular dish that features chicken cooked in tomato sauce with a spicy kick.

Local Beverages

Visitors can experience Matadi’s local refreshments while enjoying the local edibles, including:

  • Palm Wine: A popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented palm tree sap, known for its sweet and fiery taste.

  • Makayabu: A non-alcoholic drink that is unique to Congo, produced from cassava, cornmeal, sugar, and spices.

Where to Stay in Matadi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matadi offers accommodation options for every budget and style. The most popular options include:

  • Hotel Olive Branch: A luxury hotel located in the city center, boasting modern facilities, stunning decor, and views of the Congo River.

  • Hotel Clairvoyant: A mid-range hotel with all the essential amenities and fantastic views of the city.

  • Guest House Lopango: A more economical choice, Guest House Lopango offers basic rooms and essential amenities, with easy access to the city’s major sites.

What to Buy in Matadi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matadi’s markets offer a wide range of souvenirs to take back home, including:

  • Kuba Cloth: Hand-woven fabrics that are intricately patterned, representing traditional Kuba culture.

  • Wooden Crafts: Hand-carved wooden sculptures and masks that represent the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  • Maisha Tea: A popular brand of locally produced tea from Congo that you can take back for yourself or as a gift for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Matadi

Matadi boasts a rich culture that visitors should know before traveling:

  • Greeting: It is customary to greet people with a salute referred to as “mbote.”

  • Clothing: The dress code varies from casual to formal, with traditional wear being the preferred dress for celebrations.

  • Food: Sharing meals is an essential part of the Congolese culture, with most social gatherings involving a hearty meal or snack.

Best Time to Visit Matadi

The best time to visit Matadi is during the warm, dry season, from May to October when temperatures average around 25 to 30°C. This season provides optimal conditions for outdoor activities, sightseeing, and exploration, without the hindrance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matadi

There are some key do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Matadi:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress conservatively and appropriately.

  • Always carry necessary identification documents and currency.

  • Observe cleanliness and environmental conservation.

Don’t:

  • Do not carry excessive jewelry, cash or other valuable possessions in public areas.

  • Do not take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Do not buy or sell illegal or counterfeit items.

Safety and Health

Matadi is generally safe for visitors, but like any other city, certain precautions are necessary.

Safety in Matadi

  • Visitors should avoid walking alone in desolate areas or late in the night.

  • Keep an eye on personal belongings and do not leave them unattended.

Health in Matadi

  • Visitors are advised to take malaria prophylaxis before traveling.

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.

  • Seek medical assistance immediately in case of any health issues.

Getting to Matadi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matadi is accessible by different modes of transport, including:

Airports Near Matadi

The closest airports to Matadi are in Kinshasa, the national capital, which has two major airports: N’djili International Airport and N’dolo Airport.

Bus Stations Near Matadi

There are various bus stations in Matadi that link it to other cities, such as Kinshasa.

Other Options

Visitors to Matadi can also access the city using ferries, private vehicles, and rented cars from Kinshasa.

How to Get Around in Matadi

The best way to explore Matadi is by using taxis or by renting a car. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or bicycle, as some sites are near each other and offer the opportunity to observe and appreciate the local culture and lifestyle.

Where to Go Next from Matadi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are eager to explore other destinations in Congo, here are some recommendations:

  • Kinshasa: Congo’s capital city boasts several landmarks and bustling nightlife.

  • Boma: Known for its colonial architecture and sense of history, Boma is worth visiting for a day trip.

Conclusion

Matadi is an exciting destination that offers visitors an opportunity to indulge in nature, history, and culture, setting it apart from other cities in Central Africa. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their itinerary to Matadi and explore this hidden African gem.
