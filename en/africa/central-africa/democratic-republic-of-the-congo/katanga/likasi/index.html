Introduction to Likasi

Located in the province of Katanga, in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Likasi is a city that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and history. Known as a thriving mining city, Likasi has become a popular tourist destination in recent years. The city is home to a diverse population with a unique blend of customs, traditions, and languages. If you’re looking for off-the-beaten-track destinations in Africa, Likasi is definitely worth a visit.

List of Places to Visit in Likasi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, or simply interested in learning more about the culture of Likasi, there are several places you must visit.

Kasombo Museum

The Kasombo Museum is a must-see for anyone interested in the history of Likasi. This museum showcases the region’s mining history and the cultural heritage of the local population. The museum includes exhibits of minerals, mining tools, and old photographs. Visitors to the museum can learn about the copper and cobalt mining industry that has shaped the region’s history.

Lushinda Falls

Lushinda Falls is a historical site with a breathtaking waterfall that’s located a short drive from Likasi. The falls are known for their natural beauty, and they also hold historical significance. The area surrounding the falls was an important trading center in pre-colonial times.

Natural Attractions of Likasi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Likasi has no shortage of natural beauty, with stunning landscapes and outdoor adventures available to visitors.

Lake Fwa

Lake Fwa is one of the most stunning natural attractions in Likasi. Located in the south of the city, the lake is surrounded by lush mountains and is a perfect spot for fishing, kayaking, or simply enjoying the natural beauty of the region.

Kibwezi Trail

The Kibwezi Trail is a popular hiking and biking trail that takes visitors through the forests and hills around Likasi. Along the trail, hikers will enjoy the fantastic views of the city and the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Likasi

Likasi offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and mountain biking, with trails suited for both beginners and experienced hikers. The Kibwezi Trail is the most popular hiking trail, as mentioned above.

Best Things to Do in Likasi: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Likasi, whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation.

Visit a Mining Site

Likasi is known for its copper and cobalt mining industry, and visiting one of the mines is an incredible experience. Visitors can see historic mines, learn about mining methods, and even take a tour of a working mine.

Enjoy a Boat Ride on Lake Fwa

A great way to experience the natural beauty of Likasi is to take a boat ride on Lake Fwa. Visitors can enjoy the calm waters of the lake and the stunning landscape that surrounds it.

Food and Drink Scene of Likasi

The food and drink scene of Likasi is a unique blend of traditional Congolese cuisine and international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

Kwanga

Kwanga is a local delicacy, made from cassava that is fermented before being cooked. Served with a variety of stews, kwanga is a must-try for anyone visiting Likasi.

Ndakala

Ndakala is a traditional Congolese dish made from chicken, goat, or beef. The meat is cooked with a blend of spices, and served with rice, manioc, or fufu.

Local Beverages

Palm wine

Palm wine is a popular local beverage, made from the sap of the oil palm. Visitors can try fresh palm wine from the trees, or buy bottles of palm wine from local vendors.

Where to Stay in Likasi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Likasi, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

Hotel Restaurant Scolastique

Hotel Restaurant Scolastique is a popular hotel in Likasi, located close to the city center. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

Safari Lodge

Safari Lodge is a beautiful guesthouse located in the countryside around Likasi. The lodge offers traditional African-style accommodation and is an excellent place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Likasi.

  • Tribal masks
  • Copper and cobalt jewelry
  • Traditional Congolese fabric

Local Culture and Customs of Likasi

The people of Likasi have a rich culture that is influenced by the city’s mining history and the traditions of the local population. Visitors to Likasi should respect local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Likasi

The best time to visit Likasi is during the dry season, from May to September. The weather is pleasant, and the landscapes are beautiful during this time. It is recommended to avoid the rainy season, from October to April, as many roads and trails become inaccessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Likasi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Travel alone at night.
  • Carry large amounts of cash on you.
  • Ignore warnings from locals or authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Likasi

Likasi can be a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take basic safety precautions to avoid any issues. It is recommended not to travel alone at night and to avoid carrying large amounts of cash on you.

Health in Likasi

Visitors to Likasi should take precautions to protect their health while traveling. This includes drinking only bottled water, using insect repellent, and avoiding undercooked food.

Getting to Likasi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Likasi can be a bit of a challenge, but there are several transportation options available.

Airports Near Likasi

The nearest airport is Lubumbashi International Airport, which is located approximately 200 km north of Likasi.

Bus Stations Near Likasi

There are several bus stations in Lubumbashi with buses that travel to Likasi.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be arranged in Lubumbashi for travel to Likasi.

How to Get Around in Likasi

The best way to get around Likasi is by taxi or private car. There are also buses available for travel within the city, but they can be crowded.

Where to Go Next from Likasi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting.

Lubumbashi

Lubumbashi is a nearby city that is worth a visit for its historical and cultural attractions.

The Copperbelt

The Copperbelt is a mining region in Zambia, located near the border with Congo. It is known for its copper mining history and offers a range of activities for tourists, including mine tours and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Likasi is a hidden gem in Central Africa, full of natural beauty, history, and culture. With a fantastic range of activities and attractions, Likasi is definitely worth a visit for anyone looking to explore off-the-beaten-track destinations.
