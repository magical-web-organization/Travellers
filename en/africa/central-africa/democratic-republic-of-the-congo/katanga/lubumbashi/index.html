Introduction to Lubumbashi

Lubumbashi is a hidden gem of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, located in the province of Katanga. It is a city with an authentic African atmosphere, surrounded by vast savannah, lakes, and forests. Lubumbashi is known for its mining industries, natural beauty, and historical significance. It was once the capital of the Katanga Province and played a significant role in the Congo crisis. Today, it is a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage and a friendly population. Here is a comprehensive tour guide for Lubumbashi that will help you navigate this vibrant city with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Lubumbashi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lubumbashi has a rich culture and history, and there are numerous museums and historical places worth visiting, such as:

  • National Museum of Lubumbashi: A must-visit for history buffs, this museum houses exhibits on the geological makeup of the region, early human settlement, and the impact of Belgian colonialism.
  • Ruins of the 16th century Kilwa Kisiwani Mosque: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this mosque is considered the oldest mosque in Central Africa and an important testimony to the Arab and Swahili presence in the region.
  • Lubumbashi Cathedral: Built-in 1912, this cathedral is considered an architectural masterpiece and an important landmark of Lubumbashi.

Natural Attractions of Lubumbashi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lubumbashi has an abundance of natural wonders and outdoor activities. Some of the top attractions include:

  • The Kundelungu National Park: This park is home to rare species of animals such as the Okapi and the African elephant. Visitors can experience wildlife through guided walking safaris or game drives.
  • Lubumbashi Zoo: The zoo is home to a variety of animals, such as monkeys, lions, tigers, and snakes, and is a fun attraction for families and animal lovers.
  • Lake Tshangalele: Located 20 km outside of Lubumbashi, this lake offers scenic views and a peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy fishing, swimming, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lubumbashi

For adventure seekers, Lubumbashi boasts some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region. Some of the top trails include:

  • Lubumbashi to Kisenge: This route takes you through the savannah and forest, offering stunning views of the landscape and wildlife along the way.
  • The Mines Trail: This route takes you through the copper mines surrounding Lubumbashi, offering unique insight into the mining history and industry of the region.
  • Lubumbashi to Zambia: This route takes you through the bush and along the border of Zambia. It’s a challenging route, but the views and experiences make it worthwhile.

Best Things to Do in Lubumbashi: Activities and Tours

Lubumbashi has an abundance of activities and tours available to visitors. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Visit artisan markets: Lubumbashi has several markets where you can buy souvenirs and local products. The streets around these markets are colorful and lively, providing visitors with a glimpse into Congolese daily life.
  • Take a city tour: A city tour of Lubumbashi is a great way to get a taste of the local culture and history. Your guide can take you around the city and explain the significant sights and attractions.
  • Visit a local mine: Lubumbashi’s mining industry is one of the most significant in the region, and visitors can take a tour of a working copper mine to learn about the process and the industry’s impact on the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lubumbashi

Here is a week-long itinerary for Lubumbashi that covers the city’s top attractions and activities:

Food and Drink Scene of Lubumbashi

Must-Try Dishes

Lubumbashi’s food scene is heavily influenced by Congolese cuisine, but also has hints of Belgian and French cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Pondu: Cooked cassava leaves mixed with palm oil and spices and served with rice or fufu.
  • Moambé Chicken: A chicken dish cooked with palm nut sauce, served with rice or Couscous.
  • Matembele: A Congolese spinach dish cooked with peanuts and spices and served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

Lubumbashi has a unique drinks scene, with local beverages made from fermented fruits and grains. Some of the must-try beverages include:

  • Chikwangue Beer: A beer made from cassava, and is incredibly popular among locals.
  • Tchibuku: A traditional beer made from cornmeal, available in local bars and restaurants.
  • Munkoyo: A fermented drink made from maize and cassava and served cold.

Where to Stay in Lubumbashi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lubumbashi has a range of accommodation options with different budgets and styles. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Hotel Pullman Lubumbashi Grand Karavia: A five-star hotel with a swimming pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • Hotel Lubumbashi: A mid-range hotel located in the city center but offering a quiet atmosphere.
  • Doxa Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse, offering clean and comfortable rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Lubumbashi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lubumbashi has several artisan markets that sell local products and souvenirs. Some of the top items to bring back home include:

  • Copper crafts: Copper is an integral part of the Lubumbashi economy, and visitors can buy locally made copper crafts.
  • Congolese art: Lubumbashi has several art galleries and markets selling traditional Congolese art and crafts.
  • Tribal Masks: Visitors can purchase traditional tribal masks from artisans at local markets, making great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lubumbashi

Lubumbashi has a vibrant and unique culture. Congolese is the official language, but Swahili and French are also widely spoken. It’s important to dress modestly when visiting places of worship, and tipping is customary in restaurants and for tour guides.

Best Time to Visit Lubumbashi

The best time to visit Lubumbashi is during the dry season, from May to September. Temperatures during this time are lower, and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities and exploration.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lubumbashi

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting places of worship.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without permission.
  • Use your left hand to hand/shake hands or point at someone/something.
  • Disrespect locals or their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lubumbashi

Lubumbashi is a safe city overall but requires common sense safety measures such as avoiding walking alone at night, and keeping valuables safe. Visitors should be aware of street crime, particularly in crowded areas such as markets and transport hubs.

Health in Lubumbashi

Visitors should ensure they are up to date with recommended vaccinations and take preventative measures against Malaria. Drinking bottled water is also recommended. Visitors should also avoid eating uncooked foods or unpasteurized dairy products.

Getting to Lubumbashi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lubumbashi

  • Lubumbashi International Airport: The main airport that serving Lubumbashi with flights from several African countries.

Bus Stations Near Lubumbashi

  • Lubumbashi Central Bus Station: It is the main bus station in the city and serves buses to several destinations across the country.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in the city center and can be negotiated for a fair price.

How to Get Around in Lubumbashi

Lubumbashi has several public transportation options, including buses and taxis. Taxis are the most common and can be easily negotiated for a fair price.

Where to Go Next from Lubumbashi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lubumbashi is surrounded by natural beauty and interesting destinations for further exploration, such as:

  • Likasi: Known as the “Copper Town,” Likasi is a historic town nearby Lubumbashi, with copper mines and minerals that played a crucial role in Katanga’s development.
  • Fungurume: A small town outside Lubumbashi, known for its natural beauty, mining industry, and fantastic waterfalls.
  • Kananga: A city located in Central Congo, known for its palm oil and tobacco plantations and natural reserves.

Conclusion

Lubumbashi is an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers a unique and authentic experience for travelers seeking adventure, culture, and exploration. With its natural wonders, rich culture, and friendly population, it’s no wonder that Lubumbashi is quickly becoming a popular tourist destination. This comprehensive tour guide for Lubumbashi will help you to navigate this vibrant city with ease, and experience all the exciting activities and attractions the region has to offer.
