A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Loango National Park

Welcome to our comprehensive travel guide for Loango National Park! Located in Gabon, Central Africa, Loango National Park is a natural wonderland that boasts diverse flora and fauna and a rich cultural heritage. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the park’s top attractions, provide tips for planning your visit, and offer insight into local customs and culture. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Loango National Park

Covering an area of 1,550 square kilometers, Loango National Park is a protected area in the west of Gabon. It is comprised of diverse habitats, including forests, savannas, wetlands, and lagoons, which support a wide variety of wildlife. Visitors to Loango National Park can expect to see elephants, gorillas, hippos, and a plethora of bird species. The park is also home to cultural attractions, including historical sites and museums that showcase the local heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Loango National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Loango Lodge: a luxury eco-tourism resort that provides exceptional amenities and guided tours.
  • The Brazil-Angola slave trade route, and the cemetery of former slaves, in Porto Amboim.
  • The Musee National, located in the city of Libreville, showcases Gabon’s rich cultural heritage through exhibits on art, history, and archaeology.

Natural Attractions of Loango National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Loango Lagoon: A great spot to view hippos, crocodiles, and waterfowls. Guided canoe tours are available.
  • Langoue forest: Located in the northeast of the park, this protected area is home to predators like leopards and hyenas, as well as rare African grey parrots and mandrills. Guided forest walks and bird watching are available.
  • The Atlantic coastline: Stretching for over 30km, visitors can stroll along the pristine beaches and witness migrating humpback whales (July-September). Fishing, surfing, and camel rides are available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Loango National Park

Loango National Park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for adventurous travelers to explore. Popular routes include:

  • Sam Sita Loop: This 6km route winds through various ecosystems in the park, from dense forests to open savannas.
  • Rabi Circuit: This 14 km circuit along the Loango Lagoon is perfect for bird watching and exploring the coast.
  • Kokoua Trail: This 15 km trail takes visitors through the lush heart of the park, offering a great opportunity to spot gorillas.

Best Things to Do in Loango National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife watching: Join a guided tour or go solo in search of gorillas, hippos, elephants, and forest buffalo.
  • Bird watching: With over 200 national bird species, visitors can take a guided tour to spot African penguins, pelicans, and ibis.
  • Surfing: The Atlantic coast welcomes both experienced and beginner-level surfers to catch a wave.
  • Fishing: The park’s rivers are home to a plethora of fish species, including bream and catfish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Loango National Park

Day 1-2: Wildlife watching in Langoue forest and gorilla trekking
Day 3: Discovering the Loango Lagoon and exploring the coastline
Day 4: Kayaking on the Ndogo Lagoon
Day 5-6: Hiking in the Kokoua National Park and searching for forest buffalo
Day 7: Visiting cultural attractions such as the Brazil-Angola slave trade route and the cemetery of former slaves.

Food and Drink Scene of Loango National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mbika: A spicy stew made with cassava leaves, beef, and palm oil.
  • Fang fries: Thinly sliced yam or plantain chips, fried and sprinkled with local spices.
  • Fish stew: A hearty dish made with fresh tilapia or other local fish, tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Palm wine: A sweet, milky beverage made from palm tree sap.
  • Bissap: A refreshing hibiscus tea, sometimes mixed with ginger and mint.
  • Haschich: Traditional sorghum beer, usually fermented for three days and served cold.

Where to Stay in Loango National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Loango Lodge: A luxury eco-resort located outside the park, which boasts comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a wildlife observation deck.
  • Gamba Camp: Located within the park on the Atlantic coast, this campsite offers both camping and furnished accommodations, with guided tours to the park’s top attractions.
  • Tassi Guest House: A cozy guest house located in nearby Pointe Denis, with a garden setting and a family-friendly environment.

What to Buy in Loango National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-carved wooden masks and figures depicting the local wildlife.
  • Palm oil soap, made from the highest-grade palm oil from local plantations.
  • Raffia bags and baskets, handcrafted by local villagers.

Local Culture and Customs of Loango National Park

The Fang and the Mpongwe are the dominant ethnic groups in Gabon. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly, particularly when visiting cultural sites. Greeting people with a handshake is customary, with the right hand being extended towards the other person.

Best Time to Visit Loango National Park

The optimal time to visit Loango National Park is from June to September during dry season, where visitors can explore the park in pleasant weather conditions. The wetter months from November to April can often lead to slippery and difficult traveling conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Loango National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and ecosystem.
  • Bring sunscreen and bug spray, as well as comfortable shoes for hiking.
  • Engage with the park’s cultural heritage and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the park’s flora and fauna.
  • Approach wild animals without the guidance of a trained guide.
  • Disrespect local customs or dress inappropriately.

Safety and Health

Safety in Loango National Park

Loango National Park is a safe place for visitors to explore, but certain precautions should be taken. Never walk or swim in the rivers, and avoid exploring at night unless in a group. Be mindful of the weather patterns and flooding, which can impact certain zones of the park or surrounding areas.

Health in Loango National Park

Visitors to Loango National Park are encouraged to vaccinate against yellow fever and other tropical diseases. Malaria is prevalent in Gabon, so it’s recommended to take preventative measures such as taking prophylactic medication or applying mosquito repellent.

Getting to Loango National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Loango National Park

  • Port Gentil International Airport (POG): Located 20 km north of the park, this airport serves domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Loango National Park

  • Buses run from larger surrounding towns such as Gamba, Mayumba, and Tchibanga. However, routes are often unpredictable or infrequent and might not reach the park, and schedules can be unreliable.

Other Options

  • There are organised, guided tours that include transport, accommodations, and meals.
  • Renting a car or taxi driver can be arranged from the point of departure.

How to Get Around in Loango National Park

The park’s vast terrain and varying ecosystems make it difficult to travel independently. Guided tours are an ideal way to explore the park’s top attractions, but visitors can also rent a car or taxi service to move around the park.

Where to Go Next from Loango National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Loango Peninsula: Travel south to Langoue, where visitors can experience incredible wildlife encounters and scenic views of the park’s diverse habitats.
  • Pointe Denise: This beautiful beach resort is perfect for relaxation with pristine beaches, yoga classes, or water sports.
  • Mayumba National Park: Head south to this national park, featuring beautiful savannah areas and dynamic elephant habitat.

Conclusion

Loango National Park remains an untapped gem in Central Africa with a vast expanse of landscape that boasts exotic wildlife habitats, scenic beaches, and cultural attractions. Loango offers a unique experience in Africa that should be part of nature enthusiasts and adventurous backpackers itineraries. Whether it is wildlife treks, beach adventures, cultural exposure, or culinary dining, Loango National Park offers something for everyone.
