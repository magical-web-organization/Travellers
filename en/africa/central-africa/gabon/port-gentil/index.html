Introduction to Port-Gentil

Port-Gentil, also known as the “Petroleum Capital of Africa”, is the economic hub of Gabon, a country located in the Central African region. As the second largest city in Gabon, Port-Gentil is located on the western edge of Gabon, just southeast of the Cape Lopez peninsula.

Port-Gentil is a beautiful city with amazing attractions, including fantastic museums, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. In this guide, we will take a look at some of the best places to visit in Port-Gentil, including the must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, food and drink, accommodations, and transport options.

List of Places to Visit in Port-Gentil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Whether you are interested in learning about the history of Gabon or exploring the local culture, Port-Gentil offers several museums and historical sites that cater to all tastes.

One of the must-visit attractions in Port-Gentil is the Musee du Marechal Bernadotte, which is located in the heart of the city. The museum, which is named after Marshal Bernadotte, a French soldier who later became King of Sweden, showcases the country’s history, culture, and arts.

For those interested in African art, the Museum of African Art is a must-visit. This museum, which is relatively new, opened in 2020, and it features a collection of contemporary and traditional African art.

Another historical site worth visiting is the Fort de l’Eveche, which is a colonial fort that was built in 1875, and it served as a prison during Gabon’s colonial period.

Natural Attractions of Port-Gentil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Port-Gentil boasts of some of the most stunning natural attractions in Gabon, which makes it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

One of the places that you must visit while in Port-Gentil is the Pointe Denis beach. This beautiful beach, which is located on the west coast of Cape Lopez, has crystal clear waters and breathtaking sandy beaches, perfect for sunbathing and swimming. It is also a great spot to enjoy a range of watersports like snorkeling, diving, and fishing.

For those interested in hiking or biking, the Lagune Nyonie trail is a must-try. This 11km trail, which is located on the outskirts of Port-Gentil, offers stunning views of trees towering over the marshy areas in the Laguna Nyonie As a haven for birdwatchers, it is home to several bird species- notably the African fish eagle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port-Gentil

For those seeking to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of Port-Gentil, there are many hiking and biking routes to choose from.

Cape Lopez, the peninsula that separates the Atlantic Ocean from the Gulf of Guinea, offers the most scenic trails that are great for hiking and biking.

The 9km Lighthouse Trail is one of the most popular hiking routes that offers breathtaking views of the lighthouse and the vast coastline. The trail, which can be challenging, takes approximately 4 hours to complete.

For those who love mountain biking, the Kongou Trail is a perfect route. The 21km trail offers challenging terrain ranging from dirt tracks to single-track trails. It can take up to 3 hours depending on your fitness level and speed.

Food and Drink Scene of Port-Gentil

Port-Gentil has a great food and drink culture that reflects its diverse people and location. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled fish and seafood

  • Poulet nyembwe (chicken stewed in red palm oil)

  • Ndole (bitter leaf and peanut sauce)

  • Fufu (a cassava-based dish)

  • Saka-Saka (cassava leaf sauce)

Local Beverages

  • Palm wine

  • Bissap (Hibiscus tea)

  • Tangawisi (Ginger beer)

  • Muscat (sweet wine)

Where to Stay in Port-Gentil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options to suit different tastes and budgets in Port-Gentil. Whether you are looking for a hotel, hostel, or guesthouse, there is something for everyone.

For those seeking luxury, the Hotel Boulevard is the perfect choice. This four-star hotel located in the heart of the city boasts of comfortable rooms, modern amenities, and great dining options.

If you are on a budget, then the Chez Gertrude Guest House is an excellent option. This budget guest house has comfortable rooms and is located a short walk from the city center.

What to Buy in Port-Gentil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Port-Gentil is an excellent place to shop for souvenirs and local products. Some unique items you should look out for include:

  • Carvings and sculptures made of wood and other materials

  • Beaded and woven necklaces and bracelets

  • Handwoven baskets and bags

  • Coffee and cocoa

  • Artwork and paintings by local artists

Local Culture and Customs of Port-Gentil

The people of Port-Gentil are known for their warm hospitality, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake or a hug. Gabon is a predominantly Christian country, and religion plays a significant role in the people’s lives. English and French are the official languages, with French being the most commonly spoken.

Best Time to Visit Port-Gentil

The best time to visit Port-Gentil is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. This period is characterized by slightly cooler temperatures and less rainfall, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port-Gentil

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Be respectful of the local culture.

  • Carry insect repellent during the rainy season.

  • Use taxis instead of walking alone at night.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission.

  • Don’t litter.

  • Don’t buy items made from endangered animals or plants.

  • Don’t wander in unsafe areas of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port-Gentil

While Port-Gentil is relatively safe, it is always best to take precautions when traveling to any foreign destination. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Don’t carry excessive cash or valuables.

  • Be cautious of pickpockets and bag snatchers.

  • Follow the local customs and codes of conduct.

Health in Port-Gentil

Before traveling to Port-Gentil, ensure that you are up to date with routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella, and hepatitis A and B.

Also, consider taking anti-malaria prophylaxis medication, which is prevalent in the area. It is essential to carry insect repellent to minimize your risk of contracting mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Port-Gentil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port-Gentil

The city has its own airport, the Port-Gentil International Airport, which serves local and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Port-Gentil

While there are no bus stations in Port-Gentil, there are several bus companies that offer trips to and from the nearby cities of Libreville and Omboue.

Other Options

You can also get to Port-Gentil by taking a taxi or a private car from the airport or the bus station.

How to Get Around in Port-Gentil

While taxis are the most common mode of transport in Port-Gentil, you can also rent a car or a bike to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Port-Gentil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, here are some nearby destinations that you can visit:

  • Libreville, the capital city of Gabon, which is located about 4 hours’ drive from Port-Gentil.

  • Loango National Park, which is located south of Port-Gentil and is known for its stunning wildlife and landscapes.

  • Mayumba National Park, which is located on the southern coast of Gabon and is known for its amazing Atlantic coastline and marine life.

Conclusion

Port-Gentil is a hidden gem in Gabon that offers a perfect blend of modernity and tradition. It boasts of natural and man-made attractions that cater to all interests, making it a destination worth visiting. Whether you are interested in exploring the city’s museums and historic sites, hiking or biking in the natural parks, sampling local cuisines or immersing yourself in the local culture and customs, Port-Gentil has something for everyone.
