Introduction to Wau

Wau is a city in Bahr el Ghazal, South Sudan, known for its historical and cultural significance. The city’s roots date back to the 19th century, and it has survived numerous wars and conflicts to become an essential cultural hub in the region. Wau offers a unique blend of modern and traditional African culture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers interested in exploring the continent’s rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Wau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wau Museum

The Wau Museum is one of the city’s most popular attractions, showcasing the region’s history, culture, and traditions. Visitors can explore exhibits that cover everything from pre-colonial life to modern-day South Sudan.

Jur River Church

The Jur River Church is one of Wau’s oldest and most significant religious landmarks, dating back to the early 20th century. The church’s stunning architecture and intricate designs make it worth visiting.

Natural Attractions of Wau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Basili Waterfall

Located just outside Wau, Basili Waterfall is a popular outdoor attraction for visitors. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery, and the natural swimming pool at the bottom is perfect for cooling off on a hot day.

Savanna Plains

The savanna plains surrounding Wau are home to a diverse range of wildlife, including antelopes, gazelles, and zebras. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the area and learn more about the animals that inhabit it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wau

Abiongo Biking and Hiking Trail

The Abiongo Biking and Hiking Trail is one of the most popular outdoor activities in Wau. The trail offers stunning views of the region’s savanna plains and allows visitors to explore the area’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Wau: Activities and Tours

Cultural Tours

Wau’s rich history and unique blend of cultures make it an ideal destination for cultural tours. Visitors can explore the city’s museums, historical landmarks, and local markets to learn more about the region’s traditions.

Wildlife Safaris

South Sudan is home to some of Africa’s most diverse and fascinating wildlife. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the region’s savanna plains and see animals like antelopes, gazelles, and zebras up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Wau

Must-Try Dishes

South Sudanese cuisine is a unique blend of traditional African and Arab influences. Some of the must-try dishes in Wau include:

  • Kisra - a type of pancake made from flour and water
  • Ful Medames - a popular breakfast dish made from fava beans and spices
  • Kebab - marinated and grilled meat on skewers

Local Beverages

Sorghum beer is a popular local drink in Wau, made from fermented grains. It has a slightly sour taste and is usually served cold. Visitors can also try the local coffee, which is strong and served with lots of sugar.

Where to Stay in Wau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wau offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Golden Nile Hotel
  • Juba Road Hotel
  • Wau Guesthouse

What to Buy in Wau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a range of unique souvenirs and local products in Wau’s markets and shops. Some popular items include:

  • Woven baskets and mats
  • Beaded jewelry
  • South Sudanese coffee and tea

Local Culture and Customs of Wau

South Sudanese culture is diverse and unique, with a rich history dating back centuries. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering homes or religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Wau

The best time to visit Wau is during the dry season, from January to April. The weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are more accessible during this time. May to September is the wet season, and many roads and attractions may be closed during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wau

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people

Don’t:

  • Interfere with local wildlife
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Wau

Traveling in South Sudan can be challenging, and visitors should take precautions to stay safe. Be aware of your surroundings at all times and avoid walking alone at night. Visitors should also be mindful of potential safety risks when traveling outside of Wau.

Health in Wau

South Sudan has limited access to medical care and supplies, and visitors should take precautions to stay healthy. It is essential to drink bottled water and avoid consuming food or drinks from street vendors.

Getting to Wau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wau

Wau Airport is the main airport serving the city, with daily flights to and from Juba.

Bus Stations Near Wau

Wau has several bus stations, with regular services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to travel around Wau and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Wau

Walking is the best way to explore Wau’s city center, while taxis and minibusses are available for traveling in and around the city.

Where to Go Next from Wau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Juba

Juba, the capital of South Sudan, is located just a short flight from Wau. The city offers a unique blend of modern and traditional culture and is home to several museums and historical landmarks.

Kiir Conservation Area

Located north of Wau, the Kiir Conservation Area is a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the region’s diverse wildlife and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Wau is a unique and fascinating destination, offering visitors a chance to explore South Sudan’s rich culture and history. With its range of outdoor activities, historical landmarks, and cultural tours, Wau is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Africa.
