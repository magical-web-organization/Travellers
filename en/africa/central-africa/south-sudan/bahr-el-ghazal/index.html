A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Bahr el Ghazal, South Sudan

Welcome to Bahr el Ghazal, a region located in the heart of South Sudan in Central Africa. With its rich culture, diverse landscapes, and undiscovered natural beauty, Bahr el Ghazal is the perfect destination for adventurous travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Bahr el Ghazal, including the must-see attractions, outdoor adventures, local culture and customs, food and drink scene, accommodations, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Bahr el Ghazal

Bahr el Ghazal is a landlocked region in South Sudan, bordered by the states of Warrap, Western Bahr el Ghazal, and Northern Bahr el Ghazal. The region’s capital is Wau, a vibrant city with a rich history and cultural significance. Bahr el Ghazal is known for its diverse landscapes, ranging from savannas to wetlands, and its many rivers, including the Bahr el Ghazal River, which runs through the region.

List of Places to Visit in Bahr el Ghazal

There are many places to visit in Bahr el Ghazal, each offering a unique experience. We’ve compiled a list of the top places to see and things to do during your visit to the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wau Archaeological Museum: Located in the city of Wau, this museum displays artifacts and exhibits related to the history and culture of the region.
  • Bagara Hills: These hills offer a scenic view of the surrounding landscape and historical significance as they were used as a hideout during the Sudanese civil war by the Sudan People’s Liberation Army (SPLA) forces.
  • Kuacjok Cultural Centre: This cultural center offers visitors a chance to learn about the customs and traditions of the local Dinka people through music, dance, and other cultural activities.

Natural Attractions of Bahr el Ghazal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bahr el Jebel National Park: This park is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, giraffes, and antelopes, and offers visitors the chance to go on a game drive or walking safari.
  • Boma National Park: This park is known for its scenic views and birdlife and offers visitors the chance to hike, explore caves, and enjoy hot springs.
  • Mayen-Abun Hills: These hills offer a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape and are a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bahr el Ghazal

Bahr el Ghazal is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking for a unique adventure. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Bahr el Ghazal River Hike: This hike takes visitors along the Bahr el Ghazal River, offering scenic views of the surrounding landscape and the chance to spot wildlife along the way.
  • Wau to Bagari Bike Route: This bike route takes visitors through the countryside and offers a chance to see local villages and experience the local culture up close.

Best Things to Do in Bahr el Ghazal: Activities and Tours

In addition to the must-see attractions and outdoor adventures, there are many other activities and tours to consider during your visit to Bahr el Ghazal. Here are some of the best:

  • Cultural Tours: Take a guided tour of local villages and learn about the customs and traditions of the Dinka people.
  • River Cruises: Explore the Bahr el Ghazal River by boat and see wildlife and local villages from a different perspective.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take a hot air balloon ride over the Mayen-Abun hills for a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Bahr el Ghazal

No visit to Bahr el Ghazal is complete without trying the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kisra: A flatbread made from sorghum flour, usually eaten with a stew or sauce.
  • Shai: A spicy tea made with black tea leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, and ginger.
  • Malawah: A crispy, layered pastry served with honey or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Aradaib: A locally brewed beer made from sorghum.
  • Asida: A porridge made from sorghum or millet, often served with milk or honey.

There are several options for accommodations in Bahr el Ghazal, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best:

  • Wau Grand Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Wau.
  • Safari Lodge: A mid-range hotel with scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Sabina Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Bahr el Ghazal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bahr el Ghazal is known for its traditional crafts and souvenirs, including baskets, pottery, and handmade jewelry. Here are some of the best places to shop for local products:

  • Wau Market: A bustling market in the heart of Wau where visitors can find a variety of crafts and souvenirs.
  • Dinka Craftsmen Cooperative: A cooperative located in Kuacjok that offers handmade crafts and souvenirs made by local Dinka artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Bahr el Ghazal

Bahr el Ghazal is home to several ethnic groups, including the Dinka people, who have a rich cultural heritage. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of during your visit:

  • Respect for elders: In Dinka culture, elders are highly respected and play an important role in the community. Visitors should always show respect for elders.
  • Traditional dress: Many Dinka people wear traditional dress, which includes brightly colored robes and beaded jewelry.
  • Cattle herding: Cattle are an important part of Dinka culture and are often used as a form of currency. Visitors may see cattle herders on their travels.

Best Time to Visit Bahr el Ghazal

The best time to visit Bahr el Ghazal is during the dry season, which runs from December to March. During this time, the weather is cooler and there is less rainfall, making it easier to explore the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bahr el Ghazal

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bahr el Ghazal:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately: When visiting local villages or cultural centers, visitors should dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Learn some basic phrases in the local language: Knowing a few words in the local language can go a long way in showing respect for the local culture and people.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission: Visitors should always ask before taking photos of people or cultural sites.
  • Touch someone’s head: In Dinka culture, the head is considered a sacred part of the body and should not be touched without permission.

Safety and Health

Bahr el Ghazal is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Bahr el Ghazal

  • Always travel with a guide or travel in a group.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid isolated areas.
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites or local villages.

Health in Bahr el Ghazal

  • Drink only bottled or purified water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Consult with a healthcare provider before traveling to Bahr el Ghazal to ensure that all necessary vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Bahr el Ghazal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bahr el Ghazal is a remote region, but there are several transportation options for getting there. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Bahr el Ghazal

  • Wau Airport: Located in the city of Wau, this airport is the main point of entry for visitors to Bahr el Ghazal.

Bus Stations Near Bahr el Ghazal

  • Buses are available from Juba, the capital of South Sudan, to Wau.

Other Options

  • Private car: Visitors can hire private cars to take them to Bahr el Ghazal from Juba or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Bahr el Ghazal

The best way to get around Bahr el Ghazal is by hiring a local guide or driver, who can take you to the various attractions and help you navigate the region. Local buses and taxis are also available for those on a budget.

Where to Go Next from Bahr el Ghazal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time after exploring Bahr el Ghazal, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider, including:

  • Nimule National Park: A nature reserve located on the border between South Sudan and Uganda, known for its scenic views and diverse wildlife.
  • Juba: The capital of South Sudan and a vibrant city with a rich history and cultural significance.

Conclusion

Bahr el Ghazal is a hidden gem in Central Africa, offering visitors a chance to experience the rich culture and natural beauty of South Sudan. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your visit to Bahr el Ghazal and discover all that this region has to offer.
