Introduction to Juba

Juba is the capital city of South Sudan, one of the youngest countries in the world, located in the heart of Africa. It has a rich and diverse culture, making it an ideal destination for tourists looking to explore something new.

List of Places to Visit in Juba

Juba has a lot to offer for tourists. Here are some of the must-visit places in Juba.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Juba National Museum
  2. Dr. John Garang Memorial
  3. South Sudanese Parliament Building
  4. Liberty Square
  5. St. Theresa Cathedral

Natural Attractions of Juba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Local Culture and Customs of Juba

South Sudanese culture is diverse and unique. Here are some customs and traditions of Juba:

  1. Respect elders
  2. Greeting is taken seriously
  3. Hospitality is a priority
  4. Respect for different religions and cultures
  5. Celebrations involve a lot of dancing, singing, and drumming

Best Time to Visit Juba

The best time to visit Juba is during the dry season, from December to April. The weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Juba

Here are some do’s and don’ts in Juba:

Do:

  1. Respect the culture and traditions of locals
  2. Greet people with a smile
  3. Dress modestly
  4. Carry cash with you

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures without permission
  2. Be disrespectful towards elders
  3. Criticize the government or politics

Safety and Health

Safety in Juba

Juba is a safe place to travel, but it’s always better to take some precautions while exploring the city. Here are some tips for staying safe in Juba:

  1. Keep your valuables safe
  2. Avoid traveling alone at night
  3. Stay away from political activities and demonstrations

Health in Juba

When traveling to Juba, make sure you get all the necessary vaccinations before you arrive. Also, drink only bottled or boiled water and avoid eating street food.

Getting to Juba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Juba is well connected to other parts of the country and international cities. Here are the ways to reach Juba:

Airports Near Juba

  1. Juba International Airport
  2. Wau Airport
  3. Malakal Airport

Bus Stations Near Juba

  1. Juba Bus Station
  2. Nyayibi Bus Station

Other Options

You can also reach Juba through a ferry from Uganda or Kenya or by road from neighboring countries like Uganda, Kenya, and Ethiopia.

How to Get Around in Juba

Juba has a lot of transportation options, such as private taxis, public buses, motorcycle taxis, or boda-bodas. But, it’s always better to negotiate the fare before traveling, and make sure the vehicle is in good condition.

Conclusion

Juba, the capital of South Sudan, is a unique travel destination with a lot of natural and cultural attractions. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your visit and experience the best of Juba. Happy traveling!
