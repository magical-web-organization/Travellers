Introduction to Day Forest National Park

Day Forest National Park is a hidden gem tucked away in the northeast corner of Djibouti, East Africa. It covers 32,000 hectares of land and is home to a diverse array of plant and wildlife species, as well as cultural and historical attractions. So, whether you’re an outdoor adventurer, nature lover, or cultural enthusiast, Day Forest National Park has something to offer you. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to this stunning national park.

List of Places to Visit in Day Forest National Park

Before visiting Day Forest National Park, it’s important to plan your visits to the must-see locations in the park. The following list outlines some of the top places to visit in Day Forest National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ethnographic Museum: This museum provides an insight into the local Afar, Somali and Issa communities’ lifestyles, beliefs, and traditions.
  • Archaeological sites: Explore the ancient remains of the prehistoric settlements, the mythical Haricots, and the Bwaba Raqobjal fortress.

Natural Attractions of Day Forest National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Goda Mountains: Hike up to the Goda Mountains and witness panoramic views of the park from a height of 1,700 meters.
  • Day Forest River: Take a dip in the deep, clear waters of the Day Forest River, or even try your hand at fishing.
  • Wildlife: Keep your eyes peeled for the park’s diverse array of wildlife. You can spot antelopes, warthogs, and even the elusive cheetah if you’re lucky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Day Forest National Park

Day Forest National Park has numerous hiking and biking routes that take visitors through the breathtaking landscapes of the park. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Goda Mountain Hike: This hike takes you through Day Forest National Park’s highest point, culminating at 1,700 meters.

  • Day Forest River Walk: This route follows the footpath along the Day Forest River and is perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Best Things to Do in Day Forest National Park: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone in Day Forest National Park. Here are some of the best things to do in the park:

  • Wildlife Viewing: Day Forest National Park is home to a diverse array of wildlife species, including antelopes and warthogs. You can also spot the rare cheetah if you’re lucky.

  • Cultural Experiences: Immerse yourself in the local Afar, Somali, and Issa cultures by visiting the Ethnographic Museum and wandering through the archaeological sites.

  • Hiking: Explore the Goda Mountains and other trails in Day Forest National Park on foot and enjoy breathtaking views of the park’s natural scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Day Forest National Park

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring Day Forest National Park:

Day Activities
1 Arrive at the park and check into your accommodation. Spend the day exploring the surrounding area to get your bearings.
2 Spend the day hiking up to the Goda Mountains.
3 Join a guided cultural tour of the Ethnographic Museum and archaeological sites.
4 Relax by the Day Forest River or try your hand at fishing.
5 Take a wildlife tour and see if you can spot the rare cheetah.
6 Go on a guided hike to explore more of Day Forest National Park.
7 Enjoy your final day in the park by discovering unique souvenirs at the local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Day Forest National Park

Trying local cuisines is a crucial part of immersing yourself into the culture of a place. Djibouti has a unique culinary culture, which offers a range of dishes that will delight your taste buds:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fah-fah: A spicy soup made from meat, vegetables and Islamic vegetables.

  • Lahoh: A type of injera (a fermented flatbread) that is a staple food in Djiboutian cuisine.

  • Aho-dug-leh: A traditional breakfast dish consisting of roasted goat meat, scrambled eggs, and bread rolls.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Djiboutians love tea, so it’s no surprise that this drink is a crucial element in their social culture.

  • Shah Hawaash tea: This tea is a mixture of tea leaves, ginger, cloves, cinnamon, and cardamom. It’s a must-try if you visit Djibouti!

  • Camel milk: A popular drink in Djibouti, camel milk is sweet and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Day Forest National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some accommodation options in Day Forest National Park:

  • Day Forest Eco-Accommodation: This eco-lodge offers cosy bungalows with private bathrooms.

  • Hotel Fanilo: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with air conditioning, satellite TVs, and free Wi-Fi access.

  • Auberge Aux couleurs Du Lac: This guesthouse is located on the shore of Lake Assal and features affordable accommodation options.

What to Buy in Day Forest National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home unique souvenirs, here are some items that you should consider:

  • Djiboutian scarves: These beautiful scarves come in many colours and patterns and are perfect for keeping you warm.

  • Jewellery: Djiboutian jewellery is handmade and distinctive, with beautiful patterns and designs.

  • Khat: Khat is a green leafy plant that is popular in Djibouti. It’s widely available in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Day Forest National Park

Djibouti’s local culture and customs are rooted in traditional African Islamic practices. The following are some cultural practices to be aware of:

  • Be respectful: Djiboutians are generally conservative, and respectful behaviour is appreciated.

  • Dress modestly: Dress modestly when visiting mosques, and wear conservative clothing in public places.

  • Importation of alcohol is prohibited: Although alcohol is available in some bars, it is illegal to bring it into the country.

Best Time to Visit Day Forest National Park

The best time to visit Day Forest National Park is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this period, the park is less humid, and the chances of rain are minimal. The temperatures are also at their most comfortable, making it perfect for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Day Forest National Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Day Forest National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife: Do not feed the animals or disturb their natural habitats.

  • Carry cash: Most local shops and bars do not accept cards, so carry cash with you.

  • Follow park rules: Respect the park rules, including adhering to designated trails and respecting quiet times.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep Day Forest National Park clean by not littering.

  • Pick flora: Picking plants or flowers is prohibited, as it can damage the park’s ecosystem.

  • Take photos of locals without their permission: Always ask for permission before taking photos of local people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Day Forest National Park

Day Forest National Park is generally safe; however, you should take the following precautions:

  • Carry sunscreen: The sun in Djibouti can be relentless and can cause sunburn and skin damage.

  • Avoid drinking tap water: Stick to bottled water to avoid gastrointestinal issues.

  • Take precautions in the wild: Keep your distance from the park’s wildlife, and be mindful of snakes and other dangerous animals.

Health in Day Forest National Park

You should take the following health precautions:

  • Carry insect repellent: The park is home to mosquitoes and other biting insects that can carry diseases such as malaria.

  • Vaccinations: Ensure you have all necessary vaccinations before travelling to Djibouti.

Getting to Day Forest National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The following are some options for getting to Day Forest National Park:

Airports Near Day Forest National Park

  • Djibouti-Ambouli International Airport: This is the main airport in the city and is approximately 70 km from Day Forest National Park.

Bus Stations Near Day Forest National Park

  • Djibouti Bus Station: From here, travel via the main road to Day Forest National Park.

  • Hargeisa Bus Station: From here, travel via a coach to Day Forest National Park.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Day Forest National Park, but it’s worth considering that the park is isolated, and the terrain can be challenging to navigate.

How to Get Around in Day Forest National Park

The best way to explore Day Forest National Park is on foot. The park has several designated trails that are suitable for hiking, jogging, or mountain biking. However, if you want to explore further afield, you can also rent a car or hire a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Day Forest National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Day Forest National Park is undoubtedly the star attraction of its region, there are other nearby destinations that you can visit such as:

  • Djibouti City: Djibouti City is only an hour-long drive away and has various cultural attractions, including the central market and mosques.

  • Lake Assal: This salt lake is located 65 km southwest of Day Forest National Park and is said to be the lowest point on land in Africa.

Conclusion

Day Forest National Park is undoubtedly one of Djibouti’s most beautiful and exciting attractions. It’s a destination that offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, we hope to have given you everything you need to plan your unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in East Africa.
