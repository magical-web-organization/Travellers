Introduction to Qardho

Qardho is a city in the northeastern part of Somalia and the capital of the Karkaar region, located approximately 790 km east of Mogadishu. It is a hidden gem that is slowly gaining popularity among visitors due to its rich culture and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Qardho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kismayo War Memorial Monument: This monument is dedicated to the fallen soldiers who fought for the liberation of Qardho in the 1970s.
  • Darwiish Museum: The museum was established to display and preserve the artifacts, cultural heritage, and history of the Darwiish State.
  • Jama Darawiish Mosque: The mosque dates back to the early 20th century and is regarded as one of the most significant religious sites in Qardho.

Natural Attractions of Qardho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karkaar Mountains: Karkaar Mountains is an ideal spot for camping, hiking, and trekking. The breathtaking views of the surrounding areas make it perfect for photography enthusiasts.
  • Sacred City of Biriqo: The ancient city is located 12 km east of Qardho and is significant for its historical, cultural, and religious significance.
  • Lamadaya Beach: A sandy beach located on the shoreline of the Indian Ocean offers a relaxing environment and a panoramic view of the coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qardho

Qardho is surrounded by the Karkaar Mountains, which offers several challenging hiking and biking routes for enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Karkaar Hiking Trail: A challenging route with stunning views of the mountains.
  • Qardho to Lamadaya Beach Biking Trail: A paved road that offers a scenic view of the ocean on the way to the sandy beach.

Best Things to Do in Qardho: Activities and Tours

  • Darwiish Tour: A guided tour of the Darwiish Museum and the Jama Darwiish Mosque.
  • Karkaar Mountain Trek: A guided trekking tour through the Karkaar Mountains.
  • Lamadaya Beach Day Tour: A relaxing day trip to enjoy the sun and the ocean at Lamadaya Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Qardho

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bariis Iskukaris: A traditional Somali rice dish with meat and vegetables.
  • Canjeero: A Somali pancake that is often served with honey or jam.
  • Shaah: A spice-infused Somali tea.

Local Beverages

Somalia is predominantly Muslim, and alcohol is not widely available. Tea is a popular drink in Qardho, and Somali spiced tea is a must-try when visiting the city.

Where to Stay in Qardho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gacan Libaax Hotel: A three-star hotel located in the city center.
  • Jubba Hotel: A four-star hotel that offers top-notch amenities and services.
  • Qardho Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse that is perfect for backpackers.

What to Buy in Qardho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gold and Silver Jewelry: Qardho is known for its intricate, hand-made jewelry.
  • Shukriya Garlic: A type of garlic that is unique to Somalia and used widely in Somali cuisine.
  • Somali Incense: A natural incense made from tree resin that is often used in Somali homes.

Local Culture and Customs of Qardho

Qardho has a rich cultural history that is deeply rooted in Islam and traditional Somali practices. Visitors are expected to cover their arms and legs when visiting religious sites, and it is customary to remove shoes before entering a mosque or private home.

Best Time to Visit Qardho

The best time to visit Qardho is between December and February, during the dry season. The weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and the temperature is mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qardho

Do:

  • Show respect to the local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain when shopping in the local markets and bazaars.
  • Carry sufficient cash, as credit cards are not widely accepted.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection, especially between opposite sexes.
  • Photograph individuals without their permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qardho

Qardho is a relatively safe city, but visitors are advised to be cautious when exploring on foot, especially at night. It is also recommended to avoid any political demonstrations or gatherings that may turn violent.

Health in Qardho

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, yellow fever, and polio before traveling to Qardho. It is also recommended to carry mosquito repellent, as the city is prone to malaria.

Getting to Qardho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qardho

Garowe International Airport is the closest airport to Qardho, located approximately 260 km from the city. Visitors can catch a local bus or hire a private taxi to reach Qardho.

Bus Stations Near Qardho

The city has a bus station that connects it to adjoining cities in Somalia. Local buses run from Qardho to other destinations in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars or taxis to travel to Qardho from neighboring cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Qardho

Taxis and private cars are readily available in Qardho. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the city at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Qardho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Galkayo: A major commercial hub located approximately 450 km southwest of Qardho.
  • Erigavo: A picturesque town located approximately 600 km west of Qardho that offers stunning views of the Galgodon Mountains.

Conclusion

Qardho is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. From hiking and biking to visiting museums and historical sites, there is something for everyone in Qardho. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan a perfect trip to this beautiful city and create memorable experiences.
