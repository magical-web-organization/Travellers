Introduction to Bosaso

Bosaso is a stunning coastal city located in Puntland, Somalia. It is a bustling port town and the largest city in Puntland, attracting visitors from all over the world to its exotic beaches, pristine national parks, and stunning natural scenery. From the culturally important museums and historical landmarks to the outdoor adventures and scenic views, Bosaso offers various activities and experiences for travelers of all tastes and preferences.

This travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know about exploring Bosaso and making the most of your trip. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Bosaso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bosaso has several museums and cultural attractions that showcase the city’s history and heritage. Here are some of the must-visit museums and landmarks:

  • Bosaso Port: This major seaport is the primary entry and exit point of Bosaso, welcoming ships from all over the world. Be sure to check out the port’s architecture and watch the picturesque fishing boats. -Puntland Museum: Located in the city center, the Puntland Museum boasts an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the region’s history and evolution over the last century.
  • Sheikh Nur Museum: This museum is dedicated to Sheikh Nur Mohamed Jaamac, a revered Somali poet and social activist. The museum house’s his poetry and documents about his life and work.

Natural Attractions of Bosaso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bosaso boasts pristine natural parks, exotic beaches, and breathtaking mountain ranges. The following outdoor experiences are sure to leave you in awe:

  • Red Sea Beach: This stunning beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and golden sands. Sit back and relax in the sun while enjoying spectacular views of the sea.
  • Majeerteen Mountains: This mountain range is perfect for hikers and nature enthusiasts who love to climb and explore. Hikers can enjoy stunning panoramic views at the top of the peak of the mountains.
  • Parks and Reserves: Bosaso has several parks and wildlife reserves, such as the Baathela Wildlife Reserve and the Boholale Forest Reserve, where visitors can experience a vast range of species of birds and natural plants that are endemic to the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bosaso

Bosaso’s stunning mountains, parks and reserves offer various opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking and biking. Below are details of some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Majeerteen Mountains hiking route: If you are an expert hiker, the Majeerteen mountains have several trails, and expert hikers can hike to the top of the mountain.
  • Bahari biking route: The Bahari biking route is an off-road biking trail that offers challenging terrain with fantastic views of the coastline and the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Bosaso: Activities and Tours

Bosaso offers numerous activities and tours depending on your interest. Here are some of the top experiences to include on your itinerary:

  • Fishing Tours: Bosaso offers a variety of fishing tours that take visitors sailing into the ocean to catch fish with nets and line fishing.
  • Dhow boat cruises: Go for a cruise on a traditional wooden Dhow boat to experience the stunning coastline and surrounding islands that are also home to dolphins.
  • City tours: A city tour is a great way to see the many sights and sounds of Bosaso with experienced guides who are familiar with the history of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bosaso

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Bosaso:

Day 1 Visit the Puntland Museum and Sheikh Nur Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Bosaso

Must-Try Dishes

Bosaso is famous for its exotic and delicious cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Somali-Anjero: This is a Somali version of a pancake, made with wheat or Cornflour that is usually served with meat or vegetables.
  • Meat Skewers: Beef, chicken, or lamb pieces grilled on skewers are a favourite among the locals.
  • Samosas: These are crispy, deep-fried dough triangles filled with meat or vegetarian stuffing.

Local Beverages

Bosaso offers a variety of refreshing beverages to try, including:

  • Somali Tea: This tea is a blend of black tea and spices and is usually served with milk and sugar.
  • Fruit Juices: Bosaso has a great range of fresh-pressed fruit juices, grapefruits, and mango juices are top favourites.

Where to Stay in Bosaso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bosaso provides a range of accommodation options for visitors to choose from, varying from budget-friendly guesthouses to high-end hotels. Below are some of our recommendations:

  • Heritage guest house: This nautically themed guesthouse located in the town centre is budget-friendly.
  • Star Hotel: Located in the heart of Bosaso, Star Hotel provides luxury rooms and a restaurant.
  • Plaza Palace Hotel: This hotel has affordable rooms equipped with balconies overlooking the city and decent amenities.

What to Buy in Bosaso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bosaso has several markets and shops where you can find beautiful and unique souvenirs to commemorate your trip. Here are some of the most popular items to look out for:

  • Wood carvings: The beautiful handcrafted wooden artifacts make a great souvenir.
  • Kikois: These are brightly coloured, lightweight cotton sarongs that are great for the beach and make a beautiful souvenir.
  • Jewelry: Somali women are well known for wearing striking gold and silver jewellery.

Local Culture and Customs of Bosaso

The culture and customs of Bosaso are fascinating and unique, rooted in its history and heritage. Here are some key facts to keep in mind:

  • Religion: Bosaso is predominantly Islamic, so visitors should take care to be respectful of cultural and religious practices.
  • Dress Code: Modest dress code is usually admired, and foreigners are advised not to wear shorts but light clothing.
  • Social Etiquette: Greeting each other in Bosaso is essential, and it’s considered friendly to offer tea to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Bosaso

The optimal time to visit Bosaso is between December and February when the weather is cooler and more bearable. The ideal temperature ranges between 24°C and 30°C. The summer months tend to be hot and humid, making it tough for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bosaso

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Bosaso:

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs
  • Do bring comfortable footwear suitable for hiking
  • Do carry cash as some shops don’t accept credit cards
  • Do try out the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of people and locations that reject it
  • Don’t remove any historical artefacts or natural resources from Bosaso
  • Don’t leave belongings unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Bosaso

Like any travel destination, visitors to Bosaso should be mindful of their safety. Here are some precautions to observe:

  • Visitors are advised to be cautious and avoid political rallies or other large public gatherings.
  • Avoid travelling or walking in deserted areas or alleyways at night.
  • Always keep your belongings with you while moving around the city.

Health in Bosaso

It is recommended that visitors take the necessary precautions to stay healthy while in Bosaso. Some of the steps include:

  • Make sure to stay well-hydrated and drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Visitors should only consume food that has been thoroughly cooked and served hot.
  • Always establish travel insurance that includes medical coverages in case of unexpected emergencies.

Getting to Bosaso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bosaso

  • Bosaso International Airport is the primary airport in Bosaso with flights available from all over the world.

Bus Stations Near Bosaso

  • Bosaso’s central bus station provides regular bus routes from Garowe, Hargeisa and Galkayo.

Other Options

  • Private transports: Taxis and private automobiles are available for visitors who prefer private modes of transportation.

How to Get Around in Bosaso

The best mode of transportation in Bosaso is hired cars, auto-rickshaws, and taxis, which are readily available throughout the city. Visitors can also hire bikes from their hotels or bicycle rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Bosaso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Garowe: Garowe is the capital city of Puntland and located around 600km from Bosaso city. Are being here you can relax and visit the local attractions.
  • Hargeisa: A city around 770km away from Bosaso and features a stunning range of natural scenery, including mountains and deserts.

Conclusion

Travelling to Bosaso provides an incredible adventure that every traveller should experience at least once in their life. With the right planning and guidance, you can enjoy the city’s cultural sites, natural beauty, cultural customs, and gastronomic delights while staying safe and healthy. We hope this travel guide has provided you with the information you need to embark on a successful trip to Bosaso. Have a happy journey!
