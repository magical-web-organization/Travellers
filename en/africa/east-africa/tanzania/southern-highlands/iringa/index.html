Introduction to Iringa

Located in the Southern Highlands of Tanzania, Iringa is a picturesque region that boasts a rich culture, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality. With its inviting climate and diverse tourist attractions, it’s no surprise that Iringa has become a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic African experience. In this article, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more, in Iringa.

List of Places to Visit in Iringa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the history and culture of Iringa, then make sure to visit the following attractions:

  • The Isimila Stone Age Site: This site is a must-visit for history buffs. It features a collection of ancient stone tools and fossils that date back to the Stone Age.
  • The Kalenga Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Iringa region, from pre-colonial times to present day. The exhibits include traditional art, historical artifacts, and photographs.
  • The Neema Crafts Centre: This center offers an interactive experience that showcases the creativity and talents of nearby physically disabled artisans.

Natural Attractions of Iringa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Iringa’s natural beauty is unparalleled. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Ruaha National Park: Known as the “Serengeti of the South,” Ruaha National Park is home to a variety of wildlife, including lions, elephants, and giraffes. It also offers great hiking trails and beautiful landscapes.
  • Kitulo Plateau National Park: Known as “God’s Garden,” this park is home to beautiful fields of wildflowers, waterfalls, and endemic fauna.
  • The Iringa Highlands: The highlands are a network of mountains, valleys, and rolling hills that are popular hiking spots. The beautiful landscapes and stunning views make this an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iringa

If you’re seeking an outdoor adventure, then Iringa has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the most popular:

  • The Livingstone Mountains: This mountain range offers hiking trails that lead to waterfalls, rainforests, and beautiful views of Tanzania’s countryside.
  • The Udzungwa Mountains National Park: This park offers hiking trails that lead to stunning views of the Kilombero Valley and the Njombe district, as well as the beautiful Sanje Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Iringa: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in Iringa. Here are some of the top activities:

  • Cultural tours: Take a tour led by locals to experience the authentic culture and traditions of the region.
  • Wildlife tours: Explore the Ruaha National Park to witness Tanzania’s wildlife.
  • Safari: A safari is a must-do when visiting Tanzania. See the Big 5 (lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard and rhino) on safari excursions, day trips or with multi-day packages.
  • Rock climbing: The Isimila Gorge offers a great rock-climbing experience for adventurers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iringa

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Iringa:

  • Day 1-2: Visit the Isimila Stone Age Site and the Kalenga Historical Museum.
  • Day 3: Take a hiking trip to the Livingstone Mountains.
  • Day 4-5: Enjoy a safari at Ruaha National Park.
  • Day 6: Take a cultural tour of Iringa.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at local marketplaces and drink in the scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Iringa

Must-Try Dishes

When visiting Iringa, make sure to try the following dishes:

  • Wali Wa Nazi: This dish consists of rice cooked in coconut milk and served with meat and/or vegetables. It’s a staple dish of Tanzania.
  • Ugali: Ugali is a maize meal that is often eaten with stews or meats, and is another staple dish of Tanzanian cuisine.
  • Nyama Choma: This is barbecued meat, often goat or beef, that is cooked on an open flame and served with vegetables and spices.

Local Beverages

Local drinks in Iringa include:

  • Kilimanjaro beer: a lager beer brewed in Tanzania
  • Konyagi Spirit, a local gin served with tonic filled with Tanzania’s unique flavors.

Where to Stay in Iringa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great options for accommodation in Iringa. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Iringa Sun Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Iringa and offers comfortable, well-appointed rooms.
  • Sunset Mountain Lodge: This lodge is located in the highlands outside of Iringa, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Neema Crafts Guesthouse: This environmentally friendly guesthouse offers comfortable accommodation and is situated next to a workshop for physically disabled artisans.

What to Buy in Iringa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Iringa is known for its high-quality handicrafts and textiles. Here are some popular souvenirs to bring home:

  • The Iringa Basket: These baskets are handwoven from natural fibers found in the region and are known for their durability and strength.
  • Tingatinga Paintings: Tingatinga is a popular style of East African painting, known for its highly colorful and detailed depictions of wildlife and landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Iringa

It’s important to respect the local culture and customs of Iringa when visiting. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly: Iringa is a conservative region, so it’s important to dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Greet locals: Greetings go a long way! You never know what a simple hello could lead to in Iringa.
  • Respect cultural norms: When visiting Iringa, it’s important to be thoughtful and respectful of cultural differences.

Best Time to Visit Iringa

The best time to visit Iringa is between June and October, when the weather is dry and sunny, and wildlife viewing is at its peak. Iringa can have a tropical climate with long rains from March to May, and short rains between November and January. From June to September, temperatures often reach over 30°C in the day, but can reach as low as 16°C at night.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iringa

Do:

  • Respect the natural surroundings.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try to learn some basic Swahili phrases.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Acknowledge and take photos of locals without their consent.
  • Disrespect customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iringa

Iringa is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some travel tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Don’t flaunt your valuables.
  • Always hire reputable tour guides and taxi drivers.

Health in Iringa

Before traveling to Iringa, it’s recommended to consult with your doctor and get any necessary vaccinations. It’s also important to be aware of the following health risks:

  • Malaria: The risk of malaria is high in Iringa. It’s important to take preventative measures, such as mosquito nets or sprays, and to take anti-malarial medication.
  • Dehydration: Be sure to drink plenty of water and carry enough drinking water on your treks.
  • Altitude sickness: Iringa is a high altitude destination, and altitude sickness can occur. Take necessary precautions if trekking or hiking.

Getting to Iringa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iringa

Iringa Airport is the main airport serving the region. Daily flights from Dar es Salaam via Precision Airways are available.

Bus Stations Near Iringa

A bus ride from Dar es Salaam will take seven to eight hours. Buses make stops in Morogoro, Mikumi and Ifakara before arriving at Iringa Bus station.

Other Options

Private car hire or a taxi is an option for more flexible, personalized transfers. Note that car travel will take several hours and there can be poor road conditions and limited signs in some areas.

How to Get Around in Iringa

The most common and easiest form of transportation to explore Iringa is by hiring a local guide, driver or car hire service. Some accommodation options can organize transport for you. Taxis are also available in Iringa town.

Where to Go Next from Iringa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ruaha National Park: This park is a safari destination, offering 4WD game drives.
  • Morogoro: A bustling town near Kilombero Valley, known for it’s hiking trails, coffee plantations and national parks, and is a four hour drive from Iringa.
  • Udzungwa Mountains National Park: Great for scenic walks and waterfall treks, it’s a three hour drive from Iringa.

Conclusion

Iringa is a fascinating and beautiful region of Tanzania that offers something for everyone - from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Iringa, and that you have an unforgettable trip exploring all that this wonderful region has to offer.
