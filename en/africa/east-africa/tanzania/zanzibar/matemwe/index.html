Introduction to Matemwe

Matemwe is a scenic village located on the northeastern coast of Zanzibar Island, a semi-autonomous region of Tanzania. Home to a long stretch of white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters, this off-the-beaten-path destination is the perfect place for travelers who want to relax, soak up the sun, and experience the culture and traditions of a small Swahili fishing community. Matemwe is also close to a number of natural and cultural attractions, and offers hiking and biking trails, water sports, and local tours.

Local Culture and Customs of Matemwe

Matemwe is a predominantly Muslim community, and visitors are expected to respect the local traditions and customs. It is recommended to dress modestly and cover shoulders and legs when walking around the village. Visitors should also avoid drinking alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit Matemwe

The best time to visit Matemwe is between June and October, during the dry season when the weather is sunny and mild. However, travelers should keep in mind that this is also peak tourist season, and the beaches and attractions can be crowded. The low season, from March to May, offers good deals on accommodation but is characterized by heavy rainfall.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matemwe

Matemwe is a relatively safe village, but visitors should take standard precautions against theft, and avoid walking alone at night. There are no known terrorist threats, but travelers should remain vigilant due to the potential for petty crime.

Health in Matemwe

Visitors should consult a travel health professional before their trip to Matemwe and ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations for common diseases such as hepatitis A and typhoid. It is recommended to drink bottled or boiled water, and avoid salads and raw vegetables. Malaria is endemic in the area, and visitors should take appropriate prophylactic medication.

Getting to Matemwe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matemwe

The nearest airport to Matemwe is Abeid Amani Karume International Airport in Zanzibar City, which is approximately one hour away by road. Domestic flights to Zanzibar are available from Dar Es Salaam and Kilimanjaro airports.

Bus Stations Near Matemwe

Matemwe is connected to Zanzibar City by a regular bus service, with buses leaving from the Darajani Bus Terminal. Taxis and private transfers are also available for hire.

Other Options

Ferries run daily between Dar Es Salaam and Zanzibar, taking approximately two hours to reach Stone Town. From there, travelers can hire a taxi, private transfer, or use the public bus service to reach Matemwe.

How to Get Around in Matemwe

Visitors can explore Matemwe on foot, or rent a bicycle or scooter to explore further afield. Taxi services are also available for hire, and some accommodations offer shuttle services to nearby attractions.
