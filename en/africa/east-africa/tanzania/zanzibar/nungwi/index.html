Nungwi is a quaint fishing village located in the north of Zanzibar, a semi-autonomous region of Tanzania, in East Africa. Known for its pristine white sandy beaches, turquoise ocean waters, and untouched natural beauty, Nungwi is the perfect destination for travelers who seek a laid-back vacation away from the hustle-bustle of the city. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover all the best places to visit, activities, food and drink, accommodation, local culture and customs, safety, transportation, and more in Nungwi.

Introduction to Nungwi

Nungwi is located on the northern tip of Zanzibar and is one of the island’s major tourist destinations. The village is situated on a long stretch of beach, edged by a coral reef, and is famous for its picturesque sunsets. Nungwi is also home to several luxury resorts and hotels, making it an upscale vacation destination.

List of Places to Visit in Nungwi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mnarani Natural Aquarium: This aquarium is a popular attraction for tourists and locals alike because it is home to several species of marine life native to Zanzibar.
  • Nungwi Village: This fishing village is home to several traditional Zanzibari boats, known as dhows, and is a great place to experience local culture.
  • Ruins of Mkokotoni: These ruins are located about 45 minutes south of Nungwi and offer insight into Zanzibari history.

Natural Attractions of Nungwi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kendwa Beach: This stunning beach is located just south of Nungwi, and it is known for its clear water and lively atmosphere.
  • Nungwi Beach: This beach is known for its powder-white sand and is perfect for swimming or sunbathing.
  • Nungwi Natural Aquarium: This is another aquarium in Nungwi that offers an interactive experience where visitors can swim with turtles and feed fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nungwi

Nungwi is not known for its hiking or biking trails, but there are a few scenic routes that you can take.

  • Biking to Kendwa Rocks: Kendwa Rocks is a great destination for a bike ride. The route is relatively straightforward and offers breathtaking ocean views.
  • Walking to Ras Nungwi: Ras Nungwi is located at the northernmost point of Zanzibar and offers stunning views of the ocean. The walk takes about an hour, but it is worth it.

Best Things to Do in Nungwi: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Nungwi is famous for its coral reefs and marine life, including turtles and dolphins.
  • Jet Skiing and Parasailing: Watersports such as jet skiing and parasailing are available in Nungwi.
  • Sunset Dhow Cruise: Take a sunset cruise on a traditional Zanzibari dhow and enjoy the breathtaking views of the ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nungwi

Day 1: Relax on Nungwi Beach and explore the village. Day 2: Go snorkeling or scuba diving in the coral reefs. Day 3: Take a day trip to nearby Kendwa Beach. Day 4: Visit Mnarani Natural Aquarium and Ruins of Mkokotoni. Day 5: Go on a dhow cruise and watch the sunset. Day 6: Take a bike ride to Kendwa Rocks. Day 7: Relax and take in the breathtaking scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Nungwi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Zanzibar is known for its seafood, and Nungwi is no exception. The local restaurants serve fresh catch of the day.
  • Zanzibari Pizza: A unique hybrid of Italian and local cuisine, Zanzibari Pizza is a must-try in Nungwi.
  • Coconut Curry: A spicy, flavorful coconut curry served with rice is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Kilimanjaro Beer: A locally brewed beer that pairs well with seafood.
  • Zanzibar Coffee: A strong and aromatic coffee made with locally grown beans.

Where to Stay in Nungwi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Essque Zalu Zanzibar: A luxury hotel that offers an indulgent experience.
  • Dhow Inn: A budget-friendly hotel located within walking distance to Nungwi Beach.
  • Z Hotel: A boutique hotel known for its modern amenities and stunning views.

What to Buy in Nungwi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Spices: Zanzibar is famous for its spices, and visitors to Nungwi can buy fresh spices from the local markets.
  • Handicrafts: Local artisans make intricate handmade crafts such as baskets, jewelry, and carving.
  • Tinga Tinga Paintings: These brightly colored paintings depict African life and are unique to Tanzania.

Local Culture and Customs of Nungwi

Nungwi is a predominantly Muslim area, and visitors are expected to dress modestly when not on the beach. Locals are hospitable but appreciate when visitors take time to learn a few basic Swahili phrases to communicate.

Best Time to Visit Nungwi

The best time to visit Nungwi is from June to October, when the weather is dry, and temperatures are pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nungwi

Do:

  • Dress modestly outside of the beach.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their permission.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nungwi

Nungwi is a safe area, but visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended on the beach.

Health in Nungwi

Visitors should drink bottled water and carry mosquito repellent to protect against malaria.

Getting to Nungwi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nungwi

The closest airport to Nungwi is Abeid Amani Karume International Airport in Zanzibar City, which is about an hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Nungwi

The local bus system, also known as dala dala, is the most affordable way to get around, but it can be confusing for tourists. There is a local bus station in Nungwi.

Other Options

Taxis and tuk-tuks (motorized rickshaws) are available in Nungwi for visitors who prefer private transportation.

How to Get Around in Nungwi

Most areas in Nungwi are within walking distance of each other, and visitors can also get around by bike or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Nungwi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stone Town: The capital of Zanzibar is an hour away by car and is known for its historic buildings and winding alleyways.
  • Jozani Forest: Located on the south part of the island, Jozani Forest is a protected area that is home to several species of monkeys and other wildlife.
  • Pemba Island: This island is known for its diving and snorkeling sites and is reachable by ferry from Zanzibar.

Conclusion

Nungwi is a destination that offers natural beauty, culture, and history. With pristine beaches, local cuisine and handicrafts, and a vibrant culture, Nungwi is the perfect place to escape and enjoy an authentic travel experience in Zanzibar.
