Introduction to Chake Chake

Located in Pemba Island, in Pembwe and the Southeast, in Tanzania, in East Africa, in Africa, Chake Chake is a charming town worth exploring. The town is the capital of Pemba Island, and it is known for its historical sites and natural attractions. From hiking/biking to exploring historical sites, there is something for everyone in Chake Chake.

List of Places to Visit in Chake Chake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chake Chake Old Fort: The fort was built in the 19th century, and it is one of the oldest buildings in Chake Chake. It is made of coral stone and has traditional Swahili architecture.
  • Mangapwani Ruins: These ruins date back to the 15th century, and they are believed to be the remains of the palace of the Pemba Sultan.
  • Pujini Ruins: The ruins are located in a forest and are believed to be the remains of a palace built by the Omanis.
  • Chwaka Bay: The Bay is the ideal place to experience island life while exploring the mangrove forest and swim in crystal-clear waters.

Natural Attractions of Chake Chake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ngezi Forest Reserve: This national reserve is home to various unique bird species like the Pemba scops owl, pemba green pigeon and pemba flycatcher.
  • Makangale Village: A traditional fishing village located between the mangrove forest and Ngezi forest reserve. Here is an opportunity to observe the traditional way of life of fishermen.
  • Chake Chake Market: A popular market in the town where vendors sell various items. A place to grab a bite too.
  • Vumawimbi Beach: A beautiful beach offering a unique swimming experience on the East Coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chake Chake

Chake Chake is not only known for its cultural and natural attractions but also for its exciting hiking and biking routes. The mountainous terrain of the region makes it an ideal destination for hiking/biking enthusiasts. The popular bike and hike routes in Chake Chake are:

  • Makangale Route: This route begins at Makangale village and stretches through the heart of the Ngezi forest reserve.
  • Chake Chake Old Fort Route: This route goes through the old fort and continues to the outskirts of Chake Chake.

Best Things to Do in Chake Chake: Activities and Tours

  • A Traditional Dhow Boat Tour: Go on a dhow boat tour with experienced local fishermen who impart knowledge about the marine life in Chake Chake.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the coral reefs and exotic marine life in the waters surrounding Chake Chake.
  • Visiting the Pemba Channel Spice Island Farm: A fascinating educational tour of the spice farm to learn about the different spices grown in Pemba.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chake Chake

Day 1: Visit Chake Chake market in the morning and the Mangapwani Ruins in the afternoon.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Makangale Route, stopping at Makangale village along the way.

Day 3: Go on a snorkeling and diving tour of the coral reefs in Vumawimbi beach.

Day 4: Explore Ngezi forest reserve and visit the Pemba Channel Spice Island Farm.

Day 5: Visit Chwaka Bay and experience the traditional way of life.

Day 6: Go on a traditional dhow tour of the waters surrounding Chake Chake.

Day 7: Relax at Vumawimbi beach and take in the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Chake Chake

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pilau Rice: A delicious spiced rice dish that goes well with curry and beef.
  • Fish Curry: A local curry made with fresh fish, spices, and coconut milk.
  • Mkate wa Kumimina: A traditional cake made from maize and coconut for the perfect breakfast snack.

Local Beverages

  • BaoBab Juice: A refreshing drink made from the baobab fruit.
  • Pemba Coconut Juice: Fresh juice made from local coconuts.

Where to Stay in Chake Chake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tharia Lodge: A luxury beachfront resort with a spa, restaurant, and outdoor pool.
  • Tandani Villa: A cozy villa located near Vumawimbi beach offering a serene atmosphere.
  • Swahili Lodge: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Chake Chake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pemba Cloves: locally-grown, aromatic cloves.
  • Pemba Kanga: Vibrant colorful traditional sarongs.
  • Jewelry: Energetic colorful jewelry made with local gemstones.

Local Culture and Customs of Chake Chake

The primary language spoken in Chake Chake is Swahili, and most residents are Muslim. Dress modestly when visiting mosques or villages.

Best Time to Visit Chake Chake

The best time to visit Chake Chake is between June to September and December to March, when the weather is dry and cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chake Chake

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting mosques or villages.
  • Learn a few Swahili phrases.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Wear revealing clothing, especially women.
  • Take photos of or harass locals without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chake Chake

Chake Chake is generally a safe place to visit. However, take standard safety precautions and avoid going out alone at night.

Health in Chake Chake

The recommended vaccine for Pemba Island is the hepatitis A & B, typhoid, and diphtheria-tetanus vaccine. Be prepared to protect yourself against malaria

How to Get Around in Chake Chake

Getting around in Chake Chake is easy by foot or public transportation. Hiring a taxi or car rentals are also an option for more extended trips.
