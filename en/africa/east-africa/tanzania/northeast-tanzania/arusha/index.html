Arusha, nestled at the foot of Mount Meru, is a vibrant city located in the northeast region of Tanzania. Known as the “safari capital” of Tanzania, Arusha is the perfect gateway city for exploring Tanzania’s incredible wildlife and safari parks while also offering a wealth of cultural and natural attractions.

Introduction to Arusha

Arusha is a bustling city that serves as a major commercial and political hub, welcoming visitors from around the world. It is home to over half a million people, and its central location makes it a popular starting point for travelers looking to explore all that Tanzania has to offer. From wildlife safaris to cultural expeditions, Arusha has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Arusha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Tanzanite Experience Museum - Learn about the rare gemstone, Tanzanite, that is only found in Tanzania.
  2. The Arusha Declaration Museum - Explore the history of Tanzania’s fight for independence.
  3. Cultural Heritage Center - Discover traditional crafts and culture of Tanzania.

Natural Attractions of Arusha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Meru - Take a hike to the summit of the fifth highest peak in Africa.
  2. Arusha National Park - Go on a wildlife safari and see the diverse range of wildlife.
  3. Lake Duluti - Enjoy kayaking and other water activities on this beautiful lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arusha

  1. Mount Meru - A three-day trek to the summit with stunning views of Kilimanjaro and the surrounding area.
  2. Olmoti and Empakaai Craters - A day trek to the top of the craters to see stunning views of the Rift Valley.

Best Things to Do in Arusha: Activities and Tours

  1. Wildlife Safari - Go on a full-day game drive to see Tanzania’s incredible wildlife.
  2. Hot Air Balloon Safari - Take a hot air balloon ride above the Serengeti for breathtaking views.
  3. Maasai Village Visit - Learn about the Maasai culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arusha

Day Activity
1 Explore Arusha National Park and hike Mount Meru
2 Visit the Tanzanite Experience Museum and Arusha Declaration Museum
3 Go on a wildlife safari and visit a Maasai Village
4 Take a hot air balloon ride over Serengeti National Park
5 Visit Cultural Heritage Center and learn about local crafts
6 Go on a hiking expedition to Olmoti and Empakaai Craters
7 Take a day trip to Lake Duluti and indulge in water-based activities

Food and Drink Scene of Arusha

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nyama Choma - Grilled meat served with ugali and vegetables.
  2. Ugali - A cornmeal porridge often served as a staple in Tanzanian cuisine.
  3. Chapati - A type of flatbread served with a variety of dishes.

Local Beverages

  1. Tanzanian Coffee - Known around the world for its exceptional aroma and flavor.
  2. Kilimanjaro Beer - A local beer perfect for quenching thirst after a long day of safari.

Where to Stay in Arusha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Four Points by Sheraton Arusha - A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  2. Green Hills of Africa - A serene boutique hotel with beautiful gardens and pools.
  3. McEllys Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Arusha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Maasai Beaded Jewelry - Handcrafted jewelry made by the Maasai tribe.
  2. Tanzanite Stones - Purchase a piece of the rare gemstone found only in Tanzania.
  3. Tinga Tinga Paintings - Bright, vibrant paintings that capture the essence of Tanzania.

Local Culture and Customs of Arusha

Tanzania is a diverse country, with over 120 different ethnic groups. The Maasai people, who are known for their distinctive dress and customs, are prevalent in Arusha. It is important to respect their traditions and culture when interacting with them.

Best Time to Visit Arusha

The best time to visit Arusha is typically during the dry season, between June and October. This is the optimal time for safari and outdoor activities. However, Arusha can be visited year-round, it’s just best to plan accordingly based on the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arusha

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Always carry identification.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and culture.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without asking permission.
  • Walk alone at night.
  • Touch or feed wild animals on a safari.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arusha

Arusha city is generally safe, but it’s still important to take extra precautions when traveling. It’s recommended to avoid walking alone at night and keep your personal belongings secure.

Health in Arusha

It’s recommended to speak with a medical professional before traveling to Arusha to discuss necessary vaccinations and preventative measures for malaria.

Getting to Arusha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arusha

  1. Kilimanjaro International Airport - located about 60 kilometers from Arusha, which is the easiest and most common way to get to Arusha.

Bus Stations Near Arusha

  1. Arusha Bus Station - located in the city center and has daily buses to a variety of nearby cities.

Other Options

  1. Private car or taxi - Can be arranged through hotels, travel agents, or directly with taxi drivers.

How to Get Around in Arusha

Most visitors to Arusha choose to get around the city by taxi, which is cost-effective, safe, and convenient. Alternatively, there is the option to rent a car, but this is not recommended due to the challenging driving conditions in Tanzania.

Where to Go Next from Arusha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Serengeti National Park - One of the most famous national parks in the world, renowned for its abundance of wildlife and incredible landscapes.
  2. Ngorongoro Conservation Area - Home to the famous Ngorongoro Crater, teeming with an incredible diversity of plant and animal species.

Conclusion

Arusha is a vibrant city that offers an incredible array of cultural and natural attractions. From wildlife safaris to hiking and outdoor expeditions, there is something for everyone to experience. With this comprehensive guide, you are sure to have a memorable time in Arusha, Tanzania.
