Introduction to Arusha National Park

Arusha National Park is one of Tanzania’s hidden gems, located in the northeastern region of Tanzania in East Africa. The park is home to stunning landscapes and remarkable wildlife, including elephants, giraffes, zebras, and over 400 species of birds. The diverse landscapes are characterized by lush forests, savannahs, and majestic peaks of Mount Meru and Kilimanjaro. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best things to do in Arusha National Park, where to stay, what to buy and eat, and everything else you need to know to make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Arusha National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arusha National Park has a rich cultural heritage and history that visitors can explore through various museums and historical monuments. The following are some of the best cultural attractions to visit in the park:

  • Ngurdoto Crater - This is one of the park’s most striking landmarks and a popular spot for sightseeing. The crater is surrounded by lush forests and a variety of wildlife species.

  • Tululusia Hill - This hill is located in the western part of the park and is a place of historical significance to the local Maasai people. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill to enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Museum of Natural History - Located near the park’s entrance, this museum showcases the park’s diverse flora and fauna, including a collection of birds, reptiles, and insects.

Natural Attractions of Arusha National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arusha National Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with endless opportunities to explore the park’s diverse natural landscapes. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in the park:

  • Mount Meru - This is the fifth highest mountain in Africa and one of the park’s most iconic landmarks. Visitors can hike to the summit, which takes around 3-4 days, and enjoy breathtaking views of the park’s landscapes.

  • Momella Lakes - These are a group of seven shallow alkaline lakes that are home to a variety of bird species, including flamingos, herons, and pelicans.

  • The Fig Tree Arch - This natural arch is located near the Ngurdoto Crater and is one of the park’s lesser-known attractions. Visitors can hike to the arch and enjoy a picnic while taking in the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arusha National Park

Arusha National Park is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a variety of trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the park:

  • Meru Crater Rim Trail - This is a challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Mount Meru. The trail is about 46 kilometers long and takes around 4 days to complete.

  • Little Meru - This is a shorter alternative to climbing Mount Meru, and takes around 2 days to complete. The trail offers stunning views of the park’s landscapes, including the crater and lake.

  • Momella Nature Walk - This is an easy trail that is perfect for those who want to enjoy a leisurely walk and appreciate the park’s natural beauty. The trail takes you through a lush forest with a variety of bird species and ends at the base of Mount Meru.

Best Things to Do in Arusha National Park: Activities and Tours

Arusha National Park offers visitors a range of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do in the park:

  • Game Drives - This is the most popular activity in the park and allows visitors to get up close with the park’s diverse wildlife, including leopards, buffalos, and baboons.

  • Canoeing - Visitors can take a guided canoe tour of the Momella Lakes and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Climbing Mount Meru - This is a challenging but rewarding experience that offers breathtaking views of the park’s landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arusha National Park

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for visitors who want to explore Arusha National Park:

Day 1: Arrival in Arusha and check-in at a hotel Day 2: Hiking to the summit of Mount Meru Day 3: Game drives and guided tours of the park Day 4: Canoeing in the Momella Lakes Day 5: Visit the Ngurdoto Crater and Fig Tree Arch Day 6: Cultural tour of the Maasai villages near the park Day 7: Departure from Arusha

Food and Drink Scene of Arusha National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Arusha National Park is home to a range of delicious local dishes that visitors should try during their stay. Here are some of the must-try dishes in the park:

  • Nyama Choma - This is a popular dish in Tanzania and consists of roasted meat, usually goat or beef, served with a variety of side dishes such as ugali and vegetables.

  • Pilau - This is a fragrant rice dish that is cooked with local spices and often served with meat or vegetables.

  • Chips Mayai - This is a simple but delicious dish that consists of fries and eggs mixed and cooked together.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Arusha National Park can enjoy a variety of local beverages during their stay. Here are some of the best drinks to try in the park:

  • Kahawa - This is a strong and delicious coffee brewed from locally grown beans.

  • Tanzanian Beer - There are a variety of local beers to choose from in Tanzania, including Safari and Kilimanjaro.

  • Maji ya Chai - This is a traditional drink made from tea leaves and flavored with spices such as cinnamon and ginger.

Where to Stay in Arusha National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arusha National Park has a range of accommodation options for visitors to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay in the park:

  • Arusha Coffee Lodge - This luxurious lodge is located on a coffee plantation and offers breathtaking views of Mount Meru. The lodge’s facilities include a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

  • Mount Meru Game Lodge - This lodge is located just outside the park and offers comfortable accommodations and on-site game drives.

  • Meru View Lodge - This budget-friendly lodge is located near the park’s entrance and offers camping and self-catering accommodations.

What to Buy in Arusha National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Arusha National Park can purchase a range of local products and souvenirs during their stay. Here are some of the best things to buy in the park:

  • Maasai Beadwork - The Maasai people are known for their elaborate beadwork, and visitors can purchase handmade jewelry, belts, and other accessories.

  • Tanzanite - This is a rare gemstone that is found only in Tanzania. Visitors can purchase Tanzanite jewelry and other items in the park.

  • Coffee - Arusha is known for its high-quality coffee, and visitors can purchase locally grown coffee beans to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Arusha National Park

Arusha National Park is home to a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and traditions during their stay. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Respect Local Communities - Visitors should be respectful of local communities and seek their permission before taking photographs or exploring cultural sites.

  • Dress Appropriately - Visitors should dress modestly and avoid shorts or revealing clothing when visiting cultural or religious sites.

  • Tipping - Tipping is not expected in Tanzania but is appreciated for exceptional service.

Best Time to Visit Arusha National Park

The best time to visit Arusha National Park is during the dry season, from June to October. This is when the park is less crowded, and wildlife sightings are more frequent. The wet season, from November to May, can be challenging for hiking and game drives due to heavy rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arusha National Park

Do:

  • Respect the Wildlife - Visitors should always maintain a safe distance from wildlife and avoid disturbing their natural habitats.

  • Hire a Guide - A guide can provide valuable insights and enhance your overall experience of the park.

  • Stay Hydrated - The park’s high altitudes can cause dehydration, so visitors should carry plenty of water during their hikes and tours.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Visitors should always dispose of their trash in designated areas and avoid leaving any waste behind.

  • Feed Wildlife - Feeding wildlife is prohibited in the park and can be dangerous for both visitors and animals.

  • Wander Off the Trails - Visitors should stick to designated trails and avoid wandering off into the park’s wilderness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arusha National Park

Arusha National Park is generally safe for visitors, but it’s essential to take certain precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable stay. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Carry a First Aid Kit - Visitors should carry a basic first aid kit for minor injuries during hikes and tours.

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night - Visitors should avoid walking alone at night and stay within the park’s designated boundaries.

Health in Arusha National Park

Visitors to Arusha National Park should take certain health precautions to avoid illnesses and diseases. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink Bottled Water - Visitors should only drink bottled or boiled water to avoid getting sick from contaminated water sources.

  • Get Vaccinated - It’s recommended to get vaccinated for diseases such as typhoid and hepatitis A before traveling to Tanzania.

Getting to Arusha National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arusha National Park is easily accessible from major cities in Tanzania and neighboring countries. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Arusha National Park

  • Kilimanjaro International Airport - This is the nearest airport to the park and is located about 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Arusha National Park

  • Arusha Bus Station - This is the main bus terminal in Arusha and offers regular bus services to the park.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car in Arusha or other major cities in Tanzania and drive to the park.

How to Get Around in Arusha National Park

The best way to explore Arusha National Park is by taking a guided tour or hiring a car with a driver. Visitors can also rent bicycles or walk along designated trails.

Where to Go Next from Arusha National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Arusha National Park can explore other nearby destinations and attractions in Tanzania, including:

  • Serengeti National Park - This is Tanzania’s largest national park and is renowned for its wildlife and migration of wildebeests and zebras.

  • Ngorongoro Crater - This is the world’s largest inactive volcanic crater and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

  • Zanzibar - This is a group of islands off the coast of Tanzania and a popular destination for beach holidays and water sports.

Conclusion

Arusha National Park is a magical and diverse destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, a cultural explorer, or a wildlife lover, there’s plenty to see and do in this remarkable park. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and make the most out of your stay in Arusha National Park.
