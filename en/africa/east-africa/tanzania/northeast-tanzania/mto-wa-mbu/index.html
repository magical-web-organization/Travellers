Mto Wa Mbu is a small town located in Northeast Tanzania, in East Africa. It is situated at the foot of the Great Rift Valley, surrounded by lush greenery, and is known for its vibrant culture and diverse scenery. With its range of attractions and activities, Mto Wa Mbu has something to offer for every type of traveler. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to this beautiful town:

Introduction to Mto Wa Mbu

Mto Wa Mbu is a charming town located in the Manyara Region of Tanzania. Its name means “Mosquito River” in Swahili, and it got its name from the many mosquitoes that used to breed near the river that flows through the town. The town is home to over 120 different ethnic groups, each with their unique languages, traditions, and customs. This cultural diversity makes Mto Wa Mbu a fascinating and vibrant place to visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Mto Wa Mbu

Mto Wa Mbu has a rich cultural heritage, with over 120 different ethnic groups living in the town. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes and greetings.

Best Time to Visit Mto Wa Mbu

The best time to visit Mto Wa Mbu is from June to October, during the dry season. This period offers the best wildlife viewing opportunities in the nearby national parks, and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mto Wa Mbu

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Mto Wa Mbu:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting cultural sites or local villages
  • Learn a few words of Swahili to communicate with locals
  • Try the local cuisine and support local businesses

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their permission
  • Leave trash or litter in the town or national parks
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions

Safety and Health

While Mto Wa Mbu is generally a safe town to visit, visitors should take common-sense precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Mto Wa Mbu

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables
  • Follow the advice of your guide while on safari

Health in Mto Wa Mbu

  • Stay hydrated and drink only bottled water
  • Take mosquito repellent and use it liberally
  • Get any necessary vaccinations before traveling to Tanzania
