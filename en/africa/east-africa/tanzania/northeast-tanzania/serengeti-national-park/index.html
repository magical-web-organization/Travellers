A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Serengeti National Park

Serengeti National Park is a world-renowned wildlife reserve located in Northeast Tanzania, East Africa. With sprawling grasslands, dense forests and home to the greatest wildlife event on the planet, the Great Migration, this national park has become one of the most sought after travel destinations in the world.

In this guide, we will provide you with all the necessary information you need to plan your trip to Serengeti National Park. From popular tourist attractions to lesser-known hidden gems, this guide will cover everything you need to know to make the most of your time in Serengeti.

Introduction to Serengeti National Park

The Serengeti is one of the oldest and most renowned national parks in Tanzania. It is home to over 70 species of large mammals and over 500 species of birds. The name Serengeti comes from the Maasai language and means “endless plains”. The park’s wide-open spaces provide a unique opportunity to get up close and personal with some of Africa’s most iconic animals.

List of Places to Visit in Serengeti National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Olduvai Gorge - A historical site that contains a collection of fossils, remains, and artifacts that display evidence of human existence for over 2 million years.
  2. Africa Amini Maasai Cultural Village - A cultural center that showcases traditional Maasai dances and rituals, and offers visitors an opportunity to interact with the local communities and appreciate their way of life.

Natural Attractions of Serengeti National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ngorongoro Crater - A natural wonder and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is the world’s largest unbroken caldera that teems with wildlife, including all members of the ‘Big Five’.
  2. Lake Victoria - The largest lake in Africa and the second-largest freshwater lake in the world, Lake Victoria is a popular location for birdwatchers, fishermen, and water sports enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Serengeti National Park

  1. Empakaai Crater Hike - A scenic hike that takes you to the heart of the Ngorongoro Conservation Area, an area with picturesque landscapes and stunning views of the crater.
  2. Lobo Trail - A gentle hiking trail that takes you through the Lobo Hills, an area known for its incredible wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in Serengeti National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Hot Air Balloon Safari - An exhilarating experience that allows you to witness the Great Migration from above and see the immense herds of wildebeests and zebras crossing the Mara River.
  2. Game Drives - Witness the amazing African wildlife on game drives through the different types of flora and fauna found in the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Serengeti National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nyama Choma - Grilled meat that is a staple diet of the locals.
  2. Ugali - A typically African dish made with maize flour that has a similar texture to polenta.

Local Beverages

  1. Amarula - A cream liqueur made from the fruit of the African Marula tree.
  2. Kilimanjaro Beer - An iconic Tanzanian beer that is brewed locally.

Where to Stay in Serengeti National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Four Seasons Safari Lodge Serengeti - A luxurious safari lodge with 77 guest rooms located in the heart of the Serengeti.
  2. Kati Kati Tented Camp - A budget-friendly option perfect for backpackers and those looking for a unique glamping experience.
  3. Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge - A lavish hotel that offers breathtaking views of the savannah and phenomenal wildlife experiences.

What to Buy in Serengeti National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tinga Tinga Paintings - Colorful hand-painted artwork that depicts African life and culture.
  2. Maasai Jewelry - Intricately beaded jewelry that is handmade by the Maasai people.

Local Culture and Customs of Serengeti National Park

The Maasai are the most prominent tribe that inhabits the area surrounding the Serengeti. Their culture and customs are deeply rooted in their daily life and have been passed down from generation to generation. Visitors are encouraged to respect the Maasai people, their way of life, and their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Serengeti National Park

The dry season, from June to October, is the best time to visit the park. During this time, the weather is mild, and the animals congregate around water sources, making it easier to spot them. The wet season, from November to May, can be challenging to navigate as some areas of the park become inaccessible due to flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Serengeti National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  2. Follow the park rules and regulations.
  3. Carry appropriate and comfortable clothing, light and comfortable hiking shoes, sunscreen, and insect repellant.

Don’t:

  1. Feed the animals or throw anything in the park.
  2. Get out of your vehicle while on a game drive.
  3. Take photos with the locals or wildlife without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Serengeti National Park

It is safe to visit Serengeti National Park. However, visitors are encouraged to follow the park’s rules and regulations, and stay in designated safe areas. As always, it is recommended to take necessary precautions such as keeping your valuables safe and avoiding walking alone through the park.

Health in Serengeti National Park

Visitors are advised to seek medical advice before traveling to Serengeti National Park. It is essential to take necessary vaccinations and precautions for malaria, as this is a prevalent issue in the region. Visitors should also carry sufficient amounts of mosquito repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Serengeti National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Serengeti National Park

  1. Kilimanjaro Airport - The closest airport to Serengeti National Park.
  2. Arusha Airport - A domestic airport with frequent flights to Kilimanjaro Airport.

Bus Stations Near Serengeti National Park

  1. Arusha Bus Station - The main bus station in Arusha, Tanzania, with regular connections to Serengeti National Park.

Other Options

  1. Car Rental - Renting a car is a popular transportation option in Tanzania.
  2. Private Tour Operators - Organized private tours are available from several tour operators at different price points.

How to Get Around in Serengeti National Park

Visitors can use 4x4 vehicles rentals, which are available at different prices from tour operators or car rental companies to move around in the park.

Where to Go Next from Serengeti National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Zanzibar - A tropical paradise located off the coast of Tanzania with pristine beaches and a mix of Arab, Indian, and African cultures.
  2. Mount Kilimanjaro - A dormant volcano located in northern Tanzania, and the highest peak in Africa, for a challenging climbing adventure.

Conclusion

Serengeti National Park is a wildlife enthusiast’s paradise, and a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Tanzania. With this comprehensive guide, you have everything you need to make the most of your trip, from the best places to visit to where to stay and everything in between. Don’t miss out on the chance to witness the Great Migration and experience the incredible natural beauty that the Serengeti has to offer. Happy travels!
