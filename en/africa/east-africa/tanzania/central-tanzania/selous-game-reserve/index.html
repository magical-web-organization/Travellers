Introduction to Selous Game Reserve

Located in Central Tanzania, Selous Game Reserve is one of the largest protected areas in Africa, covering an area of over 50,000 square kilometers. The game reserve is named after Frederick Selous, a famous big game hunter and explorer, who was killed during World War I while fighting in East Africa. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and boasts a diverse range of wildlife, including the African elephant, African wild dog, and rare black rhinoceros.

List of Places to Visit in Selous Game Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Selous Game Reserve has plenty of museums and historical places to explore. These include:

  • The Rufiji River Camp: Known for its rich history, the Rufiji River Camp was originally used as a hunting camp by British hunters in the 1950s.

  • The Stiegler’s Gorge Power Station: Located on the Rufiji River, this power station was built in the 1960s to provide electricity to Tanzania. It is named after German engineer, Franz Stiegler.

Natural Attractions of Selous Game Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Selous Game Reserve is breathtaking, with vast wetlands, rivers, and forests. Here are some natural attractions to visit:

  • Lake Tagalala: The largest lake in Selous Game Reserve, Lake Tagalala is known for its resident hippos and crocodiles. You can take a boat safari to view them up close.

  • Hot Springs: The hot springs of Maji Moto and Mto wa Mbu are popular attractions in Selous Game Reserve. The water in these springs is believed to have some therapeutic benefits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Selous Game Reserve

The best way to explore the natural wonders of Selous Game Reserve is on foot or by bike. Here are some hiking and biking routes to try:

  • Mtemere Nature Trail: This trail is about 2.5 kilometers long and takes you through the forest, providing excellent views of the Rufiji River.

  • Kirurumu-Kibogo Trail: This is a longer trail, about 6 kilometers long, that takes you through the bush and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Selous Game Reserve: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Selous Game Reserve. Here are some of the best:

  • Game Drives: Selous Game Reserve is famous for its wildlife, and game drives are a great way to explore the various ecosystems and see the resident animals.

  • Boat Safaris: The Rufiji River is a lifeline of Selous Game Reserve, and boat safaris offer a unique perspective of the reserve’s flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Selous Game Reserve

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Selous Game Reserve:

  • Day 1: Arrival and settling in at the Rufiji River Camp.

  • Day 2: Game drive in the northern part of the reserve, with a stop at the hot springs.

  • Day 3: Boat safari on the Rufiji River, with a stop at Lake Tagalala.

  • Day 4: Walking safari in the southern part of the reserve.

  • Day 5: Hike the Kirurumu-Kibogo Trail.

  • Day 6: Cultural tour of the nearby village and meeting with the locals.

  • Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Selous Game Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Selous Game Reserve is diverse, and there are plenty of local dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Ugali: A staple food in Tanzania, ugali is a cornmeal porridge that is served with meat or vegetables.

  • Nyama Choma: Grilled meat is a popular dish in Selous Game Reserve, and nyama choma is one of the most famous.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking to quench your thirst, here are some local beverages to try in Selous Game Reserve:

  • Mbege: A traditional beer made from bananas, mbege is a popular drink among the locals.

  • Chai: Tanzania’s version of tea, chai is often served with milk and sugar.

Where to Stay in Selous Game Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Selous Game Reserve to suit every budget. Here are some of the best:

  • Rufiji River Camp: Offering tented accommodation with en-suite bathrooms, Rufiji River Camp is a great option for those who want to be close to the wildlife.

  • Sand Rivers Selous: Located on the Rufiji River, this lodge offers luxurious accommodation with stunning views of the reserve.

What to Buy in Selous Game Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, here are some options:

  • Baskets: Made by the locals, baskets are a popular souvenir in Selous Game Reserve.

  • Wooden Carvings: Hand-carved wooden animals and masks are also available for purchase in Selous Game Reserve.

Local Culture and Customs of Selous Game Reserve

Tanzanian culture is diverse, and Selous Game Reserve is no exception. Here are some local customs and traditions you should be aware of:

  • Greetings: Greetings are an essential part of Tanzanian culture, and it’s customary to greet people you meet with “habari” (hello) or “shikamoo” (respectful greeting).

  • Dress Code: Tanzanians tend to dress conservatively, so it’s best to avoid wearing revealing clothing.

Best Time to Visit Selous Game Reserve

The best time to visit Selous Game Reserve is during the dry season, which runs from June to October. During this time, the wildlife is concentrated around water sources, making it easier to spot animals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Selous Game Reserve

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.

  • Hire local guides and support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the environment.

  • Approach the wildlife too closely or attempt to feed them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Selous Game Reserve

While Selous Game Reserve is safe to visit, there are certain precautions you should take:

  • Hire a reputable guide.

  • Do not wander off on your own.

Health in Selous Game Reserve

Make sure to take the following precautions to stay healthy in Selous Game Reserve:

  • Check with your doctor to ensure you are up to date on all vaccinations.

  • Drink bottled or treated water.

Getting to Selous Game Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Selous Game Reserve

The nearest international airport to Selous Game Reserve is Julius Nyerere International Airport in Dar es Salaam. From there, you can take a connecting flight to Mtemere Airstrip, which is located in the reserve.

Bus Stations Near Selous Game Reserve

There are no direct bus routes to Selous Game Reserve, but you can take a bus from Dar es Salaam to Kisarawe and then from there to Kibiti. From Kibiti, you can hire a private car to take you to the reserve.

Other Options

Another option to consider is hiring a private car or taking a taxi from Dar es Salaam to Selous Game Reserve, which takes about six hours.

How to Get Around in Selous Game Reserve

The best way to get around Selous Game Reserve is by hiring a local guide, who can arrange transportation for you. Game drives and boat safaris are usually part of the package, but you can also hire bicycles or take walking tours as well.

Where to Go Next from Selous Game Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Zanzibar: Known for its white sandy beaches and rich history, Zanzibar is an excellent destination to visit after Selous Game Reserve.

  • Mikumi National Park: Located to the west of Selous Game Reserve, Mikumi National Park is another great wildlife destination to explore.

Conclusion

Selous Game Reserve is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty of African wildlife and culture. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip with ease and experience all that Selous Game Reserve has to offer.
