Morogoro is a hidden gem in Central Tanzania, offering tourists a chance to explore its unspoiled natural attractions, rich culture, and history. If you are planning a trip to Tanzania, Morogoro should definitely be on your list of places to visit. Read on for a comprehensive tour guide that covers essential details about this beautiful city.

Introduction to Morogoro

Morogoro is a city located in the eastern part of Tanzania, in East Africa. It is the capital of the Morogoro Region and the country’s sixth-largest city. Morogoro is home to a diverse population, with different ethnic groups, cultures, and religions represented. The city has a warm and humid climate, with temperatures averaging at 28°C.

Morogoro is known for its lush greenery, stunning mountains, and valleys. It is also home to several wildlife reserves, making it a popular destination for nature lovers. The city is also famous for its rich history dating back to the colonial era.

List of Places to Visit in Morogoro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ngerengere Ruins: Located 30 km from Morogoro, the Ngerengere Ruins are a series of ancient structures that date back to the 13th century. They are believed to have been a center of trade and religion during that period.

  2. Museum of the Uluguru Mountains: This museum is dedicated to the culture and history of the Uluguru people, an ethnic group that has lived in the region for centuries.

  3. Sokoine University of Agriculture: Founded in 1984, this university is one of the top agriculture institutions in Africa. It is named after the late Tanzanian Prime Minister, Edward Moringe Sokoine.

Natural Attractions of Morogoro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ulumuru Nature Reserve: This reserve is located in the Uluguru Mountains and covers over 24,000 hectares of land. It is home to over 400 species of birds, as well as several different mammals and reptiles.

  2. Ndanda Falls: This stunning waterfall is located just outside of Morogoro and provides visitors with a chance to swim and relax in the cool waters.

  3. Morogoro Trekking Trails: Morogoro is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails leading up to the Uluguru Mountains. The most popular routes are the Lukwangule Ridge and the Bunduki Trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morogoro

  1. Lukwangule Ridge: This trail is 6.2 km long and takes around 4 hours to complete. It is a moderately challenging hike, with beautiful views of the Uluguru Mountains.

  2. Bunduki Trail: This trail is 12 km long and takes around 6 hours to complete. It is a challenging hike, with steep climbs and descents. The trail offers stunning views of the Uluguru Mountains and the surrounding valleys.

Best Things to Do in Morogoro: Activities and Tours

  1. Safari Tours: Morogoro is home to several wildlife reserves, including the Mikumi National Park and Selous Game Reserve. Visitors can go on a game drive or a walking safari to see different animals like elephants, lions, and giraffes.

  2. Cultural Tours: Visitors can learn about the local culture by visiting a traditional village or attending a cultural festival.

  3. Canoeing: The Kilombero River is perfect for canoeing, with its calm waters and stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Morogoro

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pilau: This is a popular rice dish flavored with spices like cumin, cinnamon, and cardamom.

  2. Nyama Choma: This is a grilled meat dish, usually made with beef or goat meat. It is served with a side of ugali, a type of cornmeal porridge.

Local Beverages

  1. Mbege: This is a traditional Tanzanian beer made from millet or maize.

  2. Chai: Tea is a staple drink in Tanzania, and Morogoro is no exception. Visitors can try the local spiced tea, which is usually made with ginger and cardamom.

Where to Stay in Morogoro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Morogoro Hotel: This is a luxury hotel located in the city center. It offers a swimming pool, a fitness center, and several restaurants.

  2. Kiboko Lodge: This lodge is located in the Uluguru Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding areas. It is perfect for visitors who want to be close to nature.

  3. Motel Mzizima: This is a budget-friendly motel located in the city center. It is perfect for visitors who want to save money on accommodation.

What to Buy in Morogoro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kanga: This is a colorful piece of fabric often worn by women in Tanzania. It can be used as a wrap, a headscarf, or a beach towel.

  2. Maasai Beaded Jewelry: Maasai beads are a popular souvenir in Tanzania. Visitors can buy necklaces, bracelets, and earrings made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Morogoro

Morogoro is home to several different cultural groups, each with its own language, food, and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting traditional villages or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Morogoro

The best time to visit Morogoro is between June and October, when the weather is dry and cool. This is also the best time to see wildlife in the nearby parks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morogoro

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting traditional villages or religious sites.

  2. Try the local food and drinks.

  3. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in public displays of affection.

  2. Take photos of people without their permission.

  3. Enter sacred or restricted areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morogoro

Morogoro is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should be cautious when walking alone at night or in crowded areas. Keep your valuables close to you and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Morogoro

Visitors should take precautions against malaria, which is prevalent in the area. It is recommended to take anti-malarial medication and use insect repellent.

Getting to Morogoro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morogoro

The closest international airport to Morogoro is Julius Nyerere International Airport in Dar es Salaam. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Morogoro.

Bus Stations Near Morogoro

Morogoro has two bus stations: Msamvu and Mwembesongo. Buses from Dar es Salaam, Dodoma, and other nearby cities stop at these stations.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Morogoro from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Morogoro

The best way to get around Morogoro is by taxi or tuk-tuk. Visitors can also hire a car or bike to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Morogoro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Dar es Salaam: Tanzania’s largest city is just a few hours’ drive from Morogoro. Visitors can explore its museums, beaches, and markets.

  2. Mikumi National Park: This park is located just a few hours’ drive from Morogoro and is home to a variety of wildlife, including lions, elephants, and zebras.

  3. Zanzibar: This group of islands off the coast of Tanzania is known for its white sand beaches, coral reefs, and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Morogoro is a must-visit destination in Tanzania, offering visitors a chance to explore its natural beauty, rich culture, and history. From hiking in the Uluguru Mountains to canoeing down the Kilombero River, there is no shortage of activities to do in Morogoro. Plan your Tanzania trip today and don’t miss out on this hidden gem.
