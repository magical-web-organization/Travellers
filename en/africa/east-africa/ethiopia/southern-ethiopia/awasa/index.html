Looking for a unique travel destination in Ethiopia, away from the crowds and typical tourist spots? Look no further than Awasa, a scenic city located in Southern Ethiopia. Perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, culture seekers, and nature lovers, Awasa offers a range of attractions and experiences for travelers of all ages.

Introduction to Awasa

Awasa is a serene and peaceful city located in the Great Rift Valley of Ethiopia, approximately 275 km southeast of Addis Ababa, the capital city. Known for its beautiful Lake Awasa, lush forests, coffee plantations, and varied wildlife, Awasa is often referred to as Ethiopia’s garden city. The city is home to about 350,000 people, predominantly the Sidama ethnic group, known for their farming traditions and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Awasa

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature or adventure, Awasa has plenty to offer. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sidama Cultural Centre and Museum: Located in the heart of Awasa, this museum is dedicated to the Sidama people and showcases their history, traditional customs, beliefs, and crafts.
  • Wondo Genet College of Forestry and Natural Resources: This historic institution, established in 1964, houses a museum that provides a glimpse into the history of Ethiopian forests, biodiversity conservation, and sustainable development.
  • Senkele Wildlife Sanctuary: About 30km from Awasa, this sanctuary is home to the endemic Swayne’s hartebeest, as well as zebras, baboons, and over 80 bird species. Guided tours can be arranged.

Natural Attractions of Awasa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Awasa: One of the most beautiful attractions in Awasa, Lake Awasa offers a serene and peaceful environment for bird watching, boat riding, fishing, and swimming. You can also enjoy a picnic and watch the sunset on the lake’s shores.
  • Mount Fike: This mountain, located 15 km from Awasa, offers a scenic view of Awasa and its surroundings. It’s a popular spot for hiking and picnics.
  • Yirgalem Forest: About 40km from Awasa, this beautiful forest offers nature lovers endless opportunities for hiking, birding, and wildlife viewing. You can also visit the Aregash Lodge, a traditional eco-lodge within the forest, and learn about Sidama customs and rituals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Awasa

For those seeking outdoor adventure, Awasa offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the city’s beautiful landscapes and natural wonders. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Awasa–Yirgalem Hiking Trail: This trail takes you through beautiful coffee plantations, forests, and stunning landscapes. You can enjoy a picnic while listening to the sounds of nature and watch the sunset over the mountains.
  • Awasa–Wondo Genet Biking Trail: This route takes you through the Great Rift Valley’s beautiful landscapes, forests, and fresh streams. It’s an excellent way to explore local life and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Awasa: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Awasa. Here are some of the best:

  • Traditional Coffee Ceremony: The Sidama people are known for their coffee, and a traditional coffee ceremony is a must-do experience when visiting Awasa. You’ll learn about the coffee-making process, try the freshly brewed coffee, and enjoy delicious local snacks.
  • Visit local markets: Awasa has several local markets where you can find traditional crafts, fresh fruits, and vegetables, as well as spices and clothing. These markets offer an excellent opportunity to meet locals and learn more about the city’s culture and customs.
  • Bird watching: With over 400 bird species, Ethiopia is a bird-watcher’s paradise, and Awasa is no exception. You can watch different species of birds in Lake Awasa or visit Senkele Wildlife Sanctuary, where you will have a chance to see critically endangered Swayne’s hartebeest and other wildlife species.

Food and Drink Scene of Awasa

No visit to Awasa is complete without trying some of its delicious local dishes and drinks. Here are some must-try items:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kitfo: A traditional Ethiopian dish made from raw beef and seasoned with spices and clarified butter.
  • Injera: A sourdough flatbread that’s a staple of Ethiopian cuisine. It’s served with different stews and curries.
  • Doro Wat: A spicy chicken stew served with injera.

Local Beverages

  • Sidama Coffee: Known for its rich flavor and aroma, Sidama coffee is a must-try when visiting Awasa. You can also visit local coffee farms and learn about the coffee-making process.
  • Tella: A local beer made from barley and teff grains. It’s often served in a jar or a large bottle.

Where to Stay in Awasa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Awasa has several accommodation options to suit different budgets and travel styles. Here are some of the best:

  • Haile Resort Awasa: A luxurious resort located on the shores of Lake Awasa, offering stunning lake views, elegant rooms, spa and wellness facilities, and an outdoor pool.
  • Lakeview Hotel and Resort: Another hotel located on the shores of Lake Awasa, offering comfortable rooms, delicious food, and excellent service.
  • Asse Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the city centre, offering clean and comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Awasa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From colorful textiles to traditional crafts and souvenirs, Awasa has plenty of local products to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Some popular items include:

  • Basketry: Hand-woven baskets made of grass, bamboo, and other materials, perfect for storage or decoration.
  • Shawls and scarves: Ethiopia is known for its unique textiles, and Awasa is no exception. You can find colorful shawls and scarves made of cotton, silk, or wool.
  • Spices and coffee: Awasa is a center for coffee and spice production, and you can find a variety of fresh and aromatic coffee beans, as well as spices like cumin, cardamom, and cinnamon.

Local Culture and Customs of Awasa

Awasa is a city steeped in tradition and culture, and it’s important to respect local customs and beliefs. Here are a few to keep in mind:

  • Dressing modestly: Ethiopia is a conservative country, and it’s essential to dress appropriately, especially in religious places like churches and mosques.
  • Greeting etiquette: Ethiopians value hospitality and respect, and it’s customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile.
  • Eating with right hand: Unlike in the West, Ethiopians eat with their right hand, and it’s essential to respect this custom when dining with locals.

Best Time to Visit Awasa

The best time to visit Awasa is during the dry season, which runs from October to May. The weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, bird watching, and visiting local attractions. The rainy season, from June to September, can make some roads and attractions inaccessible, and the weather can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Awasa

Here’s a quick list of do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Awasa:

Do:

  • Learn basic Amharic phrases: Ethiopians appreciate it when visitors make an effort to learn some local phrases, such as greetings and thank-you.
  • Tip service providers: It’s customary to tip waiters, tour guides, and hotel staff when good service is provided.
  • Try the local cuisine: Awasa is known for its delicious food and unique coffee, so be sure to try some of the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission: It’s essential to ask for permission before taking someone’s photo, especially in rural areas or among the Sidama people, who may not feel comfortable being photographed.
  • Public display of affection: Ethiopians are conservative when it comes to displays of affection, and it’s important to avoid any public displays of affection, such as holding hands, kissing, or hugging.
  • Disrespect religious customs: Ethiopia is a deeply religious country, and it’s important to respect local religious customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

While Awasa is generally safe for travelers, some precautions are necessary to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Awasa

  • Avoid walking alone at night: As in any city, it’s safer to walk in groups, especially at night. Avoid dark and deserted areas and be aware of your surroundings.
  • Beware of pickpockets: Petty theft can occur in crowded areas like markets or bus stations. Keep your valuables close to you, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Awasa

  • Drink bottled or boiled water: Tap water in Ethiopia is not safe for drinking, so make sure to drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Get vaccinated: Before traveling to Ethiopia, it’s essential to consult your doctor and get vaccinated for yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid.
  • Use mosquito repellent: Malaria is prevalent in Ethiopia, so it’s important to use mosquito repellent and take other precautions, such as wearing long-sleeved clothing and sleeping under a mosquito net.

Getting to Awasa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Awasa is relatively easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Awasa

The nearest airport to Awasa is Hawassa Airport, which is approximately 10km from the city center. Ethiopian Airlines operates daily flights from Addis Ababa to Hawassa.

Bus Stations Near Awasa

Several bus stations serve Awasa, with daily connections to most major cities and towns in Ethiopia. Some of the major bus companies include Selam Bus, Sky Bus, and Dahlak Bus.

Other Options

Taxis, minivans, and tuk-tuks are widely available in Awasa and can be used for short trips or day trips to nearby attractions. It’s essential to agree on a fare before getting in.

How to Get Around in Awasa

Walking or biking is the best option for exploring Awasa’s center, while taxis and buses can be used for longer trips or reaching nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Awasa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Awasa serves as an excellent base for exploring other destinations in Southern Ethiopia. Here are some nearby attractions worth adding to your itinerary:

  • Arba Minch: A beautiful town known for its two scenic lakes, Abaya and Chamo, and the Nechisar National Park, which is home to various wildlife species.
  • Konso: A cultural and historical town known for its unique terraced agriculture, traditional dress, and handicrafts.
  • Dorze: A small village located in the Gamo Highlands, known for its unique woven bamboo houses, local dishes, and traditional textile crafts.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a unique and authentic Ethiopian travel experience, then Awasa is the perfect destination. From stunning natural scenery and wildlife to beautiful cultural and historical attractions, Awasa offers something for everyone. So pack your bags and explore this beautiful city, and discover Ethiopia’s hidden gem.
