Introduction to Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is an oasis of natural beauty located in Southern Ethiopia, in East Africa. Named after its two most prominent lakes - Abijatta and Shalla - this national park spans over 887.88 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, landscapes, and cultural attractions. The park is known for its stunning scenery, including scenic birdwatching opportunities and outdoor adventures such as hiking, biking, and canoeing.

List of Places to Visit in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rift Valley Research Centre

One of the most popular museums in the park is the Rift Valley Research Centre, which provides extensive information about the wildlife, landscapes, and natural history of the region. Visitors can learn about the traditional lifestyles and customs of the local communities through interactive exhibits and displays.

Volcanic Mountains

Another popular cultural attraction is the Volcanic Mountains, which serve as a testament to the region’s geological history. Visitors can explore the area’s active and dormant volcanoes, climb the mountain peaks, and enjoy the panoramic vistas.

Natural Attractions of Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Birdwatching

Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is home to more than 300 species of birds, making it a birdwatcher’s paradise. Visitors can take a guided tour and spot a vast range of bird species, including eagles, flamingos, spoonbills, pelicans, and more.

Abijatta Lake

Abijatta Lake is one of the most prominent lakes in the park, known for its vibrant pink hue and high salt levels. Visitors can take a dip in the saltwater lake or walk along its scenic shoreline.

Shalla Lake

Shalla Lake is the deepest lake in the park and is surrounded by dense vegetation and wooded areas. Visitors can explore the lake by canoe or kayak and enjoy the calm waters and stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park offers a vast range of hiking and biking trails that traverse the park’s diverse landscape. Some of the popular routes include the Shalla volcano, the natural hot springs, and the Hyena Cave Trail. Visitors can enjoy scenic vistas, natural wonders, and abundant wildlife while exploring the park’s lush forests and expansive mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park: Activities and Tours

Wildlife Safari

A wildlife safari is one of the best ways to experience the diversity of wildlife in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park. Visitors can embark on a guided tour and witness the park’s rich wildlife, including zebras, buffalos, warthogs, and baboons.

Canoeing/Kayaking

Canoeing or kayaking on the park’s lakes is an excellent way to explore the park and soak in its unparalleled beauty. Visitors can rent a boat from one of the park’s many rental services and paddle through the serene waters, enjoying the stunning scenery and abundant wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Day 1: Arrival in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park and check-in at your hotel.

Day 2: Visit the Rift Valley Research Centre and explore the museum’s interactive exhibits.

Day 3: Take a wildlife safari and enjoy sightings of buffalo, zebras, and other wildlife.

Day 4: Go canoeing or kayaking on Abijatta Lake to enjoy the serene waters and stunning scenery.

Day 5: Hike the Shalla Volcano trail and enjoy the breathtaking views from the mountain peak.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of the natural hot springs and Hyena Cave Trail.

Day 7: Departure from Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular local dishes in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park include injera - a type of sourdough flatbread served with savory stews and vegetables, and kolo - a roasted and spiced grain mix, enjoyed as a snack.

Local Beverages

Coffee is a popular drink in Ethiopia, and Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a cup of locally brewed coffee while soaking in the park’s natural beauty.

Where to Stay in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in and around Abijatta Shalla National Park, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury lodges. Some popular options include the Lake View Hotel, the Sunnyside Villas, and the Saro-Maria Hotel.

What to Buy in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park can bring home a variety of souvenirs and local products, including handmade crafts, textiles, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

The local culture in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park reflects the diversity of the region and its ancient history. Visitors can learn about the customs, traditions, and beliefs of the local communities through a variety of interactive exhibits and displays.

Best Time to Visit Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

The best time to visit Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is between November and February when the weather is mild and dry, and wildlife sightings are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Follow park rules and regulations
  • Hire an experienced guide for hiking and wildlife safaris
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks while exploring the park

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the wildlife
  • Approach wild animals too closely
  • Venture off the designated trails or areas
  • Ignore safety guidelines or warnings

Safety and Health

Safety in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors are advised to follow park rules and regulations, hire experienced guides, and carry sufficient water and snacks while exploring the park.

Health in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

Visitors to Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park are advised to take basic precautions, such as carrying mosquito repellent and taking anti-malaria medication. It’s also essential to stay hydrated and follow food safety guidelines while dining in the park.

Getting to Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

The nearest airport to Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa. Visitors can take a direct flight from most major cities in Africa, Asia, and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

There are several bus stations near Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park that offer regular bus services to and from Addis Ababa and other major cities in Ethiopia.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car or hire a taxi from Addis Ababa to reach Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park.

How to Get Around in Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park

The best way to get around Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is on foot or by renting a bike or boat. Most accommodation options offer bike and boat rental services for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park can also explore nearby destinations and excursions, including the Bale Mountains National Park, Sof Omar Cave, and the Awash National Park.

Conclusion

Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse wildlife, Abijatta Shalla Lakes National Park is an unparalleled destination for anyone looking to explore the wonders of Ethiopia.
