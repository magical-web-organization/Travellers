Jimma, a city in Western Ethiopia, is fast becoming a popular tourist destination. With its rich cultural heritage, scenic views, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Jimma offers travelers a unique experience that is hard to come by elsewhere in the country. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the best things to see and do in Jimma, as well as provide information on where to stay, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Jimma

Jimma is a city located in the Western part of Ethiopia. It is the largest city in the Oromia Region, and is known for its coffee production. Jimma is also a significant transport hub, linking the southwest of Ethiopia with the capital city of Addis Ababa.

List of Places to Visit in Jimma

Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions or seeking outdoor adventures, Jimma has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Jimma:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jimma Museum: This museum is home to a collection of traditional artifacts, paintings, and more. Visitors can learn about the history of the Oromo people as well as the kingdom of Jimma.

  2. Aba Jifar Palace: This palace was once the residence of the legendary king, Aba Jifar. Visitors can take a tour of the palace and admire its intricate carvings, paintings, and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Jimma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jimma Waterfall: Located on the outskirts of the city, Jimma Waterfall is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a peaceful environment for visitors to relax.

  2. Coffee Plantations and Farms: Jimma is known for its coffee production, and visitors can take a tour of coffee plantations and farms to learn more about the process of coffee production.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jimma

Jimma is surrounded by hills and mountains, making it an ideal destination for hikers and bikers. Some of the popular trails include:

  1. Hiking to Mount Gombar: This is a challenging hike that takes visitors to the top of Mount Gombar. The hike offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Biking to the Waterfall: Visitors can rent a bike and explore the trails that lead to Jimma Waterfall. The bike ride is an enjoyable way to take in the views and experience the natural beauty of Jimma.

Best Things to Do in Jimma: Activities and Tours

  1. Coffee Tours: Visitors can take a tour of coffee farms and experience the process of coffee production.

  2. Cultural Tours: Visitors can take a tour of Jimma Museum, Aba Jifar Palace, and other cultural attractions to learn more about the heritage of the Oromo people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jimma

For visitors who want to explore Jimma in-depth, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Hike to Mount Gombar
Day 2 Visit Jimma Museum and Aba Jifar Palace
Day 3 Take a coffee tour
Day 4 Bike to Jimma Waterfall
Day 5 Explore local markets and try local cuisine
Day 6 Visit nearby historical sites (Jima Arjo and other places of cultural significance)
Day 7 Relax and enjoy Jimma’s natural beauty

Food and Drink Scene of Jimma

Jimma is known for its coffee production, but its cuisine is equally exciting. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Injera: This traditional Ethiopian dish is a sourdough flatbread that is served with various stews and vegetable dishes.

  2. Kitfo: This is a dish made from raw minced beef that is mixed with spices and eaten with injera.

Local Beverages

  1. Coffee: Jimma is a significant coffee-producing region, and visitors must try the locally grown coffee for a truly authentic experience.

  2. Tej: This is a traditional Ethiopian honey wine that is commonly enjoyed by locals.

Where to Stay in Jimma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jimma has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Honeyland Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of Jimma city.

  2. Abyssinia Hotel: This hotel is a budget-friendly option and is ideal for travelers who want to explore the city without breaking the bank.

What to Buy in Jimma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Jimma can find a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some of the most popular items:

  1. Coffee: Jimma is known for its coffee production, and visitors can take home a bag of freshly roasted coffee beans.

  2. Handmade Crafts: Visitors can explore local markets and purchase handmade crafts such as woven baskets and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Jimma

The Oromo people are the dominant ethnic group in Jimma, and visitors should respect the local culture and customs. It is customary to shake hands with both hands and to remove shoes before entering someone’s home. Visitors should also dress modestly and avoid carrying or consuming alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit Jimma

The best time to visit Jimma is during the dry season between September and January. The weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy the outdoor attractions without worrying about rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jimma

Do:

  1. Learn some basic phrases in the local language (Afaan Oromo).

  2. Respect local customs and dress modestly.

Don’t:

  1. Carry or consume alcohol in public places.

  2. Photograph people without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jimma

While Jimma is generally safe, visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety. It is advisable to avoid travelling alone at night and to keep an eye on personal belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Jimma

Visitors to Jimma should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis (DTaP), and hepatitis A and B. It is also advisable to bring insect repellent to protect against malaria.

Getting to Jimma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jimma

  1. Jimma Airport: This airport serves domestic flights within Ethiopia.

Bus Stations Near Jimma

  1. Jimma Bus Terminal: This bus terminal offers bus services to nearby destinations.

Other Options

  1. Private car hire is available for visitors who prefer to travel at their convenience.

How to Get Around in Jimma

Visitors can get around Jimma by using taxis and buses. It is advisable to agree on a fare before getting into a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Jimma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Agaro: This town is located a few kilometers from Jimma and is known for its honey production.

  2. Mizan Teferi: This town is located about 100 kilometers from Jimma, and is known for its national parks and forest reserves.

Conclusion

Jimma is a unique tourist destination that offers a rich cultural experience and breathtaking outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip in advance and make the most out of their stay. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, indulging in delicious cuisine, or embarking on outdoor adventures, Jimma has something to offer everyone.
