Introduction to Jijiga

Jijiga is a charming city situated in Eastern Ethiopia. It is the capital of the Somali Regional State of Ethiopia and is known for its fascinating blend of cultures and traditions. The city serves as an interesting base for camel trekking, bird watching, and hiking.

In this comprehensive tour guide of Jijiga, we will walk you through the unique cultural and natural attractions of the city, the best things to do, where to stay, the food and drink scene, the local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Jijiga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jijiga has several museums and historical places that reflect the city’s rich cultural heritage. The Jijiga Cultural museum is a must-visit site for anyone interested in the local culture and history. The museum has a collection of traditional clothes, jewelry, musical instruments, and tools that tell the story of the Somali people’s way of life.

Other historical places that visitors should check out include the Adadle Gabooye Battlefields, where the Somali people fought against the British colonial rule, and the Sheikh Hussein Shrine, a pilgrimage site for Muslims from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Jijiga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jijiga has a variety of natural attractions that appeal to adventurous travelers. The city is surrounded by mountains that are perfect for hiking and trekking. The Awdu Hills, located about 30 kilometers from Jijiga, offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and a chance to experience the local flora and fauna.

Jijiga is also famous for its bird watching opportunities. The wetlands around the city are home to different bird species, including the rare Somali ostrich.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jijiga

Jijiga is an excellent destination for hiking enthusiasts. The trek from Jijiga to the Awdu Hills is an adventurous experience that takes you through mountains, grasslands, and wetlands. Another popular hiking route is the trek from Jijiga to Babille Elephant Sanctuary, where visitors can see herds of elephants in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Jijiga: Activities and Tours

Jijiga has an array of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers.

For history buffs, the tour to the Jijiga Cultural Museum and the Adadle Gabooye Battlefields is a must-do. For bird watching enthusiasts, a visit to the wetlands around the city is a must, while adventurous travelers can undertake a camel trekking expedition in the local desert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jijiga

Day 1: Visit the Jijiga Cultural Museum

Day 2: Trek to the Awdu Hills

Day 3: Explore the wetlands and go bird watching

Day 4: Camel trekking in the desert

Day 5: Visit Sheikh Hussein Shrine

Day 6: Trek to Babille Elephant Sanctuary

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Jijiga

Must-Try Dishes

Jijiga has a diverse culinary culture that blends Ethiopian and Somalian dishes. A must-try dish is the Somali breakfast “foul,” which is made with beans, onion, tomato, and chili. Other popular dishes include “injera,” a sourdough flatbread, and “sambusa,” a pastry filled with vegetables, meat, or cheese.

Local Beverages

Jijiga is famous for its traditional coffee ceremony, where coffee beans are roasted, ground, and brewed right in front of you. Other popular beverages include “shaah,” a sweet tea made with cardamom and cinnamon, and “laas,” a fermented camel milk that has a tangy taste.

Where to Stay in Jijiga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jijiga has an array of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. For budget travelers, there are several guesthouses and hostels that offer basic amenities.

For those who want comfort and luxury, there are several hotels in the city that offer excellent services and amenities.

What to Buy in Jijiga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jijiga is famous for its traditional handicrafts, including baskets, pottery, and woven textiles. Visitors can buy these souvenirs in the local markets, where bargaining is a part of the shopping experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Jijiga

Jijiga has a rich culture and tradition that are heavily influenced by its Muslim and Somali heritage. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Jijiga

The best time to visit Jijiga is during the dry season, from October to March. The weather is mild, and the landscape is green and lush. However, visitors should note that the temperatures can still be quite high during the day, especially in the desert areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jijiga

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Bargain in the local markets

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Visit religious sites during prayer times
  • Eat with your left hand (it is considered impolite)

Safety and Health

Safety in Jijiga

Jijiga is generally a safe city, but visitors should exercise caution while travelling around the area. Visitors are advised not to venture out alone at night and should avoid isolated areas.

Health in Jijiga

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or boiled water to prevent illnesses. Visitors should also carry a basic first aid kit and any prescribed medications with them.

Getting to Jijiga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jijiga

Jijiga Airport is located 10 kilometers from the city center and offers daily flights to Addis Ababa.

Bus Stations Near Jijiga

Jijiga Bus Station is located in the city center and offers daily bus services to nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to reach Jijiga from other destinations.

How to Get Around in Jijiga

Visitors can hire a taxi or a tuk-tuk to get around the city. Walking is also a viable option, especially in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Jijiga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to Harar, a UNESCO World Heritage site located 200 kilometers from Jijiga. Harar is known for its narrow alleys, traditional houses, and bustling markets.

Conclusion

Jijiga is a unique destination that offers visitors a glimpse into the local culture and traditions. With its beautiful natural attractions, exciting outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Jijiga is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Ethiopia.
