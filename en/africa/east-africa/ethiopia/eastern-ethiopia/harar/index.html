Introduction to Harar

Harar, the ancient walled city situated in eastern Ethiopia, is a destination that every traveler should add to their list. Founded in the 7th century, Harar has been declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its well-preserved Islamic architecture and its unique cultural traditions. The city is also known for its hyenas, which are fed by hand at night by the local “hyena man”.

List of Places to Visit in Harar

When you visit Harar, here are the places you must include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harar Jugol: The UNESCO World Heritage Site that includes the walled city of Harar and its many mosques, shrines, and markets.
  • Harar Museum: An interactive museum that features exhibits on the city’s traditions, history, and daily life.
  • Rimbaud House: The former home of the French poet Arthur Rimbaud who lived in Harar for a time, now a museum.
  • Jami Mosque: A 16th-century mosque that is one of the oldest structures in Harar.
  • The Tombs of Emir Nur and his Family: A graveyard and a religious site where the grandsons of Saladdin are buried.

Natural Attractions of Harar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Miraflor Mountain: A nearby mountain that is known for excellent hikes and offers a panoramic view of the walled city.
  • Babile Elephant Sanctuary: A sanctuary that is home to elephants, baboons, and other wildlife, located only 40 km from Harar.
  • Lake Haromaya: A crater lake situated 60 km northwest of Harar known for fantastic bird watching.
  • Valley of Marvels: A valley filled with unique geological formations, suitable for hiking and sightseeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harar

For travelers looking for outdoor adventure, Harar offers some fantastic hiking and biking routes, such as:

  • Hike to Miraflor Mountain: This is a beginner level hike that is 11 km roundtrip and takes about 4 hours.
  • Hike to the Valley of Marvels: This intermediate hike is 21 km roundtrip and takes about 6 hours.
  • Cycling the Walls of Harar: This beginner level bike ride that takes you around the walls of the ancient city, covering approximately 2.5 km.

Best Things to Do in Harar: Activities and Tours

There are many things to see and do in Harar, including:

  • Visit the Harar market to see traditional handicrafts such as woven baskets and colorful textiles.
  • Experience the feeding of the hyenas with the locals.
  • Take a walking tour of the city with a knowledgeable guide to learn about the city’s history and culture.
  • Try traditional Harari food like dulet, khat, shiro, and injera.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harar

If you’re planning to spend a week in Harar, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Day 2: Visit Harar market in the morning and then Miraflor Mountain in the afternoon. Day 3: Visit Jami Mosque and Rimbaud House and explore the walled city in the evening. Day 4: Hike the Valley of Marvels and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 5: Visit Babile elephant sanctuary and enjoy wildlife sightings. Day 6: Visit the Harari Museum and Tombs of Emir Nur in the morning and khat plantation in the afternoon. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Harar

Harar has a unique And nonetheless rich culinary culture. There are many dishes and drinks you must-try in Harar:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dulet: A spicy meat dish that is traditionally eaten for breakfast in Harar.
  • Shiro: A stew made from ground chickpeas and served with injera.
  • Injera: A type of pancake made from teff flour and fermented water.
  • Bula: A delicious Harari street food snack that is made from ground beans and fried in oil.

Local Beverages

  • Khat: A mild narcotic leaf that is chewed by locals for its stimulant effects.
  • Arake: A local alcoholic drink that is produced from grain, similar to vodka.
  • Tella: A traditional beer made from barely and has a distinct taste, accompanied by a slice of lime.

Where to Stay in Harar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harar has a wide range of accommodation options such as guesthouses, hotels and hostels to suitable any budget. The following are the best places to stay in Harar:

  • Heritage Plaza Hotel: A high-end hotel located in the heart of the city within close proximity to the city’s main attractions.
  • Harar Ras Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers fantastic city views and excellent customer service.
  • Meshesha Guesthouse: Located only a 10-minute walk from the old city, this guesthouse offers budget-friendly accommodation in a traditional Harari dwelling.
  • Harar Midawha Lodge: A rustic eco-lodge that offers splendid valley views, but is located a bit far from the city center.

What to Buy in Harar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you visit Harar, there are many souvenirs you can take back home such as:

  • Handmade baskets and traditional textiles.
  • Small carvings made from coffee wood.
  • Local coffee beans, considered some of the best in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Harar

Harar has its distinct culture and customs, you should be respectful towards them. For instance:

  • Respect local customs by covering up, especially when visiting mosques.
  • Refrain from photographing people without asking for their permission first.
  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.
  • Women should avoid walking alone late at night and should cover their hair in public.

Best Time to Visit Harar

The dry season from October to March is the best time to visit Harar. During this time, temperatures are comfortable, and there is almost no rain. However, the city can get quite crowded during the holidays, and accommodation prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harar

When you visit Harar, there are some Do’s and Don’ts that you should consider:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain in the market but in a friendly manner.
  • Try traditional Harari food.
  • Take a guided walking tour of the city.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission.
  • Drink tap water.
  • Litter on the streets.
  • Wander around the town alone at night.

Safety and Health

Here are some important safety and health tips to keep in mind when traveling to Harar:

Safety in Harar

  • Be cautious when using public transportation, as pickpocketing can occur.
  • Avoid walking alone late at night.
  • Keep your valuable belongings in a safe locker.
  • Do not carry large amounts of cash or flaunt your wealth.

Health in Harar

  • Drink bottled water and avoid ice cubes or fruits that may have been washed in tap water.
  • Use bug spray to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Dress appropriately to avoid sunburn and heatstroke.
  • Get vaccinated against yellow fever and other diseases before traveling.

Getting to Harar: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several options for getting to Harar:

Airports Near Harar

  • Dire Dawa International Airport: located 55 km west of Harar, which is the closest airport to Harar.
  • Addis Ababa Bole International Airport: A major airport, located approximately 540 km from Harar.

Bus Stations Near Harar

  • Dire Dawa Bus Station: Located in Dire Dawa town and is approximately 45 minutes from Harar and offers services to most of the cities in the country.
  • Addis Ababa Autobus Terra: Major bus station located in Addis Ababa that offers services to most cities of Ethiopia.

Other Options

  • You can also take a private taxi from the airport or bus station to Harar.

How to Get Around in Harar

The old city of Harar is relatively small, and a lot of the landmarks are within walking distance. However, during the peak season, it can get crowded and chaotic, so taking a minibus or a taxi is a better idea.

Where to Go Next from Harar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby places that you can visit after exploring Harar:

  • Dire Dawa: A bustling town located only 52 km from Harar, which is known for its diverse culture and heritage.
  • Babile Elephant Sanctuary and Valley of Marvels: These natural wonders are located within a day trip distance from Harar.
  • Addis Ababa: The capital and largest city of Ethiopia, located about 540 km away, which you can reach by a bus or a flight.

Conclusion

Harar is truly an underrated destination that offers visitors an authentic Ethiopian experience. From exploring its ancient landmarks to hiking its scenic trails, to trying its unique cuisine, there is something for every traveler in Harar. While respecting the local culture and following basic safety tips, you can have the adventure of a lifetime in this amazing city.
