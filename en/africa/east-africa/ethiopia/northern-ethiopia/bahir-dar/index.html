Bahir Dar Travel Guide - Explore the Best of Northern Ethiopia

If you’re looking to discover the best of Northern Ethiopia, look no further than Bahir Dar. This charming city is located on the southern shores of Lake Tana, and is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, rich history, and a thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re a first-time visitor to Ethiopia or a seasoned traveler, there’s plenty to discover in Bahir Dar.

Introduction to Bahir Dar

Bahir Dar is a vibrant and bustling city, known for its stunning natural beauty and fascinating historical sites. Its location on the shores of Lake Tana makes it an important hub for the surrounding region, and a popular spot for tourists. The city is home to a range of hotels, restaurants, and cafes, as well as museums, markets, and other attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Bahir Dar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bahir Dar Market - The largest market in the city, and a great place to experience the local culture.
  • The Blue Nile Falls - One of Ethiopia’s most famous natural attractions.
  • Fasil Ghebbi - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this ancient fortress complex is an impressive reminder of Ethiopia’s rich history and culture.
  • Church of St. George - Built in the 12th century, this church is a unique example of Ethiopian architecture.
  • The Monasteries of Lake Tana - This group of 19 monasteries on islands around Lake Tana are home to some of the most important religious artifacts in Ethiopia.

Natural Attractions of Bahir Dar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Nile Gorge - A stunning canyon that offers fantastic hiking and scenic views.
  • Lake Tana - The largest lake in Ethiopia, and a great spot for fishing, swimming, and boating.
  • Tis Abay - a small town located on the edge of the Blue Nile Falls that provides access to the falls and other hikes in the area.
  • Zege Peninsula - A scenic area located to the east of Bahir Dar, offering scenic views of Lake Tana and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bahir Dar

  • Blue Nile Gorge Trail - Hike down to the Blue Nile and take in stunning views of the gorge.
  • Zegabi Mountain Trail - This trail takes you up to the top of the Zege Peninsula, offering stunning views of Lake Tana and the surrounding mountains.
  • Tis Abay Trail - This scenic hike takes you to the Blue Nile Falls, one of Ethiopia’s most famous natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Bahir Dar: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of Lake Tana, including a visit to some of the monasteries on its islands.
  • Visit the Blue Nile Falls and hike around the canyon.
  • Explore the charming streets of Bahir Dar and experience its vibrant culture and rich history.
  • Take a guided tour of Fasil Ghebbi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Sample the local food and drink scene, including traditional Ethiopian coffee and injera.

Food and Drink Scene of Bahir Dar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Injera - A traditional Ethiopian flatbread made from teff flour and served with a variety of stews and sauces.
  • Kitfo - A spicy Ethiopian beef dish served with injera.
  • Tibs - A grilled meat dish served with injera or rice.

Local Beverages

  • Tej - A local honey wine that is sweet and refreshing.
  • Tella - A traditional Ethiopian beer made from barley.

Where to Stay in Bahir Dar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Bahir Dar to suit every budget, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some top choices include:

  • Kuriftu Resort & Spa - A five-star luxury resort with a pool, spa, and restaurant.
  • Blue Nile Resort - A hotel with stunning views of Lake Tana and great amenities.
  • Red Rocks Ethiopia - A backpacker-friendly guesthouse with a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Bahir Dar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handwoven textiles - Produced by local artisans, these textiles are made using traditional methods and come in a range of colors and patterns.
  • Jewelry - Handmade silver jewelry is a popular souvenir in Bahir Dar.
  • Coffee - Ethiopia is known for producing some of the best coffee in the world, and Bahir Dar is a great place to sample and purchase this local delicacy.

Local Culture and Customs of Bahir Dar

Ethiopia is a very traditional country, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions in Bahir Dar. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly - It’s considered disrespectful to wear shorts, sleeveless tops, or revealing clothing in public.
  • Be respectful of religious sites - Many of the attractions in Bahir Dar are religious in nature, so it’s important to behave respectfully when visiting these sites.
  • Be polite - Ethiopians value politeness and courtesy, so it’s important to use please and thank you when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Bahir Dar

The best time to visit Bahir Dar is from October to May, when the weather is dry and mild. June to September is the rainy season, and many of the hiking trails and outdoor attractions may be closed or difficult to access during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bahir Dar

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect religious sites.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bahir Dar

Bahir Dar is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take standard safety precautions like avoiding walking alone at night and keeping your possessions close.

Health in Bahir Dar

Travelers to Bahir Dar should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), and typhoid. Malaria is a risk in certain parts of Ethiopia, including Bahir Dar, so it’s important to take preventative measures like using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Bahir Dar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travelers can reach Bahir Dar by flying into Bahir Dar Airport or by taking a bus from Addis Ababa. Once in the city, taxis and buses are the primary modes of transportation.

Airports Near Bahir Dar

  • Bahir Dar Airport (BJR) - Located just a few kilometers outside the city center, this airport offers flights to other destinations in Ethiopia.

Bus Stations Near Bahir Dar

  • Addis Ababa Bus Station - Buses depart regularly from Addis Ababa to Bahir Dar.

Other Options

  • Minivans - Minivans are a popular mode of transportation for shorter trips in and around Bahir Dar.

How to Get Around in Bahir Dar

Buses, taxis, and minivans are the primary modes of transportation in Bahir Dar. Taxis are the most expensive option but offer the most convenience, while buses and minivans are cheaper but can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Bahir Dar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gonder - Located just a few hours away from Bahir Dar, this city is home to some of Ethiopia’s most impressive historical sites.
  • Lalibela - Home to a collection of ancient rock-hewn churches, Lalibela is one of Ethiopia’s most famous destinations.
  • Simien Mountains National Park - Located a few hours north of Bahir Dar, this mountain range is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in Ethiopia.

Conclusion

Bahir Dar is one of the most charming and unique destinations in Ethiopia, offering a fascinating blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With so much to see and do, this city is a must-visit for anyone interested in discovering the best of Northern Ethiopia.
