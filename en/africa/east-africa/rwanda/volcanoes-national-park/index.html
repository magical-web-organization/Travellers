Introduction to Volcanoes National Park

Volcanoes National Park is a protected area located in northwestern Rwanda, bordering Uganda and the Democratic Republic of Congo. The park is best known for its resident mountain gorilla population, which attracts thousands of visitors each year. While gorilla trekking is the park’s main attraction, there is much more to discover in this stunning region of Africa, including a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking volcanic landscapes, and exciting outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Volcanoes National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iby’Iwacu Cultural Village: This cultural center offers visitors a chance to experience traditional Rwandan life, including music, dance, food, and crafts.
  • The Kigali Genocide Memorial: Located in Rwanda’s capital city, this sobering museum is dedicated to the memory of the 1994 genocide and serves as a meaningful reminder of the country’s past.
  • Ruhengeri: This small market town offers a glimpse into local life and the opportunity to explore the region’s vibrant markets and colorful streets.

Natural Attractions of Volcanoes National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gorilla Trekking: A chance to encounter the park’s magnificent mountain gorillas up close and personal, this once-in-a-lifetime experience is a must for any wildlife enthusiast.
  • Golden Monkey Trekking: Another unique primate experience, the park is also home to the endangered golden monkeys who live in the bamboo forests.
  • Hiking Mount Bisoke: This impressive volcano offers a challenging hike to its summit, where trekkers can take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Dian Fossey Trek: This hike takes visitors to the former research station of Dian Fossey, who was a pioneer in the study of mountain gorillas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volcanoes National Park

  • Bisoke Summit Hike: This challenging hike takes approximately 6 hours and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Karisimbi Hike: A two-day hike, this trail offers stunning views of the Virunga volcanoes, and the chance to observe a number of plant and bird species along the way.
  • Biking through Ruhengeri: Guests can bike through the charming village of Ruhengeri, taking in the sights and sounds of daily Rwandan life.

Best Things to Do in Volcanoes National Park: Activities and Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Volcanoes National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ugali: a staple food made from maize flour, usually served with beans or meat.
  • Ibihaza: a traditional Rwandan snack made from peanuts and honey.
  • Isombe: a popular side dish made from cassava leaves and served with beans and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Banana Beer: a traditional Rwandan drink made from fermented bananas.
  • Freshly-made juice: made from locally sourced fruits

Where to Stay in Volcanoes National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sabyinyo Silverback Lodge: A rustic luxury lodge that offers breathtaking views of the Virunga volcanoes and is only a 15-minute drive from the park entrance.
  • Singita Kwitonda Lodge: Another popular lodging option, this eco-lodge offers luxury accommodations, including private swimming pools and outdoor showers.
  • Virunga Hotel: This boutique hotel offers comfortable accommodations at an affordable price in the heart of Ruhengeri.

What to Buy in Volcanoes National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted baskets- typically woven out of local flora and are often intricately patterned.
  • Rwandan Coffee- Rwanda’s high-quality arabica coffee is hand-roasted and an excellent souvenir.
  • Kitenge Fabrics- these are printed on cotton and are loved for their unique patterns and vibrant colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Volcanoes National Park

  • Rwandans are incredibly friendly and welcoming, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with someone in the street!
  • The country has a strong arts and crafts culture, especially when it comes to basket making.
  • Traditional dancing is an essential part of Rwandan culture and is often performed during community events.

Best Time to Visit Volcanoes National Park

The park is open all year round, but the best time to visit is during the long dry season (from June to September) and the shorter dry season (from December to February). During these months, the weather is at its most stable, with clear skies and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volcanoes National Park

Do:

  • Hire a guide to take you gorilla and any other animal trekking
  • Dress warmly as it is high altitude especially gorilla trekking.
  • Learn a few words in Kinyarwanda(Widely spoken native language) greeting locals.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in the park.
  • Speak in a loud voice

Safety and Health

Safety in Volcanoes National Park

  • Gorilla trekking groups are small, and a park ranger accompanies all trekkers to ensure their safety.
  • Rwanda is generally one of Africa’s safest countries. However, it is still essential to take precautions as you would in any foreign country.

Health in Volcanoes National Park

  • Bring along mosquito repellent as the area around the park is a malaria zone
  • Get vaccinated against Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid.

Getting to Volcanoes National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Volcanoes National Park

The most convenient airport for Volcanoes National Park is Kigali International Airport, which is approximately 2-3 hours drive away depending on traffic

Bus Stations Near Volcanoes National Park

Ruhengeri is the nearest town to VOLCANOES NATIONAL PARK, and it is served by regional buses.

Other Options

Shared minibusses, and car rentals are readily available from Kigali.

How to Get Around in Volcanoes National Park

The easiest way to get around Volcanoes National Park is by car. Guided tours and safaris allow you to navigate through the park’s dense forest terrain with ease.

Where to Go Next from Volcanoes National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nyungwe Forest National Park: Located in southern Rwanda, Nyungwe is one of Africa’s largest remaining forests and home to many primates and bird species.
  • Kigali City: Rwanda’s capital city boasts several museums, art galleries, shopping, and dining options.
  • Lake Kivu: With its clear blue waters and lush green surroundings, Lake Kivu is a popular destination for water-based activities and relaxation.

Conclusion

Volcanoes National Park is an incredible destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventurers and wildlife enthusiasts to history buffs and cultural aficionados. Its combination of stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique wildlife makes it a must-visit destination for any traveler to East Africa. Plan a trip to this awe-inspiring park today, and you won’t be disappointed!
