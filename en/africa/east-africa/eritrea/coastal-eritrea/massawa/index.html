Introduction to Massawa

If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination with plenty of history, culture, and natural beauty, Massawa in Eritrea is a great choice. This coastal town has been an important trading hub since ancient times and is home to several fascinating cultural and historical sites. Additionally, the surrounding landscape is stunning, with rugged mountains, pristine beaches, and colorful coral reefs.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local tips for Massawa.

List of Places to Visit in Massawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dahlak Hotel Museum: This museum inside an old Italian hotel is home to several exhibits detailing Massawa’s history as a colonial port city and its role in Eritrea’s struggle for independence.
  • Biet Ghiorghis Museum: Also known as the Red Sea Museum, this building is a former church and is now home to several religious and cultural artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Massawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taulud Island: Take a boat ride to this uninhabited island for excellent snorkeling and diving. The island is surrounded by crystal-clear waters teeming with colorful marine life.
  • Green Island: This small island is a popular day trip destination and is home to several scenic beaches and clear, turquoise waters.
  • Gurgusum Beach: A pristine beach with white sand and clear waters, Gurgusum Beach is perfect for sunbathing and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Massawa

  • Filfil Solomuna: A mountain range located 30 kilometers outside of Massawa, Filfil Solomuna is a popular destination for hikers and adventurers. Trails range in difficulty, from easy paths to challenging ascents.
  • Mangura’: A popular biking trail located near Massawa, Mangura’ offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is suitable for intermediate to advanced riders.

Best Things to Do in Massawa: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Old Town: The Old Town of Massawa is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to several cultural and historical landmarks. Take a walking tour to explore the town’s unique architecture and learn about its rich history.
  • Take a Boat Tour: Several boat tour operators offer trips to nearby islands and beaches, allowing visitors to explore the stunning coastline of Massawa.
  • Try Scuba Diving: With clear waters and healthy coral reefs, Massawa is a great destination for scuba diving. There are several dive shops in town that offer guided dives for all levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Massawa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bsemmet: A traditional bread made from sorghum flour, Bsemmet is a staple food in Eritrea and is often served with stews or dips.
  • Zigni: A spicy meat stew made with beef or lamb, Zigni is a popular dish in Eritrea and is often served with injera, a sourdough flatbread.

Local Beverages

  • Suwa: A non-alcoholic beverage made from fermented grains, Suwa is a popular drink in Eritrea and is often served with meals.
  • Tej: A honey wine made from fermented honey and water, Tej is a traditional Ethiopian and Eritrean drink.

Where to Stay in Massawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dahlak Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Massawa’s Old Town and offers comfortable, spacious rooms with traditional decor.
  • Residence Massawa: This budget-friendly guesthouse is located in a quiet residential neighborhood and offers clean, comfortable rooms with air conditioning.

What to Buy in Massawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Red Sea Coral: Massawa is home to several coral reefs, and many souvenir shops sell pieces of red sea coral jewelry, sculptures, and other decorative items made from coral.
  • Traditional Clothing: Eritrean traditional clothing is colorful and ornate, with intricate embroidery and patterns. Look for traditional dresses, scarves, and other garments in Massawa’s markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Massawa

Eritrea has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors to Massawa should take some time to learn about local customs and etiquette. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect Religious Customs: Massawa has a significant Muslim population, and visitors should be respectful of Islamic customs when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Dress Conservatively: Eritrea is a conservative society, and visitors should dress modestly when in public, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Greet Locals Politely: In Eritrean culture, it is customary to greet people with a handshake and a polite greeting. Taking a moment to greet locals in this way shows respect and will help visitors feel welcome.

Best Time to Visit Massawa

The best time to visit Massawa is between November and February, when temperatures are mild and rainfall is low. Summer months (June-August) can be hot and humid, and the monsoon season (July-September) can cause heavy rainfall, making some outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Massawa

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Take the time to learn about Eritrean customs and culture, and be respectful of them during your stay in Massawa.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Massawa is home to some incredible natural beauty, so take advantage of the many opportunities to hike, bike, or swim.
  • Try the Local Food: Eritrean cuisine is delicious and full of flavor, so make sure to try a variety of local dishes during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: The tap water in Massawa is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water during your stay.
  • Disrespect Local Customs: As a visitor to Massawa, it is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, even if they differ from your own.
  • Wear Revealing Clothing: Dress conservatively when in public, particularly when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Massawa

Massawa is generally a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take the usual precautions to safeguard their belongings and avoid areas that are known to be unsafe at night.

Health in Massawa

Travelers should consult a medical professional before traveling to Massawa and ensure they are up-to-date on all necessary vaccinations. Mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever are a concern in some areas, so visitors should take steps to avoid mosquito bites, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Massawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Massawa

  • Massawa International Airport: This small airport is located about ten minutes’ drive from the center of Massawa and offers flights to several destinations within Eritrea and neighboring countries.

Bus Stations Near Massawa

  • Massawa Bus Station: This bus station is located in the center of town and offers connections to several nearby destinations within Eritrea.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi: Travelers can hire a private taxi to travel to Massawa from Asmara, the capital of Eritrea.
  • Boat: Some travelers opt to take a boat from neighboring Djibouti or Sudan to reach Massawa.

How to Get Around in Massawa

The best way to get around Massawa is on foot, as the town is small enough to explore on foot. For longer journeys, private taxis can be hired, or visitors can rent their own car or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Massawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asmara: Eritrea’s capital city is located about two hours’ drive from Massawa and is home to several cultural and historic landmarks.
  • Keren: This town is located about three hours’ drive from Massawa and is known for its colorful markets and traditional architecture.
  • Dahlak Archipelago: A group of islands located about 110 kilometers from Massawa, the Dahlak Archipelago offers excellent snorkeling and diving opportunities and is a popular destination for day trips and longer excursions.

Conclusion

Massawa is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience the history, culture, and natural beauty of Eritrea. With fascinating cultural and historical sites, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this coastal town.
