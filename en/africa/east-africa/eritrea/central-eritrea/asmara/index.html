Introduction to Asmara

Asmara is the vibrant and bustling capital city of Eritrea, located in the heart of East Africa. This historic city is known for its stunning Italian Art Deco architecture, dynamic local culture, and incredible landscapes.

Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply soaking up the local culture, Asmara has something to offer every kind of traveler.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, where to stay, and how to get around in Asmara, Eritrea.

List of Places to Visit in Asmara

Settle into exploring Asmara and visit these amazing places that are must-see on any itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Asmara is steeped in a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. A few of the most iconic include:

  • National Museum of Eritrea: With a collection of over 12,000 artifacts, including archaeological finds, traditional costumes, and art, this museum is a great place to start your exploration of Eritrea’s cultural heritage.
  • Independence Monument and Square: This iconic monument and square commemorates Eritrea’s struggle for independence from Ethiopia, and is an important symbol of the country’s history and identity.
  • Cinema Impero: This stunning Art Deco movie theater dates back to 1937, and is one of the best-preserved examples of the city’s Italian colonial architecture.

Natural Attractions of Asmara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eritrea’s diverse landscapes also offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Make sure to add these to your list:

  • Tigray Plateau: One of the most scenic areas in Eritrea, the Tigray Plateau offers stunning views of red sandstone cliffs and traditional Tigrayan villages.
  • Asmara-Massawa Railway: This historic railway takes you through some of Eritrea’s most awe-inspiring landscapes, including mountains, forests, and deserts.
  • Dahlak Archipelago: If you love snorkeling, scuba diving, or just soaking up the sun on pristine beaches, this beautiful archipelago is not to be missed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Asmara

If you’re looking to get closer to nature, Asmara is also home to some spectacular hiking and biking routes that are sure to take your breath away. Consider these carefully selected routes:

  • Asmara - Debre Bizen: This challenging 700-meter climb is a rewarding day trip that takes you along beautiful mountain trails and past many ancient monasteries.
  • Tessenay - Asmara: This three-day hike starts in the beautiful Tessenay area and takes you through some of Eritrea’s most stunning landscapes, including river valleys, forests, and mountains.
  • Asmara - Massawa: This historic railway route is also a beautiful and challenging cycling route that takes you through some of the country’s most beautiful natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Asmara: Activities and Tours

To get the most out of your Asmara experience, there are many activities and tours that you can partake in. Here are a few of the top picks:

  • Coffee Culture Tour: Eritrea is known for its delicious coffee, and a coffee culture tour is a great way to experience the local coffee scene and learn about the traditional coffee-making process.
  • Old Town Walking Tour: A great way to get a feel for Asmara’s local culture and history is by taking a walking tour through the city’s charming Old Town.
  • Cycling Tour: See the city from a different perspective by joining a cycling tour that takes you through Asmara’s most scenic streets and neighborhoods.

Food and Drink Scene of Asmara

One of the best ways to experience Eritrea’s vibrant culture is through its delicious food and drink. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Injera: This traditional Eritrean staple is a sourdough flatbread that is eaten with your hands and used to scoop up stews and curries.
  • Zigni: This spicy beef stew is one of Eritrea’s most iconic dishes and is typically served with injera.
  • Ful: A popular breakfast dish, ful is a hearty bean stew that is served with bread.

Local Beverages

  • Buna: Coffee culture is alive and well in Eritrea, and buna (coffee) is a must-try beverage for anyone visiting the country.
  • Sowaa: This refreshing drink is made from ginger and lime juice and is perfect for hot days.
  • Tej: For something a little stronger, try tej, a traditional honey wine that is popular throughout the region.

Where to Stay in Asmara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Asmara has a range of accommodation options for every budget and travel style, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Consider these top picks:

  • Hotel Asmara Palace: This grand Art Deco hotel is one of Asmara’s most iconic buildings and offers luxurious accommodation in the heart of the city.
  • The 23 Boutique Hotel: For a more affordable option, The 23 Boutique Hotel offers comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • Highlands Hotel: This charming guesthouse offers a homey atmosphere and delicious home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Asmara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring back some local treasures, Asmara has a vibrant market scene and a range of products to choose from. Here are a few top picks:

  • Traditional Jewelry: Eritrea is known for its beautiful silver jewelry, which is often intricate and ornate.
  • Handwoven Textiles: Eritrean textiles are often handwoven and come in a range of beautiful patterns and colors.
  • Coffee: Take home some of Eritrea’s delicious coffee beans as a delicious souvenir that you can enjoy long after your trip has ended.

Local Culture and Customs of Asmara

Asmara is known for its diverse and vibrant local culture, with a rich history and traditions that are still celebrated today. Consider these cultural customs:

  • Dress Code: Modesty is important in Eritrean culture, so dress conservatively and respectfully when exploring the city.
  • Greeting Customs: It’s customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile, and to take some time to exchange pleasantries before launching into business or conversation.

Best Time to Visit Asmara

The best time to visit Asmara is during the dry season, which typically runs from December to February. During this time, the weather is mild and comfortable, and there is little rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Asmara

To ensure that you have an enjoyable and respectful trip, consider these do’s and don’ts when visiting Asmara:

Do:

  • Respect Cultural Customs: Take the time to learn about local customs and behaviors, and show respect for cultural traditions.
  • Try Local Foods: Eritrean cuisine is delicious and diverse, so be sure to try as much as you can during your trip.

Don’t:

  • Take Pictures Without Permission: Always ask for permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.
  • Engage in PDA: Public displays of affection are not considered appropriate in Eritrean culture, so avoid engaging in intimate displays of affection in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Asmara is generally a safe and welcoming city, but like any destination, there are a few important safety and health considerations to keep in mind.

Safety in Asmara

  • Be Mindful of Robberies: Petty theft can occur in some areas of the city, so keep your belongings close to you and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Avoid Demonstrations: Protests and demonstrations can occur in the city, and while they are often peaceful and non-violent, it’s best to steer clear of these events to avoid any potential safety issues.

Health in Asmara

  • Stay Hydrated: Eritrea is a dry and arid country, so make sure to drink plenty of water throughout your trip.
  • Malaria Prevention: Malaria is present in some areas of Eritrea, including Asmara, so take appropriate preventative measures, such as mosquito repellent and taking antimalarial medication.

Getting to Asmara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Asmara is well connected to other parts of Eritrea and neighboring countries, with several transportation options available. Consider these methods of transportation:

Airports Near Asmara

  • Asmara International Airport (ASM): This is the primary airport serving Asmara, with daily flights to and from a number of destinations in the region.

Bus Stations Near Asmara

  • Central Bus Terminal: This is the main bus station in Asmara, with connections to other parts of Eritrea.

Other Options

  • Train: The Asmara-Massawa Railway is a historic and scenic train route that connects Asmara to the port city of Massawa.
  • Car Rental: While driving in Eritrea can be challenging, car rental is available for those who prefer to navigate the city on their own.

How to Get Around in Asmara

Getting around Asmara is relatively easy and affordable, with several transportation options available.

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout the city and are a quick and convenient way to get around.
  • Buses: Local buses are also available, but they can be crowded and confusing for those unfamiliar with the area.
  • Walking: Asmara is a relatively small city, and many of its top attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Asmara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Eritrea, there are many amazing destinations and excursions that are easily accessible from Asmara. Consider these popular options:

  • Massawa: This historic port city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with well-preserved Ottoman and Italian architecture, beautiful beaches, and fantastic scuba diving.
  • Keren: Keren is a bustling city known for its vibrant market scene, beautiful landscapes, and rich history.

Conclusion

Asmara is a destination that truly has it all – rich cultural traditions, stunning natural landscapes, incredible food and drink, and a vibrant city vibe. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be fully equipped to explore all that this amazing city has to offer.
