Located in Central Eritrea, Keren is a fascinating city that offers visitors a blend of culture, history, and natural wonder. From ancient archaeological sites to breathtaking landscapes, there’s something for everyone in Keren. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see in Keren, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and provide tips and advice for planning your trip.

Introduction to Keren

Keren is a bustling city with a rich history that dates back centuries. It’s known for its ancient ruins, colorful markets, and lively atmosphere. The city is also home to the Eritrean War Memorial, which honors the soldiers who fought for Eritrea’s independence.

List of Places to Visit in Keren

Keren has a wide range of attractions and activities for travelers to enjoy. Here, we’ve broken down the top places to visit in Keren by category:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eritrean War Memorial
  • Bahti Meskerem Square
  • Italian War Cemetery
  • Debre Sina Monastery

Natural Attractions of Keren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keren Natural Reserve
  • Ansha Hot Springs
  • Mount Emba Soira
  • Sabur River

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keren

Keren is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and biking to horseback riding and birdwatching, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Keren:

  • Keren to Aderde Canyon Trail
  • Sabur River Trail
  • Keren to Massawa Trail

Best Things to Do in Keren: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the colorful markets and bazaars
  • Take a guided city tour
  • Attend traditional cultural festivals
  • Explore nearby archaeological sites

Week-Long Itinerary for Keren

Day 1: Arrive in Keren and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit the Eritrean War Memorial and the Italian War Cemetery. Day 3: Take a hike in Keren Natural Reserve or go hot springs dipping at Ansha. Day 4: Explore the nearby archaeological sites. Day 5: Visit Debre Sina Monastery and Bahti Meskerem Square. Day 6: Take a day trip to Mount Emba Soira. Day 7: Relax and enjoy Keren’s vibrant culture by attending a local festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Keren

Keren offers a wide variety of culinary experiences. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Injera and tsebhi: a stew made with various meats and vegetables, served with a spongy sourdough flatbread called injera.
  • Basta: a pasta dish with a tomato-based sauce and vegetables.
  • Sambusa: a fried pastry filled with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Suwa: a homemade fermented beer made from sorghum or barley.
  • Adega: a traditional Eritrean honey wine made from fermented honey and water.

What to Buy in Keren: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Keren is known for its traditional handicrafts and textiles. Here are the best souvenirs to bring home:

  • Traditional jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Keren

Eritrea is a conservative country, and it’s important to show respect for the local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing.
  • Always ask permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.
  • Avoid eating or drinking in public during Ramadan.

Best Time to Visit Keren

The best time to visit Keren is from November to February when the weather is mild and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keren

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the ancient historical sites.
  • Show respect for the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission.
  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.
  • Display public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keren

Keren is generally safe, but travelers should take certain precautions, including:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas.
  • Follow local security advisories, especially in the border areas.

Health in Keren

Eritrea has a high risk of malaria, and travelers should take precautions before traveling. Other health concerns include:

  • Water- and food-borne illnesses.
  • Dengue fever and other insect-borne diseases.

Getting to Keren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keren

The nearest airport to Keren is Asmara International Airport, about a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Keren

Keren has a central bus station with links to other towns and cities in Eritrea.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Keren.

How to Get Around in Keren

Keren is a small city, and it’s easy to explore on foot. Taxis and minibusses are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Keren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asmara: the capital of Eritrea, known for its colonial architecture and vibrant cafes.
  • Massawa: a historic port city with Turkish and Egyptian influence.
  • Dahlak Islands: pristine islands with clear waters for diving and snorkeling.

Conclusion

Keren is a unique and captivating city that offers travelers a wealth of cultural, historical, and natural wonders. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you’ll be able to plan a memorable trip to Keren, Eritrea, and experience everything this fascinating city has to offer.
