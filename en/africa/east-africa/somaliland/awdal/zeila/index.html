Introduction to Zeila

Located in the Awdal region of Somaliland in East Africa, Zeila is a coastal town that boasts a rich history and natural beauty. It has served as a center of commerce and trade for centuries due to its strategic location, and it continues to attract visitors with its stunning scenery, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you everything you need to know to plan your trip to Zeila.

List of Places to Visit in Zeila

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zeila Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Zeila, from its ancient origins to its modern-day significance.
  • Sultanate of Adal Ruins: These ruins are the remains of a medieval kingdom that once ruled the region and are a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Saylac Rock Art: The oldest rock art in Somalia is located near Zeila and depicts animals, humans, and geometric patterns dating back to the Neolithic period.

Natural Attractions of Zeila: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laas Geel: This prehistoric rock art site near Hargeisa features vivid and well-preserved depictions of humans and animals, including giraffes, cows, and dogs.
  • Horseback riding on the beach: Enjoy a leisurely ride on horseback along the beautiful beaches of Zeila.
  • Bulaale Beach: Relax at one of the most beautiful beaches in Africa and admire the turquoise waters and white sand.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zeila

  • Mount Harbarda: Hike to the summit of this mountain for breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean and surrounding landscape.
  • Zeila Trekking Trail: This scenic trail takes you through the lush countryside, past grazing cattle and goats, and along the coast.

Best Things to Do in Zeila: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Tours: Rent a boat and spend a day fishing with a local guide for a chance to catch a variety of fish including barracuda, kingfish, and yellowfin tuna.
  • City Tour of Zeila: Learn about the history and culture of Zeila with a guided tour of the town’s most famous landmarks, including the ancient ruins and the pristine beaches.
  • Water Sports: Zeila’s beaches offer the perfect setting for activities such as kayaking, snorkeling, and scuba diving.

Food and Drink Scene of Zeila

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cambuulo: A traditional Somali dish made with black-eyed peas, butter, and spices, served with rice or bread.
  • Sabayad: Similar to a pancake, this Somali delicacy is made with flour, water, and oil, and is often served with honey and butter.
  • Bariis Isku-karis: Spiced rice with beef, served with vegetables and salad.

Local Beverages

  • Shaah: A popular Somali tea that is often spiced with cardamom, cinnamon, and other herbs.
  • Camel Milk: A staple in Somali cuisine, camel milk is often mixed with honey and spices to make a delicious and refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Zeila: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Damal Hotel: A luxury hotel with comfortable rooms overlooking the Indian Ocean.
  • Greenhouse Hostel: A budget-friendly option with dorm-style rooms and a communal kitchen.
  • Mansoor Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse with clean rooms and a pleasant courtyard.

What to Buy in Zeila: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Terra-cotta pottery: Handmade ceramics such as bowls, vases, and plates can be found in local markets.
  • Khat: A stimulant plant that is widely used in Somaliland and can be purchased in many markets.
  • Henna: A natural plant dye used for body art, also used in Somali weddings and other special occasions.

Local Culture and Customs of Zeila

Zeila is a predominantly Muslim town, and visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and practices. It’s also important to ask for permission before taking photos of people, especially women.

Best Time to Visit Zeila

The best time to visit Zeila is between December and February when the weather is mild and dry, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zeila

Do:

  • Respect local customs, traditions, and dress modestly.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites as malaria is present in the area.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people, especially women, without their permission.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Travel alone at night and avoid poorly lit areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zeila

Visitors to Zeila should avoid traveling alone at night and should take precautions to prevent theft or pickpocketing. It’s also important to be cautious when swimming in the ocean as the currents can be strong.

Health in Zeila

Visitors to Zeila should take precautions to prevent malaria, including wearing long sleeves and pants, using mosquito nets, and taking medication. It’s also important to drink bottled or filtered water and avoid eating street food.

Getting to Zeila: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zeila

The nearest airport to Zeila is Hargeisa International Airport, which is located approximately 210 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a bus or hire a car to reach Zeila.

Bus Stations Near Zeila

There are several bus stations in Hargeisa that offer transportation to Zeila, including Hargeisa Bus Terminal and the Hargeisa International Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Hargeisa to Zeila.

How to Get Around in Zeila

The best way to get around Zeila is by foot or bicycle, as the town is relatively small and easy to navigate. Taxis and buses are also available for longer trips outside of the city.

Where to Go Next from Zeila: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hargeisa: The capital of Somaliland offers a variety of attractions such as the Hargeisa Cultural Center and the Martyrs’ Monument.
  • Berbera: Another coastal town with beautiful beaches and a historic port.
  • Las Anod: The capital of the Sool region, known for its market and cultural events.

Conclusion

Zeila is a hidden gem in Somaliland that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, relaxing on pristine beaches, or immersing yourself in the local culture, Zeila has something to offer every traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Zeila.
