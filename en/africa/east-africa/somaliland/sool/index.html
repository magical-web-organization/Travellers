Introduction to Sool

Sool is a region in the autonomous state of Somaliland located in the northeastern part of Somalia. Sool is a hidden gem for travelers who are interested in discovering an off-the-beaten-path destination with a rich local culture, history, and stunning natural scenery. The region offers travelers a wide range of experiences that cater to their tastes and preferences, from exploring natural reserves and scenic landscapes to learning about the region’s rich history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Sool

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sool has numerous museums and historical places that recount the region’s rich and complex history. The most notable site is the colonial town of Las Anod, which was a British protectorate during the 19th century. Here are some of the other must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Taleh Ruins
  • Alla-Yarar Monument
  • Fardowsa Mausoleum
  • Ali Matan Mosque

Natural Attractions of Sool: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sool offers a wide range of natural attractions that are sure to delight nature-lovers and adventure-seekers. The region has several national parks, including Sool Plateau National Park, which boasts a diverse wildlife and unique geological formations. Visitors can also explore the region’s natural wonders, such as the Maakhir Coast and the Aynaba Oasis. Here are some of the other must-visit natural attractions:

  • Sagole Wildlife Reserve
  • Hawd Drylands
  • Buhoodle Lake
  • Cal Madow Mountains

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sool

Sool has some of the most breathtaking views and landscapes perfect for hiking and biking. The region offers both easy and advanced hiking trails that offer stunning views and unique cultural experiences. The most popular hiking and biking routes are:

  • Las-Anod to Taleh trek
  • Buhoodle Mountain Trails
  • Daallo Forest Reserve Trails

Best Things to Do in Sool: Activities and Tours

Visitors can engage in several activities in Sool that offer a glimpse of the local culture, history, and natural wonders. They can experience the Somali nomad way of life through herding and milking camels, hike and explore the rugged and various Sool mountain landscape, or visit the Taleh Ruins monument. Here are some other popular activities and tours in Sool:

  • Camel Trekking
  • Cultural Immersion
  • History and Heritage tours

Food and Drink Scene of Sool

Somali cuisine reflects the region’s diverse cultural influences, from the Arab and Indian spice trade to European colonization. Sool offers a wide range of local delicacies and beverages that are worth trying. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Anjero (Somali Pancake)
  • Maraq (Spiced Stew)
  • Muqmad (Dried Meat)
  • Sabaayad (Somali Flatbread)

Local Beverages

  • Somali Tea (Shaah)
  • Honey and Milk (Sharmuuto)
  • Camel Milk (Caano Geel)

Local Culture and Customs of Sool

Somali culture is unique and diverse, and visitors should be aware of the local customs and traditions to show respect to the communities they are visiting. Some local customs to keep in mind are:

  • Dress modestly in public places to show respect for the local culture and customs.
  • Seek permission before taking pictures of people or their property.
  • Use your right hand to greet locals or receive food and drinks.

Best Time to Visit Sool

The best time to visit Sool is during the dry season, which runs from December to March. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the landscapes are lush and green. The rainy season, which lasts from April to November, can make traveling difficult due to floods and muddy roads.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sool

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the local communities.
  • Observe local dress codes.
  • Seek permission before taking photos of people or their property.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water as it may not be safe for consumption.
  • Ignore local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sool

Sool is a relatively safe region to travel, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid traveling alone at night, stay in well-lit areas, and keep an eye on your belongings at all times. Always carry a copy of your travel documents with you, and keep valuables in a hotel safe deposit box.

Health in Sool

Visitors should take certain measures to ensure their health and avoid getting sick during their trip to Sool. We recommend getting vaccinated against hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies before traveling. Visitors should also drink bottled water and avoid consuming raw or uncooked food.
