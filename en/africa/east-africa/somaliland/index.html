Introduction to Somaliland

Somaliland, located in the Horn of Africa, is an autonomous region that is not recognized as a sovereign state by the international community. However, despite its lack of recognition, Somaliland remains a popular destination for travelers seeking off-the-beaten-path experiences. This region boasts a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a true hidden gem in East Africa.

List of Places to Visit in Somaliland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hargeisa Cultural Center - a contemporary art museum that showcases the works of local artists and promotes cultural exchange.
  2. Laas Geel - a complex of caves containing ancient rock art that dates back over 5,000 years.
  3. Zeila - an ancient port town that served as a trading center for the Kingdom of Axum and the Arab world.
  4. Berbera - a port city that was once a major trading hub for the Ottoman Empire.

Natural Attractions of Somaliland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Daallo Mountain - offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular destination for hiking and camping.
  2. Golis Range - a scenic mountain range that offers opportunities for hiking and wildlife watching.
  3. Laas Geel Hot Springs - a series of natural hot springs that are said to have therapeutic properties.
  4. Sheikh Pass - a mountain pass that offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Somaliland

  1. Daallo Mountain Trek - a challenging three-day trek that takes hikers to the summit of Daallo Mountain.
  2. Golis Range Trek - a moderate four-day trek that takes hikers into the heart of the Golis Range.
  3. Sheikh Pass Mountain Bike Trail - a thrilling mountain bike trail that takes riders through rugged terrain and offers stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Somaliland: Activities and Tours

  1. Camel Trekking - explore the desert landscape of Somaliland on the back of a camel.
  2. Surfing - catch some waves at the popular surf spot in Berbera.
  3. Scuba Diving - discover the underwater world off the coast of Berbera.
  4. Wildlife Watching - spot wildlife such as gazelles, hyenas, and baboons in the Golis Range.

Week-Long Itinerary for Somaliland

Day 1 Arrival in Hargeisa, visit Hargeisa Cultural Center
Day 2 Visit Laas Geel, drive to Berbera
Day 3 Explore Berbera, go surfing
Day 4 Take a boat tour of the coast, go scuba diving
Day 5 Drive to Sheikh Pass, go hiking or mountain biking
Day 6 Trek in the Golis Range, go wildlife watching
Day 7 Return to Hargeisa for departure

Food and Drink Scene of Somaliland

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cambuulo - a hearty stew made with butter, sugar, and beans, usually served with rice or bread.
  2. Somali Tea - a sweet milky tea that is a staple of Somali culture.
  3. Spicy Rice - rice cooked with meat, vegetables, and spices, often served with a side of salad.

Local Beverages

  1. Camel Milk - a popular drink among Somalilanders, camel milk is said to have many health benefits.
  2. Shax - a sweet, thick drink made from sorghum and spices.
  3. Coffee - Somaliland is known for its high-quality coffee beans, which are roasted and served in traditional Somalian style.

Where to Stay in Somaliland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hargeisa Palace Hotel - a luxury hotel in the heart of Hargeisa.
  2. Damal Hotel - a budget-friendly hotel in the heart of Berbera.
  3. Red Sea Hotel - a beachside hotel with stunning views of the Gulf of Aden.

What to Buy in Somaliland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Somali Incense - a fragrant resin that is burned as incense in Somali culture.
  2. Somali Cloth - a colorful and intricately patterned fabric that is used to make traditional Somali clothing.
  3. Camel Leather Goods - handcrafted leather products made from camel hide, such as bags and sandals.

Local Culture and Customs of Somaliland

Somali culture is renowned for its hospitality and traditional values. When visiting someone’s home, it is customary to remove your shoes before entering and to bring a small gift, such as sweets or a small souvenir. Additionally, Islam is the predominant religion in Somaliland, and visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Somaliland

The best time to visit Somaliland is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. During this time, temperatures are mild and rainfall is low, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The rest of the year can be hot and humid, with occasional rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Somaliland

Do:

  1. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  2. Try local cuisine, such as Cambuulo and Somali Tea.
  3. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Drink alcohol in public, as it is forbidden in Somalian culture.
  2. Take photographs of people without their permission.
  3. Disrespect Islam or other religious beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Somaliland

Somaliland is generally considered safe for travelers, but visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings at all times. Petty crime is not uncommon, so travelers should take steps to secure their belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. Additionally, the region is prone to occasional violence and instability, so it is important to stay informed about local developments before and during your trip.

Health in Somaliland

Travelers to Somaliland should take precautions to avoid common illnesses such as diarrhea and malaria. It is recommended to drink only bottled water, avoid street food, and seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness. It is also a good idea to visit a travel health clinic before your trip to ensure that you have received all necessary vaccinations and medications.

Getting to Somaliland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Somaliland

  1. Hargeisa Egal International Airport (HGA) - the main airport in Somaliland, with flights from several international destinations and major cities in East Africa.
  2. Berbera Airport (BBO) - a smaller airport with limited regional service.

Bus Stations Near Somaliland

  1. Hargeisa Bus Station - the main bus station in Somaliland, with frequent service to cities throughout the region.
  2. Berbera Bus Station - a smaller bus station with limited service to regional destinations.

Other Options

Private car/taxi, shared taxis, and local buses are also available for transportation within Somaliland.

How to Get Around in Somaliland

The most common way to get around Somaliland is by private car/taxi or shared taxi. Local buses are also available for longer distances, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable. Bicycles and motorbikes can be rented in some cities for shorter trips.

Where to Go Next from Somaliland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Djibouti - a small country located to the north of Somaliland, known for its hot springs and French-influenced culture.
  2. Ethiopia - a large African country located to the west of Somaliland, known for its ancient history, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes.
  3. Kenya - a popular tourist destination located to the south of Somaliland, known for its wildlife, beaches, and diverse culture.

Conclusion

Somaliland may not be on the top of many people’s travel list, but it is a destination that should not be overlooked. With its rich history, culture, and natural beauty, Somaliland offers a unique and authentic travel experience. Whether you are interested in hiking in the mountains, surfing on the coast, or exploring ancient rock art, Somaliland has something for everyone. So why not plan your trip to this hidden gem in East Africa today?
