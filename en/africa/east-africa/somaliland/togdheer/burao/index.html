Introduction to Burao

Burao is a city located in the Togdheer region of Somaliland, in East Africa. It is an important commercial hub in the region, known for its lively markets, friendly locals, and breathtaking views. The city’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty attract tourists from all parts of the world.

In this guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive tour of Burao, including the places to visit, things to do, what to eat, where to stay, and much more. So, let’s get started and explore this amazing city!

List of Places to Visit in Burao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Burao is a city that is steeped in history and culture, which is reflected in its numerous museums and historical places. Here are some of the must-visit attractions for history and culture enthusiasts:

  • Burao Museum: This museum provides an insight into the city’s history and culture, showcasing traditional Somali artifacts, artwork, and handicrafts.
  • Saaxil Beach: This beautiful beach is a popular spot for picnics, swimming, and relaxing. It is also an important historical site, featuring ancient ruins of a palace of the Adal Sultanate.
  • Hargeisa National Museum: The museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibitions on the history and culture of Somaliland and Somalia.

Natural Attractions of Burao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burao boasts a range of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts looking for adventure or serene views. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in Burao:

  • Daallo Mountain: This scenic mountain offers stunning views and is perfect for trekking and hiking.
  • Haud Plateau: This vast plateau is a beautiful, peaceful, and a great place to explore and enjoy nature.
  • Waterfalls: Burao offers several breathtaking waterfalls, including Golis Waterfalls and Teferi Waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burao

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love Burao’s range of hiking and biking routes. Ranging from easy to challenging, you can explore the city’s beautiful landscapes, mountains, and trails at your own pace. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Daallo Mountain Hike: This challenging trek will take you to the top of Daallo Mountain and is perfect for those looking for a strenuous adventure.
  • Hargeisa To Gabilay Bike Ride: This easy-to-moderate bike ride takes you through the beautiful countryside, passing through beautiful landscapes, ruins, and traditional villages.

Best Things to Do in Burao: Activities and Tours

Burao offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Shop at the Market: Burao’s markets are famous for their lively atmosphere, bazaars, and exciting shopping experiences.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Burao’s local cuisine is a blend of Somali, Ethiopian, and Eritrean flavors, with must-try dishes like camel meat, rice, and Somali tea.
  • Explore the City: From admiring historic buildings and monuments to discovering traditional villages, walking through the streets of Burao is an adventure in itself.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burao

Here is a sample week-long itinerary that will help you make the most out of your visit to Burao:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Burao, relax and explore the city on foot.
  • Day 2: Visit the Burao Museum and Hargeisa National Museum, and head to Saaxil Beach for a natural and historical excursion.
  • Day 3: Go trekking or hiking on Daallo Mountain and explore the scenic views.
  • Day 4: Explore the city’s bustling markets for shopping and local cuisine tasting.
  • Day 5: Head to the Haud Plateau for a calming and picturesque escape.
  • Day 6: Take a bike ride from Hargeisa to Gabilay.
  • Day 7: Relax, reflect and prepare to leave for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Burao

Must-Try Dishes

Burao’s local cuisine is a must-try experience for any visitor. The blend of Somali, Ethiopian, and Eritrean flavors will tantalize your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes in Burao:

  • Camel Meat: This staple food is unique to Somalia and is a must-try when in Burao.
  • Rice: Rice is an essential part of Somali cuisine, and Burao offers variations seasoned with spices such as cumin, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  • Somali Tea: Made with black tea, fresh milk, and spices, Somali tea is a delicious and comforting treat.

Local Beverages

Somaliland has a long tradition of producing and consuming beverages. Here are some must-try local beverages in Burao:

  • Somali Tea: Somali tea is a quintessential beverage in the region, perfect to quench your thirst and for relaxing moments.
  • Somali Coffee: Somali coffee is a must-try coffee experience, with a blend of spices locals prefer, it is the perfect drink when taking a break.

Where to Stay in Burao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burao offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some top recommendations for places to stay in Burao:

  • Burao Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a scenic view of the city.
  • Sangub Hostel: This budget-friendly accommodation offers cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Daryeel Hotel: This hotel offers a range of room options and a fantastic dining experience with Somali cuisine at its best.

What to Buy in Burao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burao’s markets are famous for their affordable and diverse range of souvenirs and local products. Here are some must-buy products:

  • Kudu Horns: Kudu horns and horn products are widely sold locally and make for unique and ecologically friendly souvenirs.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: Burao is famous for its hand-woven mats, glassware, and pottery products.

Local Culture and Customs of Burao

Somali culture is diverse and often revolves around traditional Islam values. Here are some customs that travelers visiting Burao should follow:

  • Dress modestly: Public displays of affection and lack of modesty are considered taboo in Somali culture, so dress conservatively when in public places.
  • Show respect: Always respect elders and anyone in positions of authority, they are highly valued in Somali culture.
  • Eat with your right hand: In Somali culture, eating with your left hand is considered impolite, so make sure you eat with your right hand.

Best Time to Visit Burao

The best time to visit Burao is from November to April when the weather is mild and sunny. During this period, temperatures in the city range from 25 to 32 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burao

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture: Always show respect for the local culture and abide by the customs when visiting Burao.
  • Cover up: Dress modestly and appropriately for public places.
  • Try the local food: Burao’s cuisine is a must-try experience.

Don’t:

  • Display affection in public: Public displays of affection, including hand-holding, kissing, and cuddling, are not considered acceptable in Somali culture.
  • Take photographs without permission: Always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or places.
  • Eat with your left hand: In Somali culture, eating with your left hand is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burao

Burao is a relatively safe city to visit, but travelers should exercise caution, especially when walking in crowded areas. Here are some general safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night: When walking at night, it is advisable to walk with a companion.
  • Be vigilant in crowded areas: Always be mindful of pickpockets and bag snatchers in crowded areas.

Health in Burao

To stay healthy while in Burao, travelers should adhere to the following health tips:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Get vaccinated: Before traveling to Burao, it is recommended that you get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B, tetanus, and measles.
  • Avoid uncooked food: To avoid getting sick, avoid eating raw or uncooked food.

Getting to Burao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burao

  • Egal International Airport, Hargeisa: Egal International Airport is the nearest airport to Burao, located at a distance of 123 kilometers.

Bus Stations Near Burao

  • Hargeisa Central Bus Station: The central bus station in Hargeisa is the nearest large bus station, located at a distance of approximately 129 kilometers from Burao.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are available as both shared and private options, and it takes around 2-3 hours to reach Burao.

How to Get Around in Burao

The most convenient way to get around Burao is by taxi or private car. Shared taxis are also available for short distances in the city. Buses do not regularly run within the city.

Where to Go Next from Burao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can visit from Burao:

  • Hargeisa: Hargeisa, the capital city of Somaliland, is the nearest large city and is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.
  • Las Geel: Las Geel is a set of ancient rock paintings in Somaliland, considered among the earliest and most well-preserved in Africa.
  • Berbera: Berbera is a coastal city known for its stunning beaches and scenic views.

Conclusion

Burao is a beautiful city in Somaliland that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. This guide covers everything you need to know to plan your visit to Burao, from where to stay, what to do, and where to go next. So pack your bags, and embark on an adventure to this enchanting East African city.
