Introduction to Soroti

Soroti is a vibrant town in Eastern Uganda that is often overlooked by travelers to the country. However, this bustling town has a lot to offer to those who take the time to explore it. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks to outdoor activities, Soroti has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll help you plan the ultimate trip to Soroti, Uganda.

List of Places to Visit in Soroti

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Soroti Rock Paintings: These prehistoric paintings, estimated to be over 4000 years old, can be found along the rock outcrops of Kadam Hill. They depict animals, human beings, and other symbols, and are believed to have been used for religious and cultural purposes.
  • Teso Cultural Museum: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Teso people. Visitors can see traditional dance performances, view historical artifacts, and learn about the history and customs of the local people.
  • Nyero Rock Paintings: These rock paintings, estimated to date back to 1250 AD, depict human figures, animals, and geometric designs. They are believed to have been created by the Sotho people, who lived in the area at the time.

Natural Attractions of Soroti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Soroti Flying Club: Visitors can take scenic flights over the town and surrounding countryside, offering spectacular views of the area.
  • Mt. Napak: This mountain offers hiking and climbing opportunities. Along the way, you will encounter stunning scenery and breathtaking views of the surrounding plains.
  • Lake Opeta: This serene lake, located just outside the town, is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soroti

  • Kadam Hill: This hill offers hiking trails with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Teso Hills: This range of hills offers challenging bike trails and spectacular views.

Best Things to Do in Soroti: Activities and Tours

  • Teso Safari Lodge: This lodge offers a range of activities, including game drives, horseback riding, hiking, and cultural activities.
  • Teso Cultural Centre: This center offers a range of activities, including traditional dance performances, cultural tours, and workshops on traditional crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Soroti

Must-Try Dishes

  • Esabalu: A traditional dish made from yams and sorghum flour. It is often served with a side of beans and vegetables.
  • Ekyumakyuma: Grilled beef skewers, served with a side of maize bread and vegetables.
  • Eshabwe: A traditional sauce made from ghee and milk, often served with fresh vegetables or millet bread.

Local Beverages

  • Tonto: A locally brewed beer made from millet.
  • Adumu: A locally brewed gin made from bananas.

Where to Stay in Soroti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Teso Safari Lodge: This lodge offers comfortable accommodations in a serene setting, with a range of activities and amenities.
  • Soroti Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable and affordable rooms in the heart of the town.
  • Victoria Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers affordable accommodations with a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Soroti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Crafts: Teso culture has a rich tradition of weaving, pottery, and basketry, all of which make great souvenirs.
  • Beaded Jewelry: The Teso people are known for their intricate and colorful beadwork, which make great gifts.
  • Local Produce: Soroti is known for its fresh fruits and vegetables, including mangoes, passion fruit, and avocados.

Local Culture and Customs of Soroti

The Teso people are known for their welcoming spirit and hospitality. Visitors should respect local customs, including dressing modestly when visiting cultural sites and greeting locals with a respectful “Hello” or “Ejolem.”

Best Time to Visit Soroti

The best time to visit Soroti is during the dry season, which runs from June to August and December to February. During this time, the weather is mild and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soroti

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites.
  • Greet locals with a respectful “Hello” or “Ejolem.”

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without asking for permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Soroti

Soroti is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take usual precautions, such as not carrying valuables in crowded areas and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Soroti

Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date on all vaccinations and take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as malaria is prevalent in the area. Visitors should also drink only bottled or boiled water and avoid street food to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Soroti: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Soroti

The nearest international airport to Soroti is Entebbe International Airport, which is located approximately 290 km away. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight to Soroti Airport, which is located just outside the town.

Bus Stations Near Soroti

Visitors can take a bus to Soroti from Kampala or Mbale. The journey takes approximately six hours from Kampala and two hours from Mbale.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Soroti from Kampala or Mbale.

How to Get Around in Soroti

Visitors can get around Soroti by taking a taxi or hiring a private car. It is also possible to hire bicycles from local vendors.

Where to Go Next from Soroti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mbale: This town is located approximately 95 km away and is known for its stunning views of Mount Elgon, waterfalls, and coffee plantations.
  • Jinja: This town is located approximately 250 km away and is known as the source of the Nile River. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including white-water rafting and bungee jumping.
  • Kampala: Uganda’s capital city is located approximately 270 km away and offers a range of attractions for visitors, including historical sites, cultural landmarks, shopping malls, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Soroti is a hidden gem in Uganda, with a wealth of natural and cultural attractions to explore. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or cultural experiences, Soroti has something to offer. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan the ultimate trip to this vibrant town and take home memories that will last a lifetime.
