Introduction to Mbale

Nestled in the foothills of Mount Elgon, Mbale is a charming town in Eastern Uganda that offers a perfect blend of natural wonders, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The town is a popular stopover for tourists visiting Uganda and boasts a rich history that reflects the diversity of the region. Known as the ‘Land of the Wanale’ in the local dialect, Mbale is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Uganda.

List of Places to Visit in Mbale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bamunanika Cultural Site: This historic site showcases the cultural heritage of the Baganda people, with traditional buildings, a royal palace, and a sacred tree believed to offer blessings.
  • Ssezibwa Falls: A sacred natural site that forms part of local folklore, Ssezibwa Falls is believed to have healing powers and is a popular destination for tourists.
  • Igongo Cultural Centre: This center is a museum that highlights the history and traditions of the Banyakitara people and Ugandan cultures through exhibits, art, and performances.
  • Jinja: A town on the banks of the Nile River, Jinja is known as the adventure capital of Uganda, home to excellent whitewater rafting, kayaking, and bungee jumping.

Natural Attractions of Mbale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Elgon National Park: The park is home to the ancient Mount Elgon volcano, the fourth-highest mountain in East Africa. Hiking and wildlife tours are popular activities in the park.
  • Sipi Falls: A series of three breath-taking waterfalls located on the foothills of Mount Elgon, Sipi Falls is a popular destination for hiking, bird watching, and cultural tours.
  • Lake Victoria: The largest freshwater lake in Africa, Lake Victoria is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and relaxing on the shores.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mbale

  • Mount Elgon Hiking: A hike to the peak of Mount Elgon is a must-do activity in Mbale. The mountain offers a variety of routes for beginners and experienced hikers.
  • Sipi Falls Trekking: A trek to the Sipi Falls offers a chance to explore the beautiful landscapes of the area, including coffee plantations, waterfalls, and rock formations.
  • Biking in Jinja: Biking through the villages and the surrounding hills is a great way to explore the region’s natural and cultural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Mbale: Activities and Tours

  • Coffee Tour: One of the main exports of the region is coffee, and a coffee tour in Mbale offers an opportunity to visit local farms and learn about the coffee-making process.
  • Cultural Tour: A cultural tour in Mbale offers an insight into the daily lives of the local people and their traditions and customs.
  • Wildlife Safari: The region is home to various wildlife species such as elephants, buffaloes, and leopards. A guided tour to the national park can provide an unforgettable experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Mbale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rolex: A popular Ugandan street food that contains a rolled chapati, vegetables, and eggs.
  • Matoke: A traditional dish containing mashed green bananas and other ingredients such as meat or groundnuts.
  • Posho and beans: One of the staple foods of Uganda, posho is made from maize flour and served with beans.

Local Beverages

  • Waragi: A locally brewed gin made from bananas and other ingredients.
  • Ugandan coffee: Uganda is one of Africa’s biggest coffee producers, and a visit to the local coffee shop or plantation is a must.

Where to Stay in Mbale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options in Mbale, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some recommended options include:

  • Mount Elgon Hotel: A budget hotel located in the center of Mbale, offering comfortable rooms and excellent facilities.
  • Lacam Lodge: A luxurious lodge located on the outskirts of Mbale, offering beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Mbale Resort Hotel: A five-star hotel located in the heart of Mbale, offering luxurious amenities such as a swimming pool, gym, and spa.

What to Buy in Mbale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • African crafts: Mbale is famous for its traditional crafts, including woodwork, fabrics, and sculptures.
  • Coffee and tea: Uganda is known for producing some of the best coffee and tea in Africa, and visitors can buy freshly roasted beans or tea leave to take home.
  • Batiks: Batiks are a popular form of traditional clothing made from brightly colored fabric and can be found in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Mbale

The people of Mbale are friendly, welcoming, and hospitable, with a strong sense of community. Visitors are advised to respect the cultural practices and traditions of the locals, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes when entering homes. Tipping is not compulsory, but a small token of appreciation is appreciated, particularly in the hospitality industry.

Best Time to Visit Mbale

The best time to visit Mbale is during the dry season, which runs from December to February, and from June to August. During this time, the weather is favorable for outdoor activities, and the wildlife is more visible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mbale

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting places of worship and other cultural sites.
  • Try the local delicacies and coffee.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.
  • Show public displays of affection, which can offend locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mbale

Mbale is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise caution and remain aware of their surroundings, particularly when traveling alone or at night. Avoid walking in isolated areas, and always take the necessary precautions to protect your belongings.

Health in Mbale

Medical facilities in Mbale are limited, and visitors are advised to carry a comprehensive travel insurance policy that covers medical emergencies. It is recommended to take malaria medication and drink bottled or boiled water to prevent illnesses while in Uganda.

Getting to Mbale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mbale

  • Entebbe International Airport: The main international airport in Uganda, located around 280 kilometers from Mbale.
  • Soroti Airport: A regional airport located around 80 kilometers from Mbale, offering domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Mbale

  • Mbale Bus Station
  • Jinja Bus Station
  • Kampala Bus Station

Other Options

  • Private car hire
  • Taxi service

How to Get Around in Mbale

Visitors to Mbale can use various modes of transportation, including buses, taxis, and boda-bodas (motorcycles). Boda-bodas can be a quicker and more affordable option for short trips within the town, but they can also be dangerous.

Where to Go Next from Mbale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kampala: Uganda’s capital city, Kampala is the largest city in Uganda and offers a blend of modern and traditional attractions.
  • Murchison Falls National Park: A popular destination for wildlife and adventure lovers, the national park is known for its waterfalls and wildlife.
  • Kidepo Valley National Park: Located in the far north of Uganda, this remote park is a hidden gem, featuring stunning landscapes and wildlife species such as lions, elephants, and giraffes.

Conclusion

Mbale is a perfect destination for tourists looking for a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. With its fantastic hiking trails, scenic landscapes, and rich cultural experience, Mbale is worth adding to your Uganda itinerary.
