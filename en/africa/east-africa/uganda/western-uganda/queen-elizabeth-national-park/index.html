Introduction to Queen Elizabeth National Park

Queen Elizabeth National Park is a 1978km² protected wilderness reserve situated in Western Uganda, in East Africa. Established in 1952, the park’s diverse landscapes include rolling savannah plains, dense forests, wetlands, lakes, and deep gorges. The park is home to over 95 mammal species, 500 bird species, and various primate species like chimpanzees and black-and-white colobus monkeys. Queen Elizabeth National Park is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to experience Africa’s wildlife and landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Queen Elizabeth National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kikorongo Cultural Center

Kikorongo Cultural Center is a community-based tourism initiative that showcases the traditions and way of life of the indigenous communities around Queen Elizabeth National Park. The center offers a guided tour of a typical Bakiga homestead, where visitors can learn about traditional hunting practices, food preservation, and craft making.

Kyambura Gorge

Kyambura Gorge is a deep canyon in the park surrounded by lush forest cover. The gorge is home to a variety of primates, including chimpanzees, baboons, and red-tailed monkeys. Visitors can go on a guided nature walk through the gorge, which offers stunning views of the park’s landscapes and wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Queen Elizabeth National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kazinga Channel

Kazinga Channel is a 32km-long natural waterway that connects Lake George to Lake Edward. The channel teems with a variety of aquatic life, such as hippos, crocodiles, and various fish species. Visitors can take a boat cruise along the channel and witness various wildlife species that congregate on its banks.

Ishasha Sector

Ishasha Sector is a 100km² area within the park’s Southern region known for its pristine wilderness and diverse wildlife. The sector is famous for its tree-climbing lions, which have adapted to life in the area’s fig trees, where they can easily spot their prey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Queen Elizabeth National Park

The Nature Trail

The Nature Trail is a 2km-long guided hiking trail that meanders through the park’s grasslands and woodland areas. Visitors can choose from a variety of options, including early morning birding walks, forest walks, and community walks.

Ishasha Wilderness Trail

The Ishasha Wilderness Trail is a 5km-long guided hiking trail that takes visitors through the Ishasha Sector’s pristine wilderness. The trail offers a unique opportunity to experience Africa’s wild landscapes, savannah plains, and diverse wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Queen Elizabeth National Park: Activities and Tours

Wildlife Game Drives

Wildlife game drives are one of the most popular activities in Queen Elizabeth National Park. Visitors can choose to go on a morning or evening game drive, which provides an opportunity to see lions, elephants, cape buffaloes, leopards, antelopes, and various bird species.

Chimpanzee Tracking

Chimpanzee tracking is a must-do activity in Queen Elizabeth National Park. Visitors can track chimpanzees in Kyambura Gorge or Kalinzu Forest, where they can also witness other primates such as red-tailed monkeys and black-and-white colobus monkeys.

Food and Drink Scene of Queen Elizabeth National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Ugali, Matooke, and Luwombo are the most popular dishes in the region. These dishes are made from locally sourced ingredients such as cassava, plantains, and meat.

Local Beverages

Ugandans love their tea and coffee. Visitors can sample the region’s locally sourced tea or taste some of Uganda’s finest coffee at any of the park’s lodges and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Queen Elizabeth National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Queen Elizabeth National Park offers a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget to luxury. Some of the options include:

  • Mweya Safari Lodge
  • Kasenyi Safari Camp
  • Enganzi Game Lodge
  • Pumba Safari Cottages

What to Buy in Queen Elizabeth National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The park has various craft markets where visitors can buy souvenirs such as traditional African wear, jewelry, and local crafts like carvings and woven baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Queen Elizabeth National Park

The local tribes of Bakiga and Banyankole form a significant part of the region’s cultural identity. Visitors are encouraged to respect the customs and traditions of the indigenous communities while visiting the park.

Best Time to Visit Queen Elizabeth National Park

The best time to visit Queen Elizabeth National Park is during the dry season, which runs from December to February and June to August. During this period, the park’s wildlife is more active, and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Queen Elizabeth National Park

Do:

  • Carry binoculars and a camera to capture the park’s stunning landscapes and wildlife.
  • Go on guided tours or hikes to explore the park’s wilderness safely.
  • Respect the park’s wildlife and do not interfere with their habitats or feeding patterns.

Don’t:

  • Litter the park or engage in activities that could harm the environment.
  • Approach the park’s wildlife, especially the predators.
  • Ignore park rules or regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Queen Elizabeth National Park

Queen Elizabeth National Park has experienced occasional seismic activity in recent years, making it crucial to adhere to park rules and regulations to ensure visitor safety. Additionally, visitors are required to take extra precautions during wildlife sightings to ensure their safety.

Health in Queen Elizabeth National Park

Visitors are required to obtain necessary vaccinations before visiting the park. Malaria is prevalent in the region, and it’s advisable to take precautionary measures such as using mosquito nets and repellents.

Getting to Queen Elizabeth National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Queen Elizabeth National Park

Kasese Airport is the nearest airport to Queen Elizabeth National Park, located 45km south of the park. The airport has regular flights to Entebbe International Airport, Uganda’s main airport.

Bus Stations Near Queen Elizabeth National Park

Visitors can take a bus from Kampala or Entebbe to Kasese, which is the nearest town to Queen Elizabeth National Park. Buses offer a cheaper option for visitors traveling on a budget.

Other Options

Visitors can hire private cars or taxis to transport them from Entebbe or Kampala to Queen Elizabeth National Park.

How to Get Around in Queen Elizabeth National Park

Tour companies operating in the park offer shuttle services to transport visitors from one destination to another. Alternatively, visitors can rent a car from car rental companies located in Kasese or hire a safari vehicle with a driver-guide.
