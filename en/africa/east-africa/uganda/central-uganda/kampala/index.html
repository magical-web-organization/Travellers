Introduction to Kampala

Nestled in the heart of Central Uganda, Kampala is a sprawling metropolis that blends modern development with rich cultural heritage. Boasting a diverse array of tourist attractions, Kampala is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring Uganda.

In this guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit in Kampala, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, must-try dishes, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Kampala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kampala is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into Uganda’s rich cultural history. The Uganda Museum, located in the heart of Kampala, showcases the country’s art, culture, and history. Other must-visit museums in Kampala include the Ndere Cultural Centre and the Uganda National Cultural Centre.

Kampala also boasts several historical sites, including the Kasubi Tombs, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and the Uganda Martyrs Shrine, which commemorates the deaths of 22 Ugandan Christians who were killed for refusing to renounce their faith in 1886.

Natural Attractions of Kampala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty to explore in Kampala. The city is home to several beautiful parks, including the expansive and picturesque Entebbe Botanical Garden, the Mabira Forest Reserve, and the Kasubi Hill Nature Site.

Kampala also offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, including hiking and biking trails, bird watching, and wildlife safaris. The Ziwa Rhino Sanctuary, located about two hours from Kampala, is a top destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kampala

Kampala boasts several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Popular trails include the Namugongo Martyrs Trail, which runs from the Uganda Martyrs Shrine to Namugongo Martyrs Shrine, and the Kampala Hill Trail, which winds through the hills of Kampala and offers panoramic views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Kampala: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy during your stay in Kampala. Take a city tour to get a sense of Kampala’s history and culture, explore the local art scene at the Makerere Art Gallery, or enjoy a relaxing boat ride on Lake Victoria.

For a truly immersive experience, consider booking a cultural tour. The Kampala Cultural Trail offers a unique opportunity to explore Kampala’s vibrant cultural scene, including traditional dance and music performances, food tours, and craft markets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kampala

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kampala, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Visit the Uganda Museum, the Ndere Cultural Centre, and the Uganda National Cultural Centre.

Day 2: Head out of the city to explore the Mabira Forest Reserve and the Kasubi Tombs.

Day 3: Take a city tour of Kampala, including stops at the Makerere Art Gallery and the bustling Owino Market.

Day 4: Explore Kampala’s outdoor adventures, including hiking in the surrounding hills and bird watching at the Entebbe Botanical Garden.

Day 5: Visit the Ziwa Rhino Sanctuary and enjoy a wildlife safari.

Day 6: Take a cultural tour of Kampala, including traditional dance performances, food tours, and craft markets.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Lake Victoria or take a boat ride to one of the nearby islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Kampala

Must-Try Dishes

Kampala’s vibrant food scene is a testament to its rich cultural heritage. One of the must-try dishes in Kampala is Rolex, a kind of Ugandan egg wrap made with chapati, eggs, and vegetables.

Other traditional dishes to sample include Ugandan peanut sauce, matooke (boiled plantains), and nyama choma (grilled meat).

Local Beverages

Kampala is home to several local beverages, including banana beer, a fermented drink made from mashed bananas, and Waragi, a spirit made from sugarcane.

Where to Stay in Kampala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kampala offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. If you’re looking for budget-friendly options, consider Kampala Backpackers, Mirembe Resort Beach Hotel, and the Fat Cat Backpackers.

For a more luxurious stay, check out the Kampala Serena Hotel, Sheraton Kampala Hotel, or the Munyonyo Commonwealth Resort.

What to Buy in Kampala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kampala offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, including woven baskets, handmade crafts, and traditional clothing. You can pick up souvenirs at local markets, such as the bustling Owino Market or the more refined Craft Village.

Local Culture and Customs of Kampala

Ugandans are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors. However, it’s important to keep in mind that Uganda is a conservative country, and visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kampala

The best time to visit Kampala is during the dry season, from December to February and from June to September. During these months, the weather is mild and dry, offering perfect conditions for outdoor adventures and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kampala

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few words of Luganda, the national language of Uganda, and use them to greet the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures of people or places without asking for permission first.
  • Don’t buy souvenirs made from endangered species or protected materials, such as ivory or tortoise shells.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kampala

Kampala is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to keep your wits about you and take precautions to prevent theft and pickpocketing. Avoid wearing flashy jewelry or carrying large sums of money, and keep your valuables close to your body when in public.

Health in Kampala

Visitors to Kampala should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as malaria and dengue fever, by using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants. It’s also recommended that visitors get a yellow fever vaccine before traveling to Uganda.

Getting to Kampala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kampala

The Entebbe International Airport is the main airport serving Kampala and is located about 40 kilometers from the city center. Several airlines, including Ethiopian Airlines, Kenya Airways, and Emirates, offer flights to Entebbe from major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Kampala

Kampala is also accessible by bus from other cities in Uganda and neighboring countries, such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Rwanda. Popular bus companies include Kampala Coach and Modern Coast.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Kampala by train, although train service in Uganda is limited. The Kampala railway station is located in the city center and offers service to other cities in Uganda.

How to Get Around in Kampala

Kampala offers several options for getting around the city, including boda bodas (motorcycle taxis), taxis, and public buses. It’s important to make sure that you only use licensed taxis and boda bodas, and negotiate the fare before starting your journey.

Where to Go Next from Kampala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions to explore from Kampala, including the stunning Sipi Falls, the adventurous Jinja, and the wildlife-rich Queen Elizabeth National Park.

Conclusion

Kampala is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers something for everyone, from its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural attractions. Plan your trip to Kampala today and discover all that Uganda has to offer.
