Introduction to Shimba Hills National Reserve

Nestled in Coastal Kenya, Shimba Hills National Reserve is a unique and biodiverse ecosystem that is home to a wide range of endemic species, including elephants, giraffes, and antelopes. This reserve is an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers, offering a variety of outdoor activities and attractions. Shimba Hills is also renowned for its picturesque landscapes, which feature pristine forests, waterfalls, and scenic coastal views.

List of Places to Visit in Shimba Hills National Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in history and culture, Shimba Hills National Reserve has several attractions that are worth a visit. One such place is the Shimba Hills Historical Site, which is located near the entrance to the reserve. This historic site is an ancient settlement that dates back to the pre-colonial era, and it features several ruins and artifacts.

Another important cultural attraction is the Sheldrick Falls, which is situated in a beautiful forested area of the reserve. This waterfall is named after the famous conservationist David Sheldrick and is a popular picnic spot among visitors.

Natural Attractions of Shimba Hills National Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shimba Hills National Reserve is famous for its natural beauty and diverse flora and fauna. The reserve features several natural attractions, including:

  • Shimba Hills Forest: This pristine forest spans over 250 km2 and is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, buffalos, and leopards.
  • Shimba Hills Waterfalls: The reserve is home to several waterfalls, including the magnificent Sheldrick Falls and the vibrant Marere Dam, which is surrounded by lush vegetation.
  • Scenic Views: Visitors to Shimba Hills can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and coastline from viewpoints such as the Mwaluganje Elephant Sanctuary and the Kivuli Hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shimba Hills National Reserve

Shimba Hills National Reserve offers several well-marked hiking and biking trails that offer a unique way to experience the reserve’s natural beauty. The following routes are popular among visitors:

  • Sheldrick Falls Trail: This scenic trail takes visitors through the lush forests of the reserve to the awe-inspiring Sheldrick Falls.
  • Marere Dam Trail: This 5 km trail leads to the tranquil Marere Dam, which is surrounded by dense vegetation and towering cliffs.
  • Kwale Island Trail: This biking trail takes visitors through the villages of Kwale Island, allowing them to experience the local culture and customs.

Best Things to Do in Shimba Hills National Reserve: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the reserve’s natural and cultural attractions, visitors to Shimba Hills can engage in a range of activities and tours, including:

  • Game Drives: Shimba Hills is home to several species of wildlife, and visitors can experience these animals up close via a game drive.
  • Bird Watching: The reserve is home to over 100 species of birds, including the rare and endangered Fischer’s Turaco.
  • Camping: Several campsites are available within the reserve, and visitors can enjoy a wild camping experience surrounded by nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shimba Hills National Reserve

For those interested in spending more time exploring Shimba Hills National Reserve, the following week-long itinerary is recommended:

  • Day 1-3: Explore the reserve on foot or via a game drive, and visit cultural attractions such as the Shimba Hills Historical Site and Sheldrick Falls.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of the nearby Mwaluganje Elephant Sanctuary, which is home to a herd of wild elephants.
  • Day 5-6: Go hiking or biking on the reserve’s scenic trails, and visit natural attractions such as the Marere Dam and Kivuli Hill.
  • Day 7: Relax at one of the reserve’s campsites or picnic areas, and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Shimba Hills National Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

Shimba Hills National Reserve offers a range of culinary experiences, including traditional coastal cuisine and international dishes. Some must-try local dishes include:

  • Pilau: A fragrant rice dish cooked with spices, meat (usually chicken), and vegetables.
  • Biryani: A festive rice dish that is traditionally served during special occasions, such as weddings and Eid celebrations.
  • Swahili Curry: A flavorful sauce made with coconut milk and spices, usually served with rice or chapati.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Shimba Hills can sample a variety of locally-made beverages, including:

  • Chai: A spiced tea made with milk and a blend of local spices.
  • Maji Ya Ubuyu: A refreshing drink made from the fruit of the baobab tree, which is believed to have medicinal properties.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is readily available in Shimba Hills and is a popular drink among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Shimba Hills National Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shimba Hills National Reserve offers several accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. These include:

  • Shimba Hills Lodge: This luxury lodge offers spacious rooms and stunning views of the reserve.
  • Shimba Rainforest Lodge: This eco-lodge features sustainable design and is situated on the edge of the Shimba Hills Forest.
  • Shimba Hills Backpackers: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style accommodation and is located near the entrance to the reserve.

What to Buy in Shimba Hills National Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Shimba Hills can take home a variety of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Handmade Crafts: The local communities in and around Shimba Hills produce a wide range of handmade crafts, including baskets, jewelry, and carvings.
  • Spices: The coast of Kenya is renowned for its spices, and visitors can purchase these locally-produced spices to take home.
  • Khanga and Kitenge: These brightly-colored fabrics are popular in East Africa and make for unique and vibrant souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Shimba Hills National Reserve

The local communities in Shimba Hills are diverse and vibrant, with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors to the reserve are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Dressing modestly, especially when visiting cultural attractions such as the Shimba Hills Historical Site and local villages.
  • Greeting local people with a handshake or greeting in Kiswahili.
  • Being respectful of local wildlife and the environment.

Best Time to Visit Shimba Hills National Reserve

The best time to visit Shimba Hills National Reserve is during the dry season, which runs from June to October and from December to March. During this time, the weather is warm and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and wildlife spotting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shimba Hills National Reserve

Do:

  • Observe the wildlife from a safe distance.
  • Follow park rules and regulations.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife.
  • Litter or damage park property.
  • Disturb the peace and tranquility of the reserve.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shimba Hills National Reserve

Shimba Hills National Reserve is generally safe, but visitors are advised to take basic precautions, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Stay on marked trails and avoid walking in isolated areas.
  • Carry a first aid kit and know basic first aid procedures.

Health in Shimba Hills National Reserve

Visitors to Shimba Hills National Reserve should take basic health precautions, such as:

  • Drinking only bottled or purified water.
  • Applying insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing to reduce the risk of insect bites.
  • Seeking medical attention if they develop symptoms of malaria or other tropical diseases.

Getting to Shimba Hills National Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shimba Hills National Reserve

The closest airport to Shimba Hills National Reserve is Moi International Airport in Mombasa, which is approximately 40 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or private car to the reserve.

Bus Stations Near Shimba Hills National Reserve

There are several bus stations in and around Mombasa that offer connections to Shimba Hills National Reserve. These include:

  • Mombasa Bus Station
  • Likoni Ferry Bus Station
  • Digo Bus Park

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Shimba Hills National Reserve from Mombasa or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Shimba Hills National Reserve

The best way to experience the natural beauty of Shimba Hills National Reserve is on foot, via a guided tour, or on a game drive. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the reserve’s trails.

Where to Go Next from Shimba Hills National Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions from Shimba Hills National Reserve include:

  • Mombasa: The vibrant coastal city of Mombasa is only a short drive away and offers a range of attractions, including historic sites, beaches, and cultural experiences.
  • Diani Beach: This popular beach destination is located south of Mombasa and offers a range of water sports and activities.
  • Tsavo East National Park: This large and diverse national park is situated north of Shimba Hills and is renowned for its large elephant herds.

Conclusion

Shimba Hills National Reserve is a unique and biodiverse ecosystem that offers a wide range of natural and cultural attractions. From hiking and biking to wildlife spotting and cultural tours, there is something for every type of traveler at Shimba Hills. With this comprehensive guide, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful destination.
