Introduction to Lamu

Lamu is a peaceful town located on the Indian Ocean in Kenya, East Africa. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its unique coastal culture and rich history. The island is a popular tourist destination due to its pristine beaches, scenic views, and vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Lamu

Lamu has a lot to offer in terms of tourist attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit in Lamu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lamu Museum

The Lamu Museum is located in the town and is housed within a historic building. The museum houses artifacts, exhibits, and artworks that detail the rich history and culture of the Swahili people.

Lamu Fort

Lamu Fort is a historic building that was built in the 19th century by the Sultan of Zanzibar to protect the island from Portuguese and Omani invaders. Today, the fort hosts various exhibitions that showcase the traditional way of life of the locals.

Donkey Sanctuary

Donkey Sanctuary is a non-profit organization that provides shelter, medical care, and rehabilitation to the working donkeys of Lamu. It is located in the village of Shela and is a great attraction to visit if you love animals or want to support a good cause.

Natural Attractions of Lamu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Takwa Ruins

Takwa Ruins is an ancient Swahili settlement that dates back to the 15th century. The ruins are located on Manda Island and are a popular tourist destination for history buffs and those looking to experience the local culture.

Shela Beach

Shela Beach is one of the best beaches in Lamu, featuring soft, white sand and crystal-clear waters. The beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and strolling along the shore.

Pate Island

Pate Island is a lush, green island located just off the coast of Lamu. It is home to stunning coral reefs and abundant sea life, making it a popular spot for diving and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lamu

Lamu Island is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

Manda Island Circuit

This is an ideal biking route for those looking to explore the natural beauty of Lamu Island. The circuit takes you through small villages, scenic beaches, and lush green forests.

Dhow Safari

A dhow safari is a popular activity in Lamu. It involves sailing on traditional wooden boats, known as dhows, along the coastline of the island. This is a unique way to explore the island’s natural beauty while taking in the local culture.

Best Things to Do in Lamu: Activities and Tours

There are numerous activities and tours to enjoy while in Lamu. Here are some of the best:

Swahili Cooking Classes

Swahili cooking classes are a great way to learn about the local cuisine and culture. You’ll learn how to prepare traditional Swahili dishes using locally sourced ingredients.

Snorkeling and Diving

Snorkeling and diving are popular activities in Lamu due to the island’s abundant coral reefs and sea life. You can rent equipment or take a guided tour to explore the underwater attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lamu

Here is an example of a week-long itinerary to explore Lamu:

Day 1 Arrival and relaxation
Day 2 Visit Lamu Museum and explore the Old Town

Food and Drink Scene of Lamu

Must-Try Dishes

Lamu is known for its unique and delicious cuisine, influenced by the Swahili and Arab cultures. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Biriani

This is a spicy rice dish cooked with animal protein, vegetables, and a blend of aromatic spices.

Pilau

Pilau is another rice dish that is cooked with cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves, giving it a fragrant aroma.

Samosas

Samosas are a popular snack in Lamu. They are a pastry filled with either meat or vegetables and are usually served as an appetizer.

Local Beverages

Kaskazi

Kaskazi is a local beer made with a blend of sorghum, maize, and other grains. It is best enjoyed cold and goes well with most meals.

Mbege

Mbege is a traditional drink made from fermented bananas mixed with sugar and spices. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Lamu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lamu Island has a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Kizingo Beach Resort

This is a luxury resort located on a private beach on Lamu Island. It features spacious bungalows, a swimming pool, and a restaurant that serves local and international cuisine.

Lamu House Hotel

Lamu House Hotel is located in the heart of the Old Town and is a restored 18th-century building. It features authentic architecture, modern amenities, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the town.

Kiwandani House

Kiwandani House is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in Shela village. It features clean and comfortable rooms and a communal kitchen where guests can prepare their meals.

What to Buy in Lamu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lamu Island is famous for its handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to buy:

Kikoi

A kikoi is a colorful cloth that is used as a sarong or beach towel. They are available in a variety of colors and designs, making them a great souvenir to bring home.

Handicrafts

Lamu is famous for its intricate handicrafts, including wood carvings, beaded jewelry, and woven baskets. These items make great souvenirs and gifts for loved ones back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lamu

The local culture in Lamu is a unique blend of Swahili and Arab influences. The locals are friendly and welcoming to tourists, but it is important to respect their culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Lamu

The best time to visit Lamu is during the dry season, which runs from November to March. The weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lamu

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or villages.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Haggle when shopping for souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without asking for permission first.
  • Consume drugs or engage in illegal activities.
  • Swim in the ocean during low tide, as strong currents can be dangerous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lamu

Lamu is a safe destination for tourists, but it is important to take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe while in Lamu:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables locked up in a safe place.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and report any suspicious activity to the authorities.

Health in Lamu

Lamu is a low-risk destination for malaria, but it is still important to take preventative measures such as using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for yellow fever and typhoid before traveling to Lamu.

Getting to Lamu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lamu

The nearest airport to Lamu is Manda Airport, which is serviced by small planes that connect to Nairobi, Mombasa, and other major Kenyan cities.

Bus Stations Near Lamu

Lamu is not connected to any major bus stations, but there are buses that run to the nearby town of Mokowe.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Lamu is to take a ferry from Mombasa or Malindi. This is a scenic and enjoyable way to travel, but it takes longer than flying.

How to Get Around in Lamu

The main mode of transportation in Lamu is by foot or by donkey. Cars are not allowed in the Old Town, so be prepared to walk or ride a donkey if you want to explore this area.

Where to Go Next from Lamu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mombasa

Mombasa is a popular beach destination located on the East Coast of Kenya. It is known for its white sand beaches, coral reefs, and vibrant culture.

Malindi

Malindi is a coastal town located north of Lamu. It is also known for its stunning beaches and coral reefs, as well as its historic monuments and landmarks.

Conclusion

Lamu is a unique and beautiful destination that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich culture and history of Kenya’s coastal region. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore ancient ruins, or learn to cook traditional Swahili dishes, Lamu has something for every traveler.
