A Comprehensive Mombasa Travel Guide

Mombasa is a vibrant city located on the coast of Kenya, East Africa. With a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions, and a lively food and drink scene, Mombasa is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Africa.

This guide has been prepared to help you make the most of your visit to Mombasa. We’ve included insider tips and recommendations on the best places to visit, things to do, places to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Mombasa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mombasa has many culturally significant museums and historical sites that are worth visiting. These include:

  • Fort Jesus Museum
  • Mombasa Tusks
  • Haller Park
  • Gedi Ruins

Natural Attractions of Mombasa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Mombasa has plenty of natural attractions to offer. These include:

  • Nyali Beach
  • Mamba Village Centre
  • Shimba Hills National Reserve
  • Mombasa Marine National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mombasa

If you’re looking for a more active way to explore the city, Mombasa has some great hiking and biking routes. These include:

  • Nyali Bridge Cycling Route
  • Mombasa Island Walking Tour
  • Mama Ngina Drive Walkway

Best Things to Do in Mombasa: Activities and Tours

Mombasa has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Some of the best things to do in Mombasa include:

  • Swimming with Dolphins
  • City Sightseeing Tours
  • Cultural Experiences

Week-Long Itinerary for Mombasa

If you’re planning a longer stay in Mombasa, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Fort Jesus Museum, Mombasa Tusks, and Old Town
2 Relax on Nyali Beach and explore Mamba Village Centre
3 Take a walking tour of Mombasa Island and explore Mama Ngina Drive Walkway
4 Visit Shimba Hills National Reserve
5 Go on a city sightseeing tour and visit cultural attractions
6 Swim with dolphins and explore Mombasa Marine National Park
7 Shop for souvenirs and local products, and enjoy the food and drink scene

Food and Drink Scene of Mombasa

Mombasa has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local dishes and beverages to try. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Pilau
  • Biryani
  • Mahamri

Local beverages to try include:

  • Tamarind Juice
  • Mnazi

Where to Stay in Mombasa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mombasa has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Mombasa include:

  • Sarova Whitesands Beach Resort & Spa
  • Baobab Beach Resort & Spa
  • Mombasa Backpackers

What to Buy in Mombasa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Mombasa, there are a few souvenirs and local products you should consider taking home, including:

  • Kanga
  • Kikoi
  • Beaded Jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Mombasa

Mombasa has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home

Best Time to Visit Mombasa

The best time to visit Mombasa is during the dry season, which runs from late December to March and from June to October. These months offer the best weather for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mombasa

Do:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
  • Carry a small amount of cash in local currency for small purchases
  • Negotiate prices when shopping at markets and souvenir shops

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling or treating it first
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach
  • Travel alone at night

Safety and Health

Mombasa is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s important to take a few precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Mombasa

  • Avoid displaying expensive jewelry or electronics in public
  • Do not walk alone at night
  • Obey all local laws and customs

Health in Mombasa

  • Drink bottled or boiled water
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites
  • Be up to date on all vaccinations recommended by your doctor

Getting to Mombasa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mombasa is easily accessible from other parts of Kenya and neighboring countries. Some transportation options include:

Airports Near Mombasa

  • Moi International Airport

Bus Stations Near Mombasa

  • Mombasa Bus Station

Other Options

  • Ferries from Likoni to Mombasa Island

How to Get Around in Mombasa

Mombasa has several transportation options for getting around the city, including:

  • Taxis
  • Tuk-tuks
  • Matatus (shared minivans)

Where to Go Next from Mombasa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more things to do after visiting Mombasa, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Nairobi National Park
  • Maasai Mara National Reserve
  • Tsavo National Parks

Conclusion

Mombasa is a city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. We hope this guide has inspired you to visit Mombasa and make the most of your stay. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical sites, enjoying outdoor adventures, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, Mombasa has something to offer everyone.
