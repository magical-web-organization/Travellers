Introduction to Amboseli National Park

Located in Southern Rift Valley, Kenya, Amboseli National Park is one of the most popular destinations for wildlife safaris in Africa. This 392 km² (151 mi²) national park is renowned for its breathtaking panoramic views of Mount Kilimanjaro, the highest peak in Africa, as well as its diverse wildlife, including elephants, lions, cheetahs, leopards, buffaloes, and over 400 bird species. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay, eat, and shop in Amboseli National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Amboseli National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amboseli National Park is not just a wildlife safari destination, but also a place of rich culture and significant history. One of the best places to learn about the local Maasai tribe and their traditions is the Maasai Cultural Village, located inside the park. Here, visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the village and learn about the Maasai way of life, including their clothing, food, and dances.

Another prominent cultural attraction in Amboseli National Park is the Kimana Community Wildlife Sanctuary, where visitors can learn about the conservation efforts of the local Maasai community and their coexistence with the wildlife. The sanctuary offers guided tours, bird watching, and cultural performances.

Natural Attractions of Amboseli National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the wildlife, Amboseli National Park boasts some of the most breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro and the surrounding landscapes. One of the best places to catch these views is the Observation Hill, located in the center of the park. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill and enjoy a panoramic view of the park and the majestic mountain.

For a more adventurous experience, visitors can take a guided nature walk or bike ride along the designated routes within the park. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include the Elerai Conservation Walk, the Maasai Nature Trail, and the Ol Tukai Nature Trail.

Other natural attractions worth visiting in Amboseli National Park include the swampy area known as Ol Doinyo Orok, where visitors can witness hippos, crocodiles, and elephants up close. Visitors can also enjoy a scenic drive through the park, taking in the views of the savannah grasslands and the acacia forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amboseli National Park

In Amboseli National Park, visitors can take guided nature walks and bike rides along designated routes within the park. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Elerai Conservation Walk: This 3 km (1.8 mi) walk takes visitors through the Elerai Conservation Area, where visitors can witness the diverse wildlife and landscapes of Amboseli National Park.

  • Maasai Nature Trail: This 2 km (1.2 mi) walk takes visitors through the Maasai homesteads, where they can learn about the local culture and traditions.

  • Ol Tukai Nature Trail: This 4 km (2.4 mi) walk takes visitors through the grasslands, where they can enjoy the scenic views of Mount Kilimanjaro and the wildlife.

For biking enthusiasts, there are several routes to choose from, including the Kimana Forest and the Kitirua Wildlife Sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Amboseli National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from the wildlife safaris and nature walks, Amboseli National Park offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Amboseli National Park include:

  • Hot air balloon safari: A unique and unforgettable experience, the hot air balloon safari takes visitors high above the park, providing breathtaking views of the wildlife and landscapes.

  • Night game drive: A thrilling and adventurous experience, the night game drive allows visitors to witness the nocturnal wildlife and their behaviors.

  • Bird watching: With over 400 bird species in Amboseli National Park, bird watching is a must-do activity for ornithologists and nature enthusiasts.

  • Photographic safari: Amboseli National Park is a photographer’s paradise, with stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro. Visitors can choose from a range of photographic safari packages to capture the best moments of their safari.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amboseli National Park

For visitors planning a week-long trip to Amboseli National Park, here is a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Amboseli National Park, check-in at the lodge, and enjoy an afternoon game drive.

Day 2: Early morning game drive, followed by a guided nature walk or bike ride in the afternoon.

Day 3: Hot air balloon safari in the morning, followed by a picnic lunch in the park, and a night game drive in the evening.

Day 4: Cultural tour of the Maasai village and a visit to the Kimana Community Wildlife Sanctuary in the afternoon.

Day 5: Full-day game drive, exploring various areas of the park and sighting the wildlife.

Day 6: Photographic safari in the morning, followed by a relaxing afternoon at the lodge.

Day 7: Depart Amboseli National Park for the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Amboseli National Park

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Amboseli National Park is a unique blend of traditional Kenyan and Maasai cuisines, with a touch of international flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Nyama Choma: Grilled meat, usually beef or goat, is a Kenyan specialty and a staple in most lodges and restaurants in Amboseli National Park.

  • Ugali: This is a traditional Kenyan dish made of maize flour and water. It is often served with sukuma wiki (collard greens) and other vegetables, and sometimes meat or fish.

  • Mokimo: A traditional Kikuyu dish, Mokimo is made of mashed potatoes, beans, and maize, and is usually served with beef or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Apart from the food, visitors can also sample the local beverages, including:

  • Tusker Lager: This is a popular Kenyan beer, widely available in most lodges and restaurants in Amboseli National Park.

  • Dawa: A traditional Kenyan cocktail, made of vodka, honey, and lime, is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Amboseli National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amboseli National Park has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Visitors can choose from luxury lodges, tented camps, hostels, and guesthouses, including:

  • Amboseli Serena Safari Lodge: This luxurious lodge offers stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro and the surrounding grasslands, as well as world-class services and amenities.

  • Ol Tukai Lodge: Nestled in the heart of Amboseli National Park, Ol Tukai Lodge offers comfortable and spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and stunning views of the park and the mountain.

  • Campi ya Kanzi: This is a luxury tented camp, located on a private game reserve adjacent to the park. Campi ya Kanzi offers an authentic Maasai experience, with cultural activities, wildlife safaris, and stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro.

  • Kimana Amboseli Tented Camp: This budget-friendly tented camp offers comfortable tents, a restaurant, and a safari desk, and is located outside the park.

What to Buy in Amboseli National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home some unique and authentic souvenirs and local products from Amboseli National Park, including:

  • Beaded jewelry: Maasai women are known for their intricate beadwork, and visitors can purchase beaded jewelry, such as necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.

  • African art and crafts: There are several shops and stalls in and around the park selling African art and crafts, including wooden and stone carvings, masks, and baskets.

  • Local spices and tea: Visitors can also purchase local spices and tea, such as Kenyan coffee, chai tea, and lemongrass tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Amboseli National Park

The Maasai tribe is an integral part of the culture and customs of Amboseli National Park. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting the Maasai village and other cultural attractions. Visitors should also dress modestly and avoid taking photos of the Maasai without their consent.

Best Time to Visit Amboseli National Park

The best time to visit Amboseli National Park is during the dry season, which runs from June to October and from December to February. During this time, the wildlife is more concentrated around water sources, making it easier to spot them. The weather is also dry and mild, with low humidity and clear skies, providing ideal conditions for wildlife safaris and outdoor activities. However, visitors should expect high tourist traffic during this peak season and should book their accommodation and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amboseli National Park

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and their habitats by not disturbing them or their environment.
  • Follow the park rules and regulations, including the speed limit and no off-road driving.
  • Hire a professional guide or join a guided tour for a safe and informed safari experience.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife or try to approach them too closely.
  • Litter or pollute the park environment.
  • Ignore the advice or instructions of the park rangers or guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amboseli National Park

While Amboseli National Park is considered a safe destination, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone or walking alone at night.
  • Follow the park rules and regulations and stay within designated areas.
  • Be aware of the wildlife and their behaviors and keep a safe distance.

Health in Amboseli National Park

Visitors to Amboseli National Park should take necessary health precautions, such as:

  • Consult a travel doctor before traveling to Amboseli National Park and get updated on the required vaccines and medications.
  • Use insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid bites from mosquitoes and other insects.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid water-borne diseases.

Getting to Amboseli National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Amboseli National Park by several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Amboseli National Park

The closest airport to Amboseli National Park is Amboseli Airport, which receives scheduled and charter flights from Nairobi and other destinations in Kenya.

Bus Stations Near Amboseli National Park

Visitors can also take a bus or shuttle from Nairobi to Amboseli National Park, with several operators offering daily services.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can hire a taxi or a rental car and drive to Amboseli National Park, with the journey taking about four hours from Nairobi.

How to Get Around in Amboseli National Park

The easiest and safest way to get around in Amboseli National Park is by hiring a professional guide or joining a guided tour. Most lodges and camps in and around the park offer guided safaris, nature walks, and outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Amboseli National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After an amazing safari experience in Amboseli National Park, visitors can explore other destinations and excursions nearby, including:

  • Tsavo National Park: One of the largest national parks in Kenya, Tsavo National Park offers world-class wildlife safaris, stunning landscapes, and historic landmarks, such as the Tsavo Bridge.

  • Mombasa: A coastal city located on the Indian Ocean, Mombasa offers a range of cultural and historical attractions, as well as stunning beaches and water sports.

Conclusion

Amboseli National Park is a unique and unforgettable destination that offers some of the best wildlife safaris, cultural tours, outdoor adventures, and scenic views in Africa. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their safari experience, explore the best places to visit and things to do, and learn about the local culture and customs of Amboseli National Park.
