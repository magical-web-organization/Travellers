Introduction to Naivasha

Located in Southern Rift Valley, Naivasha is a picturesque town in Kenya that offers visitors an opportunity to explore diverse natural and cultural attractions. From outdoor adventures and scenic views to historical museums and local cuisine, Naivasha has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide will take you on a tour of the best places to visit in Naivasha and help you plan a memorable vacation.

Local Culture and Customs of Naivasha

Naivasha is home to many Maasai communities, and visitors can immerse themselves in local cultures by participating in cultural experiences like a traditional Maasai dance.

Best Time to Visit Naivasha

The best time to visit Naivasha is from June to September when the weather is dry and pleasant for outdoor activities like hiking and boating. However, temperatures can be hot during the day.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naivasha

Do:

  • Respect local cultures and traditions
  • Take precautions against mosquitoes and other insects
  • Carry sunscreen and a hat for protection against the sun
  • Research visa and vaccination requirements before travelling to Kenya

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Swim in Lake Naivasha as it is infested with hippos and crocodiles
  • Litter or engage in any behaviour that would be harmful to the environment or wildlife
  • Ignore instructions given by local guides during hikes or wildlife tours

Safety and Health

Safety in Naivasha

While Naivasha is generally a safe place to travel, visitors should take precautions against pickpocketing and theft. It is also advisable to use licensed taxis or private transportation services for travel within the area.

Health in Naivasha

Visitors to Naivasha should take precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne illnesses. It is also advisable to drink bottled water and seek medical attention in case of any health concerns.

How to Get Around in Naivasha

The best way to get around in Naivasha is by private transportation services, including taxis and rental cars. Visitors can also use tour operators and guides for outdoor activities like hiking and boating.
