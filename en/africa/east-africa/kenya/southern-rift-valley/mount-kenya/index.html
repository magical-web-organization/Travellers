Introduction to Mount Kenya

Mount Kenya is Africa’s second-highest peak, and the highest mountain in Kenya, located in the southern Rift Valley. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular destination for hikers, climbers, and adventurers. Mount Kenya is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, and visitors can experience the local culture and customs of the surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Kenya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mount Kenya National Park Museum - visitors can learn about the history and culture of the area, including traditional methods of farming, hunting, and building.
  2. Mau Mau Caves - these were used by the Mau Mau during Kenya’s struggle for independence and are a symbol of the country’s fight for freedom.
  3. Chogoria Town - this is a bustling market town where visitors can experience the local culture and enjoy local cuisine.

Natural Attractions of Mount Kenya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Chogoria Forest - this is a pristine rainforest and home to rare species of birds and animals.
  2. Mount Kenya National Park - the park contains the mountain’s peaks and stunning scenery.
  3. Lake Ellis - located on the slopes of Mount Kenya, visitors can enjoy hiking and picnicking at this beautiful lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Kenya

  1. Chogoria Route - the most scenic route to the summit, taking in incredible views and varied terrain.
  2. Sirimon Route - the most popular route, taking in stunning waterfalls and breathtaking views.
  3. Naro Moru Route - a challenging route but with incredible views of the mountain and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Mount Kenya: Activities and Tours

  1. Climbing Mount Kenya - a challenging but rewarding experience for experienced mountaineers.
  2. Game drives - visitors can take guided tours of the Mount Kenya National Park to see the diverse range of flora and fauna.
  3. Cultural experiences - visitors can experience the local culture and customs, including traditional dances and cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Kenya

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Mount Kenya and settle into accommodation
2 Hike the Sirimon Route to Old Moses Camp
3 Hike to Shipton’s Camp
4 Acclimatization day at Shipton’s Camp
5 Summit day - early morning start to reach Point Lenana
6 Descend to Chogoria Gate, then drive to Chogoria Town
7 Depart Mount Kenya

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Kenya

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nyama Choma - grilled meat served with ugali (maize meal) and kachumbari (fresh salsa).
  2. Pilau Rice - spiced rice with beef, chicken or lamb.
  3. Irio - mashed potatoes, green peas, and corn.

Local Beverages

  1. Dawa - a cocktail made with honey, lime, vodka and sugar.
  2. Tusker Beer - the most popular beer in Kenya.
  3. Chai Tea - a hot, spiced tea made with black tea, milk, cinnamon, and ginger.

Where to Stay in Mount Kenya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Serena Mountain Lodge - a luxury lodge nestled in the forest with fantastic views of Mount Kenya.
  2. Castle Forest Lodge - a charming guesthouse with beautiful gardens and easy access to hiking trails.
  3. Mount Kenya Safari Club - a luxury resort with fantastic views of the mountain and a range of activities and amenities.

What to Buy in Mount Kenya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hand-woven baskets - made from local materials, these make a great souvenir or gift.
  2. Hand-carved wooden figurines - intricately crafted by local artisans.
  3. Coffee and tea - Kenya is known for producing some of the best coffee and tea in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Kenya

Visitors to Mount Kenya can experience the local culture and customs of the surrounding areas. The Kikuyu people, who live around the mountain, have a rich culture and tradition, including colorful dances, music, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Mount Kenya

The best time to visit Mount Kenya is from January to February and from August to September. These months provide clear weather, great visibility, and excellent climbing conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Kenya

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately for the climate and terrain.
  2. Respect local customs and traditions.
  3. Hire a reputable guide when hiking or climbing the mountain.

Don’t:

  1. Leave rubbish on the mountain or in the park.
  2. Take flora and fauna from the national park.
  3. Ignore the advice of park rangers or mountain guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Kenya

Visitors to Mount Kenya should take necessary precautions to stay safe, including staying with a guide when hiking and climbing the mountain, knowing the weather conditions, and dressing appropriately for the climate and terrain.

Health in Mount Kenya

Visitors should take precautions against altitude sickness when climbing the mountain. Drinking plenty of water, taking time to acclimatize, and knowing the symptoms of altitude sickness are all essential for staying healthy on the mountain.

Getting to Mount Kenya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Kenya

  1. Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in Nairobi is the nearest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Mount Kenya

  1. Chogoria Bus Station - buses from Nairobi to Chogoria run regularly.

Other Options

  1. Private transfers - many companies offer private transfers from Nairobi to Mount Kenya.

How to Get Around in Mount Kenya

Visitors to Mount Kenya can get around by hiring a car, using public transportation, or hiring a guide or tour company for organized activities and transportation.

Where to Go Next from Mount Kenya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Nairobi - Kenya’s capital city, with its museums, shops, and restaurants.
  2. Samburu National Reserve - a stunning wildlife reserve with rare species of birds and animals.
  3. Lake Nakuru National Park - home to flamingos, rhinos, and other African animals.

Conclusion

Visiting Mount Kenya is a once-in-a-lifetime experience, with its stunning natural scenery, diverse range of flora and fauna, and rich cultural traditions. This comprehensive tour guide provides visitors with all the information they need to plan a fantastic trip, from hiking trails and outdoor activities to accommodations and local customs.
