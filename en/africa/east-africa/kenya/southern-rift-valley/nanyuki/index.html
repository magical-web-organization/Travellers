Nestled in Southern Rift Valley in Kenya, Nanyuki is a hidden gem for travelers seeking adventure and unique experiences. This small town offers visitors a wide range of attractions, from cultural and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and stunning natural views. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your trip to Nanyuki, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Nanyuki

Nanyuki is a bustling market town located at the foot of Mount Kenya, the second highest mountain in Africa. The town is home to a diverse mix of cultures, with the majority of its inhabitants made up of Kenyans, Maasai, and Somali ethnic groups. Visitors to Nanyuki will be enchanted by the town’s warm climate, friendly locals, and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Nanyuki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nanyuki is steeped in history and culture, with several museums and historical landmarks to explore. These include:

  • Mount Kenya Wildlife Conservancy
  • Ol Pejeta Conservancy
  • Nanyuki Spinners and Weavers
  • Lord Baden-Powell Monument and Grave
  • Nanyuki Railway Station

Natural Attractions of Nanyuki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to do in Nanyuki, with a range of activities, from hiking and biking to wildlife spotting and scenic drives. Our top picks include:

  • Mount Kenya National Park
  • Aberdare National Park
  • Ngare Ndare Forest Reserve
  • Ol Jogi Wildlife Conservancy
  • Thompson’s Falls

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanyuki

Nanyuki is a gateway to hiking and biking routes for all levels of physical ability. These trails are well-marked and afford hikers and bikers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Some popular routes are:

  • The Nanyuki-Old Moses trail
  • The Chogoria-Naro Moru trail
  • The Burguret-Chogoria trail
  • The Sirimon-Chogoria trail

Best Things to Do in Nanyuki: Activities and Tours

In Nanyuki, there’s no shortage of exciting activities to enjoy. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit a Maasai village
  • Go horseback riding at Borana Ranch
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the savannah
  • Explore Mount Kenya on a four-wheel-drive safari
  • Take a guided tour of the Ol Pejeta Conservancy

Week-Long Itinerary for Nanyuki

To make the most of your time in Nanyuki, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that includes the best attractions and activities.

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive and check-in at a hotel or guesthouse. Take a guided tour of the Ol Pejeta Conservancy.
Day 2 Hike the Nanyuki-Old Moses trail.
Day 3 Visit the Mount Kenya Wildlife Conservancy and go horseback riding at Borana Ranch.
Day 4 Explore the Aberdare National Park and Ngare Ndare Forest Reserve.
Day 5 Take a hot air balloon ride over the savannah and visit a Maasai village.
Day 6 Drive to Thompson’s Falls and enjoy a scenic picnic.
Day 7 Depart Nanyuki.

Food and Drink Scene of Nanyuki

Nanyuki boasts a diverse food scene, with local restaurants serving up traditional Kenyan fare and international cuisine. For a truly authentic experience, be sure to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nyama Choma (barbecued meat)
  • Ugali (maize meal)
  • Githeri (a Kenyan stew made with beans and maize)

Local Beverages

  • Tusker Lager (popular Kenyan beer)
  • Dawa (a local cocktail made with vodka, honey, and lime)

Where to Stay in Nanyuki: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nanyuki accommodations range from swanky hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Fairmont Mount Kenya Safari Club
  • Sangare Tented Camp
  • The White Rhino Hotel
  • Sweetwaters Serena Camp

What to Buy in Nanyuki: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nanyuki is famous for its beautiful handicrafts and handmade goods. Some of the must-buy souvenirs include:

  • Maasai blankets and jewelry
  • Beaded leather sandals and belts
  • Handmade candles and soap
  • Kenyan coffee and tea sets

Local Culture and Customs of Nanyuki

Nanyuki locals are known for their warm hospitality and love of community. Visitors should keep in mind the following cultural customs while in Nanyuki:

  • It is considered polite to greet locals with a handshake and a smile.
  • Public displays of affection are considered inappropriate.
  • Dress modestly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Nanyuki

The best time to visit Nanyuki is during the dry season between January and February or from June to October. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and wildlife sightings are abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nanyuki

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Learn a few Swahili phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Dress modestly in public places.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection
  • Disrespect local traditions or values
  • Wander off into unprotected wilderness areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Nanyuki

Overall, Nanyuki is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should keep in mind these safety tips while exploring the town and its surroundings:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.
  • Keep track of your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Hire a reputable tour guide for outdoor activities.

Health in Nanyuki

Travelers to Nanyuki should take the necessary precautions to avoid falling ill. Here are some helpful tips:

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Observe food hygiene rules and eat only well-cooked foods.

Getting to Nanyuki: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nanyuki

The closest airport to Nanyuki is the Nanyuki Airport, which caters to domestic and regional flights. Visitors can also opt to fly into Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in Nairobi and then take a domestic flight to Nanyuki.

Bus Stations Near Nanyuki

Several bus companies offer services to and from Nanyuki, with most routes passing through Nairobi. The most popular bus lines in the area include Modern Coast, Riverside Shuttle, and Easy Coach.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car and driver or take a taxi to Nanyuki from Nairobi or other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Nanyuki

Nanyuki is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also opt to hire a taxi or private car to get around or rent a bicycle to explore the scenic countryside.

Where to Go Next from Nanyuki: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nanyuki is centrally located and offers visitors easy access to some of Kenya’s most stunning destinations, including:

  • Samburu National Reserve
  • Marsabit National Park
  • Laikipia Plateau

Conclusion

Nanyuki is a hidden gem in Kenya’s Southern Rift Valley, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, and culture. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to plan your trip to Nanyuki to explore all that this charming town has to offer.
