Introduction to Tsavo West National Park

Tsavo West National Park is one of Kenya’s oldest and largest national parks, covering an area of 9,065 square kilometers. It is located in the Southern Rift Valley, in the Coast Province of Kenya and borders the Chyulu Hills National Park, Mkomazi Game Reserve in Tanzania, and Tsavo East National Park to the east. The park’s diverse habitats range from savannah grasslands, rocky hills, and acacia woodlands to the rugged terrain of volcanic cones, lava flows, and springs, making it home to a vast array of wildlife.

To make the most of your trip to Tsavo West National Park, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Mzima Springs and Shetani Lava Flows Day 2: Explore the Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary and the Tsavo River. Day 3: Go on an early morning game drive and visit Lake Jipe. Food and Drink Scene of Tsavo West National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Tsavo West National Park offers a range of traditional Kenyan dishes that are a must-try for every visitor, including:

  • Ugali (a maize-based meal)
  • Nyama Choma (roasted meat)
  • Sukuma Wiki (collard greens stew)
  • Smoked Fish

Local Beverages

To quench your thirst, try out these local Kenyan beverages:

  • Tusker (local beer)
  • Stoney Tangawizi (ginger beer)
  • Fresh fruit juices

Local Culture and Customs of Tsavo West National Park

The local communities of the Tsavo West National Park have a rich culture and traditions that can be experienced through visiting the park. Visitors should respect local culture and customs and take care of the environment during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Tsavo West National Park

The best time to visit Tsavo West National Park is during the dry season, which runs from June to October and from January to February. At this time, the park has better wildlife viewing possibilities due to the animals congregating around water sources, and the weather is usually pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsavo West National Park

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Follow the park rules and regulations.
  • Ensure your safety at all times.

Don’t:

  • Feed wildlife
  • Get too close to wildlife
  • Litter in the park

Safety and Health

Visitors to Tsavo West National Park should take the necessary safety precautions while on safari. Here are some tips:

Safety in Tsavo West National Park

  • Always stay inside your vehicle or within the designated area while on game drives.
  • Keep all doors locked and windows shut while in the park.
  • Don’t leave food or valuables in your tent or car.

Health in Tsavo West National Park

  • Visitors should take anti-malaria medication before entering the park.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid contamination.
  • Apply insect repellent regularly to prevent insect bites and diseases.
