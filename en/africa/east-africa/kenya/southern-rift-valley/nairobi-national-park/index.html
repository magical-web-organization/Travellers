Your Comprehensive Tour Guide to Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. The only protected area in the world that is situated within a capital city, Nairobi National Park is a short drive from the bustling central business district of Nairobi. Covering an area of 117 square kilometers, this park provides a unique opportunity to witness the Big Five (lions, elephants, buffalos, rhinos, and leopards) in their natural habitat, along with hundreds of bird species and diverse flora. In this tour guide, we will cover all the essential information that you need to know before visiting Nairobi National Park.

Introduction to Nairobi National Park

Established in 1946, Nairobi National Park is Kenya’s first game reserve, stretching across a variety of landscapes such as forests, grassy plains, and rocky valleys. It is one of the most accessible national parks in Africa and is a favored spot for a day trip for residents of Nairobi and tourists alike. With state-of-the-art facilities and diverse flora and fauna, Nairobi National Park has become a must-visit destination for travelers visiting Kenya.

List of Places to Visit in Nairobi National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

In addition to its natural beauty, Nairobi National Park also boasts several cultural sites of interest.

  • Nairobi Mamba Village: A crocodile farm in the Ongata Rongai suburb of Nairobi.

  • David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust: This elephant orphanage rescues and rehabilitates orphaned elephants, provides medical care, and releases them back into the wild when they are ready.

  • Karen Blixen Museum: The farmhouse of the Danish author Karen Blixen.

Natural Attractions of Nairobi National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nairobi National Park is a natural wonder with diverse landscapes and breathtaking views.

  • Safari Walk: A raised boardwalk that offers an opportunity to view wildlife up close.

  • Ivory Burning Site: Located near the main gate, this site marks the burning of Kenya’s ivory stockpiles by the late president Daniel arap Moi in 1989 as a statement against illegal poaching.

  • Hippo Pool: A natural pool where hippos gather in the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park boasts a range of hiking and biking routes, suitable for all levels of fitness:

  • Unguja Lodge: A popular biking trail that offers spectacular views of the park’s diverse wildlife.

  • KWS HQ circuit: A scenic hike upfront from the KWS headquarters that takes you deep into the park.

Best Things to Do in Nairobi National Park: Activities and Tours

Nairobi National Park has something for every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do and tours to take:

  • Game drives: The most popular activity in the park is a game drive, which involves a designated driver and a tour guide to explore the park’s wildlife.

  • Bird watching: Nairobi National Park has over 400 bird species that can be spotted in and around the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Nairobi National Park

Must-try Dishes

Kenya’s food scene is a fusion of cultures, and Nairobi National Park’s cuisine is no different. Don’t miss these must-try dishes:

  • Ugali: A traditional maize-based dish that is typically served with a stew.

  • Pilau: This spicy rice dish is flavored with a combination of spices, including cinnamon, cumin, and turmeric.

Local Beverages

  • Tusker Lager: Kenya’s most popular beer, this crisp lager is widely available throughout the country.

  • Chai/Masala tea: A Kenyan classic, chai tea is a blend of black tea, milk, and spices.

Where to Stay in Nairobi National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nairobi National Park has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget, including:

  • Nairobi Tented Camp: A luxury tented camp situated in the heart of Nairobi National Park.

  • Ibis Styles: A budget-friendly hotel that caters to all your basic needs.

What to Buy in Nairobi National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maasai Shuka: A vibrantly colored shawl made from wool that is worn by the Maasai people

  • Sisal baskets: Handwoven sisal baskets are a popular souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Nairobi National Park

When visiting Nairobi National Park, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs:

  • Dress modestly: Ensure that you dress modestly and avoid wearing revealing clothing.

  • Avoid public displays of affection: Public displays of affection are not common in Kenya, so it’s best to avoid them.

Best Time to Visit Nairobi National Park

The best time to visit Nairobi National Park is during the dry season, which runs from December to March and from June to October. The animals are more visible during this time as they congregate around waterholes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nairobi National Park

Do:

  • Follow your guide’s instructions: When on a game drive, listen to your guide’s instructions and follow the park rules to ensure your safety.

Don’t:

  • Feed the animals: Feeding the animals is strictly prohibited and can cause harm to both humans and animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park is a safe destination for visitors, but it’s essential to take precautions:

  • Stay in designated areas: Never venture out of designated areas as it can be dangerous and prohibited.

Health in Nairobi National Park

Travelers should take the following health precautions while visiting the park:

  • Malaria: Nairobi National Park is located in a malaria-prone area, so it’s important to take precautionary measures, including malaria medication.

Getting to Nairobi National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nairobi National Park

  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport: The nearest international airport to Nairobi National Park is located 15 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Nairobi National Park

  • Machakos Country Bus Stop: This bus stop is located near the park gate and is a convenient option for travelers.

Other Options

  • Private transfers: Private transfers are available from Nairobi and its suburbs.

How to Get Around in Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park is best explored in a car, either on a game drive or with a private driver. Alternatively, visitors can opt to rent a bike and explore the park’s biking trails.

Where to Go Next from Nairobi National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nairobi National Park is an excellent jumping-off point to explore Kenya’s wildlife and culture. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Amboseli National Park: A four-hour drive from Nairobi National Park, Amboseli National Park is known for its elephant herds and views of Mount Kilimanjaro.

  • Maasai Mara National Reserve: A six-hour drive from Nairobi National Park, the Maasai Mara National Reserve is a vast natural wonder with diverse wildlife and a rich Maasai culture.

Conclusion

Nairobi National Park offers a unique combination of wildlife and urban lifestyle, providing travelers with an unforgettable experience. From diverse wildlife, rich cultural sites, hiking trails, biking routes, and amazing cuisine, Nairobi National Park has something for every type of traveler. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your visit and make the most of your experience in Nairobi National Park.
