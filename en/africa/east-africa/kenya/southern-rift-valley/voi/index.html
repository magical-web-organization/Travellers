Introduction to Voi

Voi is a small town in the Southern Rift Valley of Kenya, situated between Mombasa and Nairobi. Known as the gateway to Tsavo East National Park, Voi is a popular stopover for wildlife enthusiasts and adventurers. The town is surrounded by savannah grasslands and rolling hills, and the nearby Taita hills offer stunning panoramic views. Here’s everything you need to know when planning your trip to Voi!

List of Places to Visit in Voi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several places to visit in Voi that offer insight into the local culture and history:

  1. Taita Hills Wildlife Sanctuary Museum: Learn about the wildlife and conservation efforts in the Taita Hills.

  2. Taita Hills Historical Village: Explore the traditional village and see how locals have lived for centuries.

  3. Mzima Springs: A popular tourist attraction in Tsavo West National Park, it is known for its crystal clear waters and the hippos and crocodiles that call it home.

Natural Attractions of Voi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Voi is surrounded by natural attractions that offer visitors the opportunity to explore the outdoors:

  1. Tsavo East National Park: Covering an area of more than 13,500 sq km, Tsavo East is Kenya’s largest national park. Visitors can expect to see wildlife such as elephants, lions, giraffes, and zebras on game drives.

  2. Lugard Falls: Located inside Tsavo East National Park, Lugard Falls is a series of rapids and waterfalls on the Galana River.

  3. Taita Hills: With an altitude of over 2,200 meters, the Taita Hills offer panoramic views of the surrounding area. Hikers can take one of several trails to the top and enjoy an unforgettable sunrise or sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Voi

For those looking for an outdoor adventure, Voi offers several hiking and biking routes, including:

  1. Kamungi Hills Hiking Trail: A 5km trail that takes visitors through the Kamungi Hills and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Bura Mission Hiking Trail: A 10km trail that takes visitors through the Bura Mission Station and offers a glimpse of local life in rural Kenya.

  3. Vuria Peak Hiking Trail: A 6km trail that leads to the summit of Vuria Peak, the highest peak in the Taita Hills.

Best Things to Do in Voi: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Voi offers a variety of activities and tours:

  1. Game drives: Visitors to Tsavo East National Park can take a game drive to see wildlife such as elephants, lions, and giraffes.

  2. Bird watching: With more than 500 species of birds, Voi is a paradise for bird watchers.

  3. Cultural tours: Visitors can learn about the local culture and customs by taking a guided tour of a traditional village or interacting with local people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Voi

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Voi, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Voi and check into your hotel. Visit the Taita Hills Historical Village to learn about the local culture and history.

Day 2: Take a morning game drive in Tsavo East National Park. In the afternoon, visit the Mzima Springs to see hippos and crocodiles.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Vuria Peak for stunning views of the surrounding area.

Day 4: Take a cultural tour and learn about the traditions and customs of the local people.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of relaxation at your hotel or explore the town of Voi.

Day 6: Take a biking tour through the Kamungi Hills.

Day 7: Depart Voi and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Voi

Must-Try Dishes

Voi is known for its traditional Kenyan cuisine, and there are several must-try dishes:

  1. Ugali: A staple food made from maize flour, it is often served with a meat or vegetable stew.

  2. Nyama Choma: Grilled meat, usually beef or goat, served with Ugali.

  3. Mbaazi: A dish made from pigeon peas cooked in coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  1. Chai: Kenyan tea made with milk and spices.

  2. Tusker: A popular Kenyan beer.

  3. Dawa: A traditional cocktail made from vodka, honey, and lime juice.

Where to Stay in Voi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Voi offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets, including:

  1. Voi Wildlife Lodge: A luxury lodge located in Tsavo East National Park, offering views of the nearby watering hole.

  2. Voi Safari Lodge: Another luxury lodge located inside the park, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Sarova Taita Hills Game Lodge: A mid-range lodge located in the Taita Hills, offering game drives and cultural tours.

What to Buy in Voi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, here are some local products to consider:

  1. Maasai Shuka: A traditional fabric worn by the Maasai people.

  2. Beaded jewelry: Handmade jewelry made from beads by local artisans.

  3. Kikoys: Colorful cotton wraps that can be used as beach towels or scarves.

Local Culture and Customs of Voi

The people of Voi are a mix of different ethnic groups, including Taita, Kamba, and Maasai. Respect for elders is an important part of the local culture, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and ask permission before taking photographs of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Voi

The best time to visit Voi is during the dry season, from January to March and July to October, when game viewing is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Voi

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Dress modestly, especially when visiting rural areas.
  3. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  4. Take precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Don’t:

  1. Touch or disturb wildlife.
  2. Take photographs without permission from locals.
  3. Engage in illegal activities, such as poaching or drug use.
  4. Leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Voi

Voi is considered a safe place for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions:

  1. Don’t walk alone at night.
  2. Keep valuables out of sight when walking in public areas.
  3. Use licensed taxis, especially at night.

Health in Voi

Visitors to Voi should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and be up to date on vaccinations for yellow fever, typhoid, and hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Voi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Voi

  1. Moi International Airport: Located in Mombasa, approximately 150 km from Voi.

  2. Jomo Kenyatta International Airport: Located in Nairobi, approximately 350 km from Voi.

Bus Stations Near Voi

  1. Makueni Bus Station: Located in the nearby town of Makueni, approximately 50 km from Voi.

  2. Mombasa Bus Station: Located in Mombasa, approximately 150 km from Voi.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Nairobi or Mombasa to Voi.

How to Get Around in Voi

The best way to get around Voi is by car or taxi. Visitors can also take a matatu, a public minibus, but these can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Voi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels after Voi, here are some popular nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Mombasa: A coastal city known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.

  2. Nairobi: Kenya’s capital city and a gateway to many other national parks and game reserves.

  3. Amboseli National Park: Known for its large herds of elephants and views of Mount Kilimanjaro.

Conclusion

Voi is a hidden gem in Kenya, offering visitors the opportunity to experience the natural beauty, culture, and history of the region. With its variety of attractions, activities, and cultural experiences, Voi is the perfect destination for any traveler. Plan your trip to Voi today and experience the best of Kenya’s Southern Rift Valley!
