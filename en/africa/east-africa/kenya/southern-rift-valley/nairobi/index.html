Introduction to Nairobi

Nairobi, the capital city of Kenya, is a bustling metropolis located in the Southern Rift Valley in East Africa. Known as the “Green City in the Sun,” Nairobi boasts a unique blend of urban and natural attractions. From world-famous national parks and museums to vibrant nightlife and cultural experiences, Nairobi has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Nairobi

A trip to Nairobi wouldn’t be complete without visiting some of the city’s most famous attractions. Here are our top recommendations for places to visit in Nairobi.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Kenya: The National Museum of Kenya is a must-visit for anyone interested in Kenyan history and culture. The museum houses a diverse collection of artifacts, from prehistoric tools to ethnographic exhibits that showcase Kenya’s cultural diversity.
  • Karen Blixen Museum: The former home of the Danish author Karen Blixen, the museum offers visitors an intimate look into the life of one of Kenya’s most famous residents.
  • Nairobi Railway Museum: The Nairobi Railway Museum is an excellent place to learn about Kenya’s railway history. The museum has a collection of steam locomotives and railway artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Nairobi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nairobi National Park: The Nairobi National Park, located just outside the city, is home to an abundance of African wildlife, including lions, giraffes, zebras, and more.
  • Nairobi Arboretum: The Nairobi Arboretum is an urban oasis situated in the heart of the city. It features over 350 species of trees and is a great place for a picnic or a leisurely walk.
  • Ngong Hills: The Ngong Hills, located just outside Nairobi, offer stunning views of the city skyline and the Great Rift Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nairobi

Nairobi also offers an array of hiking and biking options for those looking to explore the city’s natural beauty on foot or on two wheels. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Karura Forest: The Karura Forest is a popular spot for hiking and biking, with over 50 kilometers of trails that wind through the forest’s scenic landscapes.
  • Ololua Forest: The Ololua Forest is a peaceful escape from the city, with several hiking trails that lead to waterfalls and breathtaking views of the city and surrounding areas.
  • Ngong Forest: The Ngong Forest has a network of trails that are ideal for walking, jogging, or mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Nairobi: Activities and Tours

Nairobi has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the beauty of Nairobi from above with a hot air balloon ride over the city and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Nairobi River Tour: Learn about the history and culture of Nairobi on a guided boat tour along the Nairobi River.
  • Nairobi Food Tour: Nairobi is known for its delicious cuisine, and a food tour is a great way to experience the city’s best dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Nairobi

Nairobi has a thriving food and drink scene, with mouth-watering local dishes and unique beverages that are a must-try for any visitor.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nyama Choma: Grilled meat (usually goat) served with ugali (a type of cornmeal mush) and veggies.
  • Chapati: A type of flatbread that is a popular street food in Nairobi.
  • Sukuma Wiki: A dish made of collard greens cooked with onions and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tusker Beer: Kenya’s most famous beer, known for its crisp and refreshing taste.
  • Kikombe Cha Uji: A popular breakfast beverage made of spiced tea and milk.
  • Dawa: A traditional cocktail made with vodka, honey, and lime.

Where to Stay in Nairobi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nairobi has a wide variety of accommodations to suit any budget or travel style. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Fairmont The Norfolk: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city that offers amazing hospitality and world-class amenities.
  • YHA Nairobi: A budget-friendly hostel located in a quiet suburb that offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Wildebeest Eco Camp: A unique glamping experience that offers eco-conscious travelers a sustainable and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Nairobi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nairobi is a treasure trove of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or mementos. Some popular options include:

  • Maasai Shuka: A colorful blanket that is a staple of Maasai culture.
  • Kenyan Coffee: Kenya is known for its delicious coffee, and you can bring home a bag of freshly roasted beans.
  • Handmade Soap: Crafted from natural ingredients like shea butter and lavender, handmade soap is a great way to bring a piece of Nairobi home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Nairobi

Nairobi is a diverse city with several different cultural groups living together. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, dress modestly, and ask for permission before taking photos of people or places.

Best Time to Visit Nairobi

The best time to visit Nairobi is during the dry season, which runs from January to March and from June to October. During this time, the weather is mild and sunny, and wildlife sightings are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nairobi

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Nairobi:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Carry expensive jewelry or cameras around.
  • Take photos of people or places without asking for permission.
  • Go out alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Safety and Health

Nairobi is a safe city, but visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety and health while traveling.

Safety in Nairobi

  • Avoid walking around alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables, including your passport, in a safe place.
  • Only use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services.

Health in Nairobi

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Visit a travel clinic before your trip to get necessary vaccinations and medications.

Getting to Nairobi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nairobi is easily accessible via several transportation options, including air, bus, and train.

Airports Near Nairobi

  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO): Located in the city center, the airport serves major airlines and is the busiest airport in East Africa.

Bus Stations Near Nairobi

  • Nairobi’s premier bus station, Machakos Country Bus Station, is located in the heart of the city and offers bus services to neighboring countries.

Other Options

  • Train: Nairobi is connected to other major cities in Kenya by rail.

How to Get Around in Nairobi

Nairobi has a variety of transportation options, including buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services. The most convenient and affordable way to get around the city is by using a ride-sharing service like Uber, Bolt, or Little Cab.

Where to Go Next from Nairobi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nairobi is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in Kenya and beyond. Some popular options include:

  • Mombasa: A coastal city with beautiful beaches and a vibrant culture.
  • Maasai Mara National Reserve: Kenya’s premier wildlife reserve, home to the annual wildebeest migration.
  • Zanzibar: A beautiful island off the coast of Tanzania, known for its stunning beaches and rich culture.

Conclusion

Nairobi is a fascinating and vibrant city that offers visitors an array of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions. With this comprehensive travel guide, you should be well-equipped to plan an unforgettable trip to one of East Africa’s most exciting destinations.
