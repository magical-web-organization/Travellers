Introduction to Machakos

Machakos is a hidden gem located in the Southern Rift Valley of Kenya, in East Africa. It is a bustling town filled with a rich cultural heritage and awe-inspiring natural attractions. It is a perfect destination for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and an authentic Kenyan experience. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, activities to do, where to stay, and more in Machakos.

List of Places to Visit in Machakos

Machakos town boasts a variety of attractions that will leave you enchanted. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Machakos Peoples’ Park: A serene park with beautiful gardens, a bird zoo, and a children’s play area.

  2. Machakos Museum: A museum that displays the region’s cultural history and archeological findings.

  3. Machakos Fort: A historical site built in the 19th century by the British colonial government, now serving as a police station.

Natural Attractions of Machakos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ol Donyo Sabuk National Park: A beautiful park with stunning views, wildlife, hiking trails, and a waterfall.

  2. Masinga Dam: A man-made lake with breathtaking views, boating activities, and fishing.

  3. Fourteen Falls: A series of waterfalls with a lush green jungle environment, picnic areas, and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Machakos

Machakos is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise thanks to its natural attributes. Listed below, are the best routes for you to explore:

  1. Ol Donyo Sabuk National Park Hike: This hike takes you through the park’s lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and wildlife.

  2. Kilimambogo Biking Trail: This biking trail takes you through scenic views of farms, hills and forested areas.

  3. Kyamwilu Hill Hike: This hike takes you through stunning rocky terrain and grassland areas, with panoramic views of the savannah.

Best Things to Do in Machakos: Activities and Tours

Machakos offers a range of activities and tour packages for every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Machakos:

  1. Cycling Tours: Take a cycling tour to explore the town, parks, historical sites, and scenic routes.

  2. Cultural Tours: Explore the traditional music, dance, and attire of the Kamba tribe at local cultural centers.

  3. Wildlife Safaris: Take a jeep or walking safari in Ol Donyo Sabuk National Park and other nearby wildlife conservancies.

Food and Drink Scene of Machakos

Machakos has a rich culinary culture, with a fusion of African and Indian dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Machakos:

Must-Try Dishes:

  1. Pulao: A flavorful rice dish cooked with meat or vegetables.

  2. Chapati: A soft and flaky flatbread, often served with stews or curries.

  3. Mbaazi: A delicious coconut milk and pigeon pea stew.

Local Beverages:

  1. Kiddingu: A sweet drink made with ginger, water and sugar.

  2. Mahewu: A traditional drink made from fermented maize, rich in vitamins and minerals.

Where to Stay in Machakos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are ample options to stay in Machakos, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some hotels, hostels and guesthouses to consider:

  1. Panari Resort Nyandarua: A luxurious resort set in the beautiful Cherangani Hills, featuring panoramic views of Mount Kenya.

  2. Oakwood Hotel: A boutique hotel with beautiful decor and a restaurant serving delicious African and Continental cuisine.

  3. Sirville Lodge: A perfect place for luxury lovers. It offers a beautiful outdoor pool and spa services.

What to Buy in Machakos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to pick up a souvenir to remember the beautiful sights of Machakos. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy:

  1. Kiondo: A beautiful and traditional woven basket that was originally used for carrying goods.

  2. Wood Carvings: Intricately designed wood carvings made by skilled locals are available in many shops around town.

  3. Beaded Jewelry: Colorful and unique beaded jewelry made by the Kamba tribe is a great souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Machakos

Machakos is home to the Kamba tribe, known for their traditional music, dance, and attire. It is essential to respect the cultural norms and customs. Here are some of the cultural customs to be aware of:

  1. Dress modestly and respectfully, particularly when visiting cultural and religious sites.

  2. Ask permission before taking photos of people, particularly women and children.

  3. Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or place of worship.

Best Time to Visit Machakos

The best time to visit Machakos is during the dry season, from June to October and December to March. The weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and there is less rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Machakos

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Machakos:

Do:

  1. Carry enough water and wear comfortable walking shoes when going outdoors.

  2. Respect the local customs, culture, and wildlife.

  3. Use mosquito repellent to protect yourself from mosquito-borne illnesses.

Don’t:

  1. Enter restricted or protected areas.

  2. Litter or vandalize the natural environment or historical sites.

Safety and Health

The safety and health conditions in Machakos are good. However, it’s essential to take care of yourself and follow normal precautions when traveling. Here are some safety and health tips to be aware of:

Safety in Machakos

  1. Do not walk alone at night, particularly in isolated areas.

  2. Keep your valuables safe by carrying them with you, or putting them in hotel safes.

Health in Machakos

  1. Use insect repellent to reduce the risk of catching mosquito-borne diseases.

  2. Drink boiled or bottled water to avoid any water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Machakos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Machakos is accessible through different modes of transportation, and the logistics are easy to navigate. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Machakos

Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in Nairobi is the nearest significant airport to Machakos.

Bus Stations Near Machakos

Machakos bus station is located in the city center and serves local, regional and national routes.

Other Options

You can hail taxis in the town, or book a private taxi from tours and travel companies around town.

How to Get Around in Machakos

Machakos is a small town, and it is relatively easy to get around. Many of the attractions are within walking distance, and taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Machakos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

You can venture from Machakos to explore other beautiful destinations in Kenya. Here are some places to consider:

  1. Nairobi City: Kenya’s capital, a bustling metropolis with museums, shopping centers, and restaurants.

  2. Tsavo Game Reserve: Just a few hours drive away, a great place to see wildlife, including the big five.

Conclusion

Machakos is an exciting destination that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and wilderness adventure. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan a memorable trip to Machakos. Safe travels!
