Introduction to Garissa

Garissa, located in Eastern Kenya, is a vibrant city known for its cultural and historical significance. The city is home to the largest Somali community in Kenya and is a melting pot of diverse cultures.

Garissa is also known for its natural beauty, with scenic landscapes featuring rolling hills, lush forests, and breathtaking waterfalls. It is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Garissa.

List of Places to Visit in Garissa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Garissa Museum - A must-visit for history buffs. The museum showcases the cultural heritage of the Garissa people and the city’s history.
  • Garissa Fort - Constructed in the 19th century, the fort served as a trading post for the Sultan of Zanzibar. Today, it is a historical site that offers panoramic views of the city.
  • Nasrullah Mosque - An iconic mosque with an impressive architecture built in the 1940s by the Garissa’s Indian community.

Natural Attractions of Garissa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dadaab Refugee Camp - Located on the outskirts of Garissa, Dadaab is the world’s largest refugee camp. Visitors can take a tour of the camp and learn more about the plight of refugees.
  • Tana River Primate Reserve - A wildlife reserve that’s home to a diverse range of primates, including baboons, colobus monkeys, and vervet monkeys.
  • Korisa Forest - A scenic forest with a variety of flora and fauna, including rare bird species and monkeys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Garissa

  • Kulamawe Hill and Dryland Forest - A popular destination for hiking enthusiasts. The route offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Korisa Forest Trails - A great place for nature lovers and birdwatchers. The forest has well-marked trails that are perfect for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Garissa: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a local market - Garissa’s bustling markets are a great place to experience the city’s vibrant culture and sample local delicacies.
  • Take a guided tour of the city - A knowledgeable guide can take you through the city’s history and culture, providing an insight into the daily lives of its residents.
  • Explore the countryside - Garissa’s surrounding countryside offers a range of outdoor activities, including camping, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting.

Food and Drink Scene of Garissa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bajiya - A deep-fried snack made of lentil flour, onions, and chili.
  • Toban Muri - A traditional dish made of rice, beef, and a variety of spices.
  • Kebab - Skewered meat that is marinated in a blend of spices and grilled over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Tamarind Juice - A refreshing drink made from tamarind fruit.
  • Tea with Ginger - A popular beverage that’s perfect for cold evenings.
  • Mango Juice - A sweet, fruity drink made from locally grown mangoes.

Where to Stay in Garissa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Longhorn Hotel - A luxury hotel located in the heart of Garissa.
  • Tower Top Hotel - A modern hotel that offers excellent views of the city.
  • Malaika Guest House - A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Garissa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maasai Beaded Jewelry - A popular souvenir that features intricate beadwork and vibrant colors.
  • Kikoy - A versatile cloth that’s perfect for beachwear or as a wrap.
  • Coffee - Garissa’s coffee is renowned for its unique flavor and aroma.

Local Culture and Customs of Garissa

Garissa is a city with a strong Islamic influence and is known for its hospitality. It’s important to dress modestly while in Garissa and to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Garissa

The best time to visit Garissa is between June and September, when the weather is mild and dry, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Garissa

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Touch or take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Garissa

Garissa is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take precautions, especially when traveling in and around the city. Avoid walking alone at night and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Garissa

It’s important to take extra precautions with food and water in Garissa, as the risk of gastrointestinal diseases is higher. It’s recommended to drink bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Garissa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Garissa

The closest airport to Garissa is Garissa Airport, which is served by domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Garissa

Garissa is easily accessible by bus from major cities in Kenya, including Nairobi, Mombasa, and Kisumu.

Other Options

Renting a car is also an option for those who prefer to drive, although it’s important to take extra precautions while driving in Kenya.

How to Get Around in Garissa

Taxis are the most convenient mode of transportation in Garissa. It’s important to negotiate the fare before embarking on your journey.

Where to Go Next from Garissa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lamu Island - A picturesque island with a rich history and stunning beaches.
  • Tsavo National Park - One of Kenya’s largest national parks, home to a diverse range of wildlife.
  • Malindi - A coastal town with a diverse range of attractions, including historical sites, stunning beaches, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Garissa is a city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. With its unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures, it’s a destination that has something for everyone. Use this travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Garissa, Kenya.
