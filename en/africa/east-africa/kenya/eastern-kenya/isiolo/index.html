Introduction to Isiolo

Isiolo is a small and picturesque town located in the heart of Eastern Kenya, surrounded by the rugged and majestic landscapes of Northern Kenya. It is a melting pot of different tribes, languages, and cultures that exemplifies the diversity and richness of Kenya. Isiolo has become increasingly popular among tourists seeking to explore Kenya beyond the typical tourist destinations. With its unique blend of natural wonders, rustic charm, and cultural diversity, Isiolo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Isiolo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visiting Isiolo gives you the opportunity to learn about Kenya’s rich history and culture. Here are some culturally important attractions you should visit:

  • The Isiolo Cultural Centre: It is the heart of Isiolo culture and history. The Centre showcases traditional artifacts, musical instruments, jewelry, and clothing from different tribes in Kenya.
  • The Isiolo Catholic Church: The church is a fascinating colonial-style building that dates back to the early 1900s. Its architecture is a blend of Victorian and Gothic styles, and it houses some of the oldest church artifacts in Kenya.
  • Ngare Ndare Forest: The forest is a cultural and historical attraction that was used as a grazing land by the Borana community some centuries ago. The place is now a lush and dense forest, home to different bird species, monkeys, and elephants.

Natural Attractions of Isiolo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Isiolo is blessed with natural wonders that are breathtaking and awe-inspiring. Here are some outdoor adventures and scenic views that you should not miss:

  • The Chalbi Desert: It is located to the north of Isiolo and is a unique and fascinating desert that changes its colors with sunrise and sunset. You can take a walk or a camel ride and enjoy the stunning views of the desert’s golden sands.
  • Mount Kenya: It is the second-highest peak in Africa and offers a challenging but rewarding hiking route. A trek to the summit provides spectacular views of Kenya’s highlands and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Samburu National Reserve: This protected area offers a wide range of wildlife viewing opportunities, with over 100 species of mammals and birds. The reserve’s landscape is breathtaking, with its savannah plains, rugged hills, and meandering rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isiolo

Isiolo is a great place for all outdoor enthusiasts, with its vast parks and untouched wilderness. Here are some hiking/biking routes that should be on your itinerary:

  • Mount Ololokwe: It is one of the most challenging but rewarding hikes in Kenya. With its stunning natural beauty, the peak provides a panoramic view of Isiolo town and the surrounding area.
  • Samburu Conservancy: The conservancy offers a range of hiking trails that provide a scenic view of the Samburu National Reserve. You can see wildlife grazing, and enjoy the beautiful landscapes and views.
  • Borana Conservancy: This reserve is a great place for mountain biking, with its sprawling landscapes and beautiful scenery. The conservancy provides bike rentals and guided tours for visitors.

Best Things to Do in Isiolo: Activities and Tours

Isiolo offers an array of activities and tours that cater to different preferences and interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Cultural tours: Take a cultural tour and learn about the different tribes of Kenya, their cultures, and traditions.
  • Game drives: Go on a game drive in Samburu National Reserve and see wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Horseback riding: Horseback riding is a unique way to explore Isiolo’s natural beauty, with guided tours available at different locations.
  • Camel Trekking: Take a camel tour and experience the traditional way of life in the desert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isiolo

Here is a week-long itinerary to make the best of your trip to Isiolo:

Day Activity
1 Fly to Nairobi. Drive to Isiolo. Check into your hotel. Relax, and adjust to the time zone.
2 Start with a cultural tour of Isiolo. Visit the Isiolo Cultural Centre and learn about the different tribes in Kenya.
3 Have a day trip to the Samburu National Reserve, and see the wildlife.
4 Go on a camel trek in the morning, spend the afternoon relaxing or horseback riding.
5 Go on a hiking expedition to the Chalbi Desert or Mount Ololokwe.
6 Visit Ngare Ndare Forest or Borana Conservancy for a game drive, mountain biking, or bird watching.
7 Take the morning flight back to Nairobi, do some shopping, visit the Giraffe Centre, the Elephant Orphanage, and depart in the evening.

Food and Drink Scene of Isiolo

Isiolo is famous for its own unique cuisine, which blends Kenyan and Somali spices and flavors. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nyama Choma: Grilled meat, usually goat or beef, served with ugali and kachumbari salad.
  • Pilau Rice: Rice cooked with spices, meat, and vegetables. It is usually served with kachumbari salad or tomato-based vegetable stew.
  • Chicken Biryani: A delicious and spicy rice and chicken dish served with vegetable curry or kachumbari salad.

Local Beverages

  • Bajia: A local beer made from maize, millet, and sorghum.
  • Tamarind Juice: A refreshing and tangy drink made from tamarind fruit.

Where to Stay in Isiolo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isiolo has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Sarova Shaba Game Lodge: A luxurious resort located on the banks of Ewaso Nyiro River. The lodge offers a pool, a spa, and various outdoor activities.
  • The White House Guest House and Hostel: A more affordable option, centrally located, with a friendly staff and all the basic amenities.
  • Cheetah Tented Camp: A unique tented camp that is located in the heart of Samburu National Reserve. The camp is eco-friendly and offers a range of activities.

What to Buy in Isiolo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Isiolo boasts a unique and diverse shopping experience, with its rich cultural heritage and colorfulness. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should bring home:

  • Beads and Jewelry: Isiolo is famous for its intricate and unique hand-made beads and jewelry, made from different materials such as bone, wood, and glass.
  • Kikoys and Khangas: These are traditional Kenyan clothing that comes in a wide range of colors and styles.
  • Samburu Blankets: These colorful blankets are made from wool and are a perfect souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Isiolo

Isiolo is a melting pot of different tribes, each with its own unique culture and customs. Here are some customs you should know:

  • Greeting: Greeting is an essential aspect of Kenyan culture. It is customary to greet people when you meet them, either verbally or through a handshake.
  • Dress Code: Dress conservatively, especially in rural areas. Long pants and shirts that cover the shoulders are appropriate for both men and women.
  • Hospitality: Kenyan culture is known for its hospitality. It is common for visitors to be offered food and drink when they enter people’s homes.

Best Time to Visit Isiolo

The best time to visit Isiolo is from June to September, during Kenya’s dry season. During this time, the weather is ideal for outdoor activities, and the wildlife is easier to spot. However, Isiolo is an all-year-round destination since it offers a range of activities throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isiolo

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture and Customs: Learn about the local culture and customs and act accordingly.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: The local cuisine is unique and flavorful.
  • Pack Sensibly: Pack appropriate clothing for the activities you plan to do.
  • Book Accommodation and Tours in Advance: During peak season, Isiolo can be crowded, so book your accommodation and tours in advance.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the environment clean and do not litter.
  • Touch or Disturb the Wildlife: Always maintain a safe distance and follow the park rules.
  • Exchange Money with Unapproved Vendors: Use reputable authorized vendors when exchanging money.
  • Travel Alone at Night: It is safer to travel with other people at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isiolo

Isiolo is relatively safe, but visitors should use caution, especially when walking alone at night. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large sums of money. Always be vigilant and maintain common sense safety precautions.

Health in Isiolo

Visitors should ensure they get the required vaccinations before traveling to Isiolo. Malaria is present in the region, so take appropriate preventative measures.

Getting to Isiolo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Isiolo is easily accessible by air, car, or bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Isiolo

  • Isiolo Airport: Flights are available from Nairobi, Kenya’s capital city.

Bus Stations Near Isiolo

  • Isiolo Bus Station: Buses are available from Nairobi, Kenya’s capital city.

Other Options

  • Private Shuttle Services: Private shuttle services to and from Isiolo are available from Nairobi.

How to Get Around in Isiolo

Isiolo is a small town and is easy to navigate on foot. However, for longer distances, there are car rental services, taxis, and motorbike taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Isiolo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can explore after visiting Isiolo:

  • Nakuru National Park: A stunning wildlife reserve located in the Rift Valley.
  • Lamu Island: A beautiful beach destination that is famous for its Swahili culture and heritage.
  • Nanyuki: A charming market town located at the foothills of Mount Kenya.

Conclusion

Isiolo is a hidden gem in Kenya that is waiting to be explored. It offers a unique blend of wilderness, culture, and adventure that appeals to all types of travelers. Plan your next trip to Isiolo and experience the best of Eastern Kenya.
