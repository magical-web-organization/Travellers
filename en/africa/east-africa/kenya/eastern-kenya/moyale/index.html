If you’re looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path destination in East Africa, consider Moyale. This town is located in Eastern Kenya, near the Ethiopian border. Known for its striking natural beauty, rich history, and diverse culture, Moyale is a must-visit destination for adventurous travelers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Moyale, from the best places to visit to the top things to do and where to stay. Let’s get started.

Introduction to Moyale

Moyale is a small town located in the Rift Valley Region of Kenya, roughly 700 km from Nairobi. It’s situated on the border of Kenya and Ethiopia, with the Moyale River running through it. The town is known for being a transit point between Kenya and Ethiopia, but it also has plenty to offer for those looking to explore the town and the surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Moyale

If you’re planning on exploring Moyale, there are several places you should add to your itinerary. These include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Moyale Museum: This museum showcases the cultural heritage and history of the local Borana and Gabra people.
  • The Fossil Site: This site contains the remains of prehistoric fauna and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  • The Moyale Pillar: This is a historical landmark that marks the border between Ethiopia and Kenya.

Natural Attractions of Moyale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Moyale Hill: This is a scenic hill that offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • The Moyale Wildlife Reserve: This is a protected wildlife reserve that is home to several endangered species, including the Grevy’s zebra.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moyale

Moyale is a great destination for those who love the outdoors. There are several hiking and biking routes in and around the town, including:

  • The Moyale Hill Hike: This is a challenging hike that takes you to the top of Moyale Hill.
  • The Marsabit National Park Hike: This is a multi-day hiking route that takes you through the stunning Marsabit National Park.

Best Things to Do in Moyale: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours you can enjoy in Moyale. These include:

  • Cultural tours of the local Borana and Gabra communities.
  • Game drives in the Moyale Wildlife Reserve.
  • River rafting and fishing on the Moyale River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moyale

If you’re planning on spending a week in Moyale, here’s an itinerary you can use:

Food and Drink Scene of Moyale

Moyale is known for its unique cuisine and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bule: A traditional Borana dish made from beans and maize.
  • Bajia: A local snack made from chickpeas and spices.
  • Goulash: A hearty stew made from beef, onions, and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Camel Milk: A nutritious and refreshing drink that is common in the region.
  • Tea: Tea is a popular beverage in Moyale, with several variations available, including lemongrass tea and ginger tea.

Where to Stay in Moyale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Moyale, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Lalkaka Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Moyale Resort Hotel: This hotel is located on the outskirts of the town and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Guesthouse Moyale: This guesthouse is located in the center of the town and offers affordable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Moyale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Moyale, here are some options:

  • Beaded Jewelry: The local Borana and Gabra communities are known for their intricate beaded jewelry.
  • Camel Skin Products: Camel skin is a common material used to make various products, including bags, wallets, and shoes.
  • Local Spices: Moyale is known for its unique blend of spices, which are used in several local dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Moyale

Moyale is a culturally rich town, with several unique customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind when visiting Moyale include:

  • The local Borana and Gabra communities have rich oral traditions and are known for their storytelling.
  • Respect local customs and dress conservatively.
  • Greetings are an important part of the culture, so be sure to greet people warmly when you meet them.

Best Time to Visit Moyale

The best time to visit Moyale is during the dry season, which runs from December to March. During this time, you can enjoy the outdoor activities and explore the town without worrying about heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moyale

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress conservatively.
  • Greet people warmly.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Travel after dark without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moyale

Moyale is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Avoid walking alone after dark.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings at all times.
  • Be cautious when withdrawing money from ATMs.

Health in Moyale

Moyale is a high-risk zone for malaria, so it’s important to take preventative measures, such as taking antimalarial medication and using insect repellent. It’s also important to drink bottled or purified water to avoid contracting waterborne illnesses.

Getting to Moyale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moyale is located in a remote area, but there are several transportation options available. Here are some ways to get to Moyale:

Airports Near Moyale

  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, Nairobi (700 km from Moyale)

Bus Stations Near Moyale

  • Moyale Bus Station

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: You can arrange for a private car or taxi to take you to Moyale.

How to Get Around in Moyale

The easiest way to get around Moyale is by foot, but you can also take a taxi, motorbike, or tuk-tuk to get around.

Where to Go Next from Moyale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Kenya after visiting Moyale, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Marsabit National Park: This protected wildlife reserve is located about 180 km from Moyale and is home to several endangered species.
  • Samburu National Reserve: This national reserve is located about 250 km from Moyale and is known for its diverse wildlife.
  • Lake Turkana: This large lake is located about 500 km from Moyale and is a great destination for those interested in outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Moyale is a hidden gem in East Africa, with stunning natural beauty, rich history, and a vibrant culture. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just relaxing in a unique destination, Moyale has something for everyone.
