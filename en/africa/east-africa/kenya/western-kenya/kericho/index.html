Introduction to Kericho

Kericho is a captivating town located in Western Kenya. Known as the tea capital of Kenya, it’s a beautiful destination that boasts of its lush green tea fields, beautiful forests, striking waterfalls, and wildlife reserves. The friendly locals and beautiful scenery make it an ideal place to relax and unwind.

List of Places to Visit in Kericho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tulwap Kipsigis Museum: A museum that showcases Kipsigis heritage, culture, and history. It’s a great place to learn about the Kipsigis people, their lifestyle, and their traditions.
  • Kericho Law Courts: It’s a colonial-era building that is now used as courts of law. It’s worth a visit to take a look at the architecture and learn about the colonial period.
  • Ndalo Plains: These plains are a historical site where the legendary leader Koitalel arap Samoei fought for the independence of his people. It’s a great place to learn about the history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Kericho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tea plantations: Kericho is known for its tea plantations, and taking a tour of these lush green fields is a must-do activity. You can learn how tea is grown and processed and sample different types of tea.
  • Kaptures and Chui Waterfalls: These are two of the most beautiful waterfalls in the region, and visiting them will give you a chance to appreciate the beautiful scenery.
  • Kakamega Forest Reserve: Explore the lush forest, discover bird species and other wildlife, and take a nature walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kericho

  • Kollong River Walk: This is a beautiful walk that takes you through tea plantations, forests, and along the Kollong river.
  • Nandi Hills: A scenic hike through beautiful hills and tea plantations.
  • Kakamega Forest Trail: A beautiful trail that will take you through the beautiful Kakamega forest reserve.

Best Things to Do in Kericho: Activities and Tours

  • Tea tours: Learn about the tea-making process and sample different types of tea.
  • Bird watching: Kericho is a birder’s paradise, and the Kakamega Forest Reserve is the best place to spot different species of birds.
  • Cultural tours: Take a guided tour and explore the cultural heritage of the Kipsigis people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kericho

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Kericho, relax and acclimatize to the surroundings.
2 Take a tea tour and sample different types of tea.
3 Visit the Kakamega Forest Reserve and hike the forest trail.
4 Explore the Kaptures and Chui Waterfalls.
5 Take a cultural tour to the Tulwap Kipsigis Museum and learn about the heritage and lifestyle of the Kipsigis people.
6 Go bird watching in the morning and take a hike through the Nandi Hills in the afternoon.
7 Depart Kericho for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Kericho

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chapati: A Kenyan flatbread that’s a popular breakfast food in Kericho.
  • Ugali: A staple food made from maize flour, eaten with vegetables or stew.
  • Sukuma wiki: A vegetable dish made from kale or spinach.

Local Beverages

  • Chai (tea): Tea is a way of life in Kericho, and locals take it with milk and sugar.
  • Mursik: A traditional sour milk drink that’s popular among the Kipsigis people.

Where to Stay in Kericho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kericho Tea Hotel: A luxury hotel that’s located near the tea fields. It’s known for its spacious rooms, beautiful setting, and excellent service.
  • Kericho Guesthouse: A guesthouse that is known for its affordability, comfort, and proximity to the town center.
  • Kisiwa Guesthouse and Hostel: An affordable guesthouse that’s popular among backpackers. It’s located near the tea fields and has a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Kericho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tea: Kericho is known for its tea, and taking some back home is a must-do activity.
  • Maasai Shukas: These are traditional blankets that are perfect for cold nights.
  • Handmade crafts: There are several craft shops in Kericho that sell beautiful handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Kericho

  • Kenyans are generally conservative people, and it’s important to dress appropriately in public places.
  • Respect for elders and people in authority is important.
  • Shaking hands is a common greeting among Kenyans.

Best Time to Visit Kericho

The best time to visit Kericho is from June to September when the weather is dry and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities. However, the tea fields are at their greenest between October and February, making it a great time to visit as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kericho

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Take a tea tour and sample different types of tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of people or places without permission.
  • Don’t litter or harm the environment.
  • Avoid politically sensitive discussions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kericho

Kericho is generally a safe place to visit. However, it’s important to exercise caution and be vigilant at all times, especially when walking alone at night.

Health in Kericho

It’s important to take precautions against malaria as it’s prevalent in the region. It’s recommended to take anti-malarial medication before and during your stay in Kericho. You should also ensure that you have adequate travel medical insurance.

Getting to Kericho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kericho

  • Kisumu International Airport: Located 85km from Kericho, it’s the closest airport to Kericho.

Bus Stations Near Kericho

  • Kericho Bus Station: Located in the town center, it’s the main bus station in Kericho.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or taxi to get to Kericho. It’s also possible to take a shared taxi or matatu (minibus) from neighboring towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Kericho

  • Taxis are readily available in Kericho and are a convenient way to get around.
  • Matatus are the most affordable way to get around, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.
  • Boda-bodas (motorcycle taxis) are a fast and affordable way to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Kericho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kisumu: A bustling Kenyan town that’s located 80km from Kericho. It’s known for its beautiful Lake Victoria, museums, and markets.
  • Saiwa Swamp National Park: A park that’s popular for hiking, camping, and bird watching.
  • Lake Nakuru National Park: A wildlife reserve that’s known for its flamingos, rhinos, and other wildlife.

Conclusion

Kericho is a hidden gem that’s worth visiting when traveling to Kenya. It’s known for its tea fields, beautiful scenery, friendly locals, and rich cultural heritage. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now equipped to plan your trip to Kericho and make the most of your visit.
