Introduction to Kakamega

Located in Western Kenya, Kakamega is a small town that attracts travelers with its scenic views, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage. The town is known for its lush green forests, cascading waterfalls, and distinctive customs and traditions that add to its charm.

Kakamega is an ideal destination for backpackers, adventure seekers, and wildlife enthusiasts who want to explore Africa beyond its major cities. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Kakamega.

List of Places to Visit in Kakamega

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kakamega has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. Some of the top cultural attractions to visit in Kakamega include:

  1. Kakamega Forest Heritage Center- The Forest Heritage Center showcases the cultural and natural history of the Kakamega Rainforest. The center has exhibits on the region’s flora, fauna, and traditional life of the Luhya people.

  2. Crying Stone of Ilesi - This is a unique rock boulder that has a distinctive shape and emits a crying sound. According to local folklore, the stone is associated with a weeping woman who lost her child to a crocodile.

  3. Nabongo Cultural Center - This center showcases the culture and lifestyle of the local Luhya tribe. Visitors can see and learn about traditional crafts, music, dance, food, and more.

Natural Attractions of Kakamega: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kakamega is a paradise for nature lovers, with plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Some of the top natural attractions in Kakamega include:

  1. Kakamega Forest Reserve - This forest reserve is home to a vast array of flora and fauna, including over 400 bird species, monkeys, and other primates.

  2. Lurambi Falls - Located near the town center, Lurambi Falls is a magnificent waterfall that cascades down from a height of over 300 feet.

  3. Lake Victoria - Lake Victoria is Africa’s largest freshwater lake, and it is located just a short distance from Kakamega. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, and boat rides on this scenic lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kakamega

Kakamega is also an ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Kakamega include:

  1. Kakamega Forest Reserve Nature Trail - This hiking trail is about 20 kilometers long and takes visitors through the lush green forests of Kakamega. The trail provides an opportunity to see monkeys, birds, and other wildlife.

  2. Mumias Sugar Belt Trail - The Mumias Sugar Belt Trail is a popular biking route that runs through the sugar cane plantations and rural villages around Kakamega. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kakamega: Activities and Tours

Kakamega offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to the interests of various travelers. Some of the top things to do in Kakamega include:

  1. Bird Watching - Bird Watching is a popular activity in Kakamega, thanks to its rich birdlife and diverse ecosystems. Visitors can spot over 400 bird species, including the blue-headed bee-eater, Ross’s Turaco, and the African grey parrot.

  2. Canopy Walk Tour - The Kakamega Forest Reserve Canopy Walk is a thrilling experience that lets visitors walk on an elevated rope bridge through the forest canopy.

Food and Drink Scene of Kakamega

Kakamega offers a unique dining experience with its traditional cuisine, local food markets, and street food. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Kakamega include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sukuma Wiki - This is a popular Kenyan vegetable dish made of kale or collard greens.

  2. Matoke - This is a traditional dish made of mashed plantains.

  3. Chapatis - These are soft, fluffy flatbreads that are a staple in Kenyan cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Busaa - This is a local brew made of fermented maize that is popular in Western Kenya.

  2. Muratina - This is a traditional Kenyan drink made of fermented honey and water.

Where to Stay in Kakamega: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kakamega offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the top places to stay in Kakamega include:

  1. Rondo Retreat Center: This is a mid-range accommodation option that offers comfortable rooms, beautiful views, and access to nature walks and other outdoor activities.

  2. Kakamega Golf Hotel: This is a luxury hotel that features a golf course, a swimming pool, and lush green gardens.

What to Buy in Kakamega: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Kakamega can bring home unique souvenirs and local products that reflect the local culture and craftsmanship. Some of the top things to buy in Kakamega include:

  1. Woven Baskets - These are handcrafted baskets made of grass and are used for carrying food, firewood, or other items.

  2. Wood Carvings - These are traditional wooden sculptures and carvings that reflect the local customs and traditions.

Local Culture and Customs of Kakamega

Kakamega has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. The town is predominantly inhabited by the Luhya tribe, who follow traditional practices and rituals. Some of the key cultural elements of Kakamega include:

  1. Music and Dance - Luhya music and dance are an essential part of the local culture and are performed on special occasions such as weddings, harvest festivals, or funerals.

  2. Clothing - The traditional attire of the Luhya people consists of colorful clothes made of locally produced materials such as cotton, silk, or wool.

Best Time to Visit Kakamega

The best time to visit Kakamega is between December and February and between June and October. During these periods, the weather is dry and sunny, and the forest is alive with birdlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kakamega

When visiting Kakamega, it’s essential to observe some basic do’s and don’ts to ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience. Here are some tips to keep in mind.

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Carry cash in Kenyan shillings, as most places do not accept foreign currency.

Don’t:

  1. Take photos of people without their permission.
  2. Wear revealing clothing or dress inappropriately, especially when visiting religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kakamega

Kakamega is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always best to take some safety precautions to avoid any risks. Here are a few safety tips to keep in mind:

  1. Avoid walking alone at night.
  2. Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  3. Follow local customs and traditions to prevent offending anyone.

Health in Kakamega

Kakamega is a relatively safe destination when it comes to health, but travelers are advised to take some precautions to avoid any health risks. Here are a few health tips to keep in mind:

  1. Get vaccinated for yellow fever and hepatitis A and B before traveling to Kakamega.
  2. Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and the risk of malaria.
  3. Carry a first aid kit with essential medicines and other medical supplies.

Getting to Kakamega: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kakamega is easy to get to from Kenya’s major cities and is accessible by various transportation options. Here are some logistics to keep in mind:

  1. Airports Near Kakamega: The closest airport to Kakamega is Kisumu International Airport, which is about 70 kilometers away.

  2. Bus Stations Near Kakamega: There are several bus stations in Kakamega that connect the town to various cities and towns in Kenya.

  3. Other Options: Visitors can also hire taxis or private cars to get to Kakamega from nearby cities or towns.

How to Get Around in Kakamega

Kakamega is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot. Visitors can also use taxis or motorbikes to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Kakamega: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kakamega is located in a region that has many other exciting destinations and excursions to explore. Some of the top places to visit near Kakamega include:

  1. Kisumu - A vibrant city located on the shores of Lake Victoria.

  2. Lake Nakuru National Park - A scenic game reserve that is home to rhinos, zebras, buffalos, and flamingos.

Conclusion

Kakamega is a hidden gem in Western Kenya and is an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience Africa beyond its major cities. With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse outdoor activities, Kakamega is an unforgettable destination that must be on every traveler’s bucket list.
