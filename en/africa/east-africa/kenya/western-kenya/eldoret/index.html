Looking to explore the vibrant city of Eldoret in Western Kenya? You’re in luck! This bustling metropolis offers visitors a wide range of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions that are sure to make your trip unforgettable. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll dive into the best places to visit, things to do, and more - so grab your bags and get ready for an adventure.

Introduction to Eldoret

Nestled in the fertile highlands of western Kenya, Eldoret is the fifth-largest city in Kenya and a hub of cultural and economic activity. Known for its vibrant markets, lively nightlife, and stunning natural beauty, Eldoret has something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Eldoret

Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply soaking up the local atmosphere, Eldoret has no shortage of must-visit destinations. Here are some of our favorite places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Eldoret Museum: This fascinating museum tells the story of Eldoret’s history, from prehistoric times to the present day. Visitors can browse exhibits highlighting the region’s geology, art, culture, and more.
  2. Korongo Art Centre: If you’re looking to immerse yourself in local art and culture, don’t miss the Korongo Art Centre. This creative hub is home to a diverse collection of galleries, studios, and workshops, showcasing works by local and regional artists.
  3. Lovejoy’s Mabanda Cultural Centre: For an interactive cultural experience, head to Lovejoy’s Mabanda Cultural Centre. Here, you can participate in traditional dance and music performances, learn about local customs, and even try your hand at traditional crafts like beadwork and basket weaving.

Natural Attractions of Eldoret: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital Hill: For breathtaking 360-degree views of Eldoret and the surrounding countryside, climb to the top of Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital Hill. This easy hike offers unparalleled views of the city, the Cherangani Hills, and even Mount Elgon on a clear day.
  2. Kaptagat Forest: Nature lovers won’t want to miss a trip to Kaptagat Forest, a pristine natural area just outside Eldoret. This idyllic location offers hiking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and the chance to spot local wildlife like gazelles and hyenas.
  3. Kesses Dam: If you’re looking for a peaceful respite from the bustle of Eldoret, head to Kesses Dam. This scenic spot offers fishing opportunities, picnic areas, and a tranquil atmosphere that’s perfect for a lazy afternoon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eldoret

Eldoret is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with a range of trails and routes for every skill level. Some of the most popular options include:

  1. Torok and Kessup Falls: Located just outside Eldoret, the Torok and Kessup Falls trail offers a stunning waterfall to admire and plenty of opportunities to spot local wildlife.
  2. Sergoit Hill: For a pleasant hike up a scenic hill, head to Sergoit Hill. The trail is well-marked and offers great views at the top.
  3. Tour De Eldoret: If you’re looking for a cycling challenge, consider taking the Tour De Eldoret. This 40-kilometer route takes you through some of the city’s most scenic areas and offers a challenging workout.

Best Things to Do in Eldoret: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions listed above, Eldoret offers a range of activities and tours to suit every interest. Here are a few options to consider:

  1. Eldoret City Tour: If you’re new to the city, consider taking a guided tour to get oriented and learn about the top sights and attractions.
  2. Sports and Fitness: Eldoret is known as the “Home of Champions” thanks to its many exceptional athletes. If you’re a sports fan or fitness enthusiast, consider signing up for a local race or fitness class.
  3. Local Cuisine: No visit to Eldoret is complete without sampling the local cuisine. Be sure to try traditional Kenyan dishes like ugali, sukuma wiki, and nyama choma at one of the city’s many restaurants and street food stalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eldoret

Ready to plan your trip to Eldoret? Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to get you started:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Eldoret, take a guided city tour, and check into your hotel.
Day 2 Hike up Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital Hill for stunning views, then visit the Eldoret Museum to learn about the city’s history.
Day 3 Take a trip to Kaptagat Forest for outdoor adventures, including hiking and birdwatching.

Food and Drink Scene of Eldoret

No trip to Eldoret is complete without sampling the local cuisine and beverages. Here are a few dishes and drinks to add to your must-try list:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ugali: This staple dish is made from maize flour and is a staple in Kenyan cuisine.
  2. Sukuma Wiki: This vegetable dish typically includes kale or spinach and is served alongside ugali.
  3. Nyama Choma: A delicious meat dish of roasted goat or beef, typically served with a side of kachumbari salsa.

Local Beverages

  1. Mursik: A traditional Kalenjin beverage made from fermented milk and served in a gourd.
  2. Chai: Kenyan tea, typically served with milk and sugar.
  3. Tusker Beer: Kenya’s most popular beer, known for its refreshing taste and crisp finish.

Where to Stay in Eldoret: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eldoret offers plenty of options for travelers looking for a place to stay. Here are a few choices to consider:

  1. Boma Inn Eldoret: This four-star hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of on-site amenities, including a swimming pool, fitness center, and restaurant.
  2. Sirikwa Hotel: Located in the heart of Eldoret, this mid-range hotel offers clean, comfortable rooms and easy access to the city’s top attractions.
  3. Kamels Guesthouse Eldoret: If you’re on a budget, consider a stay at Kamels Guesthouse, which offers simple but clean rooms and a convenient location near the city center.

What to Buy in Eldoret: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to bring home a piece of Eldoret? Here are a few souvenirs and local products to consider:

  1. Beaded jewelry: Eldoret is known for its intricate beaded jewelry, including necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.
  2. Tea: Kenya is known for its world-class tea, so consider bringing home a few bags of your favorite variety.
  3. Maasai blankets: These colorful blankets are a traditional part of Maasai culture and make for a unique and eye-catching souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Eldoret

Eldoret is a diverse city, with many cultural traditions and customs to discover. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and being mindful of local traditions when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Eldoret

The best time to visit Eldoret is during the dry season, which runs from January through March and from June through September. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eldoret

Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit to Eldoret:

Do:

  • Learn a few words of Swahili, the official language of Kenya
  • Take a guided city tour to learn about the top sights and attractions
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Wander around unfamiliar areas alone after dark
  • Disrespect local customs, including dress codes and religious practices
  • Take photographs without asking for permission

Safety and Health

While Eldoret is generally a safe and welcoming city, visitors should take common-sense precautions to stay healthy and secure. Here are a few tips:

Safety in Eldoret

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings secure
  • Avoid wandering around by yourself at night
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Health in Eldoret

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water
  • Wear insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria
  • Be cautious when eating street food, as it may not have been prepared under hygienic conditions

Getting to Eldoret: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eldoret is accessible by a range of transportation options, including air, bus, and car. Here are some key details to keep in mind:

Airports Near Eldoret

  • Eldoret International Airport (EDL) is the primary airport serving the city, offering direct flights to destinations throughout East Africa.

Bus Stations Near Eldoret

  • Eldoret is well-served by long-distance buses, with several major companies operating regular routes to destinations throughout Kenya and beyond.

Other Options

  • Taxis and matatus (minibusses) are readily available for local transportation in Eldoret, providing a quick and convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Eldoret

Eldoret is a compact city, making it easy to get around on foot or by bicycle. For longer trips, consider taking a matatu or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Eldoret: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eldoret is a great jumping-off point for exploring the surrounding region. Here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Kakamega Forest Reserve: This protected forest is located just over an hour from Eldoret and is home to a variety of plant and animal species, including primates, birds, and butterflies.
  • Lake Baringo: This picturesque freshwater lake is located about two hours from Eldoret and offers opportunities for fishing, birdwatching, and nature walks.
  • Kapsabet: This rural town is located about an hour from Eldoret and offers a look at traditional village life in Kenya.

Conclusion

With its vibrant culture, stunning natural beauty, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure, Eldoret is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Kenya. We hope this guide has given you an idea of what to expect and inspired you to start planning your trip today!
