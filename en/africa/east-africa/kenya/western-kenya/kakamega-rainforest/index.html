Introduction to Kakamega Rainforest

Nestled in Western Kenya, Kakamega Rainforest is a lush tropical paradise that covers over 238 square kilometers. This unique forest is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including over 400 species of birds, 380 species of plants, and hundreds of species of butterflies, trees, and mammals. It’s a must-visit destination for nature lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike.

  • Andere Forest trail

Week-Long Itinerary for Kakamega Rainforest

Day 1: Arrive in Kakamega, explore the local markets and food scene Day 2: Hike Rosterman Escarpment, visit the Kakamega Forest Heritage Center and Isukuti dances Day 3: Visit the Kakamega Crying Stone, Malava Forest, and Lavender’s Bridge Day 4: Do a guided nature walk through Kisere Forest, Isecheno Forest and Andere Forest Day 5: Scenic bike ride to Lirhanda Hill, followed by a canopy walk at Kakamega Forest Reserve Day 6: Visit the Degedege Cultural Center and learn about the traditional lifestyles of the Luhya people, followed by a rock climbing and abseiling tour Day 7: Depart from Kakamega Rainforest

Local Culture and Customs of Kakamega Rainforest

The Luhya people are the predominant ethnic group in the region and are known for their agricultural practices and traditional music. Visitors can learn about their culture through cultural tours and visits to local villages.

Best Time to Visit Kakamega Rainforest

The best time to visit Kakamega Rainforest is during the dry season, from June to October and December to February. The weather is mild, and the forest is lush and green, making it the perfect time for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kakamega Rainforest

Do:

  • Bring plenty of water and snacks on hikes and outdoor tours
  • Wear comfortable, breathable clothing and sturdy shoes for hiking and biking tours
  • Respect local cultures and customs
  • Support local businesses and purchase locally made products

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind on trails or in public areas
  • Disturb wildlife or interfere with natural habitats
  • Wear bright clothing or strong perfumes, which can attract insects

Safety and Health

Safety in Kakamega Rainforest

Kakamega Rainforest is relatively safe for visitors, but it’s important to take necessary precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. It’s best to go with a reputable guide for all visits to the forest.

Health in Kakamega Rainforest

Visitors are advised to bring mosquito repellent and take necessary malaria prophylaxis as the region is a malaria zone. It’s also essential to bring first aid supplies and any necessary medications.

How to Get Around in Kakamega Rainforest

The best way to get around Kakamega Rainforest is by car or bike. Visitors can also hire a local guide who can arrange hiking and walking tours.
