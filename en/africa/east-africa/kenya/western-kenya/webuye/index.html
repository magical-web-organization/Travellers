A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Webuye, Kenya

Webuye is a town in Western Kenya surrounded by lush green forests, rolling hills, and the Nzoia River. It is a vibrant town known for its beautiful scenery, natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and friendly people. Whether you are a nature lover, history enthusiast, or adventure seeker, Webuye has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the natural and cultural attractions of Webuye, Kenya, and help you plan your trip with our recommendations on where to go, what to see, where to stay, and what to do.

List of Places to Visit in Webuye

There are numerous attractions in Webuye that cater to different interests, including culturally important attractions, natural attractions, hiking, biking routes, and more. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Webuye:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bunyala Museum: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the people of Bunyala, who are known for their unique customs, folk songs, dances, and crafts. The museum houses historical artifacts, traditional musical instruments, and artwork.
  • Webuye Panpaper Mills: This is one of the largest paper mills in East Africa, producing a range of paper products, including books, newspapers, and cardboard boxes. Visitors can take a tour of the factory and learn about the paper-making process.
  • Webuye Railway Station: This is a historical landmark that reflects the colonial-era architecture and design. It is still in use today and serves as an important transport hub for the town.
  • Kabula Hill Ruins: These ruins are believed to be the site of an ancient fortress of the Bukusu community. The site is surrounded by dense forest, making it a popular spot for hiking and exploring.

Natural Attractions of Webuye: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nzoia River: The Nzoia River is one of the major rivers in Western Kenya and a popular destination for fishing, boating, and picnicking. Visitors can also take a walk along the riverbanks and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Turkwel Gorge: This is a beautiful gorge that stretches over 55km and is carved by the Turkwel River. Visitors can go hiking, bird watching, or camping in the nearby areas.
  • Mount Elgon National Park: This park is located on the border of Kenya and Uganda and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can go hiking, bird watching, or take a guided tour of the park to explore its natural wonders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Webuye

Webuye has plenty of hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Nabuyole Falls: This is a scenic route that takes you through beautiful forests and pastures, and ends at a beautiful waterfall. The route is easy to moderate and takes about 2-3 hours to complete.
  • Kapkokony Hills: This is a challenging route that takes you up the hills, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The route is 10 km long and takes around 4 hours to complete.
  • Weeping Rock: This route takes you to a natural rock formation that appears to be weeping. The route is easy and takes around 1 hour to complete.

Best Things to Do in Webuye: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours that visitors can enjoy when in Webuye, including:

  • Community Tours: Visitors can take a tour of the local communities and learn about their culture, traditions, and way of life.
  • Paper Factory Tours: Visitors can take a tour of the paper factory to learn about the paper-making process and see how paper products are made.
  • River Adventures: Visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, and picnicking on the Nzoia River.
  • Hiking and Biking: Visitors can explore the beautiful landscape of Webuye by going on hikes and bike rides.

If you are planning a week-long trip to Webuye, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Webuye

Webuye offers a range of delicious dishes and local beverages that are a must-try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ugali: This is a staple food made from maize flour and water, often served with vegetables or meat.
  • Chapati: This is a flatbread made from wheat flour and is a popular breakfast or snack option.
  • Sukuma Wiki: This is a vegetable dish made from kale or collard greens, often served with ugali.
  • Nyama Choma: This is grilled meat, often served with a side of fries, ugali, or a salad.

Local Beverages

  • Busaa: This is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from maize and other grains.
  • Mursik: This is a sour milk beverage that is a popular drink among the Kalenjin community.

Where to Stay in Webuye: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Webuye has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Webuye Guesthouse: This is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of the town.
  • The Panorama Hotel: This is a mid-range hotel with clean and comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • Luyali Guesthouse: This is a guesthouse that offers basic but comfortable accommodation with a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Webuye: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Webuye offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementos. Some popular options include:

  • Bukusu handicrafts: These include woven baskets, pottery, and clothing, all made by the Bukusu community.
  • Paper products: Webuye is known for its paper mills, and visitors can buy a range of paper products, including notebooks, journals, and wrapping paper.
  • Local food products: Visitors can buy local spices, teas, and other food products to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Webuye

Webuye is a multicultural town with a diverse range of customs and traditions. Some important customs include:

  • Greeting: It is customary to greet locals with a handshake and a warm smile.
  • Respect for elders and authority: Respect for elders and authority figures is highly valued in the community.
  • Celebrations and festivals: The locals celebrate various festivals and events throughout the year, including the Bunyala festival, which celebrates the culture and traditions of the Bunyala people.

Best Time to Visit Webuye

The best time to visit Webuye is during the dry season, which runs from December to February and June to September. The weather is mild and pleasant during this time, and there is less rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Webuye

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Webuye:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Carry cash, as some places may not accept credit cards.
  • Use mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing to prevent mosquito bites.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people or places without seeking permission first.
  • Display affection in public, as it is not customary.
  • Use drugs or other illegal substances.
  • Ignore health and safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Webuye is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is important to take some precautions to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Webuye

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites to prevent malaria.
  • Avoid drinking tap water, and stick to bottled or filtered water.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings.

Health in Webuye

  • Make sure to get all the required vaccinations before travelling to Webuye.
  • Carry a first aid kit and any necessary medications.
  • Use sunscreen and protect your skin from the sun.
  • Take caution when consuming street food.

Getting to Webuye: Transportation Options and Logistics

Webuye is easily accessible by air, road, or rail. Here are some transportation options to consider when travelling to Webuye:

Airports Near Webuye

The nearest airport to Webuye is the Eldoret International Airport, which is located about 67 km away.

Bus Stations Near Webuye

  • Webuye Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Webuye, and buses from Nairobi, Kisumu, and other cities in Kenya stop here.
  • Bumala Bus Station: This is another bus station that is located about 22 km away from Webuye.

Other Options

  • Railway: Webuye has a railway station that is connected to Nairobi and other major cities in Kenya.
  • Taxi and Car Rentals: Visitors can hire taxis or rent cars to get around town.

How to Get Around in Webuye

Webuye is a relatively small town that can be easily explored on foot or by hiring a taxi or motorbike. Visitors can also hire bicycles or take matatus, which are mini-buses that operate on designated routes and are a popular mode of transportation in Kenya.

Where to Go Next from Webuye: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Webuye is located in a region of the country that offers several other attractions and destinations worth visiting. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Kakamega Forest: This is a beautiful forest reserve located about 25 km from Webuye, and is home to a variety of wildlife and bird species.
  • The Saiwa Swamp National Park: This park is located about 77 km away from Webuye, and is home to the rare sitatunga antelope.
  • Lake Victoria: This is the largest lake in Africa and is located about 100 km away from Webuye. Visitors can enjoy fishing, boat rides or simply enjoy the scenic views.

Whether you are a nature lover or history enthusiast, Webuye has something for everyone.
