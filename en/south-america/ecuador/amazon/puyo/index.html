A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Puyo in Ecuador, South America

Located in the Amazon region of Ecuador, Puyo is a vibrant and charming city that offers tourists an array of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking/biking routes, mouth-watering cuisine, and unique local customs. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you explore the best things to see and do in Puyo, where to stay, what to buy, the do’s and don’ts, safety and health measures, transportation options, nearby destinations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Puyo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Housed inside a beautiful colonial building, the Alfonso Jaramillo Museum showcases the history and traditions of the indigenous tribes located in the Amazon region. Moreover, travelers can visit the nearby biblical museums and churches like the Santuario de la Virgen del Rosario, the Basílica de la Merced and the Iglesia de la Inmaculada Concepción.

Natural Attractions of Puyo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Puyo River is undoubtedly one of the most stunning natural attractions in the city. Brave visitors can explore it with white-water rafting, kayaking, and canoeing tours. Similarly, hikes, treks, and bike rides to nearby scenic viewpoints such as Bellavista, Mera, and the Salcedo lookout offer a unique opportunity to immerse travelers into the Amazon forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puyo

Puyo is home to various hiking/biking routes, including the Pailón del Diablo, Bellavista, and the beautiful waterfall of Rio Verde. These routes offer travelers a chance to experience the beauty of the Amazon rainforest first-hand.

Best Things to Do in Puyo: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and water sports, Puyo offers various fun activities like zip-lining tours, horse riding, bird watching, and chocolate tasting. Furthermore, Amazonian cultural show tours and night walks to nearby rivers with fireflies abound for the adventurous traveler.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puyo

  • Day 1: Explore the colonial streets of Puyo and visit the Alfonso Jaramillo Museum and nearby churches.
  • Day 2-4: Participate in outdoor activities such as white-water rafting, kayaking, or hiking/biking to get the best views of the Amazon rainforest.
  • Day 5: Spend a relaxing day exploring the Puyo River and trying traditional local food.
  • Day 6: Take a cultural tour and meet the indigenous tribes of the Amazon.
  • Day 7: Visit the Salcedo, enjoy the local coffee, and say goodbye to Puyo.

Food and Drink Scene of Puyo

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors are encouraged to try traditional dishes like Maito, a fish that is wrapped in leaves and roasted, or Yuca with Mote, a dish made of boiled yuca and corn. Similarly, Puyo is known for producing some of the best Amazonian coffee and chocolate that travelers will want to taste.

Local Beverages

Aguardiente, a strong spirit distilled from sugar cane, and chicha, a fermented beverage made from cassava or maize, are good options to try.

Where to Stay in Puyo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puyo has various accommodation options, including low-budget hostels like Hostel Rubi or Hostal Santa Fe, guesthouses and mid-range hotels such as Hotel Palma Real Puyo.

What to Buy in Puyo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers can take home various souvenirs, including traditional Amazonian balsa wood carvings, handmade jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Puyo

The people of Puyo have a rich cultural heritage and traditional customs, including rituals, festivals, and ceremonies like the Festival de la Fruta y de las Flores and the Festival de la Fundación de Puyo. Additionally, locals love to dance and enjoy festivities that celebrate their cultural roots.

Best Time to Visit Puyo

The best time to visit Puyo is during the rainy season from December to June when the Amazon rainforest is brimming with life and visitors can experience the beauty of the flora and fauna of the jungle.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puyo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the indigenous people.
  • Explore the Puyo River with a local guide.
  • Visit nearby indigenous communities and learn about their unique way of life.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Take photographs without permission or interfere with local customs or traditions.
  • Walk alone at night in isolated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puyo

Travelers should take precautions while exploring the Amazon rainforest and avoid isolated areas, late-night wandering, or taking risks with wildlife.

Health in Puyo

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated against tropical diseases, especially Yellow Fever or Malaria, before going to Puyo. Pack insect repellent, sun-tan lotion, and plenty of water for any tour around the city.

Getting to Puyo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puyo

The closest airport to Puyo is the Mera Airport, which is approximately 6.3 km away and serves various domestic airlines that can be booked easily online.

Bus Stations Near Puyo

There are several daily buses linking Quito, Tena and Baños to Puyo.

Other Options

Travelers renting a car or hiring a taxi can opt for a scenic Amazonian road trip to Puyo, following one of the country’s newest highways between Tena and Puyo.

How to Get Around in Puyo

Taxis, buses, and bikes are the most popular ways to travel around Puyo city. Furthermore, car rentals are available for tourists looking to venture out to the nearby countryside.

Where to Go Next from Puyo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Given its location in the heart of Amazon, Puyo offers access to nearby attractions and destinations like Tena, Baños, and Misahualli and to the Yasuni National Park, where travelers can explore the Amazon at its wildest.

Conclusion

Puyo is a city waiting to be explored, with its stunning natural wonders, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming people. This guide offers travel enthusiasts all the necessary information to choose Puyo, Ecuador, for their next adventure destination.
