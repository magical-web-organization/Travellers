Introduction to Sangay National Park

Sangay National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site situated in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, South America. The park is located in the provinces of Chimborazo, Morona Santiago, and Tungurahua, and is one of the most diverse national parks in the world. Sangay National Park is home to three massive peaks and diverse ecosystems that range from the highlands to tropical forests.

List of Places to Visit in Sangay National Park

Sangay National Park has a plethora of attractions for visitors, ranging from culturally significant locations to natural wonders. Here are a few highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sibambe: Sibambe is a small town in Chimborazo province, which is essential to the Ecuadorian history. This is where some of the last battles of the Ecuadorian War of Independence were fought in 1822, including the Battle of Sibambe.
  • Pumapungo Archaeological Site: Pumapungo is an ancient Incan site in Cuenca, Ecuador. This site was once a military fortress and has now been converted into an open-air museum.

Natural Attractions of Sangay National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tungurahua Volcano: Tungurahua is an active volcano located in Sangay National Park. Visitors can hike up to the crater and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.
  • Amazon Rainforest: The Amazon Rainforest is easy to access from the park. A boat ride down the Pastaza River is an excellent way to explore the Amazon.
  • El Altar: El Altar is a must-see location in Sangay National Park. El Altar is a volcanic mountain that has several peaks and beautiful turquoise lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sangay National Park

Sangay National Park has some of the most scenic hiking routes in the world. Visitors can hike up to the Tungurahua volcano or explore the trails around the Amazon Rainforest. The park also has biking trails that visitors can make use of.

Best Things to Do in Sangay National Park: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: Rafting in the Pastaza River is an excellent way to explore the Amazon. The river features Class III and Class IV rapids.
  • Canyoning: Canyoning is an adrenaline-pumping adventure where visitors hike, rappel, and swim through the river. The waterfalls in Sangay National Park provide an ideal canyoning experience.
  • Bird Watching: Sangay National Park has over 500 species of birds, including the Andean condor and the giant hummingbird.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sangay National Park

Day 1: Visit the Cuenca Cathedral and Pumapungo archaeological site Day 2: Hike up the Tungurahua volcano and observe the scenic views Day 3: Take a boat trip down the Amazon River and explore the rainforest Day 4: Go canyoning at one of the many waterfalls in the park Day 5: Take a bike ride through Sangay National Park Day 6: Explore the El Altar mountain and lakes Day 7: White water rafting in the Pastaza River

Food and Drink Scene of Sangay National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Locro: Locro is a traditional Ecuadorian soup that contains potatoes, cheese, and avocado. It is typically served with roasted corn on top.
  • Ceviche: Ceviche is a popular seafood dish served throughout Ecuador. The dish features raw seafood that is cured in citrus juices and flavoured with chili peppers and spices.
  • Empanadas: Empanadas are stuffed pastries typically filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: Chicha is a traditional Ecuadorian beverage made from fermented maize.
  • Canelazo: Canelazo is a warm, spiced alcoholic beverage made with aguardiente, sugar, and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Sangay National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sangay National Park has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some options:

  • Hacienda Manteles: Hacienda Manteles is a beautiful guesthouse located in the shadow of the Tungurahua volcano. The guesthouse features a restaurant and spa treatments.
  • Hostal Chimborazo: Hostal Chimborazo is a hostel located in the heart of Riobamba that features a terrace and shared lounge.
  • La Quinta Colorada: La Quinta Colorada is a hotel located in the town of Patate that features a restaurant and garden.

What to Buy in Sangay National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Sangay National Park can purchase local handicrafts, such as textiles and pottery. In particular, the town of Gualaceo is famous for its textiles. Visitors can also purchase coffee beans, chocolate, and other local produce.

Local Culture and Customs of Sangay National Park

The people of Sangay National Park are proud of their customs and rich cultural history. Visitors can expect to see traditional costumes, music, and dance performances. The Kichwa indigenous people have their languages, customs, and traditions still evident in the park. Foreigners should respect their traditions when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Sangay National Park

The best time to visit Sangay National Park is during the dry season from June to November. This is the ideal time for hiking or biking in the park. The wet season from December to May can make trails challenging, though rainforests are at their best at this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sangay National Park

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Hire a guide when hiking.
  • Be prepared for all weather types, especially in the rainy season.
  • Take adequate medical precautions when visiting.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage the environment.
  • Do not interfere with local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sangay National Park

Sangay National Park is safe to visit, but visitors should take precautions in the outdoor activities. Visitors should not leave their belongings unattended in public areas.

Health in Sangay National Park

Visitors to Sangay National Park should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue and malaria. Visitors should also ensure they have the appropriate vaccinations for the country. It is recommended to consult your physician beforehand.

Getting to Sangay National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sangay National Park

The nearest airport to Sangay National Park is Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito, Ecuador. From there, visitors can hire a car or take a bus to Riobamba.

Bus Stations Near Sangay National Park

The city of Riobamba is a central hub in the area, and there are many buses leaving from its terminal to various destinations at Sangay National Park.

Other Options

Private car and bike rentals are available in Riobamba and other nearby towns, allowing for greater freedom in exploring the area.

How to Get Around in Sangay National Park

Hiking and biking are the most popular methods of transportation within Sangay National Park. Visitors can also hire a tour or private vehicle to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Sangay National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sangay National Park is near several other popular tourist destinations in Ecuador, including the Galapagos Islands and Quito.

Conclusion

Sangay National Park is a must-visit destination for every nature lover. From its scenic hiking routes to the cultural significance to its food scene, there is plenty to see and explore. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip to Sangay National Park.
