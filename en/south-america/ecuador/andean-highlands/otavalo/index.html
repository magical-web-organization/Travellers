Introduction to Otavalo

Otavalo is a charming city nestled in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, about two hours north of Quito, the capital city. Otavalo is famous for its vibrant marketplace, where locals and tourists alike can find an array of authentic handmade textiles, artisan crafts, and unique souvenirs. However, there’s much more to Otavalo than just its famous market. The town is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery, has a rich cultural heritage, and offers a range of exciting outdoor activities suitable for all levels of adventurers. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying the laid-back vibe of a small Andean town, Otavalo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Otavalo

Otavalo has a rich array of tourist attractions, from museums and historical monuments to beautiful natural parks and hiking trails. Here are some of the must-visit places in Otavalo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museo de Arte Precolombino – A museum that showcases pre-Columbian art and artifacts
  2. Museo y Fábrica de Ponchos “Otavalo” – A traditional textile museum and factory where you can learn about the production of high-quality fabrics and garments
  3. Iglesia de San Francisco – A beautiful church built in the 17th century that houses some stunning works of colonial art
  4. Parque Cóndor – A bird sanctuary that lets you see some of the most majestic birds of prey up close.

Natural Attractions of Otavalo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Laguna de Cuicocha – A crater lake that offers stunning views and hiking trails around the water
  2. Cotacachi Cayapas Ecological Reserve – A natural park that boasts unique ecosystems and diverse flora and fauna
  3. Peguche Waterfall – A stunning waterfall located in the village of Peguche, accessible via a beautiful hiking trail
  4. Mount Imbabura – A popular hiking destination for adventure lovers that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Otavalo

Otavalo has some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ecuador, and these are some of the most popular ones:

  1. La Lechera – A breathtaking trail that starts in the town of Otavalo and takes you through scenic farmlands, forests, and hills
  2. Mojanda Volcanic Complex – A popular hiking destination that offers multiple trails with stunning views of the surrounding landscape
  3. El Angel Ecological Reserve – A unique ecosystem with plenty of high-altitude wetlands and unique flora and fauna
  4. Cuicocha Lake – A beautiful crater lake surrounded by hiking trails suitable for all levels of hiking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Otavalo: Activities and Tours

Otavalo is a town full of possibilities, with options for all types of visitors. Here are some things to do while in Otavalo:

  1. Tour Otavalo Market – visit the sprawling market and shop for handmade textiles, artisan crafts, and souvenirs from Otavalo and the surrounding towns.
  2. Hike to Mojanda Lakes – one day trek to see the beautiful semi-active volcano and the picture-perfect twin lakes that surround it.
  3. Visit Cuicocha lake – a full day trip that first entails hiking the ridge of the dormant volcano to see up close the surrounding landscapes before then descending to explore the famed crater lake located at the base.
  4. Attend Inti Raymi – an annual festival held on the summer solstice that celebrates the indigenous heritage of Otavalo and the surrounding towns – gives the visitor a spectacle of music, dance, and art.

Week-Long Itinerary for Otavalo

Here’s an itinerary for a week’s visit to Otavalo:

Day 1: Arrival and a tour of Otavalo market
Day 2: Visit La Lechera trail for a beautiful hike
Day 3: Explore more trails on the Mojanda volcano
Day 4: Take a day trip to Cotacachi for leather shopping and birdwatching
Day 5: Visit the Pachaquík Museum for an immersion into the local history and culture
Day 6: Visit El Angel Ecological Reserve and discover its unique high-altitude ecosystems
Day 7: Departure and travel back to Quito

Food and Drink Scene of Otavalo

When it comes to food and drinks, Otavalo has a lot to offer. Here are some dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hornado – A traditional Ecuadorian dish that involves roasted pork served with potatoes and aji
  2. Locro de Papas – A delicious, hearty soup made with potatoes, cheese, avocados, and corn
  3. Fanesca – A soup made of grains and pulses that is typically consumed during Holy Week in Ecuador
  4. Empanadas – A pastry-like dish that can be filled with a range of vegetables, meats, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Chicha – A fermented corn drink that is often described as sour and sweet at the same time
  2. Horchata – A light beverage made with ground almonds, rice, sugar, and cinnamon
  3. Canelazo – A hot alcoholic drink that is often consumed during the holiday season, made with aguardiente, cinnamon, cloves, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Otavalo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Otavalo has plenty of options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels. Here are some of the best accommodations in Otavalo:

  1. Hostería Cabanas del Lago – A lakeside hotel that boasts stunning views and comfortable suites for guests.
  2. La Luna Guesthouse – A relaxed budget guesthouse in the town that offers clean rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  3. Hacienda Pinsaqui – A historic hotel that is stunningly beautiful, beautiful gardens, and all reports of excellent service.
  4. Hostal Otavalos Inn – A charming hostel in the center of town, that has clean rooms, a rooftop terrace, and occasional cooking classes.

What to Buy in Otavalo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Otavalo is famous for its handicrafts and textiles, making it the ideal place to shop for souvenirs. Here are some of the must-buy items when in Otavalo:

  1. Ponchos – traditional woolen garments made by Otavaleños, typically made from alpaca, llama, or sheep wool.
  2. Handwoven Textiles – curtains, tablecloths, shawls, and blankets, handwoven by artisans in the region.
  3. Jewelry – silver and gold pieces of jewelry that are unique to the Otavalo style.
  4. Ceramic and Woodwork – handmade earthenware, and carved wooden products, ideal for kitchen décor items and trinkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Otavalo

The Otavaleños are proud of their heritage and deeply respect their indigenous traditions. The town has a unique blend of Andean, Spanish, and indigenous cultures, which is reflected in its art, food, and music. One of the customs of Otavalo is the “Taita Yahuarcocha” festival held annually during February. The town’s people gather at the Yahuarcocha lagoon to celebrate a new agricultural year with traditional dances, masquerades, and drinking. It is the perfect opportunity to immerse in their culture and witness how integral they hold these customs to their daily lives.

Best Time to Visit Otavalo

The best time to visit Otavalo is during the dry season, which typically runs from June to September. The weather is pleasant, with clear skies, moderate temperatures, and a low chance of rain. As a result, the conditions are perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing, including hiking and exploring the local trails. However, visitors should note that the town’s most famous market takes place every Saturday, so if shopping is appearing on your itinerary, Saturday is your best bet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Otavalo

Here are some tips for travellers to ensure they respect the local customs of Otavalo:

Do:

  1. Always ask before taking photographs of people or their shops, and if deemed appropriate, offer compensation for such pictures.
  2. Visit the Pronaca/Andean Market to sample the region’s authentic food and beverages.
  3. Attempt to learn basic words and phrases in the indigenous languages like ‘Kichuna’ to better relate to the locals.
  4. Ask before touching some artefacts or traditional clothing, and seek permission before trying photographs with individuals dressed in their traditional garb.

Don’t:

  1. Avoid discussing politics or religion, as these conversations can lead to tense and uncomfortable situations easily.
  2. Don’t throw trash on the street or in public spaces. A clean and safe environment is something the Otavaloanos take seriously.
  3. Don’t ignore the Andean cultural dress code, be respectful, and maybe even try it out for fun when you can.
  4. Don’t question their customs and beliefs. If you don’t understand something, simply ask with curiosity, and they would be glad to enlighten you in a respectful way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Otavalo

Otavalo has a reputation as a safe town, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should still exercise caution and keep an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded areas like the Otavalo market or bus terminals. It’s always essential to lock away valuables and passports in hotel safes or secured areas while out on day trips or exploring the trails.

Health in Otavalo

Before travelling to Otavalo, it’s important to have proper travel vaccinations and malaria prophylaxis up to date. The town is at high altitude, where altitude sickness could affect travellers going above 2,500 meters. Visitors planning one of the hiking routes should plan accordingly and seek medical advice beforehand. The town’s clinics and hospitals have limited access to equipment and facilities, with the nearest hospital located in Quito, so medical issues requiring attention may need immediate evacuation.

Getting to Otavalo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Otavalo is easily accessible from Quito, Ecuador’s capital city, and other nearby towns and cities. Here are some transportation options available for visitors.

Airports Near Otavalo

The nearest airport to Otavalo is the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito, about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Otavalo

Otavalo has a central bus terminal that provides services to many of the surrounding towns, cities, and trails. Buses also leave from central Quito from the northern bus terminal and take around two hours to reach Otavalo, with frequent departures throughout the day.

Other Options

Hiring a private driver or taking tour buses is a comfortable and convenient way to travel around Otavalo and the surrounding areas, and explore Ecuador.

How to Get Around in Otavalo

Otavalo is a small town that is straightforward to navigate by foot. For more extended distances, travellers can take a taxi, which is readily available throughout the town. If you prefer to cycle, you can rent a bicycle and explore the town and its trails at your leisure.

Where to Go Next from Otavalo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some ideal destinations near Otavalo worth considering:

  1. Cotopaxi National Park – A unique geographical region that houses one of the highest active volcanoes globally, with hikes to the rim and down to the Limpiopungo lagoon.
  2. Mindo Cloud Forest – A rare area that has extremely humid conditions and a wide range of avifauna species to observe.
  3. Papallacta Hot Springs – Known for its hot pools and waters rich in sulphate minerals, perfect for respite and relaxation.
  4. The Equator – marking the point where the earth’s equator crosses the country, visitors can visit the monument and have one foot in the southern hemisphere and the other in the northern hemisphere.

Conclusion

Otavalo is a charming town in Ecuador that offers a unique blend of Andean culture, Spanish colonial history, and stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re looking to shop in the famous market, hike one of the scenic trails, or immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions, Otavalo has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide as your companion, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this vibrant and beautiful town.
