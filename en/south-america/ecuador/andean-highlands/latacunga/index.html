Latacunga, located in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, is a city with a rich history and culture, surrounded by beautiful landscapes. Known as the “city of the slopes” due to its location on the slopes of the Cotopaxi volcano, Latacunga is a popular destination for local and international tourists. This travel guide aims to provide all the necessary information about Latacunga to help you plan your trip. So, let’s get started!

Introduction to Latacunga

Latacunga is the capital city of the Cotopaxi province, located about 90 kilometers (56 miles) south of Quito. With a population of around 100,000, it is a bustling and lively city known for its vibrant markets, colonial buildings, and colorful fiestas.

List of Places to Visit in Latacunga

If you are planning to visit Latacunga, here is a list of places to not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Carmen: This museum displays religious and cultural artifacts, including paintings, sculptures, and carvings.
  • Palacio de los Marqueses de Salinas: A beautiful colonial building that is now a cultural center, with exhibits and events.
  • Iglesia Santo Domingo: A 17th-century church with beautiful architecture and religious art.

Natural Attractions of Latacunga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cotopaxi National Park: One of the most popular national parks in Ecuador, known for its hiking trails and stunning views of the Cotopaxi volcano.
  • Quilotoa: A stunning crater lake located about 3 hours from Latacunga, with bicycle rentals available for a picturesque ride around the rim.
  • La Ciénega: A natural reserve located just outside of Latacunga, with hiking paths and beautiful landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Latacunga

There are several hiking and biking routes around Latacunga that are worth exploring, including:

  • Cerro Puntas: A popular hike with beautiful views of the city and surrounding Andean landscapes.
  • Yambo Lagoon: A challenging hike with stunning views of the nearby Andean peaks.
  • Chasqui Route: A historic path used by the Inca messengers, with stunning views of the Andean valleys and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Latacunga: Activities and Tours

Some of the best activities and tours to do in Latacunga include:

  • Attend a Festival: Latacunga is known for its colorful and lively festivals, such as the famous Mama Negra festival.
  • Visit a Market: Latacunga has several markets selling traditional Ecuadorian products, including food, clothing, and handicrafts.
  • Horseback Riding Tours: Experience the Andean landscapes on horseback with guided tours available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Latacunga

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Latacunga:

Day 1: Visit the Museo del Carmen and Palacio de los Marqueses de Salinas
Day 2: Hike Cerro Puntas
Day 3: Take a day trip to Quilotoa
Day 4: Attend a local market
Day 5: Visit the Iglesia Santo Domingo and Cotopaxi National Park
Day 6: Go horseback riding
Day 7: Explore La Ciénega

Food and Drink Scene of Latacunga

Ecuador is known for its delicious food, and Latacunga is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hornado: Spit-roasted pork served with potatoes, corn, and salad.
  • Locro de Papas: A hearty potato soup with cheese, avocado, and toasted corn.
  • Empanadas de Viento: Fried empanadas filled with cheese and served with sugar on top.

Local Beverages

  • Canelazo: A sweet and spicy warm cocktail made with aguardiente, cinnamon, and fruit juice.
  • Chicha: A traditional fermented corn drink.

Where to Stay in Latacunga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Latacunga, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

  • La Posada del Viajero: A cozy and affordable guesthouse located in the historic center of Latacunga.
  • Hacienda La Cienega: A beautifully restored colonial hacienda located just outside of Latacunga.
  • Hotel Andalucia: A modern and comfortable hotel located in the center of Latacunga.

What to Buy in Latacunga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring back from Latacunga include:

  • Handicrafts: Beautifully woven textiles, pottery, and wood carvings.
  • Chocolate: Ecuador is known for its delicious chocolate, and Latacunga has several local chocolate shops.
  • Aguardiente: A strong liquor made from sugarcane, popular in Ecuador.

Local Culture and Customs of Latacunga

Latacunga is a culturally rich city with several unique customs and traditions. Some important things to keep in mind while visiting include:

  • Respect Cultural Festivals: Latacunga is known for its famous Mama Negra festival, which celebrates the mixing of indigenous and Spanish cultures.
  • Try Local Foods: Ecuador is known for its delicious cuisine, so make sure to try some traditional dishes while visiting Latacunga.
  • Learn Some Spanish: Although many locals speak some English, it is always appreciated to try and communicate in Spanish.

Best Time to Visit Latacunga

The best time to visit Latacunga is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. This is the best time to enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking, without the risk of rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Latacunga

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Latacunga:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Try Local Foods: Ecuadorian cuisine is delicious, so make sure to try some local dishes.
  • Bring Warm Clothing: Although Latacunga is located near the equator, it can get cold due to its high altitude.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate Altitude Sickness: Latacunga is located at a high altitude, so make sure to take necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Take Unlicensed Taxis: Only take licensed taxis for safety reasons.
  • Costume Appropriately: Avoid wearing revealing clothing in public places.

Getting to Latacunga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Latacunga is easily accessible by air and bus, and there are several transportation options available.

Airports Near Latacunga

The closest airport to Latacunga is the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito, which is about 90 kilometers (56 miles) away.

Bus Stations Near Latacunga

There are several bus stations in Latacunga, including the Terminal Terrestre de Latacunga and Terminal Terrestre La Estación.

Other Options

Taxi and private shuttle services are also available, and some hotels offer airport pick-up and drop-off services.

How to Get Around in Latacunga

The best way to get around in Latacunga is by taxi or public transportation, such as buses or trolleys. Walking and biking are also popular options, depending on your destination.

Where to Go Next from Latacunga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Banos: A popular tourist destination known for its hot springs and adventure activities, located about 2 hours from Latacunga.
  • Mindo: A beautiful cloud forest located about 3 hours from Latacunga, known for birdwatching and ecotourism.
  • Quito: The capital city of Ecuador, located about 90 kilometers (56 miles) from Latacunga.

Conclusion
