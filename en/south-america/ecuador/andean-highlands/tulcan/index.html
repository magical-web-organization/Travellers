A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Tulcán, Ecuador

Nestled in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, Tulcán is a town full of culture, history, and stunning natural beauty. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to discover everything Tulcán has to offer, from its culturally significant attractions to its incredible outdoor adventures.

Introduction to Tulcán

Tulcán is located in the province of Carchi, just a few kilometers from the Colombia-Ecuador border. The town is known for its beautifully manicured gardens, stunning cathedrals, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Tulcán

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Tulcán - Discover the history of Tulcán through artifacts dating back to pre-Columbian times to modern-day.

Las Lajas Sanctuary - This beautiful sanctuary was built in the early 1900s and features stunning neogothic architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tulcán: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cayambe Coca Ecological Reserve - This reserve offers some of the most spectacular views of glaciers, waterfalls and lakes and is perfect for outdoor activities.

Jardín Botánico de Tulcán - Known for its living green sculptures, created out of bushes and trees, that depict Ecuadorian myths and legends. It is a popular spot for photo enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tulcán

La Cascarita Trail - This trail takes you through the highlands of the Carchi Province and offers some of the most stunning views of the Andes mountains.

Pan-American Highway - This highway runs through Tulcán and offers a range of scenic views of the Carchi province where the town is situated.

Best Things to Do in Tulcán: Activities and Tours

Visit the local markets - Tulcan locals are well-known for their traditional weaving using alpaca wool. You can also find delicious local delicacies and freshly grown produce at the markets.

Take a guided city tour - Learn about the history, culture and beauty of Tulcán with a local guide who will take you to all the must-see spots and hidden gems.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tulcán

Day 1-2: Visit the town’s museums and historical monuments.

Day 3-4: Explore the outdoor activities in the area, such as the Cajambe Coca Ecological Reserve and La Cascarita Trail.

Day 5-6: Take a guided city tour and visit the local markets for some authentic experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Tulcán

Must-Try Dishes

Locro de papas - an Andean potato soup that is served with avocado, cheese, and toasted corn.

Cuy - an Ecuadorian delicacy consisting of roasted or fried guinea pig.

Local Beverages

Canelazo - a warm cocktail made of cinnamon, orange juice, and sugar cane alcohol.

Pájaro Azul - a local blue-coloured alcoholic beverage made from aguardiente and curaçao.

Where to Stay in Tulcán: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hacienda Full Moon - Located 10km from Tulcán, this hacienda offers a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle and bustle.

Hotel Palacio Imperial - This hotel is located in the heart of Tulcán and is walking distance from many of the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Tulcán: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alpaca wool products - Tulcán is famous for its traditional weaving using alpaca wool. You could bring home high-quality sweaters, hats, and scarves.

Local handicrafts - You can also find unique and authentic handicrafts made by local artisans in Tulcán.

Local Culture and Customs of Tulcán

Tulcán is a melting pot of indigenous and Spanish cultures. Locals still practice their ancient traditions with pride, including their dance and music styles. Be respectful and courteous when exploring the town and interacting with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Tulcán

The best time to visit Tulcán is between June and October when the weather is dry and cool, which makes exploring easier and more comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tulcán

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Learn a few Spanish phrases.

Don’t:

  • Carry large amounts of cash on you.

  • Leave your belongings unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tulcán

Tulcán is a safe town for visitors. However, it is always important to remain aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Tulcán

Carry your medical essentials with you such as mosquito repellent and sunscreen, to prevent getting sunburned, and insect bites. Don’t forget to stay hydrated and dress appropriately.

Getting to Tulcán: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tulcán

The Antonio Nariño Airport is the closest airport to Tulcán.

Bus Stations Near Tulcán

The bus terminal in Tulcán has frequent services to other parts of the country such as Quito, Otavalo, and Ibarra.

Other Options

Renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Tulcán

The best way to get around Tulcán is on foot or by taxi. Taxis are affordable and convenient, but always negotiate the fare beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Tulcán: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Otavalo - a bustling town that is home to Ecuador’s largest indigenous market.

Baños de Agua Santa - a small town known for its hot springs, waterfalls, and adventure activities such as mountain biking and white-water rafting.

Conclusion

Tulcán is a hidden gem, full of stunning beauty, culture, and friendly locals. From historical landmarks to outdoor adventures to authentic local experiences, there’s something for everyone in Tulcán. Visit this Andean Highlands town, and you won’t be disappointed.
