Santiago de Bolivar is a hidden gem in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, South America. With its culturally vibrant museums and historical sites, stunning natural landscapes, and a food and drink scene that will leave your taste buds buzzing, there is no shortage of reasons to visit Santiago de Bolivar. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best of what Santiago de Bolivar has to offer.

Introduction to Santiago de Bolivar

Santiago de Bolivar is a small town perched high in the Andean range, offering visitors a stunning insight into traditional Ecuadorian life. Named after Simon Bolivar, a hero of the South American independence movement, Santiago de Bolivar has a rich history and culture that is felt in every corner of the town. Visitors will be impressed by the friendliness of the locals, the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, and the abundance of activities and experiences available.

List of Places to Visit in Santiago de Bolivar

Whether you’re looking to learn about the culture and history of Santiago de Bolivar or immerse yourself in its natural beauty, there’s plenty to see and do in this unique town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Santiago de Bolivar is packed with cultural and historical sites, making it the perfect destination for history buffs.

  • Casa Museo Simon Bolivar: This museum is a tribute to the life of Simon Bolivar and is located in the house where Bolivar stayed during his time in Santiago de Bolivar.

  • Casa de la Cultura Ecuatoriana: This cultural center offers a range of exhibitions and performances and is dedicated to promoting traditional Ecuadorian arts and crafts.

  • Iglesia de Santiago: This charming church was built in the seventeenth century and features stunning architecture and artwork.

Natural Attractions of Santiago de Bolivar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Santiago de Bolivar will not disappoint. From hiking to biking and even horseback riding, there are plenty of ways to explore the natural beauty of this town.

  • Chimborazo National Reserve: Explore one of the most impressive natural wonders in the area, Chimborazo National Reserve. You can hike, birdwatch, and even camp in this stunning reserve.

  • Cuatro Esquinas: This viewpoint offers breathtaking panoramic views of Santiago de Bolivar and the surrounding mountains.

  • Laguna de Colta: This stunning lagoon is a peaceful retreat and the perfect place to go for a swim, boat ride, or to enjoy a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santiago de Bolivar

For those who love to hike or bike, Santiago de Bolivar has plenty to offer. From rugged mountain trails to gentle walks through the countryside, there is something for everyone.

  • The Inca Trail: This historical trail was once used by the Incas to traverse the Andes and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • El Altar: A day hike to El Altar is a must for those who enjoy environmental exploration. Follow the trail through an ecological reserve, cross rivers and climb to an altitude of over 5,000 meters to witness one of the most stunning views in the area.

  • Biking routes: The roads are not too busy, making them ideal for exploring the area by bike. There is a local tour operator who can tailor rides to your level of skill and experience.

Best Things to Do in Santiago de Bolivar: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a day of adventure or a more laidback experience, Santiago de Bolivar has plenty of activities that will keep you entertained.

  • Artisan Market: Browse through handmade artisan crafts at the local market, perfect for picking up a unique souvenir of your trip.

  • Horse Trekking: Experience the Andes from a different perspective, by taking a horse trekking tour in Santiago de Bolivar.

  • Paragliding: Feel the wind in your hair and enjoy stunning views of the Andes with a paragliding adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santiago de Bolivar

If you’re looking for a week-long experience in Santiago de Bolivar, here is an itinerary that will take you through the best of what the town has to offer.

  • Day 1-2: Visit the Museums and Churches of Santiago de Bolivar and take a guided hike to Cuatro Esquinas.

  • Day 3-4: Explore the natural beauty of Santiago de Bolivar with a trip to Chimborazo National Reserve and Laguna de Colta.

  • Day 5-6: Take a bike or horse ride through the Andes and visit artisan markets and other local attractions.

  • Day 7: Take a day to relax and enjoy the local cuisine of Santiago de Bolivar.

Food and Drink Scene of Santiago de Bolivar

Ecuador has a diverse and impressive food culture, and Santiago de Bolivar is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you won’t want to miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fanesca: A traditional Ecuadorian dish made with grains and beans, served with dried cod and served around Easter time.

  • Cholas: A regional specialty, Cholas are pork dishes cooked over firewood and served with potatoes and mote.

  • Espumilla: a sweet dessert made with whipped egg whites and fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Canelazo: A warm drink made from cinnamon, sugar, and aguardiente, perfect for sipping on chilly Andean evenings.

  • Chicha: A traditional Ecuadorian beverage made from corn, hot and alcoholic

  • Coffee : Ecuador is known for the cultivation of high-quality coffee, so having a cup of local coffee in a cafe or farmer’s market is a must.

Where to Stay in Santiago de Bolivar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santiago de Bolivar has a range of accommodation options for every budget, from luxury hotels to affordable hostels and guest houses.

  • Hotel Doris Maria: A mid-range hotel with beautiful, clean rooms, and useful amenities.

  • Hostal Santa Clara: Smaller, more affordable hostel with a friendly atmosphere and welcoming owners

  • Guest House El Ranchito: Located in the peaceful countryside of the Andes, this guest house is a family-run business; they serve you traditional meals from their farm and a delightful stay.

What to Buy in Santiago de Bolivar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Santiago de Bolivar is a haven for artisanal crafts, so it’s the perfect place to buy souvenirs or bring home a piece of traditional Ecuadorian culture.

  • Shawls and Ponchos: Handwoven by local artisans using traditional techniques

  • Handmade Jewelry: From elegant silver necklaces to intricate beaded bracelets, there is plenty of locally made jewelry to choose from.

  • Leather products: Get your hands on expertly crafted cowhide products, such as guitar cases, belts, and wallets.

Local Culture and Customs of Santiago de Bolivar

Ecuador has a rich and intricate culture, and Santiago de Bolivar is no exception. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting.

  • Family is hugely important: Visitors will notice that family comes first in the local culture, so expect to be warmly welcomed into this close-knit community.

  • Religious holidays: Santiago de Bolivar is a religious community, so visitors should be aware that local customs and traditions may differ from what they are used to.

Best Time to Visit Santiago de Bolivar

Santiago de Bolivar is enjoyable any time of the year, but the dry season (June - September) offers the best weather for outdoor activities. However, visitors should be aware that temperatures can drop significantly at night, even during the dry season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santiago de Bolivar

It’s essential to know the local customs when visiting Santiago de Bolivar to ensure a positive experience for everyone.

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish before you go

  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals

  • Respect local customs and dress codes when visiting religious sites

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture by littering or engaging in behavior that is considered disrespectful

  • Give money to beggars

Safety and Health

Santiago de Bolivar is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure they have a pleasant trip.

Safety in Santiago de Bolivar

  • Travel with a group or guide when traveling off the beaten path

  • Avoid traveling alone after dark

Health in Santiago de Bolivar

  • High altitude: Santiago de Bolivar is over 3,000 meters above sea level, so visitors may experience altitude sickness. It is recommended to stay hydrated and balmy during your stay.

  • Food and drink: Visitors should take care when eating and drinking to avoid stomach upset. Be cautious of street food vendors and only drink bottled water.

Getting to Santiago de Bolivar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Santiago de Bolivar is relatively isolated, but visitors have several transportation options to get there.

Airports Near Santiago de Bolivar

The closest airport to Santiago de Bolivar is Mariscal Lamar International Airport in Cuenca, although visitors can fly into Quito or Guayaquil airports and then travel by bus to Santiago de Bolivar.

Bus Stations Near Santiago de Bolivar

Santiago de Bolivar has a bus station located near the town center, providing transportation to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

There are several taxi and car rental services available in Cuenca if visitors prefer a more private mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Santiago de Bolivar

The best way to get around Santiago de Bolivar is by foot, enjoying the compacted and friendly town much like the locals.

Where to Go Next from Santiago de Bolivar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cuenca: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Cuenca is a colonial city nearby Santiago de Bolivar with beautiful architecture, cobbled streets, and picturesque plazas.

  • Baños: Head to Baños for some spectacular waterfalls, adventure activities, and paradisiacal views.

Conclusion

Santiago de Bolivar is a unique destination that perfectly blends Ecuadorian culture and natural beauty. Visitors can immerse themselves in traditional Ecuadorian life, explore the stunning Andean Mountains, and enjoy a range of activities and cultural experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip and experience Santiago de Bolivar to the fullest.
