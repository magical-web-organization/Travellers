Introduction to Tababela

Tababela is a small mountain town situated in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, South America. The town is located in the province of Pichincha, approximately 26 km east of Quito, the capital city of Ecuador, and 5 km from the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito. Tababela draws its visitors with its rich history, cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Tababela

If you’re planning a trip to Tababela, make sure you visit the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Casa de la Cultura Ecuatoriana
  • Church of San Juan de Tababela
  • Plaza Central de Tababela

Natural Attractions of Tababela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pasochoa Wildlife Refuge
  • Pululahua Geobotanical Reserve
  • Pita River Waterfall
  • Maquipucuna Cloud Forest Reserve

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tababela

Tababela is a haven for hikers and bikers. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Pasochoa Hike
  • Pita River Hike
  • Yanacocha Reserve Trail
  • Maquipucuna Loop

Best Things to Do in Tababela: Activities and Tours

Tababela offers various activities and tours. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit the Museo de la Casa de la Cultura Ecuatoriana
  • Take a walking tour of the town
  • Go hiking or biking
  • Take a tour of the Pasochoa Wildlife Refuge
  • Enjoy the scenic views of the Pululahua Geobotanical Reserve

Week-Long Itinerary for Tababela

Day 1: Visit the Museo de la Casa de la Cultura Ecuatoriana, Church of San Juan de Tababela, and Plaza Central de Tababela.
Day 2: Go hiking or biking in the Pita River Hike or Yanacocha Reserve Trail.
Day 3: Take a tour of the Pasochoa Wildlife Refuge and enjoy the scenic views.
Day 4: Visit the Pululahua Geobotanical Reserve and the Pita River Waterfall.
Day 5: Take a tour of the Maquipucuna Cloud Forest Reserve.
Day 6: Explore the town on foot and relax.
Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Tababela

Tababela offers various traditional Ecuadorian dishes. Some of the best food and drinks you can try include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche - Fish or shrimp marinated in citrus juice
  • Hornado - Roasted pork served with hominy, potatoes, and corn
  • Locro - A soup made with potatoes, cheese, and avocado
  • Empanadas - Baked or fried turnover pastry filled with cheese, meat, or vegetables
  • Churrasco - Grilled steak served with rice, French fries, and salad.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha - A fermented drink made from maize
  • Canelazo - A hot alcoholic drink made from sugarcane alcohol, sugar, and cinnamon
  • Ron - A locally produced rum.

Local Culture and Customs of Tababela

Tababela has a mix of indigenous and Spanish colonial culture. Some of the local customs include:

  • Respect for the elderly
  • A strong sense of community
  • Politeness
  • Festivals and religious celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Tababela

The best time to visit Tababela is during the dry season from June to September. The temperature during this period ranges from 12°C to 22°C (53°F to 72°F).

Do’s and Don’ts of Tababela

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Learn a few Spanish phrases before you travel.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the elderly
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are essential when traveling to Tababela.

Safety in Tababela

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Take care when withdrawing cash
  • Always carry a copy of your passport with you.

Health in Tababela

  • Ensure you have adequate travel health insurance
  • Use insect repellant to avoid mosquito bites
  • Drink bottled water only.

Getting to Tababela: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are various ways to get to Tababela. Some of the options include:

Airports Near Tababela

  • Mariscal Sucre International Airport - located in Quito, 5 km from Tababela.

Bus Stations Near Tababela

  • Terminal Terrestre Quitumbe - located in Quito, approximately 35 km from Tababela.

Other Options

  • Private taxi
  • Rent a car.

How to Get Around in Tababela

The town is small enough to explore on foot. Taxis and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Tababela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tababela is close to various tourist attractions. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Quito - The capital city of Ecuador
  • Otavalo - A market town famous for its textiles and handicrafts
  • Mindo - A mountain cloud forest reserve
  • Cotopaxi National Park - An active volcano with hiking trails

Conclusion

Tababela offers its visitors a mix of culture, adventure, and outdoor activities. With this travel guide, you now have all the information needed to plan your perfect trip to Tababela, Ecuador. Remember to stay safe, try the local cuisine, and respect the local customs and traditions. Happy travels!
