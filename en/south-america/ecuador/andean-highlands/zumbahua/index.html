Introduction to Zumbahua

Zumbahua is a small indigenous town situated in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador. It’s commonly known as the ‘land of blankets’, as this town is famous for its striking handwoven blankets. Zumbahua is a unique place that has retained its traditional culture and customs, making it an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience a true cultural immersion. In recent years, this town has gained increasing popularity due to its array of outdoor adventures, cultural activities, and breathtaking landscapes. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat while exploring Zumbahua.

List of Places to Visit in Zumbahua

Before diving into the places to visit in Zumbahua, it is essential to know that this town is still relatively undiscovered, and the roads leading up to some of the sights can be bumpy and challenging to navigate. But, this is part of what makes Zumbahua so unique and worth exploring!

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museo de la Cultura Puruha

This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Puruhan indigenous people. The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts, traditional clothing, and instruments that are integral to the Puruhan culture. This museum is a must-visit for those who are interested in indigenous cultures and traditions.

Iglesia de la Compania de Jesus

This impressive church dates back to the 18th century and is an excellent example of colonial architecture. The church has a beautiful interior with intricate carvings and paintings that make it worth visiting.

Natural Attractions of Zumbahua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Laguna Quilotoa

Laguna Quilotoa is a massive volcanic crater lake that is one of the standout natural attractions near Zumbahua. The lake has an otherworldly turquoise hue that contrasts beautifully with the surrounding mountains. The Laguna Quilotoa trek is popular among visitors because it offers views of the lake from several angles.

Chimborazo Volcano

The highest peak in Ecuador is the Chimborazo Volcano, situated about 100 km from Zumbahua. At the base of the volcano is the Chimborazo Wildlife Reserve, which is home to various species of wildlife such as vicunas, llamas, and Andean foxes. Visitors can enjoy hiking, trekking, and climbing the summit of the volcano if they have relevant experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zumbahua

Zumbahua is an ideal location for travelers who love adventure and being outdoors. There are several hiking and biking routes that visitors can take when exploring this town. Here are two of the most popular:

Zumbahua - Quilotoa Trek

The trek from Zumbahua to Quilotoa is a popular multi-day hike that takes visitors through small indigenous communities, past fields of crops, and up high mountains. The trek ends at Laguna Quilotoa, where visitors can marvel at the beauty of this volcanic crater lake.

Ciclovia de los Chagras

This bike route is known as the ‘path of the cowboys,’ as it takes visitors through traditional farming and cowboy country. The path offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, and visitors can choose how far they want to travel along it.

Best Things to Do in Zumbahua: Activities and Tours

Zumbahua is one of those destinations where visitors can get a unique cultural experience alongside a variety of adventure activities. Here are a few standout things to do in Zumbahua:

Visit a Local Market

Zumbahua is famous for its vibrant markets where visitors can see locals selling all types of goods and produce. These markets are an excellent opportunity to learn more about the local culture and pick up some souvenirs.

Take a Traditional Weaving Class

Visitors to Zumbahua can take part in traditional weaving classes, where locals will teach them about the use of natural materials and methods such as backstrap loom weaving. This activity is an excellent way to learn about the town’s cultural heritage while also taking home a unique, handmade item.

Enjoy a Traditional Meal with a Local Family

Getting to know a local family and sharing a traditional meal with them can be a rewarding experience when visiting Zumbahua. This activity allows visitors to see how locals live and experience the hospitality and warmth of the people in this town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zumbahua

Food and Drink Scene of Zumbahua

Zumbahua offers a unique culinary experience that visitors should not miss. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Cuy Asado

Cuy is a local delicacy that is a species of guinea pig, which is an Andean staple food. It is usually served grilled and on a skewer, accompanied by potatoes and corn.

Locro de Papas

This is a hearty potato soup made with cheese and avocado and served with toasted corn kernels and roasted pork or a fried egg.

Local Beverages

Chicha

Chicha is an Andean alcoholic drink made from fermented maize. It’s traditionally made by the indigenous peoples here and is still enjoyed today.

Horchata

Horchata is a refreshing traditional drink made from ground almonds, rice, and cinnamon. It’s a perfect drink to cool off on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Zumbahua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zumbahua has a range of accommodations options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from rustic hostels to luxurious guesthouses that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Here are a few recommended places to stay:

Hostal Mama Hilda

Hostal Mama Hilda is a family-run guesthouse that offers basic accommodations and homemade meals made from fresh, local ingredients.

Quilotoa Crater Lake Lodge

This lodge is located near Laguna Quilotoa and offers private rooms with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

What to Buy in Zumbahua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Zumbahua can find a wide assortment of unique and locally made crafts and products. Here are a few items that visitors should consider taking home as souvenirs:

Handmade Woven Blankets

Zumbahua is famous for its traditional handwoven blankets that are made from sheep’s wool. These blankets are vibrant, unique, and would make a perfect souvenir or gift.

Leather Products

Zumbahua is known for its leather crafts such as belts, bags, and shoes, which are made by local artisans from high-quality materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Zumbahua

Zumbahua is a town that takes pride in its cultural heritage and customs. Visitors should try and respect some of the following customs:

  • Greetings and acknowledgement: It is customary to greet everyone you encounter, even if you don’t know them personally. Not acknowledging someone is considered rude.
  • Dress code: Visitors should dress modestly to show respect for the locals.
  • Language: The official language is Spanish, but many locals also speak the indigenous language Quechua.

Best Time to Visit Zumbahua

The best time to visit Zumbahua is from June to August when the weather is generally dry and pleasant. During this period, visitors will be able to enjoy all of the town’s attractions without having to worry about heavy rainfall or muddy roads. Visitors should also note that December and January can be busier due to the Christmas and New Year holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zumbahua

Do:

  • Greet locals when you see them and show respect for the local culture.
  • Take a traditional weaving class and learn more about the town’s cultural heritage.
  • Try the local delicacies such as Cuy Asado and Locro de Papas.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or dress inappropriately.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zumbahua

Zumbahua is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take common-sense precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Zumbahua

Visitors should be aware of certain health risks such as altitude sickness. Zumbahua is situated at an altitude of 3400 meters, and visitors may experience symptoms such as headaches, nausea, and dizziness. Visitors should also ensure that they are up-to-date with their vaccinations.

Getting to Zumbahua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zumbahua

The nearest airport to Zumbahua is the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Zumbahua, which takes approximately three hours.

Bus Stations Near Zumbahua

The city of Latacunga has a bus station that connects to Zumbahua. Visitors can take a bus from Latacunga to Zumbahua, which takes approximately two hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to transport them to Zumbahua.

How to Get Around in Zumbahua

Zumbahua is a small town that can be easily navigated on foot. However, visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas can take a taxi or hire a private driver.

Where to Go Next from Zumbahua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can continue their Ecuadorian adventure by exploring some of the nearby destinations:

Baños de Agua Santa

Baños de Agua Santa is a small town located in the Central Andean region of Ecuador. It is known for its natural hot springs, adventure activities, and stunning waterfalls.

Otavalo

Otavalo is a town in the northern Andean Highlands of Ecuador. It’s famous for its Otavalo Markets, where visitors can find an array of handmade crafts and textiles.

Conclusion

Zumbahua is a unique destination that offers visitors a combination of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and stunning landscapes. The town’s vibrant markets, traditional weaving classes, and beautiful natural landmarks make it a must-visit location for those who want to experience Ecuador’s indigenous culture fully. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your unforgettable trip to Zumbahua!
