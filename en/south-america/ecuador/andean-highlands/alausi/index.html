Introduction to Alausi

Nestled in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, Alausi is a charming town with a rich culture and history. Famous for the exhilarating Nariz del Diablo train ride that takes you through the breathtaking mountains, Alausi is a popular destination for adventurous travelers looking for natural beauty and cultural experiences.

The town is small and walkable, and you can explore it easily on foot. The people of Alausi are known for their friendliness and warmth, and you’ll feel welcome in their community.

List of Places to Visit in Alausi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Machu Llaqtapata: This archaeological site is a pre-Incan structure located just outside Alausi. It’s a great place to learn about the local history and architecture.
  • Museo de la Asociación de Artesanos de Chunchi: This museum showcases the work of local artisans and their traditional crafts.
  • Museo de Cultura Saraguro: This museum is dedicated to the indigenous people of the Saraguro region. It has exhibits on their history, culture, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Alausi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nariz del Diablo Train: This train ride takes you through the stunning Andean Mountains, with beautiful views of the countryside and valleys.
  • Cerro de Panecillo: Located just outside Alausi, this hill offers a panoramic view of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Sibambe: It’s a small town located near Alausi that has a lovely traditional plaza and other attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alausi

Alausi has many hiking and biking trails to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Cerro de Panecillo Hike: A 1.5-hour hike that takes you to the top of Cerro de Panecillo for breathtaking views of Alausi.
  • Tribu Trail: This 9-km trail starts at the Machu Llaqtapata archaeological site and takes you through beautiful valleys and mountains.
  • Cordillera del Cóndor: This multi-day trek through the mountains starts in Alausi and takes you through some of the most scenic areas of Ecuador.

Best Things to Do in Alausi: Activities and Tours

  • Nariz del Diablo Train: A must-do activity, this train ride takes you through the Andean Mountains and offers some of the most spectacular views in the country.
  • Canyoning and Zip Lining: Alausi has many opportunities for adventure sports, including canyoning and zip-lining tours.
  • Saraguro Market: Located just outside Alausi, this market is a great place to buy traditional crafts and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alausi

Day 1: Arrive and explore the town center of Alausi. Day 2: Take the Nariz del Diablo train ride and explore Sibambe. Day 3: Visit the Machu Llaqtapata archaeological site and hike the Cerro de Panecillo trail. Day 4: Take a canyoning or zip-lining tour. Day 5: Visit the Saraguro Market and learn about the indigenous people of the region. Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Cuenca, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Day 7: Relax in Alausi and enjoy the local food and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Alausi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Locro soup: A hearty soup made with potatoes, corn, cheese, and avocado.
  • Cuy: A traditional Ecuadorian dish made with roasted guinea pig.
  • Empanadas: A popular savor*y pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A traditional fermented corn drink.
  • Canelazo: A hot cocktail made with aguardiente, juice, and spices.

Where to Stay in Alausi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alausi has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget:

  • La Quinta Resort & Spa: A luxurious hotel with spa facilities and beautiful views of the mountains.
  • Hostal de la Rabida: A budget-friendly hostel located in the town center of Alausi.
  • Casa Los Geranios: A cozy and comfortable guesthouse with a charming garden and great views of the town.

What to Buy in Alausi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Alausi is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, weaving, and carving.
  • Panama Hats: These iconic hats are made from a special type of straw and are a popular souvenir from Ecuador.
  • Chocolate: Ecuador is known for its high-quality chocolate, and you can find many varieties in Alausi.

Local Culture and Customs of Alausi

Alausi is a multicultural town, with indigenous and mestizo communities living together. The people of Alausi are known for their friendly and welcoming attitude, and visitors are often invited to participate in local festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Alausi

The best time to visit Alausi is during the dry season, from June to September. During these months, the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alausi

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional dishes.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and don’t leave trash behind.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Take photos of people without asking for their permission.
  • Buy souvenirs made from endangered species or illegal products.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alausi

Alausi is a safe town for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings when walking around town at night.

Health in Alausi

Alausi is located at a high altitude, so visitors may experience altitude sickness. It’s important to take it slow and drink plenty of water to avoid discomfort. Make sure to bring sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun during outdoor activities.

Getting to Alausi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alausi

The closest international airport to Alausi is Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito. From there, you can take a bus or hire a private car to Alausi.

Bus Stations Near Alausi

Alausi has a bus station with connections to many cities in Ecuador, including Quito, Cuenca, and Guayaquil.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or private car to take you to Alausi from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Alausi

Alausi is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation around town and to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Alausi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cuenca: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Cuenca is a charming colonial city located just a few hours from Alausi.
  • Ingapirca: A pre-Columbian archaeological site located near Cuenca.
  • Baños: A popular destination for adventure sports and hot springs, Baños is located a few hours from Alausi.

Conclusion

Alausi is a hidden gem in Ecuador’s Andean Highlands, with a rich culture, stunning scenery, and exciting activities for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or relaxation, Alausi is a destination you won’t want to miss.
