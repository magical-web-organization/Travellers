Isinliví is a charming town in the Andean Highlands of Ecuador, located in the Cotopaxi province. With its beautiful mountains and rich culture, Isinliví offers a unique travel experience that’s different from the more popular Ecuadorian destinations of Galapagos and Quito. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, culture enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Isinliví has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan a trip to Isinliví.

Introduction to Isinliví

Isinliví is a small town nestled in the Andean Mountains, located a five-hour drive south of Quito. The town is known for its strong cultural heritage, which is evident in its preserved colonial architecture, indigenous cuisine, and ancient Quechua language still spoken by many locals. The serene landscape surrounding the town also makes it a popular destination for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Isinliví

Isinliví has a lot to offer in terms of attractions and activities. Here’s a breakdown of the top places to visit in Isinliví:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Isinliví is steeped in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places to visit that give a glimpse into the town’s past. Here are the must-visit museums and historical places in Isinliví:

  • Museo de la Cultura Inca: This museum is dedicated to the Inca civilization and houses artifacts and exhibits related to the ancient culture.

  • Iglesia de San Francisco: This colonial-era church is one of the oldest buildings in Isinliví and an architectural marvel.

  • Monument to the Agrarian Struggle: This monument was erected to commemorate the struggles of the indigenous people of the area and their fight against agricultural exploitation.

Natural Attractions of Isinliví: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Isinliví’s picturesque surroundings make it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore in the area:

  • Fundo Hacienda El Porvenir: This is a working ranch where visitors can enjoy horseback riding, hiking, and mountain biking.

  • Quilotoa Crater Lake: This stunning turquoise lake formed in a collapsed volcano offers scenic hiking trails with panoramic views.

  • Salto de Guayama: This hidden waterfall is tucked away in the mountains and requires a moderate hike to reach, but the trek is worth it for the breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isinliví

Isinliví’s natural surroundings offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Caminata Yanayacu-Chuquiragua: This trek is perfect for nature lovers, with a trail that goes through forests and mountains. The trail is a 4-5 hour round trip journey.

  • Biking in El Porvenir: Rent a bike at El Porvenir and explore the surrounding area on a self-guided tour, with panoramic views of the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Isinliví: Activities and Tours

Isinliví offers several activities and tours that can make your trip unforgettable. Here are some of the best things to do in Isinliví:

  • Indigenous Community Visit: Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting an indigenous community and learning about their lifestyle, traditions, and cuisine.

  • Canyoning: For the adventurous, try canyoning in Isinliví’s mountains, with rappelling, zip-lining, and waterfalls.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore Isinliví’s vast landscapes on horseback, with tours that take you through remote mountains and valleys.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isinliví

To make the most of your trip to Isinliví, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival in Isinliví and check-in at a hotel, followed by a visit to the local market to try out some local cuisine.

Day 2: Take a walking tour of Isinliví’s colonial architecture and historical landmarks, such as Iglesia de San Francisco and Monument to the Agrarian Struggle.

Day 3: Hike the Yanayacu-Chuquiragua trail through the mountains and visit nearby waterfalls.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Quilotoa Crater Lake and hike around the stunning lake, with stunning views of the Andean Highlands.

Day 5: Visit an indigenous community to learn about their traditional lifestyle and enjoy a home-cooked meal.

Day 6: Go horseback riding at El Porvenir and explore the surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Depart from Isinliví and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Isinliví

Food and drink are an integral part of Isinliví’s culture, with many traditional dishes and beverages to try out. Here are some must-try foods and drinks in Isinliví:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Locro de Papas: A hearty Andean soup made with local potatoes, cheese, and avocado.

  • Fanesca: A traditional soup made with a variety of grains, legumes, and vegetables, typically served during Easter.

  • Cuy Asado: A roasted guinea pig, prepared with traditional spices and herbs, a popular delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A fermented corn drink, made by the indigenous people. It comes in two types, sweet and sour.

  • Cañelazo: A spiced, warm drink made from cane liquor, cinnamon, and fruits, served in Andean festivities.

  • Intï-Milla: A traditional spirit made from corn, barley, and wheat.

Where to Stay in Isinliví: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isinliví has several options for accommodation, catering to all travel budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Isinliví:

  • Hostel Tiana: A budget-friendly hostel with cozy rooms, a communal kitchen, and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • Hostal El Parador De Isinliví: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a garden area with stunning mountain views.

  • Fundo Hacienda El Porvenir: This working ranch provides visitors with the perfect blend of rustic accommodations and modern amenities, with rooms in hacienda-style houses and panoramic views.

What to Buy in Isinliví: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Isinliví is known for its textiles and handicrafts, made by the indigenous people. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Isinliví:

  • Weaving and Textiles: Handmade textiles like ponchos, rugs, and table runners, made with natural fibres, are a popular souvenir.

  • Hats: Panama hats are famous in the region, and you can find them in different styles and colours.

Local Culture and Customs of Isinliví

Isinliví has a rich cultural heritage, with several customs and traditions upheld by the local community. Here are some aspects of Isinliví’s culture and customs:

  • Quechua Language: The Quechua language is still spoken by many locals and is an essential part of the region’s cultural identity.

  • Indigenous Festivals: Isinliví celebrates different festivals year-round, such as the Kulla Raymi, Chakra Raymi, and Pawkar Raymi, where locals showcase their traditional clothing, food, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Isinliví

The best time to visit Isinliví is from June to September, during the dry season, when the temperatures are cool, and there are clear skies. However, Isinliví is a beautiful place to visit any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isinliví

Do:

  • Learn Some Basic Spanish: Although some locals speak English, it’s always advisable to learn some basic Spanish to communicate effectively.

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions: Isinliví is a culturally rich place with several customs and traditions. As a visitor, it’s important to respect them and maintain proper decorum.

Don’t:

  • Expect High-End Amenities: Isinliví is a small town and doesn’t provide high-end amenities like you would find in bigger cities.

  • Be Overly Flashy: Avoid carrying flashy jewellery or wearing expensive watches as it can attract unwanted attention.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are essential considerations while travelling to Isinliví.

Safety in Isinliví

  • Avoid Travelling Alone at Night: It’s not advisable to travel alone in Isinliví at night, especially on deserted roads.

  • Beware of Theft: Petty theft can happen, so it’s advisable to keep valuables safe and avoid carrying large sums of money.

Health in Isinliví

  • Stay Hydrated: The high altitude of Isinliví can result in altitude sickness. It’s advisable to stay hydrated and avoid alcohol for the first few days.

  • Pack Appropriate Clothing and Sunblock: The Andean sun can be harsh, so it’s essential to pack appropriate clothing and sunblock to avoid sunburn and heat exhaustion.

Getting to Isinliví: Transportation Options and Logistics

Isinliví is connected to Quito and other nearby cities by road. Here are some of the transportation options to get to Isinliví:

Airports Near Isinliví

  • Quito Airport: The closest airport to Isinliví is the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito, which is a five-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Isinliví

  • Latacunga Bus Station: From Quito, take a bus or taxi to Latacunga, then transfer to a local bus to Isinliví.

Other Options

  • Private Transfers: Private transfers are available from Quito or other nearby cities to Isinliví.

How to Get Around in Isinliví

Isinliví is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Alternatively, you can rent a bike or take a taxi to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Isinliví: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Isinliví is situated in the central Andean Highlands, making it a popular destination to explore other nearby cities and towns. Here are some of the nearby destinations to add to your itinerary:

  • Baños: A few hours drive from Isinliví, Baños is a picturesque town known for its hot springs and adventure sports.

  • Cuenca: A four-hour drive from Isinliví, Cuenca boasts exquisite colonial architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Quito: Five hours drive from Isinliví, Quito is a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage, known for its colonial architecture, museums, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Isinliví is a hidden gem in the Andean Highlands, offering an off-the-beaten-path travel experience with its unique culture, stunning landscapes, and outdoor adventures. We hope this comprehensive Isinliví travel guide will help you plan your trip to this beautiful town and experience the best of Andean Ecuador.
