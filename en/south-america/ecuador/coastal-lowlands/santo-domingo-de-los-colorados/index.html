Introduction to Santo Domingo de los Colorados

Located in the Coastal Lowlands of Ecuador, Santo Domingo de los Colorados is a vibrant city that offers visitors a unique mix of culture and outdoor adventure. The city is known for its indigenous community, which has a strong presence in the area’s art, music, and food. Santo Domingo de los Colorados is also a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, as it is surrounded by beautiful forests, waterfalls, and hiking trails. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more in Santo Domingo de los Colorados.

List of Places to Visit in Santo Domingo de los Colorados

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Folklorico Pachamama: This museum showcases the history and culture of the indigenous community in Santo Domingo de los Colorados. Visitors can see traditional clothing, musical instruments, and other artifacts.
  • Museo del Banco Central: This museum offers an overview of Ecuador’s history and culture, with exhibits on indigenous art, colonial history, and contemporary art.
  • Centro Cultural Libertad: This cultural center hosts regular events and exhibits that showcase the local art and music scenes.

Natural Attractions of Santo Domingo de los Colorados: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cascada del Duende: This waterfall is located just a short hike from the city center. Visitors can swim in the natural pool at the base of the waterfall.
  • Reserva Ecologica Bosque Nublado: This cloud forest reserve offers hiking trails that lead to stunning mountain views and waterfalls.
  • Mirador El Molino: This viewpoint offers panoramic views of Santo Domingo de los Colorados and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santo Domingo de los Colorados

  • Reserva Ecologica Bosque Nublado: This cloud forest reserve offers several well-marked hiking trails of varying difficulty levels.
  • Ciclovia: This bike path runs along the main road in Santo Domingo de los Colorados, offering a safe and scenic route for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Santo Domingo de los Colorados: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Toachi River: This exciting adventure takes visitors through challenging rapids and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Indigenous Market: This weekly market showcases the vibrant art, music, and food of the local indigenous community.
  • Chiriboga Blue Mozzarella Farm Tour: This cheese-making tour takes visitors through the process of making mozzarella, and offers the opportunity to sample the delicious finished product.

Food and Drink Scene of Santo Domingo de los Colorados

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fanesca: This traditional Ecuadorian soup is made with a variety of grains, beans, and vegetables, and is served during the Easter season.
  • Seco de Chivo: This hearty stew is made with goat meat cooked in a tomato and beer sauce, and is typically served with rice and beans.
  • Arroz con Leche: This popular dessert is made with sweetened rice, milk, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This fermented corn drink is a staple in the indigenous community, and is often served at celebrations and festivals.
  • Canelazo: This hot drink is made with aguardiente (a strong alcohol), sugar, water, and cinnamon, and is served during the holidays and on cold nights.

Where to Stay in Santo Domingo de los Colorados: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Jardines del Colorados: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with Wi-Fi and air conditioning, and is located close to the city center.
  • Hostal La Posada: This hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms, and is known for its friendly atmosphere and convenient location.
  • La Molienda Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms with access to a shared kitchen and common area, and has a beautiful garden for guests to relax in.

What to Buy in Santo Domingo de los Colorados: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tagua Jewelry: Tagua, also known as “vegetable ivory,” is a sustainable alternative to elephant ivory. Visitors can find a variety of handmade jewelry and crafts made from tagua in Santo Domingo de los Colorados.
  • Ceramic Figurines: The local indigenous community is known for their intricate ceramic figurines, which depict animals, plants, and other cultural symbols.
  • Chocolate: Ecuador is known for its high-quality chocolate, and visitors can find a variety of chocolate bars and truffles in Santo Domingo de los Colorados.

Local Culture and Customs of Santo Domingo de los Colorados

The local indigenous community in Santo Domingo de los Colorados is known for their vibrant art, music, and food. Visitors should take care to respect their customs and traditions, and can learn more about their culture by visiting the Museo Folklorico Pachamama and attending the weekly Indigenous Market. It’s also worth noting that the local indigenous people often use natural materials, such as tagua and ceramic, in their crafts as a way to promote sustainability.

Best Time to Visit Santo Domingo de los Colorados

The best time to visit Santo Domingo de los Colorados is between May and November, when the weather is dry and sunny. The rainy season runs from December through April, and can make hiking and outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santo Domingo de los Colorados

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the local indigenous community.
  • Try the local food and beverages, including fanesca, seco de chivo, and chicha.
  • Explore the natural attractions, such as the Reserva Ecologica Bosque Nublado and Cascada del Duende.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb the wildlife in the natural reserves.
  • Disrespect the customs and traditions of the local indigenous community.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santo Domingo de los Colorados

Santo Domingo de los Colorados is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should exercise caution in crowded areas and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. It’s also a good idea to be aware of your surroundings when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Santo Domingo de los Colorados

Visitors to Santo Domingo de los Colorados should make sure they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella. The city is located at a lower elevation, which can cause altitude sickness for some visitors. It’s also important to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Santo Domingo de los Colorados: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santo Domingo de los Colorados

The nearest airport to Santo Domingo de los Colorados is the Aeropuerto Internacional Mariscal Sucre, located in Quito, approximately 3 hours away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Santo Domingo de los Colorados

The city has a central bus station, Terminal Terrestre Santo Domingo, which offers regular connections to other cities in Ecuador.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired from Quito.

How to Get Around in Santo Domingo de los Colorados

The city center is easily walkable, but taxis and buses are also available for longer distances. Bicycles can be rented from local shops for those who want to explore the area on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Santo Domingo de los Colorados: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mindo: This small town is known for its cloud forest reserve and bird-watching opportunities, and is located approximately 2 hours away from Santo Domingo de los Colorados by car or bus.
  • Cotopaxi National Park: This national park features the Cotopaxi volcano, as well as hiking and camping opportunities. It is approximately 3 hours away from Santo Domingo de los Colorados.
  • Quito: Ecuador’s capital city is approximately 3 hours away from Santo Domingo de los Colorados, and offers a variety of cultural attractions, including museums, art galleries, and historic sites.

Santo Domingo de los Colorados is a city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the local culture, hiking through beautiful natural reserves, or trying the delicious local food and drink, you’re sure to have a memorable trip.
