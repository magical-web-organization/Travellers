Introduction to Quevedo

Quevedo is a small city located in the Coastal Lowlands region of Ecuador, South America. It is the capital of the Los Rios province and is situated on the banks of the Quevedo River. Despite being small, Quevedo is a bustling city with a population of around 200,000 people. It is an important commercial center for agriculture and livestock as well as an excellent gateway to some of Ecuador’s most stunning natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Quevedo

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or natural beauty, Quevedo has something to offer every type of traveler. Here is a list of some of the must-see places that you can’t miss during your visit to Quevedo.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Anthropology and Contemporary Art: This museum houses a large collection of contemporary artwork and exhibitions of cultural artifacts significant to the local Quevedo culture.

  2. Mother’s Park: A beautiful tribute park dedicated to all mother’s in the world that includes a large lake and a playground.

  3. Paredones Ruins: An impressive archaeological site dating back to the 15th century, featuring the remains of a large, fortified settlement.

Natural Attractions of Quevedo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Jumón Waterfall: An ideal spot for day trips and picnic outings, this waterfall offers stunning views and a natural swimming pool with a small beach.

  2. Cascada De San Rafael: This 150-meter-high cascading waterfall is a truly breathtaking sight and represents the tallest waterfall in the country.

  3. Reserva Ecologica Manglares Churute: This is the perfect place for birdwatching and features a range of hiking trails through mangrove forests to see amazing wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quevedo

Quevedo is surrounded by mountains, hills, and natural reserves that are ideal for adventurous travelers who enjoy hiking and biking. Here are some excellent hiking and biking routes for anyone who wants to explore the natural beauty of Quevedo:

  1. Naranjal Road: This route will guide you through the winding roads of Los Rios Province to admire the picturesque landscapes and natural beauty of the area.

  2. Limoncocha Lake: An easy hike that brings hikers to one of the most spectacular views in the Ecuadorian Amazon jungle to witness colorful exotic birds.

  3. La Loma Hike: A challenging uphill hike that leads to a stunning view of Quevado and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Quevedo: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the most popular activities and tours that we recommend to tourists visiting Quevedo:

  1. Horseback Treks: Explore the vast nature reserves on a horseback trek, an exciting and immersive way to experience the local scenery.

  2. Canoe Tours: Experience the mangroves and wildlife from a different perspective with a guided canoe tour through the local ecosystem.

  3. Gastronomical Tours: Discover the history and unique flavors of traditional Ecuadorian cuisine through a guided tour with local food connoisseurs.

Food and Drink Scene of Quevedo

Discovering the local cuisine of Quevedo is an essential part of any visit to this city. Here’s our guide to some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Locro: A hearty stew made with potatoes, cheese, and herbs.

  2. Llapingachos: A popular dish made of mashed potatoes and filled with cheese and served with meat and vegetables.

  3. Encebollado: A dish made of fish, cassava, and onions that is usually served as a soup.

Local Beverages

  1. Chicha: A fermented corn drink that is popular in many South American countries.

  2. Canelazo: A hot beverage made with naranjilla fruit, cinnamon, and aguardiente (alcohol).

  3. Colada Morada: A traditional drink made with black corn flour, fruits, and spices, served during “Day of the Deceased” celebrations.

Where to Stay in Quevedo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here is a list of some of the best hotels, hostels, and guest houses available to stay in Quevedo:

  1. Hotel Palmar del Rio: A luxurious hotel located near the city center with a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.

  2. Hostal Costa Azul: A budget-friendly hostel near the city center, perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

  3. Hacienda La Danesa: A beautiful guesthouse located near the Limoncocha lake, offering visitors a chance to experience the local plantations and enjoy morning horseback rides.

What to Buy in Quevedo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Quevedo can purchase a variety of unique local products and souvenirs. Some of the items that you can purchase include:

  1. Artisanal Crafts: Traditional Ecuadorian crafts, including ceramics, textiles, and hand-carved wood items.

  2. Coffee Beans: Ecuador is known for its excellent coffee, and Quevedo produces some of the best and most sustainably grown varieties.

  3. Chocolate: Ecuadorian chocolate is some of the most delicious chocolate in the world, so pick up a bag of local chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of Quevedo

Quevedo is a culturally rich city with many traditions and customs. Visitors should be aware of and respect these norms during their stay in the city.

Best Time to Visit Quevedo

The best time to visit Quevedo is during the dry season from June to December when tourists can enjoy a variety of outdoor attractions without worry of rain and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quevedo

Before visiting Quevedo, here are some important do’s and don’ts that tourists should be aware of:

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions of the city.

  2. Carry cash and small bills when shopping in local markets or stores.

  3. Learn some basic Spanish, as most locals do not speak English.

Don’t:

  1. Wander outside of the city center at night time.

  2. Wear expensive jewelry or flash cash in public.

  3. Disrespect the environment by littering or damaging natural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety is a top priority for tourists visiting Quevedo. Here is some essential information about staying safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Quevedo

  1. Be aware of pickpockets and theft in crowded places.

  2. Only drink bottled water and avoid unpasteurized dairy products.

Health in Quevedo

  1. Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated when spending time outdoors.

  2. Get vaccinated against yellow fever and other diseases to stay safe during your visit.

Getting to Quevedo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the best transportation options for traveling to and around Quevedo:

Airports Near Quevedo

The nearest airport to Quevedo is the Eloy Alfaro International Airport in Guayaquil, which is 105 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Quevedo

There are several bus companies that offer routes to and from Quevedo, including:

  1. Flota Bolivar
  2. Transportes Occidental
  3. San Francisco

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Guayaquil to reach Quevedo

How to Get Around in Quevedo

Taxis are plentiful around the city and are an affordable and convenient way to get around. Buses and shuttles also operate on fixed routes, making them perfect for traveling between popular spots.

Where to Go Next from Quevedo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some popular destinations from Quevedo to continue your Ecuadorian adventure:

  1. Montanita
  2. Guayaquil
  3. Baltra Island in the Galapagos

Conclusion

Quevedo is a hidden gem of Ecuadorian tourism, with something for every type of traveler. With its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural history, Quevedo truly is a must-see destination for anyone visiting Ecuador.
