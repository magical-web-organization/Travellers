Introduction to Portoviejo

Portoviejo is the capital of the Manabí Province and one of Ecuador’s most vibrant and dynamic cities. Located in the Coastal Lowlands region, the city boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a buzzing food and drink scene. Portoviejo is also an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations such as Manta, Bahia de Caraquez, or the Machalilla National Park. In this travel guide, we will give you a comprehensive overview of the city, including the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs.

Food and Drink Scene of Portoviejo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Encebollado: Fish soup with onions and yuca
  • Seco de Chivo: Stewed goat with rice and beans
  • Churrasco: Grilled steak with potato chips

Local Beverages

  • Canelazo: Hot drink made with cinnamon, sugar, and aguardiente
  • Chicha: Fermented corn drink

What to Buy in Portoviejo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Panama hats: Made from toquilla straw fiber, a popular artisanal product in the region.
  • Gold and silver jewelry: Manabí is known for its elaborate filigree work.
  • Handicrafts and textiles: Beautifully embroidered clothing and accessories made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Portoviejo

  • Religion: Catholicism is deeply ingrained in the local culture, with numerous religious festivals and events throughout the year.
  • Dress code: Portoviejo is a relatively conservative city, and visitors are advised to dress modestly.
  • Greetings: It is customary to greet people with a handshake and a smile, and to use formal titles and last names when addressing them.

Best Time to Visit Portoviejo

The best time to visit Portoviejo is from December to April when the climate is dry and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities. However, the city can get crowded during the high season, and prices may be higher. The low season from May to November offers cheaper accommodations, fewer crowds and milder temperatures but may have some occasional rain.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portoviejo

While Portoviejo is a relatively safe city, visitors should take standard precautions like keeping valuables close, avoiding dark alleys, and staying aware of their surroundings. It’s better to travel in groups at night as in any other city.

Health in Portoviejo

Visitors should drink bottled water rather than tap water, which may cause stomach troubles. It is advisable to take repellent, sunscreen, and a hat, as the sun can be especially strong. Visitors are also advised to consult with a doctor or travel clinic before traveling to Ecuador.

Getting to Portoviejo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portoviejo

The nearest airport to Portoviejo is the Manta-Manabí Airport (MEC), located about 40 km away from Portoviejo. Other nearby airports include the Jose Joaquin de Olmedo International Airport (GYE) in Guayaquil, approximately 170 km away, and the Quito International Airport (UIO), located around 400 km away.

Bus Stations Near Portoviejo

Portoviejo has a central bus terminal with connections to major cities in Ecuador, including Quito and Guayaquil. Buses to nearby destinations such as Manta and Bahia de Caraquez can be boarded at the Terminal Terrestre Puerto de Crucita.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange for a private transfer or taxi from Guayaquil or Manta. This is especially recommended if traveling at night. Additionally, there are car rental options available, although traffic in the city can be chaotic.

How to Get Around in Portoviejo

Public transportation in Portoviejo includes buses and taxis, both of which are affordable. Visitors should be wary of unofficial taxis and always negotiate the fare beforehand. Visitors can also rent bicycles or a car to explore the city and surrounding areas at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Portoviejo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manta, for beaches and water sports
  • Bahia de Caraquez, for wildlife and nature
  • Machalilla National Park, for hiking and ecotourism
  • Montecristi, for handicrafts and local art

Conclusion

Portoviejo is an authentic and vibrant city with plenty of options for history, culture, nature, food, and fun. From its rich cultural heritage to its beautiful natural surroundings, Portoviejo has something for everyone seeking an enriching travel experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan a trip to Portoviejo with ease, knowing the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs and traditions.
