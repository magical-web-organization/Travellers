Montecristi Travel Guide

Located in the Coastal Lowlands of Ecuador, Montecristi is a vibrant destination known for its rich culture and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, adventure seeker, or foodie, this town has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, local culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Montecristi

Montecristi boasts an array of cultural and natural attractions. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Sombrero: This museum displays the history and cultural significance of Montecristi’s iconic Panama hats.

  • Iglesia Matriz de Montecristi: Built in the 16th century, this church is a beautiful example of colonial architecture.

  • Monumento a la Montecristi: This monument honors Montecristi’s heroes and is an excellent spot for scenic views.

Natural Attractions of Montecristi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cerro de Montecristi: This hill offers a stunning view of the town and nearby coast. It’s an ideal spot to watch the sunset.

  • Isla de La Plata: Known as the “Poor Man’s Galapagos,” this island offers a chance to see unique wildlife and go snorkeling.

  • Playa de Crucita: This tranquil beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and taking a relaxing stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montecristi

Montecristi has several scenic routes that are perfect for hiking or biking. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Cerro de Montecristi: The trail to the top of Cerro de Montecristi is an excellent option for hikers and bikers. The route offers panoramic views of the town, the countryside, and the coast.

  • Ruta del Agua: This path winds through a lush forest and features several waterfalls along the way.

  • Ciclovia de la Costa: This bike path runs along the coast for over 600km, connecting Montecristi with other coastal towns and villages.

Best Things to Do in Montecristi: Activities and Tours

Montecristi offers an array of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Panama Hat Factory Tour: Learn about the history and production of Montecristi’s famed Panama hats.

  • Whale Watching Tour: From June to September, visitors can spot humpback whales in the waters off the coast of Montecristi.

  • Paragliding: Take a thrilling paragliding flight over Montecristi and enjoy breathtaking views of the coast and countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montecristi

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Montecristi:

Day Activity
1 Visit Museo del Sombrero and Iglesia Matriz de Montecristi
2 Take a bike tour to Cerro de Montecristi and Ruta del Agua

4 Join a whale watching tour and visit Isla de La Plata

7 Depart for your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Montecristi

Montecristi has a diverse food scene that combines local flavors with international influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Encebollado: This hearty soup is made with fish, yuca, onions, and spices.

  • Ceviche: Montecristi’s version of this classic dish features shrimp, lime, tomato, and cilantro.

  • Arroz con menestra y carne asada: Rice with beans and grilled steak is a staple dish in Montecristi.

Local Beverages

  • Guayusa: This tea made from the leaves of the guayusa plant is popular in the town and throughout Ecuador.

  • Canelazo: This warm drink is made with cinnamon, sugar, and aguardiente, a local spirit.

  • Chicha: A fermented drink made from cassava, chicha is a traditional beverage in Ecuador.

Where to Stay in Montecristi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montecristi has several accommodation options to choose from depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hostal Royal Palace: This budget-friendly hostel is clean and centrally located in Montecristi.

  • **Hotel Chi: **With its elegant rooms and views of the ocean, Hotel Chi is a perfect choice for visitors looking for luxury.

  • Hostería Sol y Mar: This charming hotel is built in the traditional Hacienda style and features a beautiful garden and pool.

What to Buy in Montecristi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montecristi is famous for its Panama hats, but there are also many other local products to bring home as souvenirs. Here are some of the best options:

  • Panama Hats: Made from Toquilla straw, these hats are woven by hand and can take several months to create. They’re sold in many shops throughout Montecristi.

  • Handicrafts: Montecristi is home to many local artisans who create beautiful pottery, textiles, and other handicrafts.

  • Aguardiente: This local spirit is made from sugar cane and can be found in many stores and markets throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Montecristi

Montecristi has a distinct culture that reflects its history and traditions. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Greeting: When meeting someone, it’s customary to shake hands and exchange pleasantries.

  • Dress Code: Montecristi is a conservative town, so it’s best to dress modestly.

  • Etiquette: It’s important to be respectful and polite when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Montecristi

The best time to visit Montecristi is between June and September when the weather is dry and warm, and whale watching is in season. However, the town is enjoyable year-round, with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 28°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montecristi

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Montecristi:

Do:

  • Try local foods and drinks: Montecristi’s cuisine is a fusion of traditional Ecuadorian dishes and international flavors.

  • Visit the historic landmarks: Montecristi has several museums and monuments that are perfect for history buffs.

  • Take part in outdoor activities: Montecristi offers many thrilling adventures, including paragliding and whale watching.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs: Montecristi is a conservative town, so it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Litter or damage natural areas: Montecristi is home to many beautiful natural areas, so it’s important to preserve them for future visitors.

Safety and Health

Montecristi is a safe town to visit, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions like not carrying valuables with you and being aware of your surroundings. The town has a good healthcare system, but it’s best to bring any necessary medications with you.

Safety in Montecristi

  • Carry a copy of your passport: It’s best to carry a copy of your passport, leaving the original in a safe place.

  • Use a money belt: Keep your valuables safe by using a money belt instead of carrying a purse or wallet.

  • Don’t walk alone at night: Stick to well-lit and busy areas, especially at night.

Health in Montecristi

  • Stay hydrated: The sun can be intense in Montecristi, so carry water with you at all times.

  • Use sunscreen: Protect your skin from sunburn by using sunscreen with at least SPF 30.

  • Watch what you eat: When trying local street food, ensure that the vendor practices good hygiene.

Getting to Montecristi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Montecristi is located just a few hours away from Quito by car or bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Montecristi

  • José Joaquín de Olmedo International Airport: Located in Guayaquil, this airport is the closest to Montecristi, around a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Montecristi

  • Terminal Terrestre de Guayaquil: This bus station is the main one serving Guayaquil and offers frequent connections to Montecristi.

  • Terminal Terrestre de Quito: This bus station is a few hours away from Montecristi and offers connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: Many tour operators offer private transfers to Montecristi from nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Montecristi

Montecristi is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot. If you prefer to use public transportation or taxis, there are many options available.

Where to Go Next from Montecristi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re up for more adventures, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Manta: This coastal town is a popular spot for surfing and has several beautiful beaches.

  • Manabí: This province is known for its unspoiled beaches, national parks, and archaeological sites.

  • Cuenca: Located in the Andean highlands, Cuenca is a charming colonial town that’s listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site.
