Introduction to Isabela

Isabela, located in the Galapagos Islands of Ecuador, is the largest and one of the youngest islands in the archipelago. It offers a unique experience for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and those looking for a relaxing vacation. The island is known for its pristine beaches, volcanic landscapes, and diverse marine life. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Isabela

When it comes to attractions, Isabela has a lot to offer. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Casa de Ceibo - This restored wooden house turned museum reveals the history and culture of the early settlers of the island.
  • Centro de Crianza Arnaldo Tupiza - A research center dedicated to the preservation of tortoises and other marine species.
  • Pozas de las Diablas - These natural pools are believed to hold ancient spirits by the locals, and visitors can swim and enjoy the view.

Natural Attractions of Isabela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tintoreras - This is a series of small islets providing sanctuary to a variety of wildlife, such as Galapagos Penguins, marine turtles, sea lions, and white-tipped reef sharks.
  • Sierra Negra - This is one of the most active volcanoes in the Galapagos, and you can go on a guided hiking tour to explore its scenic lava fields.
  • Concha De Perla - This beautiful clear-water lagoon is ideal for snorkeling and swimming with numerous marine species, including sea turtles, sea lions, and penguins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isabela

Isabela offers a variety of eco-friendly ways to explore its natural beauty on foot or wheels. Here are some popular trails and bike routes:

  • Los Tunneles Trail - A 2-3 hour walk through lava tunnels and observation of a variety of marine wildlife, including different species of rays, green sea turtles, and sharks.
  • Wall of Tears Trail - A 7-8 km hike with impressive views of the sea and a chance to look at the Wall of Tears built by prisoners in the 1940s.
  • Biking Route to Sierra Negra - A thrilling 30-kilometer cycling tour across volcanic landscapes to reach the Sierra Negra Volcano summit.

Best Things to Do in Isabela: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking - explore the coast by kayak with a guided tour.
  • Horseback riding trot along sandy beaches and get a chance to see a variety of wildlife along the way.
  • Scuba Diving - Discover the underwater riches of Isabela with several scuba diving shops and tour operators.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isabela

Planning to spend a week in Isabela? Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Isabela and relax on the beach
Day 2 Visit the Turtle Breeding Center and explore Concha de Perla
Day 3 Take a bike trip around Sierra Negra volcano and visit Los Tunneles
Day 4 Go on a guided boat tour to observe Galapagos Sea lions, blue-footed boobies, and marine iguanas
Day 5 Take a day trip to explore uninhabited islets such as Isla Seymour and Isla Plazas

Food and Drink Scene of Isabela

Isabela is home to a fantastic culinary scene unique to the island’s inhabitants. Here is what you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Ceviche - Fresh ceviche made with lobster is a specialty of the island.
  • Empanadas - A pastry-like dish stuffed with cheese, meats, or vegetables.
  • Encebollado - A fish soup made with yuca, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Colada Morada - A thick, sweetened juice-like beverage made from purple corn and fruit options like guava and blackberries.
  • Canelazo - A warm cocktail made with local spices and Aguardiente (sugar cane liquor).

Where to Stay in Isabela: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious or budget-friendly accommodation option, Isabela caters to all. Some popular hotels and hostels include:

  • Iguana Crossing Boutique Hotel - A luxurious beach resort with modern amenities and stunning suites.
  • Casa de Marita - A boutique hotel overlooking a tranquil bay offering a relaxed atmosphere ideal for couples.
  • Hotel Albemarle - A family-friendly hotel right on the beach and close to colonial monuments.

What to Buy in Isabela: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products unique to Isabela you can bring home as a souvenir:

  • Giant Tortoise Souvenirs - Miniature ornaments and keyholders in the shape of giant tortoises make perfect souvenirs.
  • Handmade Jewelry - Made with natural materials such as shells, local stones, and volcanic ash.
  • Coffee and Chocolate - Isabela is home to coffee and chocolate plantations, and you can bring home freshly roasted and packaged beans and bars.

Local Culture and Customs of Isabela

The locals in Isabela appreciate when visitors respect their customs and etiquette. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Language - While Spanish is the official language in Ecuador, some locals on Isabela speak English. Know some essential Spanish phrases to get around.
  • Tipping - Tipping is not required but is appreciated, 10-15% of the bill is the usual amount.
  • Dress Code - Island life can be casual, so dress for comfort. However, when visiting historic sites or religious areas, dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Isabela

The best time to visit Isabela is from December to May, the warm and dry season. During this time, you can enjoy snorkeling, diving, and swimming in the sea. For land-based activities, visit the island sometime between June and November when there are fewer crowds, and rates are much lower, but it’s a bit cooler and there’s a chance of light rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isabela

Do:

  • Respect the natural habitats and wildlife. 90% of the island is protected by the Ecuadorian government for conservation purposes.
  • Hire a local guide instead of exploring on your own. They are knowledgeable about the islands and know how to respect the natural park’s rules.
  • Protect yourself from the sun and stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Pick the flowers or take rocks or sand from the islands. This is forbidden by the law and can cause damage to the fragile ecosystem.
  • Feed the animals or throw garbage outside litter cans.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isabela

Isabela is a safe place to visit, but as a tourist, you should keep some basic safety measures in mind, such as:

  • Don’t carry valuables around the island.
  • Always wear a life vest when on a boat tour.
  • Listen to your local guide and follow their instructions for land and sea activities.

Health in Isabela

Although there are no major health risks for visiting Isabela, it’s recommended to take the following precautions:

  • Pack sunscreen, a hat, and a reusable water bottle to protect yourself from the sun and stay hydrated.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Isabela: Transportation Options and Logistics

Reaching Isabela can be challenging, but the journey and experience are worth it. Here are some options to get there:

Airports Near Isabela

  • Seymour Airport is the closest airport to Isabela where you can fly from the mainland or other islands in the Galapagos.

Bus Stations Near Isabela

  • There are no bus stations, but taxis can take you to the ports to catch a ferry or boat to the island.

Other Options

  • Ferry: You can take a ferry from Santa Cruz Islands and San Cristobal Island to Isabela. The trip takes 2-3 hours, and ferries hold around 30-40 passengers.
  • Speedboat: A fast option for getting to Isabela, but the ride can sometimes be bumpy.

How to Get Around in Isabela

Getting around Isabela is easy with various transportation modes, including:

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in the town and pueblo on the Island.
  • Bicycle Hire: This is the best way to explore the Island.
  • Boat Tour: This option is best for exploring surrounding Islands.

Where to Go Next from Isabela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time on your trip to Ecuador, here are some nearby destinations worth a visit:

  • San Cristobal Island: San Cristobal offers a similar experience to Isabela, with opportunities for wildlife viewing and natural beauty.
  • Santa Cruz Island: Santa Cruz is a hub for island-hopping tours and has a wide range of dining and shopping options.
  • Quito: The capital of Ecuador is rich in history, culture, and architecture, making it an excellent place to explore before or after Isabela.

Conclusion

Isabela, Ecuador’s natural paradise, offers an unforgettable experience full of adventure, culture, and nature. Use this comprehensive guide to help plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Isabela.
