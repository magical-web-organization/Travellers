Salto Travel Guide - Explore the Best of Northern Interior, Uruguay

Located in Northern Interior, Uruguay, Salto is a city with a rich history and vibrant culture. It is known for its hot springs, vineyards, and National Parks. In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive tour of Salto, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. Let’s jump right into it!

List of Places to Visit in Salto

If you are planning a trip to Salto, there are plenty of places to explore. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Salto:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Artigas Mausoleum
  • Museo del Hombre y la Tecnología
  • Museo de Bellas Artes

Natural Attractions of Salto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salto del Penitente Waterfall
  • Termas del Daymán hot springs
  • Parque Solari

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salto

Salto is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a variety of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Parque del Lago: a scenic trail around the lake.
  • Cerro Arequita: a strenuous hike to the top of the mountain.
  • Quebrada de los Cuervos National Park: a beautiful hike in the heart of the National Park.

Best Things to Do in Salto: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours in Salto for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Wine tasting at Bodegas de los Puentes.
  • Kayaking along the Rio Uruguay.
  • Horseback riding in the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salto

If you have a week to spare, we highly recommend the following itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Parque del Lago and Termas del Daymán hot springs
Day 2 Explore Quebrada de los Cuervos National Park
Day 3 Hike in Cerro Arequita and visit the Artigas Mausoleum
Day 4 Take a wine tour at Bodegas de los Puentes
Day 5 Kayak along Rio Uruguay
Day 6 Experience horseback riding in the countryside
Day 7 Relax at Parque Solari

Food and Drink Scene of Salto

One of the best parts of traveling to Salto is indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: grilled meat cooked over an open flame.
  • Milanesa: breaded and fried meat or chicken.
  • Chivito: a Uruguayan-style sandwich.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: a traditional herbal tea.
  • Tannat wine: a red wine that is Uruguay’s signature varietal.
  • Patricia beer: a popular lager in Uruguay.

Where to Stay in Salto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodations in Salto, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Mirador del Hotel: A luxury hotel with stunning panoramic views of the city.
  • Azahares del Ayui: A quiet and cozy guesthouse.
  • Hostel Salto: A budget-friendly hostel with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Salto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping in Salto is an unforgettable experience. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home:

  • Leather goods, including belts and wallets.
  • Handmade ceramics.
  • Local wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Salto

Uruguayan culture is heavily influenced by European traditions, especially Spanish and Italian. The people are friendly and welcoming, and they value family and community.

Best Time to Visit Salto

The best time to visit Salto is during the summer season, from December to March, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy the mild temperatures, visit during the shoulder season, from September to November or March to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salto

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Forget to carry cash at all times.
  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Ignore the traffic rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salto

Salto is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always important to take basic precautions like keeping your valuables safe and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Salto

Make sure to drink bottled water and wear insect repellent, especially when exploring outdoor attractions.

Getting to Salto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Salto is easily accessible by air, bus or car. Here are some travel tips:

Airports Near Salto

The closest airport to Salto is the Nueva Hesperides International Airport, which is located about 11 miles away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Salto

There are several bus stations near Salto where you can take a bus to other parts of Uruguay, including the Tres Cruces Bus Station in Montevideo.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around Salto and explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Salto

The easiest way to get around Salto is by taxi or rental car. However, if you want a more authentic experience, consider taking a bus or walking.

Where to Go Next from Salto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Salto:

  • Montevideo: the capital city of Uruguay.
  • Punta del Este: a popular beach resort town.
  • Colonia del Sacramento: a historic colonial city.

Conclusion

Salto is a wonderful city that offers a blend of history, culture and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re a foodie, nature enthusiast or history buff, Salto has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip to Salto and get ready for an unforgettable adventure. Happy travels!
