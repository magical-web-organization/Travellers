Introduction to Juan Fernández Islands

Juan Fernández Islands, located in the Pacific Ocean, about 670 km off the coast of Chile, is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. The archipelago, consisting of three islands: Robinson Crusoe, Alexander Selkirk, and Santa Clara, is a UNESCO World Biosphere Reserve with diverse flora and fauna. Home to many endemic species, including the Juan Fernández fur seal, the islands offer an exciting opportunity for visitors to explore unique landscapes, relax on pristine beaches, and indulge in local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Juan Fernández Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Juan Fernández Islands have a rich cultural history, and several museums showcase the island’s heritage. The Juan Fernández Island Museum in Robinson Crusoe has exhibits that cover the history of the island and its people, including the Robinson Crusoe legend. The Museo Histórico y Antropológico in Santa Clara is dedicated to preserving and showcasing artifacts and objects related to the indigenous Rapa Nui culture.

Natural Attractions of Juan Fernández Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Juan Fernández Islands offer some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views in the world. The rugged terrain of the islands makes it perfect for activities such as hiking, mountain biking, and bird watching. The islands are home to several endemic species of birds, including the Juan Fernández Firecrown and the Juan Fernández Tit-tyrant, making it a haven for bird enthusiasts. Visitors can also enjoy diving and snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters surrounding the islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Juan Fernández Islands

The Juan Fernández Islands offer some of the best hiking and biking trails in the world. The Parque Nacional Archipiélago de Juan Fernández, located on Robinson Crusoe Island, has several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s landscape. The trail to the highest point of the island, El Yunque, offers a panoramic view of the island and the surrounding sea. For biking enthusiasts, the gravel roads of the islands provide an excellent opportunity for adventure.

Best Things to Do in Juan Fernández Islands: Activities and Tours

The Juan Fernández Islands offer many exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. One of the island’s most popular activities is a boat tour to the Alejandro Selkirk Island. Visitors can explore the island’s beaches, hike through its forest and observe amazing wildlife in its natural habitat. Additionally, visitors can also go on guided tours to explore the various hiking routes and learn about the island’s fascinating history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Juan Fernández Islands

To make the most of your visit to the Juan Fernández Islands, we recommend a week-long itinerary. Day 1-2: Explore Robinson Crusoe Island, visit the Juan Fernández Island Museum and observe the endemic species of birds. Day 3: Boat tour to Alexander Selkirk Island. Day 4: Hike to El Yunque and enjoy panoramic views of the island. Day 5: Diving and snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of the islands. Day 6-7: Relax on the beaches of Santa Clara Island and visit the Museo Histórico y Antropológico.

Food and Drink Scene of Juan Fernández Islands

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene on the Juan Fernández Islands is a combination of Chilean and Peruvian cuisine, with a focus on seafood. One of the island’s most popular dishes is ceviche, a dish made with raw fish marinated in lime juice and spices. Other must-try dishes include Chilean-style empanadas, seafood stew, and lobster.

Local Beverages

The Juan Fernández Islands have a rich history of producing beer and wine. The islands’ microbrewery produces several types of beer, including pale ale and porter, and visitors can sample local wines made from the grapes grown in the islands’ vineyards.

Where to Stay in Juan Fernández Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Juan Fernández Islands offer a range of accommodations, from hotels to guesthouses, to suit different budgets and preferences. On Robinson Crusoe Island, visitors can stay at the Hotel Robinson Crusoe, which offers comfortable rooms and excellent service. The eco-lodge Lodge El Mirador de Santa Clara is a popular choice for those looking for a more remote experience.

What to Buy in Juan Fernández Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Juan Fernández Islands offer several unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. The islands’ microbrewery produces locally-made beer that visitors can take home as a souvenir. Additionally, visitors can purchase artisanal products such as handmade wooden carvings and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Juan Fernández Islands

The people of the Juan Fernández Islands have a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the island’s unique history and geography. The island’s indigenous Rapa Nui culture is characterized by its unique language, music, and dance. Visitors can experience the island’s cultural heritage through local festivals, such as the annual Rapa Nui Festival.

Best Time to Visit Juan Fernández Islands

The best time to visit the Juan Fernández Islands is from September to May, when the weather is mild and dry. The summer months (December to February) are the busiest and most expensive, but visitors can enjoy the island’s pristine beaches and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Juan Fernández Islands

Do:

  • Respect the island’s wildlife and flora.
  • Follow all safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Learn about the island’s rich cultural heritage and history.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island.
  • Disturb the island’s wildlife and flora.
  • Attempt to climb El Yunque without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Juan Fernández Islands

The Juan Fernández Islands are safe for visitors, but it’s important to follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities. Visitors should avoid swimming on the island’s beaches during high tide and should hire a guide when exploring the hiking trails.

Health in Juan Fernández Islands

Visitors to the Juan Fernández Islands should take precautions to avoid getting sick, such as drinking only bottled water and avoiding uncooked food. Additionally, visitors should ensure they are up to date with their routine vaccinations and are prepared to handle minor medical issues.

Getting to Juan Fernández Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Juan Fernández Islands

There are no airports on the Juan Fernández Islands. Visitors can fly to Santiago de Chile and then take a flight, which operates twice a week, to Robinson Crusoe Island.

Bus Stations Near Juan Fernández Islands

There are no bus stations on the Juan Fernández Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a boat from Valparaiso, which takes around 24-30 hours.

How to Get Around in Juan Fernández Islands

The Juan Fernández Islands are best explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a guide to explore the hiking trails and navigate the island’s remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Juan Fernández Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to the Juan Fernández Islands can extend their trip by visiting nearby destinations such as Easter Island or the Chilean mainland. Alternatively, visitors can explore the beautiful coastal towns of Valparaiso and Viña del Mar.

Conclusion

The Juan Fernández Islands offer visitors a unique opportunity to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Chile. Whether you’re interested in hiking, diving, or relaxing on the beach, the Juan Fernández Islands offer something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip with confidence and make the most of their time in this stunning destination.
