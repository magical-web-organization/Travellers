Introduction to Bellavista and North

Located in the heart of Santiago de Chile, Bellavista and North offer an eclectic mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. Bellavista is a bohemian neighborhood that has become a tourist hot-spot over the years, thanks to its winding streets, colorful houses, and vibrant nightlife. The North, on the other hand, is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and outdoor adventures.

This comprehensive tour guide will take you through all the must-visit places, best things to do, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options and logistics, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Bellavista and North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Chascona: former home of celebrated Chilean poet Pablo Neruda, now a museum
  • Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes: national museum of fine arts, featuring a vast collection of Chilean and international art, including works by Van Gogh and Matisse
  • Cerro San Cristobal: a hilltop park with stunning city views and the iconic Virgin Mary statue
  • Cementerio General de Chile: a historic cemetery with striking mausoleums and monuments
  • Palacio de La Moneda: the seat of Chilean government, with impressive architecture and a historical significance

Natural Attractions of Bellavista and North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Bicentenario: a scenic park that boasts a picturesque lake, gardens, playgrounds, and a bikeway
  • Cerro Santa Lucia: a hilltop park with well-maintained gardens, fountains, and panoramic views
  • Parque Forestal: a long and narrow park that runs along the Mapocho River and offers a perfect spot for a lazy afternoon picnic
  • La Vega Central: an enormous indoor and outdoor market famous for its fresh produce, seafood, and meat
  • Teleferico: a cable car ride with breathtaking views of the city and the Andes mountains

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bellavista and North

  • Cerro San Cristobal: an uphill hike that takes about 45 minutes to reach the top, but the views are worth it
  • BiciTour Santiago: a guided bike tour of Bellavista and North that takes you through the major landmarks and hidden gems
  • Sendero Mirador: a moderate hike that rewards you with stunning views of the city and the Andes mountains

Best Things to Do in Bellavista and North: Activities and Tours

  • Street art tours of Bellavista: a guided tour that lets you discover the vibrant street art scene of the neighborhood
  • Wine tasting tours of the North: a guided tour of the wineries and vineyards near Bellavista with a chance to sample Chile’s famous wines
  • Nightlife in Bellavista: the neighborhood comes alive after dark, with a wide variety of bars, clubs, and live music venues
  • Shopping in Lastarria: a quaint and charming neighborhood with trendy shops, galleries, and cafes

Food and Drink Scene of Bellavista and North

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastel de choclo: a traditional Chilean dish made with sweetcorn and meat, similar to shepherd’s pie
  • Completo: a classic Chilean hot dog topped with avocado, tomato, and mayonnaise
  • Empanadas de pino: a savory pastry filled with meat, onions, and sometimes raisins and olives

Local Beverages

  • Pisco sour: a cocktail made with pisco, a clear grape brandy, lemon juice, simple syrup, and egg white
  • Chilean wine: the country’s wine industry is world-class, and there are plenty of wine tours and tastings to choose from.

Where to Stay in Bellavista and North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Casa Bellavista Hostel: a budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Bellavista, with comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace
  • Lastarria Boutique Hotel: a luxurious boutique hotel in the charming Lastarria neighborhood, with elegant rooms and a fine-dining restaurant
  • Matildas Hotel Boutique: a cozy and charming guesthouse in the North, with excellent service and a beautiful garden

What to Buy in Bellavista and North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lapis lazuli jewelry: a blue gemstone unique to Chile, available in various forms, including earrings, necklaces, and rings
  • Chilean wine: bottles of wine from the country’s many vineyards and wineries make for excellent souvenirs
  • Handicrafts: Bellavista and North are home to many artisan markets and shops, selling traditional Chilean handicrafts such as pottery, textiles, and woven baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Bellavista and North

  • Chileans are typically friendly and welcoming, and it is common to greet people with a kiss on the right cheek.
  • Tipping is not expected but is always appreciated for good service.
  • The siesta or afternoon nap is still a common practice, and many shops and restaurants close during this time.

Best Time to Visit Bellavista and North

The best time to visit Bellavista and North is during the spring (September-November) or the fall (March-May) when the temperature is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer (December-February) can be quite hot and crowded, while winter (June-August) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bellavista and North

Do:

  • Do explore the street art scene of Bellavista, with many murals and sculptures to discover around every corner
  • Do try the local street food, which is both delicious and affordable

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended in crowded areas, as pickpocketing can be a problem in some areas
  • Don’t forget to carry cash, as some vendors and shops may not accept credit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bellavista and North

Bellavista and North are generally safe for tourists, but like any other city, it is important to take basic precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Bellavista and North

The tap water is generally safe to drink in Santiago de Chile, and there are many hospitals and clinics in the area in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Bellavista and North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bellavista and North

  • Comodoro Arturo Merino Benitez International Airport (SCL) is the nearest international airport, about 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Bellavista and North

  • Terminal de Buses Santiago: the main bus terminal in Santiago, with many national and international routes.

Other Options

  • Uber and other ride-hailing apps are widely used in Santiago, and taxis are widely available.

How to Get Around in Bellavista and North

Walking or biking are the best ways to get around Bellavista and North, as the city is pedestrian-friendly. Taxis and public transportation systems such as the metro and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Bellavista and North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Valparaiso: a charming coastal town known for its colorful houses and street art, about 1.5 hours away from Santiago
  • Cajon del Maipo: a stunning nature reserve with towering peaks, rivers, and waterfalls, about 1 hour away from Santiago
  • Santiago wine valley: home to many wineries and vineyards, with tours and tastings available.
