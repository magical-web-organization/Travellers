Introduction to Sanhattan and East

Located in the heart of Santiago de Chile, Sanhattan is a financial and commercial district that boasts some of the city’s most modern buildings, luxurious hotels, and upscale restaurants. The district is characterized by its impressive skyscrapers, glitzy malls, and bustling streets filled with people dressed in business attire. East Santiago, on the other hand, is a more residential district that offers a glimpse into the city’s cultural heritage. This district is home to historic landmarks, street art, and vibrant markets. Together, Sanhattan and East form a unique blend of modernity and tradition that makes Santiago de Chile a fascinating destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Sanhattan and East

Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler to Sanhattan and East, there are plenty of attractions to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best places to visit in this area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sanhattan and East offer a wealth of cultural attractions that reflect the city’s rich history and diverse heritage. Some of the top sites worth checking out include:

  • La Moneda Palace: This historic landmark was originally built in the 18th century and serves as the seat of the president of Chile. Visitors can take a guided tour of the palace to learn about its history and admire its neoclassical architecture.

  • Museum of Memory and Human Rights: This museum showcases Chile’s recent past, including the dictatorship of Augusto Pinochet. Visitors can explore exhibits that chronicle the human rights abuses that occurred during this time and learn about the country’s ongoing efforts to hold perpetrators accountable.

  • Bellavista Neighborhood: This bohemian neighborhood is a hub of art, culture, and nightlife. Visitors can explore the colorful streets of Bellavista, visit historic landmarks like La Chascona (the former residence of the poet Pablo Neruda), and enjoy a drink or meal at one of the many restaurants and bars in the area.

Natural Attractions of Sanhattan and East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sanhattan and East are also home to several natural attractions that offer breathtaking views and outdoor adventures. These include:

  • Cerro San Cristobal: This towering hill is one of the most iconic landmarks in Santiago de Chile. Visitors can hike or take a cable car to the top to enjoy panoramic views of the city.

  • Parque Bicentenario: This beautiful park offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can stroll along the lake, admire the sculptures, and enjoy a picnic in the shade of the trees.

  • Santiago Metropolitan Park: This vast park covers over 700 hectares and offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, and horseback riding. Visitors can also explore the zoo, botanical garden, and outdoor pool in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sanhattan and East

Sanhattan and East are surrounded by natural beauty, and there are several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the area. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Parque Metropolitano Mountain Bike Trail: This 10-kilometer trail is perfect for mountain biking enthusiasts. It runs through the Santiago Metropolitan Park and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.

  • Cerro Provincia Hike: This demanding hike takes visitors to the top of Cerro Provincia, which offers panoramic views of the Andes Mountains and the Santiago Valley.

  • Paseo Balmaceda Bike Path: This scenic bike path runs along the Mapocho River and takes visitors past several landmarks, including the Museum of Fine Arts, Parque Forestal, and Santa Lucia Hill.

Best Things to Do in Sanhattan and East: Activities and Tours

Sanhattan and East are full of activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Some of the best things to do in this area include:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Chile is famous for its wine, and visitors can take a wine tasting tour of the nearby vineyards to sample some of the region’s finest varietals.

  • City Walking Tour: A walking tour is an excellent way to explore the historic landmarks and cultural highlights of Sanhattan and East. Visitors can learn about the city’s history, architecture, and art while taking in the sights at a leisurely pace.

  • Adventure Sports: Sanhattan and East offer a range of adventure sports, including paragliding, white water rafting, and skiing/snowboarding in the winter months.

Food and Drink Scene of Sanhattan and East

Chilean cuisine is famous for its fresh seafood, hearty meat dishes, and delectable desserts. Sanhattan and East offer plenty of options for foodies looking to sample some local delights. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanada: A savory pastry stuffed with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

  • Pastel de Choclo: A traditional dish made with ground beef, chicken, onions, and corn.

  • Churrasco: A sandwich made with grilled beef, tomato, avocado, and mayonnaise.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: A cocktail made with pisco (a type of grape brandy), lime juice, simple syrup, and egg white.

  • Chilean Wine: Chile is renowned for its wine, with local varietals like Carménère and Cabernet Sauvignon being particularly popular.

What to Buy in Sanhattan and East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sanhattan and East offer plenty of opportunities for shopping, from upscale malls to artisan markets. Some of the best things to buy in this area include:

  • Small pieces of Street Art: Various small art objects such as bottle cap keychains, stickers and magnets.

  • Wine: Chile’s wine industry is booming, and visitors can bring home a bottle of their favorite varietal as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sanhattan and East

Chilean culture is a mix of indigenous and Spanish influences, and Sanhattan and East offer a glimpse into this rich heritage. Some customs and cultural norms to keep in mind include:

  • Chileans are generally reserved and polite, and visitors are expected to greet people with a handshake or kiss on the cheek.

  • Dress code is semi-formal, in the sense that people dress business casual or smart casual even on weekends and for social events. Comfortable shoes are a must for walking around the city.

  • Chileans take pride in their food, and visitors are expected to sample the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Sanhattan and East

Santiago de Chile has a Mediterranean climate, with hot summers and cool winters. The best time to visit Sanhattan and East is during the spring (September-November) or fall (March-May) when the weather is pleasant and there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sanhattan and East

Here are some tips to help you navigate Sanhattan and East like a pro:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases. While many locals speak English, it’s always helpful to know a few key phrases.

  • Carry pesos with you. While credit cards are accepted in many places, it’s always a good idea to have some cash on hand.

  • Explore the neighborhoods on foot. The best way to see Sanhattan and East is by walking the streets and taking in the sights.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water. Chilean tap water is not potable, so be sure to drink bottled water or bring a water filter.

  • Don’t rely on public transportation. While there are buses and subways in Santiago de Chile, they can be crowded and confusing for first-time visitors.

  • Don’t ignore safety warnings. While Sanhattan and East are generally safe areas, it’s always a good idea to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions against theft and scams.

Safety and Health

Chile is a safe country overall, and Sanhattan and East are considered safe areas for travelers. However, visitors should still take standard precautions to ensure their safety and wellbeing. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Sanhattan and East

  • Keep valuables out of sight. Pickpocketing and purse snatching can occur in crowded areas, so keep your belongings close and secure.

  • Be cautious at night. While Sanhattan and East are generally safe areas, it’s still a good idea to avoid walking alone at night.

  • Stay aware of protests. Chile has seen occasional protests and demonstrations in recent years, and visitors should avoid these gatherings to avoid getting caught up in any violence.

Health in Sanhattan and East

  • Check with your doctor before traveling. Chile is a safe country, but visitors should still take necessary health precautions and get any necessary vaccinations before their trip.

  • Protect yourself from the sun. Santiago de Chile can get very sunny, so be sure to wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from harmful UV rays.

  • Watch out for altitude sickness. Some natural attractions in Sanhattan and East are located at high altitudes, which can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. Be sure to drink plenty of water and take things slow to prevent this from happening.
