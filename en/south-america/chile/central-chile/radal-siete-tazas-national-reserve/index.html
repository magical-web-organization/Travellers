The Ultimate Travel Guide to Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

Located in central Chile, in South America, Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve is a breathtaking destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. With its seven stunning waterfalls and picturesque scenery, this reserve is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Chile. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve.

Introduction to Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve spans over 9,000 hectares and is known for its magnificent waterfalls, rivers, and mountains. The reserve gets its name from the seven cataracts that flow into each other, forming natural pools that are perfect for swimming and relaxing. The reserve is also home to a diverse ecosystem, including native bird species, such as the Humboldt penguin and Andean condor.

List of Places to Visit in Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve has something for every type of traveler. Here is a list of places to visit and things to do in the reserve:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • None

Natural Attractions of Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Siete Tazas Waterfalls: The seven cascades (tazas) are the main attraction of the reserve. Visitors can hike through the lush forests to witness the waterfalls, swim in the natural pools, and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Altos de Lircay National Reserve: Located near Radal Siete Tazas, this national reserve is an ideal destination for hiking, rock climbing, and camping.
  • Rio Claro: A crystal-clear river that flows through the reserve, Rio Claro offers visitors a chance to kayak or raft through its rapids.
  • La Gloria viewpoint: The highest point in the reserve, La Gloria viewpoint provides panoramic views of the waterfalls and surrounding mountains.
  • Los Coipos waterfall: A stunning waterfall located in a remote area of the reserve, Los Coipos is worth the hike for its breathtaking beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve offers a range of hiking and biking trails for all levels of ability. Here are some popular trails in the reserve:

  • Los Peumos Trail: A 6-kilometer trail that takes visitors through a beautiful forest and ends at the Los Peumos waterfall.
  • El Canelo Trail: A 7-kilometer trail that takes hikers through the reserve’s lush forests, past the El Canelo waterfall, and up to the La Gloria viewpoint.
  • Las Invernadas Trail: A 9-kilometer trail that starts at the park’s entrance and takes hikers through the jungle, past the Siete Tazas waterfalls, and ends at the El Indio waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve: Activities and Tours

Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Canyoning: Explore Rio Claro and its rapids by rappelling down waterfalls and sliding down natural waterslides.
  • Horseback riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through the reserve’s forests and mountain trails.
  • Fishing: The reserve’s rivers and lakes are home to a variety of fish species, including trout and salmon. Visitors can try their hand at fishing with a tour guide.
  • Camping: There are several camping sites within the reserve, offering visitors a chance to sleep under the stars.
  • Night sky observation: The remote location of the reserve makes it an ideal spot for stargazing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve:

Day 1-2: Explore the Siete Tazas waterfalls and surrounding trails. Day 3: Take a full-day canyoning tour of Rio Claro. Day 4: Go horseback riding through the reserve’s forests. Day 5: Fish in one of the reserve’s lakes or rivers. Day 6: Camp overnight in the reserve. Day 7: End the trip with a scenic drive through the reserve and surrounding areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

Chilean cuisine is a blend of Spanish, indigenous, and other influences, making it a unique culinary experience. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanadas: A savory pastry filled with beef, chicken, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Churrasco: A thin steak served with avocado, tomato, and mayonnaise on a toasted roll.
  • Curanto: A traditional dish made with seafood, meat, and vegetables, cooked in a hole in the ground.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: A popular Chilean cocktail made with pisco, lemon juice, sugar, and egg white.
  • Wines: Chile is known for its excellent wines, and the reserve offers a chance to try local varieties.

Getting to Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve can be reached via various modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

The nearest major airport is Arturo Merino Benítez International Airport in Santiago, Chile, which is approximately a four-hour drive from the reserve.

Bus Stations Near Radal Siete Tazas National Reserve

There are several bus stations located near the reserve, including those in the cities of Talca and Curicó. Visitors can take a bus to Molina and then transfer to a local bus to reach the reserve.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private shuttle service to reach the reserve.
