Puerto Natales is a charming port town in the heart of Patagonia, Chile. It boasts an array of cultural, natural, and adventure attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic South American experience. In this guide, we’ll dive into everything you need to know about visiting Puerto Natales, from must-see sights to local customs and recommended accommodations.

Introduction to Puerto Natales

Puerto Natales is located in the Última Esperanza province of Chilean Patagonia, just steps away from the stunning Torres del Paine National Park. Known for its unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, Puerto Natales has become a popular destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike.

The town was founded in 1911 as a port for ships traveling across the Strait of Magellan. Today, it remains a hub for maritime commerce and a gateway to the natural wonders of Patagonia.

List of Places to Visit in Puerto Natales

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Puerto Bories: An old industrial sheep farming complex that has turned into an eco-luxury hotel. You can visit it to learn about the history of Patagonian sheep farming.

  • Museo Histórico Municipal de Puerto Natales: The local archaeological and historical museum, displaying interesting facts and items related to indigenous cultures and old times of Puerto Natales.

  • Galpón 33: An old cold storage facility converted into the cultural center of the town. Here you can find art exhibits, live music performances, and more.

Natural Attractions of Puerto Natales: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Balmaceda Glacier: Hop on a boat from Puerto Natales to explore this majestic glacier and its surrounding marine life.

  • Cueva del Milodón Natural Monument: A giant cave that holds the history of the extinct animal “milodon” that lived in the area 12,000 years ago.

  • Torres del Paine National Park: The crown jewel of Patagonia offers breathtaking vistas, stunning hikes, and outdoor activities like horseback riding and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puerto Natales

  • Sendero de Chile: The Chilean National Trail has plenty of hiking routes that cover Patagonia’s beautiful landscapes, with many routes starting from Puerto Natales.

  • Biking route to Dorotea lookout: Take the bike to a fantastic viewpoint that offers views of Puerto Natales and Last Hope Sound.

  • Laguna Esmeralda: This spectacular hike is an eight-hour round trip that takes you through forests, along streams, and to a series of stunning emerald lagoons.

Best Things to Do in Puerto Natales: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking in Last Hope Sound: Enjoy a kayaking tour amidst stunning scenic views.

  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided horseback tour through the fields and mountains of Patagonia.

  • Whale Watching: Experience the breathtaking sight of humpback whales and other marine life off the coast of Puerto Natales.

Food and Drink Scene of Puerto Natales

Must-Try Dishes

  • Centolla: Local crab, a must in any seafood lover’s bucket list.

  • Cordero Magallánico: Patagonian lamb cooked to perfection, for the meat lovers.

  • Curanto: A traditional dish made with meat, seafood, and potatoes, typically cooked in a hole in the ground.

Local Beverages

  • Calafate Sour: A cocktail made with Calafate berries, a native fruit from Patagonia.

  • Pisco Sour: This classic drink is popular throughout Chile, but it’s especially good with dinner at restaurants in town.

Where to Stay in Puerto Natales: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Singular Patagonia: An eco-luxury hotel set in the restored Puerto Bories cold-storage plant.

  • Weskar Lodge: A hotel located outside of town that offers modern rooms, a panoramic view of the fjord, and a peaceful retreat.

  • Erratic Rock Hostel: A budget-friendly option known for its friendly and welcoming vibe.

What to Buy in Puerto Natales: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sheepskin Products: Patagonian sheep farming is pretty remarkable, and their wool’s products are perfect for souvenirs.

  • Calafate Jam or Liqueur: A sweet treat made with the delicious, deeply hued Patagonian berry.

Local Culture and Customs of Puerto Natales

Puerto Natales has a unique culture, influenced by the region’s harsh geography, indigenous history, and rugged lifestyle. Expect a warm, welcoming people, along with tasty food and drinks.

Best Time to Visit Puerto Natales

The best time to visit Puerto Natales is between November and March, during the summer months when the temperatures are warmer and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puerto Natales

Do:

  • Try local seafood dishes: The seafood in Puerto Natales is some of the freshest in the world and doesn’t disappoint.

  • Pack accordingly: The weather in Puerto Natales can be unpredictable, so pack layers for all types of weather.

Don’t:

  • Overplan: You don’t want to miss out on spontaneous moments while exploring Puerto Natales.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puerto Natales

Puerto Natales is relatively safe, but it’s always good to be aware of your surroundings and take standard safety precautions.

Health in Puerto Natales

Before your trip to Puerto Natales, make sure your regular vaccinations are updated. Also, be careful when consuming tap water. Stick to bottled water.

Getting to Puerto Natales: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puerto Natales

The closest airport to Puerto Natales is the Teniente Julio Gallardo Airport, located approximately 10 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Puerto Natales

Buses from Punta Arenas or El Calafate (Argentina) service the bus station of Puerto Natales.

Other Options

You can also take a private shuttle or rent a car from nearby cities like Punta Arenas.

How to Get Around in Puerto Natales

Puerto Natales is compact, so it’s easy to get around on foot. Car and bike rentals are also available in town.

Where to Go Next from Puerto Natales: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Torres del Paine National Park: Expand your journey in Patagonia with additional days in one of the most beautiful parks in the world.

  • El Calafate, Argentina: Visit Perito Moreno Glacier and explore Los Glaciares National Park.

Conclusion

Puerto Natales is a destination that shouldn’t go unnoticed. With a wide variety of activities and attractions to choose from, it is a perfect place for adventure, relaxation, and learning about the unique culture of Patagonia. By following this guide, you’ll be able to plan a memorable, safe, and enjoyable trip to Puerto Natales, Chile.
