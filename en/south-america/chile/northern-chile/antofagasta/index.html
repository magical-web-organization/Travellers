Food and Drink Scene of Antofagasta

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Antofagasta is a fusion of indigenous and Spanish cuisine, with a lot of emphasis on seafood. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Machas a la Parmesana
  • Locos con Mayonesa
  • Chupe de Camarones
  • Churrasco
  • Pastel de Jaiba

Local Beverages

Chile is famous for its wine, and Antofagasta has a number of wineries in the surrounding regions. Some of the best local beverages to try are:

  • Pisco Sour
  • Carmenere and Cabernet Sauvignon wines
  • Mote con Huesillo (sweet peach drink)

Where to Stay in Antofagasta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Antofagasta has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • NH Antofagasta
  • Hotel Terrado Suites
  • Hostal Reina del Mar
  • Terrantai Lodge
  • Hotel Patrimonial

What to Buy in Antofagasta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back from Antofagasta are:

  • Alpaca wool products
  • Carvings and pottery made by local artisans
  • Chilean wine
  • Pisco

Local Culture and Customs of Antofagasta

Antofagasta, like the rest of Chile, has a unique blend of indigenous and Spanish cultures. Customs and traditions here include:

  • Celebrating New Year with fireworks and family gatherings
  • La Tirana Festival
  • Chimuchina dance
  • Goat barbecue (asado de chivo)

Best Time to Visit Antofagasta

Antofagasta has a warm and dry climate, with temperatures ranging from 18°C in winter to 25°C in summer. The best time to visit is from September to May, when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities can be enjoyed fully.

Do’s and Don’ts of Antofagasta

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially seafood dishes
  • Explore the natural attractions, especially La Portada and the Atacama Desert
  • Learn about the culture and history of Antofagasta

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, always opt for bottled water
  • Show disrespect towards indigenous customs and traditions
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Antofagasta

Antofagasta is a safe city, but like any other place, petty theft can occur. Travelers should take precautions like keeping valuable items close and being aware of their surroundings when in crowded areas.

Health in Antofagasta

Antofagasta doesn’t have any major health concerns. However, travelers should be wary of sunburn and dehydration due to the dry climate, and should ensure they have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Antofagasta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Antofagasta can be reached by air, bus, and car. Some of the best options are:

Airports Near Antofagasta

Bus Stations Near Antofagasta

Other Options

  • Taking a taxi or private transfer

How to Get Around in Antofagasta

Antofagasta has public buses, taxis, and car rentals available for transport. Walking and biking are also popular options, especially when exploring the city centre.

Where to Go Next from Antofagasta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions that can be taken from Antofagasta are:

  • San Pedro de Atacama
  • Iquique
  • Taltal
  • Cerro Paranal Observatory

Conclusion

Antofagasta is a must-visit destination in Chile for travelers who enjoy natural beauty, cultural experiences, and great food and drink.
