Introduction to Northern Chile

Northern Chile boasts a diverse array of landscapes ranging from the towering Andes Mountains, arid Atacama Desert, serene beaches, and lush valleys. This region also offers a plethora of natural and cultural attractions that will surely make your trip to Chile memorable.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Chile

Whether you are interested in exploring the cultural or natural attractions, Northern Chile has something for everyone. Here are some of the places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northern Chile has a rich cultural history that you can explore through its museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • La Moneda Palace in Santiago
  • Museo Chileno de Arte Precolombino in Santiago
  • Museo Arqueológico San Miguel de Azapa in Arica
  • Pukará de Quitor in San Pedro de Atacama
  • Humberstone and Santa Laura Saltpeter Works in Iquique

Natural Attractions of Northern Chile: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural scenery in Northern Chile is breathtaking. Here are some natural attractions that you wouldn’t want to miss:

  • Parque Nacional Lauca
  • Valle de la Luna in San Pedro de Atacama
  • El Tatio Geysers in San Pedro de Atacama
  • Salar de Atacama in San Pedro de Atacama
  • Punta de Lobos in Pichilemu

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Chile

If you are a hiking or biking enthusiast, Northern Chile will offer you some of the most spectacular trails with breathtaking views. Here are some popular hiking/biking trails:

  • Cerro San Cristóbal in Santiago
  • Mirador Los Cóndores in La Serena
  • Parque Nacional Lauca
  • Valle de la Luna in San Pedro de Atacama
  • Putre-Parinacota Trek in Putre

Best Things to Do in Northern Chile: Activities and Tours

Northern Chile offers a variety of activities and tours for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Stargazing in the Atacama Desert
  • Surfing in Pichilemu
  • Visiting the geysers in El Tatio
  • Sandboarding in the Atacama Desert
  • Trekking in the Andes

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Chile

For those who have a week to explore Northern Chile, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Santiago
2 Visit La Serena and the Elqui Valley
3 Tour the Atacama Desert
4 Trek the Andes
5 Go surfing in Pichilemu
6 Visit the Elqui Valley
7 Return to Santiago

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Chile

Northern Chile’s culinary scene is heavily influenced by the indigenous cultures and Spanish colonialism. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanadas de Pino
  • Cazuela
  • Humitas
  • Pastel de Jaiba
  • Curanto

Local Beverages

  • Pisco sour
  • Chilean wine
  • Mote con Huesillo

Where to Stay in Northern Chile: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Chile offers different accommodation options ranging from backpacker hostels to luxury lodges. Here are some places you should consider staying:

  • RQ Santiago Suites in Santiago
  • Hostal el Arbol in La Serena
  • Hotel La Casa de Don Tomas in San Pedro de Atacama
  • Terrantai Lodge in San Pedro de Atacama
  • Cabañas en Pichilemu

What to Buy in Northern Chile: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Chile is famous for its unique products, including handicrafts, wine, and Alpaca wool products. Here are some of the best souvenirs to take home:

  • Lapis lazuli jewelry
  • Handwoven textiles
  • Alpaca wool clothes
  • Pisco and wines

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Chile

The culture of Northern Chile is heavily influenced by the indigenous communities and Spanish colonialism. Here are some local customs and traditions you might encounter:

  • Respect for the indigenous people and their traditions
  • Siesta (midday nap) is a common practice
  • Greeting others with a kiss on the cheek

Best Time to Visit Northern Chile

The best time to visit Northern Chile is between September to November and March to May when the temperature is comfortable and rainfall is at a minimum.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Chile

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish phrases to communicate with locals
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove any archaeological artifacts
  • Wear revealing clothes while visiting religious sites
  • Be unprepared for the altitude while hiking in the mountains

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Chile

Northern Chile is generally safe, but some areas can be prone to natural disasters such as earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. It is essential to be aware of these circumstances and stay informed.

Health in Northern Chile

It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid tap water to avoid stomach illnesses. It is also important to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Northern Chile: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Chile

The major airports in Northern Chile are:

  • Comodoro Arturo Merino Benitez International Airport in Santiago
  • El Tepual International Airport in Puerto Montt
  • Diego Aracena International Airport in Iquique

Bus Stations Near Northern Chile

The major bus stations in Northern Chile are:

  • Terminal de Buses La Paz in Santiago
  • Terminal de Buses ChileBus in La Serena
  • Terminal de Buses de Arica

Other Options

There are also options for car rentals and private tours, which offer a more flexible and personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Northern Chile

The most convenient and affordable way to get around Northern Chile is by public transportation or guided tours. Taxis and rental cars are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Northern Chile: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations and excursions after exploring Northern Chile include:

  • Torres del Paine National Park in Patagonia
  • Valparaíso and Viña del Mar in central Chile
  • Easter Island, known for the Moai statues

Conclusion

Exploring Northern Chile is an unforgettable experience that offers a mix of culture, adventure, and stunning landscapes. From the Andes Mountains to the Atacama Desert, you’ll find plenty of unique experiences in this South American gem.
